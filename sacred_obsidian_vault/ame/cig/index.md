 <p>
 This is a folklore miscellany originally published by the
 Texas Folk-lore society in 1923, and reprinted in 1935.
 The articles reflect the cultural diversity of Texas, including
 articles on African-American, Tejano, German and Native American folklore.
 One of the highlights is <a href="cig12.htm">an interview</a>
 with W.C. Handy, 'the father of the blues;'
 the lyrics in this piece foreshadow some of the
 great rock compositions of the sixties.
 The articles vary widely from academic to amateur in tone.
 As usual, the original text has been transcribed verbatim. 
 It reflects the time and place in which it
 was written, and includes ethnic terms and characterizations which today
 would be considered wildly offensive, even in an ethnographic context.
 <i>Caveat lector</i>.
 </p>
 <p>
 Thanks to Eliza Yetter for transcribing this collection.
