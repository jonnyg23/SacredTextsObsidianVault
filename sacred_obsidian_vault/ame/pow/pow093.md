 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Pow-Wows, or Long Lost Friend</i>, by John George Hoffman, [1820], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> 
 <p align="CENTER">CURE FOR THE TOOTHACHE.</p> 
 <p>Hohman, the author of this book, has cured the severest toothache more than sixty times, with this remedy.</p> 
 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="green">p. 31</font></a></p> 
 <p>and, out of the sixty times he applied it, it failed but once in affecting a cure. Take blue vitriol and put a piece of it in the hollow tooth, yet not too much; spit out the water that collects in the mouth, and be careful to swallow none. I do not know whether it is good for teeth that are not hollow, but I should judge it would cure any kind of toothache.</p>
