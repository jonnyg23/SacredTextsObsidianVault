 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Pow-Wows, or Long Lost Friend</i>, by John George Hoffman, [1820], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> 
 <p align="CENTER">A VERY GOOD PLASTER.</p> 
 <p>I doubt very much whether any physician in the United States can make a plaster equal to this. It heals the white swelling, and has cured the sore leg of a woman who for eighteen years had used the prescriptions of doctors in vain.</p> 
 <p>Take two quarts of cider, one pound of bees-wax, one pound of sheep-tallow, and one pound of tobacco; boil the tobacco in the cider till the strength is out, and then strain it, and add the other articles to the liquid: stir it over a gentle fire till all is dissolved.</p>
