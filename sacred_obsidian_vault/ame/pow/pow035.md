 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Pow-Wows, or Long Lost Friend</i>, by John George Hoffman, [1820], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> 
 <p align="CENTER">TO MAKE A GOOD EYE-WATER.</p> 
 <p>Take four cents' worth of white vitriol, four cents' worth of prepared spicewort (calamus root), four cents'</p> 
 <p><a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="green">p. 18</font></a></p> 
 <p>worth of cloves, a gill of good whiskey and a gill of water. Make the calamus fine and mix all together; then use it after it has stood a few hours.</p>
