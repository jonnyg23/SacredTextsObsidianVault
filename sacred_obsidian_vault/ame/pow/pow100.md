 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Pow-Wows, or Long Lost Friend</i>, by John George Hoffman, [1820], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> 
 <p align="CENTER">A VERY GOOD PLASTER.</p> 
 <p>Take wormwood, rue, medels, sheeprip-wort, pointy plantain, in equal proportions, a larger proportion of bees'-wax and tallow, and some spirits of turpentine; put it together in a pot, boil it well, and then strain it, and you have a very good plaster.</p>
