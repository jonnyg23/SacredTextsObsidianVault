 <hr>
 
 <p>
 Finding texts about Australian Aboriginal religion in the public
 domain was extremely difficult.
 There is a landslide of 19th and early 20th
 Century books and articles about American and African 
 indigenous traditions, many treating the subject with
 sensitivity and great depth.
 Only a few Australian works from this period are available,
 and most of them are pretty vague on the details.
 Some of this can be attributed to the traditional tribal secrecy,
 which is maintained even to this day.
 </p>
 
 <p>
 Please be mindful of the historical context in which these books were written.
 The obvious problem with these books is that they were
 written by White Australians during the early twentieth century.
 This material contains racist and sexist
 characterizations, inaccurate depictions
 of aboriginal life and misrepresentations of their spiritual practices.
 Some of these documents contain completely fictional material which was the
 invention of the author, and have no actual basis in aboriginal belief or
 traditions.
 Descriptions of some of the ceremonies in these documents
 or the naming of dead people may regrettably violate Aboriginal tabus.
 These texts are provided for scholarly purposes and should not be
 considered definitive material on Aboriginal culture, only as
 a starting point for discussion.</p>
 <p>In short, we encourage you to 'read between the lines'.</p>
 
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Ethnographic Descriptions</h3>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../pac/om/index.htm">Oceanic Mythology</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Roland B. Dixon</span>. [<span class="c_d">1916</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b"><a href="../pac/om/om24.htm">Part five of this book</a>
 contains an overview of Australian mythology, as well as
 comparative material on other Pacific cultures.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ntna/index.htm">Native Tribes of the Northern Territory of Australia</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Baldwin Spencer</span>. [<span class="c_d">1914</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">This massive ethnography is one of the few books in the public domain which details pre-20th Century Aboriginal culture and traditions in a non-ethnocentric perspective.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ntca/index.htm">The Native Tribes of Central Australia</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Baldwin Spencer</span> and <span class="c_a">F. J. Gillen</span> [<span class="c_d">1899</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A primary source for ethnographic information on the Aboriginal culture of central Australia including details of kinship, ceremonies and everyday life.</span>
 </span>
 <br>
 
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Works of K. Langloh Parker</h3>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="alt/index.htm">Australian Legendary Tales</a></span><br>
 collected by <span class="c_a">K. Langloh Parker</span>, Introduction by <span class="c_a">Andrew Lang</span>. [<span class="c_d">1897</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">This is a turn of the century collection of folklore of the Euahlayi (Noongahburrah).</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="tet/index.htm">The Euahlayi Tribe</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">K. Langloh Parker</span>. Introduction by <span class="c_a">Andrew Lang</span>. [<span class="c_d">1905</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">An amateur ethnography of the Eulayhi.  This book includes more depth on the culture, spiritual beliefs and ceremonies of the Euahlayi.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
