 <p><a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="green">p. 78</font></a> <a name="page_79"><font size="1" color="green">p. 79</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">The Fifth Tablet</h1>  
 <p>1. He.(i.e. Marduk) made the stations for the great gods;</p>  
 <p>2. The stars, their images, as the stars of the Zodiac, he fixed.</p>  
 <p>3. He ordained the year and into sections he divided it;</p>  
 <p>4. For the twelve months he fixed three stars.</p>  
 <p>5. After he had [...] the days of the year [...] images,</p>  
 <p>6. He founded the station of Nibir <a name="fr_151"></a><a href="#fn_151"><font size="1">1</font></a>to determine their bounds;</p>  
 <p>7. That none might err or go astray,</p>  
 <p>8. He set the station of Bêl and Ea along with him.</p>  
 <p>9. He opened great gates on both sides,</p>  
 <p>10. He made strong the bolt on the left and on the right.</p>  
 <p>11. In the midst thereof he fixed the zenith;</p>  
 <p>12. The Moon-god he caused to shine forth, the night he entrusted to him.</p>  
 <p>13. He appointed him, a being of the night, to determine the days;</p>  
 <p>14. Every month without ceasing with the crown he covered(?) him, (saying):</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_80"><font size="1" color="green">p. 80</font></a> <a name="page_81"><font size="1" color="green">p. 81</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. "At the beginning of the month, when thou shinest upon the land,</p>  
 <p>16. "Thou commandest the horns to determine six days,</p>  
 <p>17. "And on the seventh day to [divide] the crown.</p>  
 <p>18. "On the fourteenth day thou shalt stand opposite, the half [...].</p>  
 <p>19. "When the Sun-god on the foundation of heaven [...] thee,</p>  
 <p>20. "The [...] thou shalt cause to ..., and thou shalt make his [...].</p>  
 <p>21. "[...] ... unto the path of the Sun-god shalt thou cause to draw nigh,</p>  
 <p>22. "[And on the ... day] thou shalt stand opposite, and the Sun-god shall ... [...]</p>  
 <p>23. "[...] to traverse her way.</p>  
 <p>24. "[...] thou shalt cause to draw nigh, and thou shalt judge the right.</p>  
 <p>25. "[...] to destroy</p>  
 <p>26. "[...] me.</p>  
 <p>"..."</p>
