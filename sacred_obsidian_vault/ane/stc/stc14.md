 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="green">p. 128</font></a> <a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="green">p. 129</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">IV. An Address to the River of Creation <a name="fr_178"></a><a href="#fn_178"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>1. O, thou River, who didst create all things,</p>  
 <p>2. When the great gods dug thee out,</p>  
 <p>3. They set prosperity upon thy banks,</p>  
 <p>4. Within thee Ea, the King of the Deep, created his dwelling,</p>  
 <p>5. The deluge they sent not before thou wert!</p>  
 <p>6. Fire, and wrath, and splendour, and terror</p>  
 <p>7. Have Ea and Marduk presented unto thee!</p>  
 <p>8. Thou judgest the cause of mankind!</p>  
 <p>9. O, River, thou art mighty! O River, thou art supreme! O River, thou art righteous!</p>
