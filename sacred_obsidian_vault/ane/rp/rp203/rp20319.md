Sayce, [1890], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VIII.</h4>  <h5 align="center">No. II <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">4</font></a></h5>  
 <p align="center"><small>OBVERSE</small></p>  
 <p>1. [To Nim]muariya king of the land of [<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>] my brother, my son-in-law,<br> 2. [whom I] love (and) who loves [me], speak<br> 3. [thus:] Dusratta the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mi[tanni]</span>, thy father-in-law,<br> 4. loves thee as thy brother; [unto] me is peace; unto thee</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. [may there be] peace; to thy house, to [thy] wives, to thy sons, to thy nobles,<br> 6. [to] thy chariots, to thy horses, to thy officers,<br> 7. to thy country and thy property may there be exceeding abundant peace!</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>8. Manê the ambassador of my brother went to demand a wife for<br> 9. my brother that he might take her to be queen of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>, and the letter <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_310"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 10. which he took I read, and to his message listened<br> 11. my good heart attentively, and the words of my brother as the person of my brother<br> 12. I saw and rejoiced on that day very exceedingly.<br> 13. Day and night it produced pleasure. <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_311"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>14. And all the words of my brother which Manê took [to me]<br> 15. I performed in the same <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_312"><font size="1">3</font></a> year; again the wife of my brother,<br> 16. the queen of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>, I … and have despatched to my brother;<br> 17. … on that day the countries of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khani-rabbat</span> <a name="fr_314"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">4</font></a> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>18. And as regards this, Manê will [speak?] before my brother: Giliya <a name="fr_315"></a><a href="#fn_314"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> 19. and Manê I [presented with?] a costly present, but I did not address them.<br> 20. Again, O my brother, for … and for [their] dismissal …</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. But I did not cause them to go up (to Egypt) to [convey my daughter] that she might be the wife of [my] brother;<br> 22. even now I did not cause them to go up.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>23. After six months Giliya my ambassador and Manê the [ambassador]<br> 24. [of] my brother I dismissed; the wife of my brother, [to be queen of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>],<br> 25. [my daughter], to my brother they brought. May <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Beltis</span> the lady [of battle]<br> 26. my [goddess], and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amânu</span> <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">1</font></a> the god of my brother according to [his heart]<br> 27. advise <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">2</font></a> [him].</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>28. [His wife] to my brother they brought, and when my brother [had children by her]<br> 29. his son grew up stoutly and also …<br> 30. … according to the heart of my brother he became tall, <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">3</font></a> and …<br> 31. … which to give my brother …<br> 32. … above the face …</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>33. [Now Na]khâramassi has spoken, O my brother, a word to [my brother],<br> 34. … and a letter <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">4</font></a> into his hand I have given …<br> 35. … and his words may [my brother] hear, [saying]<br> 36. [thus: Nakharâ]massi to my brother I have sent …<br> 37. the soldiers [of] my brother have not made …</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Lacuna.</i></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>REVERSE</small></p>  
 <p><i>The commencement is lost</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. … of my brother they have brought …</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. … my [ambassador] has collected everything, O [my] brother.<br> 3. … before all of them, and we …<br> 4. … among them, and these seals and the gold …<br> 5. … and they regarded (them) very intently;<br> 6. they [considered] these treasures <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">1</font></a> as water, all of them, treasures of gold without [number];<br> 7. [and] they said: "in the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> (are) treasures of gold, more than the dust in abundance."<br> 8. [My heart], O my brother, now loves thee in rivalry; the men …<br> 9. … whom they love, and yet nothing is given to their backs.<br> 10. [There are] quantities of what is needed, quantities more than the dust in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> in plenty.<br> 11. As myself (are) these men; to whom is the gift made? …<br> 12. … and it has no gate; and truly when I [heard of] the treasures<br> 13. [which lie] before you, I did not question if he loves me very greatly<br> 14. [and] my country, even the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>15. And my brother lifts me up in his heart when my heart was vexed at aught;<br> 16. and he does not <i>fail</i> (?) at any time, and the great god my lord does not forsake me;<br> 17. Along with my brother am I not <i>remembered</i> (?) by thee? and to country and city …<br> 18. like my brother …</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>19. Manê the ambassador of my brother [and the dragoman] of my brother who with him<br> 20. journeyed, I honoured: [I spoke to] them and honoured them</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="green">p. 78</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. exceedingly; now Manê has departed, and my brother<br> 22. has exalted him even as I honoured him very greatly.<br> 23. To my brother also I spoke, and my brother heard from them how<br> 24. I had done unto them: when Manê lacked (aught which) is not supplied<br> 25. to him, he indeed is not vexed.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>26. And, O my brother, I have sent much worked <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">1</font></a> gold which cannot be counted, and the <i>aqqât</i><br> 27. of my father may my brother return to me, as my brother regards friendship.<br> 28. As my brother before my country, as before my <i>numerous</i> (?) brethren,<br> 29. my brother has caused me greatly to flourish, so may the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanu</span> <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>sustain</i> (?) (him), and<br> 30. act for ever according to the heart of my brother, and may my brother,<br> 31. my beloved one, act according to my heart, like the Sun-god<br> 32. who loves mankind, and like the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Na-nînu</span> may the gods<br> 33. direct us, and for ever<br> 34. let us love [one another].</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>35. [And now I send] by way of a present one spear of <i>ubbi</i> wood<br> 36. … and an <i>isizzu</i> of Aleppo <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">3</font></a> stone set in gold<br> 37. … [which] he folds together on his throne<br> 38. … <i>khulal</i> stone set in gold for a throne<br> 39. … to my brother I have sent.</p>
