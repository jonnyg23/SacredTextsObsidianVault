 <p><a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="green">p. 61</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">I.—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Letter of Assur-yuballidh, King of Assyria, to Amenophis IV, King Of Egypt</span> <a name="fr_243"></a><a href="#fn_243"><font size="1">1</font></a></h4>  
 <p>1. To Napkhurîya (Neferu-kheper-Ra) [the great king]<br> 2. the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>, <a name="fr_244"></a><a href="#fn_244"><font size="1">2</font></a> my brother, [I write]<br> 3. thus, (even I) Assur-yuballidh king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyria</span>,<br> 4. the great king, thy brother.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>5. To thyself, to thy house and thy country may there be peace!</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>6. That I have seen thy ambassadors<br> 7. has pleased (me) greatly; thy ambassadors<br> 8. I have sent for to appear in my presence.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>9. A chariot [the choicest?] in the kingdom with (its) harness<br> 10. and two white horses, [together with]<br> 11. one chariot without harness and a seal of white alabaster<br> 12. I have despatched as a present for thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>13. For the great king is produced perpetually<br> 14. the gold (which) in thy country (is like) the dust</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="green">p. 62</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. (that) they collect: why in thy presence<br> 16. is it brought and kept back, is it withheld and not sent?<br> 17. All the gold that is my property,<br> 18. as well as what is lacking to it, send.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>19. When Asur-nadin-akhi my father<br> 20. sent (an embassy) to the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> (<i>Mitsri</i>),<br> 21. 20 talents of gold did they despatch to him.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>22. When the king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khani-rabbatû</span> <a name="fr_245"></a><a href="#fn_245"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 23. to thy father and the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span><br> 24. sent (an embassy), 20 talents of gold<br> 25. did they despatch to him.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>26. [As] to the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khani-[rab]bati</span><br> 27. [so] also to myself<br> 28. despatch the gold.<br> 29. [The road both in] going and returning<br> 30. for the hands of my ambassadors<br> 31. I have made secure. <a name="fr_246"></a><a href="#fn_246"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>32. If thou inclinest thy face <a name="fr_247"></a><a href="#fn_247"><font size="1">3</font></a> favourably<br> 33. despatch much gold, and thy letter in return<br> 34. write (to me) and what thou desirest let them take.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>35. Behold, distant <a name="fr_248"></a><a href="#fn_248"><font size="1">4</font></a> lands<br> 36. have the ambassadors visited <a name="fr_249"></a><a href="#fn_249"><font size="1">5</font></a> and they have journeyed to (many) cities.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="green">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. As for thy ambassadors<br> 38. they have delayed on the way because the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">’Suti</span> <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_250"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 39. threatened <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_251"><font size="1">2</font></a> them with death, until I sent and<br> 40. the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">’Suti</span> took <i>fright</i> (?) <a name="fr_252"></a><a href="#fn_252"><font size="1">3</font></a>.<br> 41. My ambassadors … them<br> 42. [and] they did not delay.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>43. When the ambassadors (reached) the frontier (of Assyria)<br> 44. why do they not wait? <a name="fr_253"></a><a href="#fn_253"><font size="1">4</font></a> and<br> 45. at the frontier they <i>are in a hurry</i> (?) <a name="fr_254"></a><a href="#fn_254"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> 46. <i>It is fitting</i> (?) at the frontier they should wait<br> 47. for the king; everything is there<br> 48. and he has established (it) and at the frontier<br> 49 he has arranged (it). Against the king who fulfils everything<br> 50. there is no <i>charge</i> (?): why<br> 51. at the frontier are they <i>in a hurry</i> (?),<br> 52. even the ambassadors who …</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>The last three lines are too obliterated for translation</i>.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_243"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_243">61:1</a> Winckler and Abel: <i>Mittheilungen</i>, i. p. 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_244"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_244">61:2</a> Written Mitstsari as in the letters of the king of Mitanni. The Babylonian form of the name is Mitsri, corresponding to the Hebrew Matsor and Mitsraim. In the later Assyrian inscriptions the name appears as Mutsur or Mutsri, probably through confusion with the name of Mutsri, a district to the north of Assyria.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_245"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_245">62:1</a> "Khani the great," or Eastern Kappadokia, the capital of which was Malatiyeh.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_246"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_246">62:2</a> <i>Ultammatstsi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_247"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_247">62:3</a> Literally, "If thou art good as to thy face."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_248"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_248">62:4</a> <i>Ruqatum</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_249"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_249">62:5</a> <i>Ilika</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_250"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_250">63:1</a> Nomads, who ranged through the desert on either side of the Tigris, in Mesopotamia and the south-east of Assyria.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_251"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_251">63:2</a> <i>Ura‘u</i>, the Heb. <i>yârê</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_252"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_252">63:3</a> Probably to be read <i>khardê</i>, "fear."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_253"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_253">63:4</a> <i>Ultannazazu</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_254"></a><a href="rp20312.htm#fr_254">63:5</a> <i>Imattu;</i> the verb <i>imati</i> occurs in K 1282, Rev. 18.</p>
