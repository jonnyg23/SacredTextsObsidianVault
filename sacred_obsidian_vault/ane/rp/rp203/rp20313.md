Sayce, [1890], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">II.—LETTER OF BURNA-BURYAS, KING OF BABYLONIA, TO AMENOPHIS IV OF EGYPT</span> <a name="fr_255"></a><a href="#fn_255"><font size="1">6</font></a></h4>  
 <p align="center"><small>OBVERSE</small></p>  
 <p>1. To Nipkhurri-riya king of the country of [<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>]<br> 2. by letter<br> 3. I speak (even I) Burra-buriyas king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kara-duniyas</span> <a name="fr_256"></a><a href="#fn_256"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. thy brother: unto myself (is) peace;<br> 5. to thyself, thy house, thy wives, thy children, thy country,<br> 6. thy officers, thy horses (and) thy chariots, may there ever be peace!<br> 7. Ever since my father and thy father with one another</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>8. conferred in amity,<br> 9. they sent beautiful present[s] to one another,<br> 10. but they did not address one another in fair (and) beautiful letter[s].<br> 11. Again, O my brother, 2 manehs of gold I have sent as my present.<br> 12. In return send (me) abundance of gold as much as thy father (sent),<br> 13. or if that is displeasing send half of what thy father (sent).<br> 14. Wherefore shouldst thou send two manehs of gold (only)?<br> 15. For the sake of <a name="fr_257"></a><a href="#fn_257"><font size="1">1</font></a> the folding doors in the temple <a name="fr_258"></a><a href="#fn_258"><font size="1">2</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mât</span> and the palace<br> 16. (which) I have undertaken <a name="fr_259"></a><a href="#fn_259"><font size="1">3</font></a> to build send much gold,<br> 17. and whatsoever thou desirest in my country<br> 18. write for and let them take it to thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>19. In the time of Kuri-galzu my father the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kuna(?)khians</span>, all of them,<br> 20. sent unto him saying: Against the <i>government of the country</i> (?) <a name="fr_260"></a><a href="#fn_260"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> 21. let us sin and rebel.<br> 22. [With] thee will we make (a league). My father</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="green">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. sent this (answer) to them<br> 24. saying:</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>REVERSE</small></p>  
 <p>1. Cease (to ask) to ally thyself with me:<br> 2. if thou art estranged from the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> my brother, and<br> 3. alliest thyself with another,<br> 4. I will not go and assist <a name="fr_261"></a><a href="#fn_261"><font size="1">1</font></a> you.<br> 5. Thus my father was like-minded with me,<br> 6. because of thy father he did not listen to them:<br> 7. Again, by an <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyrian</span> who regards [my face <a name="fr_262"></a><a href="#fn_262"><font size="1">2</font></a>]<br> 8. have I not sent to thee after the news (I have) of them, asking<br> 9. why they have gone to thy country.<br> 10. If thou lovest me, no success<br> 11. will they obtain; dismiss them to their distant land.<br> 12. For a present to thee 3 manehs of alabaster,<br> 13. (and) 14 spans of horses with 5 chariots of wood<br> 14. I have despatched to thee.</p>
