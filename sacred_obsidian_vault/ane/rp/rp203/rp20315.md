 <p>1. Unto thee may there be peace,<br> 4. and from the soldiers of the palace<br> 5. of the king my lord may there be much peace!<br> 6. What immediately<br> 7. I speak before the king my lord,</p>

 <p>8. publicly I speak,<br> 9. (even) I and my sons<br> 10. and my brothers, all (being) servants<br> 11. of the king my lord before (him).</p>

 <p>12. Now I and Khatib<br> 13. have gone again<br> 14. with a present to Khazai <a name="fr_276"></a><a href="#fn_276"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 15. [who is] among you; <a name="fr_277"></a><a href="#fn_277"><font size="1">2</font></a> verily the frontier,<br> 16. behold! I have reached.</p>

 <p>17. From the orders of my lord<br> 18. I do not free myself,<br> 19. or from your <a name="fr_278"></a><a href="#fn_277"><font size="1">2</font></a> orders,<br> 20. (even) I the servant of my lord.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>21. The king of the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span> <a name="fr_279"></a><a href="#fn_278"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nukhasse</span> <a name="fr_280"></a><a href="#fn_279"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> 22. is staying and I am afraid<br> 23. of him (and) have defended myself.<br> 24. To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Phœnicia</span> he ascends;<br> 25. and if the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dunip</span> <a name="fr_281"></a><a href="#fn_280"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> 26. falls, he stays in a place (only) 2 <i>parasangs from here</i> (?),<br> 27. and I am afraid of him;<br> 28. yet according to this order<br> 29. he remains until he quits it. <a name="fr_282"></a><a href="#fn_281"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_273"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_273">67:3</a> Winckler and Abel: <i>Mittheilungen</i>, ii. p. 38.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_274"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_274">67:4</a> The Hebrew <i>khay</i>, "living"; compare the name of Hiel, 1 Kings xvi. 34.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_275"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_275">67:5</a> Aziru or Aziri (Ezir in the Old Testament was the son of Dûdu (the Biblical Dodo or David), a high official at the court of the Pharaoh. See Letter v.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_276"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_276">68:1</a> This seems to be the name of the Hittite king.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_277"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_278">68:2</a> Plural.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_278"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_279">68:3</a> <i>Khatte</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_279"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_280">68:4</a> Nukhasse was in northern Syria; Dunip appears to have been one of its cities. M. Halévy suggests that Nukhasse is the land of "bronze" (Heb. <i>nekhosheth</i>), and compares 2 Sam. viii. 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_280"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_281">68:5</a> The Tunip of the Egyptian monuments, now Tennib, between Azaz and Arpad (Erfad), north-west of Aleppo. In the Assyrian period, from the ninth century B.C. downwards, Tunip disappears from history, its place being taken by Khazazu (Azaz) and Arpad.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_281"></a><a href="rp20315.htm#fr_282">68:6</a> <i>Adi patari-se</i>. Compare another letter of Aziru (No. 32): "The king of the Hittites is staying in the land of Nukhasse and I am afraid of him. He is staying in a place [only two] <i>parasangs distant</i> (?), [in] the city of Tunip and I am afraid. The place he has quitted (<i>iptur</i>) also I <a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="green">p. 69</font></a> have reached. [So] again . we have gone into the land of Phœnicia, the country of my lord, and I am afraid for the country of my lord." So also in another despatch (No. 33): "And again [the king of the Hittites] is staying in the land of Nukhasse; [he is staying] two <i>parasangs distant</i> (?) in the city of Tunip, and I am afraid it may fall … may [the king of the Hittites] quit (<i>liptur</i>) the city of Tunip."</p>
