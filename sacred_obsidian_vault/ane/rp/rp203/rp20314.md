Sayce, [1890], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">III.—Letter of Burna-buryas to Amenophis IV</span> <a name="fr_263"></a><a href="#fn_263"><font size="1">3</font></a></h4>  
 <p align="center"><small>OBVERSE</small></p>  
 <p>1. [To] Napkhuhru-ri[ya]<br> 2. the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> <a name="fr_264"></a><a href="#fn_264"><font size="1">4</font></a> my brother [it is spoken]<br> 3. thus: Burra-buryas the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kara-[Duniyas]</span><br> 4. thy brother-unto myself (is) peace;<br> 5. unto thee, thy country, thy house, thy wives, thy children,<br> 6. thy officers, thy horses (and) thy chariots<br> 7. may there ever be peace!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="green">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. I and my brother with one another<br> 9. have conferred amicably,<br> 10. and this is what we have said<br> 11. as follows: as our fathers with one another,<br> 12. we also have friendly dealings.<br> 13. Again my ministers<br> 14. who came with Akhi-dhâbu <a name="fr_265"></a><a href="#fn_265"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 15. into the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kinakhkhi</span> <a name="fr_266"></a><a href="#fn_266"><font size="1">2</font></a> trusted to destiny,<br> 16. from Akhi-dhâbu to visit my brother they passed;<br> 17. in the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kikhinnatuni</span> of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kinakhkhi</span><br> 18. Sum-Adda the son of Balumme <a name="fr_267"></a><a href="#fn_267"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> 19. (and) Sutatna the son of Saratum <a name="fr_268"></a><a href="#fn_268"><font size="1">4</font></a> of the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Akku</span>, <a name="fr_269"></a><a href="#fn_269"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> 20. when they had sent their men, slew<br> 21. my ministers and carried off their treasures<br> 22. which they [were taking] for [a present to the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>].<br> 23. I have sent to you [therefore] a complainant <a name="fr_270"></a><a href="#fn_270"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> 24. [who] may speak to thee [thus]:</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>REVERSE</small></p>  
 <p>1. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kinakhkhi</span> (is) thy country and the king [is thy servant?].<br> 2. In thy country I have been injured; do thou punish [the offender].<br> 3. The silver which they carried off [was] a present [for thee],<br> 4. and the men who (are) my servants they have slain.<br> 5. Slay them and requite the blood (of my messengers);<br> 6. but if thou dost not put these men to death,<br> 7. (the inhabitants) of the high-road that belongs to me will turn</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. and verily will slay thy ambassadors, and<br> 9. a breach will be made in the agreement (to respect the persons) of ambassadors,<br> 10. and this man <a name="fr_271"></a><a href="#fn_271"><font size="1">1</font></a> will be estranged from thee.<br> 11. One of my men Sum-Adda,<br> 12. having cut off his feet,<br> 13. detained him with him;<br> 14. and as for another man, Sutatna of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Akku</span>,<br> 15. having made him stand on (his) head,<br> 16. he stood upon his face. As for these men<br> 17. … one has spoken thus: I have seen indeed<br> 18. … [what] thou askest that indeed thou knowest.<br> 19. [By way of a pre]sent, I maneh of alabaster I have despatched to thee.<br> 20. [By] my ambassadors a costly gift I have [sent to thee].<br> 21. [On account of the re]port <a name="fr_272"></a><a href="#fn_272"><font size="1">2</font></a> which my brother has heard<br> 22. my ambassadors do not detain;<br> 23. the costly present let them offer [to thee].</p>
