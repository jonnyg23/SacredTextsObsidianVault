Sayce, [1890], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VI.—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Letter from Rib-Addu to the King of Egypt</span> <a name="fr_287"></a><a href="#fn_286"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p>1. [To] the king my lord,<br> 2. my Sun-god I speak, (even I) Rib-Addu <a name="fr_288"></a><a href="#fn_287"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="green">p. 71</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. thy servant: at the feet of my lord<br> 4. my Sun-god seven times seven<br> 5. do I prostrate myself. The king my lord<br> 6. has heard the words of the servant<br> 7. of his justice. <a name="fr_289"></a><a href="#fn_288"><font size="1">1</font></a> (I am) very sick. <a name="fr_290"></a><a href="#fn_289"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 8. Unto me has hostility approached.<br> 9. The sons of Ebed-Asirta <a name="fr_291"></a><a href="#fn_290"><font size="1">3</font></a> descended<br> 10. into <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Phœnicia</span> <a name="fr_292"></a><a href="#fn_291"><font size="1">4</font></a> they <a name="fr_293"></a><a href="#fn_292"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> 11. (and) all of the country of the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tsumura</span> <a name="fr_294"></a><a href="#fn_293"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> 12. and the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Irqata</span>, <a name="fr_295"></a><a href="#fn_294"><font size="1">7</font></a>armed themselves<br> 13. against the governor; and now in<br> 14. the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tsumura</span> (is their) station.<br> 15. Behold, the governor is sick.<br> 16. On account of the attack he has left <a name="fr_296"></a><a href="#fn_295"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> 17. the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gubla</span>, <a name="fr_297"></a><a href="#fn_296"><font size="1">9</font></a> and there are not <a name="fr_298"></a><a href="#fn_297"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> 18. Zimrida [and]<br> 19. Yapa-Addu along with me.<br> 20. Now [accordingly] has the governor<br> 21. sent to them, and<br> 22. [they have sent] 30 manehs to him.<br> 23. [Now] has the king my lord heard<br> 24. the report of the servant of his justice,<br> 25. and has despatched reinforcements<br> 26. in haste to<br> 27. the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tsumura</span> to defend (it)<br> 28. [and] capture the soldiers</p>

 <p>29. of the palace of the king, the Sun-god;<br> 30. and the king, the Sun-god, has supplied <a name="fr_299"></a><a href="#fn_298"><font size="1">1</font></a> me<br> 31. with the soldiers of the kingdom <a name="fr_300"></a><a href="#fn_299"><font size="1">2</font></a> from<br> 32. the midst of his own country. A second time<br> 33. has the king, my lord, heard the report of his servant<br> 34. and has despatched <a name="fr_301"></a><a href="#fn_300"><font size="1">3</font></a> the garrison <a name="fr_302"></a><a href="#fn_301"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> 35. to the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tsumura</span> and<br> 36. to the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Irqata</span>. Since<br> 37. there have fled all<br> 38. the garrison from<br> 39. the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tsumura</span>, and<br> 40. there has applied (his) ear the king,<br> 41. the lord, the Sun-god of the world, and<br> 42. has given me 20 convoys<br> 43. of horses for myself,<br> 44. and has despatched reinforcements<br> 45. in haste<br> 46. to the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tsumura</span> to<br> 47. defend it, even all<br> 48. the garrison<br> 49. who had armed themselves with difficulty,<br> 50. and the men had …<br> 51. in the midst of the city, since<br> 52. the soldiers of the palace had not<br> 53. …, and<br> 54. the said city had not armed itself,<br> 55. on the capture and <i>slaughter</i> (?) of the soldiers of the palace<br> 56. it is intent. All the world<br> 57. have gone <a name="fr_303"></a><a href="#fn_302"><font size="1">5</font></a> to the king.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_286"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_287">70:2</a> Winckler and Abel, <i>Mittheilungen</i>, ii. p. 81.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_287"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_288">70:3</a> Or Rip-Hadad. Addu or Hadad is given as the Syrian equivalent of the Assyrian god Rimmon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_288"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_289">71:1</a> That is to say, "his righteous servant."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_289"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_290">71:2</a> Or "distressed."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_290"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_291">71:3</a> Also written Ebed-Asrâti, "the servant of Asherah." The name of Asrâti is more than once preceded by the determinative of divinity, showing that Ashêrah, mistranslated "grove" in the authorised version of the Old Testament, was a goddess. She was, in fact, the goddess of fertility, who was symbolised by a cone or branchless trunk, and must be carefully distinguished from Ashtoreth or Astartê.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_291"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_292">71:4</a> <i>Mat Akharrâ</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_292"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_293">71:5</a> It is possible also to translate, "into Phœnicia, even they: all the country."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_293"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_294">71:6</a> The Zemar of the Old Testament (Gen. x. 18), called Simyra in classical geography, now Sumra, north of Tripoli.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_294"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_295">71:7</a> The "Arkites" of Gen. x. 17, now Tel ’Arqa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_295"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_296">71:8</a> <i>Itizi</i>[<i>b</i>].</p>
 <p><a name="fn_296"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_297">71:9</a> The Gebal of the Old Testament, the Byblos of Greek writers, now Jebêl north of Beyrût. Rib-Addu, the Egyptian governor of Phœnicia, had his residence at Gebal.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_297"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_298">71:10</a> <i>Ya</i>[<i>nu</i>].</p>
 <p><a name="fn_298"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_299">72:1</a> <i>Usamri-ni</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_299"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_300">72:2</a> Native Egyptian soldiers.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_300"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_301">72:3</a> The scribe has omitted the final syllable of <i>ussira:</i> "he has despatched."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_301"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_302">72:4</a> Literally "men of the guard."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_302"></a><a href="rp20317.htm#fr_303">72:5</a> Literally "we have gone."</p>
