 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="green">p. 104</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">THE INDIA HOUSE INSCRIPTION OF NEBUCHADREZZAR</h4>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN I</small></p>  
 <p>1. Nebuchadrezzar<br> 2. king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span>,<br> 3. the prince exalted,<br> 4. the favourite of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span>,<br> 5. the pontiff supreme,<br> 6. the beloved of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nebo</span>,<br> 7. the serene, the possessor of wisdom,<br> 8. who the way of their godhead<br> 9. regardeth,<br> 10. who feareth their lordship;<br> 11. the servant <a name="fr_374"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">1</font></a> unwearied,<br> 12. who for the maintenance <a name="fr_375"></a><a href="#fn_374"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 13. of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Esagilla</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ezida</span> <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_375"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> 14. daily bethought him, and<br> 15. the weal of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span><br> 16. and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Borsippa</span></p>

 <p>17. regardeth ever;<br> 18. the wise, the prayerful,<br> 19. the maintainer <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">1</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Esagilla</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ezida</span>,<br> 20. the chiefest son<br> 21. of Nabopalassar,<br> 22. king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span>, am I.<br> 23. After that the lord my god had created me,<br> 24. that <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span> had framed<br> 25. the creature in the mother;<br> 26. when I was born,<br> 27. when I was created, even I,<br> 28. the holy places of the god I regarded,<br> 29. the way of the god I walked in.<br> 30. Of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span>, the great lord, the god my creator,<br> 31. his cunning works<br> 32. highly do I extol.<br> 33. Of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nebo</span>, his true son,<br> 34. the beloved of my majesty,<br> 35. the way of his supreme godhead<br> 36. steadfastly do I exalt;<br> 37. with all my true heart<br> 38. I love the fear of their godhead,<br> 39. I worship their lordship.<br> 40. When <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span>, the great lord,<br> 41. lifted up the head of my majesty and<br> 42. with lordship over the multitude of peoples invested me; and<br> 43. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nebo</span>, the overseer of the multitude of heaven and earth,<br> 44. for the governing of the peoples<br> 45. a righteous sceptre<br> 46. placed in my hands:<br> 47. for me, of them I am heedful,<br> 48. I have regard unto their godhead;<br> 49. for the mention of their glorious name,<br> 50. I worship the god and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ishtar</span>. <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_377"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>

 <p>51. To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span> my lord I made supplication,<br> 52. prayers to him I undertook, and<br> 53. the word which my heart looked for,<br> 54. to him I spate:<br> 55. "Of old, O prince, lord of all that is!<br> 56. for the king whom thou lovest, and<br> 57. whose name thou callest,<br> 58. that to thee is pleasing;<br> 59. thou leadest him aright, <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 60. a straight path thou appointest him.<br> 61. I am a prince obedient unto thee,<br> 62. a creature of thy hands;<br> 63. thou it was that madest me, and<br> 64. with sovereignty over the multitude of the peoples<br> 65. didst invest me;<br> 66. according to thy goodness, O Lord,<br> 67. wherewith thou crownest<br> 68. all of them.<br> 69. Thy lordship supreme do thou make loving, <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_379"><font size="1">2</font></a> and<br> 70. the fear of thy godhead<br> 71. cause thou to be in my heart!<br> 72. Yea, grant that to thee is pleasing,</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN II</small></p>  
 <p>1. for my life truly thou makest."<br> 2. Himself; the leader glorious,<br> 3. the open-eyed of the gods, the prince <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span>,<br> 4. my supplications heard and<br> 5. received my prayers.<br> 6. Yea, he made gracious his supreme lordship,<br> 7. the fear of his godhead<br> 8. he implanted in my heart;<br> 9. to draw his car (?) <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_380"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>

 <p>10. he made me submit the heart;<br> 11. I worshipped his lordship.<br> 12. In his high trust, <a name="fr_382"></a><a href="#fn_381"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 13. to far-off lands,<br> 14. distant hills,<br> 15. from the Upper Sea<br> 16. to the Lower Sea, <a name="fr_383"></a><a href="#fn_382"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 17. immense journeys,<br> 18. blocked ways,<br> 19. a place where the path is broken,<br> 20. feet are not;<br> 21. a road of hardships,<br> 22. a journey of straits,<br> 23. I pursued, and<br> 24. the unyielding I reduced,<br> 25. I fettered the rebels.<br> 26. The land I ordered aright, and<br> 27. the people I made to thrive;<br> 28. bad and good<br> 29. among the people I removed. <a name="fr_384"></a><a href="#fn_383"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> 30. Silver, gold, glitter of precious stones,<br> 31. copper, <i>mismakanna</i>-wood, cedar<br> 32. what thing soever is precious,<br> 33. a large abundance;<br> 34. the produce of mountains,<br> 35. the fulness of seas,<br> 36. a rich present,<br> 37. a splendid gift,<br> 38. to my city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span><br> 39. into his presence I brought.<br> 40. In <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Esagilla</span>,<br> 41. the palace of his lordship,<br> 42.  <p>44. of the lord of the gods, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span>,<br> 45. I made to glisten like suns<br> 46. the walls thereof;<br> 47. with large gold,<br> 48. like rubble (?) stone, <a name="fr_386"></a><a href="#fn_385"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 49. with <i>uknû</i> <a name="fr_387"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">2</font></a> and alabaster,<br> 50. the habitation of the house I overlaid.<br> 51. The gate <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khilisu</span>, even the Beautiful Gate,<br> 52. and the gate of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ezida</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Esagilla</span>,<br> 53. I had them made brilliant as the sun.<br> 54. The bright seat, the place of them that determine destinies, .<br> 55. which is the Quarter of Assembly, the chapel of the Fates,<br> 56. wherein, at <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zagmuku</span>, <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">3</font></a> the opening of the year,<br> 57. on the 8th day (and) the 11th day,<br> 58. the divine king, the god of heaven (and) earth, the lord of heaven,<br> 59. taketh up his abode;<br> 60. the gods of heaven (and) earth<br> 61. with awe submit unto him;<br> 62. they bow, they take their stand before him;<br> 63. a destiny of enduring days,<br> 64. as the destiny of my life,<br> 65. they predestine in the midst (thereof):—</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN III</small></p>

To make <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Esagilla</span><br> 19. my heart lifted me up <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">2</font></a>;<br> 20. in chief have I regarded it.<br> 2 I. For the making of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Esagilla</span><br> 34. daily I besought<br> 35. the King of the gods, the Lord of lords.<br> 36. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Borsippa</span> the city of his abode<br> 37. With silver, gold, precious stones,<br> 45. copper, <i>mismakanna</i>-wood, <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">1</font></a> cedar-wood,<br> 42. I finished the work of it.<br> 43. The bulls, the leaves of the gate of the cell,<br> 49. the lintels, the bars, the bolt, <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 50. the door-sill, <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>Zarirû</i>-stone.<br> 51. The cedar of the roofing<br> 52. of its chambers (?) <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> 53. with silver I made bright.<br> 54. The bulls, the leaves of the gates,<br> 60. with plates of bronze (?),<br> 61. brightly I made to glisten.<br> 62. The house I made gloriously bright, and,<br> 63. for gazings (of wonder),<br> 64. with carved work <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">5</font></a> I had (it) filled.<br> 65. The Bark of the river of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gan-ulu</span>, <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 71. the car of his princeliness</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN IV</small></p>  
 <p>1. the Bark of the Way of Zagmuku,<br> 2. the festival of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span>,<br> 3. its sides<br> 4. (and) the pavilion within it,<br> 5.  <p><a name="fn_421"></a><a href="rp20326.htm#fr_425">123:1</a> The Blackheads were the aboriginal population of Babylonia, <i>i.e.</i> the Accadians. The Chinese anciently bore the same title, a fact which is one of the innumerable proofs of their Accadian origin. The meaning appears to be black-haired; though the expression is said to refer to the colour of their caps or kerchiefs. The Chinese still call themselves by several synonymous titles meaning the black-haired folk.</p>
