 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_591"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_595">205:1</a> In W. A. I. i. 35. 3. 24–26, we must read <i>Bel-kapkapi sarru pani alik makhri qudmu sarruti sa ana tsulili-sa ultu ullâ Assur ibbû ’simasu</i>, "Bel-kapkapu a former king who went before me, the founder of the monarchy, for whose protection Assur had from remote times proclaimed his destiny." There is no mention of a king Tsulili.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_592"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_596">206:1</a> According to the "Synchronistic Tablet" Buzur-Assur was a contemporary of Burna-buryas of Babylonia, and since two of the royal correspondents of Amenophis IV Khu-en-Aten of Egypt, as we learn from the newly-discovered cuneiform tablets of Tel el-Amarna, were Assur-yuballidh of Assyria and Burna-buryas of Babylonia, it is probable that Assur-yuballidh was the successor of Buzur-Assur. According to the "Synchronistic Tablet" Assur-yuballidh's daughter Muballidhat-Serûa was the mother of Kara-Urus, king of Babylonia, who was murdered and succeeded by an usurper Nazi-bugas. Nazi-bugas himself had to make way for Kur-galzu "the younger," the son of Burna-buryas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_593"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_597">206:2</a> A seal belonging to Tiglath-Uras was carried to Babylon B.C. 1290 and recovered by Sennacherib 600 years later. Unfortunately we do not know whether the seal was carried away during the lifetime of Tiglath-Uras or after his death. In any case his date must be earlier than B.C. 1290.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_594"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_598">207:1</a> These two kings were contemporaries of the Babylonian king Rimmon-suma-natsir, for whom cf. <i>Records of the Past</i>, new Ser., i. p. 16, no. 24.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_595"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_599">207:2</a> A contemporary of the Babylonian king Zamama-nadin-sumi, <i>Records</i>, new Ser., i. p. 16, no. 27.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_596"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_600">207:3</a> A contemporary of the Babylonian king Nebo-kudurra-utsur.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_597"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_601">207:4</a> Defeated by Merodach-nadin-akhi. of Babylonia in B.C. 1106 according to Sennacherib; see <i>Records</i>, new Ser., i. p. 87.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_598"></a><a href="rp20240.htm#fr_602">208:1</a> He was still reigning over Babylonia in his 4th year.</p>
