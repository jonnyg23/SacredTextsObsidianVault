 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="green">p. 62</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">DESPATCHES FROM PALESTINE IN THE CENTURY BEFORE THE EXODUS.</h4>  <h4 align="center">No. I <a name="fr_155"></a><a href="#fn_155"><font size="1">1</font></a></h4>  
 <p>1. To the king, my lord,<br> 2. my gods, <a name="fr_156"></a><a href="#fn_156"><font size="1">2</font></a> my Sun-god, <a name="fr_157"></a><a href="#fn_157"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> 3. by letter<br> 4. I speak, <a name="fr_158"></a><a href="#fn_158"><font size="1">4</font></a> even I Su-arda-ka <a name="fr_159"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> 5. thy servant, the dust of thy feet:<br> 6. at the feet of the king my lord,<br> 7. my gods, my Sun-god,<br> 8. seven times seven do I prostrate myself.<br> 9. The king of (the country of) … directed the mouth<br> 10. to make war:<br> 11. in the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kelte</span> <a name="fr_160"></a><a href="#fn_160"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> 12. he made war against thee the third time.<br> 13. A cry (for assistance) to myself<br> 14. was brought. My city<br> 15. belonging to myself<br> 16. <i>adhered to</i> (?) me.<br> 17. Ebed-tob <a name="fr_161"></a><a href="#fn_161"><font size="1">7</font></a> sends<br> 18. to the men of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kelte</span>;</p>

 <p>19. he sends 14 pieces of silver, and<br> 20. they marched against my rear;<br> 21. and the domains of the king my lord<br> 22. they overran. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kelte</span><br> 23. my city Ebed-tob<br> 24. removed from my jurisdiction;<br> 25. the <i>pleasure park</i> (?) of the king my lord<br> 26. and the fortress of Bel-nathan <a name="fr_162"></a><a href="#fn_162"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 27. and the fortress of Hamor <a name="fr_163"></a><a href="#fn_163"><font size="1">2</font></a> from<br> 28. before him and his justice<br> 29. he removed. Lab-api<br> 30. the <i>halting</i> (?) in speech occupied<br> 31. the fortress of … <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ninu</span> and<br> 32. now Lab-api<br> 33. together with Ebed-tob and<br> 34. [his men] has occupied the fortress of … <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ninu</span><br> 35. …when the king to his servant</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Lacuna</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">On the Edge</span></p>  
 <p>1. As regards this matter, No!<br> 2. twice has the king returned (this) answer.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_155"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_155">62:1</a> No. XI in my forthcoming paper on the tablets of Tel el-Amarna.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_156"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_156">62:2</a> This is a curious parallelism to the use of the plural <i>Elohim</i> in Hebrew for the singular "God."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_157"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_157">62:3</a> The Egyptian Pharaoh was not only "the son of the Sun," but was also identified with the Sun-god himself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_158"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_158">62:4</a> <i>Ki dhema atma</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_159"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_159">62:5</a> Su-arda-ka is a purely Assyro-Babylonian name, and shows how far the cultivated classes of Western Asia had gone in adopting the Babylonian language.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_160"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_160">62:6</a> The Hebrew Keilah (Josh. xv. 44, I Sam. xxiii.), now Kilâ.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_161"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_161">62:7</a> Abd-Dhabba, which may, however, also be read Abd-Khima. Compare the names of Tab-Rimmon (1 Kings, xv. 18), and Tab-el (Is. vii. 6).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_162"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_162">63:1</a> Written ideographically EN-MU, in Assyrian Bil-nadin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_163"></a><a href="rp20213.htm#fr_163">63:2</a> Written with the ideograph of "ass" <i>emer</i>, Heb. <i>khamôr</i>. There is a similar play upon the name of the Amorite in the Old Testament, Gen. xxxiv. 2, etc. compared with xlviii. 22.</p>
