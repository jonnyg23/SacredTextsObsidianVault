Sayce, [1888], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">No. VI <a name="fr_199"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">4</font></a></h4>  
 <p>1. To the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>, my lord,<br> 2. by letter<br> 3. I speak (even I), the king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Alasiya</span> <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">5</font></a> thy brother.<br> 4. Unto myself (is) peace,<br> 5. and upon thee may there be peace!<br> 6. To thy house, thy children, thy son,<br> 7. thy wives, thy many chariots, thy horses,<br> 8. and in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> thy country</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>9. may there be abundance of peace!<br> 10. O my brother, my messenger<br> 11. a costly gift carefully<br> 12. has carried to them, and has heard<br> 13. thy salutation.</p>
