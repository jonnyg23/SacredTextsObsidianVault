 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">VI. Inscriptions of Ur-Bau and his Reign</span></h4>  
 <p align="center">No. I.—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">On a Statue</span> <a name="fr_156"></a><a href="#fn_155"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN I</small></p>  
 <p>1. To the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span><br> 2. the powerful warrior<br> 3. of the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ellilla</span>,<br> 4. Ur-Bau<br> 5. the patesi<br> 6. of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shirpurla-ki</span>,<br> 7. the offspring begotten<br> 8. by the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-âgal</span>,<br> 9. chosen by the immutable will of the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Niná</span>,<br> 10. endowed with power by the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span>,<br> 11. named with a favourable name by the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bau</span>,<br> 12. endowed with intelligence by the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">En-ki</span>, <a name="fr_157"></a><a href="#fn_156"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN II</small></p>  
 <p>1. covered with renown by the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ninni</span>,<br> 2. the favourite servant of the god who is king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gishgalla-ki</span>,<br> 3. the favourite of the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Duzi-abzu</span>.<br> 4. I am <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ur-Bau</span>;<br> 5. the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span> is my king.<br> 6. The site of … <a name="fr_158"></a><a href="#fn_157"><font size="1">3</font></a> he has excavated.<br> 7. The earth thence extracted, like precious stones, he has measured (?);<br> 8. like a precious metal he has weighed (?) it.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN III</small></p>  
 <p>1. According to the plan adopted he has marked out a large space;<br> 2. into the middle (of it) he has carried this earth,<br> 3. and he has made its <i>mundus</i>. <a name="fr_159"></a><a href="#fn_158"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 4. Above, a substructure 6 cubits high, he has built.<br> 5. Above this substructure<br> 6. the temple <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">E-ninnû</span>, which illumines the darkness (?), 30 cubits in height,<br> 7. he has built.<br> 8. For the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-gharsag</span>, <a name="fr_160"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">2</font></a> the mother of the gods,</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN IV</small></p>  
 <p>1. her temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Girsu-ki</span><br> 2. he has constructed.<br> 3. For the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bau</span>,<br> 4. the good lady,<br> 5. the daughter of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anna</span>,<br> 6. her temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Uru-azagga</span><br> 7. he has constructed.<br> 8. For the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ninni</span>, the lady august, the <i>sovereign</i> (?),<br> 9. her temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gishgalla-ki</span><br> 10. he has constructed.<br> 11. For the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">En-ki</span>, the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Eridu</span>,<br> 12. his temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Girsu-ki</span></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN V</small></p>  
 <p>1. he has constructed.<br> 2. For the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-dara</span>, <a name="fr_161"></a><a href="#fn_160"><font size="1">3</font></a> the lord of <i>destinies</i> (?),<br> 3. his temple he has constructed.<br> 4. For the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-âgal</span>,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. his god,<br> 6. his temple<br> 7. he has constructed.<br> 8. For the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-mar-ki</span> <a name="fr_162"></a><a href="#fn_161"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 9. the good lady,<br> 10. the eldest daughter of the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Niná</span>,<br> 11. the <i>Esh-gu-tur</i> (?), the temple of her constant choice,<br> 12. he has constructed.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN VI</small></p>  
 <p>1. For the god …<br> 2. the shepherd … [of] <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gir</span>-[<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">su-ki</span>],<br> 3. his temple …<br> 4. he has constructed.<br> 5. For the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kû-Anna</span>, <a name="fr_163"></a><a href="#fn_162"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 6. the lady of the cloudy sky (?),<br> 7. her temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Girsu-ki</span><br> 8. he has constructed.<br> 9. For the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Duzi-abzu</span>,<br> 10. the lady of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kinunir-ki</span>,<br> 11. her temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Girsu-ki</span><br> 12. he has constructed.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>The remaining inscriptions of Telloh will be translated in the next volume</i>.</p>
