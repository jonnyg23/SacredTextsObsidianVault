 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN I</small></p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Lacuna</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. [pate]si<br> 2. [of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shirpur</span>]<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">la</span></p>  
 <p align="center">…………</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN II</small></p>  
 <p>1. [of the god] <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span><br> 2. [the . . . ] <i>dun</i><br> 3. has constructed.<br> 4. The palace of <i>Ti-ra-ash-di</i> (?)<br> 5. he has built,<br> 6. and he has . . .<br> 7. E-an-[na]-du <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 8. covered with renown</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN III</small></p>  
 <p>1. by the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span>,<br> 2. for the countries<br> 3. by the power of the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span></p>  
 <p align="center">…………</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_144"></a><a href="rp20114.htm#fr_145">67:1</a> Découvertes, pl. 2, No. 3. The writing used in this inscription resembles that of the inscriptions of Ur-ninâ and the Stêlê of the Vultures more than any other. However, the little that remains of the first column seems to indicate that it belongs to a patesi and not to a king, perhaps to an E-anna-du.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_145"></a><a href="rp20114.htm#fr_146">67:2</a> This proper name is mutilated, but I believe my reading very probable. Cf. the Stêlê of the Vultures, Obv. i. 1.</p>
