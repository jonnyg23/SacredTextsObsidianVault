 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Babylonian Legends of Creation</i>, by E. A. Wallis Budge, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h2 align="center">The "Bilingual" Version of the Creation Legend.</h2>  
 <p>1. "The holy house, the house of the gods in the holy place had not yet been made.</p>  
 <p>2. "No reed had sprung up, no tree had been made.</p>  
 <p>3. "No brick had been laid, no structure of brick had been erected.</p>  
 <p>4. "No house had been made, no city had been built.</p>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_003"></a>
 <span align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/003.png"><img src="tn/003.png" alt="The Bilingual Version of the Creation Legend. [No. 93,014.]"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>The Bilingual Version of the Creation Legend. [No. 93,014.]<br></span></p>  
 <p>5. "No city had been made, no creature had been constituted.</p>  
 <p>6. "Enlil's city, <a name="img_c001"></a>
 <img src="img/c001.png"> (i.e., Nippur) had not been made, E-kur <a name="img_c002"></a>
 <img src="img/c002.png"> had not been built,</p>  
 <p>7. "Erech <a name="img_c003"></a>
 <img src="img/c003.png"> had not been made, E-Aena <a name="img_c004"></a>
 <img src="img/c004.png"> had not been built,</p>  
 <p>8. The Deep <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="blc17.htm#fn_3"><font size="1">4</font></a> (or Abyss) had not been made, Eridu <a name="img_c005"></a>
 <img src="img/c005.png"> had not been built.</p>  
 <p>9. "Of the holy house, the house of the gods, the dwelling-place had not been made.</p>  
 <p>10. "All the lands were sea</p>  
 <p>11. "At the time that the mid-most sea was [shaped like] a trough,</p>  
 <p>12. "At that time Eridu was made, and E-sagil was built,</p>  
 <p>13. "The E-sagil where in the midst of the Deep the god Lugal-dul-azaga <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="blc17.htm#fn_4"><font size="1">5</font></a> dwelleth,</p>  
 <p>14. "Babylon was made, E-sagil was completed.</p>  
 <p>15. "The gods the Anunnaki he created at one time.</p>  
 <p>16. "They proclaimed supreme the holy city, the dwelling of their heart's happiness.</p>  
 <p>17. "Marduk laid a rush mat upon the face of the waters,</p>  
 <p>18. "He mixed up earth and moulded it upon the rush mat,</p>  
 <p>19. "To enable the gods to dwell in the place where they fain would be.</p>  
 <p>20. "He fashioned man.</p>  
 <p>21. "The goddess Aruru <a name="img_c006"></a>
 <img src="img/c006.png"> with him created the seed of mankind.</p>  
 <p>22. "He created the beasts of the field and [all] the living things in the field.</p>  
 <p>23. "He created the river Idiglat (Tigris) and the river Purattu (Euphrates), and he set them in their places,</p>  
 <p>24. "He proclaimed their names rightly.</p>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_004"></a>
 <span align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/004.png"><img src="tn/004.png" alt="Terra-cotta figure of a god. From a foundation deposit at Babylon. [No. 90,9961]"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>Terra-cotta figure of a god. From a foundation deposit at Babylon. [No. 90,9961]<br></span></p>  
 <p>25. "He created grass, the vegetation of the marsh, seed and shrub;</p>  
 <p>26. "He created the green plants of the plain,</p>  
 <p>27. "Lands, marshes, swamps,</p>  
 <p>28. "The wild cow and the calf she carried, the wild calf, the sheep and the young she carried, the lamb of the fold,</p>  
 <p>29. "Plantations and shrub land,</p>  
 <p>30. "The he-goat and the mountain goat ...</p>  
 <p>31. "The lord Marduk piled up a dam in the region of the sea (<i>i.e.</i>, he reclaimed land)</p>  
 <p>32. "He ... a swamp, he founded a marsh.</p>  
 <p>33. "... he made to be</p>  
 <p>34. "Reeds he created, trees he created,</p>  
 <p>35. "... in place he created</p>  
 <p>36. "He laid bricks, he built a brick-work,</p>  
 <p>37. "He constructed houses, he formed cities.</p>  
 <p>38. "He constructed cities, creatures he set [therein].</p>  
 <p>39. "Nippur he made, E-Kur he built.</p>  
 <p>40. "[Erech he made, E-Anna] he built.</p>  
 <p>[The remainder of the text is fragmentary, and shows that the text formed part of an incantation which was recited in the Temple of E-Zida, possibly the great temple of Nabu at Borsippa.]</p>
