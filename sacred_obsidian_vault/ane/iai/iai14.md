 <h2 align="CENTER">TABLET III--COLUMN I</h2>
 <h3 align="CENTER">HEABANI'S WISDOM-SONG OF THE KHAU-IK-I</h3>
 <p>The dark-eyed maids are dancing in the halls<br>
 Of Erech's palace: music fills the walls<br>
 Of splendor where the Sar-dan-nu <a name="fr_135"></a><a href="#fn_135"><font size="1">1</font></a> enthroned,<br>
 His hours is whiling by the maidens zoned;<br>
 A whirling garland chanting forth a song,<br>
 Accompanied with harps thus sang the throng:</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Heabani's wisdom chant and sing<br>
   To Erech's king our mighty Sar. <a name="fr_136"></a><a href="#fn_136"><font size="1">2</font></a><br>
 When Hea did Heabani bring,<br>
   Who now to Erech comes afar,<br>
 He taught him then all hidden things<br>
   Of Ki <a name="fr_137"></a><a href="#fn_137"><font size="1">3</font></a> or bright Samu <a name="fr_138"></a><a href="#fn_138"><font size="1">4</font></a> above,<br>
 That to the Mu-di <a name="fr_139"></a><a href="#fn_139"><font size="1">5</font></a> mystery brings.<br>
   Oh, how Heabani we shall love!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Chorus</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Then sing with joy ye Khau-ik-i! <a name="fr_140"></a><a href="#fn_140"><font size="1">6b</font></a><br>
   The Khau-ga <a name="fr_141"></a><a href="#fn_141"><font size="1">7</font></a> chant with waving arms,<br>
 The Nin-uit <a name="fr_142"></a><a href="#fn_142"><font size="1">8</font></a> sing Au-un-na-ci <a name="fr_143"></a><a href="#fn_143"><font size="1">9</font></a><br>
   Give to our Sar your sweetest charms.</p>

 <p>"All knowledge that is visible<br>
   Heabani holds it in his glance,<br>
 Sees visions inconceivable,<br>
   The Zi <a name="fr_144"></a><a href="#fn_144"><font size="1">10</font></a> his wizard eyes entrance.<br>
 Sweet peace he brings from troubled dreams,<br>
   He copies to El-li-tar-du-si, <a name="fr_145"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 From a far road by mountain streams;<br>
   Then sing with joy ye Khau-ik-i!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Chorus</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Then sing with joy ye Khau-ik-i!<br>
   The Khau-ga chant with waving arms,<br>
 The Nin-uit sing An-un-na-ci!<br>
   Give to our Sar your sweetest charms.</p>
 <p>"E'en all that on the tablet rests,<br>
   In Erech's tower, the Su-bu-ri <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">2</font></a><br>
 The beautiful, with glorious crests,<br>
   He wrote for far posterity.<br>
 We plead with him to leave us not,<br>
   But Zi-Gab-ri <a name="fr_147"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">3</font></a> him led away,<br>
 When our great Shal-man <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">4</font></a> joy us brought,<br>
   And Elam fled to the blue sea.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Chorus</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Then sing with joy ye Khau-ik-i!<br>
   Il-gi-sa-kis-sat <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">5</font></a> from above,<br>
 The Nin-nit sing An-un-na-ci!<br>
   Oh, how Heabani we shall love!"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>The maidens note their monarch's moody face,<br>
 And turn their songs to him with easy grace,<br>
 Of their great ruler tune a joyous lay,<br>
 Arid oft into his eyes hurl glances gay;<br>
 And trumpets join the chorus, rolling drums,<br>
 And wild applause from all the chieftains comes,</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 44</font></a></p>
 <p>Till the grave seers and councillors now cry<br>
 In praise of him they love so tenderly:<br>
 With arms upraised the mighty chorus join,<br>
 Until his heart is filled with joy divine;<br>
 And thus they sing with more than royal praise,<br>
 Their love for him in every face doth blaze.</p>
