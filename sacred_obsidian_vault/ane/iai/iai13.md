 <h2 align="CENTER">COLUMN VI</h2>
 <h3 align="CENTER">HEABANI RESOLVES TO RETURN TO ERECH</h3>
 <p>As Zaidu sadly turns and rides away,<br>
 The hermit from his cave comes forth to pray:<br>
 "Alas! hath all these wilds their charms here lost?<br>
 And is my breast with wild ambition tost?<br>
 My lonely cot I look upon with shame;<br>
 Again I long to seek the fields of fame,<br>
 Where luxury my remaining years<br>
 May crown, and happiness may find--or tears;<br>
 'Tis true! I should have welcomed the <i>bar-ru</i>; <a name="fr_119"></a><a href="#fn_119"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 But he hath since returned to Subartu." <a name="fr_120"></a><a href="#fn_120"><font size="1">2</font></a><br>
 His harp he took from its dust-covered case,<br>
 And kissed its carved and well-remembered face;<br>
 And tuning it, he glanced toward the wood,<br>
 And sang his farewell ode to solitude:</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Farewell, ye mountains, woods and trees--<br>
   My heart doth long again for joy;<br>
 I love your wilds and mossy leas,<br>
   But oh, your solitude doth cloy!</p>
 <p>I love to see the <i>bur-khi-is</i> <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_121"><font size="1">3</font></a><br>
   Sweep stately o'er the mossy rocks;<br>
 And <i>tsabi</i> <a name="fr_122"></a><a href="#fn_122"><font size="1">4</font></a> in a wild like this,<br>
   Hear the tattoo of red woodchucks.</p>
 <p>I love the cries of <i>lig-bar-ri</i> <a name="fr_123"></a><a href="#fn_123"><font size="1">5</font></a><br>
   The <i>nes-i</i> <a name="fr_124"></a><a href="#fn_124"><font size="1">6</font></a> calling for their prey;<br>
 And leaping of the <i>na-a-li</i>, <a name="fr_125"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">7</font></a><br>
   That fly in wildest fear away.</p>
 <p>I love the <i>bu-hir-tser-i</i> <a name="fr_126"></a><a href="#fn_126"><font size="1">8</font></a> all,<br>
   <i>Khar-sa-a-nu sa-qu-u-tu</i>; <a name="fr_127"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">9</font></a><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> </p>
 <p> </p></dir>
 </dir>

 <p>Hear <i>cu-uts-tsi</i> <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">10</font></a> with thunder roll<br>
   Across the skies within my view.</p>
 <p>I love to see the <i>ca-ca-bi</i> <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_129"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
   Peep through the pine-trees o'er my home,<br>
 And watch the wild <i>tu-ra-a-khi</i> <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_130"><font size="1">2</font></a><br>
   And <i>arme</i> <a name="fr_131"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">3</font></a> welcome, to me come.</p>
 <p>Farewell! ye solitudes, farewell!<br>
   I will not moulder rotting lie<br>
 With no one's lips to wish me well;<br>
   O give me immortality!</p>
 <p>But what is fame? A bubble blown<br>
   Upon the breeze, that bursts its shell,<br>
 And all our brightest hopes are flown,<br>
   And leaves our solitude a hell.</p>
 <p>The holy minstrel bows his head in woe,<br>
 And sweeps the harpstrings with a movement slow;<br>
 Then lifts his eyes toward the setting sun,<br>
 His evening invocation thus begun:</p>
 <p> <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_132"><font size="1">4</font></a>O Samas! to the lifting of my hands<br>
   Show favor! unto me thy servant turn!<br>
 What man before thy blessèd Light withstands?<br>
   O thou! what mortal thine own words can learn?<br>
 And who can rival them inviolate',<br>
    <a name="fr_133"></a><a href="#fn_133"><font size="1">5</font></a>Among the gods no equal thou hast found.<br>
 In Heaven who of all the gods is great?<br>
   O thou alone! art great through Heaven's bound!</p>
 <p>On earth what man is great? alas! no one,<br>
   For thou alone art great! through earth's vast bounds.<br>
 When wide thy awful voice in Heaven resounds,<br>
   The gods fall prostrate to our Holy One;</p>
 <p> </p></dir>
 </dir>

 <p>When on the earth thy voice afar resounds,<br>
   The genii <a name="fr_134"></a><a href="#fn_134"><font size="1">6a</font></a> bow to thee and kiss the dust.<br>
 In thee, O Samas! do I put my trust,<br>
   For thy great love and mercy wide abounds!</p>
 <p>O my Creator, God, thy watchfulness<br>
   O'er me, oh may it never cease!<br>
   Keep thou the opening of my lips! the fleece<br>
 Of purest snow be my soul's daily dress.<br>
 Guard thou my hands! O Samas, Lord of Light!<br>
 And ever keep my life and heart aright!</p>
