 <p>This was one of the first books published about the UFO phenomena.
 We are fortunate that it ended up in the public domain.</p>
 
 <p>
 It is a template for much of what would follow: the paranoia, the
 government disinformation, the inescapable conclusion that the
 saucers are <i>not of this earth</i>.
 Keyhoe, with his spare, matter of fact writing style, which also
 conveys a profound sense of wonder, has to be the prototype for the deadpan
 Fox Mulder of the X-Files.</p>
 
 <p>
 On one hand we can see the birth of a key modern mythology.
 On the other, there is a body of almost naive evidence in this text
 unpolluted by that very mythology.
 The case studies are real.
 The witnesses were highly reliable.
 These cases are still unexplained.
 </p>
