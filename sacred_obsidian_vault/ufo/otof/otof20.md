 <p><a name="page_448"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 448</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BIBLIOGRAPHY</h3>  
 <p>The following works are recommended to those interested in the Saucer phenomena for they are considered authentic and contain excellent source material.</p>  
 <p>Arnold, Kenneth and Palmer, Ray, <i>The Coming Of The Saucers</i>, Amherst, Wisconsin, 1952.</p>  
 <p>Bethurum, Truman, <i>Aboard A Flying Saucer</i>, Los Angeles, Calif., 1954.</p>  
 <p>Fry, Daniel W., <i>The White Sands Incident</i>, Los Angeles, Calif., 1954.</p>  
 <p>Heard, Gerald, <i>Is Another World Watching?</i> New York, N. Y., 1951.</p>  
 <p>Keyhoe, Maj. Donald E., <i>The Flying Saucers Are Real</i>, New York, N. Y., 1950.</p>  
 <p>Keyhoe, Maj. Donald E., <i>Flying Saucers From Outer Space</i>, New York, N. Y., 1953.</p>  
 <p>Layne, Meade, <i>The Ether Ship Mystery And Its Solution</i>, San Diego, Calif.</p>  
 <p>Layne, Meade, <i>The Coming of The Guardians</i>, San Diego, Calif., 1954.</p>  
 <p>Leslie, Desmond and Adamski, George, <i>Flying Saucers Have Landed</i>, London, England, 1953.</p>  
 <p>Pelly, W. D., <i>Star Guests</i>, Noblesville, Indiana, 1950.</p>  
 <p>Van Tassel, George W., <i>I Rode A Flying Saucer</i>, Los Angeles, Calif., 1952.</p>  
 <p>Wilkins, Harold T., <i>Flying Saucers On The Attack</i>, New York, N. Y., 1954.</p>  
 <p>Williamson, George H. Bailey, Alfred C., <i>The Saucers Speak!</i> Los Angeles, Calif., 1954.</p>    </body>
