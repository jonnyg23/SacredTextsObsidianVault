 <h1 align="CENTER">VI. Poetry.</h1>
 <h2 align="CENTER">THE CAT'S SONG.</h2>
 <p align="CENTER">Ha<small>[1]</small> ha ha,<br>
 Ha, Ha,</p>
 <p>I am the one whom the Lynx derides,<br>
 I am the one who did not run fast;<br>
 For, the Lynx is the one who runs fast,</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ha<small>[1]</small> ha ha,<br>
 Ha, Ha,</p>
 <p>I am the one whom the Lynx derides.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ya Ya Ya,<br>
 Ya Ya,</p>
 <p>I am the one whom the Lynx derides.<br>
 I am the one who could not run fast,</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ya Ya Ya,<br>
 Ya Ya,</p>
 <p>I am the one whom the Lynx derides,<br>
 "The Cat could not run fast."</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ya Ya Ya,<br>
 Ya Ya,</p>
 <p>The Cat is the one whom the Lynx derides,<br>
 "It is the one who could not run fast,"</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ya Ya Ya,<br>
 Ya Ya,</p>
 <p>The Cat is the one who could not run fast,<br>
 It was not cunning.</p>
 <p><small>[1. Here the cat opens its mouth wide in singing.]</small></p>
 <p>It did foolish things;<br>
 For, the Lynx is one who understands,<br>
 The Cat does not understand."<br>
 The Cat (nevertheless) is cunning.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ya Ya Ya,<br>
 Ya Ya,</p>
 <p>The Cat is the one about whom the Lynx talked.<br>
 "It is the one who could not run fast."<br>
 It had to be cunning.<small>[1]</small><br>
 For, the Lynx is one who is cunning.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Haggla<small>[2]</small> haggla haggla<br>
 haggla haggla<br>
 Heggle heggle heggle<br>
 heggle heggle<br>
 Heggli heggli heggli<br>
 Heggli ng!</p>
 <p><small>[1. Reference is here made to the Cat's way of doubling when pursued.</small></p>
 <p>2. The narrator here explains that the Cat "talks with its tongue", assenting to what it has been saying.]</p>
