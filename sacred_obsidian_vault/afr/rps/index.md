 <h1 align="CENTER">The Royal Parchment Scroll of Black Supremacy</h1>
 <h2 align="CENTER">By Fitz Balintine Pettersburg</h2>
 <h4 align="CENTER">[1926?]</h4>
 
 <hr>
 <p>
 This is a text from Jamaica, written during the 1920s by a proto-Rastafarian
 preacher, Fitz Balintine Pettersburg.
 The text is primarily of historical significance.
 Along with <a href="../piby/index.htm"><cite>The Holy Piby</cite></a>,
 the <cite>Royal Parchment Scroll</cite> is today
 recognized as one of the root documents of Rastafarian thought.
 It is a rambling, surrealistic stream-of-consciousness polemic against the
 White colonial power structure, a 'found' palimpsest of Afrocentric thought,
 brimming with rage and energy.
 </p>
 <p>
 It is very difficult to obtain copies of this text today.
 <cite>The Royal Parchment Scroll</cite> 
 was used in the 1930s as the basis for
 <a href="../tpk/index.htm"><cite>The Promised Key</cite></a>
 by Leonard Percival Howell.
 </p>
