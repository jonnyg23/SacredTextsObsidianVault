 <h3 align="CENTER">139. The Fifer.</h3>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Richard Roe, Maroon Town, Cock-pit country.</p>
 <p>There's a boy once, mother got only the one boy an' 'he love him so much that 'he give him a flute. So one day they go to far groun' an' coming back the boy leave the flute at the groun'. When he catch half-way, he remember it an' he tell him papa. Papa say to go back for it, but he mus' be careful not to blow it coming back because he got a lot of wil' beasts to pass. So as he come home he begin to blow,[1]</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p><a name="16900.jpg"><img src="img/16900.jpg"></a><br>
 Min-nie, Min-nie, wa-yo da Lim-ba,<br>
 Min-nie, Min nie wa-yo da Lim-ba.<br>
 Min-nie, Min-nie wa-yo da Lim-ba,<br>
 Min-nie, Min-nie wa-yo da Lim-ba.</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Wild beast rush out, say, "Who's dat blowing de pipe, sah?"--"Oh, no, not me blowing!" An' go 'way, blow again. Wil' beast rush out. "Ha! you?" "No, grandpapa, not me blowing!"--"Den who blowing?"--"He gone on befo', massa; not me blowing!"--"Blow, let me see."--"Flee flitty flee, flee flitty flee."</p>
 <p>Wil' beast go away. He commence the right tune now,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Minnie Minnie, wa-yo da lim-ba, <br>
 Minnie Minnie, wa-yo da lim-ba,<br>
 Minnie Minnie, wa-yo da lim-ba."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>(Wild beast rush out, catch him, compel him to play.)</p>
 <p>"Ah, I catch you now, sah! Play de tune now, sah I blow, sah!"</p>
 <p>Then he began to blow the right tune, both dance. Different</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. The song was sung by Alfred Williams.]</p>
 </font><p>{p. 170}</p>
 <p>wil' beats--Tiger, Asoonah, all the wil' beasts come out an' dance. An' the father get frightened, come shoot all the wild beasts, all drop save him boy. An' flog the boy.</p>
