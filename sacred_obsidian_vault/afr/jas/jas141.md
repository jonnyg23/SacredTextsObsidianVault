 <h3 align="CENTER">141. Tacoomah makes a Dance.</h3>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">William Forbes, Dry River, Cock-pit country.</p>
 <p>Tacoomah make a dance, get Anansi for de fiddler, an' Grass-quit was coming as a fiddler too. Robin Redbreast was to play de music, Monkey was to blow de bugle. Well, after de night de dance commence. Anansi gwine to play,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Queena bunna, ring-ki-ting,<br>
 You sen' fe great Grass-quit,<br>
 You don' sen' fe me!<br>
 Anansi draw bow so sweet, ring-ki-ting,<br>
 Anansi draw bow so sweet, ring-ki-ting."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Tacoomah say,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"You ya, you ya, so ya, me ya,<br>
 Wid a fort tumba like a tenky bunna,<br>
 Wid a jump, wid a jump, like a tenky bunna."[1]</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>[1. The tune is that of the Devil in the Cock and Corn story, number 85.]</p>
 <p>{p. 171}</p>
 <p>Robin Redbreast say,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Jock, Jock, when you coming home?"</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Jock said,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Tomorrow evening."<br>
 "What in your right?"<br>
 "Boot an' spur."<br>
 "What in your left?"<br>
 "Bow an' arrow.[1]"</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Jock[2] said,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Robin redbreast<br>
 Was pretty well dressed,<br>
 And he was into his nest,<br>
 And a puppy went into his nest<br>
 And broke his neck t'ru distress."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Well, den, Turtle an' Duck goin' in de river fe go an swim, an' dem is to run to a hill-side in de river. An' Cock is de judge. Den Cock went to sing fe dem--</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Co co re co."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Duck an' Turtle swim--</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Shekey, shekey, shee-e-e."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>[1. The dialogue is taken from a popular game.</p>
 <p>2. I asked, 'Who is Jock?"--"Jock man dora."]</p>
