 <h3 align="CENTER">60. The Animal Race.</h3>
 <h5 align="CENTER">a. Horse and Turtle.</h5>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Alfred Williams, Maroon Town, Cock-pit country.</p>
 <p>Horse bet Turtle say a get to Kingston before him. Turtle bet him say him will get to Kingston before him, Brar Horse. An' Turtle tak up one of him pickney an' drop dem ev'ry mile-post, an' drop de last one in at Kingston at de wharf-house, tell 'im 'em going for a sack of salt. An' de night when dem start, as Brar Horse catch to de firs' mile-post an' sing out in a harsh note,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p><a name="06500.jpg"><img src="img/06500.jpg"></a><br>
 I-ya-a ya-o sa, nom-be, ya-o ya ya-o sa-a, nom-be,<br>
 a nom-be, sa-ka be-ne sa-bi-na, nom-be, ya ya-o sa, a, nom-be.</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Turtle answer quite yonder, soft an' sweet,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p><a name="06501.jpg"><img src="img/06501.jpg"></a><br>
 I-ya-a ya-o sa-a, nom-be, ya-o sa ya-o sa-a, nom-be,<br>
 se sa-ka be-ne sa-bi-na, nom-be, ya ya-o sa-a, nom-be.</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>[1. A round tin cooking pot is called a "bonpon". So is a high round hat.]</p>
 <p>{p. 66}</p>
 <p>Horse say, "Well! Brar Turtle gone!" Gallop, draw rein an' 'pur As he get to de nex' mile-post, hear,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"I-ya-a ya-o sa, nom-be, ya-o ya."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Gallop an' gallop till he get to de nex' mile-post. Turtle sing,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"I-ya-a ya-o sa, nom-be, ya-o ya."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Trabbel on, ride on, ride on, ride on, catch to de nex' mile-post, sing out,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"I-ya-a ya-o sa, nom-be, ya-o ya."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Turtle answer de same song quite at de mile-post,--</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"I-ya-a ya-o sa, nom-be, ya-o ya."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>As Horse catch to dat mile-post go in to Kingston, drop down dead!</p>
 <h5 align="CENTER">b. Pigeon and Parrot.</h5>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Julia Gentle, Santa Cruz Mountains.</p>
 <p>Pigeon an' Parrot was co'rtin' one girl an' she say whichever one firs' come in de house de mornin' she would marry dat one. Parrot could not fly very fas'. He went an' mek bargain wid anodder Parrot. He went before an' leave de odder one to follow Pigeon behin'. He went near to de girl house an' sit down in a tree. Pigeon call, saying,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Come on, me pretty Poll, come on, me pretty Poll,<br>
     Stay on de tree so long,<br>
 For de sun an' de moon gwine down,<br>
     Stay on de tree so long."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Parrot answer Pigeon behind,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Go on, me pretty Pigeon, go on, me pretty Pigeon,<br>
     Stay on de tree so long,<br>
 Go on, me pretty Pigeon, go on, me pretty Pigeon,<br>
     Stay on de tree so long."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Pigeon sail again. He stop, call again,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Come on, me pretty Poll, come on, me pretty Poll,<br>
     Stay on de tree so long,<br>
 Come on, me pretty Poll, come on, me pretty Poll,<br>
     Stay on de tree so long."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Parrot answer,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <font size="2"><p>"Go on, me pretty Pigeon, go on, me pretty Pigeon,<br>
 Stay on de tree so long,<br>
 Go on, me pretty Pigeon, go on, me pretty Pigeon,<br>
 Stay on de tree so long."</p></font></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>{p. 67}</p>
 <p>Pigeon sail. When Pigeon nearly catch to de house, call again in de same tune. Parrot answer before now. Pigeon say, "Stop! a lil' while Parrot was behin'; how Parrot get before?" When Pigeon went to de house, Parrot was in de house. Pigeon has to stay outside an' Parrot married to de girl.</p>
