 <h2 align="center">PERSONS</h2>
 
 <table cellpadding="5">
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Arámfè</i></td><td></td><td> God of Thunder and Father of the Gods.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Orísha</i></td><td></td><td> Creator of men. Son of Arámfè.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Odúwa or<br>Odudúwa</i></td><td>⎱<br>⎰</td><td> King of men. Son of Arámfè.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Ógun</i></td><td></td><td> God of Iron. Son of Odúwa.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Oráyan</i></td><td></td><td> The warrior son of Ógun.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Ládi</i></td><td></td><td> Smith of Ógun.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Obálufon</i></td><td></td><td> A worker in brass.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Mórimi</i></td><td></td><td> Wife of Obálufon.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Ífa</i></td><td></td><td> The Messenger of the Gods, principally known by reason of divination.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Olókun</i></td><td></td><td> Goddess of the Sea.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Olóssa</i></td><td></td><td> Goddess of the Lagoons.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Óshun</i></td><td></td><td> A Goddess who transformed and became the River Oshun.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Édi</i></td><td></td><td> The Perverter. A God of Evil who led men astray.</td></tr>

 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Éshu</i></td><td></td><td> Now regarded as the Devil, but originally as the Undoer of the favours of the Gods.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Peregún ’Gbo</i></td><td></td><td> A Forest God who caused the Forest to bring forth wild animals and watched over the birth of Orúnmila.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Orúnmila</i></td><td></td><td> A God who watches over the birth of children.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Offun Kánran</i></td><td></td><td> A messenger of Ífa.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Órní Odúm’la</i></td><td></td><td> The ancestor of the Órnís of Ífè.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Ojúmu</i></td><td></td><td> A priest.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td valign="top"><i>Osányi</i></td><td></td><td> A priest and maker of charms.</td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>The Sun, Moon, Night, Day, Dawn and Evening were also Gods and Goddesses sent by Arámfè, who is often spoken of as God. But a higher and very distant Being is mentioned by some of the Priests.</p>
 
 <p>Oíbo means White Man.</p>
 
 <p>Okpéllè is a charm used in the divination of Ífa.</p>
 
 <p>The final N is as in bon, and French pronunciation is nearly correct in all the above names.</p>
