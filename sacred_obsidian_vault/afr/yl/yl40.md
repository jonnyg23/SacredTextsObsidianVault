 <h2 align="center">XXXVII<br> TORTOISE AND CRAB</h2>
 
 <p>E<small>VERYONE</small> knows that Tortoise and Crab are enemies.</p>
 
 <p> One morning on the seashore they decided to fight to see which was the stronger, but, as both of them are protected by a hard shell, neither could succeed in injuring the other.</p>
 
 <p> Finally they came to an agreement that they were equal in strength.</p>
 
 <p> “We are so well protected by our
 
 <!------------------------page 77------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 77</font></a>
 
 armour,” said Tortoise, “that no one can harm us.”</p>
 
 <p> “And thus,” said Crab, “we are the strongest creatures in the world.”</p>
 
 <p> But at this moment a boy passed by and picked them both up. Tortoise was boiled in a pot and his shell was made into ornaments, while Crab was cooked in a stew for the boy’s supper. Since that day the descendants of the two boasters have always been ashamed to meet, and that is why they always shun one another.</p>
