 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Georgian Folk Tales, by Marjory Wardrop</i> [1894], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XIV</h3>  <h3 align="center">A Witty Answer</h3>  
 <p>A CERTAIN king was angry with one of his lords, and put him in prison; wishing to keep him there, he said he would only set him free if he could bring to the court a horse which was neither grey, nor black, brown nor</p>  
 <p><a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="green">p. 175</font></a></p>  
 <p>bay, white nor roan, dun, chestnut, nor piebald--and, in short, the king enumerated every possible colour that a horse could be. The imprisoned lord promised to get such a horse if the king would set him free at once. As soon as he was at liberty, the lord asked the king to send a groom for the horse, but begged that the groom might come neither on Monday nor Tuesday, Wednesday nor Thursday, Friday, Saturday, nor Sunday, but on any other day of the week that suited His Majesty.</p>
