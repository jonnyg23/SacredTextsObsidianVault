 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 17</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">INCENSE</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY ZABELLE ESSAYAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(<i>Born</i> 1878)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>THE incense at the altar slowly burns<br> Swayed in the silver censer to and fro;<br> Around the crucifix it coils and turns,<br> The brows of saints it wreathes with misty glow.  
 <p>And tremulous petitions, long drawn out,<br> Beneath the lofty arches faint away;<br> To weary eyes the candles round about<br> Heave as they flicker with their pallid ray.</p>  
 <p>The sacred columns, grey and mouldering,<br> Support a veil that stirs with voiceless sobs.<br> Beneath it, like the incense smouldering,<br> A woman's darkened heart in anguish throbs.</p>  
 <p>Consumed within the censer now, and burned,<br> The incense through the boundless ether soars.<br> What Matter was to Fragrance sweet is turned--<br> The cleansing fire its purity restores.</p>  
 <p>Nor shall that woman's smouldering heart be freed,--<br> Saved from its cold and adamantine shell,--<br> Till it is melted, tried, and cleansed indeed,<br> Till the pure flames shall all its dross expel!</p></dir>
