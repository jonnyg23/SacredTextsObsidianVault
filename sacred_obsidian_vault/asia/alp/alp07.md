 <p><a name="page_xvi"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. xvi</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">A TRIAL OF ORTHODOXY</h3>  
 <p align="center">(<i>Sonnet on Armenia)</i></p>  <h5 align="center">BY WILLIAM WATSON</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>THE clinging children at their mother's knee<br>   Slain; and the sire and kindred one by one<br>   Flayed or hewn piecemeal; and things nameless done,<br> Not to be told: while imperturbably<br> The nations gaze, where Rhine unto the sea,<br>   Where Seine and Danube, Thames and Tiber run,<br>   And where great armies glitter in the sun,<br> And great Kings rule, and man is boasted free!<br>   What wonder if yon torn and naked throng<br> Should doubt a Heaven that seems to wink and nod,<br>   And having mourned at noontide, "Lord, how long?"<br>     Should cry, "Where hidest Thou?" at evenfall,<br> At midnight, "Is He deaf and blind, our God?"<br>     And ere day dawn, "Is He indeed at all?"</dir>
