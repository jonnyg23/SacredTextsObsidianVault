 <p><a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 25</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LAMENT OVER THE HEROES FALLEN IN THE BATTLE OF AVARAIR</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY KAREKIN SRVANSTIAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(1840-1892)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>IF Goghtan's bards no longer crown<br>     Armenia's heroes with their lays,<br> Let deathless souls from Heaven come down,<br>     Our valiant ones to praise!  
 <p>Ye shining angel hosts, descend:<br>     On Ararat's white summit pause;<br> Let God Himself the heavens rend,<br>     To come and judge our cause.</p>  
 <p>Fly, clouds, from Shavarshan away,<br>     Pour not on it your gentle rain:--<br> ’Tis drenched with streams of blood to-day<br>     Shed by our brave ones slain.</p>  
 <p>Henceforth the rose and asphodel<br>     No more shall on our plains appear;<br> But in the land where Vartan fell<br>     Shall Faith her blossoms rear.</p>  
 <p>Fit monument to Vartan's name,<br>     Mount Ararat soars to the sky.<br> And Cross-crowned convents tell his fame,<br>     And churches vast and high.</p></dir>  
 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 26</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>Thy record too shall ever stand,<br>     O Eghishé, for where they fell,<br> Thou forthwith camest, pen in hand,<br>     Their faith and death to tell.  
 <p>Bright sun, pierce with thy rays the gloom,<br>     Where Khaghdik's crags thy light repel,<br> There lies our brave Hmayag's tomb,--<br>     There, where he martyred fell.</p>  
 <p>And, moon, thy sleepless vigil keep<br>     O’er our Armenian martyrs' bones;<br> With the soft dews of Maytime steep<br>     Their nameless funeral stones.</p>  
 <p>Armenia's Stork, our summer guest,<br>     And all ye hawks and eagles, come,<br> Watch o’er this land--’tis our bequest--<br>     We leave to you our home.</p>  
 <p>About the ashes hover still,<br>     Your nests among the ruins make;<br> And, swallows, come and go until<br>     Spring for Armenia break!</p></dir>
