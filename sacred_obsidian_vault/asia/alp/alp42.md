 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 51</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE EAGLE'S LOVE</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY SHUSHANIK GOURGHINIAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(<i>Born</i> 1876)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>AN eagle sat upon the fell,--<br>      He sat and sang alone.<br> A pretty maid passed in the dell,<br>      He saw--his heart was won.  
 <p>"Ah, lovely maid, enchanting maid,<br>      Alas, thou canst not fly!<br> Down in the vale thou soon shalt fade,<br>      And like a floweret die.</p>  
 <p>"I'd make thee queen, if thou could’st fly,<br>      Of all my mountains steep;<br> At night I'd sing thy lullaby,<br>      And in my wings thou'dst sleep.</p>  
 <p>"Those eyes are like black night to me,<br>      That smile like sunshine bright;<br> And heaven itself would quickly be<br>      Subdued before thy might.</p>  
 <p>"Good Lord, canst thou not fly at all?<br>      Who made thee without wings?<br> Art thou content down there to crawl?<br>      With loathsome creeping things?"</p>  
 <p>Thus on his rock the eagle proud<br>      Sat singing, then he sailed<br> O’er hill and valley, and aloud<br>      The maiden's fate bewailed.</p></dir>
