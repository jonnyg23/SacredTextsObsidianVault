 <p><a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 18</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE LITTLE LAKE <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="#fn_3"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h5 align="center">BY BEDROS TOURIAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(1852-1872)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>WHY dost thou lie in hushed surprise,<br>     Thou little lonely mere?<br> Did some fair woman wistfully<br>     Gaze in thy mirror clear?  
 <p>Or are thy waters calm and still<br>     Admiring the blue sky,<br> Where shining cloudlets, like thy foam,<br>     Are drifting softly by?</p>  
 <p>Sad little lake, let us be friends!<br>     I too am desolate;<br> I too would fain, beneath the sky,<br>     In silence meditate.</p>  
 <p>As many thoughts are in my mind<br>     As wavelets o’er thee roam;<br> As many wounds are in my heart<br>     As thou hast flakes of foam.</p>  
 <p>But if heaven's constellations all<br>     Should drop into thy breast,<br> Thou still wouldst not be like my soul,--<br>     A flame-sea without rest.</p>  
 <p>There, when the air and thou are calm;<br>     The clouds let fall no showers;<br> The stars that rise there do not set,<br>     And fadeless are the flowers.</p></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_19"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 19</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>Thou art my queen, O little lake!<br>     For e’en when ripples thrill<br> Thy surface, in thy quivering depths<br>     Thou hold’st me, trembling, still.  
 <p>Full many have rejected me:<br>     "What has he but his lyre?"<br> "He trembles, and his face is pale;<br>     His life must soon expire!"</p>  
 <p>None said, "Poor child, why pines he thus?<br>     If he beloved should be,<br> Haply he might not die, but live,<br>     Live, and grow fair to see."</p>  
 <p>None sought the boy's sad heart to read,<br>     Nor in its depths to look.<br> They would have found it was a fire,<br>     And not a printed book!</p>  
 <p>Nay, ashes now! a memory!<br>     Grow stormy, little mere,<br> For a despairing man has gazed<br>     Into thy waters clear!</p>  
 <p>              <i>Translated by Alice Stone Blackwell</i>.</p></dir>
