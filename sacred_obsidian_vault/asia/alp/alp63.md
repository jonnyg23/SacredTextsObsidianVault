 <p><a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 89</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SPRING</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY MUGGURDICH BESHIGTASHLIAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(1829-1868)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>O LITTLE breeze, how fresh and sweet<br> Thou blowest in the morning air!<br> Upon the flowers caressingly,<br> And on the gentle maiden's hair.<br> But not my country's breath thou art:<br> Blow elsewhere, come not near my heart!  
 <p>O little bird among the trees,<br> The sweetness of thy joyful voice<br> Entrances all the Hours of Love,<br> And makes the listening woods rejoice.<br> But not my country's bird thou art:<br> Sing elsewhere, come not near my heart!</p>  
 <p>How peacefully thou murmurest,<br> O gentle, limpid little brook;<br> Within thy mirror crystal-bright<br> The rose and maiden bend to look.<br> But not my country's brook thou art:<br> Flow elsewhere--come not near my heart!</p>  
 <p>Although Armenia's breeze and bird<br> Above a land of ruins fly;<br> Although through mourning cypress groves<br> Armenia's turbid stream flows by,--<br> They are the sighing of her heart,<br> And never shall from mine depart!</p></dir>
