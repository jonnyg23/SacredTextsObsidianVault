 <p><a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 47</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE SORROWS OF ARMENIA</h3>  
 <p></p><dir>IN many a distant, unknown land,<br>      My sons belovèd exiled roam,<br> Servile they kiss the stranger's hand;<br>      How shall I find and bring them home?  
 <p>The ages pass, no tidings come;<br>      My brave ones fall, are lost and gone.<br> My blood is chilled, my voice is dumb,<br>      And friend or comfort I have none.</p>  
 <p>With endless griefs my heart is worn,<br>      Eternal sorrow is my doom;<br> Far from my sons, despis’d, forlorn,<br>      I must descend the darksome tomb.</p>  
 <p>Thou shepherd wandering o’er the hill,<br>      Come weep with me my children lost;<br> Let mournful strains the valleys fill<br>      For those we loved and valued most.</p>  
 <p>Fly, crane, Armenia's bird, depart;<br>      Tell them I die of grief; and tell<br> How hope is dead within my heart--<br>      Bear to my sons my last farewell!</p></dir>
