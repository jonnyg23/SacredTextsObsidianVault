 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">NO BIRD CAN REACH THE MOUNTAIN'S CREST</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY HOVHANNES COSTANIANTZ</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>No bird can reach the mountain's crest.<br> There blow the winds that never rest;<br> And ’midst the stars that crown the height,<br> Saint Gregory's fair lamp shines bright. <a name="fr_14"></a><a href="#fn_14"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>      Ah, gentle brother, sweet and brave,<br>      That Light thy sword and spirit save!  
 <p>How many rills the mountain yields!<br> Those rills are streams, that dew the fields.<br> My brother sweet, those rushing streams<br> Are like my longings and my dreams.<br>      Happy the maid that loveth thee!<br>      When shall thy heart's desire be?</p>  
 <p>See, in the South a tempest breaks--<br> A tempest howls, the leaflet quakes;<br> The bluebell hangs its petals bright,<br> The cock cries out with all his might.<br>      Like showers of gold comes down the rain<br>      Why comes my love not home again?</p></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>The Star of Light begins his course,<br> The brave one mounts upon his horse.<br> He drives his spurs into its flanks,<br> And rides away to join the ranks.<br>     Happy the maid that loveth thee,<br>     When shall thy heart's desire be?  
 <p>There comes no news from far away,<br> Our brave ones rest not from the fray.<br> ’Tis long that sleep my eyes doth flee--<br> Our foemen press unceasingly.<br>     ’Tis long for sleep I vainly pray:<br>     There comes no news from far away.</p></dir>
