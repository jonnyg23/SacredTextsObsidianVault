 <p><a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 44</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE CHRAGAN PALACE</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY THOMAS TERZYAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(1842-1909)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>HAVE you ever seen that wondrous building,<br>      Whose white shadows in the blue wave sleep?<br> There Carrara sent vast mounds of marble,<br>      And Propontis, beauty of the deep.  
 <p>From the tombs of centuries awaking,<br>      Souls of every clime and every land<br> Have poured forth their rarest gifts and treasures<br>      Where those shining halls in glory stand.</p>  
 <p>Ships that pass before that stately palace,<br>      Gliding by with open sails agleam,<br> In its shadow pause and gaze, astonished,<br>      Thinking it some Oriental dream.</p>  
 <p>New its form, more wondrous than the Gothic,<br>      Than the Doric or Ionic fair;<br> At command of an Armenian genius <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>      Did the master builder rear it there.</p>  
 <p>By the windows, rich with twisted scroll-work,<br>      Rising upward, marble columns shine,<br> And the sunbeams lose their way there, wandering<br>      Where a myriad ornaments entwine.</p></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 45</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>An immortal smile, its bright reflection<br>      In the water of the blue sea lies,<br> And it shames Granada's famed Alhambra,<br>      O’er whose beauty wondering bend the skies.  
 <p>Oft at midnight, in the pale, faint starlight,<br>      When its airy outline, clear and fair,<br> On the far horizon is depicted,<br>      With its trees and groves around it there,</p>  
 <p>You can fancy that those stones grow living,<br>      And, amid the darkness of the night,<br> Change to lovely songs, to which the spirit,<br>      Dreaming, listens with a vague delight.</p>  
 <p>Have you ever seen that wondrous building<br>      Whose white shadows in the blue wave sleep?<br> There Carrara sent vast mounds of marble,<br>      And Propontis, beauty of the deep.</p>  
 <p>It is not a mass of earthly matter,<br>      Not a work from clay or marble wrought;<br> From the mind of an Armenian genius<br> Stands embodied there a noble thought.</p>  
 <p>                <i>Translated by Alice Stone Blackwell</i>.</p></dir>
