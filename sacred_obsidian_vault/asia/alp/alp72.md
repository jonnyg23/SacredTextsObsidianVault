 <p><a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 111</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE WANDERING ARMENIAN TO THE SWALLOW</h3>  <h4 align="center">BY C. A. DODOCHIAN</h4>  
 <p></p><dir>O SWALLOW, gentle swallow,<br>      Thou lovely bird of spring!<br> Say, whither art thou flying<br>      So swift on gleaming wing?  
 <p>Fly to my birthplace, Ashdarag,<br>      The spot I love the best;<br> Beneath my father's roof-tree,<br>      O swallow, build thy nest.</p>  
 <p>There dwells afar my father,<br>      A mournful man and grey,<br> Who for his only son's return<br>      Waits vainly, day by day.</p>  
 <p>If thou shouldst chance to see him,<br>      Greet him with love from me;<br> Bid him sit down and mourn with tears<br>      His son's sad destiny.</p>  
 <p>In poverty and loneliness,<br>      Tell him, my days are passed:<br> My life is only half a life,<br>      My tears are falling fast.</p></dir>  
 <p><a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 112</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>To me, amid bright daylight,<br>      The sun is dark at noon;<br> To my wet eyes at midnight<br>      Sleep comes not, late or soon.  
 <p>Tell him that, like a beauteous flower<br>      Smit by a cruel doom,<br> Uprooted from my native soil,<br>      I wither ere my bloom.</p>  
 <p>Fly on swift wing, dear swallow,<br>      Across the quickening earth,<br> And seek in fair Armenia<br>      The village of my birth!</p>  
 <p>                     <i>Translated by Alice Stone Blackwell</i>.</p></dir>
