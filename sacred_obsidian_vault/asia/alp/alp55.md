 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 77</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ARAXES CAME DEVOURINGLY</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY HOVHANNES HOVHANNESSIAN</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>ARAXES came devouringly,<br> Swept o’er her boulders scouringly--<br> Where shall I lay my aching head<br> Bowed down with grief o’erpoweringly?  
 <p>Oh my Araxes, flow serene;--<br> Tell me, hast thou my sweet love seen?<br> My heart's desire is unfulfilled;--<br> Arax, hast thou more happy been?</p>  
 <p>Mount Ararat with clouds is veiled,<br> My love is lost, my hope has failed.<br> For pity's sake an answer give<br> To my sad heart with grief assailed.</p>  
 <p>I sob and weep the livelong night;<br> Till dawn I watch--I watch and write;<br> Arax, ere sunrise gild thy waves<br> To thee I bring my spirit's blight.</p>  
 <p>Upon the rocks the sunbeams dart,<br> Red flames devour my mourning heart;<br> Those eyes and brows have left with me<br> A sorrow which shall ne’er depart.</p></dir>
