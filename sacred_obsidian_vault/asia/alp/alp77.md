 <p><a name="page_121"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 121</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LOVE ONE ANOTHER</h3>  <h4 align="center">BY BEDROS TOURIAN</h4>  
 <p></p><dir>UNDYING Love, Whose beams forever glow<br> On rose-red Golgotha's stupendous brow;<br> Wilt Thou those shafts still in Thy bosom keep?<br> What guardest Thou?--bones, spectres, chasms deep,--<br> That in the echoes of the mountain-side<br> Thy noble words, "Love one another," died?  
 <p>Effaced and trampled is the poor man's tomb;<br> The poor man's candle flickers out in gloom;<br> And in that darkness starving children weep,<br> While in the palace revels high they keep.<br> The rich man's carriage dashes gaily past,<br> The beggar's lonely corpse to earth is cast.</p>  
 <p>The pallid angel of Gethsemane<br> Tears doth not heed nor flowers, nor glory's plea.<br> The poor find rest in his cold arms alone,<br> For in Death's shroud the high and low are one.<br> Though lightning-winged the winds cry o’er the moor,<br> "Love one another," here none love the poor.</p></dir>
