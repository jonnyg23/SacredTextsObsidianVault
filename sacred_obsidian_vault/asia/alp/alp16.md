 <p><a name="page_12"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 12</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LIBERTY</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY MIKAEL NALBANDIAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(1829-1866)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>WHEN the God of Liberty<br> Formed of earth this mortal frame,<br> Breathed the breath of life in me,<br> And a spirit I became,  
 <p>Wrapped within my swaddling bands,<br> Bound and fettered helplessly, <a name="fr_2"></a><a href="#fn_2"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> I stretched forth my infant hands<br> To embrace sweet Liberty.</p>  
 <p>All night long, until the dawn,<br> In my cradle bound I lay;<br> And my sobbing's ceaseless moan<br> Drove my mother's sleep away.</p>  
 <p>As I begged her, weeping loud,<br> To unbind and set me free;<br> From that very day I vowed<br> I would love thee, Liberty!</p>  
 <p>When upon my parents' ear<br> First my lisping accents fell,<br> And their hearts rejoiced to hear<br> Me my childish wishes tell,</p></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>Then the words that first I spoke<br> Were not "father, mother dear":<br> "Liberty!" the accents broke<br> In my infant utterance clear.  
 <p>"Liberty!" The voice of Doom<br> Echoed to me from above,<br> "Wilt thou swear until the tomb<br> Liberty to serve and love?</p>  
 <p>"Thorny is the path, and dim;<br> Many trials wait for thee:<br> Far too small this world for him<br> Who doth worship Liberty!"</p>  
 <p>"Liberty!" I made reply,<br> "O’er my head let thunders burst,<br> Lightnings flash, and missiles fly--<br> Foes conspire to do their worst;</p>  
 <p>"Till I die, or meet my doom,<br> On the shameful gallows-tree,-<br> Till the portals of the tomb,<br> I will shout forth Liberty!"</p></dir>
