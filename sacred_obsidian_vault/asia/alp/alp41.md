 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MY DEATH</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY BEDROS TOUR IAN</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>WHEN Death's pale angel comes to me,<br> And smiling sweetly on my head,<br> Bids all my pains and sorrows fleet,--<br> Believe not then that I am dead.  
 <p>When my cold limbs they shroud with care,<br> And on my brow love's tear-drops shed,<br> And lay me on my ebon bier,--<br> Believe not then that I am dead.</p>  
 <p>And when the tolling bell shall ring<br> To my black coffin's muted tread<br> --Death's fiendish laughter, quivering,--<br> Believe not then that I am dead.</p>  
 <p>And when the black-robed priests shall sing,<br> And prayers and incense round me spread,<br> With faces dark and sorrowing--<br> Believe not still that I am dead.</p>  
 <p>When on my tomb they heap the clay,<br> And leave me in my lonely bed,<br> And loved ones turn with sobs away--<br> Then never think that I am dead.</p>  
 <p>But if my grave neglected lie,<br> My memory too be gone and fled,<br> And dear ones pass unheeding by,<br> Ah, then believe that I am dead!</p></dir>
