 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">A DAY AFTER</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY BEDROS TOURIAN</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>SLUMBERING darkly yesterday,<br> As in deathly sweats I lay,<br> And two roses fiery red<br> On my faded cheeks were spread--<br> On my brow perchance a ray<br> Of Death's pallor trembling lay.<br> And I prayed and longed for death.<br> Then I heard a sobbing breath . . .<br> I unclosed my weary eyes<br> And beheld my mother's tears--<br> Saw those priceless pearls arise,<br> Tokens of her love, her fears.<br> In her heart what grief she bore!<br> And I was that anguish sore.<br> Ah, I raged tempestuously,<br> That black torrent outward leapt <a name="fr_10"></a><a href="#fn_10"><font size="1">1</font></a>:--<br> God of mercy, pardon me,<br> For the tears my mother wept!</dir>
