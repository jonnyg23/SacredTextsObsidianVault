 <h3 align="CENTER">Transcription</h3>
 <p><a name="page_7"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 7</font></a></p>
 <p>The following alphabet is used in transcribing native words:--</p>
 <p>a, e, i, u have their continental sounds (in Chukchee and Koryak always long).</p>
 <p>o like <i>o</i> in <i>nor</i>.</p>
 <p>ä obscure vowel (long).</p>
 <p>ë like <i>a</i> in make.</p>
 <p>A, E, I obscure vowels (short).</p>
 <p>ê like e in bell, but prolonged.</p>
 <sup><p>e</p></sup>i a <a href="errata.htm#0">diphthong</a> with an accent on i. It always has a laryngeal intonation, <sup>e</sup>i<sup>?</sup>.
 <strike></strike><p>o between <i>o</i> and <i>u</i> long.</p>
 <p>ü mouth in <i>i</i> position, lips in <i>u</i> position (short).</p>
 <p>w, y as in English.</p>
 <p>Very long and very short vowels are indicated by the macron and breve respectively.</p>
 <p>The diphthongs are formed by combining any of the vowels with <i>i</i> and <i>u</i>. Thus:--</p>
 <p>ai like <i>i</i> in <i>hide</i>.</p>
 <p>ei like <i>ei</i> in <i>vein</i>.</p>
 <p>oi like <i>oi</i> in <i>choice</i>.</p>
 <p>au like <i>ow</i> in <i>how</i>.</p>
 <p>l as in German.</p>
 <p>ł pronounced with the tip of the tongue touching the palate a little above the alveoli of the upper jaw, the back of the tongue free.</p>
 <p>L posterior palatal l, surd and exploded (affricative), the tip of the tongue pressed against the hard palate.</p>
 <p>Ł posterior palatal l, sonant.</p>
 <p>r as in French.</p>
 <p>ř dental with slight trill.</p>
 <p>ŗ velar.</p>
 <p>m as in English.</p>
 <p>n as in English</p>
 <p>ñ nasal n sound.</p>
 <p>n* palatized <i>n</i> (similar to <i>ny</i>).</p>
 <p>b, p as in English.</p>
 <p>b`, p` d`, d`, g`, k` have a spirant added (<i>gehauchter Asatz</i> of Sievers).</p>
 <p>v bilabial.</p>
 <p>g like <i>g</i> in <i>good</i>.</p>
 <p>h as in English.</p>
 <p>x like <i>ch</i> in German <i>Bach</i>.</p>
 <p>x* like <i>ch</i> in German <i>ich</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="page_8"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 8</font></a></p>
 <p>q velar k.</p>
 <p>k as in English.</p>
 <p>ġ velar g.</p>
 <p>d, t as in English.</p>
 <p>d*, t* palatized (similar to <i>dy</i> and <i>ty</i>).</p>
 <p>s as in English.</p>
 <p>s* palatized (similar to <i>sy</i>).</p>
 <p>š palatized German <i>z</i>.</p>
 <p>c like English <i>sh</i>.</p>
 <p>č like English <i>ch</i>.</p>
 <p>j like j in French jour.</p>
 <p>ĵ like j in joy.</p>
 <p>c* strongly palatized</p>
 <p>ĵ* strongly palatized j.</p>
 <p>! designates increased stress of articulation.</p>
 <sup><p>?</p></sup> a very deep laryngeal intonation.
 <p>? a full pause between two vowels: yiñe?a.</p>
