 <p>
 This is the little book which provided the rationale for the American
 colonists to struggle for independence from English rule.
 Relative to the size of the population at the time,
 it is the US best seller of all time; it sold half a million copies
 in the first year, going through twenty-five editions.
 Paine wrote Common Sense like a sermon, using Biblical references to
 make his point, despite his Deist views.
 However, this style appealed to a broad audience, not just
 intellectuals.
 Paine even put his money on the line: he donated all royalties from
 <cite>Common Sense</cite>, which
 were substantial, to the Continental Army of George Washington.--J.B. Hare
 </p>
