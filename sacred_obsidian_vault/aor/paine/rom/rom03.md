 <p align="center">RIGHTS OF MAN</p>  
 <p align="center">BEING AN ANSWER TO MR. BURKE'S ATTACK ON THE FRENCH</p>  
 <p align="center">REVOLOUTION</p>  
 <p align="center">BY</p>  
 <p align="center">THOMAS PAINE</p>  
 <p align="center">SECRETARY FOR FOREIGN AFFAIRS TO CONGRESS IN THE</p>  
 <p align="center">AMERICAN WAR, AND</p>  
 <p align="center">AUTHOR OF THE WORKS ENTITLED "COMMON SENSE' AND 'A LETTER TO ABBÉ RAYNAL"</p>  
 <p align="center">DEDICATION</p>  
 <p align="center">George Washington</p>  
 <p align="center">President Of The United States Of America</p>  
 <p>Sir,</p>  
 <p>I present you a small treatise in defence of those principles of freedom which your exemplary virtue hath so eminently contributed to establish. That the Rights of Man may become as universal as your benevolence can wish, and that you may enjoy the happiness of seeing the New World regenerate the Old, is the prayer of</p>  
 <p>Sir,</p>  
 <p>Your much obliged, and</p>  
 <p>  Obedient humble Servant,</p>  
 <p>    Thomas Paine</p>
