 <p></p><h3 align="CENTER">FIFTH BOOK ON LIGHT AND SHADE.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">OF THE WAY IN WHICH THE SHADOWS CAST BY OBJECTS OUGHT TO BE DEFINED.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Principles of reflection (203. 204).</font></span>If the object is the mountain here figured, and the light is at the
 point <i>a</i>, I say that from <i>b d</i> and also from <i>c f</i> there will be
 no light but from reflected rays. And this results from the fact
 that rays of light can only act in straight lines; and the same is
 the case with the secondary or reflected rays.
