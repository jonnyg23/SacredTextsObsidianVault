 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">WHY BODIES IN LIGHT AND SHADE HAVE THEIR OUTLINES ALTERED BY THE COLOUR AND BRIGHTNESS OF THE OBJECTS SERVING AS A BACKGROUND TO THEM.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v113400"></a>
 <img src="img/v113400.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 If you look at a body of which the illuminated portion lies and ends
 against a dark background, that part of the light which will look
 brightest will be that which lies against the dark [background] at
 <i>d</i>. But if this brighter part lies against a light background, the
 edge of the object, which is itself light, will be less distinct
 than before, and the highest light will appear to be between the
 limit of the background <i>m f</i> and the shadow. The same thing is seen
 with regard to the dark [side],
 </p><p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a></p>
 <p>inasmuch as that edge of the shaded
 portion of the object which lies against a light background, as at
 <i>l</i>, it looks much darker than the rest. But if this shadow lies
 against a dark background, the edge of the shaded part will appear
 lighter than before, and the deepest shade will appear between the
 edge and the light at the point <i>o</i>.
 </p><p>
