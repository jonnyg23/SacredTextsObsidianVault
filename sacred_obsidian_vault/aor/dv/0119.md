 <h3 align="CENTER">119.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">WHAT LIGHT AND SHADOW ARE.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Definition of the nature of shadows (119--122).</font></span>Shadow is the absence of light, merely the obstruction of the
 luminous rays by an opaque body. Shadow is of the nature of
 darkness. Light [on an object] is of the nature of a luminous body;
 one conceals and the other reveals. They are always associated and
 inseparable from all objects. But shadow
 </p><p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 73</font></a></p>
 <p>is a more powerful agent
 than light, for it can impede and entirely deprive bodies of their
 light, while light can never entirely expel shadow from a body, that
 is from an opaque body.
 </p><p>
