 <p></p><p>
 The motion of a spectator who sees an object at rest often makes it
 seem as though the object at rest had acquired the motion of the
 moving body, while the moving person appears to be at rest.
 </p><p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">ON PAINTING.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v102100"></a>
 <img src="img/v102100.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 Objects in relief, when seen from a short distance with one eye,
 look like a perfect picture. If you look with the eye <i>a</i>, <i>b</i> at
 the spot <i>c</i>, this point <i>c</i> will appear to be at <i>d</i>, <i>f</i>, and if
 you look at it with the eye <i>g</i>, <i>h</i> will appear to be at <i>m</i>. A
 picture can never contain in itself both aspects.
 </p><p>
