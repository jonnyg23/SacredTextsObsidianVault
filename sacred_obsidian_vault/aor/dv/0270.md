 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">OF THE RAYS WHICH CONVEY THROUGH THE AIR THE IMAGES OF OBJECTS.</h3>
 <p>
 All the minutest parts of the image intersect each other without
 interfering with
 </p><p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 145</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v114500"></a>
 <img src="img/v114500.jpg"></p>
 each other. To prove this let <i>r</i> be one of the
 sides of the hole, opposite to which let <i>s</i> be the eye which sees
 the lower end <i>o</i> of the line <i>n o</i>. The other extremity cannot
 transmit its image to the eye <i>s</i> as it has to strike the end <i>r</i>
 and it is the same with regard to <i>m</i> at the middle of the line. The
 case is the same with the upper extremity <i>n</i> and the eye <i>u</i>. And
 if the end <i>n</i> is red the eye <i>u</i> on that side of the holes will not
 see the green colour of <i>o</i>, but only the red of <i>n</i> according to
 the 7th of this where it is said: Every form projects images from
 itself by the shortest line, which necessarily is a straight line,
