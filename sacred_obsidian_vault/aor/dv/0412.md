 <h3 align="CENTER">412.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">OF THE INSERTION OF THE BRANCHES ON PLANTS.</h3>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> The insertion of the leaves (412--419).</font></span><p>
 Such as the growth of the ramification of plants is on their
 principal branches, so is that of the leaves on the shoots of the
 same plant. These leaves have  <a name="fr_209"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">207</font></a>
  four modes of growing one
 above another. The first, which is the most general, is that the
 sixth always originates over the sixth below  <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">208</font></a>
 ; the second is that two third ones
 above are over the two third ones below  <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">209</font></a>
 ; and the
 third way is that the third above is over the third below  <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">210</font></a>
 .
 </p><p>
  <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">211</font></a>

 <p><a name="fn_209"></a><a href="0412.htm#fr_209">211:207</a>  6: <i>Quattro modi</i> (four
 modes). Only three are described in the text, the fourth is only
 suggested by a sketch.
 </p><p>
 This passage occurs in MANZI'S edition of the Trattato, p. 399, but
 without the sketches and the text is mutilated in an important part.
 The whole passage has been commented on, from MANZI'S version, in
 Part I of the <i>Nuovo Giornale Botanico Italiano</i>, by Prof. G.
 UZIELLI (Florence 1869, Vol. I). He remarks as to the 'four modes':
 "<i>Leonardo, come si vede nelle linie sententi da solo tre esempli.
 Questa ed altre inessattezze fanno desiderare, sia esaminato di
 nuovo il manoscritto Vaticano</i>". This has since been done by D.
 KNAPP of Tubingen, and his accurate copy has been published by H.
 LUDWIG, the painter. The passage in question occurs in his edition
 as No. 833; and there also the drawings are wanting. The space for
 them has been left vacant, but in the Vatican copy '<i>niente</i>' has
 been written on the margin; and in it, as well as in LUDWIG'S and
 MANZI'S edition, the text is mutilated.</p>
