 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_452"></a><a href="1444.htm#fr_452">433:758</a>  1: <i>Pandolfino, Agnolo</i>, of Florence. It is to
 this day doubtful whether he or L. B. Alberti was
 the author of the famous work '<i>Del Governo della
 Famiglia</i>'. It is the more probable that Leonardo
 should have meant this work by the words <i>il libro</i>,
 because no other book is known to have been
 written by Pandolfino. This being the case this allusion
 of Leonardo's is an important evidence in
 favour of Pandolfino's authorship (compare No. 1454,
 line 3).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_453"></a><a href="1444.htm#fr_453">433:759</a>  7: The works of Lactantius were published
 very often in Italy during Leonardo's lifetime. The
 first edition published in 1465 "<i>in monastero
 sublacensi</i>" was also the first book printed in
 Italy.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_454"></a><a href="1444.htm#fr_454">433:760</a>  15: <i>Sansovino</i>, Andrea--the <i>sculptor</i>; 1460-1529.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_455"></a><a href="1444.htm#fr_455">433:761</a>  25: Leonardo here probably alludes to the map,
 not executed by him (See p. 224), which is with the
 collection of his MSS. at Windsor, and was published
 in the <i>Archaeologia</i> Vol. XI (see p. 224).</p>
