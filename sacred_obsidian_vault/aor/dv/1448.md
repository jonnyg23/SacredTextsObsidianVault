 <h3 align="CENTER">1448.</h3>
 <p>
 An algebra, which the Marliani have,
 written by their father,  <a name="fr_457"></a><a href="#fn_457"><font size="1">763</font></a>
 --
 </p><p>
 On the bone, by the Marliani,--
 </p><p>
 On the bone which penetrates, Gian Giacomo
 of Bellinzona, to draw out the nail with
 facility,--
 </p><p>
 The measurement of Boccalino,--
 </p><p>
 The measurement of Milan and the suburbs,
  <a name="fr_458"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">764</font></a>
 --
 </p><p>
 A book, treating of Milan and its churches
 which is to be had at the last stationer's
 on the way to Corduso  <a name="fr_459"></a><a href="#fn_459"><font size="1">765</font></a>
 ,--
 </p><p>
 The measurement of the Corte Vecchia,--
 </p><p>
 The measurement of the Castle,--
 </p><p>
 Get the master of arithmetic to show you
 how to square a....,--
 </p><p>
 Get Messer Fazio to show you [the book]
 on proportion,--
 </p><p><a name="page_435"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 435</font></a></p>
 <p>Get the Friar di Brera to show you [the
 book] '<i>de Ponderibus</i>',--  <a name="fr_460"></a><a href="#fn_460"><font size="1">766</font></a>
 
 </p><p>
 Of the measurement of San Lorenzo,-- <a name="fr_461"></a><a href="#fn_461"><font size="1">767</font></a>
 
 </p><p>
 I lent certain groups to Fra Filippo de
 Brera,--  <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_462"><font size="1">768</font></a>
 
 </p><p>
 Memorandum: to ask Maestro Giovannino
 as to the mode in which the tower of
 Ferrara is walled without loopholes,--
 </p><p>
 Ask Maestro Antonio how mortars are
 placed on bastions by day or by night,--
 </p><p>
 Ask Benedetto Portinari how the people
 go on the ice in Flanders,--  <a name="fr_463"></a><a href="#fn_463"><font size="1">769</font></a>
 
 </p><p>
 On proportions by Alchino, with notes
 by Marliano, from Messer Fazio,--
 </p><p>
 The measurement of the sun, promised me
 by Maestro Giovanni, the Frenchman,--
 </p><p>
 The cross bow of Maestro Gianetto,--
 </p><p>
 The book by Giovanni Taverna that
 Messer Fazio,--
 </p><p>
 You will draw Milan [21],--
 </p><p>
 The measurement of the canal, locks and
 supports, and large boats; and the expense,--
 </p><p>
 Plan of Milan  <a name="fr_464"></a><a href="#fn_464"><font size="1">770</font></a>
 ,--
 </p><p>
 Groups by Bramante  <a name="fr_465"></a><a href="#fn_465"><font size="1">771</font></a>
 ,--
 </p><p>
 The book on celestial phenomena by
 Aristoteles, in Italian  <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">772</font></a>
 ,--
 </p><p>
 Try to get Vitolone, which is in the
 library at Pavia  <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">773</font></a>
  and which treats of
 Mathematics,--He had a master [learned] in
 waterworks and get him to explain the repairs
 and the costs, and a lock and a canal and
 a mill in the Lombard fashion.
 </p><p>
 A grandson of Gian Angelo's, the painter
 has a book on water which was his fathers.
 </p><p>
 Paolino Scarpellino, called Assiolo has
 great knowledge of water works.
 </p><p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_457"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_457">434:763</a>  1:
 <i>Marliani</i>, an old Milanese family, now
 extinct.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_458"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_458">434:764</a>  5: 21. See <a href="pl109.htm#img_pl109">Pl. CIX</a> and No. 1016.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_459"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_459">434:765</a>  6: <i>Corduso</i>, see No. 1413, note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_460"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_460">435:766</a> : 11. 13.  <i>Brera</i>, now <i>Palazzo delle Scienze ed Arti</i>.
 Until 1571 it was the monastery of the order of the
 Umiliati and afterwards of the Jesuits.
 </p><p>
 <i>De ponderibus</i>, compare No. 1436, 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_461"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_461">435:767</a> : 12.  <i>Sco Lorenzo</i>. A church at Milan, see pp. 39, 40 and 50.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_462"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_462">435:768</a> : 13. 24. <i>Gruppi</i>. See Vol. I p. 355, No. 600, note 9.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_463"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_463">435:769</a> : 16. The <i>Portinari</i> were one of the great merchant-
 families of Florence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_464"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_464">435:770</a>  23: <i>Fondamento</i> is commonly used by Leonardo to
 mean ground-plan. See for instance p. 53.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_465"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_465">435:771</a>  24: <i>Gruppi</i>. See Vol. I p. 355, No. 600, note 9.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_466"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_466">435:772</a>  25: <i>Meteora</i>. By this Leonardo means no doubt
 the four books τὰ μετεωρολογιχά. He must refer
 here to a MS. translation, as no Italian translation
 is known to have been published (see No. 1477
 note).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_467"></a><a href="1448.htm#fr_467">435:773</a>  26: <i>Vitolone</i> see No. 1506, note.
 </p><p>
 <i>Libreria di Pavia</i>. One of the most famous of
 Italian libraries. After the victory of Novara in
 April 1500, Louis XII had it conveyed to France,
 '<i>come trofeo di vittoria</i>'!</p>
