 <h3 align="CENTER">1116.</h3>
 <p><a name="page_276"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 276</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v227600"></a>

 <p>
 If you want to build an armada for the sea employ these ships to ram
 in the enemy's ships. That is, make ships 100 feet long and 8 feet
 wide, but arranged so that the left hand rowers may have their oars
 to the right side of the ship, and the right hand ones to the left
 side, as is shown at M, so that the leverage of the oars may be
 longer. And the
 </p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v227601"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/v227601.jpg"><img src="tn/v227601.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></p>
 said ship may be one foot and a half thick, that is
 made with cross beams within and without,
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v227602"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/v227602.jpg"><img src="tn/v227602.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v227603"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/v227603.jpg"><img src="tn/v227603.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></p>
 with planks in contrary
 directions. And this ship must have attached to it, a foot below the
 <p><a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 277</font></a></p>
 <p>water, an iron-shod spike of about the weight and size of an anvil;
 and this, by force of oars may, after it has given the first blow,
 be drawn back, and driven forward again with fury give a second
 blow, and then a third, and so many as to destroy the other ship.
