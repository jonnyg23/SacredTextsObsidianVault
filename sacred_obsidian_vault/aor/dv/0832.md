 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Remarks on the organs of speech (832. 833).</font></span>The tongue is found to have 24 muscles which correspond to the six
 muscles which compose the portion of the tongue which moves in the
 mouth.
 </p><p>
 And when <i>a o u</i> are spoken with a clear and rapid pronunciation, it
 is necessary, in order to pronounce continuously, without any pause
 between, that the opening of the lips should close by degrees; that
 is, they are wide apart in saying <i>a</i>, closer in saying <i>o</i>, and
 much closer still to pronounce <i>u</i>.
 </p><p>
 It may be shown how all the vowels are pronounced with the farthest
 portion of the false palate which is above the epiglottis.
