 <h3 align="CENTER">780.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">ON THE STRENGTH OF THE ARCH.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v208703"></a>
 <img src="img/v208703.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 The way to give stability to the arch is to fill the spandrils with
 good masonry up to the level of its summit.

 </p><h3 align="CENTER">ON THE DAMAGE DONE TO THE POINTED ARCH BY THROWING THE PRESSURE ON THE FLANKS.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v208800"></a>
 <img src="img/v208800.jpg"></p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v208801"></a>
 <img src="img/v208801.jpg"></p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v208802"></a>
 <img src="img/v208802.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 An arch of small curve is safe in itself, but if it be heavily
 charged, it is necessary to strengthen the flanks well. An arch of a
 very large curve is weak in itself, and stronger if it be charged,
 and will do little harm to its abutments, and its places of giving
 way are <i>o p</i>.
