 <h3 align="CENTER">328.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Proportions of the leg (328-331).</font></span>The greatest thickness of the calf of the leg is at a third of its
 height <i>a b</i>, and is a twentieth part thicker than the greatest
 thickness of the foot.
 </p><p>
 <i>a c</i> is half of the head, and equal to <i>d b</i> and to the insertion
 of the five toes <i>e f</i>. <i>d k</i> diminishes one sixth in the leg <i>g h</i>.
 <i>g h</i> is 1/3 of the head; <i>m n</i> increases one sixth from <i>a e</i> and
 is 7/12 of the head, <i>o p</i> is 1/10 less than <i>d k</i> and is 6/17 of
 the head. <i>a</i> is at half the distance between <i>b q</i>, and is 1/4 of
 the man. <i>r</i> is half way between <i>s</i> and <i>b</i> <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">144</font></a>
 . The concavity of the knee outside <i>r</i> is higher
 than that inside <i>a</i>. The half of the whole height of the leg from
 the foot <i>r</i>, is half way between the prominence <i>s</i> and the ground
 <i>b</i>. <i>v</i> is half way between <i>t</i> and <i>b</i>. The thickness of the thigh
 seen in front is equal to the greatest width of the face, that is
 2/3 of the length from the chin to the top of the head; <i>z r</i> is 5/6
 of 7 to <i>v</i>; <i>m n</i> is equal to 7 <i>v</i> and is 1/4 of <i>r b</i>, <i>x y</i> goes
 3 times into <i>r b</i>, and into <i>r s</i>.
 </p><p>

 </p><p>

 times into <i>c f</i> and six times into <i>c n</i> and is equal to <i>g h</i>; <i>i
 k l m</i> goes 4 times into <i>d f</i>, and 4 times into <i>d n</i> and is 3/7 of
 the foot; <i>p q r s</i> goes 3 times into <i>d f</i>, and 3 times into <i>b n</i>;
  <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">147</font></a>

 and is equal to <i>n q</i>. 3 7 is 1/9 of <i>n f</i>; 4 5 is 1/10 of <i>n f</i>
  <a name="fr_150"></a><a href="#fn_150"><font size="1">148</font></a>

 </p><p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 178</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p>
 I want to know how much a man increases in height by standing on
 tip-toe and how much <i>p g</i> diminishes by stooping; and how much it
 increases at <i>n q</i> likewise in bending the foot.
 </p><p>
