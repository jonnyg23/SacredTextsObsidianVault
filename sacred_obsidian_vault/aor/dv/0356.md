 <h3 align="CENTER">356.</h3>
 <p>
 Three are the principal muscles of the shoulder, that is <i>b c d</i>,
 and two are the lateral muscles which move it forward and backward,
 that is <i>a o</i>; <i>a</i> moves it forward, and <i>o</i> pulls it back; and bed
 raises it; <i>a b c</i> moves it upwards and forwards, and <i>c d o</i>
 upwards and backwards. Its own weight almost suffices to move it
 downwards.
 </p><p>
 The muscle <i>d</i> acts with the muscle <i>c</i> when the arm moves forward;
 and in moving backward the muscle <i>b</i> acts with the muscle <i>c</i>.
 </p><p>
