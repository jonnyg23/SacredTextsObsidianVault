 </p><h3 align="CENTER">SECOND BOOK ON LIGHT AND SHADE.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">THAT PORTION OF A BODY IN LIGHT AND SHADE WILL BE LEAST LUMINOUS WHICH IS SEEN UNDER THE LEAST AMOUNT OF LIGHT.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Gradations of strength in the shadows (148. 149).</font></span>That part of the object which is marked <i>m</i> is in the highest light
 because it faces the window <i>a d</i> by the line <i>a f</i>; <i>n</i> is in the
 second grade because the light <i>b d</i> strikes it by the line <i>b e</i>;
 <i>o</i> is in the third grade, as the light falls on it from <i>c d</i> by
 the line <i>c h</i>; <i>p</i> is the lowest light but one as <i>c d</i> falls on it
 by the line <i>d v</i>; <i>q</i> is the deepest shadow for no light falls on
 it from any part of the window.
 </p><p>
 In proportion as <i>c d</i> goes into <i>a d</i> so will <i>n r s</i> be darker
 than <i>m</i>, and all the rest is space without shadow.
 </p><p>
