 <h3 align="CENTER">776.</h3>
 <p>
 This wall will break under the arch <i>e f</i>, because the seven whole
 square bricks are not sufficient to sustain the spring of the arch
 placed on them. And these seven bricks will give way in their middle
 exactly as appears in <i>a b</i>. The reason is, that the brick <i>a</i> has
 above it only the weight <i>a k</i>, whilst the last brick under the arch
 has above it the weight <i>c d x a</i>.
 </p><p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a></p>
 <p><i>c d</i> seems to press on the arch towards the abutment at the point
 <i>p</i> but the weight <i>p o</i> opposes resistence to it, whence the whole
 </p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v208200"></a>
 <img src="img/v208200.jpg"></p>
 <p>pressure is transmitted to the root of the arch. Therefore the foot
 of the arch acts like 7 6, which is more than double of <i>x z</i>.
