 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_157"></a><a href="0915.htm#fr_157">171:491</a> : The statement that CICERO, <i>De Divin.</i> ascribes the
 discovery of astrology to a period 57000 years before the Trojan war
 I believe to be quite erroneous. According to ERNESTI, <i>Clavis
 Ciceroniana,</i> CH. G. SCHULZ (<i>Lexic. Cicer.</i>) and the edition of <i>De
 Divin.</i> by GIESE the word Astrologia occurs only twice in CICERO:
 <i>De Divin. II</i>, 42. <i>Ad Chaldaeorum monstra veniamus, de quibus
 Eudoxus, Platonis auditor, in astrologia judicio doctissimorum
 hominum facile princeps, sic opinatur (id quod scriptum reliquit):
 Chaldaeis in praedictione et in notatione cujusque vitae ex natali
 die minime esse credendum.</i>" He then quotes the condemnatory verdict
 of other philosophers as to the teaching of the Chaldaeans but says
 nothing as to the antiquity and origin of astronomy. CICERO further
 notes <i>De oratore</i> I, 16 that Aratus was "<i>ignarus astrologiae</i>" but
 that is all. So far as I know the word occurs nowhere else in
 CICERO; and the word <i>Astronomia</i> he does not seem to have used at
 all. (H. MULLER-STRUBING.)</p>
