 <h3 align="CENTER">790.</h3>
 <p>
 The heaviest part of the foundations of buildings settles most, and
 leaves the lighter part above it separated from it.
 </p><p>
 And the soil which is most pressed, if it be porous yields most.
 </p><p>
 You should always make the foundations project equally beyond the
 weight of the walls and piers, as shown at <i>m a b</i>. If you do as
 many do, that is to say if you make a foundation of equal width from
 the bottom up to the surface of the ground, and charge
 </p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v209600"></a>
 <img src="img/v209600.jpg"></p>
 <p>it above with
 unequal weights, as shown at <i>b e</i> and at <i>e o</i>, at the part of the
 foundation at <i>b e</i>, the pier of the angle will weigh most and
 thrust its foundation downwards, which the wall at <i>e o</i> will not
 do; since it does not cover the whole of its foundation, and
 therefore thrusts less heavily and settles less. Hence, the pier <i>b
 e</i> in settling cracks and parts from the wall <i>e o</i>. This may be
 seen in most buildings which are cracked round the piers.
 </p><p>
