 <h3 align="CENTER">282.</h3>
 <p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 151</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">PERSPECTIVE.</h3>
 <p>
 That side of an object in light and shade which is towards the light
 transmits the images of its details more distinctly and immediately
 to the eye than the side which is in shadow.
 </p><p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">PERSPECTIVE.</h3>
 <p>
 The solar rays reflected on a square mirror will be thrown back to
 distant objects in a circular form.
 </p><p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">PERSPECTIVE.</h3>
 <p>
 Any white and opaque surface will be partially coloured by
 reflections from surrounding objects.
 </p><p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_128"></a><a href="0282.htm#fr_128">151:126</a>  281. 282: The title line of these chapters is in the
 original simply <i>"pro"</i>, which may be an abbreviation for either
 <i>Propositione</i> or <i>Prospettiva</i>--taking Prospettiva of course in its
 widest sense, as we often find it used in Leonardo's writings. The
 title <i>"pro"</i> has here been understood to mean <i>Prospettiva</i>, in
 accordance with the suggestion afforded by page 10b of this same
 MS., where the first section is headed <i>Prospettiva</i> in full (see
 No. 94), while the four following sections are headed merely <i>"pro"</i>
 (see No. 85).</p>
