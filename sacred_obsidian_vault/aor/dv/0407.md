 <h3 align="CENTER">407.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v120902"></a>
 <img src="img/v120902.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 The lowest branches, after they have formed the angle of their
 separation from the parent stem, always bend downwards so as not to
 crowd against the other branches which follow them on the same stem and
 </p><p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>
 <p>to be better able to take the air which nourishes them. As is
 shown by the angle <i>b a c</i>; the branch <i>a c</i> after it has made the
 corner of the angle <i>a c</i> bends downwards to <i>c d</i> and the lesser
 shoot <i>c</i> dries up, being too thin.
 </p><p>
 The main branch always goes below, as is shown by the branch <i>f n
 m</i>, which does not go to <i>f n o</i>.
 </p><p>
