 <h3 align="CENTER">272.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Combination of different colours in cast shadows.</font></span>That which casts the shadow does not face it, because the shadows
 are produced by the light which causes and surrounds the shadows.
 The shadow caused by the light <i>e</i>,
 </p><p><a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 146</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v114600"></a>
 <img src="img/v114600.jpg"></p>
 which is yellow, has a blue
 tinge, because the shadow of the body <i>a</i> is cast upon the pavement
 at <i>b</i>, where the blue light falls; and the shadow produced by the
 light <i>d</i>, which is blue, will be yellow at <i>c</i>, because the yellow
 light falls there and the surrounding background to these shadows <i>b
 c</i> will, besides its natural colour, assume a hue compounded of
 yellow and blue, because it is lighted by the yellow light and by
 the blue light both at once.
 <p>
 Shadows of various colours, as affected by the lights falling on
 them. That light which causes the shadow does not face it.
 </p><p>
