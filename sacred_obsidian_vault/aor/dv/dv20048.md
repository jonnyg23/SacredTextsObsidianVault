 </p><h3 align="CENTER"><i>III. Castles and Villas.</i></h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER"><i>A. Castles.</i></h3>
 <p>
 <i><a href="pl080.htm#img_pl080">Pl. LXXX</a>, No. 1 (P. V. fol. 39b; No. d'ordre 2282). The fortified
 place here represented is said by Vallardi to be the</i> "castello" <i>at
 Milan, but without any satisfactory reason. The high tower behind
 the</i> "rivellino" <i>ravelin--seems to be intended as a watch-tower.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl080.htm#img_pl080">Pl. LXXX</a>, No. 2 (MS. B, 23b). A similarly constructed tower probably
 intended for the same use.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl080.htm#img_pl080">Pl. LXXX</a>, No. 3 (MS. B). Sketches for corner towers with steps for a
 citadel.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl080.htm#img_pl080">Pl. LXXX</a>, No. 4 (W. XVI). A cupola crowning a corner tower; an
 interesting example of decorative fortification. In this
 reproduction of the original pen and ink drawing it appears
 reversed.</i>
 </p><p>

 <p>
 <i>The three following sketches greatly resemble each other. <a href="pl082.htm#img_pl082">Pl. LXXXII</a>, No. 2 (MS. K3 36b; see No. 749).</i>
 </p><p><a name="page_33"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 33</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p>
 <i><a href="pl082.htm#img_pl082">Pl. LXXXII</a>, No. 3 (MS. B 60a; See No. 750).</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl083.htm#img_pl083">Pl. LXXXIII</a> (W. XVII). The text on this sheet refers to Cyprus (see
 Topographical Notes No. 1103), but seems to have no direct
 connection with the sketches inserted between.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl088.htm#img_pl088">Pl. LXXXVIII</a>, Nos. 6 and 7 (MS. B, 12a; see No. 751). A section of a
 circular pavilion with the plan of a similar building by the side of
 it. These two drawings have a special historical interest because
 the text written below mentions the Duke and Duchess of Milan.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i>The sketch of a villa on a terrace at the end of a garden occurs in
 C. A. 150; and in C. A. 77b; 225b is another sketch of a villa
 somewhat resembling the</i> Belvedere <i>of Pope Innocent VIII, at Rome.
 In C. A. 62b; 193b there is a Loggia.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl082.htm#img_pl082">Pl. LXXXII</a>, No. 4 (C. A. 387a; 1198a) is a tower-shaped</i> Loggia
 <i>above a fountain. The machinery is very ingeniously screened from
 view.</i>
