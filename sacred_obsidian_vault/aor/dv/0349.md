 <h3 align="CENTER">349.</h3>
 <p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 186</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p>
 From the top of the shoulder to the point of the elbow is as far as
 from that point to the joints of the four fingers with the palm of
 the hand, and each is 2 faces.
 </p><p>
 [5]<i>a e</i> is equal to the palm of the hand, <i>r f</i> and <i>o g</i> are equal
 to half a head and each goes 4 times into <i>a b</i> and <i>b c</i>. From <i>c</i>
 to <i>m</i> is 1/2 a head; <i>m n</i> is 1/3 of a head and goes 6 times into
 <i>c b</i> and into <i>b a</i>; <i>a b</i> loses 1/7 of its length when the arm is
 extended; <i>c b</i> never alters; <i>o</i> will always be the middle point
 between <i>a</i> and <i>s</i>.
 </p><p>
 <i>y l</i> is the fleshy part of the arm and measures one head; and when
 the arm is bent this shrinks 2/5 of its length; <i>o a</i> in bending
 loses 1/6 and so does <i>o r</i>.
 </p><p>
 <i>a b</i> is 1/7 of <i>r c</i>. <i>f s</i> will be 1/8 of <i>r c</i>, and each of those
 2 measurements is the largest of the arm; <i>k h</i> is the thinnest part
 between the shoulder and the elbow and it is 1/8 of the whole arm <i>r
 c</i>; <i>o p</i> is 1/5 of <i>r l</i>; <i>c z</i> goes 13 times into <i>r c</i>.
 </p><p>
