 <hr>
 
 <p>
 <span class="c_e">
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../cla/plato/timaeus.htm">Timaeus</a></span><br>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../cla/plato/critias.htm">Critias</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Plato</span> [<span class="c_d">360 B.C</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The two dialogs of Plato which contain the primary ancient account of Atlantis.</span><br>
 There is a short framing story about Solon in Egypt in Timaeus,
 and Critias, which contains the description of Atlantis,
 breaks off mid-narrative.
 Did Plato mean the tale literally or as an allegory?
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <hr align="CENTER" width="50%">
 <p>
 These books, which were written at the end of the
 19th century, are the core texts of the modern Atlantis mythos.
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="vril/index.htm">Vril, the Power of the Coming Race</a></span><br>
 by Sir <span class="c_a">Edward Bulwer-Lytton</span> [<span class="c_d">1871</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The Victorian science fiction novel from which introduced the concept of 'Vril.'</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="olb/index.htm">The Oera Linda Book</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Wiliam R. Sandbach</span> [<span class="c_d">1876</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A purported chronicle of the descendants of the lost Atlantis--of the North Sea.</span><br>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a name="ataw"></a>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ataw/index.htm">Atlantis, the Antediluvian World</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Ignatius Donnelly</span> [<span class="c_d">1882</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The complete text of the 19th Century bestseller that started the modern Atlantis craze.</span><br>
 Required reading for anyone interested in Atlantis, in spite of 
 much dated and incorrect information.
 This etext was scanned at sacred-texts and appears here
 in its entirety for the first time on the Internet.
 This text is also available through the <a href="https://digital.library.upenn.edu/webbin/gutbook/lookup?num=4032">Project Gutenberg</a> (thanks to Norman Wolcott, for working on the text version of this file).
 The illustrations, proofing and formatting of this etext
 were revised as of July 2003.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="rag/index.htm">Ragnarok: The Age of Fire and Gravel</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Ignatius Donnelly</span> [<span class="c_d">1883</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A sensational description of how a cometary impact could have
 destroyed an unknown prehistoric civilization.</span><br>
 Donnelly ties his comet impact into the death of Atlantis, and
 the lost origins of our own civilization.
 A perennially rediscovered hypothesis.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="dzyan/dzyan.htm">The Book of Dzyan</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">H.P. Blatavsky</span> [<span class="c_d">1888</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Blatavskys' channeled sacred text from Lemuria.</span><br>
 This excerpt from Helena Blatavskys'
 <a href="../the/sd/index.htm">"The Secret Doctrine"</a> is
 supposed to be an actual book from Lemuria summarizing
 occult knowledge about the evolution of the universe.
 In reality it was 'channeled'.  The Theosophists greatly elaborated the
 Atlantis story, adding numerous additional lost continents such as
 Hyperborea, Lemuria, Daitya, Ruta, Poseidonis; and peopled them with
 an entire succession of pre-human species.  (See the
 <a href="#soa">Scott-Elliot</a> text, below).
 </span>
 </p><p>
 
 
 </p><p>
 <a name="soa"></a>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="soa/index.htm">The Story of Atlantis</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W. Scott-Elliot</span> [<span class="c_d">1896</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">An imaginative Theosophic history of the Earth, the Theosophic concept of human evolution and everyday life in old Atlantis.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="tlc/index.htm">The Lost Continent</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Cutcliffe Hyne</span> [<span class="c_d">1900</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The decline and fall of decadent Atlantis; a cruel queen, and ancient magic: a long-forgotten swashbuckling adventure.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="tll/index.htm">The Lost Lemuria</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W. Scott-Elliot</span> [<span class="c_d">1904</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A short essay by Scott-Elliot on the lost continent which preceeded Atlantis in Theosophic beliefs: Lemuria.</span>
 </span>
 
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="dtp/index.htm">A Dweller on Two Planets</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Frederick S. Oliver</span> [<span class="c_d">1905</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A very influential speculative novel of Atlantis and points beyond, purportedly composed via automatic writing by a teenager in the shadow of Mount Shasta.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hif/index.htm">How I Found the Lost Atlantis, The Source of All Civilization</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Paul Schliemann</span> [<span class="c_d">1912</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A famous and often cited Atlantis hoax, republished at sacred-texts for the first time in over ninety years.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <img src="../new2.gif">
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="toa/index.htm">The Treasure of Atlantis</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">J. Allan Dunn</span> [<span class="c_d">1916</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A pulp-fiction era Atlantis in South America yarn.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ant/index.htm">Altantida (L'Antlantide)</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Pierre Benoit</span>, tr. by <span class="c_a">Mary C. Tongue</span> and <span class="c_a">Mary Ross</span> [<span class="c_d">1920</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A harrowing trip through the Sahara to ... Atlantis, and the strange allure of its ultimate queen.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ssm/index.htm">The Sacred Symbols of Mu</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">James Churchward</span> [<span class="c_d">1933</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Mu, the other lost continent, and its sacred symbology.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="smoa/index.htm">Selestor's Men of Atlantis</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Clara Iza von Ravn</span> [<span class="c_d">1937</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The history of Atlantis, told as a channeled vision of an ancient sage.</span>
 </span>
 </p>

 by <span class="c_a">Richard S. Shaver</span> [<span class="c_d">1948</span>]<br>
