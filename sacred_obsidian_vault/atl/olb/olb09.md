 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Oera Linda Book</i>, by Wiliam R. Sandbach, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Here Follow the Laws which were thus Established.</span></h3>  
 <p>1. Every Frisian must resist the assailants with such weapons as he can procure, invent, and use.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="green">p. 34</font></a> <a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="green">p. 35</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. When a boy is twelve years old he must devote one day in seven to learning how to use his weapons.</p>  
 <p>3. As soon as he is perfect in the use of them they are to be given to him, and he is to be admitted as a warrior.</p>  
 <p>4. After serving as a warrior three years, he may become a citizen, and may have a vote in the election of the headman.</p>  
 <p>5. When he has been seven years a voter he then may have a vote for the chief or king, and may be himself elected.</p>  
 <p>6. Every year he must be re-elected.</p>  
 <p>7. Except the king, all other officials are re-eligible who act according to Frya's laws.</p>  
 <p>8. No king may be in office more than three years, in order that the office may not be permanent.</p>  
 <p>9. After an interval of seven years he may be elected again.</p>  
 <p>10. If the king is killed by the enemy, his nearest relative may be a candidate to succeed. him.</p>  
 <p>11. If he dies a natural death, or if his period of service has expired, he shall not be succeeded by any blood relation nearer than the fourth degree.</p>  
 <p>12. Those who fight with arms are not men of counsel, therefore no king must bear arms. His wisdom must be his weapon, and the love of his warriors his shield.</p>
