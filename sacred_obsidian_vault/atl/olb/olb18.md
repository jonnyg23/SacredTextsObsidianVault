 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Oera Linda Book</i>, by Wiliam R. Sandbach, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">These Rules are made for Angry People.</span></h3>  
 <p>1. If a man in a passion or out of illwill breaks another's limb or puts out an eye or a tooth, he must pay whatever the injured man demands. If he cannot pay, he must suffer the same injury as he has done to the other. If he refuses this, he must appeal to the Burgtmaagd in order to be sent to work in the iron or tin mines until he has expiated his crime under the general law.</p>  
 <p>2. If a man is so wicked as to kill a Frisian, he must forfeit his own life; but if the Burgtmaagd can send him to the tin mines for his life before he is taken, she may do so.</p>  
 <p>3. If the prisoner can prove by proper witnesses that</p>  
 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="green">p. 62</font></a> <a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="green">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p>the death was accidental, he may go free; but if it happens a second time, he must go to the tin mines, in order to avoid any unseemly hatred or vengeance.</p>
