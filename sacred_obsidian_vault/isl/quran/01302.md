 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XIII.: Ra’d, or Thunder.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 2</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  All<u>a</u>hu yaAAlamu m<u>a</u> ta<u>h</u>milu kullu onth<u>a</u> wam<u>a</u> taghee<u>d</u>u al-ar<u>ha</u>mu wam<u>a</u> tazd<u>a</u>du wakullu shay-in AAindahu bimiqd<u>a</u>r<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  God doth know what<br> Every female (womb) doth bear,<br> By how much the wombs<br> Fall short (of their time<br> Or number) or do exceed.<br> Every single thing is before<br> His sight, in (due) proportion.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.   AA<u>a</u>limu alghaybi wa<b>al</b>shshah<u>a</u>dati alkabeeru almutaAA<u>a</u>l<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  He knoweth the Unseen<br> And that which is open:<br> He is the Great,<br> The most High.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Saw<u>a</u>on minkum man asarra alqawla waman jahara bihi waman huwa mustakhfin bi<b>a</b>llayli was<u>a</u>ribun bi<b>al</b>nnah<u>a</u>r<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  It is the same (to Him)<br> Whether any of you<br> Conceal his speech or<br> Declare it openly;<br> Whether he lie hid by night<br> Or walk forth freely by day.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Lahu muAAaqqib<u>a</u>tun min bayni yadayhi wamin khalfihi ya<u>h</u>fa<i><u>th</u></i>oonahu min amri All<u>a</u>hi inna All<u>a</u>ha l<u>a</u> yughayyiru m<u>a</u> biqawmin <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> yughayyiroo m<u>a</u> bi-anfusihim wa-i<u>tha</u> ar<u>a</u>da All<u>a</u>hu biqawmin soo-an fal<u>a</u> maradda lahu wam<u>a</u> lahum min doonihi min w<u>a</u>l<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  For each (such person)<br> There are (angels) in succession,<br> Before and behind him:<br> They guard him by command<br>Of God. Verily never<br> Will God change the condition<br> Of a people until they<br> Change it themselves<br> (With their own souls).<br> But when (once) God willeth<br> A people's punishment,<br> There can be no<br> Turning it back, nor<br> Will they find, besides Him,<br> Any to protect</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Huwa alla<u>th</u>ee yureekumu albarqa khawfan wa<u>t</u>amaAAan wayunshi-o a<b>l</b>ssa<u>ha</u>ba a<b>l</b>ththiq<u>a</u>l<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  It is He Who doth show you<br> The lightning, by way<br> Both of fear and of hope:<br> It is He Who doth raise up<br> The clouds, heavy<br> With (fertilising) rain!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Wayusabbi<u>h</u>u a<b>l</b>rraAAdu bi<u>h</u>amdihi wa<b>a</b>lmal<u>a</u>-ikatu min kheefatihi wayursilu a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>aw<u>a</u>AAiqa fayu<u>s</u>eebu bih<u>a</u> man yash<u>a</u>o wahum yuj<u>a</u>diloona fee All<u>a</u>hi wahuwa shadeedu almi<u>ha</u>l<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  Nay, thunder repeateth His praises,<br> And so do the angels, with awe:<br> He flingeth the loud-voiced<br> Thunder-bolts, and therewith<br> He striketh whomsoever He will…<br> Yet these (are the men)<br> Who (dare to) dispute<br> About God, with the strength<br> Of His power (supreme)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Lahu daAAwatu al<u>h</u>aqqi wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena yadAAoona min doonihi l<u>a</u> yastajeeboona lahum bishay-in ill<u>a</u> kab<u>a</u>si<u>t</u>i kaffayhi il<u>a</u> alm<u>a</u>-i liyablugha f<u>a</u>hu wam<u>a</u> huwa bib<u>a</u>lighihi wam<u>a</u> duAA<u>a</u>o alk<u>a</u>fireena ill<u>a</u> fee <u>d</u>al<u>a</u>l<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  For Him (alone) is prayer<br> In Truth: any others that they<br> Call upon besides Him hear them<br> No more than if they were<br> To stretch forth their hands<br> For water to reach their mouths<br> But it reaches them not:<br> For the prayer of those<br> Without Faith is nothing<br> But (futile) wandering (in the mind).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Walill<u>a</u>hi yasjudu man fee a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>i <u>t</u>awAAan wakarhan wa<i><u>th</u></i>il<u>a</u>luhum bi<b>a</b>lghuduwwi wa<b>a</b>l-<u>asa</u>l<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  Whatever beings there are<br> In the heavens and the earth<br> Do prostrate themselves to God<br> (Acknowledging subjection),—with good-will<br> Or in spite of themselves:<br> So do their shadows<br> In the mornings and evenings.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Qul man rabbu a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>i quli All<u>a</u>hu qul afa<b>i</b>ttakha<u>th</u>tum min doonihi awliy<u>a</u>a l<u>a</u> yamlikoona li-anfusihim nafAAan wal<u>a</u> <u>d</u>arran qul hal yastawee al-aAAm<u>a</u> wa<b>a</b>lba<u>s</u>eeru am hal tastawee a<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i>ulum<u>a</u>tu wa<b>al</b>nnooru am jaAAaloo lill<u>a</u>hi shurak<u>a</u>a khalaqoo kakhalqihi fatash<u>a</u>baha alkhalqu AAalayhim quli All<u>a</u>hu kh<u>a</u>liqu kulli shay-in wahuwa alw<u>ah</u>idu alqahh<u>a</u>r<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  Say: "Who is the Lord and Sustainer<br> Of the heavens and the earth?"<br> Say: "(It is) God."<br> Say: "Do ye then take<br> (For worship) protectors other<br> Than Him, such as have<br> No power either for good<br> Or for harm to themselves?"<br> Say: "Are the blind equal<br> With those who see?<br> Or the depths of darkness<br> Equal with Light?"<br> Or do they assign to God<br> Partners who have created<br> (Anything) as He has created,<br> So that the creation seemed<br> To them similar?<br> Say: "God is the Creator<br> Of all things: He is<br> The One, the Supreme and<br> Irresistible."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Anzala mina a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i m<u>a</u>an fas<u>a</u>lat awdiyatun biqadarih<u>a</u> fa<b>i</b><u>h</u>tamala a<b>l</b>ssaylu zabadan r<u>a</u>biyan wamimm<u>a</u> yooqidoona AAalayhi fee a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri ibtigh<u>a</u>a <u>h</u>ilyatin aw mat<u>a</u>AAin zabadun mithluhu ka<u>tha</u>lika ya<u>d</u>ribu All<u>a</u>hu al<u>h</u>aqqa wa<b>a</b>lb<u>at</u>ila faamm<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>zzabadu faya<u>th</u>habu juf<u>a</u>an waamm<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> yanfaAAu a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>sa fayamkuthu fee al-ar<u>d</u>i ka<u>tha</u>lika ya<u>d</u>ribu All<u>a</u>hu al-amth<u>a</u>l<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  He sends down water<br> From the sides, and the channels<br> Flow, each according to its measure:<br> But the torrent bears away<br> The foam that mounts up<br> To the surface. Even so,<br>Form that (ore) which they heat<br> In the fire, to make ornaments<br> Or utensils therewith,<br> There is a scum likewise.<br> Thus doth God (by parables)<br> Show forth Truth and Vanity.<br> For the scum disappears<br> Like froth cast out;<br> While that which is for the good<br> Of mankind remains<br> On the earth. Thus doth God<br> Set forth parables.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Lilla<u>th</u>eena istaj<u>a</u>boo lirabbihimu al<u>h</u>usn<u>a</u> wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena lam yastajeeboo lahu law anna lahum m<u>a</u> fee al-ar<u>d</u>i jameeAAan wamithlahu maAAahu la<b>i</b>ftadaw bihi ol<u>a</u>-ika lahum soo-o al<u>h</u>is<u>a</u>bi wama/w<u>a</u>hum jahannamu wabi/sa almih<u>a</u>d<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  For those who respond<br> To their Lord, are (all)<br> Good things. But those<br> Who respond not to Him,<br> Even if they had all<br> That is in the heavens<br> And on earth, and as much more,<br> (In vain) would they offer it<br> For ransom. For them<br> Will the reckoning be terrible:<br> Their abode will be Hell,——<br> What a bed of misery!</p> <div class="filenav">
