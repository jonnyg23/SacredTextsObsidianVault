 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XVI.: Naḥl or The Bee.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 3</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Il<u>a</u>hukum il<u>a</u>hun w<u>ah</u>idun fa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena l<u>a</u> yu/minoona bi<b>a</b>l-<u>a</u>khirati quloobuhum munkiratun wahum mustakbiroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  Your God is One God:<br> As to those who believe not<br> In the Hereafter, their hearts<br> Refuse to know, and they<br> Are arrogant.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  L<u>a</u> jarama anna All<u>a</u>ha yaAAlamu m<u>a</u> yusirroona wam<u>a</u> yuAAlinoona innahu l<u>a</u> yu<u>h</u>ibbu almustakbireen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  Undoubtedly God doth know<br> What they conceal,<br> And what they reveal:<br> Verily He loveth not the arrogant.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  Wa-i<u>tha</u> qeela lahum m<u>atha</u> anzala rabbukum q<u>a</u>loo as<u>at</u>eeru al-awwaleen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  When it is said to them,<br> "What is it that your Lord<br>Has revealed?" they say,<br> "Tales of the ancients!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  Liya<u>h</u>miloo awz<u>a</u>rahum k<u>a</u>milatan yawma alqiy<u>a</u>mati wamin awz<u>a</u>ri alla<u>th</u>eena yu<u>d</u>illoonahum bighayri AAilmin al<u>a</u> s<u>a</u>a m<u>a</u> yaziroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  Let them bear, on the Day<br> Of Judgment, their own burdens<br> In full, and also (something)<br> Of the burdens of those<br> Without knowledge, whom they<br> Misled. Alas, how grievous<br> The burdens they will bear!</p> <div class="filenav">
