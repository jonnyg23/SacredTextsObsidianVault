 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXXIX.: Zumar, or the Crowds.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 3</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Afaman shara<u>h</u>a All<u>a</u>hu <u>s</u>adrahu lil-isl<u>a</u>mi fahuwa AAal<u>a</u> noorin min rabbihi fawaylun lilq<u>a</u>siyati quloobuhum min <u>th</u>ikri All<u>a</u>hi ol<u>a</u>-ika fee <u>d</u>al<u>a</u>lin mubeen<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  Is one whose heart<br> God has opened to Islam,<br> So that he has received<br> Enlightenment from God,<br> (No better than one hard-hearted)?<br> Woe to those whose hearts<br> Are hardened against celebrating<br> The praises of God! They<br> Are manifestly wandering<br> (In error)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  All<u>a</u>hu nazzala a<u>h</u>sana al<u>h</u>adeethi kit<u>a</u>ban mutash<u>a</u>bihan math<u>a</u>niya taqshaAAirru minhu juloodu alla<u>th</u>eena yakhshawna rabbahum thumma taleenu julooduhum waquloobuhum il<u>a</u> <u>th</u>ikri All<u>a</u>hi <u>tha</u>lika hud<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hi yahdee bihi man yash<u>a</u>o waman yu<u>d</u>lili All<u>a</u>hu fam<u>a</u> lahu min h<u>a</u>d<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  God has revealed<br> (From time to time)<br> The most beautiful Message<br> In the form of a Book,<br> Consistent with itself,<br> (Yet) repeating (its teaching<br>In various aspects):<br> The skins of those who<br> Fear their Lord tremble<br> Thereat; then their skins<br> And their hearts do soften<br> To the celebration of<br> God's praises. Such is<br> The guidance of God:<br> He guides therewith<br> Whom He pleases, but such<br> As God leaves to stray,<br> Can have none to guide.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  Afaman yattaqee biwajhihi soo-a alAAa<u>tha</u>bi yawma alqiy<u>a</u>mati waqeela li<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeena <u>th</u>ooqoo m<u>a</u> kuntum taksiboon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  Is, then, one who<br> Has to fear the brunt<br> Of the Penalty on the Day<br> Of Judgment (and receive it)<br> On his face, (like one<br> Guarded therefrom)? It will<br>Be said to the wrong-doers:<br> "Taste ye (the fruits<br> Of) what ye earned!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  Ka<u>thth</u>aba alla<u>th</u>eena min qablihim faat<u>a</u>humu alAAa<u>tha</u>bu min <u>h</u>aythu l<u>a</u> yashAAuroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  Those before them (also)<br> Rejected (revelation), and so<br> The Punishment came to them<br> From directions they did not<br> Perceive.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_026.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>26.  Faa<u>tha</u>qahumu All<u>a</u>hu alkhizya fee al<u>h</u>ay<u>a</u>ti a<b>l</b>dduny<u>a</u> walaAAa<u>tha</u>bu al-<u>a</u>khirati akbaru law k<u>a</u>noo yaAAlamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>26.  So God gave them<br> A taste of humiliation<br> In the present life,<br> But greater is the Punishment<br> Of the Hereafter,<br> If they only knew!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_027.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>27.  Walaqad <u>d</u>arabn<u>a</u> li<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>si fee h<u>atha</u> alqur-<u>a</u>ni min kulli mathalin laAAallahum yata<u>th</u>akkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>27.  We have put forth<br> For men, in this Qur-ān<br> Every kind of Parable,<br> In order that they<br> May receive admonition.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_028.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>28.  Qur-<u>a</u>nan AAarabiyyan ghayra <u>th</u>ee AAiwajin laAAallahum yattaqoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>28.  (It is) a Qur-ān<br> In Arabic, without any<br>Crookedness (therein):<br> In order that they<br> May guard against Evil.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_029.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>29.  <u>D</u>araba All<u>a</u>hu mathalan rajulan feehi shurak<u>a</u>o mutash<u>a</u>kisoona warajulan salaman lirajulin hal yastawiy<u>a</u>ni mathalan al<u>h</u>amdu lill<u>a</u>hi bal aktharuhum l<u>a</u> yaAAlamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>29.  God puts forth a Parable—<br> A man belonging to many<br> Partners at variance with each other,<br> And a man belonging entirely<br> To one master: are those two<br> Equal in comparison?<br> Praise be to God!<br> But most of them<br> Have no knowledge.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_030.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>30.  Innaka mayyitun wa-innahum mayyitoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>30.  Truly thou wilt die<br> (One day), and truly they<br> (Too) will die (one day).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_031.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>31.  Thumma innakum yawma alqiy<u>a</u>mati AAinda rabbikum takhta<u>s</u>imoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>31.  In the End will ye<br> (All), on the Day<br> Of Judgment, settle your disputes<br> In the presence of your Lord.</p> <div class="filenav">
