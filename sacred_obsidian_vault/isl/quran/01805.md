 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XVIII.: Kahf, or the Cave.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 5</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_032.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>32.  Wa<b>i</b><u>d</u>rib lahum mathalan rajulayni jaAAaln<u>a</u> li-a<u>h</u>adihim<u>a</u> jannatayni min aAAn<u>a</u>bin wa<u>h</u>afafn<u>a</u>hum<u>a</u> binakhlin wajaAAaln<u>a</u> baynahum<u>a</u> zarAA<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>32.  Set forth to them<br> The parable of two men:<br> For one of them We provided<br> Two gardens of grape-vines<br> And surrounded them<br> With date palms;<br> In between the two<br> We placed corn-fields.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_033.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>33.  Kilt<u>a</u> aljannatayni <u>a</u>tat okulah<u>a</u> walam ta<i><u>th</u></i>lim minhu shay-an wafajjarn<u>a</u> khil<u>a</u>lahum<u>a</u> nahar<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>33.  Each of those gardens<br> Brought forth its produce,<br> And failed not in the least<br> Therein: in the midst<br> Of them We caused<br> A river to flow.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_034.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>34.  Wak<u>a</u>na lahu thamarun faq<u>a</u>la li<u>sah</u>ibihi wahuwa yu<u>ha</u>wiruhu an<u>a</u> aktharu minka m<u>a</u>lan waaAAazzu nafar<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>34.  (Abundant) was the produce<br> This man had: he said<br> To his companion, in the course<br> Of a mutual argument:<br> "More wealth have I<br> Than you, and more honour<br> And power in (my following<br> Of) men."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_035.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>35.  Wadakhala jannatahu wahuwa <i><u>th</u></i><u>a</u>limun linafsihi q<u>a</u>la m<u>a</u> a<i><u>th</u></i>unnu an tabeeda h<u>ath</u>ihi abad<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>35.  He went into his garden<br> In a state (of mind)<br> Unjust to his soul:<br> He said, "I deem not<br> That this will ever perish,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_036.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>36.  Wam<u>a</u> a<i><u>th</u></i>unnu a<b>l</b>ss<u>a</u>AAata q<u>a</u>-imatan wala-in rudidtu il<u>a</u> rabbee laajidanna khayran minh<u>a</u> munqalab<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>36.  "Nor do I deem<br> That the Hour (of Judgment)<br> Will (ever) come:<br> Even if I am brought back<br> To my Lord, I shall<br> Surely find (there)<br> Something better in exchange."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_037.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>37.  Q<u>a</u>la lahu <u>sah</u>ibuhu wahuwa yu<u>ha</u>wiruhu akafarta bi<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee khalaqaka min tur<u>a</u>bin thumma min nu<u>t</u>fatin thumma saww<u>a</u>ka rajul<u>a</u><b>n</b>    </p>
 <p>37.  His companion said to him,<br> In the course of the argument<br> With him: "Dost thou deny<br> Him Who created thee<br> Out of dust, then out of<br> A sperm-drop, then fashioned<br> Thee into a man?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_038.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>38.  L<u>a</u>kinna huwa All<u>a</u>hu rabbee wal<u>a</u> oshriku birabbee a<u>h</u>ad<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>38.  "But (I think) for my part<br> That He is God,<br> My Lord, and none shall I<br> Associate with my Lord.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_039.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>39.  Walawl<u>a</u> i<u>th</u> dakhalta jannataka qulta m<u>a</u> sh<u>a</u>a All<u>a</u>hu l<u>a</u> quwwata ill<u>a</u> bi<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hi in tarani an<u>a</u> aqalla minka m<u>a</u>lan wawalad<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>39.  "Why didst thou not,<br> As thou wentest into<br> Thy garden, say, "God's Will<br> (Be done)! There is no power<br> But with God!" If thou<br>Dost see me less than<br> Thee in wealth and sons,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_040.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>40.  FaAAas<u>a</u> rabbee an yu/tiyani khayran min jannatika wayursila AAalayh<u>a</u> <u>h</u>usb<u>a</u>nan mina a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i fatu<u>s</u>bi<u>h</u>a <u>s</u>aAAeedan zalaq<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>40.  "It may be that my Lord<br> Will give me something<br> Better than thy garden,<br> And that He will send<br> On thy garden thunderbolts<br> (By way of reckoning)<br> From heaven, making it<br> (But) slippery sand!—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_041.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>41.  Aw yu<u>s</u>bi<u>h</u>a m<u>a</u>oh<u>a</u> ghawran falan tasta<u>t</u>eeAAa lahu <u>t</u>alab<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>41.  "Or the water of the garden<br> Will run off underground<br> So that thou wilt never<br> Be able to find it."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_042.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>42.  Wao<u>h</u>ee<u>t</u>a bithamarihi faa<u>s</u>ba<u>h</u>a yuqallibu kaffayhi AAal<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> anfaqa feeh<u>a</u> wahiya kh<u>a</u>wiyatun AAal<u>a</u> AAurooshih<u>a</u> wayaqoolu y<u>a</u> laytanee lam oshrik birabbee a<u>h</u>ad<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>42.  So his fruits (and enjoyment)<br> Were encompassed (with ruin),<br> And he remained twisting<br> And turning his hands<br> Over what he had spent<br> On his property, which had<br> (Now) tumbled to pieces<br> To its very foundations,<br> And he could only say,<br> "Woe is me! Would I had<br> Never ascribed partners<br> To my Lord and Cherisher!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_043.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>43.  Walam takun lahu fi-atun yan<u>s</u>uroonahu min dooni All<u>a</u>hi wam<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>na munta<u>s</u>ir<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>43.  Nor had he numbers<br> To help him against God,<br> Nor was he able<br> To deliver himself.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/018/018_044.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>44.  Hun<u>a</u>lika alwal<u>a</u>yatu lill<u>a</u>hi al<u>h</u>aqqi huwa khayrun thaw<u>a</u>ban wakhayrun AAuqb<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>44.  There, the (only) protection comes<br> From God, the True One.<br> He is the Best to reward,<br> And the Best to give success.</p> <div class="filenav">
