 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra IV.: Nisāa, or The Women.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 3</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Wa<b>a</b>ll<u>a</u>tee ya/teena alf<u>ah</u>ishata min nis<u>a</u>-ikum fa<b>i</b>stashhidoo AAalayhinna arbaAAatan minkum fa-in shahidoo faamsikoohunna fee albuyooti <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> yatawaff<u>a</u>hunna almawtu aw yajAAala All<u>a</u>hu lahunna sabeel<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  If any of your women<br> Are guilty of lewdness,<br> Take the evidence of four<br> (Reliable) witnesses from amongst you<br> Against them; and if they testify,<br> Confine them to houses until<br> Death do claim them,<br>Or God ordain for them<br> Some (other) way.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>tha</u>ni ya/tiy<u>a</u>nih<u>a</u> minkum fa<u>ath</u>oohum<u>a</u> fa-in t<u>a</u>b<u>a</u> waa<u>s</u>la<u>ha</u> faaAAri<u>d</u>oo AAanhum<u>a</u> inna All<u>a</u>ha k<u>a</u>na taww<u>a</u>ban ra<u>h</u>eem<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  If two men among you<br> Are guilty of lewdness,<br> Punish them both.<br> If they repent and amend,<br> Leave them alone; for God<br> Is Oft-returning, Most Merciful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Innam<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>ttawbatu AAal<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hi lilla<u>th</u>eena yaAAmaloona a<b>l</b>ssoo-a bijah<u>a</u>latin thumma yatooboona min qareebin faol<u>a</u>-ika yatoobu All<u>a</u>hu AAalayhim wak<u>a</u>na All<u>a</u>hu AAaleeman <u>h</u>akeem<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  God accepts the repentance<br> Of those who do evil<br> In ignorance and repent<br> Soon afterwards; to them<br> Will God turn in mercy:<br> For God is full of knowledge<br> And wisdom.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Walaysati a<b>l</b>ttawbatu lilla<u>th</u>eena yaAAmaloona a<b>l</b>ssayyi-<u>a</u>ti <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> i<u>tha</u> <u>h</u>a<u>d</u>ara a<u>h</u>adahumu almawtu q<u>a</u>la innee tubtu al-<u>a</u>na wal<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena yamootoona wahum kuff<u>a</u>run ol<u>a</u>-ika aAAtadn<u>a</u> lahum AAa<u>tha</u>ban aleem<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  Of no effect is the repentance<br> Of those who continue<br> To do evil, until Death<br> Faces one of them, and he says,<br> "Now have I repented indeed;"<br> Nor of those who die<br> Rejecting Faith: for them<br> Have we prepared<br> A punishment most grievous.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Y<u>a</u> ayyuh<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo l<u>a</u> ya<u>h</u>illu lakum an tarithoo a<b>l</b>nnis<u>a</u>a karhan wal<u>a</u> taAA<u>d</u>uloohunna lita<u>th</u>haboo bibaAA<u>d</u>i m<u>a</u> <u>a</u>taytumoohunna ill<u>a</u> an ya/teena bif<u>ah</u>ishatin mubayyinatin waAA<u>a</u>shiroohunna bi<b>a</b>lmaAAroofi fa-in karihtumoohunna faAAas<u>a</u> an takrahoo shay-an wayajAAala All<u>a</u>hu feehi khayran katheer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  O ye who believe!<br> Ye are forbidden to inherit<br> Women against their will.<br> Nor should ye treat them<br>With harshness, that ye may<br> Take away part of the dower<br> Ye have given them,—except<br> Where they have been guilty<br> Of open lewdness;<br> On the contrary live with them<br> On a footing of kindness and equity.<br> If ye take a dislike to them<br> It may be that ye dislike<br> A thing, and God brings about<br> Through it a great deal of good.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Wa-in aradtumu istibd<u>a</u>la zawjin mak<u>a</u>na zawjin wa<u>a</u>taytum i<u>h</u>d<u>a</u>hunna qin<u>ta</u>ran fal<u>a</u> ta/khu<u>th</u>oo minhu shay-an ata/khu<u>th</u>oonahu buht<u>a</u>nan wa-ithman mubeen<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  But if ye decide to take<br> One wife in place of another,<br> Even if ye had given the latter<br> A whole treasure for dower,<br> Take not the least bit of it back:<br> Would ye take it by slander<br> And a manifest wrong?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_021.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>21.  Wakayfa ta/khu<u>th</u>oonahu waqad af<u>da</u> baAA<u>d</u>ukum il<u>a</u> baAA<u>d</u>in waakha<u>th</u>na minkum meeth<u>a</u>qan ghalee<i><u>th</u></i><u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>21.  And how could ye take it<br> When ye have gone in<br> Unto each other, and they have<br> Taken from you a solemn covenant?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/004/004_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Wal<u>a</u> tanki<u>h</u>oo m<u>a</u> naka<u>h</u>a <u>a</u>b<u>a</u>okum mina a<b>l</b>nnis<u>a</u>-i ill<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> qad salafa innahu k<u>a</u>na f<u>ah</u>ishatan wamaqtan was<u>a</u>a sabeel<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  And marry not women<br> Whom your fathers married,—<br> Except what is past:<br> It was shameful and odious,—<br> An abominable custom indeed.</p> <div class="filenav">
