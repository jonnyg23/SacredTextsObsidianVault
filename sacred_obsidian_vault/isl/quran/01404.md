 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/014/014_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Waq<u>a</u>la a<b>l</b>shshay<u>ta</u>nu lamm<u>a</u> qu<u>d</u>iya al-amru inna All<u>a</u>ha waAAadakum waAAda al<u>h</u>aqqi wawaAAadtukum faakhlaftukum wam<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>na liya AAalaykum min sul<u>ta</u>nin ill<u>a</u> an daAAawtukum fa<b>i</b>stajabtum lee fal<u>a</u> taloomoonee waloomoo anfusakum m<u>a</u> an<u>a</u> bimu<u>s</u>rikhikum wam<u>a</u> antum bimu<u>s</u>rikhiyya innee kafartu bim<u>a</u> ashraktumooni min qablu inna a<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeena lahum AAa<u>tha</u>bun aleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  And Satan will say<br> When the matter is decided:<br> "It was God Who gave you<br> A promise of Truth: I too<br> Promised, but I failed<br> In my promise to you.<br>I had no authority over you<br> Except to call you, but ye<br> Listened to me: then<br> Reproach not me, but reproach<br> Your own souls. I cannot listen<br> To your cries, nor can ye<br> Listen to mine. I reject<br> Your former act in associating<br> Me with God.<br> For wrong-doers there must be<br> A grievous Penalty."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/014/014_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  Waodkhila alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo waAAamiloo a<b>l</b><u>ssa</u>li<u>ha</u>ti jann<u>a</u>tin tajree min ta<u>h</u>tih<u>a</u> al-anh<u>a</u>ru kh<u>a</u>lideena feeh<u>a</u> bi-i<u>th</u>ni rabbihim ta<u>h</u>iyyatuhum feeh<u>a</u> sal<u>a</u>m<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  But those who believe<br> And work righteousness<br> Will be admitted to Gardens<br> Beneath which rivers flow,—<br> To dwell therein for aye<br> With the leave of their Lord.<br> Their greeting therein<br> Will be: "Peace!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/014/014_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  Alam tara kayfa <u>d</u>araba All<u>a</u>hu mathalan kalimatan <u>t</u>ayyibatan kashajaratin <u>t</u>ayyibatin a<u>s</u>luh<u>a</u> th<u>a</u>bitun wafarAAuh<u>a</u> fee a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>/-<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  Seest thou not how<br> God sets forth a parable?—<br> A goodly Word<br> Like a goodly tree,<br> Whose root is firmly fixed,<br> And its branches (reach)<br> To the heavens,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/014/014_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  Tu/tee okulah<u>a</u> kulla <u>h</u>eenin bi-i<u>th</u>ni rabbih<u>a</u> waya<u>d</u>ribu All<u>a</u>hu al-amth<u>a</u>la li<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>si laAAallahum yata<u>th</u>akkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  It brings forth its fruit<br> At all times, by the leave<br>Of its Lord.<br> So God sets forth parables<br> For men, in order that<br> They may receive admonition.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/014/014_026.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>26.  Wamathalu kalimatin khabeethatin kashajaratin khabeethatin ijtuththat min fawqi al-ar<u>d</u>i m<u>a</u> lah<u>a</u> min qar<u>a</u>r<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>26.  And the parable<br> Of an evil Word<br> Is that of an evil tree:<br> It is torn up by the root<br> From the surface of the earth:<br> It has no stability.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/014/014_027.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>27.  Yuthabbitu All<u>a</u>hu alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo bi<b>a</b>lqawli a<b>l</b>thth<u>a</u>biti fee al<u>h</u>ay<u>a</u>ti a<b>l</b>dduny<u>a</u> wafee al-<u>a</u>khirati wayu<u>d</u>illu All<u>a</u>hu a<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeena wayafAAalu All<u>a</u>hu m<u>a</u> yash<u>a</u>/<b>o</b>   </p>
 <p>27.  God will establish in strength<br> Those who believe, with the Word<br> That stands firm, in this world<br> And in the Hereafter; but God<br> Will leave, to stray, those<br> Who do wrong: God doeth<br> What He willeth.</p> <div class="filenav">
