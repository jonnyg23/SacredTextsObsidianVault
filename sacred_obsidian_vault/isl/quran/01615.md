 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XVI.: Naḥl or The Bee.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 15</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_111.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>111.  Yawma ta/tee kullu nafsin tuj<u>a</u>dilu AAan nafsih<u>a</u> watuwaff<u>a</u> kullu nafsin m<u>a</u> AAamilat wahum l<u>a</u> yu<i><u>th</u></i>lamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>111.  One Day every soul<br> Will come up struggling<br> For itself, and every soul<br> Will be recompensed (fully)<br> For all its actions, and none<br> Will be unjustly dealt with.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_112.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>112.  Wa<u>d</u>araba All<u>a</u>hu mathalan qaryatan k<u>a</u>nat <u>a</u>minatan mu<u>t</u>ma-innatan ya/teeh<u>a</u> rizquh<u>a</u> raghadan min kulli mak<u>a</u>nin fakafarat bi-anAAumi All<u>a</u>hi faa<u>tha</u>qah<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hu lib<u>a</u>sa aljooAAi wa<b>a</b>lkhawfi bim<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>noo ya<u>s</u>naAAoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>112.  God sets forth a Parable:<br> A city enjoying security<br> And quiet, abundantly supplied<br> With sustenance from every place:<br> Yet was it ungrateful<br> For the favours of God:<br> So God made it taste<br> Of hunger and terror (in extremes)<br> (Closing in on it) like a garment<br> (From every side), because<br>Of the (evil) which<br> (Its people) wrought.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_113.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>113.  Walaqad j<u>a</u>ahum rasoolun minhum faka<u>thth</u>aboohu faakha<u>th</u>ahumu alAAa<u>tha</u>bu wahum <i><u>th</u></i><u>a</u>limoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>113.  And there came to them<br> An Apostle from among themselves,<br> But they falsely rejected him;<br> So the Wrath seized them<br> Even in the midst<br> Of their iniquities.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_114.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>114.  Fakuloo mimm<u>a</u> razaqakumu All<u>a</u>hu <u>h</u>al<u>a</u>lan <u>t</u>ayyiban wa<b>o</b>shkuroo niAAmata All<u>a</u>hi in kuntum iyy<u>a</u>hu taAAbudoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>114.  So eat of the sustenance<br> Which God has provided<br> For you, lawful and good;<br> And be grateful for the favours<br> Of God, if it is He<br> Whom ye serve.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_115.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>115.  Innam<u>a</u> <u>h</u>arrama AAalaykumu almaytata wa<b>al</b>ddama wala<u>h</u>ma alkhinzeeri wam<u>a</u> ohilla lighayri All<u>a</u>hi bihi famani i<u>dt</u>urra ghayra b<u>a</u>ghin wal<u>a</u> AA<u>a</u>din fa-inna All<u>a</u>ha ghafoorun ra<u>h</u>eem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>115.  He has only forbidden you<br> Dead meat, and blood,<br> And the flesh of swine,<br> And any (food) over which<br> The name of other than God<br> Has been invoked.<br> But if one is forced by necessity,<br> Without wilful disobedience,<br> Nor transgressing due limits,—<br> Then God is Oft-Forgiving,<br> Most Merciful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_116.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>116.  Wal<u>a</u> taqooloo lim<u>a</u> ta<u>s</u>ifu alsinatukumu alka<u>th</u>iba h<u>atha</u> <u>h</u>al<u>a</u>lun wah<u>atha</u> <u>h</u>ar<u>a</u>mun litaftaroo AAal<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hi alka<u>th</u>iba inna alla<u>th</u>eena yaftaroona AAal<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hi alka<u>th</u>iba l<u>a</u> yufli<u>h</u>oon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>116.  But say not—for any false thing<br> That your tongues may put forth,—<br> "This is lawful, and this<br> Is forbidden," so as to ascribe<br> False things to God. For those<br> Who ascribe false things<br> To God, will never prosper.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_117.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>117.  Mat<u>a</u>AAun qaleelun walahum AAa<u>tha</u>bun aleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>117.  (In such falsehood)<br> Is but a paltry profit;<br> But they will have<br> A most grievous Penalty.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_118.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>118.  WaAAal<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena h<u>a</u>doo <u>h</u>arramn<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> qa<u>s</u>a<u>s</u>n<u>a</u> AAalayka min qablu wam<u>a</u> <i><u>th</u></i>alamn<u>a</u>hum wal<u>a</u>kin k<u>a</u>noo anfusahum ya<i><u>th</u></i>limoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>118.  To the Jews We prohibited<br> Such things as We have<br> Mentioned to thee before:<br> We did them no wrong,<br> But they were used to<br> Doing wrong to themselves.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_119.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>119.  Thumma inna rabbaka lilla<u>th</u>eena AAamiloo a<b>l</b>ssoo-a bijah<u>a</u>latin thumma t<u>a</u>boo min baAAdi <u>tha</u>lika waa<u>s</u>la<u>h</u>oo inna rabbaka min baAAdih<u>a</u> laghafoorun ra<u>h</u>eem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>119.  But verily thy Lord,—<br> To those who do wrong<br> In ignorance, but who<br> Thereafter repent and make amends,—<br> Thy Lord, after all this,<br> Is Oft-Forgiving, Most<br> Merciful.</p> <div class="filenav">
