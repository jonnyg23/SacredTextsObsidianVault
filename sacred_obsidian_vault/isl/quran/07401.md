 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra LXXIV.: Mudda<u>th</u><u>th</u>ir, or One Wrapped Up.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  Y<u>a</u> ayyuh<u>a</u> almuddaththir<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>1.  O thou wrapped up<br> (In a mantle)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  Qum faan<u>th</u>ir    </p>
 <p>2.  Arise and deliver thy warning!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Warabbaka fakabbir   </p>
 <p>3.  And thy Lord<br> Do thou magnify!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  Wathiy<u>a</u>baka fa<u>t</u>ahhir   </p>
 <p>4.  And thy garments<br> Keep free from stain!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Wa<b>al</b>rrujza fa<b>o</b>hjur    </p>
 <p>5.  And all abomination shun!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Wal<u>a</u> tamnun tastakthir<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  Nor expect, in giving,<br> Any increase (for thyself)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Walirabbika fa<b>i</b><u>s</u>bir   </p>
 <p>7.  But, for thy Lord's (Cause),<br> Be patient and constant!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Fa-i<u>tha</u> nuqira fee a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>qoor<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  Finally, when the trumpet<br> Is sounded,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Fa<u>thal</u>ika yawma-i<u>th</u>in yawmun AAaseer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  That will be—that Day—<br> A Day of Distress,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  AAal<u>a</u> alk<u>a</u>fireena ghayru yaseer<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  Far from easy<br> For those without Faith.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  <u>Th</u>arnee waman khalaqtu wa<u>h</u>eed<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  Leave Me alone, (to deal)<br> With the (creature) whom<br> I created (bare and) alone!—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  WajaAAaltu lahu m<u>a</u>lan mamdood<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  To whom I granted<br> Resources in abundance,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Wabaneena shuhood<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  And sons to be<br> By his side!—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Wamahhadtu lahu tamheed<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  To whom I made<br> (Life) smooth and comfortable!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Thumma ya<u>t</u>maAAu an azeed<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  Yet is he greedy—<br> That I should add<br> (Yet more);—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Kall<u>a</u> innahu k<u>a</u>na li-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin<u>a</u> AAaneed<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  By no means!<br> For to Our Signs<br> He has been refractory!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Saorhiquhu <u>s</u>aAAood<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  Soon will I visit him<br> With a mount of calamities!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Innahu fakkara waqaddar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  For he thought<br> And he plotted;—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Faqutila kayfa qaddar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  And woe to him!<br> How he plotted!—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Thumma qutila kayfa qaddar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  Yea, woe to him:<br> How he plotted!—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_021.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>21.  Thumma na<i><u>th</u></i>ar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>21.  Then he looked round;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Thumma AAabasa wabasar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  Then he frowned<br> And he scowled;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  Thumma adbara wa<b>i</b>stakbar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  Then he turned back<br> And was haughty;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  Faq<u>a</u>la in h<u>atha</u> ill<u>a</u> si<u>h</u>run yu/thar<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  Then said he:<br> "This is nothing but magic,<br> Derived from of old;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  In h<u>atha</u> ill<u>a</u> qawlu albashar<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  "This is nothing but<br> The word of a mortal!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_026.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>26.  Sao<u>s</u>leehi saqar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>26.  Soon will I<br> Cast him into Hell-Fire!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_027.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>27.  Wam<u>a</u> adr<u>a</u>ka m<u>a</u> saqar<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>27.  And what will explain<br> To thee what Hell-Fire is?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_028.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>28.  L<u>a</u> tubqee wal<u>a</u> ta<u>th</u>ar<b>u</b>    </p>
 <p>28.  Naught doth it permit<br> To endure, and naught<br> Doth it leave alone!—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_029.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>29.  Laww<u>ah</u>atun lilbashar<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>29.  Darkening and changing<br> The colour of man!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_030.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>30.  AAalayh<u>a</u> tisAAata AAashar<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>30.  Over it are Nineteen.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/074/074_031.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>31.  Wam<u>a</u> jaAAaln<u>a</u> a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>ba a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri ill<u>a</u> mal<u>a</u>-ikatan wam<u>a</u> jaAAaln<u>a</u> AAiddatahum ill<u>a</u> fitnatan lilla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo liyastayqina alla<u>th</u>eena ootoo alkit<u>a</u>ba wayazd<u>a</u>da alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo eem<u>a</u>nan wal<u>a</u> yart<u>a</u>ba alla<u>th</u>eena ootoo alkit<u>a</u>ba wa<b>a</b>lmu/minoona waliyaqoola alla<u>th</u>eena fee quloobihim mara<u>d</u>un wa<b>a</b>lk<u>a</u>firoona m<u>atha</u> ar<u>a</u>da All<u>a</u>hu bih<u>atha</u> mathalan ka<u>tha</u>lika yu<u>d</u>illu All<u>a</u>hu man yash<u>a</u>o wayahdee man yash<u>a</u>o wam<u>a</u> yaAAlamu junooda rabbika ill<u>a</u> huwa wam<u>a</u> hiya ill<u>a</u> <u>th</u>ikr<u>a</u> lilbashar<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>31.  And We have set none<br> But angels as guardians<br> Of the Fire; and We<br> Have fixed their number<br>Only as a trial<br> For Unbelievers,—in order<br> That the People of the Book<br> May arrive at certainty,<br> And the Believers may increase<br> In Faith,—and that no doubts<br> May be left for the People<br> Of the Book and the Believers,<br> And that those in whose hearts<br> Is a disease and the Unbelievers<br> May say, "What symbol<br> Doth God intend by this?<br> Thus doth God leave to stray<br> Whom He pleaseth, and guide<br> Whom He pleaseth: and none<br> Can know the forces<br> Of thy Lord, except He.<br> And this is no other than<br> A warning to mankind.</p> <div class="filenav">
