 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXII.: Ḥajj, or The Pilgrimage.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 5</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_034.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>34.  Walikulli ommatin jaAAaln<u>a</u> mansakan liya<u>th</u>kuroo isma All<u>a</u>hi AAal<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> razaqahum min baheemati al-anAA<u>a</u>mi fa-il<u>a</u>hukum il<u>a</u>hun w<u>ah</u>idun falahu aslimoo wabashshiri almukhbiteen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>34.  To every people did We<br> Appoint rites (of sacrifice),<br> That they might celebrate<br> The name of God over<br>The sustenance He gave them<br> From animals (fit for food).<br> But your god is One God:<br> Submit then your wills to him<br> (In Islām): and give thou<br> The good news to those<br> Who humble themselves,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_035.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>35.  Alla<u>th</u>eena i<u>tha</u> <u>th</u>ukira All<u>a</u>hu wajilat quloobuhum wa<b>al</b><u>ssa</u>bireena AAal<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> a<u>sa</u>bahum wa<b>a</b>lmuqeemee a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>al<u>a</u>ti wamimm<u>a</u> razaqn<u>a</u>hum yunfiqoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>35.  To those whose hearts,<br> When God is mentioned,<br> Are filled with fear,<br> Who show patient perseverance<br> Over their afflictions, keep up<br> Regular prayer, and spend<br> (In charity) out of what<br> We have bestowed upon them.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_036.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>36.  Wa<b>a</b>lbudna jaAAaln<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> lakum min shaAA<u>a</u>-iri All<u>a</u>hi lakum feeh<u>a</u> khayrun fa<b>o</b><u>th</u>kuroo isma All<u>a</u>hi AAalayh<u>a</u> <u>s</u>aw<u>a</u>ffa fa-i<u>tha</u> wajabat junoobuh<u>a</u> fakuloo minh<u>a</u> waa<u>t</u>AAimoo alq<u>a</u>niAAa wa<b>a</b>lmuAAtarra ka<u>tha</u>lika sakhkharn<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> lakum laAAallakum tashkuroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>36.  The sacrificial camels<br> We have made for you<br> As among the symbols from<br> God: in them is (much)<br> Good for you: then pronounce<br> The name of God over them<br> As they line up (for sacrifice):<br> When they are down<br> On their sides (after slaughter),<br> Eat ye thereof, and feed<br> Such as (beg not but)<br> Live in contentment,<br> And such as beg<br> With due humility: thus have<br>We made animals subject<br> To you, that ye<br> May be grateful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_037.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>37.  Lan yan<u>a</u>la All<u>a</u>ha lu<u>h</u>oomuh<u>a</u> wal<u>a</u> dim<u>a</u>oh<u>a</u> wal<u>a</u>kin yan<u>a</u>luhu a<b>l</b>ttaqw<u>a</u> minkum ka<u>tha</u>lika sakhkharah<u>a</u> lakum litukabbiroo All<u>a</u>ha AAal<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> had<u>a</u>kum wabashshiri almu<u>h</u>sineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>37.  It is not their meat<br> Nor their blood, that reaches<br> God: it is your piety<br> That reaches Him: He<br> Has thus made them subject<br> To you, that ye may glorify<br> God for his guidance to you:<br> And proclaim the Good News<br> To all who do right.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_038.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>38.  Inna All<u>a</u>ha yud<u>a</u>fiAAu AAani alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo inna All<u>a</u>ha l<u>a</u> yu<u>h</u>ibbu kulla khaww<u>a</u>nin kafoor<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>38.  Verily God will defend<br> (From ill) those who believe:<br> Verily, God loveth not<br> Any that is a traitor<br> To faith, or shows ingratitude.</p> <div class="filenav">
