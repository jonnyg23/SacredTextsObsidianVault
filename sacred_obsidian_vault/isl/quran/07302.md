 <p>20.  Thy Lord doth know<br> That thou standest forth<br> (To prayer) nigh two-thirds<br> Of the night, or half<br>The night, or a third<br> Of the night, and so doth<br> A party of those with thee.<br> But God doth appoint Night<br> And Day in due measure.<br> He knoweth that ye are<br> Unable to keep count thereof.<br> So He hath turned to you<br> (In mercy): read ye,<br> Therefore, of the Qur-ān<br> As much as may be<br> Easy for you. He knoweth<br> That there may be (some)<br> Among you in ill-health;<br> Others travelling through the land,<br> Seeking of God's bounty;<br> Yet others fighting<br> In God's Cause. Read ye,<br> Therefore, as much of the Qur-ān<br> As may be easy (for you);<br> And establish regular Prayer<br> And give regular Charity;<br> And loan to God<br> A Beautiful Loan.<br>And whatever good<br> Ye send forth<br> For your souls,<br> Ye shall find it<br> In God's Presence,—<br> Yea, better and<br> Greater, in Reward.<br> And seek ye the Grace<br> Of God: for God is<br> Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.</p>  <div class="filenav">
