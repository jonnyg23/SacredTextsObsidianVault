 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXV.: Furqān, or The Criterion.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 4</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_035.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>35.  Walaqad <u>a</u>tayn<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u> alkit<u>a</u>ba wajaAAaln<u>a</u> maAAahu akh<u>a</u>hu h<u>a</u>roona wazeer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>35.  (Before this,) We sent Moses<br> The Book, and appointed<br> His brother Aaron with him<br> As Minister;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_036.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>36.  Faquln<u>a</u> i<u>th</u>hab<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> alqawmi alla<u>th</u>eena ka<u>thth</u>aboo bi-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin<u>a</u> fadammarn<u>a</u>hum tadmeer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>36.  And We commanded: "Go ye<br> Both, to the people who<br> Have rejected our Signs:"<br> And those (people) We destroyed<br> With utter destruction.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_037.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>37.  Waqawma noo<u>h</u>in lamm<u>a</u> ka<u>thth</u>aboo a<b>l</b>rrusula aghraqn<u>a</u>hum wajaAAaln<u>a</u>hum li<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>si <u>a</u>yatan waaAAtadn<u>a</u> li<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeena AAa<u>tha</u>ban aleem<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>37.  And the people of Noah,—<br> When they rejected the apostles,<br> We drowned them,<br> And We made them<br> As a Sign for mankind;<br> And We have prepared<br> For (all) wrong-doers<br> A grievous Penalty;—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_038.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>38.  WaAA<u>a</u>dan wathamooda waa<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>ba a<b>l</b>rrassi waquroonan bayna <u>tha</u>lika katheer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>38.  As also ’Ad and <u>Th</u>amūd,<br> And the Companions<br>Of the Rass, and many<br> A generation between them.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_039.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>39.  Wakullan <u>d</u>arabn<u>a</u> lahu al-amth<u>a</u>la wakullan tabbarn<u>a</u> tatbeer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>39.  To each one We set forth<br> Parables and examples;<br> And each one We broke<br> To utter annihilation<br> (For their sins).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_040.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>40.  Walaqad ataw AAal<u>a</u> alqaryati allatee om<u>t</u>irat ma<u>t</u>ara a<b>l</b>ssaw-i afalam yakoonoo yarawnah<u>a</u> bal k<u>a</u>noo l<u>a</u> yarjoona nushoor<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>40.  And the (Unbelievers) must indeed<br> Have passed by the town<br> On which was rained<br> A shower of evil: did they not<br> Then see it (with their own<br> Eyes)? But they fear not<br> The Resurrection.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_041.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>41.  Wa-i<u>tha</u> raawka in yattakhi<u>th</u>oonaka ill<u>a</u> huzuwan ah<u>atha</u> alla<u>th</u>ee baAAatha All<u>a</u>hu rasool<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>41.  When they see thee,<br> They treat thee no otherwise<br> Than in mockery: "Is this<br> The one whom God has sent<br> As an apostle?"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_042.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>42.  In k<u>a</u>da layu<u>d</u>illun<u>a</u> AAan <u>a</u>lihatin<u>a</u> lawl<u>a</u> an <u>s</u>abarn<u>a</u> AAalayh<u>a </u>wasawfa yaAAlamoona <u>h</u>eena yarawna alAAa<u>tha</u>ba man a<u>d</u>allu sabeel<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>42.  "He indeed would well-nigh<br> Have misled us from<br> Our gods, had it not been<br> That we were constant<br> To them!"—Soon will they<br> Know, when they see<br> The Penalty, who it is<br> That is most misled<br> In Path!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_043.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>43.  Araayta mani ittakha<u>th</u>a il<u>a</u>hahu haw<u>a</u>hu afaanta takoonu AAalayhi wakeel<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>43.  Seest thou such a one<br> As taketh for his god<br> His own passion (or impulse)?<br> Couldst thou be a disposer<br> Of affairs for him?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/025/025_044.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>44.  Am ta<u>h</u>sabu anna aktharahum yasmaAAoona aw yaAAqiloona in hum ill<u>a</u> ka<b>a</b>l-anAA<u>a</u>mi bal hum a<u>d</u>allu sabeel<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>44.  Or thinkest thou that most<br> Of them listen or understand?<br> They are only like cattle;—<br> Nay, they are worse astray<br> In Path.</p> <div class="filenav">
