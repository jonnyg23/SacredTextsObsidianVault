 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XLVI.: Aḥqāf, or Winding Sand-tracts.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 2</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Waq<u>a</u>la alla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo lilla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo law k<u>a</u>na khayran m<u>a</u> sabaqoon<u>a</u> ilayhi wa-i<u>th</u> lam yahtadoo bihi fasayaqooloona h<u>atha</u> ifkun qadeem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  The Unbelievers say<br> Of those who believe:<br>"If (this Message) were<br> A good thing, (such men)<br> Would not have gone<br> To it first, before us!"<br> And seeing that they<br> Guide not themselves thereby,<br> They will say, "This is<br> An (old,) old falsehood!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Wamin qablihi kit<u>a</u>bu moos<u>a</u> im<u>a</u>man wara<u>h</u>matan wah<u>atha</u> kit<u>a</u>bun mu<u>s</u>addiqun lis<u>a</u>nan AAarabiyyan liyun<u>th</u>ira alla<u>th</u>eena <i><u>th</u></i>alamoo wabushr<u>a</u> lilmu<u>h</u>sineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  And before this, was<br> The Book of Moses<br> As a guide and a mercy:<br> And this Book confirms (it)<br> In the Arabic tongue;<br> To admonish the unjust,<br> And as Glad Tidings.<br> To those who do right.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Inna alla<u>th</u>eena q<u>a</u>loo rabbun<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hu thumma istaq<u>a</u>moo fal<u>a</u> khawfun AAalayhim wal<u>a</u> hum ya<u>h</u>zanoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  Verily those who say,<br> "Our Lord is God,"<br> And remain firm<br> (On that Path),<br> On them shall be no fear,<br> Nor shall they grieve.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Ol<u>a</u>-ika a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>bu aljannati kh<u>a</u>lideena feeh<u>a</u> jaz<u>a</u>an bim<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>noo yaAAmaloon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  Such shall be Companions<br> Of the Garden, dwelling<br> Therein (for aye): a recompense<br> For their (good) deeds.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Wawa<u>ss</u>ayn<u>a</u> al-ins<u>a</u>na biw<u>a</u>lidayhi i<u>h</u>s<u>a</u>nan <u>h</u>amalat-hu ommuhu kurhan wawa<u>d</u>aAAat-hu kurhan wa<u>h</u>amluhu wafi<u>sa</u>luhu thal<u>a</u>thoona shahran <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> i<u>tha</u> balagha ashuddahu wabalagha arbaAAeena sanatan q<u>a</u>la rabbi awziAAnee an ashkura niAAmataka allatee anAAamta AAalayya waAAal<u>a</u> w<u>a</u>lidayya waan aAAmala <u>sa</u>li<u>h</u>an tar<u>da</u>hu waa<u>s</u>li<u>h</u> lee fee <u>th</u>urriyyatee innee tubtu ilayka wa-innee mina almuslimeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  We have enjoined on man<br> Kindness to his parents:<br> In pain did his mother<br> Bear him, and in pain<br> Did she give him birth.<br> The carrying of the (child)<br> To his weaning is<br> (A period of) thirty months.<br> At length, when he reaches<br> The age of full strength<br> And attains forty years,<br> He says, "O my Lord!<br> Grant me that I may be<br> Grateful for Thy favour<br> Which Thou hast bestowed<br> Upon me, and upon both<br> My parents, and that I<br> May work righteousness<br> Such as Thou mayest approve;<br> And be gracious to me<br> In my issue. Truly<br> Have I turned to Thee<br> And truly do I bow<br> (To Thee) in Islām."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Ol<u>a</u>-ika alla<u>th</u>eena nataqabbalu AAanhum a<u>h</u>sana m<u>a</u> AAamiloo wanataj<u>a</u>wazu AAan sayyi-<u>a</u>tihim fee a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>bi aljannati waAAda a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>idqi alla<u>th</u>ee k<u>a</u>noo yooAAadoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  Such are they from whom<br> We shall accept the best<br> Of their deeds and pass by<br> Their ill deeds: (they shall<br>Be) among the Companions<br> Of the Garden: a promise!<br> Of truth, which was<br> Made to them<br> (In this life).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee q<u>a</u>la liw<u>a</u>lidayhi offin lakum<u>a</u> ataAAid<u>a</u>ninee an okhraja waqad khalati alquroonu min qablee wahum<u>a</u> yastagheeth<u>a</u>ni All<u>a</u>ha waylaka <u>a</u>min inna waAAda All<u>a</u>hi <u>h</u>aqqun fayaqoolu m<u>a</u> h<u>atha</u> ill<u>a</u> as<u>at</u>eeru al-awwaleen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  But (there is one)<br> Who says to his parents,<br> "Fie on you! Do ye<br> Hold out the promise<br> To me that I<br> Shall be raised up,<br> Even though generations<br> Have passed before me<br> (Without rising again)?"<br> And they two seek<br> God's aid, (and rebuke<br> The son): "Woe to thee!<br> Have Faith! For the promise<br> Of God is true."<br> But he says, "This is<br> Nothing but tales<br> Of the ancients!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Ol<u>a</u>-ika alla<u>th</u>eena <u>h</u>aqqa AAalayhimu alqawlu fee umamin qad khalat min qablihim mina aljinni wa<b>a</b>l-insi innahum k<u>a</u>noo kh<u>a</u>sireen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  Such are they against whom<br> Is proved the Sentence<br> Among the previous generations<br> Of Jinns and men, that have<br> Passed away; for they will<br> Be (utterly) lost.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Walikullin daraj<u>a</u>tun mimm<u>a</u> AAamiloo waliyuwaffiyahum aAAm<u>a</u>lahum wahum l<u>a</u> yu<i><u>th</u></i>lamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  And to all<br> Are (assigned) degrees<br>According to the deeds<br> Which they (have done),<br> And in order that (God)<br> May recompense their deeds,<br> And no injustice be done<br> To them.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/046/046_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Wayawma yuAAra<u>d</u>u alla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo AAal<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri a<u>th</u>habtum <u>t</u>ayyib<u>a</u>tikum fee <u>h</u>ay<u>a</u>tikumu a<b>l</b>dduny<u>a</u> wa<b>i</b>stamtaAAtum bih<u>a</u> fa<b>a</b>lyawma tujzawna AAa<u>tha</u>ba alhooni bim<u>a</u> kuntum tastakbiroona fee al-ar<u>d</u>i bighayri al<u>h</u>aqqi wabim<u>a</u> kuntum tafsuqoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  And on the Day that<br> The Unbelievers will be<br> Placed before the Fire,<br> (It will be said to them):<br> "Ye received your good things<br> In the life of the world,<br> And ye took your pleasure<br> Out of them: but to-day<br> Shall ye be recompensed<br> With a Penalty of humiliation:<br> For that ye were arrogant<br> On earth without just cause,<br> And that ye (ever) transgressed."</p> <div class="filenav">
