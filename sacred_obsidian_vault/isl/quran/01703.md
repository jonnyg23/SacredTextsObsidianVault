 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XVII.: Banī Isrā-īl, or the Children of Israel,</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 3</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  Waqa<u>da</u> rabbuka all<u>a</u> taAAbudoo ill<u>a</u> iyy<u>a</u>hu wabi<b>a</b>lw<u>a</u>lidayni i<u>h</u>s<u>a</u>nan imm<u>a</u> yablughanna AAindaka alkibara a<u>h</u>aduhum<u>a</u> aw kil<u>a</u>hum<u>a</u> fal<u>a</u> taqul lahum<u>a</u> offin wal<u>a</u> tanharhum<u>a</u> waqul lahum<u>a</u> qawlan kareem<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  Why Lord hath decreed<br> That ye worship hone but Him,<br> And that ye be kind<br> To parents. Whether one<br> Or both of them attain<br> Old age in thy life,<br> Say not to them a word<br> Of contempt, nor repel them,<br> But address them<br> In terms of honour.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  Wa<b>i</b>khfi<u>d</u> lahum<u>a</u> jan<u>ah</u>a a<b>l</b><u>thth</u>ulli mina a<b>l</b>rra<u>h</u>mati waqul rabbi ir<u>h</u>amhum<u>a</u> kam<u>a</u> rabbay<u>a</u>nee <u>s</u>agheer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  And, out of kindness,<br> Lower to them the wing<br> Of humility, and say:<br> "My Lord! bestow on them<br> Thy Mercy even as they<br> Cherished me in childhood.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  Rabbukum aAAlamu bim<u>a</u> fee nufoosikum in takoonoo <u>sa</u>li<u>h</u>eena fa-innahu k<u>a</u>na lil-aww<u>a</u>beena ghafoor<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  Your Lord knoweth best<br> What is in your hearts:<br> If ye do deeds of righteousness,<br> Verily He is Most Forgiving<br> To those who turn to Him<br> Again and again<br> (in true penitence).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_026.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>26.  Wa<u>a</u>ti <u>tha</u> alqurb<u>a</u> <u>h</u>aqqahu wa<b>a</b>lmiskeena wa<b>i</b>bna a<b>l</b>ssabeeli wal<u>a</u> tuba<u>thth</u>ir tab<u>th</u>eer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>26.  And render to the kindred<br> Their due rights, as (also)<br> To those in want,<br> And to the wayfarer:<br> But squander not (your wealth)<br> In the manner of a spendthrift.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_027.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>27.  Inna almuba<u>thth</u>ireena k<u>a</u>noo ikhw<u>a</u>na a<b>l</b>shshay<u>at</u>eeni wak<u>a</u>na a<b>l</b>shshay<u>ta</u>nu lirabbihi kafoor<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>27.  Verily spendthrifts are brothers<br> Of the Evil Ones;<br> And the Evil One<br> Is to his Lord (Himself)<br> Ungrateful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_028.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>28.  Wa-imm<u>a</u> tuAAri<u>d</u>anna AAanhumu ibtigh<u>a</u>a ra<u>h</u>matin min rabbika tarjooh<u>a</u> faqul lahum qawlan maysoor<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>28.  And even if thou hast<br> To turn away from them<br> In pursuit of the Mercy<br> From thy Lord which thou<br> Dost expect, yet speak<br> To them a word<br> Of easy kindness.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_029.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>29.  Wal<u>a</u> tajAAal yadaka maghloolatan il<u>a</u> AAunuqika wal<u>a</u> tabsu<u>t</u>h<u>a</u> kulla albas<u>t</u>i fataqAAuda malooman ma<u>h</u>soor<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>29.  Make not thy hand tied<br> (Like a niggard's) to thy neck,<br> Nor stretch it forth<br> To its utmost reach,<br> So that thou become<br> Blameworthy and destitute.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/017/017_030.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>30.  Inna rabbaka yabsu<u>t</u>u a<b>l</b>rrizqa liman yash<u>a</u>o wayaqdiru innahu k<u>a</u>na biAAib<u>a</u>dihi khabeeran ba<u>s</u>eer<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>30.  Verily thy Lord doth provide<br> Sustenance in abundance<br> For whom He pleaseth, and He<br> Provideth in a just measure.<br> For He doth know<br> And regard all His servants.</p> <div class="filenav">
