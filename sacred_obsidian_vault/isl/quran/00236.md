 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra II.: Baqara, or the Heifer.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 36</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_261.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>261.  Mathalu alla<u>th</u>eena yunfiqoona amw<u>a</u>lahum fee sabeeli All<u>a</u>hi kamathali <u>h</u>abbatin anbatat sabAAa san<u>a</u>bila fee kulli sunbulatin mi-atu <u>h</u>abbatin wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu yu<u>da</u>AAifu liman yash<u>a</u>o wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu w<u>a</u>siAAun AAaleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>261.  The parable of those<br> Who spend their substance<br> In the way of God is that<br> Of a grain of corn: it groweth<br> Seven ears, and each ear<br> Hath a hundred grains.<br> God giveth manifold increase<br> To whom He pleaseth:<br> And God careth for all<br> And He knoweth all things</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_262.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>262.  Alla<u>th</u>eena yunfiqoona amw<u>a</u>lahum fee sabeeli All<u>a</u>hi thumma l<u>a</u> yutbiAAoona m<u>a</u> anfaqoo mannan wal<u>a</u> a<u>th</u>an lahum ajruhum AAinda rabbihim wal<u>a</u> khawfun AAalayhim wal<u>a</u> hum ya<u>h</u>zanoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>262.  Those who spend<br> Their substance in the cause<br> Of God, and follow not up<br> Their gifts with reminders<br> Of their generosity<br> Or with injury,—for them<br> Their reward is with their Lord:<br> On them shall be no fear,<br> Nor shall they grieve.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_263.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>263.  Qawlun maAAroofun wamaghfiratun khayrun min <u>s</u>adaqatin yatbaAAuh<u>a</u> a<u>th</u>an wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu ghaniyyun <u>h</u>aleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>263.  Kind words<br> And the covering of faults<br>Are better than charity<br> Followed by injury.<br> God is Free of all wants,<br> And He is most Forbearing.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_264.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>264.  Y<u>a</u> ayyuh<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo l<u>a</u> tub<u>t</u>iloo <u>s</u>adaq<u>a</u>tikum bi<b>a</b>lmanni wa<b>a</b>l-a<u>tha</u> ka<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee yunfiqu m<u>a</u>lahu ri-<u>a</u>a a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>si wal<u>a</u> yu/minu bi<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hi wa<b>a</b>lyawmi al-<u>a</u>khiri famathaluhu kamathali <u>s</u>afw<u>a</u>nin AAalayhi tur<u>a</u>bun faa<u>sa</u>bahu w<u>a</u>bilun fatarakahu <u>s</u>aldan l<u>a</u> yaqdiroona AAal<u>a</u> shay-in mimm<u>a</u> kasaboo wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu l<u>a</u> yahdee alqawma alk<u>a</u>fireen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>264.  O ye who believe!<br> Cancel not your charity<br> By reminders of your generosity<br> Or by injury,—like those<br> Who spend their substance<br> To be seen of men,<br> But believe neither<br> In God nor in the Last Day.<br> They are in Parable like a hard,<br> Barren rock, on which<br> Is a little soil: on it<br> Falls heavy rain,<br> Which leaves it<br> (Just) a bare stone.<br> They will be able to do nothing<br> With aught they have earned.<br> And God guideth not<br> Those who reject faith.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_265.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>265.  Wamathalu alla<u>th</u>eena yunfiqoona amw<u>a</u>lahumu ibtigh<u>a</u>a mar<u>da</u>ti All<u>a</u>hi watathbeetan min anfusihim kamathali jannatin birabwatin a<u>sa</u>bah<u>a</u> w<u>a</u>bilun fa<u>a</u>tat okulah<u>a</u> <u>d</u>iAAfayni fa-in lam yu<u>s</u>ibh<u>a</u> w<u>a</u>bilun fa<u>t</u>allun wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu bim<u>a</u> taAAmaloona ba<u>s</u>eer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>265.  And the likeness of those<br> Who spend their substance,<br> Seeking to please God<br> And to strengthen their souls,<br> Is as a garden, high<br> And fertile: heavy rain<br> Falls on it but makes it yield<br> A double increase<br> Of harvest, and if it receives not<br> Heavy rain, light moisture<br> Sufficeth it. God seeth well<br> Whatever ye do.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_266.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>266.  Ayawaddu a<u>h</u>adukum an takoona lahu jannatun min nakheelin waaAAn<u>a</u>bin tajree min ta<u>h</u>tih<u>a</u> al-anh<u>a</u>ru lahu feeh<u>a</u> min kulli a<b>l</b>ththamar<u>a</u>ti waa<u>sa</u>bahu alkibaru walahu <u>th</u>urriyyatun <u>d</u>uAAaf<u>a</u>o faa<u>sa</u>bah<u>a</u> iAA<u>sa</u>run feehi n<u>a</u>run fa<b>i</b><u>h</u>taraqat ka<u>tha</u>lika yubayyinu All<u>a</u>hu lakumu al-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>ti laAAallakum tatafakkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>266.  Does any of you wish<br> That he should have a garden<br> With date-palms and vines<br> And streams flowing<br> Underneath, and all kinds<br> Of fruit, while he is stricken<br> With old age, and his children<br> Are not strong (enough<br> To look after themselves)—<br> That it should be caught<br> In a whirlwind,<br> With fire therein,<br> And be burnt up?<br> Thus doth God make clear<br> To you (His) Signs;<br> That ye may consider.</p> <div class="filenav">
