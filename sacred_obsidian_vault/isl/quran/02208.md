 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXII.: Ḥajj, or The Pilgrimage.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 8</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_058.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>58.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena h<u>a</u>jaroo fee sabeeli All<u>a</u>hi thumma qutiloo aw m<u>a</u>too layarzuqannahumu All<u>a</u>hu rizqan <u>h</u>asanan wa-inna All<u>a</u>ha lahuwa khayru a<b>l</b>rr<u>a</u>ziqeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>58.  Those who leave their homes<br> In the cause of God,<br> And are then slain or die,<br> On them will God bestow verily<br> A goodly Provision:<br> Truly God is He Who<br> Bestows the best Provision.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_059.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>59.  Layudkhilannahum mudkhalan yar<u>d</u>awnahu wa-inna All<u>a</u>ha laAAaleemun <u>h</u>aleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>59.  Verily He will admit then<br> To a place with which<br> They shall be well pleased:<br> For God is All-Knowing,<br> Most Forbearing.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_060.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>60.  <u>Tha</u>lika waman AA<u>a</u>qaba bimithli m<u>a</u> AAooqiba bihi thumma bughiya AAalayhi layan<u>s</u>urannahu All<u>a</u>hu inna All<u>a</u>ha laAAafuwwun ghafoor<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>60.  That (is so). And if one<br> Has retaliated to no greater<br> Extent than the injury he received,<br> And is again set upon<br> Inordinately, God will help<br> Him: for God is One<br> That blots out (sins)<br> And forgives (again and again).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_061.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>61.  <u>Tha</u>lika bi-anna All<u>a</u>ha yooliju allayla fee a<b>l</b>nnah<u>a</u>ri wayooliju a<b>l</b>nnah<u>a</u>ra fee allayli waanna All<u>a</u>ha sameeAAun ba<u>s</u>eer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>61.  What is because God merges<br> Night into Day, and He<br> Merges Day into Night, and<br> Verily it is God Who hears<br>And sees (all things).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_062.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>62.  <u>Tha</u>lika bi-anna All<u>a</u>ha huwa al<u>h</u>aqqu waanna m<u>a</u> yadAAoona min doonihi huwa alb<u>at</u>ilu waanna All<u>a</u>ha huwa alAAaliyyu alkabeer<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>62.  That is because God—He<br> Is the Reality; and those<br> Besides Him whom they invoke,—<br> They are but vain Falsehood:<br> Verily God is He, Most High,<br> Most Great.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_063.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>63.  Alam tara anna All<u>a</u>ha anzala mina a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i m<u>a</u>an fatu<u>s</u>bi<u>h</u>u al-ar<u>d</u>u mukh<u>d</u>arratan inna All<u>a</u>ha la<u>t</u>eefun khabeer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>63.  Seest thou not that God<br> Sends down rain from the sky,<br> And forthwith the earth<br> Becomes clothed with green?<br> For God is He Who understands<br> The finest mysteries, and<br> Is well-acquainted (with them).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_064.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>64.  Lahu m<u>a</u> fee a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wam<u>a</u> fee al-ar<u>d</u>i wa-inna All<u>a</u>ha lahuwa alghaniyyu al<u>h</u>ameed<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>64.  To Him belongs all that is<br> In the heavens and on earth:<br> For verily God,—He is<br> Free of all wants,<br> Worthy of all praise.</p> <div class="filenav">
