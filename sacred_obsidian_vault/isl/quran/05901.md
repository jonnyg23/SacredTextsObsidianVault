 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra LIX.: Ḥa<u>sh</u>r, or The Gathering</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  Sabba<u>h</u>a lill<u>a</u>hi m<u>a</u> fee a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wam<u>a</u> fee al-ar<u>d</u>i wahuwa alAAazeezu al<u>h</u>akeem<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>1.  Whatever is<br> In the heavens and<br> On earth, let it declare<br> The Praises and Glory<br> Of God: for He is<br> The Exalted in Might,<br> The Wise.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  Huwa alla<u>th</u>ee akhraja alla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo min ahli alkit<u>a</u>bi min diy<u>a</u>rihim li-awwali al<u>h</u>ashri m<u>a</u> <i><u>th</u></i>anantum an yakhrujoo wa<i><u>th</u></i>annoo annahum m<u>a</u>niAAatuhum <u>h</u>u<u>s</u>oonuhum mina All<u>a</u>hi faat<u>a</u>humu All<u>a</u>hu min <u>h</u>aythu lam ya<u>h</u>tasiboo waqa<u>th</u>afa fee quloobihimu a<b>l</b>rruAAba yukhriboona buyootahum bi-aydeehim waaydee almu/mineena fa<b>i</b>AAtabiroo y<u>a</u> olee al-ab<u>sa</u>r<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>2.  It is He Who got out<br> The Unbelievers among<br> The People of the Book<br> From their homes<br> At the first gathering<br> (Of the forces).<br> Little did ye think<br> That they would get out:<br> And they thought<br> That their fortresses<br> Would defend them from God!<br> But the (Wrath of) God<br>Came to them from quarters<br> From which they little<br> Expected (it), and cast<br> Terror into their hearts,<br> So that they destroyed<br> Their dwellings by their own<br> Hands and the hands<br> Of the Believers.<br> Take warning, then,<br> O ye with eyes (to see)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Walawl<u>a</u> an kataba All<u>a</u>hu AAalayhimu aljal<u>a</u>a laAAa<u>thth</u>abahum fee a<b>l</b>dduny<u>a</u> walahum fee al-<u>a</u>khirati AAa<u>tha</u>bu a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>r<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>3.  And had it not been<br> That God had decreed<br> Banishment for them,<br> He would certainly have<br> Punished them in this world:<br> And in the Hereafter<br> They shall (certainly) have<br> The Punishment of the Fire.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  <u>Tha</u>lika bi-annahum sh<u>a</u>qqoo All<u>a</u>ha warasoolahu waman yush<u>a</u>qqi All<u>a</u>ha fa-inna All<u>a</u>ha shadeedu alAAiq<u>a</u>b<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>4.  That is because they<br> Resisted God and His Apostle:<br> And if any one resists God,<br> Verily God is severe<br> In Punishment.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  M<u>a</u> qa<u>t</u>aAAtum min leenatin aw taraktumooh<u>a</u> q<u>a</u>-imatan AAal<u>a</u> o<u>s</u>oolih<u>a</u> fabi-i<u>th</u>ni All<u>a</u>hi waliyukhziya alf<u>a</u>siqeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>5.  Whether ye cut down<br> (O ye Muslims!)<br> The tender palm-trees,<br> Or ye left them standing<br> On their roots, it was<br> By leave of God, and<br> In order that He might<br> Cover with shame<br> The rebellious transgressors.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Wam<u>a</u> af<u>a</u>a All<u>a</u>hu AAal<u>a</u> rasoolihi minhum fam<u>a</u> awjaftum AAalayhi min khaylin wal<u>a</u> rik<u>a</u>bin wal<u>a</u>kinna All<u>a</u>ha yusalli<u>t</u>u rusulahu AAal<u>a</u> man yash<u>a</u>o wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu AAal<u>a</u> kulli shay-in qadeer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  What God has bestowed<br> On His Apostle (and taken<br> Away) from them—for this<br> Ye made no expedition<br> With either cavalry or camelry:<br> But God gives power<br> To His apostles over<br> Any He pleases: and God<br> Has power over all things.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  M<u>a</u> af<u>a</u>a All<u>a</u>hu AAal<u>a</u> rasoolihi min ahli alqur<u>a</u> falill<u>a</u>hi wali<b>l</b>rrasooli wali<u>th</u>ee alqurb<u>a</u> wa<b>a</b>lyat<u>a</u>m<u>a</u> wa<b>a</b>lmas<u>a</u>keeni wa<b>i</b>bni a<b>l</b>ssabeeli kay l<u>a</u> yakoona doolatan bayna al-aghniy<u>a</u>-i minkum wam<u>a</u> <u>a</u>t<u>a</u>kumu a<b>l</b>rrasoolu fakhu<u>th</u>oohu wam<u>a</u> nah<u>a</u>kum AAanhu fa<b>i</b>ntahoo wa<b>i</b>ttaqoo All<u>a</u>ha inna All<u>a</u>ha shadeedu alAAiq<u>a</u>b<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  What God has bestowed<br> On His Apostle (and taken<br> Away) from the people<br> Of the townships,—belongs<br>To God,—to His Apostle<br> And to kindred and orphans,<br> The needy and the wayfarer;<br> In order that it may not<br> (Merely) make a circuit<br> Between the wealthy among you.<br> So take what the Apostle<br> Assigns to you, and deny<br> Yourselves that which he<br> Withholds from you.<br> And fear God; for God<br> Is strict in Punishment.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Lilfuqar<u>a</u>-i almuh<u>a</u>jireena alla<u>th</u>eena okhrijoo min diy<u>a</u>rihim waamw<u>a</u>lihim yabtaghoona fa<u>d</u>lan mina All<u>a</u>hi wari<u>d</u>w<u>a</u>nan wayan<u>s</u>uroona All<u>a</u>ha warasoolahu ol<u>a</u>-ika humu a<b>l</b><u>ssa</u>diqoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  (Some part is due)<br> To the indigent Muhājirs,<br> Those who were expelled<br> From their homes and their property,<br> While seeking Grace from God<br> And (His) Good Pleasure,<br> And aiding God and His Apostle:<br> Such are indeed<br> The sincere ones;—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena tabawwaoo a<b>l</b>dd<u>a</u>ra wa<b>a</b>l-eem<u>a</u>na min qablihim yu<u>h</u>ibboona man h<u>a</u>jara ilayhim wal<u>a</u> yajidoona fee <u>s</u>udoorihim <u>ha</u>jatan mimm<u>a</u> ootoo wayu/thiroona AAal<u>a</u> anfusihim walaw k<u>a</u>na bihim kha<u>sas</u>atun waman yooqa shu<u>hh</u>a nafsihi faol<u>a</u>-ika humu almufli<u>h</u>oon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  But those who<br> Before them, had homes<br> (In Medina)<br> And had adopted the Faith,—<br> Show their affection to such<br> As came to them for refuge,<br> And entertain no desire<br> In their hearts for things<br>Given to the (latter),<br> But give them preference<br> Over themselves, even though<br> Poverty was their (own lot).<br> And those saved from<br> The covetousness of their own<br> Souls,—they are the ones<br> That achieve prosperity.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/059/059_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena j<u>a</u>oo min baAAdihim yaqooloona rabban<u>a</u> ighfir lan<u>a</u> wali-ikhw<u>a</u>nin<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena sabaqoon<u>a</u> bi<b>a</b>l-eem<u>a</u>ni wal<u>a</u> tajAAal fee quloobin<u>a</u> ghillan lilla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo rabban<u>a</u> innaka raoofun ra<u>h</u>eem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  And those who came<br> After them say: "Our Lord!<br> Forgive us, and our brethren<br> Who came before us<br> Into the Faith,<br> And leave not,<br> In our hearts,<br> Rancour (or sense of injury)<br> Against those who have believed.<br> Our Lord! Thou art<br> Indeed Full of Kindness,<br> Most Merciful."</p> <div class="filenav">
