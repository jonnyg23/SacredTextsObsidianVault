 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XIII.: Ra’d, or Thunder.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  Alif-l<u>a</u>m-meem-r<u>a</u> tilka <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tu alkit<u>a</u>bi wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee onzila ilayka min rabbika al<u>h</u>aqqu wal<u>a</u>kinna akthara a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>si l<u>a</u> yu/minoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>1.  A. L. M. R. These are<br> The Signs (or Verses)<br> Of the Book: that which<br> Hath been revealed unto thee<br> From thy Lord is the Truth;<br> But most men believe not.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  All<u>a</u>hu alla<u>th</u>ee rafaAAa a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti bighayri AAamadin tarawnah<u>a</u> thumma istaw<u>a</u> AAal<u>a</u> alAAarshi wasakhkhara a<b>l</b>shshamsa wa<b>a</b>lqamara kullun yajree li-ajalin musamman yudabbiru al-amra yufa<u>ss</u>ilu al-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>ti laAAallakum biliq<u>a</u>-i rabbikum tooqinoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>2.  God is He Who raised<br> The heavens without any pillars<br> That ye can see; is firmly<br> Established on the Throne (of Authority);<br> He has subjected the sun<br> And the moon (to his Law)!<br> Each one runs (its course)<br> For a term appointed.<br> He doth regulate all affairs,<br> Explaining the Signs in detail,<br>That ye may believe with certainty<br> In the meeting with your Lord.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Wahuwa alla<u>th</u>ee madda al-ar<u>d</u>a wajaAAala feeh<u>a</u> raw<u>a</u>siya waanh<u>a</u>ran wamin kulli a<b>l</b>ththamar<u>a</u>ti jaAAala feeh<u>a</u> zawjayni ithnayni yughshee allayla a<b>l</b>nnah<u>a</u>ra inna fee <u>tha</u>lika la<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin liqawmin yatafakkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>3.  And it is He Who spread out<br> The earth, and set thereon<br> Mountains standing firm,<br> And (flowing) rivers: and fruit<br> Of every kind He made<br> In pairs, two and two:<br> He draweth the Night as a veil<br> O'er the Day. Behold, verily<br> In these things there are Signs<br> For those who consider!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  Wafee al-ar<u>d</u>i qi<u>t</u>aAAun mutaj<u>a</u>wir<u>a</u>tun wajann<u>a</u>tun min aAAn<u>a</u>bin wazarAAun wanakheelun <u>s</u>inw<u>a</u>nun waghayru <u>s</u>inw<u>a</u>nin yusq<u>a</u> bim<u>a</u>-in w<u>ah</u>idin wanufa<u>dd</u>ilu baAA<u>d</u>ah<u>a</u> AAal<u>a</u> baAA<u>d</u>in fee alokuli inna fee <u>tha</u>lika la<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin liqawmin yaAAqiloon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>4.  And in the earth are tracts<br> (Diverse though) neighbouring,<br> And gardens of vines<br> And fields sown with corn,<br> And palm trees—growing<br> Out of single roots or otherwise:<br> Watered with the same water,<br> Yet some of them We make<br> More excellent than others to eat.<br> Behold, verily in these things<br>There are Signs for those<br> Who understand!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Wa-in taAAjab faAAajabun qawluhum a-i<u>tha</u> kunn<u>a</u> tur<u>a</u>ban a-inn<u>a</u> lafee khalqin jadeedin ol<u>a</u>-ika alla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo birabbihim waol<u>a</u>-ika al-aghl<u>a</u>lu fee aAAn<u>a</u>qihim waol<u>a</u>-ika a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>bu a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri hum feeh<u>a</u> kh<u>a</u>lidoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>5.  If thou dost marvel<br> (At their want of faith),<br> Strange is their saying:<br> "When we are (actually) dust,<br> Shall we indeed then be<br> In a creation renewed?" They are<br> Those who deny their Lord! They<br> Are those round whose necks<br> Will be yokes (of servitude):<br> They will be Companions<br> Of the Fire, to dwell therein<br> (For aye)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  WayastaAAjiloonaka bi<b>al</b>ssayyi-ati qabla al<u>h</u>asanati waqad khalat min qablihimu almathul<u>a</u>tu wa-inna rabbaka la<u>th</u>oo maghfiratin li<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>si AAal<u>a</u> <i><u>th</u></i>ulmihim wa-inna rabbaka lashadeedu alAAiq<u>a</u>b<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  They ask thee to hasten on<br> The evil in preference to the good:<br> Yet have come to pass,<br> Before them, (many) exemplary<br> Punishments! But verily<br> Thy Lord is full of forgiveness<br> For mankind for their wrong-doing,<br> And verily thy Lord<br> Is (also) strict in punishment.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/013/013_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Wayaqoolu alla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo lawl<u>a</u> onzila AAalayhi <u>a</u>yatun min rabbihi innam<u>a</u> anta mun<u>th</u>irun walikulli qawmin h<u>a</u>d<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  And the Unbelievers say:<br> "Why is not a Sign sent down<br> To him from his Lord?<br> But thou art truly<br>A warner, and to every people<br> A guide.</p> <div class="filenav">
