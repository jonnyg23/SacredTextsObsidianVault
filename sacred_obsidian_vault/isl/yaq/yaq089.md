 <p><a name="page_1731"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1731</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Sūra LXXXIX.</h3>  <h3 align="center"><i>Fajr,</i> or The Break of Day.</h3>  
 <p><i>In the name of God, Most Gracious,<br> Most Merciful</i></p>  
 <p>1. By the Break of Day;</p>  
 <p>2. By the Nights twice five;</p>  
 <p>3. By the Even<br> And Odd (contrasted);</p>  
 <p>4. And by the Night<br> When it passeth away;—</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1732"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1732</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Is there (not) in these<br> An adjuration (or evidence)<br> For those who understand?</p>  
 <p>6. Seest thou not<br> How thy Lord dealt<br> With the ‘Ād (people),—</p>  
 <p>7. Of the (city of) Iram,<br> With lofty pillars,</p>  
 <p>8. The like of which<br> Were not produced<br> In (all) the land?</p>  
 <p>9. And with the <u>Th</u>amūd<br> (People), who cut out<br> (Huge) rocks in the-valley?—</p>  
 <p>10. And with Pharaoh,<br> Lord of Stakes?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1733"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1733</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (All) these transgressed<br> Beyond bounds in the lands.</p>  
 <p>12. And heaped therein<br> Mischief (on mischief).</p>  
 <p>13. Therefore did thy Lord<br> Pour on them a scourge<br> Of diverse chastisements:</p>  
 <p>14. For thy Lord is<br> (As a Guardian)<br> On a watch-tower.</p>  
 <p>15. Now, as for man,<br> When his Lord trieth him,<br> Giving him honour and gifts,<br> Then saith he, (puffed up),<br> "My Lord hath honoured me"</p>  
 <p>16. But when he trieth him,<br> Restricting his subsistence<br> For him, then saith he<br> (In despair), "My Lord<br> Hath humiliated me I."</p>  
 <p>17. Nay, nay! But <i>ye</i><br> Honour not the orphans!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1734"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1734</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Nor do ye encourage<br> One another<br> To feed the poor!—</p>  
 <p>19. And ye devour Inheritance—<br> All with greed,</p>  
 <p>20. And ye love wealth<br> With inordinate love!</p>  
 <p>21. Nay! When the earth<br> Is pounded to powder,</p>  
 <p>22. And thy Lord cometh,<br> And His angels,<br> Rank upon rank,</p>  
 <p>23. And Hell, that Day,<br> Is brought (face to face),—<br> On that Day will man<br> Remember, but how will<br> That remembrance profit him?</p>  
 <p>24. He will say: "Ah!<br> Would that I had<br> Sent forth (Good Deeds)<br> For (this) my (Future) Life!"</p>  
 <p>25. For, that Day,<br> His Chastisement will be</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1735"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1735</font></a></p>  
 <p>Such as none (else)<br> Can inflict,</p>  
 <p>26. And His bonds<br> Will be such as<br> None (other) can bind.</p>  
 <p>27. (To the righteous soul<br> Will be said:)<br> "O (thou) soul,<br> In (complete) rest<br> And satisfaction!</p>  
 <p>28. "Come back thou<br> To thy Lord,—<br> Well pleased (thyself),<br> And well-pleasing<br> Unto Him!</p>  
 <p>29. "Enter thou, then,<br> Among my Devotees!</p>  
 <p>30. "Yea, enter thou<br> My Heaven!</p>
