 <p><a name="page_1774"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1774</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Sūra C.</h3>  <h3 align="center">’Adiyāt, or Those that run.</h3>  
 <p><i>In the name of God, Most Gracious,<br> Most Merciful.</i></p>  
 <p>1. By the (Steeds)<br> That run, with panting (breath),</p>  
 <p>2. And strike sparks of fire,</p>  
 <p>3. And push home the charge<br> In the morning,</p>  
 <p>4. And raise the dust<br> In clouds the while,</p>  
 <p>5. And penetrate forthwith<br> Into the midst (of the foe)<br> <i>En masse;</i>—</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1775"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1775</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Truly Man is,<br> To his Lord,<br> Ungrateful;</p>  
 <p>7. And to that (fact)<br> He bears witness<br> (By his deeds);</p>  
 <p>8. And violent is he<br> In his love of wealth.</p>  
 <p>9. Does he not know,—<br> When that which is<br> In the graves is<br> Scattered abroad</p>  
 <p>10. And that which is<br> (Locked up) in (human) breasts<br> Is made manifest—</p>  
 <p>11. That their Lord had been<br> Well-acquainted with them,<br> (Even to) that Day?</p>
