 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_1472"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1472</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Sūra LV.</h3>  <h3 align="center"><i>Raḥmān,</i> or (God) Most Gracious.</h3>  
 <p><i>In the name of God, Most Gracious,<br> Most Merciful.</i></p>  
 <p>1. (God) Most Gracious!</p>  
 <p>2. It is He Who has<br> Taught the Qur-ān.</p>  
 <p>3. He has created man:</p>  
 <p>4. He has taught him speech<br> (And Intelligence).</p>  
 <p>5. The sun and the moon<br> Follow courses (exactly) computed;</p>  
 <p>6. And the herbs and the trees—<br> Both (alike) bow in adoration.</p>  
 <p>7. And the Firmament has He<br> Raised high, and He has set up<br> The Balance (of Justice),</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1473"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1473</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. In order that ye may<br> Not transgress (due) balance.</p>  
 <p>9. So establish weight with justice<br> And fall not short<br> In the balance.</p>  
 <p>10. It is He Who has<br> Spread out the earth<br> For (His) creatures:</p>  
 <p>11. Therein is fruit<br> And date-palms, producing<br> Spathes (enclosing dates);</p>  
 <p>12. Also corn, with (its)<br> Leaves and stalk for fodder,<br> And sweet-smelling plants.</p>  
 <p>13. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1474"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1474</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. He created man<br> From sounding clay<br> Like unto pottery,</p>  
 <p>15. And He created Jinns<br> From fire free of smoke:</p>  
 <p>16. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>17. (He is) Lord<br> Of the two Easts<br> And Lord<br> Of the two Wests:</p>  
 <p>18. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>19. He has let free<br> The two bodies<br> Of flowing water,<br> Meeting together:</p>  
 <p>20. Between them is a Barrier<br> Which they do not transgress:</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1475"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1475</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>22. Out of them come<br> Pearls and Coral:</p>  
 <p>23. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>24. And His are the Ships<br> Sailing smoothly through the seas,<br> Lofty as mountains:</p>  
 <p>25. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  <h5 align="center">SECTION 2.</h5>  
 <p>26. All that is on earth<br> Will perish:</p>  
 <p>27. But will abide (for ever)<br> The Face of thy Lord,—</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1476"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1476</font></a></p>  
 <p>Full of Majesty,<br> Bounty and Honour.</p>  
 <p>28. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>29. Of Him seeks (its need)<br> Every creature in the heavens<br> And on earth:<br> Every day in (new) Splendour<br> Doth He (shine)!</p>  
 <p>30. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>31. Soon shall We<br> Settle your affairs,<br> O both ye worlds!</p>  
 <p>32. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>33. O ye assembly of Jinns,<br> And men! If it be</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1477"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1477</font></a></p>  
 <p>Ye can pass beyond<br> The zones of the heavens<br> And the earth, pass ye!<br> Not without authority<br> Shall ye be able to pass!</p>  
 <p>34. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>35. On you will be sent<br> (O ye evil ones twain!)<br> A flame of fire (to burn)<br> And a smoke (to choke):<br> No defence will ye have:</p>  
 <p>36. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1478"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1478</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. When the sky is rent<br> Asunder, and it becomes red<br> Like ointment:</p>  
 <p>38. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>39. On that Day<br> No question will be asked<br> Of man or Jinn<br> As to his sin,</p>  
 <p>40. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>41. (For) the sinners will be<br> Known by their Marks:<br> And they will be seized<br> By their forelocks and<br> Their feet.</p>  
 <p>42. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>43. This is the Hell which<br> The Sinners deny:</p>  
 <p>44. In its midst<br> And in the midst<br> Of boiling hot water<br> Will they wander round!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1479"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1479</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  <h5 align="center">SECTION 3.</h5>  
 <p>46. But for such as fear<br> The time when they will<br> Stand before (the Judgment Seat<br> Of) their Lord,<br> There will be two Gardens—</p>  
 <p>47. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>48. Containing all kinds<br> (Of trees and delights);—</p>  
 <p>49. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>50. In them (each) will be<br> Two Springs flowing (free);</p>  
 <p>51. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>52. In them will be Fruits<br> Of every kind, two and two.</p>  
 <p>53. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1480"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1480</font></a></p>  
 <p>54. They will recline on Carpets,<br> Whose inner linings will be<br> Of rich brocade: the Fruit<br> Of the Gardens will be<br> Near (and easy of reach).</p>  
 <p>55. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>56. In them will be (Maidens),<br> Chaste, restraining their glances,<br> Whom no man or Jinn<br> Before them has touched;—</p>  
 <p>57. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>58. Like unto rubies and coral.</p>  
 <p>59. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>60. Is there any Reward<br> For Good-other than Good?</p>  
 <p>61. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>62. And besides these two,<br> There are two other Gardens,—</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1481"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1481</font></a></p>  
 <p>63. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>64. Dark-green in colour<br> (From plentiful watering).</p>  
 <p>65. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>66. In them (each) will be<br> Two Springs pouring forth water<br> In continuous abundance:</p>  
 <p>67. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>68. In them will be Fruits,<br> And dates and pomegranates:</p>  
 <p>69. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>70. In them will be<br> Fair (Companions), good,<br> beautiful;—</p>  
 <p>71. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>72. Companions restrained (as to<br> Their glances), in (goodly)<br> pavilions;—</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1482"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1482</font></a></p>  
 <p>73. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>74. Whom no man or Jinn<br> Before them has touched;—</p>  
 <p>75. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?—</p>  
 <p>76. Reclining on green Cushions<br> And rich Carpets of beauty.</p>  
 <p>77. Then which of the favours<br> Of your Lord will ye deny?</p>  
 <p>78. Blessed be the name<br> Of thy Lord,<br> Full of Majesty,<br> Bounty and Honour.</p>
