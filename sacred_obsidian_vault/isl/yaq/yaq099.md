 <p><a name="page_1771"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1771</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Sūra XCIX.</h3>  <h3 align="center"><i>Zilzāl,</i> or The Convulsion.</h3>  
 <p><i>In the name of God, Most Gracious,<br> Most Merciful.</i></p>  
 <p>1. When the Earth is<br> Shaken to her (utmost)<br> convulsion,</p>  
 <p>2. And the Earth throws up<br> Her burdens (from within),</p>  
 <p>3. And man cries (distressed):<br> "What is the matter with her?"—</p>  
 <p>4. On that Day will she<br> Declare her tidings:</p>  
 <p>5. For that thy Lord will<br> Have given her inspiration,</p>  
 <p>6. On that Day will men<br> Proceed in companies sorted out,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1772"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1772</font></a></p>  
 <p>To be shown the Deeds<br> That they (had done).</p>  
 <p>7. Then shall anyone who<br> Has done an atom's weight<br> Of good, see it!</p>  
 <p>8. And anyone who<br> Has done an atom's weight<br> Of evil, shall see it.</p>
