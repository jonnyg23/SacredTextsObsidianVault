 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF EL KÂUTHAR.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(CVIII. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> Verily, we have given thee El KâuTHar <a name="fr_504"></a><a href="#fn_504"><font size="1">3</font></a>; <a name="page_343"><font size="1" color="green">p. 343</font></a><br> So pray to thy Lord and slaughter (victims).<br> Verily, he who hates thee shall be childless <a name="fr_505"></a><a href="#fn_505"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_504"></a><a href="108.htm#fr_504">342:3</a> The word signifies 'abundance.' It is also the name of a river in Paradise.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_505"></a><a href="108.htm#fr_505">343:1</a> This is directed against Âs ibn Wail, who, when Mohammed's son El Qâsim died, called him abtar, which means 'docktailed,' i.e. childless.</p>
