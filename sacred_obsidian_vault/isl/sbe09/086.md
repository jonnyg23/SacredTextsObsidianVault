 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE NIGHT STAR.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(LXXXVI. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>By the heaven and by the night star! And what</p>  
 <p><a name="page_328"><font size="1" color="green">p. 328</font></a></p>  
 <p>shall make thee know what the night star is?--The star of piercing brightness.</p>  
 <p>Verily, every soul has a guardian over it.</p>  
 <p>[5] Then let man look from what he is created: he is created from water poured forth, that comes out from between the loins and the breast bones <a name="fr_478"></a><a href="#fn_478"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>Verily, He is able to send him back again, on the day when the secrets shall be tried, [10] and he shall have no strength nor helper.</p>  
 <p>By the heaven that sends back the rain!</p>  
 <p>And the earth with its sprouting!</p>  
 <p>Verily, it is indeed a distinguishing speech, and it is no frivolity!</p>  
 <p>[15] Verily, they do plot a plot!</p>  
 <p>But I plot my plot too! let the misbelievers bide; do thou then let them bide awhile!</p>
