 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE SMITING.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(CI. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> The smiting!<br> What is the smiting?<br> And what shall make thee know what the smiting is?<br> The day when men shall be like scattered moths; and the mountains shall be like flocks of carded wool!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_340"><font size="1" color="green">p. 340</font></a></p>  
 <p>[5] And as for him whose balance is heavy, he shall be in a well-pleasing life.<br> But as for him whose balance is light, his dwelling shall be the pit of hell <a name="fr_496"></a><a href="#fn_496"><font size="1">1</font></a>.<br> And who shall make thee know what it is?--a burning fire!</p>
