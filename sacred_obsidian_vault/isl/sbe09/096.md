 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF CONGEALED BLOOD <a name="fr_492"></a><a href="#fn_492"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(XCVI. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> READ, in the name of thy Lord!<br> Who created man from congealed blood!<br> Read, for thy Lord is most generous!<br> [5] Who taught the pen!<br> Taught man what he did not know!<br> Nay, verily, man is indeed outrageous at seeing himself get rich!<br> Verily, unto thy Lord is the return!<br> Hast thou considered him who forbids [10] a servant <a name="fr_493"></a><a href="#fn_493"><font size="1">3</font></a> when he prays <a name="fr_494"></a><a href="#fn_494"><font size="1">4</font></a>?</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="green">p. 337</font></a></p>  
 <p>Hast thou considered if he were in guidance or bade piety?<br> Hast thou considered if he said it was a lie, and turned his back?<br> Did he not know that God can see?<br> [15] Nay, surely, if he do not desist we will drag him by the forelock!--the lying sinful forelock!<br> So let him call his counsel: we will call the guards of hell!<br> Nay, obey him not, but adore and draw nigh!</p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_492"></a><a href="096.htm#fr_492">336:2</a> The five opening verses of the chapter are generally allowed to have been the first that were revealed. See Introduction, p. xx, and note 1, idem.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_493"></a><a href="096.htm#fr_493">336:3</a> I.e. Mohammed.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_494"></a><a href="096.htm#fr_494">336:4</a> The allusion is to Abu <i>G</i>ahl, who threatened to set his foot on Mohammed's neck if he caught him in the act of adoration.</p>
