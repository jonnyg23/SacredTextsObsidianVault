 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE BACKBITER.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(CIV. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>Woe to every slanderous backbiter, who collects wealth and counts it.</p>  
 <p>He thinks that his wealth can immortalize him. Not so! he shall be hurled into El ‘Hu<i>t</i>amah! [5] And what shall make thee understand what El ‘Hu<i>t</i>amah <a name="fr_500"></a><a href="#fn_500"><font size="1">1</font></a> is?--the fire of God kindled; which rises above the hearts. Verily, it is an archway over them on long-drawn columns.</p>
