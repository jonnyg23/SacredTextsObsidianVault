 <p><a name="page_332"><font size="1" color="green">p. 332</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE LAND.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(XC. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>I need not swear by the Lord of this land <a name="fr_486"></a><a href="#fn_486"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and thou a dweller in this land <a name="fr_487"></a><a href="#fn_487"><font size="1">2</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>Nor by the begetter and what he begets!</p>  
 <p>We have surely created man in trouble.</p>  
 <p>[5] Does he think that none can do aught against him?</p>  
 <p>He says, 'I have wasted wealth in plenty;' does he think that no one sees him?</p>  
 <p>Have we not made for him two eyes and a tongue, and two lips? [10] and guided him in the two highways? but he will not attempt the steep!</p>  
 <p>And what shall make thee know what the steep is? It is freeing captives, or feeding on the day of famine, [15] an orphan who is akin, or a poor man who lies in the dust; and again (it is) to be of these who believe and encourage each other to patience, and encourage each other to mercy,--these are the fellows of the right <a name="fr_488"></a><a href="#fn_488"><font size="1">3</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>But those who disbelieve in our signs, they are the fellows of the left, [20] for them is fire that closes in!</p>
