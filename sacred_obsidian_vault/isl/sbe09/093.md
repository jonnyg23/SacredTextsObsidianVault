 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE FORENOON.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(XCIII. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>By the forenoon!</p>  
 <p>And the night when it darkens!</p>  
 <p>Thy Lord has not forsaken thee, nor hated thee! and surely the hereafter is better for thee than the former; [5] and in the end thy Lord will give thee, and thou shalt be well pleased!</p>  
 <p>Did He not find thee an orphan, and give thee</p>  
 <p><a name="page_335"><font size="1" color="green">p. 335</font></a></p>  
 <p>shelter? and find thee erring, and guide thee? and find thee poor with a family, and nourish thee?</p>  
 <p>But as for the orphan oppress him not; [10] and as for the beggar drive him not away; and as for the favour of thy Lord discourse thereof.</p>
