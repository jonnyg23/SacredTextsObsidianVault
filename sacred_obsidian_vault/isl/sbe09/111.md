 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF ABU LAHEB <a name="fr_506"></a><a href="#fn_506"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(CXI. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> Abu Laheb's two hands shall perish, and he shall perish! <a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="green">p. 344</font></a><br> His wealth shall not avail him, nor what he has earned!<br> He shall broil in a fire that flames <a name="fr_507"></a><a href="#fn_507"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and his wife carrying faggots!--[5] on her neck a cord of palm fibres.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_506"></a><a href="111.htm#fr_506">343:2</a> See Introduction, p. xxviii. Abu Laheb, 'the father of the flame,' <a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="green">p. 344</font></a> was the nickname of ‘Abd el ’Huzzâ, uncle of Mohammed, and a bitter opponent of Islam.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_507"></a><a href="111.htm#fr_507">344:1</a> A pun upon his name.</p>
