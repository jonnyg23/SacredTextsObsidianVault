 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="green">p. 40</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Opportunities</span></h3>  
 <p>Opportunities move like clouds, or pass rapidly like meteors.</p>  
 <p>Seize a thief before he seizes you.</p>  
 <p>Take advantage of the light of day before the night sets in.</p>  
 <p>Seize on opportunities, for they are either a spoil if improved, or a grief if neglected.</p>  
 <p>Good judgment means a seizure of opportunities.</p>  
 <p>Keep quiet until the occasion presents itself.</p>  
 <p>An action may be good if done at a fitting season, or evil if done at an improper time.</p>  
 <p>Procrastination means evil.</p>  
 <p>Put your bread into the oven while it is hot.</p>  
 <p>If you undertake a work do it speedily.</p>  
 <p>Profit by occasions when they turn up, and do not worry about an evil which has not yet come.</p>  
 <p>Time is a sharp sword—strike with it before you are struck by it.</p>  
 <p>If you have not sown, and see a reaper in the field, you will regret a lost opportunity.</p>
