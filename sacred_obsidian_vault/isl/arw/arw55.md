 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Health</span></h3>  
 <p>Health is a crown on the head of the hale, invisible except to the sick.</p>  
 <p>Sound health is beyond all price.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="green">p. 60</font></a></p>  
 <p>The greatest gift to man is a long healthy life.</p>  
 <p>If there be anything more valuable than life, it is sound health.</p>  
 <p>It is wonderful that the envious see not the blessing of good health.</p>  
 <p>No man appreciates the worth of health until he is afflicted with disease.</p>  
 <p>If your dinner is light, your dreams will be pleasant.</p>  
 <p>So long as the head is free from trouble, the body will be sound.</p>
