 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Forgiving Others</span></h3>  
 <p><i>Koran.</i>    God forgiveth past sins; let men forgive and pardon. Forgive freely. Forgiving others is the nearest thing to piety.<i></i></p>  
 <p><i>Traditions.</i>    He who forgiveth others, God forgiveth him.</p>  
 <p>Be merciful, and you will have mercy; forgive and you will be forgiven.</p>  
 <p><i>Sayings and Proverbs.</i>    Of all things God loveth best forgiveness when one is able to inflict harm, and forbearance when one is angry.</p>  
 <p>The pleasure of forgiving is sweeter than the pleasure of revenge.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="green">p. 18</font></a></p>  
 <p>Forgiveness is perfect when the sin is not remembered.</p>  
 <p>The most wicked of men is he who accepts no apology, covers no sin, and forgives no fault.</p>  
 <p>Small men transgress, great men forgive.</p>  
 <p>A noble man condones and pardons, and when by chance he finds out a sin, he conceals it.</p>  
 <p>A man said to another who had spoken evil of him: "If what you have said be true, may God forgive me; and if false, may He forgive you."</p>
