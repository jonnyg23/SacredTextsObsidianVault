 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anger</span></h3>  
 <p>The first part of anger is madness and the second is regret.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="green">p. 47</font></a></p>  
 <p>Passion and blindness are inseparable mates.</p>  
 <p>Beware of anger, for it ends in the humiliation of apology.</p>  
 <p>Anger leads to all kinds of evil.</p>  
 <p>When you are angry be silent.</p>  
 <p>It is not a trait of noble character to be hasty either in anger or in revenge.</p>  
 <p>The anger of a fool reveals itself in what he says, and the anger of a wise man in what he does.</p>  
 <p>Quarrelsomeness is a contemptible habit.</p>  
 <p>Call not yourself a man so long as you are angry.</p>
