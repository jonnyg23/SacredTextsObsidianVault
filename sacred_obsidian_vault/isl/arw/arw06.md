 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">A Sinner's Cry unto God</span> <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>O Thou who knowest every thought, and hearest every cry,<br> Who art the source of all that is, or ever shall be,<br> Who art the only hope in every trouble,<br> The only help in every plaint and every woe,<br> Whose treasures of bounty and word creative are one,<br> God of all good, hear my prayer!</p>  
 <p>One sole plea I have—my need of Thee;<br> But needing Thee my need is filled.<br> One only resource I have—to stand and knock;<br> And if unheard at Thy mercy-gate, to whom shall I go?</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="green">p. 17</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Whom shall I call, what Name shall I invoke,<br> If Thy needy servant shall in vain Thy bounty seek?<br> But far be it from Thee, God of grace, to refuse a sinner's cry.<br> Too good and gracious art Thou to send me thus away.</p>  
 <p>Contrite, I stand at Thy door,<br> Believing that contrite prayer availeth much with Thee.<br> Suppliant, I stretch forth my hands,<br> And with all my soul look up to Thee.<br> Save me, God, from every ill, and be Thy favour ever mine!</p>
