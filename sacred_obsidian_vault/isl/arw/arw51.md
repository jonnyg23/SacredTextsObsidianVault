 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Neighbours</span></h3>  
 <p>He is a good man who is a good neighbour.</p>  
 <p>No man enters heaven who is a bad neighbour.</p>  
 <p>A good neighbour is he who is not only harmless, but bears harm with patience.</p>  
 <p>Be friends, but do not become neighbours.</p>  
 <p>In social life be as friends, in business as strangers.</p>  
 <p>Prefer a near neighbour to a distant brother.</p>
