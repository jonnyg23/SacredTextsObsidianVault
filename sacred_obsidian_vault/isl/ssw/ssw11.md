 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IN CONDEMNATION OF PRIDE</h3>  
 <p>Beware, O son! that thou dost not become proud,<br> Lest fortune thereby slip from thine hands.<br> Pride on the part of a wise man is not comely;<br> Sad is such demeanour in the case of a prudent person.<br> Pride is the habit of the ignorant,<br> Pride doth not proceed from men of intelligence.<br> Pride caused the fall of Azazil, <a name="fr_7"></a><a href="#fn_7"><font size="1">1</font></a>
 <br> And led to his being ensnared in the prison of the accursed.<br> Whoever is by nature arrogant, <a name="page_37"><font size="1" color="green">p. 37</font></a><br> His head is filled with pride beyond imagination.<br> Pride is the source of adversity,<br> Pride is the nature of the evil-disposed.<br> Since thou knowest about pride, why dost thou pursue it?<br> If thou actest thus, thou doest wrong.</p>
