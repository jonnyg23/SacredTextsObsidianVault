 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">DESCRIPTION OF RECTITUDE</h3>  
 <p>O soul! if thou makest choice of rectitude,<br> Fortune will become to thee an auspicious companion.<br> A wise man doth not turn aside his head from rectitude,<br> Since from rectitude a man's name is exalted.<br> If thou breathest truth at morn<br> Thou wilt avoid the darkness of ignorance.<br> Beware that thou breathest nought save rectitude,<br> Since the right hand holdeth pre-eminence over the left, <a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="green">p. 56</font></a><br> Nought is better in the world than rectitude,<br> For in the rosebud of rectitude there is no thorn.</p>
