 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">DESCRIPTION OF CONTENTMENT</h3>  
 <p>O soul! if thou acquirest contentment,<br> Thou wilt exercise sway in the kingdom of repose.<br> If thou art pinched with the trials of poverty,<br> Then, in the estimation of the wise, wealth is nought.<br> The poor man is not disgraced by poverty,<br> For mankind attain glory from penury. <a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="green">p. 44</font></a><br> Gold and silver are the glory of the rich,<br> But the poor have inward repose.<br> Be not distressed if thou art not wealthy,<br> Since a sovereign cannot extract taxes from the desolate.<br> In all circumstances contentment is preferable.<br> Whoever is born under a lucky star is happy.<br> Enlighten thy soul with the radiance of contentment,<br> If thou hast any token of good fortune.</p>
