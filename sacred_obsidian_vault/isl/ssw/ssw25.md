 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IN CONDEMNATION OF LYING</h3>  
 <p>When a person followeth after unrighteousness,<br> Where will he find deliverance on the day of judgment?<br> Whosoever assumeth the habit of false speaking<br> Hath no splendour for the lamp of his soul.<br> Falsehood putteth a man to shame,<br> Falsehood depriveth a man of dignity.<br> A wise man blusheth at a liar,<br> Since no one esteemeth such a person.<br> O brother! beware that thou speakest not falsely,<br> For a liar is despised and without repute.<br> Nought is worse than unrighteousness;<br> O son! therefrom ariseth loss of fair name.</p>
