 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ON THE EXCELLENCE OF GRATITUDE</h3>  
 <p>Whosoever hath a heart filled with gratitude to God,<br> It is not becoming that he should tie up the tongue of praise.<br> Teach thy soul nought but gratitude to God,<br> For it is necessary to praise the Creator.<br> Thy wealth and possessions are increased by gratitude;<br> Victory entereth thy door, owing to gratitude.<br> Wert thou to show gratitude to God till the day of reckoning,<br> Thou would’st not discharge a thousandth part (of thy duties).<br> Yes I it is best to lisp thy gratitude, <a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="green">p. 54</font></a><br> For gratitude to Him is an ornament to Islam.<br> If thou restrainest not thy tongue from gratitude to God,<br> Thou wilt attain everlasting felicity.</p>
