 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IN CONDEMNATION OF PARSIMONY</h3>  
 <p>Were the spheres to fall to the share of the miser,<br> Were fortune the slave of the miser,<br> Were the wealth of Korah <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">1</font></a>
  in his hand,<br> Were a quarter of the universe subservient to him<br> The miser is not worthy that thou shouldest mention his name.<br> And were fortune to become his servant,<br> Pay no regard to the property of the miser—<br> Speak not of the miser's property and possessions.<br> Were the miser a devotee both on land and sea, <a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="green">p. 34</font></a><br> He would be so it is ordered—unknown in Paradise.<br> Though the miser be rich in possessions,<br> He would suffer distress, as if he were a poor man.<br> Benevolent persons enjoy the fruit of their wealth;<br> The niggardly suffer grief from their gold and silver.</p>
