   <h3 align="center">VII</h3>  <h3 align="center">ANALYSIS OF THE <i>Taqrib</i> OF ABU SHUJA AL-ISPAHANI <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book I</i>. <i>Of Ceremonial Purity</i> (<i>Tahara</i>)</p>  
 <p>1. The water which may be used for ceremonial ablutions.</p>  
 <p>2. Legal materials for utensils; what can be purified and what cannot.</p>  
 <p>3. The use of the toothpick.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_352"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 352</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Description of the different stages of a ceremonial ablution (<i>wudu</i>).</p>  
 <p>5. On cleansing from excrement and its ritual generally.</p>  
 <p>6. The five things which require a fresh <i>wudu</i>.</p>  
 <p>7. The six things which require a complete ablution of the whole body (<i>ghusl</i>) and its ritual.</p>  
 <p>8. The seventeen occasions on which a <i>ghusl</i> is prescribed.</p>  
 <p>9. When it is allowable to wash the inner shoes (<i>khuffs</i>) instead of the feet.</p>  
 <p>10. The conditions and ritual for the use of sand (<i>tayammum</i>) instead of water.</p>  
 <p>11. On uncleannesses (<i>najasat</i>) and how and how far they can be removed.</p>  
 <p>12. On ailments of women; duration of pregnancy and their conditions.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book II. Of Prayer</i></p>  
 <p>1. The times of prayer (<i>salat</i>).</p>  
 <p>2. Upon whom prayer is incumbent, and</p>  
 <p>3. On what occasions.</p>  
 <p>4. The antecedent requirements of prayer.</p>  
 <p>5. The eighteen essential parts of prayer.</p>  
 <p>6. The four things in which the prayer of a woman differs from that of a man.</p>  
 <p>7. The eleven things which nullify prayer.</p>  
 <p>8. A reckoning of the occurrences of certain frequently repeated elements in prayer.</p>  
 <p>9. On omissions in prayer.</p>  
 <p>10. The five occasions on which prayer is not allowable.</p>  
 <p>11. The duty and ritual of congregational prayer.</p>  
 <p>12. The prayer of a traveller.</p>  
 <p>13. The conditions under which congregational prayer is required and those under which it is lawful.</p>  
 <p>14. The requirements in congregational prayer.</p>  
 <p>15. The prayers of the Two Festivals and their ritual.</p>  
 <p>16. The prayers on occasion of an eclipse.</p>  
 <p>17. Prayer for rain.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_353"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 353</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Prayer in presence of the enemy.</p>  
 <p>19. What is forbidden of clothing.</p>  
 <p>20. The ritual of the dead.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book III. Of Rates for the Poor, etc</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The condition of the rate (<i>zakat</i>) and of the rate-payer; what it is levied on and consists of.</p>  
 <p>2. On camels.</p>  
 <p>3. On cattle.</p>  
 <p>4. On sheep.</p>  
 <p>5. How it affects partners.</p>  
 <p>6. On gold and silver.</p>  
 <p>7. On grain-stuff.</p>  
 <p>8. On merchandise.</p>  
 <p>9. The conditions and nature of the rate to be paid at the end of the fast.</p>  
 <p>10. Uses to which the rate may be applied.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book IV. Of the Fast</i></p>  
 <p>1. The conditions for the fast (<i>siyam</i>); its description; what breaks it.</p>  
 <p>2. What is meritorious in fasting; when and for whom it is forbidden; how breaking the fast must be expiated.</p>  
 <p>3. The conditions and nature of religious retreat (<i>i‘tikaf</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book V. Of the Pilgrimage</i></p>  
 <p>1. The conditions of pilgrimaging (<i>hajj</i>); its essentials and other elements.</p>  
 <p>2. The ten things forbidden on pilgrimage.</p>  
 <p>3. The five sacrifices of the pilgrimage.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book VI. Of Barter and Other Business Transactions</i></p>  
 <p>1. Conditions and kinds of barter (<i>bay</i>); what may be bartered and what not.</p>  
 <p>2. Description and conditions of the bargain with payment in advance (<i>salam</i>).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_354"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 354</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Of pledging (<i>rahn</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. Of those who are not to be permitted to administer their own property (<i>hajar as-safih</i>).</p>  
 <p>5. Of bankruptcy and composition and common rights in a highway (<i>sulh</i>).</p>  
 <p>6. The conditions for the transfer of debts and credits (<i>hawala</i>).</p>  
 <p>7. Of security for debts (<i>daman</i>).</p>  
 <p>8. Of personal security for debts (<i>kafala</i>).</p>  
 <p>9. Of partnership (<i>shirka</i>).</p>  
 <p>10. Of agency (<i>wakala</i>).</p>  
 <p>11. Of confession (<i>iqrar</i>).</p>  
 <p>12. Of loans (i‘ara).</p>  
 <p>13. Of illegal seizure and use of property; indemnity for it and its damage (<i>ghasb</i>).</p>  
 <p>14. Of right of pre-emption (<i>shuf‘a</i>).</p>  
 <p>15. The conditions of advancing capital with participation in the profits (<i>qirad</i>).</p>  
 <p>16. Of the letting of date-palms and vines (<i>musaqat</i>).</p>  
 <p>17. Of hiring a thing out (<i>ijara</i>).</p>  
 <p>18. Of reward for return of a thing lost (<i>ja‘ala</i>).</p>  
 <p>19. That land may not be let for a fixed amount of its produce (<i>mukhabara</i>).</p>  
 <p>20. Of irrigation of waste lands (<i>ihya al-mawat</i>).</p>  
 <p>21. Of foundations in mortmain (<i>waqf</i>).</p>  
 <p>22. Of gifts (<i>hiba</i>).</p>  
 <p>23. Of found property (<i>luqta</i>).</p>  
 <p>24. Of foundlings (<i>laqit</i>).</p>  
 <p>25. Of deposits (<i>wadi‘a</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book VII. Of Inheritance and Wills</i></p>  
 <p>1. Of legal heirs (<i>warith</i>).</p>  
 <p>2. The conditions and proportions of inheritance (<i>farida</i>).</p>  
 <p>3. Of legacies (<i>wasiya</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book VIII. Of Marriage and Related Subjects</i></p>  
 <p>1. The conditions of marriage (<i>nikah</i>). What women a man may see and to what extent.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_355"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 355</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. The form of a legal marriage.</p>  
 <p>3. The conditions of asking (<i>khitba</i>) and giving in marriage; whom a man may not marry; conditions for nullity of marriage.</p>  
 <p>4. The settlement (<i>mahr</i>) on a wife by her husband.</p>  
 <p>5. On the wedding feast (<i>walima</i>).</p>  
 <p>6. On the equality of the rights of the wives and the authority of the husband.</p>  
 <p>7. On divorce for incompatibility (<i>khul</i>).</p>  
 <p>8. The forms of divorce (<i>talaq</i>).</p>  
 <p>9. On taking a wife back and the three-fold divorce.</p>  
 <p>10. The oath not to cohabit (<i>ila</i>).</p>  
 <p>11. The temporary separation by the formula, <i>zihar</i> Qur. 58.</p>  
 <p>12. The form of accusation of adultery and the defence (<i>li‘an</i>).</p>  
 <p>13. The period during which a previously married woman cannot remarry (<i>idda</i>).</p>  
 <p>14. Of relations with female slaves.</p>  
 <p>15. The support and behavior of a woman, divorced or a widow; mourning.</p>  
 <p>16. Law of relationship through suckling (<i>irda</i>).</p>  
 <p>17. The support (<i>nafaqa</i>) due to a wife.</p>  
 <p>18. The support due to children and parents, slaves and domestic animals.</p>  
 <p>19. Of the custody of children (<i>hidana</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book IX. Of Crimes of Violence to the Person</i> (<i>jinaya</i>)</p>  
 <p>1. On murder, homicide and chance medley.</p>  
 <p>2. The <i>lax talionis</i> (<i>qisas</i>) for murder, and</p>  
 <p>3. For wounds and mutilations.</p>  
 <p>4. The blood-wit (<i>diya</i>).</p>  
 <p>5. Use of weak evidence in case of murder.</p>  
 <p>6. Personal penance for homicide.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book X. Of Restrictive Ordinances of God</i> (<i>hadd</i>)</p>  
 <p>1. Of fornication (<i>zina</i>) of one who. has been or is married (<i>muhsan</i>), and of one who has not been or is not married.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_356"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 356</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Of accusing of fornication.</p>  
 <p>3. Of drinking wine or any intoxicating drink.</p>  
 <p>4. Of theft.</p>  
 <p>5. Of highway robbery.</p>  
 <p>6. Of killing in defence.</p>  
 <p>7. Of rebelling against a just government.</p>  
 <p>8. Of apostasy.</p>  
 <p>9. Of abandoning the usage of prayer.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book XI. Of the Holy War</i> (<i>jihad</i>)</p>  
 <p>1. The general law of <i>jihad</i>.</p>  
 <p>2. The distribution of booty taken in the field (<i>ghanima</i>).</p>  
 <p>3. The law of the tax on unbelievers (<i>fay</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. The law of the poll-tax on unbelievers (<i>jizya</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book XII. Of Hunting and the Slaughter of Animals</i></p>  
 <p>1. How an animal may be killed in the chase or otherwise.</p>  
 <p>2. What flesh may be eaten.</p>  
 <p>3. The ritual of sacrifice (<i>udhiya</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. The ritual of sacrifice for a child (<i>aqiqa</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book XIII. Of Racing and Shooting with the Bow</i></p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book XIV. Of Oaths and Vows</i> (<i>yamin</i>, <i>nadhr</i>)</p>  
 <p>1. What oaths are allowable and binding; how expiated.</p>  
 <p>2. Lawful and unlawful vows.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book XV. Of Judgments and Evidence</i> (<i>qada</i>, <i>shahada</i>)</p>  
 <p>1. Of the judge (<i>qadi</i>) and court usage.</p>  
 <p>2. The division (<i>qasm</i>) of property held in common.</p>  
 <p>3. Of evidence and oaths.</p>  
 <p>4. The conditions of being a legal witness (<i>adil</i>).</p>  
 <p>5. The difference of claims (<i>haqq</i>), on the part. of God, and on the part of man, and their legal treatment.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_357"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 357</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Book XVI. Of Manumission of Slaves</i></p>  
 <p>1. General conditions of manumission (<i>itq</i>).</p>  
 <p>2. The clientship which follows (<i>wala</i>).</p>  
 <p>3. Of freeing at death (<i>tadbir</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. Of the slave buying his freedom (<i>kitaba</i>).</p>  
 <p>5. Of the slave (<i>umm walad</i>) that has borne a child to her master or to another and of her children.</p>
