 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">XV</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Showing that the purpose of the Moslem's life is to exalt the Word of Allah, and that the Jihád (war against unbelievers), if it be prompted by land-hunger, is unlawful in the religion of Islam</i>.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Imbue</span> thine heart with the tincture of Allah,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1320</font></span> Give honour and glory to Love!<br> The Moslem's nature prevails by means of love:<br> The Moslem, if he be not loving, is an infidel.<br> Upon God depends his seeing and not- seeing,<br> His eating, drinking, and sleeping.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>  
 <p>In his will that which God wills becomes lost<span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1325</font></span><br> How shall a man believe this saying?" <a name="fr_91"></a><a href="#fn_91"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> He encamps in the field of "There is no god but Allah";<br> In the world he is a witness against mankind.<br> His high estate is attested by the Prophet that was sent to men and Jinn<br> By the most truthful of witnesses. <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1330</font></span><br> Leave words and seek that spiritual state,<br> Shed the light of God oer the darkness of works!<br> Albeit clad in kingly robe, live as a dervish,<br> Live wakeful and meditating on God!<br> Whatever thou doest, let it be thine aim therein to draw nigh to God, <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1335</font></span><br> That His glory may be made manifest by thee.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 118</font></a></p>  
 <p>Peace becomes an evil, if its object be aught else;<br> War is good if its object is God.<br> If God be not exalted by our swords,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1340</font></span> War dishonours the people.<br> The holy Sheikh Miyán Mír Walí, <a name="fr_92"></a><a href="#fn_92"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> By the light of whose soul every hidden thing was revealed<br> His feet were firmly planted on the path of Mohammed,<br> He was a flute for the impassioned music of love.<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1345</font></span> His tomb keeps our city safe from harm<br> And causes the beams of true religion to shine on us.<br> Heaven stooped its brow to his threshold,<br> The Emperor of India was one of his disciples. <a name="fr_93"></a><a href="#fn_93"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Now, this monarch had sown the seed of ambition in his heart<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1350</font></span> And was resolved on conquest.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 119</font></a></p>  
 <p>The flames of vain desire were alight in him,<br> He was teaching his sword to ask, "Is there any more?" <a name="fr_94"></a><a href="#fn_94"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> In the Deccan was a great noise of war,<br> His army stood on the battlefield.<br> He went to the Sheikh of heaven-high dignity <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1355</font></span><br> That he might receive his blessing:<br> The Moslem turns from this world to God<br> And strengthens policy with prayer.<br> The Sheikh made no answer to the Emperor's speech,<br> The assembly of dervishes was all ears, <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1360</font></span><br> Until a disciple, in his hand a silver coin,<br> Opened his lips and broke the silence,<br> Saying, "Accept this poor offering from me,<br> O guide of them that have lost the way to God!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_120"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 120</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1365</font></span> My limbs were bathed in sweat of labour<br> Before I put away a dirhem in my skirt."<br> The Sheikh said: "This money ought to be given to our Sultan,<br> Who is a beggar wearing the raiment of a king.<br> Though he holds sway over sun, moon, and stars,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1370</font></span> Our Emperor is the most penniless of mankind.<br> His eye is fixed on the table of strangers,<br> The fire of his hunger hath consumed a whole world.<br> His sword is followed by famine and plague,<br> His culture lays a wide land waste.<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1375</font></span> The folk are crying out because of his indigence,<br> His empty-headedness, and his oppression of the weak.<br> His power is an enemy to all:</p>  
 <p><a name="page_121"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 121</font></a></p>  
 <p>Humankind are the caravan and he the brigand.<br> In his self-delusion and ignorance<br> He calls pillage by the name of empire. <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1380</font></span><br> Both the royal troops and those of the enemy<br> Are cloven in twain by the sword of his hunger.<br> The beggar's hunger consumes his own soul,<br> But the sultan's hunger destroys state and religion.<br> Whoso shall draw the sword for anything except Allah, <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">1385</font></span><br> His sword is sheathed in his own breast."</p>
