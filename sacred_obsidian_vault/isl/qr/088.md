 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, Rodwell edition [1876]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SURA LXXXVIII.--THE OVERSHADOWING [XXXVIII.]</h3>  
 <p align="center">MECCA.--26 Verses</p>  
 <p>In the Name of God, the Compassionate, the Merciful<br> Hath the tidings of the day that shall OVERSHADOW, reached thee?<br> Downcast on that day shall be the countenances of some,<br> Travailing and worn,<br> Burnt at the scorching fire,<br> Made to drink from a fountain fiercely boiling.<br> No food shall they have but the fruit of Darih, <a name="fr_101"></a><a href="#fn_101"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Which shall not fatten, nor appease their hunger.<br> Joyous too, on that day, the countenances of others,<br> Well pleased with their labours past,<br> In a lofty garden:<br> No vain discourse shalt thou hear therein:<br> Therein shall be a gushing fountain,<br> Therein shall be raised couches,<br> And goblets ready placed,<br> And cushions laid in order,<br> And carpets spread forth.<br> Can they not look up to the clouds, how they are created;<br> And to the heaven how it is upraised;<br> And to the mountains how they are rooted;<br> And to the earth how it is outspread?<br> Warn thou then; for thou art a warner only:<br> Thou hast no authority over them:<br> But whoever shall turn back and disbelieve,<br> God shall punish him with the greater punishment.<br> Verily to Us shall they return;<br> Then shall it be Our's to reckon with them.</p>
