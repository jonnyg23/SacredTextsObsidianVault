 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, Rodwell edition [1876]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SURA LXXXVII.--THE MOST HIGH [XXV.]</h3>  
 <p align="center">MECCA.--19 Verses</p>  
 <p>In the Name of God, the Compassionate, the Merciful<br> PRAISE the name of thy Lord THE MOST HIGH,<br> Who hath created and balanced all things,<br> Who hath fixed their destinies and guideth them,<br> Who bringeth forth the pasture,<br> And reduceth it to dusky stubble.<br> We will teach thee to recite the Koran, nor aught shalt thou forget,<br> Save what God pleaseth; for he knoweth alike things manifest and hidden;<br> And we will make easy to thee our easy ways.<br> Warn, therefore, for the warning is profitable:<br> He that feareth God will receive the warning,--<br> And the most reprobate only will turn aside from it,<br> Who shall be exposed to the terrible fire,<br> In which he shall not die, and shall not live.<br> Happy he who is purified by Islam,<br> And who remembereth the name of his Lord and prayeth.<br> But ye prefer this present life,<br> Though the life to come is better and more enduring.<br> This truly is in the Books of old,<br> The Books of Abraham <a name="fr_69"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">1</font></a> and Moses.</p>
