 <a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">HE WHO IS INDIFFERENT TO THE WORLD FINDS A KINGDOM THAT SHALL NOT WANE. (Qur. 20:118)</h1>
 <p>There was an old ascetic in Ba<i><u>s</u></i>râ, none in that age so devout as he. He said, I rise every morning determined to fly from this vile</p>
 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a></p>
 <p>Self. My Self says to me, Come, old man, what wilt thou eat this morning? Make some preparation, come, tell me what I am to eat. I tell him, Death; and leave the subject. Then my Self says to me, What shall I put on? I say, The winding-sheet. Then he questions me, and makes most absurd requests, such as, O thou of blind heart, where dost thou wish to go? I say to him, Silence! to the grave-side; so that perhaps while in rebellion against my Self may draw a breath in freedom from the fear of the night-watchman.</p>
 <p>Honour to him who contemns Self, and does not permit it to stand before him.</p>
