 <a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 99</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">ON CHARITY AND GIFTS.</h1>
 <p>Whatsoever thou hast, relinquish it for the sake of God; for charity is the greater marvel when it comes from beggars. Bestow thy life and soul, for the endeavour of the poor is the best gift of mortal clay; the prince and chief of the family of the cloak was honoured by the Sûra "<i>Does there not come</i>,"--such regard he found with God from those three poor barley-cakes.</p>
