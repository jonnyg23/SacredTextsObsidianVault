 <a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">ON THE ABOVE ALLEGORY.</h1>
 <p>One talks of 'the foot', the other of 'the hand', pushing beyond all limits their foolish words; that other speaks of 'fingers' and 'change</p>
 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a></p>
 <p>of place' and 'descending', and of His coming as an incarnation. Another considers in his science His 'settling himself' and 'throne' and 'couch', and in his folly speaks of 'He sat' and 'He reclined', making of his foolish fancy a bell to tie round his neck. 'His face' says one; 'His feet' another; and no one says to him, 'Where is thy object?' From all this talk there comes altercation, and there results what happened in the case of the blind men and the elephant.</p>
 <p>Exalted be the name of Him who is exempt from 'what' and 'how'! the livers of the prophets have become blood. Reason</p>
 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>
 <p>hamstringed by this saying; the sciences of the learned are folded up. All have come to acknowledge their weakness; woe to him who persists in his folly! Say, It is allegorical; depend not on it, and fly from foolish conceptions. The text of the Qur'ân--we believe it all; and the traditions--we admit the whole of them.</p>
