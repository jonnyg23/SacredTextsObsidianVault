 <a name="page_88"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 88</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">ON DREAMS OF VESSELS AND GARMENTS.</h1>
 <p>An old garment is grief and sorrow; a new garment is great wealth; best of all is a garment that is closely woven, so my master told me. For women, a garment of many colours is a cause of joy and happiness and honour. A red garment brings gladness and the unrestricted enjoyment of a lasting good-fortune. The garment of fear is black; if yellow, it is pain and trouble and sighing; blue clothes are grief, a sorrow heavier than a mountain on the heart. Mantle and cloak are beauty; purse and moneybag are a source of riches.</p>
 <p>A ladder will result in a journey, but one full of danger for the man. A millstone is a trusty man, the chosen one of a house. A snare in a dream is a block in the business in hand. A mirror is a</p>
 <p><a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 89</font></a></p>
 <p>woman; be well on thy guard. Captivity is plainly shown thee by a lock; so by a key thou obtainest thy release.</p>
