 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="green">p. 102</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSES</h3>  <h5 align="center">ADDRESSED TO A KINDRED TRIBE AT VARIANCE WITH THE ONE TO WHICH THE POET BELONGED.</h5>  <h5 align="center">BY ALFADHEL IBN ALABAS.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">WHY thus to passion give the rein?<br>   Why seek your kindred tribe to wrong?<br> Why strive to drag to light again<br>   The fatal feud entombed so long?  
 <p>Think not, if fury ye display,<br>   But equal fury we can deal;<br> Hope not, if wronged, but we repay<br>   Revenge for every wrong we feel.</p>  
 <p>Why thus to passion give the rein?<br>   Why seek the robe of peace to tear?<br> Rash youths, desist! your course restrain;<br>   Or dread the wrath ye blindly dare!</p>  
 <p>Yet friendship we nor ask from foes,<br>   Nor favour hope from you to prove:<br> We loved you not, great Allah knows!<br>   Nor blamed you that ye could not love.</p>  
 <p>To each are different feelings given;<br>   This slights, and that regards his brother:<br> ’Tis ours to live—thanks to kind Heaven—<br>   Hating and hated by each other.</p></div>
