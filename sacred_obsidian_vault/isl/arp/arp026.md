 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Poetry</i>, by W. A. Clouston, [1881], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="green">p. 96</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">ON THE</h5>  <h3 align="center">TOMB OF SAYID.</h3>  <h4 align="center">BY ABD ALMALEC ALHARITHY.</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">BLEST are the tenants of the tomb!<br>   With envy I their lot survey;<br> For Sayid shares the solemn gloom,<br>   And mingles with their mouldering clay.  
 <p>Dear youth! I'm doomed thy loss to mourn,<br>   When gathering ills around combine;<br> And whither now shall Malec turn?<br>   Where look for any help but thine?</p>  
 <p>At this dread moment, when the foe<br>   My life with rage insatiate seeks,<br> In vain I strive to ward the blow—<br>   My buckler falls, my sabre breaks.</p>  
 <p>Upon thy grassy tomb I knelt,<br>   And sought from pain a short relief:<br> Th’ attempt was vain—I only felt<br>   Intenser pangs and livelier grief.</p>  
 <p>The bud of woe, no more represt,<br>   Fed by the tears that drenched it there,<br> Shot forth and filled my labouring breast,<br>   Soon to expand and shed despair.</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="green">p. 97</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">But though of Sayid I'm bereft,<br>   From whom the stream of bounty came,<br> Sayid a nobler meed has left—<br>   Th’ exhaustless heritage of fame.  
 <p>Though mute the lips on which I hung,<br>   Their silence speaks more loud to me<br> Than any voice from mortal tongue:<br>   "What Sayid was, let Malec be!"</p></div>
