 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Poetry</i>, by W. A. Clouston, [1881], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="green">p. 148</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">ON THE</h5>  <h4 align="center">CAPTURE OF JERUSALEM,</h4>  <h5 align="center">IN THE FIRST CRUSADE.</h5>  <h5 align="center">BY ALMODHAFER ALABIWERDY.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">FROM our distended eyeballs flow<br> A mingled stream of tears and blood;<br> Nor care we feel, nor wish we know,<br> But who shall pour the largest flood.  
 <p>But what defence can tears afford?<br> What aid supply in this dread hour?<br> When, kindled by the sparkling sword,<br> War's raging flames the land devour!</p>  
 <p>No more let sleep's seductive charms<br> Upon your torpid souls be shed:<br> A crash like this, such dire alarms,<br> Might burst the slumbers of the dead.</p>  
 <p>Think where your dear companions lie—<br> Survey their fate, and hear their woes:<br> How some through trackless deserts fly,<br> Some in the vulture's maw repose;</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="green">p. 149</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">While some, more wretched still, must bear<br> The tauntings of a Christian's tongue;—<br> Hear this—and blush ye not to wear<br> The silken robe of peace so long?  
 <p>Remember what ensanguined showers<br> The Syrian plains with crimson dyed;<br> And think how many blooming flowers<br> In Syrian forts their beauties hide.</p>  
 <p>Arabian youths! in such a cause<br> Can ye the voice of glory slight?<br> Warriors of Persia! Can ye pause,<br> Or fear to mingle in the fight?</p>  
 <p>If neither piety nor shame<br> Your breasts can warm, your souls can move,<br> Let emulation's bursting flame<br> Wake you to Vengeance and to Love!</p></div>
