 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="green">p. 128</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ON THE VICISSITUDES OF LIFE.</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY THE SAME.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">MORTAL joys, however pure,<br>   Soon their turbid source betray;<br> Mortal bliss, however sure,<br>   Soon must totter and decay.  
 <p>Ye who now, with footsteps keen,<br>   Range through Hope's delusive field,<br> Tell us what the smiling scene<br>   To your ardent grasp can yield?</p>  
 <p>Other youths have oft before<br>   Deemed their joys would never fade,<br> Till themselves were seen no more—<br>   Swept into oblivion's shade.</p>  
 <p>Who, with health and pleasure gay,<br>   E’er his fragile state could know,<br> Were not age and pain to say—<br>   Man is but the child of woe?</p></div>
