 <p><a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="green">p. 119</font></a></p>  
 <p><small>It was Rais’ turn next, who charmed us with this plaintive little dialogue, supposed to pass betwixt himself and a Lady:</small></p>  <h4 align="center">DIALOGUE BY RAIS.</h4>  
 <p align="center">RAIS.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">MAID of sorrow, tell us why<br>   Sad and drooping hangs thy head?<br> Is it grief that bids thee sigh?<br>   Is it sleep that flies thy bed?</div>  
 <p align="center">LADY.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">AH! I mourn no fancied wound;<br>   Pangs too true this heart have wrung,<br> Since the snakes which curl around<br>   Selim's brows my bosom stung.  
 <p>Destined now to keener woes,<br>   I must see the youth depart;<br> He must go, and, as he goes,<br>   Rend at once my bursting heart.</p>  
 <p>Slumber may desert my bed;<br>   ’Tis not slumber's charms I seek:<br> ’Tis the robe of beauty spread<br>   O’er my Selim's rosy cheek.</p></div>
