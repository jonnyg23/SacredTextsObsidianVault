 <p><a name="page_132"><font size="1" color="green">p. 132</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">ON THE</h5>  <h4 align="center">CRUCIFIXION OF EBN BAKIAH.</h4>  <h5 align="center">BY ABU HASSAN ALANBARY.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">WHATE’ER thy fate, in life and death,<br>   Thou’rt doomed <i>above</i> us still to rise,<br> Whilst at a distance far beneath<br>   We view thee with admiring eyes.  
 <p>The gazing crowds still <i>round</i> thee throng,<br>   Still to thy well-known voice repair,<br> As when erewhile thy hallowed tongue<br>   Poured in the mosque the solemn prayer.</p>  
 <p>Still, generous Vizier, we survey<br>   Thine arms <i>extended</i> o’er our head,<br> As lately, in the festive day,<br>   When they were <i>stretched</i> thy gifts to shed.</p>  
 <p>Earth's narrow bound’ries strove in vain<br>   To limit thy aspiring mind;<br> And now we see thy dust disdain<br>   Within her breast to be confined.</p>  
 <p>The earth's too small for one so great;<br>   Another mansion thou shalt have—<br> The clouds shall be thy winding-sheet,<br>   The spacious vault of heaven thy grave.</p></div>
