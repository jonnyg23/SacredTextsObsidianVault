 <p><a name="page_120"><font size="1" color="green">p. 120</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TO A LADY WEEPING.</h3>  <h4 align="center">BY EBN ALRUMI.</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">WHEN I beheld thy blue eye shine<br>   Through the bright drop that Pity drew,<br> I saw beneath those tears of thine<br>   A blue-eyed violet bathed in dew.  
 <p>The violet ever scents the gale,<br>   Its hues adorn the fairest wreath;<br> But sweetest through a dewy veil<br>   Its colours glow, its odours breathe.</p>  
 <p>And thus thy charms in brightness rise:<br>   When Wit and Pleasure round thee play;<br> When Mirth sits smiling in thine eyes,<br>   Who but <i>admires</i> their sprightly ray?<br> But when through Pity's flood they gleam,<br>   Who but must <i>love</i> their softened beam?</p></div>
