 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="green">p. 150</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">TO A LADY,</h4>  <h5 align="center">WHO ACCUSED HER LOVER OF FLATTERY.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">O, Abla, no—when Selim tells<br> Of many an unknown grace that dwells<br>     In Abla's face and mien;<br> When he describes the sense refined<br> That lights thine eye, and fills thy mind,<br>     By thee alone unseen,—  
 <p>’Tis not that, drunk with Love, he sees<br> Ideal charms which only please<br>     Through Passion's partial veil;<br> ’Tis not that Flattery's glozing tongue<br> Hath basely framed an idle song,<br>     But Truth that breathed the tale.</p>  
 <p>Thine eyes unaided ne’er could trace<br> Each opening charm, each varied grace,<br>     That round thy person plays:<br> Some must remain concealed from thee,<br> For Selim's watchful eye to see,<br>     For Selim's tongue to praise.</p>  
 <p>One polished mirror can declare<br> That eye so bright, that face so fair,<br>     That cheek which shames the rose;.<br> But how thy mantle waves behind,<br> How float thy tresses on the wind,<br>     Another only shows.</p></div>
