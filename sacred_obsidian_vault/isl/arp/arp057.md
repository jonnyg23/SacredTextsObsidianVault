 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="green">p. 129</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">TO A DOVE.</h4>  <h5 align="center">BY SERAGE ALWARAK.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">THE Dove, to ease an aching breast,<br>   In piteous murmurs vents her cares;<br> Like me, she sorrows, for, oppressed,<br>   Like me, a load of grief she bears.  
 <p>Her plaints are heard in every wood,<br>   While I would fain conceal my woes:<br> But vain's my wish—the briny flood,<br>   The more I strive, the faster flows.</p>  
 <p>Sure, gentle bird, my drooping heart<br>   Divides the pangs of love with thine;<br> And plaintive murm’rings are thy part,<br>   And silent grief and tears are mine.</p></div>
