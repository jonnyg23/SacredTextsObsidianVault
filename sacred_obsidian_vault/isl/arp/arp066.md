 <p><a name="page_138"><font size="1" color="green">p. 138</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">ON MODERATION IN OUR PLEASURES.</h4>  <h5 align="center">BY ABU ALCASSIM EBN TABATABA.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">HOW oft does passion's grasp destroy<br>   The pleasure that it strives to gain!<br> How soon the thoughtless course of joy<br>   Is doomed to terminate in pain!  
 <p>When Prudence would thy steps delay,<br>   She but restrains to make thee blest;<br> Whate’er from joy she lops away<br>   But heightens and secures the rest.</p>  
 <p>Wouldst thou a trembling flame expand<br>   That hastens in the lamp to die?<br> With careful touch, with sparing hand,<br>   The feeding stream of life supply.</p>  
 <p>But if thy flask profusely sheds<br>   A rushing torrent o’er the blaze,<br> Swift round the sinking flame it spreads,<br>   And kills the fire it fain would raise.</p></div>
