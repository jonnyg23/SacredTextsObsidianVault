 <p><a name="page_168"><font size="1" color="green">p. 168</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">THE INTERVIEW.</h4>  <h5 align="center">A SONG IN THE RHYTHM OF THE ORIGINAL, WITH THE MUSIC ANNEXED.</h5>  
 <p><small>[THE music to this little piece was written down, by a friend, from, the singing of David Zamir, a native of Bagdad, who resided with the translator for some time at Cambridge.]</small></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">DARKNESS closed around, loud the tempest drove,<br> When through yonder glen I saw my lover rove,<br>     Dearest youth!<br> Soon he reached our cot, weary, wet, and cold,<br> But warmth, wine, and I to cheer his spirits strove,<br>     Dearest youth!  
 <p>"How, my love," cried I, "durst thou hither stray<br> Through the gloom, nor fear the ghosts that haunt the grove,<br>     Dearest youth?"<br> "In this heart," said he, "fear no seat can find,<br> When each thought is filled alone with thee and love,<br>     Dearest maid!"</p></div>
