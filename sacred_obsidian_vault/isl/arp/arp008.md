 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">THE GENEALOGY OF THE SEVEN ARABIAN POETS.</font></p>  
 <p>THIS Table is reprinted from the Genealogy prefixed to Sir W. Jones’ translation of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mu‘allaqāt</span>, as given in the tenth volume of the octavo edition of his works. The mode of transliterating Arabic proper names adopted by Sir W. Jones is different from most of the systems in use among modern English Arabists: the letter <i>Qāf</i> he represents by K, and <i>Kāf</i> by C; <i>‘Ayn</i>, for which there is no corresponding letter in our alphabet, and which is now generally represented by [‘] or, when <i>Hamza</i> is not represented, by [’], he seems often to transliterate by <i>aa</i>, or <i>ā</i>; and the strongly aspirated <i>Hā</i>, by <i>hh</i>.</p>  
 <p>It has been remarked, in the first section of the Introduction, that those Arabs who claim to be descended from Ishmael usually trace their genealogies no higher than ‘Adnān, because of the uncertainty of the descents between ‘Adnān and Ishmael. According to Sale, "the most approved series enumerates eight generations between these two persons, in the following order: <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ismael</span>, Kidâr, Hamal, Nabet, Salâmân, al-Homeisa, al-Yasá, Odad, Odd, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Adnan</span>."</p>
