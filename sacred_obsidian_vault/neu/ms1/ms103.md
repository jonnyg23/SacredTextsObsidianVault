 <p><a name="page_xiii"><font size="1" color="green">p. xiii</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE VALUE OF ADDITIONAL LETTERS OF THE ALPHABET</h3>  
 <p><i>c</i> in East Finnish = <i>ts</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>č</i> = Eng. <i>ch</i> in choose.</p>  
 <p><i>ḷ</i> = <i>tl</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>ǰ</i> = Eng. <i>j</i> in joke.</p>  
 <p><i>ṅ</i>, <i>ñ̇</i> = Eng. <i>ng</i> in bring.</p>  
 <p><i>š</i> = Eng. <i>sh</i> in shall.</p>  
 <p><i>ž</i> = French <i>j</i> in <i>jour</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>χ</i> =<i>kh</i> or <i>ch</i> in Sc. loch.</p>  
 <p>Consonants with a dash over or beside them, <i>e.g.</i> <i>b´</i>, <i>c´</i>, <i>d´</i>, are soft and followed by a slight <i>y</i> sound.</p>  
 <p><i>ị</i> is a thick guttural <i>i</i>, the Russian <i>yerŭ</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>y</i> in Finnish words= <i>ü</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>i</i> before another vowel in Russian words = <i>y</i> in yam, yield.</p>
