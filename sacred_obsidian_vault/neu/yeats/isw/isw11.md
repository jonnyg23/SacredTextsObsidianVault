 <h3 align="CENTER">THE PLAYERS ASK FOR A BLESSING ON THE PSALTERIES AND THEMSELVES.</h3>
 <p>Three Voices together<br>
   Hurry to bless the hands that play,<br>
   The mouths that speak, the notes and strings,<br>
   O masters of the glittering town!<br>
   O! lay the shrilly trumpet down,<br>
   Though drunken with the flags that sway<br>
   Over the ramparts and the towers,<br>
   And with the waving of your wings.<br>
 First Voice<br>
   Maybe they linger by the way;<br>
   One gathers up his purple gown;<br>
   One leans and mutters by the wall;<br>
   He dreads the weight of mortal hours.<br>
 Second Voice<br>
   O no, O no, they hurry down<br>
   Like plovers that have heard the call.<br>
 Third Voice<br>
   O, kinsmen of the Three in One, <a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 30</font></a><br>
   O, kinsmen bless the hands that play.<br>
   The notes they waken shall live on<br>
   When all this heavy history's done.<br>
   Our hands, our hands must ebb away.<br>
 Three Voices together<br>
   The proud and careless notes live on<br>
   But bless our hands that ebb away.</p>
