 <p><a name="page_245"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 245</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">AN IRISH AIRMAN FORESEES HIS DEATH</h3>  
 <p>I KNOW that I shall meet my fate<br> Somewhere among the clouds above;<br> Those that I fight I do not hate,<br> Those that I guard I do not love;<br> My country is Kiltartan Cross,<br> My countrymen Kiltartan's poor,<br> No likely end could bring them loss<br> Or leave them happier than before.<br> Nor law, nor duty bade me fight,<br> Nor public men, nor cheering crowds,<br> A lonely impulse of delight<br> Drove to this tumult in the clouds;<br> I balanced all, brought all to mind,<br> The years to come seemed waste of breath,<br> A waste of breath the years behind<br> In balance with this life, this death.</p>
