 <p><a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 87</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE PLAYERS ASK FOR A BLESSING ON THE PSALTERIES AND ON THEMSELVES</h3>  
 <p><i>Three voices together</i>:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>HURRY to bless the hands that play,<br> The mouths that speak, the notes and strings,<br> O masters of the glittering town!<br> O ! lay the shrilly trumpet down,<br> Though drunken with the flags that sway<br> Over the ramparts and the towers,<br> And with the waving of your wings.</dir>  
 <p><i>First voice</i>:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Maybe they linger by the way.<br> One gathers up his purple gown;<br> One leans and mutters by the wall--<br> He dreads the weight of mortal hours.</dir>  
 <p><i>Second voice</i>:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>O no, O no! they hurry down<br> Like plovers that have heard the call.</dir>  
 <p><a name="page_88"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 88</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Third voice</i>:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>O kinsmen of the Three in One,<br> O kinsmen bless the hands that play.<br> The notes they waken shall live on<br> When all this heavy history's done;<br> Our hands, our hands must ebb away.</dir>  
 <p><i>Three voices together</i>:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>The proud and careless notes live on<br> But bless our hands that ebb away.</dir>
