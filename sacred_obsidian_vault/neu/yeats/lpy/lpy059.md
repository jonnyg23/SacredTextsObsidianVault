 <p><a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 146</font></a> <a name="page_147"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 147</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">FROM THE GREEN HELMET AND OTHER POEMS (1912)</h1>  
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 148</font></a> <a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 149</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HIS DREAM</h3>  
 <p>I SWAYED upon the gaudy stern<br> The butt end of a steering oar,<br> And saw wherever I could turn<br> A crowd upon a shore.</p>  
 <p>And though I would have hushed the crowd,<br> There was no mother's son but said,<br> "What is the figure in a shroud<br> Upon a gaudy bed?"</p>  
 <p>And after running at the brim<br> Cried out upon that thing beneath,<br> --It had such dignity of limb--<br> By the sweet name of Death.</p>  
 <p>Though I'd my finger on my lip,<br> What could I but take up the song?<br> And running crowd and gaudy ship<br> Cried out the whole night long,</p>  
 <p>Crying amid the glittering sea,<br> Naming it with ecstatic breath,<br> Because it had such dignity<br> By the sweet name of Death.</p>
