 <p><a name="page_10"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 10</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">A CRADLE SONG</h3>  
 <p>THE Danaan children laugh, in cradles of wrought gold,<br> And clap their hands together, and half close their eyes,<br> For they will ride the North when the ger-eagle flies,<br> With heavy whitening wings, and a heart fallen cold:<br> I kiss my wailing child and press it to my breast,<br> And hear the narrow graves calling my child and me.<br> Desolate winds that cry over the wandering sea;<br> Desolate winds that hover in the flaming West;<br> Desolate winds that beat the doors of Heaven, and beat<br> The doors of Hell and blow there many a whimpering ghost;<br> O heart the winds have shaken; the unappeasable host<br> Is comelier than candles at Mother Mary's feet.</p>
