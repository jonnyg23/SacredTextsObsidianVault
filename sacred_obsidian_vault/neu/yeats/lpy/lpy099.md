 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 221</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE MOUNTAIN TOMB</h3>  
 <p>POUR wine and dance if Manhood still have pride,<br> Bring roses if the rose be yet in bloom;<br> The cataract smokes upon the mountain side,<br> Our Father Rosicross is in his tomb.</p>  
 <p>Pull down the blinds, bring fiddle and clarionet<br> That there be no foot silent in the room<br> Nor mouth from kissing, nor from wine unwet;<br> Our Father Rosicross is in his tomb.</p>  
 <p>In vain, in vain; the cataract still cries<br> The everlasting taper lights the gloom;<br> All wisdom shut into his onyx eyes<br> Our Father Rosicross sleeps in his tomb.</p>
