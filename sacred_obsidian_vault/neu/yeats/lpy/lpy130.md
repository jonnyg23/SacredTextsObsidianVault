 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE HAWK</h3>  
 <p>"CALL down the hawk from the air;<br> Let him be hooded or caged<br> Till the yellow eye has grown mild,<br> For larder and spit are bare,<br> The old cook enraged,<br> The scullion gone wild."</p>  
 <p>"I will not be clapped in a hood,<br> Nor a cage, nor alight upon wrist,<br> Now I have learnt to be proud<br> Hovering over the wood<br> In the broken mist<br> Or tumbling cloud."</p>  
 <p>"What tumbling cloud did you cleave,<br> Yellow-eyed hawk of the mind,<br> Last evening? that I, who had sat<br> Dumfounded before a knave,<br> Should give to my friend<br> A pretence of wit."</p>
