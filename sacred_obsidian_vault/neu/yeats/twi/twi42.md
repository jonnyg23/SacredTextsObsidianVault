 <p><a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="green">p. 235</font></a></p> <h1 align="CENTER">INTO THE TWILIGHT</h1> <i>
 </i><p>Out-worn heart, in a time out-worn,<br> Come clear of the nets of wrong and right;<br> Laugh, heart, again in the gray twilight;<br> Sigh, heart, again in the dew of the morn.<br> Thy mother Eire is always young,<br> Dew ever shining and twilight gray,<br> Though hope fall from thee or love decay<br> Burning in fires of a slanderous tongue.<br> Come, heart, where hill is heaped upon hill,<br> For there the mystical brotherhood<br> Of hollow wood and the hilly wood<br> And the changing moon work out their will.<br> And God stands winding his lonely horn;<br> And Time and World are ever in flight,<br> And love is less kind than the gray twilight,<br> And hope is less dear than the dew of the morn.</p></body>
