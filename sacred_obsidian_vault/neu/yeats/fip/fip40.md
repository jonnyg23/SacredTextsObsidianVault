 <h3 align="CENTER">SONG OF THE GHOST.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER">ALFRED PERCIVAL GRAVES.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>When all were dreaming<br>
   But Pastheen Power,<br>
 A light came streaming<br>
   Beneath her bower: <a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a><br>
 A heavy foot<br>
   At her door delayed,<br>
 A heavy hand<br>
   On the latch was laid.</p>
 <p>"Now who dare venture,<br>
   At this dark hour,<br>
 Unbid to enter<br>
   My maiden bower?"<br>
 "Dear Pastheen, open<br>
   The door to me,<br>
 And your true lover<br>
   You'll surely see."</p>
 <p>"My own true lover,<br>
   So tall and brave,<br>
 Lives exiled over<br>
   The angry wave."<br>
 "Your true love's body<br>
   Lies on the bier,<br>
 His faithful spirit<br>
   Is with you here."</p>
 <p>"His look was cheerful,<br>
   His voice was gay;<br>
 Your speech is fearful,<br>
   Your face is grey;<br>
 And sad and sunken<br>
   Your eye of blue,<br>
 But Patrick, Patrick,<br>
   Alas! 'tis you!"</p>
 <p>Ere dawn was breaking<br>
   She heard below<br>
 The two cocks shaking<br>
   Their wings to crow. <a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a><br>
 "Oh, hush you, hush you,<br>
   Both red and grey,<br>
 Or will you hurry<br>
   My love away.</p>
 <p>"Oh, hush your crowing,<br>
   Both grey and red,<br>
 Or he'll be going<br>
   To join the dead;<br>
 Or, cease from calling<br>
   His ghost to the mould,<br>
 And I'll come crowning<br>
   Your combs with gold."</p>
 <p>When all were dreaming<br>
   But Pastheen Power,<br>
 A light went streaming<br>
   From out her bower,<br>
 And on the morrow,<br>
   When they awoke,<br>
 They knew that sorrow<br>
   Her heart had broke.</p>
