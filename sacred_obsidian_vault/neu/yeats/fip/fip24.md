 <h3 align="CENTER">THE LEPRACAUN; OR FAIRY SHOEMAKER</h3>
 <p align="CENTER">WILLIAM ALLINGHAM</p>
 <p align="CENTER">I.</p>
 <p>
 </p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Little Cowboy, what have you heard,<br>
   Up on the lonely rath's green mound?<br>
 Only the plaintive yellow bird <a name="fr_10"></a><a href="#fn_10"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
   Sighing in sultry fields around,<br>
 Chary, chary, chary, chee-ee!--<br>
 Only the grasshopper and the bee?-- <a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a><br>
     "Tip-tap, rip-rap,<br>
     Tick-a-tack-too!<br>
   Scarlet leather, sewn together,<br>
     This will make a shoe.<br>
   Left, right, pull it tight;<br>
     Summer days are warm;<br>
   Underground in winter,<br>
     Laughing at the storm!<br>
 Lay your ear close to the hill.<br>
 Do you not catch the tiny clamour,<br>
 Busy click of an elfin hammer,<br>
 Voice of the Lepracaun singing shrill<br>
   As he merrily plies his trade?<br>
     He's a span<br>
       And a quarter in height.<br>
 Get him in sight, hold him tight,<br>
       And you're a made<br>
         Man!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">II.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>You watch your cattle the summer day,<br>
 Sup on potatoes, sleep in the hay;<br>
   How would you like to roll in your carriage.<br>
   Look for a duchess's daughter in marriage?<br>
 Seize the Shoemaker--then you may!<br>
     "Big boots a-hunting,<br>
     Sandals in the hall,<br>
   White for a wedding-feast,<br>
     Pink for a ball.<br>
   This way, that way,<br>
     So we make a shoe;<br>
   Getting rich every stitch,<br>
     Tick-tack-too!"<br>
 Nine-and-ninety treasure-crocks<br>
 This keen miser-fairy hath,<br>
 Hid in mountains, woods, and rocks,<br>
 Ruin and round-tow'r, cave and rath,<br>
   And where the cormorants build; <a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 83</font></a><br>
     From times of old<br>
     Guarded by him;<br>
     Each of them fill'd<br>
     Full to the brim<br>
       With gold!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">III.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>I caught him at work one day, myself,<br>
   In the castle-ditch, where foxglove grows,--<br>
 A wrinkled, wizen'd and bearded Elf,<br>
   Spectacles stuck on his pointed nose,<br>
   Silver buckles to his hose,<br>
   Leather apron-shot in his lap--<br>
       "Rip-rap, tip-tap,<br>
       Tick-tack-too!<br>
     (A grasshopper on my cap!<br>
        Away the moth flew!)<br>
     Buskins for a fairy prince,<br>
       Brogues for his son,--<br>
     Pay me well, pay me well,<br>
       When the job is done! "<br>
 The rogue was mine, beyond a doubt.<br>
 I stared at him; he stared at me;<br>
 "Servant, Sir!" "Humph!" says he,<br>
   And pull'd a snuff-box out.<br>
 He took a long pinch, look'd better pleased,<br>
   The queer little Lepracaun;<br>
 Offer'd the box with a whimsical grace,-<br>
 Pouf! he flung the dust in my face,<br>
     And, while I sneezed,<br>
       Was gone!</p>
 <p> </p></dir>
 </dir>
