 <p>This collection of Slavic folktales at first glance appears to
 have all of the usual suspects of European Märchen.
 Evil stepmothers: check; plucky youths overcome impossible odds to marry
 kings' daughter: check; dimwitted peasants given magical gifts: check.
 </p>
 <p>
 What makes this book special are some tales and motifs that hint
 at even older lore.
 Number <a href="sfs37.htm">27</a> is for all intents
 and purposes identical to the Native American 'Earth Diver' creation myth.
 In <a href="sfs63.htm">47</a>, man is created from a drop
 of God's sweat.
 There are two tales of a global flood in <a href="sfs64.htm">48</a> and
 <a href="sfs65.htm">49</a>.
 In <a href="sfs49.htm">36</a> there is a very dark 'Abraham and Isaac' story
 which goes a bit further than the Bible.
 In <a href="sfs67.htm">51</a>, there is a story of a hundred-leaved
 rose bush which resembles images from the Kabbalah and Yogic lore.
 And number <a href="sfs77.htm">59</a> is the tale of St. Patrick in a
 Balkan setting.
 All in all, a great anthology of eastern European folklore.
 </p>
