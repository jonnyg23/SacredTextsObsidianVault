 <p>
 <cite>Child Christopher and Goldilind the Fair</cite>
 was William Morris' retelling
 of the medieval <cite>Lay of Havelock the Dane</cite>.
 Unlike the original, Morris puts more emphasis on the romantic
 side of the story.
 The novel, with its development of a royal character unaware of their
 true status, had an influence on <cite>Prince Caspian</cite> by C.S. Lewis.
 </p>
