 <p>This is the second part of a three part etext of William Morris' epic-length
 poem, <cite>The Earthly Paradise</cite>.
 In this portion, Morris expands his scope from classical mythology
 into the Near East with <cite>The Man who never laughed again</cite>,
 and the Northern realm with the extended <cite>The Lovers of Gudrun</cite>,
 a retelling of an episode from the Icelandic <cite><a href="../../ice/lax/index.htm">Laxdaela Saga</a></cite>, and <cite>East of the Moon, West of the Sun</cite>.
 </p>
 <p>
 Confusingly, this is actually part III of the original edition (the first volume was considered part I and II).
 </p>
