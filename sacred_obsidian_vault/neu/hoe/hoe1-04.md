 <h1 align="center">PART I</h1>
 
 <h2 align="center">THE HERO OF ESTHONIA</h2>
 
 <p>T<small>HE</small> <i>Kalevipoeg</i>, which may be called the national epic of Esthonia, contains the adventures of a mythical hero of gigantic size, who ruled over the country in its days of independence and prosperity. He is always called by his patronymic, Kalevipoeg, or Kalevide, the son of Kalev; and, notwithstanding the great differences between them, he is evidently the Kullervo of the Finnish <i>Kalevala</i>.</p>
 
 <p> The <i>Kalevipoeg</i> consists of twenty cantos and about 19,000 lines; and a fairly complete prose outline of the story is here given, all the tedious lyrical interludes which break its continuity, especially at the beginning of several of the cantos, being entirely omitted. For further
