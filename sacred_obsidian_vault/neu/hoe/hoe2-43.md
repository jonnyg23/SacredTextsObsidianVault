 <h2 align="center">THE WONDERFUL HAYCOCK.<br> (J<small>ANNSEN.</small>)</h2>
 
 <p>O<small>NE</small> autumn evening a girl was going home across a frozen heath, but though she walked fast, she shivered. Presently she was pestered by a moving
 
 <!------------------------page 134------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 134</font></a>
 
 haycock without a band, which pressed upon her so closely that the hay pricked her face. This continued till midnight; but when a cock crew in the village, the haycock vanished, and the girl made her way home exhausted, and died within a week. Since then, the people say that cries for help have been heard from the heath by night. But they are very particular that every haycock shall be tied with a band. If thus secured, no evil spirit can interfere with it.</p>
