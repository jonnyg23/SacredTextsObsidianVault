 <cite>Orlando Furioso</cite>, Ludovico Ariosto's 16th century epic Italian poem,
 is a sequel to <cite>Orlando Innamorato</cite>, an earlier
 work by Matteo Maria Boiardo.
 The titular Orlando is very loosely based on
 <a href="../roland/index.htm">Roland</a>,
 a heroic knight in Charlemagne's court, with admixtures from
 <a href="../eng/index.htm#arthurian">Arthurian</a> and
 other sources.
 Orlando is driven insane with love for the beautful Angelica, and must be
 restored to sanity in time to save Paris from the Moslem armies.
 There are numerous subplots, minor characters, and twists, along with
 many fantasy elements.
 Orlando Furioso was one of several European epic poems written during
 this era, including the <cite><a href="../lus/index.htm">Lusiad</a></cite>,
 and Tasso's <cite>Jerusalem Delivered</cite>,
 typically in the <i>Ottava Rima</i> rhyming scheme.
 Vivaldi and Handel wrote operas about Orlando,
 and <a href="../../chr/milton/index.htm">Milton</a> and Cervantes
 also referenced Ariosto's epic.
