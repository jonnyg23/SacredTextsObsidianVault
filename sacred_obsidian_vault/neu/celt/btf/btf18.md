 <p align="CENTER">Riddles</p>
 <blockquote> </blockquote>
 <blockquote>[170]</blockquote>
 <blockquote>A great great house it is,<br>
 A golden candlestick it is,<br>
 Guess it rightly,<br>
 Let it not go by thee.</blockquote><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <blockquote>Heaven.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>There's a garden that I ken,<br>
 Full of little gentlemen,<br>
 Little caps of blue they wear,<br>
 And green ribbons very fair.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>Flax.</blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote><br>
 I went up the boreen, I went down the boreen,<br>
 I brought the boreen with myself on my back.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>A Ladder.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>He comes to ye amidst the brine<br>
 The butterfly of the sun,<br>
 The man of the coat so blue and fine;<br>
 With red thread his shirt is done.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>Lobster.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>I threw it up as white as snow,<br>
 Like gold on a flag it fell below.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>Egg.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>I ran and I got,<br>
 I sat and I searched,<br>
 If could get it I would not bring it with me,<br>
 And as I got it not I brought it.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>Thorn in the foot.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>You see it come in on the shoulders of men,<br>
 Like a thread of the silk it will leave us again.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>Smoke.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>[171]</blockquote>
 <blockquote>He comes through the <i>lis* </i>to me over the sward,<br>
 The man of the foot that is narrow and hard,<br>
 I would he were running the opposite way,<br>
 For o'er all that are living 'tis he who bears sway.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>The Death.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>In the garden's a castle with hundreds within,<br>
 Yet though stripped to my shirt I would never fit in.</blockquote><dir>
 
 <blockquote>Ant-hill.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote></dir>
 
 <blockquote>From house to house he goes,<br>
 A messenger small and slight,<br>
 And whether it rains or snows,<br>
 He sleeps outside in the night.</blockquote><dir>

 And three feet overhead,<br>
 And the head of the living<br>
 In the mouth of the dead.</blockquote><dir>
