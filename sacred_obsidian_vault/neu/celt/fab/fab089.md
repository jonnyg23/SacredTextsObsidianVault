 <h3 align="CENTER">LXXIV.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">THE CYNGHOGION OF ELAETH.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BLACK BOOK OF CAERMARTHEN XX.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>I. NOW gone are my ardour and liveliness;<br>
 If I have erred, I truly acknowledge it;<br>
 May the Lord not inflict upon me severe pain!</p>
 <p>II. May not the Lord inflict severe pain<br>
 On man for his anger and passion.<br>
 A reprobate of Heaven is reprobate of earth.</p>
 <p>III. Let sinful mortal believe in God,<br>
 And wake at midnight;<br>
 Let him who offends Christ sleep not.</p>
 <p><a name="page_502"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 502</font></a></p>
 <p>IV. Let not a son of man sleep for the sake of the passion<br>
 Of the Son of God, but wake up at the early dawn;<br>
 And he will obtain heaven and forgiveness.</p>
 <p>V. Pardon will he obtain, who will call upon<br>
 God, and despise Him not,<br>
 And heaven the night he dies.</p>
 <p>VI. If a son of man dies without being reconciled<br>
 To God ' for the sins which he has committed,<br>
 It is not well that a soul entered his flesh.</p>
 <p>VII. It is not common for the mischievous to employ himself in converse<br>
 With God, against the day of affliction,<br>
 The bold thinks that he shall not die.<br>
           Now gone--</p>
