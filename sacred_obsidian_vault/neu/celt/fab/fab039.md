 <h3 align="CENTER">XXIII.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BLACK BOOK OF CAERMARTHEN XXXVIII.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>I. SEITHENHIN, stand thou forth,<br>
 And behold the billowy rows;<br>
 The sea has covered the plain of Gwydneu.</p>
 <p>II. Accursed be the damsel,<br>
 Who, after the wailing,<br>
 Let loose the Fountain of Venus, the raging deep.</p>
 <p>III. Accursed be the maiden,<br>
 Who, after the conflict, let loose<br>
 The fountain of Venus, the desolating sea.</p>
 <p>IV. A great cry from the roaring sea arises above the summit of the rampart,<br>
 To-day even to God does the supplication come!<br>
 Common after excess there ensues restraint.</p>
 <p>V. A cry from the roaring sea overpowers me this night,<br>
 And it is not easy to relieve me;<br>
 Common after excess succeeds adversity.</p>
 <p>VI. A cry from the roaring sea comes upon the winds<br>
 The mighty and beneficent God has caused it!<br>
 Common after excess is want.</p>
 <p>VII. A cry from the roaring sea<br>
 Impels me from my resting-place this night;<br>
 Common after excess is far-extending destruction.</p>
 <p>VIII. The grave of Seithenhin the weak-minded<br>
 Between Caer Cenedir and the shore<br>
 Of the great sea and Cinran.</p>
