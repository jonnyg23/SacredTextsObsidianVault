 <h3 align="CENTER">LVIII.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">RED BOOK OF HERGEST XV.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>I. CADWALLAWN, before he came,<br>
 Fought, to our ample satisfaction,<br>
 Fourteen great battles,<br>
 For fairest Prydein,<br>
 And sixty skirmishes.</p>
 <p>II. Cadwallawn encamped on Ceint;<br>
 Birds presaged the troubles of Lloegyr;<br>
 I His hand was open, and honour flowed.</p>
 <p><a name="page_434"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 434</font></a></p>
 <p>III. Cadwallawn encamped on Yddon,<br>
 The fierce affliction of his foes,<br>
 A lion prosperous over the Saxons.</p>
 <p>IV. Cadwallawn the illustrious<br>
 Encamped on Digoll Mount,<br>
 For seven months and seven battles daily.</p>
 <p>V. Cadwallawn encamped on the Havren,<br>
 And on the further side of Dygen,<br>
 And the devourers were burning Meigen.</p>
 <p>VI. Cadwallawn encamped on the Wy,<br>
 The multitude, after passing the water,<br>
 Followed to the battle of shield.</p>
 <p>VII. Cadwallawn encamped by the well<br>
 Of Bedwyr; before soldiers he cherished virtue;<br>
 There Cynon showed how to assert the right.</p>
 <p>VIII. Cadwallawn encamped on the Tav;<br>
 Very numerous I see<br>
 The sharers in the fame of the powerful chief.</p>
 <p>IX. Cadwallawn encamped on the Tawy;<br>
 He had the hand of slaughter in the breach;<br>
 Illustrious was he, eager he sought the conflict.</p>
 <p>X. Cadwallawn encamped beyond the Caer<br>
 Of Caew, with an army urgent in tumult<br>
 A hundred battles, and the breaking of a hundred Caers.</p>
 <p>XI. Cadwallawn encamped on the Cowyn;<br>
 The hand was weary of the rein;<br>
 The men of Lloegyr, numerous their complaints.</p>
 <p><a name="page_435"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 435</font></a></p>
 <p>XII. Cadwallawn encamped this night<br>
 In the extremity of the region of Penvro,<br>
 For refuge to retreat where the difficulty was great.</p>
 <p>XIII. Cadwallawn encamped on the Teivi;<br>
 The blood mixed with the brine;<br>
 The fury of Gwynedd violently raged.</p>
 <p>XIV. Cadwallawn encamped on the river Duffyrdd,<br>
 He made the eagles full:<br>
 After the battle gifts were conferred.</p>
 <p>XV. Cadwallawn encamped, my brother,<br>
 In the upper part of the country of Dunawd;<br>
 His wrath was violent in the gushing fight.</p>
 <p>XVI. Cadwallawn encamped on Menin,<br>
 The lion with a numerous host,<br>
 Great the tumult, extremely harassing to the rear.</p>
 <p>XVII. From the plotting of strangers and iniquitous<br>
 Monks, as the water flows from the fountain,<br>
 Sad and heavy will be the day for Cadwallawn.</p>
 <p>XVIII. The trees have put on the gay robes<br>
 Of summer; let wrath be hastened by fate;<br>
 Let us meet around Elved.</p>
