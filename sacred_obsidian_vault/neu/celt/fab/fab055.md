 <h3 align="CENTER">XL.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BOOK OF TALIESSIN XXXIII.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>IN rest,<br>
 A song I kept.<br>
 Respect and plenty<br>
 And mead I possessed.<br>
 I possessed mead.<br>
 His triumph,<br>
 And fair lands,<br>
 A great wonder.<br>
 And gold and hour,<br>
 10 And hour and treasure,<br>
 And plenty<br>
 And esteem.<br>
 And giving a desire,<br>
 A desire of giving it,<br>
 To encourage me.<br>
 He slays, he plagues,<br>
 He cherishes, he honours, <a name="page_347"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 347</font></a><br>
 He honours, he cherishes,<br>
 He slays before him.<br>
 20 Presence was given<br>
 To the bards of the world.<br>
 Ever certainly<br>
 To thee they say<br>
 According to thy will.<br>
 God hath caused to thee<br>
 The shoulder of kings<br>
 Against despicable fear.<br>
 Incitement of battle<br>
 The protection of a country.<br>
 30 The country protected<br>
 Battle of incitement<br>
 Usual about thee<br>
 The tumult of capering,<br>
 The capering of tumult<br>
 And drinking of ale.<br>
 Ale for the drinking,<br>
 And a fair homestead,<br>
 And beautiful clothing,<br>
 To me has been extended.<br>
 40 The lofty Llwyvenydd,<br>
 And requests open.<br>
 In one dwell<br>
 Great and little.<br>
 Taliessin's song<br>
 Thou comfortest it.<br>
 Thou art the best<br>
 Of those that have heard<br>
 His vehement animosities.<br>
 I also will praise<br>
 50 Thy deeds,<br>
 And until I fail in old age,<a name="page_348"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 348</font></a><br>
 In the sore necessity of death,<br>
 May I not be smiling<br>
 If I praise not Urien.</p>
