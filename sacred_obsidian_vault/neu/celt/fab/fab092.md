 <h3 align="CENTER">LXXVII.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BLACK BOOK OF CAERMARTHEN VI.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>SOUL, since I was made in necessity blameless<br>
 True it is, woe is me that thou shouldst have come to my design,<br>
 Neither for my own sake, nor for death, nor for end, nor for beginning.<br>
 It was with seven faculties that I was thus blessed,<br>
 With seven created beings I was placed for purification;<br>
 I was gleaming fire when I was caused to exist;<br>
 I was dust of the earth, and grief could not reach me;<br>
 I was a high wind, being less evil than good;<br>
 I was a mist on a mountain seeking supplies of stags;<br>
 10 I was blossoms of trees on the face of the earth.<br>
 If the Lord had blessed me, He would have placed me on matter.<br>
           Soul, since I was made--</p>
