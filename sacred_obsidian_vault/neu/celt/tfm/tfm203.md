 <p align="CENTER">VERSES</p>
 <blockquote> </blockquote>
 <blockquote>WRITTEN AT BATH IN 1840, FOR A LITTLE BOY WHO KEPT AN ALBUM, AND WAS A GREAT ADMIRER OF ROBIN HOOD AND HIS MERRY<i> </i>MEN.</blockquote>
 <blockquote><br>
 HAD the kind Muse, young friend, on me <br>
 Her pleasing gifts bestowed,<br>
 And taught to tread of poesy<br>
 The smooth and flowery road;</blockquote>
 <blockquote>Then should the deeds of Robin Hood,<br>
 And Little John, so bold,<br>
 And of the Friar, stout and good,<br>
 In numbers high be told.</blockquote>
 <blockquote>The merry greenwood should resound<br>
 With feats of archery,<br>
 And antlered deer along should bound<br>
 So light and gracefully!</blockquote>
 <blockquote>But vain the hopes: 'gainst Fate's decrees <br>
 To struggle I must cease;<br>
 I only can write histories<br>
 Of England, Rome, and Greece.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote>
