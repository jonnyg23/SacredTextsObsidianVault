 <p> </p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">THE LAY OF THE GREAT FOOL.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER">1--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>TALE of wonder that was heard without lie,<br>
 Of the idiot to whom hosts yield,<br>
 A haughty son who yields not to arms,<br>
 Whose name was the mighty fool.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">2*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>The might of the world he had seized<br>
 In his hands, and it was no rude deed.<br>
 It was not the strength of his blade or his shield,<br>
 But that the mightiest was in his grasp.</p>
 <p><font size="-1">On his falling on a hidden glen,<br>
 Wherein he never before had staid,<br>
 Of loveliest strath and grass and plain<br>
 And sound of the waves 'gainst each bright stone. (See 5.)</font></p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_169"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 169</font></a></p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_16900"></a>
 <img src="img/16900.jpg" alt="ELK.--From a stone in the churchyard of Meigle.--&lt;I&gt;Sculptured Stones of Scotland&lt;/I&gt;, Pl. lxxii. &lt;I&gt;See note&lt;/I&gt; (2)."><br><font size="-3">ELK.--From a stone in the churchyard of Meigle.--<i>Sculptured Stones of Scotland</i>, Pl. lxxii. <i>See note</i> (2).</font></p>Air tachairt a'n gleann diomhair dhò<br>
 Anns nach robh e fos roimh riamh,<br>
 A b' ailte srath a's fear a's fonn,<br>
 Fuain nan tonn ri slios gach leug. (Gairloch 4.)
 
 
 <p><a name="page_170"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 170</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">3*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Lasting long ere we had come,<br>
 Many a chief is beneath his sway;<br>
 Another little tale I'd tell,<br>
 But it there discerned, and it is strange.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">4†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>A day when the mighty fool<br>
 Was in Lochlan's bounds in a magic cloud,<br>
 Himself and one beauteous dame,<br>
 As a woman he beauty sufficed.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">5</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Meeting in a vast shore-glen,<br>
 As a rose ever growing through it,<br>
 Floods, and strife, and grass, and sound,<br>
 Roar of waves on shore of sea.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">6--GILVEEN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"There was seen," said Gilveen young,<br>
 "A gruagach of the yellowest cloak in the way,<br>
 A new coloured vessel in his grasp, <a name="fr_24"></a><a href="#fn_24"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 Like to a cup in which was drink."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">7†--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>It was then that the great hero spoke,<br>
 "Is it an empty flower I see?<br>
 It is, when greatest is my thirst,<br>
 That it's coming were best for health."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">8--GILVEEN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"An earnest entreaty I make to thee,<br>
 Drink not his draught, take not his food,<br>
 Till thou knowest what is the glen<br>
 Wherein thou wert, never before."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_171"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 171</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">3*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>A leantuin fad air dhuine teachd<br>
 S iomadh ceud a tha fu 'smachd;<br>
 Sgeul beag eile dh' innsean ann<br>
 Tuigear thall 's gu bheil e ait.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">4†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Latha do'n Amadan mhor,<br>
 An criochan Lochlann fo cheo draodh,--<br>
 E fhein agus aona mhaca mna;<br>
 Bu leoir a h-ailleachd mar mhnai.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">5</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Tachairt an gleann diomhair, ròdh,<br>
 Mar ròs fas roimhe riamh,<br>
 Sionan, streubh, a's feur, a's fonn,<br>
 Stoirm nan tonn ri stios na léi.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">6</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Chunnacas," arsa Gilmhin og,<br>
 "Gruagach 'san ròd a's buidh brot,<br>
 Soitheach ur, daite 'na dorn <a name="fr_25"></a><a href="#fn_25"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 Coltach ri corn am biodh deoch.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">7†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Sin 'n uair labhair Macabh mor,<br>
 "An e ròs fòs ( <a href="pt325.htm#xref_190-1"><font color="GREEN" size="1">190-1</font></a>) tha mi a reir?<br>
 San uair a's motha mo thart,<br>
 S gu'm b' fhearr a theachd gu beatha."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">8</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Achanaich a dh' iarram's art,<br>
 Na ol a dheoch 's na gabh a bhiadh,<br>
 Gu fiosraich gu dé 'n gleann<br>
 Nach robh thu ann roimhe riamh."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_172"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 172</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">9†--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>After that had been spoken to him,<br>
 Out spoke the Gruagach of the Cup,<br>
 "Be not downcast stalwart youth,<br>
 Be merry and quaff thy draught."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">10--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>In the like commune with him<br>
 Out spoke he, and it was no wise speech.<br>
 He gave a haughty clashing dart,<br>
 And there was no drop in the cup but he drank.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">11</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Away went the Gruagach of the Cup<br>
 Unlucky was the cup to drink,<br>
 The two legs down from the knee<br>
 Were wanting to the mighty fool.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">12--GILVEEN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then it was young Gilveen spoke,<br>
 "Great is this woe has befallen thee,<br>
 Scarce are thy friends in the great world<br>
 Unliked by them is thy want of feet. <a name="fr_26"></a><a href="#fn_26"><font size="1">1</font></a></p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">13--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Hist! now thou Gilveen young,<br>
 Cease thy woe and be thou still,<br>
 No leg shall be under one in the land,<br>
 Or I myself will get my two feet."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">14†--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>There they wended the pair,<br>
 The woman and the hero of heaviest tread,<br>
 Swifter was he on his two knees<br>
 Than six at their swiftness of foot.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">9†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>An deigh sin fhoclachadh dhò<br>
 Gu 'n do bheannaich gruagach a' chuirn,<br>
 "Na bi dubhach oglaich mhoir<br>
 Bi subhuch a's ol do dheoch."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">10</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Air a' chomain chiadhna dhó,<br>
 Labhair esan 's cha b' e 'ghloir ghlic;<br>
 Thug e sitheadh bruaisgneach borb,<br>
 S cha robh braon 's a' chorn nach d' ibh.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">11</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Gu. 'n d' imich gruagach a' chuirn,<br>
 Neo-bhuadhach a' chuirn ri ol;<br>
 An da chois, fo'n ghluin-shios,<br>
 Bha 'dhith an Amadain mhoir.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">12</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Sin 'n uair labhair Gilmhin og,<br>
 "S mor am bron seo thainig ort,<br>
 S tearc do charaid 'san domhan mhor,<br>
 S ni neo-oil leo thu 'bhith gun chois." <a name="fr_27"></a><a href="#fn_27"><font size="1">1</font></a></p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">13</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Uist a nis a Ghilmhin og,<br>
 Sguir ad' bhron, a's bi nad' thosd;<br>
 Cha bhi cas fo fhear as tir<br>
 Neo gheibh mi fhin mo dha chois."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">14†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' imich iad an siud 'nan dis,<br>
 A bhean 'san laoch bu truime trosd;<br>
 Bu luaithe esan air a dha ghluin<br>
 Na seisear air luathas an cos.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">15</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>They heard the hunt in the glen,<br>
 The voice of the hound and music sweet,<br>
 Rapidly chasing the elk ( <a href="pt325.htm#xref_190-2"><font color="GREEN" size="1">190-2</font></a>)<br>
 On the moorland that suited best.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">16</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>On the moorland that suited best<br>
 Was seen the deer from the mountains wild,<br>
 The red cared and very white hound<br>
 Keenly baying upon his track.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">17</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Swiftly he gave a dart,<br>
 That sudden cast with his keen blade,<br>
 There was driven by force of the hero's hand<br>
 The spear through the deer's two sides.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">18--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>They caught hold of the white stag-hound,<br>
 And out of hand put him on leash.<br>
 "Be there making music by me,<br>
 Till one come after thee from the chase."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">19--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>There was seen descending a glen<br>
 A Gruagach in full splendour of gold,<br>
 Hand on blade on his left side,<br>
 And his two spears and his shield in his grasp.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">20</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Certes they asked a tale from him,<br>
 Or what road the stranger used.<br>
 * They took an alternate tale about<br>
 * What was the land where they used to be.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 175</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">15</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Chual iad an fhaghaid 's a' ghleann<br>
 Guth gadhair ann a 's binn ceol<br>
 A' ruith na h-eilid gu than<br>
 Air an fhireach a b' fhearr doigh.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">16</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Air an fhireach a b' fhearr doigh,<br>
 Chunnacas fiadh o bheannaibh borb,<br>
 S gadhar cluas-dearg gle gheal<br>
 A' tabhann gu geur 'na lorg.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">17</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Thug esan sitheadh gu grad;<br>
 An urchair chlis ud le lann geur;<br>
 Thártadh, le neart lamh an laoich,<br>
 An t-sleagh roimh dha thaobh an Fheidh.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">18</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Bheir iad air a' ghadhar bhán,<br>
 S air a laimh gu 'n chuir air eill.<br>
 "Bi tu agam deanadh ciuil<br>
 Gu 'n d' thig fear o 'n iuil a' d' deigh."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">19</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Chunnacas a' tearnadh le gleann<br>
 Gruagach ann lan dearsadh òir<br>
 Lamh air lann air a thaobh cli,<br>
 'S a dha shleagh 's a sgiath 'na dhorn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">20</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' fhoighneachd iad sgeul deth gu beachd,<br>
 Na co 'n ròd a chleachd an aoidh?<br>
 * Gabh iad sgeula dheth mu seach,<br>
 * Ciod i n' tir a'n do chleachd a bhi.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 176</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">21--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Ridire Corcair is my name,<br>
 And on each spot I have victory won.<br>
 I am the Gruagach of the white stag-hound<br>
 That has fallen into thine hand."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">22--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Thou Gruagach of the handsomest mien,<br>
 I will give thee assurance of this<br>
 That Gruagach of the white stag-hound<br>
 Shall not henceforth be said to thee.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">23.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Will't not suffice thee, mighty son,<br>
 A make-weight or two to be in the scale?<br>
 As the whole of the hunt is beneath thy power<br>
 To leave the white stag-hound with me.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">24--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"It is I, indeed, who made the hunt,<br>
 As the idiot is wrathful and fierce,<br>
 Whichever one is of strongest hand,<br>
 His be the white stag-hound and the deer."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">25 <a name="fr_28"></a><a href="#fn_28"><font size="1">1</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Since my stag-hound has fallen to thee,<br>
 And thy feet are awanting,<br>
 Food and clothing take thy desire<br>
 I would give that to thee and thy wife.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">26--GILVEEN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Accept that, said Gilveen young,<br>
 And give the white stag-hound to him,"<br>
 "I'll give him, and a speckled hound,<br>
 And if it pleased thee, a greater thing."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 177</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">21</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Ridire Corcair b'e m' ainm,<br>
 S air gach ball gu 'n d' thug mi buaidh.<br>
 S mise gruagach a' ghadhair bhàn<br>
 A tharladh ann an laimh thu;"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">22</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"A Ghruagach ud a 's ailne dealbh<br>
 Bheir mise 'dhearbhadh sin duit,<br>
 Nach bi gruagach gadhair bain<br>
 As an seo ri ràdh riut."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">23</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Nach fhoghainn leatsa 'Mhice mhoir<br>
 Leatrom no dha 'bhith 'san roinn,<br>
 S an t-seilg uile 'bhith fo d' bhinn,<br>
 S an gadhar bàn a leiginn leam."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">24</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S mise sin a rinn an t-seilg;<br>
 Mar tha'n t-Amadan garg, dian;<br>
 'S ge b' e neach a's treise lamh<br>
 'S leis an gadhar bàn 's am fiadh."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">25</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S o tharlladh mo ghadhar ort,<br>
 Agus do chosan gu d' dhìth,<br>
 Biadh a's aodach, gabh d'a reir,<br>
 Bheirinn dhuit fhein 's do d' mhnaoi.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">26</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Gabh siud," arsa Gilmhin og,<br>
 S an gadhar bán a thoirt dhó."<br>
 "Bheiream agus gadhar breac,<br>
 S na' b' aill leat na bu mhó."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 178</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">27--BAIRD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then wended they on the three,<br>
 Under the guidance of the man.<br>
 He raised in the hollows of his shield<br>
 The sling-shaft ( <a href="pt325.htm#xref_190-3"><font color="GREEN" size="1">190-3</font></a>), and the woman, and the deer.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">28</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then was seen, appearing aside,<br>
 A still city filled with the glitter of gold,<br>
 And there was no hue that eye hath seen<br>
 That was not in plenty the court within.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">29--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then asked the mighty fool,<br>
 "What was the city of gold by the way,<br>
 Of noblest form and most beauteous mien.<br>
 Shall I find out from whence it came?"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">30--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Gold Yellow City is its name,<br>
 From Mount Modest and glens of gloom," <a name="fr_29"></a><a href="#fn_29"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 And there are in it of guileful men,<br>
 But I only and my single dame.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">31†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Yonder glen that thou camest through,<br>
 Full of glamour it is always,<br>
 But little it has taught to me<br>
 But to behold the worth of my dame.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">32†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"A young wife that I found in the tower,<br>
 The sight of an eye no better was,<br>
 Whiter than very snow is her form,<br>
 Gentle her eye and her teeth like a flower."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 179</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">27</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' imich iad, an sin 'nan triuir,<br>
 Anns an iuil a rin am fear;<br>
 Thog e 'n crannagaibh a sgiath<br>
 An crann-tabhuill, 's am fiadh, 's a' bhean.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">28</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Chunnacas a' tighinn ri taobh<br>
 Cathair chaomh 's lan dearsadh oir<br>
 S cha robh dath a chunnaic suil<br>
 Nach robh anns a' chuirt na 's leoir.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">29</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' fhoighneachd an t-Amadan mor,<br>
 "Co i 'chathair oir, ri 'h-iuil,<br>
 A's breagh cruth 's is ailne dreach?<br>
 'S am faigh mi mach co dheth a tùs?"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">30</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Chathair orbhuidh gu 'm b' e 'h-ainm<br>
 O Dhun Tuirbh 's o ghleannta Smol,<br>
 S cha 'n 'eil innt' a dh'fhearaibh fòil<br>
 Ach mise fòs a's m' aona bhean. <a name="fr_30"></a><a href="#fn_30"><font size="1">1</font></a></p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">31</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"An gleann sin roimh d' thainig thu trid<br>
 Lan de dhraodhachd tha e 'ghnath;<br>
 S beag a dh' fhaoghluim e dhomh fhein<br>
 Ach 'bhith 'g amharc beus mo mhná.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">32†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Bean og a fhuair mi 'san tur<br>
 Nach robh amhare sùl na b' fhearr;<br>
 S gile na gach sneachd a corp,<br>
 S mall a rosg, 's a deud mar bhlath."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 180</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">33 *--LADY.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>The dame of exceeding beauty blessed<br>
 The Gruagach so lovely and brown.<br>
 "Who is the lady stately and young,<br>
 Or the big man thou hast yielded to?"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">34 *--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"The mighty Fool is his name,<br>
 And his wife is the young Fairfine;<br>
 The men of the world are at his beck,<br>
 And the yielding to him was mine."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">35 *--LADY.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"I think marvellous what thou say'st,"<br>
 So said the young Fairfine;<br>
 "If the men of the world are at his beck,<br>
 That he'd let his legs go with them."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">36 *--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"I'll give thee my word, oh dame,<br>
 That the men of the world are at his beck<br>
 And were it not glamour of Comain cross,<br>
 He'd not let his legs go with them."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">37</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"And now that I may go to the chase,<br>
 To the ruddy mountains and glens of gloom,<br>
 Do thou watch, my brother of love,<br>
 My house, my wife, and my store of gold!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">38</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"So long as I am without,<br>
 Do thou nor slumber or droop thy head.<br>
 Let never a man within<br>
 Or a man out, if one come in."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_181"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 181</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">33*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Bheannaich a bhean a b' fhearr snuagh<br>
 Do na Gruagach aluinn donn.<br>
 "Co macan steud gheal og<br>

 </dir>

 <dir>
 
 <p>"An t-amadan mor gur e ainm.<br>
 S a Gheilbhinn og gur i 'bhean<br>

 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">35*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S ioghnadh leam na tha thu ag radh,"<br>
 Se labhair a Gheilbhinn og;<br>

 S gu leigeadh e a chasan leo."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">36*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Bheir mise mo bhriathra' bhean<br>
 Fir an domhain gu bheil gu mhein;<br>
 S mar b' e druidheachd Chomain chrois<br>
 Cha leigeadh e a chasan leò."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">37</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Gu'n d' theid mise 'nis a shealg<br>
 A bheanntai dearg 's a ghleanntai smol;<br>
 Mathaich thusa, 'bhrathair ghraidh,<br>
 Mo theach, 's mo mhnai, 's mo chuid oir.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">38</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Cho fad 's gu'm bi mise muigh<br>
 Na deansa lochd 's na crom do cheann<br>
 Na leig duin' idir a steach;<br>
 Na duine 'mach ma thig ann.'</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_182"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 182</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">39--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>They went to the chase the three,<br>
 The dog, and the Gruagach, and the white stag-hound.<br>
 The two fair ones and the great son<br>
 Stayed waiting within the city of gold.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">40--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then outspoke the hero large,<br>
 "Gilveen young, here at my head,<br>
 A heavy sleep is enticing me.<br>
 We did not yield up in the glen."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">41 *--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>He was not long in his sleep<br>
 A Gruagach came in from the way,<br>
 And gave a kiss to the Gruagach's dame,<br>
 And the lady was not ill pleased that he came.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">42†--BARD AND GILVEEN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>The young wife sat beneath his head;<br>
 In her mien she pictured a sun,<br>
 And said she to the stalwart youth,<br>
 "Thou hast slumbered, but not for thy good.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">43</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Thou hast slumbered, but not for thy good.<br>
 There came a mighty warrior in<br>
 And gave a kiss to the Gruagach dame;<br>
 Unlucky it is that the stranger came."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">44--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Up rose the mighty fool,<br>
 To the doorway went he,<br>
 Never struck blacksmith, tinker, or wright<br>
 A door more strongly than the angry wight.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_183"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 183</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">39</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' imich iad a shealg 'nan triuir<br>
 An cu, 's an gruagach, 's an gadhar bán;<br>
 An dithis ban 's am macabh mor<br>
 Dh' fhan 's a' chathair oir ri h-iuil.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">40</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Sin do labhair Macabh mor,<br>
 "Ghilmhin og seo aig mo cheann<br>
 Tha 'n cadal trom 'gam bhuaireadh;<br>
 Ni 'n òbamaid suas 's a' ghleann."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">41*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Cha b' fhada bha e na shuain<br>
 Thainig Gruagach a' ròd a steach<br>
 S do bhean a Ghruagaich thug e pog<br>
 S cha b' fhuathach leis an oigh a theachd.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">42†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Gu'n shuidh an og-bhean fo 'cheann<br>
 Mac samhlaih dealbha i ri grein;<br>
 S thuirt i ris an oglach mhor,<br>
 "Rinn thu suaimhneas, 's cha b' e t' fheum.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">43</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Rinn thu suaimhneas, 's cha b' e t' fheum,<br>
 Thainig gaisgeach treun a steach,<br>
 S do 'n mhnaoi Ghruagaich thug e pog;<br>
 S neo-bhuadhach an aoidh a theachd."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">44</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' eiricn an t-Amadan mor;<br>
 Thun an doruis a ghabh e.<br>
 Cha d' bhuail gobha, ceard, na saor,<br>
 Comhla, 's treise na 'n laoch borb.</p></dir>

 <p align="CENTER">45 *--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Unless I were sound enough<br>
 He had not come in from the road,<br>
 Till comes the Gruagach of the golden doon,<br>
 With my will he goes not out."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">46--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Up rose the warrior straight and brown,<br>
 And the arms were seized by grasp.<br>
 "Leave the doorway, stalwart youth,<br>
 Thou art there instead of right."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">47†--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Thus answered the warrior great<br>
 To the hero of the firm speech,<br>
 "Till he comes, the Gruagach who is out,<br>
 Thou shalt he in or thy head."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">48*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Still will I give my vows,<br>
 Though thou thinkest much of thy speech<br>
 When comes the Gruagach of the golden doon<br>
 He will repay thee for his wife's kiss."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">49 *--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Wilt not suffice thee, thou mighty man,<br>
 Seven vats full of glittering gold,<br>
 Cattle and horses, and untaxed land,<br>
 Plain of the plains and the woman's doon?"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">50*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Thou mayest get that, and to boot,<br>
 My tissue vesture and horse,<br>
 Who's as ready on sea as on land,<br>
 If thou wilt but let me go out."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 185</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">45*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Mur bhithinn a' m' shuain gu leor<br>
 Cha tigeadh e a' rod a steach,<br>
 'S gus an tig Gruagach dun an oir<br>
 Le mo dheoin cha teid e mach."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">46</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Dh' eirich an gaisgeach deas, donn,<br>
 S ghlacadh leis na h-airm 'na dhorn,<br>
 "Fag an dorus oglaich mhoir;<br>
 An aite coir a bhiodh tu ann."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">47†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Air freagairt do Mhacabh mor,<br>
 Air a' ghaisgeach na gloir theann,<br>
 "Gu 'n d' thig an gruagach tha muigh<br>
 Bidh tusa steach no do cheann."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">48*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Bheir mise mo bhriathra fos<br>
 Ge mor leat na bheil thu ag radh;<br>
 Nuair thig Gruagach dun an oir<br>
 Gu'n diol e ort pog a mhna."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">49*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Nach foghnadh leatsa mhic a mhoir<br>
 Seachd lan dabhaich 'a dh'or glan<br>
 Crodh a's eich fearann saor<br>
 Raon nan raon a's dun nam ban."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">50*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Gheibheadh tu sin a's ni 's mó<br>
 Mo chulaidh shroil agus m' each<br>
 S co deas leis muir agus tìr<br>
 A chionn mo ligeadh a mach."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 186</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">51 *--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Still will I give my vows,<br>
 Though thou thinkest much of thy speech<br>
 When comes the Gruagach of the tissue cloak,<br>
 He will repay thee for his wife's kiss."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">52--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"From the Gruagach of the Cup I got<br>
 Thy one foot to let me in;<br>
 I'll blow it beneath thee with joy<br>
 If thou leave the way to go out."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">53--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>With his own magic he blew<br>
 His one foot beneath him as ever it was,<br>
 And said the Gruagach, who was wise,<br>
 "It is time for me now to depart."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">54--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Thus answered the warrior great,<br>
 "A little stay yet for a slow space,<br>
 The other foot for a sturdy step,<br>
 I'll take from thee or thy head."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">55--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>When the hero was in hard straits,<br>
 He suddenly sprang to the breast of his dame;<br>
 "My comeliness I throw upon thee,<br>
 Guard me my feet and my hand."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">56 †--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"If death be a terror to thee,<br>
 For the poor wife of most beauteous mien,<br>
 The other foot thou wouldst give away<br>
 A refuge in that hour for thy head."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 187</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">51 *</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Bheir mise mo bhriathra fos<br>
 Ge mor leat na bheil thu ag radh<br>
 Gu'n tig Gruagach a bhrait shroil<br>
 'S gu'n diol c ort pog a mhna."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">52</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Fhuair mi o ghruagach a' chuirn<br>
 Do leith chos a m' leig a steach;<br>
 Seideam fodhad i gu m' dheoin,<br>
 S ma leigeas an ròd a mach."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">53</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Shéid esan le dhraodhachd fhein<br>
 A leith-chos foidh mar bha i riamh<br>
 S thuirt an gruagach a bha glic,<br>
 "Gur tim dhomh bhith nis a triall."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">54</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Air freagairt do Mhacabh mor,<br>
 "Stad beag fathasd gu fòil mall;<br>
 A chas eile gu ceum cruaidh<br>
 Bheir mise uait no do cheann."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">55†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Nuair a bha 'n gaisgeach an càs cruaidh,<br>
 Duibh leum gu, luath ri uchd na mnai,<br>
 "Tilgidh mi mo chuimrin ( <a href="pt325.htm#xref_190-4"><font color="GREEN" size="1">190-4</font></a>) ort;<br>
 Dion domh mo chosan 's mo lamh."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">56†</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Ma 's h-eagal leat am bàs,<br>
 Do 'n ghraidh-bhean a's ailne dealbh<br>
 A' chas eile bheireadh tu uait;--<br>
 Didean, air an uair, dho d' cheann."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 188</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">57 †--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>He was not allowed to come to words,<br>
 When the great one was upon him,<br>
 And unless t' other foot was given him<br>
 Slice in two! ere he could cry for Fionn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">50 *--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then with his own glamour he put<br>
 His two legs under him as they were,<br>
 And said the Gruagach, who was wise,<br>
 "It is time for me now to begone."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">59--FOOL.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"These feet I have now got below<br>
 I yield not to thee nor to them,<br>
 The day will not come that thou goest out,<br>
 Till comes the Gruagach of the golden doon."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">60--GRUAGACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"I am the Gruagach of the golden doon,<br>
 And great is my boast of thy good will,<br>
 And (I am) the Gruagach of the white stag hound<br>
 That fell into thy hand,</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">61 †</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"And that took these feet off thee<br>
 To try thy courage and thy force;<br>
 I blow them beneath thee again,<br>
 Right as straight as thou wert."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">62 *</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Youth of most beauteous mein,<br>
 I esteem thee great in each thing;<br>
 It was I took thy leg off thee<br>
 To try thy valour and mind."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 189</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">57t</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Cha d' leigeadh gu focail dhò<br>
 Nuair bha 'm fear mor as a chionn;<br>
 'S gun a chos eile thoir dhò,<br>
 Sgiol na dhò ma 'n goirear Fionn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">58*</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Chuir e'n sin lè dhruideachd fein<br>
 A dha chos fodha mar bha, iad riamh<br>
 S thu'irt an Gruagach a bha glic<br>
 "S mithich a nis a bhi triall."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">59</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Na casan seo fhuair mi fodham<br>
 Cha leig mi leat iad na leo.<br>
 Cha d' thig an la a theid thu mach<br>
 Gus an d' thig gruagach Dhun an oir."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">60</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S mise gruagach Dhun an oir,<br>
 S bu mhor mo bhosd as do ruin,<br>
 Agus gruagach gadhair bhàin<br>
 A tharladh ann an lamh thu.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">61 t</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S a thug na cosan ud diot<br>
 A dhearbadh do ghniomh a's do lugh;<br>
 Seideam fodhad iad a rìs<br>
 Cheart cho direach 's a bha thu."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">62 *</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Oganaich is ailte dreach<br>
 Gur mor mo bheach ort 's gach cuis;<br>
 S gur mi bhun do chasan d' iot<br>
 A dhearbhadh do ghniomh 's do thurn."</p></dir>
 </dir>

 <p align="CENTER">63--BARD.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Then they laid hold hand on hand,<br>
 Love on love, and good will on good will,<br>
 One little tale on the dames, <a name="fr_31"></a><a href="#fn_31"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 A wondrous tale heard without a lie.</p></dir>

 <p><font size="-1">(<a name="xref_190-1"><font color="GREEN">190-1</font></a>) Fàs, empty. Ròs, a flower--probably the cap is called a flower.</font></p>
 <p>(<a name="xref_190-2"><font color="GREEN">190-2</font></a>) The word is translated hind, roe, etc. It is the same word as elk, but it means a stag here.</p>
 <p>(<a name="xref_190-3"><font color="GREEN">190-3</font></a>) Crann tabhail is translated "sling" in Armstrong; but tabhail, according to the same authority, is "catapult;" "Crann" is a tree. It seems, then, that the word means some instrument made partly of wood, and used in the chase; and I suspect it means cross-bow. Men are represented on the sculptured stones of Scotland shooting with cross-bows. <i>See</i> <a href="pt310.htm#page_100">p. 100</a>.</p>
 <p>(<a name="xref_190-4"><font color="GREEN">190-4</font></a>) <i>Cuimeir</i>, neat, trim, well-formed, elegance; <i>cuimrin</i>, my elegance, my elegant self.</p>

 <p><font size="-1">CAOGAD, fifty. IBH, to drink.</font></p>
 <p>SIONAN, I do not know this word, unless it is a corruption of sithean, a knoll. STREUBH, I do not know unless it is a strath. The line might be read this way--</p><dir>
 <dir>
