 <h3 align="CENTER">LXXXI.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">THE LAY OF OSGAR.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1">From Donald MacPhie, Smith, Breubhaig, Barra; and others.</font></p>
 <p>IT was said at that time that Eirinn was the better chase than Albainn; that there were many great beamed deer in it, rather than in this Albainn. It was this which used to cause the Fhinn to be so often in Eirinn; but true Albanian Gaul (Scotch highlanders) they were.</p>
 <p>The red-haired Cairbre came in as king over the fifth part of Eirinn, at the southern end. The Fhinn were now over in Eirinn, and Fionn had dwelling-houses in every place in which it was most usual for them to stay.</p>
 <p>The ord Fhiann (hammer of Fionn) was a bell. It was not to be struck but in time of great rejoicing, and in time of bard straits, and it could be heard in the five-fifths of Eirinn.</p>
 <p>They had a house on the land of the red-haired Cairbre, and they came on the northern side of Eirinn to bunt.</p>
 <p>Padruig was asking Oisean--</p>
 <p>"Would their set of arms be on them when they went to hunt?"</p>
 <p>Oisean said to him--</p>
 <p>"Without our armour and our arms;<br>
 We should not go to hunt like that.<br>
 There would be arms, and stout headgear,<br>
 And in each man's grasp were two great spears."</p>
 <p>When the Fhinn went to hunt to the northward, they left Osgar to keep the house, and three hundred of the old warriors with him, for they were heavy for walking. Said Oisean, when he was going to tell the old story to Padruig ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_346-a"><font color="GREEN" size="1">346-a</font></a>).</p>
 <p><a name="page_321"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 321</font></a></p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">LAOIDH OSGAIR.</h3>
 <p>Bha e air a radh 'san am ud gu 'm b'e Eirinn a b' fhearr sealg na Albainn; gu 'n robh moran de dh' fheidh chabrach mhor innte seach an Albainn seo. 'Se sin a bhiodh a' toirt do 'n Fhinn gu 'm biodh iad cho tric an Eirinn; ach 'se fior Ghaidheil Albannach a bha annta.</p>
 <p>Thainig an Cairbre ruadh a stigh 'na righ air a' choigeamh cuid de dh' Eirinn air a' cheann deas. Bha 'n Fhinn 'san am seo thall an Eirinn, 's bha tighean comhnuidh aig Fionn anns a' h-uile h-àite a bu tric leo a bhith stad ann.</p>
 <p>Clag a bha anns an ord Fhiannta. Cha robh e ri 'bhualadh ach an am toil-inntinn mhoir, 'san am eigin chruaidh 's chluinnt' ann an coig choigeamh na h-Eirionn e.</p>
 <p>Bha tigh aca air fearann a' Chairbre ruaidh, 's thainig iad air an taobh mu thuath de dh' Eirinn a shealgaireachd.</p>
 <p>Bha Padruig a foighneachd de dh' Oisean,</p>
 <p>"Am biodh an cuid arm orr' uile nuair a rachadh iad a shealgaireachd?"</p>
 <p>Thuirt Oisean ris,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Gun ar n-eideadh, 's gun ar n-airm,<br>
 Cha rachamaid a shealg mar siud;<br>
 Bhiodh airm, agus ceannabheart chorr,<br>
 S da shleagh mhor an dorn gach fir."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Nuair a chaidh an Fhinn a shealgaireachd do'n taobh tuath dh' fhag iad Osgar a' gleidheadh an tighe 's tri cheud de na seann laoich comhla ris, o bha iad trom gu coiseachd.</p>
 <p>Thuirt Oisean nuair a bha e 'dol a dh' innseadh an t-seanachais do Phadruig. ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_346-a"><font color="GREEN" size="1">346-a</font></a>)</p>
 <p><a name="page_322"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 322</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">1--OISEAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"I will not style my strain, 'my Prince,'<br>
 How sad is Oisean to-night,<br>
 Osgar and the Cairbre stalwart<br>
 Ebb away in strife of corses. ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-1"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-1</font></a>)</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">2</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"The venomous spear in the hand of Cairbre,<br>
 In anger's hour, how baneful was it;<br>
 The raven would utter with fear,<br>
 That with it should be slain the Osgar."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">3--RAVEN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"It is worse," to himself he'd say<br>
 That black raven in his craze,<br>
 "These five to be washing about a trough,<br>
 Than blood of men to be gorging us."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">4--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>    .        .        .        .        .        .<br>
 "Why should our own blood choke us?<br>
 What weakness is on our eyelids?<br>
 That we for such small cause should weep."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">5--WASHERWOMAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"The raven will croak to-morrow early<br>
 Upon thy cheek in the field of battle;<br>
 From the socket thine eye shall be forced;<br>
 Out of this thus much will come."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">________________</p>
 <p>Said one of the old warriors, as she saw the hue of the blood on the water that she had washing the clothes of Osgar.</p>
 <p><a name="page_323"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 323</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">1--OISEAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Cha 'n abair mi, mo thriath, ri m' cheol,<br>
 Cia b' oil le Oisean e nochd;<br>
 Osgar agus Cairbre calma,<br>
 Traghar iad ann an cath cairbhre.( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-1"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-1</font></a>)</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">2</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"An t-sleagh nimhe, 's i 'n laimh Chairbre,<br>
 Gu'n craidhteach i ri uair feirge;<br>
 Theireadh am fitheach ri 'ghiomh,<br>
 Gur h-ann leatha 'mharbht' an t-Osgar."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">3--FITHEACH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S miosa theireadh e ris fhein,--<br>
 Am fitheach dubh 'na mhi-cheill;<br>
 A' choigear a' taiseadh mu ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_346-b"><font color="GREEN" size="1">346-b</font></a>) 'n chlar,<br>
 Ach fuil fir a bhith 'gar tacadh.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">4</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Com' an tacadh ar fuil fhein,<br>
 De ghiamh a th' air ar rasgabh;<br>
 Nuair a chaoineamaid chaol reachdaibh.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">5--BEAN NIGHIDH.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Gairidh am fitheach, moch a maireach,<br>
 Air do ghruaidh-sa anns an araich.<br>
 Cuireader do shuil a gluchd;<br>
 As an sin a thig na h-uiread."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Arsa fear de na sean ii laoich, 's e 'faicinn dath na fala air an uisge a bh' aice a' nigheadh aodach Osgair. ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-2"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-2</font></a>)</p>
 <p><a name="page_324"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 324</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">6</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Surely it is a shroud thou washest<br>
 Red is the look that is upon it,<br>
 But until this day had come,<br>
 That shroud's spaeing was not evil."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">7---OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Thou witch there thy clothes that washest,<br>
 Make for us a sure soothsaying;<br>
 By us shall a man of them fall,<br>
 Ere that we all go to nothing?"</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">8--SHE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Five hundred by thee shall die;<br>
 Wounded by thee the king's self;<br>
 Thus much, and a man of law (cut) off,<br>
 Off the world all that came ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_346-c"><font color="GREEN" size="1">346-c</font></a>)."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">9--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Let him not hear thee, Rasg MacRuaidh,<br>
 Nor one that belongs to his people;<br>
 Let not the Een hear thee this night,<br>
 Lest we be spiritless all."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">10--OISEAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Heard ye of the raid of Een,<br>
 The time he wended to Eirinn;<br>
 There came the fierce Carbre of spears,<br>
 And grasped all Eirinn under sway.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">11</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Away went we with eager hurry,<br>
 As many Feen as were of us;<br>
 We laid our army and our people<br>
 On the northern side of Eirinn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_325"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 325</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">6</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S dearbh gur n-aobh sin tha thu nigheadh,<br>
 S dearg an t-aogasg a tha orra;<br>
 Ach gus an d' thainig an diugh<br>
 An aobh sin cha b' olc a h-inneal.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">7--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"A bhaobh sin, a nigheas t' aodach,<br>
 Dean-sa dhuinne faisneachd chinnteach;<br>
 An tuit aon duine dhiu leinn<br>
 Ma'n d' theid sinn uile do neo-ni?</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">8--ISE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Marbhar leatsa coig ceud,<br>
 A's gonar leat an righ fhein;<br>
 Mar sin a's fear Lagha dheth ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-2"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-2</font></a>)<br>
 Bhar saoghail uile gu 'n d' thainig. ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-3"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-3</font></a>)</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">9--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Na cluinneadh e thu, Rasg MacRuaidh,<br>
 Na duine 'bheanas dh' a shluagh;<br>
 Na cluinneadh an Fhinn thu nochd,<br>
 Ma 'm bi sinn uile gun mhisneach. ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-4"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-4</font></a>)</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">10--OISEAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"An cuala sibhse turas Fhinn<br>
 An uair a ghluais e gu h-Eirinn?<br>
 Thainig an Cairbre sleaghach, garg,<br>
 A's ghlac e Eirinn fo aon smachd.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">11</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Dh' fhalbh sinne le than damhair,--( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-5"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-5</font></a>)<br>
 A lion de'n Fhinn 's a bha dhinn;<br>
 Leagadh leinn ar feachd, 's ar sluagh,<br>
 Air an taobh mu thuath de dh' Eirinn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_326"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 326</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">12</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"There was sent down by Cairbre<br>
 Word for Feene's hardy Osgar,<br>
 To go down to the Feen's carousal,<br>
 And he would get his cess according.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">13</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"He rose who never baulked a foeman,<br>
 The beauteous Osgar to the king's hearth,<br>
 Three hundred stalwart men with him,<br>
 To answer his will and need.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">14</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"We found honour, we found food,<br>
 As we ever before had found;<br>
 We were merrily within,<br>
 With Cairbre in the house of the king."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">15--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Upon the last drinking day,"<br>
 Said the Cairbre with a voice so high,<br>
 "An exchange of spear-shafts I'd like from thee,<br>
 Thou brown Osgar of the Alba."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">16--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"What shaft's exchange wouldst thou wish,<br>
 Thou red-haired Cairbre of the ports of ships?<br>
 Oft were my spear and myself with thee,<br>
 In the day of battle and combat (free)."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">17--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"I'd need no less than cess and kain<br>
 From any warrior your shores within,<br>
 And I'd need no less for my life's term,<br>
 Than to get as I ask for it every arm."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_327"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 327</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">12</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Chuireadh le Cairbre 'nuas<br>
 Fios air Osgar cruaidh na Finne,<br>
 A dhol a dh' ionnsuidh fleadh na Finne,<br>
 S gu'm faigheadh e cis a reir sin.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">13</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Dh' eirich, o nach d' ob e namhaid.<br>
 An t-Osgar aluinn gu Leac Righ;<br>
 Tri cheud fear treun dh' imich leis<br>
 A fhreasdal dh' a thoil 's dh' a fheum.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">14</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Fhuair sinn onair, fhuair sinn biadh,<br>
 Mar a fhuair sinn roimhe riamh;<br>
 Bha sinn gu subhach a steach<br>
 Maille ri Cairbre 'san Teamraidh." ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-6"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-6</font></a>)</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">15--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"An latha mu dheireadh dh' an ol"<br>
 Thuirt an Cairbre le guth mor.<br>
 "Iomlaid croinn sleagh b' aill leam uait<br>
 Osgair dhuinn na h-Albann."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">16--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Gu de an iomlaid croinn a bhiodh ort,<br>
 A Chairbre ruaidh nan long-phort?<br>
 A's tric 'bu leat mi fhein 's mo shleagh<br>
 An latha cath agus comhraig."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">17--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Cha b' uilear leamsa cis a's cain<br>
 Bhar aon seoid a bhiodh 'nar tir;<br>
 S cha b' uilear leam ri m' linn a bhos,<br>
 Gach seud a dh' iarrainn gu 'm faighinn."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_328"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 328</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">18--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"In sooth, there's nor gold nor precious thing,<br>
 That might be asked from us by the king,<br>
 Without dishonour or disgrace,<br>
 That were not thine, oh Tjeearnai's."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">19</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"But exchange of shafts without head's exchange,<br>
 That were unjust to demand from us<br>
 The reason thou hast asked it is,<br>
 That I am without Een and father."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">20--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Although the Feene and thy father<br>
 Were as good as they were ever;<br>
 I'd need no less for my life's term,<br>
 Than to get as I ask for it every arm."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">21--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Were but the Feene and my father<br>
 As well in life as they were ever<br>
 That thou scarcely shouldest win<br>
 Thy dwelling's breadth in Eirinn."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">22--OISEAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Coldness fell on the warrior's keen,<br>
 At hearing the skirmishing;<br>
 There were rough vows bandied there,<br>
 Between the Cairbre and the Osgar."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">23--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>I will give a lasting vow,"<br>
 So would say the red-haired Cairbre,<br>
 That he'd plant the seven-edged spear ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_346-d"><font color="GREEN" size="1">346-d</font></a>)<br>
 Between his reins and his navel.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_329"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 329</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">18--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Cha n-'eil or, na earras, gu flor,<br>
 A dh' iarradh oirnn an righ,<br>
 Gun tair, na tailceas dhuinn e,<br>
 Nach bu leatsa 'thighearnais." ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_344-7"><font color="GREEN" size="1">344-7</font></a>)</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">19</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Ach malairt croinn, gun mhalairt cinn,<br>
 B' eucorach siad iarraidh oirnn;<br>
 S e'm fath mu 'n iarradh tu oirnn e<br>
 Mise bhith gun Fhinn, gun athair."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">20--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Ged a bhiodh an Fhinn a's t' athair<br>
 Co math 's a bha iad riamh 'nam beatha,<br>
 Cha b' uilear leamsa ri m' linn,<br>
 Gach seud a dh' iarrainn gu'm faighainn."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">21--OSGAR.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Na 'm biodh an Fhinn agus m' athair<br>
 Cha math 's bha iad riamh 'nam beatha,<br>
 S teann air am faigheadh tu sin,--<br>
 Leud do thaigh ann an Eirinn."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">22--OISEAN.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Lion fuarrachd na laoch loin<br>
 Ri claistinn na h-iomarbhaidh:--<br>
 Bha briathrin garbha, leith mar leith,<br>
 Eadar an Cairbre 's an t-Osgar."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">23--CAIRBRE.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Bheireamsa briathar buan"<br>
 S e 'theireadh an Cairbre ruadh;<br>
 Gu'n cuireadh e sleagh nan seachd seang ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_346-d"><font color="GREEN" size="1">346-d</font></a>)<br>
 Eadar 'airnean agus 'imleag.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_330"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 330</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">24</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Another vow against that,"<br>
 So would say the Osgar valiant,<br>
 That he'd plant the nine-edged spear<br>
 About the shaping of face and hair.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">RECITER.</p>
 <p>The Cairbre had a place made in a pillar of rock, and there would not be a bit of him out but his face.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">25</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"That lasting vow, then; that lasting vow,"<br>
 So would say the red-haired Cairbre,<br>
 That he would bring chase and sorrow<br>
 To Albainn upon the morrow.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">26</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Another vow against that,"<br>
 So would say the Osgar valiant,<br>
 That he'd plant the nine-edged spear<br>
 About the shaping of face and hair.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">27</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"That night we were without aid,<br>
 Hither and thither about the river<br>
 There was an isthmus in the midst; ( <a href="pt335.htm#xref_347-f"><font color="GREEN" size="1">347-f</font></a>)<br>
 There was a great isthmus betwixt us.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">28</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"An olla was heard with a soft voice,<br>
 On a sweet-toned harp, bewailing death<br>
 Up rose Osgar in heavy wrath,<br>
 And seized his arms in his mighty grasp.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_331"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 331</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">24</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Briathar eile 'n aghaidh sin,"<br>
 S e 'theireadh an t-Osgar calma;<br>
 Gu'n cuireadh e sleagh nan naoi seang<br>
 Mu chumadh fhuilt agus aodainn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">AM FEAR-AITHRIS.</p>
 <p>Bha hit aig a' Chairbre, air a dheanadh ann an carragh creige, 's cha bhiodh mir a mach deth ach an t-aodann.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">25</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Briathar buan sin,--briathar buan,"<br>
 Se 'theireadh an Cairbre ruadh;<br>
 Gu 'n d' thugadh e sealg agus creach,<br>
 Do dh' Albainn an la'r na mhaireach.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">26</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Briathir eile 'n aghaidh sin,"<br>
 Se 'theireadh an t-Osgar calma;<br>
 Gu 'n cuireadh e sleagh nan naoi seang,<br>
 Mu chumadh fhuilt agus aodainn.</p></dir>

 <p align="CENTER">27</p><dir>

 <p>"An oidhche sin duinne gun chobhair<br>
 Thall agus a bhos mu 'n amhainn;<br>

 Bha doirlinn mhor eadaruinn.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">28</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Chualas Olla, le guth tim,<br>
 Air chlarsaich bhinn a' tuireadh bàis.<br>
 Dh' eirich Osgar ann am feirg,-<br>
 S ghlac e 'airm 'na dhornaibh aidh.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_332"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 332</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">29</p><dir>
 <dir>

 <p>(<a name="xref_345-19"><font color="GREEN">345-19</font></a>) These lines are put in the mouth of Oscar by some reciters, and in the version published by MacCallum, which would imply that Oscar's fortitude gave way from the pain of his wounds; but this is altogether inconsistent with the character ascribed to Oscar in all Fenian tales and poems, while, on the contrary, when uttered by Fionn, the loftiest heroism that can be conceived is represented; the steel-encased holly heart overpowered by deep feeling; the stern, indomitable old captain completely subdued by the tears of warm and generous affection.  <p>Part of this poem was recited to me by Patrick Smith, South Boisdale, South Uist, September 17, 1860. The whole of it as written here, excepting a few lines peculiar to Smith's version, was got from Donald MacPhie, smith, Breubhaigh, Barra, October 1, 1860. It seems to be a fragment of a much larger poem, some peculiar ancient drama. The commencement is rather obscure, and it is not easy to make sense of some of the lines. H. ML.</p>

 <p>(<a name="xref_346-b"><font color="GREEN">346-b</font></a>) Moistening, Taiseadh. This seems to refer to some ancient method of soothsaying connected with washing clothes. In broad Scotch it is mentioned in an old song, which I quote from memory-</p><dir>

 <p>My droukit sark sleeve I was waulking,<br>

 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> In <a href="errata.htm#12">Brittany</a> (Foyer Breton, vol. i., 144), the night washerwomen (kannérez-noz) were a troop of ghosts, which appeared on a certain night in November. They washed, they dried, and they sewed the shroud of the dead who yet walk and talk, singing,--</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Till there come Christians' saviour,<br>

 Under the snow and the wind."</p></dir>

 <p>(<a name="xref_346-d"><font color="GREEN">346-d</font></a>) Seang probably refers to the slender, sharp, tough, qualities of a spear.  <p>(<a name="xref_347-g"><font color="GREEN">347-g</font></a>) The geese might float. This, taken literally, is absurd, and is at variance with the spirit of the rest of the poem.
