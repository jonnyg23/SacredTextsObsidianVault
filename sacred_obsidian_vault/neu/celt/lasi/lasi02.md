 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Legends and Stories of Ireland</i>, by Samuel Lover, [1831, 1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Glossary</h1> 
 <p>ALPEEN--A cudgel.</p> 
 <p>BAD SCRAN--Bad food.</p> 
 <p>BAD WIN, BAD CESS--Malediction. Cess is an abbreviation of success.</p> 
 <p>BAITHERSHIN--It may be so.</p> 
 <p>BALLYRAG--To scold.</p> 
 <p>CAUREEN--An old bat. Strictly, a <i>little </i>old hat. <i>Een, </i>in Irish, is diminutive.</p> 
 <p>COLLEEN DHAS--Pretty girl.</p> 
 <p>COMETHER--Corruption, of "Come hither." "Putting his comether" means forcing his acquaintance.</p> 
 <p>GOMMOCH--A simpleton.</p> 
 <p>HARD WORD--Hint.</p> 
 <p>HUNKERS--Haunches.</p> 
 <p>KIMMEENS--Sly tricks.</p> 
 <p>MACHREE--My dear.</p> 
 <p>MAVOURNEEN--My darling.</p> 
 <p>MUSHA!--An exclamation, as "Oh, my!" "Oh, la!"</p> 
 <p>NOGGIN--A small wooden drinking vessel.</p> 
 <p>PHILLELEW--An outcry.</p> 
 <p>SPALPHEEN--A contemptible person.</p> 
 <p>STRAVAIG--To ramble.</p> 
 <p>ULICAN--The funeral cry.</p> 
 <p>WAKE--Watching the body of the departed previously to interment.</p> 
 <p>WEIRASTHRU!--Mary, have pity!</p>
