 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>THE people believed in omens of birds and beasts, fishes and insects, and of men and women. These omens were innumerable, and a few only can be mentioned.</small></p>  
 <p>The fisher would deem it a bad omen to meet a red-haired woman when on his way to fish; and were the woman defective in mind or body, probably the man would return home muttering strong adjectives beneath his breath. On the other <a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="green">p. 179</font></a> hand, it was lucky for a girl to find the red hair of a woman in the nest of certain birds, particularly in the nest of the wheatear.</p>  <div align="center">  <table border="0">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><small>'Gruag ruadh   boirionnaich,<br>   Fiasag liath firionnaich,<br>   Ruth agus rath na leirist<br>   Gheobh an nead a chlacharain.'<br>        [bhigirein</small></p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><small>The red hair of   a woman,<br>   The grey beard of a man,<br>   Are love and luck to the sloven<br>   Who gets them in the nest of the<br>   wheatear.                     [tit.</small></p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>

 <p>MOCH maduinn   Luan,<br>   Chualas meaghal uan,</p>   
 <p>Agus meigead eunaraig,<br>   Seimh am shuidhe crom,</p>   
 <p>Agus cuthag   liath-ghorm,<br>   ’S gun am biadh am bhronn.</p>   
 <p>Feasgar finidh   Mhart,<br>   Chunnas air lic mhin,<br>   Seilicheag shlim, bhan,</p>   
 <p>Agus an   clacharan fionn<br>   Air barr a gharraidh toll,</p>   
 <p>Searrach seann   larach<br>   Spagail ’s a chula rium.</p>   
 <p>Dh’ aithnich   mi fein ’n an deigh<br>   Nach eireadh a bhliadhna liom.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>EARLY on the   morning of Monday,<br>   I heard the bleating of a lamb,</p>   
 <p>And the   kid-like cry of snipe,<br>   While gently sitting bent,</p>   
 <p>And the   grey-blue cuckoo,<br>   And no food on my stomach.</p>   
 <p>On the fair   evening of Tuesday,<br>   I saw on the smooth stone,<br>   The snail slimy, pale,</p>   
 <p>And the ashy   wheatear<br>   On the top of the dyke of holes,</p>   
 <p>The foal of the   old mare<br>   Of sprauchly gait and its back to me.</p>   
 <p>And I knew from   these<br>   That the year would not go well with me.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
