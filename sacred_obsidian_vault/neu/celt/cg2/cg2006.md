 <p>EOLAS a rinn   Gille-Caluim<br>   A dh’ aona bho na caillich,<br>   Air ruaidh, air chruaidh, air chradh,<br>   Air at, air pat, air mam,<br>   Air dhair, air chairr, air bhleoghan,<br>   Air tri corracha crith,<br>   Air tri corracha cnamh,<br>   Air tri corracha creothail,<br>   Na ob e do bhruid,<br>   Na diult e do mhne,<br>   Na tar e ’s an Domhnach.<br>   Eolas a rinn Fionn fial,<br>   Da dhearbh phiuthair,<br>   Air ruaidh, air chruaidh,<br>   Air at ciche.</p>   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THE charm made   by Gillecaluim,<br>   On the one cow of the carlin,<br>   For rose, for hardness, for pain,<br>   For swelling, for lump, for growth,<br>   For uzzening, for udder, for milking,<br>   For the three 'corracha crith,'<br>   For the three 'corracha cnamh,'<br>   For the three 'corracha creothail,'<br>   Do not deny it to beast,<br>   Do not refuse it to wife,<br>   Do not withhold it on Sunday.<br>   The charm made of generous Fionn,<br>   To his very sister,<br>   For rose, for hardness,<br>   For swelling of breast.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
