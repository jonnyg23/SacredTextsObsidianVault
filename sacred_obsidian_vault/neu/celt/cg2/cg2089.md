 <p>MOCH La Luan   Casg,<br>   Chunna mi air sal<br>   Lach is eala bhan<br>        A snamh le cheile.</p>   
 <p>Chuala mi   Di-mart<br>   Eunarag nan trath,<br>   Meannanaich ’s an ard<br>        ’S ag eigheach.</p>   
 <p>Di-ciadain bha   mi<br>   Buain na feamain-chir,<br>   Is chunna mi na tri<br>        Ri eirigh.</p>   
 <p>Dh’   aithnich mi air ball<br>   Gun robh an imirig ann,<br>   Beannachd nach biodh ann<br>        An deigh sin.</p>   
 <p>Comraig Bhride   bhith,<br>   Comraig Mhoire mhin,<br>   Comraig Mhicheil mhil,<br>   Dhomh fhi’ ’s dha m’ eudail,<br>        Dhomh fhi’ ’s dha m’ eudail.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>EARLY on the   day of Easter Monday,<br>   I saw on the brine<br>   A duck and a white swan<br>        Swim together.</p>   
 <p>I heard on   Tuesday<br>   The snipe of the seasons,<br>   Bleating on high<br>        And calling.</p>   
 <p>On Wednesday I   had been<br>   Cutting the channelled fucus,<br>   And then saw I the three<br>        Arising.</p>   
 <p>I knew   immediately<br>   That a flitting there was,<br>   Blessing there would not be<br>        After that.</p>   
 <p>The girth of   Bride calm,<br>   The girth of Mary mild,<br>   The girth of Michael strong,<br>   Upon me and mine,<br>        Upon me and mine.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
