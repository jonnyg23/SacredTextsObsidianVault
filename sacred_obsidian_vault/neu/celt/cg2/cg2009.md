 <p>RANN a rinn   ban-naomh Bride<br>   Dh’ an mharaiche chrubach,<br>   Air ghlun, air lug, air chuagas,<br>   Air na naodh galara gith, air na tri galara cuara,<br>   Na ob e do bhruid, na diult e do mhne.</p>   
 <p>Chaidh Criosd   air each,<br>   Bhrist each a chas,<br>   Chaidh Criosd a bhan,<br>   Rinn e slan a chas.</p>   
 <p>Mar a shlanuich   Criosd sin,<br>   Gun slanuich Criosd seo,<br>   Agus na ’s mo na seo,<br>   Ma ’s e thoil a dheanamh.</p>   
 <p>An t-eolas a   rinn Calum-cille,<br>   Air eorlain a ghlinne,<br>   Do sgocha feithe, do leum cnamha--<br>   Tha thu tinn an diugh, bithidh thu slan am maireach.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THE rune made   by the holy maiden Bride<br>   To the lame mariner,<br>   For knee, for crookedness, for crippleness,<br>   For the nine painful diseases, for the three venomous diseases,<br>   Refuse it not to beast, deny it not to dame.</p>   
 <p>Christ went on   a horse,<br>   A horse broke his leg,<br>   Christ went down,<br>   He made whole the leg.</p>   
 <p>As Christ made whole   that,<br>   May Christ make whole this,<br>   And more than this,<br>   If it be His will so to do.</p>   
 <p>The charm made   by Columba,<br>   On the bottom of the glen,<br>   For bursting of vein, for dislocation of bone--<br>   Thou art ill to-day, thou shalt be well to-morrow.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
