 <p>CHUALA mi guth   binn nan eala,<br>   Ann an dealachadh nan trath,<br>   Glugalaich air sgiathaibh siubhlach,<br>        Cur nan cura dhiubh gu h-ard.</p>   
 <p>Ghrad sheas mi,   cha d’ rinn mi gluasad,<br>   Suil dh’an tug mi bhuam co bha<br>   Deanamh iuil air an toiseach?<br>        Righinn an t-sonais an eala bhan.</p>   
 <p>Bha seo air   feasgar Di-aona,<br>   Bha mo smaontan air Di-mart--<br>   Chaill mi mo chuid ’s mo dhaona<br>        Bliadhn o’n Aona sin gu brath.</p>   
 <p>Ma chi thu eala   air Di-aona,<br>   Moch ’s a mhaduinn fhaoilidh, agh,<br>   Bidh cinneas air do chuid ’s do dhaona,<br>        Do bhuar cha chaochail a ghnath.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>I HEARD the   sweet voice of the swans,<br>   At the parting of night and day,<br>   Gurgling on the wings of travelling,<br>        Pouring forth their strength on high.</p>   
 <p>I quickly stood   me, nor made I move,<br>   A look which I gave from me forth<br>   Who should be guiding in front?<br>        The queen of luck, the white swan.</p>   
 <p>This was on the   evening of Friday,<br>   My thoughts were of the Tuesday--<br>   I lost my means and my kinsfolk<br>        A year from that Friday for ever.</p>   
 <p>Shouldst thou   see a swan on Friday,<br>   In the joyous morning dawn,<br>   There shall be increase on thy means and thy kin,<br>        Nor shall thy flocks be always dying.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
