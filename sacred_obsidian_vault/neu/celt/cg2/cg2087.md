 <p>SIN ’d   uair labhair a bhean bhorb--<br>   'Is iad na coirb a rinn mo chreach,<br>   Cuir am breugaire sios fo lorg,<br>   ’S bidh do bheatha nios dha m’ theach.</p>   
 <p>An coileach sin   agad ’s a phoit,<br>   Air a phronnadh cho broit ri cal,<br>   Cha teid am breugadair an sloc<br>   Gon an goir e air an sparr.'</p>   
 <p>Chair an   coileach air an sparr,<br>   Chairich e dha sgiath r’a chorp,<br>   Ghoir e ann gu blasdar, binn,<br>   Is thainig mo Righ bho ’n chroibh.</p>   
 <p>An dream nach   miannach le Dia<br>   Luchd nam breug is luchd nam mionn;<br>   B’ annsa leis an urnuigh fhior<br>   Is li nan rosg a ruith gu teann.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>IT was then   spoke the rude woman--<br>   'It was the wicked who made my ruin,<br>   Drive the liar down below the beam,<br>   And thou shalt be welcome to my house.</p>   
 <p>That cock thou   hast in the pot,<br>   Chopped as broken as the kail,<br>   The liar shall not go to the pit<br>   Till he shall crow upon the spar.'</p>   
 <p>The cock went   upon the spar,<br>   He placed his two wings to his body,<br>   He crew sweetly, melodiously,<br>   And my King came from the tree.</p>   
 <p>The people not   liked of God<br>   Are those who lie and those who swear;<br>   Rather would He have the genuine prayer<br>   And water from the eyelids flowing swiftly.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
