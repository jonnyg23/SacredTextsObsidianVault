 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>THIS and other poems were obtained from Isabella Chisholm, a travelling tinker. Though old, Isabella Chisholm was still tall and straight, fine-featured, and fresh-complexioned. She was endowed with personal attraction, mental ability, and astute diplomacy of no common order. Her father, John Chisholm, is said to have been a 'pious, prayerful man'--terms not usually applied to his class. Isabella <a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="green">p. 155</font></a> Chisholm had none of the swarthy skin and far-away look of the ordinary gipsy. But she had the gipsy habits and the gipsy language, variously called 'Cant,' 'Shelta,' 'Romany,' with rich fluent Gaelic and English. She had many curious spells, runes, and hymns, that would have enriched Gaelic literature, and many rare words and phrases and expressions that would have improved the Gaelic dictionary.</small></p>

 <p>ULC a dhean mo   lochd<br>   Gun gabh e ’n galar gluc gloc,<br>   Guirneanach, gioirneanach, guairneach,<br>   Gaornanach, garnanach, gruam.</p>   
 <p>Gum bu cruaidhe   c na chlach,<br>   Gum bu duibhe e na ’n gual,<br>   Gum bu luaithe e na ’n lach,<br>   Gum bu truime e na ’n luaidh.</p>   
 <p>Gum bu gointe,   gointe, geuire, gairbhe, guiniche e,<br>   Na’n cuilionn cruaidh cnea-chridheach,<br>   Gum bu gairge e na’n salann sion, sionn, searbh, sailte,<br>   Seachd seachd uair.</p>   
 <p>A turabal a   null,<br>   A tarabal a nall,<br>   A treosdail a sios,<br>   A dreochail a suas,</p>   
 <p>A breochail a   muigh,<br>   A geochail a staigh,<br>   Dol a mach minic,<br>   Tighinn a steach ainmic.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THE wicked who   would do me harm<br>   May he take the [throat] disease,<br>   Globularly, spirally, circularly,<br>   Fluxy, pellety, horny-grim.</p>   
 <p>Be it harder   than the stone,<br>   Be it blacker than the coal,<br>   Be it swifter than the duck,<br>   Be it heavier than the lead.</p>   
 <p>Be it fiercer,   fiercer, sharper, harsher, more malignant,<br>   Than the hard, wound-quivering holly,<br>   Be it sourer than the sained, lustrous, bitter, salt salt,<br>   Seven seven times.</p>   
 <p>Oscillating   thither,<br>   Undulating hither,<br>   Staggering downwards,<br>   Floundering upwards.</p>   
 <p>Drivelling   outwards,<br>   Snivelling inwards,<br>   Oft hurrying out,<br>   Seldom coming in.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">

 <p>Sop an luib   gach laimhe,<br>   Cas an cois gach cailbhe,<br>   Lurg am bun gach ursann,<br>   Sput ga chur ’s ga chairbinn.</p>   
 <p>Gearrach fhala   le cridhe, le crutha, le cnamha,<br>   Le gruthan, le sgumhan, le sgamha,<br>   Agus sgrudadh cuisil, ugan is arna,<br>   Dha mo luchd-tair agus tuaileis.</p>   
 <p>An ainm Dhia   nam feart,<br>   A shiab uam gach olc,<br>   ’S a dhion mi le neart,<br>   Bho lion mo luchd-freachd<br>        Agus fuathachd.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>A wisp the   portion of each hand,<br>   A foot in the base of each pillar,<br>   A leg the prop of each jamb,<br>   A flux driving and dragging him.</p>   
 <p>A dysentery of   blood from heart, from form, from bones,<br>   From the liver, from the lobe, from the lungs,<br>   And a searching of veins, of throat, and of kidneys,<br>   To my contemners and traducers.</p>   
 <p>In name of the   God of might,<br>   Who warded from me every evil,<br>   And who shielded me in strength,<br>   From the net of my breakers<br>        And destroyers.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
