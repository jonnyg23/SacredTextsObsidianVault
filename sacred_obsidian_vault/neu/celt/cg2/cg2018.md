 <p>SIAN a chuir   Bride nam buadh,<br>   M’a mise, m’a cire, m’a buar,<br>   M’a capuill, m’a cathmhil, m’a cual,<br>   Moch is anamach dol dachaidh is uaith.</p>   
 <p>Gan cumail bho   chreagan, bho chleitean,<br>   Bho ladhara ’s bho adhaircean a cheile,<br>   Bho iana na Creige Ruaidh,<br>   Is bho Luath na Feinne.</p>   
 <p>Bho lannaire   liath Creag Duilionn,<br>   Bho iolaire riabhach Beinn-Ard,<br>   Bho sheobhag luth Torr-an-Duin,<br>   Is fitheach dur Creag-a-Bhaird.</p>   
 <p>Bho mhada-ruadh   nan cuireid,<br>   Bho mhada-ulai a Mhaim,<br>   Bho thaghan tocaidh na tuide,<br>   ’S bho mhaghan udail a mhais.</p>   
 <p>  *       *       *       *       *<br>     *       *       *       *       *<br>   Bho gach ceithir-chasach spuireach,<br>   Agus guireach da sgiath.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THE charm put   by Bride the beneficent,<br>   On her goats, on her sheep, on her kine,<br>   On her horses, on her chargers, on her herds,<br>   Early and late going home, and from home.</p>   
 <p>To keep them   from rocks and ridges,<br>   From the heels and the horns of one another<br>   From the birds of the Red Rock,<br>   And from Luath of the Feinne.</p>   
 <p>From the blue   peregrine hawk of Creag Duilion,<br>   From the brindled eagle of Ben-Ard,<br>   From the swift hawk of Tordun,<br>   From the surly raven of Bard's Creag.</p>   
 <p>From the fox of   the wiles,<br>   From the wolf of the Mam,<br>   From the foul-smelling fumart,<br>   And from the restless great-hipped bear.</p>   
 <p>  *       *       *       *       *<br>     *       *       *       *       *<br>   From every hoofed of four feet,<br>   And from every hatched of two wings.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
