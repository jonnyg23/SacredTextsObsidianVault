 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>THIS verse, the only verse of the poem he could remember, was obtained from John Kane, a native of Ireland. John Kane had many traditional stories of Saint Columba showing that he 'being dead yet speaketh.' These stories were <a name="page_169"><font size="1" color="green">p. 169</font></a> vivid and graphic, the probable and improbable, possible and impossible, blending and diffusing throughout.</small></p>

 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>COLUMBA tells   to us, that<br>   To hell the generous shall never go;<br>   But those who steal and those who swear,<br>   They shall lose their right to God.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
