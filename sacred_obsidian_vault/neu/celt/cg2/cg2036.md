 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>THE exorcism of the stye is variously called 'Cunntas an t-Sleamhnain'--Counting of the Stye, 'Bolas an t-Sleamhnain'--Exorcism of the Stye, and 'Eoir an t-Sleamhnain'--Charm of the Stye.</small></p>  
 <p>When making the charm the exorcist holds some sharp-pointed instrument, preferably a nail, or the tongue of a brooch or buckle, between the thumb and forefinger of the right hand. With each question the operator makes a feint with the instrument at the stye, going perilously near the eye. The sensation caused by the thrusting is extremely painful to the sufferer and even to the observer.</p>  
 <p>The reciter assured the writer that a cure immediately follows the operation. Possibly the thrusting acts upon the nervous system of the patient.</p>  
 <p>Ordinarily the exorcist omits mentioning the word 'sleamhnan' after the first two times, abbreviating thus:--</p>  
 <p> </p>  <div align="center">  <table border="0">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><small>'C’uim   an tainig a dha an seo<br>   Gun a tri an seo?'</small></p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><small>Why came the   two here<br>   Without the three here?</small></p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>After the incantation the Lord's Prayer is intoned, and the following is repeated:</small></p>  
 <p> </p>  <div align="center">  <table border="0">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><small>'Paidir a   h-aon,<br>   Paidir a dha,<br>   Paidir a tri,<br>   Paidir a ceithir,<br>   Paidir a coig,<br>   Paidir a sia,<br>   Paidir a seachd,<br>   Paidir a h-ochd,<br>   Paidir a naodh,<br>   Paidir a h-aon<br>   ’S a h-ochd,<br>   Paidir Chriosda chaoimh<br>   Ort an oidhche nochd,<br>   Paidir Tri nan dul<br>   Air a shuil gun lochd.'</small></p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><small>Pater one,<br>   Pater two,<br>   Pater three,<br>   Pater four,<br>   Pater five,<br>   Pater six,<br>   Pater seven,<br>   Pater eight,<br>   Pater nine,<br>   Pater one<br>   And eight,<br>   Pater of Christ the kindly<br>   Be upon thee to-night,<br>   Pater of the Three of life<br>   Upon thine eye without harm.</small></p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>  
 <p><small>This seems to indicate that the Lord's Prayer was originally repeated nine times.</small></p>

 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>WHY came the   one stye,<br>   Without the two styes here?<br>   Why came the two styes,<br>   Without the three styes here?<br>   Why came the three styes,<br>   Without the four styes here?<br>   Why came the four styes,<br>   Without the five styes here?<br>   Why came the five styes,<br>   Without the six styes here?<br>   Why came the six styes,<br>   Without the seven styes here?<br>   Why came the seven styes,<br>   Without the eight styes here?<br>   Why came the eight styes,<br>   Without the nine styes here?<br>   Why came the nine,<br>   Or one at all here?</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
