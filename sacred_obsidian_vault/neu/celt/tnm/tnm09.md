 <p align="center"> </p>  <h3 align="center">VII.--HEIMDALL.--THE SYBIL HYNDLA.</h3>  
 <p>A fragment at Jurby (<a href="#img_pl08">pl. VIII.</a>) shows on one face,. above the right arm of the cross, a figure of a Man in a tunic with a row of large buttons; in his left hand a short, pointed sword, his right holding to his mouth a long Alpine horn (<i>Lur</i>). On his head is a curious helmet, above the horn a flying raven.</p>  
 <p>Evidently this is intended for Heimdall, Warder of the Gods, who is stationed at the foot of the rainbow <i>Bif-rost</i>, "quaking bridge," leading from earth to Asgard. At Ragnarök he summons the gods to the last great battle by a. blast on the Giallar horn, which rings through all the nine worlds:--</p>  
 <p></p><dir><font size="-1">"Loud blows Heimdall,<br> his horn is up-lift."</font></dir>  
 <p>The Raven flies before him carrying the tidings to Odin. On the other face (<a href="#img_pl09">pl. IX.</a>), in like position, is a female</p>  
 <p><a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 29</font></a></p>  
 <p>figure, dog-headed, with long, braided hair. As suggested by Professor S. Bugge, this may be the Sibyl Hyndla, " little Hound," who prophesies of Heimdall and of Ragnarök.</p>
