 <p align="CENTER">Notes on Welsh Pronunciation</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>Welsh pronunciation is not nearly so difficult as it is popularly supposed to be. With the exception of y, every letter has an<i> </i>invariable value, and is always<i> </i>pronounced.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER">VOWELS.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>a short: pronounced as in p<i>a</i>n<i>.</i></p>
 <p>a long    ,,     ,,               f<i>a</i>ther.</p>
 <p>e short   ,,     ,,               p<i>e</i>n.</p>
 <p>e long    ,,                       like the first element of the diphthong in l<i>a</i>ne.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>i short    ,,                       as in p<i>i</i>n.</p>
 <p>i long     ,,     ,,               mach<i>i</i>ne.</p>
 <p>o short   ,,     ,,               c<i>o</i>t.</p>
 <p>o long    ,,                       like the first element of the diphthong in n<i>o</i>te<i>.</i></p>
 <p>u           ,,                       (approximately) like the French <i>u </i>in s<i>u</i>r.</p>
 <p>w short  ,,                       like the <i>oo</i> in w<i>oo</i>d.</p>
 <p>w long   ,,      ,,              like the <i>oo</i> in w<i>oo</i>ed.</p>
 <p>y           ,,                       sometimes like <i>u </i>in b<i>u</i>t, and sometimes (approximately) like the French <i>u</i> in s<i>u</i>r.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER">DIPHTHONGS.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>They may be pronounced by<i> </i>giving to each of the component vowels the value indicated above.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER">CONSONANTS.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <i></i><p>c is always pronounced as <i>k, chi </i>as in the Scotch lo<i>ch</i>, <i>dd</i> as <i>th </i>in breathe, <i>f</i> as <i>v, ff </i>as <i>f</i>, s is always hard, as in lo<i>ss</i>; <i>ll </i>represents a spirant <i>l, </i>a very difficult sound to represent in English--Englishmen generally render it as <i>thl</i>.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER">ACCENT.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>The accent is nearly always on the last syllable but one (or penult) as Elidyr, Seithényn.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER">EXAMPLES.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>Gwyddno = Gwithno, th as in breathe; Gwenhúdiw = Gwenhid-ue; Syfáddon = Syváthon, th as in breathe; Dwt = Dŏot, oo as in wood; Pwca = Pŏoka, oo as in wood; Ardúdwy = Ardidŏoi; Llwyd = Llōōid, ll the spirant l, oo as in wooed.</p>
