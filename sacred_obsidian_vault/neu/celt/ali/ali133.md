 <p align="CENTER">For A Sprain</p>
 <blockquote>In the Western Isles the following charm is used for a sprain--</blockquote>
 <blockquote>A strand of black wool is wound round and round the ankle, while the operator recites in a low voice--</blockquote><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <blockquote>"The Lord rade and the foal slade,<br>
 He lighted and He righted;<br>
 Set joint to joint and bone to bone,<br>
 And sinew unto sinew. <br>
 In the name of God and the Saints, <br>
 Of Mary and her Son, <br>
 Let this man be healed. AMEN."</blockquote>
 <blockquote>A. similar charm was used in Germany in the tenth century, according to Jacob Grimm.</blockquote></dir>
