 <p align="CENTER">Against Sorrow</p>
 <blockquote>A charm set by Mary for her Son, before the fair man and the turbulent woman laid Him in the grave.</blockquote><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <blockquote>The charm of Michael with the shield;<br>
 Of the palm-branch of Christ;<br>
 Of Bridget with her veil.</blockquote>
 <blockquote>The charm which God set for Himself when the divinity within Him was darkened.</blockquote>
 <i><blockquote>A. </blockquote></i>charm to be said by the cross when the night is black and the soul is heavy with sorrow.
 <blockquote>A charm to be said at sunrise, with the hands on the breast when the eyes are red with weeping, and the madness of grief is strong.</blockquote>
 <blockquote>A charm that has no words, only the silent prayer.</blockquote></dir>
 </dir>
