   <h3 align="center">IX</h3>  <h3 align="center">From Gilla Modutu's poem <i>Senchas Ban</i>, written A.D. 1141, Book of Leinster, p. 140 a, 29.</h3>  
 <p></p><dir>'Ingen do Ch<i>a</i>mmáin Dub-Lacha,<br>   lennán Mongáin, maith a ciand,<br> [20] Colgo, Conall, ba lucht láthair,<br>   Cáintig<i>er</i>n a máthair mall:<br> ingen maic Demmáin Dub-Lacha<br>   na n-gellám cen tacha thall.'  
 <p>Cammán's daughter was Dub-Lacha,<br> [25]  The beloved of Mongán, their offspring was good,<br> Colgo, Conall, that were folk of strength,<br>   Cáintigern was his gentle mother.<br> Daughter of Demmán's son was Dub-Lacha<br>   Of the white arms, without fault, of yore.'</p></dir>  
 <p>[30] Cammán Dub, the daughter of Furudrán mac Bécce, of the royal race of the Ui Turtri, was the wife of Fiachna Dub mac Demmáin (LL: 140 <i>a</i>, 27).</p>  
 <p>Unless <i>máthair mall</i> may mean 'grandmother,' we must translate as I have done, and refer the <i>a</i> 'his' to Mongán.</p>  
 <p>[35] As to Dub-Lacha being called 'of the white arms,' cf. her by-name Láimhghel, p. 61, 10 above.</p>
