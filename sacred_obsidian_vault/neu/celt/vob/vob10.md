   <h3 align="center">VII</h3>  <h3 align="center">Irische Texte iii. page 89.</h3>  
 <p></p><dir>'A Mongáín, a Manandáin,<br> ni minec bar merugud<br>   isin brug co m-beócraidi<br> ó Tuind Clidna comfada<br> is torachta in tebugud [20]<br>   co Trácht n-álaind n-Eóthaili.'  
 <p>'O Monagán, O Manannán,<br> Your wandering is not frequent<br>   In the land with living heart<br> From Tonn Clidna of even length [25]<br> The . . . is winding<br> To the beautiful strand of Eothaile.'</p></dir>  
 <p>Quoted as an example of the metre called Casbairdne seisedach (ṡedradhach). Tonn Clidna (Toun Cleena) is a loud surge in the bay of Glandore, co. Cork. See its dinnshenchas, Rev. [30]</p>  
 <p><a name="page_86"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 86</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Celt. xv. p. 437. Tracht Eóthaili (Trawohelly) is on the coast of Sligo.</p>
