   <h3 align="center">VI. From the Annals</h3>  
 <p align="center">(a)</p>  
 <p>Mongán mac Fíachna Lurgan ab Artur filio Bicoir Pretene lapide percussus interit, unde dictum est--Bec Boirche [20] dixit:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>'Is fúar in gáeth dar ’Ile,<br> dosfuil ócu Cind-Tire:<br> dogénat gním n-amnas de,<br> mairbfit Mongán mac Fíachnæ.</dir>  
 <p><a name="page_85"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 85</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>Land Chlúana Airthir indíu,<br> amra in cethrar forsriadad:<br> Cormac Cáem fri imfochid,<br> ocus Illand mac Fiachach.  
 <p>Ocus in dias ele [5]<br> dia fognad mór de thúathaib:<br> Mongán mac Fiachnai Lurgan,<br> ocus Rónán mac Túatheil.'</p></dir>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p align="center">(b)</p>  
 <p align="center">From the Annals of Clonmacnois.</p>  
 <p align="center">Quoted by O’Donovan, FM. vol. i. p. 243, note z.</p>  
 <p>A.D. 624. Mangan mac Fiaghna, a very well-spoken man, and much given to the wooing of women, was killed by one ??? [Arthur ap] Bicoir, a Welshman, with a stone. [15]</p>
