 <p><a name="page_88"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 88</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">From MS. Laud 615, p. 18.</h3>  <h3 align="center">Mongan cecinitdo Colum Cille.</h3>  
 <p>Caomh-Colum cáidh ciuin cub<i>aid</i> cob<i>said</i> comdalach com ramach cumachtach Cille mirbuilech,</p>  
 <p></p><dir>[5] ag nach fail gradh ilṡelba,<br> cabras da mainib gan dimda<br> gach dam imda ilarda,<br> nach fail <i>tar</i>ut na <a name="fr_167"></a><a href="#fn_167"><font size="1">1</font></a> fich na ferg,<br> gnais derg lethan lainnerdha,<br> [10] corp gel ar n-derbad a rúin,<br> ocus clú gan imharbus,<br> rosg glas gan locht is gan lasg<br> ocus folt cas coinnelda. <a name="fr_168"></a><a href="#fn_168"><font size="1">2</font></a>  
 <p>Foghar gotha Coluim Cille,<br> [15] lor a binne os gach cleir,<br> co tend cuig <i>fichet déc</i> ceimenn,<br> aidble remenn, sedh ba reil.</p>  
 <p>Mac Eit[h]ni is Fei[d]limid finn<br> cuigi romcinn Dia do cein<br> a Tir Tarrngaire na finn,<br> [20] mar a cantar fír gan bréig.</p>  
 <p>Tri caoguit inis rea rim<br> ma docuired on rig r<i>éd</i>, <a name="fr_169"></a><a href="#fn_169"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> in gach innsi dar mo leighend<br> tri coibheis Eirenn fodein.</p></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p></p><dir>[25] Mar domsdíur mac De gu haghmar,<br> om tir fein tang ane<br> gu Carraig Eolairg gan mebail,<br> cu bord Locha Febail fein. <a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 89</font></a><br> Loch Febail fial nocho míbladh<br> ag díl<i>ad</i> aidh<i>ed</i> <a name="fr_170"></a><a href="#fn_170"><font size="1">1</font></a> o Néill.  
 <p>Colum Cille cáin gan gó,<br> briathra an laoich gersat ra ló,<br> anté nach cabair na fainn [5]<br> noca carann <a name="fr_171"></a><a href="#fn_171"><font size="1">2</font></a> caom-Choluim.<br>            Caomh-Cholum caidh.</p></dir>  
 <p>Beloved, chaste, gentle, just, firm, disputant, combative, <a href="errata.htm#1">powerful</a>, miraculous Colum Cille,</p>  
 <p></p><dir>'Who loveth not many possessions, [10]<br> Who with his gifts without displeasure<br> Helpeth every numerous multitudinous band.<br> Over thee there is neither wrath nor anger.<br> Red broad radiant face,<br> White body that hath proved mysteries, [15]<br> And fame without sin,<br> Grey eye without fault and without . . .,<br> And curly luminous hair.  
 <p>The sound of Colum Cilia's voice--<br> Abundant its sweetness above every train, [20]<br> To the end of fifteen score paces,<br> Vastness of courses! it was clear. <a name="fr_172"></a><a href="#fn_172"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>The son of Ethne and of Fedlimid the Fair,<br> To him God sent me from afar,<br> From the Land of Promise of the blessed, [25]<br> Where truth is sung without falsehood.</p>  
 <p>Thrice fifty isles are counted,<br> As they were set by the bright King;<br> In every isle, by my lore!<br> There is three times the size of Erin herself. [30]</p></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 90</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>As the Son of God directed me prosperously,<br> From my own land I have come yesterday<br> To Carraic Eolairg without disgrace,<br> To the edge of Lough Foyle itself.<br> [5] Loch Foyle, hospitable without ill-fame,<br> Contenting the guests of the Ui Néill.  
 <p>Colum Cille, fair without falsehood,<br> Though the words of the warrior were . . .<br> He that doth not help the weak,<br> [10] He is no friend of beloved Colum.'</p></dir>
