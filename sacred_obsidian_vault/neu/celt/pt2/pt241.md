 <h3 align="CENTER">XLIX.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">THE CAT AND THE MOUSE. <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>
 <p align="CENTER">From Hector MacLean, Islay.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">1.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>THUIRT an luch bheag 's i 'san toll,<br>
 "Dé 'm fonn a th' air a' chat ghlas?"<br>
 "Fonn math is deagh shaod<br>
 Gum faodadh thusa tighinn a mach."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">2.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"S mor m' eagal romh na dubhain chrom,<br>
 A th' agad ann am bonn do chas<br>
 Mharbh thu mo phiuthrag an dé<br>
 'S fhuair mi fein air eigin as."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">3.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Cha mhis' a bha 'sin ach cat mhic Iain Ruaigh<br>
 A b' àbhaist a bhi ruagadh chearc,<br>
 Ghoid i 'n caise 'bha 's a' chliabh,<br>
 'S dh'ith i 'n t-iasg a bha 's a' phreas."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">TRANSLATION.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">1.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Said the mousie in the hole,<br>
 "What is that purr of the grey cat?"<br>
 "A good purr and a pleasant mood,<br>
 That thou mightest come out of that."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_405"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 405</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">2.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Great is my fear for the crooked hooks<br>
 That thou hast got in the sole of thy feet;<br>
 Thou killedst my sister yesterday,<br>
 And I myself got hardly quit."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">3.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"That was not me, but John Roy's cat,<br>
 That used to be the hen's distress:<br>
 She stole the cheese that was in the creel,<br>
 And ate the fish that was in the press."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>This old rhyme has become proverbial. A part of it was sent as a proverb from Inverary.</p>
 <p align="RIGHT">J. F. C.</p>
