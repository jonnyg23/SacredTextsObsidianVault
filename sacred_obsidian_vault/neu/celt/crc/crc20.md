Winifred Faraday, [1904], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">The Death of the Princes <a name="fr_67"></a><a href="#fn_66"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>'Let a sword-truce be asked of Cuchulainn for us,' said Ailill and Medb.</p>  
 <p>Lugaid goes on that errand, and Cuchulainn grants the truce.</p>  
 <p>'Put a man on the ford for me to-morrow,' said Cuchulainn.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="green">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p>There were with Medb six princes, <i>i.e.</i> six king's heirs of the Clanna Dedad, the three Blacks of Imlech, and the three Reds of Sruthair.</p>  
 <p>'Why should we not go against Cuchulainn?' said they.</p>  
 <p>They go next day, and Cuchulainn slew the six of them.</p>
