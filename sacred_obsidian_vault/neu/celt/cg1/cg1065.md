 <p>CHUNNACAS an   Oigh a teachd,<br>   Criosda gu h-og ’na h-uchd.</p>   
 <p>A Mhoir Oighe,   agus a Mhic,<br>   Beannaich an taigh agus a luchd.</p>   
 <p>Beannaich am   biadh, beannaich am bord,<br>   Beannaich an dias, an triall ’s an stor.</p>   
 <p>An trath bha   oirnn an raithe gann,<br>   Is tu fein, Oighe, bu mhathair dhuinn.</p>   
 <p>Is gil thu na   ghealach earra-gheal<br>   Ag eirigh air an tulaich.</p>   
 <p>Is gil thu na   ghrian cheit-ghil,<br>   Fo eibhneas subhach.</p>   
 <p>Bho nach faod   am bard fuireach,<br>   Cuiribh uilim ’s a bhalg le beannachd.</p>   
 <p>Mise gille Mhic   De an cois an doruis,<br>   A uchd De, eirich fein is fosgail domh e.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>BEHOLD the   Virgin approaching,<br>   Christ so young on her breast.</p>   
 <p>O Mary Virgin!   and O Holy Son!<br>   Bless ye the house and all therein,</p>   
 <p>Bless ye the   food, bless ye the board,<br>   Bless ye the corn, the flock and the store.</p>   
 <p>What time to us   the quarter was scarce,<br>   It is thou thyself, Virgin, who wast mother to us.</p>   
 <p>Thou art   brighter than the waxing moon<br>   Rising over the mountains.</p>   
 <p>Thou art   brighter than the summer sun,<br>   Under his fullness of joy.</p>   
 <p>Since the bard   must not tarry,<br>   Place ye alms in the bag with a blessing.</p>   
 <p>Servant am I of   God the Son on the threshold,<br>   For the sake of God, arise thyself and open to me.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
