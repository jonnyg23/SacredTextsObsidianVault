 <p><small>THIS poem was taken down at Creagorry, Benbecula, on the 16th of December 1872, from Janet Campbell, nurse, Lochskiport, South Uist. The reciter had many beautiful songs and lullabies of the nursery, and many instructive sayings and fables of the animal world. These she sang and told in the most pleasing and natural manner, to the delight of her listeners. Birds and beasts, reptiles and insects, whales and fishes talked and acted through her in the most amusing manner, and in the <a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="green">p. 61</font></a> most idiomatic Gaelic. Her stories had a charm for children, and it was delightful to see a small cluster of little ones pressing round the narrator, all eyes, all ears, all mouth, and all attention, listening to what the bear said to the bee, the fox to the lamb, the harrier to the hen, the serpent to the pipet, the whale to the herring, and the brown otter of the stream to the silvery grilse of the current. Those fair young heads, now, alas! widely apart, probably remember some of the stories heard at Janet Campbell's knee better than those they afterwards heard in more formal schools.</small></p>

 <p>BOISILEAG air   th’ aois,<br>        Boisileag air th’ fhas,<br>   Boisileag air th’ ugan,<br>        Tuilim air a chail.</p>   
 <p>Air do chuid an   chugan dhut,<br>        Gruidhim agus cal;<br>   Air do chuid an ghabhail dhut,<br>        Meal is bainne blath.</p>   
 <p>Air do chuid an   chomaidh dhut,<br>        Omhan agus ais;<br>   Air do chuid an chobhartaich<br>        Le bogha agus gais.</p>   
 <p>Air do chuid an   uidheam dhut,<br>        Uibhean buidhe Chasg;<br>   Air do chuid an chuileagan,<br>        M’ ulaidh agus m’ agh.</p>   
 <p>Air do chuid an   chuilm dhut,<br>        Uilim agus can;<br>   Air do chuid an chuilidh dhut<br>        Cuisilin mo ghraidh.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>A PALMFUL for   thine age,<br>        A palmful for thy growth,<br>   A palmful for thy throat,<br>        A flood for thine appetite.</p>   
 <p>For thy share   of the dainty,<br>        Crowdie and kail;<br>   For thy share of the taking,<br>        Honey and warm milk.</p>   
 <p>For thy share   of the supping,<br>        Whisked whey and milk-product;<br>   For thy share of the spoil,<br>        With bow and with spear.</p>   
 <p>For thy share   of the preparation,<br>        The yellow eggs of Easter;<br>   For thy share of the treat,<br>        My treasure and my joy,</p>   
 <p>For thy share   of the feast<br>        With gifts and with tribute;<br>   For thy share of the treasure,<br>        Pulset of my love.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">

 <p>Air do chuid an   fhaghaid dhut,<br>        Ri aghaidh Beinn-a-cheo;<br>   Air do chuid an fhiadhach dhut,<br>        Is riaghladh air sloigh.</p>   
 <p>Air do chuid an   luchairt,<br>        An curtaibh nan righ;<br>   Air do chuid a fhlathas dhut,<br>        Le mhathas is le shith.</p>   
 <p>A chuid nach   fas ’s a chumhanaich,<br>        Gum fas ’s an dubha-thrath;<br>   A chuid nach fas ’s an oidhche dhiot,<br>        Air dhruim a mheadhon la.</p>   
 <p>       Tri   baslach<br>          Nan Tri run,<br>          Dha do chumhn<br>          Bho gach tnu,<br>          Suil agus bas;<br>          Baslach Ti nan dul<br>          Baslach Chriosda chumh,<br>          Baslach Spiorad numh,<br>              Tri-un<br>              Nan gras.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">

 <p>For thy share   of the chase<br>        Up the face of the Beinn-a-cheo;<br>   For thy share of the hunting<br>        And the ruling over hosts.</p>   
 <p>For thy share   of palaces,<br>        In the courts of kings;<br>   For thy share of Paradise<br>        With its goodness and its peace.</p>   
 <p>The part of   thee that does not grow at dawn,<br>        May it grow at eventide;<br>   The part of thee that does not grow at night,<br>        May it grow at ridge of middle-day.</p>   
 <p>       The   three palmfuls<br>          Of the Secret Three,<br>          To preserve thee<br>          From every envy,<br>          Evil eye and death;<br>          The palmful of the God of Life,<br>          The palmful of the Christ of Love,<br>          The palmful of the Spirit of Peace,<br>              Triune<br>              Of Grace.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
