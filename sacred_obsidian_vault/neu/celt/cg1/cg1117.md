 <p>WHEN a man has shorn a sheep and has set <a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="green">p. 293</font></a> it free, he waves his hand after it and says:--</p>

 <p>FALBH lom   ’s thig molach,<br>   Beir am boirionn Bealltain,<br>   Bride mhin a bhi dha d’ chonaill,<br>   Moire gheal dha t’ aurais,<br>        Moire gheal dha t’ aurais.</p>   
 <p>Micheal mil a   bhi dha d’ dhion<br>   Bho ’n mhi-chu is bho ’n an-chu,<br>   Bho ’n mhac-tir ’s bho ’n mhadhan stig,<br>   ’S bho ianaibh ineach call-ghob,<br>        Bho ianaibh ineach cam-ghob.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>Go shorn and   come woolly,<br>   Bear the Beltane female lamb,<br>   Be the lovely Bride thee endowing,<br>   And the fair Mary thee sustaining,<br>        The fair Mary sustaining thee.</p>   
 <p>Michael the   chief be shielding thee<br>   From the evil dog and from the fox,<br>   From the wolf and from the sly bear,<br>   And from the taloned birds of destructive bills,<br>        From the taloned birds of hooked bills.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
