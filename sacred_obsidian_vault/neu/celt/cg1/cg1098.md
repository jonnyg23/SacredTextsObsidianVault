 <p>DI-MAIRT feille   ri eirigh greine,<br>   Is cul na deise ’s an aird an ear,<br>   Theid mi mach le m’ chorran fo m’ sgeith,<br>   Is buainidh mi am beum an ceud char.</p>   
 <p>Leigidh mi mo   chorran sios<br>   ’S an dias biadhchar fo mo ghlac,<br>   Togam suas mo shuil an aird,<br>   Tionndam air mo shail gu grad,</p>   
 <p>Deiseil mar   thriallas a ghrian<br>   Bho ’n airde ’n ear gu ruig an iar,<br>   Bho ’n airde tuath le gluasadh reidh,<br>   Gu fior chre na h-airde deas.</p>   
 <p>Bheir mi cliu   do Righ nan gras<br>   Airson cinneas barr na h-uir,<br>   Bheir e lon dhuinn fein ’s dh’ an al<br>   Mar a bhairigeas e dhuinn.</p>   
 <p>Seumas is Eoin,   Peadail is Pal,<br>   Moire ghraidh-gheal lan soluis,<br>   *       *       *       *       *</p>   
 <p>*       *       *       *       *<br>   Oidhch Fheill-Micheil agus Nollaig,<br>   Biasaidh sinn uile dhe ’n bhonnach.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>ON Tuesday of   the feast at the rise of the sun,<br>   And the back of the ear of corn to the east,<br>   I will go forth with my sickle under my arm,<br>   And I will reap the cut the first act.</p>   
 <p>I will let my   sickle down<br>   While the fruitful ear is in my grasp,<br>   I will raise mine eye upwards,<br>   I will turn me on my heel quickly,</p>   
 <p>Rightway as   travels the sun<br>   From the airt of the east to the west,<br>   From the airt of the north with motion calm<br>   To the very core of the airt of the south.</p>   
 <p>I will give   thanks to the King of grace<br>   For the growing crops of the ground,<br>   He will give food to ourselves and to the flocks<br>   According as He disposeth to us.</p>   
 <p>James and John,   Peter and Paul,<br>   Mary beloved, the fullness of light,<br>   *       *       *       *       *</p>   
 <p>*       *       *       *       *<br>   On Michaelmas Eve and Christmas,<br>   We will all taste of the bannock.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
