 <p><small>THE night prayers of the people are numerous. They are called by various names, as: 'Beannachadh Beinge'--Bench-Blessing, 'Beannachadh Bobhstair'--Bolster Blessing, 'Beannachadh Cluasaig'--Pillow Blessing, 'Beannachadh Cuaiche'--Couch Blessing, 'Coich Chuaiche'--Couch Shrining, 'Altachadh Cadail'--Sleep Prayer; and other terms. Many of these prayers are become mere fragments and phrases, supplemented by the people according to their wants and wishes at the time. <a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a></small></p>  
 <p>It is touching and instructive to hear these simple old men and women in their lowly homes addressing, as they say themselves, 'Dia mor nan dui, Athair nan uile bheo,' the great God of life, the Father of all living. They press upon Him their needs and their desires fully and familiarly, but with all the awe and deference due to the Great Chief whom they wish to approach and to attract, and whose forgiveness and aid they would secure. And all this in language so homely yet so eloquent, so simple yet so dignified, that the impressiveness could not be greater in proudest fane.</p>

 <p>A Righ na fola   firinnich,<br>   Na dibir mi a d’ mhuinntireas,<br>   Na tagair orm mo mhi-cheartan,<br>   Is na dichuimhnich a d’ chunntadh mi,<br>        Na dichuimhnich a d’ chunntadh mi.</p>   
 <p>Crois Mhoir is   Mhicheil, bhi tharam ann an sith,<br>   M’ anam a bhi am firinn, gun mhi-run am chom,<br>   M’ anam a bhi an sith aig Sorchair na frithe,<br>   Micheal crodhal an codhail m’ anama,<br>        Moch agus anmoch, la agus oidhche.  <p>BE Thy right   hand, O God, under my head,<br>   Be Thy light, O Spirit, over me shining.<br>   And be the cross of the nine angels over me down,<br>   From the crown of my head to the soles of my feet,<br>        From the crown of my head to the soles of my   feet.</p>   
 <p>O Jesu without   offence, crucified cruelly,<br>   Under ban of the wicked Thou wert scourged,<br>   The many evils done of me in the body!<br>   That I cannot this night enumerate,<br>        That I cannot this night enumerate.</p>   
 <p>O Thou King of   the blood of truth,<br>   Cast me not from Thy covenant,<br>   Exact not from me for my transgressions,<br>   Nor omit me in Thy numbering,<br>        Nor omit me in Thy numbering.</p>   
 <p>Be the cross of   Mary and of Michael over me in peace,<br>   Be my soul dwelling in truth, be my heart free of guile,<br>   Be my soul in peace with thee, Brightness of the mountains.<br>   Valiant Michael, meet thou my soul,<br>        Morn and eve, day and night.
