 <p>CLANN Israil is   Dia da ’n gabhail,<br>        Troimh ’n Mhuir Ruaidh fhuair iad rathad,<br>   Is ann a fhuair iad casg am pathaidh,<br>        An creag nach d’ fhaodadh le saor a   shnaidheadh.</p>   
 <p>Co iad air faim   mo stiuir<br>        Deanamh falbh da m’ iubhraich shoir?<br>   Peadail, Pal, is Eoin mo ruin,<br>        Triuir da ’n talmaich fiu is foir.</p>   
 <p>Co ’n   croil an coir mo stiuir?<br>        Peadail, Poil, is Eoin Baistidh,<br>   Criosda na shuidh air mo stiuir,<br>        Deanamh iuil da ’n ghaoith a deas.</p>   
 <p>Co da ’n   criothnaich guth na gaoith?<br>        Co da ’n caonaich caol is cuan?<br>   Iosa Criosda, Triath gach naoimh,<br>        Mac Moire, Friamh nam buadh,<br>        Mac Moire, Friamh nam buadh.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THE Children of   Israel, God taking them,<br>        Through the Red Sea obtained a path,<br>   They obtained the quenching of their thirst<br>        From a rock that might not by craftsman be   hewn.</p>   
 <p>Who are they on   the tiller of my rudder,<br>        Giving speed to my east bound barge?<br>   Peter and Paul and John the beloved,<br>        Three to whom laud and obeisance are due.</p>   
 <p>Who are the   group near to my helm?<br>        Peter and Paul and John the Baptist;<br>   Christ is sitting on my helm,<br>        Making guidance to the wind from the south.</p>   
 <p>To whom does   tremble the voice of the wind?<br>        To whom become tranquil strait and ocean?<br>   To Jesus Christ, Chief of each saint,<br>        Son of Mary, Root of victory,<br>        Son of Mary, Root of victory.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
