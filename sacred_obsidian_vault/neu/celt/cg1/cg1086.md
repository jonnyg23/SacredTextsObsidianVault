 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>THIS poem was obtained from Janet Currie, Staonabrig, South Uist, a descendant of the Mac Mhuirichs (corrupted into Currie) of Staoligearry, the famous poet-historians to the Clanranalds. She was a tall, strong, dark-haired, ruddy-complexioned woman, with a clear, sonorous voice. Her <a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="green">p. 217</font></a> language was remarkably fluent and copious, though many of her words and phrases, being obsolete, were unintelligible to the stranger. I took down versions of the poem from several other persons, but they are all more or less corrupt and obscure. Poems similar to this can be traced back to the eighth century.</small></p>

 <p>DUAN an Domhnuich,   a Dhe ghil,<br>   Firinn fo neart Chriosd a chomhnuidh.</p>   
 <p>Di-domhnuich   rugadh Muire,<br>   Mathair Chriosd an or-fhuilt bhuidhe,<br>   Di-domhnuich rugadh Criosda<br>        Mar onair dhaoine.</p>   
 <p>Di-domhnuich,   an seachdamh latha,<br>   Dh’ orduich Dia gu fois a ghabhail,<br>   Gu cumail na beath-maireannaich,<br>   Gun feum a thoir a damh no duine,<br>   No a creubh mar dheonaich Muire,<br>   Gun sniamh snath sioda no strol,<br>   Gun fuaigheal, gun ghreiseadh ni’s mo,<br>   Gun churachd, gun chliathadh, gun bhuain,<br>   Gun iomaradh, gun iomairt, gun iasgaireachd,<br>   Gun dol a mach dh’ an t-sliabh sheilg,<br>   Gun snaitheadh deilgne Di-domhnuich,<br>   Gun chartadh taighe, gun bhualadh,<br>   Gun atha, gun mhuileann Di-domhnuich.</p>   
 <p>Ge be chumadh   an Domhnuch,<br>   Bu chomhnard da-san ’s bu bhuan,<br>   Bho dhol fotha greine Di-Sathuirn<br>   Gu eirigh greine Di-luain.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THE poem of the   Lord's Day, O bright God,<br>   Truth under the strength of Christ always.</p>   
 <p>On the Lord's   Day Mary was born,<br>   Mother of Christ of golden yellow hair,<br>   On the Lord's Day Christ was born<br>        As an honour to men.</p>   
 <p>The Lord's Day,   the seventh day,<br>   God ordained to take rest,<br>   To keep the life everlasting,<br>   Without taking use of ox or man,<br>   Or of creature as Mary desired,<br>   Without spinning thread of silk or of satin,<br>   Without sewing, without embroidery either,<br>   Without sowing, without harrowing, without reaping,<br>   Without rowing, without games, without fishing,<br>   Without going out to the hunting hill,<br>   Without trimming arrows on the Lord's Day,<br>   Without cleaning byre, without threshing corn,<br>   Without kiln, without mill on the Lord's Day.</p>   
 <p>Whosoever would   keep the Lord's Day,<br>   Even would it be to him and lasting,<br>   From setting of sun on Saturday<br>   Till rising of sun on Monday.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">

 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="green">p. 219</font></a></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>Gheobhadh e   feich ga chionn,<br>   Toradh an deigh nan crann,<br>   Iasg air abhuinn fior ghlan sala,<br>   Sar iasg an ionnar gach abhuinn.</p>   
 <p>Uisg an   Domhnuich blath mar mhil,<br>   Ge be dh’ oladh e mar dhibh<br>   Gheobhadh e solas ga chion<br>   Bho gach dolas a bhiodh na char.</p>   
 <p>Gul an   Domhnuich gu ra-luath,<br>   Bean ga dheanadh an an-uair;<br>   Guileadh i gu moch Di-luain,<br>   Ach na guileadh i uair ’s an Domhnuch.</p>   
 <p>Fiodh an   Domhnuich gu ra-luath,<br>   Anns an linge mar is truagh,<br>   Ge d’ thuiteadh a cheann na ghual,<br>   Bhiodh e gu Di-luain na chadal.<br>   Mu thrath-nona Di-luain,<br>   Eiridh am fiodh gu ra-luath,<br>   ’S air an dile mhor a muigh<br>   Greas air sgeula mo chuimire.<br>   Gun chnuasachd uan, meile, meinne no minsich<br>   Nach buineadh dh’ an Righ anns a bhlagh.<br>   Is ann a nist bu choir a losgadh,<br>   Gun eisdeachd ri gleadhraich nan gall,<br>   No ri dall sgeileireachd choitchinn.</p>   
 <p>Gart a   ghleidheadh air cnoc ard,<br>   Leigh a thoir gu galar garga,<br>   Bo chur gu tarbh treun na tana,<br>   Falbh le beothach gu cuthaidh,</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>He would obtain   recompense therefrom,<br>   Produce after the ploughs,<br>   Fish on the pure salt-water stream,<br>   Fish excelling in every river confluence.</p>   
 <p>The water of   the Lord's Day mild as honey,<br>   Whoso would partake of it as drink<br>   Would obtain health in consequence<br>   From every disease afflicting him.</p>   
 <p>The weeping of   the Lord's Day is out of place,<br>   A woman doing it is untimely;<br>   Let her weep betimes on Monday,<br>   But not weep once on the Lord's Day.</p>   
 <p>The wood of the   Lord's Day is too soon.<br>   In the pool it is pitiful,<br>   Though its head should fall in char,<br>   It would till Monday be dormant.<br>   About noon on the Monday,<br>   The wood will arise very quickly,<br>   And by the great flood without<br>   Hasten the story of my trouble.<br>   Without any searching for lamb, sheep, kid or goat<br>   That would not belong to the King in the cause.<br>   It is now it ought to be burnt,<br>   Without listening to the clamour of the stranger,<br>   Nor to the blind babbling of the public.</p>   
 <p>To keep corn on   a high hillock,<br>   To bring physician to a violent disease,<br>   To send a cow to the potent bull of the herd,<br>   To go with a beast to a cattle-fold,</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="green">p. 220</font></a></p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="green">p. 221</font></a></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>Fada no fagasg   anns a cheum,<br>   Feumaidh gach creatair umhail.<br>   Eathar a leigeil fo breid-shiuil bho thir,<br>   Bho thir gu duthaich a h-aineoil.</p>   
 <p>Ge be   mheoraicheadh mo dhuan<br>   ’S a ghabhadh e gach oidhche Luan,<br>   Bhiodh rath Mhicheil air a cheann,<br>   ’S a chaoidh cha bu teann da irionn.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>Far or near be   the distance,<br>   Every creature needs attention.<br>   To allow a boat under her sail from land,<br>   From land to the country of her unacquaintance.</p>   
 <p>Whoso would   meditate my lay,<br>   And say it every Monday eve,<br>   The luck of Michael would be on his head,<br>   And never would he see perdition.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   
 <p>DOIGHEAN EILE--</p>   
 <p>Abhuinn sleibh   fior bhlasda,<br>   A sior ialadh gu Iordan,<br>   Is ra mhath chum i a cain,<br>        Di-domhnuich ge lan a tuil.</p>   
 <p>Cha ruith braon   ge glan a h-uisge,<br>   An inne na Mara Ruaidh.</p>   
 <p>Fiodh an   Domhnuich nis, mo nuar!<br>   An inne na Mara Ruaidh<br>   Ged thuiteadh an ruadh-cheann deth<br>        Bhiodh e gu Di-luain na chadal.</p>   
 <p>Na fagairt mi   ni air mo dheigh,<br>   Greis thoir air sgeula mo chumraidh.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   
 <p>ALTERNATIVE   VERSIONS--</p>   
 <p>Hill river is   very palatable,<br>   Ever meandering to Jordan,<br>   Right well it retained its tribute<br>        On the Lord's Day though great its flood.</p>   
 <p>No drop, though   pure be its water,<br>   Shall run in the channel of the Red Sea.</p>   
 <p>The wood of the   Lord's Day now, alas!<br>   In the channel of the Red Sea,<br>   Though the red head should fall off<br>        It would be till Monday asleep.</p>   
 <p>Let me not   leave aught behind,<br>   To talk a while of the redemption.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
