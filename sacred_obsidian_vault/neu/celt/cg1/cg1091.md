 <p>TOGAIDH mis an   tula<br>   Mar a thogadh Muire.<br>   Cairn Bhride ’s Mhuire<br>   Air an tula ’s air an lar,<br>   ’S air an fhardaich uile.</p>   
 <p>Co iad ri luim   an lair?<br>   Eoin, Peadail agus Pail.<br>   Co iad ri bruaich mo leap?<br>   Bride bhuidheach ’s a Dalt.<br>   Co iad ri fath mo shuain?<br>   Muire ghraidh-gheal ’s a h-Uan.<br>   Co siud a tha ’n am theann?<br>   Righ na grein e fein a th’ ann,<br>   Co siud ri cul mo chinn?<br>   Mac nan dul gun tus, gun linn.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>I WILL raise   the hearth-fire<br>   As Mary would.<br>   The encirclement of Bride and of Mary<br>   On the fire, and on the floor,<br>   And on the household all.</p>   
 <p>Who are they on   the bare floor?<br>   John and Peter and Paul.<br>   Who are they by my bed?<br>   The lovely Bride and her Fosterling.<br>   Who are those watching over my sleep?<br>   The fair loving Mary and her Lamb.<br>   Who is that anear me?<br>   The King of the sun, He himself it is.<br>   Who is that at the back of my head?<br>   The Son of Life without beginning, without time.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
