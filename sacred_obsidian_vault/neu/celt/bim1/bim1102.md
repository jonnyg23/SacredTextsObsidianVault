 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ABRED.</h3>  
 <p>To consociate with evil will make one the lowest and meanest of all animated beings; therefore a wicked man,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_244"><font size="1" color="green">p. 244</font></a> <a name="page_245"><font size="1" color="green">p. 245</font></a></p>  
 <p>when he dies, and his soul enters the meanest worm <a name="fr_256"></a><a href="#fn_254"><font size="1">1</font></a> in existence, becomes better, and ascends in the migration of Abred. From this has arisen the saying,--"Trample not on thy better," addressed to one who tramples on a worm voluntarily, and without a cause.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_254"></a><a href="bim1102.htm#fr_256">245:1</a> <a name="page_244"><font size="1" color="green">p. 244</font></a> "Pryf," a worm, originally written <i>prif</i>, probably obtained its name from its being the first <i>prif</i> = <i>primus</i>--link in the chain of Abred.</p>  
 <p>There seems to be some allusion to the doctrine enunciated here in those lines by Casnodyn, A.D. 1290-1340;</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Trefnaist wern uffern affaith sathan;<br> Trefred i <i>bryfed</i>, lle yd ymbrofan’.  
 <p>Thou didst prepare the slough of hell suitable for Satan;<br> The habitation for worms, where they will be in mutual strife.</p></div>
