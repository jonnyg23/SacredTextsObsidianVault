 <p align="center"><a name="img_07301"></a>
 <img src="img/07301.jpg"></p>  
 <p>The above Alphabets are from Llywelyn Sion. <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">*</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_07302"></a>
 <img src="img/07302.jpg"></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a> <a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a></p>  
 <p>Abcedilros; so were called the ten primary letters, because they are put in one word of four syllables, being arranged according to the word, thus,</p>  
 <p align="center">A. B. C. E. D. I. L. R. O. S.</p>  
 <p>After that M and N were invented, and thence there were twelve letters, which were called Mabcednilros, the letters being thus arranged--</p>  
 <p align="center">M. A. B. C. E. D. N. I. L. R. O. S.</p>  
 <p>making twelve letters. After that four other letters were devised, namely, G. T. P. F. And then there was a new arrangement of the letters; all that were partially co-vocal being placed next to each other, as if of one family in respect of sound. That is to say, firstly, the simple ones, namely,</p>  <div align="center">  <table border="0">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p align="center">A. E. I. O.</p>   
 <p>Then the   labials, namely, M. B. P. F.</p>   
 <p>Then the   dentals, namely, D. T. N.</p>   
 <p>Then the   palatals, G. C.</p>   
 <p>Then the non   congeners, namely, L. R. S.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>  
 <p>And thus were they arranged,--</p>  
 <p align="center">A. E. I. O. B. M. P. F. D. T. N. G. C. L. R. S.</p>  
 <p>and were called after their primitive name Abcedilros, though the authentic letters might, in respect of kind and number, be more than what are found in the special word.</p>  
 <p>After that, two other letters were devised, namely <a name="img_07500"></a>
 <img src="img/07500.jpg"> or<a name="img_07501"></a>
 <img src="img/07501.jpg">, and <a name="img_07502"></a>
 <img src="img/07502.jpg"> or <a name="img_07503"></a>
 <img src="img/07503.jpg">, then there were eighteen letters. After that, two others, which made twenty, namely, <a name="img_07504"></a>
 <img src="img/07504.jpg"> and <a name="img_07505"></a>
 <img src="img/07505.jpg">. After that, four other letters, namely, <a name="img_07506"></a>
 <img src="img/07506.jpg">. <a name="img_07507"></a>
 <img src="img/07507.jpg">. <a name="img_07508"></a>
 <img src="img/07508.jpg">; <a name="fr_108"></a><a href="#fn_107"><font size="1">1</font></a> which were arranged on the Coelbren according to their families in respect of sound and vocalness, and mutual relation. After that secondary letters were devised, which are <a name="img_07509"></a>
 <img src="img/07509.jpg">. <a name="img_07510"></a>
 <img src="img/07510.jpg">. <a name="img_07511"></a>
 <img src="img/07511.jpg">. <a name="img_07512"></a>
 <img src="img/07512.jpg">, &amp;c., as far as thirty-eight. Still their old</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a></p>  
 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a></p>  
 <p>designation is retained, which is, Abcedilros. An old Book calls them, Abcednilroswm.</p>
