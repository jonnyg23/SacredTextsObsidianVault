 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE THREE PRINCIPAL MATERIALS OF KNOWLEDGE.</h3>  
 <p>The three principal materials of knowledge of the Bards of the Isle of Britain: wood, that is Coelbren; and Coelvain; which two existed before the faith in Christ was obtained; and when that was obtained, knowledge and understanding was had of the third, namely Plagawd, that is, the dressed skin of a beast, which is called parchment. (Grawen. <a name="fr_170"></a><a href="#fn_169"><font size="1">1</font></a> There are two grawens: one of wood, the other of a beast.)</p>
