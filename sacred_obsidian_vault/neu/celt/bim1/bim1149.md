 <p><a name="fn_368"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_370">403:3</a> <a name="page_402"><font size="1" color="green">p. 402</font></a> The daughter of Don, and styled in the Triads (Myv. Mentioned in " Hanes Taliesin," Ap. Myv. Arch. i. 19.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_371"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_373">403:6</a> The Great Bear.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_372"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_374">403:7</a> The Orion.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_373"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_375">403:8</a> The Pleiades. Mentioned in "Hanes Taliesin," Ap. Myv. Arch. i. 19.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_374"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_376">403:9</a> Cassiopeia.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_375"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_377">405:1</a> <a name="page_404"><font size="1" color="green">p. 404</font></a> The title of one of Taliesin's poems, Myv. Arch. i. 65.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_376"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_378">405:2</a> The zodiac, or ecliptic. Mentioned in "Hanes Taliesin."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_377"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_379">405:3</a> Styled in the Triads as one of "the three monarch bulls." Tr. 73; third Series.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_379"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_381">405:5</a> Mentioned in "Hanes Taliesin."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_380"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_382">405:6</a> The Twins.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_381"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_383">405:7</a> The son of Bleiddyd--the celebrated king Lear of Shakespeare.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_382"></a><a href="bim1149.htm#fr_384">405:8</a> Elfin is said to have first discovered Taliesin, in a leathern bag, fastened to one of the poles of a weir. He is frequently mentioned by the Bard.</p>
