 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">BARDIC APHORISMS.</h3>  
 <p align="center">Thus says the Bard:--</p>  
 <p>There is no advantage but what can in no ways be dispensed with, that is, God;<br> There is no want but the want of God;<br> There is no enjoyment but the enjoyment of God;<br> There is no loss but the loss of God;<br> There is no sufficiency but God;<br> There is nothing immeasurable but God;<br> There is nothing knowing but God;<br> There is nothing in every place but God;<br> There is nothing powerful but God;<br> There is nothing in every thing but God;<br> There is no whole but God;<br> There is no God but what is whole.</p>
