 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE BARDIC SECRET.</h3>  
 <p>O I W are the three letters, and in very old books O I U, because U was used instead of W, in the olden times. It is the secret word of the primitive Bards, which it is not lawful to speak or utter audibly to any man in the</p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="green">p. 66</font></a> <a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>world, except to a Bard who is under the vow of an oath. The letters may be shown to any one in the world we like, without uttering the vocalization, which, under the protection of secrecy, is due to them, though he be not under an oath; but should he utter them in speech audibly, he violates his protection, and he cannot be a Bard, nor will it be lawful to shew him any more of the secret, either in this world that perishes, or in the other world that will not perish for ever and ever. <a name="fr_99"></a><a href="#fn_98"><font size="1">1</font></a> Sion Bradford. <a name="fr_100"></a><a href="#fn_99"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
