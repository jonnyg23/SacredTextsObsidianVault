 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE FOUR QUARTERS OF THE YEAR.</h3>  
 <p>1. Winter. 2. Spring. 3. Summer. 4. Autumn.</p>  
 <p>Or thus:--</p>  
 <p>1. Winter. 2. Spring. 3. Summer.--White grasses.</p>  
 <p>And the four points of the sun are thus:--</p>  
 <p>1. The point of roughness. 2. The point of regeneration. 3. The point of summer. 4. The point of reaping time. <a name="fr_415"></a><a href="#fn_411"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>Also vulgarly, thus:--</p>  
 <p>1. Summer. 2. Winter. 3. Spring.</p>
