 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">LAUNCELOT</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">SINCLAIR LEWIS</h2>  </center>  <br>  Blow, weary wind,<br>  The golden rod scarce chiding;<br>  Sir Launcelot is riding<br>  By shady wood-paths pleasant<br>  To fields of yellow corn.<br>  He starts a whirring pheasant,<br>  And clearly winds his horn.<br>  The Queen's Tower gleams mid distant hills;<br>  A thought like joyous sunshine thrills,<br>  "My love grows kind."<br>  <br>  Blow, weary wind,<br>  O'er lakes, o'er dead swamps crying,<br>  Amid the gray stumps sighing<br>  While slow, and cold, and sullen,<br>  The waves splash on the shore.<br>  O'er wastes of bush and mullen,<br>  Dull crows flap, evermore.<br>  The Autumn day is chill and drear<br>  As you knight, thinking Guenevere<br>  Proves  almost unkind.
