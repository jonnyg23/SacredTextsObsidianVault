 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE KING AND THE BARD</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">SALLIE BRIDGES</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>  "Come, sing us a lay!" quoth Arthur,<br>       "My Bard of the Table Round! <br>  Some ballad of lofty courage, <br>       That shall make our heart's-blood bound!" <br>  And the monarch drain'd his goblet, <br>       While the minstrel tuned his lyre, <br>  And fill'd it again, that the singer<br>       Might win from wine new fire. <br>  <br>  "Now drink," said the generous sovereign, <br>        "That, when thy song shall be o'er, <br>  We may fill with bright gold pieces<br>       And hand thee the cup once more." <br>  But the minstrel's voice was silent, <br>       And the ruby wine undrain'd, <br>  While Arthur, impatient, wondered<br>       Why the guerdon was not gain'd. <br>  <br>  The bard from his seat rose slowly, <br>       And spoke to the waiting king: <br>  "Sire, to-day my soul is tuneless, <br>       And no worthy lay can sing. <br>  Not e'en or your tempting liquor, <br>       Not e'en for your promised gold, <br>  Will my inner voice yield music; <br>       For true song cannot be sold! <br>  <br>  "But when fitting words can utter<br>       Dreams that stir my own deep heart, <br>  In thine shall the chords re-echo, <br>       Till it feels of mine a part. <br>  Not till inspiration smiteth<br>       On the rock of silent Thought, <br>  Can be welcome living waters<br>       To the king or people brought!" <br>  <br>  "Thou art right!" the sovereign answer'd; <br>        "'Tis a lesson nobly told: <br>  Monarchs cannot rule men's <i> spirits</i> <br>       By the might of law or gold! <br>  Thou art first of all my minstrels, <br>       Thou art best of Britain's boast; <br>  But take now my brimming goblet, <br>       And quaff it to Arthur's toast. <br>  <br>  "Drink, gallant knights, to the minstrel<br>       Who dreads neither prince nor peer, --<br>  Who can speak the truth to power, <br>       Nor flatters for price or fear, --<br>  To the bard who freely renders<br>       The gift he has been given, <br>  And sings but when his strain exalts<br>       His hearers nigher heaven!" <br>    <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>
