 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE WAKING OF KING ARTHUR</h2> <h3 align="CENTER">BRECHVA'S HARP-SONG</h3>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ERNEST RHYS</h2>  </center>  <br>    <br>    Little harp, at the cry<br>       He shall come in his time;<br>    And thy sword-song on high,<br>       High shall chime.<br>    <br>    Little harp, in his heart<br>       Is the fire; in his hand<br>    Are the sword, and the Art,<br>       To command.<br>    <br>    Little harp, like the wind<br>       Is his strength; like thy sound<br>    Are his words, to unbind<br>       Every wound.<br>    <br>    Little harp, if his name<br>       Be unknown, ye shall hear<br>    How the Stars tell his fame<br>       Far and near.<br>    <br>    Little harp, if unknown<br>       He awake, there shall sing<br>    All the Stars, as they crown<br>     Him All-King!<br>  <br>
