 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h2 align="CENTER">CAMELFORD</h2>     <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <div align="center">  </div>  <h2 align="CENTER">DOUGLAS B. W. SLADAN</h2>  
 <p align="left">  </p>  
 <p align="left">            Camelford--Camelot</p>  
 <p align="left">                         I.</p>  
 <p align="left"> Not Camelot the towered--the goodly town <br>       Upon the shining rive, whither passed<br>       The Lady of Shalott, when fallen at last<br>    A victim to her spell, slow-wafted down!<br>    Not Camelot the towered, the glittering crown<br>       Of all King Arthur's cities! Yet thou hast<br>       Thy legend of the King--how Modred massed<br>    His traitor legions, where the waters brown<br>    Run neath the Bridge of Slaughter, how the King,<br>       With Launcelot dishonoured, Tristram slain<br>    And half of his Round-table following<br>       Dead or apostate--triumphed; then was ta'en;<br>    Stricken to death, by bold Sir Bedivere<br>    To Dozmary and passed upon the mere. 
 </p><p align="left">   
 </p><p align="left">           Camelford--Slaughter    Bridge 
 </p><p align="left">                         II. 
 </p><p align="left"> In the soft prelude of an August night<br>       We sallied forth from Camelford in quest<br>       Of where his last great battle in the west<br>    Brought death to Athur. Grey the gloaming light<br>    Ere we were in the valley of the fight,<br>       A spot by Nature framed for fierce contest,<br>       With ridge commanding ridge, and crest on crest,<br>    On either side a little river, bright<br>       With waving sedge and darting trout. The bridge<br>          Was wreathed with blackhaired spleenwort    and wild flowers,<br>       And the rank grass beneath the lowest bridge<br>          Guarded a stone, in characters not ours,<br>       Claimed by the country-folk with wondering eyes<br>       To tell that Arthur underneath it lies.  
 </p><p align="left">
