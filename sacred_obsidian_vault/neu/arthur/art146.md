 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">SIR LAUNCELOT AND THE SANCGREAL</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ERNEST RHYS</h2>  </center>  <br>                                              "Car il<sup> 1</sup>   n'or à nul pech'eour<br>                                            Ne compaignie ne amour."<br> <br> <br>  He found a chamber where the door was shut,<br>      And thereto set his hand to open it;<br>  And mightily he tried, and still might not:<br>      And then he heard a voice which sang so sweet,<br>  It seemed none earthly thing that he heard sing,<br>                            "Honour and joy be given<br>                            To the High King of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  It seemed none earthly thing that sung therein,<br>      So sweet the voice, it near had made him greet,--<br>  For well he knew his body, stained with sin,<br>      Was for that mystic chamber all unmeet,<br>  Wherein those voices rang, yes, choired and sang;<br>                           "Honour and joy be given<br>                           To the High King of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  For well he knew that there the Sancgreal<br>      Upon the board was set for sinless souls,<br>  While the three rays shone sidelong down the wall;<br>      While he without did kneel with many a stain,<br>  And there to that hid noise he joined his voice,<br>                           "Pity and grace be given,<br>                           To me, lost child of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  With that he saw the chamber door unclose,<br>      And out there shone a clearness and a light<br>  As all the torches in the world that house<br>      Had lighted and been borne there burning bright<br>  About the Sancgreal, while sang they all,<br>                           "Honour and joy be given,<br>                           To the sweet lord of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  Oh, much he marvelled, and would  enter in,<br>      And cried, "Fair Father Jesu" in his need,<br>  Remembering then men's woe and mortal sin<br>      For which the Christ upon the Cross did bleed,--<br>  Yes, crying still that prayer, he entered there,--<br>                           "Pity and grace be given<br>                           To me, poor knight of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  Right so he entered, where the Sancgreal<br>      Did shine to greet him; but a gust of fire,<br>  And a grim smoke, there smote and made him fall;<br>      It took his body's might, and all desire;<br>  He had no voice nor will, though they sang still,<br>                           "Honour and joy be given,<br>                           To the High King of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  Then many hands did raise and bear him out,<br>      And there all night he lay, till morning time;<br>  And many a day like dead lay Launcelot,<br>      He heard no bell at matin or at prime:<br>  Nathless he deem'd did sing, none earthly thing,<br>                           "Honour and joy be given,<br>                           To the High King of Heaven!"<br>  <br>  Then came a dayspring and a fair white dawn,<br>      And he rose up, yet did not rise the same:<br>  For all the bitterness and pain were gone:<br>      For he who sinn'd the sin had borne the shame,<br>  And seen the Sancgreal, and heard them call,<br>                           "Honour and joy be given,<br>                           To the High King of Heaven!"<br>  <br>             *          *           *           *           * <br> <br>  Oh now, frail sons of earth, who fell in sin;<br>      Learn from the piteous deed of this dread knight,<br>  Beat at the door, and cry, and enter in,<br>      And you shall win the Grail, and see the Light,<br>  Yes, like none earthly thing, shall hear them sing,<br>                           "Honour and joy be given,<br>                           To the High King of Heaven!"<br>   <br> <br>  <a> <sup>
