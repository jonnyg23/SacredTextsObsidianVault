 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER"> MERLIN I</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">RALPH WALDO EMERSON</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> Thy trivial harp will never please<br> Or fill my craving ear;<br> Its chords should ring as blows the breeze,<br> Free, peremptory, clear.<br> No jingling serenader's art,<br> Nor tinkle of piano strings,<br> Can make the wild blood start<br> In its mystic springs.<br> The kingly bard<br> Must smite the chords rudely and hard,<br> As with hammer or with mace;<br> That they may render back<br> Artful thunder, which conveys<br> Secrets of the solar track,<br> Sparks of the supersolar blaze.<br> <br> Merlin's blows are strokes of fate,<br> Chiming with the forest tone,<br> When boughs buffet boughs in the wood;<br> Chiming with the gasp and moan<br> Of the ice-imprisoned flood;<br> With the pulse of manly hearts;<br> With the voice or orators;<br> With the din of city arts;<br> With the cannonade of wars;<br> With the marches of the brave;<br> And prayers of might from martyrs' cave.<br> <br> Great is the art,<br> Great be the manners, of the bard.<br> He shall not his brain encumber<br> With the coil of rhythm and number;<br> But, leaving rule and pale forethought,<br> He shall aye climb<br> For his rhyme.<br> "Pass in, pass in," the angels say,<br> "In to the upper doors,<br> Nor count compartments of the floors,<br> But mount to paradise<br> By the stairway of surprise."<br> <br> Blameless master of the games,<br> King of sport that never shames,<br> He shall daily joy dispense<br> Hid in song's sweet influence.<br> Forms more cheerly live and go,<br> What time the subtle mind<br> Sings aloud the tune whereto<br> Their pulses beat,<br> And march their feet,<br> And their members are combined.<br> <br> By Sybarites beguiled,<br> He shall no task decline;<br> Merlin's mighty line <br> Extremes of nature reconciled,--<br> Bereaved a tyrant of his will,<br> And made the lion mild.<br> Songs can the tempest still,<br> Scattered on the stormy air,<br> Mold the year to fair increase,<br> And bring in poetic peace.<br> He shall not seek to weave,<br> In weak, unhappy times,<br> Efficacious rhymes;<br> Wait his returning strength.<br> Bird that from the nadir's floor<br> To the zenith's top can soar,--<br> The soaring orbit of the muse exceeds that journey's length.<br> Nor profane affect to hit<br> Or compass that, by meddling wit,<br> Which only the propitious mind<br> Publishes when 'tis inclined.<br> There are open hours<br> When the God's will sallies free,<br> And the dull idiot might see<br> The flowing fortunes of a thousand years;--<br> Sudden, at unawares,<br> Self-moved, fly-to the doors,<br> Nor sword of angels could reveal<br> What they conceal.<br>
