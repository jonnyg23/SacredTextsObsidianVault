 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER"> MERLIN'S LAST PROPHECY </h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER"> GEORGE DARLEY</h2>  </center>  <br>  "Come ye from far, wild Ocean Daughters!<br>  Shell-borne on the dangerous sea,<br>  With pearly oars that ply the waters,<br>  Say, bright Strangers, whence ye be?"<br>  <br>  From a far Isle in unknown waters<br>  Fleeting like mist the windswept sea,<br>  We come--wise Merlin's potent Daughters,<br>  Morgain-le-fay's handmaidens we!<br>  <br>  Wide our gossamer sails unfurling,<br>  With coral prow we stem the spray,<br>  Our crisp shell behind us curling<br>  Keeps the plashy surge away.<br>  <br>  Fear not baleful charms or chidings,<br>  Sweet our words as dropping balm,<br>  Peace we bring and gentle tidings,<br>  Keep thy heart in holiest calm.<br>  <br>  The wild winds lull to our harps' soft numbers,<br>  Lo! when music meet our hands,<br>  Even the restless Ocean slumbers<br>  Hugely on his bed of sands.<br>  <br>  Lo! how swift our notes of pleasure<br>  Sparkle along the golden strings,<br>  While in rapturous mood and measure<br>  Her dulcet verse each Maiden sings.<br>  <br>  We o'er your land, like Guardian Spirits,<br>  From our far isle of Avalon,<br>  Watch, and o'er all that here inherits,<br>  Beautiful daughter and brave son.<br>  <br>  Favour to One grew strong and stronger,<br>  Who from her bright looks aye hath been<br>  Our Princess Fair-star: now no longer,<br>  But Britain's fair and starry Queen.<br>  <br>  Say to that young and sovran Beauty<br>  This message hymned to thee alone,<br>  Offering these gifts with proudest duty<br>  At the bright foot which gems her throne.<br>  <br>  Morgain to Britain's Queen commendeth<br>  This magic Trident, virtue-stored;<br>  Pendragon's Son, fair greeting, sendeth<br>  <i> Caliburn</i> , his enchanted sword.<br>  <br>  Rapt Merlin sings: "where a strengthless Woman<br>  This sceptre holds with a firm strain,<br>  That Land, maugre East and Western foeman,<br>  Shall rule both East and Western main.<br>  <br>  Where, with the same small clasp and slender,<br>  This sword pale Resolution draws,<br>  That Land need pray nought else defend her<br>  But grace of God and her good Cause!"<br>  <br>  Farewell!--the mortal arms that kept her<br>  Safe, thro' the Past, its rage might quell,<br>  But 'tis no common sword and sceptre<br>  Shall sway Futurity!--Farewell!<br>
