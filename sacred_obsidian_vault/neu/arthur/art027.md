 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">EXCALIBUR</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">SALLIE BRIDGES</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>  For months it rested in the stone,<br>            The sword Excalibur;<br>  The noblest knights of England's realm<br>            Strove hard the steel to stir;<br>  For word had gone through all the land<br>            That he who drew the blade<br>  Should fill the sovereign's empty throne,<br>            The rightful king be made.<br>  <br>  The flower of island chivalry<br>            Had come from far and near,<br>  To try their skill at tournament<br>            The first day of the year.<br>  Mid the barons went Sir Ector,<br>            His valiant son, Sir Kaye,<br>  And his foster-child, young Arthur,<br>            Forth to the courtly fray.<br>  <br>  Unknown to all, dead Uther's son<br>            Mix'd with the noble throng,<br>  Who dream'd not that to stripling page<br>            Could crown and throne belong.<br>  "Now, grammercy," quoth Arthur,<br>            In riding by Sir Kaye,<br>  "Good brother mine, how came you out<br>            Without a sword to-day?"<br>  <br>  Sir Kaye look'd down, and paled to see<br>            No weapon at his side:<br>  Then back his comrade spurr'd his steed,<br>            Across the meadows wide,<br>  To where lay idle in its sheath<br>            The knight's forgotten blade,<br>  But found that not a single squire<br>            Had in the castle stay'd.<br>  <br>  Quoth Arthur then, with sudden wrath,<br>             "From yonder mystic stone<br>  I'll pluck the sword, that good Sir Kaye<br>            May wield it as his own!"<br>  So, lighting down from off his horse,<br>            Towards the empty tent<br>  In which was kept Excalibur<br>            His eager footsteps bent.<br>  <br>  In golden-letter'd hilt was bright,<br>            Its knightly guards away;<br>  And so, with brave and fearless heart,<br>            He made his bold essay.<br>  He grasp'd the handle in his hand,<br>            Its point leap'd sharp and free.<br>  "My brother shall not go unarm'd<br>            To battle now!" cried he.<br>  <br>  When old Sir Ector saw the blade<br>            Flash in the morning light,<br>  He knew it was the Sword of Fate<br>            That met his wondering sight,<br>  And ask'd of Arthur, "Whither came<br>            The steel thou gavest Sir Kaye?"<br>  "I bore it," was the plain reply,<br>             "From stone and tent away."<br>  <br>  "Then, by my faith," the gray knight swore,<br>             "An' thou canst draw again<br>  The sword from out the selfsame place,<br>            A monarch thou shalt reign!"<br>  And back within the marble stone<br>            Prince Arthur thrust the blade,<br>  While long in vain to pluck it thence<br>            Both high and low essay'd.<br>  <br>  "Come hither; strive again, my son!"<br>            And quick on Ector's sight,<br>  In Arthur's hand, the marvellous steel<br>            Was flashing keen and bright.<br>  Then kneel'd Sir Ector and Sir Kaye,<br>            With every squire and lord,<br>  To greet as lawful king the youth<br>            Who lean'd upon the Sword!<br>  <br>  Then spoke his aged foster-sire.<br>             "Ye hail no child of mine!"<br>  But wist not buried Uther's heir<br>            Was king by right divine.<br>  Thus Arthur through Excalibur<br>            Received his father's crown;<br>  And ever through Excalibur<br>            He kept his high renown!<br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>
