 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">VIVIEN</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ALAN SEEGER</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> Her eyes under their lashes were blue pools<br> Fringed round with lilies; her bright hair unfurled<br> Clothed her as sunshine clothes the summer world.<br> Her robes were gauzes--gold and green and gules,<br> All furry things flocked round her, from her hand<br> Nibbling their foods and fawning at her feet.<br> Two peacocks watched her where she made her seat<br> Beside a fountain in Broceliande.<br> Sometimes she sang. . . .  Whoever heard forgot<br> Errand and aim, and knights at noontide here,<br> Riding from fabulous gestes beyond the seas,<br> Would follow, tranced, and seek . . . and find her not . . .<br> But wake that night, lost, by some woodland mere,<br> Powdered with stars and rimmed with silent trees.<br>
