 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE LEGEND OF KING ARTHUR<br>  from <i> RELIQUES OF ANCIENT ENGLISH POETRY</i> </h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">BISHOP THOMAS PERCY</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>  Of Brutus' blood, in Brittaine borne,<br>     King Arthur I am to name;<br>  Through Christendome and Heathynesse<br>     Well knowne is my worthy fame.<br>  <br>  In Jesus Christ I doe beleeve;<br>     I am a Christyan bore;<br>  The Father, Sone, and Holy Gost,<br>     One God, I doe adore.<br>  <br>  In the four hundred ninetieth yeere,<br>     Oer Brittaine I did rayne,<br>  After my Savior Christ his byrth,<br>     What time I did maintaine<br>  <br>  The fellowshipp of the Table Round,<br>     Soe famous in those dayes;<br>  Whereatt a hundred noble knights<br>     And thirty sat alwayes:<br>  <br>  Who for their deeds and martiall feates,<br>     As bookes done yett record,<br>  Amongst all other nations<br>     Wer feared through the world<br>  <br>  And in the castle of Tyntagill<br>     King Uther mee begate,<br>  Of Agyana, a bewtyous ladye,<br>     And come of hie estate.<br>  <br>  And when I was fifteen yeere old,<br>     Then was I crowned kinge:<br>  All Brittaine, that was att an upròre,<br>     I did to quiett bringe;<br>  <br>  And drove the Saxons from the realme,<br>     Who had opprest this land;<br>  All Scotland then, throughe manly feates,<br>     I conquered with my hand.<br>  <br>  Ireland, Denmarke, Norwaye,<br>     These countryes wan I all;<br>  Iseland, Gotheland, and Swetheland;<br>     And made their kings my thrall.<br>  <br>  I conquered all Gallya,<br>     That now is called France;<br>  And slew the hardye Froll in feild,<br>     My honor to advance.<br>  <br>  And the ugly gyant Dynabus,<br>     Soe terrible to vewe,<br>  That in Saint Barnards mount did lye,<br>     By force of armes I slew.<br>  <br>  And Lucyus, the emperour of Rome,<br>     I brought to deadly wracke;<br>  And a thousand more of noble knightes<br>     For feare did turne their backe.<br>  <br>  Five kinges of paynims I did kill<br>     Amidst that bloody strife;<br>  Besides the Grecian emperour,<br>     Who alsoe lost his liffe.<br>  <br>  Whose carcasse I did send to Rome,<br>     Cladd poorlye on a beere;<br>  And afterward I past Mount-Joye<br>     The next approaching yeere.<br>  <br>  Then I came to Rome, where I was mett<br>     Right as a conquerour,<br>  And by all the cardinalls solempnelye<br>     I was crowned an emperour.<br>  <br>  One winter there I made abode,<br>     Then word to mee was brought,<br>  How Mordred had oppressd the crowne,<br>     What treason he had wrought<br>  <br>  Att home in Brittaine with my queene:<br>     Therfore I came with speede<br>  To Brittaine backe, with all my power,<br>     To quitt that traiterous deede;<br>  <br>  And soone at Sandwiche I arrivde,<br>     Where Mordred me withstoode:<br>  But yett at last I landed there,<br>     With effusion of much blood.<br>  <br>  For there my nephew Sir Gawaine dyed,<br>     Being wounded in that sore<br>  The whiche Sir Launcelot in fight<br>     Had given him before.<br>  <br>  Then chased I Mordered away,<br>     Who fledd to London right,<br>  From London to Winchester, and<br>     To Cornwalle tooke his flyght.<br>  <br>  And still I him pursued with speed,<br>     Till at the last wee mett;<br>  Wherby an appointed day of fight<br>     Was there agreed and set:<br>  <br>  Where we did fight, of mortal life<br>     Eche other to deprive,<br>  Till of a hundred thousand men<br>     Scarce one was left alive.<br>  <br>  There all the noble chivalrye<br>     Of Brittaine took their end.<br>  O see how fickle is their state<br>     That doe on fates depend!<br>  <br>  There all the traiterous men were slaine,<br>     Not one escapte away;<br>  And there dyed all my vallyant knightes.<br>     Alas! that woefull day!<br>  <br>  Two and twenty yeere I ware the crowne<br>     In honor and great fame,<br>  And thus by death was suddenlye<br>     Deprived of the same.<br>
