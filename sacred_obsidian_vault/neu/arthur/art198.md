 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">EXCALIBUR</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">JOHN GROSVENOR WILSON</h2>  </center>  <br>   "Thou art here, the Lord's Anointed,  <br>          King of men and knight of heaven, <br>  To the trust thou art appointed, <br>          Unto thee the sword is given; <br>  As a sign for thee, a token<br>          That the light again is breaking <br>  Thro' the gloom of time unspoken <br>          To the dawn and to the waking. <br>          <br>  "When the fields of heaven sounded<br>          With the roar and shout of battle, <br>  And the whirling spears rebounded<br>          From the shields with ring and rattle, <br>  This the sword that Michael wielded<br>          With a fire and strength supernal, <br>  Till the rebel hosts, unshielded, <br>          Fled from light to night eternal. <br>          <br>  "Strong as Michael shalt thou fight them, <br>          They that bow before the idols, <br>  And the blazing sword shall smite them <br>          From the horses and the bridles; <br>  Yea, the heathen, stunned with terror, <br>          Shall behold the blade advancing, <br>  With the flame that burneth error<br>          From the hilt and jewels glancing. <br>          <br>  "Lo, the weary world is weeping<br>          In the toil of her transgression<br>  For the justice that is sleeping, <br>          For the wrong and the oppression,<br>  For the nights that quake and quiver, <br>          For the days that dawn in thunder, <br>  For the years that shrink and shiver<br>          At the shame and at the wonder. <br>          <br>  "But the guilty shalt thou scatter, <br>          And the sword will bravely brighten<br>  As the standards sink and shatter, <br>          And the foeman's face shall whiten<br>  At the sound and at the glitter<br>          Of the brand above him flashing,<br>  Till the death-shriek, shrill and bitter, <br>          Drown the din of armor clashing. <br>          <br>  "So thy kingdom shall be founded, <br>          And by justice thou shalt hold it, <br>  And its fame shall be unbounded<br>          As the wide seas that enfold it; <br>  And the harvest hailing hither<br>          Shall encamp on all the acres, <br>  And the noxious weeds shall wither<br>          As the sea-foam on the breakers. <br>          <br>  "Till the right grown wrong with surfeit <br>          Shall uprise and challenge proudly, <br>  Then the peace shall all be forfeit, <br>          And the trumpet-call sound loudly; <br>  Then the sword shall rouse the thunder, <br>          And the echoes will awaken<br>  All along the sea, and under, <br>          Where the mermaid rides the kraken. <br>          <br>  "For the cycle will be ended, <br>          And shall come the desolation, <br>  And the close shall be attended<br>          With a noise of devastation; <br>  And the ruin will be utter, <br>          For the foe shall overmaster, <br>  And the royal flag shall flutter<br>          To defeat and to disaster. <br>          <br>  "When from out the nether regions, <br>          With a mighty thunder-rattle, <br>  Ride the clanging, shouting legions<br>          Of the storm and of the battle, <br>  Then the solemn doom shall follow, <br>          And the King shall bow before it; <br>  When the great sword striketh hollow, <br>          To the great giver then restore it." <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>
