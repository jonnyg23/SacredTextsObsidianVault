 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE DAY BEFORE THE TRIAL</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ALGERNON CHARLES SWINBURNE</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> King Arthur says being alone.<br> 	Now the day comes near and near<br> 	I feel its hot breath, and see it clear,<br> 	How strange it is and full of fear;<br> 	And I grow old waiting here,<br> 	Grow sick with pain of Guenevere,<br> 	  My wife, that loves not me.<br> 	So strange it seems to me, so new<br> 	To have such shame between us two,<br> 	I dare not hold this Mador true<br> 	Nor false, because his words ran thro'<br> 	My blood with all the shame they drew<br> 	And burnt me to the bone; I knew<br> 	  That some such tale would be<br> 	For all these years she grew more fair,<br> 	More sweet her low sweet speeches were,<br> 	More long and heavy grew her hair,<br> 	Not such as other women wear;<br> 	But ever as I looked on her<br> 	  Her face seemed fierce and thin.<br> 	I felt half sick, and on my head<br> 	The gold crown seemed not gold but lead;<br> 	Strange words I heard that no man said,<br> 	Strange noises where all noise was dead;<br> 	Was it pure blood that made her red<br> 	  From brows to rounded chin?<br> 	Sometimes I knew she loved me not;<br> 	Down to my hands the blood went hot<br> 	In a dull hate of Launcelot<br> 	For all the praise of her he got,<br> 	  Being so pure of sin.<br> 	For he was clean as any maid,<br> 	And on his head God's hand was laid<br> 	As on a maiden's; so men said;<br> 	But I, a woman's hands there weighed<br> 	Instead of God's upon my head,<br> 	  No maid was I, to see<br> 	The white Sangreal borne up in air,<br> 	To touch at last God's body fair,<br> 	To feel strange terror stir my hair<br> 	As a slow light went past; but here<br> 	I had to my honours year by year,<br> 	I had the name of king to bear,<br> 	And watch the eyes of Guenevere,<br> 	  My wife, who loves not me.<br>
