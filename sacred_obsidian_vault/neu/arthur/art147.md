 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE CITY OF SARRAS</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ERNEST RHYS</h2>  </center>  <br>    <blockquote>      "I require you that ye bury me not in this country, but as soon as I am dead put me in a boat at the next haven, . . . and as soon as ye three come to the city of Sarras, there to achieve the Holy Grail, . . . there  bury me in the spiritual place."</blockquote> <br> <br>                            I<br>  OH, have you not heard of Sir Galahad,<br>      Sir Bors and Sir Percival,--<br>  How they came to the Castle of Carbonek,--<br>      On the Quest of the Sancgreal?<br> <br>                             II<br>  They sate at King Pelles' table,--<br>      And they saw a Spear that bled<br>  Three drops of blood most marvellous,--<br>      And a marvellous sweet voice said,--<br> <br>                             III<br>  "Sir Galahad,--Sir Galahad!<br>      Sir Bors, and Sir Percival!"--<br>  And all three saw a shining form<br>      By the cup of the Sancgreal.<br> <br>                             IV<br>  "This is Joseph of Arimathea,"<br>      It said, "The which had grace;<br>  Which was saved in the City of Sarras<br>      In the Spiritual Place!"<br> <br>                             V<br>  They grew adread to see the form<br>      Of one dead, three hundred year!<br>  But Joseph said, "A man like you,<br>      Look on me,--have no fear!"<br> <br>                             VI<br>  Then they saw two angels stand there,<br>      Wax candles in their hand:<br>  And Joseph of Arimathea<br>      Between that twain did stand.<br> <br>                             VII<br>  "Now," said he, "servants of Jesu Christ<br>      All three, you shall be fed<br>  Afore this table with meats, more sweet<br>      Than any knight ate," he said:<br>  But when he had said it, he vanished away,<br>      And the greater grew their dread.<br> <br>                             VIII<br>  Then came One from the Holy Grail,--<br>      They saw his blood; they knew the Light!<br>  My knights, he said, my true children:<br>      You shall taste of the Grail this night.<br> <br>                             IX<br>  Straightway Sir Galahad kneeled down,--<br>      Sir Bors, and Sir Percival:<br>  And they humbly received their Saviour<br>      And partook of the Sancgreal.<br> <br>                             X<br>  Too sweet for earth its savour was;--<br>      Too marvellous to be told<br>  Was the Mystery, and beyond man's sight<br>      What the three knights saw unroll'd.<br> <br>                             XI<br>  This night, said he, you have seen much:<br>      But after Night, the Day;<br>  And here in the realm of Logris<br>      The Sancgreal cannot stay.<br> <br>                             XII<br>  You have seen this night your souls' desire;<br>      But there waits a Mystery<br>  More strange, my knights, than you can think<br>      Till to Sarras you sail the sea,--<br> <br>                             XIII<br>  Till you come where Joseph of Arimathea<br>      Stood with me, face to face;--<br>  Till you stand in the City of Sarras,<br>      In the Spiritual Place.<br> <br>
