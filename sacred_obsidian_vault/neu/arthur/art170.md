 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">GALAHAD IN THE CASTLE OF THE MAIDENS</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">SARA TEASDALE</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>   (To the maiden with the hidden face in Abbey's painting)<br> <br> The other maidens raised their eyes to him<br> Who stumbled in before them when the fight<br> Had left him victor, with a victor's right.<br> I think his eyes with quick hot tears grew dim;<br> He scarcely saw her swaying white and slim,<br> And trembling slightly, dreaming of his might,<br> Nor knew he touched her hand, as cool and light<br> As a wan wraith's beside a river's rim.<br> The other maidens raised their eyes to see<br> And only she has hid her face away,<br> And yet I think she loved him more than they,<br> And very fairly fashioned was her face.<br> Yet for Love's shame and sweet humility,<br> She could not meet him with their queenlike grace.<br>
