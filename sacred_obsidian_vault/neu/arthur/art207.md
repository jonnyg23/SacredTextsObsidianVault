 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE SAN-GRAIL</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ELLA YOUNG</h2>  </center>  <br>  Sir Lancelot rode between the trees,<br>  The evening sun was red:<br>  He thought upon Queen Guinevere<br>  With hair outspread.<br>  <br>  He thought upon the Holy Grail<br>  That he had come to win,<br>  And knew his love of Guinevere<br>  Was deadly sin.<br>  <br>  Then from his horse he lighted down<br>  Beneath the treen<br>  And prayed that God would sain his soul<br>  Of Arthur's queen.<br>  <br>  A sudden glory filled the wood,<br>  And there his eyes<br>  Beheld a wonder that had come<br>  From Paradise.<br>  <br>  He saw the Grail-Maid with the Grail<br>  Between her hands,<br>  The sight that is most beautiful<br>  In the world's lands.<br>  <br>  Full humbly Lancelot bowed himself<br>  Shamed and afraid<br>  But the Grail-Maiden said to him<br>  "Be not dismayed."<br>  <br>  "Look up," she said, "and see the Grail<br>  If thine eyes endure<br>  And if thy heart hath flame of love<br>  Through Love made pure."<br>  <br>  He lifted up his eyes if so<br>  The San-Grail might be found:<br>  The splendour smote him to the earth<br>  Deep in a swound.<br>  <br>  But through the swound Sir Lancelot knew<br>  The Grail-Maid stood<br>  Like a bright-coloured bird of Heaven<br>  In a dark wood.<br>  <br>  And he besought of Christ the Lord<br>  To lend him grace,<br>  Although he might not see the Grail,<br>  To see her face.<br>  <br>  "Look up," she said, "and see my face,<br>  The grace is won:<br>  Quenched in me now the moon-fire is<br>  And the fire of the sun."<br>  <br>  He looked upon the Grail-Maid's face<br>  Enshadowed by her hair:<br>  He knew her, wan, and white, and still,<br>  Queen Guinevere!<br>
