 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">MERLIN AND VIVIAN: A LYRIC DRAMA</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">poem by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ETHEL WATTS MUMFORD</h2>  </center>  <br>  <center>CHARACTERS</center> <br>  <br>  MORGAN-LE-FAY, <i> enchantress, queen of Avalon; soprano</i> <br>  VIVIAN, <i> sorceress; mezzo-soprano</i> <br>  KING ARTHUR, <i> tenor</i> <br>  MERLIN, <i> the enchanter, Arthur's councilor; tenor</i> <br>  ADRIHIM, <i> the Spirit of the architect of King Sueliman; bass</i> <br>  ARIEL, <i> the Spirit of music and light; soprano</i> <br>  A HERALD, <i> tenor</i> <br>  <br>  <center><i> Chorus of Fairies, Knights and Spirits</i></center>  <br>  <br>  <center><i> The scene of PART I is laid on the Isle of Avalon; of PART II,<br>  At King Arthur's court; of PART III, at Castle Joyousguard.</i></center>  <br>  <br>  <br>  <center>PART I</center> <br>  <br>  <i> A fairy isle, Avalon, which floats at the will of Morgan-le-Fay, its queen, the enemy of Merlin and King Arthur.  In the distance rises the palace, where dwell Vivian, the sorceress, and the ladies of the court; terraces descend to the sea, where are marble quays, and the anchorage of the Ship of Dreams.</i> <br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  O Isle of Dreams, O Avalon,<br>     Thy shores are strewn with pearl and gold,<br>  Across thy lakes the rainbows gleam,<br>     Around thy hills the sea-mists fold;<br>  All that is fair, all that is fell,<br>     Is in thy form of fairy mold.<br>  <br>  Across the drowsy purple seas<br>     Thou floatest as a thing of light.<br>  No tempests vex thy golden days,<br>     No bitter winds assail thy night,<br>  O charmèd Isle by magic raised,<br>     O dear rose-garden of Delight!<br>  <br>  Enchanted Island, Avalon,<br>     Morgan-le-Fay is owned thy Queen,<br>  She of the veiled, mysterious eyes,<br>     She of the cold and courtly mien,<br>  She of the skilled and subtle hand<br>     That feeds the ring-doves as they preen.<br>  <br>  Upon thy marble terraces<br>     The Queen sits in her regal state,<br>  Upon her hand the witchcraft ring<br>     That gleams with evil eyes of Fate,<br>  Within her heart an evil wish,<br>     Within her soul the soul of Hate.<br>  <br>  MORGAN-LE-FAY<br>  Now who shall slay mine enemy?<br>     And who shall dupe Merlin the wise?<br>  Destroy the power of his hands,<br>     And close his all unsleeping eyes?<br>  Lo!  all my wisdom fails and dies,<br>     My blackest arts are shorn of span;<br>  I may not reach his soul unstained,<br>     I may not place him 'neath my ban.<br>  By love alone may he be led:<br>     Send me the Lady Vivian!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  Across the lawns with daisies pied<br>     She comes, the Lady Vivian!<br>  Her eyes are bright as sparkling jet,<br>     She moves as only fairies can;<br>  Her hair is gold, and bound with gems,<br>     She bears a jewelled peacock fan.<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>  Within my chamber, rose-bedight,<br>     Subtle and sure I felt thy call;<br>  My one desire thou long hast known:<br>     Of magic power I must know all!<br>  I felt thy wish, and thy will of me,<br>  Draw as the tides draw in the sea;<br>  And I am come, my Queen, to thee,<br>     From Bower and Hall.<br>  <br>  Lo!  I am fair as the gods were fair,<br>     Fair as the nymphs that haunt the sea,<br>  And I shall learn, ere I do thy will,<br>     The magic power of witchery.<br>  I will seek the court where Merlin dwells,<br>  I will win his love by magic spells,<br>  I will damn his soul to a thousand hells<br>     With his own sorcery!<br>  <br>  I shall be wise as thou art wise,<br>     And in my hand he shall be as clay;<br>  I will lure his heart as the fowler lures,<br>     Who calls the bird but to maim and slay.<br>  Give me the ring of Love and Hate;<br>  My hand shall be as the hand of Fate;<br>  He shall find Death when he seeks a mate,<br>     Morgan-le-Fay!<br>  <br>  <center><i> Vivian and Morgan-le-Fay prepare<br>  for the incantation.</i></center>  <br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  See, they have brought the golden lamps,<br>     And magic oils of strange perfume.<br>  And lo!  they sing the ancient songs,<br>     They chant the long-forgotten rune.<br>  The magic ship draws close amain,<br>     Across the sapphire seas of June.<br>  <br>  <center><i> The Incantation of the Magic Ship.</i> </center> <br>  <br>  MORGAN-LE-FAY AND VIVIAN<br>        Come, I command thee,<br>           Ship of enchantment,<br>        Rune of the waters<br>           Sing I to thee;<br>        Come from thy harbor,<br>           The far, golden haven,<br>        Where lost ships foregather<br>           Mysteriously.<br>        Come, come!<br>           Come, come!<br>        Cordage of silver,<br>           Silken of sail;<br>        Come through the calms,<br>           The foam from thee flying,<br>        Swift as though borne<br>           On the wings of the gale.<br>        Charms of Phoenicia,<br>           Runes of the Northland,<br>        All the sea's magic,<br>           To thee I sing.<br>        Hither, O hither,<br>           Ship of enchantment!<br>        Come, I command thee, come!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  She comes, she comes, the ship of dreams<br>     Across the far, mysterious sea;<br>  Red as blood are her sails of samite,<br>     Her banners golden 'broidery,<br>  With seamaids floating by her side,<br>     She comes, the ship of Sorcery!<br>  <br>  <i> Vivian departing for the Court of King Arthur.</i> <br>  <br>  MORGAN-LE-FAY<br>  Farewell, farewell, my Vivian!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>     Oh, Fair and Fell,<br>     Farewell, farewell!<br>  <br>  MORGAN-LE-FAY AND CHORUS<br>  Now all the demons down in hell<br>  Shall guard thee, Lady Vivian!<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>  For life or death, for weal or woe,<br>  Morgan-le-Fay, I go, I go!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>     Oh, Fair and Fell,<br>     Farewell, farewell!<br>  <br>  MORGAN-LE-FAY AND CHORUS<br>  Now all the hosts of blackest hell<br>  Guard thee, O Lady Vivian!<br>  <br>  VIVIAN AND CHORUS<br>     Farewell, farewell!<br>  <br>  <br>  <center>PART II</center>  <br>  <br>  <i> Hall of the Knights of the Round Table:  King Arthur presiding, with Merlin, his chief councilor, at his right hand.  The knights are assembled and the feast is in progress when Vivian craves admission as the bearer of a peace-token from Morgan-le-Fay, kinswoman and enemy to King Arthur.</i> <br>  <br>  CHORUS OF MEN<br>     Hail to Arthur!<br>        Hail to the lord!<br>     King and Host<br>        Of the Knightly board!<br>     Hail, all hail!<br>        Let the hall resound <br>     To the ringing mirth<br>        Of the Table Round!<br>  <br>  Hail!  hail!  hail!<br>     With a roar of right good cheer<br>  We drink the health in ruby wine<br>     Of fair Queen Guenevere.<br>  <br>  To Guenevere and her gentle King<br>     Love and loyalty here we bring.<br>  What ho!  squires and pages fair!<br>     Wine!  wine!  in goblets rich and rare!<br>  For the pledge is the Table Round,<br>     And with mirth let the halls resound.<br>        Hail, all hail!<br>           Let the hall resound<br>        To the ringing mirth<br>           Of the Table Round!<br>  <br>  KING ARTHUR<br>  Good men and true, I give ye royal greeting,<br>     And quaff with joy your pledge of fealty;<br>  May love preside at this, our joyful meeting,<br>     And love prevail 'twixt all the world and me!<br>  <br>  HERALD<br>  My Lord, without the hall doth stand<br>     The Princess of Northumberland.<br>  <br>  CHORUS <br>  Across the floors vermilion-tiled<br>     She comes, my Lady Vivian!<br>  Her eyes are bright as sparkling jet,<br>     She moves as only fairies can;<br>  Her hair is gold, and bound with gems,<br>     She bears a jewelled peacock fan.<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>        I sing a land<br>     Most radiant and serene;<br>        With roses dight,<br>        And silver paths;<br>     The emerald lawns between,<br>        There clad in white,<br>        Fair ladies move,<br>     As fair as lilies of<br>        The beauteous night;<br>        And one most fair,<br>     Our radiant queen,<br>        Hath sent me here<br>     To bear her token,<br>        And sweetest greeting,<br>     Her faith unbroken,<br>        Her pledge this ring:<br>  <br>        "Upon his hand,"<br>        She smiling saith,<br>     "Who shall be provèd<br>        Wisest and best,<br>     This ring to him is given,<br>        This ring for love;<br>        And it shall know,<br>     Upon no other finger go<br>        In all the land,<br>        Of all the rest!"<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  Lo, she hath paced about the hall,<br>     To each in turn she bears the ring;<br>  It grows, it shrinks, it changes shape,<br>     It sets the knights to marveling;<br>  Sir Launcelot, Sir Bores have failed,<br>     It will not fit the knightly King.<br>  <br>  Now to Merlin she slowly kneels,<br>     Upon his finger slips the band:<br>  The magic gold holds fast and strong!<br>     He may not tear it from his hand!<br>  About his heart and through his soul<br>     Rush the dark spells of Fairyland.<br>  <br>  <i> Because of the magic of the ring, Merlin is con-<br>     sumed with passion for Lady Vivian.</i> <br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>     Belovèd, O Belovèd! <br>        O gaze on me,<br>     Eyes of immortal splendor,<br>  And let me lay my hand on thy bright hair;<br>        Thy lips are scarlet<br>     And thy voice is tender,<br>  Thy cheek is like the rose of Persia.<br>     Listen, O Belovèd!<br>  <br>     Lo!  all my power<br>  I hold alone for thee!<br>     Give me thy heart,<br>     Thy love for an hour,<br>  And thou shalt have dominion<br>     O'er all the sea and earth;<br>     Joyance and mirth<br>        Shall fill thy days,<br>  For thee I'll rear another Fairyland!<br>  <br>  MERLIN, VIVIAN <i> (feigning love that she may<br>     acquire Merlin's learning and then destroy him)</i> <br>  At last, 't is thou for whom my soul hath cried!<br>  At last, 't is thou!  What matter aught beside?<br>  O royal love, I know that thou art mine!<br>  And all my joy, to know that I am thine!<br>  Beyond all goals for which the world hath<br>     striven,<br>  E'en to the stars our loyal fate is riven!<br>  Ah!  this is life!  that thou at last art near!<br>  Ah!  this is life!  that thou hast found me dear!<br>  Joy of the world, in one mad moment given,<br>  Joy of the world, and glorious ecstasy of heaven!<br>  Lo!  I am thine, for ever thine,<br>     Through life and in death!<br>  <br>  KING ARTHUR<br>     Ah, heed her not!<br>        I fear for thee!<br>     Merlin, beware,<br>        'T is sorcery!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>     O, heed her not!<br>        We fear for thee!<br>     Merlin, beware,<br>        'T is sorcery!<br>     Behold, her hair<br>        Is rusted gold,<br>     Her face is young,<br>        Her eyes are old,<br>     Her cheeks are pale,<br>        She bringeth dole;<br>     Her glance doth show<br>        A forfeit soul!<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>     O heed them not!<br>        O come with me!<br>     Have faith, my lord,<br>        I love but thee!<br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>     Behold, thy hair<br>        Is shining gold,<br>     Thy beauty young,<br>        Thy wisdom old;<br>     Thy face is pale<br>        For love's sweet dole,<br>     But, O, my love,<br>        Thou art my soul!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  Now fare they forth across the world<br>     To find the fairyland of joy,<br>  Where dwell the Ladies of the Lake<br>     'Mid pleasures that can never cloy.<br>  And now they pause within a wood,<br>     The haunted forest of Arroy.<br>  <br>  <i> The fabled forest-haunt of the fairies-a glade sur-<br>  rounded by giant trees, in the centre of which rises<br>  a hill whereon, at the bidding of Merlin, the Spirits<br>  will build the Palace of Joyousguard.</i> <br>  <br>  MERLIN <i> (calling upon the Spirits)</i> <br>     Out of the womb of earth,<br>        Out of the caverned air,<br>     From seawaves green and gold,<br>        From fires' burning lair,<br>        Spirits of flame and wind,<br>        Ye who must serve the Tryne,<br>     The Star of the Mystic Inde,<br>        Come to my magic call!<br>        Come, I command ye!<br>  <br>  ARIEL <i> (the Spirit of music and light)</i> <br>     We come, we are here,<br>        We kiss thy hand.<br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS<br>     We come, we are near!<br>  <br>  ADRIHIM <i> (Spirit of the architect of Sueliman's <br>     Temple)</i> <br>  Salaam to thee!<br>  May the Wise One live a thousand years!<br>  For I am thy servant Adrihim,<br>  Adrihim, who builded <br>  The Palace of King Sueliman.<br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>     Rear me there a glorious dwelling,<br>        Gold and azure cramoisie,<br>     Rear its beauty past all telling,<br>        Fill it sweet with minstrelsy;<br>     Set its gardens fair and spacious,<br>        Fairest ever made for man,<br>     Fashion all things bright and gracious <br>        Fit for Lady Vivian!<br>  <br>  ARIEL<br>     We obey, obey!<br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS<br>     Thy will is law!<br>  <br>  ADRIHIM<br>     I hear, and obey!<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  So:  Joyousguard is builded fair,<br>  Formed from azure of the air,<br>  Warmed bright with sunset golden glows,<br>  Capped with shining argent snows;<br>  Throughout its courts perfumed with Spring<br>  Sweet young voices softly sing.<br>  <br>  ADRIHIM, ARIEL<br>  Master, thy slaves have built aright.<br>  Enter the Palace of Delight!<br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS<br>  Enter the Palace of Delight!<br>  <br>  <br>  <center>PART III</center> <br>  <br>  <br>  <i> The banquet-hall of Joyousguard, decked for a feast and garlanded with flowers.  Invisible attendants pass to and fro to strains of mysterious music.  Vivian has decided upon the destruction of Merlin.</i> <br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>     A year hath passed, and Vivian<br>        Hath all of Merlin's magic learned.<br>     Alas!  her heart of adamant<br>        Hath all his love and dolor spurned.<br>     Yet hath she smiled as lovers smile,<br>        While in her soul an hatred burned.<br>  <br>  <i> Vivian prepares a feast in honor of Merlin, where-<br>     at she will rid herself for ever of his presence.</i> <br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>     To me, Spirits all!<br>     List to my magic call!<br>     Rouse ye!  appear!<br>        Heed my command!<br>     Behold, a year<br>        Hath passed away,<br>  And I would feast my lord, Merlin;<br>  <br>  So I will brew a cup of Sleep,<br>  A draught of Silence, deep on deep;<br>  Into a tomb of carven stone<br>  He shall descend to dream alone;<br>  By his own thoughts companionèd,<br>  Living, to lie among the dead.<br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS <i> (terrified and angered)</i> <br>     Alas!  alas!  Pity!  pity!  O Vivian!<br>     We obey not thy behest!<br>        Woe!  woe!<br>     Guile, in the robe of Beauty drest!<br>     Now, by the souls with God at rest,<br>     We do defy thy will unblest!<br>  <br>  <i> Vivian compels the Spirits by the greater force <br>     of her evil magic.</i> <br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>     Obey!  obey!<br>        Heed my will!<br>     Your souls are mine,<br>        Good or ill!<br>     On ye all<br>        I lay the spell:<br>     Heaven's thrall,<br>        Strength of Hell!<br>     Come, then, minions,<br>        I forbid ye:<br>     Warn not my lord<br>        Of the fate that awaits him!<br>     Lo!  he comes.<br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  All heedless now the master comes,<br>     By his great love made wholly blind,<br>  Save to the festive garlands hung,<br>     And the fair music of the wind,<br>  And to the glance of Vivian,<br>     Who smiles upon him fair and kind.<br>  <br>  <i> Merlin, though able to read the future for others,<br>     Was blinded to his own doom.</i> <br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>  Wherefore these garlands, Lady mine!<br>     Wherefore this music in the air?<br>  These fragrant jewelled robes of thine,<br>     And thy sage eyes grown debonnaire?<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>        Master, it is for thee;<br>        A year ago this day<br>     From Arthur's court we came away.<br>  <br>  MERLIN AND VIVIAN<br>     A year of love, a year of joy,<br>     A year of gold without alloy!<br>     Wisdom and youth together blent,<br>     With every sense in full content.<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>  This feast for thee, my Lord, my Love!<br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>     I drink to thee, O Lady mine!<br>     Bring me the rarest perfumed wine<br>     Wherein to pledge my love and thine.<br>  <br>  VIVIAN <i> (tendering the cup of death)</i> <br>  Take thou this cup:  with mine own hand<br>  I poured the wine; drink deep, my Lord!<br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS <i> (seeking to warn Merlin)</i> <br>     Woe!  woe!  Master, beware!<br>     Have mercy, Vivian!<br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>  Now what befalls?-My senses reel-<br>  Help!  Vivian!  I feel<br>  My being ebb-thou art not nigh-<br>  Kiss me, my Lady, before I die!<br>  <br>  <center><i> Vivian spins the magic shroud.</i> </center> <br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS<br>     Woe!  woe!  woe!  woe!<br>  She bends above him, laughs aloud,<br>     Unbinds her hair of rusted gold;<br>  See, from her quick and cunning hands<br>     A shroud of golden hair unfold<br>  Like a huge spiderweb of Hate,<br>     That wraps him in its meshes cold.<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>     From my hair a shroud I spin,<br>     Wrought of magic and of sin;<br>     Merlin, all I learned of thee,<br>     Yet know naught to set thee free!<br>     Here in stupor shalt thou lie,<br>     Till a thousand years go by,<br>     But one thought for company:<br>     Thou liest here for love of me,<br>     Love of Lady Vivian.<br>  <br>  <center><i> Merlin is sealed within the tomb.</i> </center> <br>  <br>  CHORUS<br>  She signs, and waves her peacock fan:<br>     Behold, a yawning cavern tomb!<br>  And now the Spirits at her word<br>     Bear Merlin's corpse across the room,<br>  'Mid lighted tapers, row on row,<br>     And voices wailing through the gloom.<br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>     Vivian!  Vivian!  Vivian!<br>  <br>  VIVIAN<br>  Farewell, then!  Lie thou there in state,<br>  Canopied by mine own Hate.<br>  <br>  CHORUS OF SPIRITS<br>     Curses on thee, Vivian!<br>     Every Spirit gives thee hate;<br>     Thou hast no need of book or bell<br>     To condemn thy soul to hell!<br>  <br>  MERLIN<br>     I forgive thee, Vivian!<br>  <br>
