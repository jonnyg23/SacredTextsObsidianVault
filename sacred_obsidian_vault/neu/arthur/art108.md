 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">A BALLAD OF CORNWALL</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">F. B. MONEY-COUTTS</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> 	I<br> Sir Tristram lay by a well,<br>        Making sad moan;<br> Fast his tears tell,<br> For wild the wood through,<br>        Stricken with shrewd<br>        Sorrow, he ran,<br> When he deemed her untrue --<br>        La Beale Isoud!<br> For he loved her alone.<br> <br> 	II<br> So as he lay,<br>        Wasted and wan,<br>        Scarce like a man,<br> Pricking that way<br>        His lady-love came,<br>           With her damsels around,<br>        And her face all a-flame<br> With the breezes of May;<br> While a brachet beside her<br> Still bayed the fair rider,<br>        Still leaped up and bayed her;<br>           A small scenting hound<br>        That Sir Tristram purveyed her.<br> <br> 	III<br> So she rode on;<br>       But the brachet behind<br>       Hung snuffing the wind,<br>          Till seeking and crying<br>             Faster and faster,<br>          Beside the well lying<br>             She found her dear master!<br>         Then licking his ears<br>         And cheeks wet with tears, <br>             For joy never resting<br>             Kept whining and questing.<br> <br> 	IV<br> Isoud (returned<br>         Seeking her hound)<br> Soon as she learned<br>         Tristram was found,<br> Straightway alighting, <br>         Fell in a swound.<br> <br> 	V<br> Won by her lover<br> Thence to recover,<br> Who shall the greeting<br> Tell of their meeting?<br> Joy, by no tongue<br> E'er to be sung<br> Passed in that plighting!<br> <br> 	VI<br>         Thus while they dallied,<br>         Forth the wood sallied<br> An horrible libbard, and bare<br> The brachet away to his lair!<br>
