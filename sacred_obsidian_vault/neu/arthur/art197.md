 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">BALLAD OF SIR LAUNCELOT</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">JOHN GROSVENOR WILSON</h2>  </center>  <br>  "Riding the quest of the Grail alone, <br>      Guinevere, Guinevere, pity me! <br>  All thro' the day and the night I moan, <br>      Yearning to catch but a glimpse of thee. <br>  Tho' I make halt by the wan west sea, <br>      Seeking a sign in the high God's name, <br>  Lo, as I tremble and bow the knee, <br>      Gleameth thy face with the eyes aflame! <br>  <br>  "Me have I scourged till the blood hath flown, <br>      Weeping hot tears of misery; <br>  Still thro' the day and the night I moan, <br>      Yearning to catch but a glimpse of thee. --  <br>  Yea, have I striven and sought to flee, <br>      Ever and ever the same -- the same -- <br>  Blinding my soul with sweet rarity, <br>      Gleameth thy face with the eyes aflame! <br>  <br>  "Sleeping, I dreamed that the Grail was shown, <br>      Marvellous bright with clemency; <br>  Waking, I lay on the chancel-stone -- <br>      Ah, but I yearned for the touch of thee! -- <br>  Tho' I may bow and make piteous plea <br>      Unto the Christ and our Holy Dame, <br>  Sweeter by far than all visions be, <br>      Gleameth thy face with the eyes aflame." <br>  <br>  ENVOY.  <br>  <br>  Guinevere maketh a day of glee, -- <br>      "Who is it cometh to join our game?" --  <br>  "Launcelot rideth with bridle free, <br>      Gleameth thy face with the eyes aflame." <br>
