 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">KING RYENCE'S CHALLENGE<br>  from <i> RELIQUES OF ANCIENT ENGLISH POETRY</i> </h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">BISHOP THOMAS PERCY</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>  As it fell out on a Pentecost day,<br>       King Arthur at Camelot kept his court royall,<br>  With his faire queene dame Guenever the gay,<br>       And many bold barons sitting in hall,<br>  With ladies attired in purple and pall,<br>       And heraults in hewkes, hooting on high,<br>  Cryed, <i> Largesse</i> , <i> Largesse</i> , <i> Chevaliers</i>  <i> tres-hardie</i> .<br>  <br>  A doughty dwarfe to the uppermost deas<br>       Right pertlye gan pricke, kneeling on knee;<br>  With steven fulle stoute amids all the preas,<br>       Say'd, "Nowe Sir King Arthur, God save thee and see!<br>  Sir Ryence of North-Gales greeteth well thee,<br>       And bids thee thy beard anon to him send,<br>  Or else from thy jaws hewill it off rend.<br>  <br>  "For his robe of state is a rich scarlet mantle,<br>       With eleven kings beards bordered about,<br>  And there is room lefte yet in a kantle,<br>  For thine to stande, to make the twelfth out.<br>       This must be done, be thou never so stout;<br>  This must be done, I tell thee no fable,<br>  Maugre the teethe of all thy Round Table."<br>  <br>  When this mortal message from his mouthe past,<br>       Great was the noyse bothe in hall and in bower:<br>  The king fum'd; the queene screecht; ladies were aghast;<br>       Princes puff'd; barons blustred; lords began lower;<br>       Knights stormed; squires startled, like steeds in a stower;<br>  Pages and yeomen yell'd out in the hall;<br>  Then in came Sir Kay, the king's seneschal.<br>  <br>  "Silence, my soveraignes," quoth this courteous knight,<br>       And in that stound the stowre began still:<br>  Then the dwarfe's dinner full deerely was dight;<br>       Of wine and wassel he had his wille,<br>       And when he had eaten and drunken his fill,<br>  An hundred pieces of fine coynd gold<br>  Were given this dwarf for his message bold.<br>  <br>  "But say to Sir Ryence, thou dwarf," quoth the king,<br>       "That for his bold message I do him defye,<br>  And shortlyewith basins and pans will him ring<br>       Out of North-Gales; where he and I<br>       With swords, and not razors, quickly shall trye,<br>  Whether he, or King Arthur, will prove the best barbor:"<br>  And therewith he shook his good sword Escalàbor.<br>
