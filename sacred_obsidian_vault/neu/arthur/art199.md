 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER"> ISOLT AT THE TOMB OF TRISTRAM </h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">JOHN GROSVENOR WILSON</h2>  </center>  <br>            <br>  "Here, with my arms curled round the sacred cross  <br>           That in white warning stands above his bones,  <br>           I crouch, with hot limbs pressed against the stones,  <br>  And moan his name, and wail and weep his loss.  <br>            <br>  "Stand back, good sirs -- ye shall not drag me hence;  <br>           But, pray ye, keep between me and King Mark;  <br>          My husband? yes -- God pity me -- but hark, <br>  Surely ye know not his most foul offence. <br>          <br>  "Yet know ye how he came, that rainy day, <br>          When Tristram played the harp to me, to keep <br>          The hours in joyance. -- Ah, sirs, could ye sleep, <br>  And in a dream but hear Sir Tristram play -- <br>          <br>  "How like the tinkle of a silver bell<br>          The sweet notes from his cunning fingers tripped, <br>          In airy melody that softly slipped<br>  Into the heart, with sunny stir and swell. -- <br>          <br>  "In very heaven of joy I heard him play, <br>          And saw his eyes with love grow master-ful; <br>          When, at a sudden, stealthy, noiseless pull, <br>  The curtains parted and for Mark made way. <br>          <br>  "I felt my lips were frozen in a smile, <br>          And with that fixèd smile I watched King Mark <br>          Drive his sharp glaive thro' Tristram's throat, still stark <br>  My Tristram lay -- and I said naught the while, <br>          <br>  "Until that man came to me -- half in fear, <br>          I wot -- whereat I shrieked, and in a swoon <br>          Fell down, and knew no more until the moon <br>  Rose yesternight, and then I hurried here. <br>          <br>  "Now shall Mark come to judge me? -- he? -- who knew <br>          All of my Tristram's love from first to last, <br>          And knowing how that love would bind him fast <br>  Made use of him to keep his kingdom true. <br>          <br>  "Has any man who profits by a sin, <br>          In ways direct or indirect, a right <br>          To sit as judge of what is black or white, <br>  Or join his voice to the accusing din? <br>          <br>  "O, I can say no more! -- do what ye will! <br>          My heart grows sick above my Tristram's tomb -- <br>          O loyal love, how fares it in the gloom? <br>  Dost hear my voice? hath it the old-time thrill? <br>          <br>  "Rest, rest, my love -- I grow so weak and faint, <br>          Thou shalt not wait me long. O blessed hour! <br>          When from the gate of heaven's portal-tower<br>  Thou wilt rush forth to still my cold complaint." <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>
