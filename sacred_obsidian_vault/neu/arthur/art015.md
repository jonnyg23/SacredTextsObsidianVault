 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">BALLADE OF TRISTRAM'S LAST HARPING</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">GERTRUDE BARTLETT</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>  <br>  The end that Love doth seek, what bard can say,<br>     In that fair season when the tender green<br>  Of opening leaves doth roof the woods of May,<br>     And sweet wild buds from out their places lean<br>  To touch the dainty feet that heedless stray<br>     Among them, with a youth in knight's attire?<br>  His lady's will capricious to obey,<br>     This is the end of dawning Love's desire.<br>  <br>  And when amid the summer's bright array<br>     Of blossoms, are the crimson roses seen,<br>  And one young maid, fairer than any spray<br>     In perfect bloom, wanders their lines between,<br>  What blessëd solace can the lover pray<br>     Of her compassion, for his heart of fire?<br>  With kisses on her mouth all words to stay--<br>     This is the end of eager Love's desire.<br>  <br>  With driven clouds the hovering sky is grey;<br>     The winds above the frozen hills are keen,<br>  And all fair buds have fallen in decay;<br>     What joy hath now the true knight of his Queen?<br>  No rapture less exultant can allay<br>     His need, than softly craves this faulty lyre:<br>  To answer all his pleading with sweet 'Yea'--<br>     This is the end of yearning Love's desire.<br>  <br>              Envoy<br>  <br>  Beloved, now is done our life's brief day;<br>     Not with the day howe'er doth Love expire.<br>  Within thine arms the night to dream away--<br>     This is the end of Love's supreme desire.<br>
