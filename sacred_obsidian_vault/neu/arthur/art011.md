 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">LA MORT D'ARTHUR</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">NOT BY ALFRED TENNYSON</h3>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">WILLIAM EDMONDSTOUNE AYTOUN</h2>  </center>  <br>  Slowly, as one who bears a mortal hurt,<br>  Through which the fountain of his life runs dry,<br>  Crept good King Arthur down unto the lake.<br>  A roughening wind was bringing in the waves<br>  With cold dull plash and plunging to the shore,<br>  And a great bank of clouds  came sailing up<br>  Athwart the aspect of the gibbous moon,<br>  Leaving no glimpse save starlight, as he sank,<br>  With a short stagger, senseless on the stones.<br>  <br>     No man yet knows how long he lay in swound;<br>  But long enough it was to let the rust<br>  Lick half the surface of his polished shield;<br>  For it was made by far inferior hands,<br>  Than forged his helm, his breastplate, and his greaves,<br>  Whereon no canker lighted, for they bore<br>  The magic stamp of MECHI'S  SILVER  STEEL .
