 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">TRISTRAM TO ISOLT</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">MADISON CAWEIN</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> Yea, there are some who always seek<br> The love that lasts an hour;<br> And some who in love's language speak,<br> Yet never know his power.<br> <br> Of such was I, who knew not what<br> Sweet mysteries can rise<br> Within the heart when 't is its lot<br> To love and realize.<br> <br> Of such was I, Isolt!  till, lo,<br> Your face on mine did gleam,<br> And changed that world, I used to know,<br> Into an evil dream.<br> <br> That world wherein, on hill and plain,<br> Great blood-red poppies bloomed;<br> Their hot hearts thirsty for the rain,<br> And sleepily perfumed.<br> <br> Above, below, on every part,<br> A crimson shadow lay;<br> As if the red sun streamed athwart,<br> And sunset was alway.<br> <br> I know not how;  I know not when;<br> I only know that there<br> She met me in the haunted glen,<br> A poppy in her hair.<br> <br> Her face seemed fair as Mary's is,<br> That knows nor sin nor wrong;<br> Her presence filled the silences<br> As music fills a song.<br> <br> And she was clad like the Mother of God,<br> As 't were for Christ's sweet sake;<br> But when she moved and where she trod<br> A hiss went of a snake.<br> <br> Though seeming sinless, till I die<br> I shall not know for sure<br> Why to my soul she seemed a lie<br> And otherwise than pure.<br> <br> Nor why I kissed her soon and late,<br> And for her felt desire,<br> While loathing of her passion ate<br> Into my heart like fire.<br> <br> Was it because my soul could tell<br> That, like the poppy flower,<br> She had no soul?  a thing of Hell,<br> That o'er mine had no power.<br> <br> Or was it that your love at last,<br> My soul so long had craved,<br> From that sweet sin which held me fast<br> At that last moment, saved?<br>
