 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center><h2> MERLIN AND THE GLEAM</h2>  <h3 align="center"> by</h3>  <h2> ALFRED, LORD TENNYSON</h2>  </center>  <br>                    I.<br> <br>     O YOUNG Mariner,<br>     You from the haven<br>     Under the sea-cliff,<br>     You that are watching<br>     The gray Magician<br>     With eyes of wonder,<br>     <i> I</i>  am Merlin,<br>     And <i> I</i>  am dying,<br>     <i> I</i>  am Merlin<br>     Who follow The Gleam.<br>  <br>  <br>                    II.<br>  <br>     Mighty the Wizard<br>     Who found me at sunrise<br>     Sleeping, and woke me<br>     And learn'd me Magic!<br>     Great the Master,<br>     And sweet the Magic,<br>     When over the valley,<br>     In early summers,<br>     Over the mountain,<br>     On human faces,<br>     And all around me,<br>     Moving to melody,<br>     Floated The Gleam.<br>  <br>  <br>                    III.<br>  <br>     Once at the croak of a Raven who crost it,<br>     A barbarous people,<br>     Blind to the magic,<br>     And deaf to the melody,<br>     Snarl'd at and cursed me.<br>     A demon vext me,<br>     The light retreated,<br>     The landskip darken'd,<br>     The melody deaden'd,<br>     The Master whisper'd<br>     "Follow The Gleam."<br>  <br>  <br>                    IV.<br>  <br>     Then to the melody,<br>     Over a wilderness<br>     Gliding, and glancing at<br>     Elf of the woodland,<br>     Gnome of the cavern,<br>     Griffin and Giant,<br>     And dancing of Fairies<br>     In desolate hollows,<br>     And wraiths of the mountain,<br>     And rolling of dragons<br>     By warble of water,<br>     Or cataract music<br>     Of falling torrents,<br>     Flitted The Gleam.<br>  <br>  <br>                    V.<br>  <br>     Down from the mountain<br>     And over the level,<br>     And streaming and shining on<br>     Silent river,<br>     Silvery willow,<br>     Pasture and plowland,<br>     Horses and oxen,<br>     Innocent maidens,<br>     Garrulous children,<br>     Homestead and harvest,<br>     Reaper and gleaner,<br>     And rough-ruddy faces<br>     Of lowly labour,<br>     Slided The Gleam.--<br>  <br>  <br>                    VI.<br>  <br>     Then, with a melody<br>     Stronger and statelier,<br>     Led me at length<br>     To the city and palace<br>     Of Arthur the king;<br>     Touch'd at the golden<br>     Cross of the churches,<br>     Flash'd on the Tournament,<br>     Flicker'd and bicker'd<br>     From helmet to helmet,<br>     And last on the forehead<br>     Of Arthur the blameless<br>     Rested The Gleam.<br>  <br>  <br>                    VII.<br>  <br>     Clouds and darkness<br>     Closed upon Camelot;<br>     Arthur had vanish'd<br>     I knew not whither,<br>     The king who loved me,<br>     And cannot die;<br>     For out of the darkness<br>     Silent and slowly<br>     The Gleam, that had waned to a wintry glimmer<br>     On icy fallow<br>     And faded forest,<br>     Drew to the valley<br>     Named of the shadow,<br>     And slowly brightening<br>     Out of the glimmer,<br>     And slowly moving again to a melody<br>     Yearningly tender,<br>     Fell on the shadow,<br>     No longer a shadow,<br>     But clothed with The Gleam.<br>  <br>  <br>                    VIII.<br>  <br>     And broader and brighter<br>     The Gleam flying onward,<br>     Wed to the melody,<br>     Sang thro' the world;<br>     And slower and fainter,<br>     Old and weary,<br>     But eager to follow,<br>     I saw, whenever<br>     In passing it glanced upon<br>     Hamlet or city,<br>     That under the Crosses<br>     The dead man's garden,<br>     The mortal hillock,<br>     Would break into blossom;<br>     And so to the land's<br>     Last limit I came--<br>     And can no longer,<br>     But die rejoicing,<br>     For thro' the Magic<br>     Of Him the Mighty,<br>     Who taught me in childhood,<br>     There on the border<br>     Of boundless Ocean,<br>     And all but in Heaven<br>     Hovers The Gleam.<br>  <br>  <br>                    IX.<br>  <br>     Not of the sunlight,<br>     Not of the moonlight,<br>     Not of the starlight!<br>     O young Mariner,<br>     Down to the haven,<br>     Call your companions,<br>     Launch your vessel,<br>     And crowd your canvas,<br>     And, ere it vanishes<br>     Over the margin,<br>     After it, follow it,<br>     Follow The Gleam.<br>
