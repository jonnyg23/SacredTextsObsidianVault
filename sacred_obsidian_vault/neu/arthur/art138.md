 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">ELAINE AND ELAINE</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ELIZABETH STUART PHELPS</h2>  </center>  <br> <br> 	   I.<br> Dead, she drifted to his feet.<br> Tell us, Love, is Death so sweet?<br> <br> Oh!  the river floweth deep.<br> Fathoms deeper is her sleep.<br> <br> Oh!  the current driveth strong.<br> Wilder tides drive souls along.<br> <br> Drifting, though he loved her not,<br> To the heart of Launcelot,<br> <br> Let her pass; it is her place.<br> Death hath given her this grace.<br> <br> Let her pass; she resteth well.<br> What her dreams are, who can tell?<br> <br> Mute the steersman; why, if he<br> Speaketh not a word, should we?<br> <br> 	   II.<br> Dead, she drifteth to his feet.<br> Close, her eyes keep secrets sweet.<br> <br> Living, he had loved her well.<br> High as Heaven and deep as Hell.<br> <br> Yet that voyage she stayeth not.<br> Wait you for her, Launcelot?<br> <br> Oh!  the river floweth fast.<br> Who is justified at last?<br> <br> Locked her lips are.  Hush!  If she<br> Sayeth nothing, how should we?
