 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Gypsy Folk Tales</i>, by Francis Hindes Groome, [1899], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">Gypsy variants.</h3>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> We have five Gypsy versions of 'The Master Thief' (Nos. <a href="gft071.htm#an_11">11</a>, <a href="gft072.htm#an_12">12</a>), one from Roumania, two from Hungary, and two from Wales; and two of the cognate story, 'Tropsyn' (Nos. <a href="gft087.htm#an_27">27</a>, <a href="gft088.htm#an_28">28</a>), from the Bukowina and Wales. We have two of 'The Vampire' (<a href="gft065.htm#an_05">No. 5</a>), Roumanian and Hungarian; three of 'The Bad Mother' (Nos. <a href="gft068.htm#an_08">8</a>, <a href="gft069.htm#an_09">9</a>), Roumanian, Bukowinian, and Hungarian; two of 'Mare's Son' (Nos. 20, 58), Bukowinian and Welsh; three of 'It all comes to Light' (Nos. <a href="gft077.htm#an_17">17</a>, <a href="gft078.htm#an_18">18</a>, <a href="gft079.htm#an_19">19</a>), Bukowinian, Roumanian, and Slovak; two of 'The Rich and the Poor Brother' (Nos. <a href="gft090.htm#an_30">30</a>, <a href="gft091.htm#an_31">31</a>), Bukowinian and Hungarian; three of 'The Robber Bridegroom' (<a href="gft107.htm#an_47">No. 47</a>), Polish, Hungarian, and Welsh; three of 'The Master Smith' (Nos. <a href="gft119.htm#an_59">59</a>, <a href="gft120.htm#an_60">60</a>), Welsh, Catalonian, and Slovak; two of 'The Golden Bush and the Good Hare' (Nos. <a href="gft109.htm#an_49">49</a>, <a href="gft135.htm#an_75">75</a>), Polish and Scotch; and four of 'The Deluded Dragon' (Nos. <a href="gft081.htm#an_21">21</a>, <a href="gft082.htm#an_22">22</a>), Bukowinian, Slovak, Transylvanian, and Turkish. It is something to have established this much; and it will be seen how</p>  
 <p><a name="page_lxv"><font size="1" color="green">p. lxv</font></a></p>  
 <p>enormously Mr. Sampson has extended the area of Gypsy folk-tales since 1896. But it still needs much greater extension.</p>
