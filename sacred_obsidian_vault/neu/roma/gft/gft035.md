 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Gypsy Folk Tales</i>, by Francis Hindes Groome, [1899], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">Dr. R. von Sowa.</h3>  
 <p><i>Die Mundart der Slovakischen Zigeuner</i> (Göttingen, 1887), by Dr. Rudolf von Sowa, of Brünn, is based on nineteen Slovak-Gypsy stories which he collected at Teplicz in 1884-85, and nine of which are given in the original Rómani without a translation. Dr. von Sowa also contributed four Gypsy folk-tales--Slovak and Moravian--to the <i>Gypsy Lore Journal</i>; and the Bohemian-Gypsy story of 'The Three Dragons' he sent me in manuscript. His stories have a high value for the purposes of comparison, but are inferior as stories to those of several other collections. I have given eight of them--Nos. <a href="gft072.htm#an_12">12</a>, <a href="gft079.htm#an_19">19</a>, <a href="gft082.htm#an_22">22</a>, <a href="gft101.htm#an_41">41</a>, <a href="gft102.htm#an_42">42</a>, <a href="gft103.htm#an_43">43</a>, <a href="gft104.htm#an_44">44</a>, <a href="gft120.htm#an_60">60</a>.</p>
