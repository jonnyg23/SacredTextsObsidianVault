 <h1>The Paris Psalter: Psalm 99</h1>
 <p><cite>Verse Indeterminate Saxon</cite></p>
 <dl compact>
 <dt></dt>
 <dd>
 Nu ge mycle gefean      mihtigum drihtne <br>
 eall þeos eorðe      elne hyre, <br>
 and blisse gode      bealde þeowie. <br>
 Gangað on ansyne      ealle bliðe; <br>
 </dd>
 <dt>5</dt>
 <dd>
 witað wislice      þæt he is wealdend god; <br>
 he us geworhte      and we his syndon. <br>
 We his folc syndan      and his fæle sceap, <br>
 ða he on his edisce      ealle afedde; <br>
 gað nu on his doru,      god <strong>andettað,</strong> <br>
 </dd>
 <dt>10</dt>
 <dd>
 and hine weorðiað      on wictunum <br>
 mid lofsangum      lustum myclum. <br>
 Heriað naman drihtnes,      forþon he is niðum swæs; <br>
 is þin milde mod      ofer manna bearn. <br>
 </dd>
