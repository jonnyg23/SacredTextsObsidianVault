 <h4 align="CENTER">THE STORY OF</h4>
 <h1 align="CENTER">GRETTIR THE STRONG.</h1>
 <i><font size="2"><p>This First Part tells of the forefathers of Grettir in Norway, and how they fled away before Harald Fairhair, and settled in Iceland; and of their deeds in Iceland before Grettir was born</p></font></i>.
 <h2 align="CENTER">CHAP. I.</h2>
 <p>THERE was a man named Onund, who was the son of Ufeigh Clubfoot, the son of Ivar the Smiter; Onund was brother of Gudbiorg, the mother of Gudbrand Ball, the father of Asta, the mother of King Olaf the Saint. Onund was an Uplander by the kin of his mother; but the kin of his father dwelt chiefly about Rogaland and Hordaland. He was a great viking, and went harrying west over the Sea. <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="#fn_3"><font size="1">*</font></a> Balk of Sotanes, the son of Blæng, was with him herein, and Orm the Wealthy withal, and Hallvard was the name of the third of them. They had five ships, all well manned, and therewith they harried in the South-isles; <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">+</font></a> and when they came to Barra, they found there a king, called Kiarval, and he, too, had five ships. They gave him battle, and a hard fray there was. The men of Onund were of the eagerest, and on either side many fell; but the</p>
 <p><a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 2</font></a></p>
 <p>end of it was that the king fled with only one ship. So there the men of Onund took both ships and much wealth, and abode there through the winter. For three summers they harried throughout Ireland and Scotland, and thereafter went to Norway.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_4"></a><a href="gre06.htm#fr_4">1:+</a> South-isles are the Hebrides, and the other islands down to Man.</p>
