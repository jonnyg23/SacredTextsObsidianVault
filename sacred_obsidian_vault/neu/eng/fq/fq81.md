 <center> <i>The
 VIII. Canto, vnperfite. </i></center><p>
 
 </p><p>
 </p><blockquote>
 W
 Hen I bethinke me on that speech whyleare,<br>
 Of <i>Mutability</i>, and well it way:<br>
 Me seemes, that though she all vnworthy were<br>
 Of the Heav'ns Rule ; yet very sooth to say,<br>
 In all things else she beares the greatest sway.<br>
 Which makes me loath this state of life so tickle,<br>
 And loue of things so vaine to cast away;<br>
 Whose flowring pride, so fading and so fickle,<br>
 Short <i>Time</i> shall soon cut down with his consuming sickle.<p>
 
 Then gin I thinke on that which Nature sayd,<br>
 Of that same time when no more <i>Change</i> shall be,<br>
 But stedfast rest of all things firmely stayd<br>
 Vpon the pillours of Eternity,<br>
 That is contrayr to <i>Mutabilitie:</i><br>
 For, all that moueth, doth in <i>Change</i> delight:<br>
 But thence-forth all shall rest eternally<br>
 With Him that is the God of Sabbaoth hight:<br>
 O thou great Sabbaoth God, graunt me that Sabaoths sight.</p><p>
 </p></blockquote>
 <center><i>FINIS</i></center><p>
