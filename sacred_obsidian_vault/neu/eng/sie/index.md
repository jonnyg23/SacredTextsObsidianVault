 <p>
 This is a fascimile reproduction, along with transcribed text, of
 William Blake's masterpiece of bookmaking,
 <cite>Songs of Innocence and of Experience</cite>.
 These poems are one of the treasures of world literature,
 simple enough to resonate with children, but with enough
 exoteric and esoteric meaning to keep a gnostic sage pondering for several
 lifetimes.
 Blake's vision of a universe alive on all scales of being
 is luminously represented in the hand-colored illustrations,
 which contain clues to the overtones of the text.
 The poems are also firmly rooted in the misery of 18th century
 London, and many of them are embued with a politically radical (but still
 bardic) outlook on the squalid everyday life which surrounded Blake.
 This is a text which needs to be experienced with both sides of the brain.
 <small>--John Bruno Hare, March 11, 2009.</small>
