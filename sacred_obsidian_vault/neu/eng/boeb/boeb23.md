 <h1 align="CENTER">The Mermaid</h1>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>To yon fause stream that, near the sea,<br>
   Hides mony an elf and plum,<br>
 And rives wi' fearful din the stanes,<br>
   A witless knicht did come.</p>
 <p>The day shines clear--far in he's gane<br>
   Whar shells are silver bright,<br>
 Fishes war loupin' a' aroun',<br>
   And sparklin' to the light.</p>
 <p>Whan, as he laved, sounds cam sae sweet<br>
   Frae ilka rock an' tree;<br>
 The brief was out, 'twas him it doomed<br>
   The mermaid's face to see.</p>
 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 152</font></a></p>
 <p>Frae 'neath a rock, sune, sune she rose,<br>
   And stately on she swam,<br>
 Stopped i' the midst, and becked and sang<br>
   To him to stretch his han'.</p>
 <p>Gowden glist the yellow links<br>
   That round her neck she'd twine;<br>
 Her een war o' the skyie blue,<br>
   Her lips did mock the wine;</p>
 <p>The smile upon her bonnie cheek<br>
   Was sweeter than the bee;<br>
 Her voice excelled the birdie's sang<br>
   Upon the birchen tree.</p>
 <p>Sae couthie, couthie did she look,<br>
   And meikle had she fleeched;<br>
 Out shot his hand--alas! alas!<br>
   Fast in the swirl he screeched.</p>
 <p>The mermaid leuch, her brief was gane,<br>
   And kelpie's blast was blawin',<br>
 Fu' low she duked, ne'er raise again,<br>
   For deep, deep was the fawin'.</p>
 <p><a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 153</font></a></p>
 <p>Aboon the stream his wraith was seen,<br>
   Warlochs tirled lang at gloamin';<br>
 That e'en was coarse, the blast blew hoarse,<br>
   Ere lang the waves war foamin'.</p>
