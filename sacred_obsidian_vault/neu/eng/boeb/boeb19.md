 <h1 align="CENTER">The Twa Corbies</h1>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>As I was walking all alane,<br>
 I heard twa corbies making a maen:<br>
 The tane unto the t'ither did say,<br>
 "Whaur shall we gang and dine the day?"</p>
 <p>"O doun beside yon auld fail dyke,<br>
 I wot there lies a new-slain knight;<br>
 And naebody kens that he lies there<br>
 But his hawk, his hound, and his lady fair.</p>
 <p>"His hound is to the hunting gane,<br>
 His hawk to fetch the wild-fowl hame,<br>
 His lady's ta'en another mate,<br>
 Sae we may mak' our dinner sweet.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_12401"></a>
 <a href="img/12401.jpg"><img src="tn/12401.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></p>
 <p> </p>
 <p></p>
 <p>O we'll sit on his white hause bane,<br>
 And I'll pyke out his bonny blue e'en;<br>
 Wi' ae lock o' his gowden hair<br>
 We'll theek our nest when it blaws bare.</p>
 <p>"Mony a ane for him makes maen,<br>
 But nane shall ken whaur he is gane.<br>
 Over his banes when they are bare,<br>
 The wind shall blaw for evermair."</p>
