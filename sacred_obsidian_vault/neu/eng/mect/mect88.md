I soughte in<br> <a name="an_9"></a> the bakhalf of myn Astrelabie and fond the<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> cercle of the daies, the whiche I knowe by<br> <a name="an_11"></a> the names of the monthes writen under the<br> <a name="an_12"></a> same cercle. And thus knowe I this<br> <a name="an_16"></a> conclusioun.<br> <a name="an_17"></a> Anothir day I wolde knowen the degre of<br> <a name="an_18"></a> my sonne, and this was at midday in the 13<br> <a name="an_19"></a> day of Decembre. And than had I of this conclusioun the<br> <a name="an_25"></a> ful experience.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_Rub">Rub</a></font></span> To knowe the altitude of the sonne or of<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_RubA">RubA</a></font></span> othre celestial bodies.<br> <a name="an_1"></a> Put the ryng of thyn Astrelabie upon thy<br> <a name="an_2"></a> right thombe, and turne thi lift syde ageyn<br> <a name="an_3"></a> the light of the sonne; and remewe thy rewle<br> <a name="an_4"></a> up and doun til that the stremes of the sonne<br> <a name="an_5"></a> shine thorugh bothe holes of thi rewle. Tak this manere of settyng for a<br> <a name="an_8"></a> general rule, ones for evere. I tok the altitude of my sonne,<br> <a name="an_18"></a> and fond that it was 25 degrees and 30 minutes<br> <a name="an_19"></a> of height in the bordure on the bak<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_20">20</a></font></span> side. Tho loked I doun upon myn<br> <a name="an_33"></a> est orizonte, and fond there the 20 degre of<br> <a name="an_34"></a> Geminis ascendyng, which that I tok for myn<br> <a name="an_35"></a> ascendent. Tho sette I the centre<br> <a name="an_47"></a> of this Alhabor upon 12 degrees among myn<br> <a name="an_48"></a> almykanteras upon the west side, by cause that<br> <a name="an_49"></a> she was founde on the west side. Tho<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_50">50</a></font></span> leyde I my label over the degre of the<br> <a name="an_51"></a> sonne, that was discendid under the west<br> <a name="an_52"></a> orisounte, and rekned all the lettres capitals<br> <a name="an_53"></a> fro the lyne of midday unto the point of my<br> <a name="an_54"></a> label in the bordure, and fond that it was<br> <a name="an_55"></a> passed 9 of the clokke the space of 10 degrees.<br> <a name="an_56"></a> Tho lokid I doun upon myn est orisounte, and<br> <a name="an_57"></a> fond there 10 degrees of Scorpius ascendyng,<br> <a name="an_58"></a> whom I tok for myn ascendent. And thus<br> <a name="an_59"></a> lerned I to knowe onys for ever in which<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_60">60</a></font></span> manere I shuld come to the houre of the<br> <a name="an_61"></a> nyght, and to myn ascendent, as verrely as<br> <a name="an_62"></a> may be taken by so smal an instrument.<br> <a name="an_63"></a> But natheles this rule in generall wol I warne<br> <a name="an_64"></a> the for evere: Ne make the nevere bold to<br> <a name="an_65"></a> have take a just ascendent by thin Astrelabie,<br> <a name="an_66"></a> or elles to have set justly a clokke, whan eny<br> <a name="an_67"></a> celestial body by which that thou wenyst governe<br> <a name="an_68"></a> thilke thinges be nigh the south lyne.<br> <a name="an_69"></a> For trust wel, whan the sonne is nygh the<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_70">70</a></font></span> meridional lyne, the degre of the sonne<br> <a name="an_71"></a> renneth so longe consentrik upon the almykanteras<br> <a name="an_72"></a> that sothly thou shalt erre fro the<br> <a name="an_73"></a> just ascendent. The same conclusioun sey I by<br> <a name="an_74"></a> the centre of eny sterre fix by nyght. And<br> <a name="an_75"></a> more over, by experience I wot wel that in<br> <a name="an_76"></a> oure orisounte, from xi of the clokke unto oon<br> <a name="an_77"></a> of the clokke, in taking of a just ascendent in<br> <a name="an_78"></a> a portatif Astrelabie it is to hard to knowe --<br> <a name="an_79"></a> I mene from xi of the clokke before the<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_80">80</a></font></span> houre of noon til oon of the clokke next<br> <a name="an_81"></a> folewyng.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_Rub">Rub</a></font></span> A special declaracioun of the ascendent.<br> <a name="an_1"></a> The ascendent sothly, as wel in alle nativites<br> <a name="an_2"></a> as in questions and eleccions of tymes, is a<br> <a name="an_3"></a> thing which that these astrologiens gretly observen.<br> <a name="an_4"></a> Wherfore me semeth convenyent, syth<br> <a name="an_5"></a> that I speke of the ascendent, to make of it<br> <a name="an_6"></a> speciall declaracioun.<br> <a name="an_7"></a> The ascendent sothly, to take it at the largest,<br> <a name="an_8"></a> is thilke degre that ascendith at eny of<br> <a name="an_9"></a> these forseide tymes upon the est orisounte.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> And therfore, yf that eny planete ascende<br> <a name="an_11"></a> at thatt same tyme in thilke forseide degre,<br> <a name="an_12"></a> than hath he no latitude fro the ecliptik lyne,<br> <a name="an_13"></a> but he is than in the degre of the ecliptik<br> <a name="an_14"></a> which that is the degre of his longitude. Men<br> <a name="an_15"></a> sayn that thilke planete is in horoscopo.<br> <a name="an_16"></a> But sothly the hous of the ascendent, that<br> <a name="an_17"></a> is to seyn, the first hous or the est angle, is a<br> <a name="an_18"></a> thing more brod and large. But sothly, if he<br> <a name="an_28"></a> passe the boundes of these forseide spaces,<br> <a name="an_29"></a> above or bynethe, thei seyn that the planete<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_30">30</a></font></span> is "fallyng fro the ascendent." The lord of the ascendent, sey thei<br> <a name="an_45"></a> that he is fortunat whan he is in god place<br> <a name="an_46"></a> fro the ascendent, as in an angle, or in a succident<br> <a name="an_47"></a> where as he is in hys dignite and comfortid<br> <a name="an_48"></a> with frendly aspectes of planetes and<br> <a name="an_49"></a> wel resceyved; and eke that he may seen<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_50">50</a></font></span> the ascendent; and that he be not retrograd,<br> <a name="an_51"></a> ne combust, ne joyned with no<br> <a name="an_52"></a> shrewe in the same signe; ne that he be not<br> <a name="an_53"></a> in his discencioun, ne joyned with no planete<br> <a name="an_54"></a> in his descencioun, ne have upon him noon<br> <a name="an_55"></a> aspect infortunat; and than sey thei that he is<br> <a name="an_56"></a> well.<br> <a name="an_57"></a> Natheles these ben observaunces of judicial<br> <a name="an_58"></a> matere and rytes of payens, in whiche my<br> <a name="an_59"></a> spirit hath no feith, ne knowing of her<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_60">60</a></font></span> horoscopum. Sett doun agayn the<br> <a name="an_19"></a> degre of the sunne upon the nether almykanteras<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_20">20</a></font></span> of bothe, and sett there another<br> <a name="an_21"></a> pricke. Ley than thy label over the degre of<br> <a name="an_26"></a> thi sonne, and fynd in the bordure the verrey<br> <a name="an_27"></a> tyde of the day, or of the night. And as verraily<br> <a name="an_28"></a> shalt thou fynde upon thin est orisonte<br> <a name="an_29"></a> thin ascendent.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_Rub">Rub</a></font></span> To knowe the spryng of the dawenyng<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_RubA">RubA</a></font></span> and the ende of the evenyng, the whiche ben<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_RubB">RubB</a></font></span> called the two crepuscules.<br> <a name="an_1"></a> Set the nadir of thy sonne upon 18 degrees<br> <a name="an_2"></a> of height among thyn almykanteras on the west<br> <a name="an_3"></a> syde; and ley thy label on the degre of thy<br> <a name="an_4"></a> sonne, and than shal the point of thy label<br> <a name="an_5"></a> shewen the spryng of the day.
