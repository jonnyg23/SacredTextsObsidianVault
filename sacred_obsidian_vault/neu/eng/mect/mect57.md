Thanne seide I, "Certes<br> <a name="an_4"></a> ryghtful is thin amonestynge and ful digne by<br> <a name="an_5"></a> auctorite. But that thou seydest whilom that<br> <a name="an_6"></a> the questioun of the devyne purveaunce is enlaced<br> <a name="an_7"></a> with many othere questiouns, I undirstande<br> <a name="an_8"></a> wel and prove it by the same thing.<br> <a name="an_9"></a> But I axe yif that thou wenest that hap be<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> anything in any weys; and yif thou wenest<br> <a name="an_11"></a> that hap be anything, what is it?"<br> <a name="an_12"></a> Thanne quod sche, "I haste me to yelden<br> <a name="an_13"></a> and assoilen to the the dette of my byheste, and<br> <a name="an_14"></a> to schewen and openen the the<br> <a name="an_15"></a> wey, by whiche wey thou maist comen ayein to thi contre. But<br> <a name="an_16"></a> al be it so that the thingis whiche that thou axest<br> <a name="an_17"></a> ben ryght profitable to knowe, yit ben thei<br> <a name="an_18"></a> divers somwhat fro the path of my purpos; and<br> <a name="an_19"></a> it is to douten that thou ne be makid weery<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_20">20</a></font></span> by mysweyes, so that thou ne maist nat<br> <a name="an_21"></a> suffise to mesuren the ryghte weie."<br> <a name="an_22"></a> "Ne doute the therof nothing," quod I; "for<br> <a name="an_23"></a> for to knowen thilke thingis togidre, in the<br> <a name="an_24"></a> whiche thinges I delite me gretly -- that schal<br> <a name="an_25"></a> ben to me in stede of reste, syn it nis nat to<br> <a name="an_26"></a> douten of the thingis folwynge, whan every syde<br> <a name="an_27"></a> of thi disputesoun schal han ben stedfast to me<br> <a name="an_28"></a> by undoutous feyth."<br> <a name="an_29"></a> "Thanne," seide sche, "that manere wol<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_30">30</a></font></span> I don the," and bygan to speken ryght thus:<br> <a name="an_31"></a> "Certes," quod sche, "yif any wyght<br> <a name="an_32"></a> diffynisse hap in this manere, that is to seyn that<br> <a name="an_33"></a> `hap is bytydynge ibrought forth by foolisshe<br> <a name="an_34"></a> moevynge and by no knyttynge of causes,' I<br> <a name="an_35"></a> conferme that hap nis ryght naught in no wise;<br> <a name="an_36"></a> and I deme al outrely that hap nis but an idel<br> <a name="an_37"></a> voys (as who seith, but an idel word), withouten<br> <a name="an_38"></a> any significacioun of thing summitted<br> <a name="an_39"></a> to that voys. For what place myght ben<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_40">40</a></font></span> left or duellynge to folie and to disordenaunce,<br> <a name="an_41"></a> syn that God ledeth and<br> <a name="an_42"></a> constreyneth alle thingis by ordre? And yif that any thing is<br> <a name="an_51"></a> woxen or comen of no causes, thanne schal<br> <a name="an_52"></a> it seme that thilke thing is comen or woxen of<br> <a name="an_53"></a> nawght; but yif this ne mai nat ben don, thanne<br> <a name="an_54"></a> is it nat possible that hap be any swich thing as<br> <a name="an_55"></a> I have diffynysschid a litil herebyforn."<br> <a name="an_56"></a> "How schal it thanne be?" quod I. "Nys ther<br> <a name="an_57"></a> thanne nothing that by right may ben clepid<br> <a name="an_58"></a> other hap or elles aventure of fortune; or is ther<br> <a name="an_59"></a> awght, al be it so that it is hidd fro the<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_60">60</a></font></span> peple, to whiche thing thise wordes ben<br> <a name="an_61"></a> covenable?"<br> <a name="an_62"></a> "Myn Aristotle," quod sche, "in the book of<br> <a name="an_63"></a> his Phisic diffynysseth this thing by schort<br> <a name="an_64"></a> resoun, and nyghe to the sothe."<br> <a name="an_65"></a> "In whiche manere?" quod I.<br> <a name="an_66"></a> "As ofte, quod sche, "as men don any thing<br> <a name="an_67"></a> for grace of any other thing, and another thing<br> <a name="an_68"></a> than thilke thing that men entenden to don<br> <a name="an_69"></a> bytideth by some causes, it is clepid hap.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_70">70</a></font></span> Ryght as a man dalf the erthe bycause of<br> <a name="an_71"></a> tylyinge of the feld, and founde ther a<br> <a name="an_72"></a> gobet of gold bydolven; thanne wenen folk<br> <a name="an_73"></a> that it is byfalle by fortunous bytydynge. But<br> <a name="an_74"></a> forsothe it nis nat of naught, for it hath his<br> <a name="an_75"></a> propre causes, of whiche causes the cours unforseyn<br> <a name="an_76"></a> and unwar semeth to han makid hap.<br> <a name="an_77"></a> For yif the tiliere of the feeld ne dulve nat in the<br> <a name="an_78"></a> erthe, and yif the hidere of the gold ne hadde<br> <a name="an_79"></a> hyd the gold in thilke place, the gold ne<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_80">80</a></font></span> hadde nat ben founde. Now mai I thus diffinysshen<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_90">90</a></font></span> hap: hap is an unwar betydinge<br> <a name="an_91"></a> of causes assembled in thingis that ben<br> <a name="an_92"></a> doon for som oothir thing; but thilke ordre,<br> <a name="an_93"></a> procedinge by an uneschuable byndinge togidre,<br> <a name="an_94"></a> whiche that descendeth fro the welle of<br> <a name="an_95"></a> purveaunce that ordeyneth alle thingis in hir<br> <a name="an_96"></a> places and in hir tymes, makith that the causes<br> <a name="an_97"></a> rennen and assemblen togidre.<br> <a name="an_1"></a> "Tigrys and Eufrates resolven and springen<br> <a name="an_2"></a> of o welle in the cragges of the roche of the<br> <a name="an_3"></a> contre of Achemenye, ther as the fleinge bataile<br> <a name="an_4"></a> ficcheth hir dartes retorned in the breestis<br> <a name="an_5"></a> of hem that folwen hem. And sone aftir the<br> <a name="an_6"></a> same ryverys, Tigris and Eufrates, unjoignen<br> <a name="an_7"></a> and departen hir watres. And if thei comen togidre<br> <a name="an_8"></a> and ben assemblid and clepid togidre<br> <a name="an_9"></a> into o cours, thanne moten thilke thingis<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> fleten togidre whiche that the watir of<br> <a name="an_11"></a> the entrechaungynge flood bryngeth. The<br> <a name="an_12"></a> schippes and the stokkes araced with the flood<br> <a name="an_13"></a> moten assemblen; and the watris imedled<br> <a name="an_14"></a> wrappeth or emplieth many fortunel happes<br> <a name="an_15"></a> or maneris; the whiche wandrynge happes<br> <a name="an_16"></a> natheles thilke enclynynge lowenesse of the<br> <a name="an_17"></a> erthe and the flowinge ordre of the slydinge<br> <a name="an_18"></a> watir governeth. Right so fortune, that semeth<br> <a name="an_19"></a> as it fletith with slakid or ungoverned<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_20">20</a></font></span> bridles, it suffreth bridelis (that is to seyn,<br> <a name="an_21"></a> to ben governed), and passeth by thilke<br> <a name="an_22"></a> lawe (that is to seyn, by the devyne ordenaunce)."<br> <a name="an_1"></a> "This undirstonde I wel," quod I, "and I accorde<br> <a name="an_2"></a> me that it is ryght as thou seist. But I<br> <a name="an_3"></a> axe yif ther be any liberte of fre wille in this<br> <a name="an_4"></a> ordre of causes that clyven thus togidre in<br> <a name="an_5"></a> hemself, or elles I wolde witen yif that the<br> <a name="an_6"></a> destinal cheyne constrenith the moevynges of<br> <a name="an_7"></a> the corages of men."<br> <a name="an_8"></a> "Yis," quod sche, "ther is liberte of fre wil,<br> <a name="an_9"></a> ne ther ne was nevere no nature of resoun<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> that it ne hadde liberte of fre wil. For every<br> <a name="an_11"></a> thing that may naturely usen resoun,<br> <a name="an_12"></a> it hath doom by whiche it discernith and demeth<br> <a name="an_13"></a> every thing; thanne knoweth it by itself<br> <a name="an_14"></a> thinges that ben to fleen and thinges that ben<br> <a name="an_15"></a> to desiren. And thilke thing that any wight<br> <a name="an_16"></a> demeth to ben desired, that axeth or desireth<br> <a name="an_17"></a> he; and fleeth thilke thing that he troweth be<br> <a name="an_18"></a> to fleen. Wherfore in alle thingis that resoun<br> <a name="an_19"></a> is, in hem also is liberte of willynge and of<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_20">20</a></font></span> nillynge. But I ne ordeyne nat (as who<br> <a name="an_21"></a> seith, I ne graunte nat) that this liberte be<br> <a name="an_22"></a> evenelyk in alle thinges. Forwhy in the sovereynes<br> <a name="an_23"></a> devynes substaunces (that is to seyn,<br> <a name="an_24"></a> in spiritz) jugement is more cleer, and wil nat<br> <a name="an_25"></a> icorrumped, and myght redy to speden thinges<br> <a name="an_26"></a> that ben desired. But the soules of men moten<br> <a name="an_27"></a> nedes be more fre whan thei loken hem in the<br> <a name="an_28"></a> speculacioun or lokynge of the devyne thought;<br> <a name="an_29"></a> and lasse fre whan thei slyden into the bodyes;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_30">30</a></font></span> and yit lasse fre whan thei ben gadrid<br> <a name="an_31"></a> togidre and comprehended in erthli<br> <a name="an_32"></a> membres; but the laste servage is whan that<br> <a name="an_33"></a> thei ben yeven to vices and han ifalle fro the<br> <a name="an_34"></a> possessioun of hir propre resoun. For aftir that<br> <a name="an_35"></a> thei han cast awey hir eyghen fro the lyght<br> <a name="an_36"></a> of the sovereyn sothfastnesse to lowe thingis<br> <a name="an_37"></a> and derke, anon thei derken by the cloude of<br> <a name="an_38"></a> ignoraunce and ben troubled by felonous talentz;<br> <a name="an_39"></a> to the whiche talentz whan thei approchen<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_40">40</a></font></span> and assenten, thei helpen and<br> <a name="an_41"></a> encrecen the servage whiche thei han<br> <a name="an_42"></a> joyned to hemself; and in this manere thei ben<br> <a name="an_43"></a> caytifs fro hir propre liberte. The whiche thingis<br> <a name="an_44"></a> natheles the lokynge of the devyne purveaunce<br> <a name="an_45"></a> seth, that alle thingis byholdeth and seeth fro<br> <a name="an_46"></a> eterne, and ordeyneth hem everiche in here<br> <a name="an_47"></a> merites as thei ben predestinat; and it is seid in<br> <a name="an_48"></a> Greke that `alle thinges he seeth and alle thinges<br> <a name="an_49"></a> he herith.'<br> <a name="an_1"></a> "Homer with the hony mouth (that is to seyn,<br> <a name="an_2"></a> Homer with the swete ditees) singeth that the<br> <a name="an_3"></a> sonne is cler by pure light; natheles yit ne<br> <a name="an_4"></a> mai it nat, by the infirme light of his bemes,<br> <a name="an_5"></a> breken or percen the inward entrayles of the<br> <a name="an_6"></a> erthe or elles of the see. So ne seth nat God,<br> <a name="an_7"></a> makere of the grete werld. To hym, that loketh<br> <a name="an_8"></a> alle thinges from an hey, ne withstondeth<br> <a name="an_9"></a> no thinges by hevynesse of erthe, ne the<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> nyght ne withstondeth nat to hym by the<br> <a name="an_11"></a> blake cloudes. Thilke God seeth in o strok<br> <a name="an_12"></a> of thought alle thinges that ben, or weren, or<br> <a name="an_13"></a> schollen comen; and thilke God, for he loketh<br> <a name="an_14"></a> and seeth alle thingis alone, thou maist seyn<br> <a name="an_15"></a> that he is the verrai sonne."<br> <a name="an_1"></a> Thanne seide I, "Now am I confowndide by<br> <a name="an_2"></a> a more hard doute than I was."<br> <a name="an_3"></a> "What doute is that?" quod sche, "for certes I<br> <a name="an_4"></a> conjecte now by whiche thingis thou art trubled."<br> <a name="an_5"></a> "It semeth," quod I, "to repugnen and to<br> <a name="an_6"></a> contrarien gretly, that God knoweth byforn alle<br> <a name="an_7"></a> thinges and that ther is any fredom of liberte.<br> <a name="an_8"></a> For yif so be that God loketh alle thinges<br> <a name="an_9"></a> byforn, ne God ne mai nat ben desceyved<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_10">10</a></font></span> in no manere, thanne moot it nedes ben that<br> <a name="an_11"></a> alle thinges betyden the whiche that the<br> <a name="an_12"></a> purveaunce of God hath seyn byforn to comen.<br> <a name="an_13"></a> For whiche, yif that God knoweth byforn nat<br> <a name="an_14"></a> oonly the werkes of men, but also hir conseilles<br> <a name="an_15"></a> and hir willes, thanne ne schal ther be no liberte<br> <a name="an_16"></a> of arbitrie; ne certes ther ne may be noon<br> <a name="an_17"></a> othir dede, ne no wil, but thilke whiche that the<br> <a name="an_18"></a> devyne purveaunce, that ne mai nat ben disseyved,<br> <a name="an_19"></a> hath felid byforn. For yif that thei<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_20">20</a></font></span> myghten writhen awey in othere manere<br> <a name="an_21"></a> than thei ben purveyed, thanne ne sholde<br> <a name="an_22"></a> ther be no stedefast prescience of thing to<br> <a name="an_23"></a> comen, but rather an uncerteyn opynioun; the<br> <a name="an_24"></a> whiche thing to trowen of God, I deme it felonye<br> <a name="an_25"></a> and unleveful.<br> <a name="an_26"></a> "Ne I ne proeve nat thilke same resoun (as who<br> <a name="an_27"></a> seith, I ne allowe nat, or I ne preyse nat, thilke<br> <a name="an_28"></a> same resoun) by whiche that som men wenen<br> <a name="an_29"></a> that thei mowe assoilen and unknytten the<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_30">30</a></font></span> knotte of this questioun. For certes thei<br> <a name="an_31"></a> seyn that thing nis nat to comen for that the<br> <a name="an_32"></a> purveaunce of God hath seyn byforn that it is to<br> <a name="an_33"></a> comen, but rathir the contrarie; and that is this:<br> <a name="an_34"></a> that, for that the thing is to comen, that therfore<br> <a name="an_35"></a> ne mai it nat ben hidd fro the purveaunce of<br> <a name="an_36"></a> God; and in this manere this necessite slideth<br> <a name="an_37"></a> ayein into the contrarie partie: ne it ne byhoveth<br> <a name="an_38"></a> nat nedes that thinges betiden that ben<br> <a name="an_39"></a> ipurveied, but it byhoveth nedes that thinges<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_40">40</a></font></span> that ben to comen ben ipurveied -- but as<br> <a name="an_41"></a> it were Y travailed (as who seith, that<br> <a name="an_42"></a> thilke answere procedith ryght as though men<br> <a name="an_43"></a> travaileden or weren besy) to enqueren the<br> <a name="an_44"></a> whiche thing is cause of the whiche thing, as<br> <a name="an_45"></a> whethir the prescience is cause of the necessite<br> <a name="an_46"></a> of thinges to comen, or elles that the necessite of<br> <a name="an_47"></a> thinges to comen is cause of the purveaunce. But<br> <a name="an_48"></a> I ne enforce me nat now to schewen it, that<br> <a name="an_49"></a> the bytidynge of thingis iwyst byforn is<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_50">50</a></font></span> necessarie, how so or in what manere that<br> <a name="an_51"></a> the ordre of causes hath itself; although<br> <a name="an_52"></a> that it ne seme naught that the prescience bringe<br> <a name="an_53"></a> in necessite of bytydinge to thinges to comen.<br> <a name="an_54"></a> "For certes yif that any wyght sitteth, it byhoveth<br> <a name="an_55"></a> by necessite that the opynioun be soth of<br> <a name="an_56"></a> hym that conjecteth that he sitteth. and<br> <a name="an_57"></a> ayeinward also is it of the contrarie: yif the<br> <a name="an_58"></a> opinioun be soth of any wyght for that he sitteth,<br> <a name="an_59"></a> it byhoveth by necessite that he sitte.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_60">60</a></font></span> Thanne is here necessite in the toon and in<br> <a name="an_61"></a> the tothir; for in the toon is necessite of<br> <a name="an_62"></a> syttynge, and certes in the tothir is necessite of<br> <a name="an_63"></a> soth. But therfore ne sitteth nat a wyght for that<br> <a name="an_64"></a> the opynioun of the sittynge is soth, but the<br> <a name="an_65"></a> opinioun is rather soth for that a wyght sitteth<br> <a name="an_66"></a> byforn. And thus, althoughe that the cause of the<br> <a name="an_67"></a> soth cometh of that other side (as who seith,<br> <a name="an_68"></a> that althoughe the cause of soth cometh of the<br> <a name="an_69"></a> sittynge, and nat of the trewe opinioun),<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_70">70</a></font></span> algatis yit is ther comune necessite in that<br> <a name="an_71"></a> oon and in that othir. For I wol<br> <a name="an_172"></a> answeren the thus: that thilke thing that is futur,<br> <a name="an_173"></a> whan it is referred to the devyne knowynge,<br> <a name="an_174"></a> than is it necessarie; but certis whan it is<br> <a name="an_175"></a> undirstonden in his owene kynde, men seen it<br> <a name="an_176"></a> outrely fre and absolut fro alle necessite.<br> <a name="an_177"></a> "For certes ther ben two maneris of<br> <a name="an_178"></a> necessites: that oon necessite is symple, as thus:<br> <a name="an_179"></a> that it byhovith by necessite that alle men<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_180">180</a></font></span> ben mortal or dedly; anothir necessite is<br> <a name="an_181"></a> condicionel, as thus: yif thow wost that a<br> <a name="an_182"></a> man walketh, it byhovith by necessite that he<br> <a name="an_183"></a> walke. Thilke thing, thanne, that any wight hath<br> <a name="an_184"></a> iknowe to be, it ne mai ben noon oothir weys<br> <a name="an_185"></a> thanne he knowith it to be. But this condicion ne<br> <a name="an_186"></a> draweth nat with hir thilke necessite simple; for<br> <a name="an_187"></a> certes this necessite condicionel -- the propre<br> <a name="an_188"></a> nature of it ne makith it nat, but the adjeccioun<br> <a name="an_189"></a> of the condicioun makith it. Thise thinges thanne, yif thei ben<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_200">200</a></font></span> referrid to the devyne sighte, than ben they<br> <a name="an_201"></a> maked necessarie by the condicioun of the<br> <a name="an_202"></a> devyne knowynge.
