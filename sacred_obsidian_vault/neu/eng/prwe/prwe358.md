 <p align="CENTER">
 PECULIAR WORDS AND
 PHRASES</p>
 <p>
 AN angry Cornishman would formerly say when in anger, "I shall push a
 stone in his cairn," meaning he shall see him buried.</p>
 <p>
  "<i>Curri mi clack er du cuirn</i>" is
 the expression of a Scotch Highlander (Labbach's <i>Prehistoric Times</i>).
 This is interpreted as a mark of respect, or, I will do something to
 build you a monument.</p>
 <p>
 In the parish of Breage, the habit of
 prefixing names to the people is common. The "Tubby" Prichard's are well
 known. "Alsie's' children are common. "Scaw" was applied to several
 families.</p>
