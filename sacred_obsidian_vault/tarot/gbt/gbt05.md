 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>General Book of the Tarot</i>, by A. E. Thierens, [1930], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">THE GREATER ARCANA</h4>  
 <p>In the Greater Arcana competent authors on the subject have rightly seen a system of rendering or symbolising the great cosmic principles of Creation <i>per se</i>. Not in relation to any special plane or element but above these, and consequently to be considered as the abstract origin of all and everything in the Lesser Arcana.</p>  
 <p>Papus gives us the descriptions of the Greater</p>  
 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 38</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Arcana from the <i>Book of Hermes</i>, which probably will be the source <i>in concrete</i> of all later descriptions up to our time. How this book has been delivered through the ages is not known to me, but the clearness and systematic accuracy of these descriptions are proof of a high origin.</p>  
 <p>Where Papus, in his book on <i>The Tarot of the Bohemians</i> ventures to indicate relationship between the symbols of the Greater Arcana and zodiacal and planetary principles, he is hopelessly wrong however. He does not insist on it nor does he appear to make any use at all of these relations or explain <i>how</i> they are to be found out. Logic is entirely absent in this particular side of his renderings.</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Arcana XXII or Zero: the Fool must evidently be taken for the principle of the Earth itself, upon which we are carried away in space without being able to loose ourselves from its course. It may be as well interpreted as the <i>Pars Fortunae</i>, which must not be mistaken for the 'Wheel of Fortune' in the series of the arcanas. This has as much to do with the former as the cusp of the Tenth house has in the horoscope with the <i>Pars Fortunae</i>.</p>
