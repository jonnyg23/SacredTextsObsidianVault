 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Pictorial Key to the Tarot</i>, by A.E. Waite, ill. by Pamela Colman Smith [1911], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">THE SUIT OF CUPS</h3> <h4 align="CENTER">King</h4> 
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_cuki"></a>
 <span align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/cuki.jpg"><img src="tn/cuki.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></span></p> 
 <p>He holds a short sceptre in his left hand and a great cup in his right; his throne is set upon the sea; on one side a ship is riding and on the other a dolphin is leaping. The implicit is that the Sign of the Cup naturally refers to water, which appears in all the court cards. <i>Divinatory Meanings</i>: Fair man, man of business, law, or divinity; responsible, disposed to oblige the Querent; also equity, art and science, including those who profess science, law and art; creative intelligence. <i>Reversed</i>: Dishonest, double-dealing man; roguery, exaction, injustice, vice, scandal, pillage, considerable loss.</p>
