 <p></p><h4 align="center">XI.</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">ANONYMOUS</h3>
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">And</span> here we may conveniently append a reference to the Dialogue of an ancient Christian writer on astrology—a blend of Platonism, Astrology, and Christianity—entitled <i>Hermippus de Astrologia Dialogus</i>, <a name="fr_660"></a><a href="#fn_660"><font size="1">1</font></a> from the name of the chief speaker.</p>
 <p>This writer was undoubtedly acquainted with our Corpus, for he quotes (p. 9. 3) from <i>C. H.</i>, i. 5; (p. 21, 5) from <i>C. H.</i>, x. (xi.) 12; (p. 70, 17) from <i>C. H.</i>, x. (xi.) 6; in a general fashion (p. 24, 25) from <i>C. H.</i>, xvi.; and phrases (p. 12, 21 and p. 14, 13) from <i>C. H.</i>, xviii.</p>
