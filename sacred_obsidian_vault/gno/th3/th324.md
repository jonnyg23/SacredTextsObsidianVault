 <p></p><h4 align="center">EXCERPT XXIII.</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">FROM “APHRODITE”</h3>
 <p><small>(Title in Patrizzi (p. 45) is “The Likeness of Children,” followed by: “From Aphrodite.”</small></p>
 <p><small>Text: Stob., <i>Phys</i>., xxxvi. 2, under heading: “Of Hermes from Aphrodite”; G. pp. 297, 298; M. i. 207, 208; W. i. 295, 296.</small></p>
 <p><small>Ménard, Livre IV., No. iii. of “Fragments Divers,” p. 273.)</small></p>
 <p>[——] How, [then,] are offspring born like to their parents? Or how are they returned <a name="fr_286"></a><a href="#fn_286"><font size="1">1</font></a> to [their own] species <a name="fr_287"></a><a href="#fn_287"><font size="1">2</font></a>?</p>
 <p>[<i>Aphrodite</i>.] I will set forth the reason. When generation stores up seed from the ripe blood being sweated forth, <a name="fr_288"></a><a href="#fn_288"><font size="1">3</font></a> it comes to pass that somehow theres exhaled from the whole mass <a name="fr_289"></a><a href="#fn_289"><font size="1">4</font></a> of limbs a certain essence, following the</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="green">p. 90</font></a></p>
 <p>law of a divine activity, as though the man himself were being born; the same thing also in the womans case apparently takes place.</p>
 <p>When, then, what floweth from the man hath the ascendancy, and keeps intact, the young ones brought to light resembling its sire; contrary wise, in the same way, [resembling] its dam.</p>
 <p>Moreover, if there should be ascendancy of any part, [then] the resemblance [of the young] will favour that [especial] part.</p>
 <p>But sometimes also for long generations the offspring favoureth the husbands form, because his decan has the greater influence <a name="fr_290"></a><a href="#fn_290"><font size="1">1</font></a> at that [particular] moment when the wife conceives.</p>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h4 align="center">COMMENT</h4>
 <p>This fragment belongs to a type of Hermetic literature of which it is the sole surviving specimen. It is in form identical with the Isis and Horus type; but what the name of the questioner of Aphrodite could have been is difficult to say.</p>
