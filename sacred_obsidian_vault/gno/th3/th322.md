 <p></p><h4 align="center">EXCERPT XXI.</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">OF ISIS TO HORUS</h3>
 <p><small>(Title in Patrizzi (p. 45) is “From Isis.”</small></p>
 <p><small>Text: Stob., <i>Flor</i>., xiii. 50, under the heading: “Of Hermes from the [Sermon] of Isis to Horus”; G. i. 328; M. i. 265; H. iii. 467.</small></p>
 <p><small>Schow gives another heading, which Gaisford (in a note) thinks is from the Vienna codex, namely: “Of Hermes from the Intercession (or Supplication,—Πρεσβείας) of Isis.” <a name="fr_285"></a><a href="#fn_285"><font size="1">1</font></a></small></p>
 <p><small>Ménard, Livre IV., No. ii. of “Fragments Divers,” p. 272.)</small></p>
 <p>A <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">refutation</span>, when it is recognized, O greatest King, carries the man who is refuted towards the desire of things he did not know before.</p>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h4 align="center">COMMENT</h4>
 <p>This fragment is clearly not in the style of the excerpt from the “Sermon of Isis to Hermes” (Ex. xxvii.); it is far more closely reminiscent of <i>C. H.</i>, xvi. or xvii., and is, therefore, probably from the Sermon of Asclepius to the King.</p>
