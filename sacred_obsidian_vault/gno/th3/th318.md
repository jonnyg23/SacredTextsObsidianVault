 <p></p><h4 align="center">EXCERPT XVII.</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">[OF SOUL, IV.]</h3>
 <p><small>(Patrizzi (p. 41) runs this on to the preceding without a break.</small></p>
 <p><small>Text: Stob., <i>Phys</i>., xli. 4, under heading: “Of the Same”—that is, “Of Hermes”; G. pp. 324, 325; M. i. 228, 229; W. i. 321, 322.</small></p>
 <p><small>Ménard, Livre IV., No. vi. of “Fragments of the Book of Hermes to Ammon,” pp. 265, 266.)</small></p>
 <p>1. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Soul</span>, Ammon, then, is essence containing its own end within itself; in [its] beginning taking to itself the way of life allotted it by Fate, it draws also unto itself a reason like to matter, possessing “heart” and “appetite.” <a name="fr_247"></a><a href="#fn_247"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 <p>“Heart,” too, is matter; if it doth make its state accordant with the Souls intelligence, it, [then,] becometh courage, and is not led away by cowardice.</p>
 <p>And “appetite” is matter, too; if it doth make its state accord with the Souls rational power, it [then] becometh temperance, and is not</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a></p>
 <p>moved by pleasure, for reasoning fills up the “appetites” deficiency.</p>
 <p>2. And when both [these] <a name="fr_248"></a><a href="#fn_248"><font size="1">1</font></a> are harmonized, and equalized, and both are made subordinate to the Souls rational power, justice is born.</p>
 <p>For that their state of equilibrium doth take away the “hearts” excess, and equalizes the deficiency of “appetite.”</p>
 <p>The source of these, <a name="fr_249"></a><a href="#fn_249"><font size="1">2</font></a> however, is the penetrating essence of all thought, <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_250"><font size="1">3</font></a> its self by its own self, [working] in its own reason that doth think round everything, <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_251"><font size="1">4</font></a> with its own reason as its rule. <a name="fr_252"></a><a href="#fn_252"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>
 <p>It is the essence that doth lead and guide as ruler; its reason is as twere its counsellor who thinks about all things. <a name="fr_253"></a><a href="#fn_253"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>
 <p>3. The reason of the essence, then, is gnosis of those reasonings which furnish the irrational [part] with reasonings conjecturing, <a name="fr_254"></a><a href="#fn_254"><font size="1">7</font></a>—a faint thing as compared with reasoning [itself], but reasoning as compared with the irrational, as echo unto voice, and moonlight to the sun.</p>
 <p>And “heart” and “appetite” are harmonized upon a rational plan; they pull the one against the other, and [so] they learn to know in their own selves a circular intent. <a name="fr_255"></a><a href="#fn_255"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>
