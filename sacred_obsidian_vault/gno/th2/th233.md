 <p></p><h4 align="center">CORPUS HERMETICUM (XVII.)</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">[OF ASCLEPIUS TO THE KING]</h3>
 <p align="center"><small>(Text: R. 354; Pat. at end of last piece.)</small></p>
 <p align="center">*          *          *          *          *</p>
 <p align="center">*          *          *          *          *</p>
 <p align="center">*          *          *          *          *</p>
 <p align="center">*          *          *          *          *</p>
 <p align="center">*          *          *          *          *</p>
 <p align="center">*          *          *          *          *</p>
 <p><i>Asclepius.</i> If thou dost think [of it], O King, even of bodies there are things bodiless. <a name="fr_623"></a><a href="#fn_622"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 <p><i>The King.</i> What [are they]?—(asked the King.)</p>
 <p><i>Asc.</i> The bodies that appear in mirrors—do they not seem then to have no body?</p>
 <p><i>The King.</i> It is so, O Asclepius <a name="fr_624"></a><a href="#fn_623"><font size="1">2</font></a>; thou thinkest like a God <a name="fr_625"></a><a href="#fn_624"><font size="1">3</font></a>!—(the King replied.)</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_286"><font size="1" color="green">p. 286</font></a></p>
 <p><i>Asc.</i> There are things bodiless as well as these; for instance, forms <a name="fr_626"></a><a href="#fn_625"><font size="1">1</font></a>—do not they seem to thee to have no body, but to appear in bodies not only of the things which are ensouled, but also of those which are not ensouled?</p>
 <p><i>The King.</i> Thou sayest well, Asclepius.</p>
 <p><i>Asc.</i> Thus, [then,] there are reflexions of things bodiless on bodies, and of bodies too upon things bodiless—that is to say, [reflexions] of the Sensible on the Intelligible World, and of the [World] Intelligible on the Sensible.</p>
 <p>Wherefore, pay worship to the images, O King, since they too have their forms as from the World Intelligible.</p>
 <p>(Thereon His Majesty arose and said:)</p>
 <p><i>The King.</i> It is the hour, <a name="fr_627"></a><a href="#fn_626"><font size="1">2</font></a> O Prophet, to see about the comfort of our guests. To-morrow, [then,] will we resume our sacred converse. <a name="fr_628"></a><a href="#fn_627"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
