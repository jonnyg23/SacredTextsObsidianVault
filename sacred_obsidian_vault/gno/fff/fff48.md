 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Fragments of a Faith Forgotten</i>, by G.R.S. Mead, [1900], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">vi. From the Letter on the Community of Friends.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">Concerning the People of the Beloved.</font></span>"Many of these things which are written in the public volumes, are found written in the Church of God. For those teachings which are common, are the words which proceed from the heart, the law written in the heart. This is the People of the Beloved who are loved by and love Him."</p>  
 <p>Clement assumes that Valentinus means by "public volumes" the Jewish writings and the books of the philosophers.</p>  
 <p>The "public volumes," however, for Valentinus included not only the works of the philosophers and the scriptures of the Jews, but also the scriptures of all other religions, and also the Christian documents in general circulation. He merely asserts that the only "common" or general truths are those pertaining to the Community of Friends, or Saints, who form the Church of God, the People of the Beloved. These truths come from the heart; he protests against the narrow view that can find truth in only one set of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_306"><font size="1" color="green">p. 306</font></a></p>  
 <p>scriptures; and declares it is in all scriptures and philosophies, if one looks to the spirit and not the letter.</p>
