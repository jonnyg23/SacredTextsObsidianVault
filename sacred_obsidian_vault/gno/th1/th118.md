 <h5 align="center">ON THE DRINKING OF WINE </h5>
 
 <p>VI. 1. And as for wine, the servants of the God in Sun-city <a name="fr_819"></a><a href="#fn_820"><font size="1">2</font></a> do not at all bring it into the sacred place, as tis not right [for them] to drink by day while He, their Lord and King, looks on. </p>
 
 <p>2. The rest [of them <a name="fr_820"></a><a href="#fn_821"><font size="1">3</font></a>] use it indeed, but sparingly.</p>
 
 <p>They have, however, many times of abstinence at which they drink no wine, but spend them in the search for wisdom, learning and teaching the [truth] about the Gods. </p>
 
 <p>3. The kings used to drink it, though in certain measure according to the sacred writings, as Hecatæus has narrated, <a name="fr_821"></a><a href="#fn_822"><font size="1">4</font></a> for they were priests [as well]. </p>
 
 <p>4. They began to drink it, however, only from the time of Psammetichus; <a name="fr_822"></a><a href="#fn_823"><font size="1">5</font></a> but before that they used not to drink wine.</p>
 
 <p>Nor did they make libation of it as a thing dear to the Gods, but as the blood of those who fought against the Gods, <a name="fr_823"></a><a href="#fn_824"><font size="1">6</font></a>—from whom, when they fell and mingled with </p>
