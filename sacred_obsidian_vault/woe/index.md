 <hr>
 <p>
 This page spotlights the books in the series
 <cite>The Wisdom of the East</cite>,
 most of which were published
 in the first two decades of the twentieth century under the
 editorship of L. Cranmer-Byng and S.A. Kapadia.
 We are currently attempting a systematic inventory of the
 entire series.
 </p>
 <hr>
 
 
 <h3>Baha'i</h3>
 <p>
 <img src="../new2.gif">
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../bhi/sog/index.htm">The Splendour of God</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Eric Hammond</span> [<span class="c_d">1909</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A collection of profound Baha'i sacred texts.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <h3>Buddhism</h3>
 
 <p>
 <img src="../new2.gif">
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../bud/tpol/index.htm">The Path of Light</a></span><br>
 tr. by <span class="c_a">L.D. Barnett</span> [<span class="c_d">1909</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A translation of the Bodhicharyavatara of Santideva, a key Mahayana Buddhist text.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <img src="../new2.gif">
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../bud/busc/index.htm">Buddhist Scriptures</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">E. J. Thomas</span> [<span class="c_d">1913</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A short collection of Buddhist scripture, from the Wisdom of the East series.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../bud/bups/index.htm">Buddhist Psalms</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">S. Yamabe</span> and <span class="c_a">L. Adams Beck</span> [<span class="c_d">1921</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A key Pure Land text, by the founder of the most popular form of Buddhism in Japan.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../bud/wov/index.htm">The Buddha's Way of Virtue</a></span><br>
 tr. by <span class="c_a">W.D.C. Wagiswara</span> and <span class="c_a">K.J. Saunders</span> [<span class="c_d">1920</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A translation of the Dhammapada, one of the central Buddhist sacred texts.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <h3>Confucianism</h3>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>

 <span class="c_t"><a href="../cfu/boo/index.htm">The Book of Odes</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">L. Cranmer-Byng</span> [<span class="c_d">1908</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A selection of ancient Chinese poetry from the Shih Ching.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>

 by <span class="c_a">Ivan Chen</span> [<span class="c_d">1908</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A translation of the Hsiao Ching, a classic text which defines the web of Confucian social relationships.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">

 by <span class="c_a">L. Cranmer-Byng</span> [<span class="c_d">1916</span>]<br>

 </span>
 </p>
