 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Legends of Maui, A Demi-God of Polynesia</i>, by W. D. Westervelt, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h1 align="CENTER">HELPS TO PRONUNCIATION</h1> 
 <p>There are three simple rules which practically control Hawaiian pronunciation: (1) Give each vowel the German sound. (2) Pronounce each vowel. (3) Never allow a consonant to close a syllable.</p> 
 <p>Interchangeable consonants are many. The following are the most common: h=s; l=r; k=t; n=ng; v=w.</p>
