 <hr>
 
 <p>
 This section has texts relating to the religion and mythology of the
 Pacific Islanders.
 </p><hr>
 <a name="general"></a>
 <h3>General</h3>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="om/index.htm">Oceanic Mythology</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Roland B. Dixon</span> [<span class="c_d">1916</span>].<br>
 <span class="c_b">A highly readable and scholarly cross-cultural study
 of Pacific mythology and folklore</span>, covering Polynesia, Micronesia,
 Melanesia, Indonesia and Australia.
 It includes summaries of material only available in obscure
 19th Century scholarly journals.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <a name="easter"></a>
 <h3>Easter Island</h3>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ei/index.htm">Te Pito Te Henua, Or Easter Island</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">William J. Thomson</span> [<span class="c_d">1891</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Includes images of the famous rongo-rongo tablets and one of the few attested translations available.</span>
 </span>
 
 
 <a name="maori"></a>
 <h3>Maori</h3>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="grey/index.htm">Polynesian Mythology</a></span><br>
 by Sir <span class="c_a">George Grey</span> [<span class="c_d">1854</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A primary source for the myths and legends of the Maori people of New Zealand.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="mrm/index.htm">Maori Religion and Mythology</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Edward Shortland</span> [<span class="c_d">1882</span>]<br>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="lww/index.htm">The Lore of the Whare-Wananga</a></span><br>
 <span class="c_a">S. Percy Smith</span> [<span class="c_d">1913</span>]<br>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <a name="hawaii"></a>
 <h3>Hawaii</h3>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hm/index.htm">Hawaiian Mythology</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Martha Warren Beckwith</span> [<span class="c_d">1940</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">An extensive critical study of Hawaiian mythology</span>, with
 parallels to other Pacific islander cultures and numerous variations
 on each text.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ku/index.htm">The Kumulipo, A Hawaiian Creation Chant</a></span><br>
 translated with commentary by <span class="c_a">Martha Warren Beckwith</span> [<span class="c_d">1951</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The Kumulipo is the Royal Hawaiian Creation chant</span>, describing the
 emergence of life from the ocean and listing hundreds of generations
 of descendants from the primal gods and goddesses.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="lku/index.htm">The Kumulipo</a></span><br>
 translated by Queen <span class="c_a">Liliuokalani</span> [<span class="c_d">1897</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A translation of the Kumulipo by the last Queen of Hawaii.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="ulh/index.htm">Unwritten Literature of Hawaii: The Sacred Songs of the Hula</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Nathaniel B. Emerson</span> [<span class="c_d">1909</span>]
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hft/index.htm">Hawaiian Folk Tales</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Thomas G. Thrum</span> [<span class="c_d">1907</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">An anthology of classic Hawaiian folklore, including tales of Menehunes and Kahunas.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <center><hr width="50%"></center>
 
 <p>The following is a series of books of Hawaiian mythology,
 folkore, and legends by W.D. Westervelt from the turn of the 20th Century.
 Although Westervelt often wrote in a romanticized style,
 the folklore is genuine.</p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="maui/index.htm">Legends of Maui</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span> [<span class="c_d">1910</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A collection of Hawaiian and Polynesian legends about the culture hero, Maui.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hloh/index.htm">Hawaiian Legends of Old Honolulu</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span> [<span class="c_d">1915</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Of all of the sacred landscapes of the Pacific, the area around Honolulu is rich in lore.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hlov/index.htm">Hawaiian Legends of Volcanoes</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span> [<span class="c_d">1916</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The Fire Goddess Pele, her deeds, family and loves.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hlog/index.htm">Hawaiian Legends of Ghosts and Ghost-Gods</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span> [<span class="c_d">1916</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Tales of the Hawaiian afterlife, and those who returned from it by magic or cunning.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="hhl/index.htm">Hawaiian Historical Legends</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span> [<span class="c_d">1923</span>, no renewal]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Stories from Hawaii during the period of European contact.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <a name="samoa"></a>
 <h3>Samoa</h3>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="jpolys/ssc.htm">The Samoan Story of Creation</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">John Fraser</span>
 (<span class="c_t">Journal of the Polynesian Society, Vol. 1 164-88</span>)
 [<span class="c_d">1891</span>].
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <a name="tahiti"></a>
 <h3>Tahiti</h3>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="noa/index.htm">Noa Noa</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Paul Gauguin</span>, translated from the French by <span class="c_a">Otto Frederick Theis</span> [<span class="c_d">1919</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Gauguin came to Tahiti to find the primitive, but was amazed by the advanced knowledge embedded in their mythology.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 
 <a name="melanesia"></a>
 <h3>Melanesia</h3>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="baloma/index.htm">Baloma; the Spirits of the Dead in the Trobriand Islands</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Bronislaw Malinowski</span> [<span class="c_d">1916</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A classic ethnographic monograph.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
