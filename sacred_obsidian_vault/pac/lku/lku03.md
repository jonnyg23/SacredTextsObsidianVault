 <p><a name="page_6"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 6</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE SECOND ERA.</h3>  <h4 align="center">FIRST VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>The first child born of Powehiwehi (dusky night)<br> Tossed up land for Pouliuli (darkest night),<br> For Mahiuma or Maapuia,<br> And lived in the land of Pohomiluamea (sloughy hill of Mea);<br> Suppressed the noise of the growth of unripe fruit,<br> For fear Uliuli would cause it to burst, and the stench<br> To disagree and turn sour,<br> For pits of darkness and pits of night.<br> Then the seven waters became calm.<br> Then was born a child (kama), ’twas a Hilu and swam.<br> The Hilu is a fish with standing fins,<br> On which Pouliuli sat.<br> So undecided seemed Powehiwehi,<br> For Pouliuli was husband<br> And Powehiwehi his wife.<br> <i>                                Fish</i>.<br> And fish was born, the Naia (porpoise) was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Mano (shark) was born, the Moana was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Mau was born, the Maumau was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Nana was born, the Mana was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Nake was born, the Make was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Napa was born, the Nala was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Pala was born, the Kala was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Paka (an eel) was born, the Papa (crab) was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Kalakala was born, the Huluhulu was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Halahala was born, the Palapala was born in the sea and swam. <a name="page_7"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 7</font></a><br> The Pea (starfish) was born, the Lupe was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Ao was born, the Awa was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Aku (bonito) was born, the Ahi (same kind) was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Opelu (same as above) was born, the Akule was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Amaama (mullet) was born, the Anae (large kind) was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Ehu was born, the Nehu was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Iao (used for bait) was born, the Aoao was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Ono (large fish) was born, the Omo was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Pahau (striped flatfish) was born, the Lauhau was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Moi was born, the Loiloi was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Mao was born, the Maomao was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Kaku was born, the A'ua'u was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Kupou was born, the Kupoupou was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Weke was born, the Lele was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Palani was born, the Nuku Moni was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Ulua was born, the Hahalua was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Aoaonui was born, the Pakukui was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Maiii was born, the Alaihi was born in the sea and swam.<br> The Oo was born, the Akilolo was born in the sea and swam.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_8"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 8</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">SECOND VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>               <i>Fish and vine</i>.<br> The Nenue was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Lauhue that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; salt water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">THIRD VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>               <i>Fish and vine</i>.<br> Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Haha was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Puhala that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; salt water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">FOURTH VERSE.</h4>

 <p><a name="page_10"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 10</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">ELEVENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>          <i>Fish and large tree.</i><br> Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Kapoou was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by Kou that grew in the forest. <br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; salt water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">TWELFTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>        <i>Fish and yam or Uhi</i><br>          (<i>Impomea batatas</i>).<br> Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Hauliuli was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Uhi <a name="fr_0"></a><a href="#fn_0"><font size="1">1</font></a> that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">THIRTEENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Weke was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Wauke that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">FOURTEENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>          <i>Fish and Awa</i><br>              (<i>Kawa</i>).<br> Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Aawa was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Awa that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>

 <p><a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 11</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">FIFTEENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>            <i>Fish and grass</i>.<br> Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Ulae was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Mokae that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; salt water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">SIXTEENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Palaoa (sea-elephant) was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Aoa that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; salt water is life to fish;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">SEVENTEENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>The train of Palaoa (walrus) that swim by<br> Embracing only the deep blue waters,<br> Also the Opule that move in schools,<br> The deep is as nothing to them.<br> And the Kumimi (a crab) and Lohelohe (a locust) cling together<br> To the rolling motion of their cradle<br> On their path so narrow, so slim, to move,<br> Till Pimoe (a mermaid) is found in the depth of her cave,<br> With Hikawainui, and Hikawaina<br> Amongst piles of heated coral<br> That were thrown in piles unevenly,<br> So thin and scraggy in the blue tide.<br> Surely it must be dismal, that unknown deep;<br> ’Tis a sea of coral from the depth of Paliuli,<br> And when the land recedes from them<br> The east is still in darkness of night,<br>                 ’Tis night.</p>
