 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 77</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE SIXTEENTH ERA.</h3>  
 <p>Maui was the man and Hinakealohaila was the wife.<br> Nanamoa was the man and Hinakapaikua the wife.<br> Kulai was the man and Hinahoopaia the wife.<br> Nanakuae was the man, Keauhonua the wife.<br> Kapawa was the man, Kukuluhiokalani the wife.<br> Heleipawa was the man, Kookookumaikalani the wife.<br> Hulumalailena was the man, Hinamaikalani the wife.<br> Aikanaka was the man, Hinaaiakamalama the wife.<br> Pumainua was born, then Hema and Puna last of all.<br> Hema snatched the goblet in the scuffle with Luamahaheau.<br> Kahainui of Hema was born, Hinauluohia the wife.<br> Wahieloa the man, Hoolaukahili the wife.<br> Laka the man, Hikawaolena the wife.<br> Luanuu the man, Kapokuleiula the wife.<br> Kamea the man, Popomaile the wife.<br> Pohukaina the man, Huahuakapolei the wife.<br> Hua the man, Hikiiluna the wife.<br> Paunuikaikeanaina the man, Manokalililani the wife.<br> Huanuiekalalailaikai the man, Kapoea the wife.<br> Paunuikuakaolokea the man, Kapuhookia the wife.<br> Haho the man, Kauwilaianapu the wife.<br> Palena the man, Hikawainui the wife.<br> Hanalaanui was born, Hanalaaiki was born.<br> Hanalaaiki the man, Kapukapu was born.<br> Mauiloa the man, Kauhua was born.<br> Alau the man, Moeikeana was born.<br> Kanunokokuhelii the man, Keikauhale.</p>  <table border="0" width="100%">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="327">   
 <p>Lonomai   (heard from) was the man.<br>   Wakalana (time of straining) was the man.<br>   Alo (dodge) was the man.<br>   Kaheka (ditches).<br>   Mapuleo.<br>   Paukei.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="327">   
 <p>Kolu   (third) the wife.<br>   Kawai (the water) the wife.<br>   Puia the wife.<br>   Mailou the wife.<br>   Kamaeokalani.<br>   Painalea.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>

 <p>Kawaukaohele was born, also Keleanuinohoanaapiapi,--<br> The woman that lived at Kalamakua,<br> From whence Laielohelohe was born and who married<br> Piilani. Piikea was born and married Umi; to<br> Kumalaenuia Umi, who owned those precipices from whence slaves were held.<br> Kumalaenui of Umi was the husband of Kunuunuipuawalau.<br> Their son, Makua, was the only high chief (wohi kukahi) of the island. Kapohelemai, his wife, whose rank as sacred wohi Alii and Honor. So their heir I, the I of the Kingdom,<br> Whose power and right to execute,<br> And lord of the famed lands of Pakini,<br> Of the sliding Ohia and the weaving of the islands of Hawaii<br> To Ahu--to Ahu of I, of Lono,<br>                      Of Lonoikamakahiki.</p>
