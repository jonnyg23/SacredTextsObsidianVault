   <h3 align="center">THE FIFTEENTH ERA.</h3>  
 <p>Haumea, she of Nuumea that stood in loose ground<br> Of Mehani Nuu's thick sward at Kuaihealani at Paliuli,<br> As the deep darkness is the greatness of her rank,<br> The sacred lonely heavens from Kamahaikaua,<br> From Kamehaikaua, god of Kauakahi,<br> Who cut the sacred step, and cut the heavenly Haiuli,<br> Felt bitterness in his breast, anger and jealousy of his rival,<br> Landed on the island of Lua, of Ahu a Lua, and stayed at Wawau.<br> This female god, wife of Makea,<br> Was Haumea the great and fearless one.<br> A strange woman was Haumea in her life,<br> For she married her grandchildren,<br> And married her children;<br> Married her son Kaukahi, whose wife was Kuaimehani; <a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 74</font></a><br> Married her grandson Kauahulihonua,<br> Whose wife was Hulihoma;<br> Married her grandson Haloa, whose wife was Hinamanoulu;<br> Yes, his wife.<br> Married her grandson Waia, whose wife was Huhune;<br> Married her grandson Hinanalo, whose wife was Haunuu;<br> Married her grandson Nauakahili, whose wife was Haulani;<br> Married her grandson Wailoa, whose wife was Hikopuanaiea.<br> Kio was born and Haumea was discovered,<br> Haumea was found to be wrinkled,<br> Very aged, with watery eyes;<br> Her form was irregular,<br> Was sour temper’d and crabbed.<br> ’Twas Uaia who discovered that she was filthy.<br> Her back was wrinkled as well as front.<br> He stamped on her breast, which shook down the wall of Nuumea;<br> Shook the floor; her breast was like that of a dog;<br> Those breasts which raised high chiefs<br> Took deep root from this woman of the forest.<br> Ole was born the man and his wife.<br> Pupue was the man and Kamahele his wife.<br> Manaku was the man and Hikohoale his wife.<br> Kahiko was the man and Kaea his wife.<br> Luakahakona was the man and Koulamaikalani his wife.<br> Luanuu was the man and Kawaomaaukele his wife.<br> Kii was the man and Hinakoula his wife.<br> Ulu was born, Nanaulu was born.<br> Ulu was the man, Punuu his wife.<br> Nana was the man and Kapulani was his wife.<br> Nanaie was the man and Kahaumokuleia was his wife.<br> Nanaielani was the man and Hinakinau was his wife.<br> Waikalani was the man and Kekauilani was his wife.<br> Kuheleimoana was the man and Mapuuaiaaala was his wife.<br> Konohiki was the man and Hakaululena was his wife.<br> Waolena was the man and Mahuie was his wife. <a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a><br> Akalana was the man and Hinaakeahi was his wife.<br> First Maui was the man and Central Maui was born.<br> Crouching Maui was born, Maui with a malo was born.<br> The malo with which Akalana girded his loins<br> From which Hina became pregnant, and by fire brought to life a fowl.<br> An egg was that child, which Hina brought forth.<br> Her husband was not a fowl,<br> Yet a chicken was brought to life.<br> When the child cooed Hina asked,<br> I have no husband, yet a child is born.<br> A brave child is born to Hinaakeahi (Hina of the fire).<br> It roused the anger of Kialoa and Kiaakapoko (tall post and short post).<br> They are Hina's brothers,<br> The two posts that guarded the low cave;<br> They fought hard with Maui and were thrown,<br> And red water flowed freely from Maui's forehead.<br> This was the first shower by Maui.<br> They fetched from the sacred Awa bush of Kane and Kanaloa.<br> Then came the second shower by Maui.<br> The third shower was when the elbow of the Awa was broken.<br> The fourth shower was the sacred bamboo of Kane and Kanaloa.<br> The fifth shower was the edge of the umu (oven).<br> The sixth shower was the first rise.<br> Maui sobbed and inquired for his father.<br> Hina denied he had a father;<br> That the malo of Kalana was his father.<br> Then he longed for fish for Hinaakeahi,<br> Learnt the art of fishing, was sent by Hinaakeahi.<br> Go hence to your father;<br> ’Tis there you will find line and hook;<br> That is the hook, ’tis called Manaiakalani. <a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 76</font></a><br> When the hook catches land ’twill bring the old seas together.<br> Bring hither the large Alae of Hina,<br> The sister bird<br> Of the great fiery showers caused by Maui.<br> He is the great magician that caught<br> By the mouth and fins Pimoe,<br> The royal fish that raise commotion in the sea.<br> Pimoe was wooed and won by the Ina of Maui.<br> But pity sprang for Mahanauluehu,<br> The children of Pimoe.<br> They were taken ashore, eaten by Maui, all but the fins.<br> So Pimoe was saved by the fins.<br> Mahanauluehu was saved by the tail.<br> Hinakeka was abducted by Peapea (the bat),<br> The great god of the bats.<br> So showers in plenty were sent by Maui<br> Which scratched the eyes of Peapea with eight eyes.<br> They fought a battle with Moemoe.<br> Maui became reckless and fought the sun<br> For the noose that Maui laid.<br> And Winter (Makalii) won the sun,<br> So Summer was won by Maui.<br> They drank of the yellow waters to the dregs<br> Of Kane and Kanaloa.<br> By strategy the war<br> Embraced Hawaii, encompassed Maui,<br> Kauai, around Oahu.<br> At Kahaluu was the after birth, at Waikane the navel.<br> It dropped at Hakipuu, at Kualoa.<br> For this is Maui of the malo,<br> The wonder of the land,<br>             Yes! of the land.</p>
