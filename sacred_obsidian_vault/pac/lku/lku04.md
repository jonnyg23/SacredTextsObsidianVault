 <p><a name="page_12"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 12</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE THIRD ERA.</h3>  <h4 align="center">FIRST VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>He was the man and she the woman;<br> The man that was born in the dark age,<br> And the woman was born in the age of bubbles.<br> The sea spread, the land spread,<br> The waters spread, the mountains spread,<br> The Poniu grew tall with advancing time,<br> The Haha grew and had nine leaves,<br> And the Palai (fern) sprout that shot forth leaves of high chiefs<br> Brought forth Poeleele, a man (darkness),<br> Who lived with Pohaha, a woman (bubbles),<br> And brought forth generations of Haha (kalo tops).<br>                       The Haha was born.</p>  <h4 align="center">SECOND VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>             <i>Insects</i>.<br> The Haha was born and became parent;<br> His offspring, a Hahalelelele, was born.<br> The Peelua (caterpillar) was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was a flying Pulelehua (butterfly).<br> The Naonao (an ant) was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was a Pinao (dragonfly).<br> The Unia was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was an Uhini, and flew (grasshopper).<br> The Naio was born and became parent (waterworms);<br> Its offspring was a Nalo, and flew (flies).<br>                <i>Birds</i>.<br> The Hualua was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was a bird, and flew.<br> The Ulili was born and became parent (snipe);<br> Its offspring was a Kolea, and flew (plover).<br> The A-o was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was an Au, and flew (a species of plover).<br> The Akekeke was born and became parent (sea-bird);<br> Its offspring was Elepaio, and flew (woodpecker). <a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a><br> The Alae was born and became parent (mud hen);<br> Its offspring was an Apapane, and flew (red woodpecker).<br> The Alala was born and became parent (crow);<br> Its offspring was an Alawi, and flew.<br> The Eea was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was a Alaiaha, and flew.<br> The Mamo was born and became parent (the royal bird);<br> Its offspring was the Oo, and flew (black woodpecker).<br> The Moha was born and became parent (wingless bird);<br> Its offspring was a Moli, and flew.<br>             <i>Sea-birds</i>.<br> The Kiki was born and became parent;<br> Its offspring was the Ukihi, and flew.<br> The Kioea was born and became parent (stork);<br> Its offspring was a Kukuluaeo, and flew (crane).<br> The Ka Iwa was born and became parent (sea-bird);<br> Its offspring was a Koae, and flew (man-of-war hawk).<br> The Kala was born and became parent (sea-bird);<br> Its offspring was a Kaula, and flew (sea-bird).<br> Then was born the Unauna (shell-fish, part crab);<br> Its offspring was an Aukuu, and flew.<br>              These birds fly together in flocks<br>              And usually light on the sea beach<br>              And array themselves in line.</p>  <h4 align="center">THIRD VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>They covered the land of Kanehunamoku.<br> These were born birds of the land<br> And birds of the sea.<br> Man was born of Waiololi, woman Waiolola,<br> The Lupe was born and lived in the sea;<br> Guarded by the Lupe that grew in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises<br> Through a channel; the Io is life to birds;<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">FOURTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Noio lived on the sea,<br> Guarded by the Io that lived in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises.<br> Eggs and the Io are life to birds,<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">FIFTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Kolea of the island lived on the sea,<br> Guarded by the Kolea that flew on land.<br> A night of flight by noises.<br> Eggs and Io are life to birds,<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">SIXTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Hehe was born and lived on the sea,<br> Guarded by the Nene that lived in the forest.<br> A night of flight by noises.<br> Eggs and Io are life to birds,<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">SEVENTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Aukuu was born and lived on the sea (Pewit),<br> Guarded by the Ekupuu that grew on land.<br> A night of flight by noises.<br> Eggs and Io are life to birds,<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">EIGHTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>Man by Waiololi, woman by Waiolola,<br> The Noeo was born and lived on the sea,<br> Guarded by the Pueo that lived in the forest. <a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a><br> A night of flight by noises.<br> Eggs and Io are food for birds,<br> So the gods may enter, but not man.</p>  <h4 align="center">NINTH VERSE.</h4>  
 <p>This is the leaping point of the bird Halulu<br> Of Kiwaa, the bird of many notes,<br> And of those birds that fly closely together as to shade the sun,<br> They cover the land with their young to the rock's edge,<br> Their gall burst easily with a smack<br> As the Ape sprout whose delicate shoots<br> Shoot forth their young sprouts and spread<br> And bring forth in their birth many branches.<br>              It was so on that night,<br>              It was so this night,<br>              It was dark at the time with Poeleele,<br>              And darkest age--of bubbly night.<br>                           ’Tis night.</p>
