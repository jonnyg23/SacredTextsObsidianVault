 <h1 align="CENTER">Votive Phalli.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">BRONZES FROM HERCULANEUM.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">PLATE XXVIII.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">FIGURES 1 AND 2.</p>
 <p>THE first two figures represent amulets divided into two parts, one of which is in the form of a phallus, and the other of an arm with a hand, making the <i>figue</i>, that obscene gesture which we have spoken of in describing plate 19.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">FIGURE 3.</p>
 <p>This singular figure, to which are suspended four little bells, in its wings and head resembles a bird, and in the hinder part a lion: a threefold symbol of the generative principle, which fights with the strength of a lion, propagates with the swiftness of an eagle, and triumphs over all obstacles.</p>
