 <h1 align="CENTER">Two Idols</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">BRONZE.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">PLATE XX</p>
 <p>No. 1 is a little Hermes bearing the chlamys, the pedum, or pastoral hook the <i>pedum</i>, and the <i>rhyton</i>, the horn of an animal, which the ancients used to drink out of.</p>
 <p>Athenæus <a name="fr_26"></a><a href="#fn_26"><font size="1">1</font></a> speaks of these horns, and also adds that wooden ones were made.</p>
 <p>No. 2 is a bearded and homed Faun also bearing the chlamys and the pedum. His right arm is raised horizontally, and his forefinger is stretched out in sign of command.</p>
 <p>Of this sort of little idols there have been found, in the excavations of Pompeii and Herculaneum, a number almost equal to that of the little figures that come to us from Egypt. Their repetition would be tedious and unnecessary.</p>
