 <h1 align="CENTER">THE WORSHIP OF THE GENERATIVE POWERS:</h1>
 <h2 align="CENTER">BY THOMAS WRIGHT</h2>
 <h4 align="CENTER">[1865]</h4>
 </td>

 <hr>
 <p>
 This work first appeared as the second half of the 1865 printing of
 <cite><a href="../dwp/index.htm">A Discourse on the
 Worship of Priapus</a></cite>.
 Wright's extended essay on Phallic worship is distinguished by
 much better scholarship and writing than some of the other works
 of this genre.
 Along with the usual suspects
 (ancient and modern phallic objects, fertility rituals and so on)
 Wright devotes the longest section of this text to exploring
 what would become known as the 'Witch Cult'.
 Wright lays out a compelling case for the
 survival of ancient fertility rituals in the otherwise puzzling
 accounts of the Witches' Sabbath.
 </p>
 <p><font size="-2">Production note: this text, which has no
 chapter divisions in the
 original, has been broken down into logical sections in the production
 of this etext.</font>
