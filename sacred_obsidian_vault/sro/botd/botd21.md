 <p><a name="page_376"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 376</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VOCABULARY.</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Occult Science</span>.</p>  
 <p>The secret or esoteric science of the ancients and the mediæval Rosicrucians; the science which deals with the invisible essence of things and explains their mysterious nature.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Astral</span>.</p>  
 <p>Pertaining to the stars, or a subtle and ordinarily invisible substance which surrounds the earth and stars and pervades all things; a vapor-like aether which pervades and holds together the molecules of all things.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Akasa</span>.</p>  
 <p>The primordial, homogeneous and all-pervading substance or essence out of which the universe and all differentiated matter is evolved. The highest æther, but not the luminiferous æther of western science.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Elementals</span>.</p>  
 <p>Invisible, but intelligent centres of force; atomic lives; the beings that live on the astral plane.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Chakram</span>.</p>  
 <p>The eastern name for certain centres of force in the human organism.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_377"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 377</font></a></p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Karma</span>.</p>  
 <p>The eastern name for the universal and all-inclusive law of cause and effect, action and reaction; it includes all rewards or punishments that may be due us because of good or evil deeds we have done, either in this life or some or many past lives.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kriyasakti</span>.</p>  
 <p>The power of adept will which can condense, control, and mould into forms the astral substance around the operator.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kshatriya</span>.</p>  
 <p>The warrior cast of the four into which the people of ancient India or Aryavarta, were divided.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mantra</span>.</p>  
 <p>An eastern chant of peculiarly intonated sounds which, when rightly uttered, awaken certain occult forces in the æther.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Master</span>.</p>  
 <p>(1) The divine self or God in man.</p>  
 <p>(2) Those perfected men in whom the divine self is fully active and supreme ruler.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Yogi</span>.</p>  
 <p>The name given to the mind concentrators of India, both high and low, but not necessarily a fakir.</p>    </body>
