 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">INTRODUCTION TO ASTROLOGY.</h1>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER I.</h3>  
 <p>THERE are in the heavens several bodies which appear to shed their light directly on this Earth; and also some others which, having no light of themselves, serve to reflect that of the Sun, and thereby become visible to our organs of sight. The former are termed <i>Fixed Stars</i>, because they appear to retain the same situation, or to be fixed in the same place; but the latter, being observed to wander, are termed Planets. The number and distance of the former are so extensive, that I shall take no further notice of them here, than to observe, that they are not much used in that portion of Astrology which is denominated <i>Horary</i>, and that those persons who desire to make use of them in nativities, will find their right ascensions and declinations given with great accuracy in the Nautical Almanack for each year. In the Appendix to this work, I shall give rules, to ascertain their latitude and longitude by, trigonometry, for the benefit of such persons as may be curious to make experiments as to their influence; though I do not, in general, pay much attention to them when judging a nativity.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Of the Planets</i>.</p>  
 <p>These are ♅ Herschel, ♄ Saturn, ♃ Jupiter, ♂ Mars.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> ☉ Sol, the Sun, <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="#fn_3"><font size="1">1</font></a> ♀ Venus, ☿ Mercury, and ☽ Luna, Moon. These characters have been always in use, and may, (with the exception of ♅) be traced to the remotest antiquity, and their origin found among the hieroglyphics of Egypt. But as the object of this work is practical utility, no more need be said on the subject.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>The Signs of the Zodiac</i>.</p>  
 <p>They are twelve, each containing 30 degrees, thus making 360 degrees, into which every great circle is divided. The first six are,</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Northern Signs</i>.</p>  
 <p>♈ Aries, ♉ Taurus, ♊ Gemini, ♋ Cancer, ♌ Leo, ♍ Virgo.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Southern Signs</i>.</p>  
 <p>♎ Libra, ♏ Scorpio, ♐ Sagittary, ♑ Capricorn, ♒ Aquarius, ♓ Pisces.</p>  
 <p>The first sign, ♈, commences the zodiac, its beginning being that spot in the heavens where the Sun is when crossing the equator in spring; and the latter sign, ♓, finishes the circle of the zodiac, the latter end of it being that spot in the heavens where the Sun is when he has gone his round, and is again about to enter ♈.</p>  
 <p>By referring to the annexed diagram, the student will perceive, that when the Sun enters ♈ (about the 21st of March) he proceeds northward, and <i>increases in declination</i> until he reaches the tropic of ♋ Cancer (about the 21st of June), when he speedily begins to return to the south; and when he reaches ♎, he again crosses the equator (about the 23d of September), where, having no declination, he causes equal</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>  
 <p>day and night all over the world. He then <i>declines away to the south</i>; shortening our days in the northern hemisphere, until he reaches the southern tropic ♑, Capricorn; at length he returns towards the equator, and crosses it by entering the sign ♈ (about the 21st of March), where again he has no declination, and gives equal days and nights.</p>

 <p>N.B. The moveable, common, and fixed signs are always in <i>square</i> aspect to each other, three signs apart; and the fiery, earthy, airy, and watery signs are always in <i>trine</i> aspect to each other, four signs apart.</p>  <h3 align="center">THE DRAGON'S HEAD AND TAIL</h3>  
 <p>The Moon's north node is known by the character ☊,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 17</font></a></p>  
 <p>termed the Dragon's Head; and her south node by this ☋, termed the Dragon's Tail. The former of these in horary questions denotes good, and is considered of the character of ♃. and increases the good qualities of a benefic, with which it may be found; and diminishes the evil of a malefic planet. The latter is of the nature of ♄, and does the reverse. In nativities these characters have no avail, and are not to be considered, except with regard to the Moon, who is found to produce good or evil when she reaches them by direction. <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE PART OF FORTUNE.</h3>  
 <p>This is that spot in the heavens which is equally distant from the degree ascending that the Moon is from the Sun. It is found by the following rule:--</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>To find the</i> (⊕) <i>Part of Fortune in a Nativity</i>.</p>  
 <p>Add 90° to the right ascension of the meridian, and it will give the oblique ascension of the ascendant. From the oblique ascension of the ascendant subtract the oblique ascension of the Sun (having first added 360° to the former, if necessary); to the remainder add the right ascension of the Moon: the sum will be the right ascension of ⊕.</p>  
 <p>The ⊕ is always <i>under</i> the horizon <i>before</i> the full Moon, and <i>above</i> the horizon <i>after</i> the full Moon. Having found its right ascension, take it from that of the meridian above or below the earth, according as it may be situated; or, take that of the meridian from it, and the sum or difference will shew the distance of ⊕ from the cusp of the 10th or 4th house.</p>
