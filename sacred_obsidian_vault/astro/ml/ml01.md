 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Moon Lore</i>, by Timothy Harley, [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> 
 <p>"I beheld the moon walking in brightness."--<i>Job</i> xxxi. 26.</p> 
 <p>"The moon and the stars, which Thou hast ordained."--<i>Psalm</i> viii. 3.</p> 
 <p>Who is she that looketh forth, fair as the moon?"--<i>Solomon's Song</i> vi. 10.</p> 
 <p>The precious things put forth by the moon."--<i>Deuteronomy</i> xxxiii. 14.</p> 
 <p>Soon as the evening shades prevail,<br> The moon takes up the wondrous tale."--Addison's <i>Ode</i>.</p> 
 <p>In fall-orbed glory, yonder moon Divine<br> Rolls through the dark-blue depths."--Southey's <i>Thalaba</i>.</p> 
 <p>"Queen of the silver bow! by thy pale beam,<br>   Alone and pensive, I delight to stray,<br> And watch thy shadow trembling in the stream,<br>   Or mark the floating clouds that cross thy way;<br> And while I gaze, thy mild and placid light<br>   Sheds a soft calm upon my troubled breast:<br> And oft I think-fair planet of the night--<br>   That in thy orb the wretched may have rest;<br> The sufferers of the earth perhaps may go--<br>   Released by death-to thy benignant sphere;<br> And the sad children of despair and woe<br>   Forget in thee their cup of sorrow here.<br> Oh that I soon may reach thy world serene,<br>   Poor wearied pilgrim in this toiling scene!"<br>                                            --<i>Charlotte Smith</i>.</p>
