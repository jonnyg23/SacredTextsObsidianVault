 <p>Written just eighty-four years before Neil Armstrong stepped on the
 moon, this is a Victorian collection of moon lore:
 myths, folklore, superstitions and just plain whimsy from all lands.
 Although contemporary astronomers had fairly well wrapped up the question of
 whether there was water and air on the moon,
 Harley still suspected that the moon was inhabited.
 However, there was still much that was unknown about the moon until
 the first probes were crash-landed on it (for instance, whether the
 surface was covered with vast, deep layers of dust).
 So we shouldn't feel too smug, even though we've played golf there.
 The universe will continue to surprise us.
 </p><hr>
