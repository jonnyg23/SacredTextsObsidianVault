 <p><a name="page_19"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 19</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER X</h3>  <h3 align="center">CONSTELLATIONS NORTH OF THE ZODIAC</h3>  
 <p>THE constellations north of the zodiac have their respective influences, analogous to those of the planets, existing in the mode described in the following list.</p>  
 <p><i>Ursa Minor</i>. The bright stars in this constellation are like Saturn, and in some degree like Venus.</p>  
 <p><i>Ursa Major</i> is like Mars, but the nebula under the tail resembles the Moon and Venus in its influence.</p>  
 <p><i>Draco</i>. The bright stars operate like Saturn and Mars.</p>  
 <p><i>Cepheus</i> is like Saturn and Jupiter.</p>  
 <p><i>Bootes</i> is like Mercury and Saturn; but the bright and ruddy star, called Arcturus, is like Mars and Jupiter.</p>  
 <p><i>Corona Borealis</i> is like Venus and Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Hercules</i> (or the Kneeler) is like Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Lyra</i> is like Venus and Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Cygnus</i> is like Venus and Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Cassiopeia</i> is like Saturn and Venus.</p>  
 <p><i>Perseus</i> is like Jupiter and Saturn: but the nebula, in the hilt of the sword, is like Mars and Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Auriga</i>. The bright stars are like Mars and Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Serpentarius</i> is like Saturn, and moderately like Venus.</p>  
 <p><i>Serpens</i> is like Saturn and Mars.</p>  
 <p><i>Sagitta</i> is like Saturn, and moderately like Venus.</p>  
 <p><i>Aquila</i> is like Mars and Jupiter.</p>  
 <p><i>Delphinus</i> is like Saturn and Mars.</p>  
 <p><i>Equus</i> (or Pegasus). The bright stars are like Mars and Mercury.</p>  
 <p><i>Andromeda</i> is like Venus.</p>  
 <p><i>Delta</i> (or the Triangle) is like Mercury.</p>
