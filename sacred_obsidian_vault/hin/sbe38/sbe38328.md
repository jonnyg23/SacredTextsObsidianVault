 <p>12. For this reason Bâdarâya<i>n</i>a (opines that the released person is) of both kinds; as in the case of the twelve days' sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>The teacher Bâdarâya<i>n</i>a, again, thinks that for this reason, i.e. because scripture contains indications of both kinds, the proper conclusion is that the released person exists in both</p>  
 <p><a name="page_413"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 413</font></a></p>  
 <p>conditions. When he wishes to have a body, he appears with one; when he wishes to be disembodied, he is without one. For he has various wishes, and all his wishes are realised.--'As in the case of the twelve days' sacrifice.' As the soma sacrifice extending over twelve days may be viewed either as a sattra or as an ahîna sacrifice, because both alternatives are indicated by scriptural passages <a name="fr_234"></a><a href="sbe38331.htm#fn_234"><font size="1">1</font></a>; so it is here also.</p>
