 <p>7. And on account of definite rules.</p>  
 <p>'Performing works here (i.e. in this life) let a man wish to live a hundred years; thus work will not cling to thee, man; there is no other way than that' (Î<i>s</i>a. Up. 2); 'The</p>  
 <p><a name="page_290"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 290</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Agnihotra is a sattra lasting up to old age and death; for through old age one is freed from it or through death' (<i>S</i>at. Brâ. XII, 4, 1, 1); from such definite rules also it follows that knowledge is merely supplementary to works.</p>  
 <p>Against all these objections the Sûtrakâra upholds his view in the following Sûtra.</p>
