 <p>10. (The direct statement is) non-comprehensive.</p>  
 <p>The direct scriptural statement implied in 'What a man does with knowledge' &amp;c. does not refer to all knowledge, as it is connected with the knowledge forming the subject-matter of the section. And the latter is the knowledge of the udgîtha only, 'Let a man meditate on the syllable Om (as) the udgîtha.'</p>
