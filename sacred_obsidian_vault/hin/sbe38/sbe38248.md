 <p>39. Better than this is the other (state of belonging to an â<i>s</i>rama), on account of the indicatory marks.</p>  
 <p>'Than this,' i.e. 'than standing between,' a better means of knowledge it is to stand within one of the â<i>s</i>ramas, since this is confirmed by <i>S</i>ruti and Sm<i>ri</i>ti. For scripture supplies an indicatory mark in the passage, 'On that path goes whoever knows Brahman and who has done holy</p>  
 <p><a name="page_317"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 317</font></a></p>  
 <p>works (as prescribed for the â<i>s</i>ramas) and obtained splendour' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. IV, 4, 9); and Sm<i>ri</i>ti in the passage, 'Let a Brâhma<i>n</i>a stay not one day even outside the â<i>s</i>rama; having stayed outside for a year he goes to utter ruin.'</p>
