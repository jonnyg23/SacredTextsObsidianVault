 <p>39. And on account of the impossibility of meditation (samâdhi).</p>  
 <p>Moreover the meditation taught in the Vedânta-texts, whose aim is the realisation of the Self as represented by the Upanishads, is possible only if the Self is the agent <a name="fr_34"></a><a href="#fn_34"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Compare the following passages, 'Verily, the Self is to be seen, to be heard, to be perceived, to be marked' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. II, 4, 5); 'The Self we must seek out, we must try to understand' (<i>Kh</i>. Up. VIII, 7, 1); 'Meditate on the Self as Om' (Mu. Up. II, 2, 6).--Therefrom also it follows that the Self is an agent.</p>
