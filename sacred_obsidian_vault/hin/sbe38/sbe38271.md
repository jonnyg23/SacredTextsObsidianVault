 <p>10. And Sm<i>ri</i>ti-passages say the same.</p>  
 <p>Authoritative authors also teach in their Sm<i>ri</i>tis that a sitting posture subserves the act of meditation: cp. e.g. Bha. Gîtâ VI. 11,--Having made a firm seat for one's self on a pure spot.' For the same reason the Yoga<i>s</i>âstra teaches different sitting posture, viz. the so-called lotus position and so on.</p>
