 <p>18. On account of the scriptural statement of difference.</p>  
 <p>The prâ<i>n</i>a is everywhere spoken of as different from speech, &amp;c. The passage, e.g. beginning with 'They said to speech' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. I, 3, 2), enumerates speech, &amp;c., which were overwhelmed by the evil of the Asuras, concludes thereupon the section treating of speech, &amp;c., and then specially mentions the mukhya prâ<i>n</i>a as overcoming the Asuras, in the paragraph beginning 'Then they said to the breath in the mouth.'--Other passages also referring to that difference may be quoted, so, for instance, 'He made mind, speech, and breath for himself (B<i>ri</i>. Up. I, 5, 3).--For this reason also the other prâ<i>n</i>as are different in being from the chief prâ<i>n</i>a--Another reason follows.</p>
