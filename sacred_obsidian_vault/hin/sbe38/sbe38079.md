 <p>3. And on account of the going of the prâ<i>n</i>as.</p>  
 <p>Scripture states that, when a new body is obtained, the prâ<i>n</i>as also go (from the old body to the new one). Cp. 'When he thus departs the (chief) prâ<i>n</i>a departs after him, and when the prâ<i>n</i>a thus departs all the other prâ<i>n</i>as depart after it' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. IV, 4, 2), and similar passages. Now this going of the prâ<i>n</i>as is not possible without a base; hence we infer that water also--mixed with parts of the other elements--goes (from the old body to the new one), serving the purpose of supplying a base for the moving prâ<i>n</i>as. For the prâ<i>n</i>as cannot, without such a base, either move or abide anywhere; as we observe in living beings.</p>
