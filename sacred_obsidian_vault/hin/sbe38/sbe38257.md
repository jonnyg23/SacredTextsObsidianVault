 <p>48. On account of his being all, however, there is winding up with the householder.</p>  
 <p>The word 'however' is meant to lay stress on the householder's being everything. For the performance of many works belonging to his own â<i>s</i>rama, such as sacrifices and the like, which involve not a little trouble, is enjoined on him by scripture; and at the same time the duties of the other â<i>s</i>ramas--such as tenderness for all living creatures, restraint of the senses and so on--are incumbent on him also as far as circumstances allow. There is therefore nothing contradictory</p>  
 <p><a name="page_325"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 325</font></a></p>  
 <p>in the <i>Kh</i>ândogya winding up with the householder.</p>
