 <p>7. On account of definite rule.</p>
 <p>Another argument for our conclusion is that the text 'Doing works here let a man desire to live a hundred years,' &amp;c. (Is. Up. II), expressly enjoins lifelong works on him who knows the Self. The general conclusion, therefore, is that knowledge (meditation) is merely auxiliary to works. Of this view the next Sûtra finally disposes.</p>
