 <p>4. And on account of there being no statement of its being an object of knowledge.</p>
 <p>If the text meant the Non-evolved as understood by the Sâ<u>n</u>khyas it would refer to it as something to be known; for the Sâ<u>n</u>khyas, who hold the theory of Release resulting from the discriminative knowledge of the Evolved, the</p>
 <p><a name="page_360"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 360</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Non-evolved, and the soul, admit that all these are objects of knowledge. Now our text does not refer to the Un-evolved as an object of knowledge, and it cannot therefore be the Pradhâna assumed by the Sâ<u>n</u>khyas.</p>
