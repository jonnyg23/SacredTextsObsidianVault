 <p>15. And on account of the term 'Self.'</p>
 <p>That this is so further follows from the fact that in the clause 'different from this is the inner Self consisting of bliss' the term 'Self is used. For as the Self cannot really possess a head, wings, and tail, its having joy for its head, and so on, can only be meant in a metaphorical sense, for the sake of easier comprehension.--But, in the preceding sections, the term <i>Self</i> had been applied to what is <i>not</i> of the nature of Self--the text speaking of the Self of breath, the Self of mind, and so on; how then are we able to determine that in the phrase 'the Self of bliss' the term</p>
 <p><a name="page_640"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 640</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Self denotes a true <i>Self</i>?--To this the next Sûtra replies.</p>
