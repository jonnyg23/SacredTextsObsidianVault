 <p>42. A minor one, thus some; (and hence they hold) the existence (of expiation), as in the case of eating. This has been explained.</p>
 <p>Some teachers are of opinion that even on the part of Naish<i>th</i>ikas and the rest the lapse from chastity constitutes only a minor offence which can be atoned for by expiatory observances; in the same way as in the case of the eating of forbidden food the same prâya<i>sk</i>itta may be used by the ordinary Brahma<i>k</i>ârin and by Naish<i>th</i>ikas and the rest. This has been stated by the Sm<i>ri</i>ti writer, 'For the others also (i.e. the Naish<i>th</i>ikas and so on) the same (rules and</p>
 <p><a name="page_707"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 707</font></a></p>
 <p>practices as those for the Upakurvâna) hold good, in so far as not opposed to their â<i>s</i>rama.'</p>
