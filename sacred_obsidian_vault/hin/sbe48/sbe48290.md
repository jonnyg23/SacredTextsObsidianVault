 <p>44. Moreover it is so stated in Sm<i>ri</i>ti.</p>
 <p>Sm<i>ri</i>ti moreover declares the individual soul to be a part of the highest Person, 'An eternal part of myself becomes</p>
 <p><a name="page_563"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 563</font></a></p>
 <p>the individual soul (<i>g</i>îva) in the world of life' (Bha. Gî. XV, 7). For this reason also the soul must be held to be a part of Brahman.</p>
 <p>But if the soul is a part of Brahman, all the imperfections of the soul are Brahman's also! To this objection the next Sûtra replies.</p>
