 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:4">13:1:4</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:4:1">13:1:4:1</a></font></span>1. Pra<i>g</i>âpati poured forth the life-sap of the horse (a<i>s</i>va-medha) <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">1</font></a>. When poured forth, it went straight away from him and spread itself over the regions. The gods went in quest of it. By means of offerings (ish<i>t</i>i) they followed it up, by offerings they searched for it, and by offerings they found it. And when he performs ish<i>t</i>is, the Sacrificer thereby searches for the horse (a<i>s</i>va) meet for sacrifice <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">2</font></a> (medhya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:4:2">13:1:4:2</a></font></span>2. They (the ish<i>t</i>is <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">3</font></a>) belong to Savit<i>ri</i>; for Savit<i>ri</i> is this (earth): if any one hides himself thereon, if any one goes elsewhere <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">4</font></a>, it is on this</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 285</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (earth) that they find him; for no one (creature), whether walking erect or horizontally (like an animal), is able to go beyond it. Their belonging to Savit<i>ri</i> thus is in order to find the horse.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:4:3">13:1:4:3</a></font></span>3. Concerning this they say, 'Surely the horse disappears when it goes straight away; for they do not turn (drive) it back <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' Now when he performs the Dh<i>ri</i>ti offerings <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">2</font></a> in the evening--dh<i>ri</i>ti (keeping) meaning peaceful dwelling, and the night also meaning peaceful dwelling--it is by means of peaceful dwelling that he keeps it; whence both men and beasts rest peacefully at night. And when he performs offerings in the morning, he seeks that (horse); whence it is in daytime that one goes to seek for what is lost. And again when he offers the Dh<i>ri</i>tis in the evening, and the (Savit<i>ri</i>) ish<i>t</i>is in the morning, it is security of possession the Sacrificer thereby brings about, whence security of possession is brought about for the subjects where this sacrifice is performed.</p>
