 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8">11:5:8</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">EIGHTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:1">11:5:8:1</a></font></span>1. Verily, in the beginning, Pra<i>g</i>âpati alone was here. He desired, 'May I exist, may I be generated.' He wearied himself and performed fervid devotions: from him, thus wearied and heated, the three worlds were created--the earth, the air, and the sky.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:2">11:5:8:2</a></font></span>2. He heated these three worlds, and from them, thus heated, three lights (<i>g</i>yotis) were produced--Agni (the fire), he who blows here (Vâyu), and Sûrya (the sun).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:3">11:5:8:3</a></font></span>3. He heated these three lights, and from them, thus heated, the three Vedas were produced--the <i>Ri</i>g-veda from Agni, the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda from Vâyu, and the Sâma-veda from Sûrya.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:4">11:5:8:4</a></font></span>4. He heated these three Vedas, and from them, thus heated, three luminous essences <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">1</font></a> were produced--</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'bhû<i>h</i>' from the <i>Ri</i>g-veda, 'bhuva<i>h</i>' from the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, and 'svar' from the Sâma-veda. And with the <i>Ri</i>g-veda they then performed the work of the Hot<i>ri</i> priest, with the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda the work of the Adhvaryu, and with the Sâma-veda the work of the Udgât<i>ri</i>; and what luminous essence <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">1</font></a> there was in the threefold science, therewith the work of the Brahman priest then proceeded.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:5">11:5:8:5</a></font></span>5. The gods spake unto Pra<i>g</i>âpati, 'If our sacrifice were to fail in respect of either the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>, or the Ya<i>g</i>us, or the Sâman, whereby should we heal it?'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:6">11:5:8:6</a></font></span>6. He spake, ‘If (it were to fail) in respect of the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>, ye should take ghee by four ladlings and offer it in the Gârhapatya fire with 'Bhû<i>h</i>!' and if in respect of the Ya<i>g</i>us, ye should take ghee by four ladlings and offer it in the Âgnîdhrîya--or in the Anvâhâryapa<i>k</i>ana <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">2</font></a> in the case of a Havirya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a--with 'Bhuva<i>h</i>!' and if in respect of the Sâman, ye should take ghee by four ladlings and offer it in the Âhavanîya with 'Svar!' But if it should not be known (where the mistake has occurred), ye should make offering in the Âhavanîya after uttering</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 104</font></a></p>  
 <p>rapidly all (the three sacred words <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">1</font></a>): thus one heals the <i>Ri</i>g-veda by the <i>Ri</i>g-veda <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda by the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, and the Sâma-veda by the Sâma-veda;--even as one would put together joint with joint <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">3</font></a>, so does he put together (the broken part of the sacrifice) whoever heals it by means of these (three sacred words). But if he heals it in any other way than this, it would be just as if one tried to put together something that is broken with something else that is broken, or as if one were to apply some poison as lotion to a broken part <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Let him therefore appoint only one who knows this (to officiate as) his Brahman, and not one who does not know this.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:8:7">11:5:8:7</a></font></span>7. As to this they say, 'Seeing that the work of the Hot<i>ri</i> is performed with the <i>Ri</i>g-veda, that of the Adhvaryu with the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, and that of the Udgât<i>ri</i> with the Sâma-veda, wherewith then is the work of the Brahman (performed)?' Let him reply, 'With that threefold science.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_330"></a><a href="sbe44032.htm#fr_330">102:1</a> ? Sâya<i>n</i>a takes '<i>s</i>ukra' here in the sense of 'flame, light' (vyâh<i>ri</i>tirûpâ<i>n</i>i te<i>g</i>â<i>m</i>si); whilst the St. Petersb. Dict. assigns to it the meaning of 'sap, juice' (Saft, Seim, cf. next note). Ait. Br. V, 32, contains a very similar passage in which the same process of evolution is set forth:--Pra<i>g</i>âpati first creates the three worlds, earth, air, and heaven. From them, being heated by him, three lights (<i>g</i>yotis) are produced--Agni from the earth, Vâyu from the air, and Âditya from the sky (or heaven). From them, being heated, the three Vedas are produced--the <i>Ri</i>g-veda from Agni, the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda from Vâyu, and the Sâma-veda from Âditya. From the Vedas, being heated, three flames (<i>s</i>ukra, luminaries, Haug) are produced--Bhû<i>h</i> from the <i>Ri</i>g-veda, Bhuva<i>h</i> from the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, <a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a> and Svar from the Sâma-veda. From these in the same way are produced three sounds (or letters, var<i>n</i>a), â, u and m, which being combined yield the syllable 'Om.' Cp. J. Muir, Original Sanskrit Texts, vol. iii, p. 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_331"></a><a href="sbe44032.htm#fr_331">103:1</a> Here Sâya<i>n</i>a also seems to take '<i>s</i>ukra' in the sense of 'pure, essential part'--nirmala<i>m</i> rûpa<i>m</i> sâratvatâ<i>m</i><i>s</i>a<i>h</i> (!).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_332"></a><a href="sbe44032.htm#fr_332">103:2</a> That is, the Dakshi<i>n</i>âgni. See the plan in part ii, p. 475.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_333"></a><a href="sbe44032.htm#fr_333">104:1</a> According to Sâya<i>n</i>a, offering would be used with the formula 'Bhûr bhuva<i>h</i> sva<i>h</i>, svâhâ!'</p>
