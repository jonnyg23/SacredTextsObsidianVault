 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:1">13:1:1</a></font></span></p>  <h1 align="center">THIRTEENTH KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h1>  <h4 align="center">THE A<i>S</i>VAMEDHA, OR HORSE-SACRIFICE.</h4>  <h4 align="center">PRELIMINARY CEREMONIES <a name="fr_680"></a><a href="#fn_676"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h4>  <h3 align="center">FIRST ADHYÂYA. FIRST BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:1:1">13:1:1:1</a></font></span>1. He (the Adhvaryu) cooks the priests’ mess of rice <a name="fr_681"></a><a href="#fn_677"><font size="1">2</font></a>: it is seed he thereby produces. Having greased a rope with the ghee which is left over <a name="fr_682"></a><a href="#fn_678"><font size="1">3</font></a>, he takes it; for ghee is (a type of) fiery spirit, and the horse is sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati <a name="fr_683"></a><a href="#fn_679"><font size="1">4</font></a>: he thus endows Pra<i>g</i>âpati with fiery spirit. Impure, and unfit for sacrifice, indeed, is that (animal), to wit, the horse.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:1:2">13:1:1:2</a></font></span>2. The rope consists of darbha grass (poa cynosuroides);--for darbha stalks <a name="fr_684"></a><a href="#fn_680"><font size="1">5</font></a> are a means of purification: he thus purifies that (horse), and immolates it as one purified and meet for sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:1:3">13:1:1:3</a></font></span>3. Now, when the horse <a name="fr_685"></a><a href="#fn_681"><font size="1">6</font></a> was immolated, its</p>

 </p>

 <p>seed went from it and became gold <a name="fr_686"></a><a href="#fn_682"><font size="1">1</font></a>: thus, when he gives gold (to the priests) he supplies the horse with seed.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:1:4">13:1:1:4</a></font></span>4. Pra<i>g</i>âpati produced the sacrifice <a name="fr_687"></a><a href="#fn_683"><font size="1">2</font></a>. His greatness departed from him, and entered the great sacrificial priests <a name="fr_688"></a><a href="#fn_684"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Together with the great priests he went in search of it, and together with the great priests he found it: when the great priests eat the priests’ mess of rice, the Sacrificer thereby secures for himself the greatness of the sacrifice. Along with the priests’ mess of rice he presents gold (to the priests); for the mess of rice is seed, and gold is seed: by means of seed he thus lays seed into that (horse, and Sacrificer). It (the gold <a name="fr_689"></a><a href="#fn_685"><font size="1">4</font></a>) weighs a hundred (grains); for man has a life of a hundred (years), and a hundred energies: it is life, and energy, vigour, he lays into his own self. At midday he takes Vasatîvarî <a name="fr_690"></a><a href="#fn_686"><font size="1">5</font></a> water of four kinds; it is brought together from the (four) quarters, for food is in (all) the (four) quarters, and water is food: by means of food he thus secures food for him.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_676"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_680">274:1</a> The ceremonies treated of in the first six chapters (brâhma<i>n</i>as) refer to the setting apart of the horse for its sacred office, a year before the sacrifice, and to the intervening period during which the horse is allowed to roam about, though under careful supervision.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_677"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_681">274:2</a> For further particulars regarding this opening ceremony of the sacrifice see <a href="sbe44102.htm#13:4:1:1">XIII, 4, 1, 1</a> seqq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_678"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_682">274:3</a> Viz. from the ghee used for greasing the four dishes of cooked rice.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_679"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_683">274:4</a> Or, the horse is of the nature of Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_680"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_684">274:5</a> See above, <a href="sbe44061.htm#page_195">p. 195</a>, note <a href="sbe44061.htm#fn_532">1</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_681"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_685">274:6</a> That is, as would seem, Pra<i>g</i>âpati in the form of a horse, see part iv, introd., p. xiv seqq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_682"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_686">275:1</a> Pra<i>g</i>âpati is Agni, and gold is Agni's seed, cf. II, 1, 1, 5; III, 3, 1, 3 &amp;c.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_683"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_687">275:2</a> That is, the A<i>s</i>vamedha sacrifice, and thus the immolation (or emptying out) of his own self, so to speak.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_684"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_688">275:3</a> That is, the four principal officiating priests, Brahman, Hot<i>ri</i>, Adhvaryu, and Udgât<i>ri</i>. Cf. VIII, 4, 3, 1 seqq., where it is the vital airs that, in their capacity as <i>Ri</i>shis, assist Pra<i>g</i>âpati in the first sacrifice.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_685"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_689">275:4</a> That is to say, each piece of gold weighs as much. According to Kâty. XX, 1, 6 he is to give to the priests 4000 cows and as many <i>S</i>atamâna coins.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_686"></a><a href="sbe44074.htm#fr_690">275:5</a> For this water used for the Soma-sacrifice where, however, it is taken from a cistern, or some course of flowing water, see part ii, p. 222 seqq.</p>
