 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8">13:1:8</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">EIGHTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:1">13:1:8:1</a></font></span>1. Pra<i>g</i>âpati poured forth the life-sap of the horse <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">2</font></a>. When poured forth, it weighed down the <i>ri</i><i>k</i> (hymn-verse) and the sâman (hymn-tune). The Vai<i>s</i>vadeva (offerings) upheld that (A<i>s</i>vamedha): thus, when he offers the Vai<i>s</i>vadevas, it is for the upholding of the A<i>s</i>vamedha.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:2">13:1:8:2</a></font></span>2. With (Vâ<i>g</i>. S. XXII, 20), 'To Ka hail! To the Who hail! To the Whoever hail!' he makes the one relating to Pra<i>g</i>âpati the first (or chief one), and thus upholds (the A<i>s</i>vamedha) by means of the deities with Pra<i>g</i>âpati as their chief.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:3">13:1:8:3</a></font></span>3. 'Hail, meditation (we give) unto him meditated upon! Hail, the mind unto the Lord of creatures! Hail, thought unto him, the known <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">3</font></a>!' what the mystic sense of the former (utterances <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">4</font></a>) was that it is here.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 293</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:4">13:1:8:4</a></font></span>4. 'To Aditi hail! To Aditi, the mighty, hail! To Aditi, the most merciful, hail!' Aditi, doubtless, is this (earth): it is by her that he upholds it.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:5">13:1:8:5</a></font></span>5. 'To Sarasvatî hail! To Sarasvatî, the pure, hail! To Sarasvatî, the great, hail!' Sarasvatî, doubtless, is speech: by speech he thus upholds it.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:6">13:1:8:6</a></font></span>6. 'To Pûshan hail! To Pûshan, the protector of travellers, hail! To Pûshan, the watcher of men, hail!' Pûshan, doubtless, is cattle: by means of cattle he thus upholds it.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:7">13:1:8:7</a></font></span>7. 'To Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> hail! To Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, the seminal, hail! To Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, the multiform hail!' Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, doubtless, is the fashioner of the couples of animals: by means of forms he thus upholds it.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:1:8:8">13:1:8:8</a></font></span>8. 'To Vish<i>n</i>u hail! To Vish<i>n</i>u, the protector of what grows <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">1</font></a>, hail! To Vish<i>n</i>u, the bald <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">2</font></a>, hail!' Vish<i>n</i>u, doubtless, is the sacrifice: by sacrifice he thus upholds it. With (Vâ<i>g</i>. XXII, 21), 'Let every mortal espouse the friendship of the divine guide, . . . <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">3</font></a>,' he offers last of all a full (-spoon)-oblation; for the full-offering is this (earth): he thus finally establishes himself on this (earth).</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_733"></a><a href="sbe44081.htm#fr_737">292:2</a> See <a href="sbe44079.htm#page_289">p. 289</a>, note <a href="sbe44080.htm#fn_726">1</a>. It is here taken to be represented by the Ya<i>g</i>us:--a<i>s</i>vamedha<i>m</i> ya<i>g</i>urâtmakavigrahavanta<i>m</i> s<i>ri</i>sh<i>t</i>avân, Harisv.--the larger number of sacrificial formulas used at the performances being too heavy for the recited and chanted texts.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_734"></a><a href="sbe44081.htm#fr_738">292:3</a> Mahîdhara takes 'âdhim âdhîtâya' in the sense of 'âdhâna<i>m</i> prâptâya' (who has obtained a consecrated fire); and 'mana<i>h</i> pra<i>g</i>âtaye' in the sense of 'manasi vartamânâya p.' (to P. who is in our mind); and '<i>k</i>itta<i>m</i> vi<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âtâya' in the sense of 'sarveshâ<i>m</i> <i>k</i>ittasâkshi<i>n</i>e' (to the witness, or knower, of all men's thoughts).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_735"></a><a href="sbe44081.htm#fr_739">292:4</a> Harisvâmin probably is right in supplying 'vyâh<i>ri</i>tînâm;' though possibly 'devatânâm' (deities) may be understood.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_736"></a><a href="sbe44081.htm#fr_740">293:1</a> The meaning of 'nibhûyapa' is doubtful; Mahîdhara explains it by 'nitarâm bhûtvâ matsyâdyavatâra<i>m</i> k<i>ri</i>tvâ pâti.' Perhaps it may mean 'condescending protector,' though one expects a direct object with 'pa.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_737"></a><a href="sbe44081.htm#fr_741">293:2</a> The word '<i>s</i>ipivish<i>t</i>a,' as applied to Vish<i>n</i>u, is likewise of doubtful meaning. The native dictionaries assign both the meaning 'bald' and 'leprous' (or, affected with skin-disease) to it; whilst the first part '<i>s</i>ipi' is taken variously by commentators as meaning 'cattle,' or 'ray,' or 'water,' or 'living being.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_738"></a><a href="sbe44081.htm#fr_742">293:3</a> See III, 1, 4, 18; VI, 6, 1, 21; and <a href="sbe44082.htm#page_294">p. 294</a>, note <a href="sbe44082.htm#fn_739">1</a>.</p>
