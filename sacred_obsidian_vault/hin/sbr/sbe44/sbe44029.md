 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5">11:5:5</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">FIFTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  <h4 align="center">THE <i>S</i>ATÂTIRÂ<i>T</i>RAM, OR SACRIFICIAL SESSION OF A HUNDRED ATIRÂTRA-SACRIFICES.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:1">11:5:5:1</a></font></span>1. Now, when the gods were passing upwards to the world of heaven, the Asuras enveloped them in darkness. They spake, 'Verily, by nothing else save a sacrificial session is there any way of dispelling this (darkness): well, then, let us perform a sacrificial session!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:2">11:5:5:2</a></font></span>2. They entered upon a sacrificial session of a hundred Agnish<i>t</i>oma (days), and dispelled the darkness as far as one may see whilst sitting; and in like manner did they, by (a session of) a hundred Ukthya (days), dispel the darkness as far as one may see whilst standing.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:3">11:5:5:3</a></font></span>3. They spake, 'We do indeed dispel the darkness, but not the whole of it: come, let us resort to Father Pra<i>g</i>âpati.' Having come to Father Pra<i>g</i>âpati, they spake, 'Reverend sir, when we were passing upwards to the world of heaven the Asuras enveloped us in darkness.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:4">11:5:5:4</a></font></span>4. 'We entered upon a sacrificial session of a hundred Agnish<i>t</i>omas, and dispelled the darkness as far as one may see whilst sitting; and in like manner did we dispel the darkness as far as one may see whilst standing: do thou teach us, reverend sir, how, by dispelling the Asuras and darkness, and all evil, we shall find (the way to) the world of heaven!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:5">11:5:5:5</a></font></span>5. He spake, 'Surely, ye proceeded by means of two sacrifices, the Agnish<i>t</i>oma and Ukthya, which do not contain all Soma-rites <a name="fr_294"></a><a href="#fn_294"><font size="1">1</font></a>;--enter ye upon</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 92</font></a></p>  
 <p>a sacrificial session of a hundred Atirâtras: when ye have thereby repelled the Asuras and darkness, and all evil, ye shall find the world of heaven.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:6">11:5:5:6</a></font></span>6. They entered upon a sacrificial session of a hundred Atirâtras; and, having thereby repelled the Asuras and darkness, and all evil, they found (the way to) the world of heaven. In their first fifty days <a name="fr_295"></a><a href="#fn_295"><font size="1">1</font></a> the night-hymns reached into the day, and the day-hymns into the night.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:7">11:5:5:7</a></font></span>7. They spake, 'Verily, we have got into confusion and know not what to do: come, let us resort to Father Pra<i>g</i>âpati!' Having come to Father Pra<i>g</i>âpati, they spake (the verses), 'Our night-hymns are (chanted) in daytime, and those of the day at night: O sage, being learned and wise, teach thou us who are ignorant (how to perform) the sacrifices!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:8">11:5:5:8</a></font></span>8. He then recited to them as follows, 'A stronger, pursuing, has, as it were, driven a great snake from its own place, the lake: therefore the sacrificial session is not carried through.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:9">11:5:5:9</a></font></span>9. 'For your Â<i>s</i>vina (<i>s</i>astra), being recited, has indeed driven the morning-litany from its place <a name="fr_296"></a><a href="#fn_296"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'--</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'What ye, being wise, have unwise-like driven from its place, take ye up that gently through the Pra<i>s</i>âst<i>ri</i>, reciting so as not to disturb <a name="fr_297"></a><a href="#fn_297"><font size="1">1</font></a> (the Hot<i>ri</i>).'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:10">11:5:5:10</a></font></span>10. They spake, 'How, then, reverend sir, is (the Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra properly) recited and how is the recitation not disturbed?' He spake, 'When the Hot<i>ri</i>, in reciting the Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra, reaches the end of the Gâyatra metre of the Âgneya-kratu <a name="fr_298"></a><a href="#fn_298"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the Pratiprasthât<i>ri</i> <a name="fr_299"></a><a href="#fn_299"><font size="1">3</font></a> should carry round the Vasatîvarî water <a name="fr_300"></a><a href="#fn_300"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and bespeak the Prâtar-anuvâka for the Maitrâvaru<i>n</i>a (seated) between the two Havirdhâna (carts containing the offering-material). The Hot<i>ri</i> recites (the Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra) in a loud voice, and the other (the Maitrâvaru<i>n</i>a) repeats (the morning-litany) in a low voice, only just muttering it: in this</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 94</font></a></p>  
 <p>way he does not run counter to (the Hot<i>ri</i>'s) speech by (his own) speech, nor metre by metre.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:11">11:5:5:11</a></font></span>11. 'When the Prâtar-anuvâka has been completed, he (the Pratiprasthât<i>ri</i>), having offered, at their proper time <a name="fr_301"></a><a href="#fn_301"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the Upâ<i>m</i><i>s</i>u and Antaryâma cups <a name="fr_302"></a><a href="#fn_302"><font size="1">2</font></a>, presses out the straining-cloth and puts it in the Dro<i>n</i>akala<i>s</i>a <a name="fr_303"></a><a href="#fn_303"><font size="1">3</font></a>. And when ye have performed the (offering of the cups of) fermented Soma <a name="fr_304"></a><a href="#fn_304"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and returned (to the Sadas), ye should drink the fermented Soma (remaining in those cups). Having then, in the proper form, completed the "tail of the sacrifice," and taken up the cups of Soma (drawn) subsequent to the Antaryâma <a name="fr_305"></a><a href="#fn_305"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and offered the oblation of drops <a name="fr_306"></a><a href="#fn_306"><font size="1">6</font></a>, as well as the Santani-oblation <a name="fr_307"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">7</font></a>, ye should perform the Bahishpavamâna chant, and enter upon the day (-performance).'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:12">11:5:5:12</a></font></span>12. Concerning this there are these verses:--With four harnessed Saindhava (steeds) the sages left behind them the gloom--the wise gods who spun out the session of a hundred sacrifices.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:5:13">11:5:5:13</a></font></span>13. In this (sacrificial session) there are, indeed, four harnessed (steeds),--to wit, two Hot<i>ri</i>s and two Adhvaryus.--'Like unto the artificer contriving spikes to the spear, the sages coupled the ends of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 95</font></a></p>  
 <p>two days: now the Dânavas, we know <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">1</font></a>, will not disorder the sacrificial thread of them stretched out by us.--They leave undone the work of the previous day, and carry it through on the following day,--difficult to be understood. is the wisdom of the deities: streams of Soma flow, interlinked with streams of Soma!--Even as they constantly sprinkle the equal prize-winning <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">2</font></a> steeds, so (they pour out) the cups full of fiery liquor in the palace of <i>G</i>aname<i>g</i>aya.' Then the Asura-Rakshas went away.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_294"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_294">91:1</a> Viz. neither the Sho<i>d</i>a<i>s</i>in which, to (the twelve stotras, and <a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 92</font></a><i>s</i>astras of the Agnish<i>t</i>oma, and) the fifteen chants, of the Ukthya, adds a sixteenth; and the Atirâtra which has thirteen additional chants (and recitations), viz. three nocturnal rounds of four chants each, and one twilight-chant, followed by the Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra, recited by the Hot<i>ri</i>. No account is here taken of either the Atyagnish<i>t</i>oma of thirteen chants, or the Aptoryâma, which, to those of the Atirâtra, adds four more chants. Cf. part ii, p. 397, note 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_295"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_295">92:1</a> Or, perhaps, rather, in their days prior to the fiftieth (arvâkpâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>â<i>s</i>eshv aha<i>h</i>su), St. Petersb. Dict.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_296"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_296">92:2</a> The Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra, with the recitation of which, by the Hot<i>ri</i>, the Atirâtra concludes, takes the place, and is, indeed, <a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a> merely a modification, of the Prâtar-anuvâka, or morning-litany (see part ii, p. 229, note 2), by which an ordinary Soma-sacrifice is ushered in. Like it, its chief portion consists of three sections, termed kratu, of hymns and detached verses addressed to the 'early-coming' deities, Agni, Ushas and the two A<i>s</i>vins. The whole is to consist of not less than a thousand B<i>ri</i>hatîs, that is to say, the whole matter is to amount to at least 36,000 syllables. For a full account of this <i>S</i>astra, see Haug's Transl. of Ait. Br., p. 268.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_297"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_297">93:1</a> Whilst the Hot<i>ri</i> is reciting the Â<i>s</i>vina-sastra, his first assistant, the Pra<i>s</i>âst<i>ri</i> (or, as he is more commonly called, the Maitrâvaru<i>n</i>a), is to repeat the Prâtar-anuvâka in a low voice.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_298"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_298">93:2</a> The hymns and detached verses of each of the three sections--the Âgneya-, Ushasya- and Â<i>s</i>vina-kratu--of the Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra (as of the Prâtar-anuvâka) are arranged according to the seven principal metres--gâyatrî, anush<i>t</i>ubh, trish<i>t</i>ubh, b<i>ri</i>hatî, ush<i>n</i>ih, <i>g</i>agatî, and paṅkti--forming as many subdivisions of the three sections.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_299"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_299">93:3</a> That is, the first assistant of the Adhvaryu priest; the latter having to respond (pratigara) to the Hot<i>ri</i>'s calls (see part ii, p. 326, note 1) at the beginning and end of the <i>S</i>astra, and to sit through the recitations (III, 9, 3, 11).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_300"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_300">93:4</a> See III, 9, 2, 13 seqq.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_304"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_304">94:4</a> That is, having, after the completion of the Â<i>s</i>vina-<i>s</i>astra, offered to the A<i>s</i>vins some of the Soma that has been standing 'over the previous day.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_309"></a><a href="sbe44029.htm#fr_309">95:2</a> Kâsh<i>th</i>abh<i>ri</i>ta<i>h</i>, â<i>g</i>yantâ (!) kâsh<i>th</i>âni tâni bihhratîti kâsh<i>th</i>abh<i>ri</i>ta<i>h</i> svâdasa<i>m</i> (? <i>kh</i>ândasa<i>m</i>) pûrvapadasya hrasvatvam, â<i>g</i>idhâvana<i>m</i> k<i>ri</i>tavato hayân a<i>s</i>vân, Sây. According to this authority the general meaning of the verse is that even as the (king's) horses, when they have performed their task, have sweet drinks poured out on (? to) them, and thus obtain their hearts’ desire, so the gods, by performing a sacrificial session of a hundred Atirâtras, in accordance with Pra<i>g</i>âpati's directions, dispel the darkness and gain the world of heaven.</p>
