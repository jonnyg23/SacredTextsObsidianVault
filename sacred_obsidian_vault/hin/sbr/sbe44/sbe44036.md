 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3">11:6:3</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">THIRD BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:1">11:6:3:1</a></font></span>1. <i>G</i>anaka of Videha performed a sacrifice accompanied with numerous gifts to the priests. Setting apart a thousand cows, he said, 'He who is the most learned in sacred writ amongst you, O Brâhma<i>n</i>as, shall drive away these (cows) <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">1</font></a>!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:2">11:6:3:2</a></font></span>2. Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya then said, 'This way (drive) them!' They said, 'Art thou really the most learned in sacred writ amongst us, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?' He replied, 'Reverence be to him who is most learned in sacred writ! We are but hankering after cows <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">2</font></a>!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:3">11:6:3:3</a></font></span>3. They then said (to one another), 'Which of us shall question him?' The shrewd <i>S</i>âkalya said, 'I!' When he (Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya) saw him, he said, 'Have the Brâhma<i>n</i>as made of thee a thing for quenching the firebrand, <i>S</i>âkalya?'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:4">11:6:3:4</a></font></span>4. He said <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">3</font></a>, 'How many gods are there, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?'--'Three hundred and three, and three thousand and three,' he replied.--'Yea, so it is!' he said. 'How many gods are there really, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?'--'Thirty-three.'--'Yea, so it is!' he said.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'How many gods are there really, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?'--'Three.'--'Yea, so it is!' he said. 'How many gods are there really, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?'--'Two.'--'Yea, so it is!' he said. 'How many gods are there really, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?'--'One and a half.'--'Yea, so it is!' he said. 'How many gods are there really, Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya?'--'One.'--'Yea, so it is!' he said. 'Who are those three hundred and three, and three thousand and three?'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:5">11:6:3:5</a></font></span>5. He replied, 'These are their powers, but thirty-three gods indeed there are.'--'Who are those thirty-three? Eight Vasus, eleven Rudras, and twelve Âdityas,--that makes thirty-one; and Indra and Pra<i>g</i>âpati make up the thirty-three.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:6">11:6:3:6</a></font></span>6. 'Who are the Vasus?'--'Agni, the Earth, Vâyu (the wind), the Air, Âditya (the sun), Heaven, the Moon, and the Stars:--these are the Vasus, for these cause all this (universe) to abide (vas), and hence they are the Vasus.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:7">11:6:3:7</a></font></span>7. 'Who are the Rudras?'--'These ten vital airs in man, and the self (spirit) is the eleventh: when these depart from this mortal body, they cause wailing (rud), and hence they are the Rudras.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:8">11:6:3:8</a></font></span>8. 'Who are the Âdityas?'--'The twelve months of the year: these are the Âdityas, for they pass whilst laying hold on everything here; and inasmuch as they pass whilst laying hold (â-dâ) on everything here, they are the Âdityas.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:9">11:6:3:9</a></font></span>9. 'Who is Indra, and who Pra<i>g</i>âpati?'--'Indra, indeed, is thunder <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and Pra<i>g</i>âpati the sacrifice.'--'What is thunder?'--'The thunderbolt.'--'What is the sacrifice?'--'Cattle.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:10">11:6:3:10</a></font></span>10. 'Who are those three gods?'--'These three worlds, for therein all the gods are contained.'--'Who are those two gods?'--'Food and breath. (life).'--'Who is the one and a half?'--'He who is blowing here <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">1</font></a> (Vâyu, the wind).'--'Who is the one god?'--'Breath.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:6:3:11">11:6:3:11</a></font></span>11. He (Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya) said, 'Thou hast gone on questioning me beyond the deity <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">2</font></a>, beyond which there must be no questioning: thou shalt die ere such and such a day, and not even thy bones shall reach thy home!' And so, indeed, did he (<i>S</i>âkalya) die; and robbers carried off his bones <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">3</font></a>, taking them for something else <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Wherefore let no man decry <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">5</font></a> any one, for even (by) knowing this, he gets the better of him <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>

Â<i>s</i>val. G<i>ri</i>hyas. IV, 5, 1 seqq.; Kâty. <i>S</i>r. XXI, 3, 7 seqq. See also J. Muir, Orig. Sanskrit Texts, vol. v, p. 316.</p>
