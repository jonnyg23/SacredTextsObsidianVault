 <p><a name="page_1"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 1</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center"><i>S</i>ATAPATHA-BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h1>  <h2 align="center">ELEVENTH KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h2>  <h4 align="center">THE FULL AND NEW-MOON SACRIFICE.</h4>  <h3 align="center">FIRST ADHYÂYA. FIRST BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:1">11:1:1:1</a></font></span>1. Verily, Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the Sacrifice, is the Year: the night of new moon is its gate, and the moon itself is the bolt of the gate.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:2">11:1:1:2</a></font></span>2. And when one lays down the two fires at new moon <a name="fr_69"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">1</font></a>,--even as one would enter a stronghold by the gate, when the gate is open, and would thence reach the world of heaven, so it is when one lays down the fires at new moon.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:3">11:1:1:3</a></font></span>2. And if one lays down the fires under a (special) asterism <a name="fr_70"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">2</font></a>,--just as if one tried to enter a stronghold, when the gate is closed, in some other way than through the gate, and failed to get inside the stronghold, so it is when one lays down the fires under an asterism: let him therefore not lay down the fires under an asterism.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:4">11:1:1:4</a></font></span>4. On the same day on which that one (the moon) should not be seen either in the east or in the west,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 2</font></a></p>  
 <p>let him fast, for it is then that he (the moon) comes to this world <a name="fr_71"></a><a href="#fn_71"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and on that (day) he abides here (on the sacrificial ground).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:5">11:1:1:5</a></font></span>5. And all the gods abide (here), all the spirits, all the deities, all the seasons, all the Stomas (hymn-forms), all the P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>as <a name="fr_72"></a><a href="#fn_72"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and all the metres.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:6">11:1:1:6</a></font></span>6. And, verily, it is for all the gods, for all spirits, for all deities, for all seasons, for all Stomas, for all P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>as, and for all metres that the fires of him are laid down who lays them down at new moon: he should therefore lay them down at new moon.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:1:1:7">11:1:1:7</a></font></span>7. He may lay down the fires on the new moon which falls in the (month) Vai<i>s</i>âkha, for that coincides with the Rohi<i>n</i>î (asterism); for the Rohi<i>n</i>î means the self, offspring and cattle <a name="fr_73"></a><a href="#fn_73"><font size="1">3</font></a>: he thus becomes established in a self, in offspring and cattle. But, indeed, the new moon is the form of the Agnyâdheya: let him therefore lay down the fires at new moon;--let him perform the preliminary ceremony <a name="fr_74"></a><a href="#fn_74"><font size="1">4</font></a> at full moon, and the initiation ceremony at new moon.</p>
