 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3">11:4:3</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">THIRD BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  <h4 align="center">THE MITRAVINDÂ SACRIFICE.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:1">11:4:3:1</a></font></span>1. Pra<i>g</i>âpati was becoming heated (by fervid devotion), whilst creating living beings <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="#fn_216"><font size="1">1</font></a>. From him, worn out and heated, <i>S</i>rî (Fortune and Beauty) came forth. She stood there resplendent, shining, and trembling <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="#fn_217"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The gods, beholding her thus resplendent, shining, and trembling, set their minds upon her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:2">11:4:3:2</a></font></span>2. They said to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, 'Let us kill her and take (all) this from her.' He said, 'Surely, that <i>S</i>rî is a woman, and people do not kill a woman, but rather take (anything) from her (leaving her) alive.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:3">11:4:3:3</a></font></span>3. Agni then took her food, Soma her royal power, Varu<i>n</i>a her universal <i>s</i>overeignty, Mitra her noble rank, Indra her power, B<i>ri</i>haspati her holy lustre, Savit<i>ri</i> her dominion, Pûshan her wealth, Sarasvatî her prosperity, and Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> her beautiful forms.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:4">11:4:3:4</a></font></span>4. She said to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, 'Surely, they have taken (all) this from me!' He said, 'Do thou ask it back from them by sacrifice!'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:5">11:4:3:5</a></font></span>5. She perceived this offering with ten sacrificial dishes--a cake on eight potsherds for Agni, a pap for Soma, a cake on ten potsherds for Varu<i>n</i>a, a pap for Mitra, a cake on eleven potsherds for Indra, a pap for B<i>ri</i>haspati, a cake on twelve or eight potsherds for Savit<i>ri</i>, a pap for Pûshan, a pap for Sarasvatî, and a cake on ten potsherds for Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:6">11:4:3:6</a></font></span>6. She invited them by means of this invitatory formula,--'May Agni, Soma, Varu<i>n</i>a, Mitra, Indra, B<i>ri</i>haspati, and the thousandfold-bestowing Savit<i>ri</i>,--May Pûshan, for our Sacrifices, unite us with cattle, Sarasvatî with favour, Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> with beautiful forms!' They accordingly made their appearance again.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:7">11:4:3:7</a></font></span>7. By this offering-formula she then approached them in inverted order (beginning) from the last:--'May Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> grant me forms, and the bountiful Sarasvatî, and Pûshan good fortune, and may Savit<i>ri</i> bestow gifts on me, and Indra power, and Mitra noble rank, and Varu<i>n</i>a, and Soma and Agni!' They were ready to restore them to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:8">11:4:3:8</a></font></span>8. She perceived these additional oblations:--'May Agni, the food-eater, the food-lord, bestow food upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Agni, taking the oblation, departed and restored her food to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:9">11:4:3:9</a></font></span>9. 'May Soma, the king, the lord of kings, bestow royal power upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Soma, taking the oblation, departed and restored her royal power to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:10">11:4:3:10</a></font></span>10. 'May Varu<i>n</i>a, the universal sovereign, the lord of universal sovereigns, bestow universal sovereignty upon me at this sacrifice,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>svâhâ!' Varu<i>n</i>a, taking the oblation, departed and restored her universal sovereignty to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:11">11:4:3:11</a></font></span>11. 'May Mitra, the Kshatra (nobility), the lord of the Kshatra, bestow noble rank upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Mitra, taking the oblation, departed and restored her noble rank to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:12">11:4:3:12</a></font></span>12. 'May Indra, the power, the lord of power, bestow power upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Indra, taking the oblation, departed and restored her power to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:13">11:4:3:13</a></font></span>13. 'May B<i>ri</i>haspati, the Brahman (priesthood), the lord of the Brahman, bestow holy lustre upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' B<i>ri</i>haspati, taking the oblation, departed and restored her holy lustre to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:14">11:4:3:14</a></font></span>14. 'May Savit<i>ri</i>, the kingdom, the lord of the kingdom, bestow the kingdom upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Savit<i>ri</i>, taking the oblation, departed and restored her kingdom to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:15">11:4:3:15</a></font></span>15. 'May Pûshan, wealth, the lord of wealth, bestow wealth upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Pûshan, taking the oblation, departed and restored her wealth to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:16">11:4:3:16</a></font></span>16. 'May Sarasvatî, prosperity <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_218"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the lord of prosperity, bestow prosperity upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Sarasvatî, taking the oblation, departed and restored her prosperity to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:17">11:4:3:17</a></font></span>17. 'May Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, the fashioner of forms,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p>the lord of forms, bestow cattle with form <a name="fr_219"></a><a href="#fn_219"><font size="1">1</font></a> upon me at this sacrifice, svâhâ!' Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, taking the oblation, departed and restored her cattle with (beautiful) form to her.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:18">11:4:3:18</a></font></span>18. These, then, are ten deities, ten sacrificial dishes, . ten offerings, ten presents to priests,--the Virâ<i>g</i> consists of decad after decad (of syllables), and the Virâ<i>g</i> (shining one) is <i>S</i>rî (beauty, prosperity): he thus establishes (the Sacrificer) in the Virâ<i>g</i>, in prosperity and food.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:19">11:4:3:19</a></font></span>19. For this (sacrifice) there are fifteen kindling-verses <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_220"><font size="1">2</font></a>: he offers to the deities in a low voice <a name="fr_221"></a><a href="#fn_221"><font size="1">3</font></a>. There are five fore-offerings, three after-offerings, and one Samish<i>t</i>aya<i>g</i>us. The (formulas of the) two butter-portions contain the word 'affluence':--(<i>Ri</i>g-veda S. I, 1, 3), 'Through Agni may he obtain wealth and affluence day by day, famous and abounding in heroes;'--(<i>Ri</i>g-veda S. I, 91, 12), 'An increaser of the house, a remover of trouble, a procurer of wealth, an augmenter of affluence, a kind friend he thou unto us, O Soma!' The two formulas of the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t contain the word 'thousand':--(<i>Ri</i>g-veda S. III, 13, 7), 'Grant thou unto us wealth, a thousandfold, with offspring and affluence, and glorious manhood, O Agni, most excellent and never</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p>failing!'--(<i>Ri</i>g-veda S. III, 13, 6), 'Favour thou our prayer, as the best invoker of the gods for our hymns: blaze up auspiciously for us, wind-fanned, O Agni, the dispenser of a thousand bounties!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:4:3:20">11:4:3:20</a></font></span>20. Now, indeed, it was Gotama Râhûga<i>n</i>a who discovered this (sacrifice). It went away to <i>G</i>anaka of Videha, and he searched for it in the Brâhma<i>n</i>as versed in the Aṅgas <a name="fr_222"></a><a href="#fn_222"><font size="1">1</font></a> (limbs of the Veda), and found it in Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya. He said, 'A thousand we give thee, O Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya, in whom we have found that Mitravindâ.' He finds (vind) Mitra, and his is the kingdom, he conquers recurring death <a name="fr_223"></a><a href="#fn_223"><font size="1">2</font></a> and gains all life, whosoever, knowing this, performs this sacrifice; or whosoever thus knows it.</p>
