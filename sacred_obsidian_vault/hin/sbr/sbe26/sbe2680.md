 <p><a name="page_474"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 474</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ADDITIONS AND CORRECTIONS.</h3>  <h4 align="center">PART I. (VOL. XII OF SERIES.)</h4>  
 <p>Page vii, line 15. Read,--Stambhayagus.</p>  
 <p>P. xvi, l. 8. Read,--arrangement.</p>  
 <p>P. xxx, l. 2. Read,--'sixty' for 'forty.'</p>  
 <p>P. xliv, l. 28. Read,--'recensions' for 'relations.'</p>  
 <p>P. 3, l. 2 seq. I now take 'pûti' in the sense of 'foul,' and would translate thus,--The reason why he touches water is this: man forsooth is impure; in that he speaks untruth, thereby he is foul within. Now water is pure: 'Having become pure, I will enter on the vow,' &amp;c. See Part II, p. 8, n. 1.</p>  
 <p>P. 9, paragraph 18. Cf. 'mas ignis, quod ibi semen, aqua femina, quod fetus alitur humore.' Varro, L. L. 5, 61.</p>  
 <p>P. 65, last line. Read,--a composite direction.</p>  
 <p>P. 94, l. 8. Read,--'vedi' for 'prastara.'</p>  
 <p>P. 142, l. 1. Read,--'Turn ye back!' for 'draw near!' see Part II, p. 308, n. 1.</p>  
 <p>P. 166, par. 13. Read,--Now while Indra, being thus pushed aside, was moving on, he addressed . . .</p>  
 <p>P. 183, l. 6. Dele,--(the moon).</p>  
 <p>P. 210, note 1. Read twice,--'<i>s</i>astra' for '<i>s</i>âstra.'</p>  
 <p>P. 221, l. 4 seq. Instead of,--'Before the Rakshas (come),' B. R.'s Dict. interprets, perhaps rightly, 'Safely from the Rakshas.'</p>  
 <p>P. 263, par. 27.--These same deities . . .; for an improved rendering, cf. <a href="sbe2658.htm#4:4:4:5">IV, 4, 4, 5</a>-<a href="sbe2658.htm#4:4:4:6">6</a>.</p>  
 <p>P. 308, l. 21. For,--'and in pressing,' read 'to wit, in pressing.'</p>  
 <p>P. 323, l. 15. 'whom, surely, he would not eat;' for this construction, see Part II, p. 31, n. I.</p>  
 <p>P. 338, l. 9. Read,--Verily, with him . . .</p>  
 <p>P. 389, l. 26. Read,--'Âyu' for 'Âyus.'</p>  <h4 align="center">II. (VOL. XXVI.)</h4>  
 <p>P. 2, l. 4. Read,--'sacrificer' for 'sacrifice.'</p>  
 <p>P. 31, note 3. Cf. J. Muir, O. S. T. II, p. 114 note.</p>  
 <p>P. 71, note 1. According to Âp. <i>S</i>r. X, 20, 12 he is to buy the Soma from a Kautsa Brâhman; otherwise from any Brâhman; otherwise from one who is not a Brâhman.</p>  
 <p>P. 77, ll. 27, 32. Read,--Subrahma<i>n</i>yâ.</p>  
 <p>P. 103, note 1. Read,--âpyâyanam.</p>  
 <p>P. 128, l. 26. Read,--(<i>kh</i>adis).</p>  
 <p>P. 153, l. 6, 10 Rudra cf. III, 7, 3, 11.</p>  
 <p>P. 201, l. 4. Read,--Samitar.</p>  
 <p>P. 224, ll. 17-20. Dele thrice 'for.'</p>  
 <p>P. 267, par. 10. Cf. A. Bergaigne, La Réligion Védique, I, p. 171.</p>  
 <p>P. 286, note 3. Read,--sadasyânâ<i>m</i> hotrâ<i>n</i>âm.</p>  
 <p>P. 305, last line. Cf. Âp. XI, 20, 1 (prav<i>ri</i>ta-homa).</p>  
 <p>P. 324, ll. 8, 9. Read,--<i>S</i>ukra.</p>  
 <p>P. 334, note 2. Cf. Atharva-veda V, 4, 3; Kuhn, Herabkunft, p. 126 seq.</p>  
 <p>P. 441, l. 15. Read,--all-beneficent.</p>
