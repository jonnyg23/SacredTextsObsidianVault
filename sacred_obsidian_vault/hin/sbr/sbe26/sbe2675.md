 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6">4:6:6</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SIXTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:1">4:6:6:1</a></font></span>1. Now once on a time the gods, while performing sacrifice, were afraid of an attack from the Asura-Rakshas. They said, 'Who of us shall sit on the south side; we will then enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:2">4:6:6:2</a></font></span>2. They said, 'He who is the strongest of us, let him sit on the south side; we will then enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:3">4:6:6:3</a></font></span>3. They said, 'Verily, Indra is the strongest of us: let Indra sit on the south side; we will then enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:4">4:6:6:4</a></font></span>4. They said to Indra, 'Verily, thou art the strongest of us: sit thou on the south side; we will then enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:5">4:6:6:5</a></font></span>5. He said, 'What will be my reward then?'--'The office of Brâhma<i>n</i>â<i>kh</i>a<i>m</i>sin shall be thine, the</p>  
 <p><a name="page_434"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 434</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Brahmasâman <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1006"><font size="1">1</font></a> shall be thine!'--Hence one elects the Brâhma<i>n</i>â<i>kh</i>a<i>m</i>sin with, 'Indra is the Brahman, by virtue of the Brahmaship!' for to Indra belongs this (office). Indra sat on the south side, and they entered upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury. Therefore let him who is the strongest sit on the south side, and let them then enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury. Now he, forsooth, who is the most learned of Brâhmans, is the strongest of them; and as now any one is (able to become) a (superintendent) Brahman <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">2</font></a>--nay, does he not sit still?--therefore whosoever is the strongest</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_435"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 435</font></a></p>  
 <p>of them, let him sit on the south side, and let them then enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury. Hence Brâhmans sit on the south side (of the vedi), and they enter upon the sacrifice on the north side in a place free from danger and injury.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:6">4:6:6:6</a></font></span>6. When (the Prastot<i>ri</i>) says, 'Brahman, we will chant, O Pra<i>s</i>âstar!' then the Brahman mutters (Vâ<i>g</i>.. S. II, 12), 'This thy sacrifice, O divine Savitar, have they announced unto B<i>ri</i>haspati (the lord of prayer), the Brahman <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">1</font></a>: therefore speed the sacrifice, speed the lord of the sacrifice, speed me <a name="fr_1015"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">2</font></a>!--Praise ye at the impulse (prasava) of Savit<i>ri</i>!' The significance is the same (as before) <a name="fr_1016"></a><a href="#fn_1010"><font size="1">3</font></a>. With this (text) must probably enter upon (the chant).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:7">4:6:6:7</a></font></span>7. But one may also enter upon it with, 'O divine Savitar; this, O B<i>ri</i>haspati, forwards!' Therewith he hastes to Savit<i>ri</i> for his impulsion, for he is the impeller (prasavit<i>ri</i>) of the gods; and 'O B<i>ri</i>haspati, forwards!' he says, because B<i>ri</i>haspati is the Brahman of the gods,--thus he announces it to him who is the Brahman of the gods: therefore he says, 'O B<i>ri</i>haspati, forwards <a name="fr_1017"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">4</font></a>!'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:6:8">4:6:6:8</a></font></span>8. The Maitrâvaru<i>n</i>a then mutters, 'Impelled</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_436"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 436</font></a></p>  
 <p>by the divine Savit<i>ri</i>, acceptable to Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a!' Therewith he hastes to Savit<i>ri</i> for his impulsion, for he is the impeller of the gods; and 'acceptable to Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a' he says, because Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a are the deities of the Maitrâvaru<i>n</i>a (Pra<i>s</i>âst<i>ri</i>),--thus he announces it to those who are the deities of the Maitrâvaru<i>n</i>a: therefore he says, 'acceptable to Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1006"></a><a href="sbe2675.htm#fr_1012">434:1</a> That is, the Sâman which supplies the text for the Stotra chanted in connection with the Brâhma<i>n</i>â<i>kh</i>a<i>m</i>sin's Sastra, and forming the Stotriya verse of the latter. Thus, at the midday savana, the (P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>a) Stotra of that priest usually consists of the Naudhasa-sâman (Sâma-veda II, 35-36), if the Rathantara-sâman (Sâma-vela II, 30-31) is used for the Hot<i>ri</i>'s Stotra; but, if the B<i>ri</i>hat-sâman (ib. II, 159-160) is used for the latter, then the <i>S</i>yaita-sâman (II, 161-2) is used as the Brahma-sâman. See <a href="sbe2652.htm#page_339">p. 339</a>, note <a href="sbe2652.htm#fn_801">2</a>. The reason, however, why special mention is made of the Brâhma<i>n</i>â<i>kh</i>a<i>m</i>sin in this place, probably is that at the Gavâm ayana the Brahma-sâman is treated in a peculiar way. For, while on 142 days of the first half of the year,--viz. on the <i>K</i>aturvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, on all (6 × 23) Âbhiplavika days and on the three Svara-sâman days,--one and the same tune, the Abhîvarta-sâman, is to be used day by day, but each time with a different pragâtha stanza (thus the pragâtha S. V. II, 35-36, usually chanted to the Naudhasa tune, being on this occasion chanted to the Abhîvarta tune); on the corresponding days of the second half of the year, one and the same stanza, 'Indra kratu<i>m</i> na â bhara' (S. V. II, 806-7), is to be used day by day, but with different tunes (six such being given in the Calc. ed. vol. iv, pp. 529-34). Tâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ya Br. IV, 3, 1 seq.</p>
