 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:2">4:6:2</a></font></span></p>  <h4 align="center">THE GAVÂM AYANA <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_988"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</h4>  <h3 align="center">SECOND BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:2:1">4:6:2:1</a></font></span>1. Verily, they who sit (sacrificing) for a year, by means of six months go to him that burns yonder:</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_427"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 427</font></a></p>  
 <p>so it is told on the part of the Sâman, in as much as it is made of the form of that (sun) it is told on the part of the <i>Ri</i>k <a name="fr_995"></a><a href="#fn_989"><font size="1">1</font></a>; and now in like manner on the part of the Ya<i>g</i>us, by means of preparatory rite, when they draw that (graha) <a name="fr_996"></a><a href="#fn_990"><font size="1">2</font></a>, they thereby also go to him (the sun).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_428"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 428</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="4:6:2:2">4:6:2:2</a></font></span>2. He thus takes it therefrom with (Vâ<i>g</i>. S. VIII, 41; Rig-veda I, 50, 1) <a name="fr_997"></a><a href="#fn_991"><font size="1">1</font></a>, 'The lights bear on high that divine knower of beings, Sûrya, that all may see him!--Thou art drawn with a support: thee to Sûrya nor splendour!--This is thy womb: thee to Sûrya for splendour!'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_988"></a><a href="sbe2671.htm#fr_994">426:3</a> The great sacrificial session (sattra), called Gavâm ayana, or <a name="page_427"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 427</font></a> cows' walk (or course), usually extends over twelve months (of 30 days), and consists of the following parts:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Prâya<i>n</i>îya Atirâtra, or opening day.  
 <p><i>K</i>aturvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a day, an Ukthya, all the stotras of which are in the <i>K</i>aturvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a stoma.</p>  
 <p>Five months, each consisting of four Abhiplava sha<i>d</i>ahas and one P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>ya sha<i>d</i>aha (= 30 days). Cf. <a href="sbe2662.htm#page_402">p. 402</a>, n. <a href="sbe2663.htm#fn_935">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>28 days, which, with the two opening days, complete the sixth month:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Three Abhiplavas and one P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>ya.<br> Abhi<i>g</i>it day.<br> Three Svarasâman days.</dir>  
 <p>VISHUVANT or central day.</p>  
 <p>28 days, which, with the two concluding days, complete the seventh month:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Three Svarasâman days.<br> Vi<i>s</i>va<i>g</i>it day.<br> One P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>ya and three Abhiplavas.</dir>  
 <p>Four months, each consisting of four Abhiplavas and one P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>30 days:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>(Three Abhiplava sha<i>d</i>ahas.<br> One Gosh<i>t</i>oma (Agnish<i>t</i>oma, <a href="sbe2663.htm#page_403">p. 403</a>, n. <a href="sbe2663.htm#fn_937">4</a>).<br> One Âyush<i>t</i>oma (Ukthya).<br> One Da<i>s</i>arâtra (the ten central days of the Dvâda<i>s</i>âha, <a href="sbe2662.htm#page_402">p. 402</a>, note <a href="sbe2663.htm#fn_935">2</a>).</dir>  
 <p>Mahâvrata day.</p>  
 <p>Udayanîya Atirâtra.</p></dir>  
 <p>In imitation of the retrograde course of the sun, the order of the performance during the second part of the year is, generally speaking, the reverse of that of the former half.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_989"></a><a href="sbe2671.htm#fr_995">427:1</a> Ta eta<i>m</i> sha<i>d</i>bhir mâsair yanti, tasmât parâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>o grahâ g<i>ri</i>hyante parâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>i stotrâ<i>n</i>i parâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>i <i>s</i>astrâ<i>n</i>i. Ta eta<i>m</i> shash<i>th</i>e mâse ga<i>kh</i>anti tad etasya rûpa<i>m</i> kriyate. Kâ<i>n</i>va text.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_990"></a><a href="sbe2671.htm#fr_996">427:2</a> Viz. the Atigrâhya cup to Sûrya (<a href="sbe2663.htm#4:5:4:2">IV, 5, 4, 2</a> seq.), which has <a name="page_428"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 428</font></a> to be drawn on the Vishuvant or middle day of the Gavâm ayana; an animal sacrifice to the same deity being also prescribed.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_991"></a><a href="sbe2671.htm#fr_997">428:1</a> The Kâ<i>n</i>va text allows the alternative mantra, Rig-veda I, 50, 3; Vâ<i>g</i>. S. VIII, 40, Ad<i>ri</i><i>s</i>ram asya ketava<i>h</i>, &amp;c. See <a href="sbe2663.htm#4:5:4:11">IV, 5, 4, 11</a>.</p>
