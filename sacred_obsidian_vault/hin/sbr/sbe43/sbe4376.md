 <p><a name="page_405"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 405</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CORRECTIONS.</h3>  
 <p>Page 66, 1. 11, and note 1. The <i>K</i>atush<i>t</i>oma is such an arrangement of the Stotras of a Soma-sacrifice in which the Stomas (or hymn-forms) employed increase successively by four syllables. See notes on XI, 5, 2, 9; XIII, 3, 1, 4 (sic!).</p>  
 <p>P. 279, line 7. Perhaps this passage had better be translated thus,--And he who, without having performed these (rites), should officiate even for another person in the performance of any other sacrifices . . .</p>  
 <p>P. 296, l. 3 from bottom. Read,--Upavasatha.</p>  
 <p>P. 394-98, paragraphs 4-9; 11. Read,--repels recurrent death.</p>  
 <p>      ,, l. 5 from bottom. Read,--tasyâhutaya<i>s</i> (!).</p>  
 <p align="center">PART III.</p>  
 <p>Introduction, page xii, line 27. Read,--pa<i>s</i>ubandha.</p>  
 <p>P. xvi, l. 11 of notes. After--<i>S</i>yaita-sâman, add (II, 161-2).</p>  
 <p>P. xviii, l. 23. In Sat. Br. V, 5, 3, 4, the Sho<i>d</i>a<i>s</i>in is distinctly mentioned as forming part of the Ke<i>s</i>avapanîya Atirâtra.</p>  
 <p>P. xviii, l. 4 of notes. Read,--'form' instead of 'from.'</p>  
 <p>P. xxv, note. Add,--So also Sat. Br. V, 3, 4, 12; 4, 3, 2.</p>  
 <p>P. 38, l. 1. Read,--Vâ<i>g</i>aprasavîya.</p>  
 <p>P. 62, l. 22. Read,--(as it undoubtedly does in V, 4, 3, 18; as well as savyash<i>th</i>â, in Atharva-v. VIII, 8, 23).</p>  
 <p>   ,, end of note. Add,--In Sat. Br. V, 4, 3, 23, sa<i>m</i>grahît<i>ri</i> certainly means charioteer.'</p>  
 <p>P. 113, l. 8. The chant here alluded to is the first (or Hot<i>ri</i>'s) P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>a-stotra, for which see part ii, p. 339. The Abhishe<i>k</i>anîya being, however, performed on the Ukthya, not on the Agnish<i>t</i>oma, model, this stotra, on the present occasion, consists of the B<i>ri</i>hat-sâman (see Introduction, pp. xvi, note 2; xxvi).</p>  
 <p>P. 136, l. 4. Read,--Barhishada<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p>P. 140, last line of text. For 'Âra<i>n</i>i' read 'Âru<i>n</i>i.'</p>  
 <p>P. 169, l. 18. Read,--'Those kindling-sticks . . . .<font size="-1" color="green">'</font></p>  
 <p>P. 171, l. 2. Read,--Âshâ<i>dh</i>i.</p>  
 <p>P. 203, l. 10. Read,--'vâk' (voice).</p>  
 <p>P. 265, note 2. In the passage of the Ait. Br. referred to, the Vishuvat day is the central day of an Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>arâtra, or twenty-one days' performance.</p>  
 <p>P. 352, l. 10. Read,--'sand' for seed.'</p>  
 <p>P. 360, note 1. Add,--See also VIII, 7, 3, 19, where that thread is identified with the wind.</p>  
 <p>P. 363, note 1. The reference is wrong; for the real Satya-sâman. see part iv, p. 145, note a.</p>  
 <p><a href="errata.htm#8">P</a>. 67, l. 24. In the opposite direction, i.e. in the direction away from us.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_406"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 406</font></a></p>  
 <p>P. 369, note 1. The reference is wrong; for the real tetra-sâman, see part iv, p. 145, note 1.</p>  
 <p>P. 379, l. 10. Read,--the breath serves everything here.</p>  
 <p>   „    l. 20. For this Sâman, based on the word 'bhûs,' see part iv, p. 145, note 1.</p>  
 <p>P. 404, l. 15. Read,--having pre-eminently endowed the man with power, he sets him up. Cf. <a href="sbe4327.htm#8:7:2:3">VIII, 7, 2, 3</a>.</p>  
 <p>P. 415, l. 4. Read,--XIII, 53.</p>
