 <p><a name="page_449"><font size="1" color="green">p. 449</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA X, HYMN 168.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA VIII, ADHYÂYA 8, VARGA 26.</h4>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">To Vâta</span>.</h4>  
 <p>1. Now for the greatness of the chariot of Vâta <a href="sbe3262.htm#an_10:168:1:1">1</a>! Its roar goes crashing and thundering. It moves touching the sky, and creating red sheens <a href="sbe3262.htm#an_10:168:1:2">2</a>, or it goes scattering the dust of the earth.</p>  
 <p>2. Afterwards there rise the gusts of Vâta <a href="sbe3262.htm#an_10:168:2:1">1</a>, they go towards him, like women to a feast <a href="sbe3262.htm#an_10:168:2:2">2</a>. The god goes with them on the same chariot, he, the king of the whole of this world.</p>  
 <p>3. When he moves on his paths along the sky, he rests not even a single day <a href="sbe3262.htm#an_10:168:3:1">1</a>; the friend of the waters, the first-born, the holy, where was he born, whence did he spring?</p>  
 <p>4. The breath of the gods, the germ <a href="sbe3262.htm#an_10:168:4:1">1</a> of the world, that god moves wherever he listeth; his roars indeed are heard, not his form—let us offer sacrifice to that Vâta!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_450"><font size="1" color="green">p. 450</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>Ascribed to Anila Vâtâyana, and addressed to Vâyu, here called Vâta. The metre is Trish<i>t</i>ubh. This hymn does not occur in the other Vedas. See Muir, Sanskrit Texts, V, p. 145; Geldner and Kaegi, p. 95.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_10:168:1:1">1</a>. For this use of the accusative, see Pischel, Ved. Stud. p. 13.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_10:168:1:2">2</a>. Aru<i>n</i>âni is explained by Geldner, Ved. Stud. p. 274, as the reddish colours of the lightning.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_10:168:2:1">1</a>. Vish<i>th</i>â means kind or variety. Anu seems to refer to ratha, which I take as the subject of the whole of the first verse.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_10:168:2:2">2</a>. 'Sie gehn mit einander zum Tanz,' Geldner and Kaegi.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_10:168:3:1">1</a>. Geldner and Kaegi propose aha for aha<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_10:168:4:1">1</a>. Vâta seems to be called the garbha of the world, in the sense of being its source or life.</p>
