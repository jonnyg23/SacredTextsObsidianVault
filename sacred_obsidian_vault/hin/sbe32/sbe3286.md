 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>Vedic Hymns, Part I (SBE32)</i>, by Max Müller, [1891], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p>ba<i>t</i>, particle of asseveration: (V, 59, 1:1.)</p>  
 <p>badh, see vadh.</p>  
 <p>bandh, to bind: baddhám ásti tanû´shu, it clings to our bodies, VI, 74, 3.</p>  
 <p>bándhana, stem: urvârukám iva bándhanâ´t, like a gourd from its stem, VII, 59, 12.</p>  
 <p>bandhu-eshá: bandhu-eshé, when there was inquiry for their kindred, V, 52, 16.</p>  
 <p>babhrú, tawny: babhrú<i>h</i>, II, 33, 5; 9; babhráve, II, 33, 8; babhro, II, 33, 15.</p>  
 <p>barhá<i>n</i>â, weapon, bolt: barhá<i>n</i>â, I, 166, 6:6; (<a href="sbe3227.htm#page_226">226</a>.)—barhá<i>n</i>â tmánâ, by their own might, X, 77, 3.</p>  
 <p>barhís, grass-pile, altar: barhí<i>h</i>, I, 85, 6; VII, 57, 2:1; 59, 6; barhíshi, I, 85, 7; 86, 4; VII, 46, 4:1.</p>  
 <p>bála, strength: bálam, I, 37, 12; V, 57, 6.</p>  
 <p>bala-dâ´, giving strength: bala-dâ´<i>h</i>, X, 121, 2.</p>  
 <p>bahulá, manifold: bahulám, V, 55, 9.</p>  
 <p>ba<i>n</i>á, reed, arrow: (VIII, 20, 8:1.)</p>  
 <p>bâdh, to drive away: âré bâdhethâm, VI, 74, 2.—bâ´dhante ápa, I, 85, 3; ápa bâdhadhvam, VII, 56, 20.—níbâdhita, struck down, (<a href="sbe3227.htm#page_268">268</a> seq.)</p>  
 <p>bâhú, arm: bâhû´ (the regions are the two arms of Hira<i>n</i>yagarbha), X, 121, 4; bâhú-bhi<i>h</i>, I, 85, 6:1; bâhúshu, I, 166, 10; VIII, 20, 11; bâhvó<i>h</i>, V, 57, 6.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_496"><font size="1" color="green">p. 496</font></a></p>  
 <p>bâhú-o<i>g</i>as, strong-armed: bâhú-o<i>g</i>asa<i>h</i>, VIII, 20, 6.</p>  
 <p>bâhú-<i>g</i>ûta, quick with his arm: bâhú-<i>g</i>ûta<i>h</i>, V, 58, 4.</p>  
 <p>bî´<i>g</i>a, seed: bî´<i>g</i>am, V, 53, 13.</p>  
 <p>budh, to awake: Sâya<i>n</i>a, bodhi = budhyasva, (II, 33, 15:2.)—prá bodhaya, awake, I, 134, 3.</p>  
 <p>budhná, bottom: budhné apâ´m, X, 77, 4.</p>  
 <p>budhnyã, hidden: budhnyẫ, VII, 56, 14:1.</p>  
 <p>b<i>ri</i><i>h</i>: barhayati, to crush, (<a href="sbe3227.htm#page_226">226</a>.)—upabárb<i>ri</i>hat, she stretched out (dó<i>h</i>, her arm), V, 61, 5:1.—Cf. v<i>ri</i>h.</p>  
 <p>b<i>ri</i>hát, great, mighty: b<i>ri</i>hát, V, 55, I; 2; 57, 8; 58, 8; b<i>ri</i>hát váya<i>h</i>, VII, 58, 3; b<i>ri</i>hát <i>g</i>íhîte, VIII, 20, 6; b<i>ri</i>hát vadema, we shall magnify, II, 33, 25; b<i>ri</i>hántam krátum, I, 2, 8; â´pa<i>h</i> b<i>ri</i>hatî´<i>h</i>, X, 121, 7:1; 9; b<i>ri</i>hatá<i>h</i> divá<i>h</i>, V, 59, 7; 87, 3.</p>  
 <p>b<i>ri</i>hat-giri, dwelling on mighty mountains: b<i>rí</i>hat-giraya<i>h</i>, V, 57, 8:1; 58, 8.</p>  
 <p>b<i>ri</i>hát-diva, coming from the great heaven: b<i>rí</i>hat-divai<i>h</i>, I, 167, 2; (V, 57, 8:1.)</p>  
 <p>b<i>ri</i>hat-vayas, of great strength: b<i>ri</i>hat-vayasa<i>h</i> (the Maruts), (I, 37, 9:1.)</p>  
 <p>B<i>rí</i>haspáti, a variety of Agni: (I, 38, 13:1.)</p>  
 <p>bradhná, bright: bradhnám, I, 6, 1:1.</p>  
 <p>Bráhma<i>n</i>aspáti, lord of prayer: N. of Agni, (I, 38, 13:1); (<a href="sbe3227.htm#page_246">246</a>, note <sup><a href="sbe3227.htm#fn_108">a</a></sup>.)</p>  
 <p>brahma<i>n</i>yát, prayerful: brahma<i>n</i>yántah, II, 34, 11.</p>  
 <p>brahmán, m. priest: brahmâ´ ká<i>h</i>, VIII, 7, 20; brahmâ´<i>n</i>am, X, 77, 1:4.</p>  
 <p>bráhman, n., prayer, hymn: bráhma, I, 37, 4; 88, 4; 165, 11; II, 34; 7:1; bráhmâ<i>n</i>i, I, 165, 2; 4:1; 14; II, 34, 6; bráhma<i>n</i>a<i>h</i> pátim, lord of prayer, I, 38, 13:1.</p>  
 <p>brû, to speak: bruve (púmân íti), he is called, V, 61, 8.—ádhi brûhi na<i>h</i>, bless us, I, 114, 10.—úpa bruvate, they implore, I, 134, 2.—prá bruváte, they proclaim, V, 87, 2.—sám bruvate, they talk together, I, 37, 13.</p>
