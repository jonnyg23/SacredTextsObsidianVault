 <p><a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="green">p. 293</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 172.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA II, ADHYÂYA 4, VARGA 12.</h4>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">To the Maruts (the Storm-gods)</span>.</h4>  
 <p>1. May your march be brilliant, brilliant through your protection, O Maruts, you bounteous givers, shining like snakes!</p>  
 <p>2. May that straightforward shaft of yours, O Maruts, bounteous givers, be far from us, and far the stone which you hurl!</p>  
 <p>3. Spare, O bounteous givers, the people of T<i>ri</i><i>n</i>askanda, lift us up that we may live!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="green">p. 294</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The hymn is ascribed to Agastya, the metre is Gâyatrî. None of its verses occurs in SV., VS., TS., AV.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Prof. Oldenberg conjectures <i>k</i>itrâ´ ûtí<i>h</i>, and possibly mahibhânava<i>h</i> for ahibhânava<i>h</i>. See for yâ´ma<i>h</i> <i>k</i>itrá<i>h</i> ûtî´ V, 52, 2. té yâ´man pânti; also VI, 48, 9.</p>
