 <p align="center"><b>BIBLIOGRAPHY</b></p>  
 <p>The beginner will find the following works useful:</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sacred Books of the East</span>, edited by F. Max Müller: vol. 1 &amp; 15, Upanishads, translated by F. Max Mūller; vol. .34, Vedānta-sūtra (i.e. Brahma-sūtra) with the commentary by Śankara, translated by G. Thibaut. <i>Oxford, Clarendon Press,</i> 1879, etc.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Philosophy of the Upanishads</span>, by A. E. Gough. <i>London, Trübner,</i> 1882.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Upanishads</span> (in Sanskrit and English) and Śankara's commentary (in English), translated by Sītārāma Śāstri and Gangānātha Jhā. <i>Madras,</i> <i>Seshachari</i>, 1898, etc.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sechzig Upanishads des Veda</span>, übersetzt von Dr. Paul Deussen. <i>Leipzig, Brockhaus,</i> 1897</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Philosophy of the Upanishads</span>, by Paul Deussen. Translated by Rev. A. Geden. <i>Edinburgh, T. &amp; T. Clark,</i> 1906.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Das System des Vedanta</span>, von P. Deussen. <i>Leipzig, Brockhaus,</i> 1883.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Six Systems of Indian Philosophy</span>, by F. Max Müller. <i>London, Longmans,</i> 1899.</p>  
 <p>Of the numerous compendia of the later Vedānta we may mention two which have been published in English—the Vedānta-sāra of Sadānanda, translated under the title "A Manual of Hindu Pantheism" by Col. G. A. Jacob (<i>London, Trübner,</i> 1881), and the Advaita-makaranda of Lakshmīdhara,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="green">p. 113</font></a></p>  
 <p>with Svayamprakāśa's commentary, translated by A. E. Gough in "The Pandit," vol. 8, 9 (<i>Benares,</i> 1873).</p>  
 <p>Of the chief <i>Sanskrit</i> texts of the Vedānta schools numerous editions have been published in India. Among the best are those issued in the "Anandāśrama Sanskrit Series" from the Ānandāśrama Press, Poona.</p>  
 <p>The student is not likely to derive much exact knowledge from the publications of irresponsible neo-vedantic societies in Europe and America. These bodies have the commendable object of making the principles of the Vedānta intelligible to modern Western thinkers; but until they display a more scientific and historical spirit their success is likely to be limited.</p>
