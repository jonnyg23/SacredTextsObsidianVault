 <p>§ 19. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Subtle Body</span>.—According to the later Vedānta, the Soul in its wanderings from birth to birth is accompanied by the sense-organs and "breaths" as sums of potential faculties, and has for its vehicle the "subtle body," <i>sūkshmaśarīra.</i> The latter consists of portions of the five elements in their higher suprasensual form, and thus is as it were a seed which on occasion grows by the accession of gross matter into a physical body.</p>  
 <p>There is no clear evidence for the existence of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  
 <p>this idea in the Upanishads until the mention of a <i>linga</i> (the term used in the Sānkhya school for "subtle body") in B.A. IV. iv. 6; cf. Kaṭh. VI. 8, Śvet. VI. 9, Maitr. VI. 10. For Śankara's view see especially on Brahma-sūtra, III. i. 1 f., IV. ii. 6 f. The "subtle body" adheres to the soul until it attains perfect enlightenment and release in Brahma; the souls which never reach this goal are always attended by it.</p>
