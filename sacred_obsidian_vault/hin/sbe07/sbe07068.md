 <a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXVI.</h1>
 <p>1. He must not make an oblation to the gods or to the manes with water collected at night.</p>
 <p>2. He must not give any other fragrant substance than sandal, or musk, or (fragrant) wood (of the odoriferous Devadâru tree), or camphor, or saffron, or the wood of the <i>G</i>âtîphala tree;</p>
 <p>3. Nor a garment dyed with indigo;</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[11. Vâ<i>g</i>as. Sa<i>m</i>h. XXII, 1. Nand. states that this Mantra belongs to the Sâkhâ of the Ka<i>th</i>as; but I have not met with it in the Kâ<i>th</i>aka.</p>
 <p>12. Rig-veda IV, 39, 6, &amp;c.</p>
 <p>13. Rig-veda X, 121, 1-8; Kâ<i>th</i>. XL, 1, &amp;c.</p>
 <p>14. <sup>1</sup> Thus the term mâtrâ is interpreted by Nand.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a></p>
 <p>4. Nor an ornament made of factitious jewels or gold;</p>
 <p>5. Nor (a flower) having a nasty odour;</p>
 <p>6. Nor one that has no odour at all;</p>
 <p>7. Nor one grown upon a thorny plant.</p>
 <p>8. But he may give even a flower grown upon a thorny plant, if it is white and sweet-smelling.</p>
 <p>9. He may give even a red flower, if it is saffron, or a water-flower (such as the red lotus).</p>
 <p>10. (He must) not (give) any animal substance (such as claws or horns) for the incense.</p>
 <p>11. (He must) not (give) anything but clarified butter or oil for the lamp.</p>
 <p>12. (He must) not (give) forbidden food at the offering of eatables;</p>
 <p>13. Nor the milk of goats or female buffalos, though it is lawful food (otherwise);</p>
 <p>14. Nor the flesh of five-toed animals, of fishes, and of boars.</p>
 <p>15. Fully prepared for the sacrifice and pure, he must announce (and offer up to Vish<i>n</i>u) all the oblations, with his mind fixed upon the deity, with a cheerful heart, and free from precipitation or anger.</p>
