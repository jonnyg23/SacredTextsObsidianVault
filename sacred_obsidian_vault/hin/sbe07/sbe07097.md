 <a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 277</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">XCV.</h1>
 <p>1. An hermit must dry up his frame by the practice of austerities.</p>
 <p>2. In summer he must expose himself to five fires.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[6. The use of the particle <i>k</i>a implies, according to Nand., that the practice of distributing gifts should likewise be continued.</p>
 <p>11. The particle vâ here refers, according to Nand., to a third alternative mentioned by Manu (VI, 18), that he should gather provisions sufficient for six months.</p>
 <p>XCV. 1. M. VI, 24.--2-4. M. VI, 23; Y. III, 52.--5, 6. M. VI, 19; Y. III, 50.--7-11. M. VI, 5, 21; Y. III, 46; Âpast. II, 9, 22, 2; Gaut. III, 26.--12, 13. M. VI, 20; Y. III, 50.--14, 15. M. VI, 17; Y. III, 49.--16, 17. M. XI, 235, 239.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 278</font></a></p>
 <p>3. During the season of the rains he must sleep in the open air.</p>
 <p>4. In winter he must wear wet clothes.</p>
 <p>5. He must eat at night.</p>
 <p>6. He may eat after having fasted entirely for one day, or for two days, or for three days.</p>
 <p>7. He may eat flowers. 8. He may eat fruits.</p>
 <p>9. He may eat vegetables.</p>
 <p>10. He may eat leaves. 11. He may eat roots.</p>
 <p>12. Or he may eat boiled barley once at the close of a half-month.</p>
 <p>13. Or he may eat according to the rules of the <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a.</p>
 <p>14. He shall break his food with stones.</p>
 <p>15. Or he shall use his teeth as a pestle.</p>
 <p>16. This whole world of deities and of men has devotion for its root, devotion for its middle, devotion for its end, and is supported by devotion.</p>
 <p>17, What is hard to follow<sup>[1]</sup>, hard to reach, remote, or hard to do, all that may be accomplished by devotion; since there is nothing that may not be effected by devotion.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[6. Nand. considers the particle vâ to refer to the precept of Yâ<i>g</i>ñavalkya (III, 50), that the fast may also extend over a half-month or an entire month.</p>
 <p>13. The particle vâ, according to Nand., implies that he may also perform K<i>rikkh</i>ras, as ordained -by Yâ<i>g</i>ñavalkya (III, 50). Regarding the <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a, see XLVII.</p>
 <p>17. 'Du<i>sk</i>ara has been translated according to the usual acceptation of this term. Nand. interprets it by 'hard to understand.' This proverb is also found Subhâshitâr<i>n</i>ava 109, V<i>ri</i>ddha<i>k</i>ânakhya's Proverbs XVII, 3. See Böhtlingk, Ind. Sprüche, 5265.]</p>
