 <a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 176</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">XCIV.</h1>
 <p>1. A householder, when he sees his skin has become wrinkled and his hair turned grey, must go to live in a forest.</p>
 <p>2. Or (he must do so) when he sees the son of his son.</p>
 <p>3. Let him (before going into the forest) entrust the care of his wife to his sons, or let her accompany him.</p>
 <p>4. Let him keep the sacred fires in his new abode as before.</p>
 <p>5. He must not omit to perform the five sacrifices,</p>

III, 45; Âpast. II, 9, 21, 19.--8. M. VI, 6; Âpast. II, 9, 22, 1; Gaut. VI, 34.--9, 10. M. VI, 6; Y. III, 46, 48.--9, 11. Gaut III, 34, 35.--11. M. VI, 18; Y. III, 47.--12. M. VI, 15; Y. III, 47; Âpast. II, 9, 22, 24.--13. M. VI. 28; Y. III, 55. 'The duties of a householder having been declared, he now goes on to expound the duties of an hermit.' (Nand.)</p>
 <p>5. See LIX, 20 seq.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 277</font></a></p>
 <p>but (he must perform them) with (fruits, herbs, or roots) growing wild.</p>
 <p>6. He must not relinquish the private recitation of the Veda.</p>
 <p>7. He must preserve his chastity.</p>
 <p>8. He must wear a dress made of skins or bark.</p>
 <p>9. He must suffer the hairs of his head, of his beard, and of his body, and his nails to grow.</p>
 <p>10. He must bathe at morning. noon, and evening.</p>
 <p>11. He must either collect provisions, after the manner of the pigeon, for a month, or he must collect them for a year.</p>
 <p>12. He who has collected provisions for a year, must throw away what he has collected on the day of full moon in the month Â<i>s</i>vina.</p>
 <p>13. Or an hermit may bring food from a village, placing it in a dish made of leaves, or in a single leaf, or in his hand, or in a potsherd, and eat eight mouthfuls of it.</p>
