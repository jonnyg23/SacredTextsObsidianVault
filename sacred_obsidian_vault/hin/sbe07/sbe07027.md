 <a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 110</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">XXV.</h1>
 <p>1. Now the duties of a woman (are as follows):</p>
 <p>2. To live in harmony with her husband;</p>
 <p>3. To show reverence (by embracing their feet and such-like attentions) to her mother-in-law, father-in-law, to Gurus (such as elders), to divinities, and to guests;</p>
 <p>4. To keep household articles (such as the winnowing basket and the rest) in good array;</p>
 <p>5. To maintain saving habits;</p>
 <p>6. To be careful with her (pestle and mortar and other) domestic utensils;</p>
 <p>7. Not to practise incantations with roots (or other kinds of witchcraft);</p>
 <p>8. To observe auspicious customs;</p>
 <p>9. Not to decorate herself with ornaments (or to partake of amusements) while her husband is absent from home;</p>
 <p>10. Not to resort to the houses of strangers (during the absence of her husband);</p>

 <p>11. Not to stand near the doorway or by the windows (of her house);</p>
 <p>12. Not to act by herself in any matter;</p>
 <p>13. To remain subject, in her infancy, to her father; in her youth, to her husband; and in her old age, to her sons.</p>
 <p>14. After the death of her husband, to preserve her chastity, or to ascend the pile after him.</p>
 <p>15. No sacrifice, no penance, and no fasting is allowed to women apart from their husbands; to pay obedience to her lord is the only means for a woman to obtain bliss in heaven.</p>
 <p>16. A woman who keeps a fast or performs a penance in the lifetime of her lord, deprives her husband of his life, and will go to hell.</p>
 <p>17. A good wife, who perseveres in a chaste life after the death of her lord, will go to heaven like (perpetual) students, even though she has no son.</p>
