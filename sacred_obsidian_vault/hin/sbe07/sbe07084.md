 <a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 251</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXXXII.</h1>
 <p>1. At a (<i>S</i>râddha) offering to the Vi<i>s</i>vedevâs let him not enquire (into the qualities or descent of) a Brâhma<i>n</i>a (whom he means to invite).</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[LXXXII. 1, 2. M. III, 149.--3-29. M. III, 150 166; Y. I, 222-224; Âpast. II, 7, 17, 21; Gaut. XV, 16-18.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 252</font></a></p>
 <p>2. But at a (<i>S</i>râddha offering) to the manes he must enquire as closely as possible (into the qualities and descent of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, whom he means to invite).</p>
 <p>3. He must not invite (to a Srâddha) such as have a limb too little, or a limb too much;</p>
 <p>4. Nor such as follow an occupation forbidden (by the Veda or by the traditional law)<sup>[1]</sup>.</p>
 <p>5. Nor those who act (deceitfully) like cats;</p>
 <p>6. Nor those wearing the insignia of some particular order, without having a claim to them;</p>
 <p>7. Nor astrologers;</p>
 <p>8. Nor Brâhma<i>n</i>as who subsist upon the offerings made to an idol which they attend;</p>
 <p>9. Nor physicians;</p>
 <p>10. Nor sons of an unmarried woman;</p>
 <p>11. Nor sons of the son of an unmarried woman;</p>
 <p>12. Nor those who sacrifice for a multitude of persons;</p>
 <p>13. Nor those who offer sacrifices for a whole village;</p>
 <p>14. Nor those who offer sacrifices for <i>S</i>ûdras;</p>
 <p>15. Nor those who offer sacrifices for those for whom it is forbidden to sacrifice (such as outcasts and others);</p>
 <p>16. Nor those for whom the ceremony of initiation has not been performed;</p>
 <p>17. Nor those who sacrifice for such;</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[4. <sup>1</sup> The particle <i>k</i>a, according to Nand., in this Sûtra, refers to other categories, mentioned by Atri, viz. persons belonging to the same Gotra, or descended from the same <i>Ri</i>shi ancestors as the sacrificer, and unknown persons.</p>
 <p>8. '<i>K</i>a indicates here that thieves and wicked persons are also intended, as stated in a Sm<i>ri</i>ti.' (Nand.)]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_253"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 253</font></a></p>
 <p>18. Nor those who do work on holidays;</p>
 <p>19. Nor malignant informers;</p>
 <p>20. Nor those who teach (the Veda) for a fee;</p>
 <p>21. Nor those who have been taught (the Veda) for a fee;</p>
 <p>22. Nor those who subsist on food given to them by a <i>S</i>ûdra;</p>
 <p>23. Nor those who have intercourse with an outcast;</p>
 <p>24. Nor those who neglect their daily study of the Veda;</p>
 <p>25. Nor those who neglect their morning and evening prayers;</p>
 <p>26. Nor those who are in the king's service;</p>
 <p>27. Nor 'naked' persons;</p>
 <p>29. Nor those who quarrel with their father;</p>
 <p>29. Nor those who have forsaken their father, mother, Guru, holy fire, or sacred study.</p>
 <p>30. All those persons are said to defile a company, because they have been expelled from the community of Brâhma<i>n</i>as. Let a wise man avoid carefully, therefore, to entertain them at a <i>S</i>râddha.</p>
