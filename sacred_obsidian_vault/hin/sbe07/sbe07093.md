 <a name="page_270"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 270</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">XCI.</h1>
 <p>1. The digger of a well has (the consequences of) the half of his evil acts taken from him as soon as the water comes forth from it.</p>
 <p>2. A digger of pools is for ever freed from thirst, and attains the world of Varu<i>n</i>a.</p>
 <p>3. A giver of water shall never stiffer from thirst (in heaven, for a hundred Yugas or ages of the world).</p>
 <p>4. He who plants trees will have those trees for his sons in a future existence.</p>
 <p>5. A giver of trees gladdens the gods by (offering up) their blossoms to them.</p>
 <p>6. (He gladdens) his guests by (giving) their fruits to them;</p>
 <p>7. (He gladdens) travellers with their shade;</p>
 <p>[XCI. 14. Y. I, 211.--15, 16. M. IV, 229.--17, 18. Y. I, 209.]</p>
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>
 <p>8. (He gladdens) the manes with the water (trickling down from their leaves) when it rains.</p>
 <p>9. A maker of dikes attains heaven.</p>
 <p>10. A builder of temples enters the dwelling-place of that deity to whom he has erected a temple.</p>
 <p>11. He who causes (a temple erected by another) to be whitewashed acquires brilliant fame.</p>
 <p>12. He who causes (such a temple) to be painted with (a different) colour (such as blue, yellow, and others) attains the world of the Gandharvas.</p>
 <p>13. By giving flowers he becomes fortunate.</p>
 <p>14. By giving ointments he acquires renown.</p>
 <p>15. By giving a lamp he obtains an excellent eyesight and exquisite happiness.</p>
 <p>16. By giving food he obtains strength.</p>
 <p>17. By removing the remains of an offering to a deity he obtains the same reward as for giving a cow.</p>
 <p>18. The same reward is also obtained by scouring a temple, by smearing it (with cow-dung and the like), by removing the leavings of the food of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, by washing his feet, and by nursing him when sick.</p>
 <p>19. He who consecrates anew a well, or a park, or a pool, or a temple (when they have been soiled) obtains the same reward as he who first made them.</p>
