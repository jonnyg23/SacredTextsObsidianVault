 <a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 222</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXIX.</h1>
 <p>1. He must not have connection with his wife on the eighth, or fourteenth, or fifteenth day of the half-month.</p>
 <p>2. And (he must avoid connubial intercourse) after having partaken of a <i>S</i>râddha;</p>
 <p>3. And after having given (a <i>S</i>râddha);</p>
 <p>4. And after having been invited to a <i>S</i>râddha;</p>
 <p>5. And while performing a vow of abstinence (such as that to be kept on the day before a <i>S</i>râddha, or the fast to be observed on the eleventh day of the half-month);</p>
 <p>6. And one who has performed the initiatory ceremony of a Soma-sacrifice;</p>
 <p>7. And in a temple, in a burial-ground, and in an empty house;</p>
 <p>8. And at the root of a tree (or shrub);</p>
 <p>9. And in the day-time; 10. And in the twilight;</p>
 <p>11. And with one unclean (or in her courses);</p>
 <p>12. And while he is unclean himself; 13. And with one anointed with unguents; 14. And being anointed himself; 15. And with one sick; 16. And while he is sick himself,</p>
 <p>17. He must not have connection, if he wishes to enjoy a long life, with a woman who has a limb too little, nor with one who has a limb too much, nor with one older than himself, nor with a pregnant woman.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[LXIX. 1. M. IV. 128; Y. I, 79.--9. Âpast. II, 1, 1, 16.--15. Gaut. IX, 28. The subject of daily duties being absolved, he now goes on to state (in Chapters LM, LXX) the rules that must be observed during the night. (Nand.)</p>
 <p>4. The invitations to a <i>S</i>râddha are issued on the day before it is to take place. (Nand.)]</p>
