 <a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXII.</h1>
 <p>1. The part at the root of the little finger of a twice-born man is called the Tîrtha sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>
 <p>2. The part at the root of the thumb is called the Tîrtha sacred to Brahman.</p>
 <p>3. The part at the tops of the fingers is called the Tîrtha sacred to the gods.</p>
 <p>4. The part at the root of the forefinger is called the Tîrtha sacred to the manes.</p>
 <p>5. Let him sip water, which has not been put to the fire and is free from foam (and bubbles), which has not been poured out by a <i>S</i>ûdra (or other uninitiated person), or by a man who has one hand only, and which has no saline flavour<sup>[1]</sup>; and (let him sip it) in a clean place, duly seated, placing (his right hand) between his knees, facing the east or the north (or, the north-east), attentively regarding the water, and in a cheerful mood.</p>
 <p>6. Let him sip water thrice with the Tîrtha sacred</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[17. <sup>1</sup> It must be washed both before and after using it. (Nand.) LXII. 1-4. M. II, 59; Y. I, 19.--5-8. M. II, 60, 61; Y. I, 20; Âpast. I, 5, 16, 1-7; Gaut. I, 36.--9. M. II, 62; Y. I, 21.</p>
 <p>1. Nand. observes that this chapter and the preceding one follow in order upon Chapter LX, because the purificatory rite described at the end of the latter is immediately followed by the Â<i>k</i>amana (sipping of water), and then by the Dantadhâvana (cleaning the teeth), both of which acts, however, have to be performed on other occasions also, as after a meal, &amp;c.</p>
 <p>5. 'The term kshâra, 'saline flavour,' includes bad or spoiled water of any kind, according to Nand.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a></p>
 <p>to Brahman (or with the Tîrthas sacred to the gods and to Pragipati respectively).</p>
 <p>7. Let him wipe his lips twice (with the root of his thumb).</p>
 <p>8. Let him touch the cavities (above his navel)<sup>[1]</sup>, his head, and his breast with water.</p>
 <p>9. By water which reaches either their heart, or their throat, or their palate respectively, members of the three twice-born castes are purified each in his turn; a woman and a <i>S</i>ûdra are purified by water which has once touched their palate.</p>
