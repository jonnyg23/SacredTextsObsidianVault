 <a name="page_246"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 246</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXXIX.</h1>
 <p>1. He must not perform a <i>S</i>râddha with water collected at night.</p>
 <p>2. On failure of Ku<i>s</i>a grass he must employ Kâ<i>s</i>a or Dûrvâ grass instead.</p>
 <p>3. Instead of a garment (he may give) cotton thread.</p>
 <p>4. He must avoid (giving) the fringe of cloth, though it be of cloth not yet used.</p>
 <p>5. And (he must not give) flowers having a nasty odour, or no odour at all, the blossoms of thorny plants, and red flowers.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[52. <sup>1</sup> The term prâv<i>ri</i>tkâle, 'in the rainy season,' probably refers to one month only of the rainy season, the month Bhâdrapada or Praush<i>th</i>apada. See above, LXXVI, 1, and M. III, 273, 274, with Kullûka's Commentary; Y. I, 260, with Vi<i>g</i>ñânesvara's Comment.</p>
 <p>LXXIX. 8, 16. M. III, 226, 227, 235, 257; Âpast. II, 8, 19, 19-22.--19-21, M. III, 229.</p>
 <p>5. The use of the particle <i>k</i>a implies, according to Nand., who quotes a text in support of his assertion, that the leaves of the Kadamba, Bèl, Ketaka, and Bakula trees, as well as those of the Barbara plant and of the thorn-apple tree, are also included in this prohibition.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_247"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 247</font></a></p>
 <p>6. He may give white and sweet-smelling flowers, even though grown on thorny plants, and aquatic flowers, even though they be red.</p>
 <p>7. He must not give marrow or fat instead of a lamp.</p>
 <p>S. He may give clarified butter or sesamum-oil.</p>
 <p>9. He must not give (the nails or horns) of animals instead of the incense of all kinds (prescribed for a <i>S</i>râddha).</p>
 <p>10. He may give bdellium mixed up with honey and clarified butter.</p>
 <p>11. He may give sandal, saffron, camphor, aloe wood, or Padmaka wood instead of an ointment.</p>
 <p>12. He must not salt (the dishes) publicly (after they have been cooked).</p>
 <p>13. He must not give clarified butter, condiments, or the like (i. e. sour milk, milk, &amp;c.) with his hands.</p>
 <p>14. He must use metallic vessels;</p>
 <p>15. Especially vessels made of silver.</p>
 <p>16. He must place (on the sacrificial ground) vessels made of the horn of the rhinoceros, blankets made of the hair of the mountain-goat, the skin of a black antelope, sesamum, white mustard, unbroken grains, (silver and copper vessels and other) purificatory objects, and (a goat and other animals or objects), by which the demons are kept aloof.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[7. 'Or mustard-oil or any other such substance, as <i>k</i>a indicates.' (Nand.)</p>
 <p>8. 'Or the juice of plants, as mentioned by <i>S</i>a<u>n</u>kha, on account of vâ.' (Nand.)</p>
 <p>13. He must give those liquids with a spoon or similar implement. (Nand.)</p>
 <p>14. According to Nand., the particle <i>k</i>a refers to other purificatory things, viz. the following seven, 'milk, water from the Ganges, honey, silken cloth, a grandson, blankets made of the hair of the {footnote p. 248} mountain-goat, and sesamum.' The last two are, however, already contained in the above enumeration.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_248"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 248</font></a></p>
 <p>17. He must avoid to use pepper, (the onion called) Mukundaka, (the pot-herb called) Bhûst<i>rin</i>a, (the leaves, blossoms, or roots of) the <i>S</i>igru tree, mustard-seeds, (the plant) Nirgu<i>nd</i>î, (the fruit or leaves of) the <i>S</i>âl tree, the plant Suvar<i>k</i>alâ, the (pumpkin-gourd called) Kûshmâ<i>nd</i>a, the bottle-gourd, the egg-plant, (the plants or pot-herbs called) Pâlakyâ, Upo<i>t</i>akî, and Ta<i>nd</i>ulîyaka, the herbs of the safflower, the Pi<i>nd</i>âluka (root), and the milk of female buffalos.</p>
 <p>18. And (he must not use the bean called) Râ<i>g</i>amâsha, (the lentil called) Masûra, stale food, and factitious salt.</p>
 <p>19. Let him avoid wrath.</p>
 <p>20. He must not shed a tear.</p>
 <p>21. He must not be in a hurry.</p>
 <p>22. In offering the clarified butter and other (liquids, such as condiments, sour milk, milk, and the like) he must use metallic vessels, vessels made of the horn of the rhinoceros, and vessels made of the wood of the Phalgu tree.</p>
 <p>23. There is a <i>S</i>loka on this subject:</p>
 <p>24. 'That which has been offered in vessels made of gold, or of silver, or of the horn of the rhinoceros, or of copper, or of Phalgu wood, becomes imperishable (and brings infinite reward to the sacrificer).'</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[17. The term buffalo's milk' includes here, according to a text quoted by Nand., the milk of sheep, of antelopes, of camels, and of all one-hoofed animals.</p>
 <p>18. 'As shown by <i>k</i>a, chick-peas and other grains and herbs mentioned in a <i>S</i>m<i>ri</i>ti must also be avoided.' (Nand.)</p>
 <p>19. 'This rule applies both to the sacrificer and to the guests at a Srâddha.' (Nand.)]</p>
