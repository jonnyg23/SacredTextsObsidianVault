 <a name="page_138"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 138</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">XXXIX.</h1>
 <p>1. Killing domestic or wild animals are crimes degrading to a mixed caste.</p>
 <p>2. He who has committed a crime degrading to a mixed caste shall eat barley-gruel for a month (if he has committed it knowingly), or perform the penance K<i>rikkh</i>râtik<i>rikkh</i>ra (if he has committed it unawares).</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[35. Regarding the penances called <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a and Parâka see below, XLVIII and XLVII, 18.</p>
 <p>XXXVIII. 1-6, M. XI, 68.</p>
 <p>7. <sup>1</sup> See XLVI, 19, 10.</p>
 <p>XXXIX. 1. M. XI, 69.</p>
 <p>2. Regarding the penance K<i>rikkh</i>râtik<i>rikkh</i>ra, see XLVI, 13. 'The use of the causative form kârayet indicates that he may {footnote p. 139} perform the penance mentioned here through a substitute, if unable to perform it himself. (Nand.)]</p>
