 <a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXV.</h1>
 <p>1. Now then, after having duly bathed, and duly washed his hands and feet, and duly sipped water, he must worship Bhagavat Vâsudeva (Vish<i>n</i>u), who is without beginning and end, before an idol or on the sacrificial ground.</p>
 <p>2. Having called up in his mind (Vish<i>n</i>u to life, with the Mantra), 'The two A<i>s</i>vins possess life, may they (give you life),'and having invited (Vish<i>n</i>u) with the Anuvâka (beginning with the words), 'They get their minds ready<sup>[2]</sup>,' he must worship him with his knees, his hands, and his head<sup>[3]</sup>.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[LXV, LXVI. These two chapters treat of the worship of Vish<i>n</i>u. (Nand.)</p>
 <p>LXV. 1. The fittest. place for worshipping Vish<i>n</i>u is upon a <i>S</i>âlagrâma (ammonite) stone. (Nand.)</p>
 <p>2. <sup>1</sup> Kâ<i>th</i>aka XI, 7. The rendering of this Mantra is conjectural, as the reading is uncertain. Nand. states expressly that it is quoted from the Kâ<i>th</i>aka.--<sup>2</sup> See LXIV, 22.--<sup>3</sup> 'The particle <i>k</i>a indicates that he must also worship Vish<i>n</i>u in his mind, and with his speech, by saying, 'Om, adoration to Bhagavat Vâsudeva.' (Nand.)]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a></p>
 <p>3. With the three Mantras (beginning with the words), 'Ye waters are,' he must (fetch and) announce the Arghya (or water for washing the hands).</p>
 <p>4. With the four Mantras (beginning with the words), 'The golden-coloured,' (he must fetch and announce) the water for washing the feet;</p>
 <p>5. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the words), 'May the waters of the plain propitiate us,' the water which is to be sipped;</p>
 <p>6. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the words), 'Carry away (all) that, O ye waters,' the water destined for the bath;</p>
 <p>7. With (the four Mantras, beginning with the words, 'Proud) of the chariot, of the poles, the hero,' unguents and ornaments;</p>
 <p>8. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the words), 'A youth, splendidly arrayed,' a garment;</p>
 <p>9. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the word), 'Blooming,' a flower;</p>
 <p>10. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[3, 4. See LXIV, 18.</p>
 <p>5. This Mantra is found Atharva-veda I, 6, 4; XIX, 2, 2; Taitt. Âra<i>n</i>y. VI, 4, 1. Nand. states that it is ka<i>th</i>a<i>s</i>âkhîya, from the <i>S</i>âkhâ of the Ka<i>th</i>as; but I have not found it in the Berlin MS. of the Kâ<i>th</i>aka, the only complete MS. in existence of that work.</p>
 <p>6. See LXIV, 18.</p>
 <p>7.. This Mantra also belongs to the Ka<i>th</i>a school, according to Nand. It is not found in the MS. of the Kâ<i>th</i>aka, but it occurs in the Taitt. Brâhm. II, 7, 7, 2. The above translation is in part according to Sâya<i>n</i>a's Commentary on the Taitt. Brâhm.</p>
 <p>8. Rig-veda III, 8, 4, &amp;c.</p>
 <p>9. Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. IV, 2, 6, 1; Kâ<i>th</i>. XVI, 13; Atharva-veda VIII, 7, 27. Nand. says that it is a Taittirîya Mantra.</p>
 <p>10. Kâ<i>th</i>. II, 7; Vâ<i>g</i>as. Sa<i>m</i>h. I, 8 (cf. Mahîdhara's Commentary). Nand. says that it is a Taittirîya Ya<i>g</i>us.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>
 <p>words), 'Thou art murderous (dhûr), slay (dhûrva) (the slayer),' incense (dhûpa);</p>
 <p>11. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the words), 'Thou art splendour and light,' a lamp;</p>
 <p>12. With (the one Mantra, beginning with the words, 'I have praised) Dadhikrâvan,' a Madhuparka (honey-mixture);</p>
 <p>13. With the eight Mantras (beginning with the word), 'Hira<i>n</i>yagarbha,' an offering of (other) eatables.</p>
 <p>14. A chowrie, a fan, a looking-glass<sup>[1]</sup>, an umbrella, a (palanquin or other) vehicle, and a (throne or other) seat, all these objects he must announce (and place before) the god (Vish<i>n</i>u), muttering the Gâyatrî (at the same time).</p>
 <p>15. After having thus worshipped him, he must mutter the Purushasûkta. After that, he who wishes to obtain eternal bliss must make oblations of clarified butter, while reciting the same hymn.</p>
