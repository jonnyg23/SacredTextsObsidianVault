 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LXXXVII</h3> 
 <p>I. 71. <i>gagan gha<b>t</b>â ghaharânî, sâdho</i></p> 
 <p>CLOUDS thicken in the sky! O, listen to the deep voice of their roaring;<br> The rain comes from the east with its monotonous murmur.<br> Take care of the fences and boundaries <a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="green">p. 134</font></a> of your fields, lest the rains overflow them;<br> Prepare the soil of deliverance, and let the creepers of love and renunciation be soaked in this shower.<br> It is the prudent farmer who will bring his harvest home; he shall fill both his vessels, and feed both the wise men and the saints.</p>
