 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LXXXVI</h3> 
 <p>III. 96. <i>jîv mahal me<u>n</u> S'iv pahunwâ</i></p> 
 <p>SERVE your God, who has come into this temple of life!<br> Do not act the part of a madman, for the night is thickening fast.<br> He has awaited me for countless ages, <a name="page_133"><font size="1" color="green">p. 133</font></a> for love of me He has lost His heart:<br> Yet I did not know the bliss that was so near to me, for my love was not yet awake.<br> But now, my Lover has made known to me the meaning of the note that struck my ear:<br> Now, my good fortune is come.<br> Kabîr says: "Behold! how great is my good fortune! I have received the unending caress of my Beloved!"</p>
