 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XLIV</h3> 
 <p>I. 93. <i>gagan ma<b>t</b>h gaib nisân ga</i>d<i>e</i></p> 
 <p>THE Hidden Banner is planted in the temple of the sky; there the blue canopy decked with the moon and set with bright jewels is spread.<br> There the light of the sun and the moon is shining: still your mind to silence before that splendour. <a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="green">p. 92</font></a><br> Kabîr says: "He who has drunk of this nectar, wanders like one who is mad."</p>
