 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XXIII</h3> 
 <p>II. 40. <i>ti<u>n</u>wir sâñjh kâ gahirâ âwai</i></p> 
 <p>THE shadows of evening fall thick and deep, and the darkness of love envelops the body and the mind.<br> Open the window to the west, and be lost in the sky of love;<br> Drink the sweet honey that steeps the petals of the lotus of the heart.<br> Receive the waves in your body: what splendour is in the region of the sea!<br> Hark! the sounds of conches and bells are rising. <a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="green">p. 73</font></a><br> Kabîr says: "O brother, behold! the Lord is in this vessel of my body."</p>
