 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LXIII</h3> 
 <p>I. 39. <i>are man, dhîraj kâhe na dharai</i></p> 
 <p>WHY so impatient, my heart?<br> He who watches over birds, beasts, and insects, <a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="green">p. 107</font></a><br> He who cared for you whilst you were yet in your mother's womb,<br> Shall He not care for you now that you are come forth?<br> Oh my heart, how could you turn from the smile of your Lord and wander so far from Him?<br> You have left Your Beloved and are thinking of others: and this is why all your work is in vain.</p>
