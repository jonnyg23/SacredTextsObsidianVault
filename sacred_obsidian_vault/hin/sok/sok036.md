 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XXXV</h3> 
 <p>II. 113. <i>vâlam, âwo hamâre geh re</i></p> 
 <p>MY body and my mind are grieved for the want of Thee;<br> O my Beloved! come to my house. <a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="green">p. 83</font></a><br> When people say I am Thy bride, I am ashamed; for I have not touched Thy heart with my heart.<br> Then what is this love of mine? I have no taste for food, I have no sleep; my heart is ever restless within doors and without.<br> As water is to the thirsty, so is the lover to the bride. Who is there that will carry my news to my Beloved?<br> Kabîr is restless: he is dying for sight of Him.</p>
