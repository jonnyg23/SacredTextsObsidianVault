 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XXXVI</h3> 
 <p>II. 126. <i>jâg piyârî, ab kâ<u>n</u> sowai</i></p> 
 <p>O FRIEND, awake, and sleep no more!<br> The night is over and gone, would you lose your day also?<br> Others, who have wakened, have received jewels; <a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="green">p. 84</font></a><br> O foolish woman! you have lost all whilst you slept.<br> Your lover is wise, and you are foolish, O woman!<br> You never prepared the bed of your husband:<br> O mad one! you passed your time in silly play.<br> Your youth was passed in vain, for you did not know your Lord;<br> Wake, wake! See! your bed is empty: He left you in the night.<br> Kabîr says: "Only she wakes, whose heart is pierced with the arrow of His music."</p>
