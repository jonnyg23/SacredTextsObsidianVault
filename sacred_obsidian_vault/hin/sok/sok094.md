 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XCIII</h3> 
 <p>III. 111. <i>ko<b>t</b>î<u>n</u> bhânu candra târâga<b>n</b></i></p> 
 <p>BENEATH the great umbrella of my King millions of suns and moons and stars are shining!<br> He is the Mind within my mind: He is the Eye within mine eye.<br> Ah, could my mind and eyes be one! Could my love but reach to my Lover! Could but the fiery heat of my heart be cooled!<br> Kabîr says: "When you unite love with the Lover, then you have love's perfection."</p>
