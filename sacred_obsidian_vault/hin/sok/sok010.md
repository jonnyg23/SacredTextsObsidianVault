 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">IX</h3> 
 <p>I. 104. <i>aisâ lo nahî<u>n</u> taisâ lo</i></p> 
 <p>O HOW may I ever express that secret word?<br> O how can I say He is not like this, and He is like that? <a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="green">p. 53</font></a><br> If I say that He is within me, the universe is ashamed:<br> If I say that He is without me, it is falsehood.<br> He makes the inner and the outer worlds to be indivisibly one;<br> The conscious and the unconscious, both are His footstools.<br> He is neither manifest nor hidden, He is neither revealed nor unrevealed:<br> There are no words to tell that which He is.</p>
