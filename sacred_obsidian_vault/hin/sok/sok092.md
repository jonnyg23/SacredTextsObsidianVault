 <p>III. 12. <i>sa<b>m</b>skirit bhâshâ pa</i>d<i>hi lînhâ</i></p> 
 <p>I HAVE learned the Sanskrit language, so let all men call me wise:<br> But where is the use of this, when I <a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="green">p. 137</font></a> am floating adrift, and parched with thirst, and burning with the heat of desire?<br> To no purpose do you bear on your head this load of pride and vanity.<br> Kabîr says: "Lay it down in the dust, and go forth to meet the Beloved. Address Him as your Lord."</p>
