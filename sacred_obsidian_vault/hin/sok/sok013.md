 <p>II. 24. <i>ha<b>m</b>sâ, kaho purâtan vât</i></p> 
 <p>TELL me, O Swan, your ancient tale.<br> From what land do you come, O Swan? to what shore will you fly?<br> Where would you take your rest, O Swan, and what do you seek?</p> 
 <p>Even this morning, O Swan, awake, arise, follow me!<br> There is a land where no doubt nor sorrow have rule: where the terror of Death is no more. <a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="green">p. 56</font></a><br> There the woods of spring are a-bloom, and the fragrant scent "He is I" is borne on the wind:<br> There the bee of the heart is deeply immersed, and desires no other joy.</p>
