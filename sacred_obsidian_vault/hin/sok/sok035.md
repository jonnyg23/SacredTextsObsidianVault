 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XXXIV</h3> 
 <p>II. 110. <i>mohi tohi lâgî kaise chu<b>t</b>e</i></p> 
 <p>HOW could the love between Thee and me sever?<br> As the leaf of the lotus abides on the water: so thou art my Lord, and I am Thy servant.<br> As the night-bird Chakor gazes all night at the moon: so Thou art my Lord and I am Thy servant.<br> From the beginning until the ending of time, there is love between Thee and me; and how shall such love be extinguished?<br> Kabîr says: "As the river enters into the ocean, so my heart touches Thee."</p>
