 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XCIV</h3> 
 <p>I. 92. <i>avadhû begam des' hamârâ</i></p> 
 <p>O SADHU! my land is a sorrowless land.<br> I cry aloud to all, to the king and the beggar, the emperor and the fakir--<br> Whosoever seeks for shelter in the Highest, let all come and settle in my land!<br> Let the weary come and lay his burdens here!</p> 
 <p>So live here, my brother, that you may cross with ease to that other shore.<br> It is a land without earth or sky, without moon or stars;<br> For only the radiance of Truth shines in my Lord's Durbar.<br> Kabîr says: "O beloved brother! naught is essential save Truth."</p>
