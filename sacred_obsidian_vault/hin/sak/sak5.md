 <h2 align="center">BOOK V.</h2>
 
 <hr width="12.5%" align="center">
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_0a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The tenets of his Institute are completed. Next is begun a Fifth Book, in order to set aside the primâ facie notions of others in regard to his Institute. Among those, in the first place he disposes of the objection that the Benediction implied by the expression 'Well,' in the first Aphorism [of Book I.], is purposeless:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Reasons for a Benedictory Opening.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_1"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 1.</font><a href="#pp_1"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The [use of a] Benediction [is justified] by the practice of the good, by our seeing its fruit, and by Scripture.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_1a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The [use of a] Benediction, which we made, is proved to be proper to be made, by these proofs; such is the
 
 <!------------------------page 313------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_313"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 313</font></a>
 
 meaning. The word <i>iti</i> is intended to preclude the expectation of any other reasons.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_1b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels those who entertain the primâ facie view, that what was asserted in the expression, 'because it is not proved that there <i>is</i> a Lord' [see <a href="sak1.htm#pp_92">Book I., Aph. 92</a>], is not made out; because [forsooth,] his existence is proved by his being the giver of the fruits of works:<a name="page_313_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_313_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Needlessness of a Lord.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_2"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 2.</font><a href="#pp_2"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not from its [the world's,] being governed by a Lord is there the effectuation of fruit: for it is by works [i.e., by merit and demerit,] that this is accomplished.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_2a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: it is not proper [to suppose] the effectuation of the change [of the elements] into the shape of the [appropriate] fruit of works, on the ground that the cause is 'governed by a Lord;' because it is possible for
 
 <!------------------------page 314------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_314"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 314</font></a>
 
 the fruit to be effected by the works [i.e., the merit and demerit,] alone, which are indispensable; [and, if we <i>do</i> make the additional and cumbrous supposition of a Lord, he cannot reward a man otherwise than according to his works].<a name="page_314_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_314_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a>
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_2b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He declares, further, in [several] aphorisms, that it is not the case that the Lord is the giver of fruit:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The supposed Lord would be selfish.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_3"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 3.</font><a href="#pp_3"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [If a Lord were governor, then] from intending his own benefit, his government [would be selfish], as is the case [with ordinary governors] in the world.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_3a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> If the Lord were the governor, then his government would be only for his own benefit; as is the case [with ordinary rulers] in the world: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 315------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_315"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 315</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_3b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In reply to the doubt, 'grant that the Lord, also, be benefited: what harm?' he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   And, therefore, not the Lord spoken of.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_4"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 4.</font><a href="#pp_4"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [He must, then, be] just like a worldly lord, [and] otherwise [than you desire that we should conceive of him].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_4a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> If we agree that the Lord, also, is benefited, he, also, must be something mundane, 'just like a worldly lord;' because, since his desires are [on that supposition,] not [previously] satisfied, he must be liable to grief, &amp;c.: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_4b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In reply to the doubt, 'be it even so,' he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The difficulty perhaps originates in a mistaken expression.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_5"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 5.</font><a href="#pp_5"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Or [let the name of Lord be] technical.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_5a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> If, whilst there exists also a world, there be a Lord, then let yours, like ours, be merely a technical term for
 
 <!------------------------page 316------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_316"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 316</font></a>
 
 that soul which emerged at the commencement of the creation; since there cannot be an eternal lordship, because of the contradiction between mundaneness and the having an unobstructed will: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_5b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states another objection to the Lord's being the governor:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Objection to there being a Lord.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_6"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 6.</font><a href="#pp_6"><font color="RED">*</font></a> This [position, viz., that there is a Lord,] cannot be established without [assuming that he is affected by] Passion; because that is the determinate cause [of all energizing].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_6a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: moreover, it cannot be proved that he is a <i>governor</i>, unless there be Passion; because Passion is the determinate cause of activity.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 317------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_317"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 317</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_6b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, be it so, that there is Passion in the Lord, even. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This objection, further.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_7"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 7.</font><a href="#pp_7"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Moreover, were that [Pasion] conjoined with him, he could not be eternally free.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_7a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: moreover, if it be agreed that there is conjunction [of the Lord] with Passion, he cannot be eternally free; and, therefore, thy tenet [of his eternal freedom] is invalidated.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_7b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Pray [let us ask], does lordship arise from the immediate union, with Soul, of the wishes, &amp;c., which we hold to be properties of Nature, [not properties of Soul]? Or from an influence by reason of the mere existence of proximity, as in the case of the magnet? Of these he condemns the former alternative:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 318------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_318"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 318</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Objection, on one branch of an alternative.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_8"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 8.</font><a href="#pp_8"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If it were from the conjunction of the properties of Nature, it would turn out that there is association, [which Scripture denies of Soul].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_8a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> From the conjunction, with Soul, of 'the properties of Nature,' i.e., Desire, &amp;c., Soul, also, would turn out [contrary to Scripture,] to be associated with properties.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_8b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But, in regard to the latter [alternative], he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Objection, on the other branch.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_9"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 9.</font><a href="#pp_9"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If it were from the mere existence [of Nature, not in association, but simply in proximity], then lordship would belong to every one.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 319------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_319"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 319</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_9a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: if lordship is by reason of the mere existence of proximity, as in the case of the magnet [which becomes affected by the simple proximity of iron], then it is settled, as we quite intend it should be, that even all men, indifferently, experiencers in this or that [cycle of] creation, [may] have lordship; because it is only by conjunction with all experiencers, that Nature produces Mind, &amp;c. And, therefore, your tenet of there being only one Lord is invalidated.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_9b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Be it as you allege; yet these are false reasonings; because they contradict the evidence which establishes [the existence of] a Lord. Otherwise, Nature, also, could be disproved by thousands of false reasonings of the like sort. He therefore says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Denial that there is any evidence of a Lord.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_10"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 10.</font><a href="#pp_10"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is not established [that there is an eternal Lord]; because there is no evidence of it.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 320------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_320"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 320</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_10a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Its establishment, i.e., the establishing that there is an eternal Lord. Of the Lord, in the first place, there is not <i>sense</i>-evidence; so that only the evidences of inference and of testimony can be offered; and these are inapplicable: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_10b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> The inapplicability he sets forth in two aphorisms:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Denial that it can be established by inference.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_11"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 11.</font><a href="#pp_11"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no inferential proof [of there being a Lord]; because there is [here] no [case of invariable] association [between a sign and that which it might betoken].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_11a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Association,' i.e., invariable concomitancy. 'There is none;' i.e., none exists, [in this case]. And so there is no inferential proof of there being a Lord; because, in such arguments as, 'Mind, or the like, has a maker, because it is a product,' [the fact of] invariable concomitancy<a name="page_320_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_320_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> is not established, since there is no compulsion [that every product should have had an intelligent maker]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 321------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_321"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 321</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_11b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Nor, moreover, he tells us, is there [the evidence of] Testimony [to there being a Lord]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Denial that there is Scripture for it.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_12"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 12.</font><a href="#pp_12"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Moreover, there is Scripture for [this world's] being the product of Nature, [not of a Lord].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_12a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Scripture asserts, exclusively, that the world is the product of Nature, not that it has Soul for its cause.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_12b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He refutes, diffusely, by a cluster [of seven aphorisms],<a name="page_321_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_321_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> the opinion of an opponent in regard to that which was established in the first Section,<a name="page_321_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_321_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> viz., 'Bondage does not arise from Ignorance,' [conjoined with Soul].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 322------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_322"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 322</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Conjunction, in the case of the solitary, would be a contradiction.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_13"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 13.</font><a href="#pp_13"><font color="RED">*</font></a> With that which is solitary there cannot be conjunction of the property of Ignorance.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_13a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Since Soul has no association [with anything whatever], it is plainly impossible for it to be united with the property of Ignorance.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_13b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, [it may be replied,] what is to be asserted is, that the conjunction of Ignorance is simply through force of Ignorance [which is a negation, or nonentity]; and so, since this is no <i>reality</i>, there is no <i>association</i> occasioned thereby. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A suggestion repelled.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_14"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 14.</font><a href="#pp_14"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Since the existence of this [alleged <i>negative</i> Ignorance] is established [only] on the ground of its [pretended] conjunction, there is a vicious circle.<a name="page_322_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_322_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_14a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> And, if it is by the conjunction of Ignorance that Ignorance is established, there is 'a vicious circle,' [literally,
 
 <!------------------------page 323------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_323"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 323</font></a>
 
 a resting of each on the other, alternately], a resting a thing on itself; or, in short, a <i>regressus in infinitum</i>.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_14b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In reply to the doubt [suggested by the Naiyáyika], 'but then, as in the case of seed and sprout, the <i>regressus in infinitum</i> is no objection,' he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The world has a beginning.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_15"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 15.</font><a href="#pp_15"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is not as in the case of seed and sprout; for Scripture teaches that the world has a beginning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_15a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> There cannot belong to it such a <i>regressus in infinitum</i> as that of seed and sprout; because there is Scripture for the fact that the mundane state of souls, consisting of all undesirable things, viz., Ignorance, &amp;c., had a beginning. For we hear, in Scripture, that these cease to exist at the dissolution of all things, in profound sleep, &amp;c. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_15b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, [you Vedántís will say], according to us, Ignorance is technically so termed, and is not, e.g., in
 
 <!------------------------page 324------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_324"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 324</font></a>
 
 the shape, specified by the <i>Yoga</i>, of supposing what is not soul to be soul; and so, just like your 'Nature,' since this [Ignorance] of ours has an unbroken eternity, though it be lodged in Soul, there is no disparagement of the solitariness thereof: in regard to this doubt, having deliberated on this artificial sense of the word 'Ignorance,' he objects to it:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul and knowledge not identical.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_16"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 16.</font><a href="#pp_16"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Then Brahma would be found to be excluded [from existence]; because he is something else than knowledge.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_16a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> If the meaning of the word 'Ignorance' (<i>avidyá</i>) be only 'otherness than knowledge,' then <i>Brahma</i>, soul itself, would be found to be excluded, to perish, through his being annihilable by knowledge; since <i>he</i> is other than knowledge: such is the meaning. [Further]:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 325------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_325"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 325</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Knowledge, not excluding ignorance, would be resultless.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_17"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 17.</font><a href="#pp_17"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Were there not exclusion, then there would be resultlesseness.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_17a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But, if, the existence of ignorance were really not excluded by knowledge, then there would be resultlessness of knowledge, because of its not debarring Ignorance, [which is the only result competent to knowledge]: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_17b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He censures the other alternative, [viz., that knowledge <i>might</i> exclude Soul]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   On the Vedánta theory, the world ought to vanish.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_18"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 18.</font><a href="#pp_18"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If it [Ignorance,] meant the being excludible by Knowledge, it would be [predicable], in like manner, of the world, also.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_18a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> If, on the other hand, the being excludible by Knowledge, in the case of the soul, which possesses properties,
 
 <!------------------------page 326------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_326"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 326</font></a>
 
 be, indeed, what is meant by the being Ignorance, in that case 'the world,' the whole mundane system, viz., Nature, Mind, &amp;c., would, also, in like manner, be Ignorance. And so, the whole mundane system being merely Ignorance, since the Ignorance would be annihilated by one man's knowledge, the mundane system would become invisible to others, also. Such is the import.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Vedánta theory self-contradictory.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_19"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 19.</font><a href="#pp_19"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If it [Ignorance,] were of <i>that</i> nature it would be something that had a commencement.<a name="page_326_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_326_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_19a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Or suppose it to be the case, that to be Ignorance means simply the being excludible by Knowledge, still such a thing could not have had an <i>eternal</i> existence in souls [as held by Vedántís (see <a href="#pp_15b">§ 15, <i>b</i>.</a>)], but must have had a commencement. For it is proved, by such recited
 
 <!------------------------page 327------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_327"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 327</font></a>
 
 texts as, 'Consisting of knowledge alone,'<a name="page_327_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_327_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a><a name="page_327_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_327_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> &amp;c., that, at the time of the universal dissolution, &amp;c., the soul consists of Knowledge alone. Such is the meaning. Therefore, it is settled that there is no other Ignorance, annihilable by Knowledge, than that stated in the <i>Yoga</i> system; and this is a property of the understanding only, not a property of the soul.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_19b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> By a cluster of [six] aphorisms,<a name="page_327_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_327_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> he clears up the primâ facie view of an opponent, in regard to that which was stated in the same Book [<a href="sak5.htm#pp_2">Book V., § 2</a>], that Nature's energizing is due to Merit:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 328------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_328"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 328</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Merit is undeniable.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_20"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 20.</font><a href="#pp_20"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no denying Merit; because of the diversity in the operations of Nature.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_20a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Merit is not to be denied on the ground of its being no object of sense; because it is inferred; since, otherwise, 'the diversity in the operations of Nature' [accommodating one person, and inconveniencing another,] would be unaccounted for: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_20b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states further proof, also:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Proofs of this.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_21"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 21.</font><a href="#pp_21"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [the existence of Merit,] is established by Scripture, by tokens, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_21a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He shows to be a fallacy the argument of the opponent, that Merit exists not, because of there being no sense-evidence of it:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 329------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_329"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 329</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Sense-evidence not the only kind of evidence.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_22"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 22.</font><a href="#pp_22"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is, here, no necessity; for there is room for other proofs.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_22a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: there is no necessity that a thing of which there is no mundane sense-evidence must be non-existent; because things are subject to <i>other</i> proofs.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_22b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He proves that there exists Demerit, as well as Merit:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Demerit as certain as Merit.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_23"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 23.</font><a href="#pp_23"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is thus, moreover, in both cases.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_23a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the proofs apply to Demerit, just as they do to Merit.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The proof of each the same.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_24"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 24.</font><a href="#pp_24"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If the existence [of Merit] be as of course, [because, otherwise, something would be unaccounted for], the same is the case in respect of both.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_24a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, <i>merit</i> is proved to exist by a natural consequence in this shape, viz., that, otherwise, an injunction
 
 <!------------------------page 330------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_330"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 330</font></a>
 
 would be unaccounted for; but there is none such in respect of <i>demerit</i>: so how can Scriptural or logical argument be extended to <i>demerit</i>? If any one says this, it is not so; since there is proof, in the shape of natural consequence, 'it is alike, in respect of both,' i.e., of both merit and demerit; because, otherwise, a <i>prohibitory</i> injunction, such as, 'He should not approach another's wife,' would be unaccounted for. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_24b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels the doubt, that, if Merit, &amp;c., be acknowledged [to exist], then, in consequence of souls' having properties, &amp;c., they must be liable to modification, &amp;c.:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Merit, &amp;c., inhere in what.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_25"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 25.</font><a href="#pp_25"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is of the internal organ<a name="page_330_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_330_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> [not of <i>soul</i>] that Merit, &amp;c., are the properties.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 331------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_331"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 331</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_25a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> In the expression '&amp;c.' are included all those that are stated, in the <i>Vaiśeshika</i> Institute, as peculiar qualities of soul.<a name="page_331_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_331_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_25b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> [To the objection, that the existence of an internal organ, as well as of the Qualities from which such might arise, is debarred by Scripture, be replies]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Qualities exist, though not in soul.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_26"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 26.</font><a href="#pp_26"><font color="RED">*</font></a> And of the Qualities, &amp;c., there is not absolute debarment.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_26a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The Qualities, viz., Purity, &amp;c., and their properties, viz., happiness, &amp;c., and their products, also, viz., Mind, &amp;c., are not denied essentially, but are denied only adjunctively in respect of soul; just as we deny that heat [in red-hot iron,] belongs to the iron.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_26b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In regard to the doubt, 'Why, again, do we not deny
 
 <!------------------------page 332------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_332"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 332</font></a>
 
 them an essence, as we do to what is meant by the words <i>sleep</i>, <i>wish</i>, &amp;c.?' he says:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The above thesis argued.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 27.</font><a href="#pp_27"><font color="RED">*</font></a> By a conjunction of the five members [of an argumentative statement] we discern [that] Happiness [exists].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Here, in order to get a particular subject of his assertion, he takes <i>happiness</i> alone, one portion of the matter in dispute, as a representative of the entire matter. But the better reading is, 'we discern [that] Happiness, &amp;c., [exist].' The five members of an argumentative statement are the Proposition, Reason, Example, Synthesis [of the two premises], and Conclusion; and, by the 'conjunction,' i.e., the combination, of these, all things, viz., Happiness, &amp;c., are proved to exist. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 333------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 333</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> And the employment [of the argument] is this:</p>
 
 <table align="center">
 
 <tr><td>(1)</td><td> Pleasure is real;</td></tr>
 <tr><td>(2)</td><td> Because it produces motion in something.</td></tr>
 <tr><td>(3)</td><td> Whatever produces motion in anything is real, as are sentient beings;</td></tr>
 <tr><td>(4)</td><td> And pleasure produces motion in things, in the way of horripilation, &amp;c.:</td></tr>
 <tr><td>(5)</td><td> Therefore, it is real.</td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27c"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>c</i>.</font> But then the <i>Chárváka</i>, next, doubts whether there be any evidence other than sense-evidence; since [he contends,] there is no truth in the assertion [of an inductive conclusion], that such and such is pervaded by such and such, &amp;c.<a name="page_333_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_333_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 334------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_334"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 334</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The validity of inference questioned.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_28"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 28.</font><a href="#pp_28"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not from once apprehending is a connexion established.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_28a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: from <i>once</i> apprehending concomitance [of a supposed token and the thing betokened], a 'connexion,' i.e., a pervadedness [or invariable attendedness of the token by the betokened,] is not established; and <i>frequency</i> [of the same apprehension] follows<a name="page_334_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_334_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> [the rule of the single apprehension; just as a thousand times nothing amount to nothing]. Therefore [argues the sceptic,] since the apprehending of an invariable attendedness is impossible, nothing can be established by <i>Inference</i>. [This] he clears up:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This point cleared up.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_29"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 29.</font><a href="#pp_29"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Pervadedness is a constant consociation of characters, in the case of both, or of one of them.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_29a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Consociation of characters,' i.e., consociation in the fact of being characters [or properties of something]; in short, concomitancy. And so we mean, that that concomitancy is 'pervadedness,' [furnishing solid ground for inference],
 
 <!------------------------page 335------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_335"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 335</font></a>
 
 which is invariably non-errant, whether in the case of 'both,' the predicate and the reason, or in the case of 'one of them,' the reason only. 'Of both' is mentioned with reference to the case of 'equal pervadedness': [e.g., every equilateral triangle is equiangular, and, conversely, every equiangular triangle is equilateral]. And the invariableness may be apprehended through an appropriate confutation [or <i>reductio ad absurdum</i> of the denial of it]; so that there is no impossibility in apprehending 'pervadedness,' [and of inferring on the strength of it]. Such is the import.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_29b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He declares that Pervadedness is not an additional principle, consisting, e.g., of some such power as is to be mentioned [in <a href="#pp_31">§ 31</a>]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Pervadedness not an additional principle.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_30"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 30.</font><a href="#pp_30"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [Pervadedness,] is not [as some think (see <a href="#pp_31">§ 31</a>),] an additional principle [over and above the twenty-five (<a href="sak1.htm#pp_61">Book I., § 61</a>)]; for it is unsuitable to postulate entities [<i>praeter rationem</i>].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 336------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_336"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 336</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_30a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Pervadedness' is not an entity other than a fixed consociation of characters; because it is unsuitable to suppose, further, some entity as the residence of what constitutes 'pervadedness.' But we consider that what constitutes 'pervadedness' belongs to extant things simply. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_30b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states the opinion of others:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A heterodox opinion regarding 'Pervadedness.'</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_31"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 31.</font><a href="#pp_31"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [But certain] teachers say that it [Pervadedness,] is [another principle, in addition to the twenty-five,] resulting from the power of the thing itself.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_31a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But other teachers assert that 'Pervadedness' is, positively, a separate principle, in the shape of a species of power, generated by the native power of the 'pervaded.' But [they continue,] 'Pervadedness' is not simply a power of the [pervaded] thing itself; else it would exist wherever the thing is, [which 'pervadedness' does <i>not</i> do]. For smoke, when it has gone to another place [than the point of its origination], is not attended by fire; and, by going into another place, that power is put an end to. Therefore [contend these teachers,] there is no over-extension in the
 
 <!------------------------page 337------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 337</font></a>
 
 above-stated definition; for, according to our doctrine, the smoke [which betokens fire] is to be specialized as that which is at the time of origination. Such is the import.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Opinion of Panchaśikha</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_32"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 32.</font><a href="#pp_32"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Panchaśikha<a name="page_337_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_337_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> says that it ['Pervadedness,'] is the possession of the power of the sustained.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_32a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: Panchaśikha holds that <i>pervadingness</i> is the power which consists in being the <i>sustainer</i>, and that 'Pervadedness'<a name="page_337_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_337_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> is the having the power which consists in being the sustained; for Intellect, and the rest, are treated as being pervaded [or invariably attended,] by Nature, &amp;c.; 
 
 <!------------------------page 338------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 338</font></a>
 
 [and this means that each product, in succession] is <i>sustained</i> by what precedes it in the series].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_32b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, why is a 'power of the sustained' postulated? Let 'Pervadedness' be simply an essential power of the <i>thing</i> pervaded. To this he [Panchaśikha,] replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Panchaśikha's reply to the objection.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_33"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 33.</font><a href="#pp_33"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The relation is not an essential power; for we should have [in that case,] a tautology.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_33a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But 'the relation,' viz., 'Pervadedness,' is not an essential power; for we should [thus] have a tautology; because, just as there is no difference between 'water-jar' and 'jar for water,' so, also, there is none in the case of 'Intellect' and 'what is Pervaded' [by Nature, of which Intellect consists]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 339------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 339</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_33b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He himself explains the 'Tautology:'</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The reason why.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_34"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 34.</font><a href="#pp_34"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Because we should find the distinction unmeaning; [as Intellect does not differ from Nature at all, except as does the sustained from the sustainer].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_34a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> This is almost explained by the preceding aphorism.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_34b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He [Panchaśikha,] mentions another objection:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A further reason.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_35"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 35.</font><a href="#pp_35"><font color="RED">*</font></a> And because it [Pervadedness,] would not be reconcilable in shoots, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_35a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Because shoots, &amp;c., are invariably attended [at their origination,] by trees, &amp;c. But this cannot be called simply an essential power [in the shoot]; because, since the essential power [that which belongs to the shoot <i>as being </i>a shoot,] does not depart, even in the case of an amputated shoot, we should, even then, find it attended [by the tree, which, however, no longer accompanies it]. Such is the sense. But the power [(see <a href="#pp_32">§ 32</a>), which consists in having the
 
 <!------------------------page 340------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_340"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 340</font></a>
 
 character] of the 'sustained' is destroyed at the time of amputation; so that there is no 'Pervadedness' <i>then</i>. Such is the import.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_35b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then what? Panchaśikha says that 'Pervadedness' is not a result of any <i>essential</i> power. Then, since smoke is not <i>sustained</i> by fire [see <a href="#pp_32">§ 32</a>, where he contends that 'sustainedness' is what really expresses <i>pervasion</i>], it would turn out that it [viz., smoke,] is not [as token of something that is betokened,] <i>accompanied</i> by fire. To this he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Reply, that this would prove too much.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_36"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 36.</font><a href="#pp_36"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Were it [thus] settled that it is a power of the 'sustained,' then, by the like argument, its dependence on an essential power, [as pretended by the heterodox teachers
 
 <!------------------------page 341------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_341"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 341</font></a>
 
 referred to in <a href="#pp_31">§ 31</a>, might be proved, also; and thus the argument proves nothing, since it proves too much].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_36a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: 'were it settled' that 'a power of the sustained' constitutes the fact of 'Pervadedness,' it would be really settled 'by the like argument.' i.e., by parity of reasoning, that the fact of 'Pervadedness' results from essential power, also, [<a href="#pp_31a">§ 31. <i>a</i>.</a>].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_36b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It was with a view to substantiate what was stated [in <a href="#pp_27">§ 27</a>], viz., that the Qualities, and the rest, are established [as realities,] by the employment of the five-membered [form of argumentative exposition], that he has repelled, by an exposition of 'Pervadedness,' the objection to Inference as evidence, [or as a means of attaining right notions].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_36c"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>c</i>.</font> Now, in order to establish the fact that <i>words</i>, of which the five-membered [exposition] consists, are generators of knowledge, the objection of others to a <i>word's</i> being a means of right knowledge,<a name="page_341_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_341_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the shape of [the objection
 
 <!------------------------page 342------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 342</font></a>
 
 of] its being inadequate, is disposed of, by means of an exposition of the powers, &amp;c., of words:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Sound and sense.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_37"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 37.</font><a href="#pp_37"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The connexion between word and meaning is the relation of expressed and expresser.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_37a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> To the 'meaning' belongs the power termed expressibleness; to the 'word,' the power termed expression: simply this is their 'connexion;' their interrelation, as it were.<a name="page_342_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_342_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a>
 
 <!------------------------page 343------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_343"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 343</font></a>
 
 From one's knowing this [connexion between a given word and meaning], the meaning is suggested [or raised in the mind,] by the word. Such is the import.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_37b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He mentions what things cause one to apprehend the powers [in question]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Sense of words how learned.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_38"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 38.</font><a href="#pp_38"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The connexion [between a word and its sense] is determined by three [means].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_38a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the connexion [just] mentioned [in <a href="#pp_37">§ 37</a>,] is apprehended by means of these three, viz., information from one competent [to tell us the meaning], the usage of the old man [whose orders to his sons we hear, and then observe what actions ensue, in consequence (see the <i>Sáhitya-darpaṉa</i>, § 11)], and application to the same thing which has a familiar name, [whence we gather the sense of the less familiar synonym].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 344------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 344</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Imperatives and predications.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_39"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 39.</font><a href="#pp_39"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no restriction to what is to be done; because we see it both ways.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_39a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: and there is no necessity that this apprehension of the powers [<a href="#pp_37">§ 37</a>,] should occur only in the case of 'something [directed] to be done;' because, in [the secular life and dealings of] the world, we see the usage of the old man, &amp;c., [<a href="#pp_38">§ 38</a>,] in regard to what is <i>not</i> to be done [being something already extant], also, as well as in regard to what is to be done.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Scriptural and secular senses of words the same.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_40"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 40.</font><a href="#pp_40"><font color="RED">*</font></a> He who is accomplished in the secular [connexion of words with meanings] can understand the sense of the Veda.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_40a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Here he entertains a doubt:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 345------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_345"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 345</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A doubt.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_41"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 41.</font><a href="#pp_41"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not by the three [means mentioned in <a href="#pp_38">§ 38</a>, objects some one, can the sense of the Veda be gathered]; because the Veda is superhuman, and what it means transcends the senses.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_41a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Of these he first repels the assertion, that what is meant [by the Veda] is something transcending the senses:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This cleared up.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_42"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 42.</font><a href="#pp_42"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not so [i.e., what is meant by the Veda is not something transcending the senses]; because sacrificings, &amp;c., are, in themselves, what constitutes merit, preeminently.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_42a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> What is asserted [in <a href="#pp_41">§ 41</a>,] is not the case; since sacrificings, gifts, &amp;c., in the shape, e.g., of the relinquishment of some thing for the sake of the gods, are really, in themselves, 'what constitutes merit,' i.e., what is enjoined by the Veda, 'preeminently,' i.e., because of their having preeminent fruit. And sacrificings, &amp;c., since they are in the shape of wishings, &amp;c., [of which we are perfectly conscious,] are not something transcending intuition. But 'what constitutes merit' [which the objector supposes to transcend intuition,] does not belong to something mysterious that resides in sacrificings
 
 <!------------------------page 346------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_346"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 346</font></a>
 
 &amp;c., whence what is enjoined in the Veda must be beyond intuition. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_42b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels also what was asserted [in <a href="#pp_41">§ 41</a>], viz., that, inasmuch as it [the Veda,] is superhuman, there can be no instruction by any competent person, [in regard to its import]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Knowledge of the Veda traditional.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_43"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 43.</font><a href="#pp_43"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The natural force [of the terms in the Veda] is ascertained through the conversancy [therewith of those who successively transmit the knowledge].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_43a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, still, how can there be apprehension of the sense of Vaidic terms, in the case of gods, fruits [of actions], &amp;c., which transcend sense? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 347------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_347"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 347</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Intelligibility of the Veda undeniable.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_44"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 44.</font><a href="#pp_44"><font color="RED">*</font></a> This really takes place; because they [viz., the words,] give rise to knowledge, in the case both of things adapted [to sense] and of things not [so] adapted.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_44a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He defines the, peculiarities which belong to words, just because this matter is connected with the question of the power of words to cause right knowledge:<a name="page_347_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_347_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Eternity of the Vedas denied.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_45"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 45.</font><a href="#pp_45"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The Vedas are not from eternity; for there is Scripture for their being a production.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_45a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Then are the Vedas the work of [the Supreme] Man? To this he replies, 'No':</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 348------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_348"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 348</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Lord not the author.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_46"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 46.</font><a href="#pp_46"><font color="RED">*</font></a> They [the Vedas,] are not the work of [the Supreme] Man; because there is no such thing as the [Supreme] Man, [whom you allude to as being, possibly,] their maker.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_46a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Supply, 'because we deny that, there is a <i>Lord</i>.'<a name="page_348_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_348_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> [This is] simple.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_46b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Adverting to the anticipation that there may be some other author, he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Who are not authors of the Vedas.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_47"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 47.</font><a href="#pp_47"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Since the liberated is unsuited [to the work, by his indifference], and the unliberated is so, [by his want of power] neither of these can be author of the Vedas].<a name="page_348_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_348_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_47a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, in that case, since they are not the work of [the Supreme] Man, it follows that they are eternal. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 349------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_349"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 349</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   An illustration.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_48"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 48.</font><a href="#pp_48"><font color="RED">*</font></a> As in the case of sprouts, &amp;c., their eternity does not follow from their not being the work of [any Supreme] Man.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_48a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> [This is] plain.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_48b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since sprouts, &amp;c., also, just like jars, &amp;c., are productions, we must infer that they are the work of [the Supreme] Man. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Plants denied to be works.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_49"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 49.</font><a href="#pp_49"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Were this the case with these, also, [i.e., if it were the case that vegetables were works], we should find a contradiction to experience, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_49a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It is seen, in the world, as an invariable fact,<a name="page_349_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_349_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> that whatever is the work of Man is produced by a <i>body</i>. This would be debarred, &amp;c., were the case as you contend; [for we see no <i>embodied</i> Supreme Man to whose handiwork the sprouts of the earth can be referred]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_49b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since they were uttered by the Primal
 
 <!------------------------page 350------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_350"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 350</font></a>
 
 Man, the Vedas, moreover, are, really, the work of [the Supreme] Man. To this he replies:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Only what is voluntary is a work.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_50"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 50.</font><a href="#pp_50"><font color="RED">*</font></a> That [only] is Man's work, in respect of which, even be it something invisible, an effort of understanding takes place.<a name="page_350_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_350_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_50a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> As in the case of what is visible, so, too, in the case of what is invisible, in respect of what thing there takes place 'an effort of understanding,' i.e., a consciousness that Thought preceded,<a name="page_350_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_350_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> that thing alone is spoken of as Man's work: such is the meaning. Thus it has been remarked
 
 <!------------------------page 351------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_351"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 351</font></a>
 
 that a thing is not Man's work merely through its having been uttered by Man; for no one speaks of the respiration during profound sleep as being Man's work, [or voluntary act]. But what need to speak of antecedence of Understanding? The Vedas, just like an expiration, proceed, of themselves, from the Self-existent, through the force of fate, wholly unpreceded by thought. Therefore, they are not [a Supreme] Man's work.<a name="page_351_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_351_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a>
 
 </p><p>
 
 <!------------------------page 352------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_352"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 352</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_50b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, in that case, since they are not preceded by a correct knowledge of the sense of the sentences,<a name="page_352_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_352_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> the Vedas, moreover, like the speech of a parrot, can convey no right knowledge.<a name="page_352_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_352_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Vedas their own evidence.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_51"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 51.</font><a href="#pp_51"><font color="RED">*</font></a> They are, spontaneously, conveyers of right knowledge, from the patentness of their own power [to instruct rightly].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_51a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the authoritativeness<a name="page_352_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_352_note_5"><font size="1">5</font></a> of the very whole of the Vedas is established, not by such a thing as its being based on the enouncer's knowledge of the truth, but quite 'spontaneously;' because, as for the Vedas' 'own,' i.e., natural, power of generating right knowledge, <i>thereof</i> we perceive the manifestation in the invocations<a name="page_352_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_352_note_6"><font size="1">6</font></a> [which produce the result promised], and in the Medical
 
 <!------------------------page 353------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_353"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 353</font></a>
 
 Scripture, [the following of which leads to cures], &amp;c. And so there is the aphorism of the <i>Nyáya</i> [Book II., § 681]: 'And [the fact of] its being a cause of right knowledge, like the validity of invocations, and the Medical Scripture,' &amp;c.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_51b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In regard to the proposition [laid down in <a href="#pp_26">§ 26</a>, viz.], 'And of the [existence of the] Qualities, &amp;c., there is not absolute debarment,' there was duly alleged, and developed [under <a href="#pp_27">§ 27</a>], one argument, viz., by the establishing the Existence of Happiness, &amp;c. Now he states another argument in respect of that [same proposition]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Cognition is evidence of existence.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_52"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 52.</font><a href="#pp_52"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no Cognition of what is no entity, as a man's horn.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 354------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_354"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 354</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_52a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Be it, moreover, that the existence of pleasure, &amp;c., is proved by the reasoning [under <a href="#pp_27">§ 27</a>]; it is proved by mere consciousness, also. Of pleasure, &amp;c., were they absolutely <i>nonentities</i>, even the <i>consciousness</i> could not be accounted for; because there is no cognition of a man's horn, and the like. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_52b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, [interposes the <i>Naiyáyika</i>,] if such be the case, let the Qualities, &amp;c., be quite absolutely <i>real</i>; and then, in the expression 'not <i>absolute</i> debarment' [in <a href="#pp_26">§ 26</a>], the word 'absolute' is [superfluous, and, hence,] unmeaning. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Qualities, &amp;c., not absolutely real.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_53"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 53.</font><a href="#pp_53"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is not of the <i>real</i> [that there is here cognizance]; because exclusion is seen [of the Qualities].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_53a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It is not proper [to say], moreover, that the cognizance of the Qualities, &amp;c., is that of the absolutely real; because we see that they are excluded [and not admitted
 
 <!------------------------page 355------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_355"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 355</font></a>
 
 to exist,] at the time of destruction [of the mundane system], &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_53b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, even on that showing, let the world be different both from real and from unreal; nevertheless, the demurring to absolute debarment [in <a href="#pp_26">§ 26</a>,] is untenable. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A Vedántic advance rejected.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_54"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 54.</font><a href="#pp_54"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is not of what cannot be [intelligibly] expressed [that there is cognizance]; because there exists no such thing.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_54a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> And there takes place, moreover, no cognizance of such [a thing] as is not to be expressed as either existing or not existing; 'because there. exists no such thing,' i.e., because nothing is known other than what exists, or what does not exist: such is the meaning. The import is, because it is proper to form suppositions only in accordance with what is seen.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 356------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_356"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 356</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_54b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, on that showing, do you really approve of [the <i>Nyáya</i> notion of] 'cognizing otherwise,' [or our fancying that nature to belong to one, which belongs to another]? He replies, 'No':</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A Nyáya view rejected.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_55"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 55.</font><a href="#pp_55"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no such thing as cognizing otherwise [or cognizing that as belonging to one, which belongs to another]; because your own proposition is self-destructive.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_55a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> This, also, is not proper [to be said], viz., that one thing appears under the character of another thing [e.g., a rope, under the character of a serpent, for which it may be mistaken, in the dusk]; 'because your own proposition is self-destructive.'<a name="page_356_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_356_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> Of another nature [e.g., snakehood], in a different thing [e.g., a rope], <i>equivalence to a man's horn</i>, is [what is virtually] expressed by the word 'otherwise' [than the truth; both a man's horn, and the presence of snakehood in a rope mistaken for a snake, being, alike, otherwise than real]; and [yet] its <i>cognition</i> [thus] <i>otherwise</i> is asserted, [as if <i>that</i> could be cognized which is equivalent to what can <i>not</i> be cognized]: hence your own
 
 <!------------------------page 357------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_357"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 357</font></a>
 
 proposition is self-destructive. For even those who contend for 'cognizing otherwise' [as one mode of cognition,] declare that the cognition of what <i>does not exist</i> is impossible. Such is the meaning.<a name="page_357_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_357_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a>
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_55b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Expounding what he had said above, [in <a href="#pp_26">§ 26</a>,] 'not absolute debarment,' he sums up his doctrine:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Summing up.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_56"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 56.</font><a href="#pp_56"><font color="RED">*</font></a> They [the Qualities,] are cognized rightly or wrongly, through their being denied and not denied [appropriately or otherwise].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_56a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> All the Qualities, &amp;c., 'are cognized rightly and
 
 <!------------------------page 358------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_358"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 358</font></a>
 
 wrongly.' How? 'Through their being denied and not denied.' There is <i>non-denial</i>, as far as regards their existing at all; because all things [and things are made up of the Qualities,] are eternal. But there is denial, <i>relatively</i>, in Soul, of all things; just as is the case with the imaginary silver, for example, in a pearl-oyster, &amp;c., or with the redness, &amp;c., in crystal, &amp;c., [which has no redness, without its following that redness, altogether and everywhere, is non-existent].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_56b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> This investigation is concluded. Now the consideration of Words, it having presented itself in this connexion, is taken in hand incidentally, at the end; [the Sánkhya not allowing to Testimony a coordinate rank with Sense and Inference]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Yoga theory of speech rejected.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_57"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 57.</font><a href="#pp_57"><font color="RED">*</font></a> A word does not consist of [what the <i>Yogas</i> call] the 'expresser' (<i>sphoṭa</i>); by reason both of cognizance [which would disprove the existence of such imaginary
 
 <!------------------------page 359------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_359"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 359</font></a>
 
 thing,] and of non-cognizance, [which would, in like manner, disprove it].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_57a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It is held, by the followers of the <i>Yoga</i>, that there exists, in distinction from the several letters, an indivisible [unit, the] word, such as 'jar,' &amp;c., [which they call] the 'expression;'<a name="page_359_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_359_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> just as there is a jar, or the like, possessing parts, which is something else than the parts, viz., the shell-shaped neck, &amp;c.; and that particular sound, termed a word, is called the 'expresser,' because of its making apparent the meaning: such a word [we Sánkhyas assert, in opposition to the <i>Yogas</i>,] is without evidence [of its existence]. Why? 'By reason both of cognizance and of non-cognizance,' [as thus]: Pray, is that word [which you choose to call the 'expression,'] cognized, or not? On the former alternative, what need of that idle thing, [the supposed 'expression'? For,] by what collection of letters, distinguished by a particular succession, this ['expression'] is manifested, let <i>that</i> be what acquaints us with the meaning. But, on the latter alternative, [viz., that it is not cognized], the power of acquainting us with a meaning does not belong to an 'expression' which is not cognized. Therefore, the hypothesis of an 'expresser' is useless. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 360------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_360"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 360</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_57b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> The eternity of the Vedas was contradicted<a name="page_360_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_360_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> before, [under <a href="#pp_45">§ 45</a>]. Now he contradicts also the eternity of <i>letters</i>:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The eternity of letters denied.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_58"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 58.</font><a href="#pp_58"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Sound is not eternal; because we perceive it to be made.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_58a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It is not proper [to say, as the Mímánsakas say], that letters are eternal, on the strength of our recognizing, e.g., that 'This is that same G'; for they are proved to be non-eternal, by the cognition, e.g., that '[the sound of] G has been produced': such is the meaning. And the <i>recognition</i>
 
 <!------------------------page 361------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_361"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 361</font></a>
 
 has reference to the <i>homogeneousness</i> with that [one which had been previously heard]; for, otherwise, it would turn out that a <i>jar</i>, or the like, is eternal, inasmuch as it is <i>recognized</i>.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_58b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He ponders a doubt:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A doubt.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_59"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 59.</font><a href="#pp_59"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [Suppose that] there is [in the case of sounds,] the manifestation of something whose existence was previously settled; as [the manifestation] of a [preexistent] jar by a lamp.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_59a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then [some one may say], of Sound, whose existence was 'previously settled,' the manifestation, through noise, &amp;c., <i>that</i> alone is the object in the cognition of its <i>production</i>, [which you speak of in <a href="#pp_58">§ 58</a>]. An example of manifestation [of a thing previously existing] is, 'as of a jar by a lamp.'</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 362------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_362"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 362</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_59b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels this:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The doubt disposed of.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_60"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 60.</font><a href="#pp_60"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If the dogma of products' being real [is accepted by you], then this is a proving of the already proved.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_60a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> If you say that 'manifestation' means the taking of a present condition by means of rejecting an unarrived [or future,] condition, then this is our dogma of the reality of products [<a href="sak1.htm#pp_115">Book I., § 115</a>]; and <i>such</i> an eternity belongs to <i>all</i> products, [not specially to Sound]; so that you are proving the already proved [or conceded]: such is the meaning. And, if 'manifestation' is asserted to be just in the shape of the cognition of what is presently real, then we should find [on your theory,] that jars, &amp;c., also, are eternal; because it would be proper [on that theory,] that the object in the perception of production, by the operation of the causes [the potter, &amp;c.], should be that of <i>knowledge</i> only, as in the case of words, &amp;c., and also in the case of jars, &amp;c.; [for the jar is <i>shown</i> by the lamp, not made by it]. Such is the import.<a name="page_362_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_362_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 363------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_363"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 363</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_60b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> An objection to the non-duality of Soul, not previously mentioned, is to be adduced; therefore the refutation of the non-duality of Soul is recommenced, [having been already handled under <a href="sak1.htm#pp_149">Book I., § 149</a>].</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Non-duality of Soul denied on grounds of Inference.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_61"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 61.</font><a href="#pp_61"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Non-duality of Soul<a name="page_363_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_363_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> is not; for its distinctions are cognized through signs.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_61a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: because it is proved to be really different [in different persons], by the sign that <i>one</i> quits Nature [or escapes from the mundane condition], while another not does quit it, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_61b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But, he tells us, there is even sense-evidence destructive of the non-distinction of Soul from things [that are] non-Soul, asserted in the Scriptural texts, 'All this is Soul only,'<a name="page_363_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_363_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> 'All this is Brahma only:'<a name="page_363_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_363_note_6"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 364------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_364"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 364</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Non-duality denied on grounds of Sense.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_62"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 62.</font><a href="#pp_62"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Moreover, there is not [non-distinction of Soul] from non-Soul; because this is disproved by sense-evidence.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_62a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: moreover, there is <i>not</i> a non-distinction between the non-Soul, i.e., the aggregate of the experienceable, and Soul; because this is excluded also by sense-evidence, [as well as by signs, (<a href="#pp_61">§ 61</a>)]; because, if Soul were not other than the whole perceptible, it would also not be different from a jar and a web; since the jar, e.g., would not be other than the web, which [by hypothesis,] is not other than the Soul: and <i>this</i> is excluded by sense-evidence, which constrains us to apprehend a distinction [between a jar and a web].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_62b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In order to clear the minds of learners, he illustrates this point, though already established:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The reasons combined.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_63"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 63.</font><a href="#pp_63"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not between the two [Soul and non-Soul, is there non-difference]; for that same [couple of reasons].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_63a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Between the two,' i.e., between Soul and non-Soul, the two together, also, there is not an absolute non-difference;
 
 <!------------------------page 365------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_365"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 365</font></a>
 
 for the couple of reaaons [given in <a href="#pp_61">§ 61</a> and <a href="#pp_62">§ 62</a>]: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_63b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, in that case, what is the drift of such Scriptural texts as, '[All] this is Soul only?' To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Scripture accomodates itself to human frailty of understanding.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_64"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 64.</font><a href="#pp_64"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There it is for the sake of something else, in respect of the undiscriminating.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_64a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: 'in respect of the undiscriminating,' with reference to undiscriminating persons, in the case or non-difference [between Soul and non-Soul, apparently asserted in Scripture], it is 'there for the sake of something else;' i.e., the observation<a name="page_365_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_365_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> is [designed to be] provocative of worship. For, in the secular world, through want of discrimination, body and the embodied, the experienced and the experiencer, are regarded as indifferent;
 
 <!------------------------page 366------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_366"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 366</font></a>
 
 [and Scripture humours the worldling's delusion, with a view to eventually getting him out of it].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_64b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He declares, that, according to the asserters of Non-duality [of soul], there can be no material cause of the world, either:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Vedánta system supplies no material for the world.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_65"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 65.</font><a href="#pp_65"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Neither soul, nor Ignorance,<a name="page_366_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_366_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> nor both, can be the material cause of the world; because of the solitariness of [Soul].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_65a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The soul alone, or Ignorance lodged in the soul, or both together, like a pair of jar-halves [conjoined in the formation of a jar], cannot be the material of the world; 'because of the <i>solitariness</i>' of Soul. For things undergo alteration only through that particular conjunction
 
 <!------------------------page 367------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 367</font></a>
 
 which is called 'association;' hence the [ever] solitary Soul, without a second, since it is not associated, cannot serve as a material cause. Nor can it do so by means of [association with] Ignorance, either; because the conjunction of Ignorance has been already excluded by the fact of <i>solitariness</i>. Moreover, that the two together should be the material is impossible, even as it is that either, severally, should be the material; simply 'because of the solitariness.' Such is the meaning. And, if you choose that Ignorance should subsist as a substance located in the soul, as the air in the heavens, then there is an abandonment of the non-duality of Soul, [for which you Vedántís contend].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_65b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He himself [in <a href="sak1.htm#pp_145">Book I., § 145</a>] decided that the soul consists of light, [or knowledge]. In regard to this, he repels the primâ facie view, founded on the text, 'Brahma
 
 <!------------------------page 368------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_368"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 368</font></a>
 
 is reality, knowledge, and joy,'<a name="page_368_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_368_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> that the essence of the soul is <i>joy</i>, also:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul not joy and knowledge, both.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_66"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 66.</font><a href="#pp_66"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The two natures, joy and knowledge do not belong to <i>one</i>; because the two are different.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_66a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> A single subject has not the nature both of joy and of intelligence; because, since pleasure is not experienced at the time of knowing pain, pleasure and knowledge are different: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_66b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, in that case, what becomes of the Scripture, that it [Soul,] consists of joy? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 369------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_369"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 369</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A Vedánta term explained away.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_67"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 67.</font><a href="#pp_67"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Metaphorical [is the word joy, in the sense] of the cessation of pain.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_67a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the word 'joy,' in the Scriptural expression which means, really, the cessation of pain, is metaphorical. This is stated in [the maxim], 'Pleasure is the departure of both pain and pleasure.'</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_67b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states the cause of this metaphorical employment:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Why the term was used in a sense not literal.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_68"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 68.</font><a href="#pp_68"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is [as] a <i>laudation</i> of emancipation, for the sake of the dull.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_68a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the Scripture, as an incitement to 'the dull,' i.e., the ignorant, lauds, as if it were <i>joy</i>, the emancipation, consisting in the cessation of pain, which [cessation] is the essence of the soul;<a name="page_369_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_369_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> [for the soul is such joy as consists of the absence of pain].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_68b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In order to manifest immediately the origin, already
 
 <!------------------------page 370------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_370"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 370</font></a>
 
 declared,<a name="page_370_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_370_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the internal organ, he repels the primâ facie view, that the Mind is all-pervading.
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Mind not all-pervading.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_69"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 69.</font><a href="#pp_69"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The Mind is not all-pervading; because it is an instrument, and because it is, moreover, an <i>organ</i>.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_69a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The Mind, meaning the totality of the internal instruments,<a name="page_370_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_370_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> is not all-pervading; for it is an instrument, as an axe, or the like, is. The word 'and' [literally, 'or,' in the Aphorism,] implies a distributive alternative, [not an optional one]. The meaning is this, that, [while the whole of the internal instruments are <i>instruments</i>,] the particular internal instrument, the third<a name="page_370_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_370_note_5"><font size="1">5</font></a> [the Mind, <i>manas</i><a name="page_370_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_370_note_6"><font size="1">6</font></a>].
 
 <!------------------------page 371------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_371"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 371</font></a>
 
 is not all-pervading; because <i>it</i> is, moreover, an <i>organ</i>.<a name="page_371_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_371_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> But knowledge, &amp;c., pervading the body, are demonstrable as only of medium extent, [neither infinite nor atomic].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_69b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Here, there being a doubt whether this be convincing, he propounds an appropriate confutation:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Proof of this.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_70"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 70.</font><a href="#pp_70"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [The Mind is not all-pervading]; for it is moveable; since there is Scripture regarding the motion.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_70a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say; since, inasmuch as there is Scripture regarding the <i>going</i> of the Soul [which, being all-pervading, cannot <i>go</i>] into another world, it being settled that it is its adjunct, the internal organ, that is movable, [see <a href="sak1.htm#pp_51">Book I., § 51]</a>, it cannot be all-pervading.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 372------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_372"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 372</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_70b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In order to prove that it is a product, he repels also the opinion that the Mind is without parts:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Mind has no parts.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_71"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 71.</font><a href="#pp_71"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Like a jar, it [the Mind,] is not without parts; because it comes in contact therewith, [i.e., with several Senses, simultaneously].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_71a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The word 'therewith' refers to 'organ,' which occurs in a preceding aphorism, [<a href="#pp_69">§ 69</a>]. The Mind is not without parts; 'because it comes in contact,' simultaneously, with several sense-organs. But, 'like a jar,' it is of medium size, [neither infinite nor atomic], and consists of parts. Such is the meaning. And it is to be understood that the internal organ, when in the state of a <i>cause</i>, [and not modified and expanded, e.g., into knowledge, which is its product,] <i>is</i>, indeed, atomic.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 373------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_373"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 373</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_71b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He demurs to the eternity of Mind, Time, &amp;c:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Eternity belongs to what.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_72"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 72.</font><a href="#pp_72"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Everything except Nature and Soul is uneternal.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_72a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> [This is] plain. And the Mind,<a name="page_373_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_373_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> the Ether, &amp;c., when in the state of <i>cause</i>, [not developed into product], are called <i>Nature</i>, and not Intellect,<a name="page_373_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_373_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> &amp;c., by reason of the absence of the special properties, viz., judgment,<a name="page_373_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_373_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_72b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, according to such Scriptural texts as, 'He should know Illusion to be Nature, and him in whom is Illusion to be the great Lord, and this whole world to be pervaded by portions of him,'<a name="page_373_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_373_note_6"><font size="1">6</font></a> since Soul and Nature,
 
 <!------------------------page 374------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_374"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 374</font></a>
 
 also, are made up of parts, they must be uneternal. To this he replies:
 
 </p><p>
 
 <!------------------------page 375------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_375"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 375</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul and Nature not made up of parts.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_73"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 73.</font><a href="#pp_73"><font color="RED">*</font></a> No parts [from the presence of which in the discerptible, one might infer destructibility,] are found in the Experiencer; for there is Scripture for its being without parts.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_73a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Parts are not appropriate to 'the Experiencer,' i.e., to Soul, or to Nature; for there is Scripture for their being without parts; that is to say, because of such [texts] as, 'Without parts, motioniess, quiescent, unobjectionable, passionless.'<a name="page_375_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_375_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_73b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It has been stated [in <a href="sak1.htm#pp_1">Book I., § 1</a>,] that Emancipation is the cessation of pain. In order to corroborate this, he then repels the doctrines of others, in regard to Emancipation:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A view of Emancipation disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_74"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 74.</font><a href="#pp_74"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Emancipation is not a manifestation of joy; because there
 
 <!------------------------page 376------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_376"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 376</font></a>
 
 are no <i>properties</i> [in Soul, as, e.g., in the shape of joy].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_74a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> There belongs to Soul no <i>property</i> in the shape of joy, or in the shape of manifestation; and the <i>essence</i> [of Soul] is quite eternal, and, therefore, not something to be produced by means: therefore, Emancipation is not a manifestation of joy: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Second view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_75"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 75.</font><a href="#pp_75"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor, in like manner, is it [Emancipation,] the destruction of special qualities.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_75a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Emancipation is, moreover, not the destruction of all special qualities, 'In like manner.' Because there are absolutely <i>no</i> properties [in Soul, (see <a href="#pp_74">§ 74</a>)]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A third view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_76"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 76.</font><a href="#pp_76"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor is it [Emancipation,] any particular going of that [Soul,] which is motionless.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_76a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Moreover, emancipation is not a going to the world
 
 <!------------------------page 377------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_377"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 377</font></a>
 
 of Brahmá;<a name="page_377_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_377_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> because the Soul, since it is motionless, does not <i>go</i>.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A fourth view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_77"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 77.</font><a href="#pp_77"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor is it [Emancipation,] the destruction of the influence of [intellectual] forms, by reason of the faults of momentariness, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_77a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The meaning is, that also the doctrine of the Nihilist, that the Soul consists merely of momentary knowledge, that Bondage is the modifying thereof by objects, and that emancipation is the destruction of the influence thereof called Memory,<a name="page_377_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_377_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> is inadmissible; because, by reason of the faults of <i>momentariness</i>, &amp;c., [such] emancipation is not the Soul's aim.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_77b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He censures another [conception of] emancipation of the Nihilist's:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 378------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_378"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 378</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A fifth view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_78"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 78.</font><a href="#pp_78"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor is it [Emancipation,] destruction of all; for this has, among other things, the fault of <i>not</i> being the soul's aim.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_78a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Likewise, the entire destruction of the Soul, which consists of knowledge, is not emancipation; because, among other things, we do not see, in the world, that the annihilation of the soul is the soul's aim: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A sixth view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_79"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 79.</font><a href="#pp_79"><font color="RED">*</font></a> So, too, the Void.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_79a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The annihilation of the whole universe, consisting of cognition and the cognizable, is, thus, also, not emancipation; because Soul's aim is not effected by Soul's annihilation: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A seventh view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_80"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 80.</font><a href="#pp_80"><font color="RED">*</font></a> And conjunctions terminate in separations; therefore, it [Emancipation,] is not the acquisition of lands, &amp;c., either.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 379------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_379"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 379</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_80a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> From its perishableness, possessorship is not Emancipation.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   An eighth view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_81"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 81.</font><a href="#pp_81"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor is it [Emancipation,] conjunction of a Part with the Whole.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_81a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Emancipation is not absorption of 'a Part,' i.e., the Soul, into 'the Whole,' i.e., that of which it is [on the view in question,] a part, viz., the Supreme Soul; for the reason assigned [in <a href="#pp_80">§ 80</a>], viz., 'conjunctions terminate in separations,' and because we do not admit a Lord [<a href="sak1.htm#pp_92">Book I., § 92</a>], and because, thus, self-dissolution is not Soul's aim: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 380------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_380"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 380</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A ninth view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_82"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 82.</font><a href="#pp_82"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor is it [Emancipation], moreover, conjunction with the [power of] becoming as small as an atom, &amp;c.; since, as is the case with other conjunctions, the destruction of this must necessarily take place.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_82a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Moreover, conjunction with superhuman power, e.g., the assuming the size of an atom, is not Emancipation; because, just as is the case with connexions with other superhuman powers, the destruction of this, also, follows, of necessity: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A tenth view disputed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_83"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 83.</font><a href="#pp_83"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor, just as in that case, is it [Emancipation], moreover, conjunction with the rank of Indra, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_83a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Nor is the attainment of the superhuman power of Indra, &amp;c., Emancipation,—just as is the case with other superhuman powers [such as assuming atomic bulk];—by reason of perishableness: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 381------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_381"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 381</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_83b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels the objection of an opponent to what has been stated [in <a href="sak1.htm#pp_61">Book I., § 61</a>], that the Organs are products of Self-consciousness:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The organs whence.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_84"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 84.</font><a href="#pp_84"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The Organs are not formed of the Elements [as the <i>Naiyáyikas</i> assert]; because there is Scripture for their being derived from Self-consciousness.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_84a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> With advertence to the opinion that <i>Power</i>, &amp;c., also, are principles, he repels the determination of categories [insisted upon by the various sects] of his opponents, and the notion that Emancipation comes through a knowledge of these [categories] merely:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 382------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_382"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 382</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The categories of the Vaiśeshika objected to.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_85"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 85.</font><a href="#pp_85"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The rule of six categories is not [the correct one]; nor does Emancipation result from acquaintance therewith, [as the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i> maintain].</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   And those of the Nyáya, &amp;c.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_86"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 86.</font><a href="#pp_86"><font color="RED">*</font></a> So, too, is it in the case of the sixteen [categories of the <i>Nyáya</i>], &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_86a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> In order to establish, what has been already stated [in <a href="sak1.htm#pp_62">Book I., § 62</a>], that the five Elements are <i>products</i>, he rejects the eternity of the Earthy and other Atoms, which is held by the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i> and others:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The eternity of Atoms unscriptural.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_87"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 87.</font><a href="#pp_87"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [The five Elements being <i>products</i>, as declared in <a href="sak1.htm#pp_61">Book I., § 61</a>], Atoms are not eternal, [as alleged in the <i>Nyáya</i>]; for there is Scripture for their being products.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_87a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Although that text of Scripture is not seen by us, because it has disappeared, in the lapse of time, &amp;c., yet, it is to be inferred from the words of teachers, and from the tradition of Manu, [Ch. I., v. 27].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 383------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_383"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 383</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_87b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, how can an Atom, which is without parts, be a product? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Scripture decisive of the question.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_88"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 88.</font><a href="#pp_88"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Since it is a product, it is not without parts.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_88a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: since the fact, established by Scripture, of their being products, cannot be otherwise accounted for, the [so-called] Atoms of Earth, &amp;c., are <i>not</i> without parts.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_88b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels the objection of the Nihilist, that direct cognition of Nature, or of Soul, is impossible; because [forsooth,] the cause of a thing's being directly cognizable is colour:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 384------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_384"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 384</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A cavil disposed of.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_89"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 89.</font><a href="#pp_89"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no necessity that direct cognition should have <i>colour</i> as its cause.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_89a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It is no rule, that to be directly cognizible should result from colour only, [or other object of sense], as the cause; because direct cognition may result from Merit, &amp;c., [viz., mystical practices, and so forth], also: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_89b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Well, if that be the case, pray is the <i>dimension</i> of an Atom a reality, or not? With reference to this, he decides the question of dimension, [as follows]:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 385------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_385"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 385</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Dimension of what kinds.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_90"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 90.</font><a href="#pp_90"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There are not four varieties of dimension; because those can be accounted for by two.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_90a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> There are not four kinds of dimension, viz., small, great, long, and short; but there are only two sorts. 'Because those can be accounted for by two:' that is to say, the four varieties can be accounted for by merely two, the atomic [or <i>positively</i> small,] and the great. Such is the meaning. For the short and the long are merely subordinate kinds of the dimension called great; else we should have, e.g., no end of dimensions, in the shape of the crooked, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_90b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He rebuts the Nihilist's denial of genera, [as follows]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Genus proved by recognition.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_91"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 91.</font><a href="#pp_91"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Though these [individuals] be uneternal, recognition, as being associated with constancy, is of genus.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 386------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_386"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 386</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_91a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Hence, he says, it is not proper to deny [the existence] of genus:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   And not to be denied.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_92"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 92.</font><a href="#pp_92"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Therefore it [genus,] is not to be denied.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_92a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then [it may be said], recognition is to be accounted for simply by a <i>non-existence</i>, in the shape of the exclusion of what is not the thing [recognized]: and let <i>this</i> be what is meant by the word 'genus.' To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Genus positive, not negative.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_93"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 93.</font><a href="#pp_93"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [genus,] does not consist in <i>exclusion</i> of something else; because it is cognized as an entity.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_93a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: genus does not consist in exclusion [of something else]; because 'This is that same' is the cognition of something <i>positive</i>; for, otherwise, the only thing cognized would be, 'This is not a non-jar.'</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 387------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_387"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 387</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_93b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But still, recognition may be caused by <i>likeness</i>. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Likewise not a distinct principle.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_94"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 94.</font><a href="#pp_94"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Likeness is not a separate principle; for it is directly apprehended, [as one manifestation of Community].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_94a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: likeness is nothing other than sameness in many parts, &amp;c.; for it is directly apprehended as consisting in sameness; [the <i>likeness</i> of a fair face to the moon, e.g., consisting in the <i>sameness</i> of the pleasurable feeling, &amp;c., occasioned by the sight of either].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_94b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> The conjecture, 'But then, let likeness be really an inherent power, and not [a modified aspect of] Community,' he repels:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 388------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_388"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 388</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nor a peculiar power.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_95"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 95.</font><a href="#pp_95"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor is it [likeness,] a manifestation of [something's] own power; because the apprehension of it is different.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_95a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Moreover, likeness is not the manifestation of a particular natural power of a thing; because the apprehension of likeness is different from the apprehension of power. For the cognition of a power is not dependent on the cognition of another thing; the cognition of likeness, on the other hand, is dependent on the cognition of a correlative,<a name="page_388_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_388_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> as is the case with the cognition of a non-existence; so that the two conceptions are heterogeneous. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_95b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But still, let the likeness among individual jars, &amp;c., be merely that they have [all alike,] the name, e.g., of jar. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 389------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_389"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 389</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nor the relation between names and things.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_96"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 96.</font><a href="#pp_96"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nor, moreover, is it [likeness,] the connexion between name and named.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_96a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Because even he who does not know the connexion between a name and the thing named may cognize a likeness, [e.g., between two jars].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_96b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Moreover:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   How it cannot be so.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_97"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 97.</font><a href="#pp_97"><font color="RED">*</font></a> That connexion [viz., between name and named,] is not eternal; since both [the correlatives] are uneternal.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_97a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Since both the name and the named are uneternal, the relation between them, also, is not eternal. How, then, can there be, through <i>that</i>, the likeness of a departed thing in a thing present? Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_97b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, though the correlatives be uneternal, let
 
 <!------------------------page 390------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_390"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 390</font></a>
 
 the <i>relation</i> be eternal. What is to hinder <i>this</i>? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Another suggestion repelled.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_98"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 98.</font><a href="#pp_98"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The connexion is not so [not eternal], for this reason, viz., because this is debarred by the evidence which acquaints us with the thing; [i.e., the supposition is inconsistent with the definition of the term].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_98a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> <i>Connexion</i> is proved only where <i>disjunction</i> incidentally subsists; because, otherwise, there is no room for the supposition of <i>connexion</i>; the case being accounted for,—as will be explained,—simply by <i>the natural state of the matter</i>. And this incidental disjunction is impossible, if connexion be eternal. Therefore, connexion is not eternal; for this is debarred by the very evidence that acquaints us with Connexion. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 391------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_391"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 391</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_98b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But, on this showing, there could be no such thing as the eternal [connexion called] Coinherence<a name="page_391_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_391_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> between those two eternals, a Quality and the thing qualified; [which Coinherence, or intimate relation, is one of the categories of the <i>Nyáya</i>]. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Category of Intimate Relation rejected.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_99"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 99.</font><a href="#pp_99"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no [such thing as] Coinherence, [such as the Naiyáyikas insist upon]; for there is no evidence [for it].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_99a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then [it may be said], the evidence of it is, the perception that something is qualified [or conjoined with a quality which inheres in it], and the unaccountableness, otherwise, of the cognition of something as qualified. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 392------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_392"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 392</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This argued.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_100"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 100.</font><a href="#pp_100"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Neither perception nor inference [is evidence for the existence of Coinherence]; since, as regards both alike, the case is otherwise disposed of.<a name="page_392_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_392_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_100a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Since, 'as regards both alike,' i.e., the perception of qualifiedness, and the inferring of it, 'the case is otherwise disposed of;'<a name="page_392_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_392_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> viz., simply by <i>the natural state</i> [of the thing and its qualities], neither of the two is evidence for [the imaginary category called] Coinherence: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_100b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It is a tenet, that, from the agitation of Nature the conjunction of Nature and soul takes place, and thence results creation. In regard to that, there is this objection of the atheists, that 'Nothing whatever possesses the action called agitation; everything is momentary; where
 
 <!------------------------page 393------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_393"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 393</font></a>
 
 it arises, even there it perishes; therefore, no motion is proved to be inferrible from conjunction [of anything] with another place;' [the fruit, for instance, which appears to reach the ground not being that fruit, any longer existent, which appeared to drop from the tree]. To this he replies:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Motion is a matter of perception.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_101"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 101.</font><a href="#pp_101"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Motion is not a matter of inference; for he who stands very near has, indeed, direct cognition both of it and of what it belongs to.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_101a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> In Book Second the different opinions were merely mentioned, that the Body is formed of five elements, and so forth; but no particular one was considered. In regard to this question, he denies the view of an opponent:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 394------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_394"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 394</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Body is of Earth only.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_102"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 102.</font><a href="#pp_102"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The Body does not consist of <i>five</i> elements; because many [heterogeneous things] are unsuitable as the material.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_102a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He will mention, that, whilst there is but one material, the material of every Body is earth:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   There is a Subtile as well as a Gross, Body.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_103"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 103.</font><a href="#pp_103"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [the Body,] is not, necessarily, the Gross one; for there is, also, the vehicular [transmigrating or Subtile] one.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_103a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Senses, [the organ of vision, for example,] distinct from the eye-balls, have been already mentioned. In order to substantiate this [point], he refutes the opinion, that the senses reveal what they do not reach to:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 395------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_395"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 395</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Connexion between sense and object.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_104"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 104.</font><a href="#pp_104"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The senses do not reveal what they do not reach to; because of their not reaching, or because [else,] they might reach everything.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_104a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The senses do not reveal things unconnected with them. 'Because of their not reaching.' For we do not see that lamps, or the like, reveal what they do not reach to; and because, if they were to reveal what they do not reach to, we should find them revealing <i>all</i> things, viz., those intercepted, and the like. Such is the meaning. Therefore there is an organ, other than the eye-ball, for the sake of connexion with the distant sun, &amp;c. Such is the import. And the instruments reveal the objects simply by delivering the object to the soul,—for they are, themselves, unintelligent;—as a mirror reveals the face. Or [in other words], their revealing an object is simply their taking up an image of the object.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_104b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He repels the conjecture: But then, in that case, the opinion [of the Naiyáyikas,] that the sight is luminous
 
 <!------------------------page 396------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_396"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 396</font></a>
 
 is quite right; for we see Light alone glide rapidly to a distance, in the form of rays:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Sight not formed of Light.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_105"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 105.</font><a href="#pp_105"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not because Light glides [and the Sight does so, too,] is the Sight luminous [or formed of Light]; because the thing is accounted for by [the theory of] modifications, [to be now explained].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_105a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The Sight is not to be asserted to be luminous, on the ground that light is seen to glide. Why? Because, just as in the case of the vital air, where there is no luminosity, the gliding forth can be accounted for through a kind of modification. Such is the meaning. For, as the vital air, without having at all parted from the body, glides out ever so far from the end of the nose, under the modification called breathing, [and thus smells a distant flower], just so the Sight, though a non-luminous substance, without, indeed, quitting [connexion with] the body, all in a moment will dart off [like the protruded feeler of a polyp,] to a distant object, such as the sun, by means of the species of change called modification.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 397------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_397"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 397</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_105b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But what is the proof that there is any such modification? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Proof of his theory of vision, that it accounts for the phenomena.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_106"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 106.</font><a href="#pp_106"><font color="RED">*</font></a> By the sign of the display of the attained object the [existence of the] modification [which could alone account for that display,] is proved.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_106a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He shows [us] the nature of the modification, to account for the going, though without parting from the Body:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Of the theory, further.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_107"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 107.</font><a href="#pp_107"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The 'modification' is another principle than a fragment, or
 
 <!------------------------page 398------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_398"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 398</font></a>
 
 a quality, [of the Sight, or other sense]; because it is for the sake of <i>connexion</i> that it glides forth.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_107a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The modification is not a <i>fragment</i> of the Sight, or other sense, [serving as] the cause of the revealing of objects,—a part disjoined like a spark,—or a <i>quality</i>, like, e.g., Colour; but the modification, whilst a portion thereof, is something else than a fragment, or a quality. For, if there were disruption, connexion of the sun, &amp;c., with the Sight would not, through it, take place; and, if it were a quality, the motion called 'gliding forth' would be unaccountable; [for a quality cannot move by itself]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_107b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But, if, thus, the 'modifications' are <i>substances</i>, how is [the term] 'modification' applied to the <i>qualities</i> of intellect, in the shape of Desire, &amp;c.? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 399------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_399"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 399</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   'Modifications' may be qualities, as well as substances.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_108"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 108.</font><a href="#pp_108"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [the term 'modification,'] is not confined to substances; because it is etymological, [not technical, and applies, etymologically, to a quality, as well].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_108a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Since it is also stated, in Scripture, that the sense-organs are formed of the Elements, the doubt may occur, whether the Scriptural texts are, perhaps, to be applied distributively, according to the difference of particular worlds. In regard to this, he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The materials of the organs everywhere the same.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_109"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 109.</font><a href="#pp_109"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not though there be a difference of locality, is there a difference in the material [of which the organs are formed]: the rule is as with the like of us.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_109a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Not through 'difference of locality,' as the world of Brahmá, and the like, is it, again, the fact, that the organs have any other material than self-consciousness; but the rule is, that those of all alike are formed of self-consciousness; as is the case, e.g., with us who live in this terrestrial world. For we hear, in Scripture, of only one Subtile Body
 
 <!------------------------page 400------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_400"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 400</font></a>
 
 [made up of the organs], transmigrating generally throngh the different localities. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_109b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, in that case, how is the Scripture relating to the materiality [of the organs] to be accounted for? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A non-literal text accounted for.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_110"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 110.</font><a href="#pp_110"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The mention thereof [viz., of materiality, as if it belonged to the organs,] is because there is [intended to be made, thereby, a more emphatic] mention of the concomitant cause.<a name="page_400_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_400_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_110a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> There is designation as the material cause, in the case even where the cause is [but] concomitant, with a
 
 <!------------------------page 401------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_401"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 401</font></a>
 
 view to indicating its <i>importance</i>; just as fire is [spoken of as a rising] from fuel, [which fuel is a necessary concomitant of, though not really the substance of, the fire]. Hence are they [the organs,] spoken of as being formed of the Elements. Such is the meaning. For, only in reliance on the support of Light, or other Element, do the Organs, viz., the Sight, &amp;c., [formed] from the accompanying Self-consciousness, come to exist; as fire, in reliance on the support of earthly fuel, results from the attendant Light, [or Heat, which cannot manifest itself alone].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_110b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> As the subject presents itself, he determines the variety that belongs to Gross Body:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Varieties of Gross Bodies.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_111"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 111.</font><a href="#pp_111"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The heat-born, egg-born, womb-born, vegetable, thought-born, and spell-born; such is not an exhaustive division [of Gross Body, though a rough and customary one].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 402------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_402"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 402</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_111a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It was stated, before, that Body has only one Element as its material. In this same connexion, he observes discriminatively, as follows:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The material of Bodies.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_112"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 112.</font><a href="#pp_112"><font color="RED">*</font></a> In all [Bodies] Earth is the material: in consideration [however,] of some speciality, there is designation as this [or that other element than earth, as entering into the constitution of some given body], as in the preceding case [treated under <a href="#pp_110">§ 110</a>].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_112a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> In all Bodies the material is Earth only. 'In consideration of some speciality;' i.e., in consequence of intensity through excess, &amp;c., in the case of Body, as before [in the case of the Organs], there is, however, designation as consisting of Elements, five, or four, &amp;c., on the ground only of there being a <i>support</i>, as in the case of the materiality of the Organs. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_112b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since the vital air is the principal thing in
 
 <!------------------------page 403------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_403"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 403</font></a>
 
 the Body, let the vital air itself be the originant of the Body. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The vital air not the source of the Body.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_113"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 113.</font><a href="#pp_113"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The vital air is not [on the allegation that it is the principal thing in the Body, to be considered] the originant of the Body; because it [the vital air, or spirit,] subsists through the power of the organs.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_113a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The vital air, consisting in the function of the organs, does not subsist in the absence of the organs. Therefore, since, in a <i>dead</i> Body, in consequence of the absence of the organs, there is the absence of the vital air, the vital air is not the originant of the Body.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_113b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, in that case, since the vital air is not the cause of the Body, the Body might come into existence even without the vital air. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 404------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_404"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 404</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul essenial to a living body.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_114"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 114.</font><a href="#pp_114"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The site of experience [viz., the Body,] is contructed [only] through the superintendence of the experiencer [Soul]: otherwise, we should find putrefaction.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_114a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Through the superintendence,' i.e., only through the operation, 'of the experiencer,' i.e., Soul [literally, that which has the vital airs], is 'the construction of the site of experience,' i.e., the Body; because, 'otherwise,' i.e., if the operation of the vital airs were absent, we should find putrefaction in the semen and blood, just as in a dead body. Such is the meaning. And thus, by the several operations of circulating the juices, &amp;c., the vital air is a <i>concomitant</i> cause<a name="page_404_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_404_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the Body, through the sustaining of it: such is the import.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_114b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then [it may be said], it is only the vital air, itself, that can be the superintender; because it is this which
 
 <!------------------------page 405------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_405"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 405</font></a>
 
 operates, not the Soul, since <i>it</i> is motoinless, and since there is no use in the superintendence of what does not operate. To this he replies:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Soul 'acting by another's actions.'</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_115"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 115.</font><a href="#pp_115"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Through a servant, not directly, is superintendence [exercised] by the master.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_115a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> In the construction of the Body, 'superintendence,' in the shape of energizing, is not 'directly,' i.e., immediately, [exercised] 'by the master,' i.e., by Soul, but 'through its servant,' in the shape of the vital airs; as in the case of a king's building a city: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_115b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It was stated before [<a href="sak2.htm#pp_1">Book II., § 1</a>,] that Nature's [agency] is 'for the emancipation of what is [really, though not apparently,] emancipated.' In reference to the objection of opponents in regard to this, viz., 'How can the
 
 <!------------------------page 406------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_406"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 406</font></a>
 
 soul be eternally free, when we see it bound?' with a view to demonstrating its eternal freedom, he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul ever free.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_116"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 116.</font><a href="#pp_116"><font color="RED">*</font></a> In Concentration, profound sleep, and emancipation, it [Soul,] consists of Brahma.<a name="page_406_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_406_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_116a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Then what is the difference of emancipation from profound sleep and concentration? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Perfect and imperfect emancipation.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_117"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 117.</font><a href="#pp_117"><font color="RED">*</font></a> In the case of the two, it is with a seed; in the case of the other, this is wanting.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_117a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'In the case of the two,' viz., concentration and profound sleep, the identity with Brahma<a name="page_406_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_406_note_5"><font size="1">5</font></a> is 'with a seed,' i.e., associated with some cause of Bondage, [or reappearance in the mundane state]; 'in the case of the other,' i.e.,
 
 <!------------------------page 407------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_407"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 407</font></a>
 
 in emancipation, this cause is absent: this is the distinction. Such is the meaning.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_117b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, Concentration and profound sleep are <i>evident</i>: but what evidence is there of <i>Emancipation</i>? This objection of the atheist he repels:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The reality of Emancipation.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_118"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 118.</font><a href="#pp_118"><font color="RED">*</font></a> But there are not the two [only]; because the triad, also [Emancipation inclusive], is evident; as are the two.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_118a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The meaning is, that, since Emancipation, also, is 'evident,' i.e., is inferrible, through the example of Concentration and profound sleep, there are not the two, viz., profound sleep and Concentration, only; but Emancipation, also, really is. And the argument is thus. The quitting of that identity with Brahma<a name="page_407_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_407_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> which [identity] exists during profound sleep, &amp;c., takes place only through a fault, viz., Desire, or the like, lodged in the mind; and, if this fault be annihilated by knowledge, then there results
 
 <!------------------------page 408------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_408"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 408</font></a>
 
 a <i>permanent</i> condition, quite similar to profound sleep, &amp;c.; and it is precisely <i>this</i> that is Emancipation.<a name="page_408_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_408_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a>
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_118b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then [suggests some one, with reference to <a href="#pp_117">§ 117</a>], granting, that, even notwithstanding the existence of the 'seed' [or source of return to the mundane state,] called Memory,<a name="page_408_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_408_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> a mental modification after the form of any object does not arise during <i>concentration</i>, inasmuch as Memory is [then] dulled [or deadened] by apathy, &amp;c., yet, in the case of a person in <i>profound sleep</i>, since Memory prevails, there will really be cognition of objects; consequently, it is not proper to say that there is identity with Brahma during profound sleep. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 409------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_409"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 409</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Memory inactive during profound sleep.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_119"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 119.</font><a href="#pp_119"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is not the revelation, by memory, of an object likewise during the conjunction of a [more potent] fault [such as sleep]: the secondary cause does not debar the principal.<a name="page_409_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_409_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_119a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> As in the case of apathy, so, also when there is the conjunction of the fault of sleep, Memory does not reveal its own objects, does not remind us of its objects; for the 'secondary,' the subordinate, Memory,<a name="page_409_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_409_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> cannot defeat the
 
 <!------------------------page 410------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_410"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 410</font></a>
 
 more potent fault of Sleep: such is the meaning. For the really more potent fault makes the memory powerless, incompetent to produce its effects; [and so there is nothing, in this, to prevent identification of Soul with Brahma, during profound sleep, any more than during apathetic Concentration]: such is the import.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_119b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It was stated, in the Third Book [<a href="sak3.htm#pp_83">§ 83</a>], that the retention of a Body by him who is emancipated while still living, is 'in consequenue of a mere vestige of impression.'<a name="page_410_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_410_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> To this it is objected as follows. Experience is observed, in the case of the [alleged person] emancipated during life, just as in the case of the like of us, [and this experience continuous,] even though it may be constantly in respect of a single object: now, this is unaccountable [on the hypothesis of his really <i>being</i> emancipated]; because the antecedent <i>impression</i> is annihilated, exactly on its having produced the first [instant of] experience, and because no subsequent impression arises, inasmuch as <i>knowledge</i> debars it; just as is the case with Merit. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 411------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_411"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 411</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   An objection met to the possibility of emancipation in one still living.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_120"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 120.</font><a href="#pp_120"><font color="RED">*</font></a> A single impression [suffices to generate, and] lasts out<a name="page_411_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_411_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> the experience: but there are not different impressions, one to each [instant of] experience; else, we should have a postulation of many, [where a single one may suffice].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_120a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> In like manner, in the case of the whirling of the potter's wheel, the self-continuant principle,<a name="page_411_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_411_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> called motal inertia, is to be regarded as only one, continuing till the completion of the whirling.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_120b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It has been stated [<a href="#pp_111">§ 111</a>,] that there are vegetable Bodies. He repels the objection of the atheist, that, in the case in question, there is not a <i>Body</i>, inasmuch as there is no knowledge of the external:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 412------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_412"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 412</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Vegetable organism really a Body.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_121"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 121.</font><a href="#pp_121"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Knowledge of the external is not indispensable [to constitute a Body]:<a name="page_412_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_412_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> trees, shrubs, climbers, annuals, trees with invisible flowers, grasses, creepers, &amp;c., [which have internal consciousness], are, also, sites of experiencer and experience; as in the former case.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_121a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> There is no necessity that that only should be a Body, in which there is knowledge of the external; but it is to be held that the being a Body, in the form of being the site of experiencer and experience, belongs also to trees, &amp;c., which have internal consciousness; because, 'as in the former case,' meaning the putrescence already mentioned [see <a href="#pp_114">§ 114</a>], of the Bodies of men, &amp;c., [which takes place] in the absence of the superintendence of an experiencer [the living soul], even in the same way do withering, &amp;c., take place in the Bodies of trees, &amp;c., also: such is the meaning. And to this effect there is Scripture.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 413------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_413"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 413</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Law, as well as Scripture, is authority for this.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_122"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 122.</font><a href="#pp_122"><font color="RED">*</font></a> And from the Legal Institutes [the same fact may be inferred, viz., that vegetables have bodies and are conscious].<a name="page_413_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_413_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_122a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, from the fact that trees, &amp;c., also, are thus conscious, we should find merit and demerit accruing to them. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Vegetables not moral agents.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_123"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 123.</font><a href="#pp_123"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not merely through a Body is there susceptibility of Merit and Demerit; for Scripture tells us the distinction.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_123a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> The vital spirit is not liable to the production of Merit and Demerit through a <i>Body</i> merely. Why? 'For Scripture tells us the distinction:' because we are told, in Scripture, that the liability results just from the being
 
 <!------------------------page 414------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_414"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 414</font></a>
 
 distinguished by a Brahmanical Body, or the like [animal body, not vegetable]. Such is the meaning.
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_123b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Showing that the liability to Merit and Demerit is solely through the <i>kind</i> of Body, he mentions how Body is of three kinds:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Body of three principal kinds.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_124"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 124.</font><a href="#pp_124"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Among the three there is a threefold distribution; the Body of merit, the Body of experience, and the Body of both.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_124a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> There is a threefold distribution of Body 'among the three,' i.e., among those highest, lowest, and intermediate,—all living beings,—viz., the Body of merit, the Body of experience, and the Body of both: such is the meaning. Of these, a Body of merit belongs to the preeminent sages; a Body of experience, to Indra and others, and to things immovable, &amp;c.; and a Body of both, to the royal sages. Here the division is [not exhaustive, but] into three, because of the preeminence [of these]; for,
 
 <!------------------------page 415------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_415"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 415</font></a>
 
 otherwise, we should have all alike possessed of a Body of experience, [like Indra].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_124b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He mentions also a fourth Body:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A fourth kind of Body.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_125"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 125.</font><a href="#pp_125"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not any one [of these], moreover, is that of the apathetic.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_125a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the Body which belongs to the ascetics is different from all these three; such as was that of Dattátreya, Jaḍabharata, and others; for they possessed bodies consisting of mere knowledge.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_125b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> In order to establish the non-existence of a Lord, which was stated before, he disproves the eternity of
 
 <!------------------------page 416------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_416"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 416</font></a>
 
 knowledge, desire, action, &amp;c., which is accepted by others [as existing in the case of the Lord]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Argument against the existence of a Lord.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_126"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 126.</font><a href="#pp_126"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Eternity does not [as is alleged by those who wish to establish the existence of a Lord,] belong to knowledge,<a name="page_416_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_416_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> &amp;c., even in the case of the particular site, [viz., that of the supposed Lord]; as is the case with fire.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_126a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: just as we infer, from the example of ordinary fire, that the empyrean fire,<a name="page_416_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_416_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> also, is not eternal.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 417------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_417"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 417</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The argument really ex abundantiâ</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_127"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 127.</font><a href="#pp_127"><font color="RED">*</font></a> And, because the site [viz. the supposed Lord] is unreal, [it matters not, in the present instance, whether knowledge, &amp;c.. may be eternal, or not].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_127a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, in that case, how can it, indeed, be possible that there should arise Omniscience, &amp;c., adequate to the creation of the universe; since we do not behold, in mundane life, <i>such</i> superhuman powers [though we do see some,] arising from penance and the rest [of the alleged means of acquiring superhuman powers]? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The height to which asceticism may elevate.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_128"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 128.</font><a href="#pp_128"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The superhuman powers<a name="page_417_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_417_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> of concentration, just like the effects of drugs, &amp;c., are not to be gainsaid.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_128a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: by the example of the effects of drugs, &amp;c., even the superhuman powers of assuming atomic magnitude, &amp;c., which result from concentration, and are adapted to the work of creation, &amp;c., are established.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 418------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_418"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 418</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_128b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He refutes him who asserts that Thought belongs to the Elements; since this is hostile to the establishment [of the existence] of Soul:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Argument against Materialism.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_129"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 129.</font><a href="#pp_129"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Thought does not belong to the Elements; for it is not found in them separately, or, moreover, in the state of combination,—or, moreover, in the state of combination.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_129a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: Thought does not exist in the five Elements, even when in the state of combination; because we do not find Thought in them, generally, at the time of disjunction; [and there can be nothing in the product which does not preexist in the cause].</p>
 
 <p> </p>
