 <h2 align="center">BOOK VI.</h2>
 
 <hr width="12.5%" align="center">
 
 <p>   H<small>AVING</small> explained, in four Books, all the matter of the Institute, and having, in the Fifth Book, thoroughly established it, by refuting the opinions of opponents, now, in a Sixth Book, he recapitulates the same matter, which is the essence of the Institute, while condensing it. For, in addition [to what has preceded], an enumeration of the matters before mentioned, namely, a summary, having been composed, learners acquire an undoubting, accurate, and more solid knowledge; so that, therefore, reiteration is not here to be imputed as a fault; because the method is that of fixing a stake, [viz., by repeated blows], and because arguments, &amp;c., not previously stated, are adduced.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 420------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_420"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 420</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The existence of Soul.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_1"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 1.</font><a href="#pp_1"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Soul is; for there is no proof that it is not.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_1a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Soul really is existent, generically; since we are aware of this, that 'I think;' because there is no evidence to defeat this. Therefore, all that is to be done is to <i>discriminate</i> it [from things in general]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_1b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> The discrimination of it he establishes by means of two proofs:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul is not Body, &amp;c.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_2"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 2.</font><a href="#pp_2"><font color="RED">*</font></a> This [Soul,] is different from the Body, &amp;c.; because of heterogeneousness, [or complete difference between the two].</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The usage of language is evidence for this.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_3"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 3.</font><a href="#pp_3"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Also because it [Soul,] is expressed by means of the sixth [or possessive,] case.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_3a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: Soul is different from Body, &amp;c., also because the learned express it by the possessive case, in
 
 <!------------------------page 421------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_421"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 421</font></a>
 
 such examples as, 'This [is] my body,' 'This [is] my understanding;' for the possessive case would be unaccountable, if there were absolute non-difference [between the Body, or the like, and the Soul, to which it is thus attributed as a possession].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_3b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, suppose that this, also, is like the expressions, 'The Soul's Thought' [Soul and Thought being identical], 'Ráhu's head' [the trunkless Ráhu being <i>all</i> head], 'The statue's body,' &amp;c. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   An objection disposed of.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_4"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 4.</font><a href="#pp_4"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is not as in the case of the statue; because there is [there] a contradiction to the evidence which acquaints us with the thing.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_4a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> This expression by means of the possessive case,
 
 <!------------------------page 422------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_422"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 422</font></a>
 
 [viz., 'My body' (<a href="#pp_3">§ 3</a>)] is not like 'The statue's body,' &amp;c. In such a case as 'The statue's body,' there is a mere fiction; 'for it is contradicted by the evidence which acquaints is with the thing;' [sense being the evidence that there is here no body other than the statue]. But, in such an expression as 'My body,' there is no contradiction by evidence; for the contradiction, by Scripture and other evidences, is only in supposing the Body to be the Soul. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_4b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Having settled that Soul is different from Body, &amp;c., he settles its emancipation:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul's aim how accomplished.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_5"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 5.</font><a href="#pp_5"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Through the entire cessation of pain there is done what was to be done.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_5a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, since there is an equality of gain and loss, inasmuch as, through the cessation of Pain there is the
 
 <!------------------------page 423------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_423"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 423</font></a>
 
 ceasing of Pleasure, also, <i>that</i> cannot be Soul's aim. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Pleasure no compensation for Pain.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_6"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 6.</font><a href="#pp_6"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not such desire for pleasure is there to Soul, as there is annoyance from Pain.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_6a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> And so the aversion to Pain, having excluded also the desire for Pleasure, gives rise to a wish for he cessation of Pain simply; so that there is not an equality of gain and loss: [but a clear gain, in the desired release].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_6b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He declares that Soul's aim is simply the cessation of Pain; because Pain is, indeed, abundant, in comparison of Pleasure:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 424------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_424"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 424</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Pleasure sparingly dispensed.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_7"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 7.</font><a href="#pp_7"><font color="RED">*</font></a> For [only] some one, somewhere, is happy.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_7a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Among innumerable grasses, trees, brutes, birds, men, &amp;c., very few,—a man, a god, or the like,—are happy: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Pleasure undeserving of the name.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_8"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 8.</font><a href="#pp_8"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [Pleasure,] is also mixed with Pain, therefore the discriminating throw it to the side of [and reckon it as so much,] Pain.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_8a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He rejects the opinion that Soul's aim is not the simple cessation of Pain, but this [cessation] tinctured with Pleasure:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 425------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_425"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 425</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Cessation of suffering is a joy.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_9"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 9.</font><a href="#pp_9"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If you say that this [cessation of Pain] is not Soul's aim, inasmuch as there is no acquisition of Pleasure, then it is not as you say; for there are two kinds [of things desired].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_9a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> For we see, amongst men, quite a distinct aspiration: [the first,] 'May I be happy;' [the second,] 'May I not be miserable;' [and the latter is <i>our</i> conception of beatitude].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_9b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He ponders a doubt:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A doubt.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_10"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 10.</font><a href="#pp_10"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The Soul [some one may suggest,] has <i>no</i> quality; for there is Scripture for its being unaccompanied, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_10a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Therefore the cessation of Pain, indeed, [a property which does not belong to it,] cannot be Soul's aim: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_10b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He clears up this [doubt]:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 426------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_426"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 426</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This cleared up.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_11"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 11.</font><a href="#pp_11"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Though it [the Pain,] be the property of something else, yet it exists in it [the Soul,] through non-discrimination.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_11a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Though qualities, viz., pleasure, pain, &amp;c., belong [only] to the <i>Mind</i>, they exist, i.e., they abide, in the shape of a <i>reflexion</i>, in it, viz., in Soul, 'through non-discrimination,' as the cause, owing to the conjunction of Nature with Soul: such is the meaning. And this has been set forth in the First Book.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_11b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> The binding of Soul by the qualities [or fetters,] arises from non-discrimination: but from what does non-discrimination arise? With reference to this, he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Two reasons why non-discrimination must have been from eternity.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_12"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 12.</font><a href="#pp_12"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Non-discrimination [of Soul from Nature] is beginningless; because, otherwise, two objections would present themselves.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_12a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> For, had it a beginning, then, if [first,] it arose quite spontaneously, bondage might befall even the liberated;
 
 <!------------------------page 427------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_427"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 427</font></a>
 
 and, if [secondly,] it were produced by Desert, &amp;c., there would be a <i>regressus in infinitum</i>, inasmuch as we should have to search for another [previous instance of] non-discrimination, to stand as the cause of [that] Desert, &amp;c., also: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_12b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> And then, if it be without beginning, it must be everlasting. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Non-discrimination, though from eternity, may be cut short.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_13"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 13.</font><a href="#pp_13"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [non-discrimination, cannot be everlasting [in the same manner] as the soul is; else, it could not be cut short, [as we affirm that it can be].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_13a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> It is not everlasting, indivisible, and beginningless, in the same way as the soul is; but it is beginningless, in the shape of an on-flow [which may be stopped]. For, otherwise, the cutting short of a beginningless entity would, as is established by Scripture, be unfeasible, [though the beginningless antecedent non-entity of a given jar may be readily understood to terminate, on the production of the jar]. Such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 428------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_428"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 428</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_13b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Having stated the cause of [Soul's] Bondage, he states the cause of Liberation:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Bondage how destructible.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_14"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 14.</font><a href="#pp_14"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [Bondage,] is annihilable by the alloted cause, [viz., discrimination of Soul from Nature]; as darkness is [annihilable by the allotted cause, viz., Light].</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This enforced.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_15"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 15.</font><a href="#pp_15"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Here, also, [viz., in the case of Bondage and Discrimination, as in the case of Darkness and Light,] there is allotment, [as is proved] both by positive and negative consociation;<a name="page_428_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_428_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> [Liberation taking place where Discrimination is, and not where it is not].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_15a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He reminds [us] of what was mentioned in the first Book,<a name="page_428_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_428_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> viz., that Bondage cannot be innate, &amp;c:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 429------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_429"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 429</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Bondage not innate.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_16"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 16.</font><a href="#pp_16"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Since it cannot be [accounted for] in any other way, it is non-discrimination alone that is [the <i>cause</i> of] Bondage, [which cannot be innate].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_16a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Bondage' here means the <i>cause</i> of Bondage, named the conjunction of pain. The rest is plain.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_16b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since liberation, also, from its being a product, is liable to destruction, Bondage should take place over again. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Bondage does not recur.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_17"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 17.</font><a href="#pp_17"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Further, Bondage does not again attach to the liberated; because there is Scripture<a name="page_429_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_429_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> for its non-recurrence.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 430------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_430"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 430</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Evidence of this.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_18"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 18.</font><a href="#pp_18"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Else, it [liberation,] would not be the Soul's aim, [which it is].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_18a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He states the reason why this is not Soul's aim:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Force of the evidence.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_19"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 19.</font><a href="#pp_19"><font color="RED">*</font></a> What happened to both would be alike, [if liberation were perishable].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_19a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: there would be no difference between the two, the liberated and the bound; because of their being alike liable to future bondage; and, therefore, such [perishable emancipation] is not Soul's aim, [but emancipation final and complete].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_19b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, in that case, if you acknowledge that there is a <i>distinction</i> between the bond and the free, how is it
 
 <!------------------------page 431------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_431"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 431</font></a>
 
 that you have asserted [<a href="sak1.htm#pp_19">Book I., § 19</a>,] the <i>eternal</i> freedom [of <i>all</i> souls alike]? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The nature of liberation.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_20"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 20.</font><a href="#pp_20"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Liberation is nothing other than the removal<a name="page_431_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_431_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the obstacle [to the Soul's recognition of itself as free].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_20a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, in that case, since Bondage and Liberation are <i>unreal</i>, Liberation must be contradictory to the texts, &amp;c., which set forth what is Soul's aim, [as some positive and real acquisition, not merely the removal of a screen]; to which he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   An objection repelled.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_21"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 21.</font><a href="#pp_21"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Even in that case, there is no contradiction.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_21a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: 'even in that case,' i.e., even if Liberation consists [only] in the removal of an obstacle, there is no contradiction in its being Soul's aim.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 432------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_432"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 432</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_21b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, if Liberation be merely the removal of an obstacle, then it should be accomplished through mere <i>hearing</i> [of the error which stands in the way]; just as a piece of gold on one's neck, [which one has sought for in vain, while it was] withheld from one by ignorance [of the fact that it has been tied round one's neck with a string], is attained, [on one's hearing where it is]. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Another objection repelled.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_22"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 22.</font><a href="#pp_22"><font color="RED">*</font></a> This [attainment of Liberation, on the mere <i>hearing</i> of the truth,] is no necessity; for there are three sorts of those competent [to apprehend the truth; but not all are qualified to appropriate it, on merely hearing it].<a name="page_432_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_432_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_22a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He mentions that not mere <i>hearing</i> alone is seen to be the cause of knowledge, but that there are others, also:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 433------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_433"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 433</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Utility of other means besides hearing.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_23"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 23.</font><a href="#pp_23"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Of others [viz., other means besides hearing] for the sake of confirmation, [there is need].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_23a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He speaks of these same other means:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Formality in postures not imperative.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_24"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 24.</font><a href="#pp_24"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no [absolute] necessity that what is steady and promoting ease should be a [<i>particular</i>] posture, [such as any of those referred to in <a href="sak3.htm#pp_34">Book III., § 34</a>].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_24a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: there is no necessity that a 'posture' should be the 'lotus-posture,' or the like; because whatever is steady and promotes ease is a [suitable] 'posture.'</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_24b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states the principal means [of Concentration]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The efficient means of Concentration.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_25"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 25.</font><a href="#pp_25"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Mind without an object is Meditation.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_25a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: what Internal Organ is void of any modification, <i>that</i> is 'Meditation,' i.e., Concentration, in the shape of exclusion of the modifications of Intellect: by reason of the identity [here,] of effect and cause, the word 'cause' is employed for 'effect.' For it will be
 
 <!------------------------page 434------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_434"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 434</font></a>
 
 declared how Meditation effects this [exclusion of the modifications of Intellect].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_25b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since Soul is alike, whether there be Concentration or Non-concentration, what have we to do with concentration? Having pondered this doubt, he clears it up:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A distinction not without a difference.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_26"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 26.</font><a href="#pp_26"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If you say that even both ways there is no difference, it is not so: there is a difference, through the excluion [in the one case,] of the tinge [of reflected pain which exists in the other case].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_26a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But how can there exist a <i>tinge</i> in that which is unassociated [with anything whatever, as Soul is alleged to be]? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 435------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_435"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 435</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul tinged by what does not belong to it.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 27.</font><a href="#pp_27"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Though it [Soul,] be unassociated, still there is a tingeing [reflexionally,] through Non-discrimination.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: though there is not a <i>real</i> tinge in that which is unassociated [with tincture, or anything else], still there is, <i>as it were</i>, a tinge; hence the tinge is treated as simply a <i>reflexion</i>, by those who discriminate the tinge [from the Soul, which it delusively seems to belong to].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_27b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He explains this same:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Its seeming presence explained.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_28"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 28.</font><a href="#pp_28"><font color="RED">*</font></a> As is the case with the Hibiscus and the crystal [<a href="sak1.htm#pp_19c">Book I., § 19, <i>c</i></a>.], there is not a tinge, but a fancy [that there is such].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_28a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He states the means of excluding the aforesaid tinge:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   How to be got rid of.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_29"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 29.</font><a href="#pp_29"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [viz.,the aforesaid tinge,] is debarred by Meditation, Restraint, Practice, Apathy, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 436------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_436"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 436</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_29a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He shows the means settled by the ancient teachers, in regard to the exclusion—through Meditation, &amp;c., lodged in the Mind,—of the tingeing of Soul:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The ancient dogma on this point.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_30"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 30.</font><a href="#pp_30"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is by the exclusion of dissolution<a name="page_436_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_436_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> and distraction, say the teachers.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_30a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: through the removal, by means of meditation, &amp;c., of the Mind's condition of [being dissolved in] Sleep, and condition of [waking] Certainty, &amp;c., there takes place also the exclusion of the tingeing of Soul by the condition; because, on the exclusion of any [real] object, there is the exclusion also of its reflexion: so say the ancient teachers.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_30b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states that there is no compulsion that Meditation, &amp;c., should take place in caves and such places:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 437------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_437"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 437</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Meditation may take place anywhere.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_31"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 31.</font><a href="#pp_31"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no rule about localities; for it is from tranquillity of Mind.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_31a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: Meditation, or the like, results simply 'from tranquillity of Mind.' Therefore, such a place as a cave is not indispensable for it.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_31b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> The discussion of Liberation is completed. Now, with an eye to the unchangeableness of Soul, he handles compendiously the cause of the world:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature the material of the world.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_32"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 32.</font><a href="#pp_32"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nature is the primal material; for there is Scripture [to the effect] that the others are products.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_32a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: since we learn, from Scripture, that Mind, &amp;c., are products, Nature is established under the character of the radical cause of these.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_32b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, let <i>Soul</i> be the material. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 438------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_438"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 438</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul not the material of the world.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_33"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 33.</font><a href="#pp_33"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not to Soul does this [viz., the material of the world,] belong, though it be eternal; because of its want of suitableness.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_33a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: suitableness to act as material implies possession of qualities, and the being associable: [and,] by reason of the absence of both of these, Soul, though eternal, [and, therefore, no product,] cannot serve as material.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_33b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since, from such Scriptural texts as, 'Many creatures have been produced from Soul,'<a name="page_438_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_438_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> we may gather the fact that Soul is a cause, the assertions of an illusory creation, &amp;c., ought to be accepted. Having pondered this adverse suggestion, he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The opposite view unscriptural.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_34"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 34.</font><a href="#pp_34"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The despicable sophist<a name="page_438_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_438_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> does not gain [a correct apprehension of] Soul; because of the contradictoriness [of his notions] to Scripture.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 439------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_439"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 439</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_34a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the various views, in regard to Soul's being a cause, which are conceivable are, all, opposed to Scripture; therefore, the lowest of the bad reasoners, and others, who are accepters thereof,<a name="page_439_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_439_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> have no knowledge of the nature of Soul. Hence it is to be understood that those, also, [e.g., the <i>Naiyáyikas</i>,] who assert that Soul is the substance of the qualities Pleasure, Pain, &amp;c., are quite illogical; these, also, have no correct knowledge of Soul. And, if it be asserted that Soul is a cause [of the world], just as the sky is the recipient cause of the clouds, &amp;c., [and stands, towards it, in the relation of a cause, in so far as, without the room afforded by it, these could not exist], then we do not object to <i>that</i>; for, what we deny is only that there is transformation [of soul, as material, into the world, as product].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_34b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Since we see, that, in the case of things motionless, locomotive, &amp;c., the material cause is nothing else than
 
 <!------------------------page 440------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_440"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 440</font></a>
 
 <i>earth</i>, &amp;c., how can <i>Nature</i> be the material of all? To this he replies:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature the ultimate material cause.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_35"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 35.</font><a href="#pp_35"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Though but mediately [the cause of products], Nature is inferred [as the ultimate cause of the intermediate causes,]; just as are Atoms, [by the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i>].</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature all-pervading.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_36"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 36.</font><a href="#pp_36"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It [Nature,] is all-pervading; because [its] products are seen everywhere.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_36a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, only if it be <i>limited</i>, can it be said that, 'Wherever a product arises, there does it [Nature,] go [or act];' [for what is unlimited] and fills all space, can find no other space to <i>move</i> into]. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   An objection parried.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_37"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 37.</font><a href="#pp_37"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Though motion may attach to it, this does not destroy its character as ultimate cause; just as is the case with Atoms.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_37a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'Motion' means action. Though it be present, this does not prevent its [Nature's,] being the radical cause; just as is the case with the earthy and other
 
 <!------------------------page 441------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_441"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 441</font></a>
 
 Atoms, according to the opinion of the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i>: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature the proper substitute for eight of the substances in the Nyáya list.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_38"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 38.</font><a href="#pp_38"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nature is something in addition to the notorious [nine Substances of the <i>Naiyáyikas</i>]: it is no matter of necessity [that there should be precisely nine].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_38a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> And the argument, here, is the Scriptural declaration, that <i>eight</i> [of the pretended primitive substances] are <i>products</i>: such is the import.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature consists of the three Qualities.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_39"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 39.</font><a href="#pp_39"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Purity and the others are not <i>properties</i> of it [viz., Nature]; because they are its <i>essence</i>.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_39a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: Purity and the other Qualities are not <i>properties</i> of Nature; because they are what <i>constitutes</i> Nature.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_39b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He determines the motive of Nature's energizing;
 
 <!------------------------page 442------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_442"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 442</font></a>
 
 since, if we held the energizing to be without a motive, Emancipation would be inexplicable:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature's disinterestedness.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_40"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 40.</font><a href="#pp_40"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Nature, though it does not enjoy [the results of its own energizing], creates for the sake of Soul; like a cart's carrying saffron, [for the use of its master. See <a href="sak3.htm#pp_58">Book III., § 58</a>].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_40a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He states the concomitant<a name="page_442_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_442_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> cause of diversified creation:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature treats every one according to his deserts.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_41"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 41.</font><a href="#pp_41"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The diversity of creation is in consequence of the diversity of Desert.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 443------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_443"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 443</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_41a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, granting that <i>creation</i> is due to Nature, yet whence is <i>destruction</i>? For a couple of opposite results cannot belong to one and the same cause. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Contrary results from Nature how.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_42"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 42.</font><a href="#pp_42"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The two results are through equipoise and the reverse of equipoise.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_42a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Nature is the triad of Qualities, viz., Purity, &amp;c.; and their 'reverse of equipoise' is their aggregation in excess or defect; the absence of this [reverse of equipoise] is 'equipoise:'<a name="page_443_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_443_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> through these two causes two opposite results, in the shape of creation and destruction, arise from one and the same: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_42b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, since it is Nature's attribute to create, there should be the mundane state, even after [the discriminative] knowledge, [which, it is alleged, puts an end to it]. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 444------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_444"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 444</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Nature's energy does not debar emancipation.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_43"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 43.</font><a href="#pp_43"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Since [or when,] the emancipated has understood [that he never was really otherwise], Nature does not create; just as, in the world, [a minister does not toil,when the king's purpose has been accomplished].<a name="page_444_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_444_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_43a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, Nature does not rest from creating; for we see the mundane condition of the ignorant: and so, since Nature goes on creating, to the emancipated, also, Bondage may come again. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   No reason why Nature should invade the emancipated.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_44"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 44.</font><a href="#pp_44"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Even though it [Nature,] may invade others [with its creative influences], the <i>emancipated</i> does not experience, in consequence of the absence of a concurrent cause,<a name="page_444_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_444_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a> [e.g., Non-discrimination, in the absence of which there is no reason why the emancipated should be subjected  to Nature's invasion].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 445------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_445"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 445</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_44a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, <i>this</i> arrangement could be possible then, [only] if there were a <i>multiplicity</i> of souls: but <i>that</i> is quite excluded by the text of the non-duality of Soul. Having pondered this doubt, he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Multeity of Soul proved from the Veda.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_45"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 45.</font><a href="#pp_45"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The multeity of Soul [is proved] by the distribution [announced by the <i>Veda</i> itself].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_45a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: the multeity of Soul is proved, absolutely, by the distribution of Bondage and Emancipation mentioned in such Scriptural texts as, 'Whoso understand this, these are immortal, while others experience only sorrow.'<a name="page_445_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_445_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_45b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, the distribution of Bondage and Liberation may be through the difference of adjunct. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 446------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_446"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 446</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Unity excluded by the supposion of Souls.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_46"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 46.</font><a href="#pp_46"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If [you acknowledge] an adjunct [of Soul], then, on <i>its</i> being established, there is <i>duality</i>, [upsetting the dogma founded on in <a href="#pp_44">§ 44</a>].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_46a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, the adjuncts, moreover, consist of 'Ignorance,' [which, according to the Vedánta, is no reality]; so that by these there is no detriment to [the Vedántíc dogma of] non-duality. With reference to this doubt, he says:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Vedánta cannot evade non-duality.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_47"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 47.</font><a href="#pp_47"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Even by the two the authority is contradicted.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_47a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Thst is to say: even by acknowledging the two, viz., Soul and Ignorance, a contradiction is constituted to the text, [which is alleged as] the authority for non-duality.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_47b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He states another couple of objections, also:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 447------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_447"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 447</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The establishment of the Vedánta tenet implies a contradiction.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_48"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 48.</font><a href="#pp_48"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The primâ facie view [of the Vedánta] is not [to be allowed any force, as an objection]; because, by [admitting] two, [viz., Soul and Ignorance], there is no opposition [to our own dualistic theory of Soul and Nature]: and the subsequent [dogma, viz., that one single Soul is the only reality, is not to be allowed]; because of the non-existence of a proof, [which, if it <i>did</i> exist, would, along with Soul, constitute a duality].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_48a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, Soul will be demonstrated by its self-manifestation. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Self-manifestation contradictory.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_49"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 49.</font><a href="#pp_49"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [And,] in its [Soul's,] being demonstrated by the light [of itself as you Vedántís say it is], there is the [unreconciled] opposition of patient and agent [in one, which is a contradiction].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_49a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: if Soul be demonstrated by the light which Soul consists of, there is the 'opposition of patient and agent' [in one].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_49b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, there is no contradiction [here,] between patient and agent; because it [the Soul], through the property of light which is lodged in it, can, itself, furnish
 
 <!------------------------page 448------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_448"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 448</font></a>
 
 the relation to itself; just as the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i> declare, that, through the intelligence lodged in it, it is, itself, <i>object</i> to itself. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Illuminating function of Soul.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_50"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 50.</font><a href="#pp_50"><font color="RED">*</font></a> This [Soul], in the shape of Thought, discrepant from the non-intelligent, reveals the non-intelligent.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_50a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, in that case, if duality be established in accordance with proofs, &amp;c., what becomes of the Scriptural text declaring non-duality? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A salvo for the Vaidic view.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_51"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 51.</font><a href="#pp_51"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is no contradiction to Scripture [in our view]; because that [text of Scripture which seems to
 
 <!------------------------page 449------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_449"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 449</font></a>
 
 assert absolute non-duality] is [intended] to produce apathy in those who have desires, [and who would be better for believing in 'the <i>nothingness</i> of the things of time'].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_51a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He tells us that the assertors of non-duality are to be shunned, not only for the reason above mentioned, but, also, because of the non-existence of evidence to convince us that the world is unreal:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The world's reality irrefragable.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_52"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 52.</font><a href="#pp_52"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The world is real; because it results from an unobjectionable cause, and because there is [in Scripture,] no debarrer [of this view of the matter].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_52a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> We see, in the world, that no reality belongs to dream-objects, or to the [fancied] yellowness of [invariably white] conch-shells, and the like; inasmuch as these are results of the internal organ, &amp;c., when [not normal, but] injured by [i.e., under the injurious influence of] Sleep,<a name="page_449_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_449_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> &amp;c.: and this is not [the state of things] in the [waking] Universe, in which Mind is the first,<a name="page_449_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_449_note_3"><font size="1">3</font></a> [according to <a href="sak1.htm#pp_71">Book I., § 71</a>].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 450------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_450"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 450</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_52b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He declares that the Universe is real, not merely in its existent state [at any given instant], but, also, always:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Creation excluded.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_53"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 53.</font><a href="#pp_53"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Since it cannot be [accounted for] in any other way, manifestation [of whatever is manifested] is of what is real, [i.e., of what previously existed].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_53a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: since, through the aforesaid reasons, it is impossible that the unreal should come into existence, what does come into existence, or is manifested, is what really existed [previously,] in a subtile form.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_53b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Though [it is declared that] the being the agent and the being the experiencer belong to diverse subjects, be asserts the distribution [of agency to Self-consciousness, and of experience to Soul,] by two aphorisms:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The real agent who.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_54"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 54.</font><a href="#pp_54"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Self-consciousness, not Soul, is the agent.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 451------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_451"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 451</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Experience is got rid of when.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_55"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 55.</font><a href="#pp_55"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Experience ceases at [discrimination of] Soul, [as being quite distinct from Nature]; since it arises from its [Soul's,] Desert, [which is not, <i>really</i>, Soul's, but which, while Non-discrimination lasts, is made over to Soul; just as the fruits of the acts of a king's ministers are made over to the king].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_55a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He shows the reason for what was stated before, viz., that cessation of action does not result from enterings into the world of Brahmá:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Paradise no security against transmigration.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_56"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 56.</font><a href="#pp_56"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Even in the world of the moon, &amp;c., there is return [to munane existence]; because of there really being a cause [of such return].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_56a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> 'A cause,' viz., Non-discrimination, Desert, &amp;c.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_56b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, through the counsels of the persons dwelling in these various [supermundane] worlds, there ought to be no return [to mundane existence]. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 452------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_452"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 452</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   This point enforced.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_57"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 57.</font><a href="#pp_57"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Not by the counsel of [supermundane] people is there effectuation [of Emancipation]; just as in the former case, [the case, viz., of counsel given by mundane instructors].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_57a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But, in that case, what becomes of the text that there no return from the world of Brahmá? To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A salvo for Scriptural text.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_58"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 58.</font><a href="#pp_58"><font color="RED">*</font></a> There is Scripture [declaratory] of Emancipation, [on going to the world of Brahmá]; this [Emancipation] being effected [more readily in that world than in this, but only] by intermediacy [of the appropriate means].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_58a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He alleges the Scriptural text of Soul's <i>going</i> [to the locality where it is to experience], even though it be all-filling, [and can, therefore, have no place into which to move]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Another.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_59"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 59.</font><a href="#pp_59"><font color="RED">*</font></a> And, in accordance with the text of its 'going,' though it [Soul,]
 
 <!------------------------page 453------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_453"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 453</font></a>
 
 is all-pervading, yet, in time, it reaches its place of experience [or body], through conjunction with an adjunct; as in the case of Space.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_59a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> For, as Space, though it is all-pervading, is spoken of as moving to some particular place, in consequence of its conjunction with an adjunct, such as a jar, [when we say 'the space occupied by the jar is moved to the place to which the jar is carried'], just so is it [here].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_59b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> He expounds the statement, that the site of experience [the body,] is formed through the superintendence of the experiencer, [Soul]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The Body's existence dependent on Soul.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_60"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 60.</font><a href="#pp_60"><font color="RED">*</font></a> This [constitution of a body] is not accomplished in the case of what is [organic matter] not superintended [by Soul]; because we find putrefaction [in organic matter where Soul is absent].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_60a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> But then, let the construction of a site of experience [or a body,] for Experiencers [i.e., Souls,] take place
 
 <!------------------------page 454------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_454"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 454</font></a>
 
 without any superintendence at all, through Desert. To this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Desert not the author of the Body.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_61"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 61.</font><a href="#pp_61"><font color="RED">*</font></a> If you say that [independently of any superintendence,] it is through Desert [that a Body is formed, it is not so]; since what is unconnected [with the matter to be operated upon] is incompetent thereto; as is the case with [unapplied] water, &amp;c., in respect of a plant.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_61a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> That is to say: because it is impossible that Desert, which is not directly conjoined with the semen and other [elements of the Body] , should operate <i>through Soul</i>, in the construction of the Body, &amp;c.; just as it is for water, &amp;c., <i>unconnected</i> with the seed, to operate <i>through tillage</i>, &amp;c., in the production of a plant.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_61b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> According to the system of the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i> and others, it is settled that Soul is the superintendent, [in the construction of the Body], <i>in virtue of its being conjoined with Desert</i>. But he tells us, that, in his own doctrine,
 
 <!------------------------page 455------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_455"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 455</font></a>
 
 since Desert, &amp;c., are not properties of Soul, the Soul cannot, <i>through these</i>, be the cause [of the Body]:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Reason for this.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_62"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 62.</font><a href="#pp_62"><font color="RED">*</font></a> For this is impossible [viz., that the Soul should, <i>through its Desert</i>, &amp;c., be the cause of Body]; because it has <i>no</i> qualities for these [viz., Desert, &amp;c.,] are properties of Self-consciousness, [not of Soul].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_62a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> And so, in <i>our</i> opinion, it is settled that Soul superintends [in the causing of the Body,] quite directly, by conjunction simply, without reference to anything intermediate: such is the import.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_62b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But, if Soul be all-pervading, then the limitedness of the living soul, which is set forth in Scripture, is unfounded. To repel this doubt, he says:</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 456------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_456"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 456</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Soul how limited and unlimited.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_63"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 63.</font><a href="#pp_63"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The nature of a living soul belongs to that which is qualified, [not to soul devoid of qualities, as is proved] by direct and indirect argument.<a name="page_456_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_456_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_63a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> To be a living soul is the being possessed of the vital airs; and this is the character of the soul distinguished by peraonality, not of pure Soul, [which is unlimited].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_63b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> Desiring, now, to set forth the difference between the products of Mind [or the Great Principle,] and of Self-consciousness, he first states the products of Self-consciousness:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The real agent what.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_64"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 64.</font><a href="#pp_64"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The effectuation of works is dependent on the agent Self-consciousness, not dependent on a Lord, [such as is feigned by the <i>Vaiśeshikas</i>]; because there is no proof [of the reality of such].<a name="page_456_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_456_note_4"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_64a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> By this aphorism are set forth, as are also established
 
 <!------------------------page 457------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_457"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 457</font></a>
 
 by Scripture and the Legal Institutes, the creative and the destructive agencies of Brahmá and Rudra<a name="page_457_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_457_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> [respectively], owing to their adjunct, Self-consciousness, [or peronality].
 
 </p><p>   <a name="pp_64b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> But then, grant that Self-consciousness is the maker of the others, still who is the maker of Self-consciousness? to this he replies:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The real agent whence.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_65"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 65.</font><a href="#pp_65"><font color="RED">*</font></a> It is the same as in the arising of Desert.</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_65a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> Just as, at the creations, &amp;c., the manifestation of Desert, which sets Nature energizing, results solely from the particular <i>time</i>,—since, if we were to suppose other Desert as the instigator of this, we should have an infinite regress,—just so Self-consciousness arises from <i>time</i> alone. as the cause; but there is not another maker thereof, also: thus, the two [cases] are alike: such is the meaning.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 458------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_458"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 458</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   Orthodox recognition of Brahmá, Śiva, and Vishṇu, put forward.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_66"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 66.</font><a href="#pp_66"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The rest is from Mind, [the Great Principle].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_66a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> What is other than the products of Self-consciousness [or personality], viz., Creation, &amp;c., that, viz., Preservation, &amp;c., results from the Great Principle alone; because, inasmuch as it consists of pure Goodness, having no Conceit, Passion, &amp;c., it is moved solely by benevolence towards others: such is the meaning. And by this aphorism is established the character, as Preserver, of Vishṇu, owing to the Great Principle, as adjunct<a name="page_458_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_458_note_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> [of the soul, which, without adjunct, would neither create, preserve, nor destroy (see <a href="#pp_64">§ 64</a>)].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_66b"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>b</i>.</font> It has been stated, before, that the relation of Nature and Soul, as experienced and experiencer, is caused by Non-discrimination [of the one from the other]. Here, what is Non-discrimination, itself, caused by?
 
 <!------------------------page 459------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_459"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 459</font></a>
 
 With reference to this doubt, he states that all philosophers reject, in common, the doubt whether we should have an infinite regress, on the supposition of a <i>stream</i> of Non-discrimination; because <i>this</i> [regress] is <i>valid</i>; [since an infinite regress which is in conformity with the truth is no sound cause of objection]:
 
 </p><p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A theory which may be acquiesced in without detriment to the argument.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_67"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 67.</font><a href="#pp_67"><font color="RED">*</font></a> The relation of possession and possessor, also, if attributed [as it is by some,] to Desert, in the case of Nature [and Soul], like [the relation of] seed and plant, [which takes the shape of an infinite regress of alternants], is beginningless.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A second.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_68"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 68.</font><a href="#pp_68"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Or [the case is, likewise, one of infinite regress,] if it [the relation between Nature and Soul,] be attributed to Non-discrimination [of Soul from Nature], as Panchaśikha [holds].</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 460------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_460"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 460</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   A third.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_69"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 69.</font><a href="#pp_69"><font color="RED">*</font></a> [The case is the same,] if, as the teacher Sanandana does, we attribute it [the relation between Nature and Soul,] to the Subtile Body, [which, in the shape of its elemental causes, attends Soul, even during the periodical annihilations of the world].</p>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_69a"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>a</i>.</font> He sums up the import of the declarations of the Institute:</p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <table align="left" cellpadding="5" width="33%">
 
 <tr><td><small><i>   The summing up.</i></small></td></tr>
 
 </table>
 
 <p>   <a name="pp_70"></a><font color="GREEN"><i>Aph</i>. 70.</font><a href="#pp_70"><font color="RED">*</font></a> Be that the one way, or the other, the cutting short thereof [viz., of the relation between Nature and Soul,] is Soul's aim; the cutting short thereof is Soul's aim.</p>
 
 <p> </p>
