 <p><a name="page_412"><font size="1" color="green">p. 412</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 21.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 13.</h4>  
 <p>1. Let us lay thee down, as Manus did. Let us kindle thee, as Manus did. O Agni Aṅgiras, sacrifice to the gods for the worshippers of the gods as (thou didst) for Manus.</p>  
 <p>2. For thou, O Agni, art kindled, highly pleased, among human people. To thee the (sacrificial) ladles proceed in due order, O well-born one who drinkest butter.</p>  
 <p>3. Thee all the gods unanimously have made their messenger. Serving thee, O sage, they magnify at the sacrifices (thee) the god.</p>  
 <p>4. Let the mortal magnify for your sake Agni, the god, with worship as is due to the gods. Being kindled, O brilliant one, shine! Sit down in the abode <a href="sbe46125.htm#an_5:21:4:1">1</a> of <i>Ri</i>ta; sit down in the abode of herbs <a href="sbe46125.htm#an_5:21:4:2">2</a>.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Sasa Âtreya (cf. verse 4); the metre is the same.—Verse 1 = TB. III, 11, 6, 3.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:21:4:1">1</a>. Literally 'in the womb.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:21:4:2">2</a>. On sasá, see III, 5, 6, note 2. Is the abode (or womb) of the herbs the Barhis? 'Is it satyasya?' M. M.</p>
