 <p><a name="page_410"><font size="1" color="green">p. 410</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 20.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 12.</h4>  
 <p>1 <a href="sbe46124.htm#an_5:20:1:1">1</a>. Whatever good, O Agni, best acquirer of gain, thou thinkest (fit), praise thou <a href="sbe46124.htm#an_5:20:1:2">2</a> that (good), which is celebrated in songs, among the gods as our share.</p>  
 <p>2. They, Agni, who do not set into motion for thee (prayers or offerings), when grown full of mighty strength <a href="sbe46124.htm#an_5:20:2:1">1</a>, turn away to encounter the hatred and the tricks of him who follows another (i. e. a wrong) law <a href="sbe46124.htm#an_5:20:2:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>3. We choose thee as our Hot<i>ri</i>, Agni, the giver of skill; offering delight (to thee) we call with our prayer (thee), the foremost at the sacrifices.</p>  
 <p>4. So that we, O strong one, (may be ready) for thy favour, for wealth and <i>Ri</i>ta, O highly wise one: thus may we day by day rejoice <a href="sbe46124.htm#an_5:20:4:1">1</a> with cows and rejoice with heroes.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Prayasvanta Âtreya (cf. verse 3. práyasvanta<i>h</i> havâmahe), the metre Anush<i>t</i>ubh, verse 4 Paṅkti.—Verse 1 = VS. XIX, 64.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:20:1:1">1</a>. Professor Max Müller proposes to read vâ<i>g</i>asâ´tamam, as in IX, 98, 1. Pischel (Vedische Studien, I, 200) translates this verse: 'O Agni, das rühmenswerte Gut, das du für geeignet hältst, das preise du zugleich mit unsern Liedern (no gîrbhír yú<i>g</i>am) den Göttern an (diis vendita).' He explains: 'Agni soll den Göttern Gut bringen and sie</p>  
 <p><a name="page_411"><font size="1" color="green">p. 411</font></a></p>  
 <p>veranlassen, es den Menschen für die Lieder zu schenken.' I cannot follow Pischel's theory about the identity of the roots pan and pa<i>n</i> (comp. about pa<i>n</i> the quotations collected by Bartholomae, Indogermanische Forschungen, III, 180); and the paraphrase 'diis vendita' seems inadmissible to me. I differ from Pischel, besides, in the interpretation of yú<i>g</i>am; cf. rayím … yú<i>g</i>am, IV, 37, 5; râyâ´ yu<i>g</i>â´, VII, 43, 5; 95, 4.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:20:1:2">2</a>. Neisser, Bezzenberger's Beiträge, XX, 55, explains panayâ as standing for panayâma, and compares V, 56, 2 (?).</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:20:2:1">1</a>. On v<i>ri</i>dh with the genitive, compare Delbrück; Altindische Syntax, p. 158; Macdonell, Journal Roy. As. Soc., 1893, p. 433. Grassmann's conjecture v<i>rí</i>ddhâ(v) is a failure.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:20:2:2">2</a>. With the second hemistich compare VS. XXXVIII, 20 (<i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a XIV, 3, 1, 9); TÂr. IV, 11, 4 (cf. V, 9, 7).</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:20:4:1">1</a>. Syâma stands, as the accent shows, in an independent clause. Prof. Max Müller proposes to change the accent: 'So that we … may for thy favour, for wealth and <i>Ri</i>ta day by day rejoice with cows.'</p>
