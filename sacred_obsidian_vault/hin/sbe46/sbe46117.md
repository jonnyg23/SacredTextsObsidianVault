 <p><a name="page_395"><font size="1" color="green">p. 395</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 13.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 5.</h4>  
 <p>1. Praising we call <a href="sbe46117.htm#an_5:13:1:1">1</a> thee; praising let us kindle <a href="sbe46117.htm#an_5:13:1:1">1</a> thee, Agni, praising, for thy help.</p>  
 <p>2. Desirous of riches, we devise to-day an effective song of praise, of Agni the heaven-touching god <a href="sbe46117.htm#an_5:13:2:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>3. May Agni take pleasure in our prayers, he who is the Hot<i>ri</i> among men. May he sacrifice <a href="sbe46117.htm#an_5:13:3:1">1</a> to the divine host.</p>  
 <p>4. Thou, O Agni, art widely extended, the gladly accepted, desirable Hot<i>ri</i>; through thee they spread out the sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>5. The priests make thee grow, O Agni, the greatest acquirer of wealth, the highly praised one. Bestow thou on us abundance of heroes.</p>  
 <p>6. Agni! Thou encompassest the gods as the felly (encompasses) the spokes (of a wheel). Thou strivest <a href="sbe46117.htm#an_5:13:6:1">1</a> for brilliant wealth.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. The metre is Gâyatrî.—Verse 2 = MS. IV, 10, 2 (cf. TS. V, 5, 6, 1). Verses 2–4 = SV. II, 755–757. Verse 4 = TB. II, 4, 1, 6; MS. IV, 10, 2. Verse 5 = TS. I, 4, 46, 3; MS. IV, 11, 4. Verse 6 = TS. II, 5, 9, 3. As the Sâma-veda forms a T<i>ri</i><i>k</i>a of the verses 2–4, not 1–3 or 4–6, we have here an instance of those liberties which the arrangers of the Sâma-veda not unfrequently took with regard to the Rig-veda text (see H. O., Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenländischen Gesellschaft, XXXVIII, 469 seq.);</p>  
 <p><a name="page_396"><font size="1" color="green">p. 396</font></a></p>  
 <p>we have no reason, in such a case, to resort to such an expedient as changing the traditional order of verses in the Rig-veda text.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:13:1:1">1</a>. We have first the indicative, then the optative.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:13:2:1">1</a>. Divisp<i>rí</i><i>s</i>a<i>h</i> no doubt is genitive sing. referring to Agni, not nominative pl. referring to the worshippers.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:13:3:1">1</a>. Ludwig is right in observing here: 'eigentlich er spreche die yâ<i>g</i>yâs als einladung für die götter.'</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:13:6:1">1</a>. On ri<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ase, comp. Bartholomae, Indogermanische Forschungen, II, 281; Neisser, Bezzenberger's Beiträge, XX, 59. I take the form here as 2nd singular.</p>
