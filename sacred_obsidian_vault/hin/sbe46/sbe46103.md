 <p><a name="page_358"><font size="1" color="green">p. 358</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA IV, HYMN 14.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 5, VARGA 14.</h4>  
 <p>1. Agni <i>G</i>âtavedas, the god, has looked on the dawns that shine with all their might <a href="sbe46103.htm#an_4:14:1:1">1</a>. Come hither, O Nâsatyas <a href="sbe46103.htm#an_4:14:1:2">2</a>, wide-ruling (gods), on your chariot to this our sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>2. The god Savit<i>ri</i> has sent his shine upward, producing light for the whole world. The Sun, shining with his rays, has filled Heaven and Earth and the air.</p>  
 <p>3. The red one <a href="sbe46103.htm#an_4:14:3:1">1</a>, carrying hither (bliss) <a href="sbe46103.htm#an_4:14:3:2">2</a>, has come with her light, the great, brilliant one, shining with her rays. Ushas, the goddess, awakening (all beings) to welfare, goes along on her well-yoked chariot.</p>  
 <p>4. May those chariots and horses, most ready to drive, drive you <a href="sbe46103.htm#an_4:14:4:1">1</a> hither at the break of dawn. For these Somas are for you that you may drink the honey-drink <a href="sbe46103.htm#an_4:14:4:2">2</a>. Rejoice, O manly ones, at this sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>5̅ IV, 13, 5.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi and metre.—No verse of this hymn occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs. On the parallelism in which IV, 14 stands to IV, 13, see the introductory note on IV, 13.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:14:1:1">1</a>. On máhobhi<i>h</i>, see vol. xxxii, p. 196 seq. (I, 165, 5, note 3). Here the word refers to the powerful light of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_359"><font size="1" color="green">p. 359</font></a></p>  
 <p>the dawn, not of Agni, cf. VI, 64, 2. úsha<i>h</i> devi ró<i>k</i>amânâ máhobhi<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:14:1:2">2</a>. On the mention of the Nâsatyas (A<i>s</i>vins) in this connection, compare the introductory note on IV, 13.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:14:3:1">1</a>. The Dawn as before.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:14:3:2">2</a>. That an object like 'bliss' is to be supplied, is shown by such passages as I, 48, 9. úsha<i>h</i> … âváhantî bhû´ri asmábhyam saúbhagam; I, 92, 3. (the Dawns) ísham váhantî<i>h</i> suk<i>rí</i>te sudâ´nave; I, 113, 15. (the Dawn) âváhantî póshyâ vâ´ryâ<i>n</i>i.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:14:4:1">1</a>. The text has the dual of the pronoun. The A<i>s</i>vins are addressed.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:14:4:2">2</a>. It is the peculiar character of the A<i>s</i>vins that they drink mádhu; see Hillebrandt, Vedische Mythologie, vol. i, p. 239 seq. H. O., Religion des Veda, p. 208, note 4; p. 367, note 2.</p>
