 <p><a name="page_261"><font size="1" color="green">p. 261</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 11.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 9–10.</h4>  
 <p>1. Agni is the Hot<i>ri</i>, the Purohita of our worship, he who dwells among many tribes, He knows the sacrifice in due order.</p>  
 <p>2. He, the immortal carrier of oblations, the U<i>s</i>i<i>g</i> <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:2:1">1</a>, the messenger, with satisfied mind, Agni sets himself in motion <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:2:2">2</a> (incited) by the thought (of praying men?).</p>  
 <p>3. Agni takes heed <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:3:1">1</a> (of us) by the thought (the prayer?), the banner of the sacrifice, the ancient one; for his purpose triumphs <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:3:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>4. The gods have made Agni, the old-renowned son of strength, the <i>G</i>âtavedas, their carrier (towards the sacrifice) <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:4:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>5. Agni the undeceivable one who goes before the human tribes, he is the quick chariot <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:5:1">1</a>, ever new.</p>  
 <p>6. Overcoming all attacks, He, the uninjured mind (power) of the gods, Agni, is most mightily renowned.</p>  
 <p>7. Through the vehicle <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:7:1">1</a> (which carries the gods) towards the delights (of sacrifice), "the worshipping mortal attains the dwelling-place <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:7:2">2</a> of (Agni) whose flames are purifying.</p>  
 <p>8. May we, the priests, by our prayers obtain all the blissful gifts of Agni <i>G</i>âtavedas.</p>  
 <p>9. Agni! May we win all the best things in (the trials of) strength. In thee the gods have established them <a href="sbe46071.htm#an_3:11:9:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_262"><font size="1" color="green">p. 262</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. The metre is Gâyatrî.—Verse 2 = VS. XXII, 16; TS. IV, 1, 11, 4; MS. IV, 10, 1. Verses 5, 7, 6 = SV. II, 906–908. Verse 5 = TB. II, 4, 8, 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:2:1">1</a>. Comp. Bergaigne, Religion Védique, 1, 57 seq.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:2:2">2</a>. On the intransitive use of <i>ri</i><i>n</i>váti, comp. Gaedicke, Der Accusativ im Veda, p. 53.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:3:1">1</a>. The meaning seems to be that Agni is intent on his purpose (ártham, Pâda 3); comp. I, 10, 2. tát índra<i>h</i> ártham <i>k</i>etati.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:3:2">2</a>. Comp. Neisser, Bezzenberger's Beiträge, XX, 42.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:4:1">1</a>. See the note on I, 127, 8.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:5:1">1</a>. On Agni considered as a chariot, see Bergaigne, Religion Védique, I, 144.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 7.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:7:1">1</a>. Comp. 1, 127, 8, note 1.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:7:2">2</a>. Comp. above, III, 2, 6.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 9.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:11:9:1">1</a>. I. e. all the best things (Pâda 1); comp. VI, 5, 2. tvé vásûni … â´ îrire ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>íyâsa<i>h</i>.</p>
