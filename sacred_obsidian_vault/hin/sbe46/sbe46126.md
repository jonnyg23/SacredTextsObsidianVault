 <p><a name="page_413"><font size="1" color="green">p. 413</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 22.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 14.</h4>  
 <p>1. Lo, Vi<i>s</i>vasâman! Like Atri sing to him who purifies with his flames, to the Hot<i>ri</i> who should be magnified at the sacrifices, most delightful in the clan.</p>  
 <p>2 <a href="sbe46126.htm#an_5:22:2:1">1</a>. Lay down Agni <i>G</i>âtavedas, the god, the priest. May the sacrifice which best encompasses the gods, proceed to-day in due order.</p>  
 <p>3. We, the mortals, approaching thee, the attentive-minded god, for thy help, have thought of thy desirable aid.</p>  
 <p>4. Agni, be intent on this—on this our word <a href="sbe46126.htm#an_5:22:4:1">1</a>, O strong one. As such, O strong jawed <a href="sbe46126.htm#an_5:22:4:2">2</a> lord of the house, the Atris strengthen thee by their praises; the Atris beautify thee by their prayers.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Vi<i>s</i>vasâman (see verse 1); the metre is the same.—No verse of this hymn occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:22:2:1">1</a>. With this verse compare below, V, 26, 7. 8.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:22:4:1">1</a>. The verb <i>k</i>it stands here first with the genitive asyá, then with the accusative idám vá<i>k</i>a<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:22:4:2">2</a>. Compare vol. xxxii, p. 301 (II, 34, 3, note 3).</p>
