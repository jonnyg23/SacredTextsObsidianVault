 <p><a name="page_281"><font size="1" color="green">p. 281</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 20.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 20.</h4>  
 <p>1. The carrier (of the gods) <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:1:1">1</a> calls by his hymns Agni, Ushas (dawn), the two A<i>s</i>vins, Dadhikrâ <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:1:2">2</a> at daybreak. May the gods rich in light, unanimously longing for our sacrifice, hear us.</p>  
 <p>2. Agni, threefold is thy strength; three are thy abodes; three are thy many tongues, O thou who art born in <i>Ri</i>ta! And three, O Agni, are thy bodies beloved by the gods. With these protect our prayers unremittingly.</p>  
 <p>3 <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:3:1">1</a>. Agni! Many are the names, O <i>G</i>âtavedas, of thee the immortal one, O self-dependent god! And whatever the secret powers of the powerful <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:3:2">2</a> are, thou all-enlivener, in thee they have placed together (those) many (powers), O (god) after whose relations men ask <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:3:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p>4. Agni is the divine leader of the divine tribes like Bhaga, the guardian of the seasons <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:4:1">1</a>, the righteous. May He, the killer of V<i>ri</i>tra <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:4:2">2</a>, the ancient one, the possessor of all wealth, bring the singer across all troubles.</p>  
 <p>5. I invite hither <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:5:1">1</a> Dadhikrâ <a href="sbe46080.htm#an_3:20:5:2">2</a>, Agni, and the goddess Ushas, B<i>ri</i>haspati and the god Savit<i>ri</i>, the A<i>s</i>vins, Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a and Bhaga, the Vasus, Rudras, and Âdityas.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi and metre.—Verse 2 = TS. II, 4, 11, 2; III, 2, 11, 1; MS. II, 4, 4. Verse 3 = TS. III, 1, 11, 6.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_282"><font size="1" color="green">p. 282</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:1:1">1</a>. The 'carrier' of the gods is the Hot<i>ri</i>. See above, I, 127, 8, note 1, and compare the article of Dr. Neisser quoted there. See also M. M., vol. xxxii, pp. 40–43 (I, 6, 5).</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:1:2">2</a>. On Dadhikrâ or Dadhikrâvan, the deified horse of Trasadasyu, see Pischel, Vedische Studien, I, 124; Ludwig, vol. iv, p. 79; H. O., Religion des Veda, 71. Prof. Max Müller writes, 'It seems to me some form of Agni generally in company with matutinal gods.'</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:3:1">1</a>. The reader who compares this passage with 19, 4, will observe a general resemblance pointing to the conclusion that both verses belong to the same author.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:3:2">2</a>. Mâyâ´<i>h</i> mâ´yínâm: comp. concerning the idea of mâyâ´, H. O., Religion des Veda, 163, 294.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:3:3">3</a>. With p<i>ri</i>sh<i>t</i>abandhu, comp. bandhup<i>rí</i><i>kh</i>, bandhveshá.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:4:1">1</a>. Perhaps <i>ri</i>tu-pâ´<i>h</i> should be changed into <i>ri</i>ta-pâ´<i>h</i> 'the god who protects the <i>Ri</i>ta, the righteous.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:4:2">2</a>. Or 'the killer of foes.'</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:5:1">1</a>. Possibly we have to read, on account of the metre, ihá hve.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:20:5:2">2</a>. See above, verse I, note 1.</p>
