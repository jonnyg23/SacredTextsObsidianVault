 <p><a name="page_354"><font size="1" color="green">p. 354</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA IV, HYMN 12.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 5, VARGA 12.</h4>  
 <p>1. May the man who holds the sacrificial ladle and kindles thee, O Agni, who thrice prepares food for thee on this day, victoriously overcome (his foes) through his lustre, wise through the power of thy mind, O <i>G</i>âtavedas.</p>  
 <p>2 <a href="sbe46101.htm#an_4:12:2:1">1</a>. He who toiling brings fuel to thee, doing service to thy, the great (god's) face, O Agni, kindling thee at evening and at dawn—he prospers, obtains wealth, and destroys his enemies.</p>  
 <p>3. Agni is master of mighty royal power <a href="sbe46101.htm#an_4:12:3:1">1</a>; Agni (is master) of gain, of the highest wealth. He, the youngest, self-dependent (god) in the right way distributes treasures to the mortal worshipper.</p>  
 <p>4. Whatever sin, O youngest (god), we have committed against thee in thoughtlessness, men as we are <a href="sbe46101.htm#an_4:12:4:1">1</a>, make thou us sinless before Aditi; release us from (every) guilt on all sides, O Agni!</p>  
 <p>5. Even from great guilt, O Agni, from the prison of gods and of mortals—let us, thy friends, never be harmed; grant luck and weal to kith and kin.</p>  
 <p>6 <a href="sbe46101.htm#an_4:12:6:1">1</a>. As you formerly have released, O Vasus, the buffalo cow bound by the foot, O worshipful gods, thus take away from us this distress. May, O Agni, our life be further prolonged.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_355"><font size="1" color="green">p. 355</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi and metre.—Verse 4 = TS. IV, 7, 15, 6; MS. III, 16, 5. Verse 5 = MS. IV, 11, 1. Verse 6 = TS. IV, 7, 15, 7; MS. III, 16, 5; IV, 11, 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:12:2:1">1</a>. With the beginning of this verse, comp. above, IV, 2, 6.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:12:3:1">1</a>. Comp. Roth, Zeitschr. der D. Morg. Ges., XLVIII, 114.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:12:4:1">1</a>. Grassmann is right in giving to purushatrâ´ the meaning 'unter den Menschen,' and in observing with reference to our passage: 'wo die Bedeutung "nach Menschenweise" (s. purushátâ) besser passt.' The same is the opinion of Böhtlingk-Roth. No doubt we should read purushátâ; cf. VII, 57, 4 = X, 15, 6. yát va<i>h</i> â´ga<i>h</i> purushátâ kárâma; IV, 54, 3. á<i>k</i>ittî yát <i>k</i>ak<i>ri</i>má…. purushatvátâ.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:12:6:1">1</a>. This verse is identical with Rig-veda X, 126, 8.</p>
