 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="green">p. 95</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 75.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 5, VARGA .3.</h4>  
 <p>1. Accept gladly our most widely-sounding <a href="sbe46025.htm#an_1:75:1:1">1</a> speech, the most agreeable to the gods, thou who, in thy mouth, offerest the sacrificial food (to the gods).</p>  
 <p>2. And may we then pronounce to thee, O highest Aṅgiras, Agni, best worshipper, a prayer agreeable to thee and successful.</p>  
 <p>3. Who is thy kinsman among men, O Agni? Who performs worship to thee <a href="sbe46025.htm#an_1:75:3:1">1</a>? Who art thou, and where dost thou rest?</p>  
 <p>4. Thou, O Agni, art the kinsman, the dear friend ('Mitra') of men, a friend who is to be magnified by his friends.</p>  
 <p>5. Sacrifice for us to Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a. Sacrifice to the gods, (a sacrifice conforming to) the great <i>Ri</i>ta <a href="sbe46025.htm#an_1:75:5:1">1</a>. Sacrifice, O Agni, to thy own house.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi and metre.—Verse 1 = TB. III, 6, 7, 1; MS. III, 10, 1 (IV, 13, 5). Verses 3–5 = SV. II, 885–887. Verse 5. = VS. XXXIII, 3; TB. II, 7, 12, 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:75:1:1">1</a>. Comp. VI, 68, 9. manma … saprátha<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:75:3:1">1</a>. May we not take dâ<i>s</i>ú-adhvara as a compound with governed final member, like vidádvasu, sâdádyoni &amp;c.?</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:75:5:1">1</a>. Comp. Gaedicke, Der Accusativ im Veda, 159.</p>
