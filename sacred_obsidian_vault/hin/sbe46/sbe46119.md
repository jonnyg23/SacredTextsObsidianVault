 <p><a name="page_399"><font size="1" color="green">p. 399</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 15.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 7.</h4>  
 <p>1. I bring a prayer to the worshipper, the renowned sage, the glorious, ancient one. Agni is the highly gracious Asura, taking his seat in gh<i>ri</i>ta, the holder of wealth, supporting goods.</p>  
 <p>2. By <i>Ri</i>ta they have supported the supporting <i>Ri</i>ta, near the powerful (performer) <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:2:1">1</a> of sacrifice, in highest heaven, the men who sit <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:2:2">2</a> on the supporting support of the sky, and who with born (men) attained to the unborn.</p>  
 <p>3. Dispelling anguish <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:3:1">1</a> they spread out for the ancient one <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:3:2">2</a> his bodies <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:3:3">3</a>, mighty vital power, difficult to overcome. May he, the new-born, traverse the spaces. They have stood round him as round an angry lion.</p>  
 <p>4. When thou carriest, spreading out, man after man like a mother, for their nourishment and for their sight, when thou growest old <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:4:1">1</a> assuming life after life, thou goest around by thyself in manifold shapes.</p>  
 <p>5. May gain protect now the boundaries of thy strength, the wide, firmly supporting milkstream <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:5:1">1</a> of wealth, O god! Putting down thy foot in secret like a thief <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:5:2">2</a>, thou hast enlightened and freed Atri for the sake of wealth mightily <a href="sbe46119.htm#an_5:15:5:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_400"><font size="1" color="green">p. 400</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Dharu<i>n</i>a Âṅgirasa (cf. dharú<i>n</i>a<i>h</i> vásva<i>h</i> agní<i>h</i>, verse 1; <i>ri</i>tám dharú<i>n</i>am, divá<i>h</i> dhárman dharú<i>n</i>e, verse 2; dógham dharú<i>n</i>am, verse 5); the metre, Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—No verse of this hymn occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:2:1">1</a>. It may be asked whether <i>s</i>âká, beside its meaning 'the powerful (helper),' may also mean 'the power.' This would suit very well, V, 30, 10. sám tâ´<i>h</i> (scil. gâ´<i>h</i>) índra<i>h</i> as<i>ri</i><i>g</i>at asya <i>s</i>âkaí<i>h</i>; VI, 19, 4. tám va<i>h</i> índram <i>k</i>atínam asya <i>s</i>âkaí<i>h</i> ihá nûnám vâ<i>g</i>ayánta<i>h</i> huvema. The translation then would be: 'by the power of sacrifice.' Böhtlingk-Roth conjecture <i>s</i>â´ke.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:2:2">2</a>. I believe that sedúsha<i>h</i> stands for the nominative, cf. devâ´<i>h</i> ábibhyusha<i>h</i>, I, 11, 5; S. B. E. XXXII, p. 28. This sedúsha<i>h</i> led on to a second accusative standing for the nominative, n<i>rî´</i>n.—The men sitting on the support of the sky seem to be the forefathers who have established the universal laws, the Aṅgiras.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:3:1">1</a>. I consider a<i>m</i>hoyúva<i>h</i> as nom. plur. masc., but it may also be gen. sing. masc., or acc. plur. fem., as an epithet either of Agni or of his tanṽa<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:3:2">2</a>. The ancient one (pûrvyá) seems to be Agni (cf. verse i).</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:3:3">3</a>. Cf. VI, 46, 12. yátra <i>s</i>û´râsa<i>h</i> tanṽa<i>h</i> vitanvaté.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:4:1">1</a>. I think, like Ludwig, that <i>g</i>arase should be accented.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:5:1">1</a>. These are accusatives.—Cf. on this passage, Pischel, Vedische Studien, I, 39 seq.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:5:2">2</a>. Cf. H. O., Prolegomena, p. 73.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:15:5:3">3</a>. Cf. VI, 1, 2. mahá<i>h</i> râyé <i>k</i>itáyanta<i>h</i>.—See Geldner, Ved. Studien, I, 268.</p>
