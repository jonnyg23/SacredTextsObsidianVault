 <p><a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="green">p. 127</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 98.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 7, VARGA 6.</h4>  
 <p>1. May we dwell in the favour of (Agni) Vai<i>s</i>vânara. He indeed is a king, leading all beings to gloriousness <a href="sbe46034.htm#an_1:98:1:1">1</a>. As soon as born from here he looks over this whole world. Vai<i>s</i>vânara unites with the Sun <a href="sbe46034.htm#an_1:98:1:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>2. Agni who has been looked and longed for <a href="sbe46034.htm#an_1:98:2:1">1</a> in Heaven, who has been looked for on Earth—he who has been looked for, has entered all herbs. May Agni Vai<i>s</i>vânara, who has strongly been looked for, protect us from harm by day and by night.</p>  
 <p>3. Vai<i>s</i>vânara! May this be true of thee: may wealth and liberal givers attend us! May Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a grant us this, may Aditi, the Sindhu, the Earth, and the Sky!</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. Metre, Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—Verse 1 = VS. XXVI, 7; TS. I, 5, 11, 3; MS. IV, 11, 1. Verse 2 = VS. XVIII, 73; TS. I, 5, 11, 1; IV, 4, 12, 5; 7, 15, 6; TB. III, 11, 6, 4; MS. II, 13, 11.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:98:1:1">1</a>. Comp. VI, 70, 1. bhúvanânâm abhi<i>s</i>ríyâ. Abhi<i>s</i>rî´ seems to mean, going or leading towards (abhí) gloriousness (<i>s</i>rî´). Prof. Pischel's opinion on the word is different; see Vedische Studien, I, 53 seq.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:98:1:2">2</a>. As to yatate, comp. V, 4, 4, yátamâna<i>h</i> ra<i>s</i>míbhi<i>h</i> sû´ryasya; IX, 111, 3. sám ra<i>s</i>míbhi<i>h</i> yatate dar<i>s</i>atá<i>h</i> rátha<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:98:2:1">1</a>. On the disappearance of Agni who is looked for everywhere, see M. M., Physical Religion, 254 seq.; Bergaigne, Rel. Védique, II, 75.</p>
