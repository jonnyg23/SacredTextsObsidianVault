 <p><a name="page_389"><font size="1" color="green">p. 389</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 10.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 2.</h4>  
 <p>1. Agni, bring us the mightiest splendour, O liberal one <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:1:1">1</a>! With wealth and plenty cleave a path for us to booty.</p>  
 <p>2. Thou, O wonderful Agni, (protect) us, through thy power of mind, through the bounteousness of thy strength. Upon thee mysterious power has entered. (Thou art) indeed <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:2:1">1</a> like worshipful Mitra.</p>  
 <p>3. Thou, O Agni, increase for our sake the dominion and the prosperity of those liberal givers, (of those) men who have accomplished liberalities (towards us) for our songs of praise.</p>  
 <p>4, They who adorn prayers for thee, O bright Agni, the givers of horses <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:4:1">1</a>: those men are powerful in their power, whose glory awakes by itself (shining) more mightily than even the sky <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:4:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>5. Those shining flames of thine, Agni, go fiercely along, like lightnings (flashing) around the earth, like a thundering chariot bent on victory.</p>  
 <p>6. Now then, Agni, (come) for our protection, and for the reward of the urgent (worshipper)! May our liberal patrons pass across <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:6:1">1</a> all regions <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:6:2">2</a>!</p>  
 <p>7. Thou, O Agni, Aṅgiras, who hast been praised and who art being praised, bring us, O Hot<i>ri</i>, wealth which overpowers (even) skilful men, to thy praisers, and thou shalt be praised by us. And help us to grow strong in fights <a href="sbe46114.htm#an_5:10:7:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_390"><font size="1" color="green">p. 390</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. Metre, Anush<i>t</i>ubh (verses 4, 7, Paṅkti).—Verse 1 = SV. I, 81. The hymn seems to stand parallel with V, 9.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:1:1">1</a>. On adhrigo, compare above, III, 21, 4, note 1 (<a href="sbe46081.htm#page_284">p. 284</a>).</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:2:1">1</a>. Krâ<i>n</i>â´: see I, 58, 3, note 1 (<a href="sbe46012.htm#page_47">p. 47</a>); von Bradke, Dyâus Asura, p. 35; Pischel, Vedische Studien, I, 71.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:4:1">1</a>. <i>S</i>umbhánti a<i>s</i>va-râdhasa<i>h</i>; see X, 21, 2.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:4:2">2</a>. On the ablative dependent on a positive, compare Speijer, Sanskrit Syntax, p. 78, and see also Delbrück, Grundriss der vergleichenden Grammatik (Brugmann), III, 1, 216; Pischel, Göttinger Gelehrte Anzeigen, 1884, 509.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:6:1">1</a>. On the use of this infinitive, see Delbrück, Altindische Syntax, p. 416.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:6:2">2</a>. The last Pâda is identical with IV, 37, 7.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 7.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:10:7:1">1</a>. The last words are identical with those of V, 9, 16, 17.</p>
