 <p><a name="page_350"><font size="1" color="green">p. 350</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA IV, HYMN 10.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 5, VARGA 10.</h4>  
 <p>1 <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:1:1">1</a>. O Agni! May we to-day successfully perform, with thy heedfulness <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:1:2">2</a>, this praise <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:1:3">3</a> which touches thy heart, which is like a horse, like auspicious power of the mind.</p>  
 <p>2. For verily thou, O Agni, hast become the charioteer of auspicious power of the mind, of real ability, and of the mighty <i>Ri</i>ta.</p>  
 <p>3. Through these our hymns direct thyself hither-wards to us like the sun with its light <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:3:1">1</a>, O Agni, gracious with all thy faces.</p>  
 <p>4. May we to-day worship thee, O Agni, praising thee with these songs. Thy roarings thunder like (the thunder) of Heaven.</p>  
 <p>5. Thy sweetest aspect, O Agni, shines near us for glory's sake, now by day, now by night, like gold.</p>  
 <p>6. Like purified gh<i>ri</i>ta is thy stainless body; (it is) brilliant gold: that (body) of thine has shone <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:6:1">1</a>, O self-dependent one, like gold.</p>  
 <p>7. For even a malice which one has committed, thou verily drivest away entirely, O righteous Agni, from the sacrificing mortal <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:7:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>8. May our friendship, O Agni, our brotherhood with you, the gods, be blessed. This is our navel (i. e. relation) in our seat, in this udder <a href="sbe46099.htm#an_4:10:8:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_351"><font size="1" color="green">p. 351</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. The metre is stated to be Padapaṅkti (verses 4, 6, 7, Padapaṅkti or Ush<i>n</i>ih; verse 5, Mahâpadapaṅkti; verse 8, Ush<i>n</i>ih): see on this metre M. M., vol. xxxii, p. xcviii seq.; H. O., Prolegomena, p. 98; Kühnau, Die Trish<i>t</i>ubh-<i>G</i>agatî-Familie, p. 234 seq.—Verse 1 = SV. I, 434; MS. I, 10, 3. Verses 1–3 = SV. II, 1127–1129; VS. XV, 44–46. Verses 1–4 = TS. IV, 4, 4, 7. Verse 1, 2, 4 = MS. II, 13, 8. Verse 3 = MS. IV, 10, 2. Verse 6 = TS. II, 2, 12, 7; MS. IV, 12, 4.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:1:1">1</a>. The Avasâna in this verse ought to stand before h<i>ri</i>disp<i>ri</i><i>s</i>am, not after this word, as the traditional text places it. <i>Ri</i>dhyâma, consequently, cannot be accented.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:1:2">2</a>. Dr. Neisser's opinion on óha is different (Bezzenberger's Beiträge, XVIII, 312).</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:1:3">3</a>. I read stómam, which is frequently found as the object of the verb <i>ri</i>dh, and which in several passages receives the epithet h<i>ri</i>disp<i>rí</i><i>s</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:3:1">1</a>. On the syntactical form of this comparison, see Bergaigne, Mélanges Renier, p. 95.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:6:1">1</a>. Or ro<i>k</i>ate, 'shines'?</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 7.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:7:1">1</a>. The Avasâna ought to stand before mártât. Cf. above, verse 1, note 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 8.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_4:10:8:1">1</a>. Cf. above, IV, 7, 7. The meaning seems to be: in this sacrificial place, where the cows give milk.</p>
