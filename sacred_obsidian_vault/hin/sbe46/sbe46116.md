 <p><a name="page_393"><font size="1" color="green">p. 393</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 12.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 4.</h4>  
 <p>1. To the mighty, sacrificial Agni, to the bull of <i>Ri</i>ta <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:1:1">1</a>, the Asura, I bring this prayer and this song, which is turned towards him, to (him) the bull, as well-clarified gh<i>ri</i>ta (is poured) into his mouth at the sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>2. O knower of <i>Ri</i>ta, know the <i>Ri</i>ta! Bore for many streams of <i>Ri</i>ta. I (do) not (serve) a Yâtu <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:2:1">1</a> by violence nor by falsehood; I serve the <i>Ri</i>ta of the red bull <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:2:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>3. How, O Agni, performing the <i>Ri</i>ta through <i>Ri</i>ta, mayst thou become a witness of our newest <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:3:1">1</a> hymn? The god, the protector of the seasons, knows of my seasons <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:3:2">2</a>. I (do) not (know another) lord but him who attains (for us) this wealth.</p>  
 <p>4. Who, O Agni, are thy fetterers to (fetter) the impostor <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:4:1">1</a>? What brilliant guardians were successful? Who, O Agni, drink the drink of falsehood? Who are the protectors of untrue speech?</p>  
 <p>5. These friends of thine, O Agni, turning themselves from (thee) <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:5:1">1</a>, they who had been kind, have become unkind. They have harmed themselves by their own speeches, uttering wrong words to the righteous.</p>  
 <p>6. He who magnifies thy sacrifice, O Agni, by adoration, and serves <a href="sbe46116.htm#an_5:12:6:1">1</a> the <i>Ri</i>ta of the red bull: may a large, good dwelling come to him, to the offspring of the advancing Nahusha.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_394"><font size="1" color="green">p. 394</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi; the metre is Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—No verse of this hymn occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:1:1">1</a>. The genitive <i>ri</i>tásya seems to depend on v<i>rí</i>sh<i>n</i>e, not on mánma. On the connection of Agni with the <i>Ri</i>ta, see Bergaigne, III, 229 seq.; H. O., Religion des Veda, 201.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:2:1">1</a>. A bad demon.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:2:2">2</a>. Of Agni.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:3:1">1</a>. I think that návya<i>h</i> stands for návyasa<i>h</i>. Thus Ludwig translates: 'des neuen liedes.' It seems evident that it is not the nominative of návya, 'praiseworthy' (Böhtlingk-Roth, Grassmann).</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:3:2">2</a>. Probably we ought to read <i>ri</i>tapâ´ <i>ri</i>tâ´nâm. Cf. IV, 23, 4. devá<i>h</i> bhuvat návedâ<i>h</i> me ritâ´nâm, and see III, 20, 4, note 1 (above, <a href="sbe46080.htm#page_282">p. 282</a>). The translation will be: 'The god, the protector of <i>Ri</i>ta, knows of my (deeds of) <i>Ri</i>ta.'</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:4:1">1</a>. Or ripáva<i>h</i>? 'Who, O Agni, are the impostors who fetter thee?'</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:5:1">1</a>. On víshu<i>n</i>â<i>h</i>, compare V, 34, 6: ásunvata<i>h</i> víshu<i>n</i>a<i>h</i> sunvatá<i>h</i> v<i>ri</i>dhá<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:12:6:1">1</a>. The second verse (<i>ri</i>tám sapâmi arushásya v<i>rí</i>sh<i>n</i>a<i>h</i>) shows with evidence that for sá pâti we ought to read sápâti (see Roth, Kuhn's Zeitschrift, XXVI, 49, and compare on the expression <i>ri</i>tam sap, Geldner, Vedische Studien, II, 135).</p>
