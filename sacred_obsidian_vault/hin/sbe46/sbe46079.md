 <p><a name="page_279"><font size="1" color="green">p. 279</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 19.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 19.</h4>  
 <p>1. I choose Agni as Hot<i>ri</i> at this sacrificial meal, the clever sage all-knowing and not foolish. May he, the excellent sacrificer, sacrifice for us amid the host of the gods; may he obtain liberal boons (for us) for the sake of wealth and strength.</p>  
 <p>2. To thee, O Agni, I stretch forth the (ladle) rich in sacrificial food, splendid, full of gifts, full of ghee. From left to right, choosing the host of the gods <a href="sbe46079.htm#an_3:19:2:1">1</a>, he has established the sacrifice with gifts and goods <a href="sbe46079.htm#an_3:19:2:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>3. Whoever is favoured by thee, is blessed with the sharpest spirit. Favour him with good offspring, O god rich in favours <a href="sbe46079.htm#an_3:19:3:1">1</a>! Agni, may we, (dwelling) in the copiousness of manliest wealth, be rich in perfect praise of thee, the Vasu.</p>  
 <p>4. On thee indeed, O Agni, sacrificing men have put many faces of (thee) the god <a href="sbe46079.htm#an_3:19:4:1">1</a>. Bring hither then the host of the gods, O youngest one, when thou wilt sacrifice to-day to the divine host <a href="sbe46079.htm#an_3:19:4:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>5. When the gods will anoint thee as the Hot<i>ri</i> at the sacrificial meal making thee sit down for the sacrifice, be thou here, O Agni, our furtherer, and bestow glory on our bodies.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Gâthin Kau<i>s</i>ika, the metre Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—Verse 3 = TS. I, 3, 14, 6; MS. IV, 14, 15.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_280"><font size="1" color="green">p. 280</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:19:2:1">1</a>. Comp. below, IV, 6, 3. This parallel passage shows that pradakshi<i>n</i>ít belongs to urâ<i>n</i>á<i>h</i>, not to a<i>s</i>ret. Agni is represented as choosing, i. e. inviting the host of the gods by moving around the sacrificial food from left to right. See concerning the Paryagnikara<i>n</i>a, which seems to be alluded to, Hillebrandt, Neu- and Vollmondsopfer, 42 seq.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:19:2:2">2</a>. Or, 'with the (divine) givers and with the Vasus.'</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:19:3:1">1</a>. Boehtlingk-Roth seem to be right in reading <i>s</i>iksho. Comp. VIII, 52, 8. yásmai tvám … <i>s</i>íksho <i>s</i>íkshasi dâ<i>s</i>úshe.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:19:4:1">1</a>. They have inflamed many fires, each of which is a face of the god Agni.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:19:4:2">2</a>. Or, 'that thou mayest sacrifice,' &amp;c. See Delbrück, Syntaktische Forschungen, I, 148.</p>
