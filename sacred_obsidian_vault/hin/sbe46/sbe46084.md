 <p><a name="page_289"><font size="1" color="green">p. 289</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 24.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 24.</h4>  
 <p>1. Agni, be victorious in battles; thrust away the plotters. Difficult to overcome, overcoming malign powers, bestow splendour on (the worshipper) who fits out the sacrifice as a vehicle <a href="sbe46084.htm#an_3:24:1:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>2. Agni, thou art kindled with nourishment <a href="sbe46084.htm#an_3:24:2:1">1</a>, the immortal offerer of a feast (to the gods). Accept graciously our worship.</p>  
 <p>3. Agni, wakeful one, son of strength, into whom offerings are poured, sit down with thy splendour on this sacrificial grass of mine.</p>  
 <p>4. Agni, together with all Agnis, with the gods exalt our prayers and those who are respectful at the sacrifices.</p>  
 <p>5. Agni, give wealth to the worshipper, abundance in valiant men; further us <a href="sbe46084.htm#an_3:24:5:1">1</a> that we may be rich in sons.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra. the metre Gâyatrî, the first verse Anush<i>t</i>ubh. On this combination of a beginning Anush<i>t</i>ubh with Gâyatrîs following, comp. H. O., Die Hymnen des Rig-veda, vol. i, p. 148.—Verse 1 = VS. IX, 37. Verse 5 = TS. II, 2, 12, 6; MS. IV, 12, 2; 14, 6.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_290"><font size="1" color="green">p. 290</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:24:1:1">1</a>. See above, III, 8, 3, and on ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>ávâhas, I, 127, 8, note 1; Bergaigne, Religion Védique, II, 287, note 2.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:24:2:1">1</a>. I<i>l</i>â´: especially designating the nourishment coming from the cow (personified as I<i>l</i>â), such as ghee.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:24:5:1">1</a>. Literally, 'sharpen us.'</p>
