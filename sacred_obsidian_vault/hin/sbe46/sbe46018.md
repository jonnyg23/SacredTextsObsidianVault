 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 68.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 5, VARGA 12.</h4>  
 <p>1. Cooking <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:1:1">1</a> (the oblations?) the quick one has approached the sky. He has revealed the nights and what stands and moves <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:1:2">2</a>—</p>  
 <p>2. When he the god, alone of all these gods <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:2:1">1</a> encompassed (the others) by his greatness.</p>  
 <p>3. When thou, O god, hadst been born living from the dry (wood), then all (gods and men?) were pleased with thy wisdom.</p>  
 <p>4. They all obtained the name of divinity, of immortality <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:4:1">1</a>, serving the <i>Ri</i>ta in due way.</p>  
 <p>5. The instigations of <i>Ri</i>ta, the thought of <i>Ri</i>ta <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:5:1">1</a>: they all performed the works of [?] the full-lived one <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:5:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>6. Bestow wealth, thou who art the knowing one, on him who worships thee or who does service to thee <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:6:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>7. He who sits down as the Hot<i>ri</i> among the offspring of Manu: he verily is the master of all these riches.</p>  
 <p>8. They longed together for the seed in their bodies <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:8:1">1</a>, and the wise ones were concordant among each other in their minds.</p>  
 <p>9. They took pleasure in his will, as sons (take pleasure) in their father's (will), the quick ones who have listened to his command.</p>  
 <p>10. He who is rich in food has opened the gates of wealth <a href="sbe46018.htm#an_1:68:10:1">1</a>. The householder (Agni) has adorned the sky with stars.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="green">p. 65</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi and metre.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:1:1">1</a>. Boehtlingk-Roth are wrong in deriving <i>s</i>rî<i>n</i>án (which should more correctly be written <i>s</i>ri<i>n</i>án, comp. my Prolegomena, 477) from the root <i>s</i>ri. They supply an object like <i>s</i>o<i>k</i>i<i>h</i> and translate: 'Licht verbreitend hebt er sich zum Himmel.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:1:2">2</a>. Lanman, 422.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:2:1">1</a>. Bollensen conjectures devâ´nâm devá<i>h</i> (instead of devá<i>h</i> devâ´nâm) which seems to be right (comp. below, 69, 2), though this conjecture is not absolutely necessary (see my Prolegomena, 97):</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:4:1">1</a>. Am<i>rí</i>tam belongs to nâ´ma; comp. V, 57, 5. am<i>rí</i>tam nâ´ma bhe<i>g</i>ire; X, 123, 4. vidát gandharvá<i>h</i> am<i>rí</i>tâni nâ´ma.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:5:1">1</a>. With <i>ri</i>tásya dhîtí<i>h</i> comp. I, 71, 3; IV, 23, 8; IX, 76, 4; 97, 34; 111, 2.—Prof. Max Müller thinks that <i>ri</i>ta should be taken as a name of Agni: 'for the righteous (Agni) are the prayers, for the righteous the devotion.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:5:2">2</a>. Is vi<i>s</i>vâ´yu<i>h</i> an adverb meaning 'eternally'? As vi<i>s</i>vâ´yu is an epithet of Agni frequently used in the Rig-veda and especially in the Parâ<i>s</i>ara hymns (see 67, 6. 10; 68, 5; 73, 4), one feels tempted to read vi<i>s</i>vâ´yo<i>h</i> (comp. IV, 42, 1. râsh<i>t</i>rám kshatríyasya vi<i>s</i>vâ´yo<i>h</i>).</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:6:1">1</a>. Comp. III, 59, 2. yá<i>h</i> te âditya <i>s</i>íkshati vraténa.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="green">p. 66</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 8.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:8:1">1</a>. Some light is thrown on this obscure verse by the hymn, I, 72, a hymn belonging, as our hymn does, to the Parâ<i>s</i>ara collection. It is shown by the second verse of that hymn (see below), that the searching ones, 'ámûrâ<i>h</i>,' are the gods who seek Agni. It seems probable, consequently, that the 'seed' is Agni (comp. I, 164, 35, where Soma is said to be v<i>rí</i>sh<i>n</i>a<i>h</i> á<i>s</i>vasya réta<i>h</i>, 'the seed of the manly horse'). Of the same searching gods in I, 72, 5 the expression sa<i>m</i><i>g</i>ânânâ´<i>h</i> is used; comp. sám <i>g</i>ânata in our passage.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 10.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:68:10:1">1</a>. Râ´ya<i>h</i> must be a genitive; comp. I, 72, 8. râyá<i>h</i> dúra<i>h</i> ví <i>ri</i>ta<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>â´<i>h</i> a<i>g</i>ânan. Probably the accent should be râyá<i>h</i>; comp., however, Lanman, 431.</p>
