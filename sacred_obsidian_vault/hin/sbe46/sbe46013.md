 <p><a name="page_49"><font size="1" color="green">p. 49</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 59.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 4, VARGA 25.</h4>  
 <p>1. The other Agnis (the other fires) are verily thy branches, O Agni. In thee all the immortals enjoy themselves <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:1:1">1</a>. Vai<i>s</i>vânara! Thou art the centre <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:1:2">2</a> of human settlements; like a supporting column thou holdest men <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:1:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p>2. The head of heaven, the navel of the earth is Agni; he has become the steward <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:2:1">1</a> of both worlds. Thee, a god, the gods have engendered, O Vai<i>s</i>vânara, to be a light for the Ârya.</p>  
 <p>3. As in the sun the rays are firmly fixed, thus in Agni Vai<i>s</i>vânara all treasures have been laid down <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:3:1">1</a>. (The treasures) which dwell in the mountains, in the herbs, the waters, and among men—of all that thou art the king.</p>  
 <p>4. As the two great worlds to their son <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:4:1">1</a>, like a Hot<i>ri</i>, like a skilful man, (we bring) praises—manifold (praises) to him who is united with the sun, to the truly strong one, new (praises) to Vai<i>s</i>vânara, the manliest god.</p>  
 <p>5. Thy greatness, O <i>G</i>âtavedas, Vai<i>s</i>vânara, has exceeded even the great heaven. Thou art the king of the human tribes; thou hast by fighting gained wide space for the gods.</p>  
 <p>6. Let me now proclaim the greatness of the bull whom the Pûrus worship as the destroyer of enemies <a href="sbe46013.htm#an_1:59:6:1">1</a>. Agni Vai<i>s</i>vânara, having slain the Dasyu, shook the (aerial) arena and cut down <i>S</i>ambara.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="green">p. 50</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Agni Vai<i>s</i>vânara, extending by his greatness over all dominions, who is to be worshipped, the bright one, rich in loveliness, is awake (or, is praised) among the Bharadvâ<i>g</i>as, in the homestead of Puru<i>n</i>îtha <i>S</i>âtavaneya, with his hundredfold blessings.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi as in I, 58. Metre, Trish<i>t</i>ubh. None of the verses of this hymn occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:1:1">1</a>. Comp. VII, 11, 1. ná <i>ri</i>té tvát am<i>rí</i>tâ<i>h</i> mâdayante, 'the immortals do not enjoy themselves without thee.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:1:2">2</a>. Literally, 'the navel.' Comp. Muir, V, 214.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:1:3">3</a>. Comp. IV, 5, 1 (see below). úpa stabhâyat upamít ná ródha<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:2:1">1</a>. Comp. the remark on I, 58, 7 (note 1).</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:3:1">1</a>. I cannot follow Prof. von Roth (Zeitschrift der D. Morgenl. Gesellschaft, XLVIII, 116), who explains dadhire as a third person sing. of dh<i>ri</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:4:1">1</a>. The incompleteness both of the construction and of the metre shows that the text of the first Pâda is corrupt. I doubt whether it ever will be possible to restore the correct reading with full certainty, but I shall be glad if others succeed better than I did—and I may add, better than Prof. von Roth (Zeitschrift der D. Morg. Gesellschaft, XLVIII, 117 seq.) seems to me to have succeeded—in correcting and in interpreting the text. I think that after sûnáve</p>  
 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="green">p. 51</font></a></p>  
 <p>ródasî clearly one syllable is wanted to complete the Pâda: possibly we should read therefore sûnáve ródasyo<i>h</i> (comp. verse 2, Pâda 2, aratí<i>h</i> ródasyo<i>h</i>, which words form the end of the Pâda). Agni, as is well known, is the son of the two worlds, the sûnú<i>h</i> ródasyo<i>h</i>. In the beginning of the Pâda b<i>ri</i>hatî´ must either refer to the two worlds: in this case we have to read b<i>ri</i>hatyó<i>h</i> (instead of b<i>ri</i>hatî´ iva); or b<i>ri</i>hatî´ may refer, as this adjective frequently does, to the gíra<i>h</i>, and we shall possibly have to read b<i>ri</i>hatî´<i>h</i> va<i>h</i> (as to va<i>h</i>, comp. Delbrück, Altindische Syntax, 206). But of course all these are mere guesses. In every case the verb on which the accusative gíra<i>h</i> depends ('we bring,' or something like that) must be supplied.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:59:6:1">1</a>. Or, as the killer of V<i>ri</i>tra. See H. O., Religion des Veda, 135, note 2.</p>
