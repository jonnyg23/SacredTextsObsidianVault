 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="green">p. 102</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 78.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 5, VARGA 26.</h4>  
 <p>1. O <i>G</i>âtavedas, who dwellest among all tribes, we the Gotamas (praise) thee with our song—we praise thee aloud with (songs full of) splendour.</p>  
 <p>2. Gotama <a href="sbe46028.htm#an_1:78:2:1">1</a> desirous of riches exalts thee, as thou art, with his song. We praise thee aloud with (songs full of) splendour.</p>  
 <p>3. We call thee, such as thou art, the highest winner of booty, as Aṅgiras did. We praise thee aloud with (songs full of) splendour.</p>  
 <p>4. (We praise) thee, the greatest destroyer of enemies (or, of V<i>ri</i>tra), who hurlest the Dasyus away—we praise thee, such as thou art, aloud with (songs full of) splendour.</p>  
 <p>5. We the Rahûga<i>n</i>as <a href="sbe46028.htm#an_1:78:5:1">1</a> have recited a honey-sweet speech to Agni. We praise thee aloud with (songs full of) splendour.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. Metre, Gâyatrî.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:78:2:1">1</a>. This probably means, 'the descendant of Gotama.' See Zeitschrift der D. Morg. Gesellschaft, XLII, 202.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:78:5:1">1</a>. The Rahûga<i>n</i>as seem to be a branch of the Gotamas; see Â<i>s</i>valâyana <i>S</i>rautasûtra XII, 11, 1.</p>
