 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="green">p. 52</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 60.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 4, VARGA 26.</h4>  
 <p>1. Mâtari<i>s</i>van brought (Agni) to Bh<i>ri</i>gu as a gift precious like wealth, of double birth <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:1:1">1</a>, the carrier, the famous, the beacon of the sacrifice <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:1:2">2</a>, the ready and immediately successful messenger.</p>  
 <p>2. Both follow his command, the U<i>s</i>i<i>g</i>s <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:2:1">1</a> offering sacrificial food, and the mortals. The Hot<i>ri</i> (Agni) has sat down before daybreak among the clans, the lord of the clans, whose leave should be asked, the performer of worship.</p>  
 <p>3. May our new, beautiful praise, born <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:3:1">1</a> from our heart, reach him the honey-tongued (Agni), whom the human priests in our settlement <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:3:2">2</a>, the Âyus, offering enjoyment have engendered.</p>  
 <p>4. The U<i>s</i>i<i>g</i> <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:4:1">1</a>, the purifier, the Vasu has been established among men, the best Hot<i>ri</i> among the clans, the domestic <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:4:2">2</a> master of the house in the house: Agni has become the treasure-lord of treasures.</p>  
 <p>5. Thus we, the Gotamas, praise thee, O Agni, the lord of treasures, with our (pious) thoughts, rubbing thee as (they rub down) a swift racer that wins the prize. May he who gives wealth for our prayer, come quickly in the morning <a href="sbe46014.htm#an_1:60:5:1">1</a>.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p><i>Ri</i>shi and metre are the same. No verse occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs.</p>  <h3 align="center">Verse 1.</h3>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:1:1">1</a>. The celestial and the terrestrial birth of Agni. Comp. Bergaigne, Rel. Véd., II, 52.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="green">p. 53</font></a></p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:1:2">2</a>. The text has vidáthasya. Comp. I, 31, 6, note 2.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:2:1">1</a>. On u<i>s</i>í<i>g</i> ('the willing one'), as denoting the mythical priests who have first established Agni and have sacrificed as the first, comp. Bergaigne, I, 57 seq. The ubháyâsa<i>h</i> seem to be these mythical ancestors and the actual sacrificers.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:3:1">1</a>. I propose to read <i>g</i>â´yamânâ. Comp. I, 171, 2. stóma<i>h</i> … h<i>ri</i>dâ´ tash<i>t</i>á<i>h</i>; II, 35, 2. hridá<i>h</i> â´ sútash<i>t</i>am mántram; VIII, 43, 2. ágne <i>g</i>ánâmi sush<i>t</i>utím; V, 42, 13. gíram … <i>g</i>â´yamânâm, &amp;c.—Comp. Lanman, 356.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:3:2">2</a>. On the meaning of v<i>ri</i><i>g</i>ána, see Max Müller, vol. xxxii, pp. xx, 208, 304; Geldner, Vedische Studien, I, 139 seq., with my remarks, Göttinger Gelehrte Anzeigen, 1890, 410 seq.; Ludwig, Ueber Methode bei Interpretation des <i>Ri</i>g-veda, 27 seq.; Colinet, Les Principes de l’Exégèse Védique d’après MM. Pischel et Geldner, 28 seq.; von Bradke, Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morg. Gesellschaft, XLVIII, 500; Bechtel, Nachrichten der Göttinger Gesellschaft der Wiss., 1894, 392 seq.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:4:1">1</a>. See verse 2, note 1.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:4:2">2</a>. Bartholomae's theory (Bezzenberger's Beiträge, XV, 194) that the stem dámûnas has been developed out of the phrase dámû na<i>h</i>, 'in our house,' does not carry conviction.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:60:5:1">1</a>. See I, 58, 9, note 1.</p>
