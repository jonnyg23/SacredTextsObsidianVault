 <p><a name="page_401"><font size="1" color="green">p. 401</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 16.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 8.</h4>  
 <p>1. Sing <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:1:1">1</a> (a song that gives) mighty vital power, to the light, to god Agni, whom the mortals have placed in front <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:1:2">2</a> like Mitra by their praises <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:1:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p>2. For he, Agni, the Hot<i>ri</i> of men, day by day, in the arms of Daksha, discloses the offering in the due way, as Bhaga <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:2:1">1</a> (discloses) a treasure.</p>  
 <p>3. (We abide [?]) in his praise, the liberal (god's), in his friendship, the mightily brilliant one's, in whom, the loudly roaring Aryan, all (beings) have put together their strength.</p>  
 <p>4. For verily, O Agni, (thou belongest [?]) to them <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:4:1">1</a> through thy bounteousness <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:4:2">2</a> in (bestowing) abundance of heroes. Him indeed, the vigorous one, his glory the two worlds could not encompass <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:4:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p>5. Now then, Agni, come hither and, being praised, bring treasure <a href="sbe46120.htm#an_5:16:5:1">1</a> to us who, we ourselves and our liberal givers, may acquire welfare together. And help us to grow strong in fights.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Pûru Âtreya (cf. 17, 1); the metre Anush<i>t</i>ubh (verse 5, Paṅkti). This hymn and V, 17 are parallel hymns; the concluding words of both are identical (see also V, 9, 7; 10, 7).—Verse 5 = SV. I, 88.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:1:1">1</a>. Ár<i>k</i>â may be first or second person.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_402"><font size="1" color="green">p. 402</font></a></p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:1:2">2</a>. Dadhiré purá<i>h</i>: they have made him their Purohita.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:1:3">3</a>. Comp. above, V, 9, 6.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:2:1">1</a>. On Bhaga, the divine Bestower or Dispenser of riches, cf. Herbert Baynes. The Biography of Bhaga (Actes du huitième Congrès intern. des Orientalistes, Sect. II, fasc. 1, pp. 83 seq.).</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:4:1">1</a>. To them, i. e. the Maghavans. Compare below, 18, 3. 4.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:4:2">2</a>. Ma<i>m</i>hánâ seems to be instrumental. Comp. 10. 2; 18, 2.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:4:3">3</a>. Agni is himself yahvá; so ná cannot be the comparative particle, but it must be the negation. Similarly it is said in II, 16, 3 that Heaven and Earth cannot encompass the indriya of Indra; cf. also X, 27, 7.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:16:5:1">1</a>. Vâ´ryam seems to depend on â´ bhara. With the whole phrase compare the first Pâda of V, 17, 5.</p>
