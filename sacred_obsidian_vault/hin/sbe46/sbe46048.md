 <p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="green">p. 178</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 150.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA II, ADHYÂYA 2, VARGA 19.</h4>  
 <p>1. I thy indigent <a href="sbe46048.htm#an_1:150:1:1">1</a> worshipper say much to thee, O Agni, dwelling in thy protection as (in the protection) of a great impeller <a href="sbe46048.htm#an_1:150:1:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>2. Away even from the libation of a rich man who is feeble, who is a niggard, who never comes forward and does not care for the gods.</p>  
 <p>3. The mortal (who worships thee?), O priest, is brilliant, great, most powerful in heaven. May we, O Agni, addicted to thee, be always foremost.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. Metre, Ush<i>n</i>ih.—Verse 1 = SV. I, 97.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:150:1:1">1</a>. On arí, see Bergaigne, Religion Védique, II, 218.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:150:1:2">2</a>. Or, 'of the great impeller'—the Sun-god who impels or stimulates his horses? Comp. VI, 6, 6?</p>
