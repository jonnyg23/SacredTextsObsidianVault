 <p><a name="page_377"><font size="1" color="green">p. 377</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 5.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 8, VARGA 20–21.</h4>  <h4 align="center">ÂPRÎ HYMN.</h4>  
 <p>1. Sacrifice sharp gh<i>ri</i>ta to the well-kindled light, to Agni <i>G</i>âtavedas.</p>  
 <p>2. May the unbeguiled Narâ<i>s</i>a<i>m</i>sa make this sacrifice ready; for he is a sage with honey in his hand.</p>  
 <p>3 <a href="sbe46109.htm#an_5:5:3:1">1</a>. Agni, magnified by us, bring hither to our help the bright, beloved Indra, with easy-going chariots.</p>  
 <p>4. Soft like wool <a href="sbe46109.htm#an_5:5:4:1">1</a> spread thyself (O Barhis). The hymns have been sung to thee. Be to us for success, O beautiful (Barhis)!</p>  
 <p>5. O divine, easily passable doors, open yourselves for our protection. Fill the sacrifice (with bliss) further and further!</p>  
 <p>6. We approach (with prayers) Night and Morning, whose face is beautiful, the increasers of vital strength, the two young mothers of <i>Ri</i>ta.</p>  
 <p>7. On the wind's flight, magnified, ye two divine Hot<i>ri</i>s of man, come hither to this our sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>8 <a href="sbe46109.htm#an_5:5:8:1">1</a>. I<i>l</i>â, Sarasvatî, and Mahî, the three comfort-giving goddesses, they who do not fail, shall sit down on the sacrificial grass.</p>  
 <p>9. Come hither as a friend, Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> and mighty in welfare, and also by thyself, protect us in every sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>10. Where thou knowest, O tree (i. e. sacrificial</p>  
 <p><a name="page_378"><font size="1" color="green">p. 378</font></a></p>  
 <p>post), the secret names of the gods, to that place make the offerings go.</p>  
 <p>11. Svâhâ to Agni and Varu<i>n</i>a! Svâhâ to Indra and the Maruts! Svâhâ to the gods for our offering!</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. Metre, Gâyatrî.—Verse 1 = VS. III, 2. Verse 9 = TS. III, 1, 11, 2. Verse 10 = TB. III, 7, 2, 5.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:5:3:1">1</a>. The first hemistich is identical with I, 142, 4.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:5:4:1">1</a>. On û´r<i>n</i>a-mradâ<i>h</i>, see Lanman, Noun-Inflection, p. 560.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 8.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:5:8:1">1</a>. This verse is identical with I, 13, 9.</p>
