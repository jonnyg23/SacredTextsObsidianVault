 <p><a name="page_443"><font size="1" color="green">p. 443</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAP. XVII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1">Descendants of Druhyu.</font></p>  
 <p>THE son of Druhyu was Babhru; his son was Setu; his son was Áradwat <a name="fr_1189"></a><a href="#fn_1189"><font size="1">1</font></a>; his son was Gándhára <a name="fr_1190"></a><a href="#fn_1190"><font size="1">2</font></a>; his son was Dharma <a name="fr_1191"></a><a href="#fn_1191"><font size="1">3</font></a>; his son was Dhrita <a name="fr_1192"></a><a href="#fn_1192"><font size="1">4</font></a>; his son was Duryáman <a name="fr_1193"></a><a href="#fn_1193"><font size="1">5</font></a>; his son was Prachetas, who had a hundred sons, and they were the princes of the lawless Mlechchhas or barbarians of the north <a name="fr_1194"></a><a href="#fn_1194"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1189"></a><a href="vp110.htm#fr_1189">443:1</a> Also Áraddha in MSS., and Árat́t́a, Matsya, which last seems to be the preferable reading. The Váyu has Áruddha; the Bráhma, Angárasetu; but Árat́t́a is a northern country, contiguous to, or synonymous with, Gándhára.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1190"></a><a href="vp110.htm#fr_1190">443:2</a> Of Gándhára it is said in the Váyu that it is a large country named after him, and is famous for its breed of horses: ###. The Matsya reads the beginning of the second line ###, shewing that Árat́t́a and Gándhára are much the same. See <a href="vp061.htm#page_191">p. 191</a>. n. <a href="vp061.htm#fn_559">83</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1191"></a><a href="vp110.htm#fr_1191">443:3</a> The Bráhma P. and Hari V., in opposition to all the rest, make Dharma and his successors the descendants of Anu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1192"></a><a href="vp110.htm#fr_1192">443:4</a> Ghrita: Agni.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1193"></a><a href="vp110.htm#fr_1193">443:5</a> Durdama: Váyu and Bhágavata. The Matsya, Bráhma, and Agni insert a Vidupa, Duduha, or Vidula, before Prachetas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1194"></a><a href="vp110.htm#fr_1194">443:6</a> So the Bhágavata and Matsya. The Mahábhárata says the descendants of Druhya are the Vaibhojas, a people unacquainted with the use of cars or beasts of burden, and who travel on rafts: they have no kings.</p>
