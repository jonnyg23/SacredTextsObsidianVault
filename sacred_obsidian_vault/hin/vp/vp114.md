 <p><a name="page_461"><font size="1" color="green">p. 461</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAP. XXI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1">Future kings. Descendants of Paríkshit, ending with Kshemaka.</font></p>  
 <p>I WILL now enumerate the kings who, will reign in future periods <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The present monarch, Paríkshit <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">2</font></a>, will have four sons, Janamejaya, Śrutasena, Ugrasena, and Bhímasena <a name="fr_1286"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">3</font></a>. The son of Janamejaya will be Śatáníka <a name="fr_1287"></a><a href="#fn_1287"><font size="1">4</font></a>, who will study the Vedas under Yájnyawalkya, and military science with Kripa; but becoming dissatisfied with sensual enjoyments, he will acquire spiritual knowledge from the instructions of Śaunaka, and ultimately obtain salvation. His son will be Aswamedhadatta (a son given by the gods in reward for the sacrifice of a horse <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">5</font></a>); his son will be Asíma-krishńa <a name="fr_1289"></a><a href="#fn_1289"><font size="1">6</font></a>; his son will be Nichakra <a name="fr_1290"></a><a href="#fn_1290"><font size="1">7</font></a>, who will remove the capital to Kauśámbí, in consequence of Hastinápura being washed away by the Ganges; his son will be Ushńa <a name="fr_1291"></a><a href="#fn_1291"><font size="1">8</font></a>; his son will be Chitraratha; his son</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1284"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1284">461:1</a> The style now adopted is that of prophecy, as Vyása could not consistently have recorded the events which were posterior to his time.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1285"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1285">461:2</a> Also read Paríkshita, Paríksha, and Paríkshi.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1286"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1286">461:3</a> See <a href="vp113.htm#page_457">p. 457</a>. The Váyu and Matsya relate, rather obscurely, a dispute between Janamejaya and Vaiśampáyana, in consequence of the former's patronage of the Brahmans of the Vájasaneyi branch of the Yajur-veda, in opposition to the latter, who was the author of the black or original Yajush (see <a href="vp079.htm#page_279">p. 279</a>). Janamejaya twice performed the Aśwamedha according to the Vájasaneyi ritual, and established the Trisarví, or use of certain texts by Aśmaka and others, by the Brahmans of Anga, and by those of the middle country. He perished however in consequence, being cursed by Vaiśampáyana. Before their disagreement, Vaiśampáyana related the Mahábhárata to Janamejaya. Mahábh., Adi Parvan.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1287"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1287">461:4</a> The reading of the text is rather, 'his (Paríkshit's) other son will be Śatáníka;' but the commentator refers 'his' to Janamejaya. The Váyu, Matsya, and Bhágavata also make Śatáníka the son of Janamejaya. The Bráhma P. has a totally different series, or Paríkshit, Súryápid́a, Chandrápid́a, Janamejaya, Satyakarńa, Śwetakarńa, Sukumára, and Ajaśyáma.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1288"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1288">461:5</a> The Bhágavata interposes Sahasráníka. The Vrihatkathá has the same descent, but calls the son of Sahasráníka, Udayana or Vatsa. The Bhágavata has Aśwamedhaja.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1289"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1289">461:6</a> Adhisáma k.: Váyu. Adhisoma k.: Matsya. The former states that the Váyu P. was narrated in this king's reign, in the second year of a three years' sacrifice at Kurukshetra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1290"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1290">461:7</a> Nemichakra: Bhágav. Vichakshus: Matsya. They agree with the text as to the removal of the capital, and the cause.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1291"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1291">461:8</a> Ukta: Bhág. Bhúrijyesht́ha: Matsya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1292"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1292">462:9</a> Suchidratna, Váyu; Suchidrava, Mats.; Kaviratha, Bhág.; is interposed between Chitraratha and Vrishnímat.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1293"></a><a href="vp114.htm#fr_1293">462:10</a> Sutírtha: Váyu.</p>
