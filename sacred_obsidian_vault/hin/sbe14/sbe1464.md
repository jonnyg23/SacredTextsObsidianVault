   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 5, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 10.</h3>  
 <p>1. Om, I satiate the fathers, Svadhâ, adoration! the grandfathers; the great-grandfathers; the mothers; the grandmothers; the great-grandmothers; the maternal grandfathers; the maternal grandmother; the mother's grandmother; the mother's great-grandmother.</p>  
 <p>2. 'Om, I satiate the teacher (â<i>k</i>ârya), Svadhâ, adoration! the wife of the teacher; the friends; the wives of the friends; the relatives; the wives of the relatives; the inmates of the house (amâtya); the wives of the inmates of the house; all; the wives of all.'</p>  
 <p>3. He pours the water out from the several Tîrthas (of the hand sacred to the several deities). <a name="fr_1239"></a><a href="#fn_1239"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. (He recites at the end of the rite the following <a name="fr_1240"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_256"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 256</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Mantra): '(Ye waters), who bring food, ambrosia, clarified butter, milk, and barley-gruel, are food for the manes; satiate my ancestors! May you be satiated, may you be satiated!'</p>  
 <p>5. Let him not perform ceremonies in honour of the gods while his clothes are wet, or while he is dressed in one garment only,.</p>  
 <p>6. Nor those connected with the manes. That is the opinion) of some (teachers).</p>
