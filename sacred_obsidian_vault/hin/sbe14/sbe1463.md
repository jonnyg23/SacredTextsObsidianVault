   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 5, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 9.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'I satiate the deities of the eastern gate, Agni, Pra<i>g</i>âpati, Soma, Rudra, Aditi, B<i>ri</i>haspati, together with the lunar mansions, with the planets, with the days and nights, and with the Muhûrtas; Om, I also satiate the Vasus; <a name="fr_1238"></a><a href="#fn_1238"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_253"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 253</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. 'Om, I satiate the deities of the southern gate, the Pit<i>ri</i>s, Yama, Bhaga, Savit<i>ri</i>, Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, Vâyu, Indrâgnî, together with the lunar mansions, with the planets, with the days and nights, and with the Muhûrtas; Om, I also satiate the Rudras.</p>  
 <p>3. 'Om, I satiate the deities of the western gate, Mitra, Indra, the Mahâpit<i>ri</i>s, the Waters, all the gods, Brahman, Vish<i>n</i>u, together with the lunar mansions, with the planets, with the days and nights, and with the Muhûrtas; Om, I also satiate the Âdityas.</p>  
 <p>4. 'Om, I satiate the deities of the northern gate, the Vasus, Varu<i>n</i>a, A<i>g</i>a-ekapâd, Ahibudhnya, Ushas, the two A<i>s</i>vins, Yama, together with . . . . . .</p>  
 <p>5. 'Om, I satiate all the gods; the Sâdhyas; Brahman; Pra<i>g</i>âpati; the four-faced god; Hira<i>n</i>yagarbha; Svayambhû; the male attendants of Brahman; Paramesh<i>th</i>in; the female attendants of Brahman; Agni; Vâyu; Varu<i>n</i>a; Sûrya; the moon; the lunar mansions; Sadyo<i>g</i>âta; Bhû<i>h</i>-purusha; Bhuva<i>h</i>-purusha; Suva<i>h</i>-purusha; Bhû<i>h</i>; Bhuva<i>h</i>; Suva<i>h</i>; Maha<i>h</i>; Gana<i>h</i>; Tapa<i>h</i>; Satya.</p>  
 <p>6. 'Om, I satiate the god Bhava; Sarva; Î<i>s</i>âna; Pa<i>s</i>upati; Rudra; Ugra; Bhîmadeva; Mahâdeva; the wife of the god Bhava; of the god Sarva; of the god Î<i>s</i>âna; of the god Pa<i>s</i>upati; of the god Rudra; of the god Ugra; of Bhîmadeva; of Mahâdeva; the son of Bhava; of Sarva; of Î<i>s</i>âna; of Pa<i>s</i>upati; of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_254"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 254</font></a></p>  
 <p>Rudra; of Ugra; of Bhîmadeva; of Mahâdeva; Om, I also satiate the Rudras, the attendants of Rudra.</p>  
 <p>7. 'Om, I satiate Vighna; Vinâyaka; Vîra; Sthûla; Varada; Hastimukha; Vakratu<i>n</i><i>d</i>a; Ekadanta; Lambodara; the male attendants of Vighna; the female attendants of Vighna.</p>  
 <p>8. 'Om, I satiate Sanatkumâra; Skanda; Indra; Shash<i>th</i>î; Sha<i>n</i>mukha; Vi<i>s</i>âkha; Mahâsena; Subrahma<i>n</i>ya; the male attendants of Skanda; the female attendants of Skanda.</p>  
 <p>9. 'Om, I satiate Âditya; Soma; Aṅgâraka; Budha; B<i>ri</i>haspati; <i>S</i>ukra; <i>S</i>anai<i>s</i><i>k</i>ara; Râhu; Ketu.</p>  
 <p>To. 'Om, I satiate Ke<i>s</i>ava; Nârâya<i>n</i>a; Mâdhava; Govinda; Vish<i>n</i>u; Madhusûdana; Trivikrama; Vâmana; <i>S</i>rîdhara; H<i>ri</i>shîke<i>s</i>a; Padmanâbha; Dâmodara; the goddess <i>S</i>rî; the goddess Sarasvatî; Push<i>t</i>i; Tush<i>t</i>i; Vish<i>n</i>u; Garutmat; the male attendants of Vish<i>n</i>u; the female attendants of Vish<i>n</i>u.</p>  
 <p>11. 'Om, I satiate Yama; Yamarâ<i>g</i>a; Dharma; Dharmarâ<i>g</i>a; Kâla; Nîla; M<i>ri</i>tyu; M<i>ri</i>tyu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>aya; Vaivasvata; <i>K</i>itragupta; Audumbara; the male attendants of Vaivasvata; the female attendants of Vaivasvata.</p>  
 <p>12. 'Om, I satiate the gods of the earth; Kâ<i>s</i>yapa; Antariksha; Vidyâ; Dhanva<i>n</i>tari; the male attendants of Dhanva<i>n</i>tari; the female attendants of Dhanva<i>n</i>tari.'</p>  
 <p>13. Next, passing the sacrificial thread round the neck, (he offers the following libations):</p>  
 <p>14. 'Om, I satiate the <i>Ri</i>shis; the great <i>Ri</i>shis; the best <i>Ri</i>shis; the Brahmarshis; the divine <i>Ri</i>shis; the royal <i>Ri</i>shis; the <i>S</i>rutarshis; the Seven <i>Ri</i>shis;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_255"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 255</font></a></p>  
 <p>the <i>Ri</i>shis of the Kâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>as (of the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda); the <i>Ri</i>shikas; the wives of the <i>Ri</i>shis; the sons of the <i>Ri</i>shis; Ka<i>n</i>va Baudhâyana; Âpastamba, the author of the Sûtra; Satyâshâ<i>dh</i>a Hira<i>n</i>yake<i>s</i>in; Vâ<i>g</i>a<i>s</i>aneyin Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya; Â<i>s</i>valâyana <i>S</i>aunaka; Vyâsa; the syllable Om; the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis; the Sâvitrî; the Gâyatrî; the Kha<i>n</i><i>d</i>as; the Rig-vela; the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda; the Sâma-veda; the Atharvâṅgirasa; the Itihâsa and Purâ<i>n</i>a; all the Vedas; the servants of all gods; all beings.'</p>  
 <p>15. Then, passing the sacrificial string over the right shoulder, (he offers the following libations):</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1238"></a><a href="sbe1463.htm#fr_1238">252:1</a> <b>9</b>. This and the next <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>ikâs are given in full by K. only. M. gives the first and last words of both, the commentary the <a name="page_253"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 253</font></a> beginning of 9 and the end of 10 only. The text of K. is probably interpolated, as it seems impossible that Baudhâyana could have mentioned his successors, Âpastamba and Satyâshâ<i>dh</i>a Hira<i>n</i>yake<i>s</i>in, whose names occur below, II, 5, 9, 14. On the other hand, it is not doubtful that the number of Mantras must nevertheless have been very large, as the numeration in M. shows that they filled two entire Ka<i>n</i><i>d</i>ikâs.</p>
