   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, ADHYÂYA 7, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 15.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sacred fires (shall be) approached from the north, <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (And be) left in the same manner.</p>  
 <p>3. The contrary (proceeding should be adopted at sacrifices offered) to the manes. <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 193</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Let him wash that which has been touched with (his) foot.</p>  
 <p>5. Let him touch water, in case he touches his body or the hem (of his garment). <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6: Likewise (let him touch water) after cutting, splitting, digging or removing (anything, or offering oblations) to the manes, to the Râkshasas, to Nir<i>ri</i>ti, to Rudra, (and after performing sacrifices) intended as spells (against enemies).</p>  
 <p>7. Let him not turn round himself a sacrificial implement (the use of) which is accompanied by the recitation of mantras. <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. (For) the sacrificial implements (are) more nearly (connected with the sacrifice), <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. The priests, more remotely.</p>  
 <p>10. The sacrificer and his wife are even nearer than the priests. <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. After the sacrificial implements (follows) the clarified butter, after the clarified butter the sacrificial viands, after the sacrificial viands the animal to be slain, after the animal the Soma, after the Soma the sacred fires. <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 194</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. If there is work for them, the priests shall not turn away from the sacred fires. <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. If he faces the east, let him turn towards his right shoulder, <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. If he faces the west, towards the left. <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. The entrance to the sacrificial (enclosure lies) between the <i>K</i>âtvâla and the Utkara, <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. (When one comes) from the <i>K</i>âtvâla, (it lies between) the Âhavanîya fire and the Utkara. <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. The officiating (priests), the sacrificer, and his wife shall enter by that (road),</p>  
 <p>18. As long as the sacrificial rite is not completed.</p>  
 <p>19. When it has been finished (they shall) pass to and fro on the side where there is no Utkara (i.e. on the western side of the enclosure).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 195</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Let him not put on the fire logs or Samidhs which have not been sprinkled (with water),</p>  
 <p>21. The Brahman (priest) and the sacrificer shall enter in front of the Âhavanîya fire.</p>  
 <p>22. Some (declare that they shall enter) behind the Âhavanîya fire.</p>  
 <p>23. The seat of the Brahman (priest is situated) to the south of the Âhavanîya fire, (that) of the sacrificer to the west of him. <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. (The seat) of the Hot<i>ri</i> (priest is situated) to the north of the northern Sro<i>n</i>i (of the Vedi), <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. (That) of the Âgnîdhra priest near the Utkara,</p>  
 <p>26. (That) of the (sacrificer's) wife behind the Gârhapatya fire.</p>  
 <p>27. He scatters Darbha grass on these (seats) as often as (they are used).</p>  
 <p>28. A vessel filled with water, for the purpose of sipping, shall be appropriated to (the use of) each (person).</p>  
 <p>29. He who has been initiated (to the performance of a sacrifice shall) keep the (following) vows:</p>  
 <p>30. Let him not proclaim the guilt of other men; let him not become angry; let him not weep; let him not look at urine and ordure.</p>  
 <p>31. If he has looked at any unclean (substance), he mutters (the verse), 'Unrestrained is the internal <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_974"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 196</font></a></p>  
 <p>organ, wretched (my) eye-sight; the sun is the chief of the (heavenly) lights; O Dîkshâ, do not forsake me!'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_960"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_960">192:1</a> <b>15</b>. Mânava <i>S</i>rauta Sûtra I, 1, 1, and Kumârila thereon in Professor Goldstücker's lithographed copy and Kâtyâyana <i>S</i>rauta Sûtra I, 8, 24. See also Professor Haug's map of 'the sacrificial compound,' Aitareya-brâhma<i>n</i>a, vol. i.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_961"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_961">192:3</a> I.e. the entrance and exit are to be made to the south of the fires.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_962"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_962">193:5</a> Govinda explains si<i>k</i>, 'the hem of the garment,' by the garment wrapped round the loins (parihita<i>m</i> vâsa<i>h</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_963"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_963">193:7</a> The meaning is that the priest must hold the sacrificial implements, such as the sru<i>k</i> and sruva ladles, between himself and the fires, and not place himself between them and the fires (âtmano bahir na kûryât agner antara<i>h</i> svaya<i>m</i> na bhaved iti yâvat).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_964"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_964">193:8</a> 'He gives the reason for that (rule), "For the sacrificial implements (are) nearer" than the priests, that must be understood.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_965"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_965">193:10</a> 'For they obtain the reward of the sacrifice. The instances (referring) to those two are the Vaisar<i>g</i>anas and the Dâkshi<i>n</i>as:--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_966"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_966">193:11</a> Kâtyâyana <i>S</i>rauta Sûtra I, 8, 31. 'If the space on the <a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 184</font></a> Uttaravedi and the rest is confined, the Soma is made ready immediately after the fire, after that the meat and so forth, after that the grain for the sacrificial cakes, then the clarified butter, and after that the spoons called sruva, sru<i>k</i>, and so forth.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_967"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_967">194:12</a> 'It is indicated hereby that, if there is work (to be done) there, they shall not turn away from the sacred fires except ir cases of absolute necessity.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_968"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_968">194:13</a> 'This rule (refers to the case) when he walks with the sacred fires. It must be understood that he shall not turn his back on the fires.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_969"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_969">194:14</a> 'This rule (is to be interpreted) in the same manner (as the preceding one). Or it is prescribed by these two Sûtras that the men engaged (in the sacrifice) shall go out, turning their right hand towards (the fires).'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_970"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_970">194:15</a> Kâtyâyana <i>S</i>rauta Sûtra V, 1, 11.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_971"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_971">194:16</a> I read with the MSS. of the text '<i>k</i>âtvâlâd âhavanîyotkarau.' For the position of the <i>K</i>âtvâla and the Utkara, see Professor Haug's map, where the road of the priests is also marked, though somewhat differently.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_972"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_972">195:23</a> For the seats of the priests and the other persons named in this and the following rules, see Professor Haug's plan, and Dr. Hillebrandt's Altindische Neu and Vollmondopfer, p. 190.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_973"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_973">195:24</a> 'The northern Sro<i>n</i>i of the Vedi, i.e. the north-western corner of the Vedi.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_974"></a><a href="sbe1448.htm#fr_974">195:31</a> Taittirîya Sa<i>m</i>hitâ III, I, 1, 2, where the rule also is given. M. alone adds another Sûtra, the text of which is corrupt. But it ended with the mantra undatîr bala<i>m</i> dhatta, &amp;c. Taitt. S. ibid. 3.</p>
