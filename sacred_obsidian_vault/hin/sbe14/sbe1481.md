   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA III, ADHYÂYA 9.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now, therefore, we will explain the rule of the Ana<i>s</i>natpârâya<i>n</i>a (recitation of the whole Veda during a fast).</p>  
 <p>2. Let him wear a clean garment or a dress made of bark (or grass). <a name="fr_1386"></a><a href="#fn_1386"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Let him desire food, fit for a sacrifice, or water and fruit. <a name="fr_1387"></a><a href="#fn_1387"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Going forth from the village in an easterly or northerly direction, smearing a quadrangular stha<i>n</i><i>d</i>ila, 'a bull's hide' in size, with cowdung, sprinkling it, drawing the marks on it, sprinkling it with water, heaping fuel on the fire and scattering (Ku<i>s</i>a grass) around it, he offers burnt oblations to the following deities, to Agni Svâhâ, to Pra<i>g</i>âpati <a name="fr_1388"></a><a href="#fn_1388"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_308"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 308</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Svâhâ, to Soma Svâhâ, to all the gods Svâhâ, to Svaya<i>m</i>bhu, to the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as, to the Ya<i>g</i>us, to the Sâmans, to the Atharvans, to faith, to right knowledge, to wisdom, to fortune, to modesty, to Savit<i>ri</i>, to the Sâvitrî (verse), to Sadasaspati, and to Anumati.</p>  
 <p>5. Having offered (these oblations), he must begin with the beginning of the Veda and continuously recite (it).</p>  
 <p>6. Let him not interrupt (the recitation) by talking, nor by stopping, <a name="fr_1389"></a><a href="#fn_1389"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Now if he converses in between or stops, let him thrice suppress his breath, and begin just there where he left off.</p>  
 <p>8. If he has forgotten (a passage), he shall recite for as long a time as he does not recollect it, what (he may know, <i>Ri</i>k-verses) for <i>Ri</i>k-verses, (Ya<i>g</i>us-formulas) for Ya<i>g</i>us-formulas, (Sâmans) for Sâmans.</p>  
 <p>9. He may (also) recite the Brâhma<i>n</i>a of that (forgotten passage) or (the passage from the Anukrama<i>n</i>î regarding) its metre and its deities.</p>  
 <p>10. Let him recite the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ of (his) Veda twelve (times). He thereby removes (faults committed by) studying on forbidden (days, by) angering his teacher, (and through) improper acts. His (knowledge of the) Veda is sanctified, is purified.</p>  
 <p>11. (If he reads) more than that, a cumulation (of rewards will be the result).</p>  
 <p>12. If he recites the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ of the Veda another twelve (times), he gains thereby the world of U<i>s</i>anas.</p>  
 <p>13. If he recites the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ of the Veda another</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_309"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 309</font></a></p>  
 <p>twelve (times), he gains thereby the world of B<i>ri</i>haspati.</p>  
 <p>14. If he recites the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ of the Veda another twelve (times), he gains thereby the world of Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>  
 <p>15. If, fasting, he recites the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ one thou-sand (times), he becomes one with Brahman, resplendent like Brahman (and) Brahman (itself).</p>  
 <p>16. If he subsists during a year on food obtained by begging, he gains (the power of) supernatural vision.</p>  
 <p>17. If during six months he subsists on barley-gruel, during four months on water and barley-flour, during two months on fruit, (and) during one month on water, or performs K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penances of twelve days, he (obtains the power of) suddenly disappearing, and sanctifies seven descendants, seven ancestors, and himself as the fifteenth, and (any) company (of Brâhma<i>n</i>as) which he may enter.</p>  
 <p>18. They call that the ladder of the gods. <a name="fr_1390"></a><a href="#fn_1390"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. By means of that the gods reached their divine station and the sages the position of <i>Ri</i>shis.</p>  
 <p>20. The periods for beginning this sacrifice, forsooth, are three, the time of the morning libation, the time of the midday libation, and the last part of the night, (the Muhûrta) sacred to Brahman.</p>  
 <p>21. Pra<i>g</i>âpati, forsooth, proclaimed this (rite) to the seven <i>Ri</i>shis, the seven <i>Ri</i>shis to Mahâ<i>g</i>a<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>u, and Mahâ<i>g</i>a<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>u to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as. <a name="fr_1391"></a><a href="#fn_1391"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>
