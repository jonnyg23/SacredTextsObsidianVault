   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 3, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 6.</h3>  
 <p>1. Let him not put back into the dish a remnant of food. <a name="fr_1186"></a><a href="#fn_1186"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. If he eats (food), containing meat, fish, or sesamum, he shall (afterwards) wash and touch fire, <a name="fr_1187"></a><a href="#fn_1187"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. And bathe after sunset. <a name="fr_1188"></a><a href="#fn_1188"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Let him avoid a seat, clogs, sticks for cleaning the teeth, and other (implements) made of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood. <a name="fr_1189"></a><a href="#fn_1189"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_242"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 242</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Let him not eat (food placed) in his lap,</p>  
 <p>6. Nor on a chair,</p>  
 <p>7. He shall carry a staff, made of bamboo, and golden earrings.</p>  
 <p>8. Let him not rub one foot with the other while bathing, nor place the one on the other while standing, <a name="fr_1190"></a><a href="#fn_1190"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Let him not wear a visible garland. <a name="fr_1191"></a><a href="#fn_1191"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Let him not look at the sun when he rises or sets. <a name="fr_1192"></a><a href="#fn_1192"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Let him not announce (the appearance of a rainbow) to another (man, saying), 'There is Indra's bow.' <a name="fr_1193"></a><a href="#fn_1193"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. If he points it out, he shall call it 'the jewelled bow.'</p>  
 <p>13. Let him not pass between the prakîlaka and the beam, at the town gate, <a name="fr_1194"></a><a href="#fn_1194"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Nor let him pass between the two posts of a swing. <a name="fr_1195"></a><a href="#fn_1195"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Let him not step over a rope to which a calf is tied. <a name="fr_1196"></a><a href="#fn_1196"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Let him not step on ashes, bones, hair, chaff, potsherds, nor on a bathing-place (moist with) water. <a name="fr_1197"></a><a href="#fn_1197"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_243"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 243</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. Let him not announce it to another (man if) a cow suckles (her calf). <a name="fr_1198"></a><a href="#fn_1198"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Let him not say of (a cow which is) not a milch-cow, 'She is not a milch-cow.' <a name="fr_1199"></a><a href="#fn_1199"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. If he speaks (of such a one), let him say, 'It is one which will become a milch-cow.'</p>  
 <p>20. Let him not make empty, ill-sounding, or harsh speeches. <a name="fr_1200"></a><a href="#fn_1200"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. Let him not go alone on a. journey, <a name="fr_1201"></a><a href="#fn_1201"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. Nor with outcasts, nor with a woman, nor with a <i>S</i>ûdra.</p>  
 <p>23. Let him not set out (on a journey) towards evening.</p>  
 <p>24. Let him not bathe (entirely) naked. <a name="fr_1202"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. Let him not bathe at night.</p>  
 <p>26. Let him not cross a river swimming. <a name="fr_1203"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. Let him not look down into a well.</p>  
 <p>28. Let him not look down into a pit.</p>  
 <p>29. Let him not sit down there, where another person may order him to rise. <a name="fr_1204"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. Way must be made for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, a cow, a king, a blind man, an aged man, one who is suffering under a burden, a pregnant woman, and a weak man. <a name="fr_1205"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. A righteous man shall seek to dwell in a village where fuel, water, fodder, sacred fuel, Ku<i>s</i>a grass, and garlands are plentiful, access to which is <a name="fr_1206"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_244"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 244</font></a></p>  
 <p>easy, where many rich people dwell, which abounds in industrious people, where Âryans form the majority, and which is not easily entered by robbers.</p>  
 <p>32. 'A Brâhma<i>n</i>a who, having wedded a wife of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste and dwells during twelve years in a village where water (is obtainable) from wells only, becomes equal to a <i>S</i>ûdra.'</p>  
 <p>33. (If you say that) he who lives in a town and whose body is covered with the dust, (raised) by others, and whose eyes and mouth are filled with it, will obtain salvation, if he restrains himself, (I declare that) that is impossible. <a name="fr_1207"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. 'The dust raised by carriages, horses, elephants, and cows, and (that which comes) from grain is pure, blamed is (that raised) by a broom, goats, sheep, donkeys, and garments.'</p>  
 <p>35. Let him honour those who are worthy of honour.</p>  
 <p>36. 'A <i>Ri</i>shi, a learned man, a king, a bride-groom, a maternal uncle, a father-in-law, and an officiating priest are mentioned in the Sm<i>ri</i>ti as worthy of the honey-mixture at certain times and occasions.' <a name="fr_1208"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. 'A <i>Ri</i>shi, a learned man, and a king must be <a name="fr_1209"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_245"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 245</font></a></p>  
 <p>honoured whenever they come, a bridegroom and a priest at the beginning of the religious rites, a maternal uncle and a father-in-law when a year has elapsed since their last visit.'</p>  
 <p>38. 'Let him raise his right arm on (entering) the place where the sacred fire is kept, in the midst of a herd of cows, in the presence of Brâhma<i>n</i>as, at the daily recitation of the Veda, and at dinner.' <a name="fr_1210"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. 'An upper garment must be worn on the following five occasions: during the daily study, during the evacuation (of excrements), when one bestows gifts, at dinner, and while one sips water.'</p>  
 <p>40. 'While one offers oblations in the fire, while one dines, bestows gifts, offers (food to deities or Gurus), and accepts presents, (the right hand) must be placed between the knees.'</p>  
 <p>41. 'The revealed texts declare, that the creatures depend on food, food is life; therefore gifts of food must be made. Food is the most excellent of sacrificial viands.' <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1211"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. 'Sin is removed by burnt offerings, burnt oblations are surpassed by (gifts of) food, and gifts of food by kind speeches. That (is declared) to us in the revealed texts.'</p>

But Baudhâyana, G<i>ri</i>hya-sûtra I, 11, 4, says that a man who knows, besides the <i>S</i>âkhâ and its Aṅgas, the Kalpa also, is called <i>Ri</i>shikalpa, i.e. one almost a <i>Ri</i>shi.
