   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXII.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now, indeed, man (in) this (world) speaks an untruth, or sacrifices for men unworthy to offer a sacrifice, or accepts what ought not to be accepted, or eats forbidden food, or practises what ought not to be practised. <a name="fr_636"></a><a href="#fn_636"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. They are in doubt if he shall perform a penance for such (a deed), or if he shall not do it.</p>  
 <p>3. (Some) declare that he shall not do it,</p>  
 <p>4. Because the deed does not perish.</p>  
 <p>5. (The correct view is, that) he shall perform (a penance), because it is enjoined in the revealed texts, <a name="fr_637"></a><a href="#fn_637"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. 'He who offers a horse-sacrifice conquers all sin, he destroys the guilt of the murder of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>7. (Moreover), 'Let an Abhi<i>s</i>asta offer a Gosava or an Agnish<i>t</i>ut-sacrifice,'</p>  
 <p>8. Reciting the Veda, austerity, a sacrifice, fasting, giving gifts are the means for expiating such a (blamable act).</p>  
 <p>9. (The purificatory texts are) the Upanishads, the Vedântas, the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ-text of all the Vedas, the (Anuvâkas called) Madhu, the (hymn of) Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a,  <a name="fr_638"></a><a href="#fn_638"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>  
 <p>the Atharva<i>s</i>iras, the (Anuvâkas called) Rudras, the Purusha-hymn, the two Sâmans called Râ<i>g</i>a<i>n</i>a and Rauhi<i>n</i>eya, the Kûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>as, the Pâvamânîs, and the Sâvitrî,</p>  
 <p>10. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'He who performs once in each season the offerings to Vai<i>s</i>vânara and Vratapati and the Pavitresh<i>t</i>i sanctifies ten ancestors.' <a name="fr_639"></a><a href="#fn_639"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. To live on milk alone, as if one were fasting, to eat fruit only, (to live on) barley gruel prepared of a handful of grain, to eat gold, to drink Soma (are modes of subsistence which) purify. <a name="fr_640"></a><a href="#fn_640"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. All mountains, all rivers, holy lakes, places of pilgrimage, the dwellings of <i>Ri</i>shis, cowpens, and temples of the gods (are) places (which destroy sin).</p>  
 <p>13. A year, a month, twenty-four days, twelve days, six days, three days, a day and a night are the periods (for penances).</p>  
 <p>14. These (acts) may be optionally performed when no (particular penance) has been prescribed,</p>  
 <p>15. (Viz.) for great sins difficult (penances), and for trivial faults easy ones.</p>  
 <p>16. The K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra and the Atik<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra (as well as) the <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a are penances for all (offences).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_636"></a><a href="sbe1425.htm#fr_636">115:1</a> <b>XXII</b>. As this chapter is almost identical with and probably copied from Baudhâyana III, 10, and Gautama XIX, the division of the Sûtras has not been made in accordance with K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>apa<i>n</i><i>d</i>ita's commentary, but agrees with that of the chapter in Gautama's Dharma<i>s</i>âstra. The notes to the translation of the <a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a> latter work must be consulted for the explanation of the more difficult passages.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_637"></a><a href="sbe1425.htm#fr_637">116:5</a>-7. The text appears here to be corrupt. After Sûtra 5, Baudhâyana III, 10, 6 (Gautama XIX, 7), Puna<i>h</i> stomena ya<i>g</i>eta puna<i>h</i> savanamâyântîti vi<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âyate,' It is declared in the Veda, "Let him offer a Puna<i>h</i>stoma-sacrifice, (those who offer it) again come to partake of (the libations of) Soma,"' has been left out. This omission caused the insertion of the words tasmâ<i>k</i><i>kh</i>rutinidar<i>s</i>anât [dar<i>s</i>anât, Bh. F.], ('because it is enjoined in the revealed texts,') at the end of Sutra 5. The proof that the sixth Sûtra of Baudhâyana has been accidentally omitted is furnished by the fact That several MSS. of Vasish<i>th</i>a read iti <i>k</i>a after yo ’<i>s</i>vamedhena ya<i>g</i>ate (Vas. XXII, 6). This <i>k</i>a has no meaning, except if another Vedic passage preceded Sutra. 6. In order to escape this difficulty, K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>apa<i>n</i><i>d</i>ita writes yo ’<i>s</i>vamedhena ya<i>g</i>ata iti, and begins the next Sûtra with iti <i>k</i>a, which he explains by 'moreover.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_638"></a><a href="sbe1425.htm#fr_638">116:9</a> K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>apa<i>n</i><i>d</i>ita gives before 'Vedântas' another word vedâdaya<i>h</i>, which he explains by 'the Vedas, Sm<i>ri</i>tis, and Purâ<i>n</i>as.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_639"></a><a href="sbe1425.htm#fr_639">117:10</a> K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>apa<i>n</i><i>d</i>ita takes the last word da<i>s</i>apûrusham to mean ten ancestors and ten descendants.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_640"></a><a href="sbe1425.htm#fr_640">117:11</a> 'As if one were fasting,' i.e. in small quantities.--K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>apa<i>n</i><i>d</i>ita.</p>
