   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XI.</h3>  
 <p>1. Six persons are (particularly) worthy to receive the honey-mixture (madhuparka), <a name="fr_285"></a><a href="#fn_285"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (Viz.) an officiating priest, the bridegroom of one's daughter, a king, a paternal uncle, a Snâtaka, a maternal uncle, as well as (others enumerated elsewhere).</p>  
 <p>3. (A householder) shall offer, both at the morning and the evening (meals, a portion) of the prepared (food) to the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devas in the (sacred) domestic fire. <a name="fr_286"></a><a href="#fn_286"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Let him give a Bali-offering to the (guardian) deities of the house, <a name="fr_287"></a><a href="#fn_287"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. (Thereafter) let him give a portion, one Pala in weight, to a <i>S</i>rotriya or to a student, (and afterwards an offering) to the manes. <a name="fr_288"></a><a href="#fn_288"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Next let him feed his guests in due order, the worthiest first, <a name="fr_289"></a><a href="#fn_289"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. (Thereafter) the maidens, the infants, the aged, the half-grown members of his family, and pradâtâs, <a name="fr_290"></a><a href="#fn_290"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Then the other members of his family. <a name="fr_291"></a><a href="#fn_291"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (Outside the house) he shall throw (some food) on the ground for the dogs, Kâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlas, outcasts, and crows. <a name="fr_292"></a><a href="#fn_292"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. He may give to a <i>S</i>ûdra either the fragments (of the meal) or (a portion of) fresh (food). <a name="fr_293"></a><a href="#fn_293"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. The master of the house and his wife may eat what remains. <a name="fr_294"></a><a href="#fn_294"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 51</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. A fresh meal for which all (the same materials as for the first) are used (may be prepared), if a guest comes after the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva has been offered. For such a (guest) he shall cause to be prepared food (of a) particularly (good quality). <a name="fr_295"></a><a href="#fn_295"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. For it has been declared in the Veda, 'A Brâhma<i>n</i>a guest enters the house resembling the Vai<i>s</i>vânara fire. Through., him they obtain rain, and food through rain. Therefore people know that the (hospitable reception of a guest) is a ceremony averting evil.'</p>  
 <p>14. Having fed the (guest), he shall honour him. <a name="fr_296"></a><a href="#fn_296"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. He shall accompany him to the boundary (of the village) or until he receives permission (to return).</p>  
 <p>16. Let him present (funeral offerings) to the manes during the dark half of the month (on any day) after the fourth. <a name="fr_297"></a><a href="#fn_297"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. After issuing an invitation on the day preceding (the <i>S</i>râddha, he shall feed on that occasion) three ascetics or three virtuous householders, who are <i>S</i>rotriyas, who are not very aged, who do not follow forbidden occupations, and neither (have been his) pupils, nor are (living as) pupils in his house. <a name="fr_298"></a><a href="#fn_298"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. He may also feed pupils who are endowed with good qualities. <a name="fr_299"></a><a href="#fn_299"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. Let him avoid men neglecting their duties, <a name="fr_300"></a><a href="#fn_300"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 52</font></a></p>  
 <p>those afflicted with white leprosy, eunuchs, blind men, those who have black teeth, those who suffer from black leprosy, (and) those who have deformed nails.</p>  
 <p>20. Now they quote also (the following verses): 'Now, if a (Brâhma<i>n</i>a) versed in the Vedas is afflicted with bodily (defects) which exclude him from the company, Yama declares him to be irreproachable. Such (a man) sanctifies the company.' <a name="fr_301"></a><a href="#fn_301"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. 'At a funeral sacrifice the fragments (of the meal) must not be swept away until the end of the day. For streams of nectar flow (from them, and the manes of) those who have received no libations of water drink (them).' <a name="fr_302"></a><a href="#fn_302"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. 'But let him not sweep up the fragments (of the meal) before the sun has set. Thence issue rich streams of milk for those who obtain a share with difficulty.' <a name="fr_303"></a><a href="#fn_303"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. 'Manu declares that both the remainder (in the vessels) and the fragments (of the meal) certainly are the portion of those members of the family who died before receiving the sacraments.' <a name="fr_304"></a><a href="#fn_304"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. 'Let him give the fragments that have fallen on the ground and the portion scattered (on the blades of Ku<i>s</i>a grass), which consists of the wipings</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 53</font></a></p>  
 <p>and water, as their food, to the manes of those who died without offspring and of those who died young.'</p>  
 <p>25. 'The malevolent Asuras seek an opportunity (to snatch away) that food intended for the manes, which is not supported with both hands;' <a name="fr_305"></a><a href="#fn_305"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. 'Therefore let him not offer it (to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as) without holding (a spoon) in his hand; or let him stand, holding the dish (with both hands, until) leavings of both kinds (have been produced).' <a name="fr_306"></a><a href="#fn_306"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. 'He shall feed two (Brâhma<i>n</i>as) at the offering to the gods, and three at the offering to the manes, or a single man on either occasion; even a very wealthy man shall not be anxious (to entertain) a large company.' <a name="fr_307"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. 'A large company destroys these five (advantages), the respectful treatment (of the invited guests, the propriety of) time and place, purity and (the selection of) virtuous Brâhma<i>n</i>a (guests); therefore he shall not (invite a large number).' <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. 'Or he may entertain (at a <i>S</i>râddha) even a single Brâhma<i>n</i>a who has studied the whole Veda, who is distinguished by learning and virtue, and is free from all evil marks (on his body).' <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 54</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. '(But) how can the oblation to the gods be made if he feeds a single Brâhma<i>n</i>a at a funeral sacrifice? Let him take (a portion) of each (kind of) food that has been prepared (and put it) into a vessel;'</p>  
 <p>31. 'Let him place it in the sanctuary of a god and afterwards continue (the performance of) the funeral sacrifice. Let him offer that food in the fire or give it (as alms) to a student.'</p>  
 <p>32. 'As long as the food continues warm, as long as they eat in silence, as long as the qualities of the food are not declared (by them), so long the manes feast on it.' <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_310"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. 'The qualities of the food must not be declared as long as the (Brâhma<i>n</i>as who represent the) manes are not satiated. Afterwards when they are satisfied, they may say, "Beautiful is the sacrificial food."'</p>  
 <p>34. 'But an ascetic who, invited to dine at a sacrifice of the manes or of the gods, rejects meat, shall go to hell for as many years as the slaughtered beast has hairs.' <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_311"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. 'Three (things are held to) sanctify a funeral sacrifice, a daughter's son, the midday, and sesamum grains; and they recommend three (other things) for it, purity, freedom from anger and from precipitation.' <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_312"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. 'The eighth division of the day, during which the sun's (progress in the heavens) becomes slow, one must know to be midday; what is (then) given to the manes lasts (them) for a very long time.'</p>  
 <p>37. 'The ancestors of that man who has intercourse <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 55</font></a></p>  
 <p>with a woman after offering or having dined at a <i>S</i>râddha, feed during a month from that (day) on his semen.'</p>  
 <p>38. 'A child that is born from (intercourse immediately) after offering a <i>S</i>râddha or partaking of a funeral repast, is unable to acquire sacred learning and becomes short-lived.'</p>  
 <p>39. 'The father and the grandfather, likewise the great-grandfather, beset a descendant who is born to them, just as birds (fly to) a rig tree;' <a name="fr_314"></a><a href="#fn_314"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. '(Saying), "He will offer to us funeral repasts with honey and meat, with vegetables, with milk and with messes made of milk, both in the rainy season and under the constellation Maghâ<i>h</i>."</p>  
 <p>41. 'The ancestors always rejoice at a descendant who lengthens the line, who is zealous in performing funeral sacrifices, and who is rich in (images of the) gods and (virtuous) Brâhma<i>n</i>a (guests).' <a name="fr_315"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. 'The manes consider him to be their (true) descendant who offers (to them) food at Gayâ, and (by the virtue of that gift) they grant him (blessings), just as husbandmen (produce grain) on well-ploughed (fields).' <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. He shall offer (a <i>S</i>râddha) both on the full moon days of the months <i>S</i>râva<i>n</i>a and Âgrahâya<i>n</i>a and on the Anvash<i>t</i>akî. <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">43</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>  
 <p>44. There is no restriction as to time, if (particularly suitable) materials and (particularly holy) Brâhma<i>n</i>as are at hand, or (if the sacrificer is) near (a particularly sacred) place. <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a must necessarily kindle the three sacred fires. <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. He shall offer (in them) the full and new moon sacrifices, the (half-yearly) Âgraya.a Ish<i>t</i>i, the <i>K</i>âturmâsya-sacrifice, the (half-yearly) sacrifices at which animals are slain, and the (annual) Soma-sacrifices. <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. For all this is (particularly) enjoined (in the Veda), and called by way of laudation 'a debt.' <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">47</font></a></p>  
 <p>48. For it is declared in the Veda, 'A Brâhma<i>n</i>a is born, loaded with three debts,' (and further, 'He owes) sacrifices to the gods, a son to the manes, the study of the Veda to the <i>Ri</i>shis; therefore he is free from debt who has offered sacrifices, who has begotten a son, and who has lived as a student (with a teacher).' <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">48</font></a></p>  
 <p>49. Let him (ordinarily) initiate a Brâhma<i>n</i>a in the eighth (year) after conception, <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">49</font></a></p>  
 <p>50. A Kshatriya in the eleventh year after conception,</p>  
 <p>51. A Vai<i>s</i>ya in the twelfth year after conception.</p>  
 <p>52. The staff of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a (student may) optionally (be made) of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood, <a name="fr_324"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">52</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>53. (That) of a Kshatriya optionally of the wood of the Banyan tree,</p>  
 <p>54. (That) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya optionally of Udumbara wood.</p>  
 <p>55. (The staff) of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall (be of such a length as to) reach the hair, <a name="fr_325"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">55</font></a></p>  
 <p>56. (That) of a Kshatriya the forehead,</p>  
 <p>57. (That) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya the (tip of the) nose.</p>  
 <p>58. The girdle of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall be made of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass, <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">58</font></a></p>  
 <p>59. A bowstring (shall be that) of a Kshatriya,</p>  
 <p>60. (That) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya shall be made of hempen threads.</p>  
 <p>61. The upper garment of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a (shall be) the skin of a black antelope, <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">61</font></a></p>  
 <p>62. (That) of a Kshatriya the skin of a spotted deer,</p>  
 <p>63. (That) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya a cow-skin or the hide of a he-goat.</p>  
 <p>64. The (lower) garment of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a (shall be) white (and) unblemished, <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">64</font></a></p>  
 <p>65. (That) of a Kshatriya dyed with madder,</p>  
 <p>66. (That) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya dyed with turmeric, or made of (raw) silk;</p>  
 <p>67. Or (a dress made of) undyed (cotton) cloth may be worn by (students of) all (castes).</p>  
 <p>68. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall ask for alms placing (the word) 'Lady' first, <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_329"><font size="1">68</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>69. A Kshatriya placing (the word)' Lady' in the middle,</p>  
 <p>70. A Vai<i>s</i>ya placing (the word) Lady' at the end (of the formula).</p>  
 <p>71. The time (for the initiation) of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a has not passed until the completion of the sixteenth year, <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">71</font></a></p>  
 <p>72. (For that) of a Kshatriya until the completion of the twenty-second,</p>  
 <p>73. (For that) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya until the completion of the twenty-fourth.</p>  
 <p>74. After that they become 'men whose Sâvitrî has been neglected.' <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">74</font></a></p>  
 <p>75. Let him not initiate such men, nor teach them, nor sacrifice for them; let them not form matrimonial alliances (with such outcasts). <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">75</font></a></p>  
 <p>76. A man whose Sâvitrî has not been performed, may undergo the Uddâlaka-penance.</p>  
 <p>77. Let him subsist during two months on barley-gruel, during one month on milk, during half a month on curds of two-milk whey, during eight days on clarified butter, during six days on alms given without asking, (and) during three days on water, and let him fast for one day and one night.</p>  
 <p>78. (Or) he may go to bathe (with the priests) at the end of an A<i>s</i>vamedha (horse-sacrifice). <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">78</font></a></p>  
 <p>79. Or he may offer a Vrâtya-stoma. <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">79</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>

Pradâtâ<i>h</i> must be the correct reading, and a technical name for a class of female relatives. Etymologically it may mean 'those who have been perfectly cleansed.' But I am unable to trace its precise technical import, and have left it untranslated.</p>
