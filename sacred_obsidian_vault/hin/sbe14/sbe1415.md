 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XII.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now, therefore, the duties of a Snâtaka (will be explained). <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Let him not beg from anybody except from a king and a pupil. <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. But let him ask, if pressed by hunger, for some (small gift) only, a cultivated or uncultivated field, a cow, a goat or a sheep, (or) at the last extremity, for gold, grain or food. <a name="fr_337"></a><a href="#fn_337"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. But the injunction (given by those who know the law) is, 'A Snâtaka shall not be faint with hunger.' <a name="fr_338"></a><a href="#fn_338"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Let him not dwell together with a person whose clothes are foul; <a name="fr_339"></a><a href="#fn_339"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. (Let him not cohabit) with a woman during her courses,</p>  
 <p>7. Nor with an unfit one. <a name="fr_340"></a><a href="#fn_340"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Let him not be a stay-at-home. <a name="fr_341"></a><a href="#fn_341"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Let him not step over a stretched rope to which a calf (or cow) is tied. <a name="fr_342"></a><a href="#fn_342"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Let him not look at the sun when he rises or sets. <a name="fr_343"></a><a href="#fn_343"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Let him not void excrements or urine in water, <a name="fr_344"></a><a href="#fn_344"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Nor spit into it.</p>  
 <p>13. Let him ease himself, after wrapping up his head and covering the ground with grass that is not fit to be used at a sacrifice, and turning towards the north in the day-time, turning towards the south at night, sitting with his face towards the north in the twilight. <a name="fr_345"></a><a href="#fn_345"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Now they quote also (the following verses): 'But Snâtakas shall always wear a lower garment and an upper one, two sacrificial threads, (shall carry) a staff and a vessel filled with water.' <a name="fr_346"></a><a href="#fn_346"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. 'It is declared, that (a vessel becomes) pure (if cleaned) with water, or with the hand, or with a stick, or with fire: Therefore he shall clean (his) vessel with water and with his (right) hand.'</p>  
 <p>16. 'For Manu, the lord of created beings, calls (this mode of cleaning) encircling it-with fire.'</p>  
 <p>17. He who is perfectly acquainted with (the rules of) purification shall sip water (out of this vessel), after he has relieved the necessities of nature.'</p>  
 <p>18. Let him eat his food facing the east. <a name="fr_347"></a><a href="#fn_347"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 61</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. Silently let him swallow the entire mouthful, (introducing it into the mouth) with the four fingers and with the thumb; <a name="fr_348"></a><a href="#fn_348"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. And let him not make a noise (while eating).</p>  
 <p>21. Let him approach his wife in the proper season, except on the Parva days. <a name="fr_349"></a><a href="#fn_349"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. Let him not commit a crime against nature (with her).</p>  
 <p>23. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'The ancestors of a man-who commits an unnatural crime with a wedded wife, feed during that month on his semen. All unnatural intercourse is against the sacred law.'</p>  
 <p>24. It is also declared in the Kâ<i>th</i>aka, '(When) the women (asked) Indra, "May even those among us, who are soon to be mothers, (be allowed to) cohabit with their husbands," he granted that wish.'</p>  
 <p>25. Let him not ascend a tree. <a name="fr_350"></a><a href="#fn_350"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. Let him not descend into a well.</p>  
 <p>27. Let him not blow the fire with his mouth.</p>  
 <p>28. Let him not pass between a fire and a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, <a name="fr_351"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. Nor between two fires;</p>  
 <p>30. Nor between two Brâhma<i>n</i>as; or (he may do it) after having asked for permission. <a name="fr_352"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. Let him not dine together with his wife. For it is declared in the Vâ<i>g</i>asaneyaka, His children will be destitute of manly vigour.' <a name="fr_353"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 62</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. Let him not point out (a rainbow calling it) by (its proper) name, 'Indra's bow.' <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_354"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. Let him call it 'the jewelled bow' (ma<i>n</i>idhanu<i>h</i>).</p>  
 <p>34. Let him avoid seats, clogs, sticks for cleaning the teeth, (and other implements) made of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood. <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. Let him not eat (food placed) in his lap. <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. Let him not eat (food placed) on a chair. <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. Let him carry a staff of bamboo, <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. And (wear) two golden earrings. <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. Let him not wear any visible wreath excepting a golden one; <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. And let him disdain assemblies and crowds. <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">40</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'To deny the authority of the Vedas, to carp at the teaching of the <i>Ri</i>shis, to waver with respect to any (matter of duty), that is to destroy one's soul.' <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. Let him not go to a sacrifice except if he is chosen (to be an officiating priest. But) if he goes,. he must, on returning home, turn his right hand (towards the place). <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. Let him not set out on a journey when the sun stands over the trees. <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">43</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p>44. Let him not ascend an unsafe boat, or (any unsafe conveyance). <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. Let him not cross a river, swimming. <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. When he has risen in the last watch (of the night) and has recited (the Veda) he shall not lie down again. <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. In the Muhûrta sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati a Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall fulfil some sacred duties. <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">47</font></a></p>
