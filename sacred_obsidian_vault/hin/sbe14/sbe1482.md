 <p><a name="page_310"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 310</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA III, ADHYÂYA 10.</h3>  
 <p>1. The law of castes and of orders has been declared. <a name="fr_1392"></a><a href="#fn_1392"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Now, indeed, man (in) this (world is polluted) by a vile action or acts wrongly, (e.g.) sacrifices for men. unworthy to offer a sacrifice, accepts presents from those whose gifts ought not to be accepted, eats the food of those whose food ought not to be eaten, (and) practises what is forbidden. <a name="fr_1393"></a><a href="#fn_1393"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. They are in doubt if he shall perform a penance for such (a deed) or if he shall not do it.</p>  
 <p>4. (They declare that he shall not do it) because the deed does not perish.</p>  
 <p>5. (The correct opinion is) that he shall perform (a penance). <a name="fr_1394"></a><a href="#fn_1394"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. It is declared in the Veda, 'Let him offer a Punastoma; (those who offer it, may) again come to (partake of) the libations of Soma.' <a name="fr_1395"></a><a href="#fn_1395"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. He who offers a horse-sacrifice conquers all sin, he effaces the guilt of the murder of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a.' <a name="fr_1396"></a><a href="#fn_1396"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_311"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 311</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Moreover, 'He who is being accused (of a heinous crime) shall perform an Agnish<i>t</i>ut sacrifice.'</p>  
 <p>9. Reciting the Veda, austerity, a sacrifice, fasting, giving gifts are the means for expiating such (a blamable act).</p>  
 <p>10. The purificatory (texts are), the Upanishads, the initial (verses) of the Vedas, the ends of the Vedas (vedântas), the Sa<i>m</i>hitâs of all the Vedas, (the Anuvâkas called) Madhu, (the hymn of) Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a, the Atharva<i>s</i>iras, (the Anuvâkas called the) Rudras, the Purusha hymn, the two Sâmans (called) Râ<i>g</i>ina and Rauhi<i>n</i>eya, the B<i>ri</i>hat (Sâman) and the Rathantara, the Purushagati (Sâman), the Mahânâmnîs, the Mahâvairâ<i>g</i>a (Sâman), the Mahâdivâkîrtya (Sâman), any of the <i>G</i>yesh<i>th</i>a Sâmans, the Bahishpavamâna Sâman, the Kûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>îs, the Pâvamânîs, and the Sâvitrî. <a name="fr_1397"></a><a href="#fn_1397"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. To live on milk alone, as if one were fasting, to eat vegetables only, to eat fruit only, (to live on) gruel prepared of a handful of barley-grains, to eat gold, to eat clarified butter (are the modes of subsistence) which purify.</p>  
 <p>12. All mountains, all rivers, holy lakes, bathing-places, the dwellings of <i>Ri</i>shis, cowpens, (holy) plains and temples of the gods (are) places (which destroy sin) <a name="fr_1398"></a><a href="#fn_1398"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_312"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 312</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Abstention from injuring living beings, truthfulness, abstention from theft (or unrighteously appropriating anything), bathing in the morning, at noon, and in the evening, obedience towards Gurus, continence, sleeping on the ground, dressing in one garment only, and abstaining from food (are the various kinds of) austerity.</p>  
 <p>14. Gold, a cow, a dress, a horse, land, sesamum, clarified butter, and food (are) the gifts.</p>  
 <p>15. A year, six months, four (months), three (months), two (months), one (month), twenty-four days, twelve days, six days, three days, a day and a night, (and) one day are the periods (for penances).</p>  
 <p>16. These (acts) may be optionally performed if no (particular penance) has been prescribed,</p>  
 <p>17. (Viz.) for great crimes difficult (penances) and for trivial faults easy ones.</p>  
 <p>18. The K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra and the Atik<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra, as well as the <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a, are penances for all (offences).</p>
