   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 2, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'Anybody but a Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall suffer corporal punishment for adultery.' <a name="fr_1150"></a><a href="#fn_1150"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. 'The wives (of men) of all castes must be guarded more carefully than wealth.' <a name="fr_1151"></a><a href="#fn_1151"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. 'But corporal punishment (shall) not (be inflicted) for (adultery with) the wives of minstrels and with those who appear on the stage. For (the husbands) carry them (to other men), or, lying concealed (at home), permit them to hold culpable intercourse.' <a name="fr_1152"></a><a href="#fn_1152"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. 'Women (possess) an unrivalled means of purification; they never become (entirely) foul. For month by month their temporary uncleanness removes their sins.' <a name="fr_1153"></a><a href="#fn_1153"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. 'Soma gave them cleanliness, the Gandharva their melodious voice, and Fire purity of all (limbs); therefore women are free from stains.' <a name="fr_1154"></a><a href="#fn_1154"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_234"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 234</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. 'Let him abandon a barren (wife) in the tenth year, one who bears daughters (only) in the twelfth, one whose children (all) die in the fifteenth, but her who is quarrelsome without delay.' <a name="fr_1155"></a><a href="#fn_1155"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. A widow shall avoid during a year (the use of) honey, meat, spirituous liquor, and salt, and sleep on the ground. <a name="fr_1156"></a><a href="#fn_1156"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Maudgalya (declares that she shall do so) during six months.</p>  
 <p>9. After (the expiration of) that (time) she may, with the permission of her Gurus, bear a son to her brother-in-law, in case she has no son. <a name="fr_1157"></a><a href="#fn_1157"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Now they quote also (the following verse): One whose appointment can have no result, (viz.) a barren woman, one who has borne sons, one who is past child-bearing, one whose children are (all) dead, and one who is unwilling must not be appointed.' <a name="fr_1158"></a><a href="#fn_1158"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. The sister of a maternal uncle and of the father, a sister, a sister's daughter, a daughter-in-law, a maternal uncle's wife, and the wife of a <a name="fr_1159"></a><a href="#fn_1159"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 235</font></a></p>  
 <p>friend are females who must never be approached (agamyâ).</p>  
 <p>12. For intercourse with females who must not be approached (agamyâ), a K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra and an Atik<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra (and) a <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a are the penances prescribed for all.</p>  
 <p>13. Thereby (the rule regarding) intercourse with a female of the <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla caste has been declared. <a name="fr_1160"></a><a href="#fn_1160"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Now they quote also (the following verses): A Brâhma<i>n</i>a who unintentionally approaches a female of the <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla caste, eats (food given by a <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla) or receives (presents from him), becomes an outcast; but (if he does it) intentionally, he becomes equal (to a <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla). <a name="fr_1161"></a><a href="#fn_1161"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. 'He who approaches his father's, his teacher's, or a king's wife, is guilty of the crime of violating a Guru's bed; the penance ordained for him has been declared above.' <a name="fr_1162"></a><a href="#fn_1162"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. (A Brâhma<i>n</i>a) who is unable (to subsist) by teaching, sacrificing for others, or the acceptance of gifts, shall maintain himself by following the duties of Kshatriyas, because that is the next following (caste). <a name="fr_1163"></a><a href="#fn_1163"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_236"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 236</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. Gautama (declares that one shall) not (act thus). For the duties of Kshatriyas are too cruel for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a. <a name="fr_1164"></a><a href="#fn_1164"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'Out of regard for the sacred law a Brâhma<i>n</i>a and a Vai<i>s</i>ya may take up arms for (the protection of) cows or Brâhma<i>n</i>as, or when a confusion of the castes (threatens to take place). <a name="fr_1165"></a><a href="#fn_1165"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. (Or) the livelihood of a Vai<i>s</i>ya should be adopted, because that is the one following (next): <a name="fr_1166"></a><a href="#fn_1166"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. (If he lives by agriculture) he shall plough before breakfast, <a name="fr_1167"></a><a href="#fn_1167"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. With two bulls whose noses have not been pierced, not striking them with the goad, (but) frequently coaxing them.</p>  
 <p>22. The (sacred domestic) fire (shall be kindled) at the wedding; the religious ceremonies up to the Agnyâdheya (shall be) performed in that. <a name="fr_1168"></a><a href="#fn_1168"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Now, beginning with the Agnyâdheya, follow these (rites in an) uninterrupted (series), as, for instance, the Agnyâdheya, the Agnihotra, the new and full moon sacrifices, the Âgraya.a at the winter and summer solstices, the animal sacrifice, the <i>K</i>âturmâsyas at the beginning of each season, the Sha<i>d</i><i>dh</i>ot<i>ri</i> in spring, the Agnish<i>t</i>oma. Thus the attainment of bliss (is secured). <a name="fr_1169"></a><a href="#fn_1169"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'Neither he who is accustomed to sleep in the day-time, nor he who eats the food of anybody, nor he who falls from a height to which he has climbed, can reach heaven as he desires.' <a name="fr_1170"></a><a href="#fn_1170"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. Let him avoid meanness, hard-heartedness, and crookedness. <a name="fr_1171"></a><a href="#fn_1171"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. Now they quote also with reference to this (subject the following) verse in the dialogue between the daughters of U<i>s</i>anas and V<i>ri</i>shaparvan: 'Thou, forsooth, art the daughter of one who praises (others), who begs and accepts (gifts); but I am the child of one who is praised, who gives gifts and does not accept them.' <a name="fr_1172"></a><a href="#fn_1172"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1150"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1150">233:1</a> <b>4</b>. Âpastamba II, 10, 26, 20; 10, 27, 11. Govinda thinks that non-Brâhmanical offenders should be burned, in accordance with Vasish<i>th</i>a XXI, 2-3. But mutilation may also be intended. Sa<i>m</i>graha<i>n</i>a, 'adultery,' probably includes all those acts mentioned Manu VIII, 354-358.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1151"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1151">233:2</a> Manu VIII, 359.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1152"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1152">233:3</a> Manu VIII, 362. I read conjecturally, 'sa<i>m</i>sar<i>g</i>ayanti tâ hyetâ niguptâ<i>s</i> <i>k</i>âlayantyapi,' basing my emendations on Manu's text. The MSS. and Govinda have, sa<i>m</i>sar<i>g</i>ayanti tâ hyetân niguptâ<i>m</i><i>s</i> <i>k</i>âlayanty api, which gives no good sense. Govinda explains <i>k</i>âra<i>n</i>adârâ<i>h</i>, 'the wives of minstrels,' by devadâsya<i>h</i>, 'temple-slaves.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1153"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1153">233:4</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XXVIII, 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1154"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1154">233:5</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XXVIII, 6.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1155"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1155">234:6</a> Manu IX, 81.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1156"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1156">234:7</a>-8. Vasish<i>th</i>a XVII, 55. The word madya, 'spirituous liquor,' occurs in M. and the I. O. copy of the commentary. The MSS. from the Dekhan and Gu<i>g</i>arât, including K., read ma<i>g</i><i>g</i>ana or ma<i>d</i><i>d</i>âna, the compound letter being very indistinct.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1157"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1157">234:9</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XVII, 56, where the term 'Gurus' is fully explained.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1158"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1158">234:10</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XVII, 57-59. M. and the two copies of the commentary read pi<i>s</i>â<i>k</i>otpannaputrâ <i>k</i>a instead of vasâ <i>k</i>otpannaputrâ <i>k</i>a, 'a barren woman and one who has borne sons.' I follow the Dekhan and Gu<i>g</i>arât MSS., which undoubtedly give the genuine reading. Perhaps the term ava<i>s</i>âm, Vasish<i>th</i>a XVII, 57, should be corrected to va<i>s</i>âm.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1159"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1159">234:11</a>-12. These two Sûtras are additions to II, 1, 2, 13. See also Nârada XII, 73-74; Vasish<i>th</i>a XXI, 16.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1160"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1160">235:13</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XXIII, 41; Vish<i>n</i>u LIII, 5-6.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1161"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1161">235:14</a> Manu XI, 176.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1162"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1162">235:15</a> Govinda thinks that the penance intended is that mentioned in Sûtra 12. Probably a severer one is meant. The verse is interesting, as it clearly is a quotation from some metrical work on law, not merely of traditional detached <i>s</i>lokas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1163"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1163">235:16</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a II, 22. The Sûtra 'adhyâpanayâ<i>g</i>anapratigrahair a<i>s</i>akta<i>h</i> kshatradharme<i>n</i>a <i>g</i>îvet pratyanantaratvât' occurs in the two copies of the commentary only. The I. O. copy of the commentary has, however, before it the following words: [dharmya] svâdhyâyaprava<i>k</i>ane evety adhikânâ<i>m</i> [kâra<i>m</i>] dar<i>s</i>ayati pratig<i>ri</i>hîtâ tâd<i>ri</i>k pratigrahîtâra<i>m</i> g<i>ri</i>dhnuvanti [târa <i>ri</i>dhnu°] <i>ri</i>tvigya<i>g</i>amânâ yâ<i>g</i>inau <a name="page_236"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 236</font></a>[°<i>g</i>ane] tada<i>s</i>aktau kshatradharmau. M. reads, dharmanasvâdhyâyaprava<i>k</i>ana [ne] ityadhikâra<i>n</i> dar<i>s</i>ayati pratigrahîtâd<i>ri</i>k pratigrahîtâra <i>ri</i>dhnuvanti <i>ri</i>tvigya<i>g</i>amâna yâ<i>g</i>anau tada<i>s</i>aktau svâdhyâyadhyâ [°yâdhya] panaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>ayâ<i>g</i>anapratigrahair a<i>s</i>aktama [ta<i>h</i>] kshatradharmme<i>n</i>a gîvet. The Dekhan and Gu<i>g</i>arât MSS. read, dhârmye svâdhyâyaprava<i>k</i>ane ityadhikâra<i>m</i> dar<i>s</i>ayati | pratigrahe dâtâ pratigrahîtâ[ra] <i>ri</i>dhnuvanti | <i>ri</i>tvigya<i>g</i>amânâ yâ<i>g</i>ane | tada<i>s</i>aktau kshatradharme<i>n</i>a <i>g</i>îvayet, or have corruptions of this passage. I cannot come to any other conclusion than that the passage which precedes the words translated by me are a very ancient interpolation, caused by the embodiment of a portion of an old Bhâshya with the text, and that all our MSS., however much they may differ, go back to one codex archetypus.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1164"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1164">236:17</a> Gautama Introduction, p. lii.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1165"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1165">236:18</a> Gautama VII, 25.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1166"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1166">236:19</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a II, 24.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1167"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1167">236:20</a>-21. Vasish<i>th</i>a II, 32.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1168"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1168">236:22</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a VIII, 3. The religious ceremonies to be performed with the sacred domestic fire, which, according to Baudhâyana, <a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a> should be kindled at the wedding, not on the division of the paternal estate (Gautama V, q), are the so-called G<i>ri</i>hya ceremonies (Gautama V, 8-9). </p>
 <p><a name="fn_1169"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1169">237:23</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XI, 46. The sacrifices enumerated in this Sûtra require three fires, and belong to the <i>s</i>rauta or vaitânika ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>as. The Sha<i>d</i><i>dh</i>ot<i>ri</i> mentioned here seems to be the animal sacrifice mentioned in the commentary on Kâtyâyana <i>S</i>rauta-sûtra VI, 1, 36.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1170"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1170">237:24</a> An ârû<i>dh</i>apatita, 'he who falls from a height to which he has climbed,' is, according to Govinda, an ascetic who slides back into civil life.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1171"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1171">237:25</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a VI, 40; X, 30. Govinda explains <i>s</i>â<i>th</i>yam, 'hard-heartedness,' by <i>s</i>aktau satyâm api paropakârâ<i>k</i>ara<i>n</i>am, 'not doing a kindness to others though one is able to do so.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1172"></a><a href="sbe1458.htm#fr_1172">237:26</a> The dialogue mentioned is that between <i>S</i>armish<i>th</i>â and <a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 238</font></a> Devayânî, which occurs Mahâbhârata I, 78. The verse quoted is the tenth of that Adhyâya, and agrees with ours, except that sutâham is read for athâham in the beginning of the second half verse.</p>
