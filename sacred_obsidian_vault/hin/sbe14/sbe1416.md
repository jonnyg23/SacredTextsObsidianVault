   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now, therefore, the Upâkarman (or the rite preparatory to the study) of the Veda (must be performed) on the full moon day of the month <i>S</i>râva<i>n</i>a or Praush<i>th</i>apada. <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Having kindled the sacred fire, he offers (therein) unground (rice) grains,</p>  
 <p>3. To the gods, to the <i>Ri</i>shis, and to the Kha<i>n</i><i>d</i>as.</p>  
 <p>4. Let them begin to study the Vedas, after having made Brâhma<i>n</i>as (invited for the purpose) wish 'welfare' (svasti), and after having fed them with sour milk,</p>  
 <p>5. (And continue the Veda-study) during four <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>months and a half or during five months and a half.</p>  
 <p>6. After (the expiration of) that (period), he may study (the Vedas) during the bright half of each month, <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_371"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. But the supplementary treatises (Aṅgas) of the Veda at pleasure (both during the bright and the dark halves of each month).</p>  
 <p>8. Interruptions of the (Veda-study shall take place),</p>  
 <p>9. If it thunders during the twilight, <a name="fr_372"></a><a href="#fn_372"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. During (both) the twilights (of each day), <a name="fr_373"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. In towns where a corpse (lies) or <i>K</i>â<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlas (stay). <a name="fr_374"></a><a href="#fn_374"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. At pleasure (he may study seated) in (a place) which has been smeared with cowdung and around which a line has been drawn. <a name="fr_375"></a><a href="#fn_375"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. (Let him not study) near a burial-ground, <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. (Nor) lying down, <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_377"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Nor when he has eaten or received a gift at a funeral sacrifice; <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. And with reference to this (subject) they quote a verse of Manu, 'Be it fruit, or water, or <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_379"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p>sesamum, or food, or whatever be the (gift) at a <i>S</i>râddha, let him not, having just accepted it, recite the Veda; for it is declared in the Sm<i>ri</i>ti, that the hand of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a is his mouth.'</p>  
 <p>17. (Let him not recite the Veda) while he runs, (nor) while a foul smell and the like (are perceptible, nor) on barren ground, <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_380"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. (Nor) when he has ascended a tree, <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_381"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. (Nor) in a boat or in a camp, <a name="fr_382"></a><a href="#fn_382"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Nor after meals while his hands are moist, <a name="fr_383"></a><a href="#fn_383"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. (Nor) while the sound of a Vâ<i>n</i>a (is heard), <a name="fr_384"></a><a href="#fn_384"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. (Nor) on the fourteenth day (of each half-month, nor) on the new moon day, (nor) on the eighth day (of each half-month, nor) on an Ash<i>t</i>akâ, <a name="fr_385"></a><a href="#fn_385"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. (Nor) while he stretches his feet out, (nor) while he makes a lap, (nor) while he leans against (something), nor (in any other unbecoming posture), <a name="fr_386"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. (Nor) close to his Gurus,</p>  
 <p>25. (Nor) during that night in which he has had conjugal intercourse,</p>  
 <p>26. (Nor) dressed in that garment which he had on during conjugal intercourse, except if it has been washed, <a name="fr_387"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. (Nor) at the extremity of a village, <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="#fn_388"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. (Nor) after (an attack of) vomiting, <a name="fr_389"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. (Nor) while voiding urine or fæces. <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. (Let him not recite) the Rig-veda, the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, and (the Atharva-veda) while the sound of the Sâman melodies (is audible), nor (the Sâman while the other Vedas are being recited). <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. (Let him not study) before (his food is) digested, <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. (Nor) when a thunderbolt falls, <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. (Nor) when an earthquake happens,</p>  
 <p>34. Nor when the sun and the moon are eclipsed.</p>  
 <p>35. When a preternaturally loud sound is heard in the sky, when a mountain falls, (and) when showers of stones, blood or sand (fall from the sky, the Veda must not be read) during the twenty-four hours (immediately succeeding the event). <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. If meteors and lightning appear together, (the interruption shall last) three (days and) nights.</p>  
 <p>37. A meteor (alone and) a flash: of lightning (alone cause an interruption lasting) as long as the sun shines (on that or the next day).</p>  
 <p>38. (If rain or other celestial phenomena come) out of season, (the Veda must not be read) during the twenty-four hours (immediately succeeding the event). <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. If the teacher has died, (he shall not study the Veda) during three (days and) nights. <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. If the teacher's son, a pupil, or a wife (have died, he shall not study) during a day and a night. <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">40</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. Let him honour an officiating priest, a father-in-law, paternal and maternal uncles, (though they may be) younger than himself, by rising and saluting them,</p>  
 <p>42. Likewise the wives of those persons whose feet must be embraced, and the teacher's (wives) <a name="fr_398"></a><a href="#fn_398"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. And his parents.</p>  
 <p>44. Let him say to one acquainted with (the meaning of) a salute, 'I N. N. ho! (salute thee);' <a name="fr_399"></a><a href="#fn_399"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. But him who does not know it (he shall address with the same formula, omitting his name). <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_400"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. When a salute is returned, the last vowel (of the noun standing) in the vocative is produced to the length of three moras, and if it is a diphthong (e or o) changeable according to the Sandhi rules, it becomes ây or âv, e.g. bho, bhâv. <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_401"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. A father who has committed a crime causing loss of caste must be cast off. But a mother does not become an outcast for her son. <a name="fr_402"></a><a href="#fn_402"><font size="1">47</font></a></p>  
 <p>48. Now they quote also (the following verses): <a name="fr_403"></a><a href="#fn_403"><font size="1">48</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> 'The teacher (â<i>k</i>ârya) is ten times more venerable than a sub-teacher (upâdhyâya), the father a hundred times more than the teacher, and the mother a thousand times more than the father.'</p>  
 <p>49. 'A wife, sons, and pupils who are defiled by sinful deeds, must first be reproved, and (if they do not amend, then) be cast off. He who forsakes them in any other way, becomes (himself) an outcast.' <a name="fr_404"></a><a href="#fn_404"><font size="1">49</font></a></p>  
 <p>50. An officiating priest and a teacher who neglect to teach the recitation of the Veda, or to sacrifice, shall be cast off. If he does not forsake them, he becomes an outcast. <a name="fr_405"></a><a href="#fn_405"><font size="1">50</font></a></p>  
 <p>51. They declare that the male offspring of outcasts are (also) outcasts, but not the females. <a name="fr_406"></a><a href="#fn_406"><font size="1">51</font></a></p>  
 <p>52. For a female enters (the family of) a stranger.</p>  
 <p>53. He may marry such a (female) without a dowry. <a name="fr_407"></a><a href="#fn_407"><font size="1">53</font></a></p>  
 <p>54. 'If the teacher's teacher is near, he must be treated like the teacher (himself). The Veda declares that one must behave towards the teacher's son just as towards the teacher.' <a name="fr_408"></a><a href="#fn_408"><font size="1">54</font></a></p>  
 <p>55. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall not accept (as gifts) weapons, poison, and spirituous liquor.</p>  
 <p>56. Learning, wealth, age, relationship, and occupation must be honoured. <a name="fr_409"></a><a href="#fn_409"><font size="1">56</font></a></p>  
 <p>57. (But) each earlier named (quality) is more venerable than (the succeeding ones).</p>  
 <p>58. If he meets aged men, infants, sick men, load-carriers, women, and persons riding in chariots, he <a name="fr_410"></a><a href="#fn_410"><font size="1">58</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>  
 <p>must make way (for them, i.e.) for each later (named before those enumerated earlier).</p>  
 <p>59. If a king and a Snâtaka meet, the king must make (way) for the Snâtaka.</p>  
 <p>60. All (must make way) for a bride who is being conveyed (to her husband's house).</p>  
 <p>61. Grass, room (for resting), fire, water, a welcome, and kind words never fail in the houses of good men. <a name="fr_411"></a><a href="#fn_411"><font size="1">61</font></a></p>
