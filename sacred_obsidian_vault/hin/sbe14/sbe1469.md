   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 8, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 15.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now indeed (that) happens (also which the following verses teach):</p>  
 <p>2. 'Let him sprinkle that food with the remainder of the burnt oblations. But what is given without (touching it with) the thumb does not gladden the manes.' <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. 'The malevolent Asuras seek an opportunity (to snatch away) that food intended for the manes, which is not supported with both hands.' <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1282"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. 'The Yâtudhânas and Pi<i>s</i>â<i>k</i>as, who receive no share, steal the food if sesamum grains are not scattered (on the seats of the guests), and the Asuras (take it) if (the host) is under the sway of anger.' <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1283"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. 'If a person dressed in reddish clothes mutters prayers, offers burnt oblations, or receives gifts, the sacrificial viands, offered at sacrifices to the gods or to the manes, do not reach the deities.' <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_270"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 270</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. 'If gifts are given or received without (touching them with) the thumb and, if one sips water standing, (the performer of the act) is not benefited thereby.'</p>  
 <p>7. At the beginning and at the end (of a <i>S</i>râddha) water must be giver. (to the guests). <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. In every case the muttering (of sacred texts) and the other (necessary acts must be performed) according to the rule.</p>  
 <p>9. The remaining (rules) have been prescribed (in the section) on the burnt oblations on Ash<i>t</i>akâ (days). <a name="fr_1286"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. 'He shall feed two (Brâhma<i>n</i>as) at the offering to the gods and three at the offering to the manes, or a single man on either occasion. Even a very wealthy man shall not be anxious (to entertain) a large company.' <a name="fr_1287"></a><a href="#fn_1287"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. 'A large company destroys these five (advantages), the respectful treatment (of the invited guests, the propriety of) time and place, purity and (the selection of) virtuous Brâhma<i>n</i>a (guests); therefore he shall not invite (a large number).'</p>  
 <p>12. 'In front (feed) the fathers of the (sacrificer), to the left the grandfathers, to the right the great-grandfathers, and at the back those who pare off (portions) from the cakes.' <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>

O. copy of the commentary urasta<i>h</i> pitaras tasya, and in the end with the Dekhan and Gu<i>g</i>arât MSS. pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>atakshakâ<i>h</i>. M. reads pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>atarkyâ, and the copies of the commentary pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>odakâ<i>h</i>. Both these <a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a> readings are clearly corrupt, and so is the var. lect. of the G<i>ri</i>hya-sa<i>m</i>graha, quoted in the Petersburg Dictionary, pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>atarkukâ<i>h</i>. Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>atakshakâ<i>h</i>, 'the cutters or parers of the cakes,' is appropriate, because the remoter ancestors, who, as Govinda too declares, are meant by the term, obtain the fragments of the funeral cakes.</p>
