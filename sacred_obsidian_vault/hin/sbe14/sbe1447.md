   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, ADHYÂYA 6, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 14.</h3>  
 <p>1. Earthen vessels that have been touched by impure persons (must be) exposed to (the flame of) a fire of Ku<i>s</i>a grass.</p>  
 <p>2. Those which have been defiled by stains of remnants (of food must be) exposed to another burning.</p>  
 <p>3. Those which have been defiled by urine, ordure, blood, semen, and the like (must be) thrown away.</p>  
 <p>4. (Vessels) made of metal (must be) washed, after having been scrubbed as (directed) above.</p>  
 <p>5. The materials (to be used) for scrubbing (are) cowdung, earth, ashes, and so forth.</p>  
 <p>6. Those which have been defiled by urine, ordure, blood, semen, and the like (must be) recast,</p>  
 <p>7. Or (they must) be kept during seven (days and) nights completely immersed in cow's urine,</p>  
 <p>8. Or in a great river for as long (a period). <a name="fr_949"></a><a href="#fn_949"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (Vessels) made of stone or of fruits, (i.e.) gourds, Bel-fruit, and Vinâ<i>l</i>as, (shall be) brushed with (a brush of) cow's hair. <a name="fr_950"></a><a href="#fn_950"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 191</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. (Sacrificial implements made of) plaited Na<i>l</i>a-reeds, bamboo, or <i>S</i>ara-reeds (shall be) washed with cowdung, water, and the like. <a name="fr_951"></a><a href="#fn_951"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. If unhusked rice has been defiled, (it must be) washed (and afterwards be) dried. <a name="fr_952"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. But a great quantity (of unhusked rice must be) sprinkled.</p>  
 <p>13. Husked rice (which has been defiled must be) thrown away. <a name="fr_953"></a><a href="#fn_953"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. The same (rule applies) to cooked sacrificial viands. <a name="fr_954"></a><a href="#fn_954"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. But if a great quantity has been defiled by (the touch of) dogs, crows, and the like (unclean beings), one must throw away that portion (as) food for men, and sprinkle (the rest with water), reciting the Anuvâka, 'Pavamâna<i>h</i> suvar<i>g</i>ana<i>h</i>.' <a name="fr_955"></a><a href="#fn_955"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Hydromel and preparations of milk (are) purified by pouring them from one vessel into another. <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 192</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. In like manner let him pour oil and clarified butter which have been touched by an impure (person) into water, and (afterwards) use them. <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. If (any) impure (substance) is thrown (into the sacrificial fire) let him place (the two Ara<i>n</i>is one) on (the other), produce fire by friction, (and offer) a Pavamânesh<i>t</i>i. <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. If (the rules regarding) purity, the proper place, the mantras, the series of actions, the object, the materials, (their) consecration, and the proper time are conflicting, each earlier-named (point) is more important (than the following ones). <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_949"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_949">190:8</a> <b>14</b>. A great river, i.e. one which directly flows into the ocean.--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_950"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_950">190:9</a> A Vinâ<i>l</i>a, i.e. (a vessel) made of bamboo or Vidagdha<i>n</i>a<i>l</i>a; it s called a 'long vessel' (dirghabhâ<i>g</i>anam), and is used for carrying he Pra<i>n</i>îta water and the like purposes.--Govinda. The vessel <a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 191</font></a> intended is no doubt the flask made of a bamboo which is cut below the joint, and is commonly used as a bottle for oil. Govinda adds that this mode of purification is to be adopted in case the vessels have been touched by impure persons.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_951"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_951">191:10</a> Na<i>l</i>a-reeds, i.e. Amphidonax Karka; <i>S</i>ara, i.e. Saccharum Sara. Govinda says that the rule applies to cases where such implements have been defiled by remnants of food (u<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ish<i>t</i>alepa).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_952"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_952">191:11</a> 'Defiled, i.e. touched by a <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla.' (The rule) refers to a quantity less than a Dro<i>n</i>a (66 or 132 lbs.).--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_953"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_953">191:13</a> 'If it has been defiled by urine and the like and the quantity is small;' this must be understood, because he will declare (below, Sûtra 15) that if there is a great quantity (the defiled) portion only shall be thrown away.--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_954"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_954">191:14</a> This, too, refers to small quantities only.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_955"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_955">191:15</a> The Anuvâka referred to is Taittirîya-brâhma<i>n</i>a I, 4, 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_956"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_956">191:16</a> 'Hydromel, i.e. sour milk, honey, clarified butter, water, and <a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 192</font></a> grain; a preparation of milk, i.e. curd of two-milk whey (âmikshâ), if these are blemished by the fault of men, and that (blemish must have been caused by) the touch of an impure (person, u<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ish<i>t</i>a) only.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_957"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_957">192:17</a> 'And that must be done in such a manner that the oil and the clarified butter are not lost.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_958"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_958">192:18</a> 'Any impure substance, i.e. urine, ordure, and the like.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_959"></a><a href="sbe1447.htm#fr_959">192:19</a> Âv<i>ri</i>t,' the series of actions,' i.e. the growth (prâ<i>m</i><i>s</i>ubhâva) of the ceremonial (prayoga).--Govinda.</p>
