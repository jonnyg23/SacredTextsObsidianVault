   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. A woman is not defiled by a lover, nor a Brâhma<i>n</i>a by Vedic rites, nor water by urine and ordure, nor fire by consuming (impure substances). <a name="fr_700"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_133"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 133</font></a></p>  
 <p>2-3. A wife, (though) tainted by sin, whether she be quarrelsome, or have left the house, or have suffered criminal force, or have fallen into the hands of thieves, must not be abandoned; to forsake her is not prescribed (by the sacred law). Let him wait for the time of her courses; by her temporary uncleanness she becomes pure. <a name="fr_701"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Women (possess) an unequalled means of purification; they never become (entirely) foul. For month by month their temporary uncleanness removes their sins. <a name="fr_702"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Women belong first to three gods, Soma (the moon), the Gandharva, and Fire, and come afterwards into the possession of men; according to the law they cannot be contaminated. <a name="fr_703"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Soma gave them cleanliness, the Gandharva their melodious voice, and Fire purity of all (limbs); therefore women are free from stains. <a name="fr_704"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Those versed in the sacred law state that there are three acts (only) which make women outcasts, (viz.) the murder of the husband, slaying a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a, and the destruction of the fruit of their womb. <a name="fr_705"></a><a href="#fn_705"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. A calf is pure when the milk flows, a bird when it causes fruit to fall, women during dalliance, and a dog when he catches a deer. <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_706"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Pure is the mouth of a goat and of a horse, pure is the back of a cow, pure are the feet of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, but women are pure in all (limbs) <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_707"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 134</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. I will now declare the purificatory texts (which are found) in each Veda; by muttering them or reciting them at a burnt-oblation (men) are doubtlessly cleansed (from sin). <a name="fr_708"></a><a href="#fn_708"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (They are) the Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a, the Devak<i>ri</i>ta, the <i>S</i>uddhavatîs, the Taratsamas, the <i>K</i>ûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>as, the Pâvamânîs, and the Durgâsâvitri;</p>  
 <p>12. The Atîshaṅgas, the Padastobhas, and the Sâmans (called) Vyâh<i>ri</i>ti, the Bhâru<i>n</i><i>d</i>a Sâmans, the Gâyatra (Sâman), and the Raivata; <a name="fr_709"></a><a href="#fn_709"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. The Purushavrata and the Bhâsa, and likewise the Devavrata (Sâmans), the Abliṅga, the Bârhaspatya, the hymn addressed to Vâ<i>k</i>, likewise the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as (called) Madhu; <a name="fr_710"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. The <i>S</i>atarudriya, the Atharva<i>s</i>iras, the Trisupar<i>n</i>a, the Mahâvrata, the Gosûkta, and the A<i>s</i>vasûkta, and the two Sâmans (called) Suddhâ<i>s</i>uddhîya. <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. The three (Sâmans called) Â<i>g</i>yadohas, the Rathantara, the Agnervrata, the Vâmadevya, and the B<i>ri</i>hat, being muttered, purify (all) living beings. (He who sings them) may obtain the recollection of former existences, if he desires it.</p>  
 <p>16. Gold is the firstborn of Fire, through Vish<i>n</i>u exists the earth, and the cows are children of the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Sun; he who bestows as gifts gold, a cow, and land will obtain rewards without end for them.</p>  
 <p>17. A cow, a horse, gold, (and) land, bestowed on an unlearned Brâhma<i>n</i>a who neglects his sacred duties, prevent the giver (from attaining heaven). <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18-19. (If he presents), on the full moon of the month of Vai<i>s</i>âkha, (to) seven or five Brâhma<i>n</i>as, black or white sesamum grains (mixed) with honey, (saying), 'May the king of justice (Yama) rejoice!' or (expressing) some other (wish) which he may have in his mind, the guilt which he has incurred during his (whole) life will instantly vanish.</p>  
 <p>20. But hear (now) the reward of the merit acquired by that man who gives the skin of a black antelope, to which the hoofs are (still) attached and the navel of which is adorned with gold, covering it with sesamum grains. <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. 'Without doubt he has bestowed (through that gift) the four-faced earth, together with its caves filled with gold, and together with its mountains, groves, and forests.' <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. He who, placing on the skin of a black antelope, sesamum, gold, honey, and butter, gives it to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, overcomes all sin.'</p>
