   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, ADHYÂYA 10, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 19.</h3>  
 <p>1. For slaying a Kshatriya (the offender) shall <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 202</font></a></p>  
 <p>give to the king one thousand cows and besides a bull in expiation of his sin,</p>  
 <p>2. For (slaying) a Vai<i>s</i>ya one hundred cows, for (slaying) a <i>S</i>ûdra ten; and a bull (must be) added (in each case). <a name="fr_1008"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. (The punishment for) the murder of a woman--excepting a (Brâhma<i>n</i>î) who had bathed after temporary uncleanness--and for the destruction of a cow have been explained by the (rule regarding the) murder of a <i>S</i>ûdra. <a name="fr_1009"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. If he has slain a milch-cow or a draught-ox, he shall perform a <i>K</i>ândrâya<i>n</i>a (lunar penance) after (paying the prescribed fine).</p>  
 <p>5. The (punishment for the) murder of a (Brâhma<i>n</i>î) who had bathed after temporary uncleanness has been explained by (the rule regarding) the murder of a Kshatriya. <a name="fr_1010"></a><a href="#fn_1010"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. For killing a flamingo, a Bhâsa, a peacock, a Brâhma<i>n</i>î duck, a Pra<i>k</i>alâka, a crow, an owl, a frog, a musk-rat, a dog, (the large ichneumon called) Babhru, a common ichneumon, and so forth, (the offender shall pay) the same (fine) as (for the murder of) a <i>S</i>ûdra. <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. In order to gain the good opinion of men, a witness shall give evidence in accordance with what he has seen or heard. <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 203</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Of injustice (in decisions) one quarter falls on the party in the cause, one quarter on his witnesses, one quarter on all the judges, and one quarter on the king. <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>But where he who deserves condemnation is condemned, the king is guiltless and the judges free from blame; the guilt falls on the offender (alone).</p>  
 <p>9. (Therefore) a wise man should ask an appointed witness in the following manner: <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1014"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. 'The merit which thou hast acquired in the interval between the night in which thou wast born and that in which thou wilt die, all that will go to the king, if thou speakest an untruth.' <a name="fr_1015"></a><a href="#fn_1015"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. 'A witness who speaks falsely, slays three fathers and three grandfathers and seven (descendants), both the born and the unborn.' <a name="fr_1016"></a><a href="#fn_1016"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. 'By false testimony concerning gold he kills three ancestors; by false testimony regarding (small) cattle he kills five; by false testimony concerning kine he kills ten.' <a name="fr_1017"></a><a href="#fn_1017"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>'He kills a hundred by false evidence regarding horses, (and) a thousand by false evidence concerning a man. A witness Who speaks falsely, destroys the whole (world) by false evidence concerning land.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 204</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. (Men of) the four castes (var<i>n</i>a) who have sons may be witnesses excepting <i>S</i>rotriyas, the king, ascetics, and those who are destitute of human (intellect). <a name="fr_1018"></a><a href="#fn_1018"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. If (the witness rightly) recollects (the facts of) the case (he will receive) commendation from the most eminent men. <a name="fr_1019"></a><a href="#fn_1019"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. In the contrary case (he will) fall into hell. <a name="fr_1020"></a><a href="#fn_1020"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Let him (who has given false evidence), drink hot milk during twelve (days and) nights or offer burnt oblations (reciting) the Kûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>a (texts). <a name="fr_1021"></a><a href="#fn_1021"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="fn_1007"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1007">201:1</a> <b>19</b>. Âpastamba I, 9, 24, I. Govinda explains vairaniryâtanârtham <a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 201</font></a> in two ways: 1. in expiation of his sin; 2. in order to remove the enmity of the relatives of the murdered man. He adds all these punishments are really penances (prâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>ittas) to be imposed by the king. Âpastamba has these Sûtras in the section on penances.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1014"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1014">203:9</a> I read, with the Telugu copy of the commentary, sâkshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> tvevam uddish<i>t</i>am. All the MSS. of the text and C. I. read sâkshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> daivam uddish<i>t</i>am. Govinda's explanation, adhunâ nirdish<i>t</i>ân sâkshi<i>n</i>a evam pri<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ed iti padânvaya<i>h</i>, 'the construction of the words is, "let him now ask the appointed witnesses in the following manner," agrees with the reading adopted.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1015"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1015">203:10</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XVI, 32-53.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1016"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1016">203:11</a> 'Three fathers and three grandfathers,' i.e. seven ancestors.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1017"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1017">203:12</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XVI, 34. Regarding the explanation of the <a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 204</font></a> words 'he kills,' see Manu VIII, 97, and Haradatta on Gautama XIII, 14.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1018"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1018">204:13</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XVI, 28-30. The text has râ<i>g</i>anya, 'members of the royal family.' But the parallel passages of other Dharma-sûtras, e<i>g</i>. Vish<i>n</i>u VIII, 2, make it probable that the king is meant.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1019"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1019">204:14</a> Âpastamba II, 11, 29, 10. Govinda takes the Sûtra differently. His commentary runs as follows: sâkshidvaye sati râ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>â, tatpurushai<i>s</i> <i>k</i>a ki<i>m</i> kartavyam ity ata âha || srim<i>ri</i>tau pradhânata<i>h</i> pratipatti<i>h</i> || prâdhânyatas taponirdish<i>t</i>avidyâdibhi<i>h</i> | tadva<i>k</i>anât pratipattir ni<i>s</i><i>k</i>aya<i>h</i> kârya ityadhyâhâra<i>h</i> kârya<i>h</i> || 'What shall the king and his officers do, if there are two witnesses? In order to answer this question he says: "On recollection, according to pre-eminence, reliance." According to pre-eminence, i.e. on account of austerities, (being) appointed (as a witness), learning and the like; in accordance with the evidence of such person's conviction. i.e. the decision must be made. The latter word has to be understood.' Govinda then goes on to quote Manu VIII, 73.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1020"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1020">204:15</a> Âpastamba II, It, 29, 9. Govinda and M. read kart<i>ri</i>patyam for kartapatyam, the reading of the Dekhan and Gu<i>g</i>arât MSS. The explanation of the former term is said to be dosha<i>h</i>, 'sin.' Regarding the ancient word kartapatya, which Govinda and the writer of M. have not understood, see Haradatta on Âpastamba I, 2, 5, 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1021"></a><a href="sbe1452.htm#fr_1021">204:16</a> In accordance with his explanation of Sûtra 14, Govinda thinks that this penance is to be performed by the king and the judges in case they fail to weigh the evidence properly. But <a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a> according to Manu VIII, 106, Vish<i>n</i>u VIII, 16, the oblations with the Kûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>as (Taitt. Âr. X, 3-5) are to be offered for uttering in evidence a venial falsehood. That is, no doubt, here, too, the real meaning.</p>
