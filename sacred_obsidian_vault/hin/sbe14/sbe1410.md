   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VII.</h3>  
 <p>1. There are four orders, <a name="fr_223"></a><a href="#fn_223"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Viz. (that of) the student, (that of) the house-holder, (that of) the hermit, and (that of) the ascetic.</p>  
 <p>3. A man who has studied one, two, or three Vedas without violating the rules of studentship, may enter any of these (orders), whichsoever he pleases. <a name="fr_224"></a><a href="#fn_224"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. A (professed) student shall serve his teacher until death; <a name="fr_225"></a><a href="#fn_225"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. And in case the teacher dies, he shall serve the sacred fire. <a name="fr_226"></a><a href="#fn_226"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 41</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. For it has been declared in the Veda, 'The fire is thy teacher:' <a name="fr_227"></a><a href="#fn_227"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. (A student, whether professed or temporary), shall bridle his tongue; <a name="fr_228"></a><a href="#fn_228"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. He shall eat in the fourth, sixth, or eighth hour of the day. <a name="fr_229"></a><a href="#fn_229"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. He shall go out in order to beg. <a name="fr_230"></a><a href="#fn_230"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. He shall obey his teacher. <a name="fr_231"></a><a href="#fn_231"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. He either (may wear all his hair) tied in a knot or (keep merely) a lock on the crown of his head tied in a knot, (shaving the other parts of the head.) <a name="fr_232"></a><a href="#fn_232"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. If the teacher walks, he shall attend him walking after him; if the teacher is seated, standing; if the teacher lies down, seated. <a name="fr_233"></a><a href="#fn_233"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. He shall study after having been called (by the teacher, and not request the latter to begin the lesson). <a name="fr_234"></a><a href="#fn_234"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Let him announce (to the teacher) all that he has received (when begging), and eat after permission (has been given to him). <a name="fr_235"></a><a href="#fn_235"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 42</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Let him avoid to sleep on a cot, to clean his teeth, to wash (his body for pleasure), to apply collyrium (to his eyes),. to anoint (his body), and to wear shoes or a parasol. <a name="fr_236"></a><a href="#fn_236"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. (While reciting his prayers) he shall stand in the day-time and sit down at night. <a name="fr_237"></a><a href="#fn_237"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. Let him bathe three times a day. <a name="fr_238"></a><a href="#fn_238"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>
