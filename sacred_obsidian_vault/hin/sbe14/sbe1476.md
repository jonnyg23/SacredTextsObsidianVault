   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA III, ADHYÂYA 4.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now if a student commits any act against his vow, eats meat, or approaches a woman, whenever any evil befals him, <a name="fr_1354"></a><a href="#fn_1354"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. He heaps fuel on the fire in the interior of the house, scatters (Ku<i>s</i>a grass) around it, and performs the ceremonies up to the end of the Agnimukha; then he offers oblations of clarified butter, (reciting the following texts): 'It was done by lust, lust does</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_295"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 295</font></a></p>  
 <p>it, to lust (belongs) all this, to him who draws me on, Svâhâ;' 'It was done by the internal organ, the internal organ does it, to the internal organ (belongs) all this, to him who draws me on, Svâhâ;' 'It was done by passion, passion does it, to passion (belongs) all this, to him who draws me on, Svâhâ;' 'It was done by ignorance, ignorance does it, to ignorance (belongs) all this, to him who draws me on, Svâhâ;' 'It was done by sin, sin does it, to sin (belongs) all this, to him who draws me on, Svâhâ;' 'It was done by wrath, wrath does it, to wrath (belongs) all this, to him who draws me on, Svâhâ.'</p>  
 <p>3. That which begins with the muttering (of the Vedic texts) and ends with the gift of a fee (consisting of) a cow is known,</p>  
 <p>4. (Afterwards) he stays (during the night) behind (i. e. to the west of) the fire, wrapping himself in the skin of a black antelope, the neck of which is turned towards the east and the hair of which is turned outside.</p>  
 <p>5. When the day dawns, he drags himself away from the hinder part (of the skin), goes to a bathing-place, bathes (there) in the manner which is known, (but) performs, while in the water, sixteen suppressions of breath with the Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a hymn; next he performs the known (ceremonies) up to the worship of the sun, and afterwards goes to the house of his teacher. <a name="fr_1355"></a><a href="#fn_1355"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Let him know for certain that that is equally (effective) as bathing (with the priests) at the end of a horse-sacrifice.</p>
