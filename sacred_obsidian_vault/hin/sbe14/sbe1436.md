   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, ADHYÂYA 2, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 3.</h3>  
 <p>1. The (term of the) studentship for (learning the) Veda, as kept by the ancients, (is) forty-eight years, <a name="fr_755"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (Or) twenty-four (years), or twelve for each Veda, <a name="fr_756"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Or at the least one year for each Kâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>a, <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Or until (the Veda has been) learned; for life is uncertain. <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. A passage of the revealed texts declares, 'Let him kindle the sacred fires while his hair is (still) black.' <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 150</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. They do not put any (religious) restrictions on the acts of a (child) before the investiture with the girdle (is performed). For he is on a level with a <i>S</i>ûdra before (his second) birth through the Veda. <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. The number of years (must be calculated) from the conception. Let him initiate a Brâhma<i>n</i>a in the eighth (year) after that, <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. A Kshatriya three (years) later (than a Brâhma<i>n</i>a),</p>  
 <p>9. A Vai<i>s</i>ya one year later than a (Kshatriya).</p>  
 <p>10. Spring, summer, and autumn are the seasons (for the initiation) according to the order of the castes. <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_762"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (Let him perform the initiation reciting), according to the order (of the castes), a Gâyatrî, a Trish<i>t</i>ubh, (or) a <i>G</i>agatî (verse).</p>  
 <p>12. Up to the sixteenth, the twenty-second, and the twenty-fourth (years) respectively (the time for the initiation) has not passed. <a name="fr_763"></a><a href="#fn_763"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. The girdles (shall consist of a rope) made of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass, a bow-string, (or a rope) made of hemp. <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_764"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. The skins (shall be) those of a black antelope, of a spotted deer, (or) of a he-goat. <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_765"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. The staff shall reach the crown of the head, the forehead, (or) the tip of the nose, (and be made) of a tree fit for a sacrifice. The details have been stated above.  <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_766"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 151</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Let him beg, (employing a formula) consisting of seven syllables, with the word bhavat in the beginning, with the word bhikshâ in the middle, and with the (verb expressing) the request at the end; and let him not pronounce loudly (the syllables) kshâ and hi. <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_767"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a (student) shall ask for alms, placing (the word) 'Lady' first, a Kshatriya placing <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_768"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 152</font></a></p>  
 <p>it in the middle, (and) a Vai<i>s</i>ya placing it at the end (of the formula), from (men of) all castes.</p>  
 <p>18. The (persons fit to be asked) are Brâhma<i>n</i>as and so forth, who follow (their lawful) occupations.</p>  
 <p>19. Let him daily fetch fuel out of the forest and offer (it in the sacred fire). <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_769"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. (A student shall be) truthful, modest, and devoid of pride. <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_770"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. He shall rise before (his teacher in the morning) and go to rest after (him in the evening). <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_771"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. He shall never disobey the words of his teacher except (when he is ordered to commit) a crime causing loss of caste. <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_772"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Let him converse with women so much (only) as his purpose requires. <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. Let him avoid dancing, singing, playing musical instruments, the use of perfumes, garlands, shoes, (or) a parasol, applying collyrium (to his eyes), and anointing (his body). <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_774"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. Let him take hold (of his teacher's) right (foot) with the right (hand), and of the left (foot) with the left hand. <a name="fr_775"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. If he desires long life and (bliss in) heaven, <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 153</font></a></p>  
 <p>(he may act) at his pleasure (in the same manner) towards other holy (men), after having received permission from his teacher.</p>  
 <p>27. (Let him say), 'I N. N., ho! (salute thee),' touching his ears, in order to compose the internal organ. <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. (Let him embrace his teacher's leg) below the knee down to the feet. <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_778"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. (A student shall not embrace his teacher) when he (himself) is seated, or lying down, or impure, nor when (his teacher) is seated, lying down, or impure. <a name="fr_779"></a><a href="#fn_779"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. If he can (find water to sip), he shall not remain impure even during a muhûrta. <a name="fr_780"></a><a href="#fn_780"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. If he carries a load of fuel or holds a pot, flowers, or food in his hands, he shall not salute; nor (shall he do it) on similar occasions. <a name="fr_781"></a><a href="#fn_781"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. Let him not salute (the teacher) standing too close,</p>  
 <p>33. Nor, if he has reached the age of puberty, the young wives of brothers and the young wives of the teacher. <a name="fr_782"></a><a href="#fn_782"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_154"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 154</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. To sit together with (these persons) in a boat, on a rock, on a plank, on an elephant, on the roof of a house, on a mat, or in wheeled vehicles is permissible. <a name="fr_783"></a><a href="#fn_783"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. (The pupil) must assist his teacher in making his toilet, shampoo him, attend him while bathing, eat his leavings, and so forth. <a name="fr_784"></a><a href="#fn_784"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. (But he) should avoid the remnants of food left by his (teacher's) son, though he may know the Veda together with the Aṅgas, <a name="fr_785"></a><a href="#fn_785"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. And to assist at the toilet of, to shampoo, to attend in the bath, and to eat the remnants of food left by a young wife of his (teacher).</p>  
 <p>38. Let him run after (his teacher) when he runs, walk after him when he walks, attend him standing when he stands. <a name="fr_786"></a><a href="#fn_786"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. Let him not sport in the water while bathing. <a name="fr_787"></a><a href="#fn_787"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. Let him swim (motionless) like a stick.</p>  
 <p>41. To study under a non-Brâhmanical teacher (is permitted) in times of distress. <a name="fr_788"></a><a href="#fn_788"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. (The pupil shall) obey and walk after him as long as the instruction (lasts). <a name="fr_789"></a><a href="#fn_789"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. (According to some this is improper, because) just that (mutual relation) sanctifies both of them. <a name="fr_790"></a><a href="#fn_790"><font size="1">43</font></a></p>  
 <p>44. And (the behaviour) towards brothers, sons, and (other) pupils (of the teacher shall be regulated) in the same manner. <a name="fr_791"></a><a href="#fn_791"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. But officiating priests, a father-in-law, paternal and maternal uncles who are younger than (oneself must be honoured by) rising and (by being) addressed. <a name="fr_792"></a><a href="#fn_792"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. Kâtya (declares that) the salutation shall be returned. <a name="fr_793"></a><a href="#fn_793"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. For (the propriety of that rule) is apparent (from the story) about <i>S</i>i<i>s</i>u Âṅgirasa. <a name="fr_794"></a><a href="#fn_794"><font size="1">47</font></a></p>

It is curious that Govinda says nothing about the form saptâksharâm and the feminine terminations of the other adjectives, which do not agree with mantram, a masculine.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_794"></a><a href="sbe1436.htm#fr_794">155:47</a> The story of <i>S</i>i<i>s</i>u Âṅgirasa is told, Manu II, 151-153.</p>
