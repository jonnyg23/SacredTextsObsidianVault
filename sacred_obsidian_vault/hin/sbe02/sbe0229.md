 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 9, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 26.</h3>  
 <p>1. (The same penance must be performed), if a milch-cow or a full-grown ox (has been slain), without a reason. <a name="fr_477"></a><a href="#fn_476"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. And for other animals (which have no bones), if an ox-load of them has been killed. <a name="fr_478"></a><a href="#fn_477"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. He who abuses a person who (on account of his venerability) ought not to be abused, or speaks an untruth (regarding any small matter) must abstain for three days from milk, pungent condiments, and salt. <a name="fr_479"></a><a href="#fn_478"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. (If the same sins have been committed) by a <i>S</i>ûdra, he must fast for seven days.</p>  
 <p>5. And the same (penances must also be performed) by women, (but not those which follow). <a name="fr_480"></a><a href="#fn_479"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. He who cuts off a limb of a person for whose murder he would become an Abhi<i>s</i>asta (must perform the penance prescribed for killing a <i>S</i>ûdra), if the life (of the person injured) has not been endangered.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_85"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 85</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. He who has been guilty of conduct unworthy of an Aryan, of calumniating others, of actions contrary to the rule of conduct, of eating or drinking things forbidden, of connection with a woman of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste, of an unnatural crime, of performing; magic rites with intent (to harm his enemies) or (of hurting others) unintentionally, shall bathe and sprinkle himself with water, reciting the (seven) verses addressed to the Waters, or the verses addressed to Varu<i>n</i>a, or (other verses chosen from the Anuvâka, called) Pavitra, in proportion to the frequency with which the crime has been committed. <a name="fr_481"></a><a href="#fn_480"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. A (student) who has broken the vow of chastity, shall offer to Nir<i>ri</i>ti an ass, according to the manner of the Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a-rites. <a name="fr_482"></a><a href="#fn_481"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. A <i>S</i>ûdra shall eat (the remainder) of that (offering).</p>  
 <p>10. (Now follows) the penance for him who transgresses the rules of studentship.</p>  
 <p>11. He shall for a year serve his teacher silently, emitting speech only during the daily study (of the Veda, in announcing necessary business to) his teacher or his teacher's wife, and whilst collecting alms.</p>  
 <p>12. The following penances) which we are going to proclaim, may be performed for the same sin, and <a name="fr_483"></a><a href="#fn_482"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_86"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 86</font></a></p>  
 <p>also for other sinful acts, which do not cause loss of caste.</p>  
 <p>13. He may either offer oblations to Kâma and Manyu (with the following two Mantras), 'Kâma (passion) has done it; Manyu (anger) has done it.' Or he may mutter (these Mantras). <a name="fr_484"></a><a href="#fn_483"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Or, after having eaten sesamum or fasted on the days of the full and new moon he may, on the following day bathe, and stopping his breath, repeat the Gâyatrî one thousand times, or he may do so without stopping his breath.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_476"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_477">84:1</a> <b>26</b>. 'A reason' for hurting a cow is, according to Haradatta, anger, or the desire to obtain meat.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_477"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_478">84:2</a> Manu XI, 141; Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>. III, 269. That 'animals without bones,' i.e. insects or mollusks, are intended in the Sûtra is an inference, drawn by Haradatta from the parallel passages of Gautama, Manu, and Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_478"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_479">84:3</a> 'A person who ought not to be abused, i. e. a father, a teacher, and the like.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_479"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_480">84:5</a> The same penances, i. e. those prescribed I, 9, 24-I, 9, 26, 4. According to Haradatta this Sûtra is intended to teach that women shall not perform the penances which follow. Others, however, are of opinion that it is given in order to indicate that the preceding Sûtras apply to women by an atide<i>s</i>a, and that, according to a Smârta principle, applicable to such cases, it may be inferred, that women are to perform one-half only of the penances prescribed for men.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_480"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_481">85:7</a> The Anuvâka intended is Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. II, 5, 12.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_481"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_482">85:8</a> Taitt. Âr. II, 18, and Weber, Ind. Stud. X, 102; Manu XI, 199 seq.; and Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>. III, 280. Regarding the Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a-rites, see Â<i>s</i>v. G<i>ri</i>. Sû. I, 1, 2, and Max Müller's History of Ancient Sanskrit Literature, p. 203.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_482"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_483">85:12</a> Regarding the Patanîya-crimes which cause loss of caste, see above, I, 7, 21, 7 seq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_483"></a><a href="sbe0229.htm#fr_484">86:13</a> Weber, Ind. Stud. X, 102. According to the greatness of the crime the number of the burnt-oblations must be increased and the prayers be repeated.</p>
