   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a may eat the food given by twice-born men, who are praised for (the faithful performance of their) duties, <a name="fr_1295"></a><a href="#fn_1294"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. And he may accept (other gifts from them).</p>  
 <p>3. Fire-wood, water, grass, roots, fruits, honey, (a promise of) safety, food brought unsolicited, a couch, a seat, shelter, a carriage, milk, sour milk, (roasted) grain, small fish, millet, a garland, venison, and vegetables, (spontaneously offered by a man) of any (caste) must not be refused, <a name="fr_1296"></a><a href="#fn_1295"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Nor anything else that may be required for providing for (the worship of the) Manes and gods, for Gurus and dependents. <a name="fr_1297"></a><a href="#fn_1296"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. If the means for sustaining life cannot (be procured) otherwise, (they may be accepted) from a <i>S</i>ûdra. <a name="fr_1298"></a><a href="#fn_1297"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. A herdsman, a husbandman, an acquaintance <a name="fr_1299"></a><a href="#fn_1298"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_266"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 266</font></a></p>  
 <p>of the family, a barber, and a servant are persons whose food may be eaten,</p>  
 <p>7. And a trader, who is not (at the same time) an artisan. <a name="fr_1300"></a><a href="#fn_1299"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. (A householder) shall not eat every day (the food of strangers). <a name="fr_1301"></a><a href="#fn_1300"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Food into which a hair or an insect has fallen (must not be eaten), <a name="fr_1302"></a><a href="#fn_1301"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. (Nor) what has been touched by a woman during her courses, by a black bird, or with the foot, <a name="fr_1303"></a><a href="#fn_1302"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (Nor) what has been looked at by the murderer of a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a, <a name="fr_1304"></a><a href="#fn_1303"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. (Nor) what has been smelt at by a cow, <a name="fr_1305"></a><a href="#fn_1304"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. (Nor) what is naturally bad, <a name="fr_1306"></a><a href="#fn_1305"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Nor (food) that (has turned) sour by itself, excepting sour milk, <a name="fr_1307"></a><a href="#fn_1306"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. (Nor) what has been cooked twice, <a name="fr_1308"></a><a href="#fn_1307"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. (Nor) what (has become) stale (by being <a name="fr_1309"></a><a href="#fn_1308"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_267"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 267</font></a></p>  
 <p>kept), except vegetables, food that requires mastication, fatty and oily substances, meat and honey.</p>  
 <p>17. (Food given) by a person who has been cast off (by his parents), by a woman of bad character, an Abhi<i>s</i>asta, a hermaphrodite, a police-officer, a carpenter, a miser, a jailer, a surgeon, one who hunts without using the bow, a man who eats the leavings (of others), by a multitude (of men), and by an enemy (must not be eaten), <a name="fr_1310"></a><a href="#fn_1309"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Nor what is given by such men who defile the company at a funeral dinner, as have been enumerated before bald men; <a name="fr_1311"></a><a href="#fn_1310"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. (A dinner) which is prepared for no (holy) purpose or where (the guests) sip water or rise against the rule, <a name="fr_1312"></a><a href="#fn_1311"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Or where (one's) equals are honoured in a different manner, and persons who are not (one's) <a name="fr_1313"></a><a href="#fn_1312"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_268"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 268</font></a></p>  
 <p>equals are honoured in the same manner (as oneself, must not be eaten),</p>  
 <p>21. Nor (food that is given) in a disrespectful manner. <a name="fr_1314"></a><a href="#fn_1313"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. And the milk which a cow gives during the first ten days after calving (must not be drunk), <a name="fr_1315"></a><a href="#fn_1314"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Nor (that) of goats and buffalo-cows (under the same conditions).</p>  
 <p>24. (The milk) of sheep, camels, and of one-hoofed animals must not be drunk under any circumstances, <a name="fr_1316"></a><a href="#fn_1315"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. Nor (that) of animals from whose udders the milk flows spontaneously, of those that bring forth twins, and of those giving milk while big with young, <a name="fr_1317"></a><a href="#fn_1316"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. Nor the milk of a cow whose calf is dead or separated from her. <a name="fr_1318"></a><a href="#fn_1317"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. And five-toed animals (must) not (be eaten) excepting the hedgehog, the hare, the porcupine, the iguana, the rhinoceros, and the tortoise, <a name="fr_1319"></a><a href="#fn_1318"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. Nor animals which have a double row of teeth, those which are covered with an excessive quantity of hair, those which have no hair, one-hoofed animals, sparrows, the (heron called) Plava, <a href="errata.htm#1">Brâhma<i>n</i>î</a> ducks, and swans, <a name="fr_1320"></a><a href="#fn_1319"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_269"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 269</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. (Nor) crows, herons, vultures, and falcons, (birds) born in the water, (birds) with red feet and beaks, tame cocks and pigs, <a name="fr_1321"></a><a href="#fn_1320"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. (Nor) milch-cows and draught-oxen, <a name="fr_1322"></a><a href="#fn_1321"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. Nor the flesh of animals whose milk-teeth have not fallen out, which are diseased, nor the meat of those (which have been killed) for no (sacred) purpose, <a name="fr_1323"></a><a href="#fn_1322"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. Nor young sprouts, mushrooms, garlic, and substances exuding (from trees), <a name="fr_1324"></a><a href="#fn_1323"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. Nor red (juices) which issue from incisions.</p>  
 <p>34. Woodpeckers, egrets, ibis, parrots, cormorants, peewits, and flying foxes, (as well as birds) flying at night, (ought not to be eaten). <a name="fr_1325"></a><a href="#fn_1324"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. Birds that feed striking with their beaks, or scratching with their feet, and are not web-footed may be eaten, <a name="fr_1326"></a><a href="#fn_1325"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. And fishes that are not misshapen, <a name="fr_1327"></a><a href="#fn_1326"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p><a name="page_270"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 270</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. And (animals) that must be slain for (the fulfilment of) the sacred law. <a name="fr_1328"></a><a href="#fn_1327"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. Let him eat (the flesh of animals) killed by beasts of prey, after having washed it, if no blemish is visible, and if it is declared to be fit for use by the word (of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a). <a name="fr_1329"></a><a href="#fn_1328"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>
