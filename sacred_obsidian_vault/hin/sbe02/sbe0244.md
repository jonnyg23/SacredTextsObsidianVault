 <p><a name="page_122"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 122</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, PA<i>T</i>ALA 4, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 9.</h3>  
 <p>1. And on the following day (he shall search for him), feast him to his heart's content, and accompany him (on his departure). <a name="fr_611"></a><a href="#fn_610"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (If the guest) possesses a carriage, (he shall accompany him) as far as that.</p>  
 <p>3. Any other (guest he must accompany), until permission to return is given.</p>  
 <p>4. If (the guest) forgets (to give leave to depart), the (host) may return on reaching the boundary of his village.</p>  
 <p>5. To all (those who come for food) at (the end of) the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva he shall give a portion, even to dogs and <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlas.</p>  
 <p>6. Some declare that he shall not give anything to unworthy people (such as <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlas).</p>  
 <p>7. A person who has been initiated shall not eat the leavings of women or of an uninitiated person. <a name="fr_612"></a><a href="#fn_611"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. All gifts are to be preceded by (pouring out) water. <a name="fr_613"></a><a href="#fn_612"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (But gifts offered to priests) at sacrifices (are to be given) in the manner prescribed by the Veda.</p>  
 <p>10. The division of the food must be made in such a manner that those who receive daily portions (slaves) do not suffer by it.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_123"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 123</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. At his pleasure, he may stint himself, his wife, or his children, but by no means a slave who does his work.</p>  
 <p>12. And he must not stint himself so much that he becomes unable to perform his duties.</p>  
 <p>13. Now they quote also (the following two verses):</p>  
 <p>'Eight mouthfuls are the meal of an ascetic, sixteen that of a hermit living in the woods, thirty-two that of a householder, and an unlimited quantity that of a student. An Agnihotrin, a draught-ox, and a student, those three can do their work only if they eat; without eating (much), they cannot do it.' <a name="fr_614"></a><a href="#fn_613"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>
