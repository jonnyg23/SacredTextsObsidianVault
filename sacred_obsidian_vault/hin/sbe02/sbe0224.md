   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 7, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 21.</h3>  
 <p>1. With Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a-grass, Balba<i>g</i>a-grass (and articles made of them), roots, and fruits,</p>  
 <p>2. And with (other kinds of) grass and wood which have not been worked up (into objects of use). <a name="fr_437"></a><a href="#fn_436"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. He shall not be too eager (after such a livelihood).</p>  
 <p>4. If he obtains (another lawful) livelihood, he shall leave off (trading). <a name="fr_438"></a><a href="#fn_437"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 74</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Intercourse with fallen men is not ordained, <a name="fr_439"></a><a href="#fn_438"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Nor with Apapâtras. <a name="fr_440"></a><a href="#fn_439"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Now (follows the enumeration of) the actions which cause loss of caste (Patanîya).</p>  
 <p>8. (These are) stealing (gold), crimes whereby one becomes an Abhi<i>s</i>asta, homicide, neglect of the Vedas, causing abortion, incestuous connection with relations born from the same womb as one's mother or father, and with the offspring of such persons, drinking spirituous liquor, and intercourse with persons the intercourse with whom is forbidden. <a name="fr_441"></a><a href="#fn_440"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. That man falls who has connection with a female friend of a female Guru, or with a female friend of a male Guru, or with any married woman. <a name="fr_442"></a><a href="#fn_441"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Some (teachers declare), that he does not fall by having connection with any other married female except his teacher's wife. <a name="fr_443"></a><a href="#fn_442"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Constant commission of (other) sins (besides those enumerated above) also causes a man to lose his caste.</p>  
 <p>12. Now follows (the enumeration of) the acts which make men impure (Asu<i>k</i>ikara).</p>  
 <p>13. (These are) the cohabitation of Aryan women with <i>S</i>ûdras,</p>  
 <p>14. Eating the flesh of forbidden (creatures),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. As of a dog, a man, village cocks or pigs, carnivorous animals,</p>  
 <p>16. Eating the excrements of men,</p>  
 <p>17. Eating what is left by a <i>S</i>ûdra, the cohabitation of Aryans with Apapâtra women.</p>  
 <p>18. Some declare, that these acts also cause a man to lose his caste.</p>  
 <p>19. Other acts besides those (enumerated) are causes of impurity.</p>  
 <p>20. He who learns (that a man has) committed a sin, shall not be the first to make it known to others; but he shall avoid the (sinner), when performing religious ceremonies. <a name="fr_444"></a><a href="#fn_443"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>
