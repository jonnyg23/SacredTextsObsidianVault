   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IV.</h3>  
 <p>1. A householder shall take a wife (of) equal (caste), who has not belonged to another man and is younger (than himself). <a name="fr_911"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. A marriage (may be contracted) between persons who have not the same Pravaras, <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_911"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. (And) who are not related within six degrees on the father's side, <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_912"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Or on the side of the begetter, <a name="fr_914"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. (Nor) within four degrees on the mothers side. <a name="fr_915"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. (If the father) gives (his daughter) dressed (in two garments) and decked with ornaments to a person possessing (sacred) learning, of virtuous conduct, who has relatives and a (good) disposition, (that is a) Brâhma (wedding). <a name="fr_916"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. At the Prâ<i>g</i>âpatya (wedding) the marriage formula is, 'Fulfil ye the law conjointly.' <a name="fr_917"></a><a href="#fn_916"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. At the Ârsha (wedding the bridegroom) shall present a cow and a bull to him who has (authority over) the maiden. <a name="fr_918"></a><a href="#fn_917"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (If the bride) is given, decked with ornaments. to a priest at the altar, that is a Daiva wedding. <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_918"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. The spontaneous union with a willing (maiden is called) a Gândharva wedding. <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_919"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. If those who have (authority over) a female are propitiated by money, (that is) an Âsura wedding. <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_920"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. (If the bride) is taken by force, (that is) a Râkshasa wedding. <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_921"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. If (a man) embraces a female deprived of consciousness, (that is) a Pai<i>s</i>â<i>k</i>a wedding. <a name="fr_923"></a><a href="#fn_922"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. The first four (rites) are lawful; <a name="fr_924"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Some say, (the first) six. <a name="fr_925"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. (Children) born in the regular order of wives of the next, second or third lower castes (become) Savar<i>n</i>as, Ambash<i>th</i>as, Ugras, Nishâdas, Daushyantas or Pâra<i>s</i>avas. <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_925"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. (Children born) in the inverted order (of wives of higher castes become) Sûtas, Mâgadhas, Âyogavas, Kshatt<i>ri</i>s, Vaidehakas or <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlas. <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_926"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Some declare, that a woman of the Brâhma<i>n</i>a caste has born successively to (husbands of) the (four) castes, sons (who are) Brâhma<i>n</i>as, Sûtas, Mâgadhas or <i>K</i>a<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlas; <a name="fr_928"></a><a href="#fn_927"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. (And that) a woman of the Kshatriya caste (has born) to the same, Mûrdhâvasiktas, Kshatriyas, Dhîvaras, Pulkasas;</p>  
 <p>20. Further, a woman of the Vai<i>s</i>ya caste to the same, Bh<i>ri</i><i>g</i>yaka<i>n</i><i>th</i>as, Mâhishyas, Vai<i>s</i>yas, and Vaidehas;</p>  
 <p>21. (And) a woman of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste to the same, Pâra<i>s</i>avas, Yavanas, <i>K</i>ara<i>n</i>as, and <i>S</i>ûdras.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. In the seventh (generation men obtain) a change of caste, either being raised to a higher one or being degraded to a lower one. <a name="fr_929"></a><a href="#fn_928"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. The venerable teacher declares (that this happens) in the fifth (generation). <a name="fr_930"></a><a href="#fn_929"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. And (the same rule applies) to those born (from parents of different classes that are) intermediate between (two of the castes originally) created (by Brahman). <a name="fr_931"></a><a href="#fn_930"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. But those born in the inverse order (from fathers of a lower and mothers of a higher caste stand) outside (the pale of) the sacred law, <a name="fr_932"></a><a href="#fn_931"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. As well as (those born in the regular order) from a female of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste. <a name="fr_933"></a><a href="#fn_932"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. But he whom a <i>S</i>ûdra (begets) on a female of unequal caste shall be treated like an outcast. <a name="fr_934"></a><a href="#fn_933"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. The last (named, the Ka<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla), is the foulest. <a name="fr_935"></a><a href="#fn_934"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. Virtuous sons (born of wives of equal caste) and wedded according to approved rites sanctify (their father's family).</p>  
 <p>30. (A son born of a wife married) according to the Ârsha rite (saves) three ancestors (from hell), <a name="fr_936"></a><a href="#fn_935"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. (A son born of a wife married) according to the Daiva rite ten, <a name="fr_937"></a><a href="#fn_936"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. (A son born of a wife married) according to the Prâ<i>g</i>âpatya rite, also ten. <a name="fr_938"></a><a href="#fn_937"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. (But) the son of a wife married according to the Brâhma rite (saves) ten ancestors, ten descendants, and himself. <a name="fr_939"></a><a href="#fn_938"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>
