 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 51</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 4, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 14.</h3>  
 <p>1. (That is to say) the Agnihotra, hospitality, <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. And what else of this kind (is ordained).</p>  
 <p>3. He whom (a student) asks for instruction, shall certainly not refuse it; <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Provided he does not see in him a fault, (which disqualifies him from being taught).</p>  
 <p>5. If by chance (through the pupil's stupidity the teaching) is not completed, obedience towards the (teacher is the pupil's only refuge). <a name="fr_324"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Towards a mother (grandmother and great-grandmother) and a father (grandfather and great-grandfather) the same obedience must be shown as towards a teacher. <a name="fr_325"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. The feet of all Gurus must be embraced (every day) by a student who has returned home; <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. And also on meeting them, after returning from a journey. <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. The feet of (elder) brothers and sisters must be embraced, according to the order of their seniority. <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. And respect (must) always (be shown to one's elders and betters), according to the injunction <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> (given above and according to the order of their seniority).</p>  
 <p>11. He shall salute an officiating priest, a father-in-law, a father's brother, and a mother's. brother, (though they may be) younger than he himself, and (when saluting) rise to meet them. <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_329"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Or he may silently embrace their feet. <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. A friendship kept for ten years with fellow citizens (is a reason for giving a salutation, and so is) a friendship, contracted at school, which has lasted for five years. But a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a (known) for less than three years, must be saluted. <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. If the age (of several persons whom one meets) is exactly known, one must salute the eldest (first).</p>  
 <p>15. He need not salute a person, who is not a Guru, and who stands in a lower or higher place than he himself.</p>  
 <p>16. Or he may descend or ascend (to the place where such a person stands) and salute him. <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. But every one (Gurus and others) he shall salute, after having risen (from his seat). <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. If he is impure, he shall not salute (anybody); <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. (Nor shall he salute) a person who is impure.</p>

 <p>20. Nor shall he, being impure, return a salutation.</p>  
 <p>21. Married women (must be saluted) according to the (respective) ages of their husbands.</p>  
 <p>22. He shall not salute with his shoes on, or his head wrapped up, or his hands full.</p>  
 <p>23. In saluting women, a Kshatriya or a Vai<i>s</i>ya he shall use a pronoun, not his name. <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. Some (declare, that he shall salute in this manner even) his mother and the wife of his teacher. <a name="fr_337"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. Know that a Brâhma<i>n</i>a of ten years and a Kshatriya of a hundred years stand to each other in the relation of father and son. But between those two the Brâhma<i>n</i>a is the father. <a name="fr_338"></a><a href="#fn_337"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. A younger person or one of equal age he shall ask, about his well-being (employing the word ku<i>s</i>ala). <a name="fr_339"></a><a href="#fn_338"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. (He shall ask under the same conditions) a Kshatriya, about his health (employing the word anâmaya);</p>  
 <p>28. A Vai<i>s</i>ya if he has lost anything (employing the word anash<i>t</i>a). <a name="fr_340"></a><a href="#fn_339"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>

 <p>29. A <i>S</i>ûdra, about his health (employing the word ârogya).</p>  
 <p>30. He shall not pass a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a without addressing him;</p>  
 <p>31. Nor an (unprotected) woman in a forest (or any other lonely place). <a name="fr_341"></a><a href="#fn_340"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>
