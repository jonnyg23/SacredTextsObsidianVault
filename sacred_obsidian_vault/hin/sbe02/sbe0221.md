   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 6, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 18.</h3>  
 <p>1. Honey, uncooked (grain), venison, land, roots, fruits, (a promise of) safety, a pasture for cattle, a house, and fodder for a draught-ox may be accepted (even) from an Ugra. <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_400"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Hârita declares, that even these (presents) are to be accepted only if they have been obtained by a pupil.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Or they (Brâhma<i>n</i>a householders) may accept (from an Ugra) uncooked or (a little) unflavoured boiled food.</p>  
 <p>4. (Of such food) they shall not take a great quantity (but only so much as suffices to support life). <a name="fr_402"></a><a href="#fn_401"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. If (in times of distress) he is unable to keep himself, he may eat (food obtained from anybody),</p>  
 <p>6. After having touched it (once) with gold,</p>  
 <p>7. Or (having touched it with) fire.</p>  
 <p>8. He shall not be too eager after (such a way of living). He shall leave it when he has obtained a (lawful) livelihood. <a name="fr_403"></a><a href="#fn_402"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (A student of the Brahmanic caste) who has returned home shall not eat (in the house) of people belonging to the three tribes, beginning with the Kshatriya (i. e. of Kshatriyas, Vai<i>s</i>yas, and <i>S</i>ûdras). <a name="fr_404"></a><a href="#fn_403"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. He may (usually) eat (the food) of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a on account of (the giver's) character (as a Brâhma<i>n</i>a). It must be avoided for particular reasons only.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. He shall not eat in a house where (the host) performs a rite which is not a rite of penance, whilst he ought to perform a penance. <a name="fr_405"></a><a href="#fn_404"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. But when the penance has been performed, he may eat (in that house). <a name="fr_406"></a><a href="#fn_405"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. According to some (food offered by people) of any caste, who follow the laws prescribed for them, except that of <i>S</i>ûdras, may be eaten.</p>  
 <p>14. (In times of distress) even the food of a <i>S</i>ûdra, who lives under one's protection for the sake of spiritual merit, (may be eaten). <a name="fr_407"></a><a href="#fn_406"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. He may eat it, after having touched it (once) with gold or with fire. He shall not be too eager after (such a way of living). He shall leave it when he obtains a (lawful) livelihood. <a name="fr_408"></a><a href="#fn_407"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Food received from a multitude of givers must not be eaten, <a name="fr_409"></a><a href="#fn_408"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. Nor food offered by a general invitation (to all comers). <a name="fr_410"></a><a href="#fn_409"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Food offered by an artisan must not be eaten, <a name="fr_411"></a><a href="#fn_410"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. Nor (that of men) who live by the use of arms (with the exception of Kshatriyas), <a name="fr_412"></a><a href="#fn_411"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Nor (that of men) who live by letting lodgings or land.</p>  
 <p>21. A (professional) physician is a person whose food must not be eaten, <a name="fr_413"></a><a href="#fn_412"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. (Also) a usurer, <a name="fr_414"></a><a href="#fn_413"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. (Also) a Brâhma<i>n</i>a who has performed the Dîksha<i>n</i>îyesh<i>t</i>i (or initiatory ceremony of the Soma-sacrifice) before he has bought the king (Soma). <a name="fr_415"></a><a href="#fn_414"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. (The food given by a person who has performed the Dîksha<i>n</i>îyesh<i>t</i>i may be eaten), when the victim sacred to Agni and Soma has been slain.</p>  
 <p>25. Or after that the omentum of the victim (sacred to Agni and Soma) has been offered. <a name="fr_416"></a><a href="#fn_415"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. For a Brâhma<i>n</i>a declares, 'Or they may eat of the remainder of the animal, after having set apart a portion for the offering.'</p>  
 <p>27. A eunuch (is a person whose food must not be eaten), <a name="fr_417"></a><a href="#fn_416"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. (Likewise) the (professional) messenger employed by a king (or others), <a name="fr_418"></a><a href="#fn_417"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. (Likewise a Brâhma<i>n</i>a) who offers substances that are not fit for a sacrifice, <a name="fr_419"></a><a href="#fn_418"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. (Likewise) a spy, <a name="fr_420"></a><a href="#fn_419"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. (Also) a person who has become an ascetic without (being authorized thereto by) the rules (of the law), <a name="fr_421"></a><a href="#fn_420"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. (Also) he who forsakes the sacred fires without performing the sacrifice necessary on that occasion), <a name="fr_422"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. Likewise a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a who avoids everybody, or eats the food of anybody, or neglects the (daily) recitation of the Veda, (and) he whose (only living) wife is of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste. <a name="fr_423"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_400"></a><a href="sbe0221.htm#fr_401">65:1</a> <b>18</b>. Manu IV, 247. 'Ugra denotes either a bad twice-born man. or the offspring of a Vai<i>s</i>ya and of a <i>S</i>ûdra-woman. Other persons of a similar character must be understood to be included by the term.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_401"></a><a href="sbe0221.htm#fr_402">66:4</a> Also this rule seems to belong to Hârita, on account of its close connection with the preceding two.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_402"></a><a href="sbe0221.htm#fr_403">66:8</a> Haradatta quotes, in support of the last Sûtras, a passage of the <i>Kh</i>ândogya Upanishad, I, 10, 1, and one from the .<i>Ri</i><i>g</i>-veda, IV, 18, 13, according to which it would be lawful to eat even impure food, as a dog's entrails, under such circumstances. Other commentators explain this and the preceding three Sûtras differently. According to them the translation would run thus: 'If he himself does not find any livelihood (in times of distress, he may dwell even with low-caste people who give him something to eat, and) he may eat (food given by them) paying for it with (some small gift in) gold or with animals.' This second explanation is perhaps preferable.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_403"></a><a href="sbe0221.htm#fr_404">66:9</a> Manu IV, 219, and 223.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_404"></a><a href="sbe0221.htm#fr_405">67:11</a> If a Brâhma<i>n</i>a who has been ordered to perform a penance, performs a Vai<i>s</i>vadeva or other rite without heeding the order of his spiritual teacher, then a student who has returned home ought not to eat in his house, until the enjoined penance has been performed.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_405"></a><a href="sbe0221.htm#fr_406">67:12</a> 'The use of the part. perf. pass. "performed" indicates that he must not eat there, whilst the penance is being performed.'--Haradatta.</p>
