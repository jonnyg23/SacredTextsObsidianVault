   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 1, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>1. For as many years as there are uninitiated persons, reckoning (one year) for each ancestor (and the person to be initiated himself),</p>  
 <p>2. (They should bathe daily reciting) the seven <a name="fr_131"></a><a href="#fn_130"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_6"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 6</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Pâvamânîs, beginning with 'If near or far,' the Ya<i>g</i>ushpavitra, ('May the waters, the mothers purify us,' &amp;c.) the Sâmapavitra, ('With what help assists,' &amp;c.), and the Âṅgirasapavitra ('A swan, dwelling in purity'),</p>  
 <p>3. Or also reciting the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis (om, bhû<i>h</i>, bhuva<i>h</i>, suva<i>h</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. After that (such a person) may be taught (the Veda).</p>  
 <p>5. But those whose great-grandfather's (grandfather's and father's) initiation is not remembered, are called 'burial-grounds.'</p>  
 <p>6. Intercourse, dining, and intermarriage with them should be avoided. For them, if they like, the (following) penance (is prescribed). (Such a man) shall keep for twelve years the rules prescribed for a student who is studying the three Vedas. Afterwards he may be initiated. Then he shall bathe, reciting the Pâvamânîs and the other (texts mentioned above, I, 1, 2, 2).</p>  
 <p>7. Then he may be instructed in the duties of a householder.</p>  
 <p>8. He shall not be taught (the whole Veda), but only the sacred formulas required for the domestic ceremonies.</p>  
 <p>9. When he has finished this (study of the G<i>ri</i>hya-mantras), he may be initiated (after having performed the penance prescribed) for the first neglect (I, 1, 1, 28).</p>  
 <p>10. Afterwards (everything is performed) as in the case of a regular initiation. <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_7"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 7</font></a></p>  
 <p>He who has been initiated shall dwell as a religious student in the house of his teacher, <a name="fr_133"></a><a href="#fn_132"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. For forty-eight years (if he learns all the four Vedas), <a name="fr_134"></a><a href="#fn_133"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. (Or) a quarter less (i.e. for thirty-six years),</p>  
 <p>14. (Or) less by half (i.e. for twenty-four years),</p>  
 <p>15. (Or) three quarters less (i.e. for twelve years),</p>  
 <p>16. Twelve years (should be) the shortest time (for his residence with his teacher). <a name="fr_135"></a><a href="#fn_134"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. A student who studies the sacred science shall not dwell with anybody else (than his teacher). <a name="fr_136"></a><a href="#fn_135"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Now (follow) the rules for the studentship.</p>  
 <p>19. He shall obey his teacher, except (when ordered to commit) crimes which cause loss of caste. <a name="fr_137"></a><a href="#fn_136"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. He shall do what is serviceable to his teacher, he shall not contradict him. <a name="fr_138"></a><a href="#fn_137"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. He shall always occupy a couch or seat lower (than that of his teacher). <a name="fr_139"></a><a href="#fn_138"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_8"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 8</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. He shall not eat food offered (at a sacrifice to the gods or the Manes),</p>  
 <p>23. Nor pungent condiments, salt, honey, or meat. <a name="fr_140"></a><a href="#fn_139"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. He shall not sleep in the day-time.</p>  
 <p>25. He shall not use perfumes. <a name="fr_141"></a><a href="#fn_140"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. He shall preserve chastity. <a name="fr_142"></a><a href="#fn_141"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. He shall not embellish himself (by using ointments and the like). <a name="fr_143"></a><a href="#fn_142"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. He shall not wash his body (with hot water for pleasure).</p>  
 <p>29. But, if it is soiled by unclean things, he shall clean it (with earth or water), in a place where he is not seen by a Guru. <a name="fr_144"></a><a href="#fn_143"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. Let him not sport in the water whilst bathing; let him swim (motionless) like a stick. <a name="fr_145"></a><a href="#fn_144"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. He shall wear all his hair tied in one braid. <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. Or let him make a braid of the lock on the crown of the head, and shave the rest of the hair.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_9"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 9</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. The girdle of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall be made of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass, and consist of three strings; if possible, (the strings) should be twisted to the right. <a name="fr_147"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. A bowstring (should be the girdle) of a Kshatriya,</p>  
 <p>35. Or a string of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass in which pieces of iron have been tied.</p>  
 <p>36. A wool thread (shall be the girdle) of a Vai<i>s</i>ya,</p>  
 <p>37. Or a rope used for yoking the oxen to the plough, or a stringy made of Tamala-bark.</p>  
 <p>38. The staff worn by a Brâhma<i>n</i>a should be made of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood, that of a Kshatriya of a branch of the Banian tree, which grows downwards, that of a Vai<i>s</i>ya of Bâdara or Udumbara wood. Some declare, without any reference to caste, that the staff of a student should be made of the wood of a tree (that is fit to be used at the sacrifice). <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. (He shall wear) a cloth (to cover his nakedness). <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. (It shall be made) of hemp for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, of flax (for a Kshatriya), of the skin of a (clean) animal (for a Vai<i>s</i>ya). <a name="fr_150"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">40</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. Some declare that the (upper) garment (of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a) should be dyed with red Lodh, <a name="fr_151"></a><a href="#fn_150"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>

The Sâmapavitra is found Sâma-veda I, 2, 2, 3, 5. Âṅgirasapavitra = <i>Ri</i><i>g</i>-veda IV, 40, 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_131"></a><a href="sbe0205.htm#fr_132">6:10</a> The commentator observes that for those whose great-great-grandfather or remoter ancestors were not initiated, no penance is prescribed, and that it must be fixed by those who know the law.</p>

This rendering also is admissible, as the word para may mean either a 'stranger' or 'heaven' and upavâsa, 'dwelling' or 'fasting.'</p>
