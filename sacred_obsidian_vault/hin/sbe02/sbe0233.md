   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 11, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 30.</h3>  
 <p>1. Some declare, that a student shall bathe after (having acquired) the knowledge of the Veda, (however long or short the time of his studentship may have been). <a name="fr_499"></a><a href="#fn_498"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (He may) also (bathe) after having kept the student's vow for forty-eight, (thirty-six or twenty-four) years, (though he may not have mastered the Veda).</p>  
 <p>Some declare, that the student (shall bathe) after (having acquired) the knowledge of the Veda and after (the expiration of) his vow.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. To all those persons who have bathed (In accordance with any of the above rules must be shown) the honour clue to a Snâtaka.</p>  
 <p>5. The reverence (shown to a Snâtaka) brings, however, different rewards according to the degree of devotion or of learning (possessed by the person honoured).</p>  
 <p>6. Now follow the observances (chiefly to be kept) by a Snâtaka.</p>  
 <p>7. He shall usually enter the village and leave it by the eastern or the northern gate.</p>  
 <p>8. During the morning and evening twilights, he shall sit outside the village, and not speak anything (referring to worldly matters).</p>  
 <p>9. (But an Agnihotrî, who is occupied at home by oblations in the morning and evening, must not go out; for) in the case of a conflict (of duties), that enjoined by the Veda is the more important.</p>  
 <p>10. He shall avoid all dyed dresses, <a name="fr_500"></a><a href="#fn_499"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. And all naturally black cloth.</p>  
 <p>12. He shall wear a dress that is neither shining,</p>  
 <p>13. Nor despicable, if he is able (to afford it). <a name="fr_501"></a><a href="#fn_500"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. And in the day-time he shall avoid to wrap up his head, except when voiding excrements.</p>  
 <p>15. But when voiding excrements, he shall envelop his head and place some (grass or the like) on the ground. <a name="fr_502"></a><a href="#fn_501"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. He shall not void excrements in the shade (of a tree, where travellers rest).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 94</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. But he may discharge urine on his own shadow.</p>  
 <p>18. He shall not void excrements with his shoes on, nor on a ploughed field, nor on a path, nor in water. <a name="fr_503"></a><a href="#fn_502"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. He shall also avoid to spit into, or to have connection with a woman in water. <a name="fr_504"></a><a href="#fn_503"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. He shall not void excrements facing the fire, the sun, water, a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, cows, or (images of) the gods. <a name="fr_505"></a><a href="#fn_504"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. He shall avoid to clean his body from excrements with a stone, a clod of earth, or with (boughs of) herbs or trees which he has broken off, whilst they were on the tree and full of sap.</p>  
 <p>22. If possible, he shall not stretch out his feet towards a fire, water, a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, a cow, (iniages of) the gods, a door, or against the wind. <a name="fr_506"></a><a href="#fn_505"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>
