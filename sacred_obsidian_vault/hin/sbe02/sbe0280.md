   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The annual (term for studying the Veda) begins on the full moon of the month Srâva<i>n</i>a (July-August); or let him perform the Upâkarman on <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1258"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_260"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 260</font></a></p>  
 <p>(the full moon of) Bhâdrapada (August-September) and study the Vedic texts,</p>  
 <p>2. During four months and a half, or during five months, or as long as the sun moves towards the South. <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1259"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Let him remain chaste, let him not shave, nor eat flesh (during that period); <a name="fr_1261"></a><a href="#fn_1260"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Or (this) restrictive rule may (be observed) during two months.</p>  
 <p>5. He shall not recite the Veda, if the wind whirls up the dust in the day-time, <a name="fr_1262"></a><a href="#fn_1261"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Nor if it is audible at night,</p>  
 <p>7. Nor if the sound of a Vâ<i>n</i>a, of a large or a small drum, the noise of a chariot, and the wail of a person in pain (are heard), <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1262"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Nor if the barking of many dogs and jackals, or the braying of many donkeys (is heard),</p>  
 <p>9. Nor if (the sky appears flaming) red, a rainbow (is seen), or hoar-frost (lies on the ground), <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1263"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Nor if clouds rise out of season. <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1264"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (Let him not study) when he feels the necessity to void urine or excrements, <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1265"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Nor at midnight, in the twilight, and (while standing) in the water, <a name="fr_1267"></a><a href="#fn_1266"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Nor while rain falls. <a name="fr_1268"></a><a href="#fn_1267"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_261"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 261</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. Some (declare, that the recitation of the Veda must be interrupted only) when (the rain) is dripping from the edge of the roof.</p>  
 <p>15. (Nor shall he study) when the teachers (of the gods and Âsuras, i.e. the planets Jupiter and Venus) are surrounded by a halo, <a name="fr_1269"></a><a href="#fn_1268"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Nor (when this happens) to the two (great) lights (the sun and the moon), <a name="fr_1270"></a><a href="#fn_1269"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. (Nor) while he is in fear, riding in a carriage or on beasts of burden, or lying down, nor while his feet are raised, <a name="fr_1271"></a><a href="#fn_1270"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. (Nor) in a burial-ground, at the extremity of a village, on a high-road, nor during impurity, <a name="fr_1272"></a><a href="#fn_1271"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. Nor while a foul smell (is perceptible), while a corpse or a Ka<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla (is) in (the village), nor in the neighbourhood of a <i>S</i>ûdra, <a name="fr_1273"></a><a href="#fn_1272"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Nor while (he suffers from) sour eructations. <a name="fr_1274"></a><a href="#fn_1273"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. The <i>Ri</i><i>g</i>-veda and the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda (shall not be studied) while the sound of the Sâmans (is heard). <a name="fr_1275"></a><a href="#fn_1274"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. The fall of a thunderbolt, an earthquake, an eclipse, and (the fall of) meteors (are reasons for discontinuing the reading of the Veda) until the same time (next day), <a name="fr_1276"></a><a href="#fn_1275"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Likewise when it thunders and rains and <a name="fr_1277"></a><a href="#fn_1276"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_262"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 262</font></a></p>  
 <p>when lightning (flashes out of season) after the fires have become visible (in the twilight).</p>  
 <p>24. (If these phenomena appear) during the (rainy) season, (the reading must be interrupted) for a day (or a night), <a name="fr_1278"></a><a href="#fn_1277"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. And if lightning (is observed) during the night, (the recitation of the Veda shall be interrupted) until the third watch. <a name="fr_1279"></a><a href="#fn_1278"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. If (lightning) flashes during the third part of the day or later, (the Veda must not be read) during the entire (following night).</p>  
 <p>27. (According to the opinion) of some, a fiery meteor (has the same effect) as lightning,</p>  
 <p>28. Likewise thunder (which is heard) during the last part of the day,</p>  
 <p>29. (Or) also in the twilight.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_263"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 263</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. (If thunder is heard) before midnight, (the study of the Veda must be interrupted) during the whole night. <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1279"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. (If it is heard) during the (early part of the) day, (the interruption must continue) as long as the sun shines,</p>  
 <p>32. Likewise if the king of the country has died.</p>  
 <p>33. If one (pupil) has gone on a journey (and) another (stays) with (the teacher, the study of the Veda shall be interrupted until the absentee returns). <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1280"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. When an attack (is made on the village), or a fire (breaks out), when one Veda has been completed, after (an attack of) vomiting, when he has partaken of a funeral repast or of a dinner on the occasion of a sacrifice offered to men, (the study of the Veda shall be interrupted) for a day and a night, <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. Likewise on the day of the new moon.</p>  
 <p>36. (On the latter occasion it may also be interrupted) for two days. <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1282"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. (The Veda shall not be studied for a day and a night) on the full moon days of the months Kârttika, Phâlgu<i>n</i>a, and Âshâ<i>dh</i>a. <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1283"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_264"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 264</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. On the three Ash<i>t</i>akâs (the Veda shall not be studied) for three (days and) nights. <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. Some (declare, that the rule applies) to the last Ash<i>t</i>akâ (only).</p>  
 <p>40. (On the occasion of) the annual (Upâkarman and Utsarga the reading shall be interrupted) on the day (of the ceremony) and those preceding and following it. <a name="fr_1286"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">40</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. All (teachers declare, that the reading shall be interrupted for three days) when rain, thunder, and lightning (are observed) simultaneously, <a name="fr_1287"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. When the rain is very heavy, (the reading shall be interrupted as long as it lasts). <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1287"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. On a festive day (the reading shall be stopped) after the (morning) meal, <a name="fr_1289"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">43</font></a></p>  
 <p>44. And he who has begun to study (after the Upâkarman shall not read) at night for four Muhûrtas. <a name="fr_1290"></a><a href="#fn_1289"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. Some (declare, that the recitation of the Veda is) always (forbidden) in a town. <a name="fr_1291"></a><a href="#fn_1290"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. While he is impure (he shall) not even (recite the Veda) mentally. <a name="fr_1292"></a><a href="#fn_1291"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 265</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. (The study) of those who offer a funeral sacrifice (must be interrupted) until the same time next day, <a name="fr_1293"></a><a href="#fn_1292"><font size="1">47</font></a></p>  
 <p>48. Even if uncooked grain is offered at the funeral sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>49. And (those rules regarding the stoppage of the reading must be observed), which they teach in the several schools. <a name="fr_1294"></a><a href="#fn_1293"><font size="1">49</font></a></p>
