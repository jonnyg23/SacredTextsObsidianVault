 <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER XXXI</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">Brahman said .</p>
 <p>Thus <a name="fr_1820"></a><a href="#fn_1813"><font size="1">1</font></a> duly studying to the best of his power, in the way above <a name="fr_1821"></a><a href="#fn_1814"><font size="1">2</font></a> stated, and likewise living as a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin, one who is devoted to his own. duty and learned, who is a sage with all his senses restrained, who applies himself to what is agreeable and beneficial to the preceptor, who is pure <a name="fr_1822"></a><a href="#fn_1815"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and constant in veracity and piety, should, with the permission of the preceptor, take food without decrying it <a name="fr_1823"></a><a href="#fn_1816"><font size="1">4</font></a>, should eat (the leavings) of sacrificial offerings, and alms, and should stand, sit, and take exercise <a name="fr_1824"></a><a href="#fn_1817"><font size="1">5</font></a> (duly), should sacrifice twice to the fire after becoming clean and with a concentrated (mind), and should always bear a staff of the Bilva or Palâ<i>s</i>a <a name="fr_1825"></a><a href="#fn_1818"><font size="1">6</font></a> (wood). The clothing of the twice-born (man) should be of linen, or of cotton, or also a deerskin, or a cloth entirely (dyed with) reddish colour. There should also be a girdle of muñ<i>g</i>a; he should have matted hair, and likewise always (carry) water (with him), and have his sacred thread, be engaged in sacred</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_361"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 361</font></a></p>
 <p>study, and free from avarice, and of rigid observances. (Such) a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin, always making offerings likewise of pure water to satisfy the deities, being restrained in mind <a name="fr_1826"></a><a href="#fn_1819"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is esteemed. One who is thus devoted <a name="fr_1827"></a><a href="#fn_1820"><font size="1">2</font></a>, who is concentrated in mind, and continent <a name="fr_1828"></a><a href="#fn_1821"><font size="1">3</font></a>, conquers heaven, and reaching the highest seat, does not return to birth. Refined by means of all ceremonies, and likewise living as a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin <a name="fr_1829"></a><a href="#fn_1822"><font size="1">4</font></a>, a sage who has renounced <a name="fr_1830"></a><a href="#fn_1823"><font size="1">5</font></a> (all) should go out of towns and dwell in forests <a name="fr_1831"></a><a href="#fn_1824"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Wearing a skin or the bark of a tree, he should bathe (every) morning and evening, and always living within the forest, should not enter a town again. He should honour guests, and should also give them shelter at (the proper) time, living on fruits and leaves, and roots and <i>S</i>yâmâka grain. He should without sloth feed on water, air, and all forest-products down to grass as they come, in order <a name="fr_1832"></a><a href="#fn_1825"><font size="1">7</font></a>, in accordance with the (regulations <a name="fr_1833"></a><a href="#fn_1826"><font size="1">8</font></a> at his) initiation. He should honour a guest who comes, by (giving him) water accompanied with roots, fruits, and leaves. And he should always without sloth give alms out of whatever he has for food. He should also eat always after the deities and guests <a name="fr_1834"></a><a href="#fn_1827"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>

 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_362"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 362</font></a></p>
 <p>(are satisfied) and with his speech restrained, having a mind free from envy <a name="fr_1835"></a><a href="#fn_1828"><font size="1">1</font></a>, eating little, and depending on the deities. Restraining the external senses, kind, full of forgiveness, preserving his hair and moustache, performing sacrifices, addicted to sacred study, and devoted to veracity and piety, pure in body <a name="fr_1836"></a><a href="#fn_1829"><font size="1">2</font></a>, always dexterous <a name="fr_1837"></a><a href="#fn_1830"><font size="1">3</font></a>, always in forests, and concentrated in mind,--a forester whose senses are subdued and who is thus devoted <a name="fr_1838"></a><a href="#fn_1831"><font size="1">4</font></a> conquers the worlds. A householder, or a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin, or again a forester, who wishes to apply himself to final emancipation should adopt the best (line of) conduct <a name="fr_1839"></a><a href="#fn_1832"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Offering safety to all beings, the sage should become free from all action <a name="fr_1840"></a><a href="#fn_1833"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and be agreeable to all beings, kind, and restrained in all his senses. He should make a fire <a name="fr_1841"></a><a href="#fn_1834"><font size="1">7</font></a> and feed on the alms (obtained) without asking <a name="fr_1842"></a><a href="#fn_1835"><font size="1">8</font></a> and without trouble <a name="fr_1843"></a><a href="#fn_1836"><font size="1">9</font></a>, and which have come spontaneously <a name="fr_1844"></a><a href="#fn_1837"><font size="1">10</font></a>, in a place free from smoke and where people have already <a name="fr_1845"></a><a href="#fn_1838"><font size="1">11</font></a> eaten. One who</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>

 <p>

 </p>

 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_363"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 363</font></a></p>
 <p>understands final emancipation should seek to obtain alms after the cleaning <a name="fr_1846"></a><a href="#fn_1839"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the vessels (used for cooking), and should not rejoice if he obtains, and should not be dejected if he does not obtain (alms). Nor should he beg for too much alms <a name="fr_1847"></a><a href="#fn_1840"><font size="1">2</font></a>, seeking merely to sustain life. Eating only a little, he should go about for alms with a concentrated mind, looking out for the (proper) time. He should not wish for earnings in common with another, nor should he eat when honoured; for an ascetic should be averse from all earnings (accompanied) with honour <a name="fr_1848"></a><a href="#fn_1841"><font size="1">3</font></a>. When eating, he should not taste any articles of food which have been eaten by others <a name="fr_1849"></a><a href="#fn_1842"><font size="1">4</font></a>, or which are pungent, astringent, or bitter, and likewise no sweet juices. He should eat just enough for his livelihood-for the support of life. One who understands final emancipation should seek for a livelihood without obstructing (other) creatures; and when he goes about for alms, he should not go following after another <a name="fr_1850"></a><a href="#fn_1843"><font size="1">5</font></a>. He should not parade (his) piety, he should move about in a secluded place, free from passion. He should resort for shelter to an empty house, or a forest, or the foot of a tree, or a river likewise, or the cavern of a mountain. In summer, (he should pass) but a single night <a name="fr_1851"></a><a href="#fn_1844"><font size="1">6</font></a> in a town; and in the rains, he may dwell in one place. He should move about the</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_364"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 364</font></a></p>
 <p>world like a worm <a name="fr_1852"></a><a href="#fn_1845"><font size="1">1</font></a>, his path being pointed out by the sun, and he should walk with circumspection over the earth out of compassion to all beings <a name="fr_1853"></a><a href="#fn_1846"><font size="1">2</font></a>. He should not make any accumulations; and should eschew dwelling with friends <a name="fr_1854"></a><a href="#fn_1847"><font size="1">3</font></a>. And the man who understands final emancipation should verily do all acts which he has to do, always with clean water. A man should always bathe in clean water. And with his senses restrained, he should devote himself to these eight observances <a name="fr_1855"></a><a href="#fn_1848"><font size="1">4</font></a>--harmlessness, life as a Brahmakârin, veracity, and also straightforwardness, freedom from anger, freedom from (the habit of) carping, restraint of the external organs, and habitual freedom from (the habit of) backbiting. He should always practice a sinless (mode of) conduct, not deceptive and not crooked; and free from attachment should always make one who comes (as a guest) take a morsel of food. He should eat just enough for livelihood-for the support of life. And he should eat (only) what has been obtained with piety <a name="fr_1856"></a><a href="#fn_1849"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and should not follow his own (mere) desire <a name="fr_1857"></a><a href="#fn_1850"><font size="1">6</font></a>. He should not accept anything at all other than food and clothing. And he should accept as much as he eats and no more. He should not receive from others, nor should he ever give to others <a name="fr_1858"></a><a href="#fn_1851"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>
 <p>

 </p>
 <p><a name="page_365"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 365</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> But owing to the helplessness of people, a wise man should always share (with others). He should not appropriate another's riches, and should not take (anything) unasked. Nor, verily, after enjoying any object should one become afterwards attached to it. One who has anything to do <a name="fr_1859"></a><a href="#fn_1852"><font size="1">1</font></a> should take earth, water, pebbles likewise, and leaves, flowers, and fruits which are not secured <a name="fr_1860"></a><a href="#fn_1853"><font size="1">2</font></a> (by anybody), as they come <a name="fr_1861"></a><a href="#fn_1854"><font size="1">3</font></a>. One should not live by the occupation of an artisan <a name="fr_1862"></a><a href="#fn_1855"><font size="1">4</font></a>, nor should one wish for gold. One should not hate, should not teach <a name="fr_1863"></a><a href="#fn_1856"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and should be void of (all) belongings <a name="fr_1864"></a><a href="#fn_1857"><font size="1">6</font></a>. One should eat what is consecrated by faith <a name="fr_1865"></a><a href="#fn_1858"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and should avoid (all) controversies, should act without a purpose <a name="fr_1866"></a><a href="#fn_1859"><font size="1">8</font></a>, should be free from attachment, and without fixed appointments with people <a name="fr_1867"></a><a href="#fn_1860"><font size="1">9</font></a>. One should not perform, or cause to be performed, any action involving expectation of fruit, or involving any destruction of life, or the assemblage of people <a name="fr_1868"></a><a href="#fn_1861"><font size="1">10</font></a>. Rejecting all things,</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>

 </p>

 </p>

 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_366"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 366</font></a></p>
 <p>and being equable to all beings, moving and unmoving, one should become an ascetic with small belongings. One should not perturb any other (person), nor should one be perturbed by any other (person <a name="fr_1869"></a><a href="#fn_1862"><font size="1">1</font></a>). He who is trusted by all beings is said to be the foremost among those who understand final emancipation. One should not think of what is not come <a name="fr_1870"></a><a href="#fn_1863"><font size="1">2</font></a>, nor reflect on that which is past; one should disregard the present, being concentrated (in mind) and indifferent to time <a name="fr_1871"></a><a href="#fn_1864"><font size="1">3</font></a>. He should not defile <a name="fr_1872"></a><a href="#fn_1865"><font size="1">4</font></a> anything by the eye, or the mind, or by speech, nor should he do anything wrong openly or in secret. One who draws in the senses from all sides as a tortoise (draws in) his limbs <a name="fr_1873"></a><a href="#fn_1866"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and in whom the senses, mind, and understanding are absorbed <a name="fr_1874"></a><a href="#fn_1867"><font size="1">6</font></a>, who is free from desires, who understands all truth, who is free from the pairs of opposites, and from the ceremony of svâhâ, and who is free from salutations <a name="fr_1875"></a><a href="#fn_1868"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and who is free from (the thought that this or that is) mine, who is free from egoism, who is free from anxiety for new acquisitions or protection of old acquisitions, and self-controlled <a name="fr_1876"></a><a href="#fn_1869"><font size="1">8</font></a>, who is free from</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 367</font></a></p>
 <p>expectations, who is free from attachments to any entity, and who is dependent on none <a name="fr_1877"></a><a href="#fn_1870"><font size="1">1</font></a>, who is attached to the self, and who understands the truth, is emancipated, there is no doubt of that. Those who perceive the self, which is without hands, foot, or back, without a head, without a stomach, which is free from the operations of the qualities <a name="fr_1878"></a><a href="#fn_1871"><font size="1">2</font></a>, absolute, untainted, and stable, devoid of smell, devoid of taste or touch, devoid. of colour, and also devoid of sound, which is to be understood <a name="fr_1879"></a><a href="#fn_1872"><font size="1">3</font></a>, which is unattached, and which is also devoid of flesh, which is free from anxiety <a name="fr_1880"></a><a href="#fn_1873"><font size="1">4</font></a>, imperishable, divine, and though dwelling in a house <a name="fr_1881"></a><a href="#fn_1874"><font size="1">5</font></a>, always dwelling in all entities, they never die <a name="fr_1882"></a><a href="#fn_1875"><font size="1">6</font></a>. There the understanding reaches not, nor the senses, nor the deities, nor Vedas, sacrifices, nor worlds <a name="fr_1883"></a><a href="#fn_1876"><font size="1">7</font></a>, nor penance., nor valour <a name="fr_1884"></a><a href="#fn_1877"><font size="1">8</font></a>; the attainment to it of those who are possessed of knowledge is stated to be without comprehension of symbols <a name="fr_1885"></a><a href="#fn_1878"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Therefore the learned man who knows (the) property of being void of symbols <a name="fr_1886"></a><a href="#fn_1879"><font size="1">10</font></a>, being devoted to pious conduct, and</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_368"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 368</font></a></p>
 <p>resorting to concealed <a name="fr_1887"></a><a href="#fn_1880"><font size="1">1</font></a> piety should adopt the mode of life (necessary) for experience <a name="fr_1888"></a><a href="#fn_1881"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Though undeluded, he should act in the manner of the deluded <a name="fr_1889"></a><a href="#fn_1882"><font size="1">3</font></a>, not finding fault with piety <a name="fr_1890"></a><a href="#fn_1883"><font size="1">4</font></a>. He should perform piety, behaving so that others would always disrespect him <a name="fr_1891"></a><a href="#fn_1884"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and should not find fault with the ways of the good <a name="fr_1892"></a><a href="#fn_1885"><font size="1">6</font></a>. That sage is said to be the best who has adopted this (line of) conduct. The senses, and the objects of the senses, and the five great elements, and mind, understanding, egoism, the unperceived, and the Purusha likewise <a name="fr_1893"></a><a href="#fn_1886"><font size="1">7</font></a>, by an accurate determination about the truth, after understanding all these, one attains heaven <a name="fr_1894"></a><a href="#fn_1887"><font size="1">8</font></a>, being released from all bonds. One who knows the truth, understanding these same (entities) at the time of the termination (of his life), should meditate, exclusively pondering on one point <a name="fr_1895"></a><a href="#fn_1888"><font size="1">9</font></a>; and then, depending on none <a name="fr_1896"></a><a href="#fn_1889"><font size="1">10</font></a>, he gets emancipation. Freed from all attachments, like the atmosphere dwelling in space <a name="fr_1897"></a><a href="#fn_1890"><font size="1">11</font></a>, with his accumulations <a name="fr_1898"></a><a href="#fn_1891"><font size="1">12</font></a> exhausted, and free from distress <a name="fr_1899"></a><a href="#fn_1892"><font size="1">13</font></a>, he attains to the highest seat.</p>
