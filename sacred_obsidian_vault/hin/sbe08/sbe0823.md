 <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER II.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">Vaisampâyana said:</p>
 <p>Then the talented king, Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra, bowed <a name="fr_615"></a><a href="#fn_612"><font size="1">2</font></a> to those words uttered by Vidura, and, in a secluded place <a name="fr_616"></a><a href="#fn_613"><font size="1">3</font></a>, interrogated Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta regarding the highest knowledge <a name="fr_617"></a><a href="#fn_614"><font size="1">4</font></a>, wishing to become (a) high-souled (man) <a name="fr_618"></a><a href="#fn_615"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>O Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta! which of the two is correct, your teaching <a name="fr_619"></a><a href="#fn_616"><font size="1">6</font></a>, about which I have heard, that death exists not, or that <a name="fr_620"></a><a href="#fn_617"><font size="1">7</font></a> the gods and demons practised</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 152</font></a></p>
 <p>the life of Brahma<i>k</i>ârins <a name="fr_621"></a><a href="#fn_618"><font size="1">1</font></a>, for freedom from death?</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said</p>
 <p>Some (say), that freedom from death (results) from action <a name="fr_622"></a><a href="#fn_619"><font size="1">2</font></a>; and others that death exists not. Hear me explain (this), O king! have no misgiving about it <a name="fr_623"></a><a href="#fn_620"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Both truths, O Kshatriya! have been current from the beginning <a name="fr_624"></a><a href="#fn_621"><font size="1">4</font></a>. The wise maintain what (is called) delusion (to be) death. I <a name="fr_625"></a><a href="#fn_622"><font size="1">5</font></a> verily call heedlessness death, and likewise I call freedom from heedlessness immortality. Through heedlessness, verily, were the demons <a name="fr_626"></a><a href="#fn_623"><font size="1">6</font></a> vanquished; and through freedom</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 153</font></a></p>
 <p>from heedlessness the gods attained to the Brahman. Death, verily, does not devour living creatures like a tiger; for, indeed, his form is not to be perceived. Some <a name="fr_627"></a><a href="#fn_624"><font size="1">1</font></a> say that death is different from this, (named) Yama, who dwells in the self <a name="fr_628"></a><a href="#fn_625"><font size="1">2</font></a> the (practice of the) life of Brahma<i>k</i>ârins (being) immortality. That god governs his kingdom in the world of the Pit<i>ri</i>s, (being) good to the good, and not good to (those who are) not good. That death, (or) heedlessness, develops in men as desire, and afterwards as wrath, and in the shape of delusion <a name="fr_629"></a><a href="#fn_626"><font size="1">3</font></a>. And then travelling in devious paths <a name="fr_630"></a><a href="#fn_627"><font size="1">4</font></a> through egoism, one does not attain to union <a name="fr_631"></a><a href="#fn_628"><font size="1">5</font></a> with the self. Those who are deluded by it <a name="fr_632"></a><a href="#fn_629"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and who remain under its influence, depart from this (world), and there again fall down <a name="fr_633"></a><a href="#fn_630"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Then, the deities <a name="fr_634"></a><a href="#fn_631"><font size="1">8</font></a> gather around them. And then he undergoes death after death <a name="fr_635"></a><a href="#fn_632"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Being attached to the fruit of action, on action presenting itself, they follow after it <a name="fr_636"></a><a href="#fn_633"><font size="1">10</font></a>, and do not cross</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_154"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 154</font></a></p>
 <p>beyond death. And the embodied (self), in consequence of not understanding union <a name="fr_637"></a><a href="#fn_634"><font size="1">1</font></a> with the real entity, proceeds on all hands <a name="fr_638"></a><a href="#fn_635"><font size="1">2</font></a> with attachment to enjoyments. That <a name="fr_639"></a><a href="#fn_636"><font size="1">3</font></a>, verily, is the great source of delusion to the senses; for by contact <a name="fr_640"></a><a href="#fn_637"><font size="1">4</font></a> with unreal entities, his migrations <a name="fr_641"></a><a href="#fn_638"><font size="1">5</font></a> are (rendered) inevitable; because having his inner self contaminated by contact with unreal entities, he devotes himself to objects of sense on all sides, pondering on them (only). (That) pondering, verily, first. ruins <a name="fr_642"></a><a href="#fn_639"><font size="1">6</font></a> him; and soon afterwards desire and wrath, after attacking him. These <a name="fr_643"></a><a href="#fn_640"><font size="1">7</font></a> lead children to death. But sensible men cross beyond death by their good sense. He who pondering (on the self) destroys <a name="fr_644"></a><a href="#fn_641"><font size="1">8</font></a> (the) fugitive (objects of sense), not even thinking of them through contempt (for them), and who being possessed of knowledge destroys desires in this way, becomes, as it were, the death of death (itself), and swallows (it) up <a name="fr_645"></a><a href="#fn_642"><font size="1">9</font></a>. The being who</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a></p>
 <p>pursues desires, is destroyed (in pursuing) after the desires <a name="fr_646"></a><a href="#fn_643"><font size="1">1</font></a>. But casting away desires, a being gets rid of all taint <a name="fr_647"></a><a href="#fn_644"><font size="1">2</font></a> whatever, This body, void of enlightenment <a name="fr_648"></a><a href="#fn_645"><font size="1">3</font></a>, seems (to be) a hell for (all) beings. Those who are avaricious run about <a name="fr_649"></a><a href="#fn_646"><font size="1">4</font></a>, going headlong to a ditch. A man, O Kshatriya! who contemns everything else <a name="fr_650"></a><a href="#fn_647"><font size="1">5</font></a> learns nothing. To him (the body is) like a tiger made of straw <a name="fr_651"></a><a href="#fn_648"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And this internal self (joined to) delusion and fear <a name="fr_652"></a><a href="#fn_649"><font size="1">7</font></a> in consequence of wrath and avarice, within your body, that verily is death <a name="fr_653"></a><a href="#fn_650"><font size="1">8</font></a>. Understanding death <a name="fr_654"></a><a href="#fn_651"><font size="1">9</font></a> to be thus produced, and adhering to knowledge, one is not afraid of death <a name="fr_655"></a><a href="#fn_652"><font size="1">10</font></a> in this (world). In his province death is destroyed, as a mortal (is destroyed) on arriving in the province of death.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>The good, eternal, and most holy worlds <a name="fr_656"></a><a href="#fn_653"><font size="1">11</font></a>, which</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_156"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 156</font></a></p>
 <p>are mentioned (as attainable) by the twice-born by means of worship <a name="fr_657"></a><a href="#fn_654"><font size="1">1</font></a>, those, say the Vedas, are the highest aim <a name="fr_658"></a><a href="#fn_655"><font size="1">2</font></a>. How is it, then, that one who understands this does not resort to action?</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said!</p>
 <p>(Thinking) so, an ignorant man does resort to action. The Vedas likewise do lay down various benefits <a name="fr_659"></a><a href="#fn_656"><font size="1">3</font></a> (for him). But that <a name="fr_660"></a><a href="#fn_657"><font size="1">4</font></a> (man) comes not hither <a name="fr_661"></a><a href="#fn_658"><font size="1">5</font></a>. (Becoming) the supreme self <a name="fr_662"></a><a href="#fn_659"><font size="1">6</font></a>, he attains the supreme, by the (right) path destroying the wrong paths <a name="fr_663"></a><a href="#fn_660"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>Who <a name="fr_664"></a><a href="#fn_661"><font size="1">8</font></a> is it that constrains this unborn primeval (self), if it is (itself). all this severally <a name="fr_665"></a><a href="#fn_662"><font size="1">9</font></a>? And what</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_157"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 157</font></a></p>
 <p>has it to do, or what is its unhappiness <a name="fr_666"></a><a href="#fn_663"><font size="1">1</font></a>? Tell me all that accurately, O learned person!</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>There is great danger <a name="fr_667"></a><a href="#fn_664"><font size="1">2</font></a> in attributing distinctions to it. The everlasting <a name="fr_668"></a><a href="#fn_665"><font size="1">3</font></a> (principles) exist by connexion with the beginningless <a name="fr_669"></a><a href="#fn_666"><font size="1">4</font></a> (principle). So that his greatness is not lost at all <a name="fr_670"></a><a href="#fn_667"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and beings exist by connexion with the beginningless <a name="fr_671"></a><a href="#fn_666"><font size="1">4</font></a> (principle). That which is the real--the supreme Being <a name="fr_672"></a><a href="#fn_668"><font size="1">6</font></a>--is eternal. He creates the universe by means of changes <a name="fr_673"></a><a href="#fn_669"><font size="1">7</font></a>, for such is his power held to be; and for such connexions of things the Vedas are (authority) <a name="fr_674"></a><a href="#fn_670"><font size="1">8</font></a>.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_158"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 158</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>Since some practise piety <a name="fr_675"></a><a href="#fn_671"><font size="1">1</font></a> in this world, and some likewise practise impiety in this world; is the piety destroyed by the sin, or else. does the piety destroy sin?</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>Whichever <a name="fr_676"></a><a href="#fn_672"><font size="1">2</font></a> he adheres to, the man of understanding always destroys both by means of knowledge; (that is) settled <a name="fr_677"></a><a href="#fn_673"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Likewise, in the other case <a name="fr_678"></a><a href="#fn_674"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the embodied (self) obtains merit; and to such a one sin (also) accrues; (that too is) settled <a name="fr_679"></a><a href="#fn_673"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Departing (from this world), he enjoys by his actions both (kinds of) fruit, which are not enduring <a name="fr_680"></a><a href="#fn_675"><font size="1">5</font></a>--of actions (which are) pure, and of (those which are) sinful. The man of understanding casts aside sin by piety in this (world), for know that his piety is more powerful <a name="fr_681"></a><a href="#fn_676"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Those Brâhma<i>n</i>as, in whom there is emulation <a name="fr_682"></a><a href="#fn_677"><font size="1">7</font></a> about (their) piety, as there is in strong men about (their) strength, after departing from this world, become glorious in heaven <a name="fr_683"></a><a href="#fn_678"><font size="1">8</font></a>. And</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_159"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 159</font></a></p>
 <p>to those in whom there is no emulation about (their) piety, that (piety) is a means of (acquiring) knowledge <a name="fr_684"></a><a href="#fn_679"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Such Brâhma<i>n</i>as released from this (world), go to the heaven which is free from the threefold source of pain <a name="fr_685"></a><a href="#fn_680"><font size="1">2</font></a>. People who understand the Vedas call his conduct good. (But) people closely connected <a name="fr_686"></a><a href="#fn_681"><font size="1">3</font></a>, as well as strangers, do not pay much regard to him. Wherever he may believe food and drink for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a to exist in abundance, like water on grass in the autumn, there would he live and not be vexed <a name="fr_687"></a><a href="#fn_682"><font size="1">4</font></a>. (To him) only that person is good, and no other (as a companion), who does nothing in excess, and who occasions fear and injury to a taciturn man <a name="fr_688"></a><a href="#fn_683"><font size="1">5</font></a>. And his food is acceptable to the good, who does not vex the self of a taciturn man, and who does not destroy the property of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a <a name="fr_689"></a><a href="#fn_684"><font size="1">6</font></a>. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a should hold, that living in the midst of kinsmen, his actions should be always unknown <a name="fr_690"></a><a href="#fn_685"><font size="1">7</font></a>; and he should not</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_160"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 160</font></a></p>
 <p>think <a name="fr_691"></a><a href="#fn_686"><font size="1">1</font></a> (about them). What Brâhma<i>n</i>a ought to think of the inner self, which is void of symbols <a name="fr_692"></a><a href="#fn_687"><font size="1">2</font></a>, immovable, pure, and free from all pairs of opposites, in this way <a name="fr_693"></a><a href="#fn_688"><font size="1">3</font></a>? What sin is not committed by that thief, who steals away his own self <a name="fr_694"></a><a href="#fn_689"><font size="1">4</font></a>, who regards his self as one thing, when it is a different thing. The far-seeing Brâhma<i>n</i>a, who knows the Brahman, is not wearied <a name="fr_695"></a><a href="#fn_690"><font size="1">5</font></a>, he receives nothing <a name="fr_696"></a><a href="#fn_691"><font size="1">6</font></a>; he is honoured, free from trouble <a name="fr_697"></a><a href="#fn_692"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and wise, but acts as if he was not wise <a name="fr_698"></a><a href="#fn_693"><font size="1">8</font></a>. As dogs eat what is vomited, so do they, enjoying their own bravery <a name="fr_699"></a><a href="#fn_694"><font size="1">9</font></a>, eat what is vomited, always with disaster (to themselves). Those twice-born persons, who are not</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_161"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 161</font></a></p>
 <p>first <a name="fr_700"></a><a href="#fn_695"><font size="1">1</font></a> in respect of human wealth, but who are first in the Vedas <a name="fr_701"></a><a href="#fn_696"><font size="1">2</font></a>, are unconquerable, not to be shaken <a name="fr_702"></a><a href="#fn_697"><font size="1">3</font></a>; they should be understood to be forms of the Brahman. Whosoever may in this (world) know all the gods <a name="fr_703"></a><a href="#fn_698"><font size="1">4</font></a>---doers of favours--he is not equal to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, (nor even) he, <a name="fr_704"></a><a href="#fn_699"><font size="1">5</font></a> for whom he exerts himself. The man who makes no efforts <a name="fr_705"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and is respected, does not, being respected, think himself. respected <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">7</font></a>, nor does he become vexed in consequence of disrespect. One who is respected <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">8</font></a> should think it to be a natural operation of people, like their opening or closing of the eyelids, that the learned respect him in this world. One who is not respected should think, that the deluded people who do not understand piety, and who are devoid of (knowledge of) the world and the Sâstras, will never respect one who is worthy of respect. Respect and taciturnity <a name="fr_708"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">9</font></a>, verily, never dwell together; for this world is (the field) for respect, the next for taciturnity, as is understood <a name="fr_709"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">10</font></a>. For worldly wealth dwells in the</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_162"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 162</font></a></p>
 <p>sphere of respect <a name="fr_710"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fn_705"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and that, too, is an obstacle <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fn_706"><font size="1">2</font></a>. While the Brahmic wealth <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fn_707"><font size="1">3</font></a>, O Kshatriya! is difficult to be attained by any one devoid of knowledge. The ways (to it) are stated by the good to be of various descriptions, and difficult to reach--truth, straightforwardness, modesty <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fn_708"><font size="1">4</font></a>, restraint (of senses), purity, knowledge, which are the six impediments (in the way) of respect and delusion.</p>
