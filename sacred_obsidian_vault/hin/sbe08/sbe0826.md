 <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER VI.</h1>
 <p>That pure <a name="fr_899"></a><a href="#fn_894"><font size="1">1</font></a>, great light <a name="fr_900"></a><a href="#fn_895"><font size="1">2</font></a>, which is radiant; that great glory <a name="fr_901"></a><a href="#fn_896"><font size="1">3</font></a>; that, verily, which the gods worship <a name="fr_902"></a><a href="#fn_897"><font size="1">4</font></a>; that by means of which the sun shines forth <a name="fr_903"></a><a href="#fn_898"><font size="1">5</font></a>--that eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. From (that) pure (principle) the Brahman <a name="fr_904"></a><a href="#fn_899"><font size="1">6</font></a> is produced; by (that) pure (principle) the Brahman is developed <a name="fr_905"></a><a href="#fn_900"><font size="1">7</font></a>; that pure (principle), not illumined among all radiant (bodies), is (itself) luminous and illuminates (them) <a name="fr_906"></a><a href="#fn_901"><font size="1">8</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. The perfect is raised out of the perfect. It (being raised) out of the perfect is called the perfect. The perfect is withdrawn from the perfect, and the perfect only remains <a name="fr_907"></a><a href="#fn_902"><font size="1">9</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees.</p>

 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> (From the Brahman), the waters <a name="fr_908"></a><a href="#fn_903"><font size="1">1</font></a> (are produced); and then from the waters, the gross body. In the space within that <a name="fr_909"></a><a href="#fn_904"><font size="1">2</font></a>, dwelt the two divine (principles). Both enveloping the quarters and sub-quarters, support earth and heaven <a name="fr_910"></a><a href="#fn_905"><font size="1">3</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. The horse <a name="fr_911"></a><a href="#fn_906"><font size="1">4</font></a>(-like senses) lead towards heaven him, who is possessed of knowledge and divine, (who is) free from old age, and who stands on the wheel of this chariot(-like body), which is transient, but the operations of which are imperishable <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_907"><font size="1">5</font></a>. That eternal divine being <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_908"><font size="1">6</font></a> is perceived by devotees. His form has no parallel <a name="fr_914"></a><a href="#fn_909"><font size="1">7</font></a>; no one sees him with the eye <a name="fr_915"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">8</font></a>. Those who apprehend him by means, of the understanding, and also the mind and heart, become immortal <a name="fr_916"></a><a href="#fn_911"><font size="1">9</font></a>. That eternal</p>

 <p>divine being is perceived by devotees. The currents of twelve collections <a name="fr_917"></a><a href="#fn_912"><font size="1">1</font></a>, supported by the Deity, regulate the honey <a name="fr_918"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">2</font></a>; and those who follow after it move about in (this) dangerous (world). That eternal divine being <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">3</font></a> is perceived by devotees. The bee <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">4</font></a> drinks that accumulated honey for half a month <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_916"><font size="1">5</font></a>. The Lord created the oblation for all beings <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_917"><font size="1">6</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. Those who are devoid of wings <a name="fr_923"></a><a href="#fn_918"><font size="1">7</font></a>, coming</p>

 <p>to the A<i>s</i>vattha of golden leaves <a name="fr_924"></a><a href="#fn_919"><font size="1">1</font></a>, there become possessed of wings, and fly away happily <a name="fr_925"></a><a href="#fn_920"><font size="1">2</font></a>. That eternal divine being <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_921"><font size="1">3</font></a> is perceived by devotees. The upward life-wind swallows up the downward life-wind; the moon swallows up the upward life-wind; the sun swallows up the moon <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_922"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and another <a name="fr_928"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">5</font></a>, swallows up the sun. Moving about above the waters, the supreme self <a name="fr_929"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">6</font></a> does not raise one leg <a name="fr_930"></a><a href="#fn_925"><font size="1">7</font></a>. (Should he raise) that, which is always performing sacrifices <a name="fr_931"></a><a href="#fn_926"><font size="1">8</font></a>, there will be no death, no immortality <a name="fr_932"></a><a href="#fn_927"><font size="1">9</font></a>. That eternal divine being <a name="fr_933"></a><a href="#fn_928"><font size="1">10</font></a> is perceived by devotees.</p>

 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> The being which is the inner self, and which is of the size of a thumb <a name="fr_934"></a><a href="#fn_929"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is always migrating in consequence of the connexion with the subtle body <a name="fr_935"></a><a href="#fn_930"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The deluded ones do not perceive that praiseworthy lord, primeval and radiant, and possessed of creative power <a name="fr_936"></a><a href="#fn_931"><font size="1">3</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. Leading mortals to destruction by their own action <a name="fr_937"></a><a href="#fn_932"><font size="1">4</font></a>, they conceal themselves like serpents in secret recesses, <a name="fr_938"></a><a href="#fn_933"><font size="1">5</font></a>. The deluded men then become more deluded <a name="fr_939"></a><a href="#fn_934"><font size="1">6</font></a>. The enjoyments afforded by them cause delusion, and lead to worldly life <a name="fr_940"></a><a href="#fn_935"><font size="1">7</font></a>. That eternal divine being <a name="fr_941"></a><a href="#fn_936"><font size="1">8</font></a> is perceived by devotees. This <a name="fr_942"></a><a href="#fn_937"><font size="1">9</font></a> seems to be common to all mankind--whether possessed of resources <a name="fr_943"></a><a href="#fn_938"><font size="1">10</font></a> or not possessed of resources--it is common to immortality and the other <a name="fr_944"></a><a href="#fn_939"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Those who are possessed (of them) <a name="fr_945"></a><a href="#fn_940"><font size="1">12</font></a> attain there to the source of the honey <a name="fr_946"></a><a href="#fn_941"><font size="1">13</font></a>.  <p><a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 191</font></a></p>
 <p>pervading both worlds by knowledge <a name="fr_947"></a><a href="#fn_942"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Then the Agnihotra though not performed is (as good as) performed <a name="fr_948"></a><a href="#fn_943"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Your (knowledge) of the Brahman, therefore, will not lead you to littleness <a name="fr_949"></a><a href="#fn_944"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Knowledge is (his) <a name="fr_950"></a><a href="#fn_945"><font size="1">4</font></a> name. To that the talented ones attain. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. The self of this description absorbing the material cause <a name="fr_951"></a><a href="#fn_946"><font size="1">5</font></a> becomes great. And the self of him who understands that being is not degraded here <a name="fr_952"></a><a href="#fn_947"><font size="1">6</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. One should ever and always be doing good. (There is) no death, whence (can there be) immortality <a name="fr_953"></a><a href="#fn_948"><font size="1">7</font></a>? The real and the unreal have both the same real (entity) as their basis. The source of the existent and the non-existent is but one <a name="fr_954"></a><a href="#fn_949"><font size="1">8</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. The</p>

 <p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 192</font></a></p>
 <p>being who is the inner self, and who is of the size of a thumb, is not seen, being placed in the heart <a name="fr_955"></a><a href="#fn_950"><font size="1">1</font></a>. He is unborn, is moving about day and night, without sloth. Meditating on him, a wise man remains placid <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_951"><font size="1">2</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. From him comes the wind <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">3</font></a>; in him, likewise, is (everything) dissolved. From him (come) the fire and the moon; and from him comes life <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_953"><font size="1">4</font></a>. That is the support (of the universe); that is immortal; that is all things perceptible <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_954"><font size="1">5</font></a>; that is the Brahman, that glory. From that all entities were produced; and in that (they) are dissolved <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_955"><font size="1">6</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. The brilliant (Brahman) supports the two divine principles <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">7</font></a> and the universe, earth and heaven, and the quarters. He from whom the rivers flow in (various) directions, from him were created the great oceans <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">8</font></a>. That eternal divine being is perceived by devotees. Should one fly, even after furnishing oneself with thousands upon thousands of wings, and even though one should have the velocity of thought <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">9</font></a>, one would never reach the end of the (great) cause <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">10</font></a>.  <p>being is perceived by devotees. His form dwells in the unperceived <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">1</font></a>; and those whose understandings are very well refined <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">2</font></a> perceive him. The talented man who has got rid (of affection and aversion) perceives (him) by the mind. Those who understand him <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">3</font></a> become immortal. When one sees this self in all beings stationed in various places <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">4</font></a>, what should one grieve for after that <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">5</font></a>? The Brâhma<i>n</i>a has (as much interest) in all beings, as in a big reservoir of water, to which waters flow from all sides <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">6</font></a>. I alone am your mother <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">7</font></a>, father,</p>

 <p><a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 194</font></a></p>
 <p>and I too am the son. And I am the self of all this--that which exists and that which does not exist <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">1</font></a>. (I am) the aged grandfather of this, the father, and the son, O descendant of Bharata! You dwell in my self only <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">2</font></a>. You are not mine, nor I (yours). The self only is my seat <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">3</font></a>; the self too is (the source of) my birth <a name="fr_975"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">4</font></a>. I am woven through and through <a name="fr_976"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">5</font></a> (everything). And my seat is free from (the attacks of) old age <a name="fr_977"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">6</font></a>. I am unborn, moving about day and night, without sloth. Knowing (me), verily, a wise man remains placid <a name="fr_978"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Minuter than an atom <a name="fr_979"></a><a href="#fn_974"><font size="1">8</font></a>, possessed of a good mind <a name="fr_980"></a><a href="#fn_975"><font size="1">9</font></a>, I am stationed within all beings <a name="fr_981"></a><a href="#fn_976"><font size="1">10</font></a>. (The wise) know the father of all beings to be placed in the lotus <a name="fr_982"></a><a href="#fn_977"><font size="1">11</font></a>(-like heart of every one).</p>

 <p><a name="fn_917"></a><a href="sbe0826.htm#fr_922">188:6</a> <i>S</i>a<u>n</u>kara says this is in answer to a possible difficulty that action performed here cannot have its fruit in the next world, as the fruit is so far removed in time from the action. The answer is, The Lord, the Supreme, can effect this, and taking his existence into account there is no difficulty. Oblation = food, &amp;c., <i>S</i>a<u>n</u>kara. The meaning of the whole passage, which is not very clear, seems to be that the Lord has arranged things so that each being receives some of this honey, this food, which is the fruit of his own action. Then the question arises, Do these beings always continue taking the honey and 'migrating,' or are they ever released? That is answered by the following sentence.</p>
