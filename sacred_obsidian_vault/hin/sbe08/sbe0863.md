 <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER XXXVI.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">Brahman said:</p>
 <p>Since the mind is ruler of these five elements, in (the matter of) absorbing or bringing (them) forth <a name="fr_2007"></a><a href="#fn_1999"><font size="1">6</font></a>,</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_386"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 386</font></a></p>
 <p>the mind itself is the individual self <a name="fr_2008"></a><a href="#fn_2000"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The mind always presides over the great elements. The understanding proclaims its power <a name="fr_2009"></a><a href="#fn_2001"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and it is called the Kshetra<i>g</i>ña. The mind yokes the senses as a charioteer (yokes) good horses. The senses, the mind, and the understanding are always joined to the Kshetra<i>g</i>ña <a name="fr_2010"></a><a href="#fn_2002"><font size="1">3</font></a>. That individual self, mounting the chariot to which big horses <a name="fr_2011"></a><a href="#fn_2003"><font size="1">4</font></a> are yoked, and in which the understanding is the drag <a name="fr_2012"></a><a href="#fn_2004"><font size="1">5</font></a>, drives about on all sides. the great chariot which is pervaded by the Brahman <a name="fr_2013"></a><a href="#fn_2005"><font size="1">6</font></a>, has the group of the senses yoked (to it), has the mind for a charioteer, and the understanding for a drag. That learned and talented person verily, who always understands thus the chariot pervaded by the Brahman, comes not by delusion in the midst of all entities <a name="fr_2014"></a><a href="#fn_2006"><font size="1">7</font></a>. This forest of the Brahman <a name="fr_2015"></a><a href="#fn_2007"><font size="1">8</font></a> begins with the unperceived, and ends with the gross objects <a name="fr_2016"></a><a href="#fn_2008"><font size="1">9</font></a>;</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_387"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 387</font></a></p>
 <p>and includes movables and immovables, receives light from the radiance of the sun and moon, is adorned with planets and nakshatras, and is decked on all sides with nets of rivers and mountains, and always beautified likewise by various (descriptions of) waters; it is (the means of) subsistence for all entities <a name="fr_2017"></a><a href="#fn_2009"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and it is the goal of all living creatures. In this the Kshetra<i>g</i>ña always moves about. Whatever entities (there are) in this world, movable or immovable, they are the very first <a name="fr_2018"></a><a href="#fn_2010"><font size="1">2</font></a> to be dissolved; and next the developments produced from the elements <a name="fr_2019"></a><a href="#fn_2011"><font size="1">3</font></a>; and (after) these developments, all the elements. Such is the upward gradation <a name="fr_2020"></a><a href="#fn_2012"><font size="1">4</font></a> among entities. Gods, men, Gandharvas, Pi<i>s</i>âkas, Asuras, Râkshasas, all have been created by nature <a name="fr_2021"></a><a href="#fn_2013"><font size="1">5</font></a>, not by actions, nor by a cause. These Brâhma<i>n</i>as <a name="fr_2022"></a><a href="#fn_2014"><font size="1">6</font></a>, the creators of the world, are born here again and again. And whatever</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_388"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 388</font></a></p>
 <p>is produced from them <a name="fr_2023"></a><a href="#fn_2015"><font size="1">1</font></a> is dissolved in due time in those very five great elements, like billows in the ocean. The great elements are in every way (beyond) the elements that make up the world <a name="fr_2024"></a><a href="#fn_2016"><font size="1">2</font></a>. And he who is released, even from those five elements, goes to the highest goal. The Lord Pra<i>g</i>âpati created all this by the mind <a name="fr_2025"></a><a href="#fn_2017"><font size="1">3</font></a> only. And in the same manner <a name="fr_2026"></a><a href="#fn_2018"><font size="1">4</font></a> the sages attained the godhead <a name="fr_2027"></a><a href="#fn_2019"><font size="1">5</font></a> by means of penance <a name="fr_2028"></a><a href="#fn_2020"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And in like manner, those who have achieved perfection, who have acquired concentration by a course of penance, and who likewise feed on fruits and roots, perceive the triple world <a name="fr_2029"></a><a href="#fn_2021"><font size="1">7</font></a> here by penance. Medicines, and herbs, and the various sciences are all acquired <a name="fr_2030"></a><a href="#fn_2022"><font size="1">8</font></a> by means of penance alone. For all acquisition <a name="fr_2031"></a><a href="#fn_2023"><font size="1">9</font></a> has penance for its root. Whatever is difficult to obtain <a name="fr_2032"></a><a href="#fn_2024"><font size="1">10</font></a>, difficult to</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_389"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 389</font></a></p>
 <p>learn, difficult to vanquish, and difficult to pass through; all that can be accomplished by penance, for penance is difficult to overcome. One who drinks spirituous liquors, one who kills a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, one who steals, one who destroys an embryo, one who violates the bed of his preceptor <a name="fr_2033"></a><a href="#fn_2025"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is released from, that sin only by penance well performed. (Those) men, Pitris, gods, (sacrificial) animals <a name="fr_2034"></a><a href="#fn_2026"><font size="1">2</font></a>, beasts and birds, and all other creatures movable or immovable, (who are) constantly devoted to penance, always reach perfection by penance. And in like manner the noble(-minded) gods went to heaven <a name="fr_2035"></a><a href="#fn_2027"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Those who without sloth perform actions with expectations, and being full of egoism, they go near Pra<i>g</i>âpati <a name="fr_2036"></a><a href="#fn_2028"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Those high-souled ones who are devoid of (the thought that this or that is) mine, and devoid of egoism, by means of a pure concentration (of mind) on contemplation, obtain the great and highest world. Those who best understand the self, attaining concentration (of mind) on contemplation <a name="fr_2037"></a><a href="#fn_2029"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and having their minds always tranquil, enter into the unperceived accumulation of happiness <a name="fr_2038"></a><a href="#fn_2030"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Those</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_390"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 390</font></a></p>
 <p>who are free from (all thought that this or that is) mine, and who are free from egoism, attaining concentration (of mind) on contemplation <a name="fr_2039"></a><a href="#fn_2031"><font size="1">1</font></a>, enter the highest world of the great, which is the unperceived. Born from that same unperceived <a name="fr_2040"></a><a href="#fn_2032"><font size="1">2</font></a> (principle), again acquiring knowledge, and getting rid of the (qualities of) passion and darkness, and resorting to the pure (quality of) goodness, a man gets rid of all sins, and abandons everything as fruitless. He should be understood to be the Kshetra<i>g</i>ña. He who understands him understands the Vedas <a name="fr_2041"></a><a href="#fn_2033"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Withdrawing from the mind the objects <a name="fr_2042"></a><a href="#fn_2034"><font size="1">4</font></a> of mental operations, a sage should sit down self-restrained. (He) necessarily (becomes) that on which his mind <a name="fr_2043"></a><a href="#fn_2035"><font size="1">5</font></a> (is fixed). This is the eternal mystery. That which begins with the unperceived and ends with the gross objects <a name="fr_2044"></a><a href="#fn_2036"><font size="1">6</font></a> is stated to be of the nature of ignorance <a name="fr_2045"></a><a href="#fn_2037"><font size="1">7</font></a>. But (you should) learn that whose nature is devoid</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_391"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 391</font></a></p>
 <p>of qualities. Two syllables <a name="fr_2046"></a><a href="#fn_2038"><font size="1">1</font></a> are death; three syllables the eternal Brahman. Mine is death, and not mine is the eternal <a name="fr_2047"></a><a href="#fn_2039"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Some men of dull understandings extol action. But as to the high-souled ancients they do not extol action <a name="fr_2048"></a><a href="#fn_2040"><font size="1">3</font></a>. By action a creature is born with a body and made up of the sixteen <a name="fr_2049"></a><a href="#fn_2041"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Knowledge brings forth <a name="fr_2050"></a><a href="#fn_2042"><font size="1">5</font></a> the being, and that is acceptable and constitutes immortality. Therefore those who are far-sighted have no attachment to actions. This being is stated to be full of knowledge, not full of action <a name="fr_2051"></a><a href="#fn_2043"><font size="1">6</font></a>. The self-restrained man who thus understands the immortal, changeless, incomprehensible, and ever indestructible and unattached (principle), he dies not <a name="fr_2052"></a><a href="#fn_2044"><font size="1">7</font></a>. He who thus understands the self to which there is nothing prior, which is uncreated, changeless, unmoving <a name="fr_2053"></a><a href="#fn_2045"><font size="1">8</font></a>, which is incomprehensible (even) to those who feed on nectar, he certainly becomes immortal <a name="fr_2054"></a><a href="#fn_2044"><font size="1">7</font></a> and not to be restrained <a name="fr_2055"></a><a href="#fn_2046"><font size="1">9</font></a>, in consequence of these means <a name="fr_2056"></a><a href="#fn_2047"><font size="1">10</font></a>.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_392"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 392</font></a></p>
 <p>Expelling all impressions <a name="fr_2057"></a><a href="#fn_2048"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and restraining the self in the Self <a name="fr_2058"></a><a href="#fn_2049"><font size="1">2</font></a>, he understands that holy Brahman, than which nothing greater exists. And when the understanding is clear, he attains tranquillity <a name="fr_2059"></a><a href="#fn_2050"><font size="1">3</font></a>. And the nature of tranquillity is as when one sees a dream <a name="fr_2060"></a><a href="#fn_2051"><font size="1">4</font></a>. This <a name="fr_2061"></a><a href="#fn_2052"><font size="1">5</font></a> is the goal of those emancipated ones who are intent on knowledge. And they see all the movements <a name="fr_2062"></a><a href="#fn_2053"><font size="1">6</font></a> which are produced by development. This is the goal of those who are indifferent (to the world). This is the eternal piety. This is what is acquired by men of knowledge. This is the uncensured (mode of) conduct. This goal can be reached by one who is alike to all beings <a name="fr_2063"></a><a href="#fn_2054"><font size="1">7</font></a>, who is without attachment, who is without expectations,</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_393"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 393</font></a></p>
 <p>and who looks alike on everything <a name="fr_2064"></a><a href="#fn_2055"><font size="1">1</font></a>. I have now declared everything to you, O best of Brâhma<i>n</i>a, sages! Act thus forthwith; then you will acquire perfection.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">The preceptor said:</p>
 <p>Thus instructed by the preceptor Brahman, those high-souled sages acted accordingly, and then attained to the worlds <a name="fr_2065"></a><a href="#fn_2056"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Do you, too, O noble person, of pure self! duly act according to the words of Brahman which I have stated. Then will you attain perfection.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Vâsudeva, said:</p>
 <p>That pupil thus instructed in the highest piety by the preceptor, did everything (accordingly), O son of Kuntî! and then attained final emancipation. And the pupil, having done all he should have done, attained to that seat, O supporter of the family of the Kauravas! going to which one grieves not <a name="fr_2066"></a><a href="#fn_2057"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Ar<i>g</i>una said:</p>
 <p>Who, indeed, was that Brâhma<i>n</i>a, O K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>a! and who the pupil, O <i>G</i>anârdana! If this verily, is fit to be heard by me, O Lord! then tell it me.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Vâsudeva said:</p>
 <p>I <a name="fr_2067"></a><a href="#fn_2058"><font size="1">4</font></a> am the preceptor, O you of mighty arms! and</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_394"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 394</font></a></p>
 <p>know the mind to be my pupil. And, O Dhanañ<i>g</i>aya! I have related this mystery to you out of love for you. If you have love for me, O supporter of the family of the Kauravas! then having heard this (instruction) relating to the self, always duly act <a name="fr_2068"></a><a href="#fn_2059"><font size="1">1</font></a> (according to it). Then when this piety is duly practised, you will attain the absolute final emancipation, getting rid of all sins. It was this same thing I stated to you before <a name="fr_2069"></a><a href="#fn_2060"><font size="1">2</font></a> when the time for battle had come, O you of mighty arms! Therefore fix your mind on this. And now <a name="fr_2070"></a><a href="#fn_2061"><font size="1">3</font></a>, O chief of the descendants of Bharata! it is long since I saw the lord my father. I wish to see him, with your consent, O Phâlguna <a name="fr_2071"></a><a href="#fn_2062"><font size="1">4</font></a>!</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Vai<i>s</i>ampâyana said,</p>
 <p>When K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>a spoke these words, Dhanañgaya replied (saying), 'O K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>a! let us verily go to-day to the city of Ga<i>g</i>asa <a name="fr_2072"></a><a href="#fn_2063"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Be pleased, O you who understand piety! to see there king Yudhish<i>th</i>ira, who is of a devout heart, and after taking leave of him to go to your own city.'</p>
