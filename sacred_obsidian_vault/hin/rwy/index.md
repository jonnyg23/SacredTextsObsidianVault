 <p>
 This is a short guide to modern western Raja Yoga, illustrated
 by a couple of dozen photos of yogic postures (asanas).
 This little book is a surprisingly comprehensive introduction to
 the metaphysical background of yogic practice, along with a host
 of related practical matters.
 </p>
 <p>
 The exercises introduced here are all fairly easy and well-described.
 When this was written, Yoga teachers were far and few between in the US,
 so this was by necessity a self-help guide.
 While, strictly speaking, I wouldn't recommend
 trying these exercises (particularly the mental
 ones) without a teacher, at least it isn't hard to find Yoga classes any more.
 </p>
 <p>
 In the <a href="rwy02.htm#page_7">first chapter (p. 7)</a>,
 Liebers traces stereotypical misconceptions of Yoga
 by mid-century Americans to sideshow
 performers and "supposedly esoteric literature."
 By the latter one infers is meant the 'Yogi Publication Society' books,
 for instance, <cite><a href="../../eso/ryo/index.htm">Raja Yoga</a></cite>, which
 bears comparison with this book.
 </p>
