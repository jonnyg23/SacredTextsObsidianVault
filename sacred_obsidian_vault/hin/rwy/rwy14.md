 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GLOSSARY OF YOGIC TERMS</h3>  
 <p><i>Akasa:</i> infinite, omnipresent material of the universe.</p>  
 <p><i>Anima:</i> power to assimilate oneself with an atom.</p>  
 <p><i>Asana:</i> a posture; a Yoga exercise.</p>  
 <p><i>Ashram:</i> Yoga retreat.</p>  
 <p><i>Avyakta:</i> that which is unperceived, indiscrete.</p>  
 <p><i>Bandha:</i> a mystic breathing exercise.</p>  
 <p><i>Brahmacarya:</i> continence; chastity; abstinence from sexual indulgence.</p>  
 <p><i>Chakra:</i> a nerve center.</p>  
 <p><i>Dharana:</i> mental effort involved in selecting one object out of many for concentration.</p>  
 <p><i>Dhyana:</i> contemplation; gazing.</p>  
 <p><i>Garima:</i> power to be as heavy as anything.</p>  
 <p><i>Guna:</i> a thing of sense.</p>  
 <p><i>Guru:</i> Yoga teacher or leader.</p>  
 <p><i>Hatha Yoga:</i> system of Yoga for care of the body; physical Yoga.</p>  
 <p><i>Ida:</i> left nostril.</p>  
 <p><i>Isvata:</i> power to create.</p>  
 <p><i>Jna:</i> that which knows or discriminates.</p>  
 <p><i>Joytis:</i> light.</p>  
 <p><i>Kaivalya:</i> state of oneness.</p>  
 <p><i>Kanda:</i> point below the navel from which 72,000 <i>nadis</i> issue.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 94</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Karma:</i> cosmic operation of a form of retributive justice, under which a person's status in life is determined by his owns deeds in a previous incarnation.</p>  
 <p><i>Karma-nadi:</i> a nerve in which the vital air is supposed to reside.</p>  
 <p><i>Kumbhaka:</i> retention or suspension of the breath.</p>  
 <p><i>Kundalini:</i> sleeping goddess within the internal organs.</p>  
 <p><i>Laghima:</i> power to be as light as cotton or any similar thing.</p>  
 <p><i>Mahavideha:</i> condition in which knowledge of every description is within easy reach of the ascetic and obtainable without effort.</p>  
 <p><i>Mahima:</i> power to expand oneself into space.</p>  
 <p><i>Mitahara:</i> moderation in diet.</p>  
 <p><i>Moksha:</i> state of bliss.</p>  
 <p><i>Mrnalakomalavapu:</i> slimness of a lotus stalk; one of the goals of Hatha Yoga.</p>  
 <p><i>Mudra:</i> one of the higher practices of Yoga; a psycho-physiological practice or exercise.</p>  
 <p><i>Muladhara:</i> navel.</p>  
 <p><i>Mukti:</i> salvation; deliverance; emancipation from rebirth.</p>  
 <p><i>Nadi:</i> a nerve.</p>  
 <p><i>Niyama:</i> cleanliness, contentment, mortification, study and self-surrender to good.</p>  
 <p><i>Padma:</i> a nerve center.</p>  
 <p><i>Pingala:</i> right nostril; also, anything conducting a positive electrical current in the human body.</p>  
 <p><i>Prakamya:</i> power of having all wishes, of whatever description, realized.</p>  
 <p><i>Prana:</i> life; vitality; breath; infinite, omnipresent power of the universe.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 95</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Pranayama:</i> regulation of the respiratory movements as a means of control of the vital forces.</p>  
 <p><i>Prapti:</i> power of reaching anywhere, even to the moon. Pratibha: intuition.</p>  
 <p><i>Pratyahara:</i> restraining or withholding the senses; introspection.</p>  
 <p><i>Puraka:</i> inhalation.</p>  
 <p><i>Purusa:</i> soul.</p>  
 <p><i>Raja Yoga:</i> system of Yoga which leads to spiritual growth; mental Yoga.</p>  
 <p><i>Rechaka:</i> exhalation.</p>  
 <p><a href="errata.htm#1"><i>Sahasradala</i></a><i>:</i> brain (literally, "the thousand-petaled lotus").</p>  
 <p><i>Samadhi:</i> a trance; state of suspended sensation; superconsciousness; perfect relaxation.</p>  
 <p><i>Samatva:</i> freedom from emotion.</p>  
 <p><i>Samyama:</i> contemplation, absorption, trance; a way to several occult powers which conducts the Yogi to conscious <i>samadhi</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Sankya Yoga:</i> Yoga of the mind.</p>  
 <p><i>Siddha:</i> a master of Yoga.</p>  
 <p><i>Siddhi:</i> one of several miraculous powers.</p>  
 <p><i>Stilhi:</i> state in which the mind is at a standstill.</p>  
 <p><i>Sunyaka:</i> suspension of the breath.</p>  
 <p><i>Susumna:</i> spinal cord.</p>  
 <p><i>Sutras:</i> writings of a sage.</p>  
 <p><i>Vasvita:</i> power to command all.</p>  
 <p><i>Vyakta:</i> that which is perceived, sensible, discrete.</p>  
 <p><i>Yama:</i> non-killing, truthfulness, non-stealing, continence and non-receiving of any gifts.</p>  
 <p><i>Yogin:</i> a practitioner of Yoga.</p>
