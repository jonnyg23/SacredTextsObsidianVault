 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SECOND KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Then <i>G</i>âna<i>s</i>ruti Pautrâya<i>n</i>a took six hundred cows, a necklace, and a carriage with mules, went to Raikva and said:</p>  
 <p>2. 'Raikva, here are six hundred cows, a necklace, and a carriage with mules; teach me the deity which you worship.'</p>  
 <p>3. The other replied: 'Fie, necklace and carriage be thine, O <i>S</i>ûdra, together with the cows.'</p>  
 <p>Then <i>G</i>âna<i>s</i>ruti Pautrâya<i>n</i>a took again a thousand cows, a necklace, a carriage with mules, and his own daughter, and went to him.</p>  
 <p>4. He said to him: 'Raikva, there are a thousand cows, a necklace, a carriage with mules, this wife, and this village in which thou dwellest. Sir, teach me!'</p>  
 <p>5. He, opening her mouth <a name="fr_199"></a><a href="#fn_198"><font size="1">2</font></a>, said: 'You have</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="green">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>brought these (cows and other presents), O <i>S</i>ûdra, but only by that mouth did you make me speak.'</p>  
 <p>These are the Raikva-par<i>n</i>a villages in the country of the Mahâv<i>ri</i>shas (mahâpu<i>n</i>yas) where Raikva dwelt under him <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">1</font></a>. And he said to him:</p>
