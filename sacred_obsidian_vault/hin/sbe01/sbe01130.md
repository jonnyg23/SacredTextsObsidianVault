 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="green">p. 104</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TWELFTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A <a name="fr_293"></a><a href="sbe01131.htm#fn_291"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'Fetch me from thence a fruit of the Nyagrodha tree.'</p>  
 <p>'Here is one, Sir.' Break it.'</p>  
 <p>'It is broken, Sir.'</p>  
 <p>'What do you see there?'</p>  
 <p>'These seeds, almost infinitesimal.'</p>  
 <p>'Break one of them.'</p>  
 <p>'It is broken, Sir.'</p>  
 <p>'What do you see there?'</p>  
 <p>'Not anything, Sir.'</p>  
 <p>2. The father said: 'My son, that subtile essence which you do not perceive there, of that very essence this great Nyagrodha tree exists.</p>  
 <p>3. 'Believe it, my son. That which is the subtile essence, in it all that exists has its self. It is the True. It is the Self, and thou, O <i>S</i>vetaketu, art it.'</p>  
 <p>'Please, Sir, inform me still more,' said the son.</p>  
 <p>'Be it so, my child,' the father replied.</p>
