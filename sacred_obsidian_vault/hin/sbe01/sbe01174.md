 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FOURTEENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. He who is called ether <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">3</font></a> (âkâ<i>s</i>a) is the revealer of all forms and names. That within which these forms and names are contained is the Brahman, the Immortal, the Self.</p>  
 <p>I come to the hall of Pra<i>g</i>âpati, to the house; I am the glorious among Brahmans, glorious among princes, glorious among men <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">4</font></a>. I obtained that glory, I am glorious among the glorious. May I never go to the white, toothless, yet devouring, white abode <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">5</font></a>; may I never go to it.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_361"></a><a href="sbe01174.htm#fr_363">143:3</a> Âkâ<i>s</i>a, ether or space, is a name of Brahman, because, like ether, Brahman has no body and is infinitely small.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_362"></a><a href="sbe01174.htm#fr_364">143:4</a> Here the three classes, commonly called castes, are clearly marked by the names of brâhma<i>n</i>a, râ<i>g</i>an, and vi<i>s</i>.</p>
