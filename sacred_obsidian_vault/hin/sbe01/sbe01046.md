 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">TWELFTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. The hiṅkâra is, he rubs (the fire-stick); the prastâva, smoke rises; the udgîtha, it burns; the pratihâra, there are glowing coals; the nidhana, it goes down; the nidhana, it is gone out. This is the Rathantara Sâman as interwoven in fire <a name="fr_143"></a><a href="#fn_142"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. He who thus knows this Rathantara interwoven in fire, becomes radiant <a name="fr_144"></a><a href="#fn_143"><font size="1">4</font></a> and strong. He reaches the full life, he lives long, becomes great with children and cattle, great by fame. The rule is, 'Do not rinse the mouth or spit before the fire.'</p>
