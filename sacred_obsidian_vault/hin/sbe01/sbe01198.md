 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THIRD KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. He recites the eighty tristichs of Gâyatrîs <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_474"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Verily, the eighty Gâyatrî tristichs are this world (earth). Whatever there is in this world of glory, greatness, wives, food, and honour, may I obtain it, may I win it, may it be mine.</p>  
 <p>2. Next comes the Sûdadohas verse. Sûdadohas verily is breath. He joins this world with breath.</p>  
 <p>3. He recites the eighty tristichs of B<i>ri</i>hatîs. Verily, the eighty B<i>ri</i>hatî tristichs are the world of the sky. Whatever there is in the world of the sky of glory, greatness, wives, food, and honour, may I obtain it, may I win it, may it be mine.</p>  
 <p>4. Next comes the Sûdadohas verse. Sûdadohas verily is breath. He joins the world of the sky with breath.</p>  
 <p>5. He recites the eighty tristichs of Ush<i>n</i>ih. Verily, the eighty Ush<i>n</i>ih tristichs are that world, the heaven. Whatever there is in that world of glory, greatness, wives, food, and honour, also the divine being of the Devas (Brahman), may I obtain it, may I win it, may it be mine.</p>  
 <p>6. Next comes the Sûdadohas verse. Sûdadohas verily is the breath. He joins that world with breath, yea, with breath.</p>
