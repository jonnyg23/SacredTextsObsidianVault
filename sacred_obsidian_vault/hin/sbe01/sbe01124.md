 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SIXTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'That which is the subtile portion of curds, when churned, rises upwards, and becomes butter.</p>  
 <p>2. 'In the same manner, my child, the subtile portion of earth (food), when eaten, rises upwards, and becomes mind.</p>  
 <p>3. 'That which is the subtile portion of water, when drunk, rises upwards, and becomes breath.</p>  
 <p>4. 'That which is the subtile portion of fire, when consumed, rises upwards, and becomes speech.</p>  
 <p>5. 'For mind, my child, comes of earth, breath of water, speech of fire.'</p>  
 <p>'Please, Sir, inform me still more,' said the son.</p>  
 <p>'Be it so, my child,' the father replied.</p>
