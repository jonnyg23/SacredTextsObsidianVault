 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SEVENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the second of these nectars the Rudras live, with Indra at their head. True, the Devas do not eat or drink, but they enjoy by seeing the nectar.</p>  
 <p>2. They enter into that white colour, and they rise from that colour.</p>  
 <p>3. He who thus knows this nectar, becomes one of the Rudras, with Indra at their head, he sees the</p>  
 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="green">p. 42</font></a></p>  
 <p>nectar and rejoices. And he, having entered that colour, rises again from that colour.</p>  
 <p>4. So long as the sun rises in the east and sets in the west, twice as long does it rise in the south and set in the north; and so long does he follow the sovereign supremacy of the Rudras.</p>
