 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FIFTEENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. He said: 'The person that is seen in the eye, that is the Self. This is the immortal, the fearless, this is Brahman <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_217"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Even though they drop melted</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="green">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p>butter or water on him, it runs away on both sides <a name="fr_219"></a><a href="#fn_218"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. 'They call him Sa<i>m</i>yadvâma, for all blessings (vâma) go towards him (sa<i>m</i>yanti). All blessings go towards him who knows this.</p>  
 <p>3. 'He is also Vâmanî, for he leads (nayati) all blessings (vâma). He leads all blessings who knows this.</p>  
 <p>4. 'He is also Bhâmanî, for he shines (bhâti) in all worlds. He who knows this, shines in all worlds.</p>  
 <p>5. 'Now (if one who knows this, dies), whether people perform obsequies for him or no, he goes to light (ar<i>k</i>is) <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_219"><font size="1">2</font></a>, from light to day, from day to the light half of the moon, from the light half of the moon to the six months during which the sun goes to the north, from the months to the year, from the year to the sun, from the sun to the moon, from the moon to the lightning. There is a person not human,</p>  
 <p>6. 'He leads them to Brahman. This is the path of the Devas, the path that leads to Brahman. Those who proceed on that path, do not return to the life of man, yea, they do not return.'</p>
