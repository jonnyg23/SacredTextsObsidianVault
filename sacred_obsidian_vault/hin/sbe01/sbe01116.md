 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">TWENTY-SECOND KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'And he who offers the fourth oblation, should offer it to Samâna (on-breathing), saying Svâhâ. Then Samâna is satisfied,</p>  
 <p>2. 'If Samâna is satisfied, the mind is satisfied, if the mind is satisfied, Par<i>g</i>anya (god of rain) is satisfied, if Par<i>g</i>anya is satisfied, lightning is satisfied, if lightning is satisfied, whatever is under Par<i>g</i>anya and under lightning is satisfied. And through their</p>  
 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="green">p. 91</font></a></p>  
 <p>satisfaction he (the sacrificer or cater) himself is satisfied with offspring, cattle, health, brightness, and Vedic splendour.</p>
