 <p><a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="green">p. 11</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Let a man meditate on the syllable Om, for the udgîtha is sung beginning with Om. And this is the full account of the syllable Om--</p>  
 <p>2. The Devas, being afraid of death, entered upon (the performance of the sacrifice prescribed in) the threefold knowledge (the three Vedas). They covered themselves with the metrical hymns. Because they covered (<i>kh</i>ad) themselves with the hymns, therefore the hymns are called <i>kh</i>andas.</p>  
 <p>3. Then, as a fisherman might observe a fish in the water, Death observed the Devas in the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>, Ya<i>g</i>us, and Sâman-(sacrifices). And the Devas seeing this, rose from the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>, Ya<i>g</i>us, and Sâman-sacrifices, and entered the Svara <a name="fr_91"></a><a href="#fn_90"><font size="1">1</font></a>, i.e. the Om (they meditated on the Om).</p>  
 <p>4. When a man has mastered the Rig-veda, he says quite loud Om; the same, when he has mastered the Sâman and the Ya<i>g</i>us. This Svara is the imperishable (syllable), the immortal, free from fear. Because the Devas entered it, therefore they became immortal, and free from fear.</p>  
 <p>5. He who knowing this loudly pronounces (pra<i>n</i>auti) <a name="fr_92"></a><a href="#fn_91"><font size="1">2</font></a>- that syllable, enters the same (imperishable) syllable, the Svara, the immortal, free from fear, and having entered it, becomes immortal, as the Devas are immortal.</p>
