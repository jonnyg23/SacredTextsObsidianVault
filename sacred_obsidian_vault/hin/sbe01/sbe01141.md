 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SEVENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'Understanding (vi<i>g</i>ñâna) is better than reflection. Through understanding we understand the Rig-veda, the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, the Sâma-veda, and as the fourth the Âtharvana, as the fifth the Itihâsa-purâ<i>n</i>a <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the Veda of the Vedas, the Pitrya, the Râ<i>s</i>i, the Daiva, the Nidhi, the Vâkovâkya, the Ekâyana, the Deva-vidyâ, the Brahma-vidyâ, the Bhûta-vidyâ, the Kshatra-vidyâ, the Nakshatra-vidyâ, the Sarpa and Deva<i>g</i>ana-vidyâ, heaven, earth, air, ether, water, fire, gods, men, cattle, birds, herbs, trees, all beasts down to worms, midges, and ants; what is right and what is wrong; what is true and what is false; what is good and what is bad; what is pleasing and what is not pleasing; food and savour, this world and that, all this we understand through understanding. Meditate on understanding.</p>  
 <p>2. 'He who meditates on understanding as Brahman, reaches the worlds where there is understanding</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="green">p. 116</font></a></p>  
 <p>and knowledge <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">1</font></a>; he is, as it were, lord and master as far as understanding reaches--he who meditates on understanding as Brahman.'</p>  
 <p>'Sir, is there something better than understanding?'</p>  
 <p>'Yes, there is something better than understanding.'</p>  
 <p>'Sir, tell it me.'</p>
