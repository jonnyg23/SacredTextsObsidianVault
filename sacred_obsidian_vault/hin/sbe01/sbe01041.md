 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SEVENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Let a man meditate on the fivefold Sâman, which is greater than great, as the prâ<i>n</i>as (senses). The hiṅkâra is smell <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">1</font></a> (nose), the prastâva speech (tongue), the udgîtha sight (eye), the pratihâra hearing (ear), the nidhana mind. These are one greater than the other.</p>  
 <p>2. What is greater than great belongs to him, nay, he conquers the worlds which are greater than</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="green">p. 26</font></a></p>  
 <p>great, who knowing this meditates on the fivefold Sâman, which is greater than great, as the prâ<i>n</i>as (senses).</p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_131"></a><a href="sbe01041.htm#fr_132">25:1</a> Prâ<i>n</i>a is explained by ghrâ<i>n</i>a, smell; possibly ghrâ<i>n</i>a may have been the original reading. Anyhow, it cannot be the mukhya prâ<i>n</i>a here, because it is distinctly represented as the lowest sense.</p>
