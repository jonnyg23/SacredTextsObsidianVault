 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SIXTEENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Then he said to Bu<i>d</i>ila Â<i>s</i>vatarâ<i>s</i>vi, 'O Vaiyâghrapadya, whom do you meditate on as the Self?' He replied: 'Water only, venerable king.' He said;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_88"><font size="1" color="green">p. 88</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'The Self which you meditate on is the Vai<i>s</i>vânara Self, called Rayi (wealth). Therefore are you wealthy and flourishing.</p>  
 <p>2. 'You eat food and see your desire, and whoever thus meditates on that Vai<i>s</i>vânara Self, eats food and sees his desire, and has Vedic glory in his house.</p>  
 <p>'That, however, is but the bladder of the Self, and your bladder would have burst, if you had not come to me.'</p>
