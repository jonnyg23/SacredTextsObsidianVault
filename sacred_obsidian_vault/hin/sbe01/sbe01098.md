 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A <a name="fr_246"></a><a href="#fn_245"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>1. 'The altar (on which the sacrifice is supposed to be offered) is that world (heaven), O Gautama; its fuel is the sun itself, the smoke his rays, the light the day, the coals the moon, the sparks the stars.</p>  
 <p>2. 'On that altar the Devas (or prâ<i>n</i>as, represented by Agni, &amp;c.) offer the <i>s</i>raddha libation (consisting of water). From that oblation rises Soma, the king <a name="fr_247"></a><a href="#fn_246"><font size="1">2</font></a> (the moon).</p>
