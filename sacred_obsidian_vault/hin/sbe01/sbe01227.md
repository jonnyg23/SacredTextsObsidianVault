 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h2 align="center">SEVENTH ADHYÂYA <a name="fr_616"></a><a href="#fn_614"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h2>  <h3 align="center">FIRST KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. My speech rests in the mind, my mind rests in speech <a name="fr_617"></a><a href="#fn_615"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Appear to me (thou, the Highest Self)! You (speech and mind) are the two pins <a name="fr_618"></a><a href="#fn_616"><font size="1">4</font></a> (that hold the wheels) of the Veda. May what I have learnt not forsake me <a name="fr_619"></a><a href="#fn_617"><font size="1">5</font></a>. I join day and night with what I have learnt <a name="fr_620"></a><a href="#fn_618"><font size="1">6</font></a>. I shall speak of the real, I shall speak the true. May this protect me, may this protect the teacher! May it protect me, may it protect the teacher, yea, the teacher!</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_614"></a><a href="sbe01227.htm#fr_616">246:2</a> This seventh adhyâya contains a propitiatory prayer (<i>s</i>ântikaro mantra<i>h</i>). It is frequently left out in the MSS. which contain the Aitareya-upanishad with <i>S</i>aṅkara's commentary, and Dr. Roer has omitted it in his edition. Sâya<i>n</i>a explains it in his commentary on the Aitareya-âranyaka; and in one MS. of <i>S</i>aṅkara's commentary on the Aitareya-upanishad, which is in my possession, the seventh adhyâya is added with the commentary of Mâdhavâmâtya, the Â<i>g</i>ñâpâlaka of Vîrabukka-mahârâ<i>g</i>a.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_615"></a><a href="sbe01227.htm#fr_617">246:3</a> The two depend on each other.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_616"></a><a href="sbe01227.htm#fr_618">246:4</a> Ant, explained by the commentator as ânayanasamartha.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_617"></a><a href="sbe01227.htm#fr_619">246:5</a> Cf. <i>Kh</i>. Up. IV, 2, 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_618"></a><a href="sbe01227.htm#fr_620">246:6</a> I repeat it day and night so that I may not forget it.</p>
