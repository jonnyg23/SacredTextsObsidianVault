 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXV. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. BEFRIEND us with their aids Indra and Agni, Indra and Varuṇa who receive oblations!<br> Indra and Soma give health, strength and comfort, Indra and Pūṣan be our help in battle.<br> 2 Auspicious Friends to us be Bhaga, Sathsa, auspicious be Purandhi aid all Riches;<br> The blessing of the true and well-conducted, and Aryaman in many forms apparent.<br> 3 Kind unto us he Maker and Sustainer, and the far-reaching Pair with God-like natures.<br> Auspicious unto us be Earth and Heaven, the Mountain, and the Gods fair invocations.<br> 4 Favour us Agni with his face of splendour, and Varuva and Mitra and the Aśvins.<br> Favour us noble actions of the pious, impetuous vita blow on us with favour.<br> 5 Early invoked, may Heaven and Earth be friendly, and Air's mid-region good for us to look on.<br> To us may Herbs and Forest-Trees be gracious, gracious the Lord Victorious of the region.<br> 6 Be the God Indra with the Vasus friendly, and, with Ādityas, Varuṇa who blesseth.<br> Kind, with the Rudras, be the Healer Rudra, and, with the Dames, may Tvaṣṭar kindly listen.<br> 7 Blest unto us be Soma, and devotions, blest be the Sacrifice, the Stones for pressing.<br> Blest be the fixing of the sacred Pillars, blest be the tender Grass and blest the Altar.<br> 8 May the far-seeing Sun rise up to bless us: be the four Quarters of the sky auspicious.<br> Auspicious be the firmly-seated Mountains, auspicious be the Rivers and the Waters.<br> 9 May Adid through holy works be gracioas, and may the Maruts, loud in song, be friendly.<br> May Viṣṇu give felicity, and Pūṣan, the Air that cherisheth our life, and Vāyu.<br> 10 Prosper us Savitar, the God who rescues, and let the radiant Mornings be propitious.<br> Auspicious to all creatures be Parjanya, auspicious be the field's benign Protector.<br> 11 May all the fellowship of Gods befriend us, Sarasvatī, with Holy Thoughts, be gracious.<br> Friendly be they, the Liberal Ones who seek us, yea, those who dwell in heaven, on earth, in waters.<br> 12 May the great Lords of Truth protect and aid us: blest to us be our horses and our cattle.<br> Kind be the pious skilful-handed Ṛbhus, kind be the Fathers at our invocations.<br> 13 May Aja-Ekapād, the God, be gracious, gracious the Dragon of the Deep, and Ocean.<br> Gracious be he the swelling Child of Waters, gracious be Pṛśni who hath Gods to guard her.<br> 14 So may the Rudras, Vasus, and Ādityas accept the new hymn which we now are making.<br> May all the Holy Ones of earth and heaven, and the Cow's offipring hear our invocation.<br> 15 They who of Holy Gods are very holy, Immortal, knowing Law, whom man must worship,<br> May these to-day give us broad paths to travel. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
