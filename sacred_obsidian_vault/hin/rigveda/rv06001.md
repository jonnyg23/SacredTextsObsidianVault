 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU, first inventor of this prayer, O Agni, Worker of Marvels, hast become our Herald.<br> Thou, Bull, hast made us strength which none may conquer, strength that shall overcome all other prowess.<br> 2 As Priest thou sattest at the seat of worship, furthering us, best Offerer, meet for honour.<br> So first to thee have pious men resorted, turning thy mind to thoughts of ample riches.<br> 3 In thee, still watching, they have followed riches, who goest with much wealth as with an army,<br> The radiant Agni, lofty, fair to look on, worshipped with marrow, evermore resplendent.<br> 4 They who approached the God's abode with homage, eager for glory, won them perfect glory:<br> Yea, they gained even sacrificial titles, and found delight in thine auspicious aspect.<br> 5 On earth the people magnify thee greatly, thee their celestial and terrestrial riches.<br> Thou, Helper, must be known as our Preserver, Father and Mother of mankind for ever.<br> 6 Dear priest among mankind, adorable Agni hath seated him, joy-giver, skilled in worship.<br> Let us approach thee shining in thy dwelling, kneeling upon our knees, with adoration.<br> 7 Longing for bliss, pure-minded, God-devoted, Agni, we seek thee, such, meet to be lauded.<br> Thou, Agni, leddest forth our men to battle, refulgent with the heaven's exalted splendour.<br> 8 Sage of mankind, all peoples' Lord and Master, the Bull of men, the sender down of blessings,<br> Still pressing on, promoting, purifying, Agni the Holy One, the Lord of riches.<br> 9 Agni, the mortal who hath toiled and worshipped, brought thee oblations with his kindled fuel,<br> And well knows sacrifice with adoration, gains every joy with thee to guard and help him.<br> 10 Mightily let us worship thee the Mighty, with reverence, Agni! fuel and oblations,<br> With songs, O Son of Strength, with hymns, with altar: so may we strive for thine auspicious favour.<br> 11 Thou who hast covered heaven and earth with splendour and with thy glories, glorious and triumphant.<br> Continue thou to shine on us, O Agni, with strength abundant, rich, and long enduring.<br> 12 Vouchsafe us ever, as man needs, O Vasu, abundant wealth of kine for son and offspring.<br> Food noble, plenteous, far from sin and evil, he with us, and fair fame to make us happy.<br> 13 May I obtain much wealth in many places by love of thee and through thy grace, King Agni;<br> For in thee Bounteous One, in thee the Sovran, Agni, are many boons for him who serves thee.</p>
