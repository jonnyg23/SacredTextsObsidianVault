 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THIS Sage, exalted by our lauds, flows to the purifying cloth,<br> Scattering foes as he is cleansed.<br> 2 As giving power and winning light, for Indra and for Vāyu he<br> Is poured upon the filtering-cloth.<br> 3 The men conduct him, Soma, Steer, Omniscient, and the Head of Heaven,<br> Effused into the vats of wood.<br> 4 Longing for kine, longing for gold hath Indu Pavamana lowed,<br> Still Conqueror, never overcome.<br> 5 This Pavamana, gladdening draught, drops on the filtering cloth, and then<br> Mounts up with Sūrya to the sky.<br> 6 To Indra in the firmament this mighty tawny Steer hath flowed,<br> This Indu, being purified.</p>
