 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVIII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. WIDE, Indra Śatakratu, spreads the bounty of thine ample grace:<br> So, Lord of fair dominion, Friend of all men, give us splendid wealth.<br> 2 The food which, Mightiest Indra, thou possessest worthy of renown<br> Is bruited as most widely famed, invincible, O Golden-hued!<br> 3 O Darter of the Stone, the powers which readily obey thy will,<br> Divinities, both thou and they, ye rule, to guard them, earth and heaven.<br> 4 And from whatever power of thine, O Vṛtra-slayer, it may be,<br> Bring thou to us heroic strength: thou hast a man's regard for us.<br> 5 In thy protection, with these aids of thine, O Lord of Hundred Powers,<br> Indra, may we be guarded well, Hero, may we be guarded well.</p>
