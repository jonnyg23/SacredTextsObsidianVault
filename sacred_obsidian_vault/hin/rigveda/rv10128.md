 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXVIII. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. LET me win glory, Agni, in our battles: enkindling thee, may we support our bodies.<br> May the four regions bend and bow before me: with thee for guardian may we win in combat.<br> 2 May all the Gods be on my side in battle, the Maruts led by Indra, Viṣṇu, Agni.<br> Mine be the middle air's extended region, and may the wind blow favouring these my wishes.<br> 3 May the Gods grant me riches; may the blessing and invocation of the Gods assist me.<br> Foremost in fight be the divine Invokers: may we, unwounded, have brave heroes round us.<br> 4 For me let them present all mine oblations, and let my mind's intention be accomplished.<br> May I he guiltless of the least transgression: and, all ye Gods, do ye combine to bless us.<br> 5 Ye six divine Expanses, grant us freedom: here, all ye Gods, acquit yourselves like heroes.<br> Let us not lose our children or our bodies: let us not benefit the foe, King Soma!<br> 6 Baffling the wrath of our opponents, Agni, guard us as our infallible Protector.<br> Let these thy foes turn back and seek their houses, and let their thought who watch at home be ruined.<br> 7 Lord of the world, Creator of creators the saviour God who overcomes the foeman.<br> May Gods, Bṛhaspati, both Aśvins shelter from ill this sacrifice and sacrificer.<br> 8 Foodful, and much-invoked, at this our calling may the great Bull vouchsafe us wide protection.<br> Lord of Bay Coursers, Indra, blew our children: harm us not, give us not as prey to others.<br> 9 Let those who are our foemen stay. afar from us: with Indra and with Agni we will drive them off.<br> Vasus, Ādityas, Rudras have exalted me, made me far-reaching, mighty, thinker, sovran lord.</p>
