 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCVI. Sarasvatī.</h3>  
 <p>1. I SING a lofty song, for she is mightiest, most divine of Streams.<br> Sarasvatī will I exalt with hymns and lauds, and, O Vasiṣṭha, Heaven and Earth.<br> 2 When in the fulness of their strength the Pūrus dwell, Beauteous One, on thy two grassy banks,<br> Favour us thou who hast the Maruts for thy friends: stir up the bounty of our chiefs.<br> 3 So may Sarasvatī auspicious send good luck; she, rich in spoil, is never niggardly in thought,<br> When praised in jamadagni's way and lauded as Vasiṣṭha lauds.<br> 4 We call upon Sarasvān, as unmarried men who long for wives,<br> As liberal men who yearn for sons.<br> 5 Be thou our kind protector, O Sarasvān, with those waves of thine<br> Laden with sweets and dropping oil.<br> 6 May we enjoy Sarasvān's breast, all-beautiful, that swells with streams,<br> May we gain food and progeny.</p>
