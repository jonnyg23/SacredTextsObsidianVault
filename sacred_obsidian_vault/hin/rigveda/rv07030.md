 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXX. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. WITH power and strength, O Mighty God, approach us: be the augmenter, Indra, of these riches;<br> Strong Thunderer, Lord of men, for potent valour, for manly exploit and for high dominion.<br> 2 Thee, worth invoking, in the din of battle, heroes invoke in fray for life and sunlight.<br> Among all people thou art foremost fighter: give up our enemies to easy slaughter.<br> 3 When fair bright days shall dawn on us, O Indra, and thou shalt bring thy banner near in battle,<br> Agni the Asura shall sit as Herald, calling Gods hither for our great good fortune.<br> 4 Thine are we, Indra, thine, both these who praise thee, and those who give rich gifts, O God and Hero.<br> Grant to our princes excellent protection, may they wax old and still be strong and happy.<br> 5 We will address this liberal Lord, this Indra that he may grant us gifts of ample riches:<br> Best favourer of the singer's prayer and praises. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
