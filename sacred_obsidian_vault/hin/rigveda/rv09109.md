 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CIX. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. PLEASANT to Indra's Mitra's, Pūṣan's Bhaga's taste, sped onward, Soma, with thy flowing stream.<br> 2 Let Indra drink, O Soma, of thy juice for wisdom, and all Deities for strength.<br> 3 So flow thou on as bright celestial juice, flow to the vast, immortal dwelling-place.<br> 4 Flow onward, Soma, as a mighty sea, as Father of the Gods to every form.<br> 5 Flow on, O Soma, radiant for the Gods and Heaven and Earth and bless our progeny.<br> 6 Thou, bright Juice, art Sustainer of the sky: flow, mighty, in accordance with true Law.<br> 7 Soma, flow splendid with thy copious stream through the great fleece as in the olden time.<br> 8 Bom, led by men, joyous, and purified, let the Light-finder make all blessings flow:<br> 9 Indu, while cleansed, keeping the people safe, shall give us all possessions for our own.<br> 10 Flow on for wisdom, Soma, and for power, as a strong courser bathed, to win the prize.<br> 11 The pressers purify this juice of thine, the Soma, for delight, and lofty fame<br> 12 They deck the Gold-hued Infant, newlyborn, even Soma, Indu, in the sieve for Gods.<br> 13 Fair Indu hath flowed on for rapturous joy, Sage for good fortune in the waters' lap.<br> 14 He bears the beauteous name of Indra, that wherewith he overcame all demon foes.<br> 15 All Deities are wont to drink of him, pressed by the men and blent with milk and curds.<br> 16 He hath flowed forth with thousand streams effused, flowed ihsough the filter and the sheep's long wool.<br> 17 With endless genial flow the Strong hath run, purified by the waters, blent with milk.<br> 18 Pressed out with stones, directed by the men, go fortli, O Soma, into Indra's throat.<br> 19 The mighty Soma with a thousand streams is poured to Indra through the cleansing sieve.<br> 20 Indu they balm with pleasant milky juice for Indra, for the Steer, for his delight.<br> 21 Lightly, for sheen, they cleanse thee for the Gods, gold-coloured, wearing water as thy robe.<br> 22 Indu to Indra streams, yea, downward streams, Strong, flowing to the floods, and mingling -there.</p>
