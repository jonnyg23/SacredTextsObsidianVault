 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVII. Ksetrapati, Etc.</h3>  
 <p>1. WE through the Master of the Field, even as through a friend, obtain<br> What nourisheth our kine and steeds. In such may he be good to us.<br> 2 As the cow yieldeth milk, pour for us freely, Lord of the Field, the wave that beareth sweetness,<br> Distilling meath, well-purified like butter, and let the. Lords of holy Law be gracious.<br> 3 Sweet be the plants for us. the heavens, the waters, and full of sweets for us be air's mid-region.<br> May the Field's Lord for us be full of sweetness, and may we follow after him uninjured.<br> 4 Happily work our steers and men, may the plough furrow happily.<br> Happily be the traces bound; happily may he ply the goad.<br> 5 Śuna and Sīra, welcome ye this laud, and with the milk which ye have made in heaven<br> Bedew ye both this earth of ours.<br> 6 Auspicious Sītā, come thou near: we venerate and worship thee<br> That thou mayst bless and prosper us and bring us fruits abundantly.<br> 7 May Indra press the furrow down, may Pūṣan guide its course aright.<br> May she, as rich in milk, be drained for us through each succeeding year.<br> 8 Happily let the shares turn up the plough-land, happily go the ploughers with the oxen.<br> With meath and milk Parjanya make us happy. Grant us prosperity, Śuna and Sīra.</p>
