 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CIII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. SWIFT, rapidly striking, like a bull who sharpens his horns, terrific, stirring up the people,<br> With eyes that close not, bellowing, Sole Hero, Indra. subdued at once a hundred armies.<br> 2 With him loud-roaring, ever watchful, Victor, bold, hard to overthrow, Rouser of battle,<br> Indra. the Strong, whose hand bears arrows, conquer, ye warriors, now, now vanquish in the combat.<br> 3 He rules with those who carry shafts and quivers, Indra who with his band rings hosts together,<br> Foe-conquering, strong of arm, the Soma-drinker, with mighty bow, shooting with well-laid arrows.<br> 4 Bṛhaspati, fly with thy chariot hither, slayer of demons, driving off our foemen.<br> Be thou protector of our cars, destroyer, victor in battle, breaker-up of armies.<br> 5 Conspicuous by thy strength, firm, foremost fighter, mighty and fierce, victorious, all-subduing,<br> The Son of Conquest, passing men and heroes, kine-winner, mount thy conquering car, O Indra.<br> 6 Cleaver of stalls, kine-winner, armed with thunder, who quells an army and with might destroys it.-<br> Follow him, brothers! quit yourselves like heroes, and like this Indra show your zeal and courage.<br> 7 Piercing the cow-stalls with surpassing vigour, Indra, the pitiless Hero, wild with anger,<br> Victor in fight, unshaken and resistless,may he protect our armies in our battles.<br> 8 Indra guide these: Bṛhaspati precede them, the guerdon, and the sacrifice, and Soma;<br> And let the banded Maruts march in forefront of heavenly hosts that conquer and demolish.<br> 9 Ours be the potent host of mighty Indra, King Varuṇa, and Maruts, and Ādityas.<br> Uplifted is the shout of Gods who conquer high-minded Gods who cause the worlds to tremble.<br> 10 Bristle thou up, O Maghavan, our weapons: excite the spirits of my warring heroes.<br> Urge on the strong steeds' might, O Vṛtra-slayer, and let the din of conquering cars go upward.<br> 11 May Indra aid us when our flags are gathered: victorious be the arrows of our army.<br> May our brave men of war prevail in battle. Ye Gods, protect us in the shout of onset.<br> 12 Bewildering the senses of our foemen, seize thou their bodies and depart, O Apva.<br> Attack them, set their hearts on fire and burn them: so let our foes abide in utter darkness.<br> 13 Advance, O heroes, win the day. May Indra be your sure defence.<br> Exceeding mighty be your arms, that none may wound or injure you.</p>
