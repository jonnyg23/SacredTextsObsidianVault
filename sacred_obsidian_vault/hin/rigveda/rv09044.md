 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIV. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. INDU, to us for this great rite, bearing as twere thy wave to Gods,<br> Unwearied, thou art flowing forQh.<br> 2 Pleased with the hymn, impelled by prayer, Soma is hurried far away,<br> The Wise One in the Singer's stream.,<br> 3 Watchful among the. gods, this juice advances to the cleansing sieve<br> Soma, most active, travels on.<br> 4 Flow onward, seeking strength for us, embellishing the sacrifice:<br> The priest with trimmed grass calleth thee.<br> 5 May Soma, ever bringing power to Bhaga and to Vāyu, Sage<br> And Hero, lead us to the Gods.<br> 6 So, to increase our wealth to-day, Inspirer, best of Furtherers,<br> Win for us strength and high renown.</p>
