 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIX. Indra</h3>  
 <p>1. O Sarakratu! truely I have made none else my Comforter.<br> Indra; be gracious unto us.<br> 2 Thou who hast ever aided us kindly of old to win the spoil,<br> As such, O Indra, favour us.<br> 3 What now? As prompter of the poor thou helpest him who sheds the juice.<br> Wilt thou not, Indra, strengthen us?<br> 4 O Indra, help our chariot on, yea, Thunderer, though it lag behind:<br> Give this my car the foremost place.<br> 5 Ho there! why sittest thou at case? Make thou my chariot to be first<br> And bring the fame of victory near.<br> 6 Assist our car that seeks the prize. What can be easier for thee?<br> So make thou us victorious.<br> 7 Indra, be firm: a fort art thou. To thine appointed place proceeds<br> The auspicious hymn in season due.<br> 8 Let not our portion be disgrace. Broad is the course, the prize is set,<br> The barriers are opened wide.<br> 9 This thing we wish. that thou mayst take thy fourth, thy sacrificial name.<br> So art thou held to be our Lord.<br> 10 Ekadyu hath exalted you, Immortals: both Goddesses and Gods hath he delighted.<br> Bestow upon him bounty meet for praises. May he, enriched with prayer, come soon and early.</p>
