 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CV. Soma Pavamana</h3>  
 <p>1. SING; ye aloud, O friends, to him who makes him pure for gladdening drink:<br> They shall make sweet the Child with sacrifice and laud.<br> 2 Like as a calf with mother cows, so Indu is urged forth and sent,<br> Glorified by our hymns, the God-delighting juice.<br> 3 Effectual means of power is he, he is a banquet for the Troop,<br> He who hath been effused, most rich in meath, for Gods.<br> 4 Flow to us, Indu, passing, strong, effused, with wealth of kine and steeds:<br> I will spread forth above the milk thy radiant hue.<br> 5 Lord of the tawny, Indu thou who art the God's most special food,<br> As Friend to friend, for splendour be thou good to men.<br> 6 Drive utterly, far away from us each godless, each voracious foe.<br> O Indu, overcome and drive the false afar.</p>
