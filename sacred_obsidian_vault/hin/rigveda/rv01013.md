 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIII. Agni</h3>  
 <p>1 AGNI, well-kindled, bring the Gods for him who offers holy gifts.<br> Worship them, Purifier, Priest.<br> 2 Son of Thyself, present, O Sage, our sacrifice to the Gods today.<br> Sweet to the taste, that they may feast.<br> 3 Dear Narāśaṁsa, sweet of tongue, the giver of oblations, I<br> Invoke to this our sacrifice.<br> 4 Agni, on thy most easy car, glorified, hither bring the Gods:<br> Manu appointed thee as Priest.<br> 5 Strew, O ye wise, the sacred grass that drips with oil, in order due,<br> Where the Immortal is beheld.<br> 6 Thrown open be the Doors Divine, unfailing, that assist the rite,<br> For sacrifice this day and now.<br> 7 I call the lovely Night and Dawn to seat them on the holy grass<br> At this our solemn sacrifice.<br> 8 The two Invokers I invite, the wise, divine and sweet of tongue,<br> To celebrate this our sacrifice.<br> 9 Iḷā, Sarasvatī, Mahī, three Goddesses who bring delight,<br> Be seated, peaceful, on the grass.<br> 10 Tvaṣṭar I call, the earliest born, the wearer of all forms at will:<br> May he be ours and ours alone.<br> 11 God, Sovran of the Wood, present this our oblation to the Gods,<br> And let the giver be renowned.<br> 12 With Svāhā pay the sacrifice to Indra in the offerer's house:<br> Thither I call the Deities.</p>
