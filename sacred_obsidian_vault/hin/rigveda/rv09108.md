 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CVIII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. FOR Indra, flow thou Soma on, as gladdening juice most sweet, intelligent,<br> Great, cheering, dwelling most in heaven.<br> 2 Thou, of whom having drunk the Steer acts like a steer. drinking of this that finds the light,<br> He, Excellently Wise, is come to strengthening food, to spoil and wealth like Etaśa.<br> 3 For, verily, Pavamana, thou hast, splendidest, called all the generations of<br> The Gods to immortality.<br> 4 By whom Dadhyac Navagva opens fastened doors, by whom the sages gained their wish,<br> By whom they won the fame of lovely Amṛta in the felicity of Gods.<br> 5 Effused, he floweth in a stream, best rapture-giver, in the long wool of the sheep,<br> Sporting, as twere the waters' wave.<br> 6 He who from out the rocky cavern took with might the redmrefulgent watery Cows,<br> Thou masterest the stable full of kine and steeds: burst it, brave Lord, like one in mail.<br> 7 Press ye and pour him, like a steed, laudworthy, speeding through the region and the flood,<br> Who swims in water, roan in wood;<br> 8 Increaser of the water, Steer with thousand streams, dear to the race of Deities;<br> Who born in Law hath waxen mighty by the Law, King, God, and lofty Ordinance.<br> 9 Make splendid glory shine on us, thou Lord of strengthening food, God, as the Friend of Gods:<br> Unclose the fount of middle air.<br> 10 Roll onward to the bowls, O Mighty One, effused, as Prince supporter of the tribes.<br> Pour on us rain from heaven, send us the waters' flow: incite our thoughts to win the spoil.<br> 11 They have drained him the Steer of heaven, him with a thousand streams, distilling rapturous joy,<br> Him who brings all things excellent.<br> 12 The Mighty One was born Immortal, giving life, lightening darkness with his shine.<br> Wcll-praised by. sages he hath. by his wondrous power assumed the Threefold as his robe.<br> 13 Effused is he who brings good things, who brings us bounteous gifts and sweet refreshing food,<br> Soma who brings us quiet homes:<br> 14 He whom our Indra and the Marut host shall drink, Bhaga shall drink with Aryarnan,<br> By whom we bring to us Mitra and Varuṇa and Indra for our great defence.<br> 15 Soma, for Indra's drink do thou, led by the men, well-wcaponcd and most gladdening,<br> Flow on with greatest store of sweets.<br> 16 Enter the Soma-holder, even Indra's heart, as rivers pass into the sea,<br> Acceptable to Mitra, Vāyu, Varuṇa, the noblest Pillar of the heavens.</p>
