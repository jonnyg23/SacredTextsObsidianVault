 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. SWIFT Soma drops have been effused in streams of meath, the gladdening drink,<br> For sacred lore of every kind.<br> 2 Hither to newer. resting-place the ancient Living Ones are come.<br> They made the Sun that he might shine.<br> 3 O Pavamana, bring to us the unsacrificing foeman's wealth,<br> And give us food with progeny.<br> 4 The living Somas being cleansed diffuse exhilarating drink,<br> Turned to the vat which drips with meath.<br> 5 Soma gows on intelligent, possessing sap and mighty strength,<br> Brave Hero who repels the curse.<br> 6 For Indra, Soma! thou art cleansed, a feast-companion for the Gods:<br> 1ndu, thou fain wilt win us strength<br> 7 When he had drunken draughts of this, Indra smote down resistless foes:<br> Yea, smote them, and shall smite them still.</p>
