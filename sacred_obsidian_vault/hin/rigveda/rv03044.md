 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. May this delightsome Soma be expressed for thee by tawny stones.<br> Joying thereat, O Indra, with thy Bay Steeds come:. ascend thy golden-coloured car.<br> 2 In love thou madest Uṣas glow, in love thou madest Sūrya shine.<br> Thou, Indra, knowing, thinking, Lord of Tawny Steeds, above all glories waxest great.<br> 3 The heaven with streams of golden hue, earth with her tints of green and gold-<br> The golden Pair yield Indra plenteous nourishment: between them moves the golden One.<br> 4 When born to life the golden Bull illumines all the realm of light.<br> He takes his golden weapon, Lord of Tawny Steeds, the golden thunder in his arms.<br> 5 The bright, the well-loved thunderbolt, girt with the bright, Indra disclosed,<br> Disclosed the Soma juice pressed out by tawny stones, with tawny steeds drave forth the kine.</p>
