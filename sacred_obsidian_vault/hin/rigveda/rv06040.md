 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XL. Indra</h3>  
 <p>1. DRINK, Indra; juice is shed to make thee joyful: loose thy Bay Steeds and give thy friends their freedom.<br> Begin the song, seated in our assembly. Give strength for sacrifice to him who singeth.<br> 2 Drink thou of this whereof at birth, O Indra, thou drankest, Mighty One for power and rapture.<br> The men, the pressing-stones, the cows, the waters have made this Soma ready for thy drinking.<br> 3 The fire is kindled, Soma pressed, O Indra: let thy Bays, best to draw, convey thee hither.<br> With mind devoted, Indra, I invoke thee. Come, for our great prosperity approach us.<br> 4 Indra, come hither: evermore thou camest through our great strong desire to drink the Soma.<br> Listen and hear the prayers which now we offer, and let this sacrifice increase thy vigour.<br> 5 Mayst thou, O Indra, on the day of trial, present or absent, wheresoe’er thou dwellest,<br> Thence, with thy team, accordant with the Maruts, Song-lover! guard our sacrifice, to help us.</p>
