 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. WE stablish thee as Manus used, as Manus used we kindle thee.<br> Like Manus, for the pious man , Aṅgiras, Agni, worship Gods.<br> 2 For well, O Agni, art thou pleased when thou art kindled mid mankind.<br> Straight go the ladles unto thee, thou highborn God whose food is oil.<br> 3 Thee have all Gods of one accord established as their messenger.<br> Serving at sacrifices men adore thee as a God, O Sage.<br> 4 Let mortal man adore your God, Agni, with worship due to Gods.<br> Shine forth enkindled, Radiant One. Sit in the chamber of the Law, sit in the chamber of the food.</p>
