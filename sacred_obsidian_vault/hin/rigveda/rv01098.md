 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCVIII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. STILL in Vaiśvānara's grace may we continue: yea, he is King supreme oer all things living.<br> Sprung hence to life upon this All he looketh. Vaiśvānara hath rivalry with Sūrya.<br> 2 Present in heaven, in earth, all-present Agni,all plants that grow on ground hath he pervaded.<br> May Agni, may Vaiśvānara with vigour, present, preserve us day and night from foemen.<br> 3 Be this thy truth, Vaiśvānara, to us-ward: let wealth in rich abundance gather round us.<br> This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.</p>
