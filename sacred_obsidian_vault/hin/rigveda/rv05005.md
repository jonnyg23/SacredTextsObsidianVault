 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V. Āprīs.</h3>  
 <p>1. To Agni, Jātavedas, to the flame, the well-enkindled God,<br> Offer thick sacrificial oil.<br> 2 He, Narāśaṁsa, neer beguiled, inspiriteth this sacrifice:<br> For sage is he, with sweets in hand.<br> 3 Adored, O Agni, hither bring Indra the Wonderful, the Friend,<br> On lightly-rolling car to aid.<br> 4 Spread thyself out, thou soft as wool The holy hymns have sung to thee.<br> Bring gain to us, O beautiful!<br> 5 Open yourselves, ye Doors Divine, easy of access for our aid:<br> Fill, more and more, the sacrifice.<br> 6 Fair strengtheners of vital power, young Mothers of eternal Law,<br> Morning and Night we supplicate.<br> 7 On the wind's flight come, glorified, ye two celestial Priests of man<br> Come ye to this our sacrifice.<br> 8 Iḷā, Sarasvatī, Mahī, three Goddesses who bring us weal,<br> Be seated harmless on the grass.<br> 9 Rich in all plenty, Tvaṣṭar, come auspicious of thine own accord<br> Help us in every sacrifice.<br> 10 Vanaspati, wherever thou knowest the Gods mysterious names,<br> Send our oblations thitherward.<br> 11 To Agni and to Varuṇa, Indra, the Maruts, and the Gods,<br> With Svāhā be oblation brought.</p>
