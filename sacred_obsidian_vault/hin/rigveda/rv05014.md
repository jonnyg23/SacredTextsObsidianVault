 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIV. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. ENKINDLING the Immortal, wake Agni with song of praise: may he bear our oblations to the Gods.<br> 2 At high solemnities mortal men glorify him the Immortal, best<br> At sacrifice among mankind.<br> 3 That he may bear their gifts to heaven, all glorify him Agni, God,<br> With ladle that distilleth oil.<br> 4 Agni shone bright when born, with light killing the Dasyus and the dark:<br> He found the Kine, the Floods, the Sun.<br> 5 Serve Agni, God adorable, the Sage whose back is balmed with oil:<br> Let him approach, and hear my call.<br> 6 They have exalted Agni, God of all mankind, with oil and hymns<br> Of praise, devout and eloquent.</p>
