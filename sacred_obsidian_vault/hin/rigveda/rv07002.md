 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II. Āprīs.</h3>  
 <p>1. GLADLY accept, this day, our fuel, Agni: send up thy sacred smoke and shine sublimely.<br> Touch the celestial summits with thy columns, and overspread thee with the rays of Sūrya.<br> 2 With sacrifice to these we men will honour the majesty of holy Narāśaṁsa-<br> To these the pure, most wise, the thought. inspirers, Gods who enjoy both sorts of our oblations.<br> 3 We will extol at sacrifice for ever, as men may do, Agni whom Manu kindled,<br> Your very skilful Asura, meet for worship, envoy between both worlds, the truthful speaker.<br> 4 Bearing the sacred grass, the men who serve him strew it with reverence, on their knees, by Agni.<br> Calling him to the spotted grass, oil-sprinkled, adorn him, ye Adhvaryus, with oblation.<br> 5 With holy thoughts the pious have thrown open Doors fain for chariots in the Gods assembly.<br> Like two full mother cows who lick their youngling, like maidens for the gathering, they adorn them.<br> 6 And let the two exalted Heavenly Ladies, Morning and Night, like a cow good at milking,<br> Come, much-invoked, and on our grass be seated ' wealthy, deserving worship, for our welfare.<br> 7 You, Bards and Singers at men's sacrifices, both filled with wisdom, I incline to worship.<br> Send up our offerings when we call upon you, and so among the Gods obtain us treasures.<br> 8 May Bhāratī with all her Sisters, Iḷā accordant with the Gods, with mortals Agni,<br> Sarasvatī with all her kindred Rivers, come to this grass, Three Goddesses, and seat them.<br> 9 Well pleased with us do thou, O God, O Tvaṣṭar, give ready issue to our procreant vigour,<br> Whence springs the hero, powerful, skilled in action, lover of Gods, adjuster of the press-stones.<br> 10 Send to the Gods the oblation, Lord of Forests, and let the Immolator, Agni, dress it.<br> He as the truer Priest shall offer worship, for the God'sgenerations well he knoweth.<br> 11 Come thou to us, O Agni, duly kindled, together with the potent Gods and Indra.<br> On this our grass sit Aditi, happy Mother, and let our Hail! delight the Gods Immortal.</p>
