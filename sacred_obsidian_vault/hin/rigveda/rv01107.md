 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CVII. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE sacrifice obtains the Gods acceptance: be graciously inclined to us, Ādityas.<br> Hitherward let your favour be directed, and be our best deliverer from trouble.<br> 2 By praise-songs of Aṅgirases exalted, may the Gods come to us with their protection.<br> May Indra with his powers, Maruts with Maruts, Aditi with Ādityas grant us shelter.<br> 3 This laud of ours may Varuṇa and Indra, Aryaman Agni, Savitar find pleasant.<br> This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.</p>
