 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXVI. Aśvins.</h3>  
 <p>1. SPLENDID, O Aśvins, is your praise. Come fountain-like, to pour the stream.<br> Of the sweet juice effused-dear is it, Chiefs, in heaven-drink like two wild bulls at a pool.<br> 2 Drink the libation rich in sweets, O Aśvins Twain: sit. Heroes, on the sacred grass.<br> Do ye with joyful heart in the abode of man preserve his life by means of wealth.<br> 3 The Priyamedhas bid you come with all the succours that are yours.<br> Come to his house whose holy grass is trimmed, to dear sacrifice at the morning rites.<br> 4 Drink ye the Soma rich in meath, ye Aśvins Twain: sit gladly on the sacred grass.<br> So, waxen mighty, to our eulogy from heaven come ye as wild-bulls to the pool.<br> 5 Come to us, O ye Aśvins, now with steeds of many a varied hue,<br> Ye Lords of splendour, wondrous, borne on paths of gold, drink Soma, ye who strengthen Law.<br> 6 For we the priestly singers, fain tohymn your praise, invoke you for the gain of strength.<br> So, wondrous, fair, and famed for great deeds come to us, through our hymn, Aśvins, when ye hear.</p>
