 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN Xl. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. AGNI is Priest, the great High Priest of sacrifice, most swift in act:<br> He knows the rite in constant course.<br> 2 Oblation-bearer, deathless, well inclined, an eager messenger,<br> Agni comes nigh us with the thought.<br> 3 Ensign of sacrifice from of old, Agni well knoweth with his thought<br> To prosper this man's aim and hope.<br> 4 Agni, illustrious from old time, the Son of Strength who knows all life,<br> The Gods have made to their Priest.<br> 5 Infallible is Agni, he who goes before the tribes of men,<br> A chariot swift and ever new.<br> 6 Strength of the Gods which none may harm, subduing all his enemies,<br> Agni is mightiest in fame.<br> 7 By offering sacred food to him the mortal worshipper obtains.<br> A home from him whose light makes pure.<br> 8 From Agni, by our hymns, may we gain all things that bring happiness,<br> Singers of him who knows all life.<br> 9 O Agni, in our deeds of might may we obtain all precious things:<br> Tle Gods are centred all in thee.</p>
