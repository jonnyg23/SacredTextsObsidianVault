 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXI Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. SOLE Lord of wealth art thou, O Lord of riches: thou in thine hands hast held the people, Indra!<br> Men have invoked thee with contending voices for seed and waters, progeny and sunlight.<br> 2 Through fear of thee, O Indra, all the regions of earth, though naught may move them, shake and tremble.<br> All that is firm is frightened at thy coming, -the earth, the heaven, the mountain, and the forest.<br> 3 With Kutsa, Indra! thou didst conquer Śuṣṇa, voracious, bane of crops, in fight for cattle.<br> In the close fray thou rentest him: thou stolest the Sun's wheel and didst drive away misfortunes.<br> 4 Thou smotest to the ground the hundred castles, impregnable, of Śambara the Dasyu,<br> When, Strong, with might thou holpest Divodāsa who poured libations out, O Soma-buyer, and madest Bharadvāja rich who praised thee.<br> 5 As such, true Hero, for great joy of battle mount thy terrific car, O Brave and Manly.<br> Come with thine help to me, thou distant Roamer, and, glorious God, spread among men my glory.</p>
