 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIV. Mitra-Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. YE Twain who rule, in heaven and earth, the region, clothed be your clouds in robes of oil and fatness.<br> May the imperial Varuṇa, and Mitra, and high-born Aryaman accept our presents.<br> 2 Kings, guards of rrtighty everlasting Order, come hitherward, ye Princes, Lords of Rivers.<br> Send us from heaven, O Varuṇa and Mitra, rain and sweet food, ye who pour down your bounties.<br> 3 May the dear God, and Varuṇa and Mitra conduct us by the most effective pathways,<br> That foes may say unto Sudās our chieftain, May, we, too, joy in food with Gods to guard us.<br> 4 Him who hath wrought for you this car in spirit, who makes the song rise upward and sustains it,<br> Bedew with fatness, Varuṇa nd Mitra ye Kings, make glad the pleasant dwelling-places.<br> 5 To you this laud, O Varuṇa and Mitra is offered like bright Soma juice to Vāyu.<br> Favour our songs of praise, wake thought and spirit. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
