 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXII. Dawn.</h3>  
 <p>1. WITH all thy beauty come: the kine approaching with full udders follow on thy path.<br> 2 Come with kind thoughts, most liberal, rousing the warrior's hymn of praise, with bounteous ones,<br> 3 As nourishers we tie the thread, and, liberal with our bounty, offer sacrifice.<br> 4 Dawn drives away her Sister's gloom, and, through her excellence, makes her retrace her path.</p>
