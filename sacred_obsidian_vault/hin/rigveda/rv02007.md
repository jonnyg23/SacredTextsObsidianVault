 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. VASU, thou most youthful God, Bhārata, Agni, bring us wealth,<br> Excellent, splendid, much-desired.<br> 2 Let no malignity prevail against us, either God's or man's.<br> Save us from this and enmity.<br> 3 So through thy favour may we force through all our enemies a way,<br> As twere through streaming water-floods.<br> 4 Thou, Purifier Agni, high shinest forth, bright, adorable,<br> When worshipped with the sacred oil.<br> 5 Ours art thou, Agni, Bhārata, honoured by us with barren cows,<br> With bullocks and with kine in calf<br> 6 Wood-fed, bedewed with sacred oil, ancient, Invoker, excellent,<br> The Son of Strength, the Wonderful.</p>
