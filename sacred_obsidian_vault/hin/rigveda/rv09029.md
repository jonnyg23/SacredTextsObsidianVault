 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIX. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. FORWARD with mighty force have flowed the currents of this Steer effused,<br> Of him who sets him by the Gods.<br> 2 The singers praise him with their song, and learned priests adorn the Steed,<br> Brought forth as light that merits laud.<br> 3 These things thou winnest lightly while purified, Soma, Lord of wealth:<br> Fill full the sea that claims our praise.<br> 4 Winning all precious things at once, flow on, O Soma, with thy stream<br> Drive to one place our enemies.<br> 5 Preserve us from the godless, from ill-omened voice of one and all,<br> That so we may be freed from blame.<br> 6 O Indu, as thou flowest on bring us the wealth of earth and heaven,<br> And splendid vigour, in thy stream.</p>
