 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXVIII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. To Indra sing the lofty hymn, Maruts that slays the Vṛtras best.<br> Whereby the Holy Ones created for the God the light divine that ever wakes.<br> 2 Indra who quells the curse blew curses far away, and then in splendour came to us.<br> Indra, refulgent with thy Marut host! the Gods strove eagerly to win thy love.<br> 3 Sing to your lofty Indra, sing, Maruts, a holy hymn of praise.<br> Let Śatakratu, Vṛtra-slayer, kill the foe with hundred-knotted thunderbolt.<br> 4 Aim and fetch boldly forth, O thou whose heart is bold: great glory will be thine thereby.<br> In rapid torrent let the mother waters spread. Slay Vṛtra, win the light of heaven.<br> 5 When thou, unequalled Maghavan, wast born to smite the Vṛtras dead,<br> Thou spreadest out the spacious earth and didst support and prop the heavens.<br> 6 Theri was the sacrifice produced for thee, the laud, and song of joy,<br> Thou in thy might surpassest all, all that now is and yet shall be.<br> 7 Raw kine thou filledst with ripe milk. Thou madest Sūrya rise to heaven.,<br> Heat him as milk is heated with pure Sāma hymns, great joy to him who loves the song.</p>
