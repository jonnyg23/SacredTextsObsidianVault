 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THY streams that never fail or waste flow forth like showers of rain from heaven,<br> To bring a thousand stores of strength.<br> 2 He flows beholding on his way all wellbeloved sacred lore,<br> Green-tinted, brandishing his, arms.<br> 3 He, when the people deck him like a docile king of elephants.<br> Sits as a falcon in the, wood.<br> 4 So bring thou hitherward to us, Indu, while thou art purified,<br> All treasures both of heaven and earth.</p>
