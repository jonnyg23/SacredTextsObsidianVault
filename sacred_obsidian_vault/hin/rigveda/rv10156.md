 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLVI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. LET songs of ours speed Agni forth like a fleet courser in the race,<br> And we will win each prize through him.<br> 2 Agni the dart whereby we gain kine for ourselves with help from thee,<br> That send us for the gain of wealth.<br> 3 O Agni, bring us wealth secure, vast wealth in horses and in kine:<br> Oil thou the socket, turn the wheel.<br> 4 O Agni, thou hast made the Sun, Eternal Star, to mount the sky,<br> Bestowing light on living men.<br> 5 Thou, Agni, art the people's light, best, dearest, seated in thy shrine:<br> Watch for the singer, give him life.</p>
