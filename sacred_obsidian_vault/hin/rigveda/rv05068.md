 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXVIII. Mitra-Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. SING forth unto your Varuṇa and Mitra with a song inspired.<br> They, Mighty Lords, are lofty Law<br> 2 Full springs of fatness, Sovran Kings, Mitra. and Varuṇa, the Twain,<br> Gods glorified among the Gods.<br> 3 So help ye us to riches, great terrestrial and celestial wealth:<br> Vast is your sway among the Gods.<br> 4 Carefully tending Law with Law they have attained their vigorous might.<br> The two Gods wax devoid of guile.<br> 5 With rainy skies and streaming floods, Lords of the strength that bringeth gifts,<br> A lofty seat have they attained.</p>
