 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XL Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. SING forth to Indu, O ye men, to him who is purified,<br> Fain to pay worship to the Gods.<br> 2 Together with thy pleasant juice the Atharvans have commingled milk,<br> Divine, devoted to the God.<br> 3 Bring, by thy flowing, weal to kine, weal to the people, weal to steeds.<br> Weal, O thou King, to growing plants<br> 4 Sing a praise-song to Soma brown of hue, of independent might.<br> The Red, who reaches up to heaven.<br> 5 Purify Soma when effused with stones which bands move rapidly,<br> And pour the sweet milk in the meath.<br> 6 With humble homage draw ye nigh; blend the libation with the curds:<br> To Indra offer Indu up.<br> 7 Soma, foe-que chief oer men, doing the will of pour forth<br> Prosperity upon our kine.<br> 8 Heart-knower, Sovran of the heart, thou art effused, O Soma, that Indra may drink thee and rejoice.<br> 9 O Soma Pavamana, give us riches and heroic strength,<br> Indu! with. Indra for ally.</p>
