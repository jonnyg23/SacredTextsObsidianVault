 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. THIS prayer, and those who shed the juice, in wars with Vṛtra thou holpest, Indra, Lord of Strength, with all thy succours.<br> O Vṛtra-slayer, from libation poured at noon, drink of the Soma juice, thou blameless Thunderer.<br> 2 Thou mighty Conqueror of hostile armaments, O Indra, Lord of Strength, with all thy saving help.<br> 3 Sole Ruler, thou art Sovran of this world of life, O Indra, Lord of Strength, with all thy saving help.<br> 4 Thou only sunderest these two consistent worlds, O Indra, Lord of Strength, with all thy saving help.<br> 5 Thou art the Lord supreme oer rest and energy, O Indra, Lord of Strength, with all thy saving help.<br> 6 Thou helpest one to power, and one thou hast not helped, O Indra, Lord of Strength, with all thy saving aid.<br> 7 Hear thou Śyāvāśva while he sings to thee, as erst thou heardest Atri when he wrought his holy rites.<br> Indra, thou only gavest Trasadasyu aid in the fierce fight with heroes, strengthening his powers.</p>
