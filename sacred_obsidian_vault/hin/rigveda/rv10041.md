 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLI. Aśvins.</h3>  
 <p>1. THAT general Car of yours, invoked by many a man, that comes to our libations, three-wheeled, meet for lauds,<br> That circumambient Car, worthy of sacrifice, we call with our pure hymns at earliest flush of dawn.<br> 2 Ye, O Nāsatyas, mount that early-harnessed Car, that travels early, laden with its freight of balm,<br> Wherewith ye, Heroes, visit clans who sacrifice, even the poor man's worship where the priest attends.<br> 3 If to the deft Adhvaryu with the meath in hand, or to the Kindler firm in strength, the household friend,<br> Or to the sage's poured libations ye approach, come thence, O Aśvins, now to drink the offered meath.</p>
