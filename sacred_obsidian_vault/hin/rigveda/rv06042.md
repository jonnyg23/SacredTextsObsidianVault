 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. BRING sacrificial gifts to him, Omniscient, for he longs to drink,<br> The Wanderer who comes with speed, the Hero ever in the van.<br> 2 With Soma go ye nigh to him chief drinker of the Soma's juice:<br> With beakers to the Impetuous God, to Indra with the drops effused.<br> 3 What time, with Soma, with the juice effused, ye come before the God,<br> Full wise he knows the hope of each, and, Bold One, strikes this foe and that.<br> 4 To him, Adhvaryu! yea, to him give offerings of the juice expressed.<br> Will he not keep us safely from the spiteful curse of each presumptuous high-born foe?</p>
