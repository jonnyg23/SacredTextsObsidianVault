 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIX. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. O SOMA, being purified bring us the wondrous treasure, meet<br> For lauds, that is in earth and heaven.<br> 2 For ye Twain, Indra, Soma, are Lords of the light, Lords of the kine:<br> Great Rulers, prosper ye our songs.<br> 3 The tawny Steer, while cleansed among the living, bellowing on the grass,<br> Hath sunk and settled in his home.<br> 4 Over the Steer's productive flow the sacred songs were resonant,<br> The mothers of the darling Son.<br> 5 Hath he not, purified, impregned the kine whb long to meet their Lord,<br> The kine who yield the shining milk?<br> 6 Bring near us those who stand aloof strike fear into our enemies:<br> O Pavamana, find us wealth.<br> 7 Soma, bring down the foeman's might, his vigorous strength and vital powe'r,<br> Whether he be afar or near.</p>
