 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>WHEN with thy mighty help, O potent Indra, the armies rush together in their fury.<br> When from the strong man's arm the lightning flieth, let not the mind go forth to side with others.<br> 2 O Indra, where the ground is hard to traverse, smite down our foes, the mortals who assail us,<br> Keep far from us the curse of the reviler: bring us accumulated store of treasures.<br> 3 God of the fair helm, give Sudās a hundred succours, a thousand blessings, and thy bounty.<br> Strike down the weapon of our mortal foeman: bestow upon us splendid fame and riches.<br> 4 I wait the power of one like thee, O Indra, gifts of a Helper such as thou art, Hero.<br> Strong, Mighty God, dwell with me now and ever: Lord of Bay Horses, do not thou desert us.<br> 5 Here are the Kutsas supplicating Indra for might, the Lord of Bays for God-sent conquest.<br> Make our foes ever easy to be vanquished: may we, victorious, win the spoil, O Hero.<br> 6 With precious things, O Indra, thus content us: may we attain to thine exalted favour.<br> Send our chiefs plenteous food with hero children. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
