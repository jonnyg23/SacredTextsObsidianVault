 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXIII. The King.</h3>  
 <p>1. BE with us; I have chosen thee: stand stedfast and immovable.<br> Let all the people wish for thee let not thy kingship fall away.<br> 2 Be even here; fall not away be like a mountain unremoved.<br> Stand stedfast here like Indra's self, and hold the kingship in the grasp.<br> 3 This man hath Indra stablished, made secure by strong oblation's power.<br> May Soma speak a benison, and Brahmaṇaspati, on him.<br> 4 Firm is the sky and firm the earth, and stedfast also are these hills.<br> Stedfast is all this living world, and stedfast is this King of men.<br> 5 Stedfast, may Varuṇa the King, stedfast, the God Bṛhaspati,<br> Stedfast, may Indra, stedfast too, may Agni keep thy stedfast reign.<br> 6 On constant Soma let us think with constant sacrificial gift<br> And then may Indra make the clans bring tribute unto thee alone.</p>
