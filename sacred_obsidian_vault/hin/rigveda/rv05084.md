 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXIV. Prthivi.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU, of a truth,O Prthivi, bearest the tool that rends the hills:<br> Thou rich in torrents, who with might quickenest earth, O Mighty One.<br> 2 To thee, O wanderer at will, ring out the lauds with beams of day,<br> Who drivest, like a neighing steed, the swelling cloud, O bright of hue.<br> 3 Who graspest with thy might on earth. een the strong sovrans of the wood,<br> When from the lightning of thy cloud the rain-floods of the heaven descend.</p>
