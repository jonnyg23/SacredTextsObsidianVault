 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. SOMA, the Steer, effused for draught, flows to the purifying sieve,<br> Slaying the fiends, loving the Gods.<br> 2 Far-sighted, tawny-coloured, he flows to the sieve, intelligent,<br> Bellowing, to his place of rest.<br> 3 This vigorous Pavamana runs forth to the luminous realm of heaven,<br> Fiend-slayer, through the fleecy sieve.<br> 4 This Payamana up above Trita's high ridge hath made the Sun,<br> Together with the Sisters, shine.<br> 5 This Vṛtra-slaying Steer, effused, Soma room-giver, neer deceived,<br> Hath gone, as twere, to win the spoil.<br> 6 Urged onward by the sage, the God speeds forward to the casks of wood,<br> Indu to Indra willingly.</p>
