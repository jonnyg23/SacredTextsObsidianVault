 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XL. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. THEE, Indra, we invoke, the Bull, what time the Soma is expressed.<br> So drink thou of the savoury juice.<br> 2 Indra, whom many laud, accept the strength-conferring Soma juice:<br> Quaff, pour down drink that satisfies.<br> 3 Indra, with all the Gods promote our wealth-bestowing sacrifice,<br> Thou highly-lauded Lord of men.<br> 4 Lord of the brave, to thee proceed these drops of Soma juice expressed,<br> The bright drops to thy dwelling-place.<br> 5 Within thy belly, Indra, take juice, Soma the most excellent: Thine are the drops celestial.<br> 6 Drink our libation, Lord of hymns: with streams of meath thou art bedewed<br> Our glory, Indra, is thy gift.<br> 7 To Indra go the treasures of the worshipper, which never fail:<br> He drinks the Soma and is strong<br> 8 From far away, from near at hand, O Vṛtra-slayer, come to us:<br> Accept the songs we sing to thee.<br> 9 When from the space between the near and far thou art invoked by us,<br> Thence, Indra. come thou hitherward.</p>
