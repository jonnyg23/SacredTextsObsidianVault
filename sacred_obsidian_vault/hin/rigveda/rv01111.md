 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXI. Ṛbhus.</h3>  
 <p>1. WORKING with skill they wrought the lightly rolling car: they wrought the Bays who bear Indra and bring great gifts.<br> The Ṛbhus for their Parents made life young again; and fashioned for the calf a mother by its side.<br> 2 For sacrifice make for us active vital power for skill and wisdom food with noble progeny.<br> Grant to our company this power most excellent, that with a family all-heroic we may dwell.<br> 3 Do ye, O Ṛbhus, make prosperity for us, prosperity for car, ye Heroes, and for steed.<br> Grant us prosperity victorious evermore,<br> conquering foes in battle, strangers or akin.<br> 4 Indra, the Ṛbhus' Lord, I invocate for aid, the Ṛbhus, Vājas, Maruts to the Soma draught.<br> Varuṇa, Mitra, both, yea, and the Aśvins Twain: let them speed us to wealth, wisdom, and victory.<br> 5 May Ṛbhu send prosperity for battle, may Vāja conquering in the fight protect us.<br> This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.</p>
