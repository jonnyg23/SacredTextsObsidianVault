 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXXIX. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. BY goodly paths lead us to riches, Agni, God who knowest every sacred duty.<br> Remove the sin that makes us stray and wander. most ample adoration will we bring thee.<br> 2 Lead us anew to happiness, O Agni; lead us beyond all danger and affliction.<br> Be unto us a wide broad ample castle bless, prosper on their way our sons and offspring.<br> 3 Far from us, Agni, put thou all diseases let them strike lauds that have no saving Agni.<br> God, make our home again to be a blessing, with all the Immortal Deities, O Holy.<br> 4 Preserve us, Agni, with perpetual succour, refulgent in the dwelling which thou lovest.<br> O Conqueror, most youthful, let no danger touch him who praises thee to-day or after.<br> 5 Give not us up a prey to sin, O Agni, the greedy enemy that brings us trouble;<br> Not to the fanged that bites, not to the toothless: give not us up, thou Conqueror, to the spoiler.<br> 6 Such as thou art, born after Law, O Agni when lauded give protection to our bodies,<br> From whosoever would reproach or injure: for thou, God, rescuest from all oppression.<br> 7 Thou, well discerning both these classes, comest to men at early morn, O holy Agni.<br> Be thou obedient unto man at evening, to be adorned, as keen, by eager suitors.<br> 8 To him have we addressed our pious speeches, I, Māna's son, to him victorious Agni.<br> May we gain countless riches with the sages. May we find strengthening food in full abundance.</p>
