 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXVII. Mitra-Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. YE Gods, Ādityas, Varuṇa, Aryaman, Mitra, verily<br> Have here obtained supremest sway, high, holy, set apart for you.<br> 2 When, Varuṇa and Mitra, ye sit in your golden dwelling-place,<br> Ye Twain, supporters of mankind, foeslayers, give felicity.<br> 3 All these, possessors of all wealth, Varuṇa, Mitra, Aryaman,<br> Follow their ways, as if with feet, and guard from injury mortal man.<br> 4 For they are true, they cleave to Law, held holy among every race,<br> Good leaders, bounteous in their gifts, deliverers even from distress.<br> 5 Which of your persons, Varuṇa or Mitra, merits not our praise?<br> Therefore our thought is turned to you, the Atris' thought is turned to you.</p>
