 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. LIKE waves of waters, skilled in song the juices of the Soma speed<br> Onward, as buffaloes to woods.<br> 2 With stream of sacrifice the brown bright drops have flowed with strength in store<br> Of kine into the wooden vats.<br> 3 To Indra, Vāyu, Varuṇa, to Viṣṇu, and the Maruts, flow<br> The drops of Soma juice effused.<br> 4 Three several words are uttered: kine are ]owing, cows who give their milk:<br> The Tawny-hued goes bellowing on.<br> 5 The young and sacred mothers of the holy rite have uttered praise:<br> They decorate the Child of Heaven.<br> 6 From every side, O Soma, for our profit, pour thou forth four seas<br> Filled full of riches thousandfold.</p>
