 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIX. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1 O SOMA DRINKER, ever true, utterly hopeless though we be,<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> 2 O Lord of Strength, whose jaws are strong, great deeds are thine, the powerful:<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> 3 Lull thou asleep, to wake no more, the pair who on each other look<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> 4 Hero, let hostile spirits sleep, and every gentler genius wake:<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> 5 Destroy this ass, O Indra, who in tones discordant brays to thee:<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> 6 Far distant on the forest fall the tempest in a circling course!<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> 7 Slay each reviler, and destroy him who in secret injures us:<br> Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine<br> In thousands, O most wealthy One.</p>
