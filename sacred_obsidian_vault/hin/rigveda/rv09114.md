 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXIV. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE man who waIketh as the Laws of Indu Pavamana bid,<br> Men call him rich in children, him, O Soma, who hath met thy thought. Flow, Indu, flow for Indra's sake.<br> 2 Kasyapa, Ṛṣi, lifting up thy voice with hymn-composers' lauds,<br> Pav reverence to King Soma born the Sovran Ruler of the plants. Flow, Indu, flow for Indra's sake.<br> 3 Seven regions have their several Suns; the ministering priests are seven;<br> Seven are the Āditya Deities,with these, O Soma, guard thou us. Flow, Indu, flow for Indra's sake.<br> 4 Guard us with this oblation which, King Soma, hath been dressed for thee.</p>
