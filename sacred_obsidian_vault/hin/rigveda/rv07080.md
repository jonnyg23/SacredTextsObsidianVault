 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXX. Dawn.</h3>  
 <p>1 THE priests, Vasisthas, are the first awakened to welcome Uṣas with their songs and praises,<br> Who makes surrounding regions part asunder,and shows apparent all existing creatures.<br> 2 Giving fresh life when she hath hid the darkness, this Dawn hath wakened there with new-born lustre.<br> Youthful and unrestrained she cometh forward: she hath turned thoughts to Sun and fire and worship.<br> 3 May blessed Mornings shine on us for ever, with wealth of kine, of horses, and of heroes,<br> Streaming with all abundance, pouring fatness. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
