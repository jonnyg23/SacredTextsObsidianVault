 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIVṢoma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. HITHERWARD have the Soma streamed,<br> the drops while they are purified:<br> When bIent, in waters they are rinsed.<br> 2 The milk hath run to meet them like floods rushing down a precipice:<br> They come to Indra, being cleansed.<br> 3 O Soma Pavamana, thou art flowing to be Indra's drink:<br> The men have seized and lead thee forth.<br> 4 Victorious, to be hailed with joy, O Soma, flow, delighting men,<br> To him who ruleth oer mankind.<br> 5 Thou, Indu, when, effused by stones, thou runnest to the filter, art,<br> Ready for Indra's high decree.<br> 6 Flow on, best Vṛtra-slayer; flow meet to be hailed with joyful lauds.<br> Pure, purifying, wonderful.<br> 7 Pure, purifying is he called the Soma of the meath eflused,<br> Slayer of sinners, dear to Gods.</p>
