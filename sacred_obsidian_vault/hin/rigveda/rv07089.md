 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXIX Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. LET me not yet, King Varuṇa, enter into the house of clay:<br> Have mercy, spare me, Mighty Lord.<br> 2 When, Thunderer! I move along tremulous like a wind-blown skin,<br> Have mercy, spare me, Mighty Lord.<br> 3 O Bright and Powerful God, through want of strength I erred and went astray<br> Have mercy, spare me, Mighty Lord.<br> 4 Thirst found thy worshipper though he stood in the midst of water-fijods:<br> Have mercy, spare me, Mighty Lord.<br> 5 O Varuṇa, whatever the offence may be which we as men commit against the heavenly host,<br> When through our want of thought we violate thy laws, punish us not, O God, for that iniquity.</p>
