 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. GLAD thee: thy glory hath been quaffed, Lord of Bay Steeds, as twere the bowl's enlivening mead.<br> For thee the Strong there is strong drink, mighty, omnipotent to win.<br> 2 Let our strong drink, most excellent, exhilarating, come to thee,<br> Victorious, Indra! bringing gain, immortal conquering in fight,<br> 3 Thou, Hero, winner of the spoil, urgest to speed the car of man.<br> Burn, like a vessel with the flame, the lawless Dasyu, Conqueror!<br> 4 Empowered by thine own might, O Sage, thou stolest Sūrya's chariot wheel.<br> Thou barest Kutsa with the steeds of Wind to Śuṣṇa as his death.<br> 5 Most mighty is thy rapturous joy, most splendid is thine active power,<br> Wherewith, foe-slaying, sending bliss, thou art supreme in gaining steeds.<br> 6 As thou, O Indra, to the ancient singers wast ever joy, as water to the thirsty,<br> So unto thee I sing this invocation. May we find strengthening food in full abundance.</p>
