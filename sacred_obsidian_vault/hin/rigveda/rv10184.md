 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. MAY Viṣṇu form and mould the womb, may Tvaṣṭar duly shape the forms,<br> Prajāpati infuse the stream, and Dhātar lay the germ for thee.<br> 2 O Sinīvālī, set the germ, set thou the germ, Sarasvatī:<br> May the Twain Gods bestow the germ, the Aśvins crowned with lotuses.<br> 3 That which the Aśvins Twain rub forth with the attrition-sticks of gold,<br> That germ of thine we invocate, that in the tenth month thou mayst bear.</p>
