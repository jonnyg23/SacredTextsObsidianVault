 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVIII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. AGNI inflamed hath sent to heaven his lustre: he shines forth widely turning unto Morning.<br> Eastward the ladle goes that brings all blessing, praising the Godswith homage and oblation.<br> 2 Enkindled, thou art King of the immortal world: him who brings offerings thou attendest for his weal.<br> He whom thou urgest on makes all possessions his: he sets before thee, Agni, gifts that guests may claim.<br> 3 Show thyself strong for mighty bliss, O Agni, most excellent be thine effulgent splendours.<br> Make easy to maintain our household lordship, and overcome the might of those who hate us.<br> 4 Thy glory, Agni, I adore, kindled, exalted in thy strength.<br> A Steer of brilliant splendour, thou art lighted well at sacred rites.<br> 5 Agni, invoked and kindled, serve the Gods, thou skilled in sacrifice:<br> For thou art bearer of our gifts.<br> 6 Invoke and worship Agni while the sacrificial rite proceeds:<br> For offering-bearer choose ye him.</p>
