 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVI. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE sages with the fingers' art have dressed and decked that vigorous Steed<br> Upon the lap of Aditi,<br> 2 The kine have called aloud to him exhaustless with a thousand streams,<br> To Indu who supporteth heaven.<br> 3 Him, nourisher of many, Sage, creative Pavamana, they<br> Have sent, by wisdom, to the sky.<br> 4 Him, dweller with Vivasvān, they with use of both arms have sent forth,<br> The Lord of Speech infallible.<br> 5 Him, green, beloved, many eyed, the Sisters with prosing stones<br> Send down to ridges of the sieve.<br> 6 O Pavamana, Indu, priests hurry thee on to Indra, thee<br> Who aidest song and cheerest him.</p>
