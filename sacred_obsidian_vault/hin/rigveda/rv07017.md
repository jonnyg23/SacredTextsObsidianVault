 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. AGNI, be kindled well with proper fuel, and let the grass be scattered wide about thee.<br> 2 Let the impatient Portals be thrown open bring thou the Gods impatient to come hither.<br> 3 Taste, Agni: serve the Gods with our oblation. Offer good sacrifices, Jātavedas!<br> 4 Let Jātavedas pay fair sacrifices, worship andgratify the Gods Immortal.<br> 5 Wise God, win for us things that are all-goodly, and let the prayers, we pray today be fruitful.<br> 6 Thee, even thee, the Son of Strength, O Agni, those Gods have made the bearer of oblations.<br> 7 To thee the God may we perform our worship: do thou, besought, grant us abundant riches.</p>
