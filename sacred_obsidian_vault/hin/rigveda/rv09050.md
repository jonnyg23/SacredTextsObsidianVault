 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN L. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. LOUD as a river's roaring wave thy powers have lifted up themselves:<br> Urge on thine arrow's sharpened point.<br> 2 At thine effusion upward rise three voices full of joy, when thou<br> Flowest upon the fleecy ridge.<br> 3 On to the fleece they urge with stone the tawny well-beloved One,<br> Even Pavamana, dropping meath.<br> 4 Flow with thy current to the sieve, O Sage most powerful to cheer,<br> To seat thee in the place of song.<br> 5 Flow, Most Exhilarating! flow anointed with the milk for balm,<br> Indu, for Indra, for his drink.</p>
