 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XC. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. MAY Varuṇa with guidance straight, and Mitra lead us, he who knows,<br> And Aryaman in accord with Gods.<br> 2 For they are dealers forth of wealth, and, not deluded, with their might<br> Guard evermore the holy laws.<br> 3 Shelter may they vouchsafe to us, Immortal Gods to mortal men,<br> Chasing our enemies away.<br> 4 May they mark out our paths to bliss, Indra, the Maruts, Pūṣan,<br> and Bhaga, the Gods to be adored.<br> 5 Yea, Pūṣan, Viṣṇu, ye who run your course, enrich our hymns with kine;<br> Bless us with all prosperity.<br> 6 The winds waft sweets, the rivers pour sweets for the man who keeps the Law<br> So may the plants be sweet for us.<br> 7 Sweet be the night and sweet the dawns, sweet the terrestrial atmosphere;<br> Sweet be our Father Heaven to us.<br> 8 May the tall tree be full of sweets for us, and full of sweets the Sun:<br> May our milch-kine be sweet for us.<br> 9 Be Mitra gracious unto us, and Varuṇa and Aryaman:<br> Indra, Bṛhaspati be kind, and Viṣṇu of the mighty stride.</p>
