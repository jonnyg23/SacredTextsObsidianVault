 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVIII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THIS Steer, this Chariot, rushes through the woollen filter, as he goes<br> To war that wins a thousand spoils.<br> 2 The Dames of Trita with the stones onward impel this Tawny One<br> Indu to Indra for his drink.<br> 3 Ten active fingers carefully adorn him here; they make him bright<br> And beauteous for the gladdening draught.<br> 4 He like a falcon settles down amid the families of men.<br> Speeding like lover to his love.<br> 5 This young exhilarating juice looks downward from its place in heaven,<br> This Soma-drop that pierced the sieve.<br> 6 Poured for the draught, this tawny juice<br> flows forth, intelligent, crying out,<br> Unto the well-beloved place.</p>
