 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CIV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. Soma hath flowed for thee, Invoked of mat Speed to our sacrifice with both thy Coursers.<br> To thee have streameld the songs or mighty singers, imploring, Indra, drink of our libation.<br> 2 Drink of the juice which men have washed in waters, and fill thee full, O Lord of Tawny Horses.<br> O Indra, hearer of the laud, with Soma which stones have mixed for thee enhance thy rapture.<br> 3 To make thee start, a strong true draught I offer to thee, the Bull, O thou whom Bay Steeds carry.<br> Here take delight, O Indra, in our voices while thou art hymned with power and all our spirit.<br> 4 O Mighty Indra, through thine aid, thy prowess, obtaining life, zealous, and skilled in Order,<br> Men in the house who share the sacred banquet stand singing praise that brings them store of children.<br> 5 Through thy directions, Lord of Tawny Coursers, thine who art firm, splendid, and blest, the people<br> Obtain most liberal aid for their salvation, and praise thee, Indra, through thine excellencies.<br> 6 Lord of the Bays, come with thy two Bay Horses, come to our prayers, to drink the juice of Soma.<br> To thee comes sacrifice which thou acceptest: thou, skilled in holy rites, art he who giveth.<br> 7 Him of a thousand powers, subduing foemen, Maghavan praised with hymns and pleased with Soma,<br> Even him our songs approach, resistless Indra: the adorations of the singer laud him.<br> 8 The way to bliss for Gods and man thou foundest, Indra, seven lovely floods, divine, untroubled,<br> Wherewith thou, rending forts, didst move the ocean, and nine-and-ninety flowing streams of water.<br> 9 Thou from the curse didst free the mighty Waters, and as their only God didst watch and guard them.<br> O Indra, cherish evermore thy body with those which thou hast won in quelling Vṛtra.<br> 10 Heroic power and noble praise is Indra yea, the song worships him invoked of many.<br> Vṛtra he quelled, and gave men room and freedom: gakra, victorious, hath conquered armies.<br> 11 Call we on Maghayan, auspicious Indra. best Hero in this fight where spoil is gathered,<br> The Strong, who listens, who gives aid in battles, who slays the Vṛtras, wins and gathers riches.</p>
