 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIV. Indra. Aśvins.</h3>  
 <p>1. O INDRA, drink this Soma, pressed out in the mortar, full of sweets.<br> Send down to us great riches,at your glad carouse-in thousands, O Most healthy. Thou art waxing great.<br> 2 To thee with sacrifices, with oblations, and with lauds we come.<br> Lord of all strength and power, grant-at your glad carouse-the best choiceworthy treasure. Thou art waxing great.<br> 3 Thou who art Lord of precious boons, inciter even of the churl.<br> Guardian of singers, Indra,at your glad carouse-save us from woe and hatred. Thou art waxing great.<br> 4 Strong, Lords of Magic power, ye Twain churned the united worlds apart,<br> When ye, implored by Vimada, Nāsatyas, forced apart the pair.<br> 5 When the united pair were rent asunder all the Gods complained.<br> The Gods to the Nāsatyas cried, Bring these together once again.<br> 6 Sweet be my going forth, and rich in sweets be my approach to home.<br> So, through your Deity, both Gods, enrich us with all pleasantness.</p>
