 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. RUBBED into life, well stablished in the dwelling, Leader of sacrifice, the Sage, the youthful,<br> Here in the wasting fuel Jātavedas, eternal, hath assumed immortal being.<br> 2 Both Bharatas, Devaśravas, Devāvata, have strongly rubbed to life effectual Agni.<br> O Agni, look thou forth with ample riches: be, every day, bearer of food to feed us.<br> 3 Him nobly born of old the fingers ten produced, him whom his Mothers counted dear.<br> Praise Devāvata's Agni, thou Devaśravas, him who shall be the people's Lord.<br> 4 He set thee in the earth's most lovely station, in Iḷā's place, in days of fair bright weather.<br> On man, on Āpayā, Agni! on the rivers Dṛṣadvati, Sarasvatī, shine richly.<br> 5 Agni, as holy food to thine invoker give wealth in cattle, lasting, rich in marvels.<br> To us be born a son and spreading offspring Agni, be this thy gracious will to us-ward</p>
