 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXIV. Indra-Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. KINGS, Indra-Varuṇa, I would turn you hither to this our sacrifice with gifts and homage.<br> Held in both arms the ladle, dropping fatness, goes of itself to you whose forms are varied.<br> 2 Dyaus quickens and promotes your high dominion who bind with bonds not wrought of rope or cordage.<br> Far from us still be Varuṇa's displeasure may Indra give us spacious room to dwell in.<br> 3 Make ye our sacrifice fair amid the assemblies: make ye our prayers approved among our princes.<br> May God-sent riches come for our possession: further ye us with your delightful succours.<br> 4 O Indra-Varuṇa, vouchsafe us riches with store of treasure, food, and every blessing;<br> For the Āditya, banisher of falsehood, the Hero, dealeth wealth in boundless plenty.<br> 5 May this my song reach Varuṇa and Indra, and, strongly urging, win me sons and offspring.<br> To the Gods banquet may we go with riches. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
