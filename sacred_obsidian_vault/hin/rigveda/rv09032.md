 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE rapture-shedding Soma-drops, effused in our assembly, have<br> Flowed forth to glorify our prince.<br> 2 Then Trita's Maidens onward urge the Tawny-coloured with the stones,<br> Indu for Indra, for his drink.<br> 3 Now like a swan he maketh all the company sing each his hymn:<br> He, like a steed, is bathed in milk.<br> 4 O Soma, viewing heaven and earth, thou runncst like a darting deer<br> Set in the place of sacrifice.<br> 5 The cows have sung with joy to him, even as a woman to her love<br> He came as to a settled race.<br> 6 Bestow illustrious fame on us, both on our liberal lords and me,<br> Glory, intelligence, and wealth.</p>
