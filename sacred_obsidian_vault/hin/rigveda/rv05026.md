 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. O AGNI, Holy and Divine, with splendour and thy pleasant tongue<br> Bring hither and adore the Gods.<br> 2 We pray thee, thou who droppest oil, bright-rayed! who lookest on the Sun,<br> Bring the Gods hither to the feast.<br> 3 We have enkindled thee, O Sage, bright caller of the Gods to feast.<br> O Agni, great in Sacrifice.<br> 4 O Agni, come with all the Gods, come to our sacrificial gift:<br> We choose thee as Invoking Priest.<br> 5 Bring, Agni, to the worshipper who pours the juice, heroic strength:<br> Sit with the Gods upon the grass.<br> 6 Victor of thousands, Agni, thou, enkindled, cherishest the laws,<br> Laud-worthy, envoy of the Gods.<br> 7 Set Agni Jātavedas down, the bearer of our sacred gifts,<br> MostYouthful, God and Minister.<br> 8 Duly proceed our sacrifice, comprising all the Gods, to-day:<br> Strew holy grass to be their seat.<br> 9 So may the Maruts sit thereon, the Aśvins, Mitra, Varuṇa:<br> The Gods with all their company.</p>
