 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXIX. Dawn.</h3>  
 <p>1. O HEAVENLY Dawn, awaken us to ample opulence to-day<br> Even as thou hast wakened us with Satyasravas, Vayya's son, high-born! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 2 Daughter of Heaven, thou dawnedst on Sunitha Sucadratha's son,<br> So dawn thou on one mightier still, on Satyasravas, Vayya's son, high-born! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 3 So, bringing treasure, dawn to-day on us thou Daughter of the Sky,<br> As thou, O mightier yet. didst shine for Satyatravas, Vayya's son, high-born! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 4 Here round about thee are the priests who laud thee, Bright One, with their hymns,<br> And men with gifts, O Bounteous Dame, splendid with wealth and offering much, high-born! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 5 Whatever these thy bands perform to please thee or to win them wealth,<br> Een fain they gird us round and give rich gifts which neer are reft away, high-born! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 6 Give to these wealthy patrons fame, O affluent Dawn, with hero sons,<br> To these our princes who have brought rich gifts neer to be reft away, highborn! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 7 Bring lofty and resplendent fame, O thou munificent Dawn, to these<br> Our wealthy patrons who bestow rich gifts on us of steeds and kine, high-born! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 8 Bring us, O Daughter of the Sky, subsistence in our herds of kine,<br> Together with the sunbeams, with the shine of pure refulgent flames, highborn! delightful with thy steeds!<br> 9 O Daughter of the Sky, shine forth; delay not to perform thy task.<br> Let not the Sun with fervent heat consume thee like a robber foe, high-born! delightful with the steeds!<br> 10 So much, and more exceedingly, O Dawn, it suits thee to bestow,<br> Thou Radiant One who ceasest not to shine for those who sing thy praise, highborn! delightful with thy steeds!</p>
