 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXVII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. MORE beauteous than the beautiful, as Indra's bolt, this Soma, rich in sweets, hath clamoured in the vat.<br> Dropping with oil, abundant, streams of sacrifice flow unto him like milch-kine, lowing, with their milk.<br> 2 On flows that Ancient One whom, hitherward, from heaven, sped through the region of the air, the Falcon snatched.<br> He, quivering with alarm and terrified in heart before bow-armed Kṛśānu, holdeth fast the sweet.<br> 3 May those first freshest drops of Soma juice effused flow on, their way to bring us mighty strength in kine.<br> Beauteous as serpents, worthy to be looked upon, they whom each sacred gift and all our prayers have pleased.<br> 4 May that much-lauded Indu, with a heart inclined to us, well-knowing, fight against our enemies.<br> He who hath brought the germ beside the Strong One's seat moves onward to the widely-opcned stall of kine.<br> 5 The active potent juice of heaven is flowing on, great Varuṇa whom the forward man can neer deceive.<br> Mitra, the Holy, hath been pressed for troubled times, neighing like an impatient horse amid the herd,</p>
