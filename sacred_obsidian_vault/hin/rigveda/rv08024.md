 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. COMPANIONS, let us learn a prayer to Indra. whom the thunder arms,<br> To glorify your bold and most heroic Friend.<br> 2 For thou by slaying Vṛtra art the Vṛtra-slayer, famed for might.<br> Thou, Hero, in rich gifts surpassest wealthy chiefs.<br> 3 As such, when glorified, bring us riches of very wondrous fame,<br> Set in the highest rank, Wealth-giver, Lord of Bays!<br> 4 Yea, Indra, thou disclosest that preeminent dear wealth of men:<br> Boldly, O Bold One, glorified, bring it to us.<br> 5 The workers of destruction stay neither thy right hand nor thy left:<br> Nor hosts that press about thee, Lord of Bays, in fight.<br> 6 O Thunder-armed, I come with songs to thee as to a stall with kine:<br> Fulfil the wish and thought of him who sings thy praise.<br> 7 Chief Vṛtra-slayer, through the hymn of Visvamanas think of all,<br> All that concerneth us, Excellent, Mighty Guide.<br> 8 May we, O Vṛtra-slayer, O Hero, find this thy newest boon, Longed-for, and excellent, thou who art much invoked!<br> 9 O Indra, Dancer, Much-invoked! as thy great power is unsurpassed,<br> So be thy bounty to the worshipper unchecked.<br> 10 Most Mighty, most heroic One, for mighty bounty fill thee full.<br> Though strong, strengthen thyself to win wealth, Maghavan!<br> 11 O Thunderer, never have our prayers gone forth to any God but thee:<br> So help us, Maghavan, with thine assistance now.<br> 12 For, Dancer, verily I find none else for bounty, saving thee,<br> For splendid wealth and power, thou Lover of the Song.<br> 13 For Indra pour ye out the drops meath blent with Soma let him drink<br> With bounty and with majesty will he further us.<br> 14 I spake to the Bay Coursers' Lord, to him who gives ability:<br> Now hear the son of Asva as he praises thee.<br> 15 Never was any Hero born before thee mightier than thou:<br> None certairdy like thee in goodness and in wealth.<br> 16 O ministering priest, pour out of the sweet juice what gladdens most:<br> So is the Hero praised who ever prospers us.<br> 17 Indra, whom Tawny Coursers bear, praise such as thine, preeminent,<br> None by his power or by his goodness hath attained.<br> 18 We, seeking glory, have invoked this Master of all power and might<br> Who must be glorified by constant sacri fice.<br> 19 Come, sing we praise to Indra, friends, the Hero who deserves the laud,<br> Him who with none to aid oercomes all tribes of men.<br> 20 To him who wins the kine, who keeps no cattle back, Celestial God,<br> Speak wondrous speech more sweet than butter and than meath.<br> 21 Whose hero powers are measureless, whose bounty neer may be surpassed,<br> Whose liberality, like light, is over all.<br> 22 As Vyaśva did, praise Indra, praise the Strong unfluctuating Guide,<br> Who gives the foe's possessions to the worshipper.<br> 23 Now, son of Vyaśva, praise thou him who to the tenth time still is new,<br> The very Wise, whom living men must glorify<br> 24 Thou knowest, Indra, Thunder-armed, how to avoid destructive powers,<br> As one secure from pitfalls each returning day.<br> 25 O Indra, bring that aid wherewith of old, Most Wondrous! thou didst slay<br> His foes for active Kutsa: send it down to us.<br> 26 So now we seek thee fresh in might, Most Wonderful in act! for gain:<br> For thou art he who conquers all our foes for us.<br> 27 Who will set free from ruinous woe, or Ārya on the Seven Streams:<br> O valiant Hero, bend the Dāsa's weapon down.<br> 28 As to Varosusaman thou broughtest great riches, for their gain,<br> To Vyaśva's sons, Blest Lady, rich in ample wealth!<br> 29 Let Narya's sacrificial meed reach Vyaśva's Soma-bearing sons:<br> In hundreds and in thousands be the great reward.<br> 30 If one should ask thee, Where is he who sacrificed? Whither lookest thou?<br> Like Vala he hath passed away and dwelleth now on Gomati.</p>
