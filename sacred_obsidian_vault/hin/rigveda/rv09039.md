 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIX Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. FLOW On, O thou of lofty thought, flow swift in thy beloved form,<br> Saying, I go where dwell the Gods.<br> 2 Preparing what is unprepared, and bringing store of food to man,<br> Make thou the rain descend from heaven.<br> 3 With might, bestowing power, thejuice enters the purifying sieve,<br> Far-seeing, sending forth its light.<br> 4 This is it which in rapid course hath with the river's wave flowed down<br> From heaven upon the straining cloth.<br> 5 Inviting him frorh far away, and even from near at hand, the juice<br> For Indra is poured forth as meath.<br> 6 In union they have sung the hymn: with stones they urge the Tawny One.<br> Sit in the place of sacrifice.</p>
