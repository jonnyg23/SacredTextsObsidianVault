 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXX. Indra. Maruts.</h3>  
 <p>1. NAUGHT is to-day, to-morrow naught. Who comprehends the mystery?<br> We must address ourselves unto another's thought, and lost is then the hope we formed.<br> 2 The Maruts are thy brothers. Why, O Indra, wouldst thou take our lives?<br> Agree with them in friendly wise, and do not slay us in the fight.<br> 3 Agastya, brother, why dost thou neglect us, thou who art our friend?<br> We know the nature of thy mind. Verity thou wilt give us naught.<br> 4 Let them prepare the altar, let them kindle fire in front: we two<br> Here will spread sacrifice for thee, that the Immortal may observe.<br> 5 Thou, Lord of Wealth, art Master of all treasures, thou, Lord of friends, art thy friends' best supporter.<br> O Indra, speak thou kindly with the Maruts, and taste oblations in their proper season.</p>
