 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLII Kapiñjala.</h3>  
 <p>1. TELLING his race aloud with cries repeated, he sends his voice out as his boat a steersman.<br> O Bird, be ominous of happy fortune from no side may calamity befall thee.<br> 2 Let not the falcon kill thee, nor the eagle let not the arrow-bearing archer reach thee.<br> Still crying in the region of the Fathers, speak here auspicious, bearing joyful tidings.<br> 3 Bringing good tidings, Bird of happy omen, call thou out loudly southward of our dwellings,<br> So that no thief, no sinner may oppress us. Loud may we speak, with heroes, in assembly.</p>
