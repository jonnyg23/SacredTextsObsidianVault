 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE Godlike hero, famousest of nobles, hath granted me two oxen with a wagon.<br> Trvrsan's son Tryaruna hath distinguished himself, Vaiśvānara Agni! with ten thousands.<br> 2 Protect Tryaruna, as thou art waxing strong and art highly praised, Vaiśvānara Agni!<br> Who granteth me a hundred kine and twenty, and two bay horses, good at draught, and harnessed.<br> 3 So Trasadasyu served thee, God Most Youthful, craving thy favour for the ninth time, Agni;<br> Tryaruya who with attentive spirit accepteth many a song from me the mighty.<br> 4 He who declares his wish to me, to Asvamedha, to the Prince,<br> Pays him who with his verse seeks gain, gives power to him who keeps the Law.<br> 5 From whom a hundred oxen, all of speckled hue, delight my heart,<br> The gifts of Asvamedha, like thrice-mingled draughts of Soma juice.<br> 6 To Asvamedha who bestows a hundred gifts grant hero power,<br> O Indra-Agni! lofty rule like the unwasting Sun in heaven.</p>
