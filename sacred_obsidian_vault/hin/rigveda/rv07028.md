 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVIII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. COME to our prayers, O Indra, thou who knowest: let thy Bay Steeds be yoked and guided hither.<br> Though mortal men on every side invoke thee, still give thine ear to us, O All-impeller.<br> 2 Thy greatness reacheth to our invocation, the sages' prayer which, Potent God, thou guardest.<br> What time thy hand, O Mighty, holds the thunder, awful in strength thou hast become resistless.<br> 3 What time thou drewest both world-halves together, like heroes led by thee who call each other-<br> For thou wast born for strength and high dominion-then een the active overthrew the sluggish.<br> 4 Honour us in these present days, O Indra, for hostile men are making expiation.<br> Our sin that sinless Varuṇa discovered, the Wondrous-Wise hath long ago forgiven.<br> 5 We will address this liberal Lord, this Indra, that he may grant us gifts of ample riches,<br> Best favourer of the singer's prayer and praises. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
