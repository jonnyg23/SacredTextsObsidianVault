 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXVI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. WITH hymns of praise their sons have told aloud the Ṛbhus' mighty deeds.<br> Who, all-supporting, have enjoyed the earth as, twere a mother cow.<br> 2 Bring forth the God with song divine, being Jātavedas hitherward,<br> To bear our gifts at once to heaven.<br> 3 He here, a God-devoted Priest, led forward comes to sacrifice.<br> Like a car covered for the road, he, glowing, knows, himself, the way.<br> 4 This Agni rescues from distress, as twere from the Immortal Race,<br> A God yet mightier than strength, a God who hath been made for life.</p>
