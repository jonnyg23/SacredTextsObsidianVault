 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. DRINK of the juice, O Indra, at thy plea. sure, for thy first draught is early morn's libation.<br> Rejoice, that thou mayst slay our foes, O Hero, and we with lauds will tell thy mighty exploits.<br> 2 Thou hast a car more swift than thought, O Indra; thercon come hither, come to drink the Soma.<br> Let thy Bay Steeds, thy Stallions, hasten hither, with whom thou cornest nigh and art delighted.<br> 3 Deck out thy body with the fairest colours, with golden splendour of the Sun adorn it.<br> O Indra, turn thee hitherward invited by us thy friends; be seated and be joyful.<br> 4 O thou whose grandeur in thy festive transports not even these two great worlds have comprehended.<br> Come, Indra, with thy dear Bay Horses harnessed, come to our dwelling and the food thou lovest.<br> 5 Pressed for thy joyous banquet is the Soma, Soma whereof thou, Indra, ever drinking,<br> Hast waged unequalled battles with thy foemen, which prompts the mighty flow of thine abundance.<br> 6 Found from of old is this thy cup, O Indra: Śatakratu, drink therefrom the Soma.<br> Filled is the beaker with the meath that gladdens, the beaker which all Deities delight in.<br> 7 From many a side with proffered entertainment the folk are calling thee, O Mighty Indra.<br> These our libations shall for thee be richest in sweet meath: dvink thereof and find them pleasant.<br> 8 I will declare thy deeds of old, O Indra, the mighty acts which thou hast first accomplished.<br> In genuine wrath thou loosenedst the mountain so that the Brahman easily found the cattle.<br> 9 Lord of the hosts, amid our bands be seated: they call thee greatest Sage among the sages.<br> Nothing is done, even far away, without thee: great, wondrous, Maghavan, is the hymn I sing thee.<br> 10 Aim of our eyes be thou, for we implore thee, O Maghavan, Friend of friends and Lord of treasures.<br> Fight, Warrior strong in truth, fight thou the battle: give us our share of undivided riches.</p>
