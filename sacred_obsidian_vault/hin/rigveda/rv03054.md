 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LIV. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. To him adorable, mighty, meet for synods, this strengthening hymn, unceasing, have they offered.<br> May Agni hear us with his homely splendours, hear us, Eternal One, with heavenly lustre.<br> 2 To mighty Heaven and Earth I sing forth loudly: my wish goes out desirous and well knowing<br> Both, at whose laud in synods, showing favour, the Gods rejoice them with the living mortal.<br> 3 O Heaven and Earth, may your great law he faithful: he ye our leaders for our high advantage.<br> To Heaven and Earth I offer this my homage, with food, O Agni, as I pray for riches.<br> 4 Yea, holy Heaven and Earth, the ancient sages whose word was ever true had power to find you;<br> And brave men in the fightwhere heroes conquer, O Earth, have known you well and paid you honour.<br> 5 What pathway leadeth to the Gods? Who knoweth this of a truth, and who will now declare it?<br> Seen are their lowest dwelling-places only, but they are in remote and secret regions.<br> 6 The Sage who looketh on mankind hath viewed them bedewed, rejoicing in the seat of Order.<br> They make a home as for a bird, though parted, with one same will finding themselves together.<br> 7 Partners though parted, with far-distant limits, on one firm place both stand for ever watchful,<br> And, being young for evermore, as sisters, speak to each other names that are united.<br> 8 All living things they part and keep asunder; though bearing up the mighty Gods they reel not.<br> One All is Lord of what is fixed and moving, that walks, that flies, this multiform creation.<br> 9 Afar the Ancient from of old I ponder, our kinship with our mighty Sire and Father,<br> Singing the praise whereof the Gods by custom stand on the spacious far-extended pathway.<br> 10 This laud, O Heaven and Earth, to you I utter: let the kind-hearted hear, whose tongue is Agni,<br> Young, Sovran Rulers, Varuṇa and Mitra, the wise and very glorious Ādityas.<br> 11 The fair-tongued Savitar, the golden-handed, comes thrice from heaven as Lord in our assembly.<br> Bear to the Gods this song of praise, and send us, then, Savitar, complete and perfect safety.<br> 12 Deft worker, skiful-handed, helpful, holy, may Tvaṣṭar, God, give us these things to aid us,<br> Take your delight, Ye Ṛbhus joined with Pūṣan: ye have prepared the rite with stones adjusted.<br> 13 Borne on their flashing car, the spear-armed Maruts, the nimble Youths of Heaven, the Sons of Order,<br> The Holy, and Sarasvatī, shall hear us: ye Mighty, give us wealth with noble offspring.<br> 14 To Viṣṇu rich in marvels, songs And praises shall go as singers on the road of Bhaga,<br> The Chieftain of the Mighty Stride, whose Mothers, the many young Dames, never disregard him.<br> 15 Indra, who rules through all his powers heroic, hath with his majesty filled earth and heaven.<br> Lord of brave hosts, Fort-crusher, Vṛtra-slayer, gather thou up and bring us store of cattle.<br> 16 My Sires are the Nāsatyas, kind tokinsmen: the Aśvins' kinship is a glorious title.<br> For ye are they who give us store of riches: ye guard your gift uncheated by the bounteous.<br> 17 This is, ye Wise, your great and glorious title, that all ye Deities abide in Indra.<br> Friend, Much-invoked! art thou with thy dear Ṛbhus: fashion ye this our hymn for our advantage.<br> 18 Aryaman, Aditi deserve our worship: the laws of Varuṇa remain unbroken.<br> The lot of childlessness remove ye from us, and let our course be rich in kine and offspring.<br> 19 May the Gods envoy, sent to many a quarter, proclaim us sinless for our perfect safety.<br> May Earth and Heaven, the Sun, the waters, hear us, and the wide firmament and constellations.<br> 20 Hear us the mouatains which distil the rain-drops, and, resting firm, rejoice in freshening moisture.<br> May Aditi with the Ādityas hear us, and Maruts grant us their auspicious shelter.<br> 21 Soft be our path for ever, well-provisioned: with pleasant meath, O Gods, the herbs besprinkle.<br> Safe be my bliss, O Agni, in thy friendship: may I attain the seat of foodful. riches,<br> 22 Enjoy the offering: beam thou strength upon us; combine thou for our good all kinds of glory.<br> Conquer in battle, Agni, all those foemen, and light us every day with loving kindness.</p>
