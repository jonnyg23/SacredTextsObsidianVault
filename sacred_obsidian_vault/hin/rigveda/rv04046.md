 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVI. Vāyu. Indra-Vāyu</h3>  
 <p>1. DRINK the best draught of Soma-juice, O Vāyu, at our holy rites:<br> For thou art he who drinketh first.<br> 2 Come, team-drawn, with thy hundred helps, with Indra, seated in the car,<br> Vāyu, and drink your fill of juice.<br> 3 May steeds a thousand bring you both, Indra. and Vāyu, hitherward<br> To drink the Soma, to the feast.<br> 4 For ye, O Indra-Vāyu, mount the golden-seated car that aids<br> The sacrifice, that reaches heaven.<br> 5 On far-refulgent chariot come unto the man who offers gifts:<br> Come, Indra-Vāyu, hitherward.<br> 6 Here, Indra-Vāyu, is the juice: drink it, accordant with the Gods,<br> Within the giver's dwelling-place.<br> 7 Hither, O Indra-Vāyu, be your journey here unyoke your steeds,<br> Here for your draught of Soma juice.</p>
