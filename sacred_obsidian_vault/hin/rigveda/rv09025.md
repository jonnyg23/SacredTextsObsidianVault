 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXV. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. GREEN-HUED! as one who giveth strength flow on for Gods to drink, a draught<br> For Vāyu and the Marut host.<br> 2 O Pavamana, sent by song, roaring about thy dwelling-place,<br> Pass into Vāyu as Law bids.<br> 3 The Steer shines with the Deities, dear Sage in his appointed home,<br> Foe-Slayer, most beloved by Gods.<br> 4 Taking each beauteous form, he goes, desirable, while purified,<br> Thither where- the Immortals sit.<br> 5 To Indra Soma flows, the Red, engendering song, exceeding wise,<br> The visitor of living men.<br> 6 Flow, best exhilarator, Sage, flow to the filter in a stream<br> To seat thee in the place of song.</p>
