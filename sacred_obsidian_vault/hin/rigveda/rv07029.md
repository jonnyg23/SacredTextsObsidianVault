 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIX Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. THIS Soma hath been pressed for thee, O Indra: come hither, Lord of Bays, for this thou lovest.<br> Drink of this fair, this well-effused libation: Maghavan, give us wealth when we implore thee.<br> 2 Come to us quickly with thy Bay Steeds, Hero, come to our prayer, accepting our devotion.<br> Enjoy thyself aright at this libation, and listen thou unto the prayers we offer.<br> 3 What satisfaction do our hymns afford thee? When, Maghavan? Now let us do thee service.<br> Hymns, only hymns, with love for thee, I weave thee: then hear, O Indra, these mine invocations.<br> 4 They, verily, were also human beings whom thou wast wont to hear, those earlier sages.<br> Hence I, O Indra Maghavan, invoke thee: thou art our Providence, even as a Father.<br> 5 We will address this liberal Lord, this Indra, that he may grant us gifts of ample riches,<br> Best favourer of the singer's prayer and praises. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
