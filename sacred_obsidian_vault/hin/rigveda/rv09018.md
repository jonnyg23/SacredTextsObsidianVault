 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVIII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU, Soma, dweller on the hills, effused, hast flowed into the sieve,:<br> All-bounteous art thou in carouse.<br> 2 Thou art a sacred Bard, a Sage; the meath is offipring of thy sap:<br> All-bountcous art thou in carouse.<br> 3 All Deities of one accord have come that they may drink of thee:<br> All-bounteous art thou in carouse.<br> 4 He who containeth in his hands all treasures much to be desired:<br> All-bounteous art thou in carouse.<br> 5 Who milketh out this mighty Pair, the Earth and Heaven, like mother kine<br> All-bounteous art thou in carouse.<br> 6 Who in a moment mightily floweth around these two world-halvcs:<br> All-bounteous art thou in carouse.<br> 7 The Strong One, being purified, hath in the pitchers cried aloud:<br> All-bounteous art thou in carouse.</p>
