 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLI. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. COME gracious to our sacrifice, O Indra: pressed Soma-drops are purified to please thee.<br> As cattle seek their home, so Thunderwielder, come, Indra, first of those who claim our worship.<br> 2 With that well-formed most wide-extending palate, wherewith thou ever drinkest streams of sweetness,<br> Drink thou; the Adhvaryu standeth up before thee: let thy spoil-winning thunderbolt attend thee.<br> 3 This drop, steer-strong and omniform, the Soma, hath been made ready for the Bull, for India.<br> Drink this, Lord of the Bays, thou Strong Supporter, this that is thine of old, thy food for ever.<br> 4 Soma when pressed excels the unpressed Soma, better, for one who knows, to give him pleasure.<br> Come to this sacrifice of ours, O Victor replenish all thy powers with this libation.<br> 5 We call on thee, O Indra: come thou hither: sufficient be the Soma for thy body.<br> Rejoice thee, Śatakratu! in the juices guard us in wars, guard us among our people.</p>
