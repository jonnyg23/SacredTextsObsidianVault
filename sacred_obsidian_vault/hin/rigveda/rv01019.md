 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIX. Agni, Maruts.</h3>  
 <p>1 To this fair sacrifice to drink the milky draught thou art invoked:<br> O Agni, with the Maruts come.<br> 2 No mortal man, no God exceeds thy mental power, O Mighty one:<br> O Agni, with the Maruts come:<br> 3 All Gods devoid of guile, who know the mighty region of mid-air:<br> O Agni, with those Maruts come.<br> 4 The terrible, who sing their song, not to be overcome by might:<br> O Agni, with those Maruts come.<br> 5 Brilliant, and awful in their form, mighty, devourers of their foes:<br> O Agni, with those Maruts come.<br> 6 Who sit as Deities in heaven, above the sky-vault's luminous sphere:<br> O Agni, with those Maruts come.<br> 7 Who scatter clouds about the sky, away over the billowy sea:<br> O Agni, with those Maruts come.<br> 8 Who with their bright beams spread them forth over the ocean in their might<br> O Agni, with those Maruts come.<br> 9 For thee, to be thine early draught, I pour the Soma-mingled meath:<br> O Agni, with the Maruts come.</p>
