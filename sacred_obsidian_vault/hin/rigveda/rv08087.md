 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXVII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. To Indra sing a Sāma hymn, a lofty song to Lofty Sage,<br> To him who guards the Law, inspired, and fain for praise.<br> 2 Thou, Indra, art the Conqueror: thou gavest splendour to the Sun.<br> Maker of all things, thou art Mighty and All-God.<br> 3 Radiant with light thou wentest to the sky, the luminous realm of heaven.<br> ne Deities, Indra strove to win thee for their Friend.<br> 4 Come unto us, O Indra, dear, still conquering, unconcealable,<br> Vast as a mountain spread on all sides, Lord of Heaven.<br> 5 O truthful Soma-drinker, thou art mightier than both the worlds.<br> Thou strengthenest him who pours libation, Lord of Heaven.<br> 6 For thou art he, O Indra, wiio stormeth all castles of the foe,<br> Slayer of Dasyus, man's Supporter, Lord of Heaven.<br> 7 Now have we, Indra, Friend of Song, sent our great wishes forth to thee,<br> Coming like floods that follow floods.<br> 8 As rivers swell the ocean, so, Hero, our prayers increase thy might,<br> Though of thyself, O Thunderer, waxing day by day.<br> 9 With holy song mey bind to the broad wide-yoked car the Bay Steeds of the rapid God,<br> Bearers of Indra, yoked by word.<br> 10 O Indra, bring great strength to us, bring valour, Śatakratu, thou most active, bring<br> A hero conquering in war.<br> 11 For, gracious Śatakratu, thou hast ever been a Mother and a Sire to us,<br> So now for bliss we pray to thee.<br> 12 To thee, Strong, Much-invoked, who showest forth thy strength, O Śatakratu, do I speak:<br> So grant thou us heroic strength.</p>
