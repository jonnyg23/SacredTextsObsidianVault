 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. WE with great reverence have approached The Youngest who hath shone forth well-kindled in his dwelling,<br> With wondrous light between wide earth and heaven, well-worshipped, looking forth in all directions.<br> 2 Through his great might oercoming all misfortunes, praised in the house is Agni Jātavedas.<br> May he protect us from disgrace and trouble, both us who laud him and our noble patrons.<br> 3 O Agni, thou art Varuṇa and Mitra: Vasisthas with their holy hymns exalt thee.<br> With thee be most abundant gain of treasure. Ye Gods, preserve us evermore with blessings.</p>
