 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVIII. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE Thirty Gods and Three besides, whose seat hath been the sacred grass,<br> From time of old have found and gained.<br> 2 Varuṇa, Mitra, Aryaman, Agnis, with Consorts, sending boons,<br> To whom our Vaṣaṭ! is addressed:<br> 3 These are our guardians in the west, and northward here, and in the south,<br> And on the cast, with all the tribe.<br> 4 Even as the Gods desire so verily shall it be. None minisheth this power of theirs,<br> No demon, and no mortal<br> 5 The Seven carry seven spears; seven are the splendours they possess,<br> And seven the glories they assume.</p>
