 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. INDRA, for our assistance bring that most effectual power of thine,<br> Which conquers men for us, and wins the spoil, invincible in fight.<br> 2 Indra, whatever aids be thine, four be they, or, O Hero, three,<br> Or those of the Five Tribes of men, bring quickly all that help to us.<br> 3 The aid most excellent of thee the Mightiest hitherward we call,<br> For thou wast born with hero might, conquering, Indra, with the Strong.<br> 4 Mighty to prosper us wast thou born, and mighty is the strength thou hast.<br> In native power thy soul is firm: thy valour, Indra, slays a host.<br> 5 O Śatakratu, Lord of Strength, O Indra, Caster of the Stone.<br> With all thy chariot's force assail the man who shows himself thy foe.<br> 6 For, Mightiest Vṛtra-slayer, thee, fierce, foremost among many, folk<br> Whose sacred grass is trimmed invite to battle where the spoil is won.<br> 7 Indra, do thou protect our car that mingles foremost in the fights,<br> That bears its part in every fray, invincible and seeking spoil.<br> 8 Come to us, Indra, and protect our car with thine intelligence.<br> May we, O Mightiest One, obtain excellent fame at break of day, and meditate our hymn at dawn.</p>
