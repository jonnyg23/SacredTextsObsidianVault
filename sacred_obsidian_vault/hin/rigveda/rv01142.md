 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXLII. Āprīs.</h3>  
 <p>1. KINDLED, bring, Agni, Gods to-day for him who lifts the ladle up.<br> Spin out the ancient thread for him who sheds, with gifts, the Soma juice.<br> 2 Thou dealest forth, Tanūnapāt, sweet sacrifice enriched with oil,<br> Brought by a singer such as I who offers gifts and toils for thee.<br> 3 He wondrous, sanctifying, bright, sprinkles the sacrifice with mead,<br> Thrice, Narāśaṁsa from the heavens, a God mid Gods adorable.<br> 4 Agni, besought, bring hitherward Indra the Friend, the Wonderful,<br> For this my hymn of praise, O sweet of tongue, is chanted forth to thee.<br> 5 The ladle-holders strew trimmed grass at this well-ordered sacrifice;<br> A home for Indra is adorned, wide, fittest to receive the Gods.<br> 6 Thrown open be the Doors Divine, unfailing, that assist the rite,<br> High, purifying, much-desired, so that the Gods may enter in.<br> 7 May Night and Morning, hymned with lauds, united, fair to look upon,<br> Strong Mothers of the sacrifice, seat them together on the grass.<br> 8 May the two Priests Divine, the sage, the sweet-voiced lovers of the hymn,<br> Complete this sacrifice of ours, effectual, reaching heaven to-day.<br> 9 Let Hotrā pure, set among Gods, amid the Maruts Bhāratī, Iḷā, Sarasvatī, Mahī, rest on the grass, adorable.<br> 10 May Tvaṣṭar send us genial dew abundant, wondrous, rich in gifts,<br> For increase and for growth of wealth, Tvaṣṭar our kinsman and our Friend.<br> 11 Vanaspati, give forth, thyself, and call the Gods to sacrifice.<br> May Agni, God intelligent, speed our oblation to the Gods.<br> 12 To Vāyu joined with Pūṣan, with the Maruts, and the host of Gods,<br> To Indra who inspires the hymn cry Glory! and present the gift.<br> 13 Come hither to enjoy the gifts prepared with cry of Glory! Come,<br> O Indra, hear their calling; they invite thee to the sacrifice.</p>
