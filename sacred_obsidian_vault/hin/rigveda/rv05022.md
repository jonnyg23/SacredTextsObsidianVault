 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. LIKE Atri, Viśvasāman! sing to him of purifying light,<br> Who must be praised in holy rites, the Priest most welcome in the house.<br> 2 Set Jātavedas in his place, Agni the God and Minister.<br> Let sacrifice proceed to-day duly, comprising all the Gods.<br> 3 All mortals come to thee for aid, the God of most observant mind.<br> Of thine excelling favour we bethink us as we long for it.<br> 4 Mark with attention this our speech, O Agni, thou victorious One.<br> Thee, Strong-jawed! as the homestead's Lord, the Atris with their lauds exalt, the Atris beautify with songs.</p>
