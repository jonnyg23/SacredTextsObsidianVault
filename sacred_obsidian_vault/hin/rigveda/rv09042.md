 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. ENGENDERING the Sun in floods, engendering heaven's lights, green-hued,<br> Robed in the waters and the milk,<br> 2 According to primeval plan this Soma, with his stream, effused<br> Flows purely on, a God for Gods.<br> 3 For him victorious, waxen great, the juices with a thousand powers<br> Are purified for winning spoil.<br> 4 Shedding the ancient fluid he is poured into the cleansing sieve:<br> He, thundering, hath produced the Gods.<br> 5 Soma, while purifying, sends hither all things to be desired,<br> He sends the Gods who strengthen Law.<br> 6 Soma, effused, pour on us wealth in kine, in heroes, steeds, and spoil,<br> Send us abundant store of food.</p>
