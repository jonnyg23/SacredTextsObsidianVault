 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1 I Laud Agni, the chosen Priest, God, minister of sacrifice,<br> The hotar, lavishest of wealth.<br> 2 Worthy is Agni to be praised by living as by ancient seers.<br> He shall bring hitherward the Gods.<br> 3 Through Agni man obtaineth wealth, yea, plenty waxing day by day,<br> Most rich in heroes, glorious.<br> 4 Agni, the perfect sacrifice which thou encompassest about<br> Verily goeth to the Gods.<br> 5 May Agni, sapient-minded Priest, truthful, most gloriously great,<br> The God, come hither with the Gods.<br> 6 Whatever blessing, Agni, thou wilt grant unto thy worshipper,<br> That, Aṅgiras, is indeed thy truth.<br> 7 To thee, dispeller of the night, O Agni, day by day with prayer<br> Bringing thee reverence, we come<br> 8 Ruler of sacrifices, guard of Law eternal, radiant One,<br> Increasing in thine own abode.<br> 9 Be to us easy of approach, even as a father to his son:<br> Agni, be with us for our weal.</p>
