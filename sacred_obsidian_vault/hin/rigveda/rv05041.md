 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLI. Viśvedevas</h3>  
 <p>1. WHO, Mitra-Varuṇa, is your pious servant to give you gifts from earth or mighty heaven?<br> Preserve us in the seat of holy Order, and give the offerer power that winneth cattle.<br> 2 May Mitra, Varuṇa, Aryaman, and Āyu, Indra Ṛbhukṣan, and the Maruts, love us,<br> And they who of one mind with bounteous Rudra accept the hymn and laud with adorations.<br> 3 You will I call to feed the car-horse, Aśvins, with the wind's flight swiftest of those who travel:<br> Or also to the Asura of heaven, Worshipful, bring a hymn as twere libation.<br> 4 The heavenly Victor, he whose priest is Kaṇva, Tṛta with Dyaus accordant, Vāta, Agni,<br> All-feeding Pūṣan, Bhaga sought the oblation, as they whose steeds are fleetest seek the contest.<br> 5 Bring ye your riches forward borne on horses: let thought be framed for help and gain of treasure.<br> Blest he the priest of Auśija through courses, the courses which are yours the fleet, O Maruts.<br> 6 Bring hither him who yokes the car, your Vāyu, who praises with his songs, the God and Singer;<br> And, praying and devout, noble and prudent, may the Gods Spouses in their thoughts retain us.<br> 7 I speed to you with powers that should be honoured, with songs distinguishing Heaven's mighty Daughters,<br> Morning and Night, the Two, as twere all-knowing: these bring the sacrifice unto the mortal.<br> 8 You I extol, the nourishers of heroes bringing you gifts, Vastospati and Tvaṣṭar-<br> Rich Dhiṣaṇā accords through our obeisance - andTrees and Plants, for the swift gain of riches.<br> 9 Ours be the Parvatas, even they, for offspring, free-moving, who are Heroes like the Vasus.<br> May holy Āptya, Friend of man, exalted, strengthen our word for ever and be near us.<br> 10 Tṛta praised him, germ of the earthly hero, with pure songs him the Offspring of the Waters.<br> Agn; with might neighs loudly like a charger: he of the flaming hair destroys the forests.<br> 11 How shall we speak to the great might of Rudra? How speak to Bhaga who takes thought for riches?<br> May Plants, the Waters, and the Sky preserve us, and Woods and Mountains with their trees for tresses.<br> 12 May the swift Wanderer, Lord of refreshments listen to our songs, who speeds throuih cloudy heaven:<br> And may the Waters, bright like castles, hear us, as they flow onward from the cloven mountain.<br> 13 We know your ways, ye Mighty Ones receiving choice meed, ye Wonderful, we will proclaim it.<br> Even strong birds descend not to the mortal who strives to reach them with swift blow and weapons.<br> 14 Celestial and terrestrial generations, and Waters will I summon to the feasting.<br> May days with bright dawns cause my songs to prosper, and may the conquered streams increase their waters.<br> 15 Duly to each one hath my laud been offered. Strong be Varūtrī with her powers to succour.<br> May the great Mother Rasā here befriend us, straight-handed, with the princes, striving forward.<br> 16 How may we serve the Liberal Ones with worship, the Maruts swift of course in invocation, the Maruts far-renowned in invocation?<br> Let not the Dragon of the Deep annoy us, and gladly may he welcome our addresses.<br> 17 Thus thinking, O ye Gods, the mortal wins you to give him increase of his herds of cattle: the mortal wins him, O ye Gods, your favour.<br> Here he wins wholesome food to feed this body: as for mine old age, Nirrti consume it<br> 18 O Gods, may we obtain from you this favour, strengthening food through the Cow's praise, ye Vasus.<br> May she who gives good gifts, the gracious Goddes. s, come speeding nigh to us for our well-being.<br> 19 May Iḷā, Mother of the herds of cattle, and Urvasi with all the streams accept us;<br> May Urvasi in lofty heaven accepting, as she partakes the oblation of the living,<br> 20 Visit us while she shares Urjavya's food.</p>
