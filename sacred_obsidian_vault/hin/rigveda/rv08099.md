 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VII. Praskaṇva's Gift.</h3>  
 <p>1. GREAT, verily, is Indra's might. I have beheld, and hither comes<br> Thy bounty, Dasyave-vrka!<br> 2 A hundred oxen white of hue are shining like the stars in heaven,<br> So tall, they seem to prop the sky.<br> 3 Bamboos a hundred, a hundred dogs, a hundred skins of beasts well-tanned,<br> A hundred tufts of Balbaja, four hundred red-hued mares are mine.<br> 4 Blest by the Gods, Kinvayanas! be ye who spread through life on life:<br> Like horses have ye stridden forth.<br> 5 Then men extolled the team of seven not yet full-grown, its fame is great.<br> The dark mares rushed along the paths, so that no eye could follow them.</p>
