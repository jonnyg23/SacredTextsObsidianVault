 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXV. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. ACCEPT our loudest-sounding hymn, food most delightful to the Gods,<br> Pouring our offerings in thy mouth.<br> 2 Now, Agni, will we say to thee, O wisest and best Aṅgiras,<br> Our precious, much-availing prayer.<br> 3 Who, Agni, is thy kin, of men? who is thy worthy worshipper?<br> On whom dependent? who art thou?<br> 4 The kinsman, Agni, of mankind, their well beloved Friend art thou,<br> A Friend whom friends may supplicate.<br> 5 Bring to us Mitra, Varuṇa, bring the Gods to mighty sacrifice.<br> Bring them, O Agni, to thine home.</p>
