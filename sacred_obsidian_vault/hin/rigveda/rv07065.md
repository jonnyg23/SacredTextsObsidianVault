 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXV. Mitra-Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. WITH hymns I call you, when the Sun hath risen, Mitra, and Varuṇa whose thoughts are holy,<br> Whose Power Divine, supreme and everlasting, comes with good heed at each man's supplication.<br> 2 For they are Asuras of Gods, the friendly make, both of you, our lands exceeding fruitful.<br> May we obtain you, Varuṇa and Mitra, wherever Heaven and Earth and days may bless us.<br> 3 Bonds of the sinner, they bear many nooses: the wicked mortal hardly may escape them.<br> Varuṇa-Mitra, may your path of Order bear us oer trouble as a boat oer waters.<br> 4 Come, taste our offering, Varuṇa and Mitra: bedew our pasture wil sweet food and fatness.<br> Pour down in plenty here upon the people the choicest of your fair celestial water.<br> 5 To you this laud, O Varuṇa and Mitra, is offered, like bright Soma juice to Vāyu.<br> Favour our songs of praise, wake thought and spirit. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
