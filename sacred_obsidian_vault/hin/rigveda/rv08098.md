 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. INDRA, the poets with. their hymns extol this hero might of thine:<br> They strengthened, loud in song, thy power that droppeth oil. With hymns the Pauras came to thee.<br> 2 Through piety they came to Indra for his aid, they whose libations give theejoy.<br> As thou with, Krsa and Samvarta hast rejoiced, so, Indra, be thou glad with us.<br> 3 Agreeing in your spirit, all ye Deities, come nigh to us.<br> Vasus and Rudras shall come near to give us aid, and Maruts listen to our call.<br> 4 May Pūṣan, Viṣṇu, and Sarasvatī befriend, and the Seven Streams, this call of mine:<br> May Waters, Wind, the Mountains, and the Forest-Lord, and Earth give ear unto my cry.<br> 5 Indra, with thine own bounteous gift, most liberal of the Mighty Ones,<br> Be our boon benefactor, Vṛtra-slayer, be our feast-companion for our weal.<br> 6 Leader of heroes, Lord of battle, lead thou us to combat, thou Most Sapient One.<br> High fame is theirs who win by invocations, feasts and entertainment of the Gods.<br> 7 Our hopes rest on the Faithful One: in Indra is the people's life.<br> O Maghavan, come nigh that thou mayst give us aid: make plenteous food stream forth for us.<br> 8 Thee would we worship, Indra, with our songs of praise: O Śatakratu, be thou ours.<br> Pour down upon Praskaṇva bounty vast and firm, exuberant, that shall never fail.</p>
