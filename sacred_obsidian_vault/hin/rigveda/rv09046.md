 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVI. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. LIKE able coursers they have been sent forth to be the feast of Gods,<br> joying in mountains, flowing on.<br> 2 To Vāyu flow the Soma-streams, the drops of juice made beautiful<br> Like a bride dowered by her sire.<br> 3 Pressed in the mortar, these, the drops of<br> juice, the Somas rich in food,<br> Give strength to Indra with their work.<br> 4 Deft-handed men, run hither, seize the brilliant juices blent with meal,<br> And cook with milk the gladdening draught.<br> 5 Thus, Soma, Conqueror of wealth! flow, finding furtherance for us,<br> Giver oF ample opulence.<br> 6 This Pavamana, meet to be adorned, the fingers ten adorn,<br> The draught that shall make Indra glad.</p>
