 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II. Vāyu.</h3>  
 <p>1 BEAUTIFUL Vāyu, come, for thee these Soma drops have been prepared:<br> Drink of them, hearken to our call.<br> 2 Knowing the days, with Soma juice poured forth, the singers glorify<br> Thee, Vāyu, with their hymns of praise.<br> 3 Vāyu, thy penetrating stream goes forth unto the worshipper,<br> Far-spreading for the Soma draught.<br> 4 These, Indra-Vāyu, have been shed; come for our offered dainties sake:<br> The drops are yearning for you both.<br> 5 Well do ye mark libations, ye Vāyu and Indra, rich in spoil!<br> So come ye swiftly hitherward.<br> 6 Vāyu and Indra, come to what the Soma-presser hath prepared:<br> Soon, Heroes, thus I make my prayer.<br> 7 Mitra, of holy strength, I call, and foe-destroying Varuṇa,<br> Who make the oil-fed rite complete.<br> 8 Mitra and Varuṇa, through Law, lovers and cherishers of Law,<br> Have ye obtained your might power<br> 9 Our Sages, Mitra-Varuṇa, wide dominion, strong by birth,<br> Vouchsafe us strength that worketh well.</p>
