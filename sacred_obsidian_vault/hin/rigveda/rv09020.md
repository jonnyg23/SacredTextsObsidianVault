 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XX Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. FORTH through the straining-cloth the Sage flows to the banquet of the Gods,<br> Subduing all our enemies.<br> 2 For he, as Pavamana, sends thousandfold treasure in the shape<br> Of cattle to the singing-men.<br> 3 Thou graspest all things with thy mind, and purifiest thee with thoughts<br> As such, O Soma, find us fame.<br> 4 Pour lofty glory on us, send sure riches to our liberal lords,<br> Bring food to those who sing thy praise.<br> 5 As thou art cleansed, O Wondrous Steed, O Soma, thou hast entered, like<br> A pious King, into the songs.<br> 6 He, Soma, like a courser in the floods invincible, made clean<br> With hands, is resting in the jars.<br> 7 Disporting, like a liberal chief, thou goest, Soma, to the sieve,<br> Lending the laud a Hero's strength.</p>
