 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLV. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. FLOW, thou who viewest men, to give delight, to entertain the Gods,<br> Indu, to Indra for his drink.<br> 2 Stream to thine embassy for us: thou hastenest, for Indra, to<br> The Gods, O better than our friends.<br> 3 We balm thee, red of hue, with milk to fit thee for the rapturous joy:<br> Unbar for us the doors of wealth.<br> 4 He through the sieve hath passed, as comes a courser to the pole, to run<br> Indu belongs unto the Gods.<br> 5 All friends have lauded him as he sports in the wood, beyond the fleece:<br> Singers have chanted Indu's praise.<br> 6 Flow, Indu, with that stream wherein steeped thou announcest to the man<br> Who worships thee heroic strength.</p>
