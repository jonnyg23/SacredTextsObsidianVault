 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. A MIGHTY Governor art thou, Wondrous, Destroyer of the foe,<br> Whose friend is never done to death, and never, never overcome.<br> 2 Lord of the clan, who brings us bliss, Strong, Warrior, Slayer of the fiend,<br> May India, Soma-drinker, go before us, Bull who gives us peace.<br> 3 Drive Rākṣasas and foes away, break thou in pieces Vṛtra'sjaws:<br> O Vṛtra-slaying Indra, quell the foeman's wrath who threatens us.<br> 4 O Indra, beat our foes away, humble the men who challenge us:<br> Send down to nether darkness him who seeks to do us injury.<br> 5 Baffle the foeman's plan, ward off his weapon who would conquer us.<br> Give shelter from his furious wrath, and keep his murdering dart afar.</p>
