 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 7, SECTION 25.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_631"></a><a href="#fn_631"><font size="1">1</font></a> (a) 'May Vish<i>n</i>u make thy womb ready; may Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> frame the shape (of the child); may Pra<i>g</i>âpati pour forth (the sperm); may Dhât<i>ri</i> give thee conception!</p>  
 <p>(b) 'Give conception, Sinîvâlî; give conception, Sarasvatî! May the two A<i>s</i>vins, wreathed with lotus, give conception to thee!</p>  
 <p>(c) 'The embryo which the two A<i>s</i>vins produce with their golden kindling-sticks: that embryo we call into thy womb, that thou mayst give birth to it after ten months.</p>  
 <p>(d) <a name="fr_632"></a><a href="#fn_632"><font size="1">d</font></a> 'As the earth is pregnant with Agni, as the heaven is with Indra pregnant, as Vâyu dwells in the womb of the regions (of the earth), thus I place an embryo into thy womb.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p>(e) 'Open thy womb; take in the sperm; may a male child, an embryo be begotten in the womb. The mother bears him ten months; may he be born, the most valiant of his kin.</p>  
 <p>(f) 'May a male embryo enter thy womb, as an arrow the quiver; may a man be born here, thy son, after ten months.</p>  
 <p>(g) <a name="fr_633"></a><a href="#fn_633"><font size="1">g</font></a> 'I do with thee (the work) that is sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati; may an embryo enter thy womb. May a child be born without deficiency, with all its limbs, not blind, not lame, not sucked out by Pi<i>s</i>â<i>k</i>as.</p>  
 <p>(h) <a name="fr_634"></a><a href="#fn_634"><font size="1">h</font></a> 'By the superior powers which the bulls shall produce for us, thereby become thou pregnant; may he be born, the most valiant of his kin.</p>  
 <p>(i) <a name="fr_635"></a><a href="#fn_635"><font size="1">i</font></a> 'Indra has laid down in the tree the embryo of the sterile cow and of the cow that prematurely produces; thereby become thou pregnant; be a well-breeding cow'</p>  
 <p>And (besides with the two Mantras), 'United are our names' (above, 24, 4), and, 'The concord of the <i>k</i>akravâka birds' (24, 6).</p>  
 <p>2. (He should cohabit with her with the formulas), 'Bhû<i>h</i>! Through Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the highest bull, I pour forth (the sperm); conceive a valiant son, N.N.! Bhuva<i>h</i>! Through Pra<i>g</i>âpati, &amp;c.Suva<i>h</i>! Through Pra<i>g</i>âpati, &amp;c.' Thus he will gain a valiant son.</p>  
 <p>3. The Mantras ought to be repeated whenever they cohabit, according to Âtreya,</p>  
 <p>4. Only the first time and after her monthly courses, according to Bâdarâya<i>n</i>a.</p>
