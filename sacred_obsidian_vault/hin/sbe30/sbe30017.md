 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 51</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 5.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="#fn_217"><font size="1">1</font></a> Now (follow) the ceremonies of the fourth day.</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_218"><font size="1">2</font></a> Having put wood on the fire, he four times sacrifices expiatory Â<i>g</i>ya oblations with (the formula), 'Agni! Thou art expiation' (MB. I, 4, 1)</p>  
 <p>3. (And with the same formula), putting instead of Agni, Vâyu, <i>K</i>andra, and Sûrya;</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_219"></a><a href="#fn_219"><font size="1">4</font></a> A fifth oblation (with the names of the four gods) together, changing (in the Mantra the singular) into the plural.</p>  
 <p>5. The remnants of each oblation he should pour into a water-pot.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_220"><font size="1">6</font></a> With that (Â<i>g</i>ya) they besmear her body, including her hair and nails, remove (that water and Â<i>g</i>ya by rubbing her), and wash her.</p>  
 <p>7. After three nights have passed, they should cohabit, according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>8. When she has had her monthly illness and the blood has ceased to flow, that is the time for cohabiting.</p>  
 <p>9. With his right hand he should touch her secret parts with the verse, 'May Vish<i>n</i>u make thy womb</p>

 <p>10. When those two verses are finished, they cohabit.</p>
