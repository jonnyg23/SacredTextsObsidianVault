 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 7, SECTION 23.</h3>  
 <p>1. (Then follow the Mantras), 'I know thee as the firm Brahman. May I become firm in this world and in this country.</p>  
 <p>'I know thee as the immovable Brahman. May I not be moved away from this world and from this country. May he who hates me, my rival, be moved away from this world and from this country.</p>  
 <p>'I know thee as the unshaken Brahman. May I not be shaken off from this world and from this country. May he who hates me, my rival, be shaken off from this world and from this country.</p>  
 <p>'I know thee as the unfailing Brahman. May I not fall from this world and from this country. May he who hates me, my rival, fall from this world and from this country.</p>  
 <p>'I know thee as the nave of the universe. May I become the nave of this country. I know thee as the centre of the universe. May I become the centre of this country. I know thee as the string that holds the universe. May I become the string that holds this country. I know thee as the pillar</p>  
 <p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 196</font></a></p>  
 <p>of the universe. May I become the pillar of this country. I know thee as the navel of the universe. May I become the navel of this country.</p>  
 <p>'As the navel is the centre of the Prâ<i>n</i>as, thus I am the navel. May hundred-and-onefold evil befall him who hates us and whom we hate; may more than hundred-and-onefold merit fall to my lot!'</p>  
 <p>2. Having spoken there with a person that he likes, and having returned to the house, he causes her to sacrifice a mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>3. The wife husks (the rice grains of which that Sthâlîpâka is prepared).</p>  
 <p>4. She cooks (that Sthâlîpâka), sprinkles (Â<i>g</i>ya) on it, takes it from the fire, sacrifices to Agni, and then sacrifices to Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_627"></a><a href="#fn_627"><font size="1">5</font></a> With (the remains of) that (Sthâlîpâka) he entertains a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a whom he reveres.</p>  
 <p>6. To that (Brâhma<i>n</i>a) he makes a present of a bull.</p>  
 <p>7. From that time he constantly sacrifices (ya<i>g</i>ate) on the days of the full and of the new moon a mess of cooked food sacred to Agni.</p>  
 <p>8. In the evening and in the morning he constantly sacrifices (<i>g</i>uhoti) with his hand (and not with the Darvî) the two following oblations of rice or of barley: 'To Agni Svâhâ! To Pra<i>g</i>âpati Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>9. Some (teachers) state that in the morning the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>former (of these oblations) should be directed to Sûrya.</p>  
 <p>10. Through a period of three nights they should eat no saline food, should sleep on the ground, wear ornaments, and should be chaste.</p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_628"></a><a href="#fn_628"><font size="1">11</font></a> In the fourth night, towards morning, he puts wood on the fire, performs the (regular) ceremonies down to the (regular) expiatory oblations, and sacrifices nine expiatory oblations (with the following Mantras):</p>

 <p><a name="fn_627"></a><a href="sbe30066.htm#fr_627">196:5</a> 5, 6. In the commentary these Sûtras are divided thus: 5. tena brâhma<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> vidyâvanta<i>m</i> parivevesh<i>t</i>i; 6. yo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>syâpa<i>k</i>ito bhavati tasmâ <i>ri</i>shabha<i>m</i> dadâti. (5. Therewith he entertains a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a. 6. To one whom he reveres, he presents a bull.) The commentator observes that some authorities make one Sûtra of the two, so that the Brâhma<i>n</i>a who receives the food and the one to whom the bull is given, would be the same person.</p>
