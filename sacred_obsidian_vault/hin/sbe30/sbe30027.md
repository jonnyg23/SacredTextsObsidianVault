 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 5.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">1</font></a> From that time he shall assume a dignified demeanour: this is in short the rule (for his behaviour).</p>  
 <p>2. Here the teachers enumerate the following (regulations).</p>  
 <p>3. Nâ<i>g</i>âtalomnyopahâsam i<i>k</i><i>kh</i>et.</p>  
 <p>4. Nor (should he wish for sport) with a girl who is the only child of her mother,</p>  
 <p>5. Nor with a woman during her courses,</p>  
 <p>6. Nor with one who descends from the same <i>Ri</i>shis.</p>  
 <p>7. Let him not eat food which has been brought by another door (than the usual),</p>  
 <p>8. Or which has been cooked twice,</p>  
 <p>9. Or which has stood over night</p>  
 <p>10. Except such as is prepared of vegetables, flesh, barley, or flour.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_86"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 86</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Let him not run while it is raining.</p>  
 <p>12. Let him not take himself his shoes in his hands (when putting them on or pulling them off).</p>  
 <p>13. Let him not look into a well.</p>  
 <p>14. Let him not gather fruits himself.</p>  
 <p>15. He should not wear a scentless wreath,</p>  
 <p>16. If it is not a wreath of gold.</p>  
 <p>17. (He should not wear a wreath) of which the expression mâlâ (garland) has been used.</p>  
 <p>18. He should cause the people to call it sra<i>g</i> (wreath). (Then he may wear it.)</p>  
 <p>19. He should avoid using the word bhadra ('blessed') without a reason.</p>  
 <p>20. <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">20</font></a> He should say (instead of it), mandra ('lovely').</p>  
 <p>21. <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_310"><font size="1">21</font></a> There are three (kinds of) Snâtakas:</p>  
 <p>22. A Vidyâsnâtaka (or a Snâtaka by knowledge), a Vratasnâtaka (or a Snâtaka by the completion of his vow), and a Vidyâvratasnâtaka (i.e. Snâtaka by both).</p>  
 <p>23. Of these the last ranks foremost; the two others are equal (to each other).</p>  
 <p>24. (A Snâtaka) should not put on a wet garment.</p>  
 <p>25. <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_311"><font size="1">25</font></a> He should not wear one garment.</p>  
 <p>26. He should not praise any person (excessively).</p>  
 <p>27. He should not speak of what he has not seen, as if he had seen it,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 87</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. Nor of what he has not heard, as if he had heard it.</p>  
 <p>29. He should give up everything that forms an impediment for his Veda-recitation.</p>  
 <p>30. He should endeavour to keep himself (pure from every defilement) like a pot of oil.</p>  
 <p>31. He should not climb a tree.</p>  
 <p>32. He should not go toward evening to another village,</p>  
 <p>33. <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_312"><font size="1">33</font></a> Nor alone,</p>  
 <p>34. Nor together with V<i>ri</i>shalas (or <i>S</i>ûdras).</p>  
 <p>35. He should not enter the village by a by-path.</p>  
 <p>36. And he should not walk without a companion.</p>  
 <p>37. These are the. observances for those who have performed the Samâvartana,</p>  
 <p>38. <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">38</font></a> And what (besides) is prescribed by <i>S</i>ish<i>t</i>as.</p>  
 <p>
