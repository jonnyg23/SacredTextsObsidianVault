 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4. <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">4</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="justify'">1. When she mounts the chariot, let him murmur the verse, 'Adorned with Ki<i>m</i><i>s</i>uka flowers, of <i>S</i>almali wood' (MB. I, 3, 11).</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">2</font></a> On the way he should address crossways, rivers and unevennesses (of the soil), big trees, and burial grounds, with (the verse), 'May no waylayers meet us' (ibid. 12).</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_214"><font size="1">3</font></a> If the axle breaks, or something that is bound gets loose, or if the chariot is overturned, or if some other accident happens, they should put wood on the fire which they carry with themselves, should</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  
 <p>make oblations (of Â<i>g</i>ya) with the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis, should procure a new piece (instead of that which has been damaged), and should besmear it with the remnants of the Â<i>g</i>ya (that has been offered), with (the verse), 'He who without binding' (Sâma-veda I, 244).</p>  
 <p>4. Having sung the Vâmadevya, he should mount (again).</p>  
 <p>5. When they have arrived, the Vâmadevya (is sung).</p>  
 <p>6. When (the bride) has reached the house, Brâhma<i>n</i>a women of good character, whose husbands and sons are living, make her descend (from the chariot), and make her sit down on a bull's hide with (the verse which the husband recites), 'Here, ye cows, bring forth calves' (MB. I, 3, 13).</p>  
 <p>7. They should place a boy in her lap.</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_215"><font size="1">8</font></a> Into the joined hands of that (boy) they should throw lotus-roots (?),</p>  
 <p>9. Or fruits.</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="#fn_216"><font size="1">10</font></a> After she has made that boy rise, she sacrifices the eight 'firm' Â<i>g</i>ya oblations with (the formula), 'Here is steadiness' (MB. I, 3, 14).</p>  
 <p>11. When she has finished, she puts a piece of wood (on the fire) and respectfully salutes the Gurus, according to seniority, with their Gotra names. Then they may do what they like.</p>
