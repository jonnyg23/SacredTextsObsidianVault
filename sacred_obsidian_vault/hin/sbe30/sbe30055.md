 <p><a name="page_170"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 170</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 4, SECTION 12.</h3>  
 <p>1. They bring him a chariot, (or) a horse, or an elephant.</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_562"></a><a href="#fn_562"><font size="1">2</font></a> 'Thou art the (Sâman called) Rathantara; thou art the Vâmadevya; thou art the B<i>ri</i>hat;' the (verse), 'The two Aṅkas, the two Nyaṅkas' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ I, 7, 7, 2); (the verse), 'May this your chariot, O A<i>s</i>vins, not suffer damage, neither in pain nor in joy. May it make its way without damage, dispersing those who infest us;' (and the formula), 'Here is holding, here is keeping asunder; here is enjoyment, here may it enjoy itself:' with (these texts) he ascends the chariot, if he enters (the village) on a chariot.</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_563"></a><a href="#fn_563"><font size="1">3</font></a> 'A horse art thou, a steed art thou'with these eleven 'horses names' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. VII, 1, 12) (he mounts) the horse, if (he intends to enter the village) on horseback.</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_564"></a><a href="#fn_564"><font size="1">4</font></a> With (the formula), 'With Indra's thunderbolt I bestride thee; carry (me); carry the time; carry me forward to bliss. An elephant art thou. The elephant's glory art thou. The elephant's splendour art thou. May I become endowed with the elephant's glory, with the elephant's splendour'(he mounts) the elephant, if (he intends to proceed to the village) on it.</p>

 <p>5. <a name="fr_565"></a><a href="#fn_565"><font size="1">5</font></a> He goes to a place where they will do honour to him.</p>  
 <p>6. With (the verse), 'May the quarters (of the horizon) stream together with me; may all delight assemble (here). May all wishes that are dear to us, come near unto us; may (our) dear (wishes) stream towards us'he worships the quarters of the horizon.</p>  
 <p>7. While approaching the person who is going to do honour to him, he looks at him with (the words), 'Glory art thou; may I become glory with thee.'</p>  
 <p>8. Then (the host who is going to offer the Argha reception to the Snâtaka), having prepared the dwelling-place (for his reception), says to him, 'The Argha (will be offered)!'</p>  
 <p>9. (The guest) replies, 'Do so!'</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_566"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">10</font></a> They prepare for him (the Madhuparka or 'honey mixture') consisting of three or of five substances.</p>  
 <p>11. The three substances are, curds, honey, and ghee.</p>  
 <p>12. The five substances are, curds, honey, ghee, water, and ground grains.</p>  
 <p>13. Having poured curds into a brass vessel, he pours honey into it, (and then the other substances stated above).</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_567"></a><a href="#fn_567"><font size="1">14</font></a> Having poured (those substances) into a smaller vessel, and having covered it with a larger (cover than the vessel is), (the host) makes (the guest) accept (the following things) separately, one after the other, viz. a bunch of grass (to sit down on),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_172"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 172</font></a></p>  
 <p>water for washing the feet, the Argha water, water for sipping, and the honey-mixture (Madhuparka).</p>  
 <p>15. <a name="fr_568"></a><a href="#fn_568"><font size="1">15</font></a> Going after (the single objects which are brought to the guest, the host) in a faultless, not faltering (?) voice, announces (each of those objects to the guest).</p>  
 <p>16. The bunch of grass (he announces by three times saying), 'The bunch of grass!'</p>  
 <p>17. <a name="fr_569"></a><a href="#fn_569"><font size="1">17</font></a> (The guest) sits down thereon facing the east, with (the formula), 'A giver of royal power art thou, a teacher's seat; may I not withdraw from thee.'</p>  
 <p>18. (The host) then utters to him the announcement, 'The water for washing the feet!'</p>  
 <p>19. <a name="fr_570"></a><a href="#fn_570"><font size="1">19</font></a> With that (water) a <i>S</i>ûdra or a <i>S</i>ûdra woman washes his feet; the left foot first for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, the right for a person of the two other castes.</p>
