 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h1 align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">ÂPASTAMBA'S YA<i>G</i><i>Ñ</i>A-PARIBHÂSHÂ-SÛTRAS. (<i>cont</i>.)</font></h1>  
 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 337</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_76">76</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXVI.</h3>  
 <p>The Ash<i>t</i>â-kapâla for Agni, the Ekâda<i>s</i>a-kapâla or Dvâda<i>s</i>a-kapâla for Indra-Agnî, form the principal acts at the Amâvâsyâ, the new moon, in the case of one who does not sacrifice with Soma.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_77">77</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXVII.</h3>  
 <p>In the case of one who sacrifices with Soma, the second principal act is the Sâ<i>m</i>nâyya (both at the full-moon and new-moon sacrifices).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_77_c">77<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>The Sâ<i>m</i>nâyya is a mixture of dadhi and payas, sour and sweet milk, and is intended for Indra or Mahendra <a name="fr_798"></a><a href="#fn_798"><font size="1">1</font></a>. It takes the place of the second Puro<i>d</i>â<i>s</i>a at the new-moon sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_78">78</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>In the case of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, who does not sacrifice with Soma, the Agnîshomîya cake is omitted.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_78_c">78<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>This rule does not seem to be accepted by all schools. It is not found in Kâtyâyana, and Hira<i>n</i>yake<i>s</i>in observes: Nâsomayâ<i>g</i>ino brâhma<i>n</i>asyâgnîshomîya<i>h</i> puro<i>d</i>â<i>s</i>o vidyata ity ekeshâm. See Hillebrandt, l.c. p. iii.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_79">79</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXIX.</h3>  
 <p>Without distinction of caste, the Aindrâgna offering is omitted for one who offers the Sâ<i>m</i>nâyya.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_79_c">79<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>Even though he be not a Somayâ<i>g</i>in, says the commentary.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 338</font></a></p>  
 <p>This whole matter is summed up in Kapardin's commentary: Amâvâsyâyâm asomayâ<i>g</i>ina aindrâgna-sâ<i>m</i>nâyyayor vikalpa<i>h</i>. Paur<i>n</i>amâsyâ<i>m</i> tv asomayâ<i>g</i>ino brâhma<i>n</i>asyâgnîshomîyayâgâbhâva<i>h</i>. Tadrahitâpi paur<i>n</i>amâsî purushârtha<i>m</i> sâdhayati. Tatra dvayor eva hi yâgayo<i>h</i> paur<i>n</i>amâsî<i>s</i>abdavâ<i>k</i>yatvam asti, pratyeka<i>m</i> nâmayogât. Tasmâd agnîshomîyayâgarahitâv evetarau purushârtha<i>m</i> sâdhayata<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_80">80</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXX.</h3>  
 <p>The Pit<i>ri</i>-ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a, the sacrifice to the fathers, is not Aṅga (auxiliary) because its own time is prescribed.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_80_c">80<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>The text should be pit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a<i>h</i> svakâlavidhânâd anaṅga<i>h</i> syât. This sacrifice for the Manes, called also the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-pit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a, falls under the new-moon sacrifice, but is to be considered as a pradhâna, a primary sacrifice, not as an aṅga, a member of the Dar<i>s</i>a.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_81">81</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXI.</h3>  
 <p>Also, because it is enumerated like the Dar<i>s</i>apûr<i>n</i>amâsa sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_81_c">81<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>This refers to such passages from the Brâhma<i>n</i>as as: There are four great sacrifices, the Agnihotram, the Dar<i>s</i>apûr<i>n</i>amâsau, the <i>K</i>âturmâsyâni, and the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-pit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_82">82</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXII.</h3>  
 <p>Also, because, when the Amâvâsyâ sacrifice is barred, the Pit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a is seen to take place.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_83">83</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXIII.</h3>  
 <p>A principal act (pradhâna) is accompanied by auxiliary acts (aṅga).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 339</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_83_c">83<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>This Sûtra forms sometimes part of the preceding Sûtra, and would then refer to the Pit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a only.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_84">84</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXIV.</h3>  
 <p>A principal act is what has its own name, and is prescribed with special reference to place, time, and performer.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_84_c">84<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>This Sûtra is sometimes divided into two; the first, dese kâle kartarîti nirdi<i>s</i>yate, the second, asva<i>s</i>abda<i>m</i> yat. The following are given as illustrations. If it is said that 'he should sacrifice with the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva on a slope inclined to the East,' we have the locality. If it is said that 'he should sacrifice with the Vâ<i>g</i>apeya in autumn,' we have the time. If it is said that 'the sacrificer himself should offer the Agnihotra on a parvan (change of the moon),' we have the performer. In each of these cases, therefore, the prescribed sacrificial act is a pradhâna sâṅgam, a principal act with auxiliary members.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_85">85</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXV.</h3>  
 <p>The Darvi-homa (libation from a ladle) stands by itself.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_85_c">85<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>Apûrva is explained by the commentator, not in its usual sense of miraculous, but as not being subject to the former regulations.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_86">86</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXVI.</h3>  
 <p>They are ordered by the word <i>g</i>uhoti, he pours out.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_87">87</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXVII.</h3>  
 <p>They are offered with the word Svâhâ.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_87_c">87<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>According to Kâtyâyana I, 2, 6-7, the <i>g</i>uhotis are</p>  
 <p><a name="page_340"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 340</font></a></p>  
 <p>offered sitting, the ya<i>g</i>atis standing. See Sûtra XCII. The <i>g</i>uhoti acts consist in pouring melted butter into the fire of the Âhavanîya altar, which is so called because 'âhûyante<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>sminn âhutaya<i>h</i> kshipyanta iti.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_88">88</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>Taking (the butter) once.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_89">89</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA LXXXIX.</h3>  
 <p>Or, if there are several Âhutis, taking (the butter) for each Âhuti.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_90">90</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XC.</h3>  
 <p>Or, doing as he likes in dividing (the butter).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_90_c">90<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>These three Sûtras belong together. They teach that one slice (avadâna) of butter should be taken, melted, and poured on the Âhavanîya fire; or, if there are more than one âhuti, then one slice should be taken for each. This, however, is made optional again by the last Sûtra.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_91">91</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCI.</h3>  
 <p>There is no fuel (in the Darvi-homa), except at the Agnihotra.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_91_c">91<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>In the case of the Agnihotra it is distinctly stated, dve samidhâv âdadhyât, let him lay down two sticks.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_92">92</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCII.</h3>  
 <p>One pours out (<i>g</i>uhoti) the Darvi-homas, sitting west of the Âhavanîya fire, and bending the right knee, or not bending it.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_93">93</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCIII.</h3>  
 <p>If it is distinctly stated, it is done in a different way.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_341"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 341</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_93_c">93<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>The vidhi, contained in Sûtra XCII, is therefore called autsargika, general, and liable to exceptions, as when it is said, that he turns to the east.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_94">94</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCIV.</h3>  
 <p>One pours out (<i>g</i>uhoti) all âhutis, west of the Âhavanîya fire, passing (the altar) southward, and then turning to the north.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_95">95</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCV.</h3>  
 <p>The Â<i>s</i>ruta and Pratyâ<i>s</i>ruta, the Yâ<i>g</i>yâ and Anuvâkyâ, the Upastara<i>n</i>a and Abhighâra<i>n</i>a, with the slicings, the <i>K</i>aturg<i>ri</i>hîta also, and the Vasha<i>t</i>kâra constitute the Darvi-homas.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_95_c">95<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>The Â<i>s</i>ruta is â <i>s</i>râvaya; the Pratyâ<i>s</i>ruta, astu <i>s</i>rausha<i>t</i>; Anuvâkyâ and Yâ<i>g</i>yâ are verses, the first inviting the deity, the second accompanying the sacrifice. Whenever vegetable, animal, or sâ<i>m</i>nâyya offerings have to be. sliced, upastara<i>n</i>a, spreading, and abhighâra<i>n</i>a, sprinkling with fat, take place. With â<i>g</i>ya offerings there is <i>K</i>aturg<i>ri</i>hîta (taking four times), and the Vasha<i>t</i>kâra.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_96">96</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCVI.</h3>  
 <p>With âhutis one should let the act (the pouring out) take place after the Vasha<i>t</i>kâra has been made, or while it is being made.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_96_c">96<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>The Vasha<i>t</i>kâra consists in the word Vasha<i>t</i>, to be uttered by the Hot<i>ri</i>-priest. The five sacrificial interjections are, svâhâ, srausha<i>t</i>, vausha<i>t</i>, vasha<i>t</i>, and svadhâ.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_97">97</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCVII.</h3>  
 <p>With the Grahas the act should be made to coincide with the Upayâma.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 342</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_97_c">97<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>Grahas are offerings of Soma, and likewise the vessels (kamasa) in which the Soma is offered. The Soma is offered with the words upayâma-g<i>ri</i>hîto<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>si, and while these words are being uttered, the fluid should be poured out (dhârâ<i>m</i> srâvayet).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_98">98</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCVIII.</h3>  
 <p>With the Ish<i>t</i>akâs, the act should be made to coincide with the words tayâ deva tena.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_98_c">98<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>When the different ish<i>t</i>akâs or bricks are placed together for building an altar, &amp;c., the act itself begins with the first and ends with the last words of the accompanying verse.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_99">99</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA XCIX.</h3>  
 <p>When there is a number of Puro<i>d</i>â<i>s</i>as, one should slice off one after another, saying for each portion vyâvartadhvam (separate)!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_99_c">99<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>Puro<i>d</i>â<i>s</i>a is a cake made of meal (pakva<i>h</i> pish<i>t</i>api<i>n</i><i>d</i>a<i>h</i>), different from karu, which is more of a pulse consisting of grains of rice or barley, and clarified butter (gh<i>ri</i>tata<i>n</i><i>d</i>ulobhayâtmakam). This puro<i>d</i>â<i>s</i>a cake has to be divided for presentation to different deities. If there are more than two deities, the plural vyâvartadhvam, separate, has to be used.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_100">100</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SÛTRA C.</h3>  
 <p>When the two last are sliced off, he should say for each portion, vyâvartethâm, separate ye two!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_100_c">100<sup>c</sup></a></font></span></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>Commentary.</b></p>  
 <p>Each slice, avadâna, is said to be about a thumb's breadth. In the case of sâ<i>m</i>nâyya, the mixture of sour and sweet milk, a kind of coagulated sour milk, each portion is to be of the same breadth, but, as it is fluid, it is</p>  
 <p><a name="page_343"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 343</font></a></p>  
 <p>taken out with a ladle (sruva) of a corresponding size; see Kâtyâyana I, 9, 7.</p>
