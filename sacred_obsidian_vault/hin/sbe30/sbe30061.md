 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 5, SECTION 18.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_606"></a><a href="#fn_606"><font size="1">1</font></a> 'May Indra and Agni make you go. May the two A<i>s</i>vins protect you. B<i>ri</i>haspati is your herdsman. May Pûshan drive you back again'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 185</font></a></p>  
 <p>this (verse) he recites over the cows when they go away (to their pasture-grounds), and (the verse), 'May Pûshan go after our cows' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. IV, 1, 11, 2).</p>  
 <p>2. With (the verse), 'These cows that have come hither, free from disease and prolific, may they swim (full of wealth) like rivers; may they pour out (wealth), as (rivers discharge their floods) into the ocean'he looks at the cows, when they are coming back.</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_607"></a><a href="#fn_607"><font size="1">3</font></a> With (the formula), 'You are a stand at rest; may I (?) become your stand at rest. You are immovable. Do not move from me. May I not move from you, the blessed ones'(he looks at them) when they are standing still.</p>  
 <p>4. With (the formula), 'I see you full of sap. Full of sap you shall see me'(he looks at them) when they are gone into the stable, and with (the formula), 'May I be prosperous through your thousandfold prospering.'</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_608"></a><a href="#fn_608"><font size="1">5</font></a> Then having put wood on the fire amid the cows, and having performed the rites down to the Vyâh<i>ri</i>ti (oblations), he makes oblations of milk with (the verses),</p>  
 <p>'Blaze brightly, O <i>G</i>âtavedas, driving destruction away from me. Bring me cattle and maintenance from all quarters of the heaven. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'May <i>G</i>âtavedas do no harm to us, to cows and horses, to men and to all that moves. Come hither,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 186</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Agni, fearlessly; make me attain to welfare! Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>And with (the two verses), 'This is the influx of the waters,' and 'Adoration to thee, the rapid one, the shining one' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. IV, 6, 1, 3).</p>  
 <p>6. Then follow oblations with the verses), 'This, O Varu<i>n</i>a' (&amp;c.; see I, 2, 8, 16, down to the end of the Sûtra).</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Fifth Pa<i>t</i>ala.</p>
