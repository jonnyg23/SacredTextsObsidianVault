 <p><a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 251</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">G<i>RI</i>HYA-SÛTRA OF ÂPASTAMBA,</h1>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 1, SECTION 1.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">1</font></a> Now (follow) the ceremonies (the knowledge of) which is derived from practice (and not from the <i>S</i>ruti).</p>  
 <p>2. They should be performed during the northern course of the sun, on days of the first fortnight (of the month), on auspicious days,</p>  
 <p>3. With the sacrificial cord suspended over (the sacrificer's) left shoulder.</p>  
 <p>4. (The rites should be performed) from left to right.</p>  
 <p>5. The beginning should be made on the east side or on the north side,</p>  
 <p>6. And also the end.</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">7</font></a> Ceremonies belonging to the Fathers (are performed) in the second fortnight (of the month),</p>  
 <p>8. With the sacrificial cord suspended over the right shoulder,</p>  
 <p>9. From right to left,</p>  
 <p>10. Ending in the south.</p>  
 <p>11. Ceremonies occasioned by special occurrences (are performed) according as their occasions demand.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 252</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">12</font></a> Having set the fire in a blaze, he strews eastward-pointed Darbha grass around it,</p>  
 <p>13. Or eastward-pointed and northward-pointed (grass);</p>  
 <p>14. Southward-pointed at sacrifices to the Fathers,</p>  
 <p>15. Or southward-pointed and eastward-pointed.</p>  
 <p>16. To the north of the fire he strews Darbha grass and (on that) he places the vessels (required for sacrifice) upside-down, two by two, if referring to ceremonies directed to the gods,</p>  
 <p>17. All at once, if to men,</p>  
 <p>18. One by one, if to the Fathers.</p>  
 <p>19. <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">19</font></a> The preparation of the (blades used as) 'purifiers,' the measure of their length, the preparation of the Proksha<i>n</i>î water, and the sprinkling of the vessels are the same here as at the sacrifices of the new and full moon, (but are performed) in silence.</p>  
 <p>20. <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">20</font></a> To the west of the fire he pours water into a vessel over which he has laid (two grass blades called) purifiers, purifies (the water) three times with two northward-pointed purifiers, holds it on a level with his nose and mouth, places it to the north of the fire on Darbha grass, and covers it with Darbha grass.</p>  
 <p>21. <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">21</font></a> On the south side he causes a Brâhma<i>n</i>a to sit down on Darbha grass.</p>  
 <p>22. He melts the Â<i>g</i>ya, pours it, to the west of the fire, into the Â<i>g</i>ya-pot, over which he has laid two purifiers, draws coals (out of the sacrificial fire) towards the mirth, puts (the Â<i>g</i>ya) on them, throws</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_253"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 253</font></a></p>  
 <p>light on it by means of a burning (grass-blade), throws two Darbha points into it, moves a firebrand round it three times, takes it from the fire towards the north, sweeps the coals back (into the fire), purifies (the Â<i>g</i>ya) three times with two northward-pointed purifiers, moving them backward and forward, and throws the purifiers into the fire.</p>
