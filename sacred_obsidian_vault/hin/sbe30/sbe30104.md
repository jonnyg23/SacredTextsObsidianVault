 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 4, SECTION 11.</h3>  
 <p>1. The boy says, 'I am come to be a student' (II, 3, 26).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 273</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. The other (i.e. the teacher) has to ask; the boy has to answer (II, 3, 27-30).</p>  
 <p>3. The other murmurs the rest (of the Anuvâka),</p>  
 <p>4. And causes the boy to repeat (the Mantra) which contains wishes for himself (II, 3, 32).</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">5</font></a> (The rites) down to the Â<i>g</i>yabhâgas have been prescribed.</p>  
 <p>6. Having then caused him to sacrifice the oblations (indicated in the) next (Mantras; M. II, 4, 1-11), he enters upon (the performance) of the <i>G</i>aya and following oblations.</p>  
 <p>7. Having performed (the rites) down to the sprinkling (of water) round (the fire), he puts down, to the west of the fire, a bunch of northward-pointed grass; on that (the teacher) who performs the initiation, sits down with the next Ya<i>g</i>us (M. II, 4, 12).</p>  
 <p>8. The boy, sitting to the east (of him), facing the west, seizes with his right hand (the teacher's) right foot and says, 'Recite the Sâvitrî, Sir!'</p>  
 <p>9. He recites (the Sâvitrî) to him, 'That (glorious splendour) of Savit<i>ri</i>' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. I, 5, 6, 4; M. 4, 13);</p>  
 <p>10. Pâda by Pâda, hemistich by hemistich, and the whole (verse).</p>  
 <p>11. (When repeating the Sâvitrî Pâda by Pâda, he pronounces) the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis singly at the beginning or at the end of the Pâdas;</p>  
 <p>12. In the same way (the first and the second Vyâh<i>ri</i>ti at the beginning or at the end) of the hemistichs; the last (Vyâh<i>ri</i>ti, when he repeats) the whole verse.</p>  
 <p>13. With the next Mantra (M. II, 4, 14) the boy touches his upper lip;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_274"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 274</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. With the next (II, 4, 15) both his ears;</p>  
 <p>15. With the next (II, 5, 1) he takes up the staff.</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">16</font></a> The staff of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a is made of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood, that of a Râ<i>g</i>anya of a branch of the Nyagrodha tree, so that the downward-turned end (of the branch) forms the tip (of the staff), that of a Vai<i>s</i>ya of Bâdara or Udumbara wood.</p>  
 <p>17. Some state (only), without any reference to caste, that the staff should be made of the wood of a tree:</p>  
 <p>18. After (the teacher) has made him repeat (the formula), 'My memory' (M. II, 5, 2), and he has bestowed an optional gift on his teacher, and (the teacher) has made him arise with (the formula, M. II, 5, 3), 'Up, with life!' (the student) worships the sun with the next (Mantras; II, 5, 4).</p>  
 <p>19. If (the teacher) wishes, 'May this (student) not be estranged from me,' let him take (the student) by the right hand with the next (verse; II, 5, 6).</p>  
 <p>20. They keep that fire (used at the Upanayana) three days,</p>  
 <p>21. And (during that time) salted and pungent food should be avoided.</p>  
 <p>22. Having wiped (with his hand wet) around (the fire) with (the formula), 'Around thee' (M. II, 6, 1), he should put (twelve) pieces of wood on that (fire) with the next Mantras (II, 6, 2-13).</p>  
 <p>23. In the same way also on another (fire, when the Upanayana fire is kept no longer),</p>  
 <p>24. Fetching fuel regularly from the forest.</p>  
 <p>25. With the next (formulaM. II, 6, 14the teacher) instructs (the student in his duties).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_275"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 275</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_762"><font size="1">26</font></a> On the fourth day (after the Upanayana the teacher) takes the garment (of the student) for himself with the next (verse; M. II, 6, 15), having made him put on another (garment).</p>
