 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_387"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">1</font></a> By (the description of) the Sthâlîpâka offered at the Anvash<i>t</i>akya ceremony the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>apit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a has been declared;</p>  
 <p>2. This is a <i>S</i>râddha offered on the day of the new moon.</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="#fn_388"><font size="1">3</font></a> Another (<i>S</i>râddha) is the Anvâhârya.</p>  
 <p>4. (It is performed) monthly.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_389"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">5</font></a> The Havis is prepared (by one who has set up the sacred <i>S</i>rauta fires) in the Dakshi<i>n</i>âgni (i.e. in that of the three fires which is situated towards the south).</p>  
 <p>6. And from the same (fire the fire is taken which)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 112</font></a></p>  
 <p>is carried forward (in order to be used at the ceremonies).</p>  
 <p>7. In the domestic fire (the Havis is prepared) by one who has not set up the (<i>S</i>rauta) fires.</p>  
 <p>8. One pit (only is made);</p>  
 <p>9. <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">9</font></a> To the south of it the fire has its place.</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">10</font></a> Here the laying down of the fire-brand is omitted,</p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">11</font></a> And (the spreading out of) the layer (of grass),</p>  
 <p>12. <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">12</font></a> And the anointing (of the bunches of Darbha grass), and the anointing (of the Fathers),</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">13</font></a> And the (offering of) perfume,</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">14</font></a> And the ceremony of deprecation.</p>  
 <p>15. <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">15</font></a> (The ceremony performed with) the vessel of water forms the conclusion (of the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>apit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a).</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">16</font></a> He should, however, put down one garment (for the Fathers in common).</p>  
 <p>17. <a name="fr_398"></a><a href="#fn_398"><font size="1">17</font></a> On the eighth day after the full moon of Mâgha a Sthâlîpâka (is prepared).</p>  
 <p>18. He should sacrifice of that (Sthâlîpâka).</p>  
 <p>19. 'To the Ash<i>t</i>akâ Svâhâ!'with (these words) he sacrifices.</p>  
 <p>20. <a name="fr_399"></a><a href="#fn_399"><font size="1">20</font></a> The rest (should be performed) according to the Sthâlîpâka rite.</p>  
 <p>21. <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_400"><font size="1">21</font></a> Vegetables (are taken instead of meat) as ingredient to the Anvâhârya(-rice).</p>  
 <p>22. At animal sacrifices offered to the Fathers let</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 113</font></a></p>  
 <p>him sacrifice the omentum with (the verse), 'Carry the omentum, O <i>G</i>âtavedas, to the Fathers' (MB. II, 3, 16);</p>  
 <p>23. At (such sacrifices) offered to the gods, with (the verse), '<i>G</i>âtavedas, go to the gods with the omentum' (ibid. 17).</p>  
 <p>24. If no (god to whom the sacrifice should be offered, and no Mantra with which the oblation should be made) is known, he sacrifices, assigning (his offering to the personified rite which he is performing), thus as (for instance), 'To the Ash<i>t</i>akâ Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>25. <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_401"><font size="1">25</font></a> The rest (should be performed) according to the Sthâlîpâka rite.</p>  
 <p>26. <a name="fr_402"></a><a href="#fn_402"><font size="1">26</font></a> If a debt turns up (which he cannot pay), he should sacrifice with the middle leaf of Golakas, with (the verse), 'The debt which' (MB. II, 3, 18).</p>  
 <p>27. Now (follows) the putting into motion of the plough.</p>  
 <p>28. Under an auspicious Nakshatra he should cook a mess of sacrificial food and should sacrifice to the following deities, namely, to Indra, to the Maruts, to Par<i>g</i>anya, to A<i>s</i>ani, to Bhaga.</p>  
 <p>29. <a name="fr_403"></a><a href="#fn_403"><font size="1">29</font></a> And he should offer (Â<i>g</i>ya) to Sîtâ, Â<i>s</i>â, Ara<i>d</i>â, Anaghâ.</p>  
 <p>30. The same deities (receive offerings) at the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_114"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 114</font></a></p>  
 <p>furrow-sacrifice, at the thrashing-floor-sacrifice, at the sowing, at the reaping of the crop, and at the putting of the crop into the barn.</p>  
 <p>31. And at mole hills he should sacrifice to the king of moles.</p>  
 <p>32. <a name="fr_404"></a><a href="#fn_404"><font size="1">32</font></a> To Indrâ<i>n</i>î a Sthâlîpâka (is prepared).</p>  
 <p>33. Of that he should make an offering with (the verse), 'The Ekâsh<i>t</i>akâ, performing austerities' (MB. II, 3, 19).</p>  
 <p>34. The rest (should be performed) according to the Sthâlîpâka ritual. The rest according to the Sthâlîpâka ritual.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_387"></a><a href="sbe30036.htm#fr_387">111:1</a> 4, 1. Khâdira-G<i>ri</i>hya III, 5, 35. Comp. M.M., 'India, what can it teach us?' p. 240. The word Sthâlîpâka is used here, as is observed in the commentary, in order to exclude the mess of meat (chap. 2, 14) from the rites of the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>apit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_388"></a><a href="sbe30036.htm#fr_388">111:3</a> Anvâhârya literally means, what is offered (or given) after something else, supplementary. In the commentary on Gobhila, p. 666, a verse is quoted:</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">amâvâsyâ<i>m</i> dvitîya<i>m</i> yad anvâhârya<i>m</i> tad u<i>k</i>yate,</div>  
 <p>'The second (<i>S</i>râddha) which is performed on the day of the new moon, that is called anvâhârya.' Comp. Manu III, 122. 123, and Kullûka's note; M.M., 'India, what can it teach us?' p. 200.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_404"></a><a href="sbe30036.htm#fr_404">114:32</a> 32-34. Khâdira-G<i>ri</i>hya III, 5, 40. I understand that this sacrifice stands in connection with the rural festivals which are treated of in the preceding Sûtras. In the commentary, from the mantra the conclusion is drawn that the ceremony in question belongs to the day of the Ekâsh<i>t</i>akâ. But the Ekâsh<i>t</i>akâ is the Ash<i>t</i>akâ of the dark fortnight of Mâgha (see S.B.E. XXIX, 102), and the description of the rites belonging to that day has already been given above, Sûtras 17-21. It very frequently occurs in the G<i>ri</i>hya ritual that Mantras are used at sacrifices standing in no connection with those for which they have originally been composed.</p>
