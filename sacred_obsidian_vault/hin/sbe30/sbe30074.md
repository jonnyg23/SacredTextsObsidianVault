 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 1, SECTION 2.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now (follows) the Pu<i>m</i>savana (i.e. the ceremony for securing the birth of a male child).</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_646"></a><a href="#fn_646"><font size="1">2</font></a> In the third month, in the fortnight of the increasing moon, under an auspicious constellation (&amp;c.; see the preceding section, Sûtras 2 and 3, down to:) in a round apartment. He gives her a barley-grain in her right hand with (the formula), 'A man art thou;'</p>  
 <p>3. With (the formula), 'The two testicles are ye,' two mustard seeds or two beans, on both sides of that barley-grain.</p>  
 <p>4. With (the formula), '<i>S</i>vâv<i>ri</i>tat' (? <i>s</i>vâv<i>ri</i>ttat?) (he pours) a drop of curds (on those grains). That he gives her to eat.</p>  
 <p>5. After she has sipped water, he touches her belly with (the formula), 'With my ten (fingers) I touch. thee that thou mayst give birth to a child after ten months.'</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_647"></a><a href="#fn_647"><font size="1">6</font></a> (He pounds) the last shoot of a Nyagrodha trunk (and mixes the powder) with ghee, or a silkworm (and mixes the powder) with a pap prepared of panick seeds, or a splinter of a sacrificial post taken from the north-easterly part (of that post) exposed to the fire, or (he takes ashes or soot [?] of)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>  
 <p>a fire that has been kindled by attrition, and inserts that into the right nostril of (the wife) whose head rests on the widely spread root (of an Udumbara tree?).</p>  
 <p>7. If she miscarries, he should three times stroke (her body), from the navel upwards, with her wet hand, with (the formula), 'Thitherwards, not hitherwards, may Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> bind thee in his bonds. Making (the mother) enter upon the seasons, live ten months (in thy mother's womb); do not bring death to men.'</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_648"></a><a href="#fn_648"><font size="1">8</font></a> When her confinement has come, he performs the kshipraprasavana (i.e. the ceremony for accelerating the confinement). Having placed a water-pot near her head and a Tûryantî plant near her feet, he touches her belly.</p>
