 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 3, SECTION 7.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_741"><font size="1">1</font></a> He then makes her offer the sacrifice of a Sthâlîpâka sacred to Agni.</p>  
 <p>2. The wife husks (the rice grains out of which this Sthâlîpâka is prepared).</p>  
 <p>3. After he has cooked (the Sthâlîpâka), and has sprinkled (Â<i>g</i>ya) over it, and has taken it from the fire towards the east or the north, and has sprinkled (Â<i>g</i>ya) over it while it stands (there near the fire), (the ceremonies) from the putting of wood on the fire down to the Â<i>g</i>yabhâga oblations (are performed), and while she takes hold of him, he sacrifices of that Sthâlîpâka.</p>  
 <p>4. The 'spreading under' and the sprinkling over (of Â<i>g</i>ya are done) once; two Avadânas (or cut-off portions are taken).</p>  
 <p>5. Agni is the deity (of the first oblation); the offering is made with the word Svâhâ.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_742"><font size="1">6</font></a> Or he may sacrifice after having picked out, once, a portion (of the sacrificial food with the Darvi spoon).</p>  
 <p>7. Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t is the second (deity).</p>  
 <p>8. (At the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation) the 'spreading under' and taking an Avadâna are done once, the sprinkling over (of Â<i>g</i>ya) twice.</p>  
 <p>9. The Avadâna for the first deity (is taken) out of the middle (of the Sthâlîpâka);</p>  
 <p>10. It is offered over the centre (of the fire).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 265</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (The Avadâna) for the second (deity is taken) from the northern part (of the Sthâlîpâka);</p>  
 <p>12. It is offered over the easterly part of the northerly part (of the fire).</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_743"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">13</font></a> Having silently anointed (a part of) the Barhis (by dipping it) into the remains both (of the Sthâlîpâka and the Â<i>g</i>ya) in the way prescribed (in the <i>S</i>rauta ritual) for the (part of the Barhis called) Prastara, he throws (that part of the Barhis) into the fire.</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_744"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">14</font></a> (The rule regarding) the second sprinkling (of water round the fire) is valid (here).</p>  
 <p>15. He gives (the remains of) that (sacrificial food) with butter to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a to eat</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_745"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">16</font></a> Whom he reveres. To that (Brâhma<i>n</i>a) he makes the present of a bull.</p>  
 <p>17. In the same way, with the exception of the sacrificial gift, they should sacrifice a Sthâlîpâka from then onwards, on the days of the new and full moon, after having fasted.</p>  
 <p>18. Some say that a vessel full (of grain) is the sacrificial gift.</p>  
 <p>19. <a name="fr_746"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">19</font></a> From then onwards he should offer morning and evening with his hand these two oblations (to Agni and to Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t) of (rice) grains or of barley.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_266"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 266</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. The deities are the same as at the Sthâlîpâka (just described).</p>  
 <p>21. Some say that the first oblation in the morning is sacred to Sûrya.</p>  
 <p>22. <a name="fr_747"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">22</font></a> Before and after (those oblations) the sprinkling (of water) round (the fire is performed) as stated above.</p>  
 <p>23. <a name="fr_748"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">23</font></a> By the sacrifice of the new and full moon the other ceremonies have been explained (the knowledge of) which is derived from practice.</p>  
 <p>24. The deities (of those rites) are as stated (with regard to each particular case), having their place between Agni (Sûtra 5) and Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t (Sûtra 7).</p>  
 <p>25. <a name="fr_749"></a><a href="#fn_749"><font size="1">25</font></a> The sacrifice (of a cow) on the arrival of a guest (should be performed as stated below) without alterations.</p>  
 <p>26. <a name="fr_750"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">26</font></a> (The deities) of the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva ceremony are the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs,</p>  
 <p>27. <a name="fr_751"></a><a href="#fn_751"><font size="1">27</font></a> Of ceremonies performed on full-moon days, the full-moon day on which they are performed.</p>
