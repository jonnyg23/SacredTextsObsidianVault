 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 6.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_415"></a><a href="#fn_415"><font size="1">1</font></a> Let him daily repeat (the formula), 'Bhû<i>h</i>!' (MB. II, 4, 14) in order to avert involuntary death.</p>  
 <p>2. (He who does so) has nothing to fear from serious diseases or from sorcery.</p>  
 <p>3. (The ceremony for) driving away misfortune (is as follows).</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_416"></a><a href="#fn_416"><font size="1">4</font></a> It is performed on the sacrificial day (i.e. on the first day of the fortnight).</p>  
 <p>5. (Oblations are made with the six verses), 'From the head' (MB. II, 5, 1 seq.), verse by verse.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_417"></a><a href="#fn_417"><font size="1">6</font></a> The seventh (verse is), 'She who athwart' (MB. I, 5, 6).</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_418"></a><a href="#fn_418"><font size="1">7</font></a> (Then follow) the verses of the Vâmadevya,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 119</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. (And) the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis.</p>  
 <p>9. The last (verse) is, 'Pra<i>g</i>âpati' (MB. II, 5, 8).</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_419"></a><a href="#fn_419"><font size="1">10</font></a> With the formula, 'I am glory' (MB. II, 5, 9) one who is desirous of glory should worship the sun in the forenoon, at noon, and in the afternoon,</p>  
 <p>11. Changing (the words), 'of the forenoon' (into 'of the noon,' and 'of the afternoon,' accordingly).</p>  
 <p>12. Worshipping (the sun) at the time of the morning twilight and of the evening twilight procures happiness, (both times) with (the formula), 'O sun! the ship' (MB. II, 5, 14), and (after that) in the morning with (the formula), 'When thou risest, O sun, I shall rise with thee' (ibid. 15); in the evening with (the formula), 'When thou goest to rest, O sun, I shall go to rest with thee' (ibid. 16).</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_420"></a><a href="#fn_420"><font size="1">13</font></a> One who desires to gain a hundred cart-loads (of gold), should keep the vow (of fasting) through one fortnight and should on the first day of a dark fortnight feed the Brâhma<i>n</i>as with boiled milk-rice prepared of one Kâ<i>m</i>sa of rice.</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_421"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">14</font></a> At the evening twilight (of every day of that fortnight), having left the village in a westerly direction, and having put wood on the fire at a place where</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_120"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 120</font></a></p>  
 <p>four roads meet, he should sacrifice the small grains (of that rice), turning his face towards the sun, with (the words), 'To Bhala Svâhâ! To Bhala Svâhâ!' (ibid. 17. 18).</p>  
 <p>15. (He should repeat those rites) in the same way the two next dark fortnights.</p>  
 <p>16. During the time between those dark fortnights he should observe chastity till the end (of the rite), till the end (of the rite).</p>
