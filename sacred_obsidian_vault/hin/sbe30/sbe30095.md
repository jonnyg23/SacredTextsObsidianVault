 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 1, SECTION 2.</h3>  
 <p>1. He warms at the fire the implement with which he sacrifices, wipes it off with Darbha blades, warms it again, sprinkles it (with water), puts it down, touches the Darbha blades with water, and throws them into the fire.</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">2</font></a> As paridhis (or pieces of wood laid round the fire) yoke-pins are used at the marriage, the Upanayana, the Samâvartana, the parting of the (wife's) hair, the tonsure of the child's hair, the cutting of the beard, and at expiatory ceremonies.</p>  
 <p>3. He sprinkles water round the fire, on the south side from west to east with (the words), 'Aditi, give thy consent!' on the west side from south to north with 'Anumati, give thy consent!' on the north side from west to east with 'Sarasvatî [sic], give thy consent!' all around with 'God Sâvitrî, give thy impulse!'</p>  
 <p>4. At ceremonies belonging to the Fathers (water is sprinkled) only all round (the fire), silently.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">5</font></a> Having put a piece of wood on the fire, he</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_254"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 254</font></a></p>  
 <p>offers the two Âghâra oblations as at the sacrifices of the new and full moon, silently.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">6</font></a> Then he offers the two Â<i>g</i>yabhâga oblations, over the easterly part of the northerly part (of the fire) with (the words), 'To Agni Svâhâ!' over the easterly part of the southerly part (another oblation) exactly like the preceding one, with (the words), 'To Soma Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">7</font></a> Having offered the chief oblations (belonging to each sacrifice) according to prescription, he adds the following oblations, viz. the <i>G</i>aya, Abhyâtâna, Râsh<i>t</i>rabh<i>ri</i>t oblations, the oblation to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis one by one, the oblation to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t with (the following formula), 'What I have done too much in this ceremony, or what I have done here too little, all that may Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t, he who knows, make well sacrificed and well offered. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">8</font></a> The sprinkling (of water) round (the fire is repeated) as above; the Mantras are altered so as to say, 'Thou hast given thy consent,' 'Thou hast given thy impulse.'</p>  
 <p>9. The designation 'Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a' is used of ceremonies connected with worldly life.</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">10</font></a> There the ritual based on the Brâhma<i>n</i>a (holds good),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_255"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 255</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_724"></a><a href="#fn_724"><font size="1">11</font></a> (To which the words allude), 'He sacrifices twice; he wipes off (his hand) twice; he partakes twice (of the sacrificial food); having gone away he sips (out of the Sru<i>k</i>) and licks off (the Sru<i>k</i>).'</p>  
 <p>12. All seasons are fit for marriage with the exception of the two months of the <i>s</i>i<i>s</i>ira season, and of the last summer month.</p>  
 <p>13. All Nakshatras which are stated to be pure, (are fit for marriage);</p>  
 <p>14. And all auspicious performances.</p>  
 <p>15. And one should learn from women what ceremonies (are required by custom).</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_725"></a><a href="#fn_725"><font size="1">16</font></a> Under the Invakâs (Nakshatra), (the wooers who go to the girl's father) are sent out: such wooers are welcome.</p>
