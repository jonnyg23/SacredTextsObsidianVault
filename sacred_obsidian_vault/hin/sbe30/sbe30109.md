 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 6, SECTION 16.</h3>  
 <p>1. In the sixth month after the child's birth he serves food to Brâhma<i>n</i>as and causes them to pronounce auspicious wishes; then he should pour together curds, honey, ghee, and boiled rice, and should give (the mixture) to the boy to eat, with the next (four) Mantras (II, 14, 7-10);</p>  
 <p>2. (He should feed him) with partridge, according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>3. In the third year after his birth the <i>K</i>aula (or tonsure is performed) under (the Nakshatra of) the two Punarvasus.</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_771"><font size="1">4</font></a> Brâhma<i>n</i>as are entertained with food as at the initiation (Upanayana).</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_772"><font size="1">5</font></a> The putting (of wood) on the fire, &amp;c. (is performed) as at the Sîmantonnayana.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">6</font></a> He makes (the boy) sit down to the west of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_284"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 284</font></a></p>  
 <p>the fire, facing the east, combs his hair silently with a porcupine's quill that has three white spots, with three Darbha blades, and with a bunch of unripe Udumbara fruits; and he arranges the locks in the fashion of his ancestral <i>Ri</i>shis,</p>  
 <p>7. Or according to their family custom.</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_774"><font size="1">8</font></a> The ceremonies beginning with the pouring together of (warm and cold) water and ending with the putting down of the hair are the same (as above; comp. M. II, 14, 11).</p>  
 <p>9. He puts down the razor after having washed it off.</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_775"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">10</font></a> The ceremony is (repeated) three days with the (same razor). (Then) the rite is finished.</p>  
 <p>11. (The father) gives an optional gift (to the Brâhma<i>n</i>a who has assisted).</p>  
 <p>12. The Godâna (or the ceremony of shaving the beard, is performed) in the sixteenth year, in exactly the same way or optionally under another constellation.</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">13</font></a> Or he may perform the Godâna sacred to Agni.</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">14</font></a> Some prescribe the keeping of a vow through one year in connection with the Godâna.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 285</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. The difference (between the Kaula and the Godâna) is that (at the Godâna) the whole hair is shaven (without leaving the locks).</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_778"><font size="1">16</font></a> According to the followers of the Sâma-veda he should 'touch water.'</p>
