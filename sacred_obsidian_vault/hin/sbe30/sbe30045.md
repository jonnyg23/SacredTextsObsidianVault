 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 1, SECTION 2.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_471"></a><a href="#fn_471"><font size="1">1</font></a> And lays the (three) pegs round (the fire).</p>  
 <p>2. On the west side (of the fire) he places the middle (peg), with its broad end to the north,</p>  
 <p>3. On the south side (of the fire the second peg), so that it touches the middle one, with its broad end to the east,</p>  
 <p>4. On the north side (of the fire the third peg), so that it touches the middle one, with its broad end to the east.</p>  
 <p>5. To the west of the fire (the teacher who is going to initiate the student), sits down with his face turned towards the east.</p>  
 <p>6. To the south (of the teacher) the boy, wearing the sacrificial cord over his left shoulder, having sipped water, sits down and touches (the teacher).</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_472"></a><a href="#fn_472"><font size="1">7</font></a> Then (the teacher) sprinkles water round the fire (in the following way):</p>  
 <p>8. On the south side (of the fire he sprinkles</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 142</font></a></p>  
 <p>water) from west to east with (the words), 'Aditi! Give thy consent!'</p>  
 <p>9. On the west side, from south to north, with (the words), 'Anumati! Give thy consent!' On the north side, from west to east, with (the words), 'Sarasvatî! Give thy consent!'</p>  
 <p>10. On all sides, so as to keep his right side turned towards (the fire), with (the Mantra), 'God Savit<i>ri</i>! Give thy impulse!' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. I, 7, 7, 1).</p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_473"></a><a href="#fn_473"><font size="1">11</font></a> Having (thus) sprinkled (water) round (the fire), and having anointed the fuel (with Â<i>g</i>ya), he puts it on (the fire) with (the Mantra), 'This fuel is thy self, <i>G</i>âtavedas! Thereby thou shalt be inflamed and shalt grow. Inflame us and make us grow; through offspring, cattle, holy lustre, and through the enjoyment of food make us increase. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>12. He then sacrifices with the (spoon called) Darvi (the following oblations):</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_474"></a><a href="#fn_474"><font size="1">13</font></a> Approaching the Darvi (to the fire) by the northerly junction of the pegs (laid round the fire), and fixing his mind on (the formula), 'To Pra<i>g</i>âpati, to Manu svâhâ!' (without pronouncing that Mantra), he sacrifices a straight, long, uninterrupted (stream of Â<i>g</i>ya), directed towards the south-east.</p>  
 <p>14. Approaching the Darvi (to the fire) by the southern junction of the pegs (laid round the fire),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 143</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (he sacrifices) a straight (stream of Â<i>g</i>ya), directed towards the northeast, with (the Mantra which he pronounces), 'To Indra svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>15. Having (thus) poured out the two Âghâra oblations, he sacrifices the two Â<i>g</i>yabhâgas,</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="#fn_475"><font size="1">16</font></a> With (the words), 'To Agni svâhâ!' over the easterly part of the northerly part (of the fire); with (the words), 'To Soma svâhâ!' over the easterly part of the southerly part (of the fire).</p>  
 <p>17. <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_476"><font size="1">17</font></a> Between them he sacrifices the other (oblations).</p>  
 <p>18. <a name="fr_477"></a><a href="#fn_477"><font size="1">18</font></a> (He makes four oblations with the following Mantras): 'Thou whom we have set to work, <i>G</i>âtavedas! carry forward (our offerings). Agni! Perceive this work (i.e. the sacrifice), as it is performed (by us). Thou art a healer, a creator of medicine. Through thee may we obtain cows, horses, and men. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'Thou who liest down athwart, thinking, "It is I who keep (all things) asunder:" to thee who art propitious (to me), I sacrifice this stream of ghee in the fire. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'To the propitious goddess svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'To the accomplishing goddess svâhâ!'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_471"></a><a href="sbe30045.htm#fr_471">141:1</a> 2, 1. The 'pegs' are the pieces of wood mentioned above, I, 19.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_472"></a><a href="sbe30045.htm#fr_472">141:7</a> 7-10. Gobhila I, 3, 1 seq. The vocative Sarasvate instead of Sarasvatî is given by the MSS. also in the Khâdira-G<i>ri</i>hya 1, 2, 19.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_473"></a><a href="sbe30045.htm#fr_473">142:11</a> As to the Mantra, compare <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana II, 10, 4, &amp;c.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_474"></a><a href="sbe30045.htm#fr_474">142:13</a> 13, 14. The two oblations described in these Sûtras are the so-called Âghâras; see Sûtra 15, and Pâraskara I, 5, 3; Â<i>s</i>valâyana I, 10, 13. Regarding the northern and the southern junction of the Paridhi woods, see above, Sûtras 3 and 4. According to Mât<i>ri</i>datta, the words 'long, uninterrupted' (Sûtra 13) are to be supplied also in Sûtra 14.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_476"></a><a href="sbe30045.htm#fr_476">143:17</a> I.e. between the places at which the two 'Â<i>g</i>ya portions' are offered. Comp. <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana I, 9, 8.</p>
