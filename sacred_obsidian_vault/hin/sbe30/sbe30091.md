 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 8, SECTION 19.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_706"><font size="1">1</font></a> For Brahman, Pra<i>g</i>âpati, B<i>ri</i>haspati, Agni, Vâyu, the Sun, the Moon, the Stars, king Indra, king Yama, king Varu<i>n</i>a, king Soma, king Vai<i>s</i>rava<i>n</i>a, for the Vasus, the Rudras, the Âdityas, the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, the Sâdhyas, the <i>Ri</i>bhus, the Bh<i>ri</i>gus, the Maruts, the Atharvans, the Aṅgiras: for these divine beings.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_244"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 244</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_707"><font size="1">2</font></a> Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra, <i>G</i>amadagni, Bharadvâ<i>g</i>a and Gautama, Atri, Vasish<i>th</i>a, Ka<i>s</i>yapa: these are the seven <i>Ri</i>shis.</p>  
 <p>3. Wearing their sacrificial cords below (round their body) they arrange towards the north, at a place that is inclined towards the north, seats of northward-pointed Darbha grass, so that they end in the east, for Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra, <i>G</i>amadagni, Bharadvâ<i>g</i>a, Gautama, Atri, Vasish<i>th</i>a, Ka<i>s</i>yapa.</p>  
 <p>4. Between Vasish<i>th</i>a and Ka<i>s</i>yapa they arrange (a seat) for Arundhatî, (the wife of Vasish<i>th</i>a);</p>  
 <p>5. Towards the south, in a place inclined towards the east, for Agastya.</p>  
 <p>6. Then for the (following) teachers, ending with those who teach (only) one Veda (?), viz. for K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>a Dvaipâyana, <i>G</i>âtûka<i>n</i>ya, Taruksha, T<i>ri</i><i>n</i>abindu, Varmin, Varûthin, Vâ<i>g</i>in, Vâ<i>g</i>a<i>s</i>ravas, Satya<i>s</i>ravas, Su<i>s</i>ravas, Suta<i>s</i>ravas, Soma<i>s</i>ushmâya<i>n</i>a, Satvavat, B<i>ri</i>haduktha Vâmadev(y)a, Vâ<i>g</i>iratna, Harya<i>g</i>vâyana, Udamaya, Gautama, <i>Ri</i><i>n</i>a<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>aya, <i>Ri</i>ta<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>aya, K<i>ri</i>ta<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>aya, Dhana<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>aya, Babhru, Tryaru<i>n</i>a, Trivarsha, Tridhâtu, <i>S</i>ibinta, Parâ<i>s</i>ara, Vish<i>n</i>u, Rudra, Skanda, Kâ<i>s</i>i<i>s</i>vara, <i>G</i>vara, Dharma, Artha, Kâma, Krodha, Vasish<i>th</i>a, Indra, Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>, Kart<i>ri</i>, Dhart<i>ri</i>, Dhât<i>ri</i>, M<i>ri</i>tyu, Savit<i>ri</i>, Sâvitrî, and for each Veda, for the <i>Ri</i>g-veda, the Ya<i>g</i>ur-veda, the Sâma-veda, the Atharva-veda, the Itihâsa and Purâ<i>n</i>a.</p>  
 <p>7. Towards the south, with their sacrificial cords suspended over their right shoulders, in a place inclined towards the south, they arrange seats of southward-pointed Darbha grass, so that they end in the west</p>
