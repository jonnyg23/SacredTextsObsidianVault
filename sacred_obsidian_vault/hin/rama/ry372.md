 <h3 align="CENTER">CANTO XXXII.: SÍTÁ'S DOUBT.</h3>
 <p>
 Her eyes the Maithil lady raised<br>
 And on the monkey speaker gazed.<br>
 She looked, and trembling at the sight<br>
 Wept bitter tears in wild affright.<br>
 She shrank a while with fear distraught,<br>
 Then, nerved again, the lady thought:<br>
 'Is this a dream mine eyes have seen,<br>
 This creature, by our laws unclean?<br>
 O, may the Gods keep Ráma, still,<br>
 And Lakshman, and my sire, from ill!<br>
 It is no dream: I have not slept,<br>
 But, trouble-worn, have watched and wept<br>
 Afar from that dear lord of mine<br>
 For whom in ceaseless woe I pine,<br>
 No art may soothe my wild distress<br>
 Or lull me to forgetfulness.<br>
 I see but him: my lips can frame<br>
 No syllable but Ráma's name.<br>
 Each sight I see, each sound I hear,<br>
 Brings Ráma to mine eye or ear,<br>
 The wish was in my heart, and hence<br>
 The sweet illusion mocked my sense.<br>
 'Twas but a phantom of the mind,<br>
 And yet the voice was soft and kind<br>
 Be glory to the Eternal Sire,  <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 Be glory to the Lord of Fire,<br>
 The mighty Teacher in the skies,  <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">2</font></a><br>
 And Indra with his thousand eyes,<br>
 And may they grant the truth to be<br>
 E'en as the words that startled me.'<br>
