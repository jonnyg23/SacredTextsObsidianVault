 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 5.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1038"></a><a href="#fn_1037"><font size="1">1-12</font></a>. On the ninth or tenth (of the dark fortnight) the Anvash<i>t</i>akya (ceremony is performed).</p>  
 <p>2. To the south-east (of the house) they partition off (a place with mats or the like), and to the northern part of that (place) he should carry a fire which has been kindled by attrition.</p>  
 <p>3. Let him take one portion of rice, let him remove the husks once, and let him cook it, stirring it up from right to left.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">5, 1. navamî<i>m</i> da<i>s</i>amî<i>m</i> vânvash<i>t</i>akya<i>m</i>. 2. dakshi<i>n</i>apûrvabhâge parivârya tatrottarârdhe mathitvâgni<i>m</i> pra<i>n</i>ayet. 3. sak<i>ri</i>dg<i>ri</i>hîtân vrîhîn sak<i>ri</i>tphalîk<i>ri</i>tân prasavyam udâyuva<i>ñ</i> <i>s</i>rapayed.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_421"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 421</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. And some flesh of that thigh (see above, chap. 4, 16).</p>  
 <p>5. He should take it from the fire in a southern direction, and should omit the second sprinkling (of Â<i>g</i>ya) on it.</p>  
 <p>6. To the west of the fire he should dig, in the southern part (of the place mentioned in Sûtra 2) three pits, four inches in depth and breadth.</p>  
 <p>7. He should carry the fire to the east of them.</p>  
 <p>8. He should strew (Darbha-grass round the fire),</p>  
 <p>9. And (into) the pits.</p>  
 <p>10. Having spread out to the west of the fire a layer of southward-pointed grass so that it is inclined towards the south, he should put (a mat) on it.</p>  
 <p>11. On that (grass) he should place the sacrificial implements, one by one.</p>  
 <p>12. Cutting off (the prescribed portions of the sacrificial food, and putting those portions) into the brazen vessel he should sacrifice, picking out (portions of the Havis) with the pot-ladle, with (the formulas), 'Svâhâ to Soma Pit<i>ri</i>mat! Svâhâ to Agni Kavyavâhana!' (MB. II. 3, 1, 2.)</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_1039"></a><a href="#fn_1038"><font size="1">13-31</font></a>. With his left hand he should lay down a firebrand on the southern side of the pits (Sûtra 6), with</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">4. amushmâ<i>k</i> <i>k</i>a sakthno mâ<i>m</i>sam iti. 5. dakshi<i>n</i>odvâsya na pratyabhighârayet. 6. pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>âd agner dakshi<i>n</i>âs tisra<i>h</i> karshû<i>h</i> khanyâ<i>k</i> <i>k</i>aturaṅgulam adhas tiryak <i>k</i>a. 7. tâsâ<i>m</i> purastâd agni<i>m</i> pra<i>n</i>ayet. 8. st<i>ri</i><i>n</i>uyât. 9. karshû<i>s</i> <i>k</i>a. 10. pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>âd agne svastara<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>âgrais t<i>ri</i><i>n</i>air dakshi<i>n</i>âprava<i>n</i>am âstîrya b<i>ri</i>sîm upari nidadhyât. 11. tasminn ekaikam âharet. 12. ka<i>m</i>se samavadâya meksha<i>n</i>enopaghâta<i>m</i> <i>g</i>uhuyât svâhâ Somâya pit<i>ri</i>mate svâhâgnaye kavyavâhanâyeti. 13. savyenolmuka<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>ata<i>h</i> karshûr nidadhyâd apahatâ iti.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_422"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 422</font></a></p>  
 <p>(the formula), '(The Asuras have been) driven away (MB. II, 3, 3).</p>  
 <p>14. (He should perform the different rites) in the eastern pit for his father,</p>  
 <p>15. In the middle for his grandfather,</p>  
 <p>16. In the last for his great-grandfather.</p>  
 <p>17. Let him pour out vessels of water. from right to left, into the pits, pronouncing the name of each one (of his ancestors), with (the formula), 'N.N.! Wash thyself, and they who follow thee here, and they whom thou followest. To thee Svadhâ!'</p>  
 <p>18. In the same way he should put down the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>as for them and should murmur, 'Here, O Fathers, enjoy yourselves; show your manly vigour each for his part' (MB. II, 3, 6).</p>  
 <p>19. After he has spoken thus, he should turn to the north, doubling his left arm, turning round from right to left.</p>  
 <p>20. Holding his breath and fixing his thoughts on something good he should, while turning back (in the same way), murmur: 'The Fathers have enjoyed themselves; they have shown their manly vigour each for his part' (MB. II, 3, 7).</p>  
 <p>21. He should sprinkle collyrium on three Darbha</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">14. pûrvasyâ<i>m</i> karshvâ<i>m</i> pitur. 15. madhyamâyâ<i>m</i> pitâmahasyo. 16. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>ttamâyâ<i>m</i> prapitâmahasyo. 17. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>dapâtrâ<i>n</i>y apasalavi karshûshu ninayed ekaikasya nâmoktvâsâv avanenikshva ye <i>k</i>âtra tvânu yâ<i>m</i><i>s</i> <i>k</i>a tram anu tasmai te svâheti (correct, svadheti). 18. tathaiva pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>ân nidhâya <i>g</i>aped atra pitaro mâdayadhva<i>m</i> yathâbhâgam âv<i>ri</i>shâyadhvam ity. 19. uktvodaṅṅ âvarteta savya<i>m</i> bâhum upasa<i>m</i>h<i>ri</i>tya prasavyam âv<i>ri</i>tyo. 20. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>patâmya kalyâ<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> dhyâyann abhiparyâvartamâno <i>g</i>aped ainîmadanta pitaro yathâbhâgam âv<i>ri</i>shâyishateti. 21. tisro darbhapi<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ûlîr a<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>anena nigh<i>ri</i>shya karshûshu nidadhyâd yathâpi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a<i>m</i>.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_423"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 423</font></a></p>  
 <p>blades, and should put them down into the pits in the same way as the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>as,</p>  
 <p>22. And sesamum oil and perfumes.</p>  
 <p>23. For the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>as and the following offerings he should alter the formula (Sûtra 17) accordingly.</p>  
 <p>24. Now (follows) the deprecation.</p>  
 <p>25. On the eastern pit he lays his hands, turning the inside of the right hand upwards, with (the formula), 'Adoration to you, O Fathers, for the sake of life! Adoration to you, O Fathers, for the sake of vital breath!' (MB. II, 3, 8.)</p>  
 <p>26. On the middle, turning the inside of the left hand upwards, with (the formula), 'Adoration to you, O Fathers, for the sake of terror! Adoration to you, O Fathers, for the sake of sap!' (MB. l.l.)</p>  
 <p>27. On the western, turning the inside of the right hand upwards, with (the formula), 'Adoration to you, O Fathers, for the sake of comfort (svadhâ)! Adoration to you, O Fathers, for the sake of wrath!' (MB. II, 3, 9.)</p>  
 <p>28. Joining his hands</p>  
 <p>29. (He should murmur the formula), 'Adoration to you' (MB. II, 3, 9).</p>  
 <p>30. He should lay down threads into the pits in the same way as the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>as, with (the formula), 'This (garment) to you.'</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">22. taila<i>m</i> surabhi <i>k</i>a. 23. pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>aprabh<i>ri</i>ti yathârtham ûhed. 24. atha nihnavana<i>m</i>. 25. pûrvasyâ<i>m</i> karshvâ<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>ottânau pâ<i>n</i>î k<i>ri</i>tvâ namo va<i>h</i> pitaro <i>g</i>îvâya namo va<i>h</i> pitara<i>s</i> <i>s</i>ûshâyeti. 26. savyottânau madhyamâyâ<i>m</i> namo va<i>h</i> pitaro ghorâya namo va<i>h</i> pitaro rasâyeti. 27. dakshi<i>n</i>ottânau pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>imâyâ<i>m</i> namo va<i>h</i> pitara svadhâyai namo va<i>h</i> pitaro manyava ity. 28. a<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ali<i>m</i> k<i>ri</i>tvâ 29. namo va iti. 30. sûtratantûn karshûshu nidadhyâd yathâpi<i>n</i><i>d</i>am etad va ity.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_424"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 424</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. He should recite over the pits (the verse), 'Bringing strength' (MB. II, 3, 13).</p>  
 <p>32. The middle Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a he should give to his wife to eat, if she is desirous of a son, with (the verse), 'Give fruit.'</p>  
 <p>33. With (the verse), '(<i>G</i>âtavedas) has been our messenger' (MB. II, 3, 15), he should throw the firebrand into the fire.</p>  
 <p>34. They should take the sacrificial vessels back two by two.</p>  
 <p>35 <a name="fr_1040"></a><a href="#fn_1039"><font size="1">35-39</font></a>. The same is the rite of the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>apit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.</p>  
 <p>36. Let him cook the Havis in the (sacred) domestic fire.</p>  
 <p>37. From that fire (let him take the fire which) he carries forward (see above, Sûtra 2).</p>  
 <p>38. (Here is only) one pit.</p>  
 <p>39. No layer of grass (Sûtra 10).</p>  
 <p>40 <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1040"><font size="1">40</font></a>. Of the mess of cooked food sacred to Indrâ<i>n</i>î he should sacrifice with (the verse), 'The Ekâsh<i>t</i>akâ' (MB. II, 3, 19). He should sacrifice with (the verse), The Ekâsh<i>t</i>akâ.'</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Third Pa<i>t</i>ala.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">31. ûr<i>g</i>a<i>m</i> vahantîr iti karshûr anumantrayeta. 32. madhyama<i>m</i> pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a<i>m</i> putrakâmâ<i>m</i> prâ<i>s</i>ayed âdhattety. 33. abhûn no dûta ity ulmukam agnau prakshiped. 34. dvandva<i>m</i> pâtrâ<i>n</i>y atihareyur. 35. esha eva pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>apit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>akalpo. 36. g<i>ri</i>hye<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gnau havi<i>s</i> <i>s</i>rapayet. 37. tata evâtipra<i>n</i>ayed. 38. ekâ karshûr. 39. na svastara. 40. Indrâ<i>n</i>yâ sthâlîpâkasyaikâsh<i>t</i>aketi <i>g</i>uhuyâd ekâsh<i>t</i>aketi <i>g</i>uhuyât. t<i>ri</i>tîyapa<i>t</i>ala<i>h</i>.</font></p>
