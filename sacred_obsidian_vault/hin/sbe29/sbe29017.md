 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 15.</h3>  
 <p>1. The three verses, 'I loosen thee' (Rig-veda X, 85, 24), when she departs from the house.</p>  
 <p>2. 'The living one they bewail' (Rig-veda X, 40, 10), if she begins to cry.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_89"></a><a href="#fn_88"><font size="1">3</font></a>. The wife then smears the axle of the chariot with clarified butter with this (verse), 'They feasted, they got drunk' (Rig-veda I, 82, 2),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 40</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. And with the two (verses), 'Pure are thy wheels,' 'Thy two wheels' (Rig-veda X, 85, 12. 16), of the two wheels the first with the first (verse) and the second with the second (verse),</p>  
 <p>5. And the two bulls.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_90"></a><a href="#fn_89"><font size="1">6</font></a>. After (the wife?) has put, with this (verse), 'In the box of the wheel' (Rig-veda VIII, 80, 7), a branch of a fruit-bearing tree into each of the holes destined for the pins,</p>  
 <p>7. Or, if (such branches) are (already) fixed, has recited (that verse) over them,</p>  
 <p>8. They then harness the two bulls with the two (verses), 'Harnessed be thy right one' (Rig-veda I, 82, 5-6), (the bridegroom) reciting the half-verse, 'White the two bulls' (Rig-veda X, 85, 10), over them when they have been harnessed.</p>  
 <p>9. Now should any part of the chariot break or burst, let him take the girl to the house of one who keeps the sacred fires,</p>  
 <p>10. And repair (the damage) with the verse, 'Cover thyself with the Khadiras' (Rig-veda III, 53, 19).</p>  
 <p>11. A knot with the verse, 'Him like a horse' (Rig-veda X, 143, 2).</p>  
 <p>12. He then murmurs the five verses, 'May prosperity give us' (Rig-veda V, 51, 11-15).</p>  
 <p>13. 'Adorned with Ki<i>m</i>suka flowers' (Rig-veda X, 85, 20), when she mounts the chariot;</p>  
 <p>14. 'May no waylayers meet us' (ibid. 32), at a cross-way;</p>  
 <p>15. 'Which the womans' (ibid. 31), near a cemetery;</p>  
 <p>16. The half-verse, 'O tree with thy hundred</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 41</font></a></p>  
 <p>branches' (Rig-veda III, 8, 11), he mutters near a big tree;</p>  
 <p>17. 'The good protectress' (Rig-Veda X, 63, 10), when she ascends a ship;</p>  
 <p>18. 'Carrying stones' (Rig-veda X, 53, 8), when she crosses a river;</p>  
 <p>19. Optionally (he) also (murmurs the same verse, if that is done) with the harnessed chariot;</p>  
 <p>20. 'Up may your wave' (Rig-veda III, 33, 13), at deep places (in the river);</p>  
 <p>21. And (at such places) let her not look out.</p>  
 <p>22. The seven verses, 'Here may delight' (Rig-veda X, 85, 27 seq.), when she has reached the house, omitting the verses already employed.</p>
