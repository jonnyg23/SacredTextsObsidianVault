 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">1-13</font></a>. On the middle (Ash<i>t</i>akâ) a cow (is sacrificed).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">4, 1. madhyamâyâ<i>m</i> gaus.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_418"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 418</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. He should place that (cow) to the east of the fire, facing the west, and should sacrifice (Â<i>g</i>ya) with (the verse), 'What, O beasts' (MB. II, 2, 5).</p>  
 <p>3. After having made that oblation he should recite over (the cow the verse), 'May to thee' (l.l. 6).</p>  
 <p>4. Let him sprinkle it with water in which barley is, with (the formula), 'Agreeable to the Ash<i>t</i>akâ I sprinkle thee!'</p>  
 <p>5. Having sprinkled it and carried a fire-brand round it, he should give the Proksha<i>n</i>î water (to the cow) to drink.</p>  
 <p>6. Going in a northern direction (from the fire) he should kill (the cow), the head of which is turned to the west, the feet to the north.</p>  
 <p>7. After it has been killed, he should sacrifice (Â<i>g</i>ya) with (the verse), 'If the beast' (l.l. 8).</p>  
 <p>8. His wife should wash the apertures of its body.</p>  
 <p>9. After (the cow's body) has been opened, so that two purifiers (i.e. grass-blades) have been put under (the knife), he should have the omentum drawn out.</p>  
 <p>10. Seizing it with one branch and with another forked branch of a sacrificially pure tree he should roast it.</p>  
 <p>11. When it has ceased to drop, he should hew (the cow) to pieces.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">2. ta<i>m</i> purastâd agne<i>h</i> pratyaṅmukhîm avasthâpya <i>g</i>uhuyâd yat pa<i>s</i>ava iti. 3. hutvâ <i>k</i>ânumantrayetânu tveti. 4. yavamatîbhir adbhi<i>h</i> prokshed ash<i>t</i>akâyai tvâ <i>g</i>ush<i>t</i>a<i>m</i> (correct, <i>g</i>ush<i>t</i>â<i>m</i>) prokshâmîti. 5. prokshyolmukena parih<i>ri</i>tya proksha<i>n</i>î<i>h</i> pâyayed. 6. udaṅṅ uts<i>ri</i>pya pratyak<i>s</i>irasam (°sim, °sîm the MSS.) udakpadî<i>m</i> sa<i>m</i><i>g</i><i>ñ</i>apayet. 7. sa<i>m</i><i>g</i><i>ñ</i>aptâyâ<i>m</i> <i>g</i>uhuyâd yat pa<i>s</i>ur iti. 8. tasyâ<i>h</i> patnî srotâ<i>m</i>si prakshâlayet. 9. pavitre antardhâyotk<i>ri</i>tya vapâm uddhârayed. 10. ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>iyasya v<i>ri</i>kshasya vi<i>s</i>âkhâ<i>s</i>âkhâbhyâ<i>m</i> parig<i>ri</i>hyâgnau <i>s</i>rapayet. 11. pras<i>ri</i>tâyâ<i>m</i> vi<i>s</i>ased.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_419"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 419</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. The 'spreading under' and sprinkling (Â<i>g</i>ya) on (the omentum) has been described. (It is clone) as at the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation (see II, 1, 24).</p>  
 <p>13. He should sacrifice with (the formula), 'To the Ash<i>t</i>akâ Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_1035"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">14-24</font></a>. The Avadânas (or portions which have to be cut off) he should have taken from all its limbs.</p>  
 <p>15. Not from the left thigh and the lungs.</p>  
 <p>16. The left thigh he keeps (for the Anvash<i>t</i>akya).</p>  
 <p>17. He should cook the Avadânas and a mess of sacrificial food, (stirring up the ones and the other) with two different pot-ladles.</p>  
 <p>18. The juice he lets drop into a brazen vessel.</p>  
 <p>19. The Avadânas he puts on branches of the Plaksha tree.</p>  
 <p>20. From each (Avadâna) he should cut off (the prescribed portions, and should put them) into a brazen vessel;</p>  
 <p>21. And from the mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>22. Let him take four portions or eight portions of Â<i>g</i>ya (?) and let him sacrifice it with (the verses), 'Into Agni' (MB. II, 2, 9 seqq.).</p>  
 <p>23. Let him make oblations out of the brazen vessel, each oblation with two of the following (verses).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">12. uktam upastara<i>n</i>âbhighâra<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> yathâ svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>to. 13. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>sh<i>t</i>akâyai svâheti <i>g</i>uhuyât. 14. sarvâṅgebhyo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>vadânâny uddhârayen. 15. na savyât sakthno na klomna<i>h</i>. 16. savya<i>m</i> sakthi nidhâya. 17. p<i>ri</i>thaṅ meksha<i>n</i>âbhyâm avadânâni sthâlîpâka<i>ñ</i> <i>k</i>a <i>s</i>rapayitvâ. 18. ka<i>m</i>se rasa<i>m</i> prasrâvya. 19. plaksha<i>s</i>âkhâsv avadânâni k<i>ri</i>tvai. 20. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>kaikasmât ka<i>m</i>se<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>vadyet. 21. sthâlîpâkâ<i>k</i> <i>k</i>a. 22. <i>k</i>aturg<i>ri</i>hîtam ash<i>t</i>ag<i>ri</i>hîta<i>m</i> vâtra (vâ<i>g</i>ya<i>m</i>? vâtra<i>m</i>, vâtra the MSS.) <i>g</i>uhuyâd Agnâv iti. 23. ka<i>m</i>sât parâbhir dvâbhyâ<i>m</i> dvâbhyâm ekaikâm âhuti<i>m</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_420"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 420</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. The oblation to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t with the eighth (verse).</p>  
 <p>25 <a name="fr_1036"></a><a href="#fn_1035"><font size="1">25-28</font></a>. At a sacrifice to the Fathers the omentum is sacrificed with (the verse), 'Carry the omentum' (MB. II, 3, 16).</p>  
 <p>26. At one to the gods with (the verse), '<i>G</i>âtavedas!' (l.l. 17.)</p>  
 <p>27. If (the deity is) unknown, (in the formula for) assigning (the oblation, instead of the name of a deity the name of) that (ceremony is put)</p>  
 <p>28. As (for instance), 'To the Ash<i>t</i>akâ.'</p>  
 <p>29. An animal is the sacrificial fee at (the sacrifice of) an animal;</p>  
 <p>30 <a name="fr_1037"></a><a href="#fn_1036"><font size="1">30</font></a>. A full vessel at (that of) a mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">24. sauvish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>tam ash<i>t</i>amyâ. 25. vaha vapâm iti pitrye vapâhomo. 26. <i>G</i>âtaveda iti daivatye. 27. tadâde<i>s</i>am anâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âte. 28. yathâsh<i>t</i>akâyâ iti. 29. pa<i>s</i>ur eva pa<i>s</i>or dakshi<i>n</i>â. 30. sthâlîpâkasya pûr<i>n</i>apâtram.</font></p>
