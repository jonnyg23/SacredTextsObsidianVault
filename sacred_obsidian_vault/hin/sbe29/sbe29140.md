 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 7.</h3>  
 <p>1. If the sun sets while he is sleeping without being sick, he should spend the rest of the night keeping silence, without sitting down, and should worship the sun (when it rises) with the five (verses), 'The light, O sun, by which thou destroyest darkness' (Rig-veda X, 37, 4 seq.).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_548"></a><a href="#fn_547"><font size="1">2</font></a>. If (the sun) rises (while he is sleeping without being sick), being fatigued without having done any work, or having done work that is not becoming, he should keep silence, &amp;c., as before, and perform his worship (to the sun) with the following four (verses, Rig-veda X, 37, 9 seq.).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_549"></a><a href="#fn_548"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Invested with the sacrificial cord, constantly fulfilling the prescribed duties regarding the use of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 226</font></a></p>  
 <p>water, he should perform the Sandhyâ (or twilight devotion), observing silence.</p>  
 <p>4. In the evening he should, turning his face to the north-west, to the region between the chief (west) point and the intermediate (north-western) point (of the horizon), murmur the Sâvitrî, (beginning) when the sun is half set, until the stars appear.</p>  
 <p>5. In the same way in the morning</p>  
 <p>6. Standing, with his face turned to the east, until the disk (of the sun) appears.</p>  
 <p>7. If a dove flies against his house or towards it, he should sacrifice with (the hymn), 'O gods, the dove' (Rig-veda X, 165), verse by verse, or should murmur (that hymn).</p>  
 <p>8. 'We have thee, O Lord of the path' (Rig-veda VI, 53)if he is going out for doing some business.</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_550"></a><a href="#fn_549"><font size="1">9</font></a>. 'Bring us together, Pûshan, with a knowing one' (Rig-Veda VI, 54)if he wishes to find something lost, or if he has strayed.</p>  
 <p>10. 'Journey over the ways, Pûshan' (Rig-veda I, 42)if he is going out on a long or dangerous way.</p>
