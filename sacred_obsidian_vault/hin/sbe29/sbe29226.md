 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">1-4</font></a>. One who has been bitten by a venomous animal, he should besprinkle with water, murmuring (the verse), 'Do not fear' (MB. IL 6, 18).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">4, 1. vishavatâ dash<i>t</i>am adbhir abhyukshan <i>g</i>apen mâ bhaishîr iti.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_433"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 433</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. A Snâtaka should, when lying down to sleep, put his bamboo staff near (his bed) with (the formula), 'Strong one, protect' (l.l. 19). This will bring him luck.</p>  
 <p>3. A place where he has a worm he should besprinkle with water, murmuring (the verses), 'Thy worm is killed' (MB. II, 7, 1-4).</p>  
 <p>4. (If doing this) for his cattle, let him fetch in the afternoon an earth-clod taken out of a furrow, and let him in the morning strew the dust of it (on the place attacked by worms), murmuring (the same texts).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">5-23</font></a>. (A guest) who is going to accept the Madhuparka should come forward murmuring, 'Here I tread on this' (MB. II, 8, 2).</p>  
 <p>6. They announce three times (to the guest) each (of the following things which are brought to him): a bed (of grass), water for washing the feet, the Argha water, water for sipping, and the Madhuparka;</p>  
 <p>7. And the cow.</p>  
 <p>8. Having spread out the bed (of grass, so that the points of the grass are) turned to the north, he should sit down thereon with (the verse), 'The herbs which' (MB. II, 8, 3).</p>  
 <p>9. With the feet (he treads on another bundle of</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">2. snâtakas sa<i>m</i>vi<i>s</i>an vai<i>n</i>ava<i>m</i> da<i>n</i><i>d</i>am upanidadhyât tura gopâyeti svastyayana<i>m</i>. 3. hatas ta (hastata, ha<i>h</i>sta, hasta, vitasta, the MSS.) iti k<i>ri</i>mimanta<i>m</i> de<i>s</i>am adbhir abhyuksha<i>ñ</i> <i>g</i>apet. 4. pa<i>s</i>ûnâ<i>m</i> <i>k</i>ed aparâh<i>n</i>e sîtâlosh<i>t</i>am âh<i>ri</i>tya tasya prâta<i>h</i> pâ<i>m</i>subhi<i>h</i> pratishkira<i>ñ</i> <i>g</i>apen. 5. madhuparka<i>m</i> pratigrahîshyann idam aham imâm iti pratitish<i>th</i>a<i>ñ</i> <i>g</i>aped. 6. vish<i>t</i>arapâdyârghyâ<i>k</i>amanîyamadhuparkâ<i>n</i>âm ekaika<i>m</i> trir vedayante. 7. gâ<i>m</i> <i>k</i>o. 8. . <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>da<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>a<i>m</i> vish<i>t</i>aram âstîrya yâ oshadhîr ity adhyâsîta. 9. pâdayor dvitîyayâ dvau <i>k</i>ed.</font></p>

 <p><a name="page_434"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 434</font></a></p>  
 <p>grass), if there are two, with the second (verse) (l.l. 4).</p>  
 <p>10. Let him look at the water with (the formula), 'From which side I see the goddesses' (l.l. 5).</p>  
 <p>11. Let him wash his left foot with (the formula), 'The left' (l.l. 6), the right with, 'The right' (I. 1. 7); both with the rest (8).</p>  
 <p>12. Let him accept the Arghya water with (the formula), 'Thou art the queen of food' (l.l. 9).</p>  
 <p>13. The water which he is to sip, (he accepts) with (the formula), 'Glory art thou' (l.l. 10).</p>  
 <p>14. The Madhuparka with (the formula), 'The glory's glory art thou' (l.l. 11).</p>  
 <p>15. Let him drink three times with (the formulas), 'The glorys,' 'The mights,' 'The fortunes' (l.l. 12).</p>  
 <p>16. Having drunk more of it a fourth time silently he should give the remainder to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a.</p>  
 <p>17. Over the cow, when it has been announced to him, he should recite (the formula), 'Let loose the cow.'</p>  
 <p>18. Instead of 'and of N.N.' (in that formula) he should put the name of the person who offers the Arghya reception.</p>  
 <p>19. Thus if it is no sacrifice (by which the Arghya ceremony has been occasioned).</p>  
 <p>20. 'Make it (ready),' if it is a sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">10. apa<i>h</i> pa<i>s</i>yed yato devîr iti. 11. savya<i>m</i> pâdam avasi<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ed savyam iti dakshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> ity ubhau <i>s</i>eshe<i>n</i>â. 12. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>nnasya râsh<i>t</i>rir asîty arghya<i>m</i> pratig<i>ri</i>h<i>n</i>îyâd. 13. ya<i>s</i>o<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>sîty â<i>k</i>amanîya<i>m</i>. 14. ya<i>s</i>aso ya<i>s</i>o<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>sîti madhuparka<i>m</i>. 15. tri<i>h</i> pibed ya<i>s</i>aso mahasa <i>s</i>riyâ iti. 16. tûsh<i>n</i>î<i>m</i> <i>k</i>aturtha<i>m</i> bhûyo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>bhipâya brâhma<i>n</i>âyo<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ish<i>t</i>a<i>m</i> dadyâd. 17. ga<i>m</i> veditâm anumantrayeta mu<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>a gâm ity. 18. amushya <i>k</i>ety arhayitur nâma brûyâd. 19. evam aya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>e. 20. kuruteti ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.</font></p>

 <p>21. The six persons to whom the Arghya reception is due are, a teacher, an officiating priest, a Snâtaka, a king, the father-in-law, a friend.</p>  
 <p>22. He should honour them (with the Arghya reception) once a year;</p>  
 <p>23. But repeatedly in the case of a sacrifice and of a wedding. But repeatedly in the case of a sacrifice and of a wedding.</p>
