 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 5.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_139"></a><a href="#fn_138"><font size="1">1</font></a>. After one year (the teacher) recites the Sâvitrî (to the student),</p>  
 <p>2. (Or) after three nights,</p>  
 <p>3. Or immediately.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_140"></a><a href="#fn_139"><font size="1">4-6</font></a>. Let him recite a Gâyatrî to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a,</p>  
 <p>5. A Trish<i>t</i>ubh to a Kshatriya,</p>  
 <p>6. A <i>G</i>agatî to a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>7. But let it be anyhow a verse sacred to Savitar.</p>  
 <p>8. They seat themselves to the north of the fire,</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_141"></a><a href="#fn_140"><font size="1">9</font></a>. The teacher with his face turned eastward, the other westward.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_142"></a><a href="#fn_141"><font size="1">10-11</font></a>. After (the student) has said, 'Recite, sir!'</p>  
 <p>11. The teacher, having pronounced the word OM, then causes the other one to say, 'Recite the Sâvitrî, sir!'</p>  
 <p>12. He then recites the Sâvitrî to him, the verse 'That glorious (splendour) of Savitar' (Rig-Veda III, 62, 10); (firstly) pâda by pâda, (then) hemistich by hemistich, (and finally) without a stop.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_138"></a><a href="sbe29035.htm#fr_139">66:1</a> 5, 1. The study of the Veda is opened by the Sâvitrî. Comp. <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a, loc. cit., §§ 6 seq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_139"></a><a href="sbe29035.htm#fr_140">66:4-6</a> The Gâyatrî which the teacher shall recite to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a is the same verse of which it is said below, chap. q, II, that it belongs to Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra (Rig-veda III, 62, to); the Trish<i>t</i>ubh which is taught to the Kshatriya is a verse ascribed to Hira<i>n</i>yastûpa, Rig-veda I, 35, 2; the <i>G</i>agatî which is to be repeated to a Vai<i>s</i>ya is Rig-veda IV, 40, 5, belonging to Vâmadeva, or Rig-veda I, 35, 9, belonging to Hira<i>n</i>yastûpa. See the note on chap. 7, 10.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_140"></a><a href="sbe29035.htm#fr_141">66:9</a> The same position is prescribed, in the same words, for the study of the main part of the Veda, below, chap. 7, 3; during <a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a> the study of the Âra<i>n</i>yaka the position is slightly different (VI, 3, 2). According to Nârâya<i>n</i>a this Sûtra would contain a nishedha of the Sûtras 828 and 829 of the Rig-veda-Prâti<i>s</i>âkhya (p. ccxcii of Professor Max Müller's edition).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_141"></a><a href="sbe29035.htm#fr_142">67:10-11</a> 10, 11. The Indian tradition divides these Sûtras after â<i>k</i>ârya<i>h</i>, so that the words adhîhi bho would have to be pronounced by the teacher. Thus also Nârâya<i>n</i>a explains, â<i>k</i>ârya adhîhi bho 3 iti mâ<i>n</i>avakam uktvâ, &amp;c. In my opinion it is the student or the students who say adhîhi bho. Thus the Prâti<i>s</i>âkhya (Sûtra 831, ed. Max Müller) says, 'They invite him with the words adhîhi bho 3, all the students the teacher, having embraced his feet.' Comp. also below, IV, 8, 12, the greater part of which Sûtra is word for word identical with these rules; VI, 3, 6; Gautama I, 46; Gobhila II, 10, 38.</p>
