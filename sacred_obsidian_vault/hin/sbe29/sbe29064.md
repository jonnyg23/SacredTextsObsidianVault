 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_237"></a><a href="#fn_236"><font size="1">1</font></a> Now (follows) the Ekoddish<i>t</i>a (i.e. the <i>S</i>râddha ceremony directed to a single dead person),</p>  
 <p>2. With one strainer,</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_238"></a><a href="#fn_237"><font size="1">3</font></a>. One (pot of) Argha-water,</p>  
 <p>4. One lump (of flour).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_239"></a><a href="#fn_238"><font size="1">5</font></a>. No inviting (takes place here), nor the putting</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_109"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 109</font></a></p>  
 <p>(of food) into the fire, nor (do) the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs (take part in this ceremony). 'Relished?'thus are they to be asked whether they are satiated. 'May it approach (the fathers),' instead of 'imperishable.'</p>  
 <p>6. 'Be satisfied,' when sending them away.</p>  
 <p>7. Thus through one year, when one has died.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_240"></a><a href="#fn_239"><font size="1">8</font></a>. And (then) omission of the fourth one.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_236"></a><a href="sbe29064.htm#fr_237">108:1</a> 2, 1. Eka uddish<i>t</i>o yasmin <i>s</i>râddhe tad ekoddish<i>t</i>am (Nâr.). This is the kind of <i>S</i>râddha sacrifice which is to be performed for one twice-born during the first year after his death; see Manu III, 247; Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya I, 250.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_237"></a><a href="sbe29064.htm#fr_238">108:3</a> This rule about the Argha water corresponds to those given with regard to the Pârva<i>n</i>a <i>S</i>râddha in the Sûtras 3 and 4 of the preceding chapter.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_238"></a><a href="sbe29064.htm#fr_239">108:5</a> 'Because the âvâhana (inviting) is forbidden here, it follows <a name="page_109"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 109</font></a> that it must take place at the Pârva<i>n</i>a <i>S</i>râddha' (Nâr.). According to Râma<i>k</i>andra's Paddhati he shall say to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as, 'I will invite hither the fathers;' and when they give their consent, he invites them with Rig-veda X, 16, 12. Comp. Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya I, 232 seq., &amp;c. Regarding the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs comp. the note on chap. 1, 2; as to the t<i>ri</i>ptapra<i>s</i>na (the question whether they are satiated) comp. Manu III, 251; Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>. I, 240. At the Pârva<i>n</i>a <i>S</i>râddha, after the Brâhma<i>n</i>as have finished their dinner and rinsed their mouths, and after the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>as have been offered, the sacrificer says, 'May what has been given at this <i>S</i>râddha to our father N.N., who belongs to the gotra N.N., be imperishable!' (comp. Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>. I, 242.) This phrase is to be altered at the Ekoddish<i>t</i>a <i>S</i>râddha in the way indicated in this Sûtra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_239"></a><a href="sbe29064.htm#fr_240">109:8</a> After the Ekoddish<i>t</i>a <i>S</i>râddha has been performed for a dead person during the first year after his death, he is to be admitted, by the Sapi<i>n</i><i>d</i>îkara<i>n</i>a ceremony, among the other Manes, and receives thenceforward his Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a together with them at the ordinary Pârva<i>n</i>a <i>S</i>râddha. As the ritual of this <i>S</i>râddha requires that the number of the 'fathers' worshipped should be three, the accession of a new person makes necessary the omission of the pra-pra-pitâmaha, who has now become fourth among the fathers.</p>
