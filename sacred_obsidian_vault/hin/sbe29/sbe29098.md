 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 6.</h3>  
 <p>1. Having worshipped the Sun with (the verses), 'Savit<i>ri</i> from the west,' 'This eye' (Rig-veda X, 36, 14; VII, 66, 16),</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_343"></a><a href="#fn_342"><font size="1">2</font></a>. They turn away, come back, sit down.</p>  
 <p>3. With (the words), 'As the water is appeased'they draw water out of the vessel used for the expiation,</p>  
 <p>4. Pour it out on the ground,</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_344"></a><a href="#fn_343"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Spread (some) of that (water over the ground) with (the words), 'As the earth (is appeased),'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 149</font></a></p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_345"></a><a href="#fn_344"><font size="1">6</font></a>. He (then) smears it on his right shoulder with (the words), 'Thus may peace dwell in me.'</p>  
 <p>7. In the same way a second time.</p>  
 <p>8. In the same way a third time.</p>  
 <p>9. 'Piece by piece thou art produced; piece by piece thou risest up; bring welfare to us, O house!'with (this text they) take pieces of Dûrvâ stalks (out of the vessel of water), put them on their heads,</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_346"></a><a href="#fn_345"><font size="1">10</font></a>. (And make water-offerings with the formulas), 'May Agni satiate himself; may Vâyu satiate himself; may Sûrya satiate himself; may Vish<i>n</i>u satiate himself; may Pra<i>g</i>âpati satiate himself; may Virûpâksha satiate himself; may Sahasrâksha satiate himself; may all beings satiate themselves.'</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_347"></a><a href="#fn_346"><font size="1">11</font></a>. (Then) Sumantu, <i>G</i>aimini, Vai<i>s</i>ampâyana, Paila, and the other teachers (receive their offerings).</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_348"></a><a href="#fn_347"><font size="1">12</font></a>. (Then) every one (worships in the same way) his fathers.</p>  
 <p>13. With (the text), 'To the sea you' (<i>S</i>raut. IV, 11, 11) they pour out the water,</p>  
 <p>14. Murmur the Vâmadevya,</p>  
 <p>15. And separate according to their pleasure.</p>  
 <p>16. (The final benedictory formula runs thus), 'Through the power of wisdom, of <i>S</i>ruti and Sm<i>ri</i>ti, as handed down by tradition, through (that power) which has its measure in (the Vedic texts) that have been gone through(?), and which is possessed of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 150</font></a></p>  
 <p>undisputed firmness, may peace be with us in welfare. Adoration be to gods, <i>Ri</i>shis, Manes, and men! May they whom we have adored, make happy life, beauty, health, peace, incolumity, imperishableness, vigour, splendour, glory, power, holy lustre, renown, age, offspring, cattle, adoration, increase. From wrongly spoken, wrongly used (prayer), from everything that is deficient or excessive, for the good of gods and <i>Ri</i>shis, may the Brahman and Truth protect me; may the Brahman and Truth protect me!'</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Sixth Adhyâya.</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya.</p>
