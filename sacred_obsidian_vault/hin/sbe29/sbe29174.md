 <p><a name="page_301"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 301</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>A II, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 1.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">1</font></a>. When (the son) is one year old, the <i>K</i>û<i>d</i>âkara<i>n</i>a (i.e. the tonsure of his head, should be performed),</p>  
 <p>2. Or before the lapse of the third (year).</p>  
 <p>3. When he is sixteen years old, the Ke<i>s</i>ânta (i.e. the shaving of his beard, is to be done),</p>  
 <p>4. Or, according as it is considered auspicious by all (the different families).</p>  
 <p>5. After food has been distributed to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as, the mother takes the boy, bathes him, puts on him an under and an upper garment which have not yet been washed, and putting him on her lap, she sits down to the west of the fire.</p>  
 <p>6. The father taking hold (of his wife) sacrifices Â<i>g</i>ya oblations, and after he has partaken of the (sacrificial) food, he pours warm water into cold water with (the words), 'With warm water come hither, Vâyu! Aditi, cut the hair.'</p>  
 <p>7. At the Ke<i>s</i>ânta ceremony (Sûtra 3), 'hair and beard' (instead of 'hair').</p>  
 <p>8. He throws a piece of fresh butter, or of ghee, or some curds into it (i.e. into the water, Sara 6).</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Taking some (water) he moistens the hair near the right ear with (the formula), 'On the impulse of Savit<i>ri</i> may the divine waters moisten</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_302"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 302</font></a></p>  
 <p>thy body in order that long life and splendour may be thine.'</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">10</font></a>. Having unravelled (the hair) with a porcupine's quill that has three white spots, he puts three young Ku<i>s</i>a shoots into it with (the formula), 'Herb' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. IV, 1).</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Taking up a copper razor with (the formula), 'Friendly by name' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. III, 63 a), he cuts (the hair) with (the formula), 'I cut off' (ibid. 63b), (and with the formula,) 'The razor with which Savit<i>ri</i>, the knowing one, has shaven (the beard) of king Soma and Varu<i>n</i>a, with that, ye Brâhma<i>n</i>as, shave his (head), in order that he may be blessed with long life and may reach old age.'</p>  
 <p>12. Cutting off (the Ku<i>s</i>a shoots) together with the hair, he throws them on a lump of bull's dung which they keep northwards of the fire.</p>  
 <p>13. In the same way two other times silently.</p>  
 <p>14. The moistening and the other rites are repeated with the two other (tufts of hair).</p>  
 <p>15. Behind with (the verse), 'The threefold age' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. III, 62).</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">16</font></a>. Then on the left side with (the verse), 'With that prayer by which mayst thou, a mighty one, go to heaven, and long mayst thou see the sun: with that prayer I shave thee for the sake of life, of existence, of glory, of welfare.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_303"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 303</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. Three times he shaves round the head, from left to right;</p>  
 <p>18. Including the face, at the Ke<i>s</i>ânta ceremony.</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">19</font></a>. (He recites the verse,) 'When the shaver shaves his hair with the razor, the wounding, the well-shaped, purify his head, but do not take away his life.'</p>  
 <p>20 <a name="fr_763"></a><a href="#fn_762"><font size="1">20</font></a>. He adds (the word), 'his face' at the Ke<i>s</i>ânta ceremony.</p>  
 <p>21. With that water (Sûtras 6, 8) he moistens his head, and gives the razor to the barber with (the words), 'Without wounding him, shave him.'</p>  
 <p>22. The locks of hair which are left over, are to be arranged as it is considered auspicious (in his family).</p>  
 <p>23 <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_763"><font size="1">23</font></a>. Having put away that lump of dung with the hair so that it is hidden in a cow-stable, or in a small pond, or in the vicinity of water; he gives an optional gift to the teacher;</p>  
 <p>24. A cow at the Ke<i>s</i>ânta ceremony.</p>  
 <p>25. After the Ke<i>s</i>ânta has been performed, (the youth) should observe chastity and should not be shaven through one year, or twelve nights, or six nights, or at least three nights.</p>
