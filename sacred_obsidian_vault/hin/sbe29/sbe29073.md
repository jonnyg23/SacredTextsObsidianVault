 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 11.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_277"></a><a href="#fn_276"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Let him not look at a naked woman, except during sexual intercourse,</p>  
 <p>2. Nor (look) at the sun while it rises or sets,</p>  
 <p>3. At an enemy,</p>  
 <p>4. At an evil-doer,</p>  
 <p>5. At a person that has to touch dead bodies.</p>  
 <p>6. Let him not talk with a woman who has recently been confined or who has her courses,</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_278"></a><a href="#fn_277"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Nor with those (mentioned before).</p>  
 <p>8. Let him not eat food from which its strength is taken away.</p>  
 <p>9. Let him not do his work with implements wasted by use.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_279"></a><a href="#fn_278"><font size="1">10</font></a>. Let him not eat together (with his wife),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_124"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 124</font></a></p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_280"></a><a href="#fn_279"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Nor remnants (of food).</p>  
 <p>12. Remnants of (food belonging to the) Manes, gods, guests, and servants he may eat.</p>  
 <p>13. Gleaning ears of corn, receiving alms unasked for, or for which he has asked the good, performing sacrifices for others, are the means of livelihood;</p>  
 <p>14. (Of these) each preceding one is the more respectable.</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_281"></a><a href="#fn_280"><font size="1">15</font></a>. Or if (his livelihood) cannot be gained (in one of the ways mentioned), let him follow the occupation of a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>16. (He shall be) careful about his duties towards Manes and gods.</p>  
 <p>17. In due time (he shall) have intercourse with his wife.</p>  
 <p>18. He shall not lie down (to sleep) in the day-time,</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_282"></a><a href="#fn_281"><font size="1">19</font></a>. Nor during the first or the last watch of the night.</p>  
 <p>20. Let him not sit on the bare ground.</p>  
 <p>21. He shall constantly perform the prescribed duties regarding the use of water.</p>  
 <p>22. (And constantly) have his sacrificial cord suspended over his left shoulder.</p>  
 <p>23. Let him not abandon his teacher,</p>  
 <p>24. Except on (his teacher's) command,</p>  
 <p>25. Or with (his) permission.</p>
