 <p><a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 45</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 19.</h3>  
 <p>1. Let him pound the root of the Adhyâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>â plant and sprinkle it at the time of her monthly period with the two (verses), 'Speed away from here; a husband has she' (Rig-veda X, 85, 21. 22), with svâhâ at the end of each, into her right nostril.</p>  
 <p>2. 'The mouth of the Gandharva Vi<i>s</i>vâvasu art thou'with these words let him touch her, when he is about to cohabit with her.</p>  
 <p>3. When he has finished, let him murmur,</p>  
 <p>4. 'Into thy breath I put the sperm, N.N.!'</p>  
 <p>5. Or, 'As the earth is pregnant with Agni, as the heaven is with Indra pregnant, as Vâyu dwells in the womb of the regions (of the earth), thus I place an embryo into thy womb, N.N.!'</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_96"></a><a href="#fn_95"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Or, 'May a male embryo enter thy womb, as an arrow the quiver; may a man be born here, a son after ten months.</p>  
 <p>'Give birth to a male child; may after him (another) male be born; their mother shalt thou be, of the born, and (to others) mayst thou give birth.</p>  
 <p>'In the male verily, in the man dwells the sperm; he shall pour it forth into the woman: thus has said Dhâtar, thus Pra<i>g</i>âpati has said.</p>  
 <p>'Pra<i>g</i>âpati has created him, Savitar has shaped him. Imparting birth of females to other (women) may he put here a man.</p>  
 <p>'From the auspicious sperms which the men pro</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 46</font></a></p>  
 <p>duce for us, produce thou a son; be a well-breeding cow.</p>  
 <p>'Roar, be strong, put into her an embryo, achieve it; a male, thou male, put into her; to generation we call thee.</p>  
 <p>'Open thy womb; take in the man's sperm; may a male child be begotten in the womb. Him thou shalt bear; (having dwelt) ten months in the womb may he be born, the most excellent of his kin.'</p>
