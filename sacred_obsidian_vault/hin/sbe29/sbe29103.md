 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_374"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">4_1</font></a>. During the northern course of the sun, in the time of the increasing moon, under an auspicious Nakshatra the tonsure (of the child's head), the initiation (of a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin), the cutting of the beard, and marriage (should be celebrated).</p>  
 <p>2. According to some (teachers), marriage (may be celebrated) at any time.</p>  
 <p>3. Before those (ceremonies) let him sacrifice four Â<i>g</i>ya oblations</p>  
 <p>4. With the three (verses), 'Agni, thou purifiest life' (Rig-veda I X, 66, 10 seq.), and with (the one verse), 'Pra<i>g</i>âpati, no other one than thou' (Rig-Veda X, 121, 10).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_375"></a><a href="#fn_374"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Or with the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_375"><font size="1">6</font></a>. According to some (teachers), the one and the other.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">7</font></a>. No such(oblations), according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>8. At the marriage the fourth oblation with the verse, 'Thou (O Agni) art Aryaman towards the girls' (Rig-veda V, 3, 2).</p>
