 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_243"></a><a href="#fn_242"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now (follows) the Âbhyudayika (i.e. the <i>S</i>râddha ceremony referring to good luck).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 111</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. In the fortnight of the increasing moon, on an auspicious day,</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_244"></a><a href="#fn_243"><font size="1">3</font></a>. After the sacrifice to the mothers has been performed,</p>  
 <p>4. And an even number of (Brâhma<i>n</i>as) versed in the Veda have been invited to sit down;</p>  
 <p>5. In the forenoon;</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_245"></a><a href="#fn_244"><font size="1">6</font></a>. The rite is performed from left to right.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_246"></a><a href="#fn_245"><font size="1">7</font></a>. The murmuring with the omission of the verses belonging to the Manes.</p>  
 <p>8. The Darbha blades are straight.</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_247"></a><a href="#fn_246"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Barley is to be used instead of sesamum.</p>  
 <p>10. The lumps are mixed with curds, jujube fruits, fried grain.</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_248"></a><a href="#fn_247"><font size="1">11</font></a>. On inviting (the Manes, he should say), 'The Nândîmukha (glad-faced?) Manes will I invite.'</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_249"></a><a href="#fn_248"><font size="1">12</font></a>. 'May the Nândîmukha Manes be rejoiced,' instead of 'imperishable.'</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_249"><font size="1">13</font></a>. 'The Nândîmukha Manes will I make speak,' when he makes (the Brâhma<i>n</i>as) speak.</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_250"><font size="1">14</font></a>. '(Was it) well done?'thus are they to be asked whether they are satiated.</p>
