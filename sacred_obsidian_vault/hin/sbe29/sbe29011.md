 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 9.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_54"></a><a href="#fn_53"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The Sruva spoon (serves as) a vessel.</p>  
 <p>2. According to the purpose the properties (of the different things to be used at each oblation) should be chosen.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_55"></a><a href="#fn_54"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Taking up Ku<i>s</i>a blades with the left, and the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Sruva at its bottom with the right hand, with the words, 'The hand of Vish<i>n</i>u art thou'</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_55"><font size="1">4</font></a>. He offers with the Sruva the Â<i>g</i>ya oblations.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_57"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Beginning from the north-west side of the fire he offers (the Â<i>g</i>ya) unintermittingly on the south side (of the fire) with (the verse), 'Thou Agni art full of foresight' (Rig-veda I, 31, 10).</p>  
 <p>6. Beginning from the south-west side of the fire he unintermittingly offers on the north side with (the verse), 'To whom these snowy mountains' (Rig-veda X, 121, 4).</p>  
 <p>7. To Agni belongs the northern Â<i>g</i>ya portion, to Soma the southern.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_58"></a><a href="#fn_57"><font size="1">8</font></a>. In the middle (are made) the other oblations,</p>  
 <p>9. (With the words,) 'Agni is the begetter; may he give to me N.N. as my wife; svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'Soma is rich in wives; may he make me rich in wives by N.N.; svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'Pûshan is rich in kindred; may he make me rich in kindred by the father, the mother, the brothers of N.N.; svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_59"></a><a href="#fn_58"><font size="1">10</font></a>. At the Â<i>g</i>ya oblations the offering of the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions and of the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation is not standing,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 28</font></a></p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_60"></a><a href="#fn_59"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Nor in the standing oblations, according to Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ûkeya.</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_61"></a><a href="#fn_60"><font size="1">12</font></a>. The place for the insertion is the interval between the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis, the general expiation, and the oblation to Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 29</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. If the oblation consists in Â<i>g</i>ya, let him seize the Ku<i>s</i>a blades in his left hand with his right hand at their points and with the left at their roots, and let him wet their points (with Â<i>g</i>ya) in the Sruva, the middle and the roots in the Â<i>g</i>ya pot;</p>  
 <p>14. In the oblations of cooked food, however, the points in the Sru<i>k</i>, the middle in the Sruva, the roots in the Â<i>g</i>ya pot.</p>  
 <p>15. When he then has thrown them (into the fire) with the words, 'Agni's garment art thou,'</p>  
 <p>16. And has put on (the fire) three pieces of wood,</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_62"></a><a href="#fn_61"><font size="1">17</font></a>. (Water) is sprinkled round (the fire) as stated above.</p>  
 <p>18. Oblations for which only the deities are indicated, but no texts prescribed, are to be made merely with the word SVÂHÂ, 'To such and such a deity svâhâ! To such and such a deity svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_63"></a><a href="#fn_62"><font size="1">19</font></a>. The ritual (here) declared of the sacrifice (to</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 30</font></a></p>  
 <p>be performed) when (the father's) assent (to give away his daughter) has been declared</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_53"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_54">26:1</a> 9, 1. 'When no special rule is stated, the Sruva is to be understood as the vessel (for the offering). Thereby the rule, "The <i>G</i>uhû is the vessel" (<i>S</i>rauta-sûtra III, 19, 5) is abolished (for the G<i>ri</i>hya rites).' Nârâya<i>n</i>a.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_54"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_55">26:3</a> The manner of holding the Sruva in which the Â<i>g</i>ya is, is described by Kâtyâyana, <i>S</i>raut. I, 10, 6 seq., Stenzler's note on Pâraskara I, 1, 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_55"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_56">27:4</a> As to the characteristics of Â<i>g</i>ya (sacrificial butter), which is the substance offered at most of the G<i>ri</i>hya sacrifices, comp the statements of the G<i>ri</i>hya-sa<i>m</i>graha-pari<i>s</i>ish<i>t</i>a I, 105 seq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_56"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_57">27:5</a> Avi<i>k</i><i>kh</i>inna<i>m</i> (unintermittingly) is explained in Nâr.'s commentary by ekadhârayâ.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_57"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_58">27:8</a> 8 seq. Here are indicated the chief oblations of this sacrifice (anyâ âhutaya<i>h</i> pradhânabhûtâ<i>h</i>, Nâr.), or the âvâpa (the insertion, Sûtra 12) which comes between the standing introductory and concluding oblations.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_58"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_59">27:10</a> On Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t, comp. Weber, Indische Studien, IX, 217.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_59"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_60">28:11</a> See chap. 8, 13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_60"></a><a href="sbe29011.htm#fr_61">28:12</a> This Sûtra prescribes where the âvâpa, i.e. the special characteristical offerings of each sacrifice, is to be inserted between the regular offerings that belong to the standing model. The same subject is treated of in the <i>S</i>rauta-sûtra in the two rules, I, 16, 3 and 4: 'Whatsoever is offered between the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions and the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t, that is called âvâpa; this is the chief part (pradhâna) (of the sacrifice); the other (oblations) are subordinate thereto (tadaṅgâni).' The position of the âvâpa among the other oblations is indicated by Pâraskara in the following rule (I, 5, 6): Between the general expiation and the oblation to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, this is the place for the âvâpa.' (The word vivâhe at the end of this Sûtra seems to me to belong not to this rule, but to Sûtra 7.) Our Sûtra is identical with that of Pâraskara word for word; only instead of sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta, as Pâraskara has, we read here, mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tisarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta. This means, I believe, that the âvâpa, preceded and followed by the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>ti oblations (comp. below, I, 12, 13), should be placed between the Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta and the Prâ<i>g</i>âpatya oblation. The oblations made with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tayas are four in number; the corresponding formulas are: bhû<i>h</i> svâhâ, bhuva<i>h</i> svâhâ, svâ<i>h</i> svâhâ, bhûr bhuva<i>h</i> sva<i>h</i> svâhâ (comp. below, chap. 12, 12). The Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta (general expiation) consists of two oblations, one with the three Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tayas, the other with the verse ayâ<i>s</i> <i>k</i>âgne, quoted in the <i>S</i>rauta-sûtra III, 19, 3, and in Â<i>s</i>valâyana's <i>S</i>rauta-sûtra I, 11, 13. (On the Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta in the <i>S</i>rauta ritual, compare Hillebrandt, Neu- und Vollmondsopfer, p. 166.) The Prâ<i>g</i>âpatya oblation is performed with the formula Pra<i>g</i>âpataye svâhâ. The discussions of Nârâya<i>n</i>a on this Sûtra (see p. 125 of the German edition) evidently fail to bring out the true meaning of the text; according to this commentator the oblations follow each other in this order: the two Â<i>g</i>yabhâgas, the principal oblations (pradhânâhutaya<i>h</i>), the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t, the four Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>ti oblations, the two Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta oblations, the Prâ<i>g</i>âpatya oblation. Finally we may mention the corrupt form in which the corresponding passage of the <i>S</i>âmbavya-sûtra is preserved <a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 29</font></a> in the MS. There the two Sûtras 10 and 11 are placed before the Mantra in Sûtra 9. This Mantra then is given down to svâheti, then follows â<i>g</i>yena, which seems to me to form part of the same Sûtra, and to refer to the oblations to which the Mantra belongs. Then the MS. goes on: mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tishu sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>ittâra<i>m</i> (sic) etad âvâpasthâna<i>m</i> â<i>g</i>yahavishi vyâh<i>ri</i>tishu sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>ittâra<i>m</i> (the syllables prâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>ittâra<i>m</i> seem to be expunged) svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>to sthâlîpâke. In the commentary I find the following <i>S</i>lokas, which I give exactly as they are read in the MS.: tis<i>ri</i><i>n</i>â<i>m</i> vyâh<i>ri</i>tinâ<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a prâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>ittâhutîr api yad antara<i>m</i> tad âpâpâsthâna<i>m</i> sarpi<i>h</i>pradhânake. sthâlîpâke vyâh<i>ri</i>tinâ<i>m</i> yat tat svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>tottara<i>m</i> âhutînâ<i>m</i> pradhânânâ<i>m</i> nânâdaivata<i>kh</i>andasâ<i>m</i> yas tu kâlas tad âvâpasthânam itâ<i>k</i>yate budhai<i>h</i> tatas tat ta<i>m</i> ma ârabhya prâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>ittâhuti<i>h</i> kramât.</p>
