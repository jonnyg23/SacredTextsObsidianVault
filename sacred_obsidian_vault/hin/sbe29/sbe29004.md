 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>1. At the end of the sacrificial acts (follows) the distribution of food to Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>  
 <p>2. Voice, (pleasantness of) form, age, learning, moral character, (right) conduct are the qualities (required in the Brâhma<i>n</i>as who are to be invited thereto).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 16</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Learning, however, outweighs every (other qualification).</p>  
 <p>4. A learned one should not be passed over.</p>  
 <p>5. 'The threefold (knowledge, viz. that) which refers to the deities, that which refers to the Âtman, and that which refers to sacrifice,</p>  
 <p>'(Handed down) in the Mantras and in the Brâhma<i>n</i>a: this is called learning.</p>  
 <p>6. 'A performer of the sacred rights, a man who has studied (the Veda), who is old in learning and devoted to austerities:</p>  
 <p>'He who gives food (even) once to such (a Brâhma<i>n</i>a), hunger will not befall that man any more.</p>  
 <p>7. 'Whatsoever deity he may wish to satiate at any sacrifice,</p>  
 <p>'Destining it to that (deity) in his mind, he shall give (the food) to a person like that.</p>  
 <p>8. 'An oblation deposited in a person like that will never miss its way to the deity;</p>  
 <p>'Treasure of men, vessel of gods (in which they receive what is given to them) he is called.'</p>
