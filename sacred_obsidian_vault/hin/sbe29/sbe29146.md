 <p><a name="page_236"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 236</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ADHYÂYA IV, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 1.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_570"></a><a href="#fn_569"><font size="1">1</font></a>. If disease befalls one who has set up the (sacred <i>S</i>rauta) fires, he should leave his home (and go away) to the eastern, or northern, or north-eastern direction.</p>  
 <p>2. 'The sacred fires are fond of the village'thus it is said.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_571"></a><a href="#fn_570"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Longing for it, desirous of returning to the village they might restore him to health-thus it is understood (in the <i>S</i>ruti).</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_572"></a><a href="#fn_571"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Being restored to health, he should offer a Soma sacrifice, or an animal sacrifice, or an ordinary sacrifice, and take his dwelling (again in the village).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_573"></a><a href="#fn_572"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Or without such a sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>6. If he dies, one should have a piece of ground dug up to the south-east or to the south-west</p>  
 <p>7. At a place which is inclined towards the south or towards the south-east.</p>  
 <p>8. According to some (teachers), inclined towards south-west.</p>  
 <p>9. (The piece of ground dug up should be) of the length of a man with upraised arms,</p>  
 <p>10. Of the breadth of one Vyâma (fathom),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Of the depth of one Vitasti (span).</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="sbe29147.htm#fn_578"><font size="1">12</font></a>. The cemetery should be free from all sides.</p>  
 <p>13. It should be fertile in herbs.</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_573"><font size="1">14</font></a>. But plants with thorns and with milky juice, &amp;c., as stated above.</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_574"><font size="1">15</font></a>. From which the waters flow off to all sides: this is a characteristic required for the cemetery (<i>s</i>ma<i>s</i>âna) where the body is to be burned.</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_577"></a><a href="#fn_575"><font size="1">16</font></a>. 'They cut off (from the dead body) the hair, the beard, the hairs of the body, and the nails'this has been stated above.</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_578"></a><a href="#fn_576"><font size="1">17</font></a>. (They should provide) plenty of sacrificial grass and of butter.</p>  
 <p>18 <a name="fr_579"></a><a href="#fn_577"><font size="1">18</font></a>. They here pour clarified butter into curds.</p>  
 <p>19. This is the 'sprinkled butter' used for the Fathers (i.e. Manes).</p>
