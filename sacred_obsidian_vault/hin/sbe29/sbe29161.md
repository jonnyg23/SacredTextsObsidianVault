 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 7.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_706"><font size="1">1</font></a>. He then makes her tread on a stone, to the north of the fire, with her right foot, (repeating the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_283"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 283</font></a></p>  
 <p>verse,) 'Tread on this stone; like a stone be firm. Tread the foes down; turn away the enemies.'</p>  
 <p>2. He then sings a song: 'Sarasvatî! Promote this (our undertaking), O gracious one, bountiful one, thou whom we sing first of all that is, in whom what is, has been born, in whom this whole world dwellsthat song I will sing to-day which will be the highest glory of women.'</p>  
 <p>3. They then go round (the fire) with (the verse, which the bridegroom repeats,)</p>  
 <p>'To thee they have in the beginning carried round Sûryâ (the Sun-bride) with the bridal procession. Mayst thou give back, Agni, to the husbands the wife together with offspring.'</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_708"></a><a href="#fn_707"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Thus (the same rites are repeated) twice again, beginning from the fried grain.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_709"></a><a href="#fn_708"><font size="1">5</font></a>. The fourth time she pours the whole fried grain by the neb of a basket (into the fire) with (the words), 'To Bhaga svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>6. After he has led her round (the fire) three times, and has sacrificed the oblation to Pra<i>g</i>âpati</p>
