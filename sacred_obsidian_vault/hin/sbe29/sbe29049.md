 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 91</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ADHYÂYA III, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 1.</h3>  
 <p>1. A bath (shall be taken by the student) when he is going to return home (from his teacher).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_202"></a><a href="#fn_201"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 'A bull's hide'this has been declared. On that hide he makes him sit down and have his hair and beard cut and the hair of the body and the nails.</p>  
 <p>3. Having had (the cut-off hair-ends, &amp;c.) thrown away together with rice and barley, with sesamum-seed and mustard-seed, with Apâmârga and Sadâpushpî flowers,</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_203"></a><a href="#fn_202"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Having sprinkled him (with water) with the Âpohish<i>th</i>îyâ-hymn (Rig-veda X, 9),</p>  
 <p>5. Having adorned him,</p>  
 <p>6. Having dressed him with two garments with (the verse), 'The garments both of you' (Rig-veda I, 152, 1),</p>  
 <p>7. He then puts on him a golden ornament (with the words), 'Giving life and vigour' (Vâ<i>g</i>asaneyi Sa<i>m</i>hitâ XXXIV, 50).</p>  
 <p>8. With (the verse), 'Mine, Agni, be vigour' (Rig-veda X, 128, 1), the veiling (of the head is done).</p>  
 <p>9. With (the verse), 'House by house the shining one' (Rig-veda I, 123, 4) (he takes) the parasol,</p>  
 <p>10. With (the verse), 'Rise up' (Rig-veda X, 18, 6), the shoes,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 92</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. With (the verse), 'Long be thy hook' (Rig-veda VIII, 17, 10) he takes a bamboo staff.</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_204"></a><a href="#fn_203"><font size="1">12</font></a>. Let him sit that day in solitude.</p>  
 <p>13. With (the verses), 'O tree! with strong limbs,' and, 'A ruler indeed' (Rig-veda VI, 47, 26; X, 152, 1) let him mount the chariot.</p>  
 <p>14. (Before returning home) let him first approach a place where they will perform Argha for him with a cow or a goat.</p>  
 <p>15. Or let him return (making his start) from cows or from a fruit-bearing tree.</p>  
 <p>16. With (the verses), 'Indra, give us best goods,' and, 'Be friendly, O earth' (Rig-veda II, 21, 6; I, 22, 15) he descends (from the chariot).</p>  
 <p>17. Let him eat that day his favourite food.</p>  
 <p>18 <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">18</font></a>. To his teacher he shall give (that) pair of garments, the turban, ear-rings and jewel, staff and shoes, and the parasol.</p>
