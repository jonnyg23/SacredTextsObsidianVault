 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_135"></a><a href="#fn_134"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 'Under my will I take thy heart; my mind shall thy mind follow; in my word thou shalt rejoice with all thy heart; may B<i>ri</i>haspati join thee to me.'</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_136"></a><a href="#fn_135"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 'Thou art the Brahma<i>k</i>ârin of Kâma, N.N.!'</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_137"></a><a href="#fn_136"><font size="1">3</font></a>. With the same text (see chap. 3, 2) he turns round as before,</p>  
 <p>4. And touching with the span of his right hand (the student's) right shoulder, he murmurs:</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_138"></a><a href="#fn_137"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 'A student art thou. Put on fuel. Eat water. Do the service. Do not sleep in the day-time. Keep silence till the putting on of fuel.'</p>  
 <p>6. With (the words), 'Thine, Agni, is this piece of wood,' he puts the fuel on (the fire), or silently.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_134"></a><a href="sbe29034.htm#fr_135">65:1</a> 4, 1. Comp. Pârask. I, 8, 8, and the note on chap. 3, 3. See also Atharva-veda VI, 94, 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_135"></a><a href="sbe29034.htm#fr_136">65:2</a> As to Kâmasya brahma<i>k</i>âry asi, see my remarks in the Introduction, p. 9.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_136"></a><a href="sbe29034.htm#fr_137">65:3</a> He turns round as described, chap. 3, 2. Nârâya<i>n</i>a here also explains paryâv<i>ri</i>tya paryâvartana<i>m</i> kârayitvâ. See our note above, loc. cit.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_137"></a><a href="sbe29034.htm#fr_138">66:5</a> According to Nârâya<i>n</i>a the student correspondingly answers, to the teacher's word, 'A student art thou,' 'I will' (asâni), to 'Put on fuel,' 'I will put it on,' &amp;c. Eating water means sipping water after having eased oneself. On the putting on of fuel, comp. Sûtra 6 and chap. 10. The whole formula given in this Sûtra is already found in the <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a XI, 5, 4, 5.</p>
