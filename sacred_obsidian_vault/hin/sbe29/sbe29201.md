 <p><a name="page_360"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 360</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 11.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_937"></a><a href="#fn_936"><font size="1">1</font></a>. If an animal (is to be sacrificed), let him wash it, if it is not a cow; let him walk round the fires and drive in front (of them) a Palâ<i>s</i>a branch into the ground.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_938"></a><a href="#fn_937"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The winding (of a Ku<i>s</i>a rope) round (that branch), the touching (of the animal with the grass-blade), the binding (of it to the branch), and the sprinkling (of the animal with water) should be performed in the way prescribed (in the <i>S</i>rauta-sûtra), and whatever else (is to be done).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_939"></a><a href="#fn_938"><font size="1">3</font></a>. After he has sacrificed the two oblations before and after the killing of the animal, (he) silently (sacrifices) five other (oblations, directed to Pra<i>g</i>âpati).</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_940"></a><a href="#fn_939"><font size="1">4</font></a>. And the omentum is taken out (of the killed animal). He should besprinkle it (with water) and name the deity (to whom the sacrifice is directed).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_941"></a><a href="#fn_940"><font size="1">5</font></a>. (He should name that deity also) at the touching (of the animal with the grass-blade), at (its) being bound (to the branch), at its being sprinkled (with water), and at (the preparation and oblation) of the mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>6. After he has sacrificed the omentum, he cuts off the Avadâna portions,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p>7 <a name="fr_942"></a><a href="#fn_941"><font size="1">7</font></a>. All of them, or three, or five.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_943"></a><a href="#fn_942"><font size="1">8</font></a>. He sacrifices the Avadâna portions together with the mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>9. A limb of the animal is the sacrificial fee.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_944"></a><a href="#fn_943"><font size="1">10-11</font></a>. At (a sacrifice) directed to a special deity he should sacrifice (an animal) belonging to that deity, should make a portion for that (god), and should say to him (i.e. to the messenger who is to convey that offering to a place sacred to that deity): 'Take care that this may reach that (god).'</p>  
 <p>11. If there is a river between (the sacrificer and that sacred place), he may have a boat made, or he may dispense with this.</p>
