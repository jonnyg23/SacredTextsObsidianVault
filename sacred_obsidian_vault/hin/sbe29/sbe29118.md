 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 19.</h3>  
 <p>1. In the eighth year let him initiate a Brâhma<i>n</i>a,</p>  
 <p>2. Or in the eighth year after the conception;</p>  
 <p>3. In the eleventh a Kshatriya;</p>  
 <p>4. In the twelfth a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>5. Until the sixteenth (year) the time has not passed for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a;</p>  
 <p>6. Until the twenty-second for a Kshatriya;</p>  
 <p>7. Until the twenty-fourth for a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>8. After that (time has passed), they become patitasâvitrîka (i.e. they have lost their right of learning the Sâvitrî).</p>  
 <p>9. No one should initiate such men, nor teach them, nor perform sacrifices for them, nor have intercourse with them.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_455"></a><a href="#fn_454"><font size="1">10</font></a>. (Let him initiate) the youth who is adorned and whose (hair on the) head is arranged, who wears a (new) garment that has not yet been washed, or an antelope-skin, if he is a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, the skin of a spotted deer, if a Kshatriya, a goat's skin, if a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>11. If they put on garments, they should put on dyed (garments): the Brâhma<i>n</i>a a reddish yellow one, the Kshatriya a light red one, the Vai<i>s</i>ya a yellow one.</p>  
 <p>12. Their girdles are: that of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a made of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass, that of a Kshatriya a bow-string, that of a Vai<i>s</i>ya woollen.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 188</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Their staffs are: that of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood, that of a Kshatriya of Udumbara wood, that of a Vai<i>s</i>ya of Bilva wood.</p>
