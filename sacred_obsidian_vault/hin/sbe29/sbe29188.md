 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 15.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the full-moon day of Praush<i>th</i>apada the sacrifice to Indra.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_848"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Having cooked milk-rice for Indra and cakes, and having put cakes round (the fire), he sacrifices the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions and Â<i>g</i>ya oblations to Indra, to Indrâ<i>n</i>î, to A<i>g</i>a Ekapad, to Ahi Budhnya, and to the Prosh<i>th</i>apadâs.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">3</font></a>. After he has eaten (his portion of the sacrificial food), he offers a Bali to the Maruts. For the <i>S</i>ruti says, 'The Maruts eat what is not-sacrificed.'</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">4</font></a>. (This Bali he offers) in A<i>s</i>vattha leaves, because it is said, 'The Maruts stood in the A<i>s</i>vattha tree.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_332"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 332</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">5</font></a>. (He offers it) with (the texts), 'Brilliantly resplendent' (Var. Sa<i>m</i>h. XVII, 80-85), Mantra by Mantra,</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And with the (Mantra called) Vimukha.</p>  
 <p>7. (This Mantra he repeats only) in his mind.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">8</font></a>. For the <i>S</i>ruti says, 'These are their names.'</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">9</font></a>. He murmurs, 'To Indra the divine' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XVII, 86).</p>  
 <p>10. Then (follows) the feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_848"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_849">331:2</a> 15, 2. After these Â<i>g</i>ya oblations follows the chief oblation of the whole sacrifice, the oblation of milk-rice to Indra. In one of Professor Stenzler's MSS. there is a special Sûtra inserted after Sûtra 2, 'Of the cooked food he makes an oblation with (the formula), "To Indra svâhâ."' I do not, however, think it right to receive this Sûtra into the text, as the other MSS. do not support it, and the commentators did not find it in the text which they read.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_849"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_850">331:3</a> Professor Stenzler's translation, 'Die Maruts essen kein Opfer,' seems to me not quite exact. I should prefer to say, 'Die Maruts essen Nicht-Opfer.' This passage, taken from <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a IV, 5, 2, 16, is quoted as supporting the rule that a Bali offering should be made to the Maruts; for in the technical language the term ahuta is applied to Bali offerings (<i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya I, 10, 7, huto<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gnihotrahomena, ahuto balikarma<i>n</i>â).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_850"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_851">331:4</a> When Indra called them to his help against V<i>ri</i>tra. <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a IV, 3, 3, 6.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_851"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_852">332:5</a> This Sûtra is identical with the last words of Kâty. XVIII, 4, 23.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_852"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_853">332:6</a> This is the first part of Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XVII, 86.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_853"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_854">332:8</a> <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a IX, 3, I, 26. There it is said that <i>s</i>ukra<i>g</i>yoti<i>s</i> ('brilliantly resplendent') &amp;c. (the words used in Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XVII, 80) are names of the Maruts.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_854"></a><a href="sbe29188.htm#fr_855">332:9</a> This Sûtra is identical with Kâty. XVIII, 4, 25.</p>
