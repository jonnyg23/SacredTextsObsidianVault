 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_693"></a><a href="#fn_692"><font size="1">1-5</font></a>. There are four kinds of Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>as, viz. the huta, the ahuta, the prahuta, and the prâ<i>s</i>ita.</p>  
 <p>2. On the following five occasions, viz. the wedding, the tonsure (of the child's head), the initiation (of the Brahma<i>k</i>ârin), the cutting of the beard, and the parting of the hair, (on these occasions) in the outer hall,</p>  
 <p>3. On a place that has been smeared (with cow-dung), which is elevated, and which has been sprinkled (with water), he establishes the fire,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 277</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Having kindled it by attrition, according to some teachers, at his marriage.</p>  
 <p>5. During the northern course of the sun, in the time of the increasing moon, on an auspicious day he shall seize the hand of a girl,</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_694"></a><a href="#fn_693"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Under one of the (three times) three Nakshatras of which a constellation designated as Uttara is first,</p>  
 <p>7. Or under (the Nakshatras) Svâti, M<i>ri</i>ga<i>s</i>iras, or Rohi<i>n</i>î.</p>  
 <p>8. Three (wives are allowed) to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, in accordance with the order of the castes,</p>  
 <p>9. Two to a Râ<i>g</i>anya,</p>  
 <p>10. One to a Vai<i>s</i>ya,</p>  
 <p>11. One <i>S</i>ûdra wife besides to all, according to some (teachers), without using Mantras (at the ceremonies of wedding, &amp;c.).</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_695"></a><a href="#fn_694"><font size="1">12</font></a>. He then makes her put on the (under) garment with (the verse), 'Live to old age; put on the garment! Be a protectress of the human tribes against imprecation. Live a hundred years full of vigour; clothe thyself in wealth and children. Blessed with life put on this garment!'</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_696"></a><a href="#fn_695"><font size="1">13</font></a>. Then the upper garment with (the verse), 'The goddesses who spun, who wove, who spread</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 278</font></a></p>  
 <p>out, and who drew out the threads on both sides, may those goddesses clothe thee for the sake of long life. Blessed with life put on this garment!'</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_697"></a><a href="#fn_696"><font size="1">14</font></a>. (The bride's father?) anoints the two, (while the bridegroom recites the verse,) 'May the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, may the waters unite our hearts. May Mâtari<i>s</i>van, may Dhât<i>ri</i>, may Desh<i>t</i>rî (the 'showing' goddess) join us.'</p>  
 <p>15. (The bridegroom), having accepted her who is given away by her father, takes her and goes away (from that place) with (the verse), 'When thou wanderest far away with thy heart to the regions of the world like the wind, may the gold-winged Vaikar<i>n</i>a (i.e. the wind?) grant that thy heart may dwell with me! N.N.!'</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_698"></a><a href="#fn_697"><font size="1">16</font></a>. He then makes them look at each other (while the bridegroom repeats the verses), 'With no evil eye, not bringing death to thy husband, bring luck to the cattle, be full of joy and vigour. Give birth to heroes; be godly and friendly. Bring us luck, to men and animals.</p>  
 <p>'Soma has acquired (thee) first (as his wife); after him the Gandharva has acquired (thee). Thy third husband is Agni; the fourth is thy human husband.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_279"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 279</font></a></p>  
 <p>'Soma has given thee to the Gandharva; the Gandharva has given thee to Agni. Wealth and children Agni has given to me, and besides this wife.</p>  
 <p>'Pûshan! Lead her to us, the highly blessed one. Sâ na ûrû u<i>s</i>atî vihara, yasyâm u<i>s</i>anta<i>h</i> praharâma <i>s</i>epa<i>m</i> yasyâm u kâmâ bahavo nivish<i>t</i>yâ (nivish<i>t</i>â?) iti.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_693"></a><a href="sbe29158.htm#fr_694">277:6</a> I.e. under the constellations Uttaraphalgunî or the two constellations following it, Uttarâshâ<i>dh</i>â or the two constellations following it, Uttarabhâdrapadâ or the two constellations following it.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_694"></a><a href="sbe29158.htm#fr_695">277:12</a> The words of the Mantra bhavâ k<i>ri</i>sh<i>t</i>înâm abhi<i>s</i>astipâvâ no doubt are an imitation of Rig-veda I, 76, 3, bhavâ ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>â<i>n</i>âm abhi<i>s</i>astipâvâ (where the words are applied to Agni). Thus the use of the masculine abhi<i>s</i>astipâvâ with reference to the bride may be accounted for.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_695"></a><a href="sbe29158.htm#fr_696">277:13</a> Comp. Atharva-veda XIV, 1, 45. This parallel passage shows us the way to correct the text of this very much corrupted Mantra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_696"></a><a href="sbe29158.htm#fr_697">278:14</a> The literal translation would be: 'He salves together (sama<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ayati) the two . . . May the waters salve together (sama<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>antu) our hearts.' It was a real anointing of the bridegroom and of the bride, that took place, and we cannot accept Professor Stenzler's translation (based on <i>G</i>ayarâma's note: sama<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ayati paraspara<i>m</i> sammukhîkaroti), by which the proper signification of sama<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ayati is effaced: Dann heisst (der Vater der Braut) sie beide zusammentreten. See the note on <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya I, 12, 5. The parallel passage of the Khâdira-G<i>ri</i>hya runs thus: apare<i>n</i>âgnim auduko gatvâ pâ<i>n</i>igrâha<i>m</i> mûrdhany avasi<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ed, vadhû<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a, sama<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>antv ity avasikta<i>h</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_697"></a><a href="sbe29158.htm#fr_698">278:16</a> Comp. Rig-veda X, 85, 44. 40. 41. 37.</p>
