 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 3.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_491"></a><a href="#fn_490"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On the full moon of Mârga<i>s</i>îrsha the 'redescent' (is performed)on the fourteenth (Tithi),</p>  
 <p>2. Or on (the Tithi of) the full moon (itself).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_492"></a><a href="#fn_491"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Having again renovated the house by (giving a new) coating (to the walls), by spreading out (a new roof), and by levelling (the floor), they should sacrifice after sunset (oblations) of milk-rice with (the texts), 'Beat away, O white one, with thy foot, with the forefoot and with the hind-foot, these seven daughters of Varu<i>n</i>a and all that belong to the king's tribe. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'Within the dominion of the white one no serpent has killed anything. To the white one, the son of Vidârva, adoration! Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>4. Here no oblation to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t (is made).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_493"></a><a href="#fn_492"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 'May we be secure from Pra<i>g</i>âpati's sons'thus he murmurs while looking at the fire.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. (Saying), 'Be happy and friendly to us'he should think in his mind of the winter.</p>  
 <p>7. To the west of the fire is a well-spread layer (of straw); on that he should sit down, murmur (the verse), 'Be soft, O earth' (Rig-veda I, 22, 15), and lie down (on that layer) with his people, with the head to the east and the face to the north.</p>  
 <p>8. The others, as there is room.</p>  
 <p>9. Or following on each other from the eldest to the youngest.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_494"></a><a href="#fn_493"><font size="1">10</font></a>. Those who know the Mantras, should murmur the Mantras.</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_495"></a><a href="#fn_494"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Arising (they should) three times (murmur the verse), 'From that place may the gods bless us' (Rig-veda I, 22, 16).</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_496"></a><a href="#fn_495"><font size="1">12</font></a>. The same (verse) a fourth time with their faces to the south, to the west, and to the north.</p>  
 <p>13. Having arisen, having murmured the hymns sacred to the Sun and the auspicious hymns, having prepared food and given to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as to eat, he should cause (them) to pronounce auspicious words.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_490"></a><a href="sbe29126.htm#fr_491">204:1</a> 3, 1. On the ceremony of 'redescent,' comp. <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya IV, 17; Pâraskara III, 2. The fourteenth Tithi of the bright fortnight, preceding the full moon, is referred to.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_491"></a><a href="sbe29126.htm#fr_492">204:3</a> 'Again' refers to chap. 2, 2. As to the words 'they should sacrifice,' comp. the note on the same Sûtra. The first Mantra reoccurs in <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya IV, 18, 1. The text of the second should be, na vai <i>s</i>vetasyâdhyâ<i>k</i>âre, &amp;c.; comp. Pâraskara II, 14, 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_492"></a><a href="sbe29126.htm#fr_493">204:5</a> The serpents are the children of Ka<i>s</i>yapa (i.e. Pra<i>g</i>âpati) and Kadrû; see Mahâbhârata I, 1074 seqq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_493"></a><a href="sbe29126.htm#fr_494">205:10</a> 'The Mantras beginning from "Be soft, O earth" (Sûtra 7) down to the auspicious hymns (Sûtra 13).' Nârâya<i>n</i>a.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_494"></a><a href="sbe29126.htm#fr_495">205:11</a> It follows from Sûtra 12 that they are to turn here their faces to the east.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_495"></a><a href="sbe29126.htm#fr_496">205:12</a> They mutter one Pâda of that verse, which is in the Gâyatrî metre, turned towards each of the three directions.</p>
