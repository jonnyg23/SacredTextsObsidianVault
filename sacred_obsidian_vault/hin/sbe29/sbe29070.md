 <p><a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 119</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 8.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_271"></a><a href="#fn_270"><font size="1">1</font></a>. And to (students) who have been duly initiated he shall set forth (the Veda);</p>  
 <p>2. The teacher sitting to the east or to the north, the other one to the south, with his face turned to the north.</p>  
 <p>3. Or two (students shall be so seated).</p>  
 <p>4. But more (than two) as there is room (for them).</p>  
 <p>5. He should not sit on a high seat in presence of a Guru,</p>  
 <p>6. Nor on the same seat (with him),</p>  
 <p>7. Nor with outstretched feet,</p>  
 <p>8. Nor stretching his arms under his knees,</p>  
 <p>9. Nor leaning his body (against a support), to. Nor forming with his feet a lap,</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_272"></a><a href="#fn_271"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Nor holding his feet like an axe.</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_273"></a><a href="#fn_272"><font size="1">12</font></a>. After (the student) has said, Recite, sir!' the teacher shall cause him to pronounce the syllable OM.</p>  
 <p>13. 'OM,' replies the other.</p>  
 <p>14. Thereafter let him recite uninterruptedly.</p>  
 <p>15. When he has recited, he embraces (his teacher's feet),</p>  
 <p>16. Says, 'We have finished, sir!' and (goes away) according to his business.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_120"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 120</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. (He shall say,) 'Leave! Pause meanwhile!' according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>18. Let no one step between (a teacher and students) who study.</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_274"></a><a href="#fn_273"><font size="1">19</font></a>. Let no one change his place during the recitation.</p>  
 <p>20. Should any fault be committed, let him fast three days, or one day and one night, repeat the Sâvitrî as long as he can, and give something to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as; then after an interruption of one day and one night the study should go on.</p>
