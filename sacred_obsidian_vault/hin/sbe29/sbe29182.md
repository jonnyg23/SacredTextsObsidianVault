 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 9.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_816"></a><a href="#fn_815"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now (follow) the five great sacrifices.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_817"></a><a href="#fn_816"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Of the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva food he should, after having sprinkled (water) round (the sacred fire), make oblations, with the word Svâhâ (each time repeated), to Brahman, to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, to the (deities) of the house, to Ka<i>s</i>yapa, and to Anumati.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_818"></a><a href="#fn_817"><font size="1">3</font></a>. To the domestic deities (he offers) three</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_320"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 320</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (Balis) in the water-pot: to Par<i>g</i>anya, to the waters, to the Earth;</p>  
 <p>4. To Dhât<i>ri</i> and Vidhât<i>ri</i> at the two doorposts;</p>  
 <p>5. To the different quarters (of the horizon), to Vâyu and (to the presiding deities) of the quarters;</p>  
 <p>6. In the middle three (Balis) to Brahman, to the Air, to the Sun.</p>  
 <p>7. To the north of those (he offers Balis) to the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs and to all the beings;</p>  
 <p>8. Further on to Ushas and to the Lord of beings;</p>  
 <p>9. To the south (to the Fathers) with (the words), To the Fathers, Svadhâ! Adoration!'</p>  
 <p>10. Having rinsed out the vessel, he should pour it out towards the north-west with (the words), 'Consumption! this to thee!'</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_819"></a><a href="#fn_818"><font size="1">11</font></a>. Taking the Brâhma<i>n</i>a's portion (of the food which he is going to distribute), he should give it to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, after he has made him wash himself, with (the words), 'Well! (this) to thee!'</p>  
 <p>12. To (religious) mendicants and to guests they should apportion (food) as due to them.</p>  
 <p>13. The persons belonging to the house, the young and the old, should eat what is due to them;</p>  
 <p>14. Afterwards the householder and his wife.</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_820"></a><a href="#fn_819"><font size="1">15</font></a>. Or the householder (should eat) first, because</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_321"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 321</font></a></p>  
 <p>the <i>S</i>ruti says, 'Therefore the householder should eat the sweetest food before his guests.'</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_821"></a><a href="#fn_820"><font size="1">16</font></a>. Every day he should sacrifice with the word svâhâ. If he has no food (to offer, he should make his offering) with something else, be it even a piece of wood (only), to the gods, or be it (only) a water-pot, to the Fathers and to men.</p>
