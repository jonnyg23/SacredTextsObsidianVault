 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 12.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_833"></a><a href="#fn_832"><font size="1">1</font></a>. In (the month) Pausha, under (the Nakshatra) Rohi<i>n</i>î, or at the middle Ash<i>t</i>akâ let them celebrate the conclusion of the study (of the Veda).</p>  
 <p>2. Let them go to the brink of water and make water oblations to the gods, the metres, the Vedas, the <i>Ri</i>shis, the ancient teachers, the Gandharvas, the other teachers, the year with its divisions, and to their own ancestors and teachers.</p>  
 <p>3. After having four times quickly recited the Sâvitrî, they should say, 'We have finished.'</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_834"></a><a href="#fn_833"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Interruption (of the study) and (continuation of the) teaching as stated above.</p>
