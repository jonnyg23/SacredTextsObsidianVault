 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Let them go to a clean spot in the north-eastern direction, that receives its light from the east.</p>  
 <p>4. The drawing of water (should be done) before sunrise,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 143</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">5</font></a>. And the entering into the circle with this verse, 'She who smells of salve' (Rig-veda X, 146, 6).</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">6</font></a>. The circle should have its entrance to the east or to the north; it should be (praised as) excellent among the people, not too spacious, not too narrow.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">7</font></a>. The final expiation (should extend) to the Vâmadevya.</p>  
 <p>8. And the invitation to resume the recitation (is done in the following way)</p>  
 <p>9. After they have sipped water that stands outside the circle,</p>  
 <p>10. Let them resume the recitation, having performed the expiation.</p>  
 <p>11. If the vessel used in the expiation is damaged, sprinkling (with water forms) the expiatory act (to be performed for it).</p>  
 <p>12. (That) sprinkling, however, (one should perform) holding gold or a bunch of Darbha grass in his hand.</p>  
 <p>13. So far what pertains to the general rules.</p>
