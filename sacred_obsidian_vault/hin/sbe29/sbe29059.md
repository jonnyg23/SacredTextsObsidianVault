 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 11.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_219"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now (follows) the V<i>ri</i>shotsarga (i.e. setting a bull at liberty).</p>  
 <p>2. On the Kârttika full moon day or on that day of the Â<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i>a (month) that falls under (the Nakshatra) Revatî</p>  
 <p>3. He sacrifices, after having kindled amid the cows a well-inflamed fire, Â<i>g</i>ya oblations (with the words),</p>  
 <p>4. Here is delight; take delight here. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Here is still-standing; here is (your) own still-standing. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'I have let the calf join its mother. May the calf, sucking its mother's breast, support increase of wealth among us. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>5. With the verse, 'May Pûshan go after our cows' (Rig-veda VI, 54, 5) he sacrifices from (a mess of sacrificial food) belonging to Pûshan.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_221"></a><a href="#fn_220"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Having murmured the Rudra-(hymns),</p>  
 <p>7. (He takes) a one-coloured, two-coloured, or three-coloured (bull),</p>  
 <p>8. Or one that protects the herd,</p>  
 <p>9. Or that is protected by the herd,</p>  
 <p>10. Or it may also be red.</p>  
 <p>11. It should have all its limbs complete, and be the finest (bull) in the herd.</p>  
 <p>12. Having adorned that (bull),</p>  
 <p>13. And the four best young cows of the herd, having adorned those too,</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_222"></a><a href="#fn_221"><font size="1">14</font></a>. (He says,) 'This young (bull) I give you as your husband; sporting with him, your lover, walk about. Do not desert us (?), being joined (with us) from your birth. In increase of wealth, in food may we rejoice. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>15. When (the bull) is in the midst (of the cows), he recites over (them), 'Refreshing,' &amp;c. (Rig-veda X, 169, I seq.) down to the end of the Anuvâka.</p>  
 <p>16. With the milk of all of them he shall cook milk-rice and feed Brâhma<i>n</i>as with it.</p>
