 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 17.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_293"></a><a href="#fn_292"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On the Âgrahâya<i>n</i>î full moon day he shall redescend,</p>  
 <p>2. (Or) under (the Nakshatra) Rohi<i>n</i>î, or under the Prosh<i>th</i>apadâs.</p>  
 <p>3. In the morning, having taken a handfull of <i>S</i>amî leaves, Madhûka flowers, reeds, Apâmârga plants, and of <i>S</i>irîsha, Udumbara, Ku<i>s</i>a shoots, and jujube fruits, and an earth-clod (taken) out of a furrow,</p>  
 <p>4. Having put (all that) into a water-pot,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 131</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_294"></a><a href="#fn_293"><font size="1">5</font></a>. And, after he has quickly repeated the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis and the Sâvitrî, having repeatedly immersed (it) therein with this hymn, 'May he burn away from us pain' (Rig-veda I, 97), he shall drive away the evil from the persons standing under his protection, from left to right, and pour out (the water) to the north.</p>  
 <p>6. A Madhuparka is the fee for the sacrifice.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_292"></a><a href="sbe29079.htm#fr_293">130:1</a> 17, 1. The Pratyavaroha<i>n</i>a (i.e. redescent) here described is the ceremony performed at the end of the time during which sleeping on high bedsteads is prescribed (chap. 15, 22). Beginning from the <i>S</i>râva<i>n</i>î full moon till the Pratyavaroha<i>n</i>a, the offerings to the Serpents mentioned above have to be repeated every day (chap. 15, 19); the Pratyavaroha<i>n</i>a is the concluding ceremony of these rites devoted to the Serpents.</p>
