 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 11.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_169"></a><a href="#fn_168"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now (follows) the directing to the (special) observances.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_170"></a><a href="#fn_169"><font size="1">2-4</font></a>. The rules for it have been explained by the initiation.</p>  
 <p>3. He does not recite the Sâvitrî.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 77</font></a></p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_171"></a><a href="#fn_170"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Some say that the handing over of the staff forms the end (of this ceremony).</p>  
 <p>5. During the northern course of the sun, in the time of the increasing moon</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_172"></a><a href="#fn_171"><font size="1">6</font></a>. The teacher having abstained through one day and one night, from sexual intercourse and from eating flesh</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_173"></a><a href="#fn_172"><font size="1">7</font></a>. With the exclusion of the fourteenth day and of the eighth (of the half-month),</p>  
 <p>8. And of the first and last, according to some (teachers),</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_174"></a><a href="#fn_173"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Or on what day else the constellation seems lucky to him, on that day he shall direct (the student) to the duties of holiness according to the <i>S</i>ukriya rite.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_175"></a><a href="#fn_174"><font size="1">10</font></a>. Let him observe (those) duties through three days, or twelve days, or one year, or as long as the master may think fit.</p>  
 <p>11. The <i>S</i>âkvara (observance), however, (is to be kept) one year.</p>  
 <p>12. (So also) the Vrâtika and Aupanishada (observances).</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_176"></a><a href="#fn_175"><font size="1">13</font></a>. When the time has elapsed, when the duties</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 78</font></a></p>  
 <p>have been observed, when the Veda has been studied down to the <i>S</i>a<i>m</i>yu-Bârhaspatya-(hymn), let (the teacher then), should he intend to instruct (the student) in the secret (part of the Veda), ascertain the time (through which the student has to observe the special rites) and the rules to be observed, from the (special) directions (that are handed down on this subject).</p>
