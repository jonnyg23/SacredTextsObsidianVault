 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 10.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_522"></a><a href="#fn_521"><font size="1">1</font></a>. It has been declared how he should enter the house (when returning from a journey).</p>  
 <p>2. The house, when he enters it, should be provided with seed-corn.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_523"></a><a href="#fn_522"><font size="1">3</font></a>. He should have his field ploughed under the Nakshatras Uttarâ<i>h</i> Prosh<i>th</i>apadâs, (Uttarâ<i>h</i>) Phâlgunyas, or Rohi<i>n</i>î.</p>  
 <p>4. In order that the wind may blow to him from the field, he should offer oblations with the hymn, 'Through the lord of the field' (Rig-veda IV, 57), verse by verse, or he should murmur (that hymn).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_524"></a><a href="#fn_523"><font size="1">5</font></a>. He should speak over the cows when they go away, the two verses, 'May refreshing wind blow over the cows' (Rig-veda X, 169, I seq.).</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_525"></a><a href="#fn_524"><font size="1">6</font></a>. When they come back, (he should recite the following verses,)</p>  
 <p>'May they whose udder with its four holes is full</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_216"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 216</font></a></p>  
 <p>of honey and ghee, be milk-givers to us; (may they be) many in our stable, rich in ghee.</p>  
 <p>'Come hither to me, giving refreshment, bringing vigour and strength. Giving inexhaustible milk, rest in my stable that I may become the highest one'</p>  
 <p>And, 'They who have raised their body up to the gods'the rest of the hymn (Rig-veda X, 169, 3. 4).</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_526"></a><a href="#fn_525"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Some recite (instead of the texts stated in Sûtra 6) the Âgâvîya hymn.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_527"></a><a href="#fn_526"><font size="1">8</font></a>. He should approach their herds, if the cows do not belong to his Guru, with (the words), 'Prospering are ye; excellent are ye, beautiful, dear. May I become dear to you. May you see bliss in me.'</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Second Adhyâya.</p>
