 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 7.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_39"></a><a href="#fn_38"><font size="1">1</font></a>. When assent has been declared (by the girl's father, the bridegroom) sacrifices.</p>  
 <p>2. He besmears a quadrangular space with cow-dung.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_40"></a><a href="#fn_39"><font size="1">3</font></a>. (Let him consider in the ceremonies to be performed,) of the two eastern intermediate directions, the southern one as that to which (the rites) should be directed, if the rites belong to the Manes,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
  of the two eastern intermediate directions, sacred to Î<i>s</i>âna, should be considered as that to which the ceremonies sacred to the gods, such as oblations, &amp;c., are to be directed.'Comp. Â<i>s</i>valâyana-<i>S</i>raut. I, 12, 4.}</p>  
 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 23</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. The northern one, if the rites belong to the gods,</p>  
 <p>5. Or rather the east (itself) according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_41"></a><a href="#fn_40"><font size="1">6-7</font></a>. He draws in the middle (of the sacrificial ground) a line from south to north,</p>  
 <p>7. Upwards from this, turned upwards, to the south one line, in the middle one, to the north one.</p>  
 <p>8. These he sprinkles (with water),</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_42"></a><a href="#fn_41"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Carries forward the fire with the verse, 'I carry forward Agni with genial mind; may he be the assembler of goods. Do no harm to us, to the old nor to the young; be a saviour to us, to men and animals,'</p>  
 <p>10. Or (he carries it forward) silently,</p>  
 <p>11. Then he wipes with his wet hand three times around the fire, turning his right side to it. This they call SAMÛHANA (sweeping together).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 24</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Once, turning his left side to it, in the rites belonging to the Manes.</p>
