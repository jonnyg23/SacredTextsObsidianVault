 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 16.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_856"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On the full-moon day of Â<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i>a the (offerings of) P<i>ri</i><i>s</i>hâtakas (are made).</p>  
 <p>2. Having cooked milk-rice for Indra he sacrifices it, mixed with curds, honey, and ghee, to Indra, Indrâ<i>n</i>î, the two A<i>s</i>vins, the full moon of Â<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i>a, and to the autumn.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_857"></a><a href="#fn_856"><font size="1">3</font></a>. After he has eaten (his portion of the sacrificial food), he sacrifices with his joined hands a P<i>ri</i>shâtaka prepared with curds, with the words, 'May what is deficient be made full to me; may what is full not decay to me. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>4. The inmates of the house look at the mixture of curds, honey, and ghee, with the Anuvâka,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 333</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'May Indra come hither' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XX, 47 seqq.).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_858"></a><a href="#fn_857"><font size="1">5</font></a>. They let the calves join their mothers that night and the Âgrahâya<i>n</i>î night.</p>  
 <p>6. Then (follows) the feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
