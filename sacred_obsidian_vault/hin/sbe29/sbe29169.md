 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 15.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the Sîmantonnayana (or parting of the pregnant wife's hair).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_736"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">2</font></a>. (it is performed) like the Pu<i>m</i>savana;</p>  
 <p>3. In her first pregnancy, in the sixth or eighth month.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">15_4</font></a>. After he has cooked a mess of sacrificial food, containing sesamum and Mudga beans, and has sacrificed to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, he parts for the wife, who is seated to the west of the fire on a soft chair, her hair upwards (i.e. beginning from the front) with a bunch containing an even number of unripe Udumbara</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 293</font></a></p>  
 <p>fruits, and with three bunches of Darbha grass, with a porcupine's quill that has three white spots, with a stick of Vîratara wood, and with a full spindle. with the words, 'Bhûr bhuva<i>h</i> sva<i>h</i>.'</p>  
 <p>5. Or (he parts the hair once) with each of the (three) Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">6</font></a>. He ties (the Udumbara fruits, &amp;c.) to a string of three twisted threads with (the words), 'Rich in sap is this tree; like the tree, rich in sap, be thou fruitful.'</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">7</font></a>. (The husband) then says to two lute-players, 'Sing ye the king, or if anybody else is still more valiant.'</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">8</font></a>. Here some also prescribe a certain stanza (to be sung by the lute-players): 'Soma alone is our king. May these human tribes dwell on thy banks, O (river) whose dominion is unbroken, N.N.!'here he names the name of the river near which they dwell.</p>  
 <p>9. Then (follows) feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
