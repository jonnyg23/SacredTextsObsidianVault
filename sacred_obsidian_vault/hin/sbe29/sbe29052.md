 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">1</font></a>. At the sacrifice to Vâstoshpati</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Having established the (sacred) domestic fire outside with (the words), 'I place (here) Agni with genial mind; may he be the assembler of goods. Do no harm to us, to the old nor to the young; be a saviour to us, to men and animals!'</p>  
 <p>3. Having put a new water-pot on fresh eastward-pointed Ku<i>s</i>a-grass,</p>  
 <p>4. And spoken over it (the words), 'Unhurt be our men, may our riches not be squandered!'</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">5-7</font></a>. He sacrifices three oblations in the forenoon with the Stotriya text of the Rathantara with repetition and Kakubh-forming;</p>  
 <p>6. (Three oblations with the Stotriya) of the Vâmadevya at midday;</p>  
 <p>7. Of the B<i>ri</i>hat in the afternoon;</p>  
 <p>8. The four Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis, the three verses, 'Vâstoshpati!' (Rig-veda VII, 54, 1-3), (the single verses,) 'Driving away calamity,' (and) 'Vâstoshpati,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a></p>  
 <p>a firm post' (Rig-veda VII, 55, 1; VIII, 17, 14), and to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t a tenth oblation of cooked food at night.</p>  
 <p>9. Taking with himself his eldest son and his wife, carrying grain, let him enter (the house with the words),</p>  
 <p>'Indra's house is blessed, wealthy, protecting; that I enter with my wife, with offspring, with cattle, with increase of wealth, with everything that is mine.'</p>
