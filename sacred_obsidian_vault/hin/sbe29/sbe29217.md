 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 5.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1022"></a><a href="#fn_1021"><font size="1">1-21</font></a>. At the Godâna (or cutting of the beard) the</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">5, 1. atha goalie <i>k</i>aulavat kalpa<i>h</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_403"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 403</font></a></p>  
 <p>rite is identical with the <i>K</i>aula (cutting of the hair; see above, chap. 3, 16 seqq.).</p>  
 <p>2. He should have (his beard) and the hair of his body shaven.</p>  
 <p>3. The sacrificial fee consists of an ox and a cow, or of a pair of horses, or of sheep, for the (three) castes respectively,</p>  
 <p>4. Or of a cow for all (castes).</p>  
 <p>5. A goat (is given) to the person who catches up the hair.</p>  
 <p>6. The initiation (connected with the Godânakarman, &amp;c.) has been declared.</p>  
 <p>7. (One should) not initiate one who does not intend to keep the vow through one year.</p>  
 <p>8. (The use of) a garment, however, which has not yet been washed (see chap. 4, 7), is not prescribed (here),</p>  
 <p>9. Nor the adornment (chap. 4, 7).</p>  
 <p>10. (The observances prescribed for the Godânavrata are the following:)</p>  
 <p>He should sleep on the ground.</p>  
 <p>11. He should avoid eating honey and flesh.</p>  
 <p>12. He should avoid sexual intercourse, shaving, (luxurious) bathing, combing his head, and cleansing his teeth and his feet (in a luxurious way).</p>  
 <p>13. nâsya kâme reta skandet.</p>  
 <p>14. Let him not mount a chariot yoked with cows,</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">2. saloma<i>m</i> vâpayed. 3. go<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font><i>s</i>vâvimithunâni dakshi<i>n</i>â<i>h</i> p<i>ri</i>thag var<i>n</i>ânâ<i>m</i>. 4. sarveshâ<i>m</i> vâ gaur. 5. a<i>g</i>a<i>h</i> ke<i>s</i>apratigrahâyo. 6. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>ktam upanayana<i>m</i>. 7. nâ<i>k</i>arishyanta<i>m</i> sa<i>m</i>vatsaram. 8. aniyuktan tv ahatam. 9. athâla<i>m</i>kâro. 10. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>dhassa<i>m</i>ve<i>s</i>y. 11. amadhumâ<i>m</i>sâ<i>s</i>î syân. 12. maithunakshurak<i>ri</i>tyasnânâvalekhanadantadhâvanapâdadhâvanâni var<i>g</i>ayen. 13. nâsya kâme reta skanden. 14. na goyuktam ârohen.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_404"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 404</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Nor (wear) shoes in the village.</p>  
 <p>16. Wearing the girdle, going the rounds for alms, (carrying) a staff, putting fuel (on the fire), touching water, reverentially saluting (the teacher) in the morning: (these are the) standing (duties).</p>  
 <p>17. The Godâna-vrata, the Vrâtika-vrata, the Âditya-vrata, the Upanishad-vrata, and the <i>G</i>yesh<i>th</i>asâma-vrata (last) one year (each).</p>  
 <p>18. The Âditya-vrata some (do) not (undergo).</p>  
 <p>19. They who undergo it, wear one garment.</p>  
 <p>20. They allow nothing to be between (themselves and) the sun.</p>  
 <p>21. And they do not descend into water.</p>  
 <p>22 <a name="fr_1023"></a><a href="#fn_1022"><font size="1">22-34</font></a>. For the <i>S</i>akvarî verses, twelve, nine, six, or three (years through which the Vrata is to be kept) make up the various possibilities.</p>  
 <p>23. He (who keeps the <i>S</i>âkvara-vrata) wears dark clothes.</p>  
 <p>24. He eats dark food.</p>  
 <p>25. He is entirely addicted to his teacher.</p>  
 <p>26. He should stand in day-time.</p>  
 <p>27. He should sit at night.</p>  
 <p>28. According to some (teachers, the Vrata may last only) one year, if the ancestors (of the student) have learnt (the <i>S</i>akvarî verses).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">25. na grâma upânahau. 16. mekhalâdhâranabhaikshâ<i>k</i>ara<i>n</i>ada<i>n</i><i>d</i>asamidâdhânopaspar<i>s</i>anaprâtarabhivâdâ nitya<i>m</i>. 1 7. godânavrâtikâdityavratopanisha<i>g</i><i>g</i>yesh<i>th</i>asâmikâs sa<i>m</i>vatsarâ. 18. nâdityavratam ekeshâ<i>m</i>. 19. ye <i>k</i>aranty ekavâsaso bhavanty. 20. âditya<i>ñ</i> <i>k</i>a nântardadhate. 21. na <i>k</i>âpo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>bhyupayanti. 22. <i>s</i>akvarî<i>n</i>â<i>m</i> dvâda<i>s</i>a nava sha<i>t</i> traya iti vikalpâ<i>h</i>. 23. k<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>avastra<i>h</i>. 24. k<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>abhaksha. 25. â<i>k</i>âryâdhînas. 26. tish<i>th</i>ed divâ. 27. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>sîta nakta<i>m</i>. 28. sa<i>m</i>vatsaram ekeshâm pûrvai<i>s</i> <i>s</i>rutâ<i>s</i> <i>k</i>ed.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_405"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 405</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. (The teacher) should sing (those verses) to (the student) who has fasted and veiled his eyes (thinking), 'May (the teacher) not burn me (with the <i>S</i>akvarî verses).'</p>  
 <p>30. In the morning they make (the student) look at such things as they expect will not burn him, viz. water, fire, a calf, the sun.</p>  
 <p>31. At water (he should look) with (the words), 'Water have I beheld!' At fire with (the words), 'Light have I beheld!' At the calf with (the words), 'Cattle have I beheld!' At the sun with (the words), 'The sky have I beheld!'thus he should break his silence.</p>  
 <p>32. A cow is the fee (for the teacher),</p>  
 <p>33. A brazen vessel, a garment, and a golden ornament.</p>  
 <p>34. At the Anuprava<i>k</i>anîya ceremonies (see Â<i>s</i>valâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya I, 22, 12) he should sacrifice Â<i>g</i>ya with (the two verses), 'To the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>, to the Sâman we sacrifice' (Sâma-veda I, 369), and, 'The lord of the seat' (Sv. I, 171).</p>  
 <p>35 <a name="fr_1024"></a><a href="#fn_1023"><font size="1">35-37</font></a>. If he has touched a fire-altar or a sacrificial post, if he has humming in his ears, or if his eye</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">29. uposhitâya pari<i>n</i>addhâkshâyânugâpayed yathâ mâ na pradhakshyatîti. 30. ta<i>m</i> prâtar abhivîkshayanti yâny apradhakshyanti manyante<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>po<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gni<i>m</i> vatsam âdityam. 31. apo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>bhivyakhyam ity apo <i>g</i>yotir abhivyakhyam ity agni<i>m</i> pa<i>s</i>ûn abhivyakhyam iti vatsa<i>m</i> sur [sic] abhivyakhyam ity âditya<i>m</i> vis<i>ri</i><i>g</i>ed vâ<i>k</i>a<i>m</i>. 32. gaur dakshi<i>n</i>â. 33. ka<i>m</i>so vâso rukma<i>s</i> <i>k</i>â. 34. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>nuprava<i>k</i>anîyeshv <i>ri</i>ka<i>m</i> sâma Sadasaspatim iti <i>k</i>â<i>g</i>ya<i>m</i> <i>g</i>uhuyâ<i>k</i>. 35. <i>k</i>ityayûpopaspar<i>s</i>anakar<i>n</i>akro<i>s</i>âkshivepaneshu sûryâbhyuditas sûryâbhinimrukta indriyai<i>s</i> <i>k</i>a pâpaspar<i>s</i>ai<i>h</i> punar mâm ity etâbhyâm âhutîr (correct, âhutî?) <i>g</i>uhuyâd.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_406"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 406</font></a></p>  
 <p>palpitates, or if the sun rises or sets while he is sleeping, or if his organs of sense have been defiled by something bad, he should sacrifice two oblations of Â<i>g</i>ya with the two (verses), 'May (my strength) return to me;</p>  
 <p>36. Or two pieces of wood anointed with Â<i>g</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>37. Or he may murmur (those verses) at light (offences). Or he may murmur (those verses) at light (offences).</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Second Pa<i>t</i>ala.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">36. â<i>g</i>yalipte vâ samidhau. 37. <i>g</i>aped vâ laghushu, <i>g</i>aped vâ laghushu. dvitîyapa<i>t</i>ala<i>h</i>.</font></p>
