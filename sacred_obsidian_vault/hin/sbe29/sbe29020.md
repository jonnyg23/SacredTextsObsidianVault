 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 18.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the rites of the fourth day.</p>  
 <p>2. When the three nights have elapsed, he makes offerings of cooked food (with the texts),</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_95"></a><a href="#fn_94"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 'Agni! Thou art expiation; thou art the expiation of the gods. What substance dwells in her that brings death to her husband, that drive away from her.</p>  
 <p>'Vâyu! Thou art expiation; thou art the expiation of the gods. What substance dwells in her that brings sonlessness, that drive away from her.</p>  
 <p>'Sûrya! Thou art expiation; thou art the expiation of the gods. What substance dwells in her that brings destruction to the cattle, that drive away from her.</p>  
 <p>'To god Aryaman the girls have made sacrifice, to Agni; may he, god Aryaman, loosen her from this, and not from that place.</p>  
 <p>'To god Varu<i>n</i>a the girls have made sacrifice, to Agni; may he, god Varu<i>n</i>a, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>'To god Pûshan the girls have made sacrifice, to Agni; may he, god Pûshan, &amp;c.'</p>  
 <p>4. The seventh oblation with the verse, 'Pra<i>g</i>âpati' (Rig-veda X, 121, 10).</p>  
 <p>5. The eighth to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t.</p>
