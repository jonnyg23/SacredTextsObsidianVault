 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 19.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the full moon day of <i>K</i>aitra,</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_296"></a><a href="#fn_295"><font size="1">2-5</font></a>. (Taking) jujube leaves, and making of meal (images) of couples of animals as it happens.</p>  
 <p>3. A figure with prominent navel to Indra and Agni.</p>  
 <p>4. Balls to Rudra.</p>  
 <p>5. According to custom the Nakshatras and (their?) images (?). According to custom the Nakshatras and (their?) images (?).</p>  
 <p align="center">Here ends the Fourth Adhyâya.</p>
