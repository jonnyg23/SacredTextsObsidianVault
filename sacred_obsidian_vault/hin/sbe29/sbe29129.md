 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 6.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_510"></a><a href="#fn_509"><font size="1">1</font></a>. When going to mount a chariot he should touch the wheels with his two hands separately with (the words), 'I touch thy two fore-feet. Thy two wheels are the B<i>ri</i>hat and the Rathantara (Sâmans).'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_511"></a><a href="#fn_510"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 'Thy axle is the Vâmadevya'with (these words he touches) the two (naves) in which the axle rests.</p>  
 <p>3. He should mount (the chariot) with the right foot first, with (the words), 'With Vâyu's strength I mount thee, with Indra's power and sovereignty.'</p>  
 <p>4. He should touch the reins, or if the horses have no reins, (he should touch) the horses with a staff, with (the words), 'With Brahman's splendour I seize you. With truth I seize you.'</p>  
 <p>5. When (the horses) put themselves in motion, he should murmur, 'Go forward to thousandfold successful vigour, divine chariot, carry us forward!'(and the verse), 'Free, strong be thy limbs!' (Rig-veda VI, 47, 26.)</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_512"></a><a href="#fn_511"><font size="1">6</font></a>. With this (verse he should touch also) other articles of wood.</p>  
 <p>7. 'May the two oxen be strong, the axle firm' (Rig-veda III, 53, i7)with (this verse) he should touch (each) part of the chariot (alluded to in that verse).</p>  
 <p>8. With (the verse), 'The earth, the good protectress, the unattained heaven' (Rig-veda X, 63, 10) (he should ascend) a ship.</p>  
 <p>9. With a new chariot he should drive round a widely known tree or round a pool that does not dry up, with his right side turned towards it, and then should fetch branches which bear fruits,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Or something else that belongs to the household.</p>  
 <p>11. (He then) should drive (in that chariot) to an assembly.</p>  
 <p>12. Having murmured, while looking at the sun, (the verse), 'Make our renown highest' (Rig-veda IV, 31, 15), he should descend.</p>  
 <p>13. 'To the bull among my equals' (Rig-veda X, 166, 1)(this verse he should murmur) while approaching (that assembly?).</p>  
 <p>14. 'May we be called to-day Indra's best friends' (Rig-veda I, 167, 10)when the sun is setting.</p>  
 <p>15. 'Thus I address you, O daughters of heaven, while you arise' (Rig-veda IV, 51, 11)when day appears.</p>
