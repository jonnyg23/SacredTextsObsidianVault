 <p><a name="page_326"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 326</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 13.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_835"></a><a href="#fn_834"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On an auspicious day the harnessing to the plough. Or under (the Nakshatra) <i>G</i>yesh<i>th</i>â, (because that rite is) sacred to Indra.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_836"></a><a href="#fn_835"><font size="1">2</font></a>. To Indra, Par<i>g</i>anya, the two A<i>s</i>vins, the Maruts, Udalâkâ<i>s</i>yapa, Svâtikârî, Sîtâ, and Anumati, he offers curds, rice grains, perfumes, and fried grains, and then makes the bullocks eat honey and ghee.</p>  
 <p>3. He should put them to the plough with (the verse), 'They harness to the ploughs' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XII, 67).</p>  
 <p>4. With (the verse), 'For luck may us the ploughshares' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XII, 69) let him plough or touch the plough-share.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_837"></a><a href="#fn_836"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Or (he may) not (do so), because (that verse) has been prescribed for (the erection of) the Agni (-altar), and the act of sowing stands in connection (with it).</p>  
 <p>6. After the front-bullock has been sprinkled (with water), they then should plough unploughed ground.</p>

 <p><a name="page_327"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 327</font></a></p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_838"></a><a href="#fn_837"><font size="1">7</font></a>. He should make oblations of cooked sacrificial food to the same deities as above, when sowing both rice and barley, and at the sacrifice to Sîtâ.</p>  
 <p>8. Then (follows) feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
