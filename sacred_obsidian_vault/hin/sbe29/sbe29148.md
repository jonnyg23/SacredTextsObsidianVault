 <p><a name="page_240"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 240</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 3.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_589"></a><a href="#fn_588"><font size="1">1</font></a>. He should then put the following (sacrificial) implements (on the dead body).</p>  
 <p>2. Into the right hand the (spoon called) <i>G</i>uhû.</p>  
 <p>3. Into the left the (other spoon called) Upabh<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p>4. On his right side the (wooden sacrificial sword called) Sphya, on his left (side) the Agnihotrahavanî (i.e. the ladle with which the Agnihotra oblations are sacrificed).</p>  
 <p>5. On his chest the (big sacrificial ladle called) Dhruvâ. On his head the dishes. On his teeth the pressing-stones.</p>  
 <p>6. On the two sides of his nose the two (smaller sacrificial ladles called) Sruvas.</p>  
 <p>7. Or, if there is only one (Sruva), breaking it (in two pieces).</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_590"></a><a href="#fn_589"><font size="1">8</font></a>. On his two ears the two Prâ<i>s</i>itrahara<i>n</i>as (i.e. the vessels into which the portion of the sacrificial food belonging to the Brahman is put).</p>  
 <p>9. Or, if there is only one (Prâ<i>s</i>itrahara<i>n</i>a), breaking it (in two pieces).</p>  
 <p>10. On his belly the (vessel called) Pâtrî,</p>  
 <p>11. And the cup into which the cut-off portions (of the sacrificial food) are put.</p>  
 <p>12. On his secret parts the (staff called) <i>S</i>amyâ.</p>  
 <p>13. On his thighs the two kindling woods.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_241"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 241</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. On his legs the mortar and the pestle.</p>  
 <p>15. On his feet the two baskets.</p>  
 <p>16. Or, if there is only one (basket), tearing it (in two pieces).</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_591"></a><a href="#fn_590"><font size="1">17</font></a>. Those (of the implements) which have a hollow (into which liquids can be poured), are filled with sprinkled butter.</p>  
 <p>18. The son (of the deceased person) should take the under and the upper mill-stone for himself.</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_592"></a><a href="#fn_591"><font size="1">19</font></a>. And the implements made of copper, iron, and earthenware.</p>  
 <p>20 <a name="fr_593"></a><a href="#fn_592"><font size="1">20</font></a>. Taking out the omentum of the she-animal he should cover therewith the head and the mouth (of the dead person) with the verse, 'Put on the armour (which will protect thee) against Agni, by (that which comes from) the cows' (Rig-veda X, 16, 7).</p>  
 <p>21. Taking out the kidneys (of the animal) he should lay them into the hands (of the dead body) with the verse, 'Escape the two hounds, the sons of Saramâ' (Rig-veda X, 14, 10), the right (kidney) into the right (hand), the left into the left.</p>  
 <p>22. The heart (of the animal he puts) on the heart (of the deceased).</p>  
 <p>23 <a name="fr_594"></a><a href="#fn_593"><font size="1">23</font></a>. And two lumps (of flour or rice), according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_242"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 242</font></a></p>  
 <p>24 <a name="fr_595"></a><a href="#fn_594"><font size="1">24</font></a>. (Only) if there are no kidneys, according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>25 <a name="fr_596"></a><a href="#fn_595"><font size="1">25</font></a>. Having distributed the whole (animal), limb by limb (placing its different limbs on the corresponding limbs of the deceased), and having covered it with its hide, he recites, when the Pra<i>n</i>îta water is carried forward, (the verse), 'Agni, do not overturn this cup' (Rig-veda X, 16, 8).</p>  
 <p>26. Bending his left knee he should sacrifice Â<i>g</i>ya oblations into the Dakshi<i>n</i>a fire with (the formulas), 'To Agni svâhâ! To Kâma svâhâ! To the world svâhâ! To Anumati svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>27 <a name="fr_597"></a><a href="#fn_596"><font size="1">27</font></a>. A fifth (oblation) on the chest of the deceased with (the formula), 'From this one verily thou hast been born. May he now be born out of thee, N.N.! To the heaven-world svâhâ!'</p>
