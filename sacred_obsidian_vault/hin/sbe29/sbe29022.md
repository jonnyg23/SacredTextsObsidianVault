 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 20.</h3>  
 <p>1. In the third month the Pu<i>m</i>savana (i.e. the ceremony to secure the birth of a male child),</p>  
 <p>2. Under (the Nakshatra) Pushya or <i>S</i>rava<i>n</i>a.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_97"></a><a href="#fn_96"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Having pounded a Soma stalk, or a Ku<i>s</i>a needle, or the last shoot of a Nyagrodha trunk, or the part of a sacrificial post which is exposed to the fire,</p>  
 <p>4. Or (having taken) after the completion of a sacrifice the remnants from the <i>G</i>uhû ladle,</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_98"></a><a href="#fn_97"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Let him sprinkle it into her right nostril with the four verses, 'By Agni may good' (Rig-veda I, 1, 3), 'That sperm to us' (III, 4, 9), 'May he succeed who lights fire' (V, 37, 2), 'Of tawny shape' (II, 3, 9), with Svâhâ at the end (of each verse).</p>
