 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the putting on of fuel.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">2</font></a>. He wipes with his hand (the ground) round the fire with (the formula), 'Agni, glorious one, make me glorious. As thou, glorious Agni, art glorious, thus, O glorious one, bring me to glory. As thou, Agni, art the preserver of the treasure of sacrifice for the gods, thus may I become the preserver of the treasure of the Veda for men.'</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Having sprinkled (water) round the fire from left to right, he stands up and puts a piece of wood on (the fire) with (the texts),</p>  
 <p>'To Agni I have brought a piece of wood, to the great <i>G</i>âtavedas. As thou, Agni, art inflamed by wood, thus I am inflamed by life, insight, vigour, offspring, cattle, holy lustre.</p>  
 <p>'May my teacher be the father of living sons; may I be full of insight, not forgetful (of what I have learned); may I become full of glory, of splendour, of holy lustre, an enjoyer of food. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_308"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 308</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. In the same way (he puts on) a second (piece of wood); and thus a third.</p>  
 <p>5. Or (each piece) with (the verse), 'Thine is this' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. II, 14).</p>  
 <p>6. Or (he uses) both (this verse and the formulas given in Sûtra 3).</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">7</font></a>. The wiping and sprinkling (of water) round (the fire are repeated) as above.</p>  
 <p>8. Having warmed his two hands, he wipes his mouth with (the formulas):</p>  
 <p>'Agni, thou art the protector of bodies. Protect my body. Agni, thou art the giver of life. Give me life. Agni, thou art the giver of vigour. Give me vigour.</p>  
 <p>'Agni, what is deficient in my body, that restore to fulness.</p>  
 <p>'May the god Savit<i>ri</i> bestow insight on me, may the goddess Sarasvatî, may the two divine A<i>s</i>vins, wreathed with lotus, (bestow) insight (on me).'</p>
