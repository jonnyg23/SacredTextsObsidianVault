 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 14.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the Pu<i>m</i>savana (i.e. the ceremony to secure the birth of a male child),</p>  
 <p>2. Before (the child in his mother's womb) moves, in the second or third month (of pregnancy).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">3</font></a>. On a day on which the moon stands in conjunction with a Nakshatra (that has a name) of masculine gender, on that day, after having caused (his wife) to fast, to bathe, and to put on two garments which have not yet been washed, and after having in the night-time crushed in water descending roots and shoots of a Nyagrodha tree, he inserts (that into her right nostril) as above, with the two (verses),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_292"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 292</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'The gold-child' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XIII, 4) and 'Formed of water' (ibid. XXXI, 17);</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">14_4</font></a>. A Ku<i>s</i>a needle and a Soma stalk, according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">5</font></a>. And he puts gall of a tortoise on her lap.</p>  
 <p>If he desires; 'May (the son) become valiant,' he recites over him (i.e. over the embryo), modifying the rite (?), 'The Supar<i>n</i>a art thou' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XII, 4), (the Ya<i>g</i>us) before (the formulas called) 'steps of Vish<i>n</i>u.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_734"></a><a href="sbe29168.htm#fr_735">292:5</a> The commentators state that kûrmapitta (gall of tortoise) means 'a dish with water.' I place no confidence in this statement, though I cannot show at present what its origin is. I am not sure about the translation of vik<i>ri</i>tyâ (or vik<i>ri</i>tya?). But it seems impossible to me that it should be the name of the metre Vik<i>ri</i>ti. 'Steps of Vish<i>n</i>u' is a name for the Ya<i>g</i>us following in the Sa<i>m</i>hitâ on the one prescribed in this Sûtra. It begins, 'Vish<i>n</i>us step art thou, &amp;c.' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XII, 5).</p>
