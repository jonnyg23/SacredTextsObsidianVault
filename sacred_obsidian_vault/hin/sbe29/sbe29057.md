 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 9.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_217"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 'May the noisy (goddesses) keep you away from slaughtering hosts. May the entire share, O cows, that belongs to this lord of cows, suffer no harm among you(and)</p>  
 <p>'May Pûshan go after our cows' (Rig-veda VI, 54, 5)this he shall speak over the cows when they go away (to their pasture-grounds).</p>  
 <p>2. 'May Pûshan hold' (Rig-veda VI, 54, 10), when they run about.</p>  
 <p>3. 'May they whose udder with its four holes is full of sweet and ghee, be milk-givers to us; (may they be) many in our stable, rich in ghee'and, 'The cows have come' (Rig-veda VI, 28), when they have come back.</p>  
 <p>4. The last (verse) when he puts them in (into the stable).</p>  
 <p>5. The hymn, 'Refreshing wind' (Rig-veda X, 169), (he recites over the cows), when they are gone into the stable.</p>
