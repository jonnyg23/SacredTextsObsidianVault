 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 17.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_751"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On the tenth day (after the birth of the child) the father, having made (his wife) get up, and having fed the Brâhma<i>n</i>as, gives a name (to the child),</p>  
 <p>2. Of two syllables, or of four syllables beginning with a sonant, with a semivowel in it, with a long vowel (or) the Visarga (at its end), with a K<i>ri</i>t (suffix), not with a Taddhita;</p>  
 <p>3. With an uneven number of syllables, ending in â, with a Taddhita (suffix) to a girl.</p>  
 <p>4. (The name) of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a (should end in)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_298"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 298</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>s</i>arman (for inst. Vish<i>n</i>u<i>s</i>arman), that of a Kshatriya in varman (for inst. Lakshmîvarman), that of a Vai<i>s</i>ya in gupta (for inst. <i>K</i>andragupta).</p>  
 <p>5. In the forth month (follows) the going out.</p>  
 <p>6. He makes (the child) look at the sun, pronouncing (the verse), 'That eye' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ XXXVI, 24).</p>
