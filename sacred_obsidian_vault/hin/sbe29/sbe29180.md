 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 7.</h3>  
 <p>1. We shall state the rules of conduct for a Snâtaka.</p>  
 <p>2. Another (may observe those rules) optionally.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_317"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 317</font></a></p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_802"></a><a href="#fn_801"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Dancing, singing, and playing musical instruments, let him neither perform himself nor go (to see or hear it).</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_803"></a><a href="#fn_802"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Sing, however, he may at his pleasure, for there is another saying, 'He sings either or he rejoices in (other people's) singing.'</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_804"></a><a href="#fn_803"><font size="1">5</font></a>. If everything goes well, he shall not go by night to another village, and shall not run.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_805"></a><a href="#fn_804"><font size="1">6</font></a>. He shall avoid looking into a well, climbing up a tree, gathering fruits, crawling through narrow openings, bathing naked, jumping over uneven ground, using harsh language, looking at the sun while it is rising or setting, and begging. For there is a <i>S</i>ruti: 'After he has bathed, he should not beg. For he who bathes, drives away from himself begging.'</p>  
 <p>7. If it rains, he shall go without an upper garment, and shall say, 'May this, my thunderbolt, drive away evil.'</p>  
 <p>8. He shall not look at himself in water.</p>  
 <p>9. A<i>g</i>âtalomnî<i>m</i> vipu<i>m</i>sî<i>m</i> sha<i>n</i><i>dh</i>a<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a nopahaset.</p>  
 <p>10. Let him call a pregnant woman 'vi<i>g</i>anyâ' (one who will give birth to a child);</p>  
 <p>11. An ichneumon (nakula), sakula;</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_806"></a><a href="#fn_805"><font size="1">12</font></a>. A skull (kapâla), bhagâla;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_318"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 318</font></a></p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_807"></a><a href="#fn_806"><font size="1">13</font></a>. A rainbow (Indra-dhanu, Indra's bow), ma<i>n</i>idhanu (the jewelled bow).</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_808"></a><a href="#fn_807"><font size="1">14</font></a>. A cow that suckles (her calf) he should not point out to another (person).</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_809"></a><a href="#fn_808"><font size="1">15</font></a>. Let him not void urine or excrements on a ploughed field, on uncovered ground, or while rising up or standing.</p>  
 <p>16. He shall wipe himself with wood that has fallen off by itself.</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_810"></a><a href="#fn_809"><font size="1">17</font></a>. He should not wear a dyed garment.</p>  
 <p>18. He should be fixed in his intentions, protect everybody's life, and be everybody's friend, as it were.</p>
