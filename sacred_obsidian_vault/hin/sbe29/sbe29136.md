 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 3.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_529"></a><a href="#fn_528"><font size="1">1</font></a>. He then should recite for himself (the following texts, viz.) the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as, the Ya<i>g</i>us, the Sâmans, the Atharvan and Aṅgiras hymns, the Brâhma<i>n</i>as, the Kalpa (Sûtras), the Gâthâs, the (texts in honour of kings and heroes, called) Nârâ<i>s</i>a<i>m</i>sîs, the Itihâsas and Purâ<i>n</i>as.</p>  
 <p>2. In that he recites the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as, he thereby satiates the gods with oblations of milkin that (he recites) the Ya<i>g</i>us, with oblations of gheethe Sâmans, with oblations of honeythe Atharvan and Aṅgiras hymns, with oblations of Somathe Brâhma<i>n</i>as, Kalpas, Gâthâs, Nârâ<i>s</i>a<i>m</i>sîs, Itihâsas and Purâ<i>n</i>as, with oblations of ambrosia.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. In that he recites the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as, rivers of milk flow, as a funeral oblation, to his Fathers. In that (he recites) the Ya<i>g</i>us, rivers of gheethe Sâmans, rivers of honeythe Atharvan and Aṅgiras hymns, rivers of Somathe Brâhma<i>n</i>as, Kalpas, Gâthâs, Nârâ<i>s</i>a<i>m</i>sîs, Itihâsas and Purâ<i>n</i>as, rivers of ambrosia.</p>  
 <p>4. After he has recited (those texts) as far as he thinks fit, he should finish with the following (verse),</p>  
 <p>'Adoration to Brahman! Adoration be to Agni! Adoration to the Earth! Adoration to the Herbs! Adoration to the Voice! Adoration to the Lord of the Voice! Adoration I bring to great Vish<i>n</i>u!'</p>
