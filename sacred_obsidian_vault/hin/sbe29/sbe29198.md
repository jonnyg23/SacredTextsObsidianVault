 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_897"></a><a href="#fn_896"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The spit-ox (sacrificed to Rudra).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_898"></a><a href="#fn_897"><font size="1">2</font></a>. It procures (to the sacrificer) heavenly rewards, cattle, sons, wealth, renown, long life.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_899"></a><a href="#fn_898"><font size="1">8_3</font></a>. Having taken the sacred domestic fire to the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_352"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 352</font></a></p>  
 <p>forest, and having performed the 'outspreading,' he should sacrifice the animal to Rudra.</p>  
 <p>4. One that is not gelded.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_900"></a><a href="#fn_899"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Or (it may be) a cow, on account of the designation.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_901"></a><a href="#fn_900"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Having cooked the omentum, a mess of sacrificial food, and the portions cut off (of the victim), he sacrifices the omentum to Rudra, the fat to the Air, and the cut-off portions together with the mess of cooked food to Agni, Rudra, <i>S</i>arva, Pa<i>s</i>upati, Ugra, A<i>s</i>ani, Bhava, Mahâdeva, <a href="errata.htm#2">Î<i>s</i>âna</a>.</p>  
 <p>7. (Then follows a sacrifice to) Vanaspati.</p>  
 <p>8. (To Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t at the end.</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_902"></a><a href="#fn_901"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Then (follows) the sprinkling round to the different quarters (of the horizon).</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_903"></a><a href="#fn_902"><font size="1">10</font></a>. After the sprinkling has been performed, they sacrifice the Patnî-sa<i>m</i>yâ<i>g</i>a offerings to Indrâ<i>n</i>î, Rudrâ<i>n</i>î, <i>S</i>arvâ<i>n</i>i, Bhavânî, and Agni G<i>ri</i>hapati.</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_904"></a><a href="#fn_903"><font size="1">11</font></a>. The blood he offers in leaves, on (grass-) bunches, as a Bali to Rudra and to his hosts, with (the Mantras),</p>  
 <p>'The hosts, Rudra, which thou hast to the east, to them this Bali (is given). To them and to thee be adoration!</p>  
 <p>'The hosts, Rudra, which thou hast to the south . . . to the west . . . to the north . . . upwards . . .</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_353"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 353</font></a></p>  
 <p>downwards, to them this Bali (is given). To them and to thee be adoration!'</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_905"></a><a href="#fn_904"><font size="1">12</font></a>. The contents of the stomach and of the entrails, besmeared with blood, he throws into the fire or buries them in the earth.</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_906"></a><a href="#fn_905"><font size="1">13</font></a>. Having placed the animal so that the wind blows from himself to it, he approaches it with the Rudra hymns, or with the first and last Anuvâka.</p>  
 <p>14. They do not take anything of that animal to the village.</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_907"></a><a href="#fn_906"><font size="1">15</font></a>. Thereby (also) the cow-sacrifice has been declared.</p>  
 <p>16. (It is combined) with (the offering of) milk-rice; (the rites) not corresponding (to that special occasion) are omitted.</p>  
 <p>17. The sacrificial fee at that (sacrifice) is a cow of the same age (as the victim).</p>
