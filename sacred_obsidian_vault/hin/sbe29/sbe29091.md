 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 10.</h3>  
 <p>1. If the bees make honey in his house,</p>  
 <p>2. Let him fast and sacrifice a hundred and eight pieces of Udumbara wood, which are besmeared with curds, honey, and ghee, with the two (verses), 'No (harm) to us in our offspring' (Rig-veda I, 114, 8. 9).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">3</font></a>. And let him murmur the hymn, 'For welfare may Indra and Agni' (Rig-veda VII, 35); and (the same hymn should be used) at all (ceremonies), such</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 140</font></a></p>  
 <p>as that of the sacrifice after assent has been declared (see above, I, 7, 1).</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">4</font></a>. After he has sacrificed seventeen one span long pieces of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood, he then seizes the Sruva.</p>  
 <p>5. Fifteen at the full and new moon sacrifices.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">6</font></a>. At the Ash<i>t</i>akâ ceremony in the middle of the rainy season there may optionally be three (pieces of wood); the sacrifice as at the Pit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.</p>
