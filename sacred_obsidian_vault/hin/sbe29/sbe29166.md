 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 12.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_729"></a><a href="#fn_728"><font size="1">1</font></a>. At the beginning of each half-month he cooks a mess of sacrificial food, sacrifices to the deities of the festivals of the new and full moon (as stated in the <i>S</i>rauta ritual), and then sacrifices to the following deities: to Brahman, to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, to the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, and to Heaven and Earth.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_730"></a><a href="#fn_729"><font size="1">2</font></a>. To the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs a Bali is offered, to the domestic deities, and to Âkâ<i>s</i>a (i.e. the Ether).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_730"><font size="1">3</font></a>. From the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva food he makes oblations in the fire with (the formulas), 'To Agni svâhâ! To Pra<i>g</i>âpati svâhâ! To the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs svâhâ! To Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>4. Outside (the house) the wife offers the Bali with (the formulas), 'Adoration to the wife! Adoration to the man! To every time of life, adoration! To the white one with the black teeth, the lord of the bad women, adoration!</p>  
 <p>'They who allure my offspring, dwelling in the village or in the forest, to them be adoration; I offer</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_291"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 291</font></a></p>  
 <p>a Bali to them. Be welfare to me! May they give me offspring.'</p>  
 <p>5. The remainder he washes out with water. Then (follows) feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
