 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 15.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now how he should mount an elephant.</p>  
 <p>2. He goes to the elephant and touches it (saying), 'The elephants glory art thou. The elephants honour art thou.'</p>  
 <p>3. He then mounts it with (the words), 'With Indra's thunder-bolt I bestride thee. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>4. Thereby it has also been declared how he should mount a horse.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_366"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 366</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. When he is going to mount a camel, he addresses it: 'Thou art the son of Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i>; Tvash<i>t</i><i>ri</i> is thy deity. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">6</font></a>. When he is going to mount a he-ass, he addresses it: 'A <i>S</i>ûdra art thou, a <i>S</i>ûdra by birth. To Agni thou belongest, with twofold sperm. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>7. A path he addresses: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells on the paths. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>8. A cross-road he addresses: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells at the cross-roads. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>9. When he intends to swim across a river, he addresses it: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells in the waters. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>10. When going on board a ship, he addresses her: 'The good ship' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XXI, 7).</p>  
 <p>11. When going to cross (the river), he addresses (the ship): 'The well-protecting' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XXI, 6).</p>  
 <p>12. A forest (through which he is wandering) he addresses: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells in the forests. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>13. A mountain (which he is going to cross) he addresses: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells on the mountains. Make me arrive <i>s</i>afely.'</p>  
 <p>14. A burial-ground he addresses: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells among the Fathers. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>15. A cow-stable he addresses: 'Adoration to Rudra who dwells among the dung-heaps. Make me arrive safely.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 367</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. And wheresoever else it be, let him always say, 'Adoration to Rudra.' For the <i>S</i>ruti says, 'Rudra is this universe.'</p>  
 <p>17. If the skirt (of his garment) is blown upon him (by the wind), he addresses (that skirt): 'A skirt art thou. Thou art not a thunder-bolt. Adoration be to thee. Do no harm to me!'</p>  
 <p>18. The thunder he addresses: 'May the rains be friendly to us; may (Indra's) darts be friendly to usmay they be friendly to us which thou throwest, O killer of V<i>ri</i>tra.'</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">19</font></a>. A howling jackal he addresses: 'Friendly by name' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. III, 63).</p>  
 <p>20 <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">20</font></a>. A shrieking bird he addresses: 'Golden-winged bird who goest where the gods send thee! Messenger of Yama, adoration be to thee! What has the Kârkâri<i>n</i>a told thee?'</p>  
 <p>21. A tree that serves as a mark (of a boundary, &amp;c.), he addresses: 'May neither the flash of lightning (destroy thee), nor axe nor wind nor punishment which the king sends. May thy shoots grow up; may rain fall on thee, in safety from the wind. May fire not destroy thy root. Blessing on thee, O lord of the forest! Blessing on me, O lord of the forest!'</p>  
 <p>22. If he receives something (given to him), he accepts it with (the formula), 'May Heaven give thee; may the Earth accept thee.' Thus (the thing given) does not decrease to him who gives it, and what he receives increases.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_368"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 368</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. If boiled rice is given to him, he accepts it with (the formula), 'May Heaven, &amp;c.,' and he partakes thereof twice. with (the formulas), 'May Brahman eat thee!''May Brahman partake of thee!'</p>  
 <p>24. If gruel is given to him, (as above) . . . . three times with (the formulas), 'May Brahman eat thee! ''May Brahman partake of thee!''May Brahman drink thee!</p>
