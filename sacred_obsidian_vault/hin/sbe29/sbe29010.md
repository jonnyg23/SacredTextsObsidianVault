 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 8.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_43"></a><a href="#fn_42"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now (follows) the strewing (of grass) around (the fire).</p>  
 <p>2. He strews eastward-pointed Ku<i>s</i>a grass around it, in three layers or in five layers,</p>  
 <p>3. Beginning on the east side, then to the west, then to the west.</p>  
 <p>4. He covers the roots (of the grass-blades) with the points.</p>  
 <p>5. And all kinds of rites are to be performed beginning south, ending north.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_44"></a><a href="#fn_43"><font size="1">6</font></a>. He places the Brahman south with the words, BHÛR BHUVA<i>H</i> SVA<i>H</i>,</p>  
 <p>7. Adorns him with flowers,</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_45"></a><a href="#fn_44"><font size="1">8</font></a>. Carries forward on the north side the Pra<i>n</i>îtâ waters with the words, 'Who carries ye forward?'</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_46"></a><a href="#fn_45"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Takes up with the left hand the Ku<i>s</i>a blades, and arranges them (on the ground) with the right hand,</p>

 <p>

 <p>10. Bending his right knee,</p>  
 <p>11. The left when worshipping the Manes.</p>  
 <p>12. The strewing around (of the grass) is not necessary in the Â<i>g</i>ya offerings,</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_47"></a><a href="#fn_46"><font size="1">13</font></a>. Nor in the standing offerings, according to Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ûkeya.</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_48"></a><a href="#fn_47"><font size="1">14-16</font></a>. He now measures off with the span (of his hand) two Ku<i>s</i>a blades, which are not unequal, with unbroken points, bearing no young shoots in them, and severs them (from their roots) with a Ku<i>s</i>a blade, saying, 'Purifiers are ye.'</p>  
 <p>15. There are two or three (of these Ku<i>s</i>a strainers).</p>  
 <p>16. He holds them with their points to the east and sprinkles them (with water, saying), 'Belonging to Vish<i>n</i>u.'</p>  
 <p>17. With the two Ku<i>s</i>a blades he sprinkles (water) around the fire three times, keeping his right side turned towards it,</p>  
 <p>18 <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_48"><font size="1">18</font></a>. Takes up the Â<i>g</i>ya pot with the words, 'Milk of the cows art thou;'</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_50"></a><a href="#fn_49"><font size="1">19</font></a>. Puts it on the fire with the words, 'For sap thee;'</p>  
 <p>20 <a name="fr_51"></a><a href="#fn_50"><font size="1">20</font></a>. Takes it from the fire towards the north with the words, 'For juice thee;'</p>  
 <p>21 <a name="fr_52"></a><a href="#fn_51"><font size="1">21</font></a>. And holding the two (Ku<i>s</i>a) strainers with their points to the north, seizing them on both sides</p>

 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 26</font></a></p>  
 <p>with his two thumbs and fourth fingers, he bends them down, the points upwards, and dips them into the Â<i>g</i>ya with the words,</p>  
 <p>'By the impulse of Savitar I purify thee with this uninjured purifier, with the rays of the good sun.'</p>  
 <p>22. (This) preparation of the Â<i>g</i>ya (takes place) each time.</p>  
 <p>23. Let him not offer (Â<i>g</i>ya) which has not been (thus) prepared.</p>  
 <p>24 <a name="fr_53"></a><a href="#fn_52"><font size="1">24-25</font></a>. Also the waters in the Sruva spoon (he purifies) with the words, '(By the impulse) of Savitar (I purify) you.'</p>  
 <p>25. This (is called) the PRA<i>N</i>ÎTÂ and the PROKSHA<i>N</i>Î water.</p>
