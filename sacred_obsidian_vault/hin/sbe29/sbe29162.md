 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_710"></a><a href="#fn_709"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Then he makes her step forward in a northern direction seven steps (with the words),</p>  
 <p>'One for sap, two for juice, three for the prospering of wealth, four for comfort, five for cattle, six for the seasons. Friend! be with seven steps (united to me). So be thou devoted to me.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_284"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 284</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (The words), 'May Vish<i>n</i>u lead thee' are added to every part (of the formula).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">3</font></a>. From the moment of their going away a man who holds a water-pot on his shoulder, stands silent to the south of the fire;</p>  
 <p>4. To the north, (according to the opinion) of some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>5. From that (pot) he sprinkles her (with water) on her head (with the formula),</p>  
 <p>'The blessed, the most blessed waters, the peaceful ones, the most peaceful ones, may they give medicine to thee'</p>  
 <p>6. And with the three (verses), Ye waters are' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XI, 50-52).</p>  
 <p>7. He then makes her look at the sun with (the verse), 'That eye' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XXXVI, 24.).</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">8</font></a>. He then touches her heart, (reaching) over her right shoulder, with (the words), 'Into my will I take thy heart; thy mind shall follow my mind; in my word thou shalt rejoice with all thy heart; may Pra<i>g</i>âpati join thee to me.'</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">9</font></a>. He then recites over her (the verse), 'Auspicious ornaments does this woman wear. Come up to her and behold her. Having brought luck to her, go away back to your houses.'</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">10</font></a>. A strong man snatches her up from the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 285</font></a></p>  
 <p>ground, and sets her down in an eastern or northern direction in an out-of-the-way house, on a red bull's hide, with (the words),</p>  
 <p>'Here may the cows sit down, here the horses, here the men. Here may sacrifice with a thousand gifts, here may Pûshan sit down.'</p>  
 <p>11. And what (the people in) the village tell them, that they should do.</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">12</font></a>. For it is said, 'At weddings and funerals he shall enter the village;'</p>  
 <p>13. (And) because the <i>S</i>ruti says, 'Therefore on these two occasions authority rests with the village.'</p>  
 <p>14. To the teacher (who helps at the wedding ceremonies) he gives an optional gift.</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">15-17</font></a>. A cow is the optional gift to be given by a Brâhma<i>n</i>a,</p>  
 <p>16. A village by a Râ<i>g</i>anya,</p>  
 <p>17. A horse by a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>18 <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">18</font></a>. A hundred (cows) with a chariot (he gives to a father) who has only daughters.</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">19</font></a>. After sunset he shows her the firm star (i.e. the polar-star) with (the words),</p>  
 <p>'Firm art thou; I see thee, the firm one. Firm be thou with me, O thriving one!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_286"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 286</font></a></p>  
 <p>'To me B<i>ri</i>haspati has given thee; obtaining offspring through me, thy husband, live with me a hundred autumns.'</p>  
 <p>20. If she does not see (the polar-star), let her say notwithstanding, 'I see,' &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>21 <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">21</font></a>. Through a period of three nights they shall eat no saline food; they shall sleep on the ground; through one year they shall refrain from conjugal intercourse, or through a period of twelve nights, or of six nights, or at least of three nights.</p>
