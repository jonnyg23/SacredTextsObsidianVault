 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 3.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now wherever he intends to perform a sacrifice, let him besmear (with cowdung) a surface of the dimension at least of an arrow on each side; let him draw six lines thereon, one turned to the north, to the west (of the spot on which the fire is to be placed); two (lines) turned to the east, at the two different ends (of the line mentioned first); three (lines) in the middle (of those two); let him sprinkle that (place with water), establish the (sacred) fire (thereon), put (two or three pieces of fuel) on it, wipe (the ground) round (the fire), strew (grass) round (it), to the east, to the south, to the west, to the north, ending (each time) in the north. Then (follows) silently the sprinkling (of water) round (the fire).</p>  
 <p>2. With two (Ku<i>s</i>a blades used as) strainers the purifying of the Â<i>g</i>ya (is done).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Having taken two Ku<i>s</i>a blades with unbroken tops, which do not bear a young shoot in them, of the measure of a span, at their two ends with his</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 163</font></a></p>  
 <p>thumbs and fourth fingers, with his hands turned with the inside upwards, he purifies (the Â<i>g</i>ya, from the west) to the east, with (the words), 'By the impulse of Savit<i>ri</i> I purify thee with this uninjured purifier, with the rays of the good sun'once with this formula, twice silently.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">4</font></a>. The strewing (of grass) round (the fire) may be done or not done in the Â<i>g</i>ya offerings.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">5</font></a>. So also the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions (may optionally be sacrificed) in the Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>as.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And the (assistance of a) Brahman (is optional), except at the sacrifice to Dhanvantari and at the sacrifice of the spit-ox (offered to Rudra).</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_372"></a><a href="#fn_371"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Let him sacrifice with (the words), 'To such and such a deity svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>8. If there is no rule (as to the deities to whom the sacrifice belongs, they are) Agni, Indra, Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, Brahman.</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_373"></a><a href="#fn_372"><font size="1">9</font></a>. (Different Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>as, when) offered at the same time, should have the same Barhis (sacrificial grass), the same fuel, the same Â<i>g</i>ya, and the same (oblation to Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p>10. With reference thereto the following sacrificial stanza is sung:</p>  
 <p>'He who has to perform (different) Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>as, should offer them with the same Â<i>g</i>ya, the same</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_164"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 164</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Barhis, and the same Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t, even if the deity (of those sacrifices) is not the same.'</p>
