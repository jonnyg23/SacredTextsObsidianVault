 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 7.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the examination of the ground (where he intends to build a house).</p>  
 <p>2. (It must be) non-salinous soil of undisputed property,</p>  
 <p>3. With herbs and trees,</p>  
 <p>4. On which much Ku<i>s</i>a and Vîra<i>n</i>a grass grows.</p>  
 <p>5. Plants with thorns and with milky juice he should dig out with their roots and remove them</p>  
 <p>6. And in the same way the following (sorts of plants), viz. Apâmârga, potherbs, Tilvaka, Parivyâdha.</p>  
 <p>7. A spot where the waters, flowing together from all sides to the centre of it, flow round the resting-place, having it on their right side, and then flow off to the east without noisethat possesses all auspicious qualities.</p>  
 <p>8. Where the waters flow off, he should have the provision-room built.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_212"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 212</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Thus it becomes rich in food.</p>  
 <p>10. On a spot which is inclined towards the south, he should have the assembly-room constructed; thus there will be no gambling in it.</p>  
 <p>11. (But others say that) in such (an assembly-room) the young people become gamblers, quarrelsome, and die early.</p>  
 <p>12. Where the waters flow together from all directions, that assembly-room (situated on such a spot) brings luck and is free from gambling.</p>
