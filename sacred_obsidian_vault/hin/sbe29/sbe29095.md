 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 3.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now after they have entered the circle</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_329"><font size="1">2-3</font></a>. The teacher sits down with his face to the east, the others, according to their rank, (sit down) towards the south, with their faces to the north.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 144</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. If that is impossible, with their faces to all directions.</p>  
 <p>4. Let them expect the rising of the sun,</p>  
 <p>5. And when they behold it in its splendour,</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Let them with (the words), 'Recite, sir!' seize with their hands, holding the right hand uppermost, the feet of the teacher, which have been washed, with the right (hand) the right (foot), with the left the left,</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">7</font></a>. And having then put (the hands) into the vessel used for the expiation, into water in which pieces of Dûrvâ stalks are, let them begin their study, when their hands have ceased to drip.</p>  
 <p>8. This is the rite. But when they are tired, let one of them bring it about that the vessel used for the expiation be not empty.</p>  
 <p>9. And all (should do so) at the beginning and the end of (each) Adhyâya.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">10</font></a>. (All) that is done continuously, without interruption.</p>  
 <p>11. Now the expiation.</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">12</font></a>. The syllable OM, the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis, the Sâvitrî, the Rathantara, the B<i>ri</i>hat, the Vâmadevya; B<i>ri</i>hat and Rathantara with repetition and Kakubh-forming.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 145</font></a></p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">13</font></a>. These (holy words and verses) are (thus) made to attain (the number of) ten.</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">14</font></a>. 'Of decades consists the Virâ<i>g</i>'thus says the Brâhma<i>n</i>a.</p>
