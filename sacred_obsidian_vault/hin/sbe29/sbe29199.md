 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 9.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_908"></a><a href="#fn_907"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now the letting loose of the bull.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_909"></a><a href="#fn_908"><font size="1">2</font></a>. (The ceremony) has been declared in the cow-sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_910"></a><a href="#fn_909"><font size="1">3</font></a>. (It is performed) on the full-moon day of Kârttika, or on the (day on which the moon stands in conjunction with) Revatî in the Â<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i>a month.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_911"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Having set a fire in a blaze in the midst of the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_354"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 354</font></a></p>  
 <p>cows, and having prepared Â<i>g</i>ya, he sacrifices six (oblations) with (the Mantras), 'Here is delight' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. VIII, 51).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_911"><font size="1">5</font></a>. With (the verses), 'May Pûshan go after our cows; may Pûshan watch over our horses; may Pûshan give us strength'he sacrifices of (the sacrificial food) destined for Pûshan.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_912"><font size="1">6</font></a>. After murmuring the Rudra hymns they adorn a one-coloured or a two-coloured (bull) who protects the herd or whom the herd protects. Or it should be red, deficient in no limb, the calf of a cow that has living calves and is a milk-giver; and it should be the finest (bull) in the herd. And besides they should adorn the best four young cows of the herd and let them loose with this (verse), 'This young (bull) I give you as your husband; run about sporting with him, your lover. Do not bring down a curse upon us, by nature blessed ones. May we rejoice in increase of wealth and in comfort.'</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_914"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">7</font></a>. When (the bull) stands in the midst of the cows, he recites over it (the texts beginning with) 'Bringing refreshment,' down to the end of the Anuvâka (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XVIII, 45-50).</p>  
 <p>8. With the milk of all (the cows) he should cook milk-rice and give it to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as to eat.</p>

 <p>9 <a name="fr_915"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">9_9</font></a>. Some also sacrifice an animal.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_916"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">9_10</font></a>. The ritual thereof has been declared by the (ritual for the) spit-ox.</p>
