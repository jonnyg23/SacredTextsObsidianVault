 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 337</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>A III, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 1.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_866"></a><a href="#fn_865"><font size="1">1</font></a>. (Now shall be explained) the partaking of the first-fruits (of the harvest), of a person who has not set up the (sacred <i>S</i>rauta) fires.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_867"></a><a href="#fn_866"><font size="1">2</font></a>. He cooks a mess of fresh sacrificial food, sacrifices the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions, and two Â<i>g</i>ya oblations, (with the formulas),</p>  
 <p>'To the hundredfold armed, hundredfold valiant, hundredfold blissful one, the vanquisher of enemieshe who may create a hundred autumns for us, Indra,may he lead us across (the gulf of) misfortune. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'The four paths that go between heaven and earth, trodden by the godsof these (paths) lead us to that which may bring us freedom from decay and decline, O all ye gods. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_868"></a><a href="#fn_867"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Having made oblations of the mess of cooked food to the Âgraya<i>n</i>a deities, he makes another oblation to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t with (the verse), 'Agni, make this (sacrifice) full, that it may be well offered. And may the god destroy all hostile powers. Come hither, showing us a good path. Bestow on us long life, full of splendour and free from decay. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>4. He then eats (of the fresh fruits with the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 338</font></a></p>  
 <p>verses), 'May Agni eat first, for he knows how the Havis (is fit for sacrifice); may he, the friend of all human tribes, make the herbs blessed to us.</p>  
 <p>From the good you have led us to the better, ye gods! Through thee, the nourishment, may we obtain thee. Thus enter into us, O potion, bringing refreshment, for the good of our children and of ourselves, and pleasant.'</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_869"></a><a href="#fn_868"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Or with the (verse) sacred to Annapati (the Lord of food).</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_870"></a><a href="#fn_869"><font size="1">6</font></a>. For barley, however, (he uses the Mantra), 'This barley, mixed with honey, they have ploughed through Sarasvatî under Manu. Indra was lord of the plough, the hundredfold wise one; ploughers were the Maruts, the exuberant givers.'</p>  
 <p>7. Then (follows) the feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
