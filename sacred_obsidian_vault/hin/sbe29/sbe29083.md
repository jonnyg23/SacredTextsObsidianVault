 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_302"></a><a href="#fn_301"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now about (the consecration of) ponds, wells, and tanks.</p>  
 <p>2. In the bright fortnight, or on an auspicious Tithi,</p>  
 <p>3. Having cooked barley-grains with milk,</p>  
 <p>4. He shall sacrifice with the two (verses), 'Thou</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a></p>  
 <p>hast us, Agni' (Rig-veda IV, 1, 4. 5), (and with the verses), 'We propitiate thy wrath' (I, 24, 14), 'This my prayer, Varu<i>n</i>a' (I, 25, 19), 'Loosen the highest, Varu<i>n</i>a' (I, 24, 15), 'This prayer of the man who exercises himself' (VIII, 42, 3),</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_303"></a><a href="#fn_302"><font size="1">5</font></a>. (And with the words), 'The domestic one, he who goes away from the house, the refreshing one, he who goes into the kennel, he who dwells in the kennel, he who comes out of it, the greedy one, the destroyer of enemies'to the different directions (of the horizon), beginning with that belonging to Varu<i>n</i>a (i.e. the west), from left to right.</p>  
 <p>6. in the centre he makes oblations with milk with (the verses), 'Having eyes all around' (Rig-veda X, 81, 3), 'This has Vish<i>n</i>u' (Rig-veda I, 22, 17),</p>  
 <p>7. Plunging (into the water) with (the verse), 'Whatever here' (Rig-veda VII, 89, 5).</p>  
 <p>8. A cow and a pair of clothes is the fee for the sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>9. Then feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
