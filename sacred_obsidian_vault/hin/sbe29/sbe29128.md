 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 5.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the following day the Anvash<i>t</i>akya (i.e. the ceremony following the Ash<i>t</i>akâ, is performed).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_503"></a><a href="#fn_502"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Having prepared (a portion) of that same meat, having established the fire on a surface inclined towards the south, having fenced it in, and made a door on the north side of the enclosure, having strewn round (the fire) three times sacrificial grass with its roots, without tossing it, turning the left side towards the fire, he should put down the things to be offered, boiled rice, boiled rice with sesamum seeds, rice-milk, meal-pap with curds, and meal-pap with honey.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_504"></a><a href="#fn_503"><font size="1">3</font></a>. (The ceremony should be performed) according to the ritual of the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>apit<i>ri</i>ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_505"></a><a href="#fn_504"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Having sacrificed (of those sorts of food) with the exception of the meal-pap with honey, let him give (lumps of those substances) to the Fathers.</p>  
 <p>5. And to (their) wives, with the addition of rum and the scum of boiled rice.</p>  
 <p>6. Some (place the lumps to be offered) into pits, into two or into six:</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. In those situated to the east he should give (the offerings) to the Fathers.</p>  
 <p>8. In those to the west, to the wives.</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_506"></a><a href="#fn_505"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Thereby the ceremony celebrated in the rainy season on the Mâgha day, in the dark fortnight after the full moon of Praush<i>th</i>apada (has been declared).</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_507"></a><a href="#fn_506"><font size="1">10</font></a>. And thus he should offer (a celebration like the Anvash<i>t</i>akya) to the Fathers every month, observing uneven numbers (i.e. selecting a day with an uneven number, inviting an uneven number of Brâhma<i>n</i>as, &amp;c.).</p>  
 <p>11. He should give food at least to nine (Brâhma<i>n</i>as),</p>  
 <p>12. Or to an uneven number;</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_508"></a><a href="#fn_507"><font size="1">13</font></a>. To an even number on auspicious occasions or on the performance of meritorious deeds (such as the consecration of ponds, &amp;c.);</p>  
 <p>14. To an uneven number on other (occasions).</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_509"></a><a href="#fn_508"><font size="1">15</font></a>. The rite is performed from left to right. Barley is to be used instead of sesamum.</p>
