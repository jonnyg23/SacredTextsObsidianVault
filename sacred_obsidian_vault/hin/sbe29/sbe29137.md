 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_530"></a><a href="#fn_529"><font size="1">1</font></a>. He satiates the deities: 'Pra<i>g</i>âpati, Brahman, the Vedas, the gods, the <i>Ri</i>shis, all metres, the word Om, the word Vasha<i>t</i>, the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis, the Sâvitrî, the sacrifices, Heaven and Earth, the air, days and nights, the numbers, the Siddhas, the oceans, the rivers, the mountains, the fields, herbs, trees, Gandharvas and Apsaras, the snakes, the birds, the cows, the Sâdhyas, the Vipras, the Yakshas, the Rakshas, the beings that have these (Rakshas, &amp;c.) at their end.'</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_531"></a><a href="#fn_530"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Then the <i>Ri</i>shis: 'The (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the hundred (<i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as), the (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the middle (Ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>alas), G<i>ri</i>tsamada,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 220</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra, Vâmadeva, Atri, Bharadvâ<i>g</i>a, Vasish<i>th</i>a, the Pragâthas, the Pavamâna hymns, the (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the short hymns, and of the long hymns.'</p>  
 <p>3. (Then) with the sacrificial cord suspended over the right shoulder:</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_532"></a><a href="#fn_531"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 'Sumantu, <i>G</i>aimini, Vai<i>s</i>ampâyana, Paila, the Sûtras, the Bhâshyas, the Bhârata, the Mahâbhârata, the teachers of law, <i>G</i>ânanti, Bâhavi, Gârgya, Gautama, <i>S</i>âkalya, Bâbhravya, Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>avya, Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ûkeya, Gârgî Vâ<i>k</i>aknavî, Va<i>d</i>avâ Prâtîtheyî, Sulabhâ Maitreyî, Kahola Kaushîtaka, Mahâkaushîtaka, Paiṅgya, Mahâpaiṅgya, Suya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana, Aitareya, Mahaitareya, the <i>S</i>âkala (text), the Bâshkala (text), Su<i>g</i>âtavaktra, Audavâhi, Mahaudavâhi, Sau<i>g</i>âmi, <i>S</i>aunaka, Â<i>s</i>valâyanaand whatsoever other teachers there are, may they all satiate themselves.'</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_533"></a><a href="#fn_532"><font size="1">5</font></a>. After he has satiated the Fathers man by man, and has returned to his house, what he gives (then), that is the sacrificial fee.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_534"></a><a href="#fn_533"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And it is also understood (in the <i>S</i>ruti), 'May he be standing, walking, sitting, or lying, (the texts belonging to) whatsoever sacrifice he repeats, that sacrifice indeed he has offered.'</p>  
 <p>7. It is understood (in the <i>S</i>ruti), 'Regarding this (Svâdhyâya) there are two cases in which the study (of the sacred texts) is forbidden: when he is impure himself, and when the place is.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_529"></a><a href="sbe29137.htm#fr_530">219:1</a> 4, 1. Comp. <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya IV, 9. Nârâya<i>n</i>a: 'Having finished (the Svâdhyâya) he satiates with water oblations these deities.'</p>  
 <p>Pra<i>g</i>âpati and the following words stand in the nominative; the verb to be supplied is t<i>ri</i>pyatu (t<i>ri</i>pyantu), 'may he (they) satiate himself (themselves).'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_530"></a><a href="sbe29137.htm#fr_531">219:2</a> <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya IV, 10. <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana has pâvamânâ<i>h</i>, the (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the Pavamâna hymns,' but pragâthâ<i>h</i> as Â<i>s</i>valâyana has, and not as we should expect, prâgâthâ<i>h</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_531"></a><a href="sbe29137.htm#fr_532">220:4</a> The names from Kahola Kaushîtaki down to Â<i>s</i>valâyana stand in the accusative; tarpayâmi, 'I satiate N.N.' is to be supplied.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_532"></a><a href="sbe29137.htm#fr_533">220:5</a> Nârâya<i>n</i>a: 'He satiates his father, grandfather, and great-grandfather, and goes to his house. What he then gives, for instance, food offered to guests, or given as alms (to religious beggars), is considered as the sacrificial fee for the Brahmaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_533"></a><a href="sbe29137.htm#fr_534">220:6</a> Comp. <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a XI, 5, 7, 3. 4.</p>
