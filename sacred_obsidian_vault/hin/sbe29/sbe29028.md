 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 53</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 26.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_113"></a><a href="#fn_112"><font size="1">1</font></a>. To Agni, to the K<i>ri</i>ttikâs.</p>  
 <p>2. To Pra<i>g</i>âpati, to Rohi<i>n</i>î.</p>  
 <p>3. To Soma, to M<i>ri</i>ga<i>s</i>iras.</p>  
 <p>4. To Rudra, to the Ârdrâs.</p>  
 <p>5. To Aditi, to the two Punarvasus.</p>  
 <p>6. To B<i>ri</i>haspati, to Pushya.</p>  
 <p>7. To the Serpents, to the A<i>s</i>leshâs.</p>  
 <p>8. To the Manes, to the Maghâs.</p>  
 <p>9. To Bhaga, to the two Phalgunîs.</p>  
 <p>10. To Aryaman, to the two Phalgunîs.</p>  
 <p>11. To Savitar, to Hasta.</p>  
 <p>12. To Tvash<i>t</i>ar, to <i>K</i>itrâ.</p>  
 <p>13. To Vâyu, to Svâti.</p>  
 <p>14. To Indra and Agni, to the two Vi<i>s</i>âkhâs.</p>  
 <p>15. To Mitra, to Anurâdhâ.</p>  
 <p>16. To Indra, to <i>G</i>yesh<i>th</i>a.</p>  
 <p>17. To Nir<i>ri</i>ti, to Mûla.</p>  
 <p>18. To the Waters, to the Ashâ<i>dh</i>âs.</p>  
 <p>19. To the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, to the Ashâ<i>dh</i>âs.</p>  
 <p>20. To Brahman, to Abhi<i>g</i>it.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 54</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. To Vish<i>n</i>u, to <i>S</i>rava<i>n</i>a.</p>  
 <p>22. To the Vasus, to the Dhanish<i>th</i>âs.</p>  
 <p>23. To Varu<i>n</i>a, to <i>S</i>atabhisha<i>g</i>.</p>  
 <p>24. To A<i>g</i>a ekapad, to the Prosh<i>th</i>apadâs.</p>  
 <p>25. To Ahi budhnya, to the Prosh<i>th</i>apadâs.</p>  
 <p>26. To Pûshan, to Revatî.</p>  
 <p>27. To the two A<i>s</i>vins, to the two A<i>s</i>vinîs.</p>  
 <p>28. To Yama, to the Bhara<i>n</i>îs.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_112"></a><a href="sbe29028.htm#fr_113">53:1</a> 26, 1. This chapter is not found in the <i>S</i>âmbavya-G<i>ri</i>hya, and Nârâya<i>n</i>a expressly designates it as kshepaka kha<i>n</i><i>d</i>a. It is a sort of appendix to the Sûtras 25, 5. 6; a sacrifice having there been prescribed to three Nakshatras with their presiding deities, an enumeration of the Nakshatras and deities is here given. Compare, on similar lists, Weber's second article on the Nakshatras seq. (Abhandlungen der Berliner Akademie der Wissenschaften, 1861), pp. 289 , 315, 367 seq.</p>
