 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now if a half-monthly sacrifice has not been performed, one or the other of them, then a mess of rice (is to be offered as an expiation),</p>  
 <p>2. With (the words), 'To Agni Vai<i>s</i>vânara svâhâ! To Agni Tantumat svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>3. In the case of an intermission of the (morning or evening) oblations</p>  
 <p>4. (He shall make expiatory oblations), in the evening with (the formula), 'Enlightener of the darkness, adoration! Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>5. In the morning with (the formula), 'Enlightener of the morning, adoration! Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_305"></a><a href="#fn_304"><font size="1">6</font></a>. After he has sacrificed as many oblations as there had been sacrifices (left out), the sacrifice (itself goes on) as (stated) above.</p>
