 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 17.</h3>  
 <p>1. With the verse, 'I praised Dadhikrâvan' (Rig-veda IV, 39, 6), let them drink together curds.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_94"></a><a href="#fn_93"><font size="1">2-3</font></a>. Let them sit silent, when the sun has set, until the polar-star appears.</p>  
 <p>3. He shows her the polar-star with the words, 'Firm be thou, thriving with me!'</p>  
 <p>4. Let her say, 'I see the polar-star; may I obtain offspring.'</p>  
 <p>5. Through a period of three nights let them refrain from conjugal intercourse.</p>  
 <p>6. Let them sleep on the ground.</p>  
 <p>7. Let them eat together boiled rice with curds, with the three verses, 'Drink and satiate yourselves' (Rig-veda VIII, 35, 10).</p>  
 <p>8. Let them serve the nuptial fire in the evening and in the morning with the words, 'To Agni svâhâ! To Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>9. 'Let the two men Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a, let the two men, the A<i>s</i>vins both, let the man Indra and also Agni make a man grow in me. Svâhâ!'with</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 44</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (these words she offers) the first oblation if she is desirous of pregnancy.</p>  
 <p>10. For ten days they are not to set out (from home).</p>
