 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 3.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_987"></a><a href="#fn_986"><font size="1">1</font></a>. A student after he has studied the Veda and has offered a present to his teacher, should, with permission (of his parents), take a wife.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_988"></a><a href="#fn_987"><font size="1">2</font></a>. And (he should take) the bath (which signifies the end of studentship).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_989"></a><a href="#fn_988"><font size="1">3-4</font></a>. Of these two (acts the taking of) the bath comes first.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">3, 1. brahma<i>k</i>ârî vedam adhîtyopanyâh<i>ri</i>tya gurave<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>nu<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âto dârân kurvîtâ. 2. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>plavana<i>ñ</i> <i>k</i>a. 3. tayor âplavana<i>m</i> pûrva<i>m</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_380"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 380</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. As, however, in the (collection of) Mantras marriage is treated of (first), it is explained (here) before (the bath).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_990"></a><a href="#fn_989"><font size="1">5</font></a>. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a with a water-pot, wrapped in his robe, keeping silence, should step in front of the fire and should station himself (to the south of it) with his face to the north.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_991"></a><a href="#fn_990"><font size="1">6</font></a>. After (the bride) has taken a bath, (the bridegroom) should dress her in a garment that has not yet been washed, with (the verse), 'They who spun' (MB. I, 1, 5). While she is led up (to him), the bridegroom should murmur (the verse), 'Soma gave her' (l.l. 7).</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_992"></a><a href="#fn_991"><font size="1">7</font></a>. To the south of the bridegroom he (who has led her to him) should make her sit down.</p>  
 <p>8. While she touches him, (the bridegroom) should make oblations of Â<i>g</i>ya with the Sruva, picking out (portions of it [comp. Pâraskara II, 14, 131), with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis.</p>  
 <p>9. A fourth (oblation) with (the three Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis) together.</p>  
 <p>10. The same at the ceremonies of the tonsure (of the child's head), of the initiation (of the Brahma<i>k</i>ârin), and of the cutting of the beard.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">4. mantrâbhivâdât tu pâ<i>n</i>igraha<i>n</i>asya (correct, pâ<i>n</i>igraha<i>n</i>a<i>m</i>?) pûrva<i>m</i> vyâkhyâta<i>m</i>. 5. brâhma<i>n</i>as sahodakumbha<i>h</i> prâv<i>ri</i>to vâgyato<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gre<i>n</i>âgni<i>m</i> gatvodaṅmukhas tish<i>th</i>et. 6. snâtâm ahatenâ<i>kh</i>âdya yâ ak<i>ri</i>ntann ity ânîyamânâyâ<i>m</i> pâ<i>n</i>igrâho<i>g</i>apet Somo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>dadad iti. 7. pâ<i>n</i>igrâhasya dakshi<i>n</i>ata upave<i>s</i>ayed. 8. anvârabdhâyâ<i>m</i> sruve<i>n</i>opaghâta<i>m</i> mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tibhir â<i>g</i>ya<i>m</i> <i>g</i>uhuyât. 9. samastâbhi<i>s</i> <i>k</i>aturthîm. 10. eva<i>m</i> <i>k</i>aulopanayanagodâneshv.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_381"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 381</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. And at the marriage (he makes oblations) with the six verses, 'May Agni go as the first' (MB. I, I, 9 seqq.).</p>  
 <p>12. At Â<i>g</i>ya oblations, unless a special rule is given, the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions and the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation (are) not (offered).</p>  
 <p>13. After (the chief oblations he should) always (make oblations) with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis,</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_993"></a><a href="#fn_992"><font size="1">14-15</font></a>. And with the (verse) sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>  
 <p>15. He should make an expiatory oblation.</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_993"><font size="1">16-31</font></a>. After the sacrifice they both arise.</p>  
 <p>17. (The bridegroom) should pass behind (the bride's) back, station himself to the south, and seize the bride's hand.</p>  
 <p>18. Her mother who has, towards the east, put fried grain mixed with <i>S</i>amî leaves into a basket,</p>  
 <p>19. Should make the bride tread with the tip of her right foot on an upper mill-stone, to the west of the fire, with (the verse which the bridegroom repeats), 'On this stone' (MB. I, 2, 1).</p>  
 <p>20. Her brother, filling once his joined hands</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">11. Agnir etu prathama iti sha<i>d</i>bhi<i>s</i> <i>k</i>a pâ<i>n</i>igraha<i>n</i>e. 12. nâ<i>g</i>yabhâgau na svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>d â<i>g</i>yâhutishv anâde<i>s</i>e. 13. sarvatroparish<i>t</i>ân mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tibhi<i>h</i></font> <a name="fr_995"></a><a href="#fn_994"><font size="1">1</font></a><font size="-1" color="green">. 14. prâ<i>g</i>âpatyayâ <i>k</i>a. 15. prâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta<i>m</i> <i>g</i>uhuyâd. 16. dhutvopottish<i>th</i>ato. 17. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>nup<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>a<i>m</i> gatvâ dakshi<i>n</i>ato<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>vasthâya vadhva<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ali<i>m</i> g<i>ri</i>h<i>n</i>îyât. 18. pûrvâ mâtâ <i>s</i>amîpalâ<i>s</i>ami<i>s</i>rân (var. lect. °mi<i>s</i>râl) lâ<i>g</i>â<i>ñ</i> <i>kh</i>ûrpe k<i>ri</i>tvâ. 19. pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>âd agner d<i>ri</i>shatputram âkramayed vadhû<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>ena prapadenemam a<i>s</i>mânam iti. 20. sak<i>ri</i>dg<i>ri</i>hîtam a<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ali<i>m</i> lâ<i>g</i>ânâ<i>m</i> vadhva<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>alâv âvaped bhrâtâ.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_382"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 382</font></a></p>  
 <p>with fried grain, should pour it into the bride's joined hands.</p>  
 <p>21 <a name="fr_996"></a><a href="#fn_995"><font size="1">21</font></a>. Or some friend (instead of the brother).</p>  
 <p>22. That she should sacrifice over the fire without opening her joined hands with (the verse which the bridegroom repeats), 'This woman' (MB. I, 2, 2).</p>  
 <p>23. (The verses), 'Aryaman' and 'Pûshan' (l.l. 3, 4) (are repeated) at the two following (oblations of fried grain).</p>  
 <p>24. After that sacrifice he should go back in the same way (see Sûtra 17), and should lead her round the fire, so that their right sides are turned towards it with (the formula), 'The maid from the fathers' (l.l. 5).</p>  
 <p>25. (These rites), beginning from his stationing himself (to the south, Sûtra 17), (are performed) thrice.</p>  
 <p>26. After (she) has poured the remnants (of the fried grain) into the fire, he should make her step forward in a north-eastern direction with (the formula), 'For sap with one step' (l.l. 6, 7).</p>  
 <p>27. The looking at the lookers-on, the mounting of the chariot, the reciting (of Mantras) at places difficult to pass (on the way of the bridegroom and the bride, is performed) with (verses) suited (to those different occasions).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">21. suh<i>ri</i>d vâ ka<i>s</i><i>k</i>it. 22. ta<i>m</i> sâgnau <i>g</i>uhuyâd avi<i>kh</i>idyâ<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>alim iya<i>m</i> nârîty. 23. Aryama<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> Pûsha<i>n</i>am ity uttarayor. 24. hute tenaiva gatvâ pradakshi<i>n</i>am agni<i>m</i> pari<i>n</i>ayet kanyalâ pit<i>ri</i>bhya ity. 25. avasthânaprabh<i>ri</i>ty eva<i>m</i> tri<i>s</i>. 26. <i>s</i>ûrpe<i>n</i>a <i>s</i>ish<i>t</i>ân agnâv opya prâgudî<i>k</i>îm utkramayed ekam isha itî. 27. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>kshakâveksha<i>n</i>arathâroha<i>n</i>adurgânumantra<i>n</i>âny abhirûpâbhir.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_383"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 383</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. Walking forward behind the fire, the water-carrier (see Sûtra 5) should besprinkle the bridegroom on his forehead.</p>  
 <p>29. So also the bride.</p>  
 <p>30. When he has thus been besprinkled, (he should repeat the verse), 'May (the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs) anoint (or, unite)' (MB. I, 2, 9).</p>  
 <p>31. He should seize her right hand, together with the thumb, with the six (verses), 'I seize thy hand' (MB. I, 2, 10 seqq.).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">28. apare<i>n</i>âgnim auduko gatvâ pâ<i>n</i>igrâha<i>m</i> mûrdhany avasi<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ed. 29. vadhû<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a. 30. sama<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>antv ity avasikto. 31. dakshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> pâ<i>n</i>i<i>m</i> sâṅgush<i>th</i>a<i>m</i> g<i>ri</i>h<i>n</i>îyâd g<i>ri</i>bh<i>n</i>âmi ta iti sha<i>d</i>bhi<i>h</i>.</font></p>
