 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 18.</h3>  
 <p>1. A student who is going to set out on a journey, speaks thus to his teacher:</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_201"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 'Of inhalation and exhalation'(this he says) in a low voice; 'Om, I will dwell'this aloud.</p>  
 <p>3. (The teacher) in a low voice (replies), 'To inhalation and exhalation I, the wide-extended one, resort with thee. To the protecting god I give thee in charge. God Savitar; this student belongs to thee; I give him in charge to thee; protect him; do not forsake him.'</p>  
 <p>4. 'Om, hail!' the teacher aloud. 'Hail!' the teacher aloud.</p>  
 <p align="center">Here ends the Second Adhyâya.</p>
