 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 12.</h3>  
 <p>1. When a battle is beginning, (the royal Purohita) should cause the king to put on his armour (in the following way).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_564"></a><a href="#fn_563"><font size="1">12_2</font></a>. (The Purohita) stations himself to the west of (the king's) chariot with (the hymn?), 'I have brought thee hither; be here' (Rig-veda X, 173).</p>  
 <p>3. With (the verse), 'Like a thunder-cloud is his countenance' (Rig-veda VI, 75, 1), he should tender the coat of mail to him.</p>  
 <p>4. With the following (verse) the bow.</p>  
 <p>5. The following (verse) he should cause him to repeat.</p>  
 <p>6. He should murmur himself the fourth.</p>  
 <p>7. With the fifth he should tender the quiver to him.</p>  
 <p>8. When (the king) starts, the sixth.</p>  
 <p>9. The seventh (he recites) over the horses.</p>  
 <p>10. The eighth he should cause (the king) to repeat while looking at the arrows;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_234"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 234</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (The verse), 'Like a serpent it encircles the arm with its windings' (Rig-veda VI, 75, 14), when he ties to his arm the leather (by which the arm is protected against the bow-string).</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_565"></a><a href="#fn_564"><font size="1">12</font></a>. He then mounts up to (the king on his chariot), while he is driving, and causes him to repeat the Abhîvarta hymn (Rig-veda X, 174) and the two verses, 'He who, Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a' (Rig-veda VIII, 101, 3 seq.).</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_566"></a><a href="#fn_565"><font size="1">13</font></a>. He then should look at him with the Apratiratha, <i>S</i>âsa, and Saupar<i>n</i>a hymns.</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_567"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">14</font></a>. The Saupar<i>n</i>a is (the hymn), 'May the streams of honey and ghee flow forwards.'</p>  
 <p>15. (The king) should drive (in his chariot successively) to all quarters (of the horizon).</p>  
 <p>16. He should commence the battle in the line of battle invented by Âditya or by U<i>s</i>anas.</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_568"></a><a href="#fn_567"><font size="1">17-18</font></a>. He should touch the drum with the three verses, 'Fill earth and heaven with thy roar' (Rig-veda VI, 47, 29 seqq.).</p>  
 <p>18. With (the verse), 'Shot off fall down' (Rig-veda VI, 75, 16), he should shoot off the arrows.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 235</font></a></p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_569"></a><a href="#fn_568"><font size="1">19</font></a>. 'Where the arrows fly' (l.l. v. 17)this (verse) he should murmur while they are fighting.</p>  
 <p>20. Or he should teach (the king the texts mentioned). Or he should teach (the king).</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Third Adhyâya.</p>
