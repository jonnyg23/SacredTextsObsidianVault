 <p align="center"><b>Chapter XX</b></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="center"><b>On the description of Brahmâ Yajñâ, Sandhyâs, etc.</b><b></b></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"><a name="page_1116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 1116</font></a></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">1-25. The twice born (Brâhma<u>n</u>a) is firstly to sip three times (make Âchamana); then to make the mârjana (sprinkle water) twice; he is to touch the water by the right hand and sprinkle water on his two feet. Next, he is to sprinkle with water his head, eyes, nose, ears, heart, and head thoroughly. Then speaking out the Desa and Kâla (place and time) he should commence the Brahmâ Yajñâ. Next for the destruction of all the sins and for getting liberation, he should have the Darbha (sacrificial grass, and the Kusa grasses), two on his right hand, three on his left hand, one grass each on his seat, sacrificial thread, his tuft, and his heels. No sin can now remain in his body.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">I am performing this Brahmâ Yajñâ for the satisfaction of the Devatâ according to the Sûtra, thus thinking he is to repeat the Gâyatrî thrice. Then he is to recite the following mantras :-- Agnimîle purohitam, etc., Yadamgeti Agnirvai, Mahâvratanchaiva panthâ, Athâta<u>h</u> Samhitâyâscha vidâmaghavat, Mahâvratasya, Î<u>s</u>etvorjetvâ, Agna âyâhi Sanno Devî rabbîs<u>t</u>aye, Tasya Samâmnâyo Briddairâdaich Sik<u>s</u>âm pravak<u>s</u>yâmi, Pa<u>n</u>cha Samvatsareti, Mayarasatajabhetyeva, Gaurgmâ, also he is to recite the two following Sûtras :-- Athâto Dharma Jijñâsâ, Athâto Brahmâ Jijñâsa. Next he is to recite the mantra Tachhamyo<u>h</u> and also the mantra Namo Brâhma<u>n</u>e namo stvagnaye nama<u>h</u> prithivyai nama Osadhîbhyo<u>h</u> nama<u>h</u>. (These mantras are the famous mantras of the Rig Veda). Next perform the Deva-tarpa<u>n</u>am, thus :-- Om Prajâpati stripyatu, Om Brahmâ stripyatu, Om Vedâs tripyantu, Om Ri<u>s</u>ayastri pyantu,  Om Devâstripantu, Om Sarva<u>n</u>i chhandâmsi tripyantu, Om Om Kâra stripyatu, Om Va<u>s</u>a<u>t</u> Kâra stripyatu, Om Vyârhitayas tripyantu, Om Sâvitrî tripyatu, Om Gâyatrî tripyatu, Om Yajñâ stripyantu, Om Dyâvâ prithivyau tripyatâm. Om antarîk<u>s</u>am tripyatu, Om Ahorâtrâni tripyantu, Om Sâmkkyâ stripyantu, Om Siddhâ stripyantu, Om Samudrâ stripyantu, Om Nadyâs tripyantu, Om girayas tripyantu, Om Ksettrau<u>s</u> adhivana spati gandharvâ Psarasas tripyantu, Om nâgâ vayâmsi gâvascha sâdhyâ viprâsta thaiva cha, yak<u>s</u>â rak<u>s</u>â<u>n</u>si bhutanî tyeva mantâni tripyantu. Next, suspending the sacrificial thread from the neck, perform the Ri<u>s</u>i tarpa<u>n</u>am, thus :-- Om Satarchinas tripyantu, Om mâdhyamâs tripyantu,</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"><a name="page_1117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 1117</font></a></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Gritsamada stripyatu, Om Visvâmitra stripyantu, Om Vâmadeva stripyantu, Om Atri stripyatu, Om Bharadvâjastripyatu, Om Vasi<u>st</u>hastripyatu, Om Pragâthastripyatu, Pâvamânyastripyantu. Next, holding the sacrificial thread over the right shoulder and under the left arm, perform the Tarpa<u>n</u>am, thus :--</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om K<u>s</u>udrasûktâ stripyantu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Mahâsûktâstripyantu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sanaka stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sananda stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sanâtana stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sanat Kumâra stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Kapila stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Âsuristripyatu,</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Vohalistripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Pañchasikha stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sumantu Jaimini Vaisampâyana Paila Sûtra Bhâ<u>s</u>ya bhârata Mahâ Bhârata Dharmâchâryah stripyantu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Jânantîvâha vigârgya Gautama Sâkalya vâbhravya Mândavya Mândûkeyâ stripyantu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Gârgî Vâchak<u>n</u>avî tripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Vadavâ prâtitheyî tripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sulabhâ maitreyî tripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Kahola stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Kau<u>s</u>îtaka stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Mahâ Kau<u>s</u>itaka stripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Bhâradvâja stripyatu,</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Paimga stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Mahâpaimga stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sujajñâ stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sâmkhyâyana stripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Aitareya stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Mahaitareya stripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Vâskala stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sâkala stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sujâta vaktra stripyatu. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Audavâhi stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Saujâmi stripyatu,</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Saunaka stripyatu,</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Âsvalâyana stripyatu.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"><a name="page_1118"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 1118</font></a></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">26-54. Let all the other Âchâryas be satisfied. Om Ye Ke châsma<u>t</u> kule Jâtâ aputrâ gotri<u>n</u>o mritâh. te grih<u>n</u>antu mayâ dattam vastrani<u>s</u>pî<u>d</u>i to dakam. Saying thus offer water squeezed out of a cloth. O Nârada! Thus I have spoken to you of the rules of Brahmâ Yajñâ. Whoever performs thus the Brahmâ Yajñâ gets the fruits of studying all the Vedas. Then performing, in due order, the Vaisva deva, Homa, Srâddha, serving the guests, and feeding the cows, the devotee is to take his meals during the fifth part of the day along with the other Brâhma<u>n</u>as. Then the sixth and the seventh parts of the day he is to spend in reading histories and the Purâ<u>n</u>as. Then the eighth part of the day he is to devote in seeing the relatives, talking with them and receiving visits from other persons; then he will be prepared to perform the evening Sandhyâ. O Nârada! I am now talking of the evening Sandhyâ. Listen. Srî Bhagavatî is pleased very quickly with him who performs the evening Sandhyâ. First make the Âchaman and make the Vâyu (air) in the body steady. With heart tranquilled and with the seat Baddha Padmâsana, be calm and quiet while engaged in performing the Sandhyâ. At the commencement of all actions prescribed in the Srutis and Smritis, first perform the Sagarbha Prâ<u>n</u>âyama. In other words recite the mantra mentally for the due number of moments and make the Prânâyama. Simply meditating is called Agarbha Prâ<u>n</u>âyama. Here no mantra is necessary to be recited. Then have the Bhutasuddhi (have the purifications of the elements) and make the Sankalpa. First of all, the purification of elements, etc., are to be done first; one becomes, then, entitled to do other actions. While doing Pûraka (inhaling), Kumbhaka (retaining) and Rechaka (exhaling) in Pra<u>n</u>âyâma, meditate on the Deity stated duly. In the evening time meditate on the Bhagavatî Sandhyâ Devî thus :-- The name of the then Gâyatrî Devî is Sarasvatî. She is old, of black colour, wearing ordinary clothes; in her hands are seen conch shell, disc, club and lotus. On Her feet the anklets are making sweet tinkling sounds; on Her loins there is the golden thread; decked with various ornaments. She is sitting on Garu<u>d</u>a. On Her head the invaluable jewel crown is seen; on Her neck, the necklaces of stars; Her forehead is shining with a brilliant lustre emitting from the pearl and jewel Tâtamka ornaments. She has put on yellow clothes; Her nature is eternal knowledge and ever-bliss. She is uttering Sâma Veda. She resides in the Heavens and daily She goes in the path of the Sun. I invoke the Devî from the Solar Orb. O Nârada! Meditate on the Devî thus and perform the Sandhyâ. Then perform the Mârjanam by the mantra Âpohi<u>st</u>hâ and next by the mantra</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"><a name="page_1119"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 1119</font></a></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> Agnischa mâ manyuscha. The remaining actions are the same as before. Next, repeat the Gâyatrî and offer, with a pure heart, the offering of Arghya to the Sun for the satisfaction of Nârâya<u>n</u>a. While offering this Arghya, keep the two legs level and similar and take water in folded palms and meditating on the Devatâ within the Solar Orb, throw it towards Him. The fool that offers Sûryârghya in the water, out of ignorance, disregarding the injunctions of the Srutis, will have to perform Prâyaschitta for that sin. Next, worship the Sun by the Sûrya mantra. Then taking ones seat, meditate on the Devî and repeat the Gâyatrî. One thousand times or five hundred times the Gâyatrî is to be repeated. The worship, etc., in the evening is the same as in the morning. Now I am speaking of the Tarpa<u>n</u>am in the Evening Sandhyâ. Hear. Vasi<u>st</u>ha is the Ri<u>s</u>i of the aforesaid Sarasvatî. Vi<u>sn</u>u in the form of Sarasvatî is the Devatâ; Gâyatrî is the Chhanda; its application is in the Evening Sandhyâ Tarpa<u>n</u>am. Now the Tarpa<u>n</u>am of the Sandhyânga (the adjunct of Sandhyâ) runs as follows :-- </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sva<u>h</u> Puru<u>s</u>am Tarpayâmi. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sâmavedam Tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sûryama<u>nd</u>alam tarpayâmi. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Hira<u>n</u>yagarbham tarpayâmi. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Paramâtmânam tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sarasvatîm tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Devamâtaram tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Samkritim tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Vriddhâm Sandhyâm tarpayâmi </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Vi<u>sn</u>u rûpinîm Usasîm tarpayâmi. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Nirmrijîm tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sarvasiddhi kâri<u>n</u>îm tarpayâmi. </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Sarvamantrâ dhipatikâm tarpayami.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Om Bhurbhuva<u>h</u> Sva<u>h</u> Puru<u>s</u>am tarpayâmi.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Thus perform the Vaidik Tarpa<u>n</u>am. O Nârada! Thus have been described the rules of the sin destroying evening Sandhyâ. By this evening Sandhyâ, all sorts of pains and afflictions and diseases are removed. And ultimately the Mok<u>s</u>a is obtained. What more than this that you should know this Sandhyâ Banda<u>n</u>am as the principal thing amongst the good conduct and right ways of living. Therefore Srî Bhagavatî fructifies all the desires of the Bhaktas who perform this Sandhyâ Vanda<u>n</u>am.</p>

 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p>Here ends the Twentieth Chapter of the Eleventh Book on the description of Brahmâ Yajñâ, Sandhyâs, etc., in the Mahâpurâ<u>n</u>am Srî Mad Devî Bhâgavatam of 18,000 verses by Mahar<u>s</u>i Veda Vyâsa.</p>
