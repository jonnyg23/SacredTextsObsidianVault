 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Hymns of the Tamil Saivite Saints</i>, by F. Kingsbury and G.P. Phillips, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="green">p. 128</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">APPENDIX I</h3>  <h3 align="center">SHRINES MENTIONED IN THESE POEMS</h3>  
 <p>Ādutuṛai, or Āvadutuṛai, in Tanjore District, now a station on the South Indian Railway.</p>  
 <p>Aiyāṛu, twelve miles from Tanjore.</p>  
 <p>Ālavāy, or Uttarakosaimangai, Madura.</p>  
 <p>Aṇṇāmalai, or Tiruvaṇṇāmalai, in South Arcot District.</p>  
 <p>Ārūr, Tiruvallur, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Athihai Viraṭṭānam, South Arcot District.</p>  
 <p>Bramāpuram, Shiyali, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Chenkāṭṭankuḍi</p>  
 <p>Chidambaram, or Tillai, or Perumpattapuliyūr, in S. Arcot District, the most venerated place of Śaivism.</p>  
 <p>Chōl̤a, one of the three great kingdoms into which the ancient Tamil country was divided.</p>  
 <p>Comorin, extreme southern point of India, still a great place of pilgrimage.</p>  
 <p>Dharmapuram, near Tranquebar, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Kāviri, or Cauvery, the most sacred river in South India.</p>  
 <p>Kachchi Ehambam, or Kānchipuram, the modern Conjeeveram, in North Arcot District.</p>  
 <p>Keḍila</p>  
 <p>Kētāram</p>  
 <p>Koḍumuḍi, near Erode, Coimbatore District.</p>  
 <p>Kōḷili, near the modern Tiruvallur, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Kongu</p>  
 <p>Kundaiyūr, near Tiruvallur, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Kuttālam, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Mal̤apādi, South Arcot District, near Trichinopoly.</p>  
 <p>Maṛaikkīdu, the modern Vetharaniam, Tanjore District. Curiously enough, the modern name is the Sanskrit translation of the Tamil, meaning 'Forest of the Vedas,' <i>i.e.</i>, lonely place where Vedas are studied.</p>  
 <p>Maruhal, on the Cauvery River, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Neyttānam, on the Cauvery River, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Ottiyūr, better known as Tiruvottiyur, a few miles north of Madras, now practically a suburb.</p>  
 <p>Pal̤anam, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Perumpattapuliyur, see Chidambaram.</p>  
 <p>Perunduṛai, Āvudaiyārkoil, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Puhalūr</p>  
 <p>Pungūr, seven miles from Shiyali, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Pūnturutti</p>  
 <p>Puvaṇam, twelve miles from Madura.</p>  
 <p>Tillai, see Chidambaram.</p>  
 <p>Tiruputtūr, Ramnad District, near Pudukottah.</p>  
 <p>Tiruvaṇṇāmalai, see Aṇṇāmalai.</p>  
 <p>Tiru Neyttānam, see Neyttānam.</p>  
 <p>Tūngānaimādam</p>  
 <p>Uttarakōsamangai, Ramnad District.</p>  
 <p>Valivalam on the Cauvery, Tanjore District.</p>  
 <p>Venneynallūr, South Arcot District.</p>  
 <p>NḄ.—"Tiru" is an epithet meaning "Holy," but where it has become practically part of the place-name, that name is given in this list as if it began with T.</p>
