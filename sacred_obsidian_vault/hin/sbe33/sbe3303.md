 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Minor Law Books (SBE33)</i>, by Julius Jolly, [1889], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_xxiii"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. xxiii</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ABBREVIATIONS.</h3>  
 <p>Aparârka = Aparârka's Commentary on the Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya-sm<i>ri</i>ti, Deccan College MS.</p>  
 <p>Âpastamba, see Manu.</p>  
 <p>Baudhâyana, see Manu.</p>  
 <p>Col. Dig. = Colebrooke's Digest of Hindu Law (translation of <i>G</i>agannâtha's work).</p>  
 <p>Dâyabhâga = Colebrooke's translation of the Dâyabhâga on Inheritance, or the Sanskrit text of the D., in the Calcutta edition of 1829.</p>  
 <p>Dâyakramasaṅgraha = Wynch's translation in Stokes's Hindu Law-Books, or the Calcutta edition.</p>  
 <p><i>G</i>agannâtha = <i>G</i>agannâtha's Vivâdabhaṅgâr<i>n</i>ava (the work translated by Colebrooke), Bengali MS. in my possession.</p>  
 <p>Gautama, see Manu.</p>  
 <p></p><dir>M. or Mitâksharâ = Mitâksharâ, the Calcutta edition of the Vyavahârâdhyâya, or Colebrooke's version of the Mitâksharâ on Inheritance.  
 <p>M. Macn. = Macnaghten's translation of the Mitâksharâ on Administration of Justice.</p></dir>  
 <p>Manu = the Code of Manu, ed. Jolly, London. 1887; or Professor Bühler's translation of the same. For the principal editions and translations of Âpastamba, Baudhâyana, and the other old law-books, I may refer to my volume of Tagore Law Lectures.</p>  
 <p></p><dir>May. = Mandlik's translation of the Vyavahâra Mayûkha, in his Hindu Law, Bombay, 1880.  
 <p>May. (text) = Mandlik's edition of the same, ibid.</p>  
 <p>Mayûkha = the same work.</p></dir>  
 <p>Minor Nârada = The Institutes of Nârada, transl. by J. Jolly, London, 1876; or the unpublished Sanskrit original of the same work. Nârada = the present translation.</p>  
 <p>Nârada-sm<i>ri</i>ti = The Institutes of Nârada, edited by J. Jolly, in the Bibliotheca Indica series.</p>  
 <p>Nepalese Nârada = Mr. Bendall's Nepalese Palm-leaf MS. of Nârada. Raghunandana = Raghunandana's Vyavahâratattva, the Calcutta edition.</p>  
 <p>Ratn. = Vivâda Ratnâkara, in the Bibliotheca Indica.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_xxiv"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. xxiv</font></a></p>  
 <p>Sm<i>ri</i>ti<i>k</i>. = Deva<i>n</i><i>n</i>abha<i>t</i><i>t</i>a's Sm<i>ri</i>ti<i>k</i>andrikâ, India Office MS.</p>  
 <p>Sm<i>ri</i>ti<i>k</i>andrikâ = the same, or the chapter on Inheritance, transl. by Iyer.</p>  
 <p><i>T</i>o<i>d</i>. or <i>T</i>o<i>d</i>arânanda = Vyavahârasaukhya in <i>T</i>o<i>d</i>ar Mall's <i>T</i>o<i>d</i>arânanda, Deccan College MS.</p>  
 <p>(Uncertain) = texts quoted as 'Sm<i>ri</i>ti.' generally, without the name of the author being given.</p>  
 <p>Vasish<i>th</i>a, see Manu.</p>  
 <p>Vîram. = Vîramitrodaya, in Jîbânanda Vidyâsâgara's edition.</p>  
 <p>Vish<i>n</i>u, see Manu.</p>  
 <p>Viv. = Vivâda<i>k</i>intâma<i>n</i>i, translated by Tagore, Calcutta, 1863.</p>  
 <p>Vivâda<i>k</i>intâma<i>n</i>i = the Sanskrit text, Calcutta edition of 1837.</p>  
 <p>V. T. = Vivâdatâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ava by Kamalâkara, India Office MS.</p>  
 <p>Vy. <i>K</i>. = Vyavahâra<i>k</i>intâma<i>n</i>i by Vâ<i>k</i>aspatimi<i>s</i>ra, Devanâgarî MS. in my possession.</p>  
 <p>Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya, see Manu.</p>
