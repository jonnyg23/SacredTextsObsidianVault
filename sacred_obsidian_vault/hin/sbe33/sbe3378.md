 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Minor Law Books (SBE33)</i>, by Julius Jolly, [1889], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XX. DEFAMATION.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_1160"></a><a href="#fn_1159"><font size="1">1</font></a> Injury (pârushya) is declared to be of two kinds, harsh speeches and beating; each of these two kinds is again divided into three species, and the punishment is pronounced to be threefold.</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_1161"></a><a href="#fn_1160"><font size="1">2</font></a> Abuse of the first (or lowest) degree means offensive language against, or defamation of, a country, village, family, or the like, without (mentioning) an (individual ignominious) act.</p>  
 <p>3. Referring (in terms of contempt) to a man's sister or mother, or charging him with a minor sin, is termed abuse of a middling sort by the learned in law.</p>  
 <p>4. Charging a man with taking forbidden food or drinks, or taxing him with a mortal sin, or maliciously exposing his weakest points, is termed abuse of the highest degree.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_1162"></a><a href="#fn_1161"><font size="1">5</font></a> When two persons abuse each other, their punishment shall be equal, if they are equals in caste; if one is inferior to the other, his punishment shall be double; for a superior, half (of the ordinary punishment) is ordained.</p>  
 <p>

 <p>6. <a name="fr_1163"></a><a href="#fn_1162"><font size="1">6</font></a> When persons equal in caste and qualities abuse one another, the punishment ordained for them in the system of law is thirteen Pa<i>n</i>as and a half.</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_1164"></a><a href="#fn_1163"><font size="1">7</font></a> For a Brahman abusing a Kshatriya, the fine shall be half of a hundred (fifty Pa<i>n</i>as); for abusing a Vai<i>s</i>ya, half of fifty (twenty-five Pa<i>n</i>as); for abusing a <i>S</i>ûdra, twelve and a half.</p>  
 <p>8. This punishment has been declared for (abusing) a virtuous <i>S</i>ûdra who has committed no wrong; no offence is imputable to a Brahman for abusing (a <i>S</i>ûdra) devoid of virtue.</p>  
 <p>9. A Vai<i>s</i>ya shall be fined a hundred (Pa<i>n</i>as) for reviling a Kshatriya; a Kshatriya reviling a Vai<i>s</i>ya shall have to pay half of that amount as a fine.</p>  
 <p>10. In the case of a Kshatriya reviling a <i>S</i>ûdra, the fine shall be twenty Pa<i>n</i>as; in the case of a Vai<i>s</i>ya, the double amount is declared to be the proper fine by persons learned in law.</p>  
 <p>11. A <i>S</i>ûdra shall be compelled to pay the first fine for abusing a Vai<i>s</i>ya; the middling fine (for abusing) a Kshatriya; and the highest fine (for abusing) a Brahman.</p>  
 <p>12. <a name="fr_1165"></a><a href="#fn_1164"><font size="1">12</font></a> (A <i>S</i>ûdra) teaching the precepts of religion, or uttering the words of the Veda, or insulting a Brahman, shall be punished by cutting out his tongue.</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_1166"></a><a href="#fn_1165"><font size="1">13</font></a> (A man) reviling a sister or other (relation) of</p>

 <p>another person shall give a fine amounting to fifty Pa<i>n</i>as.</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_1167"></a><a href="#fn_1166"><font size="1">14</font></a> He who reviles a person's native country or other (belongings of his), shall be fined twelve Pa<i>n</i>as and a half. He who through arrogance imputes an offence to him, shall be compelled to pay the first fine.</p>  
 <p>15. This gradation of fines has been declared by me, subject to modification by the sages, in conformity with the (particular caste or qualities of a) man, so as either to remain as declared, or to be reduced or raised.</p>
