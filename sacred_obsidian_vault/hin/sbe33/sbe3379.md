 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Minor Law Books (SBE33)</i>, by Julius Jolly, [1889], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXI. ASSAULT.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_1168"></a><a href="#fn_1167"><font size="1">1</font></a> Injuring (a man) with a hand, stone, club, or (throwing at him) ashes, or mud, or dust, or (attacking him with) a weapon, is termed assault.</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_1169"></a><a href="#fn_1168"><font size="1">2</font></a> Throwing ashes or the like (at a man), or striking him with a hand or the like, is (termed) an assault of the first degree; the fine to be inflicted in that case shall amount to a Mâsha.</p>  
 <p>3. This fine is ordained for (an assault on) equals in caste; (for assaults) on another man's wife or on a superior, it shall be twofold or threefold, according to the sages, according to the rank (of the person injured).</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_1170"></a><a href="#fn_1169"><font size="1">4</font></a> He who having been abused returns the abuse, or having been beaten returns the blow, or strikes an offender down, commits no wrong.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>

 <p>5. <a name="fr_1171"></a><a href="#fn_1170"><font size="1">5</font></a> When a person throws gravel, stones, or pieces of wood at another, the first (or lowest) fine shall be inflicted on him. When they mutually strike one another with a hand or foot, it shall amount to ten or twenty Pa<i>n</i>as respectively.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_1172"></a><a href="#fn_1171"><font size="1">6</font></a> The second fine shall be imposed when two persons in anger use weapons against one another; when a wound has been inflicted, the punishment shall be fixed by experts, corresponding to the severity of the hurt.</p>  
 <p>7. For injuring (a person) with bricks, stones, or a wooden club, (the fine shall be) two Mâshas; the double fine shall be inflicted, according to the sages, when blood flows.</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_1173"></a><a href="#fn_1172"><font size="1">8</font></a> For tearing the skin, the first (or lowest) fine (shall be inflicted); for tearing the flesh, the second fine; for breaking a bone, the highest fine; for killing, capital punishment.</p>  
 <p>9. For breaking the ear, nose, or hand (of a person), or injuring his teeth, or feet, the second fine shall be inflicted; and double of that, for entirely cutting off (any of those limbs).</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_1174"></a><a href="#fn_1173"><font size="1">10</font></a> He who injures a limb, or divides it, or cuts it off, shall be compelled to pay the expense of curing it; and (he who- forcibly took an article in a quarrel, shall restore) his plunder.</p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_1175"></a><a href="#fn_1174"><font size="1">11</font></a> When a man has been beaten in a solitary place, or when no wound is seen, the offender shall</p>

 <p>be found out by circumstantial evidence or by an oath or ordeal.</p>  
 <p>12. When he has been struck in the interior of a house, or in a wood, or at night, and blood becomes visible, one shall not examine witnesses.</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_1176"></a><a href="#fn_1175"><font size="1">13</font></a> When two persons strike simultaneously, the punishment shall be equal for both; the first aggressor and he who is a habitual mischief-maker shall be compelled to pay a larger fine.</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_1177"></a><a href="#fn_1176"><font size="1">14</font></a> When a low person offends a man in high position by harsh words or the like, that man must not be persecuted by the king if he beats his aggressor.</p>  
 <p>15. <a name="fr_1178"></a><a href="#fn_1177"><font size="1">15</font></a> Persons begotten in the inverse order of castes, and members of the lowest caste, are called the refuse of society; should they insult a Brahman, they shall be corporally punished, and shall never be amerced in a fine.</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_1179"></a><a href="#fn_1178"><font size="1">16</font></a> He who employs at an improper time, for drawing or carrying, tired, or hungry, or thirsty animals, shall be compelled to atone for it in the same way as a cow-killer, or to pay the first fine.</p>
