 <p>
 This is a translation of the Hindu law books written about the 
 sixth century CE by Narada and Brihaspati.
 These are later works than the other legal writings
 in the Sacred Books of the East
 series, such as <a href="../manu.htm">The Laws of Manu</a> (SBE25),
 <a href="../sbe07/index.htm">The Institutes of Vishnu</a> (SBE07),
 <a href="../sbe02/index.htm">Sacred Laws of the Aryas, Part I</a> (SBE02),
 and <a href="../sbe14/index.htm">Sacred Laws of the Aryas, Part II</a> (SBE14),
 all composed prior to 200 BCE at the latest.
 According to Jolly, the courtroom procedure discussed here is very
 similar to that described in the earliest known Hindu drama,
 <a href="../lcc/index.htm">The Little Clay Cart</a>.
 </p>
