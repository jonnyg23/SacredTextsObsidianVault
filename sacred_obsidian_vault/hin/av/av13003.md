 <p><a name="page_120"><font size="1" color="green">p. 120</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN III</h3>  <h4 align="center">A glorification of Rohita. with a malediction on the man who wrongs a Brāhman</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300301">1</a></font></span>He who engendered these, the earth and heaven, who made the<br>    worlds the mantle that he weareth,<br>   In whom abide the six wide-spreading regions through which the<br>   Bird's keen vision penetrateth,<br>   This God is wroth offended by the sinner who wrongs the<br>   Brāhman who hath gained this knowledge<br>   Agitate him, O Rohita; destroy him: entangle in thy snares the<br>   Brāman's tyrant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300302">2</a></font></span>He from whom winds blow pure in ordered season, from whom<br>    the seas flow forth in all directions, <a name="page_121"><font size="1" color="green">p. 121</font></a><br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300303">3</a></font></span>He who takes life away, he who bestows it; from whom comes<br>    breath to every living creature,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300304">4</a></font></span>Who with the breath he draws sates earth and heaven, with<br>    expiration fills the ocean's belly,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300305">5</a></font></span>In whom Virāj, Prajāpati, Parameshthin, Agni Vaisvānara abide<br>    with Pankti,<br>   He who hath taken to himself the breathing of the Supreme, the<br>    vigour of the Highest,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300306">6</a></font></span>On whom rest six expenses and five regions, four waters, and<br>    three syllables of worship,<br>   He who hath looked between both spheres in anger,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300307">7</a></font></span>He who, consuming food, became its master, the Lord of Prayer,<br>    the Regent of Devotion,<br>   The world's Lord, present and to be hereafter,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300308">8</a></font></span>He who metes out the thirteenth month, constructed with days<br>    and nights, containing thirty members,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300309">9</a></font></span>Dark the descent; the strong-winged birds are golden: they fly<br>    aloft to heaven, enrobed in waters.<br>   They have come hither from the seat of Order,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300310">10</a></font></span>What silver. Kasyapa, thou hast refulgent, what brightly-shining<br>    lotus-flower collected,<br>   Wherein are gathered seven Suns together,<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300311">11</a></font></span>In front the Brihat-Sāman is his mantle, and from behind<br>   Rathantara enfolds him,<br>   Ever with care robing themselves in splendour.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300312">12</a></font></span>One of his wings was Brihat, and the other Rathantarr., vigorous<br>    with one same purpose,<br>   What time the Gods gave Rohita his being.<br>   This God, etc. <a name="page_122"><font size="1" color="green">p. 122</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300313">13</a></font></span>At evening he is Varuna and Agni, ascending in the morning he<br>    is Mitra.<br>   As Savitar, he moves through air's mid region, as Indra warms-<br>    the heavens from the centre.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300314">14</a></font></span>This gold-hued Harisa's wings, soaring to heaven spread o'er a<br>    thousand days' continued journey.<br>   Supporting all the Gods upon his bosom, he goes his way behol-<br>    ding every creature.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300315">15</a></font></span>This is the God who dwells-within the waters, the thousand-<br>    rooted, many-powered Atri,<br>   He who brought all this world into existence.<br>   This God; etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300316">16</a></font></span>With flying feet his tawny coursers carry the bright God through,<br>    the sky, aglow with splendour.<br>   Whose limbs uplifted fire and heat the heavens: hither he shines-<br>    with beams of golden colour.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300317">17</a></font></span>He beside whom his bay steeds bear the Ādityas, by whom as<br>    sacrifice go many knowing.<br>   The sole light shining spread through various places.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300318">18</a></font></span>This seven make the one-wheeled chariot ready: bearing seven<br>    names the single courser draws it.<br>   The wheel, three-naved, is sound and undecaying: thereon these<br>    worlds of life are all dependent.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300319">19</a></font></span>Eight times attached the potent Courser draws it, Sire of the<br>   Gods, father of hymns and praises.<br>   So Mātarisvan, measuring in spirit the thread of Order, purifies<br>    all regions.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300320">20</a></font></span>The thread that goes through all celestial quarters within the<br>   Gāyatri, womb of life eternal.<br>   This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300321">21</a></font></span>There are the settings, three the upward risings, three are the-<br>    spaces, yea, and three the heavens.<br>   We know thy triple place of birth, O Agni, we know the deities''<br>    triple generations. <a name="page_123"><font size="1" color="green">p. 123</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300322">22</a></font></span>He who, as soon as born, laid broad earth open, and set the<br>    ocean in the air's mid-region, This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300323">23</a></font></span>Thou, Agni, kind with lights and mental powers, hast up in<br>    heaven shone as the Sun, enkindled.<br>   The Maruts, sons of Prisni, sang his praises what time the Gods<br>    gave Rohita his being. This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300324">24</a></font></span>Giver of breath, giver of strength and vigour, he whose com-<br>    mandment all the Gods acknowledge,<br>   He who is Lord of this, of man and cattle, This God, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300325">25</a></font></span>The single-footed hath outstepped the biped, the biped overtakes<br>    the triple-footed.<br>   The quadruped hath wrought when bipeds called him, standing<br>    and looking on the five collected.<br>   This God is wroth offended by the sinner that wrongs the<br>   Brāhman who hath gained this knowledge.<br>   Agitate him, O Rohita; destroy him: entangle in thy snares the<br>   Brāhman's tyrant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300326">26</a></font></span>Born is the darksome Mother's Son, the whitely shining Calf of<br>   Night.<br>   He, Rohita, ascendeth up to heaven, hath mounted to the<br>    heights.</p>
