 <p><a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="green">p. 103</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V</h3>  <h4 align="center">On the duty of giving cows to Brāhmans, and the sin and danger of withholding the gift</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200501">1</a></font></span>Created by toil and holy fervour, found by devotion, resting in<br>    right;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200502">2</a></font></span>Invested with truth, surrounded with honour, compassed about<br>    with glory;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200503">3</a></font></span>Girt round with inherent power, fortified with faith, protected,<br>    by consecration, installed at sacrifice, the world her resting-<br>    place;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200504">4</a></font></span>Brahma her guide, the Brāhman her lord and ruler;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200505">5</a></font></span>Of the Kshatriya who taketh to himself this Brāhman's cow and<br>    oppresseth the Brāhman.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200506">6</a></font></span>The glory, the heroism, and the favouring fortune depart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200507">7</a></font></span>The energy and vigour, the power and might the speech and<br>    mental strength, the glory and duty;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200508">8</a></font></span>Devotion and princely sway, kingship and people, brilliance and<br>    honour, and splendour and wealth;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200509">9</a></font></span>Long life and goodly form, and name and fame, inbreathing and<br>    expiration, and sight, and hearing;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200510">10</a></font></span>Milk and flavour, and food and nourishment, and right and<br>    truth, and action and fulfilment, and children and cattle;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200511">11</a></font></span>All these blessings of a Kshatriya depart from him when he<br>    oppresseth the Brāhman and taketh to himself the hhman's<br>    cow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200512">12</a></font></span>Terrible is she this Brāhman's cow, and fearfully venomous,<br>    visibly witchcraft.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200513">13</a></font></span>In her are all horrors and all death.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200514">14</a></font></span>In her are all dreadful, deeds, all slaughters of mankind.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200515">15</a></font></span>This, the Brāhman's cow, being appropriated, holdeth bound in<br>    the fetter of Death the oppressor of the Brāhman, the blas-<br>    phemer of the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200516">16</a></font></span>A hundred-killing bolt is she: she slays the Brāhman's injurer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200517">17</a></font></span>Therefore the Brāhmans' cow is held inviolable by the wise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200518">18</a></font></span>Running she is a thunderbolt, when driven away she is Vaisvā-<br>    nara;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200519">19</a></font></span>An arrow when she draweth up her hooves, and Mahādeva when<br>    she looketh around;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200520">20</a></font></span>Sharp as a razor when she beholdeth, she thundereth when she<br>    belloweth. <a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="green">p. 104</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200521">21</a></font></span>Death is she when she loweth, and a fierce God when she whis-<br>    keth her tail;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200522">22</a></font></span>Utter destruction when she moveth her ears this way and that,<br>   Consumption when she droppeth water;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200523">23</a></font></span>A missile when milking, pain in the head when milked;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200524">24</a></font></span>The taking away of strength when she approacheth, a hand-to-<br>    hand fighter when roughly touched;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200525">25</a></font></span>Wounding like an arrow when she is fastened by her mouth,<br>    contention when she is beaten;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200526">26</a></font></span>Fearfully venomous when falling, darkness when she hath fallen<br>    down.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200527">27</a></font></span>Following him, the Brāhman's cow extinguisheth the vital breath<br>    of the injurer of the Brāhman.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200528">28</a></font></span>Hostility when being cut to pieces, woe to children when the<br>    portions are distributed,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200529">29</a></font></span>A destructive missile of Gods when she is being seized,<br>    misfortune when carried away;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200530">30</a></font></span>Misery while being additionally acquired, contumely and abuse<br>    while being put in the stall;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200531">31</a></font></span>Poison when in agitation, fever when seasoned with condi-<br>    ments;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200532">32</a></font></span>Sin while she is cooking, evil dream when she is cooked;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200533">33</a></font></span>Uprooting when she is being turned round, destruction when she<br>    hath been turned round;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200534">34</a></font></span>Discord by her smell, grief when she is being eviscerated: ser-<br>    pent with poison in its fang when drawn;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200535">35</a></font></span>Loss of power while sacrificially presented, humiliation when she<br>    hath been offered;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200536">36</a></font></span>Wrathful Sarva while being carved. Simidā when cut up:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200537">37</a></font></span>Poverty while she is being eaten. Destruction when eaten.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200538">38</a></font></span>The Brāhman's cow when eaten cuts off the injurer of Brāhmans<br>    both from this world and from the world yonder.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200539">39</a></font></span>Her slaughter is the sin of witchcraft, her cutting-up is a thunder-<br>    bolt, her undigested grass is a secret spell.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200540">40</a></font></span>Homelessness is she when denied her rights.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200541">41</a></font></span>Having become Flesh-eating Agni the Brāhman's cow entereth<br>    into and devoureth the oppressor of Brāhmans.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200542">42</a></font></span>She sunders all his members, joints, and roots.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200543">43</a></font></span>She cuts off relationship on the father's side and destroys mater-<br>    nal kinship. <a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="green">p. 105</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200544">44</a></font></span>The Brāhman's cow, not restored by a Kshatriya, ruins the<br>    marriages and all the kinsmen of the Brāhman's oppressor.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200545">45</a></font></span>She makes him houseless, homeless, childless: he is extinguished<br>    without posterity to succeed him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200500">0</a></font></span>46. So shall it be with the Kshatriya who takes to himself the cow<br>    of the Braman who hath this knowledge.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200547">47</a></font></span>Quickly, when he is smitten down by death, the clamorous vul-<br>    tures cry:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200548">48</a></font></span>Quickly around his funeral fire dance women with dishevelled<br>    locks,<br>   Striking the hand upon the breast and uttering their evil shriek.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200549">49</a></font></span>Quickly the wolves are howling in the habitation where he<br>    lived:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200550">50</a></font></span>Quickly they ask about him, What is this? What thing hath<br>    happened here?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200551">51</a></font></span>Rend, rend to pieces, rend away, destroy, destroy him utterly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200552">52</a></font></span>Destroy Angirasi! the wretch who robs and wrongs the Brah-<br>    mans, born.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200553">53</a></font></span>Of evil womb, thou witchcraft hid, for Vaisvadevi is thy name,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200554">54</a></font></span>Consuming, burning all things up, the thunderbolt of spell and<br>    charm.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200555">55</a></font></span>Go thou, becoming Mrityu sharp as razor's edge pursue thy<br>    course:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200556">56</a></font></span>Thou bearest off the tyrants' strength, their store of merit, and<br>    their prayers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200557">57</a></font></span>Bearing off wrong, thou givest in that world to him who hath<br>    been wronged.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200558">58</a></font></span>O Cow, become a tracker through the curse the Brāhman hath<br>    pronounced,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200559">59</a></font></span>Become a bolt, an arrow through his sin, be terribly venomous.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200560">60</a></font></span>O Cow, break thou the head of him who wrongs the Brāhmans,<br>    criminal, niggard, blasphemer of the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200561">61</a></font></span>Let Agni burn the spiteful wretch when crushed to death and<br>    slain by thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200562">62</a></font></span>Rend, rend to bits, rend through and through, scorch and con-<br>    sume and burn to dust,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200563">63</a></font></span>Consume thou, even from the root, the Brāhmans' tyrant, god-<br>    like Cow!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200564">64</a></font></span>That he may go from Yama's home afar into the worlds of sin.<br>    its <a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="green">p. 106</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200565">65</a></font></span>So, Goddess Cow, do thou from him, the Brāhmans' tyrant,<br>    criminal, niggard, blasphemer of the Gods,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200566">66</a></font></span>With hundred-knotted thunderbolt, sharpened and edged with<br>    razor-blades,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200567">67</a></font></span>Strike off the shoulders and the head.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200568">68</a></font></span>Snatch thou the hair from off his head, and from his body strip<br>    the skin:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200569">69</a></font></span>Tear out his sinews, cause his flesh to fall in pieces from his<br>    frame.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200570">70</a></font></span>Crush thou his bones together, strike and beat the marrow out<br>    of him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200571">71</a></font></span>Dislocate all his limbs and joints.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200572">72</a></font></span>From earth let the Carnivorous Agni drive him, let Vayu burn.<br>    him from mid-air's broad region.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200573">73</a></font></span>From heaven let Sūrya drive him and consume him.</p>
