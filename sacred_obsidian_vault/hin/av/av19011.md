 <p><a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="green">p. 222</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A continuation of Hymn 10</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901101">1</a></font></span>May the great Lords of Truth protect and aid us: blest to us be<br>    our horses and our cattle.<br>   Kind be the pious, skilful-handed Ribhus, kind be the Fathers<br>    at our invocations<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901102">2</a></font></span>Kind to us be the Gods and Visve Devas, Sarasvati with Holy<br>   Thoughts be gracious.<br>   Friendly be they, the Liberal Ones, who seek us, yea, those who<br>    dwell in-heaven, on earth, in waters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901103">3</a></font></span>May Aja-Ekapād the God be gracious, gracious the Dragon of<br>    the Deep, and Ocean.<br>   Gracious be he, the swelling Child of Waters, gracious be Prisni<br>    who hath Gods to guard her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901104">4</a></font></span>So may the Rudras, Vasus, and Ādityas accept the new hymn we<br>    now are making.<br>   May all the Holy Ones of earth and heaven, and the Cow's off-<br>    spring hear our invocation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901105">5</a></font></span>Priests of the Gods, worthy of sacrifices, immortal, knowing<br>   Law, whom man must worship.<br>   May these to-day give us broad paths to travel. Preserve us<br>    evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
