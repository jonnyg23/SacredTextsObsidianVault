 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007401">1</a></font></span>O Soma-drinker, ever true, utterly hopeless though we be,<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007402">2</a></font></span>O Lord of strength, whose jaws are strong, great deeds are thine,<br>    the powerful:<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007403">3</a></font></span>Lull thou asleep, to wake no more, the pair who on each other<br>    look:<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One. <a name="page_325"><font size="1" color="green">p. 325</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007404">4</a></font></span>Hero, let hostile spirits sleep, and every gentler Genius wake:<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007405">5</a></font></span>Destroy this ass, O Indra, who in tones discordant brays to<br>    thee:<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007406">6</a></font></span>Far distant on the forest fall the tempest in a circling course;<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us hope of beauteous horses and of kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007407">7</a></font></span>Slay each reviler and destroy him who in secret injures us:<br>   Do thou, O Indra, give us thope of beauteous horses and of<br>    kine,<br>   In thousands, O most wealthy One.</p>
