 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CIX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010901">1</a></font></span>The juice of Soma thus diffused, sweet to the taste, the bright<br>   Cows drink<br>   Who for the sake of splendour close to mighty Indra's side<br>    rejoice, good in their own supremacy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010902">2</a></font></span>Craving his touch the dappled Kine mingle the Soma with their<br>    milk. <a name="page_353"><font size="1" color="green">p. 353</font></a><br>   The milch-kine dear to Indra send forth his death-dealing thun-<br>    derbolt, good in their own supremacy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010903">3</a></font></span>With veneration, passing wise, honouring his victorious might,<br>   They follow close his many laws to win them due preeminence,<br>    good in their own supremacy.</p>
