 <p><a name="page_363"><font size="1" color="green">p. 363</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXVII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn in praise of the good Government of King Kaurama</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012701">1</a></font></span>Listen to this, ye men, a laud of glorious bounty shall be sung.<br>   Thousands sixty, and ninety we, O Kaurama, among the<br>   Rusamas have received.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012702">2</a></font></span>Camels twice-ten that draw the car, with females by their side,<br>    he gave.<br>   Fain would the chariot's top bow down escaping from the stroke<br>    of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012703">3</a></font></span>A hundred chains of gold, ten wreaths, upon thee Rishi he<br>    bestowed,<br>   And thrice-a-hundred mettled steeds, ten-times-a-thousand cows<br>    he gave.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012704">4</a></font></span>Glut thee, O Singer, glut thee like a bird on a ripe-fruited tree.<br>   Thy lips and tongue move swiftly like the sharp blades of a pair<br>    of shears.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012705">5</a></font></span>Quickly and willingly like kine forth come the singers and their<br>    hymns:<br>   Their little maidens are at home, at home they wait upon the<br>    cows.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012706">6</a></font></span>O Singer, bring thou forth the hymn that findeth cattle, findeth:<br>    wealth. <a name="page_364"><font size="1" color="green">p. 364</font></a><br>   Even as an archer aims his shaft address this prayer unto the<br>   Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012707">7</a></font></span>List to Parikshit's eulogy, the sovran whom all people love,<br>   The King who ruleth over all, excelling mortals as a God.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012708">8</a></font></span>'Mounting his throne, Parikshit, best of all, hath given us peace<br>    and rest,'<br>   Saith a Kauravya to his wife as he is ordering his house.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012709">9</a></font></span>'Which shall I set before thee, curds, gruel of milk, or barley-<br>    brew?'<br>   Thus the wife asks her husband in the realm which King<br>   Parikshit rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012710">10</a></font></span>Up as it were to heavenly light springs the ripe corn above the<br>    cleft.<br>   Happily thrive the people in the land where King Parikshit<br>    reigns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012711">11</a></font></span>Indra hath waked the bard and said, Rise, wander singing here<br>    and there.<br>   Praise me, the strong: each pious man will give thee riches in<br>    return,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012712">12</a></font></span>Here, cows! increase and multiply, here ye, O horses, here, O<br>    men.<br>   Here, with a thousand rich rewards, doth Pūshan also seat him-<br>    self.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012713">13</a></font></span>O Indra, let these cows be safe, their master free from injury.<br>   Let not the hostile-hearted or the robber have control of them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012714">14</a></font></span>Oft and again we glorify the hero with our hymn of praise, with<br>    prayer, with our auspicious prayer.<br>   Take pleasure in the songs we sing: let evil never fall on us.</p>
