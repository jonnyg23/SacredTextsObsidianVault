 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001801">1</a></font></span>This, even this, O Indra, we implore: as thy devoted friends,<br>   The Kanvas praise thee with their hymns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001802">2</a></font></span>Naught else, O Thunderer, have I praised in the skilled singer's<br>    eulogy:<br>   On thy laud only have I thought.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001803">3</a></font></span>The Gods seek him who presses out the Soma; they desire not<br>    sleep:<br>   Incessantly they punish sloth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001804">4</a></font></span>Faithful to thee we loudly sing, heroic Indra, songs to thee.<br>   Mark, gracious Lord, this act of ours.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001805">5</a></font></span>Give us not up to man's reproach, to foeman's hateful calumny:<br>   In thee alone is all my strength.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001806">6</a></font></span>Thou art mine ample coat of mail, my champion, Vritra-slayes,<br>    thou.<br>   With thee for Friend I brave the foe.</p>
