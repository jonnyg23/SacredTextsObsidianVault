 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCVIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009801">1</a></font></span>That we may win us wealth and spoil we poets verily call on<br>    thee.<br>   In war men call on thee, Indra, the hero's Lord, in the steed's,<br>    race-course call on thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009802">2</a></font></span>As such, O Wonderful whose hand holds thunder, praised as<br>    mighty, Caster of the Stone.<br>   Pour on us boldly, Indra, kie and chariot-steeds, ever to be the<br>    conqueror's strength.</p>
