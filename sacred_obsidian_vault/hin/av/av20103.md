 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010301">1</a></font></span>Solicit with your hymns, for aid, Agni the God with piercing<br>    flame,<br>   For riches famous Agni, Purmilha ‘and ye men, Agni to light<br>    our dwelling well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010302">2</a></font></span>Agni, come hither with thy fires: we choose thee as our Hotai-<br>    priest.<br>   Let the extended ladle full of oil balm thee, best priest, to sit on<br>    sacred grass.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010303">3</a></font></span>For unto thee, O Angiras, O Son of Strength, move ladles in the<br>    sacrifice,<br>   To Agni, Child of Force, whose locks drop oil, we seek, fore-<br>    most in sacrificial rites.</p>
