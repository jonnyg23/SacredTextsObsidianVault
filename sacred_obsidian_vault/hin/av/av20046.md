 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004601">1</a></font></span>Him who advances men to wealth, sends light to lead them in<br>    their wars,<br>   And quells their foemen in the fray:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004602">2</a></font></span>May he, the saviour much-invoked, may Indra bear us in a ship<br>   Safely beyond all enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004603">3</a></font></span>As such, O Indra, honour us with wealth and treasure: further<br>    us,<br>   And lead us to felicity.</p>
