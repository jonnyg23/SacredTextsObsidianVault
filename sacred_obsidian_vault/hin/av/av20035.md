 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003501">1</a></font></span>To him, to him swift, strong, and high-exalted, I bring my song<br>    of praise as dainty viands;<br>   My thought to him resistless, meet for praises, prayers offered<br>    most devotedly to Indra. <a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="green">p. 293</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003502">2</a></font></span>To him I offer praise as choice refreshment, bring forth my song,<br>    with seemly laud besiege him.<br>   For Indra, Lord of olden time, the singers shall deck their hymns<br>    with heart and mind and spirit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003503">3</a></font></span>To him then with my lips my song of praises, excellent, winning<br>    heavenly light, I offer,<br>   To magnify with hymns of invocation and eulogies the Lord,<br>    most bounteous Giver.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003504">4</a></font></span>Even for him I frame a laud—so fashions the wright a chariot<br>    for the man who needs it<br>   Songs for wise Indra hymned with invocation, a song composed<br>    with care and all-impelling.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003505">5</a></font></span>So with my tongue I deck, to please that Indra, my hymn as't-<br>    were a horse, through love of glory,<br>   To reverence the Hero, bounteous Giver, famed far and wide,<br>    destroyer of the castles.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003506">6</a></font></span>Even for him hath Tvashtar forged the thunder, most deftly<br>    wrought, celestial, for the battle.<br>   Wherewith he reached the vital parts of Vritra, striking—the<br>    vast, the mighty—with the striker,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003507">7</a></font></span>As soon as, at libations of his mother, great Vishnu had drunk<br>    up the draught, he plundered.<br>   The dainty cates, the cooked mess; but One stronger transfixed<br>    the wild boar, shooting through the mountain.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003508">8</a></font></span>To him, to Indra when he slew the Dragon, the Dames too,<br>   Consorts of the Gods, wove praises.<br>   The mighty heaven and earth hath he encompassed: thy great-<br>    ness heaven and earth, combined, exceed not.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003509">9</a></font></span>Yea, of a truth, his magnitude surpasseth the magnitude of<br>    earth, mid-air and heaven.<br>   Indra whom all men praise, the Sovran Ruler, waxed in his<br>    home loud-voiced and strong for battle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003510">10</a></font></span>Through his own strength with bolt of thunder Indra smote<br>    piece-meal Vritra, drier up of waters.<br>   He let the floods go free, like cows imprisoned, for glory, with<br>    a heart inclined to bounty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003511">11</a></font></span>Through his resplendent power still stood the rivers when with<br>    his bolt on every side he stayed them. <a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="green">p. 294</font></a><br>   With lordly might favouring him who worshipped, he made a<br>    ford, victorious, for Turviti.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003512">12</a></font></span>Vast, with thine ample power, with eager movement against this<br>   Vritra cast thy bolt of thunder.<br>   Rend thou his joints, as of an ox dissevered, with bolt oblique<br>    that floods of rain may follow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003513">13</a></font></span>Sing with new lauds his exploits wrought aforetime, the deeds<br>    of him, yea, him who moveth swiftly,<br>   When, hurling forth his weapons in the battle, he with impetuous<br>    wrath lays low the foemen.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003514">14</a></font></span>When he, yea, he is born the firm-set mountains and the whole<br>    heaven and earth tremble in terror.<br>   May Nodhas ever lauding the protection of this dear Friend win<br>    straightway strength heroic.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003515">15</a></font></span>Now unto him of these things hath been given what he, who<br>    rules alone o'er much, electeth.<br>   Indra helped Etasa, the Soma presser, contending in the chariot-<br>    race with Sūrya.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003516">16</a></font></span>Thus to thee, Indra, yoker of bay coursers, the Gotamas have<br>    brought their prayers and praises.<br>   Bestow upon them thought, decked with all beauty. May he,<br>    enriched with prayer, come soon and early.</p>
