 <p><a name="page_306"><font size="1" color="green">p. 306</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004901">1</a></font></span>When voices, fain to win mid-air, ascended to the Mighty One,<br>   The vigorous God was filled with joy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004902">2</a></font></span>Praise with the voice the Mighty, praise the awful with the voice:<br>    in heaven<br>   He, the most bounteous, hath been glad.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004903">3</a></font></span>Praise with the voice the Mighty: he rules in each realm. In<br>    transport he<br>   Hath set upon the sacred grass.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004904">4</a></font></span>As cows low to their calf in stalls, so with our songs we glorify<br>   This Indra, even your wondrous God who checks attack, who<br>    joys in the delightful juice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004905">5</a></font></span>Celestial, bounteous Giver, God, with power and might, rich,<br>    mountain-like, in precious things.<br>   Him soon we see for foodful booty rich in kine, brought<br>    hundredfold and thousandfold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004906">6</a></font></span>I crave of thee that hero strength—that thou mayst first regard<br>    this prayer<br>   Wherewith thou helpest Bhrigu and the Yatis and Praskanva<br>    when the prize was staked.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004907">7</a></font></span>Wherewith thou sentest mighty waters to the sea—Indra, that<br>    manly strength of thine.<br>   For ever unattainable is this power of him to whom the worlds<br>    have cried aloud.</p>
