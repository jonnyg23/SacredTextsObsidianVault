 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIX</h3>  <h4 align="center">A protective charm</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903901">1</a></font></span>Let Kushtha from the Hill of Snow come, a divine deliverer.<br>   Banish thou all Consumption, drive all sorceresses far away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903902">2</a></font></span>Kushtha, three several names hast thou, Naghamāra,<br>   Naghārisha: let not mishap befall this man,<br>   For whom I make a charm of thee at eve, at morning, and by<br>    day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903903">3</a></font></span>Jivalā is thy mother's name, thy father's name is Jivala; let not<br>    mishap, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903904">4</a></font></span>Thou art the best amid the plants, even as the ox is best of<br>    tame, the tiger of rapacious beasts: let not mishap, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903905">5</a></font></span>Born thrice from the Ādityas, thrice from Bhrigus, thrice from<br>   Angiras' sons, born from the Visve Devas thrice, <a name="page_243"><font size="1" color="green">p. 243</font></a><br>   Healer of every malady, that Kushtha stands by Soma's side.<br>   Banish thou all Consumption, drive all sorceresses far away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903906">6</a></font></span>In the third heaven above us stands the Asvattha tree, the seat<br>    of Gods:<br>   There is embodiment of life that dies not: thence was Kushtha<br>    born.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903907">7</a></font></span>There moved through heaven a golden ship, a ship with cordage<br>    wrought of gold:<br>   There is embodiment of life that dies not; thence was Kushtha<br>    born.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903908">8</a></font></span>Where is the Sinking of the Ship, the summit of the Hill of<br>   Snow,<br>   There is embodiment of life that dies not: thence was Kustha<br>    born.<br>   Healer of every malady, that Kushtha stands by Soma's side.<br>   Banish thou all Consumption, drive all sorceresses far away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903909">9</a></font></span>Thou whom Ikshvāku's ancestor, whom he who well-loved<br>   Kushtha, knew,<br>   Whom Vāyasa and Mātsya knew, hence healer of all ills art<br>    thou.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903910">10</a></font></span>O thou who hast all-reaching might drive away Fever, drive it<br>    down,<br>   <a href="errata.htm#1">Head</a> racking Fever, tertian, continual, lasting for a year.</p>
