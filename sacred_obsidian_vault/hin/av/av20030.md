 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003001">1</a></font></span>In the great synod will I laud thy two bay steeds: I prize the<br>    sweet strong drink of thee the Warrior-God,<br>   His who pours lovely oil as'twere with yellow drops. Let my<br>    songs enter thee whose form hath golden tints.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003002">2</a></font></span>Ye who—in concert sing unto the gold-hued place, like bay steeds<br>    driving onward to the heavenly seat,<br>   For Indra laud ye strength allied with tawny steeds, laud him<br>    whom cows content as'twere with yellow drops.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003003">3</a></font></span>His is that thunderbolt, of iron, golden-hued, gold-coloured,<br>    very dear, and yellow in his arms;<br>   Bright with strong teeth, destroying with its tawny rage. In<br>   Indra are set fast all forms of golden hue.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003004">4</a></font></span>As if a lovely ray were laid upon the sky, the golden thunder-<br>    bolt spread out as in a race.<br>   That iron bolt with yellow jaw smote Ahi down. A thousand<br>    flames had he who bore the tawny-hued. <a name="page_288"><font size="1" color="green">p. 288</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003005">5</a></font></span>Thou, thou, when praised by men who sacrificed of old, hadst<br>    pleasure in their lauds, O Indra golden-haired.<br>   All that befits thy song of praise thou welcomest, the perfect:<br>    pleasant gift, O golden-hued from birth.</p>
