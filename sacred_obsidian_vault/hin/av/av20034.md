 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003401">1</a></font></span>He who just born, chief God of lofty spirit, by power and might<br>    became the God's protector,<br>   Before whose breath, through greatness of his valour, the two<br>    worlds trembled, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003402">2</a></font></span>He who fixed fast and firm the earth that staggered, and set at<br>    rest the agitated mountains,<br>   Who measured out air's wider middle region and gave the<br>    heaven support, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003403">3</a></font></span>Who slew the Dragon, freed the Seven Rivers, and draye the<br>    kine forth from the cave of Vala,<br>   Begat the fire between both stones, the spoiler in warrior's<br>    battle, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003404">4</a></font></span>By whom this universe was made to tremble, who chased away<br>    the humbled brood of demon,<br>   Who, like a gambler gathering his winnings, seized the foe's<br>    riches, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003405">5</a></font></span>Of whom, the terrible, they ask, Where is He? or verily they<br>    say of him, He is not.<br>   He wastes the foeman's wealth like stakes of gamblers. Have<br>    faith in him for He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003406">6</a></font></span>Stirrer to action of the poor and lowly, of priest, of suppliant<br>    who sings his praises<br>   Who, fair-faced, favours him who presseth Soma with stones<br>    adjusted, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003407">7</a></font></span>He under whose supreme control are horses, all chariots, and<br>    the hamlets, and the cattle:<br>   He who begat the Sun, begat the Morning, leader of waters.<br>   He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003408">8</a></font></span>To whom both armies cry in close encounter, foe against foe, the<br>    stronger and the weaker; <a name="page_291"><font size="1" color="green">p. 291</font></a><br>   Whom two invoke upon one chariot mounted, each for himself,<br>   He, O ye men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003409">9</a></font></span>He, without whom men conquer not in battle, whom, warring,<br>    they invoke for help and succour;<br>   He, all this universe's type and image, who shakes what never<br>    shook, He, men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003410">10</a></font></span>He who hath smitten, ere they know their danger, with his hurl-<br>    ed weapon many grievous sinners:<br>   Who pardons not his boldness who provokes him, who slays the<br>   Dasyu, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003411">11</a></font></span>He who discovered in the fortieth autumn Sambara dwelling in<br>    the midst of mountains:<br>   Who slew the Dragon putting forth his vigour, the demon lying<br>    there, He, men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003412">12</a></font></span>Who drank the juice poured at the seas of Order, subduing<br>   Sambara by superior prowess,<br>   Who hoarded food within the mountain's hollow wherein he<br>    grew in strength, He, men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003413">13</a></font></span>Who, with seven guiding reins, the Bull, the mighty, set the<br>   Seven Rivers free to flow at pleasure;<br>   Who, thunder-armed, rent Rauhina in pieces when scaling<br>    heaven, He, O ye men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003414">14</a></font></span>Heaven, even, and the earth bow down before him, before his<br>    very breath the mountains tremble.<br>   Known as the Soma-drinker, armed with thunder, the wielder<br>    of the bolt, He, men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003415">15</a></font></span>Who aids with favour him who pours the Soma, and him who<br>    brews it, sacrificer, singer;<br>   Whose strength our prayer and offered Soma heighten, and this<br>    our gift, He, O ye men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003416">16</a></font></span>Born, manifested in his Parents' bosom, He knoweth as a son<br>    the Highest Father.<br>   He who with vigorous energy assisted the companies of Gods,<br>   He, men, is Indra<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003417">17</a></font></span>Lord of Bay steeds, who loves the flowing Soma, He before<br>    whom all living creatures tremble.<br>   He who smote Sambara and slaughtered Sushna, He the Sole<br>   Hero, He, O men, is Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003418">18</a></font></span>Thou verily art true strong God who sendest wealth to the man<br>    who brews and pours libation. <a name="page_292"><font size="1" color="green">p. 292</font></a><br>   So may we evermore, thy friends, O Indra, address the synod<br>    with brave sons about us.</p>
