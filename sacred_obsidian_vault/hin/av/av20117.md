 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011701">1</a></font></span>Drink Soma, Lord of Bays, and let it cheer thee: Indra, the<br>    stone, like a well-guided courser,<br>   Directed by the presser's arms hath pressed it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011702">2</a></font></span>So let the draught of joy, thy dear companion, by which, O Lord<br>    of Bays, thou slayest foemen,<br>   Delight thee, Indra, Lord of princely treasures.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011703">3</a></font></span>Mark closely, Maghavan; the words I utter, this eulogy recited<br>    by Vasishtha:<br>   Accept the prayers I offer at thy banquet.</p>
