 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000601">1</a></font></span>Thee, Indra, we invoke, the Bull, what time the Soma is ex-<br>    pressed.<br>   So drink thou of the savoury juice. <a name="page_268"><font size="1" color="green">p. 268</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000602">2</a></font></span>Indra, whom many laud, accept the strength-confering Soma<br>    juice.<br>   Quaff, pour down drink that satisfies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000603">3</a></font></span>Indra, with all the Gods promote our wealth-bestowing sacrifice,<br>   Thou highly-lauded Lord of men.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000604">4</a></font></span>Lord of the brave, to thee proceed these drops of Soma juice<br>    expressed,<br>   The bright drops to thy dwelling-place.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000605">5</a></font></span>Within thy belly, Indra take Soma the juice most excellent:<br>   The heavenly drops belong to thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000606">6</a></font></span>Drink our libation, Lord of hymns: with streams of meath thou<br>    art bedewed:<br>   Our glory, Indra, is thy gift.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000607">7</a></font></span>To Indra go the treasures of the worshipper which never fail:<br>   He drinks the Soma and is strong.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000608">8</a></font></span>From far away, from near at hand, O Vritra-slayer, come to us:<br>   Accept the songs we sing to thee.</p>
