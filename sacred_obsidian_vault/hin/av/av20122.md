 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012201">1</a></font></span>With Indra splendid feasts be ours enriched with ample spoil,<br>    wherewith,<br>   Wealthy in food, we may rejoice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012202">2</a></font></span>Like thee, thyself, the singers' friend, thou movest as it were,<br>    besought,<br>   Bold One, the axle of the car.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012203">3</a></font></span>That, Satakratu, thou to grace and please thy praisers, as it<br>    were,<br>   Stirrest the axle with thy strength.</p>
