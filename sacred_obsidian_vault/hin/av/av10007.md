 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VII</h3>  <h4 align="center">Skambha, the Pillar or Fulcrum of all existence</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000701">1</a></font></span>Which of his members is the seat of Fervour: Which is the base<br>    of Ceremonial Order? <a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="green">p. 21</font></a><br>   Where in him standeth Faith? Where Holy Duty? Where, in<br>    what part of him is truth implanted?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000702">2</a></font></span>Out of which member glows the light of Agni? Form which<br>    proceeds the breath of Mātarisvan?<br>   From which doth Chandra measure out his journey, travelling<br>    over Skambha's mighty body?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000703">3</a></font></span>Which of his members is the earth's upholder? Which gives the<br>    middle air a base to rest on?<br>   Where, in which member is the sky established? Where hath<br>    the space above the sky its dwelling?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000704">4</a></font></span>Whitherward yearning blazeth Agni upward? Whitherward<br>    yearning bloweth Mātarisvan?<br>   Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha to whom with long-<br>    ing go the turning pathways?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000705">5</a></font></span>Whitheward go the half-months, and, accordant with the full<br>    year, the months in their procession?<br>   Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha to whom go seasons<br>    and the groups of seasons?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000706">6</a></font></span>Whitherward yearning speed the two young Damsels, accordant,<br>   Day and Night, of different colour?<br>   Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha to whom the Waters<br>    take their way with longing?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000707">7</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha,<br>   On whom Prajāpati set up and firmly stablished all the worlds?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000708">8</a></font></span>That universe which Prajāpati created, wearing all forms,, the<br>    highest, midmost, lowest,<br>   How far did Skambha penetrate within it? What portion did<br>    he leave unpenetrated?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000709">9</a></font></span>How far within the past hath Skambha entered? How much of<br>    him hath reached into the future?<br>   That one part which he set in thousand places,—how far did<br>   Skambha penetrate within it?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000710">10</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha in whom men<br>    recognize the Waters, Brahma,<br>   In whom they know the worlds and their enclosures, in whom<br>    are non-existence and existence?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000711">11</a></font></span>Declare that. Skambha, who is he of many,<br>   In whom, exerting every power, Fervour maintains her loftiest<br>    vow; <a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="green">p. 22</font></a><br>   In whom are comprehended Law, Waters, Devotion and Belief<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000712">12</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha<br>   On whom as their foundation earth and firmament and sky are<br>    set;<br>   In whom as their appointed place rest Fire and Moon and Sun<br>    and Wind?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000713">13</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha<br>   He in whose body are contained all three-and-thirty Deities?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000714">14</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha.<br>   In whom the Sages earliest born, the Richas, Sāman, Yajus,<br>   Earth, and the one highest Sage abide?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000715">15</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is the Skambha.<br>   Who comprehendeth, for mankind, both immortality and death,<br>   He who containeth for mankind the gathered waters as his<br>    veins?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000716">16</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha,<br>   He whose chief arteries stand there, the sky's four regions, he irk<br>    whom Sacrifice putteth forth its might?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000717">17</a></font></span>They who in Purusha understand Brahma know Him who is.<br>   Supreme.<br>   He who knows Him who is Supreme, and he who knows the<br>   Lord of Life,<br>   These know the loftiest Power Divine, and thence know Skam-<br>    bha thoroughly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000718">18</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha<br>   Of whom Vaisvānara became the head, the Angirases his eye,<br>    and Yātus his corporeal parts?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000719">19</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha<br>   Whose mouth they say is Holy Lore, his tongue the Honey-<br>    sweetened Whip, his udder is Virāj, they say?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000720">20</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha<br>   From whom they hewed the lichas off, from whom they<br>    chipped the Yajus, he<br>   Whose hairs are Sāma-verses and his mouth the Atharvāngi-<br>    rases?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000721">21</a></font></span>Men count as 'twere a thing supreme nonentity's conspicuous<br>    branch;<br>   And lower man who serve thy branch regard it as an entity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000722">22</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha <a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="green">p. 23</font></a><br>   In whom Ādityas dwell, in whom Rudras and Vasus are<br>    contained,<br>   In whom the future and the past and all the worlds are firmly<br>    set;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000723">23</a></font></span>Whose secret treasure evermore the three-and thirty Gods<br>    protect?<br>   Who knoweth now the treasure which, O Deities ye watch and<br>    guard?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000724">24</a></font></span>Where the Gods, versed in Sacred Lore, worship the loftiest<br>   Power Divine<br>   The priest who knows them face to face may be a sage who<br>    knows the truth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000725">25</a></font></span>Great, verily, are those Gods who sprang from non-existence<br>    into life.<br>   Further, men say that that one part of Skambha is nonentity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000726">26</a></font></span>Where Skambha generating gave the Ancient World its shape<br>    and form,<br>   They recognized that single part of Skambha as the Ancient<br>   World,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000727">27</a></font></span>The three-and-thirty Gods within his body were disposed as<br>    limbs:<br>   Some, deeply versed in Holy Lore, some know those three-and-<br>    thirty Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000728">28</a></font></span>Men know Hiranyagarbha as supreme and inexpressible:<br>   In the beginning, in the midst of the world, Skambha poured<br>    that gold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000729">29</a></font></span>On Skambha Fervour rests, the worlds and Holy Law repose on<br>    him.<br>   Skambha, I clearly know that all of thee on Indra is imposed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000730">30</a></font></span>On Indra Fervour rests, on him the worlds and Holy Law<br>    recline.<br>   Indra, I clearly know that all of thee on Skambha findeth rest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000731">31</a></font></span>Ere sun and dawn man calls and calls one Deity by the other's<br>    name.<br>   When the Unborn first sprang into existence he reached that<br>    independent sovran lordship; than which aught higher never<br>    hath arisen.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000732">32</a></font></span>Be reverence paid to him, that highest Brahma, whose base is<br>   Earth, his belly Air, who made the sky to be his head. <a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="green">p. 24</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000733">33</a></font></span>Homage to highest Brahma, him whose eye is Sūrya and the<br>   Moon who groweth young and new again, him who made<br>   Agni for his mouth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000734">34</a></font></span>Homage to highest Brahma, him whose two life-breathings were<br>    the Wind,<br>   The Angirases his sight: who made the regions be his means of<br>    sense.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000735">35</a></font></span>Skambha set fast these two, the earth and heaven, Skambha<br>    maintained the ample air between them.<br>   Skambha established the six spacious regions: this whole world<br>   Skambha entered and pervaded.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000736">36</a></font></span>Homage to highest Brahma, him who, sprung from Fervour and<br>    from toil,<br>   Filled all the worlds completely, who made Soma for himself<br>    alone.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000737">37</a></font></span>Why doth the Wind move ceaselessly? Why doth the spirit take<br>    no rest?<br>   Why do the Waters, seeking truth, never at any time repose?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000738">38</a></font></span>Absorbed in Fervour, is the mighty Being, in the world's centre,<br>    on the waters' surface.<br>   To him the Deities, one and all betake them. So stand the tree-<br>    trunk with the branches round it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000739">39</a></font></span>Who out of many, tell me, is that Skambha.<br>   To whom the Deities with hands, with feet, and voice, and ear,<br>    and eye.<br>   Present unmeasured tribute in the measured hall of sacrifice?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000740">40</a></font></span>Darkness is chased away from him: he is exempt from all dist-<br>    ress.<br>   In him are all the lights, the three abiding in Prajāpati.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000741">41</a></font></span>He verily who knows the Reed of Gold that stands amid the<br>    flood, is the mysterious Lord of Life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000742">42</a></font></span>Singly the two young Maids of different colours approach the<br>    six-pegged warp in turns and weave it.<br>   The one draws out the threads, the other lays them: they break<br>    them not, they reach no end of labour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000743">43</a></font></span>Of these two, dancing round as 'twere, I cannot distinguish<br>    whether ranks before the other.<br>   A Male in weaves this web, a Male divides it: a Male hath<br>    stretched it to the cope of heaven <a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="green">p. 25</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000744">44</a></font></span>These pegs have buttressed up the sky. The Sāmans have turned<br>    them into shuttles for the weaving.</p>
