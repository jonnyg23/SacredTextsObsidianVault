 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007701">1</a></font></span>Impetuous, true, let Maghavan come hither, and let his tawny<br>    coursers speed to reach us.<br>   For him have we pressed juice exceeding potent: here, praised<br>    with song, let him effect his visit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007702">2</a></font></span>Unyoke, as at thy journey's end, O Hero, to gladden thee to-day<br>    at this libation.<br>   Like Usanā, the priest a laud shall utter, a hymn to thee, the<br>   Lord Divine, who markest. <a name="page_328"><font size="1" color="green">p. 328</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007703">3</a></font></span>When the Bull quaffing praises our libation, as a sage paying<br>    holy rites in secret,<br>   Seven singers here from heaven hath he begotten, who e'en by<br>    day have wrought their works while singing.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007704">4</a></font></span>When heaven's fair light by hymns was made apparent. (they<br>    made great splendour shine at break of morning),<br>   He with his succour, best of heroes, scattered the blinding dark-<br>    ness so that men saw clearly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007705">5</a></font></span>Indra, impetuous One, hath waxed immensely: he with his<br>    vastness hath filled earth and heaven.<br>   E'en beyond this his majesty extendeth who hath exceeded all<br>    the worlds in greatness,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007706">6</a></font></span>Sakra who knoweth well all human actions hath with his eager<br>    friends let loose the waters.<br>   They with their songs cleft e'en the mountain open, and willingly<br>    disclosed the stall of cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007707">7</a></font></span>He smote away the flood's obstructer Vritra: Earth conscious<br>    lent her aid to speed thy thunder.<br>   Thou sentest forth the waters of the ocean as Lord through<br>    power and might, O daring Hero.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007708">8</a></font></span>When, Much-invoked! the waters' rock thou deftest, Saramā<br>    showed herself and went before thee.<br>   Hymned by Angirases, bursting the cowstalls, thou foundest<br>    ample strength for us as leader.</p>
