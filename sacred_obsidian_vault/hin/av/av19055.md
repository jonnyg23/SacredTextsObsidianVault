 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Agni for protection and prosperity</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905501">1</a></font></span>Bringing, as 'twere, with care unceasing fodder night after night<br>    to feed this stabled Courser,<br>   Joying in food and in the growth of riches, may we thy neigh-<br>    bours, Agni, ne'er be injured.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905502">2</a></font></span>Here is thine own desire for wealth: through this be gracious<br>    unto us.<br>   Joying in food and in the growth of riches, may we thy neigh-<br>    bours, Agni, ne'er be injured.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905503">3</a></font></span>Each eve that comes our household's Lord is Agni, showing his.<br>    loving-kindness every morning.<br>   Bestow upon us treasure after treasure: enkindling thee may we<br>    increase thy body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905504">4</a></font></span>Each morn that comes our household's Lord is Agni, showing.<br>    his loving-kindness every evening.<br>   Vouchsafe us treasure after treasure: kindling thee may we<br>    prosper through a hundred winters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905505">5</a></font></span>Never may I come short of food to feed me.<br>   Glory to Agni, Rudra, the consumer and the Lord of food!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905506">6</a></font></span>Protect my company, protect its courteous members, courteous.<br>   God! <a name="page_257"><font size="1" color="green">p. 257</font></a><br>   Only through thee, O much-invoked, may I be ever rich in kine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905507">7</a></font></span>Only to thee bringing our tribute, Agni, each day as fodder to a<br>    stabled courser,<br>   Joying in food and in the growth of riches, may we the neigh-<br>    bours, Agni ne'er be injured.</p>
