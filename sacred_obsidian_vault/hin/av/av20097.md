 <p><a name="page_346"><font size="1" color="green">p. 346</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009701">1</a></font></span>Here verily yesterday we let the Thunder-wielder drink his fill.<br>   So in like manner, offer him the juice to day. Now range you.<br>    by the Glorious One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009702">2</a></font></span>Even the wolf, the savage beast that rends the sheep, follows the<br>    path of his decrees.<br>   So, India, graciously accepting this our praise, with wondrous.<br>    thought come forth to us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009703">3</a></font></span>What manly deed of vigour now remains that Indra hath not<br>    done?<br>   Who hath not heard his glorious title and his fame, the Vritra-<br>    slayer from his birth?</p>
