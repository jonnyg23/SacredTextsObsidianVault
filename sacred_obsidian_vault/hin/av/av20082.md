 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008201">1</a></font></span>If I, O Indra, were the lord of riches ample as thine own,<br>   I should support the singer, God who scatterest wealth! and!<br>    not abandon him to woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008202">2</a></font></span>Each day would I enrich the man who sang my praise, in what-<br>    soever place he were.<br>   No kinship is there better, Maghavan, than thine: a father even)<br>    is no more.</p>
