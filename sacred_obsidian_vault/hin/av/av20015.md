 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001501">1</a></font></span>To him most liberal, lofty Lord of lofty wealth, verily powerful<br>    and strong, I bring my hymn,<br>   Whose checkless bounty, as of waters down a slope, is spread<br>    abroad for all that live, to give them strength.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001502">2</a></font></span>Now all this world, for worship, shall come after thee—the<br>    offerer's libations like descending floods,<br>   When the well-loved one seems to rest upon the hill, the thun-<br>    derbolt of Indra, shatterer wrought of gold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001503">3</a></font></span>To him the terrible, most worthy of high praise, like radiant<br>   Dawn, bring gifts with reverence in this rite,<br>   Whose being, for renown, yea, Indra-power and light, have been<br>    created, like bay steeds, to move with speed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001504">4</a></font></span>Thine, Indra, praised by many excellently rich! are we who<br>    trusting in thy help draw near to thee.<br>   Lover of praise, none else but thou receives our laud: as Earth<br>    loves all her creatures, love thou this our hymn.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001505">5</a></font></span>Great is thy power, O Indra, we are thine. Fulfil, O Maghavan,<br>    the wish of this thy worshipper.<br>   After thee lofty heaven hath measured out its strength to thee<br>    and to thy power this earth hath bowed itself.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001506">6</a></font></span>Thou, who hast thunder for thy weapon, with thy bolt hast<br>    shattered into pieces this broad massive cloud.<br>   Thou hast sent down obstructed floods that they may flow: thou<br>    hast, thine own for ever, all victorious might.</p>
