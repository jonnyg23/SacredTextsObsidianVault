 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010401">1</a></font></span>May these my songs of praise exalt thee, Lord who hast abun-<br>    dant wealth.<br>   Men skilled in holy hymns, bright with the hues of fire, have<br>    sung them with their lauds to thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010402">2</a></font></span>He with his might enhanced by Rishis thousand-fold, hath like<br>    an ocean spread himself. <a name="page_349"><font size="1" color="green">p. 349</font></a><br>   His majesty is praised as true at solemn rites, his power where<br>    holy singers rule<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010403">3</a></font></span>May Indra, who in every fight must be invoked, be near to us.<br>   May the most mighty Vritra-slayer, meet for praise, come to<br>    libations and to hymns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010404">4</a></font></span>Thou art the best of all in sending bounteous gifts, true art thou,.<br>    lordly in thine act.<br>   We claim alliance with the very Glorious One, yea, with the-<br>    mighty Son of Strength.</p>
