 <p><a name="page_356"><font size="1" color="green">p. 356</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXVIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011801">1</a></font></span>Indra with all thy saving helps give us assistance, Lord of Power<br>   For after thee we follow even as glorious bliss, thee, Hero,<br>    finder-out of wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011802">2</a></font></span>Increaser of our steeds and multiplying kine, a golden well, O<br>   God, art thou;<br>   For no one may impair the gifts laid up in thee, Bring me what-<br>    ever thing I ask.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011803">3</a></font></span>Indra for worship of the Gods, Indra while sacrifice proceeds,<br>   Indra as warriors in the battle-shock we call, Indra that we may<br>    win the spoil.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011804">4</a></font></span>With might hath Indra spread out heaven and earth, with power<br>    hath Indra lighted up the Sun.<br>   In Indra are all creatures closely held; in him meet the distilling<br>   Soma drops.</p>
