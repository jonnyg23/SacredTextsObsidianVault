 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="green">p. 71</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN X</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101001">1</a></font></span>Rise up, with all your banners rise; prepare your strength, ye<br>    vapoury Forms!<br>   Serpents and fiends and Other Folk, charge and pursue our<br>    enemies!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101002">2</a></font></span>Let those who bear an evil name, in air, in heaven on earth, and<br>    men,<br>   After Trishandhi's will, revere your power, the sway that Know-<br>    ledge gives, together with your ruddy flags.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101003">3</a></font></span>Let those with iron faces, with faces like needles or like combs,<br>   Flesh-eaters, rapid as the wind, cling closely to our foemen with<br>   Trishandhi for their thunderbolt.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101004">4</a></font></span>Omniscient Āditya, make full many a corpse to disappear.<br>   Let this devoted army of Trishandhi be in my control.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101005">5</a></font></span>Rise up, O Godlike Being, rise, Arbudi, with thine army: this,<br>   Tribute is sacrificed to thee, Trishandhi's welcome offering<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101006">6</a></font></span>May this four-footed White-foot, may this arrow brace and bind<br>    thee fast:<br>   Together with Trishandhi's host, be thou, O Witchcraft, meant<br>    for foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101007">7</a></font></span>Down let the dim-eyed demon fly, and let her shorteared sister<br>    shriek:<br>   Red be the banners when the host of Arbudi hath won the day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101008">8</a></font></span>Let all the birds that move on wings come downward, all fowls<br>    that roam the heavens and air's midregion. <a name="page_72"><font size="1" color="green">p. 72</font></a><br>   Let beasts of prey and flies attacks, and vultures that eat raw<br>    flesh mangle and gnaw the carcase.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101009">9</a></font></span>By that same binding treaty which thou madest, Brihaspati!<br>    with Indra! and with Brahma,<br>   By Indra's pledge I bid the Gods come hither. Conquer on this<br>    side, not on their side yonder.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101010">10</a></font></span>Brihaspati Angirasa, Rishis made strong and keen by prayer,<br>   Have set Trishandhi in the heaven, dire weapon that destroys<br>    the fiends.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101011">11</a></font></span>The Gods enjoyed Trishandhi for the sake of energy and power,<br>   Him under whose protection, both, Indra and yon Āditya stand.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101012">12</a></font></span>The Gods, victorious, won themselves all worlds by this oblation,<br>    which<br>   Brihaspati Angirasa effused, a very thunderbolt, a weapon to<br>    destory the friends.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101013">13</a></font></span>That fiend-destroying weapon which Brihaspati Angirasa poured<br>    out and made a thunderbolt.<br>   Even therewith, Brihaspati, I brush that hostile armament, and<br>    strike the foemen down with might.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101014">14</a></font></span>Over to us come all the Gods who eat the hallowed sacrifice<br>   With this oblation be ye pleased: conquer on this side, not on<br>    that.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101015">15</a></font></span>Over,to us let all Gods come: dear is Trishandhi's offering.<br>   Keen the great pledge through which, of old, the Asuras were<br>    overthrown.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101016">16</a></font></span>Let Vāyu bend the arrow-points of those who are our enemies.<br>   Let Indra break their arms away: no power to lay the shaft be<br>    theirs!<br>   Āditya utterly destroy their missile! Chandramās bar the path<br>    of him who lingers!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101017">17</a></font></span>If they have issued forth strongholds of Gods, and made their<br>    shields of prayer,<br>   Gaining protection for their lives, protection round about, make<br>    all their instigation powerless<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101018">18</a></font></span>With the Flesh-eater and with Death, following the Purohita,<br>   On! forward with Trishandhi's host! conquering enemies<br>    advance!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101019">19</a></font></span>Do thou, Trishandhi, with the gloom of darkness compass round<br>    our foes; <a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="green">p. 73</font></a><br>   Let none escape of them expelled with speckled butter mixt with<br>    curds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101020">20</a></font></span>Let White-foot fall upon those wings of our opponents' arma-<br>    ment;<br>   Mazed and bewildered be those hands of foes this day, O Nyar-<br>    budi.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101021">21</a></font></span>Mazed are the foemen, Nyarbudi! Slay thou each bravest man<br>    of them: with this our army slaughter them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101022">22</a></font></span>Low lie the warrior, mailed, unmailed, each foeman in the rush<br>    of war.<br>   Down-smitten with the strings of bows, the fastenings of mail,<br>    the charge!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101023">23</a></font></span>The armour-clad, the armourless, enemies clothed with coats of<br>    mail,<br>   All these struck down, O Arbudi, let dogs devour upon the<br>    earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101024">24</a></font></span>Car-borne and carless fighting men, riders and those who go<br>    on foot,<br>   All these, struck down, let vultures, kites, and all, the birds of<br>    air devour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101025">25</a></font></span>Low let the hostile army lie, thousands of corpses, on the<br>    ground,<br>   Pierced through and rent to pieces where the deadly weapons<br>    clash in fight.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101026">26</a></font></span>With eagles let them eat the evil-hearted, pierced in the vitals,<br>    lying crushed and howling.<br>   The foe whoe'er will fight against this our protecting sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1101027">27</a></font></span>With this which Gods attend, with this which never fails to gain<br>    its end,<br>   Let Indra, Vritra-slayer, smite, and with Trishandhi as a bolt.</p>
