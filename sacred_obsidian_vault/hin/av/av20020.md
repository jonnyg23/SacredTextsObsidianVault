 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002001">1</a></font></span>Drink thou the Soma for our help, bright, vigilant, exceeding<br>    strong,<br>   O Indra, Lord of Hundred Powers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002002">2</a></font></span>O Satakratu, powers which thou mid the Five Races hast dis-<br>    played<br>   These, Indra, do I claim of thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002003">3</a></font></span>Indra, great glory hast thou gained. Win splendid fame which<br>    none may mar:<br>   We make thy might perpetual.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002004">4</a></font></span>Come to us either from anear, or, Sakra, come from far away. <a name="page_280"><font size="1" color="green">p. 280</font></a><br>   Indra, wherever be thy home, come to us thence, O Thunder-<br>    armed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002005">5</a></font></span>Verily Indra, conquering all, driveth even mighty fear away;<br>   For firm is he and swift to act.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002006">6</a></font></span>Indra be gracious unto us: sin shall not reach us afterward .<br>   And good shall be before us still,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002007">7</a></font></span>From all the regions of the world let Indra send security.<br>   The foe-subduer, swift to act.</p>
