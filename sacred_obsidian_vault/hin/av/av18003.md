 <p><a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="green">p. 191</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN III</h3>  <h4 align="center">A funeral hymn, taken partly from the Rigveda</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800301">1</a></font></span>Choosing her husband's world, O man, this woman lays herself<br>    down beside thy lifeless body. <a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="green">p. 192</font></a><br>   Preserving faithfully the ancient custom. Bestow upon here both<br>    wealth and offspring.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800302">2</a></font></span>Rise, come unto the world of life, O woman: come, he is lifeless<br>    by whose side thou liest.<br>   Wifehood with this thy husband was thy portion who took thy<br>    hand and wooed thee as a lover.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800303">3</a></font></span>I looked and saw the youthful dame escorted, the living to the<br>    dead: I saw them, bear her.<br>   When she with blinding darkness was enveloped, then did I turn<br>    her back and lead her homeward.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800304">4</a></font></span>Knowing the world of living beings, Aghnyā! treading the path.<br>    of Gods which lies before thee,<br>   This is thy husband: joyfully receive him and let him mount<br>    into the world of Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800305">5</a></font></span>The speed of rivers craving heaven and cane, thou, Agni, art the<br>    waters' gall.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800306">6</a></font></span>Cool, Agni, and again refresh the spot which thou hast scorched<br>    and burnt.<br>   Here let the water-lily grow, and tender grass and leafy plant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800307">7</a></font></span>Here is one light for thee, another yonder: enter the third and<br>    be therewith united.<br>   Uniting with a body be thou lovely, dear to the Gods in their<br>    sublimest mansion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800308">8</a></font></span>Rise up, advance, run forward: make thy dwelling in water<br>    that shall be thy place to rest in<br>   There dwelling in accordance with the Fathers delight thyself<br>    with Soma and libations.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800309">9</a></font></span>Prepare thy body: speed thou on thy journey: let not thy limbs,.<br>    thy frame be left behind thee.<br>   Follow to its repose thy resting spirit: go to whatever spot of<br>    earth thou lovest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800310">10</a></font></span>With splendour may the Fathers, meet for Soma, with mead and.<br>    fatness may the Gods anoint me.<br>   Lead me on farther to extended vision, and prosper me through<br>    life of long duration.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800311">11</a></font></span>May Agni balm me thoroughly with splendour; may Vishnu.<br>    touch my lips with understanding.<br>   May all the Deities vouchsafe me riches, and pleasant Waters<br>    purify and cleanse me. <a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="green">p. 193</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800312">12</a></font></span>Mitra and Varuna have stood about me. Ādityas, Sacirifical<br>   Posts exalt me!<br>   May Indra balm my hands with strength and splendour. A long,<br>    long life may Savitar vouchsafe me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800313">13</a></font></span>Worship with sacrificial gift King Yama, Vivasvān's son who<br>    gathers men together,<br>   Yama who was the first to die of mortals, the first who travelled<br>    to the world before us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800314">14</a></font></span>Depart, O Fathers, and again come hither; this sacrifice of yours<br>    is balmed with sweetness.<br>   Enrich us here with gift of great possessions; grant blessed<br>    wealth with ample store of heroes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800315">15</a></font></span>Kanva, Kakshivān, Purumidha, Agastya, Syāvāsva Sobhari, and<br>   Archanānas,<br>   This Visvāmitra, Jamadagni, Atri, Kasyapa, Vāmadeva be our<br>    helpers!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800316">16</a></font></span>Vasishtha, Jamadagni, Visvāmitra, Gotama, Vāmadeva, Bhara-<br>    dvaja!<br>   Atri hath won your favour with homage. Gracious to us be ye<br>    praiseworthy Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800317">17</a></font></span>They, making for themselves a new existence, wash off defilement<br>    in the brazen vessel.<br>   May we be fragrant in our houses, ever increasing in our<br>    children and our riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800318">18</a></font></span>They balm him, balm him over, balm him thoroughly, caress<br>    the mighty power and balm it with the mead,<br>   They seize the flying steer at the stream's breathing-place:<br>    cleansing with gold they grasp the animal herein.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800319">19</a></font></span>Fathers, be glorious in yourselves, and follow all that is glad in<br>    you and meet for Soma.<br>   Give ear and listen, swiftly-moving Sages, benevolent, invoked in<br>    our assembly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800320">20</a></font></span>Atris, Angirases, Navagvas, givers of liberal gifts, continual<br>    sacrificers,<br>   Devout and pious, granting guerdon freely, sit on this holy grass<br>    and be ye joyful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800321">21</a></font></span>As in the days of old our ancient Fathers, speeding the work of<br>    sacred worship, Agni!<br>   Sought pure light and devotion, singing praises, they cleft the<br>    ground and made red Dawns apparent. <a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="green">p. 194</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800322">22</a></font></span>Gods, doing holy acts, devout, resplendent, smelting like ore<br>    their human generation,<br>   Brightening Agni and exalting Indra, they came encompassing<br>    the stall of cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800323">23</a></font></span>Strong One! he marked them, and the gods before them, like<br>    herds of cattle in a foodful pasture.<br>   There man moaned forth their strong desires, to strengthen even<br>    the true; the nearest One, the living.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800324">24</a></font></span>We have worked for thee, we have toiled and laboured: bright<br>   Dawns have shed their light upon our worship.<br>   All that the Gods regard with love is blessed. Loud may we<br>    speak, with heroes, in assembly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800325">25</a></font></span>From eastward Indra, Lord or Maruts, guard me, as in her<br>    arms Earth guards the heaven above us!<br>   Those who give room, who made the paths, we worship, you,<br>    mid the Gods, who share the gifts we offer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800326">26</a></font></span>Dhātar with Nirriti save me from southward, etc. (as in stanza<br>   25).<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800327">27</a></font></span>From westward Aditi was Ādityas save me! etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800328">28</a></font></span>From westward with the All-Gods save me Soma! etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800329">29</a></font></span>May the strong firm Sustainer bear thee upright, as Savitar bears<br>    light above the heaven.<br>   Those who give room, who made the paths, we worship, you<br>    mid the Gods, whe share the gifts we offer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800330">30</a></font></span>Toward the eastward region I supply thee before thou goest<br>    homeward, with oblation, as in her arms, etc. (as in stanza 25).<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800331">31</a></font></span>Toward the southern region, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800332">32</a></font></span>Toward the western region, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800333">33</a></font></span>Toward the northern region, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800334">34</a></font></span>Toward the stedfast region, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800335">35</a></font></span>Toward the upmost region I supply thee, before thou goest<br>    homeward, with oblation, as in her arms Earth bears the<br>    heaven above us.<br>   Those who give room, who made the paths, we worship, you,<br>    mid the Gods, who share the gifts we offer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800336">36</a></font></span>Thou art the Bull, Supporter, and Upholder,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800337">37</a></font></span>Who purifiest wind and mead and water.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800338">38</a></font></span>From this side and from that let both assist me. As, speeding,<br>    ye have come like two twin sisters, <a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="green">p. 195</font></a><br>   Religious-hearted votaries brought you forward. Knowing your<br>    several places be ye seated.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800339">39</a></font></span>Sit near, sit very near beside our Soma: for you I fit the ancient<br>    prayer with homage.<br>   The praise-song, like a chieftain on his pathway, spreads far and<br>    wide. Let all Immortals hear it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800340">40</a></font></span>Three paces hath the stake gone up, and followed her, the four-<br>    footed, with devout observance.<br>   He with the Syllable copies the praise-song; he thoroughly<br>    purifies at Order's centre.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800341">41</a></font></span>Chose he then, death for Gods to be their portion? Why chose<br>    he not for men a life eternal?<br>   Brihaspati span sacrifice, the Rishi; and Yama yielded up his<br>    own dear body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800342">42</a></font></span>Thou, Agni Jātavedas, when entreated, didst bear our offerings,<br>    having made them fragrant.<br>   And give them to the Fathers who consumed them with Svadhā.<br>   Eat, thou God, the gifts we bring thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800343">43</a></font></span>Lapped in the bosom of the purple Mornings, give riches to the<br>    man who brings oblations.<br>   Grant to your sons a portion of that treasure, and, present, give<br>    them energy, O Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800344">44</a></font></span>Fathers whom Agni's flames have tasted, come ye nigh: in per-<br>    fect order take ye each your proper place.<br>   Eat sacrificial food presented on the grass: grant riches with a<br>    multitude of hero sons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800345">45</a></font></span>May they, the Fathers who deserve the Soma, invited to their<br>    favourite oblations.<br>   Laid on the sacred grass, come nigh and listen. May they be<br>    gracious unto us and bless us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800346">46</a></font></span>Our Father's Fathers and their sires before them who came,<br>    most noble, to the Soma banquet,<br>   With these let Yama, yearning with the yearning, rejoicing eat<br>    our offerings at his pleasure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800347">47</a></font></span>Come to us, Agni, with the gracious Fathers who dwell in glow-<br>    ing light, the very Sages,<br>   Who thirsted mid the Gods, who hasten hither, oblation-winners,<br>    theme of singers' praises.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800348">48</a></font></span>Come, Agni, come with countless ancient Fathers, dwellers in<br>    light, primeval, God-adorers, <a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="green">p. 196</font></a><br>   Eaters and drinkers of oblation, truthful, who travel with the<br>   Deities and Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800349">49</a></font></span>Betake thee to the lap of Earth, our mother, of Earth far-spread-<br>    ing, very kind and gracious.<br>   May she, wool-soft unto the guerdon-giver, guard thee in front<br>    upon the distant pathway.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800350">50</a></font></span>Heave thyself, Earth, nor press him downward heavily: afford<br>    him easy access pleasant to approach,<br>   Cover him as a mother wraps her skirt about her child, O<br>   Earth!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800351">51</a></font></span>Now let the heaving earth be free from motion: yea, let a<br>    thousand clods remain above him.<br>   Be they to him a home distilling fatness: here let them ever be<br>    his place of refuge.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800352">52</a></font></span>I stay the earth from thee, while over thee I place this piece of<br>    earth. May I be free from injury.<br>   The Fathers firmly fix this pillar here for thee; and there let<br>   Yama make thee an abiding-place.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800353">53</a></font></span>Forbear, O Agni, to upset this chalice: the Gods and they who<br>    merit Soma love it.<br>   This cup, yea this which serves the Gods to drink from,—in this<br>    let the Immortals take their pleasure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800354">54</a></font></span>The chalice brimming o'er which erst Atharvan offered to Indra,<br>   Lord of wealth and treasure,<br>   Indu therein sets draught of virtuous action, and ever purifies<br>    himself within it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800355">55</a></font></span>What wound soe'er the dark bird hath inflicted, the emmet, or<br>    the serpent, or the jackal,<br>   May Agni who devoureth all things heal it, and Soma, who hath<br>    passed into the Brāhmans.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800356">56</a></font></span>The plants of earth are rich in milk, and rich in milk is this my<br>    milk.<br>   With all the milky essence of the Waters let them make me<br>    clean.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800357">57</a></font></span>Let these unwidowed dames with goodly husbands adorn them-<br>    selves with fragrant balm and unguent.<br>   Decked with fair jewels, tearless, free, from trouble, first let the<br>    dames go up to where he lieth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800358">58</a></font></span>Meet Yama, meet the Fathers, meet the merit of virtuous action<br>    in the loftiest heaven. <a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="green">p. 197</font></a><br>   Leave sin and evil, seek anew thy dwelling: so bright with glory<br>    let him join his body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800359">59</a></font></span>Our Father's Fathers and their sires before them, they who have<br>    entered into air's wide region,<br>   For them shall self-resplendent Asuniti form bodies now accord-<br>    ing to her pleasure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800360">60</a></font></span>Let the hoar-frost be sweet to thee. sweetly on thee the rain<br>    descend!<br>   O full of coolness, thou cool Plant, full of fresh moisture, fresh-<br>    ening Herb,<br>   Bless us in waters, female Frog: calm and allay this Agni here.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800361">61</a></font></span>Vivasvān make us free from fear and peril, good rescuer, quick-<br>    pouring, bounteous giver!<br>   Many in number be these present heroes! Increase of wealth be<br>    mine in kine and horses!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800362">62</a></font></span>In immortality Vivasvān set us! Go from us Death, come to us<br>    life eternal!<br>   To good old age may he protect these people: let not their<br>    spirits pass away to Yama.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800363">63</a></font></span>The Sage of Fathers, guardian of devotions who holds thee up<br>    with might in air's mid-region,—<br>   Praise him ye Visvāmitras, with oblation. To lengthened life<br>    shall be, this Yama, lead us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800364">64</a></font></span>Mount and ascend to highest heaven, O Rishis: be ye not afraid.<br>   Soma-drinkers to you is paid this Soma-lover's sacrifice. We<br>    have attained the loftiest light.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800365">65</a></font></span>Agni is shining forth with lofty banner: the Bull is bellowing to<br>    earth and heaven.<br>   From the sky's limit even hath he stretched near us: the Steer<br>    hath waxen in the waters' bosom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800366">66</a></font></span>They gaze on thee with longing in their spirit, as on an eagle<br>    that is mounting skyward;<br>   On thee with wings of gold, Varuna's envoy, the Bird that<br>    hasteth to the home of Yama.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800367">67</a></font></span>O Indra, bring us wisdom as a sire gives wisdom to his sons.<br>   Guide us, O much-invoked in this our way: may we still living<br>    look upon the Sun.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800368">68</a></font></span>Let these which Gods have held for thee, the beakers covered<br>    o'er with cake,<br>   Be full of sacred food for thee, distilling fatness, rich in mead. <a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="green">p. 198</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800369">69</a></font></span>Grains which for thee I scatter, mixt with Sesamum, as holy<br>    food,<br>   May they for thee be excellent and potent: King Yama look on<br>    them as thine with favour!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800370">70</a></font></span>O Tree, give back again this man who is deposited on thee.<br>   That he may dwell in Yama's home addressing the assemblies<br>    there.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800371">71</a></font></span>Seize hold O Jātavedas; let thy flame be full of fervent heat.<br>   Consume his body: to the world of pious ones transport this<br>    man.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800372">72</a></font></span>To these, thy Fathers who have passed away at first and after-<br>    ward,<br>   Let the full brook of butter run, o'erflowing with a hundred<br>    streams.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800373">73</a></font></span>Mount to this life, removing all defilement: here thine own<br>    kindred shine with lofty splendour.<br>   Depart thou; be not left behind: go forward, first of those here,<br>    unto the world of Fathers.</p>
