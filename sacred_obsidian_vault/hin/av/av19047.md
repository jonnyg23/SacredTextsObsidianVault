 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Night for protection from fiends, robbers, snakes and wolves</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904701">1</a></font></span>Night! the terrestrial realm hath been filled with the Father's<br>    power and might.<br>   Thou spreadest forth on high unto the seats of Heaven: dark-<br>    ness that strikes with awe comes near.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904702">2</a></font></span>Each moving thing finds rest in her whose yonder boundary is<br>    not seen, nor that which keeps her separate.<br>   O spacious, darksome Night, may we uninjured reach the end of<br>    thee, reach, O thou blessed One, thine end.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904703">3</a></font></span>Thy ninety-nine examiners, O Night, who look upon mankind,<br>   Eighty-and-eight in number, or seven-and-seventy are they.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904704">4</a></font></span>Sixty-and-six, O opulent, fifty-and-five, O happy One,<br>   Forty-and-four and thirty-three are they, O thou enriched with<br>    spoil.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904705">5</a></font></span>Twenty-and-two hast thou, O Night, eleven, yea, and fewer still.<br>   With these protectors guard us well. O Daughter of the Sky, to-<br>    day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904706">6</a></font></span>Let not a fiends or spiteful man, let no ill-wisher master us.<br>   Let not the robber seize our cows, nor the wolf take our sheep<br>    today.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904707">7</a></font></span>Let not the thief, O Blessed, seize our horses, nor she-fiends our<br>    men.<br>   Let thief and robber run away on pathways most remote from<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904708">8</a></font></span>Far from us let Rope with Fangs, far from us let the wicked<br>    flee.<br>   Do thou make blind and headless. Night, the serpent with his<br>    pungent breath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904709">9</a></font></span>Crush the wolf's jaws in pieces, strike the robber dead against a<br>    post. <a name="page_250"><font size="1" color="green">p. 250</font></a><br>   In thee, O Night, do we abide: we here will sleep. Be watchful<br>    thou.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904710">10</a></font></span>Give thou protection to our kine; and to our horses, and our<br>    men.</p>
