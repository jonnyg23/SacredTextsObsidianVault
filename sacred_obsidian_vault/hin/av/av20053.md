 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005301">1</a></font></span>Who knows what vital power he wins, drinking beside the<br>    flowing juice?<br>   This is the fair-cheeked God who, joying in the draught, breaks<br>    down the castles in his strength.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005302">2</a></font></span>As a wild elephant rushes on, this way and that way, mad with<br>    heat.<br>   None may restrain thee; yet come hither to the draught: thou<br>    movest mighty in thy power.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005303">3</a></font></span>When he, the mighty, ne'er o'erthrown, stedfast, made ready for<br>    the fight.<br>   When Indra, Bounteous Lord, lists to his praiser's call, he will<br>    not stand aloof, but come.</p>
