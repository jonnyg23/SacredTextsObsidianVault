 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">On the origin of some Gods and the creation of man</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100801">1</a></font></span>When Manyu brought his consort home forth from Sankalpa's<br>    dwelling-place,<br>   Who were the wooers of the bride, who was the chief who<br>    courted her?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100802">2</a></font></span>Fervour and Action were the two, in depths of the great billowy<br>    sea?<br>   These were the wooers of the bride; Brahma the chief who<br>    courted her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100803">3</a></font></span>Ten Gods before the Gods were born together in the ancient<br>    time.<br>   Whoso may know them face to face may now pronounce the<br>    mighty word.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100804">4</a></font></span>Inbreath and outbreath, eye and ear, decay and freedom from.<br>    decay, <a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="green">p. 65</font></a><br>   Spiration upward and diffused, voice, mind have brought us<br>    wish and plan.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100805">5</a></font></span>As yet the Seasons were unborn, and Dilator and Prajāpati,<br>   Both Asvins, Indra, Agni. Whom then did they worship as<br>    supreme?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100806">6</a></font></span>Fervour and Action were the two, in depths of the great billowy<br>    sea;<br>   Fervour sprang up from Action: this they served and worship-<br>    ped as supreme.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100807">7</a></font></span>He may account himself well versed in ancient time who knows<br>    by name.<br>   The earth that was before this earth, which only wisest Sages<br>    know.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100808">8</a></font></span>From whom did Indra spring? from whom sprang Soma?<br>    whence was Agni born?<br>   From whom did Tvashtar spring to life? and whence is Dilator's<br>    origin?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100809">9</a></font></span>Indra from Indra, Soma from Soma, Agni from Agni sprang<br>   Tvashtar from Tvashtar was produced, Dilator was Dhātar's<br>    origin.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100810">10</a></font></span>Those Gods who were of old, the Ten begotten earlier than the<br>   Gods,<br>   What world do they inhabit since they gave the world unto<br>    their sons?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100811">11</a></font></span>When he had brought together hair, sinew and bone, marrow<br>    and flesh.<br>   And to the body added feet, then to what world did he depart?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100812">12</a></font></span>Whence, from what region did he bring the hair, the sinews, and<br>    the bones,<br>   Marrow and limbs, and joints, and flesh? Who was the bringer,<br>    and from whence?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100813">13</a></font></span>Casters, those Gods were called who brought together all the<br>    elements:<br>   When they had fused the mortal man complete, they entered<br>    into him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100814">14</a></font></span>The thighs, the knee-bones, and the feet, the head, the face,<br>   Land both the hands,<br>   The ribs, the nipples, and the sides—what I ishi hath constructed<br>    that? <a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="green">p. 66</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100815">15</a></font></span>Head, both the hands, and face, and tongue, and neck, and inter-<br>    coastal parts,<br>   All this, investing it with skins, Mahi conjoined with bond and<br>    tie.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100816">16</a></font></span>What time the might body lay firmly compact with tie and bond,<br>   Who gave its colour to the form, the hue wherewith it shines<br>    to-day?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100817">17</a></font></span>All Deities had lent their aid: of this a noble Dame took note,<br>   Tsā, the Consort of Command. She gave its colour to the form.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100818">18</a></font></span>When Tvashtar, Tvashtar's loftier Sire, had bored it out and<br>    hollowed it.<br>   Gods made the mortal their abode, and entered and possessed<br>    the man.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100819">19</a></font></span>Sleep, specially, Sloth, Nirriti, and deities whose name is Sin,<br>   Baldness, old age, and hoary hairs within the body found their<br>    way.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100820">20</a></font></span>Theft, evil-doing, and deceit, truth, sacrifice, exalted fame,<br>   Strength, princely power, and energy entered the body as a<br>    home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100821">21</a></font></span>Prosperity and poverty, kindnesses and malignities,<br>   Hunger and thirst of every kind entered the body as a home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100822">22</a></font></span>Reproaches, freedom from reproach, all blamable, all blameless<br>    deeds,<br>   Bounty, belief, and unbelief entered the body as a home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100823">23</a></font></span>All knowledge and all ignorance, each other thing that one may<br>    learn,<br>   Entered the body, prayer, and hymns, and songs, and sacrificial<br>    texts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100824">24</a></font></span>Enjoyments, pleasures, and delights, gladness, and rapturous<br>    ecstasies.<br>   Laughter and merriment and dance entered the body as a home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100825">25</a></font></span>Discourse and conversation, and the shrill-resounding cries of<br>    woe,<br>   All entered in, the motives and the purposes combined there-<br>    with.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100826">26</a></font></span>Inbreath and outbreath, ear and eye, decay and freedom from<br>    decay.<br>   Breath upward and diffused, voice, mind, these quickly with the<br>    body move, <a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100827">27</a></font></span>All earnest wishes, all commands, directions, and admonish-<br>    ments.<br>   Reflections, all deliberate plans entered the body as a home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100828">28</a></font></span>They laid in the abhorrent frame those waters hidden, bright,<br>    and thick,<br>   Which in the bowels spring from blood, from mourning or from<br>    hasty toil.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100829">29</a></font></span>Fuel they turned to bone, and then they set light waters in the<br>    frame.<br>   The molten butter they made seed: then the Gods entered into<br>    man.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100830">30</a></font></span>All Waters, all the Deities. Virāj with Brahma at her side:<br>   Brahma into the body passed: Prajāpati is Lord thereof.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100831">31</a></font></span>The Sun and Wind formed, separate, the eye and vital breath of<br>    man.<br>   His other person have the Gods bestowed on Agni as a gift.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100832">32</a></font></span>Therefore whoever knoweth man regardeth him as Brāhman's<br>    self:<br>   For all the Deities abide in him as cattle in their pen.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100833">33</a></font></span>At his first death he goeth hence, asunder, in three separate<br>    parts.<br>   He goeth yonder with one part, with one he goeth yonder:<br>    here he sinketh downward with a third.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100834">34</a></font></span>In the primeval waters cold the body is deposited.<br>   In this there is the power of growth: from this is power of<br>    growth declared.</p>
