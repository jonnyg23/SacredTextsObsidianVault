 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001401">1</a></font></span>We call on thee, O peerless One. We, seeking help, possessing<br>    nothing firm ourselves,<br>   Call on thee wonderful in fight.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001402">2</a></font></span>On thee for aid in sacrifice. This youth of ours, the bold, the<br>    mighty, hath gone forth.<br>   We, therefore, we thy friends, Indra, have chosen thee, free-<br>    giver, as our guardian God.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001403">3</a></font></span>Him who of old hath brought to us this and that blessing, him<br>   I magnify for you,<br>   Even Indra, O my friends, for help:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001404">4</a></font></span>Borne by bay steeds, the Lord of heroes, ruling men, for it is he<br>    who takes delight. <a name="page_274"><font size="1" color="green">p. 274</font></a><br>   The Bounteous Lord bestows on us his worshippers hundreds of<br>    cattle and of steeds.</p>
