 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005601">1</a></font></span>Indra, foe-slayer, hath been raised to joy and power by the men.<br>   Him, verily, we invocate in battles whether great or small: be<br>    he our aid in fights for spoil.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005602">2</a></font></span>For, Hero, thou art like a host, art giver of abundant prey.<br>   Strengthening even the feeble, thou aidest the sacrificer, thou<br>    givest the worshipper ample wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005603">3</a></font></span>When war and battles are on foot, booty is laid before the bold. <a name="page_310"><font size="1" color="green">p. 310</font></a><br>   Yoke thou thy wildly rushing Bays. Whom wilt thou slay and<br>    whom enrich? Do thou, O Indra, make us rich,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005604">4</a></font></span>He, righteous-hearted, at each time of rapture gives us herds of<br>    kine.<br>   Gather in both thy hands for us treasures of many hundred<br>    sorts. Sharpen thou us, and bring us wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005605">5</a></font></span>Refresh thee, Hero, with the juice outpoured for bounty and<br>    for strength.<br>   We know thee Lord of ample store, to thee have sent our heart's<br>    desires: be therefore our protector thou.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005606">6</a></font></span>These people, Indra, keep for thee all that is worthy of thy<br>    choice.<br>   Discover thou, as Lord, the wealth of men who offer up no<br>    gifts: bring thou to us this wealth of theirs.</p>
