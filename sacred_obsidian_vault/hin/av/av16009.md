 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IX</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm to secure wealth and felicity</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600901">1</a></font></span>Ours is superior place and ours is conquest: may I in all fights<br>    tread down spite and malice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600902">2</a></font></span>This word hath Agni, this hath Soma spoken. May Pūshan set<br>    me in the world of virtue.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600903">3</a></font></span>We have come to the light of heaven; to the light of heaven<br>    have we come: we have united with the light of Surya.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600904">4</a></font></span>Sacrifice is fraught with wealth for the increase of prosperity. I<br>    would win riches; fain would I be wealthy. Do thou bestow<br>    wealth upon me.</p>
