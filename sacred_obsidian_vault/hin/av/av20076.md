 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007601">1</a></font></span>As sits the young bird on the tree rejoicing, ye, swift pair, have<br>    been roused by clear laudation,<br>   Whose Hoter-priest through many days is Indra, earth's<br>    guardian, friend of men, the best of heroes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007602">2</a></font></span>May we, when this Dawn and the next dance hither, be thy best<br>    servants, most heroic Hero!<br>   Let the victorious car with triple splendour bring hitherward the<br>    hundred chiefs with Kutsa.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007603">3</a></font></span>What was the gladdening draught that pleased thee, Indra?<br>   Speed to our doors, our songs, for thou art mighty.<br>   Why comest thou to me, what gift attracts thee?<br>   Fain would I bring thee food most meet to offer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007604">4</a></font></span>Indra, what fame hath one like thee mid heroes? With what<br>    plan wilt thou act? Why hast thou sought us?<br>   As a true friend, Wide-Strider! to sustain us, since food absorbs<br>    the thought of each among us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007605">5</a></font></span>Speed happily those, as Sūrya ends his journey, who meet his<br>    wish as bridegrooms meet their spouses;<br>   Men who support, O Indra strong by nature, with food the<br>    many songs that tell thy praises. <a name="page_327"><font size="1" color="green">p. 327</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007606">6</a></font></span>Thine are two measures, Indra, wide, well-meted, heaven for thy<br>    majesty, earth for thy wisdom.<br>   Here for thy choice are Somas mixed with butter: may the<br>    sweet meath be pleasant for thy drinking.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007607">7</a></font></span>They have poured out a bowl to him, to Indra, full of sweet<br>    juice, for faithful is his bounty.<br>   O'er earth's expanse hath he grown great by wisdom, the friend<br>    of man, and by heroic exploits.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007608">8</a></font></span>Indra hath conquered in his wars the mighty: men strive in<br>    multitudes to win his friendship.<br>   Ascend thy chariot as it were in battle, which thou shalt drive<br>    to us with gracious favour,</p>
