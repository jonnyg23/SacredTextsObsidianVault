 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm to overthrow a rival and gain strength, dignity, long life, children, and general prosperity</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000501">1</a></font></span>Ye are the power of Indra, ye the force and strength of Indra,<br>    ye his hero might and manliness.<br>   I join you with the bonds of Prayer to the victorious enterprise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000506">6</a></font></span>For the victorious enterprise let all creation stand by me. For<br>    me ye, Waters, are prepared. <a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="green">p. 15</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000507">7</a></font></span>Ye are the share of Agni. Grant, O heavenly Waters unto us the<br>   Waters' brilliant energy.<br>   By statute of Prajāpati I set you down for this our world.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000515">15</a></font></span>Waters, your ceremonial share of Waters which the waters hold,<br>    which aids our sacrifice to Gods,<br>   This as a remnant here I leave. Do not thou wash it clean away.<br>   With this we let the man go by who hates us and whom we<br>    abhor.<br>   Him would I fain o'erthrow and slay with this our ceremonial<br>    act, with this our prayer, our thunder-bolt.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000522">22</a></font></span>Whatever evil I have done within this last triennium,<br>   From all that woe and misery let the waters shield and guard<br>    me well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000523">23</a></font></span>Onward I urge your gathered floods: enter your own abiding-<br>    place,<br>   Uninjured and with all your strength. Let nothing bend or bow<br>    us down.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000524">24</a></font></span>May the pure Waters cleanse us from defilement,<br>   Fair to behold remove our sin and trouble, and bear away ill-<br>    dream and all pollution.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000525">25</a></font></span>Thou art the step of Vishnu, rival-slayer, sharpened by earth,<br>    aglow with fire of Agni,<br>   Earth have I ranged: from earth we bar him who hates us and<br>    whom we hate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000526">26</a></font></span>Ours is superior place and ours is conquest: may I in all fights<br>    tread down spite and malice.<br>   Let him not live, let vital breath desert him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000536">36</a></font></span>With this I here invest the power and splendour, the life of that<br>    man and his vital breathing, the son of such a sire and such a<br>    woman, here do I overthrow and cast him downward.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000537">37</a></font></span>I follow Sūrya's course in heaven, the course that takes him to<br>    the South.<br>   May that bestow upon me wealth and glory of a Brāhman's<br>    rank.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000538">38</a></font></span>I turn me to the regions bright with splendour.<br>   May they bestow upon me wealth and glory of a Brāhman's<br>    rank.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000539">39</a></font></span>I turn me to the Rishis Seven. May they, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000540">40</a></font></span>I turn me unto Prayer. May that, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000541">41</a></font></span>I turn me unto Brāhmans. May they etc. <a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="green">p. 16</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000542">42</a></font></span>We hunt that man, we beat him down and slay him with our<br>    murderous blows.<br>   We with the spell have hurried him to Parameshthin's opened<br>    jaws.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000543">43</a></font></span>Let the shot missile catch him with Vaisvānara's two mighty<br>    fangs.<br>   This offering, and the mightiest Goddess, the Fuel, eat him up!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000544">44</a></font></span>Thou art the bound of Varuna the King.<br>   Bind, such an one, the son of such a woman, in vital breath and<br>    in the food that feeds him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000545">45</a></font></span>All food of thine, O Lord of Life, that lies, upon the face of<br>    earth,<br>   Thereof bestow thou upon us. O Lord of Life, Prajāpati!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000546">46</a></font></span>Celestial Waters have I sought: with juice have I besprinkled<br>    them.<br>   With milk, O Agni, have I come; bestow upon me splendid<br>    strength.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000547">47</a></font></span>Give me the boon of splendid strength; give, Agni! progeny<br>    and life.<br>   May the Gods know this prayer of mine, may Indra with the<br>   Rishis know.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000548">48</a></font></span>What curse soever couples launch against us, whatever bitter<br>    speech the chatterers utter,<br>   With Manyu's arrow, offspring of the spirit, transfix thou to the<br>    heart the Yātudhānas,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000549">49</a></font></span>Destroy the Yātudhānas with thy fervour, consume the demons<br>    with thy wrath, O Agni.<br>   Destroy the fool's gods with thy fiery splendour, destroy the<br>    blazing ones, the insatiable.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000550">50</a></font></span>Well-skilled, against this man I hurl the Water's bolt with four<br>    spikes, to cleave his head asunder.<br>   May it destroy all members of his body. Let the whole host of<br>   Gods approve my purpose.</p>
