 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm to secure long life and dominion to a prince</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903701">1</a></font></span>To me hath come this word given by Agni, fame, force and<br>    might, and strength, and life, and lustre.<br>   May Agni too bestow on me three-times a hundred manly<br>    powers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903702">2</a></font></span>For mighty strength, for action, I receive thee, for manly power,<br>    to last a hundred autumns. <a name="page_242"><font size="1" color="green">p. 242</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903703">3</a></font></span>For conquering strength and energy and vigour<br>   I fasten thee for chieftainship, for bearing royal dominion<br>    through a hundred autumns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903704">4</a></font></span>With Seasons and with Season-groups, for vigour and extended<br>    life.<br>   With splendour of the perfect year we fasten thee about the<br>    neck.</p>
