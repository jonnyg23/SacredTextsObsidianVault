 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003701">1</a></font></span>He, like a bull with sharpened horns, terrific, singly excites and<br>    agitates all the people.<br>   Then givest him who largely pours libation his wealth who pours<br>    not, for his own possession.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003702">2</a></font></span>Thou verily, Indra, gavest help to Kutsa, willingly lending ear<br>    to him in battle.<br>   When, aiding Arjunneya, thou subduedst to him both Kuyava and<br>    the Dāsa Sushna.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003703">3</a></font></span>O Bold One, thou with all thine aids hast boldly helped Sudās<br>    whose offerings were accepted,<br>   Pūru in winning land and slaying foemen, and Trasadasyu son<br>    of Purukutsa.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003704">4</a></font></span>At the Gods' banquet, Hero-souled! with heroes, Lord of Bay<br>   Steeds, thou slewest many Vritras.<br>   Thou sentest in swift death to sleep the Dasyu, both Chumuri<br>    and Dhuni, for Dabhiti,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003705">5</a></font></span>These were thy mighty powers that, Thunder-wielder! then<br>    swiftly crushedst nine-and ninety castles. <a name="page_298"><font size="1" color="green">p. 298</font></a><br>   Thou capturedst the hundredth in thine onslaught; thou slewest<br>   Namuchi, thou slewest Vritra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003706">6</a></font></span>Old are the blessings, Indra, which thou gavest Sudās the wor-<br>    shipperwho brought oblations.<br>   For thee, the strong I yoke thy strong bay horses: let them,<br>    approach our prayers and wealth, Most Mighty!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003707">7</a></font></span>Give us not up, Lord of Bay Horses, victor, in this our time of-<br>    trouble, to the wicked.<br>   Deliver us with true and faithful succour: dear may we be to<br>    thee among the princes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003708">8</a></font></span>May we men, Bounteous Lord, the friends thou lovest, near i<br>    thee be joyful under thy protection.<br>   Fain to fulfil the wish of Atithigva, bow Turvasa, bow down,.<br>    the son of Yadu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003709">9</a></font></span>Swiftly, in truth, O Bounteous Lord, about thee men skilled im<br>    hymning sing their songs and praises.<br>   Elect us shares of their love and friendship who by their calls on,<br>    thee despoiled the niggards.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003710">10</a></font></span>Thine are these Lauds, O manliest of heroes, Lauds which revert<br>    to us and give us riches.<br>   Favour these, Indra, when they strike the foemen, as Friend and<br>   Hero and the heroes' helper.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003711">11</a></font></span>Now, lauded for thine aid, heroic Indra, sped by our prayer,,<br>    wax mighty in thy body.<br>   To us apportion wealth and habitations. Ye Gods, protect us-<br>    evermore with blessings.</p>
