 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XII</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn of prayer and praise to Prithivī or deified Earth</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200101">1</a></font></span>Truth, high and potent Law, the Consecrating Rite, Fervour,<br>   Brahma, and Sacrifice uphold the Earth.<br>   May she, the Queen of all that is and is to be, may Prithivī<br>    make ample space and room for us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200102">2</a></font></span>Not over awded by the crowd of Manu's sons, she who hath<br>    many heights and floods and level plains;<br>   She who bears plants endowed with many varied powers, may<br>   Prithivī for us spread wide and favour us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200103">3</a></font></span>In whom the sea, and Sindhu, and the waters, in whom our food<br>    and corn-lands had their being,<br>   In whom this all that breathes and moves is active, this Earth.<br>    assign us foremost rank and station!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200104">4</a></font></span>She who is Lady of the earth's four regions, in whom our food<br>    and corn-lands had their being,<br>   Nurse in each place of breathing, moving creatures, this Earth.<br>    vouchsafe us kine with milk that fails not!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200105">5</a></font></span>On whom the men of old before us battled, on whom the Gods<br>    attacked the hostile demons,<br>   The varied home of bird, and kine and horses, this Prithivī<br>    vouchsafe us luck and splendour!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200106">6</a></font></span>Firm standing-place, all-bearing, store of treasures, gold-breasted,<br>    harbourer of all that moveth.<br>   May Earth who bears Agni Vaisvānara, Consort of mighty<br>    Indra, give us great possessions<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200107">7</a></font></span>May Earth, may Prithivī, always protected with ceaseless care by<br>    Gods who never slumber,<br>   May she pour out for us delicious nectar, may she bedew us<br>    with a flood of splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200108">8</a></font></span>She who at first was water in the ocean, whom with their wond-<br>    rous powers the sages followed,<br>   May she whose heart is in the highest heaven, compassed about<br>    wit h truth, and everlasting, <a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a><br>   May she, this Earth, bestow upon us lustre, and grant us power<br>    in loftiest dominion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200109">9</a></font></span>On whom the running universal waters flow day and night with<br>    never-ceasing motion,<br>   May she with many streams pour milk to feed us, may she<br>    bedew us with a flood of splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200110">10</a></font></span>She whom the Asvins measured out, o'er whom the foot of<br>   Vishnu strode,<br>   Whom Indra, Lord of Power and Might, freed from all foemen<br>    for himself,<br>   May Earth pour out her milk for us, a mother unto me her son.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200111">11</a></font></span>O Prithivī, auspicious be thy woodlands, auspicious be thy hills<br>    and snow-clad mountains.<br>   Unslain, unwounded, unsubdued, I have set foot upon the<br>   Earth,<br>   On earth brown, black, ruddy and every-coloured, on the firm<br>    earth that Indra guards from danger.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200112">12</a></font></span>O Prithivī, thy centre and thy navel, all forces that have issued<br>    from thy body<br>   Set us amid those forces; breathe upon us. I am the son of<br>   Earth, Earth is my Mother. Parjanya is my Sire; may he<br>    promote me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200143">43</a></font></span>Earth on whose surface they enclose the altar, and all-performers<br>    spin the thread of worship;<br>   In whom the stakes of sacrifice, resplendent, are fixed and raised<br>    on high before the oblation, may she, this Earth, prospering,<br>    make us prosper.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200114">14</a></font></span>The man who hates us, Earth! who fights against us, who<br>    threaten us with thought or deadly weapon, make him our<br>    thrall as thou hast done aforetime.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200115">15</a></font></span>Produced from thee, on thee move mortal creatures: thou<br>    bearest them, both quadruped and biped.<br>   Thine, Prithivī, are these Five human Races, for whom, though<br>    mortal, Sūrya as he rises spreads with his rays the light that is<br>    immortal.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200116">16</a></font></span>In concert may these creatures yield us blessings. With honey of<br>    discourse, O Earth, endow me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200117">17</a></font></span>Kind, ever gracious be the Earth we tread on, the firm Earth,<br>   Prithivī, borne up by Order, mother of plants and herbs, the<br>    all-producer. <a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200118">18</a></font></span>A vast abode hast thou become, the Mighty. Great stress is on<br>    thee, press and agitation, but with unceasing care great Indra<br>    guards thee.<br>   So make us shine, O Earth, us with the splendour of gold. Let<br>    no man look on us with hatred.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200119">19</a></font></span>Agni is in the earth, in plants; the waters hold Agni in them,<br>    in the stones is Agni.<br>   Agni abideth deep in men: Agnis abide in cows and steeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200120">20</a></font></span>Agni gives shine and heat in heaven: the spacious air is his, the<br>   God's<br>   Lover of fatness, bearer of oblation, men enkindle him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200121">21</a></font></span>Dark-kneed, invested with a fiery mantle, Prithivī sharpen me<br>    and give me splendour!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200122">22</a></font></span>On earth they offer sacrifice and dressed oblation to the Gods.<br>   Men, mortals, live upon the earth by food in their accustomed<br>    way.<br>   May that Earth grant us breath and vital power. Prithivī give<br>    me life of long duration!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200123">23</a></font></span>Scent that hath risen from thee, O Earth, the fragrance which.<br>    growing herbs and plants and waters carry,<br>   Shared by Apsarases, shared by Gandharvas therewith make<br>    thou me sweet: let no man hate me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200124">24</a></font></span>Thy scent which entered and possessed the lotus, the scent<br>    which they prepared at Sūryā's bridal,<br>   Scent which Immortals Earth! of old collected, therewith make<br>    thou me sweet: let no man hate me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200125">25</a></font></span>Thy scent in women and in men, the luck and light that is in.<br>    males,<br>   That is in heroes and in steeds in sylvan beasts and elephants,<br>   The splendid energy of maids, therewith do thou unite us,.<br>   Earth! Let no man look on us with hate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200126">26</a></font></span>Rock earth, and stone, and dust, this Earth is held together,.<br>    firmly bound.<br>   To this gold-breasted Prithivī mine adoration have I paid.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200127">27</a></font></span>Hither we call the firmly held, the all-supporting Prithivī,<br>   On whom the trees, lords of the wood, stand evermore immov-<br>    able.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200128">28</a></font></span>Sitting at ease or rising up, standing or going on our way.<br>   With our right foot and with our left we will not reel upon the<br>    earth. <a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="green">p. 78</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200129">29</a></font></span>I speak to Prithivī the purifier, to patient Earth who groweth<br>    strong through Brahma.<br>   O Earth, may we recline on thee who bearest strength, increase,<br>    portioned share of food, and fatness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200130">30</a></font></span>Purified for our bodies flow the waters: we bring distress on<br>    him who would attack us.<br>   I cleanse myself, O Earth, with that which cleanseth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200131">31</a></font></span>Earth, be thine eastern and thy northern regions, those lying<br>    southward and those lying westward.<br>   Propitious unto me in all my movements. Long as I tread the<br>    ground let me not stumble.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200132">32</a></font></span>Drive us not from the west or east, drive us not from the north<br>    or south,<br>   Be gracious unto us, O Earth: let not the robbers find us; keep<br>    the deadly weapon far away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200133">33</a></font></span>Long as, on thee, I look around, possessing Sūrya as a friend,<br>   So long, through each succeeding year, let not my power of<br>    vision fail.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200134">34</a></font></span>When, as I lie, O Earth, I turn upon my right side and my left,<br>   When stretched at all our length we lay our ribs on thee who<br>    meetest us.<br>   Do us no injury there, O Earth who furnishest a bed for all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200135">35</a></font></span>Let what I dig from thee, O Earth, rapidly spring and grow<br>    again.<br>   O Purifier, let me not pierce through thy vitals or thy heart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200136">36</a></font></span>Earth, may thy summer, and thy rains, and autumn, thy winter,<br>    and thy dewy frosts, and spring-time.<br>   May thy years, Prithivī! and ordered seasons, and day and<br>    night pour out for us abundance.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200137">37</a></font></span>The purifier, shrinking from the Serpent, she who held fires that<br>    lie within the waters,<br>   Who gives as prey the God-blaspheming Dasyus, Earth choosing<br>   Indra for her Lord, not Vritra, hath clung to Sakra, to the<br>   Strong and Mighty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200138">38</a></font></span>Base of the seat and sheds, on whom the sacrificial stake is<br>    reared,<br>   On whom the Yajus-knowing priests recite their hymns and<br>    chant their psalms,<br>   And ministers are busied that Indra may drink the Soma juice; <a name="page_79"><font size="1" color="green">p. 79</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200139">39</a></font></span>On whom the ancient Rishis, they who made the world, sang<br>    forth the cows,<br>   Seven worshippers, by session, with their fervent zeal and<br>    sacrifice;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200140">40</a></font></span>May she, the Earth, assign to us the opulence for which we<br>    yearn,<br>   May Bhaga share and aid the task and Indra come to lead the<br>    way.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200141">41</a></font></span>May she, the Earth, whereon men sing and dance with varied<br>    shout and noise,<br>   Whereon men meet in battle, and the war-cry and the drum<br>    resound,<br>   May she drive off our foemen, may Prithivī rid me of my foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200142">42</a></font></span>On whom is food, barley and rice, to whom these Races Five<br>    belong,<br>   Homage to her, P arjanya's wife, to her whose marrow is the<br>    rain!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200143">43</a></font></span>Whose castles are the work of Gods, and men wage war upon<br>    her plain<br>   The Lord of Life make Prithivī, who beareth all things in her<br>    womb, pleasant to us on every side!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200144">44</a></font></span>May Earth the Goddess, she who bears her treasure stored up<br>    in many a place, gold, gems, and riches,<br>   Giver of opulence, grant great possessions to us bestowing them<br>    with love and favour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200145">45</a></font></span>Earth, bearing folk of many a varied language with divers rites<br>    as suits their dwelling-places,<br>   Pour, like a constant cow that never faileth, a thousand streams<br>    of treasure to enrich me!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200146">46</a></font></span>Thy snake, thy sharply stinging scorpion, lying concealed, be-<br>    wildered, chilled with cold of winter,<br>   The worm, O Prithivī, each thing that in the Rains revives and<br>    stirs,<br>   Creeping, forbear to creep on us! With all things gracious bless<br>    thou us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200147">47</a></font></span>Thy many ways on which the people travel, the road for car and<br>    wain to journey over,<br>   Thereon meet both the good and bad, that pathway may we<br>    attain without a foe or robber. With all things gracious bless<br>    thou us. <a name="page_80"><font size="1" color="green">p. 80</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200148">48</a></font></span>Supporting both the foolish and the weighty she bears the death<br>    both of the good and evil.<br>   In friendly concord with the boar, Earth opens herself for the<br>    wild swine that roams the forest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200149">49</a></font></span>All sylvan beasts of thine that love the woodlands, man-eaters,.<br>    forest-haunting, lions, tigers,<br>   Hyena, wolf, Misfortune, evil spirit, drive from us, chase the<br>    demons to a distance.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200150">50</a></font></span>Gandharvas and Apsarases, Kimīdins, and malignant sprites,<br>   Pisāchas all, and Rākshasas, these keep thou, Earth! afar from<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200151">51</a></font></span>To whom the winged bipeds fly together, birds of each various<br>    kind, the swans, the eagles;<br>   On whom the Wind comes rushing, Mātarisvan, rousing the<br>    dust and causing trees to tremble, and flame pursues the blast.<br>    hither and thither;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200152">52</a></font></span>Earth, upon whom are settled, joined together, the night and<br>    day, the dusky and the ruddy, Prithivī compassed by the rain<br>    about her,<br>   Happily may she stablish us in each delightful dwelling place.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200153">53</a></font></span>Heaven, Earth, the realm of Middle Air have granted me this<br>    ample room,<br>   Agni, Sun, Waters, all the Gods have joined to give me mental<br>    power.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200154">54</a></font></span>I am victorious, I am called the lord superior on earth,<br>   Triumphant, all-o'erpowering the conqueror on every side<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200155">55</a></font></span>There, when the Gods, O Goddess, named thee, spreading thy<br>    wide expanse as thou wast broadening eastward,<br>   Then into thee passed many a charm and glory: thou madest<br>    for thyself the world's four regions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200156">56</a></font></span>In hamlets and in woodland, and in all assemblages on earth,<br>   In gatherings, meeting of the folk, we will speak glorious things<br>    of thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200157">57</a></font></span>As the horse scattereth the dust, the people who dwelt upon<br>    the land, at birth, she scattered,<br>   Leader and head of all the world, delightful, the trees' protectress<br>    and the plants' upholder.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200158">58</a></font></span>Whate'er I say I speak with honey-sweetness, whatever I behold<br>    for that they love me.<br>   Dazzling, impetuous am I: others who fiercely stir I slay. <a name="page_81"><font size="1" color="green">p. 81</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200159">59</a></font></span>Mild, gracious, sweetly odorous, milky, with nectar in her breast,<br>   May Earth, may Prithivī bestow her benison, with milk, on me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200160">60</a></font></span>Whom Visvakarman with oblation followed, when she was set<br>    in mid-air's billowy ocean<br>   A useful vessel, hid, when, for enjoyment, she was made mani-<br>    fest to those with mothers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200161">61</a></font></span>Thou art the vessel that containeth people, Aditi, granter of the<br>    wish, far-spreading.<br>   Prajāpati, the first-born Son of Order, supplieth thee with what-<br>    soe'er thou lackest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200162">62</a></font></span>Let thy breasts, frec from sickness and Consumption, be.<br>   Prithivī, produced for our advantage.<br>   Through long-extended life wakeful and watching still may we<br>    be thy tributary servants.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200163">63</a></font></span>O Earth, my Mother, set thou me happily in a place secure.<br>   Of one accord with Heaven, O Sage, set me in glory and in<br>    wealth.</p>
