 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="green">p. 129</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XIV</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">On the Bridal of Sūryā, marriage ceremonies in general</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400101">1</a></font></span>Truth is the base that bears the earth; by Sūrya are the heavens<br>    upheld.<br>   By Law the Ādityas stand secure, and Soma holds his place in<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400102">2</a></font></span>By Soma are the Ādityas strong, by Soma mighty is the earth:<br>   Thus Soma in the lap of all these constellations hath his home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400103">3</a></font></span>One thinks, when men have brayed the plant, that he hath drunk<br>    the Soma's juice.<br>   Of him whom Brāhmans truly know as Soma never mortal eats.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400104">4</a></font></span>When they begin to drink thee, then, O God, thou swellest out<br>    again.<br>   Vāyu in Soma's sentinel. The month is that which shapes the<br>    years.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400105">5</a></font></span>Soma, preserved by covering rules, guarded by hymns in Brihatī,<br>   Thou standest listening to the stones; none tastes of thee who<br>    dwells on earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400106">6</a></font></span>Thought was her coverlet, the power of sight was unguent for her<br>    eyes:<br>   Her treasure-chest was earth and heaven, when Sūryā went unto<br>    her lord.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400107">7</a></font></span>Raibhi was her dear bridal friend, and Nārāsatisi led her home.<br>   Lovely to see was Sūryā's robe: by Gāthā beautified she moves<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400108">8</a></font></span>Songs were the cross-bars of the pole, Kurira metre docked her<br>    head.<br>   Both Asvins were the paranymphs: Agni was leader of the<br>    train.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400109">9</a></font></span>Soma was he who wooed the maid: the groomsmen were both.<br>   Asvins, when<br>   The Sun-God Savitar bestowed his willing Sūryā on her lord.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400110">10</a></font></span>Her spirit was the bridal car, the canopy thereof was heaven:<br>   Two radiant oxen formed the team when Sūryā came unto her<br>    lord. <a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="green">p. 130</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400111">11</a></font></span>Steadily went the steers upheld by holy verse and song of praise,<br>   The chariot-wheels were listening ears: thy path was tremulous<br>    in the sky.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400112">12</a></font></span>Pure, as thou wentest, were thy wheels, breath was the axle pier-<br>    cing them.<br>   Sūryā advancing to her lord rode on the chariot of her heart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400113">13</a></font></span>The bridal pomp of Sūryā, which Savitar started, moved along.<br>   In Maghā days are oxen slain, in Phalgunis they wed the bride.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400114">14</a></font></span>When on your three-wheeled chariot, O ye Asvins, ye came as<br>    suitors unto Sūrya's bridal,<br>   Where was one chariot-wheel of yours? Where stood ye for the<br>    sire's command?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400115">15</a></font></span>Twin Lords of Lustre, at the time when ye to Sūryā's wooing<br>    came,<br>   Then all the Gods agreed to your proposal Pūshan as son elected<br>    you as father.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400116">16</a></font></span>Two wheels of thine the Brāhmans know, Sūrya! according to<br>    their times.<br>   That which is hidden only those who know the highest truths<br>    have learned.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400117">17</a></font></span>Worship we pay to Aryaman, finder of husbands, kindly friend.<br>   As from its stalk a cucumber, from here I loose thee, not from<br>    there<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400118">18</a></font></span>Hence and not thence I send her free. I make her softly fettered<br>    there.<br>   That, bounteous Indra! she may live blest in her fortune and<br>    her sons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400119">19</a></font></span>Now from the noose of Varuna I free thee, where with the bless-<br>    ed Savitar hath bound thee.<br>   May bless be thine together with thy wooer in Order's dwelling,<br>    in the world of virtue.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400120">20</a></font></span>Let Bhaga take thy hand and hence conduct thee: let the two<br>   Asvins on their car transport thee.<br>   Go to the house to be the household's mistress, and speak as<br>    lady to thy gathered people.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400121">21</a></font></span>Happy be thou and prosper with thy children here: be vigilant<br>    to rule the household in this home.<br>   Closely unite thy body with this man thy lord. So shalt thou,<br>    full of years, address thy company. <a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="green">p. 131</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400122">22</a></font></span>Be not divided; dwell ye here; reach the full time of human<br>    life.<br>   With sons and grandsons sport and play, rejoicing in your happy<br>    home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400123">23</a></font></span>Moving by magic power from east to westward, these children<br>    twain go sporting round the ocean.<br>   The one beholds all creatures: thou, the other, art born anew,<br>    duly arranging seasons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400124">24</a></font></span>Thou, born afresh, art new and new for ever; ensign of days,<br>    before the Dawns thou goest.<br>   Coming, thou orderest for Gods their portion. Thou lengthenest,<br>   Moon, the days of our existence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400125">25</a></font></span>Give thou the wollen robe away: deal treasure to the Brāhman-<br>    priests.<br>   This Witchery hath got her feet: the wife attendeth on her lord.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400126">26</a></font></span>It turneth dusky-red: the witch who clingeth close is driven off.<br>   Well thrive the kinsmen of this bride: the husband is bound<br>    fast in bonds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400127">27</a></font></span>Unlovely is his body when it glistens with that wicked fiend,<br>   What time the husband wraps about his limbs the garment of<br>    his wife.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400128">28</a></font></span>The butchering, the cutting-up, the severing of limb and joint—<br>   Behold the forms which Sūryā wears: yet these the Brāhman<br>    purifies,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400129">29</a></font></span>Pungent is this, bitter is this, filled as it were with arrow barbs,<br>    empoisoned and not fit for use.<br>   The Brāhman who knows Sūryā well deserves the garment of<br>    the bride.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400130">30</a></font></span>The Brāhman takes away the robe as a fair thing that brings good<br>    luck.<br>   He knows the expiating rite whereby the wife is kept unharmed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400131">31</a></font></span>Prepare, ye twain, happy and prosperous fortune, speaking the<br>    truth in faithful utterances.<br>   Dear unto her, Brihaspati, make the husband, and pleasant be<br>    these words the wooer speaketh.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400132">32</a></font></span>Remain ye even here and go no farther: strengthen this man, ye<br>   Cows, with plenteous offspring.<br>   May Dawns that come for glory, bright with Soma, here may all<br>   Gods fix and enchant your spirits. <a name="page_132"><font size="1" color="green">p. 132</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400133">33</a></font></span>Come, O ye Cows, with offspring dwell around him: he doth not<br>    stint the Gods' alloted portion.<br>   To him, your friend, may Pūshan, all the Maruts, to him may<br>   Dhatar, Savitar send vigour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400134">34</a></font></span>Straight in direction be the paths, and thornless, whereby our<br>    fellows travel to the wooing.<br>   With Bhaga and with Aryaman Dhātar endue the pair with<br>    strength!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400135">35</a></font></span>Whatever lustre is in dice, whatever lustre is in wine,<br>   Whatever lustre is in cows, Asvins, endue this dame therewith.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400136">36</a></font></span>With all the sheen that balmeth wine, or thigh of female para-<br>    mour,<br>   With all the sheen that balmeth dice, even with this adorn the<br>    dame.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400137">37</a></font></span>He who in water shines unfed with fuel, whom sages worship in<br>    their sacrifices.<br>   May he, the Waters' Child, send us sweet waters those that en-<br>    hanced the power of mighty Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400138">38</a></font></span>I cast away a handful here, hurtful, injurious to health.<br>   I lift another handful up, sparkling and bringing happiness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400139">39</a></font></span>Hither let Brāhmans bring her bathing water; let them draw<br>    such as guards the lives of heroes.<br>   Aryaman's fire let her encircle, Pūshan! Fathers-in-law stand,<br>    with their sons, expectant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400140">40</a></font></span>Blest be the gold to thee, and blest the water, blest the yoke's<br>    opening, and blest the pillar.<br>   Blest he the waters with their hundred cleansings: blest be thy<br>    body's union with thy husband.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400141">41</a></font></span>Cleansing Apālā, Indra! thrice, thou gavest sunbright skin to<br>    her<br>   Drawn, Satakratu! through the hole of car, of wagon, and of<br>    yoke.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400142">42</a></font></span>Saying thy prayer for cheerfulness, children, prosperity, and<br>    wealth,<br>   Devoted to thy husband, gird thyself for immortality.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400143">43</a></font></span>As vigorous Sindhu won himself imperial lordship of the<br>    streams,<br>   So be imperial queen when thou hast come within thy husband's<br>    home.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400144">44</a></font></span>Over thy husband's fathers and his brothers be imperial queen. <a name="page_133"><font size="1" color="green">p. 133</font></a><br>   Over thy husband's sister and, his mother bear supreme control.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400145">45</a></font></span>They who have spun, and woven, and extended Goddesses who<br>    have drawn the ends together,<br>   May they invest thee for full long existence. Heiress of lengthen-<br>    ed life, endue this garment,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400146">46</a></font></span>They mourn the living, they arrange the sacred rite: the men<br>    have set their thoughts upon a distant cast:<br>   They who have brought the Fathers this delightful gift, when<br>    wives allowed their lords the joy of their embrace.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400147">47</a></font></span>I place upon the lap of Earth the Goddess, a firm auspicious<br>    stone to bring thee children.<br>   Stand on it, thou, greeted with joy, resplendent: a long long<br>    life may Savitar vouchsafe thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400148">48</a></font></span>As Agni in the olden time took the right hand of this our Earth.<br>   Even so I take and hold thy hand: be not disquieted, with me,<br>    with children and with store of wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400149">49</a></font></span>God Savitar shall take thy hand, and Soma the King shall make<br>    thee rich in goodly offspring,<br>   Let Agni, Lord Omniscient, make thee happy, till old old age a<br>    wife unto thy husband.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400150">50</a></font></span>I take thy hand in mine for happy fortune that thou mayst reach<br>    old age with me thy consort,<br>   Gods, Aryaman, Bhaga, Savitar, Purandhi, have given thee to be<br>    my household's mistress.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400151">51</a></font></span>Bhaga and Savitar the God have clasped that hand of thine in<br>    theirs,<br>   By rule and law thou art my wife: the master of thy house am I.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400152">52</a></font></span>Be it my care to cherish her: Brihaspati hath made thee mine.<br>   A hundred autumns live with me thy husband, mother of my<br>    sons!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400153">53</a></font></span>Tvashtar, by order of the holy sages, hath laid on her Brihas-<br>    pati's robe for glory,<br>   By means of this let Savitar and Bhaga surround this dame, like<br>   Sūryā, with her children.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400154">54</a></font></span>May Indra-Agni, Heaven-Earth, Mātarisvan, may Mitra-Varuna,<br>   Bhaga, both the Asvins,<br>   Brihaspati, the host of Maruts, Brahma, and Soma magnify this<br>    dame with offspring.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400155">55</a></font></span>It was Brihaspati who first arranged the hair on Sūryā's head, <a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="green">p. 134</font></a><br>   And therefore, O ye Asvins, we adorn this woman for her lord.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400156">56</a></font></span>This lovely form the maiden wears in spirit I long to look on<br>    as my wife approaching,<br>   Her will I follow with my nine companions. Who is the sage<br>    that loosed the bonds that held her?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400157">57</a></font></span>I free her: he who sees, within my bosom, my heart's nest<br>    knows how her fair form hath struck me.<br>   I taste no stolen food: myself untying Varuna's nooses I am<br>    freed in spirit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400158">58</a></font></span>Now from the bond of Varuna I loose thee, wherein the blessed<br>   Savitar hath bound thee.<br>   O bride, I give thee here beside thy husband fair space and room<br>    and pleasant paths to travel.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400159">59</a></font></span>Lift up your weapons. Drive away the demons. Transport this<br>    woman to the world of virtue.<br>   Dilator, most wise, hath found for her a husband. Let him who<br>    knows, King Bhaga, go before her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400160">60</a></font></span>Bhaga hath formed the four legs of the litter, wrought the four<br>    pieces that compose the frame-work.<br>   Tvashtar hath decked the straps that go across it, May it be<br>    blest, and bring us happy fortune.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400161">61</a></font></span>Mount this, all-hued. gold tinted, strong wheeled, fashioned of<br>   Kinsuka, this chariot lightly rolling,<br>   Bound for the world of life immortal, Sūryā! Made for thy<br>    lord a happy bride's procession.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400162">62</a></font></span>To us, O Varuna, bring her, kind to brothers; bring her, Brihas-<br>    pati, gentle to the cattle.<br>   Bring her, O Indra, gentle to her husband: bring her to us, O<br>   Savitar, blest with children.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400163">63</a></font></span>Hurt not the girl, ye Pillars twain upon the path which Gods<br>    have made.<br>   The portal of the heavenly home we make the bride's auspicious<br>    road.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400164">64</a></font></span>Let prayer he offered up before and after, prayer in the middle,<br>    lastly, all around her.<br>   Reaching the Gods' inviolable castle shine in thy lord's world<br>    gentle and auspicious.</p>
