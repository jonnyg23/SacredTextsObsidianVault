 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003301">1</a></font></span>Drink of the juice which men have washed in waters and fill the-<br>    full, O Lord of tawny horses.<br>   O Indra, hearer of the laud, with Soma which stones have mix-<br>    ed for thee enhance thy rapture.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003302">2</a></font></span>To make thee start, a strong true draught I offer to thee the<br>   Bull, O thou whom bay steeds carry.<br>   Here take delight, O Indra, in our voices while thou art hymned<br>    with power and all our spirit. <a name="page_290"><font size="1" color="green">p. 290</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003303">3</a></font></span>O mighty Indra, through thine aid, thy prowess, obtaining life,<br>    zealous, and skilled in worship.<br>   Men in the house who share the sacred banquet stand singing<br>    praise that brings them store of children.</p>
