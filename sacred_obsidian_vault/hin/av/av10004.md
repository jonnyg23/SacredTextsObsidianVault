 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm to destroy venomous serpents</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000402">2</a></font></span>The first of all is Indra's car, next is the chariot of the Gods<br>    the third is Varuna's alone.<br>   The last, the Serpents' chariot, struck the pillar and then sped<br>    away. <a name="page_12"><font size="1" color="green">p. 12</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000402">2</a></font></span>Their lustre is the Darbha-grass, its young shoots are their<br>    horse's tail: the reed's plume is their chariot seat.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000403">3</a></font></span>Strike out, white courser! with thy foot, strike both with fore<br>    and hinder foot,<br>   Stay the dire poison of the Snakes, and make it weak as soaking<br>    wood.<br>   -4. Loud neighing he hath dived below, and rising up again replied,<br>   Stayed the dire poison of the Snakes, and made it weak as<br>    soaking wood.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000405">5</a></font></span>Paidva kills Kasarnila, kills both the white Serpent and the<br>    black,<br>   Paidva hath struck and cleft in twain Ratharvi's and the Viper's<br>    head.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000406">6</a></font></span>Go onward, horse of Pedu! go thou first: we follow after thee.<br>   Cast thou aside the Serpents from the pathway whereupon we<br>    tread.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000407">7</a></font></span>Here was the horse of Pedu born: this is the way that takes him<br>    hence.<br>   These are the tracks the courser left, the mighty slayer of the<br>   Snakes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000408">8</a></font></span>Let him not close the opened mouth, nor open that which now<br>    is closed.<br>   Two snakes are in this field, and both, female and male, are<br>    powerless.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000409">9</a></font></span>Powerless are the serpents here, those that are near and those<br>    afar.<br>   I kill the scorpion with a club, and with a staff the new-come<br>    snake.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000410">10</a></font></span>This is the remedy against Aghāsva and the adder, both:<br>   Indra and Paidva have subdued and tamed the vicious snake for<br>    me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000411">11</a></font></span>We fix our thoughts on Pedu's horse, strong, off-spring of a<br>    stedfast line.<br>   Behind our backs the vipers here crouch down and lie in wait<br>    for us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000412">12</a></font></span>Bereft of life and poison they lie slain by bolt-armed Indra's<br>    hand. Indra and we have slaughtered them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000400">0</a></font></span>13. Tiraschirājis have been slain, and vipers crushed and brayed to<br>    bits. <a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="green">p. 13</font></a><br>   Slay Darvi in the Darbha-grass, Karikrata, and White and<br>   Black.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000414">14</a></font></span>The young maid of Kirāta race, a little damsel, digs the drug,<br>   Digs it with shovels wrought of gold on the high ridges of the<br>    hills.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000415">15</a></font></span>Hither the young uuconquered leech who slays the speckled<br>    snake hath come.<br>   He verily demolishes adder and scorpion; both of them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000416">16</a></font></span>Indra, Mitra and Varuna, and Vāta and Parjanya both have<br>    given the serpent up to me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000417">17</a></font></span>Indra hath given him up to me, the female viper and the male,<br>   The adder, him with stripes athwart. Kasarnila, Dasonasi.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000418">18</a></font></span>O Serpent, Indra hath destroyed the sire who first engendered<br>    thee:<br>   And when these snakes are pierced and bored what sap and<br>    vigour will be theirs?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000419">19</a></font></span>Their heads have I seized firmly as a fisher grasps the spotted<br>    prey,<br>   Waded half through the stream and washed the poison of the<br>    serpents off.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000420">20</a></font></span>Let the floods hurry on and bear the poison of all snakes afar.<br>   Tiraschirājis have been slain and vipers crushed and brayed to<br>    bits.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000421">21</a></font></span>As from the salutary plants I deftly pick the fibres out, And<br>    guide them skilfully like mares, so let thy venom, Snake!<br>    depart,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000422">22</a></font></span>All poison that the sun and fire, all that the earth and plants<br>    contain,<br>   Poison of most effectual power—let all thy venom pass away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000423">23</a></font></span>Serpents which fire or plants have generated, those which have<br>    sprung from waters or the lightning,<br>   Whose mighty broods are found in many places, these serpents<br>    we will reverently worship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000424">24</a></font></span>Thou art a maid called Taudi, or Ghritāchi is thy name. Thy<br>    place;<br>   Is underneath my foot. I take the poison-killing remedy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000425">25</a></font></span>From every member drive away the venom, and avoid the heart.<br>   Then let the poison's burning heat pass downward and away-<br>    from thee. <a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="green">p. 14</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000426">26</a></font></span>The bane hath fled afar. It wept, and asked the poison how it<br>    fared.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000427">27</a></font></span>Agni hath found the venom of the serpent, Soma drawn it out.<br>   Back to the biter hath returned the poison, and the snake hath<br>    died.</p>
