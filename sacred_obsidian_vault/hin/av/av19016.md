 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer for security from danger</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901601">1</a></font></span>Peace hath been given us from the east, and from the west<br>    security.<br>   Savitar guard me from the south, and from the north the Lord<br>    of Might.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901602">2</a></font></span>May the Ādityas from the sky protect me, Agni from the<br>    earth. <a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="green">p. 226</font></a><br>   May Indra-Agni guard me from the eastward, on all sides may<br>    the Asvins give me shelter.<br>   May Jātavedas guard, sidelong, our cattle.<br>   Our shield on all sides be the World-Creators!</p>
