 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="green">p. 163</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XVI</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">On the preparation and use of holy water, with, a prayer for purification and freedom from sin</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600101">1</a></font></span>The Bull of the Waters hath been let go; the heavenly fires have<br>    been let go.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600102">2</a></font></span>Breaking, breaking down, crushing, crushing to pieces,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600103">3</a></font></span>Mroka, mind-destroying, rooting up, consuming, ruiner of the<br>    soul, ruiner of the body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600104">4</a></font></span>Here I let him go: thou washest me clean of him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600105">5</a></font></span>With this we let him loose who hates us and whom we hate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600106">6</a></font></span>Thou art in front of the waters. I let loose your sea.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600107">7</a></font></span>I let loose the Agni who is within the waters, Mroka the up-<br>    rooter, the destroyer of the body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600108">8</a></font></span>Your Agni who entered into the waters, even he here is that very<br>    dread of yours.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600109">9</a></font></span>May he anoint you with Indra's own mighty power!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600110">10</a></font></span>May stainless waters cleanse us from defilement.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600111">11</a></font></span>May they carry sin away from us, may they carry away from us<br>    the evil dream.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600112">12</a></font></span>Look on me with a friendly eye, O, Waters, and touch my skin<br>    with your auspicious body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600113">13</a></font></span>We call the gracious Fires that dwell in waters. Goddesses, grant<br>    me princely power and splendour.</p>
