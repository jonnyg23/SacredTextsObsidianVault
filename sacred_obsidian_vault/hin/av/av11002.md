 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II</h3>  <h4 align="center">Prayer and praise to Bhava, Sarva and Rudra</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100201">1</a></font></span>Bhava and Sarva, spare us, be not hostile. Homage to you, twin<br>   Lords of beasts and spirits!<br>   Shoot not the arrow aimed and drawn against us: forbear to<br>    harm our quadrupeds and bipeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100202">2</a></font></span>Cast not our bodies to the dog or jackal, nor, Lord of Beasts!<br>    to carrion-kites or vultures.<br>   Let not thy black voracious flies attack them; let not thy birds<br>    obtain them for their banquet.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100203">3</a></font></span>We offer homage to thy shout, Bhava! thy breath, thy racking<br>    pains:<br>   Homage, Immortal One! to thee, to Rudra of the thousand<br>    eyes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100204">4</a></font></span>We offer reverence to thee from eastward, and from north and<br>    south,<br>   From all the compass of the sky, to thee and to the firmament.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100205">5</a></font></span>Homage, O Bhava, Lord of Beasts, unto thy face and all thine<br>    eyes,<br>   To skin, and hue, and aspect, and to thee when looked at from<br>    behind!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100206">6</a></font></span>We offer homage to thy limbs, thy belly, and thy tongue, and<br>    mouth we offer homage to thy smell.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100207">7</a></font></span>Never may we contend with him, the mighty archer, thousand-<br>    eyed. <a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="green">p. 47</font></a><br>   Rudra who wears black tufts of hair, the slaughterer of<br>   Ardhaka.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100208">8</a></font></span>May he, may Bhava from all sides avoid us, avoid us even as<br>    fire avoids the waters. Let him not threaten us. To him be<br>    homage!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100209">9</a></font></span>Four times, eight times be homage paid to Bhava, yea, Lord of<br>   Beasts, ten times be reverence paid thee!<br>   Thine are these animals, five several classes, oxen, and goats and<br>    sheep, and men, and horses<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100210">10</a></font></span>Thine the four regions, thine are earth and heaven, thine,<br>   Mighty One, this firmament between them;<br>   Thine everything with soul and breath here on the surface of the<br>    land.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100211">11</a></font></span>Thine is this ample wealth-containing storehouse that holds with-<br>    in it all these living creatures.<br>   Favour us, Lord of Beasts, to thee be homage! Far from us go<br>    ill-omens, dogs, and jackals, and wild-haired women with<br>    their horrid shrieking!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100212">12</a></font></span>A yellow bow of gold thou wieldest, slaying its hundred, tufted<br>   God! smiting its thousand.<br>   Weapon of Gods, far flies the shaft of Rudra: wherever it may<br>    be, we pay it homage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100213">13</a></font></span>Thou, Rudra, followest close the foe who lies in wait to conquer<br>    thee.<br>   Even as a hunter who pursues the footsteps of the wounded<br>    game.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100214">14</a></font></span>Accordant and allies, Bhava and Rudra, with mighty strength ye<br>    go to deeds of valour. Wherever they may be, we pay them<br>    homage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100215">15</a></font></span>Be homage, Rudra, unto thee approaching and departing hence!<br>   Homage to thee when standing still, to thee when seated and at<br>    rest!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100216">16</a></font></span>Homage at evening and at morn, homage at night, homage by<br>    day .<br>   To Bhava and to Sarva, both, have I paid lowly reverence,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100217">17</a></font></span>Let us not outrage with our tongue far-seeing Rudra, thousand-<br>    eyed,<br>   Inspired with varied lore, who shoots his arrows forward, far<br>    away. <a name="page_48"><font size="1" color="green">p. 48</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100218">18</a></font></span>Foremost we go to meet his car, the chariot of the long-haired<br>   God,<br>   Drawn by brown horses, dusky, black, o'erthrowing, slaying,<br>    terrible. Let reverence be paid to him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100219">19</a></font></span>Cast not thy club at us, thy heavenly weapon. Lord of Beasts,<br>    be not wroth with us. Let reverence be paid to thee.<br>   Shake thy celestial branch above some others elsewhere, not o'er<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100220">20</a></font></span>Do us no harm, but comfort us: avoid thou us, and be not<br>    wroth. Never let us contend with thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100221">21</a></font></span>Covet not thou our kine or men, covet not thou our goats or<br>    sheep.<br>   Elsewhither, strong One! turn thine aim: destroy the mockers'<br>    family.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100222">22</a></font></span>Homage to him whose weapon, Cough or Fever, assails one like<br>    the neighing of a stallion; to him who draws one forth and<br>    then another!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100223">23</a></font></span>Homage be paid him with ten Sakvari verses who stands<br>    established in the air's mid-region, slaying non-sacrificing<br>   God-despisers!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100224">24</a></font></span>For thee were forest beasts and sylvan creatures placed in the<br>    wood, and small birds, swans, and eagles.<br>   Floods, Lord of Beasts! contain thy living beings: to swell thy<br>    strength flow the celestial Waters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100225">25</a></font></span>Porpoises, serpents, strange aquatic monsters, fishes, and things<br>    unclean at which thou shootest.<br>   Nothing is far for thee, naught checks thee, Bhava! The whole<br>    earth in a moment thou surveyest. From the east sea thou<br>    smitest in the northern.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100226">26</a></font></span>O'erwhelm us not with Fever or with poison, nor, Rudra! with<br>    the fire that comes from heaven. Elsewhere, and not on us,<br>    cast down this lightning.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100227">27</a></font></span>Ruler of heaven and Lord of earth is Bhava: Bhava hath filled<br>    the spacious air's mid-region. Where'er he be, to him be paid<br>    our homage!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100228">28</a></font></span>King Bhava, favour him who offers worship, for thou art<br>   Pasupati, Lord of victims.<br>   Be gracious to the quadruped and biped of the believer in the<br>   Gods' existence. <a name="page_49"><font size="1" color="green">p. 49</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100229">29</a></font></span>Harm thou among us neither great nor little, not one who bears<br>    us, not our future bearers.<br>   Injure no sire among us, harm no mother. Forbear to injure our<br>    own bodies, Rudra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100230">30</a></font></span>This lowly reverence have I paid to Rudra's dogs with mighty<br>    mouths,<br>   Hounds terrible with bark and howl, who gorge unmasticated<br>    food.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100231">31</a></font></span>Homage to thy loud-shouting hosts and thy long-haired<br>    followers!<br>   Homage to hosts that are adored, homage to armies that enjoy<br>   Homage to all thy troops, O God. Security and bliss be ours!</p>
