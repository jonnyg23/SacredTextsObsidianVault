 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013201">1, 2</a></font></span>Then too the single bottle-gourd, the bottle-gourd dug<br>    from the earth,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013203">3, 4</a></font></span>The lute dug up from out the ground: this the wind stirs<br>    and agitates. <a name="page_370"><font size="1" color="green">p. 370</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013205">5, 6</a></font></span>Let him prepare a nest, they say: he shall obtain it strong<br>    and stretched.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013207">7, 8</a></font></span>He shall not gain it unspread out. Who among these will<br>    touch the lute?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013209">9, 10</a></font></span>Who among these will beat the drum? How, if he beat it,<br>    will he beat?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013211">11, 12</a></font></span>Where beating will the Goddess beat again again about the<br>    house?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013213">13, 14</a></font></span>Three are the names the camel bears, Golden is one of them,<br>    he said.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013215">15, 16</a></font></span>Glory and power, these are two. He with black tufts of hair<br>    shall strike.</p>
