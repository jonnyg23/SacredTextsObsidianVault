 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Sleep</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905601">1</a></font></span>Thou art come hither from the world of Yama: thou, resolute,<br>    affectest men with rapture.<br>   Thou, Sleep, created in the Asura's dwelling, goest, well-know-<br>    ing, with the solitary.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905602">2</a></font></span>At first the all-containing, depth beheld thee, ere Night was<br>    born, when only Day existed.<br>   Thence hast thou come, thence, Sleep, hast thou come hither,<br>    concealing, deep within, all form and figure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905603">3</a></font></span>Come from the Asuras in lofty, glory, he hath approached the<br>   Gods in search of greatness.<br>   Winners of heavenly light, the Three-and-Thirty endowed this<br>   Sleep with his supreme dominion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905604">4</a></font></span>Of him nor Fathers nor the Gods have knowledge, the Gods<br>    whose gentle talk is still about him.<br>   Urged by command of Varuna the Ādityas, Heroes, transported<br>   Sleep to Trita Āptya.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905605">5</a></font></span>Thou whose severity hath reached ill-doers, and whose reward<br>    the good have gained in slumber,<br>   Delightest heaven with thy most lofty kinship, born from his<br>    spirit who was worn and weary.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905606">6</a></font></span>Of old we know all places whence thou comest. O Sleep, we<br>    know him who is here thy ruler.<br>   Protect us here illustrious with glory. Go, from afar, with<br>    poisons, into distance.</p>
