 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A similar charm addressed to the same</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903501">1</a></font></span>While their lips uttered Indra's name the Rishis gave us Jangida..<br>   Which in the earliest time Gods made a remedy, Vishkandha's-<br>    cure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903502">2</a></font></span>So may this Jangida guard us, even as a treasurer guards wealth,.<br>   Even this which Gods and Brāhmans made a malice-quelling sure<br>    defence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903503">3</a></font></span>Hard-hearted men, the cruel eye, the sinner who hath come to-<br>    us,<br>   Destroy thou these with watchful care, O thou who hast a.<br>    thousand eyes. Thou, Jangida, art my defence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903504">4</a></font></span>Guard me from earth and guard me from the heavens, guard<br>    me from middle air, from plants protect me.<br>   Protect me from the present and the future. From every region<br>   Jangida preserve us!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903505">5</a></font></span>All sorcerers made by the Gods, all that arise from mortal men,.<br>   These, one and all, let Jangida, healer of all, make impotent.</p>
