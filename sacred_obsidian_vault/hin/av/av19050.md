 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN L</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Night for protection and prosperity</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905001">1</a></font></span>Blind him and make him headless, Night! the serpent with the<br>    pungent breath.<br>   Strike from his head the wolf's two eyes, and dash the thief<br>    against a post.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905002">2</a></font></span>Those oxen that are thine, O Night, with sharpened horns and<br>    rapid pace,<br>   With those transport us safe to-day o'er difficulties everywhere.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905003">3</a></font></span>Uninjured in our bodies may we pass through each succeeding<br>    night,<br>   And let malignities fail to pass, as men without a boat the depth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905004">4</a></font></span>As millet hurried through the air before us is beheld no more.<br>   So cause the man to vanish, Night, who plans to do us injury.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905005">5</a></font></span>The thief hast thou kept far away, the robber driver of our kine.<br>   Even him who having covered up the horse's head would lead<br>    him off.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905006">6</a></font></span>If dealing treasure thou hast come to-day, O highly favoured<br>   Night.<br>   Cause thou us to enjoy it all so that this may not pass away. <a name="page_253"><font size="1" color="green">p. 253</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905007">7</a></font></span>Do thou entrust us to the Dawn, all of us free from sin, O<br>   Night.<br>   May Dawn deliver us to Day, and Day to thee, O glorious One.</p>
