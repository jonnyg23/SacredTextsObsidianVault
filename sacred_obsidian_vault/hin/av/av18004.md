 <p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="green">p. 201</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A funeral hymn, composed partly of verses from the Rigveda</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800401">1</a></font></span>Rise to your mother, flames of Jātavedas! I send you up by<br>    paths which Fathers traverse.<br>   With headlong speed the Oblation bearer bore our gifts: toil ye,<br>    and place the offerer where the righteous dwell.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800402">2</a></font></span>The Seasons, Deities, form and order Worship, butter, cake,<br>    ladles, sacrificial weapons.<br>   Tread thou God-travelled paths whereby the righteous, payers<br>    of sacrifices, go to Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800403">3</a></font></span>Carefully look on Sacrifice's pathway whereon the Angirases,<br>    the righteous, travel.<br>   By those same pathways go thou up to Svarga where the<br>   Ādityas take their fill of sweetness, There make thy home in<br>    the third vault of heaven,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800404">4</a></font></span>Three eagles in the region's roar are standing high on heaven's<br>    ridge in their appointed station.<br>   The worlds of Svarga shall, filled full of Amrit, yield food and<br>    power to him who sacrificeth. <a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="green">p. 202</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800405">5</a></font></span>Upabhrit stablished air, Juhū the heaven, Dhruva supported<br>    earth securely founded.<br>   As meed, the Svarga worlds, o'erspread with fatness shall yield<br>    the sacrificer all his wishes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800406">6</a></font></span>Dhruvā, ascend thou earth the all sustainer: go thou, O Upa-<br>    bhrit, to air's mid-region.<br>   Juhu, go skyward with the sacrificer; go, and with Sruva be thy<br>    calf beside thee drain all the swelling unreluctant quarters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800407">7</a></font></span>They ford the mighty rivers by the pathway which they who<br>    sacrifice, the righteous, travel.<br>   There they gave room unto the sacrificer when they made regions<br>    and existing creatures.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800408">8</a></font></span>The Angirases' pathway is the eastern Agni, the Ādityas' path-<br>    way is the Gārhapatya:<br>   The southward Agni is the way of Southerns.<br>   To Agni's greatness whom the prayer divideth go powerful, un-<br>    scathed with all thy members.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800409">9</a></font></span>Eastward let east fire happily consume thee, and westward<br>    happily the Gārhapatya.<br>   Burn southern fire, thine armour and protection: from air's-<br>    mid-region from the north and centre, on all sides, Agni,<br>    guard thou him from horror.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800410">10</a></font></span>Do ye, with your most kindly forms, O Agni, waft, turned to<br>    rapid steeds whose ribs bear burthens,<br>   The sacrificer to the world of Svarga where with the Gods they<br>    banquet and are joyful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800411">11</a></font></span>Happily from the rear burn this man, Agni, happily from before,<br>    above, and under.<br>   One, triply parted, Jātavedas, place him happily in the world<br>    that holds the righteous.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800412">12</a></font></span>Happily lit, let fires, each Jātavedas, seize on Prajāpati's appoint-<br>    ed victim.<br>   Let them not cast it down while here they cook it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800413">13</a></font></span>Sacrifice, duly offered, comes preparing the sacrificer for the.<br>    world of Svarga,<br>   Let all the fires, each Jatavedas, welcome Prajāpati's completely<br>    offered victim.<br>   Let them not cast it down while here they cook it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800414">14</a></font></span>Fain to fly up from the sky's ridge to heaven, the worshipper<br>    hath mounted visible Agni. <a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="green">p. 203</font></a><br>   Lucid from out the mist to him, the pious, gleams the God-<br>    travelled path that leads to Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800415">15</a></font></span>On thy right hand let Indra be thy Brāhman, Brihaspati Adh-<br>    varyu Agni Hotar.<br>   This ordered sacrifice goes offered thither whither presented<br>    gifts have gone aforetime.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800416">16</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and milk here let the Charu rest.<br>   World-makers, makers of the path we worship you of the Gods<br>    who here partake oblations.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800417">17</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and curds, etc. (as in stanza 16).<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800418">18</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and drops, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800419">19</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and butter, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800420">20</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and flesh, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800421">21</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and food, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800422">22</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and mead, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800423">23</a></font></span>Enriched with cake and juice, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800424">24</a></font></span>Here, mixt with cake and water rest the Charu!<br>   World-makers, makers of the path, we worship those Gods of<br>    you who here partake oblations.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800425">25</a></font></span>Let these which Gods have held for thee, these beakers covered<br>    o'er with cake,<br>   Be full of sacred food for thee, distilling fatness, rich in mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800426">26</a></font></span>Grains which for thee I scatter, mixt with Sesamum, as holy<br>    food.<br>   May they for thee be excellent and potent. King Yama look on,<br>    them as thine with favour!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800427">27</a></font></span>More immortality!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800428">28</a></font></span>On all the earth, to heaven, the drop descended, on this place<br>    and on that which was before it.<br>   I offer up, throughout the seven oblations, the drop which still<br>    to one same place is moving.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800429">29</a></font></span>Those who observe men look on wealth as Vāyu with countless.<br>    streams, and as light-finding Arka;<br>   Those drain out Guerdon sprung from seven mothers, who satis-<br>    fy and evermore give presents.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800430">30</a></font></span>They for their weal drain out the cask, the beaker four-holed,.<br>    the milch-cow Idā full of sweetness,<br>   Injure not, Agni, in the loftiest heaven Aditi heightening strength<br>    among the people.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800431">31</a></font></span>On thee doth Savitar the God bestow this vesture for thy wear. <a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="green">p. 204</font></a><br>   Clothe thee herein, and find meet robe in Yama's realm to cover<br>    thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800432">32</a></font></span>The grains of corn have now become a cow, the Sesamum her<br>    calf.<br>   He in the realm of Yama lives on her the inexhaustible.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800433">33</a></font></span>Let these become thy milch-kine, man! supplying all thy heart's<br>    desires.<br>   There, speckled, white, like-hued and various-coloured, with<br>    calves of Sesamum let them stand beside thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800434">34</a></font></span>Let the green grains become thy white, and speckled, The dusky<br>    corns become thy ruddy milch-kine.<br>   Let those with calves of Sesamum for ever yield strength to him<br>    and never flinch from milking.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800435">35</a></font></span>I offer in Vaisvānara this oblation, thousandfold spring that<br>    pours a hundred steamlets.<br>   This with a swelling flow supports the Father, supports grand-<br>    fathers and their sires before them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800436">36</a></font></span>Beside the spring with hundred, thousand currents, expanding<br>    on the summit of the water,<br>   Exhaustless, yielding strength, never reluctant, the Fathers with<br>    their sacred food are seated.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800437">37</a></font></span>This pile of wood, collected, heaped together, regard it, O ye,<br>    kinsmen, and come near it.<br>   To immortality this mortal goeth: prepare a home for him, all<br>    ye his kindred.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800438">38</a></font></span>Be here, even here, acquiring wealth, here be thou thoughts here<br>    be thou strength.<br>   Be stronger here in manly power, life-giver, never beaten back.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800439">39</a></font></span>Giving the son and grandson satisfaction, let these the present<br>   Waters full of sweetness,<br>   Pouring forth food and Amrit for the Fathers, refresh both these<br>    and those, the Goddess Waters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800440">40</a></font></span>40. Waters, send Agni forward to the Fathers: let them accept the<br>    sacrifice I offer.<br>   May they who follow Vigour that abideth there send us down<br>    wealth with full store of heroes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800441">41</a></font></span>Lover of butter, deathless, him, Oblation-bearer, they inflame.<br>   He knoweth well the treasured stores gone to the Fathers, far<br>    away. <a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="green">p. 205</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800442">42</a></font></span>The mingled draught, the mess of rice, the flesh which I present<br>    to thee,<br>   May these be full of food for thee, distilling fatness, rich in<br>    sweets.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800443">43</a></font></span>Grains which for thee I scatter, mixt with Sesamum as sacred<br>    food,<br>   May these for thee be excellent and potent. King Yama look on,<br>    them, as thine, with favour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800444">44</a></font></span>This is the ancient, this the recent pathway, by which thy sires<br>    of olden time departed.<br>   They who first travelled it, and they who followed, convey thee<br>    to the world where dwell the righteous.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800445">45</a></font></span>The pious call Sarasvati: they worship Sarasvati while sacrifice<br>    proceedeth.<br>   The righteous doers of good deeds invoke her: Sarasvati send<br>    bliss to him who giveth!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800446">46</a></font></span>Approaching on the south our solemn worship, the Fathers call-<br>   Sarasvati to hear them.<br>   Sit on this holy grass and be ye joyful: give thou us strengthen-<br>    ing food that brings no sickness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800447">47</a></font></span>Sarasvati, who tamest with them, joying in hymns and food, O<br>   Goddess, with the Fathers,<br>   Here give the Sacrificer growth of riches, a portion, worth a<br>    thousand, of refreshment.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800448">48</a></font></span>As Prithivī rests on earth, so do I seat thee. May the God Dhātar<br>    lengthen our existence.<br>   For you may he who parts in turn find treasures, but let the<br>   Dead among the Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800449">49</a></font></span>Depart ye two: wipe ye away whatever omens of evil fortune<br>    here have told you.<br>   Go from this man, both Steers, to him who wills it: ye are my<br>    joys here by the giver's Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800450">50</a></font></span>From a good quarter have we gained this guerdon, gift of this<br>    man, strength giving, plenteous milker.<br>   Bringing in youth old age unto the living, may she bear these<br>    together to the Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800451">51</a></font></span>I bring this clipped grass hither for the Fathers: grass living,.<br>    higher, for the Gods I scatter.<br>   Mount this, O man, as victim: let the Fathers recognize thee:<br>    when thou hast travelled yonder. <a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="green">p. 206</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800452">52</a></font></span>Set on this grass thou hast become a victim. Fathers shall know<br>    thee yonder when they meet thee.<br>   Gather thy body, limb by limb, together: I by the power of<br>    prayer arrange thy members.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800453">53</a></font></span>The royal Parna is the caldrons' cover: strength have we gained,<br>    force, power, and might, and vigour.<br>   Bestowing length of life upon the living, for long existence<br>    through a hundred autumns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800454">54</a></font></span>The share of vigour which gave this man being, the stone won<br>    lordship over foods that nourish.<br>   Hymn this with your oblations, Visvāmitras: may he, may Yama,<br>    lengthen our existence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800455">55</a></font></span>As the Five Races of mankind for Yama set apart a house.<br>   Even so I set a house apart that greater numbers may be mine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800456">56</a></font></span>Take thou and wear this piece of gold, the gold thy father used<br>    to wear.<br>   Wipe tenderly the right hand of thy sire who goes away to<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800457">57</a></font></span>To all, the living and the dead, all that are born, the worshipful.<br>   Let the full brook of fatness run, o'erflowing, with stream of<br>    mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800458">58</a></font></span>Far-seeing he flows on, the Bull, the Lord of hymns, promoter<br>    of the Sun, of Days, of Dawns, of Heaven.<br>   Breath of the rivers he hath roared into the jars, and through<br>    his wisdom entered into Indra's heart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800459">59</a></font></span>Let thy bright smoke envelop thee, spread forth, O Bright One,<br>    in the sky.<br>   For, Purifier, like the Sun thou shinest with thy radiant glow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800460">60</a></font></span>Indu is moving forth to Indra's destined place, and slights not<br>    as a friend the promise of his friend.<br>   Thou, Soma, comest nigh as bridegroom meets the bride, reach-<br>    ing the beaker by a course of hundred paths.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800461">61</a></font></span>Well have they eaten and rejoiced: their dear ones have they<br>    shaken off.<br>   Sages, self-luminous, have praised: we who are youngest<br>    supplicate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800462">62</a></font></span>Come hither, Fathers, who deserve the Soma, by the deep path-<br>    ways which the Fathers travel.<br>   Bestow upon us life and store of children, and favour us with<br>    increase of our riches. <a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="green">p. 207</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800463">63</a></font></span>Depart, O Fathers, ye who merit Soma, by the deep pathways<br>    which the Fathers travel;<br>   But in a month, rich in fair sons and heroes, come back into<br>    our homes to eat oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800464">64</a></font></span>If Agni Jātavedas, as he bore you hence to the Fathers' world,<br>    hath left one single.<br>   Limb of your bodies, here do I restore it. Fathers, rejoice in<br>    heaven with all your members!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800465">65</a></font></span>Meet for men's praises, Agni Jātavedas was sent as envoy when<br>    the day was closing.<br>   Thou gavest to the Fathers with oblation. They ate; eat, God,<br>    our offered sacrifices.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800466">66</a></font></span>Here hast thou left thy heart; O man, as sisters leave their little<br>    pet. Do thou, O earth, envelop him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800467">67</a></font></span>Bright be to thee those worlds where dwell the Fathers! I seat<br>    thee in that sphere which they inhabit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800468">68</a></font></span>Thou art the grass whereon our Fathers seat them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800469">69</a></font></span>Loosen, O Varuna, the bond that binds us; loosen the bond<br>    above, between, and under.<br>   Then under thy protection, O Āditya, may we be sinless and<br>    restored to freedom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800470">70</a></font></span>From all those bonds, O Varuna, release us, wherewith a man<br>    is bound at length and cross-wise.<br>   Then may we live a hundred autumn seasons guarded by thee,<br>   O King, by thee protected.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800471">71</a></font></span>To Agni, bearer of oblation to the Manes, be Hail! and<br>    homage!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800472">72</a></font></span>To Soma connected with the Fathers Hail! and homage!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800473">73</a></font></span>To the Fathers connected with Soma Hail! and homage!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800474">74</a></font></span>To Yama connected with the Fathers Hail! and homage!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800475">75</a></font></span>To thee, O Great-grandfather, and those with thee be this cry of<br>   Hail<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800476">76</a></font></span>To thee, Great-grandfather, and to those with thee be this cry of<br>   Hail!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800477">77</a></font></span>To thee, O Fathers, be this cry of Hail!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800478">78</a></font></span>Hail to the Fathers who inhabit earth!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800479">79</a></font></span>Hail to the Fathers who inhabit the firmament!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800480">80</a></font></span>Hail to the Fathers who dwell in heaven!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800481">81</a></font></span>Hail, Fathers, to your energy! Hail, Fathers, to your sap!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800482">82</a></font></span>Hail Father; to your wrath! Hail, Fathers, to your ardour! <a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="green">p. 208</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800483">83</a></font></span>Hail, Fathers, to what is awful! Hail to what is terrible in you!'<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800484">84</a></font></span>Hail, Fathers, to all that is propitious! Hail to all that is plea-<br>    sant in you!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800485">85</a></font></span>Homage to you Fathers! Hail to you, Fathers!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800486">86</a></font></span>All Fathers who are here, the Fathers here are you: let then-<br>     follow you. May ye be the most excellent of these.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800487">87</a></font></span>All living fathers who are here are we here: let them follow us.<br>   May we be the most excellent of these.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800488">88</a></font></span>Bright Agni, we will kindle thee, rich in thy splendour, fading.<br>    not.<br>   So that this glorious fuel may send forth to heaven its light for<br>    thee. Bring food to those who sing thy praise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800489">89</a></font></span>Within the waters runs the Moon, the strong-winged Eagle soars.<br>    in heaven.<br>   Ye Lightnings with your golden wheels, men find not your abid-<br>    ing-place. Hear this my call, O Heaven and Earth.</p>
