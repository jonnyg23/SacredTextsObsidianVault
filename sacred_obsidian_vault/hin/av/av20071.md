 <p><a name="page_322"><font size="1" color="green">p. 322</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007101">1</a></font></span>Mighty is Indra, yea, supreme; greatness becomes the<br>   Thunderer!<br>   Wide as the heaven extends his power;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007102">2</a></font></span>Which aideth those to win them sons who come as heroes to the<br>    fight,<br>   Or singers loving holy thoughts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007103">3</a></font></span>His belly drinking deepest draughts of Soma like an ocean<br>    swells,<br>   Like wide streams from the cope of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007107">7</a></font></span>Come, Indra, and delight thee with the juice at all the Soma<br>    feasts,<br>   Protector, mighty in thy strength.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007108">8</a></font></span>To Indra pour ye forth the juice, the active gladdening juice, to<br>    him<br>   The gladdening omnific God.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007109">9</a></font></span>O Lord of all men, fair of cheek, rejoice thee in the gladdening<br>    buds,<br>   Present at these drink-offerings.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007110">10</a></font></span>Songs have outpoured themselves to thee, Indra, the strong, the<br>    guardian Lord.<br>   And raised themselves unsatisfied.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007111">11</a></font></span>Send to us bounty manifold, O Indra, worthy of our wish,<br>   For power supreme is only thine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007112">12</a></font></span>O Indra, stimulate thereto us emulously fain for wealth.<br>   And glorious, O most splendid One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007113">13</a></font></span>Give, Indra, wide and lofty fame, wealthy in cattle and in<br>    strength,<br>   Lasting our life-time, failing not.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007114">14</a></font></span>Grant us high fame, O Indra, grant riches bestowing thousands,<br>    those<br>   Fair fruits of earth borne home in wains.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007115">15</a></font></span>Praising with songs the praise-worthy who cometh to our aid,<br>    we call<br>   Indra, the Treasure-Lord of wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007116">16</a></font></span>To lofty Indra, dweller by each libation, the pious man Sings<br>    forth aloud a strengthening hymn.</p>
