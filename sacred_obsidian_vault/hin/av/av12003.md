 <p><a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="green">p. 89</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN III</h3>  <h4 align="center">An accompaniment to the preparation and presentation of sacrificial offerings by a householder and his wife, with prayer for prosperity and happiness on earth and in heaven</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200301">1</a></font></span>Mount, male from male, the skin. Go thither: summon those<br>    whom thou lovest, one and all, to meet thee,<br>   Strong as ye were when first ye met each other, still be your<br>    strength the same in Yama's kingdom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200302">2</a></font></span>So strong your sight, so many be your powers, so great your<br>    force, your energies so many,<br>   When fire attends the body as its fuel, then may, ye gain full<br>    chargers, O ye couple.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200303">3</a></font></span>Together in this world, in God-ward pathway, together be ye in<br>    the realms of Yama.<br>   Invite, made pure with means of purifying, whatever seed of<br>    yours hath been developed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200304">4</a></font></span>Do ye, O sons, unite you with the waters, meeting this living<br>    man, ye life-sustainers,<br>   Allot to them the Odana your mother is making ready, which<br>    they call immortal.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200305">5</a></font></span>That which your mother and your sire, to banish sin and un-<br>    cleanness from their lips, are cooking.<br>   That Odana with hundred streams, sky-reaching, hath in its<br>    might prevaded earth and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200306">6</a></font></span>Live with your sons, when life on earth is ended, live in the<br>    sphere most rich in light and sweetness.<br>   In skies that have been won by sacrificers make both the worlds,<br>    earth, heaven, your habitation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200307">7</a></font></span>Approach the eastern, yea: the eastern region, this is the<br>    sphere to which the faithful turn them,<br>   Your cooked oblation that in fire was offered, together, wife and<br>    husband, meet to guard it. <a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="green">p. 90</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200308">8</a></font></span>Now, as your steps approach the southern quarter, move in.<br>    your circling course about this vessel.<br>   Herein, accordant with the Fathers, Yama shall mightily protect<br>    your cooked oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200309">9</a></font></span>Best of the regions is indeed this western wherein the King and<br>    gracious Lord is Soma.<br>   Thither resort for rest, follow the pious. Then gain the laden<br>    chargers, O ye couple.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200310">10</a></font></span>Ever victorious is the northern region: may the east quarter set<br>    us first and foremost.<br>   The Man became the five-divisioned metre. May we abide wit .<br>    all our members perfect.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200311">11</a></font></span>This stedfast realm is Queen. To her be homage! To me and<br>    to my sons may she be gracious.<br>   Guard thou, O Goddess Aditi, all-bounteous, our cooked oblation<br>    as an active warder.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200312">12</a></font></span>Embrace us as a father clasps his children. Here on the Earth<br>    let kindly breezes fan us.<br>   Let the rice-mess these two cook here, O Goddess, know this<br>    our truthfulness and zealous fervour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200313">13</a></font></span>If the dark bird hath come to us and, stealing the hanging. .<br>    morsel, settled in his dwelling,<br>   Or if the slave-girl hath, wet-handed, smearing the pestle and<br>    the mortar, cleansed the waters,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200314">14</a></font></span>This pressing-stone, broad-based and strength-bestowing, made<br>    pure by cleansing means, shall chase the demon.<br>   Mount on the skin: afford us great protection, Let not the sons'<br>    sin fall on wife and husband.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200315">15</a></font></span>Together with the Gods, banning Pis5chas and demons, hath<br>   Vanaspati come hither.<br>   He shall rise up and send his voice out loudly. May we win all<br>    the worlds with him to help us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200316">16</a></font></span>Seven victims held the sacrificial essence, the bright one and the<br>    one that hath grown feeble.<br>   The three-and-thirty Deities attend them. As such, conduct us<br>    io the world of Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200317">17</a></font></span>Unto the world of Svarga shalt thou lead us: there may we dwell<br>    beside our wife and children.<br>   I take thy hand Let not Destruction, let not Malignity come<br>    hither and subdue us. <a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="green">p. 91</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200318">18</a></font></span>We have subdued that sinful-hearted Grāhi. Thou shalt speak<br>    sweetly having chased the darkness.<br>   Let not the wooden gear made ready fail us, nor harm the grain<br>    of rice that pays due worship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200319">19</a></font></span>Soon to be, decked with butter, all-embracing, come to this<br>    world wherewith birth unites thee.<br>   Seize thou the winnowing-fan which rains have nourished, and<br>    let this separate the chaff and refuse.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200320">20</a></font></span>Three worlds hath Power Divine marked out and measured,<br>   Fheaven yonder, and the earth, and airs mid-region.<br>   Grasp ye the stalks and in your hands retain them: let them be<br>    watered and again be winnowed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200321">21</a></font></span>Manifold, various are the shapes of victims. Thou growest uni-<br>    form by great abundance.<br>   Push thou away this skin of ruddy colour: the stone will cleanse<br>    as one who cleanses raiment.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200322">22</a></font></span>Earth upon earth l set thee. This thy body is con-substantial,.<br>    but in form it differs.<br>   Whate'er hath been worn off or scratched in fixing, leak not<br>    thereat: I spread a charm to mend it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200323">23</a></font></span>Thou for thy son shalt yearn as yearns a mother. I lay thee<br>    down and with the earth unite thee.<br>   Conjoined with sacrificial gear and butter may pot and jar stand<br>    firmly on the altar.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200324">24</a></font></span>Eastward may Agni as he cooks preserve thee. Southward may<br>   Indra, grit by Maruts, guard thee,<br>   Varuna strengthen and support thee westward, and Soma on<br>    the north hold thee together.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200325">25</a></font></span>Drops flow, made pure by filters, from the rain-cloud: to heaven<br>    and earth and to the worlds they travel,<br>   May Indra light them up, poured in the vessel, lively and sted-<br>    fast, quickening living creatures.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200326">26</a></font></span>From heaven they come, they visit earth, and rising from earth<br>    unite themselves with air's mid-region,<br>   Purified, excellent, they with shine in beauty. Thus may they lead us<br>    to the world of Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200327">27</a></font></span>Yea, and supreme, alike in conformation, and brilliant and<br>    refulgent and immortal,<br>   As such, enjoined, well-guarding, water-givers, dress ye the<br>   Odana for wife and husband. <a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="green">p. 92</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200328">28</a></font></span>Numbered, they visit earth, these drops of moisture, commensu-<br>    rate with plants and vital breathings,<br>   Unnumbered, scattered, beautiful in colour, the bright, ones<br>    have pervaded all refulgence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200329">29</a></font></span>Heated, they rage and boil in agitation, they cast about their<br>    foam and countless bubbles<br>   Like a fond woman when she sees her husband—what time ye<br>    waters and these rice-grains mingle,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200330">30</a></font></span>Take up these rice-grains lying at the bottom: led them be blent<br>    and mingled with the waters.<br>   This water I have measured in the vessel, if as mid-points the<br>    rice-grains have been meted.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200331">31</a></font></span>Present the sickle: quickly bring it hither. Let them out plants<br>    and joints with hands that harm not.<br>   So may the plants be free from wrath against us, they o'er<br>    whose realm Soma hath won dominion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200332">32</a></font></span>Strew ye fresh grass for the boiled rice to rest on: fair let it be,<br>    sweet to the eye and spirit.<br>   Hither come Goddesses with Gods, and sitting here taste in<br>    proper season this oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200333">33</a></font></span>On the strewn grass. Vanaspati, be seated; commensurate with<br>   Gods and Agnishtomas.<br>   Let thy fair form, wrought as by Tvashtar's hatchet, mark these<br>    that yearn for thee within the vessel.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200334">34</a></font></span>In sixty autumns may the Treasure-Guardian seek to gain<br>    heavenly light by cooked oblation.<br>   On this may sons and fathers live dependent. Send thou this<br>    mess to Fire that leads to heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200335">35</a></font></span>On the earth's breast stand firmly as supporter: may Deities<br>    stir thee who ne'er hast shaken.<br>   So living man and wife with living children remove thee from<br>    the hearth of circling Agni.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200336">36</a></font></span>All wishes that have blessed those with fulfilment, having won<br>    all the worlds have met together.<br>   Let them plunge in both stirring-spoon and ladle: raise this and<br>    set it in a single vessel.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200337">37</a></font></span>Pour out the covering butter, spread it eastward: sprinkle this<br>    vessel over with the fatness.<br>   Greet this, ye Deities, with gentle murmur, as lowing cows wel-<br>    come their tender suckling. <a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="green">p. 93</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200338">38</a></font></span>Thou hast poured oil and made the worlds: let heaven, unequal-<br>    led, be spread out in wide extension.<br>   Herein be cooked the buffalo, strong-pinioned: the Gods shall<br>    give the Deities this oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200339">39</a></font></span>Whate'er thy wife, away from thee, makes ready, or what, O<br>    wife, apart from thee, thy husband,<br>   Combine it all: let it be yours in common while ye produce one<br>    world with joint endeavour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200340">40</a></font></span>All these now dwelling on the earth, mine offspring, these whom,<br>    this woman here, my wife, hath borne me,<br>   Invite them all unto the vessel: knowing their kinship have the<br>    children met together.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200341">41</a></font></span>Swollen with savoury meath, the stream of treasures, sources of<br>    immortality blent with fatness<br>   Soma retains all these; in sixty autumns the Guardian Lord of<br>   Treasures may desire them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200342">42</a></font></span>The Lord of Treasures may desire this treasure: lordless on.<br>    every side be all the others.<br>   Our mess, presented seeking heaven, hath mounted in three<br>    divisions all three realms of Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200343">43</a></font></span>May Agni burn the God-denying demon: let no carnivorous.<br>   Pis icha drink here.<br>   We drive him off, we keep him at a distance. Ādityas and<br>   Angirases pursue him!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200344">44</a></font></span>This meath do I announce, mingled with butter, to the Angi-<br>    rases and the Ādityas.<br>   With pure hands ne'er laid roughly on a Brahman go, pious.<br>    couple, to the world of Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200345">45</a></font></span>Of this have I obtained the noblest portion from that same<br>    world whence Parmeshthin gained it.<br>   Pour forth, besprinkle butter rich in fatness: the share of<br>   Angiras is here before us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200346">46</a></font></span>To Deities, to Truth, to holy Fervour this treasure we consign,.<br>    this rich deposit,<br>   At play, in meeting led it not desert us, never give out to anyone<br>    besides me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200347">47</a></font></span>I cook the offering, I present oblation: only my wife attends<br>    the holy service.<br>   A youthful world, a son hath been begotten. Begin a life that<br>    brings success and triumph. <a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="green">p. 94</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200348">48</a></font></span>There is no fault in this, no reservation, none when it goes with<br>    friends in close alliance.<br>   We have laid down this vessel in perfection: the cooked mess<br>    shall re-enter him who cooked it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200349">49</a></font></span>To those we love may we do acts that please them. Away to<br>    darkness go all those who hate us!<br>   Cow, ox, and strength of every kind approach us! Thus let<br>    them banish death of human beings.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200350">50</a></font></span>Perfectly do the Agnis know each other, one visitor of plants<br>    and one of rivers,<br>   And all the Gods who shine and glow in heaven. Gold is the<br>    light of him who cooks oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200351">51</a></font></span>Man hath received this skin of his from nature: of other<br>    animals not one is naked.<br>   Ye make him clothe himself with might for raiment. Odana's<br>    mouth is a home-woven vesture.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200352">52</a></font></span>Whatever thou may say at dice, in meeting, whatever falsehood<br>    through desire of riches,<br>   Ye two, about one common warp uniting, deposit all impurity<br>    within it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200353">53</a></font></span>Win thou the rain: approach the Gods. Around thee thou from<br>    the skin shalt make the smoke rise upward.<br>   Soon to be, decked with butter, all-embracing, come to this<br>    world wherewith one birth unites thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200354">54</a></font></span>In many a shape hath heaven transformed its body, as in itself<br>    is known, of varied eolour.<br>   Cleansing the bright, the dark form hath it banished: the red<br>    form in the fire to thee I offer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200355">55</a></font></span>To the eastern region, to Agni the Regent, to Asita the Protector,<br>   Āditya the Archer, we present thee, this offering of ours. Do<br>    ye preserve it from aggression<br>   To full old age may Destiny conduct us; may full old age deliver<br>    us to Mrityu. Then may we be with our prepared oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200356">56</a></font></span>To the southern region, to Indra the Regent, to Tiraschirāji the<br>   Protector, to Yama the Archer, we present, etc. (as in stanza<br>    55)<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200357">57</a></font></span>To the western region, to Varuna the Regent, to Pridāku the<br>   Protector, to Food the Archer, we present, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200358">58</a></font></span>To the northern region, to Soma the Regent, to Svaja the Protec-<br>    tor, to Thunderbolt the Archer, we present, etc. <a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="green">p. 95</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200359">59</a></font></span>To the stedfast region, to Vishnu the Regent, to Kalmāshagriva<br>    the Protector, to Plants the Archers, we present, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200360">60</a></font></span>To the upper region, to Brihaspati the Regent, to Svitra the<br>   Protector, to Rain the Archer, we present thee, this offering<br>    of ours. Do ye preserve it from aggression.<br>   To full old age may Destiny conduct us, may full old age deliver<br>    us to Mrityu. Then may we be with our prepared oblation.</p>
