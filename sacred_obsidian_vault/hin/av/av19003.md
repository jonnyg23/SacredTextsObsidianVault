 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN III</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Agni for protection and prosperity</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900301">1</a></font></span>Whithersoe'er, from sky, earth, air's mid-regions from plants ands<br>    herbs, from tall trees, Jātavedas.<br>   Is carried here and there to divers places, even thence come thou<br>    to us with loving-kindness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900302">2</a></font></span>All majesty of thine in floods, in forest, in plants, in cattle, in<br>    the depths of waters<br>   Closely uniting all thy forms, O Agni, come unto us wealth-giv-<br>    ing, undecaying.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900303">3</a></font></span>Thy majesty among the Gods in Svarga, thy body which hath<br>    past into the Fathers.<br>   Thy plenty far-diffused mid human beings, even with this, O<br>   Agni, give us riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900304">4</a></font></span>To him the wise, the famous, swift to listen, with words and<br>    verses I come nigh for bounty.<br>   May we be safe from threatening danger. Soften by sacrifice the-<br>    wrath of Gods, O Agni.</p>
