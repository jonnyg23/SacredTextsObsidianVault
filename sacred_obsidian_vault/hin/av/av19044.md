 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A curative and protective charm</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904401">1</a></font></span>Thou art the lengthening of life, thy name is Universal Cure:<br>   Then, Ointment! send felicity; Waters, send happiness and<br>    peace.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904402">2</a></font></span>The yellow hue, the feverish heat, the shooting pain that rends II<br>    the limbs,<br>   All the consumptive malady let the Ointment drive from out<br>    thy frame.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904403">3</a></font></span>Let the Salve born upon the earth, benignant, giving life to man.<br>   Make the swift rider on the car sinless, exempt from sudden<br>    death.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904404">4</a></font></span>Preserve our breath, O Vital Breath, have mercy on our life, O<br>   Life.<br>   From snares of Nirriti do thou, O Nirriti, deliver us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904405">5</a></font></span>Thou art the babe of Sindhu, thou art lightnings' flower, wind,<br>    breath, and Sun: thou art the eye and milk of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904406">6</a></font></span>Gods' Ointment from the Three Peaked Hill, preserve thou me<br>    on every side.<br>   No plants of earth surpass thee, none from mountain or from<br>    cultured ground.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904407">7</a></font></span>Now hath it gently crept within, fiend-slaying, chasing malady.<br>   And driving all diseases hence, and evil omens, banished them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904408">8</a></font></span>Full many a falsehood, O thou King Varuna, man hath uttered<br>    here:<br>   Do thou who hast a thousand powers preserve us from that<br>    misery. <a name="page_247"><font size="1" color="green">p. 247</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904409">9</a></font></span>If we have cried, O Waters! Cows! if we have cried, O<br>   Varuna!<br>   For this endowed with thousand powers! deliver us from<br>    misery.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904410">10</a></font></span>Mitra and Varuna, O Salve have closely followed after thee<br>   May they, when they have followed thee afar, restore thee for<br>    our use.</p>
