 <p><a name="page_33"><font size="1" color="green">p. 33</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IX</h3>  <h4 align="center">The Sataudanā or Hundredfold Oblation</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000901">1</a></font></span>Binding the mouths of those who threaten mischief, against my<br>    rivals cast this bolt of thunder,<br>   Indra first gave the Hundredfold Oblation, welfare of him who<br>    worships, foe-destroying.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000902">2</a></font></span>Thy skin shall be the Altar; let thine hair become the Sacred<br>   Grass.<br>   This cord hath held thee firmly: let this pressing-stone dance<br>    round on thee:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000903">3</a></font></span>The holy water be thy hair: let thy tongue make thee clean, O<br>   Cow.<br>   Go, Hundredfold Oblation, made bright and adorable, to hea-<br>    ven. <a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="green">p. 34</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000904">4</a></font></span>He who prepares the Hundredfold Oblation gains each wish<br>    thereby:<br>   For all his ministering priests, contented, move as fitteth them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000905">5</a></font></span>He rises up to heaven, ascends to younder third celestial<br>    height.<br>   Whoever gives the Hundredfold Oblation with the central<br>    cake.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000906">6</a></font></span>That man completely wins those worlds, both of the heavens<br>    and of the earth,<br>   Whoever pays the Hundredfold. Oblation with its golden light.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000907">7</a></font></span>Thine Immolators, Goddess! and the men who dress thee for<br>    the feast, all these will guard thee, Hundredfold Oblation!<br>   Have no fear of them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000908">8</a></font></span>The Vasus from the South will be thy guards, the Maruts from<br>    the North,<br>   Ādityas from the West; o'ertake and pass the Agnishtoma,<br>    thou!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000909">9</a></font></span>The Gods, the Fathers, mortal men, Gandharvas, and Apsara-<br>    ses,<br>   All these will be the guards: o'ertake and pass the Atirātra,<br>    thou!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000910">10</a></font></span>The man who pays the Hundredfold Oblation winneth all the<br>    worlds,<br>   Air, heaven, and earth, Ādityas, and Maruts, and regions of the<br>    sky.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000911">11</a></font></span>Sprinkling down fatness, to the Gods will the beneficent God-<br>    dess go.<br>   Harm not thy dresser, Cow! To heaven, O Hundredfold Obla-<br>    tion, speed!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000912">12</a></font></span>From all the Gods enthroned in heaven, in air, from those who<br>    dwell on earth,<br>   Draw forth for evermore a stream of milk, of butter, and of<br>    mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000913">13</a></font></span>Let thy head, let thy mouth, let both thine ears, and those two<br>    jaws of thine.<br>   Pour for the giver mingled curd, and flowing butter, milk, and<br>    mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000914">14</a></font></span>Let both thy lips, thy nostrils, both thy horns, and these two<br>    eyes of thine. <a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="green">p. 35</font></a><br>   Pour for the given, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000915">15</a></font></span>Let heart and pericardium, let thy lungs with all the bronchial<br>    tubes, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000916">16</a></font></span>Let liver, and let kidneys, let thine entrails, and the parts within,<br>    etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000917">17</a></font></span>Let rectum and omentum, let thy belly's hollows, and thy skin,<br>    etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000918">18</a></font></span>Let all thy marrow, every bone, let all thy flesh, and all thy<br>    blood, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000919">19</a></font></span>Let both thy shoulders and thy hump, thy forelegs, and their<br>    lower parts, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000920">20</a></font></span>Let neck and nape and shoulder-joints, thy ribs and inter-costal<br>    parts, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000921">21</a></font></span>So let thy thighs and thy knee-bones, thy hinder quarters, and<br>    thy hips, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000922">22</a></font></span>So let thy tail and all the hairs thereof, thine udder, and thy<br>    teats, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000923">23</a></font></span>Let all thy legs, the refuse of thy feet, thy heelropes, and thy<br>    hooves.<br>   Pour for the giver mingled curd, and flowing butter milk, and<br>    mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000924">24</a></font></span>Let all thy skin, Sataudanā! let every hair thou hast, O Cow,<br>   Pour for the giver mingled curd, and flowing butter, milk, and<br>    mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000925">25</a></font></span>Sprinkled with molten butter, let the two meal-cakes be sport<br>    for thee.<br>   Make them thy wings, O Goddess, and bear him who dresses<br>    thee to heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000926">26</a></font></span>Each grain of rice in mortar or on pestle, all on the skin or in<br>    the winnowing-basket,<br>   Whatever purifying Mātarisvan, the Wind, hath sifted, let the<br>   Hotar Agni make of it an acceptable oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000927">27</a></font></span>In the priest's hands I lay, in separate order, the sweet celestial<br>   Waters, dropping fatness.<br>   As here I sprinkle them may all my wishes be granted unto me<br>    in perfect fulness. May we have ample wealth in our posses-<br>    sion.</p>
