 <p><a name="page_319"><font size="1" color="green">p. 319</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006901">1</a></font></span>May he stand by us in our need and in abundance for our<br>    wealth:<br>   With riches may he come to us;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006902">2</a></font></span>Whose pair of tawny horses yoked in battles foemen challenge<br>    not:<br>   To him, to Indra, sing your song.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006903">3</a></font></span>Nigh to the Soma-drinker come, for his enjoyment, these bright<br>    drops,<br>   The Somas mingled with the curd.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006904">4</a></font></span>Thou, grown at once to perfect strength, wast born to drink the<br>   Soma juice, strong Indra, for preeminence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006905">5</a></font></span>O Indra, lover of the song, may these quick Somas enter thee:<br>   May they bring bliss to thee the Sage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006900">0</a></font></span>6. O Lord of Hundred Powers, our chants of praise and lauds have<br>    strengthened thee:<br>   So strengthen thee the songs we sing!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006907">7</a></font></span>Indra, whose succour never fails, accept this treasure thousand-<br>    fold,<br>   Wherein all manly powers abide.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006908">8</a></font></span>O Indra, thou who lovest song, let no man hurt our bodies,<br>    keep.<br>   Slaughter far from us, for thou canst.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006912">12</a></font></span>Thereafter they, as is their wont, threw off the state of babes<br>    urborn,<br>   Taking their sacrificial name.</p>
