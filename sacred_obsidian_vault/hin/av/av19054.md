 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LIV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Kāla; a continuation of the preceding hymn</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905401">1</a></font></span>From Kāla sprang the Waters, sprang the regions, Brahma,<br>   Holy Fire.<br>   The Sun ascends by Kāla, and in Kāla sinks again to rest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905402">2</a></font></span>By Kāla freshly blows the wind, mighty through Kāla is the<br>   Earth: on Kāla rests the mighty Sky.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905403">3</a></font></span>In Kāla erst the text produced what is and what is yet to be.<br>   From Kāla sprang the Richas, and from Kāla was the Yajus<br>    born. <a name="page_256"><font size="1" color="green">p. 256</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905404">4</a></font></span>They formed in Kāla sacrifice, eternal portion for the Gods.<br>   In Kāla the Gandharvas and Apsarasas and worlds abide.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905405">5</a></font></span>Atharvan and this Angiras in Kāla are supreme o'er heaven.<br>   Both this world and the world that is most lofty, the pure<br>    worlds and pure intermediate spaces,—<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905406">6</a></font></span>Yea, having conquered all the worlds by Brahma, Kāla as God<br>   Supreme is supplicated.</p>
