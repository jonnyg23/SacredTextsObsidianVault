 <p><a name="page_360"><font size="1" color="green">p. 360</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012601">1</a></font></span>Men have abstained from pouring juice; nor counted Indra as a<br>   God.<br>   Where at the votary's store my friend Vrishākapi hath drunk his<br>    fill. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012602">2</a></font></span>Thou, Indra, heedless passest by the ill Vrishākapi hath<br>    wrought;<br>   Yet nowhere else thou findest place wherein to drink the Soma<br>    juice. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012603">3</a></font></span>What hath he done to injure thee, this tawny beast Vrishākapi,<br>   With whom thou art so angry now? What is the votary's food-<br>    ful store? Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012604">4</a></font></span>Soon may the hound who hunts the boar seize him and bite him<br>    in the ear,<br>   O Indra, that Vrishākapi whom thou protectest as a friend.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012605">5</a></font></span>Kapi hath marred the beauteous things, all deftly wrought, that<br>    were my joy.<br>   In pieces will I rend his head; the sinner's portion shall be woe.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012606">6</a></font></span>No dame hath ampler charms than I, or greater wealth of love's<br>    delights.<br>   None with more ardour offers all her beauty to her lord's<br>    embrace. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012607">7</a></font></span>Mother whose love is quickly won,I say what verily will be,<br>   My breast, O mother, and my head and both my hips seem<br>    quivering Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012608">8</a></font></span>Dame with the lovely hands and arms, with broad hair-plaits<br>    and ample hips,<br>   Why, O thou hero's wife, art thou angry with our Vrishākapi?<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012609">9</a></font></span>This noxious creature looks on me as one bereft of hero's love. <a name="page_361"><font size="1" color="green">p. 361</font></a><br>   Yet heroes for my sons have I, the Maruts' friend and Indra's<br>   Queen Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012610">10</a></font></span>From olden time the matron goes to feast and general sacrifice.<br>   Mother of heroes, Indra's Queen, the rite's ordainer is extolled.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012611">11</a></font></span>So have I heard Indrāni called most fortunate among these<br>    dames,<br>   For never shall her Consort die in future time through length<br>    of days. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012612">12</a></font></span>Never, Indrāni have I joyed without my friend Vrishākapi,<br>   Whose welcome offering here, made pure with water, goeth to<br>    the Gods. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012613">13</a></font></span>Wealthy Vrishākapāyi, blest with sons and consorts of thy sons,<br>   Indra will eat thy bulls, thy dear oblation that effecteth much.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012614">14</a></font></span>Fifteen in number, then, for me a score of bullocks they<br>    prepare.<br>   And I devour the fat thereof: they fill my belly full with food.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012615">15</a></font></span>Like as a bull with pointed horn, loud bellowing amid the herds,<br>   Sweet to thine heart, O Indra, is the brew which she who tends<br>    thee pours. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012616">16</a></font></span>Indrāni speaks. Non ille fortis (ad Venerem) est cujus mentula<br>    laxe inter femora dependet; fortis vero estille cujus, quum<br>    sederit, membrum pilosum se extendit. Super omnia est<br>    Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012617">17</a></font></span>Indra speaks. Non fortis est ille cujus, quum sederit, membrum<br>    pilosum se extendit: fortis vero est ille cujus mentula laxe<br>    inter femora dependet. Super omnia est Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012618">18</a></font></span>O Indra, this Vrishākapi hath found a slain wild animal,<br>   Dresser, and new-made pan, and knife, and wagon with a load<br>    of wood. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012619">19</a></font></span>Distinguishing the Dāsa and the Arya, viewing all, I go.<br>   I look upon the wise, and drink the simple votary's Soma juice.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012620">20</a></font></span>The desert plains and steep descents, how many leagues in length<br>    they spread!<br>   Go to the nearest houses, go unto thine home, Vrishākapi.<br>   Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012621">21</a></font></span>Turn thee again Vrishākapi; we twain will bring thee happiness.<br>   Thou goest homeward on thy way along this path which leads<br>    to sleep. Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012622">22</a></font></span>When, Indra and Vrishākapi, ye travelled upward to your home,<br>   Where was that noisome beast, to whom went it, the beast that<br>    troubles man? Supreme is Indra over all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012623">23</a></font></span>Daughter of Manu, Parsu bare a score of children at a birth. <a name="page_362"><font size="1" color="green">p. 362</font></a><br>   Her portion verily was bliss although her burthen caused her<br>    grief.</p>
