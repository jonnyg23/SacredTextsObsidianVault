 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008501">1</a></font></span>Glorify naught besides, O friends; so shall no sorrow trouble<br>    you.<br>   Praise only mighty Indra when the juice is shed, and say your<br>    lauds repeatedly:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008502">2</a></font></span>Even him, eternal, like a bull who rushes down, men's con-<br>    queror, bounteous like a cow;<br>   Him who is cause of both, of enmity and peace, to both sides<br>    most munificent.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008503">3</a></font></span>Although these men in sundry ways invoke thee to obtain thine<br>    aid.<br>   Be this our prayer, addressed, O Indra, unto thee, thine exalta-<br>    tion every day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008504">4</a></font></span>Those skilled in song, O Maghavan, among these men o'ercome<br>    with might the foeman's songs,<br>   Come hither, bring us strength in many a varied form most near<br>    that it may succour us.</p>
