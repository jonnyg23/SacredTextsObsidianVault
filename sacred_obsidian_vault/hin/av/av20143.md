 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXLIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">Hymn to the Asvins</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014301">1</a></font></span>We invocate this day your car, far-spreading, O Asvins, even<br>    the gathering of the sunlight,<br>   Car praised in hymns, most ample, rich in treasure, fitted with<br>    seats, the car that beareth Sūryā.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014302">2</a></font></span>Asvins, ye gained that glory by your Godhead, ye Sons of<br>   Heaven, by your own might and power.<br>   Food followeth close upon your bright appearing when stately<br>    horses in your chariot draw you.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014303">3</a></font></span>Who bringeth you to-day for help with offered oblations, or with<br>    hymns to drink the juices?<br>   Who, for the sacrifice's ancient lover, turneth you hither, Asvins,<br>    offering homage?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014304">4</a></font></span>Borne on your golden car, ye omnipresent! come to this sacrifice<br>    of ours, Nāsatyas.<br>   Drink of the pleasant liquor of the Soma: give riches to the<br>    people who adore you.<a name="page_379"><font size="1" color="green">p. 379</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014305">5</a></font></span>Come hitherward to us from earth, from heaven, borne on your<br>    golden chariot rolling lightly.<br>   Suffer not other worshippers to stay you: here are ye bound by<br>    earlier bonds of friendship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014306">6</a></font></span>Now for us both, mete out, O Wonder-Workers, riches exceed-<br>    ing great with store of heroes,<br>   Because the men have sent you praise, O Asvins, and Ajamīlhas<br>    come to the laudation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014307">7</a></font></span>Whene'er I gratified you here together, your grace was given us,<br>   O ye rich in booty.<br>   Protect, ye twain, the singer of your praises: to you, Nāsatyas,<br>    is my wish directed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014308">8</a></font></span>Sweet be the plants for us, the heavens, the waters, and full of<br>    sweets for us be air's mid-region!<br>   May the Field's Lord for us be full of sweetness, and may we<br>    follow after him uninjured.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014309">9</a></font></span>Asvins, that work of yours deserves our wonder, the Bull of<br>    firmament and earth and heaven;<br>   Yes, and your thousand promises in battle. Come near to all<br>    these men and drink beside us.</p>    </body>
