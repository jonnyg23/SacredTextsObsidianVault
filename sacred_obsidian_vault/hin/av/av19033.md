 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A protective and benedictive charm</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903301">1</a></font></span>Hundred stemmed, succulent, and worth a thousand, the Royal<br>   Rite of plants, the Water's Agni,<br>   Let this same Darbha guard us from all quarters. This Godlike<br>   Amulet shall with life endow us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903302">2</a></font></span>Drawn forth from butter, juicy, sweetly-flavoured, firm as the<br>    earth, unshaken, overthrowing.<br>   Driving off foes and casting them beneath me, mount with the<br>    strength of mighty Ones, O Darbha.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903303">3</a></font></span>Thou movest o'er the earth with vigour: lovely in sacrifice thou<br>    sittest on the altar.<br>   The Rishis bear thee as a purifier: cleanse thou us from all evil<br>    deeds' defilement.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903304">4</a></font></span>A stern and all-victorious king, foe-queller, dear to every man—<br>   That energy of Gods and mighty power, I bind this on thee for<br>    long life and welfare. <a name="page_239"><font size="1" color="green">p. 239</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903305">5</a></font></span>Achieve heroic deeds with Darbha, wearing this Darbha never<br>    let thy soul be troubled.<br>   In splendour and precedence over others illumine like the Sun<br>    the heaven's four regions.</p>
