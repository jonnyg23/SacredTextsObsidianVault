 <p><a name="page_358"><font size="1" color="green">p. 358</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012301">1</a></font></span>This is the Godhead, this the might of Sūrya: he hath with-<br>    drawn what spread o'er work unfinished.<br>   When he hath loosed his horses from their station, straight over<br>    all night spreadeth out her garment.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012302">2</a></font></span>In the sky's lap the Sun this form assumeth for Mitra and for<br>   Vāruna to look on.<br>   His bay steeds well maintain his power eternal, at one time<br>    bright and darksome at another.</p>
