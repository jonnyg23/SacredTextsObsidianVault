 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A protective charm addressed to the panacea called A Jangida</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903401">1</a></font></span>Jangida, thou art Angiras: thou art a guardian, Jangida.<br>   Let Jangida, keep safely all our bipeds and our quadrupeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903402">2</a></font></span>Dice-witcheries, the fifty-threes, the hundred witchcraft-practi-<br>    sers,<br>   All these may Jangida make weak, bereft of their effectual<br>    force.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903403">3</a></font></span>Baffle the loud factitious howl, make impotent the seven decays.<br>   As when an archer speeds the shaft, drive away want, O<br>   Jangida,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903404">4</a></font></span>This counteracts the sorceress, this banishes malignity: Then<br>    may victorious Jangida's prolong the days we have to live.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903405">5</a></font></span>Let Jangida's protecting might encompass us on every side.<br>   Wherewith he quells Vishkandha and Sanskandha, might by<br>    greater might.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903406">6</a></font></span>Three times the Gods engendered thee fixt on the surface of the<br>    earth;<br>   The Brāhmans of the olden time knew that thy name was<br>   Angiras;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903407">7</a></font></span>The ancient plants surpass thee not, nor any herbs of recent<br>    days.<br>   A potent charm is Jangida, a most felicitous defence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903408">8</a></font></span>Then when thou sprangest into life, Jangida of un-measured<br>    strength,<br>   Indra, O mighty One, bestowed great power upon thee from the<br>    first. <a name="page_240"><font size="1" color="green">p. 240</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903409">9</a></font></span>To thee in truth, O Forest Tree, Indra the mighty One gave<br>    strength.<br>   Driving away all maladies, strike thou the demons down, O<br>   Plant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903410">10</a></font></span>Lumbago and rheumatic pain, consumptive cough, and pleurisy,<br>   And fever which each Autumn brings, may Jangida make-<br>    powerless.</p>
