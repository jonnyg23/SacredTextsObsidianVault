 <p><a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="green">p. 367</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXIX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012901">1</a></font></span>These mares come springing forward to Pratipa Prātisutvana.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012903">3</a></font></span>4. One of them is Hariknikā. Hariknikā, what seekest thou?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012905">5</a></font></span>6. The excellent, the golden son: where now hast thou aban-<br>    doned him?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012907">7</a></font></span>8. There where around those distant trees, three Sisus that are<br>    standing there,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012909">9</a></font></span>10. Three adders, breathing angrily, are blowing loud the<br>    threatening horn.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012911">11</a></font></span>12. Hither hath come a stallion: he is known by droppings on<br>    his way,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012913">13</a></font></span>14. As by their dung the course of kine. What wouldst thou in<br>    the home of men?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012915">15</a></font></span>16. Barley and ripened rice I seek. On rice and barley hast thou<br>    fed,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012917">17</a></font></span>18. As the big serpent feeds on sheep. Cow's hoof and horse's<br>    tail hast thou,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012919">19</a></font></span>20. Winged with a falcon's pinion is that harmless swelling of<br>    thy tongue.</p>
