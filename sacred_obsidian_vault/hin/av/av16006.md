 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm to avert evil dreams, and to transfer them to an enemy</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600601">1</a></font></span>Now have we conquered and obtained: we have been freed<br>    from sin to-day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600602">2</a></font></span>Let Morning with her light dispel that evil dream that frightened<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600603">3</a></font></span>Bear that away to him who hates, away to him who curses us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600604">4</a></font></span>To him whom we abhor, to him who hates us do we send it hence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600605">5</a></font></span>May the Goddess Dawn in accord with Speech, and the Goddess<br>   Speech in accord with Dawn,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600606">6</a></font></span>The Lord of Dawn in accord with the Lord of Speech and the<br>   Lord of Speech in accord with the Lord of Dawn,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600607">7</a></font></span>Carry away to Such-an-one niggard fiends, hostile demons, and<br>   Sadanvas,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600608">8</a></font></span>Kumbhikas, Dushikas, and Piyakas,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600609">9</a></font></span>Evil day-dream, evil dream in sleep,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600610">10</a></font></span>Wishes for boons that will not come, thoughts of poverty, the<br>    snares of the Druh who never releases.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1600611">11</a></font></span>This, O Agni, let the Gods bear off to Such-an-one that he may<br>    be a fragile good-for-nothing eunuch.</p>
