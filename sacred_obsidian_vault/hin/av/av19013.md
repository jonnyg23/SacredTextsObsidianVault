 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer for aid and victory in battle</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901301">1</a></font></span>These the two sturdy, manly arms of Indra, these that are won-<br>    drous, mighty, and successful<br>   First, when the need hath come will I employ them by which<br>    the heaven of Asuras was conquered.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901302">2</a></font></span>Swift, like a dread bull sharpening his weapons, rapidly striking,<br>    stirring up the people,<br>   Loud shouting, vigilant, the one sole Hero, Indra subdued a<br>    hundred hosts together.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901303">3</a></font></span>With him loud-roaring, ever watchful, victor bold, hard to over-<br>    throw, whom none may vanquish,<br>   Indra the strong whose hand bears arrows, conquer, ye heroes<br>    now, now vanquish in the combat.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901304">4</a></font></span>He rules with those who carry shafts and quivers, Indra who<br>    with his hand brings hosts together,<br>   Foe-conquering, strong of arm, the Soma-drinker,<br>    with mighty bow, shooting with well-laid arrows.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901305">5</a></font></span>Conspicuous by thy strength, firm, foremost fighter, mighty and<br>    fierce, victorious, all-subduing,<br>   O'ercoming might, excelling men and heroes, mount the kine-<br>    winning conquering car, O Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901306">6</a></font></span>Troop-vanquisher, kine-winner, armed with thunder, who quells<br>    an army and with might destroys it,<br>   Follow him, comrades! quit yourselves like heroes, and like this<br>   Indra show your zeal and courage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901307">7</a></font></span>Piercing the cow-stalls with surpassing vigour, Indra the pitiless<br>    hero, wild with anger, <a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="green">p. 224</font></a><br>   Victor in fight, unshaken and resistless,—may he protect our<br>    armies in our battles.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901308">8</a></font></span>Brihaspati, fly with thy chariot hither, slayer of demons, driving.<br>    off our foemen.<br>   Be thou protector of our bodies, crushing our enemies, destroy-<br>    ing those who hate us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901309">9</a></font></span>Indra guide these! Brihaspati, the Guerdon, and Soma, and the<br>   Sacrifice precede them!<br>   And let the banded Maruts march in forefront of heavenly hosts<br>    that conquer and demolish.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901310">10</a></font></span>Ours be the potent host of mighty Indra, King Varuna, and<br>   Maruts and Ādityas.<br>   Uplifted is the shout of Gods who conquer, high-minded God<br>    who cause the world to tremble.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1901311">11</a></font></span>May Indra aid us when our flags are gathered: victorious be th<br>    arrows of our army.<br>   May our brave men of war prevail in battle. Ye Gods, protect u<br>    in the shouts of onset.</p>
