 <p><a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="green">p. 115</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II</h3>  <h4 align="center">The glorification of the Sun as Āditya, Sūrya and Rohita</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300201">1</a></font></span>Radiant, refulgent in the sky are reared the banners of his light,<br>   Āditya's, who beholdeth man, mighty in act and bountiful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300202">2</a></font></span>Let us laud him, the whole world's Herdsman, Sūrya, who with<br>    his rays illumines all the regions,<br>   Mark of the quarters, brightening them with lustre, swift,<br>    mighty-pinioned, flying in the ocean.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300203">3</a></font></span>From west to east thou speedest freely, making by magic day<br>    and night of diverse colours.<br>   This is Āditya, thy transcendent glory, that thou alone art born<br>    through all creation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300204">4</a></font></span>Victorious, inspired, and brightly shining, whom seven strong<br>    tawny-coloured coursers carry,<br>   Whom Atri lifted from the flood to heaven, thus men behold<br>    thee as thy course thou runnest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300205">5</a></font></span>Let them not snare thee speeding on thy journey: pass safely,<br>    swiftly places hard to traverse,<br>   While measuring out the day and night thou movest—O Sūrya,<br>    even Heaven and Earth the Goddess.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300206">6</a></font></span>Hail to thy rapid car whereon, O Sūrya, thou circlest in a<br>    moment both the limits,<br>   Whirled by thy bay steeds, best of all at drawing, thy hundred<br>    horses or seven goodly coursers!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300207">7</a></font></span>Mount thy strong car, O Sūrya, lightly rolling, drawn by good<br>    steeds, propitious, brightly gleaming,<br>   Whirled by thy bays, most excellent at drawing, thy hundred<br>    horses or seven goodly coursers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300208">8</a></font></span>Sūrya hath harnessed to his car to draw him seven stately bay<br>    steeds gay with wolden housings.<br>   The Bright One started from the distant region: dispelling<br>    gloom the God hath climbed the heavens.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300209">9</a></font></span>With lofty banner hath the God gone upward, and introduced<br>    the light, expelling darkness.<br>   He hath looked round on all the worlds, the Hero, the son of<br>   Aditi, Celestial Eagle. <a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="green">p. 116</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300210">10</a></font></span>Rising, thou spreadest out thy rays, thou nourishest all shapes<br>    and forms.<br>   Thou with thy power illumest both the oceans, encompassing<br>    all spheres with thy refulgence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300211">11</a></font></span>Moving by magic power to east and westward, these two young<br>    creatures, sporting, circle ocean.<br>   One of the pair beholds all living creatures: with wheels of gold<br>    the bay steeds bear the other.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300212">12</a></font></span>Atri established thee in heaven. O Surya, to create the month.<br>   So on thou goest, firmly held, heating, beholding all that is.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300213">13</a></font></span>As the Calf both his parents so thou joinest both the distant<br>    bounds,<br>   Surely the Gods up yonder knew this sacred mystery long ago.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300214">14</a></font></span>Sūrya is eager to obtain all wealth that lies along the sea,<br>   Great is the course spread out for him, his eastward and his<br>    westward path.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300215">15</a></font></span>He finishes his race with speed and never turns his thought<br>    aside,<br>   Thereby he keeps not from the Gods enjoyment of the Drink of<br>    Life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300216">16</a></font></span>His heralds bear him up aloft, the God who knoweth all that<br>    live,<br>   Sūrya, that all may look on him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300217">17</a></font></span>The Constellations pass away, like thieves, departing in the<br>    night.<br>   Before the all-beholding Sun.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300218">18</a></font></span>His herald rays are seen afar refulgent o'er the world of men,<br>   Like flames of fire that burn and blaze.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300219">19</a></font></span>Swift and all-beautiful art thou, O Sūrya, maker of the light,<br>   Illuming all the radiant realm.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300220">20</a></font></span>Thou goest to the hosts of Gods, thou comest hither to mankind,<br>   Hither, all light to behold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300221">21</a></font></span>With that same eye of thine wherewith thou seest, brilliant<br>   Varuna.<br>   The active one among mankind,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300222">22</a></font></span>Traversing sky and wide mid-air, thou metest with thy beams<br>    our days,<br>   Sun, seeing all things that have life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300223">23</a></font></span>Seven bay steeds harnessed to thy car bear thee, O thou far-<br>    seeing One, <a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="green">p. 117</font></a><br>   God, Sūrya, with the radiant hair.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300224">24</a></font></span>Sūrya, hath yoked the pure bright seven, the daughters of the<br>    car, with these,<br>   His own dear team, he travelleth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300225">25</a></font></span>Devout, aflame with fervent heat, Rohita hath gone up to<br>    heaven.<br>   He is re-born, returning to his birthplace, and hath become the<br>   Gods' imperial ruler.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300226">26</a></font></span>Dear unto all men, facing all directions, with hands and palms<br>    on every side extended,<br>   He, the sole God, engendering earth and heaven, beareth them<br>    with his wings and arms together.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300227">27</a></font></span>The single-footed hath outstepped the biped, the biped overtakes<br>    the triple-footed.<br>   The biped hath outstridden the six-footed: these sit around the<br>    single-footed's body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300228">28</a></font></span>When he, unwearied, fain to go, hath mounted his bays, he<br>    makes two colours, brightly shining.<br>   Rising with banners, conquering the regions, thou sendest light<br>    through all the floods, Āditya.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300229">29</a></font></span>Verily, Sūrya, thou art great: truly, Āditya, thou art great.<br>   Great is thy grandeur, Mighty One: thou, O Āditya, thou art<br>    great.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300230">30</a></font></span>In heaven, O Bird, and in mid-air thou shinest: thou shinest on<br>    the earth and in the waters.<br>   Thou hast pervaded both the seas with splendour: a God art<br>    thou, O God, light-winner, mighty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300231">31</a></font></span>Soaring in mid-course hither from the distance, fleet and ins-<br>    pired, the Bird that flies above us,<br>   With might advancing Vishnu manifested, he conquers all that<br>    moves with radiant banner:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300232">32</a></font></span>Brilliant, observant, mighty Lord, an Eagle illuming both the<br>    spheres and air between them.<br>   Day and the Night, clad in the robes of Sūrya, spread forth more<br>    widely all his hero powers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300233">33</a></font></span>Flaming and radiant, strengthening his body, bestowing floods<br>    that promptly come to meet us,<br>   He, luminous, winged, mighty, strength-bestower, hath mounted<br>    all the regions as he forms them. <a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="green">p. 118</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300234">34</a></font></span>Bright presence of the Gods, the luminous herald Sūrya hath<br>    mounted the celestial regions.<br>   Day's maker, he hath shone away the darkness, and radiant,<br>    passed o'er places hard to traverse.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300235">35</a></font></span>He hath gone up on high, the Gods' bright presence, the eye of<br>   Mitra, Varuna and Agni.<br>   The soul of all that moveth not or moveth, Sūrya hath filled the<br>    earth and air and heaven,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300236">36</a></font></span>High in the midst of heaven may we behold thee whom men call<br>   Savitar, the bright red Eagle,<br>   Soaring and speeding on thy way, refulgent, unwasting light<br>    which Atri erst discovered.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300237">37</a></font></span>Him, Son of Aditi, an Eagle hasting along heaven's height, I<br>    supplicate in terror,<br>   As such prolong our lengthened life, O Sūrya: may we, unha-<br>    rmed, enjoy thy gracious favour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300238">38</a></font></span>This gold-hued Hansa's wings, soaring to heaven, spread o'er a<br>    thousand days' continued journey<br>   Supporting all the Gods upon his bosom, he goes his way behold-<br>    ing every creature.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300239">39</a></font></span>Rohita, in primeval days Prajāpati, was, after, Time, Mouth of<br>    all sacrifices, he, Rohita, brought celestial light.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300240">40</a></font></span>He, Rohita, became the world: Rohita gave the heaven its heat.<br>   Rohita with his beams of light travelled along the earth and sea.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300241">41</a></font></span>To all the regions Rohita came, the imperial Lord of heaven.<br>   He watches over ocean, heaven, and earth and all existing things.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300242">42</a></font></span>Mounting the lofty ones, he, bright, unwearied, splendidly<br>    shining, makes two separate colours,<br>   While through all worlds that are he sends his lustre, radiant,<br>    observant, mighty, wind-approacher.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300243">43</a></font></span>One form comes on, the other is reverted: to day and night the<br>   Strong One shapes and fits him.<br>   With humble prayer for aid we call on Sūrya, who knows the<br>    way, whose home is in the region.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300244">44</a></font></span>The suppliant's way, filling the earth, the Mighty circleth the<br>    world with eye that none deceiveth.<br>   May he, all-seeing, well-disposed and holy, give ear and listen to<br>    the word I utter.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300245">45</a></font></span>Blazing with light his majesty hath compassed ocean and earth<br>    and heaven and air's mid-region. <a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="green">p. 119</font></a><br>   May he, all-seeing, well-disposed and holy, give ear and listen to<br>    the word I utter.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300246">46</a></font></span>Agni is weakened by the people's fuel to meet the Dawn who<br>    cometh like a milch-cow,<br>   Like young trees shooting up on high their branches, his flames<br>    are mounting to the vault of heaven.</p>
