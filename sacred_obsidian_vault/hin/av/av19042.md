 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLII</h3>  <h4 align="center">In praise of Brahma, Prayer, or Devotion</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904201">1</a></font></span>Brahma is Hotar, sacrifice: with Brahma are the stakes set up.<br>   From Brahma was the Adhvaryu born, from Brahma hidden<br>    offering.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904202">2</a></font></span>Brahma is fatness-dropping scoops: with Brahma was the altar<br>    reared. <a name="page_245"><font size="1" color="green">p. 245</font></a><br>   Brahma is worship, lengthened rite, the Rishis who pay sacrifice,<br>    the victim's Immolators. Hail!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904203">3</a></font></span>To him who frees from woe mine hymn I offer, to the Good<br>   Guardian, as I seek his favour.<br>   Accept this offering of mine, O Indra. Fulfilled be all the sacri-<br>    ficer's wishes!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904204">4</a></font></span>With prayer I call on him who frees from trouble, Prince of<br>   Gods, Splendid, chief of sacrifices,<br>   I call the Waters' Child and both the Asvins, Vigour is mine,<br>    and strength bestowed by Indra.</p>
