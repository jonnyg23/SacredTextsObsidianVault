 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="green">p. 285</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002601">1</a></font></span>In every need, in every fray we call, as friends, to succour us<br>   Indra the mightiest of all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002602">2</a></font></span>If he will hear us let him come with succour of a thousand<br>    kinds,<br>   And all that strengthens, to our call.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002603">3</a></font></span>I call him, mighty to resist, the Hero of our ancient home,<br>   Thee whom my sire invoked of old.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002604">4</a></font></span>They who stand round him as he moves harness the bright, the<br>    ruddy steed:<br>   The lights are shining in the sky.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002605">5</a></font></span>On both sides of the car they yoke the two bay coursers dear to<br>    him,<br>   Bold, tawny, bearers of the thief.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002606">6</a></font></span>Thou, making light where no light was, and form, O Men!<br>    where form was not,<br>   Wast born together with the Dawns.</p>
