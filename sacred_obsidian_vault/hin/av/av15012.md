 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501201">1</a></font></span>The man, to whose house, when the fires have been taken up<br>    from the hearth and the oblation to Agni placed therein, the<br>   Vrātya possessing this knowledge comes as a guest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501202">2</a></font></span>Should of his own accord rise to meet him and say, Vrātya, give<br>    me permission. I will sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501203">3</a></font></span>And if he gives permission he should sacrifice, if he does not<br>    permit him he should not sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501204">4</a></font></span>He who sacrifices when permitted by the Vrātya who possesses<br>    this knowledge.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501205">5</a></font></span>Well knows the path that leads to the Fathers and the way that.<br>    leads to the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501206">6</a></font></span>He does not act in opposition to the Gods. It becomes his<br>    sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501207">7</a></font></span>The abode of the man who sacrifices when permitted by the<br>   Vrātya who possesses this knowledge is long left remaining in<br>    this world.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501208">8</a></font></span>But he who sacrifices without the permission of the Vrātya who-<br>     possesses this knowledge.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501209">9</a></font></span>Knows not the path that leads to the Fathers nor the way that<br>    leads to the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501210">10</a></font></span>He is at variance with the Gods. He hath offered no accepted.<br>    sacrifice. <a name="page_158"><font size="1" color="green">p. 158</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501211">11</a></font></span>The abode of the man who sacrifices without the permission of<br>    the Vrātya who possesses this knowledge is not left remaining<br>    in this world.</p>
