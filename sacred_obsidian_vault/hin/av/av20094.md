 <p><a name="page_341"><font size="1" color="green">p. 341</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009401">1</a></font></span>May Sovran Indra come to the carousal, he who by holy Law is<br>    strong and active.<br>   The overcomer of all conquering forces with his great bull-like<br>    power that hath no limit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009402">2</a></font></span>Firm-seated is thy car, thy steeds are docile: thy hand, O King,<br>    holds, firmly grasped, the thunder.<br>   On thy fair path, O Lord of men, come quickly: we will increase<br>    thy power when thou hast drunken.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009403">3</a></font></span>Let strong and mighty steeds who bear this mighty Indra, the<br>   Lord of men, whose arm wields thunder,<br>   Bring unto us, as shares of our banquet, the Bull of conquering<br>    might, of real vigour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009404">4</a></font></span>So like a bull thou rushest to the Lord who loves the trough,<br>    the Sage, the prop of vigour, in the vat.<br>   Prepare thine energies, collect them in thyself: be for our profit<br>    as the Master of the wise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009405">5</a></font></span>May precious treasures come to us,— so will I pray. Come to the<br>    votary's gift offered with beauteous laud.<br>   Thou art the Lord, as such sit on this holy grass: thy vessels<br>    are inviolate as Law commands.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009406">6</a></font></span>Far went our earliest invocations of the Gods, and won us<br>    glories that can never be surpassed.<br>   They who could not ascend the ship of sacrifice sink down in<br>    desolation, trembling with alarm.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009407">7</a></font></span>So be the others, evil-hearted, far away, whose horses difficult to<br>    harness have been yoked.<br>   Here in advance man stand anear to offer gifts, by whom full<br>    many a work that brings reward is done.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009408">8</a></font></span>He firmly fixed the plains and mountains as they shook. Dyaus<br>    thundered forth and made the air's mid-region quake.<br>   He stays apart the two confronting bowls; he sings lauds in the<br>    potent Soma's joy when he hath drunk.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009409">9</a></font></span>I bear this deftly-fashioned goad of thine wherewith thou,<br>   Maghavan, shalt break the strikers with the hoof. <a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="green">p. 342</font></a><br>   At the libation mayst thou be well satisfied. Partake the juice,<br>    partake the banquet, Bounteous Lord.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009410">10</a></font></span>O Much-invoked, may we subdue all famine and evil want with<br>    store of grain and cattle.<br>   May we allied, as first in rank, with princes, obtain possessions<br>    by our own exertions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009411">11</a></font></span>Brihaspati protect us from the rearward, and from above, and<br>    from below, from sinners!<br>   May Indra from the front and from the centre, as friend to<br>    friends, vouchsafe us room and freedom.</p>
