 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="green">p. 55</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V</h3>  <h4 align="center">A glorification of the Brahmachgri or religious student</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100501">1</a></font></span>Stirring both worlds the Brahmachāri moveth: in him the<br>    deities are all one-minded.<br>   He hath established firmly earth and heaven: he satisfies his<br>   Master with his Fervour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100502">2</a></font></span>After the Brahmachārī go the Fathers, the heavenly hosts, all<br>   Gods in separate order.<br>   After him too have the Gandharvas followed, thirty and three,<br>    three hundred, and six thousand. He satisfies all Gods with<br>    his devotion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100503">3</a></font></span>The Master, welcoming his new disciple, into his bowels takes<br>    the Brahmachāri.<br>   Three nights he holds and bears him in this belly. When he is<br>    born, the Gods convene to see him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100504">4</a></font></span>This log is earth, the second log is heaven: he fills the air's mid<br>    region with the fuel.<br>   With fuel, with his zone the Brahmachāri contents the worlds,<br>    with labour and with Fervour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100505">5</a></font></span>The Brahmachāri, earlier born than Brahma, sprang up through<br>   Fervour, robed in hot libation.<br>   From him sprang heavenly lore, the highest Brahma, and all the<br>   Gods, with life that lasts for ever. <a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="green">p. 56</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100506">6</a></font></span>Lighted by fuel goes the Brahmachāri, clad in black-buck skin,<br>    consecrate, long-bearded.<br>   Swiftly he goes from east to northern ocean, grasping the worlds,<br>    oft bringing them anear him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100507">7</a></font></span>The Brahmachāri, fathering Prayer, world, Waters, Virāj, Prajā-<br>    pati, and Parameshthin,<br>   Lay as a germ within the Immortal's bosom, then became Indra<br>    and destroyed the demons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100508">8</a></font></span>The Master fashioned both these cloudy regions, profound and<br>    spacious pair, the earth and heaven.<br>   The Brahmachāri guards them with his Fervour. In him the<br>    deities are all one-minded.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100509">9</a></font></span>The Brahmachāri first of all brought hither this ample earth as<br>    alms, and heaven above it.<br>   He makes these twain two fuel-logs, and worships, On these sup-<br>    ported rest all living creatures.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100510">10</a></font></span>Both treasuries of sacred lore lie hidden, one hitherward, beyond<br>    heaven's ridge the other.<br>   The Brahmachārī with his Fervour guards them. He makes this<br>    all his own as knowing Brahma.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100511">11</a></font></span>Hitherward one, hence from the earth the other, two Agnis meet<br>    between these cloudy regions.<br>   Close to these two firm rays of light are clinging. The Brahma-<br>    chāri enters them through Fervour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100512">12</a></font></span>Thundering, shouting, ruddy-hued, and pallid, he bears along the<br>    earth great manly vigour.<br>   Down on the ridge of earth the Brahmachāri pours seed, and<br>    this gives life to heaven's four regions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100513">13</a></font></span>The Brahmachāri stores with fuel Waters, and Fire, and Sun, and<br>   Moon, and Mātarisvan.<br>   The Water's lights move separate in the rain-cloud, Man, rain,<br>    and water are their molten butter.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100514">14</a></font></span>The Master is Death, Varuna, Soma, the plants of earth, and<br>    milk.<br>   The thunder-clouds were men of war. By these this heavenly<br>    light was brought.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100515">15</a></font></span>Varuna, made a Master, takes at home the butter to himself.<br>   Whatever with Prajāpati he sought, the Brahmachāri gave like<br>   Mitra from his loftiest soul. <a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100516">16</a></font></span>The pupil is the Master, yea, the pupil is Prajāpati.<br>   Prajāpati shines bright; the bright Virāj grew potent Indra's self.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100517">17</a></font></span>By Fervour and by self-restraint the King protects the realm he<br>    rules.<br>   By self-restraint the Master seeks a Brahmachari to instruct.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100518">18</a></font></span>By self-restraint a maiden finds a youth to be her wedded lord.<br>   By self-restraint the ox and horse seek to win fodder for them-<br>    selves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100519">19</a></font></span>By Fervour and by self-restraint the Gods draye Death away<br>    from them,<br>   And Indra brought by self-restraint heaven's lustre to the deities.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100520">20</a></font></span>The plants, what is and what shall be, day, night, the tall tree of<br>    the wood,<br>   The year with seasons of the year, all from the Brahmachāri<br>    sprang.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100521">21</a></font></span>All creatures of the earth and heaven, tame animals and sylvan<br>    beasts,<br>   Winged and wingless creatures, from the Brahmachāri sprang<br>    to life,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100522">22</a></font></span>All children of Prajāpati have breath distinctly in themselves.<br>   The Brahma that is stored within the Brahmachāri guards them<br>    all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100523">23</a></font></span>Piled up on high, but never yet ascended, that power of deities<br>    is brightly shining.<br>   From that sprang heavenly lore, the loftiest Brahma, and all the<br>   Gods with, life that lasts for ever.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100524">24</a></font></span>The Brahmachāri wields the radiant Brahma wherein all Gods<br>    are woven close together;<br>   Creating breath, inhaling and exhaling, voice, mind, and heart,<br>   Brahma and holy wisdom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100525">25</a></font></span>Bestow on us the power of sight and hearing, glory and food and<br>    seed and blood and belly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100526">26</a></font></span>These, standing on the flood, the Brahmachāri formed practising<br>    in sea his hot devotion.<br>   When he hath bathed, brown, yellow-hued, he shines exceedingly<br>    on earth.</p>
