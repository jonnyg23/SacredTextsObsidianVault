 <p><a name="page_170"><font size="1" color="green">p. 170</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XVII</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer to Indra, Identified with Vishnu and the Sun, for the love of Gods, men, and beasts, general protection and prosperity, and all earthly and heavenly blessings</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700101">1</a></font></span>Vanquishing, overpowering, a conqueror, exceeding strong,<br>   Victorious, winner of the light, winner of cattle and of spoil,.<br>   Indra by name, adorable, I call: a long, long life be mine!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700102">2</a></font></span>Vanquishing etc.<br>   Indra by name, adorable I call: May I be dear to Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700103">3</a></font></span>Vanquishing, etc.<br>   Indra by name, adorable, I call: may creatures love me well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700104">4</a></font></span>Vanquishing, etc.<br>   Indra by name, adorable, I call: may cattle hold me dear.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700105">5</a></font></span>Vanquishing, etc.<br>   Indra by name, adorable, I call: may equals love me well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700106">6</a></font></span>Rise up, O Sūrya, rise thou up; with strength and splendour<br>    rise on me.<br>   Let him who hates me be my thrall; let me not be a thrall to him.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br>   Sate us with cattle of all forms and colours: set me in happiness,<br>    in loftiest heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700107">7</a></font></span>Rise up, O Sūrya, rise thou up; with strength and splendour rise<br>    on me.<br>   Make me the favourite of all, of those I see and do not see.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700108">8</a></font></span>Let not the fowlers who are standing ready injure thee in the<br>    flood, within the waters.<br>   Ascend this heaven, leaving each curse behind thee, Favour us:<br>    let thy gracious love attend us.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700109">9</a></font></span>Do thou, O Indra, for our great good fortune, with thine in-<br>    violable rays protect us.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700110">10</a></font></span>Be thou most gracious unto us, Indra, with favourable aid,<br>   Rising to heaven's third sphere, invoked with song to quaff the<br>   Soma juice, loving thy home to make us blest. <a name="page_171"><font size="1" color="green">p. 171</font></a><br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700111">11</a></font></span>Thou art the vanquisher of all, O Indra, omniscient Indra, and<br>    invoked of many.<br>   Indra, send forth this hymn that fitly lauds thee. Favour us<br>    let thy gracious love attend us.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700112">12</a></font></span>In heaven and on the earth thou art uninjured, none reach thy<br>    greatness in the air's mid region.<br>   Increasing by inviolate devotion as such in heaven grant us pro-<br>    tection, Indra.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700113">13</a></font></span>Grant us protection, Indra, with that body of thine that is on<br>    earth, in fire, in waters,<br>   That dwells within light-finding Pavamana, wherewith thou hast<br>    pervaded air's mid region.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700114">14</a></font></span>Indra, exalting thee with prayer, imploring, Rishis have sat them<br>    down in holy Session.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700115">15</a></font></span>Round Trita, round the spring with thousand currents thou<br>    goest, round the light-finding assembly.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700116">16</a></font></span>Thou guardest well the four celestial regions, pervading heaven,<br>    and earth with light and splendour.<br>   Thou givest help to all these living creatures, and, knowing,.<br>    followest the path of Order.<br>   Manifold are thy great deed, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700117">17</a></font></span>With five thou sendest heat: with one removing the curse thou<br>    comest in bright sunshine hither.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700118">18</a></font></span>Indra art thou, Mahendra thou, thou art the world, the Lord of<br>   Life.<br>   To thee is sacrifice performed: worshippers offer gifts to thee.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700119">19</a></font></span>What is based on what is not: the present lies on that which is..<br>   Present on future is imposed and future on the present based.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br>   Sate us with cattle of all varied colour. Set me in happiness, in<br>    loftiest heaven. <a name="page_172"><font size="1" color="green">p. 172</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700120">20</a></font></span>Bright art thou, and refulgent: as thou shinest with splendour<br>    so I fain would shine with splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700121">21</a></font></span>Lustre art thou, illuming: as thou glowest with lustre so I too<br>    would shine with cattle, with all the lustre of a Brāhman's<br>    station.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700122">22</a></font></span>Glory to him when rising, when ascending! Glory to him when<br>    he hath reached the zenith!<br>   To him far-shining, him the self-refulgent, to him the Sovran<br>   Lord and King be glory!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700123">23</a></font></span>Worship to him when he is turning westward, to him when set-<br>    ting, and when set be worship!<br>   To him far-shining, him the self-refulgent, to him the Sovran<br>   Lord and King be glory!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700124">24</a></font></span>With all his fiery fervour this Āditya hath gone up on high,<br>   Giving my foes into my hand. Let me not by my foeman's prey.<br>   Manifold are thy great deeds, thine, O Vishnu.<br>   Sate us with cattle of all varied colours. Set me in happiness, in<br>    loftiest heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700125">25</a></font></span>Thou for our weal, Āditya, hast mounted thy ship with hundred<br>    oars.<br>   Thou hast transported me to day: so bear me evermore to<br>    night.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700126">26</a></font></span>Thou for our weal, O Sūrya, hast mounted thy ship with hundred<br>    oars.<br>   Thou hast transported me to night: so bear me evermore to<br>    day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700127">27</a></font></span>Encompassed by Prajāpati's devotion as shield, with Kasyapa's<br>    bright light and splendour,<br>   Reaching old age, may I made strong and mighty live through a<br>    thousand years with happy fortune.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700128">28</a></font></span>Compassed am I with prayer, my shield and armour; compassed<br>    with Kasyapa's bright light and splendour.<br>   Let not shafts reach me shot from heaven against me, nor those<br>    sent forth by men for my destruction.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700129">29</a></font></span>Guarded am I by Order and the Seasons, protected by the past<br>    and by the future.<br>   Let not distress, yea, let not Death come nigh me: with water<br>    of my speech have I o'erwhelmed them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1700130">30</a></font></span>On every side let Agni guard and keep me; the rising Sun drive<br>    off the snares of Mrityu! <a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="green">p. 173</font></a><br>   Let brightly flushing Mornings, firm-set mountains, and lives a<br>    thousand be with me united.</p>
