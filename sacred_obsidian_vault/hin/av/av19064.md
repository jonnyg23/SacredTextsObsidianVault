 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer to Agni for children, long life, and various blessings</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1906401">1</a></font></span>For lofty Jātavedas I have brought the fuel hither first.<br>   May he who knoweth all bestow faith and intelligence on me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1906402">2</a></font></span>With fuel and with flaming wood we, Jātavedas, strengthen<br>    thee;<br>   So do thou strengthen us in turn with children and with store of<br>    wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1906403">3</a></font></span>Whatever even be the logs which, Agni, we lay down for thee,<br>    propitious be it all to me: accept it, O most youthful God.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1906404">4</a></font></span>Agni, these logs are thine: with these be, fain to burn! a<br>    flaming brand.<br>   Vouchsafe us length of life and give us hope of immortality.</p>
