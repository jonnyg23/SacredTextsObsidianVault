 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002101">1</a></font></span>We will present fair praise unto the Mighty One, our hymns to<br>   Indra in Vivasvān's dwelling-place;<br>   For he hath ne'er found wealth in those who seem to sleep;<br>    those who give wealth to men accept no paltry praise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002102">2</a></font></span>Giver of horses, Indra, giver, thou, of kine, giver of barley,<br>    thou art Lord and guard of wealth:<br>   Man's helper from of old, not disappointing hope, Friend of<br>    our friends, to thee as such we sing this praise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002103">3</a></font></span>Indra, most splendid, powerful, rich in mighty deeds, this<br>    treasure spread around is known to be thine own.<br>   Gather therefrom. O Conqueror, and bring to us: fail not the<br>    hope of him who loves and sings to thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002104">4</a></font></span>Well-pleased with these bright flames and with these Soma<br>    drops, take thou away our poverty with steeds and kine.<br>   With Indra scattering the Dasyu through these drops, freed<br>    from their hate may we obtain abundant food.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002105">5</a></font></span>Let us obtain, O Indra, plenteous wealth and food, with strength<br>    exceeding glorious, shining to the sky.<br>   May we obtain the Goddess Providence, the strength of heroes,<br>    special source of cattle, rich in steeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002106">6</a></font></span>These our libations, strength inspiring Soma draughts, gladdened<br>    thee in the fight with Vritra, Hero-Lord, <a name="page_281"><font size="1" color="green">p. 281</font></a><br>   What time thou slewest for the singer with trimmed grass ten<br>    thousand Vritras, thou resistless in thy might.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002107">7</a></font></span>Thou goest on from fight to fight intrepidly, destroying castle<br>    after castle here with strength;<br>   Thou Indra, with thy friend who makes the foe bow down,<br>    slewest from far away the guileful Namuchi.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002108">8</a></font></span>Thou hast stuck down in death Karanja, Parnaya, in Atithigva's<br>    very glorious going forth:<br>   Unyielding, when Rijisvan compassed them with siege, thou hast<br>    destroyed the hundred towns of Vangrida.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002109">9</a></font></span>With all-outstripping chariot wheel, O Indra, thou far-famed,<br>    hast overthrown the twice ten kings of men,<br>   With sixty thousand nine-and-ninety followers, who came in<br>    arms to fight with friendless Susravas.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002110">10</a></font></span>Thou hast protected Susravas with succour, and Tūrvayāna with<br>    thine aid, O Indra:<br>   Thou madest Kutsa, Atithigva, Ayu subject unto this king, the<br>    young, the mighty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002111">11</a></font></span>May we protected by the Gods hereafter remain thy very pro-<br>    sperous friends, O Indra.<br>   Thee we extol, enjoying through thy favour life-long and joyful<br>    and with store of heroes.</p>
