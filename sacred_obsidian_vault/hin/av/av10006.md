 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="green">p. 17</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI</h3>  <h4 align="center">The glorification of an all-powerful amulet</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000601">1</a></font></span>With power I cut away the head of my malignant rival, of mine<br>    evil-hearted enemy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000602">2</a></font></span>This Amulet of citron-wood shall make for me a trusty shield<br>   Filled with the mingled beverage, with sap and vigour hath it<br>    come.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000603">3</a></font></span>What though the strong-armed carpenter have cleft thee with<br>    his hand and axe.<br>   Pure animating waters shall cleanse thee and make thee bright<br>    again.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000604">4</a></font></span>This Amulet, decked with chain of gold, shall give faith,<br>    sacrifice, and might, and dwell as guest within our house.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000605">5</a></font></span>To this we give apportioned food, clarified butter, wine, and<br>    meath.<br>   May it provide each boon for us as doth a father for his sons. <a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="green">p. 18</font></a><br>   Again, again, from morn to morn, having approached the<br>    deities.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000606">6</a></font></span>The Charm Brihaspati hath bound, the fatness-dropping citron-<br>    wood, the potent Khadira for strength,<br>   This Agni hath put on: it yields clarified butter for this man.<br>   Again, again, from morn to morn. With this subdue thine<br>    enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000607">7</a></font></span>The Charm Brihaspati hath bound, the fatness-dropping citron-<br>    wood, the potent Khadira, for strength,<br>   This Charm hath Indra put on him for power and manly<br>    puissance.<br>   It yieldeth strength to strengthen him, again, again, from morn<br>    to morn, having approached the deities.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000608">8</a></font></span>The Charin Brihaspati, etc.<br>   This Charm hath Soma put on him for might, for hearing, and<br>    for sight.<br>   This yields him energy indeed, again, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000609">9</a></font></span>The Charm Brihaspati, etc.<br>   This Sūrya put on him, with this conquered the regions of the<br>    sky.<br>   This yieldeth him ability, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000610">10</a></font></span>The Charm Brihaspati, etc.<br>   This Charm did Chandra wear, with this conquered the forts of<br>   Asuras, the golden forts of Dānavas.<br>   This yields him glory and renown, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000611">11</a></font></span>The Amulet Brihaspati bound on the swiftly-moving Wind.<br>   This yieldeth him a vigorous steed, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000612">12</a></font></span>The Asvins with this Amulet protect this culture of our fields.<br>   This yields the two Physicians might, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000613">13</a></font></span>Savitar wore this Amulet: herewith he won this lucid heaven.<br>   This yields him glory and delight, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000614">14</a></font></span>Wearing this Charm the Waters flow eternally inviolate. This<br>    yieldeth them ambrosia, again etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000615">15</a></font></span>King Varuna assumed and wore this salutary Amulet.<br>   This yieldeth him his truthfulness, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000616">16</a></font></span>Wearing this Amulet the Gods conquered in battle all the<br>    worlds.<br>   This yieldeth victory for them, again, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000617">17</a></font></span>The Amulet Brihaspati formed for the swiftly-moving Wind,<br>   This salutary Amulet the Deities assumed and wore. <a name="page_19"><font size="1" color="green">p. 19</font></a><br>   This yieldeth them the universe, again, again, from morn to<br>    morn. With this subdue thine enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000618">18</a></font></span>The seasons formed that Amulet, the Groups of Seasons fashion-<br>    ed it.<br>   The Year having constructed it preserveth everything that is.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000619">19</a></font></span>The regions of the heaven, the points that lie between them<br>    fashioned it.<br>   Created by Prajāpati, may the Charm cast my foemen down.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000620">20</a></font></span>Atharvan made the Amulet, Atharvan's children fashioned it.<br>   With them the sage Angirases broke through the Dasyus'<br>    fortresses. With this subdue thine enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000621">21</a></font></span>Dhātar bound on this Amulet: he ranged and ordered all that<br>    is. With this do thou subdue thy foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000622">22</a></font></span>The Amulet Brihaspati formed for the Gods, that slew the<br>    fiends.<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me combined with sap and<br>    energy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000623">23</a></font></span>The Amulet, etc.<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me, hath come with cows, and<br>    goats, and sheep, hath come with food and progeny.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000624">24</a></font></span>The Amulet, etc.<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me with store of barley and of<br>    rice, with greatness and prosperity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000625">25</a></font></span>The Amulet, etc.<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me with streams of butter and<br>    of mead, with sweet delicious beverage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000626">26</a></font></span>The Amulet, etc.<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me with power and abundant<br>    strength, hath come with glory and with wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000627">27</a></font></span>The Amulet, etc..<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me with splendour and a blaze<br>    of light, with honour and illustrious fame.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000628">28</a></font></span>The Amulet Brihaspati made for the Gods, that slew the fiends,<br>   That Amulet here hath come to me combined with all<br>    prosperities.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000629">29</a></font></span>That Amulet may the Deities bestow on me to win success,<br>   The conquering, strength-increasing Charm, the damager of<br>    enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000630">30</a></font></span>I bind on me my happy fate with holy prayer and energy.<br>   Foeless destroyer of the foe, it hath subdued mine enemies. <a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="green">p. 20</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000631">31</a></font></span>May this Chaim, offspring of the Gods, make me superior to my<br>    foe.<br>   So may this charm whose milk expressed these three worlds<br>    longingly await,<br>   Be fastened on me here, that it may crown me with surpassing<br>    power.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000632">32</a></font></span>The Charm to which men, Fathers, Gods look ever for their<br>    maintenance,<br>   May this be fastened on me here, to crown me with surpassing<br>    power<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000633">33</a></font></span>As, when the plough hath tilled the soil, the seed springs up in.<br>    fertile land,<br>   Let cattle, progeny, and food of every kind spring up with me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000634">34</a></font></span>Charm, forwarder of sacrifice, who hast a hundred priestly fees.<br>   Speed to preeminence him to whom I have attached thy happy<br>    fate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000635">35</a></font></span>Love thou, O Agni, pleased with burnt oblations, this sacred<br>    fuel that is ranged in order.<br>   In him may we find grace and loving-kindness, happiness,<br>    progeny, and sight and cattle, in Jātavedas kindled with<br>    devotion.</p>
