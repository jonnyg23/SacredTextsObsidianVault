 <p><a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="green">p. 97</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IV</h3>  <h4 align="center">On the duty of giving cows to Brāhmans, and the sin and danger of withholding the gift</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200401">1</a></font></span>Give the gift, shall be his word: and straightway they have bound<br>    the Cow<br>   For Brāhman priests who beg the boon. That bringeth sons and<br>    progeny.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200402">2</a></font></span>He trades and traffics with his sons, and in his cattle suffers loss.<br>   Who will not give the Cow of Gods to Rishis children when<br>    they beg.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200403">3</a></font></span>They perish through a hornless cow, a lame cow sinks them in a<br>    pit.<br>   Through a maimed cow his house is burnt: a one-eyed cow<br>    destroys his wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200404">4</a></font></span>Fierce fever where her droppings fall attacks the master of the<br>    kine.<br>   So have they named her Vasa, for thou art called uncontrollable.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200405">5</a></font></span>The malady Viklindu springs on him from ground whereon she<br>    stands,<br>   And suddenly, from fell disease, perish the men on whom she<br>    sniffs.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200406">6</a></font></span>Whoever twitches up her ears is separated from the Gods.<br>   He deems he makes a mark, but he diminishes his wealth<br>    thereby.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200407">7</a></font></span>If to his own advantage one applies the long hair of her tail, <a name="page_98"><font size="1" color="green">p. 98</font></a><br>   His colts, in consequence thereof. die, and the wolf destroys his<br>    calves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200408">8</a></font></span>If, while her master owneth her, a carrion crow hath harmed her<br>    hair,<br>   His young boys die thereof, Decline o'ertakes them after fell<br>    disease.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200409">9</a></font></span>What time the Dāsi woman throws lye on the droppings of the<br>   Cow,<br>   Misshapen birth arises thence, inseparable from that sin.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200410">10</a></font></span>For Gods and Brāhmans is the Cow produced when first she<br>    springs to life,<br>   Hence to the priests must she be given: this they call guarding<br>    private wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200411">11</a></font></span>The God-created Cow belongs to those who come to ask for<br>    her.<br>   They call it outrage on the priests when one retains her as his<br>    own.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200412">12</a></font></span>He who withholds the Cow of Gods from Rishis' sons who ask<br>    the gift<br>   Is made an alien to the Gods, and subject to the Brāhmans'<br>    wrath:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200413">13</a></font></span>Then let him seek another Cow, whate'er his profit be in this.<br>   The Cow, not given, harms a man when he denies her at their<br>    prayer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200414">14</a></font></span>Like a rich treasure stored away in safety is the Brāhmans' Cow.<br>   Therefore men come to visit her, with whomsoever she is born.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200415">15</a></font></span>So when the Brāhmans come unto the Cow they come unto their<br>    own.<br>   For this is her withholding, to oppress these in another life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200416">16</a></font></span>Thus after three years may she go, speaking what is not under-<br>    stood.<br>   He, Nārads! would know the Cow, then Brāhmans must be<br>    sought unto.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200417">17</a></font></span>Whoso calls her a worthless Cow, the stored-up treasure of the<br>   Gods,<br>   Bhava and Sarva, both of them, move round and shoot a shaft<br>    at him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200418">18</a></font></span>The man who hath no knowledge of her udder and the teats<br>    thereof, <a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="green">p. 99</font></a><br>   She yields him milk with these, if he hath purposed to bestow<br>    the Cow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200419">19</a></font></span>If he withholds the Cow they beg, she lies rebellious in his stall.<br>   Vain are the wishes and the hopes which he, withholding her,<br>    would gain.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200420">20</a></font></span>The Deities have begged the Cow, using the Brāhman as their<br>    mouth:<br>   The man who gives her not incurs the enmity of all the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200421">21</a></font></span>Withholding her from Brāhmans, he incurs the anger of the<br>    beasts,<br>   When mortal man appropriates the destined portion of the<br>   Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200422">22</a></font></span>If hundred other Brāhmans beg the Cow of him who owneth<br>    her,<br>   The Gods have said, She, verily, belongs to him who knows the<br>    truth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200423">23</a></font></span>Whoso to others, not to him who hath this knowledge, gives the<br>   Cow,<br>   Earth, with the Deities, is hard for him to win and rest upon.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200424">24</a></font></span>The Deities begged the Cow from him with whom at first she<br>    was produced:<br>   Her, this one, Nārada would know: with Deities he drove her<br>    forth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200425">25</a></font></span>The Cow deprives of progeny and makes him poor in cattle who<br>   Retains in his possession her whom Brāhmans have solicited.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200426">26</a></font></span>For Agni and for Soma, for Kāma, Mitra and Varuna,<br>   For these the Brāhmans ask: from these is he who giveth not<br>    estranged.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200427">27</a></font></span>Long as her owner hath not heard, himself, the verses, let her<br>    move<br>   Among his kine: when he hath heard, let her not make her<br>    home with him;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200428">28</a></font></span>He who hath heard her verses and still makes her roam among<br>    his kine.<br>   The Gods in anger rend away his life and his prosperity<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200429">29</a></font></span>Roaming in many a place the Cow is the stored treasure of the<br>   Gods,<br>   Make manifest thy shape and form when she would seek her<br>    dwelling-place. <a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="green">p. 100</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200430">30</a></font></span>Her shape and form she manifests when she would seek her<br>    dwelling-place;<br>   Then verily the Cow attends to Brāhman priests and their<br>    request.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200431">31</a></font></span>This thought he settles in his mind. This safely goeth to the<br>   Gods.<br>   Then verily the Brāhman priests approach that they may beg the<br>   Cow<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200432">32</a></font></span>By Svadhā to the Fathers, by sacrifice to the Deities,<br>   By giving them the Cow, the Prince doth not incur the mother's.<br>    wrath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200433">33</a></font></span>The Prince's mother is the Cow: so was it ordered from of old.<br>   She, when bestowed upon the priests, cannot be given back, they<br>    say.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200434">34</a></font></span>As molten butter, held at length, drops down to Agni from the<br>    scoop,<br>   So falls away from Agni he who gives no Cow to Brāhman<br>    priests.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200435">35</a></font></span>Good milker, with rice-cake as calf, she in the world comes<br>    nigh to him,<br>   To him who gave her as a gift the Cow grants every hope and.<br>    wish.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200436">36</a></font></span>In Yama's realm the Cow fulfils each wish for him who gave her<br>    up;<br>   But hell, they say, is for the man who, when they beg, bestow<br>    her not.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200437">37</a></font></span>Enraged against her owner roams the Cow when she hath been<br>    impregned.<br>   He deemed me fruitless is her thought; let him be bound in,<br>    snares of Death!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200438">38</a></font></span>Whoever looking on the Cow as fruitless, cooks her flesh at<br>    home,<br>   Brihaspati compels his sons and children of his sons to beg.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200439">39</a></font></span>Downward she sends a mighty heat, though amid kine a Cow<br>    she roams.<br>   Poison she yields for him who owns and hath not given her away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200440">40</a></font></span>The animal is happy when it is bestowed upon the priests:<br>   But happy is the Cow when she is made a sacrifice to Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200441">41</a></font></span>Nārada chose the terrible Vilipti out of all the cows Which the <a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="green">p. 101</font></a><br>   Gods formed and framed when they had risen up from sacri-<br>    fice<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200442">42</a></font></span>The Gods considered her in doubt whether she were a Cow or<br>    not.<br>   Mirada spake of her and said, The veriest Cow of cows is she.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200443">43</a></font></span>How many cows, O Nārada, knowest thou, born among man-<br>    kind<br>   I ask thee who dost know, of which must none who is no<br>   Brāhman eat?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200444">44</a></font></span>Vilipti, cow, and she who drops no second calf, Brihaspati!<br>   Of these none not a Brāhmana should eat if he hope for emi-<br>    nence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200445">45</a></font></span>Homage, O Nārada, to thee who hast quick knowledge of the<br>    cows.<br>   Which of these is the direst, whose withholding bringeth death<br>    to man?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200446">46</a></font></span>Vilipti, O Brihaspati, cow, mother of no second calf—Of these<br>    none not a Brāhman should eat if he hope for eminence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200447">47</a></font></span>Threefold are kine, Vilipti, cow, the mother of no seeond calf:<br>   These one should give to priests, and he will not offend Prajā-<br>    pati.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200448">48</a></font></span>This Brāhmans! is your sacrifice: thus should one think when he<br>    is asked,<br>   What time they beg from him the Cow fearful in the with-<br>    holder's house.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200449">49</a></font></span>He gave her not to us, so spake the Gods, in anger, of the Cow.<br>   With these same verses they addressed Bheda: this brought<br>    him to his death.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200450">50</a></font></span>Solicited by Indra, still Bheda refused to give this Cow.<br>   In strife for victory the Gods destroyed him for that sin of his.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200451">51</a></font></span>The men of evil counsel who advise refusal of the Cow,<br>   Miscreants, through their foolishness, are subjected to Indra's<br>    wrath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200452">52</a></font></span>They who seduce the owner of the Cow and say, Bestow her<br>    not.<br>   Encounter through their want of sense the missile shot by<br>   Rudra's hand.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200453">53</a></font></span>If in his home one cooks the Cow, sacrificed or not sacrificed.<br>   Wronger of Gods and Brāhmans' he departs, dishonest, from<br>    the world.</p>
