 <p><a name="page_289"><font size="1" color="green">p. 289</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003201">1</a></font></span>Thou, comprehending with thy might the earth and heaven,<br>    acceptest the dear hymn for ever new and new.<br>   O Asura, disclose thou and make visible the Cow's beloved<br>    home to the bright golden Sun.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003202">2</a></font></span>O Indra, let the eager wishes of the folk bring thee the golden-<br>    jawed, delightful, on thy car.<br>   That, pleased with sacrifice wherein ten fingers toil, thou mayest<br>    at the feast drink of our offered mead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003203">3</a></font></span>Juices aforetime, Lord of Bays, thou drankest, and thine, and<br>    only thine, is this libation.<br>   Gladden thee, Indra, with the mead-rich Soma: pour it down<br>    ever, Mighty One, within thee.</p>
