 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="green">p. 273</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001301">1</a></font></span>Lords of great wealth, Brihaspati and Indra, rejoicing at this<br>    sacrifice drink Soma.<br>   Let the abundant drops sink deep within you: vouchsafe us<br>    riches with full store of heroes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001302">2</a></font></span>Let your swift-gliding coursers bear you hitherward with their<br>    fleet pinions. Come ye forward with your arms.<br>   Sit on the grass; a wide seat hath been made for you: delight<br>    yourselves, O Maruts, in the pleasant food.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001303">3</a></font></span>For Jātavedas, worthy of our praise, will we frame with our<br>    mind this eulogy as 'twere a car.<br>   For good in his assembly is this care of ours. Let us not in thy<br>    friendship, Agni, suffer harm.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001304">4</a></font></span>With these, borne on one car, Agni, approach us; or borne on<br>    many, for thy steeds are able.<br>   Bring, with their Dames, the Gods, the Three-and-Thirty, after<br>    thy Godlike nature, and he joyful.</p>
