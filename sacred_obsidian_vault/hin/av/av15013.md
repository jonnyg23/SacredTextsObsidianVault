 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501301">1</a></font></span>He in whose house the Vrātya who possesses this knowledge<br>    abides one night secures for himself thereby the holy realms<br>    that are on earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501302">2</a></font></span>A second night . . . . the holy realms that are in the firma-<br>    ment (the rest as in verse 1).<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501303">3</a></font></span>A third night . . . the holy realms that are in heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501304">4</a></font></span>A fourth night . . . . the holy realms of the Holy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501305">5</a></font></span>Unlimited nights . . . . unlimited holy realms.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501306">6</a></font></span>Now he to whose house a non-Vrātya, calling himself a Vrātya,<br>    and one in name only, comes as a guest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501307">7</a></font></span>Should punish him and not punish him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501308">8</a></font></span>He should serve him with food saying to himself, To this Deity<br>   I offer water: I lodge this Deity; I wait upon this, this<br>   Deity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501309">9</a></font></span>To that Deity the sacrifice of him who has this knowledge is<br>    acceptable.</p>
