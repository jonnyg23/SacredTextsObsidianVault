 <p><a name="page_377"><font size="1" color="green">p. 377</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXLI</h3>  <h4 align="center">Hymn to the Asvins</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014101">1</a></font></span>Come as home-guardians, saving us from foemen, guarding our<br>    living creatures and our bodies,<br>   Come to the house to give us seed and offspring:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014102">2</a></font></span>Whatever with Indra ye be faring, Asvins, or resting in one<br>    dwelling-place with Vāyu,<br>   In concord with the Ribhus or Ādityas, or standing still in<br>   Vishnu's striding-places.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014103">3</a></font></span>When 1, O Asvins, call on you to-day that I may gather strength,<br>   Or as all-conquering might in war, be that the Asvins' noblest<br>    grace.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014104">4</a></font></span>Now come, ye Asvins, hitherward: here are oblations set for<br>    you;<br>   These Soma draughts to aid Yadu and Turvasa, these offered<br>    you mid Kanva's sons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014105">5</a></font></span>Whatever healing balm is yours, Nāsatyas near or far away,<br>   Therewith, great Sages, grant a home to Vatsa and to Vimāda.</p>
