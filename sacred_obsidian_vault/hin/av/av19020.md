 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XX</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer for protection from death and misfortune</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902001">1</a></font></span>May Soma, Varuna the King, both Asvins, Yama and Pūshan<br>    guard us well from Mrityu—<br>   Death caused by men, which Indra-Agni, Dhātar, Brihaspati and<br>   Savitar appointed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902002">2</a></font></span>All that the World's Lord made, all that for creatures Prajāpati<br>    and Mātarisvan fashioned,<br>   All things within the quarters and their spaces, let all these be<br>    my manifold defences.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902003">3</a></font></span>That which the Gods bound on them when they battled for their<br>    royal sway.<br>   What Indra made his shield, may that protect us well on every<br>    side,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902004">4</a></font></span>My shield is Heaven and Earth, my shield is the bright Day, my<br>    shield the Sun.<br>   The Visva Devas made my shield. Let not misfortune fall on<br>    me.</p>
