 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011601">1</a></font></span>Never may we be cast aside and strangers, as it were to thee.<br>   We, Thunder-wielding Indra, count ourselves as trees rejected<br>    and unfit to burn.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011602">2</a></font></span>O Vritra slayer, we were thought slow and unready for the fray:<br>   Yet once in thy great bounty may we have delight, O Hero,<br>    after praising thee.</p>
