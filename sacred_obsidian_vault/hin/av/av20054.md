 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005401">1</a></font></span>Of one accord they made and formed for kingship Indra, the<br>   Hero who in all encounters overcometh,<br>   Most eminent for power, destroyer in the conflict, fierce and<br>    exceeding strong, stalwart and full of vigour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005402">2</a></font></span>Bards joined in song to Indra so that he might drink the Soma<br>    juice, <a name="page_309"><font size="1" color="green">p. 309</font></a><br>   The Lord of Light, that he whose laws stand fast might aid with<br>    power and with help he gives.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005403">3</a></font></span>The holy sages form a ring, looking and singing to the Ram.<br>   Your very bright inciters, void of all deceit, are with the chan-<br>    ters nigh to hear.</p>
