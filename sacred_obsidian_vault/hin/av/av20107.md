 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010701">1</a></font></span>Before his hot displeasure all the peoples, all the men bow<br>    down,<br>   As rivers bend them to the sea.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010702">2</a></font></span>This power of his shone brightly forth when Indra brought to-<br>    gether like<br>   A skin the worlds of earth and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010703">3</a></font></span>The fiercely-moving Vritra's head he severed with his thunder-<br>    bolt,<br>   His hundred-knotted thunderbolt.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010704">4</a></font></span>In all the worlds That was the best and highest whence sprang<br>    the mighty God, of splendid valour.<br>   As soon as born he overcomes his foemen, he in whom all who<br>    lend him aid are joyful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010705">5</a></font></span>Grown mighty in his strength, with ample vigour, he as a foe<br>    strikes fear into the Dāsa,<br>   Eager to win the breathing and the breathless. All sang thy<br>    praise at banquet and oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010706">6</a></font></span>All concentrate on thee their mental vigour, what time these,<br>    twice or thrice, are thine assistants.<br>   Blend what is sweeter than the sweet with sweetness: win quickly<br>    with our meath that meath in battle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010707">7</a></font></span>Therefore in thee too, thou who winnest riches, at every banquet<br>    are the sages joyful<br>   With mighter power, bold God, extend thy firmness: let not<br>    malignant Yātudhānas harm thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010708">8</a></font></span>Proudly we put our trust in thee in battles, when we behold great<br>    wealth the prize of combat.<br>   I with my words impel thy weapons onward, and sharpen with<br>    my prayer thy vital vigour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010709">9</a></font></span>Worthy of praises many-shaped, most skilful, most energetic,<br>   Āptya of the Aptyas: <a name="page_351"><font size="1" color="green">p. 351</font></a><br>   He with his might destroys the seven Dānus, subduing many<br>    who were deemed his equals.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010710">10</a></font></span>Thou in that house which thy protection guardedh bestowest-<br>    wealth, the higher and the lower.<br>   Thou stablishest the two much-wandering Mothers, and bringest<br>    many deeds to their completion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010711">11</a></font></span>Brihaddiva, the foremost of light-winners, repeasts these holy<br>    prayers, this strength to Indra.<br>   He rules the great self-luminous fold of cattle, and all the doors<br>    of light hath he thrown open.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010712">12</a></font></span>Thou hath Brihaddiva the great Atharvan, spoken to Indra as<br>    himself in person.<br>   The Mātarisvarīs, the spotless sisters, with power exalt him and<br>    impel him onward.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010713">13</a></font></span>Bright, Presence of the Gods, the luminous herald, Siirya hath<br>    mounted the celestial regions.<br>   Day's maker, he hath shone away the darkness, and radiant<br>    passed o'er places hard to traverse.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010714">14</a></font></span>The brilliant Presence of the Gods hath risen, the eye of Mitra,<br>   Varuna, and Agni.<br>   The soul of all that moveth not or moveth, Sūrya hath filled the<br>    earth and air and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2010715">15</a></font></span>Even as a lover followeth a maiden, so doth the Sun the Dawn,<br>    refulgent Goddess:<br>   Where pious men extend their generations before the Gracious<br>   One for happy fortune.</p>
