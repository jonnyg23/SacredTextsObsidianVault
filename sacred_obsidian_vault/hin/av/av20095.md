 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009501">1</a></font></span>From the three jars the Great and Strong hath drunk drink blent<br>    with meal. With Vishnu hath he quaffed the flowing Soma<br>    juice, all that he would.<br>   That hath so heightened him the Great, the Vast, to do his<br>    mighty work.<br>   So may the God attend the God, true Indu Indra who is true.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009502">2</a></font></span>Sing strength to Indra that shall set his chariot in the foremost<br>    place. <a name="page_343"><font size="1" color="green">p. 343</font></a><br>   Giver of room in closest fight, slayer of foes in shock of war, be<br>    thou our great encourager. Let the weak bowstrings break<br>    upon the bows of feeble enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009503">3</a></font></span>Thou didst destroy the Dragon: thou sentest the rivers down to<br>    earth.<br>   Foeless, O Indra, wast thou born. Thou tendest well each<br>    choicest thing. Therefore we draw us close to thee. Let the<br>    weak bowstrings break upon the bows of feeble enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009504">4</a></font></span>Destroyed be all malignities and all our enemy's designs.<br>   Thy bolt thou castest at the foe, O Indra, who would smite us<br>    dead: thy liberal bounty gives us wealth. Let the weak bow-<br>    strings break upon the bows of feeble enemies.</p>
