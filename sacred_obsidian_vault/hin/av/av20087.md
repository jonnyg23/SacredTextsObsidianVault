 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008701">1</a></font></span>Priests, offer to the Lord of all the people the milked-out stalk<br>    of Soma, radiant-coloured.<br>   No wild bull knows his drinking-place like Indra who ever seeks<br>    him who hath pressed the Soma.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008702">2</a></font></span>Thou dost desire to drink, each day that passes, the pleasant<br>    food which thou hast had aforetime.<br>   O Indra, gratified in heart and spirit, drink eagerly the Soma set<br>    before thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008703">3</a></font></span>Thou, newly-born, for strength didst drink the Soma; thy<br>    mother told thee of thy future greatness.<br>   O Indra, thou hast filled mid-air's wide region, and given the<br>   Gods by battle room and freedom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008704">4</a></font></span>When thou hast urged the arrogant to combat, proud in their<br>    strength of arm, we will subdue them.<br>   Or, Indra, when thou fightest girt by heroes, we in the glorious<br>    fray with thee will conquer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008705">5</a></font></span>I will declare the earliest deeds of Indra, and recent acts which<br>   Maghavan hath accomplished.<br>   When he had conquered godless wiles and magic, Soma became<br>    his own entire possession.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008706">6</a></font></span>Thine is this world of flocks and herds around thee, which with<br>    the eye of Surya thou beholdest.<br>   Thou, Indra, art alone the Lord of cattle: may we enjoy the<br>    treasure which thou givest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008707">7</a></font></span>Ye twain are Lords of wealth in earth and heaven, thou, O<br>   Brihaspati, and thou, O Indra .<br>   Mean though he be, give wealth to him who lauds you. Preserve<br>    us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
