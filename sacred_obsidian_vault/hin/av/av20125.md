 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012501">1</a></font></span>Drive all our enemies away, O Indra, the western, mighty Con-<br>    queror, and the eastern,<br>   Hero, drive off our northern foes and southern, that we in thy<br>    wide shelter may be joyful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012502">2</a></font></span>What then? As men whose fields are full of barley reap the ripe<br>    corn removing it in order,<br>   So bring the food of those men, bring it hither, who come not<br>    to prepare the grass for worship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012503">3</a></font></span>Men come not with one horse at sacred seasons; thus they<br>    obtain no honour in assemblies.<br>   Sages desiring herds of kine and horses strengthen the mighty<br>   Indra for his friendship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012504">4</a></font></span>Ye, Asvins, Lords of Splendour, drank full draughts of grateful<br>   Soma juice,<br>   And aided Indra in his work with Namuchi of Asura birth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012505">5</a></font></span>As parents aid a son, both Asvins, Indra, aided thee with their<br>    wondrous powers and wisdom<br>   When thou, with might, hadst drunk the draught that glad-<br>    dens, Sarasvati, O Maghavan refreshed thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012506">6</a></font></span>Indra is strong to save, rich in assistance: may he, possessing<br>    all, be kind and gracious.<br>   May he disperse our foes and give us safety, and may we be the<br>    lords of hero vigour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012507">7</a></font></span>May we enjoy his favour, his the holy: may we enjoy his blessed<br>    loving-kindness.<br>   May this rich Indra, as our good protector, drive off and keep<br>    afar all those who hate us.</p>
