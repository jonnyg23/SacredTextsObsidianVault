 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009201">1</a></font></span>Praise, even as he is known, with song Indra the guardian of the<br>    kine,<br>   The Son of Truth, Lord of the brave.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009202">2</a></font></span>Hither his bay steeds have been sent, red steeds are on the sacred<br>    grass.<br>   Where we in concert sing our songs. <a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="green">p. 338</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009203">3</a></font></span>For Indra thunder-armed the kine have yielded mingled milk<br>    and meath.<br>   What time he found them in the vault.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009204">4</a></font></span>When I and Indra amount on high up to the bright One's place<br>    and home,<br>   We, having drunk of meath, will reach his seat whose Friends<br>    are three-times-seven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009205">5</a></font></span>Sing, sing ye forth your songs of praise, ye Priyamedhas, sing<br>    your songs:<br>   Yea, let young children sing their lauds: as a strong castle praise<br>    ye him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009206">6</a></font></span>Now loudly let the viol sound, the lute send out its voice with<br>    might,<br>   Shrill be the music of the string. To Indra is the hymn upraised.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009207">7</a></font></span>When hither speed the dappled cows, unflinching, easy to be<br>    milked,<br>   Seize quickly, as it bursts away, the Soma juice for Indra's<br>    drink.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009208">8</a></font></span>Indra hath drunk; Agni hath drunk all Deities have drunk their<br>    fill.<br>   Here Varuna shall have his home, to whom the floods have sung<br>    aloud as mother-kine unto their calves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009209">9</a></font></span>Thou, Varuna, to whom belong the Seven Streams, art a glorious<br>   God.<br>   The waters flow into thy throat as'twere a pipe with ample<br>    mouth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009210">10</a></font></span>He who hath made the fleet steeds spring, well-harnessed, to the<br>    worshipper,<br>   He, the swift guide, is that fair form thot loosed the horses near<br>    at hand.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009211">11</a></font></span>Indra, the very mighty, holds his enemies in utter scorn.<br>   He, far away, and yet a child, cleft the cloud smitten by his<br>    voice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009212">12</a></font></span>He, yet a boy exceeding small, mounted his newly-fashioned car.<br>   He for his Mother and his Sire cooked the wild mighty buffalo.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009213">13</a></font></span>Lord of the Home, with beauteous cheeks, ascend thy chariot<br>    wrought of gold.<br>   We will attend the heavenly One; the thousand-footed, red of<br>    hue, matchless, who blesses where he goes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009214">14</a></font></span>With reverence they come hitherward to him as to a sovran lord, <a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="green">p. 339</font></a><br>   That they may bring him near for this man's good success, to<br>    prosper and bestow his gifts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009215">15</a></font></span>The Priyamedhas have observed the offering of the men of old,<br>   Of ancient custom, while they strewed the sacred grass and<br>    spread their sacrificial food.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009216">16</a></font></span>He who as sovran Lord of men moves with his chariots<br>    unrestrained,<br>   The Vritra-slayer, queller of all fighting hosts, preeminent, is<br>    praised in song.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009217">17</a></font></span>Honour that Indra, Puruhanman! for his aid, in whose sustain-<br>    ing hand of old.<br>   The splendid bolt of thunder was deposited, as the great Sun<br>    was set in heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009218">18</a></font></span>No one by deed attains to him who works and strengthens<br>    evermore:<br>   No, not by sacrifice, to Indra praised of all, resistless, daring,<br>    bold in might;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009219">19</a></font></span>The potent Conqueror, invincible in war, him at whose birth<br>    the mighty ones,<br>   The kine who spread afar, sent their loud voices out, heavens,<br>    earths sent their loud voices out.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009220">20</a></font></span>O Indra, if a hundred heavens and if a hundred earths were<br>    thine<br>   No, not a thousand suns could match thee at thy birth, not<br>    both the worlds, O Thunderer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009221">21</a></font></span>Thou, Hero, hast performed thy hero deeds with might, yea, all<br>    with strength, O Strongest One.<br>   Maghavan, help us to a stable full of kine, O Thunderer, with<br>    wondrous aids.</p>
