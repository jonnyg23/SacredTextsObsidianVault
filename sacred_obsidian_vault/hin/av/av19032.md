 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm, with an amulet of Darbha grass, to subdue enemies and win the affection of others</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903201">1</a></font></span>For lengthened life I bind on thee the Darbha grass, the mighty<br>    plant.<br>   Excellent, hard to overthrow, with hundred stems and thousand<br>    blades.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903202">2</a></font></span>They cut not off his hair, they strike blow upon the breast for'<br>    him.<br>   To whom one gives protection by Darbha that hath uninjured<br>    leaves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903203">3</a></font></span>O Plant, thy root is in the sky, and thou art stationed on the<br>    earth:<br>   With thee who hast a thousand stalks we strengthen all the<br>    powers of life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903204">4</a></font></span>Through all three skies the plant hath pierced, and the three<br>    regions of the earth;<br>   With thee I split in twain the tongue and words of the bad-<br>    hearted man.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903205">5</a></font></span>Thou art victorious in thy might I am endowed with conquering<br>    strength:<br>   Possessed of overpowering force we two will quell our enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903206">6</a></font></span>Subdue our foeman, overcome the men who fain would fight:<br>    with us. <a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="green">p. 238</font></a><br>   Conquer all evil-hearted ones: make many well-disposed to me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903207">7</a></font></span>With Darbha that hath sprung from Gods, stationed in heaven,<br>    full many a time.<br>   With this have I won many men, have won and may I win them<br>    still.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903208">8</a></font></span>Do thou, O Darbha, make me dear to Brāhman and Rājanya,<br>    dear to Sūdra, and to Arya dear,<br>   Yea, dear to every man we love, to every man with eyes to see.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903209">9</a></font></span>He who first born fixed earth in her position, he who established<br>    heaven and air's mid-region,<br>   Whom sinner ne'er hath known as his supporter,—this Darbha<br>    be our shelter and protection!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903210">10</a></font></span>First of all plants it sprang into existence, victorious, hundred-<br>    stemmed, the foe-subduer,<br>   So may this Darbha from all sides protect us: with this may I<br>    subdue our foes in battle.</p>
