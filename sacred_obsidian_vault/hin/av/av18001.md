 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="green">p. 174</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XVIII</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">An accompaniment to funeral ceremonies and sacrificial offerings to ancestral spirits</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800101">1</a></font></span>Fain would I win my friend to kindly friendship. So may the<br>   Sage, come through the air's wide ocean,<br>   Remembering the earth and days to follow, obtain a son the<br>    issue of his father.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800102">2</a></font></span>Thy friend loves not the friendship which considers her who is<br>    near in kindred as a stranger.<br>   Sons of the mighty Asura, the heroes, supporters of the heaven,<br>    see far around them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800103">3</a></font></span>Yea, this the Immortals seek of thee with longing, a scion of the<br>    only man existing.<br>   Then let thy soul and mine be knit together. Embrace thy con-<br>    sort as her loving husband.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800104">4</a></font></span>Shall we do now what we ne'er did aforetime? we who spoke<br>    righteously now talk impurely?<br>   Gandharva in the floods, the Dame of Waters—such is our<br>    bond, such our most lofty kinship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800105">5</a></font></span>Even in the womb God Tvashtar, vivifier, shaping all forms,<br>   Creator, made us consorts.<br>   Ne'er are his holy statutes violated: that we are his the heaven<br>    and earth acknowledge.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800106">6</a></font></span>Who yokes to-day unto the pole of Order the strong and passio-<br>    nate steers of checkless spirit,<br>   With shaft-armed mouths, heart-piercing, joy-bestowing? Long<br>    shall he live who duly pays them service.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800107">7</a></font></span>Who knows that earliest day whereof thou speakest, Who hath<br>    beheld it? Who can here declare it?<br>   Great is the law of Varuna and Mitra. What, wanton, wilt thou<br>    say to men to tempt them?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800108">8</a></font></span>Yami am possessed by love of Yama, that I may rest on the<br>    same couch beside him.<br>   I as a wife would yield me to my husband. Like car-wheels let<br>    us speed to meet each other. <a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="green">p. 175</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800109">9</a></font></span>They stand not still, they never close their eyelids, those senti-<br>    nels of Gods who wander round us.<br>   Not me—go quickly, wanton, with another, and hasten like a<br>    chariot-wheel to meet him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800110">10</a></font></span>May Sūrya's eye with days and nights endow him, and ever may<br>    his light spread out before him.<br>   In heaven and earth the kindred pair commingle. On Yami be<br>    the unbrotherly act of Yama.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800111">11</a></font></span>Sure there will come succeeding times when brothers and sisters<br>    will do acts unmeet for kinsfolk.<br>   Not me, O fair one—seek another husband, and make thine arm<br>    a pillow for thy consort.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800112">12</a></font></span>Is he a brother when no help is left her? Is she a sister when<br>   Destruction cometh?<br>   Forced by my love these many words I utter. Come near, and<br>    hold me in thy close embraces,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800113">13</a></font></span>I am no help for thee, no refuge, Yami, I will not clasp and<br>    press thee to my bosom.<br>   This is abhorrent to my mind and spirit—a brother on the couch<br>    beside a sister.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800114">14</a></font></span>I will not fold mine arms about thy body: they call it sin when<br>    one comes near a sister.<br>   Not me—prepare thy pleasure with another. Thy brother seeks<br>    not this from thee, O fair one.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800115">15</a></font></span>Alas; thou art indeed a weakling Yama. We find in thee no trace<br>    o f heart or spirit.<br>   As round a tree the woodbine clings, another will cling about<br>    thee girt as with a girdle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800116">16</a></font></span>Embrace another, Yami. Let some other, even as the woodbine<br>    rings a tree, enfold thee.<br>   Win thou his heart and let him win thy fancy; so make with<br>    him a bond of blest alliance.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800117">17</a></font></span>Three hymns the Sages have disposed in order, the many-formed,<br>    the fair, the all-beholding.<br>   These in one single world are placed and settled—the growing<br>    plants, the breezes, and the waters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800118">18</a></font></span>The Bull hath yielded for the Bull the milk of heaven: inviolable<br>    is the Son of Aditi.<br>   According to his wisdom Varuna knoweth all: he halloweth, the<br>    holy, times for sacrifice. <a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="green">p. 176</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800119">19</a></font></span>Gandharvi spake. May she, the Lady of the Flood amid the<br>    river's roaring leave my heart untouched.<br>   May Aditi accomplish all that we desire, and may our eldest<br>   Brother tell us this as chief.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800120">20</a></font></span>Yea, even this blessed Morning, rich in store of food, splendid,<br>    with heavenly lustre, hath shone out for man,<br>   Since they as was the wish of yearning Gods, brought forth that<br>    yearning Agni for the assembly as the Priest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800121">21</a></font></span>And the fleet Falcon brought for sacrifice from afar this flowing.<br>    drop most excellent and passing wise,<br>   Then when the Aryan tribes chose as invoking Priest Agni the<br>    wonder-worker, and the hymn rose up.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800122">22</a></font></span>Still art thou kind to him who feeds thee as with grass, and.<br>    skilled in sacrifice offers thee holy gifts.<br>   When thou having received the sage's strengthening food with<br>    lauds, after long toil comest with many more.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800123">23</a></font></span>Urge thou thy Parents, as a lover, to delight: the lovely One<br>    desires and craves it from his heart.<br>   As Priest he calls aloud, as Warrior shows his skill, as Asura tries<br>    his strength, and with the hymn is stirred.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800124">24</a></font></span>Far famed is he, the mortal man, O Agni thou Son of strength,<br>    who hath obtained thy favour.<br>   He, gathering power, borne onward by his horses, makes his,<br>    days lovely in his might and splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800125">25</a></font></span>Hear us, O Agni, in the great assembly: harness thy rapid car,.<br>    the car of Amrit.<br>   Bring Heaven and Earth, the Deities' Parents, hither: stay with<br>    us here, nor from the Gods be absent.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800126">26</a></font></span>When, holy Agni, the divine assembly, the holy synod mid the<br>   Gods, is gathered,<br>   And when thou, godlike One, dealest forth treasures vouchsafe<br>    us too our portion of the riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800127">27</a></font></span>Agni hath looked upon the van of Mornings, and on the days.<br>    the earliest Jātavedas.<br>   After the Dawns, after their rays of brightness, Sūrya hath enter-<br>    ed into earth and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800128">28</a></font></span>Agni hath looked against the van of Mornings, against the days-<br>    the earliest Jātavedas;<br>   In many a place against the beams of Sūrya, against the heavens<br>    and earth hath he extended. <a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="green">p. 177</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800129">29</a></font></span>Heaven and Earth, first by everlasting Order, speakers of truth,<br>    are near enough to hear us,<br>   When the God, urging men to worship, sitteth as Priest, assum-<br>    ing all his vital vigour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800130">30</a></font></span>As God comprising Gods by Law eternal, bear, as the chief who<br>    knoweth, our oblation,<br>   Smoke-bannered with the fuel, radiant, joyous, better to praise<br>    and worship, Priest for ever.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800131">31</a></font></span>I praise your work .that ye may make me prosper: hear, Heaven<br>    and Earth, twain worlds that drop with fatness!<br>   While days and Gods go to the world of spirits, have let the<br>   Parents with sweet mead refresh us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800132">32</a></font></span>When the Cow's nectar wins the God completely, men here<br>    below are heaven's and earth's sustainers<br>   All the Gods come to this thy heavenly Yajus which from the<br>    motley Pair milked oil and water<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800133">33</a></font></span>Hath the King seized us? How have we offended against his<br>    holy Ordinance? Who knoweth?<br>   For even Mitra mid the Gods is angry. There are both song and<br>    wealth for those who come not.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800134">34</a></font></span>'Tis hard to understand the Immortal's nature, where she who<br>    is akin becomes a stranger.<br>   Guard ceaselessly, great Agni, him who ponders Yama's name<br>    easy to be comprehended.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800135">35</a></font></span>They in the synod where the Gods rejoice them, where they are<br>    seated in Vivasvan's dwelling,<br>   Have given the Moon his beams, the Sun his splendour: the<br>    two unweariedly maintain their brightness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800136">36</a></font></span>The counsel which the Gods meet to consider, their secret plan,<br>    of that we have no knowledge.<br>   There let God Savitar, Aditi, and Mitra proclaim to Varuna that<br>    we are sinless.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800137">37</a></font></span>Companions, let us learn a prayer to Indra whom the thunder<br>    arms,<br>   To glorify your bold and most heroic Friend.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800138">38</a></font></span>For thou by slaying Vritra art the Vritra-slayer, famed for<br>    might.<br>   Thou, Hero, in rich gifts surpassest wealthy chiefs.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800139">39</a></font></span>O'er the broad land thou goest like a Stega: here on vast earth<br>    let breezes blow upon us, <a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="green">p. 178</font></a><br>   Here hath our dear Friend Varuna, united, like Agni in the<br>    wood, shot forth his splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800140">40</a></font></span>Sing praise to him the chariot-borne, the famous, Sovran of men,<br>    the dread and strong destroyer.<br>   O Rudra, praised be gracious to the singer; let thy darts spare<br>    us and smite down another.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800141">41</a></font></span>The pious call Sarasvati, they worship Sarasvati while sacrifice<br>    proceedeth.<br>   The virtuous call Sarasvati to hear them. Sarasvati send bliss to<br>    him who giveth!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800142">42</a></font></span>Sarasvati is called on by the Fathers who come right forward to<br>    our solemn worship.<br>   Seated upon this sacred grass rejoice you. Give thou us<br>    strengthening food that brings no sickness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800143">43</a></font></span>Sarasvati, who comest with the Fathers, joying in hymns, O<br>   Goddess, and oblations,<br>   Give plenteous wealth to this the sacrificer, a portion, worth a<br>    thousand, of refreshment.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800144">44</a></font></span>May they ascend, the lowest, highest, midmost, the Fathers, who<br>    deserve a share of Soma.<br>   May they who have attained to life, the Fathers, righteous and<br>    gentle, aid us when we call them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800145">45</a></font></span>I have attained the gracious-minded Fathers, I have gained son<br>    and progeny from Vishnu.<br>   They who enjoy pressed juices with oblation, seated on sacred<br>    grass, come oftenest hither.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800146">46</a></font></span>Now be this homage offered to the Fathers, to those who passed<br>    of old and those who followed,<br>   Those who have rested in the earthly region and those who<br>    dwell among the happy races.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800147">47</a></font></span>Mātali prospers there with Kavyas, Yama with Angiras' sons,<br>   Brihaspati with singers.<br>   Exalters of the Gods, by Gods exalted, aid us those Fathers in<br>    our invocations?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800148">48</a></font></span>Yes, this is good to taste and full of sweetness, verily it is strong<br>    and rich in flavour.<br>   No one may conquer Indra in the battle when he hath drunken<br>    of the draught we offer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800149">49</a></font></span>Honour the King with your oblations, Yama, Vivasvān's son, who<br>    gathers men together. <a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="green">p. 179</font></a><br>   Even him who travelled o'er the mighty rivers, who searches out<br>    and shows the path to many.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800150">50</a></font></span>Yama first found for us the road to travel: this pasture never<br>    can be taken from us.<br>   Men born on earth tread their own paths that lead them whither<br>    our ancient Fathers have departed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800151">51</a></font></span>Fathers who sit on sacred grass, come, help us: these offsprings<br>    have we made for you; accept them.<br>   So come to us with most auspicious favour: bestow on us<br>    unfailing health and plenty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800152">52</a></font></span>Bowing their bended knees and seated southward let all accept<br>    this sacrifice with favour.<br>   Punish us not for any sin, O fathers which we through human<br>    frailty have committed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800153">53</a></font></span>Tvashtar prepares the bridal for his daughter: therefore the<br>    whole of this our world assembles.<br>   But Yama's mother, spouse of great Vivasvān, vanished as she<br>    was carried to her dwelling.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800154">54</a></font></span>Go forth, go forth upon the homeward pathways whither our<br>    sires of old have gone before us.<br>   Then shalt thou look on both the Kings enjoying their sacred<br>    food, God Varuna and Yama.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800155">55</a></font></span>Go hence, depart ye, fly in all directions. This world for him the<br>   Fathers have provided.<br>   Yama bestow upon this man a dwelling adorned with days and<br>    beams of light and waters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800156">56</a></font></span>We set thee down with yearning, and with yearning we enkindle<br>    thee,<br>   Yearning, bring yearning Fathers nigh to eat the food of<br>    sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800157">57</a></font></span>We, splendid men, deposit thee, we, splendid men, enkindle thee.<br>   Splendid, bring splendid Fathers nigh to eat the sacrificial food.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800158">58</a></font></span>Our Fathers are Angirases, Navagvas, Atharvans, Bhrigus, who<br>    deserve the Soma.<br>   May these, the holy, look on us with favour; may we enjoy<br>    their gracious loving-kindness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800159">59</a></font></span>Come, Yama, with Angirases, the holy; rejoice thee here with<br>    children of Virūpa.<br>   Seated on sacred grass at this oblation: I call Vivasvān too, thy<br>    father, hither. <a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="green">p. 180</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800160">60</a></font></span>Come, seat thee on this bed of grass. O Yama, accordant with<br>   Angirases and Fathers.<br>   Let texts recited by the sages bring thee. O. King, let this<br>    oblation make thee joyful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800161">61</a></font></span>He hath gone hence and risen on high mounting heaven's ridges<br>    by that path<br>   Whereon the sons of Angiras, the conquerors of earth, went up.</p>
