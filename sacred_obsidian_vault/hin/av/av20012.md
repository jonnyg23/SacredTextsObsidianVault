 <p><a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="green">p. 272</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001201">1</a></font></span>Prayers have been offered up through love of glory: Vasishtha,<br>    honour Indra in the battle.<br>   He who with might extends through all existence hears words<br>    which I, his faithful servant, utter.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001202">2</a></font></span>A cry was raised which reached the Gods, O Indra, a cry to<br>    them to send us strength in combat.<br>   None among men knows his own life's duration: bear us in<br>    safety over these our troubles.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001203">3</a></font></span>The Bays, the booty-seeking car I harness: my prayers have<br>    reached him who accepts them gladly.<br>   Indra, when he had slain resistless Vritras, forced with his might<br>    the two world-halves asunder.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001204">4</a></font></span>Like barren cows, moreover, swelled the waters: the singers<br>    sought thy holy rite, O Indra.<br>   Come unto us as with his team comes Vāyu: thou, through our<br>    solemn hymns, bestowest booty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001205">5</a></font></span>So may these gladdening draughts rejoice thee, Indra, the Mighty,<br>    very bounteous to the singer.<br>   Alone among the Gods thou pitiest mortals: O Hero, make thee<br>    glad at this libation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001206">6</a></font></span>Thus the Vasishthas glorify with praises Indra, the Mighty One,<br>    whose arm wields thunder.<br>   Praised, may he guard our wealth in kine and heroes. Ye Gods,.<br>    preserve us evermore with blessings.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001207">7</a></font></span>Impetuous, Thunderer, strong, quelling the mighty, King, potent,<br>   Vritra-slayer, Soma-drinker,<br>   May he come hither with his yoked bay horses. May Indra glad-<br>    den him at noon libation.</p>
