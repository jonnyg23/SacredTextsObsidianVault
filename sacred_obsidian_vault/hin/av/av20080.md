 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008001">1</a></font></span>Bring us, O Indra, name and fame, enriching, mightiest,<br>    excellent,<br>   Wherewith, O wondrous God, fair-cheeked and thunder-armed,<br>    thou hast filled full this earth and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008002">2</a></font></span>We call on thee, O King, mighty among the Gods, ruler of men,<br>    to succour us,<br>   All that is weak in us, excellent God, make firm: make our foes<br>    easy to subdue.</p>
