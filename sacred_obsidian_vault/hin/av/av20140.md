 <p><a name="page_376"><font size="1" color="green">p. 376</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXL</h3>  <h4 align="center">Continuation of the preceding hymn to the Asvins</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014001">1</a></font></span>What force, Nāsatyas, ye exert, whatever, Gods, ye tend and<br>    heal,<br>   This your own Vatsa gains not by his hymns alone: ye visit him<br>    who offers gifts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014002">2</a></font></span>Now hath the Rishi splendidly thought out the Asvins' hymn<br>    of praise.<br>   Let the Atharvan pour the warm oblation forth, and Soma very<br>    rich in sweets.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014003">3</a></font></span>Ye Asvins, now ascend your car that lightly rolls upon its way.<br>   May these my praises make you speed hitherward like a cloud of<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014004">4</a></font></span>When, O Nāsatyas, we this day make you speed hither N ith our<br>    hymns,<br>   Or, Asvins, with our songs of praise, remember Kānva specially.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014005">5</a></font></span>As erst Kakshivān and the Rishi Vyasva, as erst Dirghatamas<br>    invoked your presence,<br>   Or, in the sacrificial chambers, Vainya Prithī, so be ye mindful<br>    of us here, O Asvins.</p>
