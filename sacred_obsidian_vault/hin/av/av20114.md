 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011401">1</a></font></span>O Indra, from all ancient time rivalless ever and companionless<br>    art thou:<br>   In war thou seekest comradeship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2011402">2</a></font></span>Thou findest not the wealthy man to be thy friend: those scorn<br>    thee who are flown with wine.<br>   What time thou thunderest and gatherest, then thou, even as a<br>    father, art invoked.</p>
