 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer, accompanying sacrifice, for the attainment of a wish</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900401">1</a></font></span>The first oblation that Atharvan oared, earliest sacrifice paid by<br>   Jātavedas, <a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="green">p. 213</font></a><br>   Even this I, foremost, with repeated worship, now offer unto<br>    thee. May Agni carry the sacrificer's present. Hail to Agni!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900402">2</a></font></span>In front I set Intention, blessed Goddess. Mother of thought,<br>    may she be prompt to hear us.<br>   Mine, and mine only, be the hope I fashion! May I gain this<br>    that hath possessed my spirit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900403">3</a></font></span>With Purpose, O Prajāpati, with Purpose come thou near to us.<br>   Bestow on us a share of luck, and so be swift to hear our call.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900404">4</a></font></span>Prajāpati Angirasa with favour regard this word and this my<br>    settled purpose!<br>   May he, whence Gods and Deities had being Kāma attend us<br>    with his gentle guidance.</p>
