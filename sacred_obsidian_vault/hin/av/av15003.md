 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN III</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500301">1</a></font></span>For a whole year he stood erect. The Gods said unto him, Why<br>    standest thou, O Vrātya? He answered and said, Let them<br>    bring my couch.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500303">3</a></font></span>They brought the couch for that Vrātya.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500304">4</a></font></span>Two of its feet were Summer and Spring, and two were Autumn<br>    and the Rains.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500305">5</a></font></span>Brihat and Rathantara were the two long boards, Yajnāyajniya<br>    and Vāmadevya the two cross-boards.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500306">6</a></font></span>Holy verses were the strings lengthwise, and Yajus formulas the<br>    cross-tapes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500307">7</a></font></span>Sacred lore was the blanket, Devotion the coverlet.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500308">8</a></font></span>The Sāman was the Cushion, and chanting the bolster.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500309">9</a></font></span>The Vrātya ascended that couch.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500310">10</a></font></span>The hosts of Gods were his attendants, solemn vows his<br>    messengers, and all creatures his worshippers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1500311">11</a></font></span>All creatures become the worshippers of him who possesses this<br>    knowledge.</p>
