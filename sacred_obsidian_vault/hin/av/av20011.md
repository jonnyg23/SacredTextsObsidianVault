 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001101">1</a></font></span>Fort-render, Lord of Wealth, dispelling foemen, Indra with<br>    lightnings hath o'ercome the Dāsa.<br>   Impelled by prayer and waxen great in body, he hath filled<br>    earth and heaven, the bounteous Giver.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001102">2</a></font></span>I stimulate this zeal, the Strong, the Hero, decking my song of<br>    praise for thee Immortal.<br>   O Indra, thou art equally the leader of heavenly hosts and<br>    human generations.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001103">3</a></font></span>Leading his band Indra encompassed Vritra; weak grew the wily<br>    leader enchanters.<br>   He who burns fierce in forests slaughtered Vyansa, and made<br>    the milch-kine of nights apparent.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001104">4</a></font></span>Indra, light-winner, days' creator, conquered, as guardian, hostile<br>    bands with those who loved him.<br>   For man the days' bright ensign he illumined, and found the<br>    light for his great joy and gladness. <a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="green">p. 271</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001105">5</a></font></span>Forward to fiercely falling blows pressed Indra, hero-like doing<br>    many hero exploits.<br>   Those holy songs he taught the bard who praised him, and<br>    widely spread these Dawns' resplendent colour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001106">6</a></font></span>They laud the mighty acts of him the mighty, the many glorious<br>    deeds performed by Indra.<br>   He in his strength, with all-surpassing prowess, through wond-<br>    rous arts crushed the malignant Dasyus.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001107">7</a></font></span>Lord of the brave, Indra who rules the people gave freedom to<br>    the Gods by might and battle.<br>   Wise singers glorify with chanted praises these his achievements<br>    in Vivasvān's dwelling.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001108">8</a></font></span>Excellent, conqueror, the victory-giver, the winner of the light<br>    and godlike waters,<br>   He who hath won this broad earth and this heaven,—in Indra<br>    they rejoice who love devotions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001109">9</a></font></span>He gained possession of the Sun and horses; Indra obtained<br>    the cow who feedeth many.<br>   Treasure of gold he won; he smote the Dasyus and gave protec-<br>    tion to the race of Aryas.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001110">10</a></font></span>He took the plants and days for his possession; he gained the<br>    forest trees and air's mid-region.<br>   Vala he cleft, and chased away opponents: thus was he tamer<br>    of the overweening.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001111">11</a></font></span>Call we on Maghavan, auspicious Indra, best Hero in the fight<br>    where spoil is gathered,<br>   The Strong, who listens, who gives aid in battles, who slays the<br>   Vritras, wins and gathers treasures.</p>
