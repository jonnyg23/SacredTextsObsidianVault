 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXLII</h3>  <h4 align="center">Hymn to Dawn and the Asvins</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014201">1</a></font></span>Together with the Goddess, with the Asvins' Speech have I<br>    awoke.<br>   Thou, Goddess, hast disclosed the hymn and holy gift from<br>    mortal men.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014202">2</a></font></span>Awake the Asvins, Goddess Dawn! Up, mighty Lady of Sweet<br>   Strains!<br>   Rise straightway, priest of sacrifice! High glory to the glad-<br>    dening draught!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014203">3</a></font></span>Thou, Dawn, approaching with thy light, shinest together with<br>    the Sun, <a name="page_378"><font size="1" color="green">p. 378</font></a><br>   And to this man-protecting home the chariot of the Asvins<br>    comes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014204">4</a></font></span>When yellow stalks give forth the juice as cows from udders<br>    pour their milk,<br>   And voices sound the song of praise, the Asvins' worshippers<br>    show first.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014205">5</a></font></span>Forward for glory and for strength, protection that shall con-<br>    quer men,<br>   And power and skill, most sapient Ones!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2014206">6</a></font></span>When, Asvins worthy of our lauds, ye seat you in the father's<br>    house.<br>   With wisdom or the bliss ye bring.</p>
