 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006301">1</a></font></span>We will, with Indra, and all Gods to aid us, bring these existing.<br>    worlds into subjection.<br>   Our sacrifice, our bodies, and our offspring, let Indra form to-<br>    gether with the Ādityas.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006302">2</a></font></span>With the Ādityas with the band of Maruts, may Indra be pro-<br>    tector of our bodies;<br>   As when the Gods came, after they had slaughtered the Asuras,.<br>    keeping safe their godlike nature,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006303">3</a></font></span>Brought the Sun hitherward with mighty powers, and looked!<br>    about them on their vigorous God-head.<br>   With this may we obtain strength God-appointed, and brave<br>    sons gladden us through a hundred winters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006304">4</a></font></span>He who alone bestoweth might on mortal man who offereth<br>    gifts,<br>   The ruler of resistless power, is Indra, sure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006305">5</a></font></span>When will he trample like a weed the man who hath no gift for<br>    him?<br>   When verily will Indra hear our songs of praise?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006306">6</a></font></span>He who with Soma juice prepared among the many harbours.<br>    thee,<br>   Verily Indra gains thereby tremendous might. <a name="page_315"><font size="1" color="green">p. 315</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006307">7</a></font></span>Joy, mightiest Indra, known and marked, sprung most from<br>   Soma draughts, wherewith<br>   Thou smitest down the greedy fiend, for that we pray.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006308">8</a></font></span>Wherewith thou helpest Adhrigu, the great Dasagva, and the<br>   God<br>   Who stirs the sunlight, and Sea, for that we pray.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006309">9</a></font></span>Wherewith thou dravest forth like cars Sindhu and all the<br>    mighty floods.<br>   To go the way ordained by Law, for that we pray.</p>
