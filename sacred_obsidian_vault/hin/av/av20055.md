 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005501">1</a></font></span>Oft, oft I call that Indra, Maghavan the mighty, who evermore<br>    possesses power, ever resistless.<br>   Holy, most liberal, may he lead us on to riches, and, thunder<br>    armed, make all our pathways pleasant for us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005502">2</a></font></span>Indra, what joys as Lord of Light thou broughtest from the<br>   Asuras,<br>   Prosper therewith, O Maghavan, him who lauds that deed, and<br>    those whose grass is trimmed for thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005503">3</a></font></span>The wasteless share of steeds and kine which, Indra, thou hast<br>    fast secured.<br>   Grant to the worshipper who presses Soma and gives guerdon,<br>    not unto the churl.</p>
