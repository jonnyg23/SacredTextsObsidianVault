 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006101">1</a></font></span>We sing this strong and wild delight of thine which conquers in<br>    the fray,<br>   Which, Caster of the Stone, gives room and shine like gold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006102">2</a></font></span>Wherewith thou also foundest lights for Āyu and for Manu's<br>    sake:<br>   Now joying in!!this sacred grass thou beamest forth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006103">3</a></font></span>This day to singers of the hymn praise, as of old, this, might of<br>    thine.<br>   Win thou the waters, day by day, thralls of the strong.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006104">4</a></font></span>Sing forth to him whom many men invoke, to him whom many<br>    laud:<br>   Invite the potent Indra with your songs of praise;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006105">5</a></font></span>Whose lofty might—for doubly strong is he—supports the<br>    heaven and earth.<br>   And hills and plains and floods and light with manly power.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006106">6</a></font></span>Such, praised by many! thou art King: alone thou smitest foe-<br>    men dead,<br>   To gain, O Indra, spoils of war and high renown.</p>
