 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm accompanying investiture with an amulet that ensures safety and victory</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904601">1</a></font></span>For manly strength Prajāpati bound thee on first, invincible.<br>   This for long life on thee I bind for splendour, strength, and<br>    energy. Invincible, let it guard thee well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904602">2</a></font></span>Erect, invincible, be this man's watchful keeper: let not the<br>   Panis or the sorcerers harm thee.<br>   Shake off thy foes as Indra scattered Dasyus: quell all enemies.<br>   Invincible, let it guard thee well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904603">3</a></font></span>Indra hath lent the power of sight, and vital breath and strength<br>    to this.<br>   Whom even a hundred combatants, striking, have failed to over-<br>    come. Invincible, let it guard thee well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904604">4</a></font></span>Around thy limbs I place the mail of Indra who hath become<br>    the Gods' imperial Sovran.<br>   Again let all the Deities bring thee hither. Invincible let it guard<br>    thee well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904605">5</a></font></span>One and a hundred manly powers, a thousand lives hath this<br>   Amulet, unconquered ever.<br>   Go forth a tiger, strike down all thy foemen: let him who would<br>    oppose fall low beneath thee. Invincible, let it guard thee<br>    well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904606">6</a></font></span>Drawn forth from butter, rich in milk and sweetness, hundred-<br>    lived, thousand-homed, bestowing vigour,<br>   Kindly, delightsome, full of sap, and mighty, invincible let it<br>    guard thee well.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904607">7</a></font></span>That thou mayst be pre-eminent, slayer of rivals, rivalless, <a name="page_249"><font size="1" color="green">p. 249</font></a><br>   May Savitar cause thee to be chief and controller of thy kin.<br>   Invincible, let it guard thee well.</p>
