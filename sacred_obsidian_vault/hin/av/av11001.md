 <p><a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="green">p. 41</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XI</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">An accompaniment to the preparation and presentation of a Brahmaudana</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100101">1</a></font></span>Agni, spring forth! Here Aditi, afflicted, cooks a Brahmaudana,<br>    yearning for children.<br>   Let the Seven Rishis, World-creators, rub thee into existence<br>    here with gift of offspring.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100102">2</a></font></span>Raise, as I bid, the smoke, my strong companions, lovers of free-<br>    dom from deceit and malice!<br>   Victor in fight heroic, here is Agni by whom the Gods subdued<br>    the hostile demons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100103">3</a></font></span>Thou, Agni, wart produced for mighty valour, to cook Brahmau-<br>    dana, O Jātavedas.<br>   Seven Rishis, makers of the world, begat thee, Grant to this<br>    woman wealth with store of heroes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100104">4</a></font></span>Burn up, O Agni, kindled with the fuel. Knowing the Gods who<br>    merit worship, bring them.<br>   Cooking, for these, oblation, Jātavedas! lift up this man to<br>    heaven's most lofty summit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100105">5</a></font></span>Your portion from of old is triply parted, portion of Gods, of<br>   Fathers, and of mortals.<br>   Know, all, your shares. I deal them out among you. The portion<br>    of the Gods shall save this woman.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100106">6</a></font></span>Strong art thou, Agni, conquering, all-surpassing. Crush down<br>    our foemen, ruin those who hate us.<br>   So let this measure, measured, being measured, make all our kin<br>    thy tributary vassals.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100107">7</a></font></span>Increase with kinsmen and with all abundance: to mighty strength<br>    and power lift up this woman.<br>   Erect, rise upward to the sky's high station, rise to the lofty<br>    world which men call Svarga.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100108">8</a></font></span>May this great Earth receive the skin, this Goddess Prithivī,<br>    showing us her love and favour. Then may we go unto the<br>    world of virtue. <a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="green">p. 42</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100109">9</a></font></span>Fix on the skin these two joined press-stones, duly rending the<br>    fibres for the sacrificer.<br>   Strike down and slay those who assail this woman, and elevating<br>    raise on high her offspring.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100110">10</a></font></span>Grasp with thy hand, O man, the well-formed press-stones: the<br>    holy Gods have come unto thy worship.<br>   Three wishes of thy heart which thou electest, these happy gains<br>    for thee I here make ready.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100111">11</a></font></span>Here thy devotion is, here is thy birthplace. Aditi, Mother of<br>    brave sons, accept thee!<br>   Wipe away those who fight against this woman with wealth and<br>    store of goodly sons endow her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100112">12</a></font></span>Rest in the roaring frame of wood: be parted from husk and<br>    chaff, ye Sacrificial Fibres.<br>   May we surpass in glory all our rivals. I cast beneath my feet<br>    the men who hate us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100113">13</a></font></span>Go, Dame, and quickly come again: the waters, enclosed, have<br>    mounted thee that thou mayst bear them.<br>   Take thou of these such as are fit for service: skilfully separating.<br>    leave the others.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100114">14</a></font></span>Hither these Dames have come in radiant beauty. Arise and seize=<br>    upon thy strength, O woman.<br>   To thee hath sacrifice come: take the pitcher, blest with a good<br>    lord, children, children's children.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100115">15</a></font></span>Instructed by the Rishis, bring those waters, the share of strength<br>    which was of old assigned you.<br>   Let this effectual sacrifice afford you protection, fortune, off-<br>    spring, men, and cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100116">16</a></font></span>Agni, on thee the sacrificial caldron hath mounted: shining,.<br>    fiercely flaming, heat it.<br>   May hottest flames, divine, sprung from the Rishis, gathering,<br>    with the Seasons, heat this portion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100117">17</a></font></span>Purified, bright, and holy, let these Women, these lucid waters<br>    glide into the caldron.<br>   Cattle and many children may they give us. May he who cooks.<br>    the Odana go to heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100118">18</a></font></span>Ye, Sacrificial Rice and Soma Fibres, cleansed and made pure by<br>    prayer and molten butter. <a name="page_43"><font size="1" color="green">p. 43</font></a><br>   Enter the water: let the caldron take you. May he who dresses<br>    this ascend to heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100119">19</a></font></span>Expand thyself abroad in all thy greatness, with thousand Prish-<br>    thas, in the world of virtue.<br>   Grandfathers, fathers, children, and descendants, fifteenth am I<br>    to thee when I have dressed it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100120">20</a></font></span>With thousand streams and Prishthas, undecaying, Brahmaudana<br>    is celestial, God-reaching.<br>   Those I give up to thee with all their children. Force them to<br>    tribute, but to me be gracious.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100121">21</a></font></span>Rise to the altar: bless this dame with offspring. Promote this<br>    woman; drive away the demons.<br>   May we surpass in glory all our rivals. I cast beneath my feet the<br>    men who hate us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100122">22</a></font></span>Approach this woman here with store of cattle: together with<br>    the deities come to meet her.<br>   Let not a curse or imprecation reach thee: in thine own seat<br>    shine forth exempt from sickness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100123">23</a></font></span>Fashioned at first by Right, set by the spirit, this altar of Brahmau-<br>    dana was appointed.<br>   Place the pure boiler on it, woman! set thou therein the rice<br>    mess of Celestial Beings.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100124">24</a></font></span>This second hand of Aditi, this ladle which the Seven Rishis,<br>    world-creators, fashioned.<br>   May this scoop deftly pile upon the altar, therein, the members<br>    of the rice-oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100125">25</a></font></span>Let the dressed offering and divine Ones serve thee: creep from.<br>    the fire again, own these as masters.<br>   Made pure with Soma rest within the Brāhmans: let not thine<br>    eaters, Rishis' sons, be injured.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100126">26</a></font></span>Give understanding unto these, King Soma! all the good Brāh<br>    mans who attend and serve thee.<br>   Oft, in Brahmaudana, and well I call on: Rishis, their sons, and<br>    those who sprang from Fervour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100127">27</a></font></span>Here I set singly in the hands of Brāhmans these cleansed and.<br>    purifie d and holy Women,<br>   May Indra, Marut girt, grant me the blessing which as I sprinkle<br>    you, my heart desireth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100128">28</a></font></span>Here is my gold, a light immortal: ripened grain from the field<br>    this Cow of Plenty give me! <a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="green">p. 44</font></a><br>   This wealth I place among the Brāhmans, making a path that<br>    leads to heaven among the Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100129">29</a></font></span>Lay thou the chaff in Agni Jātavedas: remove the husks and<br>    drive them to a distance.<br>   That, we have heard, that is the House-Lord's portion: we know<br>    the share allotted to Destruction.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100130">30</a></font></span>Mark him who toils and cooks and pours oblation: make this<br>    man climb the path that leads to heaven,<br>   That he may mount and reach life that is highest, ascending to<br>    the loftiest vault above us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100131">31</a></font></span>Adhvaryu, cleanse that face of the Supporter. Make room, well<br>    knowing, for the molten butter.<br>   Purify duly all the limbs with fatness. I make a path to heaven<br>    amid the Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100132">32</a></font></span>Supporter, send to those men fiends and battle, to all non-Brah-<br>    mans who attend and serve thee.<br>   Famous and foremost, with their great possessions, let not these<br>    here, the Rishis sons, be injured.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100133">33</a></font></span>I set thee, Odana, with Rishis' children: naught here belongs to<br>    men not sprung from Rishis.<br>   Let Agni my protector, all the Maruts, the Visve Devas guard<br>    the cooked oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100134">34</a></font></span>May we adore thee, Sacrifice that yieldeth an everlasting son,<br>    cow, home of treasures,<br>   Together with increasing store of riches, long life and immor-<br>    tality of children.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100135">35</a></font></span>Thou art a Bull that mounts to heaven: to Rishis and their off-<br>    spring go.<br>   Rest in the world of pious men: there is the place prepared for<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100136">36</a></font></span>Level the ways: go thitherward, O Agni. Make ready thou the<br>   Godward-leading pathways.<br>   By these our pious actions may we follow sacrifice dwelling in<br>    the seven-rayed heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100137">37</a></font></span>May we invested with that light go upward, ascending to the<br>    sky's most lofty summit.<br>   Wherewith the Gods, what time they had made ready<br>   Brahmaudana, mounted to the world of virtue.</p>
