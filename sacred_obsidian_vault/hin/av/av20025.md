 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002501">1</a></font></span>Indra, the mortal man well guarded by thine aid goes foremost<br>    in the wealth of horses and of kine.<br>   With amplest wealth thou fillest him, as round about the waters<br>    clearly seen afar fill Sindhu full.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002502">2</a></font></span>The heavenly waters come not nigh the priestly bowl: they but<br>    look down and see how far mid-air is spread:<br>   The Deities conduct the pious man to them: like suitors they<br>    delight in him who loveth prayer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002503">3</a></font></span>Praiseworthy blessing hast thou laid upon the pair who with<br>    uplifted ladle serve thee, man and wife.<br>   Unchecked he dwells and prospers in thy law: thy power brings<br>    blessing to the sacrificer pouring gifts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002504">4</a></font></span>First the Angirases won themselves vital power, whose fires were<br>    kindled through good deeds and sacrifice.<br>   The men together found the Pani's hoarded wealth, the cattle,<br>    and the wealth in horses and in kine,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002505">5</a></font></span>Atharvan first by sacrifices laid the path; then, guardian of the<br>   Law, sprang up the loving Sun.<br>   Usanā Kāvya drove the kine hither with him: let us with offer-<br>    ings honour Yama's deathless birth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002506">6</a></font></span>When sacred grass is trimmed to aid the auspicious work, or<br>    the hymn makes its voice of praise sound to the sky,<br>   Where the stone rings as 'twere a singer skilled in laud,—Indra<br>    in truth delights when these come near to him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002507">7</a></font></span>To make thee start, a strong true draught I offer to thee the<br>   Bull, O thou whom bay steeds carry.<br>   Here take delight. O Indra, in our voices while thou art hymned<br>    with power and all our spirit.</p>
