 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000901">1</a></font></span>As cows low to their calves in stalls, so with our songs we<br>    glorify<br>   This Indra, even your wondrous God who checks attack, who<br>    joys in the delicious juice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000902">2</a></font></span>Celestial, bounteous Giver, girt about with might, rich, moun-<br>    tain-like, in precious things<br>   Him swift we seek for foodful booty rich in kine, brought<br>    hundredfold and thousandfold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000903">3</a></font></span>I crave of thee that hero strength, that thou mayst first regard<br>    this prayer,<br>   Wherewith thou helpest Bhrigu and the Yatis and Praskanva<br>    when the prize was staked.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000904">4</a></font></span>Wherewith thou sentest mighty waters to the sea, that, Indra, is.<br>    thy hero strength.<br>   For ever unattainable is this power of him to whom the worlds.<br>    have called aloud.</p>
