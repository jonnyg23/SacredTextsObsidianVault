 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to all waters</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900201">1</a></font></span>Blest be the Streams from hills of snow, sweet be spring Waters<br>    unto thee.<br>   Sweet be swift-running Waters, sweet to thee be Water of the<br>   Rains. <a name="page_212"><font size="1" color="green">p. 212</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900202">2</a></font></span>Sweet unto thee be Waters of the waste and Waters of the pool.<br>   Sweet be the Waters dug from earth, to thee, and Waters<br>    brought in jars.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900203">3</a></font></span>To those who delve without a tool to dig, the wise, the deeply<br>    moved,<br>   To Waters better healers than physicians we address our prayer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900204">4</a></font></span>Bathed in the Waters verily divine, in water of the streams,<br>   Bathed in the Waters verily, O Horses, be ye fleet and strong.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900205">5</a></font></span>Blest be the Waters unto thee, suspicious Waters, bringing.<br>    health.<br>   They cure the injured place for thee even as thy comfort craveth<br>    it.</p>
