 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn accompanying investiture with an amulet of gold</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902601">1</a></font></span>Gold that was born from Fire is immortal hath been deposited<br>    with mortal creatures.<br>   He who knows this deserves to own this jewel, and in extreme<br>    old age dies he who wears it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902602">2</a></font></span>The men of ancient time with children round them longed for<br>    this Gold, bright with the Sun's own colour,<br>   This shall endow thee, as it shines, with splendour, and long<br>    shall be the life of him who wears it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902603">3</a></font></span>Long life and splendour let it bring energy and strength to thee.<br>   That thou mayst shine among the folk with all the brightness of<br>    the Gold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1902604">4</a></font></span>What Varuna the King knows well, and what the God<br>   Brihaspati,<br>   And Indra, Slayer of the Foe, may that bestow long life on thee,<br>    may that increase thy splendid strength.</p>
