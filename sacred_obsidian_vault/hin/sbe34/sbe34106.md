 <p>37. And on account of (Gautama) proceeding (to initiate <i>G</i>âbâla) on the ascertainment of (his) not being that (i.e. a <i>S</i>ûdra).</p> 
 <p>The <i>S</i>ûdras are not qualified for that reason also that Gautama, having ascertained <i>G</i>âbâla not to be a <i>S</i>ûdra from his speaking the truth, proceeded to initiate and instruct him. 'None who is not a Brâhma<i>n</i>a would thus speak out. Go and fetch fuel, friend, I shall initiate you. You have not swerved from the truth' (<i>Kh</i>. Up. IV, 4, 5); which scriptural passage furnishes an inferential sign (of the <i>S</i>ûdras not being capable of initiation).</p>
