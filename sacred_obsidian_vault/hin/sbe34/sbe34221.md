 <p>45. And on account of contradictions.</p> 
 <p>Moreover, manifold contradictions are met with in the Bhâgavata system, with reference to the assumption of qualities and their bearers. Eminence of knowledge and</p> 
 <p><a name="page_443"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 443</font></a></p> 
 <p>ruling capacity, strength, valour, and glory are enumerated as qualities, and then they are in some other place spoken of as Selfs, holy Vâsudevas, and so on.--Moreover, we meet with passages contradictory of the Veda. The following passage, for instance, blames the Veda, 'Not having found the highest bliss in the Vedas <i>S</i>â<i>nd</i>ilya. studied this <i>s</i>âstra.'--For this reason also the Bhâgavata doctrine cannot be accepted.</p> </body>
