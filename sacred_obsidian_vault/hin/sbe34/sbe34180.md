 <p>4. And because (the pradhâna), on account of there existing nothing beyond it, stands in no relation; (it cannot be active.)</p> 
 <p>The three gu<i>n</i>as of the Sâ<u>n</u>khyas when in a state of equipoise form the pradhâna. Beyond the pradhâna there exists no external principle which could either impel the pradhâna to activity or restrain it from activity. The soul (purusha), as we know, is indifferent, neither moves to--nor restrains from--action. As therefore the pradhâna stands in no relation, it is impossible to see why it should sometimes modify itself into the great principle (mahat) and sometimes not. The activity and non-activity (by turns) of the Lord,</p> 
 <p><a name="page_371"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 371</font></a></p> 
 <p>on the other hand, are not contrary to reason, on account of his omniscience and omnipotence, and his being connected with the power of illusion (mâyâ).</p>
