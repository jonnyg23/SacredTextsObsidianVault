 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 71</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE NINTH.</h3>  
 <p>OUR sacrifice, God Savitar, speed onward: speed to his share<br> the sacrifice's patron.<br> May the celestial Gandharva, cleanser of thought and will,<br> make clean our thought and purpose: the Lord of Speech<br> sweeten the food we offer.<br> 2 Thee, firmly set, settled in man, in spirit.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. I take thee, draught acceptable<br> to Indra. This is thy home. Thee, welcomest to Indra.<br> Thee Set in waters, butter, realm of ether. Taken upon<br> ......Indra.<br> Thee seated in the sky, earth, air's mid-region, among the<br> Gods and in the vault of heaven. Taken, etc. as above:<br> 3 The strength-arousing essence of the waters, gathered in<br> the Sun, Essence of waters essence, that, most excellent,<br> I take for you.<br> Taken, etc., as above.<br> 4 Cups of strength-giving sacrifice, inspirers of the sage's<br> hymn<br> Of you, the handleless, have I collected all the sap and<br> strength.<br> Taken, etc., as above.<br> United are ye twain: with bliss unite me. Parted are ye:<br> keep me apart from evil.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_72"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 72</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 Thou art the thunderbolt of Indra, winner of wealth: with<br> thee may this man win him riches.<br> In gain of wealth we celebrate with praises her, Aditi by<br> name, the Mighty Mother,<br> On whom this Universe of life hath settled. Thereon God<br> Savitar promote our dwelling!<br> 6 Amrit is in the Waters, in the Waters healing medicine.<br> Yea, Horses! at our praises of the Waters grow ye fleet<br> and strong.<br> Whatever wave, O ye celestial Waters, wealth-giving,<br> towering high, and swiftly rushing, is yours, therewith<br> may this man win him riches.<br> 7 It was the wind, or it was thought, or the Gandharvas<br> twenty-seven<br> These at the first harnessed the horse: they set the power<br> of speed in him.<br> 8 Steed, being yoked grow wind-swift: be beauteous as Indra's<br> right-hand steed.<br> Omniscient Maruts harness thee! Tvashtar put swiftness in<br> thy feet!<br> 9 What speed, O Horse, was laid in thee in secret, what<br> passed in wind, bestowed upon the falcon,<br> With that same strength be strong for us, O Courser,<br> wealth-winning and victorious in battle.<br> Starting to run your course, winners of riches, smell ye<br> Brihaspati's portion, O ye Horses.<br> 10 By impulse of God Savitar, true Impeller, may ascend<br> Brihaspati's highest heaven.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 73</font></a></p>  
 <p>By impulse of God Savitar, true Impeller, may I ascend<br> the highest heaven of Indra.<br> By impulse of God Savitar, true Impeller, Brihaspati's<br> highest heaven have I ascended.<br> By impulse of God Savitar, true Impeller, I have ascended<br> Indra's loftiest heaven.<br> 11 Brihaspati, win the prize. Lift up your voices to Brihaspati.<br> Make ye Brihaspati win the prize.<br> Do thou, O Indra, win the prize. To Indra lift your voices<br> up. Make Indra winner of the prize.<br> 12 True hath been this your league whereby ye made<br> Brihaspati win the prize.<br> Brihaspati have ye caused to win the prize. Be freed, ye<br> Forest-lords.<br> Faithful was this your league whereby ye have made Indra<br> win the prize.<br> Ye have made Indra win the prize. Be ye set free, ye<br> Forest-lords.<br> 13 Through impulse of God Savitar, true Impeller, mine be<br> Brihaspati's prize who winneth prizes.<br> On to the goal, ye Steeds, winners of prizes, blocking the<br> ways and meting out the courses!<br> 14 Bound by the neck and at the flanks and in the mouth,<br> that vigorous Courser lends new swiftness to his sped.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 74</font></a></p>  
 <p>Drawing himself together as his strength allows, Dadhikrâs<br> speeds along the windings of the paths. All-hail!<br> 15 His pinion, rapid runner, fans him on his way, as of a bird<br> that hastens onward to its aim,<br> And, as it were a falcon's gliding through the air, strikes<br> Dadhikrâvan's side as he speeds on with might. All-hail!<br> 16 Bless us the Coursers when we call, while slowly they move,<br> strong singers, to the Gods assembly.<br> Crushing the wolf, the serpent, and the demons, may they<br> completely banish all affliction. All-hail!<br> 17 May all those vigorous Coursers listen to our cry, hearers<br> of invocation, speeders on their way;<br> Winners of thousands, fain to win where meed is won, who<br> gather of themselves great wealth in every race.<br> 18 Deep-skilled in Law Eternal, wise, immortal, O Coursers,<br> help us in each fray for booty.<br> Drink of this meath, be satisfied, be joyful: then go on<br> paths which Gods are wont to travel.<br> 19 To me come plenteous growth of wealth! Approach me<br> these, Heaven and Earth, who wear each form and figure!<br> Hither may Father come to me, and Mother. Soma with<br> immortality approach me!<br> 20 To the Friend, Hail! To the Good Fiend, Hail! To the<br> Later-born, Hail! To Resolution, Hail! To the Vasu, Hail!<br> To the Lord of Days, Hail! To the Failing Day, Hail!<br> To the Failing sprung from the Transitory, Hail! To the<br> Transitory sprung from the Final, Hal! To the Final<br> Mundane, Hail! To the Lord of the World, Hail! To the<br> Sovran Lord, Hail!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a></p>  
 <p>21 May life succeed through sacrifice. May life-breath thrive<br> by sacrifice. May the eye thrive by sacrifice. May the<br> ear thrive by sacrifice. May the back thrive by sacrifice.<br> May sacrifice thrive by sacrifice.<br> We have become the children of Prajâpati. Gods, we have<br> gone to heaven. We have become immortal.<br> 22 In us be your great might and manly vigour, in us be your<br> intelligence and splendour.<br> Obeisance to our Mother Earth! Obeisance to our Mother<br> Earth!<br> This is thy Sovranty. Thou art the ruler, thou art controller,<br> thou art firm and stedfast.<br> Thee for land-culture, thee for peace and quiet, thee for<br> wealth, thee for increase of our substance.<br> 23 Of old the furtherance of strength urged onward this Sovran<br> Soma in the plants and waters.<br> For us may they be stored with honey: stationed in front<br> may we be watchful in the kingdom. All-hail!<br> 24 The furtherance of strength extended over this heaven and<br> all the worlds as sovran ruler.<br> He, knowing, makes the churl a bounteous giver: wealth<br> may he grant us with full store of heroes. All-hail<br> 25 Surely the furtherance of strength pervaded all these<br> existing worlds in all directions.<br> From olden time the King moves round, well knowing,<br> strengthening all the people and our welfare.<br> 26 As suppliants, for aid we grasp Soma the King, and Agni, the<br> Âdityas, Vishnu, Sûrya, and the Brahman-priest Brihaspati.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 76</font></a></p>  
 <p>27 Urge Aryaman to send us gifts, and Indra. and Brihaspati,<br> Yak, Vishnu, and Sarasvatî, and the strong Courser Savitar.<br> 28 Agni, speak kindly to us here, be graciously inclined to us.<br> Winner of thousands, grant us boons, for thou art he who<br> giveth wealth.<br> 29 Let Aryaman vouchsafe us wealth, and Pûshan, and<br> Brihaspati.<br> May Vâk the Goddess give to us. All-hail!<br> 30 Thee by the radiant Savitar's impulsion, with arms of<br> Asvins, with the hands of Pûshan.<br> To <a href="errata.htm#3">Vâk</a> Sarasvatî's controlling guidance, hers the controlling<br> leader, I consign thee.<br> I with Brihaspati's supreme dominion endow thee by the<br> balm of consecration<br> 31 With the monosyllable Agni won vital breath: may I win<br> that. With the dissyllable the Asvins won bipeds: may<br> I win those. With the trisyllable<br> Vishnu won the three worlds: may I win those. With<br> quadrisyllabic metre<br> Soma won four-footed cattle: may I win those.<br> 32 With five-syllable metre Pûshan won the five regions: may<br> I win them.<br> With six-syllable metre Savitar won the six seasons: may<br> I win them.<br> With seven-syllable metre the Maruts won the seven<br> domestic animals:<br> May I win them. With octosyllabic metre Brihaspati won<br> the Gâyatrî: may I win that.<br> 33 With nine-syllable metre Mitra won the Trivrit Stoma:<br> may I win that. With decasyllabic metre Varuna won<br> Virâj: may I win that.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 77</font></a></p>  
 <p>With hendecasyllabic metre Indra won Trishtup: may I<br> win that.<br> With dodecasyllabic metre the All-Gods won Jagatî: may<br> I win that.<br> 34 The Vasus by thirteen-syllable metre won the Thirteenfold<br> Stoma: may I win that. The Rudras by fourteen-syllable<br> metre won the fourteenfold Stoma: may I win that. The<br> Âdityas with fifteen-syllable metre won the Fifteenfold<br> Stoma: may I win that. Aditi with sixteen-syllable metre<br> won the Sixteenfold Stoma: may I win that. Prajâpati<br> with seventeenfold metre won the Seventeenfold Stoma:<br> may I win that.<br> 35 This is thy portion, Nirriti! Accept it graciously. All-hail!<br> To Gods whose guide is Agni, to the eastward-seated Gods,<br> All-hail!<br> To Gods whose guide is Yama, to the southward-seated<br> Gods, All-hail!<br> To Gods whose guides are the All-Gods, those who are seated<br> westward, Hail!<br> Hail to the northward-seated Gods, to those whose guides<br> are Mitra and Varuna or the Marut host!<br> To Gods whose guide is Soma, who, worshipful, sit on high,<br> All-hail!<br> 36 Gods who have Agni as their guide, whose seat is eastward,<br> Hail to them!<br> Gods who have Yama as their guide, whose seat is southward,<br> Hail to them!<br> Gods who have All-Gods as their guides, whose seat is westward,<br> Hail to them!<br> Gods who have Mitra-Varuna for guides, north-seated,<br> Hail to them!<br> Gods who have Soma as their guide, high-seated, worshipful,<br> Hail to them!<br> 37 Agni, subdue opposing bands and drive our enemies away.<br> Invincible, slay godless foes: give splendour to the worshipper.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 78</font></a></p>  
 <p>38 Thee at the radiant Savitar's impulsion, with Asvins arms<br> and with the hands of Pûshan,<br> I offer with the strength of the Upâmsu. Slain is the demon<br> brood. All-hail!<br> Thee for the slaughter of the brood of demons. The demons<br> have we slain, have slain. So-and-So, So-and-So is slain.<br> 39 Savitar quicken thee for sway of rulers, Agni of householders,<br> of the trees Soma,<br> Brihaspati of Speech, for lordship Indra, Rudra for cattle,<br> Mitra for true-speaking, Varuna for the sway of Law's<br> protectors.<br> 40 Gods, quicken him that none may be his rival, for mighty<br> domination, mighty lordship,<br> Him, son of Such-a-man and Such-a-woman, of Such-a-tribe.<br> This is your King, ye Tribesmen. Soma is Lord and King<br> of us the Brâhmans.</p>
