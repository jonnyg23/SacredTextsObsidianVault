 <p><a name="page_264"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 264</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE THIRTY-SECOND.</h3>  
 <p>AGNI is That; the Sun is That; Vâyu and Chandramâs are<br> That.<br> The Bright is That; Brahma is That, those Waters, that<br> Prajâpati.<br> 2 All twinklings of the eyelid sprang from Purusha,<br> resplendent One.<br> No one hath comprehended him above, across, or in the<br> midst.<br> 3 There is no counterpart of him whose glory verily is great.<br> In the beginning rose Hiranyagarbha, etc. Let not him<br> harm me, etc. Than whom there is no other born, etc.<br> 4 This very God pervadeth all the regions; yea, born aforetime,<br> in the womb he dwelleth.<br> He verily born and to be born hereafter meeteth his offspring,<br> facing all directions.<br> 5 Before whom naught whatever sprang to being; who with<br> his presence aids all living creatures,<br> Prajâpati, rejoicing in his offspring, he, Shodasî, maintains<br> the Three great Lustres.<br> 6 By whom the heavens are strong and earth stands firmly,<br> by whom light's realm and sky-vault are supported;<br> By whom the regions in mid-air were measured. What<br> God shall we adore with our oblation?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 265</font></a></p>  
 <p>7 To whom, supported by his help, two armies embattled look<br> while trembling in their spirit,<br> Where over them the risen Sun is shining. What God shall<br> we adore with our oblation?<br> What time the mighty waters, etc. He in his might surveyed, etc.<br> 8 The Sage beholdeth That mysterious Being wherein this<br> All hath found one only dwelling.<br> Therein unites the Whole, and thence it issues: far-spread it<br> is the warp and woof in creatures.<br> 9 Knowing Eternity, may the Gandharva declare that station,<br> parted, kept in secret.<br> Three steps thereof in mystery are hidden: he who knows<br> these shall be the father's father.<br> 10 He is our kin, our Father and Begetter: he knows all beings<br> and all Ordinances,<br> In whom the Gods obtaining life eternal have risen upward<br> to the third high station.<br> 11 Having encompassed round existing creatures, the world;<br> and all the Quarters and Mid-quarters,<br> Having <a href="errata.htm#15">approached</a> the first-born Child of Order he with his<br> Self into The Self hath entered.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_266"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 266</font></a></p>  
 <p>12 Having gone swiftly round the earth and heaven, around<br> the worlds, around the sky, the Quarters,<br> Having spread out the lengthened thread of Order, he views,<br> and he becomes and is That Being.<br> 13 To the Assembly's wondrous Lord, to Indra's lovely Friend<br> who gives<br> Wisdom. have I drawn near in prayer.<br> 14 That wisdom which the Companies of Gods, and Fathers,<br> recognize,<br> Even with that intelligence, O Agni, make me wise to-day.<br> All-hail!<br> 15 Varuna grant me wisdom! grant it Agni and Prajâpati!<br> Wisdom may Indra, Vâyu grant. May the Creator grant it<br> me. All-hail!<br> 16 Let these the Priests and Nobles both enjoy the splendour<br> that is mine.<br> Best splendour may the Gods bestow on me. To thee, that<br> splendour, hail!</p>
