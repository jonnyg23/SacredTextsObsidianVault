 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE TWENTY-SECOND.</h3>  
 <p>SPLENDOUR art thou, bright, deathless, life-protector.<br> Protector of my life be thou.<br> By impulse of God Savitar I take thee with arms of Asvins,<br> with the hands of Pûshan.<br> 2 This girdle, which in their religious meetings sages assumed <br> in earlier time of worship,<br> Is present with us here at this libation, in the Law's hymn,<br> proclaiming rich abundance.<br> 3 Famous art thou, thou art the world, controller and<br> upholder thou.<br> Go, consecrate by Svâhâ to Agni Vaisvânara widely-famed.<br> 4 For Gods and for Prajâpati I fit thee. For Gods and for<br> Prajâpati, O Brahman,<br> Will I tie up the horse. Thence may I prosper! Binding<br> him for Prajâpati and Gods be thou successful.<br> 5 Thee welcome to Prajâpati I sprinkle. I sprinkle thee<br> welcome to Indra-Agni. I sprinkle thee acceptable to Vâyu.<br> Thee welcome to the All-Gods I besprinkle. Thee<br> welcome to all Deities I sprinkle.<br> With fury Varuna attacks the man who fain would slay the<br> steed.<br> Avaunt the man! Avaunt the dog!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>  
 <p>6 To Agni Hail! To Soma Hail! Hail to the Waters Joy! Hail<br> to Savitar! Hail to Vâyu! Hail to Vishnu! Hail to Indra!<br> Hail to Brihaspati! Hail to Mitra! Hail to Varuna!<br> 7 Hail to the sound <i>hiṅ!</i> Hail to the uttered <i>hiṅ!</i> Hail to<br> the neigh! Hail to the down-neigh! Hail to the snort!<br> Hail to the roar! Hail to his smell! Hail to him smelt<br> at! Hail to him seated! Hail to him seated down! Hail<br> to him weary! Hail to him going! Hail to him sitting!<br> Hail to him lying! Hail to him sleeping! Hail to him<br> waking! Hail to him whinnying! Hail to him wakened!<br> Hail to him yawning! Hail to him outstretched! Hail to<br> him drawn together! Hail to him risen! Hail to his<br> going! Hail to his good going! Hail!<br> 8 Hail to him as he goes! Hail to him running! Hail to him<br> running away! Hail to him when he has run away!<br> Hail to the cry Shoo! Hail to him scared with Shoo!<br> Hail to him seated! Hail to him risen! Hail to his<br> speed! Hail to his strength! Hail to him rolling! Hail<br> to him when he has rolled! Hail to him tossing about!<br> Hail to him when he has tossed about! Hail to him<br> listening! Hail to him hearing. Hail to him looking!<br> Hail to him looked at! Hail to him closely looked at!<br> Hail to his closing his eye! Hail to his food! Hail to<br> his drink! Hail to his stale! Hail to him in action!<br> Hail to what he has done!<br> 9 May we attain that excellent glory of Savitar the God:<br> So may he stimulate our prayers.<br> 10 For our protection I invoke the golden-handed Savitar:<br> He knoweth, as a God, the place.<br> 11 We specially invoke the grace of Savitar, observant God,<br> The great good-will that gives true boons.<br> 12 We seek the eulogy and gift of Savitar who strengthens grace,<br> Yea, of the God who knows our thoughts.<br> 13 I invocate the heroes Lord, free-giving Savitar, and call<br> The Cheerer to the feast of Gods.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>  
 <p>14 The judgment of bright Savitar, that cheers the All-Gods<br> company,<br> With prayer we estimate as bliss.<br> 15 Wake Agni with thy laud and set the Immortal One aflame,<br> let him<br> Bestow our offerings on the Gods.<br> 16 Oblation-bearer, well-inclined, immortal, eager Messenger,<br> Agni comes near us with the thought.<br> 17 Agni, Envoy, I place in front, the oblation-bearer I address:<br> Here let him seat the Deities.<br> 18 Yea, Pavamâna, thou didst generate the Sun and spread<br> the moisture out with power,<br> Basting to us with plenty vivified with milk.<br> 19 Mighty through thy dam, eminent through thy sire, thou<br> art a horse, thou art a steed, thou art a courser, thou<br> art a comfort, thou art a racer, thou art a yoke-horse,<br> thou art a strong steed, thou art a stallion, thou art<br> manly-minded. Thou art called Yayu, thou art called<br> Sisu. Follow thou the flight of the Âdityas.<br> Gods, Warders of the Regions, protect for the Gods this<br> horse besprinkled for sacrifice.<br> Here is delight. Here take thy pleasure. Here is content.<br> Here is self-content.<br> 20 Hail to Ka! Hail to Who?! Hail to Which?! Hail to<br> him who has experienced pain! Hail to Prajâpati who<br> knows the mind! Hail to him who discerns the thought!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a></p>  
 <p>Hail to Aditi! Hail to good Aditi! Hail to gracious<br> Aditi! Hail to Sarasvatî! Hail to purifying Sarasvatî!<br> Hail to great Sarasvatî! Hail to Pûshan! Hail to<br> Pûshan of the highways! Hail to Pûshan observer of<br> men! Hail to Tvashtar! Hail to swift Tvashtar! Hail<br> to Tvashtar of many forms! Hail to Vishnu! Hail to<br> Vishnu Nibhûyapa! Hail to Vishnu Sipivishta!<br> 21 Let every mortal man elect, etc., repeated from IV. 8.<br> 22 O Brahman, let there be born in the kingdom the Brahman<br> illustrious for religious knowledge; let there be born the<br> Râjanya, heroic, skilled archer, piercing with shafts,<br> mighty warrior; the cow giving abundant milk; the ox<br> good at carrying; the swift courser; the industrious<br> woman. May Parjanya send rain according to our desire;<br> may our fruit-bearing plants ripen; may acquisition and<br> preservation of property be secured to us.<br> 23 Hail to vital breath! Hail to out-breathing! Hail to<br> diffusive breath! Hail to the eye! Hail to the ear! Hail<br> to Speech! Hail to Mind!<br> 24 Hail to the Eastern Region! Hail to the hitherward Region!<br> Hail to the Southern Region! Hail to the hitherward<br> Region! Hail to the Western Region! Hail to the<br> hitherward Region! Hail to the Northern Region! Hail<br> to the hitherward Region! Hail to the Upward Region!<br> Hail to the hitherward Region! Hail to the Downward<br> Region! Hail to the hitherward Region!<br> 25 Hail to waters! Hail to floods! Hail to water! Hail to<br> standing waters! flail to flowing waters! Hail to trickling<br> waters! Hail to well waters! Hail to spring waters! Hail<br> to the foaming sea! Hail to the ocean! Hail to the deep!<br> 26 Hail to wind! Hail to mist! Hail to vapour! Hail to<br> cloud! Hail to cloud lightening! Hail to cloud thundering!<br> Hail to it bursting! Hail to it raining! Hail to it<br> pouring! Hail to it violently raining! Hail to it swiftly<br> raining! Hail to it holding up! Hail to it when it has<br> held up! Hail to it sprinkling! Hail to it drizzling! Hail<br> to its drops! Hail to thunderbolts! Hail to hoar frosts!<br> 27 Hail to Agni! Hail to Soma! Hail to Indra! Hail to Earth!<br> Hail to Firmament! Hail to Sky! Hail to Regions? Hail<br> to Quarters! Hail to the Upward Region! Hail to the<br> Downward Region!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a></p>  
 <p>23 Hail to the lunar asterisms! Hail to those connected with<br> the lunar asterisms! Hail to Day and Night! Hail to<br> the half-months! Hail to the mouths! Hail to the Seasons!<br> Hail to the Season-groups! Hail to the Year!<br> Hail to Heaven and Earth! Hail to the Moon! Hail to<br> the Sun! Hail to his rays! Hail to the Vasus! Hail to<br> the Rudras! Hail to the Âdityas! Hail to the Maruts!<br> Earl to the All-Gods! Hail to roots! Hail to branches!<br> Hail to forest trees! Hail to flowers! Hail to fruits!<br> Hail to herbs!<br> 29 Hail to Earth! Hail to Firmament t Hail to Sky! Hail to<br> Sun! Hail to Moon! Hail to lunar asterisms! Hail to<br> waters! Hail to herbs! Hail to forest trees! Hail to creatures<br> that swim! Hail to things moving and stationary!<br> Hail to things that creep and crawl!<br> 30 Hail to breath! Hail to the Vasu! Hail to the Mighty!<br> Hail to Vivasvân! Hail to the trooping one! Hail to<br> the Troop's Lord! Hail to the Superior! Hail to the<br> Overlord! Hail to Strength! Hail to Samsarpa! Hail<br> to the Moon! Hail to light! Hail to Malimlucha! Hail<br> to him who flies by day!<br> 31 Hail to Madhu! Hail to Mâdhava! Hail to Sukra! Hail to<br> Suchi! Hail to Nabhas! Hail to Nabhasya! Hail to Isha!<br> Hail to Ûrja! Hail to Sahas! Hail to Sahasya! Hail to<br> Tapas! Hail to Tapasya! Hail to Amhasaspati!<br> 32 Hail to Strength! Hail to impulse! Hail to After-born!<br> Hail to will! Heaven, Hail? Hail to the head! Hail to<br> Vyasnuvin! To the final, Hail! Hail to the mundane<br> final! Hail to the Lord of the world! Hail to the Overlord!<br> Hail to the Lord of Creatures!<br> 33 May life succeed by sacrifice, Hail! May breath succeed<br> by sacrifice, Hail! May downward breath, diffusive.<br> breath, upward breath, digestive breath, vision, hearing,<br> speech, mind, self, devotion, light, heaven, hymn-arrangement,<br> sacrifice succeed by sacrifice. All-hail!<br> 34 Hail to One! Hail to Two! Hail to Hundred! Hail to<br> Hundred-and-One! Hail to Daybreak! Hail to Heaven!</p>
