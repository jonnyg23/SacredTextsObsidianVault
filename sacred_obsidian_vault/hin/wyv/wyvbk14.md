 <p><a name="page_124"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 124</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE FOURTEENTH.</h3>  
 <p>WITH stedfast site and birthplace thou art stedfast: settle<br> thou duly in thy stedfast birthplace, rejoicing in the<br> Ukhya's first appearance.<br> Here let the Asvins, the Adhvaryus, seat thee.<br> 2 Nesting, intelligent, dripping with butter, in the auspicious<br> seat of earth be seated.<br> Let Rudras, Vasus welcome thee with praises: fill full these<br> prayers for our propitious fortune.<br> Here let the Asvins, the Adhvaryus, seat thee.<br> 3 Here, Guard of Strength, with thine own powers be seated<br> for the Gods happiness and great enjoyment.<br> Even as a father to his son, be friendly: with easy entrance<br> enter with thy body. Here let the Asvins, the Adhvaryus<br> seat thee:<br> 4 Thou art the filling-stuff of earth called Apsas. May all the<br> Gods celebrate thee with praises.<br> Enriched with songs of praise, Prishthas and butter, sit here<br> and give us wealth with store of children.<br> Here let the Asvins, etc.<br> 5 Upon the back of Aditi I lay thee the sky's supporter, pillar<br> of the Quarters,<br> Queen over creatures. Wave and drop of waters art thou;<br> and Visvakarman is thy Rishi.<br> 6 Sukra and Suchi, seasons, both, of summerthou art the<br> innermost cement of Agni.<br> May Heaven and Earth, may Waters, Plants and Agnis help,<br> separate, accordant, my precedence.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_125"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 125</font></a></p>  
 <p>Let all the Agnis twixt the earth and heaven gather together<br> round these summer seasons, as the Gods gather in their<br> hosts round Indra,<br> Firm, with that Deity, Angiras-like, be seated.<br> 7 Associate with the Seasons, with the Modes with the Gods,<br> with the health-establishing Godsmay the Asvins the<br> Adhvaryus settle thee here for Agni Vaisvânara.<br> Associate.............with the Vasus, etc.<br> Associate.............with the Rudras, etc.<br> Associate.............with the Âdityas, etc.<br> Associate.............with the Visvedevas, etc.<br> 8 Guard thou my breath. Guard my out-breathing. Guard<br> my through-breathing. Illume mine eye with far-reaching<br> vision. Give power of hearing to mine ear. Pour<br> forth waters. Quicken plants. Protect bipeds. Protect<br> quadrupeds. Send rain from heaven.<br> 9 The head is vital vigour. Prajâpati became the metre.<br> Royalty is vital vigour, health-giving metre. The Supporter<br> is vital vigour, the Sovran Lord the metre. Visvakarman<br> is vital vigour, Parameshthin the metre. The he-goat is<br> vital vigour, excellent the metre. The bull is vital vigour,<br> extensive the metre. Man is vital vigour, languid the<br> metre. The tiger is vital vigour, invincible the metre. The<br> lion is vital vigour, covering the metre. The four-year<br> bull is vital vigour, Brihatî the metre. The ox is vital<br> vigour, Kakup the metre. The steer is vital vigour,<br> Satobrihatî the metre.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_126"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 126</font></a></p>  
 <p>10 The bullock is vital vigour, Pankti the metre. The milch-cow<br> is vital vigour, Jagatî the metre. The eighteen-month<br> calf is vital vigour, Trishtup the metre. The two<br> year old steer is vital vigour, Virâj the metre. The<br> thirty-month old ewe is vital vigour, Gâyatrî the metre. The<br> three year old steer is vital vigour, Ushnih the metre.<br> The four year old ox is vital vigour, Anushtup the metre.<br> Fill up the room, etc. The dappled kine, etc. All sacred<br> songs, etc. are three texts repeated from XII. 54-56.<br> See also XIII. 58.<br> 11 Indra and Agni, in its place securely set the unshaking brick.<br> Thou with thy back sunderest heaven and the broad earth<br> and firmament.<br> 12 On the air's back let Visvakarman set thee, thee the capacious,<br> thee the far-extended.<br> Control the air, fix firm the air, do thou the air no injury.<br> For all breath, out-breath, through-breath, upward breathing,<br> for high position, for prescribed observance,<br> May Vâyu keep thee safe with great well-being, with securest<br> shelter. In the manner of Angiras, with that Deity<br> lie steady.<br> 13 Queen art thou, Quarter of the East. Wide-ruler, Quarter<br> of the South. West Quarter, thou art Sovran. Thou<br> Autocrat, Quarter of the North.<br> Queen Paramount art thou, the Lofty Point.<br> 14 On the air's back may Visvakarman set thee luminous.<br> Control all light for all breath, for out-breath, up-breath,<br> through-breath.<br> Thy Lord is Vâyu, with that Deity, Angiras-like, lie firm.<br> 15 Two Rainy Seasons, Nabhas and Nabhasyathou art the<br> innermost cement of Agni, etc. (as in XIII. 25).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 127</font></a></p>  
 <p>16 Isha and Ûrja, two Autumnal Seasonsthou art the innermost<br> cement of Agni, etc.<br> 17 Preserve my life. Preserve my breath. Guard mine<br> out-breath. Preserve mine eyes. Preserve mine ears.<br> Strengthen my voice. Quicken my mind. Preserve my<br> self. Vouchsafe me light.<br> 18 Mâ metre. Pramâ metre. Pratimâ metre. Âsrivayas metre.<br> Pankti metre. Ushnih metre. Brihatî metre. Anushtup<br> metre. Virâj metre. Gâyatrî metre, Trishtup metre.<br> Jagatî metre.<br> 19 Earth metre. Sky metre. Heaven metre. Years metre.<br> Nakshatras metre. Vâk metre. Mind metre. Husbandry<br> metre. Gold metre. Cow metre. She-goat metre. Horse<br> metre.<br> 20 The Deity Agni. The Deity Vita. The Deity Sûrya. The<br> Deity Moon. The Deity Vasus. The Deity Rudras. The<br> Deity Âdityas The Deity Maruts. The Deity Visvedevas.<br> The Deity Brihaspati. The Deity Indra. The Deity<br> Varuna.<br> 21 Chief art thou, bright, supporting, firm, thou art the great<br> sustainer, Earth.<br> Thee for life, thee for lustre, thee for tillage, thee for peace<br> and rest.<br> 22 Controller, brilliant art thou, managing controller, firm<br> sustainer. For strength, for energy thee, for riches thee,<br> for prosperity thee.<br> Fill up the room, etc. The dappled kine, etc. All sacred<br> songs, etc. (as in 10).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 128</font></a></p>  
 <p>23 This Swift, the triple praise-song. The Shining, the<br> Pañchadasa hymn. Heaven, the Saptadasa. The Supporter, the<br> Ekavimsa. Speed, the Ashtâdasa. Ardour, the Navadasa.<br> Triumphant Onset, Savimsa, Vigour, Dvâvimsa. Maintenance,<br> Trayovimsa. Womb, Chaturvimsa. Embryos,<br> Pañchavimsa. Energy, the Trinava. Intention, the Ekatrimsa.<br> The Basis, the Trayastrimsa. The Bright One's<br> Station, the Chatustrimsa. The Vault of Heaven, the<br> Shattrimsa. The Revolving One, the Ashtâchatvârimsa.<br> The Support, the Four-divisioned praise-song.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 129</font></a></p>  
 <p>24 Thou art the portion of Agni, chief control of Consecration.<br> The Priesthood is saved; the Trivrit Stoma.<br> Thou art the portion of Indra, the sovranty of Vishnu. The<br> Nobility is saved; the Pañchadasa Stoma.<br> Thou art the share of the Man-beholders; the supremacy of<br> the Creator; the birthplace is saved; the Saptadasa Stoma.<br> Thou art the share of Mitra, the sovranty of Varuna. Rain<br> of heaven and wind are saved; the Ekavimsa Stoma.<br> 25 Thou art the share of the Vasus, the sovranty of the Rudras.<br> Quadrupeds are saved; the Chaturvimsa Stoma.<br> Thou art the share of the Âdityas; the sovranty of the Maruts.<br> The Embryos are saved; the <a href="errata.htm#5">Pañchavimsa</a> Stoma.<br> Thou art the share of Aditi; the sovranty of Pûshan.<br> Strength is saved; the Trinava Stoma.<br> Thou art the share of God Savitar; the sovranty of Brihaspati.<br> The universal Quarters are saved; the Chatushtoma<br> Stoma.<br> 26 Thou art the share of the Yavas; the sovranty of the Ayavas.<br> Creatures are saved; the Chatuschatvârimsa Stoma.<br> Thou art the share of the Ribhus; the sovranty of the<br> Visvedevas. The Being is saved; the Trayastrimsa Stoma.<br> 27 Sahas, Sahasya, the two Winter Seasonsthou art the innermost<br> cement of Agni, etc. (as in XIII. 25).<br> 28 With one they praised; creatures were produced. Prajâpati<br> was over-lord.<br> With three they praised; the Priesthood was created. Brihaspati<br> was over-lord.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 130</font></a></p>  
 <p>With five they praised; beings were created. The Lord of<br> Beings was over-lord.<br> With seven they praised; the Seven Rishis were created.<br> Dhâtar was over-lord.<br> 29 With nine they praised; the Fathers were created. Aditi<br> was Sovran Lady.<br> With eleven they praised; the Seasons were created. The<br> Season-Lords were over-lords.<br> With thirteen they praised; the Months were created. The<br> Year was over-lord.<br> With fifteen they praised; the Nobility was created. Indra<br> was over-lord.<br> With seventeen they praised; domestic animals were created.<br> Brihaspati was over-lord.<br> 30 With nineteen they praised; Sûdra and Arya were created.<br> Day and Night were Sovran Ladies.<br> With twenty-one they praised; solid-hoofed animals were<br> created. Varuna was over-lord.<br> With twenty-three they praised; small animals were created.<br> Pûshan was over-lord.<br> With twenty-five they praised; forest animals were created.<br> Vâyu was over-lord.<br> With twenty-seven they praised; earth and heaven came<br> apart. Vasus, Rudras, Âdityas followed separately, so<br> they were over-lords.<br> 31 With twenty-nine they praised; Trees were created. Soma<br> was over-lord.<br> With thirty-one they praised; creatures were created. The<br> Yavas and the Ayavas were over-lords.<br> With thirty-three they praised; living beings. were happy.<br> Prajâpati, the Supreme in Place, was over-lord.<br> Fill up the room, etc. The dappled kine, etc. All sacred<br> songs, etc. Repeated from XII. 14-16.</p>
