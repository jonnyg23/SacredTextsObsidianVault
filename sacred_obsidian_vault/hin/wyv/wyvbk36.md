 <p><a name="page_291"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 291</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE THIRTY-SIXTH.</h3>  
 <p>REFUGE I take in Speech as Rich: refuge in Mind as<br> Yajus-text; refuge in Breath as Sâma-chant; refuge in<br> Hearing and in Sight.<br> Speech-energy endowed with strength, inbreath and<br> outbreath are in me.<br> 2 Whatever deeply-sunk defect I have of eye, or mind, or<br> heart, that way Brihaspati amend!<br> Gracious to us be he, Protector of the world.<br> 3 Earth! Ether! Heaven! May we attain that excellent, etc.,<br> as in III. 33.<br> With what help will he come to us etc., as in XXVII. 39.<br> 5 What genuine, etc., as in XXVII. 40.<br> 6 Do thou who art, etc., as in XXVII. 41.<br> 7 O Hero, with what aid dost thou delight us, with what<br> succour bring<br> Riches to those who worship thee?<br> 8 Indra is king of all that is: may weal attend our bipeds and<br> our quadrupeds.<br> 9 Gracious he Mitra unto us, and Varuna and Aryaman;<br> Indra, Brihaspati be kind, and Vishnu of the mighty stride.<br> 10 Pleasantly blow the wind for us, may Sûrya warm us<br> pleasantly.<br> Pleasantly, with a roar, the God Parjanya send the rain on us.<br> 11 May days pass pleasantly for us, may nights draw near<br> delightfully.<br> Befriend us with their aids Indra and Agni, Indra and<br> Varuna who taste oblations.<br> Indra and Pûshan be our help in battle, Indra and Soma<br> give health, strength, and comfort.<br> 12 May the celestial Waters, our helpers, be sweet for us to drink,<br> And flow with health and strength to us.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_292"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 292</font></a></p>  
 <p>13 Pleasant be thou to us, O Earth, etc., as in XXXV. 21.<br> 14 Ye, Waters, are, etc., as in XL 50.<br> 15 Give us a portion, etc., as in XI. 51.<br> 16 To you we gladly come, etc., as in XI. 52,<br> 17 Sky alleviation, Air alleviation, Earth alleviation, Plants<br> alleviation,<br> Trees alleviation, All-Gods alleviation, Brahma alleviation,<br> Universe alleviation, just Alleviation alleviationmay<br> that alleviation come to me!<br> 18 Caldron, strengthen me. May all beings regard me with the<br> eye of a friend. May I regard all beings with the eye of<br> a friend.<br> With the eye of a friend do we regard one another.<br> 19 Do thou, O Caldron, strengthen me. Long may I live to<br> look on thee. Long may I live to look on thee.<br> 20 Obeisance to thy wrath and glow, etc., as in XXII. 11.<br> 21 Homage to thee the lightning flash, homage to thee the<br> thunder's roar!<br> Homage, O Bounteous Lord, to thee whereas thou fain<br> wouldst win to heaven!<br> 22 From whatsoever trouble thou desirest, give us safety thence.<br> Give to our children happiness and to our beasts security.<br> 23 To us let Waters and let Plants be friendly, etc., as in VI. 23.<br> 21 Through hundred autumns may we see that bright Eye,<br> God-appointed, rise,<br> A hundred autumns may we live.<br> Through hundred autumns may we hear; through hundred<br> autumns clearly speak: through hundred autumns live<br> content; a hundred autumns, yea, beyond a hundred<br> autumns may we see.</p>
