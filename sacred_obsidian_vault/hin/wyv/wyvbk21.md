 <p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 196</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE TWENTY-FIRST.</h3>  
 <p>VARUNA, hear this call of mine: be gracious unto us this<br> day.<br> Longing for help I yearn for thee.<br> 2 I ask this of thee with my prayer, etc., as in XVIII. 49.<br> 3 Do thou who knowest Varuna, O Agni, put far away from<br> us the God's displeasure.<br> Best Sacrificer, brightest One, refulgent, remove thou far<br> from us all those who hate us.<br> 4 Be thou the nearest unto us, O Agni, our closest Friend<br> while now this Morn is breaking.<br> Reconcile Varuna to us, be bounteous: show thy compassion<br> and be swift to hear us.<br> 5 We call to succour us the mighty Mother of those whose<br> sway is just, the Queen of Order,<br> Strong-ruler, far-expanding, neer decaying, Aditi gracious<br> guide and good protectress.<br> 6 Sinless may we ascend, for weal, this vessel rowed with<br> good oars, divine, that never leaketh,<br> Earth our strong guard, incomparable Heaven. Aditi gracious<br> guide and good protectress.<br> 7 May I ascend the goodly ship, free from defect, that leaketh<br> not,<br> Moved by a hundred oars, for weal.<br> 8 O Mitra-Varuna, gracious Pair, with fatness dew our<br> pasturage,<br> With mead the regions of the air.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>9 Stretch forth your arms and let our lives be lengthened:<br> with fatness dew the pastures of our cattle.<br> Ye Youthful, make us famed among the people: hear,<br> Mitra-Varuna, these mine invocations.<br> 10 Bless us the Coursers when we call, etc., as in IX. 16.<br> 11 Deep-skilled in Law, etc., as in IX. 18.<br> 12 Kindled is Agni with the brand, yea, kindled well, the<br> excellent.<br> The metre Gâyatrî, the steer of eighteen months, give power<br> and life!<br> 13 Tanûnapât whose acts are pure, our bodies guard Sarasvatî,<br> Ushnihâ metre and the steer of two years age give power<br> and life!<br> 14 Agni with offerings, meet for praise, and Soma the immortal<br> God,<br> Anushtup metre and the steer of thirty months give power<br> and life!<br> 15 Agni with goodly grass spread out, deathless with Pûshan<br> at his side,<br> Brihatî metre and a steer of three years age give power<br> and life!<br> 16 The Doors divine, the mighty Regions, Brahma, God<br> Brihaspati,<br> The metre Pankti, here a bull in his fourth year, give<br> power and life<br> 17 The two young Darns of lovely form, the deathless Universal<br> Gods,<br> The Trishtup metre, here, a bull in his sixth year, give<br> power and life!<br> 18 The two celestial Hotars, both Physicians, Indra's close-knit<br> friends,<br> The metre Jagatî, an ox who draws the wain, give power and<br> life!<br> 19 The Three, Ida, Sarasvatî, and Bhâratî, the Marut folk,<br> Virâj the metre, here, a cow in milk, a bull, give power and<br> life!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a></p>  
 <p>20 Tvashtar the wondrous, full of seed, Indrâgnî furtherers of<br> weal,<br> Dvipadâ metre, and a cow and vigorous bull give power and<br> life!<br> 21 Our slaughterer, Vanaspatî, Savitar who promoteth wealth,<br> The metre Kakup, here, a cow who casts her calf, give power<br> and life!<br> 22 With Svâhâ mighty Varuna give healing power to Sacrifice!<br> The Atichhandas, Brihat, and a steer and bull give power<br> and life!<br> 23 With the Spring Season may the Gods the Vasus praised<br> with triple hymn<br> And with Rathantara, give life to Indra, splendour, sacrifice.<br> 24 With Summer may the Rudras, Gods, praised in the Pañchadasa<br> hymn<br> With Brihat, give to Indra strength; with fame, and sacrifice<br> and life.<br> 25 May the Âdityas with the Rains, lauded in Saptadasa<br> hymn<br> And with Vairûpa, with folk, strength, give Indra sacrifice<br> and life.<br> 26 With Autumn may the Ribhus, Gods, praised in the Ekavimsa<br> hymn<br> And with Virâja, give with grace to Indra grace, life,<br> sacrifice.<br> 27 With Winter may the Maruts, Gods, praised in the laud of<br> thrice nine parts,<br> The Sakvarîs, with strength give might to Indra, sacrifice<br> and life.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a></p>  
 <p>28 With Dew-time may the deathless Gods praised in the<br> Thirty-three-part laud,<br> The Revatîs, with truth give sway to Indra, sacrifice and<br> life.<br> 29 Let the Hotar sacrifice with fuel to Agni in the place of<br> libation, to the Asvins, Indra, Sarasvatî. A grey-coloured<br> he-goat with wheat, jujube-fruit and sprouts of rice<br> becomes a sweet salutary remedy, splendour, might, milk,<br> Soma. Let them enjoy sweet butter with foaming liquor.<br> Hotar, present offerings of butter.<br> 30 Let the Hotar, Tanûnapât, worship Sarasvatî. A sheep,<br> a ram, a salutary remedy on the honey-sweet path, bearing<br> to the Asvins and Indra heroic strength, with jujube-fruit,<br> Indra-grains, sprouts of rice, becomes a salutary<br> remedy, milk, Soma. Let them enjoy, etc., as in verse 29.<br> 31 Let the Hotar worship Narâsamsa and the Lord Nagnahu.<br> A ram with Surâ a salutary remedy, Sarasvatî the Physician,<br> the golden car of the Asvins, the victim's omentum,<br> with jujube-fruit, Indra-grains, and rice-sprouts, become<br> a salutary remedy, the manly strength of Indra, milk,<br> Soma. Let them, etc.<br> 32 Let the Hotar, magnified with oblations, offering sacrifice,<br> worship Sarasvatî and Indra, increasing them with<br> strength, with a bull and a cow. Strength and medicine<br> to the Asvins and Indra are meath with jujube-fruit,<br> Mâsara with parched grain, milk, Soma. Let them, etc.<br> 33 Let the Hotar worship the wool-soft Altar-grass, the Physicians<br> Nâsatyas, the Physicians Asvins. A mare with a<br> foal, a milch-cow is a physician. Sarasvatî the Physician<br> yields medicine to Indra, milk, Soma. Let them enjoy, etc.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p>34 Let the Hotar worship the Doors, the Regions, the resounding,<br> expansive Doors, the Regions, with the Asvins. Indra<br> milks the two milky worlds. The Mulch-cow Sarasvatî<br> yields medicine for the Asvins and Indra, pure light and<br> strength. Milk, Soma. Let them, etc.<br> 35 Let the Hotar worship the two fair-formed Dawns. At<br> night and by day the Asvins with Sarasvatî compose<br> impetuous power, like healing balm, in Indra, like a falcon,<br> Mâsara with light, thought, and grace. Milk, Soma. Let<br> them enjoy, etc.<br> 36 Let the Hotar worship the two divine Hotars, the Physician<br> Asvins, and Indra. Watchfully by day and night Sarasvatî<br> as Physician, with balms, with lead, yields strength<br> and power. Milk, Soma. Let them enjoy, etc.<br> 37 Let the Hotar worship the three Goddesses. The three active<br> ones, with three sacrificial elements, lay balm and golden<br> hue on Indra. The Asvins, Idâ, BhâratîSarasvatî with<br> Speech yields might and power to Indra. Milk, Soma.<br> Let them enjoy, etc.<br> 38 Let the Hotar worship Tvashtar full of good seed, the Bull<br> active for men, Indra, the Asvins, Sarasvatî the Physician.<br> Vigour, speed, power, a fierce wolf as physician,<br> fame with Surâ is a medicine, Mâsara with grace. Milk,<br> Soma. Let them enjoy, etc.<br> 39 Let the Hotar worship Vanaspatî the Immolator, the Lord<br> of Hundred Powers, and awful Passion, the King, the<br> Tiger, and the Asvins, with reverence. Sarasvatî the<br> Physician yields wrath and power to Indra. Milk, Soma.<br> Let them enjoy, etc.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 201</font></a></p>  
 <p>40 Let the Hotar worship Agni. Of the drops of clarified butter,<br> Svâhâ! Of the fat, omentum, etc., severally, Svâhâ!<br> Svâhâ! the goat for the Asvins. Svâhâ! the ram for Sarasvatî.<br> Svâhâ! the bull for Indra. To the Lion, to his might,<br> power. Svâhâ! Agni the salutary remedy. Svâhâ! Soma,<br> the power. Svâhâ! Indra the Good Deliverer. Savitar,<br> Varuna Lord of Physicians. Svâhâ! Vanaspatî, beloved,<br> food and medicine. Svâhâ! Gods who drink clarified<br> butter. Agni accepting the medicine. Milk, Soma.<br> Let them enjoy, etc.<br> 41 Let the Hotar worship the Asvins with the omentum of a<br> he-goat. Let them enjoy the fat. Hotar, offer the sacrificial<br> oblation.<br> Let the Hotar worship Sarasvatî with the omentum of a<br> ram. Let her enjoy the fat. Hotar offer the sacrificial<br> oblation.<br> Let the Hotar worship Indra with the omentum of a bull.<br> Let him, etc.<br> 42 Let the Hotar worship the Asvins, Sarasvatî, Indra the<br> Good Deliverer. These your Somas, pressed, rejoicing<br> with goats, rams, bulls, giving pleasure with rice-shoots,<br> young blades of corn, parched grain, joy-givers adorned<br> with Mâsara, bright, milky, immortal, presented, dropping<br> honey. these let the Asvins, Sarasvatî, Indra the<br> Good Deliverer, Vritra-slayer, accept. Let them drink,<br> rejoice in, enjoy the Soma meath. Hotar, sacrifice.<br> 43 Let the Hotar worship the Asvins. Let them eat of the<br> he-goat, the sacrifice. Let them to-day eat the fat, taken<br> from the middle, before those who hate us, before human<br> handling. Yea, let them eat amid the fodder of fields<br> fresh with moisture, with their expanse of barley, limbs<br> of those tasted by Agni, belonging to the Hundred Rudras,<br> portions covered with fat, from the sides, from the thighs,<br> from the fore-feet from the chine. From every member<br> of the divided victims these two make their repast. Thus<br> let the Asvins accept. Hotar, offer the sacrificial oblation.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 202</font></a></p>  
 <p>44 Let the Hotar worship Sarasvatî. Let her approach the ram,<br> the sacrifice.<br> To-day let her eat, etc., the rest of verse 43 repeated <i>mutatis</i> <i>mutandis</i>.<br> 43 Let the Hotar worship Indra, etc., as in 44 <i>mutatis mutandis</i>.<br> 46 Let the Hotar worship Vanaspatî. He has held with a very<br> well formed and very strong rope. There where the favourite<br> stations of the Asvins are, of the he-goat the sacrifice;<br> of Sarasvatî, of the ram the sacrifice; of Indra, of the<br> bull the sacrifice; there where the favourite stations of<br> Agni are, of Soma, of Indra the Good Deliverer, of Savitar,<br> of Varuna, the favourite places of Vanaspatî, the<br> favourite stations of Gods who drink clarified butter, and<br> of Agni the Hotar, there let him arrange these victims<br> when he has praised and lauded them, and perform when<br> he has made them very strong. Let divine Vanaspatî<br> accept. Hotar, offer oblation.<br> 47 Let the Hotar worship Agni Svishtakrit. Let Agni worship<br> the favourite stations of the Asvins, of the he-goat the<br> sacrifice; of Sarasvatî, of the ram the sacrifice; of Indra,<br> of the bull the sacrifice; there, etc., to 'butter' as in 46.<br> Let him worship the favourite stations of Agni the Hotar.<br> Let him worship his own majesty. Let him win for himself<br> by sacrifice food worthy of sacrifice. Let him, Knower of<br> Beings, perform the sacred rites. Let him accept the<br> sacrificial food. Hotar, offer oblation.<br> 48 The Grass divine, for the right Gods, Sarasvatî, the Asvins<br> Twain,<br> Give Indra splendour, with the Grass, sight of his eyes and<br> mighty strength! For gain of wealth let them enjoy.<br> Thou, Hotar, offer sacrifice.<br> 49 The Doors, the Doors divine, the Two Asvins, Leeches,<br> Sarasvatî<br> May they give breath to Indra in his nostrils, and heroic<br> strength.<br> For gain of wealth, etc., as in verse 48.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 203</font></a></p>  
 <p>50 May Dawn and Night, the Goddesses, both Asvins, and<br> Sarasvatî<br> Lay, with both Dawns, strength, voice within Indra the<br> Good Deliverer's mouth.<br> For gain of wealth, etc.<br> 51 Both nursing Goddesses, the Pair of Asvins, and Sarasvatî<br> Have with both nurses given strength to Indra, fame, and<br> power to hear.<br> For gain of wealth, etc.<br> 52 Bringers of strengthening sacrifice, both Goddesses,<br> well-yielding cows,<br> Sarasvatî, both Asvins, the Physicians, these are Indra's<br> guards.<br> Forth from their breasts by sacrifice they give him brilliant<br> light and power.<br> For gain of wealth, etc.<br> 53 Both Gods, the Hotars of the Gods, the Asvins the<br> Physicians and<br> Sarasvatî with Vashat-calls, with the two Hotars have<br> bestowed on Indra brilliant light and power, and planted<br> wisdom in his heart.<br> For gain of <a href="errata.htm#14">wealth</a>, etc.<br> 54 Goddesses three, three GoddessesAsvins, Idâ, Sarasvatî<br> In Indra's midmost navel have laid store of energy and<br> power.<br> For gain of wealth, etc.<br> 55 God Narâsamsa, Indra thrice-protecting, whose car moves<br> by Sarasvatî and Asvins<br> May Tvashtar lay seed, deathless form in Indra, a fitting<br> place of birth and mighty powers.<br> For gain of wealth, etc.<br> 56 God with the Gods, Vanaspatî of golden leaves and goodly<br> fruit<br> Ripens till Indra finds it sweet, with Asvins and Sarasvatî.<br> 57 Strewn, soft as wool, in sacrifice, with Asvins and Sarasvatî,<br> The sacred robe of water-plants be, Indra, a fair seat for thee!<br> Together with the sacred grass limy they, for sovranty, bestow<br> King Passion and great power on thee.<br> For gain of wealth, etc.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 204</font></a></p>  
 <p>58 Let the God Agni Svishtakrit worship the Gods as is meet<br> and right for each, the two Hotars, Indra, the Asvins,<br> Vâk with speech, Sarasvatî, Agni, Soma. Svishtakrit<br> has been well worshipped, Indra Good Deliverer, Savitar,<br> Varuna the Physician have been worshipped. The God<br> Vanaspatî, the Gods who drink clarified butter have been<br> well worshipped, Agni by Agni. Let the Hotar Svishtakrit<br> give the Hotar fame, great power, energy, honour,<br> Ancestral libation.<br> 59 To-day this Sacrificer cooking viands, cooking sacrificial<br> rice-cakes, binding a goat for the Asvins, a ram for Sarasvatî,<br> a hull for Indra, pressing Surâ and Soma juices for<br> the Asvins, Sarasvatî, and Indra the Good Deliverer, has<br> chosen Agni as Hotar.<br> 60 To-day the divine Vanaspatî has done good service to the<br> Asvins with a goat, to Sarasvatî with a ram, to Indra<br> with a bull. They have eaten these from the marrow<br> onwards, they have accepted the cooked viands, they have<br> waxed strong with the rice-cakes. The Agnis, Sarasvatî,<br> and Indra have drunk the Surâ and Soma draughts.<br> 61 Thee, to-day, O Rishi, Rishi's son, descendant of Rishis, hath<br> this Sacrificer chosen for many collected, saying: This<br> (Agni) shall win by sacrifice for me choice-worthy treasures<br> among the Gods. O God, what gifts the Gods have<br> given, these do thou desire and approve. And thou art<br> a commissioned Hotar, a human Hotar sent forward for<br> benediction, for good speech, Speak thou good words.</p>
