 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SIXTH ANUVÂKA.</h3>  
 <p>1. There is the ether within the heart, and in it there is the Person (purusha) consisting of mind, immortal, golden.</p>  
 <p>Between the two palates there hangs the uvula, like a nipple--that is the starting-point of Indra (the lord) <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Where the root of the hair divides, there he opens the two sides of the head, and saying Bhû, he enters Agni (the fire); saying Bhuvas, he enters Vâyu (air);</p>  
 <p>2. Saying Suvas, he enters Âditya (sun); saying Mahas, he enters Brahman. He there obtains lordship, he reaches the lord of the mind. He becomes lord of speech, lord of sight, lord of hearing, lord of knowledge. Nay, more than this. There is the Brahman whose body is ether, whose nature is true, rejoicing in the senses (prâ<i>n</i>a), delighted in the mind, perfect in peace, and immortal.</p>  
 <p>(1) Worship thus, O Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya!</p>
