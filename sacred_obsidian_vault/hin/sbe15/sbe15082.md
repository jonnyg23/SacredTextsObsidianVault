 <p>1. This (heart) indeed is even that, it was indeed the true <a name="fr_554"></a><a href="#fn_554"><font size="1">2</font></a> (Brahman). And whosoever knows this great glorious first-born as the true Brahman, he conquers these worlds, and conquered likewise may that (enemy) be <a name="fr_555"></a><a href="#fn_555"><font size="1">3</font></a>! yes, whosoever knows this great</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_554"></a><a href="sbe15082.htm#fr_554">190:2</a> The true, not the truth; the truly existing. The commentator explains it as it was explained in II, 3, 1, as sat and tya, containing both sides of the Brahman.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_555"></a><a href="sbe15082.htm#fr_555">190:3</a> An elliptical expression, as explained by the commentator: May that one (his enemy) be conquered, just as that one was <a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="green">p. 191</font></a> conquered by Brahman. If he conquers the world, how much more his enemy 1' It would be better, however, if we could take <i>g</i>ita in the sense of va<i>s</i>îk<i>ri</i>ta or dânta, because we could then go on with ya eva<i>m</i> veda.</p>
