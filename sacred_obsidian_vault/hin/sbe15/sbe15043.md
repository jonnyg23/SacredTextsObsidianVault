 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">THIRD VALLÎ,</h2>  <h2 align="center">OR, THE CHAPTER OF BH<i>RI</i>GU.</h2>  
 <p>Hari<i>h</i>, Om! May it (the Brahman) protect us both! May it enjoy us both! May we acquire strength together! May our knowledge become bright! May we never quarrel! Peace! peace! peace <a name="fr_235"></a><a href="#fn_235"><font size="1">1</font></a>!</p>  <h3 align="center">FIRST ANUVÂKA.</h3>  
 <p>Bh<i>ri</i>gu Vâru<i>n</i>i went to his father Varu<i>n</i>a, saying:</p>  
 <p>Sir, teach me Brahman.' He told him this, viz. Food, breath, the eye, the ear, mind, speech.</p>  
 <p>Then he said again to him: 'That from whence these beings are born, that by which, when born, they live, that into which they enter at their death, try to know that. That is Brahman.'</p>  
 <p>He performed penance. Having performed penance--</p>
