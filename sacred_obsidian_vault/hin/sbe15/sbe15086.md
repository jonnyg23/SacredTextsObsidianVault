 <p><a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="green">p. 193</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">EIGHTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Let him meditate on speech as a cow. Her four udders are the words Svâhâ, Vasha<i>t</i>, Hanta, and Svadhâ <a name="fr_563"></a><a href="#fn_563"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The gods live on two of her udders, the Svâhâ and the Vasha<i>t</i>, men on the Hanta, the fathers on the Svadhâ. The bull of that cow is breath (prâ<i>n</i>a), the calf the mind.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_563"></a><a href="sbe15086.htm#fr_563">193:1</a> There are two udders, the Svâhâ and Vasha<i>t</i>, on which the gods feed, i.e. words with which oblations are given to the gods. With Hanta they are given to men, with Svadhâ to the fathers.</p>
