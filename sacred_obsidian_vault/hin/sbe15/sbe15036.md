 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="green">p. 56</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THIRD ANUVÂKA.</h3>  
 <p>'The Devas breathe after breath (prâ<i>n</i>a), so do men and cattle. Breath is the life of beings, therefore it is called sarvâyusha (all-enlivening).'</p>  
 <p>They who worship breath as Brahman, obtain the full life. For breath is the life of all beings, and therefore it is called sarvâyusha. The embodied Self of this (consisting of breath) is the same as that of the former (consisting of food).</p>  
 <p>Different from this, which consists of breath, is the other, the inner Self, which consists of mind. The former is filled by this. It also has the shape of man. Like the human shape of the former is the human shape of the latter. Ya<i>g</i>us is its head. <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> is its right arm. Sâman is its left arm. The doctrine (âde<i>s</i>a, i.e. the Brâhma<i>n</i>a) is its trunk. The Atharvâṅgiras (Atharva-hymns) the seat (the support).</p>
