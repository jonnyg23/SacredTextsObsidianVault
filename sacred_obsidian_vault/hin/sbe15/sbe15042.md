 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="green">p. 63</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">NINTH ANUVÂKA <a name="fr_231"></a><a href="#fn_231"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>He who knows the bliss of that Brahman, from whence all speech, with the mind, turns away unable to reach it, he fears nothing <a name="fr_232"></a><a href="#fn_232"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>He does not distress himself with the thought, Why did I not do what is good? Why did I do what is bad? He who thus knows these two (good and bad), frees himself. He who knows both, frees himself <a name="fr_233"></a><a href="#fn_233"><font size="1">3</font></a>. This is the Upanishad <a name="fr_234"></a><a href="#fn_234"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>
