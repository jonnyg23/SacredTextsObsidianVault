 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SIXTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A <a name="fr_408"></a><a href="#fn_408"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. Then Gârgî Vâ<i>k</i>aknavî asked. 'Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya,' she said, 'everything here is woven, like warp and woof, in water. What then is that in which water is woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In air, O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then is air woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of the sky, O Gârgî, 'he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of the sky woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of the Gandharvas, O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="green">p. 131</font></a></p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of the Gandharvas woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of Âditya (sun), O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of Âditya (sun) woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of <i>K</i>andra (moon), O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of <i>K</i>andra (moon) woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>, In the worlds of the Nakshatras (stars), O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of the Nakshatras (stars) woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of the Devas (gods), O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of the Devas (gods) woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of Indra, O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of Indra woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of Pra<i>g</i>âpati, O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of Pra<i>g</i>âpati woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>'In the worlds of Brahman, O Gârgî,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'In what then are the worlds of Brahman woven, like warp and woof?'</p>  
 <p>Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya said: 'O Gârgî, Do not ask too much, lest thy head should fall off. Thou askest too much about a deity about which we are not to ask too much <a name="fr_409"></a><a href="#fn_409"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Do not ask too much, O Gârgî.' After that Gargî Vâ<i>k</i>aknavî held her peace.</p>
