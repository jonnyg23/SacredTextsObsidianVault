 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#001">Revelation Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#001">Revelation 7:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rev 7:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>These things (ταῦτα)
 </p><p>Read τοῦτο this.
 </p><p>Holding (κρατοῦντας)
 </p><p>Holding fast or firmly. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar007.htm#003">Mar 7:3</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#011">Act 3:11</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#002">Revelation 7:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rev 7:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>East (ἀνατολῆς ἡλίου)
 </p><p>Rev., more literally, the sunrising. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#002">Mat 2:2</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#078">Luk 1:78</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#002">Eze 43:2</a>.
 </p><p>The four angels
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#031">Mat 24:31</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#003">Revelation 7:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rev 7:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In their foreheads
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#036">Exo 28:36-38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze009.htm#004">Eze 9:4</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#004">Revelation 7:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">rev 7:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>An hundred and forty and four thousand
 </p><p>Not literally, but the number symbolical of fixedness and full completion (12 x 12). The interpretations, as usual, vary greatly, dividing generally into two great classes: one holding that only Jews are meant, the other including the whole number of the elect both Jew and Gentile. Of the former class some regard the sealed as representing Jewish believers chosen out of the literal Israel. Others add to this the idea of these as forming the nucleus of glorified humanity to which the Gentiles are joined. Others again regard them as Jews reserved by God until Antichrist comes, to maintain in the bosom of their nation a true belief in Jehovah and His law, like the seven thousand in the days of Elijah.
 </p><p>The interpretation of the latter class seems entitled to the greater weight. According to the Apocalyptic usage, Jewish terms are "christianized and heightened in their meaning, and the word "Israel" is to be understood of all Christians, the blessed company of all faithful people, the true Israel of God." See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#028">Rom 2:28</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#029">Rom 2:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#006">Rom 9:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#007">Rom 9:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#016">Gal 6:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#003">Phi 3:3</a>. The city of God, which includes all believers, is designated by the Jewish name, New Jerusalem. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#003">Rev 7:3</a>, the sealed are designated generally as the servants of God. In chapter 14 the one hundred and forty-four thousand sealed are mentioned after the description of the enemies of Christ, who have reference to the whole Church of Christ; and the mention of the sealed is followed by the world-wide harvest and vintage of the earth. The one hundred and forty-four thousand in chapter 14, have the Father's name written in their foreheads; and in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#004">Rev 22:4</a>, all the inhabitants of the New Jerusalem are so marked. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev021.htm#012">Rev 21:12</a>, the twelve tribes include all believers. The mark of Satan which is in the forehead, is set upon all his servants without distinction of race. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#016">Rev 13:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#017">Rev 13:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#009">Rev 14:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#002">Rev 16:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#004">Rev 20:4</a>. The plagues threaten both Jews and Gentiles, as the sealing protects all. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#009">Revelation 7:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">rev 7:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I saw
 </p><p>This vision belongs to heaven, while the sealing took place on earth.
 </p><p>Arrayed (περιβεβλημένοι)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#005">Rev 3:5</a>.
 </p><p>Robes
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#011">Rev 6:11</a>.
 </p><p>"The ancient scriptures and the new
 </p><p>The mark establish, and this shows it me,
 </p><p>Of all the souls whom God hath made His friends.
 </p><p>Isaiah saith that each one garmented
 </p><p>In His own land shall be with twofold garments,
 </p><p>And his own land is this delightful life.
 </p><p>Thy brother, too, far more explicitly,
 </p><p>There where he treateth of the robes of white,
 </p><p>This revelation manifests to us."
 </p><p>Dante, "Paradiso," xxv., 88-96.
 </p><p>Palms (φοίνικες)
 </p><p>Properly, palm-trees, but used here of palm-branches. Not a heathen but a Jewish image drawn probably from the Feast of Tabernacles. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#002">Joh 7:2</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#010">Revelation 7:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">rev 7:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Cried
 </p><p>The correct reading is κράζουσιν they cry. So Rev.
 </p><p>Salvation
 </p><p>The praise of salvation, ascribing salvation to God. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#011">Revelation 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">rev 7:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Stood (ἑστήκεσαν)
 </p><p>Rev., more correctly, were standing. The tense is the pluperfect, used in this verb as an imperfect. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#012">Revelation 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">rev 7:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Blessing, etc.
 </p><p>On the doxologies, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#006">Rev 1:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#013">Revelation 7:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">rev 7:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Answered
 </p><p>In the sense of taking up speech in connection with some given occasion, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#025">Mat 11:25</a>. See also on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#018">Joh 2:18</a>.
 </p><p>What are these, etc.
 </p><p>The Rev., properly, follows the Greek order, which places first "These which are arrayed in the white robes, who are they?" emphatic and indicating the natural order of the thought as it presents itself to the inquirer. For what, render who, as Rev. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#014">Revelation 7:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">rev 7:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I said (εἴρηκα)
 </p><p>Lit., I have said. Rev., renders by the present, I say. See on cried, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#015">Joh 1:15</a>.
 </p><p>Sir (κύριε)
 </p><p>Add μου my, and render, as Rev., my Lord. An address of reverence as to a heavenly being. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#003">Mat 21:3</a>.
 </p><p>Which came (οἱ ἐρχόμενοι)
 </p><p>The present participle. Hence, as Rev., which come.
 </p><p>Out of great tribulation (ἐκ τῆς θλίψεως τῆς μεγάλης)
 </p><p>Lit., out of the tribulation, the great (tribulation). Rev., properly, gives the force of the article, "the great." See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#021">Mat 13:21</a>.
 </p><p>Have washed (ἔπλυναν)
 </p><p>The aorist tense. Rev., correctly, they washed. Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#002">Luk 5:2</a>, on which see note. For the New Testament words for washing, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#033">Act 16:33</a>.
 </p><p>Made them white
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#018">Isa 1:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#007">Psa 51:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#003">Mar 9:3</a>. Milligan remarks that robes are the expression of character, and compares the word habit used of dress. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#015">Revelation 7:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">rev 7:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Therefore
 </p><p>Because of this washing.
 </p><p>Before the throne
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#027">Eph 5:27</a>.
 </p><p>Serve (λατρεύουσιν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#074">Luk 1:74</a>. In scripture the verb never expresses any other service but that of the true God, or of the gods of heathenism.
 </p><p>Temple (ναῷ)
 </p><p>Or sanctuary. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#005">Mat 4:5</a>.
 </p><p>Dwell (σκηνώσει)
 </p><p>From σκηνή a tent or tabernacle. Hence better, as Rev., shall spread His tabernacle. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14</a>, and compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#011">Lev 26:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#005">Isa 4:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#006">Isa 4:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#027">Eze 37:27</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#016">Revelation 7:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">rev 7:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>They shall hunger no more, etc.
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#010">Isa 49:10</a>.
 </p><p>Heat (καῦμα)
 </p><p>In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#010">Isa 49:10</a>, the word καύσων the scorching wind or sirocco is used. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#012">Mat 20:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#011">Jam 1:11</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#017">Revelation 7:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">rev 7:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In the midst (ἀνά μεσον)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#006">Rev 5:6</a>.
 </p><p>Shall feed (ποιμανεῖ)
 </p><p>See on shall be shepherd of, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#006">Mat 2:6</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#028">Act 20:28</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#002">Pe1 5:2</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#001">Psa 23:1</a>.
 </p><p>Shall lead (ὁδηγήσει)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#039">Luk 6:39</a>.
 </p><p>Living fountains of waters (ζώσας πηγὰς ὑδάτων)
 </p><p>For the participle living, read ζωῆς of life, and render as Rev., fountains of waters of life. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#002">Psa 23:2</a>. In the Greek order, of life stands first as emphatic.
 </p><p>All tears (πᾶν δάκρυον)
 </p><p>Rev., correctly, every tear. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa025.htm#008">Isa 25:8</a>.
