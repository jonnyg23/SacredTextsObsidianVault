 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#001">Revelation Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#001">Revelation 11:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rev 11:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A rod
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#027">Rev 2:27</a>.
 </p><p>And the angel stood
 </p><p>Omit. The insertion of these words furnishes a subject for the agreement of the participle λέγων, which is irregular an construction. Literally the correct text reads, "there was given me a reed, saying." Accordingly Wordsworth refers the speech to the reed as an inspired medium of speech. Rev., better, and one said.
 </p><p>The temple (τὸν ναὸν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#005">Mat 4:5</a>.
 </p><p>The altar
 </p><p>Of incense, as that alone stood in the sacred place.
 </p><p>Them that worship
 </p><p>Note the peculiar expressed, measuring the worshippers with a reed. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#002">Revelation 11:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rev 11:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The court which is without the temple
 </p><p>Not merely the outer court, or Court of the Gentiles, but including all that is not within the ναός, the Holy and Most Holy places.
 </p><p>Leave out (ἔκβαλε ἔξω)
 </p><p>Lit., throw out, i.e., of the measurement.
 </p><p>Unto the Gentiles (τοῖς ἔθνεσιν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#032">Luk 2:32</a>. Rev., nations.
 </p><p>Forty and two months
 </p><p>A period which appears in three forms in Revelation: forty-two months (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#005">Rev 13:5</a>); twelve hundred and sixty days (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#003">Rev 11:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#006">Rev 12:6</a>); a time, times and half a time, or three years and a half (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#014">Rev 12:14</a>, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#025">Dan 7:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan012.htm#007">Dan 12:7</a>) 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#003">Revelation 11:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rev 11:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Power
 </p><p>Omit.
 </p><p>Two witnesses
 </p><p>The reader may profitably consult on this point the lectures of Professor Milligan on the Revelation of St. John. He maintains that the conception of the Apocalypse is powerfully molded by John's recollections of the life of Jesus; that there is a close parallelism between the Apocalypse and the delineation of the life of Christ contained in the fourth Gospel; and that the Apocalypse is, in the deeper conceptions which pervade it, a repetition of the Gospel. See pp. 59-69.
 </p><p>They shall prophesy (προφητεύσουσιν)
 </p><p>See on prophet, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#026">Luk 7:26</a>. Commonly explained of preaching repentance, though some take it in the later sense of foretelling future events.
 </p><p>Clothed in sackcloth
 </p><p>The garb of preachers of repentance. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa022.htm#012">Isa 22:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer004.htm#008">Jer 4:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jon003.htm#005">Jon 3:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#004">Mat 3:4</a>. For sackcloth see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#013">Luk 10:13</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#004">Revelation 11:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">rev 11:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Two olive trees
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#001">Zac 4:1-14</a>.
 </p><p>Candlesticks
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#001">Zac 4:1-14</a>, and note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#015">Mat 10:15</a>.
 </p><p>The God
 </p><p>Read κυρίου the Lord. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#014">Zac 4:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#005">Revelation 11:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">rev 11:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Fire proceedeth
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2001.htm#010">Kg2 1:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer005.htm#014">Jer 5:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#006">Revelation 11:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">rev 11:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To shut up the heaven
 </p><p>As Elijah, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1017.htm#001">Kg1 17:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#025">Luk 4:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam005.htm#017">Jam 5:17</a>.
 </p><p>That it rain not (ἵνα μὴ βρέχῃ ὑετὸς)
 </p><p>Lit., that the rain may not wet.
 </p><p>To turn them into blood
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#019">Exo 7:19</a>.
 </p><p>To smite (πατάξαι)
 </p><p>Used by John only in Revelation, here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#015">Rev 19:15</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#031">Mat 26:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#027">Mar 14:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#049">Luk 22:49</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#050">Luk 22:50</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act012.htm#007">Act 12:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act012.htm#023">Act 12:23</a>.
 </p><p>With all plagues (πάσῃ πληγῇ)
 </p><p>Singular number. Rev., correctly, with every plague. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#010">Mar 3:10</a>. Not merely with the plagues with which Moses smote Egypt. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#007">Revelation 11:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">rev 11:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The beast (θηρίον)
 </p><p>Wild beast. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#006">Rev 4:6</a>. A different word from that wrongly translated beast, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#006">Rev 4:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#007">Rev 4:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#006">Rev 5:6</a>, etc. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#001">Rev 13:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev017.htm#008">Rev 17:8</a>, and see Daniel 7.
 </p><p>Bottomless pit (ἀβύσσου)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev009.htm#001">Rev 9:1</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#008">Revelation 11:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">rev 11:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Dead bodies (πτώματα)
 </p><p>Read πτῶμα carcass. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#028">Mat 24:28</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar015.htm#045">Mar 15:45</a>.
 </p><p>In the street (ἐπὶ τῆς πλατείας)
 </p><p>Lit., "Upon the street." See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#021">Luk 14:21</a>.
 </p><p>The great city
 </p><p>Jerusalem is never called by this name. Different expositors refer it to Rome or Babylon. Milligan to Jerusalem.
 </p><p>Spiritually (πνευματικῶς)
 </p><p>Typically or allegorically. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#003">Co1 10:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#004">Co1 10:4</a>.
 </p><p>Our Lord
 </p><p>Read αὐτῶν their for ἡμῶν our. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#009">Revelation 11:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">rev 11:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Shall see (βλέψουσιν)
 </p><p>Read, βλέπουσιν do men look (Rev.), and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#029">Joh 1:29</a>.
 </p><p>Shall not suffer (οὐκ ἀφήσουσιν)
 </p><p>Read ἀφίουσιν do not suffer.
 </p><p>To be put in graves (τεθῆναι εἰς μνήματα)
 </p><p>Read μνῆμα a tomb, as Rev. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#004">Gen 23:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#019">Isa 14:19</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#020">Isa 14:20</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#010">Revelation 11:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">rev 11:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Shall rejoice (χαροῦσιν)
 </p><p>Read χαίρουσιν, present tense, rejoice.
 </p><p>Shall make merry (εὐφρανθήσονται)
 </p><p>Read εὐφραίνονται, present tense, make merry; and for the word see note on fared sumptuously, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#019">Luk 16:19</a>.
 </p><p>Shall send gifts
 </p><p>As on a day of festival. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh008.htm#010">Neh 8:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh008.htm#012">Neh 8:12</a>.
 </p><p>Tormented (ἐβασάνισαν)
 </p><p>See on vexed, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#008">Pe2 2:8</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#023">Mat 4:23</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#024">Mat 4:24</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#011">Revelation 11:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">rev 11:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Spirit of life (πνεῦμα ζῶης)
 </p><p>Rev., breath. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#008">Joh 3:8</a>.
 </p><p>Entered into them
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#001">Eze 37:1-10</a>.
 </p><p>Saw (θεωροῦντας)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#018">Joh 1:18</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#013">Revelation 11:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">rev 11:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Earthquake
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#012">Rev 6:12</a>.
 </p><p>Of men (ὀνόματα ἀνθρώπων)
 </p><p>Lit., names of men. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#004">Rev 3:4</a>.
 </p><p>Gave glory to the God of heaven
 </p><p>The phrase signifies not conversion, nor repentance, nor thanksgiving, but recognition, which is its usual sense in scripture. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos007.htm#019">Jos 7:19</a> (Sept.). <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh009.htm#024">Joh 9:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act012.htm#023">Act 12:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#020">Rom 4:20</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#015">Revelation 11:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">rev 11:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The kingdoms - are become (ἐγένοντο αἱ βασιλεῖαι)
 </p><p>Read ἐγένετο ἡ βασιλεία, the kingdom - is become.
 </p><p>Of our Lord, etc.
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#002">Psa 2:2-9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#017">Revelation 11:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">rev 11:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>O Lord God, etc.
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#008">Rev 4:8</a>.
 </p><p>And art to come
 </p><p>Omit.
 </p><p>Hast taken to Thee
 </p><p>Omit to thee. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#018">Revelation 11:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">rev 11:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Were angry (ὀργίσθησαν)
 </p><p>See on wrath, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#036">Joh 3:36</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#001">Psa 2:1</a>.
 </p><p>The time (ὁ καιρὸς)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#001">Mat 12:1</a>.
 </p><p>Reward (μισθὸν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#013">Pe2 2:13</a>.
 </p><p>Destroy (διαφθεῖραι)
 </p><p>Also to corrupt.
 </p><p>Which destroy (τοὺς διαφθείροντας)
 </p><p>Or, the destroyers. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#019">Revelation 11:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">rev 11:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The temple (ὁ ναὸς)
 </p><p>The sanctuary. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#001">Rev 11:1</a> and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#005">Mat 4:5</a>.
 </p><p>In heaven
 </p><p>Join with temple of God, as Rev., instead of with opened, as A.V.
 </p><p>The ark of His covenant (ἡ κιβωτὸς τῆς διαθήκης αὐτοῦ)
 </p><p>Κιβωτὸς ark, meaning generally any wooden box or chest used of the ark in the tabernacle only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#004">Heb 9:4</a>. Elsewhere of Noah's ark. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#038">Mat 24:38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk017.htm#027">Luk 17:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#007">Heb 11:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1003.htm#020">Pe1 3:20</a>. For covenant, see note on testament, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#028">Mat 26:28</a>. This is the last mention in scripture of the ark of the covenant. It was lost when the temple was destroyed by the Chaldeans (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#010">Kg2 25:10</a>), and was wanting in the second temple. Tacitus says that Pompey "by right of conquest entered the temple. Thenceforward it became generally known that the habitation was empty and the sanctuary unoccupied do representation of the deity being found within it" ("History," v., 9). According to Jewish tradition Jeremiah had taken the ark and all that the Most Holy Place contained, and concealed them, before the destruction of the temple, in a cave at Mount Sinai, whence they are to be restored to the temple in the days of Messiah.
 </p><p>Lightnings and voices, etc.
 </p><p>"The solemn salvos, so to speak, of the artillery of heaven, with which each series of visions is concluded."
