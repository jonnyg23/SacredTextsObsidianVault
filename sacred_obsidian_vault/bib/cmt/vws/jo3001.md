 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#001">3 John Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#001">3 John 1:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jo3 1:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The elder
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001">Jo2 1:1</a>.
 </p><p>Gaius
 </p><p>The name occurs several times in the New Testament, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#029">Act 19:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#004">Act 20:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom016.htm#023">Rom 16:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#014">Co1 1:14</a>. The person addressed here cannot be identified.
 </p><p>The well-beloved
 </p><p>Rev., the beloved. In the Greek order the name comes first. Gaius the beloved.
 </p><p>In the truth (ἐν αληθείᾳ)
 </p><p>Rev., properly, omitting the article, in truth. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#004">Jo2 1:4</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#002">3 John 1:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">jo3 1:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Beloved
 </p><p>Compare the plural, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#002">Jo1 3:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#021">Jo1 3:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#001">Jo1 4:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#007">Jo1 4:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#011">Jo1 4:11</a>.
 </p><p>I wish above all things (περὶ πάντων εὔχομαι)
 </p><p>Wrong. This sense of περί is contrary to New Testament usage. The preposition means concerning. So Rev. "I pray that in all things thou mayst prosper." Εὔχομαι I pray or wish, occurs only here in John's writings, and not often elsewhere. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act026.htm#029">Act 26:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#003">Rom 9:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam005.htm#016">Jam 5:16</a>.
 </p><p>Mayst prosper (εὐοδοῦσθαι)
 </p><p>Lit., have a prosperous journey. From ἐν well, and ὁδός a way. In this original sense, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#010">Rom 1:10</a>. The word occurs only three times in the New Testament. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#002">Co1 16:2</a>.
 </p><p>Be in health (ὑγιαίνειν)
 </p><p>Used in the New Testament both in a physical and moral sense. The former is found only here and in Luke's Gospel. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#031">Luk 5:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#010">Luk 7:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#027">Luk 15:27</a>. Paul uses it of soundness in faith or doctrine. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#010">Ti1 1:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#003">Ti1 6:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2001.htm#013">Ti2 1:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#002">Tit 2:2</a>. Here of Gaius' bodily health, as is shown by soul in the next clause.
 </p><p>Soul (ψυχή)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#030">Mar 12:30</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#046">Luk 1:46</a>. The soul (ψυχή) is the principle of individuality, the seat of personal impressions. It has a side in contact with both the material and the spiritual element of humanity, and is thus the mediating organ between body and spirit. Its meaning, therefore, constantly rises above life or the living individual, and takes color from its relation to either the emotional or the spiritual side of life, from the fact of its being the seat of the feelings, desires, affections, aversions, and the bearer and manifester of the divine life-principle (πνεῦμα). Consequently ψυχή is often used in our sense of heart (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#046">Luk 1:46</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#035">Luk 2:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#024">Joh 10:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act014.htm#002">Act 14:2</a>); and the meanings of ψυχή soul and πνεῦμα spirit, occasionally approach each other very closely. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#027">Joh 12:27</a>, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#033">Joh 11:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#029">Mat 11:29</a>, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#018">Co1 16:18</a>. Also both words in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#047">Luk 1:47</a>. In this passage ψυχή soul, expresses the soul regarded as moral being designed for everlasting life. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb006.htm#019">Heb 6:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#039">Heb 10:39</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#017">Heb 13:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#011">Pe1 2:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#019">Pe1 4:19</a>. John commonly uses the word to denote the principle of the natural life. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#011">Joh 10:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#015">Joh 10:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#037">Joh 13:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#013">Joh 15:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#016">Jo1 3:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#009">Rev 8:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#011">Rev 12:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#003">Rev 16:3</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#003">3 John 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">jo3 1:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Rejoiced greatly
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#004">Jo2 1:4</a>.
 </p><p>Brethren came (ἐρχομένων ἀδελφῶν)
 </p><p>Lit., coming. The present participle denotes coming from time to time, and not coming on a single occasion, which would require the aorist. On brethren, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#009">Jo1 2:9</a>.
 </p><p>Thou walkest in truth
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#008">Jo1 1:8</a>. for the phrase walk in, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#006">Jo2 1:6</a>. Thou is emphatic, suggesting a contrast with less faithful ones, as Diotrephes, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#009">Jo3 1:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#004">3 John 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">jo3 1:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Joy (χαρὰν)
 </p><p>The texts vary; some reading χάριν grace or favor from God, on which see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#003">Jo2 1:3</a>. Note the Greek order: greater joy than this have I not.
 </p><p>My children (τὰ ἐμὰ τέκνα)
 </p><p>Lit., mine own children.
 </p><p>Walk (περιπατοῦντα)
 </p><p>Rev., rightly, walking. The participle expresses something habitual. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#005">3 John 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">jo3 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Thou doest faithfully (πιστὸν ποιεῖς)
 </p><p>Rev., thou doest a faithful work. A third interpretation is thou givest a pledge or guaranty, and a fourth, akin to this, thou makest sure. The Rev. is best. There is no parallel to justify the third and fourth.
 </p><p>Thou doest (ἐργάσῃ)
 </p><p>Or lit., according to the eymology, workest (ἔργον work). See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#009">Jam 2:9</a>. The distinction between this verb and others signifying to do, such as ποιεῖν, πράσσειν, δρᾶν, which last does not occur in the New Testament, is not sharply maintained in Attic Greek. In certain connections the difference between them is great, in others, it is hardly perceptible. On ποιεῖν and πρα.σσειν, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#021">Joh 3:21</a>. Ἐργάζομαι, like πράσσειν, contemplates the process rather than the end of action, carrying the ideas of continuity and repetition. It means to labor, to be active, to perform, with the idea of continued exertion, and therefore is used of servants, or of those who have an assigned business or office. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#028">Mat 21:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#026">Mat 25:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#014">Luk 13:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#017">Joh 5:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#027">Joh 6:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh009.htm#004">Joh 9:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1002.htm#009">Th1 2:9</a>. For the phrase ἐργάσῃ εἰς thou doest toward (Rev.), see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#010">Mat 26:10</a>.
 </p><p>And to strangers (καὶ εἰς τοὺς ξένους)
 </p><p>The best texts read, instead of εἰς τοὺς to the (strangers), τοῦτο, that; so that the sentence is, literally, "to them that are brethren, and that strangers." For the phrase and that, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1006.htm#006">Co1 6:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#028">Phi 1:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#008">Eph 2:8</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#006">3 John 1:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jo3 1:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The Church (ἐκκλησίας)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018">Mat 16:18</a>.
 </p><p>If thou bring forward on their journey (προπέμψας)
 </p><p>Lit., having sent forward. The aorist tense represents the act as accomplished. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#003">Act 15:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#013">Tit 3:13</a>. Rev., set forward.
 </p><p>After a godly sort (ἀξίως τοῦ Θεοῦ)
 </p><p>Lit., worthily of God. So Rev. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1002.htm#012">Th1 2:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#010">Col 1:10</a>.
 </p><p>Thou shalt do well (καλῶς ποιὴσεις)
 </p><p>For the phrase, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#033">Act 10:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi004.htm#014">Phi 4:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#008">Jam 2:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#019">Jam 2:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#019">Pe2 1:19</a>. Rev., renders the whole: whom thou wilt do well to set forward on their journey worthily of God. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#007">3 John 1:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">jo3 1:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For His Name's sake (ὑπὲρ τοῦ ὀνόματος)
 </p><p>His is supplied by the A.V. It is not in the text. Rev., correctly, for the sake of the Name. The Name (Jesus Christ) is used thus absolutely in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#041">Act 5:41</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#007">Jam 2:7</a>. For a similar absolute use of the way, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#002">Act 4:2</a>. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#007">Jo1 1:7</a>.
 </p><p>Taking nothing of (μηδὲν λαμβάνοντες ἀπὸ)
 </p><p>For the phrase taking of, or from, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#005">Jo1 1:5</a>.
 </p><p>The Gentiles (ἐθνικῶν)
 </p><p>This word occurs elsewhere only in the Gospel of Matthew. The more common word is ἔθνη, which is the reading of the Tex. Rec. here: ἐθνῶν. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#032">Luk 2:32</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#008">3 John 1:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">jo3 1:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Ought (ὀφείλομεν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#006">Jo1 2:6</a>.
 </p><p>To receive (ἀπολαμβάνειν)
 </p><p>The best texts read ὑπολαμβάνειν to support; i.e., to welcome with the provision of hospitality. Rev., welcome. The verb means, originally, to take underneath in order to raise. Hence, to support. Figuratively, to take upon the mind, to suppose, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#043">Luk 7:43</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#015">Act 2:15</a> : to take up or follow in speech; hence to answer, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#030">Luk 10:30</a>.
 </p><p>Fellow-helpers to the truth (συνεργοὶ τῇ ἀληθείᾳ)
 </p><p>Lit., fellow-workers. The expression is explained in two ways: either fellow-workers with the teachers (τοιούτους such) in support of the truth; or fellow-workers with the truth. Adopt the latter, as Rev. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#009">3 John 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">jo3 1:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I wrote unto the Church
 </p><p>The best texts insert τι somewhat, which indicates that the apostle did not regard the communication as specially important.
 </p><p>Diotrephes (Διοτρεφὴς)
 </p><p>The name is from Δίος of Zeus (Jove), and τρέφω to nourish, and means Jove-nursed.
 </p><p>Who loveth to have the pre-eminence (ὁ φιλοπρωτεύων)
 </p><p>From the adjective φιλόπρωτος fond of being first. The word occurs here only. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#010">3 John 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">jo3 1:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Prating (φλυαρῶν)
 </p><p>From φλύω to bubble up or boil over. Hence of talk which is both fluent and empty. Compare the kindred adjective φλύαροι tattlers, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#013">Ti1 5:13</a>.
 </p><p>Them that would
 </p><p>Those who were disposed to receive the strangers.
 </p><p>Casteth them out
 </p><p>By excommunication, which, through his influence, he had power to bring about. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#011">3 John 1:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">jo3 1:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Follow (μιμοῦ)
 </p><p>More correctly, as Rev., imitate. Elsewhere only <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#007">Th2 3:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#009">Th2 3:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#007">Heb 13:7</a>. The kindred word μιμητής imitator, uniformly rendered follower in A.V., occurs <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#016">Co1 4:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#001">Co1 11:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#001">Eph 5:1</a>. Hence our word mimic; also pantomime. Μῖμος means both an actor and a kind of prose drama, intended as a familiar representation of life and character, and without any distinct plot.
 </p><p>That which is evil - that which is good (τὸ κακὸν  - τὸ ἀγαθόν).
 </p><p>Compare τὰ ἀγαθά good, τὰ φαῦλα evil, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#029">Joh 5:29</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#012">3 John 1:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">jo3 1:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Demetrius hath good report (Δημητρίῳ μεμαρτύρηται)
 </p><p>Lit., unto Demetrius witness hath been born. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#026">Joh 3:26</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#013">3 John 1:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">jo3 1:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I had (εἷχον)
 </p><p>The imperfect tense: I was having, when I began to write.
 </p><p>Pen (καλάμου)
 </p><p>Lit., reed. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#007">Mat 11:7</a>. The staff or scepter placed in mockery in Jesus' hand, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#029">Mat 27:29</a>. A measuring-reed, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#001">Rev 11:1</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#014">3 John 1:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">jo3 1:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Face to face
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#012">Jo2 1:12</a>.
