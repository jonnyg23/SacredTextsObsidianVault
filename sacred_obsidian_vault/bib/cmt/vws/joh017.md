 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#001">John Chapter 17</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#001">John 17:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">joh 17:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>These words (ταῦτα)
 </p><p>Literally, these things. So Rev.
 </p><p>Said
 </p><p>John nowhere says that Jesus prayed, as the other Evangelists do.
 </p><p>Thy Son - thy Son (σοῦ τὸν υἱὸν  - ὁ υἱός)
 </p><p>Properly, thy Son - the Son. The second phrase marks a change from the thought of personal relationship to that of the relation in which Jesus manifests the Father's glory. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#002">John 17:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">joh 17:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Power (ἐξουσίαν)
 </p><p>Rev., rightly, authority. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>.
 </p><p>All flesh
 </p><p>A Hebrew phrase, denoting the whole of humanity in its imperfection. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen006.htm#012">Gen 6:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#002">Psa 65:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#005">Isa 40:5</a>, etc.
 </p><p>That He should give eternal life to as many as Thou hast given Him (ἵνα πᾶν ὃ δέδωκας αὐτῷ, δώσῃ αὐτοῖς ζωὴν αἰώνιον)
 </p><p>Literally, that all that Thou hast given Him, to them He should give eternal life. All (πᾶν), singular number, regards the body of Christian disciples collectively: to them, individually. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#003">John 17:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">joh 17:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Life eternal
 </p><p>With the article: the life eternal. Defining the words in the previous verse. The life eternal (of which I spoke) is this.
 </p><p>That (ἵνα)
 </p><p>Expressing the aim.
 </p><p>Might know (γινώσκωσι)
 </p><p>Might recognize or perceive. This is striking, that eternal life consists in knowledge, or rather the pursuit of knowledge, since the present tense marks a continuance, a progressive perception of God in Christ. That they might learn to know. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#023">Joh 17:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#038">Joh 10:38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#020">Jo1 5:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#007">Jo1 4:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#008">Jo1 4:8</a>.
 </p><p>"I say, the acknowledgment of God in Christ
 </p><p>Accepted by thy reason, solves for thee
 </p><p>All questions in the earth and out of it,
 </p><p>And has so far advanced thee to be wise.
 </p><p>Wouldst thou improve this to reprove the proved?
 </p><p>In life's mere minute, with power to use that proof,
 </p><p>Leave knowledge and revert to how it sprung?
 </p><p>Thou hast it; use it, and forthwith, or die.
 </p><p>For this I say is death, and the sole death,
 </p><p>When a man's loss comes to him from his gain,
 </p><p>Darkness from light, from knowledge ignorance,
 </p><p>And lack of love from love made manifest."
 </p><p>Robert Browning, "A Death in the Desert."
 </p><p>The relation of perception of God to character is stated in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#002">Jo1 3:2</a>, on which see note.
 </p><p>True (ἀληθινὸν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#009">Joh 1:9</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1008.htm#004">Co1 8:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#015">Ti1 6:15</a>.
 </p><p>Jesus Christ, whom Thou hast sent
 </p><p>The Rev. brings out better the emphasis of the Greek order: and Him whom Thou didst send, even Jesus Christ. Didst send (ἀπέστειλας). The aorist tense, emphasizing the historic fact of Christ's mission. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#004">John 17:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">joh 17:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I have glorified - I have finished (ἐδόξασα  - ἐτελείωσα)
 </p><p>The best texts read, τελειώσας, having finished; the participle defining the manner in which He had glorified the Father upon earth. So Rev.
 </p><p>To do (ἵνα ποιήσω)
 </p><p>Literally, in order that I should do (it). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#005">John 17:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">joh 17:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>With Thyself (παρὰ σεαυτῷ)
 </p><p>In fellowship with Thyself. So with Thee (παρὰ σοί).
 </p><p>I had
 </p><p>Actually possessed. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#008">John 17:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">joh 17:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The words (τὰ ῥήματα)
 </p><p>Compare thy word (λόγον), <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#006">Joh 17:6</a>. That signified the gospel message in its entirety. This, the message considered in its constituent parts. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#037">Luk 1:37</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#038">Joh 5:38</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#047">Joh 5:47</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#060">Joh 6:60</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#063">Joh 6:63</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#068">Joh 6:68</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#043">Joh 8:43</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#047">Joh 8:47</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#051">Joh 8:51</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#047">Joh 12:47</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#048">Joh 12:48</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#003">Joh 15:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#007">Joh 15:7</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#009">John 17:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">joh 17:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I pray (ἐρωτῶ)
 </p><p>More strictly, I make request. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#023">Joh 16:23</a>. The I is emphatic, as throughout the prayer. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#010">John 17:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">joh 17:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>All mine (τὰ ἐμὰ πάντα)
 </p><p>All things that are mine. So Rev. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#011">John 17:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">joh 17:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I come (ἔρχομαι)
 </p><p>I am coming. Spoken of His departure to the Father.
 </p><p>Holy (ἅγιε)
 </p><p>See on saints, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act026.htm#010">Act 26:10</a>; also see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#015">Pe1 1:15</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#020">Jo1 2:20</a>, and righteous Father (δίκαιε), <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#025">Joh 17:25</a>. This epithet, now first applied to the Father, contemplates God, the holy One, as the agent of that which Christ desires for His disciples - holiness of heart and life; being kept from this evil world.
 </p><p>Those whom (οὓς)
 </p><p>The correct reading is ᾧ, referring to name. Thy name which Thou hast given me. So in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#012">Joh 17:12</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#009">Phi 2:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#010">Phi 2:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#017">Rev 2:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#012">Rev 19:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#004">Rev 22:4</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#012">John 17:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">joh 17:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In the world
 </p><p>Omit.
 </p><p>I kept (ἐτήρουν)
 </p><p>Imperfect tense. I continued to keep. The I is emphatic: I kept them, now do Thou keep them.
 </p><p>I kept (ἐτήρουν)
 </p><p>Rev., rightly, I guarded. The A.V. overlooks the distinction between the two words for keeping. The former word means, I preserved them; the latter, I guarded them as a means to their preservation. See on reserved, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#004">Pe1 1:4</a>.
 </p><p>Is lost - perdition (ἀπώλετο  - ἀπωλείας)
 </p><p>A play of words: "None of them perished, but the son of perishing" (Westcott).
 </p><p>The scripture (ἡ γραφὴ)
 </p><p>See close of note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#047">Joh 5:47</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#010">Mar 12:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#015">John 17:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">joh 17:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>From the evil (τοῦ πονηροῦ)
 </p><p>Or, the evil one. This rendering is according to John's usage. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#013">Jo1 2:13</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#014">Jo1 2:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#012">Jo1 3:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#018">Jo1 5:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#019">Jo1 5:19</a>; and compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#031">Joh 12:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#030">Joh 14:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#011">Joh 16:11</a>. From (ἐκ), literally, out of, means out of the hands of. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#017">John 17:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">joh 17:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Sanctify (ἁγίασον)
 </p><p>Constantly used in the Septuagint to express the entire dedication and consecration of both persons and things to God. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#001">Exo 29:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#036">Exo 29:36</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo040.htm#013">Exo 40:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev022.htm#002">Lev 22:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev022.htm#003">Lev 22:3</a>. Rev., in margin, consecrate. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#036">Joh 10:36</a>.
 </p><p>Through thy truth (ἐν τῇ ἀληθείᾳ σου)
 </p><p>The best texts omit thy. Through (ἐν) is to be rendered literally, in, marking the sphere or element of consecration. Rev., sanctify them in the truth.
 </p><p>Thy word (ὁ λόγος ο σὸς)
 </p><p>Properly, the word which is thine. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#009">Joh 15:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#018">John 17:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">joh 17:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Sent (ἀπέστειλας  - ἀπέστειλα)
 </p><p>On a mission. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#016">Mat 10:16</a>.
 </p><p>Sanctify
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#017">Joh 17:17</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#020">John 17:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">joh 17:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Shall believe (πιστευσόντων)
 </p><p>The best texts read πιστευόντων, the present participle, that believe. The future body of believers is conceived as actually existing.
 </p><p>On me through their word
 </p><p>The Greek order is, believe through their word on me. "Believe through their word" forms a compound idea. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#021">John 17:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">joh 17:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>One
 </p><p>Omit. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#024">John 17:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">joh 17:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>They - whom (οὓς)
 </p><p>The best texts read ὃ, that which. The construction is similar to that in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#002">Joh 17:2</a>, "that He should give eternal life," etc. Like πᾶν, all, in that passage, that which here refers to the body of believers taken collectively.
 </p><p>I will (θέλω)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#019">Mat 1:19</a>.
 </p><p>My glory
 </p><p>The glory which is mine.
