 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#001">Mark Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#002">Mark 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">mar 8:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I have compassion (σπλαγχνίξομαι)
 </p><p>A peculiar verb, from σπλάγχνα, the inward parts, especially the nobler entrails - the heart, lungs, liver, and kidneys. These came gradually to denote the seat of the affections, like our word heart. This explains the frequent use of the word bowels in the A. V. in the sense of tender mercy, affection, compassion. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#078">Luk 1:78</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2007.htm#015">Co2 7:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#008">Phi 1:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#007">Plm 1:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#012">Plm 1:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#020">Plm 1:20</a>. The Rev. has properly rejected it in every such case, using it only in its literal sense in the single passage, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#018">Act 1:18</a>.
 </p><p>They have been with me (προσμένουσιν)
 </p><p>Lit., they continue, as Rev. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#003">Mark 8:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">mar 8:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Faint
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#032">Mat 15:32</a>. Wyc., fail.
 </p><p>Some of them came from far
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#006">Mark 8:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">mar 8:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To sit down (ἀναπεσεῖν)
 </p><p>Lit., to recline.
 </p><p>Brake and gave
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#041">Mar 6:41</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#008">Mark 8:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">mar 8:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Were filled
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#006">Mat 5:6</a>. Wyc., fulfilled. Tynd., sufficed. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#009">Mark 8:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">mar 8:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Baskets
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#020">Mat 14:20</a>.
 </p><p>Four thousand
 </p><p>Matthew (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#038">Mat 15:38</a>) here adds a detail which we should rather expect in Mark: beside women and children. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#010">Mark 8:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">mar 8:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>With his disciples
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#011">Mark 8:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">mar 8:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Began
 </p><p>The beginnings of things seem to have a peculiar interest for Mark. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#001">Mar 1:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#045">Mar 1:45</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#001">Mar 4:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#017">Mar 5:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#020">Mar 5:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#002">Mar 6:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#007">Mar 6:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#034">Mar 6:34</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#055">Mar 6:55</a>.
 </p><p>Sign (σημεῖον)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#020">Mat 11:20</a>. Wyc., token. As applied to the miracles of our Lord, this word emphasizes their ethical purport, as declaring that the miraculous act points back of itself to the grace and power or divine character or authority of the doer. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#012">Mark 8:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">mar 8:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Sighed deeply in his spirit
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark.
 </p><p>There shall no sign be given (εἰ δοθήσεται σημεῖον)
 </p><p>Lit., if a sign shall be given. The expression is elliptical. It is a Hebrew idiom, and is really, at bottom, a form of imprecation. If I do not thus or so, may some judgment overtake me. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#011">Heb 3:11</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#014">Mark 8:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">mar 8:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The one loaf is a detail given by Mark only.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#023">Mark 8:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">mar 8:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Took (ἐπιλαβόμενος)
 </p><p>Tynd., caught.
 </p><p>If he saw (εἴ τι βλέπεις)
 </p><p>Rev., more accurately, renders the direct question: Seest thou aught ? The change of tenses is graphic. Asked (imperfect). Dost thou see (present). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#024">Mark 8:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">mar 8:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I see men as trees walking (following the reading, Βλέπω τοὺς ἀνθρώπρους ὡς δένδρα περιπατοῦντας)
 </p><p>The Rev. reads, following the amended text, I see men, for (ὅτι) I behold (ὁρῶ) them as trees, walking. He saw them dimly. They looked like trees, large and misshapen; but he knew they were men, for they were walking about. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#025">Mark 8:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">mar 8:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Made him look up
 </p><p>The best texts omit, and substitute διέβλεψεν, he looked stedfastly. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#005">Mat 7:5</a>. Instead of vaguely staring, he fixed his eyes on definite objects.
 </p><p>He saw (ἐνέβλεπεν)
 </p><p>Imperfect tense. Continuous action. He saw and continued to see. Compare the aorist tense above: He looked stedfastly, fastened his eyes, denoting the single act, the first exercise of his restored sight.
 </p><p>Every man
 </p><p>Following the reading ἕπαντας. But the best texts read ἅπαντα, all things. So Rev.
 </p><p>Clearly (τηλαυγῶς)
 </p><p>From τῆλε, far, αὐγή, shining. The farthest things were clearly seen. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#029">Mark 8:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">mar 8:29</a></font></span></p>
 <p>He saith (ἐπηρώτα)
 </p><p>More correctly, he questioned or asked. So Rev. Mark omits the commendation of Peter. See Introduction.
 </p><p>On <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#031">Mar 8:31-33</a>, compare notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#021">Mat 16:21-28</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#032">Mark 8:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">mar 8:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>He spake the saying openly
 </p><p>Mark only. Not as a secret or mystery, as in his words about being lifted up, or building the temple in three days. Not ambiguously, but explicitly. Wyc., plainly. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#034">Mark 8:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">mar 8:34</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Jesus now pauses; for what he has to say now is to be said to all who follow him. Hence he calls the multitude with his disciples. Peculiar to Mark.
 </p><p>Will (θέλει)
 </p><p>Rev., would. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#019">Mat 1:19</a>. It is more than is wishful.
 </p><p>His cross
 </p><p>The pronoun αὐτοῦ, his, is in an emphatic position. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#035">Mark 8:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">mar 8:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>And the gospel's
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#036">Mark 8:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">mar 8:36</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Gain - lose
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#026">Mat 16:26</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#038">Mark 8:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">mar 8:38</a></font></span></p>
 <p>My words
 </p><p>Bengel remarks that one may confess Christ in general and yet be ashamed of this or that saying.
 </p><p>In this adulterous and sinful generation
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark.
