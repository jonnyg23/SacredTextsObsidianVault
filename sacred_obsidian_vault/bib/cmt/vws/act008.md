 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#001">Acts Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#001">Acts 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">act 8:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Death (ἀναιρέσει)
 </p><p>Lit., taking off. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#032">Luk 23:32</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#002">Acts 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">act 8:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Devout
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#025">Luk 2:25</a>.
 </p><p>Carried to his burial (συνεκόμισαν)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. Lit., to carry together; hence, either to assist in burying or, better, to bring the dead to the company (σύν) of the other dead. The word is used of bringing in harvest.
 </p><p>Stephen (Στέφανον)
 </p><p>Meaning crown. He was the first who received the martyr's crown.
 </p><p>Lamentation (κοπετὸν)
 </p><p>Lit., beating (of the breast). Only here in New Testament. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#003">Acts 8:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">act 8:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Made havoc (ἐλυμαίνετο)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. In Septuagint, Psalms 79:13, it is used of the laying waste of a vineyard by the wild boar. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#021">Act 9:21</a>, where the A. V. has destroyed, but where the Greek is πορθήσας, devastated. Canon Farrar observes: "The part which he played at this time in the horrid work of persecution has, I fear, been always underrated. It is only when we collect the separate passages - they are no less than eight in number - in which allusion is made to this sad period, it is only when we weigh the terrible significance of the expressions used that we feel the load of remorse which must have lain upon him, and the taunts to which he was liable from malignant enemies" ("Life and Work of St. Paul"). Note the imperfect, of continued action. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#005">Acts 8:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">act 8:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Philip
 </p><p>The deacon (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act006.htm#005">Act 6:5</a>). Not the apostle. On the name, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#018">Mar 3:18</a>.
 </p><p>Christ (τὸν Χριστόν)
 </p><p>Note the article, "the Christ," and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001">Mat 1:1</a>.
 </p><p>He did (ἐποίει)
 </p><p>Imperfect. Kept doing from time to time, as is described in the next verse. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#007">Acts 8:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">act 8:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Taken with palsies (παραλελυμένοι)
 </p><p>Rev., more neatly, palsied. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#018">Luk 5:18</a>.
 </p><p>Were healed
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#015">Luk 5:15</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#009">Acts 8:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">act 8:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Used sorcery (μαγεύων)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. One of the wizards so numerous throughout the East at that time, and multiplied by the general expectation of a great deliverer and the spread of the Messianic notions of the Jews, who practised upon the credulity of the people by conjuring and juggling and soothsaying.
 </p><p>Bewitched (ἐξιστῶν)
 </p><p>Better as Rev., amazed. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#007">Act 2:7</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#010">Acts 8:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">act 8:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The great power of God
 </p><p>The best texts add ἡ καλουμένη, which is called, and render that power of God which is called great. They believed that Simon was an impersonated power of God, which, as the highest of powers, they designated as the great. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#011">Acts 8:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">act 8:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Bewitched
 </p><p>Amazed, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#009">Act 8:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#013">Acts 8:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">act 8:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Continued with
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#014">Act 1:14</a>.
 </p><p>Miracles and signs (σημεῖα καὶ δυνάμεις)
 </p><p>Lit., signs and powers. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#020">Mat 11:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#022">Act 2:22</a>.
 </p><p>Which were done (γινομένας)
 </p><p>The present participle. Lit., are coming to pass.
 </p><p>He was amazed
 </p><p>After having amazed the people by his tricks. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#009">Act 8:9</a>. The same word is employed. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#014">Acts 8:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">act 8:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Samaria.
 </p><p>The country, not the city. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#005">Act 8:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#009">Act 8:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#016">Acts 8:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">act 8:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>They were (ὑπῆρχον)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#015">Jam 2:15</a>. Rev., more literally, had been.
 </p><p>In the name (εἰς τὸ ὄνομα)
 </p><p>Lit., "into the name." See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#019">Mat 28:19</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#020">Acts 8:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">act 8:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Perish with thee (σὺν σοὶ εἴη εἰς ἀπώλειαν)
 </p><p>Lit., be along with thee unto destruction. Destruction overtake thy money and thyself. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#021">Acts 8:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">act 8:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Part nor lot
 </p><p>Lot expresses the same idea as part, but figuratively.
 </p><p>Matter (λόγῳ)
 </p><p>The matter of which we are talking: the subject of discourse, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#004">Luk 1:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#006">Act 15:6</a>.
 </p><p>Right (εὐθεῖα)
 </p><p>Lit., straight. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#022">Acts 8:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">act 8:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>If perhaps
 </p><p>The doubt suggested by the heinousness of the offence.
 </p><p>Thought (ἐπίνοια)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. Lit., a thinking on or contriving; and hence implying a plan or design. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#023">Acts 8:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">act 8:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In the gall (εἰς χολὴν)
 </p><p>Lit., into. Thou hast fallen into and continuest in. Gall, only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#034">Mat 27:34</a>. Gall of bitterness is bitter enmity against the Gospel.
 </p><p>Bond of iniquity (σύνδεσμον ἀδικίας)
 </p><p>Thou hast fallen into iniquity as into fetters. The word σύνδεσμον denotes a close, firm bond (σύν, together). It is used of the bond of Christian peace (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#003">Eph 4:3</a>); of the close compacting of the church represented as a body (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#019">Col 2:19</a>); and of love as the bond of perfectness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#014">Col 3:14</a> :). See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa058.htm#006">Isa 58:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#026">Acts 8:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">act 8:26</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The south (μεσημβρίαν)
 </p><p>A contracted form of μεσημερία, midday, noon, which is the rendering at <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act022.htm#006">Act 22:6</a>, the only other passage where it occurs. Rev. gives at noon in margin.
 </p><p>Desert
 </p><p>Referring to the route. On desert, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#004">Luk 15:4</a>. There were several roads from Jerusalem to Gaza. One is mentioned by the way of Bethlehem to Hebron, and thence through a region actually called a desert. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#027">Acts 8:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">act 8:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Of Ethiopia
 </p><p>The name for the lands lying south of Egypt, including the modern Nubia, Cordofan, and Northern Abyssinia. Rawlinson speaks of subjects of the Ethiopian queens living in an island near Mero, in the northern part of this district. He further remarks: "The monuments prove beyond all question that the Ethiopians borrowed from Egypt their religion and their habits of civilization. They even adopted the Egyptian as the language of religion and of the court, which it continued to be till the power of the Pharaohs had fallen, and their dominion was again confined to the frontier of Ethiopia. It was through Egypt, too, that Christianity passed into Ethiopia, even in the age of the apostles, as is shown by the eunuch of Queen Candace."
 </p><p>Of great authority (δυνάστης)
 </p><p>A general term for a potentate.
 </p><p>Candace
 </p><p>The common name of the queens of Mero: a titular distinction, like Pharaoh in Egypt, or Caesar at Rome.
 </p><p>Treasure (γάζης)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. A Persian word. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#029">Acts 8:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">act 8:29</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Join thyself (κολλήθητι)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#015">Luk 15:15</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#011">Luk 10:11</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#012">Act 5:12</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#030">Acts 8:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">act 8:30</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Understandest thou what thou readest (ἆρά γε γινώσκεις ἃ ἀναγινώσκεις);
 </p><p>The play upon the words cannot be translated. The interrogative particles which begin the question indicate a doubt on Philip's part. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#031">Acts 8:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">act 8:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>How can I (πῶς γὰρ ἂν δυναίμην)?
 </p><p>Lit., for how should I be able? the for connecting the question with an implied negative: "No; for how could I understand except," etc. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#032">Acts 8:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">act 8:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The place of the scripture (ἡ περιοχὴ τῆς γραφῆς)
 </p><p>Strictly, the contents of the passage. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#010">Mar 12:10</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#006">Pe1 2:6</a>.
 </p><p>He read
 </p><p>Rev., correctly, was reading; imperfect. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#033">Acts 8:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">act 8:33</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Humiliation
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#029">Mat 11:29</a>.
 </p><p>Generation
 </p><p>His contemporaries. Who shall declare their wickedness? 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#035">Acts 8:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">act 8:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Opened his mouth
 </p><p>Indicating a solemn announcement. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#002">Mat 5:2</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#037">Acts 8:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">act 8:37</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The best texts omit this verse. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act008.htm#039">Acts 8:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">act 8:39</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Caught away
 </p><p>Suddenly and miraculously.
 </p><p>And he went, etc. (ἐπορεύετο γὰρ)
 </p><p>A mistranslation. Rev., rightly, "for he went." A reason is given for the eunuch's seeing Philip no more. He did not stop nor take another road to seek him, but went on his way.
