 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#001">2 Corinthians Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#001">2 Corinthians 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">co2 6:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>As workers together with Him (συνεργοῦντες)
 </p><p>Lit., working together. With Him is implied in the compounded ούν with. That it refers to God, not to the fellow-Christians, is evident from the parallel <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#009">Co1 3:9</a>, laborers together with God, and because the act of exhortation or entreaty in which the fellowship is exhibited is ascribed to God in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#020">Co2 5:20</a>. The phrase Θεοῦ πάρεδροι assessors of God, occurs in Ignatius' letter to Polycarp. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#020">Mar 16:20</a>.
 </p><p>In vain (εἰς κενὸν)
 </p><p>Lit., to what is vain. Equivalent to the phrase to no purpose. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#002">2 Corinthians 6:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">co2 6:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>He saith, etc.
 </p><p>From <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#008">Isa 49:8</a>, after Septuagint. The Hebrew is: "In the time of favor I answer thee, and in the day of salvation I succor thee." The words are addressed to the servant of Jehovah, promising to invest him with spiritual power, that he may be a light to Israel and to others. Paul, taking the words in their messianic sense, urges that now is the time when God thus dispenses His favor to Christ, and through Him to men. The application turns on the words acceptable time; a time in which God receives. As He receives, receive ye Him.
 </p><p>The accepted time (καιρὸς εὐπρόσδεκτος)
 </p><p>Rev., acceptable. Paul uses for the simple adjective of the Septuagint a compound "well-received," which is stronger, and which occurs mostly in his own writings. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#016">Rom 15:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#031">Rom 15:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#005">Pe1 2:5</a>; and compare acceptable year, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#019">Luk 4:19</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#003">2 Corinthians 6:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">co2 6:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Ministry
 </p><p>Rev., ministration. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#007">Rom 12:7</a>.
 </p><p>Blamed (μωμηθῇ)
 </p><p>Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#020">Co2 8:20</a>. The kindred μῶμος blemish, is found <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#013">Pe2 2:13</a>, and in the Septuagint of bodily defects. Similarly the Septuagint ἄμωμος spotless, without bodily defect; and, in the moral sense, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#019">Pe1 1:19</a>, applied to Christ. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#014">Heb 9:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#027">Eph 5:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#024">Jde 1:24</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#004">2 Corinthians 6:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">co2 6:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Necessities (ἀνάγκαις)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#026">Co1 7:26</a>.
 </p><p>Distresses (στενοχωρίαις)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#009">Rom 2:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#005">2 Corinthians 6:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">co2 6:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Imprisonments (φυλακαῖς)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#021">Act 5:21</a>.
 </p><p>Tumults (ἀκαταστασίαις)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#009">Luk 21:9</a>, and compare ἀκατάστατος unstable, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#008">Jam 1:8</a>. This is one of the words which show the influence of political changes. From the original meaning of unsettledness, it developed, through the complications in Greece and in the East after the death of Alexander, into the sense which it has in Luke - political instability. One of the Greek translators of the Old Testament uses it in the sense of dread or anxious care.
 </p><p>Watchings (ἀγρυπνίαις)
 </p><p>Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#027">Co2 11:27</a>. See on the kindred verb, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar013.htm#033">Mar 13:33</a>. For the historical facts, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#025">Act 16:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#007">Act 20:7-11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#031">Act 20:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#008">Th2 3:8</a>.
 </p><p>Fastings (νηστείαις)
 </p><p>Mostly of voluntary fasting, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#021">Mat 17:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act014.htm#023">Act 14:23</a>; but voluntary fasting would be out of place in an enumeration of hardships. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#007">2 Corinthians 6:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">co2 6:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Right - left
 </p><p>Right-hand and left-hand weapons. Offensive, as the sword, in the right hand, defensive, as the shield, in the left. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#008">2 Corinthians 6:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">co2 6:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Deceivers
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2002.htm#017">Co2 2:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2004.htm#002">Co2 4:2</a>. The opinions concerning Paul as a deceiver are mirrored in the Clementine Homilies and Recognitions, spurious writings, ascribed to Clement of Rome, but emanating from the Ebionites, a Judaizing sect, in the latter half of the second century. In these Paul is covertly attacked, though his name is passed over in silence. His glory as the apostle to the Gentiles is passed over to Peter. The readers are warned, in the person of Peter, to beware of any teacher who does not conform to the standard of James, and come with witnesses (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2003.htm#001">Co2 3:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#012">Co2 5:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2010.htm#012">Co2 10:12-18</a>). Paul is assailed under the guise of Simon Magus, and with the same words as those in this passage, deceiver and unknown. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#009">2 Corinthians 6:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">co2 6:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Chastened
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2012.htm#007">Co2 12:7-9</a>, and compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa118.htm#018">Psa 118:18</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#010">2 Corinthians 6:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">co2 6:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Having - possessing (ἔχοντες  - κατέχοντες)
 </p><p>The contrast is twofold: between having and not having, and between temporary and permanent having, or having and keeping. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#015">Luk 8:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#002">Co1 15:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#021">Th1 5:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#006">Heb 3:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#011">2 Corinthians 6:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">co2 6:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Ye Corinthians
 </p><p>The readers are addressed by name in only two other epistles, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#001">Gal 3:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi004.htm#015">Phi 4:15</a>.
 </p><p>Is enlarged (πεπλάτυνται)
 </p><p>Only here, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#013">Co2 6:13</a>, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#005">Mat 23:5</a>, where it is used of widening the phylacteries. From πλατύς broad. Quite common in the Septuagint, and with various shades of meaning, but usually rendered enlarge. Of worldly prosperity, "waxed fat," <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#015">Deu 32:15</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#027">Gen 9:27</a>. Of pride, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu011.htm#016">Deu 11:16</a>. Of deliverance in distress, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>. Expand with joy, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#032">Psa 119:32</a>. The idea of enlargement of heart in the sense of increased breadth of sympathy and understanding, as here, is also expressed in the Old Testament by other words, as concerning Solomon, to whom God gave largeness of heart, Sept., χύμα outpouring. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#005">Isa 60:5</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#012">2 Corinthians 6:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">co2 6:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Not straitened in us
 </p><p>It is not that our hearts are too narrow to take you in. Straitened in antithesis with enlarged.
 </p><p>In your own bowels (τοῖς οπλάγχνοις ὑμῶν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1003.htm#008">Pe1 3:8</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam005.htm#011">Jam 5:11</a>. Rev., affections. It is your love that is contracted. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014">2 Corinthians 6:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">co2 6:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Unequally yoked (ἑτεροζυγοῦντες)
 </p><p>Only here in the New Testament. Not in classical Greek, nor in Septuagint, though the kindred adjective ἑτερόζυγος of a diverse kind, occurs <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#019">Lev 19:19</a>. Unequally gives an ambiguous sense. It is not inequality, but difference in kind, as is shown by the succeeding words. The suggestion was doubtless due to the prohibition in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu022.htm#009">Deu 22:9</a>, against yoking together two different animals. The reference is general, covering all forms of intimacy with the heathen, and not limited to marriage or to idolfeasts. The different shades of fellowship expressed by five different words in this and the two following verses are to be noted.
 </p><p>Fellowship (μετοχὴ)
 </p><p>Only here in the New Testament. The kindred verb μετέχω to be partaker is found only in Paul's epistles and in Hebrews: μέτοχος partner, partaker, only in Hebrews and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#007">Luk 5:7</a>. Having part with is the corresponding English expression.
 </p><p>Righteousness - unrighteousness (δικαιοσύνη  - ἀνομίᾳ)
 </p><p>Lit., what sharing is there unto righteousness and lawlessness? Δικαιοσύνῃ righteousness, though the distinctively Pauline sense of righteousness by faith underlies it, is used in the general sense of rightness according to God's standard.
 </p><p>Communion (κοινωνία)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#010">Luk 5:10</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#042">Act 2:42</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#015">2 Corinthians 6:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">co2 6:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Concord (συμφώνησις)
 </p><p>Only here in the New Testament. From σύν together, φωνή voice. Primarily of the concord of sounds. So the kindred συφωνία, A.V., music, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#025">Luk 15:25</a>. Compare σύμφωνος with consent, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#005">Co1 7:5</a>; and συμφωνέω to agree, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#019">Mat 18:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#036">Luk 5:36</a>, etc.
 </p><p>Belial (βελίαρ)
 </p><p>Beliar. Belial is a transcript of the Hebrew, meaning worthlessness or wickedness. The Septuagint renders it variously by transgressor, impious, foolish, pest. It does not occur in the Septuagint as a proper name. The form Beliar, which is preferred by critics, is mostly ascribed to the Syriac pronunciation of Belial, the change of l into r being quite common. Others, however, derive from Belyar, Lord of the forest. Here a synonym for Satan. Stanley remarks that our associations with the word are colored by the attributes ascribed to Belial by Milton ("Paradise Lost," B. ii.), who uses the word for sensual profligacy. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#016">2 Corinthians 6:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">co2 6:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Agreement (συγκατάθεσις)
 </p><p>Only here in the New Testament. Compare the kindred verb συγκατατίθεμαι to consent, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#051">Luk 23:51</a>. Lit., a putting down or depositing along with one. Hence of voting the same way with another, and so agreeing.
 </p><p>Ye are
 </p><p>Read, as Rev., we are.
 </p><p>God hath said, etc.
 </p><p>The quotation is combined and condensed from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev027.htm#011">Lev 27:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev027.htm#012">Lev 27:12</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#027">Eze 37:27</a>, after the Septuagint. Paul treats it as if directly affirmed of the christian Church, thus regarding that Church as spiritually identical with the true church of Israel. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#017">2 Corinthians 6:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">co2 6:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Come out, etc.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#011">Isa 52:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#012">Isa 52:12</a>, after the Septuagint, with several changes. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#018">2 Corinthians 6:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">co2 6:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I will be to you, etc.
 </p><p>From <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#014">Sa2 7:14</a>, where the Septuagint and Hebrew agree. Paul says sons and daughters for son.
 </p><p>Almighty (παντοκράτωρ)
 </p><p>The word is peculiar to Revelation, occurring nowhere else in the New Testament. Here it is a quotation. Frequent in the Septuagint.
