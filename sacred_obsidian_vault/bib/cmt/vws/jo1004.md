 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#001">1 John Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#001">1 John 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jo1 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Beloved
 </p><p>Again the recognition of danger from false spirits prompts this affectionate address. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#021">Jo1 3:21</a>.
 </p><p>Try (δοκιμάζετε)
 </p><p>Better, as Rev., prove. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#007">Pe1 1:7</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#055">Luk 12:55</a>. Compare the phrase discerning of spirits, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#010">Co1 12:10</a>.
 </p><p>Of God (ἐκ)
 </p><p>Out of: proceeding from.
 </p><p>False prophets (ψευδοπροφῆται)
 </p><p>The term is applied in the New Testament to rivals of true prophets under the old dispensation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#026">Luk 6:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001">Pe2 2:1</a>), and to rivals of the apostles under the gospel economy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#015">Mat 7:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#011">Mat 24:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#024">Mat 24:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar013.htm#022">Mar 13:22</a>). In Revelation to "the embodied power of spiritual falsehood" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#013">Rev 16:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#010">Rev 20:10</a>). The false prophet supports his claims by signs and portents (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#024">Mat 24:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#006">Act 13:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>) and is thus distinguished from the false teacher. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001">Pe2 2:1</a>, where the two terms occur together.
 </p><p>Are gone out (ἐξαληλύθασιν)
 </p><p>The perfect tense indicates that the influence of their going out on their false mission is in operation at the present. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#002">1 John 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">jo1 4:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Hereby (ἐν τούτῳ)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#003">Jo1 2:3</a>.
 </p><p>Know ye (γινῶσκετε)
 </p><p>Perceive. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#024">Joh 2:24</a>.
 </p><p>Confesseth (ὁμολογεῖ)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#023">Mat 7:23</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#032">Mat 10:32</a>.
 </p><p>That Jesus Christ is come in the flesh (Ἱησοῦν Χριστὸν ἐν σαρκὶ ἐληλυθότα)
 </p><p>Lit., Jesus Christ having come, etc. The whole phrase forms the direct object of the verb confesseth.
 </p><p>Of God
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#003">Co1 12:3</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#003">1 John 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">jo1 4:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Is come in the flesh
 </p><p>Omit. Render, confesseth not Jesus. So Rev. An ancient reading is λύει τὸν Ἱησοῦν annulleth or destroyeth Jesus." The simple Jesus emphasizes the humanity of our Lord considered in itself. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#026">Rom 3:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#009">Rom 10:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#004">Co2 11:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#021">Eph 4:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#009">Heb 2:9</a>.
 </p><p>This (τοῦτο)
 </p><p>Not this spirit, but this non-confession, summed up in all its manifestations.
 </p><p>Cometh
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#018">Jo1 2:18</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#004">1 John 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">jo1 4:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Have overcome
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#013">Jo1 2:13</a>.
 </p><p>Greater
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#020">Jo1 3:20</a>.
 </p><p>In you
 </p><p>The Christian society. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#056">Joh 6:56</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#020">Joh 14:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#004">Joh 15:4-10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#023">Joh 17:23</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#026">Joh 17:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020">Gal 2:20</a> (of the individual).
 </p><p>He that is in the world
 </p><p>In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#019">Jo1 5:19</a>, the world is said to be in the evil one. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#002">Eph 2:2</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#005">1 John 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">jo1 4:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Of the world (ἐκ τοῦ κόσμου)
 </p><p>Proceeding from, as their source (ἐκ). Different from ἐκ τῆς γῆς from the earth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#031">Joh 3:31</a>), as marking the whole worldly economy morally considered.
 </p><p>Speak they of the world (ἐκ τοῦ κόσμου λαλοῦσιν)
 </p><p>An ambiguous rendering, which might readily be interpreted "they speak concerning the world." Literally it is: "they speak out of the world; i.e., the character of their utterances corresponds to their origin. Rev., "speak they as of the world." The position of the world in the sentence is emphatic: "it is out of the world that they speak." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#006">1 John 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jo1 4:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>He that knoweth (ὁ γινώσκων)
 </p><p>Lit., the one knowing: he who is habitually and ever more clearly perceiving and recognizing God as his Christian life unfolds. The knowledge is regarded as progressive and not complete. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#012">Phi 3:12</a>, and He who is calling (ὁ καλῶν, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#024">Th1 5:24</a>) also ὁ ἀγαπῶν he that loves (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#007">Jo1 4:7</a>).
 </p><p>Hereby (ἐκ τούτου)
 </p><p>Not the same as the common ἐν τούτῳ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#002">Jo1 4:2</a>). It occurs only here in the Epistle. Ἑν τούτῳ is in this: ἐκ τούτου from this. The former marks the residing or consisting of the essence or truth of a thing in something the apprehension of which conveys to us the essential nature of the thing itself. The latter marks the inference or deduction of the truth from something, as contrasted with its immediate perception in that something. Rev., by this.
 </p><p>The spirit of error (τὸ πνεῦμα τῆς πλάνης)
 </p><p>The phrase occurs nowhere else in the New Testament. Compare πνεύμασι πλάνοις misleading spirits, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1004.htm#001">Ti1 4:1</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#007">1 John 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">jo1 4:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Of God (ἐκ τοῦ Θεοῦ)
 </p><p>Flows from God. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#008">1 John 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">jo1 4:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Knoweth not (οὐκ ἔγνω)
 </p><p>The aroist tense: did not know, from the beginning. He never knew.
 </p><p>Is love (ἀγάπη ἐστίν)
 </p><p>See on God is light (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#005">Jo1 1:5</a>), and the truth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#006">Jo1 1:6</a>); also God is spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#024">Joh 4:24</a>). Spirit and light are expressions of God's essential nature. Love is the expression of His personality corresponding to His nature. See on love of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#005">Jo1 2:5</a>). Truth and love stand related to each other. Loving is the condition of knowing. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#009">1 John 4:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">jo1 4:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Was manifested
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh021.htm#001">Joh 21:1</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#005">Jo1 3:5</a>.
 </p><p>Toward us (ἐν ἡμῖν)
 </p><p>Wrong. Not "among us," as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14</a>, nor "in us;" but as Rev., in margin, in our case.
 </p><p>Sent (ἀπέσταλκεν)
 </p><p>John describes the incarnation as a sending, more frequently than in any other way. Ἁποστέλλω is to send under commission, as an envoy. The perfect tense, hath sent, points to the abiding results of the sending. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#005">Jo1 3:5</a>.
 </p><p>His only-begotten Son (τὸν υἱὸν αὐτοῦ τὸν μονογενῆ)
 </p><p>Lit., His Son, the only-begotten (Son). A mode of expression common in John, enlarging upon the meaning of a noun by the addition of an adjective or a participle with the article. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#002">Jo1 1:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#007">Jo1 2:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#008">Jo1 2:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#025">Jo1 2:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#004">Jo1 5:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#041">Joh 6:41</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#044">Joh 6:44</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#050">Joh 6:50</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#051">Joh 6:51</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#001">Joh 15:1</a>, etc. On only-begotten, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#010">1 John 4:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">jo1 4:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Propitiation
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#002">Jo1 2:2</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#011">1 John 4:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">jo1 4:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>So (οὕτως)
 </p><p>Emphatic.
 </p><p>We ought
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#006">Jo1 2:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#012">1 John 4:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">jo1 4:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>God
 </p><p>Beginning the sentence emphatically, and without the article: God as God. "God hath no man ever yet seen." Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#018">Joh 1:18</a>.
 </p><p>His love
 </p><p>Not our love to Him, nor His love to us, but the love which is peculiarly His; which answers to His nature. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#014">1 John 4:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">jo1 4:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>We have seen (πεθεάμεθα)
 </p><p>Have deliberately and steadfastly contemplated. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#001">Jo1 1:1</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14</a>.
 </p><p>Do testify (μαρτυροῦμεν)
 </p><p>Rev., bear witness. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#007">Joh 1:7</a>.
 </p><p>Sent
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#009">Jo1 4:9</a>.
 </p><p>The Savior of the world
 </p><p>See the same phrase, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#042">Joh 4:42</a>, and compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#017">Joh 3:17</a>. Σωτήρ Savior, occurs in John only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#042">Joh 4:42</a>. Elsewhere it is applied both to God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#001">Ti1 1:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#003">Ti1 2:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#003">Tit 1:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#010">Tit 2:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#004">Tit 3:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#025">Jde 1:25</a>), and to Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#011">Luk 2:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#031">Act 5:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#023">Act 13:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2001.htm#010">Ti2 1:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#004">Tit 1:4</a>, etc.). The title is found in Paul's Epistles of the Captivity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#023">Eph 5:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020">Phi 3:20</a>), and in the Pastorals (see above), but not in Corinthians, Romans, Galatians, or Thessalonians. In classical writings the term is applied to many deities, especially to Zeus (Jupiter); also to Hermes (Mercury), Apollo, Hercules, and even to female deities, as Fortune and Aphrodite (Venus). "Zeus Soter" (Zeus Savior) was used as a formula in drinking at banquets. The third cup was dedicated to him. Compare Plato: "Then, by way of a third libation to the savior Zeus, let us sum up and reassert what has been said" ("Philebus," 66). The drinking of this cup was a symbol of good fortune, and the third time came to mean the lucky time. "Twice then has the just man overthrown the unjust; and now comes the third trial, which, after Olympic fashion, is sacred to Zeus the savior,... and surely this will prove the greatest and most decisive of falls" (Plato, "Republic," 583). Hence the proverb, τὸ τρίτον τῳ σωτῆρι, lit., the third to the savior; i.e., the third or lucky time. The name was also given later to princes or public benefactors. The kindred noun σωτηρία salvation, does not occur in John's Epistles, and appears only once in the Gospel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#022">Joh 4:22</a>). It is found thrice in Revelation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#010">Rev 7:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#010">Rev 12:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#001">Rev 19:1</a>). Σώζειν to save occurs six times in John's Gospel, and once in Revelation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev021.htm#024">Rev 21:24</a>). It does not appear in the Epistles. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#015">1 John 4:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">jo1 4:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Whosoever (ὀς ἐὰν)
 </p><p>Lit., who if there be any.
 </p><p>Shall confess
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#009">Jo1 1:9</a>.
 </p><p>Son of God
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#007">Jo1 1:7</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#016">1 John 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">jo1 4:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The love which God hath
 </p><p>On this use of ἔχειν to have, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#022">Joh 16:22</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#035">Joh 8:35</a>.
 </p><p>To us (ἐν ἡμῖν)
 </p><p>Rev., in us. Compare God abideth in Him.
 </p><p>Dwelleth in love, etc.
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#009">Joh 15:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#010">Joh 15:10</a>. Rev., abideth. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#017">1 John 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">jo1 4:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Herein (ἐν τούτῳ)
 </p><p>To what does this refer? Two explanations are given. (1.) To the following that we may have boldness. So Huther, who argues thus on the ground that <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#018">Jo1 4:18</a> shows that the drift of the writer's thought is toward the fearlessness of love. According to this, therefore, love has its fulfillment in freeing us from fear, and inspiring us with boldness even in view of the final judgment. (2.) To what precedes, viz., our dwelling in God and He in us. So Westcott: "The fellowship of God with man and of man with God, carries with it the consummation of love." I prefer the latter, principally on the ground that in such phrases as ἐν τούτῳ in this, διὰ τοῦτο on this account, therefore, the pronoun usually refers to something preceding, though more fully developed in what follows. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#016">Joh 5:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#018">Joh 5:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#065">Joh 6:65</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#047">Joh 8:47</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#017">Joh 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#018">Joh 12:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#015">Joh 16:15</a>.
 </p><p>Our love (ἡ ἀγάπη μεθ' ἡμῶν)
 </p><p>The A.V. construes μεθ' ἡμῶν with us, with love, making with us equivalent to our. In that case it might mean either the love which is between Christians, or the love which is between God and Christians. The Rev. construes with us with the verb: love is made perfect with us. The latter is preferable. I do not think it would be easy to point out a parallel in the New Testament to the expression ἀγάπη μεθ' love that with us = our love. The true idea is that love is perfected in fellowship. The love of God is perfected with us, in communion with us, through our abiding in Him and He in us. "Love is not simply perfected in man, but in fulfilling this issue God works with man" (Westcott). Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#003">Jo2 1:3</a>, "grace shall be with us" (true reading); and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act025.htm#004">Act 25:4</a>, "what things God had done with them." See also <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#023">Mat 1:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#024">Co1 16:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#018">Gal 6:18</a>. Μετά with, is used constantly in the New Testament of ethical relations. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#002">Mat 20:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#003">Mat 2:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#012">Luk 23:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#009">Act 7:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#015">Rom 12:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#006">Jo1 1:6</a>.
 </p><p>Boldness (παῤῥησίαν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#028">Jo1 2:28</a>.
 </p><p>The day of judgment (τῇ ἡμέρᾳ τῆς κρίσεως)
 </p><p>Lit., the day of judgment. The exact phrase occurs here only. Ἡμέρᾳ κρίσεως day of judgment, without the articles, is found <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#015">Mat 10:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#022">Mat 11:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#024">Mat 11:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#036">Mat 12:36</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#009">Pe2 2:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2003.htm#007">Pe2 3:7</a>. The day is called the great day of their wrath (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#017">Rev 6:17</a>); the day of wrath and of revelation of the righteous judgement of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#005">Rom 2:5</a>); the day of visitation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#012">Pe1 2:12</a>); the last day (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#039">Joh 6:39</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#040">Joh 6:40</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#044">Joh 6:44</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#054">Joh 6:54</a>); that day (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#022">Mat 7:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#023">Luk 6:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#012">Luk 10:12</a>). The judgment is found <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#041">Mat 12:41</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#042">Mat 12:42</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#014">Luk 10:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#031">Luk 11:31</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#032">Luk 11:32</a>.
 </p><p>Because
 </p><p>Likeness to Christ is the ground of boldness.
 </p><p>As (καθὼς)
 </p><p>Not absolutely, but according to our measure, as men in this world.
 </p><p>He is
 </p><p>The present tense is very significant. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#007">Jo1 3:7</a>, "is righteous even as He is righteous." The essence of out being as He is lies in perfected love; and Christ is eternally love. "He that abideth in love abideth in God and God in him." Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#002">Jo1 3:2</a>.
 </p><p>In this world
 </p><p>This present economy, physical and moral. The phrase limits the conception of likeness. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#018">1 John 4:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">jo1 4:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>There is no fear in love (φόβος οὐκ ἔστιν ἐν τῇ ἀγάπῃ)
 </p><p>Lit., fear is not. It has no existence. The fear is that spoken of in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#017">Pe1 1:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#028">Heb 12:28</a>; godly fear; filial reverence; not slavish fear, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#015">Rom 8:15</a>. In love, lit., the love, that perfected love of which John has been speaking.
 </p><p>Perfect (τελεία)
 </p><p>Not perfected, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#017">Jo1 4:17</a> but perfect as the result of having been perfected. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#014">Heb 5:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#004">Jam 1:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam003.htm#002">Jam 3:2</a>.
 </p><p>Casteth out (ἔξω βάλλει)
 </p><p>A strong expression: turneth out of doors. Fear is cast out of the sphere of the fellowship of love. See the phrase in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#037">Joh 6:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh009.htm#034">Joh 9:34</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh009.htm#035">Joh 9:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#031">Joh 12:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#006">Joh 15:6</a>.
 </p><p>Hath torment (κόλασιν ἔχει)
 </p><p>Torment is a faulty translation. The word means punishment, penalty. It occurs in the New Testament only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#046">Mat 25:46</a>. The kindred verb, κολάζομαι to punish, is found <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#021">Act 4:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#009">Pe2 2:9</a>. Note the present tense, hath. The punishment is present. Fear by anticipating punishment has it even now. The phrase hath punishment (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#022">Joh 16:22</a>) indicates that the punishment is inherent in the fear. Fear carries its own punishment. Augustine, commenting on the expulsion of fear by love, says: "As in sewing, we see the thread passed through by the needle. The needle is first pushed in, but the thread cannot be introduced until the needle is brought out. So fear first occupies the mind, but does not remain permanently, because it entered for the purpose of introducing love." The words because fear hath punishment are parenthetical.
 </p><p>He that feareth
 </p><p>The A.V. omits and (δὲ), which is important as closely connecting this clause with there is no fear in love, etc. That is an abstract statement; this is personal; two modes of stating the same truth. Rev. "and he that feareth."
 </p><p>Is not made perfect
 </p><p>"Men's condition is varied; without fear and love; with fear without love; with fear and love; without fear with love" (Bengel). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#019">1 John 4:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">jo1 4:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>We love Him (ἡμεῖς ἀγαπῶμεν αὐτὸν)
 </p><p>The best texts omit Him. Some render let us love, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#007">Jo1 4:7</a>. The statement is general, relating to the entire operation of the principle of love. All human love is preceded and generated by the love of God. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#020">1 John 4:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">jo1 4:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>He that loveth not his brother, etc.
 </p><p>Note the striking inversion of the clauses: He that loveth not his brother whom he hath seen, God whom he hath not seen cannot love.
 </p><p>How
 </p><p>The best tests omit, and give the direct statement cannot love. So Rev. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#021">1 John 4:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">jo1 4:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>That (ἵνα)
 </p><p>Not defining the contents of the commandment, but expressing intent. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#034">Joh 13:34</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#013">Joh 15:13</a>.
 </p><p>His brother
 </p><p>"To the persecutor Saul, Christ said, 'Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou Me? I have ascended into heaven, yet still I lie upon earth. Here I sit at the right hand of the Father; there I still hunger, thirst, and am a stranger'" (Augustine).
