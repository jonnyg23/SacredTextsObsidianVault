 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#001">2 Thessalonians Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#001">2 Thessalonians 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">th2 3:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Finally (τὸ λοιπὸν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#001">Th1 4:1</a>.
 </p><p>May have free course (τρέχῃ)
 </p><p>More literally, simply, and better, may run. Have swift progress through the world. An O.T. idea. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#015">Psa 147:15</a>, and comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#011">Isa 55:11</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act012.htm#024">Act 12:24</a>.
 </p><p>Be glorified (δοξάζηται)
 </p><p>Acknowledged in its true power and glory. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#028">Joh 12:28</a>. The phrase the word of the Lord - be glorified, only here. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#002">2 Thessalonians 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">th2 3:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Unreasonable (ἀτόπων)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#041">Luk 23:41</a>, and comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act025.htm#005">Act 25:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#006">Act 28:6</a>. In lxx in a moral sense, iniquitous, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job004.htm#008">Job 4:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#011">Job 11:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#012">Job 34:12</a>. The word originally means out of place.
 </p><p>All men have not faith
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act006.htm#007">Act 6:7</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#023">Gal 1:23</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#003">2 Thessalonians 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">th2 3:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>From evil (ἀπὸ τοῦ πονηροῦ)
 </p><p>Possibly, from the evil one. Τὸ πονηρόν evil is found <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#009">Rom 12:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#039">Mat 5:39</a>; but general N.T. usage favors the masculine, personal sense. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#019">Mat 13:19</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#038">Mat 13:38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#016">Eph 6:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#013">Jo1 2:13</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#014">Jo1 2:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#012">Jo1 3:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#018">Jo1 5:18</a>. In lxx, τὸ πονηρόν evil is very common: ὁ πονηρὸς a few times, but always of men. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu024.htm#007">Deu 24:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/est007.htm#006">Est 7:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job021.htm#030">Job 21:30</a>. In Tob 3:8, 17, τὸ πονηρόν δαιμόνιον the wicked demon. The masculine is favored by the Jewish formularies, of which traces appear in the Lord's prayer; by the unanimous tradition of Greek interpreters; by the interpretations of Tertullian and Cyprian, and by the evidence of the Syriac and Sahidic Versions. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#005">2 Thessalonians 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">th2 3:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Hearts (καρδίας)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#021">Rom 1:21</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#010">Rom 10:10</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#018">Eph 1:18</a>.
 </p><p>Patient waiting for Christ (ὑπομονὴν τοῦ χριστοῦ)
 </p><p>Rather patience of Christ. The prayer is that their hearts may be directed to love God and to exhibit the patience of Christ. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#006">2 Thessalonians 3:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">th2 3:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Withdraw yourselves from (στέλλεσθαι ὑμᾶς ἀπὸ)
 </p><p>Στέλλεσθαι, Po. In the active voice, to place, arrange, equip: in the middle voice, to provide for, take care. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#020">Co2 8:20</a>. Here with ἀπὸ from, to place one's self away from.
 </p><p>Disorderly (ἀτάκτως)
 </p><p>This adverb, the verb ἀτακτέω, and the adjective ἄτακτος are found only in Paul, and only in the Thessalonian Epistles. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#014">Th1 5:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#007">2 Thessalonians 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">th2 3:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Follow (μιμεῖσθαι)
 </p><p>Better, imitate. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#016">Co1 4:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#001">Co1 11:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#017">Phi 3:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#006">Th1 1:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#008">2 Thessalonians 3:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">th2 3:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Any man's bread (ἄρτον παρά τινος)
 </p><p>Lit. bread from any one, or at any man's hand.
 </p><p>For nought (δωρεὰν)
 </p><p>The word is a noun, meaning a gift. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#010">Joh 4:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#038">Act 2:38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#015">Rom 5:15</a>. The accusative often adverbially as here; as a gift, gratis. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#008">Mat 10:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev021.htm#006">Rev 21:6</a>.
 </p><p>Labor and travail
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#003">Th1 1:3</a>.
 </p><p>Be chargeable (ἐπιβαρῆσαι)
 </p><p>Po. Better, burden. By depending upon them for pecuniary support. Comp. 1 Corinthians 9:3-18, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1002.htm#006">Th1 2:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#009">2 Thessalonians 3:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">th2 3:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Power (ἐξουσίαν)
 </p><p>Better, right. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar002.htm#010">Mar 2:10</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#010">2 Thessalonians 3:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">th2 3:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>If any would not work, etc.
 </p><p>A Jewish proverb. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#011">2 Thessalonians 3:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">th2 3:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Working not at all - busybodies (μηδὲν ἐργαζομένους -  περιεργαζομένους)
 </p><p>One of Paul's frequent wordplays. See on reprobate mind, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#028">Rom 1:28</a>. Not busy, but busybodies. Περιεργάζεσθαι (N.T.o.) is to bustle about a thing: here, to be officious in others' affairs. See on τὰ περίεργα curious arts, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#019">Act 19:19</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#013">Ti1 5:13</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#012">2 Thessalonians 3:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">th2 3:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>With quietness - work
 </p><p>See on study to be quiet, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#011">Th1 4:11</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#013">2 Thessalonians 3:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">th2 3:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Be not weary (ἐντραπῇ)
 </p><p>With one exception, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#001">Luk 13:1</a>, only in Paul. To faint or lose heart.
 </p><p>Well doing (καλοποιοῦντες)
 </p><p>N.T.o. According to the Greek idiom, doing well, be not weary. Not limited to works of charity, but including Christian conduct generally, as, for instance, steadily attending to their own business, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#012">Th2 3:12</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#014">2 Thessalonians 3:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">th2 3:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>By this epistle
 </p><p>Connect with our word. The message we send in this letter. Not, as some, with the following words, note that man in your epistle.
 </p><p>Note (σημειοῦσθε)
 </p><p>N.T.o. Lit. set a mark on. The nature of the mark is indicated in the next clause.
 </p><p>Have no company with (μὴ συναναμίγνυσθαι)
 </p><p>Po. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#009">Co1 5:9</a>.
 </p><p>Be ashamed (ἐντραπῇ)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#037">Mat 21:37</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#014">Co1 4:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#015">2 Thessalonians 3:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">th2 3:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Admonish (νουθετεῖτε)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#031">Act 20:31</a>, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#004">Eph 6:4</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#016">2 Thessalonians 3:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">th2 3:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The Lord of peace (ὁ κύριος τῆς εἰρήνης)
 </p><p>The only instance of the formula.
 </p><p>By all means (ἐν παντὶ τρόπῳ)
 </p><p>Or in every way. The alternative reading τόπῳ place is rejected by the principal texts. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#017">2 Thessalonians 3:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">th2 3:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The salutation of Paul with mine own hand (ἀσπασμὸς τῇ ἐμῇ χειρὶ Παύλου)
 </p><p>Rev. properly, "the salutation of me Paul." The genitive of me is contained, according to a familiar Greek idiom, in the possessive pronoun my. Paul had apparently been employing an amanuensis.
 </p><p>In every epistle
 </p><p>Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#021">Co1 16:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#018">Col 4:18</a>.
