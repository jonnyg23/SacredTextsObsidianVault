 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#001">Revelation Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#001">Revelation 10:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rev 10:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A cloud
 </p><p>The expression occurs seven times in Revelation, and in all of them is connected with the Son of Man.
 </p><p>Rainbow
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#003">Rev 4:3</a>.
 </p><p>Pillars of fire
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#015">Rev 1:15</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#002">Revelation 10:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rev 10:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In his hand (ἐν)
 </p><p>The roll of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#001">Rev 5:1-14</a> was on the hand (ἐπί, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#001">Rev 5:1</a>), being too large to be grasped within it. The roll was on the right hand; the little book is in the left. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#005">Rev 10:5</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#003">Revelation 10:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rev 10:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Cried (ἔκραξεν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#005">Mar 5:5</a>.
 </p><p>As when
 </p><p>The when of A.V. is unnecessary.
 </p><p>Roareth (μυκᾶται)
 </p><p>Only here in the New Testament. Peter uses ὠρύομαι for the voice of the lion. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#008">Pe1 5:8</a>. The verb here is originally applied to the lowing of cattle, expressing the sound, moo-ka-omai. Both Aristophanes and Theocritus use it of the roar of the lion, and the former of thunder. Homer, of the ring of the shield and the hissing of meat on the spit.
 </p><p>Seven thunders
 </p><p>The Jews were accustomed to speak of thunder as "the seven voices." Compare the sevenfold "voice of the Lord," <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#001">Psa 29:1-11</a>.
 </p><p>As usual, interpretation has run wild as to the seven thunders. As a few illustrations may be cited: Vitringa, the seven crusades; Daubuz, the seven kingdoms which received the Reformation; Elliott, the bull fulminated against Luther from the seven-hilled city, etc. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#004">Revelation 10:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">rev 10:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To write
 </p><p>According to the injunction in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#011">Rev 1:11</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#005">Revelation 10:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">rev 10:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>His hand
 </p><p>Add τὴν δεξιὰν the right, and see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#002">Rev 10:2</a>. On lifting the hand in swearing, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#022">Gen 14:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo006.htm#008">Exo 6:8</a> (margin); <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#040">Deu 32:40</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#006">Revelation 10:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">rev 10:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Swear by (ὤμοσεν ἐν)
 </p><p>Lit., "swear in," a Hebrew idiom.
 </p><p>Should be time no longer (χρόνος οὐκ ἔσται ἔπι)
 </p><p>Rev., correctly, shall be, etc. The meaning is not, as popularly understood, that time shall cease to exist, but that there shall be no more delay (so Rev., in margin) before the fulfillment of the divine purposes respecting the Church on earth. Possibly with allusion to the cry how long (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#010">Rev 6:10</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#007">Revelation 10:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">rev 10:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Shall begin to sound ((μέλλῃ σαλπίζεν)
 </p><p>Wrong. Rev., correctly, when he is about to sound.
 </p><p>The mystery (τὸ μυστήριον)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#011">Mat 13:11</a>.
 </p><p>Declared (εὐηγγέλισεν)
 </p><p>The word used of declaring the good news of salvation. Here of declaring the mystery of the kingdom. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#008">Revelation 10:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">rev 10:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Spake unto me
 </p><p>Render, as Rev., "I heard it again speaking."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev010.htm#009">Revelation 10:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">rev 10:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I went (ἀπῆλθον)
 </p><p>The preposition ἀπό has the force of away. I went away from the place where I was standing.
 </p><p>Eat it up
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze003.htm#001">Eze 3:1-3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#016">Jer 15:16</a>.
