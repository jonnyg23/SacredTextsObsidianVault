 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#001">Revelation Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#001">Revelation 15:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rev 15:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The seven last plagues (πληγὰς ἑπτὰ τὰς ἐσχάτας)
 </p><p>Lit., seven plagues the last. Rev., "which are the last." See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#010">Mar 3:10</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#030">Luk 10:30</a>.
 </p><p>Is filled up (ἐτελέσθη)
 </p><p>More correctly, brought to an end (τέλος). Rev., finished. Lit., was finished, the prophetic aorist, which speaks of a thing foreseen and decided as if already done. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#002">Revelation 15:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rev 15:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A sea of glass (θάλασσαν ὑαλίνην)
 </p><p>Rev., better, a glassy sea. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#006">Rev 4:6</a>.
 </p><p>Had gotten the victory over the beast (νικῶντας ἐκ τοῦ θηρίου)
 </p><p>The expression is peculiar. Lit., conquered out of The construction is unique in the New Testament. The phrase signifies, not as A.V., victory over, but coming triumphant out of (ἐκ). So Rev., that come victorious from the beast.
 </p><p>Over his mark
 </p><p>Omit.
 </p><p>Standing on (ἐπί)
 </p><p>Better, as Rev., by: on the shore of, as did the Israelites when they sang the song alluded to in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#003">Rev 15:3</a>.
 </p><p>The harps of God
 </p><p>Omit the. Instruments devoted wholly to His praise. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#008">Rev 5:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#002">Rev 14:2</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#003">Revelation 15:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rev 15:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The song of Moses
 </p><p>See Exodus 15. Compare Deuteronomy 32; to which some refer this allusion.
 </p><p>The servant of God
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#031">Exo 14:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num012.htm#007">Num 12:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa105.htm#026">Psa 105:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#005">Heb 3:5</a>.
 </p><p>The song of the Lamb
 </p><p>There are not two distinct songs. The song of Moses is the song of the Lamb. The Old and the New Testament churches are one.
 </p><p>Great and marvellous are Thy works
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#002">Psa 111:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa139.htm#014">Psa 139:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1016.htm#009">Ch1 16:9</a>.
 </p><p>Just and true are Thy ways
 </p><p>Rev., righteous for just. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#004">Deu 32:4</a>.
 </p><p>King of saints (βασιλεὺς τῶν ἁγίων)
 </p><p>The readings differ. Some read for saints, ἐθνῶν of the nations; others αἰώνων of the ages. So Rev. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer010.htm#007">Jer 10:7</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#004">Revelation 15:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">rev 15:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Who shall not fear Thee?
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer010.htm#007">Jer 10:7</a>. Omit thee.
 </p><p>Holy (ὅσιος)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#075">Luk 1:75</a>. The term is applied to Christ in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#027">Act 2:27</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#035">Act 2:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#026">Heb 7:26</a>. To God only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#005">Rev 16:5</a>, where the correct reading is ὁ ὅσιος thou holy one, instead of ὁ ἐσόμενος which shalt be.
 </p><p>All nations shall come
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#009">Psa 86:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#002">Isa 2:2-4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#023">Isa 66:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#002">Mic 4:2</a>.
 </p><p>Judgments (δικαιώματα)
 </p><p>Not merely divine decisions, but righteous acts generally. So Rev. Primarily, the word signifies that which has been deemed right so as to have the force of law. Hence an ordinance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#006">Luk 1:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#001">Heb 9:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#032">Rom 1:32</a>). A judicial decision for or against (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#016">Rom 5:16</a>). A righteous deed. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#008">Rev 19:8</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#005">Revelation 15:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">rev 15:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Behold
 </p><p>Omit.
 </p><p>The temple of the tabernacle (ὁ ναὸς τῆς σκηνῆς)
 </p><p>The sanctuary of the tabernacle. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#005">Mat 4:5</a>.
 </p><p>Of the testimony
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#044">Act 7:44</a>. The tabernacle was called "the Tabernacle of the Testimony" because it contained the ark with the law of God which testifies against sin. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#016">Exo 25:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#021">Exo 25:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#036">Exo 30:36</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo034.htm#029">Exo 34:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#021">Exo 38:21</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#019">Rev 11:19</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#006">Revelation 15:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">rev 15:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Linen (λίνον)
 </p><p>The Rev. follows the reading λίθον stone, after the analogy of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze028.htm#013">Eze 28:13</a>, "Every precious stone was thy covering." The idea is that of raiment studded with precious stones. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#017">Rev 2:17</a>.
 </p><p>White (λαυπρὸν)
 </p><p>Mostly applied in the New Testament to clothing, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#011">Luk 23:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#030">Act 10:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#002">Jam 2:2</a>. Also to the water of life (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#001">Rev 22:1</a>), and the morning-star (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#016">Rev 22:16</a>). Rev., bright.
 </p><p>Girt round their breasts
 </p><p>As the Lord in the vision of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#013">Rev 1:13</a>; where, however, μαστοῖς paps is used instead of στήθη breasts. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#007">Revelation 15:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">rev 15:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Vials (φιάλας)
 </p><p>Rev., bowls. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#008">Rev 5:8</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev015.htm#008">Revelation 15:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">rev 15:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Smoke
 </p><p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo040.htm#034">Exo 40:34</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#010">Kg1 8:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#008">Psa 18:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#004">Isa 6:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze010.htm#002">Eze 10:2-4</a>.
 </p><p>None was able to enter
 </p><p>"God cannot be approached at the moment when He is revealing Himself in all the terrors of His indignation" (Milligan). See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#021">Exo 19:21</a>.
