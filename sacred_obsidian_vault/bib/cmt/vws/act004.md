 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#001">Acts Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#001">Acts 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">act 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Captain of the temple
 </p><p>It was the duty of the Levites to keep guard at the gates of the temple, in order to prevent the unclean from entering. To them the duties of the temple-police were entrusted, under the command of an official known in the New Testament as "the captain of the temple," but in Jewish writings chiefly as "the man of the temple mount." Josephus speaks of him as a person of such consequence as to be sent, along with the high-priest, prisoner to Rome.
 </p><p>Came upon (ἐπέστησαν)
 </p><p>Or stood by them, suddenly. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#004">Luk 24:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act022.htm#020">Act 22:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act023.htm#011">Act 23:11</a>. Of dreams or visions, to appear to. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#002">Acts 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">act 4:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Being grieved (διαπονούμενοι)
 </p><p>Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#018">Act 16:18</a>. The Rev. renders the force of διά by "sore troubled;" vexed through and through.
 </p><p>The resurrection
 </p><p>The Sadducees denied both the resurrection and a future state. "In the Gospels the Pharisees are represented as the great opponents of Christ; in the Acts it is the Sadducees who are the most violent opponents of the apostles. The reason of this seems to be, that in the Gospels Jesus Christ came in direct collision with the Pharisees, by unmasking their hypocrisies and endangering their influence among the people; whereas the apostles, in testifying to the resurrection of Christ, opposed the creed of the Sadducees. Perhaps, also, in attacking the apostles, who taught the resurrection of that Jesus whom the Pharisees had persecuted and crucified, the Sadducees aimed an indirect blow at the favorite dogma of their rival sect" (Gloag, "Commentary on Acts"). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#003">Acts 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">act 4:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In hold (εἰς τήρησιν)
 </p><p>A somewhat antiquated rendering. Better, as Rev., in ward. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#004">Pe1 1:4</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#004">Acts 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">act 4:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The number was about five thousand
 </p><p>Translate ἐγενήθη as Rev., came to be; indicating the addition to the original number of the many that believed. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#007">Acts 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">act 4:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>What power - what name
 </p><p>Lit., what sort of power; what kind of name.
 </p><p>Have ye done
 </p><p>The ye closes the sentence in the Greek with a contemptuous emphasis: you people. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#012">Acts 4:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">act 4:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Salvation (ἡ σωτηρία)
 </p><p>Note the article: the salvation; the Messianic deliverance. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#013">Acts 4:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">act 4:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Boldness
 </p><p>See on freely, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#029">Act 2:29</a>.
 </p><p>Perceived (καταλαβόμενοι)
 </p><p>The word, meaning originally to seize upon or lay hold of, occurs frequently in the New Testament in different phases of this original sense. Thus, to apprehend or grasp, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#018">Eph 3:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#012">Phi 3:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#013">Phi 3:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#030">Rom 9:30</a> : of seizure by a demon, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#018">Mar 9:18</a> : of something coming upon or overtaking, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#035">Joh 12:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#004">Th1 5:4</a> : of comprehending, grasping mentally, as here, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#034">Act 10:34</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act025.htm#025">Act 25:25</a>.
 </p><p>Unlearned (ἀγράμματοι)
 </p><p>Or, very literally, unlettered. With special reference to Rabbinic culture, the absence of which was conspicuous in Peter's address.
 </p><p>Ignorant (ἰδιῶται)
 </p><p>Originally, one in a private station, as opposed to one in office or in public affairs. Therefore one without professional knowledge, a layman; thence, generally, ignorant, ill-informed; sometimes plebeian, common. In the absence of certainty it is as well to retain the meaning given by the A. V., perhaps with a slight emphasis on the want of professional knowledge. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#016">Co1 14:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#023">Co1 14:23</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#024">Co1 14:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#006">Co2 11:6</a>.
 </p><p>Took knowledge (ἐπεγίνωσκον)
 </p><p>Or recognized. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#010">Act 3:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#015">Acts 4:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">act 4:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Conferred (συνέβαλον)
 </p><p>See on pondered, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#019">Luk 2:19</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#017">Acts 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">act 4:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>It spread (διανεμηθῇ)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. Lit., be distributed. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#017">Ti2 2:17</a>, "their word will eat as a canker," is, literally, will have distribution or spreading (νομὴν ἕξει). Bengel, however, goes too far when he represents the members of the council as speaking in the figure of a canker. "They regard the whole as a canker." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#018">Acts 4:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">act 4:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To speak (φθέγγεσθαι)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#016">Pe2 2:16</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#021">Acts 4:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">act 4:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Punish (κολάσωνται)
 </p><p>Originally, to curtail or dock; to prune as trees: thence to check, keep in bounds, punish. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#024">Acts 4:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">act 4:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Lord (δέσποτα)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001">Pe2 2:1</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#025">Acts 4:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">act 4:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Servant (παιδός)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#013">Act 3:13</a>.
 </p><p>Rage (ἐφρύαξαν)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. Originally, to neigh or snort like a horse. Of men, to give one's self haughty airs, and to act and speak insolently. Philo describes a proud man as "walking on tiptoe, and bridling (φρυαττόμενος), with neck erect like a horse." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#027">Acts 4:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">act 4:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Didst anoint (ἔχρισας)
 </p><p>See on Christ, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001">Mat 1:1</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#028">Acts 4:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">act 4:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Thy hand
 </p><p>Thy disposing power. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#032">Acts 4:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">act 4:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Heart and soul
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#030">Mar 12:30</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#033">Acts 4:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">act 4:33</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Gave (ἀπεδίδουν)
 </p><p>Lit., gave back (ἀπό); as something which they were in duty bound to give. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#037">Acts 4:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">act 4:37</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The money (τὸ χρῆμα)
 </p><p>The sum of money.
