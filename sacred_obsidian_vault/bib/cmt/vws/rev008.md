 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#001">Revelation Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#001">Revelation 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rev 8:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>When (ὅτε)
 </p><p>Read ὅταν, the indefinite particle with the indicative mood. For a similar construction, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#019">Mar 11:19</a> (correct reading). Alford observes that it occurs in the opening of this seal only, giving it an indefiniteness which does not belong to any of the rest.
 </p><p>There was (ἐγένετο)
 </p><p>More literally, come to pass. Rev., there followed.
 </p><p>About (ὡς)
 </p><p>A usual form of expression with John. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#039">Joh 1:39</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#019">Joh 6:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#018">Joh 11:18</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#002">Revelation 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rev 8:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Stood (ἑστήκασιν)
 </p><p>Rev., correctly, stand.
 </p><p>Trumpets (σάλπιγγες)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#010">Rev 1:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#003">Revelation 8:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rev 8:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>At the altar (ἐπὶ τὸ θυσιαστήριον)
 </p><p>The best texts read θυσιαστηρίου, which justifies the Rev., "over the altar." For altar, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009">Rev 6:9</a>. Interpreters differ as to whether the altar meant is the brazen altar, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009">Rev 6:9</a>, or the altar of incense, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev009.htm#013">Rev 9:13</a>. There seems to be no reason for changing the reference from the brazen altar (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009">Rev 6:9</a>), especially as both altars are mentioned in this verse. The officiating priest in the tabernacle or temple took the fire for his censer from the brazen altar, and then offered the incense upon the golden altar.
 </p><p>Censer
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#008">Rev 5:8</a>.
 </p><p>Incense
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#008">Rev 5:8</a>.
 </p><p>Offer it with the prayers (δώσῃ ταῖς προσευχαῖς)
 </p><p>Lit., should give it unto the prayers. Rev., add it unto the prayers. So that the prayers and the incense might ascend together. Προσευχή is always used of prayer to God.
 </p><p>Golden altar
 </p><p>The altar of incense. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#001">Exo 30:1-6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#038">Exo 39:38</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#004">Revelation 8:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">rev 8:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Which came with the prayers
 </p><p>An awkward rendering, followed by Rev., though with the omission of the italicized which came. The construction is ἀνέβη went up, with the dative case, to the prayers. "The ascending smoke had reference to the prayers, was designed to accompany them and render them more acceptable" (Winer).
 </p><p>Of the saints (τῶν ἁγίων)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act026.htm#010">Act 26:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#005">Revelation 8:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">rev 8:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Took (εἴληφεν)
 </p><p>Lit., hath taken. So Rev., in margin.
 </p><p>With the fire (ἐκ τοῦ πυρὸς)
 </p><p>Lit., "from or out off the fire," i.e., the coals or hot ashes. For ἐκ out off see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#007">Rev 2:7</a>.
 </p><p>Cast it into the earth
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze010.htm#002">Eze 10:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#049">Luk 12:49</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#006">Revelation 8:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">rev 8:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To sound (ἵνα σαλπίσωσιν)
 </p><p>Lit., that they should blow the trumpets. Raised their trumpets to their mouths in act to blow. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#007">Revelation 8:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">rev 8:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The first angel
 </p><p>Omit angel.
 </p><p>Hail and fire mingled with blood (χάλαζα καὶ πῦρ μεμιγμένα αἵματι)
 </p><p>Insert ἐν in before αἵματι blood. Instead of "with blood" as A.V., and Rev., we should render "in blood." The hailstones and fire-balls fell in a shower of blood. Compare the account of the plague of fire and hail in Egypt (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo009.htm#024">Exo 9:24</a>) to which the reference is here, where the Septuagint reads and there was hail and the fire flaming in the hail. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe002.htm#030">Joe 2:30</a>.
 </p><p>And the third part of the earth was burnt up
 </p><p>This is added by the best texts.
 </p><p>Green (χλωρὸς)
 </p><p>See on pale, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#008">Rev 6:8</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#008">Revelation 8:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">rev 8:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>As it were (ὡς)
 </p><p>Not a mountain, but a fiery mass so large as to resemble one.
 </p><p>Blood
 </p><p>Reminding of the first plague in Egypt (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#020">Exo 7:20</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#021">Exo 7:21</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#009">Revelation 8:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">rev 8:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Life (ψυχὰς)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#002">Jo3 1:2</a>.
 </p><p>Ships (πλοίων)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#002">Luk 5:2</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#010">Revelation 8:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">rev 8:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Lamp (λαμπὰς)
 </p><p>Rev., torch. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev004.htm#005">Rev 4:5</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#011">Revelation 8:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">rev 8:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Wormwood (ἄψινθος)
 </p><p>Used metaphorically in the Old Testament of the idolatry of Israel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu029.htm#018">Deu 29:18</a>); of calamity and sorrow (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer009.htm#015">Jer 9:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer023.htm#015">Jer 23:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#015">Lam 3:15</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#019">Lam 3:19</a>); of false judgment (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo005.htm#007">Amo 5:7</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#013">Revelation 8:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">rev 8:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>An angel (ἑνὸς ἀγγέλου)
 </p><p>For angel read ἀετοῦ eagle. Lit., one eagle. The eagle is a symbol of vengeance in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#049">Deu 28:49</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos008.htm#001">Hos 8:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#008">Hab 1:8</a>.
 </p><p>Mid heaven (μεσουρανήματι)
 </p><p>Only in Revelation, here, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#006">Rev 14:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017">Rev 19:17</a>. It means, properly, the meridian, the highest point in the heavens which the sun occupies at noon; not the space between heaven and earth.
