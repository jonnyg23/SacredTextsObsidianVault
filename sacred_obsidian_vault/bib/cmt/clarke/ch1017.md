 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Commentary on the Bible</i>, by Adam Clarke, [1831], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 17</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001">1 Chronicles</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">ch1 17:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>David consults Nathan about building a temple for God, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001">Ch1 17:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#002">Ch1 17:2</a>. God sends him an answer by Nathan, informing him that Solomon shall build the house, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#003">Ch1 17:3-14</a>. David receives the Divine purpose with humility and joy, and gives God praise, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#015">Ch1 17:15-27</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001">1 Chronicles 17:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 17:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Now it came to pass - See every thing recorded in this chapter amply detailed in the notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#001">Sa2 7:1</a> (note), etc. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#005">1 Chronicles 17:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">ch1 17:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>But have gone from tent to tent - "I have transferred my tabernacle from Gilgal to Nob, from Nob to Shiloh, and from Shiloh to Gibeon." - Targum and Jarchi. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#009">1 Chronicles 17:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">ch1 17:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Neither shall the children of wickedness - They shall no more be brought into servitude as they were in the time they sojourned in Egypt. This is what is here referred to. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#012">1 Chronicles 17:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">ch1 17:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I will establish his throne for ever - David was a type of Christ; and concerning him the prophecy is literally true. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#007">Isa 9:7</a>, where there is evidently the same reference. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#013">1 Chronicles 17:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">ch1 17:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I will not take my mercy away from him - I will not cut off his family from the throne, as I did that of his predecessor Saul. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#016">1 Chronicles 17:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">ch1 17:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>And what is mine house, that thou hast brought me hitherto? - I am not of any regal family, and have no natural right to the throne. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#025">1 Chronicles 17:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">ch1 17:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Hath found in his heart to pray - The Targum expresses a full sense: "Therefore thy servant hath found an opening of mouth, that he might pray before Thee. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#027">1 Chronicles 17:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">ch1 17:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For thou blessest, O Lord - "Thou beginnest to bless the house of thy servant, therefore it shall be blessed for ever." - T.
 </p><p>The reader is requested to refer to 2 Samuel 7 (note), and the notes there for many particulars that belong to the parallel places here, and which it should answer no good purpose to repeat in this place.
