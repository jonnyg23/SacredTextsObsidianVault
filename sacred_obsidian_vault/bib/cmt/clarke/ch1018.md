 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Commentary on the Bible</i>, by Adam Clarke, [1831], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 18</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001">1 Chronicles</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">ch1 18:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>David smites the Philistines, and takes Gath, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#001">Ch1 18:1</a>. Reduces the Moabites, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#002">Ch1 18:2</a>. Vanquishes Hadarezer, king of Zobah, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#003">Ch1 18:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#004">Ch1 18:4</a>. Overcomes the Syrians of Damascus, and takes several of their cities, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#005">Ch1 18:5-8</a>. Tou, king of Hamath, congratulates him on his victory, and sends him vessels of silver, gold, and brass, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#009">Ch1 18:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#010">Ch1 18:10</a>. Those and the different spoils he had taken from the conquered nations, he dedicates to God, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#011">Ch1 18:11</a>. Abishai defeats the Edomites, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#012">Ch1 18:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#013">Ch1 18:13</a>. David reigns over all Israel, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#014">Ch1 18:14</a>. His officers, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#015">Ch1 18:15-17</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#001">1 Chronicles 18:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 18:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>David - took Gath and her towns - See the comparison between this chapter and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#001">Sa2 8:1</a> (note), etc., in the notes on the latter. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#002">1 Chronicles 18:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ch1 18:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Brought gifts - Were laid under tribute. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#009">1 Chronicles 18:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">ch1 18:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Tou king of Hamath - Called Toi in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#009">Sa2 8:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#012">1 Chronicles 18:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">ch1 18:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Abishai - slew of the Edomites - This victory is attributed to David, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#013">Sa2 8:13</a>. He sent Abishai against them, and he defeated them: this is with great propriety attributed to David as commander-in-chief; qui facit per alterum, facit per se. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#015">1 Chronicles 18:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">ch1 18:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Joab - was over the host - General-in-chief.
 </p><p>Jehoshaphat - recorder - The king's remembrancer, or historiographer royal. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#016">1 Chronicles 18:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">ch1 18:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Zadok - and Abimelech - priests - Both high priests; one at Gibeon, and the other at Jerusalem, as we have seen <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1016.htm#039">Ch1 16:39</a>.
 </p><p>Shavsha was scribe - Called Seraiah, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#017">Sa2 8:17</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#017">1 Chronicles 18:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ch1 18:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Cherethites and the Pelethites - See the note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#018">Sa2 8:18</a>.
 </p><p>The Targum says, "Benaiah was over the great Sanhedrin and the small Sanhedrin, and consulted Urim and Thummim. And at his command the archers and slingers went to battle."
 </p><p>The sons of David - These were the highest in authority.
