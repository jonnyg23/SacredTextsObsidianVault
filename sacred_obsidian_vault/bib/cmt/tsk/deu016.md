 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#001">Deuteronomy 16:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">deu 16:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>the month:  Exod. 12:2-20, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo034.htm#018">Exo 34:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev023.htm#005">Lev 23:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num009.htm#002">Num 9:2-5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num028.htm#016">Num 28:16</a> 
 </p><p>the passover:  This word comes from the Hebrew verb pasach, to pass, to leap or skip over.  The destroying angel passed over the houses marked with the blood of the Paschal Lamb, so the wrath of God passes over those whose souls sprinkled with the blood of Christ.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#007">Co1 5:7</a>.  As the paschal lamb was killed before Israel was delivered, so by the death of Christ, we have redemption through his blood.  It was killed before the tables of the law were delivered to Moses, or Aaron's sacrifices were enjoined; thus deliverance comes to men, not by the works of the law, but by the only true passover, the Lamb of God. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#025">Rom 3:25</a>.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#014">Heb 9:14</a>.  It was killed the first month of the year, which prefigured that Christ should suffer death in that month.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#028">Joh 18:28</a>.  it was killed in the evening.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#006">Exo 12:6</a>. Christ suffered at that time of the day.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#046">Mat 27:46</a>.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#002">Heb 1:2</a>. At even the sun sets; at Christ's passion, universal darkness was upon the whole earth.  The passover was roasted with fire, denoting the sharp and dreadful pains that Christ should suffer, not only from men, but God also.  It was to be eaten with bitter herbs, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#008">Exo 12:8</a>; not only to put them in remembrance of their bitter bondage in Egypt, but also to testify our mortification to sin, and readiness to undergo afflictions for Christ, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#024">Col 1:24</a>; and likewise to teach us the absolute necessity of true repentance in all that would profitably feed by faith on Christ, the true paschal lamb.

 </p><p>in all thy gates:  This expression may refer to the gate of the city, as the forum or place of public concourse among the Israelites, where a court of judicature was held, to try all causes and decide all affairs.  The same practice obtained among other Eastern nations.  The Ottoman court, it is well known, derived its appellation of the Porte, from the distribution of justice and the dispatch of public business at its gates.  And the square tower which forms the principal entrance to the Alhamra, or red palace of the Moorish kings of Grenada, retains to this day the appellation of the Gate of judgment, from its having been the place where justice was at one period summarily administered.
