 </p><p>took Amasa:  Thevenot says, that among the Turks it is a great affront to take one by the beard, unless it be to kiss him, in which case they often do it.  D'Arvieux, describing an assembly of Arab emirs at an entertainment, says, "After the usual civilities, caresses, kissings of the beard, and of the hand, which every one gave and received according to his rank and dignity, they sat down upon mats."  The doing this by the Arab emirs corresponds with the conduct of Joab, and illustrates this horrid assassination.

 <p>Abel:  Or rather, probably, Abel of Beth-Maachah, as in the next verse.  It appears, from Joab having marched "through all the tribes of Israel," to have been situated in the northern confines of the land of Israel, and in the half tribe of Manasseh, east of Jordan, as that was the situation of Maachah, to which it belonged.  This agrees with the situation of the Abila which Eusebius and Jerome place between Paneas, or Cesarea Philippi, and Damascus.  Josephus says it was a fortified city, and a metropolis of the Israelites; and also that it belonged to the ten tribes, having been taken from the king of Damascus. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#020">Kg1 15:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#029">Kg2 15:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2016.htm#004">Ch2 16:4</a>
