 <p>am 3126-3165, bc 878-839
 </p><p>Joash:  Joash, יואשׁ [Strong's H3101], or  יאשׁ [Strong's H3101], is merely an abbreviation of Jehoash, יהואשׁ [Strong's H3060], by the elision of ה, hay, and here also of ו, wav. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2011.htm#021">Kg2 11:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2012.htm#001">Kg2 12:1</a>, Jehoash, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1003.htm#011">Ch1 3:11</a> 
 </p><p>seven years old:  As Joash was hidden six years in the temple, and was but seven when he came to the throne, he could have been but one year old when secreted by his aunt.

 <p>remembered:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa109.htm#004">Psa 109:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk017.htm#015">Luk 17:15-18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#032">Joh 10:32</a> 
 </p><p>but slew his son:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#013">Pro 17:13</a> 
 </p><p>The Lord:  These words were prophetic, and not imprecatory; and should be rendered as Houbigant proposes, in the future tense:  "The Lord will look upon it, and avenge it."  The event soon verified this prediction; for, before the year was expired, the Syrians came up against Jerusalem, and destroyed all the princes of the people, and Joash himself was slain in his bed by his own servants.  Many circumstances served to aggravate this barbarous act.  Zechariah was a high-priest and a prophet, upright and unblameable in the discharge of his high offices; this murder was perpetrated within the very precincts of the courts of the Lord; and this truly good man was by blood the nearest relative of Joash, and the son of the man who had save him from being murdered, and raised him to the throne! <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#005">Gen 9:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer011.htm#020">Jer 11:20</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer026.htm#014">Jer 26:14</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer026.htm#015">Jer 26:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#051">Luk 11:51</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#014">Ti2 4:14</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#016">Ti2 4:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009">Rev 6:9-11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev018.htm#020">Rev 18:20</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#002">Rev 19:2</a>
