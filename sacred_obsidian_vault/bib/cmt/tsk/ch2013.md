 <p>the Lord:  We have not abandoned the Lord; and we still serve him according to His own law.  But what Abijah urged concerning the state of religion in Judah was not strictly just; and, as spoken by him, it favoured ostentation.  Abijah himself was but an indifferent character; and idolatry was evidently connived at in his days.  Yet it was true, that the men of Judah had the priests, ordinances, and worship of Jehovah among them; that there were numbers of pious worshippers in the land; that theirs was the more righteous cause; that Jehovah was on their side as their Captain, while Israel fought against him; and that the presence of the priests with the sacred trumpets was a token of His presence and favour. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2011.htm#016">Ch2 11:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2011.htm#017">Ch2 11:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#005">Exo 19:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#006">Exo 19:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#009">Zac 13:9</a>
