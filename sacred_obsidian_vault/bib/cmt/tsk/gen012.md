 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#001">Gen 12:1</a>, God calls Abram, and blesses him with a promise of Christ; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#004">Gen 12:4</a>, He departs with Lot from Haran, and comes to Canaan; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#006">Gen 12:6</a>, He journeys through Canaan, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#007">Gen 12:7</a>, which is promised to him in a vision; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#010">Gen 12:10</a>, He is driven by famine into Egypt; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#011">Gen 12:11</a>, Fear makes him feign his wife to be his sister; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#014">Gen 12:14</a>, Pharaoh, having taken her from him, by plagues is compelled to restore her; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#018">Gen 12:18</a>, He reproves Abram, whom he dismisses.

 </p><p>Canaan:  So called from Canaan the son of Ham, lies between the Mediterranean sea on the west, the wilderness of Paran, Idumea, and Egypt on the south, the mountains of Arabia on the east, and the mountains of Lebanon and Phoenicia on the north. Its length, from Dan to Beersheba, is about 200 miles, and its breadth, from the Mediterranean sea to its eastern borders, about 90.
