 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#001">Kg2 24:1</a>, Jehoiakim, first subdued by Nebuchadnezzar, then rebelling against him, procures his own ruin; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#005">Kg2 24:5</a>, Jehoiachin succeeds him; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#007">Kg2 24:7</a>, The king of Egypt is vanquished by the king of Babylon; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#008">Kg2 24:8</a>, Jehoiachin's evil reign; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#010">Kg2 24:10</a>, Jerusalem is taken and carried captive into Babylon; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#017">Kg2 24:17</a>, Zedekiah is made king, and reigns ill, unto the utter destruction of Judah.

 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">kg2 24:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>am 3405.  bc 599.
 </p><p>slept:  As Jehoiakim was "buried with the burial of an ass," by being "drawn and cast forth beyond the gates of Jerusalem," without internment, the expression "slept with his fathers," can only mean that the died, or slept the sleep of death.  In the East, a body exposed during the night would be a prey to wild animals; if any of it were left till the morning, the carnivorous birds would devour it. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2036.htm#006">Ch2 36:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2036.htm#008">Ch2 36:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#018">Jer 22:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#019">Jer 22:19</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#030">Jer 36:30</a> 
 </p><p>Jehoiachin:  As this man reigned only three months, and was a mere vassal of the king of Babylon, his reign is scarcely reckoned; and therefore Jeremiah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#030">Jer 36:30</a>) says of Jehoiakim, "he shall have none to sit upon the throne of David.
