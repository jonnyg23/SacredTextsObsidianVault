 <p>the shadow of death:  Where death projects his shadow, intercepting the light of life.  without any order, having no arrangement, no distinction of inhabitants; the poor and the rich are there, the king and the beggar, their bodies in equal corruption and disgrace.  where the light is as darkness, a palpable obscure, space and place, with only such a light or capability of distinction, as renders "darkness visible." <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job003.htm#005">Job 3:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#022">Job 34:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job038.htm#017">Job 38:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#004">Psa 23:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#019">Psa 44:19</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#012">Psa 88:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#006">Jer 2:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer013.htm#016">Jer 13:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#026">Luk 16:26</a>
