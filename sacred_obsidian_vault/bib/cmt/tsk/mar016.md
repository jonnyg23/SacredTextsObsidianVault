 <p>Mr. West supposes that the women made two different visits to the sepulchre, and, in consequence of that, two distinct reports to the disciples; that Mary Magdalene, with the other Mary and Salome, set out not only early, but very early in the morning, λιαν [Strong's G3029], πρωι [Strong's G4404], i.e., before the time appointed to meet Joanna and the other women there.  (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#010">Luk 24:10</a>).  This interpretation, which is adopted by several eminent writers, is very probable, and reconciles the apparent discrepancy in the evangelists.
