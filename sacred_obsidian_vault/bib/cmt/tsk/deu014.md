 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">deu 14:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>the wild goat:  The word akko, according to the LXX and Vulgate, signifies the tragelephus, or goat-deer; so called from its resemblance to both species.  Dr. Shaw states that an animal of this kind is found in the East, where it is called fishtull, and lerwee. 
 </p><p>pygarg:  or, bison.  Heb. dishon. The pygarg, πυγαργος, or white-buttocks, according to the LXX; and Dr. Shaw states that the liamee, as the Africans call it, is exactly such an animal; being of the same shape and colour as the antelope, and of the size of a roebuck.
 </p><p>the wild ox:  Theo, probably the oryx of the Greeks, a species of large stag; and the Bekkar el wash of Dr. Shaw. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#005">Deu 14:5</a> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#006">Deuteronomy 14:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">deu 14:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psa 1:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#002">Psa 1:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#001">Pro 18:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#017">Co2 6:17</a>; On this verse remark, that the clean beast must both chew the cud and part the hoof.  two distinct characteristics, or general signs, by which the possibility of error arising from the misinterpretation of names is obviated.  When God directs, his commands are not of doubtful interpretation.

 <p>the end:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#022">Deu 14:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu026.htm#012">Deu 26:12-15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo004.htm#004">Amo 4:4</a> 
 </p><p>thou shalt bring:  As the Levites had no inheritance, the Israelites were not to forget them, but truly tithe their increase.  For their support, the Levites had:
 </p><p>1. The tenth of all the productions of the land.  
 </p><p>2. Forty-eight cities, each forming a square of 4,000 cubits.  
 </p><p>3. Two thousand cubits of ground round each city; total of land, 53,000 acres.  
 </p><p>4. The first-fruits, and certain parts of all the animals killed in the land.  
 </p><p>But though this was a very small proportion for a whole tribe that had consented to annihilate its political existence, that it might wait upon the service of God, yet, let it be considered, that what they possessed was the best of the land, and while it was slender remuneration for their services, yet their portion was such as rendered them independent, and kept them comfortable; so that they could wait on God, and labour in his work, without distraction.
