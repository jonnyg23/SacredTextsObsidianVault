 <p>Azariah:  Dr. Kennicott complains loudly here of "the corruption in the name of this king of Judah, who is expressed by four different names in this chapter:  Ozriah, Oziah, Ozrihu, and Ozihu.  Our oldest Hebrew manuscript relieves us here, by reading truly, in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#001">Kg2 15:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#006">Kg2 15:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#007">Kg2 15:7</a>, Uzziah, where the printed text is differently corrupted.  This reading is called true:
 </p><p>1. Because it is supported by the Syriac and Arabic versions in these three verses.
 </p><p>2. Because the printed text itself has it so in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#032">Kg2 15:32</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#034">Kg2 15:34</a> of this very chapter.
 </p><p>3. Because it is so expressed in the parallel place in Chronicles.
 </p><p>4. Because it is not Αζαριας, Azarias, but Οζιας, Ozias (Uzziah) in St. Matthew's genealogy.

 <p>am 3262, bc 742
 </p><p>In those days:  "At the end of Jotham's reign."  This Jotham died at forty-one.  He was too great a blessing to be continued long to such an unworthy people.  His death was a judgment, especially considering the character of Ahaz, his son and successor.  for we read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2016.htm#003">Kg2 16:3</a>) Ahaz made his son pass through the fire.  This son may have been Hezekiah, who served the Lord, and whose prayer in sickness was most graciously herd and answered. Isa. 38:1-22
