 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#001">Psa 113:1</a>, An exhortation to praise God for his excellency; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#006">Psa 113:6</a>, for his mercy. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#001">Psalms 113:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 113:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>(Title), This and the following five Psalms form what is called by the Hebrews the great Hallel, or praise; which was sung on their most solemn festivals, and particularly after the celebration of the Passover.  (See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#030">Mat 26:30</a>.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#026">Mar 14:26</a>.)  This and the following were probably composed after the return from the captivity.
