 <p>Hadarezer:  Hadadezer, הדדעזר [Strong's H1909], in the parallel passage, seems an evident mistake for הדרעזר [Strong's H1928], Hadarezer; for the LXX and Vulgate there, as here, read Αδρααζαρ, Adarezer. The difference arises from the mistake of a ר, raish, for a ד, daleth, two letters very similar. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#003">Sa2 8:3</a>, Hadadezer
 </p><p>Zobah:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1014.htm#047">Sa1 14:47</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#006">Sa2 10:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#001">Psa 60:1</a> *title

 </p><p>David:  The words wyakker Dawid eth col haraichev, should be rendered, "and David disjointed all the chariots;" which is nearly the rendering of the LXX, και παραλυσε Δανιδ παντα τα αρματα.  To have houghed the horses would have been both unreasonable and inhuman; for, as he had gained so complete a victory, there was no danger of their falling into the hands of the enemy; and if he did not choose to keep them, which indeed the law would not permit, he ought to have killed them outright., houghed, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#016">Deu 17:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos011.htm#006">Jos 11:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos011.htm#009">Jos 11:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#007">Psa 20:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#016">Psa 33:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#017">Psa 33:17</a>
