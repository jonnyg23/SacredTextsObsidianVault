 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#001">Ch1 26:1</a>, The divisions of the porters; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#013">Ch1 26:13</a>, The gates assigned by lot; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#020">Ch1 26:20</a>, The Levites that had charge of the treasures; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#029">Ch1 26:29</a>, Officers and judges. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#001">1 Chronicles 26:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 26:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>the divisions:  There were four classes of these, each of which belonged to the four gates of the temple, which opened to the four cardinal points of heaven.  The eastern gate fell to Shelemiah; the northern to Zechariah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#014">Ch1 26:14</a>); the southern to Obed-edom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#015">Ch1 26:15</a>); and the western to Shuppim and Hosah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#016">Ch1 26:16</a>).  These several persons were captains of these porter-bands, or door-keepers, at the different gates.  There were probably a thousand men under each of these captains; as we find, from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1023.htm#005">Ch1 23:5</a>, that their whole number was four thousand.
 </p><p>the porters:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1009.htm#017">Ch1 9:17-27</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#018">Ch1 15:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#023">Ch1 15:23</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#024">Ch1 15:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2023.htm#019">Ch2 23:19</a> 
 </p><p>Korhites:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num026.htm#009">Num 26:9-11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#001">Psa 44:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#001">Psa 49:1</a> *titles
 </p><p>Meshelemiah:  שׁלמיה [Strong's H8018], Shelemiah, is merely an abbreviation of משׁלמיה  [Strong's H4920], Meshelemiah, by the apheresis of מ, mem. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#014">Ch1 26:14</a>, Shelemiah
 </p><p>Asaph:  This variation arises from the rejection of the word אב [Strong's H1], av, "father," and the mutation of י, yood, into א, aleph; being written in the parallel passages אביסף [Strong's H43], Ebiasaph, and here אסף [Strong's H623], Asaph. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#037">Ch1 6:37</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1009.htm#019">Ch1 9:19</a>, Ebiasaph 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#004">1 Chronicles 26:4</a></p>

 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#005">1 Chronicles 26:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">ch1 26:5</a></font></span></p>

 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#006">1 Chronicles 26:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ch1 26:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>mighty men of valour:  They were not only porters, or door keepers, in the ordinary sense of the word, but they were a military guard to the gate, as Dr. Delaney suggests that the word shoarim should be rendered here, and perhaps in this sense alone are we to understand their office, which appears to have been of considerable dignity, and conferred only on men of the first rank.  They were appointed to attend the temple, to guard all the avenues to it, to open and shut all the outer gates, and attend at them, not only for state but for service.  They were also required to direct and instruct those who were going to worship in the courts of the sanctuary in the conduct they were to observe, to encourage those who were timid, to send back the strangers and unclean, and to guard against thieves and others who were enemies to the house of God. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1026.htm#008">Ch1 26:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1012.htm#028">Ch1 12:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#012">Jdg 6:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2002.htm#007">Sa2 2:7</a> *marg. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2026.htm#017">Ch2 26:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh011.htm#014">Neh 11:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#012">Ti1 6:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#003">Ti2 2:3</a>
