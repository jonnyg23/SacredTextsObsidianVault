 </p><p>Philemon:  Th e apostle in this epistle indulges in some fine paronomasais on the proper names.  Thus Philemon, Φιλημον [Strong's G5371], affectionate, or beloved, is "our dearly beloved;" Apphia (Απφια [Strong's G682], from απφα), the affectionate address of a brother or sister, according to Suidas), is "the beloved sister," as several manuscripts, Vulgate, and others correctly read; Archippus (Αρχιππος [Strong's G751], the ruler of the horse, for the managing of which heros were anciently famous), is "our fellow- soldier;" and Onesimus (Ονησιμος [Strong's G3682], useful or profitable), once unprofitable, is now profitable., and fellow labourer, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#024">Plm 1:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#009">Co1 3:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#025">Phi 2:25</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi004.htm#003">Phi 4:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#011">Col 4:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1003.htm#002">Th1 3:2</a>
