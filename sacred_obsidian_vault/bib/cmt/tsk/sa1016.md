 </p><p>evil spirit:  The evil spirit was either sent immediately from the Lord, or permitted to come; but whether this was a diabolic possession, or a mere mental malady, is not agreed.  it seems to have partaken of both.  That Saul had fallen into a deep melancholy, there is little doubt; and that an evil spirit might work more effectually on such a state of mind, there can be little question.  His malady appears to have been of a mixed kind, natural and diabolical.  there is too much of apparent nature in it to permit us to believe it was all spiritual; and there is too much of apparently supernatural influence, to suffer us to believe it was all natural. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#010">Sa1 18:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1019.htm#009">Sa1 19:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1019.htm#010">Sa1 19:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg009.htm#023">Jdg 9:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1022.htm#022">Kg1 22:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#015">Act 19:15</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#016">Act 19:16</a>
