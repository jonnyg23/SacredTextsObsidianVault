 </p><p>Lubims:  Lubim, apparently the same with Lehabim (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#013">Gen 10:13</a>), were probably the ancient inhabitants of Lybia (called Lubi in the Syriac version, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#010">Act 2:10</a>), a district of Africa, adjoining to Egypt, and extending along the shore of the Mediterranean as far as the city of Cyrene. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2016.htm#008">Ch2 16:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#005">Eze 30:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#009">Nah 3:9</a> 
 </p><p>the Sukkiims:  The Sukkiim (from sachach, "to cover") are supposed to have been the Troglodites, as the LXX and Vulgate render, a people of Egypt, on the west of the Red Sea, so called because they dwelt εν τρωγλαις, in caves.
 </p><p>Ethiopians:  These Cushim were probably the inhabitants of Ethiopia, south of Egypt. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2014.htm#012">Ch2 14:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2016.htm#008">Ch2 16:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#003">Isa 43:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan011.htm#043">Dan 11:43</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#009">Nah 3:9</a>, Cushim, Heb. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#006">Gen 10:6-8</a>
