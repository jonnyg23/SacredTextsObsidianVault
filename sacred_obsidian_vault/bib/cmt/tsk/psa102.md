 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#001">Psa 102:1</a>, The prophet in his prayer makes a grievous complaint; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#012">Psa 102:12</a>, He takes comfort in the eternity, and mercy of God; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#018">Psa 102:18</a>, The mercies of God are to be recorded; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#023">Psa 102:23</a>, He sustains his weakness by the unchangeableness of God.
 </p><p>This psalm was evidently composed towards the close of the Babylonian captivity; and probably by the prophet Daniel.
