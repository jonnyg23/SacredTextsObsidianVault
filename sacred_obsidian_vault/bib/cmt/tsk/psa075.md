 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">psa 75:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001">Psa 75:1</a>, The prophet praises God; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#002">Psa 75:2</a>, He promises to judge uprightly; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#004">Psa 75:4</a>, He rebukes the proud by consideration of God's providence; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#009">Psa 75:9</a>, He praises God, and promises to execute justice.
 </p><p>not. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#001">Psa 57:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa058.htm#001">Psa 58:1</a> *titles 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001">Psalms 75:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 75:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A Psalm:  Some consider this Psalm to have been written by David on his accession to the throne over all Israel; others refer it to the time of the captivity, considering it as a continuation of the subject in the preceding; but Bp. Patrick and others are of opinion that it was composed by Asaph to commemorate the overthrow of Sennacherib's army, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#001">Kg2 19:1</a>.
 </p><p>of Asaph:  or, for Asaph
