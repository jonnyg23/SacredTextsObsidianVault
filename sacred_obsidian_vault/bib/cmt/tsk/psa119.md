 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001">Psa 119:1</a>, This psalm contains sundry prayers, praises, and professions of obedience. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001">Psalms 119:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 119:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>am 3560, bc 444 (Title), This Psalm, which was probably composed by Ezra, is another of the alphabetical Psalms.  it consists of twenty-two parts, answering to the number of the Hebrew letters; every part being divided into eight verses, and each verse beginning with that letter which forms the title of the part; that is, the first part of eight verses with א, the second with ב, etc.  It is an elegant, important, and useful composition; the chief subjects of which are the excellence of God's laws, and the happiness of those who observe them.
