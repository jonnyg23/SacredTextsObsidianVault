 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psalms</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">psa 43:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#001">Psa 43:1</a>, David, praying to be restored to the temple, promises to serve God joyfully; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#005">Psa 43:5</a>, He encourages his soul to trust in God. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#001">Psalms 43:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 43:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>am 2983, bc 1021 (Title), This Psalm is evidently a continuation of the preceding, and had the same author; and they are written as one in forty-six manuscripts  The sameness of subject, similarity of composition, and return of the same burden in both, are sufficient evidence of this opinion.
