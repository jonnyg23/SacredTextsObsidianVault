 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#008">Proverbs 26:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 26:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>bindeth a stone in a sling:  Heb. putteth a precious stone in a heap of stones, This probably refers, as Coverdale understands it, to the custom of throwing a stone to the heap under which a criminal was buried.  So the Vulgate, Sicut qui mittit lapidem in acervum Mercurii; ita qui tribuit insipienti honorem, "As he who throws a stone to Mercury's heap, so is he who gives honour to a fool."  Mercury was a heathen god of highways; and stones were erected in different parts to guide the traveller: hence those lines of Dr. Young,
 </p><p>  "Death stands like Mercuries in every way;
 </p><p>  And kindly points us to our journey's end."
 </p><p>so:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#001">Pro 26:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#010">Pro 19:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#022">Pro 30:22</a>
