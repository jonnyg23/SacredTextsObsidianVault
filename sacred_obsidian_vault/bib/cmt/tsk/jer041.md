 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer001.htm#001">Jeremiah</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">jer 41:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer041.htm#001">Jer 41:1</a>, Ishmael, treacherously killing Gedaliah and others, purposes with the residue to flee unto the Ammonites; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer041.htm#011">Jer 41:11</a>, Johanan recovers the captives, and is minded to flee into Egypt. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer041.htm#001">Jeremiah 41:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jer 41:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>the seventh month:  This was the month Tisri answering to the new moon of September, the seventh of the sacred, but the first of the civil year; on the third day of which the Jews keep a fast, in commemoration of the death of Gedaliah, to which the prophet Zechariah refers, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#019">Zac 8:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#002">Jer 39:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer052.htm#006">Jer 52:6</a>.  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#003">Kg2 25:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#008">Kg2 25:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#025">Kg2 25:25</a>. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac007.htm#005">Zac 7:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#019">Zac 8:19</a>.

 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">jer 41:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Slay:  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#004">Job 2:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#006">Psa 49:6-8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#008">Pro 13:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#025">Mat 6:25</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#026">Mat 16:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#036">Mar 8:36</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#037">Mar 8:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#007">Phi 3:7-9</a> 
 </p><p>treasures:  These "treasures hid in the field" were doubtless laid up in subterranean pits, similar to the mattamores in Barbary, in which, Dr. Shaw informs us, they deposit the grain when winnowed; two or three hundred of them being sometimes together, and the smallest holding four hundred bushels.  The same mode of keeping corn prevails in Syria and the Holy Land. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer041.htm#009">Jeremiah 41:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">jer 41:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>the pit:  This was probably a large reservoir for receiving rain water, which Asa had caused to be made in the midst of the city, in case of a siege.
 </p><p>because of Gedaliah:  or, near Gedaliah, Heb. by the hand, or the side of Gedaliah.  was it. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos010.htm#016">Jos 10:16-18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#002">Jdg 6:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1013.htm#006">Sa1 13:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1014.htm#011">Sa1 14:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1014.htm#022">Sa1 14:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1024.htm#003">Sa1 24:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2017.htm#009">Sa2 17:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#038">Heb 11:38</a>
