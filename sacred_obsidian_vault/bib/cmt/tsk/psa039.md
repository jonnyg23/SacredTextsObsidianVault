 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#001">Psa 39:1</a>, David's care of this thoughts; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#004">Psa 39:4</a>, The consideration of the brevity and vanity of life; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#007">Psa 39:7</a>, the reverence of God's judgments, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#010">Psa 39:10</a>, and prayer, are his bridles of impatience.
 </p><p>Jeduthun, probably the same as Ethan, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#044">Ch1 6:44</a>, was one of the sons of Merari, and is supposed to have been one the four masters of music, or leaders of bands, belonging to the temple service.  It is therefore probable that David, having composed this Psalm, gave it to Jeduthun and his company to sing; and it is very likely, that it was written on the same occasion as the preceding. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#001">Psa 62:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#001">Psa 77:1</a> *titles <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1016.htm#041">Ch1 16:41</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1025.htm#001">Ch1 25:1-6</a>
