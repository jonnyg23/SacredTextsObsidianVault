 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#001">Psa 132:1</a>, David in his prayer commends unto God the religious care he had for the ark; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#008">Psa 132:8</a>, His prayer at the removing of the ark; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#011">Psa 132:11</a>, with a repetition of God's promises.
 </p><p>Some attribute this Psalm to Solomon; and others refer it to the building of the second temple; but it seems more probable that it was sung at the solemn induction of the ark into the tabernacle of Mount Zion, expressing the holy joy and triumph of that event. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#001">Psa 120:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#001">Psa 121:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#001">Psa 122:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa123.htm#001">Psa 123:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psa 124:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa125.htm#001">Psa 125:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa126.htm#001">Psa 126:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa127.htm#001">Psa 127:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa128.htm#001">Psa 128:1</a> *titles <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#001">Psa 129:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001">Psa 130:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psa 131:1</a> *titles
