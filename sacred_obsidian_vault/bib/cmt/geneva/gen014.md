 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#001">Genesis 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">gen 14:1</a></font></span></p>And it came to pass in the days of Amraphel king of (a) Shinar, Arioch king of Ellasar, Chedorlaomer king of Elam, and Tidal king of (b) nations; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, of Babylon: by kings here, meaning, them that were governors of cities. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Of a people gathered from various countries.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#003">Genesis 14:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">gen 14:3</a></font></span></p>All these were (c) joined together in the vale of Siddim, which is the (d) salt sea. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Ambition is the chief cause of wars among princes. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) Called also the dead sea, or the lake Asphaltite, near Sodom and Gomorrah.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#010">Genesis 14:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">gen 14:10</a></font></span></p>And the (e) vale of Siddim [was full of] slimepits; and the kings of Sodom and Gomorrah fled, and fell there; and they that remained fled to the mountain. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) And afterward was overwhelmed with water, and so was called the salt sea.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#012">Genesis 14:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">gen 14:12</a></font></span></p>And they (f) took Lot, Abram's brother's son, who dwelt in Sodom, and his goods, and departed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) The godly are plagued many times with the wicked: therefore their company is dangerous.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#013">Genesis 14:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">gen 14:13</a></font></span></p>And there came one that had escaped, and told Abram the Hebrew; for he dwelt in the plain of Mamre the Amorite, brother of Eshcol, and brother of Aner: and these [were] (g) confederate with Abram. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) God removed them to join Abram, and preserves him from their idolatry and superstitions.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018">Genesis 14:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">gen 14:18</a></font></span></p>And Melchizedek king of Salem (h) brought forth bread and wine: and he [was] the priest of the most high God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) For Abram and his soldiers refreshment, not to offer sacrifice.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#019">Genesis 14:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">gen 14:19</a></font></span></p>And he (i) blessed him, and said, Blessed [be] Abram of the most high God, possessor of heaven and earth: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Melchizedek fed Abram, declared himself to represent a king, and he blessed him as the high priest.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#024">Genesis 14:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">gen 14:24</a></font></span></p>(k) Save only that which the young men have eaten, and the portion of the men which went with me, Aner, Eshcol, and Mamre; let them take their portion. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) He did not want his liberality to be hurtful to others.
