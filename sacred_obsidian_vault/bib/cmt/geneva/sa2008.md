 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#001">2 Kings (2 Samuel) Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#001">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">sa2 8:1</a></font></span></p>And after this it came to pass, that David smote the Philistines, and subdued them: and David took Methegammah out of the (a) hand of the Philistines. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) So that they paid no more tribute. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#002">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">sa2 8:2</a></font></span></p>And he smote Moab, and measured them with a line, casting them down to the ground; even with (b) two lines measured he to put to death, and with one full line to keep alive. And [so] the Moabites became David's servants, [and] brought gifts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He slew two parts as it pleased him, and reserved the third. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#006">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">sa2 8:6</a></font></span></p>Then David put garrisons in (c) Syria of Damascus: and the Syrians became servants to David, (d) [and] brought gifts. And the LORD preserved David whithersoever he went. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) In that part of Syria, where Damascus was. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) They paid yearly tribute. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#007">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">sa2 8:7</a></font></span></p>And David took the shields of gold that were on the servants of Hadadezer, and brought them to (e) Jerusalem. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) For the use of the temple. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#010">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">sa2 8:10</a></font></span></p>Then Toi sent Joram his son unto king David, to salute him, and to (f) bless him, because he had fought against Hadadezer, and smitten him: for Hadadezer had wars with Toi. And [Joram] brought with him vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and vessels of brass: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) For seeing David victorious, he was glad to ask for peace. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#015">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">sa2 8:15</a></font></span></p>And David reigned over all Israel; and David executed (g) judgment and justice unto all his people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) He gave judgment in controversies, and was merciful toward the people. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#018">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 8:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">sa2 8:18</a></font></span></p>And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada [was over] both the (h) Cherethites and the Pelethites; and David's sons were chief rulers. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) The Cherethites and Pelethites were as the king's guard, and had charge of his person.
