 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#001">Exodus 26:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 26:1</a></font></span></p>Moreover thou shalt make the tabernacle [with] ten curtains [of] fine twined linen, and blue, and purple, and scarlet: [with] cherubims of (a) cunning work shalt thou make them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, of most cunning or fine work.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#004">Exodus 26:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">exo 26:4</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt make loops of blue upon the edge of the one curtain from the selvedge (b) in the coupling; and likewise shalt thou make in the uttermost edge of [another] curtain, in the coupling of the second. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) On the side that the curtains might be tied together.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#005">Exodus 26:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">exo 26:5</a></font></span></p>Fifty loops shalt thou make in the one curtain, and fifty loops shalt thou make in the edge of the curtain that [is] in the coupling of the (c) second; that the loops may take hold one of another. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) In tying together both the sides.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#007">Exodus 26:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">exo 26:7</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt make curtains [of] goats' [hair] to be a (d) covering upon the tabernacle: eleven curtains shalt thou make. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Lest rain and weather should mar it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#009">Exodus 26:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">exo 26:9</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt couple five curtains by themselves, and six curtains by themselves, and shalt double the (e) sixth curtain in the forefront of the tabernacle. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) That is, five on the one side, and five on the other, and the sixth should hang over the door of the tabernacle.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#012">Exodus 26:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">exo 26:12</a></font></span></p>And the (f) remnant that remaineth of the curtains of the tent, the half curtain that remaineth, shall hang over the backside of the tabernacle. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) For these curtains were two cubits longer than the curtain of the tabernacle so that they were wider by a cubit of both sides.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#014">Exodus 26:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">exo 26:14</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt make a (g) covering for the tent [of] rams' skins dyed red, and a covering above [of] (h) badgers' skins. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) To be put on the covering that was made of goats hair. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) This was the third covering of the tabernacle.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#024">Exodus 26:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">exo 26:24</a></font></span></p>And they shall be coupled together beneath, and they shall be (i) coupled together above the head of it unto one ring: thus shall it be for them both; they shall be for the two corners. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) The Hebrew word signifies twins declaring that they should be as perfect and well joined as possible.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#032">Exodus 26:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">exo 26:32</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt hang it upon four pillars of shittim [wood] overlaid with gold: their (k) hooks [shall be of] gold, upon the four sockets of silver. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Some read "heads of the pillars".  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#033">Exodus 26:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">exo 26:33</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt hang up the vail under the taches, that thou mayest bring in thither within the vail the ark of the testimony: and the vail shall divide unto you between the holy [place] and the (l) most holy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) In which only the high priest entered and only once a year.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#035">Exodus 26:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">exo 26:35</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt set the table (m) without the vail, and the candlestick over against the table on the side of the tabernacle toward the south: and thou shalt put the table on the north side. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Meaning, in the holy place.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#036">Exodus 26:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">exo 26:36</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt make an (n) hanging for the door of the tent, [of] blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen, wrought with needlework. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) This hanging or veil was between the holy place, and there where the people were.
