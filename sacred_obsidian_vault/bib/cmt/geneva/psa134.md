 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa134.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 134</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa134.htm#001">Psalms 134:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 134:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song of degrees." Behold, bless ye the LORD, all [ye] (a) servants of the LORD, which by night stand in the house of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) You who are Levites and chiefly appointed to this office.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa134.htm#002">Psalms 134:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 134:2</a></font></span></p>Lift up your (b) hands [in] the sanctuary, and bless the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For their charge was not only to keep the temple, but to pray there and to give God thanks.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa134.htm#003">Psalms 134:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 134:3</a></font></span></p>The LORD that (c) made heaven and earth bless thee out of Zion. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) And therefore has all power, bless you with his fatherly love declared in Zion. Thus the Levites used to praise the Lord, and bless the people.
