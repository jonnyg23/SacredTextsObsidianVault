 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo005.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo005.htm#001">Exodus 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 5:1</a></font></span></p>And afterward Moses and Aaron went in, and told (a) Pharaoh, Thus saith the LORD God of Israel, Let my people go, that they may (b) hold a feast unto me in the wilderness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Faith overcomes fear, and makes men bold in their calling. (b) And offer sacrifice.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo005.htm#005">Exodus 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">exo 5:5</a></font></span></p>And Pharaoh said, Behold, the people of the land now [are] many, and ye (c) make them rest from their burdens. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) As though you would rebel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo005.htm#006">Exodus 5:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">exo 5:6</a></font></span></p>And Pharaoh commanded the same day the taskmasters of the people, and their (d) officers, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Who were of the Israelites, and had charge to see them do their work.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo005.htm#009">Exodus 5:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">exo 5:9</a></font></span></p>(e) Let there more work be laid upon the men, that they may labour therein; and let them not regard (f) vain words. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The more cruelly the tyranny rages, the nearer is God's help. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) Of Moses and Aaron.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo005.htm#021">Exodus 5:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">exo 5:21</a></font></span></p>And they said unto them, The LORD look upon you, and judge; because ye have made our savour to be abhorred in the eyes of Pharaoh, and in the eyes of his servants, to (g) put a sword in their hand to slay us. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) It is a grievous things for the servants of God to be accused of evil, especially by their brethren, when they do what their duty requires.
