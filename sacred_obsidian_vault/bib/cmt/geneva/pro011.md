 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001">Proverbs 11:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 11:1</a></font></span></p>A false (a) balance [is] abomination to the LORD: but a just weight [is] his delight. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Under this word he condemns all false weights, measures and deceit.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#002">Proverbs 11:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 11:2</a></font></span></p>[When] pride cometh, then cometh (b) shame: but with the lowly [is] wisdom. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) When man so gets himself, and thinks to be exalted above his calling then God brings him to confusion.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#008">Proverbs 11:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 11:8</a></font></span></p>The righteous is delivered out of trouble, and the wicked cometh in his (c) stead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, will enter into trouble.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#009">Proverbs 11:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 11:9</a></font></span></p>An (d) hypocrite with [his] mouth destroyeth his neighbour: but through knowledge shall the just be delivered. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) A deceiver who pretends friendship, but privately is an enemy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#010">Proverbs 11:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 11:10</a></font></span></p>When the righteous prosper, the city (e) rejoiceth: and when the wicked perish, [there is] shouting. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The country is blessed, where there are godly men, and they ought to rejoice when the wicked are taken away.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#012">Proverbs 11:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 11:12</a></font></span></p>He that is void of wisdom despiseth his neighbour: but a man of understanding (f) holdeth his peace. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Will not make light report of others.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#014">Proverbs 11:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 11:14</a></font></span></p>Where no counsel [is], the people fall: but in the multitude of (g) counsellors [there is] safety. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Where God gives store of men of wisdom and counsel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#015">Proverbs 11:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 11:15</a></font></span></p>He that is surety for a (h) stranger shall smart [for it]: and he (i) that hateth suretiship is secure. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) He who does not without judgment and consideration of the circumstances put himself in danger, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001">Pro 6:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) He who does not co-sign loans for others is very wise.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#017">Proverbs 11:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 11:17</a></font></span></p>The merciful man (k) doeth good to his own soul: but [he that is] cruel troubleth his own flesh. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Rewards both himself and others.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#021">Proverbs 11:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 11:21</a></font></span></p>(l) [Though] hand [join] in hand, the wicked shall not be unpunished: but the seed of the righteous shall be delivered. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Though they make many friends, or think themselves sure, yet they will not escape.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#023">Proverbs 11:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 11:23</a></font></span></p>The desire of the righteous [is] only good: [but] the expectation of the wicked (m) [is] wrath. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) They can look for nothing but God's vengeance.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#024">Proverbs 11:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 11:24</a></font></span></p>There is that scattereth, (n) and yet increaseth; and [there is] that withholdeth more (o) than is right, but [it tendeth] to poverty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) Meaning they who give liberally, whom God blesses. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) That is, the miser.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#026">Proverbs 11:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 11:26</a></font></span></p>He that withholdeth grain, the people shall curse him: but blessing [shall be] upon the head of him that (p) selleth [it]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) That provides for the use of them who are in need.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#029">Proverbs 11:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 11:29</a></font></span></p>He that troubleth his own (q) house shall inherit the wind: and the fool [shall be] (r) servant to the wise of heart. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) The covetous men who spare their riches to the hinderance of their families, will be deprived of it miserably. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(r) For though the wicked are rich, yet they are only slaves to the godly, who are the true possessors of the gifts of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#030">Proverbs 11:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 11:30</a></font></span></p>The fruit of the righteous [is] a tree of life; and he that (s) winneth souls [is] wise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) That is, brings them to the knowledge of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#031">Proverbs 11:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 11:31</a></font></span></p>Behold, the righteous shall be (t) recompensed upon the earth: much more the wicked and the sinner. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) Will be punished as he deserves, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#018">Pe1 4:18</a>).
