 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 108</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#001">Psalms 108:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 108:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song [or] Psalm of David." O God, my heart is (a) fixed; I will sing and give praise, even with my glory. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) This earnest affection declares that he is free from hypocrisy and that sluggishness does not stop him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#003">Psalms 108:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 108:3</a></font></span></p>I will praise thee, O LORD, among the (b) people: and I will sing praises unto thee among the nations. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He prophecies of the calling of the Gentiles: for unless they were called, they could not hear the goodness of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#005">Psalms 108:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 108:5</a></font></span></p>(c) Be thou exalted, O God, above the heavens: and thy glory above all the earth; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Let all the world see your judgments in that you are God over all, and so confess that you are glorious.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#006">Psalms 108:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 108:6</a></font></span></p>That thy beloved may be delivered: (d) save [with] thy right hand, and answer me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) When God by his benefits makes us partakers of his mercies, he admonishes us to be earnest in prayer, to desire him to continue and finish his graces.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#007">Psalms 108:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 108:7</a></font></span></p>God hath spoken in his (e) holiness; I will rejoice, I will divide Shechem, and mete out the valley of Succoth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) As he has spoken to Samuel concerning me, so will he show himself constant and holy in his promise, so that these nations following will be subject to me.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#011">Psalms 108:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 108:11</a></font></span></p>(f) [Wilt] not [thou], O God, [who] hast cast us off? and wilt not thou, O God, go forth with our hosts? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) From the sixth verse of this psalm to the last, read also (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005">Psa 60:5</a>).
