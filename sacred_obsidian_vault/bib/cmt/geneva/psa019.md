 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 19</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#001">Psalms 19:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 19:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician, A Psalm of David." The (a) heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament sheweth his handywork. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He reproaches man for his ingratitude, seeing the heavens, which are dumb creatures, set forth God's glory.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#002">Psalms 19:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 19:2</a></font></span></p>(b) Day unto day uttereth speech, and night unto night sheweth knowledge. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The continual success of the day and night is sufficient to declare God's power and goodness.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#003">Psalms 19:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 19:3</a></font></span></p>[There is] no speech nor (c) language, [where] their voice is not heard. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) The heavens are a schoolmaster to all nations, no matter how barbarous.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#004">Psalms 19:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 19:4</a></font></span></p>Their (d) line is gone out through all the earth, and their words to the end of the world. In them hath he set a tabernacle for the sun, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) The heavens are as a line of great capital letters to show God's glory to us.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#005">Psalms 19:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 19:5</a></font></span></p>Which [is] as a bridegroom coming out of his (e) chamber, [and] rejoiceth as a strong man to run a race. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Or vail. The custom was that the bride and bridegroom would stand under a vail together, and after come forward with great solemnity and rejoicing of the assembly.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#007">Psalms 19:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 19:7</a></font></span></p>The (f) law of the LORD [is] perfect, converting the soul: the testimony of the LORD [is] sure, making wise the simple. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Though the creatures cannot serve, yet this should be sufficient to lead us to him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#009">Psalms 19:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 19:9</a></font></span></p>The fear of the LORD [is] clean, enduring for ever: the judgments of the LORD [are] (g) true [and] righteous (h) altogether. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) So that all man's inventions and intentions are lies. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) Everyone without exception.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#010">Psalms 19:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 19:10</a></font></span></p>More to be (i) desired [are they] than gold, yea, than much fine gold: sweeter also than honey and the honeycomb. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Unless God's word is esteemed above all worldly things, it is contemned.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#011">Psalms 19:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 19:11</a></font></span></p>Moreover by them is thy servant warned: [and] in keeping of them [there is] great (k) reward. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) For God accepts our endeavour though it is far from perfect.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#012">Psalms 19:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 19:12</a></font></span></p>Who can understand [his] (l) errors? cleanse thou me from secret [faults]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Then there is no reward of duty, but of grace: for where sin is, there death is the reward.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#013">Psalms 19:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 19:13</a></font></span></p>Keep back thy servant also from (m) presumptuous [sins]; let them not have dominion over me: (n) then shall I be upright, and I shall be innocent from the great transgression. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Which are done purposely and from malice. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(n) If you suppress my wicked affections by your Holy Spirit.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#014">Psalms 19:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 19:14</a></font></span></p>Let the words of my mouth, and the (o) meditation of my heart, be acceptable in thy sight, O LORD, my strength, and my redeemer. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) That I may obey you in thought, word and deed.
