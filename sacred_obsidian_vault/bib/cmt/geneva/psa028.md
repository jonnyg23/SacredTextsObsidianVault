 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 28</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#002">Psalms 28:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 28:2</a></font></span></p>Hear the voice of my supplications, when I cry unto thee, when I lift up my hands toward thy (b) holy oracle. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He counts himself as a dead man, till God shows his favour toward him, and grants him his petition.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#003">Psalms 28:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 28:3</a></font></span></p>(c) Draw me not away with the wicked, and with the workers of iniquity, which speak peace to their neighbours, but mischief [is] in their hearts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Destroy not the good with the bad.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#004">Psalms 28:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 28:4</a></font></span></p>(d) Give them according to their deeds, and according to the wickedness of their endeavours: give them after the work of their hands; render to them their desert. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He thus prayed in respect to God's glory and not for his own cause, being assured that God would punish the persecutors of his Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#005">Psalms 28:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 28:5</a></font></span></p>Because they regard not the works of the LORD, nor the operation of his hands, he shall (e) destroy them, and not build them up. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Let them be utterly destroyed as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal001.htm#004">Mal 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#006">Psalms 28:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 28:6</a></font></span></p>(f) Blessed [be] the LORD, because he hath heard the voice of my supplications. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Because he felt the assurance of God's help in his heart, his mouth was opened to sing his praises.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#008">Psalms 28:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 28:8</a></font></span></p>The LORD [is] (g) their strength, and he [is] the saving strength of his anointed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Meaning his soldiers who were means by which God declared his power.
