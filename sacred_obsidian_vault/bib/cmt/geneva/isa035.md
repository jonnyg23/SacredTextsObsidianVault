 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 35</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#001">Isaiah 35:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 35:1</a></font></span></p>The (a) wilderness and the solitary place shall be glad for them; and the desert shall rejoice, and blossom as the rose. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He prophecies of the full restoration of the Church both of the Jews and Gentiles under Christ, which will be fully accomplished at the last day: although as yet it is compared to a desert and wilderness.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#002">Isaiah 35:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 35:2</a></font></span></p>It shall blossom abundantly, and rejoice even with joy and singing: the glory of Lebanon shall be given to it, the excellence of (b) Carmel and Sharon, they shall (c) see the glory of the LORD, [and] the excellence of our God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The Church which was before compared to a barren wilderness will by Christ be made most plenteous and beautiful. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) He shows that the presence of God is the reason that the Church brings forth fruit and flourishes.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#003">Isaiah 35:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 35:3</a></font></span></p>(d) Strengthen ye the weak hands, and confirm the feeble knees. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He wills all to encourage one another, and especially the ministers to exhort and strengthen the weak, that they may patiently abide the coming of God, which is at hand.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#004">Isaiah 35:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">isa 35:4</a></font></span></p>Say to them [that are] of a fearful heart, Be strong, fear not: behold, your God will come [with] (e) vengeance, [even] God [with] a recompence; he will come and save you. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) To destroy your enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#005">Isaiah 35:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 35:5</a></font></span></p>Then the eyes of the (f) blind shall be opened, and the ears of the deaf shall be unstopped. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) When the knowledge of Christ is revealed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#006">Isaiah 35:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 35:6</a></font></span></p>Then shall the lame [man] leap as an hart, and the tongue of the dumb shall sing: for in the (g) wilderness shall waters break out, and streams in the desert. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) They who were barren and destitute of the graces of God, will have them given by Christ.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#008">Isaiah 35:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 35:8</a></font></span></p>And an highway shall be there, and a way, and it shall be called The way of (h) holiness; the unclean shall not pass over it; but it (i) [shall be] for those: the wayfaring men, though fools, shall not err [in it]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) It will be for the saints of God and not for the wicked. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) God will lead and guide them, alluding to the bringing forth of Egypt.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#009">Isaiah 35:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 35:9</a></font></span></p>No lion shall be there, nor [any] (k) ravenous beast shall go up on it, it shall not be found there; but the redeemed shall walk [there]: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) As he threatens the wicked with destruction by this, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#006">Isa 30:6</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#010">Isaiah 35:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">isa 35:10</a></font></span></p>And the (l) ransomed of the LORD shall return, and come to Zion with songs and everlasting joy upon their heads: they shall obtain joy and gladness, and sorrow and sighing shall flee away. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Whom the Lord will deliver from the captivity of Babylon.
