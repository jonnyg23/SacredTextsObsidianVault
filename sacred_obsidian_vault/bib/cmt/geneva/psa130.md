 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 130</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001">Psalms 130:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 130:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song of degrees." Out of the (a) depths have I cried unto thee, O LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Being in great distress and sorrow.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#003">Psalms 130:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 130:3</a></font></span></p>If thou, LORD, shouldest mark iniquities, O Lord, (b) who shall stand? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He declares that we cannot be just before God but by forgiveness of sins.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#004">Psalms 130:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 130:4</a></font></span></p>But [there is] forgiveness with thee, that thou (c) mayest be feared. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Because by nature you are merciful therefore the faithful revere you.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#007">Psalms 130:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 130:7</a></font></span></p>Let Israel hope in the LORD: for with the LORD [there is] (d) mercy, and with him [is] plenteous redemption. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He shows to whom the mercy of God belongs, that is, Israel, to the Church and not the reprobate.
