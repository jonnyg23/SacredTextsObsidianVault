 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa099.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 99</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa099.htm#001">Psalms 99:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 99:1</a></font></span></p>The LORD reigneth; let the (a) people tremble: he sitteth [between] the cherubims; let the earth be moved. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) When God delivers his Church all the enemies will have reason to tremble.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa099.htm#003">Psalms 99:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 99:3</a></font></span></p>Let them (b) praise thy great and terrible name; [for] it [is] holy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Though the wicked rage against God, the godly will praise his Name and mighty power.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa099.htm#005">Psalms 99:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 99:5</a></font></span></p>Exalt ye the LORD our God, and worship at his (c) footstool; [for] he [is] holy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, before his temple or ark, where he promised to hear when they worshipped him, as now he promises his spiritual presence, wherever his Church is assembled.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa099.htm#006">Psalms 99:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 99:6</a></font></span></p>Moses and Aaron among his priests, (d) and Samuel among them that call upon his name; they called upon the LORD, and he answered them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Under these three he comprehends the whole people of Israel, with whom God made his promise.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa099.htm#008">Psalms 99:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 99:8</a></font></span></p>Thou answeredst them, O LORD our God: thou wast a God that forgavest them, though thou tookest vengeance of (e) their inventions. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The more liberally God deals with his people, the more he punishes them who abuse his benefits.
