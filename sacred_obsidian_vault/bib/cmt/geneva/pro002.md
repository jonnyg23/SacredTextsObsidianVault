 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001">Proverbs 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 2:1</a></font></span></p>My son, if thou wilt receive my words, and (a) hide my commandments with thee; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, keep them in your heart.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#002">Proverbs 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 2:2</a></font></span></p>So that thou incline thine ear unto wisdom, [and] apply (b) thine heart to understanding; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) If you give yourself to the true knowledge of God without hypocrisy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#003">Proverbs 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 2:3</a></font></span></p>Yea, if thou criest after knowledge, [and] (c) liftest up thy voice for understanding; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning that we must seek the knowledge of God with care and diligence.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#004">Proverbs 2:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 2:4</a></font></span></p>If thou seekest her as silver, and searchest for her as [for] hid (d) treasures; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Showing that no labour must be spared.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#005">Proverbs 2:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 2:5</a></font></span></p>Then shalt thou understand the fear of the LORD, and find the (e) knowledge of God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) This (he says) is the true wisdom to know and fear God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#011">Proverbs 2:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 2:11</a></font></span></p>(f) Discretion shall preserve thee, understanding shall keep thee: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) The word of God will guide you, and teach you how to govern yourself.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#013">Proverbs 2:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 2:13</a></font></span></p>Who leave the (g) paths of uprightness, to walk in the ways of darkness; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is, the word of God, which is the only light, to follow their own fantasies which are darkness.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#014">Proverbs 2:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 2:14</a></font></span></p>Who rejoice to do evil, [and] delight (h) in the frowardness of the wicked; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) When they see any given to evil as they are.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#016">Proverbs 2:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 2:16</a></font></span></p>To deliver thee from the strange (i) woman, [even] from the stranger [which] flattereth with her words; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Meaning, the wisdom which is the word of God, will preserve us from all vices: naming this vice of whoredom to which man is most prone.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#017">Proverbs 2:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 2:17</a></font></span></p>Which forsaketh the (k) guide of her youth, and forgetteth the covenant of her God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) That is, her husband, who is her head and guide to govern her, from whom she ought not to depart, but remain in his subjection.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#018">Proverbs 2:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 2:18</a></font></span></p>For her (m) house inclineth unto death, and her paths unto (n) the dead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Her acquaintance with her spirits and they that haunt her. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(n) To them who are dead in body and soul.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#021">Proverbs 2:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 2:21</a></font></span></p>For the upright shall dwell in the (o) land, and the perfect shall remain in it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) They will enjoy the temporal and spiritual promises of God, as the wicked will be void of them.
