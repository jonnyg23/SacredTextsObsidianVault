 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 52</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#001">Psalms 52:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 52:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician, Maschil, [A Psalm] of David, when Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, and said unto him, David is come to the house of Ahimelech." Why boastest thou thyself in mischief, O (a) mighty man? the goodness of God [endureth] continually. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) O Doeg, who half consider to be the tyrant Saul, and had the power to murder the saints of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#002">Psalms 52:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 52:2</a></font></span></p>Thy tongue deviseth (b) mischiefs; like a sharp razor, working deceitfully. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Your malice moves you by crafty flattery and lies to accuse and destroy the innocents.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#005">Psalms 52:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 52:5</a></font></span></p>God shall likewise (c) destroy thee for ever, he shall take thee away, and pluck thee out of [thy] dwelling place, and (d) root thee out of the land of the living. Selah. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Though God forbear for a time, yet at length he will recompense your falsehood. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) Even though you seem to be never so sure settled.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#006">Psalms 52:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 52:6</a></font></span></p>The (e) righteous also shall see, (f) and fear, and shall laugh at him: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) For the eyes of the reprobate are shut at God's judgments. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) With joyful reverence, seeing that he takes their part against the wicked.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#008">Psalms 52:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 52:8</a></font></span></p>But I [am] like a (g) green olive tree in the house of God: I trust in the mercy of God for ever and ever. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) He rejoices to have a place among the servant's of God, that he may grow in the knowledge of godliness.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#009">Psalms 52:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 52:9</a></font></span></p>I will praise thee for ever, because thou hast done (h) [it]: and I will wait on thy name; for [it is] good before thy saints. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Executed his vengeance.
