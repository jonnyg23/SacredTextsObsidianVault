 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 138</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#001">Psalms 138:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 138:1</a></font></span></p>"[A Psalm] of David." I will praise thee with my whole heart: before the (a) gods will I sing praise unto thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Even in the presence of angels and of them who have authority among men.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#002">Psalms 138:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 138:2</a></font></span></p>I will worship toward thy holy (b) temple, and praise thy name for thy lovingkindness and for thy truth: for thou hast magnified thy word above all thy name. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Both the temple and ceremonial service at Christ's coming were abolished: so that now God will be worshipped only in spirit and truth, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#023">Joh 4:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#003">Psalms 138:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 138:3</a></font></span></p>In the day when I cried thou answeredst me, [and] (c) strengthenedst me [with] strength in my soul. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) You have strengthened me against my outward and inward enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#004">Psalms 138:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 138:4</a></font></span></p>All the (d) kings of the earth shall praise thee, O LORD, when they hear the words of thy mouth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) All the world will confess that you have wonderfully preserved me, and performed your promise.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#006">Psalms 138:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 138:6</a></font></span></p>Though the LORD [be] high, yet hath he respect unto the lowly: but the proud he knoweth (e) afar off. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Distance of place cannot hinder God to show mercy to his, and so judge the wicked though they think that he is far off.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#008">Psalms 138:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 138:8</a></font></span></p>The LORD will (f) perfect [that which] concerneth me: thy mercy, O LORD, [endureth] for ever: forsake not the works of thine own hands. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Though my enemies rage, yet the Lord, who has begun his work in me, will continue his grace to the end.
