 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#004">Proverbs 26:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 26:4</a></font></span></p>Answer not a fool (a) according to his folly, lest thou also be like him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Consent not to him in his doings.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#005">Proverbs 26:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 26:5</a></font></span></p>Answer a fool (b) according to his folly, lest he be wise in his own conceit. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Reprove him as the matter requires.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#006">Proverbs 26:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 26:6</a></font></span></p>He that sendeth a message by the hand of a fool cutteth off (c) the feet, (d) [and] drinketh damage. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, of the messenger whom he sends. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, receives damage by it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#009">Proverbs 26:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 26:9</a></font></span></p>[As] a thorn goeth (e) up into the hand of a drunkard, so [is] a parable in the mouth of fools. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) By which he hurts both himself and others.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#010">Proverbs 26:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 26:10</a></font></span></p>(f) The great [God] that formed all [things] both rewardeth the fool, and rewardeth transgressors. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Meaning God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#012">Proverbs 26:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 26:12</a></font></span></p>Seest thou a man wise in his own conceit? [there is] (g) more hope of a fool than of him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) For the fool would rather be counselled than he: also the fool sins out of ignorance, and the other out of malice.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#013">Proverbs 26:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 26:13</a></font></span></p>The slothful [man] saith, (h) [There is] a lion in the way; a lion [is] in the streets. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#013">Pro 22:13</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#019">Proverbs 26:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 26:19</a></font></span></p>So [is] the man [that] deceiveth (i) his neighbour, and saith, Am not I in sport? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Who disguises himself to be that which he is not.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#023">Proverbs 26:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 26:23</a></font></span></p>Burning lips and (k) a wicked heart [are like] a potsherd covered with silver dross. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) They will soon break out and utter themselves.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#025">Proverbs 26:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 26:25</a></font></span></p>When he speaketh kindly, believe him not: for [there are] (l) seven abominations in his heart. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Meaning many: he uses a certain number for the uncertain.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#026">Proverbs 26:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 26:26</a></font></span></p>[Whose] hatred is covered by deceit, his wickedness shall be revealed before the [whole] (m) congregation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) In the assembly of the godly.
