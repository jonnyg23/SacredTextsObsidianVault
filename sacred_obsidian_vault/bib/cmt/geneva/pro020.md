 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 20</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001">Proverbs 20:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 20:1</a></font></span></p>(a) Wine [is] a mocker, strong drink [is] raging: and whoever is deceived by it is not wise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) By wine here is meant him that is given to wine, and so by strong drink.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002">Proverbs 20:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 20:2</a></font></span></p>The fear of a king [is] as the roaring of a lion: [whoever] provoketh him to anger (b) sinneth [against] his own soul. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Puts his life in danger.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#005">Proverbs 20:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 20:5</a></font></span></p>Counsel in the heart of (c) man [is like] deep water; but a man of understanding will draw it out. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) It is hard to find out: for it is as deep waters, whose bottom cannot be found: yet the wise man will know a man either by his words or manners.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#008">Proverbs 20:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 20:8</a></font></span></p>A king that sitteth on the throne of judgment (d) scattereth away all evil with his eyes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Where righteous judgment is executed, there sin ceases, and vice dare not appear.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#010">Proverbs 20:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 20:10</a></font></span></p>Differing weights, [and] differing measures, both of (e) them [are] alike abomination to the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#011">Pro 16:11</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#016">Proverbs 20:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 20:16</a></font></span></p>Take his (f) garment that is surety [for] a stranger: and take a pledge of him for a strange woman. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Teach him wit, that he cast not himself rashly into danger.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#025">Proverbs 20:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 20:25</a></font></span></p>[It is] a snare to the man [who] (g) devoureth [that which is] holy, and after vows to make enquiry. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is, to apply or take for his own use, that which was appointed to God's and then ask how he may be exempted from the fault.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#026">Proverbs 20:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 20:26</a></font></span></p>A wise king scattereth the wicked, and bringeth the (h) wheel over them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Which was a kind of punishment then used.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#027">Proverbs 20:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 20:27</a></font></span></p>The (i) spirit of man [is] the lamp of the LORD, searching all the inward parts of the belly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) The word of God gives life to man and causes us to see and try the secret of our dark hearts, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb004.htm#012">Heb 4:12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#030">Proverbs 20:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 20:30</a></font></span></p>(k) The blueness of a wound cleanseth away evil: so [do] stripes the inward parts of the belly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Sharp punishment that pierces even the inward parts is profitable for the wicked to bring them to amendment.
