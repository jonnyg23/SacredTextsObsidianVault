 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 38</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#004">Exodus 38:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">exo 38:4</a></font></span></p>And he made for the altar a brasen grate of network under the compass thereof beneath unto the (a) midst of it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) So that the gridiron or grate was half as high as the altar and stood within it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#008">Exodus 38:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">exo 38:8</a></font></span></p>And he made the laver [of] brass, and the foot of it [of] brass, of the (b) lookingglasses of [the women] assembling, which assembled [at] the door of the tabernacle of the congregation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) R. Kimbi says that the women brought their looking glasses, which were of brass or fine metal, and offered them freely for the use of the tabernacle: which was a bright thing and of great majesty.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#021">Exodus 38:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">exo 38:21</a></font></span></p>This is the sum of the tabernacle, [even] of the tabernacle of testimony, as it was counted, according to the commandment of Moses, [for] the service of the (c) Levites, by the hand of Ithamar, son to Aaron the priest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That the Levites might be in charge of it, and minister in the same, as did Eleazar and Ithamar, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num003.htm#004">Num 3:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#023">Exodus 38:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">exo 38:23</a></font></span></p>And with him [was] Aholiab, son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan, an engraver, and a (d) cunning workman, and an embroiderer in blue, and in purple, and in scarlet, and fine linen. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Or, a graver, or carpenter, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo036.htm#004">Exo 36:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#029">Exodus 38:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">exo 38:29</a></font></span></p>And the brass of the offering [was] seventy (e) talents, and two thousand and four hundred shekels. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Read the weight of a talent, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#039">Exo 25:39</a>).
