 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">sol 6:1</a></font></span></p>Where is thy beloved gone, (k) O thou fairest among women? where is thy beloved turned aside? that we may seek him with thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Hearing of the excellency of Christ, the faithful desire to know how to find him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#002">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">sol 6:2</a></font></span></p>My beloved is gone down into his (a) garden, to the beds of spices, to feed in the gardens, and to gather lilies. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, is conversant here in earth among men.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#004">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">sol 6:4</a></font></span></p>Thou [art] beautiful, O my love, as (b) Tirzah, comely as Jerusalem, terrible as [an army] with banners. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Which was a fair and strong city, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1014.htm#017">Kg1 14:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#005">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sol 6:5</a></font></span></p>(c) Turn away thy eyes from me, for they have overcome me: thy hair [is] as a flock of goats that appear from Gilead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) This declares the exceeding love of Christ toward his Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#008">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">sol 6:8</a></font></span></p>There are (d) sixty queens, and eighty concubines, and virgins without number. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Meaning that the gifts are infinite which Christ gives to his Church: or that his faithful are many in number.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#010">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">sol 6:10</a></font></span></p>(e) Who [is] she [that] looketh forth as the morning, fair as the moon, clear as the sun, [and] terrible as [an army] with banners? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) He shows that the beginning of the Church was small, but that it grew up to a great multitude.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#011">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">sol 6:11</a></font></span></p>I went down into the (f) garden of nuts to see the fruits of the valley, [and] to see whether the vine flourished, [and] the pomegranates budded. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He went down into the synagogue to see what fruits came from the law, and the prophets.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#012">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">sol 6:12</a></font></span></p>(g) Before I was aware, my soul made me (h) [like] the chariots of Amminadib. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) I found nothing but rebellion. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) Or, Set me on the chariots of my willing people.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol006.htm#013">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 6:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">sol 6:13</a></font></span></p>Return, return, O (i) Shulamite; return, return, that we may look upon thee. What will ye see in the Shulamite? As it were the company of two armies. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) O ye people of Jerusalem, for Jerusalem was called Shalem which signifies peace.
