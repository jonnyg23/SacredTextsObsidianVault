 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 13</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001">Psalms 13:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 13:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician, A Psalm of David." How long wilt thou forget me, O LORD? (a) for ever? how long wilt thou hide thy face from me? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He declares that his afflictions lasted a long time, and that his faith did not waver.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#002">Psalms 13:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 13:2</a></font></span></p>How long shall I take (b) counsel in my soul, [having] sorrow in my heart daily? how long shall mine enemy be exalted over me? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Changing my purposes as the sick man does his place.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#004">Psalms 13:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 13:4</a></font></span></p>Lest mine enemy say, I have (c) prevailed against him; [and] those that trouble me rejoice when I am moved. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Which might turn to God's dishonour: if he did not defend his.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#005">Psalms 13:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 13:5</a></font></span></p>But I have trusted in thy (d) mercy; my heart shall rejoice in thy salvation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) The mercy of God is the cause of our salvation.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#006">Psalms 13:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 13:6</a></font></span></p>I will sing unto the LORD, because he hath (e) dealt bountifully with me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Both by the benefits past and by others to come.
