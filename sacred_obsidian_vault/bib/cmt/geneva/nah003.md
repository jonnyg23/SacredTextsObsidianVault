 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#001">Nahum Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#001">Nahum 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">nah 3:1</a></font></span></p>Woe to the bloody city! it [is] all full of lies [and] robbery; (a) the prey departeth not; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) It never ceases to spoil and rob.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#002">Nahum 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">nah 3:2</a></font></span></p>The noise of a whip, (b) and the noise of the rattling of the wheels, and of the pransing horses, and of the jumping chariots. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He shows how the Chaldeans will hasten, and how courageous their horses will be in beating the ground when they come against the Assyrians.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#004">Nahum 3:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">nah 3:4</a></font></span></p>Because of the multitude of the whoredoms of the wellfavoured (c) harlot, the mistress of witchcrafts, that selleth nations through her whoredoms, and families through her witchcrafts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He compares Nineveh to a harlot, who by her beauty and subtilty entices young men, and brings them to destruction.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#008">Nahum 3:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">nah 3:8</a></font></span></p>Art thou better than populous (d) No, that was situate among the rivers, [that had] the waters round about it, whose rampart [was] the sea, [and] her wall [was] from the sea? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Meaning Alexandria, which had a compact of peace with so many nations, and yet was now destroyed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#015">Nahum 3:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">nah 3:15</a></font></span></p>There shall the fire devour thee; the sword shall cut thee off, it shall eat thee up like the (e) cankerworm: make thyself many as the cankerworm, make thyself many as the locusts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Signifying that God's judgments would suddenly destroy the Assyrians, as these vermin do with rain or change of weather.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#018">Nahum 3:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">nah 3:18</a></font></span></p>Thy (f) shepherds slumber, O king of Assyria: thy nobles shall dwell [in the dust]: thy people is scattered upon the mountains, and no man gathereth [them]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Your princes and counsellors.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#019">Nahum 3:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">nah 3:19</a></font></span></p>[There is] no healing of thy bruise; thy wound is grievous: all that hear the bruit of thee shall clap the hands over thee: for upon (g) whom hath not thy wickedness passed continually? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Meaning that the Assyrians had done hurt to all people.
