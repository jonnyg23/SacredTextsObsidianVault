 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 13</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#002">Numbers 13:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">num 13:2</a></font></span></p>(b) Send thou men, that they may search the land of Canaan, which I give to the children of Israel: of every tribe of their fathers shall ye send a man, every one a ruler among them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) After the people had required it of Moses, as it is in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu001.htm#022">Deu 1:22</a>) then the Lord told Moses to do it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#016">Numbers 13:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">num 13:16</a></font></span></p>These [are] the names of the (c) men whom Moses sent to spy out the land. And Moses called Oshea the son of Nun Jehoshua. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Which in number were twelve according to the twelve tribes.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#019">Numbers 13:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">num 13:19</a></font></span></p>And what the land [is] that they dwell in, whether it [be] (d) good or bad; and what cities [they be] that they dwell in, whether in tents, or in strong holds; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Plentiful or barren.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#021">Numbers 13:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">num 13:21</a></font></span></p>So they went up, and searched the land from the wilderness of (e) Zin unto Rehob, as men come to Hamath. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Which was in the wilderness of Paran.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#022">Numbers 13:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">num 13:22</a></font></span></p>And they ascended by the south, and came unto Hebron; where Ahiman, Sheshai, and Talmai, the children of (f) Anak, [were]. (Now (g) Hebron was built seven years before Zoan in Egypt.) 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Which were a type of giant. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(g) Declaring the antiquity of it: also Abraham, Sarah, Isaac and Jacob were buried there.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#026">Numbers 13:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">num 13:26</a></font></span></p>And they went and came to Moses, and to Aaron, and to all the congregation of the children of Israel, unto the wilderness of (h) Paran, to Kadesh; and brought back word unto them, and unto all the congregation, and shewed them the fruit of the land. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Called also Kadesh-barnea.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#027">Numbers 13:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">num 13:27</a></font></span></p>And they told (i) him, and said, We came unto the land whither thou sentest us, and surely it floweth with milk and honey; and this [is] the fruit of it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) That is, Moses.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#028">Numbers 13:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">num 13:28</a></font></span></p>Nevertheless the people [be] strong that dwell in the land, and the cities [are] walled, [and] very great: and moreover we saw the (k) children of Anak there. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Ahiman, Sheshai, and Talmai, whom Caleb slew afterward, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos011.htm#021">Jos 11:21-22</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#032">Numbers 13:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">num 13:32</a></font></span></p>And they brought up an evil report of the land which they had searched unto the children of Israel, saying, The land, through which we have gone to search it, [is] a land that (l) eateth up the inhabitants thereof; and all the people that we saw in it [are] men of a great stature. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) The giants were so cruel, that they spoiled and killed one another and those that came to them.
