 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#001">Judges Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#002">Judges 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">jdg 5:2</a></font></span></p>Praise ye the LORD for the avenging of Israel, when the (a) people willingly offered themselves. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, the two tribes of Zebulun and Naphtali.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#006">Judges 5:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jdg 5:6</a></font></span></p>In the days of Shamgar the son of Anath, in the days of Jael, the highways were (b) unoccupied, and the travellers walked through byways. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For fear of the enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#007">Judges 5:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">jdg 5:7</a></font></span></p>[The inhabitants of] the villages ceased, they ceased in Israel, until that I Deborah arose, that I arose a (c) mother in Israel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Miraculously stirred up by God to pity them and deliver them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#008">Judges 5:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">jdg 5:8</a></font></span></p>They chose new gods; then [was] war in the gates: was there a (d) shield or spear seen among forty thousand in Israel? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) They had no heart to resist their enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#010">Judges 5:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">jdg 5:10</a></font></span></p>Speak, ye that ride on (e) white asses, ye that sit (f) in judgment, and walk by the way. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) You governors. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) Or by Middin, as in danger of your enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#012">Judges 5:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">jdg 5:12</a></font></span></p>Awake, awake, Deborah: awake, awake, utter a song: arise, Barak, and lead (h) thy captivity captive, thou son of Abinoam. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) That is, they who kept your people in captivity.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#014">Judges 5:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">jdg 5:14</a></font></span></p>Out of Ephraim (i) [was there] a root of them against Amalek; after thee, Benjamin, among thy people; out of Machir came down governors, and out of Zebulun they that handle the pen of the writer. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Joshua first fought against Amalek, and Saul destroyed him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#015">Judges 5:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">jdg 5:15</a></font></span></p>And the princes of Issachar [were] with Deborah; even (l) Issachar, and also Barak: he was sent on foot into the valley. For the divisions of Reuben [there were] great (m) thoughts of heart. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Even the whole tribe. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(m) They marvelled that they did not cross the Jordan to help them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#017">Judges 5:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">jdg 5:17</a></font></span></p>(n) Gilead abode beyond Jordan: and why did Dan remain in ships? Asher continued on the sea shore, and abode in his (o) breaches. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) She reproves all those who did not come to help their brethren in their time of need. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) Either by hearing of the sea, or by mining,  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#019">Judges 5:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">jdg 5:19</a></font></span></p>The kings came [and] fought, then fought the kings of Canaan in Taanach by the waters of Megiddo; they took no gain of (p) money. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) They won nothing, but lost all.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#021">Judges 5:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">jdg 5:21</a></font></span></p>The river of Kishon (q) swept them away, that ancient river, the river Kishon. O my soul, thou hast trodden down strength. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) As a broom does to the filth of the house.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#023">Judges 5:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">jdg 5:23</a></font></span></p>Curse ye (r) Meroz, said the angel of the LORD, curse ye bitterly the inhabitants thereof; because they came not to the help of the LORD, to the help of the LORD against the mighty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) It was a city near Tabor, where they fought.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#025">Judges 5:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">jdg 5:25</a></font></span></p>He asked water, [and] she gave [him] milk; she brought forth (s) butter in a lordly dish. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) Some read churned milk in a great cup.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#029">Judges 5:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">jdg 5:29</a></font></span></p>Her wise ladies answered her, yea, (t) she returned answer to herself, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) That is, she comforted herself.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#030">Judges 5:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">jdg 5:30</a></font></span></p>Have they not sped? have they [not] divided the prey; to every man a damsel [or] two; to Sisera a prey of divers colours, a prey of divers colours of needlework, of divers colours of needlework on both sides, [meet] (u) for the necks of [them that take] the spoil? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) Because he was chief of the army.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#031">Judges 5:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">jdg 5:31</a></font></span></p>So let all thine enemies perish, O LORD: but [let] them that love him [be] as the (x) sun when he goeth forth in his might. And the land had rest forty years. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(x) Shall grow daily more and more in God's favour.
