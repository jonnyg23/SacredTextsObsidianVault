 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 51</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#001">Isaiah 51:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 51:1</a></font></span></p>Hearken to me, (a) ye that follow after righteousness, ye that seek the LORD: look to the (b) rock [from which] ye are hewn, and to the hole of the pit [from which] ye were dug. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He comforts the Church, that they would not be discouraged for their small number. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) That is, to Abraham, of whom you were begotten, and to Sarah of whom we were born.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#003">Isaiah 51:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 51:3</a></font></span></p>For the LORD shall comfort Zion: he will comfort all her waste places; and he will make her wilderness (c) like Eden, and her desert like the garden of the LORD; joy and gladness shall be found in it, thanksgiving, and the voice of melody. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) As plentiful as paradise, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen002.htm#008">Gen 2:8,9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#004">Isaiah 51:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">isa 51:4</a></font></span></p>Hearken to me, my people; and give ear to me, O my nation: for a (d) law shall proceed from me, and I will make my judgment to rest for a light of the people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) I will rule and govern my Church by my word and doctrine.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#005">Isaiah 51:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 51:5</a></font></span></p>My (e) righteousness [is] near; my salvation is gone forth, and my (f) arms shall judge the people; the isles shall wait upon me, and on my arm shall they trust. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The time that I will accomplish my promise. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) My power and strength.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#006">Isaiah 51:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 51:6</a></font></span></p>Lift up your eyes to the heavens, and look upon the earth beneath: for the (g) heavens shall vanish away like smoke, and the earth shall become old like a garment, and its inhabitants shall die in like manner: but my salvation shall be for ever, and my righteousness shall not be abolished. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Forewarns them of the horrible changes and mutations of all things, and how he will preserve his church in the midst of all these dangers.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#009">Isaiah 51:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 51:9</a></font></span></p>Awake, awake, put on thy strength, O arm of the LORD; awake, as in the ancient days, (h) in the generations of old. [Art] thou not that which hath cut (i) Rahab, [and] wounded the (k) dragon? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) He puts them in remembrance of his great benefit for their deliverance out of Egypt, that by it they might learn to trust in him constantly. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) Meaning, Egypt, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#004">Psa 87:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(k) That is, Pharaoh, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#003">Eze 29:3</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#011">Isaiah 51:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">isa 51:11</a></font></span></p>Therefore the redeemed of the LORD shall (l) return, and come with singing to Zion; and everlasting joy [shall be] upon their head: they shall obtain gladness and joy; sorrow and mourning shall flee away. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) From Babylon.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#014">Isaiah 51:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">isa 51:14</a></font></span></p>The captive exile (m) hasteneth that he may be loosed, and that he should not die in the pit, nor that his bread should fail. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) He comforts them by the short time of their banishment: for in seventy years they were restored and the greatest empire of the world destroyed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#016">Isaiah 51:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">isa 51:16</a></font></span></p>And I have put my words in thy (n) mouth, and have covered thee in the shadow of my hand, that I may plant the (o) heavens, and lay the foundations of the earth, and say to Zion, Thou [art] my people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) Meaning, of Isaiah and of all true ministers who are defended by his protection. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) That all things may be restored in heaven and earth, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#010">Eph 1:10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#017">Isaiah 51:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">isa 51:17</a></font></span></p>Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem, which hast drank at the hand of the LORD the (p) cup of his fury; thou hast drank the dregs of the cup of trembling, [and] wrung [them] out. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) You have been justly punished and sufficiently as (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#002">Isa 40:2</a>) and this punishment in the elect is by measure, and according as God gives grace to hear it: but in the reprobate it is the just vengeance of God to drive them to an insensibleness and madness, as (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#015">Jer 25:15-16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#019">Isaiah 51:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">isa 51:19</a></font></span></p>These two (q) [things] have come to thee; who shall be sorry for thee? desolation, and destruction, and famine, and the sword: by whom shall I comfort thee? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) Of which the one is outward as of the things that come to the body, as war, and famine and the other is inward, and belongs to the mind: that is, to be without comfort: therefore he says "How will you be comforted?"  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#021">Isaiah 51:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">isa 51:21</a></font></span></p>Therefore hear now this, thou afflicted, and drunk, but (r) not with wine: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) But with trouble and fear.
