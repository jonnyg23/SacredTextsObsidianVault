 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#002">Exodus 21:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">exo 21:2</a></font></span></p>If thou buy an Hebrew servant, six years he shall serve: and in the seventh he shall go out free for (a) nothing. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Paying no money for his freedom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#003">Exodus 21:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">exo 21:3</a></font></span></p>If he (b) came in by himself, he shall go out by himself: if he were married, then his wife shall go out with him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Not having wife nor children.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#004">Exodus 21:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">exo 21:4</a></font></span></p>If his master have given him a wife, and she have born him sons or daughters; the wife and her children shall be her (c) master's, and he shall go out by himself. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Till her time of servitude was expired which might be the seventh year or the fiftieth.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#006">Exodus 21:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">exo 21:6</a></font></span></p>Then his master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the (d) door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for (e) ever. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Where the judges sat. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(e) That is, to the year of Jubile, which was every fiftieth year.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#007">Exodus 21:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">exo 21:7</a></font></span></p>And if a man (f) sell his daughter to be a maidservant, she shall not go out as the menservants do. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Forced either by poverty, or else with the intent that the master should marry her.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#008">Exodus 21:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">exo 21:8</a></font></span></p>If she please not her master, who hath betrothed her to himself, then shall (g) he let her be redeemed: to sell her unto a strange nation he shall have no power, seeing he hath dealt deceitfully with her. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) By giving another money to buy her from him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#009">Exodus 21:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">exo 21:9</a></font></span></p>And if he have betrothed her unto his son, he shall deal with her (h) after the manner of daughters. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) That is, he shall give his dowry.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#010">Exodus 21:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">exo 21:10</a></font></span></p>If he take (i) him another [wife]; her food, her raiment, and her duty of marriage, shall he not diminish. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) For his son.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#011">Exodus 21:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 21:11</a></font></span></p>And if he do not these (k) three unto her, then shall she go out free without money. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Neither marry her himself, nor give another money to buy her, nor bestow her on his son.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#013">Exodus 21:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">exo 21:13</a></font></span></p>And if a man lie not in wait, but (l) God deliver [him] into his hand; then I will appoint thee a place whither he shall flee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Though a man be killed unawares, yet it is God's providence that it should so be.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#014">Exodus 21:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">exo 21:14</a></font></span></p>But if a man come presumptuously upon his neighbour, to slay him with guile; thou shalt take him from mine (m) altar, that he may die. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) The holiness of the place should not defend the murderer.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#018">Exodus 21:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">exo 21:18</a></font></span></p>And if men strive together, and one smite another with a (n) stone, or with [his] fist, and he die not, but keepeth [his] bed: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) Either far away from him or near.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#019">Exodus 21:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">exo 21:19</a></font></span></p>If he rise again, and walk abroad upon his staff, then shall he that smote [him] be (o) quit: only he shall pay [for] the loss of his time, and shall cause [him] to be thoroughly healed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) By the civil justice.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#021">Exodus 21:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">exo 21:21</a></font></span></p>Notwithstanding, if he continue a day or two, he shall not (p) be punished: for he [is] his money. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) By the civil magistrate, but before God he is a murderer.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#022">Exodus 21:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">exo 21:22</a></font></span></p>If men strive, and hurt a woman with child, so that her fruit depart [from her], and yet no (q) mischief follow: he shall be surely punished, according as the woman's husband will lay upon him; and he shall pay as the judges [determine]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) Or, "death": of the mother or child in the event she miscarries. Also the death on the unborn infant.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#024">Exodus 21:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">exo 21:24</a></font></span></p>(r) Eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) The execution of this law only belonged to the magistrate, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#038">Mat 5:38</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#027">Exodus 21:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">exo 21:27</a></font></span></p>And if he smite (s) out his manservant's tooth, or his maidservant's tooth; he shall let him go free for his tooth's sake. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) So God revenges cruelty in the even the least things.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#028">Exodus 21:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">exo 21:28</a></font></span></p>If an ox gore a man or a woman, that they die: then the ox shall be surely (t) stoned, and his flesh shall not be eaten; but the owner of the ox [shall be] quit. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) If the beast be punished, much more shall the murderer.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#030">Exodus 21:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">exo 21:30</a></font></span></p>If there be laid on him a (u) sum of money, then he shall give for the ransom of his life whatsoever is laid upon him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) By the next of the kindred of him that is so slain.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#032">Exodus 21:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">exo 21:32</a></font></span></p>If the ox shall push a manservant or a maidservant; he shall give unto their master thirty (x) shekels of silver, and the ox shall be stoned. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(x) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#015">Gen 23:15</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#034">Exodus 21:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">exo 21:34</a></font></span></p>The owner of the pit shall (y) make [it] good, [and] give money unto the owner of them; and the dead [beast] shall be his. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(y) This law forbids not only to hurt, but to beware lest any be hurt.
