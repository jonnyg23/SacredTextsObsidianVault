 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 131</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psalms 131:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 131:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song of degrees of David." LORD, (a) my heart is not haughty, nor mine eyes lofty: neither do I exercise myself in great (b) matters, or in things too high for me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He sets forth his great humility as an example to all rulers and governors. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Which pass the measure and limits of his calling.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#002">Psalms 131:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 131:2</a></font></span></p>Surely I have behaved and quieted myself, as a child that is weaned of his mother: my soul [is] even as a (c) weaned child. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He was void of ambition and wicked desires.
