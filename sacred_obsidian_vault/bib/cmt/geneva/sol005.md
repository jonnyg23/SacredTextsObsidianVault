 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">sol 5:1</a></font></span></p>I have come into my (a) garden, my sister, [my] spouse: I have gathered my myrrh with my spice; I have eaten my honeycomb with my honey; I have drank my wine with my milk: eat, O friends; drink, yea, drink abundantly, O beloved. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) The garden signifies the kingdom of Christ, where he prepares the banquet for his elect.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#002">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">sol 5:2</a></font></span></p>(b) I sleep, but my heart waketh: [it is] the voice of my beloved that knocketh, [saying], Open to me, my sister, my love, my dove, my undefiled: for my head is filled with dew, [and] my locks with the drops of the (c) night. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The spouse says that she is troubled with the cares of worldly things, which is meant by sleeping. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) Declaring the long patience of the Lord toward sinners.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#003">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">sol 5:3</a></font></span></p>I have put off my (d) coat; how shall I put it on? I have washed my feet; how shall I defile them? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) The spouse confesses her nakedness, and that of herself she has nothing, or seeing that she is once made clean she promises not to defile herself again.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#005">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sol 5:5</a></font></span></p>I rose up to open to my beloved; and my hands flowed [with] myrrh, and my (e) fingers [with] sweet smelling myrrh, upon the handles of the lock. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The spouse who should be anointed by Christ will not find him if she thinks to anoint him with her good works.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#007">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">sol 5:7</a></font></span></p>The (f) watchmen that went about the city found me, they smote me, they wounded me; the keepers of the walls took away my veil from me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) These are the false teachers who wound the conscience with their traditions.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#008">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">sol 5:8</a></font></span></p>I charge you, (g) O daughters of Jerusalem, if ye find my beloved, that ye tell him, that I [am] sick with love. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) She asks of them who are godly (as the law and salvation should come out of Zion and Jerusalem) that they would direct her to Christ.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#009">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">sol 5:9</a></font></span></p>(h) What [is] thy beloved more than [another] beloved, O thou fairest among women? what [is] thy beloved more than [another] beloved, that thou dost so charge us? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Thus say they of Jerusalem.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol005.htm#011">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 5:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">sol 5:11</a></font></span></p>His (i) head [is as] the most fine gold, his locks [are] bushy, [and] black as a raven. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) She describes Christ to be of perfect beauty and comeliness.
