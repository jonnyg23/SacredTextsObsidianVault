 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#001">Job Chapter 41</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#001">Job 41:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">job 41:1</a></font></span></p>Canst thou draw out (l) leviathan with an hook? or his tongue with a cord [which] thou lettest down? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Meaning the whale.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#002">Job 41:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">job 41:2</a></font></span></p>Canst thou put an hook into his nose? or (m) bore his jaw through with a thorn? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Because he fears lest you should take him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#004">Job 41:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">job 41:4</a></font></span></p>Will he make a covenant with thee? wilt thou take (n) him for a servant for ever? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) To do your business, and be at your command?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#008">Job 41:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">job 41:8</a></font></span></p>Lay thine hand upon him, remember (o) the battle, do no more. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) If you once consider the danger, you will not meddle with him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#009">Job 41:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">job 41:9</a></font></span></p>Behold, (p) the hope of him is in vain: shall not [one] be cast down even at the sight of him? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) That is, that trusts to take him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#010">Job 41:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">job 41:10</a></font></span></p>None [is so] fierce that dare stir him up: who then is able to (a) stand before me? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) If no one dare stand against a whale, which is but a creature, who is able to compare with God the creator?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#011">Job 41:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">job 41:11</a></font></span></p>Who hath prevented me, that I should (b) repay [him? whatsoever is] under the whole heaven is mine. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Who has taught me to accomplish my work?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#012">Job 41:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">job 41:12</a></font></span></p>I will not conceal (c) his parts, nor his power, nor his comely proportion. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) The parts and members of the whale?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#013">Job 41:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">job 41:13</a></font></span></p>Who can discover the face (d) of his garment? [or] who can come [to him] with his double (e) bridle? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, who dare pull off his skin? 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(e) Who dare put a bridle in his mouth?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#014">Job 41:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">job 41:14</a></font></span></p>Who can (f) open the doors of his face? his teeth [are] terrible round about. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Who dare look in his mouth?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#018">Job 41:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">job 41:18</a></font></span></p>By his neesings (g) a light doth shine, and his eyes [are] like the eyelids of the morning. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is, casts out flames of fire.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#022">Job 41:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">job 41:22</a></font></span></p>In his neck remaineth strength, and (h) sorrow is turned into joy before him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Nothing is painful or hard for him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#030">Job 41:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">job 41:30</a></font></span></p>Sharp stones (i) [are] under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things upon the mire. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) His skin is so hard that he lies with a great ease on the stones as in the mud.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#031">Job 41:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">job 41:31</a></font></span></p>He maketh the deep to (k) boil like a pot: he maketh the sea like a pot of ointment. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Either he makes the sea to seem like it is boiling by his wallowing, or else he spouts water in such abundance as it would seem that the sea boiled.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#032">Job 41:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">job 41:32</a></font></span></p>He maketh a path to (l) shine after him; [one] would think the deep [to be] hoary. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) That is, a white froth and shining stream before him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#034">Job 41:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">job 41:34</a></font></span></p>He beholdeth (m) all high [things]: he [is] a king over all the children of pride. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) He despises all other beasts and monsters, and is the proudest of all others.
