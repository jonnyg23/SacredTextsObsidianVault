 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#001">3 Kings (1 Kings) Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#002">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">kg1 4:2</a></font></span></p>And these [were] the (a) princes which he had; (b) Azariah the son of Zadok the priest, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, his chief officers. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) He was the son of Achimais and Zadok's nephew. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#004">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">kg1 4:4</a></font></span></p>And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada [was] over the host: and Zadok and (c) Abiathar [were] the priests: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Not Abiathar whom Solomon had put from his office, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#027">Kg1 2:27</a>) but another of that name. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#013">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">kg1 4:13</a></font></span></p>The son of Geber, in Ramothgilead; to him [pertained] the towns of (d) Jair the son of Manasseh, which [are] in Gilead; to him [also pertained] the region of Argob, which [is] in Bashan, threescore great cities with walls and brasen bars: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Which bore Jair's name, because he took them from the Canaanites, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num032.htm#041">Num 32:41</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#017">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">kg1 4:17</a></font></span></p>Jehoshaphat the son of Paruah, in (e) Issachar: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Solomon did not observe the division that Joshua made, but divided it as might best serve his purposes. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#020">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">kg1 4:20</a></font></span></p>Judah and Israel [were] many, as the sand which [is] by the sea in multitude, (f) eating and drinking, and making merry. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) They lived in all peace and security. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#021">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">kg1 4:21</a></font></span></p>And Solomon reigned over all kingdoms from the (g) river unto the land of the Philistines, and unto the border of Egypt: they brought presents, and served Solomon all the days of his life. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Which is the Euphrates. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#024">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">kg1 4:24</a></font></span></p>For he had dominion over all [the region] on this side the river, from Tiphsah even to Azzah, over all the (h) kings on this side the river: and he had peace on all sides round about him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) For they were all tributaries to him. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#025">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">kg1 4:25</a></font></span></p>And Judah and Israel dwelt safely, every man under his vine and under his fig tree, from (i) Dan even to Beersheba, all the days of Solomon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Throughout all Israel. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#029">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">kg1 4:29</a></font></span></p>And God gave Solomon wisdom and understanding exceeding much, and (k) largeness of heart, even as the sand that [is] on the sea shore. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Meaning, great understanding and able to comprehend all things. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#030">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">kg1 4:30</a></font></span></p>And Solomon's wisdom excelled the wisdom of all the children of the (l) east country, and all the wisdom of Egypt. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) That is, the philosophers and astronomers who were judged most wise. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#032">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">kg1 4:32</a></font></span></p>And he spake three thousand (m) proverbs: and his songs were a thousand and five. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Which for the most part are thought to have perished in the captivity of Babylon. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#033">3 Kings (1 Kings) 4:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">kg1 4:33</a></font></span></p>And he spake of trees, from the cedar tree that [is] in Lebanon even unto the (n) hyssop that springeth out of the wall: he spake also of beasts, and of fowl, and of creeping things, and of fishes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) From the highest to the lowest.
