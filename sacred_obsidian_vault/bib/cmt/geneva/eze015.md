 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze015.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze015.htm#002">Ezekiel 15:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">eze 15:2</a></font></span></p>Son of man, What is the vine tree more than any tree, [or than] a branch which is among the (a) trees of the forest? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Which brings forth no fruit, no more than the other trees of the forest do: meaning that if Jerusalem, which bore the name of his Church, did not bring forth fruit it would be utterly destroyed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze015.htm#007">Ezekiel 15:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">eze 15:7</a></font></span></p>And I will set my face against them; they shall go out from [one] (b) fire, and [another] fire shall devour them; and ye shall know that I [am] the LORD, when I set my face against them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Though they escape one danger, yet another will take them.
