 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#006">Proverbs 15:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 15:6</a></font></span></p>In the house of the righteous [is] much treasure: but in the revenues of the wicked is (a) trouble. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) For though they have much yet it is full of trouble and care.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#008">Proverbs 15:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 15:8</a></font></span></p>The (b) sacrifice of the wicked [is] an abomination to the LORD: but the prayer of the upright [is] his delight. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) That thing is abominable before God, which the wicked think to be most excellent, and by which they think most to be accepted.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#010">Proverbs 15:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 15:10</a></font></span></p>Correction [is] grievous to him that (c) forsaketh the way: [and] he that hateth reproof shall die. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He who swears from the word of God, cannot stand to be admonished.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#011">Proverbs 15:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 15:11</a></font></span></p>(d) Hell and destruction [are] before the LORD: how much more then the hearts of the children of men? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) There is nothing so deep or secret that can be hid from the eyes of God, much less man's thoughts.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#019">Proverbs 15:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 15:19</a></font></span></p>The way of the slothful [man is] as an hedge of (e) thorns: but the way of the righteous [is] made plain. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) That is, he always finds some hinderance or stay, and dares not go forward.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#022">Proverbs 15:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 15:22</a></font></span></p>Without counsel purposes are disappointed: but (f) in the multitude of counsellors they are established. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#014">Pro 11:14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#023">Proverbs 15:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 15:23</a></font></span></p>A man hath joy by the answer of his mouth: and a word [spoken] (g) in due season, how good [it is]! 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) If we will that our talk be comfortable, we must wait for time and season.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#026">Proverbs 15:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 15:26</a></font></span></p>The thoughts of the wicked [are] an abomination to the LORD: but [the words] of the pure [are] (h) pleasant words. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) That is, wholesome and profitable to the hearers.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#031">Proverbs 15:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 15:31</a></font></span></p>The ear that heareth the (i) reproof of life abideth among the wise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) That suffers himself to be admonished by God's word, which brings life: and so amends.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#033">Proverbs 15:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 15:33</a></font></span></p>The fear of the LORD [is] the instruction of wisdom; and before honour [is] (k) humility. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Meaning, that God exalts no one but them that are truly humbled.
