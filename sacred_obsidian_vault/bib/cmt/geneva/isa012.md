 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#001">Isaiah 12:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 12:1</a></font></span></p>And in that day thou (a) shalt say, O LORD, I will praise thee: though thou wast angry with me, thy anger is turned away, and thou didst comfort me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He shows how the Church will praise God, when they are delivered from their captivity.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#002">Isaiah 12:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 12:2</a></font></span></p>Behold, God [is] my (b) salvation; I will trust, and not be afraid: for the LORD [is] my strength and [my] song; he also is become my salvation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Our salvation stands only in God, who gives us an assured confidence, constancy and opportunity to praise him for the same.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#003">Isaiah 12:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 12:3</a></font></span></p>Therefore with joy shall ye (c) draw water out of the wells of salvation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) The graces of God will be so abundant that you may receive them in as great plenty as waters out of a fountain that is full.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#006">Isaiah 12:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 12:6</a></font></span></p>Cry aloud and shout, (d) thou inhabitant of Zion: for great [is] the Holy One of Israel in the midst of thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) You who are of the Church.
