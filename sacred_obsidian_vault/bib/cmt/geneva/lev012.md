 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#002">Leviticus 12:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">lev 12:2</a></font></span></p>Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, If a woman have conceived seed, and born a man child: then she shall be unclean (a) seven days; according to the days of the separation for her infirmity shall she be unclean. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) So that her husband for that time could not have relations with her.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#004">Leviticus 12:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">lev 12:4</a></font></span></p>And she shall then continue in the blood of her purifying three (b) and thirty days; she shall touch no (c) hallowed thing, nor come into the (d) sanctuary, until the days of her purifying be fulfilled. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Besides the first seven days. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) As sacrifice, or such like. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, into the court gate till after forty days.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#005">Leviticus 12:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">lev 12:5</a></font></span></p>But if she bear a maid child, then she shall be unclean two (e) weeks, as in her separation: and she shall continue in the blood of her purifying threescore and six days. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Twice as long as if she gave birth to a boy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#006">Leviticus 12:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">lev 12:6</a></font></span></p>And when the days of her purifying are fulfilled, for a son, or for a daughter, she shall bring a lamb of the first year for a burnt offering, and a young pigeon, or a turtledove, for a sin offering, unto the door of the (f) tabernacle of the congregation, unto the priest: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Where the burnt offerings were wont to be offered.
