 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#001">3 Kings (1 Kings) Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#001">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">kg1 7:1</a></font></span></p>But Solomon was building his own house thirteen years, and he (a) finished all his house. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) After he had built the temple. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#002">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">kg1 7:2</a></font></span></p>He built also the house (b) of the forest of Lebanon; the length thereof [was] an hundred cubits, and the breadth thereof fifty cubits, and the height thereof thirty cubits, upon four rows of cedar pillars, with cedar beams upon the pillars. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Because of the beauty of the place, and great abundance of cedar trees that went into the building of it, it was compared to mount Lebanon. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#004">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">kg1 7:4</a></font></span></p>And [there were] windows [in] three rows, and light [was] (c) against light [in] three ranks. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) There were as many and like proportion on the one side as the other, and at every end even three in a row one above another. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#006">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">kg1 7:6</a></font></span></p>And he made a porch of pillars; the length thereof [was] fifty cubits, and the breadth thereof thirty cubits: and the porch [was] before (d) them: and the [other] pillars and the thick beam [were] before them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Before the pillars of the house. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#007">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">kg1 7:7</a></font></span></p>Then he made a porch (e) for the throne where he might judge, [even] the porch of judgment: and [it was] covered with cedar from one side of the floor to the other. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) For his house which was at Jerusalem. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#009">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">kg1 7:9</a></font></span></p>All these [were of] costly stones, according to the measures of hewed stones, sawed with saws, within and without, even from the foundation unto (f) the coping, and [so] on the outside toward the great court. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Which were rests and stays for the beams to lie on. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#011">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">kg1 7:11</a></font></span></p>And (g) above [were] costly stones, after the measures of hewed stones, and cedars. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) From the foundation upward. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#012">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">kg1 7:12</a></font></span></p>And the great court round about [was] with three rows of hewed stones, and a row of cedar beams, (h) both for the inner court of the house of the LORD, and for the porch of the house. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) As the Lord's house was built so was this, only the great court of Solomon's house was uncovered. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#014">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">kg1 7:14</a></font></span></p>He [was] a widow's son of the tribe of Naphtali, and his father [was] a man of Tyre, a worker in brass: and (i) he was filled with wisdom, and understanding, and cunning to work all works in brass. And he came to king Solomon, and wrought all his work. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Thus when God will have his glory set forth, he raises up men, and gives them excellent gifts for the accomplishment of the same, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo031.htm#002">Exo 31:2-3</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#019">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">kg1 7:19</a></font></span></p>And the chapiters that [were] upon the top of the pillars [were] of (k) lily work in the porch, four cubits. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) As was seen commonly wrought in costly porches. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#021">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">kg1 7:21</a></font></span></p>And he set up the pillars in the (l) porch of the temple: and he set up the right pillar, and called the name thereof (m) Jachin: and he set up the left pillar, and called the name thereof (n) Boaz. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Which was in the inner court between the temple and the oracle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(m) That is, he will stablish, that is, his promise toward this house. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(n) That is, in strength: meaning the power of it will continue. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#023">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">kg1 7:23</a></font></span></p>And he made a molten (o) sea, ten cubits from the one brim to the other: [it was] round all about, and his height [was] five cubits: and a line of thirty cubits did compass it round about. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) So called for the size of the vessel. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#026">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">kg1 7:26</a></font></span></p>And it [was] an hand breadth thick, and the brim thereof was wrought like the brim of a cup, with flowers of lilies: it contained two thousand (p) baths. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) Bath and ephah seem to be one measure, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze045.htm#011">Eze 45:11</a>) a bath contains about 5 gallons. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#031">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">kg1 7:31</a></font></span></p>And the (q) mouth of it within the chapiter and above [was] a cubit: but the mouth thereof [was] round [after] the work of the base, a cubit and an half: and also upon the mouth of it [were] gravings with their borders, foursquare, not round. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) The mouth of the great base or frame entered into the chapiter, or pillar that bore up the cauldron. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#035">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">kg1 7:35</a></font></span></p>And in the top of the base [was there] a round (v) compass of half a cubit high: and on the top of the base the ledges thereof and the borders thereof [were] of the same. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(v) Which was called the pillar, chapiter or small base, in which the cauldron stood. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#038">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">kg1 7:38</a></font></span></p>Then made he (s) ten lavers of brass: one laver contained forty baths: [and] every laver was four cubits: [and] upon every one of the ten bases one laver. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) To keep waters for the use of the sacrifices. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#039">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">kg1 7:39</a></font></span></p>And he put five bases on the right side of the house, and five on the left side of the house: and he set the sea on the right side of the (t) house eastward over against the south. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) That is, of the temple or sanctuary. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#045">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:45</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_045">kg1 7:45</a></font></span></p>And the pots, and the shovels, and the basons: and all these vessels, which (u) Hiram made to king Solomon for the house of the LORD, [were of] bright brass. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) By this name also Hiram the king of Tyrus was called. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#048">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:48</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_048">kg1 7:48</a></font></span></p>And Solomon made all the vessels that [pertained] unto the house of the LORD: the (x) altar of gold, and the table of gold, whereupon the shewbread [was], 
 <p>
 </p><p>(x) This was done according to the form that the Lord prescribed to Moses in Exodus. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#050">3 Kings (1 Kings) 7:50</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_050">kg1 7:50</a></font></span></p>And the bowls, (y) and the snuffers, and the basons, and the spoons, and the censers [of] pure gold; and the hinges [of] gold, [both] for the doors of the inner house, the most holy [place, and] for the doors of the house, [to wit], of the temple. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(y) Some take this for some musical instrument.
