 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#001">Leviticus 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lev 2:1</a></font></span></p>And when any will offer a (a) meat offering unto the LORD, his offering shall be [of] fine flour; and he shall pour oil upon it, and put frankincense thereon: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Because the burnt offering could not be without the meat offering.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#002">Leviticus 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">lev 2:2</a></font></span></p>And he shall bring it to Aaron's sons the priests: and (b) he shall take thereout his handful of the flour thereof, and of the oil thereof, with all the frankincense thereof; and the priest shall burn the (c) memorial of it upon the altar, [to be] an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the LORD: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The priest. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) To signify that God remembers him that offers.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#003">Leviticus 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">lev 2:3</a></font></span></p>And the remnant of the meat offering [shall be] Aaron's and his sons': [it is] a thing (d) most holy of the offerings of the LORD made by fire. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Therefore no one could eat of it but the priest.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#005">Leviticus 2:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">lev 2:5</a></font></span></p>And if thy oblation [be] a (e) meat offering [baken] in a pan, it shall be [of] fine flour unleavened, mingled with oil. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Which is a gift offered to God to pacify him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#012">Leviticus 2:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">lev 2:12</a></font></span></p>As for the oblation of the firstfruits, ye shall offer (f) them unto the LORD: but they shall not be burnt (g) on the altar for a sweet savour. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) That is, fruits which were sweet as honey, ye may offer. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(g) But reserved for the priests.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#013">Leviticus 2:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">lev 2:13</a></font></span></p>And every oblation of thy meat offering shalt thou season with salt; neither shalt thou suffer the salt of the (h) covenant of thy God to be lacking from thy meat offering: with all thine offerings thou shalt offer salt. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Which they were bound (as by covenant) to use all sacrifices, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num018.htm#019">Num 18:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2013.htm#005">Ch2 13:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#024">Eze 43:24</a>) or it means a sure and pure covenant.
