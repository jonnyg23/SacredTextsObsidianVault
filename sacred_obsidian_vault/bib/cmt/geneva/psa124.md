 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 124</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psalms 124:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 124:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song of degrees of David." If [it had not been] the LORD who was (a) on our side, now may Israel say; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He shows that God was ready to help at need and that there was no other way to be saved, but by this means alone.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#003">Psalms 124:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 124:3</a></font></span></p>Then they had swallowed us up (b) quick, when their wrath was kindled against us: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) So unable were we to resist.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#004">Psalms 124:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 124:4</a></font></span></p>Then the (c) waters had overwhelmed us, the stream had gone over our soul: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He uses proper similitudes to express the great danger that the Church was in, and out of which God miraculously delivered them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#007">Psalms 124:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 124:7</a></font></span></p>Our soul is escaped as a bird out of the (d) snare of the fowlers: the snare is broken, and we are escaped. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) For the wicked not only furiously rage against the faithful, but craftily imagined to destroy them.
