 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#001">Job Chapter 35</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#002">Job 35:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">job 35:2</a></font></span></p>Thinkest thou this to be right, [that] thou saidst, My (a) righteousness [is] more than God's? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Job never spoke these words: but because he maintained his innocency, it seemed as though he would say, that God tormented him without just cause.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#004">Job 35:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">job 35:4</a></font></span></p>I will answer thee, and thy (b) companions with thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Such as are in the same error.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#005">Job 35:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">job 35:5</a></font></span></p>Look unto the heavens, and see; and behold the (c) clouds [which] are higher than thou. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) If you cannot control the clouds, will you presume to instruct God?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#006">Job 35:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">job 35:6</a></font></span></p>If thou sinnest, what doest thou (d) against him? or [if] thy transgressions be multiplied, what doest thou unto him? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Neither does your sin hurt God, nor your justice profit him: for he will be glorified without you.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#009">Job 35:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">job 35:9</a></font></span></p>By reason of the multitude of oppressions they make [the oppressed] (e) to cry: they cry out by reason of the arm of the mighty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The wicked may hurt man and cause him to cry, who if he sought God who lends comfort would be delivered.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#012">Job 35:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">job 35:12</a></font></span></p>There they cry, (f) but none giveth answer, because of the pride of evil men. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Because they pray not in faith, as feeling God's mercies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#014">Job 35:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">job 35:14</a></font></span></p>Although thou sayest thou shalt not see him, (g) [yet] judgment [is] before him; therefore trust thou in him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) God is just, however you judge him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job035.htm#016">Job 35:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">job 35:16</a></font></span></p>Therefore doth Job (h) open his mouth in vain; he multiplieth words without knowledge. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) For if he punished you as you deserved, you would not be able to open your mouth.
