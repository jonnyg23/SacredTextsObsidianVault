 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#002">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">sol 1:2</a></font></span></p>Let (a) him kiss me with the kisses of his mouth: for thy love [is] better than wine. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) This is spoken in the person of the Church, or of the faithful soul inflamed with the desire of Christ, whom she loves.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#003">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">sol 1:3</a></font></span></p>Because of the (b) savour of thy good ointments thy name [is as] ointment poured forth, therefore do the (c) virgins love thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The feeling of your great benefits. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) They that are pure in heart and conversation.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#004">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">sol 1:4</a></font></span></p>(d) Draw me, we will run after thee: the king hath brought me into his (e) chambers: we will be glad and rejoice in thee, we will remember thy love more than wine: the upright love thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) The faithful confess that they cannot come to Christ, unless they are drawn. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(e) Meaning the secret joy that is not known to the world.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#005">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sol 1:5</a></font></span></p>I [am] (f) black, but comely, O ye daughters of Jerusalem, as the tents of (g) Kedar, as the (h) curtains of Solomon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) The Church confesses her spots and sin, but has confidence in the favour of Christ. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(g) Kedar was Ishmael's son, of whom came the Arabians that dwelt in tents. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) Which within were all set with precious stones and jewels.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#006">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">sol 1:6</a></font></span></p>Look not upon me, because I [am] (i) black, because the (k) sun hath looked upon me: (l) my mother's children were angry with me; they made me the keeper of the vineyards; [but] my own vineyard have I not (m) kept. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Consider not the Church by the outward appearance. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(k) The corruption of nature through sin and afflictions. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(l) My own brethren who should have most favoured me. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(m) She confesses her own negligence.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#007">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">sol 1:7</a></font></span></p>Tell me, (n) O thou whom my soul loveth, where thou feedest, where thou makest [thy flock] to rest at noon: for why should I be as one that turneth aside by the flocks of (o) thy companions? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) The spouse feeling her fault flees to her husband only for comfort. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) Whom you have called to the dignity of pastors, and they set forth their own dreams instead of your doctrine.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#008">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">sol 1:8</a></font></span></p>(p) If thou knowest not, O thou fairest among women, go forth by the footsteps of the flock, and feed thy kids beside the shepherds' tents. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) Christ speaks to his Church, bidding them that are ignorant to go to the pastors to learn.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#009">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">sol 1:9</a></font></span></p>I have compared thee, O my love, to a company of horses in Pharaoh's (q) chariots. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) For your spiritual beauty and excellency there was no worldly treasure to be compared to you.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#012">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">sol 1:12</a></font></span></p>(r) While the king [sitteth] at his table, my spikenard sendeth forth its fragrance. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) The Church rejoices that she is admitted to the company of Christ.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#013">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">sol 1:13</a></font></span></p>A bundle of myrrh [is] my wellbeloved to me; he shall lie all night between my (s) breasts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) He will be most dear to me.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#016">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 1:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">sol 1:16</a></font></span></p>Behold, thou [art] fair, my beloved, yea, pleasant: also our (u) bed [is] green. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) That is, the heart of the faithful, in which Christ dwells by his Spirit.
