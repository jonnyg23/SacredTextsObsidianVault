 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 22</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#001">Proverbs 22:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 22:1</a></font></span></p>A [good] name [is] rather to be chosen than great riches, [and] (a) loving favour rather than silver and gold. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Which comes by well doing.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#002">Proverbs 22:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 22:2</a></font></span></p>The rich and poor (b) meet together: the LORD [is] the maker of them all. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Live together, and have need the one of the other.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#003">Proverbs 22:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 22:3</a></font></span></p>A prudent [man] (c) foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself: but the simple pass on, and are punished. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, the punishment, which is prepared for the wicked and flees to God for help.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#006">Proverbs 22:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 22:6</a></font></span></p>Train up a child (d) in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Bring him up virtuously and he will continue so.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#008">Proverbs 22:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 22:8</a></font></span></p>He that soweth iniquity shall reap vanity: and the (e) rod of his anger shall fail. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) His authority by which he oppressed others, will be taken from him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009">Proverbs 22:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 22:9</a></font></span></p>He that hath a bountiful (f) eye shall be blessed; for he giveth of his bread to the poor. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He that is merciful and liberal.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#011">Proverbs 22:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 22:11</a></font></span></p>He that loveth pureness of heart, [for] the grace of his lips the (g) king [shall be] his friend. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) He shows that princes should use their familiarity, whose conscience is good, and their talk wise and godly.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#012">Proverbs 22:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 22:12</a></font></span></p>The eyes of the LORD preserve (h) knowledge, and he overthroweth the words of the transgressor. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Favour them that love knowledge.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#013">Proverbs 22:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 22:13</a></font></span></p>The slothful [man] saith, (i) [There is] a lion outside, I shall be slain in the streets. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) He derides them that invent vain excuses, because they would not do their duty.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#014">Proverbs 22:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 22:14</a></font></span></p>The mouth of strange women [is] a deep pit: he that is abhorred by the LORD (k) shall fall in it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) So God punishes one sin by another, when he suffers the wicked to fall into the acquaintance of a harlot.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#015">Proverbs 22:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 22:15</a></font></span></p>Foolishness [is] bound (l) in the heart of a child; [but] the rod of correction shall drive it far from him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) He is naturally given to it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#019">Proverbs 22:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 22:19</a></font></span></p>That thy trust may be in (m) the LORD, I have made known to thee this day, even to thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) He shows what the end of wisdom is: that is, to direct us to the Lord.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#020">Proverbs 22:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 22:20</a></font></span></p>Have not I written to thee (n) excellent things in counsels and knowledge, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) That is, various times.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#024">Proverbs 22:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 22:24</a></font></span></p>Make (o) no friendship with an angry man; and with a furious man thou shalt not go: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) Have nothing to do with him that is not able to rule his affections: for he would hurt you by his evil conversation.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#026">Proverbs 22:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 22:26</a></font></span></p>Be not thou [one] of them that (p) strike hands, [or] of them that are sureties for debts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) Who rashly put themselves in danger for others, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#002">Pro 6:2</a>).
