 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 60</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#001">Isaiah 60:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 60:1</a></font></span></p>Arise, shine; for thy (a) light is come, and the glory of the LORD is risen upon thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) The time of your prosperity and happiness: while speaking of Babylon, he commanded her to go down, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa047.htm#001">Isa 47:1</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#002">Isaiah 60:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 60:2</a></font></span></p>For, behold, the darkness shall cover the (b) earth, and gross darkness the people: but the LORD shall arise upon thee, and his glory shall be seen upon thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Signifying, that all men are in darkness till God gives them the light of his Spirit, and that this light shines to no one, but to those that are in his Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#003">Isaiah 60:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 60:3</a></font></span></p>And the Gentiles shall come to (c) thy light, and kings to the brightness of thy rising. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning, that Judea would be as the morning star, and that the Gentiles would receive light from her.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#004">Isaiah 60:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">isa 60:4</a></font></span></p>Lift up thy eyes around, and see: all (d) they gather themselves together, they come to thee: thy sons shall come from far, and thy daughters shall be nursed at [thy] side. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) An infinite number from all countries as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#018">Isa 49:18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#005">Isaiah 60:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 60:5</a></font></span></p>Then thou shalt see, and flow together, and thy heart shall fear, (e) and be enlarged; because the abundance of the sea shall be converted to thee, the forces of the Gentiles shall come to thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) For joy, as the heart is drawn in for sorrow.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#006">Isaiah 60:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 60:6</a></font></span></p>The (f) multitude of camels shall cover thee, the dromedaries of Midian and Ephah; all they from Sheba shall come: they shall bring gold and incense; and they shall show forth the praises of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Meaning, that everyone will honour the Lord with that with which he is able: Signifying that it is no true serving of God unless we offer ourselves to serve his glory, and all that we have.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#007">Isaiah 60:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 60:7</a></font></span></p>All the flocks of (g) Kedar shall be gathered together to thee, the rams of Nebaioth shall minister to thee: they shall come up with acceptance on my (h) altar, and I will glorify the house of my glory. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is the Arabians, that have great abundance of cattle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) Because the altar was a figure of Christ, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#010">Heb 13:10</a>), he shows that nothing can be acceptable to him, which is not offered to him by this altar, who was both the offering and the altar itself.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#008">Isaiah 60:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 60:8</a></font></span></p>Who [are] these (i) [that] fly as a cloud, and as doves to their windows? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Showing what great number will come to the Church, and with what great diligence and zeal.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#009">Isaiah 60:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 60:9</a></font></span></p>Surely the isles shall wait for me, and the ships (k) of Tarshish first, to bring thy sons from far, their silver and their gold with them, to the name of the LORD thy God, and to the Holy One of Israel, because he hath glorified thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) The Gentiles that are now enemies, will become friends and set forth the Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#010">Isaiah 60:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">isa 60:10</a></font></span></p>And the sons of foreigners shall build up thy walls, and their (l) kings shall minister to thee: for in my wrath I smote thee, but in my favour have I had mercy on thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Meaning Cyrus and his successors, but chiefly this is accomplished in them that serve Christ, being converted by his gospel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#012">Isaiah 60:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">isa 60:12</a></font></span></p>For the nation and (m) kingdom that will not serve thee shall perish; yea, [those] nations shall be utterly wasted. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) He shows that God has given all power and authority here in earth for the use of his Church, and that they who will not serve and profit the same will be destroyed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#013">Isaiah 60:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">isa 60:13</a></font></span></p>The (n) glory of Lebanon shall come to thee, the fir tree, the pine tree, and the box together, to beautify the place of my sanctuary; and I will make the place of my (o) feet glorious. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) There is nothing so excellent which will not serve the needs of the Church. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) Signifying that God's majesty is not included in the temple, which is but the place for his feet, that we may learn to rise to the heavens.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#014">Isaiah 60:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">isa 60:14</a></font></span></p>The sons also of them that afflicted thee shall come bending to thee; and all they that despised thee shall bow themselves (p) down at the soles of thy feet; and they shall call thee, The city of the LORD, The Zion of the Holy One of Israel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) To worship their head Christ, by obeying his doctrine.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#016">Isaiah 60:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">isa 60:16</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt also suck the milk of the Gentiles, and shalt suck the (q) breast of kings: and thou shalt know that I the LORD [am] thy Saviour and thy Redeemer, the mighty One of Jacob. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) Both high and low will be ready to help and comfort you.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#017">Isaiah 60:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">isa 60:17</a></font></span></p>For brass I will bring gold, and for iron I will bring silver, and for wood brass, and for stones iron: I will also make thy officers (r) peace, and thy exactors righteousness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) Your governors will love you, and seek your wealth and prosperity.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#018">Isaiah 60:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">isa 60:18</a></font></span></p>Violence shall no more be heard in thy land, wasting nor destruction within thy borders; but thou shalt (s) call thy walls Salvation, and thy gates Praise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) Meaning not an earthly happiness, but spiritual, which is fulfilled in Christ's kingdom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#019">Isaiah 60:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">isa 60:19</a></font></span></p>The sun shall be no more thy light by day; neither for brightness shall the (t) moon give light to thee: but the LORD shall be to thee an everlasting light, and thy God thy glory. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) Signifying that all worldly means will cease, and that Christ will be all in all, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev021.htm#023">Rev 21:23</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#005">Rev 22:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#021">Isaiah 60:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">isa 60:21</a></font></span></p>Thy people also [shall be] all righteous: they shall inherit the land for ever, the (u) branch of my planting, the work of my hands, that I may be glorified. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) The children of the Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#022">Isaiah 60:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">isa 60:22</a></font></span></p>A little one shall become a (x) thousand, and a small one a strong nation: I the LORD will hasten it in its time. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(x) Meaning, that the Church would be miraculously multiplied.
