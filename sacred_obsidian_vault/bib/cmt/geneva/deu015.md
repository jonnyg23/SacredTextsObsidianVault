 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#001">Deuteronomy Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#002">Deuteronomy 15:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">deu 15:2</a></font></span></p>And this [is] the manner of the release: Every (a) creditor that lendeth [ought] unto his neighbour shall release [it]; he shall not exact [it] of his neighbour, or of his brother; because it is called the LORD'S release. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He shall only release his debtors, who are not able to pay for that year.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#004">Deuteronomy 15:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">deu 15:4</a></font></span></p>(b) Save when there shall be no poor among you; for the LORD shall greatly bless thee in the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee [for] an inheritance to possess it: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For if your debtor is rich, he may be forced to pay.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#011">Deuteronomy 15:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">deu 15:11</a></font></span></p>(c) For the poor shall never cease out of the land: therefore I command thee, saying, Thou shalt (d) open thine hand wide unto thy brother, to thy poor, and to thy needy, in thy land. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) To try your charity, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#011">Mat 26:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) You shall be liberal.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#014">Deuteronomy 15:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">deu 15:14</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt (e) furnish him liberally out of thy flock, and out of thy floor, and out of thy winepress: [of that] wherewith the LORD thy God hath blessed thee thou shalt give unto him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) In token that you acknowledge the benefit which God has given you by his labours.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#017">Deuteronomy 15:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">deu 15:17</a></font></span></p>Then thou shalt take an aul, and thrust [it] through his ear unto the door, and he shall be thy servant (f) for ever. And also unto thy maidservant thou shalt do likewise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) To the year of Jubile, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#040">Lev 25:40</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#018">Deuteronomy 15:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">deu 15:18</a></font></span></p>It shall not seem hard unto thee, when thou sendest him away free from thee; for he hath been worth a double (g) hired servant [to thee], in serving thee six years: and the LORD thy God shall bless thee in all that thou doest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) For the hired servant served but three years, and he six.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#019">Deuteronomy 15:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">deu 15:19</a></font></span></p>All the firstling males that come of thy herd and of thy flock thou shalt sanctify unto the LORD thy God: (h) thou shalt do no work with the firstling of thy bullock, nor shear the firstling of thy sheep. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) For they are the Lords.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#022">Deuteronomy 15:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">deu 15:22</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt eat it within thy gates: the unclean and the clean [person shall eat it] alike, (i) as the roebuck, and as the hart. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) You will eat them, as well as the roe buck and other wild beasts.
