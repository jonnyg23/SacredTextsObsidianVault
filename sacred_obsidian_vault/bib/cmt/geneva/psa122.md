 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 122</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#001">Psalms 122:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 122:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song of degrees of David." I (a) was glad when they said unto me, Let us go into the house of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He rejoices that God had appointed a place where the ark would still remain.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#002">Psalms 122:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 122:2</a></font></span></p>Our (b) feet shall stand within thy gates, O Jerusalem. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Which were wont to wander to and fro, as the ark moved.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#003">Psalms 122:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 122:3</a></font></span></p>Jerusalem is builded as a city that is (c) compact together: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) By the artificial joining and beauty of the houses, he means the peace and love that was between the citizens.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#004">Psalms 122:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 122:4</a></font></span></p>Whither (d) the tribes go up, the tribes of the LORD, unto the testimony of Israel, to give thanks unto the name of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) All the tribes according to God's covenant will come and pray there.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#005">Psalms 122:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 122:5</a></font></span></p>For there are set thrones of judgment, the thrones of the house of (e) David. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) In whose house God placed the throne of justice, and made it a figure of Christ's kingdom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#007">Psalms 122:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 122:7</a></font></span></p>Peace be within thy (f) walls, [and] prosperity within thy palaces. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) The favour of God prosper you both within and without.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#008">Psalms 122:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 122:8</a></font></span></p>For my (g) brethren and companions' sakes, I will now say, Peace [be] within thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Not only for my own sake but for all the faithful.
