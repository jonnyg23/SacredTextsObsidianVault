 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#001">Psalms 9:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 9:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician upon Muthlabben, A Psalm of David." I will praise [thee], O LORD, with my (a) whole heart; I will shew forth all thy marvellous works. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) God is not praised unless the whole glory is given to him alone.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#004">Psalms 9:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 9:4</a></font></span></p>For (b) thou hast maintained my right and my cause; thou satest in the throne judging right. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) However the enemy seems for a time to prevail yet God preserves the just.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#006">Psalms 9:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 9:6</a></font></span></p>(c) O thou enemy, destructions are come to a perpetual end: and thou hast destroyed cities; their memorial is perished with them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) A derision of the enemy that minds nothing but destruction: but the Lord will deliver his, and bring him into judgment.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#009">Psalms 9:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 9:9</a></font></span></p>The LORD also will be a refuge for the (d) oppressed, a refuge in times of trouble. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Our miseries are meant to cause us to feel God's present care over us.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#012">Psalms 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 9:12</a></font></span></p>(e) When he maketh inquisition for blood, he remembereth them: he forgetteth not the cry of the humble. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Though God does not suddenly avenge the wrong done to his, yet he does not permit the wicked to go unpunished.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#014">Psalms 9:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 9:14</a></font></span></p>That I may shew forth all thy praise in the (f) gates of the daughter of Zion: I will rejoice in thy salvation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) In the open assembly of the Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#015">Psalms 9:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 9:15</a></font></span></p>The heathen are (g) sunk down in the pit [that] they made: in the net which they hid is their own foot taken. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) For God overthrows the wicked in their enterprises.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#016">Psalms 9:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 9:16</a></font></span></p>(h) The LORD is known [by] the judgment [which] he executeth: the wicked is snared in the work of his own hands. Higgaion. Selah. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) The mercy of God toward his saints must be declared and the fall of the wicked must always be considered.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#018">Psalms 9:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 9:18</a></font></span></p>For the needy shall not alway be forgotten: the expectation (i) of the poor shall [not] perish for ever. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) God does not promise to help us before we have felt the cross.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#020">Psalms 9:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 9:20</a></font></span></p>Put them in fear, O LORD: [that] the nations may know themselves [to be but] (k) men. Selah. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Which they cannot learn without the fear of your judgment.
