 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 19</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#002">Leviticus 19:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">lev 19:2</a></font></span></p>Speak unto all the congregation of the children of Israel, and say unto them, Ye shall be (a) holy: for I the LORD your God [am] holy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, void of all pollution, idolatry and superstition both of soul and body.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#005">Leviticus 19:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">lev 19:5</a></font></span></p>And if ye offer a sacrifice of peace offerings unto the LORD, ye shall offer it (b) at your own will. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Of your own accord.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#007">Leviticus 19:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">lev 19:7</a></font></span></p>And if it be eaten at all on the third day, it [is] abominable; it shall not be (c) accepted. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) To wit, of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#011">Leviticus 19:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">lev 19:11</a></font></span></p>Ye shall not steal, neither (d) deal falsely, neither lie one to another. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) In that which is committed to your credit.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#016">Leviticus 19:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">lev 19:16</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt not (e) go up and down [as] a talebearer among thy people: neither shalt thou (f) stand against the blood of thy neighbour: I [am] the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) As a slanderer, backbiter, or quarrel picker. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) By consenting to his death, or conspiring with the wicked.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#019">Leviticus 19:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">lev 19:19</a></font></span></p>Ye shall keep my statutes. Thou shalt not let thy cattle gender with a (g) diverse kind: thou shalt not sow thy field with mingled seed: neither shall a garment mingled of linen and woollen come upon thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) As a horse to leap an ass, or a mule a mare.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#023">Leviticus 19:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">lev 19:23</a></font></span></p>And when ye shall come into the land, and shall have planted all manner of trees for food, then ye (h) shall count the fruit thereof as uncircumcised: three years shall it be as uncircumcised unto you: it shall not be eaten of. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) It shall be unclean as that thing, which is not circumcised.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#026">Leviticus 19:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">lev 19:26</a></font></span></p>Ye shall not eat [any thing] with the blood: neither shall ye use enchantment, nor (i) observe times. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) To measure lucky or unlucky days.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#027">Leviticus 19:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">lev 19:27</a></font></span></p>Ye shall not (k) round the corners of your heads, neither shalt thou mar the corners of thy beard. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) As did the Gentiles in sign of mourning.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#028">Leviticus 19:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">lev 19:28</a></font></span></p>Ye shall not make any cuttings in your flesh for the dead, nor print any (l) marks upon you: I [am] the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) By whipping your bodies or burning marks in them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#029">Leviticus 19:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">lev 19:29</a></font></span></p>Do not prostitute thy daughter, to cause her to be a (m) whore; lest the land fall to whoredom, and the land become full of wickedness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) As did the Cyprians, and Locrenses.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#032">Leviticus 19:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">lev 19:32</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt (n) rise up before the hoary head, and honour the face of the old man, and fear thy God: I [am] the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) In token of reverence.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#035">Leviticus 19:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">lev 19:35</a></font></span></p>Ye shall do no unrighteousness in judgment, in (o) meteyard, in weight, or in measure. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) As in measuring the ground.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#036">Leviticus 19:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">lev 19:36</a></font></span></p>Just balances, just weights, a just (p) ephah, and a just hin, shall ye have: I [am] the LORD your God, which brought you out of the land of Egypt. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) By these two measures he means all other. Of Ephah, read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#036">Exo 16:36</a>) and of Hin, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#040">Exo 29:40</a>).
