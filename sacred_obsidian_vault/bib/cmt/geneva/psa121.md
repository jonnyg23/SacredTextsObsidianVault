 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 121</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#002">Psalms 121:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 121:2</a></font></span></p>My help [cometh] from the LORD, which made (a) heaven and earth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He accuses man's ingratitude, which cannot depend on God's power.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#003">Psalms 121:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 121:3</a></font></span></p>He will not suffer thy foot to be moved: he that keepeth thee will not (b) slumber. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He shows that God's providence not only watches over his Church in general, but also over every member of it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#006">Psalms 121:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 121:6</a></font></span></p>The sun shall not (c) smite thee by day, nor the moon by night. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Neither heat nor cold, nor any inconvenience will be able to destroy God's Church, even though for a time they may molest it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#008">Psalms 121:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 121:8</a></font></span></p>The LORD shall preserve thy (d) going out and thy coming in from this time forth, and even for evermore. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Whatever you attempt will have good success.
