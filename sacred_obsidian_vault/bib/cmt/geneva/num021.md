 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#001">Numbers 21:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">num 21:1</a></font></span></p>And [when] king Arad the Canaanite, which dwelt in the south, heard tell that Israel came by the (a) way of the spies; then he fought against Israel, and took [some] of them prisoners. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) By that way which their spies, that searched the dangers found to he most safe.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#004">Numbers 21:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">num 21:4</a></font></span></p>And they journeyed from mount Hor by the way of the Red sea, to (b) compass the land of Edom: and the soul of the people was much discouraged because of the way. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For they were forbidden to destroy it, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu002.htm#005">Deu 2:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#005">Numbers 21:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">num 21:5</a></font></span></p>And the people spake against God, and against Moses, Wherefore have ye brought us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? for [there is] no bread, neither [is there any] water; and our soul loatheth this light (c) bread. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning manna, which they thought did not nourish.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#006">Numbers 21:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">num 21:6</a></font></span></p>And the LORD sent (d) fiery serpents among the people, and they bit the people; and much people of Israel died. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) For they that were bitten by them were so inflamed by the poison of them, that they died.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#014">Numbers 21:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">num 21:14</a></font></span></p>Wherefore it is said in the (e) book of the wars of the LORD, What he did in the Red sea, and in the brooks of Arnon, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Which seems to be the book of the Judges, or as some think, a book which is lost.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#017">Numbers 21:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">num 21:17</a></font></span></p>Then Israel sang this song, Spring up, O well; (f) sing ye unto it: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) You that receive the convenience of it, give praise for it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#018">Numbers 21:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">num 21:18</a></font></span></p>The princes digged the well, the nobles of the people digged it, by [the direction of] the (g) lawgiver, with their staves. And from the wilderness [they went] to Mattanah: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Only Moses and Aaron, the heads of the people, struck the rock with the rod or staff, which gave water as a well that was deep digged.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#024">Numbers 21:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">num 21:24</a></font></span></p>And Israel smote him with the edge of the sword, and possessed his land from Arnon unto (h) Jabbok, even unto the children of Ammon: for the border of the children of Ammon [was] (i) strong. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) The river. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) For the people were tall and strong like giants; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu002.htm#020">Deu 2:20</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#026">Numbers 21:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">num 21:26</a></font></span></p>For (k) Heshbon [was] the city of Sihon the king of the Amorites, who had fought against the former king of Moab, and taken all his land out of his hand, even unto Arnon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) For if it had been the Moabites, the Israelites might not have possessed it, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu002.htm#009">Deu 2:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#028">Numbers 21:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">num 21:28</a></font></span></p>For there is a (l) fire gone out of Heshbon, a flame from the city of Sihon: it hath consumed Ar of Moab, [and] the lords of the high places of Arnon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Meaning, wane.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#029">Numbers 21:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">num 21:29</a></font></span></p>Woe to thee, Moab! thou art undone, O people of (m) Chemosh: he hath given his sons that escaped, and his daughters, into captivity unto Sihon king of the Amorites. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Chemosh was the idol of the Moabites, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1011.htm#033">Kg1 11:33</a>) who was not able to defend his worshippers, who took the idol for their father.
