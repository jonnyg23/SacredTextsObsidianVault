 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 23</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#001">Proverbs 23:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 23:1</a></font></span></p>When thou sittest to eat with a ruler, (a) consider diligently what [is] before thee: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Eat with sobriety.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#002">Proverbs 23:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 23:2</a></font></span></p>(b) And put a knife to thy throat, if thou [art] a man given to appetite. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Bridle your appetite, as if by force and violence.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#003">Proverbs 23:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 23:3</a></font></span></p>Be not desirous of his dainties: (c) for they [are] deceitful food. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) For often the rich when they bid their inferiors to their tables, it is not for the love they bear them, but for their own secret purposes.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#004">Proverbs 23:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 23:4</a></font></span></p>Labour not to be rich: cease from thy own (d) wisdom. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Bestow not the gifts that God has given you, to get worldly riches.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#006">Proverbs 23:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 23:6</a></font></span></p>Eat thou not the bread of [him that hath] an (e) evil eye, neither desire thou his delicacies: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) That is, covetous, as contrary a good eye is taken for liberal as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009">Pro 22:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#008">Proverbs 23:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 23:8</a></font></span></p>The (f) morsel [which] thou hast eaten shalt thou vomit up, and lose thy sweet words. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He will not cease till he has done you some harm, and his flattering words will come to no use.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#014">Proverbs 23:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 23:14</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt beat him with the rod, and shalt deliver his soul from (g) hell. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is, from destruction.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#018">Proverbs 23:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 23:18</a></font></span></p>For surely there is an end; (h) and thy expectation shall not be cut off. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) The prosperity of the wicked will not continue.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#019">Proverbs 23:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 23:19</a></font></span></p>Hear thou, my son, and be wise, and guide thy heart in the (i) way. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) In the observation of God's commandments.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#023">Proverbs 23:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 23:23</a></font></span></p>Buy (k) the truth, and sell [it] not; [also] wisdom, and instruction, and understanding. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Spare no cost for truths sake, neither depart from it for any gain.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#026">Proverbs 23:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 23:26</a></font></span></p>My son, give me (l) thy heart, and let thy eyes observe my ways. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Give yourself wholly to wisdom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#028">Proverbs 23:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 23:28</a></font></span></p>She also lieth in wait as [for] a prey, (m) and increaseth the transgressors among men. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) She seduces many and causes them to offend God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#030">Proverbs 23:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 23:30</a></font></span></p>They that tarry long at the wine; they that go (n) to seek mixed wine. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) Who by art make wine stronger and more pleasant.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#033">Proverbs 23:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 23:33</a></font></span></p>Thy (o) eyes shall behold strange women, and thy heart shall utter perverse things. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) That is, drunkenness will bring you to whoredom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#034">Proverbs 23:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">pro 23:34</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt be as he that lieth down in the midst of the (p) sea, or as he that lieth upon the top of a mast. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) In such great danger will you be.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#035">Proverbs 23:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">pro 23:35</a></font></span></p>They have stricken me, [shalt thou say, and] I was not sick; they have beaten me, [and] I felt [it] not: when shall I awake? I will (q) seek it yet again. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) Though drunkenness makes them more insensible then beasts, yet they can not refrain.
