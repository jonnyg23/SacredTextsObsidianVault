 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 23</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#003">Genesis 23:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">gen 23:3</a></font></span></p>And Abraham (a) stood up from before his dead, and spake unto the sons of Heth, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, when he had mourned: so the godly may mourn if they do not pass measure, and the natural affection is commendable.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#006">Genesis 23:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">gen 23:6</a></font></span></p>Hear us, my lord: thou [art] a (b) mighty prince among us: in the choice of our sepulchres bury thy dead; none of us shall withhold from thee his sepulchre, but that thou mayest bury thy dead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) That is godly or excellent: for so do the Hebrews speak of all things that are notable, because all excellency comes from God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#010">Genesis 23:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">gen 23:10</a></font></span></p>And Ephron dwelt among the children of Heth: and Ephron the Hittite answered Abraham in the audience of the children of Heth, [even] of all that (c) went in at the gate of his city, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning all the citizens and inhabitants.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#012">Genesis 23:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">gen 23:12</a></font></span></p>And Abraham (d) bowed down himself before the people of the land. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) To show that he had them in good estimation and reverence.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#015">Genesis 23:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">gen 23:15</a></font></span></p>My lord, hearken unto me: the land [is worth] four hundred (e) shekels of silver; what [is] that betwixt me and thee? bury therefore thy dead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The common shekel is about 20 pence, so then 400 shekels is equal to 33 pounds, 6 shillings and 8 pence at 5 shilling sterling to the ounce.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#020">Genesis 23:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">gen 23:20</a></font></span></p>And the field, and the cave that [is] therein, were made sure unto Abraham for a possession of a buryingplace (f) by the sons of Heth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) That is, all the people confirmed the sale.
