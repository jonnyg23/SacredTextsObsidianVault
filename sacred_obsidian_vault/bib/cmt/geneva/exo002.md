 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#001">Exodus 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 2:1</a></font></span></p>And there went a (a) man of the house of Levi, and took [to wife] a daughter of Levi. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) This Levite was called Amram, who married Jochebed in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo006.htm#020">Exo 6:20</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#003">Exodus 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">exo 2:3</a></font></span></p>And when she could not longer hide him, she took for him an ark of bulrushes, and daubed it with slime and with pitch, and (b) put the child therein; and she laid [it] in the flags by the river's brink. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Committing him to the providence of God, whom she could not keep from the rage of the tyrant.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#008">Exodus 2:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">exo 2:8</a></font></span></p>And Pharaoh's daughter said to her, Go. And the maid went and called the (c) child's mother. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Man's counsel cannot hinder that which God has determined shall come to pass.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#011">Exodus 2:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 2:11</a></font></span></p>And it came to pass in those days, when Moses was (d) grown, that he went out unto his brethren, and looked on their burdens: and he spied an Egyptian smiting an Hebrew, one of his brethren. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, was forty years old; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#023">Act 7:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#012">Exodus 2:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">exo 2:12</a></font></span></p>And he looked this way and that way, and when he saw that [there was] no man, he (e) slew the Egyptian, and hid him in the sand. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Being assured that God had appointed him to deliver the Israelites; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#025">Act 7:25</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#014">Exodus 2:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">exo 2:14</a></font></span></p>And he said, Who made thee a prince and a judge over us? intendest thou to kill me, as thou killedst the Egyptian? And Moses (f) feared, and said, Surely this thing is known. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Though by his fear he showed his weakness, yet faith covered it; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#027">Heb 11:27</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#020">Exodus 2:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">exo 2:20</a></font></span></p>And he said unto his daughters, And where [is] he? why [is] it [that] ye have left the man? (g) call him, that he may eat bread. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) In which he demonstrated a thankful mind, which would reward the good done to his.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#023">Exodus 2:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">exo 2:23</a></font></span></p>And it came to pass in process of time, that the king of Egypt died: and the children of Israel sighed by reason of the bondage, and they (h) cried, and their cry came up unto God by reason of the bondage. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) God humbles his by afflictions, that they should cry to him, and receive the fruit of his promise.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#025">Exodus 2:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">exo 2:25</a></font></span></p>And God looked upon the children of Israel, and God had (i) respect unto [them]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) He judges their causes or acknowledged them as his own.
