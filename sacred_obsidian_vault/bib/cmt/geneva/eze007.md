 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#003">Ezekiel 7:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 7:3</a></font></span></p>Now [is] the end [come] upon thee, and I will send my anger upon thee, and will judge thee according to thy ways, and will recompense upon thee all (a) thy abominations. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) I will punish you as you have deserved for your idolatry.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#006">Ezekiel 7:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 7:6</a></font></span></p>An end is come, the end is come: it (b) watcheth for thee; behold, it is come. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He shows that the judgments of God ever watch to destroy the sinners, who notwithstanding he delays till there is no more hope of repentance.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#007">Ezekiel 7:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">eze 7:7</a></font></span></p>The (c) morning is come upon thee, O thou that dwellest in the land: the time is come, the day of trouble [is] near, and not the joyful (d) shouting upon the mountains. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) The beginning of his punishments is already come. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) Which was a voice of joy and mirth.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#010">Ezekiel 7:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">eze 7:10</a></font></span></p>Behold the day, behold, it is come: the morning is gone forth; the (e) rod hath blossomed, (f) pride hath budded. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The scourge is ready. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) That is, the proud tyrant Nebuchadnezzar has gathered his force and is ready.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#011">Ezekiel 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">eze 7:11</a></font></span></p>(g) Violence hath risen into a rod of wickedness: none of them [shall remain], nor of their multitude, nor of any of theirs: neither [shall there be] wailing for them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) This cruel enemy will be a sharp scourge for their wickedness.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#012">Ezekiel 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 7:12</a></font></span></p>The time is come, the day draweth near: let not the buyer (i) rejoice, nor the seller (k) mourn: for wrath [is] upon all its multitude. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) For the present profit. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(k) For he will lose nothing.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#013">Ezekiel 7:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">eze 7:13</a></font></span></p>For the seller shall not (l) return to that which is sold, although they were yet alive: for the (m) vision [is] concerning its whole multitude, [which] shall not return; (n) neither shall any strengthen himself in the iniquity of his life. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) In the year of the Jubile, meaning that none would enjoy the privilege of the law, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#013">Lev 25:13</a>) for they would all be carried away captives. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(m) This vision signified that all would be carried away, and none would return for the Jubile. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(n) No man for all this endeavours himself or takes heart to repent for his evil life. Some read, for none will be strengthened in his iniquity of his life: meaning that they would gain nothing by flattering themselves in evil.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#014">Ezekiel 7:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 7:14</a></font></span></p>(o) They have blown the trumpet, even to make all ready; but none goeth to the battle: for my wrath [is] upon all its multitude. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) The Israelites made a brag, but their hearts failed them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#020">Ezekiel 7:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">eze 7:20</a></font></span></p>As for the beauty of his (p) ornament, he set it in majesty: but they made the images of their abominations [and] of their detestable things in it: therefore have I set it far from them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) Meaning, the sanctuary.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#021">Ezekiel 7:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">eze 7:21</a></font></span></p>And I will give it into the hands of the (q) strangers for a prey, and to the wicked of the earth for a spoil; and they shall pollute it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) That is, of the Babylonians.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#022">Ezekiel 7:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">eze 7:22</a></font></span></p>My face will I turn also from them, and they shall pollute my (r) secret [place]: for the robbers shall enter into it, and defile it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) Which signifies the most holy place, into which none might enter but the high priest.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#023">Ezekiel 7:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">eze 7:23</a></font></span></p>Make a (s) chain: for the land is full of (t) bloody crimes, and the city is full of violence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) Signifying that they would be bound and led away captives. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(t) That is, of sins that deserve death.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#024">Ezekiel 7:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">eze 7:24</a></font></span></p>Wherefore I will bring the worst of the nations, and they shall possess their houses: I will also make the pomp of the strong to cease; and their (u) holy places shall be defiled. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) Which was the temple that was divided into three parts.
