 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#001">2 Kings (2 Samuel) Chapter 22</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#001">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">sa2 22:1</a></font></span></p>And David spake unto the LORD the words of this (a) song in the day [that] the LORD had delivered him out of the hand of all his enemies, and out of the hand of Saul: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) In token of the wonderful benefits that he received from God. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#002">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">sa2 22:2</a></font></span></p>And he said, The LORD [is] my (b) rock, and my fortress, and my deliverer; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) By the diversity of these comfortable means, he shows how his faith was strengthened in all temptations. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#005">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sa2 22:5</a></font></span></p>When the (c) waves of death compassed me, the floods of ungodly men made me afraid; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) As David (who was the figure of Christ) was by God's power delivered from all dangers: so Christ and his Church will overcome most grievous dangers, tyranny and death. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#009">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">sa2 22:9</a></font></span></p>There went up a (d) smoke out of his nostrils, and (e) fire out of his mouth devoured: coals were kindled by it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, clouds, and vapours. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(e) Lightening and thundering. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#010">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">sa2 22:10</a></font></span></p>He (f) bowed the heavens also, and came down; and darkness [was] under his feet. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) So it seems when the air is dark. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#011">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">sa2 22:11</a></font></span></p>And he rode upon a (g) cherub, and did fly: and he was seen upon the wings of the wind. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) To fly in a moment through the world. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#013">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">sa2 22:13</a></font></span></p>Through the brightness before him were (h) coals of fire kindled. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) By this description of a tempest he declares the power of God against his enemies. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#016">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">sa2 22:16</a></font></span></p>And the (i) channels of the sea appeared, the foundations of the world were discovered, at the rebuking of the LORD, at the blast of the breath of his nostrils. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) He alludes to the miracle of the Red Sea. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#019">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">sa2 22:19</a></font></span></p>They (k) prevented me in the day of my calamity: but the LORD was my stay. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) I was so beset, that all means seemed to fail. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#021">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">sa2 22:21</a></font></span></p>The LORD rewarded me according to my (l) righteousness: according to the cleanness of my hands hath he recompensed me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Toward Saul and my enemies. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#022">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">sa2 22:22</a></font></span></p>For I have kept the ways of the LORD, and have not (m) wickedly departed from my God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) I attempted nothing without his commandment. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#027">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">sa2 22:27</a></font></span></p>With the pure thou wilt shew thyself pure; and with the (n) froward thou wilt shew thyself unsavoury. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) Their wickedness is cause that you seem to forget your usual mercy. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#031">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">sa2 22:31</a></font></span></p>[As for] God, his way [is] (o) perfect; the word of the LORD [is] tried: he [is] a buckler to all them that trust in him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) The manner that God uses to aid his own, never fails. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#034">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">sa2 22:34</a></font></span></p>He maketh my feet like (p) hinds' [feet]: and setteth me upon my high places. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) He uses extraordinary means to make me win most strongholds. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#040">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">sa2 22:40</a></font></span></p>For thou hast (q) girded me with strength to battle: them that rose up against me hast thou subdued under me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) He acknowledges that God was the author of his victories, who gave him strength. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#042">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:42</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_042">sa2 22:42</a></font></span></p>They looked, but [there was] none to save; [even] unto the (r) LORD, but he answered them not. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) The wicked in their time of need are forced to flee to God, but it is too late. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#044">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:44</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_044">sa2 22:44</a></font></span></p>Thou also hast delivered me from the strivings of my (s) people, thou hast kept me [to be] head of the heathen: a people [which] I knew not shall serve me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) Meaning, of the Jews, who conspired against me. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#045">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:45</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_045">sa2 22:45</a></font></span></p>Strangers (t) shall submit themselves unto me: as soon as they hear, they shall be obedient unto me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) Not willingly obeying me, but deceitfully. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#047">2 Kings (2 Samuel) 22:47</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_047">sa2 22:47</a></font></span></p>The LORD liveth; (u) and blessed [be] my rock; and exalted be the God of the rock of my salvation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(u) Let him show his power that he is the governor of all the world.
