 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#003">Proverbs 12:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 12:3</a></font></span></p>A man shall not be established by wickedness: but the (a) root of the righteous shall not be moved. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) They are so grounded in the favour of God, that their root will prosper continually.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#006">Proverbs 12:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 12:6</a></font></span></p>The words of the wicked [are] to lie in wait for blood: but the mouth of the upright shall (b) deliver them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) As their conscience is upright, so will they be able to speak for themselves against their accusers.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#009">Proverbs 12:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 12:9</a></font></span></p>[He that is] despised, (c) and hath a servant, [is] better than he that honoureth himself, and is destitute of bread. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) The poor man that is contemned and yet lives of his own travail.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#010">Proverbs 12:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 12:10</a></font></span></p>A righteous [man] (d) regardeth the life of his beast: but the tender mercies of the wicked [are] cruel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Is merciful, even to the very beast who does him service.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#012">Proverbs 12:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 12:12</a></font></span></p>The wicked desireth the (e) net of evil [men]: but the (f) root of the righteous yieldeth [fruit]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Continually imagines ways to harm others. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) Meaning, their heart within, which is upright, and does good to all.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#015">Proverbs 12:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 12:15</a></font></span></p>The way of a fool [is] (g) right in his own eyes: but he that hearkeneth to counsel [is] wise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) He stands in his own conceit, and condemns all others in respect to himself.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#016">Proverbs 12:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 12:16</a></font></span></p>A fool's wrath is presently known: but (h) a prudent [man] covereth shame. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Who bridles his affections.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#018">Proverbs 12:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 12:18</a></font></span></p>There is that speaketh like the piercings of (i) a sword: but the tongue of the wise [is] health. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Which seek nothing more then to provoke others to anger.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#025">Proverbs 12:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 12:25</a></font></span></p>Heaviness in the heart of man weigheth it down: but a (k) good word maketh it glad. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) That is, words of comfort, or a cheerful mind which is declared by his words, rejoices a man, as a covetous mind kills him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#026">Proverbs 12:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 12:26</a></font></span></p>The righteous (l) [is] more excellent than his neighbour: but the way of the wicked seduceth them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) That is, more liberal in giving.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#027">Proverbs 12:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 12:27</a></font></span></p>The slothful [man] roasteth not that which he (m) took in hunting: but the substance of a diligent man [is] precious. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Although he gets much by unlawful means, yet he will not spend it on himself.
