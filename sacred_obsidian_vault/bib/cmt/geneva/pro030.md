 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 30</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#001">Proverbs 30:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 30:1</a></font></span></p>The words of (a) Agur the son of Jakeh, [even] the prophecy: the man spoke to Ithiel, even to (b) Ithiel and Ucal, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Who was an excellent man in virtue and knowledge in the time of Solomon. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Who were Agur's scholars or friends.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#002">Proverbs 30:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 30:2</a></font></span></p>Surely I [am] more (c) senseless than [any] man, and have not the understanding of a man. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) In this he declares his great humility who would not attribute any wisdom to himself but all to God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#004">Proverbs 30:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 30:4</a></font></span></p>Who hath ascended into (d) heaven, or descended? who hath gathered the wind in his fists? who hath bound the waters in a garment? who hath established all the ends of the earth? what [is] his name, and what [is] his son's name, if thou canst tell? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Meaning, to know the secrets of God, as though he would say, "None".  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#007">Proverbs 30:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 30:7</a></font></span></p>Two (e) [things] have I required of thee; deny [them] not to me before I die: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) He makes this request to God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#009">Proverbs 30:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 30:9</a></font></span></p>Lest I be full, and deny [thee], and say, (f) Who [is] the LORD? or lest I be poor, and steal, and take the name of my God [in vain]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Meaning, that they who put their trust in their riches forget God and that by too much wealth men have an opportunity to the same.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#010">Proverbs 30:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 30:10</a></font></span></p>Accuse not a servant to his master, lest he curse thee, (g) and thou be found guilty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) In accusing him without cause.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#015">Proverbs 30:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 30:15</a></font></span></p>The horseleach hath two (h) daughters, [crying], Give, give. There are three [things that] are never satisfied, [yea], four [things] say not, [It is] enough: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) The leach has two forks in her tongue, which here he calls her two daughters, by which she sucks the blood, and is never satisfied: even so, the covetous extortioners are insatiable.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#017">Proverbs 30:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 30:17</a></font></span></p>The eye [that] mocketh at [its] father, and despiseth to obey [its] mother, the ravens (i) of the valley shall pick it out, and the young eagles shall eat it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Which hunt in the valley for carrion.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#020">Proverbs 30:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 30:20</a></font></span></p>Such [is] the way of an adulterous woman; she eateth, and (k) wipeth her mouth, and saith, I have done no wickedness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) She has her desires, and later counterfeits as though she were an honest woman.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#022">Proverbs 30:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 30:22</a></font></span></p>For (l) a servant when he reigneth; and a fool when he is filled with food; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) These commonly abuse the state to which they are called.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#023">Proverbs 30:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 30:23</a></font></span></p>For an odious [woman] when she is married; and an handmaid that is (m) heir to her mistress. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) Who is married to her master after the death of her mistress.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#024">Proverbs 30:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 30:24</a></font></span></p>There are four [things which are] little upon the earth, but they [are] very (n) wise: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) They contain great doctrine and wisdom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#028">Proverbs 30:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 30:28</a></font></span></p>The spider taketh hold (o) with her hands, and is in kings' palaces. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) If man is not able to compass these common things by his wisdom, we cannot attribute wisdom to man, but folly.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#032">Proverbs 30:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 30:32</a></font></span></p>If thou hast done foolishly in lifting up thyself, or if thou hast thought evil, [lay] thy hand (p) upon thy mouth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) Make a stay and continue not in doing evil.
