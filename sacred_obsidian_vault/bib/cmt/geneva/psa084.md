 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 84</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#001">Psalms 84:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 84:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician upon Gittith, A Psalm for the sons of Korah." How amiable [are] thy tabernacles, O (a) LORD of hosts! 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) David complains that he cannot have access to the Church of God to make profession of his faith, and to profit in religion.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#002">Psalms 84:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 84:2</a></font></span></p>My soul longeth, yea, even fainteth for the (b) courts of the LORD: my heart and my flesh crieth out for the living God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For only the priests could enter the sanctuary and the rest of the people into the courts.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#003">Psalms 84:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 84:3</a></font></span></p>Yea, the sparrow hath found an house, and the swallow a nest for herself, where she may lay her young, [even] thine (c) altars, O LORD of hosts, my King, and my God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) So that the poor birds have more freedom than I.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#005">Psalms 84:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 84:5</a></font></span></p>Blessed [is] the man whose (d) strength [is] in thee; in whose heart [are] the ways [of them]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Who trusts nothing in himself but in you only, and learns from you to rule his life.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#006">Psalms 84:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 84:6</a></font></span></p>[Who] passing through the valley of (c) Baca make it a well; the rain also filleth the pools. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) So that the poor birds have more freedom than I.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#007">Psalms 84:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 84:7</a></font></span></p>They go from (f) strength to strength, [every one of them] in Zion appeareth before God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) They are never weary but increase in strength and courage till they come to God's house.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#009">Psalms 84:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 84:9</a></font></span></p>Behold, O God our shield, and look upon the face of thine (g) anointed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is, for Christ's sake, whose figure I represent.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#010">Psalms 84:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 84:10</a></font></span></p>For (h) a day in thy courts [is] better than a thousand. I had rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God, than to dwell in the tents of wickedness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) He would rather live one day in God's Church than a thousand in the world.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#011">Psalms 84:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 84:11</a></font></span></p>For the LORD God [is] a sun and shield: the LORD will give grace and glory: no (i) good [thing] will he withhold from them that walk uprightly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) But will from time to time increase his blessings toward his more and more.
