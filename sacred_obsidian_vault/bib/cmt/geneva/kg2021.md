 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#001">4 Kings (2 Kings) Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#006">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">kg2 21:6</a></font></span></p>And he made his son (a) pass through the fire, and observed times, and used enchantments, and dealt with familiar spirits and wizards: he wrought much wickedness in the sight of the LORD, to provoke [him] to anger. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2016.htm#003">Kg2 16:3</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#008">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">kg2 21:8</a></font></span></p>Neither will I make the feet of Israel move any more out of the land which I gave their fathers; only if they will (b) observe to do according to all that I have commanded them, and according to all the law that my servant Moses commanded them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Therefore seeing they did not obey the commandment of God, they were justly cast from the land which they had only on condition. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#012">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">kg2 21:12</a></font></span></p>Therefore thus saith the LORD God of Israel, Behold, I [am] bringing [such] evil upon Jerusalem and Judah, that whosoever heareth of it, both his ears shall (c) tingle. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning, that whoever hears of this great plague, will be astonished. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#013">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">kg2 21:13</a></font></span></p>And I will stretch over Jerusalem the line (d) of Samaria, and the plummet of the house of Ahab: and I will wipe Jerusalem as [a man] wipeth a dish, wiping [it], and turning [it] upside down. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) As I have destroyed Samaria and the house of Ahab so will I destroy Judah. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#014">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">kg2 21:14</a></font></span></p>And I will forsake the (e) remnant of mine inheritance, and deliver them into the hand of their enemies; and they shall become a prey and a spoil to all their enemies; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Meaning, Judah and Benjamin, which were the only ones left of the rest of the tribes. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#016">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">kg2 21:16</a></font></span></p>Moreover Manasseh shed (s) innocent blood very much, till he had filled Jerusalem from one end to another; beside his sin wherewith he made Judah to sin, in doing [that which was] evil in the sight of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) The Hebrews write that he slew Isaiah the prophet, who was his father-in-law. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2021.htm#022">4 Kings (2 Kings) 21:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">kg2 21:22</a></font></span></p>And he forsook the LORD God of his fathers, and walked not in the (g) way of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is, according to his commandment.
