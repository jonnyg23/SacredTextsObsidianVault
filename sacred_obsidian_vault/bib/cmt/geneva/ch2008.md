 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#001">2 Chronicles Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#001">2 Chronicles 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch2 8:1</a></font></span></p>And it came to pass at the end of (a) twenty years, wherein Solomon had built the house of the LORD, and his own house, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Signifying that he was 20 years in building them. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#002">2 Chronicles 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ch2 8:2</a></font></span></p>That the cities which Huram had (b) restored to Solomon, Solomon built them, and caused the children of Israel to dwell there. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) That is, which Hiram gave again to Solomon because they did not please him and therefore called them Cabul, that is, dirt or filth, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1009.htm#013">Kg1 9:13</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#004">2 Chronicles 8:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">ch2 8:4</a></font></span></p>And he built Tadmor in the wilderness, and all the (c) store cities, which he built in Hamath. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning of munitions and treasures for the war. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#005">2 Chronicles 8:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">ch2 8:5</a></font></span></p>Also he built (d) Bethhoron the upper, and Bethhoron the nether, fenced cities, with walls, gates, and bars; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, he repaired and fortified them, for they were built long before by Sherah a noble woman of the tribe of Ephraim, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#068">Ch1 6:68</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1007.htm#024">Ch1 7:24</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#010">2 Chronicles 8:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">ch2 8:10</a></font></span></p>And these [were] the chief of king Solomon's officers, [even] (f) two hundred and fifty, that bare rule over the people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) For in all there were 3300 but here he means of them who had the principal charge, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1009.htm#023">Kg1 9:23</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#013">2 Chronicles 8:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">ch2 8:13</a></font></span></p>Even after a certain rate every day, offering according to the commandment of Moses, on the sabbaths, and on the new moons, and on the solemn feasts, (g) three times in the year, [even] in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Read (Lev. 23:1-44). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#016">2 Chronicles 8:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">ch2 8:16</a></font></span></p>Now all the (h) work of Solomon was prepared unto the day of the foundation of the house of the LORD, and until it was finished. [So] the house of the LORD was perfected. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Both for the matte and also for the workmanship. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#017">2 Chronicles 8:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ch2 8:17</a></font></span></p>Then went Solomon to Eziongeber, and to Eloth, at the (i) sea side in the land of Edom. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Meaning, the Red Sea. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#018">2 Chronicles 8:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">ch2 8:18</a></font></span></p>And Huram sent him by the hands of his servants ships, and servants that had knowledge of the sea; and they went with the servants of Solomon to Ophir, and took thence (k) four hundred and fifty talents of gold, and brought [them] to king Solomon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Which is thought to amount to 3,600,000 crowns, for here mention is made of 30 more than are spoken of, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1009.htm#028">Kg1 9:28</a>).
