 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol007.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol007.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 7:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">sol 7:1</a></font></span></p>How beautiful are thy (a) feet with shoes, O prince's daughter! the joints of thy thighs [are] like jewels, the work of the hands of a skilful workman. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He describes the comely beauty of the Church in every part, which is to be understood spiritually.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol007.htm#003">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 7:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">sol 7:3</a></font></span></p>(b) Thy two breasts [are] like two young roes [that are] twins. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#005">Sol 4:5</a>  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol007.htm#005">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 7:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sol 7:5</a></font></span></p>Thy head upon thee [is] like Carmel, and the hair of thy head like purple; the king [is] (c) held captive by [its] locks of hair. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He delights to come near you, and to be in your company.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol007.htm#010">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 7:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">sol 7:10</a></font></span></p>(d) I [am] my beloved's, and his desire [is] toward me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) This the spouse speaks.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol007.htm#012">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">sol 7:12</a></font></span></p>Let us get up early to the vineyards; let us see if the (e) vine flourisheth, [whether] the tender grape appeareth, [and] the pomegranates bud forth: there will I give thee my loves. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) If the people that are called to Christ bring forth any fruit.
