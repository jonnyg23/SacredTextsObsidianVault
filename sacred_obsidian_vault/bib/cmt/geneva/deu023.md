 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#001">Deuteronomy Chapter 23</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#001">Deuteronomy 23:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">deu 23:1</a></font></span></p>He that is wounded in the stones, or hath his privy member cut off, (a) shall not enter into the congregation of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Either to bear office, or to marry a wife.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#002">Deuteronomy 23:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">deu 23:2</a></font></span></p>(b) A bastard shall not enter into the congregation of the LORD; even to his tenth generation shall he not enter into the congregation of the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) This was to cause them to live chastely, that their posterity might not be rejected.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#004">Deuteronomy 23:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">deu 23:4</a></font></span></p>Because they (c) met you not with bread and with water in the way, when ye came forth out of Egypt; and because they hired against thee Balaam the son of Beor of Pethor of Mesopotamia, to curse thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) By this he condemns all who do not aid the children of God in their calling.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#006">Deuteronomy 23:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">deu 23:6</a></font></span></p>Thou (d) shalt not seek their peace nor their prosperity all thy days for ever. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) You shall have nothing to do with them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#008">Deuteronomy 23:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">deu 23:8</a></font></span></p>The children that are begotten (e) of them shall enter into the congregation of the LORD in their third generation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) If the fathers have renounced their idolatry, and received circumcision.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#012">Deuteronomy 23:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">deu 23:12</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt have a place also without the camp, whither 
 <p>
 </p><p>thou shalt (f) go forth abroad: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) For the necessities of nature.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#013">Deuteronomy 23:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">deu 23:13</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt have a paddle upon thy weapon; and it shall be, when thou wilt ease thyself abroad, thou shalt dig therewith, and shalt turn back and (g) cover that which cometh from thee: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Meaning by this that his people should be pure both in body and soul.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#015">Deuteronomy 23:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">deu 23:15</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt not (h) deliver unto his master the servant which is escaped from his master unto thee: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) This is meant of the heathen , who fled because of their masters' cruelty, and embrace the true religion.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#018">Deuteronomy 23:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">deu 23:18</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt not bring the (i) hire of a whore, or the price of a dog, into the house of the LORD thy God for any vow: for even both these [are] abomination unto the LORD thy God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Forbidding that any income gained from evil things should be applied to the service of God, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic002.htm#007">Mic 2:7</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#020">Deuteronomy 23:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">deu 23:20</a></font></span></p>Unto a (k) stranger thou mayest lend upon usury; but unto thy brother thou shalt not lend upon usury: that the LORD thy God may (l) bless thee in all that thou settest thine hand to in the land whither thou goest to possess it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) This was permitted for a time because of the hardness of their hearts. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(l) If you show charity to your brother, God will declare his love toward you.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#023">Deuteronomy 23:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">deu 23:23</a></font></span></p>That which is gone out of thy lips thou shalt (m) keep and perform; [even] a freewill offering, according as thou hast vowed unto the LORD thy God, which thou hast promised with thy mouth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) If the vow is lawful and godly.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#024">Deuteronomy 23:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">deu 23:24</a></font></span></p>When thou comest into (n) thy neighbour's vineyard, then thou mayest eat grapes thy fill at thine own pleasure; but thou shalt not put [any] in thy (o) vessel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) Being hired for labour. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) To bring home to your house.
