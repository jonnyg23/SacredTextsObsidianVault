 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#001">3 Kings (1 Kings) 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">kg1 8:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Then Solomon 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The consecration of the temple illustrates all consecration. The temple, like the believer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#023">Th1 5:23</a>) was threefold: the court, that which was outward, visible, answered to the body; the holy place, where everything appealed to the sacred emotions, answered to the soul; the holy of holies, the place of communion with God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#022">Exo 25:22</a>) answered to the spirit of man. The ark was the most all-inclusive type of Christ of any one of the vessels of the tabernacle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#009">Exo 25:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>When, therefore, the priests brought the ark into the court, the holy place, and the holy of holies, they were, in type, enthroning Christ over the body, with its powers and appetites; the soul, seat of the emotions and desires; and the mind, seat of the capacity to know and commune with God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026">Gen 1:26</a>) note 3. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In Christian experience this answers to; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#001">Rom 12:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#018">Eph 5:18</a>).
