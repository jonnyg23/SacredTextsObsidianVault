 <p>
 </p><p>That the Gentiles 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That the Gentiles were to be saved was no mystery (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#024">Rom 9:24-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#019">Rom 10:19-21</a>). The mystery, "hid in God", was the divine purpose to make of Jew and Gentile a wholly new thing -- "the church, which is his Christ's body," formed by the baptism with the Holy Spirit, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#012">Co1 12:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#013">Co1 12:13</a>), and in which the earthly distinction of Jew and Gentile disappears; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#014">Eph 2:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#015">Eph 2:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#010">Col 3:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#011">Col 3:11</a>). The revelation of this mystery, which was foretold, but not explained, by Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018">Mat 16:18</a>) was committed to Paul. In his writings alone, we find the doctrine, position, walk, and destiny of the church.
