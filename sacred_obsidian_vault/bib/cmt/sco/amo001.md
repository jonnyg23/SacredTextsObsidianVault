 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#001">Amos Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#002">Amos 1:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">amo 1:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>roar 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Roar," etc. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#013">Isa 42:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#030">Jer 25:30-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos011.htm#010">Hos 11:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos011.htm#011">Hos 11:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#016">Joe 3:16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It will be found that wherever the phrase occurs it is connected with the destruction of Gentile dominion 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(see "Times of the Gentiles," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024">Luk 21:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#019">Rev 16:19</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and the blessing of Israel in the kingdom. Without a doubt a near fulfilment upon Syria occurred (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2014.htm#028">Kg2 14:28</a>) but the expression, "the Lord will roar," looks forward to a vaster fulfilment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe001.htm#004">Joe 1:4</a>).
