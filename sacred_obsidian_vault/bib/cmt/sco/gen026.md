 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#020">Genesis 26:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">gen 26:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>name of the well 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The wells of Genesis have significant names, and are associated with significant events: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Beer-lahai-roi, the well of him that liveth and seeth me (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen016.htm#014">Gen 16:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#062">Gen 24:62</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#011">Gen 25:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Beer-sheba, the well of the oath or covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen021.htm#015">Gen 21:15-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#019">Gen 22:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#023">Gen 26:23-25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen046.htm#001">Gen 46:1-5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Esek, contention (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#020">Gen 26:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Sitnah, hatred (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#021">Gen 26:21</a>). Esek and Sitnah were Isaac's own attempts at well-digging. Afterward, he dwelt by the old wells of his father. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) Rehoboth, enlargement (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#022">Gen 26:22</a>).
