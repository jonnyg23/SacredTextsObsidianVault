 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#010">Numbers 16:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">num 16:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Seek ye the priesthood also 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "gainsaying of Korah" was intrusion into the priest's office ("no man taketh this honour unto himself,") (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#004">Heb 5:4</a>). It was an attempt to create a priestly order without the divine authority (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#010">Heb 5:10</a>). The modern analogue is Nicolaitanism (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#006">Rev 2:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#015">Rev 2:15</a>) the division of an equal brotherhood (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#008">Mat 23:8</a>) into "clergy" and "laity"; a vastly different thing from the due recognition of ministry-gifts; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#004">Co1 12:4-31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#008">Eph 4:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#011">Eph 4:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#012">Eph 4:12</a>) or of elders and deacons; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#001">Ti1 3:1-13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#005">Tit 1:5-9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#047">Numbers 16:47</a></p>
