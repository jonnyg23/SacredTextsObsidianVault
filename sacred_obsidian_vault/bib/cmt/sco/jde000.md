 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#001">Jude Introduction</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#001">Jude</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">jde 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - Jude
 </p><p>Writer: Jude, the brother of James (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#001">Jde 1:1</a>). 
 </p><p>Date: Probably A.D. 66 
 </p><p>Theme: It is not so much Jude who speaks, as the constraining Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#003">Jde 1:3</a>) and the theme is, "Contending for the faith" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#008">Luk 18:8</a>),  
 </p><p>See Scofield  - <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#008">Luk 18:8</a>) . In this brief letter the apostasy 
 </p><p>See Scofield  - <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#003">Th2 2:3</a>) of the professing church is predicted, and the cause and course described. As in Second Timothy and Second Peter the apostasy is treated as having already set in. 
 </p><p>The Epistle is in five divisions: 
 </p><p>1. Introduction (Jude 1:1-2). 
 </p><p>2. Occasion of the Epistle (Jude 1:3-4). 
 </p><p>3. Apostasy is possible (Jude 1:5-7). 
 </p><p>4. Apostate teachers described (Jude 1:8-19). 
 </p><p>5. The saints assured and comforted (Jude 1:20-25).
