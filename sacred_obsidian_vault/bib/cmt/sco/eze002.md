 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze002.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze002.htm#001">Ezekiel 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">eze 2:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Son of man 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Son of man," used by our Lord of Himself seventy-nine times, is used by Jehovah ninety one times when addressing Ezekiel. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) In the case of our Lord the meaning is clear: it is His racial name as the representative Man in the sense of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#045">Co1 15:45-47</a>). The same thought, implying transcendence of mere Judaism, is involved in the phrase when applied to Ezekiel. Israel had forgotten her mission. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#010">Gen 11:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze005.htm#005">Eze 5:5-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Now, in her captivity, Jehovah will not forsake His people, but He will remind them that they are but a small part of the race for whom He also cares. Hence the emphasis upon the word "man." The Cherubim "had the likeness of a man" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#005">Eze 1:5</a>) and when the prophet beheld the throne of God, he saw "the likeness as the appearance of a man above upon it" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#026">Eze 1:26</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#020">Mat 8:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#012">Rev 1:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#013">Rev 1:13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) As used of Ezekiel, the expression indicates, not what the prophet is in himself, but what he is to God; a son of man 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) chosen, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) endued with the Spirit, and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (c) sent of God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>All this is true also of Christ who was, furthermore, the representative man -- the head of regenerate humanity.
