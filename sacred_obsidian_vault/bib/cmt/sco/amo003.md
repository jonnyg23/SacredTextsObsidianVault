 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo003.htm#001">Amos Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo003.htm#001">Amos 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">amo 3:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>whole family 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The language here, and the expression "house of Jacob," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo003.htm#013">Amo 3:13</a>) evidently gives the prophecy a wider application than to "Israel," the ten-tribe northern kingdom, though the judgment was, in the event, executed first upon the northern kingdom. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#018">Kg2 17:18-23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo003.htm#002">Amos 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">amo 3:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>therefore 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It is noteworthy that Jehovah's controversy with the Gentile cities which hated Israel is brief: "I will send a fire." But Israel had been brought into the place of privilege and so of responsibility, and the Lord's indictment is detailed and unsparing. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#023">Mat 11:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#047">Luk 12:47</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#048">Luk 12:48</a>).
