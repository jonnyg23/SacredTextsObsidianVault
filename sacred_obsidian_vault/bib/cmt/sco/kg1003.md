 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1003.htm#001">3 Kings (1 Kings) Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1003.htm#002">3 Kings (1 Kings) 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">kg1 3:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>high places 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#030">Lev 26:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#011">Deu 12:11-14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The use of commanding elevations for altars seems to have been immemorial and universal. In itself the practice was not evil; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#007">Gen 12:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#008">Gen 12:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#002">Gen 22:2-4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen031.htm#054">Gen 31:54</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#025">Jdg 6:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#026">Jdg 6:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg013.htm#016">Jdg 13:16-23</a>). After the establishment of Mount Moriah and the temple as the centre of divine worship; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#005">Deu 12:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#012">Ch2 7:12</a>) the pentateuchal prohibition of the use of high places (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#011">Deu 12:11-14</a>) which had looked forward to the setting up of such a centre, came into effect, and high places became identified with idolatrous practices. The constant recurrence to the use of high places, even for Jehovistic worship 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#014">Kg1 15:14</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and after the building of the temple, proves how deeply rooted the custom was. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2018.htm#004">Kg2 18:4-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2023.htm#001">Kg2 23:1-37</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#003">Ch2 33:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#017">Ch2 33:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#019">Ch2 33:19</a>). Note below refers to "Groves." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg003.htm#007">Jdg 3:7</a>).
