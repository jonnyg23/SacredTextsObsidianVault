 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#001">Galatians Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#004">Galatians 5:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">gal 5:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>no effect 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, of no experimental effect: the sense of liberty is lost. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#021">Gal 2:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#023">Col 1:23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>grace 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Grace (in salvation). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#006">Eph 1:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#007">Eph 1:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#006">Gal 1:6</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#017">Joh 1:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#005">Galatians 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">gal 5:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#010">Rom 10:10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#018">Galatians 5:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">gal 5:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>under the law 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, not under bondage of effort to please God by law-works. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2003.htm#017">Co2 3:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#022">Galatians 5:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">gal 5:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>But the fruit 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christian character is not mere moral or legal correctness, but the possession and manifestation of nine graces: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>love, joy, peace -- character as an inward state; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>longsuffering, gentleness, goodness -- character in expression toward man; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>faith, meekness, temperance -- character in expression toward God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Taken together, they present a moral portrait of Christ, and may be taken as the apostle's explanation of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020">Gal 2:20</a>). "Not I, but Christ," and as a definition of "fruit" in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#001">Joh 15:1-8</a>). This character is possible because of the believer's vital union to Christ; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#005">Joh 15:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#012">Co1 12:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#013">Co1 12:13</a>); and is wholly the fruit of the Spirit in those believers who are yielded to Him. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#022">Gal 5:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#023">Gal 5:23</a>).
