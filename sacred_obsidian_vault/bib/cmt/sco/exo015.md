 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo015.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo015.htm#013">Exodus 15:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">exo 15:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>redeemed 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hebrew, "goel," Redemption (Kinsman type). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa059.htm#020">Isa 59:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa059.htm#020">Isa 59:20</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo015.htm#025">Exodus 15:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">exo 15:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>when he had cast into the waters 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>These bitter waters were in the very path of the Lord's leading, and stand for the trials of God's people, which are educatory and not punitive. The "tree" is the cross (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#013">Gal 3:13</a>) which became sweet to Christ as the expression of the Father's will (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#011">Joh 18:11</a>). When our Marahs are so taken we cast the "tree" into the waters. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#003">Rom 5:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#004">Rom 5:4</a>).
