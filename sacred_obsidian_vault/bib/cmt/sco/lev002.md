 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#001">Leviticus 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lev 2:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>fine flour 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The meal-offering. The fine flour speaks of the evenness and balance of the character of Christ; of that perfection in which no quality was in excess, none lacking; the fire, of His testing by suffering, even unto death; frankincense; the fragrance of His life Godward (see) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#034">Exo 30:34</a>) absence of leaven, His character as "the Truth" (see) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#008">Exo 12:8</a>) absence of honey; -- His was not that mere natural sweetness which may exist quite apart from grace; oil mingled, Christ as born of the Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#018">Mat 1:18-23</a>) oil upon, Christ as baptized with the Spirit; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#032">Joh 1:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#027">Joh 6:27</a>) the oven, the unseen sufferings of Christ -- His inner agonies; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#018">Heb 2:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#045">Mat 27:45-46</a>) the pan, His more evident sufferings (for example) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#027">Mat 27:27-31</a>) salt, the pungency of the truth of God -- that which arrests the action of leaven. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#011">Leviticus 2:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">lev 2:11</a></font></span></p>

 </p><p>
 </p><p>For meanings of leaven, see (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#000">Mat 13:.33</a>). Also (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#013">Lev 7:13</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#013">Lev 7:13</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Honey 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>honey is mere natural sweetness and could not symbolize the divine graciousness of the Lord Jesus.
