 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg016.htm#001">Judges Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg016.htm#020">Judges 16:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">jdg 16:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>wist not 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Contra, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo034.htm#029">Exo 34:29</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg016.htm#031">Judges 16:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">jdg 16:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>judged 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The character and work of Samson are alike enigmatical. Announced by an angel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg013.htm#001">Jdg 13:1-21</a>). He was a Nazarite; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#001">Num 6:1-27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg013.htm#005">Jdg 13:5</a>) who constantly defiled his Nazarite separation through fleshly appetites. Called of God to judge Israel, and endued wonderfully with the Spirit, he wrought no abiding work for Israel, and perished in captivity to his enemies the Philistines. What was real in the man was his mighty faith in Jehovah in a time of doubt and apostasy, and this faith God honoured (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#032">Heb 11:32</a>).
