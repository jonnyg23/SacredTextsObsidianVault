 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#001">Jeremiah Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#001">Jeremiah 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jer 2:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>word of the Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The general character of the first message from Jehovah to Judah by Jeremiah is threefold: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) He reminds Israel of the days of blessing and deliverance, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#001">Jer 2:1-7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) He reproaches them with forsaking Him, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#013">Jer 2:13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) He accuses them of choosing other, and impotent, gods, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#010">Jer 2:10-12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#026">Jer 2:26-28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>All these messages are to be thought of as inspired sermons, spoken to the people and subsequently written. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#001">Jer 36:1-32</a>).
