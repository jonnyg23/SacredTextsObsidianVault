 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 28</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#009">Genesis 28:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">gen 28:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Mahalath 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen036.htm#003">Gen 36:3</a>) is called Bashemath:  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#010">Genesis 28:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">gen 28:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And Jacob went out 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Bethel becomes, because of Jacob's vision there, one of the significant places of Scripture. To the Christian it stands for a realization, however imperfect, of the heavenly and spiritual contents of faith, answering to Paul's prayer in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#017">Eph 1:17-23</a>). Dispensationally, the scene speaks of Israel the nation, cast out of the Land of Promise because of evil-doing there, but holding the promise of restoration and blessing; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#015">Gen 28:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#001">Deu 30:1-10</a>). To "an Israelite indeed" Christ speaks of Jacob's vision as to be fulfilled in the Son of man (cf); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#012">Gen 28:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#047">Joh 1:47-51</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#012">Genesis 28:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">gen 28:12</a></font></span></p>
