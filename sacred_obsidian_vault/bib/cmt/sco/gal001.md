 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#001">Galatians Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#003">Galatians 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">gal 1:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Grace 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Grace (in salvation). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#006">Gal 1:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#015">Gal 1:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#021">Gal 2:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#017">Joh 1:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#004">Galatians 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">gal 1:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>sins 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Sin 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#023">Rom 3:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#006">Galatians 1:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">gal 1:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>grace 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The test of the Gospel is grace. If the message excludes grace, or mingles law with grace as the means of either of justification or sanctification, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#021">Gal 2:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#001">Gal 3:1-3</a>), or denies the fact or guilt of sin which alone gives grace its occasion and opportunity, it is "another" gospel, and the preacher of it is under the anathema of God. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#008">Gal 1:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#009">Gal 1:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#010">Galatians 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">gal 1:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>For now do 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The demonstration is as follows: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The Galatians know Paul, that he is no seeker after popularity. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#010">Gal 1:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) He puts his known character back of the assertion that his Gospel of grace was a revelation from God. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#011">Gal 1:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#012">Gal 1:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) As for the Judaizers, Paul had been a foremost Jew, and had forsaken Judaism for something better. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#013">Gal 1:13-14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) He had preached grace years before he saw any of the other apostles. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#015">Gal 1:15-24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) When he did meet the other apostles they had nothing to add to his revelations. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#001">Gal 2:1-6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) The other apostles fully recognized Paul's apostleship. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#007">Gal 2:7-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) If the legalizers pleaded Peter's authority, the answer was that he himself had claimed none when rebuked. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#011">Gal 2:11-14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#013">Galatians 1:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">gal 1:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Jews' religion 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The new dispensation of grace having come in, the Mosaic system, if still persisted in, becomes a mere "Jews' religion."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#014">Galatians 1:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">gal 1:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>religion 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In verses 13 and 14 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#013">Gal 1:13-14</a>), the Greek word for "the Jews' religion" is "Ioudaismos", (meaning "Judaism"). In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act026.htm#005">Act 26:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#026">Jam 1:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#027">Jam 1:27</a>), the Greek word "threskeia" -- "religious service" -- is translated as "religion," and in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#018">Col 2:18</a>); it is translated as "worshipping." Excepting (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#027">Jam 1:27</a>); "religion" has always a bad sense, and nowhere is it synonymous with salvation or spirituality.
