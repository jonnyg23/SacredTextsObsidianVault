 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#001">Acts Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#019">Acts 3:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">act 3:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>refreshing 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Namely, seasons in which, through the appearance of the Messiah in His kingdom, there shall occur blessed rest and refreshment for the people of God." -- Heinrich A. W. Meyer. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>sins 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Sin 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#023">Rom 3:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#020">Acts 3:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">act 3:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And he shall 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The appeal here is national to the Jewish people as such, not individuals as in Peter's first sermon (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#038">Act 2:38</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#039">Act 2:39</a>). There those who were pricked in heart were exhorted to save themselves from (among) the untoward nation; here the whole people is addressed, and the promise to national repentance is national deliverance: "and he shall send Jesus Christ" to bring in the times which the prophets had foretold. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#014">Act 2:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The official answer was the imprisonment of the apostles, and the inhibition to preach, so fulfilling (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#014">Luk 19:14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#021">Acts 3:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">act 3:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>restitution 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "apokatastasis"", means "restoration", occurring here and (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#006">Act 1:6</a>) only.) The meaning is limited by the words: "Which God hath spoken by the mouth of all his holy prophets." The prophets speak of the restoration of Israel to the land 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See "Israel,"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#002">Gen 12:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#026">Rom 11:26</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>also "Palestinian Covenant," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#001">Deu 30:1-9</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#003">Deu 30:3</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and of the restoration, the theocracy, under David's Son. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See "Davidic Covenant," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008">Sa2 7:8-17</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#016">Sa2 7:16</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Kingdom," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026">Gen 1:26-28</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>No prediction of the conversion and restoration of the wicked dead is found in the prophets, or elsewhere. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#011">Rev 20:11-15</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#025">Acts 3:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">act 3:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>children 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "uihos", "sons").
