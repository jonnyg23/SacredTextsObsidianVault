 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo024.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo024.htm#004">Exodus 24:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">exo 24:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>builded 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#007">Exo 33:7-11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This arrangement for worship was temporarily called the "tabernacle."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo024.htm#011">Exodus 24:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 24:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>eat and drink 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Symbol of communion. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#015">Luk 22:15-18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>A blood-sprinkled people, see (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#019">Heb 9:19-22</a>) who had not yet broken the law could thus commune with God. Never again was this repeated. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo024.htm#009">Exo 24:9-11</a>) with (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#002">Lev 16:2</a>) and (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#006">Heb 9:6-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>But compare, under grace, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#013">Eph 2:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#019">Heb 10:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#020">Heb 10:20</a>).
