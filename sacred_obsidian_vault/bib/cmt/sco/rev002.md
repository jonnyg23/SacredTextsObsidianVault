 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#006">Revelation 2:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">rev 2:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Nicolaitanes 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>From Greek, "nikao", "to conquer," and Greek, "laos", "the people," or "laity." There is no ancient authority for a sect of the Nicolaitanes. If the word is symbolic, it refers to the earliest form of the notion of a priestly order, or "clergy," which later divided an equal brotherhood (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#008">Mat 23:8</a>) into "priests" and "laity." What in Ephesus was "deeds" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#006">Rev 2:6</a>) had become in Pergamos a "doctrine (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#015">Rev 2:15</a>). 
 </p><p>

 </p><p>The "doctrine" of Balaam 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The doctrine of Balaam 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#015">Pe2 2:15</a>); 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#011">Jde 1:11</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>was his teaching Balak to corrupt the people who could not be cursed. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num031.htm#015">Num 31:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num031.htm#016">Num 31:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num022.htm#005">Num 22:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num023.htm#008">Num 23:8</a>) by tempting them to marry women of Moab, defile their separation, and abandon their pilgrim character. It is that union with the world and the church which is spiritual unchastity. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#004">Jam 4:4</a>). Pergamos had lost the pilgrim character and was "dwelling" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#013">Rev 2:13</a>) "where Satan's throne is," in the world. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#031">Joh 12:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#030">Joh 14:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#011">Joh 16:11</a>).
