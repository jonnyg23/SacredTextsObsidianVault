 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 32</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#001">Genesis 32:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">gen 32:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>angel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#004">Heb 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#002">Genesis 32:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">gen 32:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Mahanaim 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, two hosts, or bands -- the visible band, Jacob and his servants; the invisible band, God's angels. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2006.htm#013">Kg2 6:13-17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#003">Genesis 32:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">gen 32:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Edom 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, Esau's country. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#030">Gen 25:30</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen036.htm#001">Gen 36:1</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#024">Genesis 32:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">gen 32:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>and there wrestled 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jacob's crisis. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#013">Jos 5:13-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#005">Job 42:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#006">Job 42:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#001">Isa 6:1-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer001.htm#004">Jer 1:4-9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#028">Eze 1:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze002.htm#001">Eze 2:1-7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan010.htm#001">Dan 10:1-12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#001">Act 9:1-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#013">Rev 1:13-18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#028">Genesis 32:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">gen 32:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>shall be no more called Jacob 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Both names are applied to the nation descended from Jacob. When used characteristically "Jacob" is the name for the natural posterity of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob; "Israel" for the spiritual part of the nation. See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#008">Isa 9:8</a>). The "word" was sent to all the people, "Jacob," but it "lighted upon Israel," that is, was comprehended by the spiritual part of the people. See "Israel" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#002">Gen 12:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#026">Rom 11:26</a>).
