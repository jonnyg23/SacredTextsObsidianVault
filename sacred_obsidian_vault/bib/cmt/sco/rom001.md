 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#003">Romans 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rom 1:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>seed 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See note on the Davidic descent of Christ, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#023">Luk 3:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#005">Romans 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">rom 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>for obedience 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Unto obedience to faith, that is, faith as a principle, or method of divine dealing. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#001">Rom 10:1-11</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#008">Romans 1:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">rom 1:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "kosmos", means "mankind". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#008">Mat 4:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016">Romans 1:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">rom 1:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>salvation 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Hebrew, and (Greek, "sōtēria", meaning "safety", "preservation", "healing", and "soundness"). Salvation is the great inclusive word of the Gospel, gathering into itself all the redemptive acts and processes: as justification, redemption, grace, propitiation, imputation, forgiveness, sanctification, and glorification. Salvation is in three tenses: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The believer has been saved from the guilt and penalty of sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#050">Luk 7:50</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#018">Co1 1:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2002.htm#015">Co2 2:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#005">Eph 2:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#008">Eph 2:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2001.htm#009">Ti2 1:9</a>) and is safe. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) the believer is being saved from the habit and dominion of sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#014">Rom 6:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#019">Phi 1:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#012">Phi 2:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#013">Phi 2:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#013">Th2 2:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#002">Rom 8:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#019">Gal 2:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020">Gal 2:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2003.htm#018">Co2 3:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The believer is to be saved in the sense of entire conformity to Christ. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#011">Rom 13:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#036">Heb 10:36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#005">Pe1 1:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#002">Jo1 3:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Salvation is by grace through faith, is a free gift, and wholly without works; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#027">Rom 3:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#028">Rom 3:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#001">Rom 4:1-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#023">Rom 6:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#008">Eph 2:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The divine order is: 
 </p><p>     first, salvation, 
 </p><p>     then, works; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#009">Eph 2:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#010">Eph 2:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#005">Tit 3:5-8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#028">Romans 1:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">rom 1:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>did not like 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Refused to have. Literally, did not approve God.
