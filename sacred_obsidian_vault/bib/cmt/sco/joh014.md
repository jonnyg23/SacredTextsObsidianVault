 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#001">John Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#003">John 14:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">joh 14:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>receive you unto myself 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This promise of a second advent of Christ is to be distinguished from His return in glory to the earth; it is the first intimation in Scripture of "the day of Christ". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#008">Co1 1:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Here He comes for His saints (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014">Th1 4:14-17</a>); there (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#029">Mat 24:29</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030">Mat 24:30</a>), He come to judge the nations, etc.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#016">John 14:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">joh 14:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Comforter 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "Parakletos", meaning "one called alongside to help." Translated "advocate," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#001">Jo1 2:1</a>). Christ is the believer's Paraclete with the Father when he sins; the Holy Spirit the believer's indwelling Paraclete to help his ignorance and infirmity and to make intercession (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#026">Rom 8:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#027">Rom 8:27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See "Holy Spirit," New Testament doctrine,) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#018">Mat 1:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#004">Act 2:4</a>).
