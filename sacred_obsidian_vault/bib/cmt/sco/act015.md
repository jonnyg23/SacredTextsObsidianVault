 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#001">Acts Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#003">Acts 15:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">act 15:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Phenice 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Phoenicia.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#007">Acts 15:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">act 15:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>my mouth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#019">Mat 16:19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Peter used the keys first for the Jews on the day of Pentecost; secondly, in the house of Cornelius for the Gentiles, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Act 15:14</a>); But Paul was distinctively the apostle to the Gentiles. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#007">Gal 2:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#008">Gal 2:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#013">Acts 15:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">act 15:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Men and brethren 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>dispensationally, this is the most important passage in the New Testament It gives the divine purpose for this age, and for the beginning of the next. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The taking out from among the Gentiles of a people for His name, the distinctive work of the present, or church-age. The church is the ecclesia -- the "called-out assembly." Precisely this has been in progress since Pentecost. The Gospel has never anywhere converted all, but everywhere has called out some. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) "After this namely, the out-calling I will return." James quotes from (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#011">Amo 9:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#012">Amo 9:12</a>). The verses which follow in Amos describe the final regathering of Israel, which the other prophets invariably connect with the fulfilment of the Davidic Covenant (for example); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#001">Isa 11:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010">Isa 11:10-12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer023.htm#005">Jer 23:5-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) "And will build again the tabernacle of David," that is, re-establish the Davidic rule over Israel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008">Sa2 7:8-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) "That the residue of men Israelites may seek after the Lord" Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#007">Zac 12:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#001">Zac 13:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#002">Zac 13:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) "And all the Gentiles," etc. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#002">Mic 4:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#021">Zac 8:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#022">Zac 8:22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This is also the order of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#024">Rom 11:24-27</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Acts 15:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">act 15:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>at the first 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Literally, for the first time, that is, in the house of Cornelius. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#008">Act 15:8-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#034">Act 10:34-48</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#011">Act 10:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#012">Act 10:12-18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#016">Acts 15:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">act 15:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>tabernacle of David 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Kingdom (New Testament); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Act 15:14-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#021">Rev 3:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#034">Co1 15:34</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#017">Acts 15:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">act 15:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jehovah. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#016">Act 15:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#017">Act 15:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#011">Amo 9:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#012">Amo 9:12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#019">Acts 15:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">act 15:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Wherefore 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The scope of the decision goes far beyond the mere question of circumcision. The whole question of the relation of the law to Gentile believers had been put in issue (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#005">Act 15:5</a>), and their exemption is declared in the decision (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#019">Act 15:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#024">Act 15:24</a>). The decision might be otherwise stated in the terms of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#014">Rom 6:14</a>). "Ye are not under the law, but under grace." Gentile believers were to show grace by abstaining from the practices offensive to godly Jews; 
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#020">Act 15:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#021">Act 15:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#028">Act 15:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#029">Act 15:29</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom014.htm#012">Rom 14:12-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1008.htm#001">Co1 8:1-13</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#032">Acts 15:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">act 15:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>prophets 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#003">Co1 14:3</a>) defines the New Testament gift of prophecy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#039">Acts 15:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">act 15:39</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Barnabas 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>And is heard of no more in the Bible story.
