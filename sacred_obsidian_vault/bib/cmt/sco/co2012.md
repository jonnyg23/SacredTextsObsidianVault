 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">co2 12:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>thorn 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It has been conjectured that Paul's "thorn in the flesh" was chronic ophthalmia, inducing bodily weakness, and a repulsive appearance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#015">Gal 4:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#003">Co1 2:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#004">Co1 2:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2010.htm#010">Co2 10:10</a>). This cannot be positively known, and the reserve of Scripture is as sure a mark of inspiration as its revelations. Paul's particular "thorn" is not described that his consolations may avail for all to who any thorn is given.
