 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#001">1 Kings (1 Samuel) Chapter 31</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#003">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 31:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">sa1 31:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>hit him 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2001.htm#010">Sa2 1:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2021.htm#012">Sa2 21:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The order is: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Saul is "hit" -- wounded mortally, potentially "slain," by the Philistines; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) either to escape agony, or insult by the enemy, he falls upon his sword, and his armour-bearer, supposing him to be dead, slew himself; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) but Saul was not dead; raising himself upon his spear, he besought the Amalekite to put him to death. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2001.htm#010">Sa2 1:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2021.htm#012">Sa2 21:12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#012">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 31:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">sa1 31:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>burnt 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Contrast (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2016.htm#014">Ch2 16:14</a>).
