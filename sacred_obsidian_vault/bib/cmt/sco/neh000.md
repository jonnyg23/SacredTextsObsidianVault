 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh001.htm#001">Nehemiah</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">neh 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - Nehemiah
 </p><p>Fourteen years after the return of Ezra to Jerusalem, Nehemiah led up a company (B.C. 444) and restored the walls and the civil authority. Of those events this book is the record. It is in eight divisions: 
 </p><p>1. The journey to Jerusalem (Nehemiah 1:1-2:20). 
 </p><p>2. The building of the wall (Nehemiah 3:1 - 6:19). 
 </p><p>3. The census (Nehemiah 7:1-73). 
 </p><p>4. The revival (Nehemiah 8:1 - 11:36). 
 </p><p>5. The census of the priests and Levites (Nehemiah 12:1-2)6 
 </p><p>6. Dedication of the wall (Nehemiah 12:27-43). 
 </p><p>7. Restoration of the temple worship (Nehemiah 12:44-47). 
 </p><p>8. The legal order restored (Nehemiah 13:1-31). 
 </p><p>The moral state of the time is disclosed by the prophet Malachi. This book affords many instances of individual faith acting on the written word (e.g. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh001.htm#008">Neh 1:8-9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh013.htm#001">Neh 13:1</a>). It is the principle of 2 Timothy 2. 
 </p><p>The events recorded in Nehemiah cover a period of 11 years (Ussher).
