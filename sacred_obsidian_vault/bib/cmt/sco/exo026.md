 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#001">Exodus 26:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 26:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>fine twined linen 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Fine linen typifies personal righteousness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#008">Rev 19:8</a>). The fine linen here typifies the sinless life of Christ. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>blue 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Blue. Christ's heavenly origin; purple, His royalty as David's son; scarlet, His sacrifice. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>cherubims 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#005">Eze 1:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#007">Exodus 26:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">exo 26:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>goats' hair 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#005">Lev 16:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#007">Lev 16:7-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The reference seems to be to the result of the ordinance of the two goats as "covering" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#005">Lev 16:5</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>thus speaking of Christ in atonement 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#021">Gen 3:21</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This thought is intensified in the colour of the rams' skins, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#014">Exo 26:14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#015">Exodus 26:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">exo 26:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>boards 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The typical meaning of the boards is clear as to Christ. Acacia wood, a desert growth, is a fitting symbol of Christ in His humanity as "a root out of dry ground" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#002">Isa 53:2</a>). The covering, gold, typifies Deity in manifestation, speaks of His divine glory. As applied to the individual believer the meaning of the boards is less clear. The connection may be found in; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#021">Joh 17:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#022">Joh 17:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#023">Joh 17:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#004">Eph 1:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#006">Eph 1:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#013">Jo1 4:13</a>). Only as seen "in Him" could the boards be taken as representing the believer. So viewed the type is beautiful. In the world, and yet separated from it by the silver of redemption; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#004">Gal 1:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#011">Exo 30:11-16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#025">Exo 38:25-27</a>) as the boards of the tabernacle were separated from the earth by the sockets of silver, and united by the "middle bar" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#028">Exo 26:28</a>) representing both the one life (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020">Gal 2:20</a>) and one Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#003">Eph 4:3</a>) "all the building, fitly framed together, groweth unto an holy temple in the Lord." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#021">Eph 2:21</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#019">Exodus 26:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">exo 26:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>sockets of silver 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Silver symbolizes redemption. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#001">Exo 25:1</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#027">Exo 38:27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>All the tabernacle rests upon silver except the hangings of the gate, the way of access. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo027.htm#017">Exo 27:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#026">Exodus 26:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">exo 26:26</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>shittim 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, acacia.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#031">Exodus 26:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">exo 26:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>vail 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The inner veil, type of Christ's human body (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#026">Mat 26:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#050">Mat 27:50</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#020">Heb 10:20</a>). This veil, barring entrance into the holiest, was the most expressive symbol of the truth that "by the deeds of the law shall no flesh be justified"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#020">Rom 3:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#008">Heb 9:8</a>). Rent by an unseen hand when Christ Died (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#051">Mat 27:51</a>) thus giving instant access to God to all who come by faith in Him, it was the end of all legality; the way to God was open. It is deeply significant that the priests must have patched together again the veil that God had rent, for the temple services went on yet for nearly forty years. That patched veil is Galatianism -- the attempt to put saint or sinner back under the law. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#006">Gal 1:6-9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Anything but "the grace of Christ" is "another gospel," and under anathema.
