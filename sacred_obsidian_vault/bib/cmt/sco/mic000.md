 <p>Book Introduction - Micah
 </p><p>Micah, a contemporary of Isaiah, prophesied during the reigns of Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah over Judah, and of Pekahiah, Pekah, and Hoshea over Israel <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#023">Kg2 15:23-30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#001">Kg2 17:1-6</a>. He was a prophet in Judah <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer026.htm#017">Jer 26:17-19</a> but the book called by his name chiefly concerns Samaria. 
 </p><p>Micah falls into three prophetic strains, each beginning, "Hear": 
 </p><p>1. (Micah 1:1 - 2:13) 
 </p><p>2. (Micah 3:1 - 5:15) 
 </p><p>3. (Micah 6:1 - 7:20) 
 </p><p>The events recorded Micah cover a period of 40 years (Ussher).
