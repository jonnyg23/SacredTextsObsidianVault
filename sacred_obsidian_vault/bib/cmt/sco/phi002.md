 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#006">Philippians 2:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">phi 2:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>form of God 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Form," etc. (Greek, "morphē", "the form by which a person or thing strikes the vision", the "external appearance"). -- Thayer. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#015">Joh 17:15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"The glory which I had with Thee before the world was." Nothing in this passage teaches that the Eternal Word (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">Joh 1:1</a>) emptied Himself of either His divine nature, or His attributes, but only of the outward and visible manifestation of the Godhead. "He emptied, stripped Himself of the insignia of Majesty." -- Lightfoot. "When occasion demanded He exercised His divine attributes." -- Moorehead. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">Joh 1:1</a>).
