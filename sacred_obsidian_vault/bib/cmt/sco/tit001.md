 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#003">Titus 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">tit 1:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Saviour 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016">Rom 1:16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#004">Titus 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">tit 1:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Saviour 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016">Rom 1:16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#005">Titus 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">tit 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>wanting 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It is not at all a question of the presence in the assembly of persons having the qualifications of elders, made overseers by the Holy Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#028">Act 20:28</a>) that such persons were in the churches of Crete is assumed; the question is altogether one of the appointment of such persons. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>These assemblies were not destitute of elders; but were "wanting," in that they were not duly appointed. There is a progress of doctrine in respect of the appointing of elders. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#005">Tit 1:5</a>), note. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>elders 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Elder (Greek, "presbuteros") and bishop (Greek, "episcopos" means "overseer") designate the same office, (compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#007">Tit 1:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#017">Act 20:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#028">Act 20:28</a>), the former referring to the man, the latter to a function of the office. The eldership in the apostolic local churches was always plural. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>There is no instance of one elder in a local church. The functions of the elders are: to rule; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#004">Ti1 3:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#005">Ti1 3:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#017">Ti1 5:17</a>) to guard the body of revealed truth from perversion and error (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#009">Tit 1:9</a>) to "oversee" the church as a shepherd his flock; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#028">Act 20:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh021.htm#016">Joh 21:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#017">Heb 13:17</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#002">Pe1 5:2</a>). Elders are made or "set" in the churches by the Holy Spirit, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#028">Act 20:28</a>), but great stress is laid upon their due appointment. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act014.htm#023">Act 14:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#005">Tit 1:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>At first, they were ordained (Greek, "kathistēmi", "to elect," "to designate with the hand,") by an apostle; for example, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act014.htm#023">Act 14:23</a>), but in Titus and First Timothy, the qualifications of an elder become part of the Scriptures for the guidance of the churches in such appointment. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#001">Ti1 3:1-7</a>).
