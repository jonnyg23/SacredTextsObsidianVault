 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#002">Isaiah 6:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 6:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>seraphims 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hebrew, Burners. The word occurs only here. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#005">Eze 1:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Seraphim are, in many respects, in contrast with the Cherubim, though both are expressive of the divine holiness, which demands that the sinner shall have access to divine presence only through a sacrifice which really vindicates the righteousness of God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#025">Rom 3:25</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#026">Rom 3:26</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and that the saint shall be cleansed before serving. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#022">Gen 3:22-24</a>) illustrates the first; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#001">Isa 6:1-8</a>) the second. The Cherubim may be said to have to do with the altar, the Seraphim with the laver. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#007">Psa 51:7</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#010">Joh 13:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Seraphim appear to be actual angelic beings.
