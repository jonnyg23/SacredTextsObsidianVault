 <p>
 </p><p>die 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Death, physical, Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Physical death is a consequence of sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#019">Gen 3:19</a>) and the universality of death proves the universality of sin. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#012">Rom 5:12-14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Physical death affects the body only, and is neither cessation of life nor of consciousness. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#005">Hab 2:5</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#023">Luk 16:23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009">Rev 6:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#010">Rev 6:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) All physical death ends in the resurrection of the body. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Resurrection" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job019.htm#025">Job 19:25</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052">Co1 15:52</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Because physical death is a consequence of sin, it is not inevitable to the redeemed. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen005.htm#024">Gen 5:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#051">Co1 15:51</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052">Co1 15:52</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#015">Th1 4:15-17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) Physical death has for the believer a peculiar qualification. It is called "sleep," because his body may be "awakened" at any moment. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020">Phi 3:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#021">Phi 3:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014">Th1 4:14-18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) The soul and spirit live, independently of the death of the body, which is described as a "tabernacle" (tent), in which the "I" dwells, and which may be put off. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#001">Co2 5:1-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#042">Co1 15:42-44</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#013">Pe2 1:13-15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) At the believer's death, he is "clothed upon" with a "house from heaven" pending the resurrection of the "earthly house," and is at once "with the Lord". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#001">Co2 5:1-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#023">Phi 1:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#043">Luk 23:43</a>).
