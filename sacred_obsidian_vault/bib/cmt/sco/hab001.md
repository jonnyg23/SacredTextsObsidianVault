 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#001">Habakkuk Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#001">Habakkuk 1:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">hab 1:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>burden 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See note 1, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa013.htm#001">Isa 13:1</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#005">Habakkuk 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">hab 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>for I will work 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Verse 5 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#005">Hab 1:5</a>) anticipates the dispersion "among the nations". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#064">Deu 28:64-67</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>While Israel as a nation is thus dispersed, Jehovah will "work a work" which Israel "will not believe." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#037">Act 13:37-41</a>); interprets this prediction of the redemptive work of Christ. It is significant that Paul quotes this to Jews of the dispersion in the synagogue at Antioch.
