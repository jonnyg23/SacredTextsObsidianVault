 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#002">Zechariah 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">zac 4:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And I said 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The vision of the candlestick and olive trees (Literally, trees of oil) is, as we know, from (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#003">Rev 11:3-12</a>) a prophecy to be fulfilled in the last days of the present age. That which marks the ministry of the "two witnesses" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#003">Rev 11:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#004">Rev 11:4</a>) is power. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#006">Zac 4:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In measure this power would rest upon Zerubbabel, who, having begun the restoration temple of Zechariah's time, would finish it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#009">Zac 4:9</a>) laying the "headstone" amid the shoutings of the people. The whole scene forms a precursive fulfilment of the ministry of the two witnesses of Revelation 11 and of the coming of the true "headstone," Prince Messiah, of whom prince Zerubbabel is a type. Oil is a uniform symbol of the Spirit. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#004">Act 2:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Joshua and Zerubbabel were doubtless the two olive trees for that day, as the two witnesses of Revelation 11 may, in turn, but point to Christ as Priest-King in the kingdom-age. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#012">Zac 6:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#013">Zac 6:13</a>).
