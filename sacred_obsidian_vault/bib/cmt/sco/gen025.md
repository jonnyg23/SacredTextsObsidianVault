 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 25</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#001">Genesis 25:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">gen 25:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Keturah 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>As Sarah stands for "the mother of us all," that is, of those who, by grace, are one with the true Son of promise, of whom Isaac was the type (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#006">Joh 3:6-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#026">Gal 4:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#028">Gal 4:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#029">Gal 4:29</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#011">Heb 2:11-13</a>) and joint heirs of His wealth; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#002">Heb 1:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#016">Rom 8:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#017">Rom 8:17</a>); so Keturah (wedded after the full blessing of Isaac) and her children by Abraham may well stand for the fertility of Israel the natural seed, Jehovah's wife (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos002.htm#001">Hos 2:1-23</a>) after the future national restoration under the Palestinian covenant. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#003">Deu 30:3</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#025">Genesis 25:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">gen 25:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Esau 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Esau stands for the mere man of the earth. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#016">Heb 12:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#017">Heb 12:17</a>). In many respects a nobler man, naturally, than Jacob, he was destitute of faith, and despised the birthright because it was a spiritual thing, of value only as there was faith to apprehend it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#031">Genesis 25:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">gen 25:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>birthright 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "birthright" had three elements: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Until the establishment of the Aaronic priesthood the head of the family exercised priestly rights. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) The Abrahamic family held the Edenic promise of the Satan-Bruiser (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015">Gen 3:15</a>). -- Abel, Seth, Shem, Abraham, Isaac, Esau. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Esau, as the firstborn, was in the direct line of the Abrahamic promise of the Earth-Blesser (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>For all that was revealed, in Esau might have fulfilled those two great Messianic promises. This birthright Esau sold for a momentary fleshly gratification. Jacob's conception of the birthright at that time was, doubtless, carnal and inadequate, but his desire for it evidenced true faith.
