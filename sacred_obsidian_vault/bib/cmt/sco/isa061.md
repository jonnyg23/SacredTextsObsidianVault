 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 61</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#002">Isaiah 61:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 61:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>acceptable year of the Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Observe that Jesus suspended the reading of this passage in the synagogue at Nazareth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#016">Luk 4:16-21</a>) at the comma in the middle of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#002">Isa 61:2</a>). The first advent, therefore, opened the day of grace, "the acceptable year of Jehovah," but does not fulfil the day of vengeance. That will be taken up when Messiah returns (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2001.htm#007">Th2 1:7-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa034.htm#008">Isa 34:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#004">Isa 35:4-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The last verse (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#011">Isa 61:11</a>), taken with the 4th (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#004">Isa 61:4</a>), gives the historic connection: the vengeance precedes the regathering of Israel, and synchronizes with the day of the Lord. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa063.htm#001">Isa 63:1-6</a>).
