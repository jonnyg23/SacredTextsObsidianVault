 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen049.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 49</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen049.htm#028">Genesis 49:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">gen 49:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>blessed them 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jacob's life, ending in serenity and blessing, testifies to the power of God to transform character. His spiritual progress has six notable phases: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) the first exercise of faith, as shown in the purchase of the birthright (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#028">Gen 25:28-34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen027.htm#010">Gen 27:10-22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) the vision at bethel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#010">Gen 28:10-19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) walking in the flesh (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen029.htm#001">Gen 29:1-31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen029.htm#055">Gen 29:55</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) the transforming experience (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#024">Gen 32:24-31</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) the return to Bethel: idols put away (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen035.htm#001">Gen 35:1-7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) the walk of faith (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#001">Gen 37:1-49</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#033">Gen 37:33</a>).
