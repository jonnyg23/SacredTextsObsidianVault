 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze004.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze004.htm#001">Ezekiel 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">eze 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>take 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The symbolic actions during the prophet's dumbness were testimonies to the past wickedness and chastisement of the house of Israel (the whole nation), and prophetic of a coming siege. They therefore intermediate between the siege of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#010">Kg2 24:10-16</a>) at which time Ezekiel was carried to Babylon, and the siege of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#001">Kg2 25:1-11</a>) eleven years later.
