 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#001">Genesis 24:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">gen 24:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And Abraham 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The entire chapter is highly typical: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Abraham, type of a certain king who would make a marriage for his son (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#002">Mat 22:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#044">Joh 6:44</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) the unnamed servant, type of the Holy Spirit, who does not "speak of himself," but takes of the things of the Bridegroom with which to win the bride (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#013">Joh 16:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#014">Joh 16:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) the servant, type of the Spirit as enriching the bride with the Bridegroom's gifts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#022">Gal 5:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#007">Co1 12:7-11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) the servant, type of the Spirit as bringing the bride to the meeting with the Bridegroom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#004">Act 13:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#006">Act 16:6-7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#011">Rom 8:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014">Th1 4:14-16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) Rebekah, type of the Church, the ecclesia, the "called out" virgin bride of Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#016">Gen 24:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#002">Co2 11:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#025">Eph 5:25-32</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) Isaac, type of the Bridegroom, "whom not having seen," the bride loves through the testimony of the unnamed Servant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#008">Pe1 1:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) Isaac, type of the Bridegroom who goes out to meet and receive His bride (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#063">Gen 24:63</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014">Th1 4:14-16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#007">Genesis 24:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">gen 24:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>angel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#004">Heb 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#014">Genesis 24:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">gen 24:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>thereby 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#017">Jdg 6:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#037">Jdg 6:37</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2020.htm#009">Kg2 20:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#033">Pro 16:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#026">Act 1:26</a>). Cf: (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#039">Mat 12:39</a>). Signs are given to faith, not to doubt.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#040">Genesis 24:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">gen 24:40</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>angel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#004">Heb 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#062">Genesis 24:62</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_062">gen 24:62</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Lahairoi 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, "the well of him that liveth and seeth me." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen016.htm#014">Gen 16:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#011">Gen 25:11</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#066">Genesis 24:66</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_066">gen 24:66</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>servant This is the model servant: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) he does not run unsent, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#002">Gen 24:2-9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) goes where he is sent, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#004">Gen 24:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#010">Gen 24:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) does nothing else; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) is prayerful and thankful, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#012">Gen 24:12-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#026">Gen 24:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#027">Gen 24:27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) is wise to win, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#017">Gen 24:17-18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#021">Gen 24:21</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#007">Joh 4:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) speaks not of himself, but of his master's riches and Isaac's heirship, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#022">Gen 24:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#034">Gen 24:34-36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#008">Act 1:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) presents the true issue, and requires clear decision, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#049">Gen 24:49</a>).
