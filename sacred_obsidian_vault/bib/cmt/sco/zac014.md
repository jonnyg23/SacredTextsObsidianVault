 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001">Zechariah Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#004">Zechariah 14:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">zac 14:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>and the mount of Olives 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#005">Zac 14:5</a>) implies that the cleavage of the Mount of Olives is due to an earthquake, and this is confirmed by; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#006">Isa 29:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#019">Rev 16:19</a>). In both passages the context, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001">Zac 14:1-3</a>) associates the earthquake with the Gentile invasion under the Beast; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#008">Dan 7:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>). Surely, in a land seamed by seismic disturbances it should not be difficult to believe that another earthquake might cleave the little hill called the Mount of Olives. Not one of the associated events of Zechariah 14 occurred at the first coming of Christ, closely associated though He then was with the Mount of Olives.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#009">Zechariah 14:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">zac 14:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>king over all the earth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The final answer to the prayer of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#010">Mat 6:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#044">Dan 2:44-45</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#024">Dan 7:24-27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Kingdom (New Testament)"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#028">Co1 15:28</a>).
