 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 37</a></h3> 
 <p>
 </p><p>bones 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Having announced (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#024">Eze 36:24-38</a>) the restoration of the nation, Jehovah now gives in vision and symbol the method of its accomplishment. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#011">Eze 37:11</a>) gives the clue. The "bones" are the whole house of Israel who shall then be living. The "graves" are the nations where they dwell. The order of procedure is: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) the bringing of the people out (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#012">Eze 37:12-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#019">Eze 37:19-27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) the bringing of them in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#012">Eze 37:12</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) their conversion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#013">Eze 37:13</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) the filling with the Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#014">Eze 37:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The symbol follows. The two sticks are Judah and the ten tribes; united, they are one nation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#019">Eze 37:19-21</a>). Then follows (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#021">Eze 37:21-27</a>) the plain declaration as to Jehovah's purpose, and (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#028">Eze 37:28</a>) implies that then Jehovah will become known to the Gentiles in a marked way. This is also the order of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#016">Act 15:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#017">Act 15:17</a>) and the two passages strongly indicate the time of full Gentile conversion. See also (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010">Isa 11:10</a>).
