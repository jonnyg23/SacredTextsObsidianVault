 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#001">Zephaniah Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#004">Zephaniah 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">zep 1:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Chemarims 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, idolatrous priests. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2023.htm#005">Kg2 23:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#005">Zephaniah 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">zep 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Malcham 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>An idol of the Ammonites, same as Molech, or Milcom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#007">Zephaniah 1:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">zep 1:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>for the day of the Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>As in the other Prophets, the approaching invasion of Nebuchadnezzar is treated as an adumbration of the true day of the Lord in which all earth-judgments will culminate, to be followed by the restoration and blessing of Israel and the nations in the kingdom. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Day of the Lord" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Israel"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#002">Gen 12:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#026">Rom 11:26</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare Joel 1, 2.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#011">Zephaniah 1:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">zep 1:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Maktesh 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Literally, The Mortar, a depression in Jerusalem where the bazaars were.
