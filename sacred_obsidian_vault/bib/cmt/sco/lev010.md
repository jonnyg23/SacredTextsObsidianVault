 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#001">Leviticus 10:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lev 10:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>strange fire 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Fire "from before the Lord" had kindled upon the altar of burnt-offering the fire which the care of the priests was to keep burning (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#012">Lev 6:12</a>). No commandment had yet been given (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#012">Lev 16:12</a>) how the incense should be kindled. The sin of Nadab and Abihu was in acting in the things of God without seeking the mind of God. It was "will worship" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#023">Col 2:23</a>) which often has a "show of wisdom and humility." It typifies any use of carnal means to kindle the fire of devotion and praise.
