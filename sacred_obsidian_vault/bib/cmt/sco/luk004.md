 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">luk 4:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jehovah. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu006.htm#013">Deu 6:13</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#010">Luke 4:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">luk 4:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>angels 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#004">Heb 1:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to keep 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>After Satan's failure to tempt the Lord away from the Word, he seeks to tempt Him by it. He however misquotes by the omission of "in all thy ways" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#011">Psa 91:11</a>). The Lord's "ways" were those marked out for Him in perfect dependence upon His Father's will. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#007">Heb 10:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#009">Heb 10:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#016">Luke 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">luk 4:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>came to Nazareth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Our Lord visited Nazareth twice after beginning His public ministry. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#054">Mat 13:54-58</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#001">Mar 6:1-6</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#018">Luke 4:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">luk 4:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>

 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#019">Luke 4:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">luk 4:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>acceptable 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>A comparison with the passage quoted, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#001">Isa 61:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#002">Isa 61:2</a>) affords an instance of the exquisite accuracy of Scripture. Jesus stopped at, "The acceptable year of the Lord," which is connected with the first advent and the dispensation of grace, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015">Gen 3:15</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#011">Act 1:11</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"the day of vengeance of our God" belongs to the second advent, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#003">Deu 30:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#011">Act 1:11</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and judgment.
