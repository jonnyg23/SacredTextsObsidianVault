 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num014.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num014.htm#023">Numbers 14:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">num 14:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Surely 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Kadesh-barnea is, by the unbelief of Israel there, and the divine comment on that unbelief (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num014.htm#022">Num 14:22-38</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu001.htm#019">Deu 1:19-40</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#001">Co1 10:1-5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#012">Heb 3:12-19</a>) invested with immense spiritual significance. The people had faith to sprinkle the blood of atonement (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#028">Exo 12:28</a>) and to come out of Egypt (the world), but had not faith to enter their Canaan rest. Therefore, though redeemed, they were a forty years' grief to Jehovah. The spiritual application is made in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb006.htm#003">Heb 6:3-11</a>): 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb006.htm#004">Heb 6:4</a>).
