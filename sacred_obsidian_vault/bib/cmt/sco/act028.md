 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#030">Acts 28:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">act 28:30</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>his own hired house 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It has been much disputed whether Paul endured two Roman imprisonments, from A.D. 62 to 68 or one. The tradition from Clement to Eusebius favours two imprisonments with a year of liberty between. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Erdman (W.J.) has pointed out the leaving of Trophimus sick at Miletus, mentioned in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#020">Ti2 4:20</a>) could not have been an occurrence of Paul's last journey to Jerusalem, for then Trophimus was not left; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#004">Act 20:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act021.htm#029">Act 21:29</a>) nor of the journey to Rome to appear before Caesar, for then he did not touch at Miletus. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>To make this incident possible, there must have been a release from the first imprisonment, and an interval of ministry and travel.
