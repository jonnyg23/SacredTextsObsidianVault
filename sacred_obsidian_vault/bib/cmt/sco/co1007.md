 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#012">1 Corinthians 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">co1 7:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>speak 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>So far from disclaiming inspiration, the apostle associates his teaching with the Lord's. Cases had arisen (for example) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#012">Co1 7:12-16</a>) as the Gospel overflowed Jewish limitations, not comprehended in the words of Jesus; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#031">Mat 5:31-32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#005">Mat 19:5-9</a>) which were an instruction, primarily, to Israel. These new conditions demanded authoritative settlement, and only the inspired words of an apostle could give that. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#040">Co1 7:40</a>).
