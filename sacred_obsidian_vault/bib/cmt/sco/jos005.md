 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#001">Joshua Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#002">Joshua 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">jos 5:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>circumcision 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Circumcision is the "sign" of the Abrahamic Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#007">Gen 17:7-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#011">Rom 4:11</a>). "The reproach of Egypt" was that, during the later years of the Egyptian bondage, this separating sign had been neglected (Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#024">Exo 4:24-26</a>) and this neglect had continued during the wilderness wanderings. The New Testament analogue is world conformity; the failure openly to take a believer's place with Christ in death and resurrection. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#002">Rom 6:2-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#014">Gal 6:14-16</a>). Spiritually it is mortifying the deeds of the body through the Spirit. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#013">Rom 8:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#016">Gal 5:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#017">Gal 5:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#011">Col 2:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#012">Col 2:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#005">Col 3:5-10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#011">Joshua 5:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">jos 5:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>corn 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The manna is a type of Christ in humiliation, known "after the flesh," giving his flesh that the believer might have life (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#049">Joh 6:49-51</a>) while the "old corn of the land" is Christ apprehended as risen, glorified, and seated in the heavenlies. Occupation with Christ on earth, "crucified through weakness," tends to a wilderness experience. An experience befitting the believer's place in the heavenlies demands an apprehension of the power of His resurrection; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#016">Co2 5:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2013.htm#004">Co2 13:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#010">Phi 3:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#015">Eph 1:15-23</a>). It is the contrast between "milk" and "meat" in Paul's writings. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#001">Co1 3:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#002">Co1 3:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#012">Heb 5:12-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb006.htm#001">Heb 6:1-3</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#015">Joshua 5:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">jos 5:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>holy 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>translated, "consecrated," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos006.htm#019">Jos 6:19</a>) in R.V. "holy."
