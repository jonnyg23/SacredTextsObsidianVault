 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#001">Deuteronomy Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#001">Deuteronomy 16:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">deu 16:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>keep the passover 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare the order of the feasts in Leviticus 23. Here the Passover and Tabernacles are given especial emphasis as marking the beginning and the consummation of God's ways with Israel; the former speaking of redemption, the foundation of all: the latter, or re-gathered Israel blessed in the kingdom. Between, in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#009">Deu 16:9-12</a>) comes the Feast of Weeks -- the joy of a redeemed people, anticipating a greater blessing yet to come. It is, morally, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#001">Rom 5:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#002">Rom 5:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Abib 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>First monththat is, April.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#021">Deuteronomy 16:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">deu 16:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>grove 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The groves (Hebrew, "Asherim") so often mentioned in the Old Testament were devoted to the worship of Ashtereth, the Babylonian goddess, "Ishtar", the "Aphrodite" of the Greeks, the Roman, "Venus". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg002.htm#013">Jdg 2:13</a>).
