 <p>
 </p><p>his Father 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "patēr", "his own Father"). The Jews understood perfectly that Jesus was claiming to be God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#033">Joh 10:33</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#031">John 5:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">joh 5:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>witness 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#014">Joh 8:14</a>). In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#031">Joh 5:31</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Our Lord, defending His Messianic claims before Jews who denied those claims, accepts the biblical rule of evidence, which required "two witnesses"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#017">Joh 8:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num035.htm#030">Num 35:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#006">Deu 17:6</a>). A paraphrase of verse (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#031">Joh 5:31</a>) would be: "If I bear witness of myself ye will say my witness is not true." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#014">Joh 8:14</a>).
