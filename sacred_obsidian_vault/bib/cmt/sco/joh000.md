 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">John</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">joh 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - John
 </p><p>Writer: The fourth Gospel was written by the Apostle John <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh021.htm#024">Joh 21:24</a>. This has been questioned on critical grounds, but on the same grounds and with equal scholarship, the early date and Johanean authorship have been maintained. 
 </p><p>Date: The date of John's Gospel falls between A.D. 85 and 90. Probably the latter. 
 </p><p>Theme: This is indicated both in the Prologue (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">Joh 1:1-14</a>), and in the last verse of the Gospel proper (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#031">Joh 20:31</a>), and is: The incarnation of the eternal Word, and Son of life; (2) that as many as believe on Him as "the Christ, the Son of God" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#031">Joh 20:31</a>) may have eternal life. The prominent words are, "believed" and "life." 
 </p><p>The book is in seven natural divisions: 
 </p><p>1. Prologue: The eternal Word incarnate in Jesus the Christ (John 1:1-14). 
 </p><p>2. The witness of John the Baptist (John 1:15-34). 
 </p><p>3. The public ministry of Christ (John 1:35 - 12:50). 
 </p><p>4. The private ministry of Christ to His own (John 13:1 - 17:26). 
 </p><p>5. The sacrifice of Christ (John 18:1 - 19:42). 
 </p><p>6. The manifestation of Christ in resurrection (John 20:1-31). 
 </p><p>7. Epilogue: Christ the Master of life and service (John 21:1-25). 
 </p><p>The events recorded in this book cover a period of 7 years.
