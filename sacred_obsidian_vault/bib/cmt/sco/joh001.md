 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">John Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">John 1:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">joh 1:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Word 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "logos"); (Aramaic, "Memra," used in the Targums, or Hebrew, paraphrases, for "God"). The Greek term means, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) a thought or concept; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) the expression or utterance of that thought. As a designation of Christ, therefore, Logos is peculiarly felicitous because, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) in Him are embodied all the treasures of the divine wisdom, the collective "thought" of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#024">Co1 1:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#011">Eph 3:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#002">Col 2:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#003">Col 2:3</a>) and, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) He is from eternity, but especially in His incarnation, the utterance or expression of the Person, and "thought" of Deity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#003">Joh 1:3-5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#009">Joh 1:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14-18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#009">Joh 14:9-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#009">Col 2:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In the Being, Person, and work of Christ, Deity is told out.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#005">John 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">joh 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>comprehended 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, apprehended; literally, "laid not hold of it."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#010">John 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">joh 1:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "kosmos" means "mankind". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#008">Mat 4:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#011">John 1:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">joh 1:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>He came 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, He came unto his own things, and his own people received him not.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#017">John 1:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">joh 1:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>grace 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Grace. Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Grace is "the kindness and love of God our Saviour toward man. . . Not by works of righteousness which we have done" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#004">Tit 3:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#005">Tit 3:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It is, therefore, constantly set in contrast to law, under which God demands righteousness from man, as, under grace, he gives righteousness to man (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#021">Rom 3:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#022">Rom 3:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#004">Rom 8:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm003.htm#009">Plm 3:9</a>). Law is connected with Moses and works; grace with Christ and faith; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#017">Joh 1:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#004">Rom 10:4-10</a>). Law blesses the good; grace saves the bad; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#005">Exo 19:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#001">Eph 2:1-9</a>). Law demands that blessings be earned; grace is a free gift; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#001">Deu 28:1-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#008">Eph 2:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#004">Rom 4:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#005">Rom 4:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) As a dispensation, grace begins with the death and resurrection of Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24-26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#004">Rom 3:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#025">Rom 3:25</a>). The point of testing is no longer legal obedience as the condition of salvation, but acceptance or rejection of Christ, with good works as a fruit of salvation, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#013">Joh 1:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#036">Joh 3:36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#037">Mat 21:37</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#024">Mat 22:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#022">Joh 15:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#025">Joh 15:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#002">Heb 1:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#010">Jo1 5:10-12</a>). The immediate result of this testing was the rejection of Christ by the Jews, and His crucifixion by Jew and Gentile (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#027">Act 4:27</a>). The predicted end of the testing of man under grace is the apostasy of the professing church: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(see "Apostasy" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#001">Ti2 3:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#001">Ti2 3:1-8</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and the resultant apocalyptic judgments. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Grace has a twofold manifestation: in salvation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024">Rom 3:24</a>) and in the walk and service of the saved (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#015">Rom 6:15</a>). 
 </p><p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">joh 1:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>hath seen God 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#020">Gen 32:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo024.htm#010">Exo 24:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#018">Exo 33:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#022">Jdg 6:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg013.htm#022">Jdg 13:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#004">Rev 22:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The divine essence, God, veiled in angelic form, and especially as incarnate in Jesus Christ, has been seen of men. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#002">Gen 18:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#022">Gen 18:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#008">Joh 14:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#009">Joh 14:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
