 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#001">Mark Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#013">Mark 10:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">mar 10:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>young 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>little. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#016">Mat 5:16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#016">Mark 10:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">mar 10:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>blessed them 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In Hebrew custom, a father's act. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen027.htm#038">Gen 27:38</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"He had no children that He might adopt all children." -- Bengel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#018">Mark 10:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">mar 10:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Why callest 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Believing Me to be but a human teacher, why callest thou Me "good," etc.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#024">Mark 10:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">mar 10:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>astonished 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, amazed, that is, as Jews: knowing that temporal prosperity was, to the Jew as such, a token of divine favour. For example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#001">Deu 28:1-12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#025">Mark 10:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">mar 10:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>eye of 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It has been thought the reference here was to a postern door set in a gate of Jerusalem. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#028">Mark 10:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">mar 10:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Then Peter 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare note 2, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#027">Mat 19:27-30</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#028">Mat 19:28</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#033">Mark 10:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">mar 10:33</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Son of man 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#020">Mat 8:20</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#045">Mark 10:45</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_045">mar 10:45</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>life 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, soul. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#010">Isa 53:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#012">Isa 53:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "psuchē", "the soul" or "the essential life", not as commonly, Greek, "zoe," "the active life").  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#046">Mark 10:46</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_046">mar 10:46</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And they 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#030">Mat 20:30</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#051">Mark 10:51</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_051">mar 10:51</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "Rabboni," "my Master", a term of reverent love. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#016">Joh 20:16</a>).
