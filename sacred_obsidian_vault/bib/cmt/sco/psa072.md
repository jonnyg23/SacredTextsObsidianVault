 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 72</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#001">Psalms 72:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 72:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Give the king 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Psalm as a whole forms a complete vision of Messiah's kingdom so far as the Old Testament revelation extended. All David's prayers will find their fruition in the kingdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#020">Psa 72:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#001">Sa2 23:1-4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Verse 1 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#001">Psa 72:1</a>) refers to the investiture of the King's Son with the kingdom, of which investiture the formal description is given in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#013">Dan 7:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#014">Dan 7:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#005">Rev 5:5-10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#002">Psa 72:2-7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#012">Psa 72:12-14</a>) give the character of the kingdom. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#003">Isa 11:3-9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The emphatic word is righteousness. The sermon on the Mount describes the kingdom of righteousness. Verses 8-11 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#008">Psa 72:8-11</a>) speak of the universality of the kingdom. Verse 16 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#016">Psa 72:16</a>) hints at the means by which universal blessing is to be brought in. Converted Israel will be the "handful of corn" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#009">Amo 9:9</a>) as the King Himself in death and resurrection was the single grain, the "corn of wheat" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#024">Joh 12:24</a>) "To the Jew first" is the order alike of Church and kingdom. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016">Rom 1:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#046">Act 13:46</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#016">Act 15:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#017">Act 15:17</a>). It is through restored Israel that the kingdom is to be extended over the earth. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#013">Zac 8:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#020">Zac 8:20-23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Psalm 89, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
