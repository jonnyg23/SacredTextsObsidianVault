 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg021.htm#001">Judges Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg021.htm#003">Judges 21:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">jdg 21:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>tribe 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>There is here no mourning for sin, no humbling because of national transgression, no return to Jehovah. Accordingly, no word from Jehovah comes to them. They act wholly in self-will. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg021.htm#010">Jdg 21:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#003">Dan 9:3-13</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg021.htm#006">Judges 21:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jdg 21:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>repented 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#014">Zac 8:14</a>).
