 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#006">Acts 1:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">act 1:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>again the kingdom to Israel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>For forty days, the risen Lord had been instructing the apostles "of the things pertaining to the kingdom of God," doubtless, according to His custom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#027">Luk 24:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#032">Luk 24:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#044">Luk 24:44</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#045">Luk 24:45</a>) teaching them out of the Scriptures. One point was left untouched, namely, the time when He would restore the kingdom to Israel; hence the apostles' question. The answer was according to His repeated teaching; the time was God's secret. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#036">Mat 24:36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#042">Mat 24:42</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#044">Mat 24:44</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#013">Mat 25:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#001">Th1 5:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>restore 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#002">Mat 3:2</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#011">Acts 1:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">act 1:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>come 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The two Advents -- Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The Old Testament foreview of the coming Messiah is in two aspects -- that of rejection and suffering (as, for example, in Isaiah 53), and that of earthly glory and power (as, for example, in Isaiah 11; Jeremiah 23, and Ezekiel 37). Often these two aspects blend in one passage (for example Psalm 2). The prophets themselves were perplexed by this seeming contradiction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#010">Pe1 1:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#011">Pe1 1:11</a>). It was solved by partial fulfilment. In due time the Messiah, born of a virgin according to Isaiah, appeared among men and began His ministry by announcing the predicted kingdom as "at hand." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#017">Mat 4:17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The rejection of King and kingdom followed. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Thereupon the rejected King announced His approaching crucifixion, resurrection, departure, and return (Matthew 24, 25). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#038">Mat 12:38-40</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#001">Mat 16:1-4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#021">Mat 16:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#027">Mat 16:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#035">Luk 12:35-46</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk017.htm#020">Luk 17:20-36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#031">Luk 18:31-34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#012">Luk 19:12-27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) He uttered predictions concerning the course of events between His departure and return (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#001">Mat 13:1-50</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018">Mat 16:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#004">Mat 24:4-26</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) This promised return of Christ becomes a prominent theme in the Acts, Epistles, and Revelation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Taken together, the New Testament teachings concerning the return of Jesus Christ may be summarized as follows: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) That return is an event, not a process, and is personal and corporeal (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#039">Mat 23:39</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030">Mat 24:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Mat 25:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#062">Mar 14:62</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk017.htm#024">Luk 17:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#003">Joh 14:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#011">Act 1:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020">Phi 3:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#021">Phi 3:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014">Th1 4:14-17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) His coming has a threefold relation: to the church, to Israel, to the nations. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) To the church the descent of the Lord into the air to raise the sleeping and change the living saints is set forth as a constant expectation and hope (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#036">Mat 24:36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#044">Mat 24:44</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#048">Mat 24:48-51</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#013">Mat 25:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#051">Co1 15:51</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052">Co1 15:52</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020">Phi 3:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#010">Th1 1:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014">Th1 4:14-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#014">Ti1 6:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#013">Tit 2:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#020">Rev 22:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) To Israel, the return of the Lord is predicted to accomplish the yet unfulfilled prophecies of her national regathering, conversion, and establishment in peace and power under the Davidic Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Act 15:14-17</a>) with (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001">Zac 14:1-9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Kingdom (Old Testament)" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008">Sa2 7:8-17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#008">Zac 13:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#024">Co1 15:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (c) To the Gentile nations, the return of Christ is predicted to bring the destruction of the present political world-system (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#034">Dan 2:34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#035">Dan 2:35</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the judgment of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Mat 25:31-46</a>) followed by world-wide Gentile conversion and participation in the blessings of the kingdom; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#002">Isa 2:2-4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010">Isa 11:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#003">Isa 60:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#003">Zac 8:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#020">Zac 8:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#023">Zac 8:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#016">Zac 14:16-21</a>).
