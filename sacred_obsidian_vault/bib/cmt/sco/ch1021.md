 <p>
 </p><p>six hundred shekels of gold 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>A discrepancy has been imagined in the two accounts, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2024.htm#024">Sa2 24:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#025">Ch1 21:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2024.htm#024">Sa2 24:24</a>) records the price of the threshingfloor (Hebrew, "goren"); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#025">Ch1 21:25</a>) of the place (Hebrew, "magom", Literally, "home," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1002.htm#020">Sa1 2:20</a>) same word or area on which afterward the great temple, with its spacious courts was built. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2003.htm#001">Ch2 3:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>David gave fifty shekels of sliver for the "goren"; six hundred shekels of gold for the "magom."
