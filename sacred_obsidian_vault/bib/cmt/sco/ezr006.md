 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#001">Ezra Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#006">Ezra 6:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ezr 6:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>your companions 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Chaldean, "their societies."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#014">Ezra 6:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">ezr 6:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>finished it 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The worship of Jehovah was thus re-established in Jerusalem, but the theocracy was not restored. The remnant which returned from the Babylonian captivity lived in the land by Gentile sufferance, though doubtless by the providential care of Jehovah, till Messiah came, and was crucified by soldiers of the fourth Gentile world-empire Rome, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#040">Dan 2:40</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#007">Dan 7:7</a>). Soon after (A.D. 70) Rome destroyed the city and temple. See "Times of the Gentiles"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024">Luk 21:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#019">Rev 16:19</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#015">Ezra 6:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">ezr 6:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Adar 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>twelfth monththat is, March.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#019">Ezra 6:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">ezr 6:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>first month 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, April.
