 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#001">Micah Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#005">Micah 7:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">mic 7:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>trust 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#007">Micah 7:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">mic 7:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Therefore 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#007">Mic 7:7-20</a>) is, primarily, the confession and intercession of the prophet, who identifies himself with Israel. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#003">Dan 9:3-19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Intercession was a test of the prophetic office; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer027.htm#018">Jer 27:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen020.htm#007">Gen 20:7</a>) But Micah's prayer voices also the heart exercise of the remnant in the last days. Such is prophecy, an intermingling of the near and the far. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psa 22:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#046">Mat 27:46</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#018">Micah 7:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">mic 7:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>remnant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#021">Jer 15:21</a>).
