 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#006">Jeremiah 15:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jer 15:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>repenting 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#014">Zac 8:14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#011">Jeremiah 15:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">jer 15:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>the Lord said 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Remnant, of whom Jeremiah was the representative, are carefully distinguished from the unbelieving mass of the people. The coming captivity, which they must share, for they too have sinned (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#013">Jer 15:13</a>) though Jehovah's judgment upon the nation, will be but a purifying chastisement to them, and they receive a special promise (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#011">Jer 15:11</a>). Verses (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#015">Jer 15:15-18</a>) give the answer of the Remnant to verses (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#011">Jer 15:11-14</a>). Two things characterize the believing Remnant always-loyal to the word of God, and separation from those who mock at that word (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#016">Jer 15:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#017">Jer 15:17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#008">Rev 3:8-10</a>).
