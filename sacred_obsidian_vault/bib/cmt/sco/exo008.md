 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#023">Exodus 8:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">exo 8:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>division 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hebrew, peduth, translated, "reemption."; 
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#009">Psa 111:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#007">Psa 130:7</a>). It is, in type, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#014">Gal 6:14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#025">Exodus 8:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">exo 8:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>in the land 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The compromises proposed by Pharaoh are those urged upon Christians today. The first says in effect: "Be a Christian if you will, but not a 'narrow' one -- stay in Egypt." Invariably it ends in world-conformity, world-pleasing, and seeking the world's money for God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#009">Psa 50:9-17</a>) (Cf); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014">Co2 6:14-18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#004">Gal 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#028">Exodus 8:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">exo 8:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>very far 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The second compromise is a modification merely of the first. "Do not be too unwordly." Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#003">Sa1 15:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#009">Sa1 15:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#013">Sa1 15:13-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#019">Sa1 15:19-23</a>).
