 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num023.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 23</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num023.htm#007">Numbers 23:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">num 23:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>and said 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In the prophecies of Balaam God testifies on behalf of His people rather than (as usual) to them. It is the divine testimony to their standing as a redeemed people in view of the serpent "lifted up," and of the water from the smitten rock. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num021.htm#005">Num 21:5-9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num020.htm#011">Num 20:11</a>). Their state was morally bad, but this was a matter concerning the discipline of God, not His judgment. The interpretation of the prophecies is literal as to Israel, typical as to Christians. Through Christ "lifted up" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#014">Joh 3:14</a>) our standing is eternally secure and perfect, though our state may require the Father's discipline; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#030">Co1 11:30-32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#004">Co2 1:4-9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#010">Co2 1:10-13</a>) meantime, against all enemies, God is "for us." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#031">Rom 8:31</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num023.htm#019">Numbers 23:19</a></p>
