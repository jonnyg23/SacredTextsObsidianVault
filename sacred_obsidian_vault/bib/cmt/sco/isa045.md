 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 45</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#001">Isaiah 45:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 45:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>anointed 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The only instance where the word is applied to a Gentile. Nebuchadnezzar is called the "servant" of Jehovah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#009">Jer 25:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer027.htm#006">Jer 27:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer043.htm#010">Jer 43:10</a>) This, with the designation "My shepherd" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#028">Isa 44:28</a>) also a Messianic title, marks Cyrus as that startling exception, a Gentile type of Christ. The points are: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) both are irresistible conquerors of Israel's enemies. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#001">Isa 45:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#019">Rev 19:19-21</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) both are restorers of the holy city (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#028">Isa 44:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001">Zac 14:1-11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) through both is the name of the one true God glorified (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#006">Isa 45:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#028">Co1 15:28</a>). 
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#007">Isaiah 45:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 45:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>create evil 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hebrew, "ra" translated "sorrow," "wretchedness," "adversity," "afflictions," "calamities," but never translated sin. God created evil only in the sense that He made sorrow, wretchedness, etc, to be the sure fruits of sin.
