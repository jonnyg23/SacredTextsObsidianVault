 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#002">Isaiah 1:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 1:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Hear, O heavens 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The chapter, down to verse 23, states the case of Jehovah against Judah. Chastening, according to Deuteronomy 28, 29, had been visited upon Israel in the land (vs. 5-8 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#005">Isa 1:5-8</a>), and now the time of expulsion from the land is near. But just here Jehovah renews the promise of the Palestinian Covenant of future restoration and exaltation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#026">Isa 1:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#027">Isa 1:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#001">Isa 2:1-4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009">Isaiah 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 1:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>remnant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Remnant. See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa010.htm#020">Isa 10:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005">Rom 11:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005">Rom 11:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#013">Isaiah 1:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">isa 1:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Bring no more 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#001">Co2 8:1</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#026">Isaiah 1:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">isa 1:26</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>thy judges 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Under the kingdom the ancient method of administering the theocratic government over Israel is to be restored. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg002.htm#018">Jdg 2:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#028">Mat 19:28</a>).
