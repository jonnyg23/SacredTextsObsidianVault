 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#001">Luke Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#015">Luke 14:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">luk 14:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>kingdom 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See note, (See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#033">Mat 6:33</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#026">Luke 14:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">luk 14:26</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>hate 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>All terms which define the emotions or affections are comparative. Natural affection is to be, as compared with the believer's devotedness to Christ, as if it were hate. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#047">Mat 12:47-50</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>where Christ illustrates this principle in His own person. But in the Lord the natural affections are sanctified and lifted to the level of the divine love. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#026">Joh 19:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#027">Joh 19:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#025">Eph 5:25-28</a>).
