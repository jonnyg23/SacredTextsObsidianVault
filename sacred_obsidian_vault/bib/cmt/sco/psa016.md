 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#009">Psalms 16:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 16:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>thy flesh 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The 16th Psalm is a prediction of the resurrection of the King. As a prophet David understood that, not at His first advent, but at some time subsequent to His death and resurrection Messiah would assume the Davidic throne. See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#025">Act 2:25-31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#032">Luk 1:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#033">Luk 1:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#013">Act 15:13-17</a>). See "Davidic Covenant," 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#014">Sa2 7:14</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Kingdom (Old Testament)," 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Psalm 22, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
