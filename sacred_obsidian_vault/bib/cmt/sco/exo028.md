 </p><p>Aaron 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Type of Christ, our High Priest. Christ is a priest after the order of Melchizedek, but He executes his priestly office after the pattern of Aaron. Hebrew, 7. gives the order; Hebrew, 9, the pattern. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018">Gen 14:18</a>).

 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#003">Zac 8:3</a>).

 <p>
 </p><p>Urim and Thummim 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Urim and Thummim, meaning "lights and perfections." Some make these to be simply a collective name for the stones of the breastplate, so that the total effect of the twelve stones is to manifest the "lights and perfections" of Him who is the antitype of the Aaronic high priest. Per contra, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev008.htm#008">Lev 8:8</a>). This would seem to be conclusive that "the Urim and Thummim" are additional to the stones of the breastplate. In use the Urim and Thummim were connected, in some way not clearly expressed, with the ascertainment of the divine will in particular cases; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num027.htm#021">Num 27:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#008">Deu 33:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1028.htm#006">Sa1 28:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr002.htm#063">Ezr 2:63</a>).
