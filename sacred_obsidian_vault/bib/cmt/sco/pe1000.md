 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#001">1 Peter</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">pe1 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - 1 Peter
 </p><p>Writer: The Apostle Peter (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#001">Pe1 1:1</a>). 
 </p><p>Date: Probably A.D. 60. That "Babylon" refers to the former city on the Euphrates, or to Rome, cannot be inferred from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#013">Pe1 5:13</a>. The text is obscure. 
 </p><p>Theme: While Peter undoubtedly has scattered Jewish believers in mind, his Epistles comprehend Gentile believers also (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#010">Pe1 2:10</a>). The present Epistle, written from a church on Gentile ground (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#013">Pe1 5:13</a>), presents all the foundational truths of the Christian faith, with special emphasis on the atonement. The distinctive note of First Peter is preparation for victory over suffering. The last-name word occurs about fifteen times, and is the key-word to the Epistle. 
 </p><p>The Epistle is in three parts: 
 </p><p>1. Christian suffering and conduct in the light of full salvation (1 Peter 1:1 - 2:8) 
 </p><p>2. The believer's life in view of his sevenfold position, and of the vicarious suffering of Christ (1 Peter 2:9 - 4:19). 
 </p><p>3. Christian service in the light of the coming of the Chief Shepherd (1 Peter 5:1-14).
