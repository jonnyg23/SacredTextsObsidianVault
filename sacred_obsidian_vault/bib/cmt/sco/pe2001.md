 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#001">2 Peter Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#002">2 Peter 1:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pe2 1:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>grace 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Grace (imparted). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#001">Rom 6:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2003.htm#018">Pe2 3:18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#004">2 Peter 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pe2 1:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "kosmos", means "world-system". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#020">Pe2 2:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#007">Joh 7:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#008">Rev 13:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#009">2 Peter 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pe2 1:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>sins 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Sin 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#023">Rom 3:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#018">2 Peter 1:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pe2 1:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>holy mount 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Where the reference is to things, the meaning of "holy" or "sanctified" is, simply, set apart for the use of God, or rendered sacred by the divine presence.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#019">2 Peter 1:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pe2 1:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>whereunto 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, made more sure by fulfilment in part. Fulfilled prophecy is a proof of inspiration because the Scripture predictions of future events were uttered so long before the events transpired that no merely human sagacity or foresight could have anticipated them, and these predictions are so detailed, minute, and specific, as to exclude the possibility that they were mere fortunate guesses. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hundreds of predictions concerning Israel, the land of Canaan, Babylon, Assyria, Egypt, and numerous personages -- so ancient, so singular, so seemingly improbable, as well as so detailed and definite that no mortal could have anticipated them -- have been fulfilled by the elements, and by men who were ignorant of them, or who utterly disbelieved them, or who struggled with frantic desperation to avoid their fulfilment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It is certain, therefore, that the Scriptures which contain them are inspired. "Prophecy came not in olden time by the will of man; but holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#021">Pe2 1:21</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>a more sure 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, the word of prophecy made more sure. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>dark place 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, squalid place. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#105">Psa 119:105</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#004">Joh 1:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#009">Joh 1:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#020">2 Peter 1:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pe2 1:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>any private 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Its own interpretation; that is, not isolated from all that the Word has given elsewhere.
