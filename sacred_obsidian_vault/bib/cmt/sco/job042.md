 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#001">Job Chapter 42</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#006">Job 42:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">job 42:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Wherefore I abhor myself 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The problem, of which the book of Job is the profound discussion, finds here its solution. Brought into the presence of God, Job is revealed to himself. In no sense a hypocrite, but godly and possessing a faith which all his afflictions could not shake, Job was yet self-righteous and lacking in humility. Chapter 29 fully discloses this. But in the presence of God he anticipates, as it were, the experience of Paul. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#004">Phi 3:4-9</a>) and the problem is solved. The godly are afflicted that they may be brought to self-knowledge and self-judgment. Such afflictions are not penal for their sins, but remedial and purifying. The book of Job affords a sublime illustration of the truth announced in; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#031">Co1 11:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#032">Co1 11:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#007">Heb 12:7-11</a>). Best of all, such self-knowledge and self-judgment is the prelude to greater fruitfulness. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#007">Job 42:7-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#002">Joh 15:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#013">Jos 5:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#014">Jos 5:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#028">Eze 1:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze002.htm#001">Eze 2:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan010.htm#005">Dan 10:5-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#017">Rev 1:17-19</a>). 
 </p><p>
