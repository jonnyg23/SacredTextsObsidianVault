 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Micah Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Micah 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mic 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>mountain 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>General predictions concerning the kingdom. In Scripture a mountain is the symbol of a great earth power (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#035">Dan 2:35</a>); hills, of smaller power. The prediction asserts 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) the ultimate establishment of the kingdom, with Jerusalem for the capital (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Mic 4:1</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) the universality of the future kingdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#002">Mic 4:2</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) its character -- peace (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#003">Mic 4:3</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) its effect -- prosperity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#004">Mic 4:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#004">Mic 4:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#001">Isa 2:1-5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#001">Isa 11:1-12</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>it shall be exalted 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "remnant" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#021">Jer 15:21</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#010">Micah 4:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">mic 4:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>redeem 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hebrew, "goel," Redemption (Kinsman type). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa059.htm#020">Isa 59:20</a>).
