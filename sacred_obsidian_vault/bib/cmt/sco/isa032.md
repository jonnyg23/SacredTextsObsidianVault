 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa032.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 32</a></h3> 
 <p>
 </p><p>king 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#003">Isa 29:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In chapters 32-35, the same blended meanings of near and far fulfilments are found. The near view is still of Sennacherib's invasion, the far view of the day of the Lord. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>) and the kingdom blessing to follow.
