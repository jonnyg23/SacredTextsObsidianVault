 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 22</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psalms 22:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 22:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Aijeleth Shahar 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, Ay-ys-leth Shachar, "hind of the morning," a title, not a musical instrument. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>My God, My God 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Psalms 22, 23, and 24 form a trilogy. In Psalm 22, the good Shepherd gives His life for the sheep (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#011">Joh 10:11</a>); in Psalm 23 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#001">Psa 23:1-6</a>), the great Shepherd, "brought again from the dead through the blood of the everlasting covenant." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#020">Heb 13:20</a>) tenderly cares for the sheep; in Psalm 24 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001">Psa 24:1-10</a>), the chief Shepherd appears as King of glory to own and reward the sheep (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#004">Pe1 5:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#007">Psalms 22:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 22:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>All they that see me 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Psalm 22 is a graphic picture of death by crucifixion. The bones (of the hands, arms, shoulders, and pelvis) out of joint (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014">Psa 22:14</a>) the profuse perspiration caused by intense suffering (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014">Psa 22:14</a>); the action of the heart affected (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014">Psa 22:14</a>); strength exhausted, and extreme thirst (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#015">Psa 22:15</a>); the hands and feet pierced (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#016">Psa 22:16</a>) partial nudity with hurt to modesty (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#017">Psa 22:17</a>), are all incidental to that mode of death. The accompanying circumstances are precisely those fulfilled in the crucifixion of Christ. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014">Psa 22:14-17</a>). The desolate cry of; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psa 22:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#046">Mat 27:46</a>); the periods of light and darkness of; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#002">Psa 22:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#045">Mat 27:45</a>) the contumely of; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#006">Psa 22:6-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#012">Psa 22:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#013">Psa 22:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#018">Psa 22:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#029">Mat 27:29-43</a>) the casting lots of verse (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#018">Psa 22:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#035">Mat 27:35</a>), all were literally fulfilled. When it is remembered that crucifixion was a Roman, not Jewish form of execution, the proof of inspiration is irresistible.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#022">Psalms 22:22</a></p>

 <p>
 </p><p>I will declare 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>At verse 22 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#022">Psa 22:22</a>), the Psalm breaks from crucifixion to resurrection; fulfilled in the "Go to my brethren," etc, of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#017">Joh 20:17</a>). The risen Christ declares to His brethren the name, "Father."

 <p>
 </p><p>For the kingdom is 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#030">Psa 22:30</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The kingdom is Jehovah's. In verse 30 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#030">Psa 22:30</a>), Adonai is in view as ruling on behalf of Jehovah. See Psalm 110 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#001">Psa 110:1-7</a>) with (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#042">Mat 22:42-45</a>). The great end and object of the rule of Adonai (Lord) is the restoration of the kingdom to Jehovah (Lord).
