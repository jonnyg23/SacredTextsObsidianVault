 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#001">Hebrews Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#008">Hebrews 8:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">heb 8:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>    The New Covenant, Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) "Better" than the Mosaic covenant not morally, but efficaciously (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#019">Heb 7:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#003">Rom 8:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#004">Rom 8:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Established on "better" (that is, unconditional) promises. In the Mosaic Covenant, God said, "If ye will"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#005">Exo 19:5</a>); in the New Covenant, He says, "I will"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#010">Heb 8:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#012">Heb 8:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Under the Mosaic Covenant, obedience sprang from fear; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#002">Heb 2:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#025">Heb 12:25-27</a>); under the New Covenant, from a willing heart and mind. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#010">Heb 8:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) The New Covenant secures the personal revelation of the Lord to every believer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#011">Heb 8:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) the complete oblivion of sins (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#012">Heb 8:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#017">Heb 10:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#003">Heb 10:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) rests upon an accomplished redemption (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#027">Mat 26:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#028">Mat 26:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#025">Co1 11:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#011">Heb 9:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#012">Heb 9:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#018">Heb 9:18-23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) and secures the perpetuity, future conversion, and blessing of Israel. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer031.htm#031">Jer 31:31-40</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See also "Kingdom (Old Testament)," and (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008">Sa2 7:8-17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The New Covenant is the eighth, thus speaking of resurrection and of eternal completeness. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>I. The Eight Covenants, Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The Edenic Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026">Gen 1:26</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>conditioned the life of man in innocency. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) The Adamic Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#014">Gen 3:14</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>conditions the life of fallen man and gives promise of a Redeemer. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The Noahic Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#001">Gen 9:1</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>establishes the principle of human government. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) The Abrahamic Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#018">Gen 15:18</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>founds the nation of Israel, and confirms, with specific additions, the Adamic promise of redemption. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) The Mosaic Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#025">Exo 19:25</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>condemns all men, "for that all have sinned." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) The Palestinian Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#003">Deu 30:3</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>secures the final restoration and conversion of Israel. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) The Davidic Covenant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#016">Sa2 7:16</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>establishes the perpetuity of the Davidic family (fulfilled in Christ), (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001">Mat 1:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#003">Rom 1:3</a>) and of the Davidic kingdom, over Israel, and over the whole earth; to be fulfilled in and by Christ. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008">Sa2 7:8-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Act 15:14-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#024">Co1 15:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(8) The New Covenant rests upon the sacrifice of Christ, and secures the eternal blessedness, under the Abrahamic Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#013">Gal 3:13-29</a>) of all who believe. It is absolutely unconditional, and, since no responsibility is by it committed to man, it is final and irreversible. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>II. The relation of Christ to the eight covenants is as follows: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) To the Edenic Covenant, Christ, as the "second Man," the "last Adam" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#045">Co1 15:45-47</a>) takes the place over all things which the first Adam lost. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#010">Col 2:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#007">Heb 2:7-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) He is the "Seed of the woman" of the Adamic Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015">Gen 3:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#031">Joh 12:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#008">Jo1 3:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#004">Gal 4:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#010">Rev 20:10</a>) and fulfilled its conditions of toil (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#003">Mar 6:3</a>) and obedience. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) As the greatest son of Shem, in Him was fulfilled supremely the promise to Shem in the Noahic Covenant. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#001">Gen 9:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#009">Col 2:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) He is the "Seed to whom the promises were made" in the Abrahamic Covenant; the son of Abraham obedient unto death. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#018">Gen 22:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#016">Gal 3:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#008">Phi 2:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) He lived sinlessly under the Mosaic Covenant and bore for us its curse. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#010">Gal 3:10-13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) He lived obediently as a Jew in the land under the Palestinian Covenant, and will yet perform it gracious promises. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#001">Deu 28:1-30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#009">Deu 28:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) He is the "Seed," "Heir," and "King" under the Davidic Covenant. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001">Mat 1:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(8) His sacrifice is the foundation of the New Covenant. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#028">Mat 26:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#025">Co1 11:25</a>).
