 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev013.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 13</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev013.htm#002">Leviticus 13:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">lev 13:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Leprosy 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Leprosy speaks of sin as 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>1. in the blood; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>2. becoming overt in loathsome ways; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>3. incurable by human means. The anti-type as applied to the people of God is "sin," demanding self-judgment (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#031">Co1 11:31</a>) and "sins," demanding confession and cleansing. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#009">Jo1 1:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev013.htm#003">Leviticus 13:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">lev 13:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>priest 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Some have found in the regulations of this chapter concerning an inquest by the priest of a case of leprosy, elaborate provisions for the exercise of discipline in the local church. No little self-righteousness and cruelty have come in thereby. The explicit instructions of the New Testament are the alone and sufficient rule of discipline.
