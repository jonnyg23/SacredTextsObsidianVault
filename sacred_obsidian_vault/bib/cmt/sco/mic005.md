 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#001">Micah Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#001">Micah 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mic 5:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Now 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "word of the Lord that came to Micah" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Mic 4:1</a>) having described the future kingdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Mic 4:1-8</a>) and glanced at the Babylonian captivities (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#009">Mic 4:9-10</a>) goes forward into the last days to refer to the great battle 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(see "Armageddon,") (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014">Rev 16:14</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017">Rev 19:17</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>which immediately precedes the setting up of the Messianic kingdom 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(see "Kingdom (Old Testament)," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026">Gen 1:26</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>also, "Kingdom (New Testament), (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#028">Co1 15:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#001">Mic 5:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#002">Mic 5:2</a>) forms a parenthesis in which the "word of the Lord" goes back from the time of the great battle (yet future) to the birth and rejection of the King, Messiah-Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#024">Mat 27:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#025">Mat 27:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#037">Mat 27:37</a>). This is followed by the statement that He will "give them up until the time that she which travaileth hath brought forth" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#003">Mic 5:3</a>). There is a twofold "travail" of Israel: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) that which brings forth the "man child" (Christ) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#001">Rev 12:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#002">Rev 12:2</a>) and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) that which, in the last days, brings forth a believing "remnant" out of the still dispersed and unbelieving nation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#003">Mic 5:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer030.htm#006">Jer 30:6-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#010">Mic 4:10</a>). Both aspects are combined in Isaiah 66. In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#007">Mic 5:7</a>). we have the "man-child" (Christ) of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#001">Rev 12:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#002">Rev 12:2</a>) in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#008">Mic 5:8-24</a>) the remnant, established in kingdom blessing. The meaning of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#003">Mic 5:3</a>) is that, from the rejection of Christ at His first coming Jehovah will give Israel up till the believing remnant appears; then He stands and feeds in His proper strength as Jehovah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#004">Mic 5:4</a>); He is the defence of His people as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#003">Mic 4:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#011">Mic 4:11-13</a>) and afterward the remnant go as missionaries to Israel and to all the world. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#007">Mic 5:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#008">Mic 5:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#023">Zac 8:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#002">Micah 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">mic 5:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>everlasting 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#013">Isa 7:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#014">Isa 7:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#006">Isa 9:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#007">Isa 9:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "child" was born in Bethlehem, but the "Son" was "from everlasting."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#003">Micah 5:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">mic 5:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>remnant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#021">Jer 15:21</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#007">Micah 5:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">mic 5:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>remnant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The ministry of the Jewish remnant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009">Isa 1:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005">Rom 11:5</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>has a twofold aspect, "a dew from the Lord"; "a lion among the beasts." Turning to the Lord in the great tribulation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#005">Psa 2:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#014">Rev 7:14</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the remnant takes up the beautiful gospel of the kingdom. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#006">Rev 14:6</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and proclaims it under awful persecution "unto all nations, for a witness." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#014">Mat 24:14</a>). The result is seen in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#004">Rev 7:4-14</a>) This is the "dew" aspect, and is followed by the "day of the Lord"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>). In the morning of which the kingdom is set up in power. Again there is a world-wide preaching to Jew and Gentile, but now it is the word that the King is on His holy hill of Zion (Psalm 2), and the unrepentant will be broken with His rod of iron. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6-9</a>). The preaching is given in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#010">Psa 2:10-12</a>). This is the "lion" aspect of the remnant's testimony. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#026">Rev 2:26-28</a>). The full kingdom-age of blessing follows the "rod of iron" aspect.
