 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#002">Exodus 12:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">exo 12:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>first month 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, April.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#011">Exodus 12:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 12:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>passover 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Passover, type of Christ our Redeemer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#001">Exo 12:1-28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#029">Joh 1:29</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#006">Co1 5:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#007">Co1 5:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#018">Pe1 1:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#019">Pe1 1:19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The lamb must be without blemish, and to test this it was kept up for four days (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#005">Exo 12:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#006">Exo 12:6</a>). So our Lord's public life, under hostile scrutiny, was the testing which proved his holiness; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#053">Luk 11:53</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#054">Luk 11:54</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#046">Joh 8:46</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#038">Joh 18:38</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) The Lamb thus tested must be slain (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#006">Exo 12:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#024">Joh 12:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#022">Heb 9:22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The blood must be applied (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#007">Exo 12:7</a>) This answers to appropriation by personal faith, and refutes universalism (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#036">Joh 3:36</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) The blood thus applied of itself, without anything, constituted a perfect protection from judgment (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#013">Exo 12:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#007">Jo1 1:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#010">Heb 10:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#014">Heb 10:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) The feast typified Christ as the bread of life, answering to the memorial supper. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#026">Mat 26:26-28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#023">Co1 11:23-26</a>). To observe the feast was a duty and privilege, but not a condition of safety. As a matter of fact, the bread was not eaten by the Israelites on the night in which, nevertheless, they were preserved from the judgment upon the firstborn. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#034">Exo 12:34-39</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#038">Exodus 12:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">exo 12:38</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>mixed 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This mixed multitude, standing for unconverted church-members, was a source of weakness and division, then as now (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num011.htm#004">Num 11:4-6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num011.htm#004">Num 11:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>There had been a manifestation of divine power, and men were drawn to it without change of heart. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#025">Luk 14:25-27</a>).
