 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#003">Isaiah 26:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 26:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>trusteth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019">Isaiah 26:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">isa 26:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>thy dead men 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Eliminate the supplied words, men, and, together with. "Body" is in the plural, "bodies." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019">Isa 26:19-21</a>) with chapter 27, constitute Jehovah's answer to the plaint of Israel, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#011">Isa 26:11-18</a>). Verse 19 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019">Isa 26:19</a>) should read: "Thy dead shall live: my dead bodies shall rise" (that is, the dead bodies of Jehovah's people). The restoration and re-establishment of Israel as a nation is also spoken of as a resurrection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#001">Eze 37:1-11</a>) and many hold that no more than this is meant in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019">Isa 26:19</a>). But since the first resurrection is unto participation in the kingdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#004">Rev 20:4-6</a>) it seems the better view that both meanings are here.
