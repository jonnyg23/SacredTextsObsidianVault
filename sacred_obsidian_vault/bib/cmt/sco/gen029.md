 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen029.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 29</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen029.htm#001">Genesis 29:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">gen 29:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>and came into the land 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jacob at Haran becomes a sterling illustration, if not type, of the nation descended from him in its present long dispersion. Like Israel, he was: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Out of the place of blessing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#003">Gen 26:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) without an altar (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos003.htm#004">Hos 3:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos003.htm#005">Hos 3:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) gained an evil name (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen031.htm#001">Gen 31:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#017">Rom 2:17-24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) but was under the covenant care of Jehovah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#013">Gen 28:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#014">Gen 28:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#001">Rom 11:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#025">Rom 11:25-30</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) and was ultimately brought back (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen031.htm#003">Gen 31:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen035.htm#001">Gen 35:1-4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#021">Eze 37:21-23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The personal lesson is obvious: while Jacob is not forsaken, he is permitted to reap the shame and sorrow of his self-chosen way.
