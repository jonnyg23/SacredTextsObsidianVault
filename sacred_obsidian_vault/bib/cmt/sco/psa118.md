 </p><p>O give thanks unto the Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Messianic Psalms: Summary. That the Psalms contain a testimony to Christ our Lord Himself affirmed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#044">Luk 24:44</a>) and the New Testament quotations from the Psalter point unerringly to those Psalms which have the Messianic character. A close spiritual and prophetic character as surely identifies others. Christ is seen in the Psalms 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) in two general character, as suffering (for example Psa. 22), and as entering into His kingdom glory (for example Psalms 2; 24.) Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#025">Luk 24:25-27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Christ is seen in His person 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) as Son of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#007">Psa 2:7</a>) and very God; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#006">Psa 45:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#007">Psa 45:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#025">Psa 102:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#001">Psa 110:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) as Son of man (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#004">Psa 8:4-6</a>)
 </p><p>    (c) as Son of David (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#003">Psa 89:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#004">Psa 89:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#027">Psa 89:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#029">Psa 89:29</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Christ is seen in His offices 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) as Prophet (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#022">Psa 22:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#025">Psa 22:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#009">Psa 40:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#010">Psa 40:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) as Priest (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#004">Psa 110:4</a>) and
 </p><p>    (c) as King (for example Psa 2, 24.) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Christ is seen in His varied work. As Priest He offers Himself in sacrifice (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psa 22:1-31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#006">Psa 40:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#005">Heb 10:5-12</a>) and, in resurrection, as the Priest-Shepherd, ever living to make intercession; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#001">Psa 23:1-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#021">Heb 7:21-25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#020">Heb 13:20</a>). As Prophet He proclaims the name of Jehovah as Father; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#022">Psa 22:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#017">Joh 20:17</a>). As King He fulfils the Davidic Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#001">Psa 89:1-52</a>) and restores alike the dominion of man over creation; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#004">Psa 8:4-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#017">Rom 8:17-21</a>) and of the Father over all. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#025">Co1 15:25-28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) The Messianic Psalms give, also, the inner thoughts, the exercises of soul, of Christ in His earthly experiences. (See, e.g, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#008">Psa 16:8-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psa 22:1-2140</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psa 22:1-17</a>).
