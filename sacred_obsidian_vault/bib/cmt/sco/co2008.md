 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#001">2 Corinthians Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#001">2 Corinthians 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">co2 8:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Moreover 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In 2 Corintians 8-9, the apostle sums up the Christian doctrine of giving. It may be thus summarized: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) It is a "grace," that is, a disposition created by the Spirit. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#007">Co2 8:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) In contrast with the law, which imposed giving as a divine requirement, Christian giving is voluntary, and a test of sincerity and love. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#008">Co2 8:8-12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#001">Co2 9:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#002">Co2 9:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#005">Co2 9:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#007">Co2 9:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The privilege is universal, belonging, according to ability, to rich and poor. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#001">Co2 8:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#012">Co2 8:12-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#001">Co1 16:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#002">Co1 16:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Giving is to be proportioned to income. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#012">Co2 8:12-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#002">Co1 16:2</a>). The Old Testament proportion was the tithe, a proportion which antedates the law. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#020">Gen 14:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) The rewards of Christian giving are: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) joy. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#002">Co2 8:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) increased ability to give in proportion to that which has been already given. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#007">Co2 9:7-11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (c) increased thankfulness to God. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#012">Co2 9:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (d) God and the Gospel glorified. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#013">Co2 9:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#014">Co2 9:14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#012">2 Corinthians 8:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">co2 8:12</a></font></span></p>
