 <p>
 </p><p>Groves 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Groves, like high places, have been associated with idolatrous worship from time immemorial. The Hebrew, asherah, translated, grove means also the idol enshrined there. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#021">Deu 16:21</a>). This idol seems often to have been a sacred tree, the figure of which is constantly found on Assyrian monuments. In apostate Israel, however, such groves were associated with every form of idolatry, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#016">Kg2 17:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#017">Kg2 17:17</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1003.htm#002">Kg1 3:2</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and Ashtaroth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg002.htm#013">Jdg 2:13</a>).
