 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#001">Galatians Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#003">Galatians 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">gal 4:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "kosmos", means "mankind". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#008">Mat 4:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#019">Galatians 4:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">gal 4:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>little children 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The allegory (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#022">Gal 4:22-31</a>) is addressed to justified but immature believers (compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#001">Co1 3:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#002">Co1 3:2</a>) who, under the influence of legalistic teachers, "desire to be under the law," and has, therefore, no application to a sinner seeking justification. It raises and answers, for the fifth time in this Epistle, the question, Is the believer under the law? (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#019">Gal 2:19-21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#001">Gal 3:1-33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#025">Gal 3:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#026">Gal 3:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#004">Gal 4:4-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#004">Gal 4:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#009">Gal 4:9-31</a>).
