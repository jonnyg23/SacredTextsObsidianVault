 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job008.htm#001">Job Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job008.htm#001">Job 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">job 8:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Bildad 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Bildad is a religious dogmatist of the superficial kind, whose dogmatism rests upon tradition (for example) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job008.htm#008">Job 8:8-10</a>) and upon proverbial wisdom and approved pious phrases. These abound in all his discourses. His platitudes are true enough, but then every one knows them. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job009.htm#001">Job 9:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job009.htm#002">Job 9:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job013.htm#002">Job 13:2</a>) nor do they shed any light on such a problem as Job's.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job008.htm#014">Job 8:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">job 8:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>trust 
 </p><p>
