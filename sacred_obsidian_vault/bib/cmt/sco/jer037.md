 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer037.htm#001">Jeremiah Chapter 37</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer037.htm#011">Jeremiah 37:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">jer 37:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And it came to pass 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Five phases of Jeremiah's prison experiences are recorded: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) He is arrested in the gate and committed to a dungeon on the false charge of treason (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer037.htm#011">Jer 37:11-15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) he is released from the dungeon, but restrained to the court of the prison; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) he is imprisoned in the miry dungeon and kept in prison court (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer038.htm#001">Jer 38:1-6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) he is again released from the dungeon and kept in the prison court (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer038.htm#013">Jer 38:13-28</a>) until the capture of the city; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) carried in chains from the city by Nebuzar-adan, captain of the guard, he is finally released at Ramah. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer040.htm#001">Jer 40:1-4</a>).
