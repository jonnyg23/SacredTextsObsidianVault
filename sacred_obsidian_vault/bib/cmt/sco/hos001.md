 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos001.htm#001">Hosea Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos001.htm#009">Hosea 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">hos 1:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>my people 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"My people" is an expression used in the Old Testament exclusively of Israel the nation. It is never used of the patriarchs, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#006">Mat 2:6</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos001.htm#010">Hosea 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">hos 1:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Israel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Israel" in Hosea means the ten tribes forming the northern kingdom as distinguished from "Judah" (the tribes of Judah and Benjamin) forming the southern kingdom which adhered to the Davidic family. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1012.htm#001">Kg1 12:1-21</a>). The promise of verse 10 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos001.htm#010">Hos 1:10</a>) awaits fulfilment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Israel"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#002">Gen 12:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#026">Rom 11:26</a>).
