 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac010.htm#001">Zechariah Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac010.htm#001">Zechariah 10:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">zac 10:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>later rain 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos006.htm#003">Hos 6:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe002.htm#023">Joe 2:23-32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#010">Zac 12:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>There is both a physical and spiritual meaning: Rain as of old will be restored to Palestine, but, also, there will be a mighty effusion of the Spirit upon restored Israel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac010.htm#004">Zechariah 10:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">zac 10:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>came 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The tense is future: "From him Judah shall be the cornerstone (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo017.htm#006">Exo 17:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#008">Pe1 2:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>from him the nail (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa022.htm#023">Isa 22:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa022.htm#024">Isa 22:24</a>) from him the battle-bow," etc. The whole scene is of the events which group about the deliverance of the Jews in Palestine in the time of the northern invasion under the "Beast"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#008">Dan 7:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>) and "Armageddon,"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014">Rev 16:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017">Rev 19:17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The final deliverance is wholly effected by the return of the Lord (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>); but previously He strengthens the hard-pressed Israelites; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#013">Mic 4:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#013">Zac 9:13-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac010.htm#005">Zac 10:5-7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#002">Zac 12:2-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#014">Zac 14:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That there may have been a precursive fulfilment in the Maccabean victories can neither be affirmed nor denied from Scripture.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac010.htm#008">Zechariah 10:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">zac 10:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>redeemed 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa059.htm#020">Isa 59:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#030">Exo 14:30</a>). 
 </p><p>
