 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#005">1 Chronicles 11:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">ch1 11:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>castle of Zion 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hebrew, castle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Zion, the ancient Jebusite stronghold, is the southwest eminence in Jerusalem, called in Scripture the city of David, and associated with the Davidic royalty both historically and prophetically (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#007">Ch1 11:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#003">Isa 2:3</a>). The word is often used of the whole city of Jerusalem considered as the city of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#002">Psa 48:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#003">Psa 48:3</a>) especially in passages referring to the future kingdom-age; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#027">Isa 1:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#003">Isa 2:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#001">Isa 4:1-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#016">Joe 3:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac001.htm#016">Zac 1:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac001.htm#017">Zac 1:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#003">Zac 8:3-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#026">Rom 11:26</a>). In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#022">Heb 12:22</a>) the word is used symbolically of heaven. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu004.htm#048">Deu 4:48</a>) the name is given to a projection or peak of Mount Hermon.
