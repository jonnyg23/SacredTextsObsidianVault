 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#001">1 John 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jo1 3:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Greek, "kosmos", means "mankind". 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#008">Mat 4:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#004">1 John 3:4</a></p>

 </p><p>committeth sin 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Whosoever practiseth sin practiseth also lawlessness; and sin is lawlessness. 
 </p><p>

 </p><p>sin 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Sin

 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#007">1 John 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">jo1 3:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Righteousness" here, and in the passages having marginal references to this, means the righteous life which is the result of salvation through Christ. The righteous man under law became righteous by doing righteously; under grace he does righteously because he has been made righteous (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#022">Rom 3:22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#003">Rom 10:3</a>).
