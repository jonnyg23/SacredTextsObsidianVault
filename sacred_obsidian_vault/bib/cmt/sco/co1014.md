 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#001">1 Corinthians Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#001">1 Corinthians 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">co1 14:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>rather 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The subject is still the Greek, "pneumatika". Chapter 12 described the gifts and the Body; Chapter 13 described the love which alone gives ministry of gift any value; Chapter 14 regulates the ministry of gift in the primitive, apostolic assembly of saints. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The important gift is that of prophecy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#001">Co1 1:1</a>)). The New Testament prophet was not merely a preacher, but an inspired preacher, through whom, until the New Testament was written, new revelations suited to the new dispensation were given (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#029">Co1 14:29-30</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Tongues and the sign gifts are to cease, and meantime must be used with restraint, and only if an interpreter be present (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#001">Co1 14:1-19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#027">Co1 14:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#028">Co1 14:28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) In the primitive church there was liberty for the ministry of all the gifts which might be present, but for prophecy more especially (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#023">Co1 14:23-26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#031">Co1 14:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#039">Co1 14:39</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) In such meetings, when "the whole church" came together "in one place," women were required to keep silence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#034">Co1 14:34-35</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#003">Co1 11:3-16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#011">Ti1 2:11-14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) These injunctions are declared to be "the commandments of the Lord" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#036">Co1 14:36-37</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#002">1 Corinthians 14:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">co1 14:2</a></font></span></p>
