 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#001">Ecclesiastes Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#010">Ecclesiastes 9:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">ecc 9:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>there is no work 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Verse 10 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#010">Ecc 9:10</a>) is no more a divine revelation concerning the state of the dead than any other conclusion of "the Preacher" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc001.htm#001">Ecc 1:1</a>) is such a revelation. Reasoning from the standpoint of man "under the sun" the natural man can see no difference between a dead man and a dead lion. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#004">Ecc 9:4</a>). A living dog is better than either. No one would quote verse 2 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#002">Ecc 9:2</a>) as a divine revelation. These reasonings of man apart from divine revelation are set down by inspiration just as the words of Satan; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#004">Gen 3:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#004">Job 2:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#005">Job 2:5</a>) are so set down. But that life and consciousness continue between death and resurrection is directly affirmed in Scripture. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#009">Isa 14:9-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#032">Mat 22:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#043">Mar 9:43-48</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#019">Luk 16:19-31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#026">Joh 11:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#006">Co2 5:6-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#021">Phi 1:21-23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009">Rev 6:9-11</a>). 
 </p><p>
