 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#001">1 Corinthians Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#002">1 Corinthians 11:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">co1 11:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>ordinances 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Things delivered; often translated, "traditions".  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#003">1 Corinthians 11:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">co1 11:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>woman 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#016">Gen 3:16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The woman's veil, or head-covering, is a symbol of this subordination.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#010">1 Corinthians 11:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">co1 11:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>power 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Authority, that is, the sign of the husband's authority. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>angels 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, of the presence of the angels.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#027">1 Corinthians 11:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">co1 11:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>unworthily 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, in an unworthy manner. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#020">Co1 11:20-22</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#029">1 Corinthians 11:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">co1 11:29</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>damnation 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Judgment, in the sense of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#032">Co1 11:32</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#031">1 Corinthians 11:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">co1 11:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>judge 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Self-judgment is not so much the believer's moral condemnation of his own ways or habits, or of himself, for allowing such ways. Self-judgment avoids chastisement. If neglected, the Lord judges, and the result is chastisement, but never condemnation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#032">Co1 11:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#014">Sa2 7:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#015">Sa2 7:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2012.htm#013">Sa2 12:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2012.htm#014">Sa2 12:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#005">Co1 5:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#020">Ti1 1:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#007">Heb 12:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
