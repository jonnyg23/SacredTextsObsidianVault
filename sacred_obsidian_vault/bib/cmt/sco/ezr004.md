 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#001">Ezra Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#002">Ezra 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ezr 4:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>for we seek 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The people of the land sought to hinder the work in three ways: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) by seeking to draw the Jews into an unreal union, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#003">Ezr 4:3</a>). (cf) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#032">Kg2 17:32</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) by "weakening the hands of the people of Judah," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#004">Ezr 4:4</a>) i.e, by withholding supplies, etc.; and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) by accusations lodged with Ahasuerus and Darius. The first was by far the most subtle and dangerous. The lives of Ezra and Nehemiah afford many illustrations of true separation. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014">Co2 6:14-18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#019">Ti2 2:19-21</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#006">Ezra 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ezr 4:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Ahasuerus 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Cambyses of secular history (529-521 BC); not the Ahasuerus of Esther, who is the Xerxes of secular history (485 BC). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan005.htm#031">Dan 5:31</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#007">Ezra 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">ezr 4:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Artaxerxes 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Artaxerxes of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#007">Ezr 4:7</a>) is identical with Ahasuerus of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#006">Ezr 4:6</a>); that is, the Cambyses of profane history. The Artaxerxes of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr007.htm#001">Ezr 7:1</a>) is the Longimanus of secular history, BC 418. But 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan005.htm#031">Dan 5:31</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#021">Ezra 4:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">ezr 4:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Give 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Chaldean, make a new decree.
