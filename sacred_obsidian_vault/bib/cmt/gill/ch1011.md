 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">ch1 11:0</a></font></span></p>INTRODUCTION TO 1 CHRONICLES 11 
 <p>
 </p><p>This chapter treats of David's being anointed king by all Israel, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#001">Ch1 11:1</a>, which agrees with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#001">Sa2 5:1</a> See Gill on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#001">Sa2 5:1</a> See Gill on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#002">Sa2 5:2</a> See Gill on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#003">Sa2 5:3</a> and of his taking Jerusalem from the Jebusites, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#004">Ch1 11:4</a> the account of which we have <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#006">Sa2 5:6</a>. first, and so was made chief captain according to David's promise; and that he also repaired the rest of the city David built round about; perhaps the fortifications demolished in taking it, <a
