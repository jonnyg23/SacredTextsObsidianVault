Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#001">Isaiah 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 3:1</a></font></span></p>(Isa. 3:1-26)
 <p>
 </p><p>For--continuation of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#022">Isa 2:22</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Lord of hosts--therefore able to do as He says. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>doth--present for future, so certain is the accomplishment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>stay . . . staff--the same Hebrew word, the one masculine, the other feminine, an Arabic idiom for all kinds of support. What a change from the previous luxuries (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#007">Isa 2:7</a>)! Fulfilled in the siege by Nebuchadnezzar and afterwards by Titus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer037.htm#021">Jer 37:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer038.htm#009">Jer 38:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#002">Isaiah 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 3:2</a></font></span></p>Fulfilled (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#014">Kg2 24:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>prudent--the Hebrew often means a "soothsayer" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu018.htm#010">Deu 18:10-14</a>); thus it will mean, the diviners, on whom they rely, shall in that day fail. It is found in a good sense (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#010">Pro 16:10</a>), from which passage the Jews interpret it a king; "without" whom Israel long has been (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos003.htm#004">Hos 3:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ancient--old and experienced (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1012.htm#006">Kg1 12:6-8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#003">Isaiah 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 3:3</a></font></span></p>captain of fifty--not only captains of thousands, and centurions of a hundred, but even semi-centurions of fifty, shall fail. 
 <p>
 </p><p>honourable--literally, "of dignified aspect." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>cunning--skilful. The mechanic's business will come to a standstill in the siege and subsequent desolation of the state; artisans are no mean "stay" among a nation's safeguards. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>eloquent orator--rather, as Vulgate, "skilled in whispering," that is, incantation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa058.htm#005">Psa 58:5</a>). See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#019">Isa 8:19</a>, below; and on "prudent," see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#002">Isa 3:2</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#004">Isaiah 3:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">isa 3:4</a></font></span></p>children--in ability for governing; antithesis to the "ancient" (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#012">Isa 3:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#016">Ecc 10:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>babes--in warlike might; antithesis to "the mighty" and "man of war."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#005">Isaiah 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 3:5</a></font></span></p>The anarchy resulting under such imbecile rulers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#004">Isa 3:4</a>); unjust exactions mutually; the forms of respect violated (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#032">Lev 19:32</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>base--low-born. Compare the marks of "the last days" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#002">Ti2 3:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#006">Isaiah 3:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 3:6</a></font></span></p>Such will be the want of men of wealth and ability, that they will "take hold of" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#001">Isa 4:1</a>) the first man whom they meet, having any property, to make him "ruler." 
 <p>
 </p><p>brother--one having no better hereditary claim to be ruler than the "man" supplicating him. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Thou hast clothing--which none of us has. Changes of raiment are wealth in the East (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2005.htm#005">Kg2 5:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ruin--Let our ruined affairs be committed to thee to retrieve.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#007">Isaiah 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 3:7</a></font></span></p>swear--literally, "lift up," namely, his hand; the gesture used in solemn attestation. Or, his voice, that is, answer; so Vulgate. 
 <p>
 </p><p>healer--of the body politic, incurably diseased (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#006">Isa 1:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>neither . . . clothing--so as to relieve the people and maintain a ruler's dignity. A nation's state must be bad indeed, when none among men, naturally ambitious, is willing to accept office.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#008">Isaiah 3:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 3:8</a></font></span></p>Reason given by the prophet, why all shrink from the government. 
 <p>
 </p><p>eyes of his glory--to provoke His "glorious" Majesty before His "eyes" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#005">Isa 49:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#013">Hab 1:13</a>). The Syriac and LOWTH, by a slight change of the Hebrew, translate, "the cloud of His glory," the Shekinah.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#009">Isaiah 3:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 3:9</a></font></span></p>show--The Hebrew means, "that which may be known by their countenances" [GESENIUS and WEISS]. But MAURER translates, "Their respect for person"; so Syriac and Chaldee. But the parallel word "declare" favors the other view. KIMCHI, from the Arabic, translates "their hardness" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job019.htm#003">Job 19:3</a>, Margin), or impudence of countenance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#003">Jer 3:3</a>). They have lost not only the substance of virtue, but its color. 
 <p>
 </p><p>witness--literally, "corresponds" to them; their look answers to their inner character (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos005.htm#005">Hos 5:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>declare-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#013">Jde 1:13</a>). "Foaming out their own shame"; so far from making it a secret, "glorying" in it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#019">Phi 3:19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>unto themselves--Compare "in themselves" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#031">Pro 1:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#036">Pro 8:36</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#019">Jer 2:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#027">Rom 1:27</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#010">Isaiah 3:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">isa 3:10</a></font></span></p>The faithlessness of many is no proof that all are faithless. Though nothing but croaking of frogs is heard on the surface of the pool, we are not to infer there are no fish beneath [BENGEL]. (See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#019">Isa 1:19-20</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>fruit of doings-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#031">Pro 1:31</a>) in a good sense (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#008">Gal 6:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#014">Rev 22:14</a>). Not salvation by works, but by fruit-bearing faith (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#024">Isa 45:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer023.htm#006">Jer 23:6</a>). GESENIUS and WEISS translate, Declare as to the righteous that, &amp;c. MAURER, "Say that the righteous is blessed."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#011">Isaiah 3:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">isa 3:11</a></font></span></p>ill--antithesis to "well" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#010">Isa 3:10</a>); emphatic ellipsis of the words italicized. "Ill!" 
 <p>
 </p><p>hands--his conduct; "hands" being the instrument of acts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc008.htm#012">Ecc 8:12-13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#012">Isaiah 3:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">isa 3:12</a></font></span></p>(See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#004">Isa 3:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>oppressors--literally, "exactors," that is, exacting princes (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#017">Isa 60:17</a>). They who ought to be protectors are exactors; as unqualified for rule as "children," as effeminate as "women." Perhaps it is also implied that they were under the influence of their harem, the women of their court. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>lead--Hebrew, "call thee blessed"; namely, the false prophets, who flatter the people with promises of safety in sin; as the political "rulers" are meant in the first clause. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>way of thy paths-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer006.htm#016">Jer 6:16</a>). The right way set forth in the law. "Destroy"--Hebrew, "Swallow up," that is, cause so utterly to disappear that not a vestige of it is left.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#013">Isaiah 3:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">isa 3:13</a></font></span></p>standeth up--no longer sitting in silence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>plead--indignant against a wicked people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#016">Isa 66:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze020.htm#035">Eze 20:35</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#014">Isaiah 3:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">isa 3:14</a></font></span></p>ancients--Hence they are spoken of as "taken away" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#001">Isa 3:1-2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>vineyard--the Jewish theocracy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#001">Isa 5:1-7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa080.htm#009">Psa 80:9-13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>eaten up--"burnt"; namely, by "oppressive exactions" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#012">Isa 3:12</a>). Type of the crowning guilt of the husbandmen in the days of Jesus Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#034">Mat 21:34-41</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>spoil . . . houses-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#014">Mat 23:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#015">Isaiah 3:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">isa 3:15</a></font></span></p>What right have ye to beat, &amp;c. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#005">Psa 94:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic003.htm#002">Mic 3:2-3</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>grind--by exactions, so as to leave them nothing. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>faces--persons; with the additional idea of it being openly and palpably done. "Presence," equivalent to "face" (Hebrew).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#016">Isaiah 3:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">isa 3:16</a></font></span></p>Because the daughters of Zion are haughty, &amp;c.--Luxury had become great in Uzziah's prosperous reign (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2026.htm#005">Ch2 26:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>stretched forth--proudly elevated (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#005">Psa 75:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>wanton--rather, "making the eyes to glance about," namely, wantonly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#013">Pro 6:13</a>) [MAURER]. But LOWTH, "falsely setting off the eyes with paint." Women's eyelids in the East are often colored with stibium, or powder of lead (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#014">Job 42:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer004.htm#030">Jer 4:30</a>, Margin). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>mincing--tripping with short steps. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>tinkling--with their ankle-rings on both feet, joined by small chains, which sound as they walk, and compel them to take short steps; sometimes little bells were attached (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#018">Isa 3:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#020">Isa 3:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#017">Isaiah 3:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">isa 3:17</a></font></span></p>smite with a scab--literally, "make bald," namely, by disease. 
 <p>
 </p><p>discover--cause them to suffer the greatest indignity that can befall female captives, namely to be stripped naked, and have their persons exposed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa047.htm#003">Isa 47:3</a>; compare with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa020.htm#004">Isa 20:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#018">Isaiah 3:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">isa 3:18</a></font></span></p>bravery--the finery. 
 <p>
 </p><p>tinkling--(See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#016">Isa 3:16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>cauls--network for the head. Or else, from an Arabic root, "little suns," answering to the "tires" or neck-ornaments, "like the moon" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg008.htm#021">Jdg 8:21</a>). The chumarah or crescent is also worn in front of the headdress in West Asia.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#019">Isaiah 3:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">isa 3:19</a></font></span></p>chains--rather, pendants, hanging about the neck, and dropping on the breast. 
 <p>
 </p><p>mufflers--veils covering the face, with apertures for the eyes, close above and loosely flowing below. The word radically means "tremulous," referring to the changing effect of the spangles on the veil.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#020">Isaiah 3:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">isa 3:20</a></font></span></p>bonnets--turbans. 
 <p>
 </p><p>ornaments of the legs--the short stepping-chains from one foot to another, to give a measured gait; attached to the "tinkling ornaments" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#016">Isa 3:16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>headbands--literally, "girdles." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>tablets--rather, "houses of the breath," that is, smelling boxes [Vulgate]. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>earrings--rather, amulets suspended from the neck or ears, with magic formulÃ&amp;brvbr; inscribed; the root means to "whisper" or "conjure."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#021">Isaiah 3:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">isa 3:21</a></font></span></p>nose jewels--The cartilage between the nostrils was bored to receive them; they usually hung from the left nostril.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#022">Isaiah 3:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">isa 3:22</a></font></span></p>Here begin entire articles of apparel. Those before were single ornaments. 
 <p>
 </p><p>changeable--from a root, "to put off"; not worn commonly; put on and off on special occasions. So, dress-clothes (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac003.htm#004">Zac 3:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>mantles--fuller tunics with sleeves, worn over the common one, reaching down to the feet. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>wimples--that is, mufflers, or hoods. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#015">Rut 3:15</a>, "veils"; perhaps here, a broad cloak, or shawl, thrown over the head and body. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>crisping pins--rather, money bags (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2005.htm#023">Kg2 5:23</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#023">Isaiah 3:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">isa 3:23</a></font></span></p>glasses--mirrors of polished metal (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#008">Exo 38:8</a>). But the Septuagint, a transparent, gauze-like, garment. 
 <p>
 </p><p>hoods--miters, or diadems (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa062.htm#003">Isa 62:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac003.htm#005">Zac 3:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>veils--large enough to cover the head and person. Distinct from the smaller veils ("mufflers") above (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#065">Gen 24:65</a>). Token of woman's subjection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#010">Co1 11:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#024">Isaiah 3:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">isa 3:24</a></font></span></p>stink--arising from ulcers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#012">Zac 14:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>girdle--to gird up the loose Eastern garments, when the person walked. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>rent--the Septuagint, better, a "rope," an emblem of poverty; the poor have nothing else to gird up their clothes with. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>well-set hair-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1003.htm#003">Pe1 3:3-4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>baldness-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#017">Isa 3:17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>stomacher--a broad plaited girdle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>sackcloth-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2003.htm#031">Sa2 3:31</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>burning--a sunburnt countenance, owing to their hoods and veils being stripped off, while they had to work as captives under a scorching sun (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#006">Sol 1:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#025">Isaiah 3:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">isa 3:25</a></font></span></p>Thy men--of Jerusalem.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#026">Isaiah 3:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">isa 3:26</a></font></span></p>gates--The place of concourse personified is represented mourning for the loss of those multitudes which once frequented it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>desolate . . . sit upon . . . ground--the very figure under which Judea was represented on medals after the destruction by Titus: a female sitting under a palm tree in a posture of grief; the motto, JudÃ&amp;brvbr;a capta (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#013">Job 2:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam002.htm#010">Lam 2:10</a>, where, as here primarily, the destruction by Nebuchadnezzar is alluded to).
 </p><p>
 </p><p>that day--the calamitous period described in previous chapter. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>seven--indefinite number among the Jews. So many men would be slain, that there would be very many more women than men; for example, seven women, contrary to their natural bashfulness, would sue to (equivalent to "take hold of," <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#006">Isa 3:6</a>) one man to marry them. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>eat . . . own bread--foregoing the privileges, which the law (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#010">Exo 21:10</a>) gives to wives, when a man has more than one. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>reproach--of being unwedded and childless; especially felt among the Jews, who were looking for "the seed of the woman," Jesus Christ, described in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#002">Isa 4:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa054.htm#001">Isa 54:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa054.htm#004">Isa 54:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#025">Luk 1:25</a>.
 </p><p>
