Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psalms 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 5:1</a></font></span></p>Upon Nehiloth--flutes or wind instruments. The writer begs to be heard, on the ground of God's regard for His covenant-people and true worshippers as contrasted with His holy hatred to the wicked. He prays for divine guidance, on account of his watchful, malignant, and deceitful enemies; and for their destruction as being also God's enemies. At the same time he expresses his confidence that God will extend aid to His people. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psa 5:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>meditation--moanings of that half-uttered form to which deep feeling gives rise--groanings, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#026">Rom 8:26-27</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#002">Psalms 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 5:2</a></font></span></p>Hearken--incline the ear (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#017">Psa 10:17</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#002">Psa 61:2</a>) --give close attention. 
 <p>
 </p><p>my cry--that is, for help (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#002">Psa 61:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer008.htm#019">Jer 8:19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my King--thus by covenant relation interested in my cause.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#003">Psalms 5:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 5:3</a></font></span></p>direct--literally, "set in order," as the showbread was placed or set in order (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo040.htm#023">Exo 40:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#004">Psalms 5:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 5:4</a></font></span></p>For, &amp;c.--God only regards sincere worshippers. 
 <p>
 </p><p>evil--or, "the evil man." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>dwell--lodge, remain under protection.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#005">Psalms 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 5:5</a></font></span></p>foolish--vainglorious and insolent. 
 <p>
 </p><p>iniquity--especially such as denotes a negation, or defect, that is, of moral principle.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#006">Psalms 5:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 5:6</a></font></span></p>leasing--a lie. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the bloody . . . man--literally, "man of blood"--murderer.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psalms 5:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 5:7</a></font></span></p>But--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>, literally, "and." 
 <p>
 </p><p>house-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1009.htm#023">Ch1 9:23</a>), the tabernacle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>temple--literally, "palace," applied to God's residence, the Holy of Holies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1003.htm#003">Sa1 3:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#007">Sa2 22:7</a>); the inner part of the tabernacle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>toward--not in; the high priest alone was allowed to enter.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#008">Psalms 5:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 5:8</a></font></span></p>enemies--literally, "watchers" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#011">Psa 27:11</a>), hence special need of guidance. 
 <p>
 </p><p>in thy righteousness--an attribute implying faithfulness in promises as well as threatenings. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>make thy way straight--that is, make the way of providence plain.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#009">Psalms 5:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 5:9</a></font></span></p>The wicked are not reliable because by nature they are full of wickedness, or literally, "wickednesses," of every kind (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#007">Rom 8:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>sepulchre--a dwelling-place of corruption, emitting moral putridness. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>flatter--or, "make smooth." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>their tongue--speaks deceitfully.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#010">Psalms 5:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 5:10</a></font></span></p>Destroy--or, "condemn" them to destruction as guilty.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psalms 5:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 5:11</a></font></span></p>defendest--(compare Margin). 
 <p>
 </p><p>love thy name--Thy manifested perfections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#010">Psa 9:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#012">Psalms 5:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 5:12</a></font></span></p>with favour--or, "acceptance," alluding to the favor shown to an acceptable offering and worshipper (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#018">Lev 7:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#007">Lev 19:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>shield--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#003">Psa 3:3</a>).
 </p><p>
