 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#001">Psalms 79:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 79:1</a></font></span></p>This Psalm, like the seventy-fourth, probably depicts the desolations of the Chaldeans (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer052.htm#012">Jer 52:12-24</a>). It comprises the usual complaint, prayer, and promised thanks for relief. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#001">Psa 79:1-13</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#002">Psa 74:2-7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#002">Psalms 79:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 79:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#003">Jer 15:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer016.htm#004">Jer 16:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#004">Psalms 79:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 79:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#013">Psa 44:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer042.htm#018">Jer 42:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam002.htm#015">Lam 2:15</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#005">Psalms 79:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 79:5</a></font></span></p>How long-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001">Psa 13:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>be angry-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#001">Psa 74:1-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>jealousy burn-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu029.htm#020">Deu 29:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#006">Psalms 79:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 79:6</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer010.htm#025">Jer 10:25</a>). Though we deserve much, do not the heathen deserve more for their violence to us (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer051.htm#003">Jer 51:3-5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac001.htm#014">Zac 1:14</a>)? The singular denotes the chief power, and the use of the plural indicates the combined confederates. 
 <p>
 </p><p>called upon--or, "by" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>thy name--proclaimed Thy attributes and professed allegiance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#004">Isa 12:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#021">Act 2:21</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#008">Psalms 79:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 79:8</a></font></span></p>former iniquities--literally, "iniquities of former times." 
 <p>
 </p><p>prevent us--literally, "meet us," as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa021.htm#003">Psa 21:3</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#009">Psalms 79:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 79:9</a></font></span></p>for . . . glory of thy name [and for] thy name's sake--both mean for illustrating Thy attributes, faithfulness, power, &amp;c. 
 <p>
 </p><p>purge . . . sins--literally, "provide atonement for us." Deliverance from sin and suffering, for their good and God's glory, often distinguish the prayers of Old Testament saints (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#007">Eph 1:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#010">Psalms 79:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 79:10</a></font></span></p>This ground of pleading often used (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo032.htm#012">Exo 32:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num014.htm#013">Num 14:13-16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>blood . . . shed-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#003">Psa 79:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#011">Psalms 79:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 79:11</a></font></span></p>prisoner--the whole captive people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>power--literally, "arm" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#015">Psa 10:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#012">Psalms 79:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 79:12</a></font></span></p>into their bosom--The lap or folds of the dress is used by Eastern people for receiving articles. The figure denotes retaliation (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#006">Isa 65:6-7</a>). They reproached God as well as His people.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#013">Psalms 79:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 79:13</a></font></span></p>sheep . . . pasture--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#001">Psa 74:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#070">Psa 78:70</a>).
