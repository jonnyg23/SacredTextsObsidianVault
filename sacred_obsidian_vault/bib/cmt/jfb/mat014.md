 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#001">Matthew 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mat 14:1</a></font></span></p>HEROD THINKS JESUS A RESURRECTION OF THE MURDERED BAPTIST--ACCOUNT OF HIS IMPRISONMENT AND DEATH. ( = Mark 6:14-29; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#007">Luk 9:7-9</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#001">Mat 14:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>At that time Herod the tetrarch--Herod Antipas, one of the three sons of Herod the Great, and own brother of Archelaus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#022">Mat 2:22</a>), who ruled as ethnarch over Galilee and Perea. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>heard of the fame of Jesus--"for His name was spread abroad" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#014">Mar 6:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#002">Matthew 14:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">mat 14:2</a></font></span></p>And said unto his servants--his counsellors or court-ministers. 
 <p>
 </p><p>This is John the Baptist: he is risen from the dead, &amp;c.--The murdered prophet haunted his guilty breast like a specter and seemed to him alive again and clothed with unearthly powers in the person of Jesus.
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Account of the Baptist's Imprisonment and Death (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#003">Mat 14:3-12</a>). For the exposition of this portion, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#017">Mar 6:17-29</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#028">Matthew 14:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">mat 14:28</a></font></span></p>JESUS CROSSES TO THE WESTERN SIDE OF THE LAKE WALKING ON THE SEA--INCIDENTS ON LANDING. ( = <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#045">Mar 6:45</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#015">Joh 6:15-24</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#022">Mat 14:22-26</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>And Peter answered him and said, Lord, if it is thou, bid me come to thee on the water--(Also see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#050">Mar 6:50</a>.)
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#029">Matthew 14:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">mat 14:29</a></font></span></p>And he said, Come. And when Peter had come down out of the boat. he walked on the water, to go to Jesus--(Also see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#050">Mar 6:50</a>.)
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#030">Matthew 14:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">mat 14:30</a></font></span></p>But when he saw the wind boisterous, he was afraid; and beginning to sink, he cried, saying, Lord, save me--(Also see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#050">Mar 6:50</a>.)
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#031">Matthew 14:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">mat 14:31</a></font></span></p>And immediately Jesus stretched forth his hand, and caught him, and said to him, O thou of little faith, why didst thou doubt?--(Also see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#050">Mar 6:50</a>.)
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#032">Matthew 14:32</a></p>
