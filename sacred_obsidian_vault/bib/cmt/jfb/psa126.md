 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa126.htm#001">Psalms 126:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 126:1</a></font></span></p>To praise for God's favor to His people is added a prayer for its continued manifestation. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa126.htm#001">Psa 126:1-6</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>When the Lord, &amp;c.--The joy of those returned from Babylon was ecstatic, and elicited the admiration even of the heathen, as illustrating God's great power and goodness. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>turned again the captivity--that is, restored from it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job039.htm#012">Job 39:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#007">Psa 14:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#014">Pro 12:14</a>). HENGSTENBERG translates: "When the Lord turned Himself to the turning of Zion" (see Margin), God returns to His people when they return to Him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#002">Deu 30:2-3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa126.htm#004">Psalms 126:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 126:4</a></font></span></p>All did not return at once; hence the prayer for repeated favors. 
 <p>
 </p><p>as the streams in the south--or, the torrents in the desert south of Judea, dependent on rain (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos015.htm#009">Jos 15:9</a>), reappearing after dry seasons (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job006.htm#015">Job 6:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#009">Psa 68:9</a>). The point of comparison is joy at the reappearing of what has been so painfully missed.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa126.htm#005">Psalms 126:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 126:5</a></font></span></p>As in husbandry the sower may cast his seed in a dry and parched soil with desponding fears, so those shall reap abundant fruit who toil in tears with the prayer of faith. (Compare the history, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#016">Ezr 6:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr006.htm#022">Ezr 6:22</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa126.htm#006">Psalms 126:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 126:6</a></font></span></p>He that goeth forth--literally, better, "He goes--he comes, he comes," &amp;c. The repetition implies there is no end of weeping here, as there shall be no end of joy hereafter (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#010">Isa 35:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>precious seed--rather, seed to be drawn from the seed box for sowing; literally, "seed-draught." Compare on this Psalm, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer031.htm#009">Jer 31:9</a>, &amp;c.
 </p><p>
