 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#001">Psalms 47:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 47:1</a></font></span></p>Praise is given to God for victory, perhaps that recorded (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2020.htm#020">Ch2 20:20-30</a>); and His dominions over all people, Jews and Gentiles, is asserted. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#001">Psa 47:1-9</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>clap . . . hands . . . people--literally, "peoples," or "nations" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#043">Deu 32:43</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#049">Psa 18:49</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#009">Psa 98:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#002">Psalms 47:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 47:2</a></font></span></p>His universal sovereignty now exists, and will be made known.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#003">Psalms 47:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 47:3</a></font></span></p>under us--that is, His saints; Israel's temporal victories were types of the spiritual conquests of the true Church.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#004">Psalms 47:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 47:4</a></font></span></p>He shall . . . inheritance--the heathen to be possessed by His Church (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#008">Psa 2:8</a>), as Canaan by the Jews. 
 <p>
 </p><p>excellency of Jacob--literally, "pride," or, that in which he glories (not necessarily, though often, in a bad sense), the privileges of the chosen people-- 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>whom he loved--His love being the sole cause of granting them.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#005">Psalms 47:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 47:5</a></font></span></p>God, victorious over His enemies, reascends to heaven, amid the triumphant praises of His people, who celebrate His sovereign dominion. This sovereignty is what the Psalm teaches; hence he adds, 
 <p>
 </p><p>sing . . . praises with understanding--literally, "sing and play an instructive (Psalm)." The whole typifies Christ's ascension (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#018">Psa 68:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#008">Psalms 47:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 47:8</a></font></span></p>The instruction continued. 
 <p>
 </p><p>throne of . . . holiness--or, "holy throne" (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#004">Psa 23:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#009">Psalms 47:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 47:9</a></font></span></p>princes--who represent peoples. For-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>even--supply, "as," or, "to"--that is, they all become united under covenant with Abraham's God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>shields--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos004.htm#018">Hos 4:18</a>, "rulers" [Margin].
 </p><p>
