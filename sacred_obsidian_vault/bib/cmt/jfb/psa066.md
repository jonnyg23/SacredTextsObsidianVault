Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 66</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#001">Psalms 66:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 66:1</a></font></span></p>The writer invites all men to unite in praise, cites some striking occasions for it, promises special acts of thanksgiving, and celebrates God's great mercy. (Psa. 66:1-20)
 <p>
 </p><p>Make . . . noise--or, "Shout."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#002">Psalms 66:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 66:2</a></font></span></p>his name--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#002">Psa 29:2</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>make his praise glorious--literally, "place honor, His praise," or, "as to His praise"; that is, let His praise be such as will glorify Him, or, be honorable to Him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#003">Psalms 66:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 66:3</a></font></span></p>A specimen of the praise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>How terrible--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#008">Psa 65:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>submit--(Compare Margin), show a forced subjection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#044">Psa 18:44</a>), produced by terror.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#005">Psalms 66:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 66:5</a></font></span></p>The terrible works illustrated in Israel's history (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#021">Exo 14:21</a>). By this example let rebels be admonished.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#007">Psalms 66:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 66:7</a></font></span></p>behold the nations--watch their conduct.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#008">Psalms 66:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 66:8</a></font></span></p>Here is, perhaps, cited a case of recent deliverance.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#009">Psalms 66:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 66:9</a></font></span></p>holdeth . . . in life--literally, "putteth our soul in life"; that is, out of danger (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#003">Psa 30:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#015">Psa 49:15</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>to be moved--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#006">Psa 10:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#022">Psa 55:22</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#010">Psalms 66:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 66:10</a></font></span></p>Out of severe trials, God had brought them to safety (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa048.htm#010">Isa 48:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#007">Pe1 1:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#011">Psalms 66:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 66:11</a></font></span></p>affliction--literally, "pressure," or, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#003">Psa 55:3</a>, "oppression," which, laid on the 
 <p>
 </p><p>loins--the seat of strength (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#011">Deu 33:11</a>), enfeebles the frame.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#012">Psalms 66:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 66:12</a></font></span></p>men to ride over our heads--made us to pass. 
 <p>
 </p><p>through fire, &amp;c.--figures describing prostration and critical dangers (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#002">Isa 43:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#012">Eze 36:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>wealthy--literally, "overflowing," or, "irrigated," and hence fertile.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#013">Psalms 66:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 66:13</a></font></span></p>These full and varied offerings constitute the payment of vows (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev022.htm#018">Lev 22:18-23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#015">Psalms 66:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 66:15</a></font></span></p>I will offer--literally, "make to ascend," alluding to the smoke of burnt offering, which explains the use of "incense." 
 <p>
 </p><p>incense--elsewhere always denoting the fumes of aromatics.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#016">Psalms 66:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 66:16</a></font></span></p>With these he unites his public thanks, inviting those who fear God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#004">Psa 60:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#005">Psa 61:5</a>, His true worshippers) to hear. He vindicates his sincerity, inasmuch as God would not hear hypocrites, but had heard him.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#017">Psalms 66:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 66:17</a></font></span></p>he was extolled with my tongue--literally, "exaltation (was) under my tongue," as a place of deposit, whence it proceeded; that is, honoring God was habitual.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#018">Psalms 66:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 66:18</a></font></span></p>If I regard iniquity in my heart--literally, "see iniquity with pleasure."
