 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#001">Psalms 76:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 76:1</a></font></span></p>On Neginoth--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>, title). This Psalm commemorates what the preceding anticipates: God's deliverance of His people by a signal interposition of power against their enemies. The occasion was probably the events narrated in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#035">Kg2 19:35</a>; Isa. 37:1-28. (Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001">Psa 46:1-11</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#001">Psa 76:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>These well-known terms denote God's people and Church and His intimate and glorious relations to them.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#002">Psalms 76:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 76:2</a></font></span></p>Salem-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018">Gen 14:18</a>) is Jerusalem.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#003">Psalms 76:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 76:3</a></font></span></p>brake . . . the arrows--literally, "thunderbolts" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#048">Psa 78:48</a>), from their rapid flight or ignition (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#014">Psa 18:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#016">Eph 6:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>the battle--for arms (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos002.htm#018">Hos 2:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#004">Psalms 76:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 76:4</a></font></span></p>Thou--God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>mountains of prey--great victorious nations, as Assyria (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#015">Isa 41:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#011">Eze 38:11-12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#007">Zac 4:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#005">Psalms 76:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 76:5</a></font></span></p>slept their sleep--died (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#003">Psa 13:3</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>none . . . found . . . hands--are powerless.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#006">Psalms 76:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 76:6</a></font></span></p>chariot and horse--for those fighting on them (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#017">Psa 68:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#007">Psalms 76:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 76:7</a></font></span></p>may . . . sight--contend with Thee (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu009.htm#004">Deu 9:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos007.htm#012">Jos 7:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#008">Psalms 76:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 76:8</a></font></span></p>God's judgment on the wicked is His people's deliverance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#012">Psa 9:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#007">Psa 10:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#010">Psalms 76:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 76:10</a></font></span></p>Man's wrath praises God by its futility before His power. 
 <p>
 </p><p>restrain--or, "gird"; that is, Thyself, as with a sword, with which to destroy, or as an ornament to Thy praise.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#011">Psalms 76:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 76:11</a></font></span></p>Invite homage to such a God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2032.htm#023">Ch2 32:23</a>), who can stop the breath of kings and princes when He wills (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan005.htm#023">Dan 5:23</a>).
