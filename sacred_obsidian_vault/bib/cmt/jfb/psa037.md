Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 37</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#001">Psalms 37:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 37:1</a></font></span></p>A composed and uniform trust in God and a constant course of integrity are urged in view of the blessedness of the truly pious, contrasted in various aspects with the final ruin of the wicked. Thus the wisdom and justice of God's providence are vindicated, and its seeming inequalities, which excite the cavils of the wicked and the distrust of the pious, are explained. David's personal history abundantly illustrates the Psalm. (Psa. 37:1-40)
 <p>
 </p><p>The general sentiment of the whole Psalm is expressed. The righteous need not be vexed by the prosperity of the wicked; for it is transient, and their destiny undesirable.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#003">Psalms 37:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 37:3</a></font></span></p>Trust--sure of safety. 
 <p>
 </p><p>shalt thou dwell--or, "dwell thou"; repose quietly. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>verily . . . fed--or, "feed on truth," God's promise (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#005">Psa 36:5</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos012.htm#001">Hos 12:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#004">Psalms 37:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 37:4</a></font></span></p>desires-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#005">Psa 20:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa021.htm#002">Psa 21:2</a>), what is lawful and right, really good (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#011">Psa 84:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#005">Psalms 37:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 37:5</a></font></span></p>Commit thy way-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#003">Pro 16:3</a>). Works--what you have to do and cannot set forth as a burden. 
 <p>
 </p><p>trust . . . in him--literally, "on Him." He will do what you cannot (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#008">Psa 22:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#006">Psa 31:6</a>). He will not suffer your character to remain under suspicion.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#007">Psalms 37:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 37:7</a></font></span></p>Rest in--literally, "Be silent to the Lord." 
 <p>
 </p><p>and wait--Be submissive--avoid petulance and murmurings, anger and rash doing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#009">Psalms 37:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 37:9</a></font></span></p>Two reasons: The prosperity of the wicked is short; and the pious, by humble trust, will secure all covenant blessing, denoted here by "inherit the earth" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#013">Psa 25:13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#010">Psalms 37:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 37:10</a></font></span></p>shall not be--literally, "is not"--is not to be found.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#011">Psalms 37:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 37:11</a></font></span></p>peace--includes prosperity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#012">Psalms 37:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 37:12</a></font></span></p>gnasheth . . . teeth--in beastly rage.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#013">Psalms 37:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 37:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#004">Psa 2:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>seeth--knows certainly. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>his day--of punishment, long delayed, shall yet come (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#037">Heb 10:37</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#014">Psalms 37:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 37:14</a></font></span></p>sword, and . . . bow--for any instruments of violence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>slay--literally, "slaughter" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1025.htm#011">Sa1 25:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>poor and needy--God's people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#017">Psa 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa012.htm#005">Psa 12:5</a>). The punishment of the wicked as drawn on themselves--often mentioned (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#015">Psa 7:15-16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#008">Psa 35:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#016">Psalms 37:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 37:16</a></font></span></p>riches--literally, "noise and tumult," as incidental to much wealth (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#006">Psa 39:6</a>). Thus the contrast with the "little" of one man is more vivid.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#017">Psalms 37:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 37:17</a></font></span></p>Even the members of the body needed to hold weapons are destroyed.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#018">Psalms 37:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 37:18</a></font></span></p>God, who knows His people's changes, provides against evil and supplies all their need.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#020">Psalms 37:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 37:20</a></font></span></p>While the wicked, however mighty, are destroyed, and that utterly, as smoke which vanishes and leaves no trace.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#021">Psalms 37:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 37:21</a></font></span></p>payeth not--not able; having grown poor (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#007">Deu 15:7</a>). Ability of the one and inability of the other do not exclude moral dispositions. God's blessing or cursing makes the difference.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#022">Psalms 37:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 37:22</a></font></span></p>cut off--opposed to "inherit the earth" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#020">Lev 7:20-21</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#023">Psalms 37:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">psa 37:23</a></font></span></p>steps--way, or, "course of life"; as ordered by God, failures will not be permanent.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#026">Psalms 37:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">psa 37:26</a></font></span></p>his seed is blessed--literally, "for a blessing" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#002">Gen 12:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa021.htm#006">Psa 21:6</a>). This position is still true as the rule of God's economy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1004.htm#008">Ti1 4:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#006">Ti1 6:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#027">Psalms 37:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">psa 37:27</a></font></span></p>The exhortation is sustained by the assurance of God's essential rectitude in that providential government which provides perpetual blessings for the good, and perpetual misery for the wicked.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#030">Psalms 37:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">psa 37:30</a></font></span></p>The righteous described as to the elements of character, thought, word, and action.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#031">Psalms 37:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">psa 37:31</a></font></span></p>steps--or, "goings"--for conduct which is unwavering (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#036">Psa 18:36</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#032">Psalms 37:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">psa 37:32</a></font></span></p>The devices of the wicked against the good fail because God acquits them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#034">Psalms 37:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">psa 37:34</a></font></span></p>On the contrary, the good are not only blessed, but made to see the ruin of their foes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#035">Psalms 37:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">psa 37:35</a></font></span></p>of which a picture is given, under the figure of a flourishing tree (compare Margin), which soon withers.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#036">Psalms 37:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">psa 37:36</a></font></span></p>he was not--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#010">Psa 37:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#037">Psalms 37:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">psa 37:37</a></font></span></p>By "the end" is meant reward (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#018">Pro 23:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#014">Pro 24:14</a>), or expectation of success, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#038">Psa 37:38</a>, which describes the end of the wicked in contrast, and that is cut off (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#017">Psa 73:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#038">Psalms 37:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">psa 37:38</a></font></span></p>together--at once; entirely (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#008">Psa 4:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#039">Psalms 37:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">psa 37:39</a></font></span></p>strength-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psa 27:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#008">Psa 28:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>trouble--straits (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#009">Psa 9:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#001">Psa 10:1</a>). In trust and quietness is the salvation of the pious from all foes and all their devices.
 </p><p>
