Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001">Proverbs 11:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 11:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 11:1-31)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare Margin). The Hebrews used stones for weights. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>just--complete in measure.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#002">Proverbs 11:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 11:2</a></font></span></p>Self-conceit is unteachable; the humble grow wise (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018">Pro 16:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#012">Pro 18:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#003">Proverbs 11:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 11:3</a></font></span></p>guide--to lead, as a shepherd (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#007">Pro 6:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#052">Psa 78:52</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>perverseness--ill-nature. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>destroy--with violence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#004">Proverbs 11:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 11:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#002">Pro 10:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>wrath--that is, of God.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#005">Proverbs 11:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 11:5</a></font></span></p>direct--or, "make plain"; wicked ways are not plain (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#017">Pro 13:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#006">Proverbs 11:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 11:6</a></font></span></p>deliver them--that is, from evil, which the wicked suffer by their own doings (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#022">Pro 5:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#016">Psa 9:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#007">Proverbs 11:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 11:7</a></font></span></p>expectation . . . perish--for death cuts short all his plans (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#025">Luk 16:25</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hope of unjust--better, "hope of wealth," or "power" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#029">Isa 40:29</a>, Hebrew). This gives an advance on the sentiment of the first clause. Even hopes of gain die with him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#008">Proverbs 11:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 11:8</a></font></span></p>Perhaps the trouble prepared by the wicked, and which he inherits (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#006">Pro 11:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#009">Proverbs 11:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 11:9</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#016">Psa 35:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan011.htm#032">Dan 11:32</a>). The just is saved by superior discernment.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#010">Proverbs 11:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 11:10</a></font></span></p>The last may be a reason for the first. Together, they set forth the relative moral worth of good and bad men.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#011">Proverbs 11:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 11:11</a></font></span></p>By the blessing--implying active benevolence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#012">Proverbs 11:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 11:12</a></font></span></p>despiseth--or, "reviles," a course contrasted with the prudent silence of the wise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>holdeth his peace--as if neither hearing nor telling.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#013">Proverbs 11:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 11:13</a></font></span></p>tale-bearer--(Compare Margin), one trading as a peddler in scandal, whose propensity to talk leads him to betray confidence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#014">Proverbs 11:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 11:14</a></font></span></p>counsel--the art of governing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#005">Pro 1:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>counsellors--literally, "one giving counsel"; the participle used as a collective.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#015">Proverbs 11:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 11:15</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001">Pro 6:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>suretiship--(Compare Margin), the actors put for the action, which may be lawfully hated.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#016">Proverbs 11:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 11:16</a></font></span></p>retaineth--or literally, "lay hold of as a support." Honor is to a feeble woman thus as valuable as riches to men.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#017">Proverbs 11:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 11:17</a></font></span></p>merciful--kind to others; opposed to cruel. Such benefit themselves by doing good to others (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#005">Pro 24:5</a>), while the cruel injure themselves as well as others. 
 <p>
 </p><p>flesh--that is, his body, by penuriousness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#023">Col 2:23</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#018">Proverbs 11:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 11:18</a></font></span></p>a deceitful work--or, "wages," which fail to satisfy, or flee away (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#002">Pro 10:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#005">Pro 23:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>sure reward--or, "gain," as from trading (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos010.htm#012">Hos 10:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#008">Gal 6:8-9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#019">Proverbs 11:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 11:19</a></font></span></p>Inference from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#018">Pro 11:18</a> (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#005">Pro 11:5-6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#016">Pro 10:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#020">Proverbs 11:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 11:20</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#005">Pro 11:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>froward--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#015">Pro 2:15</a>, opposed to the simplicity and purity of the upright. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in their way--or, "conduct."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#021">Proverbs 11:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 11:21</a></font></span></p>The combined power of the wicked cannot free them from just punishment, while the unaided children of the righteous find deliverance by reason of their pious relationship (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#025">Psa 37:25-26</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#022">Proverbs 11:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 11:22</a></font></span></p>Jewels were often suspended from the nose (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#047">Gen 24:47</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#021">Isa 3:21</a>). Thus adorned, a hog disgusts less than a fair and indiscreet woman.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#023">Proverbs 11:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 11:23</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#028">Pro 10:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>wrath--is that of God.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#024">Proverbs 11:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 11:24</a></font></span></p>The scope of the whole is a comment on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#023">Pro 11:23</a>. Thus liberality, by God's blessing, secures increase, while penuriousness, instead of expected gain, procures poverty.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#025">Proverbs 11:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 11:25</a></font></span></p>liberal soul--(Compare Margin). 
 <p>
 </p><p>made fat--prospers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#025">Pro 28:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#015">Deu 32:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#038">Luk 6:38</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>watereth . . . watered--a common figure for blessing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#026">Proverbs 11:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 11:26</a></font></span></p>Another example of the truth of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#023">Pro 11:23</a>; the miser loses reputation, though he saves corn. 
 <p>
 </p><p>selleth it--that is, at a fair price.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#027">Proverbs 11:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 11:27</a></font></span></p>good [and] mischief--that is, of others. 
 <p>
 </p><p>procureth . . . seeketh--implying success.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#028">Proverbs 11:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 11:28</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#015">Pro 10:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#006">Psa 49:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#017">Ti1 6:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteous . . . branch-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#003">Psa 1:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#008">Jer 17:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#029">Proverbs 11:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 11:29</a></font></span></p>troubleth--as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#027">Pro 15:27</a> explains, by greediness for gain (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#017">Pro 11:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>inherit . . . wind--Even successful, his gains are of no real value. So the fool, thus acting, either comes to poverty, or heaps up for others.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#030">Proverbs 11:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 11:30</a></font></span></p>a tree of life--Blessings to others proceed from the works of the righteous (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#018">Pro 3:18</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>winneth souls--(Compare Margin) to do them good as opposed to <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#025">Pro 6:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze013.htm#018">Eze 13:18</a> (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#010">Luk 5:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#031">Proverbs 11:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 11:31</a></font></span></p>Behold--Thus calling attention to the illustrations (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#023">Pro 11:23</a>), the sentiment of which is confirmed even in time, not excluding future rewards and punishments.
