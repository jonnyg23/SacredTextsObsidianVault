Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 64</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#001">Psalms 64:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 64:1</a></font></span></p>A prayer for deliverance from cunning and malicious enemies, with a confident view of their overthrow, which will honor God and give joy to the righteous. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#001">Psa 64:1-10</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>preserve . . . fear--as well as the danger producing it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#002">Psalms 64:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 64:2</a></font></span></p>insurrection--literally, "uproar," noisy assaults, as well as their secret counsels.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#003">Psalms 64:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 64:3</a></font></span></p>Similar figures for slander (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#004">Psa 57:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#007">Psa 59:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>bend--literally, "tread," or, "prepared." The allusion is to the mode of bending a bow by treading on it; here, and in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa058.htm#007">Psa 58:7</a>, transferred to arrows.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#004">Psalms 64:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 64:4</a></font></span></p>the perfect--one innocent of the charges made (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#023">Psa 18:23</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>fear not-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#019">Psa 55:19</a>), not regarding God.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#005">Psalms 64:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 64:5</a></font></span></p>A sentiment here more fully presented, by depicting their deliberate malice.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#006">Psalms 64:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 64:6</a></font></span></p>This is further evinced by their diligent efforts and deeply laid schemes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#007">Psalms 64:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 64:7</a></font></span></p>The contrast is heightened by representing God as using weapons like theirs.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#008">Psalms 64:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 64:8</a></font></span></p>their . . . tongue to fall, &amp;c.--that is, the consequences of their slanders, &amp;c. (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#002">Psa 10:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#016">Psa 31:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>all that see . . . away--Their partners in evil shall be terrified.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#009">Psalms 64:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 64:9</a></font></span></p>Men, generally, will acknowledge God's work, and the righteous, rejoicing in it, shall be encouraged to trust Him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa058.htm#010">Psa 58:10</a>).
