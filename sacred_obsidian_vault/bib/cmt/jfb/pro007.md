Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#001">Proverbs 7:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 7:1</a></font></span></p>The subject continued, by a delineation of the arts of strange women, as a caution to the unwary. (Pro. 7:1-27)
 <p>
 </p><p>Similar calls (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#001">Pro 3:1-3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#010">Pro 4:10</a>, &amp;c.).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#002">Proverbs 7:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 7:2</a></font></span></p>apple . . . eye--pupil of eye, a custody (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#023">Pro 4:23</a>) of special value.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#003">Proverbs 7:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 7:3</a></font></span></p>Bind . . . fingers--as inscriptions on rings.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#005">Proverbs 7:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 7:5</a></font></span></p>The design of the teaching (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#016">Pro 2:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#024">Pro 6:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#006">Proverbs 7:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 7:6</a></font></span></p>For--or, "Since," introducing an example to illustrate the warning, which, whether a narrative or a parable, is equally pertinent. 
 <p>
 </p><p>window--or, "opening" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>looked--literally, "watched earnestly" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#028">Jdg 5:28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>casement--or, "lattice."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#007">Proverbs 7:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 7:7</a></font></span></p>simple--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Pro 1:4</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>void of, &amp;c.--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#032">Pro 6:32</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#008">Proverbs 7:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 7:8</a></font></span></p>her corner--where she was usually found. 
 <p>
 </p><p>went . . . house--implying, perhaps, confidence in himself by his manner, as denoted in the word 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>went--literally, "tread pompously."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#009">Proverbs 7:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 7:9</a></font></span></p>The time, twilight, ending in darkness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>black . . . night--literally, "pupil," or, "eye," that is, middle of night.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#010">Proverbs 7:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 7:10</a></font></span></p>attire--that of harlots was sometimes peculiar. 
 <p>
 </p><p>subtile--or, "wary," "cunning."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#011">Proverbs 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 7:11</a></font></span></p>loud--or, "noisy," "bustling." 
 <p>
 </p><p>stubborn--not submissive. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>without . . . streets, . . . corner--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#013">Ti1 5:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#005">Tit 2:5</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#013">Proverbs 7:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 7:13</a></font></span></p>The preparations for a feast do not necessarily imply peculiar religious professions. The offerer retained part of the victim for a feast (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev003.htm#009">Lev 3:9</a>, &amp;c.). This feast she professes was prepared for him whom she boldly addresses as one sought specially to partake of it.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#016">Proverbs 7:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 7:16</a></font></span></p>my bed--or, "couch," adorned in the costliest manner.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#017">Proverbs 7:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 7:17</a></font></span></p>bed--a place for sleeping.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#018">Proverbs 7:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 7:18</a></font></span></p>There is no fear of discovery.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#020">Proverbs 7:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 7:20</a></font></span></p>the day appointed--perhaps, literally, "a full moon," that is, a fortnight's time (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#019">Pro 7:19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#021">Proverbs 7:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 7:21</a></font></span></p>caused . . . yield--or, "inclines." 
 <p>
 </p><p>flattering--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#003">Pro 5:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>forced him--by persuasion overcoming his scruples.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#022">Proverbs 7:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 7:22</a></font></span></p>straightway--quickly, either as ignorant of danger, or incapable of resistance.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#023">Proverbs 7:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 7:23</a></font></span></p>Till--He is now caught (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#026">Pro 6:26</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#024">Proverbs 7:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 7:24</a></font></span></p>The inferential admonition is followed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#026">Pro 7:26-27</a>), by a more general allegation of the evils of this vice.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#026">Proverbs 7:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 7:26</a></font></span></p>Even the mightiest fail to resist her deathly allurements.
