Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 32</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psalms 32:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 32:1</a></font></span></p>Maschil--literally, "giving instruction." The Psalmist describes the blessings of His forgiveness, succeeding the pains of conviction, and deduces from his own experience instruction and exhortation to others. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psa 32:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#006">Rom 4:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>forgiven--literally, "taken away," opposed to retain (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#023">Joh 20:23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>covered--so that God no longer regards the sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa085.htm#003">Psa 85:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#002">Psalms 32:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 32:2</a></font></span></p>imputeth--charge to him, and treat him accordingly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>no guile--or, deceit, no false estimate of himself, nor insincerity before God (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#001">Rom 8:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#003">Psalms 32:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 32:3</a></font></span></p>A vivid description of felt, but unacknowledged, sin. 
 <p>
 </p><p>When--literally, "for," as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#004">Psa 32:4</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#004">Psalms 32:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 32:4</a></font></span></p>thy hand--of God, or power in distressing him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#002">Psa 38:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>moisture--vital juices of the body, the parching heat of which expresses the anguish of the soul. On the other figures, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#002">Psa 6:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007">Psa 6:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#009">Psa 31:9-11</a>. If composed on the occasion of the fifty-first Psalm, this distress may have been protracted for several months.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#005">Psalms 32:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 32:5</a></font></span></p>A prompt fulfilment of the purposed confession is followed by a prompt forgiveness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#006">Psalms 32:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 32:6</a></font></span></p>For this--that is, my happy experience. 
 <p>
 </p><p>godly--pious in the sense of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psa 4:3</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>a time-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#006">Isa 55:6</a>); when God's Spirit inclines us to seek pardon, He is ready to forgive. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>floods, &amp;c.--denotes great danger (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#017">Psa 18:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#012">Psa 66:12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#007">Psalms 32:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 32:7</a></font></span></p>His experience illustrates the statement of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#006">Psa 32:6</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#008">Psalms 32:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 32:8</a></font></span></p>Whether, as most likely, the language of David (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#013">Psa 51:13</a>), or that of God, this is a promise of divine guidance. 
 <p>
 </p><p>I will . . . mine eye--or, My eye shall be on thee, watching and directing thy way.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#009">Psalms 32:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 32:9</a></font></span></p>The latter clause, more literally, "in that they come not near thee"; that is, because they will not come, &amp;c., unless forced by bit and bridle.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#010">Psalms 32:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 32:10</a></font></span></p>The sorrows of the impenitent contrasted with the peace and safety secured by God's mercy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#011">Psalms 32:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 32:11</a></font></span></p>The righteous and upright, or those conforming to the divine teaching for securing the divine blessing, may well rejoice with shouting.
