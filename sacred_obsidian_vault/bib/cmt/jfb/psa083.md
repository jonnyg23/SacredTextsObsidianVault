Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 83</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#001">Psalms 83:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 83:1</a></font></span></p>Of Asaph--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#001">Psa 74:1</a>, title). The historical occasion is probably that of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2020.htm#001">Ch2 20:1-2</a> (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#001">Psa 47:1-9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#001">Psa 48:1-14</a>). After a general petition, the craft and rage of the combined enemies are described, God's former dealings recited, and a like summary and speedy destruction on them is invoked. (Psa. 83:1-18)
 <p>
 </p><p>God addressed as indifferent (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#022">Psa 35:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#012">Psa 39:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>be not still--literally, "not quiet," as opposed to action.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#002">Psalms 83:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 83:2</a></font></span></p>thine enemies--as well as ours (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#023">Psa 74:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#023">Isa 37:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#003">Psalms 83:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 83:3</a></font></span></p>hidden ones--whom God specially protects (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#005">Psa 27:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001">Psa 91:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#004">Psalms 83:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 83:4</a></font></span></p>from being a nation--utter destruction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#008">Isa 7:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa023.htm#001">Isa 23:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>Israel--here used for Judah, having been the common name.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#005">Psalms 83:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 83:5</a></font></span></p>they have consulted--with heart, or cordially. 
 <p>
 </p><p>together--all alike.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#006">Psalms 83:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 83:6</a></font></span></p>tabernacles--for people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#067">Psa 78:67</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>they--all these united with the children of Lot, or Ammonites and Moabites (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2020.htm#001">Ch2 20:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#009">Psalms 83:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 83:9</a></font></span></p>Compare the similar fate of these (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2020.htm#023">Ch2 20:23</a>) with that of the foes mentioned in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg007.htm#022">Jdg 7:22</a>, here referred to. They destroyed one another (Jdg. 4:6-24; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg007.htm#025">Jdg 7:25</a>). Human remains form manure (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2009.htm#037">Kg2 9:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer009.htm#022">Jer 9:22</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#012">Psalms 83:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 83:12</a></font></span></p>The language of the invaders. 
 <p>
 </p><p>houses--literally, "residences," enclosures, as for flocks (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#012">Psa 65:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>of God--as the proprietors of the land (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2020.htm#011">Ch2 20:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#025">Isa 14:25</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#013">Psalms 83:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 83:13</a></font></span></p>like a wheel--or, whirling of any light thing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa017.htm#013">Isa 17:13</a>), as stubble or chaff (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#004">Psa 1:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#014">Psalms 83:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 83:14</a></font></span></p>Pursue them to an utter destruction.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#016">Psalms 83:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 83:16</a></font></span></p>that they may seek--or as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#018">Psa 83:18</a>, supply "men," since <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#017">Psa 83:17-18</a> amplify the sentiment of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#016">Psa 83:16</a>, expressing more fully the measure of destruction, and the lesson of God's being and perfections (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2020.htm#029">Ch2 20:29</a>) taught to all men.
