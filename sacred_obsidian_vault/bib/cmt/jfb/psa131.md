 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psalms 131:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 131:1</a></font></span></p>This Psalm, while expressive of David's pious feelings on assuming the royal office, teaches the humble, submissive temper of a true child of God. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psa 131:1-3</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>eyes lofty--a sign of pride (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#027">Psa 18:27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>exercise myself--literally, "walk in," or "meddle with."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#002">Psalms 131:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 131:2</a></font></span></p>Surely, &amp;c.--The form is that of an oath or strongest assertion. Submission is denoted by the figure of a weaned child. As the child weaned by his mother from the breast, so I still the motions of pride in me (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#003">Mat 18:3-4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#008">Isa 11:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa028.htm#009">Isa 28:9</a>). Hebrew children were often not weaned till three years old. 
 <p>
 </p><p>soul--may be taken for desire, which gives a more definite sense, though one included in the idea conveyed by the usual meaning, myself.
