 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#001">Psalms 100:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 100:1</a></font></span></p>As closing this series (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa093.htm#001">Psa 93:1</a>), this Psalm is a general call on all the earth to render exalted praise to God, the creator, preserver, and benefactor of men. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#001">Psa 100:1-5</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>With thankful praise, unite service as the subjects of a king (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#011">Psa 2:11-12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#003">Psalms 100:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 100:3</a></font></span></p>To the obligations of a creature and subject is added that of a beneficiary (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#007">Psa 95:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#004">Psalms 100:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 100:4</a></font></span></p>Join joyfully in His public worship. The terms are, of course, figurative (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#002">Psa 84:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#013">Psa 92:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#023">Isa 66:23</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>Enter--or, "Come with solemnity" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#006">Psa 95:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#005">Psalms 100:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 100:5</a></font></span></p>The reason: God's eternal mercy and truth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#008">Psa 25:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#007">Psa 89:7</a>).
