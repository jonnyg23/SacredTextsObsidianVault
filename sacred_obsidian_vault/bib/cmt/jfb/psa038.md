Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 38</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#001">Psalms 38:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 38:1</a></font></span></p>To bring to remembrance, or, remind God of His mercy and himself of his sin. Appealing to God for relief from His heavy chastisement, the Psalmist avows his integrity before men, complains of the defection of friends and persecution of enemies, and in a submissive spirit, casting himself on God, with penitent confession he pleads God's covenant relation and his innocence of the charges of his enemies, and prays for divine comfort and help. (Psa. 38:1-22)
 <p>
 </p><p>He deprecates deserved punishment, which is described (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psa 6:1</a>), under the figure of bodily disease [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#003">Psa 38:3</a>].
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#002">Psalms 38:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 38:2</a></font></span></p>arrows . . . and thy hand--the sharp and heavy afflictions he suffered (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#023">Deu 32:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#004">Psalms 38:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 38:4</a></font></span></p>iniquities--afflictions in punishment of sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2016.htm#012">Sa2 16:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#010">Psa 31:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#012">Psa 40:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>gone over mine head--as a flood.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#005">Psalms 38:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 38:5</a></font></span></p>The loathsomeness, corruption, and wasting torture of severe physical disease set forth his mental anguish [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#006">Psa 38:6</a>]. It is possible some bodily disease was connected. The 
 <p>
 </p><p>loins are the seat of strength. His exhaustion left him only the power to groan [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#009">Psa 38:9</a>].
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#009">Psalms 38:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 38:9</a></font></span></p>That God can hear (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#026">Rom 8:26</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#010">Psalms 38:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 38:10</a></font></span></p>My heart panteth--as if barely surviving. 
 <p>
 </p><p>light . . . from me--utter exhaustion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007">Psa 6:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#003">Psa 13:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#011">Psalms 38:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 38:11</a></font></span></p>Friends desert, but foes increase in malignity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#012">Psalms 38:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 38:12</a></font></span></p>seek after my life-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1020.htm#001">Sa1 20:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1022.htm#023">Sa1 22:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#013">Psalms 38:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 38:13</a></font></span></p>He patiently submits, uttering no reproaches or replies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#009">Joh 19:9</a>) to their insulting speeches;
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#015">Psalms 38:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 38:15</a></font></span></p>for he is confident the 
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord--literally, "Sovereign" (to whom he was a servant), would answer his prayer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#004">Psa 3:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>), and not permit their triumph in his partial halting, of which he was in danger.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#018">Psalms 38:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 38:18</a></font></span></p>Consciousness of sin makes suffering pungent, and suffering, rightly received, leads to confession.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#019">Psalms 38:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 38:19</a></font></span></p>Still, while humbled before God, he is the victim of deadly enemies, full of malice and treachery. 
 <p>
 </p><p>enemies are lively--literally, "of life," who would take my life, that is, deadly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#021">Psalms 38:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 38:21</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#019">Psa 22:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#003">Psa 35:3</a>). All terms of frequent use. In this Psalm the language is generally susceptible of application to Christ as a sufferer, David, as such, typifying Him. This does not require us to apply the confessions of sin, but only the pains or penalties which He bore for us.
