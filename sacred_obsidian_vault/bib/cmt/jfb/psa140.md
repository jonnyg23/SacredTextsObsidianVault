Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 140</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#001">Psalms 140:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 140:1</a></font></span></p>The style of this Psalm resembles those of David in the former part of the book, presenting the usual complaint, prayer, and confident hope of relief. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#001">Psa 140:1-13</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>evil man--Which of David's enemies is meant is not important.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#002">Psalms 140:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 140:2</a></font></span></p>This character of the wicked, and the devices planned against the pious, correspond to <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#007">Psa 10:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#013">Psa 31:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa058.htm#004">Psa 58:4</a>, &amp;c.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#003">Psalms 140:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 140:3</a></font></span></p>sharpened . . . like a serpent--not like a serpent does, but they are thus like a serpent in cunning and venom.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#005">Psalms 140:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 140:5</a></font></span></p>snare [and] net--for threatening dangers (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#012">Psa 38:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#006">Psa 57:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#006">Psalms 140:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 140:6</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psa 5:1-12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#002">Psa 16:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#007">Psalms 140:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 140:7</a></font></span></p>day of battle--literally, "of armor," that is, when using it.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#008">Psalms 140:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 140:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#012">Psa 37:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#007">Psa 66:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>lest they exalt themselves--or, they will be exalted if permitted to prosper.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#009">Psalms 140:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 140:9</a></font></span></p>Contrasts his head covered by God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#007">Psa 140:7</a>) with theirs, or (as "head" may be used for "persons") with them, covered with the results of their wicked deeds (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#016">Psa 7:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#010">Psalms 140:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 140:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa011.htm#006">Psa 11:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#004">Psa 120:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>cast into the fire; into deep pits--figures for utter destruction.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#011">Psalms 140:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 140:11</a></font></span></p>an evil speaker--or, "slanderer" will not be tolerated (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa101.htm#007">Psa 101:7</a>). The last clause may be translated: "an evil (man) He (God) shall hunt," &amp;c.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#012">Psalms 140:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 140:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#004">Psa 9:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa140.htm#013">Psalms 140:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 140:13</a></font></span></p>After all changes, the righteous shall have cause for praise. Such 
 <p>
 </p><p>shall dwell--shall sit securely, under God's protection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa021.htm#006">Psa 21:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa041.htm#012">Psa 41:12</a>).
 </p><p>
