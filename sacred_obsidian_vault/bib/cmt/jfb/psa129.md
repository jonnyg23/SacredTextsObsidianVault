Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 129</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#001">Psalms 129:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 129:1</a></font></span></p>The people of God, often delivered from enemies, are confident of His favor, by their overthrow in the future. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#001">Psa 129:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>may Israel now say--or, "oh! let Israel say" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psa 124:1</a>). Israel's youth was the sojourn in Egypt (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#002">Jer 2:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos002.htm#015">Hos 2:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#002">Psalms 129:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 129:2</a></font></span></p>prevailed--literally, "been able," that is, to accomplish their purpose against me (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#004">Psa 13:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#003">Psalms 129:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 129:3</a></font></span></p>The ploughing is a figure of scourging, which most severe physical infliction aptly represents all kinds.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#004">Psalms 129:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 129:4</a></font></span></p>the cords--that is, which fasten the plough to the ox; and cutting denotes God's arresting the persecution;
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#005">Psalms 129:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 129:5</a></font></span></p>The ill-rooted roof grass, which withers before it grows up and procures for those gathering it no harvest blessing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut002.htm#004">Rut 2:4</a>), sets forth the utter uselessness and the rejection of the wicked.
