 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#001">Psalms 88:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 88:1</a></font></span></p>Upon Mahalath--either an instrument, as a lute, to be used as an accompaniment (Leannoth, "for singing") or, as others think, an enigmatic title (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psa 5:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001">Psa 22:1</a>, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#001">Psa 45:1</a>, titles), denoting the subject--that is, "sickness or disease, for humbling," the idea of spiritual maladies being often represented by disease (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#005">Psa 6:5-6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014">Psa 22:14-15</a>, &amp;c.). On the other terms, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#001">Psa 42:1</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psa 32:1</a>. Heman and Ethan (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#001">Psa 89:1</a>, title) were David's singers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#018">Ch1 6:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#033">Ch1 6:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#017">Ch1 15:17</a>), of the family of Kohath. If the persons alluded to (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#031">Kg1 4:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1002.htm#006">Ch1 2:6</a>), they were probably adopted into the tribe of Judah. Though called a song, which usually implies joy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#001">Psa 83:1</a>), both the style and matter of the Psalm are very despondent; yet the appeals to God evince faith, and we may suppose that the word "song" might be extended to such compositions. (Psa. 88:1-18)
 <p>
 </p><p>Compare on the terms used, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#002">Psa 22:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#002">Psa 31:2</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#003">Psalms 88:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 88:3</a></font></span></p>grave--literally, "hell" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#010">Psa 16:10</a>), death in wide sense.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#004">Psalms 88:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 88:4</a></font></span></p>go . . . pit--of destruction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001">Psa 28:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>as a man--literally, "a stout man," whose strength is utterly gone.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#005">Psalms 88:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 88:5</a></font></span></p>Free . . . dead--Cut off from God's care, as are the slain, who, falling under His wrath, are left, no longer sustained by His hand.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#006">Psalms 88:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 88:6</a></font></span></p>Similar figures for distress in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#009">Psa 63:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#003">Psa 69:3</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#007">Psalms 88:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 88:7</a></font></span></p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#002">Psa 38:2</a>, on first, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#007">Psa 42:7</a>, on last clause.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#008">Psalms 88:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 88:8</a></font></span></p>Both cut off from sympathy and made hateful to friends (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#011">Psa 31:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#009">Psalms 88:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 88:9</a></font></span></p>Mine eye mourneth--literally, "decays," or fails, denoting exhaustion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007">Psa 6:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#009">Psa 31:9</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>I . . . called-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#005">Psa 86:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#007">Psa 86:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>stretched out--for help (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#020">Psa 44:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#010">Psalms 88:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 88:10</a></font></span></p>shall the dead--the remains of ghosts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>arise--literally, "rise up," that is, as dead persons.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#011">Psalms 88:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 88:11</a></font></span></p>amplify the foregoing, the whole purport (as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#005">Psa 6:5</a>) being to contrast death and life as seasons for praising God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#013">Psalms 88:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 88:13</a></font></span></p>prevent--meet--that is, he will diligently come before God for help (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#041">Psa 18:41</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#014">Psalms 88:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 88:14</a></font></span></p>On the terms (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#009">Psa 27:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#001">Psa 74:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#007">Psa 77:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#015">Psalms 88:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 88:15</a></font></span></p>from . . . youth up--all my life.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#016">Psalms 88:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 88:16</a></font></span></p>the extremes of anguish and despair are depicted.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#018">Psalms 88:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 88:18</a></font></span></p>into darkness--Better omit "into"--"mine acquaintances (are) darkness," the gloom of death, &amp;c. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job017.htm#013">Job 17:13-14</a>).
