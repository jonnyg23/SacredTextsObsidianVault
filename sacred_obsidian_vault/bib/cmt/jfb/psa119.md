Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 119</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001">Psalms 119:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 119:1</a></font></span></p>ALEPH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001">Psa 119:1-8</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>This celebrated Psalm has several peculiarities. It is divided into twenty-two parts or stanzas, denoted by the twenty-two letters of the Hebrew alphabet. Each stanza contains eight verses, and the first letter of each verse is that which gives name to the stanza. Its contents are mainly praises of God's Word, exhortations to its perusal, and reverence for it, prayers for its proper influence, and complaints of the wicked for despising it. There are but two verses (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#122">Psa 119:122</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#132">Psa 119:132</a>) which do not contain some term or description of God's Word. These terms are of various derivations, but here used, for the most part, synonymously, though the use of a variety of terms seems designed, in order to express better the several aspects in which our relations to the revealed word of God are presented. The Psalm does not appear to have any relation to any special occasion or interest of the Jewish Church or nation, but was evidently "intended as a manual of pious thoughts, especially for instructing the young, and its peculiar artificial structure was probably adopted to aid the memory in retaining the language." (Psa. 119:1-176)
 </p><p>
 </p><p>undefiled--literally, "complete," perfect, or sincere (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#037">Psa 37:37</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in--or, "of" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the way--course of life. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>walk--act 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in the law--according to it (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#006">Luk 1:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>law--from a word meaning "to teach," is a term of rather general purport, denoting the instruction of God's Word.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#002">Psalms 119:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 119:2</a></font></span></p>testimonies--The word of God is so called, because in it He testifies for truth and against sin. 
 <p>
 </p><p>seek him--that is, a knowledge of Him, with desire for conformity to His will.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#003">Psalms 119:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 119:3</a></font></span></p>his ways--the course He reveals as right.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#004">Psalms 119:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 119:4</a></font></span></p>precepts--are those directions which relate to special conduct, from a word meaning "to inspect." 
 <p>
 </p><p>statutes--or ordinances, positive laws of permanent nature. Both words originally denote rather positive than moral laws, such as derive force from the divine appointment, whether their nature or the reasons for them are apprehended by us or not. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>commandments--or institutions. The term is comprehensive, but rather denotes fundamental directions for conduct, both enjoining and forbidding. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>have respect unto--or regard carefully as to their whole purport.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#007">Psalms 119:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 119:7</a></font></span></p>judgments--rules of conduct formed by God's judicial decisions; hence the wide sense of the word in the Psalms, so that it includes decisions of approval as well as condemnation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#008">Psalms 119:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 119:8</a></font></span></p>Recognizes the need of divine grace.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#009">Psalms 119:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 119:9</a></font></span></p>BETH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#009">Psa 119:9-16</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>The whole verse may be read as a question; for, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>by taking heed--is better, "for" taking heed, that is, so as to do it. The answer is implied, and inferable from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#005">Psa 119:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#010">Psa 119:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#018">Psa 119:18</a>, &amp;c., that is, by God's grace.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#010">Psalms 119:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 119:10</a></font></span></p>We must carefully treasure up the word of God, declare it to others, meditate on it, and heartily delight in it; and then by His grace we shall act according to it.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#017">Psalms 119:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 119:17</a></font></span></p>GIMEL. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#017">Psa 119:17-24</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>Life is desirable in order to serve God; that we may do so aright, we should seek to have our eyes opened to behold His truth, and earnestly desire fully to understand it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#021">Psalms 119:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 119:21</a></font></span></p>God will rebuke those who despise His word and deliver His servants from their reproach, giving them boldness in and by His truth, even before the greatest men.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#025">Psalms 119:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">psa 119:25</a></font></span></p>DALETH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#025">Psa 119:25-32</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>Submitting ourselves in depression to God, He will revive us by His promises, and lead us to declare His mercy to others.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#028">Psalms 119:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">psa 119:28</a></font></span></p>In order to adhere to His word, we must seek deliverance from temptations to sin as well as from despondency. 
 <p>
 </p><p>enlarge--or, "expand" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my heart--with gracious affections.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#033">Psalms 119:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">psa 119:33</a></font></span></p>HE. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#033">Psa 119:33-40</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>To encourage us in prayer for divine aid in adhering to His truth, we are permitted to believe that by His help we shall succeed. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the way of thy statutes--that is, the way or manner of life prescribed by them. The help we hope to obtain by prayer is to be the basis on which our resolutions should rest.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#037">Psalms 119:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">psa 119:37</a></font></span></p>Turn away mine eyes--literally, "Make my eyes to pass, not noticing evil." 
 <p>
 </p><p>vanity--literally, "falsehood;" all other objects of trust than God; idols, human power, &amp;c. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#006">Psa 31:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#004">Psa 40:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#011">Psa 60:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#009">Psa 62:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>quicken . . . in thy way--make me with living energy to pursue the way marked out by Thee. Revive me from the death of spiritual helplessness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#017">Psa 119:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#025">Psa 119:25</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#040">Psa 119:40</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#050">Psa 119:50</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#003">Psa 116:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#038">Psalms 119:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">psa 119:38</a></font></span></p>who is devoted to thy fear--or better, "which (that is, Thy word) is for Thy fear," for producing it. "Which is to those who fear Thee." God's word of promise belongs peculiarly to such (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#019">Gen 18:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#004">Kg1 2:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#025">Kg1 8:25</a>) [HENGSTENBERG].
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#039">Psalms 119:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">psa 119:39</a></font></span></p>Our hope of freedom from the reproach of inconsistency is in God's power, quickening us to live according to His Word, which He leads us to love. 
 <p>
 </p><p>for thy judgments are good--The time must therefore be at hand when Thy justice will turn the "reproach" from Thy Church upon the world (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa025.htm#008">Isa 25:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#005">Isa 66:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#008">Zep 2:8-10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#041">Psalms 119:41</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_041">psa 119:41</a></font></span></p>VAU. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#041">Psa 119:41-48</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>The sentiment more fully carried out. God's mercies and salvation, as revealed in His Word, provide hope of forgiveness for the past and security in a righteous course for the future.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#042">Psalms 119:42</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_042">psa 119:42</a></font></span></p>The possession of God's gift of "salvation" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#041">Psa 119:41</a>) will be the Psalmist's answer to the foe's "reproach," that his hope was a fallacious one.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#045">Psalms 119:45</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_045">psa 119:45</a></font></span></p>To freedom from reproach, when imbued with God's truth, there is added "great boldness in the faith" [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#013">Ti1 3:13</a>], accompanied with increasing delight in the holy law itself, which becomes an element of happiness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#048">Psalms 119:48</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_048">psa 119:48</a></font></span></p>My hands . . . lift up unto . . . commandments--that is, I will prayerfully (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#002">Psa 28:2</a>) direct my heart to keep Thy commandments.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#049">Psalms 119:49</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_049">psa 119:49</a></font></span></p>ZAIN. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#049">Psa 119:49-56</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>Resting on the promises consoles under affliction and the tauntings of the insolent. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>upon which--rather, "Remember Thy word unto Thy servant, because," &amp;c. So the Hebrew requires [HENGSTENBERG].
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#050">Psalms 119:50</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_050">psa 119:50</a></font></span></p>for--rather, "This is my comfort . . . that," &amp;c. [MAURER]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>hath quickened--What the Word has already done is to faith a pledge of what it shall yet do.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#052">Psalms 119:52</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_052">psa 119:52</a></font></span></p>The pious take comfort, when harassed and distressed by wickedness of men who forsake God's law, in remembering that the great principles of God's truth will still abide; and also God's 
 <p>
 </p><p>judgments of old--that is, His past interpositions in behalf of His people are a pledge that He will again interpose to deliver them; and they become the theme of constant and delightful meditation. The more we keep the more we love the law of God.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#053">Psalms 119:53</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_053">psa 119:53</a></font></span></p>Horror--rather, "vehement wrath" [HENGSTENBERG].
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#054">Psalms 119:54</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_054">psa 119:54</a></font></span></p>songs--As the exile sings songs of his home (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#003">Psa 137:3</a>), so the child of God, "a stranger on earth," sings the songs of heaven, his true home (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#012">Psa 39:12</a>). In ancient times, laws were put in verse, to imprint them the more on the memory of the people. So God's laws are the believer's songs. 
 <p>
 </p><p>house of my pilgrimage--present life (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#008">Gen 17:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#009">Gen 47:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#013">Heb 11:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#056">Psalms 119:56</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_056">psa 119:56</a></font></span></p>Rather, "This is peculiarly mine (literally, to me), that I keep Thy precepts" [HENGSTENBERG and MAURER].
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#057">Psalms 119:57</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_057">psa 119:57</a></font></span></p>CHETH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#057">Psa 119:57-64</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>Sincere desires for God's favor, penitence, and activity in a new obedience, truly evince the sincerity of those who profess to find God a portion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num018.htm#020">Num 18:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#005">Psa 16:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#024">Lam 3:24</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#058">Psalms 119:58</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_058">psa 119:58</a></font></span></p>favour--Hebrew, "face" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#012">Psa 45:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#059">Psalms 119:59</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_059">psa 119:59</a></font></span></p>So the prodigal son, when reduced to straits of misery (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#017">Luk 15:17-18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#061">Psalms 119:61</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_061">psa 119:61</a></font></span></p>This the more, if opposition of enemies, or love of ease is overcome in thus honoring God's law. 
 <p>
 </p><p>have robbed me--better, surrounded me, either as forcible constraints like fetters, or as the cords of their nets. HENGSTENBERG translates, "snares."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#062">Psalms 119:62</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_062">psa 119:62</a></font></span></p>At midnight--HENGSTENBERG supposes a reference to the time when the Lord went forth to slay the Egyptian first-born (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo011.htm#004">Exo 11:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#029">Exo 12:29</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#020">Job 34:20</a>). But it rather refers to the Psalmist's own praises and prayers in the night time. Compare Paul and Silas (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#025">Act 16:25</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#006">Psa 63:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#063">Psalms 119:63</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_063">psa 119:63</a></font></span></p>The communion of the saints. Delight in their company is an evidence of belonging to them (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#003">Psa 16:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo003.htm#003">Amo 3:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal003.htm#016">Mal 3:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#064">Psalms 119:64</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_064">psa 119:64</a></font></span></p>While opposed by the wicked, and opposing them, the pious delight in those who fear God, but, after all, rely for favor and guidance not on merit, but mercy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#065">Psalms 119:65</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_065">psa 119:65</a></font></span></p>TETH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#065">Psa 119:65-72</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>The reliance on promises (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#049">Psa 119:49</a>) is strengthened by experience of past dealings according with promises, and a prayer for guidance, encouraged by sanctified affliction.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#066">Psalms 119:66</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_066">psa 119:66</a></font></span></p>Teach me good judgment and knowledge--namely, in Thy word (so as to fathom its deep spirituality); for the corresponding expression (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#012">Psa 119:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#064">Psa 119:64</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#068">Psa 119:68</a>), is, "Teach me Thy statutes."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#067">Psalms 119:67</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_067">psa 119:67</a></font></span></p>Referred by HENGSTENBERG to the chastening effect produced on the Jews' minds by the captivity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer031.htm#018">Jer 31:18-19</a>). The truth is a general one (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job005.htm#006">Job 5:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#002">Joh 15:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#011">Heb 12:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#068">Psalms 119:68</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_068">psa 119:68</a></font></span></p>Compare as to the Lord Jesus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#038">Act 10:38</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#069">Psalms 119:69</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_069">psa 119:69</a></font></span></p>The crafty malice of the wicked, in slandering him, so far from turning him away, but binds him closer to God's Word, which they are too stupid in sin to appreciate. HENGSTENBERG refers the "lie" to such slanders against the Jews during the captivity, as that in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#001">Ezr 4:1-6</a>, of sedition.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#070">Psalms 119:70</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_070">psa 119:70</a></font></span></p>fat as grease--spiritually insensible (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#010">Psa 17:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#007">Psa 73:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#010">Isa 6:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#071">Psalms 119:71</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_071">psa 119:71</a></font></span></p>So also affliction of any kind acts as a wholesome discipline in leading the pious more highly to value the truth and promises of God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#073">Psalms 119:73</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_073">psa 119:73</a></font></span></p>JOD. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#073">Psa 119:73-80</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>As God made, so He can best control, us. So as to Israel, he owed to God his whole internal and external existence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#006">Deu 32:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#074">Psalms 119:74</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_074">psa 119:74</a></font></span></p>So when He has led us to rely on His truth, He will "make us to the praise of His grace" by others. "Those who fear Thee will be glad at my prosperity, as they consider my cause their cause" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#002">Psa 34:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa142.htm#007">Psa 142:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#075">Psalms 119:75</a></p>

 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#093">Psalms 119:93</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_093">psa 119:93</a></font></span></p>The bounds of created perfection may be defined, but those of God's law in its nature, application, and influence, are infinite. There is no human thing so perfect but that something is wanting to it; its limits are narrow, whereas God's law is of infinite breadth, reaching to all cases, perfectly meeting what each requires, and to all times (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#003">Psa 19:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#006">Psa 19:6-11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc003.htm#011">Ecc 3:11</a>). It cannot be cramped within any definitions of man's dogmatical systems. Man never outgrows the Word. It does not shock the ignorant man with declared anticipations of discoveries which he had not yet made; while in it the man of science finds his newest discoveries by tacit anticipations provided for.

 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#141">Psalms 119:141</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_141">psa 119:141</a></font></span></p>The pious, however despised of men, are distinguished in God's sight by a regard for His law.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#142">Psalms 119:142</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_142">psa 119:142</a></font></span></p>The principles of God's government are permanent and reliable, and in the deepest distress His people find them a theme of delightful meditation and a source of reviving power (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#017">Psa 119:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#116">Psa 119:116</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>law is the truth--It therefore cannot deceive as to its promises. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>everlasting-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#003">Psa 111:3</a>), though to outward appearance seeming dead.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#145">Psalms 119:145</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_145">psa 119:145</a></font></span></p>KOPH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#145">Psa 119:145-152</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>An intelligent devotion is led by divine promises and is directed to an increase of gracious affections, arising from a contemplation of revealed truth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#147">Psalms 119:147</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_147">psa 119:147</a></font></span></p>prevented--literally, "came before," anticipated not only the dawn, but even the usual periods of the night; when the night watches, which might be expected to find me asleep, come, they find me awake (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#006">Psa 63:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#004">Psa 77:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam002.htm#019">Lam 2:19</a>). Such is the earnestness of the desire and love for God's truth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#149">Psalms 119:149</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_149">psa 119:149</a></font></span></p>quicken me--revive my heart according to those principles of justice, founded on Thine own nature, and revealed in Thy law, which specially set forth Thy mercy to the humble as well as justice to the wicked (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#030">Psa 119:30</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#150">Psalms 119:150</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_150">psa 119:150</a></font></span></p>Though the wicked are near to injure, because far from God's law, He is near to help, and faithful to His word, which abides for ever.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#153">Psalms 119:153</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_153">psa 119:153</a></font></span></p>RESH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#153">Psa 119:153-160</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>Though the remembering of God's law is not meritorious, yet it evinces a filial temper and provides the pious with promises to plead, while the wicked in neglecting His law, reject God and despise His promises (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#013">Psa 9:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#001">Psa 43:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#018">Psa 69:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#154">Psalms 119:154</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_154">psa 119:154</a></font></span></p>Plead, &amp;c.--HENGSTENBERG translates, "Fight my fight." (See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#001">Psa 35:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#001">Psa 43:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#009">Mic 7:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#156">Psalms 119:156</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_156">psa 119:156</a></font></span></p>(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#149">Psa 119:149</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#157">Psalms 119:157</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_157">psa 119:157</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#086">Psa 119:86-87</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#095">Psa 119:95</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#158">Psalms 119:158</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_158">psa 119:158</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#136">Psa 119:136</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>transgressors--or, literally, "traitors," who are faithless to a righteous sovereign and side with His enemies (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#003">Psa 25:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#008">Psa 25:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#159">Psalms 119:159</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_159">psa 119:159</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#121">Psa 119:121-126</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#153">Psa 119:153-155</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>quicken me, O Lord, according to thy lovingkindness-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#088">Psa 119:88</a>). This prayer occurs here for the ninth time, showing a deep sense of frailty.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#160">Psalms 119:160</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_160">psa 119:160</a></font></span></p>God has been ever faithful, and the principles of His government will ever continue worthy of confidence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>from the beginning--that is, "every word from Genesis (called so by the Jews from its first words, 'In the beginning') to the end of the Scriptures is true." HENGSTENBERG translates more literally, "The sum of thy words is truth." The sense is substantially the same. The whole body of revelation is truth. "Thy Word is nothing but truth" [LUTHER].
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#161">Psalms 119:161</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_161">psa 119:161</a></font></span></p>SCHIN. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#161">Psa 119:161-168</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#046">Psa 119:46</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#086">Psa 119:86</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>awe--reverential, not slavish fear, which could not coexist with love (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#163">Psa 119:163</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#008">Jo1 4:8</a>). Instead of fearing his persecutors, he fears God's Word alone (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#004">Luk 12:4-5</a>). The Jews inscribe in the first page of the great Bible (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#017">Gen 28:17</a>), "How dreadful is this place! This is none other but the house of God, and this is the gate of heaven!"
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#162">Psalms 119:162</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_162">psa 119:162</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#044">Mat 13:44-45</a>). Though persecuted by the mighty, the pious are not turned from revering God's authority to seek their favor, but rejoice in the possession of this "pearl of great price," as great victors in spoils. Hating falsehood and loving truth, often, every day, praising God for it, they find peace and freedom from temptation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#163">Psalms 119:163</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_163">psa 119:163</a></font></span></p>lying--that is, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#029">Psa 119:29</a>, unfaithfulness to the covenant of God with His people; apostasy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#165">Psalms 119:165</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_165">psa 119:165</a></font></span></p>nothing shall offend them--or, "cause them to offend" (compare Margin).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#166">Psalms 119:166</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_166">psa 119:166</a></font></span></p>As they keep God's law from motives of love for it, and are free from slavish fear, the are ready to subject their lives to His inspection.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#168">Psalms 119:168</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_168">psa 119:168</a></font></span></p>all my ways are before thee--I wish to order my ways as before Thee, rather than in reference to man (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#001">Gen 19:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#023">Psa 73:23</a>). All men's ways are under God's eye (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#021">Pro 5:21</a>); the godly alone realize the fact, and live accordingly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#169">Psalms 119:169</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_169">psa 119:169</a></font></span></p>TAU. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#169">Psa 119:169-176</a>).
 <p>
 </p><p>The prayer for understanding of the truth precedes that for deliverance. The fulfilment of the first is the basis of the fulfilment of the second (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#011">Psa 90:11-17</a>). On the terms "cry" and "supplication" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#009">Psa 6:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#001">Psa 17:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#171">Psalms 119:171</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_171">psa 119:171</a></font></span></p>shall utter--or, "pour out praise" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#002">Psa 19:2</a>); shall cause Thy praises to stream forth as from a bubbling, overflowing fountain.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#172">Psalms 119:172</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_172">psa 119:172</a></font></span></p>My tongue shall speak of thy word--literally, "answer Thy Word," that is, with praise, respond to Thy word. Every expression in which we praise God and His Word is a response, or acknowledgment, corresponding to the perfections of Him whom we praise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#173">Psalms 119:173</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_173">psa 119:173</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#077">Psa 119:77</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#081">Psa 119:81</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#092">Psa 119:92</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>I have chosen--in preference to all other objects of delight.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#175">Psalms 119:175</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_175">psa 119:175</a></font></span></p>Save me that I may praise Thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy judgments--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#149">Psa 119:149</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#156">Psa 119:156</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#176">Psalms 119:176</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_176">psa 119:176</a></font></span></p>Though a wanderer from God, the truly pious ever desires to be drawn back to Him; and, though for a time negligent of duty, he never forgets the commandments by which it is taught. 
 <p>
 </p><p>lost--therefore utterly helpless as to recovering itself (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#006">Jer 50:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#004">Luk 15:4</a>). Not only the sinner before conversion, but the believer after conversion, is unable to recover himself; but the latter, after temporary wandering, knows to whom to look for restoration. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#175">Psa 119:175-176</a> seem to sum up the petitions, confessions, and professions of the Psalm. The writer desires God's favor, that he may praise Him for His truth, confesses that he has erred, but, in the midst of all his wanderings and adversities, professes an abiding attachment to the revealed Word of God, the theme of such repeated eulogies, and the recognized source of such great and unnumbered blessings. Thus the Psalm, though more than usually didactic, is made the medium of both parts of devotion--prayer and praise.
