Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 59</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#001">Psalms 59:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 59:1</a></font></span></p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#001">Psa 57:1</a>, title, and for history, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1019.htm#011">Sa1 19:11</a>, &amp;c. The scope is very similar to that of the fifty-seventh: prayer in view of malicious and violent foes, and joy in prospect of relief. (Psa. 59:1-17)
 <p>
 </p><p>defend me--(Compare Margin). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>rise up . . . me--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#007">Psa 17:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#002">Psalms 59:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 59:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#005">Psa 5:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#008">Psa 6:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#004">Psalms 59:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 59:4</a></font></span></p>prepare, &amp;c.--literally, "set themselves as in array." 
 <p>
 </p><p>awake--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#007">Psa 3:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#006">Psa 7:6</a>), appeals to God in His covenant relation to His people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#018">Psa 9:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#006">Psalms 59:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 59:6</a></font></span></p>They are as ravening dogs seeking prey, and as such, 
 <p>
 </p><p>belch out--that is, slanders, their impudent barkings.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#007">Psalms 59:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 59:7</a></font></span></p>for who, say they--For the full expression with the supplied words, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#005">Psa 64:5</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#008">Psalms 59:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 59:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#004">Psa 2:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#013">Psa 37:13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#009">Psalms 59:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 59:9</a></font></span></p>By judicious expositors, and on good grounds, this is better rendered, "O my strength, on Thee will I wait" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#017">Psa 59:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>defence--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#003">Psa 18:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#010">Psalms 59:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 59:10</a></font></span></p>prevent me-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa021.htm#003">Psa 21:3</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>see my desire--in their overthrow (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#007">Psa 54:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>enemies--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#008">Psa 5:8</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#011">Psalms 59:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 59:11</a></font></span></p>Slay them not--at once (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg002.htm#021">Jdg 2:21-23</a>); but perpetuate their punishment (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen004.htm#012">Gen 4:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num032.htm#013">Num 32:13</a>), by scattering or making them wander, and humble them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#012">Psalms 59:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 59:12</a></font></span></p>let them even be . . . taken in their pride--while evincing it--that is, to be punished for their lies, &amp;c.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#013">Psalms 59:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 59:13</a></font></span></p>Though delayed for wise reasons, the utter destruction of the wicked must come at last, and God's presence and power in and for His Church will be known abroad (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#046">Sa1 17:46</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#010">Psa 46:10-11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#014">Psalms 59:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 59:14</a></font></span></p>Meanwhile let the rapacious dogs prowl, they cannot hurt the pious; yea, they shall wander famished and sleepless.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#015">Psalms 59:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 59:15</a></font></span></p>grudge if, &amp;c.--literally, "they shall stay all night," that is, obtain nothing.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#016">Psalms 59:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 59:16</a></font></span></p>Contrast the lot of God's servant, who employs his time in God's praise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sing aloud . . . in the morning--when they retire famishing and disappointed, or it may denote delightful diligence in praise, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#005">Psa 30:5</a>.
 </p><p>
