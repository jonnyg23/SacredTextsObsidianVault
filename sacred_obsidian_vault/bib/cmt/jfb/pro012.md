Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001">Proverbs 12:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 12:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 12:1-28)
 <p>
 </p><p>loveth knowledge--as the fruit of instruction or training (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#002">Pro 1:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>hateth reproof-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>brutish--stupid, regardless of his own welfare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#010">Psa 49:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#022">Psa 73:22</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#003">Proverbs 12:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 12:3</a></font></span></p>Wickedness cannot give permanent prosperity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>root . . . not be moved--firm as a flourishing tree-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#003">Psa 1:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#005">Psa 15:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#008">Jer 17:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#004">Proverbs 12:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 12:4</a></font></span></p>A virtuous woman--in the wide sense of well-disposed to all moral duties (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#010">Pro 31:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>maketh ashamed--that is, by misconduct. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>rottenness--an incurable evil.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#005">Proverbs 12:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 12:5</a></font></span></p>thoughts--or, "purposes." 
 <p>
 </p><p>are right--literally, "are judgment," that is, true decisions. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>counsels--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#014">Pro 11:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>deceit--contrary to truth and honesty.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#006">Proverbs 12:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 12:6</a></font></span></p>The words--or, "expressed designs" of the wicked are for evil purposes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the mouth--or, "words" of the righteous delivering instead of ensnaring men.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#007">Proverbs 12:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 12:7</a></font></span></p>Such conduct brings a proper return, by the destruction of the wicked and well-being of the righteous and his family.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#008">Proverbs 12:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 12:8</a></font></span></p>despised--as opposed to commended (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#012">Pro 11:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>perverse heart--or, "wicked principles," as opposed to one of wisdom.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#009">Proverbs 12:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 12:9</a></font></span></p>despised--held in little repute, obscure (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#023">Sa1 18:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#005">Isa 3:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hath a servant--implying some means of honest living. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>honoureth himself--is self-conceited.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#010">Proverbs 12:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 12:10</a></font></span></p>regardeth--literally, "knoweth" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#006">Psa 1:6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>mercies . . . cruel--as acts of compassion ungraciously rendered to the needy. The righteous more regards a beast than the wicked a man.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#011">Proverbs 12:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 12:11</a></font></span></p>The idler's fate is the result of indolence and want of principle (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#032">Pro 6:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#007">Pro 7:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#012">Proverbs 12:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 12:12</a></font></span></p>the wicked . . . evil--They love the crafty arts of deception. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the root . . . fruit--their own resources supply them; or, it may be rendered: "He (God) giveth, or, sets (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze017.htm#022">Eze 17:22</a>) the root of the righteous," and hence it is firm: or, the verb is impersonal; "As to the root . . . it is firm" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#019">Pro 17:19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#013">Proverbs 12:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 12:13</a></font></span></p>The wicked is snared, &amp;c.--The sentiment expanded. While the wicked, such as liars, flatterers, &amp;c., fall by their own words, the righteous are unhurt. Their good conduct makes friends, and God rewards them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#015">Proverbs 12:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 12:15</a></font></span></p>The way . . . eyes--The fool is self-conceited (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001">Pro 12:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#032">Pro 1:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam003.htm#017">Jam 3:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#016">Proverbs 12:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 12:16</a></font></span></p>prudent . . . shame--He is slow to denounce his insulters (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#019">Jam 1:19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#018">Proverbs 12:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 12:18</a></font></span></p>speaketh--literally, "speaketh hastily," or indiscreetly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa106.htm#033">Psa 106:33</a>), as an angry man retorts harsh and provoking invectives. 
 <p>
 </p><p>tongue . . . health--by soothing and gentle language.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#019">Proverbs 12:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 12:19</a></font></span></p>Words of truth are consistent, and stand all tests, while lies are soon discovered and exposed.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#020">Proverbs 12:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 12:20</a></font></span></p>that imagine--or, "plan" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#029">Pro 3:29</a>). They design a deceitful course, to which, with all its evils and dangers to others and themselves, the happiness of peace-makers is opposed (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#009">Mat 5:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#018">Rom 12:18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#021">Proverbs 12:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 12:21</a></font></span></p>no evil--(as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#010">Psa 91:10</a>), under God's wise limitations (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#028">Rom 8:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>mischief--as penal evil.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#022">Proverbs 12:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 12:22</a></font></span></p>deal truly--or, "faithfully," that is, according to promises (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#021">Joh 3:21</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#023">Proverbs 12:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 12:23</a></font></span></p>concealeth--by his modesty (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#014">Pro 10:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#013">Pro 11:13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>heart . . . proclaimeth--as his lips speak his thoughts (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#003">Ecc 10:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#024">Proverbs 12:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 12:24</a></font></span></p>slothful--(Compare Margin), so called because he fails to meet his promises. 
 <p>
 </p><p>under tribute--not denoting legal taxes, but the obligation of dependence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#025">Proverbs 12:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 12:25</a></font></span></p>a good word--one of comfort.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#026">Proverbs 12:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 12:26</a></font></span></p>more excellent--(Compare Margin); or, "more successful," while the wicked fail; or, we may read it: "The righteous guides his friend, but," &amp;c., that is, The ability of the righteous to aid others is contrasted with the ruin to which the way of the wicked leads themselves.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#027">Proverbs 12:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 12:27</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#024">Pro 12:24</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>took in hunting--or, "his venison." He does not improve his advantages. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the substance . . . precious--or, "the wealth of a man of honor is being diligent," or "diligence." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>precious--literally, "honor" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#001">Ecc 10:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#028">Proverbs 12:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 12:28</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#008">Pro 8:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#020">Pro 8:20</a>, &amp;c.). A sentiment often stated; here first affirmatively, then negatively.
