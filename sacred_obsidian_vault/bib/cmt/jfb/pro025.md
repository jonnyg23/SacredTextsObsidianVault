 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#001">Proverbs 25:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 25:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 25:1-28)
 <p>
 </p><p>The character of these proverbs sustains the title (see Introduction). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>also--refers to the former part of the book. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>copied out--literally, "transferred," that is, from some other book to this; not given from memory.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#002">Proverbs 25:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 25:2</a></font></span></p>God's unsearchableness impresses us with awe (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#015">Isa 45:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#033">Rom 11:33</a>). But kings, being finite, should confer with wise counsellors;
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#003">Proverbs 25:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 25:3</a></font></span></p>Ye wisely keeping state secrets, which to common men are as inaccessible heights and depths.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#004">Proverbs 25:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 25:4</a></font></span></p>As separating impurities from ore leaves pure silver, so taking from a king wicked counsellors leaves a wise and beneficent government.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#005">Proverbs 25:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 25:5</a></font></span></p>before--or, "in presence of," as courtiers stood about a king.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#006">Proverbs 25:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 25:6</a></font></span></p>Do not intrude into the presence of the king, for the elevation of the humble is honorable, but the humbling of the proud disgraceful (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#008">Luk 14:8-10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#008">Proverbs 25:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 25:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#030">Pro 3:30</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>lest . . . shame--lest you do what you ought not, when shamed by defeat, or "lest thou art shut out from doing any thing."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#009">Proverbs 25:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 25:9</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#025">Mat 5:25</a>, Margin). 
 <p>
 </p><p>secret--that is, of your opponent, for his disadvantage, and so you be disgraced, not having discussed your difficulties with him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#011">Proverbs 25:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 25:11</a></font></span></p>a word fitly--literally, "quickly," as wheels roll, just in time. The comparison as apples . . . silver gives a like sense. 
 <p>
 </p><p>apples, &amp;c.--either real apples of golden color, in a silver network basket, or imitations on silver embroidery.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#012">Proverbs 25:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 25:12</a></font></span></p>Those who desire to know and do rightly, most highly esteem good counsel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#009">Pro 9:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#031">Pro 15:31</a>). The listening ear is better than one hung with gold.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#013">Proverbs 25:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 25:13</a></font></span></p>Snow from mountains was used to cool drinks; so refreshing is a faithful messenger (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#017">Pro 13:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#014">Proverbs 25:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 25:14</a></font></span></p>clouds--literally, "vapors" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer010.htm#013">Jer 10:13</a>), clouds only in appearance. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a false gift--promised, but not given.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#015">Proverbs 25:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 25:15</a></font></span></p>Gentleness and kindness overcome the most powerful and obstinate. 
 <p>
 </p><p>long forbearing--or, "slowness to anger" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029">Pro 14:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#018">Pro 15:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#016">Proverbs 25:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 25:16</a></font></span></p>A comparison, as a surfeit of honey produces physical disgust, so your company, however agreeable in moderation, may, if excessive, lead your friend to hate you.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#018">Proverbs 25:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 25:18</a></font></span></p>A false witness is as destructive to reputation, as such weapons to the body (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#028">Pro 24:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>beareth . . . witness--literally, "answereth questions," as before a judge, against his neighbor.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#019">Proverbs 25:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 25:19</a></font></span></p>Treachery annoys as well as deceives.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#020">Proverbs 25:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 25:20</a></font></span></p>Not only is the incongruity of songs (that is, joyful) and sadness meant, but an accession of sadness, by want of sympathy, is implied.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#021">Proverbs 25:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 25:21</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#044">Mat 5:44</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#020">Rom 12:20</a>). As metals are melted by heaping coals upon them, so is the heart softened by kindness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#023">Proverbs 25:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 25:23</a></font></span></p>Better, "As the north wind bringeth forth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#002">Psa 90:2</a>) or produces rain, so does a concealed or slandering tongue produce anger."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#024">Proverbs 25:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 25:24</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#009">Pro 21:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#019">Pro 21:19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#025">Proverbs 25:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 25:25</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#013">Pro 25:13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>good news--that is, of some loved interest or absent friend, the more grateful as coming from afar.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#026">Proverbs 25:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 25:26</a></font></span></p>From troubled fountains and corrupt springs no healthy water is to be had, so when the righteous are oppressed by the wicked, their power for good is lessened or destroyed.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#027">Proverbs 25:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 25:27</a></font></span></p>Satiety surfeits (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#016">Pro 25:16</a>); so men who are self-glorious find shame. 
 <p>
 </p><p>is not glory--"not" is supplied from the first clause, or "is grievous," in which sense a similar word is used (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#002">Pro 27:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro025.htm#028">Proverbs 25:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 25:28</a></font></span></p>Such are exposed to the incursions of evil thoughts and successful temptations.
