 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa117.htm#002">Psalms 117:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 117:2</a></font></span></p>This may be regarded as a doxology, suitable to be appended to any Psalm of similar character, and prophetical of the prevalence of God's grace in the world, in which aspect Paul quotes it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#011">Rom 15:11</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#002">Psa 47:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#008">Psa 66:8</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa117.htm#001">Psa 117:1-2</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>is great toward us--literally, "prevailed over" or "protected us."
