 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#001">Proverbs 26:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 26:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 26:1-28)
 <p>
 </p><p>The incongruities of nature illustrate also those of the moral world. The fool's unworthiness is also implied (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#007">Pro 17:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#010">Pro 19:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#002">Proverbs 26:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 26:2</a></font></span></p>Though not obvious to us, 
 <p>
 </p><p>the bird--literally, "sparrow"--and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>swallow--have an object in their motions, so penal evil falls on none without a reason.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#003">Proverbs 26:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 26:3</a></font></span></p>The rod is as much needed by fools and as well suited to them, as whips and bridles are for beasts.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#004">Proverbs 26:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 26:4</a></font></span></p>Answer not--that is, approvingly by like folly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#005">Proverbs 26:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 26:5</a></font></span></p>Answer--by reproof.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#006">Proverbs 26:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 26:6</a></font></span></p>A fool fails by folly as surely as if he were maimed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>drinketh damage--that is, gets it abundantly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#016">Job 15:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#007">Job 34:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#007">Proverbs 26:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 26:7</a></font></span></p>legs . . . equal--or, "take away the legs," or "the legs . . . are weak." In any case the idea is that they are the occasion of an awkwardness, such as the fool shows in using a parable or proverb (see Introduction; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#007">Pro 17:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#008">Proverbs 26:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 26:8</a></font></span></p>A stone, bound in a sling, is useless; so honor, conferred on a fool, is thrown away.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#009">Proverbs 26:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 26:9</a></font></span></p>As vexatious and unmanageable as a thorn in a drunkard's hand is a parable to a fool. He will be as apt to misuse is as to use it rightly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#010">Proverbs 26:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 26:10</a></font></span></p>Various versions of this are proposed (compare Margin). Better perhaps--"Much He injures (or literally, "wounds") all who reward," &amp;c., that is, society is injured by encouraging evil men. 
 <p>
 </p><p>transgressors--may be rendered "vagrants." The word "God" is improperly supplied.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#011">Proverbs 26:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 26:11</a></font></span></p>returneth . . . folly--Though disgusting to others, the fool delights in his folly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#012">Proverbs 26:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 26:12</a></font></span></p>The self-conceited are taught with more difficulty than the stupid.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#013">Proverbs 26:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 26:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#013">Pro 22:13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#014">Proverbs 26:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 26:14</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#010">Pro 6:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#033">Pro 24:33</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#015">Proverbs 26:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 26:15</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#024">Pro 19:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#016">Proverbs 26:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 26:16</a></font></span></p>The thoughtless being ignorant of their ignorance are conceited.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#017">Proverbs 26:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 26:17</a></font></span></p>meddleth--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#019">Pro 20:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#021">Pro 24:21</a>; as either holding a dog by the ears or letting him go involves danger, so success in another man's strife or failure involves a useless risk of reputation, does no good, and may do us harm.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#018">Proverbs 26:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 26:18</a></font></span></p>Such are reckless of results.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#020">Proverbs 26:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 26:20</a></font></span></p>The talebearers foster (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#028">Pro 16:28</a>), and the contentious excite, strife.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#022">Proverbs 26:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 26:22</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008">Pro 18:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#023">Proverbs 26:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 26:23</a></font></span></p>Warm professions can no more give value to insincerity than silver coating to rude earthenware.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#024">Proverbs 26:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 26:24</a></font></span></p>dissembleth--though an unusual sense of the word (compare Margin), is allowable, and better suits the context, which sets forth hypocrisy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#025">Proverbs 26:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 26:25</a></font></span></p>Sentiment of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#024">Pro 26:24</a> carried out. 
 <p>
 </p><p>seven abominations in his heart--that is, very many (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#016">Pro 24:16</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#026">Proverbs 26:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 26:26</a></font></span></p>Deceit will at last be exposed, and the wicked by their own arts often bring on retribution (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#013">Pro 12:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#016">Psa 7:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#017">Psa 9:17</a>, &amp;c.).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#028">Proverbs 26:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 26:28</a></font></span></p>Men hate those they injure. 
 <p>
 </p><p>A lying tongue--"lips" for the persons (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#024">Pro 4:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa012.htm#003">Psa 12:3</a>).
 </p><p>
