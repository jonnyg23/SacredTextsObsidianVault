Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 63</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001">Psalms 63:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 63:1</a></font></span></p>The historical occasion referred to by the title was probably during Absalom's rebellion (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2015.htm#023">Sa2 15:23</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2015.htm#028">Sa2 15:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2016.htm#002">Sa2 16:2</a>). David expresses an earnest desire for God's favor, and a confident expectation of realizing it in his deliverance and the ruin of his enemies. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001">Psa 63:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>early . . . seek thee--earnestly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#009">Isa 26:9</a>). The figurative terms-- 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>dry and thirsty--literally, "weary," denoting moral destitution, suited his outward circumstances. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>soul--and--flesh--the whole man (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#009">Psa 16:9-10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#002">Psalms 63:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 63:2</a></font></span></p>The special object of desire was God's perfections as displayed in his worship (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#004">Psa 27:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#003">Psalms 63:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 63:3</a></font></span></p>Experiencing God's mercy, which exceeds all the blessings of life, his lips will be opened for his praise (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#015">Psa 51:15</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#004">Psalms 63:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 63:4</a></font></span></p>Thus--literally, "Truly." 
 <p>
 </p><p>will I bless--praise Thee (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#001">Psa 34:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>lift up my hands--in worship (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#002">Psa 28:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in thy name--in praise of Thy perfections.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#005">Psalms 63:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 63:5</a></font></span></p>Full spiritual blessings satisfy his desires, and acts of praise fill his thoughts and time.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#006">Psalms 63:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 63:6</a></font></span></p>night--as well as day. Past favors assure him of future, and hence he presses earnestly near to God, whose power sustains him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#008">Psa 17:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005">Psa 60:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#009">Psalms 63:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 63:9</a></font></span></p>those . . . to destroy it--or literally, "to ruin," or, "for ruin"; that is, such as seek to injure me (are) for ruin, appointed to it (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#008">Psa 35:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>shall go . . . earth--into the grave, or, to death; as their bodies are represented as a portion for--
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#010">Psalms 63:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 63:10</a></font></span></p>foxes--literally, "jackals."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#011">Psalms 63:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 63:11</a></font></span></p>the king--that is, David himself, and all who reverence God, "shall share a glorious part," while treacherous foes shall be for ever silenced (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#004">Psa 62:4</a>).
