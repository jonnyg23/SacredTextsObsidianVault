Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 98</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#001">Psalms 98:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 98:1</a></font></span></p>In view of the wonders of grace and righteousness displayed in God's salvation, the whole creation is invited to unite in praise. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#001">Psa 98:1-9</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>gotten . . . victory--literally, "made salvation," enabled Him to save His people. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>right hand, and . . . arm--denote power. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>holy arm--or, "arm of holiness," the power of His united moral perfections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#003">Psa 22:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#011">Psa 32:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#002">Psalms 98:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 98:2</a></font></span></p>salvation--the result of His righteousness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#017">Psa 7:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#001">Psa 31:1</a>), and both are publicly displayed.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#003">Psalms 98:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 98:3</a></font></span></p>The union of mercy and truth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#003">Psa 57:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa085.htm#010">Psa 85:10</a>) secure the blessings of the promise (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#018">Gen 18:18</a>) to all the world (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#010">Isa 52:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#004">Psalms 98:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 98:4</a></font></span></p>make a loud noise--or, "burst forth" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#007">Isa 14:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#023">Isa 44:23</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>before . . . King--hail Him as your sovereign; and while, with every aid to demonstrate zeal and joy, intelligent creatures are invited to praise, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa096.htm#011">Psa 96:11-13</a>, inanimate nature is also summoned to honor Him who triumphs and rules in righteousness and equity.
 </p><p>
