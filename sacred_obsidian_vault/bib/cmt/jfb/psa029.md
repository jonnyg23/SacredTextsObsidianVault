Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 29</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#001">Psalms 29:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 29:1</a></font></span></p>Trust in God is encouraged by the celebration of His mighty power as illustrated in His dominion over the natural world, in some of its most terrible and wonderful exhibitions. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#001">Psa 29:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Give--or, "ascribe" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#003">Deu 32:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>mighty--or, "sons of the mighty" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#006">Psa 89:6</a>). Heavenly beings, as angels.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#002">Psalms 29:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 29:2</a></font></span></p>name--as (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psa 5:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#001">Psa 8:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>beauty of holiness--the loveliness of a spiritual worship, of which the perceptible beauty of the sanctuary worship was but a type.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#003">Psalms 29:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 29:3</a></font></span></p>The voice of the Lord--audible exhibition of His power in the tempest, of which thunder is a specimen, but not the uniform or sole example. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the waters--the clouds or vapors (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#011">Psa 18:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer010.htm#013">Jer 10:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#004">Psalms 29:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 29:4</a></font></span></p>powerful . . . majesty--literally, "in power, in majesty."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#005">Psalms 29:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 29:5</a></font></span></p>The tall and large cedars, especially of Lebanon, are shivered, utterly broken. The waving of the mountain forests before the wind is expressed by the figure of skipping or leaping.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#007">Psalms 29:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 29:7</a></font></span></p>divideth--literally, "hews off." The lightning, like flakes and splinters hewed from stone or wood, flies through the air.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#008">Psalms 29:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 29:8</a></font></span></p>the wilderness--especially Kadesh, south of Judea, is selected as another scene of this display of divine power, as a vast and desolate region impresses the mind, like mountains, with images of grandeur.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#009">Psalms 29:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 29:9</a></font></span></p>Terror-stricken animals and denuded forests close the illustration. In view of this scene of awful sublimity, God's worshippers respond to the call of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#002">Psa 29:2</a>, and speak or cry, "Glory!" By "temple," or "palace" (God's residence, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psa 5:7</a>), may here be meant heaven, or the whole frame of nature, as the angels are called on for praise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#010">Psalms 29:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 29:10</a></font></span></p>Over this terrible raging of the elements God is enthroned, directing and restraining by sovereign power; and hence the comfort of His people. "This awful God is ours, our Father and our Love."
