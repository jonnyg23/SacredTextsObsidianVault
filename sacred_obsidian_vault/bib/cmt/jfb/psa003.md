Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#001">Psalms 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 3:1</a></font></span></p>For the historical occasion mentioned, compare 2Sa. 15:1-17:29. David, in the midst of great distress, with filial confidence, implores God's aid, and, anticipating relief, offers praise. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#001">Psa 3:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord . . . increased--The extent of the rebellion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2015.htm#013">Sa2 15:13</a>) surprises and grieves him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#002">Psalms 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 3:2</a></font></span></p>say of my soul--that is, "of me" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#003">Psa 25:3</a>). This use of "soul" is common; perhaps it arose from regarding the soul as man's chief part. 
 <p>
 </p><p>no help . . . in God--rejected by Him. This is the bitterest reproach for a pious man, and denotes a spirit of malignant triumph. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Selah--This word is of very obscure meaning. It probably denotes rest or pause, both as to the music and singing, intimating something emphatic in the sentiment (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#016">Psa 9:16</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#003">Psalms 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 3:3</a></font></span></p>But--literally, "and" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>). He repels the reproach by avowing his continued trust. 
 <p>
 </p><p>shield--a favorite and often-used figure for protection. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my glory--its source. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>lifter up of mine head--one who raises me from despondency.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#004">Psalms 3:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 3:4</a></font></span></p>cried . . . heard--Such has been my experience. The latter verb denotes a gracious hearing or answering. 
 <p>
 </p><p>out of--or, "from." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>his holy hill--Zion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>). His visible earthly residence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#005">Psalms 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 3:5</a></font></span></p>the Lord sustained me--literally, "will sustain me," as if his language or thought when he laid down, and the reason of his composure.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#006">Psalms 3:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 3:6</a></font></span></p>ten thousands of people--or, "myriads," any very great number (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2016.htm#018">Sa2 16:18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#007">Psalms 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 3:7</a></font></span></p>Arise, O Lord--God is figuratively represented as asleep to denote His apparent indifference (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#006">Psa 7:6</a>). The use of "cheekbone" and "teeth" represents his enemies as fierce, like wild beasts ready to devour (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#002">Psa 27:2</a>), and smiting their cheekbone (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1022.htm#024">Kg1 22:24</a>) denotes violence and insult. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thou hast broken--God took his part, utterly depriving the enemy of power to injure.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#008">Psalms 3:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 3:8</a></font></span></p>An ascription of praise to a delivering God, whose favor is an efficient benefit.
