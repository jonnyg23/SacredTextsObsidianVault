Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 75</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001">Psalms 75:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 75:1</a></font></span></p>Al-taschith--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#001">Psa 57:1</a>, title). In impending danger, the Psalmist, anticipating relief in view of God's righteous government, takes courage and renders praise. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001">Psa 75:1-10</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>God's name or perfections are set forth by His wondrous works.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#002">Psalms 75:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 75:2</a></font></span></p>These verses express the purpose of God to administer a just government, and in a time of anarchy that He sustains the nation. Some apply the words to the Psalmist. 
 <p>
 </p><p>receive the congregation--literally, "take a set time" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#013">Psa 102:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos002.htm#003">Hos 2:3</a>), or an assembly at a set time--that is, for judging.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#003">Psalms 75:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 75:3</a></font></span></p>pillars of it-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1002.htm#008">Sa1 2:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#004">Psalms 75:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 75:4</a></font></span></p>Here the writer speaks in view of God's declaration, warning the wicked. 
 <p>
 </p><p>Lift . . . up the horn--to exalt power, here, of the wicked himself--that is, to be arrogant or self-elated.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#005">Psalms 75:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 75:5</a></font></span></p>speak . . . neck--insolently.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#006">Psalms 75:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 75:6</a></font></span></p>promotion--literally, "a lifting up." God is the only right judge of merit.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#008">Psalms 75:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 75:8</a></font></span></p>in the hand . . . a cup . . . red--God's wrath often thus represented (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#017">Isa 51:17</a> <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#015">Jer 25:15</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>but the dregs--literally, "surely the dregs, they shall drain it."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#009">Psalms 75:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 75:9</a></font></span></p>Contrasted is the lot of the pious who will praise God, and, acting under His direction, will destroy the power of the wicked, and exalt that of the righteous.
