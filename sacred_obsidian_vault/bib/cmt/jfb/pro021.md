Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#001">Proverbs 21:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 21:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 21:1-31)
 <p>
 </p><p>rivers--irrigating channels (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#003">Psa 1:3</a>), whose course was easily turned (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu011.htm#010">Deu 11:10</a>). God disposes even kings as He pleases (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#009">Pro 16:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#015">Psa 33:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#002">Proverbs 21:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 21:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#002">Pro 14:2</a>; Pro. 16:2-25).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#003">Proverbs 21:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 21:3</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#007">Psa 50:7-15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#011">Isa 1:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#017">Isa 1:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#004">Proverbs 21:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 21:4</a></font></span></p>high look--(Compare Margin; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psa 131:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>proud heart--or, "heart of breadth," one that is swollen (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa101.htm#005">Psa 101:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ploughing--better "lamp," a frequent figure for prosperity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#020">Pro 20:20</a>); hence joy or delight.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#005">Proverbs 21:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 21:5</a></font></span></p>The contrast is between steady industry and rashness (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#002">Pro 19:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#006">Proverbs 21:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 21:6</a></font></span></p>The getting--or, "what is obtained" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job007.htm#002">Job 7:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#013">Jer 22:13</a>, Hebrew). 
 <p>
 </p><p>vanity . . . to and fro--as fleeting as chaff or stubble in the wind (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#017">Pro 20:17-21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#010">Psa 62:10</a>). Such gettings are unsatisfactory. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>them . . . death--act as if they did (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#036">Pro 8:36</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#019">Pro 17:19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#007">Proverbs 21:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 21:7</a></font></span></p>robbery--or, "destruction," especially oppression, of which they are authors. 
 <p>
 </p><p>shall destroy--literally, "cut with a saw" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#009">Kg1 7:9</a>), that is, utterly ruin them. Their sins shall be visited on them in kind. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to do judgment--what is just and right.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#008">Proverbs 21:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 21:8</a></font></span></p>of man--any one; his way is opposed to truth, and also estranged from it. The pure proves himself such by his right conduct.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#009">Proverbs 21:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 21:9</a></font></span></p>corner--a turret or arbor on the roof. 
 <p>
 </p><p>brawling--or contentious. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>wide house--literally, "house of fellowship," large enough for several families.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#010">Proverbs 21:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 21:10</a></font></span></p>So strongly does he desire to do evil (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#003">Psa 10:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc008.htm#011">Ecc 8:11</a>), that he will not even spare his friend if in his way.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#011">Proverbs 21:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 21:11</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#025">Pro 19:25</a>). That which the simple learn by the terrors of punishment, the wise learn by teaching.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#012">Proverbs 21:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 21:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#035">Psa 37:35-38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#017">Psa 73:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#020">Psa 73:20</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>house--family or interests. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>overthroweth--either supply "God" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#024">Pro 10:24</a>), or the word is used impersonally.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#013">Proverbs 21:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 21:13</a></font></span></p>The principles of retribution, often taught (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#026">Psa 18:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#001">Mat 7:1-12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#014">Proverbs 21:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 21:14</a></font></span></p>The effect of bribery (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#023">Pro 17:23</a>) is enhanced by secrecy, as the bribed person does not wish his motives made known.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#015">Proverbs 21:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 21:15</a></font></span></p>But the just love right and need no bribes. The wicked at last meet destruction, though for a time happy in concealing corruption.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#016">Proverbs 21:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 21:16</a></font></span></p>the way of understanding--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#026">Pro 12:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#022">Pro 14:22</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>remain--that is, rest as at a journey's end; death will be his unchanging home.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#017">Proverbs 21:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 21:17</a></font></span></p>Costly luxuries impoverish.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#018">Proverbs 21:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 21:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#008">Pro 11:8</a>). By suffering what they had devised for the righteous, or brought on them, the wicked became their ransom, in the usual sense of substitutes (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos007.htm#026">Jos 7:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/est007.htm#009">Est 7:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#019">Proverbs 21:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 21:19</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#009">Pro 21:9</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>wilderness--pasture, though uninhabitable ground (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#012">Psa 65:12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#020">Proverbs 21:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 21:20</a></font></span></p>The wise, by diligence and care, lay up and increase wealth, while fools 
 <p>
 </p><p>spend--literally, "swallow it up," greedily.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#021">Proverbs 21:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 21:21</a></font></span></p>He who tries to act justly and kindly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#014">Psa 34:14</a>) will prosper and obtain justice and honor.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#022">Proverbs 21:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 21:22</a></font></span></p>"Wisdom is better than strength" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc007.htm#019">Ecc 7:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#015">Ecc 9:15</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>strength . . . thereof--that in which they confide.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#023">Proverbs 21:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 21:23</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#002">Pro 13:2-3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam003.htm#006">Jam 3:6-10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#024">Proverbs 21:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 21:24</a></font></span></p>The reproachful name is deserved by those who treat others with anger and contempt.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#025">Proverbs 21:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 21:25</a></font></span></p>desire--that is, of ease and idleness brings him to starvation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#026">Proverbs 21:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 21:26</a></font></span></p>The sin of covetousness marks the sluggard, as the virtue of benevolence the righteous.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#027">Proverbs 21:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 21:27</a></font></span></p>God regards the heart, and hypocrisy is more odious than open inconsistency. 
 <p>
 </p><p>wicked mind--or, "design" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Pro 1:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#028">Proverbs 21:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 21:28</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#005">Pro 19:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>that heareth--or heeds instruction, and so grows wise. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>speaketh constantly--or sincerely (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#005">Hab 1:5</a>), and hence is believed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#019">Pro 12:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#019">Jam 1:19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#029">Proverbs 21:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 21:29</a></font></span></p>hardeneth his face--is obstinate. 
 <p>
 </p><p>directeth . . . way--considers it, and acts advisedly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#030">Proverbs 21:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 21:30</a></font></span></p>Men's best devices and reliances are vain compared with God's, or without His aid (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#021">Pro 19:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#007">Psa 20:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#017">Psa 33:17</a>).
