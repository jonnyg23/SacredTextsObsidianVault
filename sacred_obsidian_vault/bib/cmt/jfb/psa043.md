 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#001">Psalms 43:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 43:1</a></font></span></p>Excepting the recurrence of the refrain, there is no good reason to suppose this a part of the preceding, though the scope is the same. It has always been placed separate. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#001">Psa 43:1-5</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Judge--or, "vindicate" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#018">Psa 10:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>plead, &amp;c.-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#001">Psa 35:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ungodly--neither in character or condition objects of God's favor (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psa 4:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#002">Psalms 43:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 43:2</a></font></span></p>God of my strength--by covenant relation my stronghold (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#001">Psa 18:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>cast me off--in scorn. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>because--or, "in," that is, in such circumstances of oppression.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#003">Psalms 43:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 43:3</a></font></span></p>light--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psa 27:1</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>truth--or, "faithfulness" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#005">Psa 25:5</a>), manifest it by fulfilling promises. Light and truth are personified as messengers who will bring him to the privileged place of worship. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>tabernacles--plural, in allusion to the various courts.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#004">Psalms 43:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 43:4</a></font></span></p>the altar--as the chief place of worship. The mention of the harp suggests the prominence of praise in his offering.
