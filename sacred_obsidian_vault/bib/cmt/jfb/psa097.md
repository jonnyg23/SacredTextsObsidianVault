Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 97</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#001">Psalms 97:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 97:1</a></font></span></p>The writer celebrates the Lord's dominion over nations and nature, describes its effect on foes and friends, and exhorts and encourages the latter. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#001">Psa 97:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>This dominion is a cause of joy, because, even though our minds are oppressed with terror before the throne of the King of kings (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#016">Exo 19:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu005.htm#022">Deu 5:22</a>), we know it is based on righteous principles and judgments which are according to truth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#003">Psalms 97:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 97:3</a></font></span></p>The attending illustrations of God's awful justice on enemies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#014">Psa 83:14</a>) are seen in the disclosures of His almighty power on the elements of nature (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#002">Psa 46:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#017">Psa 77:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#006">Hab 3:6</a>, &amp;c.).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#006">Psalms 97:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 97:6</a></font></span></p>heavens--or, their inhabitants (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#006">Psa 50:6</a>), as opposed to "nations" in the latter clause (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#005">Isa 40:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#018">Isa 66:18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#007">Psalms 97:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 97:7</a></font></span></p>Idolaters are utterly put to shame, for if angels must worship Him, how much more those who worshipped them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>all ye gods--literally, "all ye angels" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#005">Psa 8:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#001">Psa 138:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#006">Heb 1:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#007">Heb 2:7</a>). Paul quotes, not as a prophecy, but as language used in regard to the Lord Jehovah, who in the Old Testament theophania is the second person of the Godhead.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#008">Psalms 97:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 97:8</a></font></span></p>The exaltation of Zion's king is joy to the righteous and sorrow to the wicked. 
 <p>
 </p><p>daughters of Judah--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#011">Psa 48:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#009">Psalms 97:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 97:9</a></font></span></p>above all gods-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#003">Psa 95:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#010">Psalms 97:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 97:10</a></font></span></p>Let gratitude for the blessings of providence and grace incite saints (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psa 4:3</a>) to holy living. Spiritual blessings are in store, represented by light (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psa 27:1</a>) and gladness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#011">Psalms 97:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 97:11</a></font></span></p>sown--to spring forth abundantly for such, who alone can and well may rejoice in the holy government of their sovereign Lord (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#004">Psa 30:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#011">Psa 32:11</a>).
