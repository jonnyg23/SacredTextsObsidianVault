 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job025.htm#002">Job 25:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">job 25:2</a></font></span></p>BILDAD'S REPLY. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job025.htm#001">Job 25:1-6</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Power and terror, that is, terror-inspiring power. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>peace in his high places--implying that His power is such on high as to quell all opposition, not merely there, but on earth also. The Holy Ghost here shadowed forth Gospel truths (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#020">Col 1:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#010">Eph 1:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job025.htm#003">Job 25:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">job 25:3</a></font></span></p>armies--angels and stars (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#026">Isa 40:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer033.htm#022">Jer 33:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#005">Gen 15:5</a>; "countless," <a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#010">Dan 7:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>his light-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#017">Jam 1:17</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job025.htm#004">Job 25:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">job 25:4</a></font></span></p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job004.htm#017">Job 4:17-18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job014.htm#004">Job 14:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#014">Job 15:14</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job025.htm#005">Job 25:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">job 25:5</a></font></span></p>"Look up even unto the moon" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#015">Job 15:15</a>). "Stars" here answer to "saints" (angels) there; "the moon" here to "the heavens" there. Even the "stars," the most dazzling object to man's eye, and the angels, of which the stars are emblems (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job004.htm#018">Job 4:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev009.htm#001">Rev 9:1</a>), are imperfect in His sight. Theirs is the light and purity but of creatures; His of the Creator.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job025.htm#006">Job 25:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">job 25:6</a></font></span></p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job004.htm#019">Job 4:19-21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#016">Job 15:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>worm . . . worm--Two distinct Hebrew words. The first, a worm bred in putridity; alluding to man's corruption. The second a crawling worm; implying that man is weak and grovelling.
 </p><p>
