Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001">Proverbs 10:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 10:1</a></font></span></p>Here begins the second part of the book, Pro. 10:1-22:16, which, with the third, Pro. 22:16-25:28, contains series of proverbs whose sense is complete in one or two verses, and which, having no logical connection, admit of no analysis. The parallelisms of Pro. 10:1-15:33 are mostly antithetic; and those of Pro. 16:1-22:16, synthetic. The evidences of art in the structure are very clear, and indicate, probably, a purpose of facilitating the labor of memorizing. (Pro. 10:1-32)
 <p>
 </p><p>wise [and] foolish--as they follow or reject the precepts of wisdom. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>maketh . . . father--or, "gladdens a father." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>heaviness--or, "grief."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#002">Proverbs 10:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 10:2</a></font></span></p>Treasures . . . nothing--that is, Ill-gotten gains give no true happiness (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#017">Pro 4:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#019">Mat 6:19</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness--especially beneficence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa112.htm#009">Psa 112:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>death--the greatest of all evils.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#003">Proverbs 10:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 10:3</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#016">Psa 37:16-20</a>). The last clause is better: "He will repel the greedy desires of the wicked."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#004">Proverbs 10:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 10:4</a></font></span></p>slack--literally, "deceitful," failing of its purpose (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos007.htm#016">Hos 7:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>maketh rich--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#022">Pro 10:22</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#005">Proverbs 10:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 10:5</a></font></span></p>son--as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008">Pro 1:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#010">Pro 1:10</a>, and often. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sleepeth--in indolence, and not for rest. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>causeth shame--literally, "is base" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#035">Pro 14:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#002">Pro 17:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#006">Proverbs 10:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 10:6</a></font></span></p>Blessings--literally, "Praises." The last clause is better: "The mouth of the wicked covereth (or concealeth) violence (or mischievous devices)" to be executed in due time (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#009">Psa 5:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#007">Psa 10:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#014">Rom 3:14</a>), and hence has no praises (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011">Pro 10:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#007">Proverbs 10:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 10:7</a></font></span></p>blessed--literally, "for a blessing," or praise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>shall rot--literally, "be worm-eaten," useless and disgusting.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#008">Proverbs 10:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 10:8</a></font></span></p>wise, &amp;c.--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008">Pro 9:8-9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#016">Pro 9:16</a>), opposed to 
 <p>
 </p><p>prating fool--or, "fool of lips of wicked language." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>fall--headlong, suddenly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#009">Proverbs 10:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 10:9</a></font></span></p>perverteth his ways--acts deceitfully. 
 <p>
 </p><p>known--discovered and punished.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#010">Proverbs 10:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 10:10</a></font></span></p>Two vices contrasted; hypocrisy, or insinuating evil against one (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#013">Pro 6:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#019">Psa 35:19</a>), and rashness of speech. In each case, the results are on the evildoers.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011">Proverbs 10:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 10:11</a></font></span></p>a well--or, "source" of good to himself and others (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#037">Joh 7:37-38</a>). On last clause, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#006">Pro 10:6</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#012">Proverbs 10:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 10:12</a></font></span></p>strifes--or, "litigations." 
 <p>
 </p><p>covereth--by forgiveness and forbearance.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#013">Proverbs 10:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 10:13</a></font></span></p>In the lips . . . found--hence, not beaten, as the wicked-speaking fool. 
 <p>
 </p><p>void of understanding-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#032">Pro 6:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#007">Pro 7:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#014">Proverbs 10:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 10:14</a></font></span></p>lay up knowledge--that is, as treasures for good use. 
 <p>
 </p><p>mouth . . . destruction--or, "as to the mouth," &amp;c., destruction is near; they expose themselves to evil by prating.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#015">Proverbs 10:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 10:15</a></font></span></p>Both by trusting in "uncertain riches" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#017">Ti1 6:17</a>), or by the evils of poverty (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#009">Pro 30:9</a>), men, not fearing God, fall into dangers.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#016">Proverbs 10:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 10:16</a></font></span></p>The industry of the righteous is alone truly successful, while the earnings of the wicked tempt and lead to sin.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Proverbs 10:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 10:17</a></font></span></p>keepeth--observes (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#018">Pro 3:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#022">Pro 4:22</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>refuseth--or, "turns from reproof," which might direct him aright.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#018">Proverbs 10:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 10:18</a></font></span></p>Both vices must one day be known and punished, and hence their folly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#019">Proverbs 10:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 10:19</a></font></span></p>Much speech involves risk of sin; hence the wisdom of restraining the tongue (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#001">Psa 39:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#026">Jam 1:26</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#020">Proverbs 10:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 10:20</a></font></span></p>Right speech is the fruit of a good heart, but the wicked show theirs to be useless.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#021">Proverbs 10:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 10:21</a></font></span></p>Fools not only fail to benefit others, as do the righteous, but procure their own ruin (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011">Pro 10:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos004.htm#006">Hos 4:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#022">Proverbs 10:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 10:22</a></font></span></p>it maketh, &amp;c.--"it" is emphatic. Riches from God are without the sorrow of ill-gotten wealth (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc002.htm#021">Ecc 2:21-23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#009">Ti1 6:9-10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#017">Ti1 6:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#023">Proverbs 10:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 10:23</a></font></span></p>Sin is the pleasure of the wicked; wisdom that of the good.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#024">Proverbs 10:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 10:24</a></font></span></p>it--the very thing. The wicked get dreaded evil; the righteous, desired good.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#025">Proverbs 10:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 10:25</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#004">Psa 1:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#009">Psa 37:9-10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#036">Psa 37:36</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteous . . . foundation--well laid and firm (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#024">Mat 7:24-25</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#026">Proverbs 10:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 10:26</a></font></span></p>that is, causes vexation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#027">Proverbs 10:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 10:27</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#011">Pro 9:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#023">Psa 55:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#028">Proverbs 10:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 10:28</a></font></span></p>gladness--in confidence of realizing it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>expectation . . . perish--in disappointment.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#029">Proverbs 10:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 10:29</a></font></span></p>The way, &amp;c.--that is, God's providence sustains the righteous and overthrows the wicked (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#009">Hos 14:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#030">Proverbs 10:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 10:30</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#003">Pro 12:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#009">Psa 37:9-11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#028">Psa 102:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>earth--or, "land of promise."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#031">Proverbs 10:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 10:31</a></font></span></p>bringeth forth--literally, "germinates" as a plant. 
 <p>
 </p><p>froward--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#012">Pro 2:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#014">Pro 2:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>cut off--as an unproductive plant.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#032">Proverbs 10:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 10:32</a></font></span></p>know--regard and provide for (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#006">Psa 1:6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>frowardness--all kinds of deceit and ill-nature. The word is plural.
 </p><p>
