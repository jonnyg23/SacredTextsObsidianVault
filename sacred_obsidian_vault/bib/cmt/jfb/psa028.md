Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 28</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001">Psalms 28:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 28:1</a></font></span></p>An earnest cry for divine aid against his enemies, as being also those of God, is followed by the Psalmist's praise in assurance of a favorable answer, and a prayer for all God's people. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001">Psa 28:1-9</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>my rock-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#031">Psa 18:31</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>be not silent to me--literally, "from me," deaf or inattentive. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>become like them, &amp;c.--share their fate. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>go down into the pit--or, "grave" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#003">Psa 30:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#002">Psalms 28:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 28:2</a></font></span></p>lift up my hands--a gesture of prayer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#004">Psa 63:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa141.htm#002">Psa 141:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>oracle--place of speaking (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#022">Exo 25:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num007.htm#089">Num 7:89</a>), where God answered His people (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psa 5:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#003">Psalms 28:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 28:3</a></font></span></p>Draw me not away--implies punishment as well as death (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#009">Psa 26:9</a>). Hypocrisy is the special wickedness mentioned.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#004">Psalms 28:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 28:4</a></font></span></p>The imprecation is justified in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#005">Psa 28:5</a>. The force of the passage is greatly enhanced by the accumulation of terms describing their sin. 
 <p>
 </p><p>endeavours--points out their deliberate sinfulness.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#005">Psalms 28:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 28:5</a></font></span></p>Disregard of God's judgments brings a righteous punishment. 
 <p>
 </p><p>destroy . . . build . . . up--The positive strengthened by the negative form.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#006">Psalms 28:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 28:6</a></font></span></p>supplications--or, "cries for mercy."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#007">Psalms 28:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 28:7</a></font></span></p>The repetition of "heart" denotes his sincerity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#008">Psalms 28:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 28:8</a></font></span></p>The distinction made between the people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>their strength--and the anointed--may indicate Absalom's rebellion as the occasion.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#009">Psalms 28:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 28:9</a></font></span></p>The special prayer for the people sustains this view. 
 <p>
 </p><p>feed them--as a shepherd (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#001">Psa 23:1</a>, &amp;c.).
 </p><p>
