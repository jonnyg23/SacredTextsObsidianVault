Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 95</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#001">Psalms 95:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 95:1</a></font></span></p>David (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb004.htm#007">Heb 4:7</a>) exhorts men to praise God for His greatness, and warns them, in God's words, against neglecting His service. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#001">Psa 95:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>The terms used to express the highest kind of joy. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>rock--a firm basis, giving certainty of salvation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#007">Psa 62:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#002">Psalms 95:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 95:2</a></font></span></p>come . . . presence--literally, "approach," or, meet Him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#013">Psa 17:13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#003">Psalms 95:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 95:3</a></font></span></p>above . . . gods--esteemed such by men, though really nothing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer005.htm#007">Jer 5:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer010.htm#010">Jer 10:10-15</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#004">Psalms 95:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 95:4</a></font></span></p>The terms used describe the world in its whole extent, subject to God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#006">Psalms 95:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 95:6</a></font></span></p>come--or, "enter," with solemn forms, as well as hearts.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#007">Psalms 95:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 95:7</a></font></span></p>This relation illustrates our entire dependence (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#003">Psa 23:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#001">Psa 74:1</a>). The last clause is united by Paul (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#007">Heb 3:7</a>) to the following (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#008">Psa 81:8</a>),
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#008">Psalms 95:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 95:8</a></font></span></p>warning against neglect; and this is sustained by citing the melancholy fate of their rebellious ancestors, whose provoking insolence is described by quoting the language of God's complaint (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num014.htm#011">Num 14:11</a>) of their conduct at Meribah and Massah, names given (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo017.htm#007">Exo 17:7</a>) to commemorate their strife and contention with Him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#018">Psa 78:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#041">Psa 78:41</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa095.htm#010">Psalms 95:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 95:10</a></font></span></p>err in their heart--Their wanderings in the desert were but types of their innate ignorance and perverseness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>that they should not--literally, "if they," &amp;c., part of the form of swearing (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num014.htm#030">Num 14:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#035">Psa 89:35</a>).
 </p><p>
