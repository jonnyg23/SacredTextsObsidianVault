Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 91</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001">Psalms 91:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 91:1</a></font></span></p>David is the most probable author; and the pestilence, mentioned in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2024.htm#013">Sa2 24:13-15</a>, the most probable of any special occasion to which the Psalm may refer. The changes of person allowable in poetry are here frequently made. (Psa. 91:1-16)
 <p>
 </p><p>dwelleth in the secret place-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#005">Psa 27:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#020">Psa 31:20</a>) denotes nearness to God. Such as do so abide or lodge secure from assaults, and can well use the terms of trust in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#002">Psa 91:2</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#003">Psalms 91:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 91:3</a></font></span></p>snares . . . [and] . . . noisome pestilence--literally, "plagues of mischiefs" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#009">Psa 5:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#007">Psa 52:7</a>), are expressive figures for various evils.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#004">Psalms 91:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 91:4</a></font></span></p>For the first figure compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#011">Deu 32:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#037">Mat 23:37</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>buckler--literally, "surrounding"--that is, a kind of shield covering all over.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#005">Psalms 91:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 91:5</a></font></span></p>terror--or, what causes it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002">Pro 20:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>by night--then aggravated. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>arrow--that is, of enemies.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#007">Psalms 91:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 91:7</a></font></span></p>The security is more valuable, as being special, and, therefore, evidently of God; and while ten thousands of the wicked fall, the righteous are in such safety that they only see the calamity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#009">Psalms 91:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 91:9</a></font></span></p>This exemption from evil is the result of trust in God, who employs angels as ministering spirits (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#014">Heb 1:14</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#013">Psalms 91:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 91:13</a></font></span></p>Even the fiercest, strongest, and most insidious animals may be trampled on with impunity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#014">Psalms 91:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 91:14</a></font></span></p>God Himself speaks (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#010">Psa 46:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#002">Psa 75:2-3</a>). All the terms to express safety and peace indicate the most undoubting confidence (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#001">Psa 20:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#005">Psa 22:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>set his love--that of the most ardent kind.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#016">Psalms 91:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 91:16</a></font></span></p>show him--literally, "make him see" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#023">Psa 50:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#030">Luk 2:30</a>).
