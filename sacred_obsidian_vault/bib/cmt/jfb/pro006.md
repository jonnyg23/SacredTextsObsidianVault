Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001">Proverbs 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 6:1</a></font></span></p>After admonitions against suretyship and sloth (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#006">Pro 6:6-8</a>), the character and fate of the wicked generally are set forth, and the writer (Pro. 6:20-35) resumes the warnings against incontinence, pointing out its certain and terrible results. This train of thought seems to intimate the kindred of these vices. (Pro. 6:1-35)
 <p>
 </p><p>if--The condition extends through both verses. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>be surety--art pledged. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>stricken . . . hand--bargained (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job017.htm#003">Job 17:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>with a stranger--that is, for a friend (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#015">Pro 11:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#018">Pro 17:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#003">Proverbs 6:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 6:3</a></font></span></p>come . . . friend--in his power. 
 <p>
 </p><p>humble . . . sure thy friend--urge as a suppliant; that is, induce the friend to provide otherwise for his debt, or secure the surety.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#004">Proverbs 6:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 6:4</a></font></span></p>The danger requires promptness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#006">Proverbs 6:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 6:6</a></font></span></p>The improvident sluggards usually want sureties. Hence, such are advised to industry by the ant's example.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#009">Proverbs 6:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 6:9</a></font></span></p>Their conduct graphically described;
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#011">Proverbs 6:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 6:11</a></font></span></p>and the fruits of their self-indulgence and indolence presented. 
 <p>
 </p><p>as . . . travelleth--literally, "one who walks backwards and forwards," that is, a highwayman. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>armed man--that is, one prepared to destroy.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#012">Proverbs 6:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 6:12</a></font></span></p>A naughty person--literally, "A man of Belial," or of worthlessness, that is, for good, and so depraved, or wicked (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1025.htm#025">Sa1 25:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1030.htm#022">Sa1 30:22</a>, &amp;c.). Idleness and vice are allied. Though indolent in acts, he actively and habitually (walketh) is ill-natured in speech (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#024">Pro 4:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#013">Proverbs 6:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 6:13</a></font></span></p>If, for fear of detection, he does not speak, he uses signs to carry on his intrigues. These signs are still so used in the East.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#014">Proverbs 6:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 6:14</a></font></span></p>Frowardness--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#014">Pro 2:14</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>deviseth--literally, "constructs, as an artisan." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>mischief--evil to others. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>discord--especially litigation. Cunning is the talent of the weak and lazy.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#015">Proverbs 6:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 6:15</a></font></span></p>Suddenness aggravates evil (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#011">Pro 6:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#001">Pro 29:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>calamity--literally, "a crushing weight." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>broken--shivered as a potter's vessel; utterly destroyed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#009">Psa 2:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#016">Proverbs 6:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 6:16</a></font></span></p>six . . . seven--a mode of speaking to arrest attention (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#015">Pro 30:15</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro030.htm#018">Pro 30:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job005.htm#019">Job 5:19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#017">Proverbs 6:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 6:17</a></font></span></p>proud look--literally, "eyes of loftiness" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa131.htm#001">Psa 131:1</a>). Eyes, tongue, &amp;c., for persons.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#019">Proverbs 6:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 6:19</a></font></span></p>speaketh--literally, "breathes out," habitually speaks (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#012">Psa 27:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#001">Act 9:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#020">Proverbs 6:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 6:20</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008">Pro 1:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#003">Pro 3:3</a>, &amp;c.).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#022">Proverbs 6:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 6:22</a></font></span></p>it--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#023">Pro 6:23</a>); denotes the instruction of parents (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#020">Pro 6:20</a>), to which all the qualities of a safe guide and guard and ready teacher are ascribed. It prevents the ingress of evil by supplying good thoughts, even in dreams (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#021">Pro 3:21-23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#009">Psa 19:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#019">Pe2 1:19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#023">Proverbs 6:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 6:23</a></font></span></p>reproofs-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#023">Pro 1:23</a>) the convictions of error produced by instruction.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#024">Proverbs 6:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 6:24</a></font></span></p>A specimen of its benefit. By appreciating truth, men are not affected by lying flattery.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#025">Proverbs 6:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 6:25</a></font></span></p>One of the cautions of this instruction, avoid alluring beauty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>take--or, "ensnare." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>eyelids--By painting the lashes, women enhanced beauty.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#026">Proverbs 6:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 6:26</a></font></span></p>The supplied words give a better sense than the old version: "The price of a whore is a piece of bread." 
 <p>
 </p><p>adulteress--(Compare Margin), which the parallel and context (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#029">Pro 6:29-35</a>) sustain. Of similar results of this sin, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#009">Pro 5:9-12</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>will hunt--alluding to the snares spread by harlots (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#006">Pro 7:6-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>precious life--more valuable than all else.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#027">Proverbs 6:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 6:27</a></font></span></p>The guilt and danger most obvious.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#030">Proverbs 6:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 6:30</a></font></span></p>Such a thief is pitied, though heavily punished.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#031">Proverbs 6:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 6:31</a></font></span></p>sevenfold--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo022.htm#001">Exo 22:1-4</a>), for many, ample (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen004.htm#024">Gen 4:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#021">Mat 18:21</a>), even if all his wealth is taken.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#032">Proverbs 6:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 6:32</a></font></span></p>lacketh understanding--or, "heart"; destitute of moral principle and prudence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#033">Proverbs 6:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 6:33</a></font></span></p>dishonour--or, "shame," as well as hurt of body (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#035">Pro 3:35</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>reproach . . . away--No restitution will suffice;
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#034">Proverbs 6:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">pro 6:34</a></font></span></p>nor any terms of reconciliation be admitted. 
 <p>
 </p><p>regard--or, "accept" any ransom.
