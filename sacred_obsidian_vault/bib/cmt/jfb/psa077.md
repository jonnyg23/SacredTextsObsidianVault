Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 77</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#001">Psalms 77:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 77:1</a></font></span></p>To Jeduthun--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#001">Psa 39:1</a>, title). In a time of great affliction, when ready to despair, the Psalmist derives relief from calling to mind God's former and wonderful works of delivering power and grace. (Psa. 77:1-20)
 <p>
 </p><p>expresses the purport of the Psalm.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#002">Psalms 77:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 77:2</a></font></span></p>his importunacy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>my sore ran . . . night--literally, "my hand was spread," or, "stretched out" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#020">Psa 44:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ceased not--literally, "grew not numb," or, "feeble" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen045.htm#026">Gen 45:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#008">Psa 38:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my soul . . . comforted--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#035">Gen 37:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer031.htm#015">Jer 31:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#003">Psalms 77:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 77:3</a></font></span></p>His sad state contrasted with former joys. 
 <p>
 </p><p>was troubled--literally, "violently agitated," or disquieted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#006">Psa 39:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa041.htm#005">Psa 41:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my spirit was overwhelmed--or, "fainted" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa107.htm#005">Psa 107:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jon002.htm#007">Jon 2:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#004">Psalms 77:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 77:4</a></font></span></p>holdest . . . waking--or, "fast," that I cannot sleep. Thus he is led to express his anxious feelings in several earnest questions indicative of impatient sorrow.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#010">Psalms 77:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 77:10</a></font></span></p>Omitting the supplied words, we may read, "This is my affliction--the years of," &amp;c., "years" being taken as parallel to affliction (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#015">Psa 90:15</a>), as of God's ordering.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#011">Psalms 77:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 77:11</a></font></span></p>He finds relief in contrasting God's former deliverances. Shall we receive good at His hands, and not evil? Both are orderings of unerring mercy and unfailing love.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#013">Psalms 77:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 77:13</a></font></span></p>Thy way . . . in the sanctuary--God's ways of grace and providence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#003">Psa 22:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa067.htm#002">Psa 67:2</a>), ordered on holy principles, as developed in His worship; or implied in His perfections, if "holiness" be used for "sanctuary," as some prefer translating (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo015.htm#011">Exo 15:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#014">Psalms 77:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 77:14</a></font></span></p>Illustrations of God's power in His special interventions for His people (Exo. 14:1-31), and, in the more common, but sublime, control of nature (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#011">Psa 22:11-14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#014">Hab 3:14</a>) which may have attended those miraculous events (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#024">Exo 14:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#015">Psalms 77:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 77:15</a></font></span></p>Jacob and Joseph--representing all.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#019">Psalms 77:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 77:19</a></font></span></p>waters . . . , footsteps--may refer to His actual leading the people through the sea, though also expressing the mysteries of providence.
