Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 42</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#001">Psalms 42:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 42:1</a></font></span></p>Maschil--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psa 32:1</a>, title). For, or of (see Introduction) the sons of Korah. The writer, perhaps one of this Levitical family of singers accompanying David in exile, mourns his absence from the sanctuary, a cause of grief aggravated by the taunts of enemies, and is comforted in hopes of relief. This course of thought is repeated with some variety of detail, but closing with the same refrain. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#001">Psa 42:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001">Psa 63:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>panteth--desires in a state of exhaustion.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#002">Psalms 42:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 42:2</a></font></span></p>appear before God--in acts of worship, the terms used in the command for the stated personal appearance of the Jews at the sanctuary.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#003">Psalms 42:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 42:3</a></font></span></p>Where is thy God?--implying that He had forsaken him (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2016.htm#007">Sa2 16:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#002">Psa 3:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#008">Psa 22:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#004">Psalms 42:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 42:4</a></font></span></p>The verbs are properly rendered as futures, "I will remember," &amp;c.,--that is, the recollection of this season of distress will give greater zest to the privileges of God's worship, when obtained.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#005">Psalms 42:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 42:5</a></font></span></p>Hence he chides his despondent soul, assuring himself of a time of joy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>help of his countenance--or, "face" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#025">Num 6:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#006">Psa 4:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#011">Psa 16:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#006">Psalms 42:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 42:6</a></font></span></p>Dejection again described. 
 <p>
 </p><p>therefore--that is, finding no comfort in myself, I turn to Thee, even in this distant "land of Jordan and the (mountains) Hermon, the country east of Jordan. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>hill Mizar--as a name of a small hill contrasted with the mountains round about Jerusalem, perhaps denoted the contempt with which the place of exile was regarded.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#007">Psalms 42:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 42:7</a></font></span></p>The roar of successive billows, responding to that of floods of rain, represented the heavy waves of sorrow which overwhelmed him.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#008">Psalms 42:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 42:8</a></font></span></p>Still he relies on as constant a flow of divine mercy which will elicit his praise and encourage his prayer to God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#009">Psalms 42:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 42:9</a></font></span></p>in view of which [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#008">Psa 42:8</a>], he dictates to himself a prayer based on his distress, aggravated as it was by the cruel taunts and infidel suggestions of his foes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#011">Psalms 42:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 42:11</a></font></span></p>This brings on a renewed self-chiding, and excites hopes of relief. 
 <p>
 </p><p>health--or help. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>of my countenance--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#005">Psa 42:5</a>) who cheers me, driving away clouds of sorrow from my face. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my God--It is He of whose existence and favor my foes would have me doubt.
 </p><p>
