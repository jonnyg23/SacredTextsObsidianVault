Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 73</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#001">Psalms 73:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 73:1</a></font></span></p>Of Asaph--(see Introduction). God is good to His people. For although the prosperity of the wicked, and the afflictions of the righteous, tempted the Psalmist to misgivings of God's government, yet the sudden and fearful ruin of the ungodly, seen in the light of God's revelation, reassures his heart; and, chiding himself for his folly, he is led to confide renewedly in God, and celebrate His goodness and love. (Psa. 73:1-28)
 <p>
 </p><p>The abrupt announcement of the theme indicates that it is the conclusion of a perplexing mental conflict, which is then detailed (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer012.htm#001">Jer 12:1-4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Truly--or, "Surely it is so." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>clean heart-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#026">Psa 18:26</a>) describes the true Israel.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#002">Psalms 73:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 73:2</a></font></span></p>The figures express his wavering faith, by terms denoting tottering and weakness (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#005">Psa 22:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#003">Psa 62:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#003">Psalms 73:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 73:3</a></font></span></p>The prosperous wicked are insolently proud (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#005">Psa 5:5</a>). They die, as well as live, free from perplexities: pride adorns them, and violence is their clothing; indeed they are inflated with unexpected success. With all this--
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#008">Psalms 73:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 73:8</a></font></span></p>They are corrupt--or, literally, "they deride," they speak maliciously and arrogantly and invade even heaven with blasphemy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#006">Rev 13:6</a>), and cover earth with slanders (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job021.htm#007">Job 21:7-14</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#010">Psalms 73:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 73:10</a></font></span></p>Hence God's people are confounded, turned hither (or back) and thither, perplexed with doubts of God's knowledge and care, and filled with sorrow.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#012">Psalms 73:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 73:12</a></font></span></p>prosper in the word--literally, "secure for ever."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#013">Psalms 73:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 73:13</a></font></span></p>The Psalmist, partaking of these troubles, is especially disturbed in view of his own case, that with all his diligent efforts for a holy life, he is still sorely tried.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#015">Psalms 73:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 73:15</a></font></span></p>Freed from idiomatic phrases, this verse expresses a supposition, as, "Had I thus spoken, I should," &amp;c., intimating that he had kept his troubles to himself. 
 <p>
 </p><p>generation of thy children--Thy people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#001">Jo1 3:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>offend--literally, "deceive, mislead."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#016">Psalms 73:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 73:16</a></font></span></p>Still he-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>thought--literally, "studied," or, "pondered this riddle"; but in vain; it remained a toil (compare Margin), till he--
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#017">Psalms 73:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 73:17</a></font></span></p>went into the sanctuary--to enquire (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#022">Exo 25:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psa 5:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#004">Psa 27:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#018">Psalms 73:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 73:18</a></font></span></p>their end--future (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#037">Psa 37:37-38</a>), which is dismal and terribly sudden (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#027">Pro 1:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#001">Pro 29:1</a>), aggravated and hastened by terror. As one despises an unsubstantial dream, so God, waking up to judgment (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#006">Psa 7:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#023">Psa 44:23</a>), despises their vain shadow of happiness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#006">Psa 39:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#007">Isa 29:7</a>). They are thrown into ruins as a building falling to pieces (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#003">Psa 74:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#021">Psalms 73:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 73:21</a></font></span></p>He confesses how-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>foolish--literally, "stupid," and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ignorant--literally, "not discerning," had been his course of thought.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#022">Psalms 73:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 73:22</a></font></span></p>before thee--literally, "with Thee," in conduct respecting Thee.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#023">Psalms 73:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">psa 73:23</a></font></span></p>Still he was with God, as a dependent beneficiary, and so kept from falling (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#002">Psa 73:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#024">Psalms 73:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">psa 73:24</a></font></span></p>All doubts are silenced in confidence of divine guidance and future glory. 
 <p>
 </p><p>receive me to glory--literally, "take for (me) glory" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#018">Psa 68:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#008">Eph 4:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#025">Psalms 73:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">psa 73:25</a></font></span></p>God is his only satisfying good.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#026">Psalms 73:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">psa 73:26</a></font></span></p>strength--literally, "rock" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>portion-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#005">Psa 16:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#024">Lam 3:24</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#027">Psalms 73:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">psa 73:27</a></font></span></p>The lot of apostates, described by a figure of frequent use (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#001">Jer 3:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#003">Jer 3:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#035">Eze 23:35</a>), is contrasted with his, who finds happiness in nearness to God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#008">Jam 4:8</a>), and his delightful work the declaration of His praise.
