Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 23</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#001">Proverbs 23:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 23:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 23:1-35)
 <p>
 </p><p>Avoid the dangers of gluttony.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#002">Proverbs 23:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 23:2</a></font></span></p>put a knife--an Eastern figure for putting restraint on the appetite.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#003">Proverbs 23:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 23:3</a></font></span></p>are deceitful meat--though well tasted, injurious.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#004">Proverbs 23:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 23:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#009">Ti1 6:9-10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>thine own wisdom--which regards riches intrinsically as a blessing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#005">Proverbs 23:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 23:5</a></font></span></p>Wilt . . . eyes--As the eyes fly after or seek riches, they are not, that is, either become transitory or unsatisfying; fully expressed by their flying away.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#006">Proverbs 23:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 23:6</a></font></span></p>Beware of deceitful men, whose courtesies even you will repent of having accepted. 
 <p>
 </p><p>evil eye--or purpose (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009">Pro 22:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#009">Deu 15:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#023">Mat 6:23</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#008">Proverbs 23:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 23:8</a></font></span></p>The morsel . . . words--that is, disgusted with his true character, all pleasant intercourse will be destroyed.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#009">Proverbs 23:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 23:9</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008">Pro 9:8</a>). "Cast not your pearls before swine" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#006">Mat 7:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#010">Proverbs 23:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 23:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#022">Pro 22:22-23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#011">Proverbs 23:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 23:11</a></font></span></p>redeemer--or avenger (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#025">Lev 25:25-26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num035.htm#012">Num 35:12</a>), hence advocate (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job019.htm#025">Job 19:25</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>plead . . . thee--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job031.htm#021">Job 31:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#001">Psa 35:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#005">Psa 68:5</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#012">Proverbs 23:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 23:12</a></font></span></p>Here begins another series of precepts.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#013">Proverbs 23:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 23:13</a></font></span></p>While there is little danger that the use of the "divine ordinance of the rod" will produce bodily harm, there is great hope of spiritual good.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#015">Proverbs 23:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 23:15</a></font></span></p>The pleasure afforded the teacher by the pupil's progress is a motive to diligence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#016">Proverbs 23:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 23:16</a></font></span></p>my reins--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#009">Psa 7:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#017">Proverbs 23:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 23:17</a></font></span></p>(Compare Margin). The prosperity of the wicked is short.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#018">Proverbs 23:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 23:18</a></font></span></p>an end--or, "hereafter," another time, when apparent inequalities shall be adjusted (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#028">Psa 37:28-38</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#019">Proverbs 23:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 23:19</a></font></span></p>guide . . . way--or direct thy thoughts to a right course of conduct (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#004">Pro 4:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#006">Pro 9:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#020">Proverbs 23:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 23:20</a></font></span></p>riotous . . . flesh--prodigal, or eating more than necessary. Instead of "their flesh" (compare Margin), better, "flesh to them," that is, used for pleasure.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#021">Proverbs 23:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 23:21</a></font></span></p>drowsiness--the dreamy sleep of the slothful.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#022">Proverbs 23:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 23:22</a></font></span></p>Hearken--that is, obey (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008">Pro 1:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#001">Eph 6:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>despise . . . old--Adults revere the parents whom, as children, they once obeyed.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#023">Proverbs 23:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 23:23</a></font></span></p>Buy--literally, "get" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#005">Pro 4:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>truth--generally and specially as opposed to errors of all kinds.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#024">Proverbs 23:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 23:24</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001">Pro 10:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#021">Pro 17:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#025">Pro 17:25</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#026">Proverbs 23:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 23:26</a></font></span></p>A solemn warning against whoredom and drunkenness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos004.htm#011">Hos 4:11</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>give me--This is the address of that divine wisdom so often presented (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#001">Pro 8:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#003">Pro 9:3</a>, &amp;c.). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>heart--confidence. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>observe--keep. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my ways--such as I teach you (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#017">Pro 3:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#006">Pro 9:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#027">Proverbs 23:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 23:27</a></font></span></p>deep ditch--a narrow pit, out of which it is hard to climb. 
 <p>
 </p><p>lieth in wait--to ensnare men into the pit, as hunters entrap game (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#014">Pro 22:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#028">Proverbs 23:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 23:28</a></font></span></p>increaseth . . . transgressors-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#008">Pro 5:8-10</a>). The vice alluded to is peculiarly hardening to the heart.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#029">Proverbs 23:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 23:29</a></font></span></p>This picture is often sadly realized now. 
 <p>
 </p><p>mixed wine--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#002">Pro 9:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#011">Isa 5:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#031">Proverbs 23:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 23:31</a></font></span></p>when . . . red--the color denoting greater strength (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen049.htm#011">Gen 49:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#014">Deu 32:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>giveth . . . cup--literally, "gives its eye," that is, sparkles. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>moveth . . . aright--Perhaps its foaming is meant.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#032">Proverbs 23:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 23:32</a></font></span></p>The acute miseries resulting from drunkenness contrasted with the temptations.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#033">Proverbs 23:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 23:33</a></font></span></p>The moral effects: it inflames passion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#031">Gen 19:31</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#035">Gen 19:35</a>), lays open the heart, produces insensibility to the greatest dangers, and debars from reformation, under the severest sufferings.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#035">Proverbs 23:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">pro 23:35</a></font></span></p>awake--that is, from drunkenness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#024">Gen 9:24</a>). This is the language rather of acts than of the tongue.
