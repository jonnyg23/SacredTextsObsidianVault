Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 18</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#001">Proverbs 18:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 18:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 18:1-24)
 <p>
 </p><p>Through desire . . . seeketh--that is, seeks selfish gratification. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>intermeddleth . . . wisdom--or, "rushes on" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#014">Pro 17:14</a>) against all wisdom, or what is valuable (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#007">Pro 2:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#002">Proverbs 18:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 18:2</a></font></span></p>that his heart . . . itself--that is, takes pleasure in revealing his folly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#023">Pro 12:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#002">Pro 15:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#003">Proverbs 18:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 18:3</a></font></span></p>So surely are sin and punishment connected (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#004">Pro 16:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>wicked, for "wickedness," answers to 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ignominy, or the state of such; and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>contempt, the feeling of others to them; and to 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>reproach, a manifestation of contempt.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#004">Proverbs 18:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 18:4</a></font></span></p>Wise speech is like an exhaustless stream of benefit.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#005">Proverbs 18:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 18:5</a></font></span></p>accept the person--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa082.htm#002">Psa 82:2</a>). "It is not good" is to be supplied before "to overthrow."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#006">Proverbs 18:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 18:6</a></font></span></p>The quarrelsome bring trouble on themselves. Their rash language ensnares them (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#002">Pro 6:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008">Proverbs 18:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 18:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#028">Pro 16:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>as wounds--not sustained by the Hebrew; better, as "sweet morsels," which men gladly swallow. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>innermost . . . belly--the mind, or heart (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#027">Pro 20:27-30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014">Psa 22:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#009">Proverbs 18:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 18:9</a></font></span></p>One by failing to get, the other by wasting wealth, grows poor. 
 <p>
 </p><p>waster--literally, "master of washing," a prodigal.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#010">Proverbs 18:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 18:10</a></font></span></p>name of the Lord--manifested perfections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#001">Psa 8:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#002">Psa 20:2</a>), as faithfulness, power, mercy, &amp;c., on which men rely. 
 <p>
 </p><p>is safe--literally, "set on high, out of danger" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#004">Psa 91:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#011">Proverbs 18:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 18:11</a></font></span></p>contrasts with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#010">Pro 18:10</a> (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#015">Pro 10:15</a>). Such is a vain trust (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#006">Psa 73:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#012">Proverbs 18:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 18:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#033">Pro 15:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018">Pro 16:18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#013">Proverbs 18:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 18:13</a></font></span></p>Hasty speech evinces self-conceit, and ensures shame (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#012">Pro 26:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#014">Proverbs 18:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 18:14</a></font></span></p>infirmity--bodily sickness, or outward evil. The spirit, which sustains, being wounded, no support is left, except, as implied, in God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#015">Proverbs 18:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 18:15</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#005">Pro 1:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#015">Pro 1:15</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#031">Pro 1:31</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#016">Proverbs 18:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 18:16</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#008">Pro 17:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#023">Pro 17:23</a>). Disapproval of the fact stated is implied.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#017">Proverbs 18:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 18:17</a></font></span></p>One-sided statements are not reliable. 
 <p>
 </p><p>searcheth--thoroughly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#009">Pro 17:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#019">Pro 17:19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#018">Proverbs 18:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 18:18</a></font></span></p>The lot--whose disposal is of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#013">Pro 16:13</a>), may, properly used, be a right mode of settling disputes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#019">Proverbs 18:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 18:19</a></font></span></p>No feuds so difficult of adjustment as those of relatives; hence great care should be used to avoid them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#020">Proverbs 18:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 18:20</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#014">Pro 12:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#002">Pro 13:2</a>). Men's words are the fruit, or, increase of his lips, and when good, benefit them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>satisfied with--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#031">Pro 1:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#014">Pro 14:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#021">Proverbs 18:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 18:21</a></font></span></p>Death and life--or, the greatest evil and good. 
 <p>
 </p><p>that love it--that is, the tongue, or its use for good or evil. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>eat . . . fruit--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#019">Pro 18:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#019">Jam 1:19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#022">Proverbs 18:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 18:22</a></font></span></p>The old versions supply "good" before the "wife," as the last clause and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#014">Pro 19:14</a> imply (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#010">Pro 31:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#023">Proverbs 18:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 18:23</a></font></span></p>the rich . . . roughly--He is tolerated because rich, implying that the estimate of men by wealth is wrong.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#024">Proverbs 18:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 18:24</a></font></span></p>A man . . . friendly--better, "A man . . . (is) to, or, may triumph (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#009">Psa 108:9</a>), or, shout for joy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psa 5:11</a>), that is, may congratulate himself." Indeed, there is a Friend who is better than a brother; such is the "Friend of sinners" [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#019">Mat 11:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#034">Luk 7:34</a>], who may have been before the writer's mind.
