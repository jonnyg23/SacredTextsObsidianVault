Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 94</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#001">Psalms 94:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 94:1</a></font></span></p>The writer, appealing to God in view of the oppression of enemies, rebukes them for their wickedness and folly, and encourages himself, in the confidence that God will punish evildoers, and favor His people. (Psa. 94:1-23)
 <p>
 </p><p>God's revenge is His judicial infliction of righteous punishment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>show thyself--(Compare Margin).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#002">Psalms 94:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 94:2</a></font></span></p>Lift up thyself--or, "Arise," both figures representing God as heretofore indifferent (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#007">Psa 3:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#016">Psa 22:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#020">Psa 22:20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#003">Psalms 94:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 94:3</a></font></span></p>In an earnest expostulation he expresses his desire that the insolent triumph of the wicked may be ended.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#005">Psalms 94:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 94:5</a></font></span></p>thy people [and] thine heritage--are synonymous, the people being often called God's heritage. As justice to the weak is a sign of the best government, their oppression is a sign of the worst (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu010.htm#018">Deu 10:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa010.htm#002">Isa 10:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#007">Psalms 94:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 94:7</a></font></span></p>Their cruelty is only exceeded by their wicked and absurd presumption (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#011">Psa 10:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#007">Psa 59:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#008">Psalms 94:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 94:8</a></font></span></p>ye brutish--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#022">Psa 73:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#006">Psa 92:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#009">Psalms 94:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 94:9</a></font></span></p>The evidence of God's providential government is found in His creative power and omniscience, which also assure us that He can punish the wicked in regard to all their vain purposes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#012">Psalms 94:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 94:12</a></font></span></p>On the other hand He favors though He chastens, the pious, and will teach and preserve them till the prosperous wicked are overthrown.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#014">Psalms 94:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 94:14</a></font></span></p>This results from His abiding love (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#015">Deu 32:15</a>), which is further evinced by His restoring order in His government, whose right administration will be approved by the good.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#016">Psalms 94:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 94:16</a></font></span></p>These questions imply that none other than God will help (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#009">Psa 60:9</a>),
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#017">Psalms 94:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 94:17</a></font></span></p>a fact fully confirmed by his past experience. 
 <p>
 </p><p>dwelt in silence--as in the grave (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#017">Psa 31:17</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#019">Psalms 94:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 94:19</a></font></span></p>my thoughts--or, anxious cares.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#020">Psalms 94:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 94:20</a></font></span></p>throne--power, rulers. 
 <p>
 </p><p>iniquity [and] mischief--both denote evils done to others, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#021">Psa 94:21</a> explains.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#022">Psalms 94:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 94:22</a></font></span></p>Yet he is safe in God's care. 
 <p>
 </p><p>defence-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#009">Psa 59:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>rock of . . . refuge-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#009">Psa 9:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#023">Psalms 94:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">psa 94:23</a></font></span></p>bring . . . iniquity--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#010">Psa 5:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#016">Psa 7:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>in their . . . wickedness--while they are engaged in evil doing.
 </p><p>
