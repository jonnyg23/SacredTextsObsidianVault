Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 46</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001">Psalms 46:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 46:1</a></font></span></p>Upon Alamoth--most probably denotes the treble, or part sung by female voices, the word meaning "virgins"; and which was sung with some appropriately keyed instrument (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#019">Ch1 15:19-21</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psa 6:1</a>, title). The theme may be stated in LUTHER'S well-known words, "A mighty fortress is our God." The great deliverance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#035">Kg2 19:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#036">Isa 37:36</a>) may have occasioned its composition. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001">Psa 46:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>refuge--literally, "a place of trust" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>strength-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>present help--literally, "a help He has been found exceedingly." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>trouble--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#007">Psa 18:7</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#002">Psalms 46:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 46:2</a></font></span></p>The most violent civil commotions are illustrated by the greatest physical commotions.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#003">Psalms 46:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 46:3</a></font></span></p>swelling--well represents the pride and haughtiness of insolent foes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#004">Psalms 46:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 46:4</a></font></span></p>God's favor is denoted by a river (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#008">Psa 36:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#008">Zac 14:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#001">Rev 22:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>city of God, the holy place--His earthly residence, Jerusalem and the temple (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#004">Psa 3:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#002">Psa 20:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#002">Psa 48:2</a>, &amp;c.). God's favor, like a river whose waters are conducted in channels, is distributed to all parts of His Church. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>most High--denoting His supremacy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#002">Psa 17:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#005">Psalms 46:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 46:5</a></font></span></p>right early--literally, "at the turn of morning," or change from night to day, a critical time (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#005">Psa 30:5</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#036">Isa 37:36</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#006">Psalms 46:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 46:6</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#002">Psa 46:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>earth melted--all powers dissolved by His mere word (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#003">Psa 75:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos002.htm#022">Hos 2:22</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#007">Psalms 46:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 46:7</a></font></span></p>with us--on our side; His presence is terror to our enemies, safety to us. 
 <p>
 </p><p>refuge--high place (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#009">Psa 9:9</a>; compare also <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#006">Psa 24:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#010">Psa 24:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#008">Psalms 46:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 46:8</a></font></span></p>what desolations--literally, "who hath put desolations," destroying our enemies.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#009">Psalms 46:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 46:9</a></font></span></p>The usual weapons of war (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#012">Psa 7:12</a>), as well as those using them, are brought to an end.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#010">Psalms 46:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 46:10</a></font></span></p>Be still, &amp;c.--literally, "Leave off to oppose Me and vex My people. I am over all for their safety." (Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#011">Isa 2:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#022">Eph 1:22</a>).
