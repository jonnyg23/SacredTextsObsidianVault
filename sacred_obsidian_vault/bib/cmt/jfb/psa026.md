Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#001">Psalms 26:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 26:1</a></font></span></p>After appealing to God's judgment on his avowed integrity and innocence of the charges laid by his enemies, the Psalmist professes delight in God's worship, and prays for exemption from the fate of the wicked, expressing assurance of God's favor. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#001">Psa 26:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Judge--decide on my case; the appeal of innocence. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in mine integrity--freedom from blemish (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#021">Psa 25:21</a>). His confidence of perseverance results from trust in God's sustaining grace.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#002">Psalms 26:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 26:2</a></font></span></p>He asks the most careful scrutiny of his affections and thoughts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#009">Psa 7:9</a>), or motives.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#003">Psalms 26:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 26:3</a></font></span></p>As often, the ground of prayer for present help is former favor.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#004">Psalms 26:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 26:4</a></font></span></p>As exemplified by the fruits of divine grace, presented in his life, especially in his avoiding the wicked and his purposes of cleaving to God's worship.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#006">Psalms 26:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 26:6</a></font></span></p>wash mine hands--expressive symbol of freedom from sinful acts (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#024">Mat 27:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#008">Psalms 26:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 26:8</a></font></span></p>the habitation of thy house--where Thy house rests, as the tabernacle was not yet permanently fixed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>honour dwelleth--conveys an allusion to the Holy of Holies.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#009">Psalms 26:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 26:9</a></font></span></p>Gather not, &amp;c.--Bring me not to death. 
 <p>
 </p><p>bloody men--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#006">Psa 5:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#010">Psalms 26:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 26:10</a></font></span></p>Their whole conduct is that of violence and fraud.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#011">Psalms 26:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 26:11</a></font></span></p>But, &amp;c.--He contrasts his character and destiny with that of the wicked (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#001">Psa 26:1-2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#012">Psalms 26:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 26:12</a></font></span></p>even place--free from occasions of stumbling--safety in his course is denoted. Hence he will render to God his praise publicly.
