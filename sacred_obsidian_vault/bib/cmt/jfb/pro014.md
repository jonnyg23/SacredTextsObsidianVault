Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#001">Proverbs 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 14:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 14:1-35)
 <p>
 </p><p>Every wise, &amp;c.--literally, "The wisdoms" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Pro 9:1</a>) "of women," plural, a distributive form of speech. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>buildeth . . . house--increases wealth, which the foolish, by mismanagement, lessen.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#002">Proverbs 14:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 14:2</a></font></span></p>uprightness--is the fruit of fearing God, as falsehood and ill-nature (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#015">Pro 2:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#032">Pro 3:32</a>) of despising Him and His law.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#003">Proverbs 14:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 14:3</a></font></span></p>rod of pride--that is, the punishment of pride, which they evince by their words. The words of the wise procure good to them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#004">Proverbs 14:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 14:4</a></font></span></p>crib is clean--empty; so "cleanness of teeth" denotes want of food (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo004.htm#006">Amo 4:6</a>). Men get the proper fruit of their doings (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#007">Gal 6:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#005">Proverbs 14:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 14:5</a></font></span></p>A faithful witness, &amp;c.--one tested to be such. 
 <p>
 </p><p>utter lies--or, "breathe out lies"--that is, habitually lies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#019">Pro 6:19</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#001">Act 9:1</a>). Or the sense is, that habitual truthfulness, or lying, will be evinced in witness-bearing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#006">Proverbs 14:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 14:6</a></font></span></p>An humble, teachable spirit succeeds in seeking (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#009">Pro 8:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#017">Joh 7:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#005">Jam 1:5-6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#007">Proverbs 14:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 14:7</a></font></span></p>Avoid the society of those who cannot teach you.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#008">Proverbs 14:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 14:8</a></font></span></p>Appearances deceive the thoughtless, but the prudent discriminate.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#009">Proverbs 14:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 14:9</a></font></span></p>Fools make a mock at sin--or, "Sin deludes fools." 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteous . . . favour--that is, of God, instead of the punishment of sin.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#010">Proverbs 14:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 14:10</a></font></span></p>Each one best knows his own sorrows or joys.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#011">Proverbs 14:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 14:11</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#007">Pro 12:7</a>). The contrast of the whole is enhanced by that of house and tabernacle, a permanent and a temporary dwelling.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#012">Proverbs 14:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 14:12</a></font></span></p>end thereof--or, "reward," what results (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#004">Pro 5:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>ways of death--leading to it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#013">Proverbs 14:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 14:13</a></font></span></p>The preceding sentiment illustrated by the disappointments of a wicked or untimely joy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#014">Proverbs 14:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 14:14</a></font></span></p>filled . . . ways--receive retribution (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#031">Pro 1:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>a good man . . . himself--literally, "is away from such," will not associate with him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#015">Proverbs 14:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 14:15</a></font></span></p>The simple . . . word--He is credulous, not from love, but heedlessness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#016">Pro 13:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#016">Proverbs 14:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 14:16</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#007">Pro 3:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#014">Pro 28:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>rageth--acts proudly and conceitedly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#017">Proverbs 14:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 14:17</a></font></span></p>He . . . angry--literally, "short of anger" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029">Pro 14:29</a>, opposite idea). 
 <p>
 </p><p>man . . . hated--that is, the deliberate evildoer is more hated than the rash.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#018">Proverbs 14:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 14:18</a></font></span></p>inherit--as a portion (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#035">Pro 3:35</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>are crowned--literally, "are surrounded with it," abound in it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#019">Proverbs 14:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 14:19</a></font></span></p>Describes the humbling of the wicked by the punishment their sins incur.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#020">Proverbs 14:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 14:20</a></font></span></p>This sad but true picture of human nature is not given approvingly, but only as a fact.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#021">Proverbs 14:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 14:21</a></font></span></p>For such contempt of the poor is contrasted as sinful with the virtuous compassion of the good.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#022">Proverbs 14:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 14:22</a></font></span></p>As usual, the interrogative negative strengthens the affirmative. 
 <p>
 </p><p>mercy and truth--that is, God's (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#003">Psa 57:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#007">Psa 61:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#023">Proverbs 14:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 14:23</a></font></span></p>labour--painful diligence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>talk . . . penury--idle and vain promises and plans.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#024">Proverbs 14:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 14:24</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>foolishness . . . folly--Folly remains, or produces folly; it has no benefit.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#025">Proverbs 14:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 14:25</a></font></span></p>Life often depends on truth-telling. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a deceitful . . . lies--He that breathes out lies is deceit, not to be trusted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#005">Pro 14:5</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#026">Proverbs 14:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 14:26</a></font></span></p>The blessings of piety descend to children (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#022">Pro 13:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#007">Pro 20:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo020.htm#006">Exo 20:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#027">Proverbs 14:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 14:27</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#014">Pro 13:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>fear of the Lord--or, "law of the wise," is wisdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#010">Psa 111:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#028">Proverbs 14:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 14:28</a></font></span></p>The teaching of a true political economy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029">Proverbs 14:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 14:29</a></font></span></p>slow . . . understanding--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#017">Pro 14:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hasty--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#017">Pro 14:17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>exalteth folly--makes it conspicuous, as if delighting to honor it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#030">Proverbs 14:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 14:30</a></font></span></p>A sound heart--both literally and figuratively, a source of health; in the latter sense, opposed to the known effect of evil passions on health.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#031">Proverbs 14:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 14:31</a></font></span></p>reproacheth his Maker--who is the God of such, as well as of the rich (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#002">Pro 22:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job031.htm#015">Job 31:15</a>; and specially <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1002.htm#008">Sa1 2:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#007">Psa 113:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#032">Proverbs 14:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 14:32</a></font></span></p>driven--thrust out violently (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#005">Psa 35:5-6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hath hope--trusteth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#002">Pro 10:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#004">Pro 11:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>), implying assurance of help.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#033">Proverbs 14:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 14:33</a></font></span></p>resteth--preserved in quietness for use, while fools blazon their folly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#023">Pro 12:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#016">Pro 13:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#034">Proverbs 14:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">pro 14:34</a></font></span></p>Righteousness--just principles and actions. 
 <p>
 </p><p>exalteth--raises to honor. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>is a reproach--brings on them the ill-will of others (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#006">Pro 13:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#035">Proverbs 14:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">pro 14:35</a></font></span></p>wise--discreet or prudent. 
 <p>
 </p><p>causeth shame-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#005">Pro 10:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#004">Pro 12:4</a>) acts basely.
 </p><p>
