Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001">Proverbs 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 4:1</a></font></span></p>To an earnest call for attention to his teachings, the writer adds a commendation of wisdom, preceded and enforced by the counsels of his father and teacher. To this he adds a caution (against the devices of the wicked), and a series of exhortations to docility, integrity, and uprightness. (Pro. 4:1-27)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008">Pro 1:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to know--in order to know. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>doctrine--the matter of learning (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#005">Pro 1:5</a>), such as he had received (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#001">Lam 3:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#003">Proverbs 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 4:3</a></font></span></p>father's son--emphatic, a son specially regarded, and so called tender, as an object of special care (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1022.htm#007">Ch1 22:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1029.htm#001">Ch1 29:1</a>); an idea further expressed by 
 <p>
 </p><p>only beloved--or, "as an only son" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#002">Gen 22:2</a>), though he had brothers (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1003.htm#005">Ch1 3:5</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#004">Proverbs 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 4:4</a></font></span></p>He taught--or directed me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>retain--as well as receive. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>keep . . . and live--observe, that you may live (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#002">Pro 7:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#005">Proverbs 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 4:5</a></font></span></p>Get--as a possession not to be given up. 
 <p>
 </p><p>neither decline--that is, from obeying my word.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#006">Proverbs 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 4:6</a></font></span></p>Not only accept but love wisdom, who will keep thee from evil, and evil from thee.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#007">Proverbs 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 4:7</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job028.htm#028">Job 28:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>getting--or possession; a desire for wisdom is wise.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#008">Proverbs 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 4:8</a></font></span></p>As you highly esteem her, she will raise you to honor. 
 <p>
 </p><p>embrace her--with fond affection.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#009">Proverbs 4:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 4:9</a></font></span></p>ornament--such as the chaplet or wreath of conquerors. 
 <p>
 </p><p>deliver--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#020">Gen 14:20</a>). The allusion to a shield, contained in the Hebrew, suggests protection as well as honor (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#006">Pro 4:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#010">Proverbs 4:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 4:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001">Pro 2:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#002">Pro 3:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#011">Proverbs 4:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 4:11</a></font></span></p>way of wisdom--which it prescribes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>led thee--literally, "caused thee to tread," as a path (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa107.htm#007">Psa 107:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>not be straitened--have ample room (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#036">Psa 18:36</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#013">Proverbs 4:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 4:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#018">Pro 3:18</a>). The figure of laying hold with the hand suggests earnest effort.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#014">Proverbs 4:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 4:14</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psa 1:1</a>). Avoid all temptations to the beginning of evil.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#016">Proverbs 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 4:16</a></font></span></p>The reason is found in the character of sinners, whose zeal to do evil is forcibly depicted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#004">Pro 6:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#005">Psa 36:5</a>). They live by flagrant vices (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#013">Pro 1:13</a>). Some prefer to render, "Their bread is wickedness, their drink violence" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#016">Job 15:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#007">Job 34:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#018">Proverbs 4:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 4:18</a></font></span></p>As shining light increases from twilight to noonday splendor, so the course of the just increases in purity, but that of the wicked is as thickest darkness, in which one knows not on what he stumbles.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#020">Proverbs 4:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 4:20</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#010">Pro 4:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#013">Pro 4:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#008">Pro 3:8</a>, &amp;c.).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#022">Proverbs 4:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 4:22</a></font></span></p>health . . . flesh--by preserving from vices destructive of health.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#023">Proverbs 4:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 4:23</a></font></span></p>with all diligence--or, "above," or "more than all," custody (compare Margin), all that is kept (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#007">Eze 38:7</a>), because the heart is the depository of all wisdom and the source of whatever affects life and character (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#035">Mat 12:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#019">Mat 15:19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#024">Proverbs 4:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 4:24</a></font></span></p>a froward mouth--that is, a mouth, or words of ill nature. The Hebrew word differs from that used (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#015">Pro 2:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#032">Pro 3:32</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>perverse--or, "quarreling." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>lips--or, "words."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#025">Proverbs 4:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 4:25</a></font></span></p>Let . . . before thee--that is, pursue a sincere and direct purpose, avoiding temptations.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#026">Proverbs 4:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 4:26</a></font></span></p>Ponder--Consider well; a wise course results from wise forethought.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#027">Proverbs 4:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 4:27</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#025">Pro 4:25</a>). Avoid all by-paths of evil (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu002.htm#027">Deu 2:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#011">Deu 17:11</a>). A life of integrity requires attention to heart, speech, eyes, and conduct.
