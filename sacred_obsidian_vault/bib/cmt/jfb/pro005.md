Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#001">Proverbs 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 5:1</a></font></span></p>A warning against the seductive arts of wicked women, enforced by considering the advantages of chastity, and the miserable end of the wicked. (Pro. 5:1-23)
 <p>
 </p><p>This connection of wisdom and understanding is frequent (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#002">Pro 2:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#007">Pro 3:7</a>); the first denotes the use of wise means for wise ends; the other, the exercise of a proper discrimination in their discovery.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#002">Proverbs 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 5:2</a></font></span></p>regard--or, "observe." 
 <p>
 </p><p>keep--preserve constantly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#003">Proverbs 5:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 5:3</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#016">Pro 2:16</a>). Her enticing promises are deceitful.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#004">Proverbs 5:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 5:4</a></font></span></p>her end--literally, "her future," in sense of reward, what follows (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#037">Psa 37:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#017">Psa 73:17</a>). Its nature is evinced by the use of figures, opposite those of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#003">Pro 5:3</a>. The physical and moral suffering of the deluded profligate are notoriously terrible.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#005">Proverbs 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 5:5</a></font></span></p>feet . . . , steps--that is, course of life ends in death.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#006">Proverbs 5:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 5:6</a></font></span></p>her ways . . . know--Some prefer, "that she may not ponder the path of life," &amp;c.; but perhaps a better sense is, "her ways are varied, so as to prevent your knowledge of her true character, and so of true happiness."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#008">Proverbs 5:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 5:8</a></font></span></p>Avoid the slightest temptation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#009">Proverbs 5:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 5:9</a></font></span></p>thine honour--in whatever consisting, strength (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#013">Pro 3:13</a>) or wealth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy years--by cutting them off in dissipation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>unto the cruel--for such the sensual are apt to become.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#010">Proverbs 5:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 5:10</a></font></span></p>wealth--literally, "strength," or the result of it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>labours--the fruit of thy painful exertions (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa127.htm#002">Psa 127:2</a>). There may be a reference to slavery, a commuted punishment for death due the adulterer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu022.htm#022">Deu 22:22</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#011">Proverbs 5:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 5:11</a></font></span></p>at the last--the end, or reward (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#004">Pro 5:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>mourn--roar in pain. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>flesh and . . . body--the whole person under incurable disease.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#012">Proverbs 5:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 5:12</a></font></span></p>The ruined sinner vainly laments his neglect of warning and his sad fate in being brought to public disgrace.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#014">Proverbs 5:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 5:14</a></font></span></p>evil--for affliction, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#020">Gen 19:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen049.htm#015">Gen 49:15</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#015">Proverbs 5:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 5:15</a></font></span></p>By figures, in which well, cistern, and fountain [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#015">Pro 5:15</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#018">Pro 5:18</a>] represent the wife, and rivers of waters [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#016">Pro 5:16</a>] the children, men are exhorted to constancy and satisfaction in lawful conjugal enjoyments. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#016">Pro 5:16</a>, fountains (in the plural) rather denote the produce or waters of a spring, literally, "what is from a spring," and corresponds with "rivers of waters."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#017">Proverbs 5:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 5:17</a></font></span></p>only thine own--harlots' children have no known father.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#018">Proverbs 5:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 5:18</a></font></span></p>wife . . . youth--married in youth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#019">Proverbs 5:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 5:19</a></font></span></p>loving . . . roe--other figures for a wife from the well-known beauty of these animals. 
 <p>
 </p><p>breasts--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#013">Sol 1:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#003">Eze 23:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#008">Eze 23:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ravished--literally, "intoxicated," that is, fully satisfied.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#021">Proverbs 5:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 5:21</a></font></span></p>The reason, God's eye is on you,
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#022">Proverbs 5:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 5:22</a></font></span></p>and He will cause sin to bring its punishment.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#023">Proverbs 5:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 5:23</a></font></span></p>without instruction--literally, "in want of instruction," having refused it (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job013.htm#018">Job 13:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#024">Heb 11:24</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>go astray--literally, "be drunken." The word "ravished" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#019">Pro 5:19</a>) here denotes fulness of punishment.
 </p><p>
