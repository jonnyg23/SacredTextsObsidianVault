 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#001">Psalms 138:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 138:1</a></font></span></p>David thanks God for His benefits, and anticipating a wider extension of God's glory by His means, assures himself of His continued presence and faithfulness. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#001">Psa 138:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>I will praise thee with my whole heart--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#001">Psa 9:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>before the gods--whether angels (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#005">Psa 8:5</a>); or princes (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo021.htm#006">Exo 21:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa082.htm#006">Psa 82:6</a>); or idols (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#007">Psa 97:7</a>); denotes a readiness to worship the true God alone, and a contempt of all other objects of worship.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#002">Psalms 138:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 138:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psa 5:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy word above all thy name--that is, God's promise (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#012">Sa2 7:12-16</a>), sustained by His mercy and truth, exceeded all other manifestations of Himself as subject of praise.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#003">Psalms 138:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 138:3</a></font></span></p>That promise, as an answer to his prayers in distress, revived and strengthened his faith; and, as the basis of other revelations of the Messiah, it will be the occasion of praise by all who hear and receive it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#029">Psa 68:29</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#031">Psa 68:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#003">Isa 4:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#005">Psalms 138:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 138:5</a></font></span></p>for great is the glory--or, "when the glory shall be great," in God's fulfilling His purposes of redemption.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#006">Psalms 138:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 138:6</a></font></span></p>On this general principle of God's government (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#011">Isa 2:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa057.htm#015">Isa 57:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#002">Isa 66:2</a>), he relies for God's favor in saving him, and overthrowing his enemies. 
 <p>
 </p><p>knoweth afar off--their ways and deserts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#006">Psa 1:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa138.htm#008">Psalms 138:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 138:8</a></font></span></p>God will fulfil His promise.
