Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 50</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#001">Psalms 50:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 50:1</a></font></span></p>In the grandeur and solemnity of a divine judgment, God is introduced as instructing men in the nature of true worship, exposing hypocrisy, warning the wicked, and encouraging the pious. (Psa. 50:1-23)
 <p>
 </p><p>The description of this majestic appearance of God resembles that of His giving the law (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#016">Exo 19:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo020.htm#018">Exo 20:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#001">Deu 32:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#004">Psalms 50:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 50:4</a></font></span></p>above--literally, "above" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#007">Gen 1:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>heavens . . . earth--For all creatures are witnesses (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu004.htm#026">Deu 4:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#019">Deu 30:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#002">Isa 1:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#005">Psalms 50:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 50:5</a></font></span></p>my saints-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psa 4:3</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>made--literally, "cut" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>a covenant, &amp;c.--alluding to the dividing of a victim of sacrifice, by which covenants were ratified, the parties passing between the divided portions (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#010">Gen 15:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#018">Gen 15:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#006">Psalms 50:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 50:6</a></font></span></p>The inhabitants of heaven, who well know God's character, attest His righteousness as a judge.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#007">Psalms 50:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 50:7</a></font></span></p>I will testify--that is, for failure to worship aught. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy God--and so, by covenant as well as creation, entitled to a pure worship.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#008">Psalms 50:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 50:8</a></font></span></p>However scrupulous in external worship, it was offered as if they conferred an obligation in giving God His own, and with a degrading view of Him as needing it [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#009">Psa 50:9-13</a>]. Reproving them for such foolish and blasphemous notions, He teaches them to offer, or literally, "sacrifice," thanksgiving, and pay, or perform, their vows--that is, to bring, with the external symbolical service, the homage of the heart, and faith, penitence, and love. To this is added an invitation to seek, and a promise to afford, all needed help in trouble.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#016">Psalms 50:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 50:16</a></font></span></p>the wicked--that is, the formalists, as now exposed, and who lead vicious lives (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#021">Rom 2:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#023">Rom 2:23</a>). They are unworthy to use even the words of God's law. Their hypocrisy and vice are exposed by illustrations from sins against the seventh, eighth, and ninth commandments.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#021">Psalms 50:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 50:21</a></font></span></p>God, no longer (even in appearance) disregarding such, exposes their sins and threatens a terrible punishment.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#022">Psalms 50:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 50:22</a></font></span></p>forget God--This denotes unmindfulness of His true character.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#023">Psalms 50:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">psa 50:23</a></font></span></p>offereth praise-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#014">Psa 50:14</a>), so that the external worship is a true index of the heart. 
 <p>
 </p><p>ordereth . . . aright--acts in a straight, right manner, opposed to turning aside (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#005">Psa 25:5</a>). In such, pure worship and a pure life evince their true piety, and they will enjoy God's presence and favor.
 </p><p>
