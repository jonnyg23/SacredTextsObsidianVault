 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#001">Proverbs 13:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 13:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 13:1-25)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001">Pro 6:1-5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001">Pro 10:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#002">Proverbs 13:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 13:2</a></font></span></p>shall eat--that is, obtain (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#014">Pro 12:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>transgressors--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#022">Pro 2:22</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>violence--or, "mischief" to themselves.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#003">Proverbs 13:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 13:3</a></font></span></p>He . . . mouth . . . life--because evil speeches may provoke violence from others. 
 <p>
 </p><p>he that openeth wide his lips shall have destruction--On last clause, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#014">Pro 10:14</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#004">Proverbs 13:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 13:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#011">Pro 12:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#027">Pro 12:27</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#005">Proverbs 13:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 13:5</a></font></span></p>loathsome . . . shame--better, causeth shame and reproach (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#026">Pro 19:26</a>), by slander, &amp;c., which the righteous hates.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#006">Proverbs 13:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 13:6</a></font></span></p>A sentiment of frequent recurrence, that piety benefits and sin injures.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#007">Proverbs 13:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 13:7</a></font></span></p>In opposite ways men act hypocritically for gain of honor or wealth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#008">Proverbs 13:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 13:8</a></font></span></p>Riches save some from punishment, while others suffer because they will not heed the rebuke of sloth, which makes and keeps them poor.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009">Proverbs 13:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 13:9</a></font></span></p>light . . . lamp--prosperity; the first, the greater, and it 
 <p>
 </p><p>rejoiceth--burns brightly, or continues, while the other, at best small, soon fails.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#010">Proverbs 13:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 13:10</a></font></span></p>The obstinacy which attends self-conceit, produces contention, which the well-advised, thus evincing modesty, avoid.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#011">Proverbs 13:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 13:11</a></font></span></p>by vanity--or, "nothingness," that is, which is vain or useless to the public (as card playing or similar vices). 
 <p>
 </p><p>gathereth . . . labour--(Compare Margin), little by little, laboriously.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#012">Proverbs 13:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 13:12</a></font></span></p>desire cometh--is realized. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a tree of life--or, "cause of happiness."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#013">Proverbs 13:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 13:13</a></font></span></p>the word--that is, of advice, or, instruction (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#027">Pro 10:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#031">Pro 11:31</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#014">Proverbs 13:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 13:14</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011">Pro 10:11</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>fountain--or, "source of life." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to depart--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#002">Pro 1:2-4</a>), or, "for departing," &amp;c., and so gives life.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#015">Proverbs 13:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 13:15</a></font></span></p>Right perception and action secure good will, while evil ways are difficult as a stony road. The wicked left of God find punishment of sin in sinning. 
 <p>
 </p><p>hard--or, "harsh" (compare Hebrew: <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu021.htm#004">Deu 21:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer005.htm#015">Jer 5:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#016">Proverbs 13:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 13:16</a></font></span></p>dealeth--acts with foresight. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a fool . . . folly--for want of caution.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#017">Proverbs 13:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 13:17</a></font></span></p>A wicked--or, "unfaithful" 
 <p>
 </p><p>messenger falleth into mischief--or, "by mischief," or "evil," and so his errand fails. Contrasted is the character of the faithful, whose faithfulness benefits others.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#018">Proverbs 13:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 13:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001">Pro 12:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#019">Proverbs 13:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 13:19</a></font></span></p>Self-denial, which fools will not endure, is essential to success.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#020">Proverbs 13:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 13:20</a></font></span></p>The benefits of good and evil of bad society are contrasted.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#021">Proverbs 13:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 13:21</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#031">Pro 11:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>good . . . repaid--or, "He (God) will repay good."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#022">Proverbs 13:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 13:22</a></font></span></p>wealth . . . just--While good men's estates remain in their families, God so orders that the gains of sinners enure to the just (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#008">Pro 28:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#018">Psa 37:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#022">Psa 37:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#026">Psa 37:26</a>, &amp;c.).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#023">Proverbs 13:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 13:23</a></font></span></p>The laboring poor prosper more than those who injudiciously or wickedly strive, by fraud and violence, to supersede the necessity of lawful labor.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#024">Proverbs 13:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 13:24</a></font></span></p>spareth--or, "withholds." 
 <p>
 </p><p>rod--of correction. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>hateth--or, acts as if he hated him (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#012">Pro 3:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#036">Pro 8:36</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>chasteneth . . . betimes--or, "diligently seeks for him all useful discipline."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#025">Proverbs 13:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 13:25</a></font></span></p>The comparative temporal prosperity of the righteous and wicked, rather than contentment and discontent, is noted.
