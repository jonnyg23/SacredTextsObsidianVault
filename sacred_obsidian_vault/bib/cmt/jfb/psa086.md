Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 86</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#001">Psalms 86:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 86:1</a></font></span></p>This is a prayer in which the writer, with deep emotion, mingles petitions and praises, now urgent for help, and now elated with hope, in view of former mercies. The occurrence of many terms and phrases peculiar to David's Psalms clearly intimates its authorship. (Psa. 86:1-17)
 <p>
 </p><p>poor and needy--a suffering child of God, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#012">Psa 10:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#017">Psa 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#027">Psa 18:27</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>I am holy--or, "godly," as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psa 4:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa085.htm#008">Psa 85:8</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#004">Psalms 86:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 86:4</a></font></span></p>lift up my soul--with strong desire (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psa 25:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#005">Psalms 86:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 86:5</a></font></span></p>unto all . . . that call upon thee--or, "worship Thee" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#015">Psa 50:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#015">Psa 91:15</a>) however undeserving (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo034.htm#006">Exo 34:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev011.htm#009">Lev 11:9-13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#008">Psalms 86:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 86:8</a></font></span></p>neither . . . works--literally, "nothing like thy works," the "gods" have none at all.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#009">Psalms 86:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 86:9</a></font></span></p>The pious Jews believed that God's common relation to all would be ultimately acknowledged by all men (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#012">Psa 45:12-16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa047.htm#009">Psa 47:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#011">Psalms 86:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 86:11</a></font></span></p>Teach--Show, point out. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the way--of Providence. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>walk in thy truth--according to its declarations. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>unite my heart--fix all my affections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa012.htm#002">Psa 12:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#008">Jam 4:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to fear thy name--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#012">Psa 86:12</a>) to honor Thy perfections.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#013">Psalms 86:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 86:13</a></font></span></p>The reason: God had delivered him from death and the power of insolent, violent, and godless persecutors (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#003">Psa 54:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze008.htm#012">Eze 8:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#015">Psalms 86:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 86:15</a></font></span></p>Contrasts God with his enemies (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#005">Psa 86:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#016">Psalms 86:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 86:16</a></font></span></p>son . . . handmaid--homeborn servant (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#017">Luk 15:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#017">Psalms 86:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 86:17</a></font></span></p>Show me--literally, "Make with me a token," by Thy providential care. Thus in and by his prosperity his enemies would be confounded.
