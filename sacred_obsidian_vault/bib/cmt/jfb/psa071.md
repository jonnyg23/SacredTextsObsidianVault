Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 71</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#001">Psalms 71:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 71:1</a></font></span></p>The Psalmist, probably in old age, appeals to God for help from his enemies, pleading his past favors, and stating his present need; and, in confidence of a hearing, he promises his grateful thanks and praise. (Psa. 71:1-24)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#001">Psa 30:1-3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#003">Psalms 71:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 71:3</a></font></span></p>given commandment--literally, "ordained," as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#004">Psa 44:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#028">Psa 68:28</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>rock . . . fortress-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#004">Psalms 71:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 71:4</a></font></span></p>cruel man--corrupt and ill-natured--literally, "sour."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#005">Psalms 71:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 71:5</a></font></span></p>trust--place of trust.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#006">Psalms 71:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 71:6</a></font></span></p>His history from early infancy illustrated God's care, and his wonderful deliverances were at once occasions of praise and ground of confidence for the future. 
 <p>
 </p><p>my praise . . . of thee--literally, "in" or "by Thee" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#025">Psa 22:25</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#010">Psalms 71:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 71:10</a></font></span></p>The craft and malicious taunts of his enemies now led him to call for aid (compare the terms used, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2017.htm#012">Sa2 17:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#002">Psa 3:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#002">Psa 7:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#012">Psalms 71:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 71:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#019">Psa 22:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#004">Psa 40:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#013">Psalms 71:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 71:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#004">Psa 35:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#014">Psa 40:14</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#014">Psalms 71:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 71:14</a></font></span></p>The ruin of his enemies, as illustrating God's faithfulness, is his deliverance, and a reason for future confidence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#015">Psalms 71:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 71:15</a></font></span></p>for I know . . . thereof--innumerable, as he had not time to count them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#016">Psalms 71:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 71:16</a></font></span></p>in the strength--or, relying on it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy righteousness--or, faithful performance of promises to the pious (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#017">Psa 7:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#001">Psa 31:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#017">Psalms 71:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 71:17</a></font></span></p>Past experience again encourages. 
 <p>
 </p><p>taught me, &amp;c.--by providential dealings.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#019">Psalms 71:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 71:19</a></font></span></p>is very high--distinguished (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#005">Psa 36:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#009">Isa 55:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#020">Psalms 71:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 71:20</a></font></span></p>depths of the earth--debased, low condition.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#021">Psalms 71:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 71:21</a></font></span></p>increase, &amp;c.--that is, the great things done for me (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#019">Psa 71:19</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#005">Psa 40:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa071.htm#022">Psalms 71:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 71:22</a></font></span></p>To the occasion of praise he now adds the promise to render it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>will . . . praise--literally, "will thank." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>even thy truth--as to Thy truth or faithfulness.
 </p><p>
