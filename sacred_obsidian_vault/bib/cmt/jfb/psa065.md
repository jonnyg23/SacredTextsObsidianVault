Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 65</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#001">Psalms 65:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 65:1</a></font></span></p>This is a song of praise for God's spiritual blessings to His people and His kind providence over all the earth. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#001">Psa 65:1-13</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Praise waiteth for thee--literally, "To Thee silence praise," or (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#001">Psa 62:1</a>), To Thee silence is praise--that is, Praise is waiting as a servant; it is due to Thee. So the last clause expresses the duty of paying vows. These two parts of acceptable worship, mentioned in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#014">Psa 50:14</a>, are rendered in Zion, where God chiefly displays His mercy and receives homage.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#002">Psalms 65:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 65:2</a></font></span></p>All are encouraged to pray by God's readiness to hear.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#003">Psalms 65:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 65:3</a></font></span></p>God's mercy alone delivers us from the burden of iniquities, by purging or expiating by an atonement the transgressions with which we are charged, and which are denoted by-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>Iniquities--or, literally, "Words of iniquities."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#004">Psalms 65:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 65:4</a></font></span></p>dwell in thy courts; . . . [and] satisfied with the goodness . . . temple--denote communion with God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#001">Psa 15:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#006">Psa 23:6</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psa 5:7</a>). This is a blessing for all God's people, as denoted by the change of number.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#005">Psalms 65:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 65:5</a></font></span></p>terrible things--that is, by the manifestation of justice and wrath to enemies, accompanying that of mercy to His people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#009">Psa 63:9-11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#007">Psa 64:7-9</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>the confidence--object of it. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>of all . . . earth--the whole world; that is, deservedly such, whether men think so or not.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#006">Psalms 65:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 65:6</a></font></span></p>God's great power and goodness are the grounds of this confidence. These are illustrated in His control of the mightiest agencies of nature and nations affecting men with awe and dread (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#007">Psa 26:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa098.htm#001">Psa 98:1</a>, &amp;c.), and in His fertilizing showers, causing the earth to produce abundantly for man and beast.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#008">Psalms 65:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 65:8</a></font></span></p>outgoings of . . . rejoice--all people from east to west.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#009">Psalms 65:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 65:9</a></font></span></p>visitest--in mercy (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#004">Psa 8:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>river of God--His exhaustless resources.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#011">Psalms 65:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 65:11</a></font></span></p>thy paths--ways of providence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#004">Psa 25:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#010">Psa 25:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#012">Psalms 65:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 65:12</a></font></span></p>wilderness--places, though not inhabited by men, fit for pasture (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#021">Lev 16:21-22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#005">Job 24:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>pastures--is literally, "folds," or "enclosures for flocks"; and in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#013">Psa 65:13</a> it may be "lambs," the same word used and so translated in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#020">Psa 37:20</a>; so that "the flocks are clothed with lambs" (a figure for abundant increase) would be the form of expression.
 </p><p>
