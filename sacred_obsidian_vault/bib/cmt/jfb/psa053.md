 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa053.htm#001">Psalms 53:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 53:1</a></font></span></p>Upon Mahalath--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#001">Psa 88:1</a>, title). Why this repetition of the fourteenth Psalm is given we do not know. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa053.htm#001">Psa 53:1-6</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>with few verbal changes, correspond with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#001">Psa 14:1-4</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa053.htm#005">Psalms 53:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 53:5</a></font></span></p>Instead of assurances of God's presence with the pious, and a complaint of the wicked, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#005">Psa 14:5-6</a> portrays the ruin of the latter, whose "bones" even "are scattered" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa141.htm#007">Psa 141:7</a>), and who are put to shame as contemptuously rejected of God.
