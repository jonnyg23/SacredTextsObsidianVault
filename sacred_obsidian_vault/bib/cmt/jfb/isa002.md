Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#001">Isaiah 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 2:1</a></font></span></p>(Isa. 2:1-22)
 <p>
 </p><p>The inscription. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The word--the revelation.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#002">Isaiah 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 2:2</a></font></span></p>Same as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Mic 4:1</a>. As Micah prophesied in Jotham's reign, and Isaiah in Uzziah's, Micah rests on Isaiah, whom he confirms: not vice versa. HENGSTENBERG on slight grounds makes <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#001">Mic 4:1</a> the original. 
 <p>
 </p><p>last days--that is, Messiah's: especially the days yet to come, to which all prophecy hastens, when "the house of the God of Jacob," namely, at Jerusalem, shall be the center to which the converted nations shall flock together (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#032">Mat 13:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#031">Luk 2:31-32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#006">Act 1:6-7</a>); where "the kingdom" of Israel is regarded as certain and the time alone uncertain (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#015">Psa 68:15-16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#008">Psa 72:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#011">Psa 72:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>mountain of the Lord's house . . . in the top, &amp;c.--the temple on Mount Moriah: type of the Gospel, beginning at Jerusalem, and, like an object set on the highest hill, made so conspicuous that all nations are attracted to it. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>flow--as a broad stream (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#012">Isa 66:12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#003">Isaiah 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 2:3</a></font></span></p>If the curse foretold against Israel has been literally fulfilled, so shall the promised blessing be literal. We Gentiles must not, while giving them the curse, deny them their peculiar blessing by spiritualizing it. The Holy Ghost shall be poured out for a general conversion then (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#005">Jer 50:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#021">Zac 8:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#023">Zac 8:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe002.htm#028">Joe 2:28</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>from Jerusalem-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#047">Luk 24:47</a>) an earnest of the future relations of Jerusalem to Christendom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#012">Rom 11:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#015">Rom 11:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#004">Isaiah 2:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">isa 2:4</a></font></span></p>judge--as a sovereign umpire, settling all controversies (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#004">Isa 11:4</a>). LOWTH translates "work," "conviction." 
 <p>
 </p><p>plowshares--in the East resembling a short sword (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#006">Isa 9:6-7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#010">Zac 9:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#005">Isaiah 2:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 2:5</a></font></span></p>The connection is: As Israel's high destiny is to be a blessing to all nations (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003">Gen 12:3</a>), let Israel's children walk worthy of it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#008">Eph 5:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#006">Isaiah 2:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 2:6</a></font></span></p>Therefore--rather, "For": reasons why there is the more need of the exhortation in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#005">Isa 2:5</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thou--transition to Jehovah: such rapid transitions are natural, when the mind is full of a subject. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>replenished--rather, filled, namely, with the superstitions of the East, Syria, and Chaldea. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>soothsayers--forbidden (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu018.htm#010">Deu 18:10-14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Philistines--southwest of Palestine: antithesis to "the east." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>please themselves--rather, join hands with, that is, enter into alliances, matrimonial and national: forbidden (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo023.htm#032">Exo 23:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh013.htm#023">Neh 13:23</a>, &amp;c.).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#007">Isaiah 2:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 2:7</a></font></span></p>gold--forbidden to be heaped together (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#017">Deu 17:17</a>). Solomon disobeyed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1010.htm#021">Kg1 10:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1010.htm#027">Kg1 10:27</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>horses . . . chariots--forbidden (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#016">Deu 17:16</a>). But Solomon disobeyed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1020.htm#026">Kg1 20:26</a>). Horses could be used effectively for war in the plains of Egypt; not so in the hilly Judea. God designed there should be as wide as possible a distinction between Israel and the Egyptians. He would have His people wholly dependent on Him, rather than on the ordinary means of warfare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#007">Psa 20:7</a>). Also horses were connected with idolatry (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2023.htm#011">Kg2 23:11</a>); hence His objection: so the transition to "idols" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#008">Isa 2:8</a>) is natural.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#008">Isaiah 2:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 2:8</a></font></span></p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos008.htm#004">Hos 8:4</a>). Not so much public idolatry, which was not sanctioned in Uzziah's and Jotham's reign, but (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#004">Kg2 15:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#035">Kg2 15:35</a>) as private.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#009">Isaiah 2:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 2:9</a></font></span></p>mean--in rank: not morally base: opposed to "the great man." The former is in Hebrew, Adam, the latter, ish. 
 <p>
 </p><p>boweth--namely, to idols. All ranks were idolaters. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>forgive . . . not--a threat expressed by an imperative. Isaiah so identifies himself with God's will, that he prays for that which he knows God purposes. So <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev018.htm#006">Rev 18:6</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isaiah 2:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">isa 2:10</a></font></span></p>Poetical form of expressing that, such were their sins, they would be obliged by God's judgments to seek a hiding-place from His wrath (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#015">Rev 6:15-16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>dust--equivalent to "caves of the earth," or dust (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#019">Isa 2:19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>for fear, &amp;c.--literally, "from the face of the terror of the Lord."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#011">Isaiah 2:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">isa 2:11</a></font></span></p>lofty looks--literally, "eyes of pride" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#027">Psa 18:27</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>humbled--by calamities. God will so vindicate His honor "in that day" of judgments, that none else "shall be exalted" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#009">Zac 14:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#012">Isaiah 2:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">isa 2:12</a></font></span></p>Man has had many days: "the day of the Lord" shall come at last, beginning with judgment, a never-ending day in which God shall be "all in all" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#028">Co1 15:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2003.htm#010">Pe2 3:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>every--not merely person, as English Version explains it, but every thing on which the nation prided itself.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#013">Isaiah 2:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">isa 2:13</a></font></span></p>cedars . . . oaks--image for haughty nobles and princes (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo002.htm#009">Amo 2:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac011.htm#001">Zac 11:1-2</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#018">Rev 19:18-21</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>Bashan--east of Jordan, north of the river Jabbok, famous for fine oaks, pasture, and cattle. Perhaps in "oaks" there is reference to their idolatry (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#029">Isa 1:29</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#014">Isaiah 2:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">isa 2:14</a></font></span></p>high . . . hills--referring to the "high places" on which sacrifices were unlawfully offered, even in Uzziah's (equivalent to Azariah) reign (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#004">Kg2 15:4</a>). Also, places of strength, fastnesses in which they trusted, rather than in God; so
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#015">Isaiah 2:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">isa 2:15</a></font></span></p>tower . . . wall--Towers were often made on the walls of cities. 
 <p>
 </p><p>fenced--strongly fortified.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#016">Isaiah 2:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">isa 2:16</a></font></span></p>Tarshish--Tartessus in southwest Spain, at the mouth of the Guadalquivir, near Gibraltar. It includes the adjoining region: a PhÅnician colony; hence its connection with Palestine and the Bible (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2009.htm#021">Ch2 9:21</a>). The name was also used in a wide sense for the farthest west, as our West Indies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#019">Isa 66:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#007">Psa 48:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#010">Psa 72:10</a>). "Ships of Tarshish" became a phrase for richly laden and far-voyaging vessels. The judgment shall be on all that minister to man's luxury (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev018.htm#017">Rev 18:17-19</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>pictures--ordered to be destroyed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num033.htm#052">Num 33:52</a>). Still to be seen on the walls of Nineveh's palaces. It is remarkable that whereas all other ancient civilized nations, Egypt, Assyria, Greece, Rome, have left monuments in the fine arts, Judea, while rising immeasurably above them in the possession of "the living oracles," has left none of the former. The fine arts, as in modern Rome, were so often associated with polytheism, that God required His people in this, as in other respects, to be separate from the nations (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu004.htm#015">Deu 4:15-18</a>). But Vulgate translation is perhaps better, "All that is beautiful to the sight"; not only paintings, but all luxurious ornaments. One comprehensive word for all that goes before (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev018.htm#012">Rev 18:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev018.htm#014">Rev 18:14</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev018.htm#016">Rev 18:16</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#017">Isaiah 2:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">isa 2:17</a></font></span></p>Repeated from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#011">Isa 2:11</a>, for emphatic confirmation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#018">Isaiah 2:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">isa 2:18</a></font></span></p>idols--literally, "vain things," "nothings" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1008.htm#004">Co1 8:4</a>). Fulfilled to the letter. Before the Babylonian captivity the Jews were most prone to idolatry; in no instance, ever since. For the future fulfilment, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#002">Zac 13:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#015">Rev 13:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#019">Isaiah 2:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">isa 2:19</a></font></span></p>The fulfilment answers exactly to the threat (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>they--the idol-worshippers. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>caves--abounding in Judea, a hilly country; hiding-places in times of alarm (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1013.htm#006">Sa1 13:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>shake . . . earth--and the heavens also (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#026">Heb 12:26</a>). Figure for severe and universal judgments.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#020">Isaiah 2:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">isa 2:20</a></font></span></p>moles--Others translate "mice." The sense is, under ground, in darkness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>bats--unclean birds (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev011.htm#019">Lev 11:19</a>), living amidst tenantless ruins (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev011.htm#013">Rev 11:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#022">Isaiah 2:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">isa 2:22</a></font></span></p>The high ones (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#011">Isa 2:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#013">Isa 2:13</a>) on whom the people trust, shall be "brought low" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#002">Isa 3:2</a>); therefore "cease from" depending on them, instead of on the Lord (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa146.htm#003">Psa 146:3-5</a>).
