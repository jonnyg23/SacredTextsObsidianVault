 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#001">Psalms 57:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 57:1</a></font></span></p>Altaschith--or, "Destroy not." This is perhaps an enigmatical allusion to the critical circumstances connected with the history, for which compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1022.htm#001">Sa1 22:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1026.htm#001">Sa1 26:1-3</a>. In Moses' prayer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu009.htm#026">Deu 9:26</a>) it is a prominent petition deprecating God's anger against the people. This explanation suits the fifty-eighth and fifty-ninth also. Asaph uses it for the seventy-fifth, in the scope of which there is allusion to some emergency. Michtam--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#001">Psa 16:1</a>, title). To an earnest cry for divine aid, the Psalmist adds, as often, the language of praise, in the assured hope of a favorable hearing. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#001">Psa 57:1-11</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>my soul--or self, or life, which is threatened. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>shadow of thy wings-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#008">Psa 17:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#007">Psa 36:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>calamities--literally, "mischiefs" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#002">Psa 52:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#010">Psa 55:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#002">Psalms 57:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 57:2</a></font></span></p>performeth--or, completes what He has begun.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#003">Psalms 57:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 57:3</a></font></span></p>from . . . swallow me up--that pants in rage after me (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#002">Psa 56:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>mercy and . . . truth-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#010">Psa 25:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#005">Psa 36:5</a>), as messengers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#003">Psa 43:3</a>) sent to deliver him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#004">Psalms 57:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 57:4</a></font></span></p>The mingled figures of wild beasts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#009">Psa 10:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#012">Psa 17:12</a>) and weapons of war (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa011.htm#002">Psa 11:2</a>) heighten the picture of danger. 
 <p>
 </p><p>whose . . . tongue--or slanders.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#005">Psalms 57:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 57:5</a></font></span></p>This doxology illustrates his view of the connection of his deliverance with God's glory.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#006">Psalms 57:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 57:6</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#015">Psa 7:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#015">Psa 9:15-16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#007">Psalms 57:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 57:7</a></font></span></p>I will . . . praise--both with voice and instrument.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#008">Psalms 57:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 57:8</a></font></span></p>Hence--he addresses his glory, or tongue (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#009">Psa 16:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#012">Psa 30:12</a>), and his psaltery, or lute, and harp. 
 <p>
 </p><p>I myself . . . early--literally, "I will awaken dawn," poetically expressing his zeal and diligence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#009">Psalms 57:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 57:9</a></font></span></p>As His mercy and truth, so shall His praise, fill the universe.
