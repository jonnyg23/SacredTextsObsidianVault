Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 143</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#001">Psalms 143:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 143:1</a></font></span></p>In structure and style, like the preceding (Psalms 104-142), this Psalm is clearly evinced to be David's. It is a prayer for pardon, and for relief from enemies; afflictions, as usual, producing confession and penitence. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#001">Psa 143:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>in thy faithfulness . . . and . . . righteousness--or, God's regard to the claims which He has permitted His people to make in His covenant.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#002">Psalms 143:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 143:2</a></font></span></p>enter . . . judgment--deal not in strict justice. 
 <p>
 </p><p>shall no . . . justified--or, "is no man justified," or "innocent" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job014.htm#003">Job 14:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#020">Rom 3:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#003">Psalms 143:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 143:3</a></font></span></p>The exciting reason for his prayer--his afflictions--led to confession as just made: he now makes the complaint. 
 <p>
 </p><p>as those that have been long dead--deprived of life's comforts (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#015">Psa 40:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#003">Psa 88:3-6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#005">Psalms 143:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 143:5</a></font></span></p>The distress is aggravated by the contrast of former comfort (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#003">Psa 22:3-5</a>), for whose return he longs. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a thirsty land--which needs rain, as did his spirit God's gracious visits (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001">Psa 28:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#017">Psa 89:17</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#007">Psalms 143:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 143:7</a></font></span></p>spirit faileth--is exhausted.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#008">Psalms 143:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 143:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psa 25:1-4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#016">Psa 59:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>the way . . . walk--that is, the way of safety and righteousness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa142.htm#003">Psa 142:3-6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#009">Psalms 143:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 143:9</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#015">Psa 31:15-20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#010">Psalms 143:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 143:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#008">Psa 5:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#011">Psa 27:11</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>land of uprightness--literally, "an even land" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#012">Psa 26:12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#011">Psalms 143:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 143:11</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#003">Psa 23:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#156">Psa 119:156</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#012">Psalms 143:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 143:12</a></font></span></p>God's mercy to His people is often wrath to His and their enemies (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#017">Psa 31:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy servant--as chosen to be such, entitled to divine regard.
 </p><p>
