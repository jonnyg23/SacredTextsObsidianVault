Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 17</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#001">Proverbs 17:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 17:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 17:1-28)
 <p>
 </p><p>sacrifices--or, "feasts" made with part of them (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#014">Pro 7:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#003">Lev 2:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#031">Lev 7:31</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>with--literally, "of." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>strife--its product, or attendant.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#002">Proverbs 17:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 17:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#035">Pro 14:35</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>causeth shame-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#005">Pro 10:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>shall . . . inheritance--that is, share a brother's part (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num027.htm#004">Num 27:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num027.htm#007">Num 27:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#003">Proverbs 17:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 17:3</a></font></span></p>God only knows, as He tries (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa012.htm#006">Psa 12:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#010">Psa 66:10</a>) the heart.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#004">Proverbs 17:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 17:4</a></font></span></p>Wicked doers and speakers alike delight in calumny.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#005">Proverbs 17:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 17:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#031">Pro 14:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>glad at calamities--rejoicing in others' evil. Such are rightly punished by God, who knows their hearts.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#006">Proverbs 17:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 17:6</a></font></span></p>Prolonged posterity is a blessing, its cutting off a curse (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#022">Pro 13:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa109.htm#013">Psa 109:13-15</a>), hence children may glory in virtuous ancestry.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#007">Proverbs 17:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 17:7</a></font></span></p>Excellent speech--(Compare Margin). Such language as ill suits a fool, as lying (ought to suit) a prince (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#012">Pro 16:12-13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#008">Proverbs 17:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 17:8</a></font></span></p>One so corrupt as to take a bribe evinces his high estimate of it by subjection to its influence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#016">Pro 18:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#006">Pro 19:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#009">Proverbs 17:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 17:9</a></font></span></p>seeketh love--(Compare Margin). The contrast is between the peace-maker and tale-bearer.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#010">Proverbs 17:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 17:10</a></font></span></p>Reproof more affects the wise than severe scourging, fools.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#011">Proverbs 17:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 17:11</a></font></span></p>Such meet just retribution (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#025">Kg1 2:25</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>a cruel messenger--one to inflict it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#012">Proverbs 17:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 17:12</a></font></span></p>They are less rational in anger than wild beasts.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#013">Proverbs 17:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 17:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#004">Psa 7:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#012">Psa 35:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>evil--injury to another (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#021">Pro 13:21</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#014">Proverbs 17:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 17:14</a></font></span></p>letteth . . . water--as a breach in a dam. 
 <p>
 </p><p>before . . . meddled with--before strife has become sharp, or, by an explanation better suiting the figure, before it rolls on, or increases.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#015">Proverbs 17:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 17:15</a></font></span></p>abomination . . . Lord--as reversing His method of acting (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#032">Pro 3:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#002">Pro 12:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#016">Proverbs 17:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 17:16</a></font></span></p>Though wealth cannot buy wisdom for those who do not love it, yet wisdom procures wealth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#024">Pro 14:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#017">Proverbs 17:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 17:17</a></font></span></p>To the second of these parallel clauses, there is an accession of meaning, that is, that a brother's love is specially seen in adversity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#018">Proverbs 17:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 17:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001">Pro 6:1-5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#015">Pro 11:15</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>in the presence, &amp;c.--that is, he either fails to consult his friend, or to follow his advice.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#019">Proverbs 17:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 17:19</a></font></span></p>strife--contention is, and leads to, sin. 
 <p>
 </p><p>he that exalteth his gate--gratifies a vain love of costly building. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>seeketh--or, "findeth," as if he sought (compare "loveth death," <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#036">Pro 8:36</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#020">Proverbs 17:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 17:20</a></font></span></p>The second clause advances on the first. The ill-natured fail of good, and the cavilling and fault-finding incur evil.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#021">Proverbs 17:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 17:21</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#024">Pro 23:24</a>). Different words are rendered by "fool," both denoting stupidity and impiety.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#022">Proverbs 17:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 17:22</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#030">Pro 14:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#013">Pro 15:13</a>). The effect of the mind on the body is well known. 
 <p>
 </p><p>medicine--or, "body," which better corresponds with "bone." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>drieth--as if the marrow were exhausted.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#023">Proverbs 17:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 17:23</a></font></span></p>a gift . . . bosom--Money and other valuables were borne in a fold of the garment, called the bosom. 
 <p>
 </p><p>to pervert--that is, by bribery.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#024">Proverbs 17:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 17:24</a></font></span></p>Wisdom . . . him--ever an object of regard, while a fool's affections are unsettled.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#025">Proverbs 17:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 17:25</a></font></span></p>a grief--or cross, vexation (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#021">Pro 17:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001">Pro 10:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#026">Proverbs 17:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 17:26</a></font></span></p>Also--that is, Equally to be avoided are other sins: punishing good subjects, or resisting good rulers.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#027">Proverbs 17:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 17:27</a></font></span></p>Prudence of speech is commended as is an excellent or calm spirit, not excited to vain conversation.
