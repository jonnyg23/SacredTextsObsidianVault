Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 116</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#001">Psalms 116:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 116:1</a></font></span></p>The writer celebrates the deliverance from extreme perils by which he was favored, and pledges grateful and pious public acknowledgments. (Psa. 116:1-19)
 <p>
 </p><p>A truly grateful love will be evinced by acts of worship, which calling on God expresses (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#013">Psa 116:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#016">Psa 55:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#007">Psa 86:7</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#006">Psa 17:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#002">Psa 31:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#003">Psalms 116:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 116:3</a></font></span></p>For similar figures for distress see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#004">Psa 18:4-5</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>gat hold upon me--Another sense ("found") of the same word follows, as we speak of disease finding us, and of our finding or catching disease.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#005">Psalms 116:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 116:5</a></font></span></p>The relief which he asked is the result not of his merit, but of God's known pity and tenderness, which is acknowledged in assuring himself (his "soul," <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa011.htm#001">Psa 11:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#010">Psa 16:10</a>) of rest and peace. All calamities [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#008">Psa 116:8</a>] are represented by death, tears, and falling of the feet (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#013">Psa 56:13</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#009">Psalms 116:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 116:9</a></font></span></p>walk before the Lord--act, or live under His favor and guidance (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#001">Gen 17:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#007">Psa 61:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>land of the living-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#013">Psa 27:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#010">Psalms 116:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 116:10</a></font></span></p>Confidence in God opposed to distrust of men, as not reliable (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#008">Psa 68:8-9</a>). He speaks from an experience of the result of his faith.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#011">Psalms 116:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 116:11</a></font></span></p>in my haste--literally, "terror," or "agitation," produced by his affliction (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#022">Psa 31:22</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#012">Psalms 116:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 116:12</a></font></span></p>These are modes of expressing acts of worship (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#004">Psa 116:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#014">Psa 50:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jon002.htm#009">Jon 2:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#013">Psalms 116:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 116:13</a></font></span></p>the cup of salvation--the drink offering which was part of the thank offering (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num015.htm#003">Num 15:3-5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#014">Psalms 116:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 116:14</a></font></span></p>now--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa115.htm#002">Psa 115:2</a>). "Oh, that (I may do it)" in the presence, &amp;c.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#015">Psalms 116:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 116:15</a></font></span></p>By the plea of being a homeborn servant, he intimates his claim on God's covenant love to His people.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa116.htm#017">Psalms 116:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 116:17</a></font></span></p>An ampler declaration of his purpose, designating the place, the Lord's house, or earthly residence in Jerusalem.
