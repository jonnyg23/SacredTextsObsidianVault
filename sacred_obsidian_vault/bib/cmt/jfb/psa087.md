Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 87</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#001">Psalms 87:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 87:1</a></font></span></p>This triumphal song was probably occasioned by the same event as the forty-sixth [see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001">Psa 46:1</a>, title]. The writer celebrates the glory of the Church, as the means of spiritual blessing to the nation. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#001">Psa 87:1-7</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>His--that is, God's 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>foundation--or, what He has founded, that is, Zion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#032">Isa 14:32</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>is in the holy mountains--the location of Zion, in the wide sense, for the capital, or Jerusalem, being on several hills.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#002">Psalms 87:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 87:2</a></font></span></p>gates--for the enclosures, or city to which they opened (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#014">Psa 9:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#002">Psa 122:2</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#013">Psa 132:13-14</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#003">Psalms 87:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 87:3</a></font></span></p>spoken of thee--or, "in thee," that is, the city of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#004">Psa 46:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa048.htm#002">Psa 48:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#004">Psalms 87:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 87:4</a></font></span></p>This is what is spoken by God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>to them . . . me--literally, "for My knowers," they are true worshippers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#010">Psa 36:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa019.htm#021">Isa 19:21</a>). These are mentioned as specimens. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>this--that is, nation 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>was born there--Of each it is said, "This was born," or is a native of Zion, spiritually.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#005">Psalms 87:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 87:5</a></font></span></p>The writer resumes-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>This and that man--literally, "man and man," or many (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#010">Gen 14:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#010">Exo 8:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#014">Exo 8:14</a>), or all (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#005">Isa 44:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#028">Gal 3:28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the highest . . . her--God is her protector.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#006">Psalms 87:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 87:6</a></font></span></p>The same idea is set forth under the figure of a register made by God (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#003">Isa 4:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#007">Psalms 87:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 87:7</a></font></span></p>As in a great procession of those thus written up, or registered, seeking Zion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#003">Isa 2:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#005">Jer 50:5</a>), "the singers" and "players," or pipers, shall precede. 
 <p>
 </p><p>all my springs--So each shall say, "All my sources of spiritual joy are in Thee" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#004">Psa 46:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa084.htm#006">Psa 84:6</a>).
 </p><p>
