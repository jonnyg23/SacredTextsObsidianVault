Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 34</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#001">Psalms 34:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 34:1</a></font></span></p>On the title compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1021.htm#013">Sa1 21:13</a>. Abimelech was the general name of the sovereign (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen020.htm#002">Gen 20:2</a>). After celebrating God's gracious dealings with him, the Psalmist exhorts others to make trial of His providential care, instructing them how to secure it. He then contrasts God's care of His people and His punitive providence towards the wicked. (Psa. 34:1-22)
 <p>
 </p><p>Even in distress, which excites supplication, there is always matter for praising and thanking God (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#020">Eph 5:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi004.htm#006">Phi 4:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#002">Psalms 34:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 34:2</a></font></span></p>make her boast--"glory" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa105.htm#003">Psa 105:3</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#014">Gal 6:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>humble--"the pious," as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#012">Psa 9:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#009">Psa 25:9</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#003">Psalms 34:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 34:3</a></font></span></p>magnify the Lord--ascribe greatness to Him, an act of praise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>together--"alike" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#015">Psa 33:15</a>), or, equally, without exception.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#004">Psalms 34:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 34:4</a></font></span></p>delivered . . . fears--as well as actual evil (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#001">Psa 64:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#005">Psalms 34:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 34:5</a></font></span></p>God's favor to the pious generally, and to himself specially, is celebrated. 
 <p>
 </p><p>looked--with desire for help. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>lightened--or, "brightened," expressing joy, opposed to the downcast features of those who are ashamed or disappointed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#002">Psa 25:2-3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#006">Psalms 34:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 34:6</a></font></span></p>This poor man--literally, "humble," himself as a specimen of such.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#007">Psalms 34:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 34:7</a></font></span></p>angel--of the covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa063.htm#009">Isa 63:9</a>), of whom as a leader of God's host (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#014">Jos 5:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1022.htm#019">Kg1 22:19</a>), the phrase-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>encampeth, &amp;c.--is appropriate; or, "angel" used collectively for angels (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#014">Heb 1:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#008">Psalms 34:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 34:8</a></font></span></p>taste and see--try and experience.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#009">Psalms 34:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 34:9</a></font></span></p>that fear him--who are pious--fear and love (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#007">Pro 1:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#010">Pro 9:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>saints--consecrated to His service (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#031">Isa 40:31</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#010">Psalms 34:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 34:10</a></font></span></p>not want any good--"good" is emphatic; they may be afflicted (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#010">Psa 34:10</a>); but this may be a good (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2004.htm#017">Co2 4:17-18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#010">Heb 12:10-11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#011">Psalms 34:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 34:11</a></font></span></p>children--subjects of instruction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008">Pro 1:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#010">Pro 1:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#012">Psalms 34:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 34:12</a></font></span></p>What man--Whoever desires the blessings of piety, let him attend.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#013">Psalms 34:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 34:13</a></font></span></p>Sins of thought included in those of speech (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#045">Luk 6:45</a>), avoiding evil and doing good in our relations to men are based on a right relation to God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#015">Psalms 34:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 34:15</a></font></span></p>eyes of the Lord are upon-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#008">Psa 32:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#018">Psa 33:18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#016">Psalms 34:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 34:16</a></font></span></p>face . . . against--opposed to them (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev017.htm#010">Lev 17:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev020.htm#003">Lev 20:3</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>cut off the remembrance--utterly destroy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa109.htm#013">Psa 109:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#017">Psalms 34:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 34:17</a></font></span></p>Humble penitents are objects of God's special tender regard (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#019">Psa 51:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa057.htm#015">Isa 57:15</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#020">Psalms 34:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 34:20</a></font></span></p>bones--framework of the body.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#021">Psalms 34:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 34:21</a></font></span></p>Contrast in the destiny of righteous and wicked; the former shall be delivered and never come into condemnation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#024">Joh 5:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#001">Rom 8:1</a>); the latter are left under condemnation and desolate.
