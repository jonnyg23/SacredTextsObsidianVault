Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 121</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#001">Psalms 121:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 121:1</a></font></span></p>God's guardian care of His people celebrated. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#001">Psa 121:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>I will lift up mine eyes--expresses desire (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psa 25:1</a>), mingled with expectation. The last clause, read as a question, is answered,
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#002">Psalms 121:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 121:2</a></font></span></p>by avowing God to be the helper, of whose ability His creative power is a pledge (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa115.htm#015">Psa 115:15</a>), to which,
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#003">Psalms 121:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 121:3</a></font></span></p>His sleepless vigilance is added. 
 <p>
 </p><p>to be moved--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#016">Psa 38:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#009">Psa 66:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#005">Psalms 121:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 121:5</a></font></span></p>upon thy right hand--a protector's place (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa109.htm#031">Psa 109:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#005">Psa 110:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#006">Psalms 121:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 121:6</a></font></span></p>God keeps His people at all times and in all perils. 
 <p>
 </p><p>nor the moon by night--poetically represents the dangers of the night, over which the moon presides (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#016">Gen 1:16</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#008">Psalms 121:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 121:8</a></font></span></p>thy going out, &amp;c.--all thy ways (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#019">Deu 28:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#023">Psa 104:23</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>evermore--includes a future state.
