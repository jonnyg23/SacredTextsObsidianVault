Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#001">Proverbs 1:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 1:1</a></font></span></p>After the title the writer defines the design and nature of the instructions of the book. He paternally invites attention to those instructions and warns his readers against the enticements of the wicked. In a beautiful personification, wisdom is then introduced in a most solemn and impressive manner, publicly inviting men to receive its teachings, warning those who reject, and encouraging those who accept, the proffered instructions. (Pro. 1:1-33)
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#002">Proverbs 1:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 1:2</a></font></span></p>To know . . . instruction--literally, "for knowing," that is, such is the design of these writings. 
 <p>
 </p><p>wisdom--or the use of the best means for the best ends, is generally employed in this book for true piety. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>instruction--discipline, by which men are trained. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to perceive--literally, "for perceiving," the design (as above) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>understanding--that is, words which enable one to discern good and evil.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#003">Proverbs 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 1:3</a></font></span></p>To receive . . . of wisdom--For receiving that discipline which discretion imparts. The Hebrew for "wisdom" differs from that of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#002">Pro 1:2</a>, and denotes rather discreet counsel. Compare the opposite traits of the fool (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#022">Pro 16:22</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>justice . . . equity--all the attributes of one upright in all his relations to God and man.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Proverbs 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 1:4</a></font></span></p>simple--one easily led to good or evil; so the parallel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>young man--one inexperienced. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>subtilty--or prudence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#021">Pro 3:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#021">Pro 5:21</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>discretion--literally, "device," both qualities, either good or bad, according to their use. Here good, as they imply wariness by which to escape evil and find good.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#005">Proverbs 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 1:5</a></font></span></p>Such writings the wise, who pursue right ends by right means, will value. 
 <p>
 </p><p>learning--not the act, but matter of it. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>wise counsels--or the art and principles of governing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#006">Proverbs 1:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 1:6</a></font></span></p>To understand--so as to . . . such will be the result. 
 <p>
 </p><p>interpretation--(Compare Margin). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>words of the wise--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#002">Pro 1:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>dark sayings--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#004">Psa 49:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#025">Joh 16:25</a>; and see Introduction, Part I).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#007">Proverbs 1:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 1:7</a></font></span></p>The fear of the Lord--the principle of true piety (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#005">Pro 2:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#026">Pro 14:26-27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job028.htm#028">Job 28:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#011">Psa 34:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#010">Psa 111:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#031">Act 9:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>beginning--first part, foundation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>fools--the stupid and indifferent to God's character and government; hence the wicked.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008">Proverbs 1:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 1:8</a></font></span></p>My son--This paternal form denotes a tender regard for the reader. Filial sentiments rank next to piety towards God, and ensure most distinguished rewards (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#020">Pro 6:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#002">Eph 6:2-3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#009">Proverbs 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 1:9</a></font></span></p>On the figures of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#009">Pro 1:9</a>, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen041.htm#042">Gen 41:42</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol001.htm#010">Sol 1:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#009">Sol 4:9</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#010">Proverbs 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 1:10</a></font></span></p>A solemn warning against temptation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>entice--literally, "open the way." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>consent . . . not--Sin is in consenting or yielding to temptation, not in being tempted.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#011">Proverbs 1:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 1:11</a></font></span></p>Murder and robbery are given as specific illustrations. 
 <p>
 </p><p>lay wait . . . lurk privily--express an effort and hope for successful concealment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>swallow . . . grave--utterly destroy the victim and traces of the crime (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#033">Num 16:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa055.htm#015">Psa 55:15</a>). Abundant rewards of villainy are promised as the fruits of this easy and safe course.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#015">Proverbs 1:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 1:15</a></font></span></p>The society of the wicked (way or path) is dangerous. Avoid the beginnings of sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#014">Pro 4:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psa 1:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#101">Psa 119:101</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#017">Proverbs 1:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 1:17</a></font></span></p>Men warned ought to escape danger as birds instinctively avoid visibly spread nets. But stupid sinners rush to their own ruin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#016">Psa 9:16</a>), and, greedy of gain, succeed in the very schemes which destroy them (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#010">Ti1 6:10</a>), not only failing to catch others, but procuring their own destruction.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#020">Proverbs 1:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 1:20</a></font></span></p>Some interpreters regard this address as the language of the Son of God under the name of Wisdom (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#049">Luk 11:49</a>). Others think that wisdom, as the divine attribute specially employed in acts of counsel and admonition, is here personified, and represents God. In either case the address is a most solemn and divine admonition, whose matter and spirit are eminently evangelical and impressive (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#001">Pro 8:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>Wisdom--literally, "Wisdoms," the plural used either because of the unusual sense, or as indicative of the great excellency of wisdom (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Pro 9:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>streets--or most public places, not secretly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#021">Proverbs 1:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 1:21</a></font></span></p>The publicity further indicated by terms designating places of most common resort.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#022">Proverbs 1:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 1:22</a></font></span></p>simple ones--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Pro 1:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>simplicity--implying ignorance. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>scorners-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psa 1:1</a>) --who despise, as well as reject, truth. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>fools--Though a different word is used from that of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#007">Pro 1:7</a>, yet it is of the same meaning.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#023">Proverbs 1:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 1:23</a></font></span></p>reproof--implying conviction deserving it (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#008">Joh 16:8</a>, Margin). 
 <p>
 </p><p>pour out--abundantly impart. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>my spirit--whether of wisdom personified, or of Christ, a divine agent.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#024">Proverbs 1:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 1:24</a></font></span></p>stretched . . . hand--Earnestness, especially in beseeching, is denoted by the figure (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#013">Job 11:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#031">Psa 68:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#009">Psa 88:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#025">Proverbs 1:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 1:25</a></font></span></p>set at naught--rejected as of no value. 
 <p>
 </p><p>would none of--literally, "were not willing or inclined to it."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#026">Proverbs 1:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 1:26</a></font></span></p>In their extreme distress He will not only refuse help, but aggravate it by derision.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#027">Proverbs 1:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 1:27</a></font></span></p>fear--the object of it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>desolation--literally, "a tumultuous noise," denoting their utter confusion. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>destruction--or calamity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#026">Pro 1:26</a>) compared to a whirlwind, as to fatal rapidity. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>distress-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#011">Psa 44:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>anguish--a state of inextricable oppression, the deepest despair.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#028">Proverbs 1:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 1:28</a></font></span></p>Now no prayers or most diligent seeking will avail (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#017">Pro 8:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#029">Proverbs 1:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 1:29</a></font></span></p>The sinner's infatuated rejection brings his ruin.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#031">Proverbs 1:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 1:31</a></font></span></p>fruit . . . way--result of conduct (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#010">Isa 3:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze011.htm#021">Eze 11:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#021">Rom 6:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#007">Gal 6:7-8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>be filled--even to repletion (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa123.htm#004">Psa 123:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#032">Proverbs 1:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 1:32</a></font></span></p>turning away--that is, from the call of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#023">Pro 1:23</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>simple--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#022">Pro 1:22</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>prosperity--quiet, implying indifference.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#033">Proverbs 1:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 1:33</a></font></span></p>dwell safely--literally, "in confidence" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#010">Deu 12:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>be quiet--or at ease, in real prosperity. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>from fear--without fear.
