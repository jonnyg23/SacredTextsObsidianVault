 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Complete Commentary on the Whole Bible</i>, by Matthew Henry, at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg020.htm#001">Judges Chapter 20</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg001.htm#001">Judges</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">jdg 20:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The tribe of Benjamin nearly extirpated. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg020.htm#001">Judges 20:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jdg 20:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The Israelites' abhorrence of the crime committed at Gibeah, and their resolution to punish the criminals, were right; but they formed their resolves with too much haste and self-confidence. The eternal ruin of souls will be worse, and more fearful, than these desolations of a tribe.
