 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Complete Commentary on the Whole Bible</i>, by Matthew Henry, at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001">2 John Introduction</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001">2 John</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">jo2 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This epistle is like an abridgement of the first; it touches, in few words, on the same points. The Lady Electa is commended for her virtuous and religious education of her children; is exhorted to abide in the doctrine of Christ, to persevere in the truth, and carefully to avoid the delusions of false teachers. But chiefly the apostle beseeches her to practise the great commandment of Christian love and charity.
 </p><p>The apostle salutes the elect lady and her children. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#001">Jo1 1:1-3</a>) Express his joy in their faith and love. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#004">Jo1 1:4-6</a>) Cautions them against deceivers. (7-11) And concludes. (12, 13)
