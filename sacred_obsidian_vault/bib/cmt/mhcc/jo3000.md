 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Complete Commentary on the Whole Bible</i>, by Matthew Henry, at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#001">3 John Introduction</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#001">3 John</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">jo3 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This epistle is addressed to a converted Gentile. The scope is to commend his stedfastness in the faith, and his hospitality, especially to the ministers of Christ.
 </p><p>The apostle commends Gaius for piety and hospitality. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001">Jo2 1:1-8</a>) Cautions him against siding with Diotrephes, who was a turbulent spirit; but recommends Demetrius as a man of excellent character. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#009">Jo2 1:9-12</a>) He hopes soon to see Gaius. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#013">Jo2 1:13</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001">Jo2 1:1:14</a>)
