 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The People's New Testament</i>, B.W. Johnson, [1891], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#001">Mark Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#001">Mark</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">mar 14:0</a></font></span></p>SUMMARY.--The Sanhedrim Resolves on Christ's Death. The Anointing at Bethany. Judas Sells His Lord. The Day of Unleavened Bread. The Lord's Passover. The Lord's Supper Established. Peter's Denial Predicted. The Agony in the Garden. Christ Seized by the Soldiers and Temple Guards. Christ Before the Sanhedrim. The Confession and Condemnation. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#001">Mark 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mar 14:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>After two days was the feast of the passover. See notes on Mat. 26:1-16. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#001">Joh 12:1-8</a>. The action of the Sanhedrim was taken on Wednesday, the Anointing took place the Saturday before, and is brought up here on account of its connection with the bargain Judas made with the chief priests. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#010">Mark 14:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">mar 14:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Judas Iscariot. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#014">Mat 26:14-16</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#012">Mark 14:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">mar 14:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>On the first day of unleavened bread. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#017">Mat 26:17-19</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#017">Mark 14:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">mar 14:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>In the evening he cometh. On the Lord's last passover see notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#021">Mat 26:21-25</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#021">Luk 22:21-23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#021">Joh 13:21-35</a>. See also notes on John. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#022">Mark 14:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">mar 14:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>As they did eat. On the institution of the Lord's Supper, see notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#023">Mat 26:23-29</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#019">Luk 22:19-21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#023">Co1 11:23-25</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#027">Mark 14:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">mar 14:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>All ye shall be offended. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#031">Mat 26:31-35</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#031">Luk 22:31-38</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#032">Mark 14:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">mar 14:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>They came to a place which was named Gethsemane. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#036">Mat 26:36-46</a>, where the fullest account of the agony is given. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#040">Luk 22:40-46</a>. Luke only speaks of the "sweat, as it were drops of blood." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#043">Mark 14:43</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_043">mar 14:43</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>While he yet spake, cometh Judas. For the Betrayal and Arrest, see notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#047">Mat 26:47-56</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#047">Luk 22:47-53</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#001">Joh 18:1-12</a>. See notes on John. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#051">Mar 14:51-52</a> are peculiar to Mark. Some have supposed the certain young man to be Mark. This is only conjecture. The incident may have been introduced to show the rudeness of the assailants and to emphasize the escape of all the disciples from so wanton an attack. The linen cloth was a night robe. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#053">Mark 14:53</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_053">mar 14:53</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>They led Jesus away to the high priest. For Trial of Christ, see notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#057">Mat 26:57-68</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#063">Luk 22:63-71</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#013">Joh 18:13-27</a>. Mark's account corresponds very closely with Matthew's. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#066">Mark 14:66</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_066">mar 14:66</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>As Peter was beneath in the palace. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#069">Mat 26:69-75</a>. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#054">Luk 22:54-62</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#015">Joh 18:15-17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#025">Joh 18:25-27</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>He wept. The form of the Greek verb (imperfect) implies that he continued weeping. "It is a touching and beautiful tradition, true to the sincerity of his repentance, if not as a historical reality, that, all his life long, the remembrance of this night never left him, and that, morning by morning, he rose at the hour when the look of his Master had entered his soul, to pray once more for pardon."--Geikie.
