 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The People's New Testament</i>, B.W. Johnson, [1891], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#001">Luke Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#001">Luke</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">luk 6:0</a></font></span></p>SUMMARY.--The Son of Man Lord of the Sabbath. The Man with the Withered Hand Healed. Accused of Sabbath-Breaking. The Apostles Chosen. Blessings and Woes. Returning Good for Evil. Evil and Good Fruit. The Wise and Foolish Builders. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#001">Luke 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">luk 6:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>It came to pass on a sabbath. Such is the reading of the Revision. The common text, the second-first in the Greek, is referred by Godet to second-first Sunday of the year. The first first Sabbath was the first Sabbath of the civil year; the second first was the first sabbath of the religious year. It came in the spring. For notes on the controversy caused by the Lord's disciples plucking the ears of wheat on the Sabbath, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#001">Mat 12:1-14</a>. Compare also <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar002.htm#023">Mar 2:23-28</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#001">Mar 3:1-5</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Ears of corn. Of wheat or barley. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#006">Luke 6:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">luk 6:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>It came to pass on another sabbath. For notes on the healing of the withered hand on the Sabbath, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#001">Mat 12:1-15</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#001">Mar 3:1-5</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#012">Luke 6:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">luk 6:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Went out into a mountain to pray. Preparatory to calling the apostles. Our Lord always prepared for any great crisis by prayer. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#013">Luke 6:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">luk 6:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>He called his disciples. After the night of prayer. Out of these twelve were chosen. For notes on the call of the Apostles see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#001">Mat 10:1-5</a>. These are four lists of the twelve: that here: <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#002">Mat 10:2-4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#016">Mar 3:16-19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#013">Act 1:13</a>. They differ slightly because some of the apostles had two different names: for example, Matthew is also called Levi, and Simon is called Peter. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#017">Luke 6:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">luk 6:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Came down and stood on a level place. We now come to Luke's report of the Sermon on the Mount, given much more fully in chapters 5, 6 and 7 of Matthew, on which see notes. Luke only states that the sermon was preceded by the healing of the diseased in the vast multitudes which had assembled from all parts of the country. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#020">Luke 6:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">luk 6:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Blessed. These beatitudes are given more fully in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#003">Mat 5:3-12</a>, where see notes. Luke says kingdom of God; Matthew, of heaven, showing that the two are the same. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#024">Luke 6:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">luk 6:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Woe unto you that are rich! Matthew omits these woes. Woe unto all whose heart is set upon their riches and who take their delight and consolation in them. The woe rests upon all who are engaged in the greedy chase after wealth, or make it their great good. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#024">Mar 10:24</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#009">Ti1 6:9-10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#017">Ti1 6:17</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Woe unto ye that are full now! Because, satisfied, they do not hunger after righteousness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#006">Mat 5:6</a>). Such shall hunger when stripped of all that is earthly. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Woe unto you, ye that laugh now! Give themselves to merriment, seeking fun and enjoyment instead of preparation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>When all men shall speak well of you! Those who seek to be universally popular. Whoever does his duty will meet those who oppose him. Christ was reviled and slandered. These four woes are addressed to four classes: (1) Those who worship wealth; (2) those satisfied with their present life; (3) those who live for present enjoyment; (4) those whose aim is human praise and adulation. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#027">Luke 6:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">luk 6:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Love your enemies. These precepts are found in Matthew's report in their connection. See notes there. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#037">Luke 6:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">luk 6:37</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Judge not. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#001">Mat 7:1-2</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Give into your bosom. The loose garment of a Jew was belted at the waist, and in its capacious bosom grain, or other articles, were carried. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#039">Luke 6:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">luk 6:39</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Can the blind lead the blind? See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#014">Mat 15:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#040">Luke 6:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">luk 6:40</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>The disciple is not above his master. See note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#024">Mat 10:24</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#041">Luke 6:41</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_041">luk 6:41</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>The mote that is in thy brother's eye. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#003">Mat 7:3-5</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#043">Luke 6:43</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_043">luk 6:43</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>For a good tree. See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#015">Mat 7:15-20</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#046">Luke 6:46</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_046">luk 6:46</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Why call ye me, Lord, Lord? See notes on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#021">Mat 7:21-27</a>. Luke adds: Digged deep. This is often needful to reach the rock. We must dig deep, even to our heart, to its center, to build upon the Rock, Christ our Lord. The confession of faith in Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#016">Mat 16:16</a>) must be believed with the heart (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#010">Rom 10:10</a>), confessed with the mouth, in order that we may be built upon the Rock (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018">Mat 16:18</a>).
