 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#001">Lamentations Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#001">Lamentations 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lam 4:1</a></font></span></p>The top - Are scattered in the head of every street. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#002">Lamentations 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">lam 4:2</a></font></span></p>Earthen pitchers - The nobles, the priests, and the good men, are looked upon no better than earthen vessels, the workmanship of an ordinary potter. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#003">Lamentations 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">lam 4:3</a></font></span></p>Cruel - The Jewish women are become cruel to their children, or forced to appear so, having through the famine no milk to give them, nor any thing to relieve them. Ostriches - Like ostriches that lay their eggs, and leave them in the sand. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#006">Lamentations 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">lam 4:6</a></font></span></p>Of Sodom - Their punishment was greater, because more lingering, and gradual, whereas Sodom was overthrown in a moment, and that by no human hands that abode upon her, causing her a continued torment. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#007">Lamentations 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">lam 4:7</a></font></span></p>Nazarites - Her Nazarites in this place signify her separated ones, who either in respect of birth, education, estate, or place of magistracy, were distinguished from the rest of the people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#008">Lamentations 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">lam 4:8</a></font></span></p>Not known - So that those who before knew them, do not know them now. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#013">Lamentations 4:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">lam 4:13</a></font></span></p>Priests - The ecclesiastical men were a great cause of the first and last destruction of Jerusalem. And so they are of most other places that come to ruin, through their neglect of their duty, or encouraging others in their wicked courses. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#014">Lamentations 4:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">lam 4:14</a></font></span></p>They - The prophets and priests wandered up and down the streets polluting themselves with blood, either the blood of the children which they slew, or the just men, mentioned <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#013">Lam 4:13</a>, the slaughter of whom they either encouraged, or at least did not discourage; so that one could not touch a prophet or priest, but he must be legally polluted, and there were so many of them, that men could not walk in the streets, but he must touch some of them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#015">Lamentations 4:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">lam 4:15</a></font></span></p>Touch not - The Jews that made conscience of keeping the law against touching dead bodies, cried to the other Jews to leave the city as themselves did, the city being now so full of dead bodies that they could not stay in it without polluting themselves. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#016">Lamentations 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">lam 4:16</a></font></span></p>The anger - These words seem to be the language of their enemies triumphing over them. They - Their enemies had no regard to the most venerable persons among them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#017">Lamentations 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">lam 4:17</a></font></span></p>A nation - The Egyptians. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#018">Lamentations 4:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">lam 4:18</a></font></span></p>They - The Chaldeans. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#020">Lamentations 4:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">lam 4:20</a></font></span></p>The anointed - Zedekiah, who though a bad man yet afforded some protection to the Jews. We said - We promised ourselves that though the land of Judah was encompassed with Pagan nations, yet through Zedekiah's valour and good conduct we should live comfortably. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#021">Lamentations 4:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">lam 4:21</a></font></span></p>Rejoice - The prophet speaks ironically, Rejoice; but thy joy shall be but for a little time. Drunken - Thou shalt be intoxicated with it, and make thyself naked as drunken men sometimes do. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam004.htm#022">Lamentations 4:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">lam 4:22</a></font></span></p>Captivity - Not for thy past sins. Thy sins - By the punishment of them.
