 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#001">Lamentations Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#003">Lamentations 5:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">lam 5:3</a></font></span></p>We - We are all of us without a king, (our common father) we are deprived of thy fatherly protection, and many young children among us are left without an earthly parent. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#004">Lamentations 5:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">lam 5:4</a></font></span></p>Sold - Whereas at other times there was abundance of wood and water throughout Judea. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#006">Lamentations 5:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">lam 5:6</a></font></span></p>We - The ten tribes were all carried captives into Assyria, and many of the kingdom of Judah fled into Egypt. Giving the hand may signify labouring for them: or, yielding up themselves to their power. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#007">Lamentations 5:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">lam 5:7</a></font></span></p>Their iniquities - The punishment of them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#009">Lamentations 5:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">lam 5:9</a></font></span></p>The sword - The enemies lay encamped in all the plains, so that they could stir out no way but the sword of the Chaldeans was upon them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#013">Lamentations 5:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">lam 5:13</a></font></span></p>Fell - Not being able to stand under the burdens laid upon them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#016">Lamentations 5:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">lam 5:16</a></font></span></p>The crown - All our honour, splendor and dignity. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#019">Lamentations 5:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">lam 5:19</a></font></span></p>Thy throne - Altho' for our sins thou sufferest our throne to be cast down, yet thou art the same, thy power is not diminished, nor thy goodness abated. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam005.htm#021">Lamentations 5:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">lam 5:21</a></font></span></p>Renew - Restore us to our former estate.
