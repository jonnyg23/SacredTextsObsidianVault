 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 32</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#002">Psalms 32:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 32:2</a></font></span></p>Imputeth - Whom God doth not charge with the guilt of his sins, but graciously pardons and accepts him in Christ. No guile - Who freely confesses all his sins, and turns from sin to God with all his heart. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#003">Psalms 32:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 32:3</a></font></span></p>Silence - From a full and open confession of my sins. Old - My spirit failed, and the strength of my body decayed. Roaring - Because of the continual horrors of my conscience, and sense of God's wrath. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#004">Psalms 32:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 32:4</a></font></span></p>Hand - Thy afflicting hand. My moisture - Was dried up. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#005">Psalms 32:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 32:5</a></font></span></p>The iniquity - The guilt of my sin. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#006">Psalms 32:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 32:6</a></font></span></p>For this - Upon the encouragement of my example. Found - In an acceptable and seasonable time, while God continues to offer grace and mercy. Waters - In the time of great calamities. Not come - So as to overwhelm him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#008">Psalms 32:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 32:8</a></font></span></p>I will - This and the next verse seems to be the words of God, whom David brings in as returning this answer to his prayers. Mine eye - So Christ did St. Peter, when he turned and looked upon him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#009">Psalms 32:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 32:9</a></font></span></p>Will not - Unless they be forced to it by a bit or bridle. And so all the ancient translators understand it. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#010">Psalms 32:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 32:10</a></font></span></p>Sorrows - This is an argument to enforce the foregoing admonition.
