 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 60</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#002">Psalms 60:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 60:2</a></font></span></p>Tremble - A poetical expression, signifying great changes among the people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#003">Psalms 60:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 60:3</a></font></span></p>To drink - Thou hast filled us with no less honor, than men intoxicated with strong drink. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#004">Psalms 60:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 60:4</a></font></span></p>A banner - Which is a sign and instrument, Of union. This people who were lately divided, thou hast united under one banner, under my government: Of battle. Thou hast given us an army, and power to oppose our enemies; which blessing God gave to Israel, for the sake of those few sincere Israelites who were among them. The truth - Not for any merit of ours, but to shew thy faithfulness in making good thy promises. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005">Psalms 60:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 60:5</a></font></span></p>Beloved - Thy beloved people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#006">Psalms 60:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 60:6</a></font></span></p>Rejoice - Therefore I will turn my prayers into praises, for what God has already done. Divide - Which supposeth possession and dominion. Shechem - A place within Jordan, in mount Ephraim. Succoth - A place without Jordan. He mentions Shechem, and Succoth; for all the land of Canaan, within and without Jordan. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#007">Psalms 60:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 60:7</a></font></span></p>Gilead - All the land beyond Jordan, which was possessed by Reuben and Gad, and half of the tribe of Manasseh. Manasseh - The other half of that tribe within Jordan. The strength - A chief part of my strength, either to offend mine enemies, or to defend myself. For this tribe was very numerous, and valiant and rich. Law - giver - The chief seat of my throne and kingdom, and of the inferior throne of judgment, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#005">Psa 122:5</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#008">Psalms 60:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 60:8</a></font></span></p>Wash - pot - In which I shall wash my feet. I shall bring them into the lowest degree of servitude. Shoe - I will use them like slaves; a proverbial expression. Triumph - It is an ironical expression, signifying that her triumphs were come to an end. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#009">Psalms 60:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 60:9</a></font></span></p>Who - None can do it but God. City - The cities; the singular number for the plural. Having beaten his enemies out of the field, he desires God's assistance to take their strong - holds, and so secure himself from farther attempts. Edom - Which was an high and rocky country, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/oba001.htm#001">Oba 1:1-3</a>, fortified by nature, as well as by art, and therefore not to be subdued without a Divine hand. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#010">Psalms 60:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 60:10</a></font></span></p>Hadst cut off - But now hast graciously returned to us.
