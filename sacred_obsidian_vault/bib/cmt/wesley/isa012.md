 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#001">Isaiah 12:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 12:1</a></font></span></p>In that day - When this great work of the reduction of Israel, and conversion of the Gentiles is fulfilled. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#002">Isaiah 12:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 12:2</a></font></span></p>God - My salvation hath not been brought to pass by man, but by the almighty power of God. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#003">Isaiah 12:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 12:3</a></font></span></p>With joy - Your thirsty souls shall be filled with Divine graces and comforts, which you may draw from God, in the use of gospel - ordinances.
