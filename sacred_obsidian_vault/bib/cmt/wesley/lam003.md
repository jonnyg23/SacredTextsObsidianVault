 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#001">Lamentations Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#001">Lamentations 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lam 3:1</a></font></span></p>I am the man - It seems, this is spoken in the name of the people, who were before set out under the notion of a woman. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#004">Lamentations 3:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">lam 3:4</a></font></span></p>Made old - All my beauty is gone, and all my strength. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#005">Lamentations 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">lam 3:5</a></font></span></p>Builded - He hath built forts and batteries against my walls and houses. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#009">Lamentations 3:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">lam 3:9</a></font></span></p>Enclosed - He has defeated all my methods and counsels for security, by insuperable difficulties like walls of hewn stone. Crooked - Nay, God not only defeated their counsels, but made them fatal and pernicious to them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#015">Lamentations 3:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">lam 3:15</a></font></span></p>Wormwood - With severe and bitter dispensations. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#016">Lamentations 3:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">lam 3:16</a></font></span></p>Ashes - Mourners were wont to throw ashes on their heads. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#019">Lamentations 3:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">lam 3:19</a></font></span></p>Wormwood - Wormwood and gall, are often made use of to signify great affliction. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#021">Lamentations 3:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">lam 3:21</a></font></span></p>This - Which follows, concerning the nature of God, and his good providences. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#023">Lamentations 3:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">lam 3:23</a></font></span></p>Faithfulness - In fulfilling thy promises to thy people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#027">Lamentations 3:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">lam 3:27</a></font></span></p>Bear - Quietly and patiently to bear what afflictions God will please to lay upon us. And if God tame us when young, by his word or by his rod, it is an unspeakable advantage. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#028">Lamentations 3:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">lam 3:28</a></font></span></p>Borne it - That he keep his soul in subjection to God, because God hath humbled him by his rod. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#029">Lamentations 3:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">lam 3:29</a></font></span></p>In the dust - Both this and the former verses let us know the duty of persons under afflictions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#033">Lamentations 3:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">lam 3:33</a></font></span></p>Willingly - Not from his own mere motion without a cause given him from the persons afflicted. Hence judgment is called God's strange work. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#036">Lamentations 3:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">lam 3:36</a></font></span></p>To subvert - Here are three things mentioned, which God approveth not. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#037">Lamentations 3:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">lam 3:37</a></font></span></p>Who - Nothing comes to pass in the world, but by the disposal of divine providence. This seems to be spoken in the name of the people of God, arguing themselves into a quiet submission, to their afflictions, from the consideration of the hand of God in them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#038">Lamentations 3:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">lam 3:38</a></font></span></p>Evil - Doth not evil or trouble come out of God's mouth from his direction, and providence, as well as good? 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#039">Lamentations 3:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">lam 3:39</a></font></span></p>Wherefore - The Jews, check themselves in their complaints from the consideration, that nothing had befallen them, but what was the just reward of their sins. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#042">Lamentations 3:42</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_042">lam 3:42</a></font></span></p>Thou - Thou hast plagued us according to the just desert of our sins. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#049">Lamentations 3:49</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_049">lam 3:49</a></font></span></p>Mine eye - The prophet speaks this of himself. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#053">Lamentations 3:53</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_053">lam 3:53</a></font></span></p>Dungeon - Dungeon seems here to be taken for the lowest condition of misery. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#054">Lamentations 3:54</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_054">lam 3:54</a></font></span></p>Cut off - I am undone, there is no hope for me. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#056">Lamentations 3:56</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_056">lam 3:56</a></font></span></p>Heard - In former afflictions. Hide not - Shew me now the same favour. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#058">Lamentations 3:58</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_058">lam 3:58</a></font></span></p>O Lord - Thou hast been wont to take my part against my enemies. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#060">Lamentations 3:60</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_060">lam 3:60</a></font></span></p>Seen - Thou hast been a witness to all their fury. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#063">Lamentations 3:63</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_063">lam 3:63</a></font></span></p>I am - At feasts, and at their merry meetings, I am all the subject of their discourse. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lam003.htm#066">Lamentations 3:66</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_066">lam 3:66</a></font></span></p>Persecute - Many passages of this nature which we meet with are prophecies, some of them may be both prophecies and prayers.
