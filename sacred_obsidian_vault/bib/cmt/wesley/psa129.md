 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 129</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#001">Psalms 129:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 129:1</a></font></span></p>From my youth - From the time that I was a people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#004">Psalms 129:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 129:4</a></font></span></p>Righteous - Faithful or merciful. The cords - Wherewith the plow was drawn. By these cords he understands all their plots and endeavours. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#006">Psalms 129:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 129:6</a></font></span></p>House - tops - Which there were flat. Which - Having no deep root, never comes to maturity. And so all their designs shall be abortive. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa129.htm#008">Psalms 129:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 129:8</a></font></span></p>The blessing - Which was an usual salutation given by passengers to reapers: so the meaning is, it never continues 'till the harvest comes.
