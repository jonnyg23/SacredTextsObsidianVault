 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#001">Ezekiel 26:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">eze 26:1</a></font></span></p>In the eleventh year - Of Jechoniah's captivity, the year wherein Jerusalem was taken. The month - That month which followed the taking of Jerusalem. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#002">Ezekiel 26:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">eze 26:2</a></font></span></p>Because - Probably God revealed this to the prophet as soon as these insulting Tyrians spoke it. The gates - The great mart of nations, people from all parts. She is turned - The trading interest will turn to me. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#004">Ezekiel 26:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">eze 26:4</a></font></span></p>Scrape - I will leave thee nothing; thou shalt be scraped, and swept, that not so much as dust shall remain in thee. Like - As bare as was the rock on which thy city is built. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#006">Ezekiel 26:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 26:6</a></font></span></p>Her daughters - The lesser cities. In the field - On the firm land. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#011">Ezekiel 26:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">eze 26:11</a></font></span></p>Garrisons - Bastions, or forts, or triumphal arches. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#012">Ezekiel 26:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 26:12</a></font></span></p>Shall lay - It had been a quicker way, to have burnt all; but the greedy soldier might dream of treasures hid in walls, or under the timber, and therefore take the pains to pull all down, and throw it into the sea. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#014">Ezekiel 26:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 26:14</a></font></span></p>No more - Tho' there was a city of that name built, yet it was built on the continent; and in propriety of speech, was another city. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#015">Ezekiel 26:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">eze 26:15</a></font></span></p>The isles - Isles which are places freest from danger of invasions, will shake with fear, when they learn that Tyre is fallen. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#016">Ezekiel 26:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">eze 26:16</a></font></span></p>The princes - Who were lords of the islands of that sea. Come down - In token of condolence. Trembling - They shall be afraid of their own concerns, and astonished in the midst of their fears. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#018">Ezekiel 26:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">eze 26:18</a></font></span></p>In the sea - At a great distance, and farther from land. Departure - Leaving thy ancient dwelling, to go into captivity. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#019">Ezekiel 26:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">eze 26:19</a></font></span></p>The deep - Nebuchadnezzar's army. Great waters - Great afflictions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#020">Ezekiel 26:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">eze 26:20</a></font></span></p>Bring thee down - When I shall slay thee, and throw thee into the grave. With the people - Who are long since dead, and gone to eternity. The low parts - Another description of the grave, from the situation and solitude of it. Set glory - Then I will restore the beauty, strength, and wealth of Israel, and bring them back to Jerusalem. In the land - In the land of Judea, called, land of the living, because a land, where God will bless, and give life by his word, ordinances, and spirit: thus different shall Tyre's captivity and Jerusalem's be. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze026.htm#021">Ezekiel 26:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">eze 26:21</a></font></span></p>A terror - To all that hear of thee.
