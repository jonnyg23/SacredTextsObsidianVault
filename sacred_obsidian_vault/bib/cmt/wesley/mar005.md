 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#001">Mark Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#001">Mark 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mar 5:1</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#028">Mat 8:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#026">Luk 8:26</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#002">Mark 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">mar 5:2</a></font></span></p>There met him a man with an unclean spirit - St. Matthew mentions two. Probably this, so particularly spoken of here, was the most remarkably fierce and ungovernable. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#009">Mark 5:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">mar 5:9</a></font></span></p>My name is Legion! for we are many - But all these seem to have been under one commander, who accordingly speaks all along, both for them and himself. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#015">Mark 5:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">mar 5:15</a></font></span></p>And they were afraid - It is not improbable they might otherwise have offered some rudeness, if not violence. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#018">Mark 5:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">mar 5:18</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#001">Mat 9:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#037">Luk 8:37</a>; 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#019">Mark 5:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">mar 5:19</a></font></span></p>Tell them how great things the Lord hath done for thee - This was peculiarly needful there, where Christ did not go in person. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#020">Mark 5:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">mar 5:20</a></font></span></p>He published in Decapolis - Not only at home, but in all that country where Jesus himself did not come. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#021">Mark 5:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">mar 5:21</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#040">Luk 8:40</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#022">Mark 5:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">mar 5:22</a></font></span></p>One of the rulers of the synagogue - To regulate the affairs of every synagogue, there was a council of grave men. Over these was a president, who was termed the ruler of the synagogue. Sometimes there was no more than one ruler in a synagogue. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#018">Mat 9:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#041">Luk 8:41</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#025">Mark 5:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">mar 5:25</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#020">Mat 9:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#043">Luk 8:43</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#037">Mark 5:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">mar 5:37</a></font></span></p>John, the brother of James - When St. Mark wrote, not long after our Lord's ascension, the memory of St. James, lately beheaded, was so fresh, that his name was more known than that of John himself. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#040">Mark 5:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">mar 5:40</a></font></span></p>Them that were with him - Peter, James, and John. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#043">Mark 5:43</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_043">mar 5:43</a></font></span></p>He charged them that no man should know it - That he might avoid every appearance of vain glory, might prevent too great a concourse of people, and might not farther enrage the scribes and Pharisees against him; the time for his death, and for the full manifestation of his glory, being not yet come. He commanded something should be given her to eat - So that when either natural or spiritual life is restored, even by immediate miracle, all proper means are to be used in order to preserve it.
