 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#001">Job Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#001">Job 24:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">job 24:1</a></font></span></p>Why - Why (how comes it to pass) seeing times, (the fittest seasons for every, action, and particularly for the punishment of wicked men,) are not hidden from, or unknown to the Almighty God, (seeing all times, and men that live, and things that are done, or to be done in their times and seasons, are exactly known to God) do they that know him, (who love and obey him) not see (whence is it that they cannot discern) his (that is, God's) days? His times and seasons which he takes for the punishment of ungodly men; which if they were constant and fixed in this life, they would not be unknown to good men, to whom God uses to reveal his secrets. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#003">Job 24:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">job 24:3</a></font></span></p>Pledge - Contrary to God's law, first written in mens hearts, and afterwards in holy scripture, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo022.htm#026">Exo 22:26-27</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#004">Job 24:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">job 24:4</a></font></span></p>Way - Out of the path or place in which these oppressors walk and range. They labour to keep out of their way for fear of their farther injuries. Hide - For fear of these tyrants. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#005">Job 24:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">job 24:5</a></font></span></p>Wild asses - Which are lawless, and fierce, and greedy of prey. Desert - Which is the proper habitation of wild asses. They - The oppressors. Go - To spoil and rob. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#006">Job 24:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">job 24:6</a></font></span></p>They - The oppressors. Wicked - Of such as themselves: so they promiscuously robbed all, even their brethren in iniquity. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#007">Job 24:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">job 24:7</a></font></span></p>Naked - Those whom they stripped of their garments and coverings. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#008">Job 24:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">job 24:8</a></font></span></p>Wet - With the rain - water, which runs down the rocks or mountains into the caves, to which they fled for shelter. Rock - Are glad when they can find a cleft of a rock in which they may have some protection against the weather. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#009">Job 24:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">job 24:9</a></font></span></p>They - The oppressors. Pluck - Out of covetousness; they will not allow the mother time for the suckling of her infant. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#010">Job 24:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">job 24:10</a></font></span></p>The sheaf - That single sheaf which the poor man had got with the sweat of his brow to satisfy his hunger. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#011">Job 24:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">job 24:11</a></font></span></p>Walls - Within the walls of the oppressors for their use. Suffer - Because they are not permitted to quench their thirst out of the wine which they make. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#012">Job 24:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">job 24:12</a></font></span></p>Groan - Under grievous oppressions. Soul - The life or blood of those who are wounded to death, as this word properly signifies, crieth aloud to God for vengeance. Yet - Yet God doth not punish them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#013">Job 24:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">job 24:13</a></font></span></p>Light - As well the light of reason and conscience, as the light of Divine revelation, which was then in good measure imparted to the people of God, and shortly after committed to writing. Know not - They do not approve, nor love, or chuse them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#014">Job 24:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">job 24:14</a></font></span></p>Poor - Where he finds nothing to satisfy his covetousness, he exercises his cruelty. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#016">Job 24:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">job 24:16</a></font></span></p>They - The robber: having on that occasion inserted the mention of the adulterer as one who acted his sin in the same manner as the night - thief did, he now returns to him again. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#017">Job 24:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">job 24:17</a></font></span></p>Is - Terrible and hateful. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#018">Job 24:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">job 24:18</a></font></span></p>Swift - That is, he quickly passeth away with all his glory, as the waters which never stay in one place, but are always hasting away. Portion - His habitation and estate which he left behind him. He - He shall never more see or enjoy his vineyards, or other pleasant places and things, which seem to be comprehended under this particular. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#020">Job 24:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">job 24:20</a></font></span></p>Womb - His mother that bare him in her womb. Wickedness - The wicked man. Broken - Broken to pieces, or violently broken down, as the word signifies. Tree - Which being once broken down never grows again. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#021">Job 24:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">job 24:21</a></font></span></p>He - He here returns to the declaration of his farther wickednesses, the cause of these judgments. Barren - Barrenness was esteemed a curse and reproach; and so he added affliction to the afflicted. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#022">Job 24:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">job 24:22</a></font></span></p>Draweth - Into his net. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#023">Job 24:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">job 24:23</a></font></span></p>Yet - Yet his eyes are upon their ways: although God gives them such strange successes, yet he sees and observes them all, and will in due time punish them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#024">Job 24:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">job 24:24</a></font></span></p>The way - Out of this world. Other - They can no more prevent or delay their death, than the meanest men in the world. Corn - In its greatest height and maturity.
