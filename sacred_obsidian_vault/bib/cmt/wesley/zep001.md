 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#001">Zephaniah Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#001">Zephaniah 1:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">zep 1:1</a></font></span></p>Zephaniah - He is thought to have been the great - grandson of king Hezekiah. In the days of Josiah - So he was cotemporary with Jeremiah and Ezekiel, and foretells what Jeremiah and Ezekiel did. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#004">Zephaniah 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">zep 1:4</a></font></span></p>The remnant - Whatsoever remains of the idolatry of Baal. This place - Jerusalem. The name - Both the persons, and the memory of them. The Chemarims - Either called so from their black garments they went in, or, from their swarthy colour occasioned by the black smoak of incense: they were door - keepers, and sextons of Baal. The priests - The priests of Baal. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#005">Zephaniah 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">zep 1:5</a></font></span></p>House - tops - On the flat roofs of their houses. And that swear - That mixt idol - worship, and the worship of the true God; that devote themselves to God, and Baal, or Malchim, that is, Moloch. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#007">Zephaniah 1:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">zep 1:7</a></font></span></p>Hold thy peace - Thou that murmurest against God, stand in awe. The day - A day of vengeance from the Lord. A sacrifice - The wicked Jews, whom he will sacrifice by the sword. His guests - summoned the beasts of the field, and the fowls of the air, to eat the flesh, and drink the blood. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#008">Zephaniah 1:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">zep 1:8</a></font></span></p>The princes - The great ones, who dreamed of shifting better than others, but fell with the first, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#019">Kg2 25:19-21</a>. Children - Sons and grand - children, Josiah: Jehoahaz died a captive in Egypt, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2023.htm#034">Kg2 23:34</a>, Jehoakim died in Babylon, and was buried with the burial of an ass, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#018">Jer 22:18-19</a>, Jeconiah died a captive: and Zedekiah and his children, fared still worse. Strange apparel - The garb of foreigners, imitated by the wanton Jews. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#009">Zephaniah 1:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">zep 1:9</a></font></span></p>In the same day - At the same time. Their masters houses - Either the oppressing kings, whose officers these were, or publick officers and judges, whose servants thus spoiled the poor. Violence - Goods taken by force, by false accusations, or by suborned evidence. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#010">Zephaniah 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">zep 1:10</a></font></span></p>The noise - The great out - cry and lamentation. The fish gate - At which gate the Babylonians first entered into the city. The second - This gate was in the second wall of Jerusalem, which on that side was fortified with three walls. Crashing - Of things broken into shivers; possibly the noise of doors, windows, closets, and chests broken up. The hills - On which the city stood. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#011">Zephaniah 1:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">zep 1:11</a></font></span></p>Howl - Cry aloud, and bitterly. Maktesh - The lower town. Merchant people - Who were wont to lodge in this place. That bear silver - That brought it with them to pay for what they bought. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#012">Zephaniah 1:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">zep 1:12</a></font></span></p>I will search - God speaks after the manner of men, who searches dark places with candles. He will fully discover and punish. Their lees - In allusion to liquors, which not being poured out from vessel to vessel to refine them, grow thick and foul. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#014">Zephaniah 1:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">zep 1:14</a></font></span></p>The voice if the day - The day which will come with a great noise. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#015">Zephaniah 1:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">zep 1:15</a></font></span></p>A day - Of unparalleled calamities. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#017">Zephaniah 1:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">zep 1:17</a></font></span></p>Like blind men - Not knowing where to go. As dust - As abundantly, and as carelessly as dust in the highway. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep001.htm#018">Zephaniah 1:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">zep 1:18</a></font></span></p>In the land - Therefore let not sinners be laid asleep by the patience of God; for when the measure of their iniquity is full, his justice will both overtake and overcome them, will make quick and thorough work.
