 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 132</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#001">Psalms 132:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 132:1</a></font></span></p>Afflictions - All his sufferings for thy sake. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#005">Psalms 132:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 132:5</a></font></span></p>Until - Until I have raised an house in which the ark may be put. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#006">Psalms 132:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 132:6</a></font></span></p>It - Of the ark. Ephratah - In the tribe of Ephraim, which was called also Ephratah. Found it - Afterwards we found it in Kirjath - jearim, which signifies a city of woods, in the territory whereof the ark was seated for twenty years. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#007">Psalms 132:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 132:7</a></font></span></p>Tabernacles - Into his temple. Footstool - The ark, is often said to sit between the cherubim, which were above the ark. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#008">Psalms 132:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 132:8</a></font></span></p>Rest - Into thy resting place, the temple so called, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#001">Isa 66:1</a>, where thou hast now a fixed habitation. The ark - The seat of thy powerful and glorious presence. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#010">Psalms 132:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 132:10</a></font></span></p>David's sake - In regard of thy promises vouchsafed to David. Turn not - Cast me not out of thy presence. Of - Of me whom thou hast anointed to be king over thy people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#016">Psalms 132:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 132:16</a></font></span></p>Salvation - With thy saving graces and blessings. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#017">Psalms 132:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 132:17</a></font></span></p>There - In Jerusalem. To bud - His power and glory to flourish. A lamp - A successor to continue for ever in his family, as this phrase is expounded <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1011.htm#036">Kg1 11:36</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#004">Kg1 15:4</a>, and particularly one eminent and glorious light, namely, the Messiah.
