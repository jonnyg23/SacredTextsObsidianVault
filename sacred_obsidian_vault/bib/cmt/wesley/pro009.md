 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Proverbs 9:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 9:1</a></font></span></p>Her house - For the reception of her guests. Seven - Many pillars; whereby is intimated the beauty and stability of the church. Pillars - Prophets, and apostles, and ministers. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#002">Proverbs 9:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 9:2</a></font></span></p>Killed - Made provision for the guests. Mingled - With water, as they used to do in those hot countries. Furnished - With all necessaries, and now waits for the guests. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#003">Proverbs 9:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 9:3</a></font></span></p>Maidens - Her servants to invite the guests, ministers of the word whom he calls maidens for the decency of the parable; for wisdom being compared to a great princess, was fit to be attended upon by maidens. Highest places - From such high seats as those from which judges delivered their sentences, and officers made proclamations. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#004">Proverbs 9:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 9:4</a></font></span></p>Simple - Ignorant, and weak. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#007">Proverbs 9:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 9:7</a></font></span></p>A former - He shews whom he meant by the foolish, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#006">Pro 9:6</a>, even scorners and wicked men, and presses his last advice of forsaking them because there was no good, but hurt to be got from them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008">Proverbs 9:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 9:8</a></font></span></p>A scorner - An obstinate and incorrigible sinner. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#012">Proverbs 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 9:12</a></font></span></p>For thyself - Thou dost not profit me but thyself. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#014">Proverbs 9:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 9:14</a></font></span></p>At the door - Watching for occasions of sin. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#015">Proverbs 9:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 9:15</a></font></span></p>Go right - Who are going innocently about their business. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#016">Proverbs 9:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 9:16</a></font></span></p>Simple - This title is not given them by her, but by Solomon. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#017">Proverbs 9:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 9:17</a></font></span></p>Sweet - From the difficulty of obtaining them; and because the very prohibition renders them more grateful to corrupt nature.
