 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 34</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#002">Ezekiel 34:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">eze 34:2</a></font></span></p>The shepherds - The rulers of the people kings, magistrates, and princes; as also priests, and prophets. Of Israel - The two tribes, and the few out of the ten that adhere to the house of David. That feed - Contrive their own ease, advantage, and honour. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#003">Ezekiel 34:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 34:3</a></font></span></p>Ye kill - You contrive methods, to take first the life, and next the estate of the well - fed, the rich and wealthy. But - You take care to lead, protect, provide for, and watch over them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#004">Ezekiel 34:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">eze 34:4</a></font></span></p>The diseased - The weak and languishing. Bound up - Oppressors in the state, or church, broke many then, but these shepherds bound them not up. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#005">Ezekiel 34:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">eze 34:5</a></font></span></p>No shepherd - No vigilant, faithful shepherd. Became meat - Were made a prey of, and devoured by all their neighbours. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#012">Ezekiel 34:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 34:12</a></font></span></p>In the cloudy and dark day - In the time of general distress. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#016">Ezekiel 34:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">eze 34:16</a></font></span></p>The fat - The powerful and rich. I will feed - I will judge and punish them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#017">Ezekiel 34:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">eze 34:17</a></font></span></p>I judge - Between men and men, between the smaller and weaker, and the greater and stronger, as their different state requires I will do. The rams - Rulers, who also shalt be dealt with according to their behaviour. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#018">Ezekiel 34:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">eze 34:18</a></font></span></p>But ye must tread down - You great ones, eat the fat, and sweet; and what you cannot eat, you waste and spoil. The deep waters - Which are sufficient for all. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#020">Ezekiel 34:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">eze 34:20</a></font></span></p>I will judge - I will vindicate the poor. The fat cattle - The rich. The lean - The poor. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#023">Ezekiel 34:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">eze 34:23</a></font></span></p>One shepherd - Christ, the great good, chief, only shepherd, that laid down his life for his sheep. My servant David - The seed of David, the beloved one, who was typified by David, and is in other places called by his name, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer030.htm#009">Jer 30:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#024">Eze 37:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#035">Isa 37:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos003.htm#005">Hos 3:5</a>. He shall feed - Do all the office of a good and faithful shepherd, and that for ever. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#024">Ezekiel 34:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">eze 34:24</a></font></span></p>My servant - Christ was in this great work his fathers servant, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#001">Isa 42:1</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#025">Ezekiel 34:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">eze 34:25</a></font></span></p>A covenant - A covenant of promises, which contain, and shall bring peace, that is all good. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#026">Ezekiel 34:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">eze 34:26</a></font></span></p>Them - My returned captives, The places - All the country. My hill - Jerusalem. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#029">Ezekiel 34:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">eze 34:29</a></font></span></p>A plant - The Messiah. The shame - The reproach. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#030">Ezekiel 34:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">eze 34:30</a></font></span></p>Their God - By covenant, from their forefathers. Am with them - Present with them, and reconciled to them.
