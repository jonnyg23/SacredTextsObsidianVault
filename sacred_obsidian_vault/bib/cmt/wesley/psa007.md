 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#002">Psalms 7:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 7:2</a></font></span></p>Lest - Mine enemy. Tear - Out of my body. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#003">Psalms 7:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 7:3</a></font></span></p>Hands - Which Cush and others falsely lay to my charge. Iniquity - In my actions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#004">Psalms 7:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 7:4</a></font></span></p>Deliver - When it was in my power to destroy him, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1024.htm#002">Sa1 24:2-6</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#006">Psalms 7:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 7:6</a></font></span></p>Lift up - Glorify thyself, and shew thyself to be above them. Commanded - To execute that righteous sentence, which thou hast commanded, appointed, and declared by thy prophet Samuel. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#007">Psalms 7:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 7:7</a></font></span></p>Compass - They will come from all parts to worship thee, and offer to thee praises and sacrifices. High - To thy tribunal, to sit there and judge my cause. An allusion to earthly tribunals, which generally are set up on high above the people. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#009">Psalms 7:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 7:9</a></font></span></p>O - Put a stop to their wicked practices. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#011">Psalms 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 7:11</a></font></span></p>Every day - Even then, when his providence seems to favour them, and they are most secure and confident. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#012">Psalms 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 7:12</a></font></span></p>He will - God will hasten, and speedily execute his judgments upon him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#013">Psalms 7:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 7:13</a></font></span></p>Him - For the wicked. Ordaineth - Designs or fits for this very use. Of all sinners, persecutors are set up as the fairest marks of Divine wrath. They set God at defiance but cannot set themselves out of the reach of his judgments. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#014">Psalms 7:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 7:14</a></font></span></p>Travelleth - This metaphor denotes his deep design, and vigorous endeavours for doing mischief, and his restlessness and pain 'till he have accomplished it.
