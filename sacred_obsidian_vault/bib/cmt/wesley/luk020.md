 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#001">Luke 20:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">luk 20:1</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#023">Mat 21:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#027">Mar 11:27</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#009">Luke 20:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">luk 20:9</a></font></span></p>A long time - It was a long time from the entrance of the Israelites into Canaan to the birth of Christ. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#033">Mat 21:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#001">Mar 12:1</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#016">Luke 20:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">luk 20:16</a></font></span></p>He will destroy these husbandmen - Probably he pointed to the scribes, chief priests, and elders: who allowed, he will miserably destroy those wicked men, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#041">Mat 21:41</a>; but could not bear that this should be applied to themselves. They might also mean, God forbid that we should be guilty of such a crime as your parable seems to charge us with, namely, rejecting and killing the heir. Our Saviour answers, But yet will ye do it, as is prophesied of you. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#017">Luke 20:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">luk 20:17</a></font></span></p>He looked on them - To sharpen their attention. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa118.htm#022">Psa 118:22</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#018">Luke 20:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">luk 20:18</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#045">Mat 21:45</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#020">Luke 20:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">luk 20:20</a></font></span></p>Just men - Men of a tender conscience. To take hold of his discourse - If he answered as they hoped he would. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#016">Mat 22:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#012">Mar 12:12</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#021">Luke 20:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">luk 20:21</a></font></span></p>Thou speakest - In private, and teachest - In public. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#024">Luke 20:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">luk 20:24</a></font></span></p>Show me a penny - A Roman penny, which was the money that was usually paid on that occasion. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#026">Luke 20:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">luk 20:26</a></font></span></p>They could not take hold of his words before the people - As they did afterward before the sanhedrim, in the absence of the people, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#067">Luk 22:67</a>, &amp;c. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#027">Luke 20:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">luk 20:27</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#023">Mat 22:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar012.htm#018">Mar 12:18</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#028">Luke 20:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">luk 20:28</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu025.htm#005">Deu 25:5</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#034">Luke 20:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">luk 20:34</a></font></span></p>The children of this world - The inhabitants of earth, marry and are given in marriage - As being all subject to the law of mortality; so that the species is in need of being continually repaired. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#035">Luke 20:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">luk 20:35</a></font></span></p>But they who obtain that world - Which they enter into, before the resurrection of the dead. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#036">Luke 20:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">luk 20:36</a></font></span></p>They are the children of God - In a more eminent sense when they rise again. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#037">Luke 20:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">luk 20:37</a></font></span></p>That the dead are raised, even Moses, as well as the other prophets showed, when he calleth - That is, when he recites the words which God spoke of himself, I am the God of Abraham, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#032">Mat 22:32</a>. It cannot properly be said, that God is the God of any who are totally perished. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo003.htm#006">Exo 3:6</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#038">Luke 20:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">luk 20:38</a></font></span></p>He is not a God of the dead, or, there is no God of the dead - That is, tho term God implies such a relation, as cannot possibly subsist between him and the dead; who in the Sadducees' sense are extinguished spirits; who could neither worship him, nor receive good from him. So that all live to him - All who have him for their God, live to and enjoy him. This sentence is not an argument for what went before; but the proposition which was to be proved. And the consequence is apparently just. For as all the faithful are the children of Abraham, and the Divine promise of being a God to him and his seed is entailed upon them, it implies their continued existence and happiness in a future state as much as Abraham's. And as the body is an essential part of man, it implies both his resurrection and theirs; and so overthrows the entire scheme of the Sadducean doctrine. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#040">Luke 20:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">luk 20:40</a></font></span></p>They durst not ask him any question - The Sadducees durst not. One of the scribes did, presently after.
