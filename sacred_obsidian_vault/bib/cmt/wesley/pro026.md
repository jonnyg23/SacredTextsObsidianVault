 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 26</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#002">Proverbs 26:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 26:2</a></font></span></p>By flying - Secures itself from the fowler. Not come - Upon the innocent person, but he shall escape from it like a bird. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#004">Proverbs 26:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 26:4</a></font></span></p>According - So as to imitate his folly, by passionate or reproachful speeches. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#005">Proverbs 26:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 26:5</a></font></span></p>According - So as his folly needs and requires, convincing him strongly, reproving him sharply, and exposing him to just shame. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#006">Proverbs 26:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 26:6</a></font></span></p>Cutteth off the feet - Of his messenger; bids one go that wants legs. Drinketh - Drinking, in scripture, frequently denotes the plentiful doing or receiving of any thing. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#007">Proverbs 26:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 26:7</a></font></span></p>The legs - Heb. the legs of the lame are lifted up, in going, or in dancing, which is done with great inequality and uncomeliness. So - No less incident are wise and pious speeches from a foolish and ungodly man. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#008">Proverbs 26:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 26:8</a></font></span></p>Bindeth - Whereby he hinders his own design of throwing the stone out of it. So - No less absurd is he that giveth to a fool that honour which he is not capable of using aright. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#009">Proverbs 26:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 26:9</a></font></span></p>A thorn - As a thorn is in a drunkard's hand, which he cannot manage cautiously, but employs to his own and others hurt. So - As unprofitable, and, by accident, hurtful to himself and others. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#010">Proverbs 26:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 26:10</a></font></span></p>Rewardeth - Will certainly give that recompence which is deserved by fools and transgressors, by such as sin either thro' ignorance, or wilfully. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#014">Proverbs 26:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 26:14</a></font></span></p>Turneth - Moving hither and thither upon it, but not removing from its place. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#016">Proverbs 26:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 26:16</a></font></span></p>A reason - A satisfactory reason of ail their actions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#017">Proverbs 26:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 26:17</a></font></span></p>He - Who is going upon the way. With strife - In which he is not concerned, nor any way obliged to meddle. That taketh - Exposes himself to needless hazard. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#018">Proverbs 26:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 26:18</a></font></span></p>And death - Any instruments of death. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#023">Proverbs 26:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 26:23</a></font></span></p>Burning - With malice or hatred: A slanderous or evil tongue. Dross - Such a tongue and heart are of no real worth, although sometimes they make a shew of it, as dross does of silver. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#026">Proverbs 26:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 26:26</a></font></span></p>Deceit - With false professions of love. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#027">Proverbs 26:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 26:27</a></font></span></p>Rolleth - Up the hill with design to do mischief to some person.
