 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 137</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#001">Psalms 137:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 137:1</a></font></span></p>Sat - The usual posture of mourners. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#002">Psalms 137:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 137:2</a></font></span></p>Harps - Harps are here put for all instruments of musick. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#003">Psalms 137:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 137:3</a></font></span></p>A song - Such songs as you used to sing in the temple of Zion. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#004">Psalms 137:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 137:4</a></font></span></p>The Lord's - Those songs which were appointed by God to be sung only in his service. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#006">Psalms 137:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 137:6</a></font></span></p>If - If I do not value Jerusalem's prosperity more than all other delights. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#007">Psalms 137:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 137:7</a></font></span></p>The day - In the time of its destruction. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#008">Psalms 137:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 137:8</a></font></span></p>Happy - As being God's instrument to vindicate his honour, and execute his just judgments.
