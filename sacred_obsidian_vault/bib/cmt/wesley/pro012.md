 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 12</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#006">Proverbs 12:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 12:6</a></font></span></p>Lie in wait - Are designed to entrap others, and to destroy them. Deliver them - From those that lie in wait for them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#007">Proverbs 12:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 12:7</a></font></span></p>Are not - Both they and their families suddenly perish. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#009">Proverbs 12:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 12:9</a></font></span></p>Despised - That lives in a mean condition. Honoureth - That glories in his high birth or gay attire. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#010">Proverbs 12:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 12:10</a></font></span></p>Regardeth - He will not destroy it either by labour beyond its strength, or by denying it necessary food or rest. Cruel - There is cruelty mixed even with their most merciful actions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#011">Proverbs 12:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 12:11</a></font></span></p>Tilleth - That employs his time in an honest calling. Vain persons - In an idle course of living. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#012">Proverbs 12:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 12:12</a></font></span></p>Desireth - He approves those arts, which wicked men use like nets to ensnare other men. The root - That piety, which is the root of his actions, yields him sufficient fruit both for his own need, and to do good to others. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#014">Proverbs 12:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 12:14</a></font></span></p>Mouth - By his pious and profitable discourses. Hands - Of his actions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#015">Proverbs 12:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 12:15</a></font></span></p>Hearkeneth - That distrusts his own judgments, and seeks counsel from others. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#016">Proverbs 12:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 12:16</a></font></span></p>Covereth - The shame, or injury done to him, which he conceals and bears with patience. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#017">Proverbs 12:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 12:17</a></font></span></p>Deceit - He who uses himself to lying in his common talk, will use falsehood and deceit in judgment. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#018">Proverbs 12:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 12:18</a></font></span></p>Health - Tends to the comfort and benefit of others. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#019">Proverbs 12:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 12:19</a></font></span></p>A moment - Liars, though they may make a fair shew for a season, yet are quickly convicted. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#020">Proverbs 12:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 12:20</a></font></span></p>Deceit - They whose hearts devise mischief shall be deceived in their hopes, and bring trouble upon themselves: but they who by good counsels labour to promote peace, shall reap the comfort of it themselves. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#023">Proverbs 12:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 12:23</a></font></span></p>Concealeth - He does not unseasonably utter what he knows. Foolishness - Betrays his ignorance and folly. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#025">Proverbs 12:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 12:25</a></font></span></p>A good word - A compassionate or encouraging word. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#026">Proverbs 12:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 12:26</a></font></span></p>Neighbour - Than any other men. Seduceth - Heb. maketh them to err, to lose that excellency or happiness which they had promised themselves. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#027">Proverbs 12:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 12:27</a></font></span></p>Resteth not - Does not enjoy the fruit of his labours. Precious - Yields him comfort and blessing with it.
