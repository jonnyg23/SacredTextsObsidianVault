 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#001">Luke Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#002">Luke 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">luk 8:2</a></font></span></p>Mary Magdalene - Or Mary of Magdala, a town in Galilee: probably the person mentioned in the last chapter. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#004">Luke 8:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">luk 8:4</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#001">Mat 13:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#001">Mar 4:1</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#015">Luke 8:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">luk 8:15</a></font></span></p>Who - keep it - Not like the highway side: And bring forth fruit - Not like the thorny ground: With perseverance - Not like the stony. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#016">Luke 8:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">luk 8:16</a></font></span></p>No man having lighted a candle - As if ho had said, And let your good fruit appear openly. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#015">Mat 5:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#021">Mar 4:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#033">Luk 11:33</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#017">Luke 8:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">luk 8:17</a></font></span></p>For nothing is hid - Strive not to conceal it at all; for you can conceal nothing long. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#026">Mat 10:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#022">Mar 4:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#002">Luk 12:2</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#018">Luke 8:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">luk 8:18</a></font></span></p>The word commonly translated seemeth, wherever it occurs, does not weaken, but greatly strengthens the sense. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#012">Mat 13:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#025">Mar 4:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#026">Luk 19:26</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#019">Luke 8:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">luk 8:19</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#046">Mat 12:46</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#031">Mar 3:31</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#022">Luke 8:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">luk 8:22</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#023">Mat 8:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#035">Mar 4:35</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#026">Luke 8:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">luk 8:26</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#028">Mat 8:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#001">Mar 5:1</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#029">Luke 8:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">luk 8:29</a></font></span></p>For many times it had caught him - Therefore our compassionate Lord made the more haste to cast him out. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#031">Luke 8:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">luk 8:31</a></font></span></p>The abyss - That is, the bottomless pit. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#032">Luke 8:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">luk 8:32</a></font></span></p>To enter into the swine - Not that they were any easier in the swine than out of them. Had it been so, they would not so soon have dislodged themselves, by destroying the herd. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#037">Luke 8:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">luk 8:37</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#001">Mat 9:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#018">Mar 5:18</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#040">Luke 8:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">luk 8:40</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar005.htm#021">Mar 5:21</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#052">Luke 8:52</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_052">luk 8:52</a></font></span></p>She is not dead but sleepeth - Her soul is not separated finally from the body; and this short separation is rather to be called sleep than death.
