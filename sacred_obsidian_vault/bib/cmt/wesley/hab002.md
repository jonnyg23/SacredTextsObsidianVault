 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#001">Habakkuk Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#001">Habakkuk 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">hab 2:1</a></font></span></p>Upon my watch - I will stand as a watchman on my watch - tower. He - The Lord. Reproved - Called to give an account of the mysteriousness of providence; either to satisfy doubters, or to silence quarrellers. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#002">Habakkuk 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">hab 2:2</a></font></span></p>Upon tables - What was of publick concern, and therefore to be published, was anciently written or engraven upon tables, smooth stones, or wood, and then hung up in a publick place to be read. May run - That none may need to stop, but every one may plainly and clearly discern what is written. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#003">Habakkuk 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">hab 2:3</a></font></span></p>At the end - When the period appointed of God shall come. Shall speak - Be accomplished, and not disappoint your expectation. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#004">Habakkuk 2:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">hab 2:4</a></font></span></p>Which is lifted up - That proudly contests with the justice and wisdom of the Divine Providence, and provides for his own safety by his own wit. The just - The humble and upright one, who adores the depth of divine providence, and is persuaded of the truth of divine promises. Shall live - Supports himself, by a firm expectation of the deliverance of Zion. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#005">Habakkuk 2:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">hab 2:5</a></font></span></p>He - The king of Babylon. Wine - Hereby Belshazzar, his city and kingdom of Babylon fell a prey to Darius and Cyrus. At home - Is ever abroad warring upon some or other. Unto him - To his kingdom. All nations - That are round about him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#008">Habakkuk 2:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">hab 2:8</a></font></span></p>Of the land - Of the whole land of Chaldea. The city - Babylon. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#009">Habakkuk 2:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">hab 2:9</a></font></span></p>To his house - His family which he would enrich, and raise high. Delivered - Kept secure and out of danger from all below him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#010">Habakkuk 2:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">hab 2:10</a></font></span></p>Thou - Nebuchadnezzar. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#011">Habakkuk 2:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">hab 2:11</a></font></span></p>Shall cry out - As if it had a voice, it cries to God for vengeance. Answer it - Confirm the charge against thee. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#013">Habakkuk 2:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">hab 2:13</a></font></span></p>Is it not of the Lord - Is it not a judgment from God? Shall labour - That men go thro' the most painful labour. For very vanity - For nothing; without any reward of their labour. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#016">Habakkuk 2:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">hab 2:16</a></font></span></p>Thou - O king of Babylon. Shall be turned - They turned the cup of pleasure about, God will carry the cup of indignation about also, and make them drink deep of it. Shameful spewing - Thou shalt be as much loathed as a shameful drunkard is in his vomit. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#017">Habakkuk 2:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">hab 2:17</a></font></span></p>The violence - The violence thou hast done to Judea shall overwhelm thee. The spoil of brass - Such spoil as by hunters is made among wild beasts, when they endeavour to destroy the whole kind. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#020">Habakkuk 2:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">hab 2:20</a></font></span></p>The Lord - He is Jehovah, the fountain of being, life, power, and salvation to his people. Keep silence - Fear, submit, and depend on him; let his enemies be silent, reverence, hope, pray and wait for him, who will arise and have mercy on them, who will make it to be well with the righteous, and ill with the wicked, who will fully and satisfactorily solve the doubts, and unfold the riddles of his providence.
