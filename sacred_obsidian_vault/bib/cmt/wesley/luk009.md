 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#001">Luke Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#001">Luke 9:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">luk 9:1</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#001">Mat 10:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#007">Mar 6:7</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#004">Luke 9:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">luk 9:4</a></font></span></p>There abide and thence depart - That is, stay in that house till ye leave the city. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#007">Luke 9:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">luk 9:7</a></font></span></p>It was said by some - And soon after by Herod himself. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#001">Mat 14:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#014">Mar 6:14</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#008">Luke 9:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">luk 9:8</a></font></span></p>That Elijah had appeared - He could not rise again, because he did not die. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#010">Luke 9:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">luk 9:10</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#030">Mar 6:30</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#012">Luke 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">luk 9:12</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#015">Mat 14:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar006.htm#035">Mar 6:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#003">Joh 6:3</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#018">Luke 9:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">luk 9:18</a></font></span></p>Apart - From the multitude. And he asked them - When he had done praying, during which they probably stayed at a distance. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#013">Mat 14:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#027">Mar 8:27</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#022">Luke 9:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">luk 9:22</a></font></span></p>Saying - Ye must prepare for a scene far different from this. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#023">Luke 9:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">luk 9:23</a></font></span></p>Let him deny himself, and take up his cross - The necessity of this duty has been shown in many places: the extent of it is specified here, daily - Therefore that day is lost wherein no cross is taken up. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#024">Luke 9:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">luk 9:24</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#025">Mat 16:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar008.htm#035">Mar 8:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#025">Joh 12:25</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#028">Luke 9:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">luk 9:28</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#001">Mat 17:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#002">Mar 9:2</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#031">Luke 9:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">luk 9:31</a></font></span></p>In glory - Like Christ with whom they talked. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#032">Luke 9:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">luk 9:32</a></font></span></p>They saw his glory - The very same expression in which it is described by St. John, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14</a>; and by St. Peter, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#016">Pe2 1:16</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#034">Luke 9:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">luk 9:34</a></font></span></p>A cloud came and overshadowed them all. And they, the apostles, feared, while they (Moses and Elijah) entered into the cloud, which took them away. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#037">Luke 9:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">luk 9:37</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#014">Mat 17:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#014">Mar 9:14</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#044">Luke 9:44</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_044">luk 9:44</a></font></span></p>Let these sayings sink down into your ears - That is, consider them deeply. In joy remember the cross. So wisely does our Lord balance praise with sufferings. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#022">Mat 17:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#031">Mar 9:31</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#046">Luke 9:46</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_046">luk 9:46</a></font></span></p>And there arose a reasoning among them - This kind of reasoning always arose at the most improper times that could be imagined. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#047">Luke 9:47</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_047">luk 9:47</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#002">Mat 18:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#037">Mar 9:37</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#048">Luke 9:48</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_048">luk 9:48</a></font></span></p>And said to them - If ye would be truly great, humble yourselves to the meanest offices. He that is least in his own eyes shall be great indeed. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#049">Luke 9:49</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_049">luk 9:49</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#038">Mar 9:38</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#051">Luke 9:51</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_051">luk 9:51</a></font></span></p>The days are fulfilled that he should be received up - That is, the time of his passion was now at hand. St. Luke looks through this, to the glory which was to follow. He steadfastly set his face - Without fear of his enemies, or shame of the cross, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#002">Heb 12:2</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#052">Luke 9:52</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_052">luk 9:52</a></font></span></p>He sent messengers to make ready - A lodging and needful entertainment for him and those with him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#053">Luke 9:53</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_053">luk 9:53</a></font></span></p>His face was as though he would go to Jerusalem - It plainly appeared, he was going to worship at the temple, and thereby, in effect, to condemn the Samaritan worship at Mount Gerizim. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#054">Luke 9:54</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_054">luk 9:54</a></font></span></p>As Elisha did - At or near this very place, which might put it into the minds of the apostles to make the motion now, rather than at any other time or place, where Christ had received the like affront. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#055">Luke 9:55</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_055">luk 9:55</a></font></span></p>Ye know not what manner of spirit - The spirit of Christianity is. It is not a spirit of wrath and vengeance, but of peace, and gentleness, and love. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#057">Luke 9:57</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_057">luk 9:57</a></font></span></p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#019">Mat 8:19</a>. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#058">Luke 9:58</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_058">luk 9:58</a></font></span></p>But Jesus said to him - First understand the terms: consider on what conditions thou art to follow me. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#061">Luke 9:61</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_061">luk 9:61</a></font></span></p>Suffer me first to bid them farewell that are in my house - As Elisha did after Elijah had called him from the plough, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1019.htm#019">Kg1 19:19</a>; to which our Lord's answer seems to allude. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#062">Luke 9:62</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_062">luk 9:62</a></font></span></p>Is fit for the kingdom of God - Either to propagate or to receive it.
