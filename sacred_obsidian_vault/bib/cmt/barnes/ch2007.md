 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#001">2 Chronicles Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#001">2 Chronicles 7:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch2 7:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The fire came down from heaven - As in the time of Moses on the dedication of the tabernacle <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev009.htm#024">Lev 9:24</a> The fact is omitted from the narrative of Kings; but omission is not contradiction. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#008">2 Chronicles 7:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">ch2 7:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Solomon kept the feast - i. e., Solomon kept at this same time, not the Feast of the Dedication only, but also the Feast of tabernacles. The former lasted seven days, from the 8th of Tisri to the 15th, the latter also seven days, from the 15th to the 22nd. On the day following the people were dismissed <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#010">Ch2 7:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#011">2 Chronicles 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">ch2 7:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The narrative now runs parallel with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1009.htm#001">Kg1 9:1-9</a>, but is more full, and presents less of verbal agreement. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#013">Ch2 7:13-15</a> are additional to the earlier record. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#012">2 Chronicles 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">ch2 7:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>An house of sacrifice - This expression does not elsewhere occur. Its meaning, however, is clear. God declares that Solomon's Temple is the place whereunto all Israelites were commanded to bring their burnt-offerings and sacrifices (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#005">Deu 12:5-6</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#015">2 Chronicles 7:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">ch2 7:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The prayer that is made in this place - literally, as in the margin. The unsual phrase includes the two cases of prayers offered in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#024">Ch2 6:24</a> and toward <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#034">Ch2 6:34</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#038">Ch2 6:38</a> the sanctuary.
