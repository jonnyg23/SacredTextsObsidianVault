 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2018.htm#001">2 Chronicles Chapter 18</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2018.htm#001">2 Chronicles 18:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch2 18:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The present chapter runs parallel with Kings, which it closely follows, only adding a few touches. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2018.htm#002">2 Chronicles 18:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ch2 18:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>After certain years - In Jehoshaphat's 17th year <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1022.htm#051">Kg1 22:51</a>, not less than 8 years after the marriage (marginal reference note). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2018.htm#031">2 Chronicles 18:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">ch2 18:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>And the Lord helped him ... - There is nothing correspondent to this passage in Kings. It is a pious reflection on the part of the author, who traces all deliverance to its real divine source.
