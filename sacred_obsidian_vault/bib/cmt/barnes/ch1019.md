 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 19</a></h3> 
 <p>Compare the marginal references and notes. The writer here adds one or two touches, and varies in one or two of the numbers. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#002">1 Chronicles 19:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ch1 19:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Hanun - A Philistine king of this name is mentioned in the Assyrian inscriptions as paying tribute to Tiglath-pileser and warring with Sargon. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#006">1 Chronicles 19:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ch1 19:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A thousand talents of silver - The price is not given in Samuel. On the practice of hiring troops about this time in western Asia, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#018">Kg1 15:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2007.htm#006">Kg2 7:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2025.htm#006">Ch2 25:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#007">1 Chronicles 19:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">ch1 19:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>They hired thirty and two thousand chariots - The reading is corrupt. Such a number as 32,000 chariots alone was never brought into battle on any occasion. Compare the numbers in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#007">Exo 14:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1010.htm#026">Kg1 10:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2012.htm#003">Ch2 12:3</a>. The largest force which an Assyrian king ever speaks of encountering is 3,940. The words "and horsemen" have probably fallen out of the text after the word "chariots" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#006">Ch1 19:6</a>). The 32,000 would be the number of the warriors serving on horseback or in chariots; and this number would agree closely with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#006">Sa2 10:6</a>, as the following table shows:
 </p><p>
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#006">Sa2 10:6</a> Men  Syrians of Beth-rehob and Zobah 20,000  Syrians of Ish-tob 12,000  Syrians of Maachah 1,000  Total 33,000  <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#007">Ch1 19:7</a> Men  Syrians of Zobah, etc. 32,000  Syrians of Machah ( number not given) 1,000  Total 33,000
