 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#001">1 Chronicles 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 14:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#011">Sa2 5:11-25</a>, the only important variations from which are in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#004">Ch1 14:4-7</a>, the list of the sons of David (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1003.htm#001">Ch1 3:1</a> note), and in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#012">Ch1 14:12</a>, where the fact is added that the idols taken from the Philistines were burned. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#012">1 Chronicles 14:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">ch1 14:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>When they had left their gods there - The practice of carrying images of the gods to battle was common among the nations of antiquity, and arose from the belief that there was virtue in the images themselves, and that military success would be obtained by means of them.
