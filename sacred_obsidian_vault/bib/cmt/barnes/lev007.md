 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#001">Leviticus 7:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lev 7:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev005.htm#014">Lev 5:14</a> note. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#002">Lev 7:2</a> "sprinkle" should rather be cast <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#005">Lev 1:5</a>. All the details regarding the parts put on the altar are repeated for each kind of sacrifice, because the matter was one of paramount importance. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#008">Leviticus 7:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">lev 7:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The skin of the burnt offering - It is most likely that the skins of the sin-offering and the trespass-offering also fell to the lot of the officiating priest. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#009">Leviticus 7:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">lev 7:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>See the marginal references. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#011">Leviticus 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">lev 7:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>See Lev. 3:1-17. What is here added, relates to the accompanying מנחה  mı̂nchāh <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#001">Lev 2:1</a>, the classification of peace-offerings into:
 </p><p>(1) thank-offerings,
 </p><p>(2) vow-offerings and
 </p><p>(3) voluntary-offerings, and the conditions to be observed by the worshipper in eating the flesh. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#012">Leviticus 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">lev 7:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For a thanksgiving - i. e., a thank-offering for mercies received. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#013">Leviticus 7:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">lev 7:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For his offering - The leavened bread was a distinct offering. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#014">Leviticus 7:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">lev 7:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Out of the whole oblation - Rather, out of each offering. That is, one loaf or cake out of each kind of meat-offering was to be a heave-offering <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#032">Lev 7:32</a> for the officiating priest. According to Jewish tradition, there were to be ten cakes of each kind of bread in every thank-offering. The other cakes were returned to the sacrificer. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#016">Leviticus 7:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">lev 7:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The vow-offering appears to have been a peace-offering vowed upon a certain condition; the voluntary-offering, one offered as the simple tribute of a devout heart rejoicing in peace with God and man offered on no external occasion (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev022.htm#017">Lev 22:17-25</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#018">Leviticus 7:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">lev 7:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>It was proper that the sacrificial meat should not be polluted by any approach to putrefaction. But the exclusion of a mean-spirited economy may further have furnished the ground for the distinction between the thank-offerings and the others. The most liberal distribution of the meat of the offering, particularly among the poor who were invited to partake, would plainly be becoming when the sacrifice was intended especially to express gratitude for mercies received. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#021">Leviticus 7:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">lev 7:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Unclean beast - that is, carrion of any kind. See Lev. 11.
 </p><p>Shall be cut off - See the <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo031.htm#014">Exo 31:14</a> note. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#023">Leviticus 7:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">lev 7:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This is emphatically addressed to the people. They were not to eat in their own meal what belonged to the altar of Yahweh, nor what was the perquisite of the priests. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#033">Lev 7:33-36</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#024">Leviticus 7:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">lev 7:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev011.htm#039">Lev 11:39</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#026">Leviticus 7:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">lev 7:26</a></font></span></p>
 <p>No manner of blood - See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev017.htm#010">Lev 17:10-15</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#030">Leviticus 7:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">lev 7:30</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Wave-offering ... heave-offering - The latter appears to be used (like קרבן  qorbân, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#002">Lev 1:2</a>) for offerings in general. Waving (a moving to and fro, repeated several times) or heaving (a lifting up once) the offering was a solemn form of dedicating a thing to the use of the sanctuary. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#035">Leviticus 7:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">lev 7:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The portion of the anointing ... - Rather, the appointed share of Aaron and of his sons. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#037">Leviticus 7:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">lev 7:37</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Of the consecrations - That is, of the sacrifices which were to be offered in the consecration of the priests. See the marginal references. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#038">Leviticus 7:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">lev 7:38</a></font></span></p>
 <p>wilderness of Sinai - Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#001">Exo 19:1</a>.
