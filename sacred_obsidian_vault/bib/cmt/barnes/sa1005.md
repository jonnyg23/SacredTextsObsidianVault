 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1005.htm#001">1 Kings (1 Samuel) Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1005.htm#002">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 5:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">sa1 5:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>They brought it into the house of Dagon (see the marginal reference) in order to enhance the triumph of the gods of the Philistines over the God of Israel. (Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#009">Sa1 31:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg016.htm#023">Jdg 16:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#012">Isa 37:12</a>.) 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1005.htm#005">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sa1 5:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This custom still existed among the worshippers of Dagon so late as the reign of Josiah (see the marginal reference). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1005.htm#006">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 5:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">sa1 5:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Emerods - A corruption of "hemorrhoids." It is mentioned <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#027">Deu 28:27</a> among the diseases with which God threatened to punish the Israelites for disobedience. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1005.htm#008">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 5:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">sa1 5:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The "lords" (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg003.htm#003">Jdg 3:3</a>) were very unwilling to give up their triumph, and, with the common pagan superstition, imagined that some local bad luck was against them at Ashdod. The result was to bring the whole Philistine community under the same calamity.
