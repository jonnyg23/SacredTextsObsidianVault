 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 17</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001">1 Chronicles 17:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 17:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Compare throughout 2 Sam. 7 and the notes found there. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#013">1 Chronicles 17:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">ch1 17:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>My son - The minatory clause which occurs after this in Samuel is here omitted, because the writer is not about to record the sins of Solomon, or the sufferings 1 Kings 11:9-40 which he thereby brought upon himself. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#017">1 Chronicles 17:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ch1 17:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Hast regarded me ... - i. e., "Thou hast elevated me above other men, by making my kingdom perpetual, regarding me as if I were a man of high degree." Compare the <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#019">Sa2 7:19</a> note. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#018">1 Chronicles 17:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">ch1 17:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For the honor of thy servant - i. e., "for the honor which Thou hast done for Thy servant." The Septuagint omits "Thy servant," and renders it: "What can David say more to Thee to glorify Thee? For Thou knowest," etc. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#024">1 Chronicles 17:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">ch1 17:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Some prefer, "And let Thy name be established and magnified forever:" i. e., "Let not only Thy promise stand firm, but let Thy Name also stand firm (continue to be held in honor) and be magnified," etc. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#027">1 Chronicles 17:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">ch1 17:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The marginal rendering is preferable.
