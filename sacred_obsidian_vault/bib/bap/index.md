 <h1 align="CENTER">The Biblical Antiquities of Philo</h1>
 <h2 align="CENTER">translated by M. R. James</h2>
 <h4 align="CENTER">[1917]</h4>
 <hr>
 <center>
 <a href="#contents">Contents</a>   
 <a href="bap00.htm">Start Reading</a>
 </center>
 <hr>
 <p>This book, dating from the late first century C.E. 
 (after the destruction of the second temple, 70 C.E.)
 is attributed to the Jewish writer Philo.
 However, most scholars agree that it was not written by Philo,
 and it is hence sometimes called 'Pseuophilo'.
 Consisting of a retelling of the Hebrew Bible from Genesis to the end
 of 1 Samuel, <i>Biblical Antiquities</i> embellishes and often departs 
 from the narrative as we know it today in small and large details.
 A little early to be called Midrash, <i>Biblical Antiquities</i> is one
 of the 'pseudepigrapha', writings on traditional Biblical
 subjects which were never canonized.</p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><font size="-1">--John Bruno Hare, May 13th, 2004</font></p>
