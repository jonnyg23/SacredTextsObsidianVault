 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Life and Morals of Jesus of Nazareth (the Jefferson Bible)</i>, by Thomas Jefferson, [1902], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">X.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small><i>Call of His Disciples</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>AND it came to pass in those days, that he went out into a mountain to pray, and continued all night in prayer to God.</p>  
 <p>2 And when it was day, he called unto him his disciples: and of them he chose twelve, whom also he named apostles;</p>  
 <p>3 Simon, (whom he also named Peter,) and Andrew his brother, James and John, Philip and Bartholomew,</p>  
 <p>4 Matthew and Thomas, James the <i>son</i> of Alphaeus, and Simon called Zelotes,</p>  
 <p>5 And Judas <i>the brother</i> of James, and Judas Iscariot, which also was the traitor.</p>  
 <p>6 And he came down with them, and stood in the plain, and the company of his disciples, and a great multitude of people out of all Judaea and</p>  
 <p><a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="green">p. 30</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Jerusalem, and from the sea coast of Tyre and Sidon, which came to hear him, and to be healed of their diseases.</p>
