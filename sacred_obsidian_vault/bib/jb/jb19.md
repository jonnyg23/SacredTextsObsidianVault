 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Life and Morals of Jesus of Nazareth (the Jefferson Bible)</i>, by Thomas Jefferson, [1902], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small><i>Parable of the Rich Man</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>AND he spake a parable unto them, saying, The ground of a certain rich man brought forth plentifully:</p>  
 <p>2 And he thought within himself, saying, What shall I do, because I have no room where to bestow my fruits?</p>  
 <p>3 And he said, This will I do: I will pull down my barns, and build greater; and there will I bestow all my fruits and my goods.</p>  
 <p>4 And I will say to my soul, Soul, thou hast much goods laid up for many years; take thine ease, eat, drink, and be merry.</p>  
 <p>5 But God said unto him, <i>Thou</i> fool, this night thy soul shall be required of thee: then whose shall those things be, which thou hast provided?</p>  
 <p>6 So is he that layeth up treasure for himself, and is not rich toward God.</p>
