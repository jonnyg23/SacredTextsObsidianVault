 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Life and Morals of Jesus of Nazareth (the Jefferson Bible)</i>, by Thomas Jefferson, [1902], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small><i>Parable of New Wine in Old Bottles</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>AND he spake also a parable unto them; No man putteth a piece of a new garment upon an old; if otherwise, then both the new maketh a rent, and the piece that was <i>taken</i> out of the new agreeth not with the old.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="green">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p>2 And no man putteth new wine into old bottles; else the new wine will burst the bottles, and be spilled and the bottles shall perish.</p>  
 <p>3 But new wine must be put into new bottles; and both are preserved.</p>  
 <p>4 No man also having drunk old <i>wine</i> straightway desireth new: for he saith, The old is better.</p>
