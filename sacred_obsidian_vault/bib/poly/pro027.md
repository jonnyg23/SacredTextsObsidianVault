 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro027.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Boast not thyself<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1984.htm">1984</a></sup> of tomorrow;<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4279.htm">4279</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> thou knowest<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> what<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> a day<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> may bring forth.<sup><a href="h3205.htm">3205</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  Let another man<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> praise<sup><a href="h1984.htm">1984</a></sup> thee, and not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> thine own mouth;<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> a stranger,<sup><a href="h5237.htm">5237</a></sup> and not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> thine own lips.<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> </p>

 <p>4  Wrath<sup><a href="h2534.htm">2534</a></sup> is cruel,<sup><a href="h0395.htm">395</a></sup> and anger<sup><a href="h0639.htm">639</a></sup> is outrageous;<sup><a href="h7858.htm">7858</a></sup> but who<sup><a href="h4310.htm">4310</a></sup> is able to stand<sup><a href="h5975.htm">5975</a></sup> before<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> envy?<sup><a href="h7068.htm">7068</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  Open<sup><a href="h1540.htm">1540</a></sup> rebuke<sup><a href="h8433.htm">8433</a></sup> is better<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> than secret<sup><a href="h5641.htm">5641</a></sup> love.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0160.htm">160</a></sup> </p>

 <p>7  The full<sup><a href="h7649.htm">7649</a></sup> soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> loatheth<sup><a href="h0947.htm">947</a></sup> a honeycomb;<sup><a href="h5317.htm">5317</a></sup> but to the hungry<sup><a href="h7457.htm">7457</a></sup> soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> every<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> bitter thing<sup><a href="h4751.htm">4751</a></sup> is sweet.<sup><a href="h4966.htm">4966</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  As a bird<sup><a href="h6833.htm">6833</a></sup> that wandereth<sup><a href="h5074.htm">5074</a></sup> from<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> her nest,<sup><a href="h7064.htm">7064</a></sup> so<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> is a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> that wandereth<sup><a href="h5074.htm">5074</a></sup> from his place.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4725.htm">4725</a></sup> </p>

 <p>11  My son,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> be wise,<sup><a href="h2449.htm">2449</a></sup> and make my heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> glad,<sup><a href="h8055.htm">8055</a></sup> that I may answer<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> him that reproacheth<sup><a href="h2778.htm">2778</a></sup> me. </p>
 <p>12  A prudent<sup><a href="h6175.htm">6175</a></sup> man foreseeth<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> the evil,<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> and hideth himself;<sup><a href="h5641.htm">5641</a></sup> but the simple<sup><a href="h6612.htm">6612</a></sup> pass on,<sup><a href="h5674.htm">5674</a></sup> and are punished.<sup><a href="h6064.htm">6064</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  Take<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> his garment<sup><a href="h0899.htm">899</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> is surety<sup><a href="h6148.htm">6148</a></sup> for a stranger,<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> and take a pledge<sup><a href="h2254.htm">2254</a></sup> of him for<sup><a href="h1157.htm">1157</a></sup> a strange woman.<sup><a href="h5237.htm">5237</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  He that blesseth<sup><a href="h1288.htm">1288</a></sup> his friend<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> with a loud<sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> voice,<sup><a href="h6963.htm">6963</a></sup> rising early<sup><a href="h7925.htm">7925</a></sup> in the morning,<sup><a href="h1242.htm">1242</a></sup> it shall be counted<sup><a href="h2803.htm">2803</a></sup> a curse<sup><a href="h7045.htm">7045</a></sup> to him. </p>
 <p>15  A continual<sup><a href="h2956.htm">2956</a></sup> dropping<sup><a href="h1812.htm">1812</a></sup> in a very rainy<sup><a href="h5464.htm">5464</a></sup> day<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> and a contentious<sup><a href="h4079.htm">4079</a></sup> woman<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> are alike.<sup><a href="h7737.htm">7737</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  Whosoever hideth<sup><a href="h6845.htm">6845</a></sup> her hideth<sup><a href="h6845.htm">6845</a></sup> the wind,<sup><a href="h7307.htm">7307</a></sup> and the ointment<sup><a href="h8081.htm">8081</a></sup> of his right hand,<sup><a href="h3225.htm">3225</a></sup> which betrayeth<sup><a href="h7121.htm">7121</a></sup> itself. </p>
 <p>17  Iron<sup><a href="h1270.htm">1270</a></sup> sharpeneth<sup><a href="h2300.htm">2300</a></sup> iron;<sup><a href="h1270.htm">1270</a></sup> so a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> sharpeneth<sup><a href="h2300.htm">2300</a></sup> the countenance<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> of his friend.<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  Whoso keepeth<sup><a href="h5341.htm">5341</a></sup> the fig tree<sup><a href="h8384.htm">8384</a></sup> shall eat<sup><a href="h0398.htm">398</a></sup> the fruit<sup><a href="h6529.htm">6529</a></sup> thereof: so he that waiteth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> on his master<sup><a href="h0113.htm">113</a></sup> shall be honored.<sup><a href="h3513.htm">3513</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  As in water<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> face<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> answereth to face,<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> so<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> the heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> to man.<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  Hell<sup><a href="h7585.htm">7585</a></sup> and destruction<sup><a href="h0010.htm">10</a></sup> are never<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> full;<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> so the eyes<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> are never<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> satisfied.<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> </p>

 <p>24  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> riches<sup><a href="h2633.htm">2633</a></sup> are not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> forever:<sup><a href="h5769.htm">5769</a></sup> and doth<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> the crown<sup><a href="h5145.htm">5145</a></sup> endure to every generation?<sup><a href="h1755.htm">1755</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1755.htm">1755</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro027.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Ne glorieris in crastinum,<br> ignorans quid superventura pariat dies.<br></p>
 <p>2 Laudet te alienus, et non os tuum;<br> extraneus, et non labia tua.<br></p>
 <p>3 Grave est saxum, et onerosa arena,<br> sed ira stulti utroque gravior.<br></p>
 <p>4 Ira non habet misericordiam nec erumpens furor,<br> et impetum concitati ferre quis poterit?<br></p>
 <p>5 Melior est manifesta correptio<br> quam amor absconditus.<br></p>
 <p>6 Meliora sunt vulnera diligentis<br> quam fraudulenta oscula odientis.<br></p>
 <p>7 Anima saturata calcabit favum,<br> et anima esuriens etiam amarum pro dulci sumet.<br></p>
 <p>8 Sicut avis transmigrans de nido suo,<br> sic vir qui derelinquit locum suum.<br></p>
 <p>9 Unguento et variis odoribus delectatur cor,<br> et bonis amici consiliis anima dulcoratur.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>10 [Amicum tuum et amicum patris tui ne dimiseris,<br> et domum fratris tui ne ingrediaris in die afflictionis tuæ.<br> Melior est vicinus juxta<br> quam frater procul.<br></p>
 <p>11 Stude sapientiæ, fili mi, et lætifica cor meum,<br> ut possis exprobranti respondere sermonem.<br></p>
 <p>12 Astutus videns malum, absconditus est:<br> parvuli transeuntes sustinuerunt dispendia.<br></p>
 <p>13 Tolle vestimentum ejus qui spopondit pro extraneo,<br> et pro alienis aufer ei pignus.<br></p>
 <p>14 Qui benedicit proximo suo voce grandi,<br> de nocte consurgens maledicenti similis erit.<br></p>
 <p>15 Tecta perstillantia in die frigoris<br> et litigiosa mulier comparantur.<br></p>
 <p>16 Qui retinet eam quasi qui ventum teneat,<br> et oleum dexteræ suæ vocabit.<br></p>
 <p>17 Ferrum ferro exacuitur,<br> et homo exacuit faciem amici sui.<br></p>
 <p>18 Qui servat ficum comedet fructus ejus,<br> et qui custos est domini sui glorificabitur.<br></p>
 <p>19 Quomodo in aquis resplendent vultus prospicientium,<br> sic corda hominum manifesta sunt prudentibus.<br></p>
 <p>20 Infernus et perditio numquam implentur:<br> similiter et oculi hominum insatiabiles.<br></p>
 <p>21 Quomodo probatur in conflatorio argentum et in fornace aurum,<br> sic probatur homo ore laudantis.<br> Cor iniqui inquirit mala,<br> cor autem rectum inquirit scientiam.<br></p>
 <p>22 Si contuderis stultum in pila<br> quasi ptisanas feriente desuper pilo,<br> non auferetur ab eo stultitia ejus.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>23 [Diligenter agnosce vultum pecoris tui,<br> tuosque greges considera:<br></p>
 <p>24 non enim habebis jugiter potestatem,<br> sed corona tribuetur in generationem et generationem.<br></p>
 <p>25 Aperta sunt prata, et apparuerunt herbæ virentes,<br> et collecta sunt fna de montibus.<br></p>
 <p>26 Agni ad vestimentum tuum,<br> et hædi ad agri pretium.<br></p>
 <p>27 Sufficiat tibi lac caprarum in cibos tuos,<br> et in necessaria domus tuæ, et ad victum ancillis tuis.]</p>
