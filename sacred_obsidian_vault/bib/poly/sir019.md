 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 19</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir019.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 A labouring man that A is given to drunkenness shall not be rich: and he that contemneth small things shall fall by little and little. </p>
 <p>2 Wine and women will make men of understanding to fall away: and he that cleaveth to harlots will become impudent. </p>
 <p>3 Moths and worms shall have him to heritage, and a bold man shall be taken away. </p>
 <p>4 He that is hasty to give credit is lightminded; and he that sinneth shall offend against his own soul. </p>
 <p>5 Whoso taketh pleasure in wickedness shall be condemned: but he that resisteth pleasures crowneth his life. </p>
 <p>6 He that can rule his tongue shall live without strife; and he that hateth babbling shall have less evil. </p>
 <p>7 Rehearse not unto another that which is told unto thee, and thou shalt fare never the worse. </p>
 <p>8 Whether it be to friend or foe, talk not of other men's lives; and if thou canst without offence, reveal them not. </p>
 <p>9 For he heard and observed thee, and when time cometh he will hate thee. </p>
 <p>10 If thou hast heard a word, let it die with thee; and be bold, it will not burst thee. </p>
 <p>11 A fool travaileth with a word, as a woman in labour of a child. </p>
 <p>12 As an arrow that sticketh in a man's thigh, so is a word within a fool's belly. </p>
 <p>13 Admonish a friend, it may be he hath not done it: and if he have done it, that he do it no more. </p>
 <p>14 Admonish thy friend, it may be he hath not said it: and if he have, that he speak it not again. </p>
 <p>15 Admonish a friend: for many times it is a slander, and believe not every tale. </p>
 <p>16 There is one that slippeth in his speech, but not from his heart; and who is he that hath not offended with his tongue? </p>
 <p>17 Admonish thy neighbour before thou threaten him; and not being angry, give place to the law of the most High. </p>
 <p>18 The fear of the Lord is the first step to be accepted [of him,] and wisdom obtaineth his love. </p>
 <p>19 The knowledge of the commandments of the Lord is the doctrine of life: and they that do things that please him shall receive the fruit of the tree of immortality. </p>
 <p>20 The fear of the Lord is all wisdom; and in all wisdom is the performance of the law, and the knowledge of his omnipotency. </p>
 <p>21 If a servant say to his master, I will not do as it pleaseth thee; though afterward he do it, he angereth him that nourisheth him. </p>
 <p>22 The knowledge of wickedness is not wisdom, neither at any time the counsel of sinners prudence. </p>
 <p>23 There is a wickedness, and the same an abomination; and there is a fool wanting in wisdom. </p>
 <p>24 He that hath small understanding, and feareth God, is better than one that hath much wisdom, and transgresseth the law of the most High. </p>
 <p>25 There is an exquisite subtilty, and the same is unjust; and there is one that turneth aside to make judgment appear; and there is a wise man that justifieth in judgment. </p>
 <p>26 There is a wicked man that hangeth down his head sadly; but inwardly he is full of deceit, </p>
 <p>27 Casting down his countenance, and making as if he heard not: where he is not known, he will do thee a mischief before thou be aware. </p>
 <p>28 And if for want of power he be hindered from sinning, yet when he findeth opportunity he will do evil. </p>
 <p>29 A man may be known by his look, and one that hath understanding by his countenance, when thou meetest him. </p>
 <p>30 A man's attire, and excessive laughter, and gait, shew what he is. </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir019.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 ἐργάτης μέθυσος οὐ πλουτισθήσεται ὁ ἐξουθενῶν τὰ ὀλίγα κατὰ μικρὸν πεσεῖται </p>
 <p>2 οἶνος καὶ γυναῖκες ἀποστήσουσιν συνετούς καὶ ὁ κολλώμενος πόρναις τολμηρότερος ἔσται </p>
 <p>3 σήπη καὶ σκώληκες κληρονομήσουσιν αὐτόν καὶ ψυχὴ τολμηρὰ ἐξαρθήσεται </p>
 <p>4 ὁ ταχὺ ἐμπιστεύων κοῦφος καρδίᾳ καὶ ὁ ἁμαρτάνων εἰς ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ πλημμελήσει </p>
 <p>5 ὁ εὐφραινόμενος καρδίᾳ καταγνωσθήσεται </p>
 <p>6 καὶ ὁ μισῶν λαλιὰν ἐλαττονοῦται κακίᾳ </p>
 <p>7 μηδέποτε δευτερώσῃς λόγον καὶ οὐθέν σοι οὐ μὴ ἐλαττονωθῇ </p>
 <p>8 ἐν φίλῳ καὶ ἐχθρῷ μὴ διηγοῦ καὶ εἰ μή ἐστίν σοι ἁμαρτία μὴ ἀποκάλυπτε </p>
 <p>9 ἀκήκοεν γάρ σου καὶ ἐφυλάξατό σε καὶ ἐν καιρῷ μισήσει σε </p>
 <p>10 ἀκήκοας λόγον συναποθανέτω σοι θάρσει οὐ μή σε ῥήξει </p>
 <p>11 ἀπὸ προσώπου λόγου ὠδινήσει μωρὸς ὡς ἀπὸ προσώπου βρέφους ἡ τίκτουσα </p>
 <p>12 βέλος πεπηγὸς ἐν μηρῷ σαρκός οὕτως λόγος ἐν κοιλίᾳ μωροῦ </p>
 <p>13 ἔλεγξον φίλον μήποτε οὐκ ἐποίησεν καὶ εἴ τι ἐποίησεν μήποτε προσθῇ </p>
 <p>14 ἔλεγξον τὸν πλησίον μήποτε οὐκ εἶπεν καὶ εἰ εἴρηκεν ἵνα μὴ δευτερώσῃ </p>
 <p>15 ἔλεγξον φίλον πολλάκις γὰρ γίνεται διαβολή καὶ μὴ παντὶ λόγῳ πίστευε </p>
 <p>16 ἔστιν ὀλισθάνων καὶ οὐκ ἀπὸ ψυχῆς καὶ τίς οὐχ ἥμαρτεν ἐν τῇ γλώσσῃ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>17 ἔλεγξον τὸν πλησίον σου πρὶν ἢ ἀπειλῆσαι καὶ δὸς τόπον νόμῳ ὑψίστου </p>
 <p>20 πᾶσα σοφία φόβος κυρίου καὶ ἐν πάσῃ σοφίᾳ ποίησις νόμου </p>
 <p>22 καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν σοφία πονηρίας ἐπιστήμη καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν ὅπου βουλὴ ἁμαρτωλῶν φρόνησις </p>
 <p>23 ἔστιν πανουργία καὶ αὕτη βδέλυγμα καὶ ἔστιν ἄφρων ἐλαττούμενος σοφίᾳ </p>
 <p>24 κρείττων ἡττώμενος ἐν συνέσει ἔμφοβος ἢ περισσεύων ἐν φρονήσει καὶ παραβαίνων νόμον </p>
 <p>25 ἔστιν πανουργία ἀκριβὴς καὶ αὕτη ἄδικος καὶ ἔστιν διαστρέφων χάριν τοῦ ἐκφᾶναι κρίμα </p>
 <p>26 ἔστιν πονηρευόμενος συγκεκυφὼς μελανίᾳ καὶ τὰ ἐντὸς αὐτοῦ πλήρη δόλου </p>
 <p>27 συγκρύφων πρόσωπον καὶ ἐθελοκωφῶν ὅπου οὐκ ἐπεγνώσθη προφθάσει σε </p>
 <p>28 καὶ ἐὰν ὑπὸ ἐλαττώματος ἰσχύος κωλυθῇ ἁμαρτεῖν ἐὰν εὕρῃ καιρόν κακοποιήσει </p>
 <p>29 ἀπὸ ὁράσεως ἐπιγνωσθήσεται ἀνήρ καὶ ἀπὸ ἀπαντήσεως προσώπου ἐπιγνωσθήσεται νοήμων </p>
 <p>30 στολισμὸς ἀνδρὸς καὶ γέλως ὀδόντων καὶ βήματα ἀνθρώπου ἀναγγελεῖ τὰ περὶ αὐτοῦ </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir019.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Operarius ebriosus non locupletabitur:<br> et qui spernit modica paulatim decidet.<br></p>
 <p>2 Vinum et mulieres apostatare faciunt sapientes,<br> et arguent sensatos.<br></p>
 <p>3 Et qui se jungit fornicariis erit nequam:<br> putredo et vermes hæreditabunt illum:<br> et extolletur in exemplum majus,<br> et tolletur de numero anima ejus.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>4 [Qui credit cito levis corde est, et minorabitur:<br> et qui delinquit in animam suam, insuper habebitur.<br></p>
 <p>5 Qui gaudet iniquitate, denotabitur:<br> et qui odit correptionem, minuetur vita:<br> et qui odit loquacitatem, extinguit malitiam.<br></p>
 <p>6 Qui peccat in animam suam, pnitebit:<br> et qui jucundatur in malitia, denotabitur.<br></p>
 <p>7 Ne iteres verbum nequam et durum,<br> et non minoraberis.<br></p>
 <p>8 Amico et inimico noli narrare sensum tuum:<br> et si est tibi delictum, noli denudare:<br></p>
 <p>9 audiet enim te, et custodiet te,<br> et quasi defendens peccatum, odiet te,<br> et sic aderit tibi semper.<br></p>
 <p>10 Audisti verbum adversus proximum tuum?<br> commoriatur in te, fidens quoniam non te dirumpet.<br></p>
 <p>11 A facie verbi parturit fatuus,<br> tamquam gemitus partus infantis.<br></p>
 <p>12 Sagitta infixa femori carnis,<br> sic verbum in corde stulti.<br></p>
 <p>13 Corripe amicum,<br> ne forte non intellexerit, et dicat: Non feci:<br> aut, si fecerit, ne iterum addat facere.<br></p>
 <p>14 Corripe proximum, ne forte non dixerit:<br> et si dixerit, ne forte iteret.<br></p>
 <p>15 Corripe amicum, sæpe enim fit commissio:<br></p>
 <p>16 et non omni verbo credas.<br> Est qui labitur lingua, sed non ex animo:<br></p>
 <p>17 quis est enim qui non deliquerit in lingua sua?]<br>
  [Corripe proximum antequam commineris,<br></p>
 <p>18 et da locum timori Altissimi:<br> quia omnis sapientia timor Dei, et in illa timere Deum,<br> et in omni sapientia dispositio legis.<br></p>
 <p>19 Et non est sapientia nequitiæ disciplina,<br> et non est cogitatus peccatorum prudentia.<br></p>
 <p>20 Est nequitia, et in ipsa execratio,<br> et est insipiens qui minuitur sapientia.<br></p>
 <p>21 Melior est homo qui minuitur sapientia,<br> et deficiens sensu, in timore,<br> quam qui abundat sensu,<br> et transgreditur legem Altissimi.<br></p>
 <p>22 Est solertia certa, et ipsa iniqua:<br></p>
 <p>23 et est qui emittit verbum certum enarrans veritatem.<br> Est qui nequiter humiliat se,<br> et interiora ejus plena sunt dolo:<br></p>
 <p>24 et est qui se nimium submittit a multa humilitate:<br> et est qui inclinat faciem suam,<br> et fingit se non videre quod ignoratum est:<br></p>
 <p>25 et si ab imbecillitate virium vetetur peccare,<br> si invenerit tempus malefaciendi, malefaciet.<br></p>
 <p>26 Ex visu cognoscitur vir,<br> et ab occursu faciei cognoscitur sensatus.<br></p>
 <p>27 Amictus corporis, et risus dentium,<br> et ingressus hominis, enuntiant de illo.<br></p>
 <p>28 Est correptio mendax in ira contumeliosi,<br> et est judicium quod non probatur esse bonum:<br> et est tacens, et ipse est prudens.]</p>
