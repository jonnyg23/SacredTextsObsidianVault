 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro005.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  My son,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> attend<sup><a href="h7181.htm">7181</a></sup> unto my wisdom,<sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> and bow<sup><a href="h5186.htm">5186</a></sup> thine ear<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> to my understanding:<sup><a href="h8394.htm">8394</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  That thou mayest regard<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> discretion,<sup><a href="h4209.htm">4209</a></sup> and that thy lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> may keep<sup><a href="h5341.htm">5341</a></sup> knowledge.<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> the lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> of a strange woman<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> drop<sup><a href="h5197.htm">5197</a></sup> as a honeycomb,<sup><a href="h5317.htm">5317</a></sup> and her mouth<sup><a href="h2441.htm">2441</a></sup> is smoother<sup><a href="h2509.htm">2509</a></sup> than oil:<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h8081.htm">8081</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  But her end<sup><a href="h0319.htm">319</a></sup> is bitter<sup><a href="h4751.htm">4751</a></sup> as wormwood,<sup><a href="h3939.htm">3939</a></sup> sharp<sup><a href="h2299.htm">2299</a></sup> as a twoedged<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> sword.<sup><a href="h2719.htm">2719</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  Her feet<sup><a href="h7272.htm">7272</a></sup> go down<sup><a href="h3381.htm">3381</a></sup> to death;<sup><a href="h4194.htm">4194</a></sup> her steps<sup><a href="h6806.htm">6806</a></sup> take hold<sup><a href="h8551.htm">8551</a></sup> on hell.<sup><a href="h7585.htm">7585</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  Lest<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> thou shouldest ponder<sup><a href="h6424.htm">6424</a></sup> the path<sup><a href="h0734.htm">734</a></sup> of life,<sup><a href="h2416.htm">2416</a></sup> her ways<sup><a href="h4570.htm">4570</a></sup> are movable,<sup><a href="h5128.htm">5128</a></sup> that thou canst not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> know<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> them. </p>
 <p>7  Hear<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> me now<sup><a href="h6258.htm">6258</a></sup> therefore, O ye children,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> and depart<sup><a href="h5493.htm">5493</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> from the words<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0561.htm">561</a></sup> of my mouth.<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  Remove thy way far<sup><a href="h7368.htm">7368</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> from<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> her, and come not nigh<sup><a href="h7126.htm">7126</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> the door<sup><a href="h6607.htm">6607</a></sup> of her house:<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  Lest<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> thou give<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> thine honor<sup><a href="h1935.htm">1935</a></sup> unto others,<sup><a href="h0312.htm">312</a></sup> and thy years<sup><a href="h8141.htm">8141</a></sup> unto the cruel:<sup><a href="h0394.htm">394</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  Lest<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> strangers<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> be filled<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> with thy wealth;<sup><a href="h3581.htm">3581</a></sup> and thy labors<sup><a href="h6089.htm">6089</a></sup> be in the house<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> of a stranger;<sup><a href="h5237.htm">5237</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  And thou mourn<sup><a href="h5098.htm">5098</a></sup> at the last,<sup><a href="h0319.htm">319</a></sup> when thy flesh<sup><a href="h1320.htm">1320</a></sup> and thy body<sup><a href="h7607.htm">7607</a></sup> are consumed,<sup><a href="h3615.htm">3615</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  And say,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> How<sup><a href="h0349.htm">349</a></sup> have I hated<sup><a href="h8130.htm">8130</a></sup> instruction,<sup><a href="h4148.htm">4148</a></sup> and my heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> despised<sup><a href="h5006.htm">5006</a></sup> reproof;<sup><a href="h8433.htm">8433</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  And have not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> obeyed<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> the voice<sup><a href="h6963.htm">6963</a></sup> of my teachers,<sup><a href="h3384.htm">3384</a></sup> nor<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> inclined<sup><a href="h5186.htm">5186</a></sup> mine ear<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> to them that instructed<sup><a href="h3925.htm">3925</a></sup> me! </p>
 <p>14  I was<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> almost<sup><a href="h4592.htm">4592</a></sup> in all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> in the midst<sup><a href="h8432.htm">8432</a></sup> of the congregation<sup><a href="h6951.htm">6951</a></sup> and assembly.<sup><a href="h5712.htm">5712</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  Drink<sup><a href="h8354.htm">8354</a></sup> waters<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> out of thine own cistern,<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0953.htm">953</a></sup> and running waters<sup><a href="h5140.htm">5140</a></sup> out of<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h8432.htm">8432</a></sup> thine own well.<sup><a href="h0875.htm">875</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  Let thy fountains<sup><a href="h4599.htm">4599</a></sup> be dispersed<sup><a href="h6327.htm">6327</a></sup> abroad,<sup><a href="h2351.htm">2351</a></sup> and rivers<sup><a href="h6388.htm">6388</a></sup> of waters<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> in the streets.<sup><a href="h7339.htm">7339</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  Let them be<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> only<sup><a href="h0905.htm">905</a></sup> thine own, and not<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> strangers'<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> with<sup><a href="h0854.htm">854</a></sup> thee. </p>
 <p>18  Let thy fountain<sup><a href="h4726.htm">4726</a></sup> be<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> blessed:<sup><a href="h1288.htm">1288</a></sup> and rejoice<sup><a href="h8055.htm">8055</a></sup> with the wife<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> of thy youth.<sup><a href="h5271.htm">5271</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  Let her be as the loving<sup><a href="h0158.htm">158</a></sup> hind<sup><a href="h0365.htm">365</a></sup> and pleasant<sup><a href="h2580.htm">2580</a></sup> roe;<sup><a href="h3280.htm">3280</a></sup> let her breasts<sup><a href="h1717.htm">1717</a></sup> satisfy<sup><a href="h7301.htm">7301</a></sup> thee at all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> times;<sup><a href="h6256.htm">6256</a></sup> and be thou ravished<sup><a href="h7686.htm">7686</a></sup> always<sup><a href="h8548.htm">8548</a></sup> with her love.<sup><a href="h0160.htm">160</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  And why<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> wilt thou, my son,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> be ravished<sup><a href="h7686.htm">7686</a></sup> with a strange woman,<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> and embrace<sup><a href="h2263.htm">2263</a></sup> the bosom<sup><a href="h2436.htm">2436</a></sup> of a stranger?<sup><a href="h5237.htm">5237</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> the ways<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> are before<sup><a href="h5227.htm">5227</a></sup> the eyes<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> of the LORD,<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> and he pondereth<sup><a href="h6424.htm">6424</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> his goings.<sup><a href="h4570.htm">4570</a></sup> </p>
 <p>22  His own iniquities<sup><a href="h5771.htm">5771</a></sup> shall take<sup><a href="h3920.htm">3920</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> himself, and he shall be holden<sup><a href="h8551.htm">8551</a></sup> with the cords<sup><a href="h2256.htm">2256</a></sup> of his sins.<sup><a href="h2403.htm">2403</a></sup> </p>
 <p>23  He<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> shall die<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> without<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> instruction;<sup><a href="h4148.htm">4148</a></sup> and in the greatness<sup><a href="h7230.htm">7230</a></sup> of his folly<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> he shall go astray.<sup><a href="h7686.htm">7686</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/pro005.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 υἱέ ἐμῇ σοφίᾳ πρόσεχε ἐμοῖς δὲ λόγοις παράβαλλε σὸν οὖς </p>
 <p>2 ἵνα φυλάξῃς ἔννοιαν ἀγαθήν αἴσθησιν δὲ ἐμῶν χειλέων ἐντέλλομαί σοι </p>
 <p>3 μὴ πρόσεχε φαύλῃ γυναικί μέλι γὰρ ἀποστάζει ἀπὸ χειλέων γυναικὸς πόρνης ἣ πρὸς καιρὸν λιπαίνει σὸν φάρυγγα </p>
 <p>4 ὕστερον μέντοι πικρότερον χολῆς εὑρήσεις καὶ ἠκονημένον μᾶλλον μαχαίρας διστόμου </p>
 <p>5 τῆς γὰρ ἀφροσύνης οἱ πόδες κατάγουσιν τοὺς χρωμένους αὐτῇ μετὰ θανάτου εἰς τὸν ᾅδην τὰ δὲ ἴχνη αὐτῆς οὐκ ἐρείδεται </p>
 <p>6 ὁδοὺς γὰρ ζωῆς οὐκ ἐπέρχεται σφαλεραὶ δὲ αἱ τροχιαὶ αὐτῆς καὶ οὐκ εὔγνωστοι </p>
 <p>7 νῦν οὖν υἱέ ἄκουέ μου καὶ μὴ ἀκύρους ποιήσῃς ἐμοὺς λόγους </p>
 <p>8 μακρὰν ποίησον ἀπ᾽ αὐτῆς σὴν ὁδόν μὴ ἐγγίσῃς πρὸς θύραις οἴκων αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>9 ἵνα μὴ πρόῃ ἄλλοις ζωήν σου καὶ σὸν βίον ἀνελεήμοσιν </p>
 <p>10 ἵνα μὴ πλησθῶσιν ἀλλότριοι σῆς ἰσχύος οἱ δὲ σοὶ πόνοι εἰς οἴκους ἀλλοτρίων εἰσέλθωσιν </p>
 <p>11 καὶ μεταμεληθήσῃ ἐπ᾽ ἐσχάτων ἡνίκα ἂν κατατριβῶσιν σάρκες σώματός σου </p>
 <p>12 καὶ ἐρεῖς πῶς ἐμίσησα παιδείαν καὶ ἐλέγχους ἐξέκλινεν ἡ καρδία μου </p>
 <p>13 οὐκ ἤκουον φωνὴν παιδεύοντός με καὶ διδάσκοντός με οὐδὲ παρέβαλλον τὸ οὖς μου </p>
 <p>14 παρ᾽ ὀλίγον ἐγενόμην ἐν παντὶ κακῷ ἐν μέσῳ ἐκκλησίας καὶ συναγωγῆς </p>
 <p>15 πῖνε ὕδατα ἀπὸ σῶν ἀγγείων καὶ ἀπὸ σῶν φρεάτων πηγῆς </p>
 <p>16 μὴ ὑπερεκχείσθω σοι τὰ ὕδατα ἐκ τῆς σῆς πηγῆς εἰς δὲ σὰς πλατείας διαπορευέσθω τὰ σὰ ὕδατα </p>
 <p>17 ἔστω σοι μόνῳ ὑπάρχοντα καὶ μηδεὶς ἀλλότριος μετασχέτω σοι </p>
 <p>18 ἡ πηγή σου τοῦ ὕδατος ἔστω σοι ἰδία καὶ συνευφραίνου μετὰ γυναικὸς τῆς ἐκ νεότητός σου </p>
 <p>19 ἔλαφος φιλίας καὶ πῶλος σῶν χαρίτων ὁμιλείτω σοι ἡ δὲ ἰδία ἡγείσθω σου καὶ συνέστω σοι ἐν παντὶ καιρῷ ἐν γὰρ τῇ ταύτης φιλίᾳ συμπεριφερόμενος πολλοστὸς ἔσῃ </p>
 <p>20 μὴ πολὺς ἴσθι πρὸς ἀλλοτρίαν μηδὲ συνέχου ἀγκάλαις τῆς μὴ ἰδίας </p>
 <p>21 ἐνώπιον γάρ εἰσιν τῶν τοῦ θεοῦ ὀφθαλμῶν ὁδοὶ ἀνδρός εἰς δὲ πάσας τὰς τροχιὰς αὐτοῦ σκοπεύει </p>
 <p>22 παρανομίαι ἄνδρα ἀγρεύουσιν σειραῖς δὲ τῶν ἑαυτοῦ ἁμαρτιῶν ἕκαστος σφίγγεται </p>
 <p>23 οὗτος τελευτᾷ μετὰ ἀπαιδεύτων ἐκ δὲ πλήθους τῆς ἑαυτοῦ βιότητος ἐξερρίφη καὶ ἀπώλετο δι᾽ ἀφροσύνην </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro005.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Fili mi, attende ad sapientiam meam,<br> et prudentiæ meæ inclina aurem tuam:<br></p>
 <p>2 ut custodias cogitationes, et disciplinam labia tua conservent.<br> Ne attendas fallaciæ mulieris;<br></p>
 <p>3 favus enim distillans labia meretricis,<br> et nitidius oleo guttur ejus:<br></p>
 <p>4 novissima autem illius amara quasi absinthium,<br> et acuta quasi gladius biceps.<br></p>
 <p>5 Pedes ejus descendunt in mortem,<br> et ad inferos gressus illius penetrant.<br></p>
 <p>6 Per semitam vitæ non ambulant;<br> vagi sunt gressus ejus et investigabiles.<br></p>
 <p>7 Nunc ergo fili mi, audi me,<br> et ne recedas a verbis oris mei.<br></p>
 <p>8 Longe fac ab ea viam tuam,<br> et ne appropinques foribus domus ejus.<br></p>
 <p>9 Ne des alienis honorem tuum,<br> et annos tuos crudeli:<br></p>
 <p>10 ne forte implentur extranei viribus tuis,<br> et labores tui sint in domo aliena,<br></p>
 <p>11 et gemas in novissimis,<br> quando consumpseris carnes tuas et corpus tuum,<br> et dicas:</p>
 <p>12 Cur detestatus sum disciplinam,<br> et increpationibus non acquievit cor meum,<br></p>
 <p>13 nec audivi vocem docentium me,<br> et magistris non inclinavi aurem meam?<br></p>
 <p>14 pene fui in omni malo,<br> in medio ecclesiæ et synagogæ.<br></p>
 <p>15 Bibe aquam de cisterna tua,<br> et fluenta putei tui;<br></p>
 <p>16 deriventur fontes tui foras,<br> et in plateis aquas tuas divide.<br></p>
 <p>17 Habeto eas solus,<br> nec sint alieni participes tui.<br></p>
 <p>18 Sit vena tua benedicta,<br> et lætare cum muliere adolescentiæ tuæ.<br></p>
 <p>19 Cerva carissima, et gratissimus hinnulus:<br> ubera ejus inebrient te in omni tempore;<br> in amore ejus delectare jugiter.<br></p>
 <p>20 Quare seduceris, fili mi, ab aliena,<br> et foveris in sinu alterius?<br></p>
 <p>21 Respicit Dominus vias hominis,<br> et omnes gressus ejus considerat.<br></p>
 <p>22 Iniquitates suas capiunt impium,<br> et funibus peccatorum suorum constringitur.<br></p>
 <p>23 Ipse morietur, quia non habuit disciplinam,<br> et in multitudine stultitiæ suæ decipietur.]</p>
