 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 25</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir025.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 In three things I was beautified, and stood up beautiful both before God and men: the unity of brethren, the love of neighbours, a man and a wife that agree together. </p>
 <p>2 Three sorts of men my soul hateth, and I am greatly offended at their life: a poor man that is proud, a rich man that is a liar, and an old adulterer that doateth. </p>
 <p>3 If thou hast gathered nothing in thy youth, how canst thou find any thing in thine age? </p>
 <p>4 O how comely a thing is judgment for gray hairs, and for ancient men to know counsel! </p>
 <p>5 O how comely is the wisdom of old men, and understanding and counsel to men of honour. </p>
 <p>6 Much experience is the crown of old men, and the fear of God is their glory. </p>
 <p>7 There be nine things which I have judged in mine heart to be happy, and the tenth I will utter with my tongue: A man that hath joy of his children; and he that liveth to see the fall of his enemy: </p>
 <p>8 Well is him that dwelleth with a wife of understanding, and that hath not slipped with his tongue, and that hath not served a man more unworthy than himself: </p>
 <p>9 Well is him that hath found prudence, and he that speaketh in the ears of them that will hear: </p>
 <p>10 O how great is he that findeth wisdom! yet is there none above him that feareth the Lord. </p>
 <p>11 But the love of the Lord passeth all things for illumination: he that holdeth it, whereto shall he be likened? </p>
 <p>12 The fear of the Lord is the beginning of his love: and faith is the beginning of cleaving unto him. </p>
 <p>13 [Give me] any plague, but the plague of the heart: and any wickedness, but the wickedness of a woman: </p>
 <p>14 And any affliction, but the affliction from them that hate me: and any revenge, but the revenge of enemies. </p>
 <p>15 There is no head above the head of a serpent; and there is no wrath above the wrath of an enemy. </p>
 <p>16 I had rather dwell with a lion and a dragon, than to keep house with a wicked woman. </p>
 <p>17 The wickedness of a woman changeth her face, and darkeneth her countenance like sackcloth. </p>
 <p>18 Her husband shall sit among his neighbours; and when he heareth it shall sigh bitterly. </p>
 <p>19 All wickedness is but little to the wickedness of a woman: let the portion of a sinner fall upon her. </p>
 <p>20 As the climbing up a sandy way is to the feet of the aged, so is a wife full of words to a quiet man. </p>
 <p>21 Stumble not at the beauty of a woman, and desire her not for pleasure. </p>
 <p>22 A woman, if she maintain her husband, is full of anger, impudence, and much reproach. </p>
 <p>23 A wicked woman abateth the courage, maketh an heavy countenance and a wounded heart: a woman that will not comfort her husband in distress maketh weak hands and feeble knees. </p>
 <p>24 Of the woman came the beginning of sin, and through her we all die. </p>
 <p>25 Give the water no passage; neither a wicked woman liberty to gad abroad. </p>
 <p>26 If she go not as thou wouldest have her, cut her off from thy flesh, and give her a bill of divorce, and let her go. </p>

 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir025.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 ἐν τρισὶν ὡραΐσθην καὶ ἀνέστην ὡραία ἔναντι κυρίου καὶ ἀνθρώπων ὁμόνοια ἀδελφῶν καὶ φιλία τῶν πλησίον καὶ γυνὴ καὶ ἀνὴρ ἑαυτοῖς συμπεριφερόμενοι </p>
 <p>2 τρία δὲ εἴδη ἐμίσησεν ἡ ψυχή μου καὶ προσώχθισα σφόδρα τῇ ζωῇ αὐτῶν πτωχὸν ὑπερήφανον καὶ πλούσιον ψεύστην γέροντα μοιχὸν ἐλαττούμενον συνέσει </p>
 <p>3 ἐν νεότητι οὐ συναγείοχας καὶ πῶς ἂν εὕροις ἐν τῷ γήρᾳ σου </p>
 <p>4 ὡς ὡραῖον πολιαῖς κρίσις καὶ πρεσβυτέροις ἐπιγνῶναι βουλήν </p>
 <p>5 ὡς ὡραία γερόντων σοφία καὶ δεδοξασμένοις διανόημα καὶ βουλή </p>
 <p>6 στέφανος γερόντων πολυπειρία καὶ τὸ καύχημα αὐτῶν φόβος κυρίου </p>
 <p>7 ἐννέα ὑπονοήματα ἐμακάρισα ἐν καρδίᾳ καὶ τὸ δέκατον ἐρῶ ἐπὶ γλώσσης ἄνθρωπος εὐφραινόμενος ἐπὶ τέκνοις ζῶν καὶ βλέπων ἐπὶ πτώσει ἐχθρῶν </p>
 <p>8 μακάριος ὁ συνοικῶν γυναικὶ συνετῇ καὶ ὃς ἐν γλώσσῃ οὐκ ὠλίσθησεν καὶ ὃς οὐκ ἐδούλευσεν ἀναξίῳ ἑαυτοῦ </p>
 <p>9 μακάριος ὃς εὗρεν φρόνησιν καὶ ὁ διηγούμενος εἰς ὦτα ἀκουόντων </p>
 <p>10 ὡς μέγας ὁ εὑρὼν σοφίαν ἀλλ᾽ οὐκ ἔστιν ὑπὲρ τὸν φοβούμενον τὸν κύριον </p>
 <p>11 φόβος κυρίου ὑπὲρ πᾶν ὑπερέβαλεν ὁ κρατῶν αὐτοῦ τίνι ὁμοιωθήσεται </p>
 <p>13 πᾶσαν πληγὴν καὶ μὴ πληγὴν καρδίας καὶ πᾶσαν πονηρίαν καὶ μὴ πονηρίαν γυναικός </p>
 <p>14 πᾶσαν ἐπαγωγὴν καὶ μὴ ἐπαγωγὴν μισούντων καὶ πᾶσαν ἐκδίκησιν καὶ μὴ ἐκδίκησιν ἐχθρῶν </p>
 <p>15 οὐκ ἔστιν κεφαλὴ ὑπὲρ κεφαλὴν ὄφεως καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν θυμὸς ὑπὲρ θυμὸν ἐχθροῦ </p>
 <p>16 συνοικῆσαι λέοντι καὶ δράκοντι εὐδοκήσω ἢ συνοικῆσαι μετὰ γυναικὸς πονηρᾶς </p>
 <p>17 πονηρία γυναικὸς ἀλλοιοῖ τὴν ὅρασιν αὐτῆς καὶ σκοτοῖ τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτῆς ὡς ἄρκος </p>
 <p>18 ἀνὰ μέσον τῶν πλησίον αὐτοῦ ἀναπεσεῖται ὁ ἀνὴρ αὐτῆς καὶ ἀκουσίως ἀνεστέναξεν πικρά </p>
 <p>19 μικρὰ πᾶσα κακία πρὸς κακίαν γυναικός κλῆρος ἁμαρτωλοῦ ἐπιπέσοι αὐτῇ </p>
 <p>20 ἀνάβασις ἀμμώδης ἐν ποσὶν πρεσβυτέρου οὕτως γυνὴ γλωσσώδης ἀνδρὶ ἡσύχῳ </p>
 <p>21 μὴ προσπέσῃς ἐπὶ κάλλος γυναικὸς καὶ γυναῖκα μὴ ἐπιποθήσῃς </p>
 <p>22 ὀργὴ καὶ ἀναίδεια καὶ αἰσχύνη μεγάλη γυνὴ ἐὰν ἐπιχορηγῇ τῷ ἀνδρὶ αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>23 καρδία ταπεινὴ καὶ πρόσωπον σκυθρωπὸν καὶ πληγὴ καρδίας γυνὴ πονηρά χεῖρες παρειμέναι καὶ γόνατα παραλελυμένα ἥτις οὐ μακαριεῖ τὸν ἄνδρα αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>24 ἀπὸ γυναικὸς ἀρχὴ ἁμαρτίας καὶ δι᾽ αὐτὴν ἀποθνῄσκομεν πάντες </p>
 <p>25 μὴ δῷς ὕδατι διέξοδον μηδὲ γυναικὶ πονηρᾷ παρρησίαν </p>
 <p>26 εἰ μὴ πορεύεται κατὰ χεῖράς σου ἀπὸ τῶν σαρκῶν σου ἀπότεμε αὐτήν </p>

 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir025.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [In tribus placitum est spiritui meo,<br> quæ sunt probata coram Deo et hominibus:<br></p>
 <p>2 concordia fratrum,<br> et amor proximorum,<br> et vir et mulier bene sibi consentientes.<br></p>
 <p>3 Tres species odivit anima mea,<br> et aggravor valde animæ illorum:<br></p>
 <p>4 pauperem superbum, divitem mendacem,<br> senem fatuum et insensatum.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>5 [Quæ in juventute tua non congregasti,<br> quomodo in senectute tua invenies?<br></p>
 <p>6 Quam speciosum canitiei judicium,<br> et presbyteris cognoscere consilium!<br></p>
 <p>7 Quam speciosa veteranis sapientia,<br> et gloriosus intellectus et consilium!<br></p>
 <p>8 Corona senum multa peritia,<br> et gloria illorum timor Dei.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>9 [Novem insuspicabilia cordis magnificavi:<br> et decimum dicam in lingua hominibus:<br></p>
 <p>10 homo qui jucundatur in filiis,<br> vivens et videns subversionem inimicorum suorum.<br></p>
 <p>11 Beatus qui habitat cum muliere sensata,<br> et qui lingua sua non est lapsus,<br> et qui non servivit indignis se.<br></p>
 <p>12 Beatus qui invenit amicum verum,<br> et qui enarrat justitiam auri audienti.<br></p>
 <p>13 Quam magnus qui invenit sapientiam et scientiam!<br> sed non est super timentem Dominum.<br></p>
 <p>14 Timor Dei super omnia se superposuit.<br></p>
 <p>15 Beatus homo cui donatum est habere timorem Dei:<br> qui tenet illum, cui assimilabitur?<br></p>
 <p>16 Timor Dei initium dilectionis ejus:<br> fidei autem initium agglutinandum est ei.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>17 [Omnis plaga tristitia cordis est,<br> et omnis malitia nequitia mulieris.<br></p>
 <p>18 Et omnem plagam, et non plagam videbit cordis:<br></p>
 <p>19 et omnem nequitiam, et non nequitiam mulieris:<br></p>
 <p>20 et omnem obductum, et non obductum odientium:<br></p>
 <p>21 et omnem vindictam, et non vindictam inimicorum.<br></p>
 <p>22 Non est caput nequius super caput colubri,<br></p>
 <p>23 et non est ira super iram mulieris.<br> Commorari leoni et draconi placebit,<br> quam habitare cum muliere nequam.<br></p>
 <p>24 Nequitia mulieris immutat faciem ejus:<br> et obcæcat vultum suum tamquam ursus,<br> et quasi saccum ostendit.<br> In medio proximorum ejus<br></p>
 <p>25 ingemuit vir ejus,<br> et audiens suspiravit modicum.<br></p>
 <p>26 Brevis omnis malitia super malitiam mulieris:<br> sors peccatorum cadat super illam.<br></p>
 <p>27 Sicut ascensus arenosus in pedibus veterani,<br> sic mulier linguata homini quieto.<br></p>
 <p>28 Ne respicias in mulieris speciem,<br> et non concupiscas mulierem in specie.<br></p>
 <p>29 Mulieris ira, et irreverentia,<br> et confusio magna.<br></p>
 <p>30 Mulier si primatum habeat,<br> contraria est viro suo.<br></p>
 <p>31 Cor humile, et facies tristis,<br> et plaga cordis, mulier nequam.<br></p>
 <p>32 Manus debiles et genua dissoluta,<br> mulier quæ non beatificat virum suum.<br></p>
 <p>33 A muliere initium factum est peccati,<br> et per illam omnes morimur.<br></p>
 <p>34 Non des aquæ tuæ exitum, nec modicum:<br> nec mulieri nequam veniam prodeundi.<br></p>
 <p>35 Si non ambulaverit ad manum tuam,<br> confundet te in conspectu inimicorum.<br></p>
 <p>36 A carnibus tuis abscinde illam,<br> ne semper te abutatur.]</p>
