 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 43</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir043.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 The pride of the height, the clear firmament, the beauty of heaven, with his glorious shew; </p>
 <p>2 The sun when it appeareth, declaring at his rising a marvellous instrument, the work of the most High: </p>
 <p>3 At noon it parcheth the country, and who can abide the burning heat thereof? </p>
 <p>4 A man blowing a furnace is in works of heat, but the sun burneth the mountains three times more; breathing out fiery vapours, and sending forth bright beams, it dimmeth the eyes. </p>
 <p>5 Great is the Lord that made it; and at his commandment runneth hastily. </p>
 <p>6 He made the moon also to serve in her season for a declaration of times, and a sign of the world. </p>
 <p>7 From the moon is the sign of feasts, a light that decreaseth in her perfection. </p>
 <p>8 The month is called after her name, increasing wonderfully in her changing, being an instrument of the armies above, shining in the firmament of heaven; </p>
 <p>9 The beauty of heaven, the glory of the stars, an ornament giving light in the highest places of the Lord. </p>
 <p>10 At the commandment of the Holy One they will stand in their order, and never faint in their watches. </p>
 <p>11 Look upon the rainbow, and praise him that made it; very beautiful it is in the brightness thereof. </p>
 <p>12 It compasseth the heaven about with a glorious circle, and the hands of the most High have bended it. </p>
 <p>13 By his commandment he maketh the snow to fall aplace, and sendeth swiftly the lightnings of his judgment. </p>
 <p>14 Through this the treasures are opened: and clouds fly forth as fowls. </p>
 <p>15 By his great power he maketh the clouds firm, and the hailstones are broken small. </p>
 <p>16 At his sight the mountains are shaken, and at his will the south wind bloweth. </p>
 <p>17 The noise of the thunder maketh the earth to tremble: so doth the northern storm and the whirlwind: as birds flying he scattereth the snow, and the falling down thereof is as the lighting of grasshoppers: </p>
 <p>18 The eye marvelleth at the beauty of the whiteness thereof, and the heart is astonished at the raining of it. </p>
 <p>19 The hoarfrost also as salt he poureth on the earth, and being congealed, it lieth on the top of sharp stakes. </p>
 <p>20 When the cold north wind bloweth, and the water is congealed into ice, it abideth upon every gathering together of water, and clotheth the water as with a breastplate. </p>
 <p>21 It devoureth the mountains, and burneth the wilderness, and consumeth the grass as fire. </p>
 <p>22 A present remedy of all is a mist coming speedily, a dew coming after heat refresheth. </p>
 <p>23 By his counsel he appeaseth the deep, and planteth islands therein. </p>
 <p>24 They that sail on the sea tell of the danger thereof; and when we hear it with our ears, we marvel thereat. </p>
 <p>25 For therein be strange and wondrous works, variety of all kinds of beasts and whales created. </p>
 <p>26 By him the end of them hath prosperous success, and by his word all things consist. </p>
 <p>27 We may speak much, and yet come short: wherefore in sum, he is all. </p>
 <p>28 How shall we be able to magnify him? for he is great above all his works. </p>
 <p>29 The Lord is terrible and very great, and marvellous is his power. </p>
 <p>30 When ye glorify the Lord, exalt him as much as ye can; for even yet will he far exceed: and when ye exalt him, put forth all your strength, and be not weary; for ye can never go far enough. </p>
 <p>31 Who hath seen him, that he might tell us? and who can magnify him as he is? </p>
 <p>32 There are yet hid greater things than these be, for we have seen but a few of his works. </p>
 <p>33 For the Lord hath made all things; and to the godly hath he given wisdom. </p>

 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir043.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 γαυρίαμα ὕψους στερέωμα καθαριότητος εἶδος οὐρανοῦ ἐν ὁράματι δόξης </p>
 <p>2 ἥλιος ἐν ὀπτασίᾳ διαγγέλλων ἐν ἐξόδῳ σκεῦος θαυμαστόν ἔργον ὑψίστου </p>
 <p>3 ἐν μεσημβρίᾳ αὐτοῦ ἀναξηραίνει χώραν καὶ ἐναντίον καύματος αὐτοῦ τίς ὑποστήσεται </p>
 <p>4 κάμινον φυσῶν ἐν ἔργοις καύματος τριπλασίως ἥλιος ἐκκαίων ὄρη ἀτμίδας πυρώδεις ἐκφυσῶν καὶ ἐκλάμπων ἀκτῖνας ἀμαυροῖ ὀφθαλμούς </p>
 <p>5 μέγας κύριος ὁ ποιήσας αὐτόν καὶ ἐν λόγοις αὐτοῦ κατέσπευσεν πορείαν </p>
 <p>6 καὶ ἡ σελήνη ἐν πᾶσιν εἰς καιρὸν αὐτῆς ἀνάδειξιν χρόνων καὶ σημεῖον αἰῶνος </p>
 <p>7 ἀπὸ σελήνης σημεῖον ἑορτῆς φωστὴρ μειούμενος ἐπὶ συντελείας </p>
 <p>8 μὴν κατὰ τὸ ὄνομα αὐτῆς ἐστιν αὐξανόμενος θαυμαστῶς ἐν ἀλλοιώσει σκεῦος παρεμβολῶν ἐν ὕψει ἐν στερεώματι οὐρανοῦ ἐκλάμπων </p>
 <p>9 κάλλος οὐρανοῦ δόξα ἄστρων κόσμος φωτίζων ἐν ὑψίστοις κυρίου </p>
 <p>10 ἐν λόγοις ἁγίου στήσονται κατὰ κρίμα καὶ οὐ μὴ ἐκλυθῶσιν ἐν φυλακαῖς αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>11 ἰδὲ τόξον καὶ εὐλόγησον τὸν ποιήσαντα αὐτὸ σφόδρα ὡραῖον ἐν τῷ αὐγάσματι αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>12 ἐγύρωσεν οὐρανὸν ἐν κυκλώσει δόξης χεῖρες ὑψίστου ἐτάνυσαν αὐτό </p>
 <p>13 προστάγματι αὐτοῦ κατέσπευσεν χιόνα καὶ ταχύνει ἀστραπὰς κρίματος αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>14 διὰ τοῦτο ἠνεῴχθησαν θησαυροί καὶ ἐξέπτησαν νεφέλαι ὡς πετεινά </p>
 <p>15 ἐν μεγαλείῳ αὐτοῦ ἴσχυσεν νεφέλας καὶ διεθρύβησαν λίθοι χαλάζης </p>
 <p>16 καὶ ἐν ὀπτασίᾳ αὐτοῦ σαλευθήσεται ὄρη ἐν θελήματι αὐτοῦ πνεύσεται νότος </p>
 <p>17 φωνὴ βροντῆς αὐτοῦ ὠνείδισεν γῆν καὶ καταιγὶς βορέου καὶ συστροφὴ πνεύματος </p>
 <p>18 ὡς πετεινὰ καθιπτάμενα πάσσει χιόνα καὶ ὡς ἀκρὶς καταλύουσα ἡ κατάβασις αὐτῆς κάλλος λευκότητος αὐτῆς ἐκθαυμάσει ὀφθαλμός καὶ ἐπὶ τοῦ ὑετοῦ αὐτῆς ἐκστήσεται καρδία </p>
 <p>19 καὶ πάχνην ὡς ἅλα ἐπὶ γῆς χέει καὶ παγεῖσα γίνεται σκολόπων ἄκρα </p>
 <p>20 ψυχρὸς ἄνεμος βορέης πνεύσει καὶ παγήσεται κρύσταλλος ἐφ᾽ ὕδατος ἐπὶ πᾶσαν συναγωγὴν ὕδατος καταλύσει καὶ ὡς θώρακα ἐνδύσεται τὸ ὕδωρ </p>
 <p>21 καταφάγεται ὄρη καὶ ἔρημον ἐκκαύσει καὶ ἀποσβέσει χλόην ὡς πῦρ </p>
 <p>22 ἴασις πάντων κατὰ σπουδὴν ὁμίχλη δρόσος ἀπαντῶσα ἀπὸ καύσωνος ἱλαρώσει </p>
 <p>23 λογισμῷ αὐτοῦ ἐκόπασεν ἄβυσσον καὶ ἐφύτευσεν ἐν αὐτῇ νήσους </p>
 <p>24 οἱ πλέοντες τὴν θάλασσαν διηγοῦνται τὸν κίνδυνον αὐτῆς καὶ ἀκοαῖς ὠτίων ἡμῶν θαυμάζομεν </p>
 <p>25 καὶ ἐκεῖ τὰ παράδοξα καὶ θαυμάσια ἔργα ποικιλία παντὸς ζῴου κτίσις κητῶν </p>
 <p>26 δι᾽ αὐτὸν εὐοδοῖ ἄγγελος αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐν λόγῳ αὐτοῦ σύγκειται τὰ πάντα </p>
 <p>27 πολλὰ ἐροῦμεν καὶ οὐ μὴ ἀφικώμεθα καὶ συντέλεια λόγων τὸ πᾶν ἐστιν αὐτός </p>
 <p>28 δοξάζοντες ποῦ ἰσχύσομεν αὐτὸς γὰρ ὁ μέγας παρὰ πάντα τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>29 φοβερὸς κύριος καὶ σφόδρα μέγας καὶ θαυμαστὴ ἡ δυναστεία αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>30 δοξάζοντες κύριον ὑψώσατε καθ᾽ ὅσον ἂν δύνησθε ὑπερέξει γὰρ καὶ ἔτι καὶ ὑψοῦντες αὐτὸν πληθύνατε ἐν ἰσχύι μὴ κοπιᾶτε οὐ γὰρ μὴ ἀφίκησθε </p>
 <p>31 τίς ἑόρακεν αὐτὸν καὶ ἐκδιηγήσεται καὶ τίς μεγαλυνεῖ αὐτὸν καθώς ἐστιν </p>
 <p>32 πολλὰ ἀπόκρυφά ἐστιν μείζονα τούτων ὀλίγα γὰρ ἑωράκαμεν τῶν ἔργων αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>33 πάντα γὰρ ἐποίησεν ὁ κύριος καὶ τοῖς εὐσεβέσιν ἔδωκεν σοφίαν </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir043.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Altitudinis firmamentum pulchritudo ejus est,<br> species cæli in visione gloriæ.<br></p>
 <p>2 Sol in aspectu annuntians in exitu,<br> vas admirabile, opus Excelsi.<br></p>
 <p>3 In meridiano exurit terram,<br> et in conspectu ardoris ejus quis poterit sustinere?<br> fornacem custodiens in operibus ardoris:<br></p>
 <p>4 tripliciter sol exurens montes,<br> radios igneos exsufflans,<br> et refulgens radiis suis obcæcat oculos.<br></p>
 <p>5 Magnus Dominus qui fecit illum,<br> et in sermonibus ejus festinavit iter.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>6 [Et luna in omnibus in tempore suo,<br> ostensio temporis, et signum ævi.<br></p>
 <p>7 A luna signum diei festi:<br> luminare quod minuitur in consummatione.<br></p>
 <p>8 Mensis secundum nomen ejus est,<br> crescens mirabiliter in consummatione.<br></p>
 <p>9 Vas castrorum in excelsis,<br> in firmamento cæli resplendens gloriose.<br></p>
 <p>10 Species cæli gloria stellarum:<br> mundum illuminans in excelsis Dominus.<br></p>
 <p>11 In verbis Sancti stabunt ad judicium,<br> et non deficient in vigiliis suis.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>12 [Vide arcum, et benedic eum qui fecit illum:<br> valde speciosus est in splendore suo.<br></p>
 <p>13 Gyravit cælum in circuitu gloriæ suæ:<br> manus Excelsi aperuerunt illum.<br></p>
 <p>14 Imperio suo acceleravit nivem,<br> et accelerat coruscationes emittere judicii sui.<br></p>
 <p>15 Propterea aperti sunt thesauri,<br> et evolaverunt nebulæ sicut aves.<br></p>
 <p>16 In magnitudine sua posuit nubes,<br> et confracti sunt lapides grandinis.<br></p>
 <p>17 In conspectu ejus commovebuntur montes,<br> et in voluntate ejus aspirabit notus.<br></p>
 <p>18 Vox tonitrui ejus verberavit terram,<br> tempestas aquilonis, et congregatio spiritus:<br></p>
 <p>19 et sicut avis deponens ad sedendum, aspergit nivem,<br> et sicut locusta demergens descensus ejus.<br></p>
 <p>20 Pulchritudinem candoris ejus admirabitur oculus,<br> et super imbrem ejus expavescet cor.<br></p>
 <p>21 Gelu sicut salem effundet super terram:<br> et dum gelaverit, fiet tamquam cacumina tribuli.<br></p>
 <p>22 Frigidus ventus aquilo flavit,<br> et gelavit crystallus ab aqua:<br> super omnem congregationem aquarum requiescet,<br> et sicut lorica induet se aquis:<br></p>
 <p>23 et devorabit montes, et exuret desertum,<br> et extinguet viride, sicut igne.<br></p>
 <p>24 Medicina omnium in festinatione nebulæ:<br> et ros obvians ab ardore venienti humilem efficiet eum.<br></p>
 <p>25 In sermone ejus siluit ventus,<br> et cogitatione sua placavit abyssum:<br> et plantavit in illa Dominus insulas.<br></p>
 <p>26 Qui navigant mare enarrent pericula ejus,<br> et audientes auribus nostris admirabimur.<br></p>
 <p>27 Illic præclara opera et mirabilia,<br> varia bestiarum genera, et omnium pecorum, et creatura belluarum.<br></p>
 <p>28 Propter ipsum confirmatus est itineris finis,<br> et in sermone ejus composita sunt omnia.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>29 [Multa dicemus, et deficiemus in verbis:<br> consummatio autem sermonum ipse est in omnibus.<br></p>
 <p>30 Gloriantes ad quid valebimus?<br> ipse enim omnipotens super omnia opera sua.<br></p>
 <p>31 Terribilis Dominus, et magnus vehementer,<br> et mirabilis potentia ipsius.<br></p>
 <p>32 Glorificantes Dominum quantumcumque potueritis,<br> supervalebit enim adhuc: et admirabilis magnificentia ejus.<br></p>
 <p>33 Benedicentes Dominum, exaltate illum quantum potestis:<br> major enim est omni laude.<br></p>
 <p>34 Exaltantes eum, replemini virtute, ne laboretis,<br> non enim comprehendetis.<br></p>
 <p>35 Quis videbit eum et enarrabit?<br> et quis magnificabit eum sicut est ab initio?<br></p>
 <p>36 Multa abscondita sunt majora his:<br> pauca enim vidimus operum ejus.<br></p>
 <p>37 Omnia autem Dominus fecit,<br> et pie agentibus dedit sapientiam.]</p>
