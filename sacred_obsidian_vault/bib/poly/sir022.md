 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 22</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir022.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 A slothful man is compared to a filthy stone, and every one will hiss him out to his disgrace. </p>
 <p>2 A slothful man is compared to the filth of a dunghill: every man that takes it up will shake his hand. </p>
 <p>3 An evilnurtured man is the dishonour of his father that begat him: and a [foolish] daughter is born to his loss. </p>
 <p>4 A wise daughter shall bring an inheritance to her husband: but she that liveth dishonestly is her father's heaviness. </p>
 <p>5 She that is bold dishonoureth both her father and her husband, but they both shall despise her. </p>
 <p>6 A tale out of season [is as] musick in mourning: but stripes and correction of wisdom are never out of time. </p>
 <p>7 Whoso teacheth a fool is as one that glueth a potsherd together, and as he that waketh one from a sound sleep. </p>
 <p>8 He that telleth a tale to a fool speaketh to one in a slumber: when he hath told his tale, he will say, What is the matter? </p>
 <p>9 If children live honestly, and have wherewithal, they shall cover the baseness of their parents. </p>
 <p>10 But children, being haughty, through disdain and want of nurture do stain the nobility of their kindred. </p>
 <p>11 Weep for the dead, for he hath lost the light: and weep for the fool, for he wanteth understanding: make little weeping for the dead, for he is at rest: but the life of the fool is worse than death. </p>
 <p>12 Seven days do men mourn for him that is dead; but for a fool and an ungodly man all the days of his life. </p>
 <p>13 Talk not much with a fool, and go not to him that hath no understanding: beware of him, lest thou have trouble, and thou shalt never be defiled with his fooleries: depart from him, and thou shalt find rest, and never be disquieted with madness. </p>
 <p>14 What is heavier than lead? and what is the name thereof, but a fool? </p>
 <p>15 Sand, and salt, and a mass of iron, is easier to bear, than a man without understanding. </p>
 <p>16 As timber girt and bound together in a building cannot be loosed with shaking: so the heart that is stablished by advised counsel shall fear at no time. </p>
 <p>17 A heart settled upon a thought of understanding is as a fair plaistering on the wall of a gallery. </p>
 <p>18 Pales set on an high place will never stand against the wind: so a fearful heart in the imagination of a fool cannot stand against any fear. </p>
 <p>19 He that pricketh the eye will make tears to fall: and he that pricketh the heart maketh it to shew her knowledge. </p>
 <p>20 Whoso casteth a stone at the birds frayeth them away: and he that upbraideth his friend breaketh friendship. </p>
 <p>21 Though thou drewest a sword at thy friend, yet despair not: for there may be a returning [to favour.] </p>
 <p>22 If thou hast opened thy mouth against thy friend, fear not; for there may be a reconciliation: except for upbraiding, or pride, or disclosing of secrets, or a treacherous wound: for for these things every friend will depart. </p>
 <p>23 Be faithful to thy neighbour in his poverty, that thou mayest rejoice in his prosperity: abide stedfast unto him in the time of his trouble, that thou mayest be heir with him in his heritage: for a mean estate is not always to be contemned: nor the rich that is foolish to be had in admiration. </p>
 <p>24 As the vapour and smoke of a furnace goeth before the fire; so reviling before blood. </p>
 <p>25 I will not be ashamed to defend a friend; neither will I hide myself from him. </p>
 <p>26 And if any evil happen unto me by him, every one that heareth it will beware of him. </p>
 <p>27 Who shall set a watch before my mouth, and a seal of wisdom upon my lips, that I fall not suddenly by them, and that my tongue destroy me not? </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir022.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 λίθῳ ἠρδαλωμένῳ συνεβλήθη ὀκνηρός καὶ πᾶς ἐκσυριεῖ ἐπὶ τῇ ἀτιμίᾳ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>2 βολβίτῳ κοπρίων συνεβλήθη ὀκνηρός πᾶς ὁ ἀναιρούμενος αὐτὸν ἐκτινάξει χεῖρα </p>
 <p>3 αἰσχύνη πατρὸς ἐν γεννήσει ἀπαιδεύτου θυγάτηρ δὲ ἐπ᾽ ἐλαττώσει γίνεται </p>
 <p>4 θυγάτηρ φρονίμη κληρονομήσει ἄνδρα αὐτῆς καὶ ἡ καταισχύνουσα εἰς λύπην γεννήσαντος </p>
 <p>5 πατέρα καὶ ἄνδρα καταισχύνει ἡ θρασεῖα καὶ ὑπὸ ἀμφοτέρων ἀτιμασθήσεται </p>
 <p>6 μουσικὰ ἐν πένθει ἄκαιρος διήγησις μάστιγες δὲ καὶ παιδεία ἐν παντὶ καιρῷ σοφίας </p>
 <p>9 συγκολλῶν ὄστρακον ὁ διδάσκων μωρόν ἐξεγείρων καθεύδοντα ἐκ βαθέος ὕπνου </p>
 <p>10 διηγούμενος νυστάζοντι ὁ διηγούμενος μωρῷ καὶ ἐπὶ συντελείᾳ ἐρεῖ τί ἐστιν </p>
 <p>11 ἐπὶ νεκρῷ κλαῦσον ἐξέλιπεν γὰρ φῶς καὶ ἐπὶ μωρῷ κλαῦσον ἐξέλιπεν γὰρ σύνεσιν ἥδιον κλαῦσον ἐπὶ νεκρῷ ὅτι ἀνεπαύσατο τοῦ δὲ μωροῦ ὑπὲρ θάνατον ἡ ζωὴ πονηρά </p>
 <p>12 πένθος νεκροῦ ἑπτὰ ἡμέραι μωροῦ δὲ καὶ ἀσεβοῦς πᾶσαι αἱ ἡμέραι τῆς ζωῆς αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>13 μετὰ ἄφρονος μὴ πληθύνῃς λόγον καὶ πρὸς ἀσύνετον μὴ πορεύου φύλαξαι ἀπ᾽ αὐτοῦ ἵνα μὴ κόπον ἔχῃς καὶ οὐ μὴ μολυνθῇς ἐν τῷ ἐντιναγμῷ αὐτοῦ ἔκκλινον ἀπ᾽ αὐτοῦ καὶ εὑρήσεις ἀνάπαυσιν καὶ οὐ μὴ ἀκηδιάσῃς ἐν τῇ ἀπονοίᾳ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>14 ὑπὲρ μόλιβον τί βαρυνθήσεται καὶ τί αὐτῷ ὄνομα ἀλλ᾽ ἢ μωρός </p>
 <p>15 ἄμμον καὶ ἅλα καὶ βῶλον σιδήρου εὔκοπον ὑπενεγκεῖν ἢ ἄνθρωπον ἀσύνετον </p>
 <p>16 ἱμάντωσις ξυλίνη ἐνδεδεμένη εἰς οἰκοδομὴν ἐν συσσεισμῷ οὐ διαλυθήσεται οὕτως καρδία ἐστηριγμένη ἐπὶ διανοήματος βουλῆς ἐν καιρῷ οὐ δειλιάσει </p>
 <p>17 καρδία ἡδρασμένη ἐπὶ διανοίας συνέσεως ὡς κόσμος ψαμμωτὸς τοίχου ξυστοῦ </p>
 <p>18 χάρακες ἐπὶ μετεώρου κείμενοι κατέναντι ἀνέμου οὐ μὴ ὑπομείνωσιν οὕτως καρδία δειλὴ ἐπὶ διανοήματος μωροῦ κατέναντι παντὸς φόβου οὐ μὴ ὑπομείνῃ </p>
 <p>19 ὁ νύσσων ὀφθαλμὸν κατάξει δάκρυα καὶ νύσσων καρδίαν ἐκφαίνει αἴσθησιν </p>
 <p>20 βάλλων λίθον ἐπὶ πετεινὰ ἀποσοβεῖ αὐτά καὶ ὁ ὀνειδίζων φίλον διαλύσει φιλίαν </p>
 <p>21 ἐπὶ φίλον ἐὰν σπάσῃς ῥομφαίαν μὴ ἀφελπίσῃς ἔστιν γὰρ ἐπάνοδος </p>
 <p>22 ἐπὶ φίλον ἐὰν ἀνοίξῃς στόμα μὴ εὐλαβηθῇς ἔστιν γὰρ διαλλαγή πλὴν ὀνειδισμοῦ καὶ ὑπερηφανίας καὶ μυστηρίου ἀποκαλύψεως καὶ πληγῆς δολίας ἐν τούτοις ἀποφεύξεται πᾶς φίλος </p>
 <p>23 πίστιν κτῆσαι ἐν πτωχείᾳ μετὰ τοῦ πλησίον ἵνα ἐν τοῖς ἀγαθοῖς αὐτοῦ ὁμοῦ πλησθῇς ἐν καιρῷ θλίψεως διάμενε αὐτῷ ἵνα ἐν τῇ κληρονομίᾳ αὐτοῦ συγκληρονομήσῃς </p>
 <p>24 πρὸ πυρὸς ἀτμὶς καμίνου καὶ καπνός οὕτως πρὸ αἱμάτων λοιδορίαι </p>
 <p>25 φίλον σκεπάσαι οὐκ αἰσχυνθήσομαι καὶ ἀπὸ προσώπου αὐτοῦ οὐ μὴ κρυβῶ </p>
 <p>26 καὶ εἰ κακά μοι συμβήσεται δι᾽ αὐτόν πᾶς ὁ ἀκούων φυλάξεται ἀπ᾽ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>27 τίς δώσει ἐπὶ στόμα μου φυλακὴν καὶ ἐπὶ τῶν χειλέων μου σφραγῖδα πανοῦργον ἵνα μὴ πέσω ἀπ᾽ αὐτῆς καὶ ἡ γλῶσσά μου ἀπολέσῃ με </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir022.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [In lapide luteo lapidatus est piger:<br> et omnes loquentur super aspernationem illius.<br></p>
 <p>2 De stercore boum lapidatus est piger:<br> et omnis qui tetigerit eum excutiet manus.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>3 [Confusio patris est de filio indisciplinato:<br> filia autem in deminoratione fiet.<br></p>
 <p>4 Filia prudens hæreditas viro suo:<br> nam quæ confundit, in contumeliam fit genitoris.<br></p>
 <p>5 Patrem et virum confundit audax,<br> et ab impiis non minorabitur:<br> ab utrisque autem inhonorabitur.<br></p>
 <p>6 Musica in luctu importuna narratio:<br> flagella et doctrina in omni tempore sapientia.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>7 [Qui docet fatuum,<br> quasi qui conglutinat testam.<br></p>
 <p>8 Qui narrat verbum non audienti,<br> quasi qui excitat dormientem de gravi somno.<br></p>
 <p>9 Cum dormiente loquitur qui enarrat stulto sapientiam:<br> et in fine narrationis dicit: Quis est hic?<br></p>
 <p>10 Supra mortuum plora, defecit enim lux ejus:<br> et supra fatuum plora, defecit enim sensus.<br></p>
 <p>11 Modicum plora super mortuum, quoniam requievit:<br></p>
 <p>12 nequissimi enim nequissima vita super mortem fatui.<br></p>
 <p>13 Luctus mortui septem dies:<br> fatui autem et impii omnes dies vitæ illorum.<br></p>
 <p>14 Cum stulto ne multum loquaris,<br> et cum insensato ne abieris.<br></p>
 <p>15 Serva te ab illo, ut non molestiam habeas,<br> et non coinquinaberis peccato illius.<br></p>
 <p>16 Deflecte ab illo, et invenies requiem,<br> et non acediaberis in stultitia illius.<br></p>
 <p>17 Super plumbum quid gravabitur?<br> et quod illi aliud nomen quam fatuus?<br></p>
 <p>18 Arenam, et salem, et massam ferri facilius est ferre<br> quam hominem imprudentem, et fatuum, et impium.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>19 [Loramentum ligneum colligatum in fundamento ædificii non dissolvetur,<br> sic et cor confirmatum in cogitatione consilii.<br></p>
 <p>20 Cogitatus sensati in omni tempore metu non depravabitur.<br></p>
 <p>21 Sicut pali in excelsis, et cæmenta sine impensa posita,<br> contra faciem venti non permanebunt:<br></p>
 <p>22 sic et cor timidum in cogitatione stulti<br> contra impetum timoris non resistet.<br></p>
 <p>23 Sicut cor trepidum in cogitatione fatui omni tempore non metuet,<br> sic et qui in præceptis Dei permanet semper.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>24 [Pungens oculum deducit lacrimas,<br> et qui pungit cor profert sensum.<br></p>
 <p>25 Mittens lapidem in volatilia, dejiciet illa:<br> sic et qui conviciatur amico, dissolvit amicitiam.<br></p>
 <p>26 Ad amicum etsi produxeris gladium, non desperes:<br> est enim regressus.<br> Ad amicum</p>
 <p>27 si aperueris os triste, non timeas:<br> est enim concordatio:<br> excepto convitio, et improperio, et superbia,<br> et mysterii revelatione, et plaga dolosa:<br> in his omnibus effugiet amicus.<br></p>
 <p>28 Fidem posside cum amico in paupertate illius,<br> ut et in bonis illius læteris.<br></p>
 <p>29 In tempore tribulationis illius permane illi fidelis,<br> ut et in hæreditate illius cohæres sis.<br></p>
 <p>30 Ante ignem camini vapor et fumus ignis inaltatur:<br> sic et ante sanguinem maledicta, et contumeliæ, et minæ.<br></p>
 <p>31 Amicum salutare non confundar,<br> a facie illius non me abscondam:<br> et si mala mihi evenerint per illum, sustinebo.<br></p>
 <p>32 Omnis qui audiet cavebit se ab eo.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>33 [Quis dabit ori meo custodiam,<br> et super labia mea signaculum certum,<br> ut non cadam ab ipsis,<br> et lingua mea perdat me?]</p>
