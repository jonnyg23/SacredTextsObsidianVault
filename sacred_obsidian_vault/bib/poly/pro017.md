 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro017.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Better<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> is a dry<sup><a href="h2720.htm">2720</a></sup> morsel,<sup><a href="h6595.htm">6595</a></sup> and quietness<sup><a href="h7962.htm">7962</a></sup> therewith, than a house<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> full<sup><a href="h4392.htm">4392</a></sup> of sacrifices<sup><a href="h2077.htm">2077</a></sup> with strife.<sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  A wise<sup><a href="h7919.htm">7919</a></sup> servant<sup><a href="h5650.htm">5650</a></sup> shall have rule<sup><a href="h4910.htm">4910</a></sup> over a son<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> that causeth shame,<sup><a href="h0954.htm">954</a></sup> and shall have part<sup><a href="h2505.htm">2505</a></sup> of the inheritance<sup><a href="h5159.htm">5159</a></sup> among<sup><a href="h8432.htm">8432</a></sup> the brethren.<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  The refining pot<sup><a href="h4715.htm">4715</a></sup> is for silver,<sup><a href="h3701.htm">3701</a></sup> and the furnace<sup><a href="h3564.htm">3564</a></sup> for gold:<sup><a href="h2091.htm">2091</a></sup> but the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> trieth<sup><a href="h0974.htm">974</a></sup> the hearts.<sup><a href="h3826.htm">3826</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  A wicked doer<sup><a href="h7489.htm">7489</a></sup> giveth heed<sup><a href="h7181.htm">7181</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> false<sup><a href="h0205.htm">205</a></sup> lips;<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> and a liar<sup><a href="h8267.htm">8267</a></sup> giveth ear<sup><a href="h0238.htm">238</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> a naughty<sup><a href="h1942.htm">1942</a></sup> tongue.<sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  Whoso mocketh<sup><a href="h3932.htm">3932</a></sup> the poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> reproacheth<sup><a href="h2778.htm">2778</a></sup> his Maker:<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> and he that is glad<sup><a href="h8056.htm">8056</a></sup> at calamities<sup><a href="h0343.htm">343</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> be unpunished.<sup><a href="h5352.htm">5352</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  Children's<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> children<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> are the crown<sup><a href="h5850.htm">5850</a></sup> of old men;<sup><a href="h2205.htm">2205</a></sup> and the glory<sup><a href="h8597.htm">8597</a></sup> of children<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> are their fathers.<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  Excellent<sup><a href="h3499.htm">3499</a></sup> speech<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> becometh<sup><a href="h5000.htm">5000</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> a fool:<sup><a href="h5036.htm">5036</a></sup> much less<sup><a href="h0637.htm">637</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> do lying<sup><a href="h8267.htm">8267</a></sup> lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> a prince.<sup><a href="h5081.htm">5081</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  A gift<sup><a href="h7810.htm">7810</a></sup> is as a precious<sup><a href="h2580.htm">2580</a></sup> stone<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> in the eyes<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> of him that hath<sup><a href="h1167.htm">1167</a></sup> it: whithersoever<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> it turneth,<sup><a href="h6437.htm">6437</a></sup> it prospereth.<sup><a href="h7919.htm">7919</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  He that covereth<sup><a href="h3680.htm">3680</a></sup> a transgression<sup><a href="h6588.htm">6588</a></sup> seeketh<sup><a href="h1245.htm">1245</a></sup> love;<sup><a href="h0160.htm">160</a></sup> but he that repeateth<sup><a href="h8138.htm">8138</a></sup> a matter<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> separateth<sup><a href="h6504.htm">6504</a></sup> very friends.<sup><a href="h0441.htm">441</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  A reproof<sup><a href="h1606.htm">1606</a></sup> entereth more<sup><a href="h5181.htm">5181</a></sup> into a wise<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> man than a hundred<sup><a href="h3967.htm">3967</a></sup> stripes<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> into a fool.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  An evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> man seeketh<sup><a href="h1245.htm">1245</a></sup> only<sup><a href="h0389.htm">389</a></sup> rebellion:<sup><a href="h4805.htm">4805</a></sup> therefore a cruel<sup><a href="h0394.htm">394</a></sup> messenger<sup><a href="h4397.htm">4397</a></sup> shall be sent<sup><a href="h7971.htm">7971</a></sup> against him. </p>
 <p>12  Let a bear<sup><a href="h1677.htm">1677</a></sup> robbed<sup><a href="h7909.htm">7909</a></sup> of her whelps meet<sup><a href="h6298.htm">6298</a></sup> a man,<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> rather<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> than a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> in his folly.<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  Whoso rewardeth<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h8478.htm">8478</a></sup> good,<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> depart<sup><a href="h4185.htm">4185</a></sup> from his house.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  The beginning<sup><a href="h7225.htm">7225</a></sup> of strife<sup><a href="h4066.htm">4066</a></sup> is as when one letteth out<sup><a href="h6362.htm">6362</a></sup> water:<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> therefore leave off<sup><a href="h5203.htm">5203</a></sup> contention,<sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> before<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> it be meddled with.<sup><a href="h1566.htm">1566</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  He that justifieth<sup><a href="h6663.htm">6663</a></sup> the wicked,<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> and he that condemneth<sup><a href="h7561.htm">7561</a></sup> the just,<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> even<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> they both<sup><a href="h8147.htm">8147</a></sup> are abomination<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> to the LORD.<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  Wherefore<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2088.htm">2088</a></sup> is there a price<sup><a href="h4242.htm">4242</a></sup> in the hand<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> to get<sup><a href="h7069.htm">7069</a></sup> wisdom,<sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> seeing he hath no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> to it? </p>
 <p>17  A friend<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> loveth<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> at all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> times,<sup><a href="h6256.htm">6256</a></sup> and a brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> is born<sup><a href="h3205.htm">3205</a></sup> for adversity.<sup><a href="h6869.htm">6869</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  A man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> void<sup><a href="h2638.htm">2638</a></sup> of understanding<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> striketh<sup><a href="h8628.htm">8628</a></sup> hands,<sup><a href="h3709.htm">3709</a></sup> and becometh<sup><a href="h6148.htm">6148</a></sup> surety<sup><a href="h6161.htm">6161</a></sup> in the presence<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> of his friend.<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  He loveth<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> transgression<sup><a href="h6588.htm">6588</a></sup> that loveth<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> strife:<sup><a href="h4683.htm">4683</a></sup> and he that exalteth<sup><a href="h1361.htm">1361</a></sup> his gate<sup><a href="h6607.htm">6607</a></sup> seeketh<sup><a href="h1245.htm">1245</a></sup> destruction.<sup><a href="h7667.htm">7667</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  He that hath a froward<sup><a href="h6141.htm">6141</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> findeth<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> no<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good:<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> and he that hath a perverse<sup><a href="h2015.htm">2015</a></sup> tongue<sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> falleth<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> into mischief.<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  He that begetteth<sup><a href="h3205.htm">3205</a></sup> a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> doeth it to his sorrow:<sup><a href="h8424.htm">8424</a></sup> and the father<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> of a fool<sup><a href="h5036.htm">5036</a></sup> hath no<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> joy.<sup><a href="h8055.htm">8055</a></sup> </p>
 <p>22  A merry<sup><a href="h8056.htm">8056</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> doeth good<sup><a href="h3190.htm">3190</a></sup> like a medicine:<sup><a href="h1456.htm">1456</a></sup> but a broken<sup><a href="h5218.htm">5218</a></sup> spirit<sup><a href="h7307.htm">7307</a></sup> drieth<sup><a href="h3001.htm">3001</a></sup> the bones.<sup><a href="h1634.htm">1634</a></sup> </p>
 <p>23  A wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> man taketh<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> a gift<sup><a href="h7810.htm">7810</a></sup> out of the bosom<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2436.htm">2436</a></sup> to pervert<sup><a href="h5186.htm">5186</a></sup> the ways<sup><a href="h0734.htm">734</a></sup> of judgment.<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> </p>
 <p>24  Wisdom<sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> is before<sup><a href="h0854.htm">854</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> him that hath understanding;<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> but the eyes<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> of a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> are in the ends<sup><a href="h7097.htm">7097</a></sup> of the earth.<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> </p>
 <p>25  A foolish<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> son<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> is a grief<sup><a href="h3708.htm">3708</a></sup> to his father,<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> and bitterness<sup><a href="h4470.htm">4470</a></sup> to her that bore<sup><a href="h3205.htm">3205</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>26  Also<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> to punish<sup><a href="h6064.htm">6064</a></sup> the just<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good,<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> nor to strike<sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> princes<sup><a href="h5081.htm">5081</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> equity.<sup><a href="h3476.htm">3476</a></sup> </p>
 <p>27  He that hath knowledge<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> spareth<sup><a href="h2820.htm">2820</a></sup> his words:<sup><a href="h0561.htm">561</a></sup> and a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> of understanding<sup><a href="h8394.htm">8394</a></sup> is of an excellent<sup><a href="h3368.htm">3368</a></sup> spirit.<sup><a href="h7307.htm">7307</a></sup> </p>
 <p>28  Even<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> a fool,<sup><a href="h0191.htm">191</a></sup> when he holdeth his peace,<sup><a href="h2790.htm">2790</a></sup> is counted<sup><a href="h2803.htm">2803</a></sup> wise:<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> and he that shutteth<sup><a href="h0331.htm">331</a></sup> his lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> is esteemed a man of understanding.<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> </p>

 <p>15 ὃς δίκαιον κρίνει τὸν ἄδικον ἄδικον δὲ τὸν δίκαιον ἀκάθαρτος καὶ βδελυκτὸς παρὰ θεῷ </p>

 <p>17 εἰς πάντα καιρὸν φίλος ὑπαρχέτω σοι ἀδελφοὶ δὲ ἐν ἀνάγκαις χρήσιμοι ἔστωσαν τούτου γὰρ χάριν γεννῶνται </p>
 <p>18 ἀνὴρ ἄφρων ἐπικροτεῖ καὶ ἐπιχαίρει ἑαυτῷ ὡς καὶ ὁ ἐγγυώμενος ἐγγύῃ τὸν ἑαυτοῦ φίλον </p>
 <p>19 φιλαμαρτήμων χαίρει μάχαις </p>
 <p>20 ὁ δὲ σκληροκάρδιος οὐ συναντᾷ ἀγαθοῖς ἀνὴρ εὐμετάβολος γλώσσῃ ἐμπεσεῖται εἰς κακά </p>
 <p>21 καρδία δὲ ἄφρονος ὀδύνη τῷ κεκτημένῳ αὐτήν οὐκ εὐφραίνεται πατὴρ ἐπὶ υἱῷ ἀπαιδεύτῳ υἱὸς δὲ φρόνιμος εὐφραίνει μητέρα αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>22 καρδία εὐφραινομένη εὐεκτεῖν ποιεῖ ἀνδρὸς δὲ λυπηροῦ ξηραίνεται τὰ ὀστᾶ </p>
 <p>23 λαμβάνοντος δῶρα ἐν κόλπῳ ἀδίκως οὐ κατευοδοῦνται ὁδοί ἀσεβὴς δὲ ἐκκλίνει ὁδοὺς δικαιοσύνης </p>
 <p>24 πρόσωπον συνετὸν ἀνδρὸς σοφοῦ οἱ δὲ ὀφθαλμοὶ τοῦ ἄφρονος ἐπ᾽ ἄκρα γῆς </p>
 <p>25 ὀργὴ πατρὶ υἱὸς ἄφρων καὶ ὀδύνη τῇ τεκούσῃ αὐτοῦ </p>

 <p>27 ὃς φείδεται ῥῆμα προέσθαι σκληρόν ἐπιγνώμων μακρόθυμος δὲ ἀνὴρ φρόνιμος </p>
 <p>28 ἀνοήτῳ ἐπερωτήσαντι σοφίαν σοφία λογισθήσεται ἐνεὸν δέ τις ἑαυτὸν ποιήσας δόξει φρόνιμος εἶναι </p>

 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎7 ‏לֹא־נָאוָ֣ה לְנָבָ֣ל שְׂפַת־יֶ֑תֶר אַ֝֗ף כִּֽי־לְנָדִ֥יב שְׂפַת־שָֽׁקֶר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎8 ‏אֶֽבֶן־חֵ֣ן הַ֭שֹּׁחַד בְּעֵינֵ֣י בְעָלָ֑יו אֶֽל־כָּל־אֲשֶׁ֖ר יִפְנֶ֣ה יַשְׂכִּֽיל׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎9 ‏מְֽכַסֶּה־פֶּ֭שַׁע מְבַקֵּ֣שׁ אַהֲבָ֑ה וְשֹׁנֶ֥ה בְ֝דָבָ֗ר מַפְרִ֥יד אַלּֽוּף׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎10 ‏תֵּ֣חַת גְּעָרָ֣ה בְמֵבִ֑ין מֵהַכּ֖וֹת כְּסִ֣יל מֵאָֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎11 ‏אַךְ־מְרִ֥י יְבַקֶּשׁ־רָ֑ע וּמַלְאָ֥ךְ אַ֝כְזָרִ֗י יְשֻׁלַּח־בּֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎12 ‏פָּג֬וֹשׁ דֹּ֣ב שַׁכּ֣וּל בְּאִ֑ישׁ וְאַל־כְּ֝סִ֗יל בְּאִוַּלְתּֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎13 ‏מֵשִׁ֣יב רָ֭עָה תַּ֣חַת טוֹבָ֑ה לֹא־<font color="brown">תמיש</font> <font color="blue">תָמ֥וּשׁ</font> רָ֝עָ֗ה מִבֵּיתֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎14 ‏פּ֣וֹטֵֽר מַ֭יִם רֵאשִׁ֣ית מָד֑וֹן וְלִפְנֵ֥י הִ֝תְגַּלַּ֗ע הָרִ֥יב<font color="red"><sup>3</sup></font> נְטֽוֹשׁ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎15 ‏מַצְדִּ֣יק רָ֭שָׁע וּמַרְשִׁ֣יעַ צַדִּ֑יק תּוֹעֲבַ֥ת יְ֝הוָ֗ה גַּם־שְׁנֵיהֶֽם׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎16 ‏לָמָּה־זֶּ֣ה מְחִ֣יר בְּיַד־כְּסִ֑יל לִקְנ֖וֹת חָכְמָ֣ה וְלֶב־אָֽיִן׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎17 ‏בְּכָל־עֵ֭ת אֹהֵ֣ב הָרֵ֑עַ וְאָ֥ח לְ֝צָרָ֗ה יִוָּלֵֽד׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎18 ‏אָדָ֣ם חֲסַר־לֵ֭ב תּוֹקֵ֣עַ כָּ֑ף עֹרֵ֥ב עֲ֝רֻבָּ֗ה לִפְנֵ֥י רֵעֵֽהוּ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎19 ‏אֹ֣הֵֽב פֶּ֭שַׁע אֹהֵ֣ב מַצָּ֑ה מַגְבִּ֥יהַּ פִּ֝תְח֗וֹ מְבַקֶּשׁ־שָֽׁבֶר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎20 ‏עִקֶּשׁ־לֵ֭ב לֹ֣א יִמְצָא־ט֑וֹב וְנֶהְפָּ֥ךְ בִּ֝לְשׁוֹנ֗וֹ יִפּ֥וֹל בְּרָעָֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎21 ‏יֹלֵ֣ד כְּ֭סִיל לְת֣וּגָה ל֑וֹ וְלֹֽא־יִ֝שְׂמַ֗ח אֲבִ֣י נָבָֽל׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎22 ‏לֵ֣ב שָׂ֭מֵחַ יֵיטִ֣ב גֵּהָ֑ה וְר֥וּחַ נְ֝כֵאָ֗ה תְּיַבֶּשׁ־גָּֽרֶם׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎23 ‏שֹׁ֣חַד מֵ֭חֵיק רָשָׁ֣ע יִקָּ֑ח לְ֝הַטּ֗וֹת אָרְח֥וֹת מִשְׁפָּֽט׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎24 ‏אֶת־פְּנֵ֣י מֵבִ֣ין חָכְמָ֑ה וְעֵינֵ֥י כְ֝סִ֗יל בִּקְצֵה־אָֽרֶץ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎25 ‏כַּ֣עַס לְ֭אָבִיו בֵּ֣ן כְּסִ֑יל וּ֝מֶ֗מֶר לְיוֹלַדְתּֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎26 ‏גַּ֤ם עֲנ֣וֹשׁ לַצַּדִּ֣יק לֹא־ט֑וֹב לְהַכּ֖וֹת נְדִיבִ֣ים עַל־יֹֽשֶׁר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎27 ‏חוֹשֵׂ֣ךְ אֲ֭מָרָיו יוֹדֵ֣עַ דָּ֑עַת <font color="brown">וקר־</font> <font color="blue">יְקַר־</font> ר֝֗וּחַ אִ֣ישׁ תְּבוּנָֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎28 ‏גַּ֤ם אֱוִ֣יל מַ֭חֲרִישׁ חָכָ֣ם יֵחָשֵׁ֑ב אֹטֵ֖ם שְׂפָתָ֣יו נָבֽוֹן׃ </span></p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro017.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Melior est buccella sicca cum gaudio<br> quam domus plena victimis cum jurgio.<br></p>
 <p>2 Servus sapiens dominabitur filiis stultis,<br> et inter fratres hæreditatem dividet.<br></p>
 <p>3 Sicut igne probatur argentum et aurum camino,<br> ita corda probat Dominus.<br></p>
 <p>4 Malus obedit linguæ iniquæ,<br> et fallax obtemperat labiis mendacibus.<br></p>
 <p>5 Qui despicit pauperem exprobrat factori ejus,<br> et qui ruina lætatur alterius non erit impunitus.<br></p>
 <p>6 Corona senum filii filiorum,<br> et gloria filiorum patres eorum.<br></p>
 <p>7 Non decent stultum verba composita,<br> nec principem labium mentiens.<br></p>
 <p>8 Gemma gratissima exspectatio præstolantis;<br> quocumque se vertit, prudenter intelligit.<br></p>
 <p>9 Qui celat delictum quærit amicitias;<br> qui altero sermone repetit, separat fderatos.<br></p>
 <p>10 Plus proficit correptio apud prudentem,<br> quam centum plagæ apud stultum.<br></p>
 <p>11 Semper jurgia quærit malus:<br> angelus autem crudelis mittetur contra eum.<br></p>
 <p>12 Expedit magis ursæ occurrere raptis ftibus,<br> quam fatuo confidenti in stultitia sua.<br></p>
 <p>13 Qui reddit mala pro bonis,<br> non recedet malum de domo ejus.<br></p>
 <p>14 Qui dimittit aquam caput est jurgiorum,<br> et antequam patiatur contumeliam judicium deserit.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>15 [Qui justificat impium, et qui condemnat justum,<br> abominabilis est uterque apud Deum.<br></p>
 <p>16 Quid prodest stulto habere divitias,<br> cum sapientiam emere non possit?<br> Qui altum facit domum suam quærit ruinam,<br> et qui evitat discere incidet in mala.<br></p>
 <p>17 Omni tempore diligit qui amicus est,<br> et frater in angustiis comprobatur.<br></p>
 <p>18 Stultus homo plaudet manibus,<br> cum spoponderit pro amico suo.<br></p>
 <p>19 Qui meditatur discordias diligit rixas,<br> et qui exaltat ostium quærit ruinam.<br></p>
 <p>20 Qui perversi cordis est non inveniet bonum,<br> et qui vertit linguam incidet in malum.<br></p>
 <p>21 Natus est stultus in ignominiam suam;<br> sed nec pater in fatuo lætabitur.<br></p>
 <p>22 Animus gaudens ætatem floridam facit;<br> spiritus tristis exsiccat ossa.<br></p>
 <p>23 Munera de sinu impius accipit,<br> ut pervertat semitas judicii.<br></p>
 <p>24 In facie prudentis lucet sapientia;<br> oculi stultorum in finibus terræ.<br></p>
 <p>25 Ira patris filius stultus,<br> et dolor matris quæ genuit eum.<br></p>
 <p>26 Non est bonum damnum inferre justo,<br> nec percutere principem qui recta judicat.<br></p>
 <p>27 Qui moderatur sermones suos doctus et prudens est,<br> et pretiosi spiritus vir eruditus.<br></p>
 <p>28 Stultus quoque, si tacuerit, sapiens reputabitur,<br> et si compresserit labia sua, intelligens.]</p>
