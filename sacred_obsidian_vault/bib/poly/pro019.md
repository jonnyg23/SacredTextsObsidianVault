 <h3 align="CENTER">Proverbs 19</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro019.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Better<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> is the poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> that walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> in his integrity,<sup><a href="h8537.htm">8537</a></sup> than he that is perverse<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6141.htm">6141</a></sup> in his lips,<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> and is a fool.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  Also,<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> that the soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> be without<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> knowledge,<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> it is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good;<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> and he that hasteth<sup><a href="h0213.htm">213</a></sup> with his feet<sup><a href="h7272.htm">7272</a></sup> sinneth.<sup><a href="h2398.htm">2398</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  The foolishness<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> perverteth<sup><a href="h5557.htm">5557</a></sup> his way:<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> and his heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> fretteth<sup><a href="h2196.htm">2196</a></sup> against<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the LORD.<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  Wealth<sup><a href="h1952.htm">1952</a></sup> maketh<sup><a href="h3254.htm">3254</a></sup> many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> friends;<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> but the poor<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> is separated<sup><a href="h6504.htm">6504</a></sup> from his neighbor.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  A false<sup><a href="h8267.htm">8267</a></sup> witness<sup><a href="h5707.htm">5707</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> be unpunished,<sup><a href="h5352.htm">5352</a></sup> and he that speaketh<sup><a href="h6315.htm">6315</a></sup> lies<sup><a href="h3577.htm">3577</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> escape.<sup><a href="h4422.htm">4422</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  Many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> will entreat<sup><a href="h2470.htm">2470</a></sup> the favor<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> of the prince:<sup><a href="h5081.htm">5081</a></sup> and every man<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> is a friend<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> to him that giveth gifts.<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4976.htm">4976</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  All<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> the brethren<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> of the poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> do hate<sup><a href="h8130.htm">8130</a></sup> him: how much more<sup><a href="h0637.htm">637</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> do his friends<sup><a href="h4828.htm">4828</a></sup> go far<sup><a href="h7368.htm">7368</a></sup> from<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> him? he pursueth<sup><a href="h7291.htm">7291</a></sup> them with words,<sup><a href="h0561.htm">561</a></sup> yet they<sup><a href="h1992.htm">1992</a></sup> are wanting<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> to him. </p>
 <p>8  He that getteth<sup><a href="h7069.htm">7069</a></sup> wisdom<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> loveth<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> his own soul:<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> he that keepeth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> understanding<sup><a href="h8394.htm">8394</a></sup> shall find<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> good.<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  A false<sup><a href="h8267.htm">8267</a></sup> witness<sup><a href="h5707.htm">5707</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> be unpunished,<sup><a href="h5352.htm">5352</a></sup> and he that speaketh<sup><a href="h6315.htm">6315</a></sup> lies<sup><a href="h3577.htm">3577</a></sup> shall perish.<sup><a href="h0006.htm">6</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  Delight<sup><a href="h8588.htm">8588</a></sup> is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> seemly<sup><a href="h5000.htm">5000</a></sup> for a fool;<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> much less<sup><a href="h0637.htm">637</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> a servant<sup><a href="h5650.htm">5650</a></sup> to have rule<sup><a href="h4910.htm">4910</a></sup> over princes.<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  The discretion<sup><a href="h7922.htm">7922</a></sup> of a man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> deferreth<sup><a href="h0748.htm">748</a></sup> his anger;<sup><a href="h0639.htm">639</a></sup> and it is his glory<sup><a href="h8597.htm">8597</a></sup> to pass<sup><a href="h5674.htm">5674</a></sup> over<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> a transgression.<sup><a href="h6588.htm">6588</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  The king's<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> wrath<sup><a href="h2197.htm">2197</a></sup> is as the roaring<sup><a href="h5099.htm">5099</a></sup> of a lion;<sup><a href="h3715.htm">3715</a></sup> but his favor<sup><a href="h7522.htm">7522</a></sup> is as dew<sup><a href="h2919.htm">2919</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the grass.<sup><a href="h6212.htm">6212</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  A foolish<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> son<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> is the calamity<sup><a href="h1942.htm">1942</a></sup> of his father:<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> and the contentions<sup><a href="h4079.htm">4079</a></sup> of a wife<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> are a continual<sup><a href="h2956.htm">2956</a></sup> dropping.<sup><a href="h1812.htm">1812</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  House<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> and riches<sup><a href="h1952.htm">1952</a></sup> are the inheritance<sup><a href="h5159.htm">5159</a></sup> of fathers:<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> and a prudent<sup><a href="h7919.htm">7919</a></sup> wife<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> is from the LORD.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  Slothfulness<sup><a href="h6103.htm">6103</a></sup> casteth<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> into a deep sleep;<sup><a href="h8639.htm">8639</a></sup> and an idle<sup><a href="h7423.htm">7423</a></sup> soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> shall suffer hunger.<sup><a href="h7456.htm">7456</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  He that keepeth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> the commandment<sup><a href="h4687.htm">4687</a></sup> keepeth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> his own soul;<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> but he that despiseth<sup><a href="h0959.htm">959</a></sup> his ways<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> shall die.<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  He that hath pity<sup><a href="h2603.htm">2603</a></sup> upon the poor<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> lendeth<sup><a href="h3867.htm">3867</a></sup> unto the LORD;<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> and that which he hath given<sup><a href="h1576.htm">1576</a></sup> will he pay him again.<sup><a href="h7999.htm">7999</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  Chasten<sup><a href="h3256.htm">3256</a></sup> thy son<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> while<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> there is<sup><a href="h3426.htm">3426</a></sup> hope,<sup><a href="h8615.htm">8615</a></sup> and let not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> thy soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> spare<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> his crying.<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  A man of great<sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> wrath<sup><a href="h2534.htm">2534</a></sup> shall suffer<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> punishment:<sup><a href="h6066.htm">6066</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> if<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> thou deliver<sup><a href="h5337.htm">5337</a></sup> him, yet<sup><a href="h5750.htm">5750</a></sup> thou must do it again.<sup><a href="h3254.htm">3254</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  Hear<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> counsel,<sup><a href="h6098.htm">6098</a></sup> and receive<sup><a href="h6901.htm">6901</a></sup> instruction,<sup><a href="h4148.htm">4148</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h4616.htm">4616</a></sup> thou mayest be wise<sup><a href="h2449.htm">2449</a></sup> in thy latter<sup><a href="h0319.htm">319</a></sup> end. </p>
 <p>21  There are many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> devices<sup><a href="h4284.htm">4284</a></sup> in a man's<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> heart;<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> nevertheless the counsel<sup><a href="h6098.htm">6098</a></sup> of the LORD,<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> shall stand.<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> </p>
 <p>22  The desire<sup><a href="h8378.htm">8378</a></sup> of a man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> is his kindness:<sup><a href="h2617.htm">2617</a></sup> and a poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> is better<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> than a liar.<sup><a href="h3577.htm">3577</a></sup> </p>
 <p>23  The fear<sup><a href="h3374.htm">3374</a></sup> of the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> tendeth to life:<sup><a href="h2416.htm">2416</a></sup> and he that hath it shall abide<sup><a href="h3885.htm">3885</a></sup> satisfied;<sup><a href="h7649.htm">7649</a></sup> he shall not<sup><a href="h1077.htm">1077</a></sup> be visited<sup><a href="h6485.htm">6485</a></sup> with evil.<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> </p>
 <p>24  A slothful<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> man hideth<sup><a href="h2934.htm">2934</a></sup> his hand<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> in his bosom,<sup><a href="h6747.htm">6747</a></sup> and will not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> so much<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> as bring it to his mouth again.<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> </p>
 <p>25  Smite<sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> a scorner,<sup><a href="h3887.htm">3887</a></sup> and the simple<sup><a href="h6612.htm">6612</a></sup> will beware:<sup><a href="h6191.htm">6191</a></sup> and reprove<sup><a href="h3198.htm">3198</a></sup> one that hath understanding,<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> and he will understand<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> knowledge.<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> </p>
 <p>26  He that wasteth<sup><a href="h7703.htm">7703</a></sup> his father,<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> and chaseth away<sup><a href="h1272.htm">1272</a></sup> his mother,<sup><a href="h0517.htm">517</a></sup> is a son<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> that causeth shame,<sup><a href="h0954.htm">954</a></sup> and bringeth reproach.<sup><a href="h2659.htm">2659</a></sup> </p>
 <p>27  Cease,<sup><a href="h2308.htm">2308</a></sup> my son,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> to hear<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> the instruction<sup><a href="h4148.htm">4148</a></sup> that causeth to err<sup><a href="h7686.htm">7686</a></sup> from the words<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0561.htm">561</a></sup> of knowledge.<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> </p>
 <p>28  An ungodly<sup><a href="h1100.htm">1100</a></sup> witness<sup><a href="h5707.htm">5707</a></sup> scorneth<sup><a href="h3887.htm">3887</a></sup> judgment:<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> and the mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> of the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> devoureth<sup><a href="h1104.htm">1104</a></sup> iniquity.<sup><a href="h0205.htm">205</a></sup> </p>
 <p>29  Judgments<sup><a href="h8201.htm">8201</a></sup> are prepared<sup><a href="h3559.htm">3559</a></sup> for scorners,<sup><a href="h3887.htm">3887</a></sup> and stripes<sup><a href="h4112.htm">4112</a></sup> for the back<sup><a href="h1460.htm">1460</a></sup> of fools.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/pro019.htm">Sep</a></p><p>3 ἀφροσύνη ἀνδρὸς λυμαίνεται τὰς ὁδοὺς αὐτοῦ τὸν δὲ θεὸν αἰτιᾶται τῇ καρδίᾳ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>4 πλοῦτος προστίθησιν φίλους πολλούς ὁ δὲ πτωχὸς καὶ ἀπὸ τοῦ ὑπάρχοντος φίλου λείπεται </p>
 <p>5 μάρτυς ψευδὴς οὐκ ἀτιμώρητος ἔσται ὁ δὲ ἐγκαλῶν ἀδίκως οὐ διαφεύξεται </p>
 <p>6 πολλοὶ θεραπεύουσιν πρόσωπα βασιλέων πᾶς δὲ ὁ κακὸς γίνεται ὄνειδος ἀνδρί </p>
 <p>7 πᾶς ὃς ἀδελφὸν πτωχὸν μισεῖ καὶ φιλίας μακρὰν ἔσται ἔννοια ἀγαθὴ τοῖς εἰδόσιν αὐτὴν ἐγγιεῖ ἀνὴρ δὲ φρόνιμος εὑρήσει αὐτήν ὁ πολλὰ κακοποιῶν τελεσιουργεῖ κακίαν ὃς δὲ ἐρεθίζει λόγους οὐ σωθήσεται </p>
 <p>8 ὁ κτώμενος φρόνησιν ἀγαπᾷ ἑαυτόν ὃς δὲ φυλάσσει φρόνησιν εὑρήσει ἀγαθά </p>
 <p>9 μάρτυς ψευδὴς οὐκ ἀτιμώρητος ἔσται ὃς δ᾽ ἂν ἐκκαύσῃ κακίαν ἀπολεῖται ὑπ᾽ αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>10 οὐ συμφέρει ἄφρονι τρυφή καὶ ἐὰν οἰκέτης ἄρξηται μεθ᾽ ὕβρεως δυναστεύειν </p>
 <p>11 ἐλεήμων ἀνὴρ μακροθυμεῖ τὸ δὲ καύχημα αὐτοῦ ἐπέρχεται παρανόμοις </p>
 <p>12 βασιλέως ἀπειλὴ ὁμοία βρυγμῷ λέοντος ὥσπερ δὲ δρόσος ἐπὶ χόρτῳ οὕτως τὸ ἱλαρὸν αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>13 αἰσχύνη πατρὶ υἱὸς ἄφρων καὶ οὐχ ἁγναὶ εὐχαὶ ἀπὸ μισθώματος ἑταίρας </p>
 <p>14 οἶκον καὶ ὕπαρξιν μερίζουσιν πατέρες παισίν παρὰ δὲ θεοῦ ἁρμόζεται γυνὴ ἀνδρί </p>
 <p>15 δειλία κατέχει ἀνδρογύναιον ψυχὴ δὲ ἀεργοῦ πεινάσει </p>
 <p>16 ὃς φυλάσσει ἐντολήν τηρεῖ τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ψυχήν ὁ δὲ καταφρονῶν τῶν ἑαυτοῦ ὁδῶν ἀπολεῖται </p>
 <p>17 δανίζει θεῷ ὁ ἐλεῶν πτωχόν κατὰ δὲ τὸ δόμα αὐτοῦ ἀνταποδώσει αὐτῷ </p>
 <p>18 παίδευε υἱόν σου οὕτως γὰρ ἔσται εὔελπις εἰς δὲ ὕβριν μὴ ἐπαίρου τῇ ψυχῇ σου </p>
 <p>19 κακόφρων ἀνὴρ πολλὰ ζημιωθήσεται ἐὰν δὲ λοιμεύηται καὶ τὴν ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ προσθήσει </p>
 <p>20 ἄκουε υἱέ παιδείαν πατρός σου ἵνα σοφὸς γένῃ ἐπ᾽ ἐσχάτων σου </p>
 <p>21 πολλοὶ λογισμοὶ ἐν καρδίᾳ ἀνδρός ἡ δὲ βουλὴ τοῦ κυρίου εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα μένει </p>
 <p>22 καρπὸς ἀνδρὶ ἐλεημοσύνη κρείσσων δὲ πτωχὸς δίκαιος ἢ πλούσιος ψεύστης </p>
 <p>23 φόβος κυρίου εἰς ζωὴν ἀνδρί ὁ δὲ ἄφοβος αὐλισθήσεται ἐν τόποις οὗ οὐκ ἐπισκοπεῖται γνῶσις </p>
 <p>24 ὁ ἐγκρύπτων εἰς τὸν κόλπον αὐτοῦ χεῖρας ἀδίκως οὐδὲ τῷ στόματι οὐ μὴ προσαγάγῃ αὐτάς </p>
 <p>25 λοιμοῦ μαστιγουμένου ἄφρων πανουργότερος γίνεται ἐὰν δὲ ἐλέγχῃς ἄνδρα φρόνιμον νοήσει αἴσθησιν </p>
 <p>26 ὁ ἀτιμάζων πατέρα καὶ ἀπωθούμενος μητέρα αὐτοῦ καταισχυνθήσεται καὶ ἐπονείδιστος ἔσται </p>
 <p>27 υἱὸς ἀπολειπόμενος φυλάξαι παιδείαν πατρὸς μελετήσει ῥήσεις κακάς </p>
 <p>28 ὁ ἐγγυώμενος παῖδα ἄφρονα καθυβρίζει δικαίωμα στόμα δὲ ἀσεβῶν καταπίεται κρίσεις </p>
 <p>29 ἑτοιμάζονται ἀκολάστοις μάστιγες καὶ τιμωρίαι ὤμοις ἀφρόνων </p>

 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎7 ‏כָּ֥ל אֲחֵי־רָ֨שׁ׀ שְֽׂנֵאֻ֗הוּ אַ֤ף כִּ֣י מְ֭רֵעֵהוּ רָחֲק֣וּ מִמֶּ֑נּוּ מְרַדֵּ֖ף אֲמָרִ֣ים <font color="brown">לא־</font> <font color="blue">לוֹ־</font> הֵֽמָּה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎8 ‏קֹֽנֶה־לֵּ֭ב אֹהֵ֣ב נַפְשׁ֑וֹ שֹׁמֵ֥ר תְּ֝בוּנָ֗ה לִמְצֹא־טֽוֹב׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎9 ‏עֵ֣ד שְׁ֭קָרִים לֹ֣א יִנָּקֶ֑ה וְיָפִ֖יחַ כְּזָבִ֣ים יֹאבֵֽד׃ פ</span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎10 ‏לֹֽא־נָאוֶ֣ה לִכְסִ֣יל תַּעֲנ֑וּג אַ֝֗ף כִּֽי־לְעֶ֤בֶד׀ מְשֹׁ֬ל בְּשָׂרִֽים׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎11 ‏שֵׂ֣כֶל אָ֭דָם הֶאֱרִ֣יךְ אַפּ֑וֹ וְ֝תִפאַרְתּ֗וֹ<font color="red"><sup>1</sup></font> עֲבֹ֣ר עַל־פָּֽשַׁע׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎12 ‏נַ֣הַם כַּ֭כְּפִיר זַ֣עַף מֶ֑לֶךְ וּכְטַ֖ל עַל־עֵ֣שֶׂב רְצוֹנֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎13 ‏הַוֹּ֣ת לְ֭אָבִיו בֵּ֣ן כְּסִ֑יל וְדֶ֥לֶף טֹ֝רֵ֗ד מִדְיְנֵ֥י אִשָּֽׁה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎14 ‏בַּ֣יִת וָ֭הוֹן נַחֲלַ֣ת אָב֑וֹת וּ֝מֵיְהוָ֗ה אִשָּׁ֥ה מַשְׂכָּֽלֶת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎15 ‏עַ֭צְלָה תַּפִּ֣יל תַּרְדֵּמָ֑ה וְנֶ֖פֶשׁ רְמִיָּ֣ה תִרְעָֽב׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎16 ‏שֹׁמֵ֣ר מִ֭צְוָה שֹׁמֵ֣ר נַפְשׁ֑וֹ בּוֹזֵ֖ה דְרָכָ֣יו <font color="brown">יומת</font> <font color="blue">יָמֽוּת׃</font> </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎17 ‏מַלְוֵ֣ה יְ֭הוָה ח֣וֹנֵֽן דָּ֑ל וּ֝גְמֻל֗וֹ יְשַׁלֶּם־לֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎18 ‏יַסֵּ֣ר בִּ֭נְךָ כִּי־יֵ֣שׁ תִּקְוָ֑ה וְאֶל־הֲ֝מִית֗וֹ אַל־תִּשָּׂ֥א נַפְשֶֽׁךָ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎19 ‏<font color="brown">גרל־</font> <font color="blue">גְּֽדָל־</font> חֵ֭מָה נֹ֣שֵׂא עֹ֑נֶשׁ כִּ֥י אִם־תַּ֝צִּ֗יל וְע֣וֹד תּוֹסִֽף׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎20 ‏שְׁמַ֣ע עֵ֭צָה וְקַבֵּ֣ל מוּסָ֑ר לְ֝מַ֗עַן תֶּחְכַּ֥ם בְּאַחֲרִיתֶֽךָ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎21 ‏רַבּ֣וֹת מַחֲשָׁב֣וֹת בְּלֶב־אִ֑ישׁ וַעֲצַ֥ת יְ֝הוָ֗ה הִ֣יא תָקֽוּם׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎22 ‏תַּאֲוַ֣ת אָדָ֣ם חַסְדּ֑וֹ וְטֽוֹב־רָ֝שׁ מֵאִ֥ישׁ כָּזָֽב׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎23 ‏יִרְאַ֣ת יְהוָ֣ה לְחַיִּ֑ים וְשָׂבֵ֥עַ יָ֝לִ֗ין בַּל־יִפָּ֥קֶד רָֽע׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎24 ‏טָ֘מַ֤ן עָצֵ֣ל יָ֭דוֹ בַּצַּלָּ֑חַת גַּם־אֶל־פִּ֝֗יהוּ לֹ֣א יְשִׁיבֶֽנָּה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎25 ‏לֵ֣ץ תַּ֭כֶּה וּפֶ֣תִי יַעְרִ֑ם וְהוֹכִ֥יחַ לְ֝נָב֗וֹן יָבִ֥ין דָּֽעַת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎26 ‏מְֽשַׁדֶּד־אָ֭ב יַבְרִ֣יחַ אֵ֑ם בֵּ֝֗ן מֵבִ֥ישׁ וּמַחְפִּֽיר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎27 ‏חַֽדַל־בְּ֭נִי<font color="red"><sup>1</sup></font> לִשְׁמֹ֣עַ מוּסָ֑ר לִ֝שְׁג֗וֹת מֵֽאִמְרֵי־דָֽעַת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎28 ‏עֵ֣ד בְּ֭לִיַּעַל יָלִ֣יץ מִשְׁפָּ֑ט וּפִ֥י רְ֝שָׁעִ֗ים יְבַלַּע־אָֽוֶן׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎29 ‏נָכ֣וֹנוּ לַלֵּצִ֣ים שְׁפָטִ֑ים וּ֝מַהֲלֻמ֗וֹת לְגֵ֣ו כְּסִילִֽים׃ </span></p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro019.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Melior est pauper qui ambulat in simplicitate sua<br> quam dives torquens labia sua, et insipiens.<br></p>
 <p>2 Ubi non est scientia animæ, non est bonum,<br> et qui festinus est pedibus offendet.<br></p>
 <p>3 Stultitia hominis supplantat gressus ejus,<br> et contra Deum fervet animo suo.<br></p>
 <p>4 Divitiæ addunt amicos plurimos;<br> a paupere autem et hi quos habuit separantur.<br></p>
 <p>5 Testis falsus non erit impunitus,<br> et qui mendacia loquitur non effugiet.<br></p>
 <p>6 Multi colunt personam potentis,<br> et amici sunt dona tribuentis.<br></p>
 <p>7 Fratres hominis pauperis oderunt eum;<br> insuper et amici procul recesserunt ab eo.]<br>
  [Qui tantum verba sectatur nihil habebit;<br></p>
 <p>8 qui autem possessor est mentis diligit animam suam,<br> et custos prudentiæ inveniet bona.<br></p>
 <p>9 Falsus testis non erit impunitus,<br> et qui loquitur mendacia peribit.<br></p>
 <p>10 Non decent stultum deliciæ,<br> nec servum dominari principibus.<br></p>
 <p>11 Doctrina viri per patientiam noscitur,<br> et gloria ejus est iniqua prætergredi.<br></p>
 <p>12 Sicut fremitus leonis, ita et regis ira,<br> et sicut ros super herbam, ita et hilaritas ejus.<br></p>
 <p>13 Dolor patris filius stultus,<br> et tecta jugiter perstillantia litigiosa mulier.<br></p>
 <p>14 Domus et divitiæ dantur a parentibus;<br> a Domino autem proprie uxor prudens.<br></p>
 <p>15 Pigredo immittit soporem,<br> et anima dissoluta esuriet.<br></p>
 <p>16 Qui custodit mandatum custodit animam suam;<br> qui autem negligit viam suam mortificabitur.<br></p>
 <p>17 Fneratur Domino qui miseretur pauperis,<br> et vicissitudinem suam reddet ei.<br></p>
 <p>18 Erudi filium tuum; ne desperes:<br> ad interfectionem autem ejus ne ponas animam tuam.<br></p>
 <p>19 Qui impatiens est sustinebit damnum,<br> et cum rapuerit, aliud apponet.<br></p>
 <p>20 Audi consilium, et suscipe disciplinam,<br> ut sis sapiens in novissimis tuis.<br></p>
 <p>21 Multæ cogitationes in corde viri;<br> voluntas autem Domini permanebit.<br></p>
 <p>22 Homo indigens misericors est,<br> et melior est pauper quam vir mendax.<br></p>
 <p>23 Timor Domini ad vitam,<br> et in plenitudine commorabitur absque visitatione pessima.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>24 [Abscondit piger manum suam sub ascella,<br> nec ad os suum applicat eam.<br></p>
 <p>25 Pestilente flagellato stultus sapientior erit;<br> si autem corripueris sapientem, intelliget disciplinam.<br></p>
 <p>26 Qui affligit patrem, et fugat matrem,<br> ignominiosus est et infelix.<br></p>
 <p>27 Non cesses, fili, audire doctrinam,<br> nec ignores sermones scientiæ.<br></p>
 <p>28 Testis iniquus deridet judicium,<br> et os impiorum devorat iniquitatem.<br></p>
 <p>29 Parata sunt derisoribus judicia,<br> et mallei percutientes stultorum corporibus.]</p>
