 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro018.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Through desire<sup><a href="h8378.htm">8378</a></sup> a man, having separated himself,<sup><a href="h6504.htm">6504</a></sup> seeketh<sup><a href="h1245.htm">1245</a></sup> and intermeddleth<sup><a href="h1566.htm">1566</a></sup> with all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> wisdom.<sup><a href="h8454.htm">8454</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  A fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> hath no<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> delight<sup><a href="h2654.htm">2654</a></sup> in understanding,<sup><a href="h8394.htm">8394</a></sup> but<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> that his heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> may discover itself.<sup><a href="h1540.htm">1540</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  When the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> cometh,<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> then cometh<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> also<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> contempt,<sup><a href="h0937.htm">937</a></sup> and with<sup><a href="h5973.htm">5973</a></sup> ignominy<sup><a href="h7036.htm">7036</a></sup> reproach.<sup><a href="h2781.htm">2781</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  The words<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> of a man's<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> are as deep<sup><a href="h6013.htm">6013</a></sup> waters,<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> and the wellspring<sup><a href="h4726.htm">4726</a></sup> of wisdom<sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> as a flowing<sup><a href="h5042.htm">5042</a></sup> brook.<sup><a href="h5158.htm">5158</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  It is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> to accept<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> the person<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> of the wicked,<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> to overthrow<sup><a href="h5186.htm">5186</a></sup> the righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> in judgment.<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  A fool's<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> enter<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> into contention,<sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> and his mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> calleth<sup><a href="h7121.htm">7121</a></sup> for strokes.<sup><a href="h4112.htm">4112</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  A fool's<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> is his destruction,<sup><a href="h4288.htm">4288</a></sup> and his lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> are the snare<sup><a href="h4170.htm">4170</a></sup> of his soul.<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  The words<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> of a talebearer<sup><a href="h5372.htm">5372</a></sup> are as wounds,<sup><a href="h3859.htm">3859</a></sup> and they<sup><a href="h1992.htm">1992</a></sup> go down<sup><a href="h3381.htm">3381</a></sup> into the innermost parts<sup><a href="h2315.htm">2315</a></sup> of the belly.<sup><a href="h0990.htm">990</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  He<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> also<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> that is slothful<sup><a href="h7503.htm">7503</a></sup> in his work<sup><a href="h4399.htm">4399</a></sup> is brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> to him that is a great<sup><a href="h1167.htm">1167</a></sup> waster.<sup><a href="h4889.htm">4889</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  The name<sup><a href="h8034.htm">8034</a></sup> of the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> is a strong<sup><a href="h5797.htm">5797</a></sup> tower:<sup><a href="h4026.htm">4026</a></sup> the righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> runneth<sup><a href="h7323.htm">7323</a></sup> into it, and is safe.<sup><a href="h7682.htm">7682</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  The rich man's<sup><a href="h6223.htm">6223</a></sup> wealth<sup><a href="h1952.htm">1952</a></sup> is his strong<sup><a href="h5797.htm">5797</a></sup> city,<sup><a href="h7151.htm">7151</a></sup> and as a high<sup><a href="h7682.htm">7682</a></sup> wall<sup><a href="h2346.htm">2346</a></sup> in his own conceit.<sup><a href="h4906.htm">4906</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  Before<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> destruction<sup><a href="h7667.htm">7667</a></sup> the heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> is haughty,<sup><a href="h1361.htm">1361</a></sup> and before<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> honor<sup><a href="h3519.htm">3519</a></sup> is humility.<sup><a href="h6038.htm">6038</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  He that answereth<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> a matter<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> before<sup><a href="h2962.htm">2962</a></sup> he heareth<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> it, it<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> is folly<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> and shame<sup><a href="h3639.htm">3639</a></sup> unto him. </p>
 <p>14  The spirit<sup><a href="h7307.htm">7307</a></sup> of a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> will sustain<sup><a href="h3557.htm">3557</a></sup> his infirmity;<sup><a href="h4245.htm">4245</a></sup> but a wounded<sup><a href="h5218.htm">5218</a></sup> spirit<sup><a href="h7307.htm">7307</a></sup> who<sup><a href="h4310.htm">4310</a></sup> can bear?<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  The heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> of the prudent<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> getteth<sup><a href="h7069.htm">7069</a></sup> knowledge;<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> and the ear<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> of the wise<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> seeketh<sup><a href="h1245.htm">1245</a></sup> knowledge.<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  A man's<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> gift<sup><a href="h4976.htm">4976</a></sup> maketh room<sup><a href="h7337.htm">7337</a></sup> for him, and bringeth<sup><a href="h5148.htm">5148</a></sup> him before<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> great<sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> men. </p>
 <p>17  He that is first<sup><a href="h7223.htm">7223</a></sup> in his own cause<sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> seemeth just;<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> but his neighbor<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> cometh<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> and searcheth<sup><a href="h2713.htm">2713</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>18  The lot<sup><a href="h1486.htm">1486</a></sup> causeth contentions<sup><a href="h4079.htm">4079</a></sup> to cease,<sup><a href="h7673.htm">7673</a></sup> and parteth<sup><a href="h6504.htm">6504</a></sup> between<sup><a href="h0996.htm">996</a></sup> the mighty.<sup><a href="h6099.htm">6099</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  A brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> offended<sup><a href="h6586.htm">6586</a></sup> is harder to be won than a strong<sup><a href="h5797.htm">5797</a></sup> city:<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7151.htm">7151</a></sup> and their contentions<sup><a href="h4079.htm">4079</a></sup> are like the bars<sup><a href="h1280.htm">1280</a></sup> of a castle.<sup><a href="h0759.htm">759</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  A man's<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> belly<sup><a href="h0990.htm">990</a></sup> shall be satisfied<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> with the fruit<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6529.htm">6529</a></sup> of his mouth;<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> and with the increase<sup><a href="h8393.htm">8393</a></sup> of his lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> shall he be filled.<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  Death<sup><a href="h4194.htm">4194</a></sup> and life<sup><a href="h2416.htm">2416</a></sup> are in the power<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of the tongue:<sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> and they that love<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> it shall eat<sup><a href="h0398.htm">398</a></sup> the fruit<sup><a href="h6529.htm">6529</a></sup> thereof. </p>
 <p>22  Whoso findeth<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> a wife<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> findeth<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> a good<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> thing, and obtaineth<sup><a href="h6329.htm">6329</a></sup> favor<sup><a href="h7522.htm">7522</a></sup> of the LORD.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> </p>
 <p>23  The poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> useth<sup><a href="h1696.htm">1696</a></sup> entreaties;<sup><a href="h8469.htm">8469</a></sup> but the rich<sup><a href="h6223.htm">6223</a></sup> answereth<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> roughly.<sup><a href="h5794.htm">5794</a></sup> </p>
 <p>24  A man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> that hath friends<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> must show himself friendly:<sup><a href="h7489.htm">7489</a></sup> and there is<sup><a href="h3426.htm">3426</a></sup> a friend<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> that sticketh closer<sup><a href="h1695.htm">1695</a></sup> than a brother.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> </p>

 <p>18 ἀντιλογίας παύει κλῆρος ἐν δὲ δυνάσταις ὁρίζει </p>
 <p>19 ἀδελφὸς ὑπὸ ἀδελφοῦ βοηθούμενος ὡς πόλις ὀχυρὰ καὶ ὑψηλή ἰσχύει δὲ ὥσπερ τεθεμελιωμένον βασίλειον </p>
 <p>20 ἀπὸ καρπῶν στόματος ἀνὴρ πίμπλησιν κοιλίαν αὐτοῦ ἀπὸ δὲ καρπῶν χειλέων αὐτοῦ ἐμπλησθήσεται </p>
 <p>21 θάνατος καὶ ζωὴ ἐν χειρὶ γλώσσης οἱ δὲ κρατοῦντες αὐτῆς ἔδονται τοὺς καρποὺς αὐτῆς </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro018.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Occasiones quærit qui vult recedere ab amico:<br> omni tempore erit exprobrabilis.<br></p>
 <p>2 Non recipit stultus verba prudentiæ,<br> nisi ea dixeris quæ versantur in corde ejus.<br></p>
 <p>3 Impius, cum in profundum venerit peccatorum, contemnit;<br> sed sequitur eum ignominia et opprobrium.<br></p>
 <p>4 Aqua profunda verba ex ore viri,<br> et torrens redundans fons sapientiæ.<br></p>
 <p>5 Accipere personam impii non est bonum,<br> ut declines a veritate judicii.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>6 [Labia stulti miscent se rixis,<br> et os ejus jurgia provocat.<br></p>
 <p>7 Os stulti contritio ejus,<br> et labia ipsius ruina animæ ejus.<br></p>
 <p>8 Verba bilinguis quasi simplicia,<br> et ipsa perveniunt usque ad interiora ventris.<br> Pigrum dejicit timor;<br> animæ autem effeminatorum esurient.<br></p>
 <p>9 Qui mollis et dissolutus est in opere suo<br> frater est sua opera dissipantis.<br></p>
 <p>10 Turris fortissima nomen Domini;<br> ad ipsum currit justus, et exaltabitur.<br></p>
 <p>11 Substantia divitis urbs roboris ejus,<br> et quasi murus validus circumdans eum.<br></p>
 <p>12 Antequam conteratur, exaltatur cor hominis,<br> et antequam glorificetur, humiliatur.<br></p>
 <p>13 Qui prius respondet quam audiat,<br> stultum se esse demonstrat, et confusione dignum.<br></p>
 <p>14 Spiritus viri sustentat imbecillitatem suam;<br> spiritum vero ad irascendum facilem quis poterit sustinere?<br></p>
 <p>15 Cor prudens possidebit scientiam,<br> et auris sapientium quærit doctrinam.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>16 [Donum hominis dilatat viam ejus,<br> et ante principes spatium ei facit.<br></p>
 <p>17 Justus prior est accusator sui:<br> venit amicus ejus, et investigabit eum.<br></p>
 <p>18 Contradictiones comprimit sors,<br> et inter potentes quoque dijudicat.<br></p>
 <p>19 Frater qui adjuvatur a fratre quasi civitas firma,<br> et judicia quasi vectes urbium.<br></p>
 <p>20 De fructu oris viri replebitur venter ejus,<br> et genimina labiorum ipsius saturabunt eum.<br></p>
 <p>21 Mors et vita in manu linguæ;<br> qui diligunt eam comedent fructus ejus.<br></p>
 <p>22 Qui invenit mulierem bonam invenit bonum,<br> et hauriet jucunditatem a Domino.<br> Qui expellit mulierem bonam expellit bonum;<br> qui autem tenet adulteram stultus est et impius.<br></p>
 <p>23 Cum obsecrationibus loquetur pauper,<br> et dives effabitur rigide.<br></p>
 <p>24 Vir amabilis ad societatem<br> magis amicus erit quam frater.]</p>
