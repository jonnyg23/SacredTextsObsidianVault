 <p>2  And it shall be,<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> if<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> man be worthy to be beaten,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> that the judge<sup><a href="h8199.htm">8199</a></sup> shall cause him to lie down,<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> and to be beaten<sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> before his face,<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> according to<sup><a href="h1767.htm">1767</a></sup> his fault,<sup><a href="h7564.htm">7564</a></sup> by a certain number.<sup><a href="h4557.htm">4557</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  Forty<sup><a href="h0705.htm">705</a></sup> stripes he may give<sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> him, and not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> exceed:<sup><a href="h3254.htm">3254</a></sup> lest,<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> if he should exceed,<sup><a href="h3254.htm">3254</a></sup> and beat<sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> him above<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> these<sup><a href="h0428.htm">428</a></sup> with many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> stripes,<sup><a href="h4347.htm">4347</a></sup> then thy brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> should seem vile<sup><a href="h7034.htm">7034</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> thee. </p>
 <p>4  Thou shalt not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> muzzle<sup><a href="h2629.htm">2629</a></sup> the ox<sup><a href="h7794.htm">7794</a></sup> when he treadeth out<sup><a href="h1758.htm">1758</a></sup> the corn. </p>
 <p>5  If<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> brethren<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> dwell<sup><a href="h3427.htm">3427</a></sup> together,<sup><a href="h3162.htm">3162</a></sup> and one<sup><a href="h0259.htm">259</a></sup> of<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> them die,<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> and have no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> child,<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> the wife<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> of the dead<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> marry<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> without<sup><a href="h2351.htm">2351</a></sup> unto a stranger:<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> her husband's brother<sup><a href="h2993.htm">2993</a></sup> shall go in<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> her, and take<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> her to him to wife,<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> and perform the duty of a husband's brother<sup><a href="h2992.htm">2992</a></sup> unto her. </p>
 <p>6  And it shall be,<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> that the firstborn<sup><a href="h1060.htm">1060</a></sup> which<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> she beareth<sup><a href="h3205.htm">3205</a></sup> shall succeed<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> in<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the name<sup><a href="h8034.htm">8034</a></sup> of his brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> which is dead,<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> that his name<sup><a href="h8034.htm">8034</a></sup> be not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> put out<sup><a href="h4229.htm">4229</a></sup> of Israel.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3478.htm">3478</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  And if<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> the man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> like<sup><a href="h2654.htm">2654</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> to take<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> his brother's wife,<sup><a href="h2994.htm">2994</a></sup> then let his brother's wife<sup><a href="h2994.htm">2994</a></sup> go up<sup><a href="h5927.htm">5927</a></sup> to the gate<sup><a href="h8179.htm">8179</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> the elders,<sup><a href="h2205.htm">2205</a></sup> and say,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> My husband's brother<sup><a href="h2993.htm">2993</a></sup> refuseth<sup><a href="h3985.htm">3985</a></sup> to raise up<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> unto his brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> a name<sup><a href="h8034.htm">8034</a></sup> in Israel,<sup><a href="h3478.htm">3478</a></sup> he will<sup><a href="h0014.htm">14</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> perform the duty of my husband's brother.<sup><a href="h2992.htm">2992</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  Then the elders<sup><a href="h2205.htm">2205</a></sup> of his city<sup><a href="h5892.htm">5892</a></sup> shall call<sup><a href="h7121.htm">7121</a></sup> him, and speak<sup><a href="h1696.htm">1696</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> him: and if he stand<sup><a href="h5975.htm">5975</a></sup> to it, and say,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> I like<sup><a href="h2654.htm">2654</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> to take<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> her; </p>
 <p>9  Then shall his brother's wife<sup><a href="h2994.htm">2994</a></sup> come<sup><a href="h5066.htm">5066</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> him in the presence<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> of the elders,<sup><a href="h2205.htm">2205</a></sup> and loose<sup><a href="h2502.htm">2502</a></sup> his shoe<sup><a href="h5275.htm">5275</a></sup> from off<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> his foot,<sup><a href="h7272.htm">7272</a></sup> and spit<sup><a href="h3417.htm">3417</a></sup> in his face,<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> and shall answer<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> and say,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> So<sup><a href="h3602.htm">3602</a></sup> shall it be done<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> unto that man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> will not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> build up<sup><a href="h1129.htm">1129</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> his brother's<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> house.<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  And his name<sup><a href="h8034.htm">8034</a></sup> shall be called<sup><a href="h7121.htm">7121</a></sup> in Israel,<sup><a href="h3478.htm">3478</a></sup> The house<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> of him that hath his shoe<sup><a href="h5275.htm">5275</a></sup> loosed.<sup><a href="h2502.htm">2502</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  When<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> men<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> strive<sup><a href="h5327.htm">5327</a></sup> together<sup><a href="h3162.htm">3162</a></sup> one<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> with another,<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> and the wife<sup><a href="h0802.htm">802</a></sup> of the one<sup><a href="h0259.htm">259</a></sup> draweth near<sup><a href="h7126.htm">7126</a></sup> for to deliver<sup><a href="h5337.htm">5337</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> her husband<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> out of the hand<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of him that smiteth<sup><a href="h5221.htm">5221</a></sup> him, and putteth forth<sup><a href="h7971.htm">7971</a></sup> her hand,<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> and taketh<sup><a href="h2388.htm">2388</a></sup> him by the secrets:<sup><a href="h4016.htm">4016</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  Then thou shalt cut off<sup><a href="h7112.htm">7112</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> her hand,<sup><a href="h3709.htm">3709</a></sup> thine eye<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> pity<sup><a href="h2347.htm">2347</a></sup> her. </p>
 <p>13  Thou shalt not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> have<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> in thy bag<sup><a href="h3599.htm">3599</a></sup> divers weights,<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> a great<sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> and a small.<sup><a href="h6996.htm">6996</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  Thou shalt not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> have<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> in thine house<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> divers measures,<sup><a href="h0374.htm">374</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0374.htm">374</a></sup> a great<sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> and a small.<sup><a href="h6996.htm">6996</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  But thou shalt have<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> a perfect<sup><a href="h8003.htm">8003</a></sup> and just<sup><a href="h6664.htm">6664</a></sup> weight,<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> a perfect<sup><a href="h8003.htm">8003</a></sup> and just<sup><a href="h6664.htm">6664</a></sup> measure<sup><a href="h0374.htm">374</a></sup> shalt thou have:<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h4616.htm">4616</a></sup> thy days<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> may be lengthened<sup><a href="h0748.htm">748</a></sup> in<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the land<sup><a href="h0127.htm">127</a></sup> which<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> thy God<sup><a href="h0430.htm">430</a></sup> giveth<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> thee. </p>
 <p>16  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> that do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> such things,<sup><a href="h0428.htm">428</a></sup> and all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> that do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> unrighteously,<sup><a href="h5766.htm">5766</a></sup> are an abomination<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> unto the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> thy God.<sup><a href="h0430.htm">430</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  Remember<sup><a href="h2142.htm">2142</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> what<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> Amalek<sup><a href="h6002.htm">6002</a></sup> did<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> unto thee by the way,<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> when ye were come forth<sup><a href="h3318.htm">3318</a></sup> out of Egypt;<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4714.htm">4714</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  How<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> he met<sup><a href="h7136.htm">7136</a></sup> thee by the way,<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> and smote the hindmost<sup><a href="h2179.htm">2179</a></sup> of thee, even all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> that were feeble<sup><a href="h2826.htm">2826</a></sup> behind<sup><a href="h0310.htm">310</a></sup> thee, when thou<sup><a href="h0859.htm">859</a></sup> wast faint<sup><a href="h5889.htm">5889</a></sup> and weary;<sup><a href="h3023.htm">3023</a></sup> and he feared<sup><a href="h3372.htm">3372</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> God.<sup><a href="h0430.htm">430</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  Therefore it shall be,<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> when the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> thy God<sup><a href="h0430.htm">430</a></sup> hath given thee rest<sup><a href="h5117.htm">5117</a></sup> from all<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> thine enemies<sup><a href="h0341.htm">341</a></sup> round about,<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5439.htm">5439</a></sup> in the land<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> which<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> thy God<sup><a href="h0430.htm">430</a></sup> giveth<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> thee for an inheritance<sup><a href="h5159.htm">5159</a></sup> to possess<sup><a href="h3423.htm">3423</a></sup> it, that thou shalt blot out<sup><a href="h4229.htm">4229</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> the remembrance<sup><a href="h2143.htm">2143</a></sup> of Amalek<sup><a href="h6002.htm">6002</a></sup> from under<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h8478.htm">8478</a></sup> heaven;<sup><a href="h8064.htm">8064</a></sup> thou shalt not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> forget<sup><a href="h7911.htm">7911</a></sup> it. </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/deu025.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 ἐὰν δὲ γένηται ἀντιλογία ἀνὰ μέσον ἀνθρώπων καὶ προσέλθωσιν εἰς κρίσιν καὶ κρίνωσιν καὶ δικαιώσωσιν τὸν δίκαιον καὶ καταγνῶσιν τοῦ ἀσεβοῦς </p>
 <p>2 καὶ ἔσται ἐὰν ἄξιος ᾖ πληγῶν ὁ ἀσεβῶν καὶ καθιεῖς αὐτὸν ἔναντι τῶν κριτῶν καὶ μαστιγώσουσιν αὐτὸν ἐναντίον αὐτῶν κατὰ τὴν ἀσέβειαν αὐτοῦ ἀριθμῷ </p>
 <p>3 τεσσαράκοντα μαστιγώσουσιν αὐτόν οὐ προσθήσουσιν ἐὰν δὲ προσθῶσιν μαστιγῶσαι αὐτὸν ὑπὲρ ταύτας τὰς πληγὰς πλείους ἀσχημονήσει ὁ ἀδελφός σου ἐναντίον σου </p>
 <p>4 οὐ φιμώσεις βοῦν ἀλοῶντα </p>
 <p>5 ἐὰν δὲ κατοικῶσιν ἀδελφοὶ ἐπὶ τὸ αὐτὸ καὶ ἀποθάνῃ εἷς ἐξ αὐτῶν σπέρμα δὲ μὴ ᾖ αὐτῷ οὐκ ἔσται ἡ γυνὴ τοῦ τεθνηκότος ἔξω ἀνδρὶ μὴ ἐγγίζοντι ὁ ἀδελφὸς τοῦ ἀνδρὸς αὐτῆς εἰσελεύσεται πρὸς αὐτὴν καὶ λήμψεται αὐτὴν ἑαυτῷ γυναῖκα καὶ συνοικήσει αὐτῇ </p>
 <p>6 καὶ ἔσται τὸ παιδίον ὃ ἐὰν τέκῃ κατασταθήσεται ἐκ τοῦ ὀνόματος τοῦ τετελευτηκότος καὶ οὐκ ἐξαλειφθήσεται τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ ἐξ Ισραηλ </p>
 <p>7 ἐὰν δὲ μὴ βούληται ὁ ἄνθρωπος λαβεῖν τὴν γυναῖκα τοῦ ἀδελφοῦ αὐτοῦ καὶ ἀναβήσεται ἡ γυνὴ ἐπὶ τὴν πύλην ἐπὶ τὴν γερουσίαν καὶ ἐρεῖ οὐ θέλει ὁ ἀδελφὸς τοῦ ἀνδρός μου ἀναστῆσαι τὸ ὄνομα τοῦ ἀδελφοῦ αὐτοῦ ἐν Ισραηλ οὐκ ἠθέλησεν ὁ ἀδελφὸς τοῦ ἀνδρός μου </p>
 <p>8 καὶ καλέσουσιν αὐτὸν ἡ γερουσία τῆς πόλεως αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐροῦσιν αὐτῷ καὶ στὰς εἴπῃ οὐ βούλομαι λαβεῖν αὐτήν </p>
 <p>9 καὶ προσελθοῦσα ἡ γυνὴ τοῦ ἀδελφοῦ αὐτοῦ ἔναντι τῆς γερουσίας καὶ ὑπολύσει τὸ ὑπόδημα αὐτοῦ τὸ ἓν ἀπὸ τοῦ ποδὸς αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐμπτύσεται εἰς τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτοῦ καὶ ἀποκριθεῖσα ἐρεῖ οὕτως ποιήσουσιν τῷ ἀνθρώπῳ ὃς οὐκ οἰκοδομήσει τὸν οἶκον τοῦ ἀδελφοῦ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>10 καὶ κληθήσεται τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ ἐν Ισραηλ οἶκος τοῦ ὑπολυθέντος τὸ ὑπόδημα </p>
 <p>11 ἐὰν δὲ μάχωνται ἄνθρωποι ἐπὶ τὸ αὐτό ἄνθρωπος μετὰ τοῦ ἀδελφοῦ αὐτοῦ καὶ προσέλθῃ γυνὴ ἑνὸς αὐτῶν ἐξελέσθαι τὸν ἄνδρα αὐτῆς ἐκ χειρὸς τοῦ τύπτοντος αὐτὸν καὶ ἐκτείνασα τὴν χεῖρα ἐπιλάβηται τῶν διδύμων αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>12 ἀποκόψεις τὴν χεῖρα αὐτῆς οὐ φείσεται ὁ ὀφθαλμός σου ἐπ᾽ αὐτῇ </p>
 <p>13 οὐκ ἔσται ἐν τῷ μαρσίππῳ σου στάθμιον καὶ στάθμιον μέγα ἢ μικρόν </p>
 <p>14 οὐκ ἔσται ἐν τῇ οἰκίᾳ σου μέτρον καὶ μέτρον μέγα ἢ μικρόν </p>
 <p>15 στάθμιον ἀληθινὸν καὶ δίκαιον ἔσται σοι καὶ μέτρον ἀληθινὸν καὶ δίκαιον ἔσται σοι ἵνα πολυήμερος γένῃ ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς ἧς κύριος ὁ θεός σου δίδωσίν σοι ἐν κλήρῳ </p>
 <p>16 ὅτι βδέλυγμα κυρίῳ τῷ θεῷ σου πᾶς ποιῶν ταῦτα πᾶς ποιῶν ἄδικον </p>
 <p>17 μνήσθητι ὅσα ἐποίησέν σοι Αμαληκ ἐν τῇ ὁδῷ ἐκπορευομένου σου ἐξ Αἰγύπτου </p>
 <p>18 πῶς ἀντέστη σοι ἐν τῇ ὁδῷ καὶ ἔκοψέν σου τὴν οὐραγίαν τοὺς κοπιῶντας ὀπίσω σου σὺ δὲ ἐπείνας καὶ ἐκοπίας καὶ οὐκ ἐφοβήθη τὸν θεόν </p>
 <p>19 καὶ ἔσται ἡνίκα ἐὰν καταπαύσῃ σε κύριος ὁ θεός σου ἀπὸ πάντων τῶν ἐχθρῶν σου τῶν κύκλῳ σου ἐν τῇ γῇ ᾗ κύριος ὁ θεός σου δίδωσίν σοι ἐν κλήρῳ κατακληρονομῆσαι ἐξαλείψεις τὸ ὄνομα Αμαληκ ἐκ τῆς ὑπὸ τὸν οὐρανὸν καὶ οὐ μὴ ἐπιλάθῃ </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/deu025.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 Si fuerit causa inter aliquos, et interpellaverint judices: quem justum esse perspexerint, illi justitiæ palmam dabunt: quem impium, condemnabunt impietatis.</p>
 <p>2 Sin autem eum, qui peccavit, dignum viderint plagis: prosternent, et coram se facient verberari. Pro mensura peccati erit et plagarum modus:</p>
 <p>3 ita dumtaxat, ut quadragenarium numerum non excedant: ne fde laceratus ante oculos tuos abeat frater tuus.</p>
 <p>4 Non ligabis os bovis terentis in area fruges tuas.<br>
 </p>
 <p>5 Quando habitaverint fratres simul, et unus ex eis absque liberis mortuus fuerit, uxor defuncti non nubet alteri: sed accipiet eam frater ejus, et suscitabit semen fratris sui:</p>
 <p>6 et primogenitum ex ea filium nomine illius appellabit, ut non deleatur nomen ejus ex Israël.</p>
 <p>7 Sin autem noluerit accipere uxorem fratris sui, quæ ei lege debetur, perget mulier ad portam civitatis, et interpellabit majores natu, dicetque: Non vult frater viri mei suscitare nomen fratris sui in Israël, nec me in conjugem sumere.</p>
 <p>8 Statimque accersiri eum facient, et interrogabunt. Si responderit: Nolo eam uxorem accipere:</p>
 <p>9 accedet mulier ad eum coram senioribus, et tollet calceamentum de pede ejus, spuetque in faciem illius, et dicet: Sic fiet homini, qui non ædificat domum fratris sui.</p>
 <p>10 Et vocabitur nomen illius in Israël, Domus discalceati.<br>
 </p>
 <p>11 Si habuerint inter se jurgium viri duo, et unus contra alterum rixari cperit, volensque uxor alterius eruere virum suum de manu fortioris, miseritque manum, et apprehenderit verenda ejus:</p>
 <p>12 abscides manum illius, nec flecteris super eam ulla misericordia.<br>
 </p>
 <p>13 Non habebis in sacculo diversa pondera, majus et minus:</p>
 <p>14 nec erit in domo tua modius major, et minor.</p>
 <p>15 Pondus habebis justum et verum, et modius æqualis et verus erit tibi: ut multo vivas tempore super terram, quam Dominus Deus tuus dederit tibi.</p>
 <p>16 Abominatur enim Dominus tuus eum qui facit hæc, et aversatur omnem injustitiam.<br>
 </p>
 <p>17 Memento quæ fecerit tibi Amalec in via quando egrediebaris ex Ægypto:</p>
 <p>18 quomodo occurrerit tibi, et extremos agminis tui, qui lassi residebant, ceciderit, quando tu eras fame et labore confectus: et non timuerit Deum.</p>
 <p>19 Cum ergo Dominus Deus tuus dederit tibi requiem, et subjecerit cunctas per circuitum nationes in terra, quam tibi pollicitus est: delebis nomen ejus sub cælo. Cave ne obliviscaris.</p>
