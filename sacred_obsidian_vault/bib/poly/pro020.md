 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro020.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Wine<sup><a href="h3196.htm">3196</a></sup> is a mocker,<sup><a href="h3887.htm">3887</a></sup> strong drink<sup><a href="h7941.htm">7941</a></sup> is raging:<sup><a href="h1993.htm">1993</a></sup> and whosoever<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> is deceived<sup><a href="h7686.htm">7686</a></sup> thereby is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> wise.<sup><a href="h2449.htm">2449</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  The fear<sup><a href="h0367.htm">367</a></sup> of a king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> is as the roaring<sup><a href="h5099.htm">5099</a></sup> of a lion:<sup><a href="h3715.htm">3715</a></sup> whoso provoketh him to anger<sup><a href="h5674.htm">5674</a></sup> sinneth<sup><a href="h2398.htm">2398</a></sup> against his own soul.<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  It is an honor<sup><a href="h3519.htm">3519</a></sup> for a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> to cease<sup><a href="h7674.htm">7674</a></sup> from strife:<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> but every<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> fool<sup><a href="h0191.htm">191</a></sup> will be meddling.<sup><a href="h1566.htm">1566</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  The sluggard<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> will not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> plow<sup><a href="h2790.htm">2790</a></sup> by reason of the cold;<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2779.htm">2779</a></sup> therefore shall he beg<sup><a href="h7592.htm">7592</a></sup> in harvest,<sup><a href="h7105.htm">7105</a></sup> and have nothing.<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  Counsel<sup><a href="h6098.htm">6098</a></sup> in the heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> is like deep<sup><a href="h6013.htm">6013</a></sup> water;<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> but a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> of understanding<sup><a href="h8394.htm">8394</a></sup> will draw it out.<sup><a href="h1802.htm">1802</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  Most<sup><a href="h7230.htm">7230</a></sup> men<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> will proclaim<sup><a href="h7121.htm">7121</a></sup> every one<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> his own goodness:<sup><a href="h2617.htm">2617</a></sup> but a faithful<sup><a href="h0529.htm">529</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> who<sup><a href="h4310.htm">4310</a></sup> can find?<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  The just<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> man walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> in his integrity:<sup><a href="h8537.htm">8537</a></sup> his children<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> are blessed<sup><a href="h0835.htm">835</a></sup> after<sup><a href="h0310.htm">310</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>8  A king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> that sitteth<sup><a href="h3427.htm">3427</a></sup> in<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the throne<sup><a href="h3678.htm">3678</a></sup> of judgment<sup><a href="h1779.htm">1779</a></sup> scattereth away<sup><a href="h2219.htm">2219</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> with his eyes.<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  Who<sup><a href="h4310.htm">4310</a></sup> can say,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> I have made my heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> clean,<sup><a href="h2135.htm">2135</a></sup> I am pure<sup><a href="h2891.htm">2891</a></sup> from my sin?<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2403.htm">2403</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  Divers weights,<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> and divers measures,<sup><a href="h0374.htm">374</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0374.htm">374</a></sup> both<sup><a href="h8147.htm">8147</a></sup> of them are alike<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> abomination<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> to the LORD.<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> </p>

 <p>12  The hearing<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> ear,<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> and the seeing<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> eye,<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> hath made<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> even<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> both<sup><a href="h8147.htm">8147</a></sup> of them. </p>
 <p>13  Love<sup><a href="h0157.htm">157</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> sleep,<sup><a href="h8142.htm">8142</a></sup> lest<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> thou come to poverty;<sup><a href="h3423.htm">3423</a></sup> open<sup><a href="h6491.htm">6491</a></sup> thine eyes,<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> and thou shalt be satisfied<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> with bread.<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  It is naught,<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> it is naught,<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> saith<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> the buyer:<sup><a href="h7069.htm">7069</a></sup> but when he is gone his way,<sup><a href="h0235.htm">235</a></sup> then<sup><a href="h0227.htm">227</a></sup> he boasteth.<sup><a href="h1984.htm">1984</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  There is<sup><a href="h3426.htm">3426</a></sup> gold,<sup><a href="h2091.htm">2091</a></sup> and a multitude<sup><a href="h7230.htm">7230</a></sup> of rubies:<sup><a href="h6443.htm">6443</a></sup> but the lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> of knowledge<sup><a href="h1847.htm">1847</a></sup> are a precious<sup><a href="h3366.htm">3366</a></sup> jewel.<sup><a href="h3627.htm">3627</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  Take<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> his garment<sup><a href="h0899.htm">899</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> is surety<sup><a href="h6148.htm">6148</a></sup> for a stranger:<sup><a href="h2114.htm">2114</a></sup> and take a pledge<sup><a href="h2254.htm">2254</a></sup> of him for<sup><a href="h1157.htm">1157</a></sup> a strange woman.<sup><a href="h5237.htm">5237</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  Bread<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> of deceit<sup><a href="h8267.htm">8267</a></sup> is sweet<sup><a href="h6156.htm">6156</a></sup> to a man;<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> but afterwards<sup><a href="h0310.htm">310</a></sup> his mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> shall be filled<sup><a href="h4390.htm">4390</a></sup> with gravel.<sup><a href="h2687.htm">2687</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  Every purpose<sup><a href="h4284.htm">4284</a></sup> is established<sup><a href="h3559.htm">3559</a></sup> by counsel:<sup><a href="h6098.htm">6098</a></sup> and with good advice<sup><a href="h8458.htm">8458</a></sup> make<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> war.<sup><a href="h4421.htm">4421</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  He that goeth about<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> as a talebearer<sup><a href="h7400.htm">7400</a></sup> revealeth<sup><a href="h1540.htm">1540</a></sup> secrets:<sup><a href="h5475.htm">5475</a></sup> therefore meddle<sup><a href="h6148.htm">6148</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> with him that flattereth<sup><a href="h6601.htm">6601</a></sup> with his lips.<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  Whoso curseth<sup><a href="h7043.htm">7043</a></sup> his father<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> or his mother,<sup><a href="h0517.htm">517</a></sup> his lamp<sup><a href="h5216.htm">5216</a></sup> shall be put out<sup><a href="h1846.htm">1846</a></sup> in obscure<sup><a href="h0380.htm">380</a></sup> darkness.<sup><a href="h2822.htm">2822</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  An inheritance<sup><a href="h5159.htm">5159</a></sup> may be gotten hastily<sup><a href="h0926.htm">926</a></sup> at the beginning;<sup><a href="h7223.htm">7223</a></sup> but the end<sup><a href="h0319.htm">319</a></sup> thereof shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> be blessed.<sup><a href="h1288.htm">1288</a></sup> </p>
 <p>22  Say<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> thou, I will recompense<sup><a href="h7999.htm">7999</a></sup> evil;<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> but wait<sup><a href="h6960.htm">6960</a></sup> on the LORD,<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> and he shall save<sup><a href="h3467.htm">3467</a></sup> thee. </p>
 <p>23  Divers weights<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> are an abomination<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> unto the LORD;<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> and a false<sup><a href="h4820.htm">4820</a></sup> balance<sup><a href="h3976.htm">3976</a></sup> is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good.<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> </p>

 <p>25  It is a snare<sup><a href="h4170.htm">4170</a></sup> to the man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> who devoureth<sup><a href="h3216.htm">3216</a></sup> that which is holy,<sup><a href="h6944.htm">6944</a></sup> and after<sup><a href="h0310.htm">310</a></sup> vows<sup><a href="h5088.htm">5088</a></sup> to make inquiry.<sup><a href="h1239.htm">1239</a></sup> </p>
 <p>26  A wise<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> scattereth<sup><a href="h2219.htm">2219</a></sup> the wicked,<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> and bringeth<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> the wheel<sup><a href="h0212.htm">212</a></sup> over<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> them. </p>
 <p>27  The spirit<sup><a href="h5397.htm">5397</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> is the candle<sup><a href="h5216.htm">5216</a></sup> of the LORD,<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> searching<sup><a href="h2664.htm">2664</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> the inward parts<sup><a href="h2315.htm">2315</a></sup> of the belly.<sup><a href="h0990.htm">990</a></sup> </p>
 <p>28  Mercy<sup><a href="h2617.htm">2617</a></sup> and truth<sup><a href="h0571.htm">571</a></sup> preserve<sup><a href="h5341.htm">5341</a></sup> the king:<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> and his throne<sup><a href="h3678.htm">3678</a></sup> is upheld<sup><a href="h5582.htm">5582</a></sup> by mercy.<sup><a href="h2617.htm">2617</a></sup> </p>
 <p>29  The glory<sup><a href="h8597.htm">8597</a></sup> of young men<sup><a href="h0970.htm">970</a></sup> is their strength:<sup><a href="h3581.htm">3581</a></sup> and the beauty<sup><a href="h1926.htm">1926</a></sup> of old men<sup><a href="h2205.htm">2205</a></sup> is the gray head.<sup><a href="h7872.htm">7872</a></sup> </p>

 <p>2 οὐ διαφέρει ἀπειλὴ βασιλέως θυμοῦ λέοντος ὁ δὲ παροξύνων αὐτὸν ἁμαρτάνει εἰς τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ψυχήν </p>
 <p>3 δόξα ἀνδρὶ ἀποστρέφεσθαι λοιδορίας πᾶς δὲ ἄφρων τοιούτοις συμπλέκεται </p>
 <p>4 ὀνειδιζόμενος ὀκνηρὸς οὐκ αἰσχύνεται ὡσαύτως καὶ ὁ δανιζόμενος σῖτον ἐν ἀμήτῳ </p>
 <p>5 ὕδωρ βαθὺ βουλὴ ἐν καρδίᾳ ἀνδρός ἀνὴρ δὲ φρόνιμος ἐξαντλήσει αὐτήν </p>
 <p>6 μέγα ἄνθρωπος καὶ τίμιον ἀνὴρ ἐλεήμων ἄνδρα δὲ πιστὸν ἔργον εὑρεῖν </p>
 <p>7 ὃς ἀναστρέφεται ἄμωμος ἐν δικαιοσύνῃ μακαρίους τοὺς παῖδας αὐτοῦ καταλείψει </p>
 <p>8 ὅταν βασιλεὺς δίκαιος καθίσῃ ἐπὶ θρόνου οὐκ ἐναντιοῦται ἐν ὀφθαλμοῖς αὐτοῦ πᾶν πονηρόν </p>
 <p>9 τίς καυχήσεται ἁγνὴν ἔχειν τὴν καρδίαν ἢ τίς παρρησιάσεται καθαρὸς εἶναι ἀπὸ ἁμαρτιῶν  <br>
  κακολογοῦντος πατέρα ἢ μητέρα σβεσθήσεται λαμπτήρ αἱ δὲ κόραι τῶν ὀφθαλμῶν αὐτοῦ ὄψονται σκότος  <br>
  μερὶς ἐπισπουδαζομένη ἐν πρώτοις ἐν τοῖς τελευταίοις οὐκ εὐλογηθήσεται  <br>
  μὴ εἴπῃς τείσομαι τὸν ἐχθρόν ἀλλὰ ὑπόμεινον τὸν κύριον ἵνα σοι βοηθήσῃ </p>
 <p>10 στάθμιον μέγα καὶ μικρὸν καὶ μέτρα δισσά ἀκάθαρτα ἐνώπιον κυρίου καὶ ἀμφότερα </p>
 <p>11 καὶ ὁ ποιῶν αὐτὰ ἐν τοῖς ἐπιτηδεύμασιν αὐτοῦ συμποδισθήσεται νεανίσκος μετὰ ὁσίου καὶ εὐθεῖα ἡ ὁδὸς αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>12 οὖς ἀκούει καὶ ὀφθαλμὸς ὁρᾷ κυρίου ἔργα καὶ ἀμφότερα </p>
 <p>13 μὴ ἀγάπα καταλαλεῖν ἵνα μὴ ἐξαρθῇς διάνοιξον τοὺς ὀφθαλμούς σου καὶ ἐμπλήσθητι ἄρτων </p>
 <p>23 βδέλυγμα κυρίῳ δισσὸν στάθμιον καὶ ζυγὸς δόλιος οὐ καλὸν ἐνώπιον αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>24 παρὰ κυρίου εὐθύνεται τὰ διαβήματα ἀνδρί θνητὸς δὲ πῶς ἂν νοήσαι τὰς ὁδοὺς αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>25 παγὶς ἀνδρὶ ταχύ τι τῶν ἰδίων ἁγιάσαι μετὰ γὰρ τὸ εὔξασθαι μετανοεῖν γίνεται </p>
 <p>26 λικμήτωρ ἀσεβῶν βασιλεὺς σοφὸς καὶ ἐπιβαλεῖ αὐτοῖς τροχόν </p>
 <p>27 φῶς κυρίου πνοὴ ἀνθρώπων ὃς ἐρευνᾷ ταμίεια κοιλίας </p>
 <p>28 ἐλεημοσύνη καὶ ἀλήθεια φυλακὴ βασιλεῖ καὶ περικυκλώσουσιν ἐν δικαιοσύνῃ τὸν θρόνον αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>29 κόσμος νεανίαις σοφία δόξα δὲ πρεσβυτέρων πολιαί </p>
 <p>30 ὑπώπια καὶ συντρίμματα συναντᾷ κακοῖς πληγαὶ δὲ εἰς ταμίεια κοιλίας </p>

 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎11 ‏גַּ֣ם בְּ֭מַעֲלָלָיו יִתְנַכֶּר־נָ֑עַר אִם־זַ֖ךְ וְאִם־יָשָׁ֣ר פָּעֳלֽוֹ׃ </span></p>

 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎13 ‏אַל־תֶּֽאֱהַ֣ב שֵׁ֭נָה פֶּן־תִּוָּרֵ֑שׁ פְּקַ֖ח עֵינֶ֣יךָ שְֽׂבַֽע־לָֽחֶם׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎14 ‏רַ֣ע רַ֭ע יֹאמַ֣ר הַקּוֹנֶ֑ה וְאֹזֵ֥ל ל֝֗וֹ אָ֣ז יִתְהַלָּֽל׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎15 ‏יֵ֣שׁ זָ֭הָב וְרָב־פְּנִינִ֑ים וּכְלִ֥י יְ֝קָ֗ר שִׂפְתֵי־דָֽעַת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎16 ‏לְֽקַח־בִּ֭גְדוֹ כִּי־עָ֣רַב זָ֑ר וּבְעַ֖ד <font color="brown">נכרים</font> <font color="blue">נָכְרִיָּ֣ה</font> חַבְלֵֽהוּ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎17 ‏עָרֵ֣ב לָ֭אִישׁ לֶ֣חֶם שָׁ֑קֶר וְ֝אַחַ֗ר יִמָּֽלֵא־פִ֥יהוּ חָצָֽץ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎18 ‏מַ֭חֲשָׁבוֹת בְּעֵצָ֣ה תִכּ֑וֹן וּ֝בְתַחְבֻּל֗וֹת עֲשֵׂ֣ה מִלְחָמָֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎19 ‏גּֽוֹלֶה־סּ֭וֹד<font color="red"><sup>3</sup></font> הוֹלֵ֣ךְ רָכִ֑יל וּלְפֹתֶ֥ה שְׂ֝פָתָ֗יו לֹ֣א תִתְעָרָֽב׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎20 ‏מְ֭קַלֵּל אָבִ֣יו וְאִמּ֑וֹ יִֽדְעַ֥ךְ נֵ֝ר֗וֹ <font color="brown">באישון</font> <font color="blue">בֶּאֱשׁ֥וּן</font> חֹֽשֶׁךְ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎21 ‏נַ֭חֲלָה <font color="brown">מבחלת</font> <font color="blue">מְבֹהֶ֣לֶת</font> בָּרִאשֹׁנָ֑ה וְ֝אַחֲרִיתָ֗הּ לֹ֣א תְבֹרָֽךְ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎22 ‏אַל־תֹּאמַ֥ר אֲשַׁלְּמָה־רָ֑ע קַוֵּ֥ה לַֽ֝יהוָ֗ה וְיֹ֣שַֽׁע לָֽךְ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎23 ‏תּוֹעֲבַ֣ת יְ֭הוָה אֶ֣בֶן וָאָ֑בֶן וּמֹאזְנֵ֖י מִרְמָ֣ה לֹא־טֽוֹב׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎24 ‏מֵיְהוָ֥ה מִצְעֲדֵי־גָ֑בֶר וְ֝אָדָ֗ם מַה־יָּבִ֥ין דַּרְכּֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎25 ‏מוֹקֵ֣שׁ אָ֭דָם יָ֣לַע קֹ֑דֶשׁ וְאַחַ֖ר נְדָרִ֣ים לְבַקֵּֽר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎26 ‏מְזָרֶ֣ה רְ֭שָׁעִים מֶ֣לֶךְ חָכָ֑ם וַיָּ֖שֶׁב עֲלֵיהֶ֣ם אוֹפָֽן׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎27 ‏נֵ֣ר יְ֭הוָה נִשְׁמַ֣ת אָדָ֑ם חֹ֝פֵ֗שׂ כָּל־חַדְרֵי־בָֽטֶן׃ </span></p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro020.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Luxuriosa res vinum, et tumultuosa ebrietas:<br> quicumque his delectatur non erit sapiens.<br></p>
 <p>2 Sicut rugitus leonis, ita et terror regis:<br> qui provocat eum peccat in animam suam.<br></p>
 <p>3 Honor est homini qui separat se a contentionibus;<br> omnes autem stulti miscentur contumeliis.<br></p>
 <p>4 Propter frigus piger arare noluit;<br> mendicabit ergo æstate, et non dabitur illi.<br></p>
 <p>5 Sicut aqua profunda, sic consilium in corde viri;<br> sed homo sapiens exhauriet illud.<br></p>
 <p>6 Multi homines misericordes vocantur;<br> virum autem fidelem quis inveniet?]<br>
 </p>
 <p>7 [Justus qui ambulat in simplicitate sua<br> beatos post se filios derelinquet.<br></p>
 <p>8 Rex qui sedet in solio judicii<br> dissipat omne malum intuitu suo.<br></p>
 <p>9 Quis potest dicere: Mundum est cor meum;<br> purus sum a peccato?<br></p>
 <p>10 Pondus et pondus, mensura et mensura:<br> utrumque abominabile est apud Deum.<br></p>
 <p>11 Ex studiis suis intelligitur puer,<br> si munda et recta sint opera ejus.<br></p>
 <p>12 Aurem audientem, et oculum videntem:<br> Dominus fecit utrumque.<br></p>
 <p>13 Noli diligere somnum, ne te egestas opprimat:<br> aperi oculos tuos, et saturare panibus.<br></p>
 <p>14 Malum est, malum est, dicit omnis emptor;<br> et cum recesserit, tunc gloriabitur.<br></p>
 <p>15 Est aurum et multitudo gemmarum,<br> et vas pretiosum labia scientiæ.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>16 [Tolle vestimentum ejus qui fidejussor extitit alieni,<br> et pro extraneis aufer pignus ab eo.<br></p>
 <p>17 Suavis est homini panis mendacii,<br> et postea implebitur os ejus calculo.<br></p>
 <p>18 Cogitationes consiliis roborantur,<br> et gubernaculis tractanda sunt bella.<br></p>
 <p>19 Ei qui revelat mysteria, et ambulat fraudulenter,<br> et dilatat labia sua, ne commiscearis.<br></p>
 <p>20 Qui maledicit patri suo et matri,<br> extinguetur lucerna ejus in mediis tenebris:<br></p>
 <p>21 hæreditas ad quam festinatur in principio,<br> in novissimo benedictione carebit.<br></p>
 <p>22 Ne dicas: Reddam malum:<br> exspecta Dominum, et liberabit te.<br></p>
 <p>23 Abominatio est apud Dominum pondus et pondus;<br> statera dolosa non est bona.<br></p>
 <p>24 A Domino diriguntur gressus viri:<br> quis autem hominum intelligere potest viam suam?<br></p>
 <p>25 Ruina est homini devorare sanctos,<br> et post vota retractare.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>26 [Dissipat impios rex sapiens,<br> et incurvat super eos fornicem.<br></p>
 <p>27 Lucerna Domini spiraculum hominis,<br> quæ investigat omnia secreta ventris.<br></p>
 <p>28 Misericordia et veritas custodiunt regem,<br> et roboratur clementia thronus ejus.<br></p>
 <p>29 Exsultatio juvenum fortitudo eorum,<br> et dignitas senum canities.<br></p>
 <p>30 Livor vulneris absterget mala,<br> et plagæ in secretioribus ventris.]</p>
