 <p>3  They drive away<sup><a href="h5090.htm">5090</a></sup> the ass<sup><a href="h2543.htm">2543</a></sup> of the fatherless,<sup><a href="h3490.htm">3490</a></sup> they take the widow's ox for a pledge.<sup><a href="h2254.htm">2254</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7794.htm">7794</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0490.htm">490</a></sup> </p>

 <p>5  Behold,<sup><a href="h2005.htm">2005</a></sup> as wild asses<sup><a href="h6501.htm">6501</a></sup> in the desert,<sup><a href="h4057.htm">4057</a></sup> go they forth<sup><a href="h3318.htm">3318</a></sup> to their work;<sup><a href="h6467.htm">6467</a></sup> rising quickly<sup><a href="h7836.htm">7836</a></sup> for a prey:<sup><a href="h2964.htm">2964</a></sup> the wilderness<sup><a href="h6160.htm">6160</a></sup> yieldeth food<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> for them and for their children.<sup><a href="h5288.htm">5288</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  They reap<sup><a href="h7114.htm">7114</a></sup> every one his corn<sup><a href="h1098.htm">1098</a></sup> in the field:<sup><a href="h7704.htm">7704</a></sup> and they gather<sup><a href="h3953.htm">3953</a></sup> the vintage<sup><a href="h3754.htm">3754</a></sup> of the wicked.<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  They cause the naked<sup><a href="h6174.htm">6174</a></sup> to lodge<sup><a href="h3885.htm">3885</a></sup> without<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1097.htm">1097</a></sup> clothing,<sup><a href="h3830.htm">3830</a></sup> that they have no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> covering<sup><a href="h3682.htm">3682</a></sup> in the cold.<sup><a href="h7135.htm">7135</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  They are wet<sup><a href="h7372.htm">7372</a></sup> with the showers<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2230.htm">2230</a></sup> of the mountains,<sup><a href="h2022.htm">2022</a></sup> and embrace<sup><a href="h2263.htm">2263</a></sup> the rock<sup><a href="h6697.htm">6697</a></sup> for want<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1097.htm">1097</a></sup> of a shelter.<sup><a href="h4268.htm">4268</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  They pluck<sup><a href="h1497.htm">1497</a></sup> the fatherless<sup><a href="h3490.htm">3490</a></sup> from the breast,<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7699.htm">7699</a></sup> and take a pledge<sup><a href="h2254.htm">2254</a></sup> of<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the poor.<sup><a href="h6041.htm">6041</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  They cause him to go<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> naked<sup><a href="h6174.htm">6174</a></sup> without<sup><a href="h1097.htm">1097</a></sup> clothing,<sup><a href="h3830.htm">3830</a></sup> and they take away<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> the sheaf<sup><a href="h6016.htm">6016</a></sup> from the hungry;<sup><a href="h7457.htm">7457</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  Which make oil<sup><a href="h6671.htm">6671</a></sup> within<sup><a href="h0996.htm">996</a></sup> their walls,<sup><a href="h7791.htm">7791</a></sup> and tread<sup><a href="h1869.htm">1869</a></sup> their winepresses,<sup><a href="h3342.htm">3342</a></sup> and suffer thirst.<sup><a href="h6770.htm">6770</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  Men<sup><a href="h4962.htm">4962</a></sup> groan<sup><a href="h5008.htm">5008</a></sup> from out of the city,<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5892.htm">5892</a></sup> and the soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> of the wounded<sup><a href="h2491.htm">2491</a></sup> crieth out:<sup><a href="h7768.htm">7768</a></sup> yet God<sup><a href="h0433.htm">433</a></sup> layeth<sup><a href="h7760.htm">7760</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> folly<sup><a href="h8604.htm">8604</a></sup> to them. </p>
 <p>13  They<sup><a href="h1992.htm">1992</a></sup> are<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> of those that rebel<sup><a href="h4775.htm">4775</a></sup> against the light;<sup><a href="h0216.htm">216</a></sup> they know<sup><a href="h5234.htm">5234</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> the ways<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> thereof, nor<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> abide<sup><a href="h3427.htm">3427</a></sup> in the paths<sup><a href="h5410.htm">5410</a></sup> thereof. </p>
 <p>14  The murderer<sup><a href="h7523.htm">7523</a></sup> rising<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> with the light<sup><a href="h0216.htm">216</a></sup> killeth<sup><a href="h6991.htm">6991</a></sup> the poor<sup><a href="h6041.htm">6041</a></sup> and needy,<sup><a href="h0034.htm">34</a></sup> and in the night<sup><a href="h3915.htm">3915</a></sup> is<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> as a thief.<sup><a href="h1590.htm">1590</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  The eye<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> also of the adulterer<sup><a href="h5003.htm">5003</a></sup> waiteth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> for the twilight,<sup><a href="h5399.htm">5399</a></sup> saying,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> No<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> eye<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> shall see<sup><a href="h7789.htm">7789</a></sup> me: and disguiseth<sup><a href="h5643.htm">5643</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7760.htm">7760</a></sup> his face.<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  In the dark<sup><a href="h2822.htm">2822</a></sup> they dig through<sup><a href="h2864.htm">2864</a></sup> houses,<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> which they had marked<sup><a href="h2856.htm">2856</a></sup> for themselves in the daytime:<sup><a href="h3119.htm">3119</a></sup> they know<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> the light.<sup><a href="h0216.htm">216</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> the morning<sup><a href="h1242.htm">1242</a></sup> is to them even as<sup><a href="h3162.htm">3162</a></sup> the shadow of death:<sup><a href="h6757.htm">6757</a></sup> if<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> one know<sup><a href="h5234.htm">5234</a></sup> them, they are in the terrors<sup><a href="h1091.htm">1091</a></sup> of the shadow of death.<sup><a href="h6757.htm">6757</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  He<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> is swift<sup><a href="h7031.htm">7031</a></sup> as<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> the waters;<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> their portion<sup><a href="h2513.htm">2513</a></sup> is cursed<sup><a href="h7043.htm">7043</a></sup> in the earth:<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> he beholdeth<sup><a href="h6437.htm">6437</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> the way<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> of the vineyards.<sup><a href="h3754.htm">3754</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  Drought<sup><a href="h6723.htm">6723</a></sup> and<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> heat<sup><a href="h2527.htm">2527</a></sup> consume<sup><a href="h1497.htm">1497</a></sup> the snow<sup><a href="h7950.htm">7950</a></sup> waters:<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> so doth the grave<sup><a href="h7585.htm">7585</a></sup> those which have sinned.<sup><a href="h2398.htm">2398</a></sup> </p>

 <p>21  He evil entreateth<sup><a href="h7462.htm">7462</a></sup> the barren<sup><a href="h6135.htm">6135</a></sup> that beareth<sup><a href="h3205.htm">3205</a></sup> not:<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> and doeth not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good<sup><a href="h3190.htm">3190</a></sup> to the widow.<sup><a href="h0490.htm">490</a></sup> </p>

 <p>23  Though it be given<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> him to be in safety,<sup><a href="h0983.htm">983</a></sup> whereon he resteth;<sup><a href="h8172.htm">8172</a></sup> yet his eyes<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> are upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> their ways.<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/job024.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Ab Omnipotente non sunt abscondita tempora:<br> qui autem noverunt eum,<br> ignorant dies illius.<br></p>
 <p>2 Alii terminos transtulerunt;<br> diripuerunt greges, et paverunt eos.<br></p>
 <p>3 Asinum pupillorum abegerunt,<br> et abstulerunt pro pignore bovem viduæ.<br></p>
 <p>4 Subverterunt pauperum viam,<br> et oppresserunt pariter mansuetos terræ.<br></p>
 <p>5 Alii quasi onagri in deserto egrediuntur ad opus suum:<br> vigilantes ad prædam, præparant panem liberis.<br></p>
 <p>6 Agrum non suum demetunt,<br> et vineam ejus, quem vi oppresserint, vindemiant.<br></p>
 <p>7 Nudos dimittunt homines, indumenta tollentes,<br> quibus non est operimentum in frigore:<br></p>
 <p>8 quos imbres montium rigant,<br> et non habentes velamen, amplexantur lapides.<br></p>
 <p>9 Vim fecerunt deprædantes pupillos,<br> et vulgum pauperem spoliaverunt.<br></p>
 <p>10 Nudis et incedentibus absque vestitu,<br> et esurientibus tulerunt spicas.<br></p>
 <p>11 Inter acervos eorum meridiati sunt,<br> qui calcatis torcularibus sitiunt.<br></p>
 <p>12 De civitatibus fecerunt viros gemere,<br> et anima vulneratorum clamavit:<br> et Deus inultum abire non patitur.<br></p>
 <p>13 Ipsi fuerunt rebelles lumini:<br> nescierunt vias ejus,<br> nec reversi sunt per semitas ejus.<br></p>
 <p>14 Mane primo consurgit homicida;<br> interficit egenum et pauperem:<br> per noctem vero erit quasi fur.<br></p>
 <p>15 Oculus adulteri observat caliginem,<br> dicens: Non me videbit oculus:<br> et operiet vultum suum.<br></p>
 <p>16 Perfodit in tenebris domos,<br> sicut in die condixerant sibi,<br> et ignoraverunt lucem.<br></p>
 <p>17 Si subito apparuerit aurora, arbitrantur umbram mortis:<br> et sic in tenebris quasi in luce ambulant.<br></p>
 <p>18 Levis est super faciem aquæ:<br> maledicta sit pars ejus in terra,<br> nec ambulet per viam vinearum.<br></p>
 <p>19 Ad nimium calorem transeat ab aquis nivium,<br> et usque ad inferos peccatum illius.<br></p>
 <p>20 Obliviscatur ejus misericordia; dulcedo illius vermes:<br> non sit in recordatione,<br> sed conteratur quasi lignum infructuosum.<br></p>
 <p>21 Pavit enim sterilem quæ non parit,<br> et viduæ bene non fecit.<br></p>
 <p>22 Detraxit fortes in fortitudine sua,<br> et cum steterit, non credet vitæ suæ.<br></p>
 <p>23 Dedit ei Deus locum pnitentiæ,<br> et ille abutitur eo in superbiam:<br> oculi autem ejus sunt in viis illius.<br></p>
 <p>24 Elevati sunt ad modicum, et non subsistent:<br> et humiliabuntur sicut omnia, et auferentur,<br> et sicut summitates spicarum conterentur.<br></p>
 <p>25 Quod si non est ita, quis me potest arguere esse mentitum,<br> et ponere ante Deum verba mea?]</p>
