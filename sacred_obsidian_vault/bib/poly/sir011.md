 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 11</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir011.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 Wisdom lifteth up the head of him that is of low degree, and maketh him to sit among great men. </p>
 <p>2 Commend not a man for his beauty; neither abhor a man for his outward appearance. </p>
 <p>3 The bee is little among such as fly; but her fruit is the chief of sweet things. </p>
 <p>4 Boast not of thy clothing and raiment, and exalt not thyself in the day of honour: for the works of the Lord are wonderful, and his works among men are hidden. </p>
 <p>5 Many kings have sat down upon the ground; and one that was never thought of hath worn the crown. </p>
 <p>6 Many mighty men have been greatly disgraced; and the honourable delivered into other men's hands. </p>
 <p>7 Blame not before thou hast examined the truth: understand first, and then rebuke. </p>
 <p>8 Answer not before thou hast heard the cause: neither interrupt men in the midst of their talk. </p>
 <p>9 Strive not in a matter that concerneth thee not; and sit not in judgment with sinners. </p>
 <p>10 My son, meddle not with many matters: for if thou meddle much, thou shalt not be innocent; and if thou follow after, thou shalt not obtain, neither shalt thou escape by fleeing. </p>
 <p>11 There is one that laboureth, and taketh pains, and maketh haste, and is so much the more behind. </p>
 <p>12 Again, there is another that is slow, and hath need of help, wanting ability, and full of poverty; yet the eye of the Lord looked upon him for good, and set him up from his low estate, </p>
 <p>13 And lifted up his head from misery; so that many that saw from him is peace over all the </p>
 <p>14 Prosperity and adversity, life and death, poverty and riches, come of the Lord. </p>
 <p>15 Wisdom, knowledge, and understanding of the law, are of the Lord: love, and the way of good works, are from him. </p>
 <p>16 Error and darkness had their beginning together with sinners: and evil shall wax old with them that glory therein. </p>
 <p>17 The gift of the Lord remaineth with the ungodly, and his favour bringeth prosperity for ever. </p>
 <p>18 There is that waxeth rich by his wariness and pinching, and this his the portion of his reward: </p>
 <p>19 Whereas he saith, I have found rest, and now will eat continually of my goods; and yet he knoweth not what time shall come upon him, and that he must leave those things to others, and die. </p>
 <p>20 Be stedfast in thy covenant, and be conversant therein, and wax old in thy work. </p>
 <p>21 Marvel not at the works of sinners; but trust in the Lord, and abide in thy labour: for it is an easy thing in the sight of the Lord on the sudden to make a poor man rich. </p>
 <p>22 The blessing of the Lord is in the reward of the godly, and suddenly he maketh his blessing flourish. </p>
 <p>23 Say not, What profit is there of my service? and what good things shall I have hereafter? </p>
 <p>24 Again, say not, I have enough, and possess many things, and what evil shall I have hereafter? </p>
 <p>25 In the day of prosperity there is a forgetfulness of affliction: and in the day of affliction there is no more remembrance of prosperity. </p>
 <p>26 For it is an easy thing unto the Lord in the day of death to reward a man according to his ways. </p>
 <p>27 The affliction of an hour maketh a man forget pleasure: and in his end his deeds shall be discovered. </p>
 <p>28 Judge none blessed before his death: for a man shall be known in his children. </p>
 <p>29 Bring not every man into thine house: for the deceitful man hath many trains. </p>
 <p>30 Like as a partridge taken [and kept] in a cage, so is the heart of the proud; and like as a spy, watcheth he for thy fall: </p>
 <p>31 For he lieth in wait, and turneth good into evil, and in things worthy praise will lay blame upon thee. </p>
 <p>32 Of a spark of fire a heap of coals is kindled: and a sinful man layeth wait for blood. </p>
 <p>33 Take heed of a mischievous man, for he worketh wickedness; lest he bring upon thee a perpetual blot. </p>
 <p>34 Receive a stranger into thine house, and he will disturb thee, and turn thee out of thine own. </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir011.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 σοφία ταπεινοῦ ἀνυψώσει κεφαλὴν αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐν μέσῳ μεγιστάνων καθίσει αὐτόν </p>
 <p>2 μὴ αἰνέσῃς ἄνδρα ἐν κάλλει αὐτοῦ καὶ μὴ βδελύξῃ ἄνθρωπον ἐν ὁράσει αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>3 μικρὰ ἐν πετεινοῖς μέλισσα καὶ ἀρχὴ γλυκασμάτων ὁ καρπὸς αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>4 ἐν περιβολῇ ἱματίων μὴ καυχήσῃ καὶ ἐν ἡμέρᾳ δόξης μὴ ἐπαίρου ὅτι θαυμαστὰ τὰ ἔργα κυρίου καὶ κρυπτὰ τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ ἀνθρώποις </p>
 <p>5 πολλοὶ τύραννοι ἐκάθισαν ἐπὶ ἐδάφους ὁ δὲ ἀνυπονόητος ἐφόρεσεν διάδημα </p>
 <p>6 πολλοὶ δυνάσται ἠτιμάσθησαν σφόδρα καὶ ἔνδοξοι παρεδόθησαν εἰς χεῖρας ἑτέρων </p>
 <p>7 πρὶν ἐξετάσῃς μὴ μέμψῃ νόησον πρῶτον καὶ τότε ἐπιτίμα </p>
 <p>8 πρὶν ἢ ἀκοῦσαι μὴ ἀποκρίνου καὶ ἐν μέσῳ λόγων μὴ παρεμβάλλου </p>
 <p>9 περὶ πράγματος οὗ οὐκ ἔστιν σοι χρεία μὴ ἔριζε καὶ ἐν κρίσει ἁμαρτωλῶν μὴ συνέδρευε </p>
 <p>10 τέκνον μὴ περὶ πολλὰ ἔστωσαν αἱ πράξεις σου ἐὰν πληθύνῃς οὐκ ἀθῳωθήσῃ καὶ ἐὰν διώκῃς οὐ μὴ καταλάβῃς καὶ οὐ μὴ ἐκφύγῃς διαδράς </p>
 <p>11 ἔστιν κοπιῶν καὶ πονῶν καὶ σπεύδων καὶ τόσῳ μᾶλλον ὑστερεῖται </p>
 <p>12 ἔστιν νωθρὸς προσδεόμενος ἀντιλήμψεως ὑστερῶν ἰσχύι καὶ πτωχείᾳ περισσεύει καὶ οἱ ὀφθαλμοὶ κυρίου ἐπέβλεψαν αὐτῷ εἰς ἀγαθά καὶ ἀνώρθωσεν αὐτὸν ἐκ ταπεινώσεως αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>13 καὶ ἀνύψωσεν κεφαλὴν αὐτοῦ καὶ ἀπεθαύμασαν ἐπ᾽ αὐτῷ πολλοί </p>
 <p>14 ἀγαθὰ καὶ κακά ζωὴ καὶ θάνατος πτωχεία καὶ πλοῦτος παρὰ κυρίου ἐστίν </p>
 <p>17 δόσις κυρίου παραμένει εὐσεβέσιν καὶ ἡ εὐδοκία αὐτοῦ εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα εὐοδωθήσεται </p>
 <p>18 ἔστιν πλουτῶν ἀπὸ προσοχῆς καὶ σφιγγίας αὐτοῦ καὶ αὕτη ἡ μερὶς τοῦ μισθοῦ αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>19 ἐν τῷ εἰπεῖν αὐτόν εὗρον ἀνάπαυσιν καὶ νῦν φάγομαι ἐκ τῶν ἀγαθῶν μου καὶ οὐκ οἶδεν τίς καιρὸς παρελεύσεται καὶ καταλείψει αὐτὰ ἑτέροις καὶ ἀποθανεῖται </p>
 <p>20 στῆθι ἐν διαθήκῃ σου καὶ ὁμίλει ἐν αὐτῇ καὶ ἐν τῷ ἔργῳ σου παλαιώθητι </p>
 <p>21 μὴ θαύμαζε ἐν ἔργοις ἁμαρτωλοῦ πίστευε δὲ κυρίῳ καὶ ἔμμενε τῷ πόνῳ σου ὅτι κοῦφον ἐν ὀφθαλμοῖς κυρίου διὰ τάχους ἐξάπινα πλουτίσαι πένητα </p>
 <p>22 εὐλογία κυρίου ἐν μισθῷ εὐσεβοῦς καὶ ἐν ὥρᾳ ταχινῇ ἀναθάλλει εὐλογίαν αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>23 μὴ εἴπῃς τίς ἐστίν μου χρεία καὶ τίνα ἀπὸ τοῦ νῦν ἔσται μου τὰ ἀγαθά </p>
 <p>24 μὴ εἴπῃς αὐτάρκη μοί ἐστιν καὶ τί ἀπὸ τοῦ νῦν κακωθήσομαι </p>
 <p>25 ἐν ἡμέρᾳ ἀγαθῶν ἀμνησία κακῶν καὶ ἐν ἡμέρᾳ κακῶν οὐ μνησθήσεται ἀγαθῶν </p>
 <p>26 ὅτι κοῦφον ἔναντι κυρίου ἐν ἡμέρᾳ τελευτῆς ἀποδοῦναι ἀνθρώπῳ κατὰ τὰς ὁδοὺς αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>27 κάκωσις ὥρας ἐπιλησμονὴν ποιεῖ τρυφῆς καὶ ἐν συντελείᾳ ἀνθρώπου ἀποκάλυψις ἔργων αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>28 πρὸ τελευτῆς μὴ μακάριζε μηδένα καὶ ἐν τέκνοις αὐτοῦ γνωσθήσεται ἀνήρ </p>
 <p>29 μὴ πάντα ἄνθρωπον εἴσαγε εἰς τὸν οἶκόν σου πολλὰ γὰρ τὰ ἔνεδρα τοῦ δολίου </p>
 <p>30 πέρδιξ θηρευτὴς ἐν καρτάλλῳ οὕτως καρδία ὑπερηφάνου καὶ ὡς ὁ κατάσκοπος ἐπιβλέπει πτῶσιν </p>
 <p>31 τὰ γὰρ ἀγαθὰ εἰς κακὰ μεταστρέφων ἐνεδρεύει καὶ ἐν τοῖς αἱρετοῖς ἐπιθήσει μῶμον </p>
 <p>32 ἀπὸ σπινθῆρος πυρὸς πληθύνεται ἀνθρακιά καὶ ἄνθρωπος ἁμαρτωλὸς εἰς αἷμα ἐνεδρεύει </p>
 <p>33 πρόσεχε ἀπὸ κακούργου πονηρὰ γὰρ τεκταίνει μήποτε μῶμον εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα δῷ σοι </p>
 <p>34 ἐνοίκισον ἀλλότριον καὶ διαστρέψει σε ἐν ταραχαῖς καὶ ἀπαλλοτριώσει σε τῶν ἰδίων σου </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir011.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Sapientia humiliati exaltabit caput illius,<br> et in medio magnatorum consedere illum faciet.<br></p>
 <p>2 Non laudes virum in specie sua,<br> neque spernas hominem in visu suo.<br></p>
 <p>3 Brevis in volatilibus est apis,<br> et initium dulcoris habet fructus illius.<br></p>
 <p>4 In vestitu ne glorieris umquam,<br> nec in die honoris tui extollaris:<br> quoniam mirabilia opera Altissimi solius,<br> et gloriosa, et absconsa, et invisa opera illius.<br></p>
 <p>5 Multi tyranni sederunt in throno:<br> et insuspicabilis portavit diadema.<br></p>
 <p>6 Multi potentes oppressi sunt valide,<br> et gloriosi traditi sunt in manus alterorum.<br></p>
 <p>7 Priusquam interroges, ne vituperes quemquam:<br> et cum interrogaveris, corripe juste.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>8 [Priusquam audias, ne respondeas verbum:<br> et in medio sermonum ne adjicias loqui.<br></p>
 <p>9 De ea re quæ te non molestat, ne certeris:<br> et in judicio peccantium ne consistas.<br></p>
 <p>10 Fili, ne in multis sint actus tui:<br> et si dives fueris, non eris immunis a delicto.<br> Si enim secutus fueris, non apprehendes:<br> et non effugies, si præcucurreris.<br></p>
 <p>11 Est homo laborans et festinans, et dolens:<br> impius, et tanto magis non abundabit.<br></p>
 <p>12 Est homo marcidus egens recuperatione,<br> plus deficiens virtute, et abundans paupertate:<br></p>
 <p>13 et oculus Dei respexit illum in bono,<br> et erexit eum ab humilitate ipsius, et exaltavit caput ejus:<br> et mirati sunt in illo multi, et honoraverunt Deum.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>14 [Bona et mala, vita et mors,<br> paupertas et honestas, a Deo sunt:<br></p>
 <p>15 sapientia, et disciplina, et scientia legis, apud Deum:<br> dilectio, et viæ bonorum, apud ipsum.<br></p>
 <p>16 Error et tenebræ peccatoribus concreata sunt:<br> qui autem exsultant in malis consenescunt in malo.<br></p>
 <p>17 Datio Dei permanet justis,<br> et profectus illius successus habebit in æternum.<br></p>
 <p>18 Est qui locupletatur parce agendo,<br> et hæc est pars mercedis illius.<br></p>
 <p>19 In eo quod dicit: Inveni requiem mihi,<br> et nunc manducabo de bonis meis solus:<br></p>
 <p>20 et nescit quod tempus præteriet, et mors appropinquet,<br> et relinquat omnia aliis, et morietur.<br></p>
 <p>21 Sta in testamento tuo, et in illo colloquere,<br> et in opere mandatorum tuorum veterasce.<br></p>
 <p>22 Ne manseris in operibus peccatorum:<br> confide autem in Deo, et mane in loco tuo.<br></p>
 <p>23 Facile est enim in oculis Dei<br> subito honestare pauperem.<br></p>
 <p>24 Benedictio Dei in mercedem justi festinat,<br> et in hora veloci processus illius fructificat.<br></p>
 <p>25 Ne dicas: Quid est mihi opus?<br> et quæ erunt mihi ex hoc bona?<br></p>
 <p>26 Ne dicas: Sufficiens mihi sum:<br> et quid ex hoc pessimabor?<br></p>
 <p>27 In die bonorum ne immemor sis malorum,<br> et in die malorum ne immemor sis bonorum:<br></p>
 <p>28 quoniam facile est coram Deo in die obitus<br> retribuere unicuique secundum vias suas.<br></p>
 <p>29 Malitia horæ oblivionem facit luxuriæ magnæ,<br> et in fine hominis denudatio operum illius.<br></p>
 <p>30 Ante mortem ne laudes hominem quemquam:<br> quoniam in filiis suis agnoscitur vir.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>31 [Non omnem hominem inducas in domum tuam:<br> multæ enim sunt insidiæ dolosi.<br></p>
 <p>32 Sicut enim eructant præcordia ftentium,<br> et sicut perdix inducitur in caveam, et ut caprea in laqueum:<br> sic et cor superborum,<br> et sicut prospector videns casum proximi sui.<br></p>
 <p>33 Bona enim in mala convertens insidiatur,<br> et in electis imponet maculam.<br></p>
 <p>34 A scintilla una augetur ignis,<br> et ab uno doloso augetur sanguis:<br> homo vero peccator sanguini insidiatur.<br></p>
 <p>35 Attende tibi a pestifero, fabricat enim mala,<br> ne inducat super te subsannationem in perpetuum.<br></p>
 <p>36 Admitte ad te alienigenam:<br> et subvertet te in turbine,<br> et abalienabit te a tuis propriis.]</p>
