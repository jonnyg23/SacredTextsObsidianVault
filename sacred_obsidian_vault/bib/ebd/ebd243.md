 <p><a name="000"><b>Maschil</b></a>     Instructing, occurs in the title of thirteen Psalms (<a href="../kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psa 32:1</a>, <a href="../kjv/psa042.htm#001">Psa 42:1</a>, 44, etc.). It denotes a song enforcing some lesson of wisdom or piety, a didactic song. In <a href="../kjv/psa047.htm#007">Psa 47:7</a> it is rendered, Authorized Version, "with understanding;" Revised Version, marg., "in a skillful psalm." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Mash</b></a>     =Meshech (<a href="../kjv/ch1001.htm#017">Ch1 1:17</a>), one of the four sons of Aram, and the name of a tribe descended from him (<a href="../kjv/gen010.htm#023">Gen 10:23</a>) inhabiting some part probably of Mesopotamia. Some have supposed that they were the inhabitants of Mount Masius, the present Karja Baghlar, which forms part of the chain of Taurus. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Mashal</b></a>     Entreaty, a levitical town in the tribe of Asher (<a href="../kjv/ch1006.htm#074">Ch1 6:74</a>); called Mishal (<a href="../kjv/jos021.htm#030">Jos 21:30</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Mason</b></a>     An artificer in stone. The Tyrians seem to have been specially skilled in architecture (<a href="../kjv/kg1005.htm#017">Kg1 5:17</a>, <a href="../kjv/kg1005.htm#018">Kg1 5:18</a>; <a href="../kjv/sa2005.htm#011">Sa2 5:11</a>). This art the Hebrews no doubt learned in Egypt (<a href="../kjv/exo001.htm#011">Exo 1:11</a>, <a href="../kjv/exo001.htm#014">Exo 1:14</a>), where ruins of temples and palaces fill the traveler with wonder at the present day. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Masrekah</b></a>     Vineyard of noble vines, a place in Idumea, the native place of Samlah, one of the Edomitish kings (<a href="../kjv/gen036.htm#036">Gen 36:36</a>; <a href="../kjv/ch1001.htm#047">Ch1 1:47</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Massa</b></a>     A lifting up, gift, one of the sons of Ishmael, the founder of an Arabian tribe (<a href="../kjv/gen025.htm#014">Gen 25:14</a>); a nomad tribe inhabiting the Arabian desert toward Babylonia. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Massah</b></a>     Trial, temptation, a name given to the place where the Israelites, by their murmuring for want of water, provoked Jehovah to anger against them. It is also called Meribah (<a href="../kjv/exo017.htm#007">Exo 17:7</a>; <a href="../kjv/deu006.htm#016">Deu 6:16</a>; <a href="../kjv/psa095.htm#008">Psa 95:8</a>, <a href="../kjv/psa095.htm#009">Psa 95:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/heb003.htm#008">Heb 3:8</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Mattan</b></a>     Gift.     (1.) A priest of Baal, slain before his altar during the reformation under Jehoiada (<a href="../kjv/kg2011.htm#018">Kg2 11:18</a>). (2.) The son of Eleazar, and father of Jacob, who was the father of Joseph, the husband of the Virgin Mary (<a href="../kjv/mat001.htm#015">Mat 1:15</a>). (3.) The father of Shephatiah (<a href="../kjv/jer038.htm#001">Jer 38:1</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Mattanah</b></a>     A gift, a station of the Israelites (<a href="../kjv/num021.htm#018">Num 21:18</a>, <a href="../kjv/num021.htm#019">Num 21:19</a>) between the desert and the borders of Moab, in the Wady Waleh. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Mattaniah</b></a>     Gift of Jehovah.     (1.) A Levite, son of Heman, the chief of the ninth class of temple singers (<a href="../kjv/ch1025.htm#004">Ch1 25:4</a>, <a href="../kjv/ch1025.htm#016">Ch1 25:16</a>). (2.) A Levite who assisted in purifying the temple at the reformation under Hezekiah (<a href="../kjv/ch2029.htm#013">Ch2 29:13</a>). (3.) The original name of Zedekiah (q.v.), the last of the kings of Judah (<a href="../kjv/kg2024.htm#017">Kg2 24:17</a>). He was the third son of Josiah, who fell at Megiddo. He succeeded his nephew Jehoiakin. 
 </p>
