 <p><a name="000"><b>Shamgar</b></a>     The Philistines from the maritime plain had made incursions into the Hebrew upland for the purposes of plunder, when one of this name, the son of Anath, otherwise unknown, headed a rising for the purpose of freeing the land from this oppression. He repelled the invasion, slaying 600 men with an "ox goad" (q.v.). The goad was a formidable sharp pointed instrument, sometimes ten feet long. He was probably contemporary for a time with Deborah and Barak (<a href="../kjv/jdg003.htm#031">Jdg 3:31</a>; <a href="../kjv/jdg005.htm#006">Jdg 5:6</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Shamir</b></a>     A sharp thorn.     (1.) One of the sons of Michah (<a href="../kjv/ch1024.htm#024">Ch1 24:24</a>). (2.) A town among the mountains of Judah (<a href="../kjv/jos015.htm#048">Jos 15:48</a>); probably Somerah, 2 1/2 miles north-west of Debir. (3.) The residence of Tola, one of the judges, on Mount Ephraim (<a href="../kjv/jdg010.htm#001">Jdg 10:1</a>, <a href="../kjv/jdg010.htm#002">Jdg 10:2</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Shammah</b></a>     Desert.     (1.) One of the "dukes" of Edom (<a href="../kjv/gen036.htm#013">Gen 36:13</a>, <a href="../kjv/gen036.htm#017">Gen 36:17</a>). (2.) One of the sons of Jesse (<a href="../kjv/sa1016.htm#009">Sa1 16:9</a>). He is also called Shimeah (<a href="../kjv/sa2013.htm#003">Sa2 13:3</a>) and Shimma (<a href="../kjv/ch1002.htm#013">Ch1 2:13</a>). (3.) One of David's three mighty men (<a href="../kjv/sa2023.htm#011">Sa2 23:11</a>, <a href="../kjv/sa2023.htm#012">Sa2 23:12</a>). (4.) One of David's mighties (<a href="../kjv/sa2023.htm#025">Sa2 23:25</a>); called also Shammoth (<a href="../kjv/ch1011.htm#027">Ch1 11:27</a>) and Shamhuth (<a href="../kjv/ch1027.htm#008">Ch1 27:8</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Shammua</b></a>     Heard.     (1.) One of the spies sent out by Moses to search the land (<a href="../kjv/num013.htm#004">Num 13:4</a>). He represented the tribe of Reuben. (2.) One of David's sons (<a href="../kjv/ch1014.htm#004">Ch1 14:4</a>; <a href="../kjv/ch1003.htm#005">Ch1 3:5</a>, "Shimea;" <a href="../kjv/sa2005.htm#014">Sa2 5:14</a>). (3.) A Levite under Nehemiah (<a href="../kjv/neh011.htm#017">Neh 11:17</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Shaphan</b></a>     A coney, a scribe or secretary of king Josiah (<a href="../kjv/kg2022.htm#003">Kg2 22:3</a>). He consulted Huldah concerning the newly-discovered copy of the law which was delivered to him by Hilkiah the priest (<a href="../kjv/kg2022.htm#008">Kg2 22:8</a>). His grandson Gedaliah was governor of Judea (<a href="../kjv/kg2025.htm#022">Kg2 25:22</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Shaphat</b></a>     Judge.     (1.) One of the spies. He represented the tribe of Simeon (<a href="../kjv/num013.htm#005">Num 13:5</a>). (2.) The father of Elisha (<a href="../kjv/kg1019.htm#016">Kg1 19:16</a>). (3.) One of David's chief herdsmen (<a href="../kjv/ch1027.htm#029">Ch1 27:29</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Shapher</b></a>     Brightness, one of the stations where Israel encamped in the wilderness (<a href="../kjv/num033.htm#023">Num 33:23</a>, <a href="../kjv/num033.htm#024">Num 33:24</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Sharaim</b></a>     Two gates (<a href="../kjv/jos015.htm#036">Jos 15:36</a>), more correctly Shaaraim (<a href="../kjv/sa1017.htm#052">Sa1 17:52</a>), probably Tell Zakariya and Kefr Zakariya, in the valley of Elah, 3 1/2 miles north-west of Socoh. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Sharezer</b></a>     (god) protect the king!, a son of Sennacherib, king of Assyria. He and his brother Adrammelech murdered their father, and then fled into the land of Armenia (<a href="../kjv/kg2019.htm#037">Kg2 19:37</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Sharon</b></a>     Or Saron, a plain, a level tract extending from the Mediterranean to the hill country to the west of Jerusalem, about 30 miles long and from 8 to 15 miles broad, celebrated for its beauty and fertility (<a href="../kjv/ch1027.htm#029">Ch1 27:29</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa033.htm#009">Isa 33:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa035.htm#002">Isa 35:2</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa065.htm#010">Isa 65:10</a>). The "rose of Sharon" is celebrated (<a href="../kjv/sol002.htm#001">Sol 2:1</a>). It is called Lasharon (the article la being here a part of the word) in <a href="../kjv/jos012.htm#018">Jos 12:18</a>. 
 </p>
