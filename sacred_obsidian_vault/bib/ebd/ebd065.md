 <p><a name="000"><b>Bricks</b></a>     The making of, formed the chief labour of the Israelites in Egypt (<a href="../kjv/exo001.htm#013">Exo 1:13</a>, <a href="../kjv/exo001.htm#014">Exo 1:14</a>). Those found among the ruins of Babylon and Nineveh are about a foot square and four inches thick. They were usually dried in the sun, though also sometimes in kilns (<a href="../kjv/sa2012.htm#031">Sa2 12:31</a>; <a href="../kjv/jer043.htm#009">Jer 43:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/nah003.htm#014">Nah 3:14</a>). (See NEBUCHADNEZZAR.) The bricks used in the tower of Babel were burnt bricks, cemented in the building by bitumen (<a href="../kjv/gen011.htm#003">Gen 11:3</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Bride</b></a>     Frequently used in the ordinary sense (<a href="../kjv/isa049.htm#018">Isa 49:18</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa061.htm#010">Isa 61:10</a>, etc.). The relation between Christ and his church is set forth under the figure of that between a bridegroom and bride (<a href="../kjv/joh003.htm#029">Joh 3:29</a>). The church is called "the bride" (<a href="../kjv/rev021.htm#009">Rev 21:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/rev022.htm#017">Rev 22:17</a>). Compare parable of the Ten Virgins (<a href="../kjv/mat025.htm#001">Mat 25:1</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Bridle</b></a>     Three Hebrew words are thus rendered in the Authorized Version.     (1.) Heb.  mahsom  signifies a muzzle or halter or bridle, by which the rider governs his horse (<a href="../kjv/psa039.htm#001">Psa 39:1</a>). (2.) Metheg, rendered also "bit" in <a href="../kjv/psa032.htm#009">Psa 32:9</a>, which is its proper meaning. Found in <a href="../kjv/kg2019.htm#028">Kg2 19:28</a>, where the restraints of God's providence are metaphorically styled his "bridle" and "hook." God's placing a "bridle in the jaws of the people" (<a href="../kjv/isa030.htm#028">Isa 30:28</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa037.htm#029">Isa 37:29</a>) signifies his preventing the Assyrians from carrying out their purpose against Jerusalem. (3.) Another word, resen, was employed to represent a halter or bridle-rein, as used <a href="../kjv/psa032.htm#009">Psa 32:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa030.htm#028">Isa 30:28</a>. In <a href="../kjv/job030.htm#011">Job 30:11</a> the restraints of law and humanity are called a bridle. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Brier</b></a>     This word occurs frequently, and is the translation of several different terms.     (1.) <a href="../kjv/mic007.htm#004">Mic 7:4</a>, it denotes a species of thorn shrub used for hedges. In <a href="../kjv/pro015.htm#019">Pro 15:19</a> the word is rendered "thorn" (Heb.  hedek , "stinging"), supposed by some to be what is called the "apple of Sodom" (q.v.). (2.) <a href="../kjv/eze028.htm#024">Eze 28:24</a>, sallon, properly a "prickle," such as is found on the shoots of the palm tree. (3.) <a href="../kjv/isa055.htm#013">Isa 55:13</a>, probably simply a thorny bush. Some, following the Vulgate Version, regard it as the "nettle." (4.) <a href="../kjv/isa005.htm#006">Isa 5:6</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa007.htm#023">Isa 7:23</a>, etc., frequently used to denote thorny shrubs in general. In <a href="../kjv/isa010.htm#017">Isa 10:17</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa027.htm#004">Isa 27:4</a>, it means troublesome men. (5.) In <a href="../kjv/heb006.htm#008">Heb 6:8</a> the Greek word ( tribolos ) so rendered means "three-pronged," and denotes the land caltrop, a low thorny shrub resembling in its spikes the military "crow-foot." Compare <a href="../kjv/mat007.htm#016">Mat 7:16</a>, "thistle." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Brigandine</b></a>     (<a href="../kjv/jer046.htm#004">Jer 46:4</a>; <a href="../kjv/jer051.htm#003">Jer 51:3</a>), an obsolete English word denoting a scale coat of armour, or habergeon, worn by light armed "brigands." The Revised Version has "coat of mail." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Brimstone</b></a>     An inflammable mineral substance found in quantities on the shores of the Dead Sea. The cities of the plain were destroyed by a rain of fire and brimstone (<a href="../kjv/gen019.htm#024">Gen 19:24</a>, <a href="../kjv/gen019.htm#025">Gen 19:25</a>). In <a href="../kjv/isa034.htm#009">Isa 34:9</a> allusion is made to the destruction of these cities. This word figuratively denotes destruction or punishment (<a href="../kjv/job018.htm#015">Job 18:15</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa030.htm#033">Isa 30:33</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa034.htm#009">Isa 34:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/psa011.htm#006">Psa 11:6</a>; <a href="../kjv/eze038.htm#022">Eze 38:22</a>). It is used to express the idea of excruciating torment in <a href="../kjv/rev014.htm#010">Rev 14:10</a>; <a href="../kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>; <a href="../kjv/rev020.htm#010">Rev 20:10</a>. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Brook</b></a>     A torrent.     (1.) Applied to small streams, as the Arnon, Jabbok, etc. Isaiah (<a href="../kjv/isa015.htm#007">Isa 15:7</a>) speaks of the "book of the willows," probably the Wady-el-Asha. (2.) It is also applied to winter torrents (<a href="../kjv/job006.htm#015">Job 6:15</a>; <a href="../kjv/num034.htm#005">Num 34:5</a>; <a href="../kjv/jos015.htm#004">Jos 15:4</a>, <a href="../kjv/jos015.htm#047">Jos 15:47</a>), and to the torrent-bed or wady as well as to the torrent itself (<a href="../kjv/num013.htm#023">Num 13:23</a>; <a href="../kjv/kg1017.htm#003">Kg1 17:3</a>). (3.) In <a href="../kjv/isa019.htm#007">Isa 19:7</a> the river Nile is meant, as rendered in the Revised Version. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Brother</b></a>      (1.) In the natural and common sense (<a href="../kjv/mat001.htm#002">Mat 1:2</a>; <a href="../kjv/luk003.htm#001">Luk 3:1</a>, <a href="../kjv/luk003.htm#019">Luk 3:19</a>). (2.) A near relation, a cousin (<a href="../kjv/gen013.htm#008">Gen 13:8</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen014.htm#016">Gen 14:16</a>; <a href="../kjv/mat012.htm#046">Mat 12:46</a>; <a href="../kjv/joh007.htm#003">Joh 7:3</a>; <a href="../kjv/act001.htm#014">Act 1:14</a>; <a href="../kjv/gal001.htm#019">Gal 1:19</a>). (3.) Simply a fellow-countryman (<a href="../kjv/mat005.htm#047">Mat 5:47</a>; <a href="../kjv/act003.htm#022">Act 3:22</a>; <a href="../kjv/heb007.htm#005">Heb 7:5</a>). (4.) A disciple or follower (<a href="../kjv/mat025.htm#040">Mat 25:40</a>; <a href="../kjv/heb002.htm#011">Heb 2:11</a>, <a href="../kjv/heb002.htm#012">Heb 2:12</a>). (5.) One of the same faith (<a href="../kjv/amo001.htm#009">Amo 1:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/act009.htm#030">Act 9:30</a>; <a href="../kjv/act011.htm#029">Act 11:29</a>; <a href="../kjv/co1005.htm#011">Co1 5:11</a>); whence the early disciples of our Lord were known to each other as brethren. (6.) A colleague in office (<a href="../kjv/ezr003.htm#002">Ezr 3:2</a>; <a href="../kjv/co1001.htm#001">Co1 1:1</a>; <a href="../kjv/co2001.htm#001">Co2 1:1</a>). (7.) A fellow-man (<a href="../kjv/gen009.htm#005">Gen 9:5</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen019.htm#007">Gen 19:7</a>; <a href="../kjv/mat005.htm#022">Mat 5:22</a>, <a href="../kjv/mat005.htm#023">Mat 5:23</a>, <a href="../kjv/mat005.htm#024">Mat 5:24</a>; <a href="../kjv/mat007.htm#005">Mat 7:5</a>; <a href="../kjv/heb002.htm#017">Heb 2:17</a>). (8.) One beloved or closely united with another in affection (<a href="../kjv/sa2001.htm#026">Sa2 1:26</a>; <a href="../kjv/act006.htm#003">Act 6:3</a>; <a href="../kjv/th1005.htm#001">Th1 5:1</a>).    Brethren of Jesus (<a href="../kjv/mat001.htm#025">Mat 1:25</a>; <a href="../kjv/mat012.htm#046">Mat 12:46</a>, <a href="../kjv/mat012.htm#050">Mat 12:50</a>; <a href="../kjv/mar003.htm#031">Mar 3:31</a>, <a href="../kjv/mar003.htm#032">Mar 3:32</a>; <a href="../kjv/gal001.htm#019">Gal 1:19</a>; <a href="../kjv/co1009.htm#005">Co1 9:5</a>, etc.) were probably the younger children of Joseph and Mary. Some have supposed that they may have been the children of Joseph by a former marriage, and others that they were the children of Mary, the Virgin's sister, and wife of Cleophas. The first interpretation, however, is the most natural. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Bruit</b></a>     A rumour or report (<a href="../kjv/jer010.htm#022">Jer 10:22</a>, R.V. "rumour;" <a href="../kjv/nah003.htm#019">Nah 3:19</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Bucket</b></a>     A vessel to draw water with (<a href="../kjv/isa040.htm#015">Isa 40:15</a>); used figuratively, probably, of a numerous issue (<a href="../kjv/num024.htm#007">Num 24:7</a>). 
 </p>
