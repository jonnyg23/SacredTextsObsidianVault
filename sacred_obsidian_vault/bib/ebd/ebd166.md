 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Hashabiah</b></a>     Regarded by Jehovah.     (1.) Merarite Levite (<a href="../kjv/ch1006.htm#045">Ch1 6:45</a>; <a href="../kjv/ch1009.htm#014">Ch1 9:14</a>). (2.) A son of Jeduthun (<a href="../kjv/ch1025.htm#003">Ch1 25:3</a>, <a href="../kjv/ch1025.htm#019">Ch1 25:19</a>). (3.) Son of Kemuel (<a href="../kjv/ch1026.htm#030">Ch1 26:30</a>). (4.) One of the chief priests in the time of Ezra (<a href="../kjv/ezr008.htm#024">Ezr 8:24</a>). (7.) A chief of the Levites (<a href="../kjv/neh012.htm#024">Neh 12:24</a>). (8.) <a href="../kjv/ezr008.htm#019">Ezr 8:19</a>. (9.) <a href="../kjv/neh003.htm#017">Neh 3:17</a>. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Hashabniah</b></a>      (1.) <a href="../kjv/neh003.htm#010">Neh 3:10</a>. (2.) One of the Levites whom Ezra appointed to interpret the law to the people (<a href="../kjv/neh009.htm#005">Neh 9:5</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Hashbadana</b></a>     Consideration in judging stood at Ezra's left hand when he read the law (<a href="../kjv/neh008.htm#004">Neh 8:4</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Hashmonah</b></a>     Fatness, the thirtieth halting-place of the Israelites during their wanderings in the wilderness, not far from Mount Hor (<a href="../kjv/num033.htm#029">Num 33:29</a>, <a href="../kjv/num033.htm#030">Num 33:30</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Hashub</b></a>     Intelligent.     (1.) A Levite of the family of Merari (<a href="../kjv/neh011.htm#015">Neh 11:15</a>; <a href="../kjv/ch1009.htm#014">Ch1 9:14</a>). (2.) <a href="../kjv/neh003.htm#023">Neh 3:23</a>, <a href="../kjv/neh003.htm#011">Neh 3:11</a>. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Hashubah</b></a>     Ibid., a descendant of David (<a href="../kjv/ch1003.htm#020">Ch1 3:20</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Hashum</b></a>     Opulent.     (1.) <a href="../kjv/ezr002.htm#019">Ezr 2:19</a>; <a href="../kjv/neh007.htm#022">Neh 7:22</a>. (2.) Stood on Ezra's left hand while he read the law (<a href="../kjv/neh008.htm#004">Neh 8:4</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Hasrah</b></a>     Poverty, "keeper of the wardrobe," i.e., of the sacerdotal vestments (<a href="../kjv/ch2034.htm#022">Ch2 34:22</a>); called  Har'has  <a href="../kjv/kg2022.htm#014">Kg2 22:14</a>. He was husband of the prophetess Huldah.
