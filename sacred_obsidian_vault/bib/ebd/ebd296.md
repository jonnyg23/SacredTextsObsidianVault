 <p><a name="000"><b>Pipe</b></a>     (<a href="../kjv/sa1010.htm#005">Sa1 10:5</a>; <a href="../kjv/kg1001.htm#040">Kg1 1:40</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa005.htm#012">Isa 5:12</a>; <a href="../kjv/isa030.htm#029">Isa 30:29</a>). The Hebrew word halil, so rendered, means "bored through," and is the name given to various kinds of wind instruments, as the fife, flute, Pan-pipes, etc. In <a href="../kjv/amo006.htm#005">Amo 6:5</a> this word is rendered "instrument of music." This instrument is mentioned also in the New Testament (<a href="../kjv/mat011.htm#017">Mat 11:17</a>; <a href="../kjv/co1014.htm#007">Co1 14:7</a>). It is still used in Palestine, and is, as in ancient times, made of different materials, as reed, copper, bronze, etc. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Piram</b></a>     Like a wild ass, a king of Jarmuth, a royal city of the Canaanites, who was conquered and put to death by Joshua (<a href="../kjv/jos010.htm#003">Jos 10:3</a>, <a href="../kjv/jos010.htm#023">Jos 10:23</a>, <a href="../kjv/jos010.htm#026">Jos 10:26</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Pirathon</b></a>     Prince, or summit, a place "in the land of Ephraim" (<a href="../kjv/jdg012.htm#015">Jdg 12:15</a>), now Feron, some 10 miles south-west of Shechem. This was the home of Abdon the judge. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Pirathonite</b></a>      (1.) Abdon, the son of Hillel, so called, <a href="../kjv/jdg012.htm#013">Jdg 12:13</a>, <a href="../kjv/jdg012.htm#015">Jdg 12:15</a>. (2.) Benaiah the Ephraimite (<a href="../kjv/sa2023.htm#030">Sa2 23:30</a>), one of David's thirty heroes. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Pisgah</b></a>     A part, a mountain summit in the land of Moab, in the territory of Reuben, where Balak offered up sacrifices (<a href="../kjv/num021.htm#020">Num 21:20</a>; <a href="../kjv/num023.htm#014">Num 23:14</a>), and from which Moses viewed the promised land (<a href="../kjv/deu003.htm#027">Deu 3:27</a>). It is probably the modern Jebel Siaghah. (See NEBO.) 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Pisidia</b></a>     A district in Asia Minor, to the north of Pamphylia. The Taurus range of mountains extends through it. Antioch, one of its chief cities, was twice visited by Paul (<a href="../kjv/act013.htm#014">Act 13:14</a>; <a href="../kjv/act014.htm#021">Act 14:21</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Pison</b></a>     Babylonian, the current, broadflowing, one of the "four heads" into which the river which watered the garden of Eden was divided (<a href="../kjv/gen002.htm#011">Gen 2:11</a>). Some identify it with the modern Phasis, others with the Halys, others the Jorak or Acampis, others the Jaab, the Indus, the Ganges, etc. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Pit</b></a>     A hole in the ground (<a href="../kjv/exo021.htm#033">Exo 21:33</a>, <a href="../kjv/exo021.htm#034">Exo 21:34</a>), a cistern for water (<a href="../kjv/gen037.htm#024">Gen 37:24</a>; <a href="../kjv/jer014.htm#003">Jer 14:3</a>), a vault (<a href="../kjv/jer041.htm#009">Jer 41:9</a>), a grave (<a href="../kjv/psa030.htm#003">Psa 30:3</a>). It is used as a figure for mischief (<a href="../kjv/psa009.htm#015">Psa 9:15</a>), and is the name given to the unseen place of woe (<a href="../kjv/rev020.htm#001">Rev 20:1</a>, <a href="../kjv/rev020.htm#003">Rev 20:3</a>). The slime-pits in the vale of Siddim were wells which yielded asphalt (<a href="../kjv/gen014.htm#010">Gen 14:10</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Pitch</b></a>     (<a href="../kjv/gen006.htm#014">Gen 6:14</a>), asphalt or bitumen in its soft state, called "slime" (<a href="../kjv/gen011.htm#003">Gen 11:3</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen014.htm#010">Gen 14:10</a>; <a href="../kjv/exo002.htm#003">Exo 2:3</a>), found in pits near the Dead Sea (q.v.). It was used for various purposes, as the coating of the outside of vessels and in building. Allusion is made in <a href="../kjv/isa034.htm#009">Isa 34:9</a> to its inflammable character. (See SLIME.) 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Pitcher</b></a>     A vessel for containing liquids. In the East pitchers were usually carried on the head or shoulders (<a href="../kjv/gen024.htm#015">Gen 24:15</a>; <a href="../kjv/jdg007.htm#016">Jdg 7:16</a>, <a href="../kjv/jdg007.htm#019">Jdg 7:19</a>; <a href="../kjv/mar014.htm#013">Mar 14:13</a>). 
 </p>
