 <p><a name="000"><b>Bush</b></a>     In which Jehovah appeared to Moses in the wilderness (<a href="../kjv/exo003.htm#002">Exo 3:2</a>; <a href="../kjv/act007.htm#030">Act 7:30</a>). It is difficult to say what particular kind of plant or bush is here meant. Probably it was the mimosa or acacia. The words "in the bush" in <a href="../kjv/mar012.htm#026">Mar 12:26</a>; <a href="../kjv/luk020.htm#037">Luk 20:37</a>, mean "in the passage or paragraph on the bush;" i.e., in Ex. 3. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Butler</b></a>     Properly a servant in charge of the wine (<a href="../kjv/gen040.htm#001">Gen 40:1</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen041.htm#009">Gen 41:9</a>). The Hebrew word, mashkeh, thus translated is rendered also (plural) "cup-bearers" (<a href="../kjv/kg1010.htm#005">Kg1 10:5</a>; <a href="../kjv/ch2009.htm#004">Ch2 9:4</a>). Nehemiah (<a href="../kjv/neh001.htm#011">Neh 1:11</a>) was cup-bearer to king Artaxerxes. It was a position of great responsibility and honour in royal households. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Butter</b></a>     (Heb.  hemah ), curdled milk (<a href="../kjv/gen018.htm#008">Gen 18:8</a>; <a href="../kjv/jdg005.htm#025">Jdg 5:25</a>; <a href="../kjv/sa2017.htm#029">Sa2 17:29</a>), or butter in the form of the skim of hot milk or cream, called by the Arabs kaimak, a semi-fluid (<a href="../kjv/job020.htm#017">Job 20:17</a>; <a href="../kjv/job029.htm#006">Job 29:6</a>; <a href="../kjv/deu032.htm#014">Deu 32:14</a>). The words of <a href="../kjv/pro030.htm#033">Pro 30:33</a> have been rendered by some "the pressure [not churning] of milk bringeth forth cheese." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Buz</b></a>     Contempt.     (1.) The second son of Nahor and Milcah, and brother of Huz (<a href="../kjv/gen022.htm#021">Gen 22:21</a>). Elihu was one of his descendants (<a href="../kjv/job032.htm#002">Job 32:2</a>). (2.) One of the chiefs of the tribe of Gad (<a href="../kjv/ch1005.htm#014">Ch1 5:14</a>). (3.) A district in Arabia Petrea (<a href="../kjv/jer025.htm#023">Jer 25:23</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Buzi</b></a>     The father of the prophet Ezekiel (<a href="../kjv/eze001.htm#003">Eze 1:3</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>By</b></a>     In the expression "by myself" (A.V., <a href="../kjv/co1004.htm#004">Co1 4:4</a>), means, as rendered in the Revised Version, "against myself." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>By and by</b></a>     Immediately (<a href="../kjv/mat013.htm#021">Mat 13:21</a>; R.V., "straightway;" <a href="../kjv/luk021.htm#009">Luk 21:9</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>By-ways</b></a>     Only in <a href="../kjv/jdg005.htm#006">Jdg 5:6</a> and <a href="../kjv/psa125.htm#005">Psa 125:5</a>; literally "winding or twisted roads." The margin has "crooked ways." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>By-word</b></a>     Hebrew millah (<a href="../kjv/job030.htm#009">Job 30:9</a>), a word or speech, and hence object to talk; Hebrew mashal (<a href="../kjv/psa044.htm#014">Psa 44:14</a>), a proverb or parable. When it denotes a sharp word of derision, as in <a href="../kjv/deu028.htm#037">Deu 28:37</a>, <a href="../kjv/kg1009.htm#007">Kg1 9:7</a>, <a href="../kjv/ch2007.htm#020">Ch2 7:20</a>, the Hebrew sheninah is used. In <a href="../kjv/jer024.htm#009">Jer 24:9</a> it is rendered "taunt." 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Cab</b></a>     Hollow (R.V., "kab"), occurs only in <a href="../kjv/kg2006.htm#025">Kg2 6:25</a>; a dry measure, the sixth part of a seah, and the eighteenth part of an ephah, equal to about two English quarts. 
 </p>
