 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Forgotten Books of Eden</i>, by Rutherford H. Platt, Jr., [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">LVI.</h3> 
 <p>Methosalam asks of his father blessing, that he (<i>sc</i>. Methosalam) may make him (<i>sc</i>. Enoch) food to eat.</p> 
 <p>METHOSALAM having answered his father Enoch, said: 'What is agreeable to thy eyes, father, that I may make before thy face, that thou mayst bless our dwellings, and thy sons, and that thy people may be made glorious through thee, and then <i>that</i> thou mayst depart thus, as the Lord said?,</p> 
 <p>2 Enoch answered to his son Methosalam <i>and</i> said: 'Hear, child, from the time when the Lord anointed me with the ointment of his glory, <i>there has been no food</i> in me, and my soul remembers not earthly enjoyment, neither do I want anything earthly!</p>
