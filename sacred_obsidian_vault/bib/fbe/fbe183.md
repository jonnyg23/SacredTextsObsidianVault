 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Forgotten Books of Eden</i>, by Rutherford H. Platt, Jr., [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">VII.</h3> <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">The fine old doctrine--"<i>Thou art our Shield</i>!"</p> 
 <p>Make not Thy dwelling afar from us, O God;<br>   Lest they assail us that hate us without cause.<br> For Thou hast rejected them, O God;<br>   Let not their foot trample upon Thy holy inheritance.<br> Chasten us Thyself in Thy good pleasure;<br>   But give us not up to the nations;<br> For, if Thou sendest pestilence,<br>   Thou Thyself givest it charge concerning us;<br> For Thou art merciful,<br>   And wilt not be angry to the point of consuming us.</p> 
 <p>While Thy name dwelleth in our midst, we shall find mercy;<br>   And the nations shall not prevail against us.<br> For Thou art our shield,<br>   And when we call upon Thee, Thou hearkenest to us;<br> For Thou wilt pity the seed of Israel for ever<br>   And Thou wilt not reject them: <a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="green">p. 111</font></a><br> But we shall, be under Thy yoke for ever,<br>   And under the rod of Thy chastening.<br> Thou wilt establish us in the time that Thou helpest us,<br>   Showing mercy to the house of Jacob on the day wherein Thou didst promise to help them.</p>
