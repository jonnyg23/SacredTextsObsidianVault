 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Forgotten Books of Eden</i>, by Rutherford H. Platt, Jr., [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">XXXVII.</h3> <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">Here God summons an angel.</p> 
 <p>AND the Lord called up one of the older angels, terrible and menacing, and placed him</p> 
 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="green">p. 95</font></a></p> 
 <p>by me, in appearance white as snow, and his hands like ice, having the appearance of great frost, and he froze my face, because I could not endure the terror of the Lord, just as it is not possible to endure a stove's fire and the sun's heat, and the frost of the air.</p> 
 <p>2 And the Lord said to me: 'Enoch, if thy face be not frozen here, no man will be able to behold thy face.'</p>
