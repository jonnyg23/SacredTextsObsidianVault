 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Forgotten Books of Eden</i>, by Rutherford H. Platt, Jr., [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">XLIX.</h3> <i>
 </i><p>Enoch instructs his sons not to swear either by heaven or earth, and shows God's promise, even in the mother's womb.</p> 
 <p>I SWEAR to you, my children, but I swear not by any oath, neither by heaven nor by earth, nor by any other creature which God created.</p> 
 <p>2 The Lord said: 'There is no oath in me, nor injustice, but truth.'</p> 
 <p>3 If there is no truth in men, let them swear by the words 'yea, yea,' or else, 'nay, nay!</p> 
 <p>4 And I swear to you, yea, yea, that there has been no man in his mother's womb, <i>but that</i> already before, even to each one there is a place prepared for the repose of the soul, and a measure</p> 
 <p><a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="green">p. 99</font></a></p> 
 <p>fixed how much it is intended that a man be tried in this world.</p> 
 <p>5 Yea, children, deceive not yourselves, for there has been previously prepared a place for every soul of man.</p>
