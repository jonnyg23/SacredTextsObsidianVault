 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 81</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Sing aloud unto God our strength: make a joyful noise unto the God of <a href="../ebd/ebd194.htm#007">Jacob</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Take a psalm, and bring hither the <a href="../ebd/ebd366.htm#004">timbrel</a>, the pleasant <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#001">harp</a> with the <a href="../ebd/ebd301.htm#006">psaltery</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Blow up the trumpet in the new <a href="../ebd/ebd259.htm#004">moon</a>, in the time appointed, on our solemn <a href="../ebd/ebd132.htm#000">feast</a> day.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> For this was a statute for <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a>, and a law of the God of <a href="../ebd/ebd194.htm#007">Jacob</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> This he ordained in <a href="../ebd/ebd211.htm#005">Joseph</a> for a <a href="../ebd/ebd362.htm#008">testimony</a>, when he went out through the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>: where I heard a language that I understood not.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> I removed his shoulder from the <a href="../ebd/ebd066.htm#007">burden</a>: his hands were delivered from the pots.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Thou calledst in trouble, and I delivered thee; I answered thee in the secret place of <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#003">thunder</a>: I proved thee at the waters of <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#009">Meribah</a>. <a href="../ebd/ebd326.htm#008">Selah</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Hear, O my people, and I will testify unto thee: O <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a>, if thou wilt hearken unto me;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> There shall no strange god be in thee; neither shalt thou <a href="../ebd/ebd384.htm#002">worship</a> any strange god.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> I am the LORD thy God, which brought thee out of the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>: open thy mouth wide, and I will fill it.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> But my people would not hearken to my voice; and <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a> would none of me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> So I gave them up unto their own hearts' <a href="../ebd/ebd233.htm#005">lust</a>: and they walked in their own counsels.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Oh that my people had hearkened unto me, and <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a> had walked in my ways!<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> I should soon have subdued their enemies, and turned my hand against their adversaries.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> The haters of the LORD should have submitted themselves unto him: but their time should have endured for ever.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> He should have fed them also with the finest of the <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#000">wheat</a>: and with <a href="../ebd/ebd181.htm#001">honey</a> out of the <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#001">rock</a> should I have satisfied thee.<br>
 </p>
