 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 102</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Hear my <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#000">prayer</a>, O LORD, and let my cry come unto thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Hide not thy face from me in the day when I am in trouble; incline thine <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a> unto me: in the day when I call answer me speedily.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> For my days are consumed like smoke, and my bones are burned as an <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#002">hearth</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> My heart is smitten, and withered like <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#005">grass</a>; so that I forget to eat my <a href="../ebd/ebd064.htm#006">bread</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> By reason of the voice of my groaning my bones cleave to my skin.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> I am like a pelican of the <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#005">wilderness</a>: I am like an <a href="../ebd/ebd281.htm#006">owl</a> of the <a href="../ebd/ebd102.htm#000">desert</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> I watch, and am as a <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#003">sparrow</a> alone upon the house top.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Mine enemies reproach me all the day; and they that are mad against me are sworn against me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> For I have eaten <a href="../ebd/ebd034.htm#000">ashes</a> like <a href="../ebd/ebd064.htm#006">bread</a>, and mingled my <a href="../ebd/ebd107.htm#009">drink</a> with weeping.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Because of thine indignation and thy wrath: for thou hast lifted me up, and cast me down.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> My days are like a <a href="../ebd/ebd330.htm#002">shadow</a> that declineth; and I am withered like <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#005">grass</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> But thou, O LORD, shall endure for ever; and thy remembrance unto all generations.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Thou shalt arise, and have <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#005">mercy</a> upon <a href="../ebd/ebd394.htm#002">Zion</a>: for the time to favour her, yea, the set time, is come.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> For thy servants take pleasure in her stones, and favour the <a href="../ebd/ebd109.htm#008">dust</a> thereof.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> So the <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#005">heathen</a> shall fear the name of the LORD, and all the kings of the earth thy <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> When the LORD shall build up <a href="../ebd/ebd394.htm#002">Zion</a>, he shall appear in his <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> He will regard the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#000">prayer</a> of the destitute, and not despise their <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#000">prayer</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> This shall be written for the <a href="../ebd/ebd145.htm#008">generation</a> to come: and the people which shall be created shall praise the LORD.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> For he hath looked down from the height of his <a href="../ebd/ebd321.htm#005">sanctuary</a>; from <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#006">heaven</a> did the LORD behold the earth;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> To hear the groaning of the prisoner; to loose those that are appointed to death;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> To declare the name of the LORD in <a href="../ebd/ebd394.htm#002">Zion</a>, and his praise in <a href="../ebd/ebd204.htm#005">Jerusalem</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> When the people are gathered together, and the kingdoms, to serve the LORD.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> He weakened my strength in the way; he shortened my days.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> I said, O my God, take me not away in the midst of my days: thy years are throughout all generations.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> Of old hast thou laid the foundation of the earth: and the heavens are the work of thy hands.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> They shall perish, but thou shalt endure: yea, all of them shall <a href="../ebd/ebd380.htm#000">wax</a> old like a garment; as a vesture shalt thou change them, and they shall be changed:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> But thou art the same, and thy years shall have no end.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> The children of thy servants shall continue, and their seed shall be established before thee.<br>
 </p>
