 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Job Chapter 10</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> My soul is weary of my life; I will leave my complaint upon myself; I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> I will say unto God, Do not condemn me; shew me wherefore thou contendest with me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Is it good unto thee that thou shouldest oppress, that thou shouldest despise the work of thine hands, and shine upon the counsel of the wicked?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Hast thou eyes of <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>? or seest thou as man seeth?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Are thy days as the days of man? are thy years as man's days,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> That thou enquirest after mine iniquity, and searchest after my <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#006">sin</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Thou knowest that I am not wicked; and there is none that can deliver out of thine hand.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Thine hands have made me and fashioned me together round about; yet thou dost destroy me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#004">clay</a>; and wilt thou bring me into <a href="../ebd/ebd109.htm#008">dust</a> again?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Hast thou not poured me out as <a href="../ebd/ebd255.htm#000">milk</a>, and curdled me like <a href="../ebd/ebd078.htm#005">cheese</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Thou hast clothed me with skin and <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, and hast fenced me with bones and sinews.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Thou hast granted me life and favour, and thy visitation hath preserved my <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#009">spirit</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> And these things hast thou hid in thine heart: I know that this is with thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> If I <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#006">sin</a>, then thou markest me, and thou wilt not acquit me from mine iniquity.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> If I be wicked, woe unto me; and if I be righteous, yet will I not lift up my head. I am full of confusion; therefore see thou mine affliction;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> For it increaseth. Thou huntest me as a fierce lion: and again thou shewest thyself marvellous upon me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Thou renewest thy witnesses against me, and increasest thine indignation upon me; changes and war are against me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> Wherefore then hast thou brought me forth out of the womb? Oh that I had given up the <a href="../ebd/ebd147.htm#005">ghost</a>, and no eye had seen me!<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> I should have been as though I had not been; I should have been carried from the womb to the <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#008">grave</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> Are not my days few? cease then, and let me alone, that I may take comfort a little,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> Before I go whence I shall not return, even to the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd097.htm#008">darkness</a> and the <a href="../ebd/ebd330.htm#002">shadow</a> of death;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> A land of <a href="../ebd/ebd097.htm#008">darkness</a>, as <a href="../ebd/ebd097.htm#008">darkness</a> itself; and of the <a href="../ebd/ebd330.htm#002">shadow</a> of death, without any order, and where the light is as <a href="../ebd/ebd097.htm#008">darkness</a>.<br>
 </p>
