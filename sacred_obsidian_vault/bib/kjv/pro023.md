 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Proverbs Chapter 23</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> When thou sittest to eat with a ruler, consider diligently what is before thee:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> And put a <a href="../ebd/ebd221.htm#007">knife</a> to thy throat, if thou be a man given to appetite.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Be not desirous of his dainties: for they are deceitful meat.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Labour not to be rich: cease from thine own wisdom.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Wilt thou set thine eyes upon that which is not? for riches certainly make themselves wings; they <a href="../ebd/ebd136.htm#004">fly</a> away as an <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#003">eagle</a> toward <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#006">heaven</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Eat thou not the <a href="../ebd/ebd064.htm#006">bread</a> of him that hath an evil eye, neither desire thou his dainty meats:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> For as he thinketh in his heart, so is he: Eat and <a href="../ebd/ebd107.htm#009">drink</a>, saith he to thee; but his heart is not with thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> The morsel which thou hast eaten shalt thou vomit up, and lose thy sweet words.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Speak not in the ears of a fool: for he will despise the wisdom of thy words.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Remove not the old <a href="../ebd/ebd223.htm#008">landmark</a>; and enter not into the fields of the fatherless:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> For their <a href="../ebd/ebd308.htm#008">redeemer</a> is mighty; he shall plead their cause with thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Apply thine heart unto instruction, and thine ears to the words of knowledge.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Withhold not correction from the child: for if thou beatest him with the rod, he shall not die.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Thou shalt beat him with the rod, and shalt deliver his soul from <a href="../ebd/ebd173.htm#003">hell</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> My son, if thine heart be <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a>, my heart shall rejoice, even mine.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Yea, my <a href="../ebd/ebd310.htm#001">reins</a> shall rejoice, when thy lips speak right things.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Let not thine heart envy sinners: but be thou in the fear of the LORD all the day long.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> For surely there is an end; and thine expectation shall not be cut off.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Hear thou, my son, and be <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a>, and guide thine heart in the way.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> Be not among winebibbers; among riotous eaters of <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> For the drunkard and the <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#007">glutton</a> shall come to poverty: and drowsiness shall clothe a man with rags.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> Hearken unto thy father that begat thee, and despise not thy mother when she is old.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> Buy the truth, and sell it not; also wisdom, and instruction, and understanding.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> The father of the righteous shall greatly rejoice: and he that begetteth a <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a> child shall have joy of him.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> Thy father and thy mother shall be glad, and she that bare thee shall rejoice.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> My son, give me thine heart, and let thine eyes observe my ways.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> For a whore is a <a href="../ebd/ebd100.htm#008">deep</a> ditch; and a strange woman is a narrow <a href="../ebd/ebd296.htm#007">pit</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> She also lieth in wait as for a prey, and increaseth the transgressors among men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="029">29</a> Who hath woe? who hath sorrow? who hath contentions? who hath babbling? who hath wounds without cause? who hath redness of eyes?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="030">30</a> They that tarry long at the <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a>; they that go to seek mixed <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="031">31</a> Look not thou upon the <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a> when it is red, when it giveth his <a href="../ebd/ebd086.htm#008">colour</a> in the <a href="../ebd/ebd094.htm#000">cup</a>, when it moveth itself aright.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="032">32</a> At the last it biteth like a <a href="../ebd/ebd328.htm#009">serpent</a>, and stingeth like an <a href="../ebd/ebd008.htm#005">adder</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="033">33</a> Thine eyes shall behold strange women, and thine heart shall utter perverse things.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="034">34</a> Yea, thou shalt be as he that lieth down in the midst of the sea, or as he that lieth upon the top of a mast.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="035">35</a> They have stricken me, shalt thou say, and I was not sick; they have beaten me, and I felt it not: when shall I awake? I will seek it yet again.<br>
 </p>
