 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Proverbs Chapter 5</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> My son, attend unto my wisdom, and <a href="../ebd/ebd063.htm#003">bow</a> thine <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a> to my understanding:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> That thou mayest regard discretion, and that thy lips may keep knowledge.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> For the lips of a strange woman drop as an honeycomb, and her mouth is smoother than oil:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> But her end is <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a> as <a href="../ebd/ebd384.htm#001">wormwood</a>, sharp as a two-edged <a href="../ebd/ebd354.htm#003">sword</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Her feet go down to death; her steps take hold on <a href="../ebd/ebd173.htm#003">hell</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Lest thou shouldest ponder the path of life, her ways are moveable, that thou canst not know them.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Hear me now therefore, O ye children, and depart not from the words of my mouth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Remove thy way far from her, and come not nigh the door of her house:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Lest thou give thine honour unto others, and thy years unto the cruel:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Lest strangers be filled with thy wealth; and thy labours be in the house of a <a href="../ebd/ebd351.htm#009">stranger</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> And thou <a href="../ebd/ebd261.htm#004">mourn</a> at the last, when thy <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> and thy body are consumed,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> And say, How have I hated instruction, and my heart despised reproof;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> And have not obeyed the voice of my teachers, nor inclined mine <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a> to them that instructed me!<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> I was almost in all evil in the midst of the <a href="../ebd/ebd088.htm#004">congregation</a> and assembly.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd107.htm#009">Drink</a> waters out of thine own <a href="../ebd/ebd083.htm#008">cistern</a>, and running waters out of thine own well.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Let thy fountains be dispersed abroad, and rivers of waters in the streets.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Let them be only thine own, and not strangers' with thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> Let thy <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#000">fountain</a> be blessed: and rejoice with the wife of thy youth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Let her be as the loving <a href="../ebd/ebd179.htm#000">hind</a> and pleasant <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#002">roe</a>; let her breasts satisfy thee at all times; and be thou ravished always with her love.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> And why wilt thou, my son, be ravished with a strange woman, and embrace the <a href="../ebd/ebd062.htm#008">bosom</a> of a <a href="../ebd/ebd351.htm#009">stranger</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> For the ways of man are before the eyes of the LORD, and he pondereth all his goings.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> His own iniquities shall take the wicked himself, and he shall be holden with the cords of his sins.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> He shall die without instruction; and in the greatness of his folly he shall go astray.<br>
 </p>
