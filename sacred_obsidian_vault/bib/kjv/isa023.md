 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Isaiah Chapter 23</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd066.htm#007">burden</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a>. Howl, ye <a href="../ebd/ebd339.htm#003">ships</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd359.htm#002">Tarshish</a>; for it is laid waste, so that there is no house, no entering in: from the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd081.htm#003">Chittim</a> it is revealed to them.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Be still, ye inhabitants of the isle; thou whom the merchants of <a href="../ebd/ebd393.htm#000">Zidon</a>, that pass over the sea, have replenished.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> And by great waters the seed of <a href="../ebd/ebd343.htm#003">Sihor</a>, the <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#008">harvest</a> of the river, is her revenue; and she is a mart of nations.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Be thou ashamed, O <a href="../ebd/ebd393.htm#000">Zidon</a>: for the sea hath spoken, even the strength of the sea, saying, I travail not, nor bring forth children, neither do I nourish up young men, nor bring up virgins.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> As at the report concerning <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>, so shall they be sorely pained at the report of <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Pass ye over to <a href="../ebd/ebd359.htm#002">Tarshish</a>; howl, ye inhabitants of the isle.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Is this your joyous city, whose antiquity is of ancient days? her own feet shall carry her afar off to sojourn.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Who hath taken this counsel against <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a>, the crowning city, whose merchants are princes, whose traffickers are the honourable of the earth?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> The LORD of hosts hath purposed it, to stain the pride of all <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a>, and to bring into contempt all the honourable of the earth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Pass through thy land as a river, O daughter of <a href="../ebd/ebd359.htm#002">Tarshish</a>: there is no more strength.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> He stretched out his hand over the sea, he shook the kingdoms: the LORD hath given a commandment against the <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#003">merchant</a> city, to destroy the strong holds thereof.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> And he said, Thou shalt no more rejoice, O thou oppressed <a href="../ebd/ebd377.htm#009">virgin</a>, daughter of <a href="../ebd/ebd393.htm#000">Zidon</a>: arise, pass over to <a href="../ebd/ebd081.htm#003">Chittim</a>; there also shalt thou have no rest.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Behold the land of the Chaldeans; this people was not, till the Assyrian founded it for them that dwell in the <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#005">wilderness</a>: they set up the <a href="../ebd/ebd371.htm#002">towers</a> thereof, they raised up the palaces thereof; and he brought it to ruin.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Howl, ye <a href="../ebd/ebd339.htm#003">ships</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd359.htm#002">Tarshish</a>: for your strength is laid waste.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> And it shall come to pass in that day, that <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a> shall be forgotten seventy years, according to the days of one <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a>: after the end of seventy years shall <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a> sing as an <a href="../ebd/ebd164.htm#005">harlot</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Take an <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#001">harp</a>, go about the city, thou <a href="../ebd/ebd164.htm#005">harlot</a> that hast been forgotten; make sweet melody, sing many <a href="../ebd/ebd348.htm#002">songs</a>, that thou mayest be remembered.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> And it shall come to pass after the end of seventy years, that the LORD will visit <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a>, and she shall turn to her hire, and shall commit <a href="../ebd/ebd137.htm#008">fornication</a> with all the kingdoms of the world upon the face of the earth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> And her merchandise and her hire shall be <a href="../ebd/ebd180.htm#006">holiness</a> to the LORD: it shall not be treasured nor laid up; for her merchandise shall be for them that dwell before the LORD, to eat sufficiently, and for durable clothing.<br>
 </p>
