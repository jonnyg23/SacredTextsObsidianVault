 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 45</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> My heart is inditing a good matter: I speak of the things which I have made touching the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a>: my tongue is the pen of a ready writer.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Thou art fairer than the children of men: <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#000">grace</a> is poured into thy lips: therefore God hath blessed thee for ever.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Gird thy <a href="../ebd/ebd354.htm#003">sword</a> upon thy thigh, O most mighty, with thy <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a> and thy majesty.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> And in thy majesty ride prosperously because of truth and <a href="../ebd/ebd246.htm#003">meekness</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a>; and thy right hand shall teach thee terrible things.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Thine <a href="../ebd/ebd032.htm#005">arrows</a> are sharp in the heart of the king's enemies; whereby the people fall under thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Thy <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#001">throne</a>, O God, is for ever and ever: the <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#006">sceptre</a> of thy kingdom is a right <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#006">sceptre</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Thou lovest <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a>, and hatest wickedness: therefore God, thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> All thy garments smell of <a href="../ebd/ebd263.htm#003">myrrh</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd018.htm#004">aloes</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd073.htm#006">cassia</a>, out of the <a href="../ebd/ebd192.htm#000">ivory</a> palaces, whereby they have made thee glad.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Kings' daughters were among thy honourable women: upon thy right hand did stand the <a href="../ebd/ebd303.htm#007">queen</a> in <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd279.htm#007">Ophir</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Hearken, O daughter, and consider, and incline thine <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a>; forget also thine own people, and thy father's house;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> So shall the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a> greatly desire thy beauty: for he is thy Lord; and <a href="../ebd/ebd384.htm#002">worship</a> thou him.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> And the daughter of <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#001">Tyre</a> shall be there with a <a href="../ebd/ebd148.htm#005">gift</a>; even the rich among the people shall intreat thy favour.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> The king's daughter is all glorious within: her clothing is of wrought <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> She shall be brought unto the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a> in raiment of needlework: the virgins her companions that follow her shall be brought unto thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> With gladness and rejoicing shall they be brought: they shall enter into the king's <a href="../ebd/ebd282.htm#008">palace</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Instead of thy fathers shall be thy children, whom thou mayest make princes in all the earth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> I will make thy name to be remembered in all generations: therefore shall the people praise thee for ever and ever.<br>
 </p>
