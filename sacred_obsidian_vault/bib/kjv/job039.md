 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Job Chapter 39</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Knowest thou the time when the wild goats of the <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#001">rock</a> bring forth? or canst thou mark when the hinds do calve?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Canst thou number the months that they fulfil? or knowest thou the time when they bring forth?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> They <a href="../ebd/ebd063.htm#003">bow</a> themselves, they bring forth their young ones, they cast out their sorrows.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Their young ones are in good liking, they grow up with <a href="../ebd/ebd090.htm#006">corn</a>; they go forth, and return not unto them.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Who hath sent out the wild <a href="../ebd/ebd035.htm#001">ass</a> free? or who hath loosed the <a href="../ebd/ebd043.htm#003">bands</a> of the wild <a href="../ebd/ebd035.htm#001">ass</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Whose house I have made the <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#005">wilderness</a>, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd045.htm#007">barren</a> land his <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#001">dwellings</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> He scorneth the multitude of the city, neither regardeth he the crying of the driver.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> The range of the mountains is his pasture, and he searcheth after every green thing.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Will the <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#007">unicorn</a> be willing to serve thee, or abide by thy crib?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Canst thou bind the <a href="../ebd/ebd374.htm#007">unicorn</a> with his band in the <a href="../ebd/ebd140.htm#001">furrow</a>? or will he <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#002">harrow</a> the valleys after thee?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Wilt thou trust him, because his strength is great? or wilt thou leave thy labour to him?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Wilt thou believe him, that he will bring home thy seed, and gather it into thy <a href="../ebd/ebd045.htm#004">barn</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Gavest thou the goodly wings unto the peacocks? or wings and feathers unto the <a href="../ebd/ebd281.htm#001">ostrich</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Which leaveth her eggs in the earth, and warmeth them in <a href="../ebd/ebd109.htm#008">dust</a>,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> And forgetteth that the foot may crush them, or that the wild <a href="../ebd/ebd049.htm#001">beast</a> may break them.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> She is hardened against her young ones, as though they were not her's: her labour is in vain without fear;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Because God hath deprived her of wisdom, neither hath he imparted to her understanding.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> What time she lifteth up herself on high, she scorneth the horse and his rider.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Hast thou given the horse strength? hast thou clothed his <a href="../ebd/ebd269.htm#000">neck</a> with <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#003">thunder</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> Canst thou make him afraid as a <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#006">grasshopper</a>? the <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a> of his nostrils is terrible.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> He paweth in the <a href="../ebd/ebd376.htm#008">valley</a>, and rejoiceth in his strength: he goeth on to meet the armed men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> He mocketh at fear, and is not affrighted; neither turneth he back from the <a href="../ebd/ebd354.htm#003">sword</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd304.htm#000">quiver</a> rattleth against him, the glittering spear and the <a href="../ebd/ebd337.htm#001">shield</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> He swalloweth the ground with fierceness and rage: neither believeth he that it is the sound of the trumpet.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> He saith among the <a href="../ebd/ebd373.htm#001">trumpets</a>, Ha, ha; and he smelleth the battle afar off, the <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#003">thunder</a> of the captains, and the shouting.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> Doth the <a href="../ebd/ebd168.htm#001">hawk</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd136.htm#004">fly</a> by thy wisdom, and stretch her wings toward the south?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> Doth the <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#003">eagle</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#007">mount</a> up at thy command, and make her nest on high?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> She dwelleth and abideth on the <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#001">rock</a>, upon the crag of the <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#001">rock</a>, and the strong place.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="029">29</a> From thence she seeketh the prey, and her eyes behold afar off.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="030">30</a> Her young ones also suck up <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#000">blood</a>: and where the slain are, there is she.<br>
 </p>
