 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Ezekiel Chapter 27</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> The word of the LORD came again unto me, saying,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Now, thou son of man, take up a <a href="../ebd/ebd223.htm#005">lamentation</a> for Tyrus;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> And say unto Tyrus, O thou that art situate at the entry of the sea, which art a <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#003">merchant</a> of the people for many isles, Thus saith the Lord GOD; O Tyrus, thou hast said, I am of perfect beauty.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Thy borders are in the midst of the seas, thy builders have perfected thy beauty.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> They have made all thy ship boards of <a href="../ebd/ebd133.htm#005">fir</a> trees of <a href="../ebd/ebd327.htm#004">Senir</a>: they have taken cedars from <a href="../ebd/ebd226.htm#001">Lebanon</a> to make masts for thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Of the oaks of <a href="../ebd/ebd046.htm#003">Bashan</a> have they made thine oars; the company of the <a href="../ebd/ebd034.htm#007">Ashurites</a> have made thy benches of <a href="../ebd/ebd192.htm#000">ivory</a>, brought out of the isles of <a href="../ebd/ebd081.htm#003">Chittim</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Fine <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#008">linen</a> with broidered work from <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a> was that which thou spreadest forth to be thy sail; <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#003">blue</a> and purple from the isles of <a href="../ebd/ebd118.htm#000">Elishah</a> was that which covered thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> The inhabitants of <a href="../ebd/ebd393.htm#000">Zidon</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd032.htm#009">Arvad</a> were thy mariners: thy <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a> men, O Tyrus, that were in thee, were thy pilots.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> The ancients of <a href="../ebd/ebd144.htm#005">Gebal</a> and the <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a> men thereof were in thee thy <a href="../ebd/ebd069.htm#005">calkers</a>: all the <a href="../ebd/ebd339.htm#003">ships</a> of the sea with their mariners were in thee to occupy thy merchandise.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> They of <a href="../ebd/ebd290.htm#009">Persia</a> and of <a href="../ebd/ebd232.htm#009">Lud</a> and of <a href="../ebd/ebd294.htm#007">Phut</a> were in thine army, thy men of war: they hanged the <a href="../ebd/ebd337.htm#001">shield</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd173.htm#004">helmet</a> in thee; they set forth thy comeliness.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> The men of <a href="../ebd/ebd032.htm#009">Arvad</a> with thine army were upon thy walls round about, and the Gammadims were in thy <a href="../ebd/ebd371.htm#002">towers</a>: they hanged their shields upon thy walls round about; they have made thy beauty perfect.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd359.htm#002">Tarshish</a> was thy <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#003">merchant</a> by reason of the multitude of all kind of riches; with <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#000">silver</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd189.htm#004">iron</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd367.htm#005">tin</a>, and lead, they traded in thy <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#003">fairs</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd198.htm#005">Javan</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd373.htm#005">Tubal</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd250.htm#008">Meshech</a>, they were thy merchants: they traded the persons of men and vessels of <a href="../ebd/ebd064.htm#003">brass</a> in thy market.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> They of the house of <a href="../ebd/ebd369.htm#004">Togarmah</a> traded in thy <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#003">fairs</a> with horses and horsemen and mules.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> The men of <a href="../ebd/ebd100.htm#005">Dedan</a> were thy merchants; many isles were the merchandise of thine hand: they brought thee for a present horns of <a href="../ebd/ebd192.htm#000">ivory</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd112.htm#002">ebony</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd355.htm#002">Syria</a> was thy <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#003">merchant</a> by reason of the multitude of the wares of thy making: they occupied in thy <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#003">fairs</a> with emeralds, purple, and broidered work, and fine <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#008">linen</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd089.htm#008">coral</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd012.htm#000">agate</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd212.htm#007">Judah</a>, and the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a>, they were thy merchants: they traded in thy market <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#000">wheat</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd255.htm#009">Minnith</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd284.htm#000">Pannag</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd181.htm#001">honey</a>, and oil, and <a href="../ebd/ebd042.htm#009">balm</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd096.htm#007">Damascus</a> was thy <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#003">merchant</a> in the multitude of the wares of thy making, for the multitude of all riches; in the <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd172.htm#001">Helbon</a>, and white <a href="../ebd/ebd383.htm#005">wool</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd096.htm#009">Dan</a> also and <a href="../ebd/ebd198.htm#005">Javan</a> going to and fro occupied in thy <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#003">fairs</a>: bright <a href="../ebd/ebd189.htm#004">iron</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd073.htm#006">cassia</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd069.htm#001">calamus</a>, were in thy market.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd100.htm#005">Dedan</a> was thy <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#003">merchant</a> in precious clothes for chariots.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd027.htm#009">Arabia</a>, and all the princes of <a href="../ebd/ebd216.htm#003">Kedar</a>, they occupied with thee in lambs, and rams, and goats: in these were they thy merchants.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> The merchants of <a href="../ebd/ebd332.htm#006">Sheba</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd304.htm#002">Raamah</a>, they were thy merchants: they occupied in thy <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#003">fairs</a> with chief of all <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#005">spices</a>, and with all precious stones, and <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd163.htm#007">Haran</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd071.htm#005">Canneh</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd112.htm#009">Eden</a>, the merchants of <a href="../ebd/ebd332.htm#006">Sheba</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd035.htm#002">Asshur</a>, and <a href="../ebd/ebd080.htm#008">Chilmad</a>, were thy merchants.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> These were thy merchants in all sorts of things, in <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#003">blue</a> clothes, and broidered work, and in chests of rich <a href="../ebd/ebd027.htm#000">apparel</a>, bound with cords, and made of <a href="../ebd/ebd074.htm#007">cedar</a>, among thy merchandise.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd339.htm#003">ships</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd359.htm#002">Tarshish</a> did sing of thee in thy market: and thou wast replenished, and made very glorious in the midst of the seas.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> Thy rowers have brought thee into great waters: the <a href="../ebd/ebd111.htm#000">east</a> wind hath broken thee in the midst of the seas.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> Thy riches, and thy <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#003">fairs</a>, thy merchandise, thy mariners, and thy pilots, thy <a href="../ebd/ebd069.htm#005">calkers</a>, and the occupiers of thy merchandise, and all thy men of war, that are in thee, and in all thy company which is in the midst of thee, shall fall into the midst of the seas in the day of thy ruin.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd352.htm#007">suburbs</a> shall shake at the sound of the cry of thy pilots.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="029">29</a> And all that handle the oar, the mariners, and all the pilots of the sea, shall come down from their <a href="../ebd/ebd339.htm#003">ships</a>, they shall stand upon the land;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="030">30</a> And shall cause their voice to be heard against thee, and shall cry bitterly, and shall cast up <a href="../ebd/ebd109.htm#008">dust</a> upon their heads, they shall wallow themselves in the <a href="../ebd/ebd034.htm#000">ashes</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="031">31</a> And they shall make themselves utterly bald for thee, and gird them with <a href="../ebd/ebd318.htm#001">sackcloth</a>, and they shall weep for thee with bitterness of heart and <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a> wailing.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="032">32</a> And in their wailing they shall take up a <a href="../ebd/ebd223.htm#005">lamentation</a> for thee, and lament over thee, saying, What city is like Tyrus, like the destroyed in the midst of the sea?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="033">33</a> When thy wares went forth out of the seas, thou filledst many people; thou didst enrich the kings of the earth with the multitude of thy riches and of thy merchandise.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="034">34</a> In the time when thou shalt be broken by the seas in the depths of the waters thy merchandise and all thy company in the midst of thee shall fall.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="035">35</a> All the inhabitants of the isles shall be astonished at thee, and their kings shall be sore afraid, they shall be troubled in their countenance.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="036">36</a> The merchants among the people shall <a href="../ebd/ebd179.htm#005">hiss</a> at thee; thou shalt be a terror, and never shalt be any more.<br>
 </p>
