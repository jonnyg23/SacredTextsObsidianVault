 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Titus Chapter 1</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd287.htm#002">Paul</a>, a servant of God, and an <a href="../ebd/ebd026.htm#008">apostle</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd205.htm#006">Jesus</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd082.htm#000">Christ</a>, according to the faith of God's elect, and the acknowledging of the truth which is after <a href="../ebd/ebd151.htm#007">godliness</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#003">Saviour</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> To <a href="../ebd/ebd368.htm#007">Titus</a>, mine own son after the common faith: <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#000">Grace</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#005">mercy</a>, and peace, from God the Father and the Lord <a href="../ebd/ebd205.htm#006">Jesus</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd082.htm#000">Christ</a> our <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#003">Saviour</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> For this cause left I thee in <a href="../ebd/ebd092.htm#006">Crete</a>, that thou shouldest set in order the things that are wanting, and ordain elders in every city, as I had appointed thee:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> If any be blameless, the husband of one wife, having <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#005">faithful</a> children not accused of riot or unruly.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> For a <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#005">bishop</a> must be blameless, as the steward of God; not selfwilled, not soon angry, not given to <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a>, no striker, not given to filthy <a href="../ebd/ebd232.htm#008">lucre</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> But a lover of hospitality, a lover of good men, sober, just, holy, temperate;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Holding <a href="../ebd/ebd131.htm#004">fast</a> the <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#005">faithful</a> word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to convince the gainsayers.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> For there are many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers, specially they of the <a href="../ebd/ebd083.htm#007">circumcision</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy lucre's sake.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> One of themselves, even a <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#008">prophet</a> of their own, said, The Cretians are alway liars, evil beasts, slow bellies.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> This <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#008">witness</a> is true. Wherefore rebuke them sharply, that they may be sound in the faith;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Not giving heed to Jewish fables, and commandments of men, that turn from the truth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Unto the pure all things are pure: but unto them that are defiled and unbelieving is nothing pure; but even their mind and <a href="../ebd/ebd088.htm#006">conscience</a> is defiled.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> They profess that they know God; but in works they deny him, being abominable, and disobedient, and unto every good work <a href="../ebd/ebd311.htm#003">reprobate</a>.<br>
 </p>
