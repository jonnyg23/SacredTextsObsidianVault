 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: 1 Timothy Chapter 4</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Now the <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#009">Spirit</a> speaketh expressly, that in the latter times some shall depart from the faith, giving heed to seducing spirits, and doctrines of devils;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Speaking lies in hypocrisy; having their <a href="../ebd/ebd088.htm#006">conscience</a> seared with a hot <a href="../ebd/ebd189.htm#004">iron</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Forbidding to marry, and commanding to abstain from meats, which God hath created to be received with thanksgiving of them which believe and know the truth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> For every <a href="../ebd/ebd092.htm#004">creature</a> of God is good, and nothing to be refused, if it be received with thanksgiving:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> For it is sanctified by the word of God and <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#000">prayer</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> If thou put the brethren in remembrance of these things, thou shalt be a good <a href="../ebd/ebd255.htm#007">minister</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd205.htm#006">Jesus</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd082.htm#000">Christ</a>, nourished up in the words of faith and of good doctrine, whereunto thou hast attained.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> But refuse profane and old wives' fables, and exercise thyself rather unto <a href="../ebd/ebd151.htm#007">godliness</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> For bodily exercise profiteth little: but <a href="../ebd/ebd151.htm#007">godliness</a> is profitable unto all things, having promise of the life that now is, and of that which is to come.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> This is a <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#005">faithful</a> saying and worthy of all acceptation.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> For therefore we both labour and suffer reproach, because we trust in the living God, who is the <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#003">Saviour</a> of all men, specially of those that believe.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> These things command and teach.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Let no man despise thy youth; but be thou an example of the believers, in word, in <a href="../ebd/ebd089.htm#001">conversation</a>, in <a href="../ebd/ebd077.htm#009">charity</a>, in <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#009">spirit</a>, in faith, in purity.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Till I come, give attendance to reading, to exhortation, to doctrine.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Neglect not the <a href="../ebd/ebd148.htm#005">gift</a> that is in thee, which was given thee by <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#007">prophecy</a>, with the laying on of the hands of the presbytery.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Meditate upon these things; give thyself wholly to them; that thy profiting may appear to all.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Take heed unto thyself, and unto the doctrine; continue in them: for in doing this thou shalt both save thyself, and them that hear thee.<br>
 </p>
