 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Proverbs Chapter 25</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> These are also proverbs of <a href="../ebd/ebd347.htm#007">Solomon</a>, which the men of <a href="../ebd/ebd177.htm#003">Hezekiah</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd212.htm#007">Judah</a> copied out.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> It is the <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a> of God to conceal a thing: but the honour of kings is to search out a matter.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#006">heaven</a> for height, and the earth for depth, and the heart of kings is unsearchable.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Take away the <a href="../ebd/ebd108.htm#004">dross</a> from the <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#000">silver</a>, and there shall come forth a vessel for the <a href="../ebd/ebd133.htm#003">finer</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Take away the wicked from before the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a>, and his <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#001">throne</a> shall be established in <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Put not forth thyself in the presence of the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a>, and stand not in the place of great men:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> For better it is that it be said unto thee, Come up hither; than that thou shouldest be put lower in the presence of the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#004">prince</a> whom thine eyes have seen.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Go not forth hastily to strive, lest thou know not what to do in the end thereof, when thy neighbour hath put thee to shame.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Debate thy cause with thy neighbour himself; and discover not a secret to another:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Lest he that heareth it put thee to shame, and thine infamy turn not away.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> A word fitly spoken is like apples of <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a> in pictures of <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#000">silver</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> As an earring of <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>, and an ornament of fine <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>, so is a <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a> reprover upon an obedient <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> As the cold of <a href="../ebd/ebd347.htm#000">snow</a> in the time of <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#008">harvest</a>, so is a <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#005">faithful</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd251.htm#005">messenger</a> to them that send him: for he refresheth the soul of his masters.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Whoso boasteth himself of a false <a href="../ebd/ebd148.htm#005">gift</a> is like clouds and wind without <a href="../ebd/ebd305.htm#008">rain</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> By long forbearing is a <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#004">prince</a> persuaded, and a soft tongue breaketh the bone.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Hast thou found <a href="../ebd/ebd181.htm#001">honey</a>? eat so much as is sufficient for thee, lest thou be filled therewith, and vomit it.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Withdraw thy foot from thy neighbour's house; lest he be weary of thee, and so hate thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> A man that beareth false <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#008">witness</a> against his neighbour is a <a href="../ebd/ebd244.htm#008">maul</a>, and a <a href="../ebd/ebd354.htm#003">sword</a>, and a sharp arrow.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Confidence in an unfaithful man in time of trouble is like a broken tooth, and a foot out of joint.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> As he that taketh away a garment in cold weather, and as <a href="../ebd/ebd377.htm#006">vinegar</a> upon <a href="../ebd/ebd273.htm#009">nitre</a>, so is he that singeth <a href="../ebd/ebd348.htm#002">songs</a> to an heavy heart.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> If thine enemy be hungry, give him <a href="../ebd/ebd064.htm#006">bread</a> to eat; and if he be thirsty, give him water to <a href="../ebd/ebd107.htm#009">drink</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> For thou shalt <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#000">heap</a> coals of fire upon his head, and the LORD shall reward thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> The north wind driveth away <a href="../ebd/ebd305.htm#008">rain</a>: so doth an angry countenance a backbiting tongue.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> It is better to dwell in the corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman and in a wide house.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> As cold waters to a thirsty soul, so is good news from a far country.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> A righteous man falling down before the wicked is as a troubled <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#000">fountain</a>, and a corrupt spring.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> It is not good to eat much <a href="../ebd/ebd181.htm#001">honey</a>: so for men to search their own <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a> is not <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> He that hath no rule over his own <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#009">spirit</a> is like a city that is broken down, and without walls.<br>
 </p>
