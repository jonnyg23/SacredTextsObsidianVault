 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 77</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> I cried unto God with my voice, even unto God with my voice; and he gave <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a> unto me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> In the day of my trouble I sought the Lord: my sore ran in the night, and ceased not: my soul refused to be comforted.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> I remembered God, and was troubled: I complained, and my <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#009">spirit</a> was overwhelmed. <a href="../ebd/ebd326.htm#008">Selah</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Thou holdest mine eyes waking: I am so troubled that I cannot speak.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> I have considered the days of old, the years of ancient times.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> I call to remembrance my song in the night: I commune with mine own heart: and my <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#009">spirit</a> made diligent search.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Will the Lord cast off for ever? and will he be favourable no more?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Is his <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#005">mercy</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a> gone for ever? doth his promise fail for evermore?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Hath God forgotten to be gracious? hath he in <a href="../ebd/ebd024.htm#002">anger</a> shut up his tender mercies? <a href="../ebd/ebd326.htm#008">Selah</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> And I said, This is my infirmity: but I will remember the years of the right hand of the most High.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> I will remember the works of the LORD: surely I will remember thy wonders of old.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> I will meditate also of all thy work, and talk of thy doings.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Thy way, O God, is in the <a href="../ebd/ebd321.htm#005">sanctuary</a>: who is so great a God as our God?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Thou art the God that doest wonders: thou hast declared thy strength among the people.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Thou hast with thine arm redeemed thy people, the sons of <a href="../ebd/ebd194.htm#007">Jacob</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd211.htm#005">Joseph</a>. <a href="../ebd/ebd326.htm#008">Selah</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> The waters saw thee, O God, the waters saw thee; they were afraid: the depths also were troubled.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> The clouds poured out water: the skies sent out a sound: thine <a href="../ebd/ebd032.htm#005">arrows</a> also went abroad.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> The voice of thy <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#003">thunder</a> was in the <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#006">heaven</a>: the lightnings lightened the world: the earth trembled and shook.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Thy way is in the sea, and thy path in the great waters, and thy footsteps are not known.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> Thou leddest thy people like a flock by the hand of <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd000.htm#001">Aaron</a>.<br>
 </p>
