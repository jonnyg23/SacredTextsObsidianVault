 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Job Chapter 11</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Then answered <a href="../ebd/ebd396.htm#000">Zophar</a> the <a href="../ebd/ebd264.htm#000">Naamathite</a>, and said,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Should not the multitude of words be answered? and should a man full of talk be justified?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Should thy lies make men hold their peace? and when thou mockest, shall no man make thee ashamed?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> For thou hast said, My doctrine is pure, and I am <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a> in thine eyes.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> But oh that God would speak, and open his lips against thee;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> And that he would shew thee the secrets of wisdom, that they are double to that which is! Know therefore that God exacteth of thee less than thine iniquity deserveth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Canst thou by searching find out God? canst thou find out the Almighty unto <a href="../ebd/ebd290.htm#001">perfection</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> It is as high as <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#006">heaven</a>; what canst thou do? deeper than <a href="../ebd/ebd173.htm#003">hell</a>; what canst thou know?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> The measure thereof is longer than the earth, and broader than the sea.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> If he cut off, and shut up, or gather together, then who can hinder him?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> For he knoweth vain men: he seeth wickedness also; will he not then consider it?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> For vain men would be <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a>, though man be born like a wild ass's colt.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> If thou prepare thine heart, and stretch out thine hands toward him;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> If iniquity be in thine hand, put it far away, and let not wickedness dwell in thy tabernacles.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> For then shalt thou lift up thy face without spot; yea, thou shalt be stedfast, and shalt not fear:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Because thou shalt forget thy misery, and remember it as waters that pass away:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> And thine age shall be clearer than the noonday: thou shalt shine forth, thou shalt be as the morning.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> And thou shalt be secure, because there is hope; yea, thou shalt dig about thee, and thou shalt take thy rest in safety.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Also thou shalt lie down, and none shall make thee afraid; yea, many shall make suit unto thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> But the eyes of the wicked shall fail, and they shall not escape, and their hope shall be as the giving up of the <a href="../ebd/ebd147.htm#005">ghost</a>.<br>
 </p>
