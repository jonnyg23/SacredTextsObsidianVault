 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 65</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Praise waiteth for thee, O God, in <a href="../ebd/ebd345.htm#003">Sion</a>: and unto thee shall the vow be performed.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> O thou that hearest <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#000">prayer</a>, unto thee shall all <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> come.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Iniquities prevail against me: as for our transgressions, thou shalt purge them away.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Blessed is the man whom thou choosest, and causest to approach unto thee, that he may dwell in thy courts: we shall be satisfied with the <a href="../ebd/ebd153.htm#000">goodness</a> of thy house, even of thy holy <a href="../ebd/ebd361.htm#004">temple</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> By terrible things in <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a> wilt thou answer us, O God of our <a href="../ebd/ebd320.htm#003">salvation</a>; who art the confidence of all the ends of the earth, and of them that are afar off upon the sea:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Which by his strength setteth <a href="../ebd/ebd131.htm#004">fast</a> the mountains; being girded with power:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Which stilleth the noise of the seas, the noise of their waves, and the tumult of the people.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> They also that dwell in the uttermost parts are afraid at thy tokens: thou makest the outgoings of the morning and evening to rejoice.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Thou visitest the earth, and waterest it: thou greatly enrichest it with the river of God, which is full of water: thou preparest them <a href="../ebd/ebd090.htm#006">corn</a>, when thou hast so provided for it.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Thou waterest the ridges thereof abundantly: thou settlest the furrows thereof: thou makest it soft with showers: thou blessest the springing thereof.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Thou crownest the year with thy <a href="../ebd/ebd153.htm#000">goodness</a>; and thy paths drop fatness.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> They drop upon the pastures of the <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#005">wilderness</a>: and the little hills rejoice on every side.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> The pastures are clothed with flocks; the valleys also are covered over with <a href="../ebd/ebd090.htm#006">corn</a>; they shout for joy, they also sing.<br>
 </p>
