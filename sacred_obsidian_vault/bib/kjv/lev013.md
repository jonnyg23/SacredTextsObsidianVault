 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Leviticus Chapter 13</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> And the LORD spake unto <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd000.htm#001">Aaron</a>, saying,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> When a man shall have in the skin of his <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> a rising, a scab, or bright spot, and it be in the skin of his <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> like the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>; then he shall be brought unto <a href="../ebd/ebd000.htm#001">Aaron</a> the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a>, or unto one of his sons the priests:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> in the skin of the <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>: and when the hair in the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is turned white, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> in sight be deeper than the skin of his <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>: and the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on him, and pronounce him unclean.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> If the bright spot be white in the skin of his <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, and in sight be not deeper than the skin, and the hair thereof be not turned white; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall shut up him that hath the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on him the seventh day: and, behold, if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> in his sight be at a stay, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> spread not in the skin; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall shut him up <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days more:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on him again the seventh day: and, behold, if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be somewhat dark, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> spread not in the skin, the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>: it is but a scab: and he shall wash his clothes, and be <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> But if the scab spread much abroad in the skin, after that he hath been seen of the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> for his cleansing, he shall be seen of the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> again.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> And if the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> see that, behold, the scab spreadeth in the skin, then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean: it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> When the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> is in a man, then he shall be brought unto the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall see him: and, behold, if the rising be white in the skin, and it have turned the hair white, and there be quick raw <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> in the rising;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> It is an old <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> in the skin of his <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean, and shall not shut him up: for he is unclean.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> And if a <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> break out abroad in the skin, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> cover all the skin of him that hath the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> from his head even to his foot, wheresoever the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> looketh;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall consider: and, behold, if the <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> have covered all his <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, he shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a> that hath the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>: it is all turned white: he is <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> But when raw <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> appeareth in him, he shall be unclean.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall see the raw <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, and pronounce him to be unclean: for the raw <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> is unclean: it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Or if the raw <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> turn again, and be changed unto white, he shall come unto the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall see him: and, behold, if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be turned into white; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a> that hath the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>: he is <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> also, in which, even in the skin thereof, was a <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#008">boil</a>, and is healed,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> And in the place of the <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#008">boil</a> there be a white rising, or a bright spot, white, and somewhat reddish, and it be shewed to the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> And if, when the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> seeth it, behold, it be in sight lower than the skin, and the hair thereof be turned white; the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean: it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> broken out of the <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#008">boil</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> But if the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> look on it, and, behold, there be no white hairs therein, and if it be not lower than the skin, but be somewhat dark; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall shut him up <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> And if it spread much abroad in the skin, then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean: it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> But if the bright spot stay in his place, and spread not, it is a burning <a href="../ebd/ebd061.htm#008">boil</a>; and the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> Or if there be any <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>, in the skin whereof there is a hot burning, and the quick <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> that burneth have a white bright spot, somewhat reddish, or white;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look upon it: and, behold, if the hair in the bright spot be turned white, and it be in sight deeper than the skin; it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> broken out of the burning: wherefore the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean: it is the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> But if the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> look on it, and, behold, there be no white hair in the bright spot, and it be no lower than the other skin, but be somewhat dark; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall shut him up <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look upon him the seventh day: and if it be spread much abroad in the skin, then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean: it is the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> And if the bright spot stay in his place, and spread not in the skin, but it be somewhat dark; it is a rising of the burning, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>: for it is an inflammation of the burning.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="029">29</a> If a man or woman have a <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> upon the head or the <a href="../ebd/ebd049.htm#000">beard</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="030">30</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall see the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>: and, behold, if it be in sight deeper than the skin; and there be in it a yellow thin hair; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him unclean: it is a dry scall, even a <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> upon the head or <a href="../ebd/ebd049.htm#000">beard</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="031">31</a> And if the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> look on the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of the scall, and, behold, it be not in sight deeper than the skin, and that there is no black hair in it; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall shut up him that hath the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of the scall <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="032">32</a> And in the seventh day the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>: and, behold, if the scall spread not, and there be in it no yellow hair, and the scall be not in sight deeper than the skin;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="033">33</a> He shall be shaven, but the scall shall he not shave; and the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall shut up him that hath the scall <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days more:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="034">34</a> And in the seventh day the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on the scall: and, behold, if the scall be not spread in the skin, nor be in sight deeper than the skin; then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>: and he shall wash his clothes, and be <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="035">35</a> But if the scall spread much in the skin after his cleansing;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="036">36</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on him: and, behold, if the scall be spread in the skin, the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall not seek for yellow hair; he is unclean.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="037">37</a> But if the scall be in his sight at a stay, and that there is black hair grown up therein; the scall is healed, he is <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>: and the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="038">38</a> If a man also or a woman have in the skin of their <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> bright spots, even white bright spots;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="039">39</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look: and, behold, if the bright spots in the skin of their <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> be darkish white; it is a freckled spot that groweth in the skin; he is <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="040">40</a> And the man whose hair is fallen off his head, he is bald; yet is he <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="041">41</a> And he that hath his hair fallen off from the part of his head toward his face, he is <a href="../ebd/ebd137.htm#002">forehead</a> bald: yet is he <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="042">42</a> And if there be in the bald head, or bald <a href="../ebd/ebd137.htm#002">forehead</a>, a white reddish sore; it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> sprung up in his bald head, or his bald <a href="../ebd/ebd137.htm#002">forehead</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="043">43</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look upon it: and, behold, if the rising of the sore be white reddish in his bald head, or in his bald <a href="../ebd/ebd137.htm#002">forehead</a>, as the <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> appeareth in the skin of the <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="044">44</a> He is a leprous man, he is unclean: the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall pronounce him utterly unclean; his <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is in his head.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="045">45</a> And the leper in whom the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is, his clothes shall be <a href="../ebd/ebd310.htm#007">rent</a>, and his head bare, and he shall put a covering upon his upper <a href="../ebd/ebd230.htm#002">lip</a>, and shall cry, Unclean, unclean.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="046">46</a> All the days wherein the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> shall be in him he shall be defiled; he is unclean: he shall dwell alone; without the <a href="../ebd/ebd070.htm#002">camp</a> shall his <a href="../ebd/ebd156.htm#007">habitation</a> be.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="047">47</a> The garment also that the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> is in, whether it be a woollen garment, or a <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#008">linen</a> garment;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="048">48</a> Whether it be in the warp, or woof; of <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#008">linen</a>, or of woollen; whether in a skin, or in any thing made of skin;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="049">49</a> And if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be greenish or reddish in the garment, or in the skin, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin; it is a <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>, and shall be shewed unto the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="050">50</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look upon the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>, and shut up it that hath the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="051">51</a> And he shall look on the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> on the seventh day: if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be spread in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in a skin, or in any work that is made of skin; the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is a fretting <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>; it is unclean.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="052">52</a> He shall therefore burn that garment, whether warp or woof, in woollen or in <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#008">linen</a>, or any thing of skin, wherein the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is: for it is a fretting <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a>; it shall be burnt in the fire.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="053">53</a> And if the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look, and, behold, the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be not spread in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="054">54</a> Then the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall command that they wash the thing wherein the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is, and he shall shut it up <a href="../ebd/ebd329.htm#005">seven</a> days more:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="055">55</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> shall look on the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>, after that it is washed: and, behold, if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> have not changed his <a href="../ebd/ebd086.htm#008">colour</a>, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be not spread; it is unclean; thou shalt burn it in the fire; it is fret inward, whether it be bare within or without.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="056">56</a> And if the <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#003">priest</a> look, and, behold, the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be somewhat dark after the <a href="../ebd/ebd379.htm#002">washing</a> of it; then he shall rend it out of the garment, or out of the skin, or out of the warp, or out of the woof:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="057">57</a> And if it appear still in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin; it is a spreading <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a>: thou shalt burn that wherein the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> is with fire.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="058">58</a> And the garment, either warp, or woof, or whatsoever thing of skin it be, which thou shalt wash, if the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> be departed from them, then it shall be washed the second time, and shall be <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="059">59</a> This is the law of the <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd227.htm#003">leprosy</a> in a garment of woollen or <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#008">linen</a>, either in the warp, or woof, or any thing of skins, to pronounce it <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#005">clean</a>, or to pronounce it unclean.<br>
 </p>
