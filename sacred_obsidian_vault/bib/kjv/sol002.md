 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 2</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> I am the rose of <a href="../ebd/ebd331.htm#009">Sharon</a>, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#005">lily</a> of the valleys.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> As the <a href="../ebd/ebd229.htm#005">lily</a> among thorns, so is my love among the daughters.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> As the <a href="../ebd/ebd027.htm#004">apple</a> tree among the trees of the <a href="../ebd/ebd383.htm#003">wood</a>, so is my beloved among the sons. I sat down under his <a href="../ebd/ebd330.htm#002">shadow</a> with great delight, and his <a href="../ebd/ebd139.htm#000">fruit</a> was sweet to my taste.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> He brought me to the banqueting house, and his <a href="../ebd/ebd043.htm#005">banner</a> over me was love.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Stay me with flagons, comfort me with apples: for I am sick of love.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> His left hand is under my head, and his right hand doth embrace me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> I charge you, O ye daughters of <a href="../ebd/ebd204.htm#005">Jerusalem</a>, by the roes, and by the hinds of the field, that ye stir not up, nor awake my love, till he please.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> The voice of my beloved! behold, he cometh leaping upon the mountains, skipping upon the hills.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> My beloved is like a <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#002">roe</a> or a young <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#004">hart</a>: behold, he standeth behind our wall, he looketh forth at the windows, shewing himself through the <a href="../ebd/ebd224.htm#008">lattice</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> My beloved spake, and said unto me, Rise up, my love, my fair one, and come away.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> For, lo, the winter is past, the <a href="../ebd/ebd305.htm#008">rain</a> is over and gone;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd136.htm#002">flowers</a> appear on the earth; the time of the singing of birds is come, and the voice of the turtle is heard in our land;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd133.htm#001">fig</a> tree putteth forth her green figs, and the vines with the tender <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#004">grape</a> give a good smell. Arise, my love, my fair one, and come away.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> O my <a href="../ebd/ebd106.htm#007">dove</a>, that art in the clefts of the <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#001">rock</a>, in the secret places of the stairs, let me see thy countenance, let me hear thy voice; for sweet is thy voice, and thy countenance is comely.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Take us the foxes, the little foxes, that spoil the vines: for our vines have tender grapes.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> My beloved is mine, and I am his: he feedeth among the lilies.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Until the day break, and the shadows flee away, turn, my beloved, and be thou like a <a href="../ebd/ebd315.htm#002">roe</a> or a young <a href="../ebd/ebd165.htm#004">hart</a> upon the mountains of <a href="../ebd/ebd055.htm#008">Bether</a>.<br>
 </p>
