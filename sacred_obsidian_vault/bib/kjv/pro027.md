 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Proverbs Chapter 27</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Boast not thyself of to morrow; for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; a <a href="../ebd/ebd351.htm#009">stranger</a>, and not thine own lips.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> A <a href="../ebd/ebd351.htm#005">stone</a> is heavy, and the sand weighty; but a fool's wrath is heavier than them both.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Wrath is cruel, and <a href="../ebd/ebd024.htm#002">anger</a> is outrageous; but who is able to stand before envy?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Open rebuke is better than secret love.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#005">Faithful</a> are the wounds of a friend; but the kisses of an enemy are deceitful.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> The full soul loatheth an honeycomb; but to the hungry soul every <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a> thing is sweet.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> As a <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#000">bird</a> that wandereth from her nest, so is a man that wandereth from his place.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd277.htm#007">Ointment</a> and perfume rejoice the heart: so doth the sweetness of a man's friend by hearty counsel.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Thine own friend, and thy father's friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother's house in the day of thy calamity: for better is a neighbour that is near than a brother far off.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> My son, be <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a>, and make my heart glad, that I may answer him that reproacheth me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> A prudent man foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself; but the simple pass on, and are punished.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Take his garment that is <a href="../ebd/ebd353.htm#005">surety</a> for a <a href="../ebd/ebd351.htm#009">stranger</a>, and take a <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#005">pledge</a> of him for a strange woman.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, it shall be counted a <a href="../ebd/ebd094.htm#003">curse</a> to him.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> A continual dropping in a very rainy day and a contentious woman are alike.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Whosoever hideth her hideth the wind, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd277.htm#007">ointment</a> of his right hand, which bewrayeth itself.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd189.htm#004">Iron</a> sharpeneth <a href="../ebd/ebd189.htm#004">iron</a>; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> Whoso keepeth the <a href="../ebd/ebd133.htm#001">fig</a> tree shall eat the <a href="../ebd/ebd139.htm#000">fruit</a> thereof: so he that waiteth on his master shall be honoured.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> As in water face answereth to face, so the heart of man to man.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd173.htm#003">Hell</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd102.htm#004">destruction</a> are never full; so the eyes of man are never satisfied.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> As the fining pot for <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#000">silver</a>, and the <a href="../ebd/ebd140.htm#000">furnace</a> for <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>; so is a man to his praise.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> Though thou shouldest bray a fool in a <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#000">mortar</a> among <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#000">wheat</a> with a pestle, yet will not his foolishness depart from him.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> Be thou diligent to know the state of thy flocks, and look well to thy herds.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> For riches are not for ever: and doth the <a href="../ebd/ebd093.htm#001">crown</a> endure to every <a href="../ebd/ebd145.htm#008">generation</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd168.htm#002">hay</a> appeareth, and the tender <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#005">grass</a> sheweth itself, and herbs of the mountains are gathered.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> The lambs are for thy clothing, and the goats are the price of the field.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> And thou shalt have goats' <a href="../ebd/ebd255.htm#000">milk</a> enough for thy food, for the food of thy household, and for the maintenance for thy maidens.<br>
 </p>
