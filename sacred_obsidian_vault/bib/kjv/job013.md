 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Job Chapter 13</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Lo, mine eye hath seen all this, mine <a href="../ebd/ebd110.htm#004">ear</a> hath heard and understood it.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> What ye know, the same do I know also: I am not inferior unto you.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Surely I would speak to the Almighty, and I desire to reason with God.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> But ye are forgers of lies, ye are all physicians of no value.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> O that ye would altogether hold your peace! and it should be your wisdom.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Hear now my reasoning, and hearken to the pleadings of my lips.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Will ye speak wickedly for God? and talk deceitfully for him?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Will ye accept his person? will ye contend for God?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Is it good that he should search you out? or as one man mocketh another, do ye so mock him?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> He will surely reprove you, if ye do secretly accept persons.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Shall not his excellency make you afraid? and his dread fall upon you?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Your remembrances are like unto <a href="../ebd/ebd034.htm#000">ashes</a>, your bodies to bodies of <a href="../ebd/ebd084.htm#004">clay</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Hold your peace, let me alone, that I may speak, and let come on me what will.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> Wherefore do I take my <a href="../ebd/ebd135.htm#007">flesh</a> in my teeth, and put my life in mine hand?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him: but I will maintain mine own ways before him.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> He also shall be my <a href="../ebd/ebd320.htm#003">salvation</a>: for an <a href="../ebd/ebd185.htm#006">hypocrite</a> shall not come before him.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> Hear diligently my speech, and my declaration with your ears.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> Behold now, I have ordered my cause; I know that I shall be justified.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="019">19</a> Who is he that will plead with me? for now, if I hold my tongue, I shall give up the <a href="../ebd/ebd147.htm#005">ghost</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="020">20</a> Only do not two things unto me: then will I not hide myself from thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="021">21</a> Withdraw thine hand far from me: and let not thy dread make me afraid.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="022">22</a> Then call thou, and I will answer: or let me speak, and answer thou me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="023">23</a> How many are mine iniquities and sins? make me to know my transgression and my <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#006">sin</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="024">24</a> Wherefore hidest thou thy face, and holdest me for thine enemy?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="025">25</a> Wilt thou break a <a href="../ebd/ebd225.htm#004">leaf</a> driven to and fro? and wilt thou pursue the dry stubble?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="026">26</a> For thou writest <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a> things against me, and makest me to possess the iniquities of my youth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="027">27</a> Thou puttest my feet also in the stocks, and lookest narrowly unto all my paths; thou settest a print upon the heels of my feet.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="028">28</a> And he, as a rotten thing, consumeth, as a garment that is <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#005">moth</a> eaten.<br>
 </p>
