 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Titus Chapter 3</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Put them in mind to be subject to principalities and powers, to obey magistrates, to be ready to every good work,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> To speak evil of no man, to be no brawlers, but gentle, shewing all <a href="../ebd/ebd246.htm#003">meekness</a> unto all men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> For we ourselves also were sometimes foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving divers lusts and pleasures, living in malice and envy, hateful, and hating one another.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> But after that the kindness and love of God our <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#003">Saviour</a> toward man appeared,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Not by works of <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a> which we have done, but according to his <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#005">mercy</a> he saved us, by the <a href="../ebd/ebd379.htm#002">washing</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd309.htm#004">regeneration</a>, and renewing of the Holy <a href="../ebd/ebd147.htm#005">Ghost</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Which he shed on us abundantly through <a href="../ebd/ebd205.htm#006">Jesus</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd082.htm#000">Christ</a> our <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#003">Saviour</a>;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> That being justified by his <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#000">grace</a>, we should be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> This is a <a href="../ebd/ebd130.htm#005">faithful</a> saying, and these things I will that thou affirm constantly, that they which have believed in God might be careful to maintain good works. These things are good and profitable unto men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> But avoid foolish questions, and genealogies, and contentions, and strivings about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> A man that is an heretick after the first and second admonition reject;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Knowing that he that is such is subverted, and sinneth, being condemned of himself.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> When I shall send Artemas unto thee, or <a href="../ebd/ebd373.htm#008">Tychicus</a>, be diligent to come unto me to <a href="../ebd/ebd272.htm#008">Nicopolis</a>: for I have determined there to winter.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Bring <a href="../ebd/ebd390.htm#007">Zenas</a> the <a href="../ebd/ebd225.htm#002">lawyer</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd026.htm#006">Apollos</a> on their <a href="../ebd/ebd212.htm#001">journey</a> diligently, that nothing be wanting unto them.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> And let our's also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> All that are with me salute thee. Greet them that love us in the faith. <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#000">Grace</a> be with you all. <a href="../ebd/ebd020.htm#001">Amen</a>.<br>
 </p>
