 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 64</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Hear my voice, O God, in my <a href="../ebd/ebd300.htm#000">prayer</a>: preserve my life from fear of the enemy.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Hide me from the secret counsel of the wicked; from the insurrection of the workers of iniquity:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Who whet their tongue like a <a href="../ebd/ebd354.htm#003">sword</a>, and bend their bows to shoot their <a href="../ebd/ebd032.htm#005">arrows</a>, even <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a> words:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> That they may shoot in secret at the perfect: suddenly do they shoot at him, and fear not.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> They encourage themselves in an evil matter: they commune of laying snares privily; they say, Who shall see them?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> They search out iniquities; they accomplish a diligent search: both the inward thought of every one of them, and the heart, is <a href="../ebd/ebd100.htm#008">deep</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> But God shall shoot at them with an arrow; suddenly shall they be wounded.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> So they shall make their own tongue to fall upon themselves: all that see them shall flee away.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> And all men shall fear, and shall declare the work of God; for they shall wisely consider of his doing.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> The righteous shall be glad in the LORD, and shall trust in him; and all the upright in heart shall <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a>.<br>
 </p>
