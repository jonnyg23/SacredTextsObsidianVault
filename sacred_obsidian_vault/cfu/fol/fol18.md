 <p><a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="green">p. 47</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE TWO VISITS</h3>  <h4 align="center"><i>Visit to the Cold Clear Spring</i></h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Ah! when the darkness blinds the orb of day<br> This cold clear spring chatters my grief away,<br> And, as the current whirls along,<br> She lilts a little wordless song;<br> This little wizard, clear and cold,<br> Echoes the thoughts I left untold,<br> And for music I have the sound<br> Of the tall pines surging round.</div>  <h4 align="center"><i>Visit to the White Stream Rapids</i></h4>  
 <p>I crossed the White Stream at its slender source<br> When Dawn first cleft the tangle of the stars <br> And shook the darkness from her. And I saw,<br> Passing a while from the worn tracks of men,<br> Islands innumerable environèd<br> In Nature's colours gold and green. The sky<br> Laid the blue mirror of eternity<br> Upon the shining waters. One by one<br> The clouds sailed out to sea. My random thoughts<br> Went wandering where monsters silver-mailed<br> Flash down their native streams. I sang the songs<br> That swelled with noon and faltered with the dusk<br> And failed in the twilight. Then I sought the gleam<br> Of cottage eaves amid the moonlit fields.</p>
