 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">AUTUMN MOONLIGHT</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Not yet has the cool moon topped the hill.<br> White are the floating clouds that fill<br> Half heaven's void; while to and fro<br> By the verandah windows go<br> My halting steps that pause as though<br> Stilled for the sound of one I love.<br> The flying brightness shimmers through the grove,<br> And, mirrored on the pine-ringed pool,<br> Makes her dream-waters beautiful.<br> Now Autumn's purest alchemy anew<br> Quickens the moonlight and distils the dew,<br> And silence, coiled more closely round my walls, <br> Strangles each tiny rumour that befalls.</div>
