 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p align="center">THE SECRET LAND</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The flower fairies bring<br> Their playmate Spring,<br> But the Spring goes<br> And takes no rose.<br> She breaks all hearts<br> To incense and departs.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="green">p. 93</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The river fain would keep<br> One cloud upon its breast<br> Of the twilight flocks that sweep<br> Like red flamingoes fading west,<br> Away, away,<br> To build beyond the day.  
 <p>Give me the green gloom of a lofty tree,<br> Leaf and bough to shutter and bar<br> My dream of the world that ought to be<br> From the drifting ghosts of the things that are.</p>  
 <p>Mine is a secret land where Spring<br> And sunset clouds cease wandering.</p></div>
