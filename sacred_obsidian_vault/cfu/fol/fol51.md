 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">EVENING</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Now pale flocks glimmer as they wind along;<br> Into the deep ravine the herd goes down;<br> The cold dumb pool awaits the nightly throng<br> Of wild geese wailing through the twilight brown.<br> With jars of new-made wine old farmer Wang<br> Gladdens the neighbours. Gloomy faces shine<br> And dark robes kindle to the flush of wine.</div>
