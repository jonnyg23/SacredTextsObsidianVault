 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FAREWELL TO CHAO TÂ-LIN</h3>  
 <p>Long do I sorrow that the spring should end;<br> Fain is the host to stay the parting friend.<br> When for a while the dull routine is done,<br> We statesmen idle in the sun.<br> The kettle yields its stream of golden tea,<br> And warm winds spread the odours of congee.<br> Finished the cup, faded the crimson peach,<br> Twilight, the green embankment levelled to the beach,<br> My boat is poled along the shore and soon<br> In the pure night unlanterned we recline;<br> Until, caps off to conquering wine,<br> We nod, the dream companions of the moon.</p>
