 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="green">p. 92</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ILLUSION</h3>  
 <p>’Tis we that wail the hour of birth,<br> ’Tis others weep the hour we die.<br> If I am sad, ’tis others sing;<br> Should they lament, I will be feasting.<br> All flows, all passes, like yon stream;<br> Like yonder wind-wheel all revolves.<br> We change the fire-drill, changing not the fire;<br> New lamps or old, what matters it?<br> ’Tis laughable that all men flock in crowds<br> To worship Buddhas and the Genii;<br> Austerities mean cramp and weariness,<br> And genuflections to the Rites a headache.<br> ’Tis but a tangle of marsh-lights after all,<br> We cannot seize the shadow of the wind.<br> What if the gods made answer to our prayers?<br> With shouts of laughter I should drive the crowd.</p>
