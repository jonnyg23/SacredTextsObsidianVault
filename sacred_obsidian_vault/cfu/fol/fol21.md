 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="green">p. 52</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>III. Sorrow</i></h3>  
 <p>Dawn reddens in the wake of night; but the days<br>       of our life return not.<br> Sweet-scented orchids blot out the path; but<br>       they die in the drift of waters and their<br>       flowers are blotted out.<br> The Yang-tse-Kiang splashes through shelving<br>       maple-woods.<br> The eye contains a far horizon, but the wound of<br>       spring lies deep in the heart.<br> O Poet! turn thee to the Capital—to the men<br>       who shall make thee forget.<br> Surely, the Earth-sorrow for the passing of spring<br>       from her quiet places is overwhelming.</p>
