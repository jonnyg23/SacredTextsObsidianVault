 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="green">p. 40</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">A WORD FROM THE WIND</h3>  
 <p><small><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">From</span> an ancient Chinese Ballad of the fourth century A.D.</small></p>  
 <p>There is some one of whom I keep a-thinking;<br> There is some one whom I visit in my dreams,<br> Though a hundred hills stand sentinel between us,<br> And the dark rage of a hundred sunless streams.<br> For the same bright moon is kind to us,<br> And the same untrammelled wind to us, <br> Daring a hundred hills,<br> Whispers the word that thrills.<br> And the dust of my heart, laid bare,<br> Shows the lilies that linger there.</p>
