 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ON WAKING FROM SLEEP</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">At noon comes rest from the long routine;<br> I launch my boat on the lilied pond and float<br> Till I drift without will into sleep.<br> Green shadows lattice the waters green;<br> Courtyard and house the silence keep.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="green">p. 82</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Then a bird breaks over the mountain-side<br> And falls and calls from the crimson coronals<br> Of the woods that awake to her cry.<br> My silken robes in the wind float wide.<br> O wings of delight, draw nigh! draw nigh!</div>
