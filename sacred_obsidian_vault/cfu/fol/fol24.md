 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IN EXILE</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Through the green blinds that shelter me<br> Two butterflies at play—<br> Four wings of flame whirl joyously<br> Around me and away;<br> While swallows breasting to the shore<br> Ripple the waves they wander o’er.  
 <p>And I that scan the distant view<br> Of torn white clouds and mountains blue<br> Lift to the north my aching eyes;<br> ’Tis there—’tis there the city lies!<br> Chang-an arise! arise!</p></div>
