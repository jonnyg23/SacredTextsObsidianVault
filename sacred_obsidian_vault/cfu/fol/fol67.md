 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IN AN OLD LIBRARY</h3>  
 <p>Ten thousand tomes with pendant discs of jade,<br>    Bowls of old Shang with bronze of Chow displayed,<br>    And suddenly the small<br> Tinkle of girdle gems floats through the hall,<br>    As though the wind custodian sings:—<br>    "I guard the fragrance of a thousand springs.<br>    Draw near! draw near!<br> Ten thousand yesterdays are gathered here."</p>
