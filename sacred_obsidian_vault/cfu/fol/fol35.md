 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE LITTLE CROW</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The little lonely crow<br> Hovered around a little empty nest,<br> Waiting and wailing for the mother breast.<br> Ah! cold and far afield she cannot hear<br> The call incredulous of death. And so<br> It lurked in the old forest for a year.<br> And through the night its little piteous cry<br> Brought tears to all who wandered nigh;<br> As though in broken song it would repay<br> The debt of life to her who silent lay.<br> All other nestlings know a mother's care;<br> Thou, only thou shalt find not anywhere,<br> Nor warm dark wings fold down on thy despair.</div>
