 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">WILD GEESE</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">When wild geese leave the uplands lone<br> The frost turns sand and rock to stone.<br> When over Chiang Nan they scream<br> Pale leaves go drifting down the stream.<br> Broad is the water; heaven leans low;<br> Sullen and dun the cloud-wracks show.<br> When north winds tear the ragged sky<br> Their taper files go whistling by.</div>
