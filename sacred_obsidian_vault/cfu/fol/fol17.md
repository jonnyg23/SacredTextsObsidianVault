 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE PALACE OF CHAO-YANG</h3>  
 <p>No more the peach-tree droops beneath the snow;<br>    Spring draws her breath the willow boughs among.<br>    The mango-bird now maddens into song,<br> And the swift-building swallows come and go.<br> ’Tis the time of the long daydreams, when laughing maybeams,<br>    On the mats of slothful revellers play;<br> ’Tis the time of glancing wings, and the dancing<br>    Of moon-moths whirling the hours away;<br> When the golden armoured guardians are withdrawn,<br> And pleasure haunts the rustling woods till dawn.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  
 <p>A warm and perfumed wind<br>    Strays through the palace blind<br> And wandering prys into some dim retreat<br>    Where every whisper stirs the heart to beat.<br> Now all the gay parterres<br>    Are rivals for the sun<br> That drains their jewelled goblets one by one<br>    From dimpled terrace and green dewy stairs.<br> And the water-lily renders to the spring<br> The wonder of her white unbosoming.<br>    Far away in the tall woods there is an oriole calling;<br> There are shadows in the blue pavilion of dancers,<br>       and music rising and falling,<br> In the month of peach-bloom and plum-bloom,<br>       in the silken-screened recess<br> Love is the burden of sweet voices, and the brief<br>       night melting, and the long caress.</p>
