 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="green">p. 23</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>LOVE AND THE MAGISTRATE</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">When</span> the great carriage rumbles by,<br> I see him in his robes of state,<br> Calm, pitiless, sedate.<br> Man of the cold far-piercing eye,<br> O but I long for you,<br> Right for you, wrong for you,<br> Naught could keep us apart,<br> But the cold eye reading my heart.</p>  
 <p>When the great carriage rumbles on,<br> In robes of state carnation red<br> I see the man of dread,<br> Bright gleaming robes and glance of stone,<br> O then I long for you,<br> Right for you, wrong for you,<br> Naught could keep us apart<br> But the cold eye reading my heart.</p>  
 <p>Together we may never bide,<br> Nor you and me one roof contain,<br> But death shall not divide;<br> The same close grave shall wed the twain.<br> Say! am I cold to you?<br> Nay! I will hold to you,<br> By the bright sun I swear,<br> O my life, my love, my despair.</p>
