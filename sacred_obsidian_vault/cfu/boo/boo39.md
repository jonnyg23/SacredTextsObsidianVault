 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="green">p. 55</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>THE HOUSEHOLDER'S LAMENT</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> marshland holds the carambola tree;<br> Soft and pliant its branches be.<br> With its careless beauty and tender sheen,<br> The life of a tree is the life for me.</p>  
 <p>The marshland rears the carambola tree;<br> All purple and red its blossoms be.<br> In careless beauty and tender sheen,<br> Would I were childless and bland like thee.</p>  
 <p>The marshland loves the carambola tree;<br> Soft and sweet are the fruits I see.<br> Clothed with beauty and sunlit sheen,<br> The rateless and roofless life for me.</p>
