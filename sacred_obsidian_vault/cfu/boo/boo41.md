 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>GREY DAWNS AND RED</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Cold</span> is the rain and cold the wind,<br>     The cock gives dawn shrill greeting;<br> But a shadow steals across the blind,<br>     And oh! my heart is beating.</p>  
 <p>The rain drives down, the wind tears past,<br>     The cock shrills through the gloaming;<br> But love is in the house at last,<br>     And sorrow flies his homing.</p>  
 <p>Though the world look dark through wind and rain,<br>     And the dismal cock's a-crowing;<br> I'll sigh no more for the nights to wane,<br>     And its oh! for the red dawn's glowing.</p>
