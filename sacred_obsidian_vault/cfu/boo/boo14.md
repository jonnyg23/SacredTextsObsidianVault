 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>MAYTIME</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Deep</span> in the grass there lies a dead gazelle,<br> The tall white grass enwraps her where she fell.<br>      With sweet thoughts natural to spring,<br>      A pretty girl goes wandering<br>      With lover that would lead astray.</p>  
 <p>The little dwarf oaks hide a leafy dell,<br> Far in the wilds there lies a dead gazelle;<br> The tall white grass enwraps her where she fell,<br>      And beauty, like a gem, doth fling<br>      Bright radiance through the blinds of spring.<br>      "Ah, gently! do not disarray<br>      My kerchief! gently, pray!<br>      Nor make the watch-dog bark<br>      Under my lattice dark."</p>
