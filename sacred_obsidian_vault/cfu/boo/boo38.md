 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="green">p. 53</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>THE FOREST LOVER</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Këen-Kwan</span> the axles cried,<br> As I drove to claim my bride.<br> Hunger for her beauty presses,<br> I am parched for her caresses;<br> Though we lack good company,<br> We shall revel—I and she.</p>  
 <p>Dense the forest in the plain,<br> Where the long-tailed pheasants reign;<br> Happy is the house that owns her,<br> Where a lover's choice enthrones her.<br> Pledge me while I praise you, dear!<br> Love shall ever need you near.</p>  
 <p>Though I have but little wine,<br> Love makes little cups divine.<br> Though but one poor meal await us,<br> Simple fare shall amply sate us;<br> Though small worth is mine to bring,<br> Gaily we will dance and sing.</p>  
 <p>Yon tall ridges I ascend<br> And the stubborn firewood rend.<br> When the riven oaks are ringing<br> All my thoughts fly homeward winging;<br> Though their green abysses hide,<br> My whole heart is satisfied.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="green">p. 54</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Yon dim mountains disappear,<br> On the road the course is clear.<br> Gathering hooves go loudly drumming,<br> Reins like lute-strings join their thrumming;<br> Till beside the open door,<br> She is in my arms once more.</p>
