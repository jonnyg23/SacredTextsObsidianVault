 <p><a name="page_37"><font size="1" color="green">p. 37</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>A WIFE'S MEMORIES</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">With</span> taper rod of tall bamboo<br> You angle in the K’e,<br> Do I not go by dream to you<br> Who cannot come to me?</p>  
 <p>To left the Ts’euen waters roam,<br> The K’e flows on to right,<br> Ah! never gleams the newer home<br> Like that lost home to sight.</p>  
 <p>Leftward the Ts’euen stream beguiles,<br> And rightward calls the K’e,<br> Return, O light of happy smiles<br> And girdle-gems, to me!</p>  
 <p>The oars of cedar rise and fall<br> From boats of yellow pine,<br> Would I might roam the banks where all<br> The ghosts of girlhood shine!</p>
