 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="green">p. 51</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>FAINT HEART</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">I pray</span> you, dear,<br> My little hamlet leave,<br> Nor break my willow-boughs;<br> ’Tis not that I should grieve,<br> But I fear my sire to rouse.<br> Love pleads with passion disarrayed,—<br> "A sire's commands must be obeyed."</p>  
 <p>I pray you, dear,<br> Leap not across my wall,<br> Nor break my mulberry-boughs;<br> Not that I fear their fall,<br> But, lest my brother's wrath should rouse,<br> Love pleads with passion disarrayed,—<br> "A brother's words must be obeyed."</p>  
 <p>I pray you, dear,<br> Steal not my garden down,<br> Nor break my sandal-trees;<br> Not that I care for these,<br> But, oh! I dread the talk of town.<br> Should lovers have their wilful way,<br> Whatever would the neighbours say?</p>
