 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="green">p. 26</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>II</i></h3>  <h3 align="center"><i>THE PRAYER OF THE EMPEROR CHING</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Even</span> as a little helpless child am I,<br> On whom hath fallen the perplexed affairs<br> Of this unsettled state. High loneliness<br> And sorrow are my portion. Thou great Father,<br> Thou kingly pattern of parental awe,<br> Whose mind for ever in the courts beheld,<br> Roaming, the royal image of thy sire,<br> Night long and day long, I—the little child—<br> Will so be reverent.</p>  
 <p>                         O ye great kings!<br> Your crowned successor crowns you in his heart.<br> Live unforgotten. Here, upon the verge<br> Of the momentous years, I pause and trace<br> The shining footsteps of my forefathers,<br> And the far-distant goal that drew them on—<br> Too distant for my range. Howe’er resolved<br> I may go forward, lo! a thousand tracks<br> Cause me to swerve aside. A little child—<br> Only a little child—I am too frail<br> To cope with the anxieties of state<br> And cares of king-craft. Yet I will ascend</p>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Into my Father's room, and through the courts<br> Below, for ever seeking, I will pass,<br> To brush the skirts of inspiration<br> And touch the sleeves of memory.</p>  
 <p>                                     O great<br> And gracious Father, hear and condescend<br> To guard, to cherish, to enlighten me.</p>
