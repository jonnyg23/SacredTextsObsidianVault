 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>SORROW</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">She</span> sought her native land again.<br> <i>The swallow takes its ragged flight.<br> </i>We went together day and night,<br> Till parting drew her from my sight<br> And the tears fell down like rain.</p>  
 <p>She went her native land to seek.<br> <i>Now up, now down the swallow flies.<br> </i>And oh!—the last of tender ties,<br> The form that fades from aching eyes<br> And the tears coursing down my cheek.</p>  
 <p><i>Around, about the swallows dar<br> </i>She fared into a far countree,<br> And when I vainly sought to see<br> The empty landscape mocked at me,<br> And great grief settled on my heart.</p>
