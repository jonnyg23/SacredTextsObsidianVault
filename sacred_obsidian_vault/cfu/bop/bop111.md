 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="green">p. 117</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Fên Chü Ju;</i> allusive. Against the parsimoniousness of the officers of Wei.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>  Where near the Fên damp is the ground,<br>   The sorrel gatherers are found,<br>     To eke their scanty food.<br>   Such arts that officer displays,<br>   (Whose elegance exceeds all praise;)<br>     In him they are not good.<br> Charged with the cars of state, we look to find<br> His conduct show a higher style of mind.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  On the Fên's banks the poor are found,<br>   Who pluck the mulberry leaves around,<br>     A little gain to make.<br>   In grace and beauty like a flower,<br>   That officer himself doth lower,<br>     Such small mean ways to take.<br> The cars of state to marshal is his charge;—<br> Strange such high post his mind should not enlarge!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Where the Fên bends to join the Ho,<br>   For ox-lip leaves the people go,<br>     Some nourishment to find. <a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="green">p. 118</font></a><br>   That officer we gemlike call,<br>   Yet shrinks he not from ways as small,<br>     To greed too much inclined.<br> The ruler's kindred he has for his care;—<br> Should he not show a loftier character?</p></div>
