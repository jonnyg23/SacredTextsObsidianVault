 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="green">p. 128</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Shan Yu Ch‘u;</i> allusive. The folly of not enjoying the good things which we have, and letting death put them into the hands of others.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The thorny elms on the mountains grow,<br> And the white elms rise where the grounds are low.<br> You have suits of robes which you never wear;<br> You have steeds and cars you are fain to spare.<br> All these another will have by and by,<br> For the time will come when you shall die.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The <i>k‘ao</i> trees thrive on the mountain's brow,<br> And the <i>new</i> trees rise where the grounds are low.<br> Unwatered your courts, your rooms are unwept;<br> Your drums and your bells all silent are kept.<br> All these another shall have by and by,<br> For the time will come when you shall die.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The varnish trees on the mountains grow,<br> And the chestnuts rise where the grounds are low.<br> Why not, at the feast, your lute gayly play,<br> To add to your joy, and lengthen the day.<br> Another's your house will be by and by,<br> For the time will come when you shall die.</p></div>
