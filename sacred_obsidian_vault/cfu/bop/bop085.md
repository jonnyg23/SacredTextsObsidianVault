 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VIII. Scoticè</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Nü Yüeh Chi Ming;</i> narrative. A pleasant picture of domestic life. A wife sends her husband from her side to his hunting, expresses her affection, and encourages him to cultivate virtuous friendships.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Says oor gudewife, "The cock is crawin’."<br> Quoth oor gudeman, "The day is dawin’."<br> "Get up, gudeman, an’ tak a spy;<br> See gin the mornin’ star be high,<br> Syne tak a saunter roon’ aboot;<br> There’s rowth o’ dyukes and geese to shoot.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>"Lat flee, and bring them hame to me,<br> An’ sic a dish as ye sail pree.<br> In comin’ times as ower the strings<br> Your noddin’ heed in rapture hings,<br> Supreme ower care, nor fasht wi’ fears,<br> We’ll baith grow auld in worth and years, <a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="green">p. 94</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>"An’ when we meet the friends ye like,<br> I’ll gie to each some little fyke;—<br> The lasses beads, trocks to their brithers,<br> An’ auld-warld fairlies to their mithers.<br> Some knickknack lovin’ hands will fin’<br> To show the love that dwalls within.</p></div>
