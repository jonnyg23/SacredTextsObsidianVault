 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Mao Ch‘iu;</i> allusive and narrative. The refugee ministers of Li complain of those of Wei for not assisting them.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>  On that high sloping mound,<br>     With joints now parted wide,<br>   The plants of dolichos<br>     Show here we long abide.<br> Wei's nobles, whom we uncles style,<br> Why thus delay on us to smile?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  They rest and do not stir;—<br>     Do they allies expect?<br>   Wherefore protract the time?<br>     Why us so much neglect?<br> Some reason they could surely plead<br> For conduct, strange in this our need. <a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="green">p. 40</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>  In chariots of the west,<br>     Hither from danger borne,<br>   In Wei we live depressed,<br>     Our fox furs frayed and worn.<br> Ye nobles, uncles, sooth to say,<br> For us no sympathy display.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>  A remnant small of Li,<br>     Driven from our proper home;<br>   Children dispersed, we hoped<br>     That help from Wei would come.<br> Alas! though grand the robes you wear,<br> You stop your ears against our prayer.</p></div>
