 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Nan Shan;</i> allusive. On the disgraceful connection between Wên Chiang, the marchioness of Lu, and her brother;—against Hsiang of Ch'i and Huan of Lu.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>There where the south hill rises high and great<br>   A male fox sits, suspicious and alone.<br> Ch‘i’s daughter went to Lu, to wed her mate;<br>   The pathway, plain and easy, is well known.<br> From you, her brother, thus away she went;<br> Why further think of her, like fox intent?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The dolichos five kinds of shoes supplies,<br>   Made always so that two shall form a pair.<br> On caps they match the strings that serve for ties,<br>   The same in length, not differing a hair. <a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="green">p. 110</font></a><br> The road to Lu all plain and easy lies,<br>   By which Ch‘i’s daughter joined her husband there.<br> Since she has traveled that way leaving you,<br> Why do you her continue to pursue?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Hemp seed to sow, this is the course we take,—<br>   The acres lengthwise and across we dress.<br> Taking a wife, the thing at once we make<br>   Known to our parents, and their leave possess.<br> Since, prince of Lu, Ch‘i’s daughter thus you took;—<br> Why on her evil ways indulgent look?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>How do we act when firewood we would split?<br>   ’Tis through the ax in hand that we succeed.<br> Taking a wife, this form we must admit,—<br>   Without the go-between we cannot speed.<br> When you brought home your wife, all this was done;—<br> Why let her now to such excesses run?</p></div>
