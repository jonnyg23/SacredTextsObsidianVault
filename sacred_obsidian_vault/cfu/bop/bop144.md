 <p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="green">p. 155</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Mu Mên</i>; allusive. On some evil person who was going on obstinately to his ruin.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Where through the gate in to the tombs we go,<br> Thick jujube trees, the ax requiring, grow.<br> Like them that man, who ill befits his place,<br> And through the state is reckoned a disgrace!<br> All know him bad, but to his course he'll hold,<br> So long to evil has the man been sold.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Where through the gate in to the tombs we turn,<br> Owls perched upon the plum trees we discern.<br> Such omen well may to that man belong,<br> Whom to admonish I now sing this song.<br> No welcome will the admonition find;<br> When overthrown, my words he'll call to mind.</p></div>
