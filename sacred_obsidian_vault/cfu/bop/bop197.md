 <p><a name="page_230"><font size="1" color="green">p. 230</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Wo Hsing Ch‘i Yeh;</i> narrative. An officer, who had left the royal domain, and sought for protection in a state where he had affinities by marriage, relates his disappointment, and the unworthy cause of it.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>All through the fields I traveled sad,<br>   Th’ ailanthus foul my only shade.<br> Through our relationship I came,<br>   Shelter to find with you and aid.<br> But me you show no wish to entertain;—<br> Back to my state and clan I go again!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>All through the fields I traveled sad,<br>   And for my food the sheep's-foot cooked.<br> Through our relationship, to you<br>   For lodging for a time I looked.<br> But me you show no wish to entertain;—<br> Back to my kindred now I go again!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>All through the fields I went and tried<br>   Hunger with pokeweed to appease.<br> I came to you; your love is cold;<br>   And your new mate you seek to please.<br> Grant that your heart her riches have not won,—<br> Her charms are new; my relative's are gone!</p></div>
