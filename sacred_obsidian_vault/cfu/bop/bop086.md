 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yu Nü T‘ung Ch‘ê;</i> narrative. The praise of some lady.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>There by his side in chariot rideth she,<br> As lovely flower of the hibiscus tree,<br> So fair her face; and when about they wheel,<br> Her girdle gems of <i>chü</i> themselves reveal.<br> For beauty all the house of Chiang have fame;<br> Its eldest daughter,—she beseems her name.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>There on the path, close by him, walketh she,<br> Bright as the blossom of hibiscus tree<br> And fair her face; and when around they flit,<br> Her girdle gems a tinkling sound emit.<br> Among the Chiang she has distinguished place,<br> For virtuous fame renowned, and peerless grace.</p></div>
