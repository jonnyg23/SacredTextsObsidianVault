 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yung;</i> narrative. Appropriate at a sacrifice by King Wu to his father Wên.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">The helping princes stand around,<br>   With reverent air, in concord fine.<br> The king, Heaven's son, with looks profound,<br>   Thus prays before his father's shrine:— <a name="page_447"><font size="1" color="green">p. 447</font></a>  
 <p>"This noble bull I bring to thee,<br>   And these assist me in the rite.<br> Father, august and great, on me,<br>   Thy filial son, pour down thy light!</p>  
 <p>"All-sagely didst thou play the man,<br>   Alike in peace and war a king.<br> Heaven rested in thee, O great Wên,<br>   Who to thy sons still good dost bring.</p>  
 <p>"The eyebrows of long life to me,<br>   Great source of comfort, thou hast given.<br> Thou mak’st me great, for ‘tis through thee<br>   Come all the other gifts of Heaven.</p>  
 <p>"O thou, my meritorious sire,<br>   And thou in whose fond breast I lay,<br> With power and grace your son inspire<br>   His reverent sacrifice to pay.</p></div>
