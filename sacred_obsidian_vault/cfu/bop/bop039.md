 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Shih Wei;</i> narrative. The officers of some state, who were refugees and in distress in Wei, exhort their ruler to return with them.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>At this low ebb! At this low ebb!<br>   Why not, O prince, return to Li? <a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="green">p. 39</font></a><br> But for your sake, why bide we here,<br>   Houseless beneath the dew to be?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>At this low ebb! At this low ebb!<br>   Why not to Li go back again?<br> But for your person, how should we<br>   Here in the mire so long have lain?</p></div>
