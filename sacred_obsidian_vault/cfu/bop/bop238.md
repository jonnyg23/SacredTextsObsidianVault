 <p><a name="page_322"><font size="1" color="green">p. 322</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">V</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Pai Hua;</i> metaphorical. The wife of King Yu complains of being degraded and forsaken.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The fibers of the white-flowered rush<br>   Are with the white grass bound.<br> So do the two together go,<br>   In closest union found.<br> And thus should man and wife abide,<br>   The twain combined in one;<br> But this bad man sends me away,<br>   And bids me dwell alone.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Both rush and grass from the bright clouds<br>   The genial dew partake.<br> Kind and impartial, nature's laws<br>   No odious difference make.<br> But providence appears unkind;<br>   Events are often hard.<br> This man, to principle untrue,<br>   Denies me his regard.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Northward the pools their waters send,<br>   To flood each paddy field;<br> So get the fields the sap they need,<br>   Their store of rice to yield. <a name="page_323"><font size="1" color="green">p. 323</font></a><br> But that great man no deed of grace<br>   Deigns to bestow on me.<br> My songs are sighs. At thought of him<br>   My heart aches wearily.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>The mulberry branches they collect,<br>   And use their food to cook.<br> But I must use a furnace small,<br>   That pot nor pan will brook.<br> So me that great man badly treats,<br>   Nor uses as his wife,<br> Degrades me from my proper place,<br>   And fills with grief my life.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>The bells and drums inside the court<br>   Men stand without and hear;<br> So should the feelings in my breast,<br>   To him distinct appear.<br> All-sorrowful, I think of him,<br>   Longing to move his love;<br> But he vouchsafes no kind response;<br>   His thoughts far from me rove.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>The marabou stands on the dam,<br>   And to repletion feeds; <a name="page_324"><font size="1" color="green">p. 324</font></a><br> The crane deep in the forest cries,<br>   Nor finds the food it needs.<br> So in my room the concubine<br>   By the great man is placed;<br> While I with cruel banishment<br>   Am cast out and disgraced.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_7">7</a></font></span>The yellow ducks sit on the dam,<br>   With left wing gathered low;<br> So on each other do they lean,<br>   And their attachment show.<br> And love should thus the man and wife<br>   In closest concord bind;<br> But that man turns away from me,<br>   And shows a fickle mind.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_8">8</a></font></span>When one stands on a slab of stone,<br>   No higher than the ground,<br> Nothing is added to his height;—<br>   Low with the stone he's found.<br> So does the favorite's mean estate<br>   Render that great man mean,<br> While I by him, to distance sent,<br>   Am pierced with sorrow keen.</p></div>
