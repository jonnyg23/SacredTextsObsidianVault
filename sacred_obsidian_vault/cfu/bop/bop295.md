The Decade of Min Yü Hsiao Tzŭ</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Min Yü Hsiao Tzŭ;</i> narrative. Appropriate to the young King Chêng, declaring his sentiments in the temple of his father.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">Alas for me, still but a child!<br>   For all too soon I know<br> The cares of the unsettled state.<br>   Too heavy will they grow.<br> I shrink in sorrow from the task;<br>   But, O my mighty sire,<br> To filial virtue, all thy life,<br>   Thou ever didst aspire. <a name="page_451"><font size="1" color="green">p. 451</font></a>  
 <p>My great grandsire, though now enshrined,<br>   To thee still living proved,<br> As if in courtyard and in hall<br>   His royal person moved,<br> And I, the little child, will be<br>   As reverent night and day.<br> To you, great kings, such homage I<br>   Now on your throne will pay.</p></div>
