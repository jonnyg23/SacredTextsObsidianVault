 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ho Pi Nung I</i>; allusive. The marriage of one of the royal princesses.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Of flowers in clusters large and gay,<br> How bright the sparrow plum's display!<br> In reverent harmony on glide<br> The chariots of the royal bride.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>These flowers their clusters large and gay,<br> As of the peach or plum display.<br> This grandchild of the peaceful king<br> Joy to the marquis’ son shall bring.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>As threads of silk together twine,<br> To form the angler's faithful line;<br> So may the union close abide<br> Of gallant prince and royal bride!</p></div>
