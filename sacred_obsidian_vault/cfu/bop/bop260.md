 <p><a name="page_375"><font size="1" color="green">p. 375</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chiung Cho;</i> allusive. The most unlikely things may by human ingenuity be made useful; how much more should a sovereign fulfill the duties of his position!</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Bring water from the distant pool,<br>   And let it settle in a jar.<br> Millet or rice ’twill steam—such use<br>   May come from things that paltry are.<br> You are our happy, courteous king;<br>   On you a noble duty lies.<br> Father and mother, joined in one,<br>   With all the people sympathize!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Bring water from the distant pool,<br>   And let it settle in a jar.<br> A spirit vase ’twill wash;—such use<br>   May come from things that paltry are.<br> You are our happy, courteous king,<br>   Born subject to a noble law.<br> Like center to which all should turn,<br>   So you the people ever draw. <a name="page_376"><font size="1" color="green">p. 376</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Bring water from the distant pool,<br>   And let it settle in a jar.<br> All things ’twill serve to cleanse;—such use<br>   May come from things that paltry are.<br> You are our happy, courteous king,<br>   Set to fulfill a noble end.<br> The center in which all have rest,<br>   The people's hearts to you should tend.</p></div>
