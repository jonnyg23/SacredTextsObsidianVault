 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chang Ti;</i> allusive and narrative. Setting forth the close relation and affection that ought to obtain between brothers.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>      With mass of gorgeous flowers<br>       The cherry trees are crowned,<br> But none within this world of ours<br>       Like brothers can be found.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>      When awful death comes near,<br>       ’Tis brothers sympathize.<br> When headlong flight fills plain and height,<br>       To brother brother flies. <a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>      See how the wagtail's head<br>       Quick answers to its tail!<br> When hardships great befall our state,<br>       Friends, are of no avail.<br>       In times of urgent need,<br>       We brothers’ help receive.<br> Then friends, though good, of different blood,<br>       Long sighs will only heave.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>      Brothers indoors may fight;<br>       But insults from without<br> Join them at once, and they unite<br>       The common foe to rout.<br>       In cases such as this,<br>       In vain to friends we turn.<br> They may be true, but they'll eschew<br>       The danger they discern.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>      Deaths and disorder o’er,<br>       ’Mid peace and rest now cold,<br> Some men, alas! their brothers pass,<br>       Nor them as friends will hold. <a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>      With dishes in array,<br>       The cup may oft go round;<br> But only where brothers are there,<br>       The feast is truly found.<br>       ’Tis when they all appear,<br>       And each is in his place,<br> That childlike joy, without alloy,<br>       Crowns harmony with grace.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_7">7</a></font></span>      Children and wife we love;<br>       Union with them is sweet<br> As lute's soft strain that soothes our pain.<br>       How joyous do we meet!<br>       But brothers, more than they,<br>       Can satisfy the heart.<br> ’Tis <i>their</i> accord does peace afford,<br>       And lasting joy impart.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_8">8</a></font></span>      For ordering of your homes,<br>       For joy with child and wife,<br> Consider well the truth I tell;—<br>       This is the charm of life!</p></div>
