 <p>The <i>Ho Kuang;</i> narrative. Other things more difficult to overcome than distance may keep one from a place.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>They tell me that the Ho is wide;—<br>   With a few reeds I could cross through.<br> They tell me Sung is distant far;—<br>   Rising on tiptoe Sung I view. <a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="green">p. 71</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>They tell me that the Ho is wide;—<br>   A little boat it will not bear.<br> They tell me Sung is distant far;—<br>   Ere morning fades I might be there.</p></div>
