 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Lin Chih Chih;</i> allusive. Celebrating the goodness of the offspring and descendants of King Wên.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>As the feet of the <i>lin</i>, which avoid each living thing,<br> So our prince's noble sons no harm to men will bring.<br> <i>            They</i> are the <i>lin!</i>   
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>As the front of the <i>lin</i>, never forward thrust in wrath, <a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="green">p. 11</font></a><br> So our prince's noble grandsons of love tread the path.<br> <i>            They</i> are the <i>lin!</i> </p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>As the horn of the <i>lin</i>, flesh-tipped, no wound to give,<br> So our prince's noble kindred kindly with all live.<br> <i>            They</i> are the <i>lin!</i> </p></div>
