 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chung Nan;</i> allusive. Celebrating the growing opulence of some ruler of Ch‘in, and admonishing, while praising, him.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>What trees grow on the Chung-nan hill?<br>   The white fir and the plum.<br> In fur of fox, ’neath broidered robe,<br>   Thither our prince is come. <a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="green">p. 145</font></a><br> His face glows with vermilion hue.<br> O may he prove a ruler true!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>What find we on the Chung-nan hill?<br>   Deep nook and open glade.<br> Our prince shows there the double chi<br>   On lower robe displayed.<br> His pendant holds each tinkling gem.<br> Long life be his, and deathless fame!</p></div>
