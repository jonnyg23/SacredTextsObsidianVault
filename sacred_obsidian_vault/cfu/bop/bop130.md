 <p><a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="green">p. 140</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ssŭ T‘ieh;</i> narrative. Celebrating the growing opulence of the lords of Ch‘in, as seen in their hunting.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Our ruler to the hunt proceeds;<br> And black as iron are his steeds<br> That heed the charioteer's command,<br> Who holds the six reins in his hand.<br> His favorites follow to the chase,<br> Rejoicing in his special grace.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The season's males, alarmed, arise,—<br> The season's males, of wondrous size.<br> Driven by the beaters, forth they spring,<br> Soon caught within the hunters’ ring.<br> "Drive on their left," the ruler cries;<br> And to its mark his arrow flies.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The hunting done, northward he goes;<br> And in the park the driver shows<br> The horses’ points, and his own skill<br> That rules and guides them at his will.<br> Light cars, whose teams small bells display,<br> The long and short-mouthed dogs convey.</p></div>
