 <p><a name="page_250"><font size="1" color="green">p. 250</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book V. The Decade of Hsiao Min</span></h3>  <h4 align="center"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span></h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hsiao Min;</i> narrative. A lamentation over the recklessness and incapacity of the king's plans and of his counselors.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Oh! pitying Heaven grows black with frown,<br>   That darkens far this lower sphere,<br> For crooked schemes mislead the crown;<br>   Nor halts the king in his career!<br> All counsels good and wise he spurns;<br> To counsels bad he eager turns.<br>   I mark his ways with pain and fear.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>His creatures, impotent and vain,<br>   Now chime, now chafe, in rival mood.<br> The case deserves our saddest strain!<br>   If one proposes aught that's good,<br> Against it all are firmly bound;<br> If bad, then all will rally round.<br>   Where will it end?—I sadly brood. <a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="green">p. 251</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Our wearied oracles are dumb,<br>   And silence keep when we consult.<br> Our counselors still thronging come,<br>   With counsels barren of result.<br> Though full the court, none dares to do;<br> We plan the way we ne’er pursue,<br>   And halting, helpless still we halt.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Ah me! the men who lead the state,<br>   Forsake the wisdom of the past.<br> Unruled by maxims wise and great,<br>   They veer with every fitful blast.<br> They cannot on themselves rely.<br> Builders, they ask each passer-by,<br>   And leave their work undone at last.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>Unsettled though the land we see,<br>   The many foolish, some are wise;<br> And scanty though the people be,<br>   Yet some can see, and some devise. <a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="green">p. 252</font></a><br> Some gravely think, and some have tact;<br> Yet borne upon the cataract,<br>   We sink in ruin, ne’er to rise.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>Who dares unarmed the tiger face?<br>   Who boatless dares to tempt the Ho?<br> E’en their small wits see such a case,<br>   But nothing greater do they know.<br> With fear and caution should we tread,<br> Like men above some torrent's bed,<br>   Or those upon thin ice who go.</p></div>
