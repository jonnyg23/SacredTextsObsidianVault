 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yüeh Ch‘u;</i> allusive. A gentleman tells all the excitement of his desire for the possession of a beautiful lady.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The moon comes forth, bright in the sky;<br> A lovelier sight to draw my eye<br>   Is she, that lady fair.<br> She round my heart has fixed love's chain,<br> But all my longings are in vain.<br>   ’Tis hard the grief to bear.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The moon comes forth, a splendid sight;<br> More winning far that lady bright,<br>   Object of my desire!<br> Deep-seated is my anxious grief;<br> In vain I seek to find relief,<br>   While glows the secret fire. <a name="page_157"><font size="1" color="green">p. 157</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The rising moon shines mild and fair;<br> More bright is she, whose beauty rare<br>   My heart with longing fills.<br> With eager wish I pine in vain;<br> O for relief from constant pain,<br>   Which through my bosom thrills!</p></div>
