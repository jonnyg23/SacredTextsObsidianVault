 <p><a name="page_300"><font size="1" color="green">p. 300</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘ê Hsia;</i> narrative and allusive. The rejoicing of a bridegroom over his young, beautiful, and virtuous bride.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>With axle creaking, all on fire I went,<br>   To fetch my young and lovely bride.<br> No thirst or hunger pangs my bosom rent,—<br>   I only longed to have her by my side.<br> I feast with her, whose virtue fame had told,<br> Nor need we friends our rapture to behold.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The long-tailed pheasants surest covert find,<br>   Amid the forest on the plain.<br> Here from my virtuous bride, of noble mind,<br>   And person tall, I wisdom gain.<br> I praise her while we feast, and to her say,<br> "The love I bear you ne’er will know decay.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>"Poor we may be; spirits and viands fine<br>   My humble means will not afford. <a name="page_301"><font size="1" color="green">p. 301</font></a><br> But what we have, we'll taste and not repine;<br>   From us will come no grumbling word.<br> And though to you no virtue I can add,<br> Yet we will sing and dance, in spirit glad.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>"I oft ascend that lofty ridge with toil,<br>   And hew large branches from the oaks;<br> Then of their leafy glory them I spoil,<br>   And fagots form with vigorous strokes.<br> Returning tired, your matchless grace I see,<br> And my whole soul dissolves in ecstasy.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>"To the high hills I looked, and urged each steed;<br>   The great road next was smooth and plain.<br> Up hill, o’er dale, I never slackened speed;<br>   Like lutestring sounded every rein.<br> I knew, my journey ended, I should come<br> To you, sweet bride, the comfort of my home."</p></div>
