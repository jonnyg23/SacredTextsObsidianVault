 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IX</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Yu Hu;</i> metaphorical. A woman expresses her desire for a husband.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Lonely, suspicious, is that fox,<br>   At that dame cross the Ch‘i.<br> Like him that man, for whom I'm sad!<br>   No lower dress has he.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Lonely, suspicious, is that fox,<br>   At that deep ford of Ch‘i.<br> Like him that man, for whom I'm sad!<br>   No girdle sash has he!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Lonely, suspicious, is that fox,<br>   There, on the bank of Ch‘i.<br> Like him that man, for whom I'm sad!<br>   No clothes at all has he!</p></div>
