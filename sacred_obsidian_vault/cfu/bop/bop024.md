 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">X</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hsiao Hsing;</i> allusive. The thankful submission to their lot of the inferior members of a harem.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>  Behold those starlets small,<br>     How three or five the east illume! <a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="green">p. 20</font></a><br>     Swiftly we came when fell the gloom,<br>   And now at dawn the hall<br> Of the harêm we leave, nor dare gainsay<br> Our lot which grants us here no longer stay.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  Behold those starlets small,<br>     Orion and the Pleiads bright!<br>     Swiftly we came as failed the light,<br>   And here brought to the hall<br> Our coverlets and sheets. Now we return,<br> Nor our inferior lot presume to mourn.</p></div>
