 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The Chi Ku; narrative. Soldiers of Wei bewail their separation from their families, and anticipate that it will be final.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>List to the thunder and roll of the drum!<br>   See how we spring and brandish the dart!<br> Some raise Ts‘ao’s walls; some do field work at home;<br>   But we to the southward lonely depart.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Our chief, Sun Tzŭ-chung, agreement has made,<br>   Our forces to join with Chen and with Sung.<br> When shall we back from this service be led?<br>   Our hearts are all sad, our courage unstrung. <a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="green">p. 32</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Here we are halting, and there we delay;<br>   Anon we soon lose our high-mettled steeds.<br> The forest's gloom makes our steps go astray;<br>   Each thicket of trees our searching misleads.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>For death as for life, at home or abroad,<br>   We pledged to our wives our faithfulest word.<br> Their hands clasped in ours, together we vowed,<br>   We'd live to old age in sweetest accord.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>This march to the south can end but in ill;<br>   Oh! never shall we our wives again meet.<br> The word that we pledged we cannot fulfill;<br>   Us home returning they never will greet.</p></div>
