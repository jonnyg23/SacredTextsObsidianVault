 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>I Chieh;</i> narrative. Lament over Duke Chuang of Lu, notwithstanding his beauty of person, elegance of manners, and skill in archery.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>A grand man is the prince of Lu,<br>   With person large and high.<br> Lofty his front, and suited to<br>   The fine glance of his eye! <a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="green">p. 115</font></a><br> Swift are his feet. In archery<br>   What man with him can vie?<br> With all these goodly qualities,<br>   We see him and we sigh!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Renowned through all the land is he,<br>   The nephew of our lord.<br> With clear and lovely eyes, his grace<br>   May not be told by word.<br> All day at target practice,<br>   He'll never miss the bird.<br> Such is the prince of Lu, and yet<br>   With grief for him we're stirred!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>All grace and beauty he displays,<br>   High forehead, and eyes bright.<br> And dancing choice! His arrows all<br>   The target hit aright.<br> Straight through they go, and every one<br>   Lights on the selfsame spot.<br> Rebellion he could well withstand,<br>   And yet we mourn his lot!</p></div>
