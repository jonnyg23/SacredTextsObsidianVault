 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ku Chung;</i> narrative. Supposed to refer to and deplore some expedition of King Yu to the country about the Huai, where he abandoned himself to the delights of music.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>          How peal the royal bells,<br> As the Huai sweeps along to the main!<br>           A tale their music tells,<br> Waking thoughts in my mind full of pain.<br>           Before me back it sadly brings<br>           The memory of our virtuous kings;<br> And they live in my fancy again! <a name="page_280"><font size="1" color="green">p. 280</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>          Loud roll the royal drums,<br> As the Huai rushes on to the deep.<br>           A vanished memory comes<br> In their sound which compels me to weep,—<br>           The memory of our kings of old,<br>           Whose virtue flawless still we hold,<br> Though the kings in their sepulchers sleep.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>          Bells peal and drums resound,<br> As the Huai its three islands displays.<br>           They stir a grief profound<br> In my heart that no revel allays.<br>           The virtue of our kings of yore<br>           A stamp of truth and beauty bore,<br> Such as never we see nowadays.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>          <i>Ch‘in-ch‘in</i> the bells peal on,<br> And the lutes in the concert we hear.<br>           Deep breathes the organ tone;<br> Sounding stones join their notes, rich and clear.<br>           The while through the vessel there ring<br>           The <i>Ya</i> and the <i>Nan</i> which they sing,<br> And the dancers with flutes now appear.</p></div>
