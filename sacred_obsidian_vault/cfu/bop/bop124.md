 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Pao Yü;</i> allusive or metaphorical. The men of Chin, called out to warfare by the king's order, mourn over the consequent suffering of their parents, and long for their return to their ordinary agricultural pursuits.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The wild geese fly the bushy oaks around,<br> With clamor loud. <i>Su-su</i> their wings resound,<br> As for their feet poor resting place is found. <a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="green">p. 134</font></a><br> The king's affairs admit of no delay;<br> Our millets still unsown, we haste away.<br> No food is left our parents to supply;<br> When we are gone, on whom can they rely?<br> O azure Heaven, that shinest there afar,<br> When shall our homes receive us from the war?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The wild geese on the bushy jujube trees<br> Attempt to settle, and are ill at ease;—<br> <i>Su-su</i> their wings go flapping in the breeze.<br> The king's affairs admit of no delay;<br> Our millets still unsown, we haste away.<br> How shall our parents their requirements get?<br> How in our absence shall their wants be met?<br> O azure Heaven, that shinest there afar,<br> When shall our homes receive us from the war?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The bushy mulberry trees the geese in rows<br> Seek eager, and to rest around them close,—<br> With rustling loud, as disappointment grows.<br> The king's affairs admit of no delay;<br> To plant our rice and maize we cannot stay.<br> How shall our parents find their wonted food?<br> When we are gone, who will to them be good?<br> O azure Heaven, that shinest there afar,<br> When shall our homes receive us from the war?</p></div>
