 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>I Hsi;</i> narrative. Instructions to the officers of husbandry:—probably like the preceding ode, after a sacrifice to God for a good year.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">O yes! the spirit of King Ch‘êng,<br>   When we inquired of him, drew nigh,<br> And clearly told us when to bring<br>   The gifts that now before him lie.<br> Lead forth your men, with energy<br>   O’er their own fields to sow the grain, <a name="page_443"><font size="1" color="green">p. 443</font></a><br> Those, in each square of thirty li,<br>   Ten thousand families can maintain.  
 <p>Now let the plows turn up the soil,<br>   The men attending, pair by pair.<br> The harvest will repay their toil;—<br>   The bounteous produce all shall share.</p></div>
