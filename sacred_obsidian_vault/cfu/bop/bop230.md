 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yü Tsao;</i> allusive. Praise of the king by the princes at some feast:—his quiet happiness in Hao.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Fishes there among the pondweed lie;<br> From the bank their large heads we espy;—<br>        Fishes could not happier be. <a name="page_308"><font size="1" color="green">p. 308</font></a><br> Here in Hao resides our lord, the king;<br> To him joy his festive pleasures bring.<br>        Happy and at ease is he.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Fishes there among the pondweed glide;<br> From the bank their long tails are descried;—<br>        Fishes could not happier be.<br> Here in Hao resides the king, our lord;<br> Festive pleasures joy to him afford.<br>        Happy and at ease is he.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Fishes there among the pondweed live,<br> Shelter to them where the rushes give;—<br>        Fishes could not happier be.<br> Here in Hao the king, our lord, resides;<br> Safe and tranquil ever here he bides.<br>        Happy and at ease is he.</p></div>
