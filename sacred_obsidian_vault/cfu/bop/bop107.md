 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IX</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Pi Kou;</i> metaphorical. The bold licentious freedom of Wên Chiang in returning to Ch‘i;—against Duke Chuang of Lu, her son.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Rent is the basket at the dam,<br>   Where bream and kuan abound;<br> As useless is the prince of Lu,<br>   In vigor wanting found.<br> Ch‘i’s daughter now to Chi comes back,<br>   Her followers as a cloud;<br> Her son should hold her fast in Lu,<br>   Her wickedness to shroud.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Rent is the basket at the dam,<br>   Where bream and tench abound;<br> As useless is the prince of Lu,<br>   In vigor wanting found.<br> Back comes Ch‘i’s daughter, with a shower<br>   Of followers at her side, <a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="green">p. 113</font></a><br> Her son should hold her fast in Lu,<br>   Her wanton ways to hide.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Rent is the basket at the dam;<br>   Fish in and out can go;<br> As useless is the prince of Lu,<br>   And vigor fails to show.<br> Back comes Ch‘i’s daughter, with a stream<br>   Of followers behind;<br> Her son should hold her fast in Lu,<br>   From such lewd ways confined.</p></div>
