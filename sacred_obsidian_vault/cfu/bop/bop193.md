 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ho Ming;</i> metaphorical. Certain moral lessons from natural facts.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>All true words fly, as from yon reedy marsh<br> The crane rings o’er the wild its screaming harsh.<br> Vainly you try reason in chains to keep;—<br> Freely it moves as fish sweeps through the deep.<br> Hate follows love, as ’neath those sandal trees<br> The withered leaves the eager searcher sees.<br> The hurtful ne’er without some good was born;—<br> The stones that mar the hill will grind the corn. <a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="green">p. 226</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>All true words spread, as from the marsh's eye<br> The crane's sonorous note ascends the sky.<br> Goodness throughout the widest sphere abides,<br> As fish round isle and through the ocean glides.<br> And lesser good near greater you shall see,<br> As grows the paper shrub ’neath sandal tree.<br> And good emerges from what man condemns;—<br> Those stones that mar the hill will polish gems.</p></div>
