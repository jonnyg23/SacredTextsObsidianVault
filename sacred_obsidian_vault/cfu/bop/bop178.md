 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="green">p. 205</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">V</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Nan Yu Chia Yü;</i> allusive. A festal ode appropriate to the entertainment of worthy guests, and celebrating the generous sympathy of the entertainer.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The fishers of the south for barbel make<br> Sharp search, and many with their baskets take.<br> The host his noble guests has gathered here;<br> They drink with him, and joyous share his cheer.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The barbel of the south are largely caught<br> In wicker nets, which then to land are brought.<br> The host has round him called each noble guest;<br> They drink with him, delighted with the feast.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The southern trees with drooping branches grow,<br> Round which the sweet gourds clasp and twine below.<br> His noble guests around the host we see;<br> They drink with him, and feast in cheerful glee.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>About the filial doves incessant fly;<br> Flock follows flock, in wheeling circles high.<br> The noble guests long at their cups remain;<br> They freely drink, and then they drink again.</p></div>
