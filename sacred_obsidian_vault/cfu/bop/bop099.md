 <p><a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="green">p. 105</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book VIII. The Odes of Ch‘i</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chi Ming;</i> narrative. A model marchioness stimulating her husband to rise early, and attend to his duties at court.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>His lady to the marquis says,<br>   "The cock has crowed; ‘tis late.<br> Get up, my lord, and haste to court.<br>   ’Tis full; for you they wait."<br> She did not hear the cock's shrill sound,<br> Only the blue flies buzzing round.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Again she wakes him with the words,<br>   "The east, my lord, is bright.<br> A crowded court your presence seeks;<br>   Get up, and hail the light."<br> ’Twas not the dawning light which shone,<br> But that which by the moon was thrown.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>He sleeping still, once more she says,<br>   "The flies are buzzing loud.<br> To lie and dream here by your side<br>   Were pleasant, but the crowd<br> Of officers will soon retire;<br> Draw not on you and me their ire!"</p></div>
