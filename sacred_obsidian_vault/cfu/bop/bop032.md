 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Jih Yüeh;</i> narrative. Chuang Chiang complains of, and appeals against, the bad treatment which she received from her husband.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>  O sun so bright, O moon so fair,<br>     This lower earth that light,<br>   Behold this man, so bold to dare<br>     Transgress the ancient right.<br> How shall he fix his restless mind?<br> Would he not then to me be kind? <a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="green">p. 29</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  O sun, O moon, whose shining vault<br>     O’erspreads this earth below,<br>   Behold this man, with willful fault,<br>     Kindness refuse to show.<br> His restless mind how shall he turn?<br> He could not then my fondness spurn.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>  O sun, O moon, in upper sphere,<br>     That from the east come forth,<br>   This man speaks phrases sounding fair,<br>     But all of little worth.<br> Were but his mind to goodness set,<br> He could not me so much forget.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>  O sun so bright, O moon so fair,<br>     That from the east forth come;<br>   O parents dear, whose tender care<br>     Ne’er comes in this new home;<br> If fixed his mind, ’gainst reason sage<br> He could not thus my heart outrage.</p></div>
