 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>T‘ien Pao;</i> narrative. An ode responsive to any of the five preceding. His officers and guests, feasted by the king, celebrate his praises, and desire for him the blessing of Heaven and of his ancestors.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Heaven shields and sets thee fast.<br> It round thee fair has cast <a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="green">p. 193</font></a><br>     Thy virtue pure.<br> Thus richest joy is thine;—<br> Increase of corn and wine,<br> And every gift divine,<br>     Abundant, sure.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Heaven shields and sets thee fast.<br> From it thou goodness hast;<br>     Right are thy ways.<br> Its choicest gifts ’twill pour,<br> That last forevermore,<br> Nor time exhaust the store<br>     Through endless days.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Heaven shields and sets thee fast,<br> Makes thine endeavor last,<br>     And prosper well.<br> Like hills and mountains high,<br> Whose masses touch the sky;<br> Like stream aye surging by;<br>     Thine increase swell! <a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="green">p. 194</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>With rite and auspice fair,<br> Thine offerings thou dost bear,<br>     And sonlike give,<br> The seasons round from spring,<br> To olden duke and king,<br> Whose words to thee we bring:—<br>     "Forever live."</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>The spirits of thy dead<br> Pour blessings on thy head,<br>     Unnumbered, sweet.<br> Thy subjects, simple, good,<br> Enjoy their drink and food.<br> Our tribes of every blood<br>     Follow thy feet.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>Like moons that wax in light;<br> Or suns that scale the height;<br>     Or ageless hill;<br> Nor change, nor autumn know;<br> As pine and cypress grow;<br> The sons that from thee flow<br>     Be lasting still!</p></div>
