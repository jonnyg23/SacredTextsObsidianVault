 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘ên Fêng;</i> allusive. A wife tells her grief because of the absence of her husband, and his forgetfulness of her.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The falcon swiftly seeks the north,<br> And forest gloom that sent it forth.<br> Since I no more my husband see,<br> My heart from grief is never free.<br> O how is it, I long to know,<br> That he, my lord, forgets me so? <a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="green">p. 148</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Bushy oaks on the mountain grow,<br> And six elms where the ground is low.<br> But I, my husband seen no more,<br> My sad and joyless fate deplore.<br> O how is it, I long to know,<br> That he, my lord, forgets me so?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The hills the bushy wild plums show,<br> And pear trees grace the ground below.<br> But, with my husband from me gone,<br> As drunk with grief, I dwell alone.<br> O how is it, I long to know,<br> That he, my lord, forgets me so?</p></div>
