 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chiao Liao;</i> allusive and metaphorical. Supposed to celebrate the power and prosperity of Huanshu, and to predict the growth of his family.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>                What clusters so rare<br>                 The pepper plants bear!<br> A measure, luxuriant and large, they would fill.<br>                 As much without peer<br>                 Is this hero here!<br> Sing hey for the shoots of the pepper plant still!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>                What clusters so rare<br>                 The pepper plants bear!<br> Luxuriant and large, both your hands they would fill.<br>                 So generous and great<br>                 This chief of our state!<br> And it's hey for the far-shooting pepper plant still!</p></div>
