 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yeh Yu Man Ts‘ao;</i> narrative and allusive. A lady rejoices in an unlawful connection which she had formed.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>On the moor, where thickly grew<br> Creeping grass, bent down with dew,<br> There a handsome man drew nigh,<br> ’Neath whose forehead, broad and high,<br> Gleamed his clear and piercing eye.<br> ’Twas by accident we met;<br> Glad was I my wish to get. <a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="green">p. 103</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Where the grass creeps o’er the moor,<br> With the dew all covered o’er,<br> There the finest man found I,<br> ’Bove whose clear and piercing eye,<br> Rose his forehead, broad and high.<br> Chance gave us a meeting rare,<br> And we both were happy there.</p></div>
