 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Wu Yang;</i> narrative. Supposed to celebrate the largeness and condition of King Hsüan's flocks and herds; with an auspice of the prosperity of the kingdom.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>  Who dares to say your sheep are few?<br>     The flocks are all three hundred strong.<br>   Who dares despise your cattle too?<br>     <i>There</i> ninety, black-lipped, press along. <a name="page_234"><font size="1" color="green">p. 234</font></a><br> Though horned the sheep, yet peaceful each appears;<br> The cattle come, with moist and flapping ears.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  These climb the heights, those drink the pool;<br>     Some lie at rest, while others roam.<br>   With raincoats, and thin splint hats cool,<br>     And bearing food, your herdsmen come.<br> In thirties, ranged by hues, the creatures stand;<br> Fit victims they will yield at your command.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>  Your herdsmen twigs and fagots bring,<br>     With prey of birds and beasts for food.<br>   Your sheep, untouched by evil thing,<br>     Approach, their health and vigor good.<br> The herdsman's waving hand they all behold,<br> And docile come, and pass into the fold.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>  Your herdsmen dream;—fish take the place<br>     Of men; on banners falcons fly, <a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="green">p. 235</font></a><br>   Displacing snakes and tortoises.<br>     The augur tells his prophecy:—<br> "The first betoken plenteous years; the change<br> Of banners shows of homes a widening range."</p></div>
