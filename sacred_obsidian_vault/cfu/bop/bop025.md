 <p>XI</p>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chiang Yu Ssŭ;</i> allusive. Jealousy cured; the restoration of good feeling in a harem.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>See how the Chiang's great branches flow,<br> Here leave its stream, and there back go!<br> When first our lady came as bride,<br> She would not have us by her side.<br> She would not have us by her side;<br> But soon a better course she tried. <a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="green">p. 21</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The islets part the Chiang's broad course,<br> Which soon resumes its wonted force.<br> When first as bride our lady came,<br> To be with us she thought it shame.<br> To be with us she thought it shame;<br> Erelong she knew she was to blame.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The T‘o streams, from the river led,<br> Flow devious, and rejoin its bed.<br> When first we saw our lady here,<br> She would not deign us to come near.<br> She would not deign us to come near;<br> But to a song she changed her sneer.</p></div>
