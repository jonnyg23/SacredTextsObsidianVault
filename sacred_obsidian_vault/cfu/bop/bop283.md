 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chih Ching;</i> narrative. An ode appropriate in sacrificing to the kings Wu, Ch‘êng, and K‘ang.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">The arm of Wu was full of might;<br>   None could his fire withstand; <a name="page_440"><font size="1" color="green">p. 440</font></a><br> And Ch‘êng and K‘ang stood forth to sight,<br>   As kinged by God's own hand.<br> We err not when we call then sage.<br>   How grandly they maintained<br> Their hold of all the heritage<br>   That Wên and Wu had gained!<br> As here we worship, they descend,<br>   While bells and drums resound,<br> And stones and lutes their music blend.<br>   With blessings we are crowned.<br> The rites correctly we discharge;<br>   The feast we freely share.<br> Those sires Chou's glory will enlarge,<br>   And ever for it care.</div>
