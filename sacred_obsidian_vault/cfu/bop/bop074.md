 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ko Lei;</i> allusive. A wanderer from Chou, separated from his kin, mourns over his lot.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Around the creepers thickly spread,<br>   On the borders of the Ho.<br> My native soil no more I tread;<br>   Into exile forth I go.<br>     Far removed from kindred all,<br>     Father I a stranger call.<br>     Though so called, he does not brook,<br>     Kindly upon me to look.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Around the creepers thickly spread,<br>   On the green banks of the Ho. <a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="green">p. 82</font></a><br> My native soil no more I tread;<br>   Into exile forth I go.<br>     Far removed from kindred all,<br>     Mother I a stranger call.<br>     Though so called, she does not deign<br>     Me as child to entertain.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Around the creepers thickly spread,<br>   On the bank lips of the Ho.<br> My native soil no more I tread;<br>   Into exile forth I go.<br>     Far from all who bear my name,<br>     Elder brother I would claim<br>     In a stranger, but he spurns<br>     Such a claim, and from me turns.</p></div>
