 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="green">p. 273</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book VI. The Decade of Pei Shan</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Pei Shan;</i> narrative. An officer complains of the arduous and continual duties unequally imposed upon him, and keeping him away from his parents, while others were left to enjoy their ease.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>I climb that hill upon the north,<br>   And gather medlars on its side.<br> Active and vigorous, I go forth,<br>   And morn and night I walk or ride.<br> I serve the king with eager will;<br> But great the grief my parents feel!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Where’er their arch the heavens expand,<br>   The king can claim the land below.<br> Within the seabounds of the land,<br>   All at his summons come or go.<br> His ministers unfairly act;<br> They praise me, but with toils distract. <a name="page_274"><font size="1" color="green">p. 274</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Four ceaseless steeds my care engage;<br>   The king's affairs no rest allow.<br> They say I bear no trace of age,<br>   While few, they think, such vigor show.<br> While my backbone remains unbent,<br> In work my life must still be spent.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Some rest in careless ease, supine;<br>   Some for the state themselves wear out.<br> On softest couches some recline;<br>   Others, unhalting, march about.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>Some never hear a clamorous sound;<br>   Others toil on ’midst rude alarms.<br> Some idle on their backs are found;<br>   And some bear loads with head and arms.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>Some feast, and fearless seek new joys;<br>   Some live in constant dread of blame.<br> Some the harsh critic's work employs;<br>   Others their numerous duties claim.</p></div>
