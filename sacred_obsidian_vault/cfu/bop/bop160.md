 <p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="green">p. 178</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>P‘o Fu;</i> narrative. Responsive to the last ode. His soldiers praise the duke of Chou for his magnanimity and sympathy with the people.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>  We splintered our axes, and brought<br>   Our hatchets all to the same plight.<br> But the duke of Chou meant, when eastward he went,<br>   What was wrong in those, four 'states to right.<br>     Oh! the pity was great<br>     Which he felt for their state!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  Our axes and chisels we broke<br>   To pieces, and splintered and rent.<br> But the duke of Chou meant, when eastward he went,<br>   The four states all reformed to present.<br>     Oh! the pity was good<br>     That on them he bestowed!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>  Our axes we broke, and our clubs<br>   To fragments were splintered and split. <a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="green">p. 179</font></a><br> But the duke of Chou meant, when eastward he went,<br>   The four states in close union to knit.<br>     Oh! the pity was rare<br>     That he showed for them there!</p></div>
