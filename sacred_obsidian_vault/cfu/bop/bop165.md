 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="green">p. 184</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ssŭ Mu;</i> narrative and allusive. A festal ode, complimentary to an officer on his return from an expedition, celebrating the union in him of loyal duty and filial feeling.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>On dashed my four steeds, without halt, without stay,<br> Though toilsome and winding from Chou was the way.<br> I wished to return,—but the monarch's command<br> Forbade that his business be done with slack hand;<br> And my heart was with sadness oppressed.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>On dashed my four steeds; I ne’er slackened the reins.<br> They snorted and panted,—all white, with black manes.<br> I wished to return, but our sovereign's command<br> Forbade that his business be done with slack hand;<br> And I dared not to pause or to rest.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Unresting the filial doves speed in their flight,<br> Ascending, then sweeping swift down from the height,<br> Now grouped on the oaks. The king's high command<br> Forbade that his business be done with slack hand;<br> And my father I left, sore distressed. <a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="green">p. 185</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Unresting the filial doves speed in their flight,<br> Now fanning the air, and anon they alight<br> On the medlars thick grouped. But our monarch's command<br> Forbade that his business be done with slack hand;—<br> Of my mother I thought with sad breast.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>My four steeds I harnessed, all white and black-maned,<br> Which straight on their way, fleet and emulous, strained.<br> I wished to return; and now venture in song<br> The wish to express, and announce how I long<br> For my mother my care to attest.</p></div>
