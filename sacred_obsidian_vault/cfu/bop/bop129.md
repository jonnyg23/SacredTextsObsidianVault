 <p><a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="green">p. 139</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book XI. The Odes of Ch‘in</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘ê Lin;</i> narrative and allusive. Celebrating the growing opulence and style of some lord of Ch‘in, and the pleasures and freedom of his court.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>His many chariots rush along,<br> Drawn by white-fronted steeds and strong.<br> When audience now we wish to gain,<br> His eunuchs’ aid we must obtain.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The varnish trees on hillsides grow,<br> And chestnuts on the lands below.<br> When access to the prince we've found,<br> We sit and hear the lutes’ sweet sound.<br> If we seize not this joy to-day,<br> Old age will have us for its prey.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The mulberries on the hillsides grow,<br> And willows where the grounds are low.<br> When to the prince our way we've made<br> We sit and hear the organs played.<br> If we pass by this joy to-day,<br> Old age will bear us all away.</p></div>
