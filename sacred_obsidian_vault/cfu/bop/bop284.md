 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ssŭ Wên;</i> narrative. Celebrating Hou-chi;—an ode appropriate to the border sacrifice, when Hou-chi was worshiped as the correlate of God.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">O thou, accomplished, great Hou-chi,<br>   To thee alone ’twas given<br> To be, by what we trace to thee,<br>   The correlate of Heaven.<br> On all who dwell within our land<br>   Grain food didst thou bestow. <a name="page_441"><font size="1" color="green">p. 441</font></a><br> ’Tis to thy wonder-working hand<br>   This gracious boon we owe.<br> God had the wheat and barley meant<br>   To nourish all mankind.<br> None would have fathomed His intent,<br>   But for thy guiding mind.<br> Man's social duties thou didst show<br>   To every tribe and state.<br> From thee the polished manners flow<br>   That stamp our land "The Great."</div>
