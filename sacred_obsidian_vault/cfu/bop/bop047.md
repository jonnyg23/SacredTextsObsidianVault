 <p><a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="green">p. 47</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">XIX</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The Erh Tsŭ Ch‘êng Chou; narrative. Surmises as to the death of two sons of Duke Hsüan.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The two youths went into their boats,<br> Whose shadow on the water floats.<br>     What evil to them came?<br> Anxious and wondering, long we muse;<br> Our hearts are tossed with tossing views.<br>     Some one must be to blame.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Into their boats the two youths passed,<br> And on the stream were carried fast.<br>     What was there to alarm?<br> With longing thought we fain would trace,<br> The secret of their ill-starred race.<br>     Did they not come to harm?</p></div>
