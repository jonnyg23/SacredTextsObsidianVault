 <p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="green">p. 151</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book XII. The Odes of Ch‘ên</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yüan Ch‘iu;</i> narrative. The dissipation and pleasure seeking of the officers of Ch‘ên.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>How gay and volatile you are,<br>   When upon Yüan Ch‘iu’s top you stand!<br> Kindly you are indeed, but want<br>   All that would men's respect command.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>How at the foot of Yüan Ch‘iu’s height<br>   Your blows upon the drum resound!<br> In winter and in summer, there<br>   With egret's plume in hand you're found.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>To Yüan Ch‘iu as you move along,<br>   You beat your sounding earthenware.<br> In winter and in summer both,<br>   Your fan of egret's plumes you bear.</p></div>
