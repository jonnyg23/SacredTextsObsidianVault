 <p><a name="page_316"><font size="1" color="green">p. 316</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book VIII. The Decade of Tu Jên Shih</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Tu Jên Shih;</i> narrative. Praise of the ladies and gentlemen of a former time for the simplicity of their dress, the correctness of their deportment, and the elegance of their manners.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>In the old capital they stood,<br>   With yellow fox furs plain,<br> Their manners all correct and good,<br>   Speech free from vulgar stain.<br> Could we go back to Chow's old days,<br>   All would look up to them with praise.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>In the old capital they wore<br>   <i>T‘ai</i> hats and black caps small;<br> And ladies, who famed surnames bore,<br>   Their own thick hair let fall.<br> Such simple ways are seen no more,<br> And the changed manners I deplore. <a name="page_317"><font size="1" color="green">p. 317</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Ear stoppers, made of sew stones fine,<br>   In the old days were worn.<br> Each lady of a noble line<br>   A Yin or Chi seemed born.<br> Such officers and ladies now<br> I see not, and my sorrows grow.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>With graceful sweep their girdles fell,<br>   Then in the days of old.<br> The ladies’ side hair, with a swell,<br>   Like scorpion's tail, rose bold.<br> Such, if I saw them in these days,<br> I'd follow with admiring gaze.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>So hung their girdles, not for show;—<br>   To their own length ’twas due.<br> ’Twas not by art the hair curled so;—<br>   By nature so it grew.<br> I seek such manners now in vain,<br> And pine for them with longing pain.</p></div>
