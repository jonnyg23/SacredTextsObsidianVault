 <p><a name="page_1"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">THE BOOK OF POETRY</h1>  <h2 align="center">PART I</h2>  <h2 align="center">LESSONS FROM THE STATES</h2>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book I. The Odes of Chou and the South</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Kuan Chü;</i> mainly allusive. Celebrating the virtue of the bride of King Wên, his quest for her, and welcoming her to his palace.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Hark! from the islet in the stream the voice<br> Of the fish hawks that o’er their nest rejoice!<br> From them our thoughts to that young lady go,<br> Modest and virtuous, loth herself to show.<br> Where could be found, to share our prince's state<br> So fair, so virtuous, and so fit a mate?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>See how the duckweed's stalks, or short or long,<br> Sway left and right, as moves the current strong!<br> So hard it was for him the maid to find!<br> By day, by night, our prince with constant mind <a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="green">p. 2</font></a><br> Sought for her long, but all his search was vain.<br> Awake, asleep, he ever felt the pain<br> Of longing thought, as when on restless bed,<br> Tossing about, one turns his fevered head.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Here long, there short, afloat the duckweed lies;<br> But caught at last, we seize the longed-for prize.<br> The maiden modest, virtuous, coy, is found;<br> Strike every lute, and joyous welcome sound.<br> Ours now, the duckweed from the stream we bear,<br> And cook to use with other viands rare.<br> He has the maiden, modest, virtuous, bright;<br> Let bells and drums proclaim our great delight.</p></div>
