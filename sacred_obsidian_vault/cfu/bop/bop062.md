 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="green">p. 69</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">V</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chu Kan;</i> narrative. A daughter of the house of Wei, married in another state, expresses her longing to revisit Wei.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>With long and tapering rods,<br>   You angle in the Ch‘i.<br> I think of you, dear friends,<br>   Here far removed from Wei.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Ch‘üan-yüan upon the left,<br>   Ch‘i on the right I view.<br> But married far away,<br>   To home I bade adieu.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Those streams, this on the right,<br>   That on the left, appear.<br> The laugh that shows your teeth,<br>   Your tinkling gems, I hear.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>I watch the cedar oars<br>   On Ch‘i, and boats of pine.<br> O might I travel there,<br>   And soothe this heart of mine!</p></div>
