 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Shih Yu Ch‘ang Ts‘oo;</i> narrative. Some one, groaning under the oppression of the government, wishes he were an unconscious tree.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Where the grounds are wet and low,<br> There the trees of goat-peach grow,<br> With their branches small and smooth,<br> Glossy in their tender youth.<br> Joy it were to me, O tree,<br> Consciousness to want like thee.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Where the grounds are wet and low,<br> There the trees of goat-peach grow.<br> Soft and fragrant are their flowers,<br> Glossy from the vernal showers.<br> Joy it were to me, O tree,<br> Ties of home to want like thee. <a name="page_162"><font size="1" color="green">p. 162</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Where the grounds are wet and low,<br> There the trees of goat-peach grow.<br> What delicious fruits they bear,<br> Glossy, soft, of beauty rare!<br> Joy it were to me, O tree,<br> Household cares to want like thee.?</p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">
