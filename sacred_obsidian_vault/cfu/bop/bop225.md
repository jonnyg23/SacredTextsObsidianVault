 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yüan Yang;</i> allusive. Responsive to the last ode;—the princes express their prayers and wishes for the king.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The Yellow ducks, full grown, take wing and fly;<br> For them the men both hand and spread nets ply;—<br>   So greatly they their beauty prize. <a name="page_297"><font size="1" color="green">p. 297</font></a><br> May the king's life ten thousand years extend,<br> While wealth and happiness that know no end<br>   Heaven, as deserved, to him supplies!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The yellow ducks upon the dam oft rest,<br> Each with its left wing ’gainst its neighbor's pressed;—<br>   Their mutual fondness thus they show.<br> Heaven to the king ten thousand years assign!<br> And blessings lasting in unbroken line<br>   Upon his merit great bestow!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The stables large the teams of steeds contain;—<br> In peace with forage, and in war with grain,<br>   Abundantly they are supplied.<br> May the king's life extend ten thousand years,<br> While all that lengthened time no sign appears<br>   Of wealth or happiness denied!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>The stables large contain the numerous teams;—<br> Forage they get in peace; in war there gleams<br>   In every manger store of grain.<br> Ten thousand years may our great sovereign live,<br> And Heaven the wealth and blessing ever give,<br>   Which shall his comforting maintain!</p></div>
