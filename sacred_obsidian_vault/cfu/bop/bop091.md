 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XIV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Fêng;</i> narrative. A woman regrets lost opportunities, and would welcome a fresh suitor.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Handsome the suitor was and stout,<br> Who for me in the lane looked out:<br> I should have gone with him I doubt. <a name="page_98"><font size="1" color="green">p. 98</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Can I that suitor's form forget,<br> Who for me in the hall did wait?<br> That I held off I now regret.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>I'm here, my broidered upper robe<br>   Concealed beneath a garment plain.<br> As lovely is my lower robe,<br>   With the same guard ’gainst travel stain.<br> O sir, O sir, come and me hence convey;<br> Your waiting chariot I shall not delay!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>I'm here, my broidered lower robe<br>   Concealed beneath a garment plain.<br> As lovely is my upper robe,<br>   With the same guard ’gainst travel stain.<br> O sir, O sir, if you would only come,<br> At once your chariot should convey me home!</p></div>
