 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ts‘ai Ling;</i> metaphorical. Against giving ear to slanderers.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>When told to Shou-yang's top to go,<br>   The <i>ling</i> plants there to take,<br> The speaker false at once you'd know,<br>   Nor heed the words he spake. <a name="page_138"><font size="1" color="green">p. 138</font></a><br> And so, when men their stories feign,<br>   To credit them be slow.<br> Put them aside, put them aside;<br>   Belief should slowly grow.<br> ’Tis thus the stories told by men<br>   Subside, nor farther go.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>When told to search round Shou-yang's base,<br>   Sow thistles there to find,<br> The search you'd think a hopeless case,<br>   Nor would the counsel mind.<br> And so, when men their stories feign,<br>   Do not approve in haste.<br> Put them aside, put them aside;<br>   Assent should be repressed.<br> ’Tis thus the stories told by men<br>   Soon unregarded rest.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>When told on th’ east of Shou-yang hill<br>   For mustard plants to try,<br> You'd know the quest would speed but ill,<br>   And let the words pass by.<br> And so, when men their stories feign,<br>   No hearing to them lend.<br>   Put them aside, put them aside,<br> Your faith loth to extend.<br> ’Tis thus the stories told by men<br>   Come shortly to an end.</p></div>
