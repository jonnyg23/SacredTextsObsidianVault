 <p><a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="green">p. 18</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yin Ch‘i Lei;</i> allusive. A lady's admiration of her husband absent on public service, and her longing for his return.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Grand in the south the thunder rolls,<br>   Beyond that lofty hill.<br> Why must he go, nor dare to stay<br>   Brief space at rest and still?<br> Absent my noble lord I mourn,<br> May he return! May he return!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Grand in the south the thunder rolls<br>   Along that mountain's side.<br> Why must he go from this, nor dare<br>   Brief time at rest to bide?<br> Absent my noble lord I mourn,<br> May he return! may he return!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Grand in the south the thunder rolls,<br>   Around that mountain's base.<br> Why must he go from this, nor dare<br>   Indulge himself a space?<br> Absent my noble lord I mourn,<br> May he return! may he return!</p></div>
