 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">X</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘iu Chung Yu Ma;</i> narrative. A woman longs for the presence of her lovers, who, she thinks, are detained from her by another woman.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Where the hemp grows on the mound,<br>   There some one keeps Tsŭ-chieh;<br>   There some one keeps Tsŭ-chieh:—<br> Why comes not <i>he</i> to <i>me</i> with a bound?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>On the mound where grows the wheat,<br>   There some one keeps Tzŭ-kuo;<br>   There some one keeps Tzŭ-kuo:—<br> Why comes not <i>he</i> with <i>me</i> here to eat?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>On the mound plum trees have place.<br>   There some one keeps those youths;<br>   There some one keeps those youths:—<br> O for their <i>chiu</i>-stones girdle to grace!</p></div>
