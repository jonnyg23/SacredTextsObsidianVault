 <p><a name="page_158"><font size="1" color="green">p. 158</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">X</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Tsê P‘o;</i> allusive. A gentleman's admiration of and longing for a certain lady.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>There where its shores the marsh surround,<br> Rushes and lotus plants abound.<br> Their loveliness brings to my mind<br> The lovelier one that I would find.<br> In vain I try to ease the smart<br> Of wounded love that wrings my heart.<br> In waking thought and likely dreams,<br> From every pore the water streams.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>All round the marsh's shores are seen<br> Valerian flowers and rushes green.<br> But lovelier is that Beauty rare,<br> Handsome and large, and tall, and fair.<br> I wish and long to call her mine,<br> Doomed with the longing still to pine.<br> Nor day nor night e’er brings relief;<br> My inmost heart is full of grief.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Around the marsh, in rich display,<br> Grow rush and lotus flowers, all gay. <a name="page_159"><font size="1" color="green">p. 159</font></a><br> But not with her do they compare,<br> So tall and large, majestic, fair.<br> Both day and night, I nothing speed;<br> Still clings to me the aching need.<br> On side, on back, on face, I lie,<br> But vain each change of posture try.</p></div>
