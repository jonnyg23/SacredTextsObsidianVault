 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Huang Niao;</i> allusive. Lament for three worthy brothers of Ch‘in who were buried alive in the same grave with Duke Mu.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>They flit about, the yellow birds,<br>   And rest upon the jujubes find.<br> Who buried were in Duke Mu's grave,<br>   Alive to awful death consigned?  
 <p>’Mong brothers three, who met that fate,<br>   ’Twas sad the first, Yen-hsi, to see.<br> He stood alone; a hundred men<br>   Could show no other such as he. <a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="green">p. 146</font></a><br> When to the yawning grave he came,<br>   Terror unnerved and shook his frame.</p>  
 <p>Why thus destroy our noblest men,<br>   To thee we cry, O azure Heaven!<br> To save Yen-hsi from death, we would<br>   A hundred lives have freely given.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>They flit about, the yellow birds,<br>   And on the mulberry trees rest find.<br> Who buried were in Duke Mu's grave,<br>   Alive to awful death consigned?</p>  
 <p>’Mong brothers three, who met that fate,<br>   ’Twas sad the next, Chung-hang, to see.<br> When on him pressed a hundred men,<br>   A match for all of them was he.<br> When to the yawning grave he came,<br>   Terror unnerved and shook his frame.</p>  
 <p>Why thus destroy our noblest men,<br>   To thee we cry, O azure Heaven!<br> To save Chung-hang from death, we would<br>   A hundred lives have freely given. <a name="page_147"><font size="1" color="green">p. 147</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>They flit about, the yellow birds,<br>   And rest upon the thorn trees find.<br> Who buried were in Duke Mu's grave,<br>   Alive to awful death consigned?</p>  
 <p>’Mong brothers three, who met that fate,<br>   ’Twas sad the third, Chên-hu, to see.<br> A hundred men in desperate fight<br>   Successfully withstand could he.<br> When to the yawning grave he came,<br>   Terror unnerved and shook his frame.</p>  
 <p>Why thus destroy our noblest men,<br>   To thee we cry, O azure Heaven!<br> To save Chên-hu from death, we would<br>   A hundred lives have freely given.</p></div>
