 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="green">p. 13</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ts‘ao Ch‘ung;</i> narrative. The wife of some great officer bewails his absence on duty, and longs for the joy of his return.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Shrill chirp the insects in the grass;<br>   All about the hoppers spring.<br> While I my husband do not see,<br>   Sorrow must my bosom wring.<br>     O to meet him!<br>     O to greet him!<br> Then my heart would rest and sing.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Ascending high that southern hill,<br>   Turtle ferns I strove to get.<br> While I my husband do not see,<br>   Sorrow must my heart beset.<br>     O to meet him!<br>     O to greet him!<br> Then my heart would cease to fret.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Ascending high that southern hill,<br>   Spinous ferns I sought to find.<br> While I my husband do not see,<br>   Rankles sorrow in my mind.<br>     O to meet him!<br>     O to greet him!<br> Then my heart would peace be shrined.</p></div>
