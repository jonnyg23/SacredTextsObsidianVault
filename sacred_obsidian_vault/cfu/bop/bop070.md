 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">III. Scoticè</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chün Tzŭ Yang Yang;</i> narrative. The husband's satisfaction, and the wife's joy, on his return.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The gudeman’s come hame, an’ his face weers a bloom,<br>   His organ o’ reeds he hads in his left han’;<br> An’ his richt han’ ca’s me to come till his room:—<br>   It’s siccan a joy; it’s mair nor I can stan’.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The gudeman’s come hame, an’ he’s pleesed I’ll engage,<br>   His gran’ fether screen he hads in his left han’;<br> An’ his richt han’ ca’s me to come till the stage:—<br>   It’s siccan a joy; it’s mair nor I can stan’.</p></div>
