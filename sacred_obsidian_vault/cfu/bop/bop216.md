 <p><a name="page_276"><font size="1" color="green">p. 276</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hsiao Ming;</i> narrative. An officer, kept long abroad on distant service, deplores the hardships of his lot, and tenders good advice to his more fortunate friends at court.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>O Heaven above, before whose light<br> Revealed is every deed and thought,<br>            To thee I cry.<br> Hither on toilsome service brought,<br> In this wild Chiao Yeh watch time's flight,<br>            And sadly sigh.<br> The second month had just begun,<br> When from the east we took our way.<br>            Through summer hot<br> We passed, and many a wintry day.<br> Summer again its course has run.<br>            O bitter lot!<br> There are my compeers, gay at court,<br> While here the tears my face begrime.<br>            I'd fain return.<br> But there is that dread net for crime!<br> The fear of it the wish cuts short.<br>            In vain I burn! <a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="green">p. 277</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Ere we the royal city left,<br> The sun and moon renewed the year.<br>            We marched in hope.<br> Now to its close this year is near.<br> Return deferred, of hope bereft,<br>            All mourn and mope.<br> My lonesome state haunts aye my breast,<br> While duties grow, and cares increase,<br>            Too hard to bear.<br> Toils that oppress me never cease;<br> Not for a moment dare I rest,<br>            Nigh to despair.<br> I think with fond regard of those,<br> Who in their posts at court remain,<br>            My friends of old.<br> Fain would I be with them again,<br> But fierce reproof return would cause.<br>            This post I hold.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>When for the west I left my home,<br> The sun and moon both mildly shone,<br>            Our hearts to cheer.<br> We'd soon be back, our service done!<br> Alas! affairs more urgent come,<br>            And fix us here. <a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="green">p. 278</font></a><br> The year is hastening to expire.<br> We gather now the southernwood,<br>            The beans we reap;—<br> That for its fragrance, these for food.<br> Such things that constant care require<br>            Me anxious keep.<br> Thinking of friends still at their posts,<br> I rise and pass the night outside,<br>            So vexed my mind.<br> But soon what changes may betide?<br> I here will stay, whate’er it costs,<br>            And be resigned.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>My honored friends, O do not deem<br> Your rest which seems secure from ill<br>            Will ever last!<br> Your duties quietly fulfill,<br> And hold the upright in esteem,<br>            With friendship fast.<br> So shall the spirits hear your cry,<br> You virtuous make, and good supply,<br>            In measure vast. <a name="page_279"><font size="1" color="green">p. 279</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>My honored friends, O do not deem<br> Repose that seems secure from ill<br>            Will lasting prove.<br> Your duties quietly fulfill.<br> And hold the upright in esteem,<br>            With earnest love.<br> So shall the spirits hear your prayer,<br> And on you happiness confer,<br>            Your hopes above.</p></div>
