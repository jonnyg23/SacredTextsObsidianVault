 <p><a name="page_181"><font size="1" color="green">p. 181</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Lang Po;</i> allusive. The praise of the duke of Chou, more distinguished through his trials.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Dewlap o’ergrown and heavy tail<br>   Th’ impatient wolf impede or trip.<br> But see the duke, humble while tried!<br>   Along his red shoes quiet slip.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Tail heavy and dewlap o’ergrown,<br>   Th’ impatient wolf trip or impede.<br> But see the duke, humble while tried,<br>   His fame unflawed by hasty deed!</p></div>
