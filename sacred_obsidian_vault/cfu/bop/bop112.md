 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yüan Yu T‘ao;</i> allusive. An officer tells his grief because of the misgovernment of the state, and how he was misunderstood.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>A fruit, small as the garden peach,<br>   May still be used for food.<br> A state, though poor as ours, might thrive,<br>   If but its rule were good.<br> Our rule is bad, our state is sad,<br>   With mournful heart I grieve.<br> All can from instrument and voice<br>   My mood of mind perceive.<br> Who know me not, with scornful thought,<br>   Deem me a scholar proud.<br> "Those men are right," they fiercely say,<br>   "What mean your words so loud?" <a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="green">p. 119</font></a><br> Deep in my heart my sorrows lie,<br>   And none the cause may know.<br> How should they know, who never try<br>   To learn whence comes our woe?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The garden jujube, although small,<br>   May still be used for food.<br> A state, though poor as ours, might thrive,<br>   If but its rule were good.<br> Our rule is bad, our state is sad,<br>   With mournful heart I grieve.<br> Methinks I'll wander through the land,<br>   My misery to relieve.<br> Who know me not, with scornful thought,<br>   Deem that wild views I hold.<br> "Those men are right," they fiercely say,<br>   "What mean your words so bold?"<br> Deep in my heart my sorrows lie,<br>   And none the cause may know.<br> How can they know, who never try<br>   To learn whence comes our woe?</p></div>
