 <p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="green">p. 265</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ku Fêng;</i> allusive. Some one complains of the alienation from him of an old friend, produced by the change for the better in the circumstances of the latter.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Gently and soft the east wind blows,<br>   And then there falls the pelting rain.<br> When anxious fears pressed round you close,<br>   Then linked together were we twain.<br> Now happy, and your mind at rest,<br> You turn and cast me from your breast.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Gently and soft the east wind blows,<br>   And then there comes the whirlwind wild.<br> When anxious fears pressed round you close,<br>   Your bosom held me as a child.<br> Now happy, and in peaceful state,<br> You throw me off and quite forget.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Gently and soft the east wind blows,<br>   Then round the rocky height it storms.<br> Each plant its leaves all dying shows;<br>   The trees display their withered forms.<br> My virtues great forgotten all,<br> You keep in mind my faults, though small.</p></div>
