 <p><a name="page_132"><font size="1" color="green">p. 132</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ti Tu;</i> allusive. Lament of an individual bereaved of his brothers and relatives, or forsaken by them.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>A russet pear tree rises all alone,<br> But rich the growth of leaves upon it shown?<br> I walk alone, without one brother left,<br> And thus of natural aid am I bereft.<br> Plenty of people there are all around,<br> But none like my own father's sons are found.<br> Ye travelers, who forever hurry by,<br> Why on me turn the unsympathizing eye?<br> No brother lives with whom my cause to plead;—<br> Why not perform for me the helping deed?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>A russet pear tree rises all alone,<br> But rich with verdant foliage o’ergrown.<br> I walk alone, without one brother's care,<br> To whom I might, amid my straits, repair.<br> Plenty of people there are all around,<br> But none like those of my own name are found.<br> Ye travelers, who forever hurry by,<br> Why on me turn the unsympathizing eye?<br> No brother lives with whom my cause to plead;—<br> Why not perform for me the helping deed?</p></div>
