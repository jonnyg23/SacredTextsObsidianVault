 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ts‘ai Fan;</i> narrative. The industry and reverence of a prince's wife, assisting him in sacrificing.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Around the pools, the islets o’er,<br>   Fast she plucks white southernwood,<br> To help the sacrificial store;<br>   And for our prince does service good.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Where streams among the valleys shine,<br>   Of southernwoods she plucks the white;<br> And brings it to the sacred shrine,<br>   To aid our prince in solemn rite.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>In headdress high, most reverent, she<br>   The temple seeks at early dawn.<br> The service o’er, the headdress see<br>   To her own chamber slow withdrawn.</p></div>
