The Odes of Shao and the South</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘iao Ch‘ao</i>; allusive: Celebrating the marriage of a princess to the prince of another state.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>      In the magpie's nest<br>       Dwells the dove at rest.<br> This young bride goes to her future home;<br> To meet her a hundred chariots come.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>      Of the magpie's nest<br>       Is the dove possest.<br> This bride goes to her new home to live;<br> And escort a hundred chariots give. <a name="page_12"><font size="1" color="green">p. 12</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>      The nest magpie wove<br>       Now filled by the dove.<br> This bride now takes to her home her way;<br> And this numerous cars her state display.</p></div>
