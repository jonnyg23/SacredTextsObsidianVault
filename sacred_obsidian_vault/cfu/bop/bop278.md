 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Lieh Wên;</i> narrative. A song in praise of the princes who have assisted at a sacrifice.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">Ye brilliant and accomplished lords,<br>   Who with your help my worship crown,<br> The favors that your grace affords<br>   My sons in future reigns shall own.<br> Ever in your own states eschew<br>   Alike all greed and wastefulness;<br> So shall the king still honor you;<br>   And when your present services<br> He calls to mind, your sons shall know<br> New honors he will oft bestow.<br> Let each be strong, and play the man;—<br>   So shall your influence be great. <a name="page_436"><font size="1" color="green">p. 436</font></a><br> Ever in virtue lead the van;—<br>   All chiefs your course will imitate.<br> The former kings in mind still bear;<br> What glory can with theirs compare?</div>
