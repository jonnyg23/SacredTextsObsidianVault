 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ssŭ I;</i> narrative. An ode, appropriate to a sacrifice and the feast after it.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">In robes of silk, all bright and clean,<br> And temple cap, with reverent mien, <a name="page_459"><font size="1" color="green">p. 459</font></a><br> The officer walks from the hall<br> Straight to the porch, and looks at all<br> The sheep and oxen doomed to bleed.<br> Back he returns, and gives good heed<br> To tripods and the mighty horn<br> By the rhinoceros once borne.<br> No need for it! The feasters drink<br> The spirits good and mild, but shrink<br> From wanton revelry and pride.<br> By this an auspice is supplied<br> Of the long life which those shall reap<br> Who thus the rules of virtue keep.</div>
