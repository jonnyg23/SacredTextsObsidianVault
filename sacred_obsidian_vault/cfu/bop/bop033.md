 <p><a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="green">p. 30</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">V</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chung Fêng</i>; metaphorical and allusive. Chuang Chiang bemoans the supercilious treatment which she received from her husband.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Fierce is the wind and cold;<br>     And such is he.<br> Smiling he looks, and bold<br>     Speaks mockingly.<br> Scornful and lewd his words,<br>     Haughty his smile.<br> Bound is my heart with cords<br>     In sorrow's coil.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>As cloud of dust wind-blown,<br>     Just such is he.<br> Ready he seems to own,<br>     And come to me.<br> But he comes not nor goes,<br>     Stands in his pride.<br> Long, long, with painful throes,<br>     Grieved I abide.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Strong blew the wind; the cloud<br>     Hastened away.<br> Soon dark again, the shroud<br>     Covers the day.<br> I wake, and sleep no more<br>     Visits my eyes.<br> His course I sad deplore,<br>     With heavy sighs. <a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="green">p. 31</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Cloudy the sky, and dark;<br>     The thunders roll.<br> Such outward signs will mark<br>     My troubled soul.<br> I wake, and sleep no more<br>     Comes to give rest.<br> His course I sad deplore,<br>     In anguished breast.</p></div>
