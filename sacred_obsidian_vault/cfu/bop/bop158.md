 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="green">p. 174</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘ih Hsiao;</i> metaphorical. The duke of Chou, in the person of a bird, whose young ones have been destroyed by an owl, vindicates the decisive course he had taken with rebellion.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Owl, O owl, hear my request,<br> And do not, owl, destroy my nest.<br>   You have taken my young,<br>   Though I over them hung,<br> With the nursing of love and of care.<br> Pity me, pity me! Hear my prayer.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Ere the clouds the sky had obscured,<br> The mulberry roots I secured.<br>   Door and window around,<br>   Them so firmly I bound,<br> That I said, casting downward my eyes,<br> "Dare any of you my house despise?"</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>I tugged with my claws and I tore,<br> And my mouth and my claws were sore.<br>   So the rushes I sought,<br>   And all other things brought;<br> For to perfect the house I was bent,<br> And I grudged no toil with this intent. <a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="green">p. 175</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>My wings are deplorably torn,<br> And my tail is much injured and worn.<br>   Tossed about by the wind,<br>   While the rain beats unkind,<br> Oh! my house is in peril of harm,<br> And this note I scream out in alarm.</p></div>
