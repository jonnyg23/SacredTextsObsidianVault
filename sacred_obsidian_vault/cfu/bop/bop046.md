 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">XVIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hsin T‘ai;</i> allusive and narrative. Satirizing the marriage of Duke Hsüan and Hsüan Chiang.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The New tower, fresh and bright, they show,<br> Where its vast volume rolls the Ho;—<br>     For bride a palace rare.<br> To Wei she came, a mate to find;<br> She sought a husband young and kind,<br>     But found this misshaped bear.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>There stands the New tower grand and high,<br> Where with still stream the Ho flows by;—<br>     For bride a palace rare.<br> To Wei she came, a mate to find;<br> She sought a husband young and kind,<br>     But found this misshaped bear.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>As when the net for fish they set,<br> And lo! a goose ensnared they get,<br>     They stamp with sudden ire;<br> So might she stamp who came to wed<br> The genial son, and in his stead<br>     Got but the humpbacked sire.</p></div>
