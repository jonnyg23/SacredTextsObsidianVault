 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IX</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Han Kuang;</i> allusive and metaphorical. The virtuous manners of the young women about the Han and Kiang rivers.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>High and compressed, the southern trees<br>   No shelter from the sun afford.<br> The girls free ramble by the Han,<br>   But will not hear enticing word. <a name="page_9"><font size="1" color="green">p. 9</font></a><br>     Like the broad Han are they,<br>       Through which one cannot dive;<br>     And like the Kiang's long stream,<br>       Wherewith no raft can strive.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Many the fagots bound and piled;<br>   The thorns I'd hew still more to make.<br> As brides, those girls their new homes seek;<br>   Their colts to feed I'd undertake.<br>     Like the broad Han are they,<br>       Through which one cannot dive;<br>     And like the Kiang's long stream,<br>       Wherewith no raft can strive.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Many the fagots bound and piled;<br>   The southernwood I'd cut for more.<br> As brides, those girls their new homes seek;<br>   Food for their colts I'd bring large store.<br>     Like the broad Han are they,<br>       Through which one cannot dive;<br>     And like the Kiang's long stream,<br>       Wherewith no raft can strive.</p></div>
