 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book IV. The Decade of Ch‘i Fu</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘i Fu;</i> narrative. The soldiers of the royal guard complain of the service imposed on them by the minister of war.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Hear, minister of war, the charge we bring!<br> We are the teeth and talons of the king;<br>   Close to his person is our place.<br> Why have you sent us to this homeless life,<br> Where far from court we roam, ’mid miseries rife?<br>   Why are we doomed to this disgrace? <a name="page_227"><font size="1" color="green">p. 227</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Hear, minister of war, the accusing word!<br> We are the taloned soldiers of our lord,<br>   And near his person should have rest.<br> But you from court have sent us far away,<br> Where ceaselessly we toil from day to day,<br>   By constant misery oppressed.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Hear, minister of war, whose erring deed<br> Has paid our valor with a sorry meed,<br>   When we should near the court reside.<br> Why have you sent us far to suffer grief,<br> And leave our mothers longing for relief,<br>   With all their cooking labors tried?</p></div>
