 <p><a name="page_386"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 386</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">ODE 4, STANZAS 1 AND 2. THE YÎ PHO.</h4>  <h4 align="center">IN PRAISE OF KING WĂN, CELEBRATING HIS INFLUENCE, DIGNITY IN THE TEMPLE SERVICES, ACTIVITY, AND CAPACITY TO RULE.</h4>  
 <p>Abundant is the growth of the buckthorn and shrubby trees, Supplying firewood; yea, stores of it <a name="fr_515"></a><a href="#fn_498"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Elegant and dignified was our prince and king; On the left and the right they hastened to him.</p>  
 <p>Elegant and dignified was our prince and king; On his left and his right they bore their half-mace (libation-cups) <a name="fr_516"></a><a href="#fn_499"><font size="1">2</font></a>:--They bore them with solemn gravity, As beseemed such eminent officers.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_498"></a><a href="sbe03130.htm#fr_515">386:1</a> It is difficult to trace the connexion between-these allusive lines and the rest of the piece.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_499"></a><a href="sbe03130.htm#fr_516">386:2</a> Here we have the lord of <i>K</i>âu in his ancestral temple, assisted by his ministers or great officers in pouring out the libations to the spirits of the departed. The libation-cup was fitted with a handle of jade, that used by the king having a complete kwei, the obelisk-like symbol of rank, while the cups used by a minister had for a handle only half a kwei.</p>
