 <p><a name="page_361"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 361</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">ODE 4, STANZA 1. THE <i>KH</i>IÂO YEN.</h4>  <h4>SOME ONE, SUFFERING FROM THE KING THROUGH SLANDER, APPEALS TO HEAVEN, AND GOES ON TO DWELL ON THE NATURE AND EVIL OF SLANDER.</h4>  
 <p><font size="-1">This piece has been referred to the time of king Lî, B.C. 878 to 828.</font></p>  
 <p>O vast and distant Heaven, Who art called our parent, That, without crime or offence, I should suffer from disorders thus great! The terrors of great Heaven are excessive, But indeed I have committed no crime. (The terrors. of) great Heaven are very excessive, But indeed I have committed no offence.</p>
