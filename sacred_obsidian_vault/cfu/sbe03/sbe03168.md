 <p><a name="page_440"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 440</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">ODE 9, STANZAS 1 AND 3. THE TÂ KÜ.</h4>  <h4>A LADY EXCUSES HERSELF FOR NOT FLYING TO HER LOVER BY HER FEAR OF A SEVERE AND VIRTUOUS MAGISTRATE, AND SWEARS TO HIS THAT SHE IS SINCERE IN HER ATTACHMENT TO HIM.</h4>  
 <p>His great carriage rolls along, And his robes of rank glitter like the young sedge. Do I not think of you? But I am afraid of this officer, and dare not (fly to you).</p>  
 <p>While living we may have to occupy different apartments; But, when dead, we shall share the same grave. If you say that I am not sincere, By the bright sun I swear that I am <a name="fr_636"></a><a href="#fn_616"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>
