   <h5 align="center">ODE 3. THE <i>K</i>ÂU LÛ.</h5>  <h5 align="center">CELEBRATING THE REPRESENTATIVES OF FORMER DYNASTIES, WHO HAD COME TO COURT TO ASSIST AT A SACRIFICE IN THE ANCESTRAL TEMPLE.</h5>  
 <p><font size="-1">This piece may have been used when the king was dismissing his distinguished guests in the ancestral temple. See the introductory note to this Part, pp. <a href="sbe03063.htm#page_300">300</a>, 301.</font></p>  
 <p>A flock of egrets is flying, About the marsh there in the west <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">3</font></a>. My visitors came, With an (elegant) carriage like those birds.</p>  
 <p>There, (in their states), not disliked, Here, (in <i>K</i>âu), never tired of;--They are sure, day and night, To perpetuate their fame.</p>
