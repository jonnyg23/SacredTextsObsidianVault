   <h5 align="center">ODE 10. THE LÂI.</h5>  <h5 align="center">CELEBRATING THE PRAISE OF KING WĂN.</h5>  
 <p><font size="-1">This is the only account of the piece that can be given from itself. The Ȝo <i>K</i>wan, however, refers it to the dance of king Wû; and the Preface says it contains the words with which Wû accompanied his grant of fiefs and appanages in the ancestral temple to his principal followers.</font></p>  
 <p>King Wăn laboured earnestly:--Right is it we should have received (the kingdom). We will diffuse (his virtue), ever cherishing the thought of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_336"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 336</font></a></p>  
 <p>him; Henceforth we will seek only the settlement (of the kingdom). It was he through whom came the appointment of Kâu. Oh! let us ever cherish the thought of him.</p>
