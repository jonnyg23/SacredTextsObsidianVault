   <h5 align="center">ODE 2. THE WEI THIEN <i>K</i>IH MING.</h5>  <h5 align="center">CELEBRATING THE VIRTUE OF KING WĂN AS COMPARABLE TO THAT OF HEAVEN, AND LOOKING TO HIM FOR BLESSING IN THE FUTURE.</h5>  
 <p><font size="-1">According to the Preface, there is an announcement here of the realization of complete peace throughout the kingdom, and some of the old critics refer the ode to a sacrifice to king Wăn by the duke of <i>K</i>âu, when he had completed the statutes for the new dynasty. But there is nothing to authorize a more definite argument of the contents than I have given.</font></p>  
 <p>The ordinances of Heaven,--How deep are they and unintermitting! And oh! how illustrious Was the singleness of the virtue of king Wăn <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_347"><font size="1">3</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>How does he (now) show his kindness? We will receive it, Striving to be in accord with him, our}</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_315"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 315</font></a></p>  
 <p>king Wăn; And may his remotest descendant be abundantly the same!</p>
