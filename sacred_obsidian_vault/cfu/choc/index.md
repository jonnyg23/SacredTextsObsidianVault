 <p>
 This is an extended excerpt from a collection of essays by Paul Carus
 on Chinese topics.
 Carus discusses the I Ching and other methods of divination,
 the five Chinese elements (water, fire, wood, metal and earth);
 the Chinese Zodiac, Fung-shui, the Lo-pan, the Chinese invention
 of the magnetic compass, and the personification of constellations.
 He gives ancient near eastern parallels, and proposes
 a speculative diffusionist thesis for some aspects, particularly
 the calendrical system.
 This essay serves as a good introduction to these topics, with
 numerous illustrations and tables to fill in the details.
 </p>
