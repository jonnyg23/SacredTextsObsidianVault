 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="green">p. 82</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[50] THE GRAND HOUSES AT LO-YANG</h3>  <h4 align="center">(<i>Circa</i> A.D. <i>829</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By</span> woods and water, whose houses are these<br> With high gates and wide-stretching lands?<br> From their blue gables gilded fishes hang;<br> By their red pillars carven coursers run.<br> Their spring arbours, warm with caged mist;<br> Their autumn yards with locked moonlight cold.<br> To the stem of the pine-tree amber beads cling;<br> The bamboo-branches ooze ruby-drops.<br> Of lake and terrace who may the masters be?<br> Staff-officers, Councillors-of-State.<br> All their lives they have never come to see,<br> But know their houses only from the bailiff's map !</p>
