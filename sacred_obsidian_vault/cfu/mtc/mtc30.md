 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[29] STARTING EARLY FROM THE CH‘U–CH‘ĒNG INN</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>815</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Washed</span> by the rain, dust and grime are laid;<br> Skirting the river, the road's course is flat.<br> The moon has risen on the last remnants of night;<br> The travellers' speed profits by the early cold.<br> In the great silence I whisper a faint song;<br> In the black darkness are bred sombre thoughts.<br> On the lotus-banks hovers a dewy breeze;<br> Through the rice-furrows trickles a singing stream.<br> At the noise of our bells a sleeping dog stirs;<br> At the sight of our torches a roosting bird wakes.<br> Dawn glimmers through the shapes of misty trees…<br> For ten miles, till day at last breaks.</p>
