 <p><a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="green">p. 32</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[9] TO TAN CH‘IU</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">My</span> friend is lodging high in the Eastern Range,<br> Dearly loving the beauty of valleys and hills.<br> At green Spring he lies in the empty woods,<br> And is still asleep when the sun shines on high.<br> A pine-tree wind dusts his sleeves and coat;<br> A pebbly stream cleans his heart and ears. <br> I envy you, who far from strife and talk<br> Are high-propped on a pillow of blue cloud.</p>
