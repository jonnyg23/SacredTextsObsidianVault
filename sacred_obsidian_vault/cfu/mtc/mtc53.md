 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="green">p. 84</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[52] ON HIS BALDNESS</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>832</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">At</span> dawn I sighed to see my hairs fall;<br> At dusk I sighed to see my hairs fall.<br> For I dreaded the time when the last lock should go…<br> They are all gone and I do not mind at all!<br> I have done with that cumbrous washing and getting dry;<br> My tiresome comb for ever is laid aside.<br> Best of all, when the weather is hot and wet,<br> To have no top-knot weighing down on one's head!<br> I put aside my dusty conical cap;<br>     And loose my collar-fringe.<br> In a silver jar I have stored a cold stream;<br> On my bald pate I trickle a ladle-full.<br> Like one baptized with the Water of Buddha's Law,<br> I sit and receive this cool, cleansing joy.<br> <i>Now</i> I know why the priest who seeks Repose<br> Frees his heart by first shaving his head.</p>
