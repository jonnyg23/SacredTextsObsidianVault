 <p><a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="green">p. 45</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[19] AT THE END OF SPRING</h3>  <h4 align="center"><i>To Yüan Chēn.</i> <a name="fr_8"></a><a href="#fn_7"><font size="1">1</font></a> (A.D. <i>810</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> flower of the pear-tree gathers and turns to fruit;<br> The swallows' eggs have hatched into young birds.<br> When the Seasons' changes thus confront the mind<br> What comfort can the Doctrine of Tao give?<br> It will teach me to watch the days and months fly<br> Without grieving that Youth slips away;<br> If the Fleeting World is but a long dream,<br> It does not matter whether one is young or old.<br> But ever since the day that my friend left my side<br> And has lived an exile in the City of Chiang-ling,<br> There is one wish I cannot quite destroy: <br> That from time to time we may chance to meet again.</p>
