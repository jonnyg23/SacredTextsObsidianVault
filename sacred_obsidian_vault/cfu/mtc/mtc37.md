 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[36] THE FIFTEENTH VOLUME</h3>  <h4 align="center"><i>(Having completed the fifteenth volume of his works, the poet sends it to his friends Yüan Chēn and Li Chien, with a jesting poem.)</i></h4>  <h4 align="center"><i>(Written in 818)</i></h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">My</span> long poem, the "Eternal Grief," <a name="fr_25"></a><a href="#fn_23"><font size="1">1</font></a> is a beautiful and moving work;<br> My ten "Songs of Shensi" are models of tunefulness.<br> I cannot prevent Old Yüan from stealing my best rhymes;<br> But I earnestly beg Little Li to respect my ballads and songs.<br> While I am alive riches and honour will never fall to my lot;<br> But well I know that after I am dead the fame of my books will live.<br> This random talk and foolish boasting forgive me, for to-day<br> I have added Volume Fifteen to the row that stands to my name.</p>
