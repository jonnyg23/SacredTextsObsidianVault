 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[3-5] DRINKING ALONE BY MOONLIGHT</h3>  <h4 align="center"><i>(Three Poems)</i></h4>  <h4>I</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">A cup</span> of wine, under the flowering trees;<br> I drink alone, for no friend is near.<br> Raising my cup I beckon the bright moon,<br> For he, with my shadow, will make three men.<br> The moon, alas, is no drinker of wine;<br> Listless, my shadow creeps about at my side.<br> Yet with the moon as friend and the shadow as slave<br> I must make merry before the Spring is spent.<br> To the songs I sing the moon flickers her beams;<br> In the dance I weave my shadow tangles and breaks.<br> While we were sober, three shared the fun;<br> Now we are drunk, each goes his way.<br> May we long share our odd, inanimate feast,<br> And meet at last on the Cloudy River of the sky. <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="#fn_3"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  <h4>II</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">In</span> the third month the town of Hsien-yang<br> Is thick-spread with a carpet of fallen flowers. <br> Who in Spring can bear to grieve alone?<br> Who, sober, look on sights like these?<br> Riches and Poverty, long or short life,<br> By the Maker of Things are portioned and disposed;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a></p>  
 <p>But a cup of wine levels life and death<br> And a thousand things obstinately hard to prove.<br> When I am drunk, I lose Heaven and Earth.<br> Motionless—I cleave to my lonely bed.<br> At last I forget that I exist at all,<br> And at <i>that</i> moment my joy is great indeed.</p>  <h4>III</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">If</span> High Heaven had no love for wine,<br> There would not be a Wine Star in the sky.<br> If Earth herself had no love for wine,<br> There would not be a city called Wine Springs. <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Since Heaven and Earth both love wine,<br> I can love wine, without shame before God.<br> Clear wine was once called a Saint; <a name="fr_5"></a><a href="#fn_5"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Thick wine was once called "a Sage." <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_5"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>Of Saint and Sage I have long quaffed deep,<br> What need for me to study spirits and <i>hsien</i><i>?</i> <a name="fr_7"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> At the third cup I penetrate the Great Way;<br> A full gallon—Nature and I are one…<br> But the things I feel when wine possesses my soul<br> I will never tell to those who are not drunk.</p>
