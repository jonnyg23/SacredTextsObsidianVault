 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="green">p. 50</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[22] FISHING IN THE WEI RIVER</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>811</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">In</span> waters still as a burnished mirror's face,<br> In the depths of Wei, carp and grayling swim.<br> Idly I come with my bamboo fishing-rod<br> And hang my hook by the banks of Wei stream.<br> A gentle wind blows on my fishing-gear<br> Softly shaking my ten feet of line.<br> Though my body sits waiting for fish to come,<br> My heart has wandered to the Land of Nothingness. <a name="fr_9"></a><a href="#fn_8"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Long ago a white-headed man <a name="fr_10"></a><a href="#fn_9"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Also fished at the same river's side;<br> A hooker of men, not a hooker of fish,<br> At seventy years, he caught Wēn Wang. <a name="fr_11"></a><a href="#fn_9"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> But <i>I,</i> when I come to cast my hook in the stream,<br> Have no thought either of fish or men.<br> Lacking the skill to capture either prey,<br> I can only bask in the autumn water's light.<br> When I tire of this, my fishing also stops;<br> I go to my home and drink my cup of wine.</p>
