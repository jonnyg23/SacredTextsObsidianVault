 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="green">p. 52</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[24] ILLNESS AND IDLENESS</h3>  <h4 align="center">(<i>Circa</i> A.D. <i>812</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Illness</span> and idleness give me much leisure.<br> What do I do with my leisure, when it comes?<br> I cannot bring myself to discard inkstone and brush;<br> Now and then I make a new poem.<br> When the poem is made, it is slight and flavourless,<br> A thing of derision to almost every one.<br> Superior people will be pained at the flatness of the metre;<br> Common people will hate the plainness of the words.<br> I sing it to myself, then stop and think about it…</p>  
 <p>The Prefects of Soochow and P’ēng-tsē <a name="fr_13"></a><a href="#fn_11"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Would perhaps have praised it, but they died long ago.<br> Who else would care to hear it?<br> No one to-day except Yüan Chēn,<br> And <i>he</i> is banished to the City of Chiang-ling,<br> For three years an usher in the Penal Court.<br> Parted from me by three thousand leagues<br> He will never know even that the poem was made.</p>
