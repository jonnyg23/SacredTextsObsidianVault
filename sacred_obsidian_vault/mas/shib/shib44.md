 <p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="green">p. 144</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SOME WORDS NOT FULLY UNDERSTOOD</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Acre</span>, <i>Ack´-er</i>, A city of Syria of Crusade interest.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Acacia</span>, <i>Acay´-shi-a</i>, An evergreen, thorny plant.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Adonai</span>, <i>Ay-do´-nah</i>, Lord, used where Jehovah occurs.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Agnus Dei</span>, <i>Ag´-nus Dee´-i</i>, Lamb of God.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ahasuerus</span>, <i>Ay-has-yu-ee´-rus</i>, King of Persia.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Almoner</span>, <i>Al´-mo-ner</i>, One who distributes alms.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anno Ordinis</span>, <i>An´-no Or´-din-is</i>, Year of the Order.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Artaxerxes</span>, <i>Ar-tax-erks´-eez</i>, King of Persia.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Barabbas</span>, <i>Bah-rab´-bas</i>, One of the Robbers.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Beauceant</span>, <i>Bo´-see-an</i>, Battle Flag of Templars.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Belshazzar</span>, <i>Bel-shaz´-zar</i>, King of the Chaldees.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Boaz</span>, <i>Boh´-az</i>, A Pillar of the Porch of the Temple.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Chapeau</span>, <i>Shah´-po</i>, A hat.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Clandestine</span>, <i>Klan-des´-tin</i>, Hidden, Fraudulent.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Consummatum Est</span>, <i>Kon-sum-mah´-tum est</i>, It is finished.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Crux Ansata</span>, <i>Kroox An-sah´-ta</i>, Cross with a handle.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Darius</span>, <i>Day-ri´-us</i>, King of Persia.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dieu Le Veut</span>, <i>Dyuh la Voot</i>, God´s will.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Deus Vult</span>, <i>Dee´-us Vult</i>, God´s will.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dimit</span>, <i>Dy-mit´</i>, To let go.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Eccosaise</span>, <i>Ay´-ko-zayze</i>, (Rite) Scotch.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ecbatana</span>, <i>Ec-bat´-ah-nah</i>. Capital cf the Medes.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Elu</span>, <i>Ayl´-yu</i>, Elect.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Emeritus</span>, <i>Ee´-mer-i-tus</i>, Out of active service by honorable discharge.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Emmanuel</span>, <i>Em-man´-u-el</i>, (Immanuel) God with us.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Frater</span>, <i>Fray´-ter</i>, or <i>Frah´-ter</i>, Brother.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Fratres</span>, Brethren.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gavel</span>, <i>Gah´-vel</i>, The mallet of a Presiding Officer.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gethsemane</span>, <i>Geth-sem´-a-nee</i>, Garden where Jesus was arrested.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Golgotha</span>, <i>Gol´-go-thah</i>, A place of a skull.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Heredom</span>, <i>Her´-ee-dom</i>, Holy House.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Houri</span>, <i>Hoo´-ree</i>, Black-eyed nymph.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Impostor</span>, <i>Im-pos´-tor</i>, One who deceives.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Insignia</span>, <i>In-sig´-ni-ya</i>, A badge.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jakin</span>, <i>Jah´-kin</i>, A pillar of the Porch of the Temple. (<i>Yahkin</i>.)</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kabala</span>, <i>Kah´-bah-lah</i>, Jewish Theosophy.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kadosh Kadoshim</span>, <i>Kay-doash´ Kay-doash´-im</i>, Holy of Holies.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Koran</span>, <i>Ko-ran´</i>, Mahometan sacred book.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="green">p. 145</font></a></p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Laus Deo</span>, <i>Laws Dee´-o</i>, Praise God.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Legend</span> <i>Lej´-end</i>, A story handed down from ancient times.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Libanus</span>, <i>Ly-bah´-nus</i>, Lebanon.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lux e Tenebris</span>, <i>Lux ee Ten´-e-bris</i>, Light out of darkness.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Magdalene</span>, <i>Mag-day-lee´-nay</i>, She of New Testament fame.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Magus</span>, <i>May´-jus</i>, Persian Wise Mau.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Magi</span>, <i>May´-ji</i>, Priest of Magian religion.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Magian</span>, <i>May´-ji-an</i>, Believer in religion of the Magi.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz</span>, <i>Mah´-her-shal´-al-hash-baz</i>, Make speed to the spoil, hasten the prey.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Melita</span>, <i>Mel´-i-ta</i>, Malta.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nebuchadnezzar</span>, <i>Neb-uk-ad-nez´-zar</i>, King of the Chaldeans.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nebuzaradan</span>, <i>Neb-yu-zar-ah´-dan</i>, General of Nebuchadnezzar.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ne Varietur</span>, <i>Nee Var-i´-tur</i>, To prevent change.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Omnific</span>, <i>Om-nif´-ik</i>, All creating.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Palestine</span>, <i>Pal´-es-tine</i>, The Holy Land.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pro Deo et Patria</span>, <i>Pro Dee´-o et Pay-tree´-a</i>, For God and my country.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pax Vobiscum</span>, <i>Pax Voh-bis´-kum</i>, Peace be with you.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pentateuch</span>, <i>Pent´-ah-tuke</i>, Five books of Moses.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Persepolis</span>, <i>Per-sep´-o-lis</i>, Chief city of Persia.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Prelate</span>, <i>Prel´-ate</i>, Officer of a Commandery,</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pursuivant</span>, <i>Pur´-swee-vant</i>, A messenger.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pythagoras</span>, <i>Py-thag´-oh-ras</i>, A Philosopher of Greece.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Rose Croix</span>, <i>Roze Krwah</i>, Rose Cross.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Satrap</span>, <i>Sat´-rap</i>, A Persian Governor.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shethar-Boznai</span>, <i>Shee´-thar-boz´-nay-i</i>, Persian nobleman.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shushan</span>, <i>Shoo´-shan</i>, A Persian Capital.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tatnai</span>, <i>Tat´-nay-i</i>, A Persian Satrap, or Governor of a province.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tetragrammaton</span>, <i>Tet-ra gram´ mah-ton</i>, The concealing number Four.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tirshatha</span>, <i>Tur-shah´-thah</i>, Persian Governor of Judea.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Turcopolier</span>, <i>Tur-ko-po´-ly-er</i>, An ancient Officer of Knights of Malta.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Viva Voce</span>, <i>Vy´-vah Voh´-see</i>, By the living voice.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Xerxes</span>, <i>Zerks´-eez</i>, King of Persia.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zeradatha</span>, <i>Zer-e-dah´-thah</i>, Ancient city of Judea.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zerubbabel</span>, <i>Zer-ub´-bah-bel</i>, Judean Prince, and Tirshatha.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zoroaster</span>, <i>Zoh-roh-as´-ter</i>, Founder of Magian religion.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zarathrustra</span>, Persian name of Zoroaster.</p>
