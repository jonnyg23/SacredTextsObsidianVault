 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">THE ANCIENT MOTTO.</h4>  
 <p>The Grand Encampment has adopted this reading of the Motto of the ancient Templars:</p>  
 <p align="center">"<i>Non nobis Domine; non nobis, sed Nomini tuo da gloriam</i>."</p>  
 <p>In the "Biblia Sacra," published in Antwerp in 1605, we find this reading of it:</p>  
 <p align="center">"<i>Non nobis Domine, non nobis: sed Nomini tuo da gloriam</i>."</p>  
 <p>These words are found in the "<i>Biblia Sacra</i>" as part of Psalm cxiii, verse 31. In the Accepted Version they form part of Psalm cxv: 1. It is probable that the omission of the comma after the second <i>nobis</i>, page 79, St. Louis Ritual, was a typographical blunder. As that Ritual no longer exists, the official reading adopted for the Grand Standard remains in force.</p>
