 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Shibboleth: A Templar Monitor</i>, by George Cooper Connor, [1894], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">"IT IS FINISHED."</h4>  
 <p>In its Latin form,"<i>Consummatum est</i>,"this was a cherished phrase of the ancient Templar usage. It is to be regretted that its use appears to be entirely dropped by the Modern Order in the United States.</p>  
 <p>Would it not be a proper complement of the phrase, "<i>In hoc Signo Vinces</i>," and on reversing the position to use "<i>Consummatum est</i>"<i>?</i> </p>
