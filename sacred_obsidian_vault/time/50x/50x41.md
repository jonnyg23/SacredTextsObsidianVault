 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Fifty Christmas Poems for Children</i>, by Florence B. Hyett, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">AN ODE TO THE BIRTH OF OUR SAVIOUR</h3>  
 <p>IN numbers, and but these few,<br> I sing Thy birth, O Jesu!<br> Thou pretty baby, born here,<br> With sup'rabundant scorn here;<br> Who for Thy princely port here,<br> Hadst for Thy place<br> Of birth a base<br> Out-stable for Thy court here.</p>  
 <p>Instead of neat enclosures<br> Of interwoven osiers,<br> Instead of fragrant posies<br> Of daffodils and roses,<br> Thy cradle, Kingly Stranger,<br> As Gospel tells,<br> Was nothing else<br> But here a homely manger.</p>  
 <p>The Jews they did disdain Thee,<br> But we will entertain Thee,<br> With glories to await here,<br> Upon Thy princely state here;<br> And more for love than pity,<br> From year to year,<br> We'll make Thee, here,<br> A free-born of our city.</p>  
 <p>--ROBERT HERRICK</p>
