 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Fifty Christmas Poems for Children</i>, by Florence B. Hyett, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHRISTMAS EVE</h3>  
 <p>IN Christmas Eve the little stars<br> Sparkle and glisten with delight,<br> Like strings of glitt'ring diamonds,<br> Across the darkness of the night.</p>  
 <p>On Christmas Eve the little stars<br> Dance in their places in the sky;<br> Ah! I would go and trip with them<br> If I could only climb as high.</p>  
 <p>On Christmas Eve the little stars<br> Sing merry carols all night long;<br> But O! I am so far away<br> I cannot even hear their song.</p>  
 <p>On Christmas Eve the little stars<br> Sparkle, and dance, and sing till dawn;<br> And I am singing too, because<br> 'Tomorrow will be Christmas Morn.</p>  
 <p>--CHARLOTTE DRUITT COLE</p>
