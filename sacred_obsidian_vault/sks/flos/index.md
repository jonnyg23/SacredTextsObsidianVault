 <p>
 This is a comprehensive studies of the folklore aspects of Shakespeare,
 providing a full-spectrum exposure to the cultural background of
 Elizabethan society.
 The Reverend Dyer, who also wrote 
 <a href="../../wmn/fow/index.htm">Folk-lore of Women</a>, 
 delves into the source of innumerable passages in Shakespeare
 which were mysterious even back in Victorian times.
 Although usually he manages to clear up the mystery, in few instances
 he has to admit defeat.
 </p>
 <p>
 This book is vital if you want to really understand Shakespeare's
 cultural context and times.
 He covers everything from the supernatural (fairies, witches, mermaids)
 to the mundane: games, weddings, dance, punishments, proverbs, animal lore.
 You can read it straight through, but it is also a browser's delight;
 you never know what bit of the bard lodged
 in your mind he will shed light on next.--J.B. Hare, April 16th, 2009.
 </p>
