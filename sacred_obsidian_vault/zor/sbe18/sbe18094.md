 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the eighty-sixth question and reply, that which you ask is thus: How is it good when they give a gift <i>for</i> the ceremonial?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_250"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 250</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that as it is necessary, so that the ceremonial of the sacred beings <i>may</i> be more advanced, and such wealth <i>may</i> more come on to the good work, for the proper stipends of the undertakers <i>and</i> solemnizers--<i>that</i> they <i>may</i> become less lukewarm as regards the accompanying proprieties, and thereby diligent <i>in</i> performing <i>them</i>--and there is not in <i>it</i> <a name="fr_939"></a><a href="#fn_936"><font size="1">1</font></a> an express connection manifested with different work, and with that <i>which has</i> proceeded from so many previous good <i>people</i>, I deem the introduction <i>of it</i> <a name="fr_940"></a><a href="#fn_937"><font size="1">2</font></a> more expressly better.</p>
