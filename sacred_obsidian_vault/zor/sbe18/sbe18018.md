 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER X.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninth question is that which you ask thus: How much does the growth <a name="fr_81"></a><a href="#fn_80"><font size="1">2</font></a> of his good works increase, from the<i></i> time when the good works are done, so long as <i>he</i> is living?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that from the <i>time</i> when a good work comes into progress <i>its</i> growth remains on the increase so long as <i>he</i> is living; moreover,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 30</font></a></p>  
 <p>when he is distressed by that good work <a name="fr_82"></a><a href="#fn_81"><font size="1">1</font></a>, while the increase does not desist <i>from</i> increase, it grows just as a child becomes enlarged in the womb of a mother.</p>
