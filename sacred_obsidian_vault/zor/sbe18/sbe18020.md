 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eleventh question is that you ask thus: Does the growth of a good work eradicate sin just like the original good work, or not?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that the growth does eradicate <i>it</i>, as happens <i>with</i> the good work which is for atonement for sin; it shall be done as retribution for sin, <i>and</i> it eradicates the sin, which is specially mentioned in revelation <a name="fr_86"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 3. 'Then the place of his</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 31</font></a></p>  
 <p>other good work is evidently the soul; and, in order to be with the sin at <i>its</i> origin, it remains <i>and</i> is taken into account.' 4. 'Through good works and the growth of good works is the recompense of the soul, so that they should do those good works in atonement for sin.' 5. And concerning the sin eradicated it is said: 'An original good work eradicates original sin <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_86"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and the growth of a good work <i>eradicates</i> the growth of a sin.'</p>
