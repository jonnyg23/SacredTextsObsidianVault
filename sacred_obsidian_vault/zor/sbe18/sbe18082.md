 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXV.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the seventy-fourth question <i>and</i> reply, that which you ask is thus: Do the angels have <i>his</i> dead <i>body</i> restored, or not?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that <i>there</i> was a high-priest who said that the angels do not have <i>his</i> dead <i>body</i> restored, because of the sin of the mutually-polluting, full of stench, and inglorious victims (khvâpî<i>d</i>ŏân) <a name="fr_824"></a><a href="#fn_821"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the terrible kind of means for the exculpation of creatures <a name="fr_825"></a><a href="#fn_822"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and that practice when males keep specially imperfect in <i>their</i> duty; it <i>being</i> then suitable for mankind to become <i>free</i> from him who--like A<i>z</i>-î Dahâk <a name="fr_826"></a><a href="#fn_823"><font size="1">3</font></a>, who wanted many most powerful demons--resists <i>and</i> struggles, and is not possessing the perception to extract (patka<i>s</i>istanŏ) a pardon, owing to the course of many demoniacal causes. 3. But innumerable multitudes (amarakânîhâ), happily persevering <a name="fr_827"></a><a href="#fn_824"><font size="1">4</font></a> in diligence, have with united observation, unanimously, <i>and</i> with mutual assistance (ham-ban<i>g</i>i<i>s</i>nîhâ) insisted upon this, that they have the dead <i>bodies</i> of all men restored; for</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a></p>  
 <p>the good creator, granting forgiveness <i>and</i> full of goodness, would not abandon any creature to the fiend <a name="fr_828"></a><a href="#fn_825"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 4. In revelation (dînô) it is said that every dead <i>body</i> is raised up, both of the righteous and of the wicked <a name="fr_829"></a><a href="#fn_826"><font size="1">2</font></a>; <i>there</i> is none whom they shall abandon to the fiend.</p>  
 <p>5. And this, also, is thus decided by them <a name="fr_830"></a><a href="#fn_827"><font size="1">3</font></a>, that even <i>as to</i> him <i>who</i> is most grievously sinful, when he becomes mentally <i>seeking</i> pardon <i>and</i> repentant of the sin, <i>and, being</i> as much an atoner as <i>he</i> is well able, <i>has</i> delivered <i>up his</i> body <i>and</i> wealth for retribution <i>and</i> punishment, in reliance upon the atonement for sin of the good religion, then it is possible <i>for</i> his soul, also, to come to the place of the righteous <a name="fr_831"></a><a href="#fn_828"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>
