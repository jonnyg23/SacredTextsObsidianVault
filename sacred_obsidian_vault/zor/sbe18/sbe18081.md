 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 221</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the seventy-third question <i>and</i> reply, that which you ask is thus: Is <i>there any</i> discomfiture (vânî<i>d</i>ârîh) of the archangels from that stench, or not?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that the archangels are immortal and undistressed; their place, also, is in that best existence of light, all-glorious, all-delightful, <i>and</i> undisturbed; and the strength of the stench due to the demons <a name="fr_820"></a><a href="#fn_817"><font size="1">1</font></a> does not reach unto <i>anything</i> pertaining to the archangels. 3. The, archangels are omniscient <a name="fr_821"></a><a href="#fn_818"><font size="1">2</font></a>, friendly to the creatures, persistent, <i>and</i> procure forgiveness; they know that heinous practice which is the heinous practice <a name="fr_822"></a><a href="#fn_819"><font size="1">3</font></a> of that wretched dupe (frîftakŏ) who <i>has</i> become defiled in that most filthy manner (zî<i>s</i>ttûm ârang), which is like that which is provided <i>and</i> which is applied to him even in the terrible punishment <a name="fr_823"></a><a href="#fn_820"><font size="1">4</font></a> that <i>has</i> come upon him from the demons; and then, on account of <i>their</i> friendliness to the creatures, it <i>has</i> </p>
