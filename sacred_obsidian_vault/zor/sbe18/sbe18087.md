 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXX.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the seventy-ninth question <i>and</i> reply, that which you ask is thus: Concerning him who does not order <i>ceremonies</i> what is then the decision?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that, excepting those. among which is specially the selected religious rite (dînô) of him whose ceremony is not performed--who, even though <i>having</i> many good works, does not afterwards attain unto the supreme heaven, which is determined <a name="fr_891"></a><a href="#fn_888"><font size="1">1</font></a>--this, moreover, is thus said, that he who is not able to perform his ritual himself, when he orders a Gêtô-kharî<i>d</i> <a name="fr_892"></a><a href="#fn_889"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>ceremony</i> and they shall perform <i>it</i>, can become <i>fit</i> for the supreme heaven (garô<i>d</i>mânîkŏ); this is greatly to be commended.</p>
