 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the seventy-fifth question <i>and</i> reply, that which you ask is thus: <i>As to</i> him who shall slay those who shall commit unnatural intercourse, how is then his account as to good works and crime?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that the high-priests, in <i>their</i> decision, <i>have</i> thus specially said, that all worthy of death are <i>so</i> by the decision of judges and the</p>  
 <p><a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a></p>  
 <p>command of kings, whose business is execution. 3. Whoever shall slay him who <i>has</i> heinous sins <a name="fr_832"></a><a href="sbe18084.htm#fn_829"><font size="1">1</font></a> after controversies three times with him, about the decision of those acquainted with the religion <i>and about</i> the command of kings, when he <i>has</i> thus remained in the sin in defiance of his own relations--and not inimically to the man <i>and</i> injuriously to the religion, but inimically to the sin <i>and</i> in order to keep away intercourse with demons--is to consider <i>it</i> as a great good work. 4. No command is given about the decision of <i>what one</i> is to do <i>in</i> the same matter, more heedfully and more authorisedly in <i>cases of</i> doubtful attention, for the good work exists undoubtedly more <i>and</i> more abundantly <a name="fr_833"></a><a href="sbe18084.htm#fn_830"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>
