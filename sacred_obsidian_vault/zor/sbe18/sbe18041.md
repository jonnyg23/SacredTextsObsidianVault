 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 76</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the thirty-third question and reply, that which you ask is thus: In what manner is there one way of the righteous from the Dâîtih peak <a name="fr_249"></a><a href="#fn_248"><font size="1">1</font></a> to heaven, and one of the wicked to hell; and <i>what is</i> their nature?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that one is for ascent, and one for descent; and on account of both being of one appearance I write thus much for understanding and full explanation, that is to say:--3. The righteous souls pass over on the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_249"><font size="1">2</font></a> by spiritual flight and the power of good works; and they step forth up to the star, or to the moon, or to the sun station, or to the endless light <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_250"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 4. The soul of the wicked, owing to <i>its</i> falling <i>from</i> the bridge, its lying demon, and the pollution collected by its sin, they shall lead therefrom to the descent into the earth, as both ways <i>lead</i> from that bridge on the Dâîtih peak.</p>
