 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part V: Marvels of Zoroastrianism (SBE47)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1897], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. These were his tokens at birth:—One day, one of those five brothers of the Karaps saw Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and he looked a long while upwards, downwards, <i>and</i> on all sides around <a name="fr_455"></a><a href="#fn_451"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. Pôrûshaspô enquired thus: 'What <i>was</i> there when thou lookedst upwards, what when <i>thou lookedst</i> downwards, <i>and</i> what when thou lookedst on all sides?'</p>  
 <p>3. And he replied thus, namely: 'When I looked upwards, it <i>was</i> for this reason, when I saw that our souls <a name="fr_456"></a><a href="#fn_452"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>that</i> go up to the sky, <i>will</i> go up to the best</p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 148</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>existence</i> <a name="fr_457"></a><a href="sbe4726.htm#fn_453"><font size="1">1</font></a>, owing to the words of this soul. of mankind. 4. When I looked downwards, I saw that, owing to the action of this <i>one</i>, the demon and fiend, the wizard and witch become buried below the earth, <i>and</i> fall paralyzed back to hell. 5. And when I looked on all sides, I saw that the words of this <i>one will</i> extend through the whole earth; and when <i>they have</i> become as the law of the seven regions, each person is kept clothed <i>with</i> a robe (kapâh) of seven skins, in which the glory of the seven archangels <i>has</i> arisen.'</p>  
 <p>6. And Brâ<i>d</i>-rûkhsh the Tûr went forth; when he went to the right side (arakŏ), Zaratû<i>s</i>t hastened away to the left, and when he went to the left side, Zaratû<i>s</i>t hastened to the right, and he is thereby concealed from Brâ<i>d</i>-rûkhsh the Tûr, <i>who</i> has not met with him.</p>

XV, 3-5, and even there the medial m is really missing, though apparently existing in the final <i>â</i>; the irregularity of using s for sh is common in Pahlavi.</p>
