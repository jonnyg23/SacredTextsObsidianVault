 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part V: Marvels of Zoroastrianism (SBE47)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1897], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XI.</h3>  
 <p>1. About the marvellousness which is after the end of the millennium of Aûshê<i>d</i>ar-mâh and the arrival of the Triumphant Benefiter, until the end of the fifty-seventh year of Sôshâns and the production of the renovation among the existences.</p>  
 <p>2. Concerning the marvellousness of Sôshâns as to splendour <i>and</i> glory of person, it says that 'when the coming of the last rotation of those rotations of the seasons of Aûshê<i>d</i>ar-mâh <i>occurs</i>, the man Sôshâns is born <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">1</font></a> whose food is spiritual <i>and</i> body sunny (that is, his body is as radiant as the sun);' also this, that 'he looks on all sides with six-eyed power (6-dôîsarîh), <i>and</i> sees the remedy for persecution by the fiend.'</p>  
 <p>3. This, too, that with him is the triumphant Kayân glory 'which the mighty Frê<i>d</i>ûn <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">2</font></a> bore when Az-4 Dahâk <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>was</i> smitten by him; also Kai-Khûsrôî <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">4</font></a> was bearing it when the Tûr Frangrâsîyâk <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>was</i> smitten by him; also Frangrâsîyâk bore it when the Drvê Zênîgâk <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>was</i> smitten by him; <i>and</i></p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Kai-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">1</font></a> shall bear it when you fully attract him to righteousness, <i>and</i> through it he shall carry off the fiend <i>from</i> the concerns of the world of righteousness <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_329"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>4. And this, too, that in fifty-seven <i>of</i> his years <i>there</i> occur the annihilation of the fiendishness of the two-legged race <i>and</i> others, and the subjugation of disease and decrepitude, <i>of</i> death and persecution, and <i>of</i> the original evil <i>of</i> tyranny, apostasy, and depravity; <i>there</i> arise a perpetual verdant growth of vegetation <i>and</i> the primitive gift of joyfulness; <i>and there</i> are seventeen years <i>of</i> vegetable-eating, thirty years <i>of</i> water-drinking, <i>and</i> ten years of spiritual food.</p>  
 <p>5. And all the splendour, glory, and power, which <i>have</i> arisen in all those possessing splendour, glory, <i>and</i> power, <i>are</i> in him on whom they arrive together and for those who are his, when many inferior human beings are aroused splendid <i>and</i> powerful; and through their power and glory all the troops of the fiend are smitten. 6. And all mankind remain of one accord in the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, owing to the will of the creator, the command <i>of</i> that <i>apostle</i>, and the resources of his companions.</p>  
 <p>7. At the end of the fifty-seven years the fiend <i>and</i> Aharman are annihilated, the renovation <i>for</i> the future existence occurs, <i>and</i> the whole of the good creation is provided with purity, <i>and</i> perfect</p>

 <p>splendour. 8. <i>Just</i> as revelation states thus: 'When that millennium has fully elapsed, which is the third of the religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, that Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipper whose name is so Triumphant <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">1</font></a> then marches forward from the water Kanyisâ <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">2</font></a> with a thousand companions and also maidens <i>of</i> restrained disposition and blindly-striving behaviour <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">3</font></a>; and he smites the wicked people who <i>are</i> tyrannical, and annihilates them.'</p>  
 <p>9. Then those Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers smite, and none are smiting them. 0. Then those Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers produce a longing for a renovation among the existences, <i>one</i> ever-living, ever-beneficial, and ever desiring a Lord. 11. 'Then I, who am Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, produce the renovation according to the longing among the existences, <i>one</i> ever-living, ever-beneficial, <i>and ever</i> desiring a Lord.'</p>
