 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 163</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the completion of revelation, that is, at the end of the ten years, Mê<i>d</i>yômâh, son of Arâstâî, became faithful to Zaratû<i>s</i>t.</p>  
 <p>2. Afterwards, on <i>having</i> obtained <i>his</i> requests <a name="fr_513"></a><a href="#fn_508"><font size="1">1</font></a>, he came back to the conference of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and he spoke thus: 'In ten years <i>only</i> one man <i>has been</i> attracted by me.' 3. <i>And</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: '<i>There will</i> be days when so few <i>are</i> not attracted by you, who <i>are</i> themselves the occasion of the resurrection of the world; when, apart from Dahâk <a name="fr_514"></a><a href="#fn_509"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the beneficence of the formation of the renovation <i>of the universe</i> attracts every one besides, and the impenitence of Dahâk is destroyed.'</p>  
 <p>4. When he came out from the presence of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, with the same paradox, he thereby indicated his religion as complete to Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> through <i>his</i> intelligence.</p>  
 <p>5. In the two years after that <a name="fr_515"></a><a href="#fn_510"><font size="1">3</font></a>, the Kavîgs and</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Karaps of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, <i>in</i> the manner of opponents (hamêstarânîh), propounded thirty-three enquiries (khvâstakŏ) <a name="fr_516"></a><a href="#fn_511"><font size="1">1</font></a> to him, so that <i>by</i> command of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp he became the explainer of those thirty-three enquiries. 6. Of the thirty-three habits <a name="fr_517"></a><a href="#fn_512"><font size="1">2</font></a> of iniquity, come for opposition to the religion of the sacred beings; of the declaration, by revelation, of those thirty-three indications of fetters <a name="fr_518"></a><a href="#fn_513"><font size="1">3</font></a>; <i>and</i> of the restraint of the thirty-three iniquitous practices by the thirty-three best good works, <i>there are</i> statements in revelation. 7. Including the acceptance of the religion <i>by</i> Vi<i>s</i>tâsp from Zaratû<i>s</i>t, after the redemonstration of its judicially multiform prophecy and spiritual character, which are looked into through the evidence of three speakers about <i>them</i>, the archangels who, with worldly manifestation, have become apparent unto Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and <i>his</i> councillors <i>and</i> mighty ones; <i>they</i> are Vohûmanô, A<i>r</i><i>d</i>avahi<i>s</i>tô, and the Bûr<i>z</i>în-Mit<i>r</i>ô fire <a name="fr_519"></a><a href="#fn_514"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. About the reward which <i>existed</i> before the beneficence of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and <i>its</i> being seen how, through guidance by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, <i>it</i> is demonstrated by him to those of the world, so that Mê<i>d</i>yômâh is attracted in the tenth year in the forest of reedy hollows (kanyâstânŏ <a name="fr_520"></a><a href="#fn_515"><font size="1">5</font></a> vê<i>s</i>akŏ) which is the haunt of swine of the wild-boar <i>species</i> (khazûrâ-î var<i>â</i><i>z</i>ŏ gâs); in the twentieth year the Kavîg who is son</p>

 <p>of Kûndah <a name="fr_521"></a><a href="#fn_516"><font size="1">1</font></a> is attracted; in the thirtieth year the Khyôns <a name="fr_522"></a><a href="#fn_517"><font size="1">2</font></a> arrive, who make an incursion (var<i>d</i>akŏ) into the countries of Irân, owing to the Kavîgs, those who are more of their own race; and in the fortieth year Vohûnêm, son of Avarô<i>s</i>tar <a name="fr_523"></a><a href="#fn_518"><font size="1">3</font></a>, is born.</p>  
 <p>9. In the forty-seventh year Zaratû<i>s</i>t passes away, who attains seventy-seven years and forty days in the month A<i>r</i><i>d</i>avahi<i>s</i>tô, <i>on</i> the day Khûr <a name="fr_524"></a><a href="#fn_519"><font size="1">4</font></a>; <i>and</i> for eight rectified (vêhi<i>k</i>akŏ) months, till the month Dadvô and day Khûr, he should be brought <i>forward</i> as to be reverenced.</p>  
 <p>10. <i>In</i> the same month A<i>r</i><i>d</i>avahi<i>s</i>tô, in the sixty-third <a name="fr_525"></a><a href="#fn_520"><font size="1">5</font></a> year, Frashô<i>s</i>tar <a name="fr_526"></a><a href="#fn_521"><font size="1">6</font></a> passed away, and in the sixty-fourth year <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_527"></a><a href="#fn_522"><font size="1">7</font></a>, <i>the same</i> as became the priest of priests after Zaratû<i>s</i>t; in the seventy-third year Hangâûrûsh, son of <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_528"></a><a href="#fn_523"><font size="1">8</font></a>; in the eightieth year Asmôk-khanvatô <a name="fr_529"></a><a href="#fn_524"><font size="1">9</font></a>, and <i>also</i> in the eightieth</p>

 <p>11. Of the six great upholders of the religion there are the two daughters of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, whose names are Frênŏ and Srîtŏ <a name="fr_531"></a><a href="#fn_526"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>with</i> Aharûbŏ-stôtô, <i>son</i> of Mê<i>d</i>yômâh <a name="fr_532"></a><a href="#fn_527"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and another three, who <i>are</i> renowned for their religion for a hundred years, who are Vohûnêm <a name="fr_533"></a><a href="#fn_528"><font size="1">4</font></a> that is born in the fortieth year of the religion, Sênô <a name="fr_534"></a><a href="#fn_529"><font size="1">5</font></a> is <i>afterwards</i> born and passes away in the two-hundredth year, <i>and</i> as to his hundred-discipledom <a name="fr_535"></a><a href="#fn_530"><font size="1">6</font></a>, it exists day and night till the three-hundredth year. 12. Afterwards the religion is disturbed and the monarchy is contested (<i>g</i>angî-aîtŏ).</p>  
 <p>13. About the three customs (dâ<i>d</i>ŏ) which Zaratû<i>s</i>t prescribed as the best:—The first of them is this: 'Do not go without others, except with magisterial authority (apatkâr ra<i>d</i>îhâ);' the second is: 'Though they shall proceed unlawfully as to you, consider <i>your</i> actions lawfully beforehand;' <i>and</i> the third is: 'Next-of-kin marriage, for the</p>
