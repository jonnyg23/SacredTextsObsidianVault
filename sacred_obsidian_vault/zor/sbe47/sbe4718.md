 <p><a name="page_126"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 126</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER III.</h3>  
 <p>1. About that which is manifested as regards the propitiousness, forward intellect, and complete virtue of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, it is openly declared that, according to his explaining statements, <i>there occurred such events</i> as the victory of Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and the Irânians over Ar<i>g</i>âsp <a name="fr_374"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">1</font></a> and an army of Khyôns and other foreigners <i>of</i> innumerable kinds, <i>and</i> details on that subject; also other assistance of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and those of the realm in other necessities.</p>  
 <p>2. The killing of Zaratû<i>s</i>t himself <i>by</i> Brâ<i>d</i>rô-rêsh <a name="fr_375"></a><a href="#fn_371"><font size="1">2</font></a> the Tûr; the provision of a chariot <i>by</i> Srîtô <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_372"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the Vîsraps; and whatever is on those subjects.</p>  
 <p>3. Also, specially, matters in the times to come, each consecutively, <i>when</i> therein arrive devastators <i>such</i> as Alexander <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and the killer of Akrê-khira<i>d</i>ŏ <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_374"><font size="1">5</font></a>, Mahrkûs <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_375"><font size="1">6</font></a>, Dahâk <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and other devastators; extenders of belief (vîravî<i>s</i>n-vâlân) <i>such</i> as the Messiah (Mashikh), Mânih <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_377"><font size="1">8</font></a>, and others; periods <i>such</i> as the steel <i>age</i> <a name="fr_382"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">9</font></a>, <i>that</i> mingled with iron, and others; and organizers, restorers, <i>and</i> introducers of religion, <i>such</i></p>

 <p>4. The formation of custom, and the indications which <i>have</i> come to manifestation and <i>will</i> arrive at various periods; and the proclamation of these, too, by <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="sbe4719.htm#fn_384"><font size="1">6</font></a>, from the teaching of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, is what he wrote, together with the Avesta and Zand, upon oxhides, and it was written <i>with</i> gold, and kept in the royal treasury <a name="fr_389"></a><a href="sbe4719.htm#fn_385"><font size="1">7</font></a>. 5. And it was from it the high-priests of the rulers made many copies and afterwards, too, it is from it that it happened, both to the less intelligent and the corrupters, that <i>they</i> were introducing different opinions <i>and</i> different views.</p>
