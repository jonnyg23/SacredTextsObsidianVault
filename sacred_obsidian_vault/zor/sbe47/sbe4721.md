 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part V: Marvels of Zoroastrianism (SBE47)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1897], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. About the glory of Zaratû<i>s</i>t becoming manifest even before <i>his</i> birth, <i>it</i> is thus declared, that forty-five years before <i>the time</i> when Zaratû<i>s</i>t came out to his conference <a name="fr_420"></a><a href="#fn_416"><font size="1">2</font></a>, when Frênŏ gave birth <i>to</i> the mother of Zaratû<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_421"></a><a href="#fn_417"><font size="1">3</font></a>, whom they called Dûk<i>d</i>ak, it</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 139</font></a></p>  
 <p>came down from the endless light, in the manner of fire, <i>and</i> mingled with the fire which was before her; <i>and</i> from the fire it mingled with the mother of Zaratû<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_422"></a><a href="#fn_418"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. <i>For</i> three nights it was manifest, to all passers-by, as a species of fire <i>in</i> the direction of the house, <i>and</i> passers <i>on</i> the road always saw great radiance. 3. Also when she became fifteen years old, the radiance of that glory which <i>was</i> in her, <i>was</i> even such <i>that</i>, on the path she was walking <i>along</i>, its brightness <i>was</i> then shed by her <a name="fr_423"></a><a href="#fn_419"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. About the combination of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>whose</i> guardian spirit is reverenced; that is, his guardian spirit, in the Hôm, and his glory are given the cow's milk, by his father and mother drinking <i>it</i> up <a name="fr_424"></a><a href="#fn_420"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>and</i> his spiritual life (ahvô) came into the combination, as was proclaimed <i>by</i> his statements in the manuscript about the Guidance of the Ceremonial <a name="fr_425"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. About the backward connection of Zaratû<i>s</i>t with Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, through the two who <i>are</i> Yim, the best of the worldly existences, and Nêryôsang of the spirits. 6. The enumeration of the lineage of Zaratû<i>s</i>t is Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>son of</i> Pôrûshaspô <a name="fr_426"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">5</font></a>, of Purtarâspô,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 140</font></a></p>  
 <p>of Ahûrva<i>d</i>âspô, of Hâê<i>k</i>a<i>d</i>âspô, of <i>K</i>îkhshnû<i>s</i>, of Paîtiraspô, of Are<i>g</i>a<i>d</i>arsî*, of Kharedhar*, of Spîtâmân, of Vaêdî<i>s</i>tô, of Ayazem, of Frî<i>s</i>, of Ari<i>g</i>*; of Dûrasrôbô, of Mânû<i>s</i><i>k</i>îhar, of Mânû<i>s</i>-khûrnar, of Mânû<i>s</i>-khûrnâk, whom Nêryôsang the messenger of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>brought, and</i> whose mother was Vîzag, <i>daughter</i> of Aryag, of Srîtak, of Bîtak, of Frazûsak, of Zû<i>s</i>ak, of Fragûzag, of Gûzag, <i>daughter</i> of Aîrî<i>k</i>, <i>son</i> of Frê<i>d</i>ûn of the Âspîgâns to the total of ten Âspîgâns, of Yim, of the Vîvang,has, of the Ayang,has, of the Anang,has, of Hôshâng, of Fravâk, of Sîyâmak, of Masîyâ, of Gâyômar<i>d</i>.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_417"></a><a href="sbe4721.htm#fr_421">138:3</a> It is necessary to translate in this manner, to agree with Dk. VII, ii, 3, but the text, which is ambiguous. runs as follows: 'amat Frênŏg (Frênŏ-î?) Zaratû<i>s</i>t am, zya<i>s</i>ân Dûk<i>d</i>akŏg ka<i>r</i>îtuntô, <a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 139</font></a> zerkhûntô;' which might also be translated thus: 'when Frênŏ, the mother of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, whom they called Dûk<i>d</i>ak, was born.' This Frênŏ, whether mother or daughter, does not seem to correspond with any Fr<i>e</i>ni mentioned in the Avesta.</p>
