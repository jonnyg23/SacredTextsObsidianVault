 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part V: Marvels of Zoroastrianism (SBE47)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1897], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. About his interfering talk (andarg-gôbi<i>s</i>nŏîh) with the iniquitous, this also is declared, that one</p>  
 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 150</font></a></p>  
 <p>day Dûrêsrôbô <a name="fr_459"></a><a href="#fn_455"><font size="1">1</font></a> the Karap, as <i>it were</i> from the same five brethren, came out to the house of Pôrûshaspô; <i>and</i> Pôrûshaspô placed a bowl (<i>g</i>amakŏ) of mare's milk before <i>him</i>, and spoke to him thus: 'Consecrate <i>it</i>.'</p>  
 <p>2. Zaratû<i>s</i>t expostulated with Pôrûshaspô thus: 'I <i>will</i> consecrate it.' 3. Pôrûshaspô spoke thus: 'He should consecrate, and the grace is to be offered up by you <a name="fr_460"></a><a href="#fn_456"><font size="1">2</font></a>;' <i>and</i> as many as three times <i>they</i> mutually disputed.</p>  
 <p>4. Then up stood Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and his right foot struck at the bowl and emptied it, and he spoke thus: 'I reverence righteousness, I reverence the righteous <i>and</i> the poor, men <i>and</i> women; do thou, <i>O</i> Pôrûshaspô! prepare a portion for him in whom <i>there</i> is worthiness.'</p>  
 <p>5. And Dûrêsrôbô spoke unto Zaratû<i>s</i>t thus: 'As <i>some</i> of my portion <i>of</i> daily <i>food was</i> first thrown away by thee, it is I who <i>will</i> bring <i>it</i> on both thy lives, and <i>will</i> utterly destroy thee.' 6. Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke interruptingly <a name="fr_461"></a><a href="#fn_457"><font size="1">3</font></a> thus: 'With complete mindfulness I <i>will</i> look upon thee with both eyes, and <i>will</i> utterly destroy thee.'</p>  
 <p>7. And, for a long time, they constantly looked, one at the other, with unshrinking gaze; <i>but</i> the divine nature of Zaratû<i>s</i>t is victorious over the witchcraft of that <i>wizard, and</i> Dûrêsrôbô is further disturbed; he also asked for <i>his</i> horse <i>and</i> spoke thus: 'On account of this <i>boy, it</i> is impossible for me to stay.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 151</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. He sat upon the horse, <i>and</i> when he had gone a little <i>way</i>, he fell off from the horse, through severe distress, and died; and the children of his children's children have died upon the same spot <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>
