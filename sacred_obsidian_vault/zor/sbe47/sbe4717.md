 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part V: Marvels of Zoroastrianism (SBE47)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1897], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER II.</h3>  
 <p>1. In the pure light <i>and</i> equal to the archangels—collected together and reckoned together, since the original creation, <i>and</i> completely unmixed—Zaratû<i>s</i>t was spiritually fashioned and provided undefectively for the acceptance and propagation of the religion in the world <a name="fr_350"></a><a href="#fn_346"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 2. And when he is sent for the manifestation of restrained saintliness (môkîh) and bodily substance, great glory and radiance become as much discernible in <i>him</i> as the same <i>had</i> come into the posterity of Yim who are Pôrûshâspô <a name="fr_351"></a><a href="#fn_347"><font size="1">2</font></a>, his father, <i>and</i> Dûk<i>d</i>âûb <a name="fr_352"></a><a href="#fn_348"><font size="1">3</font></a> who is his mother; and also while <i>he</i> is <i>being</i> born and <i>for</i> the duration of life, he produced a radiance, glow, and brilliance from the place of his own abode, that issued intensely and strongly, like the splendour of fire, to distant lands <a name="fr_353"></a><a href="#fn_349"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. Also <i>about</i> the wizards and witches, tyrants, Kîgs, and Karaps, and other miscreants who have produced outrage for him during his birth and child-hood, with desire for his destruction; who <i>have</i> manifestly come about <i>his</i> death <i>or</i> disablement, and even so far as <i>those</i> who <i>have come into</i> the visible assembly <i>about</i> the destruction of <i>his</i> glory and other <i>causes of</i> helplessness <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_350"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 4. And also including the wolves <i>and</i> other wild beasts, to whom <i>he was</i></p>

 <p>cast forth by those of Pôrûshâspô's kinsmen who <i>were</i> wizards, <i>and</i> the kindred of those frequenting demon-worship, but only as a test <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. <i>In</i> like manner it is declared <i>that</i>, on account of Vohûmanô <i>being</i> welcome to him, he laughed out-right at birth <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 6. His coming for conference <i>with</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and owing to <i>his</i> accepting the religion, Aharman and many demons and fiends have come to him with exhibition of fear, offering of service, and the argument and entreaty of supplicants; and all these have returned from him disapprovingly, defeatedly, and confusedly, through the pure proclamation of his Avesta, the unique perfection of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s will <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_354"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and the indication of a prepared armament (vîrâstŏ-zênîh), a declared reward, a powerful sovereignty, and a way of injury <i>to</i> the destroyer.</p>  
 <p>7. Also the triumph of the sacred beings <i>in</i> the end, and the peculiarity <i>of</i> the joint control <i>of</i> the demons; how, before that, they rushed openly into the world <i>and</i> have dwindled, <i>their</i> bodily forms <i>are</i> shattered <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and they are so converted into secret decay, that <i>their</i> supplicants, deceived and deceivers, became awful disputants through confession of it.</p>  
 <p>8. And when, through completely accepting the religion from Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, he came to the obedient king Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp to attract those of the world, he exhibited glorious actions of many kinds and the</p>

 <p>smiting of demons and fiends; and <i>he</i> openly spoke the thoughts of king Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and many of the multitude <i>about</i> hidden matters <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>thus</i>: 'A person is possessing life only by destiny, and without his body, <i>but with</i> a like destiny, the person possessing life is restored;' <i>he was</i> also opposing many wizards, demon-worshippers, demon-consulters, <i>and</i> those seduced <i>by</i> <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">2</font></a> deceivers who are awful disputants. 9. And this <i>was</i> the utterance of those twelve stars whose names <i>are those</i> of the twelve <i>zodiacal</i> constellations, and the primitive learned of Bâpêl <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">3</font></a>, that have come to these, who <i>are</i> asking questions of the chief rulers of Khvanîras, <i>have</i> to justify <i>it</i>, in the controversy, with the spiritual and material customs and rude observances which are diversely manifested, onwards from <i>the time of</i> Yim the splendid; and the evidence of it, discernible with him, <i>was that of</i> Vohûmanô, Ashavahi<i>s</i>tô, the fire, and some other good spirits <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>10. Afterwards, too, the estimating and weighing of the whole utterance of the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>were</i> held out by him to Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and those of the world, as very accountably a wonder; and further, too, he remained as <i>it were</i> more conflicting with fellow-disputants than his own words opposing the fellow-disputants.</p>  
 <p>11. Also when, through the effect of glory and the struggle <i>for being</i> saved, every class of apostles and prophets and manifestation of tokens and such-like <i>were</i>, with certain <i>and</i> striking evidence, casting down blessings, even thereupon the obedient king</p>

 <p>

 <p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp's fear of the adversary of religion, and also other causes, arose, on account of which he <i>was</i> then accepting the propagation of the religion <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">1</font></a>, when its great glory <i>and</i> beauty <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>were</i> seen by him. 12. At first, Zarîr <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">3</font></a>, Spend-dâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">4</font></a>, Frashô<i>s</i>tar, and <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">5</font></a>, several of the realm <i>who were</i> noble, conspicuous, and well-acting, the good <i>and</i> princes <i>of</i> mankind, beheld visibly the will and desirableness of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> and the archangels, and the progressive religion of the creatures, fit for those completely victorious (hû<i>k</i>îragânîg).</p>  
 <p>13. Lastly, the seizure and capturing (gîrini<i>s</i>nŏ) by the demons <i>are</i> beheld <i>and</i> watched; <i>and</i> they have remained opposing, owing to the number <i>of</i> idolators competing wranglingly. 14. And much conflict and slaughter occurred, which arose purely, so that the mingling <i>of</i> the religion in the world proceeded <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>15. <i>There</i> are also his pure new bringers and complete controllers of the same religion, Aûshê<i>d</i>ar <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">7</font></a>, Aûshê<i>d</i>ar-mâh <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">8</font></a>, and Sôshâns <a name="fr_372"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">9</font></a>; and through the complete progress of this religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, all the good creatures become without disturbance and all-radiant <a name="fr_373"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">10</font></a>.</p>
