 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part V: Marvels of Zoroastrianism (SBE47)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1897], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IV.</h3>  
 <p>1. About the selectness and perfection of the race of Irân, that is, how the destroyer and the mingling of defects came into the creation, first into the distribution of the race [of mankind] <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">8</font></a> from the children of Sîyâmak <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">9</font></a>, <i>and</i> good intellect, good disposition,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 128</font></a></p>  
 <p>proper affection, proper praise, proper modesty, observance of hope, liberality, truth, generosity, good friendship, <i>and</i> other capability, glory, and proper duty are more particularly included for Fravâk <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_388"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and again altered for frontier peoples.</p>  
 <p>2. Such-like goodness and glory, again, through selection from a promiscuous origin—even till the coming on of various new contaminations, from the demons, among the children of Fravâk—<i>were</i> for Hâôshâng and Vâêgere<i>d</i> <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">2</font></a>, as Hâôshâng through superior glory <i>was</i> ruler of the world, and Vâêgere<i>d</i> through provincial government (dahyûkânîh) became the cultivator and cherisher of the world; and their well-destined descendants <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">3</font></a> aggrandized the race of the monarchs among the well-born provincial governors (dahyûkân).</p>  
 <p>3. In union, such-like glory and goodness proceeded, mutually connected, in various ages as settled by the annals (mâ<i>d</i>îgân), and the frontiers of Atûr <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">4</font></a> are penetrated; also in various annals <i>there</i> were in succession <i>such men</i> as Tâkhmôrup, Yim, Frê<i>d</i>ûn the Âspîgân, and Aîrî<i>k</i> of Irân, the progenitor of Mânû<i>s</i>, Mânû<i>s</i>-khvârnar, and Mânû<i>s</i><i>k</i>îhar <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. And, besides including this one, who <i>was</i> a monarch of superior glory, and other monarchs <i>such</i> as <i>are</i> also in the same race <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">6</font></a>, the Kayâns <a name="fr_398"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">7</font></a> <i>were</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 129</font></a></p>  
 <p>chosen. 5. Even thus, as regards the Kayâns, the annals <i>have</i> always to consider him who is monarch as the right <i>one</i>, who is a fellow-descendant <i>of</i> the lineage, and in whom the glory is residing.</p>  
 <p>6. The creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> has informed Nêryôsang <a name="fr_399"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">1</font></a>, his own reminder, <i>that</i> a similar instance is the supreme Zaratû<i>s</i>tship, and the glory of the religion due to the same; also many other reasons, which <i>are</i> recountable, <i>are</i> declared in the Avesta, indications known only <i>to</i> an Irânian person of the best lineage. 7. And this, too, he has said, namely: 'Great glory and goodness of this description <i>are</i> appropriatable by the same race, and <i>are</i> even now <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">2</font></a> visibly manifest.'</p>  
 <p>8,. About what <i>are</i> the requirements of the tribe of those who call <i>this</i> really their Gyêmarâ and how <i>they are</i> effected by our acquaintance with revelation, that is, <i>by</i> much recitation of it; only, several occasions (yâvar 1-<i>k</i>and) are necessary in these times. 9. Also action and precaution <i>are</i> possible for them, and among the actions and precautions <i>one</i> is to assume more especially law and custom, and whatever <i>was</i> perverted by us and again made true; and, as to those taught, several persons who <i>are</i> intellectual, seeking means, and friends of the soul, <i>have</i> come to teach complete delusions to them; also for the same tribe and whomever it is proper to teach, when it is necessary for them, even a worldly</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 130</font></a></p>  
 <p>priestly authority is guiding for <i>that</i> same tribe, because he has come <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>
