 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The forty-second subject is this, that it is necessary to practise strict abstinence <i>from</i> that sin <i>which affects</i> accusers <a name="fr_1319"></a><a href="#fn_1308"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 2. That would be when any one slanders (buhtânad) <a name="fr_1320"></a><a href="#fn_1309"><font size="1">3</font></a>, or any one commits a rape on the wife of some one <a name="fr_1321"></a><a href="#fn_1310"><font size="1">4</font></a>, or causes a woman <i>to occupy</i> a separate bed from her own husband.</p>  
 <p>3. These are sins <i>for</i> which there is no retribution, except when thou beggest forgiveness of that person whom thy sin has assailed. 4. Afterwards, they keep back the soul, <i>at</i> the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge, till the time when its antagonist arrives and exacts justice from it; then it obtains release.</p>  
 <p>5. Every time that any one applies a falsehood or a slander to some person, so that <i>people</i> are afterwards</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_306"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 306</font></a></p>  
 <p>telling that falsehood again, and it vexes the heart of that person, they are bringing punishment ever anew on the soul of that <i>former</i> one. 6. The sin does not depart through <i>the performance of</i> duties and good works, so long as he does not make <i>his</i> antagonist satisfied.</p>  
 <p>7. This is a grave sin, <i>and</i> it is requisite to he careful that they do not commit <i>it</i>.</p>
