 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-seventh subject is this, that, when</p>  
 <p><a name="page_351"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 351</font></a></p>  
 <p>any one departs from the world <a name="fr_1536"></a><a href="#fn_1525"><font size="1">1</font></a>, it is necessary to make an effort, in those three days, so that they <i>may</i> continuously perform the ceremonial of Srôsh <a name="fr_1537"></a><a href="#fn_1526"><font size="1">2</font></a> and make the fire blaze, and <i>may</i> recite the Avesta; because the soul is three days in this world <a name="fr_1538"></a><a href="#fn_1527"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. The fourth night it is requisite to consecrate three sacred cakes; one <i>with</i> a dedication <i>to</i> Rashn <a name="fr_1539"></a><a href="#fn_1528"><font size="1">4</font></a> and Â<i>s</i>tâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1540"></a><a href="#fn_1529"><font size="1">5</font></a>, one <i>with</i> a dedication <i>to</i> the spirit Râm <a name="fr_1541"></a><a href="#fn_1530"><font size="1">6</font></a>, <i>and</i> one <i>with</i> a dedication to the righteous guardian spirit; <i>and one is</i> to consecrate a dress and something <i>as</i> a righteous gift for that soul <a name="fr_1542"></a><a href="#fn_1531"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. It is necessary that the dress be new and of uniform quality (<i>g</i>ins), and such as turban, shirt, vest, girdle, trowsers, shoes (pasandil) <a name="fr_1543"></a><a href="#fn_1532"><font size="1">8</font></a>, and mouth-veil. 4. Since they give those among the spirits a counterpart of those garments, therefore, whatever is more beautiful, <i>and</i> more surpassing <i>in</i> grandeur for the soul <i>in</i> that place, is necessary where that place is, because our fathers and mothers and the whole of <i>our</i> relations are <i>in</i> that place. 5. <i>And</i> since the souls recognise and ask after one another in that world, they are, therefore, more joyful on account of every one whose dress <a name="fr_1544"></a><a href="#fn_1533"><font size="1">9</font></a> and grandeur are more surpassing. 6. In a similar manner, when the dress is old and ragged, they are ashamed, and exhibit heaviness of heart.</p>

 <p>7. They call that dress a righteous gift because they consecrate <i>it</i>; and it is necessary to give <i>it to</i> the priests and high-priests, as it is a righteous gift on account of their position. 8. And it is suitable for them to keep <i>it</i> for the reason that the souls are nearer to them; <i>people should also</i> make an effort that the dress may be stitched like the dress of a priest.</p>  
 <p>9. The sacred beings make up the account <i>and</i> reckoning for the soul when the priest recites frasasti ahurahê mazd<i>a</i>u <a name="fr_1545"></a><a href="#fn_1534"><font size="1">1</font></a> and removes the Frasast <a name="fr_1546"></a><a href="#fn_1535"><font size="1">2</font></a> from this side to that side. 10. The soul passes over the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge when, <i>on</i> the fourth night, it arrives from the world <i>at</i> the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge. 11. First it goes to the abode of fire (âta<i>s</i>-gâh) <a name="fr_1547"></a><a href="#fn_1536"><font size="1">3</font></a>; afterwards, one step reaches to the star station, the second step reaches to the moon station, the third step to the sun station, <i>and</i> with the fourth step it reaches the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge <a name="fr_1548"></a><a href="#fn_1537"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> they convey it to its own place.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1531"></a><a href="sbe24175.htm#fr_1542">351:7</a> B29 has 'on that cake.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1532"></a><a href="sbe24175.htm#fr_1543">351:8</a> B29 has mûzah, 'boots,' and places them last.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1534"></a><a href="sbe24175.htm#fr_1545">352:1</a> 'Glory <i>be</i> to Ahura-mazda.' Lp adds 'ahunahê vairyêhê as far as ashaya nô paiti-<i>g</i>amyâ<i>d</i>,' that is 'to the Ahuna-vairya <i>formula</i>,' &amp;c. as far as 'may he come to us in righteousness' (Vas. VIII, 1-3).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1535"></a><a href="sbe24175.htm#fr_1546">352:2</a> A Frasast is a sacred cake marked on the upper side with nine superficial cuts (in three rows of three each) made with a fingernail while repeating the words humat hûkht huvar<i>s</i>t, 'well-thought, well-spoken, well-done,' thrice, one word to each of the nine cuts. It is placed before the consecrating priest, but to his right, while the ordinary sacred cakes are to his left (see Haug's Essays, pp. 396, 407, 408).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1536"></a><a href="sbe24175.htm#fr_1547">352:3</a> That is, when it leaves the vicinity of the body, after hovering about it for three nights (see Sls. XII, 5).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1537"></a><a href="sbe24175.htm#fr_1548">352:4</a> In other accounts the soul has to pass over this bridge before it steps forwards to the stars and moon and sun (see Mkh. II., 123, 145, VII, 9-12, Dd. XXXIV, 3, AV. V, 2, VII-IX, 1).</p>
