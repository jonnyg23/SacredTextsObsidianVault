 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eleventh subject is this, that it is necessary to maintain the fire-place <a name="fr_1157"></a><a href="#fn_1148"><font size="1">3</font></a> properly, and to keep watch <a name="fr_1158"></a><a href="#fn_1149"><font size="1">4</font></a>, so that <i>the fire shall</i> not die <i>out</i>, and <i>that</i> nothing polluted and impure shall attain to the fire; and it is necessary to make a menstruous woman avoid <i>being</i> within three steps <i>of it</i>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Because every time that they maintain a fire properly, which is within a dwelling, every fire which is in the earth of seven regions becomes pleased with those persons, and, when they ask a favour, or beg a necessity (hâ<i>g</i>at), it becomes quickly operative. 3. And every time that <i>one</i> does not maintain <i>it</i> properly, every fire which is in the earth of seven regions receives injury from that person, and the necessity he begs does not become operative. 4. If any one does not maintain the fire-place properly, if he gives a hundred dînârs <a name="fr_1159"></a><a href="#fn_1150"><font size="1">1</font></a> to the fire Gu<i>s</i>asp <a name="fr_1160"></a><a href="#fn_1151"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>there</i> is no acceptance <i>of it</i>, and that sin does not depart from him.</p>  
 <p>5. For it is declared in revelation <a name="fr_1161"></a><a href="#fn_1152"><font size="1">3</font></a>, that the creator Hôrmazd has given sovereignty in heaven to Ardibahi<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1162"></a><a href="#fn_1153"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the archangel, and has spoken thus: '<i>As to</i> every one with whom thou art not pleased, do not let him escape into heaven.' 6. And this is also declared in revelation, that, every time that they do not maintain the fire properly, pregnancy becomes scarcer for the women, fewer male children are born, and honour (hurmat) in the vicinity of the king becomes less for the men, and <i>there</i> is no approbation (qabûl) of <i>their</i> words.</p>  
 <p>7. <i>For</i> every single fire which dies out in a dwelling a loss of three dirhams and two dângs <a name="fr_1163"></a><a href="#fn_1154"><font size="1">5</font></a> falls</p>
