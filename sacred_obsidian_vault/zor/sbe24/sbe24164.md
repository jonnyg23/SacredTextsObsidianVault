 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventy-sixth subject is this, when a woman brings forth, it is necessary that she <i>should</i> not wash <i>her</i> head <i>for</i> twenty-one days, nor put <i>her</i> hand again on anything, nor walk on a terrace-roof, nor put <i>her</i> foot on a threshold in <i>her</i> habitation. 2. And after the twenty-one days, if she sees herself <i>in</i> such a state that she is able to wash <i>her</i> head, she washes <i>her</i> head. 3. And, after that, until the coming on of the fortieth day, it is requisite to abstain from the vicinity of a fire and anything that is wooden <a name="fr_1485"></a><a href="#fn_1474"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>or</i> earthen; it is also requisite to abstain from everything of her cooking and pot-boiling <a name="fr_1486"></a><a href="#fn_1475"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 4. Afterwards, when it is forty days, <i>she is</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_340"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 340</font></a></p>  
 <p>to wash <i>her</i> head, and it is proper <i>for her</i> to do every <i>kind of</i> work.</p>  
 <p>5. Till the lapse of a second <a name="fr_1487"></a><a href="sbe24165.htm#fn_1476"><font size="1">1</font></a> forty days it is not proper <i>for her</i> husband to make an approach to her, for it is a great sin, and it is possible that she <i>may</i> become pregnant a second time, as within a <i>period of</i> forty days women become very quickly pregnant <a name="fr_1488"></a><a href="sbe24165.htm#fn_1477"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>6. And if after the <i>first</i> forty days she sees herself impure, unless she knows with accuracy that it has come from the infant, it is necessary to consider if <i>she be</i> menstruous.</p>
