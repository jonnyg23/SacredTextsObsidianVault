 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore is <i>it</i> when the treasure of the spiritual <i>existence</i> is allotted so truly, <i>and</i> that of the worldly <i>existence</i> so falsely?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'The treasure of the worldly <i>existence</i> was <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">2</font></a> allotted as truly, in the original creation, as that of the spiritual <i>existence</i>. 5. And the creator, Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, provided the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 38</font></a></p>  
 <p>happiness of every kind, that is in these creatures and creation, for the use (bûn) of the sun <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">1</font></a> and moon and those twelve constellations which are called the twelve chieftains <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">2</font></a> by revelation; (6) and they, too, accepted <i>it</i> in order to allot <i>it</i> truly and deservedly.</p>  
 <p>7. 'And, afterwards, Aharman produced those seven planets, such as are called the seven chieftains of Aharman, for dissipating <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>and</i> carrying off that happiness from the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, in opposition to the sun and moon and those twelve constellations. 8. And <i>as to</i> every happiness which those-constellations bestow on the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, (9) those planets take away as much of it as <i>it</i> is possible for them (the constellations) to give <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">4</font></a>, (10) and give <i>it</i> up to the power of the demons <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">5</font></a> and fiends and the bad.</p>  
 <p>11. 'And the treasure of the spiritual <i>existence</i> is so true on this account, because Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the lord, with all the angels <i>and</i> archangels, is <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">6</font></a> undisturbed, (12) and they make the struggle with Aharman and the demons, and also the account of the souls of men, with justice. 13. And the place of <i>him</i> whose good work is more is in heaven, (14) the place of <i>him</i> whose good work and sin are equal is among the ever-stationary <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="#fn_214"><font size="1">7</font></a>, (15) and when the crime is more, his path is then to hell.'</p>
