 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Who is the more forgiving (vakhshayânîktar)? 3. What is the more in strength? 4. What is the swifter <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_349"><font size="1">1</font></a>? 5. What is the happier? 6. What is the more miserable <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_350"><font size="1">2</font></a>?'</p>  
 <p>7. The spirit of wisdom answered (8) thus: 'Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the lord, is the more forgiving. 9. He saw <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">3</font></a> the nine thousand years mischief <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">4</font></a> among his own creatures, owing to Aharman, yet afterwards, through justice and forgiveness, he does not then smite him <i>for</i> it <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>10. 'And the <i>celestial</i> sphere is the more in strength. 11. The intellect <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_354"><font size="1">6</font></a> of mankind is the swifter. 12. The souls of the righteous are the happier. 13. <i>And</i> those of the wicked are the more penitent <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">7</font></a>.'</p>
