 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eightieth subject is this, that there is a time when thou recitest one Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_1502"></a><a href="#fn_1491"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>and</i> the merit of it may be as much as <i>that of</i> ten. 2. There is a time when thou recitest one Ashem-vohû, <i>and</i> the merit of it may be as much as <i>that of</i> a hundred. 3. A time may be when thou recitest one <a name="fr_1503"></a><a href="#fn_1492"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> the merit of it may be as much as <i>that of</i> a thousand. 4. A time may be when the merit of one Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_1504"></a><a href="#fn_1493"><font size="1">5</font></a> may be as much as <i>that of</i> ten thousand. 5.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 344</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> A time may be when the merit of one Ashem-vohû is as much as the value (qîmat) <a name="fr_1505"></a><a href="#fn_1494"><font size="1">1</font></a> of this world and that <i>other</i> world <a name="fr_1506"></a><a href="#fn_1495"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>6. As for that Ashem-vohû whose nature <a name="fr_1507"></a><a href="#fn_1496"><font size="1">3</font></a> is as much as ten, that is when they recite <i>it</i> as they eat bread. 7. That which is, from nature, as much as a hundred is when they recite <i>it</i> after eating <a name="fr_1508"></a><a href="#fn_1497"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 8. That which is so much by nature that, <i>having</i> turned side over side, they recite <i>it</i> correctly may be a merit of a thousand <a name="fr_1509"></a><a href="#fn_1498"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 9. That which is of the nature of ten thousand is that which thou recitest when thou risest up from sleep <a name="fr_1510"></a><a href="#fn_1499"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 10. And that whose nature is as much as this world and that <i>other</i> world <a name="fr_1511"></a><a href="#fn_1500"><font size="1">7</font></a> is when they recite <i>it</i> at the time of the dissolution of life; for, if he be not able to recite <i>it</i> himself, friends and relations give <i>it</i> into his mouth. 11 <a name="fr_1512"></a><a href="#fn_1501"><font size="1">8</font></a>. If he be fit for hell he becomes fit for the ever-stationary, if he be fit for the ever-stationary he becomes fit for heaven, <i>and</i> if he be fit for heaven he becomes fit for the supreme heaven <a name="fr_1513"></a><a href="#fn_1502"><font size="1">9</font></a>.</p>
