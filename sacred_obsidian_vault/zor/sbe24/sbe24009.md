 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER III.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Is liberality good, or truth <a name="fr_161"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">1</font></a>, (3) or gratitude <a name="fr_162"></a><a href="#fn_160"><font size="1">2</font></a>, or wisdom, (4) or complete mindfulness <a name="fr_163"></a><a href="#fn_161"><font size="1">3</font></a>, or contentment?'</p>  
 <p>5. The spirit of wisdom answered (6) thus: 'As to the soul <i>it is</i> liberality, as to all the world <i>it is</i> truth, (7) unto the sacred beings <i>it is</i> gratitude, as to a man's self <a name="fr_164"></a><a href="#fn_162"><font size="1">4</font></a> <i>it is</i> wisdom, (8) as to all business <i>it is</i> complete mindfulness, and as to the comfort of the body and the vanquishing of Aharman and the demons contentment is good.'</p>
