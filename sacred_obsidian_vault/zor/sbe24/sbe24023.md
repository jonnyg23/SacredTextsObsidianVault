 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: Which is that pleasure which is worse than unhappiness?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'Whoever has acquired wealth by crime, and he becomes glad of it thereby <a name="fr_270"></a><a href="#fn_266"><font size="1">5</font></a>, then that pleasure is worse for him than unhappiness.'</p>
