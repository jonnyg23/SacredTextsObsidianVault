 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER II.</h3>  
 <p>1. The second subject is this, that it is necessary to make an effort (<i>g</i>ahd), so that they <i>may</i> not</p>  
 <p><a name="page_259"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 259</font></a></p>  
 <p>commit any sin. 2. If even a trifling sin occurs it is not desirable to assume that this small quantity does not possess harm hereafter.</p>  
 <p>3. For it is said in revelation, that if such <i>be</i> the quantity of sin that the sin is one filament of the hair <a name="fr_1093"></a><a href="#fn_1085"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the eyelashes more in weight than the good works are, that person arrives in hell. 4. And if such a quantity of good works be in excess, he arrives righteous in the radiant locality of heaven.</p>  
 <p>5. Therefore, even if a sin be trifling it is not desirable to commit <i>it</i>; and it is requisite to refrain, so that they <i>may</i> not commit <i>it</i>, and <i>may</i> become without doubt as to the religion.</p>
