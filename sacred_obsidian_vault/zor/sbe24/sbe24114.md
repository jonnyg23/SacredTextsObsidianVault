 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twenty-sixth subject is this, that the wise and the ancients say that when a man becomes fifteen years <i>of age</i> it is necessary that he takes</p>  
 <p><a name="page_289"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 289</font></a></p>  
 <p>one of the angels <a name="fr_1241"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">1</font></a> as his own protection <a name="fr_1242"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>that</i> he takes one of the wise as his own sage, and <i>that</i> he takes one of the high-priests and officiating priests as his own high-priest. 2. So that, if, any time, a bereavement (âstânah) approaches, he <i>may</i> beg a favour from the archangels <a name="fr_1243"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">3</font></a>, in order that it <i>may</i> furnish <a name="fr_1244"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">4</font></a> an escape <i>from that</i> bereavement. 3. And <a name="fr_1245"></a><a href="#fn_1235"><font size="1">5</font></a>, any time any affair comes forward, and <i>he has</i> <a name="fr_1246"></a><a href="#fn_1236"><font size="1">6</font></a> to have opinion (râî) <i>and</i> advice (ma<i>s</i>varat), he holds a consultation with that sage, while the sage tells him <i>his</i> opinion (tadbîr). 4. And, if any <i>question as to</i> proper and improper comes forward, he speaks with that high-priest, so that he <i>may</i> tell him <i>in</i> reply.</p>  
 <p>5. When the instructions of these three persons are brought to pass, carrying <i>out</i> the commands of the sacred being is accomplished. 6. Especially the instructions of the high-priests, because their satisfaction is connected with the satisfaction of the sacred being; and the high-priests possess so much dignity (martabat) <i>in</i> the presence of the sacred being, the good <i>and</i> propitious, that they are quite able to forgive <i>any</i> trivial one of the sins of mankind <a name="fr_1247"></a><a href="#fn_1237"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and Hôrmazd, the good <i>and</i> propitious, quickly <a name="fr_1248"></a><a href="#fn_1238"><font size="1">8</font></a> forgives that sin for the high-priest. 7.</p>
