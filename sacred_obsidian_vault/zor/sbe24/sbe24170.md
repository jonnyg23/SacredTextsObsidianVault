 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-second subject is this, that, when thou risest up from the bed-clothes, it is necessary to tie the <i>sacred thread-</i>girdle <a name="fr_1523"></a><a href="#fn_1512"><font size="1">3</font></a> again <i>at</i> that same place, and it is not desirable to put <i>forth</i> a step without the girdle.</p>  
 <p>2. For it is declared in revelation, that every single step which <i>one</i> puts forth without the <i>sacred thread-</i>girdle is a Farmân sin <a name="fr_1524"></a><a href="#fn_1513"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> through four steps it becomes a Tanâvar sin which would be a weight of a thousand <i>and</i> two hundred dirhams <a name="fr_1525"></a><a href="#fn_1514"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 3. Therefore, it is necessary to keep watch <i>over</i> oneself, as regards this sin, and to tie on the <i>sacred thread-</i>girdle.</p>
