 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Of mankind which are more conversant with good and evil?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'Of mankind <i>he</i> whose sojourn <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_568"><font size="1">3</font></a> and business are with the bad <a name="fr_577"></a><a href="#fn_569"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and they provide him a name for good repute and goodness, is the man more conversant with good. 5. And he whose sojourn and business are with the good <a name="fr_578"></a><a href="#fn_570"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and they provide him a name for disrepute, is the man more conversant with evil.</p>  
 <p>6. 'Because it is said, (7, 8) that whoever joins with the good brings good with <i>him</i>, and whoever</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a></p>  
 <p>joins with the bad <i>brings</i> <a name="fr_579"></a><a href="#fn_571"><font size="1">1</font></a> evil(9) just like the wind which, when it impinges on stench, is <a name="fr_580"></a><a href="#fn_572"><font size="1">2</font></a> stench, (10) and when it impinges on perfume, is perfume,(11) <i>it</i> is, therefore, notorious <a name="fr_581"></a><a href="#fn_573"><font size="1">3</font></a>, (12) that he whose business is with the good receives good, (13) and <i>he</i> whose business is with the bad <i>receives</i> <a name="fr_582"></a><a href="#fn_574"><font size="1">4</font></a> evil; (14) but, even then, both are to be considered as an experiment (aû<i>z</i>mâyi<i>s</i>nŏ) <a name="fr_583"></a><a href="#fn_575"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_568"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_576">106:3</a> Reading ni<i>s</i>astŏ. L19 has 'whose business is most (vê<i>s</i>-ast),<font size="-1" color="green">'</font> both here and in § 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_569"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_577">106:4</a> L19 has 'the good.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_570"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_578">106:5</a> L19 has 'the bad.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_571"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_579">107:1</a> L19 has 'will bring with <i>him</i>' in both clauses, but the repetition is unnecessary in Pahlavi.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_572"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_580">107:2</a> L19 has 'will bring with <i>it</i>,' both here and in § 10.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_573"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_581">107:3</a> L19 has 'proper to know.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_574"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_582">107:4</a> K43 does not repeat this verb.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_575"></a><a href="sbe24066.htm#fr_583">107:5</a> L19 has 'by the result (a<i>n</i><i>z</i>âme<i>s</i>n).' The meaning is that, though a man's character is generally in accordance with the company he keeps, this must not be assumed without proof; and when the contrary is the case, as stated in §§ 4, 5, his own disposition must be of a very decided nature. Nêryôsang seems to have misunderstood the author's argument, and, supposing §§ 6-13 to contain a mere illustration of §§ 4, 5, he considered it necessary to transpose 'the bad' and 'the good' in §§ 4, 5, so as to make the illustration applicable.</p>
