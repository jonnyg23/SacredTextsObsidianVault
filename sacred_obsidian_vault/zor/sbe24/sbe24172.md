 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-fourth subject is this, when they wish to sleep, it is requisite to utter one Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <i>and</i> one Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_1526"></a><a href="#fn_1515"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and to accomplish repentance <i>one is</i> to speak thus: 'I am sorrowing for, and repentant and in renunciation of all that sin which I have spoken and <i>was</i> imagined by</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_349"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 349</font></a></p>  
 <p>me, and <i>has</i> assailed me; of these actions I am in renunciation <a name="fr_1527"></a><a href="#fn_1516"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>2. Afterwards <i>one is</i> to lie down; and every time that <i>one</i> acts <i>in</i> the manner that I have mentioned, and wears the <i>sacred thread-</i>girdle on the waistwhile <i>he is</i> equally sharing the whole of the good works which they are performing in all the world during that night, <i>and he is</i> of similar merit <a name="fr_1528"></a><a href="#fn_1517"><font size="1">2</font></a>every single breath that he inhales and exhales is a good work of a weight of three dirhams. 3. <i>And</i> when he turns from side to side he <i>should, in</i> like manner, recite one Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_1529"></a><a href="#fn_1518"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>
