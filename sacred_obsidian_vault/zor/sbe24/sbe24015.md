 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Is it possible to go from region to region <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_202"><font size="1">6</font></a>,or not?</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 36</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 3. From what substance is the sky made? 4. And how and in what manner is the mingling of the water in the earth?'</p>  
 <p>5. The spirit of wisdom answered (6) thus: 'Without the permission of the sacred beings, or the permission of the demons, it is then <a name="fr_206"></a><a href="#fn_203"><font size="1">1</font></a> not possible for one to go from region to region <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>7. 'The sky is made from the substance of the blood-stone <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_205"><font size="1">3</font></a>, such as they also call diamond (almâst).</p>  
 <p>8. 'And the mingling of the water in the earth is just like the blood in the body of man.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_202"></a><a href="sbe24015.htm#fr_205">35:6</a> The earth is supposed to be divided into seven regions, of which the central one is as large as the other six united; two of <a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 36</font></a> the six lie to the north, two to the south, one to the east, and one to the west; and they are said to be separated by seas or mountains, difficult to cross (see Bd. XI). For their names, see Chaps. XVI, 10, XXVII, 40.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_203"></a><a href="sbe24015.htm#fr_206">36:1</a> Instead of adîna<i>s</i>, 'then for one,' Nêr. has manufactured a word ainâ, 'otherwise' (see also Chap. VII, 19 n).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_204"></a><a href="sbe24015.htm#fr_207">36:2</a> This information is derived from Pahl. Vend. I, 4 <i>a</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_205"></a><a href="sbe24015.htm#fr_208">36:3</a> Or 'ruby,' referring to the rosy tints of dawn and sunset. The same statement is made in Bd. XII, 6. Nêr. has 'steel,' and the word can be translated 'blood-metal.'</p>
