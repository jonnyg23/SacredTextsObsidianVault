 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore is he who is an ill-natured man no friend of the good, <i>nor</i> an untalented man of a talented <i>one?</i>'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'For this reason, because he who is an ill-talented <a name="fr_519"></a><a href="#fn_512"><font size="1">2</font></a> man is <i>at</i> [all] <a name="fr_520"></a><a href="#fn_513"><font size="1">3</font></a> times <i>in</i> fear of the talented, (5) lest "they should trouble <a name="fr_521"></a><a href="#fn_514"><font size="1">4</font></a> us by <i>their</i> skill and talent, and, owing to that circumstance, shame <i>may</i> come upon <i>us</i> before the good and <i>our</i> opponents."</p>  
 <p>6. 'And the ill-natured are no friends of the good for this reason, because <i>there</i> is a time for their annihilation and destruction by the hands of the good.'</p>
