 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The thirty-eighth subject is this, that, so far as effort and endeavour prevail <a name="fr_1294"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">1</font></a>, it is requisite to abstain from the same cup as those of a different religion, and it is not desirable to drink the water of any goblet of theirs. 2. And if the goblet be of copper or of tin <a name="fr_1295"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">2</font></a>, it is requisite <a name="fr_1296"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">3</font></a> to wash <i>it</i> with <a name="fr_1297"></a><a href="#fn_1287"><font size="1">4</font></a> water, so that it <i>may</i> be proper to drink the water <a name="fr_1298"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 3. If the goblet be of earthenware <i>or</i> wooden, it is altogether improper <a name="fr_1299"></a><a href="#fn_1289"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. Because, when <a name="fr_1300"></a><a href="#fn_1290"><font size="1">7</font></a> any one drinks with a stranger, it makes <i>his</i> heart inclined (maîl) towards him, for it would be a sin; and, on account of the sin committed, he becomes bold, and <i>his</i> soul has an inclination for wickedness.</p>
