 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The thirty-third subject is this, that, when <i>there</i> is a place and any risk <i>or</i> fear exists that a corpse <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1253"><font size="1">2</font></a> is concealed beneath the ground, <i>one is</i> to make <i>it</i> apparent <i>and</i> visible <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1254"><font size="1">3</font></a>, because it is a great good work.</p>  
 <p>2. For it is declared in the good religion, that, <i>when</i> they conceal a corpse beneath the ground, Spendârma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1255"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the archangel, shudders; it is just as severe as a serpent <i>or</i> scorpion would be to any one in private sleep <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1256"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and <a href="errata.htm#5">is also</a> just like that to the ground. 3. When thou makest a corpse</p>
