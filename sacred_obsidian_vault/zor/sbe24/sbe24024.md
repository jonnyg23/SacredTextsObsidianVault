 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore do people consider these very little,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  
 <p>these four things which it is necessary for them to consider more, as warnings (dakhshak), (3) the changeableness of the things of the worldly <i>existence</i>, the death of the body, the account of the soul <a name="fr_271"></a><a href="#fn_267"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>and</i> the fear of hell?'</p>  
 <p>4. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: 'On account of the delusiveness (nîyâ<i>z</i>ânîh) of the demon of greediness <a name="fr_272"></a><a href="#fn_268"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and of discontent.'</p>
