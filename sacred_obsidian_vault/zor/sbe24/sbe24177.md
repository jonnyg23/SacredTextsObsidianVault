 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-ninth subject is this, that <a name="fr_1551"></a><a href="#fn_1540"><font size="1">3</font></a> Hôrmazd keeps watch when any one, through imposition (hîlat) <i>and</i> unawares, eats dead matter, <i>or</i> gives <i>it to</i> one of the good religion, or throws dead matter upon one of the good religion. 2. While his will and command are, <i>that</i> it is necessary that <i>such a person should</i> undergo the Bareshnûm <i>ceremony</i> <a name="fr_1552"></a><a href="#fn_1541"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and accomplish repentance <a name="fr_1553"></a><a href="#fn_1542"><font size="1">5</font></a> before the <i>spiritual</i> chiefs and high-priests. 3. So that, after that, <i>one may</i> indicate to him the sin in these <i>actions, and</i> he <i>may</i> perform the retribution which the high-priest mentions, in order that, owing to this, his sin <i>may</i> depart.</p>
