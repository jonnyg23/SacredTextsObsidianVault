 <p><a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 296</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The thirty-fifth subject is this, that, when they wish to wash the face <a name="fr_1273"></a><a href="#fn_1263"><font size="1">1</font></a>, they <i>should</i> recite ore Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_1274"></a><a href="#fn_1264"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i> set the mouth firmly closed, so that the water, not staying <i>away</i> from it <a name="fr_1275"></a><a href="#fn_1265"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>shall</i> not go into the mouth. 2. <i>And</i>, as <i>one</i> washes over the face, they <i>should</i> recite the K<i>e</i>m-nâ-mazdâ <a name="fr_1276"></a><a href="#fn_1266"><font size="1">4</font></a>, so that the fiend Nasru<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1277"></a><a href="#fn_1267"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>may</i> become smitten.</p>
