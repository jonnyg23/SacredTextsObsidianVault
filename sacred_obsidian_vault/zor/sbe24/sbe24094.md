 <p><a name="page_264"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 264</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sixth subject is this, that of the many good works there are those which, when they accomplish <i>them</i>, obtain great (ha<i>th</i>îm) rewards; <i>and</i> if <i>one</i> does not perform <i>them</i> severe punishment seizes upon <i>one</i> at the head of the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge <a name="fr_1119"></a><a href="#fn_1111"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 2. One is <i>the celebration of</i> the season festivals <a name="fr_1120"></a><a href="#fn_1112"><font size="1">2</font></a>; the second is keeping <i>the days of</i> the guardian spirits <a name="fr_1121"></a><a href="#fn_1113"><font size="1">3</font></a>; the third is <i>attending to</i> the souls of fathers, mothers, <i>and</i> other relations <a name="fr_1122"></a><a href="#fn_1114"><font size="1">4</font></a>; the fourth is <i>reciting</i> the Khûrshê<i>d</i> Nyâyi<i>s</i> <a name="fr_1123"></a><a href="#fn_1115"><font size="1">5</font></a> three times every day; the fifth is <i>reciting</i> the Mâh Nyâyi<i>s</i> <a name="fr_1124"></a><a href="#fn_1116"><font size="1">6</font></a> three times every month, once when it becomes new, once when it becomes full, and once when it becomes slender <a name="fr_1125"></a><a href="#fn_1117"><font size="1">7</font></a>; <i>and</i> the sixth is celebrating the Rapithwin <a name="fr_1126"></a><a href="#fn_1118"><font size="1">8</font></a> <i>ceremony</i> once every year. 3. If not able to celebrate <i>them</i> oneself, it is requisite to order <i>them</i>, so that they <i>may</i> celebrate them every single time <a name="fr_1127"></a><a href="#fn_1119"><font size="1">9</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. These six good works are things indispensable unto every one. 5. When any one <i>of them</i> is not <i>performed</i>be it that which, if omitted at its own time <a name="fr_1128"></a><a href="#fn_1120"><font size="1">10</font></a>, it is not possible to accomplish, or if it be that one time <i>one</i> omits an occasion, <i>and</i> another time <a name="fr_1129"></a><a href="#fn_1121"><font size="1">11</font></a> they accomplish twice as much<i>one should</i> consider <a name="fr_1130"></a><a href="#fn_1122"><font size="1">12</font></a> that as an advantage, <i>which</i> occurs in retribution</p>

 <p>for it, or <i>as</i> atonement for the transgression. 6. Because they call the transgression of each of these six a bridge-sin; that is, every one <i>through</i> whom a transgression of these may have arisen they keep back, at the head of the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge, till punishment for it happens to him, and no good work is possible <a name="fr_1131"></a><a href="#fn_1123"><font size="1">1</font></a> in this place, which is torment and punishment for him <a name="fr_1132"></a><a href="#fn_1124"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>7. Therefore it is necessary to make an effort, that they <i>may</i> be performed each one at its own time, so that they <i>may</i> obtain a recompense, <i>and</i> not a severe punishment.</p>
