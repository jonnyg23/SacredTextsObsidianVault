 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER L <a name="fr_1346"></a><a href="#fn_1335"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fiftieth subject is this, that, every day <i>at</i> dawn, when they rise up from sleep, it is not proper to wash the hands first with water.</p>  
 <p>2. The <i>ceremonial</i> ablution is to wash the hands, face, nose, eyes, and feet thoroughly, either with fruit (mîvah) <a name="fr_1347"></a><a href="#fn_1336"><font size="1">2</font></a> or some grass upon which no water has come; afterwards, to make <i>them</i> dry, <i>and</i> to wash <i>them</i> three times with water <i>in</i> such a manner that it becomes wet from the face as far as the ear <a name="fr_1348"></a><a href="#fn_1337"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 3. It is necessary to keep the mouth closed, so that the water <i>shall</i> not go into the mouth; for if a drop, owing to not stopping, goes into the mouth, it is a sin of three hundred stîrs <a name="fr_1349"></a><a href="#fn_1338"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 4. Afterwards, <i>one is</i> to wash the hands three times with water, as far as the <i>upper</i> arms; first the right hand, <i>and</i> afterwards the left hand; and, <i>in</i> like manner, <i>he is</i> to wash the right foot and left foot.</p>

 <p>5. Then they recite the K<i>e</i>m-nâ-mazdâ <a name="fr_1350"></a><a href="#fn_1339"><font size="1">1</font></a>, for, every time that they wish to recite anything as an <i>inward</i> prayer, it is necessary that the hands be washed with <i>ceremonial</i> ablution <a name="fr_1351"></a><a href="#fn_1340"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and, if <i>they are</i> not, the Avesta is not accepted, and the fiend of corruption (nasu<i>s</i>) does not rush <i>away, and</i> it becomes a Tanâvar sin.</p>
