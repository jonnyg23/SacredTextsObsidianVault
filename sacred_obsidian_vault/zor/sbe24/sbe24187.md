 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XCIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninety-ninth subject is this, that it is not proper for officiating priests, high-priests, <i>spiritual</i> chiefs, and priests, that they teach Pahlavi to every one.</p>  
 <p>2. For Zaratu<i>s</i>t enquired of Hôrmazd thus: 'To whom is it proper to teach Pahlavi?' 3. <i>And</i> Hôrmazd, the good <i>and</i> propitious, gave a reply thus: '<i>To</i> every one who is of thy family (nasl), an officiating priest, a high-priest, a <i>spiritual</i> chief, and every one who is an intelligent priest. 4. <a name="fr_1583"></a><a href="#fn_1572"><font size="1">4</font></a> Besides these that I have mentioned, if <i>one</i> teaches <i>it</i> to others <a name="fr_1584"></a><a href="#fn_1573"><font size="1">5</font></a> it is a great sin for him; <i>and</i> if he has performed many duties <i>and</i> good works, the end for him may <i>still</i> be hell.'</p>
