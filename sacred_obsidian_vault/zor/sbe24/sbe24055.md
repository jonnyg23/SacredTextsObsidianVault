 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: '<i>As to</i> these stars which are apparent in the sky, and their number is so great, what is then their duty and influence? 3. And how is the motion of the sun and moon and stars?'</p>  
 <p>4. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: 'Of the stars which are in the sky the first star is Tî<i>s</i>tar <a name="fr_479"></a><a href="#fn_474"><font size="1">4</font></a>, which is said <i>to be</i> great and good, more valuable and more glorious <a name="fr_480"></a><a href="#fn_475"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 6. And prosperity of every kind and the fertility of the world are in the path of Tî<i>s</i>tar.</p>  
 <p>7, 8. 'And the star of water germs is for the increase of the star of plant germs <a name="fr_481"></a><a href="#fn_476"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 9, 10. And the star of plant germs is for the increase of cattle germs <a name="fr_482"></a><a href="#fn_477"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>

 <p>12. 'And the star Vanand <a name="fr_485"></a><a href="#fn_480"><font size="1">3</font></a> is intrusted with the passes <i>and</i> gates of Albûr<i>z</i> <a name="fr_486"></a><a href="#fn_481"><font size="1">4</font></a>; (13) so that the demons and witches and fiends <i>may</i> turn from those gates and passes, (14) that <i>it may</i> not <i>be</i> possible for them to cut off and break up the road and passage of the sun and moon and stars <a name="fr_487"></a><a href="#fn_482"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>15. Its motion, also, is round about hell; (18) and its special business is this, as <i>it were</i> it holds the twelve signs of the zodiac by the hand, in <i>their</i> proper going and corning. 19. And those twelve constellations also proceed <i>in</i> like manner by the power and help of Haptôk-ring <a name="fr_491"></a><a href="#fn_486"><font size="1">1</font></a>; (20) and every single constellation, when it comes in at Albûr<i>z</i>, provides support for Haptôk-ring <a name="fr_492"></a><a href="#fn_487"><font size="1">2</font></a>, (21) and begs protection from Haptôk-ring.</p>  
 <p>22. 'The remaining unnumbered and innumerable constellations <a name="fr_493"></a><a href="#fn_488"><font size="1">3</font></a> which are apparent are said <i>to be</i> the guardian spirits of the worldly <i>existences</i>. 23. Because, <i>as to</i> the creatures and creations of every kind, that the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> created for the worldly <i>existence</i>, which are procreative and also which are developable (ârôdi<i>s</i>nîk) <a name="fr_494"></a><a href="#fn_489"><font size="1">4</font></a>, for every single body <i>there</i> is apparent its own single guardian spirit of a like nature.</p>  
 <p>24. 'And the motion of the sun and moon is the special illumination of the world, (25) and the maturing of procreations and growths of all kinds. 26. And the correct keeping of the day, month, and year, summer and winter, spring and autumn, and other calculations and accounts of all kinds which men ought to obtain, perceive, and understand, (27)</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_474"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_479">90:4</a> Av. Ti<i>s</i>trya, the eastern leader of the stars and special opponent of the planet Tîr (Mercury), which can be identified only with Sirius. It is personified as an angel who contends with the demon of drought and produces rain (see Bd. II, 7, V, 1, VII, 1-13).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_475"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_480">90:5</a> The usual Avesta epithets of Tî<i>s</i>tar are 'the radiant and glorious.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_476"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_481">90:6</a> L19 has 'for the increase of water. And the star of earth germs is for the increase of earth.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_477"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_482">90:7</a> L19 has 'for the increase of plants. And the star of cattle germs is for the increase of cattle.' The stars of water, earth, and plant germs are mentioned in the formula of dedication to Tî<i>s</i>tar (Sîr. 13), and the moon is said to possess the germs of cattle (Sîr. 12). <a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 91</font></a> Reference is also made to all of them in Rashnu Yt. 29-31, 33, and to those of water germs in Vend. XXI, 33, Tî<i>s</i>tar Yt. 39, 45, 46.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_478"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_483">91:1</a> L19 has 'earth.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_479"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_484">91:2</a> L19 has 'for the increase of men.' As both the Pahlavi and Pâzand versions of §§ 7-11 are complete and consistent in themselves, it is uncertain which of them gives the original text. The Pâzand corresponds more closely to certain passages in the Avesta, but a wish to produce such a correspondence may have led Nêryôsang to alter the text. That the Pahlavi writer was thinking of some other passage, as yet unidentified, is evident from the omission of the star Satavês (which follows Tî<i>s</i>tar in Sîr. 13) and from the details he gives concerning the others.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_480"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_485">91:3</a> The southern leader of the stars and special opponent of the planet Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> (Jupiter), which is perhaps best identified with Fomalhaut (see Bd. II, 7, V, 1). The Avesta mentions it in connection with Tî<i>s</i>tar (Sîr. 13).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_481"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_486">91:4</a> See Chap. XLIV, 16 n.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_482"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_487">91:5</a> Which are supposed to rise and set through openings or passes in the mountain range of Albûr<i>z</i>, which encircles the world (see Bd. V, 5).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_483"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_488">91:6</a> Av. Haptôiri<i>n</i>ga, the northern leader of the stars and special opponent of the planet Vâhrâm (Mars), which corresponds to Ursa Major (see Bd. II, 7, V, 1). The Avesta mentions it, in connection with the other stars named in the text, in Sîr. 13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_484"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_489">91:7</a> See Fravar<i>d</i>in Yt. 60. The number here mentioned is that generally used in the Avesta to express an indefinitely large number.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_485"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_490">91:8</a> Which is supposed to be in the north, so that the circumpolar <a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a> constellation of Ursa Major seems to revolve around it, and to remain on the watch.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_486"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_491">92:1</a> Written Haptâôrig in §§19-21 in K43.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_487"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_492">92:2</a> L19 has 'holds to Haptôk-ring by the hand.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_488"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_493">92:3</a> L19 has 'stars.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_489"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_494">92:4</a> L19 has azâi<i>s</i>nî, 'unprocreative.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_490"></a><a href="sbe24055.htm#fr_495">93:1</a> Nêr. reads vah<i>e</i><i>z</i>a and translates 'new year's day.'</p>
