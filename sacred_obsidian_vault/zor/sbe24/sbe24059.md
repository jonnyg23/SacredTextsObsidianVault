 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'How are the homage and glorifying of the sacred beings to be performed?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus:</p>  
 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'Every day three times, standing opposite the sun and Mit<i>r</i>ô <a name="fr_508"></a><a href="#fn_501"><font size="1">1</font></a>, as they proceed together <a name="fr_509"></a><a href="#fn_502"><font size="1">2</font></a>, (5) and <a name="fr_510"></a><a href="#fn_503"><font size="1">3</font></a> the moon and fire of Vâhrâm <a name="fr_511"></a><a href="#fn_504"><font size="1">4</font></a>, or the fire of fires <a name="fr_512"></a><a href="#fn_505"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>in</i> like manner, morning, noon, and evening, homage and glorifying are performed, (6) and <i>one has</i> become grateful <a name="fr_513"></a><a href="#fn_506"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 7. And if a sin, or a deficiency (frô<i>d</i>mând-1) <a name="fr_514"></a><a href="#fn_507"><font size="1">7</font></a>, <i>has</i> occurred, especially <a name="fr_515"></a><a href="#fn_508"><font size="1">8</font></a> as regards the angels of the spiritual and worldly <i>existences</i>, then and beasts <i>of burden</i> <a name="fr_516"></a><a href="#fn_509"><font size="1">9</font></a>, oxen and sheep, dogs and the dog species, and other creatures and creations of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> the lord, (8) <i>one</i> is to become sorrowful, penitent, and in renunciation <i>of sin</i> before the sun and Mit<i>r</i>ô, the moon and the fire of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_517"></a><a href="#fn_510"><font size="1">10</font></a>; (9) and, <i>for</i> the sake of atonement for the sin, good works are to be practised as much as is well possible.'</p>
