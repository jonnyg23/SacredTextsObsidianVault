 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore <i>is it</i> when there are <i>instances</i> when a lazy, ignorant, and bad man attains to eminence and great welfare, (3) and there are <i>instances</i> when a worthy, wise, and good man attains to grievous misery, perplexity, and indigence?'</p>  
 <p>4. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: '<i>As to</i> him who is a lazy, ignorant, and bad man, when his destiny becomes a helper, that laziness of his then becomes like unto diligence, that ignorance unto <a name="fr_496"></a><a href="#fn_491"><font size="1">2</font></a> knowledge, and that vileness unto <a name="fr_497"></a><a href="#fn_491"><font size="1">2</font></a> goodness. 6. And <i>as to</i> him who is a wise, worthy, and good man, when his destiny is an opponent, that wisdom of his then turns to stupidity and foolishness (alakîh), and that worthiness to ignorance; (7) and <i>his</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 94</font></a></p>  
 <p>knowledge, skill, and worthiness become manifestly secluded <a name="fr_498"></a><a href="#fn_492"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>
