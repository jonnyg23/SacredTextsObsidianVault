 <p><a name="page_354"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 354</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XC.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninetieth subject is this, that it is not proper that they <i>should</i> give anything to a sinful person <i>or one</i> worthy of death, because <a name="fr_1554"></a><a href="#fn_1543"><font size="1">1</font></a> it is like that they have placed in the jaws of a destructive serpent (a<i>z</i>dahâ). 2. And, if this be food which he devours <i>and</i> they give, they pass into the committing of sin; <i>and</i> that person <i>who</i> may have given food to him is a participater with him. 3. In eating food, if <i>there</i> be no danger and fear of them <a name="fr_1555"></a><a href="#fn_1544"><font size="1">2</font></a>, it is not desirable to give anything to them, for it would be a great sin.</p>
