 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sixty-eighth subject is this, that for a menstruous woman who casts an eye upon a fire it is a sin of twelve dirhams <i>in</i> weight <a name="fr_1442"></a><a href="#fn_1431"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and if she goes <i>within</i> three steps of the fire it is a sin of a thousand <i>and</i> two hundred dirhams <a name="fr_1443"></a><a href="#fn_1432"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>in</i> weight for her; and when she puts her hand <i>to</i> the fire it is a sin of fifteen Tanâvars for her.</p>  
 <p>2. In like manner, if she takes a look <i>at</i> running water it is a sin of twelve dirhams <i>in</i> weight for her <a name="fr_1444"></a><a href="#fn_1433"><font size="1">6</font></a>; if she goes <i>within</i> fifteen steps of running water it is a sin of fifteen dirhams <i>in</i> weight for her; and when she sits down in running water it is a sin of fifteen Tanâvars for her. 3. And when she walks in the rain, through every drop that drops upon her limbs <i>there</i> arises a sin of one Tanâvar for her.</p>  
 <p>4. <i>If</i> she comes to a Khûrshê<i>d</i> Nyâyi<i>s</i> <a name="fr_1445"></a><a href="#fn_1434"><font size="1">7</font></a>, to observe <i>it</i>, it is not proper <i>for her</i> to speak a word with a</p>

 <p>righteous man <a name="fr_1446"></a><a href="#fn_1435"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 5. It is not proper <i>for her</i> to put a bare foot on the ground. 6. It is not proper <i>for her</i> to eat any food with a <i>bare</i> hand; it is not proper <i>for her</i> to eat bread <i>when</i> satisfied <a name="fr_1447"></a><a href="#fn_1436"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 7. It is not proper <i>for</i> two menstruous women to eat together; it is not proper for them to sleep <i>so</i> <a name="fr_1448"></a><a href="#fn_1437"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. And so long as three days <i>have</i> not elapsed it is not proper to wash the hands <a name="fr_1449"></a><a href="#fn_1438"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and three days after that, if she <i>has</i> perceived herself clean, it is requisite to remain another day, and <i>so</i> until the lapse of nine days, <i>when</i>, if she <i>has</i> perceived herself clean, it is not necessary <i>to remain</i> to the end of <a name="fr_1450"></a><a href="#fn_1439"><font size="1">5</font></a> another <i>interval of</i> time. 9. If menstruation occurs <i>for</i> twenty-nine days, it is necessary <a name="fr_1451"></a><a href="#fn_1440"><font size="1">6</font></a> to consider that she is menstruous a second time, and during three other days it is not proper to wash again, and it is necessary to exercise care, just like that which <a name="fr_1452"></a><a href="#fn_1441"><font size="1">7</font></a> I first wrote <i>about</i>.</p>  
 <p>10. If she be doubtful whether menstruation is come to her, it is requisite <i>for her</i> to strip off <i>her</i> dress, and then to take notice if she has become menstruous, <i>or if</i> the dress that is stripped off be clean. 11. If she has an infant <i>to feed</i> with <i>her</i> milk,</p>

 <p><i>one</i> puts away the dress from the infant, and gives <i>it to</i> the mother till it <i>has</i> fed <i>on</i> the milk, <i>and</i> then it is proper to give <i>it</i> to other persons. 12. It is pure, but when the mother washes <i>her</i> head she also washes <a name="fr_1453"></a><a href="#fn_1442"><font size="1">1</font></a> the head of the infant.</p>  
 <p>13. And it is necessary <i>for</i> a menstruous <i>woman</i> that she <i>should</i> not pass by the end of <a name="fr_1454"></a><a href="#fn_1443"><font size="1">2</font></a> anything ceremonially washed, for, if it be a thousand cubits (gaz) <i>in length</i>, she makes the whole <i>of it</i> polluted, <i>and</i> it becomes unclean. 14. With any one <i>who</i> holds a sacred-twig stand <a name="fr_1455"></a><a href="#fn_1444"><font size="1">3</font></a> she <i>should</i> not speak a word; <i>and</i> if a priest holds the sacred twigs in <i>his</i> hand, <i>and</i> a menstruous <i>woman</i> speaks some (bahʓ̣ê) words <a name="fr_1456"></a><a href="#fn_1445"><font size="1">4</font></a> from afar, or he walks within three steps of a menstruous woman, she makes <i>it</i> <a name="fr_1457"></a><a href="#fn_1446"><font size="1">5</font></a> unclean.</p>
