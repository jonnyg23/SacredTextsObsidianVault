 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventy-first subject is this, that, forasmuch as it is not desirable <i>for</i> any one to eat dead matter for the sake of medicine and remedy, <i>let them</i> beware (zinhâr) when they eat it <a name="fr_1470"></a><a href="#fn_1459"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. For it asserts, in the commentary of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1471"></a><a href="#fn_1460"><font size="1">5</font></a>, that it is requisite to demolish the habitation, house, and abode of any one <i>who</i> has <a name="fr_1472"></a><a href="#fn_1461"><font size="1">6</font></a> eaten dead matter, and to fetch his heart out of <i>his</i> body, and it is necessary to scoop out his eyes. 3. And <i>along</i> with these torments, which they accomplish on him, his soul is in hell till the resurrection.</p>
