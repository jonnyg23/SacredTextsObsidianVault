 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER I.</h3>  
 <p>1. The first subject is this, that it is necessary that they become steadfast in the religion, and do not introduce any hesitation (<i>s</i>akk) and doubt into the heart. 2. And <i>that</i> they make a statement (haqîqat) with confidence (ihtiqâd), that the good religion, the true and perfect, <i>which</i> the Lord sent into the world (<i>h</i>alq), is that which Zaratu<i>s</i>t has brought; which is this I hold <a name="fr_1086"></a><a href="#fn_1078"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. Every time <a name="fr_1087"></a><a href="#fn_1079"><font size="1">5</font></a> that mankind are like this, and do</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_258"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 258</font></a></p>  
 <p>not introduce any hesitation <i>and</i> doubt into the heart, of every duty <i>and</i> good work that <i>others</i> have done, from the days of Zaratu<i>s</i>t until these days, and of whatever <i>one</i> does after this until the resurrection, <i>there</i> is a share <a name="fr_1088"></a><a href="#fn_1080"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>for</i> that person. 4. When the soul, on the fourth night <a name="fr_1089"></a><a href="#fn_1081"><font size="1">2</font></a>, arrives at the head of the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge, the angel Mihir and the angel Rashn <a name="fr_1090"></a><a href="#fn_1082"><font size="1">3</font></a> make up its account (hisâb) and reckoning. 5. And, if the good works it has done <i>be</i> deficient <i>in</i> quantity, of every duty <i>and</i> good work that those of the good religion have done <i>in</i> the earth of seven regions they appoint it a like portion (naʓîb), till the good works become more in weight <a name="fr_1091"></a><a href="#fn_1083"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and the soul arrives righteous in the radiant locality of heaven.</p>  
 <p>6. For it is declared in revelation, that of the duty <a name="fr_1092"></a><a href="#fn_1084"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>and</i> good work which they perform in doubtthat is (yahnî), they entertain a suspicion like this, that 'I do not know that this faith, which I possess, is better <i>in comparison</i> with other faiths'no merit whatever comes to their souls. 7. Therefore, the first (avval) thing is to become steadfast in the religion; and this is the chief of all good works.</p>
