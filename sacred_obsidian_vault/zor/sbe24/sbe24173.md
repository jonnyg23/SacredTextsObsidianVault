 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-fifth subject is this, that, <i>in</i> every matter that comes forward, it is necessary to enquire of the wise and relations, so as to have their advice, and not to transact any business according to one's own idea <i>and</i> opinion.</p>  
 <p>2. For it is declared in revelation, that the sacred being, the good <i>and</i> propitious, spoke to Zaratu<i>s</i>t thus: '<i>As to</i> every business that thou wishest to transact, do thou receive wisdom and knowledge at one place with the wise <i>who</i> reply, and cast away <i>what is</i> unconsidered, so that Aharman <i>may</i> not reach <i>it</i> midway, and injury (<i>h</i>alal) <i>may</i> not occur to that business.'</p>  
 <p><a name="page_350"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 350</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. <i>In</i> like manner the archangel Spendârma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1530"></a><a href="#fn_1519"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>at</i> the time when <i>her</i> gaze passed on to Minô<i>k</i>ihar <a name="fr_1531"></a><a href="#fn_1520"><font size="1">2</font></a>, issued to him this admonition <i>and</i> precept (vaʓîyat), <i>and</i> said: 'O Minô<i>k</i>ihar! although <i>there</i> be deliberation <i>in</i> an affair, this may be no reason for it as regards the spirits <a name="fr_1532"></a><a href="#fn_1521"><font size="1">3</font></a>; although a horse may be good, <i>there</i> may be no resource <i>except</i> a whip for it; and although <i>one</i> may be a wise man, <i>there</i> should be no retreat on his part from having advice, so that his business <i>may</i> become complete.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1520"></a><a href="sbe24173.htm#fr_1531">350:2</a> Pahl. Mânû<i>s</i><i>k</i>îhar (see Mkh. XXVII, 41 n). It appears from Sls. X, 28, where a portion of this tale is quoted, that it comes originally from the <i>K</i>î<i>d</i>ra<i>s</i>t Nask.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1521"></a><a href="sbe24173.htm#fr_1532">350:3</a> B29 has 'although a knife be sharp, <i>there</i> may be no resource except a whetstone for it,' which follows the next clause in Sls. X, 28. In the original text this change of meaning is produced by a difference in only four words, and the author of the Sad Dar has probably misunderstood the Pahlavi original when translating it.</p>
