 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XCIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninety-third subject is this, that it is necessary for all those of the good religion to practise abstinence from uttering slander (<i>gh</i>aîbat) behind <i>one's</i> back <a name="fr_1564"></a><a href="#fn_1553"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. In the commentary of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> it states, that <a name="fr_1565"></a><a href="#fn_1554"><font size="1">3</font></a> slander is the greatest of all sins. 3. Every one who perpetrates slander <i>about</i> any one is like him who has eaten dead matterand the eating of dead matter is a sin <a name="fr_1566"></a><a href="#fn_1555"><font size="1">4</font></a> <i>that</i> has likewise been mentioned, before this, in this book <a name="fr_1567"></a><a href="#fn_1556"><font size="1">5</font></a>but the statement is like this, while they do not indicate <i>any</i> punishment for it in this world <a name="fr_1568"></a><a href="#fn_1557"><font size="1">6</font></a>, it does not go <i>without it</i> in that <i>other</i> world. 4. Therefore it is necessary for those of the good religion to make an effort, so that they <i>may</i> guard themselves from this slander.</p>  
 <p>5. In order that they <i>may</i> show thy soul, <i>when</i> <a name="fr_1569"></a><a href="#fn_1558"><font size="1">7</font></a> resigning life, the satisfaction (i<i>g</i>zâ) for the sin, it</p>
