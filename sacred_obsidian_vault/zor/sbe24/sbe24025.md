 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Is living in fear <i>and</i> falsehood worse, or death?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'To live in fear and falsehood is worse than death. 5. Because every one's life is necessary for the enjoyment <i>and</i> pleasure of the worldly <i>existence</i>, (6) and when the enjoyment <i>and</i> pleasure of the worldly <i>existence</i> are not his, and fear and even falsehood <a name="fr_273"></a><a href="#fn_269"><font size="1">3</font></a> are with him, it is called worse than death.'</p>
