 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XCVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninety-seventh subject is this, that it is expedient for <a name="fr_1579"></a><a href="#fn_1568"><font size="1">1</font></a> those of the good religion, that they converse, according to their own ability, <i>in</i> the presence of officiating priests, high-priests, <i>spiritual</i> chiefs, and priests, and hearken cordially to whatever they say. 2. And they <i>should</i> understand <i>their</i> statements, and, during them, they <i>should</i> not utter any reply <i>or</i> question (sûâl).</p>  
 <p>3. For in the commentary of the Avesta it says, <i>as to</i> every one who brings altercation (hu<i>g</i><i>g</i>at) into any statement of the elders of the religion, '<i>one</i> breaks out his tongue, or he goes out from this world abortively (muba<i>t</i><i>t</i>alâ).'</p>
