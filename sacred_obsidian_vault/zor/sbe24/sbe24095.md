 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventh subject is this, that, when a sneeze (ha<i>t</i>sat) comes forth from any one, it is requisite to recite one Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <a name="fr_1133"></a><a href="#fn_1125"><font size="1">3</font></a> and one Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_1134"></a><a href="#fn_1126"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 2. Because there is a fiend in our bodies, and she is an adversary who is connected with mankind, and strives so that she <i>may</i> make misfortune (hillat) and sickness predominant (mustaûlî) over mankind. 3 And in our bodies there is a fire which they call a dispositionin Arabic they say <i>t</i>abîhatand they call <i>it</i> the sneezing instinct (<i>gh</i>arîzî). 4. <i>It is</i> connected with that fiend, <i>and</i> they wage warfare, and it keeps her away from the body of man. 5. Then, as the fire becomes successful over</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_266"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 266</font></a></p>  
 <p>that fiend, <i>and</i> puts her to flight (hazîmat), a sneeze comes because that fiend comes out.</p>  
 <p>6. Afterwards, because it is necessary, they recite these <i>inward</i> prayers <a name="fr_1135"></a><a href="#fn_1127"><font size="1">1</font></a> and perform the benediction (âfrîn) of the fire, so that it may remain <i>for</i> a long period while thou art keeping <a name="fr_1136"></a><a href="#fn_1128"><font size="1">2</font></a> this fiend defeated. 7. When another person hears the sneeze, it is likewise requisite for him to utter the said prayers, and to accomplish the benediction of that spirit <a name="fr_1137"></a><a href="#fn_1129"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>
