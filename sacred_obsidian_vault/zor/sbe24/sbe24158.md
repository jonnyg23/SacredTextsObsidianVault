 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventieth subject is this, that, <i>as to</i> any persons, when they carry a corpse to the appointed place, it is necessary that two suits of clothes be put on, on account of this work. 2. It is requisite <i>for</i> those clothes to be on <a name="fr_1461"></a><a href="#fn_1450"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and it is necessary <a name="fr_1462"></a><a href="#fn_1451"><font size="1">3</font></a> to make a dog gaze at the corpse twice, once <i>at</i> the time when life becomes separated <i>from</i> it, and once <i>at</i> the time when they wish to take <i>it</i> up.</p>  
 <p>3. Then it is necessary that both those persons be connected, <i>and each of them is</i> to tie a cord on one of his own hands, so that the hand <i>may</i> go away <a name="fr_1463"></a><a href="#fn_1452"><font size="1">4</font></a> from <i>that of</i> the other one. 4. And, when they are moving, it is necessary for him to be prepared and not to speak a word with any one. 5. And if it be a pregnant woman <i>they are</i> to take <i>her</i> up by four persons, because there are two corpses <a name="fr_1464"></a><a href="#fn_1453"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>6. When, avoiding dead matter, <i>one</i> comes again upon it, <i>he has</i>, in the end, to wash <a name="fr_1465"></a><a href="#fn_1454"><font size="1">6</font></a> with <i>ceremonial</i> ablution <a name="fr_1466"></a><a href="#fn_1455"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and that is requisite for the reason that he</p>

 <p>may not thereby be rapidly a <i>cause of</i> wickedness or death for any one <a name="fr_1467"></a><a href="#fn_1456"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>7. And if <i>one</i> does not show a dog to the corpse, <i>and</i> they take <i>it</i> up, how many soever <i>there</i> be, the whole <i>of them</i> become polluted <a name="fr_1468"></a><a href="#fn_1457"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 8. In the commentary of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> it is asserted, that every one who takes up a corpse that a dog <i>has</i> not seen is polluted and worthy of death <a name="fr_1469"></a><a href="#fn_1458"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and never becomes clean; his soul <i>also</i> would be wicked.</p>
