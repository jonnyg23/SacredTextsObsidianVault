 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventy-eighth subject is this, that in every habitation <i>where</i> any one departs, passing away from</p>  
 <p><a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 342</font></a></p>  
 <p>the world, it is necessary to endeavour that they <i>may</i> not eat and not consecrate fresh meat <i>for</i> three days therein <a name="fr_1496"></a><a href="#fn_1485"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 2. Because the danger is that some one else <i>may</i> depart, passing away; <i>so</i> the relations of that <i>former</i> person <i>should</i> not eat meat <i>for</i> three days.</p>
