 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The forty-fourth subject is this, that it is not desirable <i>for</i> those of the good religion, so far as they are able to manage <i>it</i>, to put a bare foot upon the ground <a name="fr_1329"></a><a href="#fn_1318"><font size="1">3</font></a>, because it is a sin, <i>and</i> injury <a name="fr_1330"></a><a href="#fn_1319"><font size="1">4</font></a> occurs to Spendârma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1331"></a><a href="#fn_1320"><font size="1">5</font></a>, the archangel. 2. And they call that the sin of running <i>about</i> uncovered.</p>
