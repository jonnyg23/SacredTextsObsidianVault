 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twenty-ninth subject is this, that, when they provide any munificence (sa<i>h</i>âvat) <i>or</i> liberality, it is necessary that they provide <i>it for</i> the worthy; and <i>one is</i> to consider thus: 'Is this person, to whom I am giving this thing, worthy or not?'</p>  
 <p>2. Therefore it is necessary to make an effort, so that they <i>may</i> not give to the unworthy. 3. For in revelation, as regards <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1245"><font size="1">2</font></a> that person who provides any munificence for the unworthy, they call <i>it</i> a vain work <i>and</i> a gift without advantage; and day by day <i>it</i> is <a name="fr_1256"></a><a href="#fn_1246"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_292"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 292</font></a></p>  
 <p>the punishment <i>and</i> torment of that person. 4. And, whatever they give to the unworthy, they have made that thing extinct.</p>
