 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: On account of the begging of favours, and the practice and worthiness of good works, do the sacred beings also grant anything to men otherwise <a name="fr_286"></a><a href="#fn_282"><font size="1">2</font></a>, or not?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'They grant; (5) for <i>there are</i> such as they call thus: "Destiny <i>and</i> divine providence." 6. Destiny is that which is ordained from the beginning, (7) <i>and</i> divine providence is that which they also grant otherwise. 8. But the sacred beings provide and manifest in the spiritual <i>existence</i> little of that grant, on this account, because Aharman, the wicked <a name="fr_287"></a><a href="#fn_283"><font size="1">3</font></a>, through the power of the seven planets extorts wealth, <i>and</i> also every other benefit of the worldly <i>existence</i>, from the good <i>and</i> worthy, <i>and</i> grants <i>them</i> more fully to the bad <i>and</i> unworthy.'</p>
