 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part III (SBE31)</i>, L.H. Mills, tr. [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">YASNA XIII (SP. XIV).</h3>  <h4 align="center">INVOCATIONS AND DEDICATIONS.</h4>  
 <p>1. I address (my invocation to) Ahura Mazda. And I invoke (among guardian beings) the chief <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">3</font></a> of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 251</font></a></p>  
 <p>the house-lord, and the chief of the Vîs-lord <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1046"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and the chief of the Za<i>n</i>tu-lord <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1047"><font size="1">2</font></a>. And I invoke the chief of the province-lord <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">3</font></a>. And the chief of women I invoke, the Mazdayasnian Faith, the blessed and good Pare<i>n</i>di <a name="fr_1055"></a><a href="#fn_1049"><font size="1">4</font></a>, her who is the holy one of human-kind <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">5</font></a>. And I invoke this (holy) earth which bears us.</p>  
 <p>2. And I invoke the friendly and most helpful person's <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">6</font></a> lord, the Fire of Ahura Mazda, and also the most energetic lords of holy men, those who are most strenuous <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1052"><font size="1">7</font></a> in their care of cattle and the fields, and the chief of the thrifty tiller of the earth. And I invoke the steady settler <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1053"><font size="1">8</font></a> of sanctity, (and) the chief of the charioteer.</p>  
 <p>3. And I invoke the chief of the fire-priest by means of the most imposing sciences of the Mazdayasnian Faith. And I invoke the chief of the Âtharvan, and his pupils I invoke; yea, the lords of each of them. I invoke these lords, and I summon the Bountiful Immortals here, and the Prophets who shall serve us, the wisest as they are, the most scrupulous</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 252</font></a></p>  
 <p>in their exactness (as) they utter words (of doctrine and of service), the most devoted (to their duties likewise), and the most glorious in their thoughts (?) <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1054"><font size="1">1</font></a>. And I invoke the most imposing forces of the Mazdayasnian Faith, and the fire-priests I invoke, and the charioteers, the warriors, and the thrifty tillers of the soil.</p>  
 <p>4. And to You, O Ye Bountiful Immortals! Ye who rule aright, and dispose (of all) aright, I offer the flesh of my very frame, and all the blessings of my life.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________________________</p>  
 <p>Thus <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1055"><font size="1">2</font></a> the two spirits <a name="fr_1062"></a><a href="#fn_1056"><font size="1">3</font></a> thought, thus they spoke, and thus they did;</p>  
 <p>5. And therefore as Thou, O Ahura Mazda! didst think, speak, dispose, and do all things good (for us), so to Thee would we give, so would we assign to Thee our homage; so would we worship Thee with our sacrifices. So would we bow before Thee with these gifts, and so direct our prayers to Thee with confessions of our debt.</p>  
 <p>6. By the kinship of the good kindred <a name="fr_1063"></a><a href="#fn_1057"><font size="1">4</font></a>, by that of Righteousness the good (Thy righteous servant's nature) would we approach Thee, and by that of the good thrift-law, and of Piety the good.</p>  
 <p>7. And we would worship the Fravashi of the Kine of blessed gift <a name="fr_1064"></a><a href="#fn_1058"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and that of the holy Gaya Maretan, and we would worship the holy Fravashi <a name="fr_1065"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_253"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 253</font></a></p>  
 <p>of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra Spitâma, the saint. Yea, that one of beings do we worship whose better (service) in the sacrifice Ahura Mazda knows; (even those women do we worship) whose <a name="fr_1066"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">1</font></a> (better service thus is known). Yea, both (holy) men and women (do we worship whom Ahura Mazda knows <a name="fr_1067"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">2</font></a>). As the Ahû is excelling, so is our Ratu, one who rules from the Righteous Order, a creator of mental goodness, and of life's actions done for Mazda; and the Kingdom is to Ahura which to the poor (may offer) nurture <a name="fr_1068"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>A blessing is the Right called the best, there is weal, there is weal to this (man), when toward Righteousness Best (he does) right <a name="fr_1069"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. We worship the Ahuna-vairya; and we worship Asha Vahi<i>s</i>ta the best(?), the bountiful Immortal. And we sacrifice to the Hâ fraoreti, even to the confession and laudation of the Mazdayasnian Faith!</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1052"></a><a href="sbe31035.htm#fr_1058">251:7</a> Ashethwôzgatemã (several manuscripts have ashe) finds its explanation from the Pahlavi of Dastur Hoshang<i>g</i>i <i>G</i>âmâsp<i>g</i>i's MS. it may be read kabed ran<i>g</i> rasi<i>s</i>ntûm instead of kabed yôm rasi<i>s</i>ntûm. The ancient error of yôm arose from the fact that the copyist had before him a form which might be read either rô<i>g</i> or ran<i>g</i>, the characters being identical for either word. He could not reconcile himself to ran<i>g</i> in the sense of effort, and so decided for rô<i>g</i>; but in order to guide his successors aright, he changed it for its synonym yôm, which, as Spiegel well remarks, affords but little sense. But the word is ran<i>g</i>, as I believe, and this is at once corroborated by Ner.'s bahukle<i>s</i>a. Read a<i>s</i> + thwakh<i>s</i>a + gatemã = kabed + ran<i>g</i> + rasi<i>s</i>ntûm, the most progressing with painful energy.</p>
