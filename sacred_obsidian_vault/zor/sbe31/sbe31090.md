 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part III (SBE31)</i>, L.H. Mills, tr. [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VISPARAD XIII (SP. XV) <a name="fr_1473"></a><a href="#fn_1459"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. According to the ritual we worship Ahura Mazda; according to the ritual we worship the Bountiful Immortals; and we sacrifice to the sacrificial word correctly spoken, and to every Mãthra (as to a sacred word of reason). And we sacrifice to Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, him who is especially the possessor of the Mãthra <a name="fr_1474"></a><a href="#fn_1460"><font size="1">3</font></a>; and we sacrifice to the 'blessings for the saints' <a name="fr_1475"></a><a href="#fn_1461"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and we worship the 'hail' <a name="fr_1476"></a><a href="#fn_1462"><font size="1">5</font></a> addressed to the Bountiful Immortals.</p>  
 <p>2. Also we worship the three principal (chapters) uttered (in the Yasna) without addition or omission <a name="fr_1477"></a><a href="#fn_1463"><font size="1">6</font></a>;</p>

 <p>and we worship the three principal ones without addition or omission; we worship the three commencing ones entire without addition or omission <a name="fr_1478"></a><a href="#fn_1464"><font size="1">1</font></a>. And we worship the entirety <a name="fr_1479"></a><a href="#fn_1465"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the three principal ones without addition or omission; and their Hâs, their metrical lines, their words, and their word-structure [and their recital, memorising, chanting, and their steadfast offering].</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1459"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1473">355:2</a> This fragment follows Y. XXX in the Vendîdâd Sâdah, and was written in allusion to Y. XXVIII, Y. XXIX, and Y. XXX.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1460"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1474">355:3</a> Referring to mãthra srevaêmâ in Yasna XXVIII, 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1461"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1475">355:4</a> Referring to the words sava<i>k</i>â ashavabyô in Yasna XXX, 11.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1462"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1476">355:5</a> Referring to the word u<i>s</i>tâ in Yasna XXX, 11.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1463"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1477">355:6</a> The three first chapters XXVIII-XXX; the text has bad grammar, or broken connection.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1464"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1478">356:1</a> It is difficult to see how anapishûtâ can mean 'without retrenchment,' but the context seems to require it, and the Pahlavi translation bears evidence to it. Perhaps read anapashûtâ.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1465"></a><a href="sbe31090.htm#fr_1479">356:2</a> 'The whole three first.' Some suppose the three prayers to be intended (the Ahuna-vairya, the Ashem Vohû, and the Yê<font size="-1">N</font>hê hâtãm). I think that the three chapters XXVIII-XXX are meant. As the piece follows those three chapters in the Vendîdâd Sâdah, so its expressions indicate a reference to them. This might tend to show that the Ahunavaiti was at one time, if not originally, divided at this place.</p>
