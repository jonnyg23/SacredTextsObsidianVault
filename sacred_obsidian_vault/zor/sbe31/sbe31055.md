 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part III (SBE31)</i>, L.H. Mills, tr. [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">YASNA XL.</h3>  <h4 align="center">PRAYERS FOR HELPERS.</h4>  
 <p>1. And now in these Thy dispensations, O Ahura Mazda! do Thou wisely <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1201"><font size="1">4</font></a> act for us, and with abundance</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_289"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 289</font></a></p>  
 <p>with Thy bounty and Thy tenderness <a name="fr_1212"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">1</font></a> as touching us; and grant that reward which Thou hast appointed to our souls, O Ahura Mazda! 2. Of this do Thou Thyself bestow upon us for this world and the spiritual; and now as part thereof (do Thou grant) that we may attain to fellowship with Thee, and Thy Righteousness for all duration.</p>  
 <p>3. And do Thou grant us, O Ahura! men who are righteous, and both lovers and producers of the Right as well. And give us trained beasts for the pastures, broken in for riding <a name="fr_1213"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and for bearing, (that they may be) in helpful <a name="fr_1214"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">3</font></a> companionship with us, and as a source of long enduring vigour, and a means of rejoicing grace to us for this <a name="fr_1215"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. So let there be a kinsman lord for us, with the labourers of the village, and so likewise let there be the clients (or the peers <a name="fr_1216"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">5</font></a>). And by the help of those may we arise.</p>  
 <p>So may we be to You, O Mazda Ahura! holy and true <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and with free giving of our gifts.</p>
