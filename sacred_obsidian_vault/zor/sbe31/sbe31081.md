 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part III (SBE31)</i>, L.H. Mills, tr. [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VISPARAD IV (SP. V) <a name="fr_1435"></a><a href="#fn_1422"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. (Yea,) we sacrifice to the thoughts of the mind, and to the good wisdom, and to the good and blessed</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 344</font></a></p>  
 <p>sanctity, and to the good religious knowledge, and to good health (of soul and body). [At their (several) seasons, and with the presence of seasonable circumstances, they are hymned <a name="fr_1436"></a><a href="#fn_1423"><font size="1">1</font></a>.] 2. Confession is to be made for the Kine; we, Zarathu<i>s</i>trian Mazdayasnians, celebrate at the sacrificial time for the Myazda-offering, at the time for the Ratufrîti, the prayer for blessings, for the sacrificial worship, homage, propitiation, and praise of the entire creation of the holy (and the clean).</p>
