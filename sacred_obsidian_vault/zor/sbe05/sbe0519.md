 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part I (SBE05)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER X.</h3>  
 <p>0. On the conflict waged with the primeval ox.</p>  
 <p>1. As it passed away <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">5</font></a>, owing to the vegetable principle (<i>k</i>îharak) <i>proceeding</i>, from every limb of the ox, fifty and five species of grain <a name="fr_150"></a><a href="#fn_150"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>and</i> twelve species of medicinal plants grew forth from the earth, and their splendour and strength were the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 32</font></a></p>  
 <p>seminal energy (tôkhmîh) of the ox. 2. Delivered to the moon station <a name="fr_151"></a><a href="#fn_151"><font size="1">1</font></a>, that seed was thoroughly purified by the light of the moon, fully prepared in every way, and produced life in a body. 3. Thence <i>arose</i> two oxen, one male <i>and</i> one female; and, afterwards, two hundred and eighty-two species of each kind <a name="fr_152"></a><a href="#fn_152"><font size="1">2</font></a> became manifest upon the earth. 4. The dwelling (mânîst) of the birds is in the air, and the fish are in the midst of the water.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_151"></a><a href="sbe0519.htm#fr_151">32:1</a> See Chap. XIV, 3. In the Mâh Yt. 0, 7, blessings are invoked for 'the moon of ox lineage' (gao<i>k</i>ithra) in conjunction with the 'sole-created ox and the ox of many species.' In the Avesta the gender of these two primeval oxen appears doubtful, owing probably to the dual gen. masc. of their epithets being of the same form as a sing. gen. fem.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_152"></a><a href="sbe0519.htm#fr_152">32:2</a> That is, of each sex. See Chap. XIV, 13, 27. In all three occurrences of this number K20 has 272, but all other MSS. have 282 (except M6 in this place only).</p>
