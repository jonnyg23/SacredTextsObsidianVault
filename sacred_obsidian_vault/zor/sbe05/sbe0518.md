 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part I (SBE05)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The conflict waged with plants was that when <a name="fr_144"></a><a href="#fn_144"><font size="1">5</font></a> they became quite dry. 2. Amerôda<i>d</i> the arch-angel,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 31</font></a></p>  
 <p>as the vegetation was his own, pounded the plants small, <i>and</i> mixed <i>them</i> up with the water <i>which</i> Tî<i>s</i>tar seized, and Tî<i>s</i>tar made that water rain down upon the whole earth. 3. On the whole earth plants grew up like hair upon the heads of men. 4. Ten thousand <a name="fr_145"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">1</font></a> of them grew forth of one special description, for keeping away the ten thousand species of disease <i>which</i> the evil spirit produced for the creatures; <i>and</i> from those ten thousand, the 100,000 species <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">2</font></a> of plants have grown forth.</p>  
 <p>5. From that same germ of plants the tree of all germs <a name="fr_147"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">3</font></a> was given forth, <i>and</i> grew up in the wide-formed ocean, from which the germs of all species of plants ever increased. 6. And near to that tree of all germs the Gôkar<i>d</i> tree <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">4</font></a> was produced, for keeping away deformed (dûspa<i>d</i>) decrepitude; <i>and</i> the full perfection of the world arose therefrom.</p>
