 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part I (SBE05)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the nature of the ape <i>and</i> the bear they say, that Yim, when reason (nismô) departed from, him <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_472"><font size="1">3</font></a>, for fear of the demons took a demoness as wife, and gave Yimak, who was <i>his</i> sister, to a demon as wife; and from them have originated the tailed ape and bear <i>and</i> other species of degeneracy.</p>  
 <p>2. This, too, they say, that in the reign of A<i>z</i>-i Dahâk <a name="fr_477"></a><a href="#fn_473"><font size="1">4</font></a> a young woman was admitted to a demon, and a young man was admitted to a witch (parîk), and on seeing them they had intercourse; owing to that one intercourse the black-skinned negro arose from them. 3. When Frê<i>d</i>ûn <a name="fr_478"></a><a href="#fn_474"><font size="1">5</font></a> came to them they fled from the country of Iran, <i>and</i> settled upon the sea-coast; now, <i>through</i> the invasion of the Arabs, they are again diffused through the country of Iran.</p>
