 <p><a name="page_400"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 400</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">OBSERVATIONS.</h3>  
 <p>1-5. (The same as on page <a href="sbe37003.htm#page_2">2</a>.)</p>  
 <p>6. The manuscripts mentioned are:</p>  
 <p>B (written A.D. 1659), see page <a href="sbe37003.htm#page_2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>B29 (written A.D. 1679), a Persian Rivâyat, No. 29 in the University Library at Bombay.</p>  
 <p>DH (written A.D. 1813), a Dîn-vi<i>g</i>irgard in the library of Dastûr Hôshangji Jâmâspji at Poona.</p>  
 <p>K35 (probably written A.D. 1572), a Dâ<i>d</i>istân-î Dînîk, No. 35 in the University Library at Kopenhagen.</p>  
 <p>Mf4, Pt4 (written about A. n. 1780), in the Mullâ Fîrûz Library and in that of Dastûr Peshotanji Behramji in Bombay, respectively, both copied from a Yasna with Pahlavi, written in Irân and brought to India about A.D. 1478, which was a descendant of an ancestor of J2 and K5, and independent of those two authorities.</p>  
 <p>MH10 (about 150 years old), a Persian Rivâyat, No. 10 of Haug's Collection in the State Library at Munich.</p>  
 <p>O225, a Persian Rivâyat in No. 225 of Ouseley's Collection in the Bodleian Library at Oxford.</p>
