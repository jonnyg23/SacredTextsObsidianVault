 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLVIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Bakŏ Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The second fargar<i>d</i> is the Ashem <a name="fr_1225"></a><a href="#fn_1212"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the Bakân <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1213"><font size="1">3</font></a>; it is by it <i>that</i> perfect excellence is produced for every one who produces for any one else that which is suitable for him <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1214"><font size="1">4</font></a>; for this reason, because, for the sake of perfect production, <i>there is</i> much unprofitable production, <i>but</i> profitable production is suitably producing. 2. This, too, that the reward of every good work is given by it to mankind, which keeps mankind in diligence when it instructs <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1215"><font size="1">5</font></a>; because, as the business <i>of</i> all good works is that which instructs and keeps mankind in diligence, the reward of good works which mankind</p>

 </p>

 <p>can appropriate by diligence is appropriated by it. 3. And this, too, that advancement is given by it to every good work <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="sbe37098.htm#fn_1216"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. He who is understanding good works, and <i>yet</i> a suppliant, has thereby made the learned foolish (<i>â</i><i>z</i>ân akhanî<i>d</i>înî<i>d</i>ŏ); whoever possesses authority through virtuousness <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="sbe37098.htm#fn_1217"><font size="1">2</font></a> is more particularly for rewarding the doers of good works; whoever, too, can make true decision <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="sbe37098.htm#fn_1218"><font size="1">3</font></a> and adjudication is more particularly for causing the bridge <i>judgment</i> of a criminal, and <i>for</i> thrusting <i>him</i> aside owing to the exhaustion of <i>his</i> good works; and whoever, too, can exercise mediation <i>and</i> wisdom is more particularly for the good government of the world.</p>  
 <p>5. Of righteousness the excellence is perfect.</p>
