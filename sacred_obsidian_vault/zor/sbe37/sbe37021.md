 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Nîkâ</i>d<i>ûm Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The third section is the Rêshistân ('<i>wound code</i>'), particulars about cutting, tearing, cleaving,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 42</font></a></p>  
 <p>disembowelling, stabbing, gnawing <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">1</font></a>, rupturing, hacking, mutilating, and withering <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">2</font></a>; such as are all called wounds. 2. The upheaving circular movement of a certain serpent-scourge <a name="fr_150"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">3</font></a>, the throwing down of the person, and the flow of blood from the bodies of the people.</p>  
 <p>3. How the various members are divided into seventy-six that are more particularly called principal, which are comprised in two classes; two of these, which are clothed <i>and</i> different, one from the other, are female, and <i>some</i> out of the surrounding parts (girvôgânîh), which are <i>apart</i> from eight of the principal, that are comprised in the members of the two classes <i>and</i> among those seventy-six<i>and</i> which, in like manner, are different one from the otherare female, and are <i>of</i> different purpose and different design, one from the other.</p>  
 <p>4. These, too, namely, when any one, through an assault, produces, for any other, stupefaction, swelling, or leanness, blackness <a name="fr_151"></a><a href="#fn_150"><font size="1">4</font></a>, or paleness, shortness, or tallness, want of intelligence, much eating, little eating, or moderate eating, indolence, or diligence, or dulness of hearing; or he wishes to speak <i>some</i> words, and they strike him in return; or <i>one</i> altogether diminishes <i>any</i> one's speech, sight, <i>or</i> hearing,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_43"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 43</font></a></p>  
 <p>wisdom, strength, <i>or</i> semen, milk, <i>or</i> pregnancy; <i>or</i> when <i>one</i> destroys the spleen (s<i>p</i>ur) or milk of females, or, in revenge (gî<i>f</i>ar), kills <i>his</i> son outright; <i>or</i> when they would inflict a wound upon a wound, and one's blood goes streaming forth.</p>  
 <p>5. Also about an assault with one, two, or three weapons, or more, in conjunction; or they may commit <i>it</i> on the spot, or in confederacy, or as a first offence <a name="fr_152"></a><a href="sbe37022.htm#fn_151"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 6. About the measure of a wound when a two-edged sword (dôbarakŏ) plunges down, the area (sarâî), walls, and surroundings, and the shape which is plunged; that <i>which</i> is hacked, or cleft, or mutilated, or a torrent <i>of</i> blood streaming; the affliction (vamang) of the furious serpent-scourge (mârvanô) <a name="fr_153"></a><a href="sbe37022.htm#fn_152"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and the length, glitter, and weight of the weapon.</p>  
 <p>7. The ritual for the departure of a wound and the departure of pain, watching over <i>it for</i> the duration of three nights <i>or</i> a year, <i>its</i> greater wretchedness <i>or</i> less wretchedness, <i>its</i> cure (s<i>p</i>ôrîkîh) or incurableness, and whatever is on the same subject. 8. Trivial enumerations, and decisions upon each separately.</p>
