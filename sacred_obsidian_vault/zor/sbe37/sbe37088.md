 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXIX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. <i>In</i> the sixteenth fargar<i>d</i>, Kamnamaêza <a name="fr_1027"></a><a href="#fn_1018"><font size="1">3</font></a>, about departure to any land whatever <a name="fr_1028"></a><a href="#fn_1019"><font size="1">4</font></a>, in renewed search <i>of</i> fortune, <i>there is</i> also this, namely: 'Do not stay <i>away</i> discontentedly from this thy ceremonial and obeisance, O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! through love of us, when they do not satisfy thee<i>neither</i> thy own, nor the confederate, nor the companion, nor the serf, nor the wicked tyrant <a name="fr_1029"></a><a href="#fn_1020"><font size="1">5</font></a>by whom those who are demons are <i>wont</i> to be worshipped. 2. And where and when thou art far from us, even then do not stand aloof from our affairs; and also when the affairs of the worldly <i>existence</i> shall not stand well for thee, even then thou shouldst reverence us and shouldst pay us homage.' 3. So also this, that the wish <i>of</i> the evil spirit is thus: 'Thou shouldst not reverence and shouldst not pay homage to the archangels; and here <a name="fr_1030"></a><a href="#fn_1021"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>the people</i> shall possess neither lordship, nor priestly instructionthat is, ruler and</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 277</font></a></p>  
 <p>high-priestand their desire is not <i>for</i> perfect righteousness.'</p>  
 <p>4. And this, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: '<i>Of</i> the contracted <a name="fr_1031"></a><a href="#fn_1022"><font size="1">1</font></a> spirituality and deficient wealth <a name="fr_1032"></a><a href="#fn_1023"><font size="1">2</font></a>, owing to the little progress of men who are self-gratifiers, thou art aware, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! <i>thou</i> who art no seeker <i>of</i> thisthat is, this want of opulence of thinebecause thou dost not know <i>it</i> <a name="fr_1033"></a><a href="#fn_1024"><font size="1">3</font></a>; <i>but</i> I perceive those words of complaint of thine, of which I demand an account from thee <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1025"><font size="1">4</font></a>.' 5. And this, <i>too</i>, namely: 'Thou art aware <i>of</i> the gratification of desire <a name="fr_1035"></a><a href="#fn_1026"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>by</i> us who are archangels, and <i>which</i> we give for the gratification that thou bringest forth (zihi<i>h</i>); we also give thee the liberty which a friend gives to him who is a friend <a name="fr_1036"></a><a href="#fn_1026"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>6. About <i>what</i> occurs in <i>future</i> ages <a name="fr_1037"></a><a href="#fn_1027"><font size="1">6</font></a>: the experienced (arvandân) who are beneficial through teaching <i>and</i> practising wisdom <a name="fr_1038"></a><a href="#fn_1028"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and the thirst of youths is increased by them; by the assistance of complete mindfulness they improve the world of righteousness and produce distress for the fiend; and the advantage due to virtue extends to them <a name="fr_1039"></a><a href="#fn_1029"><font size="1">8</font></a>. 7. And this, too, that he who is evilly oppressive has died off through his own deeds <a name="fr_1040"></a><a href="#fn_1030"><font size="1">9</font></a>. 8. About always opposing villains with as much strength as exists, so that he who is a good ruler <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1031"><font size="1">10</font></a>, whose high-priest is the bounteous liturgy (Mânsars<i>p</i>end), may become predominant <a name="fr_1042"></a><a href="#fn_1032"><font size="1">11</font></a> over Wrath.</p>  
 <p>9. About the praise of the renovators <i>there</i> is this,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 278</font></a></p>  
 <p>too, namely: 'Blessings on good understanding <i>and</i> also on Mit<i>r</i>ô, whose punishment of sinners they shall inflict for this consideration, that <i>he is</i> intelligent <i>and</i> friendly (mit<i>r</i>ô-pân) <a name="fr_1043"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' 10. And, about adjudication <i>as to</i> a kinsman (naf<i>s</i>man) of any one whatever, <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'Through a revival of Rashnû, whoever is righteous and also whoever is wickedthat is, every oneis to be kept for judicial investigation <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">2</font></a>.' 11. This, too, namely: 'A kinsman is to be considered as virtuous <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1035"><font size="1">3</font></a>, by whom his own soul is preserved from wickedness <a name="fr_1046"></a><a href="#fn_1036"><font size="1">4</font></a>.' 12. <i>And</i> this, too, namely: 'So thy high-priest is he whose own religion is pure <a name="fr_1047"></a><a href="#fn_1037"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>13. About the characteristics of the fiend, the broken-down (khastakŏ) Mânih <a name="fr_1048"></a><a href="#fn_1038"><font size="1">6</font></a>,and the destruction of the wicked who <i>were</i> listening to him, that which came from him who <i>was</i> monarch. 14. And this, too, namely: 'The wicked <i>one</i>, who gives my world to that which the malicious <a name="fr_1049"></a><a href="#fn_1039"><font size="1">7</font></a> Aharman has established <i>as</i> supremacy (lâlâîh), is he who is a self-wounding <a name="fr_1050"></a><a href="#fn_1040"><font size="1">8</font></a> demon that is set going for the death of the world of righteousness which he praises. 15. The ceremonial</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_279"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 279</font></a></p>  
 <p>of righteousness is not such as that he praises, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! the priestly authority of the worldly settlements (gêhânân) that he mentions thus: "In priestly authority and high-priestship I <i>am</i> better (sapîr) <i>and</i> am better suitable;" and not so, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! is that excretion (mûtri<i>s</i>nŏ) <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1041"><font size="1">1</font></a> he stirs up for mankind; that <i>which</i> he mentions to them becomes a perpetual effusion from him, and they who stirred up the excretion afterwards think it theirs, <i>and</i> that which is a perfect ceremonial of the demons occurs. 16. Through the opposing arrival <i>of</i> Srôsh <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1042"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the righteous, the ruler is in vexation with that person; that ruler who is a protection of these <i>others</i> through good emanation <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">3</font></a>not through evil livingand at every time a distresser <i>of</i> the wicked <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>17. About the peculiarity of attracters <i>to</i> the religion, <i>and</i> the good works of those attracted <a name="fr_1055"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 18. About the signs of the last times, which <i>are</i> the millenniums of the sons of Zaratû<i>s</i>t.</p>  
 <p>19. This, too, that they cause disturbance (aâramênd) unto the sovereignty, and <i>they</i> who are Kaîs and Karaps <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1046"><font size="1">6</font></a>, those even who are the most evil-ruling in the countrywho by villanous deeds are those <i>who</i> destroy the existence of mankind through statements, and destroy their own souls <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1047"><font size="1">7</font></a><i>also</i> destroy the material world which, confused by them,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_280"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 280</font></a></p>  
 <p>is more beloved than righteousness; even the sovereignty is a scanty shelter, among the existences, from those whose command is villanous, when they produce <i>that</i> which is vicious and deliver their pupils (âmûkhtagânŏ) to that which is <i>their</i> end (<i>a</i>fdûm), to the fiendish abode <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>20. And here, too, about the praise of the family of the Fryânaks <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1049"><font size="1">2</font></a> it speaks thus: 'Righteousness comes up, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân! from the descendants and posterity of Tûrân; when extracted by the Fryânaks <i>it</i> is stated <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">3</font></a> just as <i>though</i> it were by Tûrân; through the assistance of complete mindfulness they develope the world <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">4</font></a> of righteousness and produce distress for the fiend; they likewise think about <i>it</i> with Good Thought, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! <i>and</i> thou shouldst bring forth (zâyê<i>s</i>) their gratification <a name="fr_1062"></a><a href="#fn_1052"><font size="1">5</font></a> from us, who are archangels, by words, that is, do thou demand <i>it</i>.'</p>  
 <p>21. This, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'This liberality which is <i>for</i> thee is <i>for us</i> who are archangels; by him who shall provide liberality for thee <a name="fr_1063"></a><a href="#fn_1053"><font size="1">6</font></a>, it is provided for us.' 22. About the praise of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'Kai-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_1064"></a><a href="#fn_1054"><font size="1">7</font></a> <i>has</i> propitiated thee, among the existences, by liberal giving; that Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, whose coming forth to thee in distress is through the reign of Vohûman, has developed the material world of righteousness; thou</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_281"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 281</font></a></p>  
 <p>shouldst think of him, the good companion, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! the pure friend who is Kat-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_1065"></a><a href="#fn_1055"><font size="1">1</font></a>; such is that Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, the active, who, when he praises the religion, is attracting fellow-dwellers <i>and</i> converts <i>them</i>, that is, he brings them on to the religion <a name="fr_1066"></a><a href="#fn_1056"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>23. About attracting the Spîtâmas to the religion <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'Thou shouldst speak thus to the Spîtâmas: "Praise righteousness with much homage about <i>it</i> mentally; and a concession is to be discriminated by you, as well as whatever is no concession; even for those deeds <i>of</i> yours righteousness is the reward given unto you, that reward which is much given by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1067"></a><a href="#fn_1057"><font size="1">3</font></a>."' 24. About the place of the four marvels produced by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>in</i> yonder <i>world</i>: there where is the reign of Vohûman <a name="fr_1068"></a><a href="#fn_1058"><font size="1">4</font></a>, there where is the hospitality of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1069"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">5</font></a>, there where religion is <i>along</i> with complete mindfulness <a name="fr_1070"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">6</font></a>, <i>and</i> there where are the souls of the liberal <a name="fr_1071"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>25. About advice to Zaratû<i>s</i>t as to speech, made for mankind, which is proportionateabandoning want of proportionwhich is an appropriation of liberality with humility and a wise proportion <a name="fr_1072"></a><a href="#fn_1062"><font size="1">8</font></a> for good works. 26. This, too, namely: 'To him who gives himself mentally up to thee in discipleship, thou also shouldst give up the best which <i>thou hast</i> to give of thine own; and thou shouldst give wealth to him who shall give wealth to thee <a name="fr_1073"></a><a href="#fn_1063"><font size="1">9</font></a>, because so thy soul would be perfect, <i>O</i> righteous Zaratû<i>s</i>t! when it shall act thus.' 27. This, too: 'Thou</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_282"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 282</font></a></p>  
 <p>shouldst select this religion of mine with wisdom <i>and</i> also with thought <a name="fr_1074"></a><a href="#fn_1064"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' 28. This, too, that <i>as to</i> him who <i>has</i> to act with the freedom from effort (<i>a</i>pêsitûnagîh) of righteousness <a name="fr_1075"></a><a href="#fn_1065"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>and</i> owing to <i>it</i>, for the good works done by him the gift is good. 29. This, too, that whoever seeks by good works, <i>and</i> seeks good works by innocence, obtains freedom from harm (<i>a</i>-nâsîh); <i>and</i> whoever is liberal to the sacred beings <a name="fr_1076"></a><a href="#fn_1066"><font size="1">3</font></a> is free from destruction (<i>a</i>-nasîni<i>s</i>nŏ), owing to the liberality of the sacred beings. 30. <i>And</i> this, too, namely: 'These <i>are</i> the rewards I am aware of <a name="fr_1077"></a><a href="#fn_1067"><font size="1">4</font></a>, which have been, which still are, and which ever <i>will</i> be.'</p>  
 <p>31. Perfect excellence is righteousness.</p>
