 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXVIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Bakŏ Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The beginning of the twenty-second fargar<i>d</i>, the Airyaman <a name="fr_1520"></a><a href="#fn_1480"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is the last question (fra<i>s</i>nŏ) beyond the five Gâthas; it is taught for the dominion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> only by himthat is, it is making him ruler of himselfwho shall do that which is declared by the passage:Yâ ere<i>z</i>e<i>g</i>yôi dâhî</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_384"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 384</font></a></p>  
 <p>drigaovê vahyô <a name="fr_1521"></a><a href="#fn_1481"><font size="1">1</font></a>: who gives delights (vâyagânŏ) to him who is a right-living poor <i>man</i> <a name="fr_1522"></a><a href="#fn_1482"><font size="1">2</font></a>preservation from the destroyer, <i>and</i> the consummation of every happiness.</p>  
 <p>2. It is perfect excellence <i>that is</i> righteousness.</p>
