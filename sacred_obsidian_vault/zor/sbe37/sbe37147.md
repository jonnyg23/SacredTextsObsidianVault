 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XIX. VENDÎDÂD NASK <a name="fr_1832"></a><a href="#fn_1782"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>The whole of this Nask (as described in Dk. VIII, Chap. XLIV) is still extant, and is considered by the writers of the Persian Rivâyats to be a complete work. Its fragmentary character, which is obvious enough to European scholars, must, therefore, be</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_482"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 482</font></a></p>  
 <p>attributed, in all probability, to losses it sustained before the revival of Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship by the Sasanian dynasty. It is remarkable that the compiler of the account in the Dinka<i>r</i><i>d</i> makes no allusion to the twelfth fargar<i>d</i> of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i>, which is also omitted in all old MSS. of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> with Pahlavi that have been examined, although the copyists appear to have been aware of the existence of a twelfth fargar<i>d</i>.</p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1782"></a><a href="sbe37147.htm#fr_1832">481:3</a> The extent of this Nask appears to be the same now as it was in Sasanian times, and may be estimated at about 23,000 Avesta and 48,000 Pahlavi words. The moderately long account of it in Dk. VIII, contains 1,272 Pahlavi words.</p>
