 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Hûspâram Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. One section is the Nîrangistân ('<i>ritual code</i>'), particulars about the ritual of the ceremonial of the sacred beings, that which is important <i>and</i> goes to the bridge <i>of judgment</i> <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_249"><font size="1">2</font></a>; the exceeding meritoriousness owing to an ample number of Râspîs <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_250"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the ceremonial; and, as to the Avesta, the Zôti and Râspî are both for various phrases, those which are for the speaking of the one are for the hearing of the other. 2. About the sacred cake <a name="fr_252"></a><a href="#fn_251"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 3. About abstaining from the drinking of wines at the same time as the ceremonial. 4. About the quality (sâmân) of the voice in reciting the Avesta in a ceremonial, and the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 95</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Avesta which is twice recited and thrice or four times recited. 5. About the ceremonial, and the conducting of that ceremonial whose Zôti, or Râspî, is a Tanâpûhar <i>sinner</i> <a name="fr_253"></a><a href="#fn_252"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 6. About the Zôti duty of a woman <a name="fr_254"></a><a href="#fn_253"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>or</i> child. 7. About a decision as regards him who is cursed by the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion.</p>  
 <p>8. About the sin of him who does not solemnize a season-festival <a name="fr_255"></a><a href="#fn_254"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and how the <i>case</i> is when it is solemnized by him. 9. About the limits of the five periods <a name="fr_256"></a><a href="#fn_255"><font size="1">4</font></a> <i>of</i> the day and night, and the ceremonies <i>of</i> the same periods. 10. About the kinds of peculiarity of the things for the season-festivals <i>and</i> other good works produced authorisedly.</p>  
 <p>11. About the quantity of holy-water which is due to one sheep <a name="fr_257"></a><a href="#fn_256"><font size="1">5</font></a>, the inspection <i>and</i> consideration in providing the sheep, the freedom from sickness due to contamination and other defects even in a lawful place, and the exemption from the appliances and attacks of noxious creatures; the ritual for making <i>it</i> <a name="fr_258"></a><a href="#fn_257"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and deciding about the maker, producer, and carrier, the taster <i>and</i> the giver to him. 12. The reason of the slaughter, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>13. About the position and duty of the Zôti <i>and</i> Râspîs in the ceremonial. 14. About the perfect ceremonial, the gift to a righteous man who <i>has</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a></p>  
 <p>become a teacher <i>and</i> examiner <i>of</i> the wisdom of the righteous, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subjects.</p>  
 <p>15. About the <i>sacred</i> shirt and <i>thread</i>-girdle, that is, from what it is proper <i>to make them</i>, and whatever is on the same subjects. 16. About gathering <i>and</i> tying the sacred twigs, and on the same subject. 17. About the proportion of firewood in various parts of the ceremonial, <i>and</i> the mode of bringing it forward; that for the household fire, <i>and</i> the priestly fire of Bahirâm (Varahrân).</p>  
 <p>18. About a ceremonial amid great <i>opulence</i>, that which is amid medium <i>opulence</i>, that which is amid little opulence, and a decision as regards want of opulence. 19. About always celebrating the ceremonies of the sacred beings for that which has occurred, and not neglecting <i>them</i> in any way. 20. About <i>the cases</i> where mankind observantly, and also unobservantly, celebrate the ceremonies of the sacred beings; that is, which is he who observantly and he who unobservantly <i>does so; with</i> advice about observantly celebrating the ceremonies of the sacred beings.</p>  
 <p>21. About the cleanliness of the body <i>and</i> clothing of the celebrator of the ceremony, the assurance of his mind from sin, the ablution of the apparatus of the place of the exalted (vulandânîh), the cleanliness of the place of the ceremonial, the distance therefrom for <i>any</i> degree of manifest pollution and stench, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>22. About the ceremonial of the waters and their creatures, the vigour <a name="fr_259"></a><a href="#fn_258"><font size="1">1</font></a> of healthfulness, the possession of the brilliancy <i>of</i> heaven, the bountifulness of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 97</font></a></p>  
 <p>the spirit of the waters, and whatever is on the same subject. 23. About the celebration of a ceremonial, which is an ordinance of duties for the sake of a happy state of gladness (khûp parkânîh) and happy consequences; and also many other statements on the same subject. 24. <i>About</i> the ceremonial as proper <i>and</i> improper, beneficial <i>and</i> not beneficial.</p>  
 <p>25. About the families of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, Hvôv <a name="fr_260"></a><a href="#fn_259"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, as regards the account (aûshmûri<i>s</i>nŏ) <i>and</i> ceremonial of the religion and their nature.</p>
