 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The fourteenth fargar<i>d</i>, A<i>d</i>-fravakhshyâ <a name="fr_600"></a><a href="#fn_595"><font size="1">5</font></a>, is</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a></p>  
 <p>about Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s showing to Zaratû<i>s</i>t the terrible condition of the soul of Keresâsp <a name="fr_601"></a><a href="#fn_596"><font size="1">1</font></a>; the dismay of Zaratû<i>s</i>t owing to that terrible condition; the sorrowful speaking of Keresâsp as regards the slaying of multitudes, for which mankind extol him, whereby abstentions from sin <i>occurred</i>; and the recognition of him by the creator, Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, as smiting his fire. 2. The supplication of Keresâsp <i>for</i> the best existence from. Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> for those exploits when the serpent Srôbar <a name="fr_602"></a><a href="#fn_597"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>was</i> slain by him, and the violence of that adversary; when Gandarep <a name="fr_603"></a><a href="#fn_598"><font size="1">3</font></a> with the golden heels <i>was</i> smitten by him, <i>and</i> the marvellousness of that fiend; when the Ve<i>s</i>kŏ progeny <a name="fr_604"></a><a href="#fn_599"><font size="1">4</font></a> who <i>were descendants</i> of Nîvîk <i>and</i> Dâ<i>s</i>tânîk <i>were</i> slain by him, and the grievous harm and disaster owing to them; and when the mighty wind <a name="fr_605"></a><a href="#fn_600"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>was</i> appeased by him, <i>and</i> brought back from damaging the world to benefiting the creatures; <i>and</i> for that which <i>happens</i> when owing to confinement <a name="fr_606"></a><a href="#fn_601"><font size="1">6</font></a>, Dahâk becomes eager, rushes on for the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a></p>  
 <p>destruction of the world, and attempts (girâyê<i>d</i>ŏ) the annihilation of the creatures; <i>when</i> he (<i>Keres</i>âsp) is roused to smite him, and to tame that powerful fiend for the world and creatures.</p>  
 <p>3. The enmity of fire to Keresâsp, through the distress which he occasioned to it, and the keeping of him away <a name="fr_607"></a><a href="#fn_602"><font size="1">1</font></a> [from heaven;. also the friendship of Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan <a name="fr_608"></a><a href="#fn_603"><font size="1">2</font></a> for him, through the prosperity which he occasioned to it, and the protection of him] from hell. 4. The petition of Zaratû<i>s</i>t to the fire to have compassion upon what <i>was</i> owing to <i>Keresâsp's</i> sin; the compliance (han<i>g</i>âftanŏ) of the fire <i>with</i> that petition, and the departure of the soul of Keresâsp to the ever-stationary existence <a name="fr_609"></a><a href="#fn_604"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. Of righteousness perfect is the excellence.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_595"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_600">197:5</a> The first two words of the third hâ of the second Gâtha (Yas. XLV, 1), here written a<i>d</i>-fravakhshê (B) and a<i>d</i>-fravakhshâ <a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a> (K) in Pahlavi. This chapter has been already translated in S.B.E., vol. xviii, pp. 370-372.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_596"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_601">198:1</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XIII, 12; S.B.E., vol. xviii, pp. 369-382.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_597"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_602">198:2</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XXXV, 13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_598"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_603">198:3</a> Av. Ga<i>n</i>darewa of Yt. V, 38, XV, 28, XIX, 41; the 'watery demon' of Mkh. XXVII, 50.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_599"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_604">198:4</a> Reading hûn Ve<i>s</i>kŏ, the Av. hunavô Vaêskaya of Yt. V, 54, 57, who were enemies of the warrior Tusa; but the hunavô of Nivika and of the Dâ<i>s</i>tayâni were slain by Keresâspa (see Yt. XIX, 41). It is also possible to read khûnŏ-dâkŏ, 'blood-producing.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_600"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_605">198:5</a> When it becomes a storm-demon, the vatô-daêva of Vend. X, 14, instead of being the angel of useful wind.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_601"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_606">198:6</a> In the volcano, Mount Dimâvand, where he was confined by Frê<i>d</i>ûn in olden times, and whence he is expected to break loose hereafter (see Bd. XII, 31, XXIX, 9; Byt. III, 55-61).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_602"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_607">199:1</a> The words in brackets occur only in K; their meaning is, however, given in the Pahlavi Rivâyat accompanying Dd. and quoted in S.B.E., vol. xviii, pp. 379, 380. The enmity of the fire to Keresâsp was owing to its having been extinguished (when kindled upon the serpent Srôbar) by the upsetting of Keresâsp's caldron, as described in Yas. IX, 11, and Yt. XIX, 40.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_603"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_608">199:2</a> Av. g<i>e</i>us urva, 'the soul of the ox,' the spirit which departed from the primeval ox when the evil spirit attacked it; she is supposed to be the heavenly protector of all animals, and is also called Drvâspa (see Yt. IX, 1; Bd. III, 24, 18, IV, 2-5; Sls. XXII, 14).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_604"></a><a href="sbe37064.htm#fr_609">199:3</a> A locality intermediate between heaven and hell, where the souls of those whose sins and good works exactly balance remain in a passive and immovable state till the resurrection (see Sls. VI, 2; Mkh. VII, 18, XII, 14; Dd. XX, 3).</p>
