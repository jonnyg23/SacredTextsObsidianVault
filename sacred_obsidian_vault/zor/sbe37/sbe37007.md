 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The Bakŏ <a name="fr_52"></a><a href="#fn_52"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>contains</i> particulars about the division of the recital of the first saying of revelation <a name="fr_53"></a><a href="#fn_53"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the first creature <a name="fr_54"></a><a href="#fn_54"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>in</i> that saying, the first occurrence of it, the adaptation of the creature, and the greatness <i>of</i> that saying which is incorporating the creature, owing thereto; also, especially, the intermingling of thought (mê<i>d</i>) <a name="fr_55"></a><a href="#fn_55"><font size="1">4</font></a> with it. 2. Very comprehensive knowledge about everything, each separately its own offspring, and many an appendage as much connected with it as that which is said concerning the Bakŏ <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">5</font></a>, that 'the Bakŏ of the community (dâhmân) is heard where it is spoken for the community,' that is, whoever shall do this good work, for him this good work <i>will</i> be done.</p>  
 <p>3. Righteousness is perfect excellence.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_55"></a><a href="sbe37007.htm#fr_55">13:4</a> Av. maiti, Pâz. mit in Bk. IX, Chap. XLVII, 5, the Av. manas of Yas. XIX, 29 (Sp.).</p>
