 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The twenty-first fargar<i>d</i>, Vahi<i>s</i>tôi<i>s</i>ti <a name="fr_701"></a><a href="#fn_696"><font size="1">3</font></a>, is about where the best prayers <a name="fr_702"></a><a href="#fn_697"><font size="1">4</font></a> of the good religion <i>are:</i> unto Mit<i>r</i>ô <a name="fr_703"></a><a href="#fn_698"><font size="1">5</font></a> once every night for dismissing and lessening Wrath <i>in</i> the whole world, <i>and</i> a second time <i>for doing so with</i> Lethargy; a third <i>unto</i> Srôsh <a name="fr_704"></a><a href="#fn_699"><font size="1">6</font></a> the righteous, and the fourth is the Dâhmân <i>Â</i>f<i>rî</i>n <a name="fr_705"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">7</font></a> for further gifts and increasing gifts; and the most</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 220</font></a></p>  
 <p>preservative of them was the Dâhmân <i>Â</i>f<i>rî</i>n. 2. And this, too, that the most admirable of shapes of women <i>was</i> Hûmâî <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the noble family of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, of horses the splendid horse of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, of oxen the male ox Barmâyûn <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">2</font></a>, of sheep the very much celebrated <a name="fr_708"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">3</font></a> sheep that is fat, white-jawed, <i>and</i> star-spotted, <i>with its</i> upper half in a manufacture (pa<i>s</i>akhtakŏ) embroidered with gold and the topmost part yellow; and yet not one of them attains an equality to even a single thousandth <i>part</i> of the glory of a righteous man, a member of the community, by whom the Dâhmân <i>Â</i>f<i>rî</i>n of the good <i>is uttered</i>. 3. And this, too, as much as <i>its</i> goodness for the man and <i>his</i> wife is <i>its</i> evil <i>for</i> a villain and <i>his</i> paramour <a name="fr_709"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. About the exercise of sovereignty <i>by</i> Kâî-Ûs <a name="fr_710"></a><a href="#fn_705"><font size="1">5</font></a>, with triumph, over the earth of seven <i>regions;</i> the advancement of his commands, by the people of the creation <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="#fn_706"><font size="1">6</font></a>, more swiftly than a wave of the hands; the construction of his seven dwellings (mân) <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_707"><font size="1">7</font></a> in the midst of Albûr<i>z</i> <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_708"><font size="1">8</font></a>, one of gold, two of silver, two</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 221</font></a></p>  
 <p>of steel, and two of crystal (<i>â</i><i>v</i>gînakînŏ); the restraining of the many Mâzônîk demons <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_709"><font size="1">1</font></a> who are the ruin of the world, and confining <i>them</i> to their own duty; the arrival at those dwellings of his, and the swift winding (v<i>â</i>fînî<i>d</i>anŏ) around those dwellings, of a person whose strength is overpowered by decrepitude, and the approach of <i>whose</i> life to departure from the body <i>has</i> taken place; the reduction (khûsânî-hastanŏ) of the decrepitude thereby, and the return of his strength and manhood, that is, a command is given by him thus: 'Keep no people away at the door!' and he might make a domestic of fifteen years <i>of age</i>.</p>  
 <p>5. Afterwards, the consultation of the demons about the death of Kâî-Ûs, and the coming of Aeshm <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">2</font></a> to Kâî-Ûs, approving his death, and, therefore, making <i>him</i> wretched in his mind <i>about</i> the great sovereignty which <i>was</i> possessed by him over the seven lands, and causing him to long for the sovereignty <i>of</i> the heavenly region (asâmânŏ gâs) of the archangels <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 6. And, owing to the seductiveness of Aeshm, and the other demons who remained his co-operators for that undoing, Kâî-Ûs <i>was</i> even <i>engaged</i> in opposing and molesting the sacred beings. 7. Also his not returning across Albûr<i>z</i>, <i>but</i> rushing upwards, with many demons and wicked people, unto the <i>outer</i> edge of darkness <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">4</font></a>;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 222</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and</i> the reason <i>of</i> the glory of the Kayâns <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">1</font></a> becoming a figure of clay on that border. 8. The previous separation (madam rê<i>g</i>î-hastanŏ) of Kâî-Ûs from the troops, and <i>his</i> not turning from that ill-advisedness even on renewed strife aloft <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>with</i> the supreme sacred beings. 9. Afterwards, the creator's calling back the glory of the Kayâns to himself, the falling of the troops of Kâî-Ûs to the earth from that height, and the flying of Kâî-Ûs to the wide-formed ocean <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>10. This, too, it says, that, besides him, some one <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">4</font></a> flew behind him, thus associated with him; <i>and</i> after him flew Nêryôsang <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">5</font></a>, the promoter (frê<i>h</i>-dâ<i>d</i>âr) of the world, for diverting that person <i>from</i> him. 11. And the cry of him, the unborn Khûsrôî, who <i>was</i> thus associated with him, like that of a regiment (s<i>i</i>pâh) a thousand <i>strong, was</i> thus: 'Thou shouldst not smite him, <i>O</i> Nêryôsang, promoter <i>of</i> the world! for if thou shouldst smite this man, <i>O</i> Nêryôsang, promoter <i>of</i> the world! <i>there will</i> not be afterwards obtained, for acquirement, a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a></p>  
 <p>thorough destroyer <i>of</i> the high-priest of Tûrân <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">1</font></a>; because owing to this man <i>will</i> be born <i>him whose</i> name is Sîyâvakhsh <a name="fr_724"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and owing to Sîyâvakhsh I <i>shall</i> be born, who am the Khûsrôî who <i>will</i> entice the most heroic <a name="fr_725"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>one</i> of Tûrânwho is mostly the destruction of champions and troopsto the numerous heroes of the religion, so that I <i>may</i> accomplish the destruction of his champions and troops, when <a name="fr_726"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">4</font></a> I would occasion a distant flight of the sovereign of Tûrân.' 12. Through these words the guardian spirit of Khûsrôî delighted Nêryôsang, the promoter <i>of</i> the world; and, on these words, <i>the latter was</i> releasing him and that Kâî-Ûs who thereby became discreet.</p>  
 <p>13. Perfect is the excellence <i>of</i> righteousness.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_697"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_702">219:4</a> The Pahlavi explanation of Av. vahi<i>s</i>tâ î<i>s</i>ti<i>s</i>.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_700"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_705">219:7</a> 'The blessing of members of the community.' The Dahmân Âfrînagân consists of Yas. LX, 2-7 with Âf. I, 14-18; but the Âf<i>rî</i>n is another formula, otherwise called 'the Âf<i>rî</i>n of the seven Ameshâspends,' and it is uncertain which of the two is meant here.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_701"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_706">220:1</a> Av. (gen.) Humay<i>a</i>u of Yt. XIII, 139.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_702"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_707">220:2</a> See Chap. XXI, 22.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_703"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_708">220:3</a> Reading frêh-ôkhtar (for frêh-ôkhttar), as Bd. XXIV, 3 states that 'the black sheep which is fat and white-jawed is the chief of sheep.' It might be 'the sheep of Frashôkhtar,' and this name might be a miswriting of Frashô<i>s</i>tar, but we have no record of any such sheep of his.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_704"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_709">220:4</a> It is easy to trace a connection between §§ 1, 2 and Yas. LIII, 1, and between § 3 and the Pahl. version of Yas. LIII, 6 a.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_705"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_710">220:5</a> Av. Kava Usa (see Bk. VIII, Chap. XIII, 13).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_706"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_711">220:6</a> K has 'by demons and men.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_707"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_712">220:7</a> Probably the origin of the legends of the seven halting-places of Rustam and Isfendiyâr in the Shâhnâmah.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_708"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_713">220:8</a> Here meaning the mountain-range south of the Caspian (see Chap. XX, 3).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_709"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_714">221:1</a> Av. Mâzainya daêva, the idolators of Mâzendarân.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_710"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_715">221:2</a> The demon of wrath (see Bk. VIII, Chap. IX, 3 n).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_711"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_716">221:3</a> §§ 5-9 are evidently a summary of the original form of the legend of Kâvûs's attempt to reach the sky, otherwise described in the Shâhnâmah.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_712"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_717">221:4</a> Where the endless light commences. Reading par-i tom; or it might be 'to the utmost,' if we read frêtum as equivalent to frêhtûm.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_713"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_718">222:1</a> K omits 'of the Kayâns.' It is the royal glory of Yt. XIX, which descended from heaven and accompanies the faithful rulers and champions of the religion, successively (see Chap. XXIV, 3).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_714"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_719">222:2</a> B has 'pitying strife;' khv<i>â</i>parîk being written instead of <i>a</i><i>v</i>arîk.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_715"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_720">222:3</a> Meaning the Caspian, as in Chap. XXI, 17.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_716"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_721">222:4</a> It will be seen, from what follows, that this was the fravashi, or guardian spirit, of his future grandson, Kaî-Khûsrôî. Every being and object belonging to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s creation is supposed to have its spiritual representative, created before the universe and perpetually existing (see Bd. I, 8; Mkh. XLIX, 23).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_717"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_722">222:5</a> Av. Nairyôsangha, an angel who is supposed to be the usual messenger of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to mankind (see Byt. III, 25, 26, 59, 60). K has only 'besides him <i>and</i> behind him flew Nêryôsang.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_718"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_723">223:1</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XIII, 15.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_719"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_724">223:2</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XIII, 14.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_720"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_725">223:3</a> A single particular hero appears to be meant, although this is not quite certain.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_721"></a><a href="sbe37071.htm#fr_726">223:4</a> Assuming that mûn, 'who,' stands for amat, as in Chap. XIII, 2.</p>
