 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The thirteenth fargar<i>d</i>, Ta<i>d</i>-thwâ-peresâ <a name="fr_595"></a><a href="#fn_590"><font size="1">3</font></a>,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>is about the strength <i>and</i> mightiness of the spirit of the sacred cake <a name="fr_596"></a><a href="#fn_591"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 2. This, too, that every night the demons rush from hell <a name="fr_597"></a><a href="#fn_592"><font size="1">2</font></a> into the world, to injure <i>and</i> cause the death of the creatures; and, when <i>people</i> consecrate a sacred cake, that spirit descends to attack and keep back the demons, and to engage in combat with the demons ninety-nine times during every night; he also smites and stupefies them, and keeps <i>them</i> back from destroying the world.</p>  
 <p>3. This, too, that any one whatever of those men who utter these words <a name="fr_598"></a><a href="#fn_593"><font size="1">3</font></a> in prayer becomes righteous, except those men who shall contentedly, <i>or</i> wishfully, carry out a command for evil deeds, and they deceive (suftênd), or make <i>others</i> deceive, by statements proposed to them; <i>and</i> whose evil thoughts are thus more than <i>their</i> good thoughts, their evil words more than <i>their</i> good words, and their evil deeds more than <i>their</i> good deeds. 4. About carrying off the reliance produceable that <i>a sin</i> worthy of death is the obliteration (f<i>r</i><i>â</i><i>z</i> mûshtanŏ) of other sin, like an awful <i>and</i> mighty wind when it sweeps swiftly over the plain <a name="fr_599"></a><a href="#fn_594"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. Of righteousness the excellence is perfect.</p>
