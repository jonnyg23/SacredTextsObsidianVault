 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXVI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Hûspâram Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. Six <a name="fr_287"></a><a href="#fn_286"><font size="1">1</font></a> Fargar<i>d</i>s of one section of the <i>last</i> fourteen <i>contain</i> particulars about the enumeration of species of ownership, their precedence one over the other, <i>and</i> their good report in conducting legal proceedings. 2. About property that is brought up to the judges, which, owing to an accuser, becomes <i>a source of</i> litigation for a judge. 3. About a decree as to restoring possession, <i>or</i> as to keeping possession, of whatever is among such matters. 4. About property</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 113</font></a></p>  
 <p>which is, <i>or</i> is brought, out of the possession of a defendant, <i>and</i> property which is extorted from a man by worrying, or by a noticeable crime upon him; <i>with</i> a statement about it.</p>  
 <p>5. About the earnings (vindi<i>s</i>nŏ) of fellow-combatants <i>and</i> fellow-subordinates, <i>with</i> a statement about them. 6. About the coming of land, property, <i>or</i> anything, held by foreigners, into the princely possession of one from Irân.</p>  
 <p>7. About the guardianship of a family (dû<i>d</i>akŏ); likewise the varieties of it, <i>and</i> the fitness of a man for it. 8. About one's own family, and whatever is on the same subject. 9. About the income (vindi<i>s</i>nŏ) of wife <i>and</i> child.</p>  
 <p>10. About the trouble <i>of</i> the business <i>of</i> obtaining (vindi<i>s</i>nŏ) a wife, and also her marriage, owing to the urgency of the husband, after the trouble. 11. About <i>her</i> guardian and paramour, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 12. About the proportion who <i>have</i> to keep a wife to seek <i>for</i> offspring, <i>and</i> the proportion who <i>have</i> to satisfy menstrual excitement.</p>  
 <p>13. About adoption; likewise the varieties of it, <i>and</i> fitness for it; the violation of adoption, the sin of the son who is accepted, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 14. About the partnership of brothers <i>that has</i> existed, is formed, <i>or</i> is designed; <i>its</i> abandonment (a-bûkhtîkîh), the surplus property, the wealth that becomes quite sacrificed (za<i>d</i>akŏ), <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 15. About property that comes to next of kin through relationship, and that through adoption. 16. About the residue that lapses into ways of righteousness.</p>  
 <p>17. About where <i>and</i> in whom, after the father, is</p>  
 <p><a name="page_114"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 114</font></a></p>  
 <p>the prerogative as to a daughter <i>being</i> given away to a husband.</p>
