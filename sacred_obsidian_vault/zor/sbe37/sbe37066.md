 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The sixteenth fargar<i>d</i>, Spe<i>n</i>tâ-mainyû <a name="fr_633"></a><a href="#fn_628"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is about effecting the bridge<i>-judgment</i> of sinners, as declared by revelation. 2. About performing the ceremony (ya<i>s</i>tô) for a man and a woman, and it is ordered <i>for</i> the woman before the man; the fitness for the supreme heaven (garô<i>d</i>mânîkîh) arisen through the liturgy (ya<i>s</i>tô) to be recited itself, or <i>through</i> purchasing <i>heaven in</i> the worldly <i>existence</i> <a name="fr_634"></a><a href="#fn_629"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. About the immunity of the soul from hell through the righteousness <i>of having</i> respectfully given a horse of a <i>good</i> race, the land of a cultivated field, <i>or</i> a virtuous woman, to a righteous man; and also the woman who gives herself in marriage to</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a></p>  
 <p>the righteous man; and that liberal good work increases <i>from</i> time <i>to</i> time <a name="fr_635"></a><a href="#fn_630"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and <i>from</i> day <i>to</i> day.</p>  
 <p>4. About the bridge <i>penalty</i> of him who is a mourner (navînî<i>d</i>âr) and <i>self-</i>wounder in the three nights <i>after a death, and</i> how <i>it is</i> as though <i>they</i> who are living <i>should</i> again pour melted ore on a human being. 5. About the punishment for a woman who gives herself in marriage to a righteous man, <i>and</i> comes away <a name="fr_636"></a><a href="#fn_631"><font size="1">2</font></a> from him; such as when a hedgehog <a name="fr_637"></a><a href="#fn_632"><font size="1">3</font></a> should be constantly going in and coming out by her sexual organ; and the cutting off of her way from the best existence. 6. About the non-deliverance of a soul of the wicked from hell till the future existence. 7. About the punishment of the wicked <i>there is</i> this, too, <i>it is</i> as though a sheep which is alive should be remaining tied by the legs, head downwards, and <i>there</i> should be a specific exudation of its toes through running at the nose <a name="fr_638"></a><a href="#fn_633"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. About the Gâthas for an ordeal <a name="fr_639"></a><a href="#fn_634"><font size="1">5</font></a> of the spiritual <i>existence</i>, which is concealed in every mode, <i>being</i> without a footing (<i>a</i>p<i>â</i>-pâstakŏ), as it were, for him who is a righteous chanter of the Gâthas.</p>  
 <p>9. The excellence <i>of</i> righteousness is perfect.</p>
