 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The third fargar<i>d</i>, A she m-vohû <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_765"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is about</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_233"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 233</font></a></p>  
 <p>admonition as to the praising of righteousness, which is itself the production of true awe of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the perfection of existences, the better state of prayers <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_766"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and the greatest assemblage of righteousness, good breeding, humility (avŏpatagîh), awe of the spiritual <i>existence</i>, extreme joyfulness, and comfort and enlightenment of soul. 2. <i>Also</i> the equipment (pa<i>d</i>mûkîh) of him who is practising as a high-priest is righteousness and the maintenance of the worship <i>and</i> obeisance for the spirit of righteousness.</p>  
 <p>3. Of righteousness perfect is the excellence.</p>
