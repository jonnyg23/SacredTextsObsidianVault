 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Ganabâ-sar-ni</i>g<i>a</i>d<i> Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The fourth section is miscellaneous: about a warm bath <i>being</i> in a house of what kind, the position of security of the fireplace, the watchfulness to be upheld there, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 2. About the strength that a horse <i>has</i> to exert for the sake of the earth, and that which is to be exerted in that mode for the sake of fire. 3. About food <i>and</i> other matters which may be prepared with fire, <i>and</i> the security of the fire in like manner. 4. About fire which, even on the road, is <i>free</i> from throwing away, bodily refuse <a name="fr_239"></a><a href="#fn_238"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and dead matter <a name="fr_240"></a><a href="#fn_239"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and <i>from</i> the injury and harm owing thereto; the various safeguards of fire from <i>being</i> given to an infidel (ag-denô) <i>or</i> a child; the distance of the fire from a rivulet <a name="fr_241"></a><a href="#fn_240"><font size="1">3</font></a>; the penalty for throwing <i>it</i> away, <i>or</i> other sin as regards it; <i>and</i> the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 91</font></a></p>  
 <p>proportion of nourishment and preparation for the fire in summer, <i>and</i> also in winter.</p>  
 <p>5. About picketing (ba<i>r</i>â ni<i>s</i>âstanŏ) a horse, that is, how <i>it is</i> justifiable when <i>it is</i> in water and dust, how it is <i>so</i> when really in very distressing bodily refuse, and how it is so when even in bodily refuse <i>that is</i> tolerable. 6. About the proportion of nourishment for mankind, fire, and cattle. 7. About receiving a guest, the praise of liberality, and the grandeur of the liberal, the contempt for stinginess, and the want of the wanderer.</p>  
 <p>8. About the mode of wearing garments in a dwelling of Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, even so far as a bandage of four rags for protection <a name="fr_242"></a><a href="#fn_241"><font size="1">1</font></a>; the care of them each separately, the wages of the makers <i>and</i> ornamenters of each one, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 9. About <i>having</i> procured a street-keeper (kûgpânŏ) for the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, the business of the street-keeper thereof, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>10. About preparing in the summer a store for the winter. 11. About reaping a field of corn, the Avesta <a name="fr_243"></a><a href="#fn_242"><font size="1">2</font></a> for the first reaping, and <i>having</i> consecrated the first sheaf with the dedication (shnûman) to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> the lord. 12. About the union of those of the good religion together, both in removing want and <i>in</i> union even with infidels in that which</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 92</font></a></p>  
 <p>is not detrimental to the religion, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 13. About duty as regards the produce of plants <i>and</i> animals; first, suitable eating; <i>and</i> secondly, moderate eating and avoidance of profusion.</p>  
 <p>14. About possessions which belong to the nobles, <i>and</i> those which belong to the multitude; <i>in</i> what manner that which belongs to the multitude <i>has</i> to come into the possession of the nobles; <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 15. About the enviousness (zigûrîh) of the beast <i>of burden</i>, ox, and sheep, and also <i>of</i> people; that is, in how many of the multitudes, each separately, it is produced; and whatever is on the same subject. 16. And also much other adjudication and information on similar intelligence.</p>  
 <p>17. Perfect is the excellence of righteousness.</p>
