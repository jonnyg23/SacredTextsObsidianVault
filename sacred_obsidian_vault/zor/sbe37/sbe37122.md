 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">CHAPTER CLXI.</h4>  
 <p>1. About <i>one</i> supremely <a name="fr_1650"></a><a href="#fn_1609"><font size="1">2</font></a> acquainted with the three <a name="fr_1651"></a><a href="#fn_1610"><font size="1">3</font></a> codes (dâ<i>d</i>ŏ) of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion there is <i>this:One</i> supremely acquainted</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_408"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 408</font></a></p>  
 <p>with the three codes of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion is he <i>of</i> the primitive faith whose insight into the good religion is even such that he knows <i>how</i> to discriminate <i>and</i> announce the statements (v<i>â</i><i>k</i>akŏ) of the Hadha-mãthric and Gâthic from <i>those of</i> the Law, <i>those</i> of the Legal <i>and</i> Gâthic from <i>those of</i> the Hadha-mãthric, and <i>those</i> of the Hadha-mãthric and Legal from <i>those of</i> the Gâthas. 2. Also to the statements (v<i>â</i><i>k</i>akŏ) in the Lawwhich is superior <a name="fr_1652"></a><a href="sbe37123.htm#fn_1611"><font size="1">1</font></a> knowledge about the worldly <i>existences</i>is allotted (vakhtŏ) the worldliness <i>of</i> the Hadha-mãthric and also <i>of</i> the Gâthic; <i>to those</i> in the Gâthaswhich are superior knowledge about the spiritual <i>existences</i>is <i>allotted</i> the spirituality <i>of</i> the Hadha-mãthric and even that of the Law; and <i>to those</i> in the Hadha-mãthrawhich is superior knowledge about <i>things</i> intermediate <i>between</i> the spiritual and worldly <i>existences</i>is <i>allotted</i> the intermediate matter (mîyânîkîh) <i>of</i> the Gâthic <i>and</i> also <i>of</i> the Legal <a name="fr_1653"></a><a href="sbe37123.htm#fn_1612"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>
