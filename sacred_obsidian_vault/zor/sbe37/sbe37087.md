 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 273</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXVIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The fifteenth fargar<i>d</i>, A<i>d</i>-fravakhshyâ <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_992"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about the seven <a name="fr_1002"></a><a href="#fn_993"><font size="1">2</font></a> perfections of the admonitions of the religion. 2. First, association with the beneficent spirit of the creator, through hearing <a name="fr_1003"></a><a href="#fn_994"><font size="1">3</font></a>, learning, <i>and</i> practising his religion; <i>and</i> this, too, that thereby arises the preservation <i>of</i> the good creation when the destroyer is separated (van<i>g</i>î<i>d</i>). 3. Second, about separation from the destructive evil spirit <a name="fr_1004"></a><a href="#fn_995"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> the contempt which is due to his arrogance and falsehood, the chief of all his vice. 4. Third, governing the temper <a name="fr_1005"></a><a href="#fn_996"><font size="1">5</font></a> by good thoughts, good words, and good deeds; and this, that, whoever <i>of</i> you does not so use this liturgy as thought and word <a name="fr_1006"></a><a href="#fn_997"><font size="1">6</font></a>, they <i>will</i> not allot him light, they <i>will</i> not allot <i>him</i> the best existence, <i>and</i> he is miserable up to the last <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="#fn_998"><font size="1">7</font></a>. 5. Fourth, about the perfection <i>of</i> the nature of next-of-kin marriage <a name="fr_1008"></a><a href="#fn_999"><font size="1">8</font></a>, which is when <i>it is</i> a giving of one's own (khû<i>d</i>îh-dahi<i>s</i>nîh); and the decision</p>

 <p>given <i>about</i> it, which is the goodness of one's own progeny for the manifestation of progeny; also the relationship, sturdiness, effectiveness, advantageousness, ownership, and giving in next-of-kin marriage. 6. <i>Its</i> first accomplishment <i>was by</i> the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> in the fatherhood <i>of</i> Vohûman <a name="fr_1009"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">1</font></a> who <i>was</i> the first progeny, and from that arising of the practice (var<i>z</i>-yehevûnîh) <i>came</i> the progress of the spiritual and worldly creatures and much connected <i>therewith</i>, such as the arising of splendour from light, radiance from splendour, <i>and</i> lustrousness from radiance, <i>and</i> the fully progressive diffusion and succession <i>of</i> mankind till the renovation <i>of the universe;</i> also, through spiritual and worldly passing on in the spiritual and worldly <i>existences</i>, Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i>'s <a name="fr_1010"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">2</font></a> acceptance of the motherly glory <i>was</i> an ennoblement. 7. Fifth, about providing <i>and</i> maintaining the high-priests <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1002"><font size="1">3</font></a> who are provided with a spiritual lord and possessing priestly instruction; the listening of his authorities of every kind to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and the reward of the beneficent good works <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1003"><font size="1">4</font></a> of the high-priesthood, <i>are</i> authority for Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>; <i>and</i> the reward of the good works of the high-priesthood is <i>their</i> relation <i>to</i> the best existence. 8. Sixth, about the praise, obeisance, <i>and</i> ceremonial <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1004"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>for</i> the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>; and this, too, that further conference <i>with</i> Vohûman <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1005"><font size="1">6</font></a> arises, and wisdom <i>and</i> advantage <a name="fr_1015"></a><a href="#fn_1006"><font size="1">7</font></a> are taught by</p>

 <p>him thus: 'Thou shouldst be a supplicant for the immortal progress of the soul <a name="fr_1016"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">1</font></a>, O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! so that Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>may be</i> lord of the creatures <a name="fr_1017"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and the practice of propitiation <i>by</i> mankind <i>may be</i> that <i>for</i> him, also a proportion of the ordering of obeisance <a name="fr_1018"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">3</font></a>.' 9. About the sovereignty of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1019"></a><a href="#fn_1010"><font size="1">4</font></a>even through the reward given <i>at</i> the bridge <i>of judgment</i>which is in his good assemblies <a name="fr_1020"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">5</font></a>, those of the restorer of the world, the destroyer of the evil <i>one, and</i> the benefiter.</p>  
 <p>10. This, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'Thou becomest, through complete mindfulness, O Spîtâmân! a perpetual adopter (giriftâr) of this ceremonial of mine <a name="fr_1021"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">6</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>11. About Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>having</i> given power <a name="fr_1022"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">7</font></a> to the creatures, the preparation <a name="fr_1023"></a><a href="#fn_1014"><font size="1">8</font></a> of the power, and the contempt <a name="fr_1024"></a><a href="#fn_1015"><font size="1">9</font></a> <i>for</i> the evil spirit and his appliances; Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> and the creations gave that contempt back to the evil spirit and the primary (kâdmon) demons who are those produced by the demons.</p>

 <p>13. Perfect <i>is</i> the excellence <i>of</i> righteousness.</p>
