 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XL.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The seventeenth fargar<i>d</i>, Spe<i>n</i>tâ-mainyû <a name="fr_1078"></a><a href="#fn_1068"><font size="1">5</font></a>, is about <i>this</i>, that Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> produced the creatures through wisdom, <i>and</i> maintains <i>them</i> in truth. 2. This, too, that the best thing <a name="fr_1079"></a><a href="#fn_1069"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>for</i> every one is thought in a high-priest who is the tongue of a spiritual lord <a name="fr_1080"></a><a href="#fn_1070"><font size="1">7</font></a>; in a high-priest, who <i>has</i> to maintain thought, no appliances of the body <i>are</i> to lie unto the spiritual lord on account of affection <i>for</i> the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_283"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 283</font></a></p>  
 <p>worldly <i>existence</i>. 3. Also that the spiritual lord is always true; of the tonguewhen he (<i>the priest</i>) speaks falsehood with the tongueare those words which he does not believe through the spiritual lord, and <i>it is</i> owing to this, too, when, of all the body, the tongue first dies. 4. 'I say unto thee, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân! that thou shouldst speak with the tongue just as thou thinkest with the mind, and thou shouldst accomplish work with both hands in complete mindfulness <a name="fr_1081"></a><a href="#fn_1071"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' 5. And this, too, that he who shall act thus is sagacious, and he is the father of righteousness through wisdom <a name="fr_1082"></a><a href="#fn_1072"><font size="1">2</font></a>; and whoever would do that which has happened, thoroughly observes <i>it</i> on account of that <i>which</i> has not happened. 6. Also this, <i>that</i> in the person of him who shall do that which he understands, <i>and</i> asks again about that which he does not understand, the propitious spirit of wisdom is lodging.</p>  
 <p>7. About cattle <i>being</i> produced for the assistance <i>of</i> mankind, and the pastures of pleasure for the assistance <i>of</i> cattle <a name="fr_1083"></a><a href="#fn_1073"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 8. This, too, that the archangels injure the evil demon and wicked people, <i>but</i> they do not injure righteous people <a name="fr_1084"></a><a href="#fn_1074"><font size="1">4</font></a> and the sage. 9. This, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'In scanty opulence do not murmur (a<i>l</i> mang) owing to good works <a name="fr_1085"></a><a href="#fn_1075"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>and</i> thus in great opulence much good work arises.' 10. This, too, that beneficence gives all to the good, <i>and</i> it is no further the villain whom the sacred beings maintain <a name="fr_1086"></a><a href="#fn_1076"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>11. About the tongue <i>of</i> a true speaker <i>being</i> given for the satisfying (vi<i>g</i>âri<i>s</i>nŏ) of disputants,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_284"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 284</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and for</i> declaring who is acquitted <i>or</i> incriminated; and the ordeal that is a demonstrator, to acquit <i>or</i> convict, which he whose tongue is truthful <i>has</i> acceptedand it shall make his statement currenthas developed its jurisdiction in the world, <i>and</i> diminished distress. 12. <i>And</i> this, too, <i>is said</i>, that he gives out fire for disputes, so that it may make manifest the acquitted and incriminated, when he in whom are his immense complete mindfulness, and also righteousness, is guardian of the ordeal; and, when many inspect it, that which is the ritual of the ordeal believes them wicked <a name="fr_1087"></a><a href="#fn_1077"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>13. Perfect is the excellence of righteousness.</p>
