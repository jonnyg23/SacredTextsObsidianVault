 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The twenty-second fargar<i>d</i>, Airyaman <a name="fr_727"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">5</font></a>, is about the meeting of Kaî-Khûsrôî <a name="fr_728"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">6</font></a> and Vâê, the long-continuing lord <a name="fr_729"></a><a href="#fn_724"><font size="1">7</font></a> next to the renovation <i>of the</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>universe; and</i> Kaî-Khûsrôî's asking Vâê, the long-continuing lord, about his smiting so many of the ancients who have been the highest of mankind in splendour and glory. 2. The reply of Vâê, the long-continuing lord, about his smiting them; and, upon that answer, Kaî-Khûsrôî's taking Vâê, the long-continuing lord, and transforming <i>him</i> into the shape of a camel, mounting <i>him, and</i> going, with the Irânian levies (han<i>g</i>amanôîkân), to the place where the immortal Haoi<i>s</i>t, <i>son</i> of Geurva <a name="fr_730"></a><a href="#fn_725"><font size="1">1</font></a>, lies in strength <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_726"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and his letting him lie; also <i>his</i> going beyond (kâdmon) him to the place where Tûs <a name="fr_732"></a><a href="#fn_727"><font size="1">3</font></a>, the banisher of strife, lay in strength, and his letting him also lie; and his going beyond him to the place where Kaî-<i>A</i>pîvêh <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_728"><font size="1">4</font></a> lies, and his letting him also lie.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_225"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 225</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. His proceeding beyond them, and meeting on the road with that beneficial victor Sôshâns <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_729"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and <i>being</i> asked by that beneficial victor thus: 'What man art thou who sittest aloft on Vâê, the long-continuing lord, so that thou makest Vâê fly, the long-continuing lord transformed into the shape of a camel?' 4. The speaking of Kai-Khûsrôî, in reply to Sôshâns, thus: 'I am Kai-Khûsrôî.' 5. The extolling of Kai-Khûsrôî, by Sôshâns, as regards his <i>having</i> extirpated the idol-temples on the shore of Lake <i>K</i>ê<i>k</i>ast <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_730"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and his smiting the wizard Frâsîy<i>â</i><i>v</i> <a name="fr_736"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>6. The glorifying of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion <i>by</i> Kai-Khûsrôî; the coming of the powerful being Keresâsp <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">4</font></a>, club in hand, advancing towards them at the dwelling of that wizard Ges <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">5</font></a>; the standing up of Tûs, the banisher of strife, and his calling to Keresâsp for reliance upon the Gâtha lore and <i>for</i> union with them; and the praising of righteousness <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>by</i> Keresâsp, and his throwing <i>away</i> the arm-breaker.</p>  
 <p>7. As to these, too, it says that so those men come together <i>for</i> producing the renovation <i>of the universe who</i> are mentioned in this fargar<i>d</i>, and also in other places, <i>and</i> are all experienced <i>and</i> eminent</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 226</font></a></p>  
 <p>doers, and all powerful <i>and</i> brave; and they shall produce the renovation through a desire for an existence undecaying, immortal, hungerless, and thirstless for ever <i>and</i> everlasting.</p>  
 <p>8. It is perfect excellence <i>that is</i> righteousness.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_722"></a><a href="sbe37072.htm#fr_727">223:5</a> The appellation of Yas. LIV which begins with the words â airy<i>e</i>mâ i<i>s</i><i>h</i>yô; it is here written aîrêmanŏ (B) and aîremanŏ (K) in Pahlavi.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_723"></a><a href="sbe37072.htm#fr_728">223:6</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XIII, 14.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_724"></a><a href="sbe37072.htm#fr_729">223:7</a> Pahl. vâê-î dêrang-khû<i>d</i>âî = Av. vaya dareghô<i>h</i><i>v</i>adhâta who is mentioned as a good spirit in Ny. I, 1. There are, however, <a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a> two Vâês (see Dd. XXX, 4; Mkh. II, 115), the good Vâê who assists the departed soul, and the bad Vâê who opposes it; the former is closely connected with the angel Râm in Yt. XV, 0, 58, and the latter with Astô-vîdhôtu, the demon of death, in Vend. V, 8, 9; Bd. XXVIII, 35. They appear to be personifications of the upper and lower air, respectively; the former being considered pure through its connection with the sacred beings, and the latter impure through contamination by the demons. Possibly the legend about Vâê in our text may have been suggested by the words vayû-beredubyô and vayôi in Yas. LIII, 6, 7; in which case, this fargar<i>d</i> must be considered, to some extent, as a continuation of the preceding one. According to Dd. XXXVI, 3 Kaî-Khûsrôî was made to pass away by Vâê.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_726"></a><a href="sbe37072.htm#fr_731">224:2</a> Reading hang, which can also mean 'a cave;' but we can likewise read hûg, 'spiritual life.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_728"></a><a href="sbe37072.htm#fr_733">224:4</a> Av. Kavi Aipivanghu of Yt. XIII, 132, XIX, 71. He was son of Kaî-Kavâ<i>d</i>, brother of Kâî-Ûs, and great-grandfather of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp's grandfather (see Bd. XXXI, 25, 28, 31, 34).</p>
