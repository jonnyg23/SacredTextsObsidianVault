 <p><a name="page_12"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 12</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER III.</h3>  
 <p>1. The Var<i>s</i>tmânsar <a name="fr_48"></a><a href="#fn_48"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>contains</i> particulars about the birth of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, his attaining the religion <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_49"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject <a name="fr_50"></a><a href="#fn_50"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 2. A notice (numâ<i>d</i>) of the priestliness, discipleship, spiritual lordship, priestly authority, and steadfastness which are in his original more concise words of the Gâthas <a name="fr_51"></a><a href="#fn_51"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 3. The explanation (zand) of the statements about everything and also the good arrangement (khu<i>s</i>ra<i>d</i>akŏ) are such as that which <i>one</i> speaks of thus:'<i>It</i> is the Var<i>s</i>tmânsar which has given forth an exposition upon every<i>thing</i>.' 4. So that, in the Var<i>s</i>tmânsar, something is said about every<i>thing</i> that is mentioned in the Gâthas.</p>  
 <p>5. Of righteousness the excellence is perfect.</p>
