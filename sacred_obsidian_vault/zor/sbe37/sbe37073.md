 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. Of the Var<i>s</i>tmânsar <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">1</font></a> there are twenty-three fargar<i>d</i>s, <i>and</i> the first is the Aêthrapaiti<i>s</i> <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">2</font></a>, on the asking of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, by Maî<i>d</i>ôk-mâh <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">3</font></a>, about the nature of the birth of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and his coming to the religion. 2. And the reply of Zaratû<i>s</i>t about the combative coming together of the life-causing and death-causing spirits at his birth <a name="fr_743"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 3. This, too, that when the fellow-villagers <a name="fr_744"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">5</font></a> of her who bore <i>him</i> saw his head <a name="fr_745"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_227"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 227</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>they</i> considered <i>it</i> the shoulder <i>of</i> <a name="fr_746"></a><a href="#fn_741"><font size="1">1</font></a> Arekdvîksûr <a name="fr_747"></a><a href="#fn_742"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and his</i> chest and back <i>those of</i> Aharî<i>s</i>vang <a name="fr_748"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>and</i> when <i>they saw</i> his full <a name="fr_749"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">4</font></a> bosom <i>they considered it that of</i> the spirit of liberality <a name="fr_750"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">5</font></a>; and by <i>his</i> side <i>was</i> the Kayân glory <a name="fr_751"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">6</font></a> to rub (mû<i>s</i>tanŏ) his bosom.</p>  
 <p>4. The speaking of Zaratû<i>s</i>t spiritually, on the grievous bringing forth of <i>his</i> head <a name="fr_752"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">7</font></a>, thus: 'As a spiritual lord is <i>my</i> desire, do thou who art the Zôti speak forth to me <a name="fr_753"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">8</font></a>;' and the reply from Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> thus: 'So shouldst thou be the priestly master as regards whatever righteousness I speak forth with righteous intelligence; thou art of very much value, thou <i>art</i> very righteous, thou <i>art</i> most intelligent, and thou <i>wilt</i> state the religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers to creatures of every kind.' 5. Through that saying an arrow reaches spiritually unto the demons, just as from a mighty chief warrior of Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_754"></a><a href="#fn_749"><font size="1">9</font></a>, like him in a mountain dwelling (garânŏ mân) who <i>has</i> shot an arrow for an attack (patkopi<i>s</i>nŏ) opposing those in coats of mail. 6. The evil spirit grumbled (dandî<i>d</i>ŏ) to the demons thus: 'Evil <i>has</i> it become <i>for</i> you <i>who</i> are demons, but</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_228"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 228</font></a></p>  
 <p>you are unobservant.' 7. Even so Zaratû<i>s</i>t proclaimed life <i>free</i> from the control of the demons, when this same <i>saying was</i> uttered by him, thus: 'As a spiritual lord is <i>my</i> desire;' and, <i>at</i> the falling of the demons upon Zaratû<i>s</i>t for <i>his</i> destruction, an incarnation (tanû) of <i>its</i> spiritual existence stood opposed to them, in that weapon <i>proceeding</i> from Zaratû<i>s</i>t, to keep them back.</p>  
 <p>8. And he spoke again thus: 'The religion of the benefiters progresses there in him who, through good actions, <i>has</i> joyfulness owing to his righteousness <a name="fr_755"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">1</font></a>;' and, through that saying, an arrow reaches spiritually unto the demons, equal to ten <i>of</i> that which <i>was</i> first spoken, and, <i>at</i> the falling of the demons upon Zaratû<i>s</i>t for <i>his</i> destruction, it stood spiritually opposed to them, and that weapon <i>proceeding</i> from Zaratû<i>s</i>t kept <i>them</i> back.</p>  
 <p>9. The third utterance of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, on the bringing forth of <i>his</i> arms, <i>was</i> thus: 'That which the first existence produced is to be so practised, with attention, through actions to be concealed <i>by</i> him who is a priestly authority (ra<i>d</i>ŏ) <a name="fr_756"></a><a href="#fn_751"><font size="1">2</font></a>;' and through that saying an arrow reaches spiritually unto the demons, equal to one hundred <i>of</i> that which <i>was</i> first spoken, and, <i>at</i> the falling of a demon upon Zaratû<i>s</i>t for <i>his</i> death, <i>its</i> spirit, <i>as</i> a sacred being, kept the demon away from Zaratû<i>s</i>t.</p>  
 <p>10. And, when the whole body of Zaratû<i>s</i>t <i>was</i> brought forth, trouble (âsipŏ) fell among the demons, and the demons rushed back to hell in haste; light</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_229"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 229</font></a></p>  
 <p>increased among the creatures, and every creature of the beneficent sacred being is pleased and talked of virtuous conduct. 11. And Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> took away Zaratû<i>s</i>t with joyfulness to provide security for <i>him</i>, and Arekdvîksûr, Aharî<i>s</i>vang, and the primitive and Kayân glory <i>in</i> the body of Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke to Zaratû<i>s</i>t of <i>its</i> production <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> thus: 'Thou shouldst think of him who is wise.' 12. Thereupon Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke spiritually, <i>in</i> reply, thus: 'I am a Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipper, I profess the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship of Zaratû<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_752"><font size="1">1</font></a>; <i>and</i> this <i>means</i> that I am an apostle of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and am sent <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>.'</p>  
 <p>13. And Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke to Zaratû<i>s</i>t thus: '<i>As to</i> the sacred beings <i>of</i> the worldly <i>existence</i>, do thou beg companionship from them; <i>but as to</i> the demons, do thou long for (dôsh) <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_753"><font size="1">2</font></a> separation from every one of them; practise good thoughts, good words, and good deeds, and abstain from evil thoughts, evil words, and evil deeds.' 14. Also about vigilantly reverencing the sacred beings, and the reward thereof; not strengthening the vile, nor weakening <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_754"><font size="1">3</font></a> the good; expounding <i>for</i> the disturber of religion (dênô-pa<i>d</i>rêsh), and producing liberality for the accepters of religion; and not turning <i>away</i> from the religion on account of fondness for body and life.</p>  
 <p>15. The accepting of such advice spiritually <i>by</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and his glorifying Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, for creativeness,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_230"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 230</font></a></p>  
 <p>sovereignty, and all goodness, and the primary archangels <i>and</i> other good creations, each separately, for their own special glory <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 16. And, afterwards, the grumbling of the evil spirit maliciously, at that vexation, thus: 'I have produced, for the annoyance <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">2</font></a> of <i>any</i> upholder of that religion of thine, 99,999 wizards, 99,999 wolf-worshippers <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and 99,999 apostates.'</p>  
 <p>17. Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke to Zaratû<i>s</i>t thus: 'Maintain this religion steadfastly, for through the assistance of this religion I, who am Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, <i>will</i> be with thee, and the omniscient wisdom becomes thine, and extends to thy disciples, Maî<i>d</i>ôk-mâh <a name="fr_763"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">4</font></a>, Parsha<i>d</i>-gâvô <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>S</i>ênô <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">6</font></a>, Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, Frashô<i>s</i>tar, and <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">7</font></a>, the teacher of public observance and will to the righteous, <i>besides</i> many of the people who are diligent and even those who are idle, and their good works and praise <i>will</i> be owned by thee.' 18. Likewise</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_231"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 231</font></a></p>  
 <p>about the worldly display of the religion to Zaratû<i>s</i>t <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the accepting of the religion <i>by</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t through recitation and faith, and the reverence of the Ahunavair <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_762"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>by</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t.</p>  
 <p>19. Also about Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>having</i> created the creatures in the spiritual <i>existence, and</i> their allotment out to the worldly <i>existence</i>, the superiority of the righteous man as compared with other creatures, and, among mankind, <i>of</i> him who is relying on the provisions of the law and <i>its</i> unchangeableness from goodness, <i>and</i> who is a teacher and provider of teaching <i>as to</i> the pre-eminent existence of the good religion of the sacred beings.</p>  
 <p>20. And a summary about the bringing together of that fire which is the residue of a fire in a house, for the reverence of that water which is nearest to the dwelling, and <i>of</i> any spirit of a kinsman; and as <i>to him</i> who leaves that fire, water, and spirit, and, on account of a similar desire, reverences another fire, water, and spirit, <i>but</i> none of them can accept that ceremonial, and the acceptance of that man's ceremonial <i>by</i> the others <i>will </i>have occurred just when the <i>former</i> three are reverenced by him.</p>  
 <p>21. Righteousness is perfect excellence.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_735"></a><a href="sbe37073.htm#fr_740">226:1</a> The second of the Nasks and third of the Gâthic division (see Bk. VIII, Chap. I, 9, 12). It is a second commentary on the Gâthas, devoting a fargar<i>d</i> to each ha of the Gâthas and to each Gâthic formula, as in the Sû<i>d</i>kar Nask, but beginning with an extra fargar<i>d</i> about the birth and calling of Zaratû<i>s</i>t. Its chief object appears to be the quotation of texts, both from the Gâthas and from sources no longer known.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_736"></a><a href="sbe37073.htm#fr_741">226:2</a> Here written asrapâitô<i>s</i> (B) and asrapâîti<i>s</i> (K) in Pahlavi, which, no doubt, stand for Av. aêthrapaiti<i>s</i>, Pers. hêrbad, 'a Zoroastrian priest.' This name may either refer to the general subject of the fargar<i>d</i>, or have been the first word of its Avesta text; as it seems not intended to quote any section of the Yasna, although the guardian spirits of the priests are reverenced in Yas. XXVI, 7, 8, before commencing the recitation of the Gâthas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_737"></a><a href="sbe37073.htm#fr_742">226:3</a> Av. Maidhyô-m<i>a</i>ungha; he was first cousin of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and also his first disciple (see Bd. XXXII, 2, 3; Zs. XI, 10 n).</p>
