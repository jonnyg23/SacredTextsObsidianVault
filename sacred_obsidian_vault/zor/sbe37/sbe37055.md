 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The fifth fargar<i>d</i>, Khshmaibyâ <a name="fr_510"></a><a href="#fn_506"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about the forgetfulness of a father for a son, a son for a father, a brother for a brother, a friend for a friend, a husband (mânpatŏ) for a wife (nârîk), and a wife for a husband in a measurable time, through excess and festivity (khang); and the unforgetfulness of the spirit of the Gâthas for so many reciters and chanters of the Gâthas. 2. About the complaint of the spirit of the Gâthas when a high-priest, although priest of the country-folk (dehîgânŏ), passes away in an out-district <a name="fr_511"></a><a href="#fn_507"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and the body of that man does not come back to his own land; whatever is relating to that, <i>and</i>, besides that, what is to be born in that</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 179</font></a></p>  
 <p>land, and the oppressiveness of apostates <i>which</i> arises. 3. About the superior power of the spirit of the Gâthas, <i>and</i> also that of liberality, in preserving the soul from hell.</p>  
 <p>4. Excellence <i>that is</i> perfect is righteousness.</p>
