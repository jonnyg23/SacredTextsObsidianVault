 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-sâstô <a name="fr_84"></a><a href="#fn_83"><font size="1">3</font></a> is about particulars of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 24</font></a></p>  
 <p>every kind <i>relating</i> to Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_85"></a><a href="#fn_84"><font size="1">1</font></a>; the temper, character, demeanour, knowledge, learning, and law for sovereignty; the government of the creatures, <i>and</i> the advancement of the will of the sacred beings requisite for it.</p>  
 <p>2. The creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> sends the archangels <a name="fr_86"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">2</font></a> on to Kai -Vi<i>s</i>tâsp as evidence about Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, <i>and</i> a reminder of Spîtâmân Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>of</i> the pure goodness of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion, <i>and of</i> the command for the ruler Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, as to <i>its</i> triumph, on accepting the religion from Zaratû<i>s</i>t. 3. The visible coming of the archangels to the metropolis, and, secondly, <i>their</i> domestication (handêmânîh) at the residence <i>of</i> Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and his companions; the envoys explanation of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s message to Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, <i>and</i> the accepting of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion <i>by</i> the obedient king Vi<i>s</i>tâsp.</p>  
 <p>4. The outpouring (sârînî<i>d</i>anŏ) of Ar<i>g</i>âsp the Khyôn <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_86"><font size="1">3</font></a>, by the demon of wrath, for war with Vi<i>s</i>tâsp</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 25</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and</i> disturbance of Zaratû<i>s</i>t; the arrangements <i>and</i> movements of king Vi<i>s</i>tâsp for that war, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>5. Excellence <i>that is</i> perfect is righteousness.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_83"></a><a href="sbe37014.htm#fr_84">23:3</a> Corresponding to the tenth word, dazdâ, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the eleventh Nask in other Rivâyats. <a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 24</font></a> Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-sâstô means 'the instruction of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp,' and is corrupted into Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-shâh, Vi<i>s</i>tâspâd, or Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, in the Rivâyats, which also state that it contained originally sixty kardah, or ʓûrat, of which only ten, or eight, were recovered after the time of Alexander. The last number refers, no doubt, to the eight fargar<i>d</i>s still extant under the corrupt name Vi<i>s</i>tâsp Ya<i>s</i>t, which probably consist of fragments of the Avesta text of this Nask; but in comparing that text with this description it must be remembered that the author is describing the contents of the Pahlavi version which would contain much commentary.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_84"></a><a href="sbe37014.htm#fr_85">24:1</a> The king of Iran in the time of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, who accepted the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion; the last king of the old history derived from the Avesta (see Chap. XIII, 15, 16).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_85"></a><a href="sbe37014.htm#fr_86">24:2</a> Compare Vi<i>s</i>tâsp Yt. 40.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_86"></a><a href="sbe37014.htm#fr_87">24:3</a> The 'deadly <i>H</i><i>v</i>yaona Are<i>g</i>a<i>d</i>-aspa' of Yt. IX, 30, XVII, 50, whom Kavi Vî<i>s</i>tâspa prayed to be delivered from. According to the Yâ<i>d</i>kâr-î Zarîrân, Ar<i>g</i>âsp, king of the Khyôns, made war upon <a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 25</font></a> Vi<i>s</i>tâsp on account of the latter's conversion to Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship and was defeated with great difficulty in a most desperate battle which is also described in the Shâhnâmah. Whether the Khyôns were the Chionitae of later times is uncertain.</p>
