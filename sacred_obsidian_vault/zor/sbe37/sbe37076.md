 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The fourth fargar<i>d</i>, Yê<font size="-1">N</font>hê-hâtãm <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_767"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>states that</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke to Zaratû<i>s</i>t the Spîtâmân thus: 'Utter the words of the ceremonial and obeisance <i>for</i> us who are Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>and</i> <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_768"><font size="1">3</font></a> the archangels, because they are, O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! thy ritual <i>for</i> water <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_769"><font size="1">4</font></a>, ritual <i>for</i> plants, ritual <i>for</i> a guardian spirit of the righteous, and ritual <i>for</i> an angel of a spiritual <i>existence, or</i> who is even appointed for a worldly <i>existence</i>.'</p>  
 <p>2. And Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke thus: 'I <i>will</i> utter the words of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, which are opposed to harm</p>  
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>

 </p>

 <p>3. Of righteousness perfect is the excellence.</p>
