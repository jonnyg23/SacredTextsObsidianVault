 <p><a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 131</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXIX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sakâ</i>d<i>ûm Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>I. One section is the <i>H</i>a<i>k</i>î<i>d</i>akânistân ('<i>code of sequestrations</i>'), particulars about a statement of seized property, the retention thereof, <i>and</i> how was the confinement <i>of</i> that <i>which was</i> animate; how it is when <i>one</i> keeps <i>it</i> in a shepherd's-dog's care, and how it is when in the sequestrator's care (<i>h</i>a<i>k</i>î<i>d</i>akŏdârîh). 2. And when <i>it is</i> a seized horse of the warriors, how to keep <i>it</i> when it is not possible to retain <i>it</i> in confinement <i>of</i> any kind, and the damage which <i>has</i> arisen therefrom; what is the danger to occasion by it, how it is when the shelter (srâyi<i>s</i>nŏ) <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">1</font></a> is on all sides, <i>and</i> how it is when on one side; while the trust, when <i>there is</i> shelter, is in the extent of the shelter, how much <i>and of</i> what kind is the shelter. 3. When <i>it is</i> a seized beast <i>of burden</i>, after <i>its</i> coming into the possession of the sequestrator (<i>h</i>a<i>k</i>î<i>d</i>ak-dâr), for how long <i>he has</i> to order work for the reasoning thought of the herdsman, <i>and</i> how is that of the sequestrator, <i>in</i> like manner, before he quite attains to his share; even through his own reasoning thought the work is authorisedly ordered, and how <i>and in</i> what manner is the ordering of his work. 4. And when the seized <i>animal</i> has offspring, <i>in</i> what mode <i>he has</i> to milk <i>it</i>, as well as the nourishment of the young, and whatever is on the same subject; <i>also</i> the sin owing to doing <i>it</i> unlawfully.</p>  
 <p>5. About the sequestrator when the beast <i>of burden</i> seized comes into his possession, how it is</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_132"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 132</font></a></p>  
 <p>when its special reputation is altered, <i>and</i> how it is when it comes with utility and advantage for him. 6. About the seizer's keeping a sheep, which is seized, in <i>his</i> flock; that is, how the custom is produced, owing to its milk <i>being</i> for the <i>sacred</i> feast, and the notification of the feasts is owing to the seized <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">1</font></a> sheep; when, too, it is not possible to keep <i>it</i> in the flock, what is the mode of confining <i>it</i>; and when it is not possible to keep <i>it</i> in confinement, what <i>he has</i> to do with it. 7. About the wool of a sheep which is seized; that is, how it is when the shearing is even before the various times specified, <i>and</i> the sin of shearing when <i>it is</i> before the time specified, <i>or one</i> shears when there is no reason for shearing. 8. About the lambing (gurû<i>s</i>î<i>d</i>ŏ) of the sheep seized, and the sin owing to <i>its</i> not lambing.</p>  
 <p>9. About sheltering (sru<i>d</i>anŏ) <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">2</font></a> the seized <i>animal</i> in the most public place in a house, village, community, <i>or</i> province. 10. About the sin of the shepherd when, without saving <i>it</i> for the sequestrator, <i>and</i> through the guilelessness of the sequestrator, he shall carry away a female; <i>and</i> the sin which is owing to the offence as regards unlawfully beating <i>and</i> wounding <i>it</i>, before <i>it is</i> seized for the buyers of meat (khûr-kharânŏ), <i>and</i> other offences regarding it. 11. About the time appointed, between the shepherd and the sequestrator, for leading <i>and</i> bringing the female, <i>belonging to</i> the sequestrator, to the place for which the time is appointed; <i>in</i> the case when the shepherd arrives <i>and</i> the sequestrator <i>does</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_133"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 133</font></a></p>  
 <p>not, how that which <i>belongs to</i> the sequestrator is to come into the possession of the sequestrator, and when; when <i>it is</i> the sheep <i>or</i> beast <i>of burden</i> of a sequestrator <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">1</font></a>, how <i>it is</i> to come into the possession of that sequestrator; when the sheep <i>or</i> beast <i>of burden</i> which is seized dies in the possession of the sequestrator, how <i>and</i> how long <i>he has</i> to shelter (srû<i>d</i>an) the young ones (gurû<i>s</i>) and wool of the same several sheep; <i>and</i> the sin when he does not shelter <i>them</i>, or <i>does it</i> otherwise.</p>  
 <p>12. About a sheep <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">2</font></a> which is mingled among the flock of any one that is in sequestration, how it is when the shepherd, <i>and</i> how it is when the shepherd's dog, is its own; and when it is mingled among any flock owing to sequestration, how it is when the shepherd, <i>and</i> how it is when the shepherd's dog, [who is its own] <a name="fr_324"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">3</font></a> goes to another flock; how it is when the first flock-owner, <i>and</i> how it is when the second, is its own. 13. About the killing of a seized sheep <i>by</i> a shepherd's dog for necessary provisions; that is, how <i>it is</i> allowable, and <i>in</i> what mode <i>it is</i> to be done.</p>  
 <p>14. About him unto whom the sheep <i>or</i> beast <i>of burden</i> which is seized <i>is delivered</i> when it comes into a district; <i>and</i> the sequestrator's informing the governor of the district, in whose herd the sheep <i>or</i> beast <i>of burden</i> which is seized <i>remains</i>, as to the species, colour, and form of it <a name="fr_325"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 15. Watching over</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 134</font></a></p>  
 <p>a man with sheep, who is in a disabled state of illness owing to a wound <i>received</i> in his duty as regards slaughtering; the case when <i>he is</i> concealed from a passer-by (amat nîhân min vi<i>d</i>âr) and there is protection, when <i>he is</i> an eater <i>and</i> there is no protection, when <i>he is</i> not eating <i>and</i> there is protection, and when <i>he is</i> not eating <i>and</i> there is no protection.</p>  
 <p>16. About the distraction <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">1</font></a> of a sequestrator as regards a sheep <i>or</i> beast <i>of burden</i> which is seized, when <i>it</i> is one <i>out</i> of four varieties <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and when one <i>out</i> of three; when he nourishes <i>it for</i> half a year, <i>and</i> when <i>for</i> the duration of a year; when that which he obtains is a young <i>one</i>, and when that which he obtains is large, where <i>and</i> what is a shelter for it, and, <i>as to</i> the care of it, how it is when <i>in</i> a grain vault (<i>k</i>igârakŏ-1), <i>and</i> when it is <i>under</i> a tree; how it is when <i>in</i> a damaged cellar (varkhŏ-1-î kû<i>s</i>takŏ), and how it is when <i>in</i> a cage (pan<i>g</i>ar-1) which is not incomplete, <i>but is</i> broken, or <i>is</i> not incomplete and <i>is</i> sound, or <i>is</i> complete and sound.</p>  
 <p>17. About treasure which <i>they find</i> in the surroundings of a dwelling, <i>and</i> that which they find within the limits of the dwelling of any one. 18. About buried treasure when it is found by the side of a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a></p>  
 <p>road, <i>and</i> the ground is hard, how it is when it is one finger<i>-breadth</i> below, <i>and</i> how it is when <i>it is</i> two finger<i>-breadths;</i> as well as (ham-gûn) when the ground is soft, how it is when it is two finger<i>-breadths</i> below, <i>and</i> how it is when <i>it is</i> three finger<i>-breadths</i>. 19. When it is found within the road, and the ground is hard, how it is when it is two finger<i>-breadths</i> below, and how it is when <i>it is</i> three finger<i>-breadths;</i> <i>and</i> when the ground is soft, how it is when it is three finger<i>-breadths</i> below, <i>and</i> how it is when <i>it is</i> four finger<i>-breadths</i>. 20. When <i>it is</i> in an ascent <i>or</i> descent, there where <i>one</i> turns out from the road, and the ground is hard, how it is when it is below <i>up</i> to the instep <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and how <i>it is</i> when it is up to the middle of the leg (patî<i>s</i>tân) <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">2</font></a>; <i>and if</i> soft, how it is when it is below up to the middle of the leg, and how it is when <i>it is</i> up to the knee. 21. When <i>it is</i> in a stream of water, <i>and</i> the ground is hard, how it is when it is below up to the knee, and how <i>it is</i> when it is up to mid-thigh; and when the ground is soft, how it is when it is below up to mid-thigh, <i>and</i> how <i>it is</i> when it is up to the testicles. 22. When <i>it is</i> in a ford <i>through</i> the water, and the ground is hard, how it is when it is below up to the testicles, <i>and</i> how <i>it is</i> when it is up to the navel; and when the ground is soft, how it is when it is below <i>up to</i> the navel, and how <i>it is</i> when it is up to the mouth. 23. And when <i>it is</i> in a kitchen (â<i>s</i>khânŏ), the middle of a garden (van), <i>or</i> a sheepfold (pâh-hastŏ); that is, how <i>it is</i> when it is not a permanent residence (af<i>r</i><i>â</i><i>z</i>-mâni<i>s</i>nŏ) of <i>any</i>body,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and</i> how it is when <i>it is</i> a permanent residence <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>24. About <i>him</i> who nourishes a sheep which is seized; that is, how it is when <i>it is</i> out of his store, <i>and</i> how it is when he nourishes it <i>as</i> it arrives. 25. About a dispute as regards a sheep that is seized, when one <i>person</i> says <i>it was</i> born of the colour of the mother, <i>and another</i> one <i>says it was of</i> her form <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_329"><font size="1">2</font></a>, both <i>being</i> true; or one <i>person</i> mentions a single characteristic truly, <i>and another</i> one <i>mentions</i> many characteristics of it untruly; the <i>cases</i> when they mention <i>its</i> peculiarities otherwise, <i>and</i> in what manner and whatever is on the same subject. 26. About a sheep <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">3</font></a> seized, which <i>has</i> to pass on through the loftiest places in which <i>there</i> is lawfully shelter; <i>and</i> how<i></i> there are three years, three existences (ahvôn), three places, nine occasions, and also many other regulations on the same subject.</p>
