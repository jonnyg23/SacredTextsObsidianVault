 <p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 265</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The twelfth fargar<i>d</i>, the Yasna <a name="fr_954"></a><a href="#fn_946"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about the manifestation of good thoughts, good words, <i>and</i> good deeds by the religion <a name="fr_955"></a><a href="#fn_947"><font size="1">2</font></a>; the lodgment of the religion in good thoughts, good words, and good deeds; <i>and</i> whoever possesses good thoughts, whoever <i>has</i> good words, <i>and</i> whoever <i>has</i> good deeds, by him righteousness and the reward of the righteous are possessed. 2. This, too, that neither is he, who is not to be born <i>for</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t, an issue from parents who are not righteous, nor yet is he, for him, who is a manifestation of the righteous.</p>  
 <p>3. This, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'Thou shouldst give a glad-thinking desire <i>for</i> a spiritual lord, and an easy-bodied constitution, to their minds, the religion which I spoke forth <i>to</i> thee; so that the greatest, best, and most beneficial of existences <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_948"><font size="1">3</font></a>, that <i>are</i> those which cattle <i>are</i> wanting from men, <i>are</i> water, pasture, and freedom from danger <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_949"><font size="1">4</font></a>; <i>and</i> those which men <i>are wanting</i> from cattle are also food and clothing.' 4. This, too, that that which mankind ought to give to the sacred beings is a power for completeness <i>of</i> control; and that which the sacred beings ought to give to men is ever that which is good for them. 5. And this, too, that thou who <i>art</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> also suppliest <i>it</i> from those sacred beings, and <i>thou who art</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t also teachest <i>it</i> thoroughly to that best-ruling sovereignty <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_950"><font size="1">5</font></a> and authority.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>6. This, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'Let no <i>one</i> practise ill-perpetrated deeds, even though in a wilderness when far from publicity, nor in distress, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân! because Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the observer of everything, is aware of them; and the rule is <i>that</i> just as any one whatever <i>of</i> the embodied existence thinks, speaks, <i>and</i> practises, so great is his punishment.' 7. And this, too, that the best ceremonial and obeisance <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_951"><font size="1">1</font></a> are the ceremonial <i>and</i> obeisance of a righteous man.</p>  
 <p>8. About begging for life and receiving <i>it, there</i> is this, that <i>it is</i> customarily due to two methods (babâ): one, through leadership of righteousness <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is that <i>through</i> which <i>it</i> is evident that <i>it is</i> owing to virtuousness; and one, through service of righteousness <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is that which is not an evidence that <i>it is</i> owing to viciousness. 9. About <i>the case</i> where virtuousness is producing authority over truth, and truth over the tongue, so that thou speakest words through the will of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>. 10. And this, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'I am the propitious spirit who <i>was at</i> first <i>and</i> ever <i>will be</i>, and am not really deceived by anything.'</p>  
 <p>11. About fire <i>being</i> given <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> for shelter and assistance <i>by</i> the protection of mankind; <i>its</i> maintenance and assistance <i>by</i> mankind; and the openheartedness of the spirit of fire for him who shall perform obeisance to it, and for him who is to perform obeisance to it <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_953"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 12. The work which is the greatest that exists, <i>and</i> is accomplished in the future existence <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_954"><font size="1">4</font></a>, whereby the creatures become pure,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_267"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 267</font></a></p>  
 <p>occurs through fire; <i>and one</i> prays <i>for it</i> for the sake of the requirements which mankind acquired from the sacred beings. 13. This, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'Since thou <i>art</i> thus, '<i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! most propitiatory, that is, able to perform most for our pleasure, we are more promptly coming than Mânû<i>s</i><i>k</i>îhar <i>was</i> able to come, when thou beggest of us who are archangels, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_955"><font size="1">1</font></a>!'</p>  
 <p>14. About Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s exhibiting the creatures in the future existence to Zaratû<i>s</i>t. 15. <i>And</i> this, too, namely, the all-brilliance of the earth, the all-brilliance <i>of</i> the cattle, the all-brilliance <i>of</i> the plants, and the all-brilliance <i>of</i> every excellence <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">2</font></a> which is a manifestation of righteousness. 16. About the worshipping of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> by worshippers, through advancing <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s covenant (pa<i>d</i>mânŏ), which gave the world his righteousness; also the good protectiveness of his rule, and <i>of</i> the greatness therein, is owing to it <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and the name of the ruler is Wisdom <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">5</font></a>; likewise his ceremonialperformed while the creations owing to him live. when possessing bodies and possessing life <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">6</font></a>is a benefit <i>to</i> all the worldly and spiritual <i>existences</i>.</p>  
 <p>17. And this, too, <i>is said</i>, namely: 'Thou art our own <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and also <i>our</i> confederate, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân! likewise unto us thou comest with the reverence that is good <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">8</font></a>; thine, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! are the greatness and completeness in performance <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">9</font></a>, so that they</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_268"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 268</font></a></p>  
 <p>become thy greatness and completeness, that is, they are thine, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! and are boundless onwards from the middle, that is, we give thee a reward <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">1</font></a> so enormous that, when thou shouldst stand in the middle <i>of it</i>, thou <i>wouldst</i> not see to <i>its</i> limits, the width of the earth, the length of a river, and the height of the sun <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>18. Zaratû<i>s</i>t begged of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> thus: 'Give unto me him who becomes a disciple of men <a name="fr_975"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the mighty through meditation for the religion, <i>of them</i> who shall produce the actual progress of this my religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, <i>and</i> who <i>will</i> also explain the good practices to this <i>one</i> of mine, even the blessings set forth <i>by</i> me in the benedictions they possess.' 19. <i>And</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'I <i>will</i> give unto thee him who becomes a disciple of other men of the mighty; they are thy kinsmen and those confederates <i>of</i> theirs, and thine are their companions and their serfs <a name="fr_976"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">4</font></a>, who produce the progress of this thy religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers. 20. Mostly thine, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! are their worship and their homage; and, through their ceremonial and obeisance, the liberality of him who is worshipped is given to thee, <i>and</i> righteousness for the soul is with thee; also thy life exists owing to us, and likewise <i>thy</i> body <a name="fr_977"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! 21. Forth to thee <i>will</i> I, who am the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, come in both existences <a name="fr_978"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">6</font></a>, as assistance; thou becomest worthy, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! through Khû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <i>and</i> Amû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <a name="fr_979"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">7</font></a>, both of them, <i>and</i> through the gratification of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_269"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 269</font></a></p>  
 <p>me, who am Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, by those sayings <i>and</i> deeds which I, <i>who</i> am the most propitious of spirits, proclaimed unto thee.'</p>  
 <p>22. Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke thus: 'They have become applicants on him who is powerful <i>with</i> thee <a name="fr_980"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' 23. <i>And</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Thou becomest an applicant and powerful in the embodied existence.' 24. Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke thus: '<i>Be</i> thou a gratification to us in the slow progress <i>of</i> life, <i>thou</i> most beneficent (hû-dahâktûm) of existences! that is, thou shouldst give to us <a name="fr_981"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">2</font></a>.' 25. <i>And</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'I <i>will</i> gratify thee, <i>O</i> righteous Zaratû<i>s</i>t! in that best existence <a name="fr_982"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>26. Excellence <i>that</i> is perfect <i>is</i> righteousness.</p>
