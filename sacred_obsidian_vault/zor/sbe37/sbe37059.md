 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER X.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The ninth fargar<i>d</i>, Yathâi<i>s</i> <a name="fr_545"></a><a href="#fn_541"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about the devilry, the blighted destiny, the complete pollution, the grievous stench, the heinous sinfulness, and the annoyance <i>to</i> all spiritual and worldly virtue of the sodomite. 2. The atonement for grievous sinfulness and the appropriation of great good works by him who is a molester, and the awful sinfulness of him who is a propitiator, of that sinner. 3. Of the seven one mentions as evil, who are accounted equal to the evil spirit in vileness<i>such</i> as <i>A</i><i>z</i>-î Dahâk <a name="fr_546"></a><a href="#fn_542"><font size="1">2</font></a> in witchcraft, the serpent Srôbar in violence, Va<i>d</i>ak <a name="fr_547"></a><a href="#fn_543"><font size="1">3</font></a> in producing evil progeny <a name="fr_548"></a><a href="#fn_544"><font size="1">4</font></a>, Tûr-î Brâ<i>d</i>ar-vakhsh in destroying a righteous <i>man</i>, and an apostate <a name="fr_549"></a><a href="#fn_545"><font size="1">5</font></a> in grievous sinfulnessthe permitter and performer of</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_541"></a><a href="sbe37059.htm#fr_545">185:1</a> The first word of the sixth hâ of the first Gâtha (Yas. XXXIII, 1), here written yâsâî<i>s</i> in Pahlavi in both MSS.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_542"></a><a href="sbe37059.htm#fr_546">185:2</a> See Bk. VIII, Chaps. XIII, 8, XXXV, 13, and Dd. LXXII, 2-9, which last chapter contains further details regarding these seven heinous sinners, probably derived from the actual text of this ninth fargar<i>d</i> of the Sû<i>d</i>kar Nask.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_543"></a><a href="sbe37059.htm#fr_547">185:3</a> The mother of Dahâk (Dd. LXXII, 5), the same as Udaî in Bd. XXXI, 6; for her viciousness see Dd. LXXVIII, 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_544"></a><a href="sbe37059.htm#fr_548">185:4</a> Pahl. saryâ hûnŏ-dahakîh, which last word indicates an original Av. hunu<i>s</i>ta (see Pahl. Yas. L, 10 b).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_545"></a><a href="sbe37059.htm#fr_549">185:5</a> Both MSS. have Aharmanŏ, but this differs only in its last letter from aharmôk, 'an apostate,' which is the reading of Dd. LXXII, 9 and more suitable to the context.</p>
