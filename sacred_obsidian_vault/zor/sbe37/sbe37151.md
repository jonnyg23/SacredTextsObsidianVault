 <p><a name="page_501"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 501</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ERRATA.</h3>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37029.htm#page_89">89</a>, ll. 9, 10, for 'the Irânian nationality (Aîrîh)' read 'subjection (hêrih)' </p>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37059.htm#page_186">186</a>, ll. 19, 20, for 'preparation' read 'bespattering'</p>  
 <p>In several places it would be better to read âmûkŏ,' teaching,' instead of hamô-kun, 'every kind (or mode),' so as to obtain the following amended passages:</p>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37012.htm#page_23">23</a>, ll. 14-16. Then the exalting chanted teaching of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> for Zaratû<i>s</i>t is called, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37014.htm#page_24">24</a>, l. 1, the teaching for Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp; &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37039.htm#page_114">114</a>, ll. 15-18, the meritoriousness in the guardianship and teaching by the keepers of those flocks; the happy effect of the flock and that of the keeper's teaching; &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>        ll. 23-28, that of the disciple through the teaching by the priestly instructor; the teaching of the priestly instructor for the pupil, and the happy effect of the priestly instructor's teaching in similar matters.</p>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37040.htm#page_119">119</a>, ll. 2-5, the want of eminence of any one through a teaching that is an offence to others, which is owing to his closed doors and evil eminence in teaching; &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>Professor Darmesteter has suggested the following correction:</p>  
 <p>P. <a href="sbe37016.htm#page_26">26</a>, ll. 22, 23, for '<i>based</i> upon the traditional early law (vâsarî<i>d</i> pê<i>s</i>dâdŏ)' read '<i>dependent</i> upon Vâêgerê<i>d</i> the Pê<i>s</i>dâ<i>d</i>ian;' [who was the twin brother of Hôshâng; see Sachau's Albîrûnî's Chronology of Ancient Nations, pp. 206, 211].</p>
