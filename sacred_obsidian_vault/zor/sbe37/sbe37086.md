 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXVII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>. . . . . . . . . . . . . . 'produced the dawn <i>and</i> noontide (aûsh r<i>ê</i>pis<i>p</i>) <a name="fr_988"></a><a href="#fn_979"><font size="1">3</font></a>. <i>e</i>. I fashioned sovereignty <i>and</i> the desired complete mindfulness together <a name="fr_989"></a><a href="#fn_980"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> produced, for more advantageous disclosure, a son (be<i>r</i>manar-aê) for a father <a name="fr_990"></a><a href="#fn_981"><font size="1">5</font></a>; the disclosure that discloses a male and the impregnation of a female, and in that disclosure a son <i>was</i> produced by me for the father, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t!' <i>f</i>. So the evil spirit observed, and he called upwards from the abyss thus: '<i>O</i> beneficent spirit! thou art the creator <i>of</i> all creatures, <i>but</i> I <i>will</i> make all thy creatures old, <i>O</i> beneficent spirit!<font size="-1" color="green">'</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>g</i>. About the religion becoming progressive <a name="fr_991"></a><a href="#fn_982"><font size="1">1</font></a> in every one, through its renovation <i>of the universe and its</i> future existence, <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'This thy religion of Zaratû<i>s</i>t is the width of the world, <i>and</i> righteousness is the best of religions; this thy religion of Zaratû<i>s</i>t is the improvement of the world, which is first supplied <i>by</i> righteousness <i>and</i> complete mindfulness in the reason (vârôm) of those who recite this thy revelation (dênô) <a name="fr_992"></a><a href="#fn_983"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! this thy good religion is the best which it is possible to provide with righteousness for one's own. <i>h</i>. Thou shouldst proclaim this to kinsmen <i>and</i> confederates, to priests and him who is most active in the country; <i>as to those</i> who will dispute <a name="fr_993"></a><a href="#fn_984"><font size="1">3</font></a> this thy religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, thou shouldst proclaim this over the earth of seven regions, unto that which is the furthest of houses, villages, communities, and provinces: "Do thou openly curse <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_985"><font size="1">4</font></a> these who are heretical <i>towards</i> me, <i>thou</i> united Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, opposed to the demons, which is the ordinance of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_995"></a><a href="#fn_986"><font size="1">5</font></a>!"'</p>  
 <p><i>i</i>. Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'I <i>will</i> exalt this which is beloved by thee, the religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship <i>of</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t, opposed to the demons, which is the ordinance of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>. <i>j</i>. If this which is thine had not been further loved by me, the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, that is opposed to the demons <i>and is</i> the ordinance of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, would have lapsed into disaster (vinâsi<i>s</i>nŏ) <a name="fr_996"></a><a href="#fn_987"><font size="1">6</font></a>, so that the profession</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 272</font></a></p>  
 <p>of the religion <i>of</i> the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers would be destroyed, that is, the religion would not have become progressive, <i>and</i> no one <i>would be</i> after the benefiters. <i>k</i>. But, owing to that <i>love</i>, O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! the religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers becomes progressive <a name="fr_997"></a><a href="#fn_988"><font size="1">1</font></a> even then up to the production of the renovation <i>of the universe</i>, even then until the perpetual life of the existences, even then till the raising up of the dead, <i>and</i> even then up to the full atonement of the spirits.'</p>  
 <p><i>l</i>. About <i>being</i> despised in hell; the wicked are scornful to a wicked <i>one, and</i> to the spirits <i>apart</i> from the wicked; and it is the creator who, even after saving the others from hell, and the three nights stewing in hell <a name="fr_998"></a><a href="#fn_989"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>is</i> to cause the preservation of them alsoafter those three nightsfrom that misery, and <i>every one</i> attains to happiness. <i>m</i>. This, too, that Zaratû<i>s</i>t enquired of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> thus: 'How have the ignorant demons, <i>O</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>! ever been good rulers <a name="fr_999"></a><a href="#fn_990"><font size="1">3</font></a>? How do they think of them in the world thus, <i>that</i> their happiness arose from them?' <i>n</i>. And Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'They have been demons, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! <i>and</i> evil-ruling; not well-ruling, even for a reward, do they produce the work of righteousness <a name="fr_1000"></a><a href="#fn_991"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p><i>o</i>. Perfect righteousness is excellence.</p>
