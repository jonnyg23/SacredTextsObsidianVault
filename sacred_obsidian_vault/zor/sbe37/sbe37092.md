 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The twentieth fargar<i>d</i>, Ka<i>d</i>-môi-urvâ <a name="fr_1132"></a><a href="#fn_1121"><font size="1">4</font></a>, is about anything whatever <i>being</i> begged <i>as</i> provision for the soul <a name="fr_1133"></a><a href="#fn_1122"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and as to the speaking of Shatraver <a name="fr_1134"></a><a href="#fn_1123"><font size="1">6</font></a> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t thus: 'Thou shouldst think thus, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân! that Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> assists thee.' 2. This, too, <i>is said</i>, that the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> live through Khû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1135"></a><a href="#fn_1124"><font size="1">7</font></a>, are immortal through Amû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1136"></a><a href="#fn_1124"><font size="1">7</font></a>, possess complete mindfulness of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> through</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_292"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 292</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1137"></a><a href="#fn_1125"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and <i>possess</i> him as ruler through Shatraver.</p>  
 <p>3. About wealth being <i>begged</i> owing to virtuousness <i>there</i> is this, too, that, to him who, owing to virtuousness, begs that which is not allowed to him owing to the oppressiveness <i>of</i> the vile, or on account of <i>some</i> other opposition, they then give essentially that reward, in the spiritual <i>existence which is</i> greater <i>and</i> better than that wealth. 4. About the cattle suitable <i>for</i> that warrior who possesses virtuous habits <i>and</i> strength <a name="fr_1138"></a><a href="#fn_1126"><font size="1">2</font></a>, through the assistance of the will of the sacred beings and <i>for</i> the benefit of Irân <i>and</i> the defeat of the diminishing foreign force (kâstârîh-i an-Aîrânŏ).</p>  
 <p>5. About the seizure <a name="fr_1139"></a><a href="#fn_1127"><font size="1">3</font></a> of mankind for the advancement of the admonition <i>and</i> command <i>of</i> the sacred beings, so far as force is an assistant to them in knowledge due to the sacred beings <a name="fr_1140"></a><a href="#fn_1128"><font size="1">4</font></a>; <i>and</i> their appropriation <i>of</i> the best existence through the advancement of that admonition <i>and</i> command. 6. About the assistance of the righteous, on the passage to the best existence, by the spirit of the wisdom of sovereignty, liberality, <i>and</i> truth, Aharî<i>s</i>vang <a name="fr_1141"></a><a href="#fn_1129"><font size="1">5</font></a> and the angel Hôm <a name="fr_1142"></a><a href="#fn_1130"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>7. About the reason of the three steps walked forward <i>by</i> the Zôti from the place of the Zôti, while uttering the Avesta (<i>a</i><i>v</i>istâkŏ-gôbi<i>s</i>nîhâ), after the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 293</font></a></p>  
 <p>end of the ritual for the fire, on delivering (parv<i>â</i><i>z</i>i<i>s</i>nŏ) the offering of holy-water to the water <a name="fr_1143"></a><a href="#fn_1131"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>being</i> the leading up of the archangels, always at the end of an assembly of conference <i>with</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t, by three steps from the earth to the sun station, through <i>the places of</i> good thoughts, good words, <i>and</i> good deeds <a name="fr_1144"></a><a href="#fn_1132"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. Advice to Zaratû<i>s</i>t also as to the nature of the archangels; likewise a reminder to worship on <i>their</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 294</font></a></p>  
 <p>account after separation from the sight <i>of them</i>. 9. And this, too, <i>is said</i>, that <i>there</i> arises therefrom a conception (ham-giriftârîh), <i>by</i> him whose disposition and character are sagacious <a name="fr_1145"></a><a href="#fn_1133"><font size="1">1</font></a>, also as to the adaptation of his own deeds to that nature of his. 10. And about the good affinity of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, even for abundance of good works, <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'So, <i>for</i> all those deeds which <i>thou hast</i> to accomplish, and which are also accomplished, there is reward for thee through their righteousness, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t!'</p>  
 <p>11. And about the advice to Zaratû<i>s</i>t <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: '<i>Thou hast</i> to become reverent to them <a name="fr_1146"></a><a href="#fn_1134"><font size="1">2</font></a>, so that mankind <i>may</i> become reverent to thee.' 12. About considering the time of the days <i>and</i> nights <i>as</i> all for good accumulation in good works <i>there</i> is this, too, that whoever is diligent <i>and</i> always doing good works, <i>and</i> that whoever shall perform as many good works as is possible for him, is given as much reward <a name="fr_1147"></a><a href="#fn_1135"><font size="1">3</font></a> as is his desire.</p>  
 <p>13. It is righteousness <i>that is</i> perfect excellence.</p>
