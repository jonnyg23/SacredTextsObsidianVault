 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Ganabâ-sar-ni</i>g<i>a</i>d<i> Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. One section of <i>the next</i> twelve is the Pasû<i>s</i>-haûrvastân ('<i>shepherd's-dog code</i>'), about the shepherd who is selecting a shepherd's dog for the sheep, and the shepherd with various shepherd's dogs; about the shepherd's comprehension of their serviceableness, one with the other, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 2. The extent of authorised efficiency (<i>s</i>alît-gârîkîh) accomplished by the shepherd's-dog nature of a shepherd's dog, after his <i>being</i> appointed by the shepherd.</p>  
 <p>3. About the shepherd's preparing the means of bedding <a name="fr_227"></a><a href="#fn_226"><font size="1">1</font></a> for the shepherd's dog, giving the amount of the price of the daily <i>food</i> of a shepherd's dog, provisions for the dog in the winter, and the preparation of a fire beforehand which it is necessary to make in the sheepfold (pâh-hastŏ). 4. About the mode of preparing the appointed <i>fire</i>place <i>of</i> the sheepfold, the position of the shepherd's dog and the dog's fire, the means of lodging <i>and</i> provisioning the shepherd's dog in the sheepfold, the sin owing to <i>the occasions</i> when <i>one</i> proceeds to provide <i>another</i> mode, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>5. About the diligence of the shepherd's dog, and about <i>his</i> being guardian <i>of</i> the sheep asleep at night</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a></p>  
 <p>in flocks <a name="fr_228"></a><a href="#fn_227"><font size="1">1</font></a> dreading distress; the dog, <i>their</i> protection, is not provided with bedding, <i>nor</i> with pillow, and they are happy; every night <i>he has</i> to come out, through the whole flock, three times, besides when one of the guards (pa<i>d</i>ânŏ), who is apprehensive, counts <i>them, who</i>, every day <i>at</i> dawn, <i>has</i> to walk out among the sheep, with good words, to inspect <i>them</i>, to apply remedies properly <i>to</i> the sheep that are sick, wounded, bruised, <i>or</i> defective, <i>and</i> to be <i>their</i> guardian; <i>also</i> the sin owing to worrying <i>them, and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 6. About that which is to be done by him as regards the breeding of the sheep, <i>and</i> likewise for the sake of the young ones; <i>and</i> the sin when <i>he does</i> not <i>do it</i>, or shall act otherwise. 7. About his fully understanding where <i>and</i> which is the sheep for <i>each</i> young one. 8. About his habit and means of keeping away the thief <i>and</i> the wolf from the sheep, <i>and</i> the preservation of the sheep thereby when an awful cloud and wind and rain arise, <i>or</i> when the position of those distressed ones, at the fords of rivers, comes opposite a locality (nisi<i>s</i>no) of bad footing; when it is not possible for him <i>to save</i> all, <i>he has</i> to save the greater in value, or the more in number.</p>  
 <p>9. About his <i>having</i> guarded a sheep from the pasture of others <i>and</i> the retribution for the, sin of not <i>having</i> guarded as to the eating <i>and</i> damaging of the corn and pasture of others by the sheep. 10. About the extent of preservation by the shepherd's dog's driving the sheep from the corn and pasture <i>of</i> others of various species, such as that which <i>one</i> calls the very stupid (gôltar) pig; there is, moreover,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 83</font></a></p>  
 <p>the specified pasture as regards those sheep, <i>but</i> the pig, which feeds upon its own predecessors, is also that <i>which</i> may commit another sin, for it feeds upon even <i>its</i> progeny at birth.</p>  
 <p>11. About the indication of an assembly place (garang) for the sheep, in a warm <i>or</i> cool locality, by the shepherd's dog. 12. About the characteristics of sheep from one to four years <i>of age</i>. 13. About the village (vîs) of the shepherd, where the shepherd's dog is known when he arrives; how it is when a sheep <i>has</i> to be kept out of the sheepfold <i>by</i> the shepherd's dog, <i>and</i> how <i>it is</i> when it has to be driven by him to the village of the heedful shepherd. 14. About the coming of the shepherd unto a sheep, <i>and</i> the path from the village which the shepherd <i>has</i> provided for <a name="fr_229"></a><a href="#fn_228"><font size="1">1</font></a> the flock.</p>  
 <p>15. About a shepherd when he withholds the daily <i>food</i> of a shepherd's dog, and the exhaustion of life thereby; after the fourth deprival of food (atapak-dâ<i>d</i>ŏ) <a name="fr_230"></a><a href="#fn_229"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>it</i> is allowable for the shepherd's dog to kill a sheep for nourishment. 16. About a sheep, <i>which</i> comes astray into the flock to be slaughtered, <i>being</i> the perquisite of the butcher (bâhar-î kû<i>s</i>târ), and that of the shepherd's dog <i>being</i> its dog <a name="fr_231"></a><a href="#fn_230"><font size="1">3</font></a> and the appointed number of one sheep. 17. About <i>their</i> extent of movement, and their pregnancy and growing old (bâr va-khasân). 18. About the sin of the shepherd, as regards the shepherd's dog, through injustice as to work,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>  
 <p>reward, <i>and</i> chastisement; <i>and of</i> the shepherd's dog, as regards the shepherd, through improperly tending a sheep, <i>or</i> worrying <i>it</i> by exertion; also his chastisement, and the payment that occurs for the incompetence <i>and</i> unworthiness therein; <i>besides</i> adjudications between the shepherd <i>and</i> shepherd's dog.</p>  
 <p>19. About the instruction which the shepherd <i>gives</i> to the shepherd's dog, through reminders (pavan ayâ<i>d</i>îhâ), to control a sheep, when, the shepherd's dog <i>having</i> heard <i>some musical</i> notes (srû<i>d</i>ŏ gâsânŏ), the instruction took place in the form of words; and, when the notes <i>were</i> not heard, even by a blow (zatam), the means of that instructor <i>being</i> a blow. 20. About the peculiarity of the shepherd's dog as regards <i>its</i> employment (rô<i>g</i>kâr) at the periods of satisfying menstrual excitement, solemnizing the season-festivals, <i>and</i> other important good works.</p>
