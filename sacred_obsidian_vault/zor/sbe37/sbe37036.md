 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Hûspâram Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. One section is about the mode and object <i>of</i> confinement as regards a beast <i>of burden</i>, sheep, and dog that are mad (dêvânakŏ), and the operation of the affliction (vakhsi<i>s</i>nŏ); also to what extent is their restoration; <i>and</i> when not restored, <i>but</i> come for slaughter, the care of them even in confinement,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject. 2. About the harm (vinâs) which the beast <i>of burden</i>, sheep, <i>and</i> dog shall commit. 3. About the sin which killed <i>one who is</i> no offender <a name="fr_274"></a><a href="#fn_273"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 4. About the care and remedy <i>for</i> a sick dog, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>
