 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The eighth fargar<i>d</i>, <i>H</i><i>v</i>aêtumaiti <a name="fr_519"></a><a href="#fn_515"><font size="1">4</font></a>, is about the abstinence of mankind, for special propitiation, from <i>being</i> unreliant <i>upon</i> religion, on account of reverence for the evil spirit <a name="fr_520"></a><a href="#fn_516"><font size="1">5</font></a>; that from the habit of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_182"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 182</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>being</i> ungirdled, on account of reverence for Andar <a name="fr_521"></a><a href="#fn_517"><font size="1">1</font></a> and that for <i>S</i>ôvar <a name="fr_522"></a><a href="#fn_518"><font size="1">2</font></a>; that from walking with one boot <a name="fr_523"></a><a href="#fn_519"><font size="1">3</font></a>, on account of reverence for Tâûr<i>v</i>ŏ and Zârî<i>k</i>ŏ <a name="fr_524"></a><a href="#fn_520"><font size="1">4</font></a>; that from being harmfully inquisitorial, on account of reverence for Akatâsh <a name="fr_525"></a><a href="#fn_521"><font size="1">5</font></a>; and that from the habit of <i>being</i> without a serpent-scourge, on account of reverence for all the demons <a name="fr_526"></a><a href="#fn_522"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. About the hungry intention (gu<i>s</i>nakŏ-mîni<i>s</i>nîh) of him who eats <a name="fr_527"></a><a href="#fn_523"><font size="1">7</font></a> <i>and</i> drinks chattering; the delight of the demons on that account; and advice as regards not speaking a word during eating and drinking. 3. As to the praise and gratification of the sacred beings before eating and drinking,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_183"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 183</font></a></p>  
 <p>and also on finishing; and the purity <a name="fr_528"></a><a href="#fn_524"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the mouth owing to its praise of righteousness <a name="fr_529"></a><a href="#fn_525"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 4. About him whose ownership of any good work, that they <a name="fr_530"></a><a href="#fn_526"><font size="1">3</font></a> may perform, does not attain to the best existence, on account of not possessing a high-priest by habit.</p>  
 <p>5. About the period of the ceremonial of Srôsh <a name="fr_531"></a><a href="#fn_527"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the righteous, <i>being</i> mostly on the passing away of the first half of the night, and the announcement <a name="fr_532"></a><a href="#fn_528"><font size="1">5</font></a> of him who is the celebrator (ya<i>s</i>târ) is for his protection from the fiend spirit. 6. The period of the ceremonial of Rashnû <a name="fr_533"></a><a href="#fn_529"><font size="1">6</font></a> and Â<i>s</i>tâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_534"></a><a href="#fn_530"><font size="1">7</font></a> is mostly after that, in the jurisdiction (ra<i>d</i>îh) of the Aûshahîn <a name="fr_535"></a><a href="#fn_531"><font size="1">8</font></a>, and the announcement of him who is the celebrator is abundance of grain. 7. The period of the ceremonial of Mit<i>r</i>ô <a name="fr_536"></a><a href="#fn_532"><font size="1">9</font></a> of the wide cattle-pastures, and <i>of</i> the spirit of the pleasure of eating <a name="fr_537"></a><a href="#fn_533"><font size="1">10</font></a>, is mostly in the</p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 184</font></a></p>  
 <p>jurisdiction of the Hâvan <a name="fr_538"></a><a href="#fn_534"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and the announcement of him who is the celebrator is a flock of sheep. 8. The period of the ceremonial of Ashavahi<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_539"></a><a href="#fn_535"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and also <i>of</i> the fire of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, is mostly in the jurisdiction of the Rapithwin <a name="fr_540"></a><a href="#fn_536"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and the announcement of him who is the celebrator is an assemblage of righteousness. 9. The period of the ceremonial of the lofty lord <i>of</i> females, the descendant of waters <a name="fr_541"></a><a href="#fn_537"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and also <i>of</i> the water created by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, is mostly in the jurisdiction of the Aûzâêrin <a name="fr_542"></a><a href="#fn_538"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and the announcement of him who is the celebrator is a troop of heroes (vîrân ramakŏ). 10. And the period of the ceremonial of the guardian spirits of the righteous, <i>of</i> the females with troops of heroes and years <i>of</i> pleasant dwelling, <i>of</i> the might which is well-formed and handsome, as well as victorious and created by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and <i>of</i> the fighting which is in the ascendant <a name="fr_543"></a><a href="#fn_539"><font size="1">6</font></a>, is mostly in the jurisdiction of the Aiwisrûthrim <a name="fr_544"></a><a href="#fn_540"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and the announcement of him who is the celebrator is the origin of all excellence,</p>

 <p>and the produce of all manifestation of righteousness.</p>  
 <p>11. Righteousness is perfect excellence.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_512"></a><a href="sbe37057.htm#fr_516">181:1</a> Compare Yas. XXXI, 14; Byt. I, 1-5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_513"></a><a href="sbe37057.htm#fr_517">181:2</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XI, 1-3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_514"></a><a href="sbe37057.htm#fr_518">181:3</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. I, 22.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_515"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_519">181:4</a> The appellation of the fifth hâ of the first Gâtha (Yas. XXXII) which begins with the words a<i>h</i><i>v</i>yâ<i>k</i>â <i>h</i><i>v</i>aêtu<i>s</i>; it is here written khvatamaîtŏ in Pahlavi in both MSS.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_516"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_520">181:5</a> Compare Yas. XXXII, 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_517"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_521">182:1</a> Av. Andra, or I<i>n</i>dra; one of the arch-demons produced by the evil spirit, and the special opponent of the archangel Ashavahi<i>s</i>t; he seduces from virtue and opposes the use of the sacred shirt and girdle (see Bd. I, 27, XXVIII, 8, 10, XXX, 29; Ep. I, x, 9; Pahl. Yas. XLVII, 1).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_518"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_522">182:2</a> Av. Sauru; another of the arch-demons and the special opponent of the archangel Shatvaîrô; he encourages anarchy and drunkenness, and opposes the use of the sacred shirt and girdle (see Bd. I, 27, XXVIII, 9, 10, XXX, 29; Ep. I, X, 9).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_519"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_523">182:3</a> Probably equivalent to 'walking in stockings,' though some think it means 'walking barefoot.' It is sinful on account of the risk of pollution from stepping on impurities.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_520"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_524">182:4</a> Av. Tauru and Zairi<i>k</i>a; two more of the arch-demons and the special opponents of the archangels Khû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> and Amû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i>; they produce and diffuse poison, and are propitiated by walking with one boot (see Bd. I, 27, XXVIII, 11, 13, XXX, 29; Ep. 1. X, 9).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_521"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_525">182:5</a> Av. Akatasha; 'the fiend of inquisitiveness, who makes the creatures look away from proper things' (Bd. XXVIII, 20), and appears to be closely connected with the demon Aeshm, 'wrath.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_522"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_526">182:6</a> See Bd. XXVIII, 21, 22, and Bk. VIII, Chap. XVIII, 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_523"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_527">182:7</a> B omits 'eats.' Talking during eating is sinful because the eater has muttered an inward prayer, as a protective spell, the good effect of which would be destroyed by speaking aloud (compare Bk. VIII, Chap. XLIII, 37).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_524"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_528">183:1</a> K has 'protection.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_525"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_529">183:2</a> That is, its muttering the Ashem-vohû formula which is recited thrice, as a conclusion of the inward prayer (see Dd. LXXIX, 1 n).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_526"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_530">183:3</a> Or it may be 'he,' as the optative 3rd plural is often used for the singular; but it is a doctrine of the religion that a person who causes good works to be done by others, as he does when he employs a priest to perform ceremonies, is as much the owner of the good works as the actual performer is (see Sls. X, 22, 23 for cases of less direct agency).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_527"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_531">183:4</a> See Bk. VIII, Chaps. IX, 3, XLIV, 16, and Pahl. Yas. I, 22.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_528"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_532">183:5</a> As an offering, referring to the verb nivaêdhayêmi, 'I announce or invite,' with which most of the clauses of Yas. I. commence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_529"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_533">183:6</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XX, 153, and Pahl. Yas. I, 23.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_530"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_534">183:7</a> Av. Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>d</i>, 'rectitude,' a female angel who assists the soul on its way to the other world (see AV. V, 3).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_531"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_535">183:8</a> The period from midnight till dawn (see Bk. VIII, Chap. XXIX, 9).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_532"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_536">183:9</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XLIV, i6.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_533"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_537">183:10</a> Av. râma <i>h</i><i>v</i>âstrem, Pahl. râmi<i>s</i>nŏ khvârôm, who cooperates with Mit<i>r</i>ô (see Pahl. Yas. I, 9).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_534"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_538">184:1</a> The period from dawn till noon, and in winter it extends into the afternoon (see Bd. XXV, 9-14).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_535"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_539">184:2</a> See Bk. VIII, Chap. XXXVII, 14, and Pahl. Yas. I, 12.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_536"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_540">184:3</a> The afternoon till 3 p<i>m</i>. during summer (see Bd. XXV, 9-14). Here written Rapis<i>v</i>ag.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_537"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_541">184:4</a> Av. berezatô ahurahê nafedhrô apãm, Pahl. bûr<i>z</i>and khû<i>d</i>âî nekedân-î <i>â</i><i>v</i>ânŏ nâpô (see Pahl. Yas. I, 15).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_538"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_542">184:5</a> The evening from the middle of the afternoon till dusk (see Bd. XXV, 9; Sls. XXI, 4-7).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_539"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_543">184:6</a> See Pahl. Yas. I, 18, 19.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_540"></a><a href="sbe37058.htm#fr_544">184:7</a> The period from dusk till midnight; here written ay<i>î</i><i>v</i>îk-srûksrîm. It will be noticed that the periods for the ceremonials of the beings here detailed correspond with those with which their names are connected in Yas. I, 3-7, II, 3-7, III, 5-9, IV, 8-12, VI, 2-6, VII, 5-9 (W.).</p>
