 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The seventeenth fargar<i>d</i>, Yêzî <a name="fr_640"></a><a href="#fn_635"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about where <i>he</i> is who shall commit <i>any of</i> these five sins <a name="fr_641"></a><a href="#fn_636"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i>, thereby perverted from the religion, has diminished his own life and destiny <a name="fr_642"></a><a href="#fn_637"><font size="1">3</font></a>:A human being when he contentedly reverences a demon in spiritual lordship (ahûîh) and priestly authority (ra<i>d</i>îh), <i>one</i> steadfast in religion when <i>he so reverences one</i> unsteadfast in religion, a teacher when <i>he so reverences one who is</i> no teacher <i>and</i> ignorant, <i>one</i> acquainted with the Gâthas when <i>he so reverences one</i> unacquainted with the Gâthas <i>and</i> unintelligent (anashnâs) <a name="fr_643"></a><a href="#fn_638"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> a helpful <i>one</i> when <i>he so reverences</i> an unhelpful <i>and</i> unwise <i>one</i>.</p>  
 <p>2. This, too, where also they are who unlawfully slaughter a sheep, <i>or</i> beast <i>of burden</i>, which diminishes their life and destiny. 3. And so, too, those also who think scornfully of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, O pure <i>and</i> righteous Spîtâmân! and their own religion, the strength of the righteous <i>and</i> thy disciples.</p>  
 <p>4. Excellence <i>that is</i> perfect is righteousness.</p>
