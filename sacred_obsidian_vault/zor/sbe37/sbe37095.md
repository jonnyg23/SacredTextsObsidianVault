 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLVI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The twenty-third fargar<i>d</i>, Airyaman <a name="fr_1198"></a><a href="#fn_1186"><font size="1">3</font></a>, is the Airman supplication <a name="fr_1199"></a><a href="#fn_1187"><font size="1">4</font></a>:'That is the greatest, I tell thee, O Spîtâmân! of the pure sayings of every kind, in so much Avesta lore, this is the best, because it is given forth by him who is a very eminent producer (madam-kârtar) of sayings of every kind. 2. Which <i>A</i>îrmân supplication they should recite who are beneficial <a name="fr_1200"></a><a href="#fn_1188"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>and</i> the benefiter <a name="fr_1201"></a><a href="#fn_1188"><font size="1">5</font></a>, through the recital of it aloud, O Spîtâmân! becomes predominant. 3. The evil spirit, who is heretical (dû<i>s</i>dênŏ), O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! with his own creatures, O Spîtâmân! becomes buried <i>in</i> the earth; the evil spirit is among <i>those</i> buried <i>in</i> the earthwho are the demonswhere their bodily form (kâlpu<i>d</i>ŏ) is completely</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_303"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 303</font></a></p>  
 <p>shattered. 4. And up the dead are arrayed by it; through its assistance they give life back unto the body, and the embodied life they <i>then</i> possess is <i>such</i> that they do not die.'</p>  
 <p>5. It is perfect <i>is</i> the excellence of righteousness; it is perfect excellence <i>that is</i> righteousness.</p>
