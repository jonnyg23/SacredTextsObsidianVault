 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The second fargar<i>d</i>, Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_763"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is about the worthiness, as to worldly and spiritual virtue, in a ruler and <i>in</i> the production of a high-priest's</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_232"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 232</font></a></p>  
 <p>efficiency; <i>and</i> they <i>have</i> been suitable for leadership and priestly authority with whom <i>there is</i> an existence of it; <i>also</i> other talent through which sovereignty and priestly authority are appropriated, and <i>which</i> the ruler or high-priest himself possesses.</p>  
 <p>2. 'My wish (dôsh), O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! is <i>that</i> thou <i>be</i> in spiritual lordship and priestly authority, because thou art, O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! provided with a spiritual lord <i>and</i> possessing priestly instructionthat is, they consider thee, too, as high-priest<i>and</i> it is because thine is the accomplishment of rites, that thou art quite preserved when <i>there is</i> an encounter of the demons with theethat is, a dispute of apostates with thee.' 3. It is non-possession of a ruler and high-priest, <i>or</i> non-possession of a ruler <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_764"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>that</i> became the nature and law of the demons; and the maintenance of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>and</i> the archangels, as ruler and high-priest, and the dominion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>are</i> combined with beneficence.</p>  
 <p>4. This, too, that through righteousness a priestly instructor (ra<i>d</i>ŏ) is a ruler at will, a sage and benefactor, a cherisher and cleanser (âsnî<i>d</i>âr) of the poor; also the fitness for the supreme heaven (garô<i>d</i>mânîkîh) of all those who are accepting the religion which <i>proceeds</i> from Zaratû<i>s</i>t.</p>  
 <p>5. Of righteousness the excellence is perfect.</p>
