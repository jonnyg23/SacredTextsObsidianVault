 <p><a name="page_401"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 401</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">FROM THE SELECTIONS</h5>  <h5 align="center">OF</h5>  <h2 align="center">ZÂ<i>D</i>-SPARAM <a name="fr_1614"></a><a href="#fn_1573"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h2>  
 <p>1. About the three divisions of revelation there is a condensed medium, beneficial <i>and</i> small, of whose subdivision one category (ra<i>g</i>istakô) is collection together; that is, the Ahunavair <a name="fr_1615"></a><a href="#fn_1574"><font size="1">2</font></a> itself is a symbol of the Nasks.</p>  
 <p>2. First, the Ahunavair is apportioned into its three degrees (pa<i>d</i>mân), as shown in another chapter; <i>and</i> by a like system (ra<i>g</i>istak) the Gâthas <a name="fr_1616"></a><a href="#fn_1575"><font size="1">3</font></a>, too, are into three, which are the three-lined, four-lined, and five-lined <a name="fr_1617"></a><a href="#fn_1576"><font size="1">4</font></a>; even so the Nasks</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_402"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 402</font></a></p>  
 <p>are denominated Gâthic, Hadha-mãthric, and Law. 3. Then the Ahunavair is apportioned into six which they call half-lines (nêm-gâs); so, too, the Gâthas are into six, which are called the Ahunavaiti Gâtha, the Yasna, the U<i>s</i>tavaiti <a name="fr_1618"></a><a href="#fn_1577"><font size="1">1</font></a> Gâtha, the Spe<i>n</i>tâ-mainyû (S<i>p</i>etamatŏ) Gâtha, the Vohû-khshathra Gâtha, <i>and</i> the Vahi<i>s</i>tôi<i>s</i>ti Gâtha; even so the Nasks are into six, as the Gâthas are into two, which are called one the Gâthic creation<i>which</i> is the Yast <a name="fr_1619"></a><a href="#fn_1578"><font size="1">2</font></a><i>and</i> one the rest <i>of</i> the Gâthic; also the Hadha-mãthric into two, one the Mãthra of the arrangerwhich is the P<i>â</i><i>k</i>înŏ and Ra<i>d</i>ŏ-dâ<i>d</i>ŏ-aîtŏ <a name="fr_1620"></a><a href="#fn_1579"><font size="1">3</font></a>and one the Mãthra full of good tokens, which is the rest <i>of</i> the Hadha-mãthra; and also the Law into two, one the law against the demonswhich is the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1621"></a><a href="#fn_1580"><font size="1">4</font></a>and one the law of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>which</i> is the rest <i>of</i> the Law. 4. Then it is apportioned into twenty-one, such as the twenty-one words (mârîk) of the Ahunavair; <i>also</i> the Gâthas are <i>into</i> twenty-one, which are the Ahunavair, the praise of righteousness, the performance of the good, <i>and</i> from Yânîm-manô unto Airyaman <a name="fr_1622"></a><a href="#fn_1581"><font size="1">5</font></a> which, <i>being</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_403"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 403</font></a></p>  
 <p>accomplished (âka<i>r</i><i>d</i>ŏ), are twenty-one; and the Nasks are twenty-one.</p>  
 <p>5. Then the Gâthas are apportioned into 278 <a name="fr_1623"></a><a href="#fn_1582"><font size="1">1</font></a> stanzas (v<i>ê</i><i>k</i>êstŏ); and the Nasks also into 278 categories, every single category <i>having</i> borne a form like a single verse <a name="fr_1624"></a><a href="#fn_1583"><font size="1">2</font></a>, as regards how much and how any<i>thing</i> good is indicated, such as the Patkâr-ra<i>d</i>istân <a name="fr_1625"></a><a href="#fn_1584"><font size="1">3</font></a>, in which <i>what is</i> legally disputable is reported (pê<i>d</i>âkŏ); the Zâkhmistân <a name="fr_1626"></a><a href="#fn_1585"><font size="1">4</font></a>, by which the penalty of assault (zâkhm) is reported; the Stôristân <a name="fr_1627"></a><a href="#fn_1586"><font size="1">5</font></a>, by which the sin and amount of penalty <i>for</i> a wound, as regard beasts <i>of burden and</i> cattle, are reported; the Aratê<i>s</i>târistân <a name="fr_1628"></a><a href="#fn_1587"><font size="1">6</font></a>, by which battle is reported; the Pasû<i>s</i>-haûrvastân <a name="fr_1629"></a><a href="#fn_1588"><font size="1">7</font></a>, by which the customary keeping of sheep in control is reported; the <i>G</i>ûr<i>d</i>âî-zarîtunistân ('<i>corn-sowing code</i>') <a name="fr_1630"></a><a href="#fn_1589"><font size="1">8</font></a>, by which agriculture is reported; the Varistân <a name="fr_1631"></a><a href="#fn_1590"><font size="1">9</font></a>, by which an ordeal <i>being</i> accomplished is reported; and others of a like description.</p>

 <p>6. Then the Gâthas are apportioned into 1061 <a name="fr_1632"></a><a href="#fn_1591"><font size="1">1</font></a> metrical lines (gâs), and the Nasks into 1000 Hâs <i>and</i> Fargar<i>d</i>s <a name="fr_1633"></a><a href="#fn_1592"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i>, since the Hâ<i>d</i>ôkht <a name="fr_1634"></a><a href="#fn_1593"><font size="1">3</font></a> is the priestly master (ra<i>d</i>ŏ) of the Nasks, <i>and</i> the remedy <a name="fr_1635"></a><a href="#fn_1594"><font size="1">4</font></a> (darmôn) which is a perfect statement about the master of the resurrection, the existence of <i>its</i> fargar<i>d</i>s about the other fargar<i>d</i>s is therefore 1000 remedies fully combined, <i>being</i> the corn and fodder that are shut up (bastakŏ) when, over that thousand, they supply one that is great, which <i>in</i> every way protects <i>them</i> from hail and rain, <i>from</i> the wind which is hot <i>and</i> that which is cold.</p>  
 <p>7. Then the Gâthas are apportioned into 6666 words (mârîk) <a name="fr_1636"></a><a href="#fn_1595"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and <i>as to</i> the Nasks, too, their own 6666 ordinances (dâ<i>d</i>istânŏ) are therein severed. 8. And the 6666 words, which are in the Gâthas, are</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_405"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 405</font></a></p>  
 <p>an indicator of <i>the period</i> from the adversary <i>having</i> come to the creatures, as far as unto the end of the six millenniums <a name="fr_1637"></a><a href="#fn_1596"><font size="1">1</font></a>each millennium <i>being</i> ten centurieswhich amount to 60 single centuriesa century <i>being</i> ten tens <a name="fr_1638"></a><a href="#fn_1597"><font size="1">2</font></a>and up to <i>the time</i> when its <a name="fr_1639"></a><a href="#fn_1598"><font size="1">3</font></a> cold <i>and</i> distress arrive, which become awful; the 600, including the excess as far as one ten <a name="fr_1640"></a><a href="#fn_1599"><font size="1">4</font></a>, are years of the 6000 years which are the words of the six Gâthas that are the first indicator of the six millenniums; therefore of the 60 centuries are then the 600 and those which are <i>added</i> to <i>them</i> (zak-î gha<i>l</i>).</p>  
 <p>9. And after those 6000, which are the 6000 years, are the Airyaman <a name="fr_1641"></a><a href="#fn_1600"><font size="1">5</font></a> of Ashavahi<i>s</i>t <i>and</i> the accompanying sayings (ham-v<i>â</i><i>k</i>ŏ) which are at the end of the Gâthas; those are the 57 years of Sôshâns <a name="fr_1642"></a><a href="#fn_1601"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and for the sake of them, too, are the Airyaman <i>and</i> from the praise of righteousness at <i>its</i> end <i>to</i> the consecration of the Airyaman, originally 57 words (mârîk), because the praise of righteousness for the Airyaman is 12, and the consecration of the Airyaman is 21, <i>of</i> the original 57 <a name="fr_1643"></a><a href="#fn_1602"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>

In our present text, however, it must have its usual meaning of 'word,' as the number of 6666 words in the six Gâthas can be obtained by including the customary repetition of the first stanza of each Hâ of the five real Gâthas, with the text of the Airyaman and of the introductions to Yas.
