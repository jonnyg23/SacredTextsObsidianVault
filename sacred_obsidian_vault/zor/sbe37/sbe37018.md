 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The Bakân-ya<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>contains</i> particulars, first, about the worship of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the highest of divinities (bakân), and, secondly, <i>of</i> the worship of the angels of other invisible and visible worldly existences, out of whom are likewise the names of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a></p>  
 <p>the days <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">1</font></a>; <i>also</i> their glory, power, triumph, <i>and</i> marvellousness. 2. Besides, also, many angels who are invoked by name in their worship, and the attention <i>and</i> obeisance <i>due</i> to them.</p>  
 <p>3. The worthiness <i>and</i> dispensation of favour for worshippers, <i>and</i> the duty of their many separate recitations unto the angels. 4. The duty of unlimited acquaintance <i>with</i> knowledge about the possessions <i>and</i> arrangements of the period, over which the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>has</i> appointed them, and they remain to cause industry.</p>  
 <p>5. Perfect is the excellence <i>of</i> righteousness.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_127"></a><a href="sbe37018.htm#fr_128">34:3</a> Corresponding to the fourteenth word, mazdâi, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the fifteenth Nask in other Rivâyats. Bakân-ya<i>s</i>t means 'worship of the divinities,' and is written Baghân-ya<i>s</i>t, or Bayân-ya<i>s</i>t, in the Rivâyats, which also state that it contained seventeen kardah, or subdivisions.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_128"></a><a href="sbe37018.htm#fr_129">35:1</a> Each of the days of the Parsi month being named after some particular angel, or spirit. From this description it appears probable that the Ya<i>s</i>ts formed a part of this Nask; but, if so, it ought to have contained at least thirty chapters.</p>
