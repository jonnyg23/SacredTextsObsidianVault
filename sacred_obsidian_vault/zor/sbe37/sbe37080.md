 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The eighth fargar<i>d</i>, Tâ-v<i>e</i>-urvâtâ <a name="fr_841"></a><a href="#fn_835"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about advice as to reciting <a name="fr_842"></a><a href="#fn_836"><font size="1">2</font></a> the revelation, the information therefrom for the faithful, about which <i>they have</i> to report to the unfaithful, by mentioning conspicuous specimens <i>and</i> explanatory knowledge, and <i>by</i> thinking <i>of</i> anything whatever which <i>they have</i> to accept, <i>or</i> even which <i>they have</i> not to accept <a name="fr_843"></a><a href="#fn_837"><font size="1">3</font></a>; <i>also</i>, for <i>one</i> called <i>to</i> the religion, the advantage owing to the attraction of mankind to the numerous actual disciple-hood of the religion, and the increasing greatness materially, and further reward spiritually, owing to the numerous disciplehood; and the progress of the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> even among the irreligious (adênôân) <a name="fr_844"></a><a href="#fn_838"><font size="1">4</font></a> and actual apostates <a name="fr_845"></a><a href="#fn_839"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. This, too, that the life of the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> and also all other benefit are owing to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_846"></a><a href="#fn_840"><font size="1">6</font></a> and the inclination (kâmvar<i>i</i><i>k</i>akŏ) of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> thereto; moreover, reward and recompense come from Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>. 3. And the creatures of Aharman proceed from Aharman, all misery is owing to Aharman, and Aharman becomes worse and more oppressive and a further producer of misery when they worship him.</p>

 <p>4. About the continuance and arranging of both spirits as to their own creations (stî) and the self-acting of their own appliances; the achievement of each one through his own natural resources and through the trifling (ga<i>d</i>agânîk) operation of the other; the spiritual lordship and priestly authority, true confession <i>and</i> the progress of the good religion, <i>being from</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, <i>and</i>, through enmity <i>to</i> the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, Aharman is contesting these. 5. Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, for setting aside that contester, is the producer of true intelligence, <i>and</i> gave language and also the ritual of ordeal <a name="fr_847"></a><a href="#fn_841"><font size="1">1</font></a>; the invocation of the sacred beings <a name="fr_848"></a><a href="#fn_842"><font size="1">2</font></a> for assistance, and the arrival of an angel for the assistance of the invokers; the overcoming of their affliction, the production of their immunity and even righteousness, and also <i>of</i> that good ruler <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_843"><font size="1">3</font></a> who is a reminder of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and the restoration of bodies, <i>which is</i> the hope of all good creations, <i>are</i> through the sacred beings being invoked for assistance and <i>their</i> arrival where the diffusion <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_844"><font size="1">4</font></a> is that of virtuous knowledge through Vohûman <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_845"><font size="1">5</font></a>, the good religion which is whatever <i>may be</i> the knowledge <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_846"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>of</i> all those who are, <i>and</i> were, <i>and</i> will be.</p>  
 <p>6. About the shouting of the demon Aresh <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_847"><font size="1">7</font></a> to</p>

 <p><a name="page_247"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 247</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Zaratû<i>s</i>t and the reply of Zaratû<i>s</i>t as to the advice of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> and whatever is on the same subject, just as revelation (dênô) states it, that the demon Aresh spoke to him thus: 'Then the Franâmam <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_848"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! is <i>applicable</i> to the assembly of demons who sit in the same place three nights and four days on account of thee.' 7. Zaratû<i>s</i>t enquired of him thus: '<i>O</i> Aresh, <i>thou</i> most deceitful to me! what recompense <i>would</i> there be for it <i>to</i> me, if I <i>should</i> worship you in words?' 8. And Aresh, the most deceitful of demons, spoke to him thus: 'Thou <i>wouldst</i> become predominant among mankind, through producing at will among the existences just as is requisite for thyself; and thou <i>wouldst</i> become immortal, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân! 9. Zaratû<i>s</i>t also enquired of him thus: '<i>O</i> Aresh, most deceitful of demons! <i>as to</i> the people by whom you are worshipped, whether for the birth of a son, <i>or</i> even for a concubine sought for enjoyment, so that <i>the favour</i> is considered by them as your property, how can any one of them be immortal?' 10. And Aresh, the most deceitful of demons, <i>could</i> not tell him who <i>had</i> the more intelligence. 11. So Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke thus: 'I am <i>for</i> that <i>being</i> and I like him, that is, I am his</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_248"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 248</font></a></p>  
 <p>own and would transact his affairs, and I <i>will</i> recite the law and the benedictions of the sagacious Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, the gratifier of desires.'</p>  
 <p>12. About the deliverance of all creatures through the liturgy <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and, so long as <i>it is</i> continued by them, it is for the power through which the immortality of the separate creations is prepared in the renovation <i>of the universe;</i> the increase <i>of</i> the good creatures through the complete continuance <i>of</i> the liturgy, <i>and</i> the existence of purity and development of goodness in the world when he who is a good ruler arrives.</p>  
 <p>13. The arising of the spiritual creation, the first thought of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>; <i>and, as to</i> the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, first the spiritual achievement, and then the material formation and the mingling of spirit with matter; [the advancement of the creatures thereby, through his wisdom <i>and</i> the righteousness of Vohûman being lodged <a name="fr_856"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">2</font></a> in the creatures,] and all good creatures being goaded (zakhamî-hastanŏ) thereby into purity and joyfulness. 14. This, too, that a complete understanding of things arises through Vohûman having made a home in <i>one's</i> reason (vârôm).</p>  
 <p>15. About the great reward of him who shall produce benefit for cattle <a name="fr_857"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">3</font></a>; also the deceitfully and seductively assuming of religion and colouring of thought, talking of righteousness and adopting evil practices, through the recitation of righteousness even hypocritically (davânsîhâ-<i>k</i>); <i>and</i> an instance <i>of</i> the reward of an undutiful (avar<i>z</i>î<i>d</i>âr)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_249"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 249</font></a></p>  
 <p>apostate <a name="fr_858"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 16. About the work of the creator; <i>and</i>, for the completion thereof, the most eminent is understood <i>to be</i> when the world <i>and</i> religion <i>were</i> formed <a name="fr_859"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">2</font></a> by him, when life <i>was</i> given by him to those possessing bodies <a name="fr_860"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and <a name="fr_861"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">4</font></a> he provides instruction and employment <a name="fr_862"></a><a href="#fn_856"><font size="1">5</font></a> for it, and when spiritual life (hûkŏ) <a name="fr_863"></a><a href="#fn_857"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>was</i> given by him to the wishful <i>man</i>, so that he may more <i>fully</i> appropriate a share of the worldly and spiritual <i>existences</i>.</p>  
 <p>17. <i>He</i> who makes complete mindfulness <a name="fr_864"></a><a href="#fn_858"><font size="1">7</font></a> lodge in <i>his</i> body consults complete mindfulness, and <a name="fr_865"></a><a href="#fn_859"><font size="1">8</font></a>, through the much investigation of <i>his</i> spiritual life (ahvô) and mind into the attraction of both spirits<i>that</i> which is good and also <i>that</i> which is evileach separately for its own appliances, and <i>into</i> the duties of the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, is explaining the inefficiency of mankind, as regards the dissipation of their sin, because Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> is aware of all they practise, that which is public and that, too, which is concealed <a name="fr_866"></a><a href="#fn_860"><font size="1">9</font></a>. 18. The great reward of him who is liberal of gifts (dâsar) from his own property to a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_250"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 250</font></a></p>  
 <p>righteous man <a name="fr_867"></a><a href="#fn_861"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and this, too, that whoever gives him who is wicked <a name="fr_868"></a><a href="#fn_862"><font size="1">2</font></a> a gift, for the sake of improper expectations, assists darkness and not light.</p>  
 <p>19. This, too, that the worst ruler is he of evil religion <i>and</i> evil deeds, who even for a bribe <a name="fr_869"></a><a href="#fn_863"><font size="1">3</font></a> would not occasion happiness; he who is a destroyer of an innocent <i>man;</i> also the grievous state of punishment <i>of</i> that person, in hell, who shall make that wicked <i>one</i> a ruler <a name="fr_870"></a><a href="#fn_864"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 20. And advice to mankind as to providing a judge and guardian over every dwelling, the probation of a man for appointment <i>to</i> that important duty, <i>and</i> the development of all creations <i>in</i> the world when its ruler is sagacious <a name="fr_871"></a><a href="#fn_865"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>21. Also causing the disturbance (va-<i>s</i>iklinî<i>d</i>anŏ) of the evil spirit for satisfying a man who is rightly thinking, rightly speaking, <i>and</i> rightly acting; the opposition <i>to</i> a righteous <i>man of</i> a wicked <i>one belonging</i> to the evil spirit, who is an evil-reciting <i>and</i> improperly-disputing apostate; the enticement (lûsinî<i>d</i>anŏ) of mankind to devious <i>ways</i> (âvârîhâ), <i>by</i> an apostate, <i>being</i> more than that which attracts to the true way for a righteous <i>man</i>; and afterwards also, in the end, the defeat <i>of</i> the army of the fiend <i>by him</i> who is beneficial <i>to</i> mankind. 22. Advice to mankind about abstaining from the suite of him who is an apostate, not hearing and not solemnizing the Avesta and Zand of the sacred beings from him <a name="fr_872"></a><a href="#fn_866"><font size="1">6</font></a>; also the evil behaviour (dû<i>s</i>-bari<i>s</i>nîh), slander, strife, death, and fear in the world owing to apostates <a name="fr_873"></a><a href="#fn_867"><font size="1">7</font></a>. 23. Advice to upholders of the religion about the means of thoroughly understanding apostates,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 251</font></a></p>  
 <p>and preparing <i>and</i> keeping a weapon for them <a name="fr_874"></a><a href="#fn_868"><font size="1">1</font></a>, so that he who is authorised <i>and</i> fearless <i>may be</i> more eager for truthful speaking; <i>and</i>, when the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> is liked by him, his truthful speaking and other righteousness have then allured (kâmakînî<i>d</i>ŏ) <a name="fr_875"></a><a href="#fn_869"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>24. Also <i>what</i> happens in the three <i>nights</i> <a name="fr_876"></a><a href="#fn_870"><font size="1">3</font></a>, for the assistance and preservation of the righteous, through <i>what is</i> accomplished <i>by</i> the propitious fire <a name="fr_877"></a><a href="#fn_871"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and the progress of his lamentation who deceives and vexes a righteous man <a name="fr_878"></a><a href="#fn_872"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>and</i> is leading the wicked <i>by</i> their own befitting deeds to hell <a name="fr_879"></a><a href="#fn_873"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 25. This, too, that the complete worthiness which <i>exists</i> in Khû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <i>and</i> Amû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <a name="fr_880"></a><a href="#fn_874"><font size="1">7</font></a> arises <i>in</i> him who maintains the prerogative which is his <a name="fr_881"></a><a href="#fn_875"><font size="1">8</font></a> through virtuousness, who must become such a friend of whatever is his own spirit, through <i>his</i> actions <a name="fr_882"></a><a href="#fn_876"><font size="1">9</font></a>, as the creator is <i>of</i> his own creatures. 26. This, too, that whatever is thus in the world is perfect, when every one thinks, speaks, <i>and</i> shall act just like his spiritual lord <i>and</i> high-priest <a name="fr_883"></a><a href="#fn_877"><font size="1">10</font></a>; so that a good ruler is he with whom virtuous speaking arises, as well as proper action <a name="fr_884"></a><a href="#fn_878"><font size="1">11</font></a>. 27. <i>And</i> this, too, that the lodgment of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> in the worldly <i>existence</i> is most in the person of that ruler <a name="fr_885"></a><a href="#fn_879"><font size="1">12</font></a>, and that lodgment in him is manifest.</p>  
 <p>28. The excellence <i>of</i> righteousness is perfect.</p>
