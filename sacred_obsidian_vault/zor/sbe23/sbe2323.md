 <p><a name="page_283"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 283</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">XVIII. Â<i>S</i>TÂD YA<i>S</i>T.</h4>  
 <p><font size="-1">Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i> is Truthfulness: she is invoked in company with the Genius of Truth, Rashnu Razi<i>s</i>ta (Sîrôzah, § 18), on the day Rashn. On the day especially dedicated to her, the 26th day of the month, she is invoked in company with Mount Ushi-darena, which accounts for the singular fact that her Ya<i>s</i>t is wholly devoted to the <i>H</i><i>v</i>arenô, and thus is hardly distinguishable from the Zamyâd Ya<i>s</i>t, as Mount Ushi-darena is the actual seat of the <i>H</i><i>v</i>arenô (Yt. I, 31, text and note; cf. Yt. XIX, 66). Whence comes this particular connection of Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i> with Mount Ushi-darena is uncertain, unless it alludes to the fact that the possession of the <i>H</i><i>v</i>arenô can be secured only through truthfulness: as soon as Yima 'began to find delight in words of falsehood and untruth,' the <i>H</i><i>v</i>arenô flew away from him (Yt. XIX, 34).</font></p>  
 <p align="center">______________________</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">0. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced! . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, one who hates the Daêvas and obeys the laws of Ahura;</p>  
 <p>For sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification unto [Hâvani], the holy and master of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>Unto the Glory of the Aryans, made by Mazda, Be propitiation, with sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Yathâ ahû vairyô; The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>1. Ahura Mazda spake unto Spitama Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, saying: 'I made the Aryan Glory, rich in food, rich in flocks, rich in wealth <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1190"><font size="1">1</font></a>, rich in Glory; provided with full store of intelligence, with full store of money, to withstand Need, and to withstand enemies.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_284"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 284</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. 'It destroys Angra Mainyu, who is all death: it destroys Aêshma, the fiend of the wounding spear <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1191"><font size="1">1</font></a>; it destroys the yellow Bûshyãsta <a name="fr_1254"></a><a href="#fn_1192"><font size="1">2</font></a>; it destroys the contagion <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1193"><font size="1">3</font></a> of Aêkha <a name="fr_1256"></a><a href="#fn_1194"><font size="1">4</font></a>; it destroys the fiend of death, Apaosha <a name="fr_1257"></a><a href="#fn_1195"><font size="1">5</font></a>; it destroys the non-Aryan nations.</p>  
 <p>3. 'And I made the great Ashi Vanguhi; she comes in, amid the family; she comes in, inside the fine royal palace <a name="fr_1258"></a><a href="#fn_1196"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. 'Let Ashi, with fulness of welfare, follow the man who gladdens the faithful with his gifts <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1197"><font size="1">7</font></a>! she comes in, inside his family; she comes in, inside his fine royal palace.</p>  
 <p>'With all sorts of flocks, with all victory, with all intelligence, with all Glory, the great Ashi Vanguhi puts one foot <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1198"><font size="1">8</font></a> inside his family; she comes in, inside his fine royal palace.</p>  
 <p>5. 'Horses multiply a thousandfold, flocks multiply a thousandfold; and so does his virtuous offspring, (as) the bright, glorious star Ti<i>s</i>trya moves on equally <a name="fr_1261"></a><a href="#fn_1199"><font size="1">9</font></a>, and so does the strong wind made by Mazda, and so does the Glory of the Aryas.</p>  
 <p>6. 'And they bring increase on the tops of all mountains, down the depths of all vales; they bring increase to all the growing plants <a name="fr_1262"></a><a href="#fn_1200"><font size="1">10</font></a>, the fair, the golden-hued. And they bring (away) <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1201"><font size="1">11</font></a> the contagion</p>

 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 285</font></a></p>  
 <p>of Aêkha, they bring (away) the fiend of death, Apaosha.</p>  
 <p>7. 'Hail to the bright and glorious star Ti<i>s</i>trya! Hail to the strong wind, made by Mazda! Hail to the Glory of the Aryas!</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">'Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>'Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good. . . . .</p>  
 <p>8. 'We worship the Ahuna Vairya.</p>  
 <p>'We worship Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta, the fairest Amesha Spe<i>n</i>ta.</p>  
 <p>'We worship the rightly-spoken Words <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">1</font></a>, fiend-smiting and healing.</p>  
 <p>'We worship the healing, well-spoken Words, the fiend-smiting.</p>  
 <p>'We worship the Mãthra Spe<i>n</i>ta and the Law of Mazda, and (piety) that delights in Haoma <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">2</font></a>. We worship the Glory of the Aryas.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">'Yê<i>n</i>hê hâtãm: All those beings of whom Ahura Mazda . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>9. 'Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>'I bless the sacrifice and prayer, and the strength and vigour of the Glory of the Aryas, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">'Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>'[Give] unto that man <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">3</font></a> brightness and glory, . . give him the bright, all-happy, blissful abode of the holy Ones.'</p>
