 <p><a name="page_88"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 88</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VII. MÂH YA<i>S</i>T.</h3>  
 <p><font size="-1">This Ya<i>s</i>t to the Moon is recited on the day of the Moon, and on those of Bahman, Gô<i>s</i>, and Râm <a name="fr_451"></a><a href="#fn_430"><font size="1">1</font></a> (Anquetil, II, 185). Bah-man and Gô<i>s</i> are so far connected with the Moon that all three are gao<i>k</i>ithra: 'Bahman <a name="fr_452"></a><a href="#fn_431"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the Moon, and Gô<i>s</i> <a name="fr_453"></a><a href="#fn_432"><font size="1">3</font></a>, all three, are having in them the seed of the bull; Bahman can neither be seen nor seized with the hand; the Moon proceeded from Bahman <a name="fr_454"></a><a href="#fn_433"><font size="1">4</font></a> and can be seen, but cannot be seized with the hand; Gô<i>s</i> proceeded from the Moon <a name="fr_455"></a><a href="#fn_434"><font size="1">5</font></a> and can both be seen and seized with the hand <a name="fr_456"></a><a href="#fn_435"><font size="1">6</font></a>.' Râm is referred to here as being <i>h</i><i>v</i>âstra, 'lord of good pastures <a name="fr_457"></a><a href="#fn_436"><font size="1">7</font></a>.'</font></p>  
 <p>Of this Ya<i>s</i>t we have translations in Pahlavi, Persian, and Sanskrit (edited in Études Iraniennes, II).</p>

 <p><font size="-1">0. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced! . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, one who hates the Daêvas and obeys the laws of Ahura; For sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification unto [Hâvani], the holy and master of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>Unto the Moon that keeps in it the seed of the</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Bull; unto the only-created Bull and unto the Bull <a name="fr_458"></a><a href="#fn_437"><font size="1">1</font></a> of many species;</p>  
 <p>Be propitiation, with sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>1. Hail to Ahura Mazda! Hail to the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas! Hail to the Moon that keeps in it the seed of the Bull <a name="fr_459"></a><a href="#fn_438"><font size="1">2</font></a>! Hail to thee when we look at thee! Hail to thee when thou lookest at us <a name="fr_460"></a><a href="#fn_439"><font size="1">3</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>2. How does the moon wax? How does the moon wane?</p>  
 <p>For fifteen days does the moon wax <a name="fr_461"></a><a href="#fn_440"><font size="1">4</font></a>; for fifteen days does the moon wane. As long as her waxing, so long is the waning <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_441"><font size="1">5</font></a>; as long as her waning, so long is the waxing.</p>  
 <p>'Who is there but thee <a name="fr_463"></a><a href="#fn_442"><font size="1">6</font></a> who makes the moon wax and wane <a name="fr_464"></a><a href="#fn_443"><font size="1">7</font></a>?'</p>

 <p>3. We sacrifice unto the Moon that keeps in it the seed of the Bull, the holy and master of holiness.</p>  
 <p>Here I look at the moon, here I perceive the moon; here I look at the light of the moon, here I perceive the light of the moon. The Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas stand up <a name="fr_465"></a><a href="#fn_444"><font size="1">1</font></a>, holding its glory; the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas stand up, pouring its glory upon the earth, made by Mazda <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_445"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. And when the light of the moon waxes warmer, golden-hued plants grow on <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_446"><font size="1">3</font></a> from the earth during the spring <a name="fr_468"></a><a href="#fn_447"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the new moons, the full moons, and the Vîshaptathas <a name="fr_469"></a><a href="#fn_448"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the new moon, the holy and master of holiness;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the full moon, the holy and master of holiness;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Vîshaptatha, the holy and master of holiness.</p>

 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 91</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. I will sacrifice unto the Moon, that keeps in it the seed of the Bull, the liberal, bright, glorious, water-giving <a name="fr_470"></a><a href="#fn_449"><font size="1">1</font></a>, warmth-giving, wisdom-giving <a name="fr_471"></a><a href="#fn_450"><font size="1">2</font></a>, wealth-giving <a name="fr_472"></a><a href="#fn_451"><font size="1">3</font></a>, riches-giving, thoughtfulness-giving <a name="fr_473"></a><a href="#fn_452"><font size="1">4</font></a>, weal-giving, freshness-giving <a name="fr_474"></a><a href="#fn_453"><font size="1">5</font></a>, prosperity-giving <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="#fn_454"><font size="1">6</font></a>, the liberal, the healing.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">6. For its brightness and glory, I will offer unto it a sacrifice worth being heard, namely, unto the Moon that keeps in it the seed of the Bull.</font></p>  
 <p>Unto the Moon that keeps in it the seed of the Bull, we offer up the libations, the Haoma and meat, the baresma, the wisdom of the tongue, the holy spells, the speech, the deeds, the libations, and the rightly-spoken words.</p>  
 <p>Yê<i>n</i>hê Ham: All those beings of whom Ahura Mazda . . . .</p>  
 <p>7. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>I bless the sacrifice and prayer, and the strength and vigour of the Moon, that keeps in it the seed of the Bull, and of the only-created Bull, and of the Bull of many species.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good.</font></p>  
 <p>Give unto that man brightness and glory, give him health of body, . . . . give him the bright, all-happy, blissful abode of the holy Ones.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_441"></a><a href="sbe2312.htm#fr_462">89:5</a> According to the Parsis this waning does not refer to the moon, but to the constellations that help it in the struggle against the planets, which are supposed to belong to the Ahrimanian world (see Ormazd et Ahriman, §§ 223-226): 'while it waxes—namely, the moon—they wane,—namely, those that are opposed to the planets, to the bad stars; for instance, Haftôiring, Vana<i>n</i><i>d</i>, Ti<i>s</i>tar, Satvês; . . . . while it wanes—namely, the moon—they wax, that is to say, they are strong for doing good.' Thus the moon and the stars relieve each other in the battle against Ahriman.</p>
