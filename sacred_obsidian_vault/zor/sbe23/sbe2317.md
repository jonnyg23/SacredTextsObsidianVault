 <p><a name="page_168"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 168</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">XII. RASHN YA<i>S</i>T.</h4>  
 <p><font size="-1">Rashnu Razi<i>s</i>ta, 'the truest True,' is the Genius of Truth: he is one of the three judges of the departed, with Mithra and Sraosha: he holds the balance in which the deeds of men are weighed after their death: 'he makes no unjust balance . . . ., neither for the pious nor yet the wicked, neither for lords nor yet rulers; as much as a hair's breadth he will not vary, and he shows no favour <a name="fr_810"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' He is an offshoot either of Mithra, the God of Truth and the avenger of lies, or of Ahura Mazda himself, the all-knowing lord (§ 2 seq.).</font></p>  
 <p>This Ya<i>s</i>t seems to be an appeal made to Rashnu to come and attend the performance of the var nîrang or ordeal (see <a href="#page_170">p. 170</a>, note <a href="#fn_787">3</a>), of which Rashnu, as the Genius of Truth, was the natural witness and arbiter (cf. Vend. IV, 54-55 [154-156]). As a god of Truth must know everything and be present everywhere, he is called from whatever part of the world he may actually be in. This brings about an enumeration of all the parts of the world, from this earth (§§ 9-22) to the highest heaven (§ 37), passing through the Albôrz (§§ 23-26), the star region (§§ 26-32), the moon region (§ 33), and the sun region (§ 34; cf. <a href="sbe2310.htm#page_73">p. 73</a>, note <a href="sbe2310.htm#fn_359">2</a>).</p>  
 <p>This Ya<i>s</i>t is recited on the days of Rashn, Murdâd, Âshtâd, and Zemyâd (the 18th, 7th, 26th, and 28th of the month).</p>

 <p><font size="-1">0. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced </font></p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, one who hates the Daêvas and obeys the laws of Ahura;</p>  
 <p>For sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification unto [Hâvani], the holy and master of holiness.</p>  
 <p>Unto Rashnu Razi<i>s</i>ta; unto Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i>, who makes the world grow, who makes the world increase; unto the true-spoken speech, that makes the world grow <a name="fr_811"></a><a href="#fn_778"><font size="1">2</font></a>;</p>

 <p>Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>  <h4 align="center">I.</h4>  
 <p>1. The holy (Zarathu<i>s</i>tra) asked him <a name="fr_812"></a><a href="#fn_779"><font size="1">1</font></a>: 'O holy Ahura Mazda! I ask thee; answer me with words of truth, thou who knowest the truth. Thou art undeceivable, thou hast an undeceivable understanding; thou art undeceivable, as thou knowest everything.</p>  
 <p>'What of the Holy Word is created true? what is created progress-making? what is fit to discern? what is healthful? what is wise? what is happy and more powerful to destroy than all other creatures <a name="fr_813"></a><a href="#fn_780"><font size="1">2</font></a>?</p>  
 <p>2. Ahura Mazda answered: 'I will declare that unto thee, O pure, holy Spitama!</p>  
 <p>'The most glorious Holy Word (itself), this is what in the Holy Word is created true, what is created progress making, what is fit to discern, what is healthful, wise, and happy, what is more powerful to destroy than all other creatures.'</p>  
 <p>3. Ahura Mazda said: 'Bind up a three-twigged baresma against the way of the sun. [Address] unto me, Ahura Mazda, these words: "We invoke, we bless [Ahura] <a name="fr_814"></a><a href="#fn_781"><font size="1">3</font></a>; I invoke the friendship [of Ahura] towards this var <a name="fr_815"></a><a href="#fn_782"><font size="1">4</font></a> prepared, towards the fire and the baresma, towards the full boiling [milk <a name="fr_816"></a><a href="#fn_783"><font size="1">5</font></a>], towards the var <a name="fr_817"></a><a href="#fn_782"><font size="1">4</font></a> of oil and the sap <a name="fr_818"></a><a href="#fn_784"><font size="1">6</font></a> of the plants."</p>

 <p>'Along with the fiend-smiting Wind, along with the cursing thought of the wise <a name="fr_819"></a><a href="#fn_785"><font size="1">1</font></a>, along with the kingly Glory, along with Saoka <a name="fr_820"></a><a href="#fn_786"><font size="1">2</font></a>, made by Mazda.</p>

 <p>6. 'Then Rashnu the tall, the strong, will come for help unto thee, towards this var prepared, towards the fire and the baresma, towards the full boiling [milk], towards the var of oil and the sap of the plants:</p>  
 <p>'Along with the fiend-smiting Wind, along with the cursing thought of the wise, along with the kingly Glory, along with Saoka, made by Mazda.</p>
