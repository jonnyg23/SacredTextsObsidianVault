 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part II (SBE23)</i>, James Darmesteter, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">II. MIHIR NYÂYI<i>S</i>.</h3>  
 <p><font size="-1">1 <a name="fr_1590"></a><a href="#fn_1519"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Hail unto thee, O Ahura Mazda, in the threefold way! [Hail unto thee] before all other creatures!</font></p>  
 <p>Hail unto you, O Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas, who are all of you of one accord with the Sun!</p>  
 <p>May this prayer come unto Ahura Mazda! May it come unto the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas! May it come unto the Fravashis of the holy Ones! May it come unto the sovereign Vayu of the long Period!</p>  
 <p>2. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced! . . . .</p>  
 <p>3. I praise well-thought, well-spoken, and well-done thoughts, words, and deeds . . . .</p>  
 <p>4. I give sacrifice and prayer unto you, O Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas! . . . .</p>  
 <p>5. Hail to Ahura Mazda! . . . .</p>  
 <p>May I grow in health of body through Vohu-Manô, Khshathra, and Asha, and come to that luminous space, to that highest of all high things, when the world, O Spe<i>n</i>ta Mainyu, has come to an end!</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_1591"></a><a href="#fn_1520"><font size="1">5</font></a>. We sacrifice unto Mithra, the lord of wide pastures, who is truth-speaking, a chief in assemblies, with a thousand ears, well-shapen, with ten thousand</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_354"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 354</font></a></p>  
 <p>eyes, high, with full knowledge, strong, sleepless, and ever awake.</p>  
 <p>7. We sacrifice unto Mithra, the lord of all countries, whom Ahura Mazda made the most glorious of all the heavenly gods.</p>  
 <p>So may Mithra and Ahura, the two great gods, come to us for help!</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the undying, shining, swift-horsed Sun.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">8 <a name="fr_1592"></a><a href="#fn_1521"><font size="1">1</font></a>. We sacrifice unto Ti<i>s</i>trya, whose sight is sound . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>9. We sacrifice unto all the holy gods of the world unseen . . . .</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_1593"></a><a href="#fn_1522"><font size="1">2</font></a>. I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra . . . .</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_1594"></a><a href="#fn_1523"><font size="1">3</font></a>. We sacrifice unto Mithra, the lord of wide pastures, who is truth-speaking, a chief in assemblies, with a thousand ears, well-shapen, with a thousand eyes, high, with full knowledge, strong, sleepless, and ever awake.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra around countries;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra within countries;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra in this country;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra above countries;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra under countries;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra before countries;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the Mithra behind countries.</p>  
 <p>12. We sacrifice unto Mithra and Ahura, the two great, imperishable, holy gods; and unto the stars, and the moon, and the sun, with the trees that yield baresma. We sacrifice unto Mithra, the lord of all countries.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">13 <a name="fr_1595"></a><a href="#fn_1524"><font size="1">4</font></a>. For his brightness and glory, I will offer unto him a sacrifice worth being heard . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_355"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 355</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-1">We offer up libations unto Mithra . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>14. May he come to us for help! May he come to us for ease! . . . .</p>  
 <p>15. I will offer <i>u</i>p libations unto him, the strong Yazata, the powerful Mithra . . . .</p>  
 <p>Yê<i>n</i>hê hâtãm: All those beings of whom Ahura Mazda . . . .</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_1596"></a><a href="#fn_1525"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Yathâ aha vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>I bless the sacrifice and prayer, and the strength and vigour of Mithra . . . .</p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>[Give] unto that man brightness and glory, . . . . give him the bright, all-happy, blissful abode of the holy Ones.</p>
