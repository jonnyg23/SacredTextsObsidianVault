 <p><a name="page_159"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 159</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">XI. SRÔSH YA<i>S</i>T HÂDHÔKHT.</h3>  
 <p><font size="-1">There are two Ya<i>s</i>ts dedicated to Sraosha, the angel of divine worship <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">1</font></a>: one is a part of the Yasna (LVII [LVI]), and this, the other, is called the Srôsh Ya<i>s</i>t Hâdhôkht. Whether it belonged to the so-called Hâdhôkht Nosk <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">2</font></a>, one of the twenty-one Nosks of which the original Avesta was formed <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">3</font></a>, or whether it was recited in the Hâdhôkht sacrifice <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">4</font></a>, a particular liturgy, is a matter on which we have no sufficient information.</font></p>  
 <p>The two Ya<i>s</i>ts have a few developments in common (see §§ 89, 10-13): the Hâdhôkht is more liturgical, the Yasna Ya<i>s</i>t is more descriptive, and has to a greater degree the poetical imagery of a Ya<i>s</i>t.</p>  
 <p>The Srôsh Ya<i>s</i>t Hâdhôkht is recited every day, during any gâh except the Rapitvîn. A Pahlavi translation of this Ya<i>s</i>t is extant (East India Office, XII, 102; Paris, Supplément Persan, XXXIII, 259; edited in Études Iraniennes, II), and Anquetil mentions a Sanskrit translation.</p>  
 <p align="center">_____________________</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">0. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced! . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, one who hates the Daêvas and obeys the laws of Ahura;</p>  
 <p>For sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification unto [Hâvani], the holy and master of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>Unto the holy, strong Sraosha, who is the incarnate Word, a mighty-speared and lordly god,</p>  
 <p>Be propitiation, with sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>

 <p><a name="page_160"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 160</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">I.</h4>  
 <p>1. We sacrifice unto the holy, tall-formed, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world increase, the holy and master of holiness.</p>  
 <p>Good prayer, excellent prayer to the worlds <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">1</font></a>, O Zarathu<i>s</i>tra!</p>  
 <p>2. This it is that takes away the friendship of the fiend and fiends, of the he-fiend and of the she-fiend; it turns away in giddiness their eyes, minds, ears <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">2</font></a>, hands, feet, mouths, and tongues <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">3</font></a>; as good prayer, without deceit and without harm, is Manly Courage <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and turns away the Dru<i>g</i> <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. The holy Sraosha, the best protector of the poor, is fiend-smiting; he is the best smiter of the Dru<i>g</i>.</p>  
 <p>The faithful one who pronounces most words of blessing is the most victorious in victory; the Mãthra Spe<i>n</i>ta takes best the unseen Dru<i>g</i> away. The Ahuna Vairya <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">6</font></a> is the best fiend-smiter among all spells; the word of truth is the fighter <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_741"><font size="1">7</font></a> that is the best of all fiend-smiters.</p>  
 <p>The Law of the worshippers of Mazda is the truest giver of all the good things, of all those that are the offspring of the good principle; and so is the Law of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra.</p>  
 <p>4. And he who should pronounce that word <a name="fr_775"></a><a href="#fn_742"><font size="1">8</font></a>, O Zarathu<i>s</i>tra! either a man or a woman, with a mind all intent on holiness, with words all intent on</p>

 <p>holiness, with deeds all intent on holiness, when he is in fear either of high waters or of the darkness of a rainy night;</p>  
 <p>Or at the fords of a river, or at the branching-off of roads;</p>  
 <p>Or in the meeting together of the faithful, or the rushing together of the worshippers of the Daêvas <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">1</font></a>;</p>  
 <p>5. Whether on the road <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">2</font></a> or in the law <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">3</font></a> he has to fear, not in that day nor in that night shall the tormenting fiend, who wants to torment him, prevail to throw upon him the look of his evil eye, and the malice of the thief <a name="fr_779"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">4</font></a> who carries off cattle shall not reach him.</p>  
 <p>6. Pronounce then that word, O Zarathu<i>s</i>tra! that word to be spoken <a name="fr_780"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">5</font></a>, when thou fall upon the idolaters <a name="fr_781"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">6</font></a> and thieves and Daêvas rushing together. Then the malice of the wicked worshippers of the Daêvas, of the Yâtus and their followers, of the Pairikas and their followers, will be affrighted and rush away. Down are the Daêvas! Down are the Daêva-worshippers, and they take back their mouths from biting <a name="fr_782"></a><a href="#fn_749"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>

 <p>7. And therefore we take around us the holy-natured Sraosha, the holy, the fiend-smiter, as one does with shepherds’ dogs; therefore we sacrifice unto the holy-natured Sraosha, the holy, the fiend-smiter, with good thoughts, good words, and good deeds.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_783"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">1</font></a>. For his brightness and glory, for his strength and victorious power, for his offering sacrifices unto the gods <a name="fr_784"></a><a href="#fn_751"><font size="1">2</font></a>, I will offer him a sacrifice worth being heard. I will offer up libations unto the holy Sraosha, unto the great Ashi Vanguhi <a name="fr_785"></a><a href="#fn_752"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and unto Nairyô-sangha <a name="fr_786"></a><a href="#fn_753"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the tall-formed.</p>  
 <p>So may the holy Sraosha, the fiend-smiter, come to us for help!</p>  
 <p>9. We worship the holy Sraosha; we worship the great master, Ahura Mazda, who is supreme in holiness, who is the foremost to do deeds of holiness.</p>  
 <p>We worship all the words <a name="fr_787"></a><a href="#fn_754"><font size="1">5</font></a> of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, and all the good deeds, those done and those to be done.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Yê<i>n</i>hê hâtãm: All those beings of whom Ahura Mazda . . . .</font></p>  <h4 align="center">II.</h4>  
 <p><font size="-1">10 <a name="fr_788"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">6</font></a>. We sacrifice unto the holy, tall-formed, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world increase, the holy and master of holiness;</font></p>  
 <p>Who strikes the evil-doing <a name="fr_789"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">7</font></a> man, who strikes the evil-doing woman; who smites the fiendish</p>

 <p>11. Who, never sleeping, wakefully guards the creation of Mazda; who, never sleeping, wakefully maintains the creation of Mazda; who protects all the material world with his club uplifted, from the hour when the sun is down;</p>  
 <p>12. Who never more did enjoy sleep from the time when the two Spirits made the world, namely, the good Spirit and the evil One; who every day, every night, fights with the Mâzainya Daêvas.</p>  
 <p>13. He bows not for fear and fright before the Daêvas: before him all the Daêvas bow for fear and fright reluctantly, and rush away to darkness <a name="fr_791"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">For his brightness and glory, for his strength and victorious power . . . . <a name="fr_792"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">3</font></a></font></p>  <h3 align="center">III.</h3>  
 <p><font size="-1">14. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the holy, tall-formed, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world increase, the holy and master of holiness;</p>  
 <p>Who with peace and friendship <a name="fr_793"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">4</font></a> watches the Dru<i>g</i> and the most beneficent Spirit: so that the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas may go along the seven Karshvares of the earth <a name="fr_794"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">5</font></a>; who is the teacher of the</p>

 <p><font size="-1">For his brightness and glory, for his strength and victorious power . . . .</font></p>  <h4 align="center">IV.</h4>  
 <p><font size="-1">15. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the holy, tall-formed, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world increase, the holy and master of holiness;</p>  
 <p>Whom the holy Ahura Mazda has created to withstand Aêshma, the fiend of the wounding spear; we sacrifice unto Peace, whose breath is friendly, and unto the two withstanders of sin and guilt <a name="fr_796"></a><a href="#fn_763"><font size="1">2</font></a>,</p>  
 <p>16. The friends of the holy Sraosha;</p>  
 <p>The friends of Rashnu Razi<i>s</i>ta <a name="fr_797"></a><a href="#fn_764"><font size="1">3</font></a>;</p>  
 <p>The friends of the good Law of the worshippers of Mazda;</p>  
 <p>The friends of Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i> <a name="fr_798"></a><a href="#fn_765"><font size="1">4</font></a>, who makes the world grow, who makes the world increase, who makes the world prosper;</p>  
 <p>The friends of Ashi Vanguhi <a name="fr_799"></a><a href="#fn_766"><font size="1">5</font></a>;</p>  
 <p>The friends of the good <i>K</i>isti <a name="fr_800"></a><a href="#fn_767"><font size="1">6</font></a>;</p>

 <p>The friends of the Mãthra Spe<i>n</i>ta;</p>  
 <p>The friends of the fiend-destroying Law;</p>  
 <p>The friends of the long-traditional teaching;</p>  
 <p>The friends of the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas;</p>  
 <p>The friends of ourselves, the Saoshya<i>n</i><i>t</i>s <a name="fr_802"></a><a href="#fn_769"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the two-footed part of the holy creation;</p>  
 <p>The friends of all the beings of the holy world.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">For his brightness and glory, for his strength and victorious power. . . .</font></p>  <h4 align="center">V.</h4>  
 <p><font size="-1">18. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the holy, tall-formed, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world increase, the holy and master of holiness;</p>  
 <p>The first [Sraosha], the next, the middle, and the highest; with the first sacrifice, with the next, with the middle, and with the highest <a name="fr_803"></a><a href="#fn_770"><font size="1">2</font></a>. We sacrifice unto all [the moments] <a name="fr_804"></a><a href="#fn_771"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the holy and strong Sraosha, who is the incarnate Word;</p>  
 <p>19. The strong Sraosha, of the manly courage, the warrior of the strong arms, who breaks the skulls of the Daêvas; who smites with heavy blows <a name="fr_805"></a><a href="#fn_772"><font size="1">4</font></a> and is strong to smite; the holy Sraosha, who smites</p>

 <p>with heavy blows; we sacrifice unto the crushing Ascendant of both the holy Sraosha and Ar<i>s</i>ti <a name="fr_806"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>20. We sacrifice for all the houses protected by Sraosha, where the holy Sraosha is dear and friendly treated and satisfied, as well as the faithful man <a name="fr_807"></a><a href="#fn_774"><font size="1">2</font></a>, rich in good thoughts, rich in good words, rich in good deeds.</p>  
 <p>21. We sacrifice unto the body of the holy Sraosha;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of Rashnu Razi<i>s</i>ta;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of Mithra, the lord of wide pastures;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of the holy wind;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of the good Law of the worshippers of Mazda;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i>, who makes the world grow, who makes the world increase, who makes the world prosper;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of Ashi Vanguhi;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of the good <i>K</i>isti;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of the most right <i>K</i>ista;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the bodies of all the gods;</p>  
 <p>22. We sacrifice unto the body of the Mãthra Spe<i>n</i>ta;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of the fiend-destroying Law;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the body of the long-traditional teaching;</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the bodies of the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas;</p>

 <p>We sacrifice unto the bodies of all the beings of the holy world <a name="fr_808"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">For his brightness and glory, for his strength and victorious power . . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>23. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>I bless the sacrifice and prayer, the strength and vigour of the holy, strong Sraosha, who is the incarnate Word, a mighty-speared and lordly god.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">[Give] unto that man <a name="fr_809"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">2</font></a> brightness and glory, . . . . give him the bright, all-happy, blissful abode of the holy Ones!</font></p>

 <p><a name="fn_763"></a><a href="sbe2316.htm#fr_796">164:2</a> Pare<i>s</i>tas<i>k</i>a mravay<i>â</i>os<i>k</i>a, to be corrected, according to various readings, into staretas<i>k</i>a mavay<i>â</i>os<i>k</i>a or something like it; the two genii here alluded to are Anâstareta and Amuyamna, Sinlessness and Innocence, who are invoked in company with Akh<i>s</i>ti hamvai<i>ñ</i>ti in Vispêrad VIII, 4.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_770"></a><a href="sbe2316.htm#fr_803">165:2</a> The first sacrifice is the Yasna sacrifice; the next (literally, superior) is the Vispêrad; the middle sacrifice is the Hâdhôkht [and] êvak hômâst; the highest sacrifice is the Dvâzdah hômâst (Pahl. Comm.). Sraosha is called the first, next, middle, and highest, accordingly as he presides over one or the other of those sacrifices. For a definition of the êvak hômâst and Dvâzdah hômâst, see West, Pahlavi Texts, I, 212, note 5.</p>
