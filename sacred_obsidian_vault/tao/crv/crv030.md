 <h3 align="CENTER">24. TROUBLE FROM INDULGENCE.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao24"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao24.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>1. One on tiptoe is not steady;<br>
 One astride makes no advance.<br>
 Seff-displayers are not enlightened,<br>
 Self-asserters lack distinction, <a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 90</font></a><br>
 Self-approvers have no merit,<br>
 And self-seekers stunt their lives.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>2. Before Reason this is like surfeit of food; it is like a wen on the body with which people are apt to be disgusted.</p>
 <p>3. Therefore the man of reason will not indulge in it.</p>
