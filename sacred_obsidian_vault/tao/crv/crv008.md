 <h3 align="CENTER">2. SELF-CULTURE.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao02"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao02.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. Everywhere it is obvious that if beauty makes a display of beauty, it is sheer ugliness. It is obvious that if goodness makes a display of goodness, it is sheer badness. For<dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>2. "To be and not to be are mutually conditioned.<br>
 The difficult, the easy, are mutually definitioned.<br>
 The long, the short, are mutually exhibitioned.<br>
 Above, below, are mutually cognitioned.<br>
 The sound, the voice, are mutually coalitioned.<br>
 Before and after are mutually positioned."</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>3. Therefore</p>
 <p>The holy man abides by non-assertion in his affairs and conveys by silence his instruction. When the ten thousand things arise, verily, he refuses them not. <a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a> He quickens but owns not. He acts but claims not. Merit he accomplishes, but he does not dwell on it.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>"Since he does not dwell on it<br>
 It will never leave him."</p>
