 <h3 align="CENTER">63. CONSIDER BEGINNINGS.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao63"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao63.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table> 1. Assert non-assertion.
 <p>Practise non-practice.</p>
 <p>Taste the tasteless.</p>
 <p>Make great the small.</p>
 <p>Make much the little.</p>
 <p>2. Requite hatred with virtue.</p>
 <p>3. Contemplate a difficulty when it is easy. Manage a great thing when it is small.</p>
 <p>4. The world's most difficult undertakings necessarily originate while easy, and the world's greatest undertakings necessarily originate while small.</p>
 <p>5. Therefore the holy man to the end does not venture to play the great, and thus he can accomplish his greatness.</p>
 <p>6. Rash promises surely lack faith, and many easy things surely involve in many difficulties.</p>
 <p>7. Therefore, the holy man regards everything as difficult, and thus to the end encounters no difficulties.</p>
