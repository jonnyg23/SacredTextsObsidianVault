 <h3 align="CENTER">CHAPTER 27.</h3>
 <p>In Section 4 we have adopted an entirely new interpretation. In following a suggestion of Prof. H. A. Giles, we construe the two characters <i>shan</i> (words 6 and 14) denoting "good" or "goodness," as verbs in the sense to consider as good, and translate "to respect"; and further</p>
 <p><a name="page_162"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 162</font></a></p>
 <p>the characters <i>shi</i> (words 9 and 21) in their common meaning as "multitudes," not as we had it in former editions (though it is not wrong), as "educator."</p>
