 <h3 align="CENTER">59. HOLD FAST TO REASON.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao59"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao59.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. To govern the people is the affair of heaven and there is nothing like thrift.
 <p>Now consider that thrift is said to come from early practice.</p>
 <p>2. By early practice it is said that we can accumulate an abundance of virtue. If one accumulates an abundance of virtue then there is nothing that can not be overcome.</p>
 <p>3. When nothing can not be overcome then no one knows his limit. When no one knows his limit one can have possession of the commonwealth.</p>
 <p>4. Who has possession of the commonwealth's mother [thrift] may last and abide.</p>
 <p>5. This is called the possession of deep roots and of a staunch stem. To life, to everlastingness, to comprehension, this is the way.</p>
