 <h3 align="CENTER">76. BEWARE OF STRENGTH.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao76"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao76.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. Man during life is tender and delicate. When he dies he is stiff and stark.
 <p><a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 127</font></a></p>
 <p>2. The ten thousand things, the grass as well as the trees, while they live are tender and supple. When they die they are rigid and dry.</p>
 <p>3. Thus the hard and the strong are the companions of death. The tender and the delicate are the companions of life.</p>
 <p>Therefore he who in arms is strong will not conquer.</p>
 <p>4. When a tree has grown strong it is doomed.</p>
 <p>5. The strong and the great stay below. The tender and the delicate stay above.</p>
