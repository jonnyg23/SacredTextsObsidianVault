 <h3 align="CENTER">CHAPTER 29.</h3>
 <p>The doctrine of "doing the not-doing" has rightly been compared to the French principle of <i>laissez faire</i>, although the two are not the same. Lao-tze wants to say here that "he who makes, mars"; we therefore should not interfere but let everything take the course of its natural development.</p>
