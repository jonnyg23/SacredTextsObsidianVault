 <h3 align="CENTER">52. RETURNING TO THE ORIGIN.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao52"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao52.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. When the world takes its beginning, Reason becomes the world's mother.
 <p>2. As one knows his mother, so she in turn knows her child; as she quickens her child, so he in turn keeps to his mother, and to the end of life he is not in danger. Who closes his mouth, and shuts his sense-gates, in the end of life he will encounter no trouble; but who opens his mouth and meddles with affairs, <a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 110</font></a> in the end of life he cannot be saved.</p>
 <p>3. Who beholds his smallness is called enlightened. Who preserves his tenderness is called strong. Who uses Reason's light and returns home to its enlightenment does not surrender his person to perdition. This is called practising the eternal.</p>
