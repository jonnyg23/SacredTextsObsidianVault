 <h3 align="CENTER">69. THE FUNCTION OF THE MYSTERIOUS.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao69"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao69.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. A military expert used to say: 'I dare not act as host [who takes the initiative] but act as guest [with reserve]. I dare not advance an inch, but I withdraw a foot."
 <p>2. This is called marching without marching, threatening without arms, charging without hostility, seizing without weapons.</p>
 <p>3. No greater misfortune than making light of the enemy! When we make light of the enemy, it is almost as though we had lost our treasure--[compassion].</p>
 <p>4. Thus, if matched armies encounter one another, the one who does so in sorrow is sure to conquer.</p>
