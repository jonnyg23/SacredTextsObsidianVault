 <h3 align="CENTER">40.</h3><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>40. 1. The movement of the Tâo<br>
 By contraries proceeds;<br>
 And weakness marks the course<br>
 Of Tâo's mighty deeds.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>
 <p>2. All things under heaven sprang from It as existing (and named); that existence sprang from It as non-existent (and not named).</p>
 <p><a name="img_08400"></a>
 <img src="img/08400.jpg">, 'Dispensing with the Use (of Means);'--with their use, that is, as it appears to us. The subject of the brief chapter is the action of the Tâo by contraries, leading to a result the opposite of what existed previously, and by means which might seem calculated to produce a contrary result.</p>
 <p>In translating par. 2 1 have followed Ziâo Hung, who finds the key to it in ch. 1. Having a name, the Tâo is 'the Mother of all things;' having no name, it is 'the Originator of Heaven and Earth.' But here is the teaching of Lâo-dze:--'If Tâo seems to be before God,' Tâo itself sprang from nothing.</p>
