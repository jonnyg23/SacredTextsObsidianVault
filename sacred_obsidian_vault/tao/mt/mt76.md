 <p><a name="an_ch_73"></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXIII.</h3>  
 <p>The recklessly rash die. The cautiously courageous live. Of these two courses it is uncertain which is advantageous and which is disadvantageous, for who can explain why heaven disapproves? Therefore even the Holy Man feels a difficulty here. <a name="fr_242"></a><a href="#fn_242"><font size="1">1</font></a> This is the way of heaven—</p>  
 <p>Goodwill, which surely overcomes.</p>  
 <p>Silence, which certainly responds. <a name="fr_243"></a><a href="#fn_243"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>Without being summoned, spontaneously arriving.</p>  
 <p>Acting leisurely, but planning effectively.</p>  
 <p>Heaven's net spreads everywhere, wide in mesh, yet losing nothing. <a name="fr_244"></a><a href="#fn_244"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 10%; margin-right: 10%;"><div style="font-size: 90%">"Merry and bright are the waves to-day,<br>  They dance round our boat like children at play;<br>  But though wild winds should rise and dark waters roar,<br>  Till our light bark be cast a wreck on the shore;<br>  Still the strength which awes us is not found here,<br>  But beneath where all is calm and clear;<br>  Where feeling the weight of the law's behest,<br>  In the depths of the ocean is calm and rest."<br>                                              (Vantia Bailey.)</div></div>
