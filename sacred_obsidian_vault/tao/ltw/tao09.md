 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IX</h3>  <h3 align="center">MODERATION</h3>  
 <p>Continuing to fill a pail after it is full the water will be wasted. Continuing to grind an axe after it is sharp will soon wear it away.</p>  
 <p>Who can protect a public hall crowded with gold and jewels? The pride of wealth and position brings about their own misfortune. To win <a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="green">p. 15</font></a> true merit, to preserve just fame, the personality must be retiring. This is the heavenly Tao.</p>
