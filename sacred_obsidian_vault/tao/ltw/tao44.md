 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLIV</h3>  <h3 align="center">PRECEPTS</h3>  
 <p>Which is nearer, a name or a person? Which is more, personality or treasure? Is it more painful to gain or to suffer loss?</p>  
 <p>Extreme indulgence certainly greatly wastes. Much hoarding certainly invites severe loss.</p>  
 <p>A contented person is not despised. One who knows when to stop is not endangered; he will be able therefore to continue.</p>
