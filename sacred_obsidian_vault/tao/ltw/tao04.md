 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IV</h3>  <h3 align="center">TAO, WITHOUT ORIGIN</h3>  
 <p>The Tao appears to be emptiness but it is never exhausted. Oh, it is profound! It appears to have preceded everything. It dulls its own <a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="green">p. 13</font></a> sharpness, unravels its own fetters, softens its own brightness, identifies itself with its own dust.</p>  
 <p>Oh, it is tranquil! It appears infinite; I do not know from what it proceeds. It even appears to be antecedent to the Lord.</p>
