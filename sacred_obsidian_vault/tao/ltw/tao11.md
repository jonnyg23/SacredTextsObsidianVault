 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XI</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE VALUE OF NON-EXISTENCE</h3>  
 <p>Although the wheel has thirty spokes its utility lies in the emptiness of the hub. The jar is made by kneading clay, but its usefulness consists in its capacity. A room is made by cutting out windows and doors through the walls, but the space the walls contain measures the room's value.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="green">p. 16</font></a></p>  
 <p>In the same way matter is necessary to form, but the value of reality lies in its immateriality.</p>  
 <p>(Or thus: a material body is necessary to existence, but the value of a life is measured by its immaterial soul.)</p>
