 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXIII</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE VIRTUE (TEH) OF DISCRIMINATION</h3>  
 <p>He who knows others is intelligent; he who understands himself is enlightened; he who is able to conquer others has force, but he who is able to control himself is mighty. He who appreciates contentment is wealthy.</p>  
 <p>He who dares to act has nerve; if he can maintain <a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a> his position he will endure, but he, who dying does not perish, is immortal.</p>
