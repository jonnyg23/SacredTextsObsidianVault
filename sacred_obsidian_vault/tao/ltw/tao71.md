 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXXI</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE DISEASE OF KNOWLEDGE</h3>  
 <p>To recognize one's ignorance of unknowable things is mental health, and to be ignorant of knowable things is sickness. Only by grieving over ignorance of knowable things are we in <a name="page_48"><font size="1" color="green">p. 48</font></a> mental health. The wise man is wise because he understands his ignorance and is grieved over it.</p>
