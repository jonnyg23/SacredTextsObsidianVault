 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VII</h3>  <h3 align="center">HUMILITY</h3>  
 <p>Heaven is eternal, earth is lasting. The reason why heaven and earth are eternal and lasting is <a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="green">p. 14</font></a> because they do not live for themselves; that is the reason they will ever endure.</p>  
 <p>Therefore the wise man will keep his personality out of sight and because of so doing he will become notable. He subordinates his personality and therefore it is preserved.</p>  
 <p>Is it not because he is disinterested, that his own interests are conserved?</p>
