 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXV</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE TEH OF SIMPLICITY</h3>  
 <p>In the olden days those who obeyed the spirit of Tao did not enlighten the people but kept them simple hearted.</p>  
 <p>The reason people are difficult to govern is because of their smartness; likewise to govern a people with guile is a curse; and to govern them with simplicity is a blessing. He who <a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="green">p. 45</font></a> remembers these two things is a model ruler. Always to follow this standard and rule is teh, the profound.</p>  
 <p>Profound teh is deep indeed and far reaching. The very opposite of common things, but by it one obtains obedient subjects.</p>
