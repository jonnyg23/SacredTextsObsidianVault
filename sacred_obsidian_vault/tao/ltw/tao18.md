 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XVIII</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE PALLIATION OF THE INFERIOR</h3>  
 <p>When the great Tao is lost sight of, we still have the <i>idea</i> of benevolence and righteousness. Prudence and wisdom come to mind when we see great hypocrisy. When relatives are! unfriendly, we still have the <i>teachings</i> of filial piety and paternal affection. When the state and the clan are in confusion and disorder, we still have the ideals of loyalty and faithfulness.</p>
