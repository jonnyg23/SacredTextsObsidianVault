 <h3 align="CENTER">(Moral Injunctions.)</h3>
 <p>The right way leads forward; the wrong way backward.<sup>[19]</sup> (148-155)</p>
 <p>Do not proceed on an evil path. (156-159)</p>
 <p>Do not sin<sup>[20]</sup> in secret.<sup>[21]</sup> (160-163)</p>
 <p>Accumulate virtue, increase merit. (164-167)</p>
 <p>With a compassionate heart turn toward all creatures. (168-171)</p>
 <p>Be faithful, filial, friendly, and brotherly.<sup>[22]</sup> (172-175)</p>
 <p>First rectify thyself and then convert others. (176-179)</p>
 <p>{p. 53}</p>
 <p>Take pity on orphans, assist widows; respect the old, be kind to children. (180-187)</p>
 <p>Even the multifarious insects, herbs, and trees should not be injured. (188-195)</p>
 <p>Be grieved at the misfortune of others and rejoice at their good luck. (196-204)</p>
 <p>Assist those in need, and rescue those in danger. (205-212)</p>
 <p>Regard your neighbor's gain as your own gain, and regard your neighbor's loss as your own loss. (213-228)</p>
 <p>Do not call attention to the faults of others, nor boast of your own excellence. (229-236)</p>
 <p>Stay evil and promote goodness. (237-240)</p>
 <p>Renounce much, accept little. (241-244)</p>
 <p>Show endurance in humiliation and bear no grudge. (245-248)</p>
 <p>Receive favors as if surprised.<sup>[23]</sup> (249-252)</p>
 <p>Extend your help without seeking reward. (253-257)</p>
 <p>Give to others and do not regret or begrudge your liberality. (258-262)</p>
