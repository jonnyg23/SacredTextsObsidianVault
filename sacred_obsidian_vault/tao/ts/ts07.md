 <h3 align="CENTER">(A Description of Evil-Doers.)</h3>
 <p>Yet<sup>[28]</sup> there are some people whose behavior is unrighteous. (317-322)</p>
 <p>Their deportment is irrational.<sup>[29]</sup> (323-326)</p>
 <p>In evil they delight.<sup>[30]</sup> (327-330)</p>
 <p>With brutality they do harm and damage. (331-334)</p>
 <p>Insidiously they injure the good and the law-abiding. (335-338)</p>
 <p>Stealthily they despise their superiors and parents. (339-342)</p>
 <p>They disregard their seniors and rebel against those whom they serve. (343-350)</p>
 <p>They deceive the uninformed. (351-354)</p>
 <p>They slander their fellow-students. (355-358)</p>
 <p>Liars they are, bearing false witness, deceivers, and hypocrites; malevolent exposers of kith and kin<sup>[31]</sup>; mischievous and malignant; not humane; cruel and irrational; self-willed. (359-374)</p>
 <p>{p. 55}</p>
 <p>Right and wrong they confound. Their avowals and disavowals are not as they ought to be.<sup>[32]</sup> (375-382)</p>
 <p>They oppress their subordinates and appropriate their merit. (383-386)</p>
 <p>They cringe to superiors to curry favor. (387-390)</p>
 <p>Insentient to favors received, they remember their hatred and are never satisfied. (391-398)</p>
 <p>They hold in contempt the lives of Heaven's people.<sup>[33]</sup> (399-402)</p>
 <p>They agitate and disturb the public order. (403-406)</p>
 <p>They patronize the unscrupulous and do harm to the inoffensive. (407-413)</p>
 <p>They murder men to take their property, or have them ousted to take their places. (414-422)</p>
 <p>They slay the yielding and slaughter those who have surrendered. (423-426)</p>
 <p>They malign the righteous and dispossess the wise. (427-430)</p>
 <p>They molest orphans and wrong widows. (431-434)</p>
 <p>Disregarders of law they are, and bribe takers. They call crooked what is straight, straight what is crooked, and what is light they make heavy. (435-450)</p>
 <p>When witnessing an execution, they aggravate it by harshness. (451-454)</p>
 <p>{p. 56}</p>
 <p>Though they know their mistakes they do not correct them; though they know the good they do not do it. (455-462)</p>
 <p>In their own guilt they implicate others.<sup>[34]</sup> (463-466)</p>
 <p>They impede and obstruct the professions and crafts.<sup>[35]</sup> (467-470)</p>
 <p>They vilify and disparage the holy and the Wise. (471-474)</p>
 <p>They ridicule and scorn reason and virtue.<sup>[36]</sup> (475-478)</p>
 <p>They shoot the flying, chase the running, expose the hiding, surprise nestlings, close up entrance holes, upset nests, injure the pregnant, and break the egg. (479-494)</p>
 <p>They wish others to incur loss. (495-498)</p>
 <p>They disparage others that achieve merit. (499-502)</p>
 <p>They endanger others to save themselves. (503-506)</p>
 <p>They impoverish others for their own gain. (507-510)</p>
 <p>For worthless things they exchange what is valuable. (511-514)</p>
 <p>For private ends they neglect public duties. (515-518)</p>
 <p>They appropriate the accomplishments of their neighbor and conceal his good qualities. They make known his foibles and expose his</p>
 <p>{p. 57}</p>
 <p>secrets. They squander his property and cause divisions in his family.<sup>[37]</sup> (519-542)</p>
 <p>They attack that which is dear to others. (543-547)</p>
 <p>They assist others in doing wrong. (548-551)</p>
 <p>Their unbridled ambition makes for power, and through the degradation of others they seek success. (552-558)</p>
 <p>They destroy the crops and fields of others. (559-562)</p>
 <p>They break up betrothals. (563-566)</p>
 <p>Improperly they have grown rich, and withal they remain vulgar. (567-570)</p>
 <p>Improperly they shirk<sup>[38]</sup> without shame. (571-574)</p>
 <p>They claim having done acts of favor and disclaim being at fault. (575-578)</p>
 <p>They give away evil in marriage<sup>[39]</sup> and they sell wrongs. (579-582)</p>
 <p>They sell and buy vainglory. (583-586)</p>
 <p>They conceal and keep a treacherous heart. (s87-59o)</p>
 <p>They crush that which is excellent in others. (591-594)</p>
 <p>They are careful in hiding their shortcomings. (595-598)</p>
 <p>Being on a high horse they threaten and intimidate. (599-6012)</p>
 <p>{p. 58}</p>
 <p>With unrestrained barbarism they kill and stab. (603-606)</p>
 <p>Recklessly they cut cloth to waste.<sup>[40]</sup> (607-610)</p>
 <p>Without festive occasions they prepare cattle for food.<sup>[41]</sup> (611-614)</p>
 <p>They scatter and waste the five cereals.<sup>[42]</sup> (615-618)</p>
 <p>They trouble and annoy many people. (619-622)</p>
 <p>They break into others' houses to take their property and valuables. (623-630)</p>
 <p>They misdirect the water and light fires to destroy the people's homes. (631-638)</p>
 <p>They upset others' plans so as to prevent their success. (639-646)</p>
 <p>They spoil a worker's utensils to hamper his efficiency. (647-654)</p>
 <p>When seeing the success and prosperity of others they wish them to run down and fail. (655-662)</p>
 <p>Seeing the wealth of others, they wish them bankrupt and ruined. (663-670)</p>
 <p>They cannot see beauty without cherishing in their hearts thoughts of seduction. (671-678)</p>
 <p>Being indebted to others for goods or property, they wish their creditors to die. (679-686)</p>
 <p>When their requests are not granted they begin to curse and wax hateful. (687-694)</p>
 <p>{p. 59}</p>
 <p>Seeing their neighbor lose his vantage they gossip of his failure. (695-702)</p>
 <p>Seeing a man imperfect in his bodily features they ridicule him. (703-711)</p>
 <p>Observing the talent and ability of a man worthy of praise, they suppress the truth. (712-720)</p>
 <p>They use charms<sup>[43]</sup> for the sake of controlling others.<sup>[44]</sup> (721-724)</p>
 <p>They employ drugs to kill trees. (725-728)</p>
 <p>Ill-humored and angry they are towards teachers and instructors. (729-732)</p>
 <p>They resist and provoke father and elders. (733-736)</p>
 <p>With violence they seize, with violence they demand. (737-740)</p>
 <p>They delight in fraud, they delight in robbery, they make raids and commit depredations to get rich. (741-748)</p>
 <p>By artful tricks they seek promotion. (749-752)</p>
 <p>They reward and punish without justice. (753-756)</p>
 <p>They indulge in comforts and enjoyments without measure. (757-761)</p>
 <p>They harass and tyrannize over their subordinates. (762-765)</p>
 <p>They terrify and threaten to overawe others. (766-768)</p>
 <p>{p. 60}</p>
 <p>They accuse heaven and find fault with man. (769-772)</p>
 <p>They blame the wind and rail at the rain. (773-776)</p>
 <p>They stir up party strife and law suits. (777-780)</p>
 <p>Unprovoked they join factious associations.<sup>[45]</sup> (781-784)</p>
 <p>They rely on their wives' and other women's gossip. (785-788)</p>
 <p>They disobey the instructions of father and mother. (789-792)</p>
 <p>They take up the new and forget the old. (793-796)</p>
 <p>Their mouth asserts what their heart denies. (797-800)</p>
 <p>Shamelessly greedy they are for wealth. (801-804)</p>
 <p>They deceive their father and their superiors. (805-808)</p>
 <p>They invent and circulate vile talk, traducing and slandering innocent men. (809-816)</p>
 <p>They slander others, yet themselves feign honesty. (817-820)</p>
 <p>They rail at spirits and claim to be right themselves. (821-824)</p>
 <p>They reject a good cause and espouse a wrong cause, spurning what is near, longing for the distant.<sup>[46]</sup> (825-832)</p>
 <p>{p. 61}</p>
 <p>They point at heaven and earth<sup>[47]</sup> to make them witnesses of their mean thoughts. (833-839)</p>
 <p>They even call on bright spirits to make them witness their degrading deeds. (840-846)</p>
 <p>When they ever give charity they regret it afterwards. (847-850)</p>
 <p>They borrow and accept without intention to return. (851-854)</p>
 <p>Beyond their due lot they scheme and contrive. (855-858)</p>
 <p>Above their means they plot and plan. (859-862)</p>
 <p>Their lusty desires exceed all measure. (863-866)</p>
 <p>Their heart is venomous while they show a compassionate face. (867-870)</p>
 <p>With filthy food they feed the poor. (871-874)</p>
 <p>With heresies they mislead others. (875-878)</p>
 <p>They shorten the foot, they narrow the measure, they lighten the scales, they reduce the peck. (879-886)</p>
 <p>They adulterate the genuine, and they seek profit<sup>[48]</sup> in illegitimate business. (887-894)</p>
 <p>They compel respectable people to become lowly. (895-898)</p>
 <p>They betray and deceive the simple-minded. (899-902)</p>
 <p>They are greedy and covetous without satiety. (903-906)</p>
 <p>{p. 62}</p>
 <p>They curse and swear to seek vindication. (907-910)</p>
 <p>Indulging in liquor they become rebellious and unruly. (911-914)</p>
 <p>With the members of their own family<sup>[37]</sup> they are angry and quarrelsome. (915-918)</p>
 <p>As husbands<sup>[49]</sup> they are neither faithful nor kind. (919-922)</p>
 <p>As wives<sup>[49]</sup> they are neither gentle nor pliant. (923-926)</p>
 <p>As husbands they are not in harmony with their wives;<sup>[50]</sup> as wives they are not respectful to their husbands. (927-934)</p>
 <p>As husbands they delight in bragging and conceit. (935-938)</p>
 <p>Always as wives they practice jealousy and suspicion. (939-952)</p>
 <p>As husbands they behave unmannerly toward their wives and children. (943-947)</p>
 <p>As wives they lack propriety to their father-in-law and their mother-in-law. (948-952)</p>
 <p>They make light of the spirit of their ancestor. (953-956)</p>
 <p>They disobey and dislike the commands of their superiors. (957-960)</p>
 <p>They make and do what is not useful. (961-964)</p>
 <p>They harbor and keep a treacherous<sup>[51]</sup> heart. (965-968)</p>
 <p>{p. 63}</p>
 <p>They curse themselves<sup>[52]</sup>, they curse others. (969-972)</p>
 <p>They are partial in their hatred and partial in their love. (973-976)</p>
 <p>They step over the well and they step over the hearth. They jump over the food and jump over a person.<sup>[53]</sup> (977-984)</p>
 <p>They kill the baby and cause abortion of the unborn. (985-988)</p>
 <p>They do many clandestine and wrong deeds. (989-992)</p>
 <p>The last day of the month and the last day of the year they sing and dance.<sup>[54]</sup> The first day of the month, the first day of the year, they start roaring and scolding. (993-1000)</p>
 <p>Facing the north, they snivel and spit; facing the hearth, they sing, hum and weep.<sup>[55]</sup> (1001-1012)</p>
 <p>Further, with hearth fire they burn incense,<sup>[56]</sup> and with filthy fagots they cook their food. (1013-1018)</p>
 <p>In the night they rise and expose their nakedness.<sup>[57]</sup> (1019-1022)</p>
 <p>On the eight festivals of the seasons they execute punishment.<sup>[58]</sup> (1023-1030)</p>
 <p>They spit at falling stars and point at the many-colored rainbow.<sup>[59]</sup> (1031-1036)</p>
 <p>Irreverently they point at the three</p>
 <p>{p. 64}</p>
 <p>luminaries;<sup>[60]</sup> intently they gaze at the sun and at the moon. (1037-1044)</p>
 <p>In the spring they hunt with fire.<sup>[61]</sup> (1045-1048)</p>
 <p>Facing the north, they use vile language.<sup>[55]</sup> (1049-1052)</p>
 <p>Causelessly they kill tortoises and snakes. (1053-1058)</p>
