 <h3>VII 0</h3>
 Qui legent hosce versus, maturé censunte :<br>
 Prophanum vulgus &amp; inscium ne attrectato :<br>
 Omnesque Astrologi, Blenni, Barbari procul sunto,<br>
 Qui aliter faxit, is rité sacer esto.<br>
 <p>
 </p><h3>VII 1</h3>
 L'Arc du thresor par Achilles deceu.<br>
 Aux procrees sceu la quadrangulaire :<br>
 Au faict Royal le comment sera sceu,<br>
 Corps veu pendu au veu du populaire.<br>
 <p>
 The arc of the treasure deceived by Achilles,<br>
 the quadrangle known to the procreators.<br>
 The invention will be known by the Royal deed;<br>
 a corpse seen hanging in the sight of the populace.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 2</h3>
 Par Mars ouuert Arles ne donra guerre,<br>
 De nuict seront les soldats estonnez :<br>
 Noir, blanc à l'inde dissimulé en terre,<br>
 Sous la saincte ombre traistre verrez &amp; sonnez.<br>
 <p>
 Opened by Mars Arles will not give war,<br>
 the soldiers will be astonished by night.<br>
 Black and white concealing indigo on land<br>
 under the false shadow you will see traitors sounded.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 3</h3>
 Apres de France la victoire nauale,<br>
 Les Barchinons, Sallinons, les Phocens,<br>
 Lierre d'or, l'enclume serré dedans la balle,<br>
 Ceux de Prolon au fraud seront contens.<br>
 <p>
 After the naval victory of France,<br>
 the people of Barcelona the Saillinons and those of Marseilles;<br>
 the robber of gold, the anvil enclosed in the ball,<br>
 the people of Ptolon will be party to the fraud.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 4</h3>
 Le Duc de Langres assiegé dedans Dole,<br>
 Accompagné d'Ostun &amp; Lyonnois :<br>
 Ceneue, Auspourg ioinct ceux de Mirandole,<br>
 Passer les monts conter les Anconnois.<br>
 <p>
 The Duke of Langres besieged at Dôle<br>
 accompanied by people from Autun and Lyons.<br>
 Geneva, Augsburg allied to those of Mirandola,<br>
 to cross the mountains against the people of Ancona.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 5</h3>
 Vin sur la table en sera respandu,<br>
 Le tiers n'aura celle qu'il pretendoit :<br>
 Deux fois du noir de Parme descendu :<br>
 Perouse à Pise fera ce qu'il cuidoit.<br>
 <p>
 Some of the wine on the table will be spilt,<br>
 the third will not have that which he claimed.<br>
 Twice descended from the black one of Parma,<br>
 Perouse will do to Pisa that which he believed.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 6</h3>
 Naples, Palerme, &amp; toute la Cecile,<br>
 Par main babare sera inhabitée,<br>
 Corsicque, Salerne &amp; de Sardeigne l'Isle,<br>
 Faim, peste guerre, fin de maux intemptée.<br>
 <p>
 Naples, Palerma and all of Sicily<br>
 will be uninhabited through Barbarian hands.<br>
 Corsica, Salerno and the island of Sardinia,<br>
 hunger, plague, war the end of extended evils.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 7</h3>
 Sur le combat des grands cheuaux legers,<br>
 On criera le grand croissant confond.<br>
 De nuict tuer mont, habits de bergers,<br>
 Abismes rouges dans le fossé profond.<br>
 <p>
 Upon the struggle of the great light horses,<br>
 it will be claimed that the great crescent is destroyed.<br>
 To kill by night, in the mountains, <br>
 dressed in shepherd’s' clothing, red gulfs in the deep ditch.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 8</h3>
 Flora, fuis, fuis le plus proche Romain,<br>
 Au Fesulan sera conflict donné :<br>
 Sang espandu, les plus grands prins à main,<br>
 Temple ne sexe ne sera pardonné.<br>
 <p>
 Florense, flee, flee the nearest Roman,<br>
 at Fiesole will be conflict given:<br>
 blood shed, the greatest one take by the hand,<br>
 neither temple nor sex will be pardoned.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 9</h3>
 Dame à l'absence de son grand capitaine,<br>
 Sera priée d'amour du Viceroy,<br>
 Faincte promesse &amp; mal'heureuse estreine.<br>
 Entre les mains du grand Prince Barroys.<br>
 <p>
 The lady in the absence of her great master<br>
 will be begged for love by the Viceroy.<br>
 Feigned promise and misfortune in love,<br>
 in the hands of the great Prince of Bar.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 10</h3>
 Par le grand Prince limitrophe du Mans,<br>
 Preux &amp; vaillant chef de grand exercite :<br>
 Par mer &amp; terre de Gallois &amp; Normans,<br>
 Caspre passer Barcelonne pillé Isle.<br>
 <p>
 By the great Prince bordering Le Mans,<br>
 brave and valiant leader of the great army;<br>
 by land and sea with Bretons and Normans,<br>
 to pass Gibraltar and Barcelona to pillage the island.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 11</h3>
 L'enfant Royal contemnera la mere,<br>
 Oeil, pieds blessez, rude, inobeissant,<br>
 Nouuelle à dame estrange &amp; bien amere,<br>
 Seront tuez des siens plus de cinq cens.<br>
 <p>
 eye, feet wounded rude disobedient;<br>
 strange and very bitter news to the lady;<br>
 more than five hundred of here people will be killed.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 12</h3>
 Le grand puisnay fera fin de la guerre.<br>
 Aux dieux assemble les excusez :<br>
 Cahors, Moissac iront long de la serre,<br>
 Reffus Lestore, les Agenois rasez.<br>
 <p>
 The great younger son will make an end of the war,<br>
 he assembles the pardoned before the gods;<br>
 Cahors and Moissac will go far from the prison,<br>
 a refusal at Lectoure, the people of Agen shaved.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 13</h3>
 De la cité marine &amp; tributaire,<br>
 La teste raze prendra la satrapie :<br>
 Chasser sordide qui puis sera contraire,<br>
 Par quatorze ans tiendra la tyrannie.<br>
 <p>
 From the marine tributary city,<br>
 the shaven head will take up the satrapy;<br>
 to chase the sordid man who will the be against him.<br>
 For fourteen years he will hold the tyranny.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 14</h3>
 Faux exposer viendra topographie,<br>
 Seront les cruches des monuments ouuertes :<br>
 Pulluler secte, faincte philosophie,<br>
 Pour blanches, noires, &amp; pour antiques vertes.<br>
 <p>
 He will come to expose the false topography,<br>
 the urns of the tombs will be opened.<br>
 Sect and holy philosophy to thrive,<br>
 black for white and the new for the old.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 15</h3>
 Deuant cité de l'Insobre contrée,<br>
 Sept ans sera le siege deuant mis :<br>
 Le tresgrand Roy y fera son entrée,<br>
 Cité puis libre hors de ses ennemis.<br>
 <p>
 Before the city of the Insubrian lands,<br>
 for seven years the siege will be laid;<br>
 a very great king enters it,<br>
 the city is then free, away from its enemies.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 16</h3>
 Entrée profonde par la grand Royne faicte;<br>
 Rendra le lieu puissant inaccessible :<br>
 L'armée des trois Lyons sera deffaicte,<br>
 Faisant dedans cas hideux &amp; terrible.<br>
 <p>
 The deep entry made by the great Queen <br>
 will make the place powerful and inaccessible;<br>
 the army of the three lions will be defeated<br>
 causing within a thing hideous and terrible.<br>
 </p><p>

 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 18</h3>
 Les assiegez couleront leurs paches,<br>
 Sept iours apres feront cruelle issuë,<br>
 Dans repoulsez, feu sang. Sept mis à l'hache<br>
 Dame captiue qu'auoit la paix tissuë.<br>
 <p>
 The besieged will color their pacts,<br>
 but seven days later they will make a cruel exit:<br>
 thrown back inside, fire and blood, seven put to the ax<br>
 the lady who had woven the peace is a captive.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 19</h3>
 Le fort Nicene ne sera combatu,<br>
 Vaincu sera par rutilant metal,<br>
 Son faict sera vn long temps debatu<br>
 Aux citadins estrange espouuantal.<br>
 <p>
 The fort at Nice will not engage in combat,<br>
 it will be overcome by shining metal.<br>
 This deed will be debated for a long time,<br>
 strange and fearful for the citizens.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 20</h3>
 Ambassadeurs de la Toscane langue,<br>
 Auril &amp; May Alpes &amp; mer passer :<br>
 Celuy de veau exposera l'harangue,<br>
 Vie Gauloise ne venant effacer.<br>
 <p>
 Ambassadors of the Tuscan language <br>
 will cross the Alps and the sea in April and May.<br>
 The man of the calf will deliver an oration, <br>
 not coming to wipe out the French way of life.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 21</h3>
 Par pestilente inimitié Volsicque,<br>
 Dissimulée chassera le tyran :<br>
 Au pont de Sorgues se fera la traffique,<br>
 De mettre à mort luy &amp; son adherant.<br>
 <p>
 By the pestilential enmity of Languedoc,<br>
 the tyrant dissimulated will be driven out.<br>
 The bargain will be made on the bridge at Sorgues<br>
 to put to death both him and his follower<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 22</h3>
 Les Citoyens de Mesopotamie,<br>
 Irez encontre amis de Tarraconne,<br>
 Ieux, rits, banquets, toute gent endormie<br>
 Vicaire au Rosne, prins cité, ceux d'Ausone.<br>
 <p>
 The citizens of Mesopotamia<br>
 angry with their friends from Tarraconne;<br>
 games, rites, banquets, every person asleep,<br>
 the vicar at Rhône, the city taken and those of Ausonia.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 23</h3>
 Le Royal sceptre sera contrainct de prendre,<br>
 Ce que ses predecesseurs auoient engagé :<br>
 Puis que l'aneau on fera mal entendre,<br>
 Lors qu'on viendra le palais saccager.<br>
 <p>
 The Royal scepter will be forced to take<br>
 that which his predecessors had pledged.<br>
 Because they do not understand about the ring<br>
 when they come to sack the palace.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 24</h3>
 L'enseuely sortira du tombeau,<br>
 Fera de chaines lier le fort du pont :<br>
 Empoisonné auec ufs de Barbeau,<br>
 Grand de Lorraine par le Marquis du Pont.<br>
 <p>
 He who was buried will come out of the tomb,<br>
 He will cause the fort of the bridge to be tied in chains:<br>
 Poisoned with the spawn of a pimp,<br>
 the great one from Lorraine by the Marquis du Pont.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 25</h3>
 Par guerre longue tout l'exercice expuiser,<br>
 Que pour soldats ne trouueront pecune :<br>
 Lieu d'or d'argent, cuir on viendra cuser,<br>
 Gaulois aerain, signe croissant de Lune :<br>
 <p>
 Through long war all the army exhausted,<br>
 so that they do not find money for the soldiers;<br>
 instead of gold or silver, they will come to coin leather,<br>
 Gallic brass, and the crescent sign of the Moon.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 26</h3>
 Fustes &amp; galees autour de sept nauires,<br>
 Sera liurée vne mortelle guerre :<br>
 Chef de Madric receura coup de vires,<br>
 Deux eschapées, &amp; cinq menées à terre.<br>
 <p>
 Foists and galleys around seven ships,<br>
 a mortal war will be let loose.<br>
 The leader from Madrid will receive a wound from arrows,<br>
 two escaped and five brought to land.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 27</h3>
 Au cainct de Vast la grand caualerie,<br>
 Proche à Ferrage empesché au bagage,<br>
 Pompe a Turin feront tel volerie,<br>
 Que dans le fort rauiront leur hostage.<br>
 <p>
 At the wall of Vasto the great cavalry<br>
 are impeded by the baggage near Ferrara.<br>
 At Turin they will speedily commit such robbery <br>
 that in the fort they will ravish their hostage.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 28</h3>
 Le capitaine conduira grande proye,<br>
 Sur la montagne des ennemis plus proche :<br>
 Enuironné, par feu fera telle voye,<br>
 Tous eschappez, or trente mis en broche.<br>
 <p>
 The captain will lead a great herd<br>
 on the mountain closest to the enemy.<br>
 Surrounded by fire he makes such a way,<br>
 all escape except for thirty put on the spit.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 29</h3>
 Le grand Duc d'Albe se viendra rebeller,<br>
 A ses grands peres fera le tradiment :<br>
 Le grand de Guise le viendra debeller,<br>
 Captif mené &amp; dressé monument.<br>
 <p>
 The great one of Alba will come to rebel,<br>
 he will betray his great forebears.<br>
 The great man of Guise will come to vanquish him,<br>
 led captive with a monument erected.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 30</h3>
 Le sac s'approche, feu, grand song espandu,<br>
 Po, grand fleuues, aux bouuiers l'entreprinse,<br>
 De Gennes, Nice, apres long attendu,<br>
 Foussan, Turin, à Sauillan la prinse.<br>
 <p>
 The sack approaches, fire and great bloodshed.<br>
 Po the great rivers, the enterprise for the clowns;<br>
 after a long wait from Genoa and Nice,<br>
 Fossano, Turin the capture at Savigliano.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 31</h3>
 De Languedoc, &amp; Guienne plus de dix,<br>
 Mille voudront les Alpes repasser :<br>
 Grans Allobroges marcher contre Brundis,<br>
 A quin &amp; Bresse les viendront recasser.<br>
 <p>
 From Languedoc and Guienne more than ten<br>
 thousand will want to cross the Alps again.<br>
 The great Savoyards march against Brindisi,<br>
 Aquino and Bresse will come to drive them back.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 32</h3>
 Du mont Royal naistra d'vne casane,<br>
 Qui caue, &amp; compte viendra tyranniser,<br>
 Dresser copie de la marche Millane,<br>
 Fauene, Florence d'or &amp; gens espuiser.<br>
 <p>
 From the bank of Montereale will be born one <br>
 who bores and calculates becoming a tyrant.<br>
 To raise a force in the marches of Milan,<br>
 to drain Faenza and Florence of gold and men<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 33</h3>
 Par fraude, regne, forces expolier,<br>
 La classe obsesse, passages à l'espie :<br>
 Deux faincts amis se viendront t'allier,<br>
 Esueiller haine de long temps assoupie.<br>
 <p>
 The kingdom stripped of its forces by fraud,<br>
 the fleet blockaded, passages for the spy;<br>
 two false friends will come to rally <br>
 to awaken hatred for a long time dormant.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 34</h3>
 En grand regret sera la gent Gauloise,<br>
 Cur vain, leger croira temerité :<br>
 Pain, sel, ne vin, eau, venin ne ceruoise,<br>
 Plus grand captif, faim, froid, necessité.<br>
 <p>
 The French nation will be in great grief,<br>
 vain and lighthearted, they will believe rash things.<br>
 No bread, salt, wine nor water, venom nor ale,<br>
 the greater one captured, hunger, cold and want.<br>

 </p><h3>VII 36</h3>
 Dieu, le ciel tout le diuin verbe à l'onde,<br>
 Porté par rouges sept razes à Bizance,<br>
 Contre les oingts trois cens de Trabisconde<br>

 carried by seven red-shaven heads to Byzantium:<br>
 against the anointed three hundred from Trebizond,<br>
 will make two laws, first horror then trust.<br>

 L'aisné Royal sur coursier voltigeant,<br>
 Picquer viendra si rudement courir :<br>
 Gueulle, lepée, pied dans l'estrein pleignant,<br>
 Trainé, tire, horriblement mourir.<br>
 <p>
 The elder royal one on a frisky horse<br>
 will spur so fiercely that it will bolt.<br>
 Mouth, mouthful, foot complaining in the embrace;<br>
 dragged, pulled, to die horribly.<br>
 </p><p>

 Le conducteur de l'armée Françoise,<br>
 Cuidant perdre le principale phalange :<br>
 Par sus paué de l'auaigne &amp; d'ardoise,<br>
 Soy parfondra par Gennes gent estrange.<br>
 <p>
 The leader of the French army<br>
 will expect to lose the main phalanx.<br>
 Upon the pavement of oats and slate<br>
 the foreign nation will be undermined through Genoa.<br>
 </p><p>

 <p>
 Within casks anointed outside with oil and grease<br>
 twenty-one will be shut before the harbor,<br>
 at second watch; through death they will do great deeds;<br>
 to win the gates and be killed by the watch.<br>
 </p><p>
 </p><h3>VII 41</h3>
 Les os des pieds &amp; des mains enserrez,<br>

 Seront par songes concauant deterrez,<br>
 Maison salubre &amp; sans bruit habitee.<br>
 <p>
 The bones of the feet and the hands locked up,<br>
 because of the noise the house is uninhabited for a long time.<br>
 Digging in dreams they will be unearthed,<br>
 the house healthy in inhabited without noise.<br>
 </p><p>

 <p>
 Two newly arrived have seized the poison,<br>
 to pour it in the kitchen of the great Prince.<br>
 By the scullion both are caught in the act,<br>
 taken he who thought to trouble the elder with death.<br>

 Then to rebel much more and by warm night.<br>

 Par le retour du voyage Barbare<br>
 Exalteront la protestante entrée.<br>

 <p>

 Wind warm, counsels, tears, timidity,<br>

 Great calamity from oppression,<br>
 The wedding song converted, weeping and tears.<br>
