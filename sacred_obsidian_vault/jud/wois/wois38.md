 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Wisdom of Israel</i>, by Edwin Collins, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHILD WHO QUESTIONED THOUGH CARRIED BY HIS FATHER.</h3>  
 <p>Commenting on Exod. xvii. 7, "Is the Lord among us or not?" our sages say that for this want of faith the Israelites were brought in contact</p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>with their enemy Amelek. This matter may be likened to the parable of a child who is riding on his father's shoulders, and on meeting a friend of his father's, calls out: "Have you seen my father anywhere?" Then his father sayeth unto him: "Thou art riding on my shoulders, and thou askest questions about me. I will put thee down for a moment in the presence of the enemy, so as to teach thee what my absence would mean."</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px"><i>Shemoth Rabbah</i>, Chap. XXVI.</div>
