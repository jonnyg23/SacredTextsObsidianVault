 <p align="center"><a name="img_vinecup"></a>
 <img src="img/vinecup.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">Kiddush</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small>On Sabbath eve begin here.</small></p>  
 <p align="center">The master of the house lifts up the wine-cup and says:</p>  
 <p><a name="img_linit"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/linit.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>LET US praise God and thank Him for all the blessings of the week that is gone; for life, health and strength; for home, love and friendship; for the discipline of our trials and temptations; for the happiness of our success and prosperity. Thou hast ennobled us, O God, by the blessings of work, and in love and grace sanctified us by the blessings of rest, through the commandment, "Six days shalt thou labor and do all thy work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath unto the Lord thy God".  
 <p align="center"><small>On weeks days begin here.</small></p>  
 <p>With song and praise, and with the symbols of our feast, let us renew the memories of our past.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who hast chosen us from all peoples and exalted anti sanctified us with Thy commandments. In love hast Thou given us, O Lord our God, solemn days of joy and festive seasons of gladness, even this day of the Feast of Unleavened Bread, a holy convocation unto us, a memorial of the departure from Egypt. Thou hast chosen us for Thy service and</p>  
 <p><a name="page_5"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 5</font></a> <a name="page_6"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 6</font></a></p>  
 <p>hast made us sharers in the blessings of Thy holy festivals. Blessed art Thou, O Lord, who sanctifiest Israel and the festive seasons.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>All read in unison:</small></p>  
 <p>BORUCH ATTO ADONOI ELOHENU MELECH HOOLOM BORE PRI HAGGOFEN.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, Ruler of the world, who hast created the fruit of the vine.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>Drink the first cup of wine.</small></p>  
 <p><a name="page__000"></a> <a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a> <a name="page__001"></a></p>

 <p align="center"><a name="img_exod00"></a>
 <img src="img/exod00.jpg"></p>  <h4 align="center">2. ‏כַּרְפַּס‎</h4>  
 <p>Some parsley, lettuce or watercress is distributed to all present who dip it in salt water or in vinegar, and before partaking of it say in unison:</p>  
 <p>BORUCH ATTO ADONOI ELOHENU MELECH HOOLOM BORE PRI HOADOMO.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, Creator of the fruit of the earth.</p>  <h4 align="center">3. ‏יַֽחַץ‎</h4>  
 <p>The leader breaks the middle Matzo, leaving one half on the Seder-dish, and hiding the other half as the Aphikomon to be eaten at the end of the meal.</p>

 <p align="center"><a name="img_torah"></a>
 <img src="img/torah.jpg"></p>  <h4 align="center">4. ‏מַגִּיר‎</h4>  
 <p align="center"><small>The leader lifts up the Matzos and says:</small></p>  
 <p>Lo! This is the bread of affliction which our fathers ate in the land of Egypt. Let all who are hungry come and eat. Let all who are in want come and celebrate the Passover with us. May it be God's will to redeem us from all trouble and from all servitude. Next year at this season, may the whole house of Israel be free!</p>
