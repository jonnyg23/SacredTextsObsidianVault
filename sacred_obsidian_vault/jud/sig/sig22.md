 <p><a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 25</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">19</h3>  <h3 align="center">REASSURANCE: A TRIALOGUE.</h3>  
 <p><i>Cantor to God:</i></p>  
 <p>"What profits it to see Thy people wallow,<br>   A prostrate lily whelmed in floods of water?<br> She twitters like a caged and frightened swallow,<br>   When Thou art girt with weapons for her slaughter.<br> Be over her, O Rock, a shield erected,<br> And make Thy corner-stone of that rejected!"</p>  
 <p><i>Congregation:</i></p>  
 <p>"Before my foe I am humiliated,<br>   He sits in fatted ease while I must wander,<br> Before his flouts and roars and blows prostrated,<br>   Yet I endure and fix my vision yonder,<br> And wait for healing, with my crying stifled,<br> Like Hannahs, and a heart subdued and rifled."</p>  
 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 26</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Cantor to Congregation:</i></p>  
 <p>"What ails thee that soul-sick and bitter-hearted,<br>   Thou faintest, face and hands with teardrops streaming?<br> Sow charity, and kindness shall be carted,<br>   Who trusts in force is ignorantly dreaming.<br> Oppression passes, trampled by oppression,<br> And violence breeds violent succession."</p>  
 <p><i>Congregation to Cantor:</i></p>  
 <p>"My years have gone in sorrow and in sighing,<br>   I hoped for respite but instead comes wailing,<br> Before the balm arrives behold me dying."</p>  
 <p><i>Cantor to Congregation:</i></p>  
 <p>"Ah wait, faint heart, that sighest, sick and failing,<br> Thyself against Gods mercy do not harden,<br> Thou, eased of foes, shalt flower like a garden."</p>  
 <p><i>Congregation to God:</i></p>  
 <p>"Mine eyes are sick and faint from hopes depression,<br>   Dumb like a sheep I bear Thy storm of fury,<br> Perchance my pain shall cancel my transgression,<br>   Crush not the plagued and stricken son of Jewry,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p>The broken-hearted, crouching neath Thy rod,<br> He waits Thee, night and day, O jealous God.</p>  
 <p>Gripped like a bird within its captors fingers,<br> And crushed to dust, I groan beyond all bearing."</p>  
 <p><i>God:</i></p>  
 <p>"Hearken, afflicted one, for hope yet lingers,<br>   And look to Me, whose angel is preparing<br> My path, for though at night be tears and sadness<br> Yet in the morning come delight and gladness."</p>
