 <p><a name="page_6"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 6</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">3</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE MESSIAH.</h3>  
 <p>Lord, tell me when<br> Shall come to men<br> Messiah blest,<br> When shall Thy care<br> His couch prepare<br> To be my guest,<br> To sleep on my golden bed,<br>   in my palace rest.</p>  
 <p>Wake, dear gazelle,<br> Shake off thy spell,<br> Nor slumber still.<br> Dawn like a flag<br> Surmounts the crag<br> Of Tabors hill,<br> And its flame it unfurls oer my<br>   Hermon, the hoar and chill.</p>  
 <p>From the wild-ass brood<br> To the grace renewed<br> Of Thy dainty roe,<br> O Lord, return,<br> For behold we yearn<br> Our love to show,<br> And our soul with Thy soul at<br>   one as of yore to know.</p>  
 <p>Thrice welcome he<br> Who comes to me<br> Of Davids line,<br> My palace treasure<br> Is at his pleasure<br> With all thats mine,<br> My pomegranate, cinnamon, spice, and<br>   the jars of my old sweet wine.</p>
