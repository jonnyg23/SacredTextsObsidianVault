 <p><a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 20</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">17</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE REDEMPTION</h3>  
 <p>The despoiled and dispersed Thou shalt gather to Zion,<br>   Restoring the slaves who were sold without fee,<br> And the priests to their ritual robes, while the scion<br>   Of families ruling shall once more be free<br>   To carol, high God, his thanksgiving to Thee.</p>  
 <p>To the heathen a banner to raise Thou wilt hasten,<br>   Thou shalt strengthen and gird up the loins that we trust,<br> And the suppliants whom Thy dispersal did chasten<br>   Thou wilt raise as of yore from captivitys dust,<br>   The breastplate of righteousness clothing the just.</p>  
 <p>My impudent foe seeks my life-faith to sever,<br>   To my face he enquires how long yet wilt thou wait,<br> But I am afflicted, not cast off for ever,<br>   For my God is the help of the low in estate,<br>   Protecting the poor as He humbles the great.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 21</font></a></p>  
 <p>His heritage shall to the exile be given,<br>   And a strong hand the sick and the punished replace,<br> The abased and abandoned, by every fang riven,<br>   Shall their freshness renew by the patriarchs grace<br>   And the strangers be scorched like a tropical place.</p>
