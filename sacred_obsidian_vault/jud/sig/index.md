 <blockquote>
 The years are a thousand<br>
 Since, broken and scattered,<br>
 We wander in exile,<br>
 Like waterfowl lost in<br>
 The depths of the desert.--<a href="sig46.htm#page_71">p. 71</a>.
 </blockquote>
 <p>
 Solomon ibn Gabirol (b. 1021, d. ca. 1058) was a Jewish Neoplatonist
 philosopher and poet who lived in Spain during the Islamic period.
 His devotional poetry, featured here, is considered among the best
 post-canon, and portions of his poetic works have been incorporated into
 the Jewish liturgy.
 However, only two extensive works of his have survived, a collection of
 his poems, translated here, and a philosophical treatise, the <cite>Fountain
 of Life</cite>, which, ironically, was thought to be the work of a Christian
 until the mid-19th century.
 </p>
 <p>
 An <a href="../fons/index.htm">extended extract</a>
 from the <cite>Fountain of Life</cite>
 is also available at this site.
 </p>
 <p>
 Of some interest here is the extensive discussion of the structure
 of the cosmos in the extended poem 'The Royal Crown,'
 which includes a section which describes each of
 the celestial spheres in turn.
 He gives specific astronomical facts which were state of the art
 in the 11th century.
 He states that the outer planets, the sun and stars
 are much larger than the earth
 (although the specific numbers he gives are a bit too small!).
 <cite>The Royal Crown</cite> additionally provides a poetic abstract of the
 philosophical theories found in the <cite>Fountain of Life</cite>.
 Also apparent are references to Kabbalistic concepts,
 or perhaps Gabirol influenced later Kabbalists.
 But the focus of these poems is Gabirol's intense relationship with God,
 which pervades every aspect of his writing.
 Interwoven are themes of the anticipation of the Messiah,
 the sorrow of the diaspora, and the meaning of life.
 Indeed, at one point (# <a href="sig40.htm">37</a>) he laments
 that his only prayer is for God to explain "life's interpretation."
 </p>

 original book have been omitted from this etext.
 I have also supplied titles for some of the files in the
 <cite>Royal Crown</cite> sequence.
 </p>
