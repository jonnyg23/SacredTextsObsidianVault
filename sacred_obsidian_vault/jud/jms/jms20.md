 <p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 265</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">APPENDIX II</h3>  <h3 align="center">MS. SEFER GEMATRIAOT, pp. 43a-44b, ON GEMS</h3>  
 <p align="center">(Notes on pp. 267-268)</p>  
 <p>This passage closely parallels Baḥya b. Asher's comment on Ex. 28:17, both apparently being derived from a common source. They were composed at about the same time, so that one could hardly have drawn from the other; each lacks passages present in the other; the language of the two is very similar, but Baḥya's text is longer and more wordy, while the present text contains several additional details about the use of the gems; and there are some interesting differences which illustrate the different environments (Spain and Germany) in which they were composed.</p>

 <p>1. Cf. <i>Gen. R.</i> ch. 98, 7; Rashi on Gen. 35:22.</p>  
 <p>2. <i>Rubino</i>, ruby; Baḥya continues with a long description of this gem which is lacking here.</p>

 <p>6. Gen. 37:21.</p>  
 <p>7. Gen. 42:22.</p>  
 <p>8. Gen. 41:42.</p>  
 <p>9. Perhaps this is the prasinum, a species of smaragd (cf. Ducange, s. v.); Baḥya: ‏ונקרא פראש״מא והיא מין נופך שהוא מרק״די שהוא גם כן ירו׳ ככרתי‎.</p>  
 <p>10. Then this should read ‏סמרלדא‎, <i>smiraldus</i>, <i>smeraldus</i>, another species of smaragd. This word does not appear in Baḥya.</p>
