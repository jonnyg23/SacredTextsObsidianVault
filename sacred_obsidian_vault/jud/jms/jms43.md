 <p><a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 333</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GLOSSARY OF HEBREW TERMS</h3>  
 <p><i>Afikomen</i>a cake of unleavened bread eaten at the end of the Passover meal.</p>  
 <p><i>Aggada</i>non-legal portion of Talmudic literature.</p>  
 <p><i>Amora</i> (pl. <i>Amoraim</i>)post-Mishnaic authorities cited in the Talmud (c. 220-500 C.E.).</p>  
 <p><i>Dibbuk</i>spirit of deceased person which has entered body of living person.</p>  
 <p><i>Gaon</i> (pl. <i>Geonim</i>)heads of Babylonian and Palestinian academies after the sixth century.</p>  
 <p><i>Gehinnom</i>realm in which the wicked expiate their sins.</p>  
 <p><i>Gemara</i>portion of the Talmud containing Amoraic commentary on the Mishna.</p>  
 <p><i>Gilgul</i>transmigration of souls.</p>  
 <p><i>Habdalah</i>ritual marking the close of Sabbath and holidays.</p>  
 <p><i>Halacha</i>legal portion of Talmudic literature; legal tradition, generally.</p>  
 <p><i>Hazan</i>precentor.</p>  
 <p><i>Hashbaah</i>invocation.</p>  
 <p><i>Hoshana Rabbah</i>seventh day of the Feast of Tabernacles.</p>  
 <p><i>Jahrzeit</i>annual commemoration of death.</p>  
 <p><i>Kaddish</i>, more specifically, <i>kaddish yatom</i>prayer for mourners.</p>  
 <p><i>Kiddush</i>blessing over wine at inception of Sabbath and holidays.</p>  
 <p><i>Kosher</i>ritually pure.</p>  
 <p><i>Keri</i>seminal pollution.</p>  
 <p><i>Lag Bomer</i>thirty-third day in the counting of the <i>Omer;</i> 18th of Iyar.</p>  
 <p><i>Malach</i>angel.</p>  
 <p><i>Mazal</i>star or constellation.</p>  
 <p><i>Maẓẓah</i>unleavened bread.</p>  
 <p><i>Mazzik</i>demon.</p>  
 <p><i>Memuneh</i>"deputy" angel.</p>  
 <p><i>Mezuzah</i> (pl. <i>Mezuzot</i>)a Biblical inscription attached to the doorpost.</p>  
 <p><i>Midrash</i>commentary on the Bible, mainly of an Aggadic nature.</p>  
 <p><i>Minyan</i>minimum of ten men required to constitute a congregation.</p>  
 <p><i>Mishna</i>the "Oral Law" forming basis of the Talmud; edited c. 220 C.E. by R. Judah HaNassi.</p>  
 <p><i>Rosh Hashana</i>New Year.</p>  
 <p><i>Ruaḥ</i>spirit.</p>  
 <p><i>Sar</i>prince.</p>  
 <p><i>Seder</i>Passover meal and service.</p>  
 <p><i>Sefirot</i>the ten creative attributes of God, according to the <i>Kabbalah</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Shechinah</i>the divine Presence.</p>  
 <p><i>Shed</i>demon.</p>  
 <p><i>Shema</i>the verse beginning, "Hear, O Israel" (Deut. 6:4), and the prayer composed of Deut. 6:4-9, 11:13-21, Nu. 15:37-41.</p>  
 <p><i>Sukkot</i>Feast of Tabernacles.</p>  
 <p><i>Tanna</i> (pl. <i>Tannaim</i>)authorities cited in the Mishna and coëval writings.</p>  
 <p><i>Targum</i>Aramaic translation of the Bible.</p>  
 <p><i>Tefillin</i>phylacteries.</p>  
 <p><i>Tekufah</i>"turning of the sun," i.e., the solstices and equinoxes.</p>  
 <p><i>Torah</i>the Pentateuch.</p>  
 <p><i>Yom Kippur</i>Day of Atonement.</p>
