 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 273</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">CHAPTER II</h4>  <h4 align="center">THE TRUTH BEHIND THE LEGEND</h4>  
 <p><a name="an_2_1">1</a>. On Biblical magic see J. G. Frazer, <i>Folk-Lore in the Old Testament</i>, 3 vols., London 1918; T. W. Davies, <i>Magic, Divination and Demonology Among the Hebrews and Their Neighbors</i>, London 1898; B. Jacob, <i>Im Namen Gottes</i>, Berlin 1903; A. Jirku, <i>Materialien zur Volksreligion Israels</i>, Leipzig 1914; on Talmudic magic see L. Blau, <i>Das altjüdische Zauberwesen</i>, Budapest 1888; D. Joel, <i>Der Aberglaube und die Stellung des Judenthums zu demselben</i>, Breslau 1881-3 (Part I devotes some space to the Biblical period); G. Brecher, <i>Das Transcendentale, Magie und magische Heilarten im Talmud</i>, Vienna 1850. Very little has been written on the magic of the Geonic period. See Joel's book, Part II, and J. A. Montgomery, <i>Aramaic Incantation Texts from Nippur</i>, Phila. 1913; J. Wohlstein, <i>Dämonenbeschwörungen aus nachtalmudischer Zeit</i>, Berlin 1894; M. Gaster, <i>The Sword of Moses</i>, London 1886; Cyrus H. Gordon, "Aramaic Magical Bowls in the Istanbul and Baghdad Museums," <i>Archiv Orientální</i>, VI (Praha 1934), 319-34.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_2">2</a>. S. Ḥas. 43, 1136, 1137, 1444; <i>Ḥochmat HaNefesh</i>, 12d; <i>Hadar Zekenim</i> on Ex. 22:17; Moses Taku, <i>Oẓar Neḥmad</i>, III, 61; cf. Grimm, II, 546. Gaster, <i>Maaseh Book</i>, II, 576 ff., has a typical German folk-tale about a magic ring that could be used to transform a person into a werwolf.<i>Ẓiyuni</i>, 55a; <i>Maaseh Book</i>, II, 320 f.; <i>S. Ḥas. B</i>, 1166; <i>Ḥochmat HaNefesh</i>, 12d; cf. Grimm, II, 898; Wuttke, 55;S. Ḥas. 1453; <i>Ẓiyuni</i>, 26e; <i>Hadar Zekenim</i> on Ex. 8:12; <i>Daat Zekenim</i> on Ex. 8:14; <i>HaḤayim</i>, IV, 10. Cf. Lea, III, 510: "One precaution, held indispensable by some experienced practitioners, was that the witch on arrest was to be placed immediately in a basket and thus be carried to prison, without allowing her feet to touch the earth, for if she were permitted to do so she could slay her captors with lightning and escape"; cf. also Grimm, II, 899, III, 444, §30.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_3">3</a>. <i>S. Ḥas.</i> 1463, 1465, 1466; Güd. I, 203, n. 8, 204, n. 5, 205, n. 3, II, 222, n. 3; <i>Paaneaḥ Raza</i> on Ex. 22: 17-18, p. 69a; Hansen, 131; Lea, III, 405; <i>Ẓiyuni</i>, 7a, 49c; <i>Nishmat Ḥayim</i>, III, 23, 24.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_4">4</a>. <i>S. Ḥas.</i> 172, 1467; <i>Testament of Judah the Pious</i>, 5; <i>Rokeaḥ</i>, §316, p. 83a; <i>Toledot Adam veḤavah</i>, 28:1, p. 182b.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_5">5</a>. Blau, 18-19, 23 ff.; <i>HaḤayim</i>, IV, 10; <i>Nishmat Ḥayim</i>, III, 23.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_6">6</a>. Cf. <i>S. Ḥas.</i> 738, 1458, 1459, 1801, 1819, 1921; Raziel, 7b.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_7">7</a>. Cf. Scholem in <i>EJ</i>, IX, 717 ff.; <i>MGWJ</i>, LXIX (1925), p. 16.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_8">8</a>. Cf. A. Epstein, "Lekorot HaKabbalah HaAshkenazit," in <i>HaḤoker</i>, II (Vienna 1894), 1-11, 37-48; <i>JE</i>, III, 465 ff.; <i>EJ</i>, IX, 646 f.; Güd. I, 156 ff.; H. Gross, "Zwei kabbalistische Traditionsketten des R. Eleasar aus Worms," <i>MGWJ</i>, XLIX (1905), 692-700.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_9">9</a>. Cf. Kammelhar, 3, 4, 42, 43-5; <i>Maaseh Book</i>, II, 396 ff., 510 ff.; Scholem, <i>Kirjath Sepher</i>, IV (Jerusalem 1927), 317; Gaster, <i>Jewish Folk-Lore in the Middle</i> Ages, 9 ff.; N. Brüll ("Beiträge zur jüdischen Sagen- und Spruchkunde im Mittelalter," in <i>Jahrbücher</i>, IX [1889], 1-7,) printed many legends from a sixteenth-century manuscript about the wonders performed by Judah the Pious and his disciples.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_10">10</a>. Joel, I, 85; Blau, 20; <i>San.</i> 67b and Rashi; <i>Yore Deah</i> 179:15.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_11">11</a>. <i>Hadar Zekenim</i> on Ex. 7:12; S. Has, 211, 212, 1449, 1455; <i>Yereim</i>, 82; <i>Toledot Adam veḤavah</i>, 17: 5, p. 127b; cf. <i>Lebush</i> on <i>Yore Deah</i> 179:15.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_2_12">12</a>. Rashi on <i>San.</i> 65b; <i>Nishmat Ḥayim</i>, III, 20; <i>Lebush</i> on <i>Yore Deah</i>, 179:1.</p>
