 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">10-11. The Ba‘al ‘Ob and the Yidd‘oni.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>VII. 7b. The Ba‘al ‘Ob, <a name="fr_315"></a><a href="#fn_312"><font size="1">1</font></a> that is, the Python <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">2</font></a> who speaks from his armpits, and the Yidd‘oni <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_314"><font size="1">3</font></a> who speaks from his mouth, are punishable by stoning; and he who has inquired of them (offends) against an explicit warning. <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><b>T</b>. X. 6. The Ba‘al ‘Ob, that is the Python who speaks from between his joints and elbows, and the Yidd‘oni, who has the bone of a Yidd‘oni <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">5</font></a> in his mouth, are to be stoned; and he who inquires of them offends against an explicit warning.  
 <p>7. HE WHO INQUIRES OF THE DEAD; <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">6</font></a>---that is, one who conjures up the dead by witchcraft, or one who inquires of a skull. What is the difference between one who inquires of a skull and one who conjures up the dead by witchcraft? When one conjures up the dead by witchcraft it (the ghost) does not come up in the proper way, <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">7</font></a> nor will it come up on the Sabbath; but when one inquires of a skull it comes up in the ordinary way, and will also come up on the Sabbath.</p></dir>
