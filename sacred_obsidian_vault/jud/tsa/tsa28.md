 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Sanhedrin</i>, Herbert Danby tr. [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">16. He who leads a Town astray.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>VII. 10<i>b</i>. The one who leads a town astray <a name="fr_337"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">3</font></a> is he who says outright, "Let us go and worship idols."</p>  
 <p></p><dir><b>T</b>. XI. 5 <i>b</i>. R. Shimeon the son of Jehuda said in the name of R. Shimeon, "He who leads a town astray is to die by strangulation," <a name="fr_338"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">4</font></a></dir>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_333"></a><a href="tsa28.htm#fr_337">106:3</a> Deut. 13. 12 ff. Verse 16 orders that all those who are led astray are to he put to the sword; cf. <i>Mishnah</i>. X. 7. But since in the case of the <i>mesith</i>, "the beguiler" (Deut. 13. 6), who is expressly condemned to be stoned, the word <i>l’haddiḥ’ka</i>, "to lead thee astray," is used, stoning must apply to him also, on the principle of <i>gezera shawa;</i> <i>i.e.</i> the misleader is to be stoned, and the misled decapitated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_334"></a><a href="tsa28.htm#fr_338">106:4</a> R. Shimeon places such a man in the category of the false prophet, <i>Mishnah</i> XI. 5.</p>
