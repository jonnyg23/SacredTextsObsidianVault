 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Sanhedrin</i>, Herbert Danby tr. [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">(1) Who strikes his Father or Mother.</h3>  
 <p>He who strikes his father or mother <a name="fr_480"></a><a href="#fn_473"><font size="1">6</font></a> is not guilty till he bruise them. Cursing is more serious</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 137</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>than striking: for he who curses after death is guilty, while he who strikes after death is free from penalty.</p>
