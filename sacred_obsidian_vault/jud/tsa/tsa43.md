 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Sanhedrin</i>, Herbert Danby tr. [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">(4) The False Prophet.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>XI. 5. " The false prophet " <a name="fr_495"></a><a href="#fn_488"><font size="1">5</font></a> is he who prophesies what he has not heard, and what was not told</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 140</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">M.</font></span>him; <a name="fr_496"></a><a href="#fn_489"><font size="1">1</font></a> whereas he who suppresses his prophetic message, or regards as exaggerated the words of another prophet, or transgresses his own words, his death is left in the hands of Heaven, as it is written: I WILL REQUIRE IT OF HIM. <a name="fr_497"></a><a href="#fn_490"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><b>T</b>. XIV. 13. He who prophesies in order to abrogate anything of what is written in the Law, is guilty. R. Shimeon says: If he prophesy in such a way as partly to abrogate and partly to support, he is free from penalty. If he have prophesied in the name of an idol, even if he maintain it one day and withdraw it another, he is guilty.  
 <p>He who prophesies what he has not heard, like Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah <a name="fr_498"></a><a href="#fn_491"><font size="1">3</font></a>; or prophesies what has not been told him, like Hananiah the son of Azzur <a name="fr_499"></a><a href="#fn_492"><font size="1">4</font></a>--who heard what Jeremiah the prophet was prophesying in the upper street and went and prophesied otherwise in the lower street; or suppresses his prophetic message, like Jonah, the son of Amittai <a name="fr_500"></a><a href="#fn_493"><font size="1">5</font></a>; or regards a prophetic message as exaggerated, like the companion of Micah <a name="fr_501"></a><a href="#fn_494"><font size="1">6</font></a>; or transgresses his own words like Iddo <a name="fr_502"></a><a href="#fn_495"><font size="1">7</font></a>; or changes his prophecy; or a stranger who ministers in the Temple; or a priest who ministers before he is fully cleansed, <a name="fr_503"></a><a href="#fn_496"><font size="1">8</font></a> or before he has made the atonement offering after his cleansing, or who ministers without the proper garments, or with hands and feet unwashed, or with hair unkempt, or after having</p></dir>
