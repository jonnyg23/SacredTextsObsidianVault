 <p><a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 177</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BIBLIOGRAPHY</h1>  
 <p>WORKS in English are unfortunately very few. On the whole subject of the mystical elements in Talmudic, Midrashic, and Kabbalistic theology, the student should see:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>A. Franck, <i>La Kabbale</i> (Paris, 1843; 2nd ed., 1889). German Trans. (with many original additions) by A. Jellinek (Leipsic, 1844).<br> Ginsburg, <i>The Kabbalah</i> (London, 1865).<br> Isaac Myer, <i>Qabbalah</i> (Philadelphia, 1888).<br> Karppe, <i>Étude sur les Origines et la Nature du Zohar</i> (Paris, 1901).<br> Joël, 'Essays on Ibn Gabirol,' in his <i>Beiträge zur Geschichte der Philosophie</i>, 1876.</dir>  
 <p>All of the above works contain many translations of the original Hebrew and Aramaic.</p>  
 <p>On the subject of the Essenes:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Graetz, <i>History of the Jews</i> (English Trans., vol. ii. pp. 16-34).<br> Ginsburg, <i>The Essenes, their History and their Doctrines</i> (London, 1864).<br> Article 'Essenes' by F. C. Conybeare in Hastings' <i>Dictionary of the Bible</i>.<br> Article 'Essenes' in <i>Jewish Encyclopædia</i>.<br> Philo's <i>The Contemplative Life</i>, ed. Conybeare (Oxford, 1895).</dir>  
 <p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 178</font></a></p>  
 <p>On the Jewish Hellenistic Literature:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Schürer, <i>Geschichte des Jüdischen Volkes im Zeitalter Jesu Christi</i>, ii. pp. 556-584.<br> Freudenthal, <i>Hellenistische Studien</i>, 1879.<br> Gerald Friedlander, <i>Hellenism and Christianity</i> (London, 1912).<br> C. G. Montefiore, <i>The Wisdom of Solomon</i> (London, 1891).</dir>  
 <p>On Philo:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Drummond, <i>Philo Judæus</i> (London, 1888).</dir>  
 <p>On Shechinah, Memra, Holy Spirit:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Volz, <i>Der Geist Gottes</i> (Tübingen, 1910).<br> Abelson, <i>The Immanence of God in Rabbinical Literature</i> (London, 1912).</dir>  
 <p>On the <i>Yetsīrah</i> book:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Franck, <i>La Kabbale</i>, pp. 53-66, 102-118.<br> Graetz, <i>Gnosticismus und Judenthum</i> (Breslau, 1846), pp. 102-132.  
 <p></p><dir>Parts of it are translated into English in W. W. Westcott's <i>Sefer Yezirah</i> (London, 1893) and into French in Karppe's <i>Étude sur les Origines et la Nature du Zohar</i> (Paris, 1901).</dir></dir>  
 <p>On the doctrines of Emanation and the Ten Sefirot:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Joël, in the work previously mentioned. It contains the best account of the relation between Jewish and Neoplatonic mysticism.<br> Ehrenpreis, <i>Die Entwickelung der Emanationslehre in der Kabbalah des XIII. Jahrhunderts</i> (Frankfurt, 1895). This work is indispensable <a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 179</font></a> for the history of the development of ideas.<br> Eliphaz Lévi, Le Livre des Splendeurs (Paris, 1894).  
 <p>A translation of the whole of the <i>Zohar</i>, into French, by the late Jean de Pauly, has recently been published. It is absolutely indispensable as the only complete translation yet attempted.</p></dir>
