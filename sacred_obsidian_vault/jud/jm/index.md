 <p>
 Although the Kabbalah has lately become 'trendy,' there is a dearth
 of well-written, scholarly books which give a larger perspective on the
 subject of Jewish Mysticism.
 In addition, many of the books on the subject are by Occultists,
 and however valuable they are, tend to have their own agenda.
 Abelson puts the Kabbalah into context as the outgrowth of a long-term
 evolution of Jewish mystical thought, starting with the Essenes and
 the Merkabah (Chariot) mysticism of the Talmundic era.
 He explains how neo-Platonism, Gnosticism, Christianity and other
 currents influenced and were in turn impacted by Jewish mysticism.
 This is a great backgrounder if you are interested in the Kabbalah or
 Mysticism at any level.
 </p>
 <p align="RIGHT">--J. B. Hare</p>
