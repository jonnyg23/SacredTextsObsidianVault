 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">On One Benediction exempting Another.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>VI. 5. If a man has said the Benediction over the wine that comes before the meal he lets the wine that is after the meal go free. <a name="fr_298"></a><a href="#fn_297"><font size="1">3</font></a> If he has said the Benediction over the <i>hors d'œuvre</i> that is before the meal, he lets the <i>hors d’œuvre</i> that comes after the meal go free. If he has said the Benediction over the piece of bread he lets the <i>hors d’œuvre</i> go free; if over the <i>hors d’œuvres</i> <a name="fr_299"></a><a href="#fn_298"><font size="1">4</font></a> he does not let the piece of bread go free. The school of Shammai says: nor anything that is cooked in the pot. <a name="fr_300"></a><a href="#fn_299"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_295"></a><a href="tbr38.htm#fr_296">47:1</a> Cf. M. VI. 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_296"></a><a href="tbr38.htm#fr_297">47:2</a> <i>rice</i>. Not one of the "seven" kinds (see <a href="tbr40.htm#page_53">p. 53</a>), or the five seeds (see <a href="tbr38.htm#page_46">p. 46</a>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_297"></a><a href="tbr39.htm#fr_298">47:3</a> <i>lets . . . go free</i>. Literally "he exempts the wine that is after the meal," and so always in this phrase. The present regulation refers only to sabbaths and feast-days, on which a man intends when he says the Benediction over the wine before the meal to partake of wine also after the meal (Bartenora).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_298"></a><a href="tbr39.htm#fr_299">47:4</a> <i>the</i> hors d’œuvres<i>.</i> The plural suggests that there may be more than one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_299"></a><a href="tbr39.htm#fr_300">47:5</a> <i>nor anything that is cooked in the pot</i>. Such as groats.</p>
