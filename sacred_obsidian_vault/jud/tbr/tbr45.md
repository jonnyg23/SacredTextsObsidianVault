 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_388"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_389">63:1</a> B. adds: "In the case of a thousand besides the speaker, he says, Bless ye. In the case of a myriad one says, Let us bless the Loan our God, the God of Israel, the God of hosts, who dwelleth between the cherubim, over the meal that we have eaten."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_389"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_390">63:2</a> <i>According to the wording of his blessing</i>. C has, literally, "According to the subject which he says, blesses" (sic).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_390"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_391">63:3</a> Ps. 68<sup>26</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_391"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_392">63:4</a> <i>in the synagogue</i>. Where people are going and coming, so that their number is not known, in contrast to a private meal, where it is. Aqiba implies that the practice in the synagogue should guide that elsewhere. Cf. <a href="tbr51.htm#page_69">p. 69</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_392"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_393">63:5</a> The <i>Halaka</i> (Rule) follows R. Ishmael (Bartenora).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_393"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fr_394">63:6</a> <i>to separate</i>. Into two or more divisions, to say the Grace after the meal. For three is the lowest number for a religious company. Therefore four or five may not be divided. Cf. <a href="tbr40.htm#page_50">p. 50</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_394"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fr_395">64:1</a> <i>ten</i>. For ten also is a company. Therefore also the numbers from eleven to nineteen may not be divided.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_395"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fr_396">64:2</a> <i>from the invitation</i>. By having already said the Benediction for himself.</p>
