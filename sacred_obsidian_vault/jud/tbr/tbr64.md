 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_540"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_541">88:3</a> <i>the Name</i>. The following examples suggest that this was the Tetragrammaton. But if so the regulation must be very early.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_541"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_542">88:4</a> Ruth 2<sup>4</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_542"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_543">88:5</a> Judges 6<sup>12</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_543"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_544">88:6</a> Prov. 23<sup>22</sup>. For "thy mother" (’imka) C reads ‘imka "with thee," as immediately before, or, more probably, <i>‘amka</i> "thy people" <i>i.e.</i> thy elders, representing thy people. In any case this is the recognized explanation of "thy mother." Cf. Hos. ii. 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_544"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_545">88:7</a> Ps. 119<sup>126</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_545"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_546">88:8</a> R. Nathan's interpretation is that zeal in God's service causes infraction of part of His Law. Here the meaning appears to be that zealous greeting of one's companions leads to a use of the Holy Name which in ordinary cases is wrong. B reads "They break Thy law, because it is time to work for the LORD," <i>i.e.</i> to work for the LORD sometimes requires the infraction of the words of the Law. It seems only to express the meaning of C more definitely.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_546"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_547">89:1</a> <i>used to dry-nurse it them</i>. Literally, "used to cause them to swallow it"; <i>i.e.</i> they taught people truths without their being aware that they were being taught.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_547"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_548">89:2</a> Ruth 2<sup>4</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_548"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_549">89:3</a> Judges 6<sup>12</sup>. Again the central truth of the Law is implied.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_549"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_550">89:4</a> Hillel the Elder, see <a href="tbr25.htm#page_27">p. 27</a> note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_550"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_551">89:5</a> When men come in to listen, do thou teach.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_551"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_552">89:6</a> Prov. 11<sup>24</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_552"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_553">89:7</a> When none regard it, do thou learn from it.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_553"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_554">89:8</a> Ps. 119<sup>126</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_554"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_555">89:9</a> <i>or the LORD to work</i>. That is, in anger.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_555"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_556">89:10</a> <i>whom the commandments</i>. The ordinary text reads "whom seven commandments," and this seems necessary.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_556"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_557">90:1</a> Ps. 119<sup>164</sup>. Cf. the Yalqut on that verse, and on Ps. 6<sup>1</sup> (§ 633).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_557"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_558">90:2</a> Ps. 12<sup>1</sup>. Circumcision made an eighth commandment. Besides it was generally performed on the eighth day.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_558"></a><a href="tbr64.htm#fr_559">90:3</a> Ps. 347. Each commandment is such an angel.</p><p></p><hr>
