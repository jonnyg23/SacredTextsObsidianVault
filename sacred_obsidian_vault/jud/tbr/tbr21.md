 <p>9 (8). If a bridegroom wishes to recite the Shma‘ the first night he may recite it. Rabban Simeon ben Gamaliel <a name="fr_139"></a><a href="#fn_139"><font size="1">4</font></a> says: Not every one who wishes to make a name for himself, makes it. <a name="fr_140"></a><a href="#fn_140"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_139"></a><a href="tbr21.htm#fr_139">21:4</a> <i>Rabban Simeon ben Gamaliel</i>. Son of Gamaliel II., flourished c. 130-160 A.D., the father of R. Judah the Prince, who was the compiler of the mishna.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_140"></a><a href="tbr21.htm#fr_140">21:5</a> A warning against vanity and pride, which lead to hypocrisy. Not every one can safely imitate R. Gamaliel (see <i>M</i>. 6(5), <a href="tbr18.htm#page_19">p. 19</a>).</p>
