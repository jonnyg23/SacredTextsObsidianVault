 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">Prayer on a Ship or on a Raft.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>IV. 6. Should one be sitting in a ship, or on a raft, <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">3</font></a> let him direct his intention towards the most Holy Sanctuary.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_33"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 33</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">B.</font></span></p><dir><b>T</b>. III. 19. If a man wakes up early to go on a journey, behold, he takes a trumpet <a name="fr_201"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fn_200"><font size="1">1</font></a> and blows it, a Lulab <a name="fr_202"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fn_201"><font size="1">2</font></a> and shakes it, a roll of the Book of Esther <a name="fr_203"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fn_202"><font size="1">3</font></a> and reads in it, and prays (the Eighteen). Then when the time of reciting the Shma‘ arrives, behold, he recites it. <a name="fr_204"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fn_203"><font size="1">4</font></a> If he wakes up early to sit in a waggon or in a ship, behold, he prays (the Eighteen). Then when the time of reciting the Shma‘ arrives, behold, he recites it.</dir>
