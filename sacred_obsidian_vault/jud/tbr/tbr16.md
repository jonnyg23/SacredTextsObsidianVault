 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">The Sections of the Shma‘ and their Order.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>II. 2. This is what is meant by "between the sections," viz.: between the first Benediction and the second; between the second and the Shma‘; <a name="fr_101"></a><a href="#fn_101"><font size="1">3</font></a> between the Shma‘ and, "And it shall come to pass if ye hearken"; <a name="fr_102"></a><a href="#fn_102"><font size="1">4</font></a> between "And it shall come to pass if ye hearken" and "And the LORD said"; <a name="fr_103"></a><a href="#fn_103"><font size="1">5</font></a> between "And the LORD said" and "True and sure." <a name="fr_104"></a><a href="#fn_104"><font size="1">6</font></a> R. Judah says: One may not pause between "And the LORD said" and "True and sure." <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_105"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>II. 3 (2 cont.). R. Joshua ben Qorcha <a name="fr_106"></a><a href="tbr17.htm#fn_106"><font size="1">1</font></a> said Why does the Shma‘ precede "And it shall come to pass if ye hearken"? So that a man may accept the yoke of the kingdom of Heaven <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="tbr17.htm#fn_107"><font size="1">2</font></a> first, and afterwards accept the yoke of commandments. [Similarly] "And it shall come to pass, if ye hearken" precedes "And the LORD said," because "And it shall come to pass, if ye hearken," applies both by day and by night, but "And the LORD said" applies by day only. <a name="fr_108"></a><a href="tbr17.htm#fn_108"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
