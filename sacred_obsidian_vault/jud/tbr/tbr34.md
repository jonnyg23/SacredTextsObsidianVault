 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">The Mention of, and the Petition for, Rain, also the Habdalah, in the Prayer.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>V. 2. They make mention of <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_249"><font size="1">3</font></a> the powers of the M. rains <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_250"><font size="1">4</font></a> in the [Benediction of] the resurrection of the dead, <a name="fr_252"></a><a href="#fn_251"><font size="1">5</font></a> and they ask for rains in the Benediction of the years <a name="fr_253"></a><a href="#fn_252"><font size="1">6</font></a> and [they add] the <i>Habdalah</i> <a name="fr_254"></a><a href="#fn_253"><font size="1">7</font></a> in "Thou that graciously givest knowledge." <a name="fr_255"></a><a href="#fn_254"><font size="1">8</font></a> R. Aqiba used to say: One says it (the Habdalah) as a fourth Benediction by itself. R. Eliezer used to say: With the <i>Hoda’ah</i>. <a name="fr_256"></a><a href="#fn_255"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><b>T</b>. III. 9. If he has not made mention of the power of the rains in [the Benediction of] the Resurrection of the dead, and has not asked for the rains in the Benediction of the years, he must be turned back.  
 <p>If he has not said the Habdalah at "Thou that graciously givest knowledge," he says it over the.</p></dir>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_249"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_250">39:3</a> <i>make mention of</i>. In contrast to direct petition, see note 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_250"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_251">39:4</a> <i>the powers of the rains</i>. So literally, but the phrase connotes the manifestations of the Divine power in sending rain.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_251"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_252">39:5</a> <i>the resurrection of the dead</i>. The words "Thou causest the wind to blow and the rain to fall" are inserted in the second Benediction of the Eighteen during the winter months (SA, P. 44).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_252"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_253">39:6</a> <i>the years</i>. The petition for rain is used in the ninth Benediction from Dec. 4 to the first day of Passover (SA, p. 47).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_253"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_254">39:7</a> <i>the Habdalah</i>. A short Benediction at the conclusion of sabbath. See Staerk, p. 26; <i>cf</i>. SA, pp. 216 <i>sq</i>., <i>Jewish Encyclopedia</i>, vi. pp. 118-121.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_254"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_255">39:8</a> <i>knowledge</i>. The fourth Benediction (SA, p. 46).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_255"></a><a href="tbr34.htm#fr_256">39:9</a> <i>the Hoda’ah</i>, <a href="tbr32.htm#page_34">p. 34</a> note.</p>
