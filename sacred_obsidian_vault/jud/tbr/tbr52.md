 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 70</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">The Wording of the Benediction over the Lamp.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>VIII. 6 (5 <i>continued</i>). The School of Shammai say: "He who created the lamp <a name="fr_422"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">1</font></a> of fire." The School of Hillel say: "Creator of the lamps of fire." <a name="fr_423"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_421"></a><a href="tbr52.htm#fr_422">70:1</a> <i>the lamp</i>. The word so translated here and in the following clause is <i>ma’ôr</i> (Gen. 1<sup>16</sup>), in the preceding mishna, <i>nêr</i>. But in <i>T</i>. <i>ma’ôr</i> is used instead of <i>nêr</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_422"></a><a href="tbr52.htm#fr_423">70:2</a> The difference in the two forms of the Benediction appears to be that the School of Shammai regarded the fire as containing the one manifestation of the divine light, and the School of Hillel as containing various particles and colours; also that the former regarded all as included in the one past creation ("created"), the latter as the result of the LORD'S present work ("creating" or "creator").</p>
