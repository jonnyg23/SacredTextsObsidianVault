 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">The Words of the Invitation depend on the Number Present.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>VII. 4 (3). How is the invitation worded? <a name="fr_385"></a><a href="#fn_384"><font size="1">1</font></a> In the case of three persons they say: "Let us bless." <a name="fr_386"></a><a href="#fn_385"><font size="1">2</font></a> If there are three besides the speaker, he says: "Blessed be He." <a name="fr_387"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">3</font></a> If there are ten he says: "Let us bless our God." If there are ten besides the speaker he says: "Blessed be He." It is all the same whether there be ten or ten myriads. <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 (3 <i>cont</i>.). In the case of a hundred he says: "Let us bless the LORD our God." In the case of a hundred besides the speaker he says: "Blessed be He." In the case of a thousand they say: "Let</p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>us bless the LORD our God, the God of Israel, the God of hosts." <a name="fr_389"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fn_388"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>In the case of a myriad besides the speaker, he says: "Blessed be He." According to the wording of his blessing <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fn_389"><font size="1">2</font></a> so do they respond after him: "Blessed be the LORD our God, the God of Israel, the God of hosts, dwelling between the cherubim for the meal that we have eaten."</p>  
 <p>R. Jose, the Galilean, says: According to the number of the congregation do they frame the Benediction, for it is said, "By congregations bless ye God the LORD, from the fountain of Israel." <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fn_390"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>6 (3 <i>cont</i>.). R. Aqiba says: How do we find it in the synagogue? <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fn_391"><font size="1">4</font></a> It is all one whether there be many or few; they say, Bless ye the LORD." R. Ishmael says: "Bless ye the LORD who is blessed." <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="tbr45.htm#fn_392"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_384"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_385">62:1</a> <i>How is the invitation worded?</i> The formulæ are essentially the same as in SA, p. 279. See also Abrahams' notes.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_385"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_386">62:2</a> "<i>Let us bless</i>." Observe the gradation. Here no name is mentioned, for ten alone is a fully religious assembly; where there are ten, "our God"; where a hundred, "the LORD our God" where a thousand, "the LORD our God, the God of Israel, the God of hosts"; where a myriad, there is the addition of "dwelling between the cherubims."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_386"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_387">62:3</a> <i>Blessed be He</i>. B has "Bless ye" wherever "Blessed be He" comes in this and the next mishna (in C.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_387"></a><a href="tbr44.htm#fr_388">62:4</a> <i>myriads</i>. See the next mishna (in C.).</p>
