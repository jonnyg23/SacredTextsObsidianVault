 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_351"></a><a href="tbr40.htm#fr_352">57:1</a> <i>three Benedictions</i>. SA, pp. 280, 282. Because they all belong to the seven kinds, Deut. 8<sup>8, 10</sup>, wheat, barley, vine, fig, pomegranate, olive oil, honey.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_352"></a><a href="tbr40.htm#fr_353">57:2</a> <i>over them</i>. B has "after them," which expresses the fact more clearly. See Deut. 8<sup>10</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_353"></a><a href="tbr40.htm#fr_354">57:3</a> SA, p. 290.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_354"></a><a href="tbr40.htm#fr_355">57:4</a> <i>Ibid</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_355"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_356">57:5</a> <i>the afternoon</i> (<i>ha minchah</i>), <i>i.e.</i> about 3.30 p.m. <i>Vide supra</i>, <a href="tbr25.htm#page_28">p. 28</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_356"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_357">58:1</a> <i>and the holy day drew on</i>. Literally, "and the clay became holy upon them," <i>i.e.</i> the sabbath began, for darkness came on.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_357"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_358">58:2</a> <i>Let us leave off for the sabbath</i>. For this meal belongs to the weekday; let us honour the sabbath by beginning a fresh one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_358"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_359">58:3</a> Esther 7<sup>8</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_359"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_360">58:4</a> <i>the Rule in Israel</i>. Or perhaps, according to the Vienna MS., and T. J. <i>Pesachim</i> 37 <sup>b</sup>, "lest perchance a Rule be decided in Israel," one way or the other.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_360"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_361">58:5</a> <i>according to the words of R. Jose</i>. That it was not necessary to leave off.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_361"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_362">58:6</a> <i>to the Lecture Hall</i>. In order to be occupied with Torah when the sabbath begins.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_362"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_363">58:7</a> <i>the sanctification of the day</i>. This was originally said only in the house, and its use in the synagogue "arose from the custom of entertaining and lodging wayfarers in the Synagogue precincts, and thus the rite was part of the meal provided for the communal guests" (Abrahams on SA, p. 124). See also Elbogen (p. 111), who dates the observance in the synagogue from the earliest Gemara teachers in Babylon (c. 200 A.D.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_363"></a><a href="tbr41.htm#fr_364">58:8</a> <i>dark</i>. For not until then is it necessary to light the sabbath lamp, and to say the necessary prayer.</p>
