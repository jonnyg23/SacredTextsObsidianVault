 <p><a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 30</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">Parenthesis: A Rabbi's Prayers on Entering and Leaving His "School."</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>IV. 2. R. Nechuniah ben ha-Qanah <a name="fr_187"></a><a href="#fn_186"><font size="1">1</font></a> used to pray on his entering the School, <a name="fr_188"></a><a href="#fn_187"><font size="1">2</font></a> and on his going out, a short prayer. They said to him, What is the nature <a name="fr_189"></a><a href="#fn_188"><font size="1">3</font></a> of this prayer? He said to them: On my entering I pray that no offence [to others] happens through me, <a name="fr_190"></a><a href="#fn_189"><font size="1">4</font></a> and on my going out I give thanks for my lot. <a name="fr_191"></a><a href="#fn_190"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_186"></a><a href="tbr27.htm#fr_187">30:1</a> <i>R. Nechuniah ben ha-Qanah</i>. One of the first generation of mishna teachers, C. 90 A.D., the teacher of R. Ishmael (<a href="tbr12.htm#page_6">p. 6</a>). He is the reputed author of the Cabbalistic <i>Sepher ha Bahir</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_187"></a><a href="tbr27.htm#fr_188">30:2</a> <i>the School</i>. Literally, the Beth ha Midrash, the lecture room.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_188"></a><a href="tbr27.htm#fr_189">30:3</a> <i>nature</i>. So Bartenora, literally "place." Possibly it means only "occasion."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_189"></a><a href="tbr27.htm#fr_190">30:4</a> <i>through me</i>. By a wrong exposition, at which other scholars may laugh, glad at my mistake, and so sin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_190"></a><a href="tbr27.htm#fr_191">30:5</a> <i>my lot</i>. My calling as a frequenter of the School. Both the prayer and the thanksgiving are given in the Gemara, 28<sup>b</sup>.</p>
