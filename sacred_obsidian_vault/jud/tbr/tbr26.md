Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">II.—PRAYER, ESPECIALLY THE PRAYER <i>PAR EXCELLENCE</i> (THE "EIGHTEEN BENEDICTIONS").</h3>  <h4 align="center">The Three Times of Prayer.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>IV. 1. THE Morning Prayer [may be said] until midday. R. Judah says [only] till the fourth hour. <a name="fr_173"></a><a href="#fn_172"><font size="1">2</font></a> The Afternoon Prayer until the evening. <a name="fr_174"></a><a href="#fn_173"><font size="1">3</font></a> R. Judah says [only] till half the afternoon. <a name="fr_175"></a><a href="#fn_174"><font size="1">4</font></a> The Evening Prayer has no fixed law, and the Additional Prayers <a name="fr_176"></a><a href="#fn_175"><font size="1">5</font></a> may be said the whole day. <a name="fr_177"></a><a href="#fn_176"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>T</b>.</font></span></p><dir>III. 1. Just as a fixed law was given <a name="fr_178"></a><a href="#fn_177"><font size="1">7</font></a> for reciting the Shma‘, so did the Majority set a time for the Prayer. Why did they say: The Morning</dir>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>T</b>.</font></span></p><dir><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Prayer [may be said] until midday? Because the <i>T</i>. morning continual burnt-offering was offered until midday. <a name="fr_179"></a><a href="#fn_178"><font size="1">1</font></a> R. Judah says: Until the fourth hour. And why did they say: The Afternoon Prayer until the evening? Because the continual burnt-offering between the evenings <a name="fr_180"></a><a href="#fn_179"><font size="1">2</font></a> was offered until the evening. R. Judah says: Until the division of the afternoon? The eleventh hour <i>minus</i> a quarter. <a name="fr_181"></a><a href="#fn_180"><font size="1">3</font></a>  
 <p>2. The evening prayer has no fixed law. R. Eleazar bar Jose <a name="fr_182"></a><a href="#fn_181"><font size="1">4</font></a> says: [It ends] with the closing of the gates. <a name="fr_183"></a><a href="#fn_182"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. If a man has prayed the additional prayers either while the morning continual burnt-sacrifice is offered, or up to a time when the morning continual burnt-sacrifice is no longer offered—he has accomplished his duty. R. Aqiba says, if his prayer flows freely in his mouth, it is a good sign for him, but if not, it is a bad sign for him. He used to say: <a name="fr_184"></a><a href="#fn_183"><font size="1">6</font></a> Everyone in whom the mind of man finds pleasure, the mind of God <a name="fr_185"></a><a href="#fn_184"><font size="1">7</font></a> finds pleasure [also]; if the mind of man does not find pleasure in him the mind of God 7 does not find pleasure in him.</p>  
 <p>Everyone whose own mind finds pleasure in what he does <a name="fr_186"></a><a href="#fn_185"><font size="1">8</font></a>—it is a good sign for him; if his own mind does not find pleasure in what he does it is a bad sign for him.</p></dir>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_172"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_173">28:2</a> <i>the fourth hour</i>. Until the end of it, which is the third part of the day (Bartenora), say so a.m.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_173"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_174">28:3</a> <i>the evening</i>. i.e. sunset, say 6 p.m.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_174"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_175">28:4</a> <i>half the afternoon</i>. The afternoon (<i>Minchah</i>) lasts from the ninth hour and a half, say 3.30 p.m., for two and a half hours, so that the half of this would be about 4.45 p.m. (cf. Bartenora).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_175"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_176">28:5</a> <i>the Additional Prayers</i>. Special prayers for Sabbaths and Feast days (SA, pp. 559-573, 233-238). Special sacrifices are ordered in Num. 28<sup>29</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_176"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_177">28:6</a> <i>the whole day</i>. B adds, "R. Judah says [the Additional Prayers may be said] until the seventh hour," say 1 p.m.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_177"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_178">28:7</a> The Vienna MS. and the common text read, "Just as the Law appointed a fixed law," and this may be right.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_178"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_179">29:1</a> <i>was offered until midday.</i> "The one lamb thou shalt offer in the morning" (<i>ba bôqer</i>), clearly not later than midday (Ex. 29<sup>39</sup>)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_179"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_180">29:2</a> <i>between the evenings</i>. Exod. 29<sup>39</sup>, R. V. margin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_180"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_181">29:3</a> <i>the eleventh hour</i> minus <i>a quarter</i>. 4.45 p.m. see mishna, note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_181"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_182">29:4</a> <i>Eleazar bar Jose</i>. Eleazar, son of Jose the Galilean (<a href="tbr40.htm#page_55">p. 55</a>), a mishna teacher of the third generation, c. 130-160 A.D.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_182"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_183">29:5</a> <i>the closing of the gates</i>. Presumably the Temple gates were closed at dusk.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_183"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_184">29:6</a> See <i>Aboth</i> III. 53 (15), where however the saying is attributed to R. Hananiah ben Dosa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_184"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_185">29:7</a> <i>God</i>. Lit.: the Place, in Whom are all things, "for He is the Place of the world, and the world is not His place." See C. Taylor on Aboth. II. 18 (17); Lukyn Williams on Col. 1<sup>17</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_185"></a><a href="tbr26.htm#fr_186">29:8</a> <i>what he does</i>. Lit.: "what is his," but the reference seems to be not to his possessions, but to his occupations and actions.</p>
