 This is the only extensive translation of a portion of the Zohar,
 the longest and one of the most important texts of the Kabbalah,
 in the public domain.
 
 <p>
 The Zohar is a Kabbalistic commentary on the Hebrew Bible.
 Long before the 'Bible Code,' Jewish scholars were attempting
 to wring deep meaning out of every syllable of the text of the Hebrew Bible,
 using numerology, gematria, and other techniques.
 Dozens of pages in this book are devoted to analyses of
 the hidden meaning behind first few letters of Genesis!
 This might seem ultra-pedantic;
 however, the Zohar makes the Biblical text come to life, brimming
 with mystical significance.
 Seemingly unimportant details and turns of phrase, which you may have
 read long ago and forgotten,
 lead to immense vistas of a mysterious world inside the world.
 </p>
 <p>
 This is not a critical edition; written by a pseudonymous Theosophist,
 probably British, it is laced with out-of-place terminology such as
 'Karma' and 'Planes.'
 It nevertheless lets you wade in the rip current of one of the most magical
 of the world's sacred texts, and leaves you gasping for air and wanting more.
 </p>
