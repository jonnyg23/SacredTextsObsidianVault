 <p><a name="page_445"><font size="1" color="green">p. 445</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GRACE AFTER THE WEDDING FEAST</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>He who says Grace commences thus:—</i></p>  
 <p>Banish, O Lord, both grief and wrath, and then the dumb shall exult in song. Guide us in the paths of righteousness. Regard the blessing of the children of Jeshurun.</p>  
 <p>With the sanction of those present we will bless our God, in whose abode is joy, and of whose bounty we have partaken.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>The others respond:—</i></p>  
 <p>Blessed be our God in whose abode is joy, and of whose bounty we have partaken, and through whose goodness we live.</p>  
 <p><i>He who says Grace repeats the last sentence, and continues the Form of Service, pp. <a href="spb43.htm#page_425">425</a>–<a href="spb43.htm#page_431">431</a>. At the conclusion of the Grace the Seven Benedictions, <a href="spb46.htm#page_444">p. 444</a>, are said</i>.</p>
