 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Duties of the Heart</i>, by Rabbi Bachye, tr. by Edwin Collins, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Duties of the Heart are more Important than any others</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> obligation to fulfil the duties of the heart and mind is greater than any other, for, whether they</p>  
 <p><a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="green">p. 20</font></a></p>  
 <p>refer to the commands of Reason, or to those of Scripture or of Tradition, they are the foundations of all the precepts; and if there chance to be even the slightest failure in the ethics of the soul, there can be no proper fulfilment of any external ethical duty.</p>
