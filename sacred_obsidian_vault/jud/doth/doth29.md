 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Duties of the Heart</i>, by Rabbi Bachye, tr. by Edwin Collins, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="green">p. 35</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Humility and Egotism</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">He</span> who has true humility will be free from all pride, conceit, self-praise and self-glorification, even in his secret thoughts, when he is occupied in works of charity or other virtuous or righteous acts, whether commanded or not; and in his own soul he will account them as nothing in comparison to the greatness of his obligation to God.</p>
