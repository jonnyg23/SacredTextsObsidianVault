 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Duties of the Heart</i>, by Rabbi Bachye, tr. by Edwin Collins, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Signs and Consequences of True Humility</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Humility</span> before the Creator obliges a man to behave meekly and unselfishly in all his transactions with his fellow-men, whether in matters of business or in any other relation of life.</p>  
 <p>The truly humble man will mourn for all the mistakes made by other men, and not triumph or rejoice over them.</p>
