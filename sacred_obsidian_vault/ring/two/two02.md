 <span align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/front.jpg"><img src="tn/front.jpg" alt="GORICE XII. IN CARCË"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>GORICE XII. IN CARCË<br></span></p> 
 <p>TRUE Thomas lay on Huntlie bank,<br>   A ferlie he spied wi his ee;<br> And there he saw a Lady bright<br>   Come riding down by the Eildon Tree.</p> 
 <p>Her skirt was o the grass-green silk,<br>   Her mantle o the velvet fyne,<br> At ilka tett of her horse's mane<br>   Hung fifty siller bells and nine.</p> 
 <p>True Thomas he pulld aff his cap,<br>   And louted low down on his knee:<br> "Hail to thee, Mary, Queen of Heaven!<br>   For thy peer on earth could never be."</p> 
 <p>"O no, O no, Thomas," she says,<br>   "That name does not belong to me;<br> I'm but the Queen of fair Elfland,<br>   That am hither come to visit thee.</p> 
 <p>"Harp and carp, Thomas," she says,<br>   "Harp and carp alang wi me.<br> And if ye dare to kiss my lips,<br>   Sure of your bodie I will be."</p> 
 <p>"Betide me weal, betide me woe,<br>   That weird shall never daunton me."<br> Syne he has kissed her rosy lips,<br>   All underneath the Eildon Tree.</p> 
 <p>THOMAS THE RHYMER</p>
