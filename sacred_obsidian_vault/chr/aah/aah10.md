 <p><a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="green">p. 110</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px"><small>'Ah! then, as now--it may be, something more--<br> Woman and man were human to the core<br> .         .         .         .         .         .<br> They too could risk, they also could rebel,<br> They could love wisely--they could love too well.<br> In that great duel of Sex--that ancient strife<br> Which is the very central fact of life,<br> They could--and did--engage it breath for breath,<br> They could--and did--get wounded unto death.<br> As at all times since time for us began,<br> Woman was truly woman, man was man.<br> .         .         .         .         .         .<br> Dead--dead and done with! Swift from shine to shade<br> The roaring generations flit and fade.<br> To this one, fading, flitting, like the rest,<br> We come to proffer--be it worst or best--<br> A sketch, a shadow, of one brave old time;<br> A hint of what it might have held sublime;<br> A dream, an idyll, call it what you will,<br> Of man still Man, and woman--Woman still!'</small></div>  
 <p>       <i>From</i> W. E. HENLY'S <i>Prologue to</i> Beau Austin.</p>
