 <h1 align="center">CHAPTER VII<a name="page_12_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_12_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>.</h1>
 
 <p align="center">OF EFFUSED (CIRCUMAMBIENT) LIGHT.</p>
 
 <p>   W<small>HEN</small> the holy angels were created on the evening of the first day, without voice, they understood not their creation, but thought within themselves that they were self-existent beings and not made. On the morning of the first day God said in an audible and commanding voice, 'Let there be light<a name="page_12_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_12_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a>,' and immediately the effused light was created. When the angels saw the creation of light, they knew of a certainty that He who had made light had created them. And they shouted with a loud voice, and praised Him, and marvelled at His creation of light, as the blessed teacher<a name="page_12_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_12_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a> saith, 'When the Creator made that light, the angels marvelled thereat,' etc.; and as it is said in Job, 'When I created the morning star, all my angels praised me<a name="page_12_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_12_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a>.' Now by nature light has no warmth.</p>
