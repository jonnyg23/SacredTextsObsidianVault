 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius of Loyola</i>, [1914], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <a></a> <h3 align="center">OF THE SUPPER AT BETHANY</h3> Matthew, Chapter 26 [1-14].
 <p> <b>First Point.</b> First: The Lord sups in the house of Simon the Leper, along with Lazarus.
 </p><p> <b>Second Point.</b> Second: Mary pours the ointment on the head of Christ.
 </p><p> <b>Third Point.</b> Third: Judas murmurs, saying: "`For what is this waste of ointment?'" But He a second time excuses Magdalen, saying: "`Why are you troublesome to this woman? for she hath wrought a good work upon Me.'"
