 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Christmas Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE III.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Cosmas</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τῷ πρὸ τῶν αιώνων.</p>  
 <p>Him, of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father's</span> very Essence,<br>   Begotten, ere the world began,<br> And, in the latter time, of Mary,<br>   Without a human sire, made Man<br>        Unto Him, this glorious morn,<br>          Be the strain outpoured!<br>        Thou That liftest up our horn,<br>          Holy art Thou, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>!</p>  
 <p>The earthly Adam, erewhile quickened<br>   By the blest breath of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> on high,<br> Now made the victim of corruption,<br>   By woman's guile betray’d to die, <a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="green">p. 134</font></a><br>        He, deceived by woman's part,<br>          Supplication pour’d;<br>        Thou Who in my nature art,<br>          Holy art Thou, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>!</p>  
 <p>Thou, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesus Christ</span>, wast consubstantial<br>   With this our perishable clay,<br> And, by assuming earthly nature,<br>   Exaltedst it to heavenly day.<br>        Thou, That wast as mortal born,<br>          Being <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> ador’d,<br>        Thou That liftest up our horn,<br>          Holy art Thou, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>!</p>  
 <p>Rejoice, O Bethlehem, the city<br>   Whence Judah's monarchs had their birth;<br> Where He that sitteth on the Cherubs,<br>   The King of Israel, came on earth: <a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="green">p. 135</font></a><br>        Manifested this blest morn,<br>          As of old time never,<br>        He hath lifted up our horn,<br>          He shall reign for ever!</p>
