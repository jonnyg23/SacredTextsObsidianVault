 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Easter Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE I.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">αναστάσεως ἡμέρα.</p>  
 <p>’Tis the Day of Resurrection:<br>   Earth! tell it out abroad!<br> The Passover of gladness!<br>   The Passover of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>!<br> From Death to Life Eternal,<br>   From this world to the sky,<br> Our <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> hath brought us over,<br>   With hymns of victory.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="green">p. 96</font></a></p>  
 <p>Our hearts be pure from evil,<br>   That we may see aright<br> The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> in rays eternal<br>   Of Resurrection-Light:<br> And, listening to His accents,<br>   May hear, so calm and plain,<br> His own—<i>All Hail!</i>—and hearing,<br>   May raise the victor strain!</p>  
 <p>Now let the Heav’ns be joyful!<br>   Let earth her song begin!<br> Let the round world keep triumph,<br>   And all that is therein:<br> Invisible and visible<br>   Their notes let all things blend,—<br> For <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> hath risen,—<br>   Our joy that hath no end.</p>  
 <p>[<small>Set by Mr. Helmore: a very spirited melody. Also in Mr. Young's book: composed by Dr. Schroeder.</small>]</p>
