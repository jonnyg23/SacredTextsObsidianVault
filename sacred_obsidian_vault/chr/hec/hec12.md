 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">STICHERA FOR CHRISTMAS-TIDE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Anatolius.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">μέγα καὶ παράδοξον θαῦμα.</p>  
 <p>A great and mighty wonder!<br>   A full and holy cure!<br> The Virgin bears the Infant,<br>   With Virgin-honour pure!</p>  
 <p>The Word becomes Incarnate,<br>   ( <a name="fr_1"></a><a href="#fn_1"><font size="1">*</font></a>) And yet remains on high:<br> And Cherubim sing anthems<br>   To shepherds from the sky.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="green">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p>And we with them triumphant<br>   Repeat the hymn again:<br> "To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> on high be glory,<br>   And peace on earth to men!"</p>  
 <p>While thus they sing your Monarch,<br>   Those bright angelic bands,<br> Rejoice, ye vales and mountains!<br>   Ye oceans, clap your hands!</p>  
 <p>Since all He comes to ransom,<br>   By all be He adored,<br> The Infant born in Bethlehem,<br>   The Saviour and the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>!</p>  
 <p>And idol forms shall perish,<br>   And error shall decay,<br> And <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> shall wield His sceptre,<br>   Our <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> for aye.</p>  
 <p>[In Mr. Young's book. Melody of <i>Christus der ist mein Leben</i>. Harmony by M. Vulpius, 1609.]</p>
