 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Ascension Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE VI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">ῥανάτωσαν ἠμῖν ἄνωθεν,</p>  
 <p>R   ain down, ye heav'ns, eternal bliss!<br>       The Cherub-cloud to-day<br>     Bears <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesus</span> where His Father is,<br>       Along the starry way!</p>  
 <p>S   under’d of old were Heaven and Earth:<br>       But Thou, Incarnate King!<br>     Hast made them one by that Thy Birth,<br>       And this Thy triumphing.</p>  
 <p>'T   hy victor-raiment, wherefore red?<br>       What mean the marks of pain<br>     That print Thy form?'—the Angels said,<br>       The ascending Monarch's train.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="green">p. 219</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Catavasía</i>.</p>  
 <p>V   ery Oblation, by the scourges torn!<br>     Nailed to the bitter Cross, O Virgin-born!<br>     As once the Prophet from the monster's maw,<br>     So now Thy love, accomplishing the Law,<br>     Adam from utter death to perfect Life would draw.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Oicos</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">τὰ τῆς γῆς ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς.</p>  
 <p>V   anities earthly in earth will we lay,<br>     Ashes with ashes, the dust with the clay:<br>     Lift up the heart, and the eye, and the love,<br>     Lift up thyself, to the regions above:<br>     Since the Immortal hath entered of late,<br>     Mortals may pass at the heavenly gate.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="green">p. 220</font></a></p>  
 <p>    Stand we on Olivet: mark Him ascend,<br>     Whose is the glory and might without end;<br>     There, with His own ones, the Giver of Good<br>     Blessing them once more, a little while stood.<br>     "Nothing can part us,—nor distance, nor foes,<br>     Lo! I am for you, and who can oppose?"</p>
