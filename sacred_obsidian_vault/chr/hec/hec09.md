 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">STICHERA FOR A SUNDAY OF THE FIRST TONE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Anatolius.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">ζοφερᾶς τρικυμίας.</p>  
 <p>Fierce was the wild billow;<br>   Dark was the night;<br> Oars labour’d heavily;<br>   Foam glimmer’d white;<br> Trembled the mariners;<br>   Peril was nigh;<br> Then said the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>,<br>  —"Peace! It is I!"</p>  
 <p>Ridge of the mountain-way;<br>   Lower thy crest!<br> Wail of Euroclydon,<br>   Be thou at rest!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="green">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>Sorrow can never be,—<br>   Darkness must fly,—<br> Where saith the Light of Light,<br>  —"Peace! It is I!</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, Deliverer!<br>   Come Thou to me:<br> Soothe Thou my voyaging<br>   Over Life's sea!<br> Thou, when the storm of Death<br>   Roars, sweeping by,<br> Whisper, O Truth of Truth!<br>  —"Peace! It is I!"</p>  
 <p>[The above hymn has been set by my friend Mr. Helmore; also in H. E. C., of which it forms No. 1: also by Miss Kerr. The last melody is, to my mind, especially beautiful.]</p>
