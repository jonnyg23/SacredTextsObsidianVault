 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Apocreos</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE III.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by Theodore of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Ὁ Κύριος ἔρχεται.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> comes;—and who shall stand before His fear?<br> Who bide His Presence, when He draweth near?</p>  
 <p>        My soul, my soul, prepare<br>         To kneel before Him there!</p>  
 <p>Haste,—weep,—be reconciled to Him before<br> The fearful judgment knocketh at the door</p>  
 <p>        Where, in the Judge's eyes,<br>         All bare and naked lies.</p>  
 <p>Have mercy, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, have mercy, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, I cry,<br> When with Thine angels Thou appear’st on high:</p>  
 <p>        And each shall doom inherit,<br>         According to his merit.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="green">p. 177</font></a></p>  
 <p>How can I bear Thy fearful anger, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>?<br> I, that have so often transgressed Thy word?</p>  
 <p>        But put my sins away,<br>         And spare me in that day!</p>  
 <p>O miserable soul, return, lament,<br> Ere earthly converse end, and life be spent:</p>  
 <p>        Ere, time for sorrow o’er,<br>         The Bridegroom close the door!</p>  
 <p>Yea, I have sinned, as no man sinned beside:<br> With more than human guilt my soul is dyed:</p>  
 <p>        But spare, and save me here,<br>         Before that day appear!</p>  
 <p>Three Persons in One Essence uncreate,<br> On Whom, both Three and One, our praises wait,</p>  
 <p>        Give everlasting light,<br>         To them that sing Thy might!</p>
