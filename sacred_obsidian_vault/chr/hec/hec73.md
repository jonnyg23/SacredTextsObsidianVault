 <p><a name="page_234"><font size="1" color="green">p. 234</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">O UNITY OF THREEFOLD LIGHT.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by Metrophanes of Smyrna</font></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>From the Canon for Sunday of the Second Tone.</small></p>  
 <p align="center">τριφεγγὴς Μονὰς θεαρχική.</p>  
 <p>O Unity of Threefold Light,<br>   Send out Thy loveliest ray,<br> And scatter our transgressions' night,<br>   And turn it into day;<br> Make us those temples pure and fair,<br>   Thy glory loveth well,<br> The spotless tabernacles, where<br>   Thou may’st vouchsafe to dwell!</p>  
 <p>The glorious hosts of peerless might<br>   That ever see Thy Face, <a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="green">p. 235</font></a><br> Thou mak’st the mirrors of Thy Light,<br>   The vessels of Thy grace:<br> Thou, when their wond'rous strain they weave,<br>   Hast pleasure in the lay:<br> Deign thus our praises to receive,<br>   Albeit from lips of clay!</p>  
 <p>And yet Thyself they cannot know,<br>   Nor pierce the veil of light<br> That hides Thee from the Thrones below,<br>   As in profoundest night:<br> How then can mortal accents frame<br>   Due tribute to the King?<br> Thou, only, while we praise Thy Name,<br>   <i>Forgive</i> us as we sing!</p>  
 <p>Beyond Metrophanes, it will not be necessary to carry our translations. The following names may, however, be mentioned.</p>
