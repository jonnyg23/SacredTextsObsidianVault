 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Ascension Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE III.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">ἐπάρατε πυλάς.</p>  
 <p>"E   xalt, exalt, the Heavenly Gates,<br>        Ye chiefs of mighty name!<br>      The Lord and King of all things waits,<br>        Enrob’d in earthly frame."<br>      So to the higher seats they cry,<br>        The humbler legions of the sky.</p>  
 <p>F   or Adam’s sake, by Serpent-guile<br>       Distress’d, deceiv’d, o’erthrown,<br>     Thou left’st Thy native Home awhile,<br>       Thou left’st the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father’s</span> Throne:<br>     Now he is deck’d afresh with grace,<br>       Thou seek’st once more the Heavenly place.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="green">p. 213</font></a></p>  
 <p>G   lad festal keeps the earth to-day,<br>       Glad festal Heav’n is keeping:<br>     The Ascension-pomp, in bright array,<br>       Goes proudly sky-ward sweeping:<br>     The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> the mighty deed hath done,<br>       And join’d the severed into one.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Catavasía</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">ἔῥῤηξε γαστρός.</p>  
 <p>H   er fetters of the barren womb it rent,<br>     It crush’d the malice of the insolent,<br>     The cry of her—the prophetess, who brought<br>     A contrite spirit, and a humble thought<br>     To Him, Who bids His Throne by earnest prayer be sought.</p>
