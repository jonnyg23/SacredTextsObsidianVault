 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">"S. Thomas's Sunday"</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE V.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">ἐκ νυκτὸς ὀρθρίζοντες.</p>  
 <p>"Reconciliation's plan devising,<br>   "Fellow-sharer of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father's</span> Throne,<br> "Thee, O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span>, we, very early rising,<br>   "Tender lover of our spirits, own!"</p>  
 <p>When Thy Friends, with deep dismay confounded,<br>   Stood amaz’d, and knew not where to fly,<br> All the darkness that their souls surrounded<br>   Thou didst scatter with Thy drawing nigh.</p>  
 <p>Touch how awful, how consolatory!<br>   When, O Thomas, thou didst stretch thine hand, <a name="page_126"><font size="1" color="green">p. 126</font></a><br> And that Side, resplendent in its glory,<br>   Didst explore, because He gave command!</p>  
 <p>Unbelief of Thomas was the Mother<br>   Of Thy Church's most unshaken Creed:<br> Thou, O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Saviour</span>, wise above all other,<br> Had’st, before the world was, thus decreed.</p>  
 <p><small><i>Catavasia</i>. "Let us rise in early morning." (p. 100.)</small></p>
