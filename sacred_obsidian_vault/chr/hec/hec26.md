 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Easter Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE VI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">κατῆλθες ἐν τοῖς κατωτάτοις.</p>  
 <p>Into the dim earth's lowest parts descending,<br>   And bursting by Thy might the infernal chain<br> That bound the prisoners, Thou, at three days’ ending,<br>   As Jonah from the whale, hast risen again.</p>  
 <p>Thou brakest not the seal, Thy surety's token,<br>   Arising from the Tomb, Who left’st in Birth<br> The portals of Virginity unbroken,<br>   Opening the gates of heaven to sons of earth.</p>  
 <p>Thou, Sacrifice ineffable and living,<br>   Didst to the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father</span> by Thyself atone<br> As <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> eternal: resurrection giving<br>   To Adam, general parent, by Thine own.</p>
