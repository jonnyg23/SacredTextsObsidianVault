 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="green">p. 206</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">STARS OF THE MORNING.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p><small>A Cento from the Canon of the "Bodiless Ones;" Tuesday in the Week of the Fourth Tone.</small></p>  
 <p>Stars of the morning, so gloriously bright,<br> Fill’d with celestial resplendence and light;<br> These that, where night never followeth day,<br> Raise the Trishagion ever and aye:</p>  
 <p>These are Thy counsellors: these dost Thou own,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of Sabaoth! the nearest Thy throne;<br> These are Thy ministers; these dost Thou send,<br> Help of the helpless ones! man to defend.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="green">p. 207</font></a></p>  
 <p>These keep the guard, amidst Salem's dear bowers:<br> Thrones, Principalities, Virtues, and Powers:<br> Where with the Living Ones, mystical Four,<br> Cherubin, Seraphin, bow and adore.</p>  
 <p>"Who like the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>? "—thunders Michael, the Chief:<br> Raphael, "the Cure of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>," comforteth grief:<br> And, as at Nazareth, prophet of peace,<br> Gabriel, "the Light of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>," bringeth release.</p>  
 <p>Then, when the earth was first pois’d in mid-space,<br> Then, when the planets first sped on their race,—<br> Then, when were ended the six days' employ,—<br> Then all the sons of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> shouted for joy.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="green">p. 208</font></a></p>  
 <p>Still let them succour us; still let them fight,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> of angelic hosts, battling for right!<br> Till, where their anthems they ceaselessly pour,<br> We with the Angels may bow and adore!</p>  
 <p align="center">[No. 6 in H.E.C]</p>
