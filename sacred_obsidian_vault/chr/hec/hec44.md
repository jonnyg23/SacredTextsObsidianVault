 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Christmas Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Cosmas</font></p>  
 <p align="center">μυστήριον ξένον.</p>  
 <p>O wond’rous mystery, full of passing grace!<br>   The grot becometh Heav’n: the Virgin's breast<br> The bright Cherubic Throne: the stall that place,<br>   Where He, Who fills all space, vouchsafes to rest:<br>   <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> our <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, to Whom we raise<br>   Hymns of thankfulness and praise!</p>  
 <p>The course propitious of the unknown Star<br>   The Wise-men follow’d on its heavenly way,—<br> Until it led them, beckoning from afar,<br>   To where the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span>, the King of all things, lay:<br>   Him in Bethlehem they find,<br>   Born the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Saviour</span> of mankind.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="green">p. 149</font></a></p>  
 <p>"Where is the Child," they ask, "the new-born King,<br>   Whose herald-light is glittering in the sky,—<br> To Whom our offerings and our praise we bring?"<br>   And Herod's heart is troubled utterly.<br>   Armed for war with <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, in vain<br>   Would he see that Infant slain.</p>
