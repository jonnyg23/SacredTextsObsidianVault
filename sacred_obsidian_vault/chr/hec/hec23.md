 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Easter Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE III.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Δεῦτε πόμα πίωμεν.</p>  
 <p>Come, and let us drink of that New River,<br>   Not from barren Rock divinely poured,<br> But the Fount of Life that is for ever<br>   From the Sepulchre of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>.</p>  
 <p>All the world hath bright illumination,—<br>   Heav’n and Earth and things beneath the earth:<br> ’Tis the Festival of all Creation:<br>   <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> hath ris’n, Who gave Creation birth:</p>  
 <p>Yesterday with Thee in burial lying,<br>   Now to-day with Thee aris’n I rise;<br> Yesterday the partner of Thy dying,<br>   With Thyself upraise me to the skies.</p>  
 <p>[<small>In Mr. Young's book: composed by Dr. Schroeder.</small>]</p>
