 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Apocreos</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by Theodore of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Ὁ Κύριος ἔρχεται.</p>  
 <p>The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> draws nigh, the righteous Throne's Assessor,<br> The just to save, to punish the transgressor:<br>         Weep we, and mourn, and pray,<br>         Regardful of that day;<br> When all the secrets of all hearts shall be<br> Lit with the blaze of full eternity.</p>  
 <p>Clouds and thick darkness o’er the Mount assembling,<br> Moses beheld the Eternal's glory, trembling:<br>         And yet he might but see<br>         <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God's</span> feebler Majesty. <a name="page_182"><font size="1" color="green">p. 182</font></a><br> And I—I needs must view His fullest Face:<br> O spare me, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>! O take me to Thy grace!</p>  
 <p>David of old beheld, in speechless terror,<br> The session of the Judge—the doom of error:<br>         And what have I to plead<br>         For mercy in my need?<br> Nothing save this: O grant me yet to be,<br> Ere that day come, renewed and true to Thee!</p>  
 <p>Here, fires of deep damnation roar and glitter:<br> The worm is deathless, and the cup is bitter:<br>         There, day that hath no morrow,<br>         And joy that hath no sorrow:<br> And who so blest that he shall fly the abyss,<br> Rais’d up to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>'S Right Hand, and speechless bliss!</p>  
 <p>My soul with many an act of sin is wounded:<br> With mortal weakness is my frame surrounded: <a name="page_183"><font size="1" color="green">p. 183</font></a><br>         My life is well nigh o’er:<br>         The Judge is at the door<br> How wilt thou, miserable spirit, fare,<br> What time He sends His summons through the air?</p>
