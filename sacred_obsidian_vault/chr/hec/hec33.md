 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">"S. Thomas's Sunday"</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE III.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">στερέωσόν με, Χριστέ.</p>  
 <p>"On the rock of Thy commandments<br>   "Fix me firmly, lest I slide:<br> "With the glory of Thy Presence<br>   "Cover me on every side;<br> "Seeing none save Thee is holy,<br>   "<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, for ever glorified!"</p>  
 <p>New immortal out of mortal,<br>   New existence out of old:<br> This the Cross of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> accomplished,<br>   This the Prophets had foretold:<br> So that we thus newly quickened,<br>   Might attain the heavenly fold.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_122"><font size="1" color="green">p. 122</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thou Who comprehendest all things,<br>   Comprehended by the tomb,<br> Gav’st Thy body to the graveclothes<br>   And the silence and the gloom:<br> Till through fast-closed doors Thou camest<br>   Thy Disciples to illume.</p>  
 <p>Every nail-print, every buffet,<br>   Thou didst freely undergo,<br> As Thy Resurrection's witness<br>   To the Twelve Thou cam’st to show:<br> So that what <i>they</i> saw in vision,<br>   Future years by faith might know.</p>  
 <p><small><i>Catavasia</i>. "Come, and let us drink of that New River." (p. 97)</small></p>  
 <p>[<small>No. 3 in Mr. Sedding's book.</small>]</p>
