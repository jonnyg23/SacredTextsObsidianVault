 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Apocreos</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by Theodore of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">ἐφέστηκεν ἡ ἡμέρα.</p>  
 <p>The Day is near, the Judgment is at hand,<br> Awake, my soul, awake, and ready stand!<br> Where chiefs shall go with them that filled the throne,<br> Where rich and poor the same tribunal own;</p>  
 <p>        And every thought and deed<br>         Shall find its righteous meed.</p>  
 <p>There with the sheep the shepherd of the fold<br> Shall stand together; there the young and old;<br> Master and slave one doom shall undergo;<br> Widow and maiden one tribunal know.</p>  
 <p>        Oh woe, oh woe, to them<br>         Whom lawless lives condemn!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="green">p. 179</font></a></p>  
 <p>That Judgment-seat, impartial in decree,<br> Accepts no bribe, admits no subtilty:<br> No orator persuasion may exert,<br> No perjured witness wrong to right convert:</p>  
 <p>        But all things, hid in night,<br>         Shall then be dragged to light.</p>  
 <p>Let me not enter in the land of woe,<br> Let me not realms of outer darkness know!<br> Nor from the wedding-feast reject Thou me,<br> For my soiled vest of immortality;</p>  
 <p>        Bound hand and foot, and cast<br>         In anguish that shall last!</p>  
 <p>When Thou, the nations ranged on either side,<br> The righteous from the sinners shalt divide,<br> Then give me to be found amongst Thy sheep,<br> Then from the goats Thy trembling servant keep:</p>  
 <p>        That I may hear the voice<br>         That bids Thy Saints rejoice!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="green">p. 180</font></a></p>  
 <p>When righteous inquisition shall be made,<br> And the books opened, and the thrones arrayed,<br> My soul, what plea to shield thee canst thou know,<br> Who hast no fruit of righteousness to show,</p>  
 <p>        No holy deeds to bring<br>         To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> and King?</p>  
 <p>I hear the rich man's wail and bitter cry,<br> Out of the torments of eternity;<br> I know, beholding that devouring flame,<br> My guilt and condemnation are the same;</p>  
 <p>        And spare me, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, I say,<br>         In the great Judgment Day!</p>  
 <p>The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Word</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit</span>, with the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father</span> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">One</span>,<br> One Light and emanation of One Sun,<br> The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Word</span> by generation, we adore,<br> The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit</span> by procession, evermore;</p>  
 <p>        And with creation raise<br>         The thankful hymn of praise.</p>
