 <p><a name="page_242"><font size="1" color="green">p. 242</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">IDIOMELA IN THE WEEK OF THE FIRST OBLIQUE TONE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Stephen the Sabaite</font></p>  
 <p align="center"><small>These Stanzas, which strike me as very sweet, are not in all the editions of the Octoechus.</small></p>  
 <p align="center">κόπον τε καὶ κάματον.</p>  
 <p>Art thou weary, art thou languid,<br>   Art thou sore distrest?<br> "Come to me "—saith One—"and coming,<br>                    Be at rest!"</p>  
 <p>Hath He marks to lead me to Him,<br>   If He be my Guide?<br> "In Hip Feet and Hands are Wound-prints,<br>                    And His Side."</p>  
 <p><a name="page_243"><font size="1" color="green">p. 243</font></a></p>  
 <p>Is there Diadem, as Monarch,<br>   That His Brow adorns?<br> "Yea, a Crown, in very surety,<br>                    But of Thorns!"</p>  
 <p>If I find Him, if I follow,<br>   What His guerdon here?<br> "Many a sorrow, many a labour,<br>                    Many a tear."</p>  
 <p>If I still hold closely to Him,<br>   What hath He at last?<br> "Sorrow vanquish’d, labour ended,<br>                    Jordan past!"</p>  
 <p>If I ask Him to receive me,<br>   Will He say me nay?<br> "Not till earth, and not till Heaven<br>                    Pass away!"</p>  
 <p><a name="page_244"><font size="1" color="green">p. 244</font></a></p>  
 <p>Finding, following, keeping, struggling,<br>   Is He sure to bless?<br> "Angels, Martyrs, Prophets, Virgins,<br>                    Answer, Yes!"</p>  
 <p>[<small>No. 4. in Mr. Sedding's book: also No. 4 in H. E. C. Both very sweet melodies;—but that in H. E. C., which gives a different version of the 4th line throughout, is, to my mind, singularly touching.</small>]</p>
