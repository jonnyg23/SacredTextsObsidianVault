 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Easter Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE VII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Ὁ παῖδας ἐκ καμίνου.</p>  
 <p>Who from the fiery furnace saved the Three,<br> Suffers as mortal; that, His Passion o’er,<br> This mortal, triumphing o’er death, might be<br> Vested with immortality once more:<br>     He Whom our fathers still confest<br>     <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> over all, for ever blest.</p>  
 <p>The women with their ointment seek the Tomb:<br> And Whom they mourned as dead, with many a tear,<br> They worship now, joy dawning on their gloom,<br> As Living <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, as mystic Passover;<br>     Then to the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord's</span> Disciples gave<br>     The tidings of the vanquished grave.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="green">p. 104</font></a></p>  
 <p>We keep the festal of the death of death;<br> Of hell o’erthrown: the first-fruits pure and bright,<br> Of life eternal; and with joyous breath<br> Praise Him that won the victory by His might:<br>     Him Whom our fathers still confest<br>     <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> over all, for ever blest.</p>  
 <p>All hallowed festival, in splendour born!<br> Night of salvation and of glory! Night<br> Fore-heralding the Resurrection morn!<br> When from the tomb the everlasting Light,<br>     A glorious frame once more his own,<br>     Upon the world in splendour shone.</p>
