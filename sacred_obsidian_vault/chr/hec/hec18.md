 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Hymns of the Eastern Church</i>, by J.M. Neale, [1884], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">STICHERA FOR THE SECOND WEEK OF THE GREAT FAST.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Andrew of Crete.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">οὐ γὰρ βλὲπεις τοὺς ταράττοντας.</p>  
 <p>Christian! dost thou <i>see</i> them<br>   On the holy ground,<br> How the troops of Midian<br>   Prowl and prowl around? <a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="green">p. 83</font></a><br> Christian! up and smite them,<br>   Counting gain but loss:<br> Smite them by the merit<br>   Of the Holy Cross!</p>  
 <p>Christian! dost thou <i>feel</i> them,<br>   How they work within,<br> Striving, tempting, luring,<br>   Goading into sin?<br> Christian! never tremble!<br>   Never be down-cast!<br> Smite them by the virtue<br>   Of the Lenten Fast!</p>  
 <p>Christian! dost thou <i>hear</i> them,<br>   How they speak thee fair?<br> "Always fast and vigil?<br>   Always watch and prayer?"<br> Christian! say but boldly:<br>   "While I breathe, I pray:" <a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="green">p. 84</font></a><br> Peace shall follow battle,<br>   Night shall end in day.</p>  
 <p>"Well I know thy trouble,<br>   O my servant true;<br> Thou art very weary,—<br>   I was weary too:<br> But that toil shall make thee,<br>   Some day, all Mine own:<br> But the end of sorrow<br>   Shall be near My Throne."</p>  
 <p>[In H. E. C., where it is No. 2. Also, as <i>Fortitude, a Sacred Song</i>, by M. E. H. S. This is, of course, not intended to be used in Church; but, as a song, it is extremely pretty.]</p>
