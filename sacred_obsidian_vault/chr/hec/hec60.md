 <p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="green">p. 201</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LET OUR CHOIR NEW ANTHEMS RAISE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small>A Cento from the Canon for SS. Timothy and Maura; May 3rd.</small></p>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τῶν ἱερῶν ἀθλοφόρων.</p>  
 <p>Let our Choir new anthems raise:<br>   Wake the morn with gladness:<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> Himself to joy and praise<br>   Turns the Martyrs’ sadness:<br> This the day that won their crown,<br>   Opened Heav’n's bright portal;<br> As they laid the mortal down.<br>   And put on th’ immortal.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="green">p. 202</font></a></p>  
 <p>Never flinched they from the flame,<br>   From the torture, never;<br> Vain the foeman's sharpest aim,<br>   Satan's best endeavour:<br> For by faith they saw the Land<br>   Decked in all its glory,<br> Where triumphant now they stand<br>   With the victor's story.</p>  
 <p>Faith they had that knew not shame,<br>   Love that could not languish;<br> And eternal Hope o’ercame<br>   Momentary anguish.<br> He Who trod the self-same road,<br>   Death and Hell defeated;<br> Wherefore these their passions show'd<br>   Calvary repeated.</p>  
 <p>Up and follow, Christian men!<br>   Press through toil and sorrow! <a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="green">p. 203</font></a><br> Spurn the night of fear, and then,—<br>   Oh the glorious morrow!<br> Who will venture on the strife?<br>   Who will first begin it?<br> Who will seize the Land of Life?<br>   Warriors, up and win it?</p>
