 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">"S. Thomas's Sunday"</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">μέγα τὸ μυστήριον.</p>  
 <p>"'<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span>, we turn our eyes to Thee,<br> "'And this mighty mystery!'<br> "Habakkuk exclaimed of old,<br> "In the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Holy Spirit</span> bold:<br> "'Thou shalt come in time appointed,<br> "For the help of Thine anointed!"</p>  
 <p>Taste of myrrh He deign’d to know,<br> Who redeemed the source of woe:<br> Now He bids all sickness cease<br> Through the honey-comb of peace:<br> And to this world deigns to give<br> That sweet food by which we live.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_124"><font size="1" color="green">p. 124</font></a></p>  
 <p>Patient <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>! with loving eye<br> Thou invitest Thomas nigh,<br> Showing Him that Wounded Side:<br> While the world is certified,<br> Flow the third day, from the grave,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesus Christ</span> arose to save.</p>  
 <p>Blest, O Didymus, the tongue<br> Where that first confession hung:<br> First the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Saviour</span> to proclaim<br> First the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> of Life to name:<br> Such the graces it supplied,<br> —That dear touch of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu's</span> side!</p>  
 <p><small><i>Catavasia</i>. "Stand on thy watch-tower, Habakkuk the Seer." (p. 98.)</small></p>
