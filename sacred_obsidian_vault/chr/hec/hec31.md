 <p><a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="green">p. 115</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">IDIOMELA FOR ALL SAINTS.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τὰς ἑδρὰς τὰς αἰωνίας.</p>  
 <p>Those eternal bowers<br>   Man hath never trod,<br> Those unfading flowers<br>   Round the Throne of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>:<br> Who may hope to gain them<br>   After weary fight?<br> Who at length attain them<br>   Clad in robes of white?</p>  
 <p>He, who gladly barters<br>   All on earthly ground;<br> He who, like the Martyrs,<br>   Says, 'I <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">will</span> be crown’d:' <a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="green">p. 116</font></a><br> He, whose one oblation<br>   Is a life of love;<br> Clinging to the nation<br>   Of the Blest above.</p>  
 <p>Shame upon you, legions<br>   Of the Heavenly King,<br> Denizens of regions<br>   Past imagining!<br> What! with pipe and tabor<br>   Fool away the light,<br> When He bids you labour,<br>   When He tells you,—'Fight!'</p>  
 <p>While I do my duty,<br>   Struggling through the tide,<br> Whisper Thou of beauty<br>   On the other side! <a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="green">p. 117</font></a><br> Tell who will the story<br>   Of our <i>now</i> distress:<br> Oh the future glory!<br>   Oh the loveliness!</p>  
 <p>[<small>No. 3 in H. E. C. A very sweet melody.</small>]</p>
