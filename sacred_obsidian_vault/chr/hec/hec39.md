 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Christmas Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Cosmas</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Ῥάβδος ἐκ τῆς πίζης.</p>  
 <p>Rod of the Root of Jesse,<br>   Thou, Flower of Mary born,<br> From that thick shady mountain <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">*</font></a><br>   Cam’st glorious forth this morn:<br> Of her, the ever Virgin,<br>   Incarnate east Thou made,<br> The immaterial Essence,<br>   The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> by all obeyed!<br>       Glory, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, Thy servants pay<br>       To Thy wondrous might to-day!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="green">p. 137</font></a></p>  
 <p>The Gentiles’ expectation,<br>   Whom Jacob's words foretell,<br> Who Syria's pride shalt vanquish,<br>   Samaria's pride shalt quell;<br> Thou from the Root of Judah<br>   Like some fair plant dost spring,<br> To turn old Gentile error<br>   To Thee, its <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> and King!<br>       Glory, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, Thy servants pay<br>       To Thy wondrous might to-day!</p>  
 <p>In Balaam's ancient vision<br>   The Eastern seers were skilled,<br> They marked the constellations,<br>   And joy their spirits filled:<br> For Thou, bright Star of Jacob,<br>   Arising in Thy might,<br> Didst call these Gentiles first-fruits<br>   To worship in Thy light. <a name="page_138"><font size="1" color="green">p. 138</font></a><br>       They in holy reverence bent,<br>       Gifts acceptable present.</p>  
 <p>As on a fleece descending<br>   The gentle dews distil,<br> As drops the earth that water,<br>   The Virgin didst Thou fill.<br> For Media, leagued with Sheba,<br>   Falls down and worships Thee:<br> Tarsihish and Ethiopia,<br>   The Isles and Araby.<br>       Glory, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, Thy servants pay<br>       To Thy wondrous might to-day</p>  
 <p>[<small>In Mr. Young's book. The melody by Dr. Schroeder.</small>]</p>
