 <p><a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="green">p. 130</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CANON FOR CHRISTMAS DAY.</h3>  
 <p>This is perhaps the finest, on the whole, of the Canons of Cosmas; and may fairly be preferred to the rival composition of S. John Damascene.</p>  <h3 align="center">ODE I.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Cosmas</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Χριστὸς γεννᾶται· δοξάσατε</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> is born! Tell forth His fame!<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> from Heaven! His love proclaim!<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> on earth! Exalt His Name!<br> Sing to the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, O world, with exultation!<br> Break forth in glad thanksgiving, every nation!<br>                 For He hath triumphed gloriously!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="green">p. 131</font></a></p>  
 <p>Man, in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God's</span> own Image made,<br> Man, by Satan's wiles betrayed,<br> Man, on whom corruption preyed,<br> Shut out from hope of life and of salvation,<br> To-day <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> maketh him a new creation,<br>                 For He hath triumphed gloriously!</p>  
 <p>For the Maker, when His foe,<br> Wrought the creature death and woe,<br> Bowed the Heav’ns, and came below, <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="#fn_3"><font size="1">*</font></a><br> And in the Virgin's womb His dwelling making,<br> Became true man, man's very nature taking;<br>                 For He hath triumphed gloriously!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>He, the Wisdom, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Word</span>, and Might,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, and &lt;sc; Son, and Light of light,<br> Undiscovered by the sight <a name="page_132"><font size="1" color="green">p. 132</font></a><br> Of earthly monarch, or infernal spirit,<br> Incarnate was, that we might Heav’n inherit;<br>                 For He hath triumphed gloriously!</p>  
 <p>[<small>In Mr. Young's book. The melody by Dr. Schroeder.</small>]</p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_3"></a><a href="hec37.htm#fr_3">131:*</a> The reference is, of course, to Psalm xviii. 9; "He bowed the Heavens also, and came down."</p>
