 <p><a name="iii.iii.iv-p3"></a><b>22.</b> Neither shalt thou set thee up <i>any </i>image; which the LORD thy God hateth.</p> 				     				  
 <p><a name="iii.iii.iv-p4"></a><b>22.</b> Non eriges tibi statuam: quod odio habet Jehova Deus tuus.</p> 				    				  			   			 
 <p><a name="iii.iii.iv-p5"></a> </p>  			 
 <p class="Normal"><a name="iii.iii.iv-p6"></a><a name="iii.iii.iv-p6.1"></a></p>  			 
 <p><a name="iii.iii.iv-p7"></a><b>22.</b> <i>Neither shalt thou set thee up.</i> Hence also it more clearly appears what is the meaning and tendency of the Second Commandment. God elsewhere commands,   				 <a name="fr_99"></a><a href="#fn_99"><font size="1">100</font></a>
  				 (as we have seen,) that statues   				 <a name="fr_100"></a><a href="#fn_100"><font size="1">101</font></a>
  				 should be erected on the borders of the land, on which the sum of the Law should be inscribed. At first sight this prohibition seems to be contradictory; and indeed it would be so, unless you understand “statue” to be a false image of God, in which men set Him before them in bodily form; and, therefore, it is added, that He hates such statues. But I have preferred translating   				 <a name="fr_101"></a><a href="#fn_101"><font size="1">102</font></a>
  				 the relative in the neuter gender, that the sentence might be fuller; <i>i.e., </i>that the erecting of statues is an abomination to the Lord; because in this way His glory is dishonored, when He is transfigured into a body, or when anything corporeal is mixed with His spiritual nature.</p>
