 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Institutes of the Christian Religion</i>, by John Calvin, tr. Henry  Beveridge [1845], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <a name="iii"></a> 		<h1 align="center"><a name="iii-p0.1"></a>GENERAL INDEX OF CHAPTERS. </h1> 		<h3 align="center"><a name="iii-p0.2"></a>BOOK FIRST. </h3>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p1"></a>OF THE KNOWLEDGE OF GOD THE CREATOR.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p2"></a>Eighteen Chapters</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p3"></a>1. Connection between the Knowledge of God and the Knowledge of Ourselves. Nature of the connection.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p4"></a>2. What it is to Know God. Tendency of this Knowledge.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p5"></a>3. The Human Mind naturally imbued with the Knowledge of God.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p6"></a>4. This Knowledge stifled or corrupted, ignorantly or maliciously.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p7"></a>5. The Knowledge of God displayed in the fabric and constant Government of the Universe.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p8"></a>6. The need of Scripture as a Guide and Teacher in coming to God as a Creator.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p9"></a>7. The Testimony of the Spirit necessary to give full authority to Scripture. The impiety of pretending that the Credibility of Scripture depends on the Judgment of the Church.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p10"></a>8. The Credibility of Scripture sufficiently proved, in so far as Natural Reason admits.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p11"></a>9. All the principles of piety subverted by fanatics who substitute revelations for Scripture.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p12"></a>10. In Scripture, the true God opposed, exclusively, to all the gods of the Heathen.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p13"></a>11. Impiety of attributing a visible form to God. The setting up of Idols a revolt against the True God.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p14"></a>12. God distinguished from Idols, that He may be the exclusive object of Worship.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p15"></a>13. The Unity of the Divine Essence in Three Persons taught in Scripture, from the foundation of the World.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p16"></a>14. In the Creation of the World, and all things in it, the True God distinguished by certain marks from fictitious gods.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p17"></a>15. State in which man was created. The Faculties of the Soul—The Image of God—Free Will—Original Righteousness.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p18"></a>16. The World, created by God, still cherished and protected by Him. Each and all of its parts governed by His Providence.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p19"></a>17. Use to be made of this Doctrine.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p20"></a>18. The instrumentality of the wicked employed by God, while He continues free from every taint.</p> 		<h3 align="center"><a name="iii-p20.1"></a>BOOK SECOND </h3>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p21"></a>OF THE KNOWLEDGE OF GOD THE REDEEMER, IN CHRIST,</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p22"></a>AS FIRST MANIFESTED TO THE FATHERS UNDER THE LAW,</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p23"></a>AND THEREAFTER TO US UNDER THE GOSPEL</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p24"></a>Seventeen Chapters</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p25"></a>1. Through the Fall and revolt of Adam the whole Human race made accursed and degenerate. Of Original Sin.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p26"></a>2. Man now deprived of Freedom of Will, and miserably enslaved.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p27"></a>3. Every thing proceeding from the corrupt Nature of Man damnable.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p28"></a>4. How God works in the hearts of men.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p29"></a>5. The Arguments usually alleged in support of Free Will refuted.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p30"></a>6. Redemption for lost man to be sought in Christ.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p31"></a>7. The Law given, not to retain a people for itself, but to keep alive the Hope of Salvation in Christ until his Advent.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p32"></a>8. Exposition of the Moral Law.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p33"></a>9. Christ, though known to the Jews under the Law, yet only manifested under the Gospel.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p34"></a>10. The resemblance between the Old Testament and the New.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p35"></a>11. The difference between the two Testaments.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p36"></a>12. Christ, to perform the Office of Mediator, behoved to become man.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p37"></a>13. Christ clothed with the true substance of Human Nature.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p38"></a>14. How two natures constitute the Person of the Mediator.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p39"></a>15. Three things chiefly to be regarded in Christ—viz. his Offices of Prophet, King, and Priest.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p40"></a>16. How Christ performed the Office of Redeemer in procuring our salvation. The Death, Resurrection, and Ascension of Christ.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p41"></a>17. Christ rightly and properly said to have merited Grace and Salvation for us.</p> 		<h3 align="center"><a name="iii-p41.1"></a>BOOK THIRD </h3>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p42"></a>THE MODE OF OBTAINING THE GRACE OF CHRIST.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p43"></a>THE BENEFITS IT CONFERS, AND THE EFFECTS RESULTING FROM IT.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p44"></a>Twenty-Five Chapters</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p45"></a>1. The Benefits of Christ made available to us by the Secret Operation of the Spirit.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p46"></a>2. Of Faith. The Definition of it. Its peculiar properties.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p47"></a>3. Regeneration by Faith. Of Repentance.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p48"></a>4. Penitence, as explained in the sophistical jargon of the Schoolmen, widely different from the purity required by the Gospel. Of Confession and Satisfactions.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p49"></a>5. Of the modes of Supplementing Satisfactions—viz. Indulgences and Purgatory.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p50"></a>6. The Life of a Christian Man. Scriptural Arguments exhorting to it.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p51"></a>7. A Summary of the Christian Life. Of Self-Denial.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p52"></a>8. Of Bearing the Cross—one branch of Self-Denial.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p53"></a>9. Of Meditating on the Future Life.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p54"></a>10. How to use the Present Life, and the comforts of it.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p55"></a>11. Of Justification by Faith. Both the name and the reality defined.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p56"></a>12. Necessity of contemplating the Judgment-seat of God, in order to be seriously convinced of the Doctrine of Gratuitous Justification.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p57"></a>13. Two things to be observed in Gratuitous Justification.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p58"></a>14. The beginning of Justification. In what sense progressive.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p59"></a>15. The boasted merit of Works subversive both of the Glory of God, in bestowing Righteousness, and of the certainty of Salvation.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p60"></a>16. Refutation of the Calumnies by which it is attempted to throw odium on this doctrine.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p61"></a>17. The Promises of the Law and the Gospel reconciled.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p62"></a>18. The Righteousness of Works improperly inferred from Rewards.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p63"></a>19. Of Christian Liberty.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p64"></a>20. Of Prayer—a perpetual exercise of Faith. The daily benefits derived from it.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p65"></a>21. Of the Eternal Election, by which God has predestinated some to Salvation and others to Destruction.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p66"></a>22. This Doctrine confirmed by Proofs from Scripture.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p67"></a>23. Refutation of the Calumnies by which this Doctrine is always unjustly assailed.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p68"></a>24. Election confirmed by the Calling of God. The Reprobate bring upon themselves the righteous destruction to which they are doomed.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p69"></a>25. Of the Last Resurrection.</p> 		<h3 align="center"><a name="iii-p69.1"></a>BOOK FOURTH </h3>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p70"></a>OF THE EXTERNAL MEANS OR HELPS BY WHICH GOD ALLURES US INTO</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p71"></a>FELLOWSHIP WITH CHRIST, AND KEEPS US IN IT.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p72"></a>Twenty Chapters</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p73"></a>1. Of the True Church. Duty of cultivating Unity with her, as the mother of all the godly.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p74"></a>2. Comparison between the False Church and the True.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p75"></a>3. Of the Teachers and Ministers of the Church. Their Election and Office.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p76"></a>4. Of the State of the Primitive Church, and the Mode of Government in use before the Papacy.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p77"></a>5. The Ancient Form of Government utterly corrupted by the tyranny of the Papacy.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p78"></a>6. Of the Primacy of the Romish See.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p79"></a>7. Of the Beginning and Rise of the Romish Papacy, till it attained a height by which the Liberty of the Church was destroyed, and all true Rule overthrown.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p80"></a>8. Of the Power of the Church in Articles of Faith. The unbridled license of the Papal Church in destroying Purity of Doctrine.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p81"></a>9. Of Councils and their Authority.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p82"></a>10. Of the Power of making Laws. The cruelty of the Pope and his adherents, in this respect, in tyrannically oppressing and destroying Souls.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p83"></a>11. Of the Jurisdiction of the Church and the Abuses of it, as exemplified in the Papacy.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p84"></a>12. Of the Discipline of the Church, and its principal use in Censures and Excommunication.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p85"></a>13. Of Vows. The miserable entanglements caused by Vowing rashly.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p86"></a>14. Of the Sacraments.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p87"></a>15. Of Baptism.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p88"></a>16. Paedobaptism. Its accordance with the Institution of Christ, and the nature of the sign.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p89"></a>17. Of the Lord’s Supper, and the benefits conferred by it.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p90"></a>18. Of the Popish Mass. How it not only profanes, but annihilates the Lord’s Supper.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p91"></a>19. Of the Five Sacraments, falsely so called. Their spuriousness proved, and their true character explained.</p>  		
 <p class="Body"><a name="iii-p92"></a>20. Of Civil Government.</p>
