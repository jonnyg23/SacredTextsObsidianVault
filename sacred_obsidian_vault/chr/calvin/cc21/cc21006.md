 <p class="Italic"><a name="ii.v-p1"></a>The last chapter, as it is historical, and all its parts have been elsewhere handled, holy Calvin did not expound in his Lectures, that he might not burden the hearers with superfluous repetitions: however, to render the book complete, we here add it.</p>    
 <p class="BOOK-CHAP-BLU-24-CEN18"><a name="ii.v-p2"></a>CHAPTER 52</p>     	  	   	
 <p class="TableCaption19"><a name="ii.v-p3"></a><a name="ii.v-p3.1"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer052.htm#001">Jeremiah 52:1-34</a><a name="ii.v-p3.1"></a></p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p4"></a><b>1</b>. Zedekiah <i>was </i>one and twenty years old when he began to reign, and he reigned eleven years in Jerusalem. And his mother’s name <i>was </i>Hamutal the daughter of Jeremiah of Libnah.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p5"></a><b>1</b>. Viginti et unius anni erat Sedechias quando regnavit, et undecim annis regnavit in Jerusalem, et nomen matris ejus Hamutal filia Jeremiae de Libnah.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p6"></a><b>2</b>. And he did <i>that which was </i>evil in the eyes of the LORD, according to all that Jehoiakim had done.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p7"></a><b>2</b>. Et fecit malum in oculis Jehovae, secundum omnia quae fecerat Jehoiakim:</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p8"></a><b>3</b>. For through the anger of the LORD it came to pass in Jerusalem and Judah, till he had cast them out from his presence, that Zedekiah rebelled against the king of Babylon.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p9"></a><b>3</b>. Nempe propter furorem Jehovae qui fuit contra Jerusalem et Jehudah, donec projiceret eos a facie sua, rebellavit Sedechias contra regem Babylonis.  	   <a name="fr_114"></a><a href="#fn_114"><font size="1">115</font></a>
 </p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p13"></a><b>4</b>. And it came to pass in the ninth year of his reign, in the tenth month, in the tenth <i>day </i>of the month, <i>that </i>Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon came, he and all his army, against Jerusalem, and pitched against it, and built forts against it round about.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p14"></a><b>4</b>. Fuit autem anno nono regni ejus, mense decimo, decima mensis, venit Nabuchadrezer rex Babylonis, ipse et universus exercitus ejus adversus Jerusalem, et castrametati sunt contra eam, et aedificaverunt contra earn munitionem undique.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p15"></a><b>5</b>. So the city was besieged unto the eleventh year of king Zedekiah.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p16"></a><b>5</b>. Venitque civitas in obsidionem usque ad undecimum annum regis Sedechiae.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p17"></a><b>6</b>. And in the fourth month, in the ninth <i>day </i>of the month, the famine was sore in the city, so that there was no bread for the people of the land.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p18"></a><b>6</b>. Mense quarto, nona mensis, invaluit fames in urbe, <i>(adeo) </i>ut non esset panis populo terrae.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p19"></a><b>7</b>. Then the city was broken up, and all the men of war fled, and went forth out of the city by night by the way of the gate between the two walls, which <i>was </i>by the king’s garden; (now the Chaldeans <i>were </i>by the city round about:) and they went by the way of the plain.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p20"></a><b>7</b>. Et dirupta fuit urbs, et omnes viri bellatores fugerunt, exieruntque de urbe noete per viam portae <i>(quoe erat) </i>inter duos muros, qui (erant) juxta hortum regis (Chaldaei autem erant juxta urbem per circuitum) abieruntque per viam solitudinis.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p21"></a><b>8</b>. But the army of the Chaldeans pursued after the king, and overtook Zedekiah in the plains of Jericho; and all his army was scattered from him.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p22"></a><b>8</b>. Persecutus vero est exereitus Chaldaeorum regem, apprehenderuntque Sedechiam in desertis Jericho; quia omnis exercitus ejus dispersus est ab eo.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p23"></a><b>9</b>. Then they took the king, and carried him up unto the king of Babylon to Riblah in the land of Hamath; where he gave judgment upon him.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p24"></a><b>9</b>. Comprehenderunt igitur regem, et duxerunt eum ad regem Babylonis in Riblatah, in terram Chamath, qui disceptavit cure eo judiciis.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p25"></a><b>10</b>. And the king of Babylon slew the sons of Zedekiah before his eyes: he slew also all the princes of Judah in Riblah.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p26"></a><b>10</b>. Et jugulavit rex Babylonis filios Sedechiam in oculis ejus, et etiam omnes principes Jehudah jugulavit in Riblatah:</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p27"></a><b>11</b>. Then he put out the eyes of Zedekiah; and the king of Babylon bound him in chains, and carried him to Babylon, and put him in prison till the day of his death.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p28"></a><b>11</b>. Oculos autem Sedechiae excaecavit, et vinxit catenis, adducique fecit eum rex Babylonis, Babylonem, et posuit eum in domo carceris   	   <a name="fr_115"></a><a href="#fn_115"><font size="1">116</font></a>
  	   usque ad diem quo mortuus est.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p30"></a><b>12</b>. Now in the fifth month, in the tenth <i>day </i>of the month, which <i>was </i>the nineteenth year of Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon, came Nebuzaradan, captain of the guard, <i>which </i>served the king of Babylon, into Jerusalem,</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p31"></a><b>12</b>. Mense autem quinto, decima mensis   	   <a name="fr_116"></a><a href="#fn_116"><font size="1">117</font></a>
  	   (is annus erat decimus nonus annus regis Nabuchadrezer regis Babylonis) venit Nebuzaradan princeps militum, qui stabat   	   <a name="fr_117"></a><a href="#fn_117"><font size="1">118</font></a>
  	   coram rege Babylonis, in Jerusalem,</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p34"></a><b>13</b>. And burnt the house of the LORD, and the king’s house; and all the houses of Jerusalem, and all the houses of the great <i>men, </i>burned he with fire.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p35"></a><b>13</b>. Et incendit domurn Jehovae et domum regis, atque cunctas domos Jerusalem, et omnem domum magnam combussit igni:   	   <a name="fr_118"></a><a href="#fn_118"><font size="1">119</font></a>
 </p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p37"></a><b>14</b>. And all the army of the Chaldeans, that <i>were </i>with with the captain of the guard, brake down all the walls of Jerusalem round about.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p38"></a><b>14</b>. Onmesque muros Jerusalem undique destruxerunt totus exercitus Chaledaeorum, qui erat cum magistro militum.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p39"></a><b>15</b>. Then Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carried away captive <i>certain </i>of the poor of the people, and the residue of the people that remained in the city, and those that fell away, that fell to the king of Babylon, and the rest of the multitude.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p40"></a><b>15</b>. De pauperibus autem populi, et de reliquo populo qui remanserant in urbe<i>, </i>et de transfugis qui transfugerunt ad regem Babylonis, et de reliquiis multitudinis transmigrare fecit Nebuzaradan nmgister militum.   	   <a name="fr_119"></a><a href="#fn_119"><font size="1">120</font></a>
 </p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p42"></a><b>16</b>. But Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard, left <i>certain </i>of the poor of the land for vinedressers and for husbandmen.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p43"></a><b>16</b>. De pauperibus vero terrae reliquit Nebuzaradan magister militurn vinitores et agricolas.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p44"></a><b>17</b>. Also the pillars of brass that <i>were </i>in the house of the LORD, and the bases, and the brasen sea that <i>was </i>in the house of the LORD, the Chaldeans brake, and carried all the brass of them to Babylon.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p45"></a><b>17</b>. Et columnas aereas quae erant in domo Jehovae, et bases et mare aereum quod erat in domo Jehovae confregerunt Chaldaei, et detulerunt omne aes eorum Babylonem.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p46"></a><b>18</b>. The caldrons also, and the shovels, and the snuffers, and the bowls, and the spoons, and all the vessels of brass wherewith they ministered, took they away.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p47"></a><b>18</b>. Lebetes quoque et scopas et psalteria et pelves et cochlearia et omnia vasa aerea quibus ministrabant, tulerunt.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p48"></a><b>19</b>. And the basons, and the firepans, and the bowls, and the caldrons, and the candlesticks, and the spoons, and the cups; <i>that </i>which <i>was </i>of gold <i>in</i> gold, and <i>that </i>which <i>was 	  </i>of silver <i>in</i> silver, took the caprain of the guard away.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p49"></a><b>19</b>. Et hydrias et thuribula et pelves et ollas et candelabra, et mortariola et cyathos, quae aurea, aurea, et quae argentea, argentea,   	   <a name="fr_120"></a><a href="#fn_120"><font size="1">121</font></a>
  	   tulit magister militum.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p51"></a><b>20</b>. The two pillars, one sea, and twelve brasen bulls that <i>were </i>under the bases, which king Solomon had made in the house of the LORD: the brass of all these vessels was without weight.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p52"></a><b>20</b>. Columnas duas, mare unum, et boves duodecim aereos, qui erant sub basibus quas fecerat rex Solomo in domo Jehovae, non erat pondus, aeris eorum omnium <i>(nempe) </i>vasorum istorum.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p53"></a><b>21</b>. And <i>concerning </i>the pillars, the height of one pillar <i>was </i>eighteen cubits; and a fillet of twelve cubits did compass it; and the thickness thereof <i>was </i>four fingers; <i>it was </i>hollow.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p54"></a><b>21</b>. Quod ad columnas, octodecim cubitorum erat altitudo columnae unius, et filum duodecim cubitortum circuibat eam, cujus crassitudo <i>(quoe) </i>erat quatuor digitorum; (erat) vacua.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p55"></a><b>22</b>. And the chapiter of brass <i>was </i>upon it; and the height of one chapiter <i>was </i>five cubits, with network and pomegranates upon the chapiters round about, all <i>of</i> brass. The second pillar also and the pomegranates <i>were </i>like unto these.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p56"></a><b>22</b>. Capitellum autem quod erat super earn aereum; altitudo vero capitelli unius, quinque cubitorum erat, et reticulum, et malogranata super capitellum per circuiturn, omnia aerea et similia erant columnae secundae et malogranata.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p57"></a><b>23</b>. And there were ninety and six pomegranates on a side; <i>and </i>all the pomegranates upon the network <i>were</i> an hundred round about.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p58"></a><b>23</b>. Fuerunt autem malogranata nonaginta et sex ad plagam (unam) onmia malogranata, centum super reticulum per circuitum.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p59"></a><b>24</b>. And the captain of the guard took Seraiah the chief priest, and Zephaniah the second priest, and the three keepers of the door:</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p60"></a><b>24</b>. Tulit quoque magister militurn Seraiah sacerdotem primum, et Sephaniah sacerdotem secundum, et tres custodes liminis.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p61"></a><b>25</b>. He took also out of the city an eunuch, which had the charge of the men of war; and seven men of them that were near the king’s person, which were found in the city; and the principal scribe of the host, who mustered the people of the land; and threescore men of the people of the land, that were found in the midst of the city.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p62"></a><b>25</b>. Et de urbe tulit eunuchum unum, qui erat praepositus super viros bellatores, et septem viros ex iis qui videbant faciem regis, qui inventi sunt in urbe, et scribam principem militiae, qui colligebat ad militiam populum terrae, et sexaginta viros de populo terrae, qui inventi sunt in medio urbis.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p63"></a><b>26</b>. So Nebuzaradan, the captain of the guard took them, and brought them to the king of Babylon to Riblah.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p64"></a><b>26</b>. Tulit, inquam, cos Nebuzaradan magister militum, et deduxit eos ad regem Babylonis in Riblatha:</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p65"></a><b>27</b>. And the king of Babylon smote them, and put them to death in Riblah in the land of Hamath. Thus Judah was carried away captive out of his own land.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p66"></a><b>27</b>. Et percussit eos rex Babylonis, et interfecit eos in Riblatha in terra Chamath; et translatus est Jehudah de terra sua.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p67"></a><b>28</b>. This <i>is </i>the people whom Nebuchadrezzar carried away captive: in the seventh year three thousand Jews and three and twenty:</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p68"></a><b>28</b>. Iste est populus quem transferre fecit Nabuchadrezer, anno septimo, Judaeos tria millia et viginti tres.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p69"></a><b>29</b>. In the eighteenth year of Nebuchadrezzar he carried away captive from Jerusalem eight hundred thirty and two persons:</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p70"></a><b>29</b>. Anno decimo octavo Nabuchadrezer transferre fecit de Jerusalem animas octingentas triginta duas.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p71"></a><b>30</b>. In the three and twentieth year of Nebuchadrezzar Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carried away captive of the Jews seven hundred forty and five persons: all the persons <i>were </i>four thousand and six hundred.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p72"></a><b>30</b>. Anno tertio et vigesimo Nabuchadrezer, transferre fecit Nelmzar-adan magister militum, Judaeorum animus septingentas quadraginta quinque; omnes animae quatuor millia et sexcentae.  	   <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_121"><font size="1">122</font></a>
 </p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p75"></a><b>31</b>. And it came to pass in the seven and thirtieth year of the captivity of Jehoiachin king of Judah, in the twelfth month, in the five and twentieth <i>day </i>of the month, <i>that </i>Evilmerodach king of Babylon in the <i>first </i>year of his reign lifted up the head of 	  Jehoiachin king of Judah, and brought him forth out of prison,</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p76"></a><b>31</b>. Fuit autem tricesimo septimo anno transmigrationis Jehoiakin regis Jehudah, duodecimo mense, vicesima quinta mensis, elevavit Evil-merodach rex Babylonis, anno regni sui, caput Jehoiakin regis Jehudah, et eduxit eum de domo carceris;</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p77"></a><b>32</b>. And spake kindly unto him, and set his throne above the throne of the kings that <i>were </i>with him in Babylon,</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p78"></a><b>32</b>. Loquutusque est eum eo bolla, et posuit thronum ejus super thronum regum, qui erant secum in Babylone;</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p79"></a><b>33</b>. And changed his prison garments: and he did continually eat bread before him all the days of his life.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p80"></a><b>33</b>. Et mutavit vestimenta carceris ejus, et comedebat panem coram eo semper omnibus diebus vitae suae.</p> 	  	  	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p81"></a><b>34</b>. And<i> for </i>his diet, there was a continual diet given him of the king of Babylon, every day a portion until the day of his death, all the days of his life.</p> 	   	
 <p><a name="ii.v-p82"></a><b>34</b>. Et portio ejus, portio perpetua dabatur ei a rege Babylonis, per singulos dies ejus, usque ad diem quo mortuus est, omnibus diebus vitae ejus.</p>

Some of the poor of 		the city were evidently left, as well as some of the poor of the land. In the following words, the <i>Vulg</i>. for the most part is followed, which is not correct: the words should be, “<a name="ii.v-p41.3"></a>et reliquum populum,” and, “<a name="ii.v-p41.4"></a>et reliquias multitudinis.” And the past words should be, “even the remnant (or remainder) of the multitude,” that is, of the people that formerly inhabited the city. See 		<a name="ii.v-p41.5"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#009">Jer 39:9</a><a name="ii.v-p41.5"></a>. — <i>Ed</i>.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_121"></a><a href="cc21006.htm#fr_121">122</a> <a name="ii.v-p72.1"></a>   		 
 </p><p class="Footnote"><a name="ii.v-p73"></a> The number here given is to be restricted to the years here specified, that is, the <i>seventh</i>, the <i>eighteenth</i>, and the <i>twenty-third </i>of Nebuchadnezzar’s reign. We read of other captives; that is, in the <i>third </i>year of Jehoiakim’s reign, (<a name="ii.v-p73.1"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan001.htm#001">Da 1:1</a><a name="ii.v-p73.1"></a>) which was the <i>first </i>of Nebuchadnezzar; and in the <i>eighth </i>year of his reign, 		(<a name="ii.v-p73.2"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#012">2Ki 24:12</a><a name="ii.v-p73.2"></a>) when Jehoiachin was taken prisoner, and with him not less than ten thousand people, (<a name="ii.v-p73.3"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#014">2Ki 24:14</a><a name="ii.v-p73.3"></a>.)</p>  		 
 <p><a name="ii.v-p74"></a>We have no account of the number in the first captivity, when Daniel was taken to Babylon. The largest number in the <i>eighth </i>year of Nebuchadnezzar’s reign, even <i>ten </i>thousand. The amount of <i>three </i>captivities mentioned here, the last of which must have been after the murder of Gedaliah, is <i>four </i>thousand <i>six </i>hundred. All these being men, and of full age, there must have been many women and children. It has been 		thought that all, taken captive all these times, could not have been less than fifty thousand. — <i>Ed</i>.</p>
