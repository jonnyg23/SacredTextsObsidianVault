 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Of Prayer</i>, by John Calvin, tr. Henry  Beveridge [1845], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <a name="i"></a>  <h2 align="center"><a name="i-p0.1"></a>INSTITUTES OF THE CHRISTIAN RELIGION</h2> <h3 align="center"><a name="i-p0.2"></a>John Calvin</h3> <h1 align="center"><a name="i-p0.3"></a>OF PRAYER</h1> 
 <p><a name="i-p1"></a></p>   <h3 align="center"><a name="i-p1.2"></a>Translated by Henry Beveridge</h3> <h3 align="center"><a name="i-p1.3"></a>1845</h3>  <h3 align="center"><a name="i-p1.4"></a>BOOK III.</h3> <h3 align="center"><a name="i-p1.5"></a>CHAPTER XX.</h3>   <h2 align="center"><a name="i-p1.6"></a>OF PRAYER — A PERPETUAL EXERCISE OF FAITH. THE DAILY BENEFITS DERIVED  FROM IT.</h2>  
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p2"></a>The principal divisions of this chapter are, —</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p3"></a>I. Connection of the subject of prayer with the previous chapters. The nature of  prayer, and its necessity as a Christian exercise, sec. 1, 2.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p4"></a>II. To whom prayer is to be offered. Refutation of an objection which is too apt to  present itself to the mind, sec. 3.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p5"></a>III. Rules to be observed in prayer, sec. 4–16.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p6"></a>IV. Through whom prayer is to be made, sec. 17–19.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p7"></a>V. Refutation of an error as to the doctrine of our Mediator and Intercessor, with  answers to the leading arguments urged in support of the intercession of saints, sec. 20–27.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p8"></a>VI. The nature of prayer, and some of its accidents, sec. 28–33.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p9"></a>VII. A perfect form of invocation, or an exposition of the Lord's Prayer, sec. 34–50.</p> 
 <p class="noindent"><a name="i-p10"></a>VIII. Some rules to be observed with regard to prayer, as time, perseverance, the  feeling of the mind, and the assurance of faith, sec. 50–52.</p>
