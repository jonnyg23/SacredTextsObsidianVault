 <p> <a name="iv.iv.vii.vii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.vii.vii-p0.1"></a>Chapter VII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.vii.vii-p1"></a>A question whether the man who causes the death of a good man is guilty, if the good man is the gainer by his death.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.vii.vii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.vii.vii-p2.1"></a>Germanus</span>: Well then, if a good man does not only suffer no evil by being killed, but actually gains a reward from his suffering, how can we accuse the man who has done him no harm but good by killing him?</p>
