 <p> <a name="iv.iv.ii.xix"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.ii.xix-p0.1"></a>Chapter XIX.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.ii.xix-p1"></a>Of the three origins of our thoughts.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.ii.xix-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.ii.xix-p2.1"></a>Above</span> all we ought at least to know that there are three origins of our thoughts, i.e., from God, from the devil, and from ourselves. They come from God when He vouchsafes to visit us with the illumination of the Holy Ghost, lifting us up to a higher state of progress, and where we have made but little progress, or through acting slothfully have been overcome, He chastens us with most salutary compunction, or when He discloses to us heavenly mysteries, or turns our purpose and will to better actions, as in the case where the king Ahasuerus, being chastened by the Lord, was prompted to ask for the books of the annals, by which he was reminded of the good deeds of Mordecai, and promoted him to a position of the highest honour and at once recalled his most cruel sentence concerning the slaughter of the Jews. <a name="fr_1131"></a><a href="#fn_1131"><font size="1">1132</font></a>
  Or when the prophet says: “I will hearken what the Lord God will say in me.” <a name="fr_1132"></a><a href="#fn_1132"><font size="1">1133</font></a>
  Another too tells us “And an angel spoke, and said in me,” <a name="fr_1133"></a><a href="#fn_1133"><font size="1">1134</font></a>
  or when the Son of God promised that He would come with His Father, and make His abode in us, <a name="fr_1134"></a><a href="#fn_1134"><font size="1">1135</font></a>
  and “It is not ye that speak, but the Spirit of your Father which speaketh in you.” <a name="fr_1135"></a><a href="#fn_1135"><font size="1">1136</font></a>
  And the chosen vessel: “Ye seek a proof of Christ that speaketh in me.” <a name="fr_1136"></a><a href="#fn_1136"><font size="1">1137</font></a>
  But a whole range of thoughts springs from the devil, when he endeavours to destroy us either by the pleasures of sin or by secret attacks, in his crafty wiles deceitfully showing us evil as good, and transforming himself into an angel of light to us: <a name="fr_1137"></a><a href="#fn_1137"><font size="1">1138</font></a>
  as when the evangelist tells us: “And when supper was ended, when the devil had already put it into the heart of Judas Iscariot, Simons son, to betray” <a name="fr_1138"></a><a href="#fn_1138"><font size="1">1139</font></a>
  the Lord: and again also “after the sop,” he says, “Satan entered into him.” <a name="fr_1139"></a><a href="#fn_1139"><font size="1">1140</font></a>
  Peter also says to Ananias: “Why hath Satan tempted thine heart, to lie to the Holy Ghost?” <a name="fr_1140"></a><a href="#fn_1140"><font size="1">1141</font></a>
  And that which we read in the gospel much earlier as predicted by Ecclesiastes: “If the spirit of the ruler rise up against thee, leave not thy place.” <a name="fr_1141"></a><a href="#fn_1141"><font size="1">1142</font></a>
  That too which is said to God against Ahab in the third book of Kings, in the character of an unclean spirit: “I will go forth and will be a lying spirit in the mouth of all his prophets.” <a name="fr_1142"></a><a href="#fn_1142"><font size="1">1143</font></a>
  But they arise from ourselves, when in the course of nature we recollect what we are doing or have done or have heard. Of which the blessed David speaks: “I thought upon the ancient days, and had in mind the years from of old, and I meditated, by night I exercised myself with my heart, and searched out my spirit.” <a name="fr_1143"></a><a href="#fn_1143"><font size="1">1144</font></a>
  And again: “the Lord knoweth the thoughts of man, that they are vain:” <a name="fr_1144"></a><a href="#fn_1144"><font size="1">1145</font></a>
  and “the thoughts of the righteous are judgments.” <a name="fr_1145"></a><a href="#fn_1145"><font size="1">1146</font></a>
  In the gospel too the Lord says to the Pharisees: “why do ye think evil in your hearts?” <a name="fr_1146"></a><a href="#fn_1146"><font size="1">1147</font></a>
 </p>
