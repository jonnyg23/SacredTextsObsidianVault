 <p> <a name="iv.iii.viii.iii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.viii.iii-p0.1"></a>Chapter III.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.viii.iii-p1"></a>Of those things which are spoken of God anthropomorphically.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.viii.iii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.viii.iii-p2.1"></a>For</span> if when these things are said of God they are to be understood literally in a material gross signification, then also He <i>sleeps</i>, as it is said, “Arise, wherefore sleepest thou, O Lord?” <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_919"><font size="1">920</font></a>
  though it is elsewhere said of Him: “Behold he that keepeth Israel shall neither slumber nor sleep.” <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_920"><font size="1">921</font></a>
  And He <i>stands</i> and <i>sits</i>, since He says, “Heaven is my seat, and earth the footstool for my feet:” <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_921"><font size="1">922</font></a>
  though He “measure out the heaven with his hand, and holdeth the earth in his fist.” <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_922"><font size="1">923</font></a>
  And He is “drunken with wine” as it is said, “The Lord awoke like a sleeper, a mighty man, drunken with wine;” <a name="fr_923"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">924</font></a>
  He “who only hath immortality and dwelleth in the light which no man can approach unto:” <a name="fr_924"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">925</font></a>
  not to say anything of the “ignorance” and “forgetfulness,” of which we often find mention in Holy Scripture: nor lastly of the outline of His limbs, which are spoken of as arranged and ordered like a mans; e.g., the hair, head, nostrils, eyes, face, hands, arms, fingers, belly, and feet: if we are willing to take all of which according to the bare literal sense, we must think of God as in fashion with the outline of limbs, and a bodily form; which indeed is shocking even to speak of, and must be far from our thoughts.</p>
