 <p> <a name="iv.iii.v.xvii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.v.xvii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XVII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.v.xvii-p1"></a>That the foundation and basis of the spiritual combat must be laid in the struggle against gluttony.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.v.xvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.v.xvii-p2.1"></a>Would</span> you like to hear a true athlete of Christ striving according to the rules and laws of the conflict? “I,” said he, “so run, not as uncertainly; I so fight, not as one that beateth the air: but I chastise my body and bring it into subjection, lest by any means when I have preached to others I myself should be a castaway.” <a name="fr_843"></a><a href="#fn_843"><font size="1">844</font></a>
  You see how he made the chief part of the struggle depend upon himself, that is upon his flesh, as if on a most sure foundation, and placed the result of the battle simply in the chastisement of the flesh and the subjection of his body. “I then so run not as uncertainly.” He does not run uncertainly, because, <a name="fr_844"></a><a href="#fn_844"><font size="1">845</font></a>
  looking to the heavenly Jerusalem, he has a mark set, towards which his heart is swiftly directed without swerving. He does not run uncertainly, because, “forgetting those things which are behind, he reaches forth to those that are before, pressing towards the mark for the prize of the high calling of God in Christ Jesus,” <a name="fr_845"></a><a href="#fn_845"><font size="1">846</font></a>
  whither he ever directs his mental gaze, and hastening towards it with all speed <a name="fr_846"></a><a href="#fn_846"><font size="1">847</font></a>
  of heart, proclaims with confidence, “I have fought a good fight, I have finished  <a name="page_240"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 240</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.v.xvii-Page_240"></a>my course, I have kept the faith.” <a name="fr_847"></a><a href="#fn_847"><font size="1">848</font></a>
  And because he knows he has run unweariedly “after the odour of the ointment” <a name="fr_848"></a><a href="#fn_848"><font size="1">849</font></a>
  of Christ with ready devotion of heart, and has won the battle of the spiritual combat by the chastisement of the flesh, he boldly concludes and says, “Henceforth there is laid up for me a crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous judge, will give to me in that day.” And that he might open up to us also a like hope of reward, if we desire to imitate him in the struggle of his course, he added: “But not to me only, but to all also who love His coming;” <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">850</font></a>
  declaring that we shall be sharers of his crown in the day of judgment, if we love the coming of Christ—not that one only which will be manifest to men even against their will; but also this one which daily comes to pass in holy souls—and if we gain the victory in the fight by chastising the body. And of this coming it is that the Lord speaks in the Gospel. “I,” says He, “and my Father will come to him, and will make our abode with him.” <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">851</font></a>
  And again: “Behold, I stand at the door and knock: if any man hear my voice and open the gate, I will come in to him and will sup with him, and he with me.” <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">852</font></a>
 </p>
