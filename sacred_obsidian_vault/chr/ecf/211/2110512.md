 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xi.i"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.xi.i-p0.1"></a>Chapter I.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.xi.i-p1"></a>How our seventh combat is against the spirit of vainglory, and what its nature.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.xi.i-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.xi.i-p2.1"></a>Our</span> seventh combat is against the spirit of <span class="Greek"><a name="iv.iii.xi.i-p2.2"></a>κενοδοξία</span>, which we may term vain or idle glory: a spirit that takes many shapes, and is changeable and subtle, so that it can with difficulty, I will not say be guarded against, but be seen through and discovered even by the keenest eyes.</p>
