 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xii.ix"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.xii.ix-p0.1"></a>Chapter IX.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.xii.ix-p1"></a>How we too may overcome pride.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.xii.ix-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.xii.ix-p2.1"></a>And</span> so we can escape the snare of this most evil spirit, if in the case of every virtue in which we feel that we make progress, we say these words of the Apostle: “Not I, but the grace of God with me,” and “by the grace of God I am what I am;” <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1041"><font size="1">1042</font></a>
  and “it is God that worketh in us both to will and to do of His good pleasure.” <a name="fr_1042"></a><a href="#fn_1042"><font size="1">1043</font></a>
  As the author of our salvation Himself also says: “If a man abide in me and I in him, the same beareth much fruit; for without me ye can do nothing.” <a name="fr_1043"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">1044</font></a>
  And “Except the Lord build the house, they labour in vain that build it. Except the Lord keep the city, the watchman waketh but in vain.” And “Vain is it for you to rise up before light.” <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">1045</font></a>
  For “it is not of him that willeth, nor of him that runneth, but of God that hath mercy.” <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">1046</font></a>
 </p>
