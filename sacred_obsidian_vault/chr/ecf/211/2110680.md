 <p> <a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XVII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p1"></a>A question with regard to the comparison of <span class="font-style:normal"><a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p1.1"></a>seven </span>nations with<span class="font-style:normal"><a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p1.2"></a> eight </span>faults.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p2.1"></a>Germanus</span>: How then is it that there are <i>eight</i> faults which assault us, when Moses reckons the nations opposed to the people of Israel as <i>seven</i>, and how is it well for us to take possession of the territory of our faults?</p>
