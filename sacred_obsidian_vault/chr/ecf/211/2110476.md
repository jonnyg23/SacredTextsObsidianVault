 <p> <a name="iv.iii.ix.v"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.ix.v-p0.1"></a>Chapter V.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.ix.v-p1"></a>That disturbances are caused in us not by the faults of other people, but by our own.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.ix.v-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.ix.v-p2.1"></a>Whence</span> it is clearly proved that the pains of disturbances are not always caused in us by other peoples faults, but rather by our own, as we have stored up in ourselves the causes of offence, and the seeds of faults, which, as soon as a shower of temptation waters our soul, at once burst forth into shoots and fruits.</p>
