 <p> <a name="iv.iv.iv.xiv"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p0.1"></a>Chapter XIV.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p1"></a>That knowledge of the law is given by the guidance and illumination of the Lord.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p2.1"></a>The</span> knowledge also of the law itself they daily endeavour to gain not by diligence in reading, but by the guidance and illumination of God as they say to Him: “Show me Thy ways, O Lord, and teach me Thy paths:” and “open Thou mine eyes: and I shall see the wondrous things of Thy law:” and “teach me to do Thy will, for Thou art my God;” and again: “Who teacheth man knowledge.” <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1255"><font size="1">1256</font></a>
