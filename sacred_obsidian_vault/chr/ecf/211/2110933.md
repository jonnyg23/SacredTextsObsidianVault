 <p> <a name="iv.v.viii.xiii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.v.viii.xiii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XIII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.v.viii.xiii-p1"></a>Our answer as to the reason which demanded an oath from us.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.v.viii.xiii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.v.viii.xiii-p2.1"></a>Germanus</span>: The first reason, as we said, was that we were afraid of vexing our Elders and resisting their orders; the second was that we very foolishly believed that, if we had learnt from you anything perfect or splendid to hear or look at, when we returned to the monastery, we should be able to perform it.</p>
