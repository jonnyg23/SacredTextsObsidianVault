 <p> <a name="iv.iii.iv.xxxiv"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.iv.xxxiv-p0.1"></a>Chapter XXXIV.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.iv.xxxiv-p1"></a>Of the way in which our renunciation is nothing but mortification and the image of the Crucified.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.iv.xxxiv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.iv.xxxiv-p2.1"></a>Renunciation</span> is nothing but the evidence of the cross and of mortification. And so you must know that to-day you are dead to this world and its deeds and desires, and that, as the Apostle says, you are crucified to this world and this world to you. <a name="fr_794"></a><a href="#fn_794"><font size="1">795</font></a>
  Consider therefore the demands of the cross under the sign <a name="fr_795"></a><a href="#fn_795"><font size="1">796</font></a>
  of which you ought henceforward to live in this life; because <i>you</i> no longer live but <i>He</i> lives in you who was crucified for you. <a name="fr_796"></a><a href="#fn_796"><font size="1">797</font></a>
  We must therefore pass our time in this life in that fashion and form in which He was crucified for us on the cross so that (as David says) piercing our flesh with the fear of the Lord, <a name="fr_797"></a><a href="#fn_797"><font size="1">798</font></a>
  we may have all our wishes and desires not subservient to our own lusts but fastened to His mortification. For so shall we fulfil the command of the Lord which says: “He that taketh not up his cross and followeth me is not worthy of me.” <a name="fr_798"></a><a href="#fn_798"><font size="1">799</font></a>
  But perhaps you will say: How can a man carry his cross continually? or how can any one who is alive be crucified? Hear briefly how this is.</p>
