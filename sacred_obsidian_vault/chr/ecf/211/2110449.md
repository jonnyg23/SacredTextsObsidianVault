 <p> <a name="iv.iii.viii.i"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.viii.i-p0.1"></a>Chapter I.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.viii.i-p1"></a>How our fourth conflict is against the sin of anger, and how many evils this passion produces.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.viii.i-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.viii.i-p2.1"></a>In</span> our fourth combat the deadly poison of anger has to be utterly rooted out from the inmost comers of our soul. For as long as this remains in our hearts, and blinds with its hurtful darkness the eye of the soul, we can neither acquire right judgment and discretion, nor gain the insight which springs from an honest gaze, or ripeness of counsel, nor can we be partakers of life, or retentive of righteousness, or even have the capacity for spiritual and true light: “for,” says one, “mine eye is disturbed by reason of anger.” <a name="fr_910"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">911</font></a>
  Nor can we become partakers of wisdom, even though we are considered wise by universal consent, for “anger rests in the bosom of fools.” <a name="fr_911"></a><a href="#fn_911"><font size="1">912</font></a>
  Nor can we even attain immortal life, although we are accounted prudent in the opinion of everybody, for “anger destroys even the prudent.” <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_912"><font size="1">913</font></a>
  Nor shall we be able with clear judgment of heart to secure the controlling power of righteousness, even though we are reckoned perfect and holy in the estimation of all men, for “the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God.” <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">914</font></a>
  Nor can we by any possibility acquire that esteem and honour which is so frequently seen even in worldlings, even though we are thought noble and honourable through the privileges of birth, because “an angry man is dishonoured.” <a name="fr_914"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">915</font></a>
  Nor again can we secure any ripeness of counsel, even though we appear to be weighty, and endowed with the utmost knowledge; because “an angry man acts without counsel.” <a name="fr_915"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">916</font></a>
  Nor can we be free from dangerous disturbances, nor be without sin, even though no sort of disturbances be brought upon us by others;  <a name="page_258"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 258</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.viii.i-Page_258"></a>because “a passionate man engenders quarrels, but an angry man digs up sins.” <a name="fr_916"></a><a href="#fn_916"><font size="1">917</font></a>
 </p>
