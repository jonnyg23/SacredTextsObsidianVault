 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xii.xii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.xii.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.xii.xii-p1"></a>That no toil is worthy to be compared with the promised bliss.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.xii.xii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.xii.xii-p2.1"></a>For</span> all the long years of this present life disappear when you have regard to the eternity of the future glory: and all our sorrows vanish away in the contemplation of that vast bliss, and like smoke melt away, and come to nothing, and like ashes are no more seen.</p>
