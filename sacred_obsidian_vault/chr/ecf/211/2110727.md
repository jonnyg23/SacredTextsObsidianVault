 <p> <a name="iv.iv.viii.xviii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p1"></a>A question whether among the devils there is any order observed in the attack, or system in its changes.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p2.1"></a>Germanus</span>: Must we then believe that wickedness is arranged and so to speak systematized among them in such a way that there is some order in the changes observed by them, and a regular plan of attack carried out, though it is clear that method and system can only exist among good and upright men, as Scripture says: “Thou shalt seek wisdom among the ungodly and shalt not find it; and: “our enemies are senseless;” and this: “There is neither wisdom, nor courage, nor counsel among the ungodly.” <a name="fr_1476"></a><a href="#fn_1476"><font size="1">1477</font></a>
