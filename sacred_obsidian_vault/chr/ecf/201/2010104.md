 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iii.viii.xxxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iii.viii.xxxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXV</span>.—<i>Justus, the Third Bishop of Jerusalem.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.viii.xxxv-p2"></a>1. <span class="c13"><a name="iii.viii.xxxv-p2.1"></a>But</span> when Symeon also had died in the manner described, <a name="fr_903"></a><a href="#fn_903"><font size="1">904</font></a>
  a certain Jew by the name of Justus <a name="fr_904"></a><a href="#fn_904"><font size="1">905</font></a>
  succeeded to the episcopal throne in Jerusalem. He was one of the many thousands of the circumcision who at that time believed in Christ.</p>
