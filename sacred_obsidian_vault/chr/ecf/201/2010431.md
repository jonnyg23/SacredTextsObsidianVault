 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlvi-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLVI</span>.—<i>Victories over the Barbarians.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlvi-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlvi-p2.1"></a>Thus</span> the emperor in all his actions honored God, the Controller of all things, and exercised an unwearied <a name="fr_3145"></a><a href="#fn_3145"><font size="1">3146</font></a>
  oversight over His churches. And God requited him, by subduing all barbarous nations under his feet, so that he was able everywhere to raise trophies over his enemies: and He proclaimed him as conqueror to all mankind, and made him a terror to his adversaries: not indeed that this was his natural character, since he was rather the meekest, and gentlest, and most benevolent of men.</p>
