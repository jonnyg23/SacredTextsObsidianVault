 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iii.viii.xxi-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iii.viii.xxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXI.—</span><i>Cerdon becomes the Third Ruler of the Church of Alexandria.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.viii.xxi-p2"></a>1. <span class="c13"><a name="iii.viii.xxi-p2.1"></a>After</span> Nerva had reigned a little more than a year <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">732</font></a>
  he was succeeded by Trajan. It was during the first year of his reign that Abilius, <a name="fr_732"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">733</font></a>
  who had ruled the church of Alexandria for thirteen years, was succeeded by Cerdon. <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">734</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="iii.viii.xxi-p6"></a>2. He was the third that presided over that church after Annianus, <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">735</font></a>
  who was the first. At that time Clement still ruled the church of Rome, being also the third that held the episcopate there after Paul and Peter.</p>  
 <p><a name="iii.viii.xxi-p8"></a>3. Linus was the first, and after him came Anencletus. <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">736</font></a>
 </p>
