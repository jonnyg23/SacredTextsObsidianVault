 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVIII</span>.—<i>Discovery of the Most Holy Sepulchre.</i> <a name="fr_3261"></a><a href="#fn_3261"><font size="1">3262</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxviii-p3"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxviii-p3.1"></a>This</span> also was accomplished without delay. But as soon as the original surface of the ground, beneath the covering of earth, appeared, immediately, and contrary to all expectation, the venerable and hollowed monument of our Saviours resurrection was discovered. Then indeed did this most holy cave present a faithful simili<a name="page_528"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 528</font></a> <a name="iv.vi.iii.xxviii-Page_528"></a>tude of his return to life, in that, after lying buried in darkness, it again emerged to light, and afforded to all who came to witness the sight, a clear and visible proof of the wonders of which that spot had once been the scene, a testimony to the resurrection of the Saviour clearer than any voice could give.</p>
