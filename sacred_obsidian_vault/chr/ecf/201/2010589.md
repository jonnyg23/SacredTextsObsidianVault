 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iv.iii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.iii-p1.1"></a>Chapter III</span>.—<i>Equalization of the More Oppressive Taxes.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iv.iii-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.iii-p2.1"></a>And</span> whereas some persons found fault with the surveys of land which had been made under former emperors, and complained that their property was unduly burdened; acting in this case also on the principles of justice, he sent commissioners to equalize the tribute, and to secure immunity to those who had made this appeal.</p>
