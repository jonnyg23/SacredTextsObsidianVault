 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLVIII</span>.—<i>Celebration of Constantines Decennalia.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p2.1"></a>While</span> he was thus circumstanced, he completed the tenth year of his reign. On this occasion he ordered the celebration of general festivals, and offered prayers of thanksgiving to God, the King of all, as sacrifices without flame or smoke. <a name="fr_3147"></a><a href="#fn_3147"><font size="1">3148</font></a>
  And from this employment he derived much pleasure: not so from the tidings he received of the ravages committed in the Eastern provinces.</p>
