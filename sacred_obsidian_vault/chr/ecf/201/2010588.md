 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iv.ii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.ii-p1.1"></a>Chapter II</span>.—<i>Remission of a Fourth Part of the Taxes.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iv.ii-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.ii-p2.1"></a>The</span> extent to which he studied the general happiness and prosperity may be understood from a single instance most beneficial and universal in its application, and still gratefully remembered. He remitted a fourth part of the yearly tribute paid for land, and bestowed it on the owners of the soil; so that if we compute this yearly reduction, we shall find that the cultivators enjoyed their produce free of tribute every fourth year. <a name="fr_3308"></a><a href="#fn_3308"><font size="1">3309</font></a>
  This privilege being established by law, and secured for the time to come, has given occasion for the emperors beneficence to be held, not merely by the then present generation, but by their children and descendants, in perpetual remembrance.</p>
