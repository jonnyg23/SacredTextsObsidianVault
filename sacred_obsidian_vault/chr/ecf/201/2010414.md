 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxix-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIX</span>.—<i>How the Christ of God appeared to him in his Sleep, and commanded him to use in his Wars a Standard made in the Form of the Cross.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxix-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxix-p2.1"></a>He</span> said, moreover, that he doubted within himself what the import of this apparition could be. And while he continued to ponder and reason on its meaning, night suddenly came on; then in his sleep the Christ of God appeared to him with the same sign which he had seen in the heavens, and commanded him to make a likeness of that sign which he had seen in the heavens, and to use it as a safeguard in all engagements with his enemies.</p>
