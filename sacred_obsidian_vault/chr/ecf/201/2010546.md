 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxvii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVII</span>.—<i>How Constantine commanded the Materials of the Idol Temple, and the Soil itself, to be removed at a Distance.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxvii-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxvii-p2.1"></a>Nor</span> did the emperors zeal stop here; but he gave further orders that the materials of what was thus destroyed, both stone and timber, should be removed and thrown as far from the spot as possible; and this command also was speedily executed. The emperor, however, was not satisfied with having proceeded thus far: once more, fired with holy ardor, he directed that the ground itself should be dug up to a considerable depth, and the soil which had been polluted by the foul impurities of demon worship transported to a far distant place.</p>
