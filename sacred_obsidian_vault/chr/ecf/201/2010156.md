 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iii.x.xvi-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iii.x.xvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XV.—</span><i>The Schism of Blastus at Rome.</i> <a name="fr_1565"></a><a href="#fn_1565"><font size="1">1566</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.x.xvi-p3"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iii.x.xvi-p3.1"></a>Others</span>, of whom Florinus <a name="fr_1566"></a><a href="#fn_1566"><font size="1">1567</font></a>
  was chief, flourished at Rome. He fell from the presbyterate of the Church, and Blastus was involved in a similar fall. They also drew away many of the Church to their opinion, each striving to introduce his own innovations in respect to the truth.</p>
