 </p><p class="c45"><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p1"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p1.1"></a>The Bishops of Rome, Alexandria, Antioch, and Jerusalem, mentioned by Eusebius.</span></p>  
 <p class="c79"><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p2"></a>Bishops of Rome.</p>  
 <p class="c80"><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p3"></a>(Dates taken from the table given by Lipsius in his <i><span><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p3.1"></a>Chronologie der röm. Bischöfe</span></i>, p. 263 sq.)</p>     
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p4"></a>Linus.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p5"></a>Pontianus, 5 years 2 months 7 days; (July 21?), 230–Sept. 28, 235.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p6"></a>Anencletus.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p7"></a>Anteros, 1 month 12 days; Nov. 21, 235–Jan. 3, 236.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p8"></a>Clement.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p9"></a>Fabianus, 14 years 10 days; 236–Jan. 20, 250. Vacancy from Jan. 21, 250–March, 251.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p10"></a>Evarestus.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p11"></a>Cornelius, 2 years 3 months 10 days; beginning of March, 251–middle of June, 253.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p12"></a>Alexander.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p13"></a>Lucius, 8 months 10 days; June (25?), 253–March 5, 254.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p14"></a>Xystus I, for about ten years; died between 124 and 126.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p15"></a>Stephanus, 3 years 2 months 21 days; (May 12?), 254–Aug. 6, 258.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p16"></a>Telesphorus, 11 years; died between 135 and 137.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p17"></a>Xystus II., 11 months 12 (6?) days; Aug. 24 (31?), 257–Aug. 6, 258.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p18"></a>Hyginus, 4 years; died between 139 and 141.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p19"></a>Dionysius, 9 years 5 months 2 days; July 22, 259–Dec. 27, 268.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p20"></a>Pius, 15–16 years; died between 154 and 156.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p21"></a>Felix I., 5 years 11 months 25 days; Jan. 5, 269–Dec. 30, 274.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p22"></a>Anicetus, 11–12 years; died in 166 or 167.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p23"></a>Eutychian, 8 years 11 months 3 days; (Jan. 5?) 275–Dec. 8, 283.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p24"></a>Soter, 8–9 years; died in 174 or 175.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p25"></a>Caius, 12 years 4 months 6 days; Dec. 17, 283–April 22, 296.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p26"></a>Eleutherus, 15 years; died in 189.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p27"></a>Marcellinus, 8 years 3 months 25 days; June 30, 296–(Oct. 25?), 304. Vacancy until 307.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p28"></a>Victor, 9–10 years; 189–198 or 199.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p29"></a>Marcellus, 1 year 7 months 21 days; (May 24?), 307–Jan. 15, 309.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p30"></a>Zephyrinus, 18–19 years; 198 or 199–217 (Aug. 26?).</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p31"></a>Eusebius, 3 (4?) months 23 (16?) days; April 23 (16?), 309–Aug. 17, 309. Vacancy until 310.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p32"></a>Callistus, 5 years; 217–Oct. 14, 222.</p>   
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p33"></a>Miltiades, 3 years 6 months 8 days; July 2, 310–Jan. 10 (11?), 314.</p>      
 <p><a name="iii.xvii.xxiv-p34"></a>Urbanus, 8 years; 222–230 (May 19?).</p>
