 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iii.xi.xxvii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iii.xi.xxvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVII</span>.—<i>How the Bishops regarded Origen.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.xi.xxvii-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iii.xi.xxvii-p2.1"></a>He</span> was so earnestly affected toward Origen, that he urged him to come to that country for the benefit of the churches, and moreover he visited him in Judea, remaining with him for some time, for the sake of improvement in divine things. And Alexander, <a name="fr_1998"></a><a href="#fn_1998"><font size="1">1999</font></a>
  bishop of Jerusalem, and Theoctistus, <a name="fr_1999"></a><a href="#fn_1999"><font size="1">2000</font></a>
  bishop of Cæsarea, attended on him constantly, <a name="fr_2000"></a><a href="#fn_2000"><font size="1">2001</font></a>
  as their only teacher, and allowed <a name="fr_2001"></a><a href="#fn_2001"><font size="1">2002</font></a>
  him to expound the Divine Scriptures, and to perform the other duties pertaining to ecclesiastical discourse. <a name="fr_2002"></a><a href="#fn_2002"><font size="1">2003</font></a>
 </p>
