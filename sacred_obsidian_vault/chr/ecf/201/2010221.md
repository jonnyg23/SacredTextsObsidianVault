 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iii.xii.iv-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iii.xii.iv-p1.1"></a>Chapter III.—</span><i>Cyprian, and the Bishops with him, first taught that it was necessary to purify by Baptism those converted from Heresy.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.xii.iv-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iii.xii.iv-p2.1"></a>First</span> of all, Cyprian, pastor of the parish of Carthage, <a name="fr_2169"></a><a href="#fn_2169"><font size="1">2170</font></a>
  maintained that they should not be received except they had been purified from their error by baptism. But Stephen considering it unnecessary to add any innovation contrary to the tradition which had been held from the beginning, was very indignant at this. <a name="fr_2170"></a><a href="#fn_2170"><font size="1">2171</font></a>
