 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iv.lxxiii-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.lxxiii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXIII</span>.—<i>How Constantine is represented on Coins in the Act of ascending to Heaven.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iv.lxxiii-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.lxxiii-p2.1"></a>A coinage</span> was also struck which bore the following device. On one side appeared the figure of our blessed prince, with the head closely veiled: the reverse exhibited him sitting as a charioteer, drawn by four horses, with a hand stretched downward from above to receive him up to heaven.</p>
