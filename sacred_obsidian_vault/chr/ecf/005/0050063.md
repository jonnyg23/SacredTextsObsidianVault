    <h2 align="CENTER"><a></a> Epistle XXXVIII.<sup><a href="footnote/fn53.htm#P5369_1637308">1</a></sup> </h2>  
 <p><a></a> The Letter of Caldonius, Herculanus, and Others, on the Excommunication of Felicissimus with His People.</p>    <ul>
 <p><a></a> Argument.-Caldonius, Herculanus, and Others Carry into Effect What the Preceding Letter Had Bidden Them.</p>    </ul>
 <p>Caldonius, with Herculanus and Victor, his colleagues, also with Rogatianus and Numidicus, presbyters.<sup><a href="footnote/fn53.htm#P5374_1637654">2</a></sup> We have rejected Felicissimus and Augendus from communion; also Repostus from among the exiles, and Irene of the Blood-stained ones;<sup><a href="footnote/fn53.htm#P5375_1637819">3</a></sup> and Paula the sempstress; which you ought to know from my subscription; also we have rejected Sophronius and Soliassus (budinarius),<sup><a href="footnote/fn53.htm#P5376_1638009">4</a></sup> -himself also one of the exiles.</p>
