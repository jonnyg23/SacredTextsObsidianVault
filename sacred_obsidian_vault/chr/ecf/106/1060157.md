 </p><p class="c39"><a name="vi.v.lxi-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="vi.v.lxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter LX.—Of His Paying the Tribute Money Out of the Mouth of the Fish, an Incident Which Matthew Alone Mentions.</span></p>  			 
 <p class="c22"><a name="vi.v.lxi-p2"></a>118. Matthew continues in these terms: <a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a> <a name="vi.v.lxi-Page_155"></a>“And when they were come to Capharnaum, they that received tribute money came to Peter, and said to him, Doth not your master pay tribute? He saith, Yes;” and so on, down to where we read: “Thou shall find a piece of money: that take, and give unto them for me and thee.” <a name="fr_1106"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">1107</font></a>
  He is the only one who relates this occurrence, after the interposition of which he follows again the order which is pursued also by Mark and Luke in company with him.</p>
