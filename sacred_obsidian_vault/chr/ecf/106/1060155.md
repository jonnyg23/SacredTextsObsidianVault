 </p><p class="c39"><a name="vi.v.lix-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="vi.v.lix-p1.1"></a>Chapter LVIII.—Of the Man Who Brought Before Him His Son, Whom the Disciples Were Unable to Heal; And of the Question Concerning the Agreement Between These Three Evangelists Also in the Matter of the Order of Narration Here.</span></p>  			 
 <p class="c22"><a name="vi.v.lix-p2"></a>116. Matthew goes on in the following terms: “And when He was come <a name="fr_1101"></a><a href="#fn_1101"><font size="1">1102</font></a>
  to the multitude, there came to Him a certain man, kneeling down before Him, and saying, Lord, have mercy on my son; for he is lunatic, and sore vexed;” and so on, down to the words, “Howbeit this kind is not cast out but by prayer and fasting.” <a name="fr_1102"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">1103</font></a>
  Both Mark and Luke record this incident, and that, too, in the same order, without any suspicion of a want of harmony. <a name="fr_1103"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">1104</font></a>
 </p>
