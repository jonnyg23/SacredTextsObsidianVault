 <p class="c20"><a name="iii-p2"></a>————————————</p>  		
 <p class="c21"><a name="iii-p3"></a><span class="c13"><a name="iii-p3.1"></a>This</span> volume contains the exegetical and homiletical writings of St. Augustin on the Gospels.</p>  		
 <p class="c22"><a name="iii-p4"></a>The seventh volume will be devoted to his Commentary on the Gospel and First Epistle of John, and the Soliloquies. It will be finished by the 1st of next April.</p>  		
 <p class="c22"><a name="iii-p5"></a>The eighth and last volume is reserved for his Commentary on the Psalms, and will appear in July, 1888.</p>  		
 <p class="c22"><a name="iii-p6"></a>These eight volumes will form the most complete edition of St. Augustins Works in the English language, embracing the Edinburgh and Oxford translations, and several treatises never before translated, with introductions and explanatory notes.</p>  		
 <p class="c22"><a name="iii-p7"></a>Arrangements have been made for the regular issue of the Works of St. Chrysostom according to the terms of the Publishers Prospectus, which so far has been promptly carried out. The favourable reception of the preceding volumes by the public and the press, including some leading theological journals of Europe (such as <i>The Church Quarterly Review</i>, and Harnacks <i><span><a name="iii-p7.1"></a>Theologische Literaturzeitung</span></i>), will encourage the editor and publisher 		  to carry on this Patristic Library with undiminished energy and zeal.</p>  		
 <p class="c23"><a name="iii-p8"></a><span class="c13"><a name="iii-p8.1"></a>Philip</span> <span class="c13"><a name="iii-p8.2"></a>Schaff</span>.</p>  		
 <p class="c22"><a name="iii-p9"></a><span class="c13"><a name="iii-p9.1"></a>New York</span>, December, 1887.</p>
