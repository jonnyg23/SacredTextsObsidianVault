 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.vii.x-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.vii.x-p1.1"></a>Chapter X.—A Cool Retreat.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.vii.x-p2"></a>And a certain one amongst them—a rich man, and possessing a garden of evergreen plants <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">1035</font></a>
 —said:  “Since it is very hot, let us retire for a little from the city to my gardens.”  Accordingly they went forth, and sat down in a place where there were pure streams of cool water, and a green shade of all sorts of trees.  There I sat pleasantly, and the others round about me; and they being silent, instead of a verbal request made to me, showed by their eager looks to me that they required the proof of my assertion.  And therefore I proceeded to speak thus:—</p>
