 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vii.xv.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vii.xv.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 7.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vii.xv.viii-p2"></a>And also a certain woman, Veronica by name, from afar off cried out to the governor:  I was flowing with blood for twelve years; and I touched the fringe of his garment, and immediately the flowing of my blood stopped.  The Jews say:  We have a law, that a woman does not come to bear witness.</p>
