 <p> <a name="v.iii.iii"></a>
 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iii.iii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.iii-p1.1"></a>Chapter III.—True Virgins Prove Themselves Such by Self-Denial, as Does the True Believer by Good Works.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iii.iii-p2"></a>For virgins are a beautiful pattern to believers, and to those who shall believe.  The name alone, indeed, without works, does not introduce into the kingdom of heaven; but, if a man be truly a believer, such an one can be saved.  For, if a person be only called a believer in name, whilst he is not such in works, he cannot possibly be a believer.  “Let no one,” therefore, “lead you astray with the empty words of error.” <a name="fr_304"></a><a href="#fn_304"><font size="1">305</font></a>
   For, merely because a person is called a virgin, if he be destitute of works excellent and comely, and suitable to virginity, he cannot possibly be saved.  For our Lord called such virginity as that “foolish,” as He said in the Gospel; <a name="fr_305"></a><a href="#fn_305"><font size="1">306</font></a>
  and because it had neither oil nor light, it was left outside of the kingdom of heaven, and was shut out from the joy of the bridegroom, and was reckoned with His enemies.  For such persons as these “have the appearance only of the fear of God, but the power of it they deny.” <a name="fr_306"></a><a href="#fn_306"><font size="1">307</font></a>
   For they “think with themselves that they are something, whilst they are nothing, and are deceived.  But let every one constantly try <a name="fr_307"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">308</font></a>
  his works,” <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">309</font></a>
  and know himself; for empty worship does he offer, whosoever he be that makes profession of virginity and <a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a> <a name="v.iii.iii-Page_56"></a>sanctity, “and denies its power.”  For virginity of such a kind is impure, and disowned by all good works.  For “every tree whatsoever is known from its fruits.” <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">310</font></a>
   “See that thou understand <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_310"><font size="1">311</font></a>
  what I say:  God will give thee understanding.” <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_311"><font size="1">312</font></a>
   For whosoever engages before God to preserve sanctity must be girded with all the holy power of God.  And, if with true fear <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_312"><font size="1">313</font></a>
  he crucify his body, he for the sake of the fear of God excuses himself from that word <i>in</i> which <i>the Scripture</i> <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">314</font></a>
 has said:  “Be fruitful, and multiply,” <a name="fr_314"></a><a href="#fn_314"><font size="1">315</font></a>
  and <i>shuns</i> all the display, and care, and sensuality, <a name="fr_315"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">316</font></a>
  and fascination of this world, and its revelries and its drunkenness, and all its luxury and ease, and withdraws from the entire life of <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">317</font></a>
  this world, and from its snares, and nets, and hindrances; and, whilst thou walkest <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">318</font></a>
  upon the earth, be zealous that thy work and thy business be in heaven.</p>
