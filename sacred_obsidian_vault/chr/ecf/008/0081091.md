 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xx.xvii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xx.xvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVII.—The Impious See True Dreams and Visions.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xx.xvii-p2"></a>“But it is manifest that the impious see true visions and dreams, and I can prove it from Scripture.  Finally, then, it is written in the law, how Abimelech, who was impious, wished to defile the wife of just Abraham by intercourse, and how he heard the commandment from God in his sleep, as the Scripture saith, not to touch her, <a name="fr_1352"></a><a href="#fn_1352"><font size="1">1353</font></a>
  because she was dwelling with her husband.  Pharaoh, also an impious man, saw a dream in regard to the fulness and thinness of the ears of corn, <a name="fr_1353"></a><a href="#fn_1353"><font size="1">1354</font></a>
  to whom Joseph said, when he gave the interpretation, that the dream had come from God. <a name="fr_1354"></a><a href="#fn_1354"><font size="1">1355</font></a>
   Nebuchadnezzar, who worshipped images, and ordered those who worshipped God to be cast into fire, saw a dream <a name="fr_1355"></a><a href="#fn_1355"><font size="1">1356</font></a>
  extending over the whole age of the world. <a name="fr_1356"></a><a href="#fn_1356"><font size="1">1357</font></a>
   And let no one say, No one who is impious sees a vision when awake.  That is false.  Nebuchadnezzar himself, having ordered three men to be cast into fire, saw a fourth when he looked into the furnace, and said, I see the fourth as the Son of God. <a name="fr_1357"></a><a href="#fn_1357"><font size="1">1358</font></a>
   And nevertheless, though they saw apparitions, visions, and dreams, they were impious.  Thus, we cannot infer with absolute certainty that the man who has seen visions, and dreams, and apparitions, is undoubtedly pious.  For in the case of the pious man, the truth gushes up natural and pure <a name="fr_1358"></a><a href="#fn_1358"><font size="1">1359</font></a>
  in his mind, not worked up through dreams, but granted to the good through intelligence.</p>
