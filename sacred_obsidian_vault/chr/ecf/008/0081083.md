 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xx.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xx.ix-p1.1"></a>Chapter IX.—God the Centre or Heart of the Universe.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xx.ix-p2"></a>“One, then, is the God who truly exists, who presides in a superior shape, being the heart of that which is above and that which is below twice, <a name="fr_1332"></a><a href="#fn_1332"><font size="1">1333</font></a>
  which sends forth from Him as from a centre the life-giving and incorporeal power; the whole universe with the stars and regions <a name="fr_1333"></a><a href="#fn_1333"><font size="1">1334</font></a>
  of the heaven, the air, the fire, and if anything else exists, is proved to be a substance infinite in height, boundless in depth, immeasurable in breadth, extending the life-giving and wise nature from Him over three infinites. <a name="fr_1334"></a><a href="#fn_1334"><font size="1">1335</font></a>
   It must be, therefore, that this infinite which proceeds from Him on every side exists, <a name="fr_1335"></a><a href="#fn_1335"><font size="1">1336</font></a>
  having as its heart Him who is above all, and who thus possesses figure; for wherever He be, He is as it were in the centre of the infinite, being the limit of the universe.  And the extensions taking their rise with Him, possess the nature of six infinites; of whom the one taking its rise with Him penetrates <a name="fr_1336"></a><a href="#fn_1336"><font size="1">1337</font></a>
  into the height above, another into the depth below, another to the right hand, another to the left, another in front, and another behind; to whom He Himself, looking as to a number that is equal on every side, <a name="fr_1337"></a><a href="#fn_1337"><font size="1">1338</font></a>
  completes the world in six temporal intervals, <a name="fr_1338"></a><a href="#fn_1338"><font size="1">1339</font></a>
  Himself being the rest, <a name="fr_1339"></a><a href="#fn_1339"><font size="1">1340</font></a>
  and having the infinite age to come as His image, being the beginning and the end.  For in Him the six infinites end, and from Him they receive their extension to infinity.</p>
