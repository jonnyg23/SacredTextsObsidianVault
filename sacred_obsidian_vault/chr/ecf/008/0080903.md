 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xii.xx-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xii.xx-p1.1"></a>Chapter XX.—“Not Almost, But Altogether Such as I Am.”</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xii.xx-p2"></a>“Do not then suppose that we do not fear demons on this account, that we are of a different nature <i>from you</i>.  For we are of the same nature, but not of the same worship.  Wherefore, being not only much but altogether superior to you, we do not grudge you becoming such <i>as we are</i>; but, on the other hand, counsel you, knowing that all these <i>demons</i> beyond measure honour and fear those who are reconciled to God.”</p>
