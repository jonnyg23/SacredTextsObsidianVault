 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLIX.—Reductio <span class="c9"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlix-p1.2"></a>a.d.</span> Absurdum.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlix-p2"></a>Then Peter answered:  “You have presented us with a matter that can easily be answered.  However, listen, how God is ignorant of nothing, but even foreknows.  But first answer me what I ask of you.  He who wrote the Bible, and told how the world was made, and said that God does not foreknow, was he a man or not?”  Then I said:  “He was a man.”  Then Peter answered:  “How, then, was it possible for him, being a man, to know assuredly how the world was made, and that God does not foreknow?”</p>
