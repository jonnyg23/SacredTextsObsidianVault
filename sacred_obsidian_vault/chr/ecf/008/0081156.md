 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xxiii.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xxiii.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—Arrival of Appion and Annubion.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xxiii.xi-p2"></a>And as we were going to take our meals, <a name="fr_1519"></a><a href="#fn_1519"><font size="1">1520</font></a>
  some one ran in and said:  “Appion Pleistonices has just come with Annubion from Antioch, and he is lodging with Simon.”  And my father hearing this, and rejoicing, said to Peter:  “If you permit me, I shall go to salute Appion and <a name="page_343"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 343</font></a> <a name="vi.iv.xxiii.xi-Page_343"></a>Annubion, who have been my friends from childhood.  For perchance I shall persuade Annubion to discuss genesis with Clement.”  And Peter said:  “I permit you, and I praise you for fulfilling the duties of a friend.  But now consider how in the providence of God there come together from all quarters considerations which contribute to your full assurance, rendering the harmony complete.  But I say this because the arrival of Annubion happens advantageously for you.”  And my father:  “In truth, I see that this is the case.”  And saying this, he went to Simon.</p>
