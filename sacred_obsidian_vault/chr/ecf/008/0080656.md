 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.v.xxiii-p1"></a><span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.v.xxiii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIII.—Simon a Disciple of the Baptist.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.v.xxiii-p2"></a>“But that he came to deal with the doctrines of religion happened on this wise.  There was one John, a day-baptist, <a name="fr_933"></a><a href="#fn_933"><font size="1">934</font></a>
  who was also, according to the method of combination, the forerunner of our Lord Jesus; and as the Lord had twelve apostles, bearing the number of the twelve months of the sun, so also he, <i>John</i>, had thirty chief men, fulfilling the monthly reckoning of the moon, in which number was a certain woman called Helena, <a name="fr_934"></a><a href="#fn_934"><font size="1">935</font></a>
  that not even this might be without a dispensational significance.  For a woman, being half a man, made up the imperfect number of the triacontad; as also in the case of the moon, whose revolution does not make the complete course of the month. <a name="fr_935"></a><a href="#fn_935"><font size="1">936</font></a>
