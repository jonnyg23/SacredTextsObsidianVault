 <p> <a name="v.iii.viii"></a>
 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iii.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Virgins, by the Laying Aside of All Carnal Affection, are Imitators of God.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iii.viii-p2"></a>For, if a man be only in name called holy, he is not holy; but he must be holy in everything:  in his body and in his spirit.  And those who are virgins rejoice at all times in becoming like God and His Christ, and are imitators of them.  For in those that are such there is not “the mind <a name="fr_351"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">352</font></a>
  of the flesh.”  In those who are truly believers, and “in whom the Spirit of Christ dwells” <a name="fr_352"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">353</font></a>
 —in them “the mind of the flesh” cannot be:  which is fornication, uncleanness, wantonness; idolatry, <a name="fr_353"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">354</font></a>
  sorcery; enmity, jealousy, rivalry, wrath, disputes, dissensions, ill-will; drunkenness, revelry; buffoonery, foolish talking, boisterous laughter; backbiting, insinuations; bitterness, rage; clamour, abuse, insolence of speech; malice, inventing of evil, falsehood; talkativeness, <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_354"><font size="1">355</font></a>
  babbling; <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">356</font></a>
  threatenings, gnashing of teeth, readiness to accuse, <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">357</font></a>
  jarring, <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">358</font></a>
  disdainings, blows; perversions <i>of the right,</i> <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">359</font></a>
 laxness <i>in judgment</i>; haughtiness, arrogance, ostentation, pompousness, <i>boasting</i> of family, of beauty, of position, of wealth, of an arm of flesh; <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">360</font></a>
  quarrelsomeness, injustice, <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">361</font></a>
  eagerness for victory; hatred, anger, envy, perfidy, retaliation; <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">362</font></a>
  debauchery, gluttony, “overreaching (which is idolatry),” <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">363</font></a>
  “the love of money (which is the root of all evils);” <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">364</font></a>
  love of display, vainglory, love of rule, assumption, pride (which is called death, and which “God fights against”). <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">365</font></a>
   Every man with whom are these and such like things—every such man is of the flesh.  For, “he that is born of the flesh is flesh; and he that is of the earth speaketh of the earth,” <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">366</font></a>
  and his thoughts are of the earth.  And “the mind of the flesh is enmity towards God.  For it does not submit itself to the law of God; for it cannot <i>do so</i>,” <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">367</font></a>
  because it is in the flesh, “in which dwells no good,” <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">368</font></a>
  because the Spirit of God is <a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a> <a name="v.iii.viii-Page_58"></a>not in it.  For this cause justly does the Scripture say regarding such a generation as this:  “My Spirit shall not dwell in men for ever, because they are flesh.” <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">369</font></a>
   “Whosoever, therefore, has not the Spirit of God in him, is none of His:” <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">370</font></a>
   as it is written, “The Spirit of God departed from Saul, and an evil spirit troubled him, which was sent upon him from God.” <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">371</font></a>
 </p>
