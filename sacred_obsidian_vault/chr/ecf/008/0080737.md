 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.vi.l-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.vi.l-p1.1"></a>Chapter L.—His Teaching Concerning the Scriptures.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.vi.l-p2"></a>Then Peter:  “As to the mixture of truth with falsehood, <a name="fr_992"></a><a href="#fn_992"><font size="1">993</font></a>
  I remember that on one occasion He, finding fault with the Sadducees, said, Wherefore ye do err, not knowing the true things of the Scriptures; and on this account ye are ignorant of the power of God. <a name="fr_993"></a><a href="#fn_993"><font size="1">994</font></a>
   But if He cast up to them that they knew not the true things of the Scriptures, it is manifest that there are false things in them.  And also, inasmuch as He said, Be ye prudent money-changers, <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_994"><font size="1">995</font></a>
  it is because there are genuine and spurious words.  And whereas He said, Wherefore do ye not <a name="page_248"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 248</font></a> <a name="vi.iv.vi.l-Page_248"></a>perceive that which is reasonable in the Scriptures?  He makes the understanding of him stronger who voluntarily judges soundly.</p>
