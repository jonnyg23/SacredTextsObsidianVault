 </p><p class="c34"><a name="ix.v.vii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="ix.v.vii-p1.1"></a>VII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="ix.v.vii-p2"></a><i>From the homily composed by the holy Mar Jacob, the teacher, on the fall of idols.</i> <a name="fr_2954"></a><a href="#fn_2954"><font size="1">2955</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="ix.v.vii-p4"></a>To Edessa he made his journey, and found in it a great work:</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p5"></a>For the king was become a labourer for the church, and was building it.</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p6"></a>The apostle Addæus stood in it like a builder,</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p7"></a>And King Abgar laid aside his diadem and builded with him.</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p8"></a>When apostle and king concurred the one with the other,</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p9"></a>What idol must not fall before them?</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p10"></a>Satan fled to the land of Babylon from the disciples,</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p11"></a>And the tale of the crucifixion had got before him to the country of the Chaldeans.</p> 
 <p class="c57"><a name="ix.v.vii-p12"></a>He said, when they were making sport of the signs of the Zodiac, that he was nothing.</p>
