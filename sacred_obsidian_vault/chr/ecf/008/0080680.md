 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlvii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLVII.—A Pertinent Question.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.v.xlvii-p2"></a>When I Clement heard this, I said, “Truly, this is a godliness; truly this is piety.”  And again I said:  “I would learn, therefore, why the Bible has written anything of this sort?  For I remember that you said that it was for the con<a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 238</font></a> <a name="vi.iv.v.xlvii-Page_238"></a>viction of those who should dare to believe anything that was spoken against God.  But since you permit us, we venture to ask, at your command:  If any one, most beloved Peter, should choose to say to us, The Scriptures are true, although to you the things spoken against God seem to be false, how should we answer him?”</p>
