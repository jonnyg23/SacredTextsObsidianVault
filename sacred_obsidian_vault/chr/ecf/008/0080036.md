 <p> <a name="v.iii.xi"></a>
 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iii.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—Perniciousness of Idleness; Warning Against the Empty Longing to Be Teachers; Advice About Teaching and the Use of Divine Gifts.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iii.xi-p2"></a>Such are the ways of all those who do not work, but go hunting for tales, and think to themselves that this is profitable and right. <a name="fr_384"></a><a href="#fn_384"><font size="1">385</font></a>
   For such persons are like those idle and prating widows “who go wandering about <a name="fr_385"></a><a href="#fn_385"><font size="1">386</font></a>
  among houses” <a name="fr_386"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">387</font></a>
  with their prating, and hunt for idle tales, and carry them from house to house with much exaggeration, without fear of God.  And besides all this, barefaced men as they are, <a name="fr_387"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">388</font></a>
  under pretence of teaching, they set forth a variety of doctrines.  And would that they taught the doctrines of truth!  But it is this which is <i>so</i> disquieting, that they understand not what they mean, and assert that which is not <i>true</i>:  because they wish to be teachers, and to display themselves as skil<a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a> <a name="v.iii.xi-Page_59"></a>ful in speaking; because they traffic in iniquity in the name of Christ—which it is not right for the servants of God <i>to do</i>.  And they hearken not to that which the Scripture has said:  “Let not many be teachers among you, my brethren, and be not all of you prophets.” <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="#fn_388"><font size="1">389</font></a>
   For “he who does not transgress in word is a perfect man, able to keep down and subjugate his whole body.” <a name="fr_389"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">390</font></a>
   And, “If a man speak, let him speak in the words <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">391</font></a>
  of God.” <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">392</font></a>
   And, “If there is in thee understanding, give an answer to thy brother but if not, put thy hand on thy mouth.” <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">393</font></a>
   For, “at one time <i>it is proper</i> to keep silence, and at another thee to speak.” <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">394</font></a>
   And again it says “When a man speaks in season, it is honourable <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">395</font></a>
  to him.” <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">396</font></a>
   And again it says:  “Let your speech be seasoned with grace.  For it is required of a man to know how to give an answer to every one in season.” <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">397</font></a>
   For “he that utters whatsoever comes to his mouth, that man produces strife; and he that utters a superfluity of words increases vexation; and he that is hasty with his lips falls into evil.  For because of the unruliness of the tongue cometh anger; but the perfect man keeps watch over his tongue, and loves his souls life.” <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">398</font></a>
   For these are they “who by good words and fair speeches lead astray the hearts of the simple, and, while offering them blessings, lead them astray.” <a name="fr_398"></a><a href="#fn_398"><font size="1">399</font></a>
   Let us, therefore, fear the judgment which awaits teachers.  For a severe judgment will those teachers receive “who teach, but do not,” <a name="fr_399"></a><a href="#fn_399"><font size="1">400</font></a>
  and those who take <i>upon them</i> the name of Christ falsely, and say:  We teach the truth, and <i>yet</i> go wandering about idly, and exalt themselves, and make their boast” in the mind of the flesh.” <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_400"><font size="1">401</font></a>
   These, moreover, are like “the blind man who leads the blind man, and they both fall into the ditch.” <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_401"><font size="1">402</font></a>
   And they will receive judgment, because in their talkativeness and their frivolous teaching they teach natural <a name="fr_402"></a><a href="#fn_402"><font size="1">403</font></a>
  wisdom and the “frivolous error of the plausible words of the wisdom of men,” <a name="fr_403"></a><a href="#fn_403"><font size="1">404</font></a>
  “according to the will of the prince of the dominion of the air, and of the spirit which works in those men who will not obey, according to the training of this world, and not according to the doctrine of Christ.” <a name="fr_404"></a><a href="#fn_404"><font size="1">405</font></a>
   But if thou hast received “the word of knowledge, or the word of instruction, or of prophecy,” <a name="fr_405"></a><a href="#fn_405"><font size="1">406</font></a>
  blessed be God, “who helps every man without grudging—that God who gives to every man and does not upbraid <i>him</i>.” <a name="fr_406"></a><a href="#fn_406"><font size="1">407</font></a>
   With the gift, therefore, which thou hast received from our Lord, serve <i>thy</i> spiritual brethren, the prophets who know that the words which thou speakest are <i>those</i> of our Lord; and declare the gift which thou hast received in the Church for the edification of the brethren in Christ (for good and excellent are those things which help the men of God), if so be that they are truly with thee. <a name="fr_407"></a><a href="#fn_407"><font size="1">408</font></a>
 </p>
