 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—</span><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026">Gen. I. 26</a><a name="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1.2"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1.3"></a>Appealed to by Simon.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xix.xi-p2"></a>And Simon said:  “Since I see that you frequently speak of the God who created you, learn from me how you are impious even to him.  For there are evidently two who created, as the Scripture says: <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">1289</font></a>
   And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness.  Now let us make, implies two or more; certainly not one only.”</p>
