 <p> <a name="v.iv.vi"></a>
 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iv.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.vi-p1.1"></a>Chapter VI.—How Christians Should Behave Themselves Among Heathens.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iv.vi-p2"></a>If, moreover, it chance that we go to a place in which there are no Christians, and it be im<a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a> <a name="v.iv.vi-Page_63"></a>portant for us to stay there a few days, let us be “wise as serpents, and harmless as doves;” <a name="fr_457"></a><a href="#fn_457"><font size="1">458</font></a>
  and let us “not be as the foolish, but as the wise,” <a name="fr_458"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">459</font></a>
  in all the <i>self</i>-restraint of the fear of God, that God may be glorified in everything through our Lord Jesus Christ, through our chaste and holy behaviour.  For, “whether we eat, or drink, or do anything else, let us do it as for the glory of God.” <a name="fr_459"></a><a href="#fn_459"><font size="1">460</font></a>
   Let “all those who see us acknowledge that we are a blessed seed,” <a name="fr_460"></a><a href="#fn_460"><font size="1">461</font></a>
  “sons of the living God,” <a name="fr_461"></a><a href="#fn_461"><font size="1">462</font></a>
  in everything—in all <i>our</i> words in shamefastness, in purity, in humility, forasmuch as we do not copy the heathen in anything, nor are <i>as</i> believers like <i>other</i> men, but in everything are estranged from the wicked.  And we “do not cast that which is holy before dogs, nor pearls before swine;” <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_462"><font size="1">463</font></a>
  but with all possible <i>self</i>-restraint, and with all discretion, and with all fear of God, and with earnestness of mind we praise God.  For we do not minister where heathens are drinking and blaspheming in their feasts with words of impurity, because of their wickedness. <a name="fr_463"></a><a href="#fn_463"><font size="1">464</font></a>
   Therefore do we not sing <i>psalms</i> to the heathens, nor do we read to them the Scriptures, that we may not be like <i>common</i> singers, either those who play on the lyre, <a name="fr_464"></a><a href="#fn_464"><font size="1">465</font></a>
  or those who sing with the voice, or <i>like</i> soothsayers, as many are, who follow these practices and do these things, that they may sate themselves with a paltry mouthful of bread, and who, for the sake of a sorry cup of wine, go <i>about</i> “singing the songs of the Lord in the strange land” <a name="fr_465"></a><a href="#fn_465"><font size="1">466</font></a>
  of the heathen, and doing what is not right.  Do not so, my brethren; we beseech you, my brethren, let not these deeds be done among you; but put away those who choose thus to behave themselves with infamy and disgrace.  It is not proper, my brethren, that these things should be so.  But we beseech you, brethren in righteousness, that these things be so <i>done</i> with you as with us, as for a pattern of believers, and of those who shall believe.  Let us be of the flock of Christ, in all righteousness, and in all holy and unblemished conduct, behaving ourselves with uprightness and sanctity, as is right for believers, and observing those things which are praiseworthy, and pure, and holy, and honourable, and noble; and do ye promote <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">467</font></a>
  all those things which are profitable.  For ye are “our joy, and our crown,” and our hope, and our life, “if so be that ye stand in the Lord.” <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">468</font></a>
   So be it! <a name="fr_468"></a><a href="#fn_468"><font size="1">469</font></a>
 </p>
