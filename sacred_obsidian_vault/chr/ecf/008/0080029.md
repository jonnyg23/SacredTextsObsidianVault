 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iii.iv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.iv-p1.1"></a>Chapter IV.—Continuation of the Remarks on Self-Denial; Object and Reward of True Virgins.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iii.iv-p2"></a>For he who covets for himself these things <i>so</i> great and excellent, withdraws and severs himself on this account from all the world, that he may go <i>and</i> live a life divine and heavenly, like the holy angels, in work pure and holy, and “in the holiness <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">319</font></a>
  of the Spirit of God,” <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">320</font></a>
  and that he may serve God Almighty through Jesus Christ for the sake of the kingdom of heaven.  On this account he severs himself from all the appetites of the body.  And not only does he excuse himself from this <i>command</i>, “Be fruitful, and multiply,” but he longs for the “hope promised” and prepared “and laid up in heaven” <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">321</font></a>
  by God, who has declared with His mouth, and He does not lie, that it is “better than sons and daughters,” <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">322</font></a>
  and that He will give to virgins a notable place in the house of God, which is <i>something</i> “better than sons and daughters,” and better than <i>the place of</i> those who have passed a wedded life in sanctity, and whose “bed has not been defiled.” <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">323</font></a>
   For God will give to virgins the kingdom of heaven, as to the holy angels, by reason of this great and noble profession.</p>
