 </p><p class="c33"><a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-p1"></a>  <span class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-p1.1"></a>Homily XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-p2"></a><span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-p2.1"></a>Chapter I.—Peter Wishes to Convert Faustus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-p3"></a><span class="c9"><a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-p3.1"></a>At</span> break of day our father, with our mother and his three sons, entered the place where Peter was, and accosting him, sat down.  Then we also did the same at his request; and Peter looking at our father, said: <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1217"><font size="1">1218</font></a>
   “I am anxious that you should become of the same mind as your wife and children, in order that here you may live along with them, and in the other world, <a name="fr_1218"></a><a href="#fn_1218"><font size="1">1219</font></a>
  after the separation of the soul from the body, you will continue to be with them free from sorrow.  For does it not grieve you exceedingly that you should not associate with each other?”  And my <a name="page_309"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 309</font></a> <a name="vi.iv.xviii.i-Page_309"></a>father said:  “Most assuredly.”  And Peter said:  “If, then, separation from each other here gives you pain, and if without doubt the penalty awaits you that after death you should not be with each other, how much greater will your grief be that you, a wise man, should be separated from your own family on account of your opinions?  They too, must <a name="fr_1219"></a><a href="#fn_1219"><font size="1">1220</font></a>
  feel the more distressed from the consciousness that eternal punishment awaits you because you entertain different opinions from theirs, and deny the established truth.” <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1220"><font size="1">1221</font></a>
 </p>
