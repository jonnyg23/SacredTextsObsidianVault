 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iv.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—Admonitory History of the Incestuous Children of David.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iv.xi-p2"></a>Hast thou not read concerning Amnon and Tamar, the children of David?  This Amnon conceived a passion for his sister, and humbled her, and did not spare her, because he longed for her with a shameful passion; and he proved wicked and profligate because of his constant intercourse with her, without the fear of God, and he “wrought uncleanness in Israel.” <a name="fr_482"></a><a href="#fn_482"><font size="1">483</font></a>
   Therefore, it is not proper for us, nor right for us, to associate with sisters, <i>indulging</i> in laughter and looseness; but <i>we ought to behave towards them</i> with all chasteness and purity, and in the fear of the Lord.</p>
