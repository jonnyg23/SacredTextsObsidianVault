    <h2 align="CENTER"><a></a> A Declaration of Faith.<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P249_29601">1</a></sup> </h2>  
 <p>------------</p>  
 <p>There is one God, the Father of the living Word, <i>who is His</i> subsistent Wisdom and Power and Eternal Image:<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P252_29970">2</a></sup> perfect Begetter of the perfect <i>Begotten</i>, Father of the only-begotten Son. There is one Lord, Only of the Only,<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P253_30107">3</a></sup> God of God, Image and Likeness of Deity, Efficient Word,<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P254_30188">4</a></sup> Wisdom comprehensive<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P255_30230">5</a></sup> of the constitution of all things, and Power formative<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P256_30301">6</a></sup> of the whole creation, true Son of true Father, Invisible of Invisible, and Incorruptible of Incorruptible, and Immortal of Immortal and Eternal of Eternal.<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P257_30472">7</a></sup> And there is One Holy Spirit, having His subsistence<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P258_30549">8</a></sup> from God, and being made manifest<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P259_30596">9</a></sup> by the Son, to wit to men:<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P260_30637">10</a></sup> Image<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P261_30758">11</a></sup> of the Son, <i>Perfect</i> Image of the Perfect;<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P262_30814">12</a></sup> Life, the Cause of the living; Holy Fount; Sanctity, the Supplier, <i>or Leader</i>,<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P263_31209">13</a></sup> of Sanctification; in whom is manifested God the Father, who is above all and in all, and God the Son, who is through all. There is a perfect Trinity, in glory and eternity and sovereignty, neither divided nor estranged.<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P264_31445">14</a></sup> Wherefore there is nothing either created or in servitude<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P265_31573">15</a></sup> in the Trinity;<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P266_31603">16</a></sup> nor anything superinduced,<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P267_31937">17</a></sup> as if at some former period it was non-existent, and at some later period it was introduced. And thus neither was the Son ever wanting to the Father, nor the Spirit to the Son;<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P268_32133">18</a></sup> but without variation and without change, the same Trinity <i>abideth</i> ever.<sup><a href="footnote/fn2.htm#P269_32532">19</a></sup> </p>
