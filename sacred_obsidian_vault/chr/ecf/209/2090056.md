 </p><p class="c33"><a name="iii.iv.ii.v-p1"></a> <span class="c12"><a name="iii.iv.ii.v-p1.1"></a>Chapter V</span>.—<i>Concerning the visible creation.</i></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.iv.ii.v-p2"></a>Our God Himself, Whom we glorify as Three in One, <i>created the heaven and the earth and all that they contain</i> <a name="fr_1681"></a><a href="#fn_1681"><font size="1">1682</font></a>
 , and brought all things out of nothing into being:  some He made out of no pre-existing basis of matter, such as heaven, earth, air, fire, water:  and the rest out of these elements that He had created, such as living creatures, plants, seeds. For these are made up of earth, and water, and air, and fire, at the bidding of the Creator.</p>
