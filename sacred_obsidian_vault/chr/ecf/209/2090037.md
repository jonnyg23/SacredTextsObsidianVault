 </p><p class="c17"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p1"></a><span class="c16"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p1.1"></a>An Exact Exposition of the Orthodox Faith.</span></p> 
 <p class="c2"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p2"></a> ————————————</p> 
 <p class="c26"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p3"></a><span class="c16"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p3.1"></a>Book I.</span></p> 
 <p class="c33"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p4"></a><span class="c12"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p4.1"></a>Chapter I</span>.—<i>That the Deity is incomprehensible, and that we ought not to pry into and meddle with the things which have not been delivered to us by the holy Prophets, and Apostles, and Evangelists.</i></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p5"></a><i>No one hath seen God at any time; the Only-begotten Son, which is in the bosom of the Father, He hath declared Him</i> <a name="fr_1405"></a><a href="#fn_1405"><font size="1">1406</font></a>
 . The Deity, therefore, is ineffable and incomprehensible. <i>For no one knoweth the Father, save the Son, nor the Son, save the Father</i> <a name="fr_1406"></a><a href="#fn_1406"><font size="1">1407</font></a>
 . And the Holy Spirit, too, so knows the things of God as the spirit of the man knows the things that are in him <a name="fr_1407"></a><a href="#fn_1407"><font size="1">1408</font></a>
 . Moreover, after the first and blessed nature no one, not of men only, but even of supramundane powers, and the Cherubim, I say, and Seraphim themselves, has ever known God, save he to whom He revealed Himself.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.iv.i.i-p9"></a>God, however, did not leave us in absolute ignorance. For the knowledge of Gods existence has been implanted by Him in all by nature. This creation, too, and its maintenance, and its government, proclaim the majesty of the Divine nature <a name="fr_1408"></a><a href="#fn_1408"><font size="1">1409</font></a>
 . Moreover, by the Law and the Prophets <a name="fr_1409"></a><a href="#fn_1409"><font size="1">1410</font></a>
  in former times and afterwards by His Only-begotten Son, our Lord and God and Saviour Jesus Christ, He disclosed to us the knowledge of Himself as that was possible for us. All things, therefore, that have been delivered to us by Law and Prophets and Apostles and Evangelists we receive, and know, and honour <a name="fr_1410"></a><a href="#fn_1410"><font size="1">1411</font></a>
 , seeking for nothing beyond these. For God, being good, is the cause of all good, subject neither to envy nor to any passion <a name="fr_1411"></a><a href="#fn_1411"><font size="1">1412</font></a>
 . For envy is far removed from the Divine nature, which is both passionless and only good. As knowing all things, therefore, and providing for what is profitable for each, He revealed that which it was to our profit to know; but what we were unable <a name="fr_1412"></a><a href="#fn_1412"><font size="1">1413</font></a>
  to bear He kept secret. With these things let us be satisfied, and let us abide by them, not removing everlasting boundaries, nor overpassing the divine tradition <a name="fr_1413"></a><a href="#fn_1413"><font size="1">1414</font></a>
 .</p>
