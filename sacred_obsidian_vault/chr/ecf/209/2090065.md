 </p><p class="c33"><a name="iii.iv.ii.xiv-p1"></a> <span class="c12"><a name="iii.iv.ii.xiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIV</span>.—<i>Concerning Pain.</i></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.iv.ii.xiv-p2"></a>There are four varieties of pain, viz., anguish <a name="fr_1822"></a><a href="#fn_1822"><font size="1">1823</font></a>
 , grief <a name="fr_1823"></a><a href="#fn_1823"><font size="1">1824</font></a>
 , envy, pity. Anguish is pain without utterance:  grief is pain that is heavy to bear like a burden:  envy is pain over the good fortune of others:  pity is pain over the evil fortune of others.</p>
