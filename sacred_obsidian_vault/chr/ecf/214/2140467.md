 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p1.1"></a>Canon LXXXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p2.1"></a>No</span> one may give the Eucharist to the bodies of the dead; for it is written “Take and eat.”  But the bodies of the dead can neither “take” nor “eat.”</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p5"></a>The Sacraments must not be given to a dead body.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxiv-p6"></a>This is canon iv. of the Council of Hippo, in the year 393.  (<i>Vide</i> Hefele, Vol. II., p. 397.)  The earlier canon includes baptism also, in its prohibition.  This is canons xviii. and xx. of the African code, according to the Greek numbering.</p>
