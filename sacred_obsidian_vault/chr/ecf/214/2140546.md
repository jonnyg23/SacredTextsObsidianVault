 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p1.1"></a>Canon XXII.  (Greek xxv.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p2"></a>That bishops or other clergymen shall give nothing to those who are not Catholics.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p3.1"></a>And</span> that to those who are not Catholic Christians, even if they be blood relations, neither bishops nor clergymen shall give anything at all by way of donation of their possessions.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p6"></a>Bishops and clergymen shall give nothing of their goods to heretics, nor confer aught upon them even if they be their relatives.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p7"></a>This is Canon xiv. of Hippo, 393.</p>
