 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p1.1"></a>Canon LIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p2.1"></a>Christians</span>, when they attend weddings, must not join in wanton dances, but modestly dine or breakfast, as is becoming to Christians.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p3"></a><a name="page_157"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 157</font></a> <a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-Page_157"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p5"></a>It is unsuitable to dance or leap at weddings.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p6.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lviii-p7"></a>This canon does not call for explanation but for reflexion, and greatly it is to be desired that it should be observed by Christians, and that through like improprieties, wedding-days, which should be days of holy joy and blessing, be not turned, even to the bride and groom themselves, into days of cursing.  Moreover the Synod of Trent admonishes bishops (<i>Sess</i>. xxiv., <i>De Reform. Mat</i>., cap. x.) to take care that at weddings there be only that which is modest and proper.</p>
