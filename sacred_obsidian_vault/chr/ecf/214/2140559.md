 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p1.1"></a>Canon XXXV.  (Greek xxxviii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p2"></a>That bishops or clergymen should not easily set free their sons.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p3.1"></a>That</span> bishops or clerics should not easily let their children pass out of their power; unless they were secure of their morals and age, that their own sins may pertain to them.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p6"></a>Bishops and clergy shall not set their children free until their morals are established.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p7"></a>This canon is Canon xiij. of the Synod of Hippo <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 393.</p>
