 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.lx"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p1.1"></a>Canon LV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p2.1"></a>Neither</span> members of the priesthood nor of the clergy, nor yet laymen, may club together for drinking entertainments.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p5"></a>Neither a layman nor a cleric shall celebrate a club feast.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p6"></a>These meals, the expenses of which were defrayed by a number clubbing together and sharing the cost, were called “symbola” by Isidore, and by Melinus and Crabbe “comissalia,” although the more ordinary form is “commensalia” or “comessalia.”  <i>Cf</i>. Ducange <i>Gloss</i>., <i>s.v.</i> Commensalia <i>and</i> Confertum.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lx-p7"></a>This Canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Dist. XLIV., c. x. (Isidores version), and c. xij., (Martin of Bragas version).</p>
