 </p><p class="c14"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p1.1"></a>Canon XCV.  (Greek xcviii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p2"></a>An universal council to be held only when necessary.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p3.1"></a>It</span> seemed good that there should be no more the yearly necessity of fatiguing the brethren; but as often as common cause, that is of the whole of Africa, demands, that letters shall be given on every side to that see in this matter, that a synod should be gathered in that province, where the desirability of it induces; but let the causes which are not of general interest be judged in their own provinces.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p4"></a><a name="page_491"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 491</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-Page_491"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XCV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p6"></a>When general necessity so urges, letters are to be sent to the chief see, and a synod held in a convenient place.  But let ordinary causes be settled in their own provinces.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p7"></a>This canon is Canon j. of the Synod of Carthage, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 407.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p8.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xcvi-p9"></a>This canon is a tacit revocation of that clause for annual synods in the 18th canon, which was made in a former council.</p>
