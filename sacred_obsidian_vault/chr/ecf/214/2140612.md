 </p><p class="c14"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p1.1"></a>Canon LXXXVIII.  (Greek xci.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p2"></a>Of Maximian, the bishop.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p3.1"></a>But</span> in the case of Maximian of Vagai <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">467</font></a>
  it seemed good that letters be sent from the council both to him and to his people; that he should vacate the bishoprick, and that they should request another to be appointed for them.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p5.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p6.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p7"></a>Let Maximian of Bagai be expelled from his church, and another be set in his room.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p8"></a>This canon is remaining part of Canon ij., of the Synod of Milevis, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxix-p8.1"></a>a.d.</span> 402.</p>
