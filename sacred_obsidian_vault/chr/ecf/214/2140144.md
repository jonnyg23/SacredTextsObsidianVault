 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p1.1"></a>Canon VII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p2.1"></a>No</span> stranger shall be received without letters pacifical.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c30"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon VII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p5"></a>A traveller having no letter pacific with him is not to be received.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p6"></a>Compare the Apostolic Canon number XXXIII.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p7"></a>For a discussion of the Letters styled <i>pacifici</i>, see notes on next canon.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vi.iv.vii-p8"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Dist. lxxi., c. ix. in Isidores version.  The Roman Correctors note that Dionysius must have had a different reading from the Greek we know.</p>
