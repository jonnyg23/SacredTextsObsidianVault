 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p1.1"></a>Canon XXXI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p2.1"></a>It</span> is not lawful to make marriages with all [sorts of] heretics, nor to give our sons and daughters to them; but rather to take of them, if they promise to become Christians.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p5"></a>It is not right to give children in marriage to heretics, but they should be received if they promise to become Christians.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p6.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvi-p7"></a>By this canon the faithful are forbidden to contract marriage with heretics or to join their children in such; for, as both Balsamon and Zonaras remark, “they imbue them with their errors, and lead them to embrace their own perverse opinions.”</p>
