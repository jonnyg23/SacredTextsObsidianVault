 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p1"></a> <a name="page_449"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 449</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-Page_449"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p1.1"></a>Canon XIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p2"></a>That one of the bishops of Tripoli should come as legate, and that a presbyter might be heard there by five bishops.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p3.1"></a>It</span> also seemed good that one bishop from Tripoli, on account of the poverty of the province, should come as a legation, and that there a presbyter might be heard by five bishops, and a deacon by three, as has been noted above, his own bishop presiding.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p6"></a>On account of the scarcity of bishops in Tripoli, one bishop shall suffice for a legation.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p7"></a>This canon is made up of two parts.  The first part is Canon v. of the synod of Hippo, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 393, and was repeated at the Carthaginian synod of 397.  The second half is from Canon viij. of the same council.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p8.1"></a>Johnson</span> (<i>See Canon</i> 12).</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xv-p9"></a>“Legate,” i.e., to a Synod, there being few bishops in that province.</p>
