 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p1.1"></a>Canon LXIII.  (Greek lxvi.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p2"></a>Of players who have become Christians.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p3.1"></a>And</span> of them also it must be sought that if anyone wishes to come to the grace of Christianity from any ludicrous art (<i>ludicra arte</i>) and to remain free of that stain, it be not lawful for anyone to induce him or compel him to return to the performance of the same things again.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p6"></a>Whoever has turned away from the stage to adopt an honest life, shall not be led back thereto.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p7"></a>This is Canon vij. of the Synod of Carthage, June 15 (or 16), <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 401.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p8.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p9"></a>This canon is probably to be understood of slaves bought by their masters for the service of the Circ, or Theatre.</p>
