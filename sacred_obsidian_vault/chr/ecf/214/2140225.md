 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.lix"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p1.1"></a>Canon LIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p2.1"></a>Members</span> of the priesthood and of the clergy must not witness the plays at weddings or banquets; but, before the players enter, they must rise and depart.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p5"></a>Priests and clerics should leave before the play.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p6.1"></a>Aristenus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p7"></a>Christians are admonished to feast modestly when they go to weddings and not to dance nor <span class="Greek"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p7.1"></a>βαλλίζειν</span> , that is to clap their hands and make a noise with them.  For this is unworthy of the Christian standing.  But consecrated persons must not see the play at weddings, but before the <i>thymelici</i> begin, they must go out.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p8"></a>Compare with this Canons XXIV. and LI., of the Synod in Trullo.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lix-p9"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars III., <i>De Consecrat</i>. Dist. v., can. xxxvij.</p>
