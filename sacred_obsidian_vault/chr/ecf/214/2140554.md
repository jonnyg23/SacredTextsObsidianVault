 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p1"></a> <a name="page_457"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 457</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-Page_457"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p1.1"></a>Canon XXX.  (Greek xxxiii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p2"></a><i>Concerning the accused or accuser</i>. <a name="fr_432"></a><a href="#fn_432"><font size="1">433</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p4.1"></a>Likewise</span> it seemed good that the accused, or the accusor, if (living in the same place as the accused) he fears some evil may be done him by the tumultuous multitude, may choose for himself a place near by, where the cause may be determined, and where there will be no difficulty in producing the witnesses.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p6"></a><i>Accuser or accused may select for himself a safe place if he fears violence.</i></p>
