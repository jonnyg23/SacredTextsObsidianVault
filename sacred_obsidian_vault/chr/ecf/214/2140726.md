 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p1"></a> <a name="page_601"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 601</font></a> <a name="xvii.vi-Page_601"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p1.1"></a>II.</span></p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xvii.vi-p2"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p2.1"></a>The Canons of the Blessed Peter, Archbishop of Alexandria, and Martyr, <a name="fr_584"></a><a href="#fn_584"><font size="1">585</font></a>
  which are found in his Sermon on Penitence.</span></p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p4.1"></a>Canon I.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p5"></a>The fourth Easter from the beginning of the persecution was now come; and orders, that they who did not fall till after they had endured severe torments, and have already been “Mourners” three years, after forty days fast, are to be admitted to communion, although they have not been before received [to penance]. <a name="fr_585"></a><a href="#fn_585"><font size="1">586</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p7"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p7.1"></a>Canon II.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p8"></a>But if they endured imprisonment only, without torments, let a year be added to their former penance.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p9"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p9.1"></a>Canon III.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p10"></a>If they fell voluntarily, without torments or imprisonments, but are come to repentance, four years are added to their former penance.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p11"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p11.1"></a>Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p12"></a>The case of them who do not repent pronounced desperate.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p13"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p13.1"></a>Canon V.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p14"></a>They that used evasion, and did not right down subscribe the abnegation, or with their own hands incense the idols, but sent a heathen to do it for them, are enjoined six months penance, though they have been pardoned by some of the Confessors.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p15"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p15.1"></a>Canon VI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p16"></a>Slaves forced by their masters to incense idols, and doing it in their masters stead, are enjoined a years penance.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p17"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p17.1"></a>Canon VII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p18"></a>The masters who forced them to it, are enjoined three years penance, as being hypocrites, and as forcing their slaves to sacrifice.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p19"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p19.1"></a>Canon VIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p20"></a>They who first fell, and afterwards recovered themselves, by professing themselves Christians, and endured torments, are forthwith admitted to communion.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p21"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p21.1"></a>Canon IX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p22"></a>That they who provoked the magistrates to persecute themselves and others are to be blamed, yet not to be denied communion.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p23"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p23.1"></a>Canon X.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p24"></a>That clergymen, who run themselves into persecution, and fell, though they did afterward recover themselves, and suffer torments, yet are not to be admitted to perform the sacred offices.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p25"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p25.1"></a>Canon XI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p26"></a>That they who prayed for them who fell after long torments, be connived at, and we pray together with them, since they lament for what they have done, with anguish and mortification. <a name="fr_586"></a><a href="#fn_586"><font size="1">587</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p28"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p28.1"></a>Canon XII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p29"></a>That they who with money purchased their ease and freedom, are to be commended.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p30"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p30.1"></a>Canon XIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p31"></a>Nor should we accuse those who ran away, and left all, though others left behind might fare the worse for it. <a name="fr_587"></a><a href="#fn_587"><font size="1">588</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p33"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p33.1"></a>Canon XIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p34"></a>That they who endured tortures, and afterwards, when they were deprived of speech and motion, had their hands forced into the fire, to offer unholy sacrifice, be placed in the Liturgy [i.e., in the diptychs] among the Confessors.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.vi-p35"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.vi-p35.1"></a>Canon XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.vi-p36"></a>Wednesday is to be fasted, because then the Jews conspired to betray Jesus; Friday, because he then suffered for us.  We keep the Lords Day as a day of joy, because then our Lord rose.  Our tradition is, not to kneel on that day.</p>
