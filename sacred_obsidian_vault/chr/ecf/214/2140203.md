 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p1.1"></a>Canon XXXII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p2.1"></a>It</span> is unlawful to receive the eulogiæ of heretics, for they are rather <span class="Greek"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p2.2"></a>ἀλογίαι</span> [<i>i.e</i>., follies], than eulogiæ [<i>i.e</i>., blessings].</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p5"></a>The blessings of heretics are cursings.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p6"></a>To keep the Latin play upon the words the translator has used <i>bene-dictiones</i> and <i>male-dictiones</i>, but at the expense of the accuracy of translation.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p7"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars II., Causa II., Quæst. I., Can. lxvj.</p>
