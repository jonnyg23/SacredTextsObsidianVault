 <p> <a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p1.1"></a>Canon LXXXVI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p2.1"></a>Those</span> who to the destruction of their own souls procure and bring up harlots, if they be clerics, they are to be [cut off and] deposed, if laymen to be cut off.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXVI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p5"></a>Whoever gathers together harlots to the ruin of souls, shall be cut off.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p6"></a>The brackets enclose the reading of Hervetus.  But Zonaras had this same text, and therefore it may be safely followed instead of that of Balsamon, as edited by Beveridge.</p>
