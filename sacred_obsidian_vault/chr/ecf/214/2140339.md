 <p> <a name="xi.xviii.xxi"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p1.1"></a>Canon XXI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p2.1"></a>Clergymen</span> and laymen bringing charges against bishops or clergymen are not to be received loosely and without examination, as accusers, but their own character shall first be investigated.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p5"></a>A cleric or layman making charges rashly against his bishop shall not be received.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p6"></a>Compare with this canon the <span class="sc"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p6.1"></a>VI</span>th Canon of those credited to the First Synod at Constantinople, the second ecumenical.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xi.xviii.xxi-p7"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars II., Causa II., Quæst. VII., canon xlix., in Isidores first version.</p>
