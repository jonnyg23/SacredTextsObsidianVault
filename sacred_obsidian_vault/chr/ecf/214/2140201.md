 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p1.1"></a>Canon XXX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p2.1"></a>None</span> of the priesthood, nor clerics [of lower rank] nor ascetics, nor any Christian or layman, shall wash in a bath with women; for this is the greatest reproach among the heathen.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p5"></a>It is an abomination to bathe with women.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p6"></a>This canon was renewed by the Synod in Trullo, canon lxxvij.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p7"></a>Zonaras explains that the bathers were entirely nude and hence arose the objection which was also felt by the heathen.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxv-p8"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Dist. LXXXI, c. xxviij.</p>
