 </p><p class="c14"><a name="xvii.xx-p1"></a> <a name="page_611"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 611</font></a> <a name="xvii.xx-Page_611"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.xx-p1.1"></a>From the Letter of Basil the Great to the Nicopolitans.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.xx-p2"></a>There is also in Tilius and Bishop Beveridge here <a name="fr_598"></a><a href="#fn_598"><font size="1">599</font></a>
  inserted an epistle of St. Basil the Great to the Nicopolitans, comforting them under the loss of their church or oratory, and telling them, that they ought not to be concerned that they worship God in the open air, for that the eleven Apostles worshipped God in an upper room, where they were cooped up, while they that crucified Jesus performed their worship in a most famous Temple.</p>
