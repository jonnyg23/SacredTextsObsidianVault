 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p1.1"></a>Canon X.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p2.1"></a>Likewise</span>, if a deacon have fallen into the same sin, let him have the rank of a minister.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon X.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p5"></a><i>A deacon found in the same crime shall remain a minister</i> (<span class="Greek"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p5.1"></a>ὑπηρέτης</span>).</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p6.1"></a>Hefele.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p7"></a>By ministers (<span class="Greek"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p7.1"></a>ὑπήρεται</span>) are meant inferior officers of the Church—the so-called minor orders, often including the subdeacons.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.iv.iii.x-p8"></a><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a> <a name="viii.iv.iii.x-Page_84"></a>This canon is in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars II., Causa xv., Quæst. viii., united with canon ix., and in the following curious form:  “Similiter et diaconus, si in eodem culpæ genere fuerit involutus, sese a ministerio cohibebit.”</p>
