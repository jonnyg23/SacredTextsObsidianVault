 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p1.1"></a>Canon LXXXII.  (Greek lxxxv.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p2"></a>Of manumissions.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p3.1"></a>Item</span>, it seemed good that the Emperor be petitioned with regard to announcing manumissions in church.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p6"></a>The imperial permission must be asked for the making of the manumission of slaves in churches.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p7.1"></a>Aristenus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p8"></a>This is the same as the sixty-fourth [Greek numbering] canon, and is there explained.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p9"></a>This is Canon xvj. of Carthage, September, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p9.1"></a>a.d.</span> 401.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p10"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p10.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxxiii-p11"></a>A repetition of Canon 64 (67).</p>
