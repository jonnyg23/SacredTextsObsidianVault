 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p1.1"></a>Canon XVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p2"></a>That any province on account of its distance, may have its own Primate.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p3.1"></a>It</span> seemed good that Mauretania Sitiphensis, as it asked, should have a Primate of its own, with the consent of the Primate of Numidia from whose synod it had been separated. <a name="fr_423"></a><a href="#fn_423"><font size="1">424</font></a>
   And with the consent of all the primates of the African Provinces and of all the bishops permission was given, by reason of the great distance between them.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p5.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p6.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p7"></a>Mauretania Sitiphensis, on account of the great distance, is permitted to have its own Primate.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p8"></a>This canon is Canon iij. of the first series of canons enacted at Hippo in 393.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p9.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p10"></a>N.B.  From this place forward the Latin and Greek numeration varies; but Justelluss Edition in Greek and Latin follows the Latin division.</p>
