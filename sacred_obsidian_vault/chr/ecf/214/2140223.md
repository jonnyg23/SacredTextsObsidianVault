 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p1.1"></a>Canon LII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p2.1"></a>Marriages</span> and birthday feasts are not to be celebrated in Lent.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p5"></a>Marriage shall not be celebrated in Lent, nor birthdays.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p6.1"></a>Hefele.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p7"></a>By “birthday feasts” in this canon the <i>natalitia martyrum</i> is not to be understood as in the preceding canon, but the birthday feasts of princes.  This, as well as the preceding rule, was renewed in the sixth century by Bishop Martin of Bracara, now Braga, in Portugal.</p>
