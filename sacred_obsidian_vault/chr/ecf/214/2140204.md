 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p1.1"></a>Canon XXXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p2.1"></a>No</span> one shall join in prayers with heretics or schismatics.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p5"></a>Thou shalt not pray with heretics or schismatics.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p6.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-p7"></a>The underlying principle of this canon is the same as the last, for as the receiving of <a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 150</font></a> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxviii-Page_150"></a>the Eulogiæ which were sent by heretics as a sign of communion, signified a communion with them in religious matters, so the sharing with them common prayer is a declaration of the same communion, and therefore to be avoided.  This is also set forth in Apostolical Canon number xlv.</p>
