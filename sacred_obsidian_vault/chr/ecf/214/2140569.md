 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p1.1"></a>Canon XLV.  (Greek xlviii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p2"></a>Concerning those who are sick and cannot answer for themselves.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p3.1"></a>That</span> the sick are to be baptized who cannot answer for themselves if their [servants] shall have spoken at their own proper peril a testimony of the good will [of the sick man].</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p4"></a>(Greek Canon xlix.)</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p5"></a><i>Concerning players who are doing penance and are converted to the Lord.</i> <a name="fr_444"></a><a href="#fn_444"><font size="1">445</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p7.1"></a>That</span> to players and actors and other persons of that kind, as also to apostates when they are converted <a name="fr_445"></a><a href="#fn_445"><font size="1">446</font></a>
  and return to God, grace or reconciliation is not to be denied.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p9"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p9.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p10"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p10.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XLV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p11"></a>That he who cannot answer for himself on account of illness is to be baptized when he shall have given evidence of his desire.</p> 
 <p class="c39"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p12"></a>A repentant actor is to be received to penance.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p13"></a>This canon is made up of Canons xxxij. and xxxiij. of the Synod of Hippo, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p13.1"></a>a.d.</span> 393.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p14"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p14.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p15"></a>“Apostates,” i.e., those who elsewhere are called <i>Lapsi</i>; those who had done sacrifice through the violence of torment in time of persecution, professing in the meantime that their consciences did not consent to what their hands did.</p>
