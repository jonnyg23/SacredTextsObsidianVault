 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p1.1"></a>Canon CVII.  (Greek cx. continued.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p2"></a>A Council concerning a bishop taking cognizance.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p3.1"></a>In</span> the consulate of the most glorious Emperors Honorius for the <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p3.2"></a>VII</span>th time and Theodosius for the IIId, Augusti, xvii. Calends of July, a synod was held at Carthage in the basilica of the second region.  In this council it seemed good that no one bishop <a name="page_496"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 496</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-Page_496"></a>should claim the right to take cognizance of a cause.  The acts of this council I have not here written down, because it was only provincial and not general.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon CVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p5"></a>One bishop shall not claim for himself to take cognizance of a cause alone.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p6"></a>(Greek cxi.)</p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p7"></a>Synod against the Donatists.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p8"></a>After the consulate of the most illustrious Emperors Honorius for the <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p8.1"></a>VIII</span>th time and Theodosius for the <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p8.2"></a>IV</span>th time, Augusti, xviii. Calends of July, at Carthage in the basilica of the second region.  In this council the bishops, Florentius, Possidius, Præsidius and Benenatus received legation against the Donatists, at that time at which a law was given that anyone might practice the Christian worship at his own will.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p9"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p9.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p10"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p10.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon CVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p11"></a>Let each one receive the practice of piety of his own free will.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p12"></a>The two first introductions belong respectively to the Synods of Carthage of June 16 and of October 13, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p12.1"></a>a.d.</span> 408.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p13"></a>Canon cvij. of the African code and that which follows it are the introductions to the Synods of Carthage of June 15, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p13.1"></a>a.d.</span> 409, and of June 14, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p13.2"></a>a.d.</span> 410.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p14"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p14.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cviii-p15"></a>See can. 10, 11, 12, 28 (31), 79 (80).  Recognises, a law of the Empire, that everyone receive christianity at his own free choice.</p>
