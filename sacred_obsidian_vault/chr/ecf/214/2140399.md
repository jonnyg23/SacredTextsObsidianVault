 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p1"></a> <a name="page_373"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 373</font></a> <a name="xiv.iii.xvi-Page_373"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p1.1"></a>Canon XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p2"></a>A <span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p2.1"></a>subdeacon</span> is not to be ordained under twenty years of age.  And if any one in any grade of the priesthood shall have been ordained contrary to the prescribed time let him be deposed.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p5"></a>Those shall be chosen as Subdeacons who are twenty years of age.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p6"></a>This age seems first to have been fixed by the Second Council of Toledo <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">362</font></a>
  (<i>circa</i>, <span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p7.2"></a>a.d.</span> 535) in its first canon.</p>

 <p class="endnote"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p7"></a> It is curious that so learned a scholar as the late Henry Bradshaw in his article “Subdeacon” in Smith &amp; Cheethams<i>Dictionary of Christ. Antiq</i>. should give the date of this synod as 447.  Hefele fixes it at 527 or 531.  Baronius, Binius, Labbe, and many others at 531.  A very ancient <span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xvi-p7.1"></a>ms.</span> assigns it to the year 565 of the Spanish era, i.e. 527, and this is the date Cardinal de Aguirre adopts, and is also the one given to the council by the editors of <i>LArt de Vérifier les dates</i>.</p>
