 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p1.1"></a>Canon CXXXI.  (Greek CXXXII.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p2"></a>Who should be allowed to give evidence.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p3.1"></a>They</span> who are forbidden to be admitted as accusers are not to be allowed to appear as witnesses, nor any that the accuser may bring from his own household.  And none shall be admitted to give witness under fourteen years of age.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon CXXXI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p6"></a>And whoso is not past fourteen years of age [is not to give witness].  An accuser is not to produce witnesses from his own house.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p7.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p8"></a>See Can. 129.</p>
