 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p1"></a> <a name="page_460"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 460</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-Page_460"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p1.1"></a>Canon XXXVI.  (Greek xxxix.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p2"></a>That bishops or clergymen are not to be ordained unless they have made all their family Christians.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p3.1"></a>None</span> shall be ordained bishop, presbyters, or deacons before all the inmates of their houses shall have become Catholic Christians.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXVI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p6"></a>He shall not be ordained who hath not made all his household orthodox.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p7"></a>This canon is Canon xvij. of the Synod of Hippo, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.xxxvii-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 393.</p>
