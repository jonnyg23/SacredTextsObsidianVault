 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p1.1"></a>Canon LIX.  (Greek lxiii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p2"></a>That clerics be not compelled to give testimony in public concerning the cognizance of their own judgment.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p3.1"></a>It</span> should be petitioned also that they deign to decree, that if perchance any shall have been willing to plead their cause in any church according to the Apostolic law imposed upon the Churches, and it happens that the decision of the clergy does not satisfy one of the parties, it be not lawful to summon that clergyman who had been cognitor or present, <a name="fr_458"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">459</font></a>
  into judgment as a witness, and that no person attached to any ecclesiastic be compelled to give testimony.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p5.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p6.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p7"></a>A cleric who has decided a case shall not, if it be displeasing, be summoned to a tribunal to give evidence concerning it; and no ecclesiastical person shall be forced to give testimony.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p8"></a>This is Canon iij. of the Synod of Carthage, June 15 (or 16). <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p8.1"></a>a.d.</span> 401.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p9.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p10"></a>“According to the Apostolic law,” viz., that of St. Paul, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1006.htm#001">1 Cor. 6:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1006.htm#002">2</a><a name="xv.iv.iv.lx-p10.1"></a>, etc.  I follow the Greek scholia in rendering this canon.  In Latin <i>cognitor</i> is he that is solicitor, or advocate, rather than the judge who takes cognizance.</p>
