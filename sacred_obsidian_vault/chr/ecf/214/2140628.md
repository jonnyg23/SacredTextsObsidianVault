 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p1.1"></a>Canon CIV.  (Greek cvii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p2"></a>Of these who ask from the Emperor that secular judges may take cognizance of their causes.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p3.1"></a>It</span> seemed good that whoever should seek from the Emperor, that secular judges should take cognizance of his business, should be deprived of his office; if however, he had asked from the Emperor an episcopal trial, no objection should be made.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon CIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p6"></a>Let not him be a bishop who from the Emperor seeks a public judgment.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p7"></a>This canon is Canon X. of Carthage, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 407.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p8.1"></a>Johnson</span>.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cv-p9"></a>See Canon Ant., 12.</p>
