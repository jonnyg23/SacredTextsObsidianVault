 <p> <a name="iv.iv.v.xii"></a> </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII.</h4>  
 <p class="c32"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p1"></a>The kingdom of the Father and of the Son is one and undivided, so likewise is the Godhead of each.</p>  
 <p class="c22"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p2"></a>92. <span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p2.1"></a>I would</span> now ask how they suppose the kingdom of the Father and the Son to be divided, when the Lord hath said, as we showed above: “Every kingdom divided against itself shall be speedily overthrown.” <a name="fr_2265"></a><a href="#fn_2265"><font size="1">2266</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p4"></a><a name="page_256"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 256</font></a> <a name="iv.iv.v.xii-Page_256"></a>93. Indeed, it was to debar the impious teaching of Arian enmity that Saint Peter himself asserted the dominion of the Father and the Son to be one, saying: “Wherefore, my brethren, labour to make your calling and election sure, for so doing you shall not go astray, for thus your entrance into the eternal realm of God and our Lord and Saviour <a name="fr_2266"></a><a href="#fn_2266"><font size="1">2267</font></a>
  Jesus Christ shall be granted with the greater abundance of grace.” <a name="fr_2267"></a><a href="#fn_2267"><font size="1">2268</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p7"></a>94. Now, if it be thought that Christs dominion alone is spoken of, and the place be therefore understood in such sense that the Father and the Son are regarded as divided in authority—yet it will be still acknowledged that it is the dominion of the Son, and that an eternal one, and thus not only will two kingdoms, separate, and so liable to fail, be brought in, but, furthermore, inasmuch as no kingdom is to be compared with Gods kingdom, which they cannot, however greatly they may desire to, deny to be the kingdom of the Son, they must either turn back upon their opinion, and acknowledge the kingdom of the Father and the Son to be one and the same; or they must ascribe to the Father the government of a lesser kingdom—which is blasphemy; or they must acknowledge Him, Whom they wickedly declare to be inferior in respect of Godhead, to possess an equal kingdom, which is inconsistent.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p8"></a>95. But this [their teaching] squares not, agrees not, holds not [with its premisses]. Let them confess, then, that the kingdom is one, even as we confess and prove, not indeed on our own evidence, but upon testimony vouchsafed from heaven.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p9"></a>96. To begin with, learn, from further testimonies [of Scripture], how that the kingdom of heaven is also the kingdom of the Son: “Verily, verily, I say unto you, that there are some amongst those which stand here with us, who shall not taste death, until they see the Son of Man coming into His kingdom.” <a name="fr_2268"></a><a href="#fn_2268"><font size="1">2269</font></a>
  There is therefore no room for doubt that the kingdom appertaineth to the Son of God.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p11"></a>97. Now learn that the kingdom of the Son is the very same as the kingdom of the Father: “Verily, I say unto you that there be some of those which stand around us, who shall not taste death until they see the kingdom of God coming in power.” <a name="fr_2269"></a><a href="#fn_2269"><font size="1">2270</font></a>
  So far, indeed, is it one kingdom, that the reward is one, the inheritor is one and the same, and so also the merit, and He Who promises [the reward].</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p13"></a>98. How can it but be one kingdom, above all when the Son Himself hath said of Himself: “Then shall the righteous shine like the sun in the kingdom of My Father”? <a name="fr_2270"></a><a href="#fn_2270"><font size="1">2271</font></a>
  For that which is the Fathers, by fitness to His majesty, is also the Sons, by unity in the same glory. <a name="fr_2271"></a><a href="#fn_2271"><font size="1">2272</font></a>
  The Scripture, therefore, hath declared the kingdom to be the kingdom both of the Father and of the Son.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p16"></a>99. Now learn that where the kingdom of God is named, there is no putting aside of the authority either of the Father or of the Son, because both the kingdom of the Father and the kingdom of the Son is included under the single name of God, saying: “When ye shall see Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, and all the prophets, in the kingdom of God.” <a name="fr_2272"></a><a href="#fn_2272"><font size="1">2273</font></a>
  Do we deny that the prophets are in the kingdom of the Son, when even to a dying robber who said, “Remember me, when Thou comest into Thy kingdom,” the Lord made answer: “Verily, I say unto thee, to-day shalt thou be with Me in paradise.” <a name="fr_2273"></a><a href="#fn_2273"><font size="1">2274</font></a>
  What, indeed, do we understand by being in the kingdom of God, if not the having escaped eternal death? But they who have escaped eternal death see the Son of Man coming into His kingdom.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p19"></a>100. How, then, can He not have in His power that which He gives, saying: “To thee will I give the keys of the kingdom of heaven”? <a name="fr_2274"></a><a href="#fn_2274"><font size="1">2275</font></a>
  See the gulf between [the one and the other]. The servant opens, the Lord bestows; the One through Himself, the other through Christ; the minister receives the keys, the Lord appoints powers: the one is the right of a giver, the other the duty of a steward.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p21"></a>101. See now yet another proof that the kingdom, the government, of the Father and the Son is one. It is written in the Epistle to Timothy: “Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ, according to the government of God, our Saviour, and Christ Jesus, our Hope.” <a name="fr_2275"></a><a href="#fn_2275"><font size="1">2276</font></a>
  One, therefore, the kingdom of the Father and the Son is plainly declared to be, even as Paul the Apostle also asserted, saying: “For know this, that no shameless person, none that is impure, or covetous (which meaneth idolatry), hath inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and of God.” <a name="fr_2276"></a><a href="#fn_2276"><font size="1">2277</font></a>
  It is, therefore, one kingdom, one Godhead.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.iv.v.xii-p24"></a><a name="page_257"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 257</font></a> <a name="iv.iv.v.xii-Page_257"></a>102. Oneness in Godhead the Law hath proved, which speaks of one God, <a name="fr_2277"></a><a href="#fn_2277"><font size="1">2278</font></a>
  as also the Apostle, by saying of Christ; “In Whom dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily.” <a name="fr_2278"></a><a href="#fn_2278"><font size="1">2279</font></a>
  For if, as the Apostle saith, all the fulness of the Godhead, bodily, is in Christ, then must the Father and the Son be confessed to be of one Godhead; or if it is desired to sunder the Godhead of the Son from the Godhead of the Father, whilst the Son possesses all the fulness of the Godhead bodily, what is supposed to be further reserved, seeing that nothing remains over and above the fulness of perfection? Therefore the Godhead is one.</p>
