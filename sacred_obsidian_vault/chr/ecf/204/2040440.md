 <p> <a name="xxv.iii.iii.xxix"></a>
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="xxv.iii.iii.xxix-p1"></a>  <span class="c10"><a name="xxv.iii.iii.xxix-p1.1"></a>From Letter XLIV</span>.—<i>(For 372.) And again, from the forty-fourth Letter, of which the commencement is, All that our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ did instead of us and for us <a name="fr_4556"></a><a href="#fn_4556"><font size="1">4557</font></a>
 .</i></p>  
 <p class="c11"><a name="xxv.iii.iii.xxix-p3"></a><span class="c10"><a name="xxv.iii.iii.xxix-p3.1"></a>When</span> therefore the servants of the Chief Priests and the Scribes saw these things, and heard from Jesus, Whosoever is athirst, let him come to Me and drink <a name="fr_4557"></a><a href="#fn_4557"><font size="1">4558</font></a>
 ; they perceived that this was not a mere man like themselves, but that this was He Who gave water to the saints, and that it was He Who was announced by the prophet Isaiah. For He was truly the splendour of the light <a name="fr_4558"></a><a href="#fn_4558"><font size="1">4559</font></a>
 , and the Word of God. And thus as a river from the fountain he gave drink also of old to Paradise; but now to all men He gives the same gift of the Spirit, and says, If any man thirst, let him come to Me and drink. Whosoever believeth on Me, as saith the Scripture, rivers of living water shall flow out of his belly <a name="fr_4559"></a><a href="#fn_4559"><font size="1">4560</font></a>
 . This was not for man to say, but for the living God, Who truly vouchsafes life, and gives the Holy Spirit.</p>
