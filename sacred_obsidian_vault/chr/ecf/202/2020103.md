 </p><p class="c32"><a name="ii.vi.v-p1"></a><span class="c11"><a name="ii.vi.v-p1.1"></a>Chapter V</span>.—<i>Of Lucifer and Eusebius.</i></p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="ii.vi.v-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="ii.vi.v-p2.1"></a>About</span> the same time Lucifer and Eusebius <a name="fr_471"></a><a href="#fn_471"><font size="1">472</font></a>
   were by an imperial order, recalled from banishment out of the Upper Thebaïs; the former being bishop of Carala, a city of Sardinia, the latter of Vercellæ, a city of the Ligurians in Italy, as I have said <a name="fr_472"></a><a href="#fn_472"><font size="1">473</font></a>
   previously. These two prelates therefore consulted together on the most effectual means of preventing the neglected canons <a name="fr_473"></a><a href="#fn_473"><font size="1">474</font></a>
   and discipline of the church from being in future violated and despised. </p>
