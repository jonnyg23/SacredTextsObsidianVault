 <p> <a name="ii.iii.ii"></a>
 </p><p class="c23"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p1.1"></a>Manuscripts.</span></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p2"></a>I.  At the <i>Vatican</i>.  (<span class="Greek"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p2.1"></a>α</span>) <i>Of the Sermons</i>.  (1)  Codd. 3835 and 6 are two volumes in Roman Character of a Lectionary of about the 8th century; the second volume contains the “Tome” (which in the 8th and 9th centuries used to be read in the Church offices before Christmas):  (2)  3828, a parchment (10th century), also a lectionary:  (3)  1195, a parchment folio (11th century), a lectionary containing <i>inter alia</i> some of Leos homilies:  (4)  1267, 8 and 9 of the same character (11th century):  (5)  1270 contains the Sermon <i>de Festo Petri cathedræ</i>, (now xiv. in Mignes Appendix), from which Cacciari restored Quesnels imperfect edition of it to its present state:  (6)  1271 and 2 are also lectionaries:  (7)  4222 in Lombardic characters (9th century), a lectionary:  (8)  5451 in Roman characters (12th century), a lectionary:  (9)  6450 parchment (12th century):  a lectionary containing the sermon <i>de Festo Petri cathedræ</i> in the form found and printed by Quesnel; (10)  6451 similar:  it contains sermons <i>de Quadragesima</i>  and others:  (11)  6454 similar.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p3"></a>(<span class="Greek"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p3.1"></a>β</span>) Of the <i>Letters</i>:  these are mostly rather later (i.e. about 12th or 13th century):  but (1)  1322 is of an older date, and contains besides the epistles, all the acts of the Council of Chalcedon:  (2)  5759 is earlier than the 9th century; it used to belong to the monastery of S. Columban at Bobbio, and contains 31 letters:  (3)  5845 is very ancient, and according to Cacciari, Lombardic:  it contains 24 letters.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p4"></a>(<span class="Greek"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p4.1"></a>γ</span>) <i>Letters and Sermons together</i>:  of these there are nine collections in the Vatican, of which 548 and 9 contain the sermon <i>de Absalom</i>  which is condemned by Cacciari.  The <i>Regio-Vaticanus codex</i> 139 is a fine collection of Leos works (12th century).</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p5"></a>II.  <i>At other places</i>:  (1)  The codex <i>Urbinas</i> 65 is thought to be a copy of the Regio-Vaticanus 139 made in the 14th century.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p6"></a>(2)  Codex <i>Grimanicus</i> <a name="fr_13"></a><a href="#fn_13"><font size="1">14</font></a>
 is a <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p7.1"></a>ms.</span> on which Quesnel lays great stress:  Quesnel assigns it to the ninth century; it contains 107 letters, of which 28 had never been printed before Quesnel.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p8"></a>(3)  The <i>Thuanei</i>; (<span class="Greek"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p8.1"></a>α</span>) 129 contains 123 letters:  (<span class="Greek"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p8.2"></a>β</span>) 780 contains the Tome:  (<span class="Greek"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p8.3"></a>γ</span>) 729 contains the spurious <i>de vocatione gentium</i> and some epistles.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p9"></a>(4)  The <i>Corbeienses</i> are old.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p10"></a>(5)  The <i>Taurinensis</i> 29 D. iv. is a fine 13th-century <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p10.1"></a>ms.</span> containing 52 letters.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p11"></a>(6)  The <i>Florentinus codex</i> belongs to the 13th century also.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p12"></a>(7)  <i>Ratisbonensis</i> 113 DD. AA., in the monastery of S. Emeramus, contains 72 letters:  it is said to date from about 750 <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.ii-p12.1"></a>a.d.</span></p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p13"></a>(8)  The two <i>Bergonenses</i> are of 12th century, and contain 12 sermons.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p14"></a>  <a name="page_xiv"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. xiv</font></a> <a name="ii.iii.ii-Page_xiv"></a>(9)  Two <i>Chigiani</i> also of 12th century contain 4 sermons.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p15"></a>(10)  The <i>Padilironenses</i> contain 9 sermons and the Tome.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p16"></a>(11)  There are three <i>Patavini</i>, of which two contain the Tome.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p17"></a>(12)  <i>Vallicellani</i>:  these are a number of 11th or 12th-century codices.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p18"></a>There are also the <i>Veneti</i>, the <i>Vercellenses</i>, the <i>Veronenses</i>, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.ii-p19"></a>N.B.  The foregoing account is taken from Schönemanns <i>Notitia Historico-Literaria</i>  (1794), and the translator has no means of knowing whether it is still correct (1890).</p>
