 <p class="c23"><a name="ii.v.i-p3"></a><span class="c16"><a name="ii.v.i-p3.1"></a>Sermon I.</span></p>  
 <p class="c26"><a name="ii.v.i-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="ii.v.i-p4.1"></a>Preached on his Birthday <a name="fr_644"></a><a href="#fn_644"><font size="1">645</font></a>
 , or day of Ordination.</span></p>  
 <p class="c27"><a name="ii.v.i-p6"></a><i>Having been elected in absence</i> <a name="fr_645"></a><a href="#fn_645"><font size="1">646</font></a>
  <i>he returns thanks for the kindness and earnestly demands the prayers of his church.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="ii.v.i-p8"></a>“<span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p8.1"></a>Let</span> my mouth speak the praise of the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p8.2"></a>Lord <a name="fr_646"></a><a href="#fn_646"><font size="1">647</font></a>
 </span>,” and my breath and spirit, my flesh and tongue bless His holy Name.  For it is a sign, not of a modest, but an ungrateful mind, to keep silence on the kindnesses of <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p9.2"></a>God</span>:  and it is very meet to begin our duty as consecrated pontiff with the sacrifices of the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p9.3"></a>Lords</span> praise <a name="fr_647"></a><a href="#fn_647"><font size="1">648</font></a>
 .  Because “in our humility” the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p10.1"></a>Lord</span> “has been mindful of us <a name="fr_648"></a><a href="#fn_648"><font size="1">649</font></a>
 ” and has blessed us:  because “He alone has done great wonders for me <a name="fr_649"></a><a href="#fn_649"><font size="1">650</font></a>
 ,” so that your holy affection for me reckoned me present, though my long journey had forced me to be absent.  Therefore I give and always shall give thanks to our <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p12.2"></a>God</span> for all the things with which He has recompensed me.  Your favourable opinion also I acknowledge publicly, paying you the thanks I owe, and thus showing that I understand how much respect, love and fidelity your affectionate zeal could expend on me who long with a shepherds anxiety for the safety of your souls, who have passed so conscientious a judgment on me, with absolutely no deserts of mine to guide you.  I entreat you, therefore, by the mercies of the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p12.3"></a>Lord</span>, aid with your prayers him whom you have sought out by your solicitations that both the Spirit of grace may abide in me and that your judgment may not change.  May He who inspired you with such unanimity of purpose, vouchsafe to us all in common the blessing of peace:  so that all the days of my life being ready for the service of Almighty <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p12.4"></a>God</span>, and for my duties towards you, I may with confidence entreat the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p12.5"></a>Lord</span>:  “Holy Father, keep in Thy name those whom Thou hast given me <a name="fr_650"></a><a href="#fn_650"><font size="1">651</font></a>
 :”  and while you ever go on unto salvation, may “my soul magnify the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.v.i-p13.2"></a>Lord <a name="fr_651"></a><a href="#fn_651"><font size="1">652</font></a>
 </span>,” and in the retribution of the judgment to come may the account of my priesthood so be rendered to the just Judge <a name="fr_652"></a><a href="#fn_652"><font size="1">653</font></a>
  that through your good deeds you may be my joy and my crown, who by your good will have given an earnest testimony to me in this present life.</p>
