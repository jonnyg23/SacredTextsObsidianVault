 </p><p class="c23"><a name="ii.iii.v-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="ii.iii.v-p1.1"></a>Authorities and Materials.</span></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="ii.iii.v-p2"></a>The chief ancient and medieval authorities for the life and times of Leo the Great are such works as <i>Prospers</i>, and <i>Idatius Chronicles, Iornandes de rebus Geticis, Anastasius Bibliothecarius Historia de vitis Romanorum Pontificum</i> (9th cent.), the <i>Historia Miscella</i> (10th cent.), &amp;c.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.iii.v-p3"></a>Among <i>lives</i> may be mentioned the following:—(1) <i><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.1"></a>La vie et religion de deux bons papes Léon premier et Gregoire premier par</span></i> <span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.3"></a>Pierre Du Moulin</span></span> (the younger:  a protestant theologian), <i>Sedan</i>, 1650. 12mo.  (2) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.4"></a>Quesnels</span> valuable <i>Dissertatio de vita et rebus gestis S. Leonis Magni</i>, originally included in his edition of Leo and re-printed by Migne in Vol. ii. of his edition with the Balleriniis annotations and critical remarks, <i>Paris</i>, 1675, <i>Lyons</i>, 1700.  (3) <i><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.5"></a>Histoire du Pontificat de Saint Léon le Grand par Monsr. L. Maimbourg La Haye</span></i>, 1687.  (4) <i>The Bollandists Life by Canisius</i> (<i>Acta Sanctorum</i>), <i>April</i>, vol. ii. pp. 14–22.  (5) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.6"></a>Alphonsi</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.7"></a>Ciaconii</span> <i>Vitæ Pontificum</i> (<i>Tom.</i> 1, pp. 303–314), <i>Rome</i>, 1677, 4<i>to</i>.  (6) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.8"></a>Le Nain de Tillemont</span><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.9"></a>, <i>Memoires pour servir à lhistoire Ecclesiastique</i></span> (vol. xv. pp. 414–832, 885–934), <i>Paris</i>, 1711.  (7)   <a name="page_xv"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. xv</font></a> <a name="ii.iii.v-Page_xv"></a><i>Breve Descrizione della vita di S. Leone Primo di</i> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.10"></a>Gabrielle</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.11"></a>Bertazzolo</span>:  <i>Mantova</i>, 1727.  (8) <i>Memoire istoriche di Sa. Leone Papa da</i> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.12"></a>Teofilo</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.13"></a>Pacifico</span>:  <i>Brescia</i>, 1791, 8<i>vo</i>.  (9) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.14"></a>Du Pin</span>, L. E., <i>History of Ecc. writers</i> (Eng Edn. vol. 1, pp. 464–480), <i>Dublin</i>, 1722.  (10) C. <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.15"></a>Oudinus</span>, <i>de Scriptoribus Ecclesiæ</i> (vol. 1, pp. 1271–5), <i>Leipzig</i>, 1722.  (11) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.16"></a>Wilhelm</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.17"></a>Amadeus</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.18"></a>Arendt</span> (<i>Roman Catholic</i>), <i>Leo der Grosse und seine Zeit, Mainz</i>, 1835, 8<i>vo</i>.  (12) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.19"></a>Eduard</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.20"></a>Perthel</span>, <i>Papst Leos I. Leben und Lehren, Jena</i>, 1843, 8<i>vo</i>. (a counterblast to No. 11, and no less exaggerated and prejudiced in statement).  (13) A. <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.21"></a>de Saint-Chron</span>, <i><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.22"></a>Histoire du pontificat de Saint Léon le Grand, Paris</span></i>, 1846.  (14) F. <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.23"></a>Böhringer</span>, <i>die Kirche Xti und ihre Zeugen</i> (vol. 1 part 4, pp. 170–309), <i>Zürich</i>, 1845.  (15) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.24"></a>Charles</span> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.25"></a>Gores</span> <i>Life of Leo the Great</i> (S.P.C.K.); also his article in Smiths <i>Dict. of Christian Biogr</i>.  (16) The article in <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.26"></a>Herzogs</span> <i>Real-Encyklopädie</i> of which a condensed English edition was edited by Dr. Philip Schaff at New York in 1883.  Other more general accounts of his times will be found in (1) labbé <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.27"></a>Fleury</span>, <i><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.28"></a>Histoire du Xtianisme</span></i> (vol. ii. pp. 384–480), <i>Paris</i>, 1836.  (2) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.29"></a>Brights</span> <i>History of the Church from</i> 313–451 (chaps. xiv., xv.), <i>Oxford and London</i>, 1860.  (3) <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.30"></a>Milmans</span> <i>Latin Christianity</i> (Book ii. chap. 4), <i>London</i>, 1864.  (4) R. J. <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.31"></a>Rohrbachers</span> <i><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.32"></a>Histoire Universelle de lEglise catholique</span></i> (15th edn., vol. 4, pp. 461–575), <i>Paris</i>, 1868.  A short account of Leos writings is given in <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.33"></a>Alzogs</span> <i>Grundriss der Patrologie</i>, § 78, pp.368–375:  a most exhaustive one in <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.34"></a>Ceilliers</span> <i><span><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.35"></a>Histoire générale des Auteurs sacrés</span></i> (new edition) (vol. x., pp. 169–276), 1858–1869.  <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.36"></a>Bährs</span> <i>Geschichte der Römischer Literatur-Supplement Band II. Abtheilung</i> (pp. 354–362), <i>im Abendland</i>, vol. 1, p. 448, may also be consulted; and <span class="sc"><a name="ii.iii.v-p3.37"></a>Eberts</span> <i>Allgemeine Geschichte der Literatur des Mittelalters</i>.</p>
