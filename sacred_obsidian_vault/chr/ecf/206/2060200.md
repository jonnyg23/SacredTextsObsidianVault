 <p> <a name="vii.iii.xi"></a>
 </p><p class="c26"><a name="vii.iii.xi-p1"></a>  <span class="c1"><a name="vii.iii.xi-p1.1"></a>Isaiah.</span></p>  
 <p class="c16"><a name="vii.iii.xi-p2"></a><i>Addressed to Paula and Eustochium, about <span class="c17"><a name="vii.iii.xi-p2.1"></a>a.d.</span> 393. This Preface speaks of Isaiah as using the polished diction natural to a man of rank and refinement, as an Evangelist more than a prophet, and a poet rather than a prose writer. He then reiterates his defence of his translation, saying that now, “The Jews can no longer scoff at our Churches because of the falsity of our Scriptures.”</i></p>
