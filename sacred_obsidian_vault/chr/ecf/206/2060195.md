 </p><p class="c26"><a name="vii.iii.vi-p1"></a>  <span class="c1"><a name="vii.iii.vi-p1.1"></a>Ezra and Nehemiah.</span></p>  
 <p class="c54"><a name="vii.iii.vi-p2"></a>This Preface is addressed to Domnio (a Roman presbyter. See Letters L., and XLVII. 3, Paulinus, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#000">Ep. 3</a><a name="vii.iii.vi-p2.1"></a>) and Rogatianus, of whom nothing is known. It was written <span class="c17"><a name="vii.iii.vi-p2.2"></a>a.d.</span> 394. It is a repetition of his constant ground of self-defence, and contains a noble expression of his determination to carry the work through. “The serpent may hiss, and</p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="vii.iii.vi-p3"></a>“Victorious Sinon hurl his brand of fire,</p>  
 <p><a name="vii.iii.vi-p4"></a><a name="page_491"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 491</font></a> <a name="vii.iii.vi-Page_491"></a><i>but never shall my mouth be closed. Cut off my tongue; it will still stammer out something.</i></p>
