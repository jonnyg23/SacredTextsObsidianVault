 <p> <a name="iv.ii.iii.xii"></a> </p><h5 align="center"><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII.—Of Righteousness.</h5>  
 <p><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p1"></a>Moreover, concerning the righteousness which the law enjoined, confirmatory utterances are found both with the prophets and in the Gospels, because they all spoke inspired by one Spirit of God. Isaiah accordingly spoke thus: “Put away the evil of your doings from your souls; learn to do well, seek judgment, relieve the oppressed, judge the fatherless, plead for the widow.” <a name="fr_654"></a><a href="#fn_654"><font size="1">655</font></a>
  And again the same prophet said: “Loose every band of wickedness, dissolve every oppressive contract, let the oppressed go free, and tear up every unrighteous bond. Deal out thy bread to the hungry, and bring the houseless poor to thy home. When thou seest the naked, cover him, and hide not thyself from thine own flesh. Then shall thy light  <a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 115</font></a> <a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-Page_115"></a>break forth as the morning, and thine health shall spring forth speedily, and thy righteousness shall go before thee.” <a name="fr_655"></a><a href="#fn_655"><font size="1">656</font></a>
  In like manner also Jeremiah says: “Stand in the ways, and see, and ask which is the good way of the <span class="sc"><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p3.2"></a>Lord</span> your God, and walk in it and ye shall find rest for your souls. Judge just judgment, for in this is the will of the <span class="sc"><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p3.3"></a>Lord</span> your God.” <a name="fr_656"></a><a href="#fn_656"><font size="1">657</font></a>
  So also says Hosea: “Keep judgment, and draw near to your God, who established the heavens and created the earth.” <a name="fr_657"></a><a href="#fn_657"><font size="1">658</font></a>
  And another, Joel, spoke in agreement with these: “Gather the people, sanctify the congregation, assemble the elders, gather the children that are in arms; let the bridegroom go forth of his chamber, and the bride out of her closet, and pray to the <span class="sc"><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p5.2"></a>Lord</span> thy God urgently that he may have mercy upon you, and blot out your sins.” <a name="fr_658"></a><a href="#fn_658"><font size="1">659</font></a>
  In like manner also another, Zachariah: “Thus saith the <span class="sc"><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p6.2"></a>Lord</span> Almighty, Execute true judgment, and show mercy and compassion every man to his brother; and oppress not the widow, nor the fatherless, nor the stranger; and let none of you imagine evil against his brother in your heart, saith the <span class="sc"><a name="iv.ii.iii.xii-p6.3"></a>Lord</span> Almighty.” <a name="fr_659"></a><a href="#fn_659"><font size="1">660</font></a>
 </p>
