 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xvi.ii.v.xxix-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xvi.ii.v.xxix-p1.1"></a>29.  The Coming of the Son of Man in Glory.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xvi.ii.v.xxix-p2"></a>“<i>For the Son of man shall come in the glory of His own Father with His angels.</i>” <a name="fr_5736"></a><a href="#fn_5736"><font size="1">5737</font></a>
   Now, indeed, the Son of man has not come in His glory; “for we saw Him, and He had no form nor beauty; but His form was dishonoured and defective compared with the sons of men; He was a man in affliction and toil, and acquainted with the enduring of sickness, because His face was turned away, He was dishonoured and not esteemed.” <a name="fr_5737"></a><a href="#fn_5737"><font size="1">5738</font></a>
   And it was necessary that He should come in such form that He might bear our sins <a name="fr_5738"></a><a href="#fn_5738"><font size="1">5739</font></a>
  and suffer pain for us; for it did not become Him in glory to bear our sins and suffer pain for us.  But He also comes in glory, having prepared <a name="fr_5739"></a><a href="#fn_5739"><font size="1">5740</font></a>
  the disciples through that epiphany of His which has no form nor beauty; and, having become as they that they might become as He, “conformed to the image of His glory,” <a name="fr_5740"></a><a href="#fn_5740"><font size="1">5741</font></a>
  since He formerly became conformed to “the body of our humiliation,” <a name="fr_5741"></a><a href="#fn_5741"><font size="1">5742</font></a>
  when He “emptied Himself and took upon Him the form of a servant,” <a name="fr_5742"></a><a href="#fn_5742"><font size="1">5743</font></a>
  He is restored to the image of God and also makes them conformed unto it.</p>
