 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.lii-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.lii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LII.—Such a Confession is Pleasing to God.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.lii-p2"></a>The Lord, brethren, stands in need of nothing; and He desires nothing of any one except that confession be made to Him.  <a name="page_245"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 245</font></a> <a name="xii.iv.lii-Page_245"></a>For, says the elect David, “I will confess unto the Lord; and that will please Him more than a young bullock <a name="fr_4277"></a><a href="#fn_4277"><font size="1">4278</font></a>
  that hath horns and hoofs.  Let the poor see it, and be glad.” <a name="fr_4278"></a><a href="#fn_4278"><font size="1">4279</font></a>
   And again he saith, “Offer <a name="fr_4279"></a><a href="#fn_4279"><font size="1">4280</font></a>
  unto God the sacrifice of praise, and pay thy vows unto the Most High.  And call upon me in the day of thy trouble:  I will deliver thee, and thou shalt glorify me.” <a name="fr_4280"></a><a href="#fn_4280"><font size="1">4281</font></a>
   For “the sacrifice of God is a broken spirit.” <a name="fr_4281"></a><a href="#fn_4281"><font size="1">4282</font></a>
 </p>
