 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xlviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p1.1"></a>Section XLVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_3308"></a><a href="#fn_3308"><font size="1">3309</font></a>
 This said Jesus, and went forth with his disciples to a place which was called Gethsemane, <a name="fr_3309"></a><a href="#fn_3309"><font size="1">3310</font></a>
  on <a name="fr_3310"></a><a href="#fn_3310"><font size="1">3311</font></a>
  the side that is in the plain <a name="fr_3311"></a><a href="#fn_3311"><font size="1">3312</font></a>
  of Kidron, the mountain, <a name="fr_3312"></a><a href="#fn_3312"><font size="1">3313</font></a>
  the place [2] in which was a garden; and he entered thither, he and his disciples.   <a name="fr_3313"></a><a href="#fn_3313"><font size="1">3314</font></a>
 And Judas the [3] betrayer knew that place:  for Jesus oft-times met with his disciples there.   <a name="fr_3314"></a><a href="#fn_3314"><font size="1">3315</font></a>
 And when Jesus came to the place, he said to his disciples, Sit ye here, so that I may go and pray; [4, 5] [Arabic, p. 181] and pray ye, that ye enter not into temptations.   <a name="fr_3315"></a><a href="#fn_3315"><font size="1">3316</font></a>
 And he took with him Cephas and the sons of Zebedee together, James and John; and he began to [6] look sorrowful, and to be anxious.   <a name="fr_3316"></a><a href="#fn_3316"><font size="1">3317</font></a>
 And he said unto them, My soul is distressed unto [7] death:  abide ye here, and watch with me.   <a name="fr_3317"></a><a href="#fn_3317"><font size="1">3318</font></a>
 And he withdrew from them a little, [8] the space of a stones throw;  <a name="fr_3318"></a><a href="#fn_3318"><font size="1">3319</font></a>
 and he kneeled, <a name="fr_3319"></a><a href="#fn_3319"><font size="1">3320</font></a>
  and fell on his face, and prayed, so [9] that, if it <i>were</i> possible, this hour <i>might</i> pass <a name="fr_3320"></a><a href="#fn_3320"><font size="1">3321</font></a>
  him.   <a name="fr_3321"></a><a href="#fn_3321"><font size="1">3322</font></a>
 And he said, Father, thou art able for all things; if thou wilt, let this cup pass me:   <a name="fr_3322"></a><a href="#fn_3322"><font size="1">3323</font></a>
 but let not my will be <i>done</i>, [10] but let thy will be <i>done</i>.   <a name="fr_3323"></a><a href="#fn_3323"><font size="1">3324</font></a>
 And he came to his disciples, and found them sleeping; [11] and he said unto Cephas, Simon, didst thou sleep?   <a name="fr_3324"></a><a href="#fn_3324"><font size="1">3325</font></a>
 Could ye thus not for one hour [12] watch with me?   <a name="fr_3325"></a><a href="#fn_3325"><font size="1">3326</font></a>
 Watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptations:  the spirit is [13] willing and ready, but the body is weak. <a name="fr_3326"></a><a href="#fn_3326"><font size="1">3327</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3327"></a><a href="#fn_3327"><font size="1">3328</font></a>
 And he went again a second time, and prayed, and said, My Father, if it is not possible with regard to <a name="fr_3328"></a><a href="#fn_3328"><font size="1">3329</font></a>
  this cup that it pass, [14] except I drink it, thy will be <i>done</i>.   <a name="fr_3329"></a><a href="#fn_3329"><font size="1">3330</font></a>
 And he returned again, and found his disciples sleeping, for their eyes were heavy from their grief and anxiety; and they knew not [15] what to say to him.   <a name="fr_3330"></a><a href="#fn_3330"><font size="1">3331</font></a>
 And he left them, and went away again, and prayed a third [16] time, and said the very same word.   <a name="fr_3331"></a><a href="#fn_3331"><font size="1">3332</font></a>
 And there appeared unto him an angel from [17] heaven, encouraging him.   <a name="fr_3332"></a><a href="#fn_3332"><font size="1">3333</font></a>
 And being afraid <a name="fr_3333"></a><a href="#fn_3333"><font size="1">3334</font></a>
  he prayed continuously: <a name="fr_3334"></a><a href="#fn_3334"><font size="1">3335</font></a>
   and his sweat <a name="fr_3335"></a><a href="#fn_3335"><font size="1">3336</font></a>
  [18] [Arabic, p. 182] became like a stream of blood, and fell on the ground.   <a name="fr_3336"></a><a href="#fn_3336"><font size="1">3337</font></a>
 Then he rose from [19] his prayer, and came to his disciples, and found them sleeping.   <a name="fr_3337"></a><a href="#fn_3337"><font size="1">3338</font></a>
 And he [20] said unto them, Sleep now, and rest:   <a name="fr_3338"></a><a href="#fn_3338"><font size="1">3339</font></a>
 the end hath arrived, <a name="fr_3339"></a><a href="#fn_3339"><font size="1">3340</font></a>
  and the hour hath come; [21] and behold, the Son of man is betrayed into the hands of sinners.   <a name="fr_3340"></a><a href="#fn_3340"><font size="1">3341</font></a>
 Arise, let us go: <a name="fr_3341"></a><a href="#fn_3341"><font size="1">3342</font></a>
   for he hath come that betrayeth me.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p37"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_3342"></a><a href="#fn_3342"><font size="1">3343</font></a>
 And while he was still speaking, came Judas the betrayer, one of the twelve, and with him a great multitude carrying lanterns and torches <a name="fr_3343"></a><a href="#fn_3343"><font size="1">3344</font></a>
  and swords and staves, from the chief priests and scribes and elders of the people, and with him the footsoldiers [23] of the Romans. <a name="fr_3344"></a><a href="#fn_3344"><font size="1">3345</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3345"></a><a href="#fn_3345"><font size="1">3346</font></a>
 And Judas the betrayer gave them a sign, and said, He whom I shall kiss, he is he:  take him with care, <a name="fr_3346"></a><a href="#fn_3346"><font size="1">3347</font></a>
  and lead him <i>away</i>. <a name="fr_3347"></a><a href="#fn_3347"><font size="1">3348</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p44"></a><a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 118</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xlviii-Page_118"></a>[24]  <a name="fr_3348"></a><a href="#fn_3348"><font size="1">3349</font></a>
 And Jesus, because he knew everything that should come upon him, went forth [25] unto them.   <a name="fr_3349"></a><a href="#fn_3349"><font size="1">3350</font></a>
 And immediately Judas the betrayer came to Jesus, and said, Peace, [26] my Master; and kissed him.   <a name="fr_3350"></a><a href="#fn_3350"><font size="1">3351</font></a>
 And Jesus said unto him, Judas, with a kiss betrayest [27] thou the Son of man?   <a name="fr_3351"></a><a href="#fn_3351"><font size="1">3352</font></a>
 <i>Was it</i> for that thou camest, my friend?  And Jesus said [28] to those that came unto him, Whom seek ye?   <a name="fr_3352"></a><a href="#fn_3352"><font size="1">3353</font></a>
 They said unto him, Jesus the Nazarene.  Jesus said unto them, I am he.  And Judas the betrayer also was standing [29] with them.   <a name="fr_3353"></a><a href="#fn_3353"><font size="1">3354</font></a>
 And when Jesus said unto them, I am he, they retreated backward, and [30] fell to the ground.   <a name="fr_3354"></a><a href="#fn_3354"><font size="1">3355</font></a>
 And Jesus asked them again, Whom seek ye?  They answered, [31] Jesus the Nazarene.   <a name="fr_3355"></a><a href="#fn_3355"><font size="1">3356</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, I told you that I am he:  and if ye seek [32] me, let these go away:  that the word might be fulfilled which he spake,  <a name="fr_3356"></a><a href="#fn_3356"><font size="1">3357</font></a>
 Of those [33] [Arabic, p. 183] whom thou hast given me I lost not even one.   <a name="fr_3357"></a><a href="#fn_3357"><font size="1">3358</font></a>
 Then came those that were with Judas, and seized Jesus, and took him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p55"></a>[34]  <a name="fr_3358"></a><a href="#fn_3358"><font size="1">3359</font></a>
 And when his disciples saw what happened, they said, Our Lord, shall we smite [35] them with swords?   <a name="fr_3359"></a><a href="#fn_3359"><font size="1">3360</font></a>
 And Simon Cephas had a sword, and he drew it, and struck the servant of the chief priest, and cut off his right ear.  And the name of that servant [36] was Malchus.   <a name="fr_3360"></a><a href="#fn_3360"><font size="1">3361</font></a>
 Jesus said unto Cephas, The cup which my Father hath given [37] me, shall I not drink it?   <a name="fr_3361"></a><a href="#fn_3361"><font size="1">3362</font></a>
 Put the sword into its sheath:  for all that take with <a name="fr_3362"></a><a href="#fn_3362"><font size="1">3363</font></a>
  the [38] sword shall die by the sword.   <a name="fr_3363"></a><a href="#fn_3363"><font size="1">3364</font></a>
 Thinkest <a name="fr_3364"></a><a href="#fn_3364"><font size="1">3365</font></a>
  thou that I am not able to ask of my [39] Father, and he shall now raise up for me more than <a name="fr_3365"></a><a href="#fn_3365"><font size="1">3366</font></a>
  twelve tribes of angels?   <a name="fr_3366"></a><a href="#fn_3366"><font size="1">3367</font></a>
 Then [40] how should the scriptures which were spoken be fulfilled, that thus it must be?   <a name="fr_3367"></a><a href="#fn_3367"><font size="1">3368</font></a>
 Your [41] leave in this. <a name="fr_3368"></a><a href="#fn_3368"><font size="1">3369</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3369"></a><a href="#fn_3369"><font size="1">3370</font></a>
 And he touched the ear of him that was struck, and healed it.  And in that hour Jesus said to the multitudes, As they come out against a thief are ye come out against me with swords and staves to take me?  Daily was I with you in [42] the temple sitting teaching, and ye took me not:   <a name="fr_3370"></a><a href="#fn_3370"><font size="1">3371</font></a>
 but this is your hour, and the power [43] of darkness.   <a name="fr_3371"></a><a href="#fn_3371"><font size="1">3372</font></a>
 And that was, that the scriptures of the prophets might be fulfilled.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p70"></a>[44]  <a name="fr_3372"></a><a href="#fn_3372"><font size="1">3373</font></a>
 Then the disciples all left him, and fled.  And the footsoldiers and the officers [45] and the soldiers <a name="fr_3373"></a><a href="#fn_3373"><font size="1">3374</font></a>
  of the Jews seized Jesus, and came.   <a name="fr_3374"></a><a href="#fn_3374"><font size="1">3375</font></a>
 And a certain <a name="fr_3375"></a><a href="#fn_3375"><font size="1">3376</font></a>
  young man [46] followed him, and he was wrapped in a towel, naked:   <a name="fr_3376"></a><a href="#fn_3376"><font size="1">3377</font></a>
 and they seized him; so he [47] [Arabic, p. 184] left the towel, and fled naked.   <a name="fr_3377"></a><a href="#fn_3377"><font size="1">3378</font></a>
 Then they took Jesus, and bound him, and brought him to Annas first; because he was the father in law of Caiaphas, [48] who was chief priest that year.   <a name="fr_3378"></a><a href="#fn_3378"><font size="1">3379</font></a>
 And Caiaphas was he that counselled the Jews, that it was necessary that one man should die instead of the people.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xlviii-p78"></a>[49]  <a name="fr_3379"></a><a href="#fn_3379"><font size="1">3380</font></a>
 And Simon Cephas and one of the other disciples followed Jesus.  And the chief [50] priest knew that disciple, and he entered with Jesus into the court;  <a name="fr_3380"></a><a href="#fn_3380"><font size="1">3381</font></a>
 but Simon was standing without at the door.  And that other disciple, whom the chief priest knew, [51] went out and spake unto her that kept the door, and she brought Simon in.   <a name="fr_3381"></a><a href="#fn_3381"><font size="1">3382</font></a>
 And when the maid that kept the door saw Simon, she looked stedfastly at him, and said unto him, Art not thou also one of the disciples of this man, I mean Jesus the [52] Nazarene?   <a name="fr_3382"></a><a href="#fn_3382"><font size="1">3383</font></a>
 But he denied, and said, Woman, I know him not, neither know I even [53] what thou sayest.   <a name="fr_3383"></a><a href="#fn_3383"><font size="1">3384</font></a>
 And the servants and the soldiers rose, and made a fire in the [54] middle of the court, that they might warm themselves; for it was cold.   <a name="fr_3384"></a><a href="#fn_3384"><font size="1">3385</font></a>
 And when [55] the fire burned up, they sat down around it.   <a name="fr_3385"></a><a href="#fn_3385"><font size="1">3386</font></a>
 And Simon also came, and sat down with them to warm himself, that he might see the end of what should happen.</p>
