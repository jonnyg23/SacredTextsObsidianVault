 <p> <a name="xii.iv.vi"></a>
 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.vi-p1.1"></a>Chapter VI.—Continuation.  Several Other Martyrs.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.vi-p2"></a>To these men who spent their lives in the practice of holiness, there is to be added a great multitude of the elect, who, having through envy endured many indignities and tortures, furnished us with a most excellent example.  Through envy, those women, the Danaids <a name="fr_4032"></a><a href="#fn_4032"><font size="1">4033</font></a>
  and Dircæ, being persecuted, after they had suffered terrible and unspeakable torments, finished the course of their faith with stedfastness, <a name="fr_4033"></a><a href="#fn_4033"><font size="1">4034</font></a>
  and though weak in body, received a noble reward.  Envy has alienated wives from their husbands, and changed that saying of our father Adam, “This is now bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh.” <a name="fr_4034"></a><a href="#fn_4034"><font size="1">4035</font></a>
   Envy and strife have overthrown <a name="fr_4035"></a><a href="#fn_4035"><font size="1">4036</font></a>
  great cities, and rooted up mighty nations.</p>
