 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxxviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p1.1"></a>Section XXXVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p2"></a>[1, 2]  <a name="fr_2595"></a><a href="#fn_2595"><font size="1">2596</font></a>
 And Jesus came to Bethany, and found him <i>already</i> four days in the grave.   <a name="fr_2596"></a><a href="#fn_2596"><font size="1">2597</font></a>
 And Bethany was beside Jerusalem, and its distance from it <i>was</i> a sum of fifteen furlongs; <a name="fr_2597"></a><a href="#fn_2597"><font size="1">2598</font></a>
  [3]  <a name="fr_2598"></a><a href="#fn_2598"><font size="1">2599</font></a>
 and many of the Jews came unto Mary and Martha, to comfort their heart [4] because of their brother.   <a name="fr_2599"></a><a href="#fn_2599"><font size="1">2600</font></a>
 And Martha, when she heard that Jesus had come, went [5] out to meet him:  but Mary was sitting in the house.   <a name="fr_2600"></a><a href="#fn_2600"><font size="1">2601</font></a>
 Martha then said unto Jesus, [6] My Lord, if thou hadst been here, my brother had not died.   <a name="fr_2601"></a><a href="#fn_2601"><font size="1">2602</font></a>
 But I know now that, [7] whatever thou shalt ask of God, he will give thee.   <a name="fr_2602"></a><a href="#fn_2602"><font size="1">2603</font></a>
 Jesus said unto her, Thy brother shall [8] rise.   <a name="fr_2603"></a><a href="#fn_2603"><font size="1">2604</font></a>
 Martha said unto him, I know that he shall rise in the resurrection at the last day.  [9]  <a name="fr_2604"></a><a href="#fn_2604"><font size="1">2605</font></a>
 Jesus said unto her, I am the resurrection, and the life:  whosoever believeth in [10] [Arabic, p. 144] me, even though he die, he shall live:   <a name="fr_2605"></a><a href="#fn_2605"><font size="1">2606</font></a>
 and every living one that believeth [11] in me shall never die.  Believest thou this?   <a name="fr_2606"></a><a href="#fn_2606"><font size="1">2607</font></a>
 She said unto him, Yea, my Lord:  I believe that thou art the Messiah, the Son of God, that cometh into the [12] world.   <a name="fr_2607"></a><a href="#fn_2607"><font size="1">2608</font></a>
 And when she had said that, she went and called Mary her sister secretly, [13] and said unto her, Our Master hath come, and summoneth thee.   <a name="fr_2608"></a><a href="#fn_2608"><font size="1">2609</font></a>
 And Mary, when [14] she heard, rose in haste, and came unto him.   <a name="fr_2609"></a><a href="#fn_2609"><font size="1">2610</font></a>
 (And Jesus then had not come into [15] the village, but was in the place where Martha met him.)   <a name="fr_2610"></a><a href="#fn_2610"><font size="1">2611</font></a>
 And the Jews also that were with her in the house, to comfort her, when they saw that Mary rose up and went out in haste, went after her, because they supposed that she was going to the [16] tomb to weep.   <a name="fr_2611"></a><a href="#fn_2611"><font size="1">2612</font></a>
 And Mary, when she came to where Jesus was, and saw him, fell at his feet, and said unto him, If thou hadst been here, my Lord, my brother had <a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-Page_102"></a>[17] not died.   <a name="fr_2612"></a><a href="#fn_2612"><font size="1">2613</font></a>
 And Jesus came; and when he saw her weeping, and the Jews that were [18] with her weeping, he was troubled <a name="fr_2613"></a><a href="#fn_2613"><font size="1">2614</font></a>
  in himself, and sighed; and he said,  <a name="fr_2614"></a><a href="#fn_2614"><font size="1">2615</font></a>
 In what [19] place have ye laid him?  And they said unto him, Our Lord, come and see.   <a name="fr_2615"></a><a href="#fn_2615"><font size="1">2616</font></a>
 And [20] the tears of Jesus came. <a name="fr_2616"></a><a href="#fn_2616"><font size="1">2617</font></a>
    <a name="fr_2617"></a><a href="#fn_2617"><font size="1">2618</font></a>
 The Jews therefore said, See the greatness of his love for [21] him!   <a name="fr_2618"></a><a href="#fn_2618"><font size="1">2619</font></a>
 But some of them said, Could not this <i>man</i>, who opened the eyes of that [22] blind <i>man</i>, have caused that this <i>man</i> also should not die?   <a name="fr_2619"></a><a href="#fn_2619"><font size="1">2620</font></a>
 And Jesus came to the place of burial, being troubled within himself.  And the place of burial was a cave, [23] and a stone was placed at its door.   <a name="fr_2620"></a><a href="#fn_2620"><font size="1">2621</font></a>
 Jesus therefore said, Take these stones <i>away</i>.  Martha, the sister of him <i>that was</i> dead, said unto him, My Lord, he hath come to [24] stink for some time:  he hath been <a name="fr_2621"></a><a href="#fn_2621"><font size="1">2622</font></a>
  four days <i>dead</i>.   <a name="fr_2622"></a><a href="#fn_2622"><font size="1">2623</font></a>
 Jesus said unto her, Did not I say [25] [Arabic, p. 145] unto thee, If thou believest, thou shalt see the glory of God?   <a name="fr_2623"></a><a href="#fn_2623"><font size="1">2624</font></a>
 And they removed those stones.  And Jesus lifted his eyes on high, and said, My Father, [26] I thank thee since thou didst hear me.   <a name="fr_2624"></a><a href="#fn_2624"><font size="1">2625</font></a>
 And I know that thou at all times hearest me:  but I say this unto thee because of this multitude that is standing, that they [27] may believe that thou didst send me.   <a name="fr_2625"></a><a href="#fn_2625"><font size="1">2626</font></a>
 And when he had said that, he cried with a [28] loud voice, Lazarus, come forth.   <a name="fr_2626"></a><a href="#fn_2626"><font size="1">2627</font></a>
 And that dead <i>man</i> came out, having his hands and feet bound with bandages, and his face wrapped in a scarf.  Jesus said unto them, Loose him, and let him go.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p35"></a>[29]  <a name="fr_2627"></a><a href="#fn_2627"><font size="1">2628</font></a>
 And many of the Jews which came unto Mary, when they saw the deed of Jesus, [30] believed in him.   <a name="fr_2628"></a><a href="#fn_2628"><font size="1">2629</font></a>
 But some of them went to the Pharisees, and informed them of all that Jesus did.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p38"></a>[31]  <a name="fr_2629"></a><a href="#fn_2629"><font size="1">2630</font></a>
 And the chief priests and the Pharisees gathered, and said, What shall we do? [32] for lo, this man doeth many signs.   <a name="fr_2630"></a><a href="#fn_2630"><font size="1">2631</font></a>
 And if we leave him thus, all men will believe [33] in him:  and the Romans will come and take our country and people.   <a name="fr_2631"></a><a href="#fn_2631"><font size="1">2632</font></a>
 And one of them, who was called Caiaphas, the chief priest he was in that year, said unto them, [34]  <a name="fr_2632"></a><a href="#fn_2632"><font size="1">2633</font></a>
 Ye know not anything, nor consider that it is more advantageous for us that one [35] man should die instead of the people, and not that the whole people perish.   <a name="fr_2633"></a><a href="#fn_2633"><font size="1">2634</font></a>
 And this he said not of himself:  but because he was the chief priest of <a name="fr_2634"></a><a href="#fn_2634"><font size="1">2635</font></a>
  that year, he [36] prophesied that Jesus was to die instead of the people;  <a name="fr_2635"></a><a href="#fn_2635"><font size="1">2636</font></a>
 and not instead of the people alone, but that he might gather the scattered children of God together.  [37]  <a name="fr_2636"></a><a href="#fn_2636"><font size="1">2637</font></a>
 And from that day they considered <i>how</i> to kill him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p47"></a>[38] [Arabic, p. 146]  <a name="fr_2637"></a><a href="#fn_2637"><font size="1">2638</font></a>
 And Jesus did not walk openly amongst the Jews, but departed thence to a place near the wilderness, to a town <a name="fr_2638"></a><a href="#fn_2638"><font size="1">2639</font></a>
  called Ephraim; and he was there, going [39] about with his disciples.   <a name="fr_2639"></a><a href="#fn_2639"><font size="1">2640</font></a>
 And the passover of the Jews was near:  and many went [40] up from the villages unto Jerusalem before the feast, to purify themselves.   <a name="fr_2640"></a><a href="#fn_2640"><font size="1">2641</font></a>
 And they sought for Jesus, and said one to another in the temple, What think ye of his [41] holding back from the feast?   <a name="fr_2641"></a><a href="#fn_2641"><font size="1">2642</font></a>
 And the chief priests and the Pharisees had given commandment, that, if any man knew in what place he was, he should reveal <i>it</i> to them, that they might take him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxviii-p53"></a>[42]  <a name="fr_2642"></a><a href="#fn_2642"><font size="1">2643</font></a>
 And when the days of his going up were accomplished, he prepared himself that [43] he might go <a name="fr_2643"></a><a href="#fn_2643"><font size="1">2644</font></a>
  to Jerusalem.   <a name="fr_2644"></a><a href="#fn_2644"><font size="1">2645</font></a>
 And he sent messengers before him, and departed, <a name="fr_2645"></a><a href="#fn_2645"><font size="1">2646</font></a>
  and [44] entered into a village <a name="fr_2646"></a><a href="#fn_2646"><font size="1">2647</font></a>
  of Samaria, that they might make ready for him.   <a name="fr_2647"></a><a href="#fn_2647"><font size="1">2648</font></a>
 And they [45] received him not, because he <a name="fr_2648"></a><a href="#fn_2648"><font size="1">2649</font></a>
  was prepared for going to Jerusalem.   <a name="fr_2649"></a><a href="#fn_2649"><font size="1">2650</font></a>
 And when James and John his disciples saw <i>it</i>, they said unto him, Our Lord, wilt thou that we speak, and fire come down from heaven, to extirpate them, as did Elijah also?  [46]  <a name="fr_2650"></a><a href="#fn_2650"><font size="1">2651</font></a>
 And Jesus turned, and rebuked them, and said, Ye know not of what spirit ye are.  [47]  <a name="fr_2651"></a><a href="#fn_2651"><font size="1">2652</font></a>
 Verily the Son of man did not come to destroy lives, but to give life.  And they went to another village.</p>
