 <p> <a name="iv.iii.ii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p1.1"></a>Section II.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p2"></a>[1] [Arabic, p. 7]  <a name="fr_172"></a><a href="#fn_172"><font size="1">173</font></a>
 Now <a name="fr_173"></a><a href="#fn_173"><font size="1">174</font></a>
  the birth of Jesus the Messiah was on this wise:  In the time when his mother was given in marriage to Joseph, before they came together, [2] she was found with child of the Holy Spirit.   <a name="fr_174"></a><a href="#fn_174"><font size="1">175</font></a>
 And Joseph her husband was a just <i>man</i> and did not wish to expose her, and he purposed to put her away secretly.  [3]  <a name="fr_175"></a><a href="#fn_175"><font size="1">176</font></a>
 But when he thought of this, the angel of the Lord appeared unto him in a dream, and said unto him, Joseph, son of David, fear not to take Mary thy wife, for that [4] which is begotten <a name="fr_176"></a><a href="#fn_176"><font size="1">177</font></a>
  in her is of the Holy Spirit.   <a name="fr_177"></a><a href="#fn_177"><font size="1">178</font></a>
 She shall bear a son, and thou shalt [5] call his name Jesus, and he shall save <a name="fr_178"></a><a href="#fn_178"><font size="1">179</font></a>
  his people from their sins.   <a name="fr_179"></a><a href="#fn_179"><font size="1">180</font></a>
 And all this was that the saying from the Lord by the prophet might be fulfilled:</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p11"></a>[6]  <a name="fr_180"></a><a href="#fn_180"><font size="1">181</font></a>
 Behold, the virgin shall conceive, and bear a son,</p> 
 <p class="c30"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p13"></a>And they shall call his name Immanuel,</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iii.ii-p14"></a>[7] which is, being interpreted, With us is our God.   <a name="fr_181"></a><a href="#fn_181"><font size="1">182</font></a>
 And when Joseph arose from his [8] sleep, he did as the angel of the Lord commanded him, and took his wife;  <a name="fr_182"></a><a href="#fn_182"><font size="1">183</font></a>
 and knew her not until she brought forth her firstborn son.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p17"></a>[9]  <a name="fr_183"></a><a href="#fn_183"><font size="1">184</font></a>
 And in those days there went forth a decree from Augustus Cæsar that all the <a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 46</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.ii-Page_46"></a>[10] people of his dominion <a name="fr_184"></a><a href="#fn_184"><font size="1">185</font></a>
  should be enrolled.  <a name="fr_185"></a><a href="#fn_185"><font size="1">186</font></a>
 This first enrolment was <a name="fr_186"></a><a href="#fn_186"><font size="1">187</font></a>
  while Quirinius [11, 12] was governor of Syria.   <a name="fr_187"></a><a href="#fn_187"><font size="1">188</font></a>
 And every man went to be enrolled in his city.   <a name="fr_188"></a><a href="#fn_188"><font size="1">189</font></a>
 And Joseph went up also from Nazareth, a city of Galilee, to Judæa, to the city of David [13] which is called Bethlehem (for he was of the house of David and of his tribe),  <a name="fr_189"></a><a href="#fn_189"><font size="1">190</font></a>
 with [14] [Arabic, p. 8] Mary his betrothed, she being with child, to be enrolled there.   <a name="fr_190"></a><a href="#fn_190"><font size="1">191</font></a>
 And while [15] she was there the days for her being delivered were accomplished.   <a name="fr_191"></a><a href="#fn_191"><font size="1">192</font></a>
 And she brought forth her firstborn son; and she wrapped him in swaddling cloths and laid him in a manger, because there was no place for them where they were staying.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p27"></a>[16]  <a name="fr_192"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">193</font></a>
 And there were in that region shepherds abiding, keeping their flock in the watch [17] of the night.   <a name="fr_193"></a><a href="#fn_193"><font size="1">194</font></a>
 And behold, the angel of God came unto them, and the glory of the [18] Lord shone upon them; and they were greatly terrified.   <a name="fr_194"></a><a href="#fn_194"><font size="1">195</font></a>
 And the angel said unto them, Be not terrified; for I bring you tidings of great joy which shall be to the [19] whole world;  <a name="fr_195"></a><a href="#fn_195"><font size="1">196</font></a>
 there is born to you this day a Saviour, which is the Lord the Messiah, [20] in the city of David.   <a name="fr_196"></a><a href="#fn_196"><font size="1">197</font></a>
 And this is a sign for you:  ye shall find a babe wrapped [21] in swaddling cloths and laid in a manger.   <a name="fr_197"></a><a href="#fn_197"><font size="1">198</font></a>
 And there appeared with the angels suddenly many heavenly forces praising <a name="fr_198"></a><a href="#fn_198"><font size="1">199</font></a>
  God and saying,</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p35"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_199"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">200</font></a>
 Praise be to God in the highest,</p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p37"></a>And on the earth peace, and good hope to men.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p38"></a>[23]  <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">201</font></a>
 And when the angels departed from them to heaven, the shepherds spake to one another and said, We will go to Bethlehem and see this word which hath been, as [24] the Lord made known unto us.   <a name="fr_201"></a><a href="#fn_201"><font size="1">202</font></a>
 And they came with haste, and found Mary and [25] Joseph, and the babe laid in a manger.   <a name="fr_202"></a><a href="#fn_202"><font size="1">203</font></a>
 And when they saw, they reported the word [26] which was spoken to them about the child.   <a name="fr_203"></a><a href="#fn_203"><font size="1">204</font></a>
 And all that heard wondered at the [27] description which the shepherds described <a name="fr_204"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">205</font></a>
  to them.   <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_205"><font size="1">206</font></a>
 But Mary kept these <a name="fr_206"></a><a href="#fn_206"><font size="1">207</font></a>
  sayings [28] and discriminated <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">208</font></a>
  them in her heart.   <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">209</font></a>
 And those shepherds returned, magnifying and praising God for all that they had seen and heard, according as it was described unto them.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p48"></a>[29] [Arabic, p. 9]  <a name="fr_209"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">210</font></a>
 And when eight days were fulfilled that the child should be circumcised, his name was called Jesus, being that by which he was called by the angel before his conception in the womb.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p50"></a>[30]  <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">211</font></a>
 And when the days of their purification according to the law of Moses were [31] completed, they took him up to Jerusalem to present him before the Lord  <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">212</font></a>
 (as it is written in the law of the Lord, Every male opening the womb shall be called the [32] holy <i>thing</i> of the Lord),  <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">213</font></a>
 and to give a sacrificial victim as it is said in the law of [33] the Lord, A pair of doves or two young pigeons.   <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">214</font></a>
 And there was in Jerusalem a man whose name was Simeon; and this man was upright and pious, and expecting [34] the consolation of Israel; and the Holy Spirit was upon him.   <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_214"><font size="1">215</font></a>
 And it had been said unto him by the Holy Spirit, that he should not see death till he had seen with [35] his eyes the Messiah <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_215"><font size="1">216</font></a>
  of the Lord.   <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="#fn_216"><font size="1">217</font></a>
 And this man came by the Spirit to the temple; and at the time when his parents brought in the child Jesus, that they might [36] present for him a sacrifice, as it is written in the law,  <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="#fn_217"><font size="1">218</font></a>
 he bare him in his arms and praised God and said,</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p59"></a>[37]  <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_218"><font size="1">219</font></a>
 Now loosest thou the bonds of thy servant, O Lord, in peace, <a name="fr_219"></a><a href="#fn_219"><font size="1">220</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p62"></a>According to thy saying;</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p63"></a>[38]  <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_220"><font size="1">221</font></a>
 For mine eye hath witnessed thy mercy,</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p65"></a>[39]  <a name="fr_221"></a><a href="#fn_221"><font size="1">222</font></a>
 Which thou hast made ready because of the whole world;</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p67"></a>[40]  <a name="fr_222"></a><a href="#fn_222"><font size="1">223</font></a>
 A light for the unveiling <a name="fr_223"></a><a href="#fn_223"><font size="1">224</font></a>
  of the nations,</p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p70"></a>And a glory to thy people Israel.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p71"></a>[41]  <a name="fr_224"></a><a href="#fn_224"><font size="1">225</font></a>
 And Joseph and his mother were marvelling at the things which were being said [42] concerning him.   <a name="fr_225"></a><a href="#fn_225"><font size="1">226</font></a>
 And Simeon blessed them and said to Mary his mother, Behold, he is set for the overthrow and rising of many in Israel; and for a sign of contention; [43]  <a name="fr_226"></a><a href="#fn_226"><font size="1">227</font></a>
 and a spear <a name="fr_227"></a><a href="#fn_227"><font size="1">228</font></a>
  shall pierce <a name="fr_228"></a><a href="#fn_228"><font size="1">229</font></a>
  through thine own soul; that the thoughts of the <a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 47</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.ii-Page_47"></a>[44] [Arabic, p. 10] hearts of many may be revealed.   <a name="fr_229"></a><a href="#fn_229"><font size="1">230</font></a>
 And Anna the prophetess, the daughter of Phanuel, of the tribe of Asher, was also advanced in years (and she dwelt [45] with her husband seven years from her virginity,  <a name="fr_230"></a><a href="#fn_230"><font size="1">231</font></a>
 and she remained a widow about eighty-four years); and she left not the temple, and served night and day with [46] fasting and prayer.   <a name="fr_231"></a><a href="#fn_231"><font size="1">232</font></a>
 And she also rose in that hour and thanked the Lord, and she [47] spake of him with every one who was expecting the deliverance of Jerusalem.   <a name="fr_232"></a><a href="#fn_232"><font size="1">233</font></a>
 And when they had accomplished everything according to what is in the law of the Lord, they returned to Galilee, to Nazareth their city.</p>

 <p class="endnote"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p4"></a> This is preceded in Vat. <span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.ii-p4.1"></a>ms</span>. by the genealogy, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001">Matt. i. 1-17</a><a name="iv.iii.ii-p4.2"></a> (see Introduction, 13), with the marginal note <i>The Beginning of the Gospel of Matthew</i>.  (Lagarde, <i>op. cit</i>., 1886, p. 154.)  The text presents nothing worthy of note in this place except that verse 16, construed on the same principle as the preceding verses, to which, except in the words printed in italics, it is strictly parallel in construction, reads thus:  “Jacob begat Joseph, <i>the husband of Mary</i>, who <i>of her</i> begat Jesus, the Messiah” (<i>cf</i>. the remarkable reading of Sin. Syriac).  As it stands, this is the only possible interpretation of the words, for <i>who</i> is masculine.  But a mistake in the gender of a relative pronoun is very common in Arabic among illiterate people, while in Syriac there is, to begin with, no distinction.  If then we correct the relative, <i>who of her</i> will become <i>of whom</i> (fem.), and <i>begat</i> will of course be construed as passive.  We thus get the text followed in Ibn-at-Tayyibs Commentary, the ordinary reading of the Peshitta, <i>of whom was born Jesus</i>.</p>
