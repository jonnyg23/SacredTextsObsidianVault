 <p> <a name="iv.iii.lii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p1.1"></a>Section LII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_3617"></a><a href="#fn_3617"><font size="1">3618</font></a>
 And after that, Jesus knew that all things were finished; and that the scripture [2] might be accomplished, he said, I thirst.   <a name="fr_3618"></a><a href="#fn_3618"><font size="1">3619</font></a>
 And there was set a vessel full of vinegar:  and in that hour one of them hasted, and took a sponge, and filled it with that [3] vinegar,  <a name="fr_3619"></a><a href="#fn_3619"><font size="1">3620</font></a>
 and fastened it on a reed, and brought it near <a name="fr_3620"></a><a href="#fn_3620"><font size="1">3621</font></a>
  his mouth to give him a [4] drink.   <a name="fr_3621"></a><a href="#fn_3621"><font size="1">3622</font></a>
 And when Jesus had taken that vinegar, he said, Everything is finished.  [5]  <a name="fr_3622"></a><a href="#fn_3622"><font size="1">3623</font></a>
 But the rest said, Let be, that we may <a name="fr_3623"></a><a href="#fn_3623"><font size="1">3624</font></a>
  see whether Elijah cometh to save him.  [6, 7]  <a name="fr_3624"></a><a href="#fn_3624"><font size="1">3625</font></a>
 And Jesus said, My Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do.  And Jesus cried again with a loud voice, and said, My Father, into thy hands I commend <a name="fr_3625"></a><a href="#fn_3625"><font size="1">3626</font></a>
  my spirit.   <a name="fr_3626"></a><a href="#fn_3626"><font size="1">3627</font></a>
 He said that, and bowed his head, and gave up his spirit.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p13"></a>[8]  <a name="fr_3627"></a><a href="#fn_3627"><font size="1">3628</font></a>
 And immediately the face of <a name="fr_3628"></a><a href="#fn_3628"><font size="1">3629</font></a>
  the door of the temple was rent into two parts from [9] top to bottom;  <a name="fr_3629"></a><a href="#fn_3629"><font size="1">3630</font></a>
 and the earth was shaken; and the stones were split to pieces; and the [Arabic, p. 197] tombs were opened; and the bodies of many saints which slept, arose and [10] came forth;  <a name="fr_3630"></a><a href="#fn_3630"><font size="1">3631</font></a>
 and after his resurrection they entered into the holy city and [11] appeared unto many.   <a name="fr_3631"></a><a href="#fn_3631"><font size="1">3632</font></a>
 And the officer of the footsoldiers, and they that were with him who were guarding Jesus, <a name="fr_3632"></a><a href="#fn_3632"><font size="1">3633</font></a>
  when they saw the earthquake, and the things which came [12] to pass, feared greatly, and praised God, and said,  <a name="fr_3633"></a><a href="#fn_3633"><font size="1">3634</font></a>
 This man <i>was</i> righteous; and, [13] Truly he was the Son of God.   <a name="fr_3634"></a><a href="#fn_3634"><font size="1">3635</font></a>
 And all the multitudes that were come together to the sight, when they saw what came to pass, returned and smote upon their breasts.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p22"></a><a name="page_124"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 124</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.lii-Page_124"></a>[14]  <a name="fr_3635"></a><a href="#fn_3635"><font size="1">3636</font></a>
 And the Jews, because of the Friday, said, Let these bodies not remain on their crosses, <a name="fr_3636"></a><a href="#fn_3636"><font size="1">3637</font></a>
  because it is the morning of the sabbath (for that sabbath was a great day); and they asked of Pilate that they might break the legs of those that were [15] crucified, and take them down.   <a name="fr_3637"></a><a href="#fn_3637"><font size="1">3638</font></a>
 And the soldiers came, and brake the legs of the [16] first, and that other which was crucified with him:   <a name="fr_3638"></a><a href="#fn_3638"><font size="1">3639</font></a>
 but when they came to Jesus, [17] they saw that he had died before, so they brake not his legs:   <a name="fr_3639"></a><a href="#fn_3639"><font size="1">3640</font></a>
 but one of the soldiers pierced <a name="fr_3640"></a><a href="#fn_3640"><font size="1">3641</font></a>
  him in his side with a spear, and immediately there came forth blood and [18] water.   <a name="fr_3641"></a><a href="#fn_3641"><font size="1">3642</font></a>
 And he that hath seen hath borne witness, and his witness is true:  and he [19] knoweth that he hath said the truth, that ye also may believe.   <a name="fr_3642"></a><a href="#fn_3642"><font size="1">3643</font></a>
 This he did, that [20] the scripture might be fulfilled, which saith, A bone shall not be broken in him;  <a name="fr_3643"></a><a href="#fn_3643"><font size="1">3644</font></a>
 and the scripture also which saith, Let them look upon him whom they pierced. <a name="fr_3644"></a><a href="#fn_3644"><font size="1">3645</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p33"></a>[21]  <a name="fr_3645"></a><a href="#fn_3645"><font size="1">3646</font></a>
 And there were in the distance all the acquaintance of Jesus standing, and the women that came with him from Galilee, those that followed him and ministered.  [22]  <a name="fr_3646"></a><a href="#fn_3646"><font size="1">3647</font></a>
 One of them was Mary Magdalene; and Mary the mother of James the little and [23] [Arabic, p. 198] Joses,  <a name="fr_3647"></a><a href="#fn_3647"><font size="1">3648</font></a>
 and the mother of the sons of Zebedee, and Salome, and many others which came up with him unto Jerusalem;  <a name="fr_3648"></a><a href="#fn_3648"><font size="1">3649</font></a>
 and they saw that.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p38"></a>[24]  <a name="fr_3649"></a><a href="#fn_3649"><font size="1">3650</font></a>
 And when the evening of the Friday was come, because of the entering of the [25] sabbath,  <a name="fr_3650"></a><a href="#fn_3650"><font size="1">3651</font></a>
 there came a rich man, <a name="fr_3651"></a><a href="#fn_3651"><font size="1">3652</font></a>
  a noble <a name="fr_3652"></a><a href="#fn_3652"><font size="1">3653</font></a>
  of Ramah, <a name="fr_3653"></a><a href="#fn_3653"><font size="1">3654</font></a>
  a city of Judah, <a name="fr_3654"></a><a href="#fn_3654"><font size="1">3655</font></a>
  named Joseph, and he was a good man and upright;  <a name="fr_3655"></a><a href="#fn_3655"><font size="1">3656</font></a>
 and he was a <a name="fr_3656"></a><a href="#fn_3656"><font size="1">3657</font></a>
  disciple of Jesus, but [26] was concealing himself for fear of the Jews.   <a name="fr_3657"></a><a href="#fn_3657"><font size="1">3658</font></a>
 And he did not agree with the accusers [27] in their desire and their deeds:   <a name="fr_3658"></a><a href="#fn_3658"><font size="1">3659</font></a>
 and he was looking for the kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_3659"></a><a href="#fn_3659"><font size="1">3660</font></a>
 And this man went boldly, and entered in unto Pilate, and asked of him the body of [28] Jesus.   <a name="fr_3660"></a><a href="#fn_3660"><font size="1">3661</font></a>
 And Pilate wondered how he had died already:  and he called the officer of [29] the footsoldiers, and asked him concerning his death before the time.   <a name="fr_3661"></a><a href="#fn_3661"><font size="1">3662</font></a>
 And when [30] he knew, he commanded him to deliver up his body unto Joseph.   <a name="fr_3662"></a><a href="#fn_3662"><font size="1">3663</font></a>
 And Joseph bought for him a winding cloth of pure linen, and took down the body of Jesus, [31] and wound it in it; and they came and took it.   <a name="fr_3663"></a><a href="#fn_3663"><font size="1">3664</font></a>
 And there came unto him Nicodemus also, who of old came unto Jesus by night; and he brought with him perfume <a name="fr_3664"></a><a href="#fn_3664"><font size="1">3665</font></a>
  [32] of myrrh and aloes, about a hundred pounds.   <a name="fr_3665"></a><a href="#fn_3665"><font size="1">3666</font></a>
 And they took the body of Jesus, and wound it in the linen and the perfume, as was the custom of the Jews to bury.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p56"></a>[33]  <a name="fr_3666"></a><a href="#fn_3666"><font size="1">3667</font></a>
 And there was in the place where Jesus was crucified a garden; and in that garden [34] a new tomb cut out in a rock, <a name="fr_3667"></a><a href="#fn_3667"><font size="1">3668</font></a>
  wherein was never man yet laid.   <a name="fr_3668"></a><a href="#fn_3668"><font size="1">3669</font></a>
 And they left [35] Jesus there because the sabbath had come in, and because the tomb was near.   <a name="fr_3669"></a><a href="#fn_3669"><font size="1">3670</font></a>
 And they pushed <a name="fr_3670"></a><a href="#fn_3670"><font size="1">3671</font></a>
  a great stone, and thrust <a name="fr_3671"></a><a href="#fn_3671"><font size="1">3672</font></a>
  it against the door of the sepulchre, and [36] went away.   <a name="fr_3672"></a><a href="#fn_3672"><font size="1">3673</font></a>
 And Mary Magdalene and Mary that was related to Joses came to [37] [Arabic, p. 199] the sepulchre after them, <a name="fr_3673"></a><a href="#fn_3673"><font size="1">3674</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3674"></a><a href="#fn_3674"><font size="1">3675</font></a>
 and sat opposite the sepulchre, <a name="fr_3675"></a><a href="#fn_3675"><font size="1">3676</font></a>
  and saw the [38] body, how they took it in and laid it there.   <a name="fr_3676"></a><a href="#fn_3676"><font size="1">3677</font></a>
 And they returned, and bought ointment <a name="fr_3677"></a><a href="#fn_3677"><font size="1">3678</font></a>
  and perfume, <a name="fr_3678"></a><a href="#fn_3678"><font size="1">3679</font></a>
  and prepared <a name="fr_3679"></a><a href="#fn_3679"><font size="1">3680</font></a>
  <i>it</i>, that they might come and anoint him.  [39]  <a name="fr_3680"></a><a href="#fn_3680"><font size="1">3681</font></a>
 And on the day which was the sabbath day they desisted according to the command.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p72"></a>[40, 41]  <a name="fr_3681"></a><a href="#fn_3681"><font size="1">3682</font></a>
 And the chief priests and the Pharisees gathered unto Pilate, and said unto him,  <a name="fr_3682"></a><a href="#fn_3682"><font size="1">3683</font></a>
 Our lord, we remember that that misleader said, while he was alive, After three days [42] I rise.   <a name="fr_3683"></a><a href="#fn_3683"><font size="1">3684</font></a>
 And now send beforehand and guard the tomb <a name="fr_3684"></a><a href="#fn_3684"><font size="1">3685</font></a>
  until the third day, <a name="fr_3685"></a><a href="#fn_3685"><font size="1">3686</font></a>
  lest his disciples come and steal him by night, and they will say unto the people that he [43] is risen from the dead:  and the last error shall be worse than the first.   <a name="fr_3686"></a><a href="#fn_3686"><font size="1">3687</font></a>
 He said unto them, And have ye not guards? <a name="fr_3687"></a><a href="#fn_3687"><font size="1">3688</font></a>
  go, and take precautions as ye know <i>how</i>.  [44]  <a name="fr_3688"></a><a href="#fn_3688"><font size="1">3689</font></a>
 And they went, and set <i>guards</i> at the tomb, and sealed that stone, with the guards.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.lii-p81"></a>[45]  <a name="fr_3689"></a><a href="#fn_3689"><font size="1">3690</font></a>
 And in the evening of the sabbath, which is the morning of the first <i>day</i>, and in [46] the dawning <a name="fr_3690"></a><a href="#fn_3690"><font size="1">3691</font></a>
  while the darkness yet remained, came Mary Magdalene and the other Mary and other women to see the tomb.   <a name="fr_3691"></a><a href="#fn_3691"><font size="1">3692</font></a>
 They brought with them the [47] perfume which they had prepared, and said among themselves,  <a name="fr_3692"></a><a href="#fn_3692"><font size="1">3693</font></a>
 Who is it that will <a name="page_125"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 125</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.lii-Page_125"></a>[48] remove for us the stone from the door of the tomb? for it was very great.   <a name="fr_3693"></a><a href="#fn_3693"><font size="1">3694</font></a>
 And when they said thus, there occurred a great earthquake; and an angel came down [49] from heaven, and came and removed the stone from the door.   <a name="fr_3694"></a><a href="#fn_3694"><font size="1">3695</font></a>
 And they came and found the stone removed from the sepulchre, and the angel sitting upon the [50] stone.   <a name="fr_3695"></a><a href="#fn_3695"><font size="1">3696</font></a>
 And his appearance was as the lightning, and his raiment white as the [51] snow:   <a name="fr_3696"></a><a href="#fn_3696"><font size="1">3697</font></a>
 and for fear of him the guards were troubled, and became as dead <i>men</i>.  [52]  <a name="fr_3697"></a><a href="#fn_3697"><font size="1">3698</font></a>
 And when he went away, the women entered into the sepulchre; and they found [53] [Arabic, p. 200] not the body of Jesus.   <a name="fr_3698"></a><a href="#fn_3698"><font size="1">3699</font></a>
 And they saw there a young man sitting on the [54] right, arrayed in a white garment; and they were amazed. <a name="fr_3699"></a><a href="#fn_3699"><font size="1">3700</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3700"></a><a href="#fn_3700"><font size="1">3701</font></a>
 And the angel answered and said unto the women, Fear ye not:  for I know that ye seek Jesus the [55] Nazarene, who hath been crucified.  He is not here; but he is risen, as he said.   <a name="fr_3701"></a><a href="#fn_3701"><font size="1">3702</font></a>
 Come and see the place where our Lord lay.</p>
