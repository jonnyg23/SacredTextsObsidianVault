 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xviii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xviii-p1.1"></a>Section XVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xviii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1265"><font size="1">1266</font></a>
 At that time Herod the tetrarch heard of the fame of Jesus, and all the things which came to pass at his hand;  <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1266"><font size="1">1267</font></a>
 and he marvelled, for he had obtained excellent [2] information concerning him. <a name="fr_1267"></a><a href="#fn_1267"><font size="1">1268</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1268"></a><a href="#fn_1268"><font size="1">1269</font></a>
 And <i>some</i> men said that John the Baptist was risen [3] from among the dead;  <a name="fr_1269"></a><a href="#fn_1269"><font size="1">1270</font></a>
 and <a name="fr_1270"></a><a href="#fn_1270"><font size="1">1271</font></a>
  others said that Elijah had appeared; and others, Jeremiah; [4] and others, that a prophet of the old prophets was risen;  <a name="fr_1271"></a><a href="#fn_1271"><font size="1">1272</font></a>
 and others said that he [5] was a prophet like one of the prophets.   <a name="fr_1272"></a><a href="#fn_1272"><font size="1">1273</font></a>
 Herod said to his servants, This is John the Baptist, he whom I beheaded; he is risen from among the dead:  therefore mighty [6] [Arabic, p. 70] works result from him.   <a name="fr_1273"></a><a href="#fn_1273"><font size="1">1274</font></a>
 For Herod him<i>self</i> had sent and taken John, and cast him into prison, for the sake of Herodias his brother Philips wife, whom he [7] had taken.   <a name="fr_1274"></a><a href="#fn_1274"><font size="1">1275</font></a>
 And John said to Herod, Thou hast no authority to take the wife of thy [8] brother.   <a name="fr_1275"></a><a href="#fn_1275"><font size="1">1276</font></a>
 And Herodias avoided him and wished to kill him; and she could not.  [9]  <a name="fr_1276"></a><a href="#fn_1276"><font size="1">1277</font></a>
 But Herod feared John, for he knew that he was a righteous man <i>and</i> a holy; and [10] he guarded him, and heard him much, and did, and obeyed him with gladness.   <a name="fr_1277"></a><a href="#fn_1277"><font size="1">1278</font></a>
 And he wished to kill him; but he feared the people, for they adhered to him as the [11] prophet.   <a name="fr_1278"></a><a href="#fn_1278"><font size="1">1279</font></a>
 And there was a celebrated day, and Herod had made a feast for his great men on the day of his anniversary, <a name="fr_1279"></a><a href="#fn_1279"><font size="1">1280</font></a>
  and for the officers and for the chief men [12] of Galilee.   <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1280"><font size="1">1281</font></a>
 And the daughter of Herodias came in and danced in the midst of the company, and pleased Herod and those that sat with him.  And the king said to the [13] damsel, Ask of me what thou wilt, and I will give it thee.   <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">1282</font></a>
 And he sware unto her, [14] Whatsoever thou shalt ask, I will give it thee, to the half of my kingdom.   <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1282"><font size="1">1283</font></a>
 And she went out, and said unto her mother, What shall I ask him? <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1283"><font size="1">1284</font></a>
   She said unto her, The [15] head of John the Baptist.   <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">1285</font></a>
 And immediately she came in hastily to the king, and said unto him, I desire in this hour that thou give me on a dish the head of John [16] the Baptist.   <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">1286</font></a>
 And the king was exceeding sorry; but because of the oath and the [17] guests he did not wish to refuse her.   <a name="fr_1286"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">1287</font></a>
 But immediately the king sent an executioner, and commanded that he should bring the head of John:  and he went and cut off <a name="page_72"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 72</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xviii-Page_72"></a>[18] the head of John in the prison,  <a name="fr_1287"></a><a href="#fn_1287"><font size="1">1288</font></a>
 and brought it on a dish, and delivered it to the [19] damsel; and the damsel gave it to her mother.   <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">1289</font></a>
 And his disciples heard, and came [Arabic, p. 71] and took his body, and buried it.   <a name="fr_1289"></a><a href="#fn_1289"><font size="1">1290</font></a>
 And they came and told <a name="fr_1290"></a><a href="#fn_1290"><font size="1">1291</font></a>
  Jesus what [20] had happened.   <a name="fr_1291"></a><a href="#fn_1291"><font size="1">1292</font></a>
 And for this cause Herod said, I beheaded John:  who [21] is this, of whom I hear these things.  And he desired to see him.   <a name="fr_1292"></a><a href="#fn_1292"><font size="1">1293</font></a>
 And Jesus, when he heard, removed thence in a boat to a waste place alone, to the other side of the sea of the Galilee of Tiberias. <a name="fr_1293"></a><a href="#fn_1293"><font size="1">1294</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xviii-p32"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_1294"></a><a href="#fn_1294"><font size="1">1295</font></a>
 And many saw them going, and knew them, and hastened by land <a name="fr_1295"></a><a href="#fn_1295"><font size="1">1296</font></a>
  from all the cities, and came thither beforehand;  <a name="fr_1296"></a><a href="#fn_1296"><font size="1">1297</font></a>
 for they saw the signs which he was doing on the [23, 24] sick.   <a name="fr_1297"></a><a href="#fn_1297"><font size="1">1298</font></a>
 And Jesus went up into the mountain, and sat there with his disciples.   <a name="fr_1298"></a><a href="#fn_1298"><font size="1">1299</font></a>
 And [25] the feast of the passover of the Jews was near.   <a name="fr_1299"></a><a href="#fn_1299"><font size="1">1300</font></a>
 And Jesus lifted up his eyes, and saw great multitudes coming to him.   <a name="fr_1300"></a><a href="#fn_1300"><font size="1">1301</font></a>
 And he was moved with compassion for them, for [26] they were like sheep that <i>were</i> without a shepherd.   <a name="fr_1301"></a><a href="#fn_1301"><font size="1">1302</font></a>
 And he received them, and spake to them concerning the kingdom of God, and healed those who had need of healing.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xviii-p41"></a>[27]  <a name="fr_1302"></a><a href="#fn_1302"><font size="1">1303</font></a>
 And when the evening approached, <a name="fr_1303"></a><a href="#fn_1303"><font size="1">1304</font></a>
  his disciples came to him, and said unto [28] him,  <a name="fr_1304"></a><a href="#fn_1304"><font size="1">1305</font></a>
 The place is desert, and the time is past; send away the multitudes of the people, <a name="fr_1305"></a><a href="#fn_1305"><font size="1">1306</font></a>
  that they may go to the towns and villages which are around us, and buy for [29] themselves bread; for they have nothing to eat.   <a name="fr_1306"></a><a href="#fn_1306"><font size="1">1307</font></a>
 But he said unto them, They have [30] no need to go away; give ye them what may be eaten.   <a name="fr_1307"></a><a href="#fn_1307"><font size="1">1308</font></a>
 They said unto him, We have not here <i>enough</i>.   <a name="fr_1308"></a><a href="#fn_1308"><font size="1">1309</font></a>
 He said unto Philip, Whence shall we buy bread that these may eat?  [31, 32]  <a name="fr_1309"></a><a href="#fn_1309"><font size="1">1310</font></a>
 And he said that proving him; and he knew what he was resolved to do.   <a name="fr_1310"></a><a href="#fn_1310"><font size="1">1311</font></a>
 Philip said [Arabic, p. 72] unto him, Two hundred pennyworth of bread would not suffice them after <a name="fr_1311"></a><a href="#fn_1311"><font size="1">1312</font></a>
  [33] every one of them hath taken a small amount.   <a name="fr_1312"></a><a href="#fn_1312"><font size="1">1313</font></a>
 One of his disciples said unto [34] him (namely, Andrew the brother of Simon Cephas),  <a name="fr_1313"></a><a href="#fn_1313"><font size="1">1314</font></a>
 Here is a lad having five loaves [35] of barley and two fishes:  but this amount, what is it for all these?   <a name="fr_1314"></a><a href="#fn_1314"><font size="1">1315</font></a>
 But wilt thou that we go and buy for all the people what may be eaten? for we have no more [36] than these five loaves and the two fishes.   <a name="fr_1315"></a><a href="#fn_1315"><font size="1">1316</font></a>
 And the grass was plentiful in that place.  Jesus said unto them, Arrange all the people that they may sit down on the grass, [37] fifty people in a company.   <a name="fr_1316"></a><a href="#fn_1316"><font size="1">1317</font></a>
 And the disciples did so.  And all the people sat down [38] by companies, by hundreds and fifties.   <a name="fr_1317"></a><a href="#fn_1317"><font size="1">1318</font></a>
 Then Jesus said unto them, Bring hither [39] those five loaves and the two fishes.   <a name="fr_1318"></a><a href="#fn_1318"><font size="1">1319</font></a>
 And when they brought him that, Jesus took the bread and the fish, and looked to heaven, and blessed, and divided, and gave to [40] his disciples to set before them;  <a name="fr_1319"></a><a href="#fn_1319"><font size="1">1320</font></a>
 and the disciples set for the multitudes the bread [41] and the fish; and they ate, all of them, and were satisfied.   <a name="fr_1320"></a><a href="#fn_1320"><font size="1">1321</font></a>
 And when they were satisfied, he said unto his disciples, Gather the fragments that remain over, that nothing [42] be lost.   <a name="fr_1321"></a><a href="#fn_1321"><font size="1">1322</font></a>
 And they gathered, and filled twelve baskets with fragments, being those that remained over from those which ate of the five barley loaves and the two [43] fishes.   <a name="fr_1322"></a><a href="#fn_1322"><font size="1">1323</font></a>
 And those people who ate were five thousand, besides the women and children.  [44] [Arabic, p. 73]  <a name="fr_1323"></a><a href="#fn_1323"><font size="1">1324</font></a>
 And straightway he pressed his disciples to go up into the ship, and that they should go before him unto the other side to Bethsaida, while he [45] him<i>self</i> should send away the multitudes.   <a name="fr_1324"></a><a href="#fn_1324"><font size="1">1325</font></a>
 And those people who saw the sign which [46] Jesus did, said, Of a truth this is a prophet who hath come into the world.   <a name="fr_1325"></a><a href="#fn_1325"><font size="1">1326</font></a>
 And Jesus knew their purpose to come and take him, and make him a king; and he left them, and went up into the mountain alone for prayer.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xviii-p66"></a>[47, 48]  <a name="fr_1326"></a><a href="#fn_1326"><font size="1">1327</font></a>
 And when the nightfall was near, his disciples went down unto the sea, and sat <a name="fr_1327"></a><a href="#fn_1327"><font size="1">1328</font></a>
  in a boat, and came to the side of Capernaum.   <a name="fr_1328"></a><a href="#fn_1328"><font size="1">1329</font></a>
 And the darkness came on, and Jesus [49] had not come to them.   <a name="fr_1329"></a><a href="#fn_1329"><font size="1">1330</font></a>
 And the sea was stirred up against them by reason of a violent [50] wind that blew.   <a name="fr_1330"></a><a href="#fn_1330"><font size="1">1331</font></a>
 And the boat was distant from the land many furlongs, and they were much damaged by the waves, and the wind was against them.</p>
