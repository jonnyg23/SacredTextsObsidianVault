 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxv"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p1.1"></a>Section XXV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1746"></a><a href="#fn_1746"><font size="1">1747</font></a>
 And in that day this thought presented itself to his disciples, and they said, which [2] haply should be the great<i>est</i> among them. <a name="fr_1747"></a><a href="#fn_1747"><font size="1">1748</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1748"></a><a href="#fn_1748"><font size="1">1749</font></a>
 And when they came to Capernaum, and entered into the house, Jesus said unto them, What were ye considering in the [3] way among yourselves?   <a name="fr_1749"></a><a href="#fn_1749"><font size="1">1750</font></a>
 And they were silent because they had considered that <i>matter</i>.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p7"></a>[4]  <a name="fr_1750"></a><a href="#fn_1750"><font size="1">1751</font></a>
 And when Simon went forth without, those that received two dirhams for the tribute came to Cephas, and said unto him, Doth your master not give his two [5] dirhams?  He said unto them, Yea.   <a name="fr_1751"></a><a href="#fn_1751"><font size="1">1752</font></a>
 And when Cephas entered the house, Jesus anticipated him, and said unto him, What thinkest thou, Simon? the kings of the earth, from whom do they receive custom and tribute? from their sons, or from [6] [Arabic, p. 97] strangers?   <a name="fr_1752"></a><a href="#fn_1752"><font size="1">1753</font></a>
 Simon said unto him, From strangers.  Jesus said unto him, Children then are free.  Simon said unto him, Yea.  Jesus said unto him, [7] Give thou also unto them, like the stranger.   <a name="fr_1753"></a><a href="#fn_1753"><font size="1">1754</font></a>
 But, lest it trouble them, go thou to the sea, and cast <i>a</i> hook; and the first fish that cometh up, open its mouth, <i>and</i> thou shalt find a stater:  take therefore that, and give for me and thee.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p12"></a>[8]  <a name="fr_1754"></a><a href="#fn_1754"><font size="1">1755</font></a>
 And in that hour came the disciples to Jesus, and said unto him, Who, thinkest [9] thou, is greater in the kingdom of heaven?   <a name="fr_1755"></a><a href="#fn_1755"><font size="1">1756</font></a>
 And Jesus knew the thought of their heart, and called a <a name="fr_1756"></a><a href="#fn_1756"><font size="1">1757</font></a>
  child, and set him in the midst, and took him in his arms, and [10] said unto them,  <a name="fr_1757"></a><a href="#fn_1757"><font size="1">1758</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, If ye do not return, and become as children, [11] ye shall not enter the kingdom of heaven.   <a name="fr_1758"></a><a href="#fn_1758"><font size="1">1759</font></a>
 Every one that shall receive in my name such as this child hath received me:   <a name="fr_1759"></a><a href="#fn_1759"><font size="1">1760</font></a>
 and whosoever receiveth me receiveth [12] not me, but him that sent me.   <a name="fr_1760"></a><a href="#fn_1760"><font size="1">1761</font></a>
 And he who is little in your company, <a name="fr_1761"></a><a href="#fn_1761"><font size="1">1762</font></a>
  the same [13] shall be great.   <a name="fr_1762"></a><a href="#fn_1762"><font size="1">1763</font></a>
 But whosoever shall injure one of these little ones that believe in me, it were better for him that a great millstone <a name="fr_1763"></a><a href="#fn_1763"><font size="1">1764</font></a>
  should be hanged about his neck, and <i>he should be</i> drowned in the depths of the sea.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p23"></a>[14]  <a name="fr_1764"></a><a href="#fn_1764"><font size="1">1765</font></a>
 John answered and said, Our Master, we saw one casting out devils in thy name; [15] and we prevented him, because he followed not thee with us.   <a name="fr_1765"></a><a href="#fn_1765"><font size="1">1766</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, Prevent him not; for no man doeth powers in my name, and can hasten to speak evil [16, 17] of me.   <a name="fr_1766"></a><a href="#fn_1766"><font size="1">1767</font></a>
 Every one who is not in opposition to you is with you.   <a name="fr_1767"></a><a href="#fn_1767"><font size="1">1768</font></a>
 Woe unto the world [Arabic, p. 98] because of trials! <a name="fr_1768"></a><a href="#fn_1768"><font size="1">1769</font></a>
  but woe unto that man by whose hand the trials come!  [18]  <a name="fr_1769"></a><a href="#fn_1769"><font size="1">1770</font></a>
 If thy hand or thy foot injure thee, cut it off, and cast it from thee; for it is better for thee to enter into life being halt or maimed, and not that thou shouldest have two hands or two feet, and fall into the hell of fire that burneth <a name="fr_1770"></a><a href="#fn_1770"><font size="1">1771</font></a>
  for ever; [19, 20]  <a name="fr_1771"></a><a href="#fn_1771"><font size="1">1772</font></a>
 where their worm dieth not, and their fire is not quenched.   <a name="fr_1772"></a><a href="#fn_1772"><font size="1">1773</font></a>
 And if thine eye seduce <a name="fr_1773"></a><a href="#fn_1773"><font size="1">1774</font></a>
  [21] thee, pluck it out, and cast it from thee;  <a name="fr_1774"></a><a href="#fn_1774"><font size="1">1775</font></a>
 for it is better for thee to enter the kingdom of God with one eye, than that thou shouldest have two eyes, and fall into the [22, 23] fire of Gehenna;  <a name="fr_1775"></a><a href="#fn_1775"><font size="1">1776</font></a>
 where their worm dieth not, and their fire is not quenched.   <a name="fr_1776"></a><a href="#fn_1776"><font size="1">1777</font></a>
 Every [24] <i>one</i> shall be salted with fire, and every sacrifice shall be salted with salt.   <a name="fr_1777"></a><a href="#fn_1777"><font size="1">1778</font></a>
 How good [25] is salt! but if the salt also be tasteless, wherewith shall it be salted?   <a name="fr_1778"></a><a href="#fn_1778"><font size="1">1779</font></a>
 It is fit neither for the land nor for dung, but they cast it out.  He that hath ears to hear, let him [26] hear.   <a name="fr_1779"></a><a href="#fn_1779"><font size="1">1780</font></a>
 Have ye salt in yourselves, and be in peace one with another.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p40"></a>[27]  <a name="fr_1780"></a><a href="#fn_1780"><font size="1">1781</font></a>
 And he arose from thence, and came to the borders of Judæa beyond Jordan:  and there went unto him thither great multitudes, and he healed them; and he taught [28] them also, according to his custom.   <a name="fr_1781"></a><a href="#fn_1781"><font size="1">1782</font></a>
 And the Pharisees came unto him, tempting [29] him, and asking him, Is it lawful for a man to put away his wife?   <a name="fr_1782"></a><a href="#fn_1782"><font size="1">1783</font></a>
 He said, What [30] did Moses command you?   <a name="fr_1783"></a><a href="#fn_1783"><font size="1">1784</font></a>
 They said, Moses made it allowable for us, <i>saying</i>, Whosoever [31] will, let him write a writing of divorcement, and put away his wife.   <a name="fr_1784"></a><a href="#fn_1784"><font size="1">1785</font></a>
 Jesus <a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 83</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxv-Page_83"></a>answered and said unto them,  <a name="fr_1785"></a><a href="#fn_1785"><font size="1">1786</font></a>
 Have ye not read, He that made <i>them</i> from the beginning [32] made them male and female, and said,  <a name="fr_1786"></a><a href="#fn_1786"><font size="1">1787</font></a>
 For this reason shall the man leave his father [Arabic, p. 99] and his mother, and cleave to his wife; and they both shall be one body?  [33]  <a name="fr_1787"></a><a href="#fn_1787"><font size="1">1788</font></a>
 So then they are not twain, but one body; the thing, then, which God hath [34] joined together, let no man put asunder.   <a name="fr_1788"></a><a href="#fn_1788"><font size="1">1789</font></a>
 And those Pharisees said unto him, Why did Moses consent <a name="fr_1789"></a><a href="#fn_1789"><font size="1">1790</font></a>
  that a <i>man</i> should give a writing of divorcement and put her away?  [35]  <a name="fr_1790"></a><a href="#fn_1790"><font size="1">1791</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, Moses because of the hardness of your hearts gave you leave [36] to divorce your wives; but in the beginning it was not so.   <a name="fr_1791"></a><a href="#fn_1791"><font size="1">1792</font></a>
 I say unto you, Whosoever putteth away <a name="fr_1792"></a><a href="#fn_1792"><font size="1">1793</font></a>
  his wife without fornication, and marrieth another, hath exposed [37] her to adultery.   <a name="fr_1793"></a><a href="#fn_1793"><font size="1">1794</font></a>
 And his disciples, when he entered the house, asked him again [38] about that.   <a name="fr_1794"></a><a href="#fn_1794"><font size="1">1795</font></a>
 And he said unto them, Every one who putteth away his wife, and [39] marrieth another, hath exposed her to adultery.   <a name="fr_1795"></a><a href="#fn_1795"><font size="1">1796</font></a>
 And any woman that leaveth her husband, and becometh anothers, hath committed adultery.   <a name="fr_1796"></a><a href="#fn_1796"><font size="1">1797</font></a>
 And whosoever marrieth [40] her that is divorced hath committed adultery.   <a name="fr_1797"></a><a href="#fn_1797"><font size="1">1798</font></a>
 And his disciples said unto him, If there be between the man and the woman such a case <a name="fr_1798"></a><a href="#fn_1798"><font size="1">1799</font></a>
  as this, it is not good for [41] a man to marry.   <a name="fr_1799"></a><a href="#fn_1799"><font size="1">1800</font></a>
 He said unto them, Not every man can endure this saying, except [42] him to whom it is given.   <a name="fr_1800"></a><a href="#fn_1800"><font size="1">1801</font></a>
 There are eunuchs which from their mothers womb <a name="fr_1801"></a><a href="#fn_1801"><font size="1">1802</font></a>
  were born so; and there are eunuchs which through men became eunuchs; and there are eunuchs which made themselves eunuchs for the sake of the kingdom of heaven.  He that is able to be content, let him be content.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxv-p63"></a>[43]  <a name="fr_1802"></a><a href="#fn_1802"><font size="1">1803</font></a>
 Then they brought to him children, that he should lay his hand upon them, and [44] pray:  and his disciples were rebuking those that were bringing them.   <a name="fr_1803"></a><a href="#fn_1803"><font size="1">1804</font></a>
 And Jesus saw, and it was distressing to him; and he said unto them, Suffer the children to [Arabic, p. 100] come unto me, and prevent them not; for those that are like these have [45] the kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_1804"></a><a href="#fn_1804"><font size="1">1805</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, Whosoever receiveth not the [46] kingdom of God as this child, shall not enter it.   <a name="fr_1805"></a><a href="#fn_1805"><font size="1">1806</font></a>
 And he took them in his arms, and laid his hand upon them, and blessed them.</p>
