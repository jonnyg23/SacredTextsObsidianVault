 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxix"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p1.1"></a>Section XXIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1995"></a><a href="#fn_1995"><font size="1">1996</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, It is difficult for a rich man to enter the kingdom of [2] heaven.   <a name="fr_1996"></a><a href="#fn_1996"><font size="1">1997</font></a>
 And I say unto you also, that it is easier for a camel to enter the eye of [3] a needle, than for a rich man to enter the kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_1997"></a><a href="#fn_1997"><font size="1">1998</font></a>
 And the disciples were wondering at these sayings.  And Jesus answered and said unto them again, My children, how hard it is for those that rely on their possessions to enter the [4] kingdom of God!   <a name="fr_1998"></a><a href="#fn_1998"><font size="1">1999</font></a>
 And those that were listening wondered more, and said amongst [5] themselves, being agitated, <a name="fr_1999"></a><a href="#fn_1999"><font size="1">2000</font></a>
  Who, thinkest thou, can be saved?   <a name="fr_2000"></a><a href="#fn_2000"><font size="1">2001</font></a>
 And Jesus looked at <a name="page_88"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 88</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxix-Page_88"></a>them intently, and said unto them, With men this is not possible, but with God <i>it is:</i>  [6] [Arabic, p. 111] it is possible for God to do everything.   <a name="fr_2001"></a><a href="#fn_2001"><font size="1">2002</font></a>
 Simon Cephas said unto him, Lo, we have left everything, and followed thee; what is it, thinkest thou, that we [7] shall have?   <a name="fr_2002"></a><a href="#fn_2002"><font size="1">2003</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, Verily I say unto you, Ye that have followed me, in the new world, when the Son of man shall sit on the throne of his glory, ye also [8] shall sit on twelve thrones, and shall judge the twelve tribes of Israel.   <a name="fr_2003"></a><a href="#fn_2003"><font size="1">2004</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, No man leaveth houses, or brothers, or sisters, or father, or mother, or wife, or children, or kinsfolk, or lands, because of the kingdom of God, or for [9] my sake, and the sake of my gospel,  <a name="fr_2004"></a><a href="#fn_2004"><font size="1">2005</font></a>
 who shall not obtain <a name="fr_2005"></a><a href="#fn_2005"><font size="1">2006</font></a>
  many times as much in this [10] time, and in the world to come inherit eternal life:   <a name="fr_2006"></a><a href="#fn_2006"><font size="1">2007</font></a>
 and now in this time, houses, and brothers, and sisters, and mothers, and children, and lands, with persecution; [11] and in the world to come <i>ever</i>lasting life.   <a name="fr_2007"></a><a href="#fn_2007"><font size="1">2008</font></a>
 Many that are first shall be last, <a name="fr_2008"></a><a href="#fn_2008"><font size="1">2009</font></a>
  and that are last shall be first.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p17"></a>[12]  <a name="fr_2009"></a><a href="#fn_2009"><font size="1">2010</font></a>
 And when the Pharisees heard all this, because of their love for wealth they [13] scoffed at him.   <a name="fr_2010"></a><a href="#fn_2010"><font size="1">2011</font></a>
 And Jesus knew what was in their hearts, and said unto them, Ye are they that justify yourselves before men; while God knows your hearts:  the thing that is lofty with men is base before God.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p20"></a>[14]  <a name="fr_2011"></a><a href="#fn_2011"><font size="1">2012</font></a>
 And he began to say, A <i>certain</i> man was rich, and wore silk and purple, and enjoyed [15] himself every day in splendour:   <a name="fr_2012"></a><a href="#fn_2012"><font size="1">2013</font></a>
 and there was a poor man named Lazarus, and [16] he was cast down at the door of the rich man,  <a name="fr_2013"></a><a href="#fn_2013"><font size="1">2014</font></a>
 afflicted with sores, and he longed to fill [Arabic, p. 112] his belly with the crumbs that fell from the table of that rich man; yea, [17] even <a name="fr_2014"></a><a href="#fn_2014"><font size="1">2015</font></a>
  the dogs used to come and lick his sores.   <a name="fr_2015"></a><a href="#fn_2015"><font size="1">2016</font></a>
 And it happened that that poor man died, and the angels conveyed him into the bosom of Abraham:  and the [18] rich man also died, and was buried.   <a name="fr_2016"></a><a href="#fn_2016"><font size="1">2017</font></a>
 And while he was being tormented in Hades, [19] he lifted up his eyes from afar, and saw Abraham with <a name="fr_2017"></a><a href="#fn_2017"><font size="1">2018</font></a>
  Lazarus in his bosom.   <a name="fr_2018"></a><a href="#fn_2018"><font size="1">2019</font></a>
 And he called with a loud voice, and said, My father Abraham, have mercy upon me, and send Lazarus to wet the tip of his finger with water, and moisten my tongue [20] for me; for, behold, I am burned in this flame.   <a name="fr_2019"></a><a href="#fn_2019"><font size="1">2020</font></a>
 Abraham said unto him, My son, remember that thou receivedst thy good things in thy life, and Lazarus his afflictions:  [21] but now, behold, he is at rest here, and thou art tormented.   <a name="fr_2020"></a><a href="#fn_2020"><font size="1">2021</font></a>
 And in addition to all this, there is between us and you a great abyss placed, so that they that would cross unto you from hence cannot, nor yet from thence do they cross unto [22] us.   <a name="fr_2021"></a><a href="#fn_2021"><font size="1">2022</font></a>
 He said unto him, Then I beseech thee, my father, to send him to my fathers [23] house;  <a name="fr_2022"></a><a href="#fn_2022"><font size="1">2023</font></a>
 for I have five brethren; let him go, that they also sin not, <a name="fr_2023"></a><a href="#fn_2023"><font size="1">2024</font></a>
  and come to [24] the abode of this torment. <a name="fr_2024"></a><a href="#fn_2024"><font size="1">2025</font></a>
    <a name="fr_2025"></a><a href="#fn_2025"><font size="1">2026</font></a>
 Abraham said unto him, They have Moses and the [25] prophets; let them hear them.   <a name="fr_2026"></a><a href="#fn_2026"><font size="1">2027</font></a>
 He said unto him, Nay, <a name="fr_2027"></a><a href="#fn_2027"><font size="1">2028</font></a>
  my father Abraham:  but [26] let a man from the dead go unto them, and they will repent.   <a name="fr_2028"></a><a href="#fn_2028"><font size="1">2029</font></a>
 Abraham said unto him, If they listen neither to Moses nor to the prophets, neither if a man from the dead rose would they believe him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p39"></a>[27]  <a name="fr_2029"></a><a href="#fn_2029"><font size="1">2030</font></a>
 The kingdom of heaven is like a man that is a householder, which went out early [28] in the morning to hire labourers for his vineyard.   <a name="fr_2030"></a><a href="#fn_2030"><font size="1">2031</font></a>
 And he agreed with the labourers on [29] one penny a day for each labourer, and he sent them into his vineyard.   <a name="fr_2031"></a><a href="#fn_2031"><font size="1">2032</font></a>
 And he went [30] [Arabic, p. 113] out in three hours, and saw others standing in the market idle.   <a name="fr_2032"></a><a href="#fn_2032"><font size="1">2033</font></a>
 He said unto them, Go ye also into my vineyard, and what is right I will pay you.  [31]  <a name="fr_2033"></a><a href="#fn_2033"><font size="1">2034</font></a>
 And they went.  And he went out also at the sixth and the ninth hour, and did likewise, [32] and sent them.   <a name="fr_2034"></a><a href="#fn_2034"><font size="1">2035</font></a>
 And about the eleventh hour he went out, and found others standing idle.  He said unto them, Why are ye standing the whole day idle?  [33]  <a name="fr_2035"></a><a href="#fn_2035"><font size="1">2036</font></a>
 They said unto him, Because no one hath hired us.  He said unto them, Go ye [34] also into the vineyard, and what is right ye shall receive.   <a name="fr_2036"></a><a href="#fn_2036"><font size="1">2037</font></a>
 So when evening came, the lord of the vineyard said unto his steward, Call the labourers, and pay them [35] their wages; and begin with the later ones, and end with the former ones.   <a name="fr_2037"></a><a href="#fn_2037"><font size="1">2038</font></a>
 And [36] those of eleven hours <a name="fr_2038"></a><a href="#fn_2038"><font size="1">2039</font></a>
  came, and received each a penny.   <a name="fr_2039"></a><a href="#fn_2039"><font size="1">2040</font></a>
 When therefore the first came, they supposed that they should receive something more; and they also [37] received each a penny.   <a name="fr_2040"></a><a href="#fn_2040"><font size="1">2041</font></a>
 And when they received <i>it</i>, they spake angrily against the <a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 89</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxix-Page_89"></a>[38] householder, and said,  <a name="fr_2041"></a><a href="#fn_2041"><font size="1">2042</font></a>
 These last worked one hour, and thou hast made them equal [39] with us, who have suffered the heat of the day, and its burden.   <a name="fr_2042"></a><a href="#fn_2042"><font size="1">2043</font></a>
 He answered and said unto one of them, My friend, I do thee no wrong:  <i>was it</i> not for a penny <i>that</i> [40] thou didst bargain with me?   <a name="fr_2043"></a><a href="#fn_2043"><font size="1">2044</font></a>
 Take what is thine, and go thy way; for I wish to [41] give this last as I have given thee.   <a name="fr_2044"></a><a href="#fn_2044"><font size="1">2045</font></a>
 Or am I not entitled to do with what is mine <a name="fr_2045"></a><a href="#fn_2045"><font size="1">2046</font></a>
  [42] what I choose?   <a name="fr_2046"></a><a href="#fn_2046"><font size="1">2047</font></a>
 Or is thine eye perchance evil, because I am good?  Thus shall the last <i>ones</i> be first, and the first last.  The called are many, and the chosen are few.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxix-p58"></a>[43]  <a name="fr_2047"></a><a href="#fn_2047"><font size="1">2048</font></a>
 And when Jesus entered into the house of one of the chiefs of the Pharisees to eat bread on the sabbath day, and they were watching him to see what he would [44, 45] do,  <a name="fr_2048"></a><a href="#fn_2048"><font size="1">2049</font></a>
 and there was before him a man which had the dropsy,  <a name="fr_2049"></a><a href="#fn_2049"><font size="1">2050</font></a>
 Jesus answered and [46] said unto the scribes and the Pharisees, Is it lawful on the sabbath to heal?   <a name="fr_2050"></a><a href="#fn_2050"><font size="1">2051</font></a>
 But [Arabic, p. 114] they were silent.  So he took him, and healed him, and sent him away.  [47]  <a name="fr_2051"></a><a href="#fn_2051"><font size="1">2052</font></a>
 And he said unto them, Which of you shall <i>have</i> his son or his ox fall on the sabbath day into a well, and not lift him up straightway, and draw water for [48] him?   <a name="fr_2052"></a><a href="#fn_2052"><font size="1">2053</font></a>
 And they were not able to answer him a word to that.</p>
