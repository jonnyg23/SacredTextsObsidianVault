 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Continuation Respecting Repentance.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.viii-p2"></a>The ministers of the grace of God have, by the Holy Spirit, spoken of repentance; and the Lord of all things has himself declared with an oath regarding it, “As I live, saith the Lord, I desire not the death of the sinner, but rather his repentance;” <a name="fr_4042"></a><a href="#fn_4042"><font size="1">4043</font></a>
  adding, moreover, this gracious declaration, “Repent, O house of Israel, of your iniquity.” <a name="fr_4043"></a><a href="#fn_4043"><font size="1">4044</font></a>
   Say to the children of my people, Though your sins reach from earth to heaven, and though they be redder <a name="fr_4044"></a><a href="#fn_4044"><font size="1">4045</font></a>
  than scarlet, and blacker than sack-cloth, yet if ye turn to me with your whole heart, and say, Father! I will listen to you, as to a holy <a name="fr_4045"></a><a href="#fn_4045"><font size="1">4046</font></a>
  people.  And in another place He speaks thus:  “Wash you and become clean; put away the wickedness of your souls from before mine eyes; cease from your evil ways, and learn to do well; seek out judgment, deliver the oppressed, judge the fatherless, and see that justice is done to the widow; and come, and let us reason together.  He declares, Though your sins be like crimson, I will make them white as snow; though they be like scarlet, I will whiten them like wool.  And if ye be willing and obey me, ye shall eat the good of the land; but if ye refuse, and will not hearken unto me, the sword shall devour you, for the mouth of the Lord hath spoken these things.” <a name="fr_4046"></a><a href="#fn_4046"><font size="1">4047</font></a>
   Desiring, therefore, that all His beloved should be partakers of repentance, He has, by His almighty will, established [these declarations].</p>
