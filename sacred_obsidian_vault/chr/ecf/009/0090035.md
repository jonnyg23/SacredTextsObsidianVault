 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxvi"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxvi-p1.1"></a>Section XXVI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxvi-p2"></a>[1, 2]  <a name="fr_1806"></a><a href="#fn_1806"><font size="1">1807</font></a>
 And there came unto him publicans and sinners to hear his word.   <a name="fr_1807"></a><a href="#fn_1807"><font size="1">1808</font></a>
 And the scribes and the Pharisees murmured, and said, This man receiveth sinners, and [3] eateth with them.   <a name="fr_1808"></a><a href="#fn_1808"><font size="1">1809</font></a>
 And Jesus, when he beheld their murmuring, spake unto them [4] this parable:   <a name="fr_1809"></a><a href="#fn_1809"><font size="1">1810</font></a>
 What man of you, having an hundred sheep, if one of them were lost, would not leave the ninety-nine in the wilderness, and go and seek the straying <i>one</i> [5] till he found it?   <a name="fr_1810"></a><a href="#fn_1810"><font size="1">1811</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, When he findeth it, he will rejoice over it [6] more than <i>over</i> the ninety-nine that went not astray;  <a name="fr_1811"></a><a href="#fn_1811"><font size="1">1812</font></a>
 and bear it on his shoulders, and bring it to his house, and call his friends and neighbours,  <a name="fr_1812"></a><a href="#fn_1812"><font size="1">1813</font></a>
 and say unto them, [7] Rejoice with me, since I have found my straying sheep.   <a name="fr_1813"></a><a href="#fn_1813"><font size="1">1814</font></a>
 So your Father which is in heaven willeth <a name="fr_1814"></a><a href="#fn_1814"><font size="1">1815</font></a>
  not that one of these little ones that have strayed should perish, [8] and he seeketh for them repentance.   <a name="fr_1815"></a><a href="#fn_1815"><font size="1">1816</font></a>
 I say unto you, Thus there shall be rejoicing in heaven over one sinner that repenteth, more than <i>over</i> ninety-nine righteous persons that do not need repentance.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxvi-p13"></a>[9]  <a name="fr_1816"></a><a href="#fn_1816"><font size="1">1817</font></a>
 And what woman having ten drachmas would lose one of them, and not light a [10] lamp, and sweep the house, and seek it with care till she found it;  <a name="fr_1817"></a><a href="#fn_1817"><font size="1">1818</font></a>
 and when she found it, call her friends and neighbours, and say unto them, Rejoice with me, as I [11] have found my drachma that was lost?   <a name="fr_1818"></a><a href="#fn_1818"><font size="1">1819</font></a>
 I say unto you, Thus there shall be joy [Arabic, p. 101] before the angels of God over the one sinner that repenteth, more than over the ninety-nine righteous <i>persons</i> that do not need repentance.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxvi-p17"></a>[12, 13]  <a name="fr_1819"></a><a href="#fn_1819"><font size="1">1820</font></a>
 And Jesus spake unto them also another parable:   <a name="fr_1820"></a><a href="#fn_1820"><font size="1">1821</font></a>
 A man had two sons:  and the younger son said unto him, My father, give me my portion that belongeth to [14] me of thy goods.   <a name="fr_1821"></a><a href="#fn_1821"><font size="1">1822</font></a>
 And he divided between them his property.  And after a few days the younger son gathered everything that belonged to him, and went into a [15] far country, and there squandered his property by living prodigally.   <a name="fr_1822"></a><a href="#fn_1822"><font size="1">1823</font></a>
 And when he <a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxvi-Page_84"></a>had exhausted everything he had, there occurred a great dearth in that country.  [16]  <a name="fr_1823"></a><a href="#fn_1823"><font size="1">1824</font></a>
 And when he was in want, he went and joined himself to one of the people of a city [17] of that country; and that <i>man</i> sent him into the field <a name="fr_1824"></a><a href="#fn_1824"><font size="1">1825</font></a>
  to feed the swine.   <a name="fr_1825"></a><a href="#fn_1825"><font size="1">1826</font></a>
 And he used to long to fill his belly with the carob that those swine were eating:  and no man [18] gave him.   <a name="fr_1826"></a><a href="#fn_1826"><font size="1">1827</font></a>
 And when he returned unto himself, he said, How many hired servants now in my fathers house have bread enough and to spare, while I here perish with [19] hunger!   <a name="fr_1827"></a><a href="#fn_1827"><font size="1">1828</font></a>
 I will arise and go to my fathers house, and say unto him, My father,  <a name="fr_1828"></a><a href="#fn_1828"><font size="1">1829</font></a>
 I [20] have sinned in heaven and before thee, and am not worthy now to be called thy [21] son:  make me as one of thy hired servants.   <a name="fr_1829"></a><a href="#fn_1829"><font size="1">1830</font></a>
 And he arose, and came to his father.  But his father saw him while he was at a distance, and was moved with compassion [22] for him, and ran, <a name="fr_1830"></a><a href="#fn_1830"><font size="1">1831</font></a>
  and fell on his breast, <a name="fr_1831"></a><a href="#fn_1831"><font size="1">1832</font></a>
  and kissed him.   <a name="fr_1832"></a><a href="#fn_1832"><font size="1">1833</font></a>
 And his son said unto him, My father, I have sinned in heaven and before thee, and am not worthy to be [23] called thy son.   <a name="fr_1833"></a><a href="#fn_1833"><font size="1">1834</font></a>
 His father said unto his servants, Bring forth a stately robe, and put [24] <i>it</i> on him; and put a ring on his hand, and put on him shoes on his feet:   <a name="fr_1834"></a><a href="#fn_1834"><font size="1">1835</font></a>
 and bring and [25] slay a fatted ox, that we may eat and make merry:   <a name="fr_1835"></a><a href="#fn_1835"><font size="1">1836</font></a>
 for this my son was dead, and is [26] [Arabic, p. 102] alive; and was lost, and is found.   <a name="fr_1836"></a><a href="#fn_1836"><font size="1">1837</font></a>
 And they began to be merry. <a name="fr_1837"></a><a href="#fn_1837"><font size="1">1838</font></a>
   Now his elder son was in the field; and when he came and drew near to the house, [27] he heard the sound of many singing. <a name="fr_1838"></a><a href="#fn_1838"><font size="1">1839</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1839"></a><a href="#fn_1839"><font size="1">1840</font></a>
 And he called one of the lads, and asked him [28] what this was.   <a name="fr_1840"></a><a href="#fn_1840"><font size="1">1841</font></a>
 He said unto him, Thy brother hath arrived; and thy father hath [29] slain a fatted ox, since he hath received him safe and sound. <a name="fr_1841"></a><a href="#fn_1841"><font size="1">1842</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1842"></a><a href="#fn_1842"><font size="1">1843</font></a>
 And he was angry, [30] and would not enter; so his father went out, and besought him to enter.   <a name="fr_1843"></a><a href="#fn_1843"><font size="1">1844</font></a>
 And he said to his father, How many years do I serve thee in bondage, and I never transgressed a commandment of thine; and thou hast never given me a kid, that I might [31] make merry with my friends?  <a name="fr_1844"></a><a href="#fn_1844"><font size="1">1845</font></a>
 but this thy son, when he had squandered thy [32] property with harlots, and come, thou hast slain for him a fatted ox.   <a name="fr_1845"></a><a href="#fn_1845"><font size="1">1846</font></a>
 His father said unto him, My son, thou art at all times with me, and everything I have is [33] thine.   <a name="fr_1846"></a><a href="#fn_1846"><font size="1">1847</font></a>
 It behoveth thee to rejoice and make merry, since this thy brother was dead, and is alive; and <i>was</i> lost, and is found.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxvi-p46"></a>[34]  <a name="fr_1847"></a><a href="#fn_1847"><font size="1">1848</font></a>
 And he spake a parable unto his disciples:  There was a rich man, and he had [35] a steward; and he was accused to him that he had squandered his property.   <a name="fr_1848"></a><a href="#fn_1848"><font size="1">1849</font></a>
 So his lord called him, and said unto him, What is this that I hear regarding thee?  Give me the account of thy stewardship; for it is now impossible that thou shouldest [36] be a steward for me.   <a name="fr_1849"></a><a href="#fn_1849"><font size="1">1850</font></a>
 The steward said within himself, What shall I do, seeing that my lord taketh from me the stewardship?  To dig I am not able; and to beg <a name="fr_1850"></a><a href="#fn_1850"><font size="1">1851</font></a>
  I [37] am ashamed.   <a name="fr_1851"></a><a href="#fn_1851"><font size="1">1852</font></a>
 I know what I will do, that, when I go out of the stewardship, they [38] may receive me into their houses.   <a name="fr_1852"></a><a href="#fn_1852"><font size="1">1853</font></a>
 And he called one after another of his lords [39] debtors, and said to the first, How much owest thou my lord?   <a name="fr_1853"></a><a href="#fn_1853"><font size="1">1854</font></a>
 He said unto him, An hundred portions <a name="fr_1854"></a><a href="#fn_1854"><font size="1">1855</font></a>
  of oil.  He said unto him, Take thy writing, and sit down, and write [40] quickly fifty portions. <a name="fr_1855"></a><a href="#fn_1855"><font size="1">1856</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1856"></a><a href="#fn_1856"><font size="1">1857</font></a>
 And he said to the next, And thou, how much owest thou my lord?  He said unto him, An hundred cors of wheat.  He said unto him, Take [41] [Arabic, p. 103] thy writing, and sit down, and write eighty cors.   <a name="fr_1857"></a><a href="#fn_1857"><font size="1">1858</font></a>
 And our <a name="fr_1858"></a><a href="#fn_1858"><font size="1">1859</font></a>
  lord commended the sinful steward <a name="fr_1859"></a><a href="#fn_1859"><font size="1">1860</font></a>
  because he had done a wise deed; for the children [42] of this world are wiser than the children of the light in this their age.   <a name="fr_1860"></a><a href="#fn_1860"><font size="1">1861</font></a>
 And I also say unto you, Make unto yourselves friends with the wealth of this unrighteousness; <a name="fr_1861"></a><a href="#fn_1861"><font size="1">1862</font></a>
  [43] so that, when it is exhausted, they may receive you into their tents for ever.   <a name="fr_1862"></a><a href="#fn_1862"><font size="1">1863</font></a>
 He who is faithful in <a name="fr_1863"></a><a href="#fn_1863"><font size="1">1864</font></a>
  a little is faithful also in much:  and he who is unrighteous in a [44] little is unrighteous also in much.   <a name="fr_1864"></a><a href="#fn_1864"><font size="1">1865</font></a>
 If then in the wealth of unrighteousness ye were [45] not trustworthy, who will intrust you with the truth? <a name="fr_1865"></a><a href="#fn_1865"><font size="1">1866</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1866"></a><a href="#fn_1866"><font size="1">1867</font></a>
 If ye are not found faithful in what does not belong to you, who will give you what belongeth to you?</p>
