 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.xlii-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.xlii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLII.—The Order of Ministers in the Church.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.xlii-p2"></a>The apostles have preached the gospel to us from <a name="fr_4219"></a><a href="#fn_4219"><font size="1">4220</font></a>
  the Lord Jesus Christ; Jesus <a name="fr_4220"></a><a href="#fn_4220"><font size="1">4221</font></a>
  Christ [has done so] from God.  Christ therefore was sent forth by God, <a name="fr_4221"></a><a href="#fn_4221"><font size="1">4222</font></a>
  and the apostles by Christ.  Both these appointments, <a name="fr_4222"></a><a href="#fn_4222"><font size="1">4223</font></a>
  then, were made in an orderly way, according to the will of God.  Having therefore received their orders, and being fully assured by the resurrection of our Lord Jesus Christ, and established <a name="fr_4223"></a><a href="#fn_4223"><font size="1">4224</font></a>
  in the word of God, with full assurance of the Holy Ghost, they went forth proclaiming that the kingdom of God was at hand.  And thus preaching through countries and cities, they appointed the first fruits [of their labours], having first proved them by the Spirit, <a name="fr_4224"></a><a href="#fn_4224"><font size="1">4225</font></a>
  to be bishops and deacons of those who should afterwards believe.  Nor was this any new thing, since indeed many ages before it was written con<a name="page_242"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 242</font></a> <a name="xii.iv.xlii-Page_242"></a>cerning bishops and deacons.  For thus saith the Scripture in a certain place, “I will appoint their bishops <a name="fr_4225"></a><a href="#fn_4225"><font size="1">4226</font></a>
  in righteousness, and their deacons <a name="fr_4226"></a><a href="#fn_4226"><font size="1">4227</font></a>
  in faith.” <a name="fr_4227"></a><a href="#fn_4227"><font size="1">4228</font></a>
 </p>
