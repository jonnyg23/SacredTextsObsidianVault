 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.xlvi-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.xlvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLVI.—Let Us Cleave to the Righteous:  Your Strife is Pernicious.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.xlvi-p2"></a>Such examples, therefore, brethren, it is right that we should follow; <a name="fr_4243"></a><a href="#fn_4243"><font size="1">4244</font></a>
  since it is written, “Cleave to the holy, for those that cleave to them shall [themselves] be made holy.” <a name="fr_4244"></a><a href="#fn_4244"><font size="1">4245</font></a>
   And again, in another place, [the Scripture] saith, “With a harmless man thou shalt prove <a name="fr_4245"></a><a href="#fn_4245"><font size="1">4246</font></a>
  thyself harmless, and with an elect man thou shalt be elect, and with a perverse man thou shalt show <a name="fr_4246"></a><a href="#fn_4246"><font size="1">4247</font></a>
  thyself perverse.” <a name="fr_4247"></a><a href="#fn_4247"><font size="1">4248</font></a>
   Let us cleave, therefore, to the innocent and righteous, since these are the elect of God.  Why are there strifes, and tumults, and divisions, and schisms, and wars <a name="fr_4248"></a><a href="#fn_4248"><font size="1">4249</font></a>
  among you?  Have we not [all] one God and one Christ?  Is there not one Spirit of grace poured out upon us?  And have we not one calling in Christ? <a name="fr_4249"></a><a href="#fn_4249"><font size="1">4250</font></a>
   Why do we divide and tear in pieces the members of Christ, and raise up strife against our own body, and have reached such a height of madness as to forget that “we are members one of another?” <a name="fr_4250"></a><a href="#fn_4250"><font size="1">4251</font></a>
   Remember the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, how <a name="fr_4251"></a><a href="#fn_4251"><font size="1">4252</font></a>
  He said, “Woe to that man [by whom <a name="fr_4252"></a><a href="#fn_4252"><font size="1">4253</font></a>
  offences come]!  It were better for him that he had never been born, than that he should cast a stumbling-block before one of my elect.  Yea, it were better for him that a millstone should be hung about [his neck], and he should be sunk in the depths of the sea, than that he should cast a stumbling-block before one of my little ones.” <a name="fr_4253"></a><a href="#fn_4253"><font size="1">4254</font></a>
   Your schism has subverted [the faith of] many, has discouraged many, has given rise to doubt in many, and has caused grief to us all.  And still your sedition continueth.</p>
