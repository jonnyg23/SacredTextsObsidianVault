 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxxix"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p1"></a> <a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxxix-Page_103"></a><span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p1.1"></a>Section XXXIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_2652"></a><a href="#fn_2652"><font size="1">2653</font></a>
 And Jesus six days before the passover <a name="fr_2653"></a><a href="#fn_2653"><font size="1">2654</font></a>
  came to Bethany, where was Lazarus, [2] whom Jesus raised from among the dead.   <a name="fr_2654"></a><a href="#fn_2654"><font size="1">2655</font></a>
 And they made <a name="fr_2655"></a><a href="#fn_2655"><font size="1">2656</font></a>
  a feast for him there:  [3] and Martha was serving; while Lazarus was one of them that sat with him.   <a name="fr_2656"></a><a href="#fn_2656"><font size="1">2657</font></a>
 And [4] at the time of Jesus being at Bethany in the house of Simon the leper,  <a name="fr_2657"></a><a href="#fn_2657"><font size="1">2658</font></a>
 great multitudes of the Jews heard that Jesus was there:  and they came, not because of Jesus alone, but [Arabic, p. 147] that they might look also on Lazarus, whom he raised from among the dead.  [5, 6]  <a name="fr_2658"></a><a href="#fn_2658"><font size="1">2659</font></a>
 And the chief priests considered <i>how</i> they might kill Lazarus also;  <a name="fr_2659"></a><a href="#fn_2659"><font size="1">2660</font></a>
 because [7] many of the Jews were going on his account, and believing in Jesus.   <a name="fr_2660"></a><a href="#fn_2660"><font size="1">2661</font></a>
 And Mary took a case of the ointment of fine nard, of great price,  <a name="fr_2661"></a><a href="#fn_2661"><font size="1">2662</font></a>
 and opened it, and poured [8] it out on the head of Jesus as he was reclining;  <a name="fr_2662"></a><a href="#fn_2662"><font size="1">2663</font></a>
 and she anointed his feet, and wiped them with her hair:  and the house was filled with the odour of the ointment.  [9, 10]  <a name="fr_2663"></a><a href="#fn_2663"><font size="1">2664</font></a>
 But Judas Iscariot, one of the disciples, he that was to betray him, said,  <a name="fr_2664"></a><a href="#fn_2664"><font size="1">2665</font></a>
 Why was [11] not this ointment sold for three hundred pence, and given unto the poor?   <a name="fr_2665"></a><a href="#fn_2665"><font size="1">2666</font></a>
 This he said, not because of his care for the poor, but because he was a thief, and the chest [12] was with him, and what was put <a name="fr_2666"></a><a href="#fn_2666"><font size="1">2667</font></a>
  into it he used to bear.   <a name="fr_2667"></a><a href="#fn_2667"><font size="1">2668</font></a>
 And that displeased the rest of the disciples also within themselves, and they said, Why went this ointment [13] to waste?   <a name="fr_2668"></a><a href="#fn_2668"><font size="1">2669</font></a>
 It was possible that it should be sold for much, and the poor be given [14] it.   <a name="fr_2669"></a><a href="#fn_2669"><font size="1">2670</font></a>
 And they were angry with <a name="fr_2670"></a><a href="#fn_2670"><font size="1">2671</font></a>
  Mary.   <a name="fr_2671"></a><a href="#fn_2671"><font size="1">2672</font></a>
 And Jesus perceived <i>it</i>, and said unto them,  <a name="fr_2672"></a><a href="#fn_2672"><font size="1">2673</font></a>
 Leave her; why molest ye her? a good work hath she accomplished on me:   <a name="fr_2673"></a><a href="#fn_2673"><font size="1">2674</font></a>
 for the [15] day of my burial kept she it.   <a name="fr_2674"></a><a href="#fn_2674"><font size="1">2675</font></a>
 At all times the poor are with you, and when ye [16] wish ye can do them a kindness:   <a name="fr_2675"></a><a href="#fn_2675"><font size="1">2676</font></a>
 but I am not at all times with you.   <a name="fr_2676"></a><a href="#fn_2676"><font size="1">2677</font></a>
 And for this <i>cause</i>, when she poured <a name="fr_2677"></a><a href="#fn_2677"><font size="1">2678</font></a>
  this ointment on my body, it is as if she did it for my burial, [17] and anointed my body beforehand.   <a name="fr_2678"></a><a href="#fn_2678"><font size="1">2679</font></a>
 And verily I say unto you, In every place where this my gospel shall be proclaimed in all the world, what she did shall be told for a memorial of her.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p30"></a>[18, 19] [Arabic, p. 148]  <a name="fr_2679"></a><a href="#fn_2679"><font size="1">2680</font></a>
 And when Jesus said that, he went out leisurely to go to Jerusalem.   <a name="fr_2680"></a><a href="#fn_2680"><font size="1">2681</font></a>
 And when he arrived at Bethphage and at Bethany, beside the mount which is [20] called the mount of Olives,  <a name="fr_2681"></a><a href="#fn_2681"><font size="1">2682</font></a>
 Jesus sent two of his disciples, and he said unto them, Go [21] into this village that is opposite you:   <a name="fr_2682"></a><a href="#fn_2682"><font size="1">2683</font></a>
 and when ye enter it, ye shall find an ass tied, and [22] a colt with him, <a name="fr_2683"></a><a href="#fn_2683"><font size="1">2684</font></a>
  which no man ever yet mounted:  loose him, and bring them <a name="fr_2684"></a><a href="#fn_2684"><font size="1">2685</font></a>
  unto me.   <a name="fr_2685"></a><a href="#fn_2685"><font size="1">2686</font></a>
 And if any man say unto you, Why loose ye them? say unto him thus, We [23] seek them for our Lord; and straightway send them hither.   <a name="fr_2686"></a><a href="#fn_2686"><font size="1">2687</font></a>
 All this was, that what was said in the prophet might be fulfilled, which said,</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p39"></a>[24]  <a name="fr_2687"></a><a href="#fn_2687"><font size="1">2688</font></a>
 Say ye unto the daughter of Zion,</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p41"></a>Behold, thy King cometh unto thee,</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p42"></a>Meek, and riding upon an ass,</p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p43"></a>And upon a colt the foal of an ass.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p44"></a>[25]  <a name="fr_2688"></a><a href="#fn_2688"><font size="1">2689</font></a>
 And the disciples did not know this at that time:  but after that Jesus was glorified, his disciples remembered that these <i>things</i> were written of him, and <i>that</i> this [26] they had done unto him.   <a name="fr_2689"></a><a href="#fn_2689"><font size="1">2690</font></a>
 And when the two disciples went, they found as he had [27] said unto them, and they did as Jesus charged them.   <a name="fr_2690"></a><a href="#fn_2690"><font size="1">2691</font></a>
 And when they loosed them, [28] their owners said unto them, Why loose ye them?   <a name="fr_2691"></a><a href="#fn_2691"><font size="1">2692</font></a>
 They said unto them, We seek [29] them for our Lord.   <a name="fr_2692"></a><a href="#fn_2692"><font size="1">2693</font></a>
 And they let them <i>go</i>.  And they brought the ass and the colt, [30] and they placed on the colt their garments; and Jesus mounted it.   <a name="fr_2693"></a><a href="#fn_2693"><font size="1">2694</font></a>
 And most of the multitudes spread their garments on the ground before him:  and others cut branches [31] from the trees, and threw <i>them</i> in the way.   <a name="fr_2694"></a><a href="#fn_2694"><font size="1">2695</font></a>
 And when he neared his <a name="fr_2695"></a><a href="#fn_2695"><font size="1">2696</font></a>
  descent from [Arabic, p. 149] the mount of Olives, all the disciples began to rejoice and to praise God with [32] a loud voice for all the powers which they had seen;  <a name="fr_2696"></a><a href="#fn_2696"><font size="1">2697</font></a>
 and they said, Praise in the highest; Praise to the Son of David: Blessed is he that cometh in the name [33] of the Lord;  <a name="fr_2697"></a><a href="#fn_2697"><font size="1">2698</font></a>
 and blessed <a name="fr_2698"></a><a href="#fn_2698"><font size="1">2699</font></a>
  is the kingdom that cometh, <i>that</i> of <a name="fr_2699"></a><a href="#fn_2699"><font size="1">2700</font></a>
  our father David:   <a name="fr_2700"></a><a href="#fn_2700"><font size="1">2701</font></a>
 Peace in heaven, and praise in the highest.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p58"></a><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 104</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxxix-Page_104"></a>[34]  <a name="fr_2701"></a><a href="#fn_2701"><font size="1">2702</font></a>
 And a great multitude, that which came to the feast, when they heard that Jesus [35] was coming to Jerusalem, took young palm branches, <a name="fr_2702"></a><a href="#fn_2702"><font size="1">2703</font></a>
   <a name="fr_2703"></a><a href="#fn_2703"><font size="1">2704</font></a>
 and went forth to meet him, and cried and said, Praise:  Blessed is he that cometh in the name of the Lord, the [36] King of Israel.   <a name="fr_2704"></a><a href="#fn_2704"><font size="1">2705</font></a>
 Certain therefore of the Pharisees from among the multitudes [37] said unto him, Our Master, rebuke thy disciples.   <a name="fr_2705"></a><a href="#fn_2705"><font size="1">2706</font></a>
 He said unto them, Verily I say unto you, If these were silent, the stones would cry out.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p64"></a>[38, 39]  <a name="fr_2706"></a><a href="#fn_2706"><font size="1">2707</font></a>
 And when he drew near, and saw the city, he wept over it, and said,  <a name="fr_2707"></a><a href="#fn_2707"><font size="1">2708</font></a>
 Would that thou hadst known the things that are <a name="fr_2708"></a><a href="#fn_2708"><font size="1">2709</font></a>
  for thy peace, in this thy day! now that is [40] hidden from thine eyes.   <a name="fr_2709"></a><a href="#fn_2709"><font size="1">2710</font></a>
 There shall come unto thee days when thine enemies [41] shall encompass thee, and straiten thee from every quarter,  <a name="fr_2710"></a><a href="#fn_2710"><font size="1">2711</font></a>
 and shall get possession of <a name="fr_2711"></a><a href="#fn_2711"><font size="1">2712</font></a>
  thee, and thy children within thee; and they shall not leave in thee a stone upon another; because thou knewest not the time of thy visitation.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxix-p71"></a>[42]  <a name="fr_2712"></a><a href="#fn_2712"><font size="1">2713</font></a>
 And when he entered into Jerusalem, the whole city was agitated, and they said, [43] Who is this?   <a name="fr_2713"></a><a href="#fn_2713"><font size="1">2714</font></a>
 And the multitudes said, This is Jesus, the prophet that is from Nazareth [44] of Galilee.   <a name="fr_2714"></a><a href="#fn_2714"><font size="1">2715</font></a>
 And the multitude which was with him bare witness that he called [45] Lazarus from the grave, and raised him from among the dead.   <a name="fr_2715"></a><a href="#fn_2715"><font size="1">2716</font></a>
 And for this <i>cause</i> great multitudes went out to meet him, because they heard the sign which he did.</p>
