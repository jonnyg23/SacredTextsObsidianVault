 <p> <a name="iv.iii.viii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p1.1"></a>Section VIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_601"></a><a href="#fn_601"><font size="1">602</font></a>
 And the Pharisees went out, and consulted together concerning him, that they [2] might destroy him.   <a name="fr_602"></a><a href="#fn_602"><font size="1">603</font></a>
 And Jesus perceived, and removed thence:  and great multitudes [3] followed him; and he healed all of them:   <a name="fr_603"></a><a href="#fn_603"><font size="1">604</font></a>
 and he forbade them that they should [4] not make him known: <a name="fr_604"></a><a href="#fn_604"><font size="1">605</font></a>
    <a name="fr_605"></a><a href="#fn_605"><font size="1">606</font></a>
 that the saying in Isaiah the prophet might be fulfilled, which said,</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p8"></a>[5]  <a name="fr_606"></a><a href="#fn_606"><font size="1">607</font></a>
 Behold, my servant <a name="fr_607"></a><a href="#fn_607"><font size="1">608</font></a>
  with whom I am pleased;</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p11"></a>My beloved in whom my soul hath delighted: <a name="fr_608"></a><a href="#fn_608"><font size="1">609</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p13"></a>My spirit have I put upon him,</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p14"></a>And he shall proclaim to the nations judgement.</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p15"></a>[6]  <a name="fr_609"></a><a href="#fn_609"><font size="1">610</font></a>
 He shall not dispute, nor cry out;</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p17"></a>And no man shall hear his voice in the marketplace.</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p18"></a>[7]  <a name="fr_610"></a><a href="#fn_610"><font size="1">611</font></a>
 And a bruised reed shall he not break,</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p20"></a>And a smoking lamp <a name="fr_611"></a><a href="#fn_611"><font size="1">612</font></a>
  shall he not extinguish,</p> 
 <p class="c29"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p22"></a><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.viii-Page_56"></a>Until he shall bring forth judgement unto victory.</p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p23"></a>[8]  <a name="fr_612"></a><a href="#fn_612"><font size="1">613</font></a>
 And the nations shall rejoice in his name. <a name="fr_613"></a><a href="#fn_613"><font size="1">614</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p26"></a>[9]  <a name="fr_614"></a><a href="#fn_614"><font size="1">615</font></a>
 And in those days Jesus went out to the mountain that he might pray, and he [10] spent the night <a name="fr_615"></a><a href="#fn_615"><font size="1">616</font></a>
  there in prayer to God.   <a name="fr_616"></a><a href="#fn_616"><font size="1">617</font></a>
 And when the morning was come, he called the disciples.   <a name="fr_617"></a><a href="#fn_617"><font size="1">618</font></a>
 And he went towards the sea:  and there followed him much people [11] from Galilee that he might pray, <a name="fr_618"></a><a href="#fn_618"><font size="1">619</font></a>
   <a name="fr_619"></a><a href="#fn_619"><font size="1">620</font></a>
 and from Judæa, and from Jerusalem, and from Idumæa, and from beyond Jordan, and from Tyre, and from Sidon, and from Decapolis; [12] and great multitudes came unto him, which had heard what he did.   <a name="fr_620"></a><a href="#fn_620"><font size="1">621</font></a>
 And he spake to his disciples to bring him the boat because of the multitudes, that they [13] might not throng him.   <a name="fr_621"></a><a href="#fn_621"><font size="1">622</font></a>
 And he healed many, so that they were almost falling on [Arabic, p. 31] him <a name="fr_622"></a><a href="#fn_622"><font size="1">623</font></a>
  on account of their seeking to get near him.  And <a name="fr_623"></a><a href="#fn_623"><font size="1">624</font></a>
  those that had [14] plagues and unclean spirits,  <a name="fr_624"></a><a href="#fn_624"><font size="1">625</font></a>
 as soon as they beheld him, would fall, and [15] cry out, and say, Thou art the Son of God.   <a name="fr_625"></a><a href="#fn_625"><font size="1">626</font></a>
 And he rebuked them much, that they [16] should not make him known.   <a name="fr_626"></a><a href="#fn_626"><font size="1">627</font></a>
 And those that were under the constraint of <a name="fr_627"></a><a href="#fn_627"><font size="1">628</font></a>
  unclean [17] spirits were healed.   <a name="fr_628"></a><a href="#fn_628"><font size="1">629</font></a>
 And all of the crowd were seeking to come near <a name="fr_629"></a><a href="#fn_629"><font size="1">630</font></a>
  him; because power went out from him, and he healed them all.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p43"></a>[18, 19]  <a name="fr_630"></a><a href="#fn_630"><font size="1">631</font></a>
 And when Jesus saw the multitudes, he went up to the mountain.   <a name="fr_631"></a><a href="#fn_631"><font size="1">632</font></a>
 And he called his disciples, and chose from them twelve; and they are those whom he named [20] apostles:   <a name="fr_632"></a><a href="#fn_632"><font size="1">633</font></a>
 Simon, whom he named Cephas, and Andrew his brother, and James and [21] John, and Philip and Bartholomew,  <a name="fr_633"></a><a href="#fn_633"><font size="1">634</font></a>
 and Matthew and Thomas, and James the son [22] of Alphæus, and Simon which <i>was</i> called the Zealot,  <a name="fr_634"></a><a href="#fn_634"><font size="1">635</font></a>
 and Judas the son of James, [23] and Judas the Iscariot, being he that had betrayed him. <a name="fr_635"></a><a href="#fn_635"><font size="1">636</font></a>
    <a name="fr_636"></a><a href="#fn_636"><font size="1">637</font></a>
 And Jesus went down with them and stood in the plain, and the company of his disciples, and the great [24] multitude of people.   <a name="fr_637"></a><a href="#fn_637"><font size="1">638</font></a>
 And these twelve he chose to be with him, and that he might [25] send them to preach, and to have power to heal the sick and to cast out devils.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p52"></a>[26]  <a name="fr_638"></a><a href="#fn_638"><font size="1">639</font></a>
 Then he lifted up his eyes unto them, and opened his mouth,  <a name="fr_639"></a><a href="#fn_639"><font size="1">640</font></a>
 and taught them, and said,</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p55"></a>[27]  <a name="fr_640"></a><a href="#fn_640"><font size="1">641</font></a>
 Blessed are the poor in spirit:  for the kingdom of heaven is theirs.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p57"></a>[28]  <a name="fr_641"></a><a href="#fn_641"><font size="1">642</font></a>
 Blessed are the sorrowful:  for they shall be comforted.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p59"></a>[29]  <a name="fr_642"></a><a href="#fn_642"><font size="1">643</font></a>
 Blessed are the humble:  for they shall inherit the earth.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p61"></a>[30]  <a name="fr_643"></a><a href="#fn_643"><font size="1">644</font></a>
 Blessed are they that hunger and thirst after righteousness:  for they shall be satisfied.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p63"></a>[31]  <a name="fr_644"></a><a href="#fn_644"><font size="1">645</font></a>
 Blessed are the merciful:  for on them shall be mercy.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p65"></a>[32] [Arabic, p. 32]  <a name="fr_645"></a><a href="#fn_645"><font size="1">646</font></a>
 Blessed are the pure in their hearts:  for they shall see God.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p67"></a>[33]  <a name="fr_646"></a><a href="#fn_646"><font size="1">647</font></a>
 Blessed are the peacemakers:  for they shall be called the sons of God.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p69"></a>[34]  <a name="fr_647"></a><a href="#fn_647"><font size="1">648</font></a>
 Blessed are they that were persecuted <a name="fr_648"></a><a href="#fn_648"><font size="1">649</font></a>
  for righteousness sake:  for the kingdom of heaven is theirs.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p72"></a>[35]  <a name="fr_649"></a><a href="#fn_649"><font size="1">650</font></a>
 Blessed are ye when men shall hate you, and separate you from them, and persecute you, and reproach you,  <a name="fr_650"></a><a href="#fn_650"><font size="1">651</font></a>
 and shall speak against you with all evil talk, for my [36] sake, falsely.   <a name="fr_651"></a><a href="#fn_651"><font size="1">652</font></a>
 Then rejoice and be glad, for your reward is great in heaven:  for so persecuted they the prophets before you.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p76"></a>[37]  <a name="fr_652"></a><a href="#fn_652"><font size="1">653</font></a>
 But woe unto you rich! for ye have received your consolation.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p78"></a>[38]  <a name="fr_653"></a><a href="#fn_653"><font size="1">654</font></a>
 Woe unto you that are satisfied! ye shall hunger.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p80"></a>Woe unto you that laugh now! ye shall weep and be sad.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p81"></a>[39]  <a name="fr_654"></a><a href="#fn_654"><font size="1">655</font></a>
 Woe unto you when men praise you! for so did their fathers use to do to the false prophets.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p83"></a>[40]  <a name="fr_655"></a><a href="#fn_655"><font size="1">656</font></a>
 Unto you do I say, <i>ye</i> which hear,  <a name="fr_656"></a><a href="#fn_656"><font size="1">657</font></a>
 Ye are the salt of the earth:  if then the salt become tasteless, wherewith shall it be salted?  For any purpose it is of no use, but [41] is thrown outside, and men tread upon it.   <a name="fr_657"></a><a href="#fn_657"><font size="1">658</font></a>
 Ye are the light of the world.  It is [42] impossible that a city built on a mountain should be hid.   <a name="fr_658"></a><a href="#fn_658"><font size="1">659</font></a>
 Neither do they light a <a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.viii-Page_57"></a>lamp and place it under a bushel, but on the lamp-stand, and it giveth light to all [43] who are in the house.   <a name="fr_659"></a><a href="#fn_659"><font size="1">660</font></a>
 So shall <a name="fr_660"></a><a href="#fn_660"><font size="1">661</font></a>
  your light shine before men, that they may see [44] your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven.   <a name="fr_661"></a><a href="#fn_661"><font size="1">662</font></a>
 There is nothing [45] secret that shall not be revealed, or hidden that shall not be known.   <a name="fr_662"></a><a href="#fn_662"><font size="1">663</font></a>
 Whoever hath ears that hear, let him hear.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p92"></a>[46]  <a name="fr_663"></a><a href="#fn_663"><font size="1">664</font></a>
 Think not that I came to destroy the law or the prophets; I came not to destroy, [47] but to complete.   <a name="fr_664"></a><a href="#fn_664"><font size="1">665</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, Until heaven and earth shall pass, there [Arabic, p. 33] shall not pass one point or one letter of the law, until all of it shall be [48] <i>accomplished</i>.   <a name="fr_665"></a><a href="#fn_665"><font size="1">666</font></a>
 Every one who shall violate now one of these small commandments, and shall teach men so, shall be called lacking in the kingdom of heaven:  every one that shall do and teach shall <a name="fr_666"></a><a href="#fn_666"><font size="1">667</font></a>
  be called great in the kingdom [49] of heaven.   <a name="fr_667"></a><a href="#fn_667"><font size="1">668</font></a>
 I say unto you now, unless your righteousness abound more than that of the scribes and Pharisees, ye shall not enter the kingdom of heaven.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p98"></a>[50]  <a name="fr_668"></a><a href="#fn_668"><font size="1">669</font></a>
 Ye have heard that it was said to the ancients, Do not kill; and every one that [51] killeth is worthy of the judgement.   <a name="fr_669"></a><a href="#fn_669"><font size="1">670</font></a>
 But I say unto you that every one who is angry with his brother without a cause is worthy of the judgement; and every one that saith to his brother, Thou foul one, is condemned <a name="fr_670"></a><a href="#fn_670"><font size="1">671</font></a>
  by the synagogue; and whosoever [52] saith to him, Thou fool, is worthy of the fire of Gehenna.   <a name="fr_671"></a><a href="#fn_671"><font size="1">672</font></a>
 If thou art now offering thy gift at the altar, and rememberest there that thy brother hath conceived [53] against thee any grudge,  <a name="fr_672"></a><a href="#fn_672"><font size="1">673</font></a>
 leave thy gift at the altar, and go first and satisfy thy [54] brother, and then return and offer thy gift.   <a name="fr_673"></a><a href="#fn_673"><font size="1">674</font></a>
 Join <a name="fr_674"></a><a href="#fn_674"><font size="1">675</font></a>
  thine adversary quickly,  <a name="fr_675"></a><a href="#fn_675"><font size="1">676</font></a>
 and while thou art still with him in the way, give a ransom and free thyself from him; [55] lest thine adversary deliver thee to the judge,  <a name="fr_676"></a><a href="#fn_676"><font size="1">677</font></a>
 and the judge deliver thee to the tax-collector, [56] and thou fall into prison.   <a name="fr_677"></a><a href="#fn_677"><font size="1">678</font></a>
 And verily I say unto thee, Thou shalt not go out thence until thou payest the last farthing.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.viii-p109"></a>[57, 58]  <a name="fr_678"></a><a href="#fn_678"><font size="1">679</font></a>
 Ye have heard that it was said, Do not commit adultery:   <a name="fr_679"></a><a href="#fn_679"><font size="1">680</font></a>
 but I now say unto you, that every one that looketh at a woman lusting after her hath forthwith already [59] [Arabic, p. 34] committed adultery with her in his heart.   <a name="fr_680"></a><a href="#fn_680"><font size="1">681</font></a>
 If thy right eye injure thee, put it out and cast it from thee; for it is preferable for thee that one of thy [60] members should perish, and not thy whole body go into the fire <i>of hell</i>.   <a name="fr_681"></a><a href="#fn_681"><font size="1">682</font></a>
 And if thy right hand injure thee, cut it off and cast it from thee; and it is better for thee that [61] one of thy members should perish, and not thy whole body fall into Gehenna.   <a name="fr_682"></a><a href="#fn_682"><font size="1">683</font></a>
 It was said that he that putteth away his wife <i>should</i> give her a writing of divorcement:  [62]  <a name="fr_683"></a><a href="#fn_683"><font size="1">684</font></a>
 but I say unto you, that every one that putteth away his wife, except for the cause of adultery, hath made it lawful for <a name="fr_684"></a><a href="#fn_684"><font size="1">685</font></a>
  her to commit adultery:  and whosoever taketh one that is put away committeth adultery.</p>
