 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xvi.ii.vii.xv-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xvi.ii.vii.xv-p1.1"></a>15.  How Men Followed Jesus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xvi.ii.vii.xv-p2"></a>Only, when Jesus had finished these words, having spoken them in Galilee about Capernaum, then “He departed thence, and came into the borders of Judæa,” <a name="fr_6150"></a><a href="#fn_6150"><font size="1">6151</font></a>
  which were different from Galilee.  But He came to the borders of Judæa, and not to the middle of it, but, as it were, to the outermost parts, where great multitudes followed Him, <a name="fr_6151"></a><a href="#fn_6151"><font size="1">6152</font></a>
  whom He healed at “the borders of Judæa beyond Jordan,”—where baptism had been given. <a name="fr_6152"></a><a href="#fn_6152"><font size="1">6153</font></a>
   But you will observe the difference between the crowds who simply followed, and Peter and the others who gave up everything and followed, and Matthew, who arose and followed him; <a name="fr_6153"></a><a href="#fn_6153"><font size="1">6154</font></a>
  he did not simply follow, but “having arisen;” for “having arisen” is an important addition.  There are always those, then, who follow like the great multitudes, who have not arisen that they may follow, nor have given up all that was theirs formerly, but few are they who have arisen and followed, who also, in the regeneration, shall sit on twelve thrones. <a name="fr_6154"></a><a href="#fn_6154"><font size="1">6155</font></a>
   Only, if one wishes to be healed, let him follow Jesus.</p>
