 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxxii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p1.1"></a>Section XXXII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_2182"></a><a href="#fn_2182"><font size="1">2183</font></a>
 And when Jesus entered Jerusalem, he went up to the temple of God, and found [2] there oxen and sheep and doves.   <a name="fr_2183"></a><a href="#fn_2183"><font size="1">2184</font></a>
 And when he beheld those that sold and those that bought, and the money-changers sitting,  <a name="fr_2184"></a><a href="#fn_2184"><font size="1">2185</font></a>
 he made for himself a scourge of rope, and drove them all out of the temple, and the sheep and the oxen, and the money-changers;  <a name="fr_2185"></a><a href="#fn_2185"><font size="1">2186</font></a>
 and he threw down their money, and upset their tables, and the seats of [3] them that sold the doves;  <a name="fr_2186"></a><a href="#fn_2186"><font size="1">2187</font></a>
 and he was teaching, and saying unto them, Is it not written, My house is a house of prayer for all peoples? and ye have made it a den [4] for robbers.   <a name="fr_2187"></a><a href="#fn_2187"><font size="1">2188</font></a>
 And he said unto those that sold the doves, Take this hence, and [5] make not my Fathers house a house of merchandise.   <a name="fr_2188"></a><a href="#fn_2188"><font size="1">2189</font></a>
 And he suffered not any [6] one to carry vessels inside the temple.   <a name="fr_2189"></a><a href="#fn_2189"><font size="1">2190</font></a>
 And his disciples remembered the scripture, [7] The zeal of thy house hath eaten me up.   <a name="fr_2190"></a><a href="#fn_2190"><font size="1">2191</font></a>
 The Jews answered and said unto him, [8] What sign hast thou shewn us, that thou doest this?   <a name="fr_2191"></a><a href="#fn_2191"><font size="1">2192</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto [9] them, Destroy this temple, and I shall raise it in three days.   <a name="fr_2192"></a><a href="#fn_2192"><font size="1">2193</font></a>
 The Jews said unto him, This temple was built in forty-six years, and wilt thou raise it in three days?  [10]  <a name="fr_2193"></a><a href="#fn_2193"><font size="1">2194</font></a>
 But he spake unto them of the temple of his body, that when <a name="fr_2194"></a><a href="#fn_2194"><font size="1">2195</font></a>
  they destroyed it, he [11] [Arabic, p. 122] would raise it in three days.   <a name="fr_2195"></a><a href="#fn_2195"><font size="1">2196</font></a>
 When therefore he rose from among the dead, his disciples remembered that he said this; and they believed the scriptures, and the word that Jesus spake.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p17"></a>[12]  <a name="fr_2196"></a><a href="#fn_2196"><font size="1">2197</font></a>
 And when Jesus sat down over against the treasury, he observed how the multitudes were casting their offerings into the treasury:  and many rich <i>men</i> were [13, 14] throwing in much.   <a name="fr_2197"></a><a href="#fn_2197"><font size="1">2198</font></a>
 And there came a poor widow, and cast in two mites.   <a name="fr_2198"></a><a href="#fn_2198"><font size="1">2199</font></a>
 And Jesus called his disciples, and said unto them, Verily I say unto you, This poor [15] widow cast into the treasury more than all the people:   <a name="fr_2199"></a><a href="#fn_2199"><font size="1">2200</font></a>
 and all of these cast into the place of the offering of God <a name="fr_2200"></a><a href="#fn_2200"><font size="1">2201</font></a>
  of the superfluity of their wealth; while this <i>woman</i> of her want threw in all that she possessed.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p23"></a>[16]  <a name="fr_2201"></a><a href="#fn_2201"><font size="1">2202</font></a>
 And he spake unto them this parable, concerning people who trusted in themselves [17] that they are righteous, and despised every man:   <a name="fr_2202"></a><a href="#fn_2202"><font size="1">2203</font></a>
 Two men went up to the [18] temple to pray; one of them a Pharisee, and the other a publican.   <a name="fr_2203"></a><a href="#fn_2203"><font size="1">2204</font></a>
 And the Pharisee stood apart, <a name="fr_2204"></a><a href="#fn_2204"><font size="1">2205</font></a>
  and prayed thus, O Lord, I thank thee, since I am not like the rest of men, the unjust, the profligate, the extortioners, or even like this publican; <a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxxii-Page_93"></a>[19]  <a name="fr_2205"></a><a href="#fn_2205"><font size="1">2206</font></a>
 but I fast two days a week, and tithe all my possessions. <a name="fr_2206"></a><a href="#fn_2206"><font size="1">2207</font></a>
    <a name="fr_2207"></a><a href="#fn_2207"><font size="1">2208</font></a>
 And the publican was [20] standing at a distance, and he would not even lift up his eyes to heaven, but was [21] beating upon his breast, and saying, O Lord, have mercy on me, me the sinner.   <a name="fr_2208"></a><a href="#fn_2208"><font size="1">2209</font></a>
 I say unto you, that this man went down justified to his house more than the Pharisee.  Every one that exalteth himself shall be abased; and every one that abaseth himself shall be exalted.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p32"></a>[22] [Arabic, p. 123]  <a name="fr_2209"></a><a href="#fn_2209"><font size="1">2210</font></a>
 And when eventide was come, he left all the people, and went outside the [23] city to Bethany, he and his twelve, and he remained there.   <a name="fr_2210"></a><a href="#fn_2210"><font size="1">2211</font></a>
 And all the people, because they knew the place, came to him, and he received them; and them that [24] had need of healing he healed.   <a name="fr_2211"></a><a href="#fn_2211"><font size="1">2212</font></a>
 And on the morning of the next day, when he returned [25] to the city from Bethany, he hungered.   <a name="fr_2212"></a><a href="#fn_2212"><font size="1">2213</font></a>
 And he saw a <a name="fr_2213"></a><a href="#fn_2213"><font size="1">2214</font></a>
  fig tree at a distance on the beaten highway, having on it leaves.  And he came unto it, <i>expecting</i> to find something on it; and when he came, he found nothing on it but the leaves—it <a name="fr_2214"></a><a href="#fn_2214"><font size="1">2215</font></a>
  was not [26] the season of figs— <a name="fr_2215"></a><a href="#fn_2215"><font size="1">2216</font></a>
 and he said unto it, Henceforward for ever let no man eat fruit of thee. And his disciples heard.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxii-p40"></a>[27]  <a name="fr_2216"></a><a href="#fn_2216"><font size="1">2217</font></a>
 And they came to Jerusalem.  And there was there a man of the Pharisees, [28] named Nicodemus, ruler of the Jews.   <a name="fr_2217"></a><a href="#fn_2217"><font size="1">2218</font></a>
 This <i>man</i> came unto Jesus by night, and said unto him, My Master, we know that thou hast been sent from God as a teacher; and no man can do these signs that thou doest, except him whom God is [29] with.   <a name="fr_2218"></a><a href="#fn_2218"><font size="1">2219</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto him, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, If a man [30] be not born a second <i>time</i>, he cannot see the kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_2219"></a><a href="#fn_2219"><font size="1">2220</font></a>
 Nicodemus said unto him, How can a man who is old be born? can he, think you, return again to [31] his mothers womb a second time, to enter and be born?   <a name="fr_2220"></a><a href="#fn_2220"><font size="1">2221</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto him, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, If a man be not born of water and the Spirit, [32] he cannot enter the kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_2221"></a><a href="#fn_2221"><font size="1">2222</font></a>
 For he that is born of flesh is flesh; and he that [33] is born of Spirit is spirit.   <a name="fr_2222"></a><a href="#fn_2222"><font size="1">2223</font></a>
 Wonder not that I said unto thee that ye must be born a [34] [Arabic, p. 124] second <i>time</i>.   <a name="fr_2223"></a><a href="#fn_2223"><font size="1">2224</font></a>
 The wind bloweth where it listeth, and thou hearest its voice, but thou knowest not from what place it cometh, nor whither it goeth:  so [35] is every man that is born of the Spirit.   <a name="fr_2224"></a><a href="#fn_2224"><font size="1">2225</font></a>
 Nicodemus answered and said unto him, [36] How can that be?   <a name="fr_2225"></a><a href="#fn_2225"><font size="1">2226</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto him, Art thou teaching <a name="fr_2226"></a><a href="#fn_2226"><font size="1">2227</font></a>
  Israel, [37] and <i>yet</i> knowest not these things?   <a name="fr_2227"></a><a href="#fn_2227"><font size="1">2228</font></a>
 Verily, verily, I say unto thee, What we know [38] we say, and what we have seen we witness:  and ye receive not our witness.   <a name="fr_2228"></a><a href="#fn_2228"><font size="1">2229</font></a>
 If I said unto you what is on earth, and ye believed not, how then, if I say unto you [39] what is in heaven, will ye believe?   <a name="fr_2229"></a><a href="#fn_2229"><font size="1">2230</font></a>
 And no man hath ascended up into heaven, except him that descended from heaven, the Son of man, which is in heaven.  [40]  <a name="fr_2230"></a><a href="#fn_2230"><font size="1">2231</font></a>
 And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so is the Son of man to be [41] lifted up;  <a name="fr_2231"></a><a href="#fn_2231"><font size="1">2232</font></a>
 so that every man who may believe in him may not perish, but have [42] eternal life.   <a name="fr_2232"></a><a href="#fn_2232"><font size="1">2233</font></a>
 God so loved the world, that <a name="fr_2233"></a><a href="#fn_2233"><font size="1">2234</font></a>
  he should give his only Son; and so every one that believeth on him should not perish, but should have eternal life.  [43]  <a name="fr_2234"></a><a href="#fn_2234"><font size="1">2235</font></a>
 God sent not his Son into the world to judge the world; but that the world might [44] be saved by his hand.   <a name="fr_2235"></a><a href="#fn_2235"><font size="1">2236</font></a>
 He that believeth in him shall not be judged:  but he that believeth not is condemned beforehand, because he hath not believed in the name [45] of the only <i>Son</i>, the Son of God. <a name="fr_2236"></a><a href="#fn_2236"><font size="1">2237</font></a>
    <a name="fr_2237"></a><a href="#fn_2237"><font size="1">2238</font></a>
 This is the judgement, that the light came into the world, and men loved the darkness more than the light; because their deeds [46] were evil.   <a name="fr_2238"></a><a href="#fn_2238"><font size="1">2239</font></a>
 Whosoever doeth evil deeds hateth the light, and cometh not to the [47] light, lest his deeds be reproved.   <a name="fr_2239"></a><a href="#fn_2239"><font size="1">2240</font></a>
 But he that doeth the truth cometh to the light, that his deeds may be known, that they have been done in God.</p>
