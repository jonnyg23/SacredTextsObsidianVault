 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxiii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p1.1"></a>Section XXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1605"></a><a href="#fn_1605"><font size="1">1606</font></a>
 And Jesus departed thence, and came to the side of the sea of Galilee, and went [2] up into the mountain, and sat there.   <a name="fr_1606"></a><a href="#fn_1606"><font size="1">1607</font></a>
 And there came unto him great multitudes, having with them lame, and blind, and dumb, and maimed, and many others, and [3] they cast them at the feet of Jesus:   <a name="fr_1607"></a><a href="#fn_1607"><font size="1">1608</font></a>
 for they had seen all the signs which he did in [4] Jerusalem, when they were gathered at the feast.   <a name="fr_1608"></a><a href="#fn_1608"><font size="1">1609</font></a>
 And he healed them all.   <a name="fr_1609"></a><a href="#fn_1609"><font size="1">1610</font></a>
 And <a name="page_79"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 79</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxiii-Page_79"></a>those multitudes marvelled when they saw dumb <i>men</i> speak, and maimed <i>men</i> healed, and lame <i>men</i> walk, and blind <i>men</i> see; and they praised the God of Israel.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p8"></a>[5]  <a name="fr_1610"></a><a href="#fn_1610"><font size="1">1611</font></a>
 And Jesus called his disciples, and said unto them, I have compassion on this multitude, because of their continuing with me three days, having nothing to eat; and to send them away fasting I am not willing, lest they faint in the way,  <a name="fr_1611"></a><a href="#fn_1611"><font size="1">1612</font></a>
 some of them having [6] [Arabic, p. 89] come from far.   <a name="fr_1612"></a><a href="#fn_1612"><font size="1">1613</font></a>
 His disciples said unto him, Whence have we in the desert [7] bread wherewith to satisfy all this multitude?   <a name="fr_1613"></a><a href="#fn_1613"><font size="1">1614</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, How [8] many loaves have ye?   <a name="fr_1614"></a><a href="#fn_1614"><font size="1">1615</font></a>
 They said unto him, Seven, and a few small fishes.  And he [9] commanded the multitudes to sit down upon the ground;  <a name="fr_1615"></a><a href="#fn_1615"><font size="1">1616</font></a>
 and he took those seven loaves and the fish, and blessed, and brake, and gave to his disciples to set before [10] them; and the disciples set before the multitudes.   <a name="fr_1616"></a><a href="#fn_1616"><font size="1">1617</font></a>
 And they all ate, and were satisfied:  and they took that which remained over of the fragments, seven basketfuls.  [11]  <a name="fr_1617"></a><a href="#fn_1617"><font size="1">1618</font></a>
 And the people that ate were four thousand men, besides the women and children.  [12]  <a name="fr_1618"></a><a href="#fn_1618"><font size="1">1619</font></a>
 And when the multitudes departed, he went up into the boat, and came to the borders of Magada. <a name="fr_1619"></a><a href="#fn_1619"><font size="1">1620</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p19"></a>[13]  <a name="fr_1620"></a><a href="#fn_1620"><font size="1">1621</font></a>
 And the Pharisees and Sadducees came to him, and began to seek a discussion with him.  And they asked him to shew them a sign from heaven, tempting him.  [14]  <a name="fr_1621"></a><a href="#fn_1621"><font size="1">1622</font></a>
 And Jesus sighed within himself, and said, What sign seeketh this evil and adulterous generation?  It seeketh a sign, and it shall not be given a sign, except the sign [15] of Jonah the prophet.   <a name="fr_1622"></a><a href="#fn_1622"><font size="1">1623</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, This generation shall not be given a [16] sign.   <a name="fr_1623"></a><a href="#fn_1623"><font size="1">1624</font></a>
 And he left <a name="fr_1624"></a><a href="#fn_1624"><font size="1">1625</font></a>
  them, and went up into the boat, and went away to that side.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p25"></a>[17]  <a name="fr_1625"></a><a href="#fn_1625"><font size="1">1626</font></a>
 And his disciples forgot to take with them bread, and there was not with them [18] in the boat, not even <a name="fr_1626"></a><a href="#fn_1626"><font size="1">1627</font></a>
  one loaf.   <a name="fr_1627"></a><a href="#fn_1627"><font size="1">1628</font></a>
 And Jesus charged them, and said, Take heed, and guard yourselves from the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees, and from the [19] leaven of Herod.   <a name="fr_1628"></a><a href="#fn_1628"><font size="1">1629</font></a>
 And they reflected within themselves that they had taken with them [20] no bread.   <a name="fr_1629"></a><a href="#fn_1629"><font size="1">1630</font></a>
 And Jesus knew, and said unto them, Why <a name="fr_1630"></a><a href="#fn_1630"><font size="1">1631</font></a>
  think ye within yourselves, O <i>ye</i> of little faith, and are anxious, because ye have no bread?  <a name="fr_1631"></a><a href="#fn_1631"><font size="1">1632</font></a>
 until now do ye not perceive, [21] neither understand? is your heart yet hard?   <a name="fr_1632"></a><a href="#fn_1632"><font size="1">1633</font></a>
 And have ye eyes, and <i>yet</i> see not? [22] [Arabic, p. 90] and have ye ears, and <i>yet</i> hear not?  <a name="fr_1633"></a><a href="#fn_1633"><font size="1">1634</font></a>
 and do ye not remember when I brake those five loaves for five thousand? and how many baskets full of broken [23] pieces took ye <a name="fr_1634"></a><a href="#fn_1634"><font size="1">1635</font></a>
  up?  They said, Twelve.   <a name="fr_1635"></a><a href="#fn_1635"><font size="1">1636</font></a>
 He said unto them, And the seven also for four thousand:  how many baskets full of broken pieces took ye <a name="fr_1636"></a><a href="#fn_1636"><font size="1">1637</font></a>
  up?  They [24] said, Seven.   <a name="fr_1637"></a><a href="#fn_1637"><font size="1">1638</font></a>
 He said unto them, How have ye not understood that I spake not to you because of <a name="fr_1638"></a><a href="#fn_1638"><font size="1">1639</font></a>
  the bread, but that ye should beware of the leaven of the Pharisees [25] and Sadducees?   <a name="fr_1639"></a><a href="#fn_1639"><font size="1">1640</font></a>
 Then they understood that he spake, not that they should beware of the leaven of the bread, but of the doctrine of the Pharisees and Sadducees, which he called leaven.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p41"></a>[26]  <a name="fr_1640"></a><a href="#fn_1640"><font size="1">1641</font></a>
 And after that, he came to Bethsaida.  And they brought to him a certain <a name="fr_1641"></a><a href="#fn_1641"><font size="1">1642</font></a>
  blind [27] <i>man</i>, and besought him that he would touch him.   <a name="fr_1642"></a><a href="#fn_1642"><font size="1">1643</font></a>
 And he took the hand of that blind man, and led him out without the village, and spat in his eyes, and laid his [28] hand on him, <a name="fr_1643"></a><a href="#fn_1643"><font size="1">1644</font></a>
  and asked him, What seest thou?   <a name="fr_1644"></a><a href="#fn_1644"><font size="1">1645</font></a>
 And that blind man looked intently, [29] and said unto him, I see men as trees walking.   <a name="fr_1645"></a><a href="#fn_1645"><font size="1">1646</font></a>
 And he placed his hand [30] again on his eyes; and they were restored, <a name="fr_1646"></a><a href="#fn_1646"><font size="1">1647</font></a>
  and he saw everything clearly.   <a name="fr_1647"></a><a href="#fn_1647"><font size="1">1648</font></a>
 And he sent him to his house, and said, Do not enter even into the village, nor tell any man in the village.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p50"></a>[31]  <a name="fr_1648"></a><a href="#fn_1648"><font size="1">1649</font></a>
 And Jesus went forth, and his disciples, to the villages of Cæsarea Philippi.  [32]  <a name="fr_1649"></a><a href="#fn_1649"><font size="1">1650</font></a>
 And while he was going in the way, and his disciples alone, <a name="fr_1650"></a><a href="#fn_1650"><font size="1">1651</font></a>
  he asked his disciples, [33] and said, What do men say of me that I am, the Son of man? <a name="fr_1651"></a><a href="#fn_1651"><font size="1">1652</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1652"></a><a href="#fn_1652"><font size="1">1653</font></a>
 They said unto him, Some say, John the Baptist; and others, Elijah; and others, Jeremiah, or one of the [34, 35] prophets.   <a name="fr_1653"></a><a href="#fn_1653"><font size="1">1654</font></a>
 He said unto them, And ye, what say ye that I am?   <a name="fr_1654"></a><a href="#fn_1654"><font size="1">1655</font></a>
 Simon Cephas answered [36] [Arabic, p. 91] and said, Thou art the Messiah, the Son of the living God.   <a name="fr_1655"></a><a href="#fn_1655"><font size="1">1656</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto him, Blessed art thou, Simon son of Jonah:  flesh and [37] blood hath not revealed <i>it</i> unto thee, but my Father which is in heaven.   <a name="fr_1656"></a><a href="#fn_1656"><font size="1">1657</font></a>
 And I say unto <a name="page_80"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 80</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxiii-Page_80"></a>thee also, that thou art Cephas, <a name="fr_1657"></a><a href="#fn_1657"><font size="1">1658</font></a>
  and on this rock will I build my church; and the [38] gates of Hades shall not prevail against it.   <a name="fr_1658"></a><a href="#fn_1658"><font size="1">1659</font></a>
 To thee will I give the keys of the kingdom of heaven:  and whatsoever thou shalt bind on earth shall be bound in heaven; and [39] whatsoever thou shalt loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven.   <a name="fr_1659"></a><a href="#fn_1659"><font size="1">1660</font></a>
 And he sternly charged his disciples, and warned them that they should not tell any man concerning him, [40] that he was the Messiah.   <a name="fr_1660"></a><a href="#fn_1660"><font size="1">1661</font></a>
 And henceforth began Jesus to shew to his disciples [41] that he was determined <a name="fr_1661"></a><a href="#fn_1661"><font size="1">1662</font></a>
  to go to Jerusalem,  <a name="fr_1662"></a><a href="#fn_1662"><font size="1">1663</font></a>
 and suffer much, and be rejected of the elders, and of the chief priests, and of the scribes, and be killed, and on the [42] third day rise.   <a name="fr_1663"></a><a href="#fn_1663"><font size="1">1664</font></a>
 And he was speaking <a name="fr_1664"></a><a href="#fn_1664"><font size="1">1665</font></a>
  plainly.   <a name="fr_1665"></a><a href="#fn_1665"><font size="1">1666</font></a>
 And Simon Cephas, as one grieved [43] for him, said, Far be thou, my Lord, from that.   <a name="fr_1666"></a><a href="#fn_1666"><font size="1">1667</font></a>
 And he turned, and looked upon [44] his disciples, and rebuked Simon, and said,  <a name="fr_1667"></a><a href="#fn_1667"><font size="1">1668</font></a>
 Get thee behind me, Satan:  for thou art a stumblingblock unto me:  for thou thinkest not of what pertains to God, but of what pertains to men.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiii-p71"></a>[45]  <a name="fr_1668"></a><a href="#fn_1668"><font size="1">1669</font></a>
 And Jesus called the multitudes with his disciples, and said unto them, Whosoever would come after me, let him deny himself, and take his cross every day, and [46] come after me.   <a name="fr_1669"></a><a href="#fn_1669"><font size="1">1670</font></a>
 And whosoever would save his life shall lose it; and whosoever [47] loseth his life for my sake, and for the sake of my gospel, shall save it.   <a name="fr_1670"></a><a href="#fn_1670"><font size="1">1671</font></a>
 What shall [48] a man profit, if he gain all the world, and destroy <a name="fr_1671"></a><a href="#fn_1671"><font size="1">1672</font></a>
  his own life, <a name="fr_1672"></a><a href="#fn_1672"><font size="1">1673</font></a>
  or lose it?  <a name="fr_1673"></a><a href="#fn_1673"><font size="1">1674</font></a>
 or what [49] [Arabic, p. 92] will a man give <i>in</i> ransom for his life? <a name="fr_1674"></a><a href="#fn_1674"><font size="1">1675</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1675"></a><a href="#fn_1675"><font size="1">1676</font></a>
 Whosoever shall deny me and my sayings in this sinful and adulterous generation, the Son of man also will [50] deny him, when he cometh in the glory of his Father with his holy angels.   <a name="fr_1676"></a><a href="#fn_1676"><font size="1">1677</font></a>
 For the Son of man is about to <a name="fr_1677"></a><a href="#fn_1677"><font size="1">1678</font></a>
  come in the glory of his Father with his holy angels; and then shall he reward each man according to his works.</p>
