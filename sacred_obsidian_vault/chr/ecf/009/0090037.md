 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p1.1"></a>Section XXVIII. <a name="fr_1931"></a><a href="#fn_1931"><font size="1">1932</font></a>
 </span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p3"></a>[1, 2] [Arabic, p. 107]  <a name="fr_1932"></a><a href="#fn_1932"><font size="1">1933</font></a>
 And at that time the feast of tabernacles of the Jews drew near.   <a name="fr_1933"></a><a href="#fn_1933"><font size="1">1934</font></a>
 So the brethren of Jesus said unto him, Remove now hence, and go to Judæa, that [3] thy disciples may see the deeds that thou doest.   <a name="fr_1934"></a><a href="#fn_1934"><font size="1">1935</font></a>
  For no man doeth a thing secretly [4] and wisheth to be apparent.   <a name="fr_1935"></a><a href="#fn_1935"><font size="1">1936</font></a>
 If thou doest this, shew thyself to the world.  For [5] up to this time not even the brethren of Jesus believed on him.   <a name="fr_1936"></a><a href="#fn_1936"><font size="1">1937</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, My time till now has not arrived; but as for you, your time is alway ready.  [6]  <a name="fr_1937"></a><a href="#fn_1937"><font size="1">1938</font></a>
 It is not possible for the world to hate you; but me it hateth, for I bear witness [7] against it, that its deeds are evil.   <a name="fr_1938"></a><a href="#fn_1938"><font size="1">1939</font></a>
 As for you, go ye up unto this feast:  but I go [8] not up now to this feast; for my time has not yet been completed.   <a name="fr_1939"></a><a href="#fn_1939"><font size="1">1940</font></a>
 He said this, and remained behind in Galilee.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p12"></a>[9]  <a name="fr_1940"></a><a href="#fn_1940"><font size="1">1941</font></a>
 But when his brethren went up unto the feast, he journeyed from Galilee, and [10] came to the borders of Judæa, to <i>the country</i> beyond Jordan;  <a name="fr_1941"></a><a href="#fn_1941"><font size="1">1942</font></a>
 and there came after [11] him great multitudes, and he healed them all there.   <a name="fr_1942"></a><a href="#fn_1942"><font size="1">1943</font></a>
 And he went out, and proceeded [12] to the feast, not openly, but as one that conceals himself.   <a name="fr_1943"></a><a href="#fn_1943"><font size="1">1944</font></a>
 And the Jews sought him [13] at the feast, and said, In what place is this <i>man?</i>   <a name="fr_1944"></a><a href="#fn_1944"><font size="1">1945</font></a>
 And there occurred much murmuring there in the great multitude that came to the feast, on his account.  For [14] some said, He is good:  and others said, Nay, but he leadeth the people astray.   <a name="fr_1945"></a><a href="#fn_1945"><font size="1">1946</font></a>
 But no man spake of him openly for fear of the Jews.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p19"></a>[15] [Arabic, p. 108]  <a name="fr_1946"></a><a href="#fn_1946"><font size="1">1947</font></a>
 But when the days of the feast of tabernacles were half over, Jesus went [16] up to the temple, and taught.   <a name="fr_1947"></a><a href="#fn_1947"><font size="1">1948</font></a>
 And the Jews wondered, and said, How doth [17] this man know writing, <a name="fr_1948"></a><a href="#fn_1948"><font size="1">1949</font></a>
  seeing he hath not learned?   <a name="fr_1949"></a><a href="#fn_1949"><font size="1">1950</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said, My doctrine <a name="fr_1950"></a><a href="#fn_1950"><font size="1">1951</font></a>
  [18] is not mine, but his that sent me.   <a name="fr_1951"></a><a href="#fn_1951"><font size="1">1952</font></a>
 Whoever wisheth to do his will understandeth my doctrine, <a name="fr_1952"></a><a href="#fn_1952"><font size="1">1953</font></a>
  whether it be from God, or whether I speak of mine own accord.  [19]  <a name="fr_1953"></a><a href="#fn_1953"><font size="1">1954</font></a>
 Whosoever speaketh of his own accord seeketh praise for himself; but whosoever seeketh praise for him that sent him, he is true, and unrighteousness in his heart [20] there is none.   <a name="fr_1954"></a><a href="#fn_1954"><font size="1">1955</font></a>
 Did not Moses give you the law, and no man of you keepeth the [21] law?   <a name="fr_1955"></a><a href="#fn_1955"><font size="1">1956</font></a>
 Why seek ye to kill me?  The multitude answered and said unto him, Thou [22] hast demons: <a name="fr_1956"></a><a href="#fn_1956"><font size="1">1957</font></a>
   who seeketh to kill thee?   <a name="fr_1957"></a><a href="#fn_1957"><font size="1">1958</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto them, I did [23] one deed, and ye all marvel because of this.   <a name="fr_1958"></a><a href="#fn_1958"><font size="1">1959</font></a>
 Moses hath given you circumcision (not because it is from Moses, but it is from the fathers); and ye on the sabbath <a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 87</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxviii-Page_87"></a>[24] circumcise a man.   <a name="fr_1959"></a><a href="#fn_1959"><font size="1">1960</font></a>
 And if a man is circumcised on the sabbath day, that the law of Moses may not be broken; are ye angry at me, because I healed on the sabbath [25] day the whole man?   <a name="fr_1960"></a><a href="#fn_1960"><font size="1">1961</font></a>
 Judge not with hypocrisy, but judge righteous judgement.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p35"></a>[26]  <a name="fr_1961"></a><a href="#fn_1961"><font size="1">1962</font></a>
 And some people from Jerusalem said, Is not this he whom they seek to slay?  [27]  <a name="fr_1962"></a><a href="#fn_1962"><font size="1">1963</font></a>
 And lo, he discourseth with them openly, and they say nothing unto him.  Think [28] you that our elders have learned that this is the Messiah indeed?   <a name="fr_1963"></a><a href="#fn_1963"><font size="1">1964</font></a>
 But this man is <a name="fr_1964"></a><a href="#fn_1964"><font size="1">1965</font></a>
  known whence he is; and the Messiah, when he cometh, no man knoweth whence [29] he is.   <a name="fr_1965"></a><a href="#fn_1965"><font size="1">1966</font></a>
 So Jesus lifted up his voice as he taught in the temple, and said, Ye both know me, and know whence I am; and of my own accord am I not come, but he [30] [Arabic, p. 109] that sent me is true, he whom ye know not:   <a name="fr_1966"></a><a href="#fn_1966"><font size="1">1967</font></a>
 but I know him; for I am [31] from him, and he sent me.   <a name="fr_1967"></a><a href="#fn_1967"><font size="1">1968</font></a>
 And they sought to seize him:  and no man [32] laid a hand on him, because his hour had not yet come.   <a name="fr_1968"></a><a href="#fn_1968"><font size="1">1969</font></a>
 But many of the multitude believed on him; and they said, The Messiah, when he cometh, can it be that he will do more than these signs that this <i>man</i> doeth?</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p44"></a>[33]  <a name="fr_1969"></a><a href="#fn_1969"><font size="1">1970</font></a>
 And a man of that multitude said unto our Lord, Teacher, say to my brother [34] that he divide with me the inheritance.   <a name="fr_1970"></a><a href="#fn_1970"><font size="1">1971</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, Man, who is it that [35] appointed me over you as a judge and divider?   <a name="fr_1971"></a><a href="#fn_1971"><font size="1">1972</font></a>
 And he said unto his disciples, Take heed within yourselves of all inordinate desire; for it is not in abundance of [36] possessions that life shall be.   <a name="fr_1972"></a><a href="#fn_1972"><font size="1">1973</font></a>
 And he gave them this parable:  The ground of a [37] rich man brought forth abundant produce:   <a name="fr_1973"></a><a href="#fn_1973"><font size="1">1974</font></a>
 and he pondered within himself, and [38] said, What shall I do, since I have no place to store my produce?   <a name="fr_1974"></a><a href="#fn_1974"><font size="1">1975</font></a>
 And he said, I will do this:  I will pull down the buildings of my barns, and build them, and make [39] them greater; and store there all my wheat and my goods.   <a name="fr_1975"></a><a href="#fn_1975"><font size="1">1976</font></a>
 And I will say to my soul, Soul, thou hast much goods laid by for many years; take thine ease, eat, [40] drink, enjoy thyself.   <a name="fr_1976"></a><a href="#fn_1976"><font size="1">1977</font></a>
 God said unto him, O <i>thou</i> of little intelligence, this night shall thy soul be taken from thee; and this that thou hast prepared, whose shall it [41] be?   <a name="fr_1977"></a><a href="#fn_1977"><font size="1">1978</font></a>
 So is he that layeth up treasures for himself, and is not rich in God.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxviii-p54"></a>[42]  <a name="fr_1978"></a><a href="#fn_1978"><font size="1">1979</font></a>
 And while Jesus was going in the way, there came near to him a young man <a name="fr_1979"></a><a href="#fn_1979"><font size="1">1980</font></a>
  of the rulers, <a name="fr_1980"></a><a href="#fn_1980"><font size="1">1981</font></a>
  and fell on his knees, and asked him, and said, Good Teacher, what is [43] it that I must do that I may have eternal life?   <a name="fr_1981"></a><a href="#fn_1981"><font size="1">1982</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, Why callest thou [44] me good, while there is none good but the one, <i>even</i> God? <a name="fr_1982"></a><a href="#fn_1982"><font size="1">1983</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1983"></a><a href="#fn_1983"><font size="1">1984</font></a>
 Thou knowest the commandments. <a name="fr_1984"></a><a href="#fn_1984"><font size="1">1985</font></a>
   [45]  <a name="fr_1985"></a><a href="#fn_1985"><font size="1">1986</font></a>
 If thou wouldest enter into life, keep the commandments. <a name="fr_1986"></a><a href="#fn_1986"><font size="1">1987</font></a>
   The young [Arabic, p. 110] man said unto him, Which of the commandments? <a name="fr_1987"></a><a href="#fn_1987"><font size="1">1988</font></a>
   Jesus said unto him, [46]  <a name="fr_1988"></a><a href="#fn_1988"><font size="1">1989</font></a>
 Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not do injury, Honour thy father [47] and thy mother:  and, Love thy neighbour as thyself.   <a name="fr_1989"></a><a href="#fn_1989"><font size="1">1990</font></a>
 That young man said unto [48] him, All these have I kept from my youth:  what then is it that I lack?   <a name="fr_1990"></a><a href="#fn_1990"><font size="1">1991</font></a>
 And Jesus [49] looked intently at him, and loved him, and said unto him,  <a name="fr_1991"></a><a href="#fn_1991"><font size="1">1992</font></a>
 If thou wouldest be perfect, what thou lackest is one thing: <a name="fr_1992"></a><a href="#fn_1992"><font size="1">1993</font></a>
   go away and sell everything that thou hast, and give to the poor, and thou shalt have treasure in heaven:  and take thy [50] cross, and follow me.   <a name="fr_1993"></a><a href="#fn_1993"><font size="1">1994</font></a>
 And that young man frowned at this word, and went away [51] feeling sad; for he was very rich.   <a name="fr_1994"></a><a href="#fn_1994"><font size="1">1995</font></a>
 And when Jesus saw his sadness, he looked towards his disciples, and said unto them, How hard it is for them that have possessions to enter the kingdom of God!</p>
