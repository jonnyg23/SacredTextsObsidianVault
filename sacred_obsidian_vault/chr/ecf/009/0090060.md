 <p> <a name="iv.iii.li"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.li-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.li-p1.1"></a>Section LI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.li-p2"></a>[1] [Arabic, p. 192]  <a name="fr_3532"></a><a href="#fn_3532"><font size="1">3533</font></a>
 And when Pilate heard this saying, he took Jesus out, and sat on the tribune in the place which was called the pavement of stones, but in the Hebrew [2] called Gabbatha.   <a name="fr_3533"></a><a href="#fn_3533"><font size="1">3534</font></a>
 And that day was the Friday of the passover:  and it had reached [3] about the sixth hour. <a name="fr_3534"></a><a href="#fn_3534"><font size="1">3535</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3535"></a><a href="#fn_3535"><font size="1">3536</font></a>
 And he said to the Jews, Behold, your King!  And they cried out, Take him, take him, crucify him, crucify him.  Pilate said unto them, Shall I crucify your King?  The chief priests said unto him, We have no king except [4] Cæsar.   <a name="fr_3536"></a><a href="#fn_3536"><font size="1">3537</font></a>
 And Pilate, when he saw <i>it</i>, and <a name="fr_3537"></a><a href="#fn_3537"><font size="1">3538</font></a>
  he was gaining nothing, but the tumult <a name="page_122"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 122</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.li-Page_122"></a>was increasing, took water, and washed his hands before the multitude, and said, I [5] am innocent of the blood of this innocent <i>man</i>:  ye shall know. <a name="fr_3538"></a><a href="#fn_3538"><font size="1">3539</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3539"></a><a href="#fn_3539"><font size="1">3540</font></a>
 And all the people [6] answered and said, His blood be on us, and on our children.   <a name="fr_3540"></a><a href="#fn_3540"><font size="1">3541</font></a>
 Then Pilate commanded to grant them their request; and delivered up Jesus to be crucified, according to their wish.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p12"></a>[7]  <a name="fr_3541"></a><a href="#fn_3541"><font size="1">3542</font></a>
 Then Judas the betrayer, when he saw Jesus wronged, went and returned the [8] thirty <i>pieces</i> of money to the chief priests and the elders,  <a name="fr_3542"></a><a href="#fn_3542"><font size="1">3543</font></a>
 and said, I have sinned in my betraying innocent blood.  And they said unto him, And we, what must we <i>do</i>? [9] know thou.   <a name="fr_3543"></a><a href="#fn_3543"><font size="1">3544</font></a>
 And he threw down the money in the temple, and departed; and <i>he</i> [10] went away <a name="fr_3544"></a><a href="#fn_3544"><font size="1">3545</font></a>
  and hanged <a name="fr_3545"></a><a href="#fn_3545"><font size="1">3546</font></a>
  himself.   <a name="fr_3546"></a><a href="#fn_3546"><font size="1">3547</font></a>
 And the chief priests took the money, and said, We have not authority to cast it into the place of the offering, <a name="fr_3547"></a><a href="#fn_3547"><font size="1">3548</font></a>
  for it is the price [11] of blood.   <a name="fr_3548"></a><a href="#fn_3548"><font size="1">3549</font></a>
 And they took counsel, and bought with it the plain of the potter, for [12] the burial of strangers.   <a name="fr_3549"></a><a href="#fn_3549"><font size="1">3550</font></a>
 Therefore that plain was called, The field of blood, unto [13] [Arabic, p. 193] this day.   <a name="fr_3550"></a><a href="#fn_3550"><font size="1">3551</font></a>
 Therein <a name="fr_3551"></a><a href="#fn_3551"><font size="1">3552</font></a>
  was fulfilled the saying in the prophet which said, I took thirty <i>pieces</i> of money, the price of the precious <i>one, which was</i> fixed [14] by the children of Israel;  <a name="fr_3552"></a><a href="#fn_3552"><font size="1">3553</font></a>
 and I paid them for the plain of the potter, as the Lord commanded me.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p25"></a>[15]  <a name="fr_3553"></a><a href="#fn_3553"><font size="1">3554</font></a>
 And the Jews took Jesus, and went away to crucify him.   <a name="fr_3554"></a><a href="#fn_3554"><font size="1">3555</font></a>
 And when he bare his [16] cross and went out, they stripped him of those purple and scarlet garments which he [17] had on, and put on him his <i>own</i> garments.   <a name="fr_3555"></a><a href="#fn_3555"><font size="1">3556</font></a>
 And while they were going with him, they found a man, a Cyrenian, coming from the country, named Simon, the father of Alexander and Rufus:   <a name="fr_3556"></a><a href="#fn_3556"><font size="1">3557</font></a>
 and they compelled this <i>man</i> to bear the cross of Jesus.  [18]  <a name="fr_3557"></a><a href="#fn_3557"><font size="1">3558</font></a>
 And they took the cross and laid it upon him, that he might bear it, and come after Jesus; and Jesus went, and his cross behind him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p31"></a>[19]  <a name="fr_3558"></a><a href="#fn_3558"><font size="1">3559</font></a>
 And there followed him much people, and women which were lamenting and [20] raving. <a name="fr_3559"></a><a href="#fn_3559"><font size="1">3560</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3560"></a><a href="#fn_3560"><font size="1">3561</font></a>
 But Jesus turned unto them and said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not [21] for me:  weep for yourselves, and for your children.   <a name="fr_3561"></a><a href="#fn_3561"><font size="1">3562</font></a>
 Days are coming, when they shall say, Blessed are the barren, and the wombs that bare not, and the breasts [22] that gave not suck.   <a name="fr_3562"></a><a href="#fn_3562"><font size="1">3563</font></a>
 Then shall they begin to say to the mountains, Fall on us; and [23] to the hills, Cover us.   <a name="fr_3563"></a><a href="#fn_3563"><font size="1">3564</font></a>
 For if they do so in the green tree, <a name="fr_3564"></a><a href="#fn_3564"><font size="1">3565</font></a>
  what shall be in the dry?</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p39"></a>[24]  <a name="fr_3565"></a><a href="#fn_3565"><font size="1">3566</font></a>
 And they brought with Jesus two others of the malefactors, <a name="fr_3566"></a><a href="#fn_3566"><font size="1">3567</font></a>
  to be put to death.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p42"></a>[25]  <a name="fr_3567"></a><a href="#fn_3567"><font size="1">3568</font></a>
 And when they came unto a certain place called The skull, and called in the Hebrew Golgotha, they crucified him there:   <a name="fr_3568"></a><a href="#fn_3568"><font size="1">3569</font></a>
 they crucified with him these two [26] malefactors, one on his right, and the other on his left.   <a name="fr_3569"></a><a href="#fn_3569"><font size="1">3570</font></a>
 And the scripture was [27] [Arabic, p. 194] fulfilled, which saith, He was numbered with the transgressors.   <a name="fr_3570"></a><a href="#fn_3570"><font size="1">3571</font></a>
 And they gave him to drink wine and myrrh, and vinegar which had been mixed with the myrrh;  <a name="fr_3571"></a><a href="#fn_3571"><font size="1">3572</font></a>
 and he tasted, and would not drink; and he received it not.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p48"></a>[28]  <a name="fr_3572"></a><a href="#fn_3572"><font size="1">3573</font></a>
 And the soldiers, when they had crucified Jesus, took his garments, and cast lots for them in four parts, to every party of the soldiers a part; and his tunic was [29] without sewing, from the top woven throughout.   <a name="fr_3573"></a><a href="#fn_3573"><font size="1">3574</font></a>
 And they said one to another, Let us not rend it, but cast lots for it, whose it shall be:  and the scripture was fulfilled, which saith,</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.li-p51"></a>They divided my garments among them,</p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="iv.iii.li-p52"></a>And cast the lot for my vesture.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p53"></a>[30, 31]  <a name="fr_3574"></a><a href="#fn_3574"><font size="1">3575</font></a>
 This the soldiers did.  And they sat and guarded him there.   <a name="fr_3575"></a><a href="#fn_3575"><font size="1">3576</font></a>
 And Pilate wrote on a tablet the cause of his death, and put it on the wood of the cross above his head. <a name="fr_3576"></a><a href="#fn_3576"><font size="1">3577</font></a>
   And there was written upon it thus:  <span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.li-p56.2"></a>This is Jesus the Nazarene, the King of the</span> [32] <span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.li-p56.3"></a>Jews</span>.   <a name="fr_3577"></a><a href="#fn_3577"><font size="1">3578</font></a>
 And this tablet <a name="fr_3578"></a><a href="#fn_3578"><font size="1">3579</font></a>
  read many of the Jews:  for the place where Jesus was crucified was near the city:  and it was written in Hebrew and Greek and Latin.  [33]  <a name="fr_3579"></a><a href="#fn_3579"><font size="1">3580</font></a>
 And the chief priests said unto Pilate, Write not, The King of the Jews; but, He it is [34] that <a name="fr_3580"></a><a href="#fn_3580"><font size="1">3581</font></a>
  said, I am the King of the Jews.   <a name="fr_3581"></a><a href="#fn_3581"><font size="1">3582</font></a>
 Pilate said unto them, What hath been [35] written hath been written. <a name="fr_3582"></a><a href="#fn_3582"><font size="1">3583</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3583"></a><a href="#fn_3583"><font size="1">3584</font></a>
 And the people were standing beholding; and they <a name="page_123"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 123</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.li-Page_123"></a>[36] that passed by were reviling <a name="fr_3584"></a><a href="#fn_3584"><font size="1">3585</font></a>
  him, and shaking <a name="fr_3585"></a><a href="#fn_3585"><font size="1">3586</font></a>
  their heads, and saying,  <a name="fr_3586"></a><a href="#fn_3586"><font size="1">3587</font></a>
 Thou that destroyest the temple, and buildest it in three days,  <a name="fr_3587"></a><a href="#fn_3587"><font size="1">3588</font></a>
 save thyself if thou art the Son [37] of God, and come down from the cross.   <a name="fr_3588"></a><a href="#fn_3588"><font size="1">3589</font></a>
 And in like manner the chief priests and the [Arabic, p. 195] scribes and the elders and the Pharisees derided him, and laughed one with [38, 39] another, and said,  <a name="fr_3589"></a><a href="#fn_3589"><font size="1">3590</font></a>
 The saviour of others cannot save himself.   <a name="fr_3590"></a><a href="#fn_3590"><font size="1">3591</font></a>
 If he is the Messiah, the chosen of God, and the King of Israel, <a name="fr_3591"></a><a href="#fn_3591"><font size="1">3592</font></a>
  let him come down now from the [40] cross, that we may see, and believe in him.   <a name="fr_3592"></a><a href="#fn_3592"><font size="1">3593</font></a>
 He that relieth on God—let him deliver him [41] now, if he is pleased with him:  for he said, I am the Son of God.   <a name="fr_3593"></a><a href="#fn_3593"><font size="1">3594</font></a>
 And the soldiers [42] also scoffed at him in that they came near unto him,  <a name="fr_3594"></a><a href="#fn_3594"><font size="1">3595</font></a>
 and brought him vinegar, and [43] said unto him, If thou art the King of the Jews, save thyself.   <a name="fr_3595"></a><a href="#fn_3595"><font size="1">3596</font></a>
 And likewise the two robbers <a name="fr_3596"></a><a href="#fn_3596"><font size="1">3597</font></a>
  also that were crucified with him reproached him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p77"></a>[44]  <a name="fr_3597"></a><a href="#fn_3597"><font size="1">3598</font></a>
 And one of those two malefactors who were crucified with him reviled him, and [45] said, If thou art the Messiah, save thyself, and save us also.   <a name="fr_3598"></a><a href="#fn_3598"><font size="1">3599</font></a>
 But his comrade rebuked him, and said, Dost thou not even fear God, being thyself also in this [46] condemnation?   <a name="fr_3599"></a><a href="#fn_3599"><font size="1">3600</font></a>
 And we with justice, and as we deserved, and according to our deed, <a name="fr_3600"></a><a href="#fn_3600"><font size="1">3601</font></a>
  have we been rewarded:  but this <i>man</i> hath not done anything unlawful.  [47]  <a name="fr_3601"></a><a href="#fn_3601"><font size="1">3602</font></a>
 And he said unto Jesus, Remember me, my Lord, when thou comest in thy kingdom.  [48]  <a name="fr_3602"></a><a href="#fn_3602"><font size="1">3603</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, Verily <a name="fr_3603"></a><a href="#fn_3603"><font size="1">3604</font></a>
  I say unto thee, To-day shalt thou be with me in Paradise.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p85"></a>[49]  <a name="fr_3604"></a><a href="#fn_3604"><font size="1">3605</font></a>
 And there stood by the cross of Jesus his mother, and his mothers sister, <a name="fr_3605"></a><a href="#fn_3605"><font size="1">3606</font></a>
  [50] Mary <a name="fr_3606"></a><a href="#fn_3606"><font size="1">3607</font></a>
  that was related to Clopas, and Mary Magdalene.   <a name="fr_3607"></a><a href="#fn_3607"><font size="1">3608</font></a>
 And Jesus saw his mother, and that disciple whom he loved standing <i>by</i>; and he said to his mother, [51] Woman, behold, thy son!   <a name="fr_3608"></a><a href="#fn_3608"><font size="1">3609</font></a>
 And he said to that disciple, Behold, thy mother!  And from that hour that disciple took her unto him<i>self</i>.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.li-p91"></a>[52] [Arabic, p. 196]  <a name="fr_3609"></a><a href="#fn_3609"><font size="1">3610</font></a>
 And from the sixth hour <a name="fr_3610"></a><a href="#fn_3610"><font size="1">3611</font></a>
  darkness was on all the land unto the ninth [53] hour, <a name="fr_3611"></a><a href="#fn_3611"><font size="1">3612</font></a>
  and the sun became dark.   <a name="fr_3612"></a><a href="#fn_3612"><font size="1">3613</font></a>
 And at the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, and said, Yail, Yaili, <a name="fr_3613"></a><a href="#fn_3613"><font size="1">3614</font></a>
  why hast thou forsaken me? which <a name="fr_3614"></a><a href="#fn_3614"><font size="1">3615</font></a>
  is, My [54] God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?   <a name="fr_3615"></a><a href="#fn_3615"><font size="1">3616</font></a>
 And some of those that stood there, when they heard, said, <a name="fr_3616"></a><a href="#fn_3616"><font size="1">3617</font></a>
  This <i>man</i> called Elijah.</p>
