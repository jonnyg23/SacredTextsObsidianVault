 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p1.1"></a>Section XII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_887"></a><a href="#fn_887"><font size="1">888</font></a>
 And all the multitude of the Gadarenes entreated him to depart from them, because that great fear took hold upon them.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p4"></a>[2, 3]  <a name="fr_888"></a><a href="#fn_888"><font size="1">889</font></a>
 But Jesus went up into the ship, and crossed, and came to his city.   <a name="fr_889"></a><a href="#fn_889"><font size="1">890</font></a>
 And that man from whom the devils went out entreated that he might stay with him; but [4] Jesus sent him away, and said unto him,  <a name="fr_890"></a><a href="#fn_890"><font size="1">891</font></a>
 Return to thy house, and make known what [5] God hath done for thee.   <a name="fr_891"></a><a href="#fn_891"><font size="1">892</font></a>
 And he went, and began to publish in Decapolis <a name="fr_892"></a><a href="#fn_892"><font size="1">893</font></a>
  what Jesus had done for him; and they all marvelled.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p10"></a>[6]  <a name="fr_893"></a><a href="#fn_893"><font size="1">894</font></a>
 And when Jesus had crossed in the ship to that side, a great multitude received [7] him; and they were all looking for him.   <a name="fr_894"></a><a href="#fn_894"><font size="1">895</font></a>
 And a man named Jairus, the chief of the [8] synagogue, fell before the feet of Jesus,  <a name="fr_895"></a><a href="#fn_895"><font size="1">896</font></a>
 and besought him much, and said unto him, I have an only daughter, and she is come nigh unto death;  <a name="fr_896"></a><a href="#fn_896"><font size="1">897</font></a>
 but come and lay thy [9] hand upon her, and she shall live.   <a name="fr_897"></a><a href="#fn_897"><font size="1">898</font></a>
 And Jesus rose, and his disciples, and they followed [10] him.   <a name="fr_898"></a><a href="#fn_898"><font size="1">899</font></a>
 And there joined him a great multitude, and they pressed him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p17"></a>[11, 12]  <a name="fr_899"></a><a href="#fn_899"><font size="1">900</font></a>
 And a woman, which had a flow of blood for twelve years,  <a name="fr_900"></a><a href="#fn_900"><font size="1">901</font></a>
 <i>had</i> suffered much of many physicians, and spent all that she had, and was not benefited at all, but her [13] trouble increased further.   <a name="fr_901"></a><a href="#fn_901"><font size="1">902</font></a>
 And when she heard of Jesus, she came in the thronging of [14] [Arabic, p. 47] the crowd behind him, and touched <a name="fr_902"></a><a href="#fn_902"><font size="1">903</font></a>
  his garments;  <a name="fr_903"></a><a href="#fn_903"><font size="1">904</font></a>
 and she thought within [15] herself, If I <i>could</i> reach to touch his garments, I should live.   <a name="fr_904"></a><a href="#fn_904"><font size="1">905</font></a>
 And immediately the fountain of her blood was dried; and she felt in her body that she was healed [16] of her plague.   <a name="fr_905"></a><a href="#fn_905"><font size="1">906</font></a>
 And Jesus straightway knew within himself that power had gone out of him; and he turned to the crowd, and said, Who approached unto my garments?  [17]  <a name="fr_906"></a><a href="#fn_906"><font size="1">907</font></a>
 And on their denying, all of them, Simon Cephas and those with him said unto him, Our Master, the multitudes throng thee and press thee, and sayest thou, Who approached [18] unto me?   <a name="fr_907"></a><a href="#fn_907"><font size="1">908</font></a>
 And he said, Some one approached unto me; and I knew that [19] power went forth from me.   <a name="fr_908"></a><a href="#fn_908"><font size="1">909</font></a>
 And that woman, when she saw that she was not hid [20] from him, came fearing and agitated (for she knew what had happened to her),  <a name="fr_909"></a><a href="#fn_909"><font size="1">910</font></a>
 and fell down and worshipped him, and told, in the presence of all the people, for what [21] reason she touched <i>him</i>, and how she was healed immediately.   <a name="fr_910"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">911</font></a>
 And Jesus said unto her, Be of good courage, daughter; thy faith hath made thee alive; depart in peace, and be whole from thy plague.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p30"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_911"></a><a href="#fn_911"><font size="1">912</font></a>
 And while he was yet speaking, there came a man from the house of the chief of the synagogue, and said unto him, Thy daughter hath died; so trouble not the [23] teacher.   <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_912"><font size="1">913</font></a>
 But Jesus heard, and said unto the father of the maid, Fear not:  but believe [24] only, and she shall live.   <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">914</font></a>
 And he suffered no man to go with him, except [25] Simon Cephas, and James, and John the brother of James.   <a name="fr_914"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">915</font></a>
 And they reached the house of the chief of the synagogue; and he saw them agitated, weeping and wailing.  [26]  <a name="fr_915"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">916</font></a>
 And he entered, and said unto them, Why are ye agitated <i>and</i> weeping? the [27] [Arabic, p. 48] maid hath not died, but she is sleeping.   <a name="fr_916"></a><a href="#fn_916"><font size="1">917</font></a>
 And they laughed at him, for [28] they knew that she had died.   <a name="fr_917"></a><a href="#fn_917"><font size="1">918</font></a>
 And he put every man forth without, and took the father of the maid, and her mother, and Simon, and James, and John, and [29] entered into the place where the maid was laid.   <a name="fr_918"></a><a href="#fn_918"><font size="1">919</font></a>
 And he took hold of the hand of the maid, and said unto her, Maid, arise.   <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_919"><font size="1">920</font></a>
 And her spirit returned, and straightway [30] she arose and walked:   <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_920"><font size="1">921</font></a>
 and she was about twelve years <i>of age</i>.   <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_921"><font size="1">922</font></a>
 And he commanded [31] that there should be given to her something to eat.   <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_922"><font size="1">923</font></a>
 And her father wondered greatly:  <a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xii-Page_63"></a>[32] and he warned them that they should tell no man what had happened.   <a name="fr_923"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">924</font></a>
 And this report spread in all that land.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p44"></a>[33]  <a name="fr_924"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">925</font></a>
 And when Jesus crossed over from there, there joined him two blind men, crying [34] out, and saying, Have mercy on us, <i>thou</i> son of David.   <a name="fr_925"></a><a href="#fn_925"><font size="1">926</font></a>
 And when he came to the house, those two blind men came to him:  and Jesus said unto them, Believe ye [35] that I am able to do this?  They said unto him, Yea, our Lord.   <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_926"><font size="1">927</font></a>
 Then he touched <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_927"><font size="1">928</font></a>
  [36] their eyes, and said, As ye have believed, it shall be unto you.   <a name="fr_928"></a><a href="#fn_928"><font size="1">929</font></a>
 And immediately their eyes were opened.  And Jesus forbade them, and said, See that no man know.  [37]  <a name="fr_929"></a><a href="#fn_929"><font size="1">930</font></a>
 But they went out and published the news in all that land.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p51"></a>[38]  <a name="fr_930"></a><a href="#fn_930"><font size="1">931</font></a>
 And when Jesus went out, they brought to him a dumb man having a devil.  [39]  <a name="fr_931"></a><a href="#fn_931"><font size="1">932</font></a>
 And on the going out of the devil that dumb man spake.  And the multitudes marvelled, and said, It was never so seen in Israel</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xii-p54"></a>[40]  <a name="fr_932"></a><a href="#fn_932"><font size="1">933</font></a>
 And Jesus was going about in all the cities and <i>in</i> the villages, and teaching in their synagogues, and proclaiming the good news of the kingdom, and healing every disease [41] [Arabic, p. 49] and sickness; and many followed him.   <a name="fr_933"></a><a href="#fn_933"><font size="1">934</font></a>
 And when Jesus saw the multitudes, he had compassion on them, for they were wearied and scattered, <a name="fr_934"></a><a href="#fn_934"><font size="1">935</font></a>
  as sheep [42] that have no shepherd.   <a name="fr_935"></a><a href="#fn_935"><font size="1">936</font></a>
 And he called his twelve disciples, and gave them power and [43] much authority over all devils and diseases;  <a name="fr_936"></a><a href="#fn_936"><font size="1">937</font></a>
 and sent them two and two, that they [44] might proclaim the kingdom of God, and <i>to</i> heal the sick.   <a name="fr_937"></a><a href="#fn_937"><font size="1">938</font></a>
 And he charged them, and said, Walk not in the way of the heathen, nor enter into the cities of the Samaritans. <a name="fr_938"></a><a href="#fn_938"><font size="1">939</font></a>
   [45, 46]  <a name="fr_939"></a><a href="#fn_939"><font size="1">940</font></a>
 Go especially unto the sheep that are lost of the sons of Israel.   <a name="fr_940"></a><a href="#fn_940"><font size="1">941</font></a>
 And [47] when ye go, proclaim and say, The kingdom of heaven is come near.   <a name="fr_941"></a><a href="#fn_941"><font size="1">942</font></a>
 And heal the sick, and cleanse the lepers, and cast out the devils:  freely ye have received, freely [48, 49] give.   <a name="fr_942"></a><a href="#fn_942"><font size="1">943</font></a>
 Get you not gold, nor silver, nor brass in your purses;  <a name="fr_943"></a><a href="#fn_943"><font size="1">944</font></a>
 and take nothing for the way, except a staff only; nor bag, nor bread; neither shall ye have two tunics, [50] nor shoes, nor staff, but be shod with sandals;  <a name="fr_944"></a><a href="#fn_944"><font size="1">945</font></a>
 for the labourer is worthy of his food.  [51]  <a name="fr_945"></a><a href="#fn_945"><font size="1">946</font></a>
 And whatever city or village ye enter, inquire who is worthy in it, and there be until [52, 53] ye go out.   <a name="fr_946"></a><a href="#fn_946"><font size="1">947</font></a>
 And when ye enter into the house, ask for the peace of the house:  and if the house is worthy, your peace shall come upon it;  <a name="fr_947"></a><a href="#fn_947"><font size="1">948</font></a>
 but if it is not worthy, your [54] peace shall return unto you.   <a name="fr_948"></a><a href="#fn_948"><font size="1">949</font></a>
 And whosoever shall not receive you, nor hear your sayings, when ye go out from that house, or from that village, shake off the dust [55] [Arabic, p. 50] that is under your feet against them for a testimony.   <a name="fr_949"></a><a href="#fn_949"><font size="1">950</font></a>
 And verily I say unto you, To the land of Sodom and Gomorrah there shall be rest in the day of judgement, rather than to that city.</p>
