 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.xxxi-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.xxxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXI.—Let Us See by What Means We May Obtain the Divine Blessing.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.xxxi-p2"></a>Let us cleave then to His blessing, and consider what are the means <a name="fr_4148"></a><a href="#fn_4148"><font size="1">4149</font></a>
  of possessing it.  Let us think <a name="fr_4149"></a><a href="#fn_4149"><font size="1">4150</font></a>
  over the things which have taken place from the beginning.  For what reason was our father Abraham blessed?  Was it not because he wrought righteousness and truth through faith?  Isaac, <a name="fr_4150"></a><a href="#fn_4150"><font size="1">4151</font></a>
  with perfect confidence, as if knowing what was to happen, <a name="fr_4151"></a><a href="#fn_4151"><font size="1">4152</font></a>
  cheerfully yielded himself as a sacrifice. <a name="fr_4152"></a><a href="#fn_4152"><font size="1">4153</font></a>
   Jacob, through reason <a name="fr_4153"></a><a href="#fn_4153"><font size="1">4154</font></a>
  of his brother, went forth with humility from his own land, and came to Laban and served him; and there was given to him the sceptre of the twelve tribes of Israel.</p>
