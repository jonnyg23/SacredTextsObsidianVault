 <p> <a name="iii.vi.iii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iii.vi.iii-p0.1"></a>General Note.</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.vi.iii-p1"></a><span><a name="iii.vi.iii-p1.1"></a>There</span> is no <span class="sc"><a name="iii.vi.iii-p1.2"></a>ms</span> authority for ascribing the above to <a name="iii.vi.iii-p1.3"></a>Lactantius. “It does not, in the least, come up to the purity and eloquence of his style,” says <a name="iii.vi.iii-p1.4"></a>Dupin; and the same candid author notes the “adoration of the cross” as fatal to any such claim. <a name="fr_2101"></a><a href="#fn_2101"><font size="1">2102</font></a>
    </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.vi.iii-p3"></a>Of the following poem, on Easter, <a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.1"></a>Dupin says: “It is attributed to <a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.2"></a>Venantius upon the testimony of some <span><a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.3"></a>mss.</span> in the Vatican Library.” This writer became known to <a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.4"></a>Gregory of Tours, who died about <span><a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.5"></a>a.d. </span>595, and seems to have succeeded him as bishop, dying soon after. <a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.6"></a>Bede quotes his verse on St. <a name="iii.vi.iii-p3.7"></a>Alban, <a name="fr_2102"></a><a href="#fn_2102"><font size="1">2103</font></a>
 —  </p>  <blockquote><a name="iii.vi.iii-p4.3"></a>   “<span><a name="iii.vi.iii-p4.5"></a>Albanum egregium fecunda Britannia profert,</span>”<br>  </blockquote>  
 <p class="Continue"><a name="iii.vi.iii-p5"></a>but styles him “<span><a name="iii.vi.iii-p5.1"></a>presbyter </span><a name="iii.vi.iii-p5.2"></a>Fortunatus.” He was the author of a poem on <a name="iii.vi.iii-p5.3"></a>St. Martin, and another, <a name="iii.vi.iii-p5.4"></a>In Laude Virginum. His works were edited by <a name="iii.vi.iii-p5.5"></a>Brouverius, a Jesuit.   </p>
