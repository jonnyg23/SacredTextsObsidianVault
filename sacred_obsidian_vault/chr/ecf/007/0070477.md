 </p><p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p1"></a><a name="page_524"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 524</font></a> <a name="xi-Page_524"></a>  </p>  <h3 align="center"><a name="xi-p1.1"></a>Nicene Council</h3>  <h2 align="center"><a name="xi-p1.2"></a>The Nicene Creed.</h2>  <h5 align="center"><a name="xi-p1.3"></a>The Creed</h5>  
 <p class="Centered"><a name="xi-p2"></a><span><a name="xi-p2.1"></a>As set forth at Nicœa,</span> <a name="fr_4013"></a><a href="#fn_4013"><font size="1">4014</font></a>
  <span><a name="xi-p3.5"></a>a.d.</span><span><a name="xi-p3.6"></a> 325.</span>  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p4"></a><span><a name="xi-p4.1"></a>We</span> believe in one God, the Father Almighty, Maker of all things, visible and invisible:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p5"></a>And in one Lord <a name="xi-p5.1"></a>Jesus Christ, the Son of God, begotten of the Father, only begotten, that is, of the substance of the Father;  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p6"></a>God of God; Light of light; very God of very God; begotten, not made; being of one substance with the Father,  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p7"></a>By whom all things were made, both things in heaven and things in earth:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p8"></a>Who for us men and for our salvation came down, and was incarnate, and was made man:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p9"></a>He suffered, and rose again the third day:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p10"></a>And ascended into heaven:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p11"></a>And shall come again to judge the quick and the dead.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p12"></a>And in the Holy Ghost, etc. <a name="fr_4014"></a><a href="#fn_4014"><font size="1">4015</font></a>
   </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="xi-p13.4"></a>The Ratification.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyTextHanging"><a name="xi-p14"></a><span><a name="xi-p14.1"></a>And those who say </span><span><a name="xi-p14.2"></a>There was a time when He was not</span><span><a name="xi-p14.3"></a>, or that </span><span><a name="xi-p14.4"></a>Before He was begotten He was not</span><span><a name="xi-p14.5"></a>, or that </span><span><a name="xi-p14.6"></a>He was made out of nothing;</span><span><a name="xi-p14.7"></a> or who say that The </span><span><a name="xi-p14.8"></a>Son of God is of any other substance</span><span><a name="xi-p14.9"></a>, or that </span><span><a name="xi-p14.10"></a>He is changeable</span><span><a name="xi-p14.11"></a> or </span><span><a name="xi-p14.12"></a>unstable</span><span><a name="xi-p14.13"></a>,—these the Catholic and Apostolic Church anathematizes.</span>  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="xi-p14.14"></a>Addenda, </h5>  
 <p class="Centered"><a name="xi-p15"></a><span><a name="xi-p15.1"></a>As Authorized at </span><span><a name="xi-p15.2"></a>Constantinople</span><span><a name="xi-p15.3"></a>, </span><span><a name="xi-p15.4"></a>a.d.</span><span><a name="xi-p15.5"></a> 381.</span>  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p16"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p16.1"></a>a</span>) Of heaven and earth.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p17"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p17.1"></a>b</span>) Begotten of the Father before all worlds.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p18"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p18.1"></a>c</span>) By the Holy Ghost of the Virgin Mary.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p19"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p19.1"></a>d</span>) Was crucified also for us, under <a name="xi-p19.2"></a>Pontius Pilate,  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p20"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p20.1"></a>e</span>) And was buried.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p21"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p21.1"></a>f</span>) Sitteth on the right hand of the Father,  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p22"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p22.1"></a>g</span>) Whose kingdom shall have no end.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p23"></a>(<span><a name="xi-p23.1"></a>h</span>) The Lord, the Giver of life,  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p24"></a>Who proceedeth from the Father; <a name="fr_4015"></a><a href="#fn_4015"><font size="1">4016</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p26"></a>Who with the Father and the Son together is worshipped and glorified; <a name="page_525"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 525</font></a> <a name="xi-Page_525"></a>  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p27"></a>Who spake by the prophets:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p28"></a>In one Holy Catholic and Apostolic Church.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p29"></a>We acknowledge one baptism for the remission of sins.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p30"></a>We look for the resurrection of the dead,  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p31"></a>And the life of the world to come. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p32"></a>  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xi-p33"></a>This Nicæno-Constantinopolitan Creed was solemnly ratified by the Council of Ephesus (<span><a name="xi-p33.1"></a>a.d.</span> 431) with the decree <a name="fr_4016"></a><a href="#fn_4016"><font size="1">4017</font></a>
  that “No one <a name="fr_4017"></a><a href="#fn_4017"><font size="1">4018</font></a>
  shall be permitted to introduce, write, or compose <span><a name="xi-p35.4"></a>any other faith,</span> <a name="fr_4018"></a><a href="#fn_4018"><font size="1">4019</font></a>
  besides that which was defined by the holy Fathers assembled in the city of Nice, with <span><a name="xi-p36.5"></a>the presence of</span> the Holy Ghost.”  </p>

 <p class="Footnote"><a name="xi-p35"></a> <span><a name="xi-p35.1"></a>No one</span>. This re-affirms the action of Nicæa itself, and forbids the imposition of anything novel as a creed by any authority whatever. Nothing, therefore, which has not been set forth by Nicene authority (or by the supplementing and co-equal councils of the whole Church, from the same primitive sources) can be a creed, strictly speaking. It may be an orthodox confession, like the <span class="Foreign"><a name="xi-p35.2"></a>Quicunque Vult</span>, but cannot be imposed in terms of communion, any more than the <span class="Foreign"><a name="xi-p35.3"></a>Te Deum</span>   </p>

 <p class="Footnote"><a name="xi-p36"></a> <span><a name="xi-p36.1"></a>Any other faith</span>. The composition and setting north of <span><a name="xi-p36.2"></a>another faith</span>, as terms of communion, by Pius IV., bishop of Rome, <span class="sc"><a name="xi-p36.3"></a>a.d.</span> 1564, and its acceptance, with additional dogmas, at the opening of the Vatican Council (so-called), <span class="sc"><a name="xi-p36.4"></a>a.d.</span> 1869, brought the whole Papal communion under this anathema of Ephesus.  </p>
