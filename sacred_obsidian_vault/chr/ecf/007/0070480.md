 <p> <a name="xii.ii"></a>  </p><h2 align="center"><a name="xii.ii-p0.1"></a>EARLY LITURGIES. <a name="fr_4085"></a><a href="#fn_4085"><font size="1">4086</font></a>
 </h2>  <h3 align="center"><a name="xii.ii-p1.1"></a>The Divine Liturgy of James the Holy Apostle and Brother of the Lord.</h3>  <h5 align="center"><a name="xii.ii-p1.2"></a>i.</h5>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p2"></a>The Priest. <a name="fr_4086"></a><a href="#fn_4086"><font size="1">4087</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p4.1"></a>I</span> O <span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p4.2"></a>Sovereign</span> Lord our God, contemn me not, defiled with a multitude of sins: for, behold, I have come to this Thy divine and heavenly mystery, not as being worthy; but looking only to Thy goodness, I direct my voice to Thee: God be merciful to me, a sinner; I have sinned against Heaven, and before Thee, and am unworthy to come into the presence of this Thy holy and spiritual table, upon which Thy only-begotten Son, and our Lord <a name="xii.ii-p4.3"></a>Jesus Christ, is mystically set forth as a sacrifice for me, a sinner, and stained with every spot. Wherefore I present to Thee this supplication and thanksgiving, that Thy Spirit the Comforter may be sent down upon me, strengthening and fitting me for this service; and count me worthy to make known without condemnation the word, delivered from Thee by me to the people, in Christ <a name="xii.ii-p4.4"></a>Jesus our Lord, with whom Thou art blessed, together with Thy all-holy, and good, and quickening, and consubstantial <a name="fr_4087"></a><a href="#fn_4087"><font size="1">4088</font></a>
  Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p6"></a>Prayer of the standing beside the altar.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p7.1"></a>II</span> Glory to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit, the triune light of the Godhead, which is unity subsisting in trinity, divided, yet indivisible: for the Trinity is the one God Almighty, whose glory the heavens declare, and the earth His dominion, and the sea His might, and every sentient and intellectual creature at all times proclaims His majesty: for all glory becomes Him, and honour and might, greatness and magnificence, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p8"></a>Prayer of the incense at the beginning. <a name="fr_4088"></a><a href="#fn_4088"><font size="1">4089</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p10"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p10.1"></a>III</span>  Sovereign Lord <a name="xii.ii-p10.2"></a>Jesus Christ, O Word of God, who didst freely offer Thyself a blameless sacrifice upon the cross to God even the Father, the coal of double nature, that didst touch the lips of the prophet with the tongs, and didst take away his sins, touch also the hearts of us sinners, and purify us from every stain, and present us holy beside Thy holy altar, that we may offer Thee a sacrifice of praise: and accept from us, Thy unprofitable servants, this incense as an odour of a sweet smell, and make fragrant the evil odour of our soul and body, and purify us with the sanctifying power of Thy all-holy Spirit: for Thou alone art holy, who sanctifiest, and art communicated to the faithful; and glory becomes Thee, with Thy eternal Father, and Thy all-holy, and good, and quickening Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p11"></a>Prayer of the commencement.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p12"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p12.1"></a>IV</span>  O beneficent King eternal, and Creator of the universe, receive Thy Church, coming unto Thee through Thy Christ: fulfil to each what is profitable; lead all to perfection, and make us perfectly worthy of the grace of Thy sanctification, gathering us together within Thy holy Church, which Thou hast purchased by the precious blood of Thy only-begotten Son, and our Lord and Saviour <a name="xii.ii-p12.2"></a>Jesus Christ, with whom Thou art blessed and glorified, together with Thy all-holy, and good, and quickening Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p13"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p14"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p14.1"></a>V</span> Let us again pray to the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p15"></a>The Priest, prayer of the incense at the entrance of the congregation.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p16"></a>God, who didst accept the gifts of <a name="xii.ii-p16.1"></a>Abel, the sacrifice of <a name="xii.ii-p16.2"></a>Noah and of <a name="xii.ii-p16.3"></a>Abram, the incense of <a name="page_538"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 538</font></a> <a name="xii.ii-Page_538"></a><a name="xii.ii-p16.4"></a>Aaron and of <a name="xii.ii-p16.5"></a>Zacharias, accept also from the hand of us sinners this incense for an odour of a sweet smell, and for remission of our sins, and those of all Thy people; for blessed art Thou, and glory becomes Thee, the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Spirit, now and ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p17"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p18"></a>Sir, pronounce the blessing. <a name="fr_4089"></a><a href="#fn_4089"><font size="1">4090</font></a>
    </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p20"></a>The Priest prays.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p21"></a>Our Lord and God, <a name="xii.ii-p21.1"></a>Jesus Christ, who through exceeding goodness and love not to be restrained wast crucified, and didst not refuse to be pierced by the spear and nails; who didst provide this mysterious and awful service as an everlasting memorial for us perpetually: bless Thy ministry in Christ the God, and bless our entrance, and fully complete the presentation of this our service by Thy unutterable compassion, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p22"></a>The responsive prayer from the Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p23"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p23.1"></a>VI. </span>The Lord bless us, and make us worthy seraphically to offer gifts, and to sing the oft-sung hymn of the divine Trisagion, by the fulness and exceeding abundance of all the perfection of holiness, now and ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p24"></a>Then the Deacon begins to sing in the entrance. <a name="fr_4090"></a><a href="#fn_4090"><font size="1">4091</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p26"></a>Thou who art the only-begotten Son and Word of God, immortal; who didst submit for our salvation to become flesh of the holy God-mother, <a name="fr_4091"></a><a href="#fn_4091"><font size="1">4092</font></a>
  and ever-virgin Mary; who didst immutably become man and wast crucified, O Christ our God, and didst by Thy death tread death underfoot; who art one of the Holy Trinity glorified together with the Father and the Holy Spirit, save us.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p28"></a>The Priest says this prayer from the gates to the altar.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p29"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xii.ii-p29.1"></a>VII</span> God Almighty, Lord great in glory, who hast given to us an entrance into the Holy of Holies, through the sojourning among men of Thy only-begotten Son, our Lord, and God, and Saviour <a name="xii.ii-p29.2"></a>Jesus Christ, we supplicate and invoke Thy goodness, since we are fearful and trembling when about to stand at Thy holy altar; send forth upon us, O God, Thy good grace, and sanctify our souls, and bodies, and spirits, and turn our thoughts to piety, in order that with a pure conscience we may bring unto Thee gifts, offerings, and fruits for the remission of our transgressions, and for the propitiation of all Thy people, by the grace and mercies and loving-kindness of Thy only-begotten Son, with whom Thou art blessed to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p30"></a>After the approach to the altar, the Priest says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p31"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p31.1"></a>VIII. </span>Peace be to all.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p32"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p33"></a>And to thy spirit.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p34"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p35"></a>The Lord bless us all, and sanctify us for the entrance and celebration of the divine and pure mysteries, giving rest to the blessed souls among the good and just, by His grace and loving-kindness, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p36"></a>Then the Deacon says the bidding prayer. <a name="fr_4092"></a><a href="#fn_4092"><font size="1">4093</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p38"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p38.1"></a>IX. </span>In peace let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p39"></a>For the peace that is from above, and for Gods love to man, and for the salvation of our souls, let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p40"></a>For the peace of the whole world, for the unity of all the holy churches of God, let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p41"></a>For the remission of our sins, and forgiveness of our transgressions, and for our deliverance from all tribulation, wrath, danger, and distress, and from the uprising of our enemies, let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p42"></a>Then the Singers sing the Trisagion Hymn.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p43"></a>Holy God, holy mighty, holy immortal, have mercy upon us.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p44"></a>Then the Priest prays, bowing.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p45"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p45.1"></a>X. </span>O compassionate and merciful, long-suffering, and very gracious and true God, look from Thy prepared dwelling-place, and hear us Thy suppliants, and deliver us from every temptation of the devil and of man; withhold not Thy aid from us, nor bring on us chastisements too heavy for our strength: for we are unable to overcome what is opposed to us; but Thou art able, Lord, to save us from everything that is against us. Save us, O God, from the difficulties of this world, according to Thy goodness, in order that, having drawn nigh with a pure conscience to Thy holy altar, we may send up to Thee without condemnation the blessed hymn Trisagion, together with the heavenly powers, and that, having performed the service, well pleasing to Thee and divine, we may be counted worthy of eternal life.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p46"></a>(Aloud.)   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p47"></a>Because Thou art holy, Lord our God, and dwellest and abidest in holy places, we send up the praise and the hymn Trisagion to Thee, the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity. <a name="page_539"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 539</font></a> <a name="xii.ii-Page_539"></a>  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p48"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p49"></a>Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p50"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p51"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p51.1"></a>XI. </span>Peace be to all.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p52"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p53"></a>And to thy spirit.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p54"></a>The Singers.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p55"></a>Alleluia.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p56"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p56.1"></a>Then there are read in order</span> <a name="fr_4093"></a><a href="#fn_4093"><font size="1">4094</font></a>
 <span><a name="xii.ii-p57.3"></a> the holy oracles of the Old Testament, and of the prophets; and the incarnation of the Son of God is set forth, and His sufferings and resurrection from the dead, His ascension into heaven, and His second appearing with glory; and this takes place daily in the holy and divine service.</span> <a name="fr_4094"></a><a href="#fn_4094"><font size="1">4095</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p59"></a>After the reading and instruction the Deacon says:—   </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p60"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p60.1"></a>XII. </span>Let us all say, Lord, be merciful. <a name="fr_4095"></a><a href="#fn_4095"><font size="1">4096</font></a>
    </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p62"></a>Lord Almighty, the God of our fathers;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p63"></a>We beseech Thee, hear us.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p64"></a>For the peace which is from above, and for the salvation of our souls;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p65"></a>Let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p66"></a>For the peace of the whole world, and the unity of all the holy churches of God;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p67"></a>Let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p68"></a>For the salvation and help of all the Christ-loving people;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p69"></a>We beseech Thee, hear us.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p70"></a>For our deliverance from all tribulation, wrath, danger, distress, from captivity, bitter death, and from our iniquities;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p71"></a>We beseech Thee, hear us.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p72"></a>For the people standing round, and waiting for the rich and plenteous mercy that is from Thee;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p73"></a>We beseech Thee, be merciful and gracious.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p74"></a>Save Thy people, O Lord, and bless Thine inheritance.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p75"></a>Visit Thy world in mercy and compassion.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p76"></a>Exalt the horn of Christians by the power of the precious and quickening cross.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p77"></a>We beseech Thee, most merciful Lord, hear us praying to Thee, and have mercy upon us.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p78"></a>The People (thrice).  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p79"></a>Lord, have mercy upon us.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p80"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p81"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p81.1"></a>XIII. </span>For the remission of our sins, and forgiveness of our transgressions, and for our deliverance from all tribulation, wrath, danger, and distress, let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p82"></a>Let us all entreat from the Lord, that we may pass the whole day, perfect, holy, peaceful, and without sin.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p83"></a>Let us entreat from the Lord a messenger of peace, a faithful guide, a guardian of our souls and bodies.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p84"></a>Let us entreat from the Lord forgiveness and remission of our sins and transgressions.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p85"></a>Let us entreat from the Lord the things which are good and proper for our souls, and peace for the world.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p86"></a>Let us entreat from the Lord, that we may spend the remaining period of our life in peace and health.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p87"></a>Let us entreat that the close of our lives may be Christian, without pain and without shame, and a good plea at the dread and awful judgment-seat of Christ.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p88"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p89"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p89.1"></a>XIV. </span>For Thou art the gospel and the light, Saviour and keeper of our souls and bodies, God, and Thy only-begotten Son, and Thy all-holy Spirit, now and ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p90"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p91"></a>Amen. <a name="fr_4096"></a><a href="#fn_4096"><font size="1">4097</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p97"></a>The Priest  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p98"></a>God, who hast taught us Thy divine and saving oracles, enlighten the souls of us sinners for the comprehension of the things which have been before spoken, so that we may not only be seen to be hearers of spiritual things, but also doers of good deeds, striving after guileless faith, blameless life, and pure conversation.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p99"></a>(Aloud.)  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p100"></a>In Christ <a name="xii.ii-p100.1"></a>Jesus our Lord, with whom Thou art blessed, together with Thy all-holy, good, and quickening Spirit, now and always, and for ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p101"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p102"></a>Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p103"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p104"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p104.1"></a>XV.</span> Peace be to all. <a name="page_540"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 540</font></a> <a name="xii.ii-Page_540"></a>  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p105"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p106"></a>And to Thy spirit.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p107"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p108"></a>Let us bow our heads to the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p109"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p110"></a>To Thee, Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p111"></a>The Priest prays, saying<span><a name="xii.ii-p111.1"></a>:</span>—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p112"></a>O Sovereign giver of life, and provider of good things, who didst give to mankind the blessed hope of eternal life, our Lord <a name="xii.ii-p112.1"></a>Jesus Christ, count us worthy in holiness, and perfect this Thy divine service to the enjoyment of future blessedness.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p113"></a>(Aloud.)  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p114"></a>So that, guarded by Thy power at all times, and led into the light of truth, we may send up the praise and the thanksgiving to Thee, the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, now and ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p115"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p116"></a>Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p117"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p118"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p118.1"></a>XVI. </span>Let none remain of the catechumens, none of the unbaptized, none of those who are unable to join with us in prayer. Look at one another. <a name="fr_4097"></a><a href="#fn_4097"><font size="1">4098</font></a>
  The door.   </p>  
 <p><a name="xii.ii-p120"></a>All erect: <a name="fr_4098"></a><a href="#fn_4098"><font size="1">4099</font></a>
  let us again pray to the Lord.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="xii.ii-p121.1"></a>II <a name="fr_4099"></a><a href="#fn_4099"><font size="1">4100</font></a>
 </h5>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p123"></a>The Priest says the prayer of incense.   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p124"></a>Sovereign Almighty, King of Glory, who knowest all things before their creation, manifest Thyself to us calling upon Thee at this holy hour, and redeem us from the shame of our transgressions; cleanse our mind and our thoughts from impure desires, from worldly deceit, from all influence of the devil; and accept from the hands of us sinners this incense, as Thou didst accept the offering of <a name="xii.ii-p124.1"></a>Abel, and <a name="xii.ii-p124.2"></a>Noah, and <a name="xii.ii-p124.3"></a>Aaron, and <a name="xii.ii-p124.4"></a>Samuel, and of all Thy saints, guarding us from everything evil, and preserving us for continually pleasing, and worshipping, and glorifying Thee, the Father, and Thy only-begotten Son, and Thy all-holy Spirit, now and always, and for ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p125"></a>And the Readers begin the Cherubic Hymn.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p126"></a>Let all mortal flesh be silent, and stand with fear and trembling, and meditate nothing earthly within itself:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p127"></a>For the King of kings and Lord of lords, Christ our God, comes forward to be sacrificed, and to be given for food to the faithful; and the bands of angels go before Him with every power and dominion, the many-eyed cherubim, and the six-winged seraphim, covering their faces, and crying aloud the hymn, Alleluia, Alleluia, Alleluia.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p128"></a>The Priest, bringing in the holy gifts, <a name="fr_4100"></a><a href="#fn_4100"><font size="1">4101</font></a>
  says this prayer:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p130"></a>XVII. O God, our God, who didst send forth the heavenly bread, the food of the whole world, our Lord <a name="xii.ii-p130.1"></a>Jesus Christ, to be a Saviour, and Redeemer, and Benefactor, blessing and sanctifying us, do Thou Thyself bless this offering, and graciously receive it to Thy altar above the skies:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p131"></a>Remember in Thy goodness and love those who have brought it, and those for whom they have brought it, and preserve us without condemnation in the service of Thy divine mysteries: for hollowed and glorified is Thy all-honoured and great name, Father, and Son, and Holy Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p132"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p133"></a>Peace be to all.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p134"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p135"></a>Sir, pronounce the blessing.  </p>

 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p420"></a>The People (twelve times).  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p421"></a>Lord, have mercy. <a name="fr_4133"></a><a href="#fn_4133"><font size="1">4134</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p423"></a>Then the Priest breaks the bread, and holds the half in his right hand, and the half in his left, and dips that in his right hand in the chalice, saying:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p424"></a>The union of the all-holy body and precious blood of our Lord and God and Saviour, <a name="xii.ii-p424.1"></a>Jesus Christ.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p425"></a>Then he makes the sign of the cross on that in his left hand: then with that which has been signed the other half: then forthwith he begins to divide, and before all to give to each chalice a single piece, saying:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p426"></a>It has been made one, and sanctified, and perfected, in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit, now and ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p427"></a>And when he makes the sign of the cross on the bread, he says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p428"></a>Behold the Lamb of God, the Son of the Father, that taketh away the sin of the world, sacrificed for the life and salvation of the world.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p429"></a>And when he gives a single piece to each chalice he says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p430"></a>A holy portion of Christ, full of grace and truth, of the Father, and of the Holy Spirit, to whom be the glory and the power to all eternity.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p431"></a>Then he begins to divide, and to say:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p432"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p432.1"></a>XLII. </span>The Lord is my Shepherd, I shall not want. In green pastures, <span><a name="xii.ii-p432.2"></a>and so on.</span> <a name="fr_4134"></a><a href="#fn_4134"><font size="1">4135</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p434"></a>Then,   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p435"></a>I will bless the Lord at all times, <span><a name="xii.ii-p435.1"></a>and so on.</span> <a name="fr_4135"></a><a href="#fn_4135"><font size="1">4136</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p437"></a>Then,   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p438"></a>I will extol Thee, my God, O King, <span><a name="xii.ii-p438.1"></a>and so on</span>. <a name="fr_4136"></a><a href="#fn_4136"><font size="1">4137</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p440"></a>Then,  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p441"></a>O praise the Lord, all ye nations, <span><a name="xii.ii-p441.1"></a>and so on</span>. <a name="fr_4137"></a><a href="#fn_4137"><font size="1">4138</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p443"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p444"></a>Sir, pronounce the blessing.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p445"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p446"></a>The Lord will bless us, and keep us without condemnation for the communion of His pure gifts, now and always, and for ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p447"></a>And when they have filled, <a name="fr_4138"></a><a href="#fn_4138"><font size="1">4139</font></a>
  the Deacon says:—  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p449"></a>Sir, pronounce the blessing.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p450"></a>The Priest says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p451"></a>The Lord will bless us, and make us worthy with the pure touchings of our fingers to take the live coal, and place it upon the mouths of the faithful for the purification and renewal of their souls and bodies, now and always.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p452"></a>Then,   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p453"></a>O taste and see that the Lord is good; who is parted and not divided; distributed to the faithful and not expended; for the remission of sins, and the life everlasting; now and always, and for ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p454"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p455"></a>In the peace of Christ, let us sing:  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p456"></a>The Singers.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p457"></a>O taste and see that the Lord is good.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p458"></a>The Priest says the prayer before the communion.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p459"></a>O Lord our God, the heavenly bread, the life of the universe, I have sinned against Heaven, and before Thee, and am not worthy to partake of Thy pure mysteries; but as a merciful God, make me worthy by Thy grace, without condemnation to partake of Thy holy body and precious blood, for the remission of sins, and life everlasting. <a name="fr_4139"></a><a href="#fn_4139"><font size="1">4140</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p461"></a>XLIII. Then he distributes to the clergy; and when the deacons take the disks <a name="fr_4140"></a><a href="#fn_4140"><font size="1">4141</font></a>
  and the chalices for distribution to the people, the Deacon, who takes the first disk, says:—   </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p463"></a>Sir, pronounce the blessing.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p464"></a>The Priest replies:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p465"></a>Glory to God who has sanctified and is sanctifying us all.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p466"></a>The Deacon says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p467"></a>Be Thou exalted, O God, over the heavens, and Thy glory over all the earth, and Thy kingdom endureth to all eternity. <a name="fr_4141"></a><a href="#fn_4141"><font size="1">4142</font></a>
 <a name="page_549"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 549</font></a> <a name="xii.ii-Page_549"></a>  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p469"></a>And when the Deacon is about to put it on the side-table <a name="fr_4142"></a><a href="#fn_4142"><font size="1">4143</font></a>
  the Priest says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p471"></a>Blessed be the name of the Lord our God for ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p472"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p473"></a>In the fear of God, and in faith and love, draw nigh.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p474"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p475"></a>Blessed is He that cometh in the name of the Lord. <a name="fr_4143"></a><a href="#fn_4143"><font size="1">4144</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p477"></a>And again, when he sets down the disk upon the side-table, he says:—  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p478"></a>Sir, pronounce the blessing.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p479"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p480"></a>Save Thy people, O God, and bless Thine inheritance.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p481"></a>The Priest again. <a name="fr_4144"></a><a href="#fn_4144"><font size="1">4145</font></a>
    </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p483"></a>Glory to our God, who has sanctified us all.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p484"></a>And when he has put the chalice back on the holy table, the Priest says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p485"></a>Blessed be the name of the Lord to all eternity.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p486"></a>The Deacons and the People say:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p487"></a>Fill our mouths with Thy praise, O Lord, and fill our lips with joy, that we may sing of Thy glory, of Thy greatness all the day.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p488"></a>And again:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p489"></a>We render thanks to Thee, Christ our God, that Thou hast made us worthy to partake of Thy body and blood, for the remission of sins, and for life everlasting. Do Thou, in Thy goodness and love, keep us, we pray Thee, without condemnation.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p490"></a>The prayer of incense at the last entrance.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p491"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p491.1"></a>XLIV. </span>We render thanks to Thee, the Saviour and God of all, for all the good things Thou hast given us, and for the participation of Thy holy and pure mysteries, and we offer to Thee this incense, praying: Keep us under the shadow of Thy wings, and count us worthy till our last breath to partake of Thy holy rites for the sanctification of our souls and bodies, for the inheritance of the kingdom of heaven: for Thou, O God, art our sanctification, and we send up praise and thanksgiving to Thee, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p492"></a>The Deacon begins in the entrance.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p493"></a>Glory to Thee, glory to Thee, glory to Thee, O Christ the King, only-begotten Word of the Father, that Thou hast counted us, Thy sinful and unworthy servants, worthy to enjoy thy pure mysteries for the remission of sins, and for life everlasting: glory to Thee. <a name="fr_4145"></a><a href="#fn_4145"><font size="1">4146</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p495"></a>And when he has made the entrance, the Deacon begins to speak thus:—  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p496"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p496.1"></a>XLV. </span>Again and again, and at all times, in peace, let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p497"></a>That the participation of His Holy rites may be to us for the turning away from every wicked thing, for our support on the journey to life everlasting, for the communion and gift of the Holy Spirit;  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p498"></a>Let us pray.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p499"></a>The Priest prays.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p500"></a>Commemorating our all-holy, pure, most glorious, blessed Lady, the God-Mother and Ever-Virgin Mary, <a name="fr_4146"></a><a href="#fn_4146"><font size="1">4147</font></a>
  and all the saints that have been well-pleasing to Thee since the world began, let us devote ourselves, and one another, and our whole life, to Christ our God:  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p502"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p503"></a>To Thee, O Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p504"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p505"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p505.1"></a>XLVI. </span>O God, who through Thy great and unspeakable love didst condescend to the weakness of Thy servants, and hast counted us worthy to partake of this heavenly table, condemn not us sinners for the participation of Thy pure mysteries; but keep us, O good One, in the sanctification of Thy Holy Spirit, that being made holy, we may find part and inheritance with all Thy saints that have been well-pleasing to Thee since the world began, in the light of Thy countenance, through the mercy of Thy only-begotten Son, our Lord and God and Saviour <a name="xii.ii-p505.2"></a>Jesus Christ, with whom Thou art blessed, together with Thy all-holy, and good, and quickening Spirit: for blessed and glorified is Thy all-precious and glorious name, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p506"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p507"></a>Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p508"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p509"></a>Peace be to all.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p510"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p511"></a>And to thy spirit.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p512"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p513"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p513.1"></a>XLVII. </span>Let us bow our heads to the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p514"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p515"></a>O God, great and marvellous, look upon Thy servants, for we have bowed our heads to Thee. Stretch forth Thy hand, strong and full of blessings, <a name="page_550"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 550</font></a> <a name="xii.ii-Page_550"></a>and bless Thy people. Keep Thine inheritance, that always and at all times we may glorify Thee, our only living and true God, the holy and consubstantial <a name="fr_4147"></a><a href="#fn_4147"><font size="1">4148</font></a>
  Trinity, Father, Son, and Holy Ghost, now and ever, and to all eternity.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p517"></a> (Aloud.)   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p518"></a>For unto Thee is becoming and is due praise from us all, and honour, and adoration, and thanksgiving, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, now and ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p519"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="Response"><a name="xii.ii-p520"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p520.1"></a>XLVIII. </span>In the peace of Christ let us sing:  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p521"></a>And again he says:—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p522"></a>In the peace of Christ let us go on:  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p523"></a>The People.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p524"></a>In the name of the Lord. Sir, pronounce the blessing. <a name="fr_4148"></a><a href="#fn_4148"><font size="1">4149</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p526"></a>Dismission prayer, spoken by the Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p527"></a>Going on from glory to glory, we praise Thee, the Saviour of our souls. Glory to Father, and Son, and Holy Spirit now and ever, and to all eternity. We praise Thee, the Saviour of our souls.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p528"></a>The Priest says a prayer from the altar to the sacristy.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p529"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p529.1"></a>XLIX. </span>Going on from strength to strength, and having fulfilled all the divine service in Thy temple, even now we beseech Thee, O Lord our God, make us worthy of perfect loving-kindness; make straight our path: root us in Thy fear, and make us worthy of the heavenly kingdom, in Christ <a name="xii.ii-p529.2"></a>Jesus our Lord, with whom Thou art blessed, together with Thy all-holy, and good, and quickening Spirit, now and always, and for ever.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p530"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p531"></a><span><a name="xii.ii-p531.1"></a>L. </span>Again and again, and at all times, in peace let us beseech the Lord.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p532"></a>Prayer said in the sacristy after the dismissal.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p533"></a>Thou hast given unto us, O Lord, sanctification in the communion of the all-holy body and precious blood of Thy only-begotten Son, our Lord <a name="xii.ii-p533.1"></a>Jesus Christ; give unto us also the grace of Thy good Spirit, and keep us blameless in the faith, lead us unto perfect adoption and redemption, and to the coming joys of eternity; for Thou art our sanctification and light, O God, and Thy only-begotten Son, and Thy all-holy Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p534"></a>The Deacon.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p535"></a>In the peace of Christ let us keep watch.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p536"></a>The Priest.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p537"></a>Blessed is God, who blesseth and sanctifieth through the communion of the holy, and quickening, and pure mysteries, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.  </p>  
 <p class="Heading7"><a name="xii.ii-p538"></a>Then the prayer of propitiation.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p539"></a>O Lord <a name="xii.ii-p539.1"></a>Jesus Christ, Son of the living God, Lamb and Shepherd, who takest away the sin of the world, who didst freely forgive their debt to the two debtors, and gavest remission of her sins to the woman that was a sinner, who gavest healing to the paralytic, with the remission of his sins; forgive, remit, pardon, O God, our offences, voluntary and involuntary, in knowledge and in ignorance, by transgression and by disobedience, which Thy all-holy Spirit knows better than Thy servants do:  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p540"></a>And if men, carnal and dwelling in this world, have in aught erred from Thy commandments, either moved by the devil, whether in word or in deed, or if they have come under a curse, or by reason of some special vow, I entreat and beseech Thy unspeakable loving-kindness, that they may be set free from their word, and released from the oath and the special vow, according to Thy goodness.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="xii.ii-p541"></a>Verily, O Sovereign Lord, hear my supplication on behalf of Thy servants, and do Thou pass by all their errors, remembering them no more; forgive them every transgression, voluntary and involuntary; deliver them from everlasting punishment: for Thou art He that hast commanded us, saying, Whatsoever things ye bind upon earth, shall be bound in heaven; and whatsoever things ye loose upon earth, shall be loosed in heaven: for, thou art our God, a God able to pity, and to save and to forgive sins; and glory is due unto Thee, with the eternal Father, and the quickening Spirit, now and ever, and to all eternity. Amen.   </p>
