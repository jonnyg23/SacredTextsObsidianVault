 <p> <a name="viii.iii.xii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="viii.iii.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII. <a name="fr_2479"></a><a href="#fn_2479"><font size="1">2480</font></a>
 —Reception of Christians.</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="viii.iii.xii-p2"></a>1. But let every one that cometh in the name of the Lord be received, <a name="fr_2480"></a><a href="#fn_2480"><font size="1">2481</font></a>
  and afterward ye shall prove and know him; for ye shall have understanding right and left. 2. If he who cometh is a wayfarer, assist him as far as ye are able; but he shall not remain with you, except for two or three days, if need be. 3. But if he willeth to abide with you, being an artisan, let him work and eat; <a name="fr_2481"></a><a href="#fn_2481"><font size="1">2482</font></a>
  but if he hath no trade, 4. according to your understanding see to it that, as a Christian, <a name="fr_2482"></a><a href="#fn_2482"><font size="1">2483</font></a>
  he shall not live with you idle. 5. But if he willeth not to do, he is a Christ-monger. <a name="fr_2483"></a><a href="#fn_2483"><font size="1">2484</font></a>
  Watch that ye keep aloof from such.  </p>
