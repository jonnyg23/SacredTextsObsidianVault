 <p> <a name="iii.v.xxxv"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iii.v.xxxv-p0.1"></a>Chap. XXXV.</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.xxxv-p1"></a>This edict was promulgated at Nicomedia on the day preceding the kalends of May, <a name="fr_1986"></a><a href="#fn_1986"><font size="1">1987</font></a>
  in the eighth consulship of <a name="iii.v.xxxv-p2.1"></a>Galerius, and the second of <a name="iii.v.xxxv-p2.2"></a>Maximin Daia. Then the prison-gates having been thrown open, you, my best beloved <a name="iii.v.xxxv-p2.3"></a>Donatus, <a name="fr_1987"></a><a href="#fn_1987"><font size="1">1988</font></a>
  together with the other confessors for the faith, were set at liberty from a jail, which had been your residence for six years. <a name="iii.v.xxxv-p3.5"></a>Galerius, however, did not, by publication of this edict, obtain the divine forgiveness. In a few days after he was consumed by the horrible disease that had brought on an universal putrefaction. Dying, he recommended his wife and son to <a name="iii.v.xxxv-p3.6"></a>Licinius, and delivered them over into his hands. This event was known at Nicomedia before the end of the month. <a name="fr_1988"></a><a href="#fn_1988"><font size="1">1989</font></a>
  His vicennial anniversary was to have been celebrated on the ensuing kalends of March. <a name="fr_1989"></a><a href="#fn_1989"><font size="1">1990</font></a>
    </p>
