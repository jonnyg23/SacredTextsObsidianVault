 <p> <a name="viii.iii.i"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="viii.iii.i-p0.1"></a>Chapter I.—The Two Ways; The First Commandment.</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="viii.iii.i-p1"></a>1. <span class="sc"><a name="viii.iii.i-p1.1"></a>There</span> are two ways, <a name="fr_2369"></a><a href="#fn_2369"><font size="1">2370</font></a>
  one of life and one of death; <a name="fr_2370"></a><a href="#fn_2370"><font size="1">2371</font></a>
  but a great difference between the two ways. 2. The way of life, then, is this: First, thou shalt love God <a name="fr_2371"></a><a href="#fn_2371"><font size="1">2372</font></a>
  who made thee; second, thy neighbour as thyself; <a name="fr_2372"></a><a href="#fn_2372"><font size="1">2373</font></a>
  and all things whatsoever thou wouldst should not occur to thee, thou also to another do not do. <a name="fr_2373"></a><a href="#fn_2373"><font size="1">2374</font></a>
  3. And of these sayings <a name="fr_2374"></a><a href="#fn_2374"><font size="1">2375</font></a>
  the teaching is this: Bless them that curse you, and pray for your enemies, and fast for them that persecute you. <a name="fr_2375"></a><a href="#fn_2375"><font size="1">2376</font></a>
  For what thank <span><a name="viii.iii.i-p8.4"></a>is there</span>, if ye love them that love you? Do not also the Gentiles do the same? <a name="fr_2376"></a><a href="#fn_2376"><font size="1">2377</font></a>
  But do ye love them that hate you; and ye shall not have an enemy. <a name="fr_2377"></a><a href="#fn_2377"><font size="1">2378</font></a>
  4. Abstain thou from fleshly and worldly lusts. <a name="fr_2378"></a><a href="#fn_2378"><font size="1">2379</font></a>
  If one give thee a blow upon thy right cheek, turn to him the other also; <a name="fr_2379"></a><a href="#fn_2379"><font size="1">2380</font></a>
  and thou shalt be perfect. If one impress thee for one mile, go with him two. <a name="fr_2380"></a><a href="#fn_2380"><font size="1">2381</font></a>
  If one take away thy cloak, give him also thy coat. <a name="fr_2381"></a><a href="#fn_2381"><font size="1">2382</font></a>
  If one take from thee thine own, ask it not back, <a name="fr_2382"></a><a href="#fn_2382"><font size="1">2383</font></a>
  for indeed thou art not able. 5. Give to every one that asketh thee, and ask it not back; <a name="fr_2383"></a><a href="#fn_2383"><font size="1">2384</font></a>
  for the Father willeth that to all should be given of our own blessings (free gifts). <a name="fr_2384"></a><a href="#fn_2384"><font size="1">2385</font></a>
  Happy <span><a name="viii.iii.i-p17.6"></a>is</span> he that giveth according to the commandment; for he is guiltless. Woe to him that receiveth; for if one having need receiveth, he is guiltless; but he <span><a name="viii.iii.i-p17.7"></a>that receiveth</span> not having need, shall pay the penalty, why he received and for what, and, coming into straits (confinement), <a name="fr_2385"></a><a href="#fn_2385"><font size="1">2386</font></a>
  he shall be examined concerning the things which he hath done, and he shall not escape thence until he pay back the last farthing. <a name="fr_2386"></a><a href="#fn_2386"><font size="1">2387</font></a>
  6. But also now concerning this, it hath been said, Let thine alms sweat <a name="fr_2387"></a><a href="#fn_2387"><font size="1">2388</font></a>
  in thy hands, until thou know to whom thou shouldst give.  </p>
