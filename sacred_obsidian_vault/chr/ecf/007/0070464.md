 <p> <a name="ix.viii.v"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.v-p0.1"></a>Sec. V.—Daily Prayers.</h4>  <h5 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.v-p0.2"></a>A Morning Prayer.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="ix.viii.v-p1"></a><span class="sc"><a name="ix.viii.v-p1.1"></a>XLVII</span>. “Glory be to God in the highest, and upon earth peace, good-will among men.” <a name="fr_3543"></a><a href="#fn_3543"><font size="1">3544</font></a>
  We praise Thee, we sing hymns to Thee, we bless Thee; we glorify Thee, we worship Thee by Thy great High Priest; Thee who art the true God, who art the One Unbegotten, the only inaccessible Being. For Thy great glory, O Lord and heavenly King, O God the Father Almighty, O Lord God, <a name="fr_3544"></a><a href="#fn_3544"><font size="1">3545</font></a>
  the Father of Christ the immaculate Lamb, who taketh away the sin of the world, receive our prayer, Thou that sittest upon the cherubim. For Thou only art holy, Thou only art the Lord <a name="ix.viii.v-p3.3"></a>Jesus, the Christ of the God of all created nature, and our King, by whom glory, honour, and worship be to Thee.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.v-p3.4"></a>An Evening Prayer.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="ix.viii.v-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="ix.viii.v-p4.1"></a>XLVIII</span>. “Ye children, praise the Lord: praise the name of the Lord.” <a name="fr_3545"></a><a href="#fn_3545"><font size="1">3546</font></a>
  We praise Thee, we sing hymns to Thee, we bless Thee for Thy great glory, O Lord our King, the Father of Christ the immaculate Lamb, who taketh away the sin of the world. Praise becomes Thee, hymns become Thee, glory becomes Thee, the God and Father, <a name="fr_3546"></a><a href="#fn_3546"><font size="1">3547</font></a>
  through the Son, in the most holy Spirit, for ever and ever. Amen. “Now, O Lord, lettest Thou Thy servant depart in peace, according to Thy word; for mine eyes have seen Thy salvation, which Thou hast prepared before the face of all people, a light for the revelation to the Gentiles, and the glory of Thy people Israel.” <a name="fr_3547"></a><a href="#fn_3547"><font size="1">3548</font></a>
   </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.v-p7.2"></a>A Prayer at Dinner.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="ix.viii.v-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="ix.viii.v-p8.1"></a>XLIX</span>. Thou art blessed, O Lord, who nourishest me from my youth, who givest food to all flesh. Fill our hearts with joy and gladness, that having always what is sufficient for us, we may abound to every good work, in Christ <a name="ix.viii.v-p8.2"></a>Jesus our Lord, through whom <a name="fr_3548"></a><a href="#fn_3548"><font size="1">3549</font></a>
  glory, honour, and power be to Thee for ever. Amen.  </p>  <h4 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.v-p9.2"></a>General notice</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="ix.viii.v-p10"></a><span class="sc"><a name="ix.viii.v-p10.1"></a>Comparing</span> the <a name="ix.viii.v-p10.2"></a>Teaching with chapters xxv. and xxvi. of these <a name="ix.viii.v-p10.3"></a>Constitutions, it seems to me that the nature of the eucharistic (thanksgiving) prayers becomes apparent. They presuppose the formulas to be found in the eighth book of the <a name="ix.viii.v-p10.4"></a>Constitutions, <a name="fr_3549"></a><a href="#fn_3549"><font size="1">3550</font></a>
  and are such instructions as were imparted only to <span><a name="ix.viii.v-p11.3"></a>catechumens</span>; the part peculiar to presbyters being withheld, of course, as <span><a name="ix.viii.v-p11.4"></a>esoteric</span> mysteries, until further knowledge was canonically appropriate. See Elucidation IV. vol. vi. p. 236; and in this volume, Elucidation I. p. 382. The Bryennios <span><a name="ix.viii.v-p11.7"></a>ms. </span>is cleared from nearly all difficulties by Dr. <a name="ix.viii.v-p11.8"></a>Riddles lucid notes, when compared with corresponding passages in the <a name="ix.viii.v-p11.9"></a>Constitutions, or illustrated by such as are supplementary.   </p>
