 <p class="c39"><a name="xx.ii-p2"></a><span class="c40"><a name="xx.ii-p2.1"></a>Book II. Chap. 67,</span></p>   
 <p class="c41"><a name="xx.ii-p3"></a><span class="c11"><a name="xx.ii-p3.1"></a>On the Following Treatise,</span></p>   
 <p class="c39"><a name="xx.ii-p4"></a><span class="c7"><a name="xx.ii-p4.1"></a>“de correptione et gratia.”</span></p>   
 <p class="c42"><a name="xx.ii-p5"></a>————————————</p>   
 <p class="c27"><a name="xx.ii-p6"></a><span class="c16"><a name="xx.ii-p6.1"></a>I Wrote</span> again to the same persons <a name="fr_3234"></a><a href="#fn_3234"><font size="1">3235</font></a>
  another treatise, which I entitled <i>On Rebuke and Grace</i>, because I had been told that some one there had said that no man ought to be rebuked for not doing Gods commandments, but that prayer only should be made on his behalf, that he may do them. This book begins on this wise, “I have read your letters, dearly beloved brother Valentine.”</p>
