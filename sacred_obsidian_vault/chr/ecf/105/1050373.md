 </p><p class="c46"><a name="xiii.xli-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xiii.xli-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVI.—(37.) The Sixth Passage.</span></p>   
 <p><a name="xiii.xli-p2"></a>He has also adduced this passage of Scripture, which is very commonly quoted against his party: “For there is not a just man upon earth, that doeth good, and sinneth not.” <a name="fr_1541"></a><a href="#fn_1541"><font size="1">1542</font></a>
  And he makes a pretence of answering it by other passages,—how, “the Lord says concerning holy Job, Hast thou considered my servant Job? For there is none like him upon earth, a man who is blameless, true, a worshipper of God, and abstaining from every evil thing.” <a name="fr_1542"></a><a href="#fn_1542"><font size="1">1543</font></a>
  On this passage we have already made some remarks. <a name="fr_1543"></a><a href="#fn_1543"><font size="1">1544</font></a>
  But he has not even attempted to show us how, on the one <a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a> <a name="xiii.xli-Page_173"></a>hand, Job was absolutely sinless upon earth,—if the words are to bear such a sense; and, on the other hand, how that can be true which he has admitted to be in the Scripture, “There is not a just man upon earth, that doeth good, and sinneth not.” <a name="fr_1544"></a><a href="#fn_1544"><font size="1">1545</font></a>
 </p>
