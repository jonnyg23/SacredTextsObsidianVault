 </p><p class="c46"><a name="xiv.xlviii-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xiv.xlviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 44 [XX.]—The Acquittal of Pelagius.</span></p>   
 <p><a name="xiv.xlviii-p2"></a>The synod said: “Now since we have received satisfaction on the points which have come before us touching the monk Pelagius, who has been present; since, too, he gives his consent to the pious doctrines, and even anathematizes everything that is contrary to the Churchs faith, we confess him to belong to the communion of the Catholic Church.”</p>
