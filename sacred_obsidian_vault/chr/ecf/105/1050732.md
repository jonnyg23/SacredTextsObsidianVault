 </p><p class="c43"><a name="xvii.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c25"><a name="xvii.vi-p1.1"></a>Book III.</span></p>   
 <p class="c41"><a name="xvii.vi-p2"></a><span class="c9"><a name="xvii.vi-p2.1"></a>Addressed to Vincentius Victor.</span></p>   
 <p class="c36"><a name="xvii.vi-p3"></a>Augustin points out to Vincentius Victor the corrections which he ought to make in his books concerning the origin of the soul, if he wishes to be a Catholic. Those opinions also which had been already refuted in the preceding books addressed to Renatus and Peter, Augustin briefly censures in this third book, which is written to Victor himself: moreover, he classifies them under eleven heads of error.</p>
