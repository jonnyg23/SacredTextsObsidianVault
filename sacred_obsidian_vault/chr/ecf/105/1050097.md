 </p><p class="c46"><a name="x.iv.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="x.iv.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 8 [VII.]—(2) Whether There is in This World a Man Without Sin.</span></p>  
 <p><a name="x.iv.viii-p2"></a>[<i>2nd.</i>] If, however, I am asked the second question which I have suggested,—whether there be a sinless man,—I believe there is not. For I rather believe the Scripture, which says: “Enter not into judgment with Thy servant; for in Thy sight shall no man living be justified.” <a name="fr_480"></a><a href="#fn_480"><font size="1">481</font></a>
  There is therefore need of the mercy of God, which “exceedingly rejoiceth against judgment,” <a name="fr_481"></a><a href="#fn_481"><font size="1">482</font></a>
  and which that man shall not obtain who does not show mercy. <a name="fr_482"></a><a href="#fn_482"><font size="1">483</font></a>
  And whereas the prophet says, “I said, I will confess my transgressions unto the Lord, and Thou forgavest the iniquity of my heart,” <a name="fr_483"></a><a href="#fn_483"><font size="1">484</font></a>
  he yet immediately adds, “For this shall every saint pray unto Thee in an acceptable time.” <a name="fr_484"></a><a href="#fn_484"><font size="1">485</font></a>
  Not indeed every sinner, but “every saint;” for it is the voice of saints which says, “If we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves, and the truth is not in us.” <a name="fr_485"></a><a href="#fn_485"><font size="1">486</font></a>
  Accordingly we read, in the Apocalypse of the same Apostle, of “the hundred and forty and four thousand” saints, “which were not defiled with women; for they continued virgins: and in their mouth was found no guile; for they are without fault.” <a name="fr_486"></a><a href="#fn_486"><font size="1">487</font></a>
  “Without fault,” indeed, they no doubt are for this reason,—because they truly found fault with themselves; and for this reason, “in their mouth was discovered no guile,”—“because if they said they had no sin, they deceived themselves, and the truth was not in them.” <a name="fr_487"></a><a href="#fn_487"><font size="1">488</font></a>
  Of course, where the truth was not, there would be guile; and when a righteous man begins a statement by accusing himself, he verily utters no falsehood.</p>
