 </p><p class="c46"><a name="x.iv.xviii-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="x.iv.xviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 18 [XIII.]—Perfect Human Righteousness is Imperfect.</span></p>  
 <p><a name="x.iv.xviii-p2"></a>There are then on earth righteous men, there are great men, brave, prudent, chaste, patient, <a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 52</font></a> <a name="x.iv.xviii-Page_52"></a>pious, merciful, who endure all kinds of temporal evil with an even mind for righteousness sake. If, however, there is truth—nay, because there is truth—in these words, “If we say we have no sin, we deceive ourselves,” <a name="fr_532"></a><a href="#fn_532"><font size="1">533</font></a>
  and in these, “In Thy sight shall no man living be justified,” they are not without sin; nor is there one among them so proud and foolish as not to think that the Lords Prayer is needful to him, by reason of his manifold sins.</p>
