 <p> <a name="xx.v"></a>   
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="xx.v-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xx.v-p1.1"></a>Chapter 2.—The Catholic Faith Concerning Law, Grace, and Free Will.</span></p>   
 <p><a name="xx.v-p2"></a>Now the Lord Himself not only shows us what evil we should shun, and what good we should do, which is all that the letter of the law is able to effect; but He moreover helps us that we may shun evil and do good, <a name="fr_3238"></a><a href="#fn_3238"><font size="1">3239</font></a>
  which none can do without the Spirit of grace; and if this be wanting, the law comes in merely to make us guilty and to slay us. It is on this account that the apostle says, “The letter killeth, but the Spirit giveth life.” <a name="fr_3239"></a><a href="#fn_3239"><font size="1">3240</font></a>
  He, then, who lawfully uses the law learns therein evil and good, and, not trusting in his own strength, flees to grace, by the help of which he may shun evil and do good. But who is there who flees to grace except when “the steps of a man are ordered by the Lord, and He shall determine his way”? <a name="fr_3240"></a><a href="#fn_3240"><font size="1">3241</font></a>
  And thus also to desire the help of grace is the beginning of grace; of which, says he, “And I said, Now I have begun; this is the change of the right hand of the Most High.” <a name="fr_3241"></a><a href="#fn_3241"><font size="1">3242</font></a>
  It is to be confessed, therefore, that we have free choice to do both evil and good; but in doing evil every one is free from righteousness and a servant of sin, while in doing good no one can be free, unless he have been made free by Him who said, “If the Son shall make you free, then you shall be free indeed.” <a name="fr_3242"></a><a href="#fn_3242"><font size="1">3243</font></a>
  Neither is it thus, that when any one has been made free from the dominion of sin, he no longer needs the help of his Deliverer; but rather thus, that hearing from Him, “Without me ye can do nothing,” <a name="fr_3243"></a><a href="#fn_3243"><font size="1">3244</font></a>
  he himself also says to Him, “Be thou my helper! Forsake me not.” <a name="fr_3244"></a><a href="#fn_3244"><font size="1">3245</font></a>
  I rejoice that I have found in our brother Florus also this faith, which without doubt is the true and prophetical and apostolical and catholic faith; whence those are the rather to be corrected—whom indeed I now think to have been corrected by the favour of God—who did not understand him.</p>
