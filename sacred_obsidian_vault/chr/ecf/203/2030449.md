 <p> <a name="v.iii.lxviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lxviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXVI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxviii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxviii-p2.1"></a>Cornelius</span>, <a name="fr_2479"></a><a href="#fn_2479"><font size="1">2480</font></a>
  bishop of Rome, to whom eight letters of Cyprian are extant, wrote a letter to Fabius, <a name="fr_2480"></a><a href="#fn_2480"><font size="1">2481</font></a>
  bishop of the church at Antioch, <i>On the Roman, Italian, and African councils,</i> and another <i>On Novatian and those who had fallen from the faith,</i> a third <i>On the acts of the council,</i> and a fourth very prolix one to the same Fabius, containing the causes of the Novatian heresy and an anathema of it. He ruled the church for two years under Gallus and Volusianus. He received the crown of martyrdom for Christ, and was succeeded by Lucius.</p>
