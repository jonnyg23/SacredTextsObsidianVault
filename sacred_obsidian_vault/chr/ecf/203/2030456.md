 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lxxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXIII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxxv-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxv-p2.1"></a>Anatolius <a name="fr_2491"></a><a href="#fn_2491"><font size="1">2492</font></a>
 </span> of Alexandria, bishop of Laodicea in Syria, who flourished under the emperors Probus and Carus, was a man of wonderful learning in arithmetic, geometry, astronomy, grammar, rhetoric, and dialectic. We can get an idea of the greatness of his genius from the volume which he wrote <i>On the passover</i> and his ten books <i>On the institutes of arithmetic.</i></p>
