 <p> <a name="v.iii.i"></a>   <a name="page_359"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 359</font></a> <a name="v.iii.i-Page_359"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="v.iii.i-p1"></a><span class="c26"><a name="v.iii.i-p1.1"></a>II. Jerome.</span></p>  
 <p class="c27"><a name="v.iii.i-p2"></a><span class="c54"><a name="v.iii.i-p2.1"></a>Lives of Illustrious Men.</span></p>  
 <p class="c45"><a name="v.iii.i-p3"></a>————————————</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="v.iii.i-p4"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.i-p4.1"></a>Preface.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.i-p5"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.i-p5.1"></a>You</span> have urged me, Dexter, <a name="fr_2319"></a><a href="#fn_2319"><font size="1">2320</font></a>
  to follow the example of Tranquillus <a name="fr_2320"></a><a href="#fn_2320"><font size="1">2321</font></a>
  in giving a systematic account of ecclesiastical writers, and to do for our writers what he did for the illustrious men of letters among the Gentiles, namely, to briefly set before you all those who have published <a name="fr_2321"></a><a href="#fn_2321"><font size="1">2322</font></a>
  any memorable writing on the Holy Scriptures, from the time of our Lords passion until the fourteenth year of the Emperor Theodosius. <a name="fr_2322"></a><a href="#fn_2322"><font size="1">2323</font></a>
  A similar work has been done by Hermippus <a name="fr_2323"></a><a href="#fn_2323"><font size="1">2324</font></a>
  the peripatetic, Antigonus Carystius, <a name="fr_2324"></a><a href="#fn_2324"><font size="1">2325</font></a>
  the learned Satyrus, <a name="fr_2325"></a><a href="#fn_2325"><font size="1">2326</font></a>
  and most learned of all, Aristoxenus the Musician, <a name="fr_2326"></a><a href="#fn_2326"><font size="1">2327</font></a>
  among the Greeks, and among the Latins by Varro, <a name="fr_2327"></a><a href="#fn_2327"><font size="1">2328</font></a>
  Santra, <a name="fr_2328"></a><a href="#fn_2328"><font size="1">2329</font></a>
  Nepos, <a name="fr_2329"></a><a href="#fn_2329"><font size="1">2330</font></a>
  Hyginus, <a name="fr_2330"></a><a href="#fn_2330"><font size="1">2331</font></a>
  and by him through whose example you seek to stimulate <a name="fr_2331"></a><a href="#fn_2331"><font size="1">2332</font></a>
  us,—Tranquillus.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="v.iii.i-p19"></a>But their situation and mine is not the same, for they, opening the old histories and chronicles could as if gathering from some great meadow, weave some <a name="fr_2332"></a><a href="#fn_2332"><font size="1">2333</font></a>
  small crown at least for their work. As for me, what shall I do, who, having no predecessor, have, as the saying is, the worst possible master, namely myself, and yet I must acknowledge that Eusebius Pamphilus in the ten books of his Church History has been of the utmost assistance, and the works of various among those of whom we are to write, often testify to the dates of their authors. And so I pray the Lord Jesus, <a name="fr_2333"></a><a href="#fn_2333"><font size="1">2334</font></a>
  that what your Cicero, who stood at the summit of Roman eloquence, did not scorn to do, compiling in his <i>Brutus,</i> a catalogue of Latin orators, this I too may accomplish in the enumeration of ecclesiastical writers, and accomplish in a fashion worthy of the exhortation which you made. But if, perchance any of those who are yet writing have been overlooked by me in this volume, they ought to ascribe it to themselves, rather than to me, for among those whom I have not read, I could not, in the first place, know those who concealed their own writings, and, in the second place, what is perhaps well known to others, would be quite unknown to me in this out of the way corner of the earth. <a name="fr_2334"></a><a href="#fn_2334"><font size="1">2335</font></a>
  But surely when they are distinguished by their writings, they will not very greatly grieve over any loss in our non-mention of them. Let Celsus, Porphyry, and Julian learn, rabid as they are against Christ, let their followers, they who think the church has had no philosophers or orators or men of learning, learn how many and what sort of men founded, built and adorned it, and cease to accuse our faith of such rustic simplicity, and recognize rather their own ignorance.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="v.iii.i-p23"></a>In the name of the Lord Jesus Christ, farewell. <a name="fr_2335"></a><a href="#fn_2335"><font size="1">2336</font></a>
 </p>
