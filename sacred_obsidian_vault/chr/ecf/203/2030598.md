 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.lxxix-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxxix-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXVIII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.lxxix-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxxix-p2.1"></a>Victor, <a name="fr_2706"></a><a href="#fn_2706"><font size="1">2707</font></a>
 </span> bishop of Cartenna in Mauritania, wrote one long book against the Arians, which he sent to king Genseric by his followers, as I learned from the preface to the work, <a name="fr_2707"></a><a href="#fn_2707"><font size="1">2708</font></a>
  and a work <i>On the repentance of the publican,</i> <a name="fr_2708"></a><a href="#fn_2708"><font size="1">2709</font></a>
  in which he drew up a rule of life for the penitent, according to the authority of Scriptures. He also wrote a consolatory work to one Basilius, <i>On the death of a son,</i> filled with resurrection hope and good counsel. He also composed many Homilies, which have been arranged as continuous works and are as I know, made use of by brethren anxious for their own salvation.</p>
