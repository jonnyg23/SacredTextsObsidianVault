 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.xxxviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xxxviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXVII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.xxxviii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xxxviii-p2.1"></a>Simplicianus, <a name="fr_2633"></a><a href="#fn_2633"><font size="1">2634</font></a>
 </span> the bishop, exhorted Augustine then presbyter, in many letters, that he should exercise his genius and take time for exposition of the Scriptures that, as it were, a new Ambrosius, the task master of Origen might appear. Wherefore also he sent to him many examinations of scriptures. There is also an epistle of his of <i>Questions</i> in which he teaches by asking questions as if wishing to learn.</p>
