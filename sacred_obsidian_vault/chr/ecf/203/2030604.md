 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.lxxxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxxxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXXIV.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.lxxxv-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxxxv-p2.1"></a>Claudianus, <a name="fr_2719"></a><a href="#fn_2719"><font size="1">2720</font></a>
 </span> presbyter of the church at Vienne, a master speaker, and shrewd in argument, composed three books, <i>On the condition and substance of the soul,</i> in which he discusses how far anything is incorporeal excepting God.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="v.iv.lxxxv-p4"></a>[He wrote also some other things, among which are, <i>A Hymn on Our Lords Passion,</i> which begins “Pange lingua gloriosi.” He was moreover brother of Mamertus, bishop of Vienne.] <a name="fr_2720"></a><a href="#fn_2720"><font size="1">2721</font></a>
  (<i>See note</i>.)</p>
