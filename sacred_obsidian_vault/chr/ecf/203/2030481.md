 <p> <a name="v.iii.c"></a>
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.c-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.c-p1.1"></a>Chapter XCVIII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.c-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.c-p2.1"></a>Acacius</span>, <a name="fr_2524"></a><a href="#fn_2524"><font size="1">2525</font></a>
  who, because he was blind in one eye, they nicknamed “the one-eyed,” bishop of the church of Cæsarea in Palestine, wrote seventeen volumes <i>On Ecclesiastes</i> and six of <i>Miscellaneous questions,</i> and many treatises besides on various subjects. He was so influential in the reign of the emperor Constantius that he made Felix bishop of Rome in the place of Liberius.</p>
