 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.xc-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xc-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXXVIII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.xc-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xc-p2.1"></a>Anthony <a name="fr_2512"></a><a href="#fn_2512"><font size="1">2513</font></a>
 </span> the monk, whose life Athanasius bishop of Alexandria wrote a long work upon, sent seven letters in Coptic to various monasteries, letters truly apostolic in idea and language, and which have been translated into Greek. The chief of these is <i>To the Arsenoites.</i> He flourished during the reign of Constantinus and his sons.</p>
