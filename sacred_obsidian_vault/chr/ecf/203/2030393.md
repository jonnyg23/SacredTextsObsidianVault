 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.xii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xii-p1.1"></a>Chapter X.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.xii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xii-p2.1"></a>Hermas <a name="fr_2385"></a><a href="#fn_2385"><font size="1">2386</font></a>
 </span>  <a name="fr_2386"></a><a href="#fn_2386"><font size="1">2387</font></a>
  whom the apostle Paul mentions in writing to the Romans “Salute <a name="fr_2387"></a><a href="#fn_2387"><font size="1">2388</font></a>
  Phlegon, Hermes, Patrobas, Hermas <a name="fr_2388"></a><a href="#fn_2388"><font size="1">2389</font></a>
  and the brethren that are with them” <a name="fr_2389"></a><a href="#fn_2389"><font size="1">2390</font></a>
  is reputed to be the author of the book which is called <i>Pastor</i> and which is also read publicly in some churches of Greece. It is in fact a useful book and many of the ancient writers quote from it as authority, but among the Latins it is almost unknown.</p>

 <p class="endnote"><a name="v.iii.xii-p3"></a> The date of Hermas depends on what Hermas is supposed to be the author. He is supposed to be 1 the Hermas of the New Testament, or 2 the brother of Pius I (139–54) or 3 a still later Hermas. All these views have distinguished advocates, but this view of Jerome taken from Origen through Eusebius is not much accepted.</p>
