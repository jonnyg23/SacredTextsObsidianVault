 <p> <a name="v.iii.lxxxiii"></a>
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lxxxiii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxxiii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXXI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxxxiii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxxiii-p2.1"></a>Eusebius <a name="fr_2502"></a><a href="#fn_2502"><font size="1">2503</font></a>
 </span> bishop of Cæsarea in Palestine was diligent in the study of Divine Scriptures and with Pamphilus the martyr a most diligent investigator of the Holy Bible. He published a great number of volumes among which are the following: <i>Demonstrations of the Gospel</i> twenty books, <i>Preparations for the Gospel</i> fifteen books, <i>Theophany</i> <a name="fr_2503"></a><a href="#fn_2503"><font size="1">2504</font></a>
 five books, <i>Church history</i> ten books, <i>Chronicle of Universal history</i> and an <i>Epitome</i> of this last. Also <i>On discrepancies between the Gospels, On Isaiah,</i> ten books, also <i>Against Porphyry,</i> who was writing at that same time in Sicily as some think, twenty-five books, also one book of <i>Topics,</i> six books of <i>Apology for Origen,</i> three books <i>On the life of Pamphilus,</i> other brief works <i>On the martyrs,</i> exceedingly learned <i>Commentaries on one hundred and fifty Psalms,</i> and many others. He flourished chiefly in the reigns of Constantine the Great and Constantius. His surname Pamphilus arose from his friendship for Pamphilus the martyr.</p>
