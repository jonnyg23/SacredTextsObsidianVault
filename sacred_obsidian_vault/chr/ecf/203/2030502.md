 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.cxxi-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cxxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter CXIX.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.cxxi-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cxxi-p2.1"></a>Diodorus</span>, <a name="fr_2551"></a><a href="#fn_2551"><font size="1">2552</font></a>
  bishop of Tarsus enjoyed a great reputation while he was still presbyter of Antioch. Commentaries of his <i>On the epistles</i> are extant, as well as many other works in the manner of Eusebius the great of Emesa, whose meaning he has followed, <a name="page_383"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 383</font></a> <a name="v.iii.cxxi-Page_383"></a>but whose eloquence he could not imitate on account of his ignorance of secular literature.</p>
