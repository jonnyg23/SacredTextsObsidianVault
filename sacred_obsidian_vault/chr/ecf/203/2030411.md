 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.xxx-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xxx-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVIII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.xxx-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xxx-p2.1"></a>Pinytus <a name="fr_2427"></a><a href="#fn_2427"><font size="1">2428</font></a>
 </span> of Crete, bishop of the city of Gnosus, wrote to Dionysius bishop of the Corinthians, an exceedingly elegant letter in which he teaches that the people are not to be forever fed on milk, lest by chance they be overtaken by the last day while yet infants, but that they ought to be fed also on solid food, that they may go on to a spiritual old age. He flourished under Marcus Antoninus Verus and Lucius Aurelius Commodus. <a name="fr_2428"></a><a href="#fn_2428"><font size="1">2429</font></a>
