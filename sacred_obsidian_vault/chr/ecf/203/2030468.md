 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lxxxvii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxxvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXXV.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxxxvii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxxvii-p2.1"></a>Eustathius</span>, <a name="fr_2507"></a><a href="#fn_2507"><font size="1">2508</font></a>
  a Pamphilian from Side, bishop <a name="fr_2508"></a><a href="#fn_2508"><font size="1">2509</font></a>
  first of Berœa in Syria and then of Antioch, ruled the church and, composing many things against the doctrine of the Arians, was driven into exile under the emperor Constantius <a name="fr_2509"></a><a href="#fn_2509"><font size="1">2510</font></a>
  into Trajanopolis in Thrace where he is until this day. Works of his are extant <i>On the soul, On ventriloquism Against Origen</i> and <i>Letters</i> too numerous to mention.</p>
