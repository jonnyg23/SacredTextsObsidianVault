 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.cvi-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter CIV.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.cvi-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cvi-p2.1"></a>Apollinarus</span>, <a name="fr_2530"></a><a href="#fn_2530"><font size="1">2531</font></a>
  bishop of Laodicea, in Syria, the son of a presbyter, applied himself in his youth to the diligent study of grammar, and afterwards, writing innumerable volumes on the Holy Scriptures, died in the reign of the Emperor Theodosius. There are extant thirty books by him <i>Against Porphyry,</i> which are generally considered as among the best of his works. <a name="fr_2531"></a><a href="#fn_2531"><font size="1">2532</font></a>
