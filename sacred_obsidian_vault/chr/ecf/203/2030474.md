 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.xciii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xciii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XCI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.xciii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xciii-p2.1"></a>Eusebius <a name="fr_2516"></a><a href="#fn_2516"><font size="1">2517</font></a>
 </span> of Emesa, who had fine rhetorical talent, composed innumerable works suited to win popular applause and writing historically he is most diligently read by those who practise public speaking. Among these the chief are, <i>Against Jews, Gentiles and Novatians</i> and <i>Homilies on the Gospels,</i> brief but numerous. He flourished in the reign of the emperor Constantius in whose reign he died, and was buried at Antioch.</p>
