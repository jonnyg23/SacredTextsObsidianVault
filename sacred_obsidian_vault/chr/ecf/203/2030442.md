 <p> <a name="v.iii.lxi"></a>
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lxi-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter LIX.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxi-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxi-p2.1"></a>Gaius</span>, <a name="fr_2471"></a><a href="#fn_2471"><font size="1">2472</font></a>
  bishop of Rome, in the time of Zephyrinus, that is, in the reign of Antoninus, the son of Severus, delivered a very notable disputation <i>Against Proculus,</i> the follower of Montanus, convicting him of <a name="page_375"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 375</font></a> <a name="v.iii.lxi-Page_375"></a>temerity in his defence of the new prophecy, and in the same volume also enumerating only thirteen epistles of Paul, says that the fourteenth, which is now called, <i>To the Hebrews,</i> is not by him, and is not considered among the Romans to the present day as being by the apostle Paul.</p>
