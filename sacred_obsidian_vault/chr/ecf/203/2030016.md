 </p><p class="c22"><a name="iv.v-p1"></a>   <a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 24</font></a> <a name="iv.v-Page_24"></a><span class="c21"><a name="iv.v-p1.1"></a>Manuscripts and Editions of Separate Works.</span></p>  
 <p class="c2"><a name="iv.v-p2"></a>————————————</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="iv.v-p3"></a>The editions of the Ecclesiastical History are the most numerous, though of several others there are many. Of the collected works the following are the principal.</p>  
 <p class="c44"><a name="iv.v-p4"></a>(i) Editio princeps, of Paulus Manutius, Latin Version only. Rome 1556.</p>  
 <p class="c44"><a name="iv.v-p5"></a>(ii) J. Birckman, fol. 2 voll. Latin only Cologne 1573.</p>  
 <p class="c44"><a name="iv.v-p6"></a>(iii) J. Sirmond, 4 voll. fol. Greek and Latin, Paris 1642.</p>  
 <p class="c44"><a name="iv.v-p7"></a>To this the Auctarium of J. Garnier, with his dissertations was added in 1684.</p>  
 <p class="c42"><a name="iv.v-p8"></a>(iv) John Lewis Schulze, Greek and Latin, based upon the preceding, in 5 voll. Halle, 1774.</p>  
 <p class="c44"><a name="iv.v-p9"></a>(v) Mignes edition of the foregoing. Paris 1860.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.v-p10"></a>(The last-named is the Edition used for the translation in this work.)</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.v-p11"></a>The <span class="c14"><a name="iv.v-p11.1"></a>mss.</span> authority for the works of Theodoret is strong. The afore-named editions are based on <span class="c14"><a name="iv.v-p11.2"></a>ms.</span> in the libraries of Augsburg, Florence, Rome and Naples.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.v-p12"></a>To works on Theodoret mentioned in the notes may be added:—</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.v-p13"></a>S. Küpper, Ausgew, Schriften des sel. Theodoret aus dem Urtext übers.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.v-p14"></a>E. Binder, Études sur Theodoret. Geneva, 1844.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.v-p15"></a>Specht, Theodor von Mopsuestia, und Theodoret von Cyrus. Munich, 1871.</p>
