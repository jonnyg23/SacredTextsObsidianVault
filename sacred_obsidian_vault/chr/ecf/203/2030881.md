 <p> <a name="vi.xiii.xxxiii"></a>
 </p><p class="c24"><a name="vi.xiii.xxxiii-p1"></a>  32. To sit at the right hand of the Father is a mystery belonging to the Incarnation. For it does not befit that incorporeal nature without the assumption of flesh; neither is the excellency of a heavenly seat sought for the divine nature, but for the human. Whence it is said of Him, “Thy seat, O God, is prepared from thence forward; Thou art from everlasting.” <a name="fr_3380"></a><a href="#fn_3380"><font size="1">3381</font></a>
  The seat, then, whereon the Lord Jesus was to sit, was prepared from everlasting, “in whose name every knee should bow, of things in heaven and things on earth, and things under the earth; and every tongue shall confess to Him that Jesus is Lord in the glory of God the Father;” <a name="fr_3381"></a><a href="#fn_3381"><font size="1">3382</font></a>
  of Whom also David thus speaks, “The Lord said unto my Lord, Sit Thou on my right hand until I make Thine enemies Thy footstool.” <a name="fr_3382"></a><a href="#fn_3382"><font size="1">3383</font></a>
  Referring to which words the Lord in the Gospel said to the Pharisees, “If therefore <a name="page_556"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 556</font></a> <a name="vi.xiii.xxxiii-Page_556"></a>David in spirit calleth Him Lord, how is He his Son?” <a name="fr_3383"></a><a href="#fn_3383"><font size="1">3384</font></a>
  By which He shewed that according to the Spirit He was the Lord, according to the flesh He was the Son, of David. Whence also the Lord Himself says in another place, “Verily I say unto you, henceforth ye shall see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of the power of God.” <a name="fr_3384"></a><a href="#fn_3384"><font size="1">3385</font></a>
  And the Apostle Peter says of Christ, “Who is on the right hand of God, seated in the heavens.” <a name="fr_3385"></a><a href="#fn_3385"><font size="1">3386</font></a>
  And Paul also, writing to the Ephesians, “According to the working of the might of His power, which He wrought in Christ, when He raised Him from the dead, and seated Him on His right hand.” <a name="fr_3386"></a><a href="#fn_3386"><font size="1">3387</font></a>
 </p>
