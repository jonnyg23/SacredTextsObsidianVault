 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.viii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.viii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.viii-p2.1"></a>Barnabas <a name="fr_2363"></a><a href="#fn_2363"><font size="1">2364</font></a>
 </span> the Cyprian, also called Joseph the Levite, ordained apostle to the Gentiles with Paul, wrote one <i>Epistle,</i> valuable for the edification of the church, which is reckoned among the apocryphal writings. He afterwards separated from Paul on account of John, a disciple also called Mark, <a name="fr_2364"></a><a href="#fn_2364"><font size="1">2365</font></a>
  none the less exercised the work laid upon him of preaching the Gospel.</p>
