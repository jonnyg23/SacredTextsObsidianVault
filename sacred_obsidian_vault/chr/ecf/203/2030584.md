 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.lxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXIV.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.lxv-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxv-p2.1"></a>Eucherius, <a name="fr_2682"></a><a href="#fn_2682"><font size="1">2683</font></a>
 </span> bishop of the church at Lyons, wrote to his relative Valerianus, <i>On contempt for the world and worldly philosophy,</i> a single letter, written in a style which shows sound learning and reasoning. He wrote also to his sons, Salonius and Veranius, afterward bishops, a discussion <i>On certain obscure passages of Holy Scriptures,</i> and besides, revising and condensing certain works of Saint Cassianus, he compressed them into one volume, and wrote other works suited to ecclesiastical or monastic pursuits. He died in the reign of Valentinianus and Martianus.</p>
