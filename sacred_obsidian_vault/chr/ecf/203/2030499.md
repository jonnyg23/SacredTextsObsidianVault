 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.cxviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cxviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter CXVI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.cxviii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cxviii-p2.1"></a>Basil</span>, <a name="fr_2548"></a><a href="#fn_2548"><font size="1">2549</font></a>
  bishop of Cæsarea in Cappadocia, the city formerly called Mazaca, composed admirable carefully written books <i>Against Eunomius,</i> a volume <i>On the Holy Spirit,</i> and nine homilies <i>On the six days of creation,</i> also a work <i>On asceticism</i> and short treatises on various subjects. He died in the reign of Gratianus.</p>
