 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.l-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.l-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLIX.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.l-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.l-p2.1"></a>Paulinus, <a name="fr_2654"></a><a href="#fn_2654"><font size="1">2655</font></a>
 </span> bishop of Nola in Campania, composed many brief works in verse, also a consolatory work to Celsus <i>On the death of a christian and baptized child,</i> a sort of epitaph, well fortified with christian hope, also many <i>Letters to Severus,</i> and <i>A panegric</i> in prose written before he became bishop, <i>On victory over tyrants</i> which was addressed to Theodosius and maintained that victory lay rather in faith and prayer, than in arms. He wrote also a <i>Sacramentary</i> and <i>Hymnal.</i></p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="v.iv.l-p4"></a>He also addressed many letters to his sister, <i>On contempt of the world,</i> and published treatises of different sorts, on various occasions. <a name="fr_2655"></a><a href="#fn_2655"><font size="1">2656</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="v.iv.l-p6"></a>The most notable of all his minor works are the works <i>On repentance,</i> and <i>A general panegyric of all the martyrs.</i> He lived in the reign of Honorius and Valentinianus, and was distinguished, not only for erudition <a name="fr_2656"></a><a href="#fn_2656"><font size="1">2657</font></a>
  and holiness of life, but also for his ability to cast out demons.</p>
