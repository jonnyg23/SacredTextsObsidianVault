 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.xxii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xxii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.xxii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xxii-p2.1"></a>Severianus</span>, <a name="fr_2608"></a><a href="#fn_2608"><font size="1">2609</font></a>
  bishop of the church of Gabala, was learned in the Holy Scriptures and a wonderful preacher of homilies. On this account he was frequently summoned by the bishop John and the emperor Arcadius to preach a sermon at Constantinople. I have read his <i>Exposition of the epistle to the Galatians</i> and a most attractive little work <i>On baptism and the feast of Epiphany.</i> He died in the reign of Theodosius, his son by baptism.</p>
