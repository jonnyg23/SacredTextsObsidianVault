 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.xcvii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xcvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XCVI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.xcvii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xcvii-p2.1"></a>Cerealis <a name="fr_2746"></a><a href="#fn_2746"><font size="1">2747</font></a>
 </span> the bishop, an African by birth, was asked by Maximus bishop of the Arians whether he could establish the catholic faith by a few testimonies of Divine Scripture and without any controversial assertions. This he did in the name of the Lord, truth itself helping him, not with a few testimonies as Maximus had derisively asked, but proving by copious proof texts from both Old and New Testaments and published in a little book.</p>
