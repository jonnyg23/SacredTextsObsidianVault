 <p> <a name="vi.xiii.xxvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c24"><a name="vi.xiii.xxvii-p1"></a>  26. It is related also that vinegar was given Him to drink, or wine mingled with myrrh which is bitterer than gall. Hear what the Prophet has foretold of this: “They gave Me gall to eat, and when I was thirsty they gave Me vinegar to drink.” <a name="fr_3339"></a><a href="#fn_3339"><font size="1">3340</font></a>
  Agreeably with which Moses, even in his day, said to the people, “Their vine is of the vineyards of Sodom, and their branch of Gomorrah; their grape is a grape of gall, and their cluster a cluster of bitterness.” <a name="fr_3340"></a><a href="#fn_3340"><font size="1">3341</font></a>
  And again, the Prophet upbraiding them says, “Oh foolish people and unwise, have ye thus requited the Lord?” <a name="fr_3341"></a><a href="#fn_3341"><font size="1">3342</font></a>
  Moreover, in the Canticles the same things are foretold, where even the garden in which the Lord was crucified is indicated: “I have come into my garden, my sister, my spouse, and have gathered in my myrrh.” <a name="fr_3342"></a><a href="#fn_3342"><font size="1">3343</font></a>
  Here the Prophet has plainly set forth the wine mingled with myrrh which the Lord has given Him to drink.</p>
