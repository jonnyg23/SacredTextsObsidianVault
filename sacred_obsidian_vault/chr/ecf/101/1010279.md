 <p> <a name="vi.XIII.XIII"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XIII.XIII-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XIII.XIII-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIII.—That the Renewal of Man is Not Completed in This World.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XIII-p2"></a>14. But as yet “by faith, not by sight,” <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1217"><font size="1">1218</font></a>
  for “we are saved by hope; but hope that is seen is not hope.” <a name="fr_1218"></a><a href="#fn_1218"><font size="1">1219</font></a>
  As yet deep calleth unto deep <a name="fr_1219"></a><a href="#fn_1219"><font size="1">1220</font></a>
  but in “the noise of Thy waterspouts.” <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1220"><font size="1">1221</font></a>
  And as yet doth he that saith, I “could not speak unto you as unto spiritual, but as unto carnal,” <a name="fr_1221"></a><a href="#fn_1221"><font size="1">1222</font></a>
  even he, as yet, doth not count himself to have apprehended, and forgetteth those things which are behind, and reacheth forth to those things which are before, <a name="fr_1222"></a><a href="#fn_1222"><font size="1">1223</font></a>
  and groaneth being burdened; <a name="fr_1223"></a><a href="#fn_1223"><font size="1">1224</font></a>
  and his soul thirsteth after the living God, as the hart after the water-brooks, and saith, “When shall I come?” <a name="fr_1224"></a><a href="#fn_1224"><font size="1">1225</font></a>
  “desiring to be clothed upon with his house which is from heaven;” <a name="fr_1225"></a><a href="#fn_1225"><font size="1">1226</font></a>
  and calleth upon this lower deep, saying, “Be not conformed to this world, but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind.” <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1226"><font size="1">1227</font></a>
  And, “Be not children in understanding, howbeit in malice be ye children,” that in “understanding ye may be perfect;” <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1227"><font size="1">1228</font></a>
  and “O foolish Galatians, who hath bewitched you?” <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1228"><font size="1">1229</font></a>
  But now not in his own voice, but in Thine who sentest Thy Spirit from above; <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="#fn_1229"><font size="1">1230</font></a>
  through Him who “ascended up on high,” <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="#fn_1230"><font size="1">1231</font></a>
  and set open the flood-gates of His gifts, <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">1232</font></a>
  that the force of His streams might make glad the city of God. <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">1233</font></a>
  For, for Him doth “the friend of the bridegroom” <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">1234</font></a>
  sigh, having now the first-fruits of the Spirit laid up with Him, yet still groaning within himself, waiting for the adoption, to wit, the redemption of his body; <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">1235</font></a>
  to Him he sighs, for he is a member of the Bride; for Him is he jealous, for he is the friend of the Bridegroom; <a name="fr_1235"></a><a href="#fn_1235"><font size="1">1236</font></a>
  for Him is he jealous, not for himself; because in the voice of Thy “waterspouts,” <a name="fr_1236"></a><a href="#fn_1236"><font size="1">1237</font></a>
  not in his own voice, doth he call on that other deep, for whom being jealous he feareth, lest that, as the serpent beguiled Eve through his subtilty, so their minds should be corrupted from the simplicity that is in our Bridegroom, Thine only Son. <a name="fr_1237"></a><a href="#fn_1237"><font size="1">1238</font></a>
  What a light of beauty will that be when “we shall see Him as He is,” <a name="fr_1238"></a><a href="#fn_1238"><font size="1">1239</font></a>
  and those tears be passed away <a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 195</font></a> <a name="vi.XIII.XIII-Page_195"></a>which “have been my meat day and night, while they continually say unto me, Where is thy God?” <a name="fr_1239"></a><a href="#fn_1239"><font size="1">1240</font></a>
 </p>
