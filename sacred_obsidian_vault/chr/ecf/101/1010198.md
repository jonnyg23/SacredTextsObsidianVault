 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.X.XLI-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.X.XLI-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLI.—Having Conquered His Triple Desire, He Arrives at Salvation.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.X.XLI-p2"></a>66. And thus have I reflected upon the wearinesses of my sins, in that threefold “lust,” <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">969</font></a>
  and have invoked Thy right hand to my aid. For with a wounded heart have I seen Thy brightness, and being beaten back I exclaimed, “Who can attain unto it?” “I am cut off from before Thine eyes.” <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">970</font></a>
  Thou art the Truth, who presidest over all things, but I, through my covetousness, wished not to lose Thee, but with Thee wished to possess a lie; as no one wishes so to speak falsely as himself to be ignorant of the truth. So then I lost Thee, because Thou deignest not to be enjoyed with a lie.</p>
