 <p> <a name="vi.XIII.XV"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XIII.XV-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XIII.XV-p1.1"></a>Chapter XV.—Allegorical Explanation of the Firmament and Upper Works, Ver. 6.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XV-p2"></a>16. Or who but Thou, our God, made for us that firmament <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1259"><font size="1">1260</font></a>
  of authority over us in Thy divine Scripture? <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1260"><font size="1">1261</font></a>
  As it is said, For heaven shall be folded up like a scroll; <a name="fr_1261"></a><a href="#fn_1261"><font size="1">1262</font></a>
  and now it is extended over us like a skin. <a name="fr_1262"></a><a href="#fn_1262"><font size="1">1263</font></a>
  For Thy divine Scripture is of more sublime authority, since those mortals through whom Thou didst dispense it unto us underwent mortality. And Thou knowest, O Lord, Thou knowest, how Thou with skins didst clothe men <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1263"><font size="1">1264</font></a>
  when by sin they became mortal. Whence as a skin hast Thou stretched out the firmament of Thy Book; <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1264"><font size="1">1265</font></a>
  that is to say, Thy harmonious words, which by the ministry of mortals Thou hast spread over us. For by their very death is that solid firmament of authority in Thy discourses set forth by them more sublimely extended above all things that are under it, the which, while they were living here, was not so eminently extended. <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1265"><font size="1">1266</font></a>
  Thou hadst not as yet spread abroad the heaven like a skin; Thou hadst not as yet noised everywhere the report of their deaths.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XV-p10"></a>17. Let us look, O Lord, “upon the heavens, the work of Thy fingers;” <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1266"><font size="1">1267</font></a>
  clear from our eyes that mist with which Thou hast covered them. There is that testimony of Thine which giveth wisdom unto the little ones. <a name="fr_1267"></a><a href="#fn_1267"><font size="1">1268</font></a>
  Perfect, O my God, Thy praise out of the mouth of babes and sucklings. <a name="fr_1268"></a><a href="#fn_1268"><font size="1">1269</font></a>
  Nor have we known any other books so destructive to pride, so destructive to the enemy and the defender, <a name="fr_1269"></a><a href="#fn_1269"><font size="1">1270</font></a>
  who resisteth Thy reconciliation in defence of his own sins. <a name="fr_1270"></a><a href="#fn_1270"><font size="1">1271</font></a>
  I know not, O Lord, I know not other such “pure” <a name="fr_1271"></a><a href="#fn_1271"><font size="1">1272</font></a>
  words which so persuade me to confession, and make my neck submissive to Thy yoke, and invite me to serve Thee for nought. Let me understand these things, good Father. Grant this to me, placed under them; because Thou hast established these things for those placed under them.</p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 196</font></a> <a name="vi.XIII.XV-Page_196"></a>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XV-p17"></a>18. Other “waters” there be “above” this “firmament,” I believe immortal, and removed from earthly corruption. Let them praise Thy Name,—those super-celestial people, Thine angels, who have no need to look up at this firmament, or by reading to attain the knowledge of Thy Word,—let them praise Thee. For they always behold Thy face, <a name="fr_1272"></a><a href="#fn_1272"><font size="1">1273</font></a>
  and therein read without any syllables in time what Thy eternal will willeth. They read, they choose, they love. <a name="fr_1273"></a><a href="#fn_1273"><font size="1">1274</font></a>
  They are always reading; and that which they read never passeth away. For, by choosing and by loving, they read the very unchangeableness of Thy counsel. Their book is not closed, nor is the scroll folded up, <a name="fr_1274"></a><a href="#fn_1274"><font size="1">1275</font></a>
  because Thou Thyself art this to them, yea, and art so eternally; because Thou hast appointed them above this firmament, which Thou hast made firm over the weakness of the lower people, where they might look up and learn Thy mercy, announcing in time Thee who hast made times. “For Thy mercy, O Lord, is in the heavens, and Thy faithfulness reacheth unto the clouds.” <a name="fr_1275"></a><a href="#fn_1275"><font size="1">1276</font></a>
  The clouds pass away, but the heaven remaineth. The preachers of Thy Word pass away from this life into another; but Thy Scripture is spread abroad over the people, even to the end of the world. Yea, both heaven and earth shall pass away, but Thy Words shall not pass away. <a name="fr_1276"></a><a href="#fn_1276"><font size="1">1277</font></a>
  Because the scroll shall be rolled together, <a name="fr_1277"></a><a href="#fn_1277"><font size="1">1278</font></a>
  and the grass over which it was spread shall with its goodliness pass away; but Thy Word remaineth for ever, <a name="fr_1278"></a><a href="#fn_1278"><font size="1">1279</font></a>
  which now appeareth unto us in the dark image of the clouds, and through the glass of the heavens, not as it is; <a name="fr_1279"></a><a href="#fn_1279"><font size="1">1280</font></a>
  because we also, although we be the well-beloved of Thy Son, yet it hath not yet appeared what we shall be. <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1280"><font size="1">1281</font></a>
  He looketh through the lattice <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">1282</font></a>
  of our flesh, and He is fair-speaking, and hath inflamed us, and we run after His odours. <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1282"><font size="1">1283</font></a>
  But “when He shall appear, then shall we be like Him, for we shall see Him as He is.” <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1283"><font size="1">1284</font></a>
  As He is, O Lord, shall we see Him, although the time be not yet.</p>
