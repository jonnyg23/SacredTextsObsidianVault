 <p> <a name="vi.VII.XI"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.VII.XI-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.VII.XI-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—That Creatures are Mutable and God Alone Immutable.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.VII.XI-p2"></a>17. And I viewed the other things below Thee, and perceived that they neither altogether are, nor altogether are not. They are, indeed, because they are from Thee; but are not, because they are not what Thou art. For that truly is which remains immutably. <a name="fr_533"></a><a href="#fn_533"><font size="1">534</font></a>
  It is good, then, for me to cleave unto God, <a name="fr_534"></a><a href="#fn_534"><font size="1">535</font></a>
  for if I remain not in Him, neither shall I in myself; but He, remaining in Himself, reneweth all things. <a name="fr_535"></a><a href="#fn_535"><font size="1">536</font></a>
  And Thou art the Lord my God, since Thou standest not in need of my goodness. <a name="fr_536"></a><a href="#fn_536"><font size="1">537</font></a>
 </p>
