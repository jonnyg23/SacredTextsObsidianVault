 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XIII.XXXVII-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XIII.XXXVII-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXVII.—Of Rest in God Who Ever Worketh, and Yet is Ever at Rest.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XXXVII-p2"></a>52. For even then shalt Thou so rest in us, as now Thou dost work in us; and thus shall that be Thy rest through us, as these are Thy works through us. <a name="fr_1431"></a><a href="#fn_1431"><font size="1">1432</font></a>
  But Thou, O Lord, ever workest, and art ever at rest. Nor seest Thou in time, nor movest Thou in time, nor restest Thou in time; and yet Thou makest the scenes of time, and the times themselves, and the rest which results from time.</p>
