 </p><p class="c20"><a name="iv.1.II-p1"></a>  II. <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p1.1"></a> BIOGRAPHIES</span>.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.1.II-p2"></a><span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p2.1"></a>Possidius</span> (Calamensis episcopus, a pupil and friend of Aug.): <i>Vita Augustini</i> (brief, but authentic, written 432, two years after his death, in tom. x. Append. 257-280, ed. Bened., and in nearly all other editions).</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.1.II-p3"></a><span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p3.1"></a>Benedictini Editores</span>: <i> Vita Augustini ex ejus potissimum scriptis concinnata</i>, in 8 books (very elaborate and extensive), in tom. xi. 1-492, ed. Bened. (in Mignes reprint, tom. i. col. 66-578).</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.1.II-p4"></a>The biographies of Aug. by <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.1"></a> Tillemont</span> (<i>Mém.</i> tom. xiii.); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.2"></a>Ellies Dupin</span> (in “Nouvelle bibliothèque des auteurs ecclésiastiques,” tom. ii. and iii.); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.3"></a>P. Bayle</span> (in his “Dictionnaire historique et critique,” art. Augustin); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.4"></a>Remi Ceillier</span> (in “Histoire générale des auteurs sacrés et ecclés.,” vol. xi. and xii.); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.5"></a>Cave</span> (in “Lives of the Fathers,” vol. ii.); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.6"></a>Kloth</span> (<i>Der heil Aug</i>., Aachen, 1840, 2 vols.); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.7"></a> Böhringer</span> (<i>Kirchengeschichte in Biographien</i>, vol. i. P. iii. p. 99 sqq., revised ed. Leipzig, 1877-78, 2 parts); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.8"></a>Poujoulat</span> (<i>Histoire de S. Aug</i>. Par. 1843 and 1852, 2 vols.; the same in German by <i>Fr. Hurter</i>, Schaff h. 1847, 2 vols.); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.9"></a>Eisenbarth</span> (Stuttg. 1853); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.10"></a>C. Bindemann</span> (<i>Der heil. Aug</i>. Berlin, 1844, 55, 69, 3 vols., the best work in German); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.11"></a>Edw. L. Cutts</span> (<i>St. Augustin</i>, London, 1880); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.12"></a>E. de Pressensé</span> (in Smith and Wace, “Dictionary of Christ. Biogr.” I. 216-225); <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.13"></a>Ph. Schaff</span> (<i>St. Augustin</i>, Berlin, 1854; English ed. New York and London, 1854, revised and enlarged in <i>St. Augustin, Melanchthon and Neander; three biographies</i>, New York and London, 1886, pp. 1-106). On Monnica see <span class="c9"><a name="iv.1.II-p4.14"></a>Braune</span>: <i>Monnica and Augustin</i>. Grimma, 1846.  </p>
