 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XII.XIV-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XII.XIV-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIV.—Of the Depth of the Sacred Scripture, and Its Enemies.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XII.XIV-p2"></a>17. Wonderful is the depth of Thy oracles, whose surface is before us, inviting the little ones; and yet wonderful is the depth, O my God, wonderful is the depth. <a name="fr_1102"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">1103</font></a>
  It is awe to look into it; and awe of honour, and a tremor of love. The enemies thereof I hate vehemently. <a name="fr_1103"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">1104</font></a>
  Oh, if Thou wouldest slay them with Thy two-edged sword, <a name="fr_1104"></a><a href="#fn_1104"><font size="1">1105</font></a>
  that they be not its enemies! For thus do I love, that they should be slain unto themselves that they may live unto Thee. But behold others not reprovers, but praisers of the book of Genesis,—“The Spirit of God,” say they, “Who by His servant Moses wrote these things, willed not that these words should be thus understood. He willed not that it should be understood as Thou sayest, but as we say.” Unto whom, O God of us all, Thyself being Judge, do I thus answer.</p>
