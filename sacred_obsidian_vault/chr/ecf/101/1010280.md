 <p> <a name="vi.XIII.XIV"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XIII.XIV-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XIII.XIV-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIV.—That Out of the Children of the Night and of the Darkness, Children of the Light and of the Day are Made.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XIV-p2"></a>15. And so say I too, O my God, where art Thou? Behold where Thou art! In Thee I breathe a little, when I pour out my soul by myself in the voice of joy and praise, the sound of him that keeps holy-day. <a name="fr_1240"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">1241</font></a>
  And yet it is “cast down,” because it relapses and becomes a deep, or rather it feels that it is still a deep. Unto it doth my faith speak which Thou hast kindled to enlighten my feet in the night, “Why art thou cast down, O my soul? and why art thou disquieted in me? hope thou in God;” <a name="fr_1241"></a><a href="#fn_1241"><font size="1">1242</font></a>
  His “word is a lamp unto my feet.” <a name="fr_1242"></a><a href="#fn_1242"><font size="1">1243</font></a>
  Hope and endure until the night,—the mother of the wicked,—until the anger of the Lord be overpast, <a name="fr_1243"></a><a href="#fn_1243"><font size="1">1244</font></a>
  whereof we also were once children who were sometimes darkness, <a name="fr_1244"></a><a href="#fn_1244"><font size="1">1245</font></a>
  the remains whereof we carry about us in our body, dead on account of sin, <a name="fr_1245"></a><a href="#fn_1245"><font size="1">1246</font></a>
  “until the day break and the shadows flee away.” <a name="fr_1246"></a><a href="#fn_1246"><font size="1">1247</font></a>
  “Hope thou in the Lord.” In the morning I shall stand in Thy presence, and contemplate Thee; <a name="fr_1247"></a><a href="#fn_1247"><font size="1">1248</font></a>
  I shall for ever confess unto Thee. <a name="fr_1248"></a><a href="#fn_1248"><font size="1">1249</font></a>
  In the morning I shall stand in Thy presence, and shall see “the health of my countenance,” <a name="fr_1249"></a><a href="#fn_1249"><font size="1">1250</font></a>
  my God, who also shall quicken our mortal bodies by the Spirit that dwelleth in us, <a name="fr_1250"></a><a href="#fn_1250"><font size="1">1251</font></a>
  because in mercy He was borne over our inner darksome and floating deep. Whence we have in this pilgrimage received “an earnest” <a name="fr_1251"></a><a href="#fn_1251"><font size="1">1252</font></a>
  that we should now be light, whilst as yet we “are saved by hope,” <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1252"><font size="1">1253</font></a>
  and are the children of light, and the children of the day,—not the children of the night nor of the darkness, <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1253"><font size="1">1254</font></a>
  which yet we have been. <a name="fr_1254"></a><a href="#fn_1254"><font size="1">1255</font></a>
  Betwixt whom and us, in this as yet uncertain state of human knowledge, Thou only dividest, who provest our hearts <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1255"><font size="1">1256</font></a>
  and callest the light day, and the darkness night. <a name="fr_1256"></a><a href="#fn_1256"><font size="1">1257</font></a>
  For who discerneth us but Thou? But what have we that we have not received of Thee? <a name="fr_1257"></a><a href="#fn_1257"><font size="1">1258</font></a>
  Out of the same lump vessels unto honour, of which others also are made to dishonour. <a name="fr_1258"></a><a href="#fn_1258"><font size="1">1259</font></a>
 </p>
