 <p> <a name="vi.XIII.XIX"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XIII.XIX-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XIII.XIX-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIX.—All Men Should Become Lights in the Firmament of Heaven.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XIX-p2"></a>24. But first, “Wash you, make you clean;” <a name="fr_1314"></a><a href="#fn_1314"><font size="1">1315</font></a>
  put away iniquity from your souls, and from before mine eyes, that the dry land may appear. “Learn to do well; judge the fatherless; plead for the widow,” <a name="fr_1315"></a><a href="#fn_1315"><font size="1">1316</font></a>
  that the earth may bring forth the green herb for meat, and the tree bearing fruit; <a name="fr_1316"></a><a href="#fn_1316"><font size="1">1317</font></a>
  and come let us reason together, saith the Lord, <a name="fr_1317"></a><a href="#fn_1317"><font size="1">1318</font></a>
  that there may be lights in the firmament of heaven, and that they may shine upon the earth. <a name="fr_1318"></a><a href="#fn_1318"><font size="1">1319</font></a>
  That rich man asked of the good Master what he should do to attain eternal life. <a name="fr_1319"></a><a href="#fn_1319"><font size="1">1320</font></a>
  Let the good Master, whom he thought a man, and nothing more, tell him (but He is “good” because He is God)—let Him tell him, that if he would “enter into life” he must “keep the commandments;” <a name="fr_1320"></a><a href="#fn_1320"><font size="1">1321</font></a>
  let him banish from himself the bitterness of malice and wickedness; <a name="fr_1321"></a><a href="#fn_1321"><font size="1">1322</font></a>
  let him not kill, nor commit adultery, nor steal, nor bear false witness; that the dry land may appear, and bud forth the honouring of father and mother, and the love of our neighbour. <a name="fr_1322"></a><a href="#fn_1322"><font size="1">1323</font></a>
  All these, saith he, have I kept. <a name="fr_1323"></a><a href="#fn_1323"><font size="1">1324</font></a>
  Whence, then, are there so many thorns, if the earth be fruitful? Go, root up the woody thicket of avarice; sell that thou hast, and be filled with fruit by giving to the poor, and thou shalt have treasure in heaven; and follow the Lord “if thou wilt be perfect,” <a name="fr_1324"></a><a href="#fn_1324"><font size="1">1325</font></a>
  coupled with those amongst whom He speaketh wisdom, Who knoweth what to distribute to the day and to the night, that thou also mayest know it, that for thee also there may be lights in the firmament of heaven, which will not be unless thy heart be there; <a name="fr_1325"></a><a href="#fn_1325"><font size="1">1326</font></a>
  which likewise also will not be unless thy treasure be there, as thou hast heard from the good Master. But the barren earth was grieved, <a name="fr_1326"></a><a href="#fn_1326"><font size="1">1327</font></a>
  and the thorns choked the word. <a name="fr_1327"></a><a href="#fn_1327"><font size="1">1328</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XIX-p17"></a>25. But you, “chosen generation, <a name="fr_1328"></a><a href="#fn_1328"><font size="1">1329</font></a>
  you weak things of the world,” who have forsaken all things that you might “follow the Lord,” go after Him, and “confound the things which are mighty;” <a name="fr_1329"></a><a href="#fn_1329"><font size="1">1330</font></a>
  go after Him, ye beautiful feet, <a name="fr_1330"></a><a href="#fn_1330"><font size="1">1331</font></a>
  and shine in the firmament, <a name="fr_1331"></a><a href="#fn_1331"><font size="1">1332</font></a>
  that the heavens may declare His glory, dividing between the light of the perfect, though not as of the angels, and the darkness of the little, though not despised ones. Shine over all the earth, and let the day, lightened by the sun, utter unto day the word of wisdom; and let night, shining by the moon, announce unto night the word of knowledge. <a name="fr_1332"></a><a href="#fn_1332"><font size="1">1333</font></a>
  The moon and the stars shine for the night, but the night obscureth them not, since they illumine it in its degree. For behold God (as it were) saying, “Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven.” There came suddenly a sound from heaven, as it had been the rushing of a mighty wind, and there appeared cloven tongues like as of fire, and it sat upon each of them. <a name="fr_1333"></a><a href="#fn_1333"><font size="1">1334</font></a>
  And there were made lights in the firmament of heaven, having the word of life. <a name="fr_1334"></a><a href="#fn_1334"><font size="1">1335</font></a>
  Run ye to and fro everywhere, ye holy fires, ye beautiful fires; for ye are the light of the world, <a name="fr_1335"></a><a href="#fn_1335"><font size="1">1336</font></a>
  nor are ye put under a bushel. <a name="fr_1336"></a><a href="#fn_1336"><font size="1">1337</font></a>
  He to whom ye cleave is exalted, and hath exalted you. Run ye to and fro, and be known unto all nations.</p>
