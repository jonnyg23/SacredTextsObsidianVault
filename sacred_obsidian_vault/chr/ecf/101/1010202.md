 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XI.I-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XI.I-p1.1"></a>Chapter I.—By Confession He Desires to Stimulate Towards God His Own Love and That of His Readers.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XI.I-p2"></a>1. <span class="c9"><a name="vi.XI.I-p2.1"></a>O Lord</span>, since eternity is Thine, art Thou ignorant of the things which I say unto Thee? Or seest Thou at the time that which cometh to pass in time? Why, therefore, do I place before Thee so many relations of things? Not surely that Thou mightest know them through me, but that I may awaken my own love and that of my readers towards Thee, that we may all say, “Great is the Lord, and greatly to be praised.” <a name="fr_998"></a><a href="#fn_998"><font size="1">999</font></a>
  I have already said, and shall say, for the love of Thy love do I this. For we also pray, and yet Truth says, “Your Father knoweth what things ye have need of before ye ask Him.” <a name="fr_999"></a><a href="#fn_999"><font size="1">1000</font></a>
  Therefore do we make known unto Thee our love, in confessing unto Thee our own miseries and Thy mercies upon us, that Thou mayest free us altogether, since Thou hast begun, that we may cease to be wretched in ourselves, and that we may be blessed in Thee; since Thou hast called us, that we may be poor in spirit, and meek, and mourners, and hungering and athirst after righteousness, and merciful, and pure in heart, and peacemakers. <a name="fr_1000"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">1001</font></a>
  Behold, I have told unto Thee many things, which I could and which I would, for Thou first wouldest that I should confess unto Thee, the Lord my God, for Thou art good, since Thy “mercy endureth for ever.” <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">1002</font></a>
 </p>
