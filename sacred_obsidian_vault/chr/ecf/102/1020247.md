 </p><p class="c29"><a name="iv.IX-p1"></a><span class="c18"><a name="iv.IX-p1.1"></a>Book IX.</span></p>  
 <p class="c19"><a name="iv.IX-p2"></a> ————————————</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="iv.IX-p3"></a>Argument—Having in the preceding book shown that the worship of demons must be abjured, since they in a thousand ways proclaim themselves to be wicked spirits, Augustin in this book meets those who allege a distinction among demons, some being evil, while others are good; and, having exploded this distinction, he proves that to no demon, but to Christ alone, belongs the office of providing men with eternal blessedness.</p>
