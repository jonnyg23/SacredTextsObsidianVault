 </p><p class="c29"><a name="II_1-p1"></a><span class="c18"><a name="II_1-p1.1"></a>Book II.</span></p>  
 <p class="c19"><a name="II_1-p2"></a> ————————————</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="II_1-p3"></a>Argument—In this book Augustin reviews those calamities which the Romans suffered before the time of Christ, and while the worship of the false gods was universally practised; and demonstrates that, far from being preserved from misfortune by the gods, the Romans have been by them overwhelmed with the only, or at least the greatest, of all calamities—the corruption of manners, and the vices of the soul.</p>
