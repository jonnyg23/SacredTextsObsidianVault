 <p> <a name="v.xviii.ii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xviii.ii-p0.1"></a>Chapter I.—Occasion of the epistle.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xviii.ii-p1"></a><a name="v.xviii.ii-p1.1"></a><span class="upper"><a name="v.xviii.ii-p1.2"></a>Since</span> Christ has, to our wonder, <a name="fr_1355"></a><a href="#fn_1355"><font size="1">1356</font></a>
  been made known among us to be the Son of the living God, and to have become man in these last times by means of the Virgin Mary, <a name="fr_1356"></a><a href="#fn_1356"><font size="1">1357</font></a>
  of the seed of David and Abraham, according to the announcements previously made regarding Him and through Him by the company of the prophets, we therefore beseech and entreat that, by thy wisdom, Maris our friend, bishop of our native Neapolis, <a name="fr_1357"></a><a href="#fn_1357"><font size="1">1358</font></a>
  which is near Zarbus, <a name="fr_1358"></a><a href="#fn_1358"><font size="1">1359</font></a>
  and Eulogius, and Sobelus the presbyter, be sent to us, that we be not destitute of such as preside over the divine word as Moses also says, “Let the Lord God look out a man who shall guide this people, and the congregation of the Lord shall not be as sheep which have no shepherd.” <a name="fr_1359"></a><a href="#fn_1359"><font size="1">1360</font></a>
 </p>
