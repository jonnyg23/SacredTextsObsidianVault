 <p> <a name="ix.viii.xxxvi"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.xxxvi-p0.1"></a>XXXVI.</h3>  
 <p><a name="ix.viii.xxxvi-p1"></a><a name="ix.viii.xxxvi-p1.1"></a>True <a name="fr_4857"></a><a href="#fn_4857"><font size="1">4858</font></a>
  knowledge, then, consists in the understanding of Christ, which Paul terms the wisdom of God hidden in a mystery, which “the natural man receiveth not,” <a name="fr_4858"></a><a href="#fn_4858"><font size="1">4859</font></a>
  the doctrine of the cross; of which if any man “taste,” <a name="fr_4859"></a><a href="#fn_4859"><font size="1">4860</font></a>
  he will not accede to the disputations and quibbles of proud and puffed-up men, <a name="fr_4860"></a><a href="#fn_4860"><font size="1">4861</font></a>
  who go into matters of which they have no perception. <a name="fr_4861"></a><a href="#fn_4861"><font size="1">4862</font></a>
  For the truth is unsophisticated (<span class="Greek"><a name="ix.viii.xxxvi-p6.2"></a>ἀσχημάτιστος</span>); and “the word is nigh thee, in thy mouth and in thy heart,” <a name="fr_4862"></a><a href="#fn_4862"><font size="1">4863</font></a>
  as the same apostle declares, being easy of comprehension to those who are obedient. For it renders us like to Christ, if we experience “the power of his resurrection and the fellowship of His sufferings.” <a name="fr_4863"></a><a href="#fn_4863"><font size="1">4864</font></a>
  For this is the affinity <a name="fr_4864"></a><a href="#fn_4864"><font size="1">4865</font></a>
  of the apostolical teaching and the most holy “faith delivered unto us,” <a name="fr_4865"></a><a href="#fn_4865"><font size="1">4866</font></a>
  which the unlearned receive, and those of slender knowledge have taught, not “giving heed to endless genealogies,” <a name="fr_4866"></a><a href="#fn_4866"><font size="1">4867</font></a>
  but studying rather [to observe] a straightforward course of life; lest, having been deprived of the Divine Spirit, they fail to attain to the kingdom of heaven. For truly the first thing is to deny ones self and to follow Christ; and those who do this are borne onward to perfection, having fulfilled all their Teachers will, becoming sons of God by spiritual regeneration, and heirs of the kingdom of heaven; those who seek which first shall not be forsaken.</p>
