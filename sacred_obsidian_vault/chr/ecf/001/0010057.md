 <p> <a name="ii.ii.lii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="ii.ii.lii-p0.1"></a>Chapter LII.—Such a confession is pleasing to God.</h3>  
 <p><a name="ii.ii.lii-p1"></a>The Lord, brethren, stands in need of nothing; and He desires nothing of any one, except that confession be made to Him. For, says the elect David, “I will confess unto the Lord; and that will please Him more than a young bullock that hath horns and hoofs. Let the poor see it, and be glad.” <a name="fr_231"></a><a href="#fn_231"><font size="1">232</font></a>
  And again he saith, “Offer <a name="fr_232"></a><a href="#fn_232"><font size="1">233</font></a>
  unto God the sacrifice of praise, and pay thy vows unto the Most High. And call upon Me in the day of thy trouble: I will deliver thee, and thou shalt glorify Me.” <a name="fr_233"></a><a href="#fn_233"><font size="1">234</font></a>
  For “the sacrifice of God is a broken spirit.” <a name="fr_234"></a><a href="#fn_234"><font size="1">235</font></a>
 </p>
