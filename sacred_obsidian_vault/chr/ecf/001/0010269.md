 <p> <a name="v.xv.vi"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xv.vi-p0.1"></a>Chapter VI.—Renewed cautions.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xv.vi-p1"></a>These things I write to you, thou new olive-tree of Christ, not that I am aware you hold any such opinions, but that I may put you on your guard, as a father does his children. Beware, therefore, of those that hasten to work mischief, those “enemies of the cross of Christ, whose end is destruction, whose glory is in their shame.” <a name="fr_1239"></a><a href="#fn_1239"><font size="1">1240</font></a>
  Beware of those “dumb dogs,” those trailing serpents, those scaly <a name="fr_1240"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">1241</font></a>
  dragons, those asps, and basilisks, and scorpions. For these are subtle wolves, <a name="fr_1241"></a><a href="#fn_1241"><font size="1">1242</font></a>
  and apes that mimic the appearance of men.</p>
