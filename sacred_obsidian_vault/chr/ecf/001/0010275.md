 <p> <a name="v.xv.xii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xv.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII.—Salutations.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xv.xii-p1"></a><a name="v.xv.xii-p1.1"></a>I salute the holy presbytery. I salute the sacred deacons, and that person most dear to me, <a name="fr_1257"></a><a href="#fn_1257"><font size="1">1258</font></a>
  whom may I behold, through the Holy Spirit, occupying my place when I shall attain to Christ. My soul be in place of his. I salute the sub-deacons, the readers, the singers, the doorkeepers, the labourers, <a name="fr_1258"></a><a href="#fn_1258"><font size="1">1259</font></a>
  the exorcists, the confessors. <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1259"><font size="1">1260</font></a>
  I salute the keepers of the holy gates, the deaconesses in Christ. I salute the virgins betrothed to Christ, of whom may I have joy in the Lord Jesus. <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1260"><font size="1">1261</font></a>
  I salute the people of the Lord, from the smallest to the greatest, and all my sisters in the Lord.</p>
