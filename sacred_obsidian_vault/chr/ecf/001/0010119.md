 <p> <a name="iv.iv.xxi"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p0.1"></a>Chapter XXI.—The date of the martyrdom.</h3>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p1"></a>Now, the blessed Polycarp suffered martyrdom on the second day of the month Xanthicus just begun, <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="#fn_475"><font size="1">476</font></a>
  the seventh day before the Kalends of May, on the great Sabbath, at the eighth hour. <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_476"><font size="1">477</font></a>
  <a name="iv.iv.xxi-p3.5"></a>He was taken by Herod, Philip the Trallian being high priest, <a name="fr_477"></a><a href="#fn_477"><font size="1">478</font></a>
  Statius Quadratus being proconsul, but Jesus Christ being King for ever, to whom be glory, honour, majesty, and an everlasting throne, from generation to generation. Amen.</p>

 <p class="endnote"><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p3"></a> Great obscurity hangs over the chronology here indicated. According to Usher, the Smyrnæans began the month Xanthicus on the 25th of March. But the seventh day before the Kalends of May is the 25th of April. Some, therefore, read <span class="Greek"><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p3.1"></a>᾽Απριλλίων</span> instead of <span class="Greek"><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p3.2"></a>Μαίων</span>. The great Sabbath is that before the passover. The “eighth hour” may correspond either to our 8 <span class="upper"><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p3.3"></a>a.m. </span>or 2 <span class="upper"><a name="iv.iv.xxi-p3.4"></a>p.m.</span></p>
