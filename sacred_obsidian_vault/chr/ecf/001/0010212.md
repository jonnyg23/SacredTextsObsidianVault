 <p> <a name="v.vii.xiii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.vii.xiii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XIII.—Conclusion.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.vii.xiii-p1"></a>I salute the families of my brethren, with their wives and children, and the virgins who are called widows. <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">1060</font></a>
  <a name="v.vii.xiii-p2.1"></a>Be ye strong, I pray, in the power of the Holy Ghost. Philo, who is with me, greets you. I salute the house of Tavias, and pray that it may be confirmed in faith and love, both corporeal and spiritual. I salute Alce, my well-beloved, <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">1061</font></a>
  and the incomparable Daphnus, and Eutecnus, and all by name. <a name="v.vii.xiii-p3.1"></a>Fare ye well in the grace of God.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.vii.xiii-p4"></a>I salute the families of my brethren, with their wives and children, and those that are ever virgins, and the widows. <a name="v.vii.xiii-p4.1"></a>Be ye strong, I pray, in the power of the Holy Ghost. Philo, my fellow-servant, who is with me, greets you. I salute the house of Tavias, and pray that it may be confirmed in faith and love, both corporeal and spiritual. I salute Alce; my well-beloved, <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">1062</font></a>
  and the incomparable Daphnus, and Eutecnus, and all by name. <a name="v.vii.xiii-p5.1"></a>Fare ye well in the grace of God, and of our Lord Jesus Christ, being filled with the Holy Spirit, and divine and sacred wisdom.</p>
