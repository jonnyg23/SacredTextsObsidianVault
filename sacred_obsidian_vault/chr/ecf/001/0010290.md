 <p> <a name="v.xvii.ii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xvii.ii-p0.1"></a>Chapter II.—Unity of the three divine persons.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xvii.ii-p1"></a><a name="v.xvii.ii-p1.1"></a>There is then one God and Father, and not two or three; One who is; and there is no other besides Him, the only true [God]. For “the Lord thy God,” saith [the Scripture], “is one Lord.” <a name="fr_1304"></a><a href="#fn_1304"><font size="1">1305</font></a>
  And again, “Hath not one God created us? Have we not all one Father? <a name="fr_1305"></a><a href="#fn_1305"><font size="1">1306</font></a>
  And there is also one Son, God the Word. For “the only-begotten Son,” saith [the Scripture], “who is in the bosom of the Father.” <a name="fr_1306"></a><a href="#fn_1306"><font size="1">1307</font></a>
  And again, “One Lord Jesus Christ.” <a name="fr_1307"></a><a href="#fn_1307"><font size="1">1308</font></a>
  And in another place, “What is His name, or what His Sons name, that we may know?” <a name="fr_1308"></a><a href="#fn_1308"><font size="1">1309</font></a>
  And there is also one Paraclete. <a name="fr_1309"></a><a href="#fn_1309"><font size="1">1310</font></a>
  For “there is also,” saith [the Scripture], “one Spirit,” <a name="fr_1310"></a><a href="#fn_1310"><font size="1">1311</font></a>
  since “we have been called in one hope of our calling.” <a name="fr_1311"></a><a href="#fn_1311"><font size="1">1312</font></a>
  And again, “We have drunk of one Spirit,” <a name="fr_1312"></a><a href="#fn_1312"><font size="1">1313</font></a>
  with what follows. And it is manifest that all these gifts [possessed by believers] “worketh one and the self-same Spirit.” <a name="fr_1313"></a><a href="#fn_1313"><font size="1">1314</font></a>
  There are not then either three Fathers, <a name="fr_1314"></a><a href="#fn_1314"><font size="1">1315</font></a>
  or three Sons, or three Paracletes, but one Father, and one Son, and one Paraclete. Wherefore also the Lord, when He sent forth the apostles to make disciples of all nations, commanded them to “baptize in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost,” <a name="fr_1315"></a><a href="#fn_1315"><font size="1">1316</font></a>
  not unto one [person] having three names, nor into three [persons] who became incarnate, but into three possessed of equal honour.</p>
