 <p> <a name="v.xv.iv"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xv.iv-p0.1"></a>Chapter IV.—Continuation.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xv.iv-p1"></a>The Evangelists, too, when they declared that the one Father was “the only true God,” <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1228"><font size="1">1229</font></a>
  did not omit what concerned our Lord, but wrote: “In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. The same was in the beginning with God. All things were made by Him, and without Him was not anything made that was made.” <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="#fn_1229"><font size="1">1230</font></a>
  And concerning the incarnation: “The Word,” says [the Scripture], “became flesh, and dwelt among us.” <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="#fn_1230"><font size="1">1231</font></a>
  And again: “The book of the generation of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham.” <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">1232</font></a>
  And those very apostles, who said “that there is one God,” <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">1233</font></a>
  said also that “there  <a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 111</font></a> <a name="v.xv.iv-Page_111"></a>  is one Mediator between God and men.” <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">1234</font></a>
  Nor were they ashamed of the incarnation and the passion. For what says [one]? “The man Christ Jesus, who gave Himself” <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">1235</font></a>
  for the life and salvation of the world.</p>
