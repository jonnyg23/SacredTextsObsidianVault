 <p> <a name="viii.ix.xix"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="viii.ix.xix-p0.1"></a>XIX.</h3>  
 <p><a name="viii.ix.xix-p1"></a>[The words] of St. Justin, philosopher and martyr, from the fifth part of his <i>Apology:</i> <a name="fr_2640"></a><a href="#fn_2640"><font size="1">2641</font></a>
 —I reckon prosperity, O men, to consist in nothing else than in living according to truth. But we do not live properly, or according to truth, unless we understand the nature of things.</p>  
 <p><a name="viii.ix.xix-p3"></a>It escapes them apparently, that he who has by a true faith come forth from error to the truth, has truly known himself, not, as they say, as being in a state of frenzy, but as free from the unstable and (as to every variety of error) changeable corruption, by the simple and ever identical truth.—<i>From the writings of</i> <span class="upper"><a name="viii.ix.xix-p3.1"></a>John of Damascus</span>.</p>
