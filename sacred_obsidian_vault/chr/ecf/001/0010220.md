 <p> <a name="v.viii.vii"></a>  <a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a> <a name="v.viii.vii-Page_96"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.viii.vii-p0.1"></a>Chapter VII.—Request that Polycarp would send a messenger to Antioch.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.viii.vii-p1"></a><a name="v.viii.vii-p1.1"></a><a name="v.viii.vii-p1.2"></a><a name="v.viii.vii-p1.3"></a>Seeing that the Church which is at Antioch in Syria is, as report has informed me, at peace, through your prayers, I also am the more encouraged, resting without anxiety in God, <a name="fr_1108"></a><a href="#fn_1108"><font size="1">1109</font></a>
  if indeed by means of suffering I may attain to God, so that, through your prayers, I may be found a disciple [of Christ]. <a name="fr_1109"></a><a href="#fn_1109"><font size="1">1110</font></a>
  It is fitting, O Polycarp, most blessed in God, to assemble a very solemn <a name="fr_1110"></a><a href="#fn_1110"><font size="1">1111</font></a>
  council, and to elect one whom you greatly love, and know to be a man of activity, who may be designated the messenger of God; <a name="fr_1111"></a><a href="#fn_1111"><font size="1">1112</font></a>
  and to bestow on him this honour that he may go into Syria, and glorify your ever active love to the praise of Christ. A Christian has not power over himself, but must always be ready for <a name="fr_1112"></a><a href="#fn_1112"><font size="1">1113</font></a>
  the service of God. Now, this work is both Gods and yours, when ye shall have completed it to His glory. <a name="fr_1113"></a><a href="#fn_1113"><font size="1">1114</font></a>
  For I trust that, through grace, ye are prepared for every good work pertaining to God. Knowing, therefore, your energetic love of the truth, I have exhorted you by this brief Epistle.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.viii.vii-p8"></a><a name="v.viii.vii-p8.1"></a><a name="v.viii.vii-p8.2"></a><a name="v.viii.vii-p8.3"></a>Seeing that the Church which is at Antioch in Syria is, as report has informed me, at peace, through your prayers, I also am the more encouraged, resting without anxiety in God, <a name="fr_1114"></a><a href="#fn_1114"><font size="1">1115</font></a>
  if indeed by means of suffering I may attain to God, so that, through your prayers, I may be found a disciple [of Christ]. It is fitting, O Polycarp, most blessed in God, to assemble a very solemn <a name="fr_1115"></a><a href="#fn_1115"><font size="1">1116</font></a>
  council, and to elect one whom you greatly love, and know to be a man of activity, who may be designated the messenger of God; <a name="fr_1116"></a><a href="#fn_1116"><font size="1">1117</font></a>
  and to bestow on him the honour of going into Syria, so that, going into Syria, he may glorify your ever active love to the praise of God. A Christian has not power over himself, but must always be ready for <a name="fr_1117"></a><a href="#fn_1117"><font size="1">1118</font></a>
  the service of God. Now, this work is both Gods and yours, when ye shall have completed it. For I trust that, through grace, ye are prepared for every good work pertaining to God. Knowing your energetic love of the truth, I have exhorted you by this brief Epistle.</p>
