 <p> <a name="v.iii.xii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.iii.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII.—Ye are superior to me.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.iii.xii-p1"></a>May I enjoy you in all respects, if indeed I be worthy! For though I am bound, I am not worthy to be compared to any of you that are at liberty. <a name="v.iii.xii-p1.1"></a><a name="v.iii.xii-p1.2"></a>I know that ye are not puffed up, for ye have Jesus Christ in yourselves. And all the more when I commend you, I know that ye cherish modesty <a name="fr_710"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">711</font></a>
  of spirit; as it is written, “The righteous man is his own accuser.” <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">712</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="v.iii.xii-p4"></a>May I enjoy you in all respects, if indeed I be worthy! For though I am bound, I am not worthy to be compared to one of you that are at liberty. <a name="v.iii.xii-p4.1"></a><a name="v.iii.xii-p4.2"></a>I know that ye are not puffed up, for ye have Jesus in yourselves. And all the more when I commend you, I know that ye cherish modesty <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">713</font></a>
  of spirit; as it is written, “The righteous man is his own accuser;” <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">714</font></a>
  and again, “Declare thou first thine iniquities, that thou mayest be justified;” <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">715</font></a>
  and again, “When ye shall have done all things that are commanded you, say, We are unprofitable servants;” <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">716</font></a>
  “for that which is highly esteemed among men is abomination in the sight of God.” <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">717</font></a>
  For says [the Scripture], “God be merciful to me a sinner.” <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">718</font></a>
  Therefore those great ones, Abraham and Job, <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">719</font></a>
  styled themselves “dust and ashes <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">720</font></a>
  before God. And David says, “Who am I before Thee, O Lord, that Thou hast glorified me hitherto?” <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">721</font></a>
  And Moses, who was “the meekest of all men,” <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">722</font></a>
  saith to God, “I am of a feeble voice, and of a slow tongue.” <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">723</font></a>
  Be ye therefore also of a humble spirit, that ye may be exalted; for “he that abaseth himself shall be exalted, and he that exalteth himself shall be abased.” <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">724</font></a>
 </p>
