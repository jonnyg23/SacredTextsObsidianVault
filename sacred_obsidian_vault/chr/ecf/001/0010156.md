 <p> <a name="v.iii.x"></a>  <a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a> <a name="v.iii.x-Page_63"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.iii.x-p0.1"></a>Chapter X.—Beware of Judaizing.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.iii.x-p1"></a><a name="v.iii.x-p1.1"></a><a name="v.iii.x-p1.2"></a>Let us not, therefore, be insensible to His kindness. <a name="v.iii.x-p1.3"></a><a name="v.iii.x-p1.4"></a>For were He to reward us according to our works, we should cease to be. Therefore, having become His disciples, let us learn to live according to the principles of Christianity. <a name="fr_694"></a><a href="#fn_694"><font size="1">695</font></a>
  For whosoever is called by any other name besides this, is not of God. <a name="v.iii.x-p2.1"></a>Lay aside, therefore, the evil, the old, the sour leaven, and be ye changed into the new leaven, which is Jesus Christ. Be ye salted in Him, lest any one among you should be corrupted, since by your savour ye shall be convicted. <a name="v.iii.x-p2.2"></a>It is absurd to profess <a name="fr_695"></a><a href="#fn_695"><font size="1">696</font></a>
  Christ Jesus, and to Judaize. For Christianity did not embrace <a name="fr_696"></a><a href="#fn_696"><font size="1">697</font></a>
  Judaism, but Judaism Christianity, that so every tongue which believeth might be gathered together to God.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.iii.x-p5"></a><a name="v.iii.x-p5.1"></a><a name="v.iii.x-p5.2"></a>Let us not, therefore, be insensible to His kindness. <a name="v.iii.x-p5.3"></a><a name="v.iii.x-p5.4"></a>For were He to reward us according to our works, we should cease to be. For “if Thou, Lord, shalt mark iniquities, O Lord, who shall stand?” <a name="fr_697"></a><a href="#fn_697"><font size="1">698</font></a>
  Let us therefore prove ourselves worthy of that name which we have received. For whosoever is called by any other name besides this, he is not of God; for he has not received the prophecy which speaks thus concerning us: “The people shall be called by a new name, which the Lord shall name them, and shall be a holy people.” <a name="fr_698"></a><a href="#fn_698"><font size="1">699</font></a>
  This was first fulfilled in Syria; for “the disciples were called Christians at Antioch,” <a name="fr_699"></a><a href="#fn_699"><font size="1">700</font></a>
  when Paul and Peter were laying the foundations of the Church. <a name="v.iii.x-p8.2"></a>Lay aside, therefore, the evil, the old, the corrupt leaven, <a name="fr_700"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">701</font></a>
  and be ye changed into the new leaven of grace. Abide in Christ, that the stranger <a name="fr_701"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">702</font></a>
  may not have dominion over you. <a name="v.iii.x-p10.1"></a>It is absurd to speak of Jesus Christ with the tongue, and to cherish in the mind a Judaism which has now come to an end. For where there is Christianity there cannot be Judaism. For Christ is one, in whom every nation that believes, and every tongue that confesses, is gathered unto God. And those that were of a stony heart have become the children of Abraham, the friend of God; <a name="fr_702"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">703</font></a>
  and in his seed all those have been blessed <a name="fr_703"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">704</font></a>
  who were ordained to eternal life <a name="fr_704"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">705</font></a>
  in Christ.</p>
