 <p> <a name="v.v.v"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.v.v-p0.1"></a>Chapter V.—I desire to die.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.v.v-p1"></a><a name="v.v.v-p1.1"></a><a name="v.v.v-p1.2"></a>From Syria even unto Rome I fight with beasts, <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">850</font></a>
  both by land and sea, both by night and day, being bound to ten leopards, I mean a band of soldiers, who, even when they receive benefits, <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">851</font></a>
  show themselves all the worse. But I am the more instructed by their injuries [to act as a disciple of Christ]; “yet am I not thereby justified.” <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">852</font></a>
  May I enjoy the wild beasts that are prepared for me; and I pray they may be found eager to rush upon me, which also I will entice to devour me speedily, and not deal with me as with some, whom, out of fear, they have not touched. But if they be unwilling to assail me, I will compel them to do so. Pardon me  <a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 76</font></a> <a name="v.v.v-Page_76"></a>  [in this]: I know what is for my benefit. Now I begin to be a disciple. And let no one, of things visible or invisible, envy <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">853</font></a>
  me that I should attain to Jesus Christ. Let fire and the cross; let the crowds of wild beasts; let tearings, <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">854</font></a>
  breakings, and dislocations of bones; let cutting off of members; let shatterings of the whole body; and let all the dreadful <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">855</font></a>
  torments of the devil come upon me: only let me attain to Jesus Christ.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.v.v-p8"></a><a name="v.v.v-p8.1"></a><a name="v.v.v-p8.2"></a>From Syria even unto Rome I fight with beasts, <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">856</font></a>
  both by land and sea, both by night and day, being bound to ten leopards, I mean a band of soldiers, who, even when they receive benefits, <a name="fr_856"></a><a href="#fn_856"><font size="1">857</font></a>
  show themselves all the worse. But I am the more instructed by their injuries [to act as a disciple of Christ]; “yet am I not thereby justified.” <a name="fr_857"></a><a href="#fn_857"><font size="1">858</font></a>
  May I enjoy the wild beasts that are prepared for me; and I pray that they may be found eager to rush upon me, which also I will entice to devour me speedily, and not deal with me as with some, whom, out of fear, they have not touched. But if they be unwilling to assail me, I will compel them to do so. Pardon me [in this] I know what is for my benefit. Now I begin to be a disciple, and have <a name="fr_858"></a><a href="#fn_858"><font size="1">859</font></a>
  no desire after anything visible or invisible, that I may attain to Jesus Christ. Let fire and the cross; let the crowds of wild beasts; let breakings, tearings, and separations of bones; let cutting off of members; let bruising to pieces of the whole body; and let the very torment of the devil come upon me: only let me attain to Jesus Christ.</p>
