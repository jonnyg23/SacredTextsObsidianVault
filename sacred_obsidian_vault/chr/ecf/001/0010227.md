 <p> <a name="v.x.iv"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.x.iv-p0.1"></a>Chapter IV.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.x.iv-p1"></a>Let not the widows be overlooked; on account of <a name="fr_1137"></a><a href="#fn_1137"><font size="1">1138</font></a>
  our Lord be thou their guardian, and let nothing be done without thy will; also do thou nothing without the will of God, as indeed thou doest not. Stand rightly. Let there be frequent <a name="fr_1138"></a><a href="#fn_1138"><font size="1">1139</font></a>
  assemblies: ask every man [to them] by his name. <a name="v.x.iv-p3.1"></a>Despise not slaves, either male or female; but neither let them be contemptuous, but let them labour the more as for the glory of  <a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 100</font></a> <a name="v.x.iv-Page_100"></a>  God, that they may be counted worthy of a more precious freedom, which is of God. Let them not desire to be set free out of the common [fund], lest they be found the slaves of lust.</p>
