 <p> <a name="v.iii.ii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.iii.ii-p0.1"></a>Chapter II.—I rejoice in your messengers.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.iii.ii-p1"></a><a name="v.iii.ii-p1.1"></a><a name="v.iii.ii-p1.2"></a><a name="v.iii.ii-p1.3"></a>Since, then, I have had the privilege of seeing you, through Damas your most worthy bishop, and through your worthy presbyters Bassus and Apollonius, and through my fellow-servant the deacon Sotio, whose friendship may I ever enjoy, inasmuch as he is subject to the bishop as to the grace of God, and to the presbytery as to the law of Jesus Christ, [I now write <a name="fr_637"></a><a href="#fn_637"><font size="1">638</font></a>
  to you].</p>  
 <p><a name="v.iii.ii-p3"></a><a name="v.iii.ii-p3.1"></a><a name="v.iii.ii-p3.2"></a><a name="v.iii.ii-p3.3"></a>Since, then, I have had the privilege of seeing you, through Damas your most worthy <a name="fr_638"></a><a href="#fn_638"><font size="1">639</font></a>
  bishop, and through your worthy <a name="fr_639"></a><a href="#fn_639"><font size="1">640</font></a>
  presbyters Bassus and Apollonius, and through my fellow-servant the deacon Sotio, whose friendship may I ever enjoy, <a name="fr_640"></a><a href="#fn_640"><font size="1">641</font></a>
  inasmuch as he, by the grace of God, is subject to the bishop and presbytery, in the law of Jesus Christ, [I now write <a name="fr_641"></a><a href="#fn_641"><font size="1">642</font></a>
  to you].</p>
