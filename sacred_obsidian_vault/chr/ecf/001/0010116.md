 <p> <a name="iv.iv.xviii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.xviii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.—The body of Polycarp is burned.</h3>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.xviii-p1"></a><a name="iv.iv.xviii-p1.1"></a>The centurion then, seeing the strife excited by the Jews, placed the body <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">468</font></a>
  in the midst of the fire, and consumed it. Accordingly, we afterwards took up his bones, as being more precious than the most exquisite jewels, and more purified <a name="fr_468"></a><a href="#fn_468"><font size="1">469</font></a>
  than gold, and deposited them in a fitting place, whither, being gathered together, as opportunity is allowed us, with joy and rejoicing, the Lord shall grant us to celebrate the anniversary <a name="fr_469"></a><a href="#fn_469"><font size="1">470</font></a>
  of his martyrdom, both in memory of those who have already finished their course, <a name="fr_470"></a><a href="#fn_470"><font size="1">471</font></a>
  and for the exercising and preparation of those yet to walk in their steps.</p>
