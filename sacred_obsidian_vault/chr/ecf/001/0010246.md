 <p> <a name="v.xii.iv"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xii.iv-p0.1"></a>Chapter IV.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xii.iv-p1"></a>I write to all the Churches, and declare to all men, that I willingly die for the sake of God, if so be that ye hinder me not. I entreat of you not to be [affected] towards me with a love which is unseasonable. Leave me to become [the prey of] the beasts, that by their means I may be accounted worthy of God. I am the wheat of God, and by the teeth of the beasts I shall be ground, <a name="fr_1162"></a><a href="#fn_1162"><font size="1">1163</font></a>
  that I may be found the pure bread of God. Provoke ye greatly <a name="fr_1163"></a><a href="#fn_1163"><font size="1">1164</font></a>
  the wild beasts, that they may be for me a grave, and may leave nothing of my body, in order that, when I have fallen asleep, I may not be a burden upon any one. Then shall I be in truth a disciple of Jesus Christ, when the world seeth not even my body. Entreat of our Lord in my behalf, that through these instruments I may be found a sacrifice to God. I do not, like Peter and Paul, issue orders unto you. They are <a name="fr_1164"></a><a href="#fn_1164"><font size="1">1165</font></a>
  apostles, but I am one condemned; they indeed are free, but I am a slave, even until now. But if I suffer, I shall be the freed-man of Jesus Christ, and I shall rise in Him from the dead, free. And now being in bonds, I learn to desire nothing.</p>
