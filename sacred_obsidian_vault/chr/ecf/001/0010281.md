 <p> <a name="v.xvi.iii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xvi.iii-p0.1"></a>Chapter III.—Exhortations as to ecclesiastical duties.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xvi.iii-p1"></a>“Honour widows that are widows indeed.” <a name="fr_1277"></a><a href="#fn_1277"><font size="1">1278</font></a>
  Be the friend of orphans; for God is “the Father of the fatherless, and the Judge of the widows.” <a name="fr_1278"></a><a href="#fn_1278"><font size="1">1279</font></a>
  Do nothing without the bishops; for they are priests, and thou a servant of the priests. They baptize, offer sacrifice, <a name="fr_1279"></a><a href="#fn_1279"><font size="1">1280</font></a>
  ordain, and lay on hands; but thou ministerest to them, as the holy Stephen  <a name="page_114"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 114</font></a> <a name="v.xvi.iii-Page_114"></a>  did at Jerusalem to James and the presbyters. Do not neglect the sacred meetings <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1280"><font size="1">1281</font></a>
  [of the saints]; inquire after every one by name. “Let no man despise thy youth, but be thou an example to the believers, both in word and conduct.” <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">1282</font></a>
 </p>
