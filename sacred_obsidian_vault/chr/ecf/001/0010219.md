 <p> <a name="v.viii.vi"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.viii.vi-p0.1"></a>Chapter VI.—The duties of the Christian flock.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.viii.vi-p1"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p1.1"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p1.2"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p1.3"></a>Give ye <a name="fr_1098"></a><a href="#fn_1098"><font size="1">1099</font></a>
  heed to the bishop, that God also may give heed to you. <a name="v.viii.vi-p2.1"></a>My soul be for theirs <a name="fr_1099"></a><a href="#fn_1099"><font size="1">1100</font></a>
  that are submissive to the bishop, to the presbyters, and to the deacons, and may my portion be along with them in God! <a name="v.viii.vi-p3.1"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p3.2"></a>Labour together with one another; strive in company together; run together; suffer together; sleep together; and awake together, as the stewards, and associates, <a name="fr_1100"></a><a href="#fn_1100"><font size="1">1101</font></a>
  and servants of God. Please ye Him under whom ye fight, and from whom ye receive your wages. Let none of you be found a deserter. Let your baptism endure as your arms; your faith as your helmet; your love as your spear; your patience as a complete panoply. Let your works be the charge <a name="fr_1101"></a><a href="#fn_1101"><font size="1">1102</font></a>
  assigned to you, that ye may receive a worthy recompense. Be long-suffering, therefore, with one another, in meekness, as God is towards you. May I have joy of you for ever! <a name="fr_1102"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">1103</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="v.viii.vi-p7"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p7.1"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p7.2"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p7.3"></a>Give ye <a name="fr_1103"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">1104</font></a>
  heed to the bishop, that God also may give heed to you. <a name="v.viii.vi-p8.1"></a>My soul be for theirs <a name="fr_1104"></a><a href="#fn_1104"><font size="1">1105</font></a>
  that are submissive to the bishop, to the presbytery, and to the deacons: may I have my portion with them from God! <a name="v.viii.vi-p9.1"></a><a name="v.viii.vi-p9.2"></a>Labour together with one another; strive in company together; run together; suffer together; sleep together; and awake together, as the stewards, and associates, <a name="fr_1105"></a><a href="#fn_1105"><font size="1">1106</font></a>
  and servants of God. Please ye Him under whom ye fight, and from whom ye shall receive your wages. Let none of you be found a deserter. Let your baptism endure as your arms; your faith as your helmet; your love as your spear; your patience as a complete panoply. Let your works be the charge assigned to you, that you may obtain for them a most worthy <a name="fr_1106"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">1107</font></a>
  recompense. Be long-suffering, therefore, with one another, in meekness, and God shall be so with you. May I have joy of you for ever! <a name="fr_1107"></a><a href="#fn_1107"><font size="1">1108</font></a>
 </p>
