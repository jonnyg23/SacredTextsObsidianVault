 <p> <a name="v.xv.x"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xv.x-p0.1"></a>Chapter X.—Duties of masters and servants.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xv.x-p1"></a>Ye masters, do not treat your servants with haughtiness, but imitate patient Job, who declares, “I did not despise <a name="fr_1251"></a><a href="#fn_1251"><font size="1">1252</font></a>
  the cause <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1252"><font size="1">1253</font></a>
  of my man-servant, or of my maid-servant, when they contended with me. For what in that case shall I do when the Lord makes an inquisition regarding me?” <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1253"><font size="1">1254</font></a>
  And you know what follows. Ye servants, do not provoke your masters to anger in anything, lest ye become the authors of incurable mischiefs to yourselves.</p>
