 <p> <a name="v.vii.viii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.vii.viii-p0.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Let nothing be done without the bishop.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.vii.viii-p1"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p1.1"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p1.2"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p1.3"></a>See that ye all follow the bishop, even as Jesus Christ does the Father, and the presbytery as ye would the apostles; and reverence the deacons, as being the institution <a name="fr_1026"></a><a href="#fn_1026"><font size="1">1027</font></a>
  of God. <a name="v.vii.viii-p2.1"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p2.2"></a>Let no man do anything connected with the Church without the bishop. <a name="v.vii.viii-p2.3"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p2.4"></a>Let that be deemed a proper <a name="fr_1027"></a><a href="#fn_1027"><font size="1">1028</font></a>
  Eucharist, which is [administered] either  <a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 90</font></a> <a name="v.vii.viii-Page_90"></a>  by the bishop, or by one to whom he has entrusted it. <a name="v.vii.viii-p3.1"></a>Wherever the bishop shall appear, there let the multitude [of the people] also be; even as, wherever Jesus Christ is, there is the Catholic Church. It is not lawful without the bishop either to baptize or to celebrate a love-feast; but whatsoever he shall approve of, that is also pleasing to God, so that everything that is done may be secure and valid. <a name="fr_1028"></a><a href="#fn_1028"><font size="1">1029</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="v.vii.viii-p5"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p5.1"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p5.2"></a>See that ye all follow the bishop, even as Christ Jesus does the Father, and the presbytery as ye would the apostles. <a name="v.vii.viii-p5.3"></a>Do ye also reverence the deacons, as those that carry out [through their office] the appointment of God. <a name="v.vii.viii-p5.4"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p5.5"></a>Let no man do anything connected with the Church without the bishop. <a name="v.vii.viii-p5.6"></a><a name="v.vii.viii-p5.7"></a>Let that be deemed a proper <a name="fr_1029"></a><a href="#fn_1029"><font size="1">1030</font></a>
  Eucharist, which is [administered] either by the bishop, or by one to whom he has entrusted it. Wherever the bishop shall appear, there let the multitude [of the people] also be; even as where Christ is, there does all the heavenly host stand by, waiting upon Him as the Chief Captain of the Lords might, and the Governor of every intelligent nature. It is not lawful without the bishop either to baptize, or to offer, or to present sacrifice, or to celebrate a love-feast. <a name="fr_1030"></a><a href="#fn_1030"><font size="1">1031</font></a>
  But that which seems good to him, is also well-pleasing to God, that everything ye do may be secure and valid.</p>
