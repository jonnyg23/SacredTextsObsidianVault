 <p> <a name="iv.ii.x"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iv.ii.x-p0.1"></a>Chapter X.—Exhortation to the practice of virtue. <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">391</font></a>
 </h3>  
 <p><a name="iv.ii.x-p2"></a><a name="iv.ii.x-p2.1"></a><a name="iv.ii.x-p2.2"></a><a name="iv.ii.x-p2.3"></a>Stand fast, therefore, in these things, and follow the example of the Lord, being firm and unchangeable in the faith, loving the brotherhood, <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">392</font></a>
  and being attached to one another, joined together in the truth, exhibiting the meekness of the Lord in your intercourse with one another, and despising no one. When you can do good, defer it not, because “alms delivers from death.” <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">393</font></a>
  Be all of you subject one to another <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">394</font></a>
  “having your conduct blameless among the Gentiles,” <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">395</font></a>
  that ye may both receive praise for your good works, and the Lord may not be blasphemed through you. But woe to him by whom the name of the Lord is blasphemed! <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">396</font></a>
  Teach, therefore, sobriety to all, and manifest it also in your own conduct.</p>
