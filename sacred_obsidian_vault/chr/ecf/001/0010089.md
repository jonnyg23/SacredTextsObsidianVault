 <p> <a name="iv.ii.vii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iv.ii.vii-p0.1"></a>Chapter VII.—Avoid the Docetæ, and persevere in fasting and prayer.</h3>  
 <p><a name="iv.ii.vii-p1"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p1.1"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p1.2"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p1.3"></a>“For whosoever does not confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh, is antichrist;” <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">377</font></a>
  and whosoever does not confess the testimony of the cross, <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_377"><font size="1">378</font></a>
  is of the devil; and whosoever perverts the oracles of the Lord to his own lusts, and says that there is neither a resurrection nor a judgment, he is the first-born of Satan. <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">379</font></a>
  <a name="iv.ii.vii-p4.1"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p4.2"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p4.3"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p4.4"></a><a name="iv.ii.vii-p4.5"></a>Wherefore, forsaking the vanity of many, and their false doctrines, let us return to the word which has been handed down to us from <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_379"><font size="1">380</font></a>
  the beginning; “watching unto prayer,” <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_380"><font size="1">381</font></a>
  and persevering in fasting; beseeching in our supplications the all-seeing God “not to lead us into temptation,” <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_381"><font size="1">382</font></a>
   <a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a> <a name="iv.ii.vii-Page_35"></a>  as the Lord has said: “The spirit truly is willing, but the flesh is weak.” <a name="fr_382"></a><a href="#fn_382"><font size="1">383</font></a>
 </p>
