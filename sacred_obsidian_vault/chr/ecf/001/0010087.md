 <p> <a name="iv.ii.v"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iv.ii.v-p0.1"></a>Chapter V.—The duties of deacons, youths, and virgins.</h3>  
 <p><a name="iv.ii.v-p1"></a>Knowing, then, that “God is not mocked,” <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">365</font></a>
  we ought to walk worthy of His commandment and glory. <a name="iv.ii.v-p2.2"></a><a name="iv.ii.v-p2.3"></a>In like manner should the deacons be blameless before the face of His righteousness, as being the servants of God and Christ, <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">366</font></a>
  and not of men. They must not be slanderers, double-tongued, <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">367</font></a>
  or lovers of money, but temperate in all things, compassionate, industrious, walking according to the truth of the Lord, who was the servant <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">368</font></a>
  of all. <a name="iv.ii.v-p5.2"></a>If we please Him in this present world, we shall receive also the future world, according as He has promised to us that He will raise us again from the dead, and that if we live <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">369</font></a>
  worthily of Him, “we shall also reign together with Him,” <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">370</font></a>
  provided only we believe. In like manner, let the young men also be blameless in all things, being especially careful to preserve purity, and keeping themselves in, as with a bridle, from every kind of evil. For it is well that they should be cut off from <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">371</font></a>
  the lusts that are in the world, since “every lust warreth against the spirit;” <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_371"><font size="1">372</font></a>
  and “neither fornicators, nor effeminate, nor abusers of themselves with mankind, shall inherit the kingdom of God,” <a name="fr_372"></a><a href="#fn_372"><font size="1">373</font></a>
  nor those who do things inconsistent and unbecoming. Wherefore, it is needful to abstain from all these things, being subject to the presbyters and deacons, as unto God and Christ. <a name="iv.ii.v-p10.2"></a>The virgins also must walk in a blameless and pure conscience.</p>
