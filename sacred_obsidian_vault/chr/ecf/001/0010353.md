 <p> <a name="vi.ii.xvii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="vi.ii.xvii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XVII.—Conclusion of the first part of the epistle.</h3>  
 <p><a name="vi.ii.xvii-p1"></a>As far as was possible, and could be done with perspicuity, I cherish the hope that, according to my desire, I have omitted none <a name="fr_1682"></a><a href="#fn_1682"><font size="1">1683</font></a>
  of those things at present [demanding consideration], which bear upon your salvation. For if I should write to you about things future, <a name="fr_1683"></a><a href="#fn_1683"><font size="1">1684</font></a>
  ye would not understand, because such knowledge is hid in parables. These things then are so.</p>
