 </p><p class="c39"><a name="iv.ii.lxxxiv-p1"></a>  <span class="c2"><a name="iv.ii.lxxxiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter 82.—The Mercy of God is Necessary to True Repentance.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.ii.lxxxiv-p2"></a>Now even penance itself, when by the law of the Church there is sufficient reason for its being gone through, is frequently evaded through infirmity; for shame is the fear of losing pleasure when the good opinion of men gives more pleasure than the righteousness which leads a man to humble himself in penitence. Wherefore the mercy of God is necessary not only when a man repents, but even to lead him to repent. How else explain what the apostle says of certain persons: “if God peradventure will give them repentance”? <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1263"><font size="1">1264</font></a>
  And before Peter wept bitterly, we are told by the evangelist, “The Lord turned, and looked upon him.” <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1264"><font size="1">1265</font></a>
