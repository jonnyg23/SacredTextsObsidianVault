 </p><p class="c39"><a name="iv.ii.iv-p1"></a>  <span class="c2"><a name="iv.ii.iv-p1.1"></a>Chapter 2.—The Fear of God is Mans True Wisdom.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.ii.iv-p2"></a>The true wisdom of man is piety. You find this in the book of holy Job. For we read there what wisdom itself has said to man: “Behold, the fear of the Lord [<i>pietas</i>], that is wisdom.” <a name="fr_1092"></a><a href="#fn_1092"><font size="1">1093</font></a>
  If you ask further what is meant in that place by <i>pietas</i>, the Greek calls it more definitely  <span class="Greek"><a name="iv.ii.iv-p3.2"></a>θεοσέβεια</span>, that is, the worship of God. The Greeks sometimes call piety <span class="Greek"><a name="iv.ii.iv-p3.3"></a>εὐσέβεια</span>, which signifies right worship, though this, of course, refers specially to the worship of God. But when we are defining <a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 238</font></a> <a name="iv.ii.iv-Page_238"></a>in what mans true wisdom consists, the most convenient word to use is that which distinctly expresses the fear of God. And can you, who are anxious that I should treat of great matters in few words, wish for a briefer form of expression? Or perhaps you are anxious that this expression should itself be briefly explained, and that I should unfold in a short discourse the proper mode of worshipping God?</p>
