 <p> <a name="v.iii.xxxi"></a>
 </p><p class="c10"><a name="v.iii.xxxi-p1"></a>  30. Ye also who have not yet made this vow, who are able to receive it, receive it. <a name="fr_2092"></a><a href="#fn_2092"><font size="1">2093</font></a>
  Run with perseverance, that ye may obtain. <a name="fr_2093"></a><a href="#fn_2093"><font size="1">2094</font></a>
  Take ye each his sacrifices, and enter ye into the courts <a name="fr_2094"></a><a href="#fn_2094"><font size="1">2095</font></a>
  of the Lord, not of necessity, having power over your own will. <a name="fr_2095"></a><a href="#fn_2095"><font size="1">2096</font></a>
  For not as, “Thou shall not commit adultery, Thou shall not kill,” <a name="fr_2096"></a><a href="#fn_2096"><font size="1">2097</font></a>
  can it so be said, Thou shalt not wed. The former are demanded, the latter are offered. If the latter are done, they are praised: unless the former are done, they are condemned. In the former the Lord commands us what is due; but in the latter, if ye shall have spent any thing more, on His return He will repay you. <a name="fr_2097"></a><a href="#fn_2097"><font size="1">2098</font></a>
  Think of (whatever that be) within His wall “a place named, much better than of sons and of daughters.” <a name="fr_2098"></a><a href="#fn_2098"><font size="1">2099</font></a>
  Think of “an eternal name” there. <a name="fr_2099"></a><a href="#fn_2099"><font size="1">2100</font></a>
  Who unfolds of what kind that name shall be? Yet, whatever it shall be, it shall be eternal. By believing and hoping and loving this, ye have been able, not to shun marriage, as forbidden, but to fly past it, as allowed.</p>
