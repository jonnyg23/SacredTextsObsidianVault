 </p><p class="c39"><a name="iv.i.v.vii-p1"></a>  <span class="c2"><a name="iv.i.v.vii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 6.—Diversity Alone Makes a Miracle.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.i.v.vii-p2"></a>And who is it that restored to the corpses their proper souls when the dead rose again, <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">379</font></a>
  unless He who gives life to the flesh in the mothers womb, in order that they may come into being who yet are to die? But when such things happen in a continuous kind of river of ever-flowing succession, passing from the hidden to the visible, and from the visible to the hidden, by a regular and beaten track, then they are called natural; when, for the admonition of men, they are thrust in by an unusual changeableness, then they are called miracles.</p>
