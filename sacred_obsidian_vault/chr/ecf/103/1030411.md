 </p><p class="c39"><a name="iv.iv.ix-p1"></a>  <span class="c2"><a name="iv.iv.ix-p1.1"></a>Chapter 8.—Of Christs Coming to Judgment.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iv.iv.ix-p2"></a>15. We believe also, that at the most seasonable time <span class="c19"><a name="iv.iv.ix-p2.1"></a>He Will Come from Thence, and Will Judge the Quick and the Dead</span>: whether by these terms are signified the righteous and sinners, or whether it be the case that those persons are here called the <i>quick</i>, whom at that period He shall find, previous to [their] death, <a name="fr_1585"></a><a href="#fn_1585"><font size="1">1586</font></a>
  upon the earth, while the <i>dead</i> denote those who shall rise again at His advent. This temporal dispensation not only <i>is</i>, as holds good of that generation which respects His being God, but also <i> hath been</i> and <i>shall be</i>. For our Lord hath been upon the earth, and at present He is in heaven, and [hereafter] He <i>shall be</i> in His brightness as the Judge of the quick and the dead. For He shall yet come, even so as He has ascended, according to the authority which is contained in the Acts of the Apostles. <a name="fr_1586"></a><a href="#fn_1586"><font size="1">1587</font></a>
  It is in accordance with this temporal dispensation, therefore, that He speaks in the Apocalypse, where it is written in this wise: “These things saith He, who is, and who was, and who is to come.” <a name="fr_1587"></a><a href="#fn_1587"><font size="1">1588</font></a>
