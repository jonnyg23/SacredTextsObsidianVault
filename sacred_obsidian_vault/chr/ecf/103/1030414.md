 </p><p class="c36"><a name="iv.v-p1"></a>   <span class="c7"><a name="iv.v-p1.1"></a>Concerning Faith of Things Not Seen</span></p>  
 <p class="c48"><a name="iv.v-p2"></a><span class="c2"><a name="iv.v-p2.1"></a>[De Fide Rerum Quæ Non Videntur.]</span></p>  
 <p class="c49"><a name="iv.v-p3"></a>This tract was thought spurious by some, but is known to be St. Augustins by his mention of it in Ep. ccxxxi.<a name="iv.v-p3.1"></a> <i>ad Darium Comitem</i>. It seems to have been written after 399, from what is said about Idols, § 10; for in that year Honorius enacted laws against them.—<i>From Bened. Ed.</i></p>  
 <p class="c49"><a name="iv.v-p4"></a>The reader of Butlers Analogy will recognise many similar turns of thought.</p>
