 <p> <a name="vii.xxv"></a>
 </p><p class="c53"><a name="vii.xxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c1"><a name="vii.xxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIV.</span></p>  
 <p class="c54"><a name="vii.xxv-p2"></a>Proof of the absurdity of the refusal to glorify the Spirit, from the comparison of things glorified in creation.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="vii.xxv-p3"></a>55.  <span class="c14"><a name="vii.xxv-p3.1"></a>Furthermore</span> man is “crowned with glory and honour,” <a name="fr_1187"></a><a href="#fn_1187"><font size="1">1188</font></a>
  and “glory, honour and peace” are laid up by promise “to every man that worketh good.” <a name="fr_1188"></a><a href="#fn_1188"><font size="1">1189</font></a>
   There is moreover a special and peculiar glory for Israelites “to whom,” it is said “pertaineth the adoption and the glory…and the service,” <a name="fr_1189"></a><a href="#fn_1189"><font size="1">1190</font></a>
  and the Psalmist speaks of a certain glory of his own, “that my glory may sing praise to Thee <a name="fr_1190"></a><a href="#fn_1190"><font size="1">1191</font></a>
 ;” and again “Awake up my glory” <a name="fr_1191"></a><a href="#fn_1191"><font size="1">1192</font></a>
  and according to the Apostle there is a certain glory of sun and moon and stars, <a name="fr_1192"></a><a href="#fn_1192"><font size="1">1193</font></a>
  and “the ministration of condemnation is glorious.” <a name="fr_1193"></a><a href="#fn_1193"><font size="1">1194</font></a>
   While then so many things are glorified, do you wish the Spirit alone of all things to be unglorified?  Yet the Apostle says “the ministration of the Spirit is glorious.” <a name="fr_1194"></a><a href="#fn_1194"><font size="1">1195</font></a>
   How then can He Himself be unworthy of glory?  How according to the Psalmist can the glory of the just man be great <a name="fr_1195"></a><a href="#fn_1195"><font size="1">1196</font></a>
  and according to you the glory of the Spirit none?  How is there not a plain peril from such arguments of our bringing on ourselves the sin from which there is no escape?  If the man who is being saved by works of righteousness glorifies even them that fear the Lord <a name="fr_1196"></a><a href="#fn_1196"><font size="1">1197</font></a>
  much less would he deprive the Spirit of the glory which is His due.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vii.xxv-p14"></a>Grant, they say, that He is to be glorified, but not with the Father and the Son.  But what reason is there in giving up the place appointed by the Lord for the Spirit, and inventing some other?  What reason is there for robbing of His share of glory Him Who is everywhere associated with the Godhead; in the confession of the Faith, in the baptism of redemption, in the working of miracles, in the indwelling of the saints, in the graces bestowed on obedience?  For there is not even one single gift which reaches creation without the Holy Ghost; <a name="fr_1197"></a><a href="#fn_1197"><font size="1">1198</font></a>
  when not even a single word can be spoken in defence of Christ except by them that are aided by the Spirit, as we have learnt in the Gospels from our Lord and Saviour. <a name="fr_1198"></a><a href="#fn_1198"><font size="1">1199</font></a>
   And I know not whether any one who has been partaker of the Holy Spirit will consent that we should overlook all this, forget His fellowship in all things, and tear the Spirit asunder from the Father and the Son.  Where then are we to take Him and rank Him?  With the creature?  Yet all the creature is in bondage, but the Spirit maketh free.  “And where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is liberty.” <a name="fr_1199"></a><a href="#fn_1199"><font size="1">1200</font></a>
   Many arguments might be adduced to them that it is unseemly to coordinate the Holy Spirit with created nature, but for the present I will pass them by.  Were I indeed to bring forward, in a manner befitting the <a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 36</font></a> <a name="vii.xxv-Page_36"></a>dignity of the discussion, all the proofs always available on our side, and so overthrow the objections of our opponents, a lengthy dissertation would be required, and my readers might be worn out by my prolixity.  I therefore propose to reserve this matter for a special treatise, <a name="fr_1200"></a><a href="#fn_1200"><font size="1">1201</font></a>
  and to apply myself to the points now more immediately before us.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vii.xxv-p19"></a>56.  Let us then examine the points one by one.  He is good by nature, in the same way as the Father is good, and the Son is good; the creature on the other hand shares in goodness by choosing the good.  He knows “The deep things of God;” <a name="fr_1201"></a><a href="#fn_1201"><font size="1">1202</font></a>
  the creature receives the manifestation of ineffable things through the Spirit.  He quickens together with God, who produces and preserves all things alive, <a name="fr_1202"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">1203</font></a>
  and together with the Son, who gives life.  “He that raised up Christ from the dead,” it is said, “shall also quicken your mortal bodies by the spirit that dwelleth in you;” <a name="fr_1203"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">1204</font></a>
  and again “my sheep hear my voice,…and I give unto them eternal life;” <a name="fr_1204"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">1205</font></a>
  but “the Spirit” also, it is said, “giveth life,” <a name="fr_1205"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">1206</font></a>
  and again “the Spirit,” it is said, “is life, because of righteousness.” <a name="fr_1206"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">1207</font></a>
   And the Lord bears witness that “it is the Spirit that quickeneth; the flesh profiteth nothing.” <a name="fr_1207"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">1208</font></a>
   How then shall we alienate the Spirit from His quickening power, and make Him belong to lifeless nature?  Who is so contentious, who is so utterly without the heavenly gift, <a name="fr_1208"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">1209</font></a>
  and unfed by Gods good words, who is so devoid of part and lot in eternal hopes, as to sever the Spirit from the Godhead and rank Him with the creature?</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vii.xxv-p28"></a>57.  Now it is urged that the Spirit is in us as a gift from God, and that the gift is not reverenced with the same honour as that which is attributed to the giver.  The Spirit is a gift of God, but a gift of life, for the law of “the Spirit of life,” it is said, “hath made” us “free;” <a name="fr_1209"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">1210</font></a>
  and a gift of power, for “ye shall receive power after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you.” <a name="fr_1210"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">1211</font></a>
   Is He on this account to be lightly esteemed?  Did not God also bestow His Son as a free gift to mankind?  “He that spared not His own Son,” it is said, “but delivered Him up for us all, how shall He not with Him also freely give us all things?” <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1211"><font size="1">1212</font></a>
   And in another place, “that we might truly know the things that are freely given us of God,” <a name="fr_1212"></a><a href="#fn_1212"><font size="1">1213</font></a>
  in reference to the mystery of the Incarnation.  It follows then that the maintainers of such arguments, in making the greatness of Gods loving kindness an occasion of blasphemy, have really surpassed the ingratitude of the Jews.  They find fault with the Spirit because He gives us freedom to call God our Father.  “For God hath sent forth the Spirit of His Son into” our “hearts crying Abba, Father,” <a name="fr_1213"></a><a href="#fn_1213"><font size="1">1214</font></a>
  that the voice of the Spirit may become the very voice of them that have received him.</p>
