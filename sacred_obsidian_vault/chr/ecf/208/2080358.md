 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p1.1"></a>Letter CCLXXXV. <a name="fr_3243"></a><a href="#fn_3243"><font size="1">3244</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c54"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p3"></a>Without Address.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p4"></a><span class="c14"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p4.1"></a>The</span> hearer of this letter is one on whom rests the care of our Church and the management of its property—our beloved son.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p5"></a>Deign to grant him freedom of speech on those points that are referred to your holiness, and attention to the expression of his own views; so shall our Church at length recover herself, and henceforth be released from this many-headed Hydra.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="ix.cclxxxvi-p6"></a>Our property is our poverty; so much so that we are ever in search of one to relieve us of it; for the expenses of the Church property amount to more than any profit that she derives from it.</p>
