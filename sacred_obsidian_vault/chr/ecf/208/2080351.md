 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.cclxxix-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.cclxxix-p1.1"></a>Letter CCLXXVIII. <a name="fr_3234"></a><a href="#fn_3234"><font size="1">3235</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c54"><a name="ix.cclxxix-p3"></a>To Valerianus.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.cclxxix-p4"></a>I <span class="c14"><a name="ix.cclxxix-p4.1"></a>desired</span>, when in Orphanene, <a name="fr_3235"></a><a href="#fn_3235"><font size="1">3236</font></a>
  to see your excellency; I had also hoped that while you were living at Corsagæna, there would have been nothing to hinder your coming to me at a synod which I had expected to hold at Attagæna; since, however, I failed to hold it, my desire was to see you in the hill-country; for here again Evesus, <a name="fr_3236"></a><a href="#fn_3236"><font size="1">3237</font></a>
  being in that neighbourhood, held out hopes of our meeting.  But since I have been disappointed on both occasions, I determined to write and beg that you would deign to visit me; for I think it is but right and proper that the young man should come to the old.  Furthermore, at our meeting, I would make you a tender of my advice, touching your negotiations with certain at Cæsarea:  a right conclusion of the matter calls for my intervention.  If agreeable then, do not be backward in coming to me.</p>
