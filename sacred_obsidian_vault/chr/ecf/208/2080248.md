 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.clxxvi-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.clxxvi-p1.1"></a>Letter CLXXV. <a name="fr_2575"></a><a href="#fn_2575"><font size="1">2576</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="ix.clxxvi-p3"></a><i>To Count Magnenianus</i>. <a name="fr_2576"></a><a href="#fn_2576"><font size="1">2577</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.clxxvi-p5"></a><span class="c14"><a name="ix.clxxvi-p5.1"></a>Your</span> excellency lately wrote to me, plainly charging me, besides other matters, to write concerning the Faith.  I admire your zeal in the matter, and I pray God that your choice of good things may be persistent, and that, advancing in knowledge and good works, you may be made perfect.  But I have no wish to leave behind me a treatise on the Faith, or to write various creeds, and so I have declined to send what you asked. <a name="fr_2577"></a><a href="#fn_2577"><font size="1">2578</font></a>
   You seem to me to be surrounded by the din of your men there, idle fellows, who say certain things to calumniate me, with the idea that they will improve their own position by lying disgracefully against me. <a name="fr_2578"></a><a href="#fn_2578"><font size="1">2579</font></a>
   The past shews what they are, and future experience will shew them in still plainer colours.  I, however, call on all who trust in Christ not to busy themselves in opposition to the ancient faith, but, as we believe, so to be baptized, and, as we are baptized, so to offer the doxology. <a name="fr_2579"></a><a href="#fn_2579"><font size="1">2580</font></a>
   It is enough for us to confess those names which we have received from Holy Scripture, and to shun all innovation about them.  Our salvation does not lie in the invention of modes of address, but in the sound confession of the Godhead in which we have professed our faith.</p>
