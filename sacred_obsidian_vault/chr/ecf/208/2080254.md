 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.clxxxii-p1"></a>  <a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 222</font></a> <a name="ix.clxxxii-Page_222"></a><span class="c18"><a name="ix.clxxxii-p1.1"></a>Letter CLXXXI. <a name="fr_2592"></a><a href="#fn_2592"><font size="1">2593</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="ix.clxxxii-p3"></a><i>To Otreius, bishop of Melitene</i>. <a name="fr_2593"></a><a href="#fn_2593"><font size="1">2594</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.clxxxii-p5"></a><span class="c14"><a name="ix.clxxxii-p5.1"></a>Your</span> reverence is, I know, no less distressed than myself at the removal of the very God-beloved bishop Eusebius.  We both of us need comfort.  Let us try to give it to one another.  Do you write to me what you hear from Samosata, and I will report to you anything that I may learn from Thrace. <a name="fr_2594"></a><a href="#fn_2594"><font size="1">2595</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="ix.clxxxii-p7"></a>It is to me no slight alleviation of our present distress to know the constancy of the people.  It will be the same to you to have news of our common father.  Of course I cannot now tell you this by letter, but I commend to you one who is fully informed, and will report to you in what condition he left him, and how he bears his troubles.  Pray, then, for him and for me that the Lord will grant him speedy release from his distress.</p>
