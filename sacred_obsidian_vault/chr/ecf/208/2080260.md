 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.clxxxviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.clxxxviii-p1.1"></a>Letter CLXXXVII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="ix.clxxxviii-p2"></a><i>Antipater to Basil</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.clxxxviii-p3"></a>“<span class="c14"><a name="ix.clxxxviii-p3.1"></a>Twice</span> cabbage is death,” says the unkind proverb.  I, however, though I have called for it often, shall die once.  Yes:  even though I had never called for it at all!  If you do die anyhow, dont fear to eat a delicious relish, unjustly reviled by the proverb!</p>
