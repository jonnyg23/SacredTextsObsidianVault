 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.ccclii-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.ccclii-p1.1"></a>Letter CCCLVII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c54"><a name="ix.ccclii-p2"></a>Libanius to Basil.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.ccclii-p3"></a><span class="c14"><a name="ix.ccclii-p3.1"></a>What</span> has made Basil object to the letter, the proof of philosophy?  I have learned to make fun from you, but nevertheless your fun is venerable and, so to say, hoary with age.  But, by our very friendship, by our common pastimes, do away, I charge you, with the distress caused by your letter…in nothing differing. <a name="fr_3286"></a><a href="#fn_3286"><font size="1">3287</font></a>
