 <p> <a name="ii.XX"></a>  
 </p><p class="c10"><a name="ii.XX-p1"></a> <a name="ii.XX-p1.1"></a><span class="c20"><a name="ii.XX-p1.2"></a>Psalm XX. <a name="fr_566"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">567</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c32"><a name="ii.XX-p3"></a>To the end, a psalm of David.</p>  
 <p class="c26"><a name="ii.XX-p4"></a>1. This is a well-known title; and it is not Christ who speaks; but the prophet speaks to Christ, under the form of wishing, foretelling things to come. <a name="fr_567"></a><a href="#fn_567"><font size="1">568</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p6"></a>2. “The Lord hear Thee in the day of trouble” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#001">Psa. 20.1</a><a name="ii.XX-p6.1"></a>). The Lord hear Thee in the day in which Thou saidst, “Father glorify Thy Son.” <a name="fr_568"></a><a href="#fn_568"><font size="1">569</font></a>
  “The name of the God of Jacob protect Thee.” For to Thee belongeth the younger people. Since “the elder shall serve the younger.” <a name="fr_569"></a><a href="#fn_569"><font size="1">570</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p9"></a>3. “Send Thee help from the Holy, and from Sion defend Thee” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#002">Psa. 20.2</a><a name="ii.XX-p9.1"></a>). Making for Thee a sanctified Body, the Church, from watching <a name="fr_570"></a><a href="#fn_570"><font size="1">571</font></a>
  safe, which waiteth when Thou shalt come from the wedding.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p11"></a>4. “Be mindful of all Thy sacrifice” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#003">Psa. 20.3</a><a name="ii.XX-p11.1"></a>). Make us mindful of all Thy injuries and despiteful treatment, which Thou hast borne for us. “And be Thy whole burnt offering made fat.” And turn the cross, whereon Thou wast wholly offered up to God, into the joy of the resurrection.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p12"></a>5. “<i>Diapsalma.</i>” The Lord render to Thee according to Thine Heart” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#004">Psa. 20.4</a><a name="ii.XX-p12.1"></a>). The Lord render to Thee, not according to their heart, who thought by persecution they could destroy Thee; but according to Thine Heart, wherein Thou knewest what profit Thy passion would have. <a name="fr_571"></a><a href="#fn_571"><font size="1">572</font></a>
  “And fulfil all Thy counsel.” “And fulfil all Thy counsel,” not only that whereby Thou didst lay down Thy life for Thy friends, <a name="fr_572"></a><a href="#fn_572"><font size="1">573</font></a>
  that the corrupted grain might rise again to more abundance; <a name="fr_573"></a><a href="#fn_573"><font size="1">574</font></a>
  but that also whereby “blindness in part hath happened unto Israel, that the fulness of the Gentiles might enter in, and so all Israel might be saved.” <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="#fn_574"><font size="1">575</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p17"></a>6. “We will exult in Thy salvation” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#005">Psa. 20.5</a><a name="ii.XX-p17.1"></a>). We will exult in that death will in no wise hurt Thee; for so Thou wilt also show that it cannot hurt us either. “And in the name of the Lord our God will we be magnified.” And the confession of Thy name shall not only not destroy us, but shall even magnify us.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p18"></a>7. “The Lord fulfil all Thy petitions.” The Lord fulfil not only the petitions which Thou madest on earth, but those also whereby Thou intercedest for us in heaven. “Now have I known that the Lord hath saved his Christ” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#006">Psa. 20.6</a><a name="ii.XX-p18.1"></a>). Now hath it been shown to me in prophecy, that the Lord will raise up His Christ again. “He will hear Him from His holy heaven.” He will hear Him not from earth only, where He  prayed to be glorified; <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_575"><font size="1">576</font></a>
  but from heaven also, where interceding for us at the Right Hand of the Father, <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_576"><font size="1">577</font></a>
  He hath from thence shed abroad the Holy Spirit on them that believe on Him. “In strength is the safety of His right hand.” Our strength is in the safety of His favour, when even out of tribulation He giveth help, that “when we are weak, then we may be strong.” <a name="fr_577"></a><a href="#fn_577"><font size="1">578</font></a>
  “For vain is” that “safety of man,” <a name="fr_578"></a><a href="#fn_578"><font size="1">579</font></a>
  which comes not of His right hand but of His left: for thereby are they lifted up to great pride, whosoever in their sins have secured a temporal safety.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p23"></a>8. “Some in chariots, and some in horses” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#007">Psa. 20.7</a><a name="ii.XX-p23.1"></a>). Some are drawn away by the ever moving succession of temporal goods; and some are preferred to proud honours, and in them exult: “But we will exult in the name of the Lord our God.” But we, fixing our hope on things eternal, and not seeking our own glory, will exult in the name of the Lord our God.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p24"></a>9. “They have been bound, and fallen” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#008">Psa. 20.8</a><a name="ii.XX-p24.1"></a>). And therefore were they bound by the lust of temporal things, fearing to spare the <a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a> <a name="ii.XX-Page_57"></a>Lord, lest they should lose their place by “the Romans:” <a name="fr_579"></a><a href="#fn_579"><font size="1">580</font></a>
  and rushing violently on the stone of offence and rock of stumbling, they fell from the heavenly hope: to whom the blindness in part of Israel hath happened, being ignorant of Gods righteousness, and wishing to establish their own. <a name="fr_580"></a><a href="#fn_580"><font size="1">581</font></a>
  “But we are risen, and stand upright.” But we, that the Gentile people might enter in, out of the stones raised up as children to Abraham, <a name="fr_581"></a><a href="#fn_581"><font size="1">582</font></a>
  who followed not after righteousness, have attained to it, and are risen; <a name="fr_582"></a><a href="#fn_582"><font size="1">583</font></a>
  and not by our own strength, but being justified by faith, we stand upright.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.XX-p29"></a>10. “O Lord, save the King:” that He, who in His Passion hath shown us an example of conflict, should also offer up our sacrifices, the Priest raised from the dead, and established in heaven. “And hear us in the day when we shall call on Thee” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#009">Psa. 20.9</a><a name="ii.XX-p29.1"></a>). And as He now offereth for us, “hear us in the day when we shall call on Thee.”</p>
