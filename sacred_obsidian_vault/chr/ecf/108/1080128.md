 </p><p class="c10"><a name="ii.CXVII-p1"></a> <a name="ii.CXVII-p1.1"></a><span class="c20"><a name="ii.CXVII-p1.2"></a>Psalm CXVII. <a name="fr_5097"></a><a href="#fn_5097"><font size="1">5098</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c26"><a name="ii.CXVII-p3"></a>1. “O praise the Lord, all ye heathen: praise Him, all ye nations” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa117.htm#001">Psa. 117.1</a><a name="ii.CXVII-p3.1"></a>). These are the courts of the Lords house, this all His people, <a name="page_557"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 557</font></a> <a name="ii.CXVII-Page_557"></a>this the true Jerusalem. Let those rather listen who have refused to be the children of this city, since they have cut themselves off from the communion of all nations. <a name="fr_5098"></a><a href="#fn_5098"><font size="1">5099</font></a>
  “For His merciful kindness is ever more and more towards us: and the truth of the Lord endureth for ever” (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa117.htm#002">Psa. 117.2</a><a name="ii.CXVII-p4.1"></a>). These are those two things, loving-kindness and truth, which in the <span class="c21"><a name="ii.CXVII-p4.2"></a> CXV</span>th Psalm I admonished you should be committed to memory. But “the merciful kindness of the Lord is ever more and more towards us,” since the furious tongues of hostile nations have  yielded to His Name, through which we have been freed: “and the truth of the Lord endureth for ever,” whether in those things which He promised to the righteous, or in those which He hath threatened to the ungodly.</p>
