 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xvi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVI.—Of the Second Baptism—With Blood.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xvi-p2"></a>We <i>have</i> indeed, likewise, a <i>second</i> font, <a name="fr_8698"></a><a href="#fn_8698"><font size="1">8699</font></a>
  (itself withal <i>one with the former</i>,) of <i>blood</i>, to wit; concerning which the Lord said, “I have to be baptized with a baptism,” <a name="fr_8699"></a><a href="#fn_8699"><font size="1">8700</font></a>
  when He had been baptized already. For He had come “by means of water and blood,” <a name="fr_8700"></a><a href="#fn_8700"><font size="1">8701</font></a>
  just as John has written; that He might be baptized by the water, glorified by the blood; to make <i>us</i>, in like manner, <i>called</i> by <i>water, chosen</i> <a name="fr_8701"></a><a href="#fn_8701"><font size="1">8702</font></a>
  by <i>blood</i>. These two baptisms He sent out from the wound in His pierced side, <a name="fr_8702"></a><a href="#fn_8702"><font size="1">8703</font></a>
  in order that they who believed in His blood might be bathed with the water; they who had been bathed in the water might likewise drink the blood. <a name="fr_8703"></a><a href="#fn_8703"><font size="1">8704</font></a>
  This is the baptism which both stands in lieu of the fontal bathing <a name="fr_8704"></a><a href="#fn_8704"><font size="1">8705</font></a>
  when that has not been received, and restores it when lost.</p>
