 <p> <a name="vi.ii.viii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.ii.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.ii.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Examples from Scripture to Prove the Lords Willingness to Pardon.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.ii.viii-p2"></a>This if you doubt, unravel <a name="fr_8488"></a><a href="#fn_8488"><font size="1">8489</font></a>
  the meaning of “what the Spirit saith to the churches.” <a name="fr_8489"></a><a href="#fn_8489"><font size="1">8490</font></a>
  He imputes to the Ephesians “forsaken love;” <a name="fr_8490"></a><a href="#fn_8490"><font size="1">8491</font></a>
  reproaches the Thyatirenes with “fornication,” and “eating of things sacrificed to idols;” <a name="fr_8491"></a><a href="#fn_8491"><font size="1">8492</font></a>
  accuses the Sardians of “works not full;” <a name="fr_8492"></a><a href="#fn_8492"><font size="1">8493</font></a>
  censures the Pergamenes for teaching perverse things; <a name="fr_8493"></a><a href="#fn_8493"><font size="1">8494</font></a>
  upbraids the Laodiceans for trusting to their riches; <a name="fr_8494"></a><a href="#fn_8494"><font size="1">8495</font></a>
  and yet gives them all general monitions to repentance—under comminations, it is true; but He would not utter comminations to one <i>un</i>repentant if He did not forgive the repentant. The matter were doubtful if He had not withal elsewhere demonstrated this profusion of His clemency. Saith He not, <a name="fr_8495"></a><a href="#fn_8495"><font size="1">8496</font></a>
  “He who hath fallen shall rise again, and he who hath been <i>a</i>verted shall be <i>con</i>verted?” He it is, indeed, who “would have mercy rather than sacrifices.” <a name="fr_8496"></a><a href="#fn_8496"><font size="1">8497</font></a>
  The heavens, and the angels who are there, are glad at a mans repentance. <a name="fr_8497"></a><a href="#fn_8497"><font size="1">8498</font></a>
  Ho! you sinner, be of good cheer! you see where it is that there is joy at your return.  What meaning for us have those themes of the Lords parables? Is not the fact that a woman has lost a drachma, and seeks it and finds it, and invites her female friends to share her joy, an example of a restored sinner? <a name="fr_8498"></a><a href="#fn_8498"><font size="1">8499</font></a>
  There strays, withal, one little ewe of the shepherds; but the flock was not more dear than the one: that one is earnestly sought; the one is longed for instead of all; and at length she is found, and is borne back on the shoulders of the shepherd himself; for much had she toiled <a name="fr_8499"></a><a href="#fn_8499"><font size="1">8500</font></a>
  in straying. <a name="fr_8500"></a><a href="#fn_8500"><font size="1">8501</font></a>
  That most gentle father, likewise, I will not pass over in silence, who calls his prodigal son home, and willingly receives him repentant after his indigence, slays his best fatted calf, and graces his joy with a banquet. <a name="fr_8501"></a><a href="#fn_8501"><font size="1">8502</font></a>
  Why not?  He had found the son whom he had lost; he had felt <i>him</i> to be all the dearer of whom he had <i>made a gain</i>. Who is that father to be understood by us to be?  God, surely: no one <i>is</i> so truly a Father; <a name="fr_8502"></a><a href="#fn_8502"><font size="1">8503</font></a>
  no one so rich in paternal love. He, then, will receive you, His own son, <a name="fr_8503"></a><a href="#fn_8503"><font size="1">8504</font></a>
  back, even if you have squandered what you had received from Him, even if you return naked—just because you <i>have</i> returned; and will joy more over your return than over the sobriety of the other; <a name="fr_8504"></a><a href="#fn_8504"><font size="1">8505</font></a>
  but <i>only</i> if you heartily repent—if you compare your own hunger with the plenty of your Fathers “hired servants”—if you leave behind you the swine, that unclean herd—if you again seek your Father, offended though He be, saying, “I have sinned, nor am worthy any longer to be called Thine.”  Confession of sins lightens, as much as dissimulation aggravates them; for confession is counselled by <a name="page_664"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 664</font></a> <a name="vi.ii.viii-Page_664"></a>(a desire to make) satisfaction, dissimulation by contumacy.</p>
