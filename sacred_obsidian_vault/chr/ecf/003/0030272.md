 <p> <a name="v.iii.vi"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.vi-p1.1"></a>Chapter VI.—Heretics are Self-Condemned. Heresy is Self-Will, Whilst Faith is Submission of Our Will to the Divine Authority.  The Heresy of Apelles.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.vi-p2"></a>On this point, however, we dwell no longer, since it is the same Paul who, in his Epistle to the Galatians, counts “heresies” among “the sins of the flesh,” <a name="fr_1896"></a><a href="#fn_1896"><font size="1">1897</font></a>
  who also intimates to Titus, that “a man who is a heretic” must be “rejected after the first admonition,” on the ground that “he that is such is perverted, and committeth sin, as a self-condemned man.” <a name="fr_1897"></a><a href="#fn_1897"><font size="1">1898</font></a>
  Indeed, in almost every epistle, when enjoining on us (the duty) of avoiding false doctrines, he sharply condemns <a name="fr_1898"></a><a href="#fn_1898"><font size="1">1899</font></a>
  heresies. Of these the practical effects <a name="fr_1899"></a><a href="#fn_1899"><font size="1">1900</font></a>
  are false doctrines, called in Greek <i>heresies</i>, <a name="fr_1900"></a><a href="#fn_1900"><font size="1">1901</font></a>
  a word used in the sense of that <i>choice</i> which a man makes when he either teaches them (to others) <a name="fr_1901"></a><a href="#fn_1901"><font size="1">1902</font></a>
  or takes up with them (for himself). <a name="fr_1902"></a><a href="#fn_1902"><font size="1">1903</font></a>
  For this reason it is that he calls the heretic <i>self-condemned</i>, <a name="fr_1903"></a><a href="#fn_1903"><font size="1">1904</font></a>
  because he has himself chosen that <a name="page_246"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 246</font></a> <a name="v.iii.vi-Page_246"></a>for which he is condemned. We, however, are not permitted to cherish any object <a name="fr_1904"></a><a href="#fn_1904"><font size="1">1905</font></a>
  after our own will, nor yet to make choice of that which another has introduced of his private fancy. In the Lords apostles we possess our authority; for even they did not of themselves choose to introduce anything, but faithfully delivered to the nations (of mankind) the doctrine <a name="fr_1905"></a><a href="#fn_1905"><font size="1">1906</font></a>
  which they had received from Christ. If, therefore, even “an angel from heaven should preach any other gospel” (than theirs), he would be called accursed <a name="fr_1906"></a><a href="#fn_1906"><font size="1">1907</font></a>
  by us. The Holy Ghost had even then foreseen that there would be in a certain virgin (called) Philumene <a name="fr_1907"></a><a href="#fn_1907"><font size="1">1908</font></a>
  an angel of deceit, “transformed into an angel of light,” <a name="fr_1908"></a><a href="#fn_1908"><font size="1">1909</font></a>
  by whose miracles and illusions <a name="fr_1909"></a><a href="#fn_1909"><font size="1">1910</font></a>
  Apelles was led (when) he introduced his new heresy.</p>
