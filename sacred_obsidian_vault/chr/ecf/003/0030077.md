 <p> <a name="iv.iv.xix"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="iv.iv.xix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="iv.iv.xix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIX.—Concerning Military Service.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iv.iv.xix-p2"></a>In that last section, decision may seem to have been given likewise concerning military service, which is between dignity and power. <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">314</font></a>
  But now inquiry is made about this point, whether a believer may turn himself unto military service, and whether the military may be admitted unto the faith, even the rank and file, or each inferior grade, to whom there is no necessity for taking part in sacrifices or capital punishments. There is no agreement between the divine and the human sacrament, <a name="fr_314"></a><a href="#fn_314"><font size="1">315</font></a>
  the standard of Christ and the standard of the devil, the camp of light and the camp of darkness. One soul cannot be due to two <i>masters</i>—God and Cæsar. And yet Moses carried a rod, <a name="fr_315"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">316</font></a>
  and Aaron wore a buckle, <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">317</font></a>
  and John (Baptist) is girt with leather <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">318</font></a>
  and Joshua the son of Nun leads a line of march; and the People warred: if it pleases you to sport with the subject. But how will <i>a Christian man</i> war, nay, how will he serve even in peace, without a sword, which the Lord has taken away? <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">319</font></a>
  For albeit soldiers had come unto John, and had received the formula of their rule; <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">320</font></a>
  albeit, likewise, a centurion had believed; <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">321</font></a>
  <i>still</i> the Lord afterward, in disarming Peter, unbe**d every soldier.  No dress is lawful among us, if assigned to any unlawful action.</p>
