 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iv.iii.xix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.iii.xix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIX.—The Minute Prescriptions of the Law Meant to Keep the People Dependent on God. The Prophets Sent by God in Pursuance of His Goodness.  Many Beautiful Passages from Them Quoted in Illustration of This Attribute.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iv.iii.xix-p2"></a>But even in the common transactions of life, and of human intercourse at home and in public, even to the care of the smallest vessels, He in every possible manner made distinct arrangement; in order that, when they everywhere encountered these legal instructions, they might not be at any moment out of the sight of God. For what could better tend to make a man happy, than having “his delight in the law of the Lord?” “In that law would he meditate day and night.” <a name="fr_2921"></a><a href="#fn_2921"><font size="1">2922</font></a>
  It was not in severity that its Author promulgated this law, but in the interest of the highest benevolence, which rather aimed at subduing <a name="fr_2922"></a><a href="#fn_2922"><font size="1">2923</font></a>
  the nations hardness of heart, and by laborious services hewing out a fealty which was (as yet) untried in obedience:  for I purposely abstain from touching on the mysterious senses of the law, considered in its spiritual and prophetic relation, and as abounding in types of almost every variety and sort.  It is enough at present, that it simply bound a man to God, so that no one ought to find fault with it, except him who does not choose to serve God. To help forward this beneficent, not onerous, purpose of the law, the prophets were also ordained by the self-same goodness of God, teaching precepts worthy of God, how that men should “cease to do evil, learn to do well, seek judgment, judge the fatherless, <a name="fr_2923"></a><a href="#fn_2923"><font size="1">2924</font></a>
  and plead for the widow:” <a name="fr_2924"></a><a href="#fn_2924"><font size="1">2925</font></a>
  be fond of the divine expostulations: <a name="fr_2925"></a><a href="#fn_2925"><font size="1">2926</font></a>
  avoid contact with the wicked: <a name="fr_2926"></a><a href="#fn_2926"><font size="1">2927</font></a>
  “let the oppressed go free:” <a name="fr_2927"></a><a href="#fn_2927"><font size="1">2928</font></a>
  dismiss the unjust sentence, <a name="fr_2928"></a><a href="#fn_2928"><font size="1">2929</font></a>
  “deal their bread to the hungry; bring the outcast into their house; cover the naked, when they see him; nor hide themselves from their own flesh and kin:” <a name="fr_2929"></a><a href="#fn_2929"><font size="1">2930</font></a>
  “keep their tongue from evil, and their lips from speaking guile: depart from evil, and do good; seek peace, and pursue it:” <a name="fr_2930"></a><a href="#fn_2930"><font size="1">2931</font></a>
  be angry, and sin not; that is, not persevere in anger, or be enraged: <a name="fr_2931"></a><a href="#fn_2931"><font size="1">2932</font></a>
  “walk not in the counsel of the ungodly; nor stand in the way of sinners; nor sit in the seat of the scornful.” <a name="fr_2932"></a><a href="#fn_2932"><font size="1">2933</font></a>
  Where then?  “Behold, how good and how pleasant it is for brethren to dwell together in unity;” <a name="fr_2933"></a><a href="#fn_2933"><font size="1">2934</font></a>
  meditating (as they do) day and night in the law of the Lord, because “it is better to trust in the Lord than to put confidence in man; better to hope in the Lord than in man.” <a name="fr_2934"></a><a href="#fn_2934"><font size="1">2935</font></a>
  For what recompense shall man receive from God? “He shall be like a tree planted by the rivers of water, that bringeth forth his fruit in his season; his leaf also shall not wither, and whatsoever he doeth shall prosper.” <a name="fr_2935"></a><a href="#fn_2935"><font size="1">2936</font></a>
  “He that hath clean hands and a pure heart, who hath not taken Gods name in vain, nor sworn deceitfully to his neighbour, he shall receive blessing from the Lord, and mercy from the God of his salvation.” <a name="fr_2936"></a><a href="#fn_2936"><font size="1">2937</font></a>
  “For the eyes of the Lord are upon them that fear Him, upon them that hope in His mercy, to deliver their souls from death,” even eternal death, “and to nourish them in their hunger,” that is, after eternal life. <a name="fr_2937"></a><a href="#fn_2937"><font size="1">2938</font></a>
  “Many are the afflictions of the righteous, but the Lord delivereth them out of them all.” <a name="fr_2938"></a><a href="#fn_2938"><font size="1">2939</font></a>
  “Precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of His saints.” <a name="fr_2939"></a><a href="#fn_2939"><font size="1">2940</font></a>
  “The Lord keepeth all their bones; not one of them shall be broken.” <a name="fr_2940"></a><a href="#fn_2940"><font size="1">2941</font></a>
  The Lord will redeem the souls of His servants. <a name="fr_2941"></a><a href="#fn_2941"><font size="1">2942</font></a>
  We have adduced these few quotations from a mass of the Creators Scriptures; and no more, I suppose, are wanted to prove Him to be a most good God, for they sufficiently indicate both the precepts of His goodness and the first-fruits <a name="fr_2942"></a><a href="#fn_2942"><font size="1">2943</font></a>
  thereof.</p>
