 <p> <a name="vi.vii.ii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.vii.ii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.vii.ii-p1.1"></a>Chapter II.—God Himself an Example of Patience.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.vii.ii-p2"></a>To <i>us</i> <a name="fr_9019"></a><a href="#fn_9019"><font size="1">9020</font></a>
  no human affectation of canine <a name="fr_9020"></a><a href="#fn_9020"><font size="1">9021</font></a>
  equanimity, modelled <a name="fr_9021"></a><a href="#fn_9021"><font size="1">9022</font></a>
  by insensibility, furnishes the warrant for exercising patience; but the divine arrangement of a living and celestial discipline, holding up before us God <a name="page_708"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 708</font></a> <a name="vi.vii.ii-Page_708"></a>Himself in the very first place as an example of patience; who scatters equally over just and unjust the bloom of this light; who suffers the good offices of the seasons, the services of the elements, the tributes of entire nature, to accrue at once to worthy and unworthy; bearing with the most ungrateful nations, adoring <i>as they do</i> the toys of the arts and the works of their own hands, persecuting His Name together with His family; <i>bearing with</i> luxury, avarice, iniquity, malignity, waxing insolent daily: <a name="fr_9022"></a><a href="#fn_9022"><font size="1">9023</font></a>
  so that by His own patience He disparages Himself; for the cause why many believe not in the Lord is that they are so long without knowing <a name="fr_9023"></a><a href="#fn_9023"><font size="1">9024</font></a>
  that He is wroth with the world. <a name="fr_9024"></a><a href="#fn_9024"><font size="1">9025</font></a>
 </p>
