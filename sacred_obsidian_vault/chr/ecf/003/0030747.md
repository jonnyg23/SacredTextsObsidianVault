 <p> <a name="vi.iv.iii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.iii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.iii-p1.1"></a>Chapter III.—The Second Clause.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.iii-p2"></a>The name of “God the Father” had been published to none. Even Moses, who had interrogated Him on that very point, had heard a different name. <a name="fr_8772"></a><a href="#fn_8772"><font size="1">8773</font></a>
  To us it has been revealed in the Son, for the Son is now the Fathers new name. “I am come,” saith He, “in the Fathers name;” <a name="fr_8773"></a><a href="#fn_8773"><font size="1">8774</font></a>
  and again, “Father, glorify Thy name;” <a name="fr_8774"></a><a href="#fn_8774"><font size="1">8775</font></a>
  and more openly, “I have manifested Thy name to men.” <a name="fr_8775"></a><a href="#fn_8775"><font size="1">8776</font></a>
  That <i>name</i>, therefore, we pray may “be hallowed.”  Not that it is becoming for men to <i>wish</i> God <i>well</i>, as if there were any other <a name="fr_8776"></a><a href="#fn_8776"><font size="1">8777</font></a>
  by whom He may be wished well, or as if He would suffer unless we do so wish. Plainly, it is universally becoming for God to be <i>blessed</i> <a name="fr_8777"></a><a href="#fn_8777"><font size="1">8778</font></a>
  in every place and time, on account of the memory of His benefits ever due from every man. But this petition also serves the turn of a blessing. Otherwise, when is the name of God not “holy,” and “hallowed” through Himself, seeing that of Himself He sanctifies all others—He to whom that surrounding circle of angels cease not to say, “Holy, holy, holy?” <a name="fr_8778"></a><a href="#fn_8778"><font size="1">8779</font></a>
  In like wise, therefore, we too, candidates for angelhood, if we succeed in deserving it, begin even here on earth to learn by heart that strain hereafter to be raised unto God, and the function of future glory. So far, for the glory of God. On the other hand, for our own petition, when we say, “Hallowed be Thy name,” we pray this; that it may be hallowed <i>in us</i> who are in Him, as well in all others for whom the grace of God is still waiting; <a name="fr_8779"></a><a href="#fn_8779"><font size="1">8780</font></a>
  that we may obey this precept, too, in “praying for all,” <a name="fr_8780"></a><a href="#fn_8780"><font size="1">8781</font></a>
  even for our personal enemies. <a name="fr_8781"></a><a href="#fn_8781"><font size="1">8782</font></a>
  And therefore with suspended utterance, not saying, “Hallowed be it <i>in us</i>,” we say,—“<i>in all</i>.”</p>
