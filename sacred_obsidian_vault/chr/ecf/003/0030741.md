 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIX.—Of the Times Most Suitable for Baptism.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xix-p2"></a>The Passover affords a more <i>than usually</i> solemn day for baptism; when, withal, the Lords passion, in which we are baptized, was completed. Nor will it be incongruous to interpret figuratively <i>the fact</i> that, when the Lord was about to celebrate the last Passover, He said to the disciples who were sent to make preparation, “Ye will meet a man bearing water.” <a name="fr_8736"></a><a href="#fn_8736"><font size="1">8737</font></a>
  He points out the place for celebrating the Passover by the sign of <i>water</i>. After that, Pentecost is a most joyous space <a name="fr_8737"></a><a href="#fn_8737"><font size="1">8738</font></a>
  for conferring baptisms; <a name="fr_8738"></a><a href="#fn_8738"><font size="1">8739</font></a>
  wherein, too, the resurrection of the Lord was repeatedly proved <a name="fr_8739"></a><a href="#fn_8739"><font size="1">8740</font></a>
  among the disciples, and the hope of the advent of the Lord indirectly pointed to, in that, at that time, when He had been received back into the heavens, the angels <a name="fr_8740"></a><a href="#fn_8740"><font size="1">8741</font></a>
  told the apostles that “He would so come, as He had withal ascended into the heavens;” <a name="fr_8741"></a><a href="#fn_8741"><font size="1">8742</font></a>
  at Pentecost, of course. But, moreover, when Jeremiah says, “And I will gather them together from the extremities of the land in the feast-day,” he signifies the day of the Passover and of Pentecost, which is properly a “feast-day.” <a name="fr_8742"></a><a href="#fn_8742"><font size="1">8743</font></a>
  However, <i>every</i> day is the Lords; every hour, every time, is apt for baptism: if there is a difference in the <i>solemnity</i>, distinction there is none in the <i>grace</i>.</p>
