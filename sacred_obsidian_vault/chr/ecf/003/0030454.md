 <p> <a name="v.iv.vi.viii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iv.vi.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.vi.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Man the Image of the Creator, and Christ the Head of the Man.  Spiritual Gifts. The Sevenfold Spirit Described by Isaiah. The Apostle and the Prophet Compared. Marcion Challenged to Produce Anything Like These Gifts of the Spirit Foretold in Prophecy in His God.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iv.vi.viii-p2"></a>“The head of every man is Christ.” <a name="fr_5528"></a><a href="#fn_5528"><font size="1">5529</font></a>
  What Christ, if He is not the author of man? The <i>head</i> he has here put for <i>authority</i>; now “authority” will accrue to none else than the “author.” Of what man indeed is He the head? Surely of him concerning whom he adds soon afterwards: “The man ought not to cover his head, forasmuch as he is the image of God.” <a name="fr_5529"></a><a href="#fn_5529"><font size="1">5530</font></a>
  Since then he is the image of the Creator (for <i>He</i>, when looking on Christ His Word, who was to become man, said, “Let us make man in our own image, after our likeness” <a name="fr_5530"></a><a href="#fn_5530"><font size="1">5531</font></a>
 ), how can I possibly have another head but Him whose image I am? For if I am the image of the Creator there is no room in me for another head. But wherefore “ought the woman to have power over her head, because of the angels?” <a name="fr_5531"></a><a href="#fn_5531"><font size="1">5532</font></a>
  If it is because “she was created for the man,” <a name="fr_5532"></a><a href="#fn_5532"><font size="1">5533</font></a>
  and taken out of the man, according to the Creators purpose, then in this way too has the apostle maintained the discipline of that God from whose institution he explains the reasons of His discipline. He adds:  “Because of the angels.” <a name="fr_5533"></a><a href="#fn_5533"><font size="1">5534</font></a>
  What angels?  In other words, whose angels? If he means the fallen angels of the Creator, <a name="fr_5534"></a><a href="#fn_5534"><font size="1">5535</font></a>
  there is great propriety in his meaning.  It is right that that face which was a snare to them should wear some mark of a humble guise and obscured beauty.  If, however, the angels of the rival god are referred to, what fear is there for them? for not even Marcions disciples, (to say nothing of his angels,) have any desire for women. We have often shown before now, that the apostle classes heresies as evil <a name="fr_5535"></a><a href="#fn_5535"><font size="1">5536</font></a>
  among “works of the flesh,” and that he would have those persons accounted estimable <a name="fr_5536"></a><a href="#fn_5536"><font size="1">5537</font></a>
  who shun heresies as an evil thing. In like manner, when treating of the gospel, <a name="fr_5537"></a><a href="#fn_5537"><font size="1">5538</font></a>
  we have proved from the sacrament of the bread and the cup <a name="fr_5538"></a><a href="#fn_5538"><font size="1">5539</font></a>
  the verity of the Lords body and blood in opposition to Marcions phantom; whilst throughout almost the whole of my work it has been contended that all mention of judicial attributes points conclusively to the Creator as to a God who judges. Now, on the subject of “spiritual gifts,” <a name="fr_5539"></a><a href="#fn_5539"><font size="1">5540</font></a>
  I have to remark that these also were promised by the Creator through Christ; and I think that we may derive from this a very just conclusion that the bestowal of a gift is not the work of a god other than Him who is proved to have given the promise. Here is a prophecy of Isaiah: “There shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a flower <a name="fr_5540"></a><a href="#fn_5540"><font size="1">5541</font></a>
  shall spring up from his root; and upon Him shall rest the Spirit of the Lord.” After which he enumerates the special gifts of the same: “The spirit of wisdom and understanding, the spirit of counsel and might, the spirit of knowledge and of religion. <a name="fr_5541"></a><a href="#fn_5541"><font size="1">5542</font></a>
  And with the fear of the Lord <a name="fr_5542"></a><a href="#fn_5542"><font size="1">5543</font></a>
  shall the Spirit fill Him.” <a name="fr_5543"></a><a href="#fn_5543"><font size="1">5544</font></a>
  In this figure of <i>a flower</i> he shows that Christ was to arise out of the rod which sprang from the stem of Jesse; in other words, from the virgin of the race of David, the son of Jesse. In this Christ the whole <i>substantia</i> of the Spirit would have to rest, not meaning that it would be as it were some subsequent acquisition accruing to Him who was always, even before His incarnation, the Spirit of God; <a name="fr_5544"></a><a href="#fn_5544"><font size="1">5545</font></a>
  so that you cannot argue from this that the prophecy has reference to that Christ who (as mere man of the race only of David) was to obtain the Spirit of his God. (The prophet says,) on the contrary, that from the time when (the true Christ) should appear in the flesh as <i>the flower predicted</i>, <a name="fr_5545"></a><a href="#fn_5545"><font size="1">5546</font></a>
  rising from the root of Jesse, there would have to rest upon Him the entire operation of the Spirit of grace, which, so far as the Jews were concerned, would cease and come to an end. This result the case itself shows; for after this time the Spirit <a name="page_446"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 446</font></a> <a name="v.iv.vi.viii-Page_446"></a>of the Creator never breathed amongst <i>them</i>. From Judah were taken away “the wise man, and the cunning artificer, and the counsellor, and the prophet;” <a name="fr_5546"></a><a href="#fn_5546"><font size="1">5547</font></a>
  that so it might prove true that “the law and the prophets were until John.” <a name="fr_5547"></a><a href="#fn_5547"><font size="1">5548</font></a>
  Now hear how he declared that by Christ Himself, when returned to heaven, these spiritual gifts were to be sent: “He ascended up on high,” that is, into heaven; “He led captivity captive,” meaning death or slavery of man; “He gave gifts to the sons of men,” <a name="fr_5548"></a><a href="#fn_5548"><font size="1">5549</font></a>
  that is, the gratuities, which we call <i><span class="c42"><a name="v.iv.vi.viii-p23.2"></a>charismata</span></i>. He says specifically “<i>sons of men</i>,” <a name="fr_5549"></a><a href="#fn_5549"><font size="1">5550</font></a>
  and not men promiscuously; thus exhibiting to us those who were the children of men truly so called, choice men, apostles.  “For,” says he, “I have begotten you through the gospel;” <a name="fr_5550"></a><a href="#fn_5550"><font size="1">5551</font></a>
  and “Ye are my children, of whom I travail again in birth.” <a name="fr_5551"></a><a href="#fn_5551"><font size="1">5552</font></a>
  Now was absolutely fulfilled that promise of the Spirit which was given by the word of Joel:  “In the last days will I pour out of my Spirit upon all flesh, and their sons and their daughters shall prophesy; and upon my servants and upon my handmaids will I pour out of my Spirit.” <a name="fr_5552"></a><a href="#fn_5552"><font size="1">5553</font></a>
  Since, then, the Creator promised the gift of His Spirit in the latter days; and since Christ has in these last days appeared as the dispenser of spiritual gifts (as the apostle says, “When the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth His Son;” <a name="fr_5553"></a><a href="#fn_5553"><font size="1">5554</font></a>
  and again, “This I say, brethren, that the time is short” <a name="fr_5554"></a><a href="#fn_5554"><font size="1">5555</font></a>
 ), it evidently follows in connection with this prediction of the last days, that this gift of the Spirit belongs to Him who is the Christ of the predicters. Now compare the Spirits specific graces, as they are described by the apostle, and promised by the prophet Isaiah. “To one is given,” says he, “by the Spirit the word of wisdom;” this we see at once is what Isaiah declared to be “the spirit of wisdom.”  “To another, the word of knowledge;” this will be “the (prophets) spirit of understanding and counsel.” “To another, faith by the same Spirit;” this will be “the spirit of religion and the fear of the Lord.” “To another, the gifts of healing, and to another the working of miracles;” this will be “the spirit of might.” “To another prophecy, to another discerning of spirits, to another divers kinds of tongues, to another the interpretation of tongues;” this will be “the spirit of knowledge.” <a name="fr_5555"></a><a href="#fn_5555"><font size="1">5556</font></a>
  See how the apostle agrees with the prophet both in making the distribution of the one Spirit, and in interpreting His special graces. This, too, I may confidently say: he who has likened the unity of our body throughout its manifold and divers members to the compacting together of the various gifts of the Spirit, <a name="fr_5556"></a><a href="#fn_5556"><font size="1">5557</font></a>
  shows also that there is but one Lord of the human body and of the Holy Spirit. This Spirit, (according to the apostles showing,) <a name="fr_5557"></a><a href="#fn_5557"><font size="1">5558</font></a>
  meant not <a name="fr_5558"></a><a href="#fn_5558"><font size="1">5559</font></a>
  that the service <a name="fr_5559"></a><a href="#fn_5559"><font size="1">5560</font></a>
  of these gifts should be in the body, <a name="fr_5560"></a><a href="#fn_5560"><font size="1">5561</font></a>
  nor did He place them in the human body); and on the subject of the superiority of love <a name="fr_5561"></a><a href="#fn_5561"><font size="1">5562</font></a>
  above all these gifts, He even taught the apostle that it was the chief commandment, <a name="fr_5562"></a><a href="#fn_5562"><font size="1">5563</font></a>
  just as Christ has shown it to be: “Thou shalt love the Lord with all thine heart and soul, <a name="fr_5563"></a><a href="#fn_5563"><font size="1">5564</font></a>
  with all thy strength, and with all thy mind, and thy neighbour as thine own self.” <a name="fr_5564"></a><a href="#fn_5564"><font size="1">5565</font></a>
  When he mentions the fact that “<i>it is written in the law</i>,” <a name="fr_5565"></a><a href="#fn_5565"><font size="1">5566</font></a>
  how that the Creator would speak with other tongues and other lips, whilst confirming indeed the gift of tongues by such a mention, he yet cannot be thought to have affirmed that the gift was that of another god by his reference to the Creators prediction. <a name="fr_5566"></a><a href="#fn_5566"><font size="1">5567</font></a>
  In precisely the same manner, <a name="fr_5567"></a><a href="#fn_5567"><font size="1">5568</font></a>
  when enjoining on women silence in the church, that they speak not for the mere sake <a name="fr_5568"></a><a href="#fn_5568"><font size="1">5569</font></a>
  of learning <a name="fr_5569"></a><a href="#fn_5569"><font size="1">5570</font></a>
  (although that even they have the right of prophesying, he has already shown <a name="fr_5570"></a><a href="#fn_5570"><font size="1">5571</font></a>
  when he covers the woman that prophesies with a veil), he goes to the law for his sanction that woman should be under obedience. <a name="fr_5571"></a><a href="#fn_5571"><font size="1">5572</font></a>
  Now this law, let me say once for all, he ought to have made no other acquaintance with, than to destroy it. But that we may now leave the subject of spiritual gifts, facts themselves will be enough to prove which of us acts rashly in claiming them for his God, and whether it is possible that they are opposed to our side, even if <a name="fr_5572"></a><a href="#fn_5572"><font size="1">5573</font></a>
  the Creator promised them for His Christ who is not yet revealed, as being destined only for the Jews, to have their operations in His time, in His Christ, and among His people. Let Marcion <a name="page_447"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 447</font></a> <a name="v.iv.vi.viii-Page_447"></a>then exhibit, as gifts of his god, some prophets, such as have not spoken by human sense, but with the Spirit of God, such as have both predicted things to come, and have made manifest <a name="fr_5573"></a><a href="#fn_5573"><font size="1">5574</font></a>
  the secrets of the heart; <a name="fr_5574"></a><a href="#fn_5574"><font size="1">5575</font></a>
  let him produce a psalm, a vision, a prayer <a name="fr_5575"></a><a href="#fn_5575"><font size="1">5576</font></a>
 —only let it be by the Spirit, <a name="fr_5576"></a><a href="#fn_5576"><font size="1">5577</font></a>
  in an ecstasy, that is, in a rapture, <a name="fr_5577"></a><a href="#fn_5577"><font size="1">5578</font></a>
  whenever an interpretation of tongues has occurred to him; let him show to me also, that any woman of boastful tongue <a name="fr_5578"></a><a href="#fn_5578"><font size="1">5579</font></a>
  in his community has ever prophesied from amongst those specially holy sisters of his. Now all these signs (of spiritual gifts) are forthcoming from my side without any difficulty, and they agree, too, with the rules, and the dispensations, and the instructions of the Creator; therefore without doubt the Christ, and the Spirit, and the apostle, belong severally <a name="fr_5579"></a><a href="#fn_5579"><font size="1">5580</font></a>
  to my God. Here, then, is my frank avowal for any one who cares to require it.</p>
