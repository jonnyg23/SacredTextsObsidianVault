 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.xxxvii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.xxxvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXVII.—Other Turgid and Ridiculous Theories About the Origin of the Æons and Creation, Stated and Condemned.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.xxxvii-p2"></a>Now listen to some other buffooneries <a name="fr_6923"></a><a href="#fn_6923"><font size="1">6924</font></a>
  of a master who is a great swell among them, <a name="fr_6924"></a><a href="#fn_6924"><font size="1">6925</font></a>
  and who has pronounced his <i>dicta</i> with an even priestly authority. They run thus: There comes, says he, before all things Pro-arche, the inconceivable, and indescribable, and nameless, which I for my own part call Monotes (<i>Solitude</i>). With this was associated another power, to which also I give the name of Henotes (<i>Unity</i>). Now, inasmuch as Monotes and Henotes—that is to say, Solitude and Union—were only one being, they produced, and yet not in the way of production, <a name="fr_6925"></a><a href="#fn_6925"><font size="1">6926</font></a>
  the intellectual, innascible, invisible beginning of all things, which human language <a name="fr_6926"></a><a href="#fn_6926"><font size="1">6927</font></a>
  has called Monad (<i>Solitude</i>). <a name="fr_6927"></a><a href="#fn_6927"><font size="1">6928</font></a>
  This has inherent in itself a consubstantial force, which it calls Unity <a name="fr_6928"></a><a href="#fn_6928"><font size="1">6929</font></a>
  These powers, accordingly, Solitude or Solitariness, <i>and</i> Unity, or Union, propagated all the other emanations of Æons. <a name="fr_6929"></a><a href="#fn_6929"><font size="1">6930</font></a>
  Wonderful distinction, to be sure! Whatever change Union and Unity may undergo, Solitariness and Solitude is profoundly supreme. Whatever designation you give the power, it is one and the same.</p>
