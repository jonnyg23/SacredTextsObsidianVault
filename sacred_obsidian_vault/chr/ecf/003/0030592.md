 <p> <a name="v.viii.x"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.viii.x-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.viii.x-p1.1"></a>Chapter X.—Holy Scripture Magnifies the Flesh, as to Its Nature and Its Prospects.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.viii.x-p2"></a>You hold to the scriptures in which the flesh is disparaged; receive also those in which it is ennobled. You read whatever passage abases it; direct your eyes also to that which elevates it. “All flesh is grass.” <a name="fr_7351"></a><a href="#fn_7351"><font size="1">7352</font></a>
  Well, but Isaiah was not content to say only this; but he also declared, “All flesh shall see the salvation of God.” <a name="fr_7352"></a><a href="#fn_7352"><font size="1">7353</font></a>
  They notice God when He says in Genesis, “My Spirit shall not remain among these men, because they are flesh;” <a name="fr_7353"></a><a href="#fn_7353"><font size="1">7354</font></a>
  but then He is also heard saying by Joel, “I will pour out of my Spirit upon all flesh.” <a name="fr_7354"></a><a href="#fn_7354"><font size="1">7355</font></a>
  Even the apostle ought not to be known for any one statement in which he is wont to reproach the flesh. For although he says that “in his flesh dwelleth no good thing;” <a name="fr_7355"></a><a href="#fn_7355"><font size="1">7356</font></a>
  although he affirms that “they who are in the flesh cannot please God,” <a name="fr_7356"></a><a href="#fn_7356"><font size="1">7357</font></a>
  because “the flesh lusteth against the Spirit;” <a name="fr_7357"></a><a href="#fn_7357"><font size="1">7358</font></a>
  yet in these and similar assertions which he makes, it is not the <i>substance</i> of the flesh, but its <i>actions</i>, which are censured. Moreover, we shall elsewhere <a name="fr_7358"></a><a href="#fn_7358"><font size="1">7359</font></a>
  take occasion to remark, that no reproaches can fairly be cast upon the flesh, without tending also to the castigation of the soul, which compels the flesh to do its bidding. However, let me meanwhile add that in the same passage Paul “carries about in his body the marks of the Lord Jesus;” <a name="fr_7359"></a><a href="#fn_7359"><font size="1">7360</font></a>
  he also forbids our body to be profaned, as being “the temple of God;” <a name="fr_7360"></a><a href="#fn_7360"><font size="1">7361</font></a>
  he makes our bodies “the members of Christ;” <a name="fr_7361"></a><a href="#fn_7361"><font size="1">7362</font></a>
  and he exhorts us to exalt and “glorify God in our body.” <a name="fr_7362"></a><a href="#fn_7362"><font size="1">7363</font></a>
  If, therefore, the humiliations of the flesh thrust off its resurrection, why shall not its high prerogatives rather avail to bring it about?—since it better suits the character of God to restore to salvation what for a while He rejected, than to surrender to perdition what He once approved.</p>
