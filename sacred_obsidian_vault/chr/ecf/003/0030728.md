 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.vi-p1.1"></a>Chapter VI.—The Angel the Forerunner of the Holy Spirit. Meaning Contained in the Baptismal Formula.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.vi-p2"></a>Not that <i>in</i> <a name="fr_8583"></a><a href="#fn_8583"><font size="1">8584</font></a>
  the waters we obtain the Holy Spirit; but in the water, under (the witness of) the angel, we are cleansed, and prepared <i>for</i> the Holy Spirit. In this case also a type has preceded; for thus was John beforehand the Lords forerunner, “preparing His ways.” <a name="fr_8584"></a><a href="#fn_8584"><font size="1">8585</font></a>
  Thus, too, does the angel, the witness <a name="fr_8585"></a><a href="#fn_8585"><font size="1">8586</font></a>
  of baptism, “make the paths straight” <a name="fr_8586"></a><a href="#fn_8586"><font size="1">8587</font></a>
  for the Holy Spirit, who is about to come upon us, by the washing away of sins, which faith, sealed in (the name of) the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Spirit, obtains. For if “in <i>the mouth of</i> three witnesses every word shall stand:” <a name="fr_8587"></a><a href="#fn_8587"><font size="1">8588</font></a>
 —while, through the benediction, we have the same (three) as witnesses of our faith whom we have as sureties <a name="fr_8588"></a><a href="#fn_8588"><font size="1">8589</font></a>
  of our salvation too—how much more does the number of the divine names suffice for the assurance of our hope likewise!  Moreover, after the pledging both of the attestation of faith and the promise <a name="fr_8589"></a><a href="#fn_8589"><font size="1">8590</font></a>
  of salvation under “three witnesses,” there is added, of necessity, mention of the Church; <a name="fr_8590"></a><a href="#fn_8590"><font size="1">8591</font></a>
  inasmuch as, wherever there are three, (that is, the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, ) there is the Church, which is a body of three. <a name="fr_8591"></a><a href="#fn_8591"><font size="1">8592</font></a>
 </p>
