 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.xi-p1"></a> <a name="page_685"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 685</font></a> <a name="vi.iv.xi-Page_685"></a><span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—When Praying the Father, You are Not to Be Angry with a Brother.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xi-p2"></a>That we may not be as far from the ears of God as we are from His precepts, <a name="fr_8825"></a><a href="#fn_8825"><font size="1">8826</font></a>
  the memory of His precepts paves for our prayers a way unto heaven; of which <i>precepts</i> the chief is, that we go not up unto Gods altar <a name="fr_8826"></a><a href="#fn_8826"><font size="1">8827</font></a>
  before we compose whatever of discord or offence we have contracted with our brethren. <a name="fr_8827"></a><a href="#fn_8827"><font size="1">8828</font></a>
  For what sort of deed is it to approach the peace of God <a name="fr_8828"></a><a href="#fn_8828"><font size="1">8829</font></a>
  without peace? the remission of debts <a name="fr_8829"></a><a href="#fn_8829"><font size="1">8830</font></a>
  while you retain them? How will he appease his <i>Father</i> who is angry with his <i>brother</i>, when from the beginning “all anger” is forbidden us? <a name="fr_8830"></a><a href="#fn_8830"><font size="1">8831</font></a>
  For even Joseph, when dismissing his brethren for the purpose of fetching their father, said, “And be not angry in the way.” <a name="fr_8831"></a><a href="#fn_8831"><font size="1">8832</font></a>
  He warned <i>us</i>, to be sure, at that time (for elsewhere our Discipline is called “the Way” <a name="fr_8832"></a><a href="#fn_8832"><font size="1">8833</font></a>
 ), that when, set in “the way” of prayer, we go not unto “the Father” with anger. After that, the Lord, “amplifying the Law,” <a name="fr_8833"></a><a href="#fn_8833"><font size="1">8834</font></a>
  openly adds <i>the prohibition of</i> anger against a brother to <i>that of</i> murder. <a name="fr_8834"></a><a href="#fn_8834"><font size="1">8835</font></a>
  Not even by an evil word does He permit it to be vented. <a name="fr_8835"></a><a href="#fn_8835"><font size="1">8836</font></a>
  Ever if we <i>must</i> be angry, our anger must not be maintained beyond sunset, as the apostle admonishes. <a name="fr_8836"></a><a href="#fn_8836"><font size="1">8837</font></a>
  But how rash is it either to pass a day without prayer, while you refuse to make satisfaction to your brother; or else, by perseverance in anger, to lose your prayer?</p>
