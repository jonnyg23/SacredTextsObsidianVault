 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iv.v.v-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.v.v-p1.1"></a>Chapter V.—By the Rule of Antiquity, the Catholic Gospels are Found to Be True, Including the Real St. Lukes. Marcions Only a Mutilated Edition. The Heretics Weakness and Inconsistency in Ignoring the Other Gospels. <a name="fr_3584"></a><a href="#fn_3584"><font size="1">3585</font></a>
 </span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iv.v.v-p3"></a>On the whole, then, if that is evidently more true which is earlier, if that is earlier which is from the very beginning, if that is from the beginning which has the apostles for its authors, then it will certainly be quite as evident, that that comes down from the apos<a name="page_350"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 350</font></a> <a name="v.iv.v.v-Page_350"></a>tles, which has been kept as a sacred deposit <a name="fr_3585"></a><a href="#fn_3585"><font size="1">3586</font></a>
  in the churches of the apostles. Let us see what milk the Corinthians drank from Paul; to what rule <i>of faith</i> the Galatians were brought for correction; what the Philippians, the Thessalonians, the Ephesians read <i>by it</i>; what utterance also the Romans give, so very near <a name="fr_3586"></a><a href="#fn_3586"><font size="1">3587</font></a>
  (to the apostles), to whom Peter and Paul conjointly <a name="fr_3587"></a><a href="#fn_3587"><font size="1">3588</font></a>
  bequeathed the gospel even sealed with their own blood. We have also <i>St.</i> Johns foster churches. <a name="fr_3588"></a><a href="#fn_3588"><font size="1">3589</font></a>
  For although Marcion rejects his Apocalypse, the order <a name="fr_3589"></a><a href="#fn_3589"><font size="1">3590</font></a>
  of the bishops (thereof), when traced up to their origin, will yet rest on John as their author. In the same manner is recognised the excellent source <a name="fr_3590"></a><a href="#fn_3590"><font size="1">3591</font></a>
  of the other churches. I say, therefore, that in them (and not simply such of them as were founded by apostles, but in all those which are united with them in the fellowship of the mystery <i>of the gospel of Christ</i> <a name="fr_3591"></a><a href="#fn_3591"><font size="1">3592</font></a>
 ) that Gospel of Luke which we are defending with all our might has stood its ground from its very first publication; whereas Marcions Gospel is not known to most people, and to none whatever is it known without being at the same time <a name="fr_3592"></a><a href="#fn_3592"><font size="1">3593</font></a>
  condemned. It too, of course, <a name="fr_3593"></a><a href="#fn_3593"><font size="1">3594</font></a>
  has its churches, but specially its own—as late as they are spurious; and should you want to know their original, <a name="fr_3594"></a><a href="#fn_3594"><font size="1">3595</font></a>
  you will more easily discover apostasy in it than apostolicity, with Marcion forsooth as their founder, or some one of Marcions swarm. <a name="fr_3595"></a><a href="#fn_3595"><font size="1">3596</font></a>
  Even wasps make combs; <a name="fr_3596"></a><a href="#fn_3596"><font size="1">3597</font></a>
  so also these Marcionites make churches.  The same authority of the apostolic churches will afford evidence <a name="fr_3597"></a><a href="#fn_3597"><font size="1">3598</font></a>
  to the other Gospels also, which we possess equally through their means, <a name="fr_3598"></a><a href="#fn_3598"><font size="1">3599</font></a>
  and according to their usage—I mean the Gospels of John and Matthew—whilst that which Mark published may be affirmed to be Peters <a name="fr_3599"></a><a href="#fn_3599"><font size="1">3600</font></a>
  whose interpreter Mark was. For even Lukes form <a name="fr_3600"></a><a href="#fn_3600"><font size="1">3601</font></a>
  of the Gospel men usually ascribe to Paul. <a name="fr_3601"></a><a href="#fn_3601"><font size="1">3602</font></a>
  And it may well seem <a name="fr_3602"></a><a href="#fn_3602"><font size="1">3603</font></a>
  that the works which disciples publish belong to their masters. Well, then, Marcion ought to be called to a strict account <a name="fr_3603"></a><a href="#fn_3603"><font size="1">3604</font></a>
  concerning these (other Gospels) also, for having omitted them, and insisted in preference <a name="fr_3604"></a><a href="#fn_3604"><font size="1">3605</font></a>
  on Luke; as if they, too, had not had free course in the churches, as well as Lukes Gospel, from the beginning. Nay, it is even more credible that they <a name="fr_3605"></a><a href="#fn_3605"><font size="1">3606</font></a>
  existed from the very beginning; for, being the work of apostles, they were prior, and coeval in origin with <a name="fr_3606"></a><a href="#fn_3606"><font size="1">3607</font></a>
  the churches themselves. But how comes it to pass, if the apostles published nothing, that their disciples were more forward in such a work; for they could not have been disciples, without any instruction from their masters? If, then, it be evident that these (Gospels) also were current in the churches, why did not Marcion touch them—either to amend them if they were adulterated, or to acknowledge them if they were uncorrupt?  For it is but natural <a name="fr_3607"></a><a href="#fn_3607"><font size="1">3608</font></a>
  that they who were perverting the gospel, should be more solicitous about the perversion of those things whose authority they knew to be more generally received. Even the false apostles (were so called) on this very account, because they imitated the apostles by means of their falsification. In <i>as</i> far, then, as he might have amended what there was to amend, if found corrupt, in <i>so</i> far did he firmly imply <a name="fr_3608"></a><a href="#fn_3608"><font size="1">3609</font></a>
  that all was free from corruption which he did not think required amendment. In short, <a name="fr_3609"></a><a href="#fn_3609"><font size="1">3610</font></a>
  he simply amended what he thought was corrupt; though, indeed, not even this justly, because it was not really corrupt.  For if the (Gospels) of the apostles <a name="fr_3610"></a><a href="#fn_3610"><font size="1">3611</font></a>
  have come down to us in their integrity, whilst Lukes, which is received amongst us, <a name="fr_3611"></a><a href="#fn_3611"><font size="1">3612</font></a>
  so far accords with their rule as to be on a par with them in permanency of reception in the churches, it clearly follows that Lukes Gospel also has come down to us in like integrity until the sacrilegious treatment of Marcion. In short, when Marcion laid hands on it, it then became diverse and hostile to the Gospels of the apostles. I will therefore advise his followers, that they either change these Gospels, however late to do so, into a conformity with their own, whereby they may seem to be in agreement with the apostolic writings (for they are daily retouching their work, as daily they are convicted by us); or else that they blush for their master, who stands self-condemned <a name="fr_3612"></a><a href="#fn_3612"><font size="1">3613</font></a>
  either way—when once <a name="fr_3613"></a><a href="#fn_3613"><font size="1">3614</font></a>
  he hands on the truth of the gospel conscience smitten, or again <a name="fr_3614"></a><a href="#fn_3614"><font size="1">3615</font></a>
  subverts it by shameless tampering. <a name="page_351"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 351</font></a> <a name="v.iv.v.v-Page_351"></a>Such are the summary arguments which we use, when we take up arms <a name="fr_3615"></a><a href="#fn_3615"><font size="1">3616</font></a>
  against heretics for the faith <a name="fr_3616"></a><a href="#fn_3616"><font size="1">3617</font></a>
  of the gospel, maintaining both that order of periods, which rules that a late date is the mark of forgers, <a name="fr_3617"></a><a href="#fn_3617"><font size="1">3618</font></a>
  and that authority of churches <a name="fr_3618"></a><a href="#fn_3618"><font size="1">3619</font></a>
  which lends support to the tradition of the apostles; because truth must needs precede the forgery, and proceed straight from those by whom it has been handed on.</p>
