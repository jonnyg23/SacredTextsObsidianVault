 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.vi-p1.1"></a>Chapter VI.—The Fifth Clause.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.vi-p2"></a>But how gracefully has the Divine Wisdom arranged the order of the prayer; so that <i>after</i> things heavenly—that is, after the “Name” of God, the “Will” of God, and the “Kingdom” of God—it should give earthly necessities also room for a petition! For the Lord had <a name="fr_8796"></a><a href="#fn_8796"><font size="1">8797</font></a>
  withal issued His edict, “Seek ye first the kingdom, and then even these shall be added:” <a name="fr_8797"></a><a href="#fn_8797"><font size="1">8798</font></a>
  albeit we may rather understand, “Give us this day our daily bread,” <i>spiritually</i>. For <i>Christ</i> is our Bread; because Christ is Life, and bread is life. “I am,” saith He, “the Bread of Life;” <a name="fr_8798"></a><a href="#fn_8798"><font size="1">8799</font></a>
  and, a little above, “The Bread is the Word of the living God, who came down from the heavens.” <a name="fr_8799"></a><a href="#fn_8799"><font size="1">8800</font></a>
  Then <i>we find</i>, too, that His body is reckoned in bread: “This is my body.” <a name="fr_8800"></a><a href="#fn_8800"><font size="1">8801</font></a>
  And so, in petitioning for “daily bread,” we ask for perpetuity in Christ, and indivisibility from His body. But, because that word is admissible in a carnal sense too, it cannot be so used without the religious remembrance withal of spiritual Discipline; for (the Lord) commands that <i>bread</i> be prayed for, which is the only <i>food</i> necessary for believers; for “all other things the nations seek after.” <a name="fr_8801"></a><a href="#fn_8801"><font size="1">8802</font></a>
  The like lesson He both inculcates by examples, and repeatedly handles in parables, when He says, “Doth a father take away <i>bread</i> from his children, and hand it to dogs?” <a name="fr_8802"></a><a href="#fn_8802"><font size="1">8803</font></a>
  and again, “Doth a father give his son a stone when he asks for <i>bread</i>?” <a name="fr_8803"></a><a href="#fn_8803"><font size="1">8804</font></a>
  For He <i>thus</i> shows what it is that sons expect from their father. Nay, even that nocturnal knocker knocked for “<i>bread</i>.” <a name="fr_8804"></a><a href="#fn_8804"><font size="1">8805</font></a>
  Moreover, He justly added, “Give us <i>this day</i>,” seeing He had previously said, “Take no careful thought about the morrow, what ye are to eat.” <a name="fr_8805"></a><a href="#fn_8805"><font size="1">8806</font></a>
  To which subject He also adapted the parable of the man who pondered on an enlargement of his barns for his forthcoming fruits, and on seasons of prolonged security; but that very night he dies. <a name="fr_8806"></a><a href="#fn_8806"><font size="1">8807</font></a>
 </p>
