 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XV.—Unity of Baptism. Remarks on Heretical And Jewish Baptism.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xv-p2"></a>I know not whether any further point is mooted to bring baptism into controversy. Permit me to call to mind what I have omitted above, lest I seem to break off the train of impending thoughts in the middle. There is to us one, and but one, baptism; as well according to the Lords gospel <a name="fr_8688"></a><a href="#fn_8688"><font size="1">8689</font></a>
  as according to the apostles letters, <a name="fr_8689"></a><a href="#fn_8689"><font size="1">8690</font></a>
  inasmuch as <i>he says</i>, “One God, and one baptism, and one church in the heavens.” <a name="fr_8690"></a><a href="#fn_8690"><font size="1">8691</font></a>
  But it must be admitted that the question, “What rules are to be observed with regard to heretics?” is worthy of being treated. For it is to <i>us</i> <a name="fr_8691"></a><a href="#fn_8691"><font size="1">8692</font></a>
  that that assertion <a name="fr_8692"></a><a href="#fn_8692"><font size="1">8693</font></a>
  refers. Heretics, however, have no fellowship in our discipline, whom the mere fact of their excommunication <a name="fr_8693"></a><a href="#fn_8693"><font size="1">8694</font></a>
  testifies to be outsiders. I am not bound to recognize in <i>them</i> a thing which is enjoined on <i>me</i>, because they and we have not the same God, nor one—that is, <i>the same</i>—Christ. And therefore their baptism is not one <i>with ours</i> either, because it is not <i>the same; a baptism</i> which, since they have it not duly, doubtless they have <i>not at all</i>; nor is that capable of being <i>counted</i> which is not <i>had</i>. <a name="fr_8694"></a><a href="#fn_8694"><font size="1">8695</font></a>
  Thus they cannot <i>receive</i> it either, because they <i>have it not</i>. But this point has already received a fuller discussion from us in Greek.  We enter, then, the font <a name="fr_8695"></a><a href="#fn_8695"><font size="1">8696</font></a>
  <i>once:  once</i> are sins washed away, because they ought never to be repeated. But the Jewish Israel bathes daily, <a name="fr_8696"></a><a href="#fn_8696"><font size="1">8697</font></a>
  because he is daily being defiled: and, for fear that <i>defilement</i> should be practised among <i>us</i> also, therefore was the definition touching the one bathing <a name="fr_8697"></a><a href="#fn_8697"><font size="1">8698</font></a>
  made. Happy water, which <i>once</i> washes away; which does not mock sinners (with vain hopes); which does not, by <a name="page_677"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 677</font></a> <a name="vi.iii.xv-Page_677"></a>being infected with the repetition of impurities, again defile them whom it has washed!</p>
