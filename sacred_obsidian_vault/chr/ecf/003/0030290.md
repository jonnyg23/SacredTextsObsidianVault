 <p> <a name="v.iii.xxiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.xxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xxiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIV.—St. Peters Further Vindication. St. Paul Not Superior to St. Peter in Teaching. Nothing Imparted to the Former in the Third Heaven Enabled Him to Add to the Faith. Heretics Boast as If Favoured with Some of the Secrets Imparted to Him.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.xxiv-p2"></a>I have not the good fortune, <a name="fr_2101"></a><a href="#fn_2101"><font size="1">2102</font></a>
  or, as I must rather say, <a name="fr_2102"></a><a href="#fn_2102"><font size="1">2103</font></a>
  I have not the unenviable task, <a name="fr_2103"></a><a href="#fn_2103"><font size="1">2104</font></a>
  of setting apostles by the ears. <a name="fr_2104"></a><a href="#fn_2104"><font size="1">2105</font></a>
  But, inasmuch as our very perverse cavillers obtrude the rebuke in question for the set purpose of bringing the earlier <a name="fr_2105"></a><a href="#fn_2105"><font size="1">2106</font></a>
  doctrine into suspicion, I will put in a defence, as it were, for Peter, to the effect that even Paul said that he was “made all things to all men—to the Jews a Jew,” to those who were not Jews as one who was not a Jew—“that he might gain all.” <a name="fr_2106"></a><a href="#fn_2106"><font size="1">2107</font></a>
  Therefore it was according to times and persons and causes that they used to censure certain practices, which they would not hesitate themselves to pursue, in like conformity to times and persons and causes. Just (<i>e.g.</i>) as if Peter too had censured Paul, because, whilst forbidding circumcision, he actually circumcised Timothy himself. Never mind <a name="fr_2107"></a><a href="#fn_2107"><font size="1">2108</font></a>
  those who pass sentence on apostles! It is a happy fact that Peter is on the same level with Paul in the very glory <a name="fr_2108"></a><a href="#fn_2108"><font size="1">2109</font></a>
  of martyrdom. Now, although Paul was carried away even to the third heaven, and was caught up to paradise, <a name="fr_2109"></a><a href="#fn_2109"><font size="1">2110</font></a>
  and heard certain revelations there, yet these cannot possibly seem to have qualified him for (teaching) another doctrine, seeing that their very nature was such as to render them communicable to no human being. <a name="fr_2110"></a><a href="#fn_2110"><font size="1">2111</font></a>
  If, however, that unspeakable mystery <a name="fr_2111"></a><a href="#fn_2111"><font size="1">2112</font></a>
  did leak out, <a name="fr_2112"></a><a href="#fn_2112"><font size="1">2113</font></a>
  and become known to any man, and if any heresy affirms that it does itself follow the same, (then) either Paul must be charged with having betrayed the secret, or some other man must actually <a name="fr_2113"></a><a href="#fn_2113"><font size="1">2114</font></a>
  be shown to have been afterwards “caught up into paradise,” who had permission to speak out plainly what Paul was not allowed (even) to mutter.</p>
