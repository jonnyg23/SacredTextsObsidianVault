 <p> <a name="v.vi.xviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.xviii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.xviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.—Blasphemous Opinion Concerning the Origin of the Demiurge, Supposed to Be the Creator of the Universe.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.xviii-p2"></a>Having become a better proficient <a name="fr_6798"></a><a href="#fn_6798"><font size="1">6799</font></a>
  in practical conduct by the authority which, we may well suppose, <a name="fr_6799"></a><a href="#fn_6799"><font size="1">6800</font></a>
  accrued to her from her three children, she determined to impart form to each of the natures. The spiritual one however, she was unable to touch, inasmuch as she was herself spiritual. For a participation in the same nature has, to a very great extent, <a name="fr_6800"></a><a href="#fn_6800"><font size="1">6801</font></a>
  disqualified like and consubstantial beings from having superior power over one another. Therefore <a name="fr_6801"></a><a href="#fn_6801"><font size="1">6802</font></a>
  she applies herself solely to the animal nature, adducing the instructions of Soter <a name="fr_6802"></a><a href="#fn_6802"><font size="1">6803</font></a>
  (for her guidance). And first of all (she does) what cannot be described and read, and heard of, without an intense horror at the blasphemy thereof: she produces this God of ours, the God of all except of the heretics, the Father and Creator <a name="fr_6803"></a><a href="#fn_6803"><font size="1">6804</font></a>
  and King of all things, which are inferior to him. For from him do they proceed. If, however, they proceed from him, and not rather from Achamoth, or if only secretly from her, without his perceiving her, he was impelled to all that he did, even like a puppet <a name="fr_6804"></a><a href="#fn_6804"><font size="1">6805</font></a>
  which is moved from the outside. In fact, it was owing to this very ambiguity about the personal agency in the works which were done, that they coined for him the mixed name of (<i>Motherly Father</i>), <a name="fr_6805"></a><a href="#fn_6805"><font size="1">6806</font></a>
  whilst his other appellations were distinctly assigned according to the conditions and positions of his works:  so that they call him <i>Father</i> in relation to the animal substances to which they give the place of honour <a name="fr_6806"></a><a href="#fn_6806"><font size="1">6807</font></a>
  on his right hand; whereas, in respect of the material substances which they banish <a name="fr_6807"></a><a href="#fn_6807"><font size="1">6808</font></a>
  to his left hand, they name him <i>Demiurgus</i>; whilst <i>his title King</i> designates his authority over both classes, <i>nay</i> over the universe. <a name="fr_6808"></a><a href="#fn_6808"><font size="1">6809</font></a>
 </p>
