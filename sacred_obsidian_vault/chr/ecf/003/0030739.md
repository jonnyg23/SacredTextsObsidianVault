 <p> <a name="vi.iii.xvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xvii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVII.—Of the Power of Conferring Baptism.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xvii-p2"></a>For concluding our brief subject, <a name="fr_8705"></a><a href="#fn_8705"><font size="1">8706</font></a>
  it remains to put you in mind also of the due observance of giving and receiving baptism. Of giving it, the chief priest <a name="fr_8706"></a><a href="#fn_8706"><font size="1">8707</font></a>
  (who is the bishop) has the right: in the next place, the presbyters and deacons, yet not without the bishops authority, on account of the honour of the Church, which being preserved, peace is preserved. Beside these, even laymen have the right; for what is equally received can be equally given. Unless bishops, or priests, or deacons, be on the spot, <i>other</i> disciples are called <i>i.e. to the work</i>. The word of the Lord ought not to be hidden by any: in like manner, too, baptism, which is equally Gods property, <a name="fr_8707"></a><a href="#fn_8707"><font size="1">8708</font></a>
  can be administered by all. But how much more is the rule <a name="fr_8708"></a><a href="#fn_8708"><font size="1">8709</font></a>
  of reverence and modesty incumbent on laymen—seeing that these <i>powers</i> <a name="fr_8709"></a><a href="#fn_8709"><font size="1">8710</font></a>
  belong to their superiors—lest they assume to themselves the <i>specific</i> <a name="fr_8710"></a><a href="#fn_8710"><font size="1">8711</font></a>
  function of the bishop! Emulation of the episcopal office is the mother of schisms.  The most holy apostle has said, that “all things are <i>lawful</i>, but not all <i>expedient</i>.” <a name="fr_8711"></a><a href="#fn_8711"><font size="1">8712</font></a>
  Let it suffice assuredly, in cases of <i>necessity</i>, to avail yourself (of that rule <a name="fr_8712"></a><a href="#fn_8712"><font size="1">8713</font></a>
 , if at any time circumstance either of place, or of time, or of person compels you (so to do); for <i>then</i> the stedfast courage of the succourer, when the situation of the endangered one is urgent, is exceptionally admissible; inasmuch as he will be guilty of a human creatures loss if he shall refrain from bestowing what he had free liberty to bestow. But the woman of pertness, <a name="fr_8713"></a><a href="#fn_8713"><font size="1">8714</font></a>
  who has usurped the power to teach, will of course not give birth for herself likewise to a right of baptizing, unless some new beast shall arise <a name="fr_8714"></a><a href="#fn_8714"><font size="1">8715</font></a>
  like the former; so that, just as the one abolished baptism, <a name="fr_8715"></a><a href="#fn_8715"><font size="1">8716</font></a>
  so some other should in her own right confer it! But if the writings which wrongly go under Pauls name, claim Theclas example as a licence for womens teaching and baptizing, let them know that, in Asia, the presbyter who composed that writing, <a name="fr_8716"></a><a href="#fn_8716"><font size="1">8717</font></a>
  as if he were augmenting Pauls fame from his own store, after being convicted, and confessing that he had done it from love of Paul, was removed <a name="fr_8717"></a><a href="#fn_8717"><font size="1">8718</font></a>
  from his office. For how credible would it seem, that he who has not permitted a <i>woman</i> <a name="fr_8718"></a><a href="#fn_8718"><font size="1">8719</font></a>
  even to <i>learn</i> with over-boldness, should give a <i>female</i> <a name="fr_8719"></a><a href="#fn_8719"><font size="1">8720</font></a>
  the power of <i>teaching</i> and of <i>baptizing</i>! “Let them be silent,” he says, “and at home consult their own husbands.” <a name="fr_8720"></a><a href="#fn_8720"><font size="1">8721</font></a>
 </p>
