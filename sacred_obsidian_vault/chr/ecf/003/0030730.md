 <p> <a name="vi.iii.viii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Of the Imposition of Hands. Types of the Deluge and the Dove.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.viii-p2"></a>In the next place the hand is laid on us, invoking and inviting the Holy Spirit through benediction. <a name="fr_8597"></a><a href="#fn_8597"><font size="1">8598</font></a>
  Shall it be granted possible for human ingenuity to summon a spirit into water, and, by the application of hands from above, to animate their union into one body <a name="fr_8598"></a><a href="#fn_8598"><font size="1">8599</font></a>
  with another spirit of so clear sound; <a name="fr_8599"></a><a href="#fn_8599"><font size="1">8600</font></a>
  and shall it not be possible for God, in the case of His own organ, <a name="fr_8600"></a><a href="#fn_8600"><font size="1">8601</font></a>
  to produce, by means of “holy hands,” <a name="fr_8601"></a><a href="#fn_8601"><font size="1">8602</font></a>
  a sublime spiritual modulation? But this, as well as the former, is derived from the old sacramental rite in which Jacob blessed his grandsons, born of Joseph, Ephrem <a name="fr_8602"></a><a href="#fn_8602"><font size="1">8603</font></a>
  and Manasses; with his hands laid on them and interchanged, and indeed so transversely slanted one over the other, that, by delineating Christ, they even portended the future benediction into Christ. <a name="fr_8603"></a><a href="#fn_8603"><font size="1">8604</font></a>
  Then, <a name="page_673"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 673</font></a> <a name="vi.iii.viii-Page_673"></a>over our cleansed and blessed bodies willingly descends from the Father that Holiest Spirit. Over the waters of baptism, recognising as it were His primeval seat, <a name="fr_8604"></a><a href="#fn_8604"><font size="1">8605</font></a>
  He reposes: (He who) glided down on the Lord “in the shape of a dove,” <a name="fr_8605"></a><a href="#fn_8605"><font size="1">8606</font></a>
  in order that the nature of the Holy Spirit might be declared by means of the creature (the emblem) of simplicity and innocence, because even in her bodily structure the dove is without literal <a name="fr_8606"></a><a href="#fn_8606"><font size="1">8607</font></a>
  gall. And accordingly He says, “Be ye simple as doves.” <a name="fr_8607"></a><a href="#fn_8607"><font size="1">8608</font></a>
  Even this is not without the supporting evidence <a name="fr_8608"></a><a href="#fn_8608"><font size="1">8609</font></a>
  of a preceding figure. For just as, after the waters of the deluge, by which the old iniquity was purged—after the baptism, so to say, of the world—a <i>dove</i> was the herald which announced to the earth the assuagement <a name="fr_8609"></a><a href="#fn_8609"><font size="1">8610</font></a>
  of celestial wrath, when she had been sent her way out of the ark, and had returned with the olive-branch, a sign which even among the nations is the fore-token of <i>peace</i>; <a name="fr_8610"></a><a href="#fn_8610"><font size="1">8611</font></a>
  so by the self-same law <a name="fr_8611"></a><a href="#fn_8611"><font size="1">8612</font></a>
  of heavenly effect, to earth—that is, to our flesh <a name="fr_8612"></a><a href="#fn_8612"><font size="1">8613</font></a>
 —as it emerges from the font, <a name="fr_8613"></a><a href="#fn_8613"><font size="1">8614</font></a>
  after its old sins flies the <i>dove</i> of the Holy Spirit, bringing us the peace of God, sent out from the heavens where is the Church, the typified ark. <a name="fr_8614"></a><a href="#fn_8614"><font size="1">8615</font></a>
  But the world returned unto sin; in which point baptism would ill be compared to the deluge. And so it is destined to fire; just as the man too is, who after baptism renews his sins: <a name="fr_8615"></a><a href="#fn_8615"><font size="1">8616</font></a>
  so that this also ought to be accepted as a sign for our admonition.</p>
