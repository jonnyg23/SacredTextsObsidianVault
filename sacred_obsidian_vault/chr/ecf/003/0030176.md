 </p><p class="c30"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XV.—The Constellations and the Genii Very Indifferent Gods. The Roman Monopoly of Gods Unsatisfactory. Other Nations Require Deities Quite as Much.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xv-p2"></a>It would be tedious to take a survey of all those, too, whom you have buried amongst the constellations, and audaciously minister to as gods. <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1056"><font size="1">1057</font></a>
  I suppose your Castors, and Perseus, and Erigona, <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1057"><font size="1">1058</font></a>
  have just the same claims for the honours of the sky as Jupiters own big boy <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1058"><font size="1">1059</font></a>
  had. But why should we wonder? You have transferred to heaven even dogs, and scorpions, and crabs. I postpone all remarks <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">1060</font></a>
  concerning those whom you worship in your oracles. That this worship exists, is attested by him who pronounces the oracle. <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">1061</font></a>
  Why; you will have your gods to be spectators even of sadness, <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">1062</font></a>
  as is Viduus, who makes a <i>widow</i> of the soul, by parting it from the body, and whom you have condemned, by not permitting him to be enclosed within your city-walls; there is Cæculus also, to deprive the eyes of their perception; and Orbana, to bereave seed of its vital power; moreover, there is the goddess of death herself. To pass hastily by all others, <a name="fr_1062"></a><a href="#fn_1062"><font size="1">1063</font></a>
  you account as gods the sites of places or of the city; such are Father Janus (there being, moreover, the archer-goddess <a name="fr_1063"></a><a href="#fn_1063"><font size="1">1064</font></a>
  Jana <a name="fr_1064"></a><a href="#fn_1064"><font size="1">1065</font></a>
 ), and Septimontius of the seven hills.</p> 
 <p class="c20"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xv-p12"></a>Men sacrifice <a name="fr_1065"></a><a href="#fn_1065"><font size="1">1066</font></a>
  to the same <i>Genii</i>, whilst <a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 145</font></a> <a name="iv.viii.ii.xv-Page_145"></a>they have altars or temples in the same places; but to others besides, when they dwell in a strange place, or live in rented houses. <a name="fr_1066"></a><a href="#fn_1066"><font size="1">1067</font></a>
  I say nothing about Ascensus, who gets his name for his <i>climbing</i> propensity, and Clivicola, from her sloping (haunts); I pass silently by the deities called Forculus from doors, and Cardea from hinges, and Limentinus the god of thresholds, and whatever others are worshipped by your neighbours as tutelar deities of their street doors. <a name="fr_1067"></a><a href="#fn_1067"><font size="1">1068</font></a>
  There is nothing strange in this, since men have their respective gods in their brothels, their kitchens, and even in their prison. Heaven, therefore, is crowded with innumerable gods of its own, both these and others belonging to the Romans, which have distributed amongst them the functions of ones whole life, in such a way that there is no want of the other <a name="fr_1068"></a><a href="#fn_1068"><font size="1">1069</font></a>
  gods. Although, it is true, <a name="fr_1069"></a><a href="#fn_1069"><font size="1">1070</font></a>
  the gods which we have enumerated are reckoned as Roman peculiarly, and as not easily recognised abroad; yet how do all those functions and circumstances, over which men have willed their gods to preside, come about, <a name="fr_1070"></a><a href="#fn_1070"><font size="1">1071</font></a>
  in every part of the human race, and in every nation, where their guarantees <a name="fr_1071"></a><a href="#fn_1071"><font size="1">1072</font></a>
  are not only without an official recognition, but even any recognition at all?</p>
