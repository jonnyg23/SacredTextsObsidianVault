 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.xxviii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xxviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVIII.—Of the Spiritual Victim, Which Prayer is.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xxviii-p2"></a>For this is the spiritual victim <a name="fr_8935"></a><a href="#fn_8935"><font size="1">8936</font></a>
  which has abolished the pristine sacrifices.  “To what purpose,” saith He, “(bring ye) me the multitude of your sacrifices? I am full of holocausts of rams, and I desire not the fat of rams, and the blood of bulls and of goats. For who hath required these from your hands?” <a name="fr_8936"></a><a href="#fn_8936"><font size="1">8937</font></a>
  What, then, God <i>has</i> required the Gospel teaches.  “An hour will come,” saith He, “when the true adorers shall adore the Father in spirit and truth. For God is a Spirit, and accordingly requires His adorers to be such.” <a name="fr_8937"></a><a href="#fn_8937"><font size="1">8938</font></a>
  We are the true adorers and the true priests, <a name="fr_8938"></a><a href="#fn_8938"><font size="1">8939</font></a>
  who, praying in spirit, <a name="fr_8939"></a><a href="#fn_8939"><font size="1">8940</font></a>
  sacrifice, in spirit, prayer,—a victim proper and acceptable to God, which assuredly He has required, which He has looked forward to <a name="fr_8940"></a><a href="#fn_8940"><font size="1">8941</font></a>
  for Himself! This <i>victim</i>, devoted from the whole heart, fed on faith, tended by truth, entire in innocence, pure in chastity, garlanded with love, <a name="fr_8941"></a><a href="#fn_8941"><font size="1">8942</font></a>
  we ought to escort with the pomp <a name="fr_8942"></a><a href="#fn_8942"><font size="1">8943</font></a>
  of good works, amid psalms and hymns, unto Gods altar, <a name="fr_8943"></a><a href="#fn_8943"><font size="1">8944</font></a>
  to obtain for us all things from God.</p>
