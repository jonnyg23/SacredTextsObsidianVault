 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.xii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XII.—We Must Be Free Likewise from All Mental Perturbation.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xii-p2"></a>Nor merely from anger, but altogether from <i>all</i> perturbation of mind, ought the exercise of prayer to be free, uttered from a spirit such as the Spirit unto whom it is sent. For a defiled spirit cannot be acknowledged by a holy Spirit, <a name="fr_8837"></a><a href="#fn_8837"><font size="1">8838</font></a>
  nor a sad by a joyful, <a name="fr_8838"></a><a href="#fn_8838"><font size="1">8839</font></a>
  nor a fettered by a free. <a name="fr_8839"></a><a href="#fn_8839"><font size="1">8840</font></a>
  No one grants reception to his adversary: no one grants admittance except to his compeer.</p>
