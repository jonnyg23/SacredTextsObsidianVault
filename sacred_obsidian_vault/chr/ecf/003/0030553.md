 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.xxxviii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.xxxviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXVIII.—Diversity in the Opinions of Secundus, as Compared with the General Doctrine of Valentinus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.xxxviii-p2"></a>Secundus is a trifle more human, as he is briefer: he divides the Ogdoad into a pair of Tetrads, a right hand one and a left hand one, <i>one</i> light and <i>the other</i> darkness. Only he is unwilling to derive the power which apostatized and fell away <a name="fr_6930"></a><a href="#fn_6930"><font size="1">6931</font></a>
  from any one of the Æons, but from the fruits which issued from their substance.</p>
