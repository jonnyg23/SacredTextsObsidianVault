 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.xvi-p1"></a> <a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 251</font></a> <a name="v.iii.xvi-Page_251"></a><span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVI.—Apostolic Sanction to This Exclusion of Heretics from the Use of the Scriptures. Heretics, According to the Apostle, are Not to Be Disputed With, But to Be Admonished.</span></p> 
 <p class="c20"><a name="v.iii.xvi-p2"></a>I might be thought to have laid down this position to remedy distrust in my case, <a name="fr_2026"></a><a href="#fn_2026"><font size="1">2027</font></a>
  or from a desire of entering on the contest <a name="fr_2027"></a><a href="#fn_2027"><font size="1">2028</font></a>
  in some other way, were there not reasons on my side, especially this, that our faith owes deference <a name="fr_2028"></a><a href="#fn_2028"><font size="1">2029</font></a>
  to the apostle, who forbids us to enter on “questions,” or to lend our ears to new-fangled statements, <a name="fr_2029"></a><a href="#fn_2029"><font size="1">2030</font></a>
  or to consort with a heretic “after the first and second admonition,” <a name="fr_2030"></a><a href="#fn_2030"><font size="1">2031</font></a>
  not, (be it observed,) after discussion.  Discussion he has inhibited in this way, by designating <i>admonition</i> as the purpose of dealing with a heretic, and the <i>first</i> one too, because he is not a Christian; in order that he might not, after the manner of a Christian, seem to require correction again and again, and “before two or three witnesses,” <a name="fr_2031"></a><a href="#fn_2031"><font size="1">2032</font></a>
  seeing that he ought to be corrected, for the very reason that he is not to be disputed with; and in the next place, because a controversy over the Scriptures can, clearly, <a name="fr_2032"></a><a href="#fn_2032"><font size="1">2033</font></a>
  produce no other effect than help to upset either the stomach or the brain.</p>
