 <p> <a name="v.iv.vi.v"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iv.vi.v-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.vi.v-p1.1"></a>Chapter V.—The First Epistle to the Corinthians. The Pauline Salutation of Grace and Peace Shown to Be Anti-Marcionite. The Cross of Christ Purposed by the Creator. Marcion Only Perpetuates the Offence and Foolishness of Christs Cross by His Impious Severance of the Gospel from the Creator. Analogies Between the Law and the Gospel in the Matter of Weak Things, and Foolish Things and Base Things.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iv.vi.v-p2"></a>My preliminary remarks <a name="fr_5385"></a><a href="#fn_5385"><font size="1">5386</font></a>
  on the preceding epistle called me away from treating of its superscription, <a name="fr_5386"></a><a href="#fn_5386"><font size="1">5387</font></a>
  for I was sure that another opportunity would occur for considering the matter, it being of constant recurrence, and in the same form too, in every epistle. The point, then, is, that it is not (the usual) <i>health</i> which the apostle prescribes for those to whom he writes, but “grace and peace.” <a name="fr_5387"></a><a href="#fn_5387"><font size="1">5388</font></a>
  I do not ask, indeed, what a destroyer of Judaism has to do with a formula which the Jews still use. For to this day they salute each other <a name="fr_5388"></a><a href="#fn_5388"><font size="1">5389</font></a>
  with the greeting of “peace,” and formerly in their Scriptures they did the same. But I understand him by his practice <a name="fr_5389"></a><a href="#fn_5389"><font size="1">5390</font></a>
  plainly enough to have corroborated the declaration of the Creator: “How beautiful are the feet of them that bring glad tidings of good, who preach the gospel of <i>peace!</i>” <a name="fr_5390"></a><a href="#fn_5390"><font size="1">5391</font></a>
  For the herald of <i>good</i>, that is, of Gods “grace” was well aware that along with it “peace” also was to be proclaimed. <a name="fr_5391"></a><a href="#fn_5391"><font size="1">5392</font></a>
  Now, when he announces these blessings as “from God the Father and the Lord Jesus,” <a name="fr_5392"></a><a href="#fn_5392"><font size="1">5393</font></a>
  he uses titles that are common to both, and which are also adapted to the mystery of our faith; <a name="fr_5393"></a><a href="#fn_5393"><font size="1">5394</font></a>
  and I suppose it to be impossible accurately to determine what God is declared to be the Father and the Lord Jesus, unless (we consider) which of their accruing attributes are more suited to them severally. <a name="fr_5394"></a><a href="#fn_5394"><font size="1">5395</font></a>
  First, then, I assert that none other than the Creator and Sustainer of both man and the universe can be acknowledged as Father <i>and</i> Lord; next, that to the Father also the title of Lord accrues by reason of His power, and that the Son too receives the same through the Father; then that “grace and peace” are not only His who had them published, but His likewise to whom offence had been given. For neither does <i>grace</i> exist, except after offence; nor <i>peace</i>, except after war. Now, both the <a name="page_439"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 439</font></a> <a name="v.iv.vi.v-Page_439"></a>people (of Israel) by their transgression of His laws, <a name="fr_5395"></a><a href="#fn_5395"><font size="1">5396</font></a>
  and the whole race of mankind by their neglect of natural duty, <a name="fr_5396"></a><a href="#fn_5396"><font size="1">5397</font></a>
  had both sinned and rebelled against the Creator. Marcions god, however, could not have been offended, both because he was unknown to everybody, and because he is incapable of being irritated. What <i>grace</i>, therefore, can be had of a god who has not been offended? What <i>peace</i> from one who has never experienced rebellion? “The cross of Christ,” he says, “is to them that perish foolishness; but unto such as shall obtain salvation, it is the power of God and the wisdom of God.” <a name="fr_5397"></a><a href="#fn_5397"><font size="1">5398</font></a>
  And then, that we may know from whence this comes, he adds: “For it is written, I will destroy the wisdom of the wise, and will bring to nothing the understanding of the prudent.” <a name="fr_5398"></a><a href="#fn_5398"><font size="1">5399</font></a>
  Now, since these are the Creators words, and since what pertains to the doctrine <a name="fr_5399"></a><a href="#fn_5399"><font size="1">5400</font></a>
  of the cross he accounts as foolishness, therefore both the cross, and also Christ by reason of the cross, will appertain to the Creator, by whom were predicted the incidents of the cross.  But if <a name="fr_5400"></a><a href="#fn_5400"><font size="1">5401</font></a>
  the Creator, as an enemy, took away their wisdom in order that the cross of Christ, considered as his adversary, should be accounted foolishness, how by any possibility can the Creator have foretold anything about the cross of a Christ who is not His own, and of whom He knew nothing, when He published the prediction? But, again, how happens it, that in the system of a Lord <a name="fr_5401"></a><a href="#fn_5401"><font size="1">5402</font></a>
  who is so very good, and so profuse in mercy, some carry off salvation, when they believe the cross to be the wisdom and power of God, whilst others incur perdition, to whom the cross of Christ is accounted folly;—(how happens it, I repeat,) unless it is in the Creators dispensation to have punished both the people <i>of Israel</i> and the human race, for some great offence committed against Him, with the loss of wisdom and prudence? What follows will confirm this suggestion, when he asks, “Hath not God infatuated the wisdom of this world?” <a name="fr_5402"></a><a href="#fn_5402"><font size="1">5403</font></a>
  and when he adds the reason why: “For after that, in the wisdom of God, the world by wisdom knew not God, it pleased God <a name="fr_5403"></a><a href="#fn_5403"><font size="1">5404</font></a>
  by the foolishness of preaching to save them that believe.” <a name="fr_5404"></a><a href="#fn_5404"><font size="1">5405</font></a>
  But first a word about the expression “<i>the world</i>;” because in this passage particularly, <a name="fr_5405"></a><a href="#fn_5405"><font size="1">5406</font></a>
  the heretics expend a great deal of their subtlety in showing that by <i>world</i> is meant <i>the lord of the world</i>. We, however, understand the term to apply to any person that is in the world, by a simple idiom of human language, which often substitutes that which contains for that which is contained. “The circus shouted,” “The forum spoke,” and “The basilica murmured,” are well-known expressions, meaning that the people in these places did so. Since then the man, not the god, of the world <a name="fr_5406"></a><a href="#fn_5406"><font size="1">5407</font></a>
  in his wisdom knew not God, whom indeed he ought to have known (both the Jew by his knowledge of the Scriptures, and all the human race by their knowledge of Gods works), therefore that God, who was not acknowledged in His wisdom, resolved to smite mens knowledge with His foolishness, by saving all those who believe in the folly of the preached cross.  “Because the Jews require signs,” who ought to have already made up their minds about God, “and the Greeks seek after wisdom,” <a name="fr_5407"></a><a href="#fn_5407"><font size="1">5408</font></a>
  who rely upon their own wisdom, and not upon Gods. If, however, it was a new god that was being preached, what sin had the Jews committed, in seeking after signs to believe; or the Greeks, when they hunted after a wisdom which they would prefer to accept? Thus the very retribution which overtook both Jews and Greeks proves that God is both a jealous God and a Judge, inasmuch as He infatuated the worlds wisdom by an angry <a name="fr_5408"></a><a href="#fn_5408"><font size="1">5409</font></a>
  and a judicial retribution. Since, then, the causes <a name="fr_5409"></a><a href="#fn_5409"><font size="1">5410</font></a>
  are in the hands of Him who gave us the Scriptures which we use, it follows that the apostle, when treating of the Creator, (as Him whom both Jew and Gentile as yet have) not known, means undoubtedly to teach us, that the God who is to become known (in Christ) is the Creator.  The very “stumbling-block” which he declares Christ to be “to the Jews,” <a name="fr_5410"></a><a href="#fn_5410"><font size="1">5411</font></a>
  points unmistakeably <a name="fr_5411"></a><a href="#fn_5411"><font size="1">5412</font></a>
  to the Creators prophecy respecting Him, when by Isaiah He says: “Behold I lay in Sion a stone of stumbling and a rock of offence.” <a name="fr_5412"></a><a href="#fn_5412"><font size="1">5413</font></a>
  This rock or stone is Christ. <a name="fr_5413"></a><a href="#fn_5413"><font size="1">5414</font></a>
  This stumbling-stone Marcion retains still. <a name="fr_5414"></a><a href="#fn_5414"><font size="1">5415</font></a>
  <a name="page_440"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 440</font></a> <a name="v.iv.vi.v-Page_440"></a>Now, what is that “foolishness of God which is wiser than men,” but the cross and death of Christ? What is that “weakness of God which is stronger than men,” <a name="fr_5415"></a><a href="#fn_5415"><font size="1">5416</font></a>
  but the nativity and incarnation <a name="fr_5416"></a><a href="#fn_5416"><font size="1">5417</font></a>
  of God? If, however, Christ was not born of the Virgin, was not constituted of human flesh, and thereby really suffered neither death nor the cross, there was nothing in Him either of foolishness or weakness; nor is it any longer true, that “God hath chosen the foolish things of the world to confound the wise;” nor, again, hath “God chosen the weak things of the world to confound the mighty;” nor “the base things” and the least things “in the world, and things which are despised, which are even as nothing” (that is, things which really <a name="fr_5417"></a><a href="#fn_5417"><font size="1">5418</font></a>
  are not), “to bring to nothing things which are” (that is, which really are). <a name="fr_5418"></a><a href="#fn_5418"><font size="1">5419</font></a>
  For nothing in the dispensation of God is found to be mean, and ignoble, and contemptible. Such only occurs in mans arrangement. The very Old Testament of the Creator <a name="fr_5419"></a><a href="#fn_5419"><font size="1">5420</font></a>
  itself, it is possible, no doubt, to charge with foolishness, and weakness, and dishonour and meanness, and contempt.  What is more foolish and more weak than Gods requirement of bloody sacrifices and of savoury holocausts?  What is weaker than the cleansing of vessels and of beds? <a name="fr_5420"></a><a href="#fn_5420"><font size="1">5421</font></a>
  What more dishonourable than the discoloration of the reddening skin? <a name="fr_5421"></a><a href="#fn_5421"><font size="1">5422</font></a>
  What so mean as the statute of retaliation? What so contemptible as the exception in meats and drinks? The whole of the Old Testament, the heretic, to the best of my belief, holds in derision. For God has chosen the foolish things of the world to confound its wisdom.  Marcions god has no such discipline, because he does not take after <a name="fr_5422"></a><a href="#fn_5422"><font size="1">5423</font></a>
  (the Creator) in the process of confusing opposites by their opposites, so that “no flesh shall glory; but, as it is written, He that glorieth, let him glory in the Lord.” <a name="fr_5423"></a><a href="#fn_5423"><font size="1">5424</font></a>
  In what Lord?  Surely in Him who gave this precept. <a name="fr_5424"></a><a href="#fn_5424"><font size="1">5425</font></a>
  Unless, forsooth, the Creator enjoined us to glory in the god of Marcion.</p>
