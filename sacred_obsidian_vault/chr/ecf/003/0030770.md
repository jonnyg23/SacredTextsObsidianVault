 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.xxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xxvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVI.—Of the Parting of Brethren.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xxvi-p2"></a>You will not dismiss a brother who has entered your house without prayer.—“Have you seen,” says <i>Scripture</i>, “a brother? you have seen your Lord;” <a name="fr_8930"></a><a href="#fn_8930"><font size="1">8931</font></a>
 —especially “a stranger,” lest perhaps he be “an angel.”  But again, when received yourself by brethren, you will not make <a name="fr_8931"></a><a href="#fn_8931"><font size="1">8932</font></a>
  earthly refreshments prior to heavenly, for your faith will forthwith be judged. Or else how will you—according to the precept <a name="fr_8932"></a><a href="#fn_8932"><font size="1">8933</font></a>
 —say, “Peace to this <i>house</i>,” unless you exchange mutual peace with them who are <i>in</i> the house?</p>
