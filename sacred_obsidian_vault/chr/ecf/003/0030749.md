 <p> <a name="vi.iv.v"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.v-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.v-p1.1"></a>Chapter V.—The Fourth Clause.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.v-p2"></a>“Thy kingdom come” has also reference to that whereto “Thy will be done” refers—<i>in us</i>, that is. For when does God not reign, in whose hand is the heart of all kings? <a name="fr_8789"></a><a href="#fn_8789"><font size="1">8790</font></a>
  But whatever we wish for ourselves we augur for Him, and <i>to</i> Him we attribute what <i>from</i> Him we expect. And so, if the <i>manifestation</i> of the Lords kingdom pertains unto the will of God and unto our anxious expectation, how do some pray for some protraction of the age, <a name="fr_8790"></a><a href="#fn_8790"><font size="1">8791</font></a>
  when the kingdom of God, which we pray may arrive, tends unto the consummation of the age? <a name="fr_8791"></a><a href="#fn_8791"><font size="1">8792</font></a>
  Our wish is, that our reign be hastened, not our servitude protracted. Even if it had not been prescribed in the Prayer that we should ask for the advent of the kingdom, we should, unbidden, have sent forth that cry, hastening toward the realization of our hope. The souls of the martyrs beneath the altar <a name="fr_8792"></a><a href="#fn_8792"><font size="1">8793</font></a>
  cry in jealousy unto the Lord, “How long, Lord, dost Thou not avenge our blood on the inhabitants of the earth?” <a name="fr_8793"></a><a href="#fn_8793"><font size="1">8794</font></a>
  for, of course, their avenging is regulated by <a name="fr_8794"></a><a href="#fn_8794"><font size="1">8795</font></a>
  the end of the age. Nay, Lord, Thy kingdom come with all speed,—the prayer of Christians the confusion of the heathen, <a name="fr_8795"></a><a href="#fn_8795"><font size="1">8796</font></a>
  the exultation of angels, for the sake of which we suffer, nay, rather, for the sake of which we pray!</p>
