 <p> <a name="vi.iii.ix"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.ix-p1.1"></a>Chapter IX.—Types of the Red Sea, and the Water from the Rock.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.ix-p2"></a>How many, therefore, are the pleas <a name="fr_8616"></a><a href="#fn_8616"><font size="1">8617</font></a>
  of nature, how many the privileges of grace, how many the solemnities of discipline, the figures, the preparations, the prayers, which have ordained the sanctity of water? First, indeed, when the people, set unconditionally free, <a name="fr_8617"></a><a href="#fn_8617"><font size="1">8618</font></a>
  escaped the violence of the Egyptian king by crossing over <i>through water</i>, it was <i>water</i> that extinguished <a name="fr_8618"></a><a href="#fn_8618"><font size="1">8619</font></a>
  the king himself, with his entire forces. <a name="fr_8619"></a><a href="#fn_8619"><font size="1">8620</font></a>
  What figure more manifestly fulfilled in the sacrament of baptism? The nations are set free from the world <a name="fr_8620"></a><a href="#fn_8620"><font size="1">8621</font></a>
  by means of <i>water</i>, to wit: and the devil, their old tyrant, they leave quite behind, overwhelmed in the <i>water</i>. Again, <i>water</i> is restored from its defect of “bitterness” to its native grace of “sweetness” by the tree <a name="fr_8621"></a><a href="#fn_8621"><font size="1">8622</font></a>
  of Moses. That tree was Christ, <a name="fr_8622"></a><a href="#fn_8622"><font size="1">8623</font></a>
  restoring, to wit, of Himself, the <i>veins</i> of sometime envenomed and bitter nature into the all-salutary <i>waters</i> of baptism. This is the <i>water</i> which flowed continuously down for the people from the “accompanying rock;” for if Christ is “the Rock,” without doubt we see baptism blest by the <i>water</i> in Christ. How mighty is the grace of <i>water</i>, in the sight of God and His Christ, for the confirmation of baptism!  Never is Christ without <i>water</i>: if, that is, He is Himself baptized in <i>water</i>; <a name="fr_8623"></a><a href="#fn_8623"><font size="1">8624</font></a>
  inaugurates in <i>water</i> the first rudimentary displays of His power, when invited to the nuptials; <a name="fr_8624"></a><a href="#fn_8624"><font size="1">8625</font></a>
  invites the thirsty, when He makes a discourse, to His own sempiternal <i>water</i>; <a name="fr_8625"></a><a href="#fn_8625"><font size="1">8626</font></a>
  approves, when teaching concerning love, <a name="fr_8626"></a><a href="#fn_8626"><font size="1">8627</font></a>
  among works of charity, <a name="fr_8627"></a><a href="#fn_8627"><font size="1">8628</font></a>
  the cup of <i>water</i> offered to a poor (child); <a name="fr_8628"></a><a href="#fn_8628"><font size="1">8629</font></a>
  recruits His strength at a <i>well</i>; <a name="fr_8629"></a><a href="#fn_8629"><font size="1">8630</font></a>
  walks over the <i>water</i>; <a name="fr_8630"></a><a href="#fn_8630"><font size="1">8631</font></a>
  willingly crosses the <i>sea</i>; <a name="fr_8631"></a><a href="#fn_8631"><font size="1">8632</font></a>
  ministers <i>water</i> to His disciples. <a name="fr_8632"></a><a href="#fn_8632"><font size="1">8633</font></a>
  Onward even to the passion does the witness of baptism last: while He is being surrendered to the cross, <i>water</i> intervenes; witness Pilates hands: <a name="fr_8633"></a><a href="#fn_8633"><font size="1">8634</font></a>
  when He is wounded, forth from His side bursts <i>water</i>; witness the soldiers lance! <a name="fr_8634"></a><a href="#fn_8634"><font size="1">8635</font></a>
 </p>
