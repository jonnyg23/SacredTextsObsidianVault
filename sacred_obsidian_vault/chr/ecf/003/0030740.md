 <p> <a name="vi.iii.xviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xviii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.—Of the Persons to Whom, and the Time When, Baptism is to Be Administered.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xviii-p2"></a>But they whose office it is, know that baptism is not rashly to be administered. “Give to every one who beggeth thee,” <a name="fr_8721"></a><a href="#fn_8721"><font size="1">8722</font></a>
  has a reference of its own, appertaining especially to almsgiving. On the contrary, this <i>precept</i> is rather to be looked at carefully: “Give not the holy thing to the dogs, nor cast your pearls before swine;” <a name="fr_8722"></a><a href="#fn_8722"><font size="1">8723</font></a>
  and, “Lay not hands easily on <i>any</i>; share not other mens sins.” <a name="fr_8723"></a><a href="#fn_8723"><font size="1">8724</font></a>
  If Philip so “easily” baptized the chamberlain, let us reflect that a manifest and conspicuous <a name="fr_8724"></a><a href="#fn_8724"><font size="1">8725</font></a>
  evidence that the Lord deemed him worthy <a name="page_678"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 678</font></a> <a name="vi.iii.xviii-Page_678"></a>had been interposed. <a name="fr_8725"></a><a href="#fn_8725"><font size="1">8726</font></a>
  The Spirit had enjoined Philip to proceed to that road: the eunuch himself, too, was not found idle, nor as one who was suddenly seized with an eager desire to be baptized; but, after going up to the temple for prayers sake, being intently engaged on the divine Scripture, was thus suitably discovered—to whom God had, unasked, sent an apostle, which one, again, the Spirit bade adjoin himself to the chamberlains chariot. The Scripture which <i>he was reading</i> <a name="fr_8726"></a><a href="#fn_8726"><font size="1">8727</font></a>
 falls in opportunely with his faith: <i>Philip</i>, being requested, is taken to sit beside him; the Lord is pointed out; faith lingers not; water needs no waiting for; the work is completed, and the apostle snatched away.  “But Paul too was, in fact, speedily baptized:” for Simon, <a name="fr_8727"></a><a href="#fn_8727"><font size="1">8728</font></a>
  his host, speedily recognized him to be “an appointed vessel of election.” Gods approbation sends sure premonitory tokens before it; every “petition” <a name="fr_8728"></a><a href="#fn_8728"><font size="1">8729</font></a>
  may both deceive and be deceived. And so, according to the circumstances and disposition, and even age, of each individual, the delay of baptism is preferable; principally, however, in the case of little children. For why is it necessary—if (baptism itself) is not so necessary <a name="fr_8729"></a><a href="#fn_8729"><font size="1">8730</font></a>
 —that the sponsors likewise should be thrust into danger? Who both themselves, by reason of mortality, may fail to fulfil their promises, and may be disappointed by the development of an evil disposition, <i>in those for whom they stood</i>? The Lord does indeed say, “Forbid them not to come unto me.” <a name="fr_8730"></a><a href="#fn_8730"><font size="1">8731</font></a>
  Let them “come,” then, while they are growing up; let them “come” while they are learning, while they are learning whither to come; <a name="fr_8731"></a><a href="#fn_8731"><font size="1">8732</font></a>
  let them become Christians <a name="fr_8732"></a><a href="#fn_8732"><font size="1">8733</font></a>
  when they have become able to know Christ.  Why does the innocent period of life hasten to the “remission of sins?” More caution will be exercised in worldly <a name="fr_8733"></a><a href="#fn_8733"><font size="1">8734</font></a>
  matters: so that one who is <i>not</i> trusted with earthly substance <i>is</i> trusted with divine! Let them know how to “ask” for salvation, that you may seem (at least) to have given “to him that asketh.” <a name="fr_8734"></a><a href="#fn_8734"><font size="1">8735</font></a>
  For no less cause must the unwedded also be deferred—in whom <i>the ground of</i> temptation is prepared, alike in such as <i>never were</i> wedded <a name="fr_8735"></a><a href="#fn_8735"><font size="1">8736</font></a>
  by means of their maturity, and in the <i>widowed</i> by means of their freedom—until they either marry, or else be more fully strengthened for continence. If any understand the weighty import of baptism, they will fear its reception more than its delay: sound faith is secure of salvation.</p>
