 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.ix.xlii-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.ix.xlii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 42.—</span>82.  Victor of Octavus <a name="fr_1866"></a><a href="#fn_1866"><font size="1">1867</font></a>
  said:  "As ye yourselves also know, I have not been long appointed a bishop, and therefore I waited for the counsel of my seniors.  This therefore I express as my opinion, that whosoever comes from heresy should undoubtedly be baptized." <a name="fr_1867"></a><a href="#fn_1867"><font size="1">1868</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.ix.xlii-p4"></a>83.  What, therefore, has been answered to those for whom he waited, may be taken as the answer also to himself.</p>
