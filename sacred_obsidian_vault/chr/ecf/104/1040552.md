 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.v.iv.lxv-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.lxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter 65.—</span>145.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.lxv-p1.2"></a>Petilianus</span> said:  "Blessed are they that mourn:  for they shall be comforted.  You, our butchers, are the cause of mourning in others:  you do not mourn yourselves."</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.v.iv.lxv-p2"></a>146.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.lxv-p2.1"></a>Augustin</span> answered:  Consider for a short space to how many, and with what intensity, the cry of "Praises be to God," proceeding from your armed men, has caused others to mourn. <a name="fr_2158"></a><a href="#fn_2158"><font size="1">2159</font></a>
   Do you say again, What is that to us?  Then I too will rejoin again your own words, What is that to us?  What is it to all the nations of the earth?  What is it to those who praise the name of the Lord from the rising of the sun to the setting of the same?  What is it to all the earth, which sings a new song?  What is it to the seed of Abraham, in which all the nations of the earth are blessed? <a name="fr_2159"></a><a href="#fn_2159"><font size="1">2160</font></a>
   And so the sacrilege of <a name="page_566"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 566</font></a> <a name="v.v.iv.lxv-Page_566"></a>your schism is chargeable on you, just because the evil deeds of your companions are not chargeable on you; and because you are from this that the deeds of those on whose account you separated from the world, even if you proved your charges to be true, do not involve the world in sin.</p>
