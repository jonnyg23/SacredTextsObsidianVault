 <p> <a name="v.iv.vi.xiv"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.vi.xiv-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.vi.xiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter 14.—</span>22.  But we must not despair of the conversion of any man, whether situated within or without, so long as "the goodness of God leadeth him to repentance," <a name="fr_1422"></a><a href="#fn_1422"><font size="1">1423</font></a>
  and "visits their transgressions with the rod, and their inquiry with stripes."  For in this way "He does not utterly take from them His loving-kindness," <a name="fr_1423"></a><a href="#fn_1423"><font size="1">1424</font></a>
  if they will themselves sometimes "love their own soul, pleasing God." <a name="fr_1424"></a><a href="#fn_1424"><font size="1">1425</font></a>
   But as the good man "that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved," <a name="fr_1425"></a><a href="#fn_1425"><font size="1">1426</font></a>
  so the bad man, whether within or without, who shall persevere in his wickedness to the end, shall not be saved.  Nor do we say that "all, wheresoever and howsoever baptized, obtain the grace of baptism," <a name="fr_1426"></a><a href="#fn_1426"><font size="1">1427</font></a>
  if by the grace of baptism is understood the actual salvation which is conferred by the celebration of the sacrament; but many fail to obtain this salvation even within the Church, although it is clear that they possess the sacrament, which is holy in itself.  Well, therefore, does the Lord warn us in the gospel that we should not company with ill-advisers, <a name="fr_1427"></a><a href="#fn_1427"><font size="1">1428</font></a>
  who walk under the pretence of Christs name; but these are found both within and without, as, in fact, they do not proceed without unless they have first been ill-disposed within.  And we know that the apostle said of the vessels placed in the great house, "If a man therefore purge himself from these, he shall be a vessel unto honor, sanctified, and meet for the Masters use, and prepared unto every good work." <a name="fr_1428"></a><a href="#fn_1428"><font size="1">1429</font></a>
   But in what manner each man ought to purge himself from these he shows a little above, saying, "Let every one that nameth the name of Christ depart from iniquity," <a name="fr_1429"></a><a href="#fn_1429"><font size="1">1430</font></a>
  that he may not in the last day, with the chaff, whether with that which has already been driven from the threshing-floor, or with that which is to be separated at the last, hear the command, "Depart from me, ye that work iniquity." <a name="fr_1430"></a><a href="#fn_1430"><font size="1">1431</font></a>
   Whence it appears, indeed, as Cyprian says, that "we are not at once to admit and adopt whatsoever is professed in the name of Christ, but only what is done in the truth of Christ." <a name="fr_1431"></a><a href="#fn_1431"><font size="1">1432</font></a>
   But it is not an action done in the truth of Christ that men should "seize on estates by fraudulent pretenses, and increase their gains by accumulated usury," <a name="fr_1432"></a><a href="#fn_1432"><font size="1">1433</font></a>
  or that they should "renounce the world in word only;" <a name="fr_1433"></a><a href="#fn_1433"><font size="1">1434</font></a>
  and yet, that all this is done within the Church, Cyprian himself bears sufficient testimony.</p>
