 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxxii-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxxii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 32.—</span>62.  Another Victor of Assuras <a name="fr_1843"></a><a href="#fn_1843"><font size="1">1844</font></a>
  said:  "It is written, that there is one God and one Christ, one Church and one baptism. <a name="fr_1844"></a><a href="#fn_1844"><font size="1">1845</font></a>
   How then can any one baptize in a place where there is not either God, or Christ, or the Church?" <a name="fr_1845"></a><a href="#fn_1845"><font size="1">1846</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxxii-p5"></a>63.  How can any one baptize either in that sand, where the Church is not, seeing that it is on the rock; nor God and Christ, seeing that there is not there the temple of God and Christ?</p>
