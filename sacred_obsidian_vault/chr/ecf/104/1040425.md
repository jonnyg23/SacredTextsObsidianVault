 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter 26.—</span>50.  Also another Lucius of Membresa <a name="fr_1829"></a><a href="#fn_1829"><font size="1">1830</font></a>
  said:  "It is written, God heareth not sinners. <a name="fr_1830"></a><a href="#fn_1830"><font size="1">1831</font></a>
   How can he who is a sinner be heard in baptism?" <a name="fr_1831"></a><a href="#fn_1831"><font size="1">1832</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxvi-p5"></a><a name="page_507"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 507</font></a> <a name="v.iv.ix.xxvi-Page_507"></a>51.  We answer:  How is the covetous man heard, or the robber, and usurer, and murderer?  Are they not sinners?  And yet Cyprian, while he finds fault with them in the Catholic Church, yet tolerates them.</p>
