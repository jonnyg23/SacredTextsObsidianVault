 <p> <a name="v.v.iv.iii"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.v.iv.iii-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.iii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 3.—</span>6.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.iii-p1.2"></a>Petilianus</span> said:  "For what we look to is the conscience of the giver, to cleanse that of the recipient."</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.v.iv.iii-p2"></a>7.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.iii-p2.1"></a>Augustin</span> answered:  We therefore need have no anxiety about the conscience of Christ.  But if you assert any man to be the giver, be he who he may, there will be no <a name="page_531"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 531</font></a> <a name="v.v.iv.iii-Page_531"></a>certainty about the cleansing of the recipient, because there is no certainty about the conscience of the giver.</p>
