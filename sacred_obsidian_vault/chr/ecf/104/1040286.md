 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.v.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.v.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 8.—</span>11.  Likewise Felix of Buslacene said:  "In admitting heretics without the baptism of the Church, let no one prefer custom to reason and truth; because reason and truth always prevail to the exclusion of custom." <a name="fr_1276"></a><a href="#fn_1276"><font size="1">1277</font></a>
   Nothing could be better, if it be reason, and if it be truth; but this we shall see presently.  Meanwhile, it is clear from the words of this man also that the custom was the other way.</p>
