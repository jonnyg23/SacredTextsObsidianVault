 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxi-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter 21.—</span>40.  Julianus of Telepte <a name="fr_1816"></a><a href="#fn_1816"><font size="1">1817</font></a>
  said:  "It is written, A man can receive nothing, except it be given him from heaven; <a name="fr_1817"></a><a href="#fn_1817"><font size="1">1818</font></a>
  if heresy is from heaven, it can give baptism." <a name="fr_1818"></a><a href="#fn_1818"><font size="1">1819</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxi-p5"></a>41.  Let him hear another also saying:  If covetousness is from heaven, it can give baptism.  And yet the covetous do confer it; so therefore also may the heretics.</p>
