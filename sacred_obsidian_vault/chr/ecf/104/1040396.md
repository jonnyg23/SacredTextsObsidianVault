 <p> <a name="v.iv.viii.xlii"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.viii.xlii-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.viii.xlii-p1.1"></a>Chapter 42.—</span>81.  Adelphius of Thasbalte <a name="fr_1742"></a><a href="#fn_1742"><font size="1">1743</font></a>
  said:  "It is surely without cause that they find fault with the truth in false and invidious terms, saying that we rebaptize, since the Church does not rebaptize heretics, but baptizes them." <a name="fr_1743"></a><a href="#fn_1743"><font size="1">1744</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.viii.xlii-p4"></a>82.  Truly enough it does not rebaptize them, because it only baptizes those who were not baptized before; and this earlier custom has only been confirmed in a later Council by a more careful perfecting of the truth.</p>
