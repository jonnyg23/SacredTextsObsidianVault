 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.v.iv.lxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.lxvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter 66.—</span>147.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.lxvi-p1.2"></a>Petilianus</span> said:  "Blessed are they which do hunger and thirst after righteousness:  for they shall be filled.  To you it seems to be righteousness that you thirst after our blood."</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.v.iv.lxvi-p2"></a>148.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.lxvi-p2.1"></a>Augustin</span> answered:  What shall I say unto thee, O man, except that thou art calumnious?  The unity of Christ, indeed, is hungering and thirsting after all of you; and I would that it might swallow you up, for then would you be no longer heretics.</p>
