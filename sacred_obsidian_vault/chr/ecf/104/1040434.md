 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxxv-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter 35.—</span>68. Pudentianus of Cuiculi <a name="fr_1850"></a><a href="#fn_1850"><font size="1">1851</font></a>
  said:  "My recent ordination to the episcopate induced me, brethren, to wait and hear what my elders would decide.  For it is plain that heresies have and can have nothing; and so, if any come from them, it is determined righteously that they should be baptized." <a name="fr_1851"></a><a href="#fn_1851"><font size="1">1852</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.ix.xxxv-p4"></a>69.  As, therefore, we have already answered those who went before, for whose judgment this man was waiting, so be it understood that we have answered himself.</p>
