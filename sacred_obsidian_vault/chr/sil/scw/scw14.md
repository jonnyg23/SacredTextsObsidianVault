 <h2 align="center"><i>XIV<br>
 THOUGHT AND DEED</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 206------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>333 <font size="1">(V. 89)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST WIN IT HERE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Here must thy deed be done.<br>
 'Twere an undreamed-of thing<br>
 That he who wins no Kingdom here<br>
 Should there become a King.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>334 <font size="1">(II. 75)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST ALSO BEAR FRUIT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou dost drink the Blood of God<br>
 And yet no fruit be found in thee,<br>
 On thee shall fall a curse more fell<br>
 Than once did blast that barren tree.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>335 <font size="1">(I. 53)</font><br>
 <big>VIRTUE REMAINETH AT REST</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If putting Virtue into act<br>
 Thou findest drudgery and moil,<br>
 Virtue thou hast not yet attained<br>
 But after Virtue still dost toil.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>336 <font size="1">(I. 273)</font><br>
 <big>RISE ABOVE HOLINESS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou art holy, it is well; but wouldst thou find<br>
 Favour with God and Man, leave holiness behind.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 207------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>337 <font size="1">(VI. 33)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MUST CHANGE HIMSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Everything changeth, Man. Canst thou remain alone<br>
 Careless of betterment and changeless as a Stone?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>338 <font size="1">(VI. 72)</font><br>
 <big>SLUGGARDRY WINNETH NOT HEAVEN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Sluggard, bestir thyself! Wilt thou<br>
 For ever lie abed and doze?<br>
 Heaven will not fly into thy mouth<br>
 While thou art taking thy repose.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>339 <font size="1">(VI. 42)</font><br>
 <big>WHO STANDS UNMOVED BELONGS NOT TO THE WHOLE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Sun gives movement unto all,<br>
 And makes the Stars dance in the sky:<br>
 If I still stand immovable<br>
 No part in the great Whole have I.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>340 <font size="1">(IV. 203)</font><br>
 <big>A DARKENED HEART SEETH NOT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Give heed unto the Flame. If lamps are burning dim,<br>
 The Bridegroom when he comes, who shall distinguish him.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 208------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>341 <font size="1">(VI. 146)</font><br>
 <big>THE WORK PROVETH THE MASTER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>A Master of the Craft art thou<br>
 When Virtue is but thought and planned:<br>
 But when it comes to Workmanship<br>
 Thou show'st thyself a Prentice-hand.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>342 <font size="1">(V. 64)</font><br>
 <big>WE SERVE OURSELVES, NOT GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is not served by Fast, Vigil or Litany;<br>
 Thou rather serv'st thyself, being purified thereby.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>343 <font size="1">(I. 153)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST BECOME A CHILD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Unless thou dost become a Child<br>
 Thou canst not enter in the place<br>
 Where all God's children are—for thee<br>
 The doorway hath too small a space.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>344 <font size="1">(V. 286)</font><br>
 <big>SIMPLICITY MUST BE INTELLIGENT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I honour that Simplicity<br>
 To which God joins intelligence,<br>
 But scarcely worth the name I count<br>
 Simplicity that lacketh sense.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 209------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>345 <font size="1">(I. 229)</font><br>
 <big>ANGER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Anger is like the fire of Hell.<br>
 If it break out within thy breast,<br>
 It burneth up the little bed<br>
 Whereon the Holy Ghost doth rest.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>346 <font size="1">(VI. 198)</font><br>
 <big>SAFETY IN SECLUSION</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou wouldst shun those strangers, Bride,<br>
  Who seek to be thy paramours,<br>
 Then close the casement shutters fast<br>
  And linger not at open doors.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>347 <font size="1">(VI. 163)</font><br>
 <big>HATE MAKETH ITSELF HATED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If Hate and envy rule thy heart<br>
 When thou for gifts dost supplicate,<br>
 The gift thy prayers achieve will be<br>
 Return of Envy and of Hate.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>348 <font size="1">(IV. 203)</font><br>
 <big>MAN FINDETH AS HE SEEKETH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>As thou dost seek, so shalt thou find;<br>
 As thou dost knock and dost implore,<br>
 So shall the gift be unto thee,<br>
 And so the opening of the door.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 210------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>349 <font size="1">(IV. 14)</font><br>
 <big>GOD GIVETH THE GREAT IN THE SMALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Take what God giveth thee. He giveth Great in Small,<br>
 Gold in base slag, where we surmise it least of all.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>350 <font size="1">(V. 49)</font><br>
 <big>THE FINEST WISDOM</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Climb not too high; frame no unneedful subtleties;<br>
 The finest Wisdom is to be not overwise.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>351 <font size="1">(IV. 122)</font><br>
 <big>GOD SEETH NOT ABOVE HIMSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God seeth not above Himself.<br>
 If thou dost seek to raise thy height<br>
 Above thy stature, have a care<br>
 Lest thou shouldst vanish from His sight.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>352 <font size="1">(I. 265)</font><br>
 <big>UNITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Ah, were men's voices like the wood-birds' melody—<br>
 Each happy note distinct, but all in harmony!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 211------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>353 <font size="1">(I. 266)</font><br>
 <big>NOTHING HATH WORTH FOR THE SCORNER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Nightingale mocks not the Cuckoo's note, 'tis true,<br>
 And yet you scorn my song if I sing not as you.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>354 <font size="1">(I. 218)</font><br>
 <big>DIVINE SEEING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Who in his fellow-man sees God and Christ, none else,<br>
 He seeth with the light wherein the Godhead dwells.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>355 <font size="1">(VI. 263)</font><br>
 <big>CONCLUSION</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Friend, it is now enough. Wouldst thou read more, go hence,<br>
 Become thyself the Writing and thyself the Sense.<br></td></tr></table>
