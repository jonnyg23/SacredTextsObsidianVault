 <h2 align="center"><i>II<br>
 GOD</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 110------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 110</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>31 <font size="1">(II. 48)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS KNOWN IN THE CREATURE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The hidden God becometh known<br>
  And general to mankind<br>
 In the created World of Things<br>
  Which He hath fashioned and designed.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>32 <font size="1">(V. 214)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS ALL IN ALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God in the Christ is God;<br>
 In angels, Form Angelical;<br>
 In men is Man; and in the rest<br>
 Is what thou wilt, the All in all.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>33 <font size="1">(V. 65)</font><br>
 <big>GOD CANNOT HIDE HIMSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God cannot ever hide Himself—if hid He seem,<br>
 'Tis thou that hast devised a hiding-place for Him.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>34 <font size="1">(V. 91)</font><br>
 <big>IN WHAT YEAR THE WORLD WAS CREATED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>How date the year when God created Heaven and Earth?<br>
 Not otherwise than thus: The First Year of God's Birth.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 111------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 111</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>35 <font size="1">(II. 40)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS THE SMALLEST AND THE GREATEST</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>My God, how great is God! My God, how God is small!<br>
 Small as the smallest thing, great—needs must be—as All.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>36 <font size="1">(V. 75)</font><br>
 <big>NAUGHT EXISTETH WITHOUT JOY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Naught that is joyless can endure.<br>
 Even the Being of God would pass,<br>
 Had He no pleasure in Himself,<br>
 And wither like the new-mown grass.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>37 <font size="1">(V. 189)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS ETERNALLY IN LOVE WITH HIS OWN BEAUTY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is so super-beautiful<br>
 That He beholdeth in a trance<br>
 Of rapture from eternity<br>
 The Splendour of His Countenance.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>38 <font size="1">(II. 190)</font><br>
 <big>OF GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God joyeth in Himself. Himself He cannot cloy.<br>
 For in Himself alone hath He the highest joy.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 112------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 112</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>39 <font size="1">(V. 50)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS NOT VIRTUOUS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is not virtuous. Out of Him virtue streams,<br>
 As water from the Sea and from the Sun sun-beams.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>40 <font size="1">(V. 93)</font><br>
 <big>GOD CANNOT BE WRATH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is not ever wrath with us.<br>
 His wrath is but our dream.<br>
 It were a thing impossible<br>
 That wrath should be in Him.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>41 <font size="1">(I. 137)</font><br>
 <big>GOD DAMNETH NO MAN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Dost thou complain of God? Thyself thyself dost damn.<br>
 Damn thee He never would—of this full sure I am.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>42 <font size="1">(V. 103)</font><br>
 <big>GOD DIED NOT FOR THE FIRST TIME ON THE CROSS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Not first upon the Cross God let Himself be slain,<br>
 For see! He lieth dead there at the feet of Cain.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 113------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 113</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>43 <font size="1">(V. 43)</font><br>
 <big>HOW GOD IS SO VERY JUST</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Behold, God is so just, were there aught higher than He,<br>
 He'd honour it above Himself on bended knee.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>44 <font size="1">(I. 207)</font><br>
 <big>THE FINEST INN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Inn most delectable! God is Himself the Wine,<br>
 Music and Feast and Servant too of them that dine.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>45 <font size="1">(III. 142)</font><br>
 <big>GOD DWELLETH IN GENTLENESS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Have quiet in thy heart; for God is sought in vain<br>
 In Fire, in Earthquake, in the roaring Hurricane.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>46 <font size="1">(IV. 177)</font><br>
 <big>ASTONISHMENT AT GOD'S FAMILIARITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>It is a wonderment that I,<br>
 Who am but dust and ash and clod,<br>
 Should dare to make myself the friend<br>
 And very hail-fellow of God!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 114------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_114"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 114</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>47 <font size="1">(I. 279)</font><br>
 <big>I-HOOD SHAPETH NAUGHT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Now this, now that, thou striv'st to shape<br>
 With thine own I for instrument.<br>
 Ah, wouldst thou but let God shape all<br>
 Accordingly to His intent!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>48 <font size="1">(IV. 143)</font><br>
 <big>THE LOVELIEST TONE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>In all eternity there is no lovelier Tone<br>
 Than when man's heart soundeth with God in Unison.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>49 <font size="1">(IV. 133)</font><br>
 <big>MAN IS A COAL.</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Man, thou art like a piece of coal;<br>
 And if thou liest not in Him,<br>
 Who is thy Fire and thy Light,<br>
 Then art thou black and cold and dim.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>50 <font size="1">(I. 79)</font><br>
 <big>GOD BEARETH PERFECT FRUIT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Vine of God bears perfect fruit.<br>
 If any man bid me resign.<br>
 God-perfectness, he first must break<br>
 Me, branch-wise, from the Vine.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 115------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 115</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>51 <font size="1">(II. 4)</font><br>
 <big>THE ETERNAL YEA AND NAY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God's everlasting word is Yea,<br>
 Nay is the Devil's counter-cry:<br>
 To be all Yea and One with God<br>
 His own denial doth deny.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>52 <font size="1">(IV. 126)</font><br>
 <big>THE INSCRUTABLE CAUSE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God hath all things within Himself—His Heaven, His bliss.<br>
 Why then hath He created us? We know not this.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>53 <font size="1">(I. 67)</font><br>
 <big>THE CHILD CRIETH AFTER THE MOTHER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>As for his mother's breast a weaned child maketh moan,<br>
 Crieth the soul for God, crieth for Him alone.<br></td></tr></table>
