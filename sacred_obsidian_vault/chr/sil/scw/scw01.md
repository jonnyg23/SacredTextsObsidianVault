 <h2 align="center"><i>I<br>
 GODHEAD</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 100------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 100</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>1 <font size="1">(II. 188)</font><br>
 <big>BEING IS NOT MEASURED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Turn wheresoe'er I will, I find no evidence<br>
 of End, Beginning, Centre or Circumference.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>2 <font size="1">(I. 263)</font><br>
 <big>GOD NEVER EXPLORETH HIMSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Thought and Deed of Deity<br>
 Are of such richness and extent<br>
 That It remaineth to Itself<br>
 An Undiscovered Continent.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>3 <font size="1">(VI. 174)</font><br>
 <big>IN THE SEA MANY ARE ONE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>A Loaf holds many grains of corn<br>
 And many myriad drops the Sea:<br>
 So is God's Oneness Multitude<br>
 And that great Multitude are we.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>4 <font size="1">(V. I)</font><br>
 <big>ALL INTO ONE AGAIN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The All proceedeth from the One,<br>
 And into One must All regress:<br>
 If otherwise, the All remains<br>
 Asunder-riven manyness.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 101------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>5 <font size="1">(I. 25)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS NOT GRASPED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is an utter Nothingness,<br>
 Beyond the touch of Time and Place:<br>
 The more thou graspest after Him,<br>
 The more he fleeth thy embrace.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>6 <font size="1">(I. 15)</font><br>
 <big>THE SUPER-DEITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>What hath been told of God is not enough for me:<br>
 My life and light flow from the Super-Deity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>7 <font size="1">(I. 284)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MUST GO BEYOND ALL KNOWLEDGE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>What Cherubs know sufficeth not: beyond their zone<br>
 I fain would take my flight unto where nothing's known.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>8 <font size="1">(I. 285)</font><br>
 <big>THE KNOWER MUST BECOME THE KNOWN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Naught ever can be known in God: One and Alone<br>
 Is He. To know Him, Knower must be one with Known.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 102------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>9 <font size="1">(I. 43)</font><br>
 <big>MAN LOVETH EVEN WITHOUT KNOWING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>One only Thing I love and know not what it is:<br>
 Because I know it not, therefore I've chosen this.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>10 <font size="1">(V. 41)</font><br>
 <big>MORE KNOWN LESS UNDERSTOOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The more thou knowest God, the more thou wilt confess<br>
 That what He truly is, thou knowest less and less.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>11 <font size="1">(I. 178)</font><br>
 <big>THE BLAME IS THINE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If gazing on the Sun endangereth thy sight,<br>
 The blame is in thine eyes, and not in that great Light.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>12 <font size="1">(I. 294)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS WITHOUT WILL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>We pray: Thy Will be done! and lo! He hath no Will:<br>
 God in His changelessness eternally is still.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 103------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>13 <font size="1">(IV. 166)</font><br>
 <big>THE REST AND WORK OF GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Rested God never hath, nor toiled—'tis manifest,<br>
 For all His rest is work and all His work is rest.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>14 <font size="1">(III. 170)</font><br>
 <big>OF ETERNAL MOTION</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The secret of Eternal Motion thou wouldst learn,<br>
 I, of Eternal Rest: which is of more concern?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>15 <font size="1">(I. 49)</font><br>
 <big>REST IS THE HIGHEST GOOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Rest is the highest Good. I'd keep both eyes close pressed,<br>
 That He might have repose, were God Himself not Rest.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>16 <font size="1">(I. 44)</font><br>
 <big>THE SOMETHING MUST BE FORSAKEN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou dost love a Something, Man,<br>
 Thou lovest naught that doth abide.<br>
 God is not This nor That—do thou<br>
 Leave Somethings utterly aside.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 104------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 104</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>17 <font size="1">(V. 328)</font><br>
 <big>SIN TROUBLETH NOT GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God feeleth pain for sin in thee<br>
  As in His son,<br>
 But in His Self of Deity<br>
  He feeleth none.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>18 <font size="1">(V. 16)</font><br>
 <big>THROUGH THEE GOD LOSETH NAUGHT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Choose, Man, which of the twain thou wilt,<br>
 Thy self-destruction or thy peace.<br>
 Through thee God suffereth no loss,<br>
 Neither through thee hath He increase.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>19 <font size="1">(V. 34)</font><br>
 <big>GOD LOVETH NAUGHT BUT HIMSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is so dear unto Himself,<br>
 Folded in self so utterly,<br>
 That He can never cherish love<br>
 For anything that is not He.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>20 <font size="1">(V. 92)</font><br>
 <big>GOD FORESEETH NOTHING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God foresees nothing—'tis thy dull and blundering sense<br>
 Doth clothe Him with the attribute of Providence.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 105------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>21 <font size="1">(V. 173)</font><br>
 <big>GOD HATH NO THOUGHTS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God thinketh naught. Yea verily,<br>
 Were thought in Him, then might He sway<br>
 —Which were a thing unthinkable—<br>
 Now this way, now the other way.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>22 <font size="1">(II. 55)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS AND LIVETH NOT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God <i>is</i>, but in God-wise. He loves and lives, 'tis true,<br>
 But not as I or thou or other beings do.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>23 <font size="1">(V. 124)</font><br>
 <big>WHAT IS SPOKEN OF GOD IS MORE FALSE THAN TRUE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Since thou dost measure God by creature qualities,<br>
 There's more of lie than truth in thy theologies.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>24 <font size="1">(V. 358)</font><br>
 <big>GOD BECOMES WHAT HE WILLS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Eternal Spirit, God becomes<br>
 All that He wills to be—but still<br>
 Abideth ever as He is,<br>
 Without a form, an aim, a will.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 106------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>25 <font size="1">(I. 115)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST THYSELF BE SUN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I must myself be Sun. I with my beams must dye<br>
 The all-uncoloured Sea of the whole Deity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>26 <font size="1">(II. 17)</font><br>
 <big>GOD DENIETH HIMSELF TO NONE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Take, drink, all that thou wilt or canst—'tis given thee free,<br>
 Thou hast the whole of Godhead for thy Hostelry.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>27 <font size="1">(V. 339)</font><br>
 <big>NO CREATURE FATHOMETH THE GODHEAD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>No creature fathometh how deep the Godhead is,<br>
 Even the soul of Christ is lost in that Abyss.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>28 <font size="1">(IV. 38)</font><br>
 <big>GOD NAUGHT AND ALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is a Spirit, a Fire, a Being and a Flame,<br>
 And yet again He is not one of all these same.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 107------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>29 <font size="1">(IV. 1)</font><br>
 <big>GOD BECOMETH WHAT HE NEVER WAS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Here in the midst of Time God doth become what He,<br>
 The Unbecome, was not in all Eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>30 <font size="1">(II. 245)</font><br>
 <big>THE GODHEAD IS MY MOTHER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God hath begotten me—such my true genesis,<br>
 But do thou never ask me who my Mother is.<br></td></tr></table>
