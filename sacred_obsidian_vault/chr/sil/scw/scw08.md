 <h2 align="center"><i>VIII<br>
 TIME AND ETERNITY</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 158------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_158"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 158</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>181 <font size="1">(II. 153)</font><br>
 <big>ETERNITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>What is Eternity? It is not That, not This,<br>
 Not Now, not Aught, not Naught—I know not what it is.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>182 <font size="1">(II. 65)</font><br>
 <big>ETERNITY IS NOT MEASURED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Eternity knows naught of hour and day and year.<br>
 Alas, I have not found the centre of its sphere!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>183 <font size="1">(II. 183)</font><br>
 <big>IN THE CENTRE EVERYTHING IS SEEN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Stand at the centre-point—instantly shall appear<br>
 All that befell or now befalls, in Heaven and here.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>184 <font size="1">(I. 47)</font><br>
 <big>TIME IS ETERNITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Eternity is one with Time,<br>
 Time with Eternity, and hence<br>
 Indifference between them lie,<br>
 Thyself dost make the difference.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 159------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_159"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 159</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>185 <font size="1">(I. 189)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MAKETH TIME</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>'Tis thou thyself that makest Time.<br>
 And like a clock thy senses run:<br>
 Do thou but quiet their unrest—<br>
 The clock is stopped and Time is gone.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>186 <font size="1">(V. 23)</font><br>
 <big>TIME IS NOT FAST</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>"Time flieth fast" we say, but who<br>
 Hath seen the fleeting of Time's wings?<br>
 Time standeth moveless in a view<br>
 That visioneth the Whole of Things.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>187 <font size="1">(V. 148)</font><br>
 <big>IN ETERNITY ALL HAPPENETH TOGETHER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>There, in Eternity, events<br>
 Together strike a single chime:<br>
 There is no After, no Before,<br>
 As here, as in the realm of Time.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>188 <font size="1">(IV. 200)</font><br>
 <big>HOW TO SHORTEN TIME</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Man, if thou findest Time on earth drag on too slow.<br>
 Turn unto God—live in the Everlasting Now.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 160------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_160"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 160</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>189 <font size="1">(V. 127)</font><br>
 <big>THE SOUL IS ABOVE TIME</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Soul, which is eternal Spirit,<br>
 Standeth above Time's sovranty:<br>
 Already in this present world<br>
 She liveth in Eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>190 <font size="1">(V. 111)</font><br>
 <big>OUT OF GOD, LIVING IS BEING DEAD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Livest thou not in God, O Man,<br>
 —And let this be well credited—<br>
 Though thou dost live a thousand years,<br>
 A thousand years thou art dead.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>191 <font size="1">(II. 168)</font><br>
 <big>ONE IS AS OLD AS THE OTHER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>A Child who on this earth but one short hour appears,<br>
 Already he hath lived Methuselah's long years.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>192 <font size="1">(III. 180)</font><br>
 <big>GOD KNOWETH NOT HIS BEGINNING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>How long hath God been God? Be silent, ask not this!<br>
 So long, God knoweth not Himself how long it is.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 161------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_161"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 161</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>193 <font size="1">(IV. 165)</font><br>
 <big>GOD STILL CREATES THE WORLD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God still creates the World. Strange doth this thing appear?<br>
 God knoweth neither Then nor Now, as men know here.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>194 <font size="1">(III. 90)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST BLOSSOM NOW</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>O blossom, blossom, frozen soul!<br>
 May is abroad before thy door.<br>
 If thou dost blossom not to-day<br>
 Then art thou dead for evermore.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>195 <font size="1">(I. 185)</font><br>
 <big>PLACE IS WITHIN THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Nowise dost thou inhabit Place,<br>
  Place doth inhabit thee.<br>
 Cast Place away!—then now and here<br>
  Standeth Eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>196 <font size="1">(III. 112)</font><br>
 <big>THE HEART IS IMMEASURABLE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>A Heart that finds its full contcnt in Time and Place<br>
 In truth knows not its own Immeasurableness.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 162------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_162"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 162</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>197 <font size="1">(I. 187)</font><br>
 <big>THE BREADTH OF THE SOUL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Heaven is too little for me, Earth a narrow cell:<br>
 Where shall I find a space wherein my Soul can dwell?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>198 <font size="1">(I. 188)</font><br>
 <big>TIME AND ETERNITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>From Time into Eternity<br>
 Thou tellest me to get me hence!<br>
 Between Eternity and Time<br>
 Is there then any difference?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>199 <font size="1">(IV. 90)</font><br>
 <big>VIRTUE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>"Virtue its own Reward"—so runs the argument:<br>
 If the Reward be here, in time, then I dissent.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>200 <font size="1">(IV. 180)</font><br>
 <big>THE DAY AND DAWN OF SOULS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Here in this present time God dawns in souls, but soon,<br>
 When they have put their splendour on, He'll be their noon.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 163------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 163</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>201 <font size="1">(V. 67)</font><br>
 <big>HOW FAR THE WAY TO HEAVEN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Think not the journey overlong if thou wouldst fare<br>
 To Heaven—take but one short stride and thou art there.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>202 <font size="1">(I. 30)</font><br>
 <big>THERE IS NO DEATH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I disbelieve in Death. Hourly I die—what then?<br>
 To new and better Life hourly I rise again.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>203 <font size="1">(I. 34)</font><br>
 <big>DEATH DEIFIETH THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>When thou art dead and all thy life<br>
 Is taken up by God in His,<br>
 Then shalt thou rightly, nor till then,<br>
 Rank with the all-high Deities.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>204 <font size="1">(IV. 81)</font><br>
 <big>DEATH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Death doth not trouble me. 'Tis through that door I come<br>
 Unto the place which long hath been my spirit's home.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 164------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_164"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 164</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>205 <font size="1">(IV. 77)</font><br>
 <big>SPIRITUAL DYING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Die ere thou diest—dying, then thou diest not:<br>
 Die not—perchance then, dying, thou shalt die and rot.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>206 <font size="1">(I. 29)</font><br>
 <big>ETERNAL DEATH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Death which no new Life within itself doth bear,<br>
 Of all deaths 'tis that death which most my soul doth fear.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>207 <font size="1">(IV. 103)</font><br>
 <big>LIFE AND DEATH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>No richer death than that which Life engendereth,<br>
 No noble Life than that which springeth out of death.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>208 <font size="1">(I. 190)</font><br>
 <big>SAMENESS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I know not what to do! All things are one to me:<br>
 Place, Unplace, Day, Night, Joy, Pain, Time, Eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
