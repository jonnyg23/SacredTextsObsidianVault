 <h2 align="center"><i>X<br>
 LOVE</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 172------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_172"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 172</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>229 <font size="1">(V. 242)</font><br>
 <big>THE HIGHEST GOOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>What is the highest Good? Much talk hath been hereof<br>
 And high debate: I swear the highest Good is Love.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>230 <font size="1">(V. 241)</font><br>
 <big>EVERYTHING IS SUBJECT TO LOVE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love is the Lord of All. Even the Trinity<br>
 Hath been in thrall to Love from all eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>231 <font size="1">(V. 243)</font><br>
 <big>THE NATURE OF GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love is God's nature. He can do naught else. Wouldst thou<br>
 Be God, then likewise love in every instant's Now.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>232 <font size="1">(V. 292)</font><br>
 <big>BEAUTY COMETH FROM LOVE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Beauty is born of Love alone.<br>
  The Countenance Divine<br>
 Hath all its Loveliness from Love—<br>
  Else it would cease to shine.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 173------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>233 <font size="1">(V. 295)</font><br>
 <big>THE MORE LOVING, THE HAPPIER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love is the measure of the heart's Felicity;<br>
 The more 'tis filled with Love, the happier thou wilt be.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>234 <font size="1">(V. 320)</font><br>
 <big>THE SHORTEST WAY TO GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Pass through Love's gate if thou wouldst go<br>
  The shortest way to God:<br>
 Who takes the Path of Knowledge, long<br>
  Must tarry on the road.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>235 <font size="1">(V. 307)</font><br>
 <big>LOVE HATH MORE IN COMMON WITH GOD THAN WISDOM</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love unannounced goes in to God,<br>
  Hath instant audience:<br>
 Long in the antechamber wait<br>
  Wit and intelligence.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>236 <font size="1">(II. 47)</font><br>
 <big>LOVE SEEKETH NOT REWARD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Man, if thou lovest God and seekest hire for this,<br>
 'Tis plain thou tastest not what Love and Loving is.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 174------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>237 <font size="1">(V. 303)</font><br>
 <big>THE TOKEN OF FALSE LOVE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Wouldst thou discern false Love from true, the token's this—<br>
 False Love seeks self and fades under adversities.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>238 <font size="1">(III. 205)</font><br>
 <big>HE WHO IS MOST IN LOVE IS MOST HOLY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Who is the greatest saint? He who is most in love.<br>
 For saintship Love alone is warranty enough.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>239 <font size="1">(V. 226)</font><br>
 <big>THE NATURE OF HOLINESS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The more thou lovest—Love's the very heart<br>
 Of Holiness—the holier thou art.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>240 <font size="1">(III. 173)</font><br>
 <big>LOVE IS THE SIGN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Thou askest which of all the people are God's friends?<br>
 Behold the folk who carry Love in heart and hands!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 175------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 175</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>241 <font size="1">(III. 164)</font><br>
 <big>LOVE IS THE SOUL OF BELIEF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Belief alone is dead. It cannot live or move<br>
 Until united with its soul—its soul is Love.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>242 <font size="1">(V. 108)</font><br>
 <big>BELIEF ALONE IS A HOLLOW CASK</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Belief alone, all void of Love,<br>
 (So have I weighed it in my thought)<br>
 Is like unto a hollow Cask—<br>
 It soundeth, but within is naught.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>243 <font size="1">(V. 161)</font><br>
 <big>NONE LAYETH HIS HEAD ON CHRIST'S BREAST BUT JOHN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Ah, think not, Child, that thou canst lay thy head upon<br>
 The breast of the Lord Christ ere thou becomest John.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>244 <font size="1">(VI. 204)</font><br>
 <big>THE SPIRlTUAL PASSWORD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love is the password. He to whom it is not given<br>
 Must never hope to cross the frontier-line of Heaven.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 176------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 176</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>245 <font size="1">(V. 297)</font><br>
 <big>GOD CANNOT BE LOVED WITHOUT GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Did not God love Himself through thee and in thee, Man,<br>
 Thy love for Him would ever fail of its full span.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>246 <font size="1">(III. 201)</font><br>
 <big>GOD LOVETH TO GIVE GREAT GIFTS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Being Himself so great, greatly God loves to give,<br>
 But ah! man's little heart is so small to receive.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>247 <font size="1">(V. 210)</font><br>
 <big>NEW AND OLD LOVE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>When Love is new, it foams like young and heady wine:<br>
 When stiller grown, 'tis proof that it is old and fine.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>248 <font size="1">(I. 163)</font><br>
 <big>MAN SHOULD LOVE MANKIND</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Thou lovest none? 'Tis well. He has the better mind<br>
 Who loves not any man, but loves in man Mankind.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 177------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 177</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>249 <font size="1">(II. 60)</font><br>
 <big>OF LOVE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>He wills and loveth right who wills and loveth naught:<br>
 Who loveth what he wills, loveth not what he ought.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>250 <font size="1">(V. 289)</font><br>
 <big>VIRTUE WITHOUT LOVE IS NOTHING WORTH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Before God Virtue cannot stand<br>
  Naked and bare;<br>
 It must put on the robe of Love—<br>
  Then is it fair.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>251 <font size="1">(II. 130)</font><br>
 <big>IT MUST BE GILDED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>All that thou dost must be o'erlaid<br>
 With gold of Love—if otherwise,<br>
 Thyself and all thy works will find<br>
 But little favour in God's eyes.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>252 <font size="1">(V. 299)</font><br>
 <big>AS THE PERSON, SO THE MERIT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Richer reward the Bride doth earn<br>
 From God with but a single kiss,<br>
 Than all the hirelings though they wear<br>
 Themselves to death with drudgeries.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>

 <a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 178</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>253 <font size="1">(II. 234)</font><br>
 <big>CHOOSE WHICH THOU WILT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love is the Queen; the Virtues are<br>
  The Maids who wait upon her;<br>
 The serving-women, Work and Deed—<br>
  Which wilt thou choose to honour?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>254 <font size="1">(V. 302)</font><br>
 <big>THE QUICKEST</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Love is the quickest thing and of itself can fly<br>
 To topmost Heaven in but the twinkling of an eye.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>255 <font size="1">(V. 306)</font><br>
 <big>AN UNWOUNDED HEART IS UNSOUND</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>A Heart which God hath never wounded with Love's wound,<br>
 Scatheless though it appear, is never whole and sound.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>256 <font size="1">(V. 211)</font><br>
 <big>SERAPHIC LOVE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The purest Love—seraphic—<br>
 Is not easy to divine,<br>
 Because it is so quiet,<br>
 By any outward sign.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>

 <a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 179</font></a>

 <p> </p>
 
 <p>257 <font size="1">(VI. 159)</font><br>
 <big>THE HEART MUST BE LOADED WITH BALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Put a live charge into your gun!<br>
  A puff of smoke and is that all?<br>
 Why, if you only load with blank,<br>
  The bang is there, but where's the ball?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>258 <font size="1">(VI. 164)</font><br>
 <big>THE ORDINANCE IS AS WE ORDAIN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>To him who wills his neighbour's weal<br>
 God gives what he soliciteth:<br>
 But if he wish his neighbour ill,<br>
 O then he prays for his own death!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>259 <font size="1">(V. 308)</font><br>
 <big>GOD'S COMMUNALITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>How broad God's communality!<br>
 He teacheth whomso'er He list,<br>
 The peasant lass no less than thee,<br>
 The art whereby He may be kissed.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>260 <font size="1">(III. 138)</font><br>
 <big>LOVE IS DEAD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Alas, Alas, Love's dead! How came she then to die?<br>
 She perished of the cold, for all men passed her by.<br></td></tr></table>
