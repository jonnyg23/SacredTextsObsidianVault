 <h2 align="center"><i>IV<br>
 ONENESS WITH THE DIVINE</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 128------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 128</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>85 <font size="1">(I. 8)</font><br>
 <big>GOD LIVETH NOT WITHOUT ME</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I know God cannot live one instant without Me:<br>
 If I should come to naught, needs must He cease to be.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>86 <font size="1">(I. 10)</font><br>
 <big>I AM AS GOD AND GOD AS I</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I am as great as God, He is as small as I:<br>
 No higher than I is He, nor I than He less high.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>87 <font size="1">(I. 14)</font><br>
 <big>A CHRISTIAN IS AS RICH AS GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I am as rich as God. He owns no particle<br>
 Of dust—believe me, Man!—that is not mine as well.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>88 <font size="1">(II. 178)</font><br>
 <big>ALL CONSISTS IN I AND THOU</big><br>
 (C<small>REATOR AND</small> C<small>REATURE</small>.)</p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Naught is but I and Thou. Were there nor Thou nor I,<br>
 Then God is no more God, and Heaven falls from the sky.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 129------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 129</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>89 <font size="1">(I. 68)</font><br>
 <big>DEEP CALLETH UNTO DEEP</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Deep calls to Deep. My spirit's Deep doth cry amain<br>
 To Deep of God: say, which is deeper of the twain?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>90 <font size="1">(II. 180)</font><br>
 <big>MAN IS NAUGHT, GOD ALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I am not I nor Thou: Thou art the I in Me:<br>
 Therefore I yield the meed of honour unto Thee.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>91 <font size="1">(II. 142)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST BE IT THYSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Ask not what is divine. It were too great a task<br>
 To comprehend—unless thou art what thou dost ask.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>92 <font size="1">(I. 81)</font><br>
 <big>GOD BLOSSOMS OUT OF HIS BRANCHES</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou art born of God, God blossometh in thee:<br>
 His Godhead is thy sap and flower-finery.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 130------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 130</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>93 <font size="1">(II. 120)</font><br>
 <big>MAN EATETH AND DRINKETH GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou art one with God, truly it may be said<br>
 Thou eat'st and drinkest God in every piece of bread.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>94 <font size="1">(III. 20)</font><br>
 <big>GOD-MAN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>That I may come to wealth, God comes to beggary:<br>
 That I may become He, lo! He becometh I.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>95 <font size="1">(I. 96)</font><br>
 <big>GOD CAN DO NOTHING WITHOUT ME</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God hath no potency to make<br>
 A single worm without my aid:<br>
 If I sustain it not with Him<br>
 Straightway its being is unmade.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>96 <font size="1">(I. 72)</font><br>
 <big>HOW IS GOD SEEN?</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>No Way there is by which to go<br>
 Unto the Light wherein God dwells:<br>
 Thou must thyself become the Light<br>
 Or God is hidden from thee else.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 131------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 131</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>97 <font size="1">(IV. 24)</font><br>
 <big>THE TRANSFORMATION</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Body must into Spirit pass,<br>
 And Spirit into Deity,<br>
 If thou wouldst have thy dearest wish<br>
 And know the perfect ecstasy.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>98 <font size="1">(II. 255)</font><br>
 <big>FIVE DEGREES IN GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Five ladder-rungs there are in God—<br>
  Slave, Friend, Son, Bride and Spouse.<br>
 Who climbeth higher unselfs himself,<br>
  Drops count of I's and Thou's.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>99 <font size="1">(V. 76)</font><br>
 <big>AS HIS FRIENDSHIP SO THE FRIEND</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Thou drinkest in the soul of him<br>
 With whom thou'rt friended—in the end<br>
 Becomest God, if friend of God,<br>
 And Devil, if the Devil's friend.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>100 <font size="1">(V. 200)</font><br>
 <big>A MAN IS CHANGED INTO WHAT HE LOVES</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Thou shalt become that thing itself<br>
 Which thou dost deem of dearest worth—<br>
 God shalt become if thou lov'st God,<br>
 And Earth if so thou lovest Earth.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 132------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_132"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 132</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>101 <font size="1">(V. 332)</font><br>
 <big>WHITHER MAN GOES WHEN HE DIES INTO GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>When I die into God, I once again return<br>
 There where I was eternally ere I was born.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>102 <font size="1">(V. 233)</font><br>
 <big>WHEN MAN IS GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Once I was God in God, or ever I was I,<br>
 And can be God again, if this I could but die.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>103 <font size="1">(VI. 175)</font><br>
 <big>UNION WITH GOD IS EASY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>'Tis easier, Man, to see thyself and God all one<br>
 Than open a closed eye—will it and it is done.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>104 <font size="1">(V. 259)</font><br>
 <big>GOD BECOMETH I BECAUSE I AFORETIME WAS HE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God doth become what now I am,<br>
 Assumes my manhood; what He is,<br>
 The same aforetime I have been:<br>
 Therefore it is He doeth this.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 133------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_133"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 133</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>105 <font size="1">(II. 159)</font><br>
 <big>SPIRIT IS AS ESSENCE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>My Spirit is a partial Being:<br>
 It yearns to be recentred in<br>
 That Essence whence it broke away,<br>
 Its primal Root and Origin.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>106 <font size="1">(IV. 12)</font><br>
 <big>ALL WEAL IN ONE THING ONLY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>From but one thing my all of Weal,<br>
 My all of Peace doth spring;<br>
 Though losing much upon the way,<br>
 I run with haste to this One Thing.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>107 <font size="1">(II. 201)</font><br>
 <big>MAN AND THE OTHER GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>What only difference lies 'twixt me and God? Confess!<br>
 I'll tell you in a word—nothing but Otherness.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>108 <font size="1">(IV. 10)</font><br>
 <big>COMPLETE BEATITUDE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>No man can ever know perfect Felicity<br>
 Till Otherness be swallowed up in Unity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 134------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 134</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>109 <font size="1">(IV. 181)</font><br>
 <big>OF THE BLESSED SOUL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Of Otherness the blessed Soul<br>
  Hath lost the very sense;<br>
 It is a single Light with God<br>
  And one Magnificence.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>110 <font size="1">(V. 126)</font><br>
 <big>THE DEATH OF I-HOOD STRENGTHENS GOD IN THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The more the I in me doth fail,<br>
 Diminish and sink lower,<br>
 So much the more the I of God<br>
 Aggrandizeth its power.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>111 <font size="1">(V. 234)</font><br>
 <big>EVERYTHING RETURNS TO ITS ORIGIN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Of earth was Body born and once<br>
  Again becometh earth:<br>
 And shall not Soul again become<br>
  God, since God gave it birth?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>112 <font size="1">(IV. 140)</font><br>
 <big>THE NOBLEST PRAYER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>That is the noblest prayer a man can pray when he<br>
 Becometh one with Him to Whom he bends his knee.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 135------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>113 <font size="1">(V. 219)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MUST NOT REMAIN MAN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Man, be not ever man! the summit must be gained!<br>
 In God's house Gods and Gods alone are entertained.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>114 <font size="1">(VI. 171)</font><br>
 <big>IN THE SEA EVERY DROP BECOMETH SEA</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>When to the Sea at last it comes<br>
 The smallest drop becometh Sea:<br>
 Even so thy Soul becometh God<br>
 When God at last absorbeth thee.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>115 <font size="1">(II. 172)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MUST BE A PHOENIX</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I will be Phoenix, burn myself in God, and then<br>
 Nothing shall sunder me from Him ever again.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>116 <font size="1">(IV. 135)</font><br>
 <big>THE STREAM BECOMETH SEA</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Here I, a Stream of Time, flow into Deity,<br>
 There I myself am the serene eternal Sea.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 136------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>117 <font size="1">(I. 23)</font><br>
 <big>THE SPIRITUAL MARY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I must be Mary and myself<br>
 Give birth to God, would I possess<br>
 —Nor can I otherwise—God's gift<br>
 Of everlasting Happiness.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>118 <font size="1">(I. 276)</font><br>
 <big>ONE THE OTHER'S BEGINNING AND END</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is my final end;<br>
 If then I am His origin,<br>
 From mine His Being floweth out,<br>
 To Him my Being floweth in.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>119 <font size="1">(I. 100)</font><br>
 <big>ONE UPHOLDETH THE OTHER</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God's need of me, my need of God,<br>
  Are equal in degree.<br>
 He helps to bear my being up<br>
  And I help Him to be.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>120 <font size="1">(IV. 153)</font><br>
 <big>THE SEA IN A LITTLE DROP</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Into this little drop, this I, how can it be<br>
 That there should flow the whole Sea of the Deity?<br></td></tr></table>
