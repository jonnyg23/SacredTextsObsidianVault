 <h2 align="center"><i>VI<br>
 SELF-ABANDONMENT</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 144------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 144</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>139 <font size="1">(I. 24)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MUST BE NAUGHT, WILL NAUGHT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou art Somewhat to thyself,<br>
 If Somewhat thou dost love and will,<br>
 If Somewhat knowest, Somewhat hast—<br>
 Thou carriest thy Burden still.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>140 <font size="1">(I. 58)</font><br>
 <big>SELF-SEEKING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If seeking God thou seekest Rest,<br>
 Then is thy reckoning out of trim:<br>
 God's hireling art thou, not His child,<br>
 Thou seekest thine own Self, not Him.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>141 <font size="1">(II. 57)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MUST GROW FREE FROM HIMSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Grow free from self, from all created things grow free,<br>
 Then God will graft His Godlike nature on to thee.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>142 <font size="1">(I. 143)</font><br>
 <big>'TIS SELFHOOD THAT DAMNS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Could but the Devil quit his His-ness, thereupon<br>
 The Devil thou wouldst see sitting upon God's throne<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 145------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 145</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>143 <font size="1">(V. 32)</font><br>
 <big>SELFWILL RUINS ALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If even in Christ himself there were Selfwill at all,<br>
 Despite his Blessedness, believe me, he would fall.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>144 <font size="1">(I. 138)</font><br>
 <big>THE MORE THOU GOEST OUT, THE MORE GOD COMETH IN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The more thyself out of thyself<br>
 Thou canst dischannel and outpour,<br>
 The more must God flow into thee<br>
 With all His Godhead more and more.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>145 <font size="1">(II. 140)</font><br>
 <big>SELF-NULLIFYING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Naught bringeth thee beyond thyself<br>
 So surely as Self-nullity:<br>
 The more thou canst annul thyself<br>
 The more thou hast of Deity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>146 <font size="1">(II. 136)</font><br>
 <big>ABANDONMENT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Go out—and so God goeth in;<br>
 Die to thyself—thou hast begun<br>
 To live to God; Be not—He is;<br>
 Do naught—His bidding's done.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 146------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 146</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>147 <font size="1">(V. 220)</font><br>
 <big>HOW GOD IS FOUND</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Seekest thou God, then must thou, Man,<br>
 First lose thy Self-identity,<br>
 Nor ever find again the trace<br>
 Of Self in all eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>148 <font size="1">(V. 186)</font><br>
 <big>OWNHOOD IS THE CAUSE OF ALL EVIL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Communion engendereth Peace;<br>
  But Ownhood giveth rise<br>
 To Persecution, War and Strife,<br>
  And all Calamities.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>149 <font size="1">(V. 238)</font><br>
 <big>"MINE" AND "THINE" ARE DAMNABLE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Two words, and nothing else in all the world, combine<br>
 To plunge thee into Hell—two hateful words, Mine, Thine.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>150 <font size="1">(II. 72)</font><br>
 <big>HE WHO CAN SING WITH THE ANGELS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>He who can soar above himself<br>
  One instant at the most,<br>
 He too can sing the Gloria<br>
  With the angeleic host.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 147------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_147"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 147</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>151 <font size="1">(V. 33)</font><br>
 <big>WHEN GOD MOST DELIGHTS TO BE WITH US</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God, whose extreme delight it is<br>
  To dwell with thee, doth come<br>
 Most willingly into thy house<br>
  When thou art not at home.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>152 <font size="1">(I. 46)</font><br>
 <big>THE BLESSED UNTHING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I am a blessed Thing if I<br>
 Can but unthing myself, forgo<br>
 All my community with things,<br>
 My cognizance of things unknow.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>153 <font size="1">(I. 92)</font><br>
 <big>HE WHO IS WHOLLY DEIFIED</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Who is as were he not, nor yet had come to be,<br>
 He is become—O Happiness!—sheer Deity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>154 <font size="1">(IV. 139)</font><br>
 <big>THE HAPPY DROWNING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou dost sail thy little ship<br>
 Upon the Sea of Deity,<br>
 It were indeed a happy chance<br>
 Shouldst thou be drowned in that great Sea.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 148------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 148</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>155 <font size="1">(II. 92)</font><br>
 <big>THE MYSTICAL ABANDONMENT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Abandonment ensnareth God:<br>
 But the Abandonment supreme,<br>
 Which few there be can comprehend,<br>
 Is to abandon even Him.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>156 <font size="1">(VI. 172)</font><br>
 <big>IN THE SEA NO DROP IS DISTINGUISHABLE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>If thou canst designate a drop<br>
 Lost in the Sea's immensity,<br>
 Then wilt thou in the Sea of God<br>
 Divine my soul's identity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>157 <font size="1">(II. 25)</font><br>
 <big>THOU MAKEST THY OWN DISQUIET</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Thee into thy disquiet nor God nor Creature brings,<br>
 Thou dost disquiet thyself caring for many things.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>158 <font size="1">(III. 177)</font><br>
 <big>THE LONG MARTYRDOM</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Martyrs fare exceeding well:<br>
 Swift passage through the mortal fires,<br>
 Then God's embrace. But we lifelong<br>
 Are martyred. How?—By our Desires.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 149------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 149</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>159 <font size="1">(I. 158)</font><br>
 <big>WHO DRINKETH THE SPRING OF LIFE?</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Fain wouldst thou drink the Spring of Life?<br>
  Then must thou first<br>
 Sweat out, whilst here upon this earth,<br>
  Thy proper thirst.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>160 <font size="1">(II. 197)</font><br>
 <big>SELF-ABNEGATION</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Lord, take the Crown away! I know not aught of Mine:<br>
 How can it rightly then be mine and yet not Thine!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>161 <font size="1">(V. 229)</font><br>
 <big>MEASUREMENT IS THE FALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Take not the measure of thy goodness—it is gone<br>
 As soon as it is self-confessed and thought upon.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>162 <font size="1">(II. 133)</font><br>
 <big>RESIGNATION</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Should God forbid me Heaven, it pleaseth me as well<br>
 Here to abide on earth or take my place in Hell.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 150------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 150</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>163 <font size="1">(V. 367)</font><br>
 <big>READY FOR ALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>He is the proper Hero who remains unshaken<br>
 Whether by God befriended or by God forsaken.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>164 <font size="1">(V. 105)</font><br>
 <big>HEAVEN CAN BE STOLEN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Who, hidden, doeth good; secret, bestoweth wealth:<br>
 He is a Master-thief and stealeth Heaven by stealth.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>165 <font size="1">(I. 39)</font><br>
 <big>IMPERFECT ABANDONMENT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>His Self is not all given to God who cannot dwell<br>
 Even in Hell itself and find in Hell no Hell.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>166 <font size="1">(VI. 170)</font><br>
 <big>TWO WAYS OF LOSING ONE'S SELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>So I may yet be lost? O ay!<br>
 If lost in death, loss limitless.<br>
 But if thou lose thyself in God<br>
 Thy loss is then all happiness.<br></td></tr></table>
