 <h2 align="center"><i>VII<br>
 SONSHIP</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 152------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 152</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>167 <font size="1">(V. 250)</font><br>
 <big>THE SPIRITUAL AND ETERNAL BIRTH ARE ONE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Spiritual Birth within my soul is one<br>
 With that whereby the Father doth beget the Son.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>168 <font size="1">(VI. 132)</font><br>
 <big>GOD'S SOLE FELICITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>There's joy in giving birth. God's sole Felicity<br>
 Is that He bringeth forth His Son eternally.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>169 <font size="1">(III. 175)</font><br>
 <big>WHAT GOD WROUGHT FROM ETERNITY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>What was God's deed, ere Time began,<br>
 Established on His ageless throne?<br>
 He loved Himself, and thus He wrought<br>
 The generation of His Son.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>170 <font size="1">(I. 201)</font><br>
 <big>WHY IS GOD BORN?</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>O Mystery! God's lost Himself, and therefore He,<br>
 To find Himself again, would be new-born in Me.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 153------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 153</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>171 <font size="1">(I. 135)</font><br>
 <big>WITH GOD IS ONLY HIS SON</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Man, be thou born of God! for standeth by His Throne<br>
 His own begotten Son—and other standeth none.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>172 <font size="1">(II. 102)</font><br>
 <big>THE EXTERNAL COMFORTETH ME NOT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>"Hail Mary!" so thou greetedst Her:<br>
 Yet, Gabriel, what doth this avail<br>
 To me, unless thou likewise come<br>
 And greet me with the self-same "Hail!"<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>173 <font size="1">(VI. 134)</font><br>
 <big>TO BE BORN OF GOD IS TO BE WHOLLY GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God doth engender naught but God.<br>
 If He engender thee, His Son,<br>
 Then thou becomest God in God,<br>
 And Lord upon the Lord God's throne.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>174 <font size="1">(I. 17)</font><br>
 <big>A CHRISTIAN IS GOD'S SON</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I also am God's Son. I sit beside His knee.<br>
 His Spirit, Flesh and Blood are known to Him in me.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 154------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_154"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 154</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>175 <font size="1">(III. 4)</font><br>
 <big>A SIGH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>When God became a man, swaddled in straw He lay—<br>
 Alas, that I have never been that straw and bay!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>176 <font size="1">(II. 53)</font><br>
 <big>THOU ONLY ART WANTING</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Could but my heart become a manger, God would then<br>
 Become a Child upon the earth yet once again.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>177 <font size="1">(V. 9)</font><br>
 <big>EVERYONE MUST BE CHRIST</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The true-born Son of God is Christ and Christ alone,<br>
 Yet must each Christian be this Christ, this selfsame Son.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>178 <font size="1">(I. 208)</font><br>
 <big>BLESSED GLUTTONY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Too much is never good. I hate all Gluttonies—<br>
 Yet wish I were as full of God as Jesus is!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 155------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>179 <font size="1">(VI. 236)</font><br>
 <big>NAUGHT IS HIGHER THAN TO BE GOD'S SON</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God's Son is God with God, rules from the selfsame Throne:<br>
 Naught standeth higher than I, if I am this same Son.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>180 <font size="1">(IV. 49)</font><br>
 <big>THE SPLENDOUR OF CHRIST IN THIS WORLD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Sceptre is a reed; a branch of thorns the Crown;<br>
 Nails are Regalia; a deadly Cross the Throne;<br>
 The Purple Robe is blood; Bodyguard, murderers;<br>
 Hangmen and the dastard crowd, the train of Courtiers;<br>
 The Wine is bitter gall; the Music, mocking mirth:<br>
 Such the Magnificence of our Lord God on earth.<br></td></tr></table>
