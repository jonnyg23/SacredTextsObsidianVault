 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Children Cry Over Dolls</i> <font size="1">(6:224)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Thou laughest that a child cries o'er its broken doll;—<br>
 The things o'er which thou mournest—are they not playthings all?<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Mine and Thine</i> <font size="1">(5:238)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Naught more than "mine and thine"—(and mark this lesson well)—<br>
 Can plunge thee bodily into the jaws of hell.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Seek No Outward Honor</i> <font size="1">(6:25)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 By outward marks of honor no wise man is elated.<br>
 Honor enough for him in being God-related.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Victory Brings Peace</i> <font size="1">(6:75)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 To strive is not enough; thou must succeed, my friend,<br>
 In order to win peace and quiet at life's end.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Wise and Foolish Beauty</i> <font size="1">(6:208)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Within the clever maiden her rich adornments shine.<br>
 It is the foolish one who thinks her clothes so fine.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Love Godlier Than Wisdom</i> <font size="1">(5:307)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Love, unannounced, to God may quickly penetrate.<br>
 Intelligence and wit long in the court must wait.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Space For Souls</i> <font size="1">(1:187)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 For me the world's too narrow and heaven itself too strait.<br>
 Where can I for my soul find space that's adequate?<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Simplicity</i> <font size="1">(1:219)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Simplicity's so great that should God lack her might<br>
 He would be neither God, nor Wisdom, nor the Light.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Death is Best</i> <font size="1">(1:35)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 I say: since it alone can set my spirit free<br>
 That death must be the best of all good things for me.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>A Standstill is Regression</i> <font size="1">(1:302)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 He who in paths of God doth every progress shun<br>
 Doth but retrace himself, and court oblivion.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Silence is the Highest State</i> <font size="1">(2:19)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 To work is very good; better, at prayer to be,<br>
 Best, to approach the Lord wordlessly, silently.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Middle View</i> <font size="1">(2:24)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 He who the centerpoint hath chosen, of earth's drama,<br>
 Sees at a glance from there life's total panorama.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Your Own Unrest</i> <font size="1">(2:25)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Not God nor yet creation your heart to turmoil brings;<br>
 It is yourself (Oh folly!) excites yourself with things.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Center Wall</i> <font size="1">(2:43)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Away with middle ways! That no light be deflected,<br>
 Suffer no walls to be before thine eyes erected.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Spiritual Mountain</i> <font size="1">(2:83)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 I am a hill of God, and must myself ascend<br>
 That God may then reveal his face to me, my friend.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Dearest Upon Earth</i> <font size="1">(2:127)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Thou askest how my soul would liefest go its way?<br>
 'Twould walk the paths of earth unspattered by its clay.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Heavenly Peace</i> <font size="1">(2:239)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 With God and with oneself to be at utter ease<br>
 Must be, in sooth, to know a peace beyond all peace.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Solstice</i> <font size="1">(2:231)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Wonder not, friend, if I the sight of all things shun<br>
 That I may better turn my eyes upon the sun.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>The Angelic Life</i> <font size="1">(2:254)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 In love to walk and halt, to breathe, speak, sing of love,<br>
 Means: all one's life to live like seraphim above.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Only Day</i> <font size="1">(3:48)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Three days: Today, Tomorrow and Yesterday, I know,<br>
 Yet if the past were cancelled within the here and now<br>
 And then the future hidden, I could regain that Day<br>
 Which I, before I was, had lived in God's own way.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Meekness Possesses the Earth</i> <font size="1">(3:100)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Pitiful, how thou yearnest for just a fleck of earth<br>
 When meekness would appoint thee master of all its girth.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>A Pure Man is Above the Angels</i> <font size="1">(3:107)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 'Twere much to be an angel, yet more to stay a man<br>
 Neither befouled nor sullied by mire along life's span.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Honor Thy Body</i> <font size="1">(3:109)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Honor thy body well, for 'tis a shrine serene<br>
 In which to keep God's image forever safe and clean.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Straight Path to Life</i> <font size="1">(3:129)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 The everlasting life if thou would'st see unfurled,<br>
 Leave standing on thy left thyself and all the world.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Servants, Friends and Children</i> <font size="1">(3:153)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 'Tis knaves who fear their God, and friends who love him tensely,<br>
 But children give to him their hearts and souls immensely.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Love Eternal</i> <font size="1">(3:160)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Hope ends, and even faith is often circumspect,<br>
 Tongues are no longer spoken, and all that we erect<br>
 Is levelled soon by time. 'Tis love alone persists,<br>
 And therefore he is wise who on her hope insists.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Fools Seek Variety</i> <font size="1">(3:171)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Wise men to gain one thing—the Highest Good—connive;<br>
 'Tis fools who for the little and many blessings strive.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Perpetual Motion</i> <font size="1">(3:170)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 You seek unceasingly Life's neverending motion;<br>
 I seek perpetual peace. Which is the wiser notion?<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Of Love</i> <font size="1">(3:179)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 A man long e'er his end his earthly love must lose;<br>
 To love unending beauty my heart doth therefore choose.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Divinity</i> <font size="1">(3:168)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Divinity's a spring; all things therefrom have motion<br>
 And yet again flow back as if it were an ocean.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Wisdom the Best Spouse</i> <font size="1">(3:194)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Desirest thou a wife resplendent, rich, and free?<br>
 Then take unto thee Wisdom, who'll be all these to thee.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>God's Great Gifts</i> <font size="1">(3:201)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 God, being great, preferreth great blessings to impart.<br>
 Ah that we needy show him but such a paltry heart!<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Of Knowledge</i> <font size="1">(3:207)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Much knowledge may be fine, but greater joy is won<br>
 By being free of guile from earliest childhood on.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Do Not Presume</i> <font size="1">(3:232)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Even though arrived at fame, tarry not till thine end.<br>
 Thou should'st proceed from one light into the next, my friend.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Great in Small</i> <font size="1">(4:14)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Take what the Lord bestows. He gives thee great-in-small,<br>
 Even in cinders gold, when prayed for least of all.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Goal of Virtue</i> <font size="1">(4:18)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 God is the goal of virtue, its purpose and renown,<br>
 Its only cause for being, its sole reward and crown.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Virtue of God</i> <font size="1">(4:23)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 The far most gleaming light of this world can be seen<br>
 Best from the darkest place where thou hast ever been.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Blessed</i> <font size="1">(4:27)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 What is the Holy Saints' most certain occupation?<br>
 They contemplate high beauty in endless adoration.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Man's Three Foes</i> <font size="1">(3:233)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Man hath three enemies:—himself, the world, the devil . .<br>
 Of these the first is far the most unyielding evil.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Love</i> <font size="1">(4:29)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Love is in this like death: it kills in me my senses,<br>
 It breaks my heart, and with my very soul dispenses.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Mystic Ascension</i> <font size="1">(4:56)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Couldst thou forget thyself, give God the leading role,<br>
 Thou wouldst experience Ascension in thy soul.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Sin</i> <font size="1">(4:69)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Sin is not otherwise than that man turns his face<br>
 From God, and finds instead Death standing in that place.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Grapes From Thorns</i> <font size="1">(4:76)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Who loves his evil-wisher, speaks kindly of his foe,<br>
 Is he not like a man who grapes from thorns can grow?<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Wise Man</i> <font size="1">(4:109)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 The wise man seeketh peace and fleeth from confusion.<br>
 His fatherland is heaven, his hell the world's illusion.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Heaven-seeker</i> <font size="1">(4:120)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 The heaven-seeker dies to earth and to creation.<br>
 Why? Since he lives alone for God and adoration.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Brooks Become Seas</i> <font size="1">(1:135)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Here I still flow in God as a small stream of time,<br>
 There I shall be a sea of blessedness sublime.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>The Sweetest Tone</i> <font size="1">(4:143)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Never in endless time will ring a sound as sweetly<br>
 As when a human heart in God is tuned completely.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Hidden Source</i> <font size="1">(4:163)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Who would believe this true? From darkness light is wrought,<br>
 Life out of death, and Something out of Nought.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>The Cross Reveals</i> <font size="1">(4:190)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Comfort and sweetness witness never what thou hast been;<br>
 Only thy Cross reveals that which thou art within.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Renunciation</i> <font size="1">(4:191)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Friend, wouldst thou leave at once the world thou knowest of?<br>
 Then see to it that thou despise thine own self-love.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Rays Without Sun</i> <font size="1">(4:202)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 If broken from its sun, a ray's no longer bright;<br>
 Thou neither, leaving God, thy necessary light.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Time is not Swift</i> <font size="1">(5:23)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 'Tis said that time is swift, but who hath seen her fly?<br>
 Rather, she lies unmoved within world-destiny.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Eyes Cannot Behold God</i> <font size="1">(5:24)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Wouldst thou behold thy God, imagine nothing new;<br>
 'Twill be an inner sight, and not external view.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>View Depths From Heights</i> <font size="1">(5:29)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 God, being a great abyss, to men his depth reveals<br>
 Who climb the highest peak of the eternal hills.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>God Plumbs the Depths</i> <font size="1">(5:37)</font></p>

 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>Insatiate</i> <font size="1">(5:39)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 The blessed may rejoice at never being filled,<br>
 Dear hunger and sweet thirstare what their hearts have willed.<br>

 <p><i>Instability</i> <font size="1">(4:168)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Nought more unstable is in affluence or pain<br>
 Than thine own heart, oh man. Ponder this yet again.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>As the School, So the Lesson</i> <font size="1">(5:267)</font></p>

 In schools throughout the world God's but described to you.<br>
 Within the spirit's school one sees and loves him too.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>No Laws For the Just</i> <font size="1">(5:277)</font></p>

 Laws are for evil-doers. Were no commandments, then<br>
 The pious still would love God and their fellow-men.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Love is Fire and Flood</i> <font size="1">(5:290)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Love is a flood, a blaze. Sheltered thine heart within,<br>
 'Twill put out God's displeasure and burn away thy sin.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 Without reincarnation what more, pray, wouldst thou be?<br>

 Let them your hands and feet, your soul and body bind.<br>

 Then must thou previously wean thee of every speech.<br>

 Mortal, if Paradise were not first set in thee,<br>
 Believe me, thou wouldst never reach its security.<br>

 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Thou needst not cry to God; within thyself's a spring.<br>
 Unless its source be clogged, 'twill keep on issuing.<br>

 Men say: wealth begets worth, but that is idle twaddle;<br>
 Men are not known by clothes, nor horses by their saddle.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <p><i>Humility</i> <font size="1">(1:94)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 Humbleness is the floor, the cover and the bin<br>
 That holds the virtues all, and they are locked therein.<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 God dwelleth in a light toward which there is no path;<br>
 He must become that light who need of vision hath.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p><i>To be Part of the Whole, Progress</i> <font size="1">(6:42)</font></p>
 <table><tr><td> </td><td>
 The sun sets all in motion, makes even the planets dance.<br>
 You are not part of all unless you too advance.<br>
 </td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>

 He has been sated here and hungers not, my friend.<br>

 How can he serve thee drink lest thou be sober first?<br>

 How can he then expect to reach the target's heart?<br>
 </td></tr></table>

 That which is blindly loaded will send its shot nowhere.<br>

 The wise man sends it on from this world to the next.<br>

 A sage would still feel poor though all the earth he'd hold.<br>
