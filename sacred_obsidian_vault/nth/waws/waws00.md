 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">DUST JACKET TEXT: BACK</font></p>  <h4 align="center">"The Life Books"</h4>  
 <p>"I know of nothing in the entire range of literature more calculated to inspire the young than the Life Books,' and to renew the soul in young and old."From a Reader.</p>  <h4 align="center">What All the World's A-Seeking</h4>  
 <p>"Its purpose is distinctly practical. It is most fascinatingly written, and deserves the remarkable success it has achieved."<i>The Review of Reviews</i>.</p>  
 <p>"The volume abounds in passages of great beauty and strength; but the striking feature of the book is, after all, the solid, sensible, healthy exposition of the one theme it is written to enforce."<i>New York Independent</i>.</p>  <h4 align="center">In Tune with the Infinite</h4>  
 <p>"It is one of the simplest, clearest works ever written, dealing with the power of the interior forces in moulding the every-day conditions of life."<i>San Francisco Bulletin</i>.</p>  
 <p>"Mr. Trine can write well upon such topics as this. He is alive, vigorous, cheery, confident. The work has distinctiveness in its style and method."<i>The Literary World</i>, London.</p>  <h4 align="center">"The Life Booklets"</h4>  <h4 align="center">The Greatest Thing Ever Known</h4>  
 <p>"It unfolds the secret of our underlying strength, and shoals what it is that gives us power to fulfil 'the real and living purposes of our being."<i>The New Christianity</i>.</p>  <h4 align="center">Every Living Creature</h4>  
 <p>"An eloquent appeal and an able argument for justice and mercy to our dumb fellow-creatures."<i>Religio-Philosophical Journal</i>.</p>  <h4 align="center">Character-Building Thought Power</h4>  
 <p>"The author here shows us how by daily effort we may train ourselves into right ways of thinking and acting."Albany Press.</p>  
 <p>Thomas Y. Crowell &amp; Co., Publishers, New York</p>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">DUST JACKET FLAP TEXT: FRONT</font></p>  
 <p align="center">JUST THE BOOK<br> TO CARRY IN<br> YOUR POCKET</p>  
 <p align="center">On<br> the<br> Open<br> Road</p>  
 <p>"A creed of wholesome living."</p>  
 <p>Mr. Trine here chooses a series of texts born of daily life and experience, and devotes a page or two to each thought. It is really a little group of lay sermons dealing with the power of inner forcespithy, practical, optimistic, and inspired by the author's keen sympathy and wide observation.</p>
