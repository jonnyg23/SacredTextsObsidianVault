 <p><a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="green">p. 100</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IV.</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE FRONTAL SIGN OF THE INITIATES ACCORDING TO THE AGROUCHADA-PARIKCHAI.</h3>  
 <p>Every morning those who have been initiated into the third degree, after terminating <a name="img_10000"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/10000.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>their ablutions, and before going to the pagoda to listen to the discourse on the occult sciences, should trace upon their foreheads, under the direction of the Gurus, the accompanying sign, which is a symbol of the highest initiation. The circle indicates infinity, the study of which is the object of the occult sciences.  
 <p>The border of triangles signifies that everything in nature is subject to the laws of the Trinity.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">BrahmaVischnouSiva.  
 <p>The germThe wombThe offspring.</p>  
 <p>The seedThe earthThe plant.</p>  
 <p>The fatherThe motherThe child.</p></div>  
 <p>The serpent is a symbol of wisdom and perseverance. It also indicates that the multitude are not to be admitted to a revelation of the higher truths, which often lead weak minds to insanity and death. The seven-knotted stick represents the seven degrees of the power of evocation and external manifestation, which form the subject</p>  
 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="green">p. 101</font></a></p>  
 <p>of study to those who have been initiated into the various degrees with which we are acquainted:</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Grihastaor House-Master.  
 <p>Pourohitaor Priest of Popular Evocations.</p>  
 <p>FakirPerforming.</p>  
 <p>SanyassisSuperior Exorcists.</p>  
 <p>NirvanysNaked Evocators,</p>  
 <p>YoguysContemplative.</p>  
 <p>BrahmatmaSupreme Chief.</p></div>
