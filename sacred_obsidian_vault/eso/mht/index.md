 <p>
 This is the only book attributed to the mysterious, supposedly
 immortal Comte St. Germain.
 (see <a href="../../sro/csg/index.htm">The Comte de St. Germain</a>,
 by Isabel Cooper-Oakley).
 This parallel French and English edition was self-published in 1933
 and introduced by Manly Hall,
 who also wrote <a href="../sta/index.htm">The Secret Teachings of All Ages</a>.
 The Trinosophia is an allegorical account of spiritual initiation,
 in the vein of <a href="../../sro/rhr/rhr08.htm">The Chymical Marriage</a>.
 Whether Hall's claim that St. Germain was (or is) a key Rosicrucian figure
 is true or not, this is indeed one of the rarest of occult books.
 It appears here for the first time on the Internet.
 </p>
