 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Comte de Gabalis</i> [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ROMULUS.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_RR">RR</a></font></span>BIRTH.--But, in my opinion, the origin of so great a city, and the establishment of an empire next in power to that of the gods, was due to the Fates. The vestal Rhea, being deflowered by force, when she had</p>  
 <p><a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 294</font></a></p>  
 <p>brought forth twins, declares Mars to be the father. . . . <small>TITUS LIVY, HISTORY OF ROME. BOOK I, CHAPTER IV.</small></p>  
 <p>TRANSLATION.--"On the nones of Qintilis or on the Quirinalia, as the king was reviewing his people, the Sun withdrew its light; and while the earth lay in darkness, Mars descended in a hurricane and tempest, and bore away his perfected son in a fiery chariot to heaven." <small>B. G. NIEBUHR, THE HISTORY OF ROME, VOL. i, PAGE 197.</small></p>
