   <h3 align="center">30.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus carried a double pingo (a yoke over the shoulders with the weight to be carried on both sides).</p>  
 <p>People murmured, &amp;c., saying, 'Like the king's porters <a name="fr_404"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to carry a double pingo. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to carry a single pingo, a pingo for two bearers <a name="fr_405"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and to carry weights on your head, or your shoulders, or against your hips <a name="fr_406"></a><a href="#fn_398"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and suspended over your backs.'</p>
