   <h3 align="center">19.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time there was a very valuable rug, and a very valuable piece of cloth, among the bedding furniture belonging to the Sa<i>m</i>gha.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One <a name="fr_619"></a><a href="#fn_609"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to barter either of these things in order to increase the stock of legally permissible furniture <a name="fr_620"></a><a href="#fn_610"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Sa<i>m</i>gha had received a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 218</font></a></p>  
 <p>bear-skin, and a <i>k</i>akkali <a name="fr_621"></a><a href="#fn_611"><font size="1">1</font></a> rug, and a <i>k</i>olaka cloth <a name="fr_622"></a><a href="#fn_612"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to use them as mats to wipe your feet on <a name="fr_623"></a><a href="#fn_613"><font size="1">3</font></a>'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p><a name="fn_610"></a><a href="sbe20071.htm#fr_620">217:4</a> Phâtikammatthâyâ ti va<i>d</i><i>dh</i>ikammatthâyâ ti.  <p><a name="fn_611"></a><a href="sbe20071.htm#fr_621">218:1</a> According to VI, 2, 2, and VI, 3, 5, this could also be used as a screen or curtain over the space in a wall left for a window.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_612"></a><a href="sbe20071.htm#fr_622">218:2</a> According to Mahâvagga VIII, 18, this might be used to wipe faces with; and according to <i>K</i>ullavagga V, 9, 4, VI, 19, to place crockery or furniture on. The word <i>k</i>ola means simply cotton cloth, but it is clear from these passages that <i>k</i>olaka has some special connotation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_613"></a><a href="sbe20071.htm#fr_623">218:3</a> Not to sit upon.</p>
