   <h3 align="center">18.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus separated themselves from the mats on which they sat down for four months <a name="fr_307"></a><a href="#fn_301"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to separate yourselves for four months from the mats on which you sit</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>  
 <p>down. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus lay down to sleep on beds scattered over with flowers.</p>  
 <p>People who came on a visit to the Vihâras saw it, and murmured, &amp;c., saying, 'Like those who still live in the pleasures of the world.'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to sleep on beds scattered over with flowers. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time people came to the Ârâma, bringing perfumes and garlands. The Bhikkhus, fearing to offend, would not accept them.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus; to accept the perfume, and to apply it to the door for a space of five finger-breadths <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_302"><font size="1">1</font></a>; and to accept the flowers, and put them on one side in the Vihâra.'</p>
