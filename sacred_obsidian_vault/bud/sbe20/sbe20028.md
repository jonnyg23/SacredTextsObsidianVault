   <h3 align="center">13.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the water as they went along could not be drunk without breaking the rules <a name="fr_240"></a><a href="#fn_234"><font size="1">3</font></a>, as they had no strainers.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of a strainer.' The little cloth (that was used for a strainer) was not sufficient (to filter enough water for the whole party).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 100</font></a></p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of a strainer fixed on to a ladle <a name="fr_241"></a><a href="#fn_235"><font size="1">1</font></a>'.'</p>  
 <p>Still the little cloth was not sufficient for the purpose.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of a regulation water-pot <a name="fr_242"></a><a href="#fn_236"><font size="1">2</font></a>'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time two Bhikkhus were travelling along the high-road in the Kosala country. One of the Bhikkhus was guilty of some transgression. The other one said to him, 'Do not, my friend, do such a thing. It is not becoming.' The first one bore a grudge against him <a name="fr_243"></a><a href="#fn_237"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Afterwards the other Bhikkhu, being tormented with thirst, said to the Bhikkhu who bore the grudge, 'Give me, friend, your strainer. I am going to drink some water.' The Bhikkhu who bore the grudge would not give it to him. The other Bhikkhu died of thirst <a name="fr_244"></a><a href="#fn_238"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Then that Bhikkhu, when he had arrived at the Ârâma, told this matter to the Bhikkhus.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a></p>  
 <p>'What then, Sir? when asked for your strainer, would you not lend it?'</p>  
 <p>'It is even so, Sirs.'</p>  
 <p>Those Bhikkhus who were moderate were annoyed and vexed, and murmured, saying, 'How can a Bhikkhu, when asked for his strainer, refuse to lend it?' And they told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>Then the Blessed One on that occasion and in that connection (&amp;c., as usual, see for instance in <i>K</i>ullavagga I, 1, 2, down to) addressed the Bhikkhus, and said:</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhu who is on a journey is not, O Bhikkhus, to refuse to lend his strainer, when he is asked for it. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. And (a Bhikkhu who is) not provided with a strainer, O Bhikkhus, is not to undertake a journey. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. If there be no strainer nor regulation water-pot, the corner of the upper robe is to be adopted <a name="fr_245"></a><a href="#fn_239"><font size="1">1</font></a> for the purpose of straining before drinking.'</p>  
 <p align="justify'">3. Now the Blessed One, journeying straight on, arrived in due course at Vesâlî. And there at Vesâlî the Blessed One lodged in the Mahâvana, in the Kû<i>t</i>âgâra Hall.</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Bhikkhus were engaged in building <a name="fr_246"></a><a href="#fn_240"><font size="1">2</font></a>; and the strainer did not act <a name="fr_247"></a><a href="#fn_241"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a></p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a double strainer <a name="fr_248"></a><a href="#fn_242"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>The double strainer did not act.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a filter <a name="fr_249"></a><a href="#fn_243"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Bhikkhus were troubled <a name="fr_250"></a><a href="#fn_244"><font size="1">3</font></a> by mosquitoes.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of mosquito curtains <a name="fr_251"></a><a href="#fn_245"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="fn_234"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_240">99:3</a> The rule, that is, against destroying the life of living things.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_235"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_241">100:1</a> Ka<i>t</i>a<i>k</i><i>kh</i>u-parissâvana<i>m</i> nâma tîsu da<i>n</i><i>d</i>akesu vinandhitvâ kata<i>m</i> (B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_236"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_242">100:2</a> Dhamma-karaka<i>m</i>. Doubtless a water-pot with a strainer so fixed into it that a quantity of water could be filtered quickly. The word occurs at Mahâva<i>m</i>sa, p. 90, and below, VI, 21, 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_237"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_243">100:3</a> So tasmi<i>m</i> upanandhi. The Introductory Story in the Sutta-vibhaṅga on the 36th Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya is, so far, word for word the same as this section. Buddhaghosa there explains upanandhi by <i>g</i>anita-upanâho. See vol. iv, p. 359, of H.O.'s edition of the Vinaya Pi<i>t</i>aka. The Introductory Story to the 31st <i>G</i>âtaka is also based on a similar incident, and there the corresponding expression is vivâda<i>m</i> aka<i>m</i>su. (Fausböll's <i>G</i>âtaka, vol. i, p. 198.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_238"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_244">100:4</a> In the <i>G</i>âtaka commentary this tragic result of the refusal is absent. The Bhikkhu who has no strainer merely drinks without straining. (Rh. D.'s 'Buddhist Birth Stories,' vol. i, p. 278.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_239"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_245">101:1</a> Adhi<i>t</i><i>th</i>âtabbo, that is, the Bhikkhu is to determine in his mind that that part of his robe is a strainer for the time.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_240"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_246">101:2</a> Navakamma<i>m</i> karonti. On the use of this and allied idioms, see <i>G</i>âtaka I, 92, line 22; <i>K</i>ullavagga I, 18, 1, VI, 5, 2; Bhikkhunî-vibhaṅga, Pârâ<i>g</i>ika I, 1; Indian Antiquary XI, 29; Senart's Ka<i>k</i><i>k</i>âyana, p. 189.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_241"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_247">101:3</a> Na sammati, which is curious. For 'did not suffice,' the standing expression would be na ppahoti.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_242"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_248">102:1</a> Da<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-parissâvana<i>m</i>. Apparently a long box, both ends of which strain the water, which is poured into the middle by means of a pipe (da<i>n</i><i>d</i>aka). Buddhaghosa says, Da<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-parisâvanan ti (sic; only one s) ra<i>g</i>anakâna<i>m</i> khâra-parisâvana<i>m</i> viya <i>k</i>atusu pâdesu baddha-nise<i>n</i>ikâya sâ<i>t</i>aka<i>m</i> bandhitvâ ma<i>g</i><i>gh</i>e da<i>n</i><i>d</i>ake udaka<i>m</i> âsi<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>itabba<i>m</i>. Tam ubhohi ko<i>t</i><i>th</i>âsehi pûretvâ parisâvati. Compare da<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-satthaka<i>m</i> and da<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-kathina<i>m</i>, above, V, 11, 1, 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_243"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_249">102:2</a> Ottharaka<i>m</i> nâma ya<i>m</i> udake ottharitvâ gha<i>t</i>akena udaka<i>m</i> ga<i>n</i>hanti. Ta<i>m</i> hi <i>k</i>atusu da<i>n</i><i>d</i>akesu vettha<i>m</i> bandhitvâ sabbe pariyante udakato mo<i>k</i>etvâ ma<i>g</i><i>gh</i>e ottharitvâ gha<i>t</i>ena udaka<i>m</i> ga<i>n</i>hanti (B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_244"></a><a href="sbe20028.htm#fr_250">102:3</a> Ubbâ<i>lh</i>â. See Mahâvagga III, 9, 1-4, and <i>G</i>âtaka I, 300.</p>
