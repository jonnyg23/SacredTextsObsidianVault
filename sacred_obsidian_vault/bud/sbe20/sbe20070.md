   <h3 align="center">18.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus made use elsewhere of beds which were appurtenances <a name="fr_616"></a><a href="#fn_606"><font size="1">2</font></a> to the Vihâra of a certain lay-disciple (upâsaka).</p>  
 <p>Then that upâsaka murmured, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>They told the matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Things appurtenant to one place are not, O Bhikkhus, to be used in another. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Bhikkhus, fearing to offend if they took (things to sit upon) even into the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 217</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Uposatha Hall, or the meeting-place, sat on the ground; and their legs and robes got soiled.</p>  
 <p>They told the matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to take things away for a certain time only <a name="fr_617"></a><a href="#fn_607"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a large Vihâra belonging to the Sa<i>m</i>gha went to ruin <a name="fr_618"></a><a href="#fn_608"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The Bhikkhus, fearing to offend, did not take the bedding in it away.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to take away things in order to save them from destruction.'</p>
