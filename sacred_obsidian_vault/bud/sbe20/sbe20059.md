   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time people provided arbours (ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>apas), and couches, and room for the use of the Sa<i>m</i>gha. And the pupils of the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus, saying, 'It has been laid down by the Blessed One that that which pertains (wholly) to the Sa<i>m</i>gha shall be used according to seniority, but not that which is given only for the temporary use of the Sa<i>m</i>gha,' went on in front of the Sa<i>m</i>gha and occupied the ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>apas, and occupied the couches, and occupied the room, saying, 'This shall be for our superiors, and this for our teachers, and this for ourselves.'</p>  
 <p>And Sâriputta (&amp;c., as in last chapter, §§ 1, 2, down to) Then the Blessed One rebuked them, &amp;c., and said to the Bhikkhus, 'Even that which has been set aside only for the temporary use of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>the Sa<i>m</i>gha is not, O Bhikkhus, to be reserved for exclusive use according to seniority.'</p>
