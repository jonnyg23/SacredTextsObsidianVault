   <h3 align="center">10.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus went on their round for alms, carrying water jugs made out of gourds <a name="fr_193"></a><a href="#fn_188"><font size="1">2</font></a>, or water-pots <a name="fr_194"></a><a href="#fn_189"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 89</font></a></p>  
 <p>People murmured, were shocked, and indignant, saying, 'As the Titthiyas do.'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to go on your rounds for alms with water jugs, or pots. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu, who had taken upon himself a vow to wear or use nothing except what he could procure from dust-heaps or cemeteries, went on his rounds for alms carrying a bowl made out of a skull. A certain woman saw him, and was afraid, and made an outcry <a name="fr_195"></a><a href="#fn_190"><font size="1">1</font></a>, saying, 'O horror! This is surely a devil!'</p>  
 <p>People murmured, were shocked, and indignant, saying, 'How can the Sakyaputtiya Sama<i>n</i>as carry about bowls made out of skulls, as the devil-worshippers <a name="fr_196"></a><a href="#fn_191"><font size="1">2</font></a> do?'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to use bowls made out of skulls. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. And you are not, O Bhikkhus, to take a vow to wear or to use nothing except what you procure from dust-heaps or cemeteries. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>3. Now at that time the Bhikkhus carried out odd bits, and bones, and dirty water <a name="fr_197"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">3</font></a> in their bowls.</p>  
 <p>People murmured, were shocked, and were indignant, saying, 'The very vessel out of which</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 90</font></a></p>  
 <p>these Sakyaputtiya Sama<i>n</i>as eat, that they use as a waste-tub!'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to carry out odd bits, and bones, and dirty water in your bowls. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of a waste-tub <a name="fr_198"></a><a href="#fn_193"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>
