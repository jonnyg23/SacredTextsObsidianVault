   <h3 align="center">10.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time Bhikkhunîs wore long girdles, and out of them they arranged fringes <a name="fr_890"></a><a href="#fn_876"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The people murmured, were indignant, and complained, saying: 'As the women who are still enjoying the pleasures of the world do!'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_341"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 341</font></a></p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to wear a long girdle. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow a Bhikkhunî, O Bhikkhus, a girdle that will go once round the body <a name="fr_891"></a><a href="#fn_877"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and fringes are not to be arranged in it. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time Bhikkhunîs arranged fringes in cloth of bambû fibre <a name="fr_892"></a><a href="#fn_878"><font size="1">2</font></a>, or in leather, or in white cotton cloth <a name="fr_893"></a><a href="#fn_879"><font size="1">3</font></a>, or in plaited cotton cloth <a name="fr_894"></a><a href="#fn_880"><font size="1">4</font></a>, or in fringed cotton cloth <a name="fr_895"></a><a href="#fn_881"><font size="1">5</font></a>, or in white or, in plaited or in fringed <i>k</i>ola cloth <a name="fr_896"></a><a href="#fn_882"><font size="1">6</font></a>, or in plaits or fringes made of thread. The people murmured, were indignant, and complained, saying: 'As the women who are still enjoying the pleasures of the world do!'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to wear any of these things. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time Bhikkhunîs had their backs scrubbed with the steak-bone of an ox, or slapped with the jaw-bone of an ox; and had their fore-arms <a name="fr_897"></a><a href="#fn_883"><font size="1">7</font></a>,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 342</font></a></p>  
 <p>and the backs of their hands <a name="fr_898"></a><a href="#fn_884"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and their calves <a name="fr_899"></a><a href="#fn_885"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and the upper part of their feet <a name="fr_900"></a><a href="#fn_886"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and their thighs, and their faces, and their gums, so slapped. The people murmured, were indignant, and complained, saying: 'As the women who are still enjoying the pleasures of the world do!'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to [do any of these things]. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>3. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhunîs [&amp;c., down to]</p>  
 <p>' <a name="fr_901"></a><a href="#fn_887"><font size="1">4</font></a>A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to anoint her face, nor to rub ointments on to her face <a name="fr_902"></a><a href="#fn_888"><font size="1">5</font></a>, nor to put chunam on to her face, nor to smear red arsenic on to her face, nor to paint her body, nor to paint her face, nor to paint her body and face. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>4. [Similar paragraph concluding,]</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to make (tattoo marks) by the corners of her eyes <a name="fr_903"></a><a href="#fn_889"><font size="1">6</font></a>, or on her cheeks <a name="fr_904"></a><a href="#fn_890"><font size="1">7</font></a>, nor to peep out of window <a name="fr_905"></a><a href="#fn_891"><font size="1">8</font></a>, nor to stand in the</p>

 <p>light (of a half-opened door) <a name="fr_906"></a><a href="#fn_892"><font size="1">1</font></a>, nor to have dances performed, nor to keep courtesans, nor to keep a tavern, nor to keep a slaughter-house, nor to open a shop, nor, to practise usury, nor to supply men slaves or women slaves, or men servants or maid servants, or animals, nor to carry on the business of florist and seedsman <a name="fr_907"></a><a href="#fn_893"><font size="1">2</font></a>, nor to carry the razor case <a name="fr_908"></a><a href="#fn_894"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>[Similar paragraph concluding,]</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to wear robes that are all of a blue, light yellow, crimson, black, brownish-yellow, or dark yellow colour nor to wear robes with skirts to them which are not made of torn pieces of cloth, or are long, or have flowers worked on them, or cobras' hoods; nor to wear jackets, nor dresses made of the fibre of the Tirîtaka plant <a name="fr_909"></a><a href="#fn_895"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>
