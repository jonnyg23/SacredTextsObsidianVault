 <p><a name="page_1"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 1</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center"><i>K</i>ULLAVAGGA.</h1>  <h2 align="center">FOURTH KHANDHAKA.</h2>  <h4 align="center">THE SETTLEMENT OF DISPUTES AMONG THE FRATERNITY.</h4>  <h3 align="center">1.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Blessed Buddha was dwelling at Sâvatthi, in the Ârâma of Anâthapi<i>n</i><i>d</i>ika. And at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus used to carry out the formal Acts--the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya, and the Nissaya, and the Pabbâ<i>g</i>aniya, and the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya, and the Ukkhepaniya--against Bhikkhus who were not present.</p>  
 <p>Then those Bhikkhus who were modest were annoyed, murmured, and became indignant, saying, 'How can the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus [act thus] <a name="fr_0"></a><a href="#fn_0"><font size="1">1</font></a>?' And those Bhikkhus told the matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Is it true, O Bhikkhus, as they say, that the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus carry out the (aforesaid) <a name="fr_1"></a><a href="#fn_0"><font size="1">1</font></a> formal Acts against Bhikkhus who are not present?'</p>  
 <p>'It is true, Lord!'</p>  
 <p>The Blessed Buddha rebuked them, saying, 'This is improper (&amp;c., as in I, 1, 2, down to the end).' And when he had thus rebuked them, and had</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 2</font></a></p>  
 <p>delivered a religious discourse, he addressed the Bhikkhus, and said: 'The formal Acts, O Bhikkhus, the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-, the Nissaya-, the Pabbâ<i>g</i>aniya-, the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-, and the Ukkhepaniya-kammas, ought not to be carried out against Bhikkhus who are not present. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>
