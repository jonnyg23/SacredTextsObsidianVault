   <h3 align="center">2.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now Mahâ-pa<i>g</i>âpatî the Gotamî went up to the place where the Blessed One was, and bowed down before him, and stood respectfully on one side. And, so standing, Mahâ-pa<i>g</i>âpatî the Gotamî spake thus to the Blessed One: 'What course, Lord,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_327"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 327</font></a></p>  
 <p>should I pursue towards these women of the Sâkya clan?'</p>  
 <p>Then the Blessed One taught Mahâ-pa<i>g</i>âpatî the Gotamî and incited her, and aroused her, and gladdened her with religious discourse; and she, so taught, incited, aroused, and gladdened, bowed down before the Blessed One, and keeping him on net right hand as she passed him, she departed thence.</p>  
 <p>Then the Blessed One, in that connexion, delivered a religious discourse, and said to the Bhikkhus, 'I allow Bhikkhunîs, O Bhikkhus, to receive the upasampadâ initiation from Bhikkhus <a name="fr_877"></a><a href="#fn_863"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now those Bhikkhunîs said to Mahâ-pa<i>g</i>âpatî the Gotamî: 'Neither have you received the upasampadâ initiation, nor have we; for it has thus been laid down by the Blessed One: "Bhikkhunîs are to be initiated by Bhikkhus."'</p>  
 <p>Then Mahâ-pa<i>g</i>âpatî the Gotamî went to the venerable Ânanda, and [repeated their words to him]. And the venerable Ânanda went to the Blessed One, and [repeated them to him].</p>  
 <p>'In that moment, Ânanda, when Mahâ-pa<i>g</i>âpatî the Gotamî took upon herself the Eight Chief Rules, that was to her as the upasampadâ initiation.'</p>
