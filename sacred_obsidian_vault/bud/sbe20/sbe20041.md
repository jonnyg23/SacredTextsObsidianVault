   <h3 align="center">26.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain multitude had</p>  
 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a></p>  
 <p>arranged for the privilege of supplying food to the Sa<i>m</i>gha, and in the dining-hall many fragments of rice were allowed to fall.</p>  
 <p>The people murmured, were annoyed, and were indignant, saying, 'How can the Sakya-puttiya Sama<i>n</i>as, when food is being given to them, take it so carelessly. Each single ball of rice is the result of hundredfold labour!'</p>  
 <p>The Bhikkhus heard of the people thus murmuring, &amp;c., and they told the matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, whatever thing falls when it is being given to you, yourselves to pick it up and eat it. That has been presented, O Bhikkhus, by the givers.'</p>
