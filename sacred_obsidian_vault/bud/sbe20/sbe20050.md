   <h3 align="center">35.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that tune the Bhikkhus made water here and there in the Ârâma, and the Ârâma was defiled.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to make water at one side (of the Ârâma).'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a></p>  
 <p>The Ârâma became offensive. . . . . .</p>  
 <p>[The rest of this chapter is scarcely translateable. It records in like manner the various sanitary difficulties which arose from the living together of a number of Bhikkhus. Each such difficulty is quite solemnly said to have been reported to the Blessed One, and he is said to have found a way out of it. The result of the whole is, that the building of privies is enjoined, and all the contrivances, such as seats, doors, steps, plastering, &amp;c., already mentioned with respect to the bath-room, above, V, 14, are here repeated verbatim <a name="fr_426"></a><a href="#fn_418"><font size="1">1</font></a>.]</p>
