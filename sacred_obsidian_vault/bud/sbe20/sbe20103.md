   <h3 align="center">12.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain woman, who had formerly belonged to the clan of the Mallas <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_898"><font size="1">2</font></a> had entered the Order of the Bhikkhunîs. She, seeing a weakly Bhikkhu on the road, struck up against him with the edge of her shoulder, and knocked him over. The Bhikkhus murmured, were indignant, and complained, saying: 'How can a Bhikkhunî assault a Bhikkhu?'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to assault a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_345"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 345</font></a></p>  
 <p>Bhikkhu. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I prescribe that a Bhikkhunî, O Bhikkhus, on seeing a Bhikkhu, should get out of the way when still at a distance, and make room for him.'</p>
