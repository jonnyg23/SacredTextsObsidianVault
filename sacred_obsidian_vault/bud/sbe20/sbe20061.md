   <h3 align="center">9.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the Blessed One, proceeding on his journey, arrived in due course at Sâvatthi; and there.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a></p>  
 <p>at Sâvatthi, the Blessed One stayed in the <i>G</i>etavana, the park of Anâtha Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>ika. Then Anâtha Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>ika the householder [invited the Blessed One for the morrow's meal, and when the meal was over, he said to the Blessed One <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="#fn_564"><font size="1">1</font></a>]:</p>  
 <p>'What, Lord, shall I do with regard to the <i>G</i>etavana?'</p>  
 <p>You may dedicate it, O householder, to the use of the Sa<i>m</i>gha of the four directions <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_565"><font size="1">2</font></a> either now here present, or hereafter to arrive.'</p>  
 <p>'Even so, Lord,' said Anâtha Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>ika the householder in assent to the Blessed One, and he did so.</p>  
 <p>2. Then the Blessed One gave thanks to Anâtha Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>ika the householder in these verses. [Here follow the same verses as were used above in VI, 1, 5 on the presentation of the <i>G</i>etavana <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">3</font></a>.]</p>
