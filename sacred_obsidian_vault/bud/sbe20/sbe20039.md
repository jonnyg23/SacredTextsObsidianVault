 <p><a name="page_134"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 134</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">24.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu tied his bowl with a string, and suspending it on a staff <a name="fr_347"></a><a href="#fn_340"><font size="1">1</font></a>, went after noon out of a certain village gate. The people calling out, 'There goes a thief; his sword is glistening,' fell upon him, and seized him. But on recognising him, they let him go. That Bhikkhu, returning to the Ârâma, told this matter to the Bhikkhus.</p>  
 <p>'What then, Sir,. did you carry a staff with a string to it?'</p>  
 <p>'It is even so, Sirs.'</p>  
 <p>Those Bhikkhus who were moderate murmured (&amp;c., as usual, see I, 1, 2, 3) . . . . told the Blessed One . . . . he addressed the Bhikkhus, and said:</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to carry a staff with a string to it. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu was sick, and he could not wander about without a staff. They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a></p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give to a sick Bhikkhu the permission (license) to use a staff. And thus, O Bhikkhus, should it be given. That sick Bhikkhu, O Bhikkhus, should go up to the Sa<i>m</i>gha [here follow the words of a Kammavâ<i>k</i>â, precisely as in V, 20, 7]'</p>  
 <p>3. [Similar paragraphs ending with Kammavâ<i>k</i>âs for license to lift the bowl with a string, and with both a staff and a string.]</p>
