   <h3 align="center">24.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain lay disciple had given to the Bhikkhunî-sa<i>m</i>gha a servant's lodge <a name="fr_948"></a><a href="#fn_932"><font size="1">2</font></a> (to live in).</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a stable.'</p>  
 <p>It did not satisfy (their wants) <a name="fr_949"></a><a href="#fn_933"><font size="1">3</font></a>. They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, a separate residence for Bhikkhunîs.'</p>  
 <p>That did not satisfy their wants. They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, building operations <a name="fr_950"></a><a href="#fn_934"><font size="1">4</font></a> (to be carried on for the benefit of Bhikkhunîs).'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_932"></a><a href="sbe20115.htm#fr_948">363:2</a> This word has already occurred, in a list of various buildings, at Mahâvagga III, 5, 9, where we have rendered it stable. Buddhaghosa says here uddositan (MS. udosita) ti bha<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-sâlâ, and as at Mahâvagga I, 61, assa-bha<i>n</i><i>d</i>â and hatthi-bha<i>n</i><i>d</i>â are evidently grooms attending on horses or elephants (compare <i>G</i>âtaka I, 62, 3), bha<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-sâlâ may mean a servant's hall for the use of that particular class of servants. A lawsuit about an uddosita forms the Introductory Story to the 1st Bhikkhunî Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa, but the passage throws no light on the special meaning of the term. See also the Sutta-vibhaṅga on Nissaggiya II, 3, 5, and Khudda Sikkhâ III, 19. The Sanskrit equivalent might perhaps be udavasita, if the reading of the Samanta Pâsâdikâ had any traditional value; but the dd is not doubtful in the MSS. of the text at any of the passages quoted. Assa-sâlâ is the word for stable at <i>G</i>âtaka I, 62, 3.</p>
