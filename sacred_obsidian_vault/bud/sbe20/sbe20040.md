   <h3 align="center">25.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu was a ruminator <a name="fr_348"></a><a href="#fn_341"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and he, continually ruminating, used to chew the cud.</p>  
 <p>The Bhikkhus murmured, were annoyed, and were indignant, saying, 'This Bhikkhu eats food out of hours <a name="fr_349"></a><a href="#fn_342"><font size="1">2</font></a>.' And they told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'This Bhikkhu, O Bhikkhus, has but lately fallen from the condition of being an ox. I allow, O Bhikkhus, to a ruminator the chew of the cud. But nothing, O Bhikkhus, brought from the door of the mouth to the outside thereof is to be so chewed <a name="fr_350"></a><a href="#fn_343"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be dealt with according to the law <a name="fr_351"></a><a href="#fn_344"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>
