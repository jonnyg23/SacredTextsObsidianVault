   <h3 align="center">19.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a sheath of felt <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_303"><font size="1">2</font></a> had come into the possession of the Sa<i>m</i>gha.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, a sheath.'</p>  
 <p>The Bhikkhus considered whether a sheath was a thing which each one might keep for himself, or a thing which ought to be handed over from time to time by one Bhikkhu to another.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>  
 <p>'A sheath, O Bhikkhus, is neither to be appropriated nor to be handed over <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_304"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus used to eat lying on decorated divans.</p>  
 <p>People murmured, &amp;c saying, 'Like those still living in the pleasures of the world.'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to eat lying on decorated divans <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_305"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu was sick, and when eating he was not able to hold his bowl in his hand.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a stand for the bowl <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_306"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus used to eat out of one dish, to drink out of one vessel, and to lie on one bed, one coverlet, or one mat <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>The people murmured, &amp;c. . . . .</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>
