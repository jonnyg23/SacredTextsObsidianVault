 <p>
 This book contains three separate, and very important,
 works detailing Tibetan Tantric Buddhist thought and practice:
 <cite>Seven Initiation Rituals of the Tibetan Tantra</cite>,
 <cite>The Six Yogas of Naropa</cite>,
 and <cite>The Vow of Mahamudra</cite>.
 These were translated and edited by Musés
 assisted by exiled Tibetan monks from manuscripts in the Library
 of Congress.
 </p>
 <p>
 There are some very strange passages here.
 There is
 <a href="ettt03.htm#page_36">the mysterious prophecy of Mi-Gyur-Dorje</a>
 found in one of the manuscripts among the initiation rituals,
 which tells of a far future time when Tibet will be shattered
 and perscuted by demons.
 Naropa relates
 <a href="ettt18.htm#page_256">the Yoga of Entering Another's Body</a>
 (which can be used to reanimate a corpse!).
 Naropa also discusses at some length what we call
 <a href="ettt15.htm">lucid dreaming</a>, but which he calls <i>The Practice of
 the Illusory Body</i>.
 Of course, these are all pretty advanced studies, but it is fascinating
 to learn what the Tibetan monks were up to, firsthand.
 If all you have read about Tibetan Tantra is western descriptions, you
 owe it to yourself to take a look at these primary texts.
 </p>
