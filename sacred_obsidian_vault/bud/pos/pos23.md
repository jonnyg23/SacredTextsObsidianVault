 <h3 align="center"><a name="NOTES">NOTES<br>
 </a></h3>
 
 
 <p>
 I. On clansmen, to which reference is so often made in the Chronicles (clansman's family=<i>kulageha</i>), see Rhys Davids, <i>Buddhist India</i>, 17-22. 'Treasurer' or 'merchant' stands for <i>seṭṭhi</i>, a leading commoner, head (<i>seṭṭhi</i>=chief) of a guild, and often treasurer to a King.
 </p>
 
 <p>
 II. On the co-existing customs of cremation and exposure of the dead, to which the Psalms testify, see Rhys Davids, <i>Buddhist India</i>, 78 ſ.
 </p>
 
 <p>
 III. In the <a href="pos10.htm#66-1">note on p. 66</a>, reference should have been made to the Viticcha-jātaka, No. 244, in the second volume of the Jātaka. Here the Buddha himself asks the question, <i>Ekaŋ nāma kiŋ</i>? whereupon the itinerant debater runs away! The birth-story is then told, the question being <i>contra</i> the theory of the <i>Ding-an-sich</i>.
 </p>
 
 
 <h3 align="center"><a name="CORRIGENDUM">CORRIGENDUM<br>
 </a></h3>
 
 
 <p>
 In <a href="pos10.htm#72">verse 72</a>, where Professors Pischel and E. Müller have read 
 </p><blockquote><i>
 ... aññā samatimaññi 'haŋ,
 </i></blockquote>
 I now incline, with the Commentary, to read
 <blockquote><i>
 aññāsam atimaññi 'haŋ,
 </i></blockquote>
 and would amend the English thus:
 <blockquote><i>
 Filled with the pride of my youth, I scorned and despised other women.
 </i></blockquote>
 
 
 <p>
 Again, in <a href="pos10.htm#74">verse 74</a>, a truer rendering would be:
 </p><blockquote><i>
 Manifold wiles I wrought, mocking with insolent laughter.
 </i></blockquote>
 
 
 <p>
 And in <a href="pos10.htm#76">verse 76</a>, for 'calm' read 'cool.'
 </p>
