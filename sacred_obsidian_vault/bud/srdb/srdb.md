 <h3>IN THE TIBETAN LANGUAGE</h3>
 
 <h3>THE COMMENTARY OF MANNERS CALLED THE TREE OF WISDOM</h3>
 
 <h3>(I DO) OBEISANCE TO THE THREE DEITIES.</h3>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <table cellpadding="10">
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_1"></a><font color="GREEN">1</font>.</td><td> Evil persons should be brought under control.<br>
 The wise should be reverenced.<br>
 Fill your treasury with honest deeds<br>
 (And) protect your own countrymen.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_2"></a><font color="GREEN">2</font>.</td><td> (As regards) your own secrets and those of others,<br>
 If you guard these as your own dear child<br>
 He for whom all earthly things are equal<br>
 Will make love for man the principal affection.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_3"></a><font color="GREEN">3</font>.</td><td> If your wife is evil and your friend evil,<br>
 If the King is evil and your relatives evil,<br>
 If your neighbour is evil and the country evil,<br>
 (Then) abandon them for a distant (land).</td></tr>

 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_4"></a><font color="GREEN">4</font>.</td><td> Avoid<a name="page_4_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_4_note_*">*</a> that friend who is greedy for wealth.<br>
 Avoid a wife who is fond of fornication.<br>
 If unskilful in curing and hard to please (or respect)<br>
 Avoid such a doctor.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_5"></a><font color="GREEN">5</font>.</td><td> Although you know the difference between good and bad deeds<br>
 You should carry out your work after consultation.<br>
 Although you may only succeed partially<br>
 Even without succeeding you are to be admired.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_6"></a><font color="GREEN">6</font>.</td><td> The steadfast who speak in few words and politely<br>
 Are very much respected by mankind.<br>
 As the sun which coming out from the shadow<br>
 By his rays creates great heat.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_7"></a><font color="GREEN">7</font>.</td><td> (As regards) these doctriries, though you may suffer<br>
 (Yet) be not anxious in your mind.<br>
 When the moon has been eclipsed<br>
 Will it not shine again?</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_8"></a><font color="GREEN">8</font>.</td><td> Just as for the garlands on the altars<br>
 Only full-blown flowers are gathered,<br>
 So a gardener, in the same way,<br>
 Does not uproot the plant.<a name="page_6_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_6_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_9"></a><font color="GREEN">9</font>.</td><td> Rewards and royal favours,<br>
 The price of prostitution and praise (from pupil),<br>
 The hire of a boat and the rent of a dwelling,<br>
 These may be asked for at once<br>
 For these six are not to be had after a long interval.<a name="page_6_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_6_note_dagger">†</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_10"></a><font color="GREEN">10</font>.</td><td> A magic spell misunderstood is poison.<br>
 Indifference to illness is poison.<br>
 An old man keeping company with woman is poison.<br>
 A poor man's sleep in the daytime is poison.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_11"></a><font color="GREEN">11</font>.</td><td> Worthy men who do not make many promises,<br>
 But if a promise is made under pressure<br>
 Then it is like a carving on stone.<br>
 Even should they die it is not altered.</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_12"></a><font color="GREEN">12</font>.</td><td> You will come to terms with your enemy on occasion<br>
 And on occasion you will quarrel with your friends.<br>
 Having learned to distinguish what should be done and what not,<br>
 The clever man will always select his opportunity.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_13"></a><font color="GREEN">13</font>.</td><td> If you sin in speech you will be damned.<br>
 The parrot, the singing bird and the waterhen,<br>
 The silent waterduck which man does not catch--<br>
 Their entire accomplishment is keeping silence.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_14"></a><font color="GREEN">14</font>.</td><td> If you keep your weapons in order your enemy will be subdued.<br>
 By wearing poor clothes a woman will be subdued.<br>
 By treatment illness will be subdued.<br>
 Ignorance will be subdued (overcome) by effort.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_15"></a><font color="GREEN">15</font>.</td><td> Keep your resolves to yourself as a secret,<br>
 Like the body which lies (hid) in the mud.<br>
 If it were not that the sprouts attract attention<br>
 Who would find the lotus root?</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_16"></a><font color="GREEN">16</font>.</td><td> Although it may become perceptible<br>
 Still a non-existent substance (reality) will not be mentioned.<br>
 How could the peacock in the painting eat the jewels?<a name="page_10_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_10_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_17"></a><font color="GREEN">17</font>.</td><td> If anyone plans in his mind to do evil<br>
 He always uses pleasant words to the other.<br>
 When the hunter sees the game to be killed<br>
 He sings a sweet song to please it.<a name="page_10_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_10_note_dagger">†</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_18"></a><font color="GREEN">18</font>.</td><td> Whatever your thought (or opinion) may be<br>
 It should be clearly impressed on all men's minds.<br>
 They are thus brought under your control,<br>
 As by the wish-granting gem.<a name="page_10_fr_ddagger"></a><a href="#page_10_note_ddagger">‡</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_19"></a><font color="GREEN">19</font>.</td><td> It is easy to live by carrying the loads of others.<br>
 It is easy to dress in tree-bark in the forest.<br>
 It is easier for men to die<br>
 Than to spend their days in quarrelling.</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_20"></a><font color="GREEN">20</font>.</td><td> The root-principle of mankind is not to quarrel.<br>
 What would you do with wealth obtained by quarrelling?<br>
 What would you do with wealth and life<br>
 Obtained by pride and the suppression of good.<a name="page_12_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_12_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_21"></a><font color="GREEN">21</font>.</td><td> He who undertakes work which he cannot carry out,<br>
 Who vies with the multitude and disputes with the powerful,<br>
 Who lets a woman know his thoughts--<br>
 The four gods of death sit at his door.<a name="page_12_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_12_note_dagger">†</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_22"></a><font color="GREEN">22</font>.</td><td> Regard not an evil prince.<br>
 Regard not deceitful relatives.<br>
 Regard not a lustful woman.<br>
 Regard not a great sinner.<a name="page_12_fr_ddagger"></a><a href="#page_12_note_ddagger">‡</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_23"></a><font color="GREEN">23</font>.</td><td> He who can go anywhere<br>
 Why should he be injured through his attachment to his own native land?<br>
 The evil man says "it is my own well"<br>
 And saying thus, drinks the brackish water.</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_24"></a><font color="GREEN">24</font>.</td><td> A highly learned man<br>
 Has two (forms of) felicity.<br>
 Either he will abandon all earthly interests<br>
 Or else has much which could be abandoned.<a name="page_14_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_14_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_25"></a><font color="GREEN">25</font>.</td><td> He whose glory has left him (and)<br>
 Whose efforts have become meaningless,<br>
 When a holy man becomes impoverished (in this way)<br>
 He is unhappy except in a forest.<a name="page_14_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_14_note_dagger">†</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_26"></a><font color="GREEN">26</font>.</td><td> The career of a holy man is of two kinds.<br>
 Like a flower waving its head<br>
 Which is either honoured by all in the world<br>
 Or else disappears alone in the forest.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_27"></a><font color="GREEN">27</font>.</td><td> Life, which perishes naturally in a moment,<br>
 Has this as its essence.<br>
 As your actions and intellect are undeveloped<br>
 Remain modest in society.<a name="page_14_fr_ddagger"></a><a href="#page_14_note_ddagger">‡</a></td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_28"></a><font color="GREEN">28</font>.</td><td> An anthill increases by accumulation.<br>
 Similarly eye-medicine is used up by distribution.<br>
 The to-be-feared grows less by association.<br>
 That is <i>the</i> thing to understand.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_29"></a><font color="GREEN">29</font>.</td><td> The anthill and honey,<br>
 The waxing moon, up to the full,<br>
 The possessions of kings and beggars<br>
 Increase by gradual accumulation.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_30"></a><font color="GREEN">30</font>.</td><td> Do not be excessively covetous.<br>
 Great greed entails punishment.<br>
 If you are stultified by excessive covetousness<br>
 You are like the fox that was killed by the bow.<a name="page_16_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_16_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_31"></a><font color="GREEN">31</font>.</td><td> He who always pursues the man who can do something for him<br>
 And pays no heed to the man who has done something<br>
 Is like the wicked man whose ear was filled with curds.<br>
 Such a one has stolen that which was guarded.<a name="page_16_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_16_note_dagger">†</a><br>
 [O, Karna, evil-minded like curdled milk,<br>
 You have conquered what you should have protected.]</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_32"></a><font color="GREEN">32</font>.</td><td> Do not go (in search of) excessive fame.<br>
 Judge honestly for yourself.<br>
 By the fall of the 'bel' fruit into the water<br>
 See, the forest was deserted.<a name="page_18_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_18_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_33"></a><font color="GREEN">33</font>.</td><td> Do not say things which hurt the feelings (of others).<br>
 Do not speak in a very injurious way.<br>
 The good man and the armed<br>
 Enemy become known.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_34"></a><font color="GREEN">34</font>.</td><td> Even if the son of his enemy speaks sweetly<br>
 The wise man is not indifferent.<a name="page_18_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_18_note_dagger">†</a><br>
 As a poisonous leaf is extremely potent (irresistible)<br>
 It will cause injury at any time.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_35"></a><font color="GREEN">35</font>.</td><td> Whosoever does benefit to his enemy<br>
 With straightforward intention,<br>
 By so doing all enemies will arrive at<br>
 The state of folding their hands in devotion.</td></tr>

 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_36"></a><font color="GREEN">36</font>.</td><td> In desiring to injure your enemy<br>
 Praise his inherent good qualities.<br>
 What do evil thoughts of injury do?<br>
 They injure you and not your enemy.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_37"></a><font color="GREEN">37</font>.</td><td> Be firm with the unruly, not with mildness<br>
 But with suitable harshness.<br>
 If the children are not diligent<a name="page_20_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_20_note_*">*</a><br>
 Does not the beneficent father threaten punishment?</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_38"></a><font color="GREEN">38</font>.</td><td> As long as you watch the 'way,'<br>
 As long as your steps are steady,<br>
 As long as your wisdom is unimpaired,<br>
 So long is there profit for you.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_39"></a><font color="GREEN">39</font>.</td><td> If you are always seeking your own advantage<br>
 What is the use of remaining among the multitude?<br>
 There exists no means whatever for<br>
 Making all beings rejoice exceedingly.<a name="page_20_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_20_note_dagger">†</a></td></tr>

 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_40"></a><font color="GREEN">40</font>.</td><td> To seek from others and yet wish for good food,<br>
 To spend your life in begging and yet have great pride,<br>
 To be ignorant of literary works and yet wish to dispute,--<br>
 These three make you ridiculous to others.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_41"></a><font color="GREEN">41</font>.</td><td> The fire which burned the forest<br>
 Became the companion of the wind,<br>
 And that same extinguished the fire.<br>
 So has the weak man no friends.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_42"></a><font color="GREEN">42</font>.</td><td> Not doing harm to others,<br>
 Not bowing down to low people,<br>
 Not abandoning the path of virtue,--<br>
 These are small (points) but (really) very many.<a name="page_22_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_22_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_43"></a><font color="GREEN">43</font>.</td><td> Having no fear of disease,<br>
 Endeavouring to associate with the holy,<br>
 Not using the (vulgar) language of the mean,--<br>
 A day (spent thus) is greater than a hundred years.</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_44"></a><font color="GREEN">44</font>.</td><td> Whenever the mean find a little wealth<br>
 They despise everybody and are filled with pride.<br>
 But the virtuous, although they may attain the possession of wealth,<br>
 Remain bowed like ripe rice.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_45"></a><font color="GREEN">45</font>.</td><td> Low class creatures,<br>
 Should they become possessed of wealth or science,<br>
 Think only of quarrelling with everybody<br>
 Like the fox with the blue skin.<a name="page_24_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_24_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_46"></a><font color="GREEN">46</font>.</td><td> If they become possessed of wealth or learning<br>
 Low people become proud.<br>
 But even when doubly honoured<br>
 The wise man will become the more humble.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_47"></a><font color="GREEN">47</font>.</td><td> Trade without profit, quarrelling with those who have a following,<br>
 Despising to beg (and thus) poor, delighting in lust,<br>
 Using rough language to young women,--<br>
 These five are improper conduct in a man.</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_48"></a><font color="GREEN">48</font>.</td><td> The peacock, although scorched by heat in summer,<br>
 Would rather hope for rain from the middle of the sky<br>
 Than bend its proud neck to the bad water of the dirty pond.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_49"></a><font color="GREEN">49</font>.</td><td> The ti-ti-ra bird preserves its own life (somehow) with dew drops,<br>
 For it fears to be placed under an obligation by begging<br>
 And will not even beg from Indra.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_50"></a><font color="GREEN">50</font>.</td><td> If you understand Real Truth, why have a teacher?<br>
 When the disease is cured, what is the use of the doctor?<br>
 When the water is crossed, what (use) is the boatman?<br>
 What use is a sorcerer to a man without passions?<a name="page_26_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_26_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_51"></a><font color="GREEN">51</font>.</td><td> As long as an evil man is weak<br>
 So long is he naturally good,<br>
 (Like) the waters of a river in autumn<br>
 Which can be easily crossed by everyone.</td></tr>

 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_52"></a><font color="GREEN">52</font>.</td><td> Where store is made by the mouse,<br>
 Where the cat guards the butter,<br>
 And the crow is the director of ceremonies--<br>
 How could reliance be placed in such an arrangement?<a name="page_28_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_28_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_53"></a><font color="GREEN">53</font>.</td><td> If there is much discussion about anything<br>
 All make boast of their skill,<br>
 And as all wish to be placed foremost<br>
 That gathering is brought to naught.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_54"></a><font color="GREEN">54</font>.</td><td> Copulation by day and sleeping by day,<br>
 Fresh beer, curds and young people drinking,<br>
 Sleeping with withered old women,--<br>
 These six waste the bodily strength.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_55"></a><font color="GREEN">55</font>.</td><td> He whose anger causes no fear,<br>
 Who, when pleased, can confer no benefit,<br>
 Who can neither destroy nor subjugate--<br>
 What avails the anger of such a man?</td></tr>

 <a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 30</font></a>
 
 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_56"></a><font color="GREEN">56</font>.</td><td> The encounter with the time of misfortune and disease,<br>
 A time of famine and danger from enemies,<br>
 Being at the king's gate or in Yama's abode,--<br>
 These are the common remedy (i.e. affect all alike).</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_57"></a><font color="GREEN">57</font>.</td><td> (People) strive for worldly things because they want them.<br>
 It is not done for the sake of righteousness.<br>
 Like the calf which, seeing that the milk is exhausted,<br>
 Leaves the cow at a distance.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_58"></a><font color="GREEN">58</font>.</td><td> Separation from the object of affection, the contempt of one's own people,<br>
 To be much in debt, association with the evil,<br>
 To be abandoned by friends who see your poverty,<br>
 These five are not fire yet they burn the body.<a name="page_30_fr_*"></a><a href="#page_30_note_*">*</a></td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_59"></a><font color="GREEN">59</font>.</td><td> He who, when small discords increase,<br>
 Does not attempt to make peace,<br>
 Is like the bee's honey leaking in drops,<br>
 And the ruin of a country.<a name="page_30_fr_dagger"></a><a href="#page_30_note_dagger">†</a></td></tr>

 <!------------------------page 32------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 32</font></a>
 
 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_60"></a><font color="GREEN">60</font>.</td><td> He who has knowledge is firm.<br>
 The holy, even when destitute, do not discard moral virtues,<br>
 Although scorched by the sun's natural heat<br>
 The natural cold of snow is not taken away.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_61"></a><font color="GREEN">61</font>.</td><td> Those who wish to terminate their sins believe in Buddha.<br>
 Those who wish to terminate their earthly existences associate with the evil.<br>
 Those who wish to terminate their families beget fools and idiots.<br>
 When cereals are to be consumed the stomach becomes heated.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_62"></a><font color="GREEN">62</font>.</td><td> Those blinded by desire do not perceive their sin.<br>
 The blind man does not see the shape of (things).<br>
 The proud do not perceive their faults.<br>
 He who regards himself (the egotist) does not perceive Real Truth.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_63"></a><font color="GREEN">63</font>.</td><td> A conqueror, a water channel, a creeping plant,<br>
 Women and the blind, these five,<br>
 How they are led by the crafty!<br>
 And this leading places them in the power of others.</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td colspan="2" align="center">
 
 <!------------------------page 34------------------------>

 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_64"></a><font color="GREEN">64</font>.</td><td> The misery which follows pleasure<br>
 Is the pleasure which follows misery.<br>
 The happiness and misery of mankind<br>
 Revolve like a wheel.</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_65"></a><font color="GREEN">65</font>.</td><td> The invivible appears before you<br>
 And again becomes invisible.<br>
 What is that of yours and what are you?<br>
 Who will be miserable on that account?</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_66"></a><font color="GREEN">66</font>.</td><td> Thus the logs of wood<br>
 Which go down together to the great ocean<br>
 Are driven apart by every wave.<br>
 Who will be miserable on that account?</td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_67"></a><font color="GREEN">67</font>.</td><td> The very wise man conquers rather by forcefulness than by anger.<br>
 The evil man fails by being angry.<br>
 Oh, evil men, defeated by defeat,<br>
 How could you be called to the society of the wise?</td></tr>
 
 <tr><td colspan="2" align="center">
 
 <!------------------------page 36------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 36</font></a>
 
 </td></tr>
 
 <tr valign="top"><td align="right"><a name="pp_68"></a><font color="GREEN">68</font>.</td><td> A big stone, by a great effort,<br>
 May be thrown to the top of a hill,<br>
