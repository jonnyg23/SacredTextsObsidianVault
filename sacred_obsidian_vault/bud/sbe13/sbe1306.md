 <h2 align="center">ANIYATÂ DHAMMÂ<a name="page_16_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_16_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a>.</h2>
 
 <p align="center">RULES REGARDING UNDETERMINED MATTERS.</p>
 
 <p>   Here, venerable Sirs, the two Rules regarding undetermined matters come into recitation.</p>
 
 <p>   </p><p>   <a name="1"></a><font color="GREEN">1</font>. Whatsoever Bhikkhu shall take a seat with a woman, one man with one woman, in secret, on a secluded seat, suitable<a name="page_16_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_16_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a> (for sexual intercourse); and if a believing woman, trustworthy of speech, who has seen (them so), shall lay it to his charge under one or other of three Rules, either under the Pârâ<i>g</i>ika<a name="page_16_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_16_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a>, or under the Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa<a name="page_16_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_16_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a> or under the Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya<a name="page_16_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_16_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a> Rules:--let then that Bhikkhu, if he acknowledge that he has so sat, be dealt with (according to the circumstances reported) for a Pârâ<i>g</i>ika, or for a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa, or fora Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya; or let that Bhikkhu be dealt with under that one of those three Rules under which the believing woman, trustworthy in speech, shall lay it to his charge.</p>
 
 <p>   This rule relates to a matter undetermined.</p>
 
 <p>   </p><p>   <a name="2"></a><font color="GREEN">2</font>. And furthermore, even if the seat be not secluded, and not convenient (for sexual intercourse), but be convenient for addressing a woman with wicked words<a name="page_16_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_16_note_6"><sup><small>6</small></sup></a>, then whatsoever Bhikkhu shall take
 
 <!------------------------page 17------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 17</font></a>
 
 a seat with a woman, one man with one woman, in secret, on such a seat, and a believing woman, trustworthy in speech, who has seen (them so), shall lay it to his charge under one or other of two Rules, either under the Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa, or under the Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya Rule--let then that Bhikkhu, if he acknowledge that he has so sat, be dealt with (according to the circumstances reported) for a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa, or for a Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya; or let that Bhikkhu be dealt with under that one of those two Rules under which the believing woman, trustworthy in speech, shall lay it to his charge.</p>
 
 <p>   This rule relates to a matter undetermined.</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p>   Venerable Sirs, the two Rules regarding uncertain matters have been recited.</p>
 
 <p>   In respect of them I ask the venerable ones, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   A second time I ask the venerable ones, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   A third time I ask the venerable ones, 'Are you pure in this matter ?'</p>
 
 <p>   The venerable ones are pure herein. Therefore do they keep silence. Thus I understand.</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p>   Here endeth the recitation of the Aniyatas.</p>
