 <h1 align="center">THE PÂTIMOKKHA.</h1>
 
 <h2 align="center">THE WORDS OF DISBURDENMENT.</h2>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <h3 align="center">REVERENCE TO THE BLESSED ONE, THE HOLY ONE, THE FULLY ENLIGHTENED ONE.</h3>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <h2 align="center">NIDANA<a name="page_1_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_1_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a>.</h2>
 
 <p align="center">INTRODUCTION.</p>
 
 <p>   May the Chapter<a name="page_1_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_1_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>, reverend Sirs, hear me!</p>
 
 <p>   To-day is the sacred day (of the full, or new, moon), the fifteenth day of the half-month<a name="page_1_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_1_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a>. If it be convenient to the Chapter, let the Chapter hold Uposatha, let it repeat the Pâtimokkha. How is it with respect to the necessary preliminaries to a meeting of the Order? Let the reverend brethren announce their purity<a name="page_1_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_1_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a>, and I will rehearse the Pâtimokkha! We all gladly give ear and do attend<a name="page_1_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_1_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a>!</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 2------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 2</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   Whosoever have incurred a fault, let him declare it! If no fault have been incurred it is meet to keep silence!</p>
 
 <p>   Now, venerable Sirs, it is by your silence, that I shall know whether you are pure. As to each one question put there must be an answer, so, in such a meeting as this, each question is put<a name="page_2_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_2_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a> as many as three times. Then if any Bhikkhu, when it has been three times put, knowingly omit to declare a fault incurred, he is guilty of uttering a conscious lie. Venerable Sirs, the uttering of a deliberate lie has been declared by the Blessed One to be a condition hurtful (to spiritual progress)<a name="page_2_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_2_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>. Therefore a fault, if there be one, should be declared by that Bhikkhu who remembers it, and desires to be cleansed therefrom. For a fault, when declared, shall be light to him.</p>
 
 <p>   Venerable Sirs, the Introduction is now recited.</p>
 
 <p>   Thus do I question you, venerable Sirs, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   A second time do I question you, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   A third time do I question you, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   The venerable ones are pure herein. Therefore do they keep silence. Thus I understand.</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p>   Here endeth the recitation of the Introduction.</p>
