   <h3 align="center">11.</h3>  
 <p>1. When he had accomplished that Mânatta, and while he was worthy to be rehabilitated, he committed an offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence--which he did not conceal.</p>  
 <p>He told the Bhikkhus [all that had happened from chapter 6 onwards down to this last offence]. They told, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>Let then the Sa<i>m</i>gha, O Bhikkhus, impose upon Udâyi the Bhikkhu a Mânatta of six days' duration, for that he when he had (&amp;c., as in first paragraph down to) not conceal, throwing him back to the commencement (of his Mânatta).</p>  
 <p>'Now thus, O Bhikkhus, [here follows the kammavâ<i>k</i>â as before.]</p>
