   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the venerable Upâli went up to the place where the Blessed One was; and on arriving there, he saluted the Blessed One, and took his seat on one side. And when he was so seated the venerable Upâli said to the Blessed One: 'Now in what case, Lord, can there be an interruption of the Mânatta discipline <a name="fr_727"></a><a href="#fn_709"><font size="1">2</font></a> of a Bhikkhu who is undergoing that discipline?'</p>  
 <p>There are four ways of interruption of the Mânatta discipline, O Upâli, of a Bhikkhu who is undergoing that discipline; (that is to say), by dwelling together <a name="fr_728"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">3</font></a>, by dwelling alone <a name="fr_729"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">3</font></a>, by not announcing <a name="fr_730"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and by living with less than four other Bhikkhus <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">4</font></a>. These are the four ways (&amp;c., as before, down to) undergoing that discipline.'</p>
