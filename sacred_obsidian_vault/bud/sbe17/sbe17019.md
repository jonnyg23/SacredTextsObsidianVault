   <h3 align="center">5.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus who were sick had need of leaves as medicine.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of leaves as</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 46</font></a></p>  
 <p>medicine--the leaves of the nimba <a name="fr_93"></a><a href="#fn_92"><font size="1">1</font></a>, of the ku<i>t</i>aga <a name="fr_94"></a><a href="#fn_92"><font size="1">1</font></a>, of the pa<i>t</i>ola <a name="fr_95"></a><a href="#fn_93"><font size="1">2</font></a>, of the tulasi <a name="fr_96"></a><a href="#fn_94"><font size="1">3</font></a>, of the kappâsika <a name="fr_97"></a><a href="#fn_95"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and whatsoever other leaves are used for medicine, and impart an appetising flavour to foods, either hard or soft, which the sick Bhikkhus could not otherwise eat. They may be received, and stored up (&amp;c., as in last chapter, down to:) guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>
