   <h3 align="center">4.</h3>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhu, after the Ka<i>th</i>ina ceremony has been held, takes a robe not ready, and goes away. And when he has got beyond the boundary he thinks, "I will have the robe made up here, and will never go back;" and he gets the robe made up, &amp;c. <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">3</font></a>'</p>  
 <p align="center">__________________</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the section entitled Âdâya-<i>kh</i>akka <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_307"></a><a href="sbe17058.htm#fr_312">160:3</a> Six of the seven cases specified in chap. 2 (with the exception of the first of the seven) are repeated here in the same words, with the only difference that instead of 'takes a robe' it is said here 'takes a robe not ready.' The first case is necessarily omitted, because it is essential to that case, that the Bhikkhu going away takes with him a robe ready for wear.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_308"></a><a href="sbe17058.htm#fr_313">160:4</a> 'The six cases in which he takes a robe away.'</p>
