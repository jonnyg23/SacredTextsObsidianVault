   <h3 align="center">39.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time, seedlings belonging to the Sa<i>m</i>gha grew upon private ground, and seedlings belonging to private persons grew upon ground which was the property of the Sa<i>m</i>gha.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Of seedlings belonging to the Sa<i>m</i>gha, grown upon private ground, half the produce, O Bhikkhus, you may have, when you have given a part to the private owner. Of seedlings belonging to a private person, grown upon ground the property of the Sa<i>m</i>gha, you may have the use, when you have given a part to the private owner <a name="fr_265"></a><a href="#fn_261"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_261"></a><a href="sbe17053.htm#fr_265">143:1</a> Buddhaghosa explains the 'part' (bhâga<i>m</i>) as the twelfth part, which, he says, is in accordance with the ancient custom of India. Used absolutely, as in this passage, bhâga usually means 'half.'</p>
