   <h3 align="center">5 <a name="fr_604"></a><a href="#fn_593"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'A Bhikkhu against whom the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma has been carried out ought to conduct himself aright. And herein this is the right conduct <a name="fr_605"></a><a href="#fn_594"><font size="1">2</font></a>: he ought not to confer the upasampadâ--he ought not to give a nissaya <a name="fr_606"></a><a href="#fn_595"><font size="1">3</font></a>--he ought not to provide</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 338</font></a></p>  
 <p>himself with a sâma<i>n</i>era <a name="fr_607"></a><a href="#fn_596"><font size="1">1</font></a>--he ought not to accept the office of giving exhortation to the nuns <a name="fr_608"></a><a href="#fn_597"><font size="1">2</font></a>--and if he has accepted the office, he ought not to exhort the nuns <a name="fr_609"></a><a href="#fn_597"><font size="1">2</font></a>--he ought not to commit the offence for which the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma has been carried out by the Sa<i>m</i>gha against him--nor any offence of a similar kind--nor any worse offence--he ought not to find fault with the proceeding (that has been carried out against him)--nor with (the Bhikkhus) who have carried it out--he ought not to raise objection against a regular <a name="fr_610"></a><a href="#fn_598"><font size="1">3</font></a> Bhikkhu's taking part in the Uposatha ceremony <a name="fr_611"></a><a href="#fn_599"><font size="1">4</font></a>--or in the Pavâra<i>n</i>â ceremony <a name="fr_612"></a><a href="#fn_600"><font size="1">5</font></a>--he ought not to issue commands (to a junior inhibiting him from going beyond the bounds <a name="fr_613"></a><a href="#fn_601"><font size="1">6</font></a>, or summoning him to come before the elders)--he ought not to set on foot a censure against any other Bhikkhu <a name="fr_614"></a><a href="#fn_602"><font size="1">7</font></a>--he ought not to ask another Bhikkhu to give him leave (to rebuke that Bhikkhu <a name="fr_615"></a><a href="#fn_603"><font size="1">8</font></a>)--he ought not to warn (another Bhikkhu <a name="fr_616"></a><a href="#fn_604"><font size="1">9</font></a> whom he supposes to be offending)--he ought</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 339</font></a></p>  
 <p>not to call upon another Bhikkhu to remember (whether he has or has not committed an offence)--and he ought not to associate with the Bhikkhus.'</p>  
 <p align="center">__________________</p>  
 <p align="center">Here end the eighteen duties which follow on a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_593"></a><a href="sbe17116.htm#fr_604">337:1</a> This chapter is repeated below for the Nissaya-, Pabbâ<i>g</i>aniya-, and Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kammas (chapters 10, 15, and 21). The corresponding rule for the first two Ukkhepaniya-kammas is different, and much more stringent (chapter 27, repeated in chapter 31); but that for the third (chapter 33) is again the same as the rule laid down in this chapter. In the second Khandhaka(s, 2) the list of restrictions is again longer.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_594"></a><a href="sbe17116.htm#fr_605">337:2</a> Sammâvattanâ. See Mahâvagga I, 26, 1; 27,1; 33, 1; 34, 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_595"></a><a href="sbe17116.htm#fr_606">337:3</a> Buddhaghosa says, âgantukâna<i>m</i> nissayo na dâtabbo. The relation of a junior Bhikkhu either to his upa<i>g</i><i>gh</i>âya or to his â<i>k</i>ariya is alike called nissaya (Mahâvagga I, 36, 1); but the term is more especially applied to the latter (Mahâvagga I, 32, 2, whereas in the corresponding formula for the upa<i>g</i><i>gh</i>âya, Mahâvagga <a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 338</font></a> I, 25, 7,-- the word nissaya does not occur). In other words, nissaya means all that is included in the phrase 'nissâya te vatthabba<i>m</i>' (<i>K</i>ullavagga I, 9, 2).</p>
