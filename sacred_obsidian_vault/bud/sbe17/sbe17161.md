   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>1. Whilst he was undergoing that probation, he committed an offence--the first, &amp;c.--which he did not conceal.</p>  
 <p>He told the Bhikkhus, saying, 'I committed, &amp;c. . . . I asked the Sa<i>m</i>gha, &amp;c. . . . The Sa<i>m</i>gha imposed upon me a probation of two--three--four--five days. Whilst I was undergoing that probation, I committed, &amp;c. . . .'</p>  
 <p><a name="page_402"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 402</font></a></p>  
 <p>They told that matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Let then the Sa<i>m</i>gha, O Bhikkhus, throw back Udâyi the Bhikkhu to the commencement (of his probationary course).'</p>  
 <p>2, 3. [Here follows the kammavâ<i>k</i>â as in chapter 1, with the necessary alterations.]</p>
