   <h3 align="center">2 <a name="fr_596"></a><a href="#fn_585"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'There are three things, O Bhikkhus, by which, when a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma is characterised, it is against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be settled; (that is to say), when it has not been carried out in a full assembly of properly qualified persons, 'according to law and justice, and in the presence of the litigant parties <a name="fr_597"></a><a href="#fn_586"><font size="1">3</font></a>--when it has been carried out without the accused person having been heard--when it has been carried out without the accused person having confessed himself guilty. A Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, O Bhikkhus, characterised</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 333</font></a></p>  
 <p>by these three things is against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be settled.</p>  
 <p>'There are other three things, O Bhikkhus, by which, when a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma has been characterised, it is against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be settled; (that is to say), when it has been carried out though no fault has been committed--when it has been carried out for a Pârâ<i>g</i>ika or a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence <a name="fr_598"></a><a href="#fn_587"><font size="1">1</font></a>--when it has been carried out though the fault has been confessed. A Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, O Bhikkhus, characterised (&amp;c., as before, down to) settled.</p>  
 <p>There are other three things, O Bhikkhus, by which, when a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma has been characterised, it is against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be settled; (that is to say), when it has been carried out without the accused person having been warned--when it has been carried out without the accused person having been called upon to remember (whether he has or has not committed the offence)--when it has been carried out without the accused person having been convicted. A Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, O Bhikkhus, characterised (&amp;c., as before, down to) settled.</p>  
 <p>'There are other three things, O Bhikkhus, by which, when a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma has been characterised, it is against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be settled; (that is to say), when it has not been carried out in a properly constituted meeting properly conducted <a name="fr_599"></a><a href="#fn_588"><font size="1">2</font></a>--when it has</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_334"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 334</font></a></p>  
 <p>been carried out without justice <a name="fr_600"></a><a href="#fn_589"><font size="1">1</font></a>--when it has been carried out without the presence and approval of all the Bhikkhus belonging to the particular circuit <a name="fr_601"></a><a href="#fn_590"><font size="1">2</font></a>. A Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, O Bhikkhus, characterised by these three things is (&amp;c., as before, down to) settled.</p>  
 <p>There are other three things, O Bhikkhus, by which, when a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma has been characterised, it is against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be settled; (that is to say), when it has been carried out without the accused person having been heard--when it has been carried out without justice--when it has been carried out without the presence and approval of all the Bhikkhus belonging to the circuit. A Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, O Bhikkhus, characterised by these three things is (&amp;c., as before, down to) settled.</p>  
 <p>'There are other three things (&amp;c., as before, down to) that is to say, when it has been carried out without the accused person having been convicted--when it has been carried out without justice--when it has been carried out without the presence and approval of all the Bhikkhus belonging to the circuit.'</p>  
 <p>[And in a similar way each of the three things in paragraphs 2 and 3 of this section are united with the two things just repeated in each of paragraphs 4, 5, and 6, to make six further cases in which a Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma is declared to be against the Dhamma, and against the Vinaya, and difficult to be revoked.]</p>  
 <p align="center">__________________</p>  
 <p align="center">Here end the twelve cases of a proceeding (Kamma) which is against the law.</p>
