   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus who were sick had need of various kinds of gum as-medicine. They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of gums as medicine--hiṅgu <a name="fr_104"></a><a href="#fn_102"><font size="1">1</font></a>, hiṅgu lac, sipâ<i>t</i>ikâ <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_103"><font size="1">2</font></a>, taka 3, taka-patti 3, taka-pa<i>n</i><i>n</i>i <a name="fr_106"></a><a href="#fn_104"><font size="1">3</font></a>, sa<i>g</i><i>g</i>ulasa <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_105"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and whatsoever other gums are used for medicine, and impart (&amp;c., as in chap. 4, down to:) is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>
