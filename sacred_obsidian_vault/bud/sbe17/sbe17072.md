   <h3 align="center">5.</h3>  
 <p>1. At that time people went to the Ârâma with robes <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_350"><font size="1">1</font></a> (which they intended to present to the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Bhikkhus). They found there no Bhikkhu who was to receive the robes; so they took them back again. (In consequence of that) few robes were given (to the Bhikkhus).</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you appoint a Bhikkhu possessed of the following five qualities, to receive the robes (presented to the Bhikkhus): (a person) who does not go in the evil course of lust, in the evil course of hatred, in the evil course of delusion, in the evil course of fear, and who knows what has been received and what has not.</p>  
 <p>2. 'And you ought, O Bhikkhus, to appoint (such a Bhikkhu) in this way: First, that Bhikkhu must be asked (to accept that commission). When he has been asked, let a learned, competent Bhikkhu proclaim the following <i>ñ</i>atti before the Sa<i>m</i>gha: "Let the Sa<i>m</i>gha, reverend Sirs, hear me. If the Sa<i>m</i>gha is ready, let the Sa<i>m</i>gha appoint the Bhikkhu N. N: to receive the robes (presented to the Bhikkhus). This is the <i>ñ</i>atti. Let the Sa<i>m</i>gha, reverend Sirs, hear me. The Sa<i>m</i>gha appoints the Bhikkhu N. N. to receive the robes (presented). Let any one of the venerable brethren who is in favour of our appointing the Bhikkhu N. N. to receive the robes (presented), be silent, and any one who is not in favour of it, speak. The Bhikkhu N. N. has been appointed by the Sa<i>m</i>gha to receive the robes (presented). The Sa<i>m</i>gha is in favour of it, therefore are you silent; thus I understand."'</p>
