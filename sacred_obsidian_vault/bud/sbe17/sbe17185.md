   <h3 align="center">32.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'And in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu who has rendered himself liable to the Mânatta discipline, or is undergoing the Mânatta discipline, or is fit to be rehabilitated, is guilty meanwhile of a number of Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences which he does not conceal; and he then throws off the robes, and again receives the upasampadâ,--then with regard to the Bhikkhu so liable to the Mânatta discipline, or undergoing the Mânatta discipline, or fit to be rehabilitated, the same rules are to apply as in the case of a Bhikkhu so acting while undergoing probation <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'And in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu who is fit to be rehabilitated <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">2</font></a> is guilty meanwhile of a number of Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences which he does not conceal; and he then becomes a Sâma<i>n</i>era, goes out of his mind, becomes weak in his mind, or becomes diseased in his sensations; his offences are some of them</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_431"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 431</font></a></p>  
 <p>concealed, some of them not concealed . . . . [and so on, as in chapter 30, down to the end, excepting that the penalty is here the same as it is in the previous chapters 31 and 32].'</p>
