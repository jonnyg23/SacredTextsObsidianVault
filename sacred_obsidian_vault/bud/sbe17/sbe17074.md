   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>At that time the Bhikkhus appointed to lay the robes by, laid the robes by in an open hall, or at the foot of a tree, or in the hollow of a Nimba tree <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">1</font></a>; thus they were eaten by rats and white ants.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you appoint what the Sa<i>m</i>gha chooses, a Vihâra, or an A<i>d</i><i>dh</i>ayoga <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">2</font></a>, or a storied building, or an attic, or a cave, to be the store-room <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">3</font></a> (of the Sa<i>m</i>gha).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 202</font></a></p>  
 <p>'And you ought, O Bhikkhus, to appoint it in this way: Let a learned, competent Bhikkhu proclaim the following <i>ñ</i>atti before the Sa<i>m</i>gha: "Let the Sa<i>m</i>gha, reverend Sirs, hear me. If the Sa<i>m</i>gha is ready, let the Sa<i>m</i>gha appoint the Vihâra called N. N, to be the store-room (of the Sa<i>m</i>gha), (&amp;c., the usual formula of a <i>ñ</i>aattidutiya kamma)."'</p>
