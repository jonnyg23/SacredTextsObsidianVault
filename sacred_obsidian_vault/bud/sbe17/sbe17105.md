   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu be litigious, contentious, quarrelsome, disputatious, and constantly raise questions before the Sa<i>m</i>gha, And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This Bhikkhu, friends, is indeed litigious, contentious, &amp;c.; well, let us proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma <a name="fr_538"></a><a href="#fn_529"><font size="1">1</font></a>." And they proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma unlawfully <a name="fr_539"></a><a href="#fn_530"><font size="1">2</font></a> with an incomplete congregation <a name="fr_540"></a><a href="#fn_531"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and he then goes from that district to another district. There the Bhikkhus say among each other: "Against this Bhikkhu, friends, the Sa<i>m</i>gha has proceeded with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma unlawfully with an incomplete congregation; well, let us proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma." And they proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma unlawfully with a complete congregation, and he then goes from that district again to- another district. And there the Bhikkhus again say among each other (&amp;c.,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_281"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 281</font></a></p>  
 <p>down to:) and they proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma lawfully with an incomplete congregation . . . . seemingly lawfully <a name="fr_541"></a><a href="#fn_532"><font size="1">1</font></a> with an congregation . . . . seemingly lawfully with a complete congregation <a name="fr_542"></a><a href="#fn_533"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2-5. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu be litigious, &amp;c. <a name="fr_543"></a><a href="#fn_534"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu be ignorant, unlearned, a constant offender, unable to discern what is an offence <a name="fr_544"></a><a href="#fn_535"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and lives in lay society, unduly associating himself with lay people. And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This Bhikkhu, friends, is indeed ignorant, unlearned, &amp;c.; well, let us proceed against him with the nissayakamma <a name="fr_545"></a><a href="#fn_536"><font size="1">5</font></a>," and they proceed against him with the nissayakamma unlawfully with an incomplete congregation, &amp;c. <a name="fr_546"></a><a href="#fn_537"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu leads a life hurtful to the laity, and devoted to evil <a name="fr_547"></a><a href="#fn_538"><font size="1">7</font></a>. And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This</p>

 </p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_282"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 282</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Bhikkhu, friends, leads a life hurtful to the laity, and devoted to evil; well, let us proceed against him with the pabbâ<i>g</i>aniyakamma <a name="fr_548"></a><a href="#fn_539"><font size="1">1</font></a>," &amp;c. <a name="fr_549"></a><a href="#fn_540"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu abuses and reviles lay people. And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This Bhikkhu, friends, abuses and reviles lay people; well, let us proceed against him with the pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iyakamma <a name="fr_550"></a><a href="#fn_541"><font size="1">3</font></a>," &amp;c.2</p>  
 <p>9-11. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu, having committed an offence, refuses to see that offence (committed by himself) <a name="fr_551"></a><a href="#fn_542"><font size="1">4</font></a>. And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This Bhikkhu, friends, has committed an offence and refuses to see that offence; well, let us pronounce expulsion against him for his refusal to see that offence <a name="fr_552"></a><a href="#fn_543"><font size="1">5</font></a>," &amp;c. <a name="fr_553"></a><a href="#fn_540"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>12-13. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu, against whom the Sa<i>m</i>gha has proceeded with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma, behaves himself properly, lives modestly, aspires to get clear of his penance, and asks for the revocation of the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma sentence. And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This Bhikkhu, friends, against whom the Sa<i>m</i>gha has proceeded with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma, in truth behaves himself properly; he lives modestly, &amp;c.; well, let us revoke the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma sentence pronounced against him." And they revoke the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma sentence</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_283"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 283</font></a></p>  
 <p>pronounced against him unlawfully with an incomplete congregation. And he then goes from that district to another district. There the Bhikkhus say among each other: "The ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma sentence, friends, pronounced against this Bhikkhu has been revoked by the Sa<i>m</i>gha unlawfully with an incomplete congregation," &amp;c. <a name="fr_554"></a><a href="#fn_544"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu against whom the Sa<i>m</i>gha has proceeded with the nissayakamma . . . . with the pabbâ<i>g</i>aniyakamma . . . . with the pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iyakamma . . . . against whom the Sa<i>m</i>gha has pronounced expulsion for his refusal to see an offence . . . . for his refusal to atone for an offence . . . . for his refusal to renounce a false doctrine, behaves himself properly, &amp;c. <a name="fr_555"></a><a href="#fn_545"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu be litigious, contentious, quarrelsome, disputatious, and constantly raise questions before the Sa<i>m</i>gha. And the other Bhikkhus say among each other: "This Bhikkhu, friends, is indeed litigious, contentious, &amp;c.; well, let us proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma." And they proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma, unlawfully with an incomplete congregation. Now among the Sa<i>m</i>gha residing in that district a contention is raised whether this is an act performed unlawfully with an incomplete congregation, or an act performed unlawfully with a complete congregation, or an act performed lawfully with an incomplete</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_284"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 284</font></a></p>  
 <p>congregation, or an act performed seemingly law-fully with an incomplete congregation, or an act performed seemingly lawfully with a complete congregation, or an act not performed, badly performed, to be performed again. In this case, O Bhikkhus, the Bhikkhus who say: "It is an act performed unlawfully with an incomplete congregation"--and the Bhikkhus who say: "It is an act not performed, badly performed, to be performed again"--these Bhikkhus are right herein.</p>  
 <p>16. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu be litigious (&amp;c., as in § 15, down to:) and they proceed against him with the ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniyakamma unlawfully with a complete congregation . . . . lawfully with an incomplete congregation . . . . seemingly lawfully with an incomplete congregation . . . . seemingly lawfully with a complete congregation. Now among the Sa<i>m</i>gha residing in that district (&amp;c., as in § 15).</p>  
 <p>17-20. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu be ignorant, unlearned,' &amp;c. <a name="fr_556"></a><a href="#fn_546"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
