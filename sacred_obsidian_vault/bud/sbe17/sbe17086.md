   <h3 align="center">19.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time Ro<i>g</i>a the Malla was a friend of the venerable Ânanda's <a name="fr_421"></a><a href="#fn_413"><font size="1">1</font></a>. And a linen cloth belonging to Ro<i>g</i>a the Malla had been deposited in the keeping of the venerable Ânanda; and the venerable Ânanda had need of a linen cloth.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to take a thing on trust (that it would be given to you) when it belongs to a person possessed of these five qualifications--he must be an intimate and familiar friend who has been spoken to (about it) <a name="fr_422"></a><a href="#fn_414"><font size="1">2</font></a> and is alive, (and the Bhikkhu taking the thing) must know "He will remain pleased with me after I have taken it." I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to take a thing on trust (that it would be given to you) <a name="fr_423"></a><a href="#fn_415"><font size="1">3</font></a> when it belongs to a person possessed of these five qualifications.'</p>
