   <h3 align="center">24.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu committed two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences which he concealed for two months. And it occurred to him, Lo! I have committed, &amp;c. . . . for two months. Let me now ask the Sa<i>m</i>gha for a probation of one month for &amp;c. . . .' He asked the Sa<i>m</i>gha for a probation of one month for &amp;c. . . . The Sa<i>m</i>gha imposed upon him a probation of one month for &amp;c. . . . Whilst he was undergoing that probation, shame (for the way</p>  
 <p><a name="page_415"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 415</font></a></p>  
 <p>in which he had acted) overcame him. 'Let me now ask the Sa<i>m</i>gha for a (further) probation of one month for the two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences concealed for two months.'</p>  
 <p>2. He told the Bhikkhus [all that had happened, in the words of § 1, and asked them], 'What now shall I do?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.,</p>  
 <p>3. 'Let then the Sa<i>m</i>gha, O Bhikkhus, impose upon that Bhikkhu a probation for a further month for those two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences concealed for two months.'</p>  
 <p>[Here follows the kammavâ<i>k</i>â in the form given in chapter 1, §§ 2, 3.]</p>  
 <p>'Thus that Bhikkhu, O Bhikkhus, should undergo probation for two months from that date <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>
