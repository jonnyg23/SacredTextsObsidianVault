   <h3 align="center">18.</h3>  
 <p>1. When he had accomplished that Mânatta, and while he was worthy to be rehabilitated, he committed an offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa--which for five days he concealed.</p>  
 <p>He told, &amp;c. [all that happened, from chapter 13 downwards].</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>Let then the Sa<i>m</i>gha, O Bhikkhus, for that when he had (&amp;c., as in the first paragraph) throw Udâyi the Bhikkhu back to the commencement (of the probationary term he had already undergone) and impose upon him an inclusive probation for this and for the first offence, and a Mânatta of six days' duration.</p>  
 <p>'Now thus, &amp;c. . . . [Here follow three kammavâ<i>k</i>âs as in chapter 17.]'</p>
