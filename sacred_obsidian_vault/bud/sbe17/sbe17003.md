   <h3 align="center">2.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus were in the habit of wearing slippers all of a blue, yellow, red, brown, black, orange, or yellowish colour <a name="fr_21"></a><a href="#fn_21"><font size="1">2</font></a>. People were annoyed, murmured, and became angry, saying, '(These act) like those who still enjoy the pleasures of the world <a name="fr_22"></a><a href="#fn_22"><font size="1">3</font></a>.' The brethren told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Do not wear, O Bhikkhus, shoes that are all of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>  
 <p>a blue, yellow, red, brown, black, orange, or yellowish colour. Whosoever does so, is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus were in the habit of wearing shoes with edges of a blue, yellow, red, brown, black, orange, or yellowish colour.</p>  
 <p>People were annoyed, murmured, and became angry, saying, 'These act like those who still enjoy the pleasures of the world.' The brethren told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Do not wear, O Bhikkhus, shoes that have edges of a blue, yellow, red, brown, black, orange, or yellowish colour. Whosoever does so, is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>  
 <p>3. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus were in the habit of wearing shoes with heel-coverings (? <a name="fr_23"></a><a href="#fn_23"><font size="1">1</font></a>); mocassins <a name="fr_24"></a><a href="#fn_24"><font size="1">2</font></a>; laced boots <a name="fr_25"></a><a href="#fn_25"><font size="1">3</font></a>; boots lined with cotton <a name="fr_26"></a><a href="#fn_26"><font size="1">4</font></a>; boots of various hues, like the wings of partridges <a name="fr_27"></a><a href="#fn_27"><font size="1">5</font></a>; boots pointed with rams' horns, and with goats' horns <a name="fr_28"></a><a href="#fn_28"><font size="1">6</font></a>; ornamented with scorpions'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 16</font></a></p>  
 <p>tails <a name="fr_29"></a><a href="#fn_29"><font size="1">1</font></a>; sewn round with peacocks' feathers <a name="fr_30"></a><a href="#fn_30"><font size="1">2</font></a>; or shoes of all kinds of colours <a name="fr_31"></a><a href="#fn_31"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>People were annoyed (&amp;c., as in § 2, down to:) told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Do not wear, O Bhikkhus, shoes with heel-coverings (&amp;c., as in § 3, down to:) shoes of all kinds of colours. Whosoever does so, is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>  
 <p>4. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus were in the habit of wearing shoes adorned with lion-skins <a name="fr_32"></a><a href="#fn_32"><font size="1">4</font></a>, tiger-skins, panther-skins, antelope-skins, otter-skins <a name="fr_33"></a><a href="#fn_33"><font size="1">5</font></a>, cat-skins, squirrel-skins, and owl-skins <a name="fr_34"></a><a href="#fn_34"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>People were annoyed (&amp;c., as in § 3, down to the end, substituting 'shoes adorned with lion-skins, &amp;c.,' for 'shoes with heel-coverings, &amp;c.')</p>
