   <h3 align="center">3.</h3>  
 <p>1. At that time the fraternity got robes of different kinds. Now the Bhikkhus thought: 'What robes are allowed to us by the Blessed One, and what robes are not allowed?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, six kinds of robes, viz.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>those made of linen, of cotton, of silk, of wool, of coarse cloth, and of hempen cloth.'</p>  
 <p>2. At that time the Bhikkhus accepted lay robes, but did not get pa<i>m</i>sukûla robes, because they had scruples (and thought): 'The Blessed One has allowed us either kind of robes only, not both kinds <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_349"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, that he who accepts lay robes, may get also pa<i>m</i>sukûla robes. If you are pleased with those both sorts of robes, I approve that also.'</p>
