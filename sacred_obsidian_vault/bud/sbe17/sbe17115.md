   <h3 align="center">4.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'There are three things, O Bhikkhus, which, when they characterise a Bhikkhu, the Sa<i>m</i>gha, if it likes, should carry out the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma against him; (that is to say), when he is a maker of strife, quarrelsome, a maker of disputes, given to idle talk, and a raiser of legal questions in the Sa<i>m</i>gha <a name="fr_602"></a><a href="#fn_591"><font size="1">1</font></a>--when he is dull, stupid, full of faults, and devoid of merit--when he is living in lay society, in unlawful</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_336"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 336</font></a></p>  
 <p>association with the world. There are three things, O Bhikkhus, which, when the Sa<i>m</i>gha suspects (&amp;c., as before, down to) against him.</p>  
 <p>There are other three things, O Bhikkhus, (&amp;c., as before, down to) against him; (that is to say), when he has failed in morality as regards moral things--when he has failed in conduct as regards transgression--when he has failed in opinion as regards the principal matters of opinion <a name="fr_603"></a><a href="#fn_592"><font size="1">1</font></a>. There are three things, O Bhikkhus, (&amp;c., as before, down to) against him.</p>  
 <p>There are other three things, O Bhikkhus, (&amp;c., as before, down to) against him; (that is to say), when he speaks in dispraise of the Buddha--when he speaks in dispraise of the Dhamma--when he speaks in dispraise of the Sa<i>m</i>gha. These are three things, O Bhikkhus, (&amp;c., as before, down to) against him.</p>  
 <p>2. 'There are three kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus, against whom, if the Sa<i>m</i>gha likes, it should carry out the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma; (that is to say), one who is a maker of strife (&amp;c., as in § 1, down to) a raiser of legal questions in the Sa<i>m</i>gha--one who is dull, stupid, full of faults, and devoid of merit--and one who is living in lay society, in unlawful association with the world. These are three kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus, (&amp;c., as before, down to) the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma.</p>  
 <p>'There are other three kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus, against whom, if the Sa<i>m</i>gha likes, it should carry out the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma; (that is to say), one who has failed in morality in regard to moral matters--one who has failed in conduct</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 337</font></a></p>  
 <p>as regards transgression--one who has failed in opinion as regards the principal matters of opinion. These are three kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus (&amp;c., as before, down to) the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma.</p>  
 <p>'There are other three kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus, against whom, if the Sa<i>m</i>gha likes, it should carry out the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma; (that is to say), one who speaks in dispraise of the Buddha--one who speaks in dispraise of the Dhamma--one who speaks in dispraise of the Sa<i>m</i>gha. These are three kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus (&amp;c., as before, down to) the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma.'</p>  
 <p align="center">__________________</p>  
 <p align="center">Here end the six permissive cases of Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma.</p>
