 <p><a name="page_229"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 229</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">20.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus were fully provided with the three robes, but they had need of water-strainers <a name="fr_424"></a><a href="#fn_416"><font size="1">1</font></a> and of bags (to carry their bowls and other things in) <a name="fr_425"></a><a href="#fn_417"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, pieces of cloth requisite (for those purposes).'</p>  
 <p>2. Then it occurred to the Bhikkhus: 'The things allowed by the Blessed One--the three robes, and the robes for the rainy season, and the mat, and the bed-covering, and the cloth to cover boils &amp;c. with, and to wipe the face with, and required (for water-strainers and bags)--are all these things things which ought to be kept to ourselves <a name="fr_426"></a><a href="#fn_418"><font size="1">3</font></a>, or things which ought to be handed over <a name="fr_427"></a><a href="#fn_419"><font size="1">4</font></a> (from time to time by one Bhikkhu to another)?'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to keep in hand the three robes, and not to assign them--to keep to yourselves the robes for the rainy season during the four months of the rains, but beyond that time to hand them over--to keep to yourselves the mats and the bed coverings, and not to hand them over--to keep to yourselves the coverings for the itch &amp;c. while the disease lasts, but beyond that time to</p>
