 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">11.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had disease of the eyes. They used to carry that Bhikkhu out to ease himself. The Blessed One as he was passing through the Bhikkhus' sleeping quarters saw them [doing so]. When he saw them, he went up to the place where they were, and asked those Bhikkhus:</p>  
 <p>'What is the disease, O Bhikkhus, from which this Bhikkhu suffers?'</p>  
 <p>2. 'This venerable one, Lord, has disease of the eyes. Therefore do we carry him out to ease himself.'</p>  
 <p>Then the Blessed One, on that account, having delivered a religious discourse, addressed the Bhikkhus, and said:</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of eye ointments <a name="fr_119"></a><a href="#fn_117"><font size="1">1</font></a>; to wit, black collyrium <a name="fr_120"></a><a href="#fn_118"><font size="1">2</font></a>, rasa ointment <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_119"><font size="1">3</font></a>, sota ointment <a name="fr_122"></a><a href="#fn_120"><font size="1">4</font></a>, geruka <a name="fr_123"></a><a href="#fn_121"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and kapalla <a name="fr_124"></a><a href="#fn_122"><font size="1">6</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>They had need of perfumes to grind up into ointments.</p>
