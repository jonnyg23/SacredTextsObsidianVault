   <h3 align="center">20 <a name="fr_669"></a><a href="#fn_655"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'There are five things, O Bhikkhus, which when they characterise a Bhikkhu, the Sa<i>m</i>gha, if it likes, should carry out the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kamma against him; (that is to say), when he goes about to bring</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_366"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 366</font></a></p>  
 <p>loss on the laity--when he goes about to do harm to the laity--when he goes about to deprive the laity of their dwellings--when he reviles and finds fault with the laity--when he brings about division between the laity. These are the five things, O Bhikkhus, which (&amp;c., as above, down to) against him.</p>  
 <p>'There are other five things, O Bhikkhus, which when they characterise a Bhikkhu, the Sa<i>m</i>gha, if it likes, should carry out the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kamma against him; (that is to say), when he speaks to the laity in dispraise of the Buddha--when he speaks to the laity in dispraise of the Dhamma--when he speaks to the laity in dispraise of the Sa<i>m</i>gha--when he puts laymen down, and lowers them by censure--when he does not fulfil a promise made in accordance with the Rules to the laity. These are the other five things, O Bhikkhus, which (&amp;c., as before, down to) against him.</p>  
 <p>'There are five kinds of Bhikkhus, O Bhikkhus, against whom the Sa<i>m</i>gha, if it likes, should carry out the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kamma; (that is to say), one who goes about to bring loss on the laity (&amp;c., as in the last paragraph, down to the end).'</p>  
 <p align="center">__________________</p>  
 <p align="center">Here end the four times five cases of suspicion.</p>
