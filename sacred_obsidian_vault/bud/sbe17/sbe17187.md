 <p><a name="page_432"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 432</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">34.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, and as touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence they are of opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">1</font></a>. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. He who has concealed it should be compelled to confess himself guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence, and a probation corresponding to the period during which he has concealed it having been imposed upon him, a Mânatta should be imposed upon them both.</p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, and as touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence they are in doubt. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. [The penalty is the same.] <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, and as touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence they are of opinion that it is a mixed offence <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">3</font></a>. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. [The penalty is the same.] <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a mixed offence, and as touching that mixed offence they are of opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence. One of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_433"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 433</font></a></p>  
 <p>them conceals, the other does not conceal it. [The penalty is the same.] <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a mixed offence, and as touching that mixed offence they are of opinion that it is a mixed offence. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. [The penalty is the same.]</p>  
 <p>Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a minor offence <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">2</font></a>; and as regarding that minor offence they are of opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. He who has concealed it should be compelled to confess himself guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence, and both of them should be dealt with according to law.</p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a minor offence, and as touching that minor offence they are of opinion that it is a minor offence. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. He who has concealed it should be compelled to confess himself guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence, and both of them should be dealt with according to law.</p>  
 <p>2. 'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, and as touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence they are of opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa. One of them thinks: "I will tell (the Sa<i>m</i>gha of it)." The other thinks: "I will not tell (the Sa<i>m</i>gha of it);" and during the first watch of the night he conceals it, and during the second watch of the night he conceals</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_434"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 434</font></a></p>  
 <p>it, and during the third watch of the night he conceals it. After the sun has arisen the offence is a concealed one. He who has concealed it [&amp;c.; the penalty is the same as in section 1, paragraph 1].</p>  
 <p>Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, and as touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence they are of opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence. They set out, intending to tell (the Sa<i>m</i>gha of it). On the way there springs up in one of them a desire to conceal it; and during the first watch of the night he conceals it, and during the second watch of the night he conceals it, and during the third watch of the night he conceals it. After the sun has arisen the offence is a concealed one. He who has concealed it [&amp;c.; the penalty is the same as before].</p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, and as touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence they are of opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence. They go out of their mind; and afterwards when they have recovered their senses one of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. He who has concealed it [&amp;c.; the penalty is the same as before] <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'Two Bhikkhus have been guilty of a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence. When the Pâtimokkha is being recited they say thus: "Now do we come to perceive it; for this rule they say has been handed down in the Suttas, is contained in the Suttas, and comes into recitation every half month." As touching that Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence, they (thus) come to be of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_435"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 435</font></a></p>  
 <p>opinion that it is a Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence. One of them conceals, the other does not conceal it. He who has concealed it [&amp;c.; the penalty is the same as before].'</p>
