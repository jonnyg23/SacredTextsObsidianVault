 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">19.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a family who were devoted to the venerable Upananda, of the Sakya clan, sent hard food for the Sa<i>m</i>gha, saying, 'This is to be given to the Sa<i>m</i>gha with especial reference to the venerable Upananda.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the venerable Upananda of the Sakya clan had gone forth to the village for alms. And those men went to the Ârâma, and asked the Bhikkhus:</p>  
 <p>'Where, Sirs, is the venerable Upananda?'</p>  
 <p>'The venerable Upananda of the Sakya clan has gone forth to the village for alms.'</p>  
 <p>'This hard food, Sirs, is to be given to the Sa<i>m</i>gha, with especial reference to the venerable Upananda.'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'In that case, O Bhikkhus, receive it, a id put it aside till Upananda returns.'</p>  
 <p>2. And the venerable Upananda, after having attended on the families in the forenoon, returned during the (after-part of the) day.</p>  
 <p>Now at that time, during the scarcity, people invited the Bhikkhus to a slender meal; and they,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 76</font></a></p>  
 <p>counting the numbers (of those invited, refused) the invitation. And the whole Sa<i>m</i>gha was (once) invited; but the Bhikkhus, fearing to offend, did not accept the invitation.</p>  
 <p>'Accept it, O Bhikkhus, that a Bhikkhu who has eaten, and who has refused food still offered, may nevertheless eat food, if it have been received before meal-time (in the forenoon), even if it has not been left over <a name="fr_176"></a><a href="#fn_174"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>
