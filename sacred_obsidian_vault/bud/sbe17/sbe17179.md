   <h3 align="center">25.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu commit two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences which he conceals for two months. And it occurs to him (&amp;c., as in last chapter). . . . And he asks . . . . And the Sa<i>m</i>gha imposes . . . . And whilst he is undergoing that probation, shame (&amp;c.) overcomes him, &amp;c. . . . And he asks the Sa<i>m</i>gha for a probation of a further month for those two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences concealed for two months. And the Sa<i>m</i>gha imposes upon him a further probation, &amp;c. . . . Then, O Bhikkhus, that Bhikkhu should from that date undergo that further</p>  
 <p><a name="page_416"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 416</font></a></p>  
 <p>probation of one month for those two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences concealed for two months <a name="fr_743"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2, 3. 'Now in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu have committed two Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences, which he conceals for two months. And of one month he is aware, but of the other month he is not aware . . . . one month he recollects, but the other month he does not recollect . . . . one month he wittingly conceals, but the other month he unwittingly conceals.'</p>  
 <p>[This chapter is word for word the same as chapter 23, reading 'month' for 'offence.']</p>
