   <h3 align="center">14.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the venerable Pilindava<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a was troubled with wind in the stomach <a name="fr_138"></a><a href="#fn_136"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The physicians said he must drink oil.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, a decoction of oil.'</p>  
 <p>It was necessary to put strong drink into the decoction.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to put strong drink in decoctions of oil.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus used to put too much strong drink into their decoctions of medicinal oils: and they got drunk.</p>  
 <p>'Oil should not be drunk, O Bhikkhus, when too much strong drink has been put into it. Whosoever does so, shall be dealt with according to law <a name="fr_139"></a><a href="#fn_137"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to drink such decoctions</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>  
 <p>of oil with strong drink in them, as wherein neither the colour, nor the smell, nor the taste of the strong drink shall be sensible.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time the Bhikkhus had a quantity of decoction in which too much strong drink had been put. Then those Bhikkhus thought: 'What shall we do with this oil, which has too much strong drink in it?'</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, to use it as an ointment.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the venerable Pilindava<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a had a quantity of oil-decoction; but he had no vessel for it.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of three kinds of pots, bronze pots, wooden pots, pots made of the shells of fruits <a name="fr_140"></a><a href="#fn_138"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>3. Now at that time the venerable Pilindava<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a had rheumatism.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to bring on sweating <a name="fr_141"></a><a href="#fn_139"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>(The disease) became no better.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to bring on sweating by the use of herbs which have that effect <a name="fr_142"></a><a href="#fn_140"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>(The disease) became no better.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of a great steam bath <a name="fr_143"></a><a href="#fn_141"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>(The disease) became no better.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of hemp-water (bang) <a name="fr_144"></a><a href="#fn_142"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>(The disease) became no better.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of hot baths in water in which medicinal herbs have been steeped <a name="fr_145"></a><a href="#fn_143"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. Now at that time the venerable Pilindava<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a had intermittent ague.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the letting of blood.'</p>  
 <p>(The disease) became no better.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to make use of a horn to let blood <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_144"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the feet of the venerable Pilindava<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a were blistered.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of ointment for the feet.'</p>  
 <p>(The disease) became no better.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to keep water ready for washing the feet (of travellers) <a name="fr_147"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had boils.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of the lancet.'</p>  
 <p>Decoctions of astringent herbs were required.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, decoctions of astringent herbs <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Sesamum salve was required.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of sesamum salve <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>5. Compresses were required.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of compresses <a name="fr_150"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>It was necessary to tie up the sore with cloth.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of bandages for tieing up wounds.'</p>  
 <p>The sore itched.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the sprinkling of a sore with mustard-powder <a name="fr_151"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>The sore became moist <a name="fr_152"></a><a href="#fn_150"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to fumigate (the sore).'</p>  
 <p>Proud flesh formed on the wound <a name="fr_153"></a><a href="#fn_151"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to cut off (proud flesh) with a lancet.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a></p>  
 <p>The wound would not close up.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of oil for wounds.'</p>  
 <p>The oil ran over.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of fine rags <a name="fr_154"></a><a href="#fn_152"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and of all kinds of ways of treating wounds.'</p>  
 <p>6. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu was bitten by a snake.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the four kinds of filth to be given--dung, urine, ashes, and clay.'</p>  
 <p>Now the Bhikkhus thought: 'Are these things among those which may be taken even without being offered to us by others, or among things which cannot be taken unless they are offered?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow these things, O Bhikkhus, to be accepted if any one be there to offer them <a name="fr_155"></a><a href="#fn_153"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and if not, then that you may take them yourselves and use them.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had drunk poison.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give (as an emetic) a decoction of dung.'</p>  
 <p>Then the Bhikkhus thought: 'Is this among those things which may be taken even without being offered to us by others, or is it among things which cannot be taken unless they are offered?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I rule, O Bhikkhus, that there is a proper taking when a man takes what he himself has made; and</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a></p>  
 <p>that such a thing need not be received again from others.'</p>  
 <p>7. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu suffered from the ghara-dinnaka <a name="fr_156"></a><a href="#fn_154"><font size="1">1</font></a> disease.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give him to drink a decoction of soil turned up by the plough <a name="fr_157"></a><a href="#fn_155"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'&lt;</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had constipation <a name="fr_158"></a><a href="#fn_156"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give him a decoction of the ashes of burnt rice <a name="fr_159"></a><a href="#fn_157"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had the jaundice.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give to drink a decoction made with (cows') urine <a name="fr_160"></a><a href="#fn_158"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had skin disease.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the anointing with perfumes.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had a superfluity of humors in his body <a name="fr_161"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">6</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give a purgative.'</p>  
 <p>Clarified gruel was required.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 61</font></a></p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of clarified gruel.'</p>  
 <p>Natural juice was required <a name="fr_162"></a><a href="#fn_160"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of natural juice.'</p>  
 <p>Artificial and natural juice was required <a name="fr_163"></a><a href="#fn_161"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of artificial and natural juice.'</p>  
 <p>Meat broth was required <a name="fr_164"></a><a href="#fn_162"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of meat broth.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_141"></a><a href="sbe17028.htm#fr_143">56:4</a> Mahâsedan ti mahanta<i>m</i> seda<i>m</i>: porisa-ppamâ<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> âvâ<i>t</i>am aṅgârâna<i>m</i> pûretvâ, pa<i>m</i>su-vâlikâdîhi pidahitvâ, tattha nânâvidhâni vâta-hara<i>n</i>a-pa<i>n</i><i>n</i>âni santharitvâ tela-makkhitena gattena tattha nipa<i>g</i><i>g</i>itvâ samparivattantena sarîra<i>m</i> sedetu<i>m</i> anu<i>g</i>ânâmîti attho (B.). A pit, six feet deep, is filled with charcoal, and covered with a coating of earth or sand. The leaves good for rheumatism are spread <a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a> over the sand. The patient reposes on the leaves on the affected limb, which has been rubbed over with oil; and turns over and over until his whole body has been well steamed.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_142"></a><a href="sbe17028.htm#fr_144">57:1</a> Bhaṅgodakan ti nânâ-pa<i>n</i><i>n</i>a-bhaṅga-kudhita-udaka<i>m</i>. Tehi pa<i>n</i><i>n</i>ehi <i>k</i>a udakena <i>k</i>a si<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>itvâ sedetabbo (B.). Bhaṅga may here mean 'broken bits,' namely, of the leaves, just as sâkhâ-bhaṅga at <i>G</i>âtaka I, 158 means 'twig.' Compare uttari-bhaṅga, <i>G</i>âtaka I, 197, 349; Dhammapada 171; <i>K</i>ullavagga VIII, 4, 4; sarîra-bhaṅga, Mahâ-parinibbâna Sutta VI, 59; and bhaṅga alone at <i>G</i>âtaka I, 392; Mahâvagga I, 25, 10; Rh. D., 'Buddhist Suttas from the Pâli,' p. 241. For kudhita we should read kuthita.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_143"></a><a href="sbe17028.htm#fr_145">57:2</a> Dakako<i>t</i><i>th</i>akan ti udaka-ko<i>t</i><i>th</i>a<i>m</i> kâ<i>t</i>i<i>m</i> vâ do<i>n</i>i<i>m</i> vâ u<i>n</i>hodakassa pûretvâ tattha tattha pavisitvâ seda-kamma-karana<i>m</i> anu<i>g</i>ânâmîti attho (B.). Compare Dhammapada, p.103.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_144"></a><a href="sbe17028.htm#fr_146">57:3</a> Wise, p.176, says, 'The local accumulation of bad blood may be removed by means of cupping, which is performed by a horn, cut smooth and even at the large extremity, and with a small opening at the narrow end.' Compare Su<i>s</i>ruta, Sûtrasthâna, chap. 27, and <i>S</i>arîrasthâna, chap. 8 (at the end).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_145"></a><a href="sbe17028.htm#fr_147">58:1</a> This would seem to be a preventive remedy.  <p><a name="fn_146"></a><a href="sbe17028.htm#fr_148">58:2</a> Compare chapter 4.</p>
