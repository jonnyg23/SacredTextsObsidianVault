 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">11.</h3>  
 <p>1. At that time the Bhikkhus spread the cloth on the floor (when they had dyed it); the cloth became dusty.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you spread grass (and put the cloth on it).'</p>  
 <p>The grass they had spread was eaten by white ants. They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you get a bambû peg or rope to hang the cloth on.'</p>  
 <p>They hung it up in the middle; the dye dropped down on both sides.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you tie it fast at the corner.'</p>  
 <p>The corner wore out.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, the use of a clothes-line.'</p>  
 <p>The dye dropped down on one side.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you turn the cloth, when dying it, whenever required, and that you do not go away before the dye has ceased to drop.'</p>  
 <p>2. At that time the cloth had become stiff <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">1</font></a>. They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, that you clip (the cloth) into water (in order to remove the excessive dye).' At that time the cloth became rough.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, (that you smooth it by) beating it with your hands.'</p>  
 <p>At that time the Bhikkhus possessed a<i>k</i><i>kh</i>innaka <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">1</font></a> robes of yellowish colour like ivory. The people were annoyed, murmured, and became angry: '(The Bhikkhus dress) like those who still live in the pleasures of the world.'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You ought not, O Bhikkhus, to possess a<i>k</i><i>kh</i>innaka robes. He who does, commits a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>
