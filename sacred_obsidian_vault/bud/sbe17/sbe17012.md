 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 31</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">11.</h3>  
 <p>Now at that time men had couches and chairs covered or bound with skins. The Bhikkhus, fearing to offend, would not sit down upon them.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to sit down on seats arranged by laymen, but not to lie down upon them.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Vihâras were bound together by thongs made of skin <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The Bhikkhus fearing to offend, would not sit down upon them.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to sit down upon (skins when they are) only used for binding things together.'</p>
