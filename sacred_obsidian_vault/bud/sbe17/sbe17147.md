   <h3 align="center">2.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the venerable Upâli went up to the place where the Blessed One was; and on arriving there, he saluted the Blessed One, and took his seat on one side. And when he was so seated the venerable Upâli said to the Blessed One: 'Now in what case, Lord, can there be an interruption of the probationary period of a Bhikkhu who has been placed on probation <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_698"><font size="1">2</font></a>?'</p>  
 <p>There are three ways of interruption of the probationary period, O Upâli, of a Bhikkhu who has been placed on probation; (that is to say), by</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_392"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 392</font></a></p>  
 <p>dwelling together <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_699"><font size="1">1</font></a>, by dwelling alone <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and by not announcing <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'These are the three ways of interruption to the probationary period, O Upâli, of a Bhikkhu who has been placed under probation.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_698"></a><a href="sbe17147.htm#fr_716">391:2</a> Literally, 'breaking of the nights.' The time of probation was reckoned, not by days, but by nights; and in either of the three cases which follow the reckoning was interrupted, and had to begin afresh.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_699"></a><a href="sbe17147.htm#fr_717">392:1</a> Buddhaghosa explains this to mean when the probationer has done any of the things forbidden in II, 1, 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_700"></a><a href="sbe17147.htm#fr_718">392:2</a> That is, when the probationer has done any of the things forbidden in II, 1, 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_701"></a><a href="sbe17147.htm#fr_719">392:3</a> That is, when the probationer has omitted to make any of the announcements prescribed at the end of II, 1, 2.</p>
