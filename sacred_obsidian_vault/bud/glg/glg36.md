 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Gateless Gate</i>, by Ekai, called Mu-mon, tr. Nyogen Senzaki and Paul Reps [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">36. Meeting a Zen Master on the Road</h3>  
 <p>Goso said: "When you meet a Zen master on the road you cannot talk to him, you cannot face him with silence. What are you going to do?"</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p><i>Mumon's comment:</i> In such a case, if you can answer him intimately, your realization will be  beautiful, but if you cannot, you should look about without seeing anything.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px"><i>Meeting a Zen master on the road,<br> Face him neither with words nor silence.<br> Give him an uppercut<br> And you will be called one who understands Zen</i>.</div>
