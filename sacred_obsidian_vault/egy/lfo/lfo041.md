 <h3 align="CENTER">THE FORTY-SECOND CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>The next line of the Liturgy is difficult to translate, for there are variants in the text. In the Vignette in the tomb of Peta-Amen-apt we see a man kneeling, with his left hand raised; it is clear that he is not making an offering, but it is probable that he is "seated" near</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_10400"></a>
 <img src="img/10400.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="1">A ministrant kneeling by the side of the offering</font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p> </p>
 <p>the offerings, with the view of partaking of them. The meaning of the text seems to be something like:--</p>
 <p>"He who sitteth down by the <i>suten pert kheru</i> (or royal offering) shall say: I have seated myself with it."</p>
