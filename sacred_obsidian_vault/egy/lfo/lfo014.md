 <h3 align="CENTER">THE NINTH CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>The first object presented to the mummy after the opening of the mouth was <i>sel</i>, or <i>ser</i>, <a name="img_06700"></a>
 <img src="img/06700.jpg"> which has been translated both by "butter" and "cheese." The Vignette represents the SEM priest offering a vessel with four balls, or round cakes, of some substance in it,</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_06701"></a>
 <img src="img/06701.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="1">The Sem priest presenting cheese</font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p> </p>
 <p>and, when we remember that the Egyptians have never made butter in our sense of the word, we are justified in accepting Dümichen's rendering of "cheese." <a name="fr_55"></a><a href="#fn_55"><font size="1">1</font></a> Whilst the four cakes of cheese were being offered, the Kher heb said the following words:--</p>
 <p>"O Unas, the Eye of Horus hath been presented</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a></p>
 <p>unto thee, and with it the god passeth (or, cometh); I have brought it unto thee: place thou it in thy mouth."</p>
