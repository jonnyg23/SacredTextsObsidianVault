 <h3 align="CENTER">THE EIGHTH CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>The SEM priest next presented before the face of the mummy two objects of the shape <a name="img_06501"></a>
 <img src="img/06501.jpg">, or <a name="img_06502"></a>
 <img src="img/06502.jpg"> made</p>
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>
 <p>of iron of the South and iron of the North respectively, and the Kher heb said twice:--</p>
 <p>"O Unas, the two gods have opened for thee thy mouth."</p>
 <p>The "two gods" are, of course, Horus and Set. It will be noted that in the text of Unas the two iron objects which represent Horus and Set are in the form of axe-heads attached to handles, <a name="img_06600"></a>
 <img src="img/06600.jpg"> and that in the</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_06601"></a>
 <img src="img/06601.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="1">The Sem priest presenting <a name="img_06600"></a>
 <img src="img/06600.jpg"></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p> </p>
 <p>text of Peta-Amen-apt they are in the form <a name="img_06602"></a>
 <img src="img/06602.jpg">. Amulets in both forms are known, for, as Professor Maspero has pointed out, small axes, with heads of metal and handles of wood, have been found in the tombs. and also small angles of haematite, of which many examples exist in our museums. <a name="fr_54"></a><a href="#fn_54"><font size="1">1</font></a> The application of these objects to the mouth of the mummy was, no</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a></p>
 <p>doubt, intended to supplement the presentation of the KEF PESESH.</p>
