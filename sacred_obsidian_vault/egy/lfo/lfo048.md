 <h3 align="CENTER">THE FORTY-NINTH CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>In the next ceremony there were presented as a <i>Shebu</i> offering a Tua cake and a Shens cake, and the Kher heb said:--</p>
 <p>"Let there be praise to thee and to thy KA, O Osiris,</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_10900"></a>
 <img src="img/10900.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="1">The Sem priest presenting the Tua cake and the Shens cake</font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p> </p>
 <p>which hath been cut away from the hand of him that doeth violence to the dead. O Unas, thou hast received these thy bread-cakes, which are from the Eye of Horus.</p>
 <p>"Osiris Unas, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, [that is to say,] that which hath been mixed together for thee by it, so that thou mayest be filled with that which hath been pressed out and hath</p>
 <p><a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 110</font></a></p>
 <p>come forth from thee." This sentence was recited four times.</p>
 <p>The presentation of the following offerings then took place; each object was offered to the mummy (or,</p>
 <p>statue) four times, and each formula was recited by the Kher heb four times.</p>
