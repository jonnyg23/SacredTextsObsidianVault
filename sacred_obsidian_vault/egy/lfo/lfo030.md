 <h3 align="CENTER">THE TWENTY-SIXTH CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>This was followed by an offering of an iron vessel</p>
 <p><a name="page_86"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 86</font></a></p>
 <p>containing one <i>hent</i> measure of beer, <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_64"><font size="1">1</font></a> and at the same time the Kher heb said:--</p>
 <p>"Unas, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, which was rescued for thee; there is no iron therein, and it belongeth to thee."</p>
 <p>In the "iron vessel" we may probably see an allusion to the iron spear with which Horus defended himself against the attack of Set. If this be so we are to understand from the text that the strength of the iron</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_08400"></a>
 <img src="img/08400.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="2">The Sem priest presenting an iron vessel of beer</font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p> </p>
 <p>weapon is transferred to the vessel, which in turn transfers it to the beer. From the beer the deceased obtains the magical power of Horus which will enable him to repulse any attack made upon him by Set.</p>
