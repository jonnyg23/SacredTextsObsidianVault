 <h1 align="CENTER">THE LITURGY OF FUNERARY OFFERINGS.</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">II. FROM THE TOMB OF PETA-AMEN-AP.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>1. THE CHAMBER WHEREIN THE CEREMONY TUAT SHALL BE PERFORMED HAVING BEEN PROVIDED WITH OFFERINGS, THE ALTAR SHALL BE PURIFIED FOR THE KA (OR THE DOUBLE) OF THE CHIEF KHER HEB OF NEB-HETEPET, THE PRIEST PETA-AMEN-AP.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>The following shall be recited by the priest four times:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"2. O Osiris, that which is hateful hath been seized and carried off for thee. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, that which is spoken in thy name Thoth</p>
 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a></p>
 <p>goeth about with and carrieth it to Osiris. What hath been spoken in</p>
 <p>"3. the name of the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP [of] evil hath been brought, and I have placed it for thee in thy hand."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here water shall be sprinkled, and the following words shall be recited four times:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"the fluid [of life] shall not be destroyed in thee, and thou shalt not be destroyed in it."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>During the recital of the following words incense shall be burned.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"4. Let him that advanceth advance with his KA. Horus advanceth with his KA. Set advanceth with his KA. Thoth advanceth with his KA. Sep advanceth with his KA. Khenti-maati advanceth with his KA. Osiris advanceth with his KA. Let thy</p>
 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>
 <p>"5. Tet advance with thy KA. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP! The arm of thy KA is before thee, the arm of thy KA is behind thee. Hail, chief KHER PETA-AMEN-AP! The foot of thy KA is before thee, the foot of thy Ka</p>
 <p>"6. is behind thee. Hail, chief KHER PETA-AMEN-AP! I have given unto thee the Eye of Horus, thy face is filled therewith, and the perfume of the Eye of Horus is to thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>The priest shall say the following words four times, and a libation, made with one grain of Shet-pet incense and one grain of incense of Nekheb, shall be poured out during their recital.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"7. This libation is for thee, O Osiris, this libation</p>
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>
 <p>is for thee, O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, Coming forth from thy son, coming forth from Horus.</p>
 <p>"8. I have come, and I have brought unto thee the Eye of Horus, that thy heart may be refreshed thereby. I have brought it unto thee under thy feet, and have presented unto thee the efflux which cometh forth therefrom.</p>
 <p>"9. No stoppage of thy heart shall arise therefrom, and there shall be a coming forth to thee through the word which is spoken (or the voice)."</p>
 <p align="CENTER">_____________________</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here pour out water in which a cake of incense of Nekhebet of the South hath been dissolved, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"10. [Cleansing by] <i>semmanu</i> (i.e., water of natron)! [Cleansing by] <i>semmanu</i>! Open thy mouth, O chief</p>
 <p><a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, and taste thou the taste thereof in the divine hall. An emission of Horus</p>
 <p>"11. is the cleansing water of natron, an emission of Set is the cleansing water of natron, and the cleansing water of natron stablisheth the heart of the Two Divine Lords. Thou art cleansed with natron, and thou art a pure being among the divine Followers of Horus."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall be poured out water in which a cake of incense of the North from Shet-pet hath been dissolved.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"12. Thou art purified with natron, and Horus is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and Set is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and Thoth is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and Sep is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and thou art stablished among them."</p>
 <p><a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a></p>
 <p>"13. Thy mouth is the mouth of a sucking calf on the day of his birth."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here take a cake of incense, and lift it up to the face, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"14. Thou art purified with natron, and Horus is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron and Set is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and Thoth is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and Sep is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, and thy KA is purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron. Thou art purified with natron, O thou who art stablished among</p>
 <p>"15. the gods thy brethren, Thou art purified in respect of thy head. Thou art purified in respect of thy mouth. Thy bones have been made pure wholly.</p>
 <p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Thou art filled by that which cometh to thee, O PETA-AMEN-AP I have given unto thee the Eye of Horus, and thy face is filled therewith, and it spreadeth abroad its odour."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here the priest shall take the instrument Kef-pesesh (i.e., "Overcomer of the division"), and shall say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>16. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, thy two jaw-bones which were separated have been established (i.e., restored to their former state).</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here the priest shall take the <a name="img_21000"></a>
 <img src="img/21000.jpg"> <i>of iron of the South and the</i> <a name="img_21000"></a>
 <img src="img/21000.jpg"> <i>of iron of the North, and shall say</i>:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"17. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP! Thy</p>
 <p><a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a></p>
 <p>mouth hath been opened for thee, O Erpa, <u>H</u>â Prince, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP.</p>
 <p>"18. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP! Thy mouth hath been opened for thee, O Erpa, <u>H</u>â Prince, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest take cakes of cheese (?) of the South and of the North, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"19. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and he cometh with it. I have brought it unto thee, place, thou it in thy mouth."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest take Sheku cakes, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"20. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP! Sheku cakes have been presented unto thee, O Osiris, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP."</p>
 <p><a name="page_212"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 212</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest take a vessel of milk and a vessel of water, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"21. [That which floweth] from the breast of Horus, and is of his body, hath been presented unto thee for thy mouth. That which cometh from the breast of thy sister Isis, the emission of the mother, 22. hath been seized by thee for thy mouth, and thou openest thy mouth by means of it, O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest pour out fresh water of the North and of the South, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"23. This [is] a libation unto thee, Osiris, a libation unto thee, O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, which cometh forth from thy son. 24. I have come and I have brought unto thee the Eye of Horus whereby thy heart shall be refreshed. I have brought it to</p>
 <p><a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 213</font></a></p>
 <p>thy feet, and have presented unto thee that which bath flowed forth from thee. No stoppage of thy heart [shall there be] to thee. Having it, 25. a going forth [of things (or, persons)] shall there be to thee through the word."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>The above shall be said four times.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">_____________________</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest take a black vessel and a white vessel, each containing one hathes measure, on the right and left, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"26. That which cometh forth from the two Eyes of Horus, the White and the Black, thou hast taken possession of, and when they are [set] before thee they illumine for thee thy face."</p>
 <p><a name="page_214"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 214</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest take a cake of Hem bread of the journey for the lifting up of the face, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"27. Ra, (i.e., Day) maketh an offering unto thee in the sky. The South and the North make an offering unto thee. Kerh (i.e., the Night) maketh an offering unto thee, and the Day maketh an offering unto thee. The South and the North make an offering unto thee. An offering is brought unto thee. An offering 28. thou seest, of an offering thou hearest. There is an offering before thee, an offering behind thee, an offering with thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest present five bunches of onions, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"29. O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP,</p>
 <p><a name="page_215"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 215</font></a></p>
 <p>are presented unto thee the white teeth of Horus for the journey, so that they may fill thy mouth."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer one Uten cake [<i>as an offering</i>] <i>for the lifting up of the face, and shall say four times</i>:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"30, 31. 'SUTEN TA HETEP' for the KA of the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>And he shall say four times:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"'SUTEN TA HETEP' for the KA of the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>And the KHER HEB shall say, whilst the SMER priest bringeth the two halves of the Uten cake for the opening of the mouth, four times:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, thy bread which thou eatest, and thy mouth hath been opened thereby."</p>
 <p><a name="page_216"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 216</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer a white vessel containing a hathes of wine, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"32. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, snatched from the hand of Set. Thou hast seized it for thy mouth, and thou hast opened thy mouth therewith."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer a black vessel containing a hathes of wine, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"33. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, thy mouth is opened through that which floweth from thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer a black vessel containing a hent of beer, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"34. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, there is presented</p>
 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 217</font></a></p>
 <p>unto thee that which hath been pressed [out of thee] and cometh forth from thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer the equipment of the table of offerings, the great one, for the lifting up of the face, <a name="fr_84"></a><a href="#fn_84"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>and shall say</i>:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"35, 36. O RA, may the worship which thou hast in heaven, may the worship which thou hast from the Company of the Gods, be to the Chief KHER HEB, the divine scribe and prophet of the Lady of Hetep, PETA-AMEN-AP, and may everything be for the KA of the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, and everything for his body, and everything every day."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer a Tept loaf, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"37. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 218</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Horus hath been presented unto thee [as] thy food (for thy tasting)."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer an Ah cake, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"38. The Ah food is spread out before thee like a field."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer a breast joint, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"39. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee that it may be united unto thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer a white vessel containing a hent of wine, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"40. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, which was won from the hand of Set, and was rescued for thee, and thou dost open thy mouth with it."</p>
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer a black vessel containing a hent of beer, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"41. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, there hath been presented Unto thee that which hath been pressed out, and cometh forth from Osiris."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer an iron vessel containing a hent of beer, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"42. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee in the iron [vessel],which is [ordained] for thee, wherein no iron cometh, it belongeth. to thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest offer a hetemet vessel containing one hent of beer, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"43. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, so that thou mayest be filled thereby."</p>
 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 220</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Then shall the priest pour out a vessel of water in which three cakes of natron have been dissolved, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"44. This [is] a libation unto thee, O Osiris, a libation unto thee, O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, which hath come forth from thy son, which hath come forth from Horus. 45. I have come, and I have brought unto thee the Eye of Horus, whereby thy heart shall be refreshed. I have brought it to thy feet, and presented unto thee that which hath flowed 46. and come forth from thee. No stoppage of thy heart [shall there be] to thee with the things which come forth at the word."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>The above (lines 44-46) <i>shall be said four times</i>.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">_____________________</p>
 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 221</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer Seth heb ointment, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"47. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, thine eye hath been filled (i.e., smeared) with <i>metchet</i> ointment."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer Heken ointment, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"48. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, thine eye hath been filled (i.e., smeared) and no injury shall [come] to thy face."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer Seft ointment, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"49. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and [Set] hath been made weak for thee thereby."</p>
 <p><a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 222</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer Nem ointment, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"50. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee that it may unite itself unto thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer Tua ointment, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"51. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee. It hath been brought [unto thee] that thou mayest worship (or, give thanks to) the gods by means of it."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer the finest cedar oil and the finest oil of the Thehennu, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"52. O ye oils which are on the forehead of Horus, O ye oils which are on the forehead of Horus, place ye yourselves on the forehead of the chief KHER HEB</p>
 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> PETA-AMEN-AP! 53. Make ye him to [smell] sweet in possessing you, make ye him to be a KHU (or, glorious) possessing you, make ye him to have the mastery over his body [again], and make ye him to have openings [before] his eyes. And let all the Spirits (KHU) see 54. him, and let them hear his name. Behold, O Osiris, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been brought unto thee, and it hath been seized for thee that it may be before thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer one bag of uatch eye-paint and one bag of mestem eye-paint, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"55. O Osiris PETA-AMEN-AP, I have painted the</p>
 <p><a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Eye of Horus for thee with mestem so that there may be health to thy face."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer swathings, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"56. Mayest thou watch in peace. The goddess Taatet watcheth in peace. The goddess Taatet watcheth in peace. The Eye of Horus which is in the city of Tep-Pe watcheth in peace. The Eye of Horus which is in the temple houses 57. of Net (Neith) watcheth in peace. Receive the milk-[white] and bleached swathings of the goddess UR-A. Cause ye, O swathings, that the lands may bow themselves before PETA-AMEN-AP even as they bow themselves before Horus, and make the lands to be in awe of the OSIRIS PETA-AMEN-AP, 58. as they are in awe of Set.</p>
 <p><a name="page_225"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 225</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Let them tarry with PETA-AMEN-AP in his divinity. Open ye his way so that he may be at the head of the Spirits (KHU), and let him stand at the head of the Spirits, O Anpu (Anubis)-Khent-Amenti, to the front, to the front--to the Osiris PETA-AMEN-AP."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest set fire to the incense, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"59. Let him advance! Let him advance with his KA! [As] Horus advanceth with his KA, [as] Set advanceth with his KA, [as] Thoth advanceth with his KA, [as] Sep advanceth with his Ka, [as] Khenti-maati advanceth 60. with his KA, [so] may advance thy backbone with thy KA.</p>
 <p><a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 226</font></a></p>
 <p>"Hail, PETA-AMEN-AP! The arm of thy KA is before thee, the arm of thy KA is behind thee.</p>
 <p>"Hail, PETA-AMEN-AP! The foot of thy KA is before thee, the foot of thy KA is behind thee.</p>
 <p>"Hail, PETA-AMEN-AP! The eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and thy face is filled therewith, and the perfume of the Eye of Horus spreadeth itself over thee."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest pour out a vase of water wherein two cakes of natron have been dissolved, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"62. This [is] a libation unto thee, O Osiris, a libation</p>
 <p><a name="page_227"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 227</font></a></p>
 <p>unto thee, O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, which hath come forth from thy son, which hath come forth from Horus. I have come and I have brought unto thee 63. the Eye of Horus, whereby thy heart shall be refreshed. I have brought it to thy feet, and have presented unto thee that which hath flowed forth and come out from thee. No stoppage of thy heart [shall there be] to thee. [Possessing it] a going forth of things (or, persons) shall be to thee at the [sound of] the voice."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>The above (lines 62, 63) <i>shall be recited four times</i>.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 </font><h3 align="CENTER">PART II.</h3><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>Here shall be set forth the food and drink and the things which are to be placed on the altar, and one shall enter with the offerings of food. And the priest shall say</p></font></i>:--</dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"64. It is Thoth who returneth bringing it (i.e.,</p>
 <p><a name="page_228"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 228</font></a></p>
 <p>"he Eye of Horus). <i>And then shall be said</i>: "He cometh forth with the Eye of Horus. 65. He hath given the Eye of Horus, and he is content therewith."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest present two cakes, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"66. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and he is content therewith."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest present two vessels [of beer] <i>in the usekh chamber, and say</i>:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"67. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and he is content therewith."</p>
 <p><a name="page_229"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 229</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>He who sitteth down by the offering shall say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"68. I have seated myself with it."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest present for the Ush a Tua cake and a Shens cake, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"69. O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and it hath been offered to thee for thy mouth."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest present a Tut cake, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"70. O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee for the smiting down of Set."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer a Rethu cake, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"71. O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee which was chained up [by Set]."</p>
 <p><a name="page_230"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 230</font></a></p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer a nemset vessel of Tcheser drink, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"72. O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, which entered into Set."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest offer a nemset vessel of Khenemes beer, and say:--</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>"73. O Osiris, the chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, which hath been smitten, for thy mouth."</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i></i><p>Here shall the priest lift up a cake and a vessel of beer, and say:--</p></dir>

 <p>"142. O chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, the Eye of Horus hath been presented unto thee, and there hath gone down into the throat for thee that which belongeth to thee.</p>
 <p>"143. Hail, chief KHER HEB PETA-AMEN-AP, one standeth and sitteth down by the thousands of cakes, and [vessels of] beer, and roasted meat, by thine altar in Amentet, which is filled with holy offerings for the meals of the dead."</p>
