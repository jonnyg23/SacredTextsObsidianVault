 <h3 align="center">52. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 671-675.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 671</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1987a. To say: O N., thou art the son of a Great One;</p>
 
 <p>1987b. thou art purified in the lake, <i>Dȝ.ti</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1987c. thou takest thy throne in the Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 672</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1988a. To say: The truth of Horus is the truth of this N., O N.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 296</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1988b. Thou art come, N., clothed; thou comest vested.</p>
 
 <p>1989a. N. has inherited him who is not mourned any more, him who comes into
 being smiling.</p>
 
 <p>1989b. Greetings to thee N.; thou comest in peace.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 673</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1990a. To say: O father N.,</p>
 
 <p>1990b. thou comest, that is, thou hast come like a god, thou who art come
 (in boat) like <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1991a. Thy messengers hasten; thy runners run;</p>
 
 <p>1991b. they ascend to heaven; they announce to Rē</p>
 
 <p>1992a. that thou standest in the double <i>itr.t</i>-palace
 of the horizon, upon Shu of Nut;</p>
 
 <p>1992b. that thou art seated upon the throne of thy father, Geb, as chief of
 the <i>itr.t</i>-palace,</p>
 
 <p>1992c. upon this throne of copper (or, iron), the wonder of the gods.</p>
 
 <p>1993a. The Two Enneads come to thee with salutations;</p>
 
 <p>1993b. thou commandest men</p>
 
 <p>1993c. like Min, who is in his house, and like Horus of <i><u>D</u>b.wt</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1993d. And Set was not free from bearing thy weight.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 674</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1994a. To say: I have come to thee, I am thy son; I have come to thee, I am
 Horus;</p>
 
 <p>1994b. I give to thee thy mdw-staff before the spirits and thy <i>nḥb.t</i>-sceptre before the imperishable
 stars.</p>
 
 <p>1995a. [I have found thee assembled], [thy (lit. his) face] like (that of) a
 jackal, thy (lit. his) seat like (that of) <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>t;</p>
 
 <p>1995b. she refreshes thy heart in thy body, in the house of her (lit. thy)
 father Anubis.</p>
 
 <p>1996a. Be pure and sit at the head of those greater than thou.</p>
 
 <p>1996b. Thou art seated on thy firm throne, on the throne of the First of the
 Westerners;</p>
 
 <p>1996c. thy <i>śtiš.w</i>, they are
 young.</p>
 
 <p>1997. <i>Śmnt.t</i> salutes thee, like Isis; <i>Hn.t</i>
 acclaims thee like Nephthys.</p>
 
 <p>1998a. Thou standest at the head of the <i>śn.wt</i>,
 of the double palace, like Min;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_297"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 297</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1998b. thou standest at the head of Egyptians (<i>km.tiw</i>), like Ḥapi;</p>
 
 <p>1998c. thou standest at <i>P<u>d</u>w-š</i>,
 like Seker.</p>
 
 <p>1999a. Thou standest before the <i>Rd-wr</i>-lake.</p>
 
 <p>1999b. Thou hast thy <i>'bȝ</i>-sceptre,
 thy wire, thy fingernails; which are at hand (lit. "at thy fingers");</p>
 
 <p>1999c. those who are before Thot are slain with the knife, coming from Set.</p>
 
 <p>1999d. Thou givest thine arm to the dead, to the spirits, who will take
 thine arm to the First of the Westerners.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 675</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2000a. To say: O N., "come in peace," says Osiris to thee;</p>
 
 <p>2000b. messenger of the Great God, "come in peace," says the Great
 God to thee.</p>
 
 <p>2001a. The double doors of heaven are open for thee; the (double doors of
 the) <i>śḥd.w</i>-stars are open for
 thee,</p>
 
 <p>2001b. after thou art descended (into the grave) as the jackal of Upper Egypt,</p>
 
 <p>2001c. as Anubis on his belly (side), as <i>Hpi.w</i>
 who resides in Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>2002a. The great damsel who lives in Heliopolis has given her arm to thee.</p>
 
 <p>2002b. O N., thou hast [no] father, among men, who conceived thee;</p>
 
 <p>2002c. thou hast no mother, among mankind, who bore thee.</p>
 
 <p>2003a. Thy mother is the great wild-cow who lives in el-Kâb,</p>
 
 <p>2003b. the white crown, the royal head-dress, she with the long feathers
 (hair?), she with the two hanging breasts,</p>
 
 <p>2003c. she will nurse thee; she will not wean thee.</p>
 
 <p>2004a. Raise thyself up, N., dress thyself in thy fringed-vestment, the
 first (best) in the house,</p>
 
 <p>2004b. thy <i>ḥ<u>d</u></i>-mace on thine arm,
 thy Horus-weapon (<i>ȝmś</i>) in thy
 hand, thine <i>ȝmś</i>-sceptre on thine
 arm, thy <i>ḥ<u>d</u></i>-mace in thy hand.</p>
 
 <p>2005a. Thou standest as he who is chief of the double <i>itr.t</i>-palace, who, judges the words of the gods.</p>
 
 <p>2005b. O N., thou belongest to the <i>nḫḫ.w</i>
 (-stars), when Rē shines behind the morning star.</p>
 
 <p>2006a. Lo, no god escapes from what he has said;</p>
 
 <p>2006b. he will offer thee thy thousand (loaves) of bread, thy thou</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_298"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 298</font></a></p>
 
 <p>sand (mugs) of beer, thy thousand of oxen, thy thousand of geese,</p>
 
 <p>2006c. thy thousand of everything on which a god lives.</p>
