 <h3 align="center">35. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 571-575.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 571</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1466a. To say: The mother of N., dweller in the lower sky, became pregnant
 with him;</p>
 
 <p>1466b. N. was given birth by his father Atum,</p>
 
 <p>1466c. before the sky came into being, before the earth came into being,</p>
 
 <p>1466d. before men came into being, before the gods were born, before death
 came into being.</p>
 
 <p>1467a. N. escapes the day of death, as Set escaped his day of death.</p>
 
 <p>1467b. N. belongs to your company (?), O gods of the lower sky,</p>
 
 <p>1468a. who cannot perish for their enemies.</p>
 
 <p>1468b. N. perishes not for his enemies.</p>
 
 <p>1468c. O ye who die not for a king--N. does not die for a king.</p>
 
 <p>1468d. O ye who die not for any death--N. does not die for any death.</p>
 
 <p>1469a. N. is an imperishable star, the great ---- of heaven in the house of <i>Śerḳset</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1469b. Rē has taken N. to heaven, that N. may live,</p>
 
 <p>1469c. as he lives who enters into the west of the sky and goes forth at the
 east of the sky.</p>
 
 <p>1470a. He who is within his <i>ḥn.ti</i>
 (two limits) has commended N. to him who is in his carrying-litter;</p>
 
 <p>1470b. they acclaim N., for N. is a star,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_234"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 234</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1470c. The protection of Rē is upon N. Rē
 will not abandon the protection of N.</p>
 
 <p>1471a. Horus has set N. on his shoulders;</p>
 
 <p>1471b. he has assigned N. to Shu (who says): "My arms are exalted under
 Nut."</p>
 
 <p>1471c. Rē, give thine arm to N.; Great God, give thy staff to N.,</p>
 
 <p>1471d. that he may live for ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 572</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1472a. To say: "How beautiful indeed is the sight, how pleasant indeed
 is the view," says Isis,</p>
 
 <p>1472b. "that this god ascends to heaven, his renown over him,</p>
 
 <p>1472c. his terror on both sides of him, his magic before him!"</p>
 
 <p>1473a. It was done for him, for N., by Atum, like that which one did for him
 (Atum).</p>
 
 <p>1473b. He brought to N. the gods belonging to heaven;</p>
 
 <p>1473c. he assembled to him the gods belonging to the earth.</p>
 
 <p>1474a. They put their arms under him.</p>
 
 <p>1474b. They made a ladder for N., that he might ascend to heaven on it.</p>
 
 <p>1474c. The double doors of heaven are open for N.; the double doors of <i>śḥd.w</i> are open for him.</p>
 
 <p>1475a. Atum united the nomes for N.;</p>
 
 <p>1475b. Geb gave him the cities, that is to say (lit. in speaking of it),</p>
 
 <p>1475c. the regions, the regions of Horus; the regions of Set,</p>
 
 <p>1475d. the Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>1476a. N. is <i>Iȝḥś</i>, chief of
 the land of Upper Egypt;</p>
 
 <p>1476b. N. is <i>Ddwn</i>, chief of the land of Nubia;</p>
 
 <p>1476c. N. is Sopdu, (who lives) under his <i>kśb.t</i>-trees.</p>
 
 <p>1477a. Have you acted against him? Have you said that he would die?</p>
 
 <p>1477b. He will not die. N. will live a life for ever.</p>
 
 <p>1477c. N. is become in spite of them as the surviving bull of the
 wild-bulls;</p>
 
 <p>1477d. N. is at their head; he will live and last for ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 573</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1478a. To say: Awake in peace, <i>Ḥsmnw</i>,
 in peace.</p>
 
 <p>1478b. Awake in peace, Eastern Horus, in peace.</p>
 
 <p>1478c. Awake in peace, Eastern Soul, in peace.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 235</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1478d. Awake in peace, Harachte, in peace.</p>
 
 <p>1479a. Thou sleepest in the evening boat; thou wakest in the morning boat,</p>
 
 <p>1479b. for thou art as he who oversees the gods; no god oversees thee.</p>
 
 <p>1479c. Father of N., Rē, take N. with thee, for life, to thy mother, Nut.</p>
 
 <p>1480a. The double doors of heaven shall be open for N.; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> shall be open for N.</p>
 
 <p>1480b. When N. comes to thee, that thou mayest make him live,</p>
 
 <p>1480c. command N. to sit by thy side,</p>
 
 <p>1480d. near the <i>dwȝ</i>-canal on the
 horizon.</p>
 
 <p>1481a. Father of N., Rē, commend N. to <i>Mśḫȝȝ.t</i>,
 she who is at thy side,</p>
 
 <p>1481b. to cause to designate a place for N. near the <i>Rd-wr</i>-lake under <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1482a. Commend N. to Ni-'nh, son of Sothis, to speak for N.,</p>
 
 <p>1482b. to establish a throne for N. in heaven.</p>
 
 <p>1482c. Commend N. to <i>Wr-špś.f</i>,
 the beloved Ptah, the son of Ptah,</p>
 
 <p>1482d. to speak for N.,</p>
 
 <p>1482e. to cause food to grow for his dining pavillion on earth,</p>
 
 <p>1483a. for N. is one of those four gods,</p>
 
 <p>1483b. <i>Imś.ti</i>, <i>Ḥȝpi</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mw.t.f</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1483c. who live on truth, who lean upon their d'm-sceptres,</p>
 
 <p>1483d. who guard the land of Upper Egypt.</p>
 
 <p>1484a. He flies, he flies from you, O men, as birds;</p>
 
 <p>1484b. he takes his flight from you (lit., he takes his arms from you) like
 a falcon;</p>
 
 <p>1484c. he takes his body from you like a kite;</p>
 
 <p>1484d. he is delivered from that which shackles his feet on earth,</p>
 
 <p>1484e. he is freed from that which ties his hands.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 574</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1485a. To say: Greetings to thee, Sycamore, who protects the god, under
 which the gods of the underworld stand,</p>
 
 <p>1485b. whose tips are seared, whose inside is burned, (whose) suffering is
 real.</p>
 
 <p>1486a. Assemble those who dwell in Nun; collect those who are among the
 bows.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_236"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 236</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1486b. Thy forehead is upon thine arm (in mourning) for Osiris, O Great
 Mooring-post,</p>
 
 <p>1486c. who art like her who is chief of the offering (to), and of the
 worship (?) of the lord of the East.</p>
 
 <p>1487a. Thou art standing, Osiris; thy shadow is over thee, Osiris;</p>
 
 <p>1487b. thy diadem repels Set,</p>
 
 <p>1487c. the generous damsel who acted for this spirit of <i>Gḥś.ti</i> is</p>
 
 <p>1487d. thy shadow, Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>1488a. Thy dread is among those in heaven; thy fear among those on earth.</p>
 
 <p>1488b. Thou hast hurled thy terror into the heart of the wings of Lower
 Egypt, dwelling in Buto.</p>
 
 <p>1489a. N. is come [to thee], Horus, heir of Geb, of whom Atum speaks;</p>
 
 <p>1489b. "all belongs to thee," say the Two Enneads; "all
 belongs to thee," thou sayest.</p>
 
 <p>1490a. It is even N. among them-the gods who are in heaven.</p>
 
 <p>1490b. Collect those who are among the bows; assemble those who are among
 the imperishable stars.</p>
 
 <p>1491a. N. rejoices; N. rejoices, O, 0.</p>
 
 <p>1491b. Day is day; night is night; Rē is Rē;</p>
 
 <p>1491c. --------------- he is for ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 979</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1492a. To say: "Behold, he comes; behold, he comes," says <i>Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1492b. "behold, the son of Rē comes; the beloved of Rē comes,"
 says<i> Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1492c. "I caused him to come; I caused him to come," says Horus.</p>
 
 <p>1493a. "Behold, he comes; behold, he comes," says <i>Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1493b. "behold, the son of Rē comes; the beloved of Re, comes,"
 says <i>Sḥpw</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1493c. "I caused him to come; I caused him to come," says Set.</p>
 
 <p>1494a. "Behold, he comes; behold, he comes," says <i>Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1494b. "behold, the son of Rē comes; the beloved of Rē comes,"
 says <i>Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1494c. "I caused him to come; I caused him to come," says Geb.</p>
 
 <p>1495a. "Behold, he comes; behold, he comes," says <i>Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1495b. "behold, the son of Rē comes; the beloved of Rē comes,"
 says <i>Sḥpw</i>;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1495c. "I caused him to come; I caused him to come," say the Souls
 of Heliopolis and the Souls of Buto.</p>
 
 <p>1496a. "O Rē," say men, when they stand by the side of N. on the
 earth,</p>
 
 <p>1496b. while thou dawnest on the east of the sky, "give thy hand . to
 N.;</p>
 
 <p>1496c. take him with thee to the eastern side of the sky."</p>
 
 <p>1497a. "O Re," say men, when they stand by the side of N. on the
 earth,</p>
 
 <p>1497b. while thou dawnest on the southern side of the sky, "give thy
 hand to N.;</p>
 
 <p>1497c. take him with thee to the southern side of the sky."</p>
 
 <p>1498a. "O Rē," say men, when they stand by the side of N. on the
 earth,</p>
 
 <p>1498b. while thou dawnest at the centre of the sky, "give thy hand to
 N.,</p>
 
 <p>1498c. take him with thee to the centre of the sky."</p>
 
 <p>1499. One hastens with thy message; the runners are before thee.</p>
