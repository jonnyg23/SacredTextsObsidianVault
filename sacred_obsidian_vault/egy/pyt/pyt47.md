 <h3 align="center">44. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 607-609.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 607</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1701a. To say: Nun has begotten N. on his left hand</p>
 
 <p>1701b. a child; the intelligence of N. is not.</p>
 
 <p>1701c. N. is freed from the evil gods;</p>
 
 <p>1701d. N. is not given to the evil gods.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 608</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1702a. To say: N., stand up for thy father, the Great One; be seated for thy
 mother, Nut.</p>
 
 <p>1702b. Give thy hand to thy son, Horus; behold, he is come; he approaches
 thee.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 609</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1703a. To say: N., thy mother Nut has given birth to thee in the West;</p>
 
 <p>1703b. thou hast descended in the West in company with the lord of
 veneration (?);</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_260"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 260</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1703c. thy mother Isis has given birth to thee at Chemmis;</p>
 
 <p>1703d. thy hand which is (full of) the north wind takes (Possession) of thee</p>
 
 <p>1703e. overflowing thee, behind the north wind, father N.</p>
 
 <p>1704a. The Lake of Reeds is full; the Winding Watercourse is inundated;</p>
 
 <p>1704b. the <i>mn</i>-canal of N.
 is open,</p>
 
 <p>1704c. whereby he may ferry over to the horizon, to the place where the gods
 will be born,</p>
 
 <p>1704d. and where thou wilt be born with them.</p>
 
 <p>1705a. The two reed-floats of heaven are placed for Rē,</p>
 
 <p>1705b. that he may ferry over therewith to the horizon to the place where
 the gods will be born,</p>
 
 <p>1705c. and where he will be born with them.</p>
 
 <p>1706a. The two reed-floats of heaven are placed for N.,</p>
 
 <p>1706b. that he may ferry over therewith to the horizon to the place where
 they will be born,</p>
 
 <p>1706c. and where he will be born with them.</p>
 
 <p>1707a. Thy sister is Sothis; thy mother (bearer) is the morning star;</p>
 
 <p>1707b. thou sittest between them on the great throne,</p>
 
 <p>1707c. which is at the side of the Two Enneads.</p>
 
 <p>1708a. Behold, let these four dwellers of the region (or, height) be
 brought,</p>
 
 <p>1708b. who sit upon their <i><u>d</u>b</i>-sceptres,
 who come forth on (or, from) the eastern side of the sky,</p>
 
 <p>1708c. that they may proclaim this thy goodly utterance to <i>Nḥb-kȝ.w</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1708d. which thy daughter, (<i>Ḥm.t</i>),
 said to thee, and</p>
 
 <p>1708e. <i>Nḥb-kȝ.w</i> shall proclaim
 this thy goodly utterance</p>
 
 <p>1708f. to the Two Enneads.</p>
 
 <p>1709a. It is <i>Hpnti</i>, he who lays hold
 of thy hand when thou descendest into the boat of Rē,</p>
 
 <p>1709b. descending into the boat with an offering which the king gives;
 descending and ferrying over.</p>
