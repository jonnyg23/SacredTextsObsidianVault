 <h3 align="center">5. THE DECEASED KING RECEIVES OFFERINGS AND IS RE-ESTABLISHED IN HIS
 FUNCTIONS AND POSSESSIONS, UTTERANCES 223-225.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 223</i>.</p>
 
 <p>214a. To say: Awake. Turn around. O! O!</p>
 
 <p>214b. O N., up, be seated before a thousand. loaves, a thousand mugs of
 beer;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>
 
 <p>214c. the roast, thy double-rib piece (comes) from the slaughtering-bench,
 the <i>rtḥ</i>-bread from the broad-hall (<i>wśḫ.t</i>).</p>
 
 <p>25a. As a god is provided with divine offerings, so N. is provided with his
 bread.</p>
 
 <p>215b. Thou art come to thy <i>ba</i>,
 Osiris, a <i>ba</i> among the spirits,
 mighty in his domains,</p>
 
 <p>215c. protected by the Ennead in the house of the prince.</p>
 
 <p>216a. O N., betake thyself to me, approach thyself to me,</p>
 
 <p>216b. be not far from the tomb, be not separated from me.</p>
 
 <p>216c. I have given thee the eye of Horus; I have reckoned it to thee. O may
 it be pleasing to thee, with thee.</p>
 
 <p>217a. O N., up, receive thy bread from (my) hand.</p>
 
 <p>217b. To say four times: O N., I will be to thee a door-keeper.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Heading
 and postscript to Utterance 224</i>.</p>
 
 <p>218a. To say four times: An offering to him in all his dignities, in all his
 places.</p>
 
 <p>218b. May Geb give an offering in all thy dignities, in all thy places.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 224</i>.</p>
 
 <p>218c. To say: Awake, N. Turn around, N.</p>
 
 <p>218d. Thou hast come that thou mayest command in the regions of Horus;</p>
 
 <p>218e. thou hast come that thou mayest command in the regions of Set;</p>
 
 <p>218f. thou hast come that thou mayest command in the regions of Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>219a. May the king make an offering: "in all thy dignities".</p>
 
 <p>219b. Thy garment is a <i>bȝ</i>-loin-cloth;
 thy garment is a <i>hśdd</i>-loincloth;</p>
 
 <p>219c. thou goest in sandals; thou slaughterest an ox;</p>
 
 <p>220a. thou goest in the <i>wȝ<u>d</u>-'n</i>-boat,
 in all thy dignities, in all thy places.</p>
 
 <p>220b. Thy <i>nḥb.t</i>-sceptre is at the
 head of the living, thy staff is at the head of the spirits,</p>
 
 <p>220c. like Anubis, First of the Westerners; like <i>n<u>d</u>.ti</i>, First of the Eastern nomes.</p>
 
 <p>221a. How fortunate is thy condition! Thou art a spirit, O N., among thy
 brothers, the gods.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 70</font></a></p>
 
 <p>221b. How changed it is! How changed it is! (So) protect thy children;
 beware of</p>
 
 <p>221c. thy border (limitation) which is in the earth. To say four times:
 Clothe thy body (and) come into their presence.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 225</i>.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>A
 Variant of Utterance 224</i>.</p>
 
 <p>222a. To say: Awake, N.! Turn around, N.!</p>
 
 <p>222b. Thou hast come that thou mayest command in the regions of Horus; (thou
 hast come) that thou mayest command in the regions of Set;</p>
 
 <p>222c. that thou mayest speak in the regions of Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>223a. May the king make an offering: Thy son is upon thy throne; thy garment
 is the <i>pḥ.ti</i>; thy garment is a <i>ḫśd<u>d</u></i>-loincloth;</p>
 
 <p>223b. thou goest in sandals; thou slaughterest an ox;</p>
 
 <p>224a. thou goest in the <i>wȝ<u>d</u>-n</i>-boat,
 in all thy places, in all thy dignities</p>
 
 <p>224b. Thy <i>nḥb.t</i>-sceptre is at the
 head of the living, thy staff is at the head of the spirits.</p>
 
 <p>224c. The aged is come; he protects his son.</p>
 
 <p>224d. May thy body be clothed, so that thou mayest come to [me]; and may
 [the eye of Horus] be pleasing to thee.</p>
