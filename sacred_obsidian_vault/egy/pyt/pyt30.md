 <h3 align="center">27. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 535-538.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 535</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1280a. To say by Isis and Nephthys:</p>
 
 <p>1280b. The <i>ḥȝ.t</i>-bird comes, the
 kite comes; they are Isis and Nephthys.</p>
 
 <p>1280c. They are come in search of their brother Osiris;</p>
 
 <p>1280d. (They are come) in search of their brother N.</p>
 
 <p>1281a. Thou who art (here), thou who art (there), weep for thy brother;
 Isis, weep for thy brother; Nephthys, weep for thy brother.</p>
 
 <p>1281b. Isis sits, her hands upon her head;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1282a. Nephthys has indeed seized the tip of (her) two breasts because of
 her brother, N.;</p>
 
 <p>1282b. Anubis being on his belly; Osiris being wounded; Anubis being before
 the fist (?).</p>
 
 <p>1283a. Thy putrefaction, N., is not; thy sweat, N., is not;</p>
 
 <p>12 83b. thy outflowing, N., is not; thy dust, N., is not.</p>
 
 <p>1284a. <i>Ḥȝ.ti</i> son of <i>Ḥȝ.ti</i> (is) at <i>Mnii</i>, coming as <i>Mn.ti</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1284b. to divide in three these your four days and your eight nights.</p>
 
 <p>1285a. The stars follow thy beloved <i>Ḳbḥ.wt</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1285b. who is chief of thy <i>nmḥ</i>
 (attendants); thou art chief of those who are chief of the <i>nmḥ.w</i> (attendants); thou hast made <i>nmḥ</i> the <i>nmḥ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1285c. Loose Horus from his bonds, that he may punish the Followers of Set;</p>
 
 <p>1286a. that he may seize them; that he may remove their heads; that he may
 take off their legs.</p>
 
 <p>1286b. Cut thou them up, take thou out their hearts;</p>
 
 <p>1286c. drink thou of their blood;</p>
 
 <p>1287a. count their hearts, in this thy name of "Anubis counter of
 hearts."</p>
 
 <p>1287b. Thy two eyes have been given to thee as thy two uraeus-serpents,</p>
 
 <p>1287c. for thou art like Wepwawet on his standard, Anubis who presides in <i>sḥ-n<u>t</u>r</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1288a. O N., the houses of the great who are in Heliopolis make thee
 "first";</p>
 
 <p>1288b. the spirits and even the imperishable stars fear thee.</p>
 
 <p>1288c. The dead fall on their face before thee; the blessed dead(?) care for
 thee.</p>
 
 <p>1289a. "Eldest (son), <i>Imȝḫ</i>
 is for N.," say the Souls of Heliopolis,</p>
 
 <p>1289b. who furnish thee with life and satisfaction.</p>
 
 <p>1289c. He lives with the living as Seker lives with the living;</p>
 
 <p>1289d. he lives with the living as N. lives with the living.</p>
 
 <p>1290a. O N., come, live thy life there, in thy name, in thy time,</p>
 
 <p>1290b. in these years, which are to be peaceful, according to (?) thy wish.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 536</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1291a. To say: Thy water belongs to thee, thine abundance belongs to thee,
 thine efflux comes out of Osiris to thee.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_212"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 212</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1291b. The double doors of heaven are open for thee; the double doors of Nut
 are open for thee;</p>
 
 <p>1291c. the double doors of heaven are open for thee; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> are open for thee.</p>
 
 <p>1292a. "Welcome," says Isis;
 "(come) in peace," says Nephthys, when they see their brother.</p>
 
 <p>1292b. Raise thyself up;</p>
 
 <p>1292c. untie thy bandages; shake off thy dust.</p>
 
 <p>1293a. Sit thou upon this thy firm throne.</p>
 
 <p>1293b. Thou art pure with thy four <i>nmś.t</i>-jars
 and thy four <i>ȝb.t</i>-jars,</p>
 
 <p>1293c. which come for thee out of thy chapel of natron, which were filled
 for thee in the natron lake,</p>
 
 <p>1293d. and which Horus of Nekhen has given thee.</p>
 
 <p>1294a. He has given to thee his spirits, the jackals,</p>
 
 <p>1294b. like (to) Horus who is in his house, like (to) <i>Ḫnti</i> (Osiris) chief of the mighty.</p>
 
 <p>1294c. A durable offering is made for thee.</p>
 
 <p>1295a. Anubis, chief of the <i>sḥ-n<u>t</u>r</i>,
 has commanded that thou come in as a star, as god of the morning (or, as god of
 the morning star),</p>
 
 <p>1295b. that thou pass through the region of Horus of the South and that thou
 pass through the region of Horus of the North.</p>
 
 <p>1296a. (And) men will construct with their arms a stairway to thy throne.</p>
 
 <p>1296b. He comes to thee his father; he comes to thee Geb.</p>
 
 <p>1297a. Do for him that which thou hast done for his brother, Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>1297b. on this day of thy feast, the water being full (i. e. at inundation),</p>
 
 <p>1297c. when (his) bones are counted, when (his) sandals are repaired,</p>
 
 <p>1297d. when his nails, upper and lower, are cleaned for him,</p>
 
 <p>1297e. There will come to him (people of) the Upper Egyptian <i>itr.t</i>-palace and of the northern <i>itr.t</i>-palace, bowing --.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 537</i>.</p>
 
 <p>11298a. To say: O N., arise, sit thou on the throne of Osiris;</p>
 
 <p>1298b. thy flesh is complete like (that of) Atum; thy face like (that of) a
 jackal.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 213</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1299a. Give thou thy mouth to Rē.</p>
 
 <p>1299b. He congratulates thee on what thou hast said; he praises thy words.</p>
 
 <p>1299c. Arise; thou ceasest not to be; thou perishest not.</p>
 
 <p>1300a. Live, N., thy mother Nut lays hold of thee, she unites her. self with
 thee;</p>
 
 <p>1300b. Geb seizes thine arm. "Thou comest in peace," say thy
 fathers.</p>
 
 <p>1300c. Thou art possessed of thy body; thou art clothed in thy body.</p>
 
 <p>1301a. Thou ascendest like Horus of the <i>Dȝ.t</i>,
 chief of the imperishable stars;</p>
 
 <p>1301b. thou sittest upon thy firm throne at the head of thy canal of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1301c. thou livest as the coleoptera (lives); thou endurest as the <i><u>d</u>d</i>, eternally.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 538</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1302a. To say: Back, thou lowing ox.</p>
 
 <p>1302b. Thy head is in the hand of Horus; thy tail is in the hand of Isis;</p>
 
 <p>1302c. the fingers of Atum are at thy horns.</p>
