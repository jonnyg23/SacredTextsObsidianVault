 <h3 align="center">14. MISCELLANEOUS UTTERANCES ON THE CAREER OF THE DECEASED KING IN THE
 HEREAFTER, 317-337.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 317</i>.</p>
 
 <p>507a. To say: N. is come forth to-day at the head of the inundation of the
 flood.</p>
 
 <p>507b. N. is a crocodile god, with green feather, with vigilant countenance,
 with forehead erect;</p>
 
 <p>507c. effervescent, proceeding from leg and tail of the Great (One) who is
 in splendour.</p>
 
 <p>508a. N. is come to his watercourses, which are in the land of the flood, in
 <i>Mḥ.t-wr.t</i>,</p>
 
 <p>508b. to the places of satisfaction, with green fields, which are in the
 horizon,</p>
 
 <p>509a. that N. may make green the herbs in both lands of the horizon,</p>
 
 <p>509b. (and) that N. may bring the green to the great eye which is in the
 midst of the field.</p>
 
 <p>509c. N. takes his throne which is in the horizon;</p>
 
 <p>510a. N. appears as Sebek, son of Neit;</p>
 
 <p>510b. N. eats with his mouth, N. urinates, N. cohabits with his phallus;</p>
 
 <p>510c. N. is lord of semen, which women receive from their husband.</p>
 
 <p>510d. wherever N. wishes, according to the desire of his heart,</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i></i></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 318</i>.</p>
 
 <p>511a. To say: N. is the <i>nw</i>-serpent,
 the bull which leadeth, which swallowed its seven uraeus-serpents,</p>
 
 <p>511b. through which came into being its seven neck-vertebrae,</p>
 
 <p>511c. which commands its Seven Enneads who hear the words of the king.</p>
 
 <p>511d. And the mother of N. is <i>Ḥnw.t</i>;
 N. is her son.</p>
 
 <p>512a. N. has come that he may swallow myrrh,</p>
 
 <p>512b. that N. may take myrrh, his nostrils (?) being full of myrrh; the
 finger-nail of N. being full of myrrh.</p>
 
 <p>512c. N. has taken away your neck, O gods;</p>
 
 <p>512d. Serve N. who will confer (upon you) your valour.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 319</i>.</p>
 
 <p>513a. To say: N. is the bull of the two splendours which are in the middle
 of his eye.</p>
 
 <p>513b. The mouth of N. is immune because of a flaming breath, the head of N.
 because of horns, (as) lord of the South (Haroëris of Ḳuṣ).</p>
 
 <p>513c. N. leads the god; N. rules over the Ennead.</p>
 
 <p>5 1 3d. N. makes the lapis lazuli grow; N. causes the Upper Egyptian <i>twn</i>-plant to sprout.</p>
 
 <p>514a. N. has tied the cords of the <i>šmšm.t</i>-plant.</p>
 
 <p>514b. N. has united the heavens; N. rules over the lands, the southern and
 the northern,</p>
 
 <p>514c. (as) the gods who were before.</p>
 
 <p>514d. N. built a divine city, according to its merits.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>514e.
 N. is the third in his dawning.</i></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 320</i>.</p>
 
 <p>515a. To say: N. has regulated the night; N. has sent the hours on their
 way.</p>
 
 <p>515b. The powers (stars) dawn; they proclaim N. as <i>Bȝ-bii</i>.</p>
 
 <p>515c. N. is that son of her who knew not that</p>
 
 <p>515d. she had borne N., to him of powerful visage, as lord of nights.</p>
 
 <p>516a. Humble (?) yourselves, ye lords; hide yourselves, ye subjects, in the
 presence of N.,</p>
 
 <p>516b. (for) N. is <i>Bȝ-bii</i>, lord of
 night,</p>
 
 <p>516c. the bull, without whom life would cease.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 110</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 321</i>.</p>
 
 <p>517a. To say: O Thou-whose-back-is-behind-him, bring to N. the <i>śfr.t-ḥtp.t</i>, which was upon the back
 of Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>517b. that N. may ascend to heaven upon it; that N. may do service of
 courtier to Rē in heaven.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 322</i>.</p>
 
 <p>518a. To say: Heaven is open; earth is open.</p>
 
 <p>518b. The double doors of <i>śȝt</i>
 are open to Horus; the double doors of <i>šȝ
 b.wt</i> are open to Set.</p>
 
 <p>518c. Turn thou for him as he who is in his fortress; N. has passed over you
 (<i>šȝb.wt</i>) as Atum.</p>
 
 <p>518d. N. is Ḫii-tȝw, who is (lives?) in the midst of the <i>Ngȝ</i>-mountains.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 323</i>.</p>
 
 <p>519a. To say: N. has purified himself with Rē in the sea of reeds.</p>
 
 <p>519b. Horus dries thy skin, O N.; Thot dries thy feet, O N.</p>
 
 <p>519c. Shu, lift N. up on high; Nut, give thine arm to N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 324</i>.</p>
 
 <p>520a. To say: Greetings to thee, doorkeeper of Horus, who art at the portal
 of Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>520b. announce now the name of N. there to Horus,</p>
 
 <p>521a. (for) he is come with temple-saliva for this his temple (of the head),</p>
 
 <p>521b. which is painful at the [beginning] of the months, which becomes bald
 at the beginning of half months.</p>
 
 <p>521c. Wilt thou cool it with the magic, [which thou didst make for the Great
 One] among the gods,</p>
 
 <p>521d. in his former state, which is come upon him?</p>
 
 <p>522a. Greetings to thee, O hippopotamus, from everlasting.</p>
 
 <p>522b. [Art] thou [come] to N. as hippopotamus from everlasting,</p>
 
 <p>522c. after he had brandished one of the two <i>ȝmś</i>-clubs of Horus against thee and slain thee therewith?</p>
 
 <p>522d. Greetings to thee in his former state, which is come upon him.</p>
 
 <p>523a. Greetings to thee, [braying] ass.</p>
 
 <p>523b. Art thou come to N. as a braying ass,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 111</font></a></p>
 
 <p>523c. after he had slain thee with the ----- tail, [which grows] in the sea of Osiris?</p>
 
 <p>524a. Greetings to thee, O Khnum, who was made harmless though he built N.</p>
 
 <p>524b. Thou art his <i>m</i>-plant,
 which his foot [trod upon],</p>
 
 <p>524c. which cannot straighten up under his toes.</p>
 
 <p>524d. Thou art one of the two <i>iwn</i>-pillars
 of the great palace.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 325</i>.</p>
 
 <p>525a. To say: The double doors of heaven are open; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> are open</p>
 
 <p>525b. for Horus of the gods, at daybreak,</p>
 
 <p>525c. that he may ascend in the Marsh of Reeds and purify himself in the
 Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>526a. The double doors of heaven are open; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> are open</p>
 
 <p>526b. for Harachte at daybreak,</p>
 
 <p>526c. that he may ascend in the Marsh of Reeds and purify himself in the
 Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>527a. The double doors of heaven are open; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> are open</p>
 
 <p>527b. for Horus of the East at daybreak,</p>
 
 <p>527c. that he may ascend in the Marsh of Reeds and purify himself in the
 Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>528a. The double doors of heaven are open; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> are open</p>
 
 <p>528b. for Horus of the <i>Šsm.t</i>-land
 at daybreak,</p>
 
 <p>528c. that he may ascend in the Marsh of Reeds, and purify himself in the
 Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>529a. The double doors of heaven are open; the double doors of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i> are open</p>
 
 <p>529b. for N. himself at daybreak</p>
 
 <p>529c. that he may ascend in the Marsh of Reeds and purify himself in the
 Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>530a. N. is clean; he takes his lasting (copper?) bones;</p>
 
 <p>530b. he has stretched out his imperishable limbs, which were (or, are) in
 the body of his mother Nut.</p>
 
 <p>531a. Rē, give thine arm to N.</p>
 
 <p>531b. Shu will draw him up to the "Companions of Shu,"</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 112</font></a></p>
 
 <p>531c. after he has caused N. to be nourished with the milk of two black
 cows, the two nurses of the Souls of Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>532a. O <i>Hpȝ<u>t</u></i>, it is grevious for
 the body of Nut, because of the fury of the divine seed, which shall be in her.</p>
 
 <p>532b. Behold also N.--N. is a divine seed which shall be in her.</p>
 
 <p>533a. <i>Hpȝ<u>t</u></i>, <i>Hnn</i>, <i>Smnn.w</i>,</p>
 
 <p>533b. N. is purified. He has taken his divine <i>śwḥ</i>-vestment,</p>
 
 <p>533c. that N. may establish himself there as a god like them.</p>
 
 <p>533d. <i>Hpȝ<u>t</u></i>, <i>Hnn</i>, <i>Smnn.w</i>,</p>
 
 <p>533e. Take N. away; let him remain with you.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 326</i>.</p>
 
 <p>534a. To say: Collar, beloved of Horus, good-looking, which is on the neck
 of Rē.</p>
 
 <p>534b. If thou goest to heaven; so will N. go to heaven.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 327</i>.</p>
 
 <p>535a. To say: The messenger of Horus, whom he loves, was N., who has brought
 back to him his eye.</p>
 
 <p>535b. The messenger of Set, whom he loves, was N., who has brought back to
 him his testicles.</p>
 
 <p>535c. The messenger of Thot, whom he loves, was N., who has brought back to
 him his arm.</p>
 
 <p>536a. The Two Enneads tremble for themselves,</p>
 
 <p>536b. for they are the messengers, whom N. loves, who should bring N. to
 food.</p>
 
 <p>536c. They bring N. to food.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 328</i>.</p>
 
 <p>537a. To say: N. is the exalted, who is in the forefront, who lifts up the
 brow;</p>
 
 <p>537b. the star before which the gods bow, before which the Two Enneads
 tremble.</p>
 
 <p>537c. It is the hand of N. which will lift him (N.) up.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 329</i>.</p>
 
 <p>538a. N. is the exalted, who is in the forefront; who lifts up the brow;</p>
 
 <p>538b. the star before which the gods bow, before which the Two Enneads
 tremble.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 113</font></a></p>
 
 <p>538c. The face of N. is the face which sees his elevation.</p>
 
 <p>538d. N. is a nose which breathes.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 330</i>.</p>
 
 <p>539a. To say: N. ascends to heaven on (or, above, or, through) the <i>šdšd</i>, which was at the separation,</p>
 
 <p>539b. after its (the heaven's) sole (of the foot) was seized by the
 up-reached hand.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 331</i>.</p>
 
 <p>540a. To say: N. ascends to heaven on the <i>šdšd</i>, which was at the separation,</p>
 
 <p>540b. after its sole (of the foot) was seized.</p>
 
 <p>540c. N. is a nose which breathes;</p>
 
 <p>540d. the face of N. is the face which sees his elevation.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 332</i>.</p>
 
 <p>541a. To say: this N. is he who comes forth from the <i>mḥn</i>-jar.</p>
 
 <p>541b. N. has ascended as his warm breath and has returned.</p>
 
 <p>541c. N. has gone, O heaven, O heaven; N. has returned, O earth, O earth.</p>
 
 <p>541d. N. has walked upon the green <i>ḳȝd</i>-herb
 under the feet of Geb;</p>
 
 <p>541e. he treads (again) upon the paths of Nut.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 333</i>.</p>
 
 <p>542a. To say: N. purified himself upon that appearing (-mound) of the earth,
 on which Rē purified himself;</p>
 
 <p>542b. he placed a <i>ḥb-ib</i>-stand
 and he set up the ladder.</p>
 
 <p>542c. Those who are in the great (heaven), they will take the arm of N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 334</i>.</p>
 
 <p>543a. To say: Greetings to thee, O Rē, traverser of heaven, voyager
 through Nut.</p>
 
 <p>543b. Thou hast traversed the Winding Watercourse.</p>
 
 <p>543c. N. has grasped thy tail; as to N., he is indeed a god, the son of a
 god.</p>
 
 <p>544a. N. is a flower, which comes out of the <i>ka</i>,</p>
 
 <p>544b. a golden flower, which comes out of <i>N<u>t</u>r.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>544c. N. has traversed Buto; he has voyaged through <i>Knm.wt</i>.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_114"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 114</font></a></p>
 
 <p>545a. N. has traversed Buto as <i><u>H</u>rti</i>,
 ruler of <i>Nsȝ.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>545b. He has voyaged through <i>Knm.wt</i>
 as <i>Šsm.w</i>, who is in his ship of the
 oil-press. May the god be pleased</p>
 
 <p>545c. that N. live as <i>Ftk.t</i> lives!</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 335</i>.</p>
 
 <p>546a. To say: How beautiful is, the sight of N., adorned with the horns of
 Rē,</p>
 
 <p>546b. his apron on him like Hathor, his feather like the feather of a
 falcon,</p>
 
 <p>546c. when (or, as) he ascends to heaven among his brothers, the gods!</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 336</i>.</p>
 
 <p>547a. To say: Greeting to thee, ox of the oxen, when thou makest the
 ascension.</p>
 
 <p>547b. N. seizes thee by thy tail; N. takes thee by thy buttocks.</p>
 
 <p>547c. When thou makest the ascension, a Great One is behind thee, a Great
 One is before thee.</p>
 
 <p>548a. Greetings to thee, Great One among the gods, take N. to thee, he
 belongs to thee.</p>
 
 <p>548b. Thy heart is whole; as to the parts of the corpse of N., they are
 young.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 337</i>.</p>
 
 <p>549a. To say: Heaven speaks, the earth quakes on account of thy fear,
 Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>549b. when thou makest the ascension.</p>
 
 <p>550a. O ye milk-cows there, O ye nurse-cows there,</p>
 
 <p>550b. go around him, weep for him, praise him, lament for him,</p>
 
 <p>550c. when he makes the ascension. He goes forth to heaven among his
 brothers, the gods.</p>
