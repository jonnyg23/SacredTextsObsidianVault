 <h3 align="center">42. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 602-605.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 602</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1672a. To say by the Earth, by Geb, by Osiris, by Anubis, by <i>Wr-ḥb</i>:</p>
 
 <p>1672b. Make N. festive at the Feast of Horus.</p>
 
 <p>1672c. Let him who is among the falcons hasten to the <i>ka</i> of N., who is <i>Ḥmmi</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1673a. Open for N. his eyes, open for him his nose;</p>
 
 <p>1673b. open for N. his mouth, open for him his ears;</p>
 
 <p>1673c. make prosperous for N. his two plumes.</p>
 
 <p>1674a. Let N. be allowed to pass, by the god,</p>
 
 <p>1674b. filled with the force of the winds.</p>
 
 <p>1674c. After you have eaten this, N. will find what is left by you.</p>
 
 <p>1674d. Give the remainder to N.; behold, he is come.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 603</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1675a. (N. Jéquier, VII 709 + 40). To say: Lift thyself up, father N.;
 fasten to thee thy head; take to thee thy limbs;</p>
 
 <p>1675b. (N. VII 709 + 40). lift thyself up upon thy feet; follow thy heart.</p>
 
 <p>1675c. Thy runners hasten; thy messengers rush on behind;</p>
 
 <p>1676a. thy herald of the horizon comes; Anubis approaches thee;</p>
 
 <p>1676b. <i>Ḥtp</i> gives his arm to thee;
 the gods desire (or, rejoice) ----</p>
 
 <p>1676c (N. VII 709 + 40: Thot comes in his dignity of spirit to the Two
 Enneads.</p>
 
 <p>1676c + 1 (N. VII 709 + 42). He ferried over the lake; h[e] avoided the <i>Dȝ.t</i></p>
 
 <p>1677a. -----------------------------------</p>
 
 <p>1677b. ----------------------- with this mighty one who endures each day.</p>
 
 <p>1678a. He comes that he may govern the cities, that he may rule over the
 settlements,</p>
 
 <p>1678b. that he may command those who are in Nun</p>
 
 <p>1678c. sitting, to him --------------</p>
 
 <p>1679a. -------------------------------------</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_257"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 257</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1679b. ---------------- he rests alive in the West (or, he is satisfied in
 living in the West),</p>
 
 <p>1679c. among the Followers of Rē, who make the way of twilight mount up.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 604</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1680a. To say: Raise thyself up, father, N., the great; sit before them;</p>
 
 <p>1680b. the apertures of the (heavenly) windows are open for thee;</p>
 
 <p>1680c. broad are thy steps of light;</p>
 
 <p>1680d. this is said to thee, father N. To say: O! Ho!'</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 605</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1681a. To say: Father N., I am come; I bring to thee the green cosmetic;</p>
 
 <p>1681b. I am come; I bring to thee the green cosmetic, which Horus put on
 Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>1682a. I put thee on my father N., as Horus put thee on his father Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>1682b. when (or, as) Horus filled his empty eye with his full eye.</p>
