 <h3 align="center">26. FOR THE PROTECTION OF THE PYRAMID ENCLOSURE AGAINST OSIRIS AND HIS
 CYCLE, UTTERANCE 534.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 534</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1264a. To say by Horus: May Geb make an offering.</p>
 
 <p>1264b. Be gone, flee (thou) whom Horus guards, whom Set protects;</p>
 
 <p>1264c. be gone, flee, (thou) whom Osiris guards, whom <i><u>H</u>rti</i> protects;</p>
 
 <p>1265a. be gone, flee, (thou) whom Isis
 guards, whom Nephthys protects;</p>
 
 <p>1265b. flee, chief, (thou) whom <i>Mḫnti-irti</i>
 guards, whom Thot protects;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1265c. be gone, flee (thou) whom the <i>ḫȝ.tiw</i>
 guard, whom the <i>imi.w-iȝw.w</i>
 protect.</p>
 
 <p>1266a. I have come; I have dedicated this house to N.;</p>
 
 <p>1266b. purer is this broad-hall than <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>;</p>
 
 <p>12 66c. at its door (or, entrance) is an obelisk; the door is double (i.e.
 with two leaves), and is sealed with two evil eyes.</p>
 
 <p>1267a. Let not Osiris come in this his evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1267b. do not open to him thine arms.</p>
 
 <p>1267c. Let him be gone; let (him) go to <i>Ndi.t</i>;
 at once; let him be gone to <i><u>d</u>ȝ</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1268a. Let not Horus come in this his evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1268b. do not open to him thine arms; that which is said to him is his name
 of <i>Šp-iri</i>--<i>šȝȝ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1268c. Let him go to <i>np.t</i>; at once;
 let him go to <i>N<u>t</u>r</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1269a. Let not Set come in this his evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1269b. do not open to him thine arms; that which is said to him is his name
 of <i>śš</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1269c. Let him go to <i><u>d</u>w.t</i>; at
 once; let him go to <i>Ḥn.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1270a. If <i>Mḫnti-irti</i> comes in
 this his evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1270b. do not open to him thine arms; that which is said to him is his name
 of <i>nš</i> (driveller).</p>
 
 <p>1270c. Let him go to <i>Ddnw</i>-(him
 whom) they found in (the condition of) quaking;</p>
 
 <p>1270d. at once; let him go to <i>Ḫm</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1271a. If Thot comes in this his evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1271b. do not open to him thine arms; that which is said to him is his name
 of "thou hast no mother."</p>
 
 <p>1271c. Let him go; let him be gone to his (?) two <i>int.wi</i>; let him go to Buto, to <i><u>H</u>ri-<u>D</u>ḥwti</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1272a. If Isis comes in this her evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1272b. do not open to her thine arms; that which is said to her is her name
 (of) "wide of <i>ḥwȝ-t</i>
 (evil-smelling)."</p>
 
 <p>1272c. Let her (lit. him) be gone; let her go to the houses of <i>Mȝnw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1272d. at once; let her go to <i>Ḥ<u>d</u>b.t</i>,
 to the place where thou hast (she has) been struck.</p>
 
 <p>1273a. If Nephthys comes in this her evil coming;</p>
 
 <p>1273b. that which. is said to her is this her name of "substitute
 without vulva."</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1273c. Go thou to the house of <i>Śerḳet</i>,
 to the place where thou didst strike thy two <i>nn.twi</i> (thighs?).</p>
 
 <p>1274a. If enemies come with those who are among the elders</p>
 
 <p>1274b. that which is said to them (lit. her) is this their (lit. her) name
 of <i>Šp-šȝ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1274c. Go to ---- <i><u>t</u>w.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1275a. If N. comes with his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1275b. the mouth of his gods opens: "(If) he desires to descend to the
 [underworld, let him descend]</p>
 
 <p>1275c. to the place where there are gods."</p>
 
 <p>1276a. If N. comes with his <i>ka</i>,
 open thou thine arms to him;</p>
 
 <p>1276b. the mouth of his gods opens: "(If) he desires to ascend to
 heaven, let him ascend."</p>
 
 <p>1277a. I am come as judge; may Geb make offerings, and Atum.</p>
 
 <p>1277b. I consecrate this pyramid, this temple, to N. and to his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1277c. that which this pyramid, this temple, contains is for N. and for his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1277d. pure is this eye (pyramid enclosure) of Horus,</p>
 
 <p>1278a. O may it be pleasing to thee. He who puts his finger against this
 pyramid, this temple of N. and of his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1278b. he who will put his finger against the house of Horus in <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1278c. may Nephthys and Isis go against him
 -------- Geb.</p>
 
 <p>1279a. His case will be heard by the Ennead,</p>
 
 <p>1279b. he will be without support, his house will be without support;</p>
 
 <p>1279c. he is accursed; he is one who eats his (own) body.</p>
