 <h3 align="center">33. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 564-569.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 564</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1421a. To say: He is pure who purifies himself in the sea of reeds;</p>
 
 <p>1421b. Rē purifies himself in the sea of reeds;</p>
 
 <p>1421c. N. himself purifies himself in the sea of reeds.</p>
 
 <p>1421d. Shu purifies, himself in the sea of reeds;</p>
 
 <p>1421e. N. himself purifies himself in the sea of reeds.</p>
 
 <p>1422a. Shu, Shu, lift N. up to heaven;</p>
 
 <p>1422b. Nut, give thine arms to N.;</p>
 
 <p>1422c. let him fly, let him fly, rejoicing, rejoicing, rejoicing, let him
 fly, let him fly.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 565</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1423a. To say: N. be thou purified, (when) thou comest to heaven.</p>
 
 <p>1423b. N. lasts longer than men; he dawns for the gods.</p>
 
 <p>1423c. N. dawned with Rē at his dawning.</p>
 
 <p>1424a. Their third is he who is with him;</p>
 
 <p>1424b. one is behind N.; the other is before N.;</p>
 
 <p>1424c. one gives, water; the other gives sand.</p>
 
 <p>1425a. N. leans upon thy two arms, Shu, just as Rē leans upon thine arm.</p>
 
 <p>142 5b. N. found them, sitting, at his approach</p>
 
 <p>1425c. the two spirits, mistresses of this land.</p>
 
 <p>1426a. Let Nut rejoice at the approach of N.;</p>
 
 <p>1426b. <i>Npnp.t</i> has received him;</p>
 
 <p>1426c. she who is in her <i>ḳ</i>, for
 life and joy, and she who wears her <i>N<u>t</u>śtn</i>-garment.</p>
 
 <p>1427a. they gave birth, for themselves, to N.</p>
 
 <p>1427b. N. is loosed from the evil which (was) in him.</p>
 
 <p>1427c. Nephthys gave her arms to N.;</p>
 
 <p>1427d. she passed her breast over the mouth of N.</p>
 
 <p>1428a. <i>Dwȝ-wr</i> shaved N.;</p>
 
 <p>1428b. Sothis washed the hands of N.,</p>
 
 <p>1428c. at his birth, on that day, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1428d. N. knows (remembers?) not his first mother whom he knew;</p>
 
 <p>1428e. it is Nut who has borne N., with Osiris.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_228"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 228</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 566</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1429a. To say: Take N. away with thee, Horus;</p>
 
 <p>1429b. transport him, Thot, on the tip of thy wing,</p>
 
 <p>1429c. like Seker who is in the <i>mȝ.t</i>-boat.</p>
 
 <p>1429d. Horus does not pass the night (lit. go to bed) behind the canal; nor
 is Thot without a boat (lit. boatless);</p>
 
 <p>1429e. and N. is not without a boat, for he has the eye of Horus.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 567</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1430a. To say: Rē is purified in the Marsh of Reeds;</p>
 
 <p>1430b. Horus is purified in the Marsh of Reeds;</p>
 
 <p>1430c. N. is purified in the Marsh of Reeds,</p>
 
 <p>1430d. that he may arise with him. Nut give him thine arm.</p>
 
 <p>1430e. Rejoice, rejoice, he flies, he flies!</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 568</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1431a. To say: He is gone who went to his <i>ka</i>; <i>Mḫnti-irti</i> is gone
 to his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1431b. N. is gone to his <i>ka</i>, to
 heaven.</p>
 
 <p>1431c. A ladder is made for him, upon which he mounts, in its name of
 "That which mounts to heaven."</p>
 
 <p>1432a. His boat is brought to him by the <i><u>d</u>m</i>-sceptres of the imperishable
 stars.</p>
 
 <p>1432b. The bull (or, ox) of heaven lowers its horn, so that he may pass
 thereon to the lakes of <i>Dȝ.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1433a. O N., thou dost not fall to the ground.</p>
 
 <p>1433b. N. lays hold of the two sycamores, which are in the middle of yonder
 side of the sky,</p>
 
 <p>1433c. which ferry him over, and they set him on the eastern side of the
 sky.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 569</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1434a. To say: N. knows thy name; N. forgets not thy name.</p>
 
 <p>1434b. "Limitless" is thy name. The name of thy father is
 "Thou art great."</p>
 
 <p>1434c. Thy mother is "Satisfaction," who bears thee morning by
 morning.</p>
 
 <p>1435a. The birth of "Limitless" in the horizon shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_229"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 229</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1435b. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1435c. The birth of <i>Śerḳet</i>
 shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1435d. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1436a. The two regions shall be forbidden to Horus,</p>
 
 <p>1436b. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1436c. The birth of <i>Śȝḥ</i> shall
 be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>436d. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1437a. The birth of Sothis shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1437b. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1437c. The (coming of) the two apes (<i>bnt.wi</i>)
 to Rē, his two beloved sons, shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1437d. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1438a. The birth of Wepwawet in the <i>pr-nw</i>-palace
 shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1438b. if thou preventest N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1438c. The (coming of) men to the king, son of a god, shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1438d. if thou prevents N. from coming to the place where thou art.</p>
 
 <p>1439a. The (coming of) thy crew of the imperishable stars to row thee over
 shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1439b. if thou preventest them from letting N. descend into thy boat.</p>
 
 <p>1439c. The (coming of) men to death shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1439d. if thou preventest N. from descending into thy boat.</p>
 
 <p>1440a. Men's eating of bread shall be prevented,</p>
 
 <p>1440b. if thou preventest N. from descending into thy boat.</p>
 
 <p>1440c. N. is <i>Śkśn</i>, the
 messenger of Rē;</p>
 
 <p>1440d. N. shall not be prevented from (entering) heaven.</p>
 
 <p>1440e. The <i>mȝ<u>t</u>.t</i>-tree, which is
 at the door of heaven, has stretched out its arms to N.</p>
 
 <p>1441a. His-face-behind-him, the ferryman of the Winding Watercourse, is
 united to him.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_230"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 230</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1441b. N. is not prevented; an obstacle is not opposed to N.,</p>
 
 <p>1441c. for N. is one of you, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1442a. N. is come to thee, O Rē;</p>
 
 <p>1442b. N. is come to thee, "Limitless,"</p>
 
 <p>1442c. that he may row thee over, that he may do service of a courtier to
 thee.</p>
 
 <p>1442d. N. loves thee in his body; N. loves thee in his heart.</p>
