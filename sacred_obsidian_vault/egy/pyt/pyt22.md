 <h3 align="center">19. IN PRAISE OF NUT, UTTERANCES 427-435</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 427</i>.</p>
 
 <p>777a. To say: Nut, spread thyself over thy son, Osiris N.;</p>
 
 <p>777b. hide him from Set; protect him, Nut.</p>
 
 <p>777c. Thou art come, that thou mayest protect (lit. hide) thy son; come now,
 protect this Great One.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 428</i>.</p>
 
 <p>778a. To say: Nut, fall upon thy son, Osiris N.;</p>
 
 <p>778b. protect him, Great Sieve (protectress), this Great One among thy
 children.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 429</i>.</p>
 
 <p>779a. To say by Geb: Nut, thou art become (spiritually) mighty:</p>
 
 <p>779b. thou wast (already physically) mighty in the womb of thy mother,
 Tefnut, before thou wast born.</p>
 
 <p>779c. Protect N. with life and well-being. He shall not die.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 430</i>.</p>
 
 <p>780a. To say: Mighty was, thy heart,</p>
 
 <p>780b. when thou wast in the body of thy mother, in thy name of
 "Nut".</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 431</i>.</p>
 
 <p>781a. (To say:) Thou art the daughter, who has gained (physical) power over
 her mother, who dawned as king of Lower Egypt.</p>
 
 <p>781b. Make N. (spiritually) mighty in thy womb. He shall not die.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 148</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 432</i>.</p>
 
 <p>782a. To say: Great lady, who didst become heaven, thou didst become
 (physically) mighty,</p>
 
 <p>782b. thou art become victorious, thou hast filled every place with thy
 beauty.</p>
 
 <p>782c. The whole earth lies (lit. is) under thee; thou hast taken possession
 of it;</p>
 
 <p>782d. thou encompassest the earth and all things (therein) in thine arms;</p>
 
 <p>782e. mayest thou establish this N. in thee as an imperishable star.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 433</i>.</p>
 
 <p>783a. To say: I have fertilized thee as Geb, in thy name of "Heaven";</p>
 
 <p>783b. I have united to thee the whole earth in every place.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 434</i>.</p>
 
 <p>784a. To say: High one over the earth, thou art above thy father Shu, who
 hast the mastery over him.</p>
 
 <p>784b. He has loved thee in that he has set himself under thee; all things
 are thine.</p>
 
 <p>785a. Thou hast taken each god to thyself with his boat;</p>
 
 <p>785b. thou hast educated them as "She of a thousand souls,"</p>
 
 <p>785c. so that they will not disappear from thee like stars.</p>
 
 <p>785d. So let not N. leave thee, in thy name of "Far off one" (or,
 "High one").</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 435</i>.</p>
 
 <p>786a. To say: I am Nut, "the Granary." I have proclaimed the name
 of Osiris N.,</p>
 
 <p>786b. namely, "Horus, beloved of the two lands, N."; "King of
 Upper and Lower Egypt, N.";</p>
 
 <p>786c. "<i>nb.ti</i>, beloved of the
 Corporation, N."; "falcon over gold, N. ";</p>
 
 <p>787a. "heir of Geb, his beloved N.", "beloved of all the
 gods, N.";</p>
 
 <p>787b. given all life, stability, prosperity, health, joy like Rē, thou
 livest for ever.</p>
