 <h3 align="center">29. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 540-552.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 540</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1328a. To say: N. comes to thee, his father; he comes to thee, Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>1328b. He has brought to thee this thy <i>ka</i>;
 how wonderful it is!</p>
 
 <p>1328c. His mother Nut has punished him who shines on her forehead.</p>
 
 <p>1329a. <i>Ḥtmw.t</i> has raised thee up;</p>
 
 <p>1329b. thy mouth is opened by <i>Šśȝ</i>,
 chief of the city of <i>Šn.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1329c. thy mouth is opened by <i>Dwȝ-wr</i>
 in the house of gold;</p>
 
 <p>1329d. [thy mouth] is opened by the <i>tt.wi</i>
 which are before the house of natron;</p>
 
 <p>1330a. thy mouth is opened by Horus with his little finger,</p>
 
 <p>1330b. with which he opened the mouth of his father, with which he opened
 the mouth of Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>1331a. N. is thy son; N. is Horus;</p>
 
 <p>1331b. N. is the beloved son of his father, in this his name of "Son
 whom he loves."</p>
 
 <p>1332a. Thou art purified: thou art dried. Thy clothing is given (to thee),</p>
 
 <p>1332b. thy thousand of alabaster (vessels), thy thousand of garments,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 217</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1332c. which N. has brought to thee, that he might clothe thee therewith.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 541</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1333a. To say: Children of Horus,</p>
 
 <p>1333b. <i>Ḥȝpi</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mu.t.f</i>, <i>Imś.ti</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1333c. protect life for your father, Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p>1333d. from the time that he is given his endurance (or, that he endure)
 among the gods.</p>
 
 <p>1334a. Smite Set, protect this Osiris N. from him before the earth is
 brightened.</p>
 
 <p>1334b. Horus is powerful; he himself will avenge this his father, Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p>1334c. The father has caused that you honour him.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 542</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1335a. To say: It is Horus; he is come to reclaim his father, Osiris N.;</p>
 
 <p>1335b. he has proclaimed a royal (death) decree in the places of
 Anubis--everyone recognizing it, he shall not live.</p>
 
 <p>1336a. Thot, spare not any among those who wronged the king;</p>
 
 <p>1336b. Thot, hasten that thou mayest see (grasp) this; O father (Thot),
 announce to him his (death) decree.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 543</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1337a. To say: Bring (him) to this Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p>1337b. Osiris N., he who killed thee is brought to thee; let him not escape
 from thee.</p>
 
 <p>1337c. Osiris N., he who killed thee is brought to thee; perform his
 execution.</p>
 
 <p>1337d. Osiris N., he who killed thee is brought to thee, cut (him) in three.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 544</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1338a. To say: Children of Horus, go to this Osiris N.;</p>
 
 <p>1338b. Children of Horus, hurry, put yourselves under this Osiris N.; let
 there be none among you who shall withdraw.</p>
 
 <p>1338c. Carry him.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 218</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 545</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1339a. To say: Osiris N., he who killed thee is brought to thee; cut (him)
 up; perform his execution.</p>
 
 <p>1339b. Children of Horus,</p>
 
 <p>1339c. <i>Ḥȝpi</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mu.t.f</i>, <i>Imś.ti</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1340a. carry your father, this Osiris N.; lead him.</p>
 
 <p>1340b. Osiris, N., make thy endurance; open thy mouth; stand up.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 546</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1341a. To say: I am Nut; bring to (me) Osiris N.,</p>
 
 <p>1341b. give him to (me), that I may embrace him.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 547</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1342a. To say: O father, Osiris N., I betake myself to thee;</p>
 
 <p>1342b. Osiris N., approach thyself to (me).</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 548</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1343a. To say: The mouth of the earth opens for Osiris N.; Geb said to him:</p>
 
 <p>1343b. "N. is great like a king, mighty like Rē."</p>
 
 <p>1343c. "Come in peace," say the Two Enneads to N.</p>
 
 <p>1343d. The eastern door of heaven is open for him, to the abode of <i>kas</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1344a. The great Nut gives her arms to him, she of the long horn, she of the
 protruding breast.</p>
 
 <p>1344b. She will nurse N.; she will not wean him.</p>
 
 <p>1345a. She takes him to herself to heaven, she does not cast him down to the
 earth.</p>
 
 <p>1345b. She makes this N. remain as chief of the two <i>itr.t</i>-palaces.</p>
 
 <p>1345c. He descends into the boat like Rē, on the shores of the Winding
 Watercourse.</p>
 
 <p>1346a. N. rows in the <i>ḥnbw</i>-boat,</p>
 
 <p>1346b. where he takes the helm, towards the field of the two lower heavens,</p>
 
 <p>1346c. to the beginning of this land of the Marsh of Reeds.</p>
 
 <p>1347a. His arm is taken by Rē; his head is raised up by Atum;</p>
 
 <p>1347b. his forward cable is taken by Isis; his stern cable is seized by
 Nephthys.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1348a. <i>Ḳbḥ.w.t</i> places him at her
 side, and puts him among the <i>ḫnti.w-š</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1348b. as the herdsmen of (his) calves.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 549</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1349a. To say: Back, <i>Bȝbwi</i>, red-eared, with coloured hind-quarters,</p>
 
 <p>1349b. pass thou the cutlet, from thy chapel (or, of thy lady), over thy
 mouth.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 550</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1350a. To say: Back, <i>Km-wr</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1350b. glide away in Babylon
 (<i><u>H</u>ri-ḥȝ</i>), in the place where they
 glided (i.e. fell).</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 551</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1351a. To say: Open, Frontier (?), open --- as its (?) barrier inclines;</p>
 
 <p>1351b. back, <i>Rw-ḥȝ.t</i>; retreat <i>Pḥ.wi</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1351c. let thou (me) pass by, the passing by of a god.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 552</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1352. To say: I am alive, says N., for ever.</p>
