 <p align="CENTER">[FOURTH EXPLANATION OF THE STORY.]</p>
 <p>[§ XLI. Osiris is the Moon, and Typhon is the Sun; Typhon is therefore called Seth, <a name="fr_343"></a><a href="#fn_341"><font size="1">1</font></a> a word meaning "violence," "force," &amp;c. Herakles accompanies the Sun, and Hermes the Moon. In § XLII. Plutarch connects the death-day of Osiris, the seventeenth of Hathor, with the seventeenth day of the Moon's revolution, when she begins to wane. The age of Osiris, twenty-eight years, suggests the comparison with the twenty-eight days of the Moon's revolution. The tree-trunk which is made into the shape of a crescent at the funeral of Osiris refers to the crescent moon when she</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_246"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 246</font></a></p>
 <p>wanes. The fourteen pieces into which Osiris was broken refer to the fourteen days in which the moon wanes.]</p>
