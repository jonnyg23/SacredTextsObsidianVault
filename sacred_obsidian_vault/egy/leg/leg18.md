 <h3 align="CENTER">THE LEGEND OF THE DESTRUCTION OF MANKIND</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">CHAPTER IV.</h3>
 <p>IV. AND THE MAJESTY OF THIS GOD SAID, "Call to me the god Thoth," and one brought the god to him forthwith. And the Majesty of this god said unto Thoth, "Let us depart to a distance from heaven, from my place, because I would make light and the god of light (KHU) in the Tuat and [in] the Land of Caves. Thou shalt write down [the things which are] in it, and thou shalt punish those who are in it, that is to say, the</p>
 <p><a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 34</font></a> <a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a></p>
 <p>workers who have worked iniquity (or, rebellion). Through thee I will keep away from the servants whom this heart [of mine] loatheth. Thou shalt be in my place (<i>ast</i>) ASTI, and thou shalt therefore be called, O Thoth, the 'ASTI of Ra.' Moreover, I give thee power to send (<i>hab</i>) forth . . . . .; thereupon shall come into being the Ibis (<i>habi</i>) bird of Thoth. I moreover give thee [power] to lift up thine hand before the two Companies of the gods who are greater than thou, and what thou doest shall be fairer than [the work of] the god KHEN; therefore shall the divine bird <i>tekni</i> of Thoth come into being. Moreover, I give thee [Power] to embrace (<i>anh</i>) the two heavens with thy beauties, and with thy rays of light; therefore shall come into being the Moon-god (<i>Aah</i>) of Thoth. Moreover, I give thee [power] to drive back (<i>anan</i>) the Ha-nebu; <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">1</font></a> therefore shall come into being the dog-headed Ape (<i>anan</i>) of Thoth, and he shall act as governor for me. Moreover, thou art now in my place in the sight of all those who see thee and who present offerings to thee, and every being shall ascribe praise unto thee, O thou who art God."</p>
