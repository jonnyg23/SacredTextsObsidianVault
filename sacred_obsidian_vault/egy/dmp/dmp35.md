   <h3 align="center">VERSO COL. III.</h3>  
 <p>(1) Medicament [for a catalepsy (?). Gall of ceras]tes, (2) pips (?) of western apples, herb of <i>klo</i>. (3) Grind them together, make into a ball, put it into wine(?), and drink (?).</p>  
 <p>(4) Lees of wine. (5) It is a white stone like (6) galbanum.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a> <a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 175</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> There is another sort which is made (7) into lime (?).</p>  
 <p>The way to know it (8) that it is genuine is this: You grind a little (9) with water; you rub it on the skin (10) of a man for a short time; then it (11) removes the skin. (12) Its name in Greek (?) ἀφροσέληνον, (13) 'Foam of the moon,' it is a white stone.</p>  
 <p>(14) A medicament for making a woman love a man: Fruit(?) of acacia; (15) grind with honey, anoint his phallus with it, (16) you (<i>sic</i>) lie with the woman.</p>  
 <p>(17) 'Foam of the moon'; this is a white stone like (18) glass, (when?) it is rubbed into fragments like orpiment.</p>
