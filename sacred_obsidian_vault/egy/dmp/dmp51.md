   <h3 align="center">VERSO COL. XIX.</h3>  
 <p>(1) A spell for bringing a woman out of her house.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 192</font></a> <a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 193</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> You take a ....(2) of a wild she-cat; you dry it; you take a heel-tendon (?) [of a (?).... which has been (?)] (3) drowned; you fashion a ring the body (? bezel) of which is variegated (?) with gold [in the form of two (?)] (4) lions, their mouths being open, the face of each being turned to the other; you put some .... its face (?). (5) If you wish to bring a woman to you at any time, you place the ring on the upper part of a lamp, (6) which is lighted, you say, 'Bring N. daughter of N. to this place (7) in which I am, quickly in these moments of to-day.'</p>  
 <p>Then she comes at once.</p>
