   <h3 align="center">VERSO COL VII.</h3>  
 <p>(1) Another to follow it: Juice of a cucumber which has been rubbed down, one ladleful (?), water of the ears of a <i>kle</i>-animal, one ladleful (?) like the ladle (2) of a (wine-)cup; you add a <i>uteh</i>-measure of good wine to them; and she drinks it at midday, before she has (3) eaten anything whatever, after bathing in the bath, which she has done before; when evening comes, you put the rag (?) (4) with honey on her as above for seven days.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 180</font></a> <a name="page_181"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 181</font></a></p>  
 <p>Another to follow: You take a new dish; you put (5) ten <i>uteh</i>-measures of old sweet wine on it; you put a half <i>kite</i> of fresh rue on it from (6) dawn till midday; let her bathe in the bath, and come out and drink it. When it is evening (7) you put honey on her as above again for seven days.</p>
