 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="green">p. 117</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">INDEX OF NAMES OF GODS</h3>  
 <p><i>Abtu-fish.</i>—A mythological fish which accompanies the Boat of Ra at sunrise.</p>  
 <p><i>Amemt.</i>—The mythical animal which devours the hearts of the wicked at the Judgment of Osiris.</p>  
 <p><i>Amon.</i>—God of Thebes. In and after the xviiith dynasty he became the supreme deity of Egypt under the name of Amon-Ra.</p>  
 <p><i>Ant-fish.</i>—A mythological fish which accompanies the Boat of Ra at sunrise.</p>  
 <p><i>Anubis.</i>—A jackal-headed deity who presided over the embalming of the dead. He was said to be the illegitimate son of Osiris and Nephthys, and, in the form of a dog, to have protected Isis in her wanderings.</p>  
 <p><i>Apep.</i>—The enemy of Ra in the Duat.</p>  
 <p><i>Astarte.</i>—A Syrian goddess, whose name is found occasionally in Egyptian inscriptions.</p>  
 <p><i>Atmu.</i>—An early name of the solar deity worshipped at Heliopolis. In later times, the name of the setting sun.</p>  
 <p><i>Bes.</i>—A bandy-legged dwarf with horns. God of music and pleasure, and protector of children. Possibly also a god of birth.</p>  
 <p><i>Besa.</i>—A spirit of the corn.</p>  
 <p><i>Geb.</i>—The earth-god, father of Osiris.</p>  
 <p><i>Harmakhis.</i>—Horus on the Horizon, i.e. the sun at its rising and setting.</p>  
 <p><i>Harpocrates.</i>—Horus the Child, son of Isis and Osiris.</p>  
 <p><i>Hathor.</i>—Goddess of love and beauty; often identified with all the other goddesses, including Sekhmet.</p>  
 <p><i>Hekt.</i>—The frog-headed goddess of birth.</p>  
 <p><i>Her-desuf.</i>—A form of Horus.</p>  
 <p><i>Horakhti.</i>—The Horizon-Horus. The same as Harmakhis.</p>  
 <p><i>Horus.</i>—The hawk-headed god is, properly speaking, the</p>  
 <p><a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="green">p. 118</font></a></p>  
 <p>brother of Isis and Osiris; but is constantly confused with Horus the Child, and is called Avenger or Protector of his Father.</p>  
 <p><i>Isis.</i>—The greatest of Egyptian goddesses, wife of Osiris, and mother of Harpocrates.</p>  
 <p><i>Khepera.</i>—The rising sun, god of resurrection.</p>  
 <p><i>Khnum.</i>—The ram-headed god of the cataract, who creates man upon the potter's wheel.</p>  
 <p><i>Khonsu.</i>—The moon-god at Thebes.</p>  
 <p><i>Mehen.</i>—The serpent who protects Ra in the Duet.</p>  
 <p><i>Mentu.</i>—God of war.</p>  
 <p><i>Meskhent.</i>—Goddess of birth.</p>  
 <p><i>Min.</i>—Father of gods and men. God of Koptos.</p>  
 <p><i>Neith.</i>—Goddess of Saïs. Identified by the Greeks with Athena.</p>  
 <p><i>Nekhbet.</i>—The vulture-goddess of Upper Egypt.</p>  
 <p><i>Nephthys.</i>—Sister of Isis and Osiris.</p>  
 <p><i>Nepra.</i>—A spirit of the corn.</p>  
 <p><i>Nun.</i>—God of the primaeval waters.</p>  
 <p><i>Nut.</i>—The sky-goddess, mother of Osiris.</p>  
 <p><i>Osiris.</i>—One of the chief gods of Egypt. Murdered and torn to pieces by his brother Set, re-vivified by Isis and Horus.</p>  
 <p><i>Ra.</i>—The Sun-god, one of the chief gods of Egypt. Heliopolis (the On of the Bible) was the principal centre of his worship.</p>  
 <p><i>Sekhmet.</i>—The lioness-headed goddess of Memphis.</p>  
 <p><i>Selk.</i>—The scorpion-goddess.</p>  
 <p><i>Set.</i>—Brother and murderer of Osiris. Looked upon, in late times, as the Author of Evil.</p>  
 <p><i>Shu.</i>—Twin-brother of Tefnut. He holds up the sky above the earth.</p>  
 <p><i>Sokar.</i>—The hawk-headed god of the dead. When fused with Ptah (Ptah-Sokar) he appears in the form of a misshapen dwarf, and is then looked upon as a god of resurrection.</p>  
 <p><i>Tatanen.</i>—An obscure god, generally fused with Ptah of Memphis as Ptah-Tatanen.</p>  
 <p><i>Ta-urt.</i>—The hippopotamus goddess of birth.</p>  
 <p><i>Tefnut.</i>—Lioness-headed. Twin-sister of Shu. The two form the constellation Gemini.</p>  
 <p><i>Tepu-yn.</i>—A spirit of the corn,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="green">p. 119</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Thoth.</i>—The ibis-headed god of all learning and magic. Chief centre of worship Khemennu or Hermopolis, now called Eshmunen.</p>  
 <p><i>Uazet.</i>—Goddess of Lower Egypt.</p>  
 <p><i>Up-uaut.</i>—The jackal-god of Siut.</p>
