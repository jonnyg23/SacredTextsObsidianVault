 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXVI. HOW THEY DENOTE THE HEART.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_05600"></a>
 <img src="img/05600.jpg"> <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_62"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>When they would denote the <i>heart</i> they delineate the Isis; for this animal is consecrated to Hermes [Thoth],</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>the lord of every heart and of reasoning. The Ibis also is itself in its own shape like the heart, respecting which great discussions are maintained by the Egyptians.</p>
