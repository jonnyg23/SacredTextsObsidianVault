 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XVII. HOW THEY DENOTE INTREPIDITY.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_03800"></a>
 <img src="img/03800.jpg"> <a name="fr_43"></a><a href="#fn_41"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>When they would denote <i>intrepidity</i>, they depict a LION, for he has a great head, and fiery eyeballs, and a round</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 39</font></a></p>  
 <p>face, and about it hairs like rays in resemblance of the sun; and hence it is, that they place lions under the throne of Horus, intimating the connexion of the animal with the god. And the sun is called Horus from presiding over the Hours.</p>
