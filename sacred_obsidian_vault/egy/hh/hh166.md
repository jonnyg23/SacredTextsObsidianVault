 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XCIII. HOW A MAN HAVING RECEIVED INJURY FROM THE GRAPE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_14201"></a>
 <img src="img/14201.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man that is injured by the grape and cures himself</i>, they depict a HOUPOO AND</p>  
 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 143</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> THE HERB ADIANTUM (maidenhair); for when injured by the grape, if he places a piece of adiantum in his mouth he is healed.</p>
