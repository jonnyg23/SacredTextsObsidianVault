 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXVI. HOW A MAN WHO HAS BEEN SUCCEEDED IN HIS PROPERTY BY A SON WHOM HE HATED.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_12601"></a>
 <img src="img/12601.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise a <i>man who has been succeeded</i> </p>  
 <p><a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 127</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>in his property by a son whom he hated</i>, they depict AN APE WITH A YOUNG APE BEHIND IT, For the ape begets two young apes, one of which he loves extravagantly, and the other he hates: and the one which he loves he keeps before him and kills with fondling; but the one which he hates he keeps behind him and brings up.</p>
