 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLV. HOW A WOMAN WHO MISCARRIES.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_11401"></a>
 <img src="img/11401.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a woman who miscarries</i>, they depict a MARE KICKING A WOLF; for not only by</p>  
 <p><a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 115</font></a></p>  
 <p>kicking a wolf does a mare miscarry, but it immediately miscarries if it should merely tread on the footstep of a wolf.</p>
