 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXVII. HOW A RAPACIOUS, OR PROLIFIC, OR FURIOUS MAN.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_08500"></a>
 <img src="img/08500.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolize a <i>rapacious</i>, or <i>prolific</i>, or <i>furious man</i>, they delineate a CROCODILE, because it is prolific [?], and fertile in offspring, and furious. For if it fails in its intention of seizing any thing it rages in anger against itself.</p>
