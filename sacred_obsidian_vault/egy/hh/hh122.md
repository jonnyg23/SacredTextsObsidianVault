 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLIX. HOW A MAN WHO DWELLS SECURELY IN A CITY.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_11601"></a>
 <img src="img/11601.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man who dwells securely in a city</i>, they depict an EAGLE CONVEYING A STONE; for he takes up a stone, either from the sea or land, and deposits it in his nest, to keep it steady.</p>
