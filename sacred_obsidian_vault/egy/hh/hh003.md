 <p><a name="page_5"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 5</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">H O R A P O L L O.</h1>  <h4 align="center">ΒΙΒΛΙΟΝ ΠΡΟΩΤΟΝ·</h4>  <h4 align="center">BOOK I.</h4>  <h3 align="center">I. HOW THEY DENOTE ETERNITY.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_00500"></a>
 <img src="img/00500.jpg"> <a name="fr_1"></a><a href="#fn_1"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>To denote Eternity <a name="fr_2"></a><a href="#fn_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> they depict the SUN and MOON, because their elements are eternal. But when they would represent Eternity <a name="fr_3"></a><a href="#fn_2"><font size="1">2</font></a> differently,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_6"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 6</font></a></p>  
 <p>they delineate a SERPENT with its tail covered by the rest of its body: the Egyptians call this Ouraius, <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_3"><font size="1">1</font></a> which in the Greek language signifies Basilisk: <a name="fr_5"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">2</font></a> And they place golden figures of it round the Gods. The Egyptians say that Eternity is represented by this animal; because of the three existing species of serpents, the others are mortal, but this alone is immortal; and <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">3</font></a> because it destroys any other animal by merely breathing upon <a name="fr_7"></a><a href="#fn_5"><font size="1">4</font></a> it even without biting. And hence, inasmuch as it thus appears to have power over life and death, they place it upon the head of the Gods.</p>
