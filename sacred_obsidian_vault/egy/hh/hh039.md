 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXVII. HOW EDUCATION.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_05700"></a>
 <img src="img/05700.jpg"></p>  
 <p>To denote <i>education</i> <a name="fr_65"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">1</font></a> they represent the HEAVEN DISTILLING DEW, intimating that as falling dew alights on all Vegetables, and softens those which have a nature susceptible of being softened, but is unable to operate upon those which essentially remain hard in the same way as upon the others; so also among men education is common to all; and a man of an apt disposition receives it as dew, while</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>a man of a disposition less docile is incapable of doing so.</p>
