 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IV. HOW A MONTH.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_01000"></a>
 <img src="img/01000.jpg">  <a name="fr_13"></a><a href="#fn_12"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>To represent a <i>month</i> they delineate a PALM BRANCH, or, the MOON INVERTED.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 11</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> A palm branch for the reason before mentioned respecting the palm tree; and the moon inverted, because they say, that, in its increase, when it has come to fifteen degrees, <a name="fr_14"></a><a href="hh007.htm#fn_14"><font size="1">1</font></a> it appears in figure with its horns erect; and in its decrease, after having completed the number of thirty days, <a name="fr_15"></a><a href="hh007.htm#fn_15"><font size="1">2</font></a> it sets with its horns inverted.</p>
