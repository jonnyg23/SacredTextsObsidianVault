 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXXXIX. HOW A MAN THAT HAS LIVED TO A PROPER AGE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_14000"></a>
 <img src="img/14000.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man that has lived to a proper age</i>, they depict a DYING CROW; for she lives an hundred years according to the Egyptians; and a year among the Egyptians consists of four (of our) years.</p>
