 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VI. WHAT THEY SIGNIFY BY DELINEATING A HAWK.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_01300"></a>
 <img src="img/01300.jpg"> <a name="fr_21"></a><a href="#fn_20"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>When they would signify <i>God</i>, or <i>height</i>, or <i>lowness</i>, or <i>excellence</i>, or <i>blood</i>, or <i>victory</i>, (or <i>Ares</i>, or <i>Aphrodite</i>,) [Hor or Hathor] <a name="fr_22"></a><a href="#fn_21"><font size="1">2</font></a>, they delineate a HAWK. They symbolize by it <i>God</i>, because the bird is prolific and long-lived, or perhaps rather because it seems to be an image of the sun, being capable of looking more intently towards his rays than all other winged creatures: and hence physicians for the cure of the eyes use the herb hawkweed: hence</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a></p>  
 <p>also it is, that under the form of a HAWK, they sometimes depict the <i>sun</i> as lord of vision. And they use it to denote <i>height</i>, because other birds, when they would soar on high, move themselves from side to side, being incapable of ascending vertically; but the hawk alone soars directly upwards. And they use it as a symbol of <i>lowness</i>, because other animals move not in a vertical line, but descend obliquely; the hawk, however, stoops directly down upon any thing beneath it. And they use it to denote <i>excellence</i>, because it appears to excel all birds—and for <i>blood</i>, because they say that this animal does not drink water, but blood—and for <i>victory</i>, because it shews itself capable of overcoming every winged creature; for when pressed by some more powerful bird, it directly turns itself in the air upon its</p>  
 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>  
 <p>back, and fights with its claws extended upwards, and its wings and back below; and its opponent being unable to do the. like, is overcome.</p>
