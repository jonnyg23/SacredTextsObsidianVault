 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CXIV. HOW A MAN THAT IS EAGER AFTER GOOD.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_15500"></a>
 <img src="img/15500.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man that is eager for good, and who instead of it falls into evil</i>, they portray a CUTTLEFISH; for this fish if it see any other longing to catch it, ejects a black liquid from its belly into the water, so that by these means it is no longer visible, and thus escapes.</p>
