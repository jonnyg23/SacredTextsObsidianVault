 <p><a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 29</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">XII. HOW THEY DENOTE HEPHÆSTUS [PHTHAH.]</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_02900"></a>
 <img src="img/02900.jpg">  <a name="fr_34"></a><a href="#fn_32"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>'To denote <i>Hephæstus</i> [<i>Phthah</i>], they delineate a SCARABÆUS and a VULTURE, and to denote <i>Athena</i> [<i>Neith</i>], <a name="fr_35"></a><a href="#fn_33"><font size="1">2</font></a> a VULTURE and a SCARABÆUS; for to them the world appears to consist both of male and female, (for Athena [Neith] however they also depict a vulture) and; according to them, these are the only Gods who are both male and female. <a name="fr_36"></a><a href="#fn_34"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
