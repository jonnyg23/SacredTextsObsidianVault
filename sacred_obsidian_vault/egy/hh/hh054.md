 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LII. HOW THEY REPRESENT KNOWLEDGE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_07201"></a>
 <img src="img/07201.jpg"></p>  
 <p>To represent <i>knowledge</i>, they delineate an ANT, for</p>  
 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 73</font></a></p>  
 <p>whatever a man may carefully conceal, this creature obtains a knowledge of: and not for this reason only, but also because beyond all other animals when it is providing for itself its winter's food, it never deviates from its home, but arrives at it unerringly.</p>
