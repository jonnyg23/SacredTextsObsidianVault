 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXXIII. HOW A MAN ANNOYED BY HIS PRIVATE ENEMIES.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_13100"></a>
 <img src="img/13100.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would represent <i>a man annoyed</i> <a name="fr_133"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>by his private enemies, and extricating himself with small loss</i>, they portray a WOLF WHICH HAS LOST THE EXTREMITY OF HIS TAIL; for the wolf, when about to be hunted, shoots the hairs and extremity of his tail.</p>
