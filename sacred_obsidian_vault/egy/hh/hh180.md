 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CVII. HOW A MAN WHO IS MARRIED TO A WOMAN.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_15100"></a>
 <img src="img/15100.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man who is married to a woman from their very infancy wherein they were born</i>, they depict PREGNANT PINNÆ; for these when produced within the shell, after a short time are joined with one another, even within the shell.</p>
