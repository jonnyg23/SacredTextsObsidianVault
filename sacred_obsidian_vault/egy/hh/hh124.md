 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LI. HOW THEY DENOTE A MAN WHO FLEES FOR REFUGE TO HIS PATRON, AND RECEIVES NO ASSISTANCE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_11701"></a>
 <img src="img/11701.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would denote <i>a man who flees for refuge to his patron, and receives no assistance</i>, they depict a SPARROW AND AN OWL; for the sparrow when pursued betakes itself to the owl, and being near it is seized.</p>
