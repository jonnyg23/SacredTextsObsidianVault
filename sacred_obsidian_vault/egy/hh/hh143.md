 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 129</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LXIX. HOW A MAN OVERCOME BY HIS INFERIORS.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_12900"></a>
 <img src="img/12900.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man overcome by his inferiors</i>, they depict TWO SKINS, one of an HYÆNA, <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">1</font></a> and the other of a PANTHER; for if these two skins be placed together, the panther's shoots its hair, but the other does not.</p>
