 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Ancilla to the Pre-Socratic Philosophers</i>, by Kathleen Freeman, [1948], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">13. ANAXIMENES OF MILÊTUS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anaximenes</span> Of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Milêtus</span> was in his prime about 546 B.C.</p>  
 <p>He wrote one book, in 'simple and unextravagant Ionic'. One whole sentence only has survived.</p>  
 <p>1. (<i>Paraphrase containing the word</i>) Loose. (= <i>rare</i>).</p>  
 <p>2. As our soul, being air, holds us together, so do breath and air surround the whole universe.</p>  
 <p>2a. (<i>The sun is broad</i>) like a leaf.</p>  
 <p>(<i>Spurious</i>)</p>  
 <p>3. Air is near to the incorporeal; and since we come into being by an efflux from this (<i>air</i>), it is bound to be both non-limited and rich so that it never fails.</p>
