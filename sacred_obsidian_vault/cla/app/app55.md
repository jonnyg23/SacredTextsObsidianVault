 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Archelâus</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Athens</span> was in his prime about 450 B.C.</p>  
 <p>He is credited with a prose work called <i>Physiologia</i>, and an elegiac poem of condolence for Cimon.</p>  
 <p>1. (<i>Reference by Plutarch to his Elegies written for Cimon on the death of his wife Isodicê</i>).</p>
