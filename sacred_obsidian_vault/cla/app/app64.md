 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Ancilla to the Pre-Socratic Philosophers</i>, by Kathleen Freeman, [1948], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">69. NESSAS OF CHIOS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nessas</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Chios</span> lived in the fifth and early fourth centuries B.C.</p>  
 <p>He was said to have been a pupil of Democritus; but the two remaining references to his writings are literary.</p>  
 <p>1. (<i>Nessas lengthens a vowel in Homer, disregarding the metre</i>).</p>  
 <p>2. (<i>Nessas derives the word 'Diaktoros' from 'Diagein': 'Conductor of souls'</i>).</p>
