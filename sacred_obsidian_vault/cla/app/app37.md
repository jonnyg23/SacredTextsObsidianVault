 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 73</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">42. HIPPOCRATES OF CHIOS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hippocrates</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Chios</span> flourished during the latter half of the fifth century B.C.</p>  
 <p>(<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Aeschylus</span> was the name of one of his pupils).</p>  
 <p>He was the first to write a text-book of mathematics, called the <i>Elements</i>. Parts of it were incorporated by Simplicius in his Commentary on the <i>Physics</i> of Aristotle; but the work itself is lost.</p>
