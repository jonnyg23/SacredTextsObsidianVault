 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Ancilla to the Pre-Socratic Philosophers</i>, by Kathleen Freeman, [1948], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">67. LEUCIPPUS OF ABDÊRA</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Leucippus</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Abdêra</span> was in his prime about 430 B.C.</p>  
 <p>He was credited with one book, called <i>The Great World-Order</i>; and perhaps a monograph <i>On Mind</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. (<i>Title of book:</i> The Great World-Order, <i>here attributed to Democritus</i>).</p>  
 <p>1a. (<i>Restoration of Herculanean Papyrus in which Democritus is accused of plagiarising from The Great World-Order' of Leucippus</i>).</p>  
 <p><i>Certain terms of the Atomic Theory can probably be traced to Leucippus</i>, <i>e.g.</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Atoms ('uncuttables').  
 <p>Close-pressed (<i>units</i>).</p>  
 <p>Great Void.</p>  
 <p>Absection.</p>  
 <p>Rhythm (<i>meaning 'form'</i>).</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 91</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Contact (<i>meaning 'arrangement'</i>).  
 <p>Aspect <i>(meaning 'position'</i>).</p>  
 <p>Entanglement.</p>  
 <p>Eddy.</p></div>  
 <p>2. (<i>From a treatise 'On Mind'</i>). Nothing happens at random; everything happens out of reason and by necessity.</p>
