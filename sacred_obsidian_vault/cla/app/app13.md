 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Thales</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Milêtus</span> was in his prime about 585 B.C.</p>  
 <p>Whether he ever wrote a book is unknown; if he did, no genuine fragment survives,.</p>  
 <p>1. (<i>Title: 'Nautical Astronomy'</i>).</p>  
 <p>2. (<i>There are two Hyades, one north and one south</i>).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_19"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 19</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Late forgery</i></p>  
 <p>3. The much-discussed four substancesof which we say the chief is Water, making it as it were the one Elementby combination and solidification and coagulation of the substances in the universe mingle with one another. In what way, I have already explained in Book One.</p>  
 <p>4. (<i>Titles: 'On the Solstice'. 'On the Equinox'</i>).</p>
