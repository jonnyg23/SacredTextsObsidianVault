 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM92">92</a></h1>
 <p>Ϝέσπερε, πάντα φέρων, ὄσα φαίνολισ ἐσκέδασ᾽ αγωσ,<br>
 φέρεισ οἴν, φέρεισ αἶγα, φέρεισ ἄπυ ματέρι παῖδα. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#117">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>Evening, thou that bringst all that bright morning scattered, thou bringst the sheep, the goat, and the child back to its mother.</p>
 <p>Hail, gentle Evening, that bringst back<br>
 All things that bright morning hath beguiled.<br>
 Thou bringst the lamb, thou bringst the kid,<br>
 And to its mother, her drowsy child.</p>
 <p>From the "Etymologicum Magnum," where the meaning of αὔωσ <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#118">[transcription]</a></font>
  ("dawn") is discussed. The beauty of the fragment needs no emphasising.</p>
