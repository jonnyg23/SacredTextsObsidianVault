 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM59">59</a></h1>
 <p>Κατθνάσκει Κυθέρἠ, ἄβροσ Ἄδωνισ, τί κε θεῖμεν,<br>
 Καττύπτεσθε κόραι καὶ κατερείκεσθε χίτωνασ. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#81">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>Gentle Adonis is dying, O Cythera, what shall we do?<br>
 Beat your breasts, O maidens, and rend your garments.</p>
 <p>Gentle Adonis wounded lies, dying, dying.<br>
 What message, O Cythera, dost thou send?<br>
 Beat, beat your white breasts, O ye weeping maidens,<br>
 And in wild grief your mourning garments rend.</p>
 <p>Quoted by Hephaestion and presumed to be written by Sappho from a passage in Pausanias.</p>
 <p>The reverbrating beat of the repetitions of the letter <tt>k</tt> is very remarkable.</p>
