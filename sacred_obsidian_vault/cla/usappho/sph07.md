 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM6">6</a></h1>
 <p>     ... Ἕλθε, Κύπρι,<br>
 Χπρυσίασιν ἐν κυλίκεσσιν ἄβραισ<br>
 συμμεμιγμένον θαλίαισι νέκταρ<br>
      οἰνοχόεισα. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#10">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>Come, goddess of Cyprus, and in golden cups serve nectar delicately mixed with delights.</p>
 <p>Come hither foam-born Cyprian goddess, come,<br>
 And in golden goblets pour richest nectar<br>
 All mixed in most ethereal perfection,<br>
      Thus to delight us.</p>
 <p>Quoted by Athenaeus, who wrote in the first half of the third century A.D. The fragment is apparently part of an invocation to Aphrodite.</p>
