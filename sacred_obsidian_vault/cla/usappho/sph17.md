 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM16">16</a></h1>
 <p>ταισι [δὲ] ψῦχροσ μέν ἔγεντο θῦμοσ<br>
 πὰρ δ᾽ ἴεισι τὰ πτέρα ... <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#23">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>But the spirit within them turned chill and down dropped their wings.</p>
 <p>The Scholist quotes this to show that Sappho says the same thing of doves as Pindar (Pyth. 1-10) says of the eagle of Zeus.</p>
 <p>Another reading is ψαῦκροσ <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#24">[transcription]</a></font>
 , "light", for ψῦχροσ <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#25">[transcription]</a></font>
 , "moist or chill." The sense would then be "the spirit within them became light and they relaxed their wings in rest."</p>
