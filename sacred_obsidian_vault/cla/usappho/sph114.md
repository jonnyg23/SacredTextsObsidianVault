 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM113">113</a></h1>
 <p>Τιμάδοσ ἄδε κόνισ, τὰν δὴ ρπὸ γάμοιο θανοῦσαν<br>
      δέξατο φερσεφόνασ κύανεοσ θάλαμοσ,<br>
 ασ καὶ ἐποφθιμένασ πᾶσαι νεοθᾶγι σιδάρῳ<br>
      ἄλικεσ ιμμερτὰν κρᾶτοσ ἔθεντο κόμαν. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#145">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>This is the dust of Timas whom the dark chamber of Persephone recieved, dead before her wedding; when she died all her companions clipped with sharpened metal all their lovely tresses.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>Here rests the dust of Timas who, unwed,<br>
 Passed the dark portals of Persephone.<br>
 With sharpened metal, when her spirit fled,<br>
 Her mourning friends each shore her fair-tressed head.</p>
 <p>The version of J.A. Symonds is as follows:</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>This is the dust of Timas, whom, unwed,<br>
 Persephone locked in her darksome bed:<br>
 For her, the maids who were her fellows, shore<br>
 Their curls and to her tomb this tribute bore.</p>
 <p>The verse is from the Greek Anthology.</p>
