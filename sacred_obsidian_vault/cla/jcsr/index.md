 <p>
 This a parallel presentation of the works of Julius Caesar in Latin and
 English translation.
 This includes his <cite>Gallic Wars</cite>
 and <cite>Civil Wars</cite>, plus three shorter works which
 may have been written by Aulus Hirtius (who is also credited with the 8th
 book of the <cite>Gallic Wars</cite>).
 Beyond its importance as a source document for military and Roman history,
 Caesar's clean prose style has long made his <cite>Gallic Wars</cite> the
 text of choice for second-year Latin.
 It is also of interest because of his first-hand observations of the
 Celtic tribes that he was waging war on.
 For instance, refer to <a href="dbg6.htm">Book 6</a>, which contains
 <a href="dbg6.htm#dbg_6_11">a long passage about Gaulish society</a>,
 <a href="dbg6.htm#dbg_6_13">the Druids</a>,
 and his famous description
 of the original <a href="dbg6.htm#dbg_6_16">burning man ritual</a>.
