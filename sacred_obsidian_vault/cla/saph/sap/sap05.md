 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">II. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The temple of Chastity.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">High</span> on a rock, coaeval with the skies,<br> A Temple stands, reard by immortal powrs<br> To Chastity divine! ambrosial flowrs<br> Twining round icicles, in columns rise,<br> Mingling with pendent gems of orient dyes!<br> Piercing the air, a golden crescent towrs,<br> Veild by transparent clouds; while smiling hours<br> Shake from their varying wingscelestial joys!<br> The steps of spotless marble, scatterd oer<br> With deathless roses armd with many a thorn,<br> Lead to the altar. On the frozen floor,<br> Studded with tear-drops petrified by scorn,<br> Pale vestals kneel the Goddess to adore,<br> While Love, his arrows broke, retires forlorn.</p>
