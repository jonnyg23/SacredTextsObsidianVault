 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXVI. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Her confirmed Despair.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lead</span> me, Sicilian Maids, to haunted bowrs,<br> While yon pale moon displays her faintest beams<br> Oer blasted woodlands, and enchanted streams,<br> Whose banks infect the breeze with poisnous flowrs.<br> Ah! lead me, where the barren mountain towrs,<br> Where no sounds echo, but the night-owls screams,<br> Where some lone spirit of the desart gleams,<br> And lurid horrors wing the fateful hours!<br> Now goaded frenzy grasps my shrinking brain,<br> Her touch absorbs the crystal fount of woe!<br> My blood rolls burning through each gasping vein;<br> Away, lost Lyre! unless thou canst bestow<br> A charm, to lull that agonizing pain,<br> Which those who never lovd, can never know!</p>
