 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XIV. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">To the Aeolian Harp.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Come</span>, soft Aeolian harp, while zephyr plays<br> Along the meek vibration of thy strings,<br> As twilights hand her modest mantle brings,<br> Blending with sober grey, the western blaze!<br> O! prompt my Phaons dreams with tendrest lays,<br> Ere night oer shade thee with its humid wings,<br> While the lorn Philomel his sorrow sings<br> In leafy cradle, red with parting rays!<br> Slow let thy dulcet tones on ether glide,<br> So steals the murmur of the amrous dove;<br> The mazy legions swarm on evry side,<br> To lulling sounds the sunny people move!<br> Let not the wise their little world deride,<br> The smallest sting can wound the breast of Love.</p>
