 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXV. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Reproaches Phaon.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">What</span> means the mist opake that veils these eyes;<br> Why does yon threatning tempest shroud the day?<br> Why does thy altar, Venus, fade away,<br> And on my breast the dews of horror rise?<br> Phaon is false! be dim ye orient Skies;<br> And let black Erebus succeed your ray;<br> Let clashing thunders roll, and lightning play;<br> Phaon is false! and hopeless Sappho dies!<br> "Farewell! my Lesbian love, you might have said,"<br> Such sweet remembrance had some pity provd,<br> "Or coldly this, farewell, Oh! Lesbian maid!"<br> No task severe, for one so fondly lovd!<br> The gentle thought had soothd my wandring shade,<br> From lifes dark valley, and its thorns removd!</p>
