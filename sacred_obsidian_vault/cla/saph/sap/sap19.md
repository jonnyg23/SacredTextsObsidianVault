 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XVI. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sappho rejects Hope.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Delusive</span> Hope! more transient than the ray<br> That leads pale twilight to her dusky bed,<br> Oer woodland glen, or breezy mountains head,<br> Lingring to catch the parting sigh of day.<br> Hence with thy visionary charms, away!<br> Nor oer my path the flowrs of fancy spread;<br> Thy airy dreams on peaceful pillows shed,<br> And weave for thoughtless brows, a garland gay.<br> Farewell low vallies; dizzy cliffs, farewell!<br> Small vagrant rills that murmur as ye flow:<br> Dark bosomd labyrinth and thorny dell;<br> The task be mine all pleasures to forego;<br> To hide, where meditation loves to dwell,<br> And feed my soul, with luxury of woe!</p>
