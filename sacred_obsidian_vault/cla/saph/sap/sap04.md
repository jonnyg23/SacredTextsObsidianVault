 <p> </p>  <h3 align="center">I. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Introductory.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Favourd</span> by Heavn are those, ordaind to taste<br> The bliss supreme that kindles fancys fire;<br> Whose magic fingers sweep the muses lyre,<br> In varying cadence, eloquently chaste!<br> Well may the mind, with tuneful numbers gracd,<br> To Fames immortal attributes aspire,<br> Above the treachrous spells of low desire,<br> That wound the sense, by vulgar joys debasd.<br> For thou, blest <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Poesy</span>! with godlike powrs<br> To calm the miseries of man wert givn;<br> When passion rends, and hopeless love devours,<br> By memry goaded, and by frenzy drivn,<br> Tis thine to guide him midst Elysian bowrs,<br> And shew his fainting soul,a glimpse of Heavn.</p>
