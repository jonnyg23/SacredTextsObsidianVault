 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VI. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Describes the characteristics of Love.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Is</span> it to love, to fix the tender gaze,<br> To hide the timid blush, and steal away;<br> To shun the busy world, and waste the day<br> In some rude mountains solitary maze?<br> Is it to chant one name in ceaseless lays,<br> To hear no words that other tongues can say,<br> To watch the pale moons melancholy ray,<br> To chide in fondness, and in folly praise?<br> Is it to pour th involuntary sigh,<br> To dream of bliss, and wake new pangs to prove;<br> To talk, in fancy, with the speaking eye,<br> Then start with jealousy, and wildly rove;<br> Is it to loathe the light, and wish to die?<br> For these I feel,and feel that they are Love.</p>
