 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXIII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Reaches Sicily.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">I Wake</span>! delusive phantoms hence, away!<br> Tempt not the weakness of a lovers breast;<br> The softest breeze can shake the halcyons nest,<br> And lightest clouds oercast the dawning ray!<br> Twas but a vision! Now, the star of day<br> Peers, like a gem on Aetnas burning crest!<br> Wellcome, ye Hills, with golden vintage drest;<br> Sicilian forests brown, and vallies gay!<br> A mournful stranger, from the Lesbian Isle,<br> Not strange, in loftiest eulogy of Song!<br> She, who could teach the Stoics cheek to smile,<br> Thaw the cold heart, and chain the wondring throng,<br> Can find no balm, loves arrows to beguile;<br> Ah! Sorrows known too soon! and felt too long!</p>
