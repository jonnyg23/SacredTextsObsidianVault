 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">III. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Bower of Pleasure.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Turn</span> to yon vale beneath, whose tangled shade<br> Excludes the blazing torch of noon-day light,<br> Where sportive Fawns, and dimpled Loves invite,<br> The bowr of Pleasure opens to the glade:<br> Lulld by soft flutes, on leaves of violets laid,<br> There witching beauty greets the ravishd sight,<br> More gentle than the arbitress of night<br> In all her silvry panoply arrayd!<br> The birds breathe bliss! light zephyrs kiss the ground,<br> Stealing the hyacinths divine perfume;<br> While from the pellucid fountains glittring round,<br> Small tinkling rills bid rival flowrets bloom!<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Here</span>, laughing Cupids bathe the bosoms wound;<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">There</span>, tyrant passion finds a glorious tomb!</p>
