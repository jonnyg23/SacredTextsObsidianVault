 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXIII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sappho's Conjectures.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">To</span> Ætnas scorching sands my Phaon flies!<br> False Youth! can other charms attractive prove?<br> Say, can Sicilian loves thy passions move,<br> Play round thy heart, and fix thy fickle eyes,<br> While in despair the Lesbian Sappho dies?<br> Has Spring for thee a crown of poppies wove,<br> Or dost thou languish in th Idalian grove,<br> Whose altar kindles, fannd by Lovers sighs?<br> Ah! think, that while on Ætnas shores you stray,<br> A fire, more fierce than Ætnas, fills my breast;<br> Nor deck Sicilian nymphs with garlands gay,<br> While Sapphos brows with cypress wreaths are drest;<br> Let one kind word my weary woes repay,<br> Or, in eternal slumbers bid them rest.</p>
