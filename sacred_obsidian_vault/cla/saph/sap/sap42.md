 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXIX. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">To the Muses.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Prepare</span> your wreaths, Aonian maids divine,<br> To strew the tranquil bed where I shall sleep;<br> In tears, the myrtle and the laurel steep,<br> And let Erato's hand the trophies twine.<br> No parian marble, there, with labour'd line,<br> Shall bid the wand'ring lover stay to weep;<br> There holy silence shall her vigils keep.<br> Save, when the nightingale such woes as mine<br> Shall sadly sing; as twilight's curtains spread,<br> There shall the branching lotos widely wave,<br> Sprinkling soft show'rs upon the lily's head,<br> Sweet drooping emblem for a lover's grave!<br> And there shall Phaon pearls of pity shed,<br> To gem the vanquish'd heart he scorn'd to save!</p>
