 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XV. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Phaon awakes.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Now</span>, round my favord grot let roses rise,<br> To strew the bank where Phaon wakes from rest;<br> O! happy buds! to kiss his burning breast,<br> And die, beneath the lustre of his eyes!<br> Now, let the timbrels echo to the skies, <br> Now damsels sprinkel cassia on his vest, <br> With odrous wreaths of constant myrtle drest,<br> And flowrs, deep tinted with the rainbows dyes!<br> From cups of porphyry let nectar flow,<br> Rich as the perfume of Phoenicias vine!<br> Now let his dimpling cheek with rapture glow,<br> While round his heart loves mystic fetters twine;<br> And let the Grecian Lyre its aid bestow,<br> In songs of triumph, to proclaim him mine!</p>
