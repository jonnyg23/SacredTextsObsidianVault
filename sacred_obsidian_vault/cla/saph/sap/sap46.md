 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLIII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Her Reflections on the Leucadian Rock before she perishes.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">While</span> from the dizzy precipice I gaze,<br> The world receding from my pensive eyes,<br> High oer my head the tyrant eagle flies,<br> Clothd in the sinking suns transcendent blaze!<br> The meek-eyd moon, midst clouds of amber plays<br> As oer the purpling plains of light she hies,<br> Till the last stream of living lustre dies,<br> And the cool concave owns her temperd rays!<br> So shall this glowing, palpitating soul,<br> Welcome returning Reasons placid beam,<br> While oer my breast the waves Lethean roll,<br> To calm rebellious Fancys fevrish dream;<br> Then shall my Lyre disdain loves dread control,<br> And loftier passions, prompt the loftier theme!</p>
