 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXVII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sappho's Address to the Stars.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Oh</span>! ye bright Stars! that on the Ebon fields<br> Of Heavns empire, trembling seems to stand;<br> Till rosy morn unlocks her portal bland,<br> Where the proud Sun his fiery banner wields!<br> To flames, less fierce than mine, your lustre yields,<br> And powrs more strong my countless tears command;<br> Love strikes the feeling heart with ruthless hand,<br> And only spares the breast which dullness shields!<br> Since, then, capricious nature but bestows<br> The fine affections of the soul, to prove<br> A keener sense of desolating woes,<br> Far, far from me the empty boast remove;<br> If bliss from coldness, pain from passion flows,<br> Ah! who would wish to feel, or learn to love?</p>
