 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XI. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Rejects the Influence of Reason.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">O! Reason</span>! vaunted Sovreign of the mind!<br> Thou pompous vision with a sounding name!<br> Canst thou, the souls rebellious passions tame!<br> Canst thou in spells the vagrant fancy bind?<br> Ah, no! capricious as the wavring wind,<br> Are sighs of Love that dim thy boasted flame, <br> While Follys torch consumes the wreath of fame,<br> And Pleasures hands the sheaves of truth unbind.<br> Pressd by the storms of Fate, hope shrinks and dies;<br> Frenzy darts forth in mightiest ills arrayd;<br> Around thy throne destructive tumults rise,<br> And hell-fraught jealousies, thy rights invade!<br> Then, what art thou? O! Idol of the wise!<br> A visionary theme!a gorgeous shade!</p>
