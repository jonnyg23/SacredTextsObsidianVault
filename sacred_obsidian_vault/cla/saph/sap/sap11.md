 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VIII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Her Passion increases.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Why</span>, through each aching vein, with lazy pace<br> Thus steals the languid fountain of my heart, <br> While, from its source, each wild convulsive start<br> Tears the scorchd roses from my burning face?<br> In vain, O Lesbian Vales! your charms I trace;<br> Vain is the poets theme, the sculptors art;<br> No more the Lyre its magic can impart,<br> Though wakd to sound, with more than mortal grace!<br> Go, tuneful maids, go bid my Phaon prove<br> That passion mocks the empty boast of fame;<br> Tell him no joys are sweet, but joys of love,<br> Melting the soul, and thrilling all the frame!<br> Oh! may th ecstatic thought in bosom move,<br> And sighs of rapture, fan the blush of shame!</p>
