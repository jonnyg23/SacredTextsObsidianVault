 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXX. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bids farewell to Lesbos.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Oer</span> the tall cliff that bounds the billowy main<br> Shadwing the surge that sweeps the lonely strand,<br> While the thin vapours break along the sand,<br> Days harbinger unfolds the liquid plain.<br> The rude Sea murmurs, mournful as the strain<br> That love-lorn minstrels strike with trembling hand,<br> While from their green beds rise the Syren band<br> With tongues aerial to repeat my pain!<br> The vessel rocks beside the pebbly shore,<br> The foamy curls its gaudy trappings lave;<br> Oh! Bark propitious! bear me gently oer,<br> Breathe soft, ye winds; rise slow, O! swelling wave!<br> Lesbos; these eyes shall meet thy sands no more:<br> I fly, to seek my Lover, or my Grave!</p>
