 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XIII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">She endeavours to fascinate him.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bring</span>, bring to deck my brow, ye Sylvan girls,<br> A roseate wreath; nor for my waving hair<br> The costly band of studded gems prepare,<br> Of sparkling crysolite or orient pearls:<br> Love, oer my head his canopy unfurls,<br> His purple pinions fan the whispring air;<br> Mocking the golden sandal, rich and rare, <br> Beneath my feet the fragrant woodbine curls.<br> Bring the thin robe, to fold about my breast,<br> White as the downy swan; while round my waist<br> Let leaves of glossy myrtle bind the vest,<br> Not idly gay, but elegantly chaste!<br> Love scorns the nymph in wanton trappings drest; <br> And charms the most concealed, are doubly gracd.</p>
