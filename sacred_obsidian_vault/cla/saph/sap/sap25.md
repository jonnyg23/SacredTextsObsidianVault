 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Phaon forsakes her.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Wild</span> is the foaming Sea! The surges roar!<br> And nimbly dart the livid lightnings round!<br> On the rent rock the angry waves rebound;<br> Ah me! the lessning bark is seen no more!<br> Along the margin of the trembling shore,<br> Loud as the blast my frantic cries shall sound,<br> My storm-drenchd limbs the flinty fragments wound,<br> And oer my bleeding breast the billows pour!<br> Phaon! return! ye winds, O! waft the strain<br> To his swift bark; ye barbrous waves forbear!<br> Taunt not the anguish of a lovers brain,<br> Nor feebly emulate the souls despair!<br> For howling winds, and foaming seas, in vain<br> Assail the breast, when passion rages there!</p>
