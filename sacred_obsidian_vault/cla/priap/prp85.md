 <h1 align="CENTER">85</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">By Marcus Valerius Martialis</h3>
 <p>Non horti neque palmitis beati<br>
 sed rari nemoris, Priape, custos,<br>
 ex quo natus es et potes renasci,<br>
 furaces, moneo, manus repellas<br>
 et siluam domini focis reserues:<br>
 si defecerit haec, et ipse lignum es.</p>
 <p>Neither of garden nor of blessèd vine<br>
 But of a little holt (Priapus!) guard,<br>
 Wherein wast born and may'st be born again;<br>
 I warn thee plundering hand alway repel<br>
 And keep the fuel for thy master's fire--<br>
 An this be wanting, mind! of wood thou art.</p>
 <p>Priapus, guardian not of a garden nor of the sacred vine but of the little grove from which thou wert born and mayst again be born. I warn thee drive off thievish hands and preserve the wood for thy master's hearth. If this be wanting, remember thou too art wooden.[1]</p>
