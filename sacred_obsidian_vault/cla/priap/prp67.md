 <h1 align="CENTER">67</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Penelopes primam Didonis prima sequatur<br>
    et primam Cadmi syllaba prima Remi,<br>
 quodque fit ex illis, mihi tu deprensus in horto,<br>
    fur, dabis: hac poena culpa luenda tua est.</p>
 <b></b><p>PEnelope's first syllable followed by firstling of <b>DI</b>do<br>
 Take, and of <b>CA</b>mus--the front also of <b>RE</b>mus the head.<br>
 Whatso thou makest of these unto me when caught in my orchard<br>
 Thief thou shalt give, such pain shall for thy thieving atone.</p>
 <p>Let the first syllable of <b>PE</b>nelope be followed by the first of <b>DI</b>do, the first of <b>CA</b>mus by that of <b>RE</b>mus.[1] What is made from these thou to me, when caught in my garden, O thief, shalt give; by this punishment thy fault is atoned for.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. PE-DI-CA-RE--<i>pedicare</i>, meaning to sodomise.]</p>
