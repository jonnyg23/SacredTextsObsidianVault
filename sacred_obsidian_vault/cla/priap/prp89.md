 <h1 align="CENTER">89</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">also by Caius Valerius Catullus</h3>
 <p>Hunc lucum tibi dedico consecroque, Priape,<br>
 qua domus tua Lampsaci est quaque cella, Priape.<br>
 Nam te praecipue in suis urbibus colit ora<br>
 Hellespontia, ceteris ostrior oris.</p>
 <p>This grove to thee devote I give, Priapus!<br>
 Whose home be Lampsacus and holt, Priapus!<br>
 For thee in cities worship most the shores<br>
 Of Hellespont the richest oystery strand.</p>
 <p>This grove I dedicate and consecrate to thee, Priapus, who hast thy home at Lampsacus, and eke thy wood lands, Priapus; for thee especially in its cities worships the coast of the Hellespont, richer in oysters[1] than all other shores.</p>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">End of the Epigrams</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. Oysters being an incentive to lust. Juvenal writes: 'She knows no difference 'twixt head and privities who devours immense oysters at midnight.']</p>
