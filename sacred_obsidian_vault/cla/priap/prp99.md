 <h1 align="CENTER">List of terms used in the Priapeia to designate the female sexual organ</h1>
 <i></i><p>Cunnus--a coynte<br>
 <i>Cunus albus</i>--coynte clothed in white, a prostitute<br>
 <i>Cunnus consule natus</i>--a woman of noble birth<br>
 <i>Cunnus garrulus</i>--a chattering coynte[1]<br>
 <i>Cunnus osseus</i>--a bony coynte, that of an old woman<br>
 <i>Cunni gemini</i>--twin coyntes, tribades<br>
 <i>Fores</i>--an entrance, gates<br>
 <i>Fossa</i>--a ditch; is also employed to designate the posteriors of a catamite<br>
 <i>Inguina</i>--loins<br>
 <i>Locus</i>--a place<br>
 <i>Media</i>--the middle<br>
 <i>Specus</i>--a cavity</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. One which utters a clucking sound (<i>poppysma</i>) during coition by reason of its moistness.</p>
 <p>When th' hast a face of which no woman may<br>
 And body without blur, have ought to say,<br>
 Why suitors thee so seldom do repeat<br>
 And seek dost wonder, Galla? the fault's great,<br>
 As oft as thou and I in the work join'd,<br>
 Thy lips; were silent, but thou prat'st behind.<br>
 Heavens grant that thou wouldst speak, but bridle that,<br>
 I'm angry with thy tatling twit come twat.<br>
 I'd rather hear thee fart, for Symmachus<br>
 Says that's a means of laughter unto us.<br>
 But who can smile to hear the foolish smack<br>
 Of thy loose toul? and when it gives a crack<br>
 Whose mind and mettle will not fall? at least<br>
 Speak something that may usher in a jest<br>
 Of thy Coynte's noise; but if thou art so mute,<br>
 Articulately learn thence to dispute.--Fletcher's <i>Martial</i>]</p>
