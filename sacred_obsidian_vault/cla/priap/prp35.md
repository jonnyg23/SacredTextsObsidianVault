 <h1 align="CENTER">35</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Pedicabere, fur, semel; sed idem<br>
 si deprensus eris bis, irrumabo.<br>
 quod si tertia furta molieris,<br>
 ut poenam patiare et hanc et illam,<br>
 pedicaberis irrumaberisque.</p>
 <p>Thief, for first thieving shalt be swived, but an<br>
 Again arrested shalt be irrumate;<br>
 And, shouldst attempt to plunder time the third,<br>
 This and that penalty thou shalt endure,<br>
 Being both pedicate and irrumate.</p>
 <p>Thou shalt be bardashed,[1] thief, for the first theft; and if twice caught, I will irrumate thee. But if thou shalt attempt a third theft, that thou mayst suffer penalties twain, I will both sodomise and irrumate thee.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. <i>Bardache</i>, meaning a catamite. Italian <i>bardascia</i>, from the Arabic <i>baradaj</i>, a captive, a slave. The old English was <i>ingle</i> or <i>yngle</i> (a bardachio, a catamite, a boy kept for sodomy). In Latin Bulgarus means a Bulgarian or a heretic, from which our vulgar modern word 'bugger' is derived, as is the Italian <i>bugiardo </i>and the French <i>bougre</i>.]</p>
