 <h1 align="CENTER">68</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Rusticus indocte si quid dixisse videbor,<br>
    da veniam: libros non lego, poma lego.<br>
 sed rudis hic dominum totiens audire legentem<br>
    cogor Homereas edidicique notas.<br>
 ille vocat, quod nos 'psolen', 'psoloenta keraunon',<br>
    et quod nos culum, 'kouleon' ille vocat.<br>
 'merdaleon' certe nisi res non munda vocatur,<br>
    et pediconum mentula merdalea est.</p>
 <p>An I rustical seem to have spoken somewhat unlearned,<br>
 Pardon me: apples I pluck, pluck I no matter of books;<br>
 Yet in my rudeness ok when hearing the dominie reading,<br>
 Stood I storing in mind much of Homerical lore.<br>
 'Psoleon' fain he calls what we 'Psoloenta' be calling;<br>
 What we 'Culum' name, 'Culeon' loves he to term;<br>
 'Smerdaleos' forsure designs what is nothing too cleanly<br>
 And is the Pedicon's yard rightly 'Smerdalea' hight.</p>
 <p>If I, a rustic, shall seem to say anything unlearnedly, pardon me: I gather not knowledge from books, I gather apples. But, untaught, I have often listened to my master, who constantly reads here, and have learnt by heart the Homeric vocabulary. He calls <i>psolen</i> [the virile member], what we call <i>psoloenta</i>. What we call <i>culum</i> [the fundament], he <i>culeon</i>. And surely, unless an unclean thing be called <i>smerdalos</i> [<i>merde</i>], the mentule of a sodomist is <i>smerdalea</i>.</p>
