 <h2 align="CENTER">Explanatory and Illustrative and Excursus</h2>
 <h3 align="CENTER">A list of terms used in the Priapeia as designations of Priapus</h3>
 <i></i><p>Assiduus custos ruris--assiduous protector of the farm<br>
 <i>Custos horti</i>--the garden protector<br>
 <i>Custos hortorum</i>--protector of gardens<br>
 <i>Custos rari nemoris</i>--guardian of a little grove<br>
 <i>Destitutor inguinum</i>--forsaker of the inguinal organ<br>
 <i>Deus</i>--god<br>
 <i>Deus posito pudore</i>--deity without shame<br>
 <i>Deus minor</i>--inferior deity<br>
 <i>Ficus</i>--fig tree<br>
 <i>Ligneus</i>--wooden<br>
 <i>Ligneus custos cucurbitarum</i>--wooden guardian of gourds<br>
 <i>Ligneus deus</i>--wooden god<br>
 <i>Marmoreus Priapus</i>--a marble Priapus<br>
 <i>Membrosior aequo</i>--larger membered than is usual<br>
 <i>Mentulatus deus</i>--the god with well-hung mentule<br>
 <i>Nervus tentus Priapus</i>--stiff-nerved Priapus<br>
 <i>Nudus Priapus</i>--naked Priapus<br>
 <i>Numen pusillum ruris culti</i>--a paltry rustic deity of scant culture<br>
 <i>Numen vilius inter cunctos deos</i>--the lowest deity amongst all the gods<br>
 <i>Pater</i>--father<br>
 <i>Rigidus deus</i>--the god of the standing prickle, the rigid god<br>
 <i>Ruber</i>--ruddy<br>
 <i>Ruber et valens</i>--ruddy and vigorous<br>
 <i>Ruber hortorum custos</i>--the ruddy protector of gardens<br>
 <i>Rusticus custos</i>--rustic guardian<br>
 <i>Salax deus</i>--lecherous god<br>
 <i>Sanctus</i>--venerable, sacred, holy one<br>
 <i>Sanctus Priapus</i>--sacred Priapus<br>
 <i>Siligineus Priapus</i>--a wheaten Priapus<br>
 <i>Terribilis deus</i>--the awe-inspiring god<br>
 <i>Tutela diligens pomarii</i>--Faithful protector of orchards<br>
 <i>Triphallus</i>--a threefold phallus<br>
 <i>Vappa</i>--a good-for-naught</p>
