 <h1 align="CENTER">33</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Naidas antiqui Dryadasque habuere Priapi,<br>
    et quo tenta dei vena subiret, erat.<br>
 nunc adeo nihil est, adeo mea plena libido est,<br>
    ut Nymphas omnis interiisse putem.<br>
 turpe quidem factu, sed ne tentigine rumpar,<br>
    falce mihi posita fiet amica manus.</p>
 <p>Wont the Priapi of old were to have both Naiads and Dryads<br>
 And the stiff vein of the God all had what causes to droop;<br>
 Now there's naught of the kind; now so fulfilled my desire is<br>
 Fain am I left to believe every Nymph to be dead.<br>
 Vile thing 'twere to be done, but lest I burst me with straining<br>
 Sickle unhanding I mistress must make of my hand.</p>
 <p>Of old, the Priapi enjoyed the Naiads and Dryads, and there was the wherewithal to cause the swollen vein of the God to droop. Now there is naught of this; moreover, I am so full of desire that I think the whole of the Nymphs have perished. 'Tis doubtless an unseemly thing to do, but lest I burst with the excessive tension [of my member], my hand, the sickle laid aside, shall become my mistress.</p>
