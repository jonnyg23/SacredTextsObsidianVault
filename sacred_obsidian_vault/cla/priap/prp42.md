 <h1 align="CENTER">42</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Laetus Aristagoras natis bene vilicus uvis<br>
    de cera facili dat tibi poma, deus.<br>
 at tu sacrati contentus imagine pomi<br>
    fac veros fructus ille, Priape, ferat.</p>
 <p>Bailiff Aristagoras of his grapes high-pedigree'd boasting<br>
 Apples moulded in wax giveth, O Godhead, to thee:<br>
 But thou, pleased with the fruit in effigy placed on thine altar,<br>
 Genuine 'fruit' vouchsafe he, O Priapus! shall bear.</p>
 <p>The steward Aristagoras, rejoicing in his promising grapes, offers to thee, O God, apples formed from wax. Do thou, O Priapus, contented with the semblance of a votive apple, cause him to bear genuine fruit.[1]</p>
