 <h1 align="CENTER">65</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Hic tibi, qui rostro crescentia lilia mersit,<br>
    caeditur e tepida victima porcus hara:<br>
 ne tamen exanimum facias pecus omne, Priape,<br>
    horti sit, facias, ianua clausa tui.</p>
 <p>This, with his snout aye alert to uproot the lilies a-blowing,<br>
 Slain for thy victim 's the pig bred in the stye's tepid reek.<br>
 But, an thy will be not to murther the herd, O Priapus,<br>
 Grant of thy grace yon gate into the garden be shut.</p>
 <p>This pig, which has crushed the rising lilies with his snout, is sacrificed to thee--a victim warm from the stye. But lest thou shouldst cause the whole herd to be annihilated, O Priapus, bid the gate of thy garden to be closed.[2]</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. The effeminate come, allured by the pleasure of being sodomised.</p>
 <p>2. The priestesses of Priapus were sometimes represented in marble sculptures as clothed with hides of swine.]</p>
