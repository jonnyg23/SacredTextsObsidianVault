 <p>I have such papers that grim Cato's wife<br>
 May read, and strictest Sabines in their life.<br>
 I will this book should laugh throughout and jest,<br>
 And be more wicked than are all the rest,<br>
 And sweat with wine, and with rich unguents flow,<br>
 And sport with boys, and with the wenches too;<br>
 Nor by periphrasis describe that thing<br>
 That common parent whence we all do spring;<br>
 Which sacred Numa once a prick did call.<br>
 Yet still suppose these verses Saturnal.<br>
 (O my Apollinaris) this my book<br>
 Has no dissembled manners, no feign'd look.</p>
 <p align="RIGHT">--FLETCHER'S <i>Martial</i></p>
