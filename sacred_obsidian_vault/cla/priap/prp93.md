 <h1 align="CENTER">93</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">On the Priapus of Hilarus</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">also by Marcus Valerius Martialis</h3>
 <p>Non rudis indocta fecit me falce colonus:<br>
     dispensatoris nobile cernis opus.<br>
 Nam Caeretani cultor ditissimus agri<br>
     hos Hilarus colles et iuga laeta tenet.<br>
 Aspice quam certo uidear non ligneus ore<br>
     nec deuota focis inguinis arma geram,<br>
 sed mihi perpetua numquam moritura cupresso<br>
     Phidiaca rigeat mentula digna manu.<br>
 Vicini, moneo, sanctum celebrate Priapum<br>
     et bis septenis parcite iugeribus.</p>
 <p>Carved me no rustic boor his artless sickle a-plying:<br>
 Here of the bailiff thou see'st noble and notable work;<br>
 For that the wealthiest swain who owns the lands Caërétan<br>
 (Hilarus) holds these hills sloping in sunniest folds.<br>
 See with my well-shaped face how seem I not to be wooden,<br>
 Nor do I bear belly-tools fitted for kitchen or fire:<br>
 Nay; my perpetual yard of cypress perishing never<br>
 Rises for ever and aye worthy the Phidian hand.<br>
 You, O ye neighbours, I warn to adore me, holy Priapus,<br>
 And to these acres twice seven show ye the highest respect.</p>
 <p>No ignorant peasant shaped me with unskilful sickle: the noble handywork of the steward thou perceivest. For the most influential cultivator of the Caeretan lands, Hilarus, owns these hills and smiling slopes. Behold, with well-shaped features I do not seem to be wooden, nor belly-weapons devoted to the kitchen-fire do I bear; but my imperishable mentule of undying cypress, worthy the hand of Phidias, stiffly raises itself. Neighbours, I warn you, worship the sacred Priapus, and these fourteen acres respect.</p>
