 <h1 align="CENTER">List of terms used in the Priapeia to designate the virile member of Priapus[1]</h1>
 <i></i><p>Arma inguinis--inguinal arms, belly weapons<br>
 <i>Arma ventris</i>--belly weapons<br>
 <i>Caput lubricum</i>--lubricious head<br>
 <i>Columna</i>--column<br>
 <i>Contus pedalis</i>--twelve-inch pole<br>
 <i>Cupressus</i>--cypress<br>
 <i>E D temoque</i>--the letters E and D with a pole or hyphen between them thus E-D<br>
 <i>Fascinum</i>--a bewitching, an enchantment<br>
 <i>Fascinum pedale</i>--twelve-inch <i>fascinum</i><br>
 <i>Fascinum rubens</i>--ruddy <i>fascinum</i><br>
 <i>Fascinum tentum</i>--turgid <i>fascinum</i><br>
 <i>Hasta</i>--spear<br>
 <i>Hasta rudis</i>--rude spear<br>
 <i>Inguen</i>--middle, groin, privities<br>
 <i>Inguen obscaenum</i>--obscene groin<br>
 <i>Medium</i>--middle<br>
 <i>Membrum</i>--member<br>
 <i>Membrum seminale</i>--seminal member<br>
 <i>Mentula</i>--mentule<br>
 <i>Mentula crassa</i>--stupid mentule<br>
 <i>Mentula magna</i>--well-grown mentule<br>
 <i>Mentula perpetua</i>--imperishable mentule<br>
 <i>Mentu1a sine arte</i>--roughly shaped mentule<br>
 <i>Mentula tenta</i>--mentule at full stretch<br>
 <i>Mentinum rubrum</i>--red-painted amulet<br>
 <i>Natura</i>--nature<br>
 <i>Nervus tentus</i>--stiff nerve<br>
 <i>Palus ruber</i>--bloody-looking pole<br>
 <i>Pars</i>--part<br>
 <i>Pars major</i>--greater part<br>
 <i>Pars mei per quam significor Priapus esse</i>--the part by which I'm signified to be Priapus<br>
 <i>Pan obscaena</i>--privy part<br>
 <i>Penis</i>--tail<br>
 <i>Pyramis</i>--pyramid<br>
 <i>Sceptrum</i>--staff of office<br>
 <i>Signum impudentiae</i>--symbol of shamelessness<br>
 <i>Tela</i>--weapons<br>
 <i>Telum</i>--spear<br>
 <i>Vena tenta</i>--swollen vein<br>
 <i>Virilis nota</i>--virile sign<br>
 <i>Virile pars</i>--the virile member</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. In <i>The Perfumed Garden of the Sheikh Nefzaoui</i> thirty nine names in Arabic are given to the sexual organ of man and forty-three to that of woman.]</p>
