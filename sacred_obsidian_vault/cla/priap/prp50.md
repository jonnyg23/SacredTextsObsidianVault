 <h1 align="CENTER">50</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Quaedam, si placet hoc tibi, Priape,<br>
 fucosissima me puella ludit<br>
 et nec dat mihi nec negat daturam,<br>
 causas invenit usque differendi.<br>
 quae si contigerit fruenda nobis,<br>
 totam cum paribus, Priape, nostris<br>
 cingemus tibi mentulam coronis.</p>
 <p>A certain person, an thou please (Priapus!),<br>
 Plays me, a girl with piles full many piled;<br>
 And nor she gives me nor denies her gift,<br>
 While for deferring ever finds she cause.<br>
 But, if to 'joy her shall our lot befall,<br>
 We will (Priapus!) gird thy total yard<br>
 With the twin garlands to thy favours due.</p>
 <p>If it like thee, O Priapus, a certain girl, most sorely troubled with the piles, sports with me, and neither gives me nor denies her favours, but hitherto has found pretexts for deferring. If it shall to my lot to enjoy her, we will encircle the whole of thy mentule, O Priapus, with our twin garlands.</p>
