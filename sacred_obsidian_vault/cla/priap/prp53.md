 <h1 align="CENTER">53</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Contentus modico Bacchus solet esse racemo,<br>
    cum capiant alti vix cita musta lacus,<br>
 magnaque fecundis cum messibus area desit,<br>
    in Cereris crines una corona datur.<br>
 tu quoque, dive minor, maiorum exempla secutus,<br>
    quamvis pauca damus, consule poma boni.</p>
 <p>Bacchus often is wont with a moderate bunch to be sated,<br>
 When the deep brim-full vats hardly the must shall contain;<br>
 So when the threshing-floors all fail for the plentiful harvest<br>
 Ceres' ringlets to crown only one garland we bring.<br>
 Thou too, a minor god, example borrow from the major--<br>
 Though few apples we give, take thou our gift in good part.</p>
 <p>Bacchus is wont to be content with a modest cluster from the vine, even when the deep vats can barely contain the must. And when the spacious threshing floors are insufficient for the rich harvest, in Ceres' locks a single garland is wreathed. Do thou also, less potent deity, guided by their greater example, although our offering be only a few apples, take it in good part.</p>
