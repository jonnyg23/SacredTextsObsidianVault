 <h1 align="CENTER">36</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Notas habemus quisque corporis formas:<br>
 Phoebus comosus, Hercules lacertosus,<br>
 trahit figuram virginis tener Bacchus,<br>
 Minerva ravo lumine est, Venus paeto,<br>
 in fronte cornua Arcados vides Fauni,<br>
 habet decentes nuntius deum plantas,<br>
 tutela Lemni dispares movet gressus,<br>
 intonsa semper Aesculapio barba est,<br>
 nemo est feroci pectorosior Marte:<br>
 quod si quis inter hos locus mihi restat,<br>
 deus Priapo mentulatior non est.</p>
 <p>We all show special notes of bodily shape:<br>
 Long-haired is Phoebus, arm-strong Hercules,<br>
 And tender Bacchus owneth virginal form;<br>
 Pallas hath grey-blue eyes, Venus a cast;<br>
 Th' Arcadian Fauns thou seest bloody-browed<br>
 And the Gods' Messenger shows proper feet;<br>
 The Guard of Lemnos moves unequal steps;<br>
 Ever untrimmed is Aesculapius' beard;<br>
 None hath a broader breast than bully Mars;<br>
 But, an Priapus' rank 'mid these remain,<br>
 There be no better-membered deity.</p>
 <p>We have each distinguishing features in the formation of our bodies: in Phoebus 'tis luxuriant locks, in Hercules muscular power; and the effeminate Bacchus has the figure of a girl. Minerva's eye is light in colour, Venus's prettily blinking. You see the forehead of the Arcadian Fauns rubicund with colour. The messenger of the gods, [Mercury] has shapely feet, the guardian of Lemnos walks with an uneven step [the lame Vulcan] and Aesculapius always wears a never shaven beard. No man is more broad-chested than the warlike Mars; but if 'mid this array there remain any place for me, than Priapus no Deity hath a larger or better-hung mentule!</p>
