 <h1 align="CENTER">86</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Vere rosa, autumno pomis, aestate frequentor<br>
     spicis; una mihi est horrida pestis hiemps.<br>
 Nam frigus metuo et vereor, ne ligneus ignem<br>
     hic deus ignavis praebeat agricolis.</p>
 <p>Roses in spring in the autumn fruits and in summer they bring me<br>
 Wheat-ears, while to my mind winter is horrible pest;<br>
 For that the cold I dread lest I being god made of timber<br>
 End me as fuel for fire chopped by those ignorant boors.</p>
 <p>In spring I am worshipped with roses, in autumn with apples, in summer with corn-wreaths, but winter is one horrid pestilence for me. For I fear the cold, and am apprehensive lest I, a wooden god, should in that season afford a fire for ignorant yokels.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. For lack of the stolen wood, his master would burn the image of Priapus.]</p>
