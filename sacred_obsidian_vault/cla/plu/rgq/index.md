 <p>
 These are the Roman and Greek Questions of Plutarch, extracted
 from Babbitt's Loeb Classics translation of the <cite>Moralia</cite>.
 Plutarch, who was an initiated priest of Apollo of Delphi,
 here attempts to shed light on numerous ancient folklore enigmas.
 Some of these are apparently survivals from the distant past,
 and very signficant for the study of the evolution of classical paganism.
 This was one of the first attempts to explain folklore in both rational
 and mythopoetic terms, as well as a treasure trove of ancient lore
 which makes fascinating reading.
 </p>
