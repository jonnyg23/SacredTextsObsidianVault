 <h6 align="CENTER">XV.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO JUNO <a name="fr_76"></a><a href="#fn_76"><font size="1">*</font></a>.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from AROMATICS.</h6>
 <p>O Royal Juno of majestic mien,<br>
 Aerial-form'd, divine, Jove's blessed queen,<br>
 Thron'd in the bosom of cærulean air,<br>
 The race of mortals is thy constant care.</p>
 <p><a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 141</font></a></p>
 <p>The cooling gales thy pow'r alone inspires,<br>
 Which nourish life, which ev'ry life desires.<br>
 Mother of clouds and winds, from thee alone<br>
 Producing all things, mortal life is known:<br>
 All natures share thy temp'rament divine,<br>
 And universal sway alone is thine. 10<br>
 With founding blasts of wind, the swelling sea<br>
 And rolling rivers roar, when shook by thee.<br>
 Come, blessed Goddess, fam'd almighty queen,<br>
 With aspect kind, rejoicing and serene.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_76"></a><a href="hoo20.htm#fr_76">140:*</a> Juno is called by the Orphic theologers, according to Proclus Ζωογόνος ϑεά or the vivific Goddess: an epithet perfectly agreeing with the attributes ascribed to her in this Hymn. And in Theol. Plat. p. 483, he says that Juno is the source of the soul's procreation.</p>
