 <h6 align="CENTER">LXII.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO EQUITY.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from FRANKINCENSE.</h6>
 <p>O Blessed Equity, mankind's delight,<br>
 Th' eternal friend of conduct just and right:<br>
 Abundant, venerable, honor'd maid,<br>
 To judgments pure, dispensing constant aid,</p>
 <p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 195</font></a></p>
 <p>A stable conscience, and an upright mind;<br>
 For men unjust, by thee are undermin'd,<br>
 Whose souls perverse thy bondage ne'er desire,<br>
 But more untam'd decline thy scourges dire:<br>
 Harmonious, friendly power, averse to strife,<br>
 In peace rejoicing, and a stable life; 10<br>
 Lovely, loquacious, of a gentle mind,<br>
 Hating excess, to equal deeds inclin'd:<br>
 Wisdom, and virtue of whate'er degree,<br>
 Receive their proper bound alone in thee.<br>
 Hear, Goddess Equity, the deeds destroy 15<br>
 Of evil men, which human life annoy;<br>
 That all may yield to thee of mortal birth,<br>
 Whether supported by the fruits of earth,<br>
 Or in her kindly fertile bosom found,<br>
 or in the depths of Marine Jove profound. 20</p>
