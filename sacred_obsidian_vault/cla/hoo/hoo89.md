 <h6 align="CENTER">LXXXIV.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO SLEEP.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from a Poppy.</h6>
 <p>SLEEP, king of Gods, and men of mortal birth,<br>
 Sov'reign of all sustain'd by mother Earth;</p>
 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a></p>
 <p>For thy dominion is supreme alone,<br>
 O'er all extended, and by all things known.<br>
 'Tis thine all bodies with benignant mind 5<br>
 In other bands than those of brass to bind:<br>
 Tamer of cares, to weary toil repose,<br>
 From whom sweet solace in affliction flows.<br>
 Thy pleasing, gentle chains preserve the soul,<br>
 And e'en the dreadful cares of death controul; 10<br>
 For death and Lethe with oblivious stream,<br>
 Mankind thy genuine brothers justly deem.<br>
 With fav'ring aspect to my pray'r incline,<br>
 And save thy mystics in their works divine.</p>
