 <h6 align="CENTER">II.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO NIGHT.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION with TORCHES.</h6>
 <p>NIGHT, parent goddess, source of sweet repose,<br>
 From whom at first both Gods and men arose,<br>
 Hear, blessed Venus, deck'd with starry light, <a name="fr_46"></a><a href="#fn_46"><font size="1">3</font></a><br>
 In sleep's deep silence dwelling Ebon night!<br>
 Dreams and soft case attend thy dusky train, 5<br>
 Pleas'd with the length'ned gloom and <b>feaftful</b> strain.</p>
 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>
 <p>Dissolving anxious care, the friend of Mirth,<br>
 With darkling coursers riding round the earth.<br>
 Goddess of phantoms and of shadowy play,<br>
 Whose drowsy pow'r divides the nat'ral day: 10<br>
 By Fate's decree you constant send the light<br>
 To deepest hell, remote from mortal sight<br>
 For dire Necessity which nought withstands,<br>
 Invests the world with adamantine bands.<br>
 Be present, Goddess, to thy suppliant's pray'r, 15<br>
 Desir'd by all, whom all alike revere,<br>
 Blessed, benevolent, with friendly aid<br>
 Dispell the fears of Twilight's dreadful shade.</p>
