 <h6 align="CENTER">LXXII.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO THE DÆMON, OR GENIUS.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from FRANKINCENSE.</h6>
 <p>THEE, mighty-ruling, Dæmon dread, I call,<br>
 Mild Jove, life-giving, and the source of all:<br>
 Great Jove, much-wand'ring, terrible and strong,<br>
 To whom revenge and tortures dire belong.<br>
 Mankind from thee, in plenteous wealth abound, 5<br>
 When in their dwellings joyful thou art found;<br>
 Or pass thro' life afflicted and distress'd,<br>
 The needful means of bliss by thee supprest.<br>
 'Tis thine alone endu'd with boundless might,<br>
 To keep the keys of sorrow and delight. 10</p>
 <p><a name="page_304"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 304</font></a></p>
 <p>O holy, blessed father, hear my pray'r,<br>
 Disperse the seeds of life-consuming care;<br>
 With fav'ring mind the sacred rites attend,<br>
 And grant my days a glorious, blessed end.</p>
