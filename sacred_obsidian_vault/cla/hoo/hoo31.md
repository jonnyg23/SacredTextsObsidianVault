 <h6 align="CENTER">XXVI.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO THE MOTHER OF THE GODS.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from a Variety of ODORIFEROUS SUBSTANCES.</h6>
 <p>Mother of Gods, great nurse of all, draw near,<br>
 Divinely honor'd, and regard my pray'r:<br>
 Thron'd on a car, by lions drawn along,<br>
 By bull-destroying lions, swift and strong,<br>
 Thou sway'st the sceptre of the pole divine, 5  <a name="fr_82"></a><a href="#fn_82"><font size="1">5</font></a><br>
 And the world's middle seat, much-fam'd, is thine.<br>
 Hence earth is thine, and needy mortals share<br>
 Their constant food, from thy protecting care:<br>
 From thee at first both Gods and men arose;<br>
 From thee, the sea and ev'ry river flows. 10<br>
 Vesta, and source of good, thy name we find<br>
 To mortal men rejoicing to be kind;<br>
 For ev'ry good to give, <a href="errata.htm#3">thy</a> soul delights;<br>
 Come, mighty pow'r, propitious to our rites,<br>
 All-taming, blessed, Phrygian saviour, come, 15<br>
 Saturn's great queen, rejoicing in the drum.</p>
 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 152</font></a></p>
 <p>Celestial, ancient, life-supporting maid,<br>
 Fanatic Goddess, give thy suppliant aid;<br>
 With joyful aspect on our incense shine,<br>
 And, pleas'd, accept the sacrifice divine.</p>
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_82"></a><a href="hoo31.htm#fr_82">151:5</a> Ver. 5.] We have already observed, that the mother of the Gods is the same with Rhea; and Proclus, in the second book of his Commentary on Euclid, informs us, that the pole of the <a href="errata.htm#4">world</a> is called by the Pythagoreans the seal of Rhea.</p>
