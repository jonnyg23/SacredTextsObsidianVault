 <p><a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 213</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">NOTES.</h1>  <h1 align="center">PREFACE.</h1>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_1"><font color="GREEN">0_1</font></a>. We are indebted for more than one useful suggestion to our colleagues Messrs. Charles Michel and Joseph Bidez, who were kind enough to read the proofs of the French edition.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_2"><font color="GREEN">0_2</font></a>. An outline of the present state of the subject will be found in a recent volume by Gruppe, <i>Griechische Mythologie</i>, 1906, pp. 1606 ff., whose views are sharply opposed to the negative conclusions formulated, with certain reservations, by Harnack, <i>Ausbreitung des Christentums,</i> II, pp. 274 ff. Among the latest studies intended for the general reader that have appeared on this subject, may be mentioned in Germany those of Geffcken (<i>Aus der Werdezeit des Christentums, Leipsic</i>, 1904, pp. 114 ff .), and in England those of Cheyne (<i>Bible Problems</i>, 1904), who expresses his opinion in these terms: "The Christian religion is a synthesis, and only those who have dim eyes can assert that the intellectual empires of Babylonia and Persia have fallen."--Very useful is the new book of Clemen, <i>Religionsgeschichtliche Erklärung des Neuen Testaments</i>, Giessen, 1909.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_3"><font color="GREEN">0_3</font></a>. <i>Mon. myst. Mithra</i>, I, p. 342, n. 4; see the new texts commented on by Usener, <i>Rhein. Museum</i>, LX, 1905, pp. 466 ff.; 489 ff., and my paper "Natalis Invicti," <i>C. R. Acad. des inscr.</i>, 1911.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_4"><font color="GREEN">0_4</font></a>. See page <a href="orrp07.htm#page_70">70</a>. Compare also <i>Mon. myst. Mithra</i>, I, p. 341. The imitation of the church is plain in the pagan reform attempted by the emperor Julian.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_5"><font color="GREEN">0_5</font></a>. See Harnack, <i>Militia Christi</i>, 1905.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_6"><font color="GREEN">0_6</font></a>. I have collected a number of texts on the religious "militias" in <i>Mon. myst. Mithra</i>, I. p. 317, n. 1. Others could certainly be discovered: Apuleius, <i>Metam.</i>, XL 14: <i>E cohorte religionis</i> </p>  
 <p><a name="page_214"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 214</font></a></p>  
 <p align="justify'"><i>unus</i> (in connection with a mystic of Isis);--Vettius Valens (V, 2, p. 220, 27, Kroll ed.): Στρατιῶται τῆς εἱμαρμένης; (VII. 3, p. 271, 28) Συστρατεύεσθαι τοῖς καιροῖς γενναίως. See Minucius Felix, 36, § 7: <i>Quod patimur non est poena, militia est</i>.--We might also mention the commonplace term <i>militia Veneris</i>, which was popular with the Augustan poets (Propertius, IV, 1, 137; see I, 6, 30; Horace, <i>Od</i>., III, 26, and especially the parallel developed by Ovid, <i>Amor</i>., I, 9, 1 ff., and <i>Ars amat.</i>, III, 233 ff).--Socrates, in Plato's Apologia (p. 28 E), incidentally likens the philosophic mission imposed on him by the divinity to the campaigns he waged under the orders of the archons, but the comparison of God with a "strategus" was developed especially by the Stoics; see Capelle, "Schrift von der Welt," <i>Neue Jahrb. für das klass. Altert.</i>, XV, 1905, p. 558, n. 6, and Seneca, <i>Epist</i>., 107, 9: <i>Optimum est Deum sine murmuratione comitari, malus miles est qui imperatorem gemens sequitur</i>.--See now also Reitzenstein, <i>Hellenistische Mysterienreligion</i>, 1910, p. 66.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_7"><font color="GREEN">0_7</font></a>. See <i>Rev. des études grecques</i>, XIV, 1901, pp. 43 ff.</p>  
 <p><a name="xref_0_8"><font color="GREEN">0_8</font></a>. This has been clearly shown by Wendland in connection with the idea of the σωτηρία, <i>Zeitschrift für neutest. Wiss.</i>, V, 1904, pp. 355 ff. More recently he has thrown light on the general influence of Hellenistic civilization on Christianity (<i>Die hellenistisch-römische Kultur in ihren Besiehungen zum Judentum und Christentum</i>, Tübingen, 1908). A first attempt to determine the character of Hellenistic mysteries is to be found in Reitzenstein's <i>Hellenistische Mysterienreligion</i>, 1910.</p>
