 <p><a name="page_8"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 8</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE ROSE</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">If</span> it pleased the whim of Zeus in an idle<br> Hour to choose a king for the flowers, he surely<br> Would have crowned the rose for its regal beauty,<br>               Deeming it peerless;</p>  
 <p>By its grace is valley and hill embellished,<br> Earth is made a shrine for the lover's ardor;<br> Dear it is to flowers as the charm of lovely<br>               Eyes are to mortals;</p>  
 <p>Joy and pride of plants, and the garden's glory,<br> Beauty's blush it brings to the cheek of meadows;<br> Draining fire and dew from the dawn for rarest<br>               Color and odor;</p>  
 <p>Softly breathed, its scent is a plea for passion,<br> When it blooms to welcome the kiss of Kypris;<br> Sheathed in fragrant leaves its tremulous petals<br>               Laugh in the zephyr.</p>
