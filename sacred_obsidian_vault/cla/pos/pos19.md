 <p><a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 24</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BRIDAL SONG</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bride</span>, that goest to the bridal chamber<br> In the dove-drawn car of Aphrodite,<br>       By a band of dimpled<br>         Loves surrounded;</p>  
 <p>Bride, of maidens all the fairest image<br> Mitylene treasures of the Goddess,<br>       Rosy-ankled Graces<br>         Are thy playmates;</p>  
 <p>Bride, O fair and lovely, thy companions<br> Are the gracious hours that onward passing<br>       For thy gladsome footsteps<br>         Scatter garlands.</p>  
 <p>Bride, that blushing like the sweetest apple<br> On the very branch's end, so strangely<br>       Overlooked, ungathered<br>         By the gleaners;</p>  
 <p>Bride, that like the apple that was never<br> Overlooked but out of reach so plainly,<br>       Only one thy rarest<br>         Fruit may gather;</p>  
 <p>Bride, that into womanhood has ripened<br> For the harvest of the bridegroom only,<br>       He alone shall taste thy<br>         Hoarded sweetness.</p>
