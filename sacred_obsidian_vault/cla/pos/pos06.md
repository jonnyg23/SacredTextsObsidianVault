 <p><a name="page_7"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 7</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE TO ANACTORIA</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Peer</span> of Gods to me is the man thy presence<br> Crowns with joy; who hears, as he sits beside thee,<br> Accents sweet of thy lips the silence breaking,<br>              With lovely laughter;</p>  
 <p>Tones that make the heart in my bosom flutter,<br> For if I, the space of a moment even,<br> Near to thee come, any word I would utter<br>              Instantly fails me;</p>  
 <p>Vain my stricken tongue would a whisper fashion,<br> Subtly under my skin runs fire ecstatic;<br> Straightway mists surge dim to my eyes and leave them<br>              Reft of their vision;</p>  
 <p>Echoes ring in my ears; a trembling seizes<br> All my body bathed in soft perspiration;<br> Pale as grass I grow in my passion's madness,<br>              Like one insensate;</p>  
 <p>But must I dare all, since to me unworthy,<br> Bliss thy beauty brings that a God might envy;<br> Never yet was fervid woman a fairer<br>              Image of Kypris.</p>  
 <p>Ah! undying Daughter of God, befriend me!<br> Calm my blood that thrills with impending transport;<br> Feed my lips the murmur of words to stir her<br>              Bosom to pity;</p>  
 <p>Overcome with kisses her faintest protest,<br> Melt her mood to mine with amorous couches,<br> Till her low assent and her sigh's abandon<br>              Lure me to rapture.</p>
