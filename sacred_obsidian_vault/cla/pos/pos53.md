 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SPRING</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Come</span>, shell divine, be vocal now for me,<br> As when the Hebrus river and the sea<br> To Lesbos bore, on waves harmonious,<br> The head and golden lyre of Orpheus.</p>  
 <p>Calliope, queen of the tuneful throng,<br> Descend and be the Muse of melic song;<br> For through my frame life's tides renewing bring<br> The glad vein-warming vigor of the spring.</p>  
 <p>The skies that dome the earth with far blue fire<br> Make the wide land one temple of desire;<br> Just now across my cheek I felt a God,<br> In the enraptured breeze, pass zephyr-shod.</p>  
 <p>Was that Pan's flute, O Atthis, that we heard,<br> Or the soft love-note of a woodland bird?<br> That flame a scarlet wing that skimmed the stream,<br> Or the red flash of our impassioned dream?</p>  
 <p>Ah, soon again we two shall gather fair<br> Garlands of dill and rose to deck our bare<br> White arms that cling, white breast that burns to breast,<br> When the long night of love shall banish rest.</p>
