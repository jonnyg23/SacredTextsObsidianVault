 <p><a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 29</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">DEATH</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Death</span> is an evil; so the Gods decree,<br> So they have judged, and such must rightly be<br> Our mortal view; for they who dwell on high<br> Had never lived, had it been good to die.</p>  
 <p>And so the poet's house should never know<br> Of tears and lamentations any show;<br> Such things befit not us who deathless sing<br> Of love and beauty, gladness and the spring.</p>  
 <p>No hint of grief should mar the features of<br> Our dreams of endless beauty, lasting love;<br> For they reflect the joy inviolate,<br> Eternal calm that fronts whatever fate.</p>  
 <p>Clëis, my darling, grieve no more, I pray!<br> Let wandering winds thy sorrow bear away,<br> And all our care; my daughter, let thy smile<br> Shine through thy tears and gladden me the while.</p>
