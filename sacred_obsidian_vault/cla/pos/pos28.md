 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CLËIS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Daughter</span> of mine, so fair,<br>   With a form like a golden flower,<br> Wherefore thy pensive air<br>   And the dreams in the myrtle bower?</p>  
 <p>Clëis, beloved, thy eyes<br>   That are turned from my gaze, thy hand<br> That trembles so, I prize<br>   More than all the Lydian land;</p>  
 <p>More than the lovely hills<br>   With the Lesbian olive crowned;<br> Tell me, darling, what ills<br>   In the gloom of thy thought are found?</p>  
 <p>Daughter of mine, come near<br>   And thy head on my knees recline;<br> Whisper and never fear,<br>   For the beat of thy heart is mine.</p>  
 <p>Sweet mother, I can turn<br>   With content to my loom no more;<br> My bosom throbs, I yearn<br>   For a youth that my eyes adore;</p>  
 <p>Lykas of Eresus,<br>   Whom I knew when a little child;<br> My heart by Love is thus<br>   With the sweetest of pain beguiled.</p>
