 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ERINNA</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Haughtier</span> than thou, O fair Erinna,<br> I have never met with any maiden.</p>  
 <p>Such a careless scorn as thine for passion<br> Proves a dire affront to Aphrodite.</p>  
 <p>When with soft desire she wounds thy bosom,<br> Thou shalt know love's pain and doubly suffer.</p>  
 <p>Keep the gifts I gave thee, long rejected;<br> Fabrics for thy lap from far Phocea,</p>  
 <p>Babylonian unguents, scented sandals,<br> And the costly mitra for thy tresses;</p>  
 <p>Tripods worked in brass to flank the altar<br> With the ivory figure of the Goddess;</p>  
 <p>Where the sacrificial fumes from sacred<br> Flames shall rise to gladden and appease her,</p>  
 <p>In the hour when at her call thy fervid<br> Breast and mouth to mine shall be relinquished.</p>
