 <p><a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 45</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">EROTIKA</h1>  <h1 align="center">DITHYRAMBS</h1>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 46</font></a> <a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 47</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN TO PAPHIA</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Immortal</span> Paphia! have I earned thy hate,<br>   That I should burn in passion's fatal flame?<br>   Is not my constant service thine to claim,<br> My prayer's appeal with praise of thee elate?</p>  
 <p>Has not my life been one sole hymn of thee,<br>   One quivering chord on Love's harp overwrought?<br>   My soul has trembled up to thee in thought,<br> Probed to its depth thy every ecstasy.</p>  
 <p>Are not my countless heart-beats each a vow,<br>   Of tribute throbs a garland? For thy gain<br>   The Fates have drenched my soul in passion's rain,<br> Pieria's roses twined about my brow.</p>  
 <p>The virgin harvest of my heart was thine,<br>   I shuddered in the joy that half consumed;<br>   The votive garlands on thy altar bloomed,<br> My days were songs to nights of bliss divine.</p>  
 <p>Why try me, then, with torture, gracious Queen?<br>   Why verge me on this rapture's dread abyss,<br>   Hold breast from breast and stay the yearning kiss?<br> Ah, couldst thou fashion pain that stung less keen?</p>  
 <p>The throe of Tantalus is mine to bear,<br>   Beauty that Thetis-like eludes my clasp;<br>   Glances that lure, that make each breath a gasp,<br> And then disdainful gloat at my despair.</p>  
 <p>Scornful she dwells beyond my ardor's clutch,<br>   Bathed in an aureole of carnal fire;<br>   O bind her equal slave to fond desire,<br> Let passion's tingling warmth her being touch!</p>  
 <p>Come to me, Goddess, come as once of old,<br>   Hearing my voice implore thee from afar,<br>   I drew to earth thy dazzling avatar;<br> Accord the smile of piercing bliss untold.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_48"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 48</font></a></p>  
 <p>Ask me the dear suave question phrased of yore;<br>   "Sappho, who grieveth now thy mad fond heart?<br>   Wouldst win her beauty, she who frowns apart?<br> Wild as thou lovest, she soon shall love thee more."</p>  
 <p>O fair Olympian, answer thus, I pray!<br>   Release me from this torment, yield my arms<br>   The transport thirsted of her folded charms,<br> In glow that welds her heart to mine for aye.</p>
