 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">AMBEUM: ALCÆUS AND SAPPHO</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small>ALCÆUS</small></p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Violet</span>-weaving Sappho, pure and lovely,<br> Softly-smiling Sappho, I would utter<br> Something that my secret hope has cherished,<br> Did no painful sense of shame deter me.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>SAPPHO</small></p>  
 <p>Had the impulse of thy heart been honest,<br> It had urged no evil supplication;<br> Shame had not abashed thy eyes before me,<br> And thy words had done thee no dishonor.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>ALCÆUS</small></p>  
 <p>Softly-smiling Sappho, longing bids me<br> Tell thee all that in my heart lies hidden.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>SAPPHO</small></p>  
 <p>Have no fear, Alcæus, to offend me!<br> Thy emotion stirs my heart to pity.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>ALCÆUS</small></p>  
 <p>I desire thee, violet-weaving Sappho!<br> Love thee madly, softly-smiling Sappho!</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>SAPPHO</small></p>  
 <p>Hush, Alcæus! thou must choose a younger<br> Comrade for thy couch, for I would never<br> Join thy years to minethe Gods forbid it<br> Youth and ardent fire to age and ashes.</p>
