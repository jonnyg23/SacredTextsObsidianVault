 <p><a name="page_5"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 5</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LOVE'S BANQUET</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">If</span> Panormus, Cyprus or Paphos hold thee,<br> Either home of Gods or the island temple,<br> Hark again and come at my invocation,<br>                Goddess benefic;</p>  
 <p>Come thou, foam-born Kypris, and pour in dainty<br> Cups of amber gold thy delicate nectar,<br> Subtly mixed with fire that will swiftly kindle<br>                Love in our bosoms;</p>  
 <p>Thus the bowl ambrosial was stirred in Paphos<br> For the feast, and taking the burnished ladle,<br> Hermes poured the wine for the Gods who lifted<br>                Reverent beakers;</p>  
 <p>High they held their goblets and made libation,<br> Spilling wine as pledge to the Fates and Hades,<br> Quaffing deep and binding their hearts to Eros,<br>                Lauding thy servant.</p>  
 <p>So to me and my Lesbians round me gathered,<br> Each made mine, an amphor of love long tasted,<br> Bid us drink, who sigh for thy thrill ecstatic,<br>                Passion's full goblet;</p>  
 <p>Grant me this, O Kypris, and on thy altar<br> Dawn will see a goat of the breed of Naxos,<br> Snowy doves from Cos and the drip of rarest<br>                Lesbian vintage;</p>  
 <p>For a regal taste is mine and the glowing<br> Zenith-lure and beauty of suns must brighten<br> Love for me, that ever upon perfection<br>                Trembles elusive.</p>
