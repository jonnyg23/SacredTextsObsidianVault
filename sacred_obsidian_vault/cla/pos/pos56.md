 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">EUNEICA</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Aphrodite's</span> handmaid,<br> Bright as gold thou camest,<br> Tender woven garlands<br> Round thy tender neck;</p>  
 <p>Sweet as soft Persuasion,<br> Lissome as the Graces,<br> Shy Euneica, lovely<br> Girl from Salamis.</p>  
 <p>Slender thou as Syrinx,<br> As the waving reed-nymph,<br> Once by Pan, the god of<br> Summer winds, deflowered.</p>  
 <p>On thy lips whose quiver<br> Seems to plead for pity,<br> Mine shall rest and linger<br> Like the mouth of Pan</p>  
 <p>On the mouth of Syrinx,<br> When his breath that filled her<br> Blew through all her body<br> Music of his love.</p>
