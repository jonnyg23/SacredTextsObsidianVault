 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LARICHUS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">While</span> charming maids plait garlands for thy brows,<br> Larichus, bring the pledge for this carouse<br> Like lovely Ganymede, brother mine,<br> And cool from thy patera pour the wine.</p>  
 <p>Thy slender limbs have all a Satyr's grace,<br> Hylas, the Wood-God, dimples in thy face;<br> These maids of mine, beloved and loving me,<br> My dreams have made thy Nymphs to sport with thee.</p>  
 <p>I heard fair Mitylene's plaudits cease<br> Oer Lykas, Menon and Dinnomenes;<br> And hail thy beauty worthy of the prize,<br> Cupbearer to the council of the wise.</p>  
 <p>No noble youth the prytaneum holds,<br> Whose graceful form the purple tunic folds<br> Can match with thee, when on affairs of state<br> All Lesbos gathers with the wise and great.</p>
