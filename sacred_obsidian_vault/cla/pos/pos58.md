 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 71</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MNASIDICA</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Set</span>, O Dica, garlands on thy lovely<br> Glinting mass of fine and golden tresses,<br> Sprays of dill with fingers soft entwining<br> While I stand apart to better judge.</p>  
 <p>Those who have fair wreaths about the forehead,<br> Breathing brentheian odor to the senses,<br> Ever first find favor with the Graces<br> Who from wreathless suppliants turn away.</p>  
 <p>Dica, Mnasidica, thou art shapely<br> With the flowing curves of Aphrodite;<br> Eyes the color of her azure ocean<br> Washing wide on Cyprus languid shore.</p>  
 <p>In thy every movement grace unconscious<br> Sways the rhythmic poem of thy body,<br> Charming with elusive undulation<br> Like a splendid lily in the wind.</p>  
 <p>As I stand apart to judge the better<br> Fair effects that roses add to beauty,<br> All thy rays of loveliness concentered<br> Sun me till I swoon with swift desire.</p>
