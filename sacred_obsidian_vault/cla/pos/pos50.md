 <p><a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 61</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TO ALCÆUS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Countless</span> are the cups thou drainest<br> In thy hymns to Dionysos,<br>                O Alcæus!</p>  
 <p>War and wine alone thou singest;<br> Wherefore not of Aphrodite,<br>                O Alcæus!</p>  
 <p>Spacious halls are thine where many<br> Trophies hang in Ares honor,<br>                O Alcæus!</p>  
 <p>Brazen shields and shining helmets,<br> Plates of brass, Chalcidian broad-swords,<br>                O Alcæus!</p>  
 <p>When with winter roars the Thracian<br> North wind through the leafless forest,<br>                O Alcæus!</p>  
 <p>Thou dost heap the fire and banish<br> Care with many a tawny goblet,<br>                O Alcæus!</p>
