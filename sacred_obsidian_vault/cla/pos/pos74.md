 <p><a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 89</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE CLIFF OF LEUCAS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">A far-seen</span> cliff<br> Stands in the western sea<br> Toward Cephallenian lands.</p>  
 <p>Apollo's temple crowns<br> Its whitened crest,<br> And at its base<br> The waves eternal beat.</p>  
 <p>Its leap has power<br> To cure the pangs<br> Of unrequited love.</p>  
 <p>Thither pale lovers go<br> With anguished hearts<br> To dare the deep and quench<br> Love's slow consuming flame.</p>  
 <p>Urged to the edge<br> By maddening desire,<br> I, too, shall fling myself<br> Imploring thee,<br> Apollo, lord and king!</p>  
 <p>Into the chill<br> Embraces of the sea,<br> Less cold than thine, O Phaon,<br> I shall fall<br> Fall with the flutter of a wounded dove;</p>  
 <p>And I shall rise<br> Indifferent forever to love's dream,<br> Or find below<br> The sea's eternal voice,<br> Eternal peace.</p>
