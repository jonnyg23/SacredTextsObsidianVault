 <p><a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 21</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">EPITHALAMIA</h1>  <h1 align="center">THRENODES</h1>  
 <p><a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 22</font></a> <a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 23</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMENAIOS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Artisans</span>, raise high the roof beam!<br> Tall is the bridegroom as Ares,<br> Taller by far than the tallest,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>Ay! towering over his fellows,<br> As over men of all other<br> Lands towers the Lesbian singer,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>Well-favored, too, is the maiden,<br> Eyes that are sweeter than honey,<br> Fair both in face and in figure,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>For there was never another<br> Virgin in loveliness like her,<br> By Aphrodite so honored,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>O happy bridegroom, the wedding<br> Comes to the point of completion;<br> Thou hast the maid of thy choosing,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>See how a paleness suffuses<br> Soft oer her exquisite features,<br> Passion's benign premonition,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>Go to the couch unreluctant,<br> Rejoicing and sweet to the bridegroom;<br> He in his turn is rejoicing,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>  
 <p>May Hesperus lead thee, and Hera,<br> She whom to-night that ye honor,<br> Silver-throned Goddess of marriage,<br>             O Hymenæus!</p>
