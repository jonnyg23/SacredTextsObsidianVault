 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 51</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">APHRODITE'S PRAISE</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">O Sappho</span>, why art thou ever<br> Singing with praises the blessed<br>       Queen of the heaven?</p>  
 <p>Why does the heart in thy bosom<br> Ever revert in its yearning<br>       Throb to the Goddess?</p>  
 <p>Why are thy senses unsated<br> Ever in quest of elusive<br>       Love that is deathless?</p>  
 <p>Ah, gracious Daughter of Cyprus,<br> Never can I as a mortal<br>       Tire of thy service.</p>  
 <p>Thou art the breath of my body,<br> The blood in my veins, and the glowing<br>       Pulse of my bosom.</p>  
 <p>Omnipotent, burning, resistless,<br> Thou art the passion that shaking<br>       Masters me ever.</p>  
 <p>Thou art the crisis of rapture<br> Relaxing my limbs, and the melting<br>       Ebb of emotion;</p>  
 <p>Bringing the tears to my lashes,<br> Sighs to my lips, in the swooning<br>       Excess of passion.</p>  
 <p>O golden-crowned Aphrodite,<br> Grant I shall ever be grateful,<br>       Sure of thy favor;</p>  
 <p>Worthy the lot of thy priestess,<br> Supreme in the song that forever<br>       Rings with thy praises.</p>
