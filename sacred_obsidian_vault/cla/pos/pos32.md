 <p><a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 39</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE BOAST OF ARES</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ares</span> said he would drag<br>   Hephestus by force<br> From Poseidon's palace<br>   Deep down in the sea;<br>   Where he had fashioned<br> The cunning throne<br>   With the secret chains.</p>  
 <p>He presented the throne,<br>   Forsooth, as a gift<br> To the queen of heaven;<br>   But Hera soon found<br>   For revenge on her<br> Who had him cast<br>   From the home of Gods.</p>  
 <p>For secure in its clasp<br>   Of adamant gold<br> She was held imprisoned,<br>   The prey of his guile;<br>   And Hephestus knew<br> By him alone<br>   Could the queen be freed.</p>  
 <p>But the great God of war<br>   Made boast of his strength;<br> He would bring the forger<br>   Of metals and tricks<br>   On high to release<br> Hera, and end<br>   Her enraged despair.</p>  
 <p>Ares said he would drag<br>   Hephestus by force,<br> But was made to waver<br>   And flee when assailed<br>   With a blazing brand<br> By the dark God<br>   Of the underworld.</p>
