 <p><a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 87</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE FAREWELL</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Beloved</span>, stand face to face,<br>   And, lifting lids, disclose to me the grace,<br> The Paphic fire that lingers yet and lies<br>   Reflected in thy eyes.</p>  
 <p>Phaon, my sole beloved,<br>   Stand not to my mad passion all unmoved;<br> O let, ere thou to far Panormus sail,<br>   One hour of love prevail.</p>  
 <p>Dear ingrate, come and let<br>   Thy breath like odor from a cassolet,<br> Thy smile, the clinging touch of lips and heart<br>   Anoint me, ere we part.</p>  
 <p>Phaon, I yearn and seek<br>   But thee alone; and what I feel must speak<br> In all these fond and wilful ways of mine,<br>   O mortal, made divine!</p>  
 <p>My girl friends now no more<br>   Hang their sweet gifts of garlands at my door;<br> Dear maids, with all your vanished empery<br>   Ye now are naught to me.</p>  
 <p>Phaon, thy galley rides<br>   Within the harbor's mouth and waits the tides<br> And favoring winds, far to the west to fly<br>   And leave me here to die.</p>  
 <p>The brawny rowers lean<br>   To bend long-stroking oars; and changing scene<br> And fairer loves than mine shall soon efface<br>   This last divine embrace.</p>  
 <p>Phaon, the lifting breeze!<br>   See, at thy feet I kneel and clasp thy knees!<br> Go not, go not! O hear my sobbing prayer,<br>   And yield to my despair!</p>
