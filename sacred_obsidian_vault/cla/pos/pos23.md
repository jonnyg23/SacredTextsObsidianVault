 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 28</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE STRICKEN FLOWER</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Think</span> not to ever look as once of yore,<br> Atthis, upon my love; for thou no more<br> Wilt find intact upon its stem the flower<br> Thy guile left slain and bleeding in that hour.</p>  
 <p>So ruthless shepherds crush beneath their feet<br> The hill flower blooming in the summer heat;<br> The hyacinth whose purple heart is found<br> Left bruised and dead, to darken on the ground.</p>
