 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LAMENT FOR ADONIS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ah</span>, for Adonis!<br> See, he is dying,<br> Delicate, lovely,<br> Slender Adonis.</p>  
 <p>Ah, for Adonis!<br> Weep, O ye maidens,<br> Beating your bosoms,<br> Rending your tunics.</p>  
 <p>O Cytherea,<br> Hasten, for never<br> Loved thou another<br> As thy Adonis.</p>  
 <p>See, on the rosy<br> Cheek with its dimple,<br> Blushing no longer,<br> Thanatos shadow.</p>  
 <p>Save him, O Goddess!<br> Thou, the beguiler,<br> All-powerful, holy,<br> Stay the dread evil.</p>  
 <p>Ah, for Adonis!<br> No more at vintage<br> Time will he come with<br> Bloom of the meadows.</p>  
 <p>Ah, for Adonis!<br> See, he is dying,<br> Fading as flowers<br> With the lost summer.</p>
