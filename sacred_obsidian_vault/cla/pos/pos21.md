 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 26</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PIERIA'S ROSE</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pale</span> death shall come, and thou and thine shall be,<br> Then and thereafter, to all memory<br> Forgotten as the wind that yesterday<br> Blew the last lingering apple buds away;</p>  
 <p>For thou hadst never that undying rose<br> To grace the brow and shed immortal glows;<br> Pieria's fadeless flower that few may claim<br> To wreathe and save thy unremembered name.</p>  
 <p>Ay! even on the fields of Dis unknown,<br> Obscure among the shadows and alone,<br> Thy flitting shade shall pass uncomforted<br> Of any heed from all the flitting dead.</p>  
 <p>But no one maid, I think, beneath the skies,<br> At any time shall live and be as wise,<br> In sooth, as I am; for the Muses Nine<br> Have made me honored and their gifts are mine;</p>  
 <p>And men, I think, will never quite forget<br> My songs or me; so long as stars shall set<br> Or sun shall rise, or hearts feel love's desire,<br> My voice shall cross their dreams, a sigh of fire.</p>
