 <p><a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 16</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE DISTAFF</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Come</span>, ye dainty Graces and lovely Muses,<br> Rosy-armed and pure and with fairest tresses,<br> Come from groves on Helicon's hill where murmur<br>                 Founts that are holy;</p>  
 <p>Come with dancing step and with lips harmonic,<br> Gather near and view my ivory distaff,<br> Gift from Cos my brother Charaxus brought me,<br>                 Sailing from Egypt;</p>  
 <p>Sailing back to Lesbos from far Naucratis,<br> From the seven mouths of the Nile and Egypt<br> Up the blue Ægean, the island-dotted<br>                 Ocean of Hellas;</p>  
 <p>Choicest wool alone will I spin for fabrics,<br> Winding reel with threads for the cloths as fleecy,<br> Soft and fine as they bring from far Phocea,<br>                 Sidon or Sardis;</p>  
 <p>While I weave my thought shall engird the giver,<br> Whether here, or far on the sea, or resting<br> Couched in shady courts with the lovely garland<br>                 Girls of Naucratis.</p>
