 <h1 align="CENTER">The Magus</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">by Francis Barrett</h3>
 <h4 align="CENTER">London, 1801</h4>
 <hr>
 <p><cite>The Magus</cite> is one of the primary sources for the study of
 ceremonial magic, and for a long time was one of the rarest and most
 sought after of the 19th century grimoires.
 Barretts' magnum opus embodies deep knowledge of Alchemy,
 Astrology, and the Kabbalah, and has been cited by the Golden
 Dawn and other occult and esoteric movements as source material.
 Written in 1801 in the middle of the 'Age of Reason',
 sandwiched between Newton and Darwin, this was possibly
 the last epoch that a work like this could be composed.
 </p>
 <p>
 <font size="-2">
 <i>BIBLIOGRAPHIC NOTE</i>:
 The Magus is broken down into two physical volumes.
 The reference to three books on the title page may be confusing.
 The third book is the biographical
 section and comprises part of the second volume.
 The biographical section has been deprecated by authorities such as Waite,
 and it's not even certain that it was written by Barrett; it may have been
 added as filler by the printer.
 </font>
 </p>
