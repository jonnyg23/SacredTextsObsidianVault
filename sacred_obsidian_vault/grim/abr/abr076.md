   <h3 align="center">THE SIXTEENTH CHAPTER.</h3>  
 <p><a name="img_tinit"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tinit.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>O find and take possession of all kinds of Treasures, provided that they be not at all (Magically) guarded.  
 <p>(1) For Treasure of Silver (or Silver Money).</p>  
 <p>(2) For Gold Money.</p>  
 <p>(3) For a great Treasure.</p>  
 <p>(4) For a small Treasure.</p>  
 <p>(5) For an unguarded Treasure.</p>  
 <p>(6) For Copper Money.</p>  
 <p>(7) For Gold in Ingots.</p>  
 <p>(8) For Silver in Ingots.</p>  
 <p>(9) For jewels.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 204</font></a></p>  
 <p>(10) For Ancient Medals (and Coins).</p>  
 <p>(11) For a Treasure hidden by a particular Person.</p>  
 <p>(12) For Pearls.</p>  
 <p>(13) For Diamonds.</p>  
 <p>(14) For Rubies.</p>  
 <p>(15) For Balassius Rubies.</p>  
 <p>(16) For Emeralds.</p>  
 <p>(17) For worked Gold.</p>  
 <p>(18) For Silver Plate.</p>  
 <p>(19) For Statues.</p>  
 <p>(20) For Specimens of Ancient Art.</p>
