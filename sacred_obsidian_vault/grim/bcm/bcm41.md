 <h3 align="CENTER">§ 5. Concerning the External Preparation of the Operator, and secondly Concerning the Vestments</h3>
 <p>The use of special garments in the ceremonies of Magic follows reasonably enough from the religious character ascribed to these operations. Peter de Abano recommends a priest's garment if possible, by which an alb may be understood. <a name="fr_84"></a><a href="#fn_84"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 149</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> <i>The Book of Black Magic</i>, following the <i>Key of Solomon</i>, and representing the Jewish rite, prescribes an outer vestment like that of the Levites, with the following characters embroidered in red silk upon the breast:--</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_14900"></a>
 <a href="img/14900.jpg"><img src="tn/generic.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to view</font></a></p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>All garments, even to the shirt, must be of linen cloth, <a name="fr_85"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">1</font></a> and when assuming them these words should be recited: ANTON, AMATOR, EMITES, THEODONIEL, PONCOR, PAGOR, ANITOR; <a name="fr_86"></a><a href="#fn_86"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>by the virtue of these most holy Angelic Names do I clothe myself, O Lord, in my Sabbath garments, that so I may fulfil, even unto their term, all things which I desire to effect through Thee, Most Holy ADONAY, Whose kingdom and rule endure for ever and ever. Amen</i>.</p>
 <p>The shoes and hat should be of white leather, bearing the same characters <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_87"><font size="1">3</font></a> written in cinnabar diluted with gum-water, the pen of the Art being used. The following names must be written about the hat: <a name="fr_88"></a><a href="#fn_88"><font size="1">4</font></a> JEHOVA, behind; ADONAY, on the right hand; ELOY, on the left; and GIBOR, <a name="fr_89"></a><a href="#fn_89"><font size="1">5</font></a> at the front.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_84"></a><a href="bcm41.htm#fr_84">148:2</a> A long garment of white linen, close before and behind, covering the whole body even to the feet, and cinctured by a girdle--such is the ordinance of pseudo-Agrippa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_85"></a><a href="bcm41.htm#fr_85">149:1</a> According to the <i>Key of Solomon</i> the thread of which they are made should be spun by a young maiden.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_86"></a><a href="bcm41.htm#fr_86">149:2</a> The Key of Solomon renders these names differently, namely: <i>Amor, Amator, Amides, Ideodaniach, Pamor, Plaior, Anitor</i>; for Sabbath garments it substitutes Vestments of Power.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_87"></a><a href="bcm41.htm#fr_87">149:3</a> Special characters are given for the garment and the shoes by the <i>Clavicle</i>. They bear no analogy to anything in the Grimoire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_88"></a><a href="bcm41.htm#fr_88">149:4</a> For the hat or bonnet a crown is prescribed in the <i>Clavicle</i>, and it should be made of virgin paper.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_89"></a><a href="bcm41.htm#fr_89">149:5</a> In place of these words the <i>Key of Solomon</i> substitutes <i>El</i> and <i>Elohim</i>.</p>
