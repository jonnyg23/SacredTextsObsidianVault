 <h1 align="CENTER">The Book of Ceremonial Magic</h1>
 <h2 align="CENTER">by Arthur Edward Waite</h2>
 <h4 align="CENTER">[1913]</h4>
 <hr>
 <p>
 This book is the second edition of a work 
 which in its first edition was titled,
 more provocatively, <cite>The Book of Black Magic and of Pacts</cite>.
 This book is an attempt to synthesize the procedures of all of the
 famous Grimoires.
 It draws on the <cite>Key of Solomon</cite>, the <cite>Grimorium Verum</cite>,
 the apocryphal <cite>Fourth Book of Cornelius Agrippa</cite>, and many others,
 including the famous <cite>Black Pullet</cite>, or <cite>Poulet Noir</cite>.
 While Waite is careful in his analysis of the various Grimoires, he
 treats the subject matter skeptically.
 The result is an unparalleled look at the details of ceremonial magic.
 </p>
