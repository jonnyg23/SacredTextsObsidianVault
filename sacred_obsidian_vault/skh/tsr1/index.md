 <h1>The Sikh Religion, Volume 1</h1>
 <h3>by Max Arthur MacAuliffe [1842-1913]</h3>
 <h4>Oxford University Press [1909]</h4>
 </center>
 <p>This is one of the first (and still one of the few)
 comprehensive books about the Sikh religion in the English language.
 MacAuliffe had extensive access to
 manuscripts of the Sikh sacred writings (the Granth), as well as
 support from Sikh scholars and leaders of the time.
 This volume covers Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism.
 This is volume one of six; future volumes are forthcoming.
