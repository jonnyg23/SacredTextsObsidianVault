 <h3 align="CENTER">61.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>I hîyaha, etc.<br>
 GustA gî'hao dî'nañ kuñg<u>î</u>ñâ'ñgAñ?<br>
 What | for | my child | cries?<br>
 Q!ô'na kun xê'txa ku<u>Lî</u>'ndala-igi î'sîñ <u>g</u>ê'da ku<u>L</u>indâ'l<u>g</u>asan<br>
 Q!ô'na | Point | in front of | to pass along by canoe | again | cries for that | she will travel by canoe<br>
     q!o'l<u>g</u>An djâ'<u>g</u>a?<br>
     my master's | wife<br>
 Îhî', etc.</p>
 <p>I hîyaha, etc.<br>
 For what is my child crying?<br>
 Does she cry to pass along again by canoe in front of Point Q!ô'na, my master's wife? <a name="fr_68"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 Îhî', etc.</p>

 </p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_63"></a><a href="hs063.htm#fr_68">42:1</a> By saying "master's wife," the singer implies that her child will marry a higher chief than herself. "Passing along again" refers to the former existence of the child. Point Q!ô'na is near Nasto.</p>
