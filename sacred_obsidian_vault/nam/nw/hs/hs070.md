 <h3 align="CENTER">68.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>Gut sî'+<strike>l</strike><u>g</u>adaga'ñ xA'nhao kî'<u>g</u>a kuqêda's at (a) lA<br>
 Each other | right | after | even | names | aristocratic | with | he (baby)<br>
     kia'gañao gûdâ'gû.<br>
     call it sitting | thought he would.<br>
 KîlsLa'-i kugwê'dalañ <a name="fr_76"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">3</font></a> sûwa'ñ.<br>
 Chief | while walking hither | he said.<br>
 Â'yaña kî'n<u>g</u>et gô'ñ<u>g</u>a <strike>l</strike>iñga'n.<br>
 Be careful | chief's | father | is going to be!<br>
 Dî'nAñ ayâ'ña q!o'ldjat xâ't<u>g</u>a l<u>î</u>ñgA'n dîna'ñ.<br>
 My child | be careful, | chief-woman | father | is going to be my child.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 46</font></a></p>
 <p>He thinks he would use high-sounding names, one after the other, (to the others of his own family.) <a name="fr_77"></a><a href="hs071.htm#fn_71"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>
 He says the chief comes walking.<br>
 Be careful of the future chief's father!<br>
 Be careful of my child, the future chief-woman's father!</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_70"></a><a href="hs070.htm#fr_76">45:3</a> Kugwê'dalañ ("while walking hither") is a low-class word referring to the chief himself.</p>
