 <h1 align="CENTER">ALPHABET</h1>
 <p align="CENTER"></p><center><table cellspacing="0" border="0" cellpadding="9" width="779">
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>a, e, i, o, u</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>have their continental sounds (short). </p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>â, ê, î, ô, û</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>long vowels.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>A, E, I, O, U</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>obscure vowels.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <sup><p>a</p></sup>, <sup>e</sup>, <sup>i</sup>, <sup>o</sup>, <sup>u</sup></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>vowels not articulated but indicated by position of the month.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>ä</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>in German <i>Bär</i>.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <u></u><p>â</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>aw in law.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <u></u><p>ô</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>in German voll.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <u></u><p>ê</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>in bell.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>-</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>separates vowels which do not form diphthongs.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>ai</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>i in island.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>au</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>ow in how.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>l</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>as in English.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>ll</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>very long, slightly palatized by allowing a greater portion of the back of the tongue to touch the palate.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <u></u><p>L</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>posterior palatal 1; the tip of the tongue touches the alveoli of the lower jaw, the back of the tongue is pressed against the hard palate, sonans.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>L</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>the same,, short and exploded (surd; Lepsius's <u>t</u>).</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>L<u>!</u></p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>the same with very great stress of explosion.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>q</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>velar k.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>k</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>English k.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>k*</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>palatized k (Lepsius's k'), almost ky.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>kX</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>might be better defined as a posterior palatal ky between k and k*.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>x</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>ch in German <i>Bach</i>.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>X</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>x pronounced at posterior border of hard palate.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>x*</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>palatal x as in German <i>ich</i>.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>s, c</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>are evidently the same sound and might be written s* or c*, both being palatized; c (English sh) is pronounced with open teeth, the tongue almost touching the palate immediately behind the alveoli; s is modified in the same manner.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>d, t, b, p, g, k</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>as in English, but surd and sonant are difficult to distinguish.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>h</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>as in English.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>y</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>as in year.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>w</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>as in English.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>m</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>is pronounced with semiclausure of the nose and with very slight compression of the lips; it partakes, therefore, of the character of b and w.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>n</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>is pronounced with semiclausure of the nose; it partakes, therefore, of the character of d.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p><a name="page_8"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 8</font></a></p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p> </p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>!</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>designates increased stress of articulation.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <u></u><p>!</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>designates increased stress of articulation due to the elision of q.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>?</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>is a very deep laryngeal intonation, due to the elision of q.</p></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td width="19%" valign="TOP">
 <p>2, 4</p></td>
 <td width="81%" valign="TOP">
 <p>designate excessive length of vowels, representing approximately the double and fourfold mora.</p></td>
 </tr>
 </table>
 </center>
 
 <p align="CENTER"> </p>
 <p>Words ending with a short vowel must be contracted with the first vowel of the next word. When a word ends with a long vowel and the next begins with a vowel, a euphonic -y- is inserted. The last consonant of a, word is united with the first vowel of the next word to one syllable.</p>
