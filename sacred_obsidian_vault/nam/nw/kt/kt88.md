 <h1 align="CENTER">46. <u>G</u>â'sag*ila.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">Tradition of the Na'k!wax*da?x<sup>u</sup>.</p>
 <u></u><p>Gâ'sag*ila was a chief who had many carvings. He came from the south. He put them up when he reached the north country. He went to Gwa'wê in the country of the Na'k!wax*da?x<sup>u</sup>, and there he staid. There he made a winter-dance, and his son became a cannibal, whose names were Q!â'sElîdzas, Ha?mêk*!â'lag*il<u>î</u>s, Ts!â'x<u>ô</u>stâla. This son's son was K*!ô'gwikîlagEmê?, whose son was Q!u'mqwax*<u>â</u>'lasEmê?.</p>
