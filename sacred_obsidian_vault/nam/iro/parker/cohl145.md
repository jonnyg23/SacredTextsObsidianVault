 <h3 align="CENTER">OUTLINES OF THE CORNPLANTING AND THE MAPLE THANKSGIVINGS</h3>
 <p align="CENTER">AN OUTLINE PROGRAM OF WAANO`'NAOGWA:`'CIOT, THE CORN-PLANTING CEREMONY</p>
 <p>1 Opening address by a chief</p>
 <p>2 A Thanksgiving speech</p>
 <p>3 Ne?'äskä'nie?, the women's dance</p>
 <p>4 Ne?'ga?da:'ciot, the jubilee dance</p>
 <p>5 Ne?'gusshêdon'dada', the jug shaking dance</p>
 <p>6 Ne?'äskän'i:e?, the women's dance</p>
 <p>7 Ne?'yiêndonêsshontâ?, the old women's song</p>
 <p>8 Ne?'äskä'ni:e?, the women's dance</p>
 <p>9 Ne?'gaianon'gayo<sup>n</sup>ka</p>
 <p>10 Ne?'osto:'wägo'wa, the great feather dance</p>
 <p>11 Closing address</p>
 <p>12 Distribution of the feast</p>
 <p>The object of the Cornplanting ceremony is to secure divine favor and help in the spring planting. Everyone is invoked to till the ground and earn the bread they eat. The ceremony lasts about four or five hours.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">THE MAPLE FESTIVAL</h3>
 <p>A council is called to set the time for this festival which has no exact day but varies according to the weather. However, it takes place soon after the sap commences to run. Its object is to thank all trees for their services to man and invoke their protection and good will for the coming year.</p>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">Outline program</p>
 <p>I The address to the maple tree. A fire is built at the foot of a large maple tree. The people gather around and a special officer advances with a basket of tobacco which he sprinkles on the fire as he recites the address to the maple:</p>
 <p>Ne? nê<sup>n</sup>ga:' gägwa:'ani saiwisa'ane gäni'sê swên'iio'<br>
 Seane ganigä'o ne?'niganigai?isek<br>
 One?' dîq' oyän'kwa (owe) soi'yê?<br>
 Negihedahadondi gaiyetionoshäs henizaiwissaho<sup>n</sup>?<br>
 One<sup>n</sup>?' dîq' kejedai` soñgwa:ni, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a></p>
 <i></i><p align="CENTER">The prayer at the maple festival</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Wa?'da Tadinion'nio?o`</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Maple Thanksgiving</p>
 <p>Êa<sup>n</sup>waiyîgwa`showine`' ode:ha'donni. Ne?'wainnondoi'shoñk<br>
 Oh partake of this tobacco the forests. This we petition</p>
 <p>nega'dogä nayût'däo<sup>n</sup> näêtgonê'igais näwâ?'dâ<br>
 may you continue -the production of sweet water Oh maple</p>
 <p>Hawe'o<sup>n</sup> Nawênniio` ê<sup>n</sup>gäo<sup>n</sup>dadegao<sup>n</sup> ê<sup>n</sup>gani'gaikse:k<br>
 The will of the Creator that a certain tree water flows from</p>
 <p>Ne?'nê<sup>n</sup>gä? ê<sup>n</sup>gä'oñk hadieo`'shä deodo<sup>n</sup>o<sup>n</sup> ne?' he:hadidûk'ke:no<sup>n</sup>die:s hadiksa:'sho<sup>n</sup>'o? gahadêgon<sup>n</sup>sho<sup>n</sup><br>
 This it may not accidents occur the running about the children in the woods.</p>
 <p>Ne?' ne<sup>n</sup>gä?' wânîshäde` i:s' êsswai'ya?dagwäni'yothet nê<sup>n</sup>gä?' wânîshäde`.<br>
 Now this day you it belongs to you to enjoy this day.</p>
 <p>Djasayawa'godûk Hawên'iio` cia?'dade: gäoya`ge:'tciojo`'.<br>

 <p>Agwai'wayiis ne?'gaiyiwanda'kho.<br>

 <p>Da'nê`ho'.<br>

 <i></i><p align="CENTER">The address to the maple, the chief of trees and the prayer to the Creator</p>
 <p align="CENTER">A Seneca ceremony</p>
 <p>The priest stands at the roots of a maple. A fire is burning and the priest casts tobacco in the fire and as its smoke arises he says:</p>
 <p>To the tree:</p>
 <p>O partake of this incense,<br>
 You the forests!<br>
 We implore you<br>
 To continue as before,<br>
 The flowing waters of the maple.</p>
 <p><a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a></p>
 <p>To the Creator and the tree:</p>
 <p>It is the will of the Creator<br>
 That a certain tree<br>
 Should flow such water.<br>
 Now may no accidents occur<br>
 To children roaming in the forests.<br>
 Now this day is yours<br>
 May you enjoy it,--this day.</p>
 <p>To the Creator:</p>
 <p>We give thanks, oh God, to you,<br>
 You who dwell in heaven.<br>
 We have done our duty<br>
 You have seen us do it.<br>
 So it is done."</p>
