 <h3 align="CENTER">SECTION 63</h3>
 <p>"'Now another message.</p>
 <p>"'Tell your friends and relatives that there will be two divisions</p>
 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 55</font></a></p>
 <p>of mind <a name="fr_68"></a><a href="#fn_68"><font size="1">1</font></a> among the chiefs and head-men and among the people. Nevermore will your race be united.'"</p>
 <p>So they said and he said. Eniaiehuk.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_68"></a><a href="cohl074.htm#fr_68">55:1</a> This seemingly obscure section is cleared of its mystery when the preacher explains that the divisions of mind refer to the Gaiwios'tûk or Christian and Oñgwe?o<sup>n</sup>weka' or Indian parties. "Dewadia'ke? gani'goî?, <i>broken in twain, the unity of purpose</i>," is Chief Cornplanter's term.</p>
