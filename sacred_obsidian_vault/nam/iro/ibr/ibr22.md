 <h1 align="CENTER">CANIENGA GLOSSARY.</h1>
 <p>The following Glossary comprises all the words of the Canienga text. The meanings of these words are given as they were received from the interpreters. For most of them these definitions are confirmed by the dictionaries of Bruyas and Cuoq. Some of the words, which are either archaic forms or peculiar to the Council ceremonies, are not found in those dictionaries; and in a few instances the precise purport of these words must be considered doubtful. In some cases, also, the force of a grammatical inflection or of an affix may not have been correctly ascertained; but it is believed that the vocabulary will be found, in general, sufficiently accurate to be of service to the student who may desire to acquire some knowledge of the Canienga speech.</p>
 <p>When the words of John Buck's copy differ in orthography from those of the Johnson MS., the former are added in brackets. Words cited from the dictionary of Bruyas are distinguished by the letter B; those from the lexicon of M. Cuoq by C.</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">A.</p>
 <p>Aerengh [orenh], far. <i>Heren</i>, <i>ahiren</i>, B., far; <i>heren</i>, <i>aheren</i>, C., far away.</p>
 <p>Aesahhahiyenenhon [ahesahhahiyenennyonhon], if thou hadst fallen (or perished) by the way. <i>Aha</i>, <i>oha</i>, <i>ohaha</i>, road, path; <i>gaienneñon</i>, B., to fall.</p>
 <p>Aesayatyenenghdon [ahesayatyenendon], thou mightest have been destroyed. <i>Gaienneñon</i>, B., to fall; <i>gaiennenton</i>,</p>
 <p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 192</font></a></p>
 <p>to cause to fall. <i>Aesaiatienenton</i> is in the perf. subj. passive.</p>
 <p>Aghsonh, scarcely, hardly, while.</p>
 <p>Ai (excl.), hail! oh!</p>
 <p>Aihaigh (excl.), hail! ah! oh! More commonly pronounced <i>haihai</i>.</p>
 <p>Akare, until.</p>
 <p>Akayongh [akayon], ancient. <i>Akaion</i>, C., old, ancient, antique.</p>
 <p>Akonikonghkahdeh, they are suffering. <i>Onikonhra</i>, mind, and <i>oga'te</i>, B., raw.,<i> i.e.</i>, having a sore mind.</p>
 <p>Akotthaghyonnighshon, one who belongs to the Wolf clan. See Sathaghyonnighshon.</p>
 <p>Akwah, indeed, truly, very, yea.</p>
 <p>Akwekon, all.</p>
 <p>Are, again, sometimes.</p>
 <p>Ayakawen, one would have said. <i>En</i>, B., to say(perf. subj.).</p>
 <p>Ayakaweron, one would have thought. <i>Eron</i>, B., to think, to wish.</p>
 <p>Ayakotyerenhon, one would be startled, surprised. From <i>katyeren</i>, to wonder, be startled.</p>
 <p>Ayawenhenstokenghske [ayawenhensthokenske], may it be true. Eñon, iaweñnon, B.,--<i>iawens</i>, C., to happen; <i>togenske</i>, B., tokenske, C., it is true. "May it happen to be true!"</p>
 <p>Ayuyenkwaroghthake [ayoyenkwarodake], there might have been tobacco smoke (apparent). <i>Oienkwa</i>, C., tobacco; <i>gar<font size="1">8</font>t</i>, B., to smoke (ppf. subj.).</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">D.</p>
 <p>Da-edewenhheye [dahedewenheyeh], we may, all die. <i>Genheion</i>, <i>genheie</i>, B., to die (subj. mood).</p>
 <p><a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 193</font></a></p>
 <p>Daghsatkaghthoghseronne [dasatkahthoseronne], thou mightest keep seeing. See <i>Tesatkaghthoghserontye</i>. Tasatkahthoscronne (as the word would be spelt in modern orthography) appears to be the aorist subjunctive of <i>atkahthos</i>, to see, in the cislocative and frequentative forms.</p>
 <p>Daondayakottondeke, that they may hear. <i>Athonde</i>, to hear.</p>
 <p>Deghniwenniyu, joint ruler; lit., they two are masters. See <i>Rawenniyo</i>.</p>
 <p>Deghsewenninekenne, thou mayest speak. See <i>Entyewenninekenneh</i>.</p>
 <p>Dendewatenonghweradon, in our mutual greetings. See <i>Dewadadenonweronh</i>.</p>
 <p>Denghsatkaghdonnyonheke [densatkatonhnyonsekeh], thou wilt be looking about thee. <i>Atkahthos</i>, to see.</p>
 <p>Denighroghkwayen [dehnihrohkwayen], let us two smoke. <i>Garok<font size="1">8</font>a</i>, B., <i>une pipe, touche de petun</i>. It is conjectured that the name Iroquois, <i>i. e.</i> "Tobacco-people," may have been derived from this word. See Appendix, Note A.</p>
 <p>Dentidewaghneghdoten, we will replace the pine-tree. <i>Ohnehta</i>, pine. <i>Oten</i>, as a suffix (according to M. Cuoq), "serves to express the condition, the manner, the kind, the nature of a thing."</p>
 <p>Denyakokwatonghsaeke [tenyakokwennhendonghsaeke], he will be dying. <i>Desakk<font size="1">8</font>atonch</i>, Onon. Dict., I am dying; <i>kanon<font size="1">8</font>enton</i>, B., sick.</p>
 <p>Denyontadenakarondako, they shall take off his horns. Onakara, horn.</p>
 <p>Desahahishonne, thou art coming troubled.</p>
 <p>Desakaghserentonyonne, thou comest weeping. <i>Gagasera</i>, B., tear.</p>
 <p><a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 194</font></a></p>
 <p>Desanyatokenh, in thy throat. <i>Oniata</i>, C., throat, neck.</p>
 <p>Desawennawenrate, thy voice coming over. From <i>owenna</i>, C., <i>ga<font size="1">8</font>enda</i> or <i>ga<font size="1">8</font>eniza</i>, B., voice, speech, word, and <i>a<font size="1">8</font>enron</i>, B., to pass over. The cislocative prefix <i>de</i> (<i>te</i>) gives the sense of "hither."</p>
 <p>Deskenonghweronne [deskenonweronne], I come again to greet and thank. <i>Kannonh<font size="1">8</font>eron</i>, B., to salute any one; <i>kannonh<font size="1">8</font>eronton</i>, to salute or thank by, or for, anything. See <i>ante</i>, page 149, for an analysis of this word.</p>
 <p>Detkanoron [detkanorons], all but, almost. From <i>kanoron</i>, costly, important, difficult.</p>
 <p>Dewadadenonweronh [dewadatenonweron], mutual greeting. <i>Kannonh<font size="1">8</font>eron</i>, B., to salute any one.</p>
 <p>Dewaghsadayenhah, in the shade. <i>Asatagon</i>, B., in secret; <i>asatakon</i>, C., in the dark.</p>
 <p>Deyakodarakeh, the two clans. <i>Ohtara</i>, C., tribe, band. (Dual or duplicative form.)</p>
 <p>Deyakonakarondon, wearing horns, <i>i. e.</i>, being chiefs. <i>Onnagara</i>, B., horn; <i>kannagaront</i>, having horns; <i>gannagaronni</i>, B., être considérable.</p>
 <p>Deyughnyonkwarakda [deyohnyonkwaraktah], at the wood's edge; near the thicket. <i>Onniong<font size="1">8</font>ar</i>, B., thorn-bush, bramble; <i>akta</i>, C., beside, near to. The word applies to the line of bushes usually found on the border between the forest and a clearing. With the cislocative prefix de it means "on this side of the thicket."</p>
 <p>Deyughsihharaonh [deyohsiharaonh], there is a stoppage. <i>Gasiharon</i>, B., to stop up, to close.</p>
 <p>Deyunennyatenyon, hostile agencies, opposing forces. <i>Gannenniani</i>, B., to surprise or defeat a band; <i>gannennaton</i>, <i>ib.</i>, to seek to destroy.</p>
 <p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 195</font></a></p>
 <p>Deyunhonghdoyenghdonh [deyonhonghdoyendonh], mourning wampum. This word appears to be composed of three of Bruyas' radices, viz.,--<i>gaionni</i>, 'wampum belt (<i>collier de porcelaine</i>),--<i>gannonton</i>, to throw wampum for the dead,--and <i>gaienton</i>, to strike, whence <i>skaienton</i>, to return the like, to strike back, and <i>gaientatonton</i>, to give satisfaction for any one wounded or killed; and the meaning will be "wampum given as a satisfaction or consolation for a death."</p>
 <p>Dhatkonkoghdaghkwanyon [thatkonkohdakwanyon], in going through. <i>Ongóon</i>, B., to penetrate, to pass through; <i>atongotahkon</i>, B., the place through which one passes.</p>
 <p>Doghkara [dohkara], only a few. <i>Tohkara</i>, C., only occasionally, a few, a small number of.</p>
 <p>Doka, if, perhaps, either, or. <i>Toka</i>, C., or, if, I don't know.</p>
 <p>Donghwenghratstanyonne [donwenratstanyonne], coming over. <i>A<font size="1">8</font>enron</i>, B., to pass over.</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">E.</p>
 <p>Eghdejisewayadoreghdonh [eghdetsisewayadorehdonh], this ye considered, ye deliberated about this. <i>Kaiato`reton</i>, B., to examine, to think, to deliberate about anything.</p>
 <p>Eghdeshotiyadoreghton they again considered. (See the preceding word.)</p>
 <p>Eghnikatarakeghne [eghnikadarakene], such were the clans. <i>Ehni</i>--, C., for <i>ethoni</i>, there are, so, it is thus that; <i>ohtara</i>, clan, band.</p>
 <p>Eghnikonh, thus, in this way.</p>
 <p>Eghnonweh, thither, yonder.</p>
 <p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 196</font></a></p>
 <p>Eghtenyontatitenranyon, they will condole with one another, or, there will be mutual condolence. <i>Gentenron</i>, B., <i>kitenre</i>, C., to pity any one. <i>Atatitenron</i>, B., to deplore one's misery.</p>
 <p>Eghyendewasenghte, we will let it fall. <i>Aseñon</i>, B., to fall; <i>asenhton</i>, <i>ib.</i>, to cause to fall.</p>
 <p>Eghyesaotonnihsen, this was his uncle. See <i>yeshodonnyh</i>.</p>
 <p>Endewaghneghdotako, we will pull up a pine tree. From <i>onehta</i>, pine, and <i>gatak<font size="1">8</font>an</i>, <i>gatako</i>, to draw out, B., <i>sub voce At</i>.</p>
 <p>Enghsitskodake, thou wilt be resting, thou wilt remain. <i>Gentskòte</i>, B., to be in any place.</p>
 <p>Entyewenninekenneh, the words which will be said. From <i>Kawenna</i>, word (q. v.) and en, B., to say.</p>
 <p>Enjerennokden (or enyerennokden), they will finish the song; or, the hymn will be finished. Karenna, song, hymn; <i>okte</i>, B., the end; to finish.</p>
 <p>Enjeyewendane [enjewendane], they will be comforted. <i>Ga<font size="1">8</font>eienthon</i>, B., to be calm. (This word should probably be written <i>enjeyeweyendane</i>.)</p>
 <p>Enjondatenikonghketsko, they will comfort, lit., will raise the mind. <i>Onikonhra</i>, mind, spirit, temper, and <i>gagetsk</i><font size="1">8</font><i>an</i>, B., to raise up.</p>
 <p>Enjondentyonko. See <i>Enyonghdentionko</i>.</p>
 <p>Enjonkwakaronny, it will cause us trouble. <i>Gagaronnion</i>, B., to do harm to any one, to cause him some loss.</p>
 <p>Enjonkwanekheren, we shall suffer a loss. Wakenekheren, C., not to know, not to recognize (<i>i.e.</i>, we shall cease to see some one).</p>
 <p>Enskat, one, once.</p>
 <p>Entkaghwadasehhon, will be vexed, excited. <i>Gah<font size="1">8</font>atase</i>, B., to twist, turn round.</p>
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>
 <p>Enwadon, it will be allowed. <i>Watons</i>, fut. <i>enwaton</i>, C., to be possible, feasible, allowed.</p>
 <p>Enwadonghwenjadethare, will make a hole through the ground. See <i>Onwentsia</i>.</p>
 <p>Enyairon, they will say, one will say. From <i>en</i>, B., fut. <i>egiron</i>, to say.</p>
 <p>Enyakaonkodaghkwe [enyakaonkohdakwe], they shall have passed. <i>Ongóon</i>, B., to penetrate, pass through; <i>ongotanni</i>, to cause to penetrate, etc.</p>
 <p>Enyakodenghte, they (or one) will be miserable. <i>Genthentéon</i>, B., to be deserving of pity.</p>
 <p>Enyakodokenghse [enyakodokenseh], they (or one) will discover. <i>Gatogeñon</i>, <i>gatogens</i>, B., to know.</p>
 <p>Enyakohetsde [enyakohetste], he (or one) will go on. <i>Kohetstha</i>, C., to pass beyond.</p>
 <p>Enyakonewarontye, they (or one) will be surprised. <i>Ganne<font size="1">8</font>aron</i>, B., to surprise.</p>
 <p>Enyeharako, they will carry it. <i>Gaha</i>, B., to carry off</p>
 <p>Enyeken, they will see. <i>Gagen</i>, B., to see.</p>
 <p>Enyenikonghkwendarake, they will be mourning. Onikonhra (q. v.) and <i>gag<font size="1">8</font>entaron</i>, stretched on the ground (<i>i.e.</i>, the mind dejected).</p>
 <p>Enyerennokden. See Enjerennokden.</p>
 <p>Enyerighwanendon [enyerihwanondon], they will ask (or, will wonder). From <i>karihwa</i> (q. v.) and <i>gannendon</i>, B., to wonder, or <i>annonton</i>, to seek. <i>Garihwanonton</i>, B., to ask the news.</p>
 <p>Enyerighwawetharho, the business will be closed. <i>Karihwa</i> (q. v.) and <i>otarhon</i>, B., to grasp; <i>kotarhos</i>, C., to grasp, to stop by grasping.</p>
 <p>Enyonderennoden, they will sing it thus. <i>Karenna</i>, q. v. and --<i>oten</i>, C., which "serves to express the condition, manner, kind, or nature of a thing."</p>
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a></p>
 <p>Enyonghdentyonko, he will walk to and fro. <i>Atention</i>, B., to go away.</p>
 <p>Enyononghsaniratston, it will strengthen the house. <i>Kanonsa</i>, house, and <i>ganniraton</i>, B., to strengthen.</p>
 <p>Enyontsdaren, they will weep. <i>Katstaha</i>, C., to weep, to shed tears.</p>
 <p>Enyontyerenjiok, they will be startled. From <i>katyeren</i>, to wonder, to be surprised.</p>
 <p>Enyurighwadatye [<i>enyorihwadatye</i>], it will continue: the affair will go on. From <i>kariwa</i> (q. v.) as a verb, in the progressive form and future tense.</p>
 <p>Etho, thus, so.</p>
 <p>Ethone, then.</p>
 <p>Ethononweh, thither.</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">H.</p>
 <p>Hasekenh, because. <i>Aseken</i>, C., for, because.</p>
 <p>Henskerighwatonte [enskerighwatonte], I will frustrate their purposes. From <i>karihwa</i> (q. v.) and <i>aton`ton</i>, B., to cause to lose, to mislead.</p>
 <p>Henyondatsjistayenhaghse [henyondatstsistayenhase], they will hold a council, lit., they will make a council fire. From <i>katsista</i>, fire; <i>gatsistaien</i>, B., to hold council, to light the council fire.</p>
 <p>Hone, also. See <i>Ony</i>.</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">I.</p>
 <p>Ie [iih], I.</p>
 <p>Iese [ise], thou, ye.</p>
 <p>Iesewengh, ye have said. <i>En</i>, B., to say.</p>
 <p>Issy [hissih], yonder, there. <i>Isi</i>, C., there.</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">J.</p>
 <p>Jadadeken, thy brother (or brothers). <i>Tsiatatekenha</i>, C., ye two are brothers.</p>
 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a></p>
 <p>Jadakweniyosaon (or jatagweniyosaon), thou wert the ruler, or, ye were the rulers. See <i>Jadakweniyu</i>.</p>
 <p>Jadakweniyu, thou art the ruler, or, ye are the rulers. See note to Sec. 28, <i>ante</i>, p. 152.</p>
 <p>Jatatawhak, father and son, lit., son of each other. <i>Gahawak</i>, B., to have for child (reciprocal form).</p>
 <p>Jathondek (or jatthontek), listen! hearken thou. Imperative sing. of <i>kathontats</i>, C., <i>athontaton</i>, B., to hear.</p>
 <p>Jatthontenyonk, keep listening! continue to hear! The frequentative form of jatthontek.</p>
 <p>Ji [tsi], that, that which, wherein. See <i>Jini</i>.</p>
 <p>Jidenghnonhon [jidennon], as, like as. <i>Tennon</i>, C., and also, but.</p>
 <p>Jinayawenhon, the consequences, the results, lit. what would happen. <i>Eñon</i>, B.,--<i>iawens</i>, C., to happen.</p>
 <p>Jinesadawen [tsinesadawen]. See <i>Jinisadawen</i>.</p>
 <p>Jini [<i>tsini</i>], that which, such, so, so much.</p>
 <p>Jinihotiyerenh, what they did. From Jini (q. v.) and --<i>kierha</i>, --<i>wakieren</i>, C., to act, do, say. This verb is always preceded by some particle, such as <i>kenni</i> (see how), <i>tsini</i> (that which) and the like.</p>
 <p>Jinikawennakeh, these the words. See <i>Jini</i> and <i>kawenna</i>.</p>
 <p>Jinisayadawen [tsinesayadawenh], that which has befallen you. <i>Eñon</i>, B., to happen; <i>gaiata<font size="1">8</font>eñon</i>, to happen to some one.</p>
 <p>Jiniyuneghrakwah [tsiniyohnerakwa], this solemn event. Gonneragoon, B., to wonder; <i>jonnerag<font size="1">8</font>at</i>, that is wonderful. See <i>yuneghrakwah</i>.</p>
 <p>Jinonweh [tsinonweh], thither, whereto.</p>
 <p>Jiratighrotonghkwakwe [tsiradirohtonhkwakwe], where they, used to smoke. <i>Gar<font size="1">8</font>t</i>, B., to smoke; <i>otonkwa</i>, C., flame. "Where they lighted their pipes."</p>
 <p>Jisanakdade [tsisanakdate], from thy seat. See Kanakta.</p>
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 200</font></a></p>
 <p>Jiyudakenrokde [tsiodakenrokde], by the fireplace, near the ashes. Akenra, ashes; okte, end, edge.</p>
 <p>Jiyathondek, listen! hearken! Imperative dual of <i>kathontats</i>, I hear. See jathondek.</p>
 <p>Jodenaghstahhere, they made additions to a house; they added a frame. <i>Gannasta</i>, B., poles for making a house; <i>onasta</i>, C., a framework; <i>kaheren</i>, B. to be upon.</p>
 <p>Joskawayendon, there is again wilderness, waste ground. <i>Gaienthon</i>, B., to have fields.</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">K.</p>
 <p>Kadon, I say, I speak. <i>Igatonk</i> (<i>sub voce En</i>), B., I say; <i>katon</i>, G, to say.</p>
 <p>Kady [kadi], therefore, then. <i>Kati</i>, C., then, consequently.</p>
 <p>Kadykenh, because. See <i>Katykenh</i>.</p>
 <p>Kaghnekonyon, floods. From <i>ohneka</i>, water, in the frequentative form. <i>Gannegonnion</i>, B., there is much water.</p>
 <p>Kaghyaton, it is written. <i>Kiatons</i>, C., to write. M. Cuoq says: "the perfect participle takes an <i>h</i>: <i>kahiaton</i>, written, it is written," <i>Gaiatare</i>, B., to paint.</p>
 <p>Kajatthondek, listen! See <i>Jathondek</i>.</p>
 <p>Kakeghrondakwe, they were collected; were assembled. <i>Gageron</i>, B., to be together, or, to put things or persons somewhere.</p>
 <p>Kanaghsdajikowah [kanastatsikowah], great framework, great building. From <i>kanasta</i>, frame, and <i>kowa</i>, great.</p>
 <p>Kanakaryonniha, on a pole. <i>Gannagare</i>, B., pole, long stick.</p>
 <p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 201</font></a></p>
 <p>Kanakdakwenniyukeh, on the principal seat. From <i>kanakta</i> (q. v.), and <i>atakwenniio</i>, C., principal.</p>
 <p>Kanakdiyuhake, the place (or seat) may be good. From kanakta, place, seat, and --<i>iyu</i>, good (subjunctive mood).</p>
 <p>Kanakta, mat,--hence couch, bed, seat, place.</p>
 <p>Kaneka, where, somewhere.</p>
 <p>Kanekhere, I believe, I suppose; surely, certainly. Probably from <i>eron</i>, <i>igere</i>, B., to think, or suppose.</p>
 <p>Kanhonghdakdeh [kanonhdakdeh], by the wall, or side of the house. <i>Onnhonta</i>, wall of house, of a cabin; <i>akte</i>, beside, athwart.</p>
 <p>Kanikonrashon, the minds, a plural form of <i>Onikonhra</i>, (q. v.)</p>
 <p>Kanikonrakeh, in mind. See <i>Onikonhra</i>.</p>
 <p>Kanonghsakdatye [kanonsakdatye], outside the house. <i>Kanonsakta</i>, near the house; from <i>Kanonsa</i>, house, and <i>akta</i>, near, beside. The progressive affix <i>tye</i> gives the meaning of "passing near the house."</p>
 <p>Kanonghsakonshon [kanonsakonshon], in the house.</p>
 <p>Kanonsa, house.</p>
 <p>Kanoron, important, valuable, serious, difficult, painful, afflicting.</p>
 <p>Karenna, song, hymn, chant.</p>
 <p>Karighwakayonh, in ancient times. From <i>Karihwa</i> (q. v.), and <i>akaion</i>, old. See <i>Orighwakayongh</i>.</p>
 <p>Karighwatehkwenh [karihwahtehkonh], this word, which the interpreters rendered simply ceremony, probably means "the fire-kindling act," from <i>Karihwa</i> (q. v.), and <i>atehken</i>, or <i>atekha</i> (<i>ategen</i>, <i>atexa</i>, B.), to burn.</p>
 <p>Karihwa or karighwa (<i>garih<font size="1">8</font>a</i>, B., <i>kariwa</i>, <i>oriwa</i>, C.), thing, affair, business, action, news, word. This</p>
 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a></p>
 <p>word, in its root-form of <i>rihwa</i> (<i>riwa</i>) or <i>riho</i>, enters largely into compounds having reference to business, law, office, news, belief, and the like.</p>
 <p>Karonta, tree, log, trunk, post.</p>
 <p>Kathonghnonweh. [kathounonweh], I fail, I lose my way. <i>Atoñon</i>, B., to lose one's self, to go astray.</p>
 <p>Kathonghdeh, away, out of sight. <i>Atonhton</i>, B. (sub voce <i>atoñon</i>), to cause to lose, to mislead.</p>
 <p>Katykenh [kadikenh], how then? <i>Kati</i>, C., then (done); <i>ken</i>, interrogative particle.</p>

 <p>Wedewennakeraghdanyon (see under Wete--).</p>
 <p><a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 213</font></a></p>
 <p>Weghniserade [wenniserade], to-day. <i>Ennisera</i>, B., day; <i>nonwa wenniserate</i>, C., to-day.</p>
 <p>Wetewayennendane, we have finished. <i>Gaweyennentáon</i>, B., to rest, to cease from working.</p>
 <p>Wetewennakeraghdanyon [wedewennakeratanyon], we have made the signs, we have gone through the ceremonies. <i>Ganneraton</i>, B., "<i>se servir de règle</i>."</p>
 <b></b><p align="CENTER">Y.</p>
 <p>Yadayakonakarondatye, he may be going with horns. From <i>onakara</i>, horn (progressive form, subjunctive mood).</p>
 <p>Yadehninhohhanonghne, they two guarded the door, they two were the doorkeepers. <i>Gannhoha</i>, B., door; <i>gannonna</i>, to guard.</p>
 <p>Yaghdekakoghsonde [yaghdegagonhsonde], invisible, (lit., without face); from <i>yahte</i>, not, and <i>kakonhsa</i> (<i>okonsa</i>) face.</p>
 <p>Yaghnonwenh, never. <i>Iah-nonwenton</i>, C., never. From <i>Iah</i> (<i>yah</i>) not, and <i>nonwa</i> or <i>onwa</i>, now.</p>
 <p>Yakwenronh, we say. <i>En</i>, B., to say.</p>
 <p>Yatehhotinhohhataghkwen, they were together at the doorway (<i>i.e.</i>, they were the doorkeepers). <i>Gannhoha</i>, B., door; <i>atakon</i>, B. (<i>sub voce At</i>), "<i>ce dans quoi il y a</i>."</p>
 <p>Yatenkarighwentaseron, to finish the business. From <i>karihwa</i> (q. v.) and <i>awentas</i>, to finish.</p>
 <p>Yejisewahhawitonh, ye have taken it with you. <i>Gaha<font size="1">8</font>i</i>, B., to bring; <i>gaha<font size="1">8</font>iton</i>, to take away.</p>
 <p>Yejisewatkonseraghkwanyon, ye have it as a pillow. <i>Esakonserak<font size="1">8</font>a</i>, B., thou wilt use as a pillow.</p>
 <p>Yejisewayadkeron [yetsisewayatakeron], ye are laid together. <i>Gageron</i>, B., to be together, to place together.</p>
 <p><a name="page_214"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 214</font></a></p>
 <p>Yejodenaghstahhere, they added a frame. See <i>Jodennaghstahhere</i>.</p>
 <p>Yendewanaghsende, we will drop (or let fall) into it. <i>Aseñon</i> (?), B., to fall; <i>asenhton</i>, to cause to fall.</p>
 <p>Yenjontahidah, they will follow. <i>Gataxon</i>, <i>gataxi</i>, B., to run.</p>
 <p>Yenyontatenontshine, they are to be led by the hand. Probably from <i>gannonna</i>, B., to keep, and <i>atsi</i>, comrade.</p>
 <p>Yenyontatideron, they shall be placed. <i>Genteron</i>, B., to put any animate thing in any place.</p>
 <p>Yeshodonnyh, or Yeshotonnyh, his uncle (properly, "his father's younger brother"); also, as pl., his uncles. <i>`Atonni</i>, C., a relative on the father's side. The prefix <i>yes</i>, in which the signs of the translocative and reiterative forms are combined, gives the sense of "the next younger (uncle) but one."</p>
 <p>Yeshohawah, or Yeshohawak, his next younger child but one. See <i>Rohhawah</i>, and <i>Yeshodonnyh</i>.</p>
 <p>Yeshonadadekenah, or Yeshondadekenah, they are brothers. <i>Rontatekenha</i>, C., they are brothers together. This word is made up of the prefix <i>ye</i>, the sign of the translocative form; <i>s</i>, of the reiterative form (see <i>Yeshodonnyh</i>); <i>ron</i> or <i>rona</i>, the plural pronoun (they); <i>tate</i>, the sign of the reciprocal form; <i>ken</i>, younger brother; and <i>ha</i>, an affectionate diminutive affix, generally added to words expressing relationship.</p>
 <p>Yeshonarase, his second cousin (lit., they are cousins). <i>Arase</i>, cousin. See <i>Yeshodonnyh</i>.</p>
 <p>Yeshonaraseshen, he was their cousin. See <i>Yeshonarase</i>.</p>
 <p>Yeshotiriwayen, they have again referred the business. From <i>karihwa</i>, q. v.</p>
 <p><a name="page_215"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 215</font></a></p>
 <p>Yetsisewanenyadanyon, ye are in your graves. Perhaps from <i>onenya</i>, stone,--ye are under the stones.</p>
 <p>Yetsisewanonwadaryon, ye have taken your intellects (lit., brains) with you. <i>Ononwara</i>, C., brain, head.</p>
 <p>Yetsisewennitskagwanion, ye have placed it under you. <i>Ennitsk<font size="1">8</font>are</i>, B., to be seated on anything.</p>
 <p>Yondonghs, it is called; they call it. <i>Katon</i>, C., to say.</p>
 <p>Yonkwakaronny, they are wasting, or injuring, us. <i>Gagaronnion</i>, B., to do harm to any one; to cause him some loss.</p>
 <p>Yonkwanikonghtaghkwenne [yonkwennikondakwenne], we depended on them.</p>
 <p>Yontkwatkennison, we are assembled. <i>Atkennison</i>, B., to be assembled.</p>
 <p>Yotdakarahon [yotdarahon], things falling on one. <i>Ga`ráon</i>, B., to fall upon.</p>
 <p>Yoyanere, it is good, it is well. From the root <i>yaner</i>, noble. See <i>Kayanerenh</i>.</p>
 <p>Yuneghrakwah, solemn event. See <i>Jiniyuneghrakwah</i>.</p>
