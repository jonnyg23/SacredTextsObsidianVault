 <p>
 This is an 'interrogatorio,' a survey taken in 1811 by the
 Spanish government of Mexico regarding the status of the Native
 Californians at each mission in Alta California,
 translated and heavily annotated by A.L. Kroeber.
 Along with <a href="../bosc/index.htm">Boscana's account</a>,
 this one of the few glimpses we have of the Native Californians, when
 pre-contact individuals were still alive.
 The accounts vary from friar to friar, but some are very detailed and
 mention completely unknown placenames and languages.
 There are reasonably value-free descriptions of religious beliefs,
 mythology, language, dance, music and ethnobotany.
 </p>
 <p>
 Most notably, this includes one of the few published accounts of the
 elusive Esselen people, who inhabited the Big Sur coast (see <a href="mrci12.htm">San Carlos</a>).
 Herein is recorded probably the only remaining complete sentence in Esselen:
 <i>egenoch lalucuimxs talogpami ege salegua lottos tahezapami laxlachis</i>,
 "The men who shoot well with a bow are esteemed and well liked."
 </p>
