 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Dawn of the World</i>, by C. Hart Merriam, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THREE BIRDS SCREAM TO FRIGHTEN THE GHOSTS</h3>  
 <p><i>The Southern Mewuk of the Chowchilla region say</i>:</p>  
 <p>After a person dies his <i>Hoo'-ne</i> or Ghost sets out toward the ocean. On the way it has to cross a broad river on a log. While it is crossing on this log, three birds scream to frighten it--<i>Hek-ek'-ke</i> the Quail, <i>Ha-chah'-we</i> the Barn Owl, and <i>Hah'-jen-nah</i> a small Heron.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="green">p. 218</font></a></p>  
 <p>If the Ghost is frightened and falls into the river it turns into a fish; but if it keeps on and crosses the log it continues westerly over the ocean and goes to the place where all the Ghosts live together, and never comes back.</p>
