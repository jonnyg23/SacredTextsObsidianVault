 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Dawn of the World</i>, by C. Hart Merriam, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">GHOSTS HIDE IN STUMPS AND WHIRLWINDS</h3>  
 <p><i>The Northern Mewuk say</i>:</p>  
 <p>Sometimes when passing <i>Too'-cha-mo</i> the stump you hear a noise inside; it is <i>Soo-lek'-ko</i> the Ghost. You had better go right on, for if you stop he might do you harm.</p>  
 <p>Whenever you see <i>Poo'-ki-yu</i> the Whirlwind whirling the dust around and around and carrying it up into the air you may know that <i>Soo-lek'-ko</i> the Ghost is inside, dancing and swinging round and round. You had better not go near it but keep away.</p>
