 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Dawn of the World</i>, by C. Hart Merriam, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">NOISE</h3>  
 <p><i>The Tuolumne Mewuk say</i>:</p>  
 <p>In the beginning all noise came from water--running water. [Their word for shouting is <i>Wah-kah-lah'-loo</i>, derived from <i>Wah-kah'-loo</i>, river.]</p>  
 <p><i>The Northern Mewuk also say</i>:</p>  
 <p>All noise came in the beginning from running water; the echo originally came from rapids or boisterous water.</p>  
 <p><i>Other tribes say</i>:</p>  
 <p>Singing came from running water--the first song was sung by the creek.</p>
