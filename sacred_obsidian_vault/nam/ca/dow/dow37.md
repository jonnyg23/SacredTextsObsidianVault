 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="green">p. 199</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TAH'-LOW THE THUNDER AND TAH'-KIP' THE LIGHTNING</h3>  
 <p>KOO-LA'-IS KA'-SUM the mother Deer died, leaving two boy fawns. Their uncle, <i>O'-ye</i> the Coyote-man, sent them away to the east, where they still live.</p>  
 <p>Once when the country was very dry an old woman who wanted water, but could not find any, went and looked at the boy fawns, and they tore her eyes out. That made <i>Tah'-kip'</i> the Lightning. Then they took the dry skin of <i>Koo'-le</i> the Bear, and shook it. That made <i>Tah'-low</i> the Thunder. Then <i>Oo'-pah</i> the Rain came.</p>
