 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Dawn of the World</i>, by C. Hart Merriam, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PO'-KO-MOO THE POISON SPIDER</h3>  
 <p><i>The Northern Mewuk say</i>:</p>  
 <p><i>Po'-ko-moo</i> the small black spider with a red spot under his belly is poison. <a name="fr_20"></a><a href="#fn_20"><font size="1">22</font></a> Sometimes he scratches people with his long fingers, and the scratch makes a bad sore.</p>  
 <p>The <i>Wal'-le</i>-kah'-pah hills at the north opening are evidently the same as the Thunder Mountain of other tribes, which is always close by the north hole in the sky, in the region of extreme cold.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_215"><font size="1" color="green">p. 215</font></a></p>  
 <p>[All the tribes know that this spider is poisonous and some of them make use of the poison.]</p>
