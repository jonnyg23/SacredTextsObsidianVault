 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Religious Practices of the Diegueño Indians</i>, by T.T. Waterman, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">OTHER CEREMONIAL MATTERS.</h3>  
 <p>A moon ceremony among the Luiseño is mentioned by Miss DuBois <a name="fr_134"></a><a href="#fn_132"><font size="1">120</font></a> in the following words: " They sing 'Wy-ot, Wy-ot' nine times, then 'Ne-yonga (My head), ne-chaya, tomave.' <a name="fr_135"></a><a href="#fn_133"><font size="1">121</font></a> The dances were to please the moon and prevent his waning."</p>  
 <p>This seems to correspond to what is known as the "Sick Moon" ceremony among the Diegueño. Information about this ceremony is not very satisfactory, but the following particulars were at length obtained by questioning several informants. The ceremony is held when the moon rises in the daytime and looks small and pale. The people at this time went out and bathed themselves and had footraces "to please the moon and make him glad." At the same time the women danced, each one by herself, and sang songs. Some of these songs are the following:</p>
