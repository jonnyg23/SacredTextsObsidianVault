 <p><a name="page_387"><font size="1" color="green">p. 387</font></a></p> <h3 align="CENTER">SCENE 4</h3> <i>
 </i><p>A corridor in the palace of Chosen Virgins.</p><dir> <dir>  
 <p>(<i>Enter</i> YMA SUMAC <i>and</i> PITU SALLA.)</p></dir> </dir>  
 <p>  <i>Yma Sumac</i>. Pitu Salla, beloved friend,<br> How long wilt thou conceal from me<br> The secret that I long to know?<br> Think, dearest, of my anxious heart,<br> How I shall be in constant grief<br> Until you tell the truth to me.<br> Within these hard and cruel bounds<br> Does some one suffer for my sins?<br> My sweet companion, do not hide<br> From me, who 'tis that mourns and weeps<br> Somewhere within the garden walls.<br> How is it she is so concealed<br> That I can never find the place?<br>   <i>Pitu Salla</i>. My Sumac, now I'll tell thee all--<br> Only concerning what you hear,<br> And still more surely what you see,<br> You must be dumb as any stone;<br> And you too must be well prepared<br> For a most sad heart-rending sight--<br> 'Twill make thee weep for many days.<br>   <i>Yma Sumac</i>. I will not tell a living soul<br> What you divulge. But tell me all,<br> I'll shut it closely in my heart.</p>
