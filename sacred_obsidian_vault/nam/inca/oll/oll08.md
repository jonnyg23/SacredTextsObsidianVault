 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Apu Ollantay</i>, by Clements Markham, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">SCENE 5</h3> <i>
 </i><p>The great hall of the palace of Pachacuti. <i>The</i> INCA, <i>as before, seated on the</i> tiana. <i>Enter to him</i> RUMI-ÑAUI.</p> 
 <p>  <i>Pachacuti</i>. I ordered a search to be made,<br> But Ollantay was not to be found.<br> My rage I can scarcely control--<br> Hast thou found this infamous wretch?</p> 
 <p><a name="page_366"><font size="1" color="green">p. 366</font></a></p> 
 <p>  <i>Rumi-ñaui</i>. His fear makes him hide from thy wrath.<br>   <i>Pachacuti</i>. Take a thousand men fully armed,<br> And at once commence the pursuit.<br>   <i>Rumi-ñaui</i>. Who can tell what direction to take?<br> Three days have gone by since his flight,<br> Perchance he 's concealed in some house,<br> And till now he is there, safely hid.</p><dir> <dir>  
 <p>(<i>Enter a </i>chasqui<i> or messenger with</i> quipus.)</p></dir> </dir>  
 <p>Behold, O King, a messenger<br> From Urubamba he has come.<br>   <i>Chasqui</i>. I was ordered to come to my King,<br> Swift as the wind, and behold me.<br>   <i>Pachacuti</i>. What news bringest thou?<br>   <i>Chasqui</i>. This <i>quipu</i> will tell thee, O King.<br>   <i>Pachacuti</i>. Examine it, O Rumi-ñaui.<br>   <i>Rumi-ñaui</i>. Behold the <i>llanta</i>, and the knots <a name="fr_35"></a><a href="#fn_35"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Announce the number of his men.<br>   <i>Pachacuti</i> (<i>to</i> Chasqui). And thou, what hast thou seen?<br>   <i>Chasqui</i>. 'Tis said that all the Anti host<br> Received Ollantay with acclaim;<br> Many have seen, and they recount,<br> Ollantay wears the royal fringe.<br>   <i>Rumi-ñaui</i>. The <i>quipu</i> record says the same.<br>   <i>Pachacuti</i>. Scarcely can I restrain my rage!<br> Brave chief, commence thy march at once,<br> Before the traitor gathers strength.<br> If thy force is not enough,<br> Add fifty thousand men of mine.<br> Advance at once with lightning speed,<br> And halt not till the foe is reached.<br>   <i>Rumi-ñaui</i>. To-morrow sees me on the route,</p> 
 <p>
 </p> 
 <p><a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="green">p. 367</font></a></p> 
 <p>I go to call the troops at once<br> The rebels on the Colla road,<br> I drive them flying down the rocks.<br> Thine enemy I bring to thee,<br> Dead or alive, Ollantay falls.<br> Meanwhile, O Inca, mighty Lord,<br> Rest and rely upon thy thrall.</p> 
 <p align="RIGHT">[<i>Exeunt</i>.</p> 
 <p>END OF ACT I.</p>
