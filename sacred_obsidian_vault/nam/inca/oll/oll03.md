 <p><a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="green">p. 339</font></a></p> <h1 align="CENTER">OLLANTAY DRAMATIS PERSONÆ</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">SCENE</h3> 
 <p align="CENTER">In <i>Cuzco</i> and its environs, and <i>Ollantay-tampu</i></p> <h3 align="CENTER">DRAMATIS PERSONÆ</h3> 
 <p>APU OLLANTAY.--General of <i>Anti-suyu</i>, the eastern province of the empire. A young chief, but not of the blood-royal. His rank was that of a <i>Tucuyricuo</i> or Viceroy. The name occurs among the witnesses examined by order of the Viceroy Toledo, being one of the six of the <i>Antasayac ayllu</i>.</p> 
 <p>PACHACUTI.--The Sovereign Inca.</p> 
 <p>TUPAC YUPANQUI.--Sovereign. Inca, son and heir of Pachacuti.</p> 
 <p>RUMI-ÑAUI.--A great chief, General of <i>Colla-suyu</i>. The word means 'Stone-eye.'</p> 
 <p>UILLAC UMA.--High Priest of the Sun. The word <i>Uma</i> means head, and <i>Uillac</i>, a councillor and diviner.</p> 
 <p>URCO HUARANCA.--A chief. The words mean' Mountain Chief.' The word <i>huaranca</i> means 1000; hence, Chief of a Thousand.</p> 
 <p>HANCO HUAYLLU AUQUI.--An old officer, of the blood-royal.</p> 
 <p>PIQUI CHAQUI.--Page to Ollantay. The words mean 'fleet-footed.'</p> 
 <p>ANAHUARQUI.--The <i>Ccoya</i> or Queen, wife of Pachacuti.</p> 
 <p><a name="page_340"><font size="1" color="green">p. 340</font></a></p> 
 <p>CUSI COYLLUR ÑUSTA.--A Princess, daughter of Pachacuti. The words mean 'the joyful star.'</p> 
 <p>YMA SUMAC.--Daughter of Cusi Coyllur. The words mean 'How beautiful.'</p> 
 <p>PITU SALLA.--A girl, companion of Yma Sumac.</p> 
 <p>CCACCA MAMA.--A matron of Virgins of the Sun. Jailer of Cusi Coyllur.</p> <i>
 </i><p>Nobles, captains, soldiers, boys and girls dancing, singers, attendants, messengers or Chasqui.</p>
