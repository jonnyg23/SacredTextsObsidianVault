 <h1 align="CENTER">XVIII. Hymn to the Master of Waters.</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">XVIII. Atlaua Icuic.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a href="#english">English</a></p>
 <center>
 <img src="rva04.jpg">
 <p>
 <font face="arial" size="2">
 ATLAUA, SINGING AND DANCING HYMN
 </font>
 </p>
 </center>
 <p>1. Auia nichalmecatl, nichalmecatl, neçaualcautla, neçaualcautla, olya quatonalla olya.</p>
 <p>2. Ueya, ueya, macxoyauh quilazteutl y tlapani macxoyauh.</p>
 <p>3. Nimitz acatecunotzaya, chimalticpac moneçoya nimitzacatecunotzaya.</p>
 <p>4. Ayac nomiuh timalla aytolloca nacatl nomiuh aca xeliui timalla.</p>
 <p>5. Tetoma amo yolcana tlamacazquinte tometl, açan axcan ye quetzaltototl, nic ya izcaltiquetla.</p>
 <p>6. Y yopuchi noteuh atlauaquetl, aça naxcan ye quetzaltototl, nic ya izcaltiquetla.</p>
 <p>1. Neçaualcactla. 2. Itlamani. 4. Aitollaca acatl. Timalli. 5. Tetonac amo yolcana tlamacaz quin tetometl.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Gloss.</h3>
 <p>1. <i>Q. n.</i>, ynichalmecatl, yn ineçaualac oqixicauhteuac y nioholti, y nioya, ixquatechimal iquatunal.</p>
 <p>2. <i>Q. n.</i>, ma xiyauh ti quilazteutl, momactemi in macxoyauh.</p>
 <p>3. <i>Q. n.</i>, iniquac onimitznotz, mochimalticpac timiçoya.</p>
 <p>4. <i>Q. n.</i>, atle nomiuh yc notimaloa, ca uel itoloc in acatl nomiuh, yn acatl xeliui yc ninotimaloa.</p>
 <p>5. <i>Q. n.</i>, oncan euac in tetuman nitlacochtetumetl. Auh inaxcan ye quetzaltotol inic ni tlazcaltia.</p>
 <p>{p. 64}</p>
 <p>6, <i>Q. n.</i>, tiacauh in oteuh in atlaua, auh inaxcan yuhqui quetzaltotol in nitlazcaltia.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER"><a name="english">The Hymn of Atlaua.</a></h3>
 <p>1. I Chalmecatl, I Chalmecatl, I leave behind my sandles, I leave my sandles and my helmet.</p>
 <p>2. Go ye forth and follow the goddess Quilaztli, follow her</p>
 <p>3. I shall call upon thee to arise when among the shields, I shall call upon thee to arise.</p>
 <p>4. I boast of my arrows, even my reed arrows, I boast of my arrows, not to be broken.</p>
 <p>5. Arrayed in priestly garb, take the arrow in thy band, for even now I shall arise and come forth like the quetzal bird.</p>
 <p>6. Mighty is my god Atlaua; truly I shall arise and come forth like the quetzal bird.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Notes.</h3>
 <p>Atlaua, mentioned by Olmos, who translates the word "Master of waters," is a divinity of whom little is known. The derivation from <i>atlatl</i>, arrow, would seem more appropriate to the words of this hymn. Chalmecatl, used as a synonym in v. 1, appears to be from <i>chalania</i>, to beat, to strike, as a drum.</p>
 <p>On <i>Quilaztli</i> see notes to Hymn XIII.</p></body>
