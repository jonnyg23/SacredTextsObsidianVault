 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XXXVI</h5>  <h5 align="center">SCALP DANCE SONG</h5>  
 <p>Next after comes Coyote, Stretched-Out-in Dew,<br> Next after braves of yesterday or the day before!<br> To Blue Earth town of the Navahos we go<br> And arriving we shall kill. So that is why<br> Coyote, Stretched-Out-in Dew, sits straight and ready.<br> <i>Wi-ya-he-na, a-nde-a-a</i>. The next scalp!</p>  
 <p>Navajo youths! your fault alone it is<br> That now you die fallen along your house.<br> Your fault alone it is that now you die<br> Fallen along your house with earth-streaked thighs,<br> That now your mouths are stopped and streaked with earth.<br> <i>Ho-o-wi-na, a-ye-a-a</i>. The next scalp!</p>
