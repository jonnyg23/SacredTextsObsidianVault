 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">IV</h5>  <h5 align="center">THE WILLOWS BY THE WATER SIDE</h5>  
 <p>My little breath, under the willows by the water side we used to sit<br> And there the yellow cottonwood bird came and sang.<br> That I remember and therefore I weep.<br> Under the growing corn we used to sit,<br> And there the little leaf bird carne and sang.<br> That I remember and therefore I weep.<br> There on the meadow of yellow flowers we used to walk<br> Oh, my little breath! Oh, my little heart!<br> There on the meadow of blue flowers we used to walk.<br> Alas! how long ago that we two walked in that pleasant way.<br> Then everything was happy, but, alas! how long ago.<br> There on the meadow of crimson flowers we used to walk.<br> Oh, my little breath, now I go there alone in sorrow.</p>
