 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XXXVIII</h5>  <h5 align="center">SONG OF THE HUNTER'S WIFE</h5>  
 <p>Now comes the deer up to our house<br> He brings the needed food of life,<br> While we give needed food to him.</p>  
 <p>Old women deer, old men deer,<br> We love you! So now come hither,<br> On the road that we have laid!<br> Come hither to our house where we<br> May love you! Eat now this little!</p>
