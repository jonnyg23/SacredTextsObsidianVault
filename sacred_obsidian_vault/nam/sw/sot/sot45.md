 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XL</h5>  <h5 align="center">WHEN THE DEER COME</h5>  
 <p><i>So the deer will hear</i><br>     Our elder brother deer,<br>     Our elder sister deer,<br>     We are going to meet you,<br>     We are going to meet you.</p>  
 <p><i>They meet the deer</i><br>     Then deer will come,<br>     Then deer will come,<br>     Come on with us<br>     Come on with us.<br>     <i>Na-e-e-e, Ya-e-a-e-na<br> </i>    <i>Na-e-e-e, ya-e-e-e-na</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>When the deer dance</i>.<br>     Now you follow us<br>     Now you follow us<br>     Here we are dancing<br>     Here we are dancing<br>     In this manner<br>     In this manner<br>     <i>Yo-ha-ri-yo, yo-ha-ri-yo<br> </i>    <i>A-a-a-he-yo-o</i>.</p>
