 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XXX</h5>  <h5 align="center">THE CORN-SILK-WOMEN'S SONG</h5>  
 <p>Ones of the Northern Lake<br> Corn-Silk-Women ye are,<br> And now ye come to us!</p>  
 <p>Then lay long life at once upon us,<br> And upon our children<br> The love of all the gods!</p>  
 <p>May our children have many children<br> And our girls of San Juan live long!</p>  
 <p>And now we seek to hear<br> The Corn-Silk-Women say<br> Such words as we have said!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="green">p. 95</font></a></p>  
 <p>Long life now we ask,<br> And to be loved,<br> And to rear many children,<br> And to be given kindly fates!</p>
