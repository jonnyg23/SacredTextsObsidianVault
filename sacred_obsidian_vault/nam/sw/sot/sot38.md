 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_98"><font size="1" color="green">p. 98</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">XXXIV</h5>  <h5 align="center">SONGS OF THE RACE DANCE</h5>  
 <p><i>The Summer People</i></p>  
 <p>Old Man of the Sun<br> Stand ready at dawn<br> On Cactus Ridge!</p>  
 <p>Old Man of the Moon!<br> Stand ready at dawn<br> On Cactus Ridge!</p>  
 <p>Stand ready at dawn,<br> Thence for San Juan!<br> Stand ready at dawn,<br> For Eagle-Tail-Rain-Standing Road!</p>  
 <p><i>The Winter People</i></p>  
 <p>Little Twin White Men,<br> Of Stone Man Mountain,<br> Stand ready at dawn!</p>  
 <p>Thence for San Juan<br> Thence for Rain-Standing Road<br> At San Juan!</p>  
 <p>Man of the Great Night-Star<br> Stand ready at dawn<br> Thence for San Juan!</p>
