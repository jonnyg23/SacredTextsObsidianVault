 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">VI</h5>  <h5 align="center">LOST LOVE</h5>  
 <p>At Su k’wa k’e there used to bloom a flower—<br> That flower, that flower, whene’er I see it now<br> Alas, so far away, why then I weep;<br> That flower, that flower, whene’er I see it now,<br> For yellow, fresh and full-blown once it bloomed.</p>
