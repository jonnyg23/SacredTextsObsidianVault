 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XIX</h5>  <h5 align="center">THE CLOUD-FLOWER LULLABY</h5>  
 <p>In the north the cloud flower blossoms,<br> And now the lightning flashes,<br> And now the thunder clashes<br> And now the rain comes down!<br> <i>A-a-aha, a-a-aha</i>, my little one.</p>  
 <p>In the west the cloud flower blossoms,<br> And now the lightning flashes,<br> And now the thunder clashes,<br> And now the rain comes down!<br> <i>A-a-aha, a-a-aha</i>, my little one.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_81"><font size="1" color="green">p. 81</font></a></p>  
 <p>In the south the cloud flower blossoms,<br> And now the lightning flashes,<br> And now the thunder clashes,<br> And now the rain comes down!<br> <i>A-a-aha, a-a-aha</i>, my little one.</p>  
 <p>In the east the cloud flower blossoms,<br> And now the lightning flashes,<br> And now the thunder clashes,<br> And now the rain comes down!<br> <i>A-a-aha, a-a-aha</i>, my little one.</p>
