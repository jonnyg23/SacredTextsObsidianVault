 <h3 align="CENTER">PLATE 11</h3>
 <p>Sun and Moon paintings (These figures are used in sand painting or on kiva wall to get power from Sun and Moon.)</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_pl11a"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/pl11a.jpg"><img src="tn/pl11a.jpg" alt="Plate 11, Figure 1"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>Plate 11, Figure 1<br></p>
 <p>FIGURE 1. The Sun. The face is red because the Sun is male and the giver of strong light; ornament on forehead (brown and black triangles, divided by white line) represents a squash blossom; two outer rims are green and orange; projections (orange) represent beams of light.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_pl11b"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/pl11b.jpg"><img src="tn/pl11b.jpg" alt="Plate 11, Figure 2"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>Plate 11, Figure 2<br></p>
 <p>FIGURE 2. The Moon. The face is yellow because the Moon is female, and the light is pale; the two outer rims (green and red) are the "ring about the moon"; ornament (brown and black triangles, divided by white line) represents a squash blossom.</p>
