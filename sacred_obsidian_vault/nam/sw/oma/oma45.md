 <h3 align="CENTER">PLATE 12</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_pl12a"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/pl12a.jpg"><img src="tn/pl12a.jpg" alt="Plate 12, Figure 1"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>Plate 12, Figure 1<br></p>
 <p>FIGURE 1. Drum. Sides are yellow with vertical blue lines; band at base, blue with white scallops; top blue; katsina symbol in center of top, yellow with green and white band and hornlike projections of red; boatlike object lower edge</p>
 <p><a name="page_122"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 122</font></a></p>
 <p>of top, yellow with blue in middle section; star on top, yellow. Symbolism of colors: Blue for sky, yellow for earth. Boatlike object represents clouds resting on horns. Corn or seed are inside drum to be the heart.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_pl12b"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/pl12b.jpg"><img src="tn/pl12b.jpg" alt="Plate 12, Figure 2"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a><br>Plate 12, Figure 2<br></p>
 <p>FIGURE 2. Katsina costume. <i>a</i>, Mantle of impersonator of female katsina, Made of cotton; the design is embroidered on one side with colored yarns; top, black and green in 4-day period; white line is a trail; serrations top and bottom represent waves of water; white body represents the earth. Unfinished (no eyes) katsina masks; the diamond (red, yellow) represents feathers on the mask, the terraces (red, yellow) on the mask face represent fields; the median line on the face represents the 4-day-and-night period. <i>b</i>, Kilt worn by impersonator of male katsina: Designs are fields (red, blue) with part of katsina face. Straight lines (red) for rain, water; bottom line (black) for the 4-night period.</p>
