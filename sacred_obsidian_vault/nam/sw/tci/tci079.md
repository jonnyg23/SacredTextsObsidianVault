 <h3 align="CENTER">COYOTE SINGS FOR THE PRAIRIE DOGS <a name="fr_159"></a><a href="#fn_158"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>
 <p>In the mountains Old Coyote Mother and Father were living. Old Man Coyote had a little drum and he sat above the road and beat the drum and sang,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p><font size="-1">Look out! Look out!<br>
 Coyote is going to hit you<br>
 On the back, on the back.</font></p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> Lots of prairie dogs came running. They cried, "Dear me, grandfather. How beautifully you sing! Sing it again and we'll dance to it." Coyote began to beat his drum again and the prairie dogs made a circle and stood out to dance. Coyote sang again,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p><font size="-1">Look out! Look out!<br>
 Coyote is going to hit you<br>
 On the back, on the back.</font></p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> They stepped in time to the song. "Grandfather, sing it again. It is such fun to dance to your song." They danced and danced.</p>
 <p>Grandmother Coyote called, "Come on, grandfather. Let me sing a while for them." The prairie dogs said to Grandfather Coyote, "You sing so nice, sing again." "No, your grandmother is in a hurry." "We know she is calling you, but please sing some more. You sing such a beautiful song. Sing it again and we can learn that song." <a name="fr_160"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>
