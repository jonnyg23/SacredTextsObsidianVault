 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_1"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_1.jpg"></p>
 <p>1. At first, in that place, at all times, above the earth,</p>
 <p>1. Sayewi talli wemiguma wokgetaki,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_2"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_2.jpg"></p>
 <p>2. On the earth, [was] an extended fog, and there the great Manito was.</p>
 <p>2. Hackung kwelik owanaku wak yutali Kitanitowit-essop.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_3"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_3.jpg"></p>
 <p>3. At first, forever, lost in space, everywhere, the great Manito was.</p>
 <p>3. Sayewis hallemiwis nolemiwi elemamik Kitanitowit-essop.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_4"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_4.jpg"></p>
 <p>4. He made the extended land and the sky.</p>
 <p>4. Sohawalawak kwelik hakik owak [read, woak] awasagamak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_5"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_5.jpg"></p>
 <p>5. He made the sun, the moon, the stars.</p>
 <p>5. Sohalawak gishuk nipahum alankwak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_6"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_6.jpg"></p>
 <p>6. He made them all to move evenly.</p>
 <p>6. Wemi-sohalawak yulikyuchaan.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_7"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_7.jpg"></p>
 <p>7. Then the wind blew violently, and it cleared, and the water flowed off far and strong.</p>
 <p>7. Wich-owagan kshakan moshakwat [Var. moshakguat.] kwelik kshipehelep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_8"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_8.jpg"></p>
 <p>8. And groups of islands grew newly, and there remained.</p>
 <p>8. Opeleken mani-menak delsin-epit.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_9"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_9.jpg"></p>
 <p>9. Anew spoke the great Manito, a manito to manitos,</p>
 <p>9. Lappinup Kitanitowit manito manitoak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_10"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_10.jpg"></p>
 <p>10. To beings, mortals, souls and all,</p>
 <p>10. Owiniwak angelatawiwak chichankwak wemiwak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_11"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_11.jpg"></p>
 <p>11. And ever after he was a manito to men, and their grandfather.</p>
 <p>11. Wtenk manito jinwis lennowak mukom.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_12"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_12.jpg"></p>
 <p>12. He gave the first mother, the mother of beings.</p>
 <p>12. Milap netami gaho owini gaho.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_13"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_13.jpg"></p>
 <p>13. He gave the fish, he gave the turtles, he gave the beasts, he gave the birds.</p>
 <p>13. Namesik milap, tulpewik milap, awesik milap, cholensak milap.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_14"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_14.jpg"></p>
 <p>14. But an evil Manito made evil beings only, monsters,</p>
 <p>14. Makimani shak sohalawak makowini nakowak amangamek.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_15"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_15.jpg"></p>
 <p>15. He made the flies, he made the gnats.</p>
 <p>15. Sohalawak uchewak, sohalawak pungusak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_16"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_16.jpg"></p>
 <p>16. All beings were then friendly.</p>
 <p>16. Nitisak wemi owini w'delisinewuap.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_17"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_17.jpg"></p>
 <p>17. Truly the manitos were active and kindly</p>
 <p>17. Kiwis, wunand wishimanitoak essopak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_18"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_18.jpg"></p>
 <p>18. To those very first men, and to those first mothers; fetched them wives,</p>
 <p>18. Nijini netami lennowak, nigoha netami okwewi, nantine'wak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_19"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_19.jpg"></p>
 <p>19. And fetched them food, when first they desired it.</p>
 <p>19. Gattamin netami mitzi nijini nantine'.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_20"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_20.jpg"></p>
 <p>20. All had cheerful knowledge, all had leisure, all thought in gladness.</p>
 <p>20. Wemi wingi-namenep, wemi ksinelendamep, wemi wullatemanuwi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_21"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_21.jpg"></p>
 <p>21. But very secretly an evil being, a mighty magician, came on earth,</p>
 <p>21. Shukand eli-kimi mekenikink wakon powako init'ako.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_22"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_22.jpg"></p>
 <p>22. And with him brought badness, quarreling, unhappiness,</p>
 <p>22. Mattalogas pallalogas maktaton owagan payat-chik yutali.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_23"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_23.jpg"></p>
 <p>23. Brought bad weather, brought sickness, brought death.</p>
 <p>23. Maktapan payat, wihillan payat, mboagan payat.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa1_24"></a>
 <img src="img/wa1_24.jpg"></p>
 <p>24. All this took place of old on the earth, beyond the great tide-water, at the first.</p>
 <p>24. Won wemi wiwunch kamik atak kitahikan netamaki epit.</p>
