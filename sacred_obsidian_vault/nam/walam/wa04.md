 <img src="img/wa4_1.jpg"></p>
 <p>1. Long ago the fathers of the Lenape were at the land of spruce pines.</p>
 <p>1. Wulamo linapioken manup shinaking.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_2"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_2.jpg"></p>
 <p>2. Hitherto the Bald Eagle band had been the pipe bearer,</p>
 <p>2. Wapallanewa sittamaganat yukepechi wemima,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_3"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_3.jpg"></p>
 <p>3. While they were searching for the Snake Island, that great and fine land.</p>
 <p>3. Akhomenis michihaki wellaki kundokanup.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_4"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_4.jpg"></p>
 <p>4. They having died, the hunters, about to depart, met together.</p>
 <p>4. Angomelchik elowichik elmusichik menalting.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_5"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_5.jpg"></p>
 <p>5. All say to Beautiful Head, "Be thou chief."</p>
 <p>5. Wemilo kolawil sakima lissilma.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_6"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_6.jpg"></p>
 <p>6. "Coming to the Snakes, slaughter at that Snake hill, that they leave it."</p>
 <p>6. Akhopayat kihillalend akhopokho aski'waal.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_7"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_7.jpg"></p>
 <p>7. All of the Snake tribe were weak, and hid themselves in the Swampy Vales.</p>
 <p>7. Showihilla akhowemi gandhaton mashkipokhing.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_8"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_8.jpg"></p>
 <p>8. After Beautiful Head, White Owl was chief at Spruce Pine land.</p>
 <p>8. Wtenkolawil shinaking sakimanep wapagokhos.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_9"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_9.jpg"></p>
 <p>9. After him, Keeping-Guard was chief of that people.</p>
 <p>9. Wtenk nekama sakimanep janoto enolowin.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_10"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_10.jpg"></p>
 <p>10. After him, Snow Bird was chief; he spoke of the south,</p>
 <p>10. Wtenk nekama sakimanep chilili shawaniluen.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_11"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_11.jpg"></p>
 <p>11. That our fathers should possess it by scattering abroad.</p>
 <p>11. Wokenapi nitaton wullaton apakchikton.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_12"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_12.jpg"></p>
 <p>12. Snow Bird went south, White Beaver went east.</p>
 <p>12. Shawaniwaen chilili, wapaniwaen tamakwi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_13"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_13.jpg"></p>
 <p>13. The Snake land was at the south, the great Spruce Pine land was toward the shore;</p>
 <p>13. Akolaki shawanaki, kitshinaki shabiyaki.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_14"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_14.jpg"></p>
 <p>14. To the east was the Fish land, toward the lakes was the buffalo land.</p>
 <p>14. Wapanaki namesaki, pemapaki sisilaki.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_15"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_15.jpg"></p>
 <p>15. After Snow Bird, the Seizer was chief, and all were killed,</p>
 <p>15. Wtenk chilili sakimanep ayamek weminilluk.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_16"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_16.jpg"></p>
 <p>16. The robbers, the snakes, the evil men, the stone men.</p>
 <p>16. Chikonapi akhonapi makatapi assinapi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_17"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_17.jpg"></p>
 <p>17. After the Seizer there were ten chiefs, and there was much warfare south and east.</p>
 <p>17. Wtenk ayamek tellen sakimak machi tonanup shawapama.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_18"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_18.jpg"></p>
 <p>18. After them, the Peaceable was chief at Snake land.</p>
 <p>18. Wtenk nellamawa sakimanep langundowi akolaking.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_19"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_19.jpg"></p>
 <p>19. After him, Not-Black was chief, who was a straight man.</p>
 <p>19. Wtenk nekama sakimanep tasukamend shakagapipi.</p>
