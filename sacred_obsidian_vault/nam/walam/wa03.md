 <p>1. After the rushing waters (had subsided) the Lenape of the turtle were close together, in hollow houses, living together there.</p>
 <p>1. Pehella wtenk lennapeva tulapewini psakwiken woliwikgun wittank talli.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_2"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_2.jpg"></p>
 <p>2. It freezes where they abode, it snows where they abode, it storms where they abode, it is cold where they abode.</p>
 <p>2. Topan-akpinep, wineu-akpinep, kshakan-akpinep, thupin akpinep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_3"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_3.jpg"></p>
 <p>3. At this northern place they speak favorably of mild, cool (lands), with many deer and buffaloes.</p>
 <p>3. Lowankwaminkwulaton wtakan tihill kelik meshautang sili ewak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_4"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_4.jpg"></p>
 <p>4. As they journeyed, some being strong, some rich, they separated into house-builders and hunters;</p>
 <p>4. Chintanes-sin powalessin peyachik wikhichik pokwihil.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_5"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_5.jpg"></p>
 <p>5. The strongest, the most united, the purest, were the hunters.</p>
 <p>5. Eluwi-chitanesit eluwi takauwesit, elowi chiksit, elowichik delsinewo.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_6"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_6.jpg"></p>
 <p>6. The hunters showed themselves at the north, at the east, at the south, at the west.</p>
 <p>6. Lowaniwi, wapaniwi, shawaniwi, wunkeniwi, elowichik apakachik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_7"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_7.jpg"></p>
 <p>7. In that ancient country, in that northern country, in that turtle country, the best of the Lenape were the Turtle men.</p>
 <p>7. Lumowaki, lowanaki tulpenaki elowaki tulapiwi linapiwi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_8"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_8.jpg"></p>
 <p>8. All the cabin fires of that land were disquieted, and all said to their priest, "Let us go."</p>
 <p>8. Wemiako yagawan tendki lakkawelendam nakopowa wemi owenluen atam.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_9"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_9.jpg"></p>
 <p>9. To the Snake land to the east they went forth, going away, earnestly grieving.</p>
 <p>9. Akhokink wapaneu wemoltin palliaal kitelendam aptelendam.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_10"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_10.jpg"></p>
 <p>10. Split asunder, weak, trembling, their land burned, they went, torn and broken, to the Snake Island.</p>
 <p>10. Pechimuin shakowen [Var. showoken] nungihillan lusasaki pikihil pokwihil akomenaki.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_11"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_11.jpg"></p>
 <p>11. Those from the north being free, without care, went forth from the land of snow, in different directions.</p>
 <p>11. Nihillapewin komelendam lowaniwi wemiten chihillen maniaken.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_12"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_12.jpg"></p>
 <p>12. The fathers of the Bald Eagle and the White Wolf remain along the sea, rich in fish and muscles.</p>
 <p>12. Namesuagipek pokhapockhapek guneunga waplanewa ouken waptumewi ouken.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_13"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_13.jpg"></p>
 <p>13. Floating up the streams in their canoes, our fathers were rich, they were in the light, when they were at those islands.</p>
 <p>13. Amokolon nallahemen agunouken pawasinep wapasinep akomenep.[Var. menakinep.]</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_14"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_14.jpg"></p>
 <p>14. Head Beaver and Big Bird said, "Let us go to Snake Island," they said.</p>
 <p>14. Wihlamok kicholen luchundi, Wematam akomen luchundi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_15"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_15.jpg"></p>
 <p>15. All say they will go along to destroy all the land.</p>
 <p>15. Witehen wemiluen wemaken nihillen.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_16"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_16.jpg"></p>
 <p>16. Those of the north agreed, Those of the east agreed. Over the water, the frozen sea, They went to enjoy it.</p>
 <p>16. Nguttichin lowaniwi, Nguttichin wapaniwi, Agamunk topanpek Wulliton epannek.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_17"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_17.jpg"></p>
 <p>17. On the wonderful slippery water, On the stone-hard water all went, On the great Tidal Sea, the muscle-bearing sea. They walk and walk, all of them.</p>
 <p>17. Wulelemil w'shakuppek, Wemopannek hakhsinipek, Kitahikan pokhakhopek.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_18"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_18.jpg"></p>
 <p>18. Ten thousand at night, All in one night, To the Snake Island, to the east, at night, They walk and walk, all of them.</p>
 <p>18. Tellenchen kittapakki nillawi, Wemoltin gutikuni nillawi, Akomen wapanawaki nillawi, Ponskan, ponskan, wemiwi olini.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_19"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_19.jpg"></p>
 <p>19. The men from the north, the east, the south, The Eagle clan, the Beaver clan, the Wolf clan, The best men, the rich men, the head men, Those with wives, those with daughters, those with dogs, </p>
 <p>19, Lowanapi, wapanapi, shawanapi, Lanewapi, tamakwapi, tumewapi, Elowapi, powatapi, wilawapi, Okwisapi, danisapi, allumapil,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa3_20"></a>
 <img src="img/wa3_20.jpg"></p>
 <p>20. They all come, they tarry at the land of the spruce pines; Those from the west come with hesitation, Esteeming highly their old home at the Turtle land.</p>
 <p>20. Wemipayat gune'unga shinaking, Wunkenapi chanelendam payaking, Allowelendam kowiyey tulpaking.</p>
