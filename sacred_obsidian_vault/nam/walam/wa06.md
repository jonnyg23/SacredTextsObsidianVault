 <p>40. Rich-Again was chief; the Painted-One was chief.</p>
 <p>40. Lapawin sakimanep, wallama sakimanep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_41"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_41.jpg"></p>
 <p>41. White-Fowl was chief; again there was war, north and south.</p>
 <p>41. Waptipatit sakimanep, lappi mahuk lowashawa.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_42"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_42.jpg"></p>
 <p>42. The Wolf-wise-in Counsel was chief.</p>
 <p>42. Wewoattan menatting tumaokan sakimanep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_43"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_43.jpg"></p>
 <p>43. He knew how to make war on all; he slew Strong-Stone.</p>
 <p>43. Nitatonep wemi palliton maskansini nihillanep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_44"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_44.jpg"></p>
 <p>44. The Always-Ready-One was chief; he fought against the Snakes.</p>
 <p>44. Messissuwi sakimanep akowini pallitonep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_45"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_45.jpg"></p>
 <p>45. The Strong-Good-One was chief, he fought against the northerners.</p>
 <p>45. Chitanwulit sakimanep lowanuski pallitonep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_46"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_46.jpg"></p>
 <p>46. The Lean-One was chief; he fought against the Tawa people.</p>
 <p>46. Alokuwi sakimanep towakon pallitonep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_47"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_47.jpg"></p>
 <p>47. The Opossum-Like was chief; he fought in sadness,</p>
 <p>47. Opekasit sakimanep sakhelendam pallitonepit.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_48"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_48.jpg"></p>
 <p>48. And said, "They are many; let us go together to the east, to the sunrise."</p>
 <p>48. Wapagishik yuknohokluen makeluhuk wapaneken.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_49"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_49.jpg"></p>
 <p>49. They separated at Fish river; the lazy ones remained there.</p>
 <p>49. Tsebepieken nemassipi [Var. mixtisipi.] nolandowak gunehunga.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_50"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_50.jpg"></p>
 <p>50. Cabin-Man was chief; the Talligewi possessed the east.</p>
 <p>50. Yagawanend sakimanep talligewi wapawullaton.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_51"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_51.jpg"></p>
 <p>51. Strong-Friend was chief; he desired the eastern land.</p>
 <p>51. Chitanitis sakimanep wapawaki gotatamen.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_52"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_52.jpg"></p>
 <p>52. Some passed on east; the Talega ruler killed some of them.</p>
 <p>52. Wapallendi pomisinep talegawil allendhilla.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_53"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_53.jpg"></p>
 <p>53. All say, in unison, "War, war."</p>
 <p>53. Mayoksuwi wemilowi palliton palliton.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_54"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_54.jpg"></p>
 <p>54. The Talamatan, friends from the north, come, and all go together.</p>
 <p>54. Talamatan nitilowan payatchik wemiten.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_55"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_55.jpg"></p>
 <p>55. The Sharp-One was chief, he was the pipe-bearer beyond the river.</p>
 <p>55. Kinehepend sakimanep tamaganat sipakgamen.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_56"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_56.jpg"></p>
 <p>56. They rejoiced greatly that they should fight and slay the Talega towns.</p>
 <p>56. Wulatonwi makelima pallihilla talegawik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_57"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_57.jpg"></p>
 <p>57. The Stirrer was chief; the Talega towns were too strong.</p>
 <p>57. Pimokhasuwi sakimanep wsamimaskan talegawik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_58"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_58.jpg"></p>
 <p>58. The Fire-Builder was chief; they all gave to him many towns.</p>
 <p>58. Tenchekentit sakimanep wemilat makelinik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_59"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_59.jpg"></p>
 <p>59. The Breaker-in-Pieces was chief, all the Talega go south.</p>
 <p>59. Pagan chichilla sakimanep shawanewak wemi talega.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_60"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_60.jpg"></p>
 <p>60. He-has-Pleasure was chief; all the people rejoice.</p>
 <p>60. Hattan wulaton sakimanep, wingelendam wemi lennowak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_61"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_61.jpg"></p>
 <p>61. They stay south of the lakes; the Talamatan friends north of the lakes.</p>
 <p>61. Shawanipekis gunehungind lowanipekis talamatanitis.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_62"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_62.jpg"></p>
 <p>62. When Long-and-Mild was chief, those who were not his friends conspired.</p>
 <p>62. Attabchinitis gishelendam gunitakan sakimanep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_63"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_63.jpg"></p>
 <p>63. Truthful-Man was chief, the Talamatans made war.</p>
 <p>63. Linniwulamen sakimanep pallitonep talamatan.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_64"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_64.jpg"></p>
 <p>64. Just-and-True was chief; the Talamatans trembled.</p>
 <p>64. Shakagapewi sakimanep nungiwi talamatan.</p>
