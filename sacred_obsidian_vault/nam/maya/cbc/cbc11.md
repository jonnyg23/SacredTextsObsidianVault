 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_219"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_220">84:4</a> It is generally agreed that Katun 11 Ahau began some time during the last half of the fourth decade of the Sixteenth Century. There was, however, a school of Maya writers during the Eighteenth Century who taught that the katun was composed of 24 years of 365 days each. They believed that a certain Katun 4 Ahau ended in 1752; and counting back to Katun 11 Ahau by their assumed katuns of 24 years, they came to the conclusion that it began in a year 10 Cauac which fell in 1512.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_220"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_221">84:5</a> Cortez landed in Cozumel in 1519.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_221"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_222">84:6</a> Santiago was a suburb of Merida occupied by the Nahua allies of the Spaniards (Cogolludo Bk. 7 Chap. 4).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_222"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_223">84:7</a> It is significant to note that the Maya still preserved the idea of a night-count at this late date.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_223"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_224">85:1</a> The uinal was the Maya month of twenty days.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_224"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_226">85:2</a> Literally Little Yaxkin. Yaxkin is the usual name of this uinal.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_225"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_227">85:3</a> The ear is left on the stalk, which is bent double so that the husk will completely shed the rain. Until the fall rains are over, the corn is said to keep better in this manner than in the granary.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_226"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_225">85:4</a> Probably due to the smoke from the fields which are burned over at this time.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_227"></a><a href="cbc11.htm#fr_228">85:5</a> Written in a different hand. The rest of the page is blank.</p>
