 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">HAIDA CRADLE SONGS</h4>  
 <p align="center"><b>I</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Again perhaps you expect to sit up high in your father's canoe <a name="fr_5"></a><a href="#fn_5"><font size="1">*</font></a><br>     chief-woman, and look around upon all things<br>     in front of Upset-Canoe.<br> Be careful, be careful, chief-woman!</div>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="green">p. 139</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Again perhaps you expect to sit up high in your father's canoe,<br>     chief-woman, and look around the place<br>     whence abalones come.<br> Be careful, be careful, chief-woman.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>II</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Perhaps you are crying and crawling<br>     to get your grandmother's hand<br>     which was hurt on a wooden tray with square sides, I hear;<br>     for that you are crying and crawling<br>     chief-woman, chief-woman,<br>     crying and crawling.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>III</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Whence have you fallen, have you fallen?<br> Whence have you fallen, have you fallen?<br> Did you fall, fall, fall, fall,<br>     from the top of a salmonberry bush?</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>IV</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Stop crying, chief's child! Stop crying, chief's child!<br> I do not expect that drums will sound again for you, chief's<br>     child, if it is for that you are crying and wriggling.<br> Stop crying, great chief's child, a child of noble family sits<br>     quietly.<br> Now, now, great chief's child; a child of noble family sits<br>     quietly.<br> Stop crying, chief's child! Stop crying, chief's child!<br> I do not expect that they are going to lay heavy planks for<br>     you again, chief's child; if it is for that you are crying and<br>     wriggling.<br> Stop crying, great chief's child, a child of noble family sits<br>     quietly.<br> Now, now, great chief's child; a child of noble family sits<br>     quietly.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="green">p. 140</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>V</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">One sits here like a common person facing the woods<br> Say, stop telling lies!<br> Your mouth will be crooked<br>     you mosquito-people-trash!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>VI</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">In your father's house, your father's house<br>     Cape Qóna seagulls eating things<br>     are making cries.<br> In the midst of all these things<br>     you are going to move<br>     proudly as you sit.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>VII</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Come, let us take it on our knees!<br>     Come, let us take it on our knees!<br> Hand it to one another inside of its father's house,<br>     hand it to one another!<br> Come, let us take it on our knees!<br>     Come, let us take it on our knees!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>VIII</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">At that time when my child<br>     goes about as a youth<br>     vainly alone<br>     I shall sit around.<br> His son<br>     making a great noise<br>     went by on the water.<br> I wonder where he is going!<br> His son<br>     making a great noise<br>     went by on the water.<br> It must be to the North islands.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="green">p. 141</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>IX</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Did you make up your mind<br>     to fall into the cradle<br>     to fall into the cradle<br>     to fall in from the top of a spruce-tree?<br>     to fall in from the top of a salmonberry bush?</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>X</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Be careful, my noble sons!<br>     you will grow to one another like leaves.<br> Be careful, my own chief!<br>     Be careful, my own chief!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XI</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Are you crying for this, chief?<br> Are you crying for this, chief?<br> Are you crying to have your sisters<br>     put you up higher, chief?<br> Crying for your cousins to make the people<br>     as numerous in front of you<br>     as when people make seagulls cry,<br>     being obliged to step on them,<br> For these things are you crying, chief?</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XII</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">You came to me, you came to me, ye he  he!<br> You came to me, you came to me.<br> You came walking to me, calling me "mother,"<br>     instead of to someone else.<br> To me my child, who is a chief's child,<br>     came walking, calling me "mother,"<br> Mother of noble family,<br> Mother of noble family, mother of noble family,<br>     mother of noble family, mother of noble family.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="green">p. 142</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>XIII</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Why does he cry softly?<br> Why does he wriggle as he sits?<br> He wriggles and cries for grandfather's house.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XIV</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">It is not now as it was in olden times.<br> Even slaves are beginning to own good abalone-shells!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XV</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Dogs, even,<br>     when they have pups<br>     to them give their love.<br> That is why<br>     mine I love.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XVI</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Still stands<br>     Skîlsîs town.<br> Do not cry<br>     upon my knees!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XVII</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Why is it<br>     your slave close by<br>     (even I, your mother!)<br>     you want something you cannot get, you say?<br> You are very foolish,<br>     uncle reborn,<br>     I bore.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XVIII</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">My child says:<br>     Look around at the waves,—<br> Then she fools me<br>     with unripe salmonberries.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="green">p. 143</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>XIX</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Behind Sea-Lion Town<br>     I was looking around a while;<br>     the future chief I found,<br>     just big enough to walk.<br> Take care, take care, my own chief!<br> Take care, my own master!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XX</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Women are better than men,<br>     women are better than men.<br> Women have more property.<br> Chiefs of my family,<br>     where are you?</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>XXI</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Upon his grandmother's land<br>     my child walks proudly.<br> For that his foot is dear.<br> Do not cry!</div>
