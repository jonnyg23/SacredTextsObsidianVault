 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">MEMORIAL ODE</h3>  
 <p>Chief John Buck, hereditary Keeper of the Wampum, made and sung his ode in 1884 on the occasion of the removal of the bones of Chief Red Jacket from their original burying place to Forest</p>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p>Lawn Cemetery. The Great League is the League of the five Iroquois nations in Western New York.</p>  
 <p align="center"><b>CHANT</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Now, listen, Ye who established the Great<br>     League,<br> Now it has become old,<br> Now there is nothing but wilderness.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Ye are in your graves who established it.<br> Ye have taken it with you and have placed it under you,<br> And there is nothing left but desert.<br> There you have taken your great minds.<br> That which you established, you have taken with you<br> Ye have placed under your heads what ye have established,<br> The Great League.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>REFRAIN</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Woe, Woe! Hearken ye!<br>     We are diminished<br> Woe, woe!<br>     The land has become a thicket.<br> Woe, woe!<br>     The clear places are deserted<br>     They are in their graves who established it.<br> Woe, the Great League!<br>     Yet they declared it should endure.<br> The Great League, Woe!<br>     Their work has grown old<br>     We are become wretched. Woe!</div>
