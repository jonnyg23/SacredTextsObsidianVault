 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">OJIBWA WAR SONGS</h3>  
 <p align="center"><b>I</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Hear my voice, Birds of War!<br> I prepare a feast for you to feed on;<br> I see you cross the enemy's lines;<br> Like you I shall go.<br> I wish the swiftness of your wings;<br> I wish the vengeance of your claws;<br> I muster my friends;<br> I follow your flight.<br> Ho, you young men warriors,<br> Bear your angers to the place of fighting!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>II</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">From the south they came, Birds of War—<br> Hark! to their passing scream.<br> I wish the body of the fiercest,<br> As swift, as cruel, as strong.<br> I cast my body to the chance of fighting.<br> Happy I shall be to lie in that place,<br> In that place where the fight was,<br> Beyond the enemy's line.</div>  
 <p align="center">III</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Here on my breast have I bled!<br> See—see! these are fighting-scars!<br> Mountains tremble at my yell!<br> I strike for life.</div>
