 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF BASKET-WEAVING</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Kulasgh, Kulasgh, my mother, <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">*</font></a><br> I sit at thy knee<br> Weaving my basket of grasses,<br> Weaving for my harvest of berries when the Ripe Days come.<br> Thy fingers gently touch my hair with fragrance,<br> Thy mouth drips a song, for the wind has kissed it—<br> (Love sings in thy mouth!)<br> The soil listens and answers;<br> I feel a stirring beneath me and hear buds opening,<br> The river chants thy song and the clouds dance to it.<br> Tonight the stars will float upon thy singing breath,<br> Gleaming like slanting flocks above the sea.<br> All the earth sings: and its voices are one song!</div>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="green">p. 176</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I alone am silent: I alone, a maid waiting him, the Fate,<br> The Stirring One, the Planter of the Harvest,<br> The Basket-Filler.<br> Kulasgh, Kulasgh, Mother!<br> See how beautiful, how liberal, is my basket,<br> How tightly woven for the waters of Love,<br> How soft for the treading of children's feet,<br> How strong to bear them up!<br> Kulasgh, Kulasgh, Mother, remember me—<br> Ere the Sunset and the Dropping Leaf!</div>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_6"></a><a href="por54.htm#fr_6">175:*</a> Kulasgh, Cedar tree, considered the source of life by the British Columbian Coast tribes, as it supplies all their necessities, even food in fish famine.</p>
