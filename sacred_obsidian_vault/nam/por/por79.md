 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE LOST LAGOON</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">It is dusk on the Lost Lagoon,<br> And we two dreaming the dusk away,<br> Beneath the drift of a twilight gray—<br> Beneath the drowse of an ending day<br> And the curve of a golden moon.  
 <p>It is dark on the Lost Lagoon,<br> And gone are the depths of haunting blue,</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="green">p. 203</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The grouping gulls, and the old canoe,<br> The singing firs, and the dusk and—you,<br> And gone is the golden moon.  
 <p>O lure of the Lost Lagoon—<br> I dream tonight that my paddle blurs<br> The purple shade where the seaweed stirs—<br> I hear the call of the singing firs<br> In the hush of the golden moon.</p></div>
