 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CALLING-ONE'S-OWN</h3>  
 <p align="center"><b>OJIBWA</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Awake! flower of the forest, sky-treading bird of the prairie.<br> Awake! awake! wonderful fawn-eyed One.<br> When you look upon me I am satisfied; as flowers that drink dew.<br> The breath of your mouth is the fragrance of flowers in the morning,<br> Your breath is their fragrance at evening in the moon-of-fading-leaf.<br> Do not the red streams of my veins run toward you<br> As forest-streams to the sun in the moon of bright nights?<br> When you are beside me my heart sings; a branch it is, dancing,<br> Dancing before the Wind-spirit in the moon of strawberries.<br> When you frown upon me, beloved, my heart grows dark—<br> A shining river the shadows of clouds darken,<br> Then with your smiles comes the sun and makes to look like gold<br> Furrows the cold wind drew in the water's face.<br> Myself! behold me! blood of my beating heart.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="green">p. 11</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Earth smiles—the waters smile—even the sky-of-clouds smiles—but I,<br> I lose the way of smiling when you are not near,<br> Awake! awake! my beloved.</div>
