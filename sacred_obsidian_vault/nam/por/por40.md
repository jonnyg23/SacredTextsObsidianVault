 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="green">p. 136</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THREE SONGS FROM THE HAIDA:<br> QUEEN CHARLOTTE'S ISLAND, B.C.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><b>LOVE SONG</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Beautiful is she, this woman,<br> As the mountain flower;<br> But cold, cold, is she,<br> Like the snowbank<br> Behind which it blooms.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>THE BEAR'S SONG</b></p>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(Whoever can sing this song is admitted forever to the friendship of the bears)<br> I have taken the woman of beauty<br> For my wife;<br> I have taken her from her friends.<br> I hope her kinsmen will not come<br> And take her away from me.<br> I will be kind to her.<br> Berries, berries I will give her from the hill<br> And roots from the ground.<br> I will do everything to please her.<br> For her I made this song and for her I sing it.</div></i></p>  
 <p align="center"><b>SONG FOR FINE WEATHER</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">O good Sun,<br> Look thou down upon us:<br> Shine, shine on us, O Sun,<br> Gather up the clouds, wet, black, under thy arms—<br> That the rains may cease to fall.<br> Because thy friends are all here on the beach</div>  
 <p><a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="green">p. 137</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Ready to go fishing<br> Ready for the hunt.<br> Therefore look kindly on us, O Good Sun!<br> Give us peace within our tribe<br> And with all our enemies.<br> Again, again, we call<br> Hear us, hear us, O Good Sun!</div>
