 <p align="center"><b>CHANT OF THE RED EAGLE CLAN</b></p>  
 <p>The First of the Race<br> Was saying, "Ho, younger brother! the children have no bodies.<br> "We shall seek bodies for our children.<br> "Ho, younger brother! you shall attend to it."<br> They reached one upper world and stood.<br> There they were not human beings.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="green">p. 42</font></a></p>  
 <p>"Ho, younger brother! the children have no bodies," he was saying.<br> "We must seek bodies for our children."<br> They reached the second upper world and stood.<br> There they were not human beings.<br> "Ho, younger brother! the children have no bodies," he was saying.<br> "We must seek bodies for our children."<br> They reached the third upper world and stood.<br> There they were not human beings.<br> "Ho, younger brother! the children have no bodies," he was saying.<br> "We must seek bodies for our children."<br> They reached the fourth upper world and stood.<br> There they became human beings.<br> Still, the children were without (human) bodies.<br> "We must continue to seek bodies for our children."<br> They returned to the third upper world and stood.<br> The children were really without bodies.<br> "Ho, younger brother! the children have no bodies," he was saying.<br> "We must continue to seek bodies for our children."<br> They returned to the second upper world and stood.<br> The children did not find bodies for themselves.<br> "Ho, younger brother! the children have no bodies," he was saying.<br> "We must make an examination awhile longer."<br> They returned to the first upper world and stood.<br> They came to red oak and were standing on it.<br> On a very fine day they came hither and stood.<br> Kaxe-wahü-sa (the Black Bear), who was then moving,<br> Came directly to them and stood.<br> "Ho, elder brother!" (said the Black Bear.)<br> "You shall continue to burn my feet for me."<br> "Ho, Kaxe-wahü-sa!" was he (the Tsicu) saying.<br> Kaxe-wahü-sa went to the star Watse-tuka.<br> "Ho, grandfather!" he was saying.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_43"><font size="1" color="green">p. 43</font></a></p>  
 <p>"The children have no bodies."<br> Watse-tuka replied, "Can I give the children bodies?<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then Kaxe-wahü-sa went to the star Watse-mika.<br> "Ho, grandmother!" he said;<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> She replied, "Can I give bodies to the children?<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one!<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then he went to the mysterious one of day.<br> "Ho, grandfather!" said he;<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> Said he, "Can I give the children bodies?<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then he went to the mysterious one of night.<br> "Ho, grandfather!" said he;<br> "The children have no bodies, grandfather!"<br> The Moon replied, "Can I give bodies to the children?<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then he went to the Pleiades, saying,<br> "Ho, grandfathers!<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> One of these replied, "Can I give bodies to the children!<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then he went to the constellation called Three Deer.<br> "Ho, grandfather," said he;<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> The latter replied, "Can I give the children bodies?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="green">p. 44</font></a></p>  
 <p>"I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then he went to the Morning Star, saying,<br> "Ho, grandfather!<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> The star replied, "Can I give bodies to the children?<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> "I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> Then he went to the Small Star, saying,<br> "Ho, grandfather!<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> The star replied, "Can I give bodies to the children?<br> "I have an everlasting road in which I must keep.<br> 'I am not the only mysterious one;<br> "You shall attend to it awhile longer."<br> The female Red Bird, who had been moving, was sitting on her nest.<br> To her he came, saying,<br> "Ho, grandmother!<br> "The children have no bodies."<br> She replied, "I can cause your children to have (human) bodies from my own.<br> "My left wing shall be a left arm for the children.<br> "My right wing shall be a right arm for them.<br> "My head shall be a head for them.<br> "My mouth shall be a mouth for them.<br> "My forehead shall be a forehead for them.<br> "My neck shall be a neck for them.<br> "My throat shall be a throat for them.<br> "My chest shall be a chest for them.<br> "My thighs shall be thighs for them.<br> "My knees shall be knees for them.<br> "My heels shall be their heels.<br> "My toes shall be their toes.<br> "My claws shall be their toenails.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="green">p. 45</font></a></p>  
 <p>"You shall live forever without destruction.<br> "Your children shall live as human beings.<br> "The speech of children I will bestow on your children."</p>  <h4 align="center">THE CHILD IS INTRODUCED TO THE COSMOS AT BIRTH</h4>  
 <p align="center"><b>OMAHA</b></p>  
 <p>Ho! Ye Sun, Moon, Stars, all ye that move in the heavens,<br>     I bid you hear me!<br> Into your midst has come a new life.<br>     Consent ye, I implore!<br> Make its path smooth, that it may reach the brow of the first hill!<br> Ho! Ye Winds, Clouds, Rain, Mist, all ye that move in the air,<br>     I bid you hear me!<br> Into your midst has come a new life.<br>     Consent ye, I implore!<br> Make its path smooth, that it may reach the brow of the second hill!<br> Ho! Ye Hills, Valleys, Rivers, Lakes, Trees, Grasses, all ye of the earth,<br>     I bid you hear me!<br> Into your midst has come a new life.<br>     Consent ye, I implore!<br> Make its path smooth, that it may reach the brow of the third hill!<br> Ho! Ye Birds, great and small, that fly in the air,<br> Ho! Ye Animals, great and small, that dwell in the forest,<br> Ho! Ye insects that creep among the grasses and burrow in the ground—<br>     I bid you hear me!<br> Into your midst has come a new life.<br>     Consent ye, I implore!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  
 <p>Make its path smooth, that it may reach the brow of the fourth hill!<br> Ho! All ye of the heavens, all ye of the air, all ye of the earth:<br>     I bid you all to hear me!<br> Into your midst has come a new life.<br>     Consent ye, consent ye all, I implore!<br> Make its path smooth—then shall it travel beyond the four hills!</p>
