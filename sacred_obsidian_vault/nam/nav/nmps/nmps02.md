 <a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 22</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">ALPHABET.</h1>
 <p>The characters used in this work, in spelling Navaho words, are given below, with the value assigned to each character.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">VOWELS.</h3>
 <p>a has the sound of English a in father.<br>
 ä has the sound of English a in hat. has the sound of English a in what.<br>
 <u>a</u> has the sound of English e in they. In some connections it varies to the sound of English e in their.<br>
 ë has the sound of English e in then.<br>
 i has the sound of English i in marine.<br>
 ï has the sound of English i in tin.<br>
 o has the sound of English o in bone.<br>
 u has the sound of English u in rude.<br>
 ai unmarked, or accented on the i (aí), is a diphthong having the sound of English i in bind. When it is accented on the a (ái), or has a diaeresis (aï), it is pronounced as two vowels.<br>
 ow has the sound of English ow in how. It is heard mostly in meaningless syllables.<br>
 A vowel followed by an inverted comma (`) is aspirated, or pronounced with a peculiar force which cannot be well represented by adding the letter h.</p>
 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 23</font></a></p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">CONSONANTS.</h3>
 <p>b has the sound of English b in bat.<br>
 d has the sound of English d in day.<br>
 <i>d</i> represents a strongly aspirated dental sonant. It is often interchanged with d.<br>
 g has the sound of English g in go, or, in some connections, the sound of English g in gay.<br>
 <i>g</i> has a sound unknown in English. It is the velar g, like the Arabic ghain, or the Dakota g.<br>
 h has the sound of English h in hat.<br>
 <i>h</i> has the sound of German ch in machen. It is sometimes interchanged with h.<br>
 k has usually the sound of English k in koran; but sometimes the sound of English k in king.<br>
 l has the sound of English l in lay.<br>
 <i>l</i> has a sound unknown in English. It is an aspirated surd l, made with the side rather than with the tip of the tongue. It is often interchanged with l.<br>
 m has the sound. of English m in man.<br>
 n has the sound of English n in name.<br>
 <i>n</i> has the effect of French n in bon. It has no equivalent in English.<br>
 s has the sound of English s in sand.<br>
 <i>s</i> has the sound of English sh in shad. It is often interchanged with s.<br>
 t has the sound of English t in tan.<br>
 <i>t</i> represents a strongly aspirated dental surd. It is often interchanged with t.<br>
 w has the sound of English w in war.<br>
 y has the sound of English y in yarn.<br>
 z has the sound of English z in zone.<br>
 z has the sound of English z in azure. It is often interchanged with z.<br>
 c, f, j, p, q, v, and x are not used. The sound of English ch in chance is represented by t<i>s</i>; that of English j in jug by d<i>z</i>.</p>
