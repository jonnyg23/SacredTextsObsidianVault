 <a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 54</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">A PRAYER OF THE FOURTH DAY OF THE NIGHT CHANT.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">(See The Night Chant, p. 97, par. 426.)</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">I.</h3>
 <p>1. Tse`gíhi.<br>
 2. House made of the dawn.<br>
 3. House made of evening light.<br>
 4. House made of the dark cloud.<br>
 5. House made of male rain.<br>
 6. House made of dark mist.<br>
 7. House made of female rain.<br>
 8. House made of pollen.<br>
 9. House made of grasshoppers.<br>
 10. Dark cloud is at the door.<br>
 11. The trail out of it is dark cloud.<br>
 12. The zigzag lightning stands high up on it.<br>
 13. Male deity!<br>
 14. Your offering I make.<br>
 15. I have prepared a smoke for you.<br>
 16. Restore my feet for me.<br>
 17. Restore my legs for me.<br>
 18. Restore my body for me.<br>
 19. Restore my mind for me.<br>
 20. Restore my voice for me.<br>
 21. This very day take out your spell for me.<br>
 22. Your spell remove for me.<br>
 23. You have taken it away for me.<br>
 24. Far off it has gone.<br>
 25. Happily I recover.<br>
 26. Happily my interior becomes cool.<br>
 27. Happily I go forth.<br>
 28. My interior feeling cold, may I walk.<br>
 29. No longer sore, may I walk.</p>
 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 55</font></a></p>
 <p>30. Impervious to pain, may I walk.<br>
 31. With lively feelings may I walk.<br>
 32. As it used to be long ago, may I walk.<br>
 33. Happily may I walk.<br>
 34. Happily with abundant dark clouds, may I walk.<br>
 35. Happily with abundant showers, may I walk.<br>
 36. Happily with abundant plants, may I walk.<br>
 37. Happily on a trail of pollen, may I walk.<br>
 38. Happily may I walk.<br>
 39. Being as it used to be long ago, may I walk.<br>
 40. May it be happy (or beautiful) before me.<br>
 41. May it be beautiful behind me.<br>
 42, May it be beautiful below me.<br>
 43. May it be beautiful above me.<br>
 44. May it be beautiful all around me.<br>
 45. In beauty it is finished.<br>
 46. In beauty it is finished.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">II.</h3>
 <p>10. Dark mist is at the door.<br>
 11. The trail out of it is dark mist.<br>
 12. The male rain stands high upon it.</p>
 <p>(With the exception of these lines and lines 40 and 41, which change places, the second part of the prayer is identical with the first. At the end it has "In beauty it is finished," repeated four times.)</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TEXT AND INTERLINEAR TRANSLATION.</h3>
 <p>1. Tse`gíhi<br>
 <font size="2">Tse'gihi</font></p>

 <p>3. Na<i>h</i>otsoí be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Evening light | house made of.</font></p>
 <p>4. Kósdïlyï<i>l</i> be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Dark cloud | house made of.</font></p>
 <p>5. Ni<i>l</i>tsabaká be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Male rain | house made of.</font></p>
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>
 <p>6. A`'dïlyï<i>l</i> be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Dark fog | house made of.</font></p>
 <p>7. Nï<i>l</i>tsabaád be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Female rain | house made of.</font></p>
 <p>8. <i>T</i>adïtdí<i>n</i> be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Pollen | house made of.</font></p>
 <p>9. Anï<i>lt</i><u>a</u>ni be<i>h</i>ogán<br>
 <font size="2">Grasshoppers | house made of.</font></p>
 <p>10. Kósdï<i>l</i>yï<i>l</i> dadïnlá'<br>
 <font size="2">Dark cloud | doorposts.</font></p>
 <p>11. Kósdï<i>l</i>yï<i>l</i> bïké d<i>z</i>eétin<br>
 <font size="2">Dark cloud | his road | the exit.</font></p>
 <p>12. Atsïnit<i>l</i>í<i>s</i> yíke dasizíni<br>
 <font size="2">Lightening | on top | standing up.</font></p>
 <p>13. <i>H</i>ast<i>s</i>ébaka<br>
 <font size="2">O, Male Divinity!</font></p>
 <p>14. Nigél is<i>l</i>á`,<br>
 <font size="2">Your offering | I make.</font></p>
 <p>15. Nadíhila`<br>
 <font size="2">For you I have prepared.</font></p>
 <p>16. <i>S</i>ïké saádï<i>l</i>i<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">My feet | for me restore.</font></p>
 <p>17. <i>S</i>ït<i>s</i>át saádï<i>l</i>i<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">My legs | for me restore.</font></p>
 <p>18. <i>S</i>ïtsís saádï<i>l</i>i<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">My body | for me restore.</font></p>
 <p>19. <i>S</i>ï'ni saádï<i>l</i>i<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">My mind | for me restore.</font></p>
 <p>20. <i>S</i>ïné saádï<i>l</i>i<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">My voice | for me restore.</font></p>
 <p>21. <i>T</i>ádïsd<i>z</i>i<i>n</i> naalíl saádï<i>l</i>i<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">This very day | your spell for me | you will take out.</font></p>
 <p>22. Naalíl <i>s</i>a<i>h</i>anéïnla`<br>
 <font size="2">Your spell | for me is removed.</font></p>
 <p>23. <i>S</i>ïtsád<i>z</i>e tahï'ndïnla`<br>
 <font size="2">Away from me | you have taken it.</font></p>
 <p>24. Nïzágo nast<i>l</i>í<i>n</i><br>
 <font size="2">Far off | it has gone.</font></p>
 <p>25. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo nadedi<i>s</i>dá<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">Happily | I will recover.</font></p>
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>
 <p>26. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo <i>s</i>ïtáha dïnoké<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">Happily | my interior | will be cool.</font></p>
 <p>27. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo t<i>s</i>ïdïsá<i>l</i><br>
 <font size="2">Happily | I shall go forth.</font></p>
 <p>28. <i>S</i>i<i>t</i>áha <i>h</i>onezkázigo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">My interior | being cool | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>29. Do<i>s</i>a<i>t</i>éhigo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">No longer sore | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>30. Dosohodi<i>l</i>nígo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Impervious to pain | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>31. Saná` ni<i>sl</i>í<i>n</i>go na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">My feelings | being lively | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>32. Daa<i>l</i>kída kitégo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Long ago | as it was | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>33. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo kósdï<i>l</i>yï<i>l</i> <i>s</i>enahot<i>l</i>édo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Happily | clouds dark | receiving again | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>34. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Happily | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>35. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo <i>s</i>edahwi<i>l</i>tí<i>n</i>do na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Happily | having abundant showers | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>36. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo nánise <i>s</i>enahot<i>l</i>édo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Happily | growing plants | receiving again | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>37. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo <i>t</i>adïtdí<i>n</i> keheetí<i>n</i>go na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Happily | pollen | its trail | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>38. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ógo na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Happily | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>39. <i>T</i>a<i>s</i>é a<i>l</i>kíd<i>z</i>i a<i>h</i>oní<i>l</i>go na<i>s</i>ádo<br>
 <font size="2">Thus | as it used to be | it having happened | may I walk.</font></p>
 <p>40. <i>S</i>ïtsíd<i>z</i>e <i>h</i>o<i>z</i>ódo<br>
 <font size="2">Before me | may it be happy.</font></p>
 <p>41. <i>S</i>ïkéde <i>h</i>o<i>z</i>ódo<br>
 <font size="2">Behind me | may it be happy.</font></p>
 <p>42. <i>S</i>iyáde <i>h</i>o<i>z</i>ódo<br>
 <font size="2">Below me | may it be happy.</font></p>
 <p>43. <i>S</i>ïkide <i>h</i>o<i>z</i>ódo<br>
 <font size="2">Above me | may it be happy.</font></p>
 <p>44. <i>S</i>ïná taá<i>l</i>tso <i>h</i>o<i>z</i>ódo<br>
 <font size="2">Around me | all | may it be happy.</font></p>
 <p>45. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ó na<i>h</i>as<i>tl</i>í<i>n</i><br>
 <font size="2">Happily | it is restored.</font></p>
 <p>46. <i>H</i>o<i>z</i>ó na<i>h</i>as<i>tl</i>í<i>n</i><br>
 <font size="2">Happily | it is restored.</font></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">II.</h3>
 <p>10. A`'di<i>l</i>yi<i>l</i> dadïnlá`.<br>
 <font size="2">dark fog | door posts.</font></p>
 <p>11. A`'di<i>l</i>yi<i>l</i> biké dzeétin<br>
 <font size="2">Dark fog | its trail | the exit.</font></p>
 <p>12. Ni<i>l</i>tsabaká yíke dasizíni.<br>
 <font size="2">Male rain | on top | standing up.</font></p>
 <p>(The second part of the prayer is identical with the first part except that lines 40 and 41 change places and the lines given above take the places of the corresponding lines in part I. The concluding lines are said four times instead of twice.)</p>
