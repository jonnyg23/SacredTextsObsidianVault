 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Jaina Sutras, Part II (SBE22)</i>, tr. by Hermann Jacobi, [1884], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ELEVENTH LECTURE <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="#fn_469"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>A monk or a nun should not resolve to go where they will hear sounds of a M<i>ri</i>daṅga, Nandîm<i>ri</i>daṅga, or <i>Gh</i>allarî <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_470"><font size="1">6</font></a>, or any such-like various sounds of drums. (1)</p>  
 <p>If a monk or a nun hear any sounds, viz. of the Vi<i>n</i>â, Vipam<i>k</i>î, Vadvîsaka, Tu<i>n</i>aka, Pa<i>n</i>aka, Tumbavî<i>n</i>ikâ, or Dha<i>m</i>ku<i>n</i>a, they should not resolve to go where they will hear any such-like various sounds of stringed instruments. (2)</p>  
 <p>The same precepts apply to sounds of kettledrums, viz. of the Tâla, Lattiyâ, Gohiyâ <a name="fr_477"></a><a href="#fn_471"><font size="1">7</font></a>, or Kirikiriyâ; (3)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="green">p. 184</font></a></p>  
 <p>Also to sounds of wind instruments, viz. the conch, flute, Kharamukhî, or Piripiriyâ. (4)</p>  
 <p>A <a name="fr_478"></a><a href="#fn_472"><font size="1">1</font></a> monk or a nun should not, for the sake of hearing sounds, go to walls or ditches, &amp;c. (see II, 3, 3, §§ 1 and 2); (5)</p>  
 <p>Nor to marshes, pasture grounds, thickets, woods, strongholds in woods, mountains, strongholds in mountains; (6)</p>  
 <p>Nor to villages, towns, markets, or a capital, hermitages, cities, halting-places for caravans; (7)</p>  
 <p>Nor to gardens, parks, woods, forests, temples, assembly halls, wells; (8)</p>  
 <p>Nor to towers, pathways, doors, or town gates; (9) Nor where three or four roads meet, nor to courtyards or squares; (1o)</p>  
 <p>Nor to stables (or nests) of buffaloes, cattle, horses, elephants, &amp;c. (see 10, § 12); (11)</p>  
 <p>Nor to places where buffaloes, bulls, horses, &amp;c., fight; (12)</p>  
 <p>Nor to places where herds of cattle, horses, or elephants are kept; (13)</p>  
 <p>Nor to places where story-tellers or acrobats perform, or where continuously story-telling, dramatical plays, singing, music, performance on the Vi<i>n</i>â, beating of time, playing on the Tûrya, clever playing on the Pa<i>t</i>aha is going on; (14)</p>  
 <p>Nor to places where quarrels, affrays, riots, conflicts between two kingdoms, anarchical or revolutionary disturbances occur; (15)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="green">p. 185</font></a></p>  
 <p>Nor to places where a young well-attended girl, well-attired and well-ornamented, is paraded, or where somebody is led to death. (16)</p>  
 <p>A monk or a nun should not, for the sake of hearing sounds, go to places where there are many great temptations <a name="fr_479"></a><a href="#fn_473"><font size="1">1</font></a>, viz. where many cars, chariots, Mle<i>k</i><i>kh</i>as, or foreigners meet. (17)</p>  
 <p>A monk or a nun should not, for the sake of hearing sounds, go to great festivals where women or men, old, young, or middle-aged ones are well-dressed and ornamented, sing, make music, dance, laugh, play, sport, or give, distribute, portion or parcel out plenty of food, drink, dainties, and spices. (18)</p>  
 <p>A monk or a nun should not like or love, desire for, or be enraptured with, sounds of this or the other world, heard or unheard ones, seen or unseen ones.</p>  
 <p>This is the whole duty, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>Thus I say. (19)</p>
