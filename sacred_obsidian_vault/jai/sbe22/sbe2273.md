 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Jaina Sutras, Part II (SBE22)</i>, tr. by Hermann Jacobi, [1884], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">NINTH LECTURE <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_456"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>When a monk or a nun wishes to go to a pure place for study, they <a name="fr_463"></a><a href="#fn_457"><font size="1">2</font></a> should not accept one which is infected by eggs or living beings, &amp;c.; for it is impure and unacceptable. But if that place for study to which they wish to go, is free from eggs or living beings, &amp;c., they may accept it; for it is pure and acceptable.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="green">p. 180</font></a></p>  
 <p>In this way all that has been said in the corresponding passage about couches <a name="fr_464"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">1</font></a> should be repeated here as far as 'water-plants.' (1)</p>  
 <p>If parties of two, three, four, or five (mendicants) resolve to go to the place for study, they should not embrace or hug, bite with their teeth or scratch with their nails each other's body.</p>  
 <p>This is the whole duty, &amp;c,</p>  
 <p>Thus I say. (2)</p>
