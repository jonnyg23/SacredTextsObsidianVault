 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Jaina Sutras, Part II (SBE22)</i>, tr. by Hermann Jacobi, [1884], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FOURTEENTH LECTURE.</h3>  
 <p>One should not be pleased with nor prohibit a reciprocal action, which relates to one's self, and produces karman.</p>  
 <p>A mendicant should not be pleased with nor prohibit it, if (he and the other) wipe or rub each other's feet, &amp;c.</p>  
 <p>In this way the whole Thirteenth Lecture should be repeated here.</p>  
 <p>This is the whole duty, &amp;c. Thus I say. (1)</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the Second Part, called the Seven Lectures.</p>
