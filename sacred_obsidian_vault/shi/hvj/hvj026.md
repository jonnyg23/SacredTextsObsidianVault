 <img src="img/02500.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">25</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE MINISTER-OF-THE-RIGHT OF THE SANJŌ (DISTRICT OF KYŌTO)</h3>  <h3 align="center">SANJŌ UDAIJIN</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Na ni shi owaba<br> Ausaka yama no<br>   Sanekazura<br> Hito ni shirarede<br> Kuru yoshi mo gana.  
 <p>HEAR thou art as modest as<br>   The little creeping spray<br> Upon Mount Ōsaka, which hides<br>   Beneath the grass; then, pray,<br>   Wander with me to-day.</p></div>  
 <p><small>The writer's real name was Sadakata Fujiwara, and he died A.D. 932. For an account of the Fujiwara family see verse No. <a href="hvj019.htm#page_18">18</a>. Mount Ōsaka mentioned here is the same place as that referred to in verse No. <a href="hvj011.htm#page_10">10</a>, and when spelled <i>Ausaka</i> it means 'a hill of meeting '. The suggestion is, that if she is really like the creeping vine which grows on Meeting Hill, she will come and meet him.</small></p>
