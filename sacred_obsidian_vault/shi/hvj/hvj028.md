 <img src="img/02700.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">27</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE IMPERIAL ADVISER KANESUKE</h3>  <h3 align="center">CHŪ-NAGON KANESUKE</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Mika no hara<br> Wakite nagaruru<br>   Izumi gawa<br> Itsu miki tote ka<br> Koishi-karuramu.  
 <p>OH! rippling River Izumi,<br>   That flows through Mika plain,<br> Why should the maid I saw but now<br>   And soon shall see again<br>   Torment my love-sick brain?</p></div>  
 <p><small>Kanesuke was a member of the Fujiwara family; he died in the year 933. The River Izumi is in the Province of Yamashiro.</small></p>  
 <p>The word-plays in this verse are—<i>Izumi</i>, in the third line, which is imitated in the next line, and <i>Mika</i>, which is also repeated in the third line. The first three lines of this verse, about the river flowing through the plain, form a 'preface', and appear to be inserted merely because <i>itsu miki</i> (when I have seen her) sounds like <i>Izumi</i>.</p>
