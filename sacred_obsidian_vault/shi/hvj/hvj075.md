 <img src="img/07400.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">74</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE MINISTER TOSHI-YORI MINAMOTO</h3>  <h3 align="center">MINAMOTO NO TOSHI-YORI ASON</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Ukari keru<br> Hito wo Hatsuse no<br>   Yama-oroshi<br> Hageshikare to wa<br> Inoranu mono wo.  
 <p>OH! Kwannon, Patron of this hill,<br>   The maid, for whom I pine,<br> Is obstinate and wayward, like<br>   The gusts around thy shrine.<br>   What of those prayers of mine?</p></div>  
 <p><small>Toshi-yori is supposed to have been the son of the writer of verse No. <a href="hvj072.htm#page_71">71</a>; he probably lived early in the twelfth century. Hatsuse is a mountain village near Nara, in the Province of Yamato; the temple there is dedicated to Kwannon, Goddess of Mercy, 'who looketh for ever down above the sound of prayer.'</small></p>
