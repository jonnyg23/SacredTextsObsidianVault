 <img src="img/07200.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">72</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE LADY KII, OF THE HOUSE OF PRINCESS YŪSHI</h3>  <h3 align="center">YŪSHI NAISHINNŌ KE KII</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Oto ni kiku<br> Takashi no hama no<br>   Adanami wa<br> Kakeji ya sode no<br> Nure mo koso sure.  
 <p>THE sound of ripples on the shore<br> Ne’er fails at Takashi;<br> My sleeves all worn and wet with tears<br> Should surely prove to thee,<br> I, too, will constant be.</p></div>  
 <p><small>The Lady Kii lived at the Court of the Emperor Horikawa, who reigned A.D. 1087-1107. Takashi is a seaside place in the Province of Izumi, not far from Ōsaka, and on the shore we see the Lady Kii, perhaps composing this verse to her lover.</small></p>
