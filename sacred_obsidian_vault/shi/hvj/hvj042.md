 <img src="img/04100.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">41</h3>  <h3 align="center">TADAMI NIBU</h3>  <h3 align="center">NIBU NO TADAMI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Koi su tefu<br> Waga na wa madaki<br>   Tachi ni keri<br> Hito shirezu koso<br> Omoi-someshi ga.  
 <p>OUR courtship, that we tried to hide,<br>   Misleading is to none;<br> And yet how could the neighbours guess,<br>   That I had yet begun<br>   To fancy any one?</p></div>  
 <p><small>This poet was the son of the writer of verse No. <a href="hvj031.htm#page_30">30</a>, and he is said to have composed the poem on the same occasion as is mentioned for No. <a href="hvj041.htm#page_40">40</a>.</small></p>  
 <p>The word <i>omoi</i> in the last line is a 'pivot-word' used firstly in connexion with the fourth line, meaning 'I thought' (nobody knew), and also in conjunction with <i>someshi</i>, where it means 'I began to be in love'.</p>
