 <img src="img/08000.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">80</h3>  <h3 align="center">LADY HORIKAWA, IN ATTENDANCE ON THE DOWAGER EMPRESS TAIKEN</h3>  <h3 align="center">TAIKEN MON-IN HORIKAWA</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Nagakaran<br> Kokoro mo shirazu<br>   Kuro kami no<br> Midarete kesa wa<br> Mono wo koso omoe.  
 <p>MY doubt about his constancy<br>   Is difficult to bear;<br> Tangled this morning are my thoughts,<br>   As is my long black hair.<br>   I wonder—Does he care?</p></div>  
 <p><small>Lady Horikawa was the daughter of the First Adviser of State, Sane-kyo, who lived about the year 1142. In this verse she is anxiously pondering, how long her lover will continue to be true to her; and she discovers, that her ideas on the subject are as tangled and disordered as her hair is.</small></p>
