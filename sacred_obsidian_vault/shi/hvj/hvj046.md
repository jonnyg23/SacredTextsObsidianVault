 <img src="img/04500.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">45</h3>  <h3 align="center">PRINCE KEN-TOKU</h3>  <h3 align="center">KEN-TOKU KO</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Aware to mo<br> Iu beki hito wa<br>   Omohoede<br> Mi no itazura ni<br> Narinu beki kana.  
 <p>I DARE not hope my lady-love<br>   Will smile on me again;<br> She knows no Pity, and my life<br>   I care not to retain,<br>   Since all my prayers are vain.</p></div>  
 <p><small>The real name of the writer of this verse was Koretada Fujiwara; he died in the year 972, and Prince Ken-toku is his posthumous name.</small></p>  
 <p><i>Aware to mo</i> means, in conjunction with the next line, 'that she would give me words of pity'; but <i>aware tomo</i> can also mean 'to meet as a friend.'</p>  
 <p>In spite of the Prince's fears, the illustration seems to suggest that his lady-love changed her mind, and came to visit him once more.</p>
