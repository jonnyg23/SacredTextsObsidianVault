 <img src="img/02100.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">21</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE PRIEST SOSEI</h3>  <h3 align="center">SOSEI HŌSHI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Ima kon to<br> Iishi bakari ni<br>   Naga-tsuki no<br> Ariake no tsuki<br> Wo machi izuru kana.  
 <p>THE moon that shone the whole night through<br>   This autumn morn I see,<br> As here I wait thy well-known step,<br>   For thou didst promise me—<br>   'I'll surely come to thee.'</p></div>  
 <p><small>Sosei is supposed to have been the son of Bishop Henjō, the writer of verse No. <a href="hvj013.htm#page_12">12</a>, born before the latter entered the church, about the year 850. His name as a layman was Hiro-nobu Yoshi-mine, and he became abbot of the Monastery of Riyau-inwin at Isono-kami, in the Province of Yamato.</small></p>
