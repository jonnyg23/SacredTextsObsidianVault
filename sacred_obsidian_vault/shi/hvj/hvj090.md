 <img src="img/08900.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">89</h3>  <h3 align="center">PRINCESS SHIKISHI</h3>  <h3 align="center">SHIKISHI NAISHINNŌ</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Tama no o yo<br> Taenaba taene<br>   Nagaraeba<br> Shinoburu koto no<br> Yowari mo zo suru.  
 <p>THE ailments of advancing years<br>   Though I should try to hide,<br> Some day the thread will break, the pearls<br>   Be scattered far and wide;<br>   Age cannot be defied.</p></div>  
 <p><small>The Princess was the daughter of the Emperor Goshirakawa, who reigned A.D. 1156-1158. In this short reign, however, the country suffered from a very severe earthquake and a devastating civil war.</small></p>  
 <p>The second line is a play upon the two verbs <i>tae</i>, which are both pronounced the same, but which are written with different ideographic characters. The first couplet, taken literally, reads, 'If the string of pearls (i.e. my life) <i>break</i>, I must <i>bear</i> it.'</p>  
 <p>The illustration seems to show the Princess sitting down with a nobleman in attendance.</p>
