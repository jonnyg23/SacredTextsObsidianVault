 <img src="img/08700.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">87</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE PRIEST JAKU-REN</h3>  <h3 align="center">JAKU-REN HŌSHI</h3>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Murasame no<br> Tsuyu mo mada hinu<br>   Maki no ha ni<br> Kiri tachi-noboru<br> Aki no yūgure.  
 <p>THE rain, which fell from passing showers,<br>   Like drops of dew, still lies<br> Upon the fir-tree needles, and<br>   The mists of evening rise<br>   Up to the autumn skies.</p></div>  
 <p><small>This verse is a good example of a picture verse, intended to call up the scene to one's imagination. Jaku-ren was another of the great Fujiwara clan, and lived about the end of the twelfth century.</small></p>  
 <p><i>Murasame</i> means 'rain falling in showers, here and there', and the illustration plainly shows it raining on one side of the house only.</p>
