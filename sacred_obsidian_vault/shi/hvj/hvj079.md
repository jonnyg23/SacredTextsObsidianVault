 <img src="img/07800.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">78</h3>  <h3 align="center">KANEMASA MINAMOTO</h3>  <h3 align="center">MINAMOTO NO KANEMASA</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Awaji shima<br> Kayou chidori no<br>   Naku koe ni<br> Iku yo nezamenu<br> Suma no seki-mori.  
 <p>BETWEEN Awaji and the shore<br>   The birds scream in their flight<br> Full oft they've made the Suma Guard<br>   Toss through a sleepless night,<br>   Until the morning light.</p></div>  
 <p><small>The writer was the son of Kanesuke, and died about the year 1112. <i>Chidori</i> are snipe or plovers, but here are apparently meant for seagulls. Awaji is a large island in the Inland Sea, near Kobe, and Suma is a point on the mainland in the Province of Settsu, immediately opposite.</small></p>
