 <img src="img/03900.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">39</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE PRIVY COUNCILLOR HITOSHI</h3>  <h3 align="center">SANGI HITOSHI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Asajū no<br> Ono no shinowara<br>   Shinoburedo<br> Amarite nado ka<br> Hito no koishiki.  
 <p>’TIS easier to hide the reeds<br>   Upon the moor that grow,<br> Than try to hide the ardent love<br>   That sets my cheeks aglow<br>   For somebody I know.</p></div>  
 <p><small>Little is known of this poet, except that he lived some time in the tenth century.</small></p>  
 <p>Note the word <i>shinowara</i>, meaning 'a bamboo moor', contrasted with <i>shinohuredo</i> in the next line, which means 'though I might manage to conceal'.</p>  
 <p>The picture shows Hitoshi on the wild moor, with the reeds growing all around him.</p>
