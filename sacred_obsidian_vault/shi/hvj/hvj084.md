 <img src="img/08300.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">83</h3>  <h3 align="center">TOSHI-NARI, A SHINTO OFFICIAL IN ATTENDANCE ON THE EMPRESS DOWAGER</h3>  <h3 align="center">KWŌ-TAI-KŌGŪ NO TAIU TOSHI-NARI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Yo no naka yo<br> Michi koso nakere<br>   Omoi iru<br> Yama no oku ni mo<br> Shika zo naku naru.  
 <p>FROM pain and sorrow all around<br>   There 's no escape, I fear;<br> To mountain wilds should I retreat,<br>   There also I should hear<br>   The cry of hunted deer.</p></div>  
 <p><small>Toshi-nari was a celebrated poet and nobleman in the reign of the Emperor Gotoba. He, however, gave up his position at Court and entered the church in the year 1176. He was the father of the writers of verses Nos. <a href="hvj095.htm#page_94">94</a> and <a href="hvj098.htm#page_97">97</a>, and died in the year 1204, at the age of ninety-one.</small></p>
