 <img src="img/09800.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">98</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE OFFICIAL IYE-TAKA</h3>  <h3 align="center">JŪNII IYE-TAKA</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Kaze soyogu<br> Nara no ogawa no<br>   Yūgure wa<br> Misogi zo natsu no<br> Shirushi nari keri.  
 <p>THE twilight dim, the gentle breeze<br>   By Nara's little stream,<br> The splash of worshippers who wash<br>   Before the shrine, all seem<br>   A perfect summer's dream.</p></div>  
 <p><small>Iye-taka was another of the great Fujiwara family; he died in the year 1237.</small></p>  
 <p>The word <i>misogi</i> means the Shinto ceremony of purifying the body before worship by washing or sprinkling with water. This verse is said to have been inscribed on a screen in the apartments of the Empress at Nara.</p>
