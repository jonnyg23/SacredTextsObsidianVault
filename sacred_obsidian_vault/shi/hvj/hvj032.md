 <img src="img/03100.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">31</h3>  <h3 align="center">KORENORI SAKA-NO-UYE</h3>  <h3 align="center">SAKA-NO-UYE NO KORENORI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Asaborake<br> Ariake no tsuki to<br>   Miru made ni<br> Yoshino no sato ni<br> Fureru shira yuki.  
 <p>SURELY the morning moon, I thought,<br>   Has bathed the hill in light<br> But, no; I see it is the snow<br>   That, falling in the night,<br>   Has made Yoshino white.</p></div>  
 <p><small>Little is known about this poet, but he is said to have lived some time in the tenth century. Yoshino is a mountain village in the Province of Yamato, famous for its cherry blossoms; at one time it contained the Imperial Summer Palace. In the illustration we see the poet looking across at the village on the hills all covered with snow.</small></p>
