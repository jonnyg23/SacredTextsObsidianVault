 <img src="img/08500.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">85</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE PRIEST SHUN-YE</h3>  <h3 align="center">SHUN-YE HŌSHI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Yomosugara<br> Mono omou koro wa<br>   Ake yarade<br> Neya no hima sae<br> Tsurena kari keri.  
 <p>ALL through the never-ending night<br>   I lie awake and think;<br> In vain I look to try and see<br>   The daybreak's feeble blink<br>   Peep through the shutter's chink.</p></div>  
 <p><small>This priest was the son of the author of verse No. <a href="hvj075.htm#page_74">74</a>. He describes in this poem a sleepless night, when he looks in vain to catch the first glimpse of daybreak through the joints of the sliding screens, that form the walls of a Japanese house. But in the picture, as will be noticed, one of the sliding screens is removed, in order to show the priest within.</small></p>
