 <img src="img/01600.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">16</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE IMPERIAL ADVISER YUKI-HIRA ARIWARA</h3>  <h3 align="center">CHŪ-NAGON ARIWARA NO YUKI-HIRA</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Tachi wakare<br> Inaba no yama no<br>   Mine ni ōru<br> Matsu to shi kikaba<br> Ima kaeri-komu.  
 <p>IF breezes on Inaba's peak<br>   Sigh through the old <i>pine</i> tree,<br> To whisper in my lonely ears<br>   That thou dost <i>pine</i> for me,—<br>   Swiftly I'll fly to thee.</p></div>  
 <p><small>Yuki-hira was the Governor of the Province of Inaba, and half-brother of the writer of the next verse (<a href="hvj018.htm#page_17">17</a>); he died in the year 893, aged 75.</small></p>  
 <p>The word <i>matsu</i> in the original may mean 'a pine tree', but it may also mean 'waiting and longing for'. This is an instance of a 'pivot-word', imitated to a certain extent in the translation, although in English we have to employ the word twice over, while it only appears once in the Japanese.</p>  
 <p>The illustration shows the pine tree on the mountain, and the poet standing under it with two attendants.</p>
