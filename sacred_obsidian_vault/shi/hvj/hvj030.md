 <img src="img/02900.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">29</h3>  <h3 align="center">MITSUNE ŌSHI-KŌCHI</h3>  <h3 align="center">ŌSHI-KŌCHI NO MITSUNE</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Kokoro-ate ni<br> Orabaya oramu<br>   Hatsu shimo no<br> Oki madowaseru<br> Shira giku no hana.  
 <p>IT was a white chrysanthemum<br>   I came to take away;<br> But, which are coloured, which are white,<br>   I'm half afraid to say,<br>   So thick the frost to-day!</p></div>  
 <p><small>Mitsune lived some time in the beginning of the tenth century, and was one of the compilers of <i>Odes Ancient and Modern</i> (the <i>Kokinshiu</i>). The illustration shows him with a boy in attendance, trying to make up his mind which flower he will pick.</small></p>
