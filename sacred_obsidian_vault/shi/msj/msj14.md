 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Master-Singers of Japan</i>, by Clara A. Walsh, [1914], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHILDREN'S SONGS</h3>  <h3 align="center">BUTTERFLY SONG</h3>  
 <p>Butterfly, butterfly! light upon the Na-leaf, pray!<br>      Or if Na-leaf tempt you not,<br>      On my hand alight and sway:<br>    Hand and Na-leaf both are nigh—<br>    Light upon one, butterfly!</p>  <h3 align="center">SNAIL SONG</h3>  
 <p>Put out your horns for a little, snail!<br> The rain falls soft, and the wind blows warm,<br> And rustles the leaves of the bamboo grass.<br> Put out your horns, ere the showers pass,<br> For the rain falls soft, and the wind blows warm!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="green">p. 73</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SIEGE SONG</h3>  
 <p>     To West and East<br>    The Land is full of foes!<br>      To North and South<br>    The foeman's standard blows,<br> And countless as the pebbles on the sand,<br>      From south of Tsukushi,<br>    And Satsuma's wild strand,<br>      Still gather foes!</p>  
 <p>  Into the void<br> Of pathless space may fall<br>   High Heaven itself—<br> The solid mountain's wall<br>   Melt in the torrents.<br> Yet one thing is sure,<br>   The Imperial Realm<br> Unshaken shall endure<br>   Firm above all!</p>  <h3 align="center">LOVER'S LAMENT IN THE EMPTY WESTERN PAVILION</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Isé Monogatari," about 901–922)</p>  
 <p>Moon! it is gone, with all its charm sublime;<br> Spring I nay, the Spring of "Once upon a time"<br> Comes nevermore with blossoms in its train;<br> All else is changed—but I unchanged remain!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE FROGS AT NIGHT</h3>  
 <p>The croaking frogs that find their lodging here,<br> Would seem to feel the loneness of the night,<br> As well as I, so plaintive is their cry!</p>  <h3 align="center">FUJI</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Arikoto</span></p>  
 <p>A thousand times I gaze upon thy form,<br> A thousand times—and each more wonderful!</p>  
 <p>Swift, with the drifting clouds, tumultuous storm,<br> The driving winds—so dost thou smile or frown—<br> Yet always beauteous, whether storm or shine,<br> O magic Mountain! Fuji the Divine</p>  <h3 align="center">THE SPRING WIND AND THE WILLOWS</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Tahira no Kanemori</span></p>  
 <p>The goddess of the Spring has spread<br>   Upon the budding willow-tree<br> Her lovely mesh of silken strands:<br>   O wind of Spring, blow lovingly<br> And gently, lest the willow thread<br>        Entangled be!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE SNOWFALL</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the Kokinshiu")</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Kujohara no Fukayabu</span></p>  
 <p>Midwinter gloom the earth enshrouds,<br>        Yet from the skies<br>        The blossoms fall<br>        A flutt’ring shower,<br>        White petals all!<br>        Can Spring be come<br> So soon beyond the clouds?</p>  <h3 align="center">ILLUSION</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Kokinshiu")</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Sakanohe no Korenori</span></p>  
 <p>In Yoshino at dawn meseemed<br> Around me paling moonlight gleamed,<br> But ’twas new-fallen snow that lay<br> Cold-shining in the light of day.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE DESERTED GARDEN</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Biwa Sadaijin</span></p>  
 <p>Once more the garden that I loved I seek,<br> Where once the footsteps of my dear ones trod,<br> Now all deserted, desolate and bleak.<br> The brown leaf flutters to the frosted sod.<br> Only the maple-foliage as of old<br> Weaves its embroidery of red and gold!</p>  <h3 align="center">CHERRY-BLOSSOM TIME</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Norinobu</span></p>  
 <p>All round the year, with careful pride,<br> My court-yard here is neatly kept,<br> Save at sweet cherry-blossom tide,<br> When ’tis untrodden and upswept.<br> White scented petals softly blow,<br> And, downward fluttering, create<br> A perfumed carpeting of snow,<br> Nor foot nor hand must desecrate.</p>  <h3 align="center">AT THE END OF THE YEAR</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Harumichi no Tsuraki</span></p>  
 <p>One speaks of yesterday,<br> Lives through to-day, <a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a><br> And on the morrow<br> Hastening away<br> Like Asu's river,<br> Ceaselessly and fast,<br> The fleeting months and days<br> Are with the past.</p>  <h3 align="center">JEWELS OF AUTUMN</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Fujiwara Kiyomasa</span></p>  
 <p>The varied grasses of the Autumn meads<br> Are gemmed with dewdrops, as with pure white beads,<br> Yet all unthreaded. As the grasses sway,<br> So, one by one, the dew-pearls slip away<br> And vanish from the radiance of the day!</p>  <h3 align="center">DEW-DRENCHED AUTUMN LEAVES</h3>  
 <p>Whence come these colours of the grass<br> That glorify the Autumn field?<br> The dew that glistens where I pass<br> One hue alone methought could yield,<br> Its crystal orbs no dyes can hold,<br> Yet the leaves change to red and gold!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="green">p. 78</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SECLUSION</h3>  
 <p>My humble dwelling, creeper-clad,<br> So lonely lies,<br> That day by day none visit me<br> Save the fire-flies!</p>  <h3 align="center">THE OLD BATTLE-FIELD</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Yorihira</span></p>  
 <p>Evanescent as dew their existence,<br> These warriors of long-vanished ages;<br> Of all their fierce contests and striving,<br> Lo! the end is the Autumn wind sighing<br> Its dirge o’er the waste place of battle!</p>  <h3 align="center">AUTUMN MOON THOUGHTS</h3>  
 <p>I look upon the harvest moon to-night,<br> She lifts my soul upon her silver rays;<br> My thoughts are tangled in a web of light<br> With vague reflections in a thousand ways.<br> Dreaming they wander in a world unknown,<br> Though Autumn's witchery is not mine alone!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_79"><font size="1" color="green">p. 79</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE WIND IN AUTUMN</h3>  
 <p>The Autumn wind tightening its plaintive strings,<br> Plays a weird nocturne, that sad thought inspires.<br> And every gust new desolation brings<br> To my lone heart, a thrill of vain desires.</p>  <h3 align="center">ILLUSIONS</h3>  
 <p>Call not the frosted branches of the trees,<br> This winter morn, all desolate and bare!<br> While the snow lies unmelted upon these,<br> I think I see the white Spring-blossom there!</p>  <h3 align="center">HAILSTONES</h3>  
 <p>Would that this hail might shower, a sparkling stream,<br> All the grey day, until the moon arise,<br> And shine upon this court, which then would gleam<br> As though a rich-gemmed pavement dazed our eyes—<br> White, glittering jewels, fresh from Paradise!</p>
