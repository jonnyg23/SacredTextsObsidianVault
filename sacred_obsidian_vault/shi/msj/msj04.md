 <p><a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="green">p. 20</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRELUDE</h3>  
 <p>I see the jewels sparkle on each spray<br> Of wind-swept moon-grass, as the reed-stems sway,<br> I try to clasp them—and they fade away<br> In ice-cold dew.</p>  
 <p>Against the turquoise of the April skies,<br> Pink haze of blossom o’er the landscape lies,<br> I try to pluck it—and its beauty dies,<br> The petals fall.</p>  
 <p>I hear a music thrilling time and space,<br> Heart-songs of Poets of a hero race<br> I try to sing them—and their dainty grace<br> Eludes me still!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="green">p. 21</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ON THE VIEW FROM MOUNT KAGU</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Manyōshiu")</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By the Mikado Jomei</span>—A.D. 623–41</p>  
 <p>Land of Yamato! of thy myriad hills,<br> Peerless and fair stands forth this Heavenly mount<br> Of high Amenokagu, from whose height,<br> Climbing its lofty brow, I stand and gaze<br> On the far-stretched champaign, whence faint blue smoke<br> Curls from a thousand dwellings, to the skies—<br> On the sea-plain, always the circling gulls<br> Rise flight on flight, and hover in the blue;<br> "Land of the Dragon-fly!" O Land to love!<br> Of rich abundance, and of fertile grain!</p>  <h3 align="center">LOVE-LAY INDITED BY THE MIKADO TEMMU</h3>  
 <p align="center">A.D. 673–86</p>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Manyôshiu")</p>  
 <p>Lo! on Mikané's heights,<br>    In fair Yoshino's Land, <a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="green">p. 22</font></a><br> Tireless the snow alights<br>    On winding mountain ways;<br> There the fierce-driven rain<br>    Ever its rage displays.<br> So, just as, ceaselessly,<br>    Snow and rain fall,<br> Dwell all my thoughts on thee,<br>    Loved above all!</p>  <h3 align="center">SPRING AND AUTUMN</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Manyôshiu")</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Princess Nukata</span>—A.D. 673–76</p>  
 <p>Now cometh Spring, all lightsomeness,<br>   From the ice-chains of Winter free,<br> And birds, whose songs erstwhile were mute,<br>   Flood all the woods with melody.</p>  
 <p>The glades where late no blossom showed,<br>   Hills that were bare, are decked with flowers,<br> But, in the tangle of the woods,<br>   I can scarce reach the songsters’ bowers.</p>  
 <p>Matted and thick with twining growths,<br>   Out of my reach the sweet buds blow,<br> While in the Autumn, undeterred,<br>   I thread the copse, where red sprays glow. <a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="green">p. 23</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thrusting the duller browns aside,<br>   Choosing the tints with flame alight,<br> Give me the Autumn hills that bring<br>   Deep breaths of pleasure and delight!</p>
