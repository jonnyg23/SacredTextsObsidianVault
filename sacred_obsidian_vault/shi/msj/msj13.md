 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="green">p. 70</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TRANSCIENCE</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Komachi</span></p>  
 <p>Swift fade the hues of hill and wold,<br> Glories of Spring and Fall depart,<br> More evanescent still, behold<br> The fading blossoms of the heart!</p>  <h3 align="center">OLD SAMURAI PRAYER</h3>  
 <p>May the Springs purely flow<br>   And sickness cease;<br> May victory crown our House<br> And treasure-stores increase;<br> Evil no longer grow,<br> And all the world know peace!</p>  <h3 align="center">ANCIENT WEDDING SONG</h3>  
 <p>The waves are still on the four seas,<br> Soft blow the time-winds, yet the trees<br> Sway not, nor rustling foliage stirs.<br> In such an age blest are the firs<br> That meet and age together.<br> Nor heavenward look and reverent gaze,<br> Nor words of gratitude and praise<br> Our thanks can tell, that all our days<br> Pass in this age with blessings stored<br> By bounty of our Sovereign Lord!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="green">p. 71</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">AT SAKUSA</h3>  
 <p align="center">(<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Verse on the Gate of Miya-Yaegaki</span>)</p>  
 <p>On the wild hills that tower to the sky,<br>      There where they fell,<br> The dead leaves lie<br> In rustling heaps piled high,<br>    There the gods dwell!</p>  <h3 align="center">THE HILL OF MEETING</h3>  
 <p align="center">(AU-SAKA, LAKE BIWA)</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Semi-Maro</span></p>  
 <p>Known and Unknown, the stranger guest,<br> Returning friends whom comrades greet,<br> Up to the Hill of Meeting's <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">*</font></a> crest,<br> Press the wayfarers' eager feet.<br> For here the fateful Barrier stands,<br> And some will part—and some will meet.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  <h3 align="center">SLUMBER SONG OF IZUMO</h3>  
 <p>Sleep, baby, sleep!<br> On mountains steep <a name="page_72"><font size="1" color="green">p. 72</font></a><br> The children of the hare are straying.<br> Why are their soft brown ears so long and slender,<br> Peeping above the rocks where they are playing?</p>  
 <p>Because their mother, ere they yet were born,<br> Ate the Loquat leaves in the dewy morn,<br> And the bamboo grass long and slender—<br> That's why the ears of baby-hares are long!<br> Sleep, baby, sleep! to the lilt of my song!</p>
