 <p><a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="green">p. 105</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KEICHIU</h3>  
 <p align="center">A.D. 1610–1701</p>  
 <p>A Priest whose reputation as a scholar attracted the attention of the famous Daimio of Mito, Mitsukuni, who caused a vast library to be collected and maintained scholars for researches which resulted in the compilation of the Dainihoushi = "History of Japan."</p>  <h3 align="center">THE COMING OF SPRING</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Keichiu</span></p>  
 <p>The Spring hath bent its magic bow<br> The heavens eterne, the earth below<br> Rich with its hidden ore, are veiled<br>        In mist-wreaths grey.<br> High on the mountain-ledge the snow<br> Melts, and once-frozen ponds o’erflow<br>        This first Spring day.<br> The plum-trees’ branches gently sway<br> From the young blossoms’ fair array<br> The nightingale trills forth her song<br>        So tender-sweet.</p>  
 <p>Now memories of the bygone year<br> Fade in our hearts, new joys appear<br>        Our hopes to greet.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="green">p. 106</font></a></p>  
 <p>When can we to the meadows pass,<br> To pluck the herbs among the grass?<br> When will the budding willows flame<br>        In living gold?<br> When will the cherry-blossoms glow?<br> To-day, in minds of high and low,<br>        These thoughts unfold!</p>
