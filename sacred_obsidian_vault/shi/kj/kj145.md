 <p><a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="green">p. 367</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CXXXVIII.—EMPEROR IN-GIYŌ (PART II.—HIS SICKNESS IS CURED BY A KOREAN PHYSICIAN).]</h2>  
 <p>The Heavenly Sovereign, when first about to rule the succession of Heaven's Sun, <a name="fr_2205"></a><a href="#fn_2200"><font size="1">1a</font></a> declined, saying: "I have a long sickness; I cannot rule the sun's succession." Nevertheless, as from the Empress downwards all the magnates strongly urged him, he forthwith ruled the Empire. At this time the ruler of Shiragi <a name="fr_2206"></a><a href="#fn_2201"><font size="1">2a</font></a> dutifully sent eighty-one vessels with august tribute. Then the chief envoy <a name="fr_2207"></a><a href="#fn_2202"><font size="1">3</font></a> sent with the august tribute, whose name was Komu-ha-chimu-kamu-ki-mu, <a name="fr_2208"></a><a href="#fn_2203"><font size="1">4</font></a> was a man deeply versed in the medical art. So he cured the Heavenly Sovereign's august sickness.</p>
