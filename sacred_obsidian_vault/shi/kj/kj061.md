 <p><a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. LIV.—EMPEROR JIM-MU (PART XI.—HIS AGE AND PLACE OF BURIAL).</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Kamu-nuna-kaha-mimi ruled the Empire. Altogether the august years of this Heavenly Sovereign Kamu-yamato-ihare-biko were one hundred and thirty-seven. <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">1</font></a> His august mausoleum is on the top of the Kashi Spur on the northern side of Mount Unebi. <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1049"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1048"></a><a href="kj061.htm#fr_1052">188:1</a> <a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a> The author's confused style must not here mislead the student. It is after the decease of the Emperor Jim-mu (Kamu-yamato-ihare-biko), who attains to the age of a hundred and thirty-seven, that the Emperor Suwi-zei (Kamu-nuna-kaha-mimi) begins to rule.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1049"></a><a href="kj061.htm#fr_1053">188:2</a> For <i>Unebi</i> see Sect. LI, Note 9. <i>Kashi</i> signifies "oak." The word rendered "spur "is <i>wo</i>.</p>
