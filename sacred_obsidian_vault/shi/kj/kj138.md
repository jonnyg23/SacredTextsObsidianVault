 <p><a name="page_357"><font size="1" color="green">p. 357</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CXXXI.—EMPEROR RI-CHIU (PART I.—GENEALOGIES).]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Iza-ho-wake dwelt in the palace of Wakasakura at Ihare, <a name="fr_2139"></a><a href="#fn_2134"><font size="1">1</font></a> and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded Her Augustness Princess Kuro, <a name="fr_2140"></a><a href="#fn_2135"><font size="1">2</font></a> daughter of the Noble of Ashida, <a name="fr_2141"></a><a href="#fn_2136"><font size="1">3</font></a> child of So-tsu-biko [287] of Kadzuraki, <a name="fr_2142"></a><a href="#fn_2137"><font size="1">4</font></a> and begot august children: King Oshiha of Ichinobe; <a name="fr_2143"></a><a href="#fn_2138"><font size="1">5</font></a> next King Mima: <a name="fr_2144"></a><a href="#fn_2139"><font size="1">6</font></a> next his younger sister Awomi-no-iratsume, <a name="fr_2145"></a><a href="#fn_2140"><font size="1">7</font></a> another name for whom was Ichi-toyo-no-iratsume. <a name="fr_2146"></a><a href="#fn_2141"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_2134"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2139">357:1</a> <a name="page_357"><font size="1" color="green">p. 357</font></a> For <i>Ihare</i> see Sect. XLIII, Note 26. <i>Waka-sakura</i> signifies "young cherry-tree." The origin of the name is traced, rightly or wrongly, to an incident mentioned in the "Chronicles" under the reign of this Emperor, 3rd year.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2135"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2140">357:2</a> <i>Kuro-hime</i> <i>i.e.</i>, "black princess." The same name occurs several times, and has reference to the black hair of the person so designated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2136"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2141">357:3</a> <i>Ashida no sukune</i>. <i>Ashida</i> signifies "reed-moor." It is the name of a place in Yamato.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2137"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2142">357:4</a> <i>Kadzuraki no So-tsu-biko</i>. For this name, which is here abbreviated, see Sect. LXI, Note 55.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2138"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2143">357:5</a> <i>Ichinobe no Oshiha no miko</i>. Ichinobe is in the province of Yamashiro, and the name seems to mean "near the market." The name of Oshiha refers to the "uneven teeth" of this personage which are mentioned in Sect. CLXVII (near Note 5).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2139"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2144">357:6</a> <i>Mima no miko</i>. The signification of this name is quite obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2140"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2145">357:7</a> <i>Awomi no iratsume</i>. <i>Awomi</i> is supposed by Motowori to be the name of a place.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2141"></a><a href="kj138.htm#fr_2146">357:8</a> <i>Ihitoyo no iratsume</i>. <i>Ihitoyo</i> is supposed by Motowori to be the name of a bird, perhaps a kind of owl.</p>
