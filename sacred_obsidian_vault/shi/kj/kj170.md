 <p><a name="page_409"><font size="1" color="green">p. 409</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CLXIII.—EMPEROR SEI-NEI (PART I.—SEARCH FOR A SUCCESSOR TO HIM).]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Shiraka-no-oho-yamato-ne-ko dwelt at the palace of Mikakuri at Ihare, <a name="fr_2361"></a><a href="#fn_2356"><font size="1">1a</font></a> and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign had no Empress, and likewise no august children. So the Shiraka-Clan <a name="fr_2362"></a><a href="#fn_2357"><font size="1">2</font></a> was established as his august proxy. So after the Heavenly Sovereign's decease, there was no King to rule the Empire. Thereupon, on enquiry [being made] for a King who should rule the sun's succession, Oshinumi-no-iratsume, <a name="fr_2363"></a><a href="#fn_2358"><font size="1">3</font></a> another name for whom was Princess Ihi-toyo, younger sister of Prince Ichinobe-oshiha-wake, <a name="fr_2364"></a><a href="#fn_2359"><font size="1">4</font></a> [was found to be] residing at the palace of Tsunusashi at Takaki in Oshinumi in Kadzuraki. <a name="fr_2365"></a><a href="#fn_2360"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_2356"></a><a href="kj170.htm#fr_2361">409:1a</a> For Ihare see Sect. XLIII, Note 26. <i>Mika-kuri</i> signifies "jar-chestnut."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2357"></a><a href="kj170.htm#fr_2362">409:2</a> <i>Shiraka-be</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2358"></a><a href="kj170.htm#fr_2363">409:3</a> In Sect. CXXXI (Note 7) this name appears as <i>Awomi-no-iratsume</i>. Both <i>Awomi</i> and <i>Oshinumi</i> are supposed to be names of places. The latter is the name of a district in Yamato. Its etymology is obscure. For <i>Ihi-toyo</i> see Sect. CXXXI, Note 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2359"></a><a href="kj170.htm#fr_2364">409:4</a> See Sect. CXXXI (Note 5), where however the title of <i>wake</i> ("Lord") is omitted.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2360"></a><a href="kj170.htm#fr_2365">409:5</a> For <i>Kadzuraki</i> see Sect. LV, Note 1, and for Oshinumi see Note 3 to the present Sect. <i>Takaki</i> seems to signify "high castle," while <i>Tsunusashi</i> is obscure. (See Motowori's remarks on these two names in Vol. XLIII, p. 3 of his Commentary.)</p>
