 <p><a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="green">p. 333</font></a>[267]</p>  <h1 align="center">VOL. III. <a name="fr_2023"></a><a href="#fn_2018"><font size="1">1</font></a></h1>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CXIX.—EMPEROR NIN-TOKU (PART I.—GENEALOGIES).]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Oho-sazaki dwelt in the palace of Takatsu <a name="fr_2024"></a><a href="#fn_2019"><font size="1">2</font></a> at Naniha, and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded (<small>the Empress <a name="fr_2025"></a><a href="#fn_2020"><font size="1">3</font></a></small>) Her Augustness Iha-no-hime, <a name="fr_2026"></a><a href="#fn_2021"><font size="1">4</font></a> daughter of Kadzuraki-no-sotsu-biko, <a name="fr_2027"></a><a href="#fn_2022"><font size="1">5</font></a> and begot august children: His Augustness Ohoye-no-izaho-wake; <a name="fr_2028"></a><a href="#fn_2023"><font size="1">6</font></a> next the Middle King of the Inlet of Sumi; <a name="fr_2029"></a><a href="#fn_2024"><font size="1">7</font></a> next His Augustness Midzu-ha-wake of Tajihi; <a name="fr_2030"></a><a href="#fn_2025"><font size="1">8</font></a> next His Augustness the Noble Wo-asadzuma-no-waku-go <a name="fr_2031"></a><a href="#fn_2026"><font size="1">9</font></a> (<small>four Deities.</small>) Again he wedded Princess Kami-naga, daughter of the Duke of Muragata in Himuka, as mentioned above, <a name="fr_2032"></a><a href="#fn_2027"><font size="1">10</font></a> and begot august children: Hatabi-no-oho-iratsuko, <a name="fr_2033"></a><a href="#fn_2028"><font size="1">11</font></a> another [268] name for whom was the King of Great Kusaka; <a name="fr_2034"></a><a href="#fn_2029"><font size="1">12</font></a> next Hatabi-no-waki-iratsume, <a name="fr_2035"></a><a href="#fn_2030"><font size="1">13</font></a> another name for whom was Her Augustness Princess Nagahi, <a name="fr_2036"></a><a href="#fn_2031"><font size="1">14</font></a> and another name was Her Augustness Waka-kusaka-be <a name="fr_2037"></a><a href="#fn_2032"><font size="1">15</font></a>(<small>Two Deities.</small>) Again he wedded his younger half-sister Yata-no-waki-iratsume <a name="fr_2038"></a><a href="#fn_2033"><font size="1">16</font></a> Again he wedded his younger half-sister Uji-no-waki-iratsume. These two Deities had no august children. Altogether the august children of this Heavenly Sovereign Oho-sakaki [numbered] in all six Deities, (<small>Five Kings and one Queen</small>). So His Augustness Izaho-wake [was he who afterwards] ruled the Empire. Next His Augustness Tajihi-no midzu-ha-wake likewise ruled the Empire. Next His Augustness the Noble Oh-asadzuma-no-waku-go likewise ruled the Empire. <a name="fr_2039"></a><a href="#fn_2034"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_2018"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2023">333:1</a> <a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="green">p. 333</font></a> Literally, "lower volume"(there being three in all). See Author's Preface, Note 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2019"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2024">333:2</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "high port."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2020"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2025">333:3</a> <a name="page_334"><font size="1" color="green">p. 334</font></a> Motowori surmises that the reason why the characters <a href="errata.htm#81">signifying</a> "Empress" are in all the text here written in small characters is on account of this personage not having been of Imperial birth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2021"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2026">333:4</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the rock princess." Motowori supposes the name to be indicative of prosperity and long life.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2022"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2027">333:5</a> See Sect. LXI, Note 55,</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2023"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2028">333:6</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the elder brother lord Izaho," the latter name being of uncertain import.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2024"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2029">333:7</a> <i>Sumi-no ye-no-naka-tsu-miko</i>. Both the phrase "middle king" and the Inlet of Sumi have been already commented on.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2025"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2030">333:8</a> <i>Tajihi no midzu-ha-wake</i>. Tajihi is the name of a place in Kahachi. The traditional origin of its application to this will be found in Motowori's Commentary, Vol. XXXV, p. 6. <i>Midzu-ha-wake</i> probably means "the lord with the beautiful teeth."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2026"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2031">333:9</a> <i>Wo-asadzuma-no-waku-go no sukune</i>. Asadzuma is the name of a place in Yamato, and <i>wo</i> (though written <a name="img_33400"></a>
 <img src="img/33400.jpg">) seems to be the slightly Honorific Prefix <i>wo</i> (<a name="img_33401"></a>
 <img src="img/33401.jpg">) whose proper signification is "small" <i>Waku-go</i> means "younger child."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2027"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2032">333:10</a> See Sect. CVII.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2028"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2033">333:11</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the great lord of Hatabi." <i>Hatabi</i> is altogether obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2029"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2034">333:12</a> <i>Oha-kusaka no miko</i>. For <i>Kusaka</i> see Sect. CI, Note 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2030"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2035">333:13</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the young lady of Hatabi." Conf. Note 1 I.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2031"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2036">333:14</a> <i>Nagahi-hime</i>. This name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2032"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2037">333:15</a> <i>Kusaka-be</i> is an alternative form of <i>Kusaka</i>. The compound therefore signifies "young princess of Kusaka."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2033"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2038">333:16</a> This name and the following have already appeared in the genealogies of the preceding reign (Sect. CIV.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2034"></a><a href="kj126.htm#fr_2039">333:17</a> These were the Emperors Ri-chiu, Han-zei, and In-giyo.</p>
