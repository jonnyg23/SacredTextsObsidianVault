 <p><a name="page_349"><font size="1" color="green">p. 349</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CXXV.—EMPEROR NIN-TOKU (PART VIII.—HE LOVES YATA-NO-WAKI-IRATSUME).]</h2>  
 <p>The Heavenly Sovereign, loving Yata-no-waki-iratsume, deigned to send her an august Song. That Song said:</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">"Will the one sedge-stem of Yata, having no children, wither as it stands? Poor sedge-moor! Sedge-moor indeed is what I may <i>say</i>—poor pure girl!" <a name="fr_2104"></a><a href="#fn_2099"><font size="1">1</font></a></div>  
 <p>Then Yata-no-waki-iratsume replied in a Song, saying:</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">"Even though the one sedge stem of Yata be alone, if the Great Lord say it is right even though it be alone [it is right.]" <a name="fr_2105"></a><a href="#fn_2100"><font size="1">2</font></a></div>  
 <p>So the Yata Tribe <a name="fr_2106"></a><a href="#fn_2101"><font size="1">3</font></a> was established as the august proxy of Yata-no-waki-iratsume.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_2099"></a><a href="kj132.htm#fr_2104">349:1</a> In this Song the Emperor condoles with his mistress on her childlessness: "Will the single sedge on the moor of Yata die without leaving any offspring? Sedge, indeed! Yes, sedge is the term I use for my metaphor, but what is in my thoughts is the girl I love."—There is in the original a <i>jeu-de-mots</i>, not capable of translation into English, between <i>suge</i> or <i>suga</i>, "sedge, and <i>sugashi</i> pure."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2100"></a><a href="kj132.htm#fr_2105">349:2</a> The girl replies: "Even though I be childless. I care not if my lord cares not."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2101"></a><a href="kj132.htm#fr_2106">349:3</a> <i>Yata-be</i>.</p>
