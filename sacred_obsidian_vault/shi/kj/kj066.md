 <p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="green">p. 195</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. LIX.—EMPEROR KOAN.]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Oho-yamato-tarashi-hiko-kuni-oshi-bito dwelt in the palace of Akidzushima at Muro <a name="fr_1110"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">1</font></a> in Kadzuraki, and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded his niece Her Augustness Princess Oshika, <a name="fr_1111"></a><a href="#fn_1107"><font size="1">2</font></a> and begot august children: His Augustness Oho-kibi-no-moro-susumi, <a name="fr_1112"></a><a href="#fn_1108"><font size="1">3</font></a> next His Augustness, Oho-yamato-ne-ko-hiko-futo-ni <a name="fr_1113"></a><a href="#fn_1109"><font size="1">4</font></a> (<small>two Deities</small>). So His Augustness Oho-yamato-ne-ko-hiko-futo-ni [159] [was he who afterwards] ruled the Empire. The heavenly Sovereign's august years were one hundred and twenty-three. His august mausoleum is on the Mound of Tamade. <a name="fr_1114"></a><a href="#fn_1110"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1106"></a><a href="kj066.htm#fr_1110">195:1</a> In Yamato. <i>Muro</i> signifies "cave" or "pit." <i>Aki-dzu-shima</i>, "the Island of the Dragon-Fly," is frequently used as an alternative name of Japan (see Sect. V, Note 26). See also the Emperor Yū-riyaku's song given in Sect. CLVI.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1107"></a><a href="kj066.htm#fr_1111">195:2</a> <i>Oshika-hime-no-mikoto</i>. The name Oshika, which is obscure, appears in the "Chronicles" under the form of <i>Oshi</i>, which has generally been interpreted by Motowori (whom the translator follows) as a corruption of <i>ohoshi</i>, "great." This version of the name makes it harmonize with that of the Princess's father.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1108"></a><a href="kj066.htm#fr_1112">195:3</a> See Sect. LX, Note 11.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1109"></a><a href="kj066.htm#fr_1113">195:4</a> This name seems to signify "Great Yamato's Lord Prince, the Vast Jewel."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1110"></a><a href="kj066.htm#fr_1114">195:5</a> <i>Tamade no woka</i>. In Yamato. For this name see Motowori's Commentary, Vol. XXI, pp. 37-38.</p>
