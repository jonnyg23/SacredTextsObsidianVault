CXLIX.—EMPEROR AN-KŌ (PART VI.—FLIGHT OF PRINCES OHOKE AND WOKE)].</h2>  
 <p>Hereupon King Ichi-no-be's children <a name="fr_2291"></a><a href="#fn_2286"><font size="1">1</font></a> King Ohoke and King Woke (two Deities), having heard of this affray, fled away. So when they reached Karibawi <a name="fr_2292"></a><a href="#fn_2287"><font size="1">2</font></a> in Yamashiro and were eating their august provisions, an old man with a tattooed face came and seized the provisions. Then the two Kings said: "We do not grudge the provisions. But</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_387"><font size="1" color="green">p. 387</font></a></p>  
 <p>who art thou? "He replied, saying: "I am a boar-herd in Yamashiro." So they fled across the River Kusuba, <a name="fr_2293"></a><a href="#fn_2288"><font size="1">3</font></a> reached the land of Harima, <a name="fr_2294"></a><a href="#fn_2289"><font size="1">4</font></a> entered the house of a native of that country named Shizhimu, <a name="fr_2295"></a><a href="#fn_2290"><font size="1">5</font></a> hid their persons, and worked as grooms and cow-herds.</p>
