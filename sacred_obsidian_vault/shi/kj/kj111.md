 <p><a name="page_299"><font size="1" color="green">p. 299</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CIV.—EMPEROR Ō-JIN <a name="fr_1819"></a><a href="#fn_1814"><font size="1">1</font></a> (PART 1.—GENEALOGIES).]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Homuda-wake dwelt at the palace of Akira at Karushima, <a name="fr_1820"></a><a href="#fn_1815"><font size="1">2</font></a> and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded three <a name="fr_1821"></a><a href="#fn_1816"><font size="1">3</font></a> queens, daughters of King Homuda-no-Ma-waka, <a name="fr_1822"></a><a href="#fn_1817"><font size="1">4</font></a> the name of one of whom was Her Augustness Princess Takagi-no-iri; <a name="fr_1823"></a><a href="#fn_1818"><font size="1">5</font></a> of the next, Her Augustness Naka-tsu-hime; <a name="fr_1824"></a><a href="#fn_1819"><font size="1">6</font></a> and of the next, Her Augustness Oto-hime. <a name="fr_1825"></a><a href="#fn_1820"><font size="1">7</font></a> (<small>The father of these Queens, King Homuda-no-ma-Waka, was the son of His Augustness Prince Iho-ki-noiri, <a name="fr_1826"></a><a href="#fn_1821"><font size="1">8</font></a> by his [241] wife Shiritsuki-tome, <a name="fr_1827"></a><a href="#fn_1822"><font size="1">9</font></a> daughter of the Noble Take-inada, <a name="fr_1828"></a><a href="#fn_1823"><font size="1">10</font></a> ancestor of the Chiefs of Wohari.</small>) <a name="fr_1829"></a><a href="#fn_1824"><font size="1">11</font></a> So the august children of Her Augustness Princess Takagi-no-iri were: His Augustness Nakuta-no-oho-waka-tsu-hiko; <a name="fr_1830"></a><a href="#fn_1825"><font size="1">12</font></a> next His Augustness Oho-yama-mori; <a name="fr_1831"></a><a href="#fn_1826"><font size="1">13</font></a> next His Augustness Iza-no-ma-waka; <a name="fr_1832"></a><a href="#fn_1827"><font size="1">14</font></a> next his younger sister the Lady of Ohohara; <a name="fr_1833"></a><a href="#fn_1828"><font size="1">15</font></a> next the Lady of Komuku. <a name="fr_1834"></a><a href="#fn_1829"><font size="1">16</font></a> (<small>Five Deities</small>). The august children of Her Augustness Naka-tsu-hime were: the Lady of Arata in Ki; <a name="fr_1835"></a><a href="#fn_1830"><font size="1">17</font></a> next His Augustness Oho-sazaki; <a name="fr_1836"></a><a href="#fn_1831"><font size="1">18</font></a> next His Augustness Netori. <a name="fr_1837"></a><a href="#fn_1832"><font size="1">19</font></a> (Three Deities). The august [242] children of Her Augustness Oto-hime were: the Lady Abe; <a name="fr_1838"></a><a href="#fn_1833"><font size="1">20</font></a> next the Lady of Mihara in Ahaji; <a name="fr_1839"></a><a href="#fn_1834"><font size="1">21</font></a> next</p>

 <p>the Lady of Unu in Ki; <a name="fr_1840"></a><a href="#fn_1835"><font size="1">22</font></a> next the Lady of Minu <a name="fr_1841"></a><a href="#fn_1836"><font size="1">23</font></a> (<small>five Deities</small>). <a name="fr_1842"></a><a href="#fn_1837"><font size="1">24</font></a> Again he wedded the Princess Miya-nushi-ya-kaha-ye, <a name="fr_1843"></a><a href="#fn_1838"><font size="1">25</font></a>daughter of the Grandee Wani-no-Hifure, and begot august children: Uji-no-waki-iratsuko; <a name="fr_1844"></a><a href="#fn_1839"><font size="1">26</font></a> next his younger sister Yata-no-waki-iratsume; <a name="fr_1845"></a><a href="#fn_1840"><font size="1">27</font></a> next Queen Medori. <a name="fr_1846"></a><a href="#fn_1841"><font size="1">28</font></a> (<small>Three <a href="errata.htm#73">Deities</a></small>). Again he wedded Wo-nabe-no-iratsume, <a name="fr_1847"></a><a href="#fn_1842"><font size="1">29</font></a> younger sister of Yakaha-ye-hime, and begot an august child: Uji-no-iratsume. <a name="fr_1848"></a><a href="#fn_1843"><font size="1">30</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again he wedded Okinaga-ma-waka-naka-tsu-hima," <a name="fr_1849"></a><a href="#fn_1844"><font size="1">31</font></a> daughter of King Kuhimata-naga-hiko, <a name="fr_1850"></a><a href="#fn_1845"><font size="1">32</font></a> and begot an august child: King Waka-nuke-futa-mata <a name="fr_1851"></a><a href="#fn_1846"><font size="1">33</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again he wedded the Princess of Itowi, <a name="fr_1852"></a><a href="#fn_1847"><font size="1">34</font></a> daughter of Shima-tari-ne, <a name="fr_1853"></a><a href="#fn_1848"><font size="1">35</font></a> ancestor [243] of the Agricultural Chiefs of Sakurawi, <a name="fr_1854"></a><a href="#fn_1849"><font size="1">36</font></a> and begot an august child; His Augustness Hayabusa-wake <a name="fr_1855"></a><a href="#fn_1850"><font size="1">37</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again, he wedded Naga-hime of Idzumi in Himuka, <a name="fr_1856"></a><a href="#fn_1851"><font size="1">38</font></a> and begot august children: King Oho-haye, <a name="fr_1857"></a><a href="#fn_1852"><font size="1">39</font></a> next King Wo-haye; <a name="fr_1858"></a><a href="#fn_1853"><font size="1">40</font></a> next Hata-bi-no-waki-iratsume <a name="fr_1859"></a><a href="#fn_1854"><font size="1">41</font></a> (<small>three Deities</small>). Again he wedded Princess Ka-guro, <a name="fr_1860"></a><a href="#fn_1855"><font size="1">42</font></a> and begot august children: Kaha-rada-no-iratsume; <a name="fr_1861"></a><a href="#fn_1856"><font size="1">43</font></a> next, Tama-no-iratsume, <a name="fr_1862"></a><a href="#fn_1857"><font size="1">44</font></a> next, Osaka-no-oho-naka-tsu-hime; <a name="fr_1863"></a><a href="#fn_1858"><font size="1">45</font></a> next, Toho-hi-no-iratsume; <a name="fr_1864"></a><a href="#fn_1859"><font size="1">46</font></a> next, King Kataji <a name="fr_1865"></a><a href="#fn_1860"><font size="1">47</font></a> (<small>five Deities</small>). Again, he wedded Nu-iro-me of Kadzuraki, <a name="fr_1866"></a><a href="#fn_1861"><font size="1">48</font></a> and begot an august [244] child: King Iza-no-ma-waka, <a name="fr_1867"></a><a href="#fn_1862"><font size="1">49</font></a> (one Deity). The august children of this Heavenly Sovereign [numbered] altogether twenty-six (<small>eleven Kings and fifteen Queens</small>). Of these His Augustness Oho-sazaki [was he who afterwards] ruled the Empire.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1814"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1819">299:1</a> Son of the Emperor Chiū-ai and the Empress Jin-gō.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1815"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1820">299:2</a> In Yamato. <i>Akira</i> Signifies "brilliant." <a href="errata.htm#69"><i>Karushima</i></a> seems to mean "the neighbourhood of Karu, Karu being the often mentioned place of that name in Yamato.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1816"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1821">299:3</a> The Auxiliary Numeral for deities is here used.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1817"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1822">299:4</a> <a name="page_301"><font size="1" color="green">p. 301</font></a> <i>Homuda-no-ma-waka no miko</i>. <i>Homuda</i> has already been met with as the came of a place in Kahachi. <i>Ma-waka</i> signifies "truly young." The name might therefore be rendered "truly young king of Homuda."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1818"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1823">299:5</a> <i>Takagi no iri-bime no mikoto</i>. Motowori identifies this princess with the Takaki-hime of Sect. LXXVI, Note 21.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1819"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1824">299:6</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "middle princess," she being the second of three sisters.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1820"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1825">299:7</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "younger princess," she being the youngest of the sisters.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1821"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1826">299:8</a> <i>I-ho-ki-no-iri-biko no mikoto</i>. See Sect. LXXVI, Note 12.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1822"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1827">299:9</a> <i>I.e.</i>, probably "old woman of Shiritsuki." But it is not certain that Shiritsuki is the name of a place.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1823"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1828">299:10</a> <i>Take-inada no sukune</i>. In the "Chronicles of Old Matters of Former Ages" the name is written <i>Take-ina-dane</i>, and it may therefore mean "brave-rice-seed."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1824"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1829">299:11</a> <i>Wohari no murazhi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1825"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1830">299:12</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "great middle Prince of Nukata," the latter being the name of a place in Yamato. It is of uncertain signification.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1826"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1831">299:13</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "great mountain-warden." For the appropriateness of this name conf. Sect. CV.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1827"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1832">299:14</a> The same name has appeared in Sect. LXIII, Note 13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1828"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1833">299:15</a> <i>Ohohara no iratsume</i>. <a href="errata.htm#70">Ohohara</a> is the name of a place in Yamato It signifies "great moor."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1829"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1834">299:16</a> <i>Komuku no iratsume</i>. This name is written <a name="img_30100"></a>
 <img src="img/30100.jpg"> and its reading as <i>Komuku</i> is somewhat <a href="errata.htm#71">hypothetical</a>. It is the name of a place in Kahachi, and probably signifies "an overflowing pool of water."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1830"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1835">299:17</a> <i>Ki no Arata no iratsume</i>. <i>Ki</i> is the <a href="errata.htm#72">province</a> of that name, and Arata is a place in it. The latter name probably means "uncultivated fields."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1831"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1836">299:18</a> This name signifies "Great Wren," and is thus accounted for by the author of the "Chronicles": "On the day when the Emperor [this Prince became the Emperor Nin-toku] was born, an owl flew into the parturition-hall. Next morning early, the Heavenly Sovereign Homuda [<i>i.e.</i>, the Emperor Ō-jin] sent for the Prime Minister, the Noble Takeuchi, and asked him whereof this might be a sign. The Prime Minister replied, saying: 'It is a good omen. Moreover yesterday, when thy servant's wife was delivered of a child, a wren flew into the parturition-house, likewise a strange thing.' Then the Heavenly Sovereign said: 'It is a portent from heaven that my child and thine should be born on the same day, and both be attended by a good omen. So let the names of the birds be taken,' and each used for the name of the other [<i>i.e.</i>, <a name="page_302"><font size="1" color="green">p. 302</font></a> the name of the owl for him into whose parturition-house the wren flew, and <i>vice-versâ</i>], as a covenant for the future.' So the wren's name was bestowed on the Heir Apparent, who was called Great-Wren Prince, and the owl's name was give Ito the Prime Minister's child. who was called the Noble Owl."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1832"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1837">299:19</a> This name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1833"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1838">299:20</a> <i>Abe no iratsume</i>. Abe is the name of several places in different provinces, and is of obscure derivation and import.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1834"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1839">299:21</a> <i>Ahaji no Mikara no iratsume</i>. The text properly has <i>Ayuchi</i>, but Motowori emends this to <i>Ahaji</i> on the authority of the "Chronicles."' Mihara is the name of a district in the island of Ahaji, and probably signifies "three moors."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1835"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1840">300:22</a> <i>Ki no Unu</i> <a href="errata.htm#74"><i>no</i></a> <i>iratsume</i>. <i>Ki</i> is the province of that name, and <i>Unu</i> a place in it. The latter name is of uncertain import.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1836"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1841">300:23</a> <i>Minu no iratsume</i>. <i>Minu</i> (<i>Mino</i>) is the province of that name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1837"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1842">300:24</a> "Five" must here be a mistake for "four."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1838"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1843">300:25</a> For this name and the next see Sect. CVI, Notes 5 and 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1839"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1844">300:26</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the young lord of Uji." Uji is the name of a district in Yamashiro, famous in classical and modern times for its tea. The etymology is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1840"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1845">300:27</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the young lady of Yata." Yata is the name of a place in Yamato. The etymology is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1841"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1846">300:28</a> <i>Medori no</i> miko. <i>Medori</i> signifies "hen-bird:" but the reason for the application of so strange a name to this princess, whose fortunes are related at some length in Sect. CXXVI and CXXVII, does not appear. A similar remark applies to the next name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1842"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1847">300:29</a> <i>I.e.</i>, probably "the lady of the little kettle."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1843"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1848">300:30</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the young lady of Uji."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1844"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1849">300:31</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the truly young middle princess of Okinaga."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1845"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1850">300:32</a> See Sect. XCII, Note 27.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1846"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1851">300:33</a> <i>Waka-muke-futa-mata no miko</i>. This name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1847"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1852">300:34</a> <i>Itowi-hime</i>. Itowi is the name of a place in Yamato, and is of uncertain origin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1848"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1853">300:35</a> <i>Shima</i> is probably the name of a place, while <i>tari</i> and <i>ne</i> are the frequently recurring Honorifics rendered respectively "perfect" and "lord "in former parts of this translation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1849"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1854">300:36</a> <i>Sakurawi no ta-be no murazhi</i>. <i>Ta-be</i>, rendered "agricultural," is literally "rice-field tribe." <i>Sakurawi</i> ("cherry well") is the name of a place in Kahachi.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1850"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1855">300:37</a> <i>I.e.</i>, falcon-lord.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1851"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1856">300:38</a> <a name="page_303"><font size="1" color="green">p. 303</font></a> <i>Himuka no Idzumi no Naga-hime</i>. Himuka is the name of a province, and Idzumi that of a district now comprised within .the limits of Satsuma. <i>Nagahime</i>, literally "long princess," probably signifies "elder princess."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1852"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1857">300:39</a> <i>Oho-haye no miko</i>. Signification obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1853"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1858">300:40</a> <i>Wo-haye no miko</i>. Signification obscure. The antithesis of the Adjectives <i>oho</i> and <i>wo</i> ("great" and "small") shows however that the names partly served to distinguish the elder from the younger brother.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1854"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1859">300:41</a> <i>Waki-iratsume</i> is "younger lady." <i>Hata-bi</i> is incomprehensible.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1855"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1860">300:42</a> See Sect. LXXVI, Note 28.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1856"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1861">300:43</a> <i>I.e.</i>, probably "the lady of Kaharada." The latter name (literally "rice-field on the border of a river") is often met with.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1857"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1862">300:44</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the jewel lady."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1858"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1863">300:45</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the great middle lady of Osaka," the latter being the name of a place in Yamato (see Sect. XLVIII, Note 1).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1859"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1864">300:46</a> Motowori identifies this name with that <i>Koto-fushi no iratsume</i> in Sect. CXVII, <i>q.v.</i>, and thinks that both this and the preceding name have only crept into this Section by mistake.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1860"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1865">300:47</a> <i>Kataji no miko</i>, Signification obscure,</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1861"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1866">300:48</a> <i>Kadzuraki no Nu-iro-me</i>. All the elements of this name have already been met with several times.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1862"></a><a href="kj111.htm#fr_1867">300:49</a> This child has already appeared early in this Section, and the name is here doubtless only repeated through some copyist's error.</p>
