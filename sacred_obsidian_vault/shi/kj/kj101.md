 <p>The Heavenly Sovereign Wata-tarashi-hiko dwelt at the palace of Taka-anaho at Shiga <a name="fr_1713"></a><a href="#fn_1708"><font size="1">1b</font></a> in Chika-tsu-Afumi and ruled the empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded [227] the Lady Oho-takara, <a name="fr_1714"></a><a href="#fn_1709"><font size="1">2</font></a> daughter of Take-oshiyama-tari-ne, <a name="fr_1715"></a><a href="#fn_1710"><font size="1">3</font></a> ancestor of the Grandees of Hodzumi, <a name="fr_1716"></a><a href="#fn_1711"><font size="1">4</font></a> and begot an august child: King Wata-nuke <a name="fr_1717"></a><a href="#fn_1712"><font size="1">5</font></a> (one Deity). So [the</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1708"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1713">281:1b</a> <i>Shiga no Taka-anaho</i>. <i>Shiga</i> is the name of a well known district, and is of uncertain, signification, as is also <i>Taka-anaho</i>. For <i>Chika-tsu-Afumi</i> see Sect. XXIX, Note 20.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1709"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1714">281:2</a> <i>Oto-takara no iratsume</i>. <i>Oto</i> signifies "younger [sister]," and <i>takara</i> is "treasure."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1710"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1715">281:3</a> <i>Oshiyama</i> is the name of a place in Ise, <i>take</i> signifies "brave" and <i>tari</i> and <i>ne</i> are Honorifics of frequent occurrence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1711"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1716">281:4</a> <i>Hodzumi no omi</i>. See Sect. LXI, Note 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1712"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1717">281:5</a> <i>Waka-nuke no miko</i>. This name is of doubtful signification, and Motowori suspects that it is corrupt, and that the true reading would be <i>Waka-take</i>, "young-brave."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1713"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1718">282:6</a> See Sect. LXI, Note 25.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1714"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1719">282:7</a> <a name="img_28200"></a>
 <img src="img/28200.jpg">. Motowori tries to prove that in the earliest times this official title was simply an Honorific surname formed by prefixing the Adjective <a name="img_28201"></a>
 <img src="img/28201.jpg"> "great" to <a name="img_28202"></a>
 <img src="img/28202.jpg">, a surname read "Omi" (the character signifies properly "attendant," "subject.") Probably like other "gentile names" it combined both characters, and had a tendency to become hereditary.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1715"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1720">282:8</a> <i>Oho-kuni wo-kuni no kuni no miyatsuko</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1716"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1721">282:9</a> <i>Oho-agata wo-agata no agata nushi</i> (<a name="img_28203"></a>
 <img src="img/28203.jpg">). Their duties are supposed to have consisted in supervising the government farms.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1717"></a><a href="kj101.htm#fr_1722">282:10</a> For <i>Saki</i> see Sect. LXXV, Note 5. <i>Tatanami</i> may perhaps signify "putting shields in a row."</p>
