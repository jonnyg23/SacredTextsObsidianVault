 <p><a name="page_303"><font size="1" color="green">p. 303</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CV.—EMPEROR Ō-JIN (PART II.—HE DIVIDES THE INHERITANCE BETWEEN HIS THREE SONS)]</h2>  
 <p>Hereupon the Heavenly Sovereign asked His Augustness Oho-yama-mori and His Augustness Oho-sazaki, saying: "Which think ye the dearer, an elder child or a younger child?" (<small>The reason why the Heavenly Sovereign propounded this question was because it was his intention <a name="fr_1868"></a><a href="#fn_1863"><font size="1">1</font></a> to make Uji-no-waki-iratsuko rule the Empire</small>). Then His Augustness Oho-yama-mori said: "The elder child is the dearer." Next His Augustness Oho-sazaki, knowing the august</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_304"><font size="1" color="green">p. 304</font></a></p>  
 <p>feeling which made the Heavenly Sovereign deign to ask [the question], said: "The elder child, having already become a man, gives no trouble; but the younger child, not being yet a man, is the dearer." Then the Heavenly Sovereign said: "My lord Sazaki's words agree with my thoughts," and forthwith ordained the division [of the inheritance] thus: His Augustness Oho-yama-mori to administer the government of the mountains and the sea, <a name="fr_1869"></a><a href="#fn_1864"><font size="1">2a</font></a> His Augustness Oho-sazaki to take and deign to report on the government of the realm, <a name="fr_1870"></a><a href="#fn_1865"><font size="1">3</font></a> and Uji-no waki-iratsuko to rule the succession of Heaven's sun. <a name="fr_1871"></a><a href="#fn_1866"><font size="1">4</font></a> So His Augustness Oho-sazaki was not disobedient to the Heavenly Sovereign's commands. <a name="fr_1872"></a><a href="#fn_1867"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>
