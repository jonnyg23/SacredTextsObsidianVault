 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="green">p. 205</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. LXII.—EMPEROR KAI-KUWA.]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Waka-yamato-ne-ko-hiko-ōho-bibi dwelt in the palace of Izakaha at Kasuga, <a name="fr_1206"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">1</font></a> and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded the Princess of Takanu, <a name="fr_1207"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">2</font></a> daughter of Yugori <a name="fr_1208"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">3</font></a> the Great Departmental Lord of Taniha, <a name="fr_1209"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">4</font></a> and begot an august child: His [167] Augustness Hiko-yumusumi, <a name="fr_1210"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">5</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again he wedded his step-mother, Her Augustness I-gaka-shiko-me, <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">6</font></a> and begot august children: His Augustness Mima-kiri-biko-iniwe; <a name="fr_1212"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">7</font></a> next Her Augustness Mima-tsuhime <a name="fr_1213"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">8</font></a> (<small>two Deities</small>). Again he wedded Her Augustness Oke-tsu-hime, <a name="fr_1214"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">9</font></a> younger sister of His Augustness Hiko-kuni-oketsu, <a name="fr_1215"></a><a href="#fn_1211"><font size="1">10</font></a> ancestor of the Grandees of Wani, <a name="fr_1216"></a><a href="#fn_1212"><font size="1">11</font></a> and begot an august child: King Hiko-imasu <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1213"><font size="1">12</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again, wedding Princess Washi, <a name="fr_1218"></a><a href="#fn_1214"><font size="1">13</font></a> daughter of the Noble Kadzuraki-no-tarumi, <a name="fr_1219"></a><a href="#fn_1215"><font size="1">14</font></a> he begot an august child,—King Take-tayo-hadzura-wake <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1216"><font size="1">15</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>); The august children of this Heavenly Sovereign [numbered] in all five Deities (four Kings and one Queen), So His Augustness Mi-maki-iri-biko-iniwe [was he who afterwards] ruled the Empire. The children of his elder brother, King Hiko-yumusumi were: King Oho-tsutsuki-tari-ne; <a name="fr_1221"></a><a href="#fn_1217"><font size="1">16</font></a> next King Sanugi-tari-ne <a name="fr_1222"></a><a href="#fn_1218"><font size="1">17</font></a> (<small>two Kings</small>). There [168] were five Deities daughters of these two Kings. Next King Hiko-imasu wedded the Princess of Yena in Yamashiro, <a name="fr_1223"></a><a href="#fn_1219"><font size="1">18</font></a> another name for whom was Kari-bata-tobe, <a name="fr_1224"></a><a href="#fn_1220"><font size="1">19</font></a> and begot children: King Ohō-mata; <a name="fr_1225"></a><a href="#fn_1221"><font size="1">20</font></a> next King Wo-mata; <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1222"><font size="1">21</font></a> next King Noble [of?] Shibumi <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1223"><font size="1">22</font></a> (<small>three Deities</small>). Again, wedding Saho-no-oho-kurami-tome, <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1224"><font size="1">23</font></a> daughter of Take-kuni-katsu-tome, of Kasuga, <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="#fn_1225"><font size="1">24</font></a> he begot children: King Saho-biko; <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="#fn_1226"><font size="1">25</font></a> next King Wo-zaho; <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="#fn_1227"><font size="1">26</font></a> next Her Augustness Saho-bime, <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1228"><font size="1">27</font></a> another name for whom is Sahaji-hime <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1229"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (<small>Her Augustness Saho-bime here mentioned was consort of the Heavenly Sovereign Ikume</small>); <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1230"><font size="1">29</font></a> and King Muro-biko, <a name="fr_1235"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">30</font></a> (four Deities). Again, wedding Okinaga-no-midzu-yori-hime, <a name="fr_1236"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">31</font></a> daughter of the [169] Heavenly Deity Mikage, <a name="fr_1237"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">32</font></a> who is held in reverence by the deacons of Mikami in Chika-tsu-Afumi, <a name="fr_1238"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">33</font></a> he begot children: King Tatatsu-michi-no-ushi, Prince of Taniha; <a name="fr_1239"></a><a href="#fn_1235"><font size="1">34</font></a> next King Midzuho-no-ma-wa-ka; <a name="fr_1240"></a><a href="#fn_1236"><font size="1">35</font></a> next King Kamu-oho-ne, <a name="fr_1241"></a><a href="#fn_1237"><font size="1">36</font></a> another name for whom is King Yatsuri-iri-biko; <a name="fr_1242"></a><a href="#fn_1238"><font size="1">37</font></a> next Midzuho-no-i-ho-yori-hime; <a name="fr_1243"></a><a href="#fn_1239"><font size="1">38</font></a> next Mimi-tsu-hime; <a name="fr_1244"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">39</font></a> (<small>three Deities</small>). Again, wedding his mother's younger sister Her Augustness Woke-tsu-hime, <a name="fr_1245"></a><a href="#fn_1241"><font size="1">40</font></a> he begot children: King Ma-wata of Oho-tsutsuki in Yamashiro; <a name="fr_1246"></a><a href="#fn_1242"><font size="1">41</font></a> next King Hiko-osu; <a name="fr_1247"></a><a href="#fn_1243"><font size="1">42</font></a> next king Iri-ne <a name="fr_1248"></a><a href="#fn_1244"><font size="1">43</font></a> (<small>two Deities</small>). Altogether the children of King Hiko-imasu [numbered] in all eleven Kings. So the children of the elder brother King Oho, mata were: King Ake-tatsu; <a name="fr_1249"></a><a href="#fn_1245"><font size="1">44</font></a> next King Unakami <a name="fr_1250"></a><a href="#fn_1246"><font size="1">45</font></a> [170] (<small>two Deities</small>). This King Ake-tatsu (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes of the Homuji Tribe in Ise <a name="fr_1251"></a><a href="#fn_1247"><font size="1">46</font></a> and of the Rulers of Sana in Ise</small>) <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1248"><font size="1">47</font></a> King Una-kami (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes of Himeda.</small> <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1249"><font size="1">48</font></a> The next King Wo-mata (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes of Magari in Tagima</small>). <a name="fr_1254"></a><a href="#fn_1250"><font size="1">49</font></a> The next King Noble Shibumi (was the ancestor of the Dukes of Sasa) <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1251"><font size="1">50</font></a> The next King Saho-biko (<small>was the ancestor of the Chiefs of the Kusaka Tribe <a name="fr_1256"></a><a href="#fn_1252"><font size="1">51</font></a> and of the Rulers of the Land of Kahi</small>). <a name="fr_1257"></a><a href="#fn_1253"><font size="1">52</font></a> The next, King Wo-zaho (<small>was the ancestor of the Lords of Kadzunu <a name="fr_1258"></a><a href="#fn_1254"><font size="1">53</font></a> and the Lords of Kanu in Chika-tsu-Afumi.</small>) <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1255"><font size="1">54</font></a> The next King Muro-biko (<small>was the ancestor of the Lords of Mimi in Wakasa.</small>). <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1256"><font size="1">55</font></a> King Michi-no-ushi wedded the Lady Masu of Kahakami in Taniha, <a name="fr_1261"></a><a href="#fn_1257"><font size="1">56</font></a> and begot children: Her Augustness Princess Hibasu; <a name="fr_1262"></a><a href="#fn_1258"><font size="1">57</font></a> next Her Augustness Princess Matonu; <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1259"><font size="1">58</font></a> next Her Augustness Oto-hime; <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1260"><font size="1">59</font></a> next King Mi-kado-wake <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1261"><font size="1">60</font></a> (<small>four Deities</small>). This King Mikado-wake [171] (<small>was the ancestor of the Lords of Ho in Mikaha</small>). <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1262"><font size="1">61</font></a> Prince Midzuho-no-ma-waka, younger brother of this King Michi-no-ushi,</p>

This [172] King was the ancestor of the Dukes of Homuji in Kibi, <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1276"><font size="1">75</font></a> and of the Dukes of Aso in Harima</small>). <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1277"><font size="1">76</font></a> Again King Noble Okinaga wedded Princess Inayori of Kahamata <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1278"><font size="1">77</font></a> and begot a child: King Oho-tama-saka <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1279"><font size="1">78</font></a> (<small>This was the ancestor of the Rulers of the land of Tajima</small>). <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1280"><font size="1">79</font></a> The above-mentioned Take-toyo-hadzu-ra-wake (<small>was the ancestor of the Grandees of Chimori, <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">80</font></a> of the Rulers of the Oshinumi Tribe, <a name="fr_1286"></a><a href="#fn_1282"><font size="1">81</font></a> of the Rulers of the Mima Tribe, <a name="fr_1287"></a><a href="#fn_1283"><font size="1">82</font></a> of the Oshinumi Tribe in Inaba, <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">83</font></a> of the Lords of Takanu in Taniba, <a name="fr_1289"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">84</font></a> and of the Abiko of Yosami</small>). <a name="fr_1290"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">85</font></a> The Heavenly Sovereign's august years were sixty-three.  <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1202"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1206">205:1</a> <a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="green">p. 207</font></a> For Kasuga see Sect. LVIII, Note 7. Izakaha is a place in Yamato. The signification of the name is uncertain.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1203"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1207">205:2</a> <i>Takanu-hime</i>. Takanu is the name of a district in Tango, and signifies "bamboo moor."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1204"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1208">205:3</a> The signification of this name is quite obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1205"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1209">205:4</a> <i>Taniha no oho-agata-nushi</i>. <i>Taniha</i> (modern <i>Tamba</i>) is the name of a province (formerly including the province of Tango) in Central Japan. It is supposed to mean "the place of rice-fields," the rice offered at the shrine of the Sun-Goddess in Ise being brought thence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1206"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1210">205:5</a> <a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="green">p. 208</font></a> <i>Hiko</i> signifies "prince." The other syllables of tie name are obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1207"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1211">205:6</a> See Sect. LXI, Note 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1208"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1212">205:7</a> <i>Biko</i> (<i>hiko</i>) signifies "prince." The other elements of this compound are obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1209"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1213">205:8</a> One is tempted to render this name by Princess of Mima. But there is no authority for regarding Mima either in this or in the preceding personal name as originally the name of a place.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1210"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1214">205:9</a> Motowori has no explanation to offer of the syllables Oke-tsu. <i>Hime</i> means "princess."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1211"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1215">205:10</a> <i>Hiko</i> means "prince "and <i>kuni</i> means "country.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1212"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1216">205:11</a> <i>Wani no omi</i>. <i>Wani</i> is a place in Yamato, and there is a pass or hill of that name (<i>Wani-zaka</i>). The only signification belonging to the word wani is "crocodile."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1213"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1217">205:12</a> <i>Hiko-imasu no miko</i>. Signification obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1214"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1218">205:13</a> Or "the Princess of Washi "or "the Eagle Princess." In Japanese <i>Washihime</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1215"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1219">205:14</a> <i>Kadzuraki</i> is the name of a district in Yamato, and Motowori believes Tarumi to be the name of a place in Settsu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1216"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1220">205:15</a> <i>Take-toyo-hadzura-wake no miko</i>. The first two elements of the compound signify respectively "brave "and "luxuriant" while the last probably means "lord." The signification of <i>hadzura</i> is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1217"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1221">205:16</a> <i>Oho-tsutsuki-tari-ne no miko</i>. Tsutsuki being the name of a district in Yamashiro, the whole compound signifies "prince great sufficing lord of great Tsutsuki."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1218"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1222">205:17</a> <i>Sanugi-tari-ne no miko</i>, <i>i.e.</i>, "prince sufficing lord of Sanugi," (Sanuki,—see Sect. V, Note 6).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1219"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1223">205:18</a> <i>Yamashiro no Yena tsu Hime</i>. Yamashiro is the name of a province, and Yena that of a place in Settsu. The signification of the latter name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1220"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1224">205:19</a> Motowori believes <i>Karibata</i> to be the name of a place, and <i>tobe</i> (<i>to-me</i>) to signify "old woman," as in the name <i>Ishi-ko-ri-do-me</i>, which latter is however extremely obscure (see Sect. XVI, Note 12).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1221"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1225">205:20</a> <i>Oho-mata no miko</i>. The signification of this name and of the parallel name of the younger brother is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1222"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1226">205:21</a> <i>Wo-mata no miko</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1223"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1227">205:22</a> <i>Shibumi no Sukune no miko</i>. Shibumi is probably the name of a place, there being a Shibumi in Ise.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1224"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1228">205:23</a> <i>Saho</i> is the name of a well-known place in Yamato, and <i>Kurami</i> <a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="green">p. 209</font></a> is supposed by Motowori to be the name of a place in Wakasa. <i>Oho</i> means "great," and <i>tome</i>, according to Motowori, means "old woman" or simply "female." <i>Conf</i>. Note 19.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1225"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1229">205:24</a> <i>Kasuga no Take-kuni-katsu-tome</i>. Motowori supposes this to be the name, not of the father, but the mother of the princess just mentioned. <i>Take</i> signifies "brave," and <i>kuni</i> "land." The meaning of <i>katsu</i> is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1226"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1230">205:25</a> <i>Saho-biko no miko</i>, <i>i.e.</i>, "Prince of Saho."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1227"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1231">205:26</a> <i>Wō-zaho no miko</i>, <i>i.e.</i>, "little (<i>q.d.</i> 'younger') prince of Saho."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1228"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1232">205:27</a> <i>I.e.</i>, the princess of Saho.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1229"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1233">205:28</a> It is uncertain whether we should understand this name to signify "princess Sahaji" or "the princess of Sahaji," but the latter seems the more probable.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1230"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1234">206:29</a> <i>I.e.</i>, the Emperor Sui-nin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1231"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1235">206:30</a> <i>More-biko no miko</i>. <i>Muro-biko</i> signifies "Prince of Muro." Muro being a place in Yamato. It signifies "dwelling "and specially "cave."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1232"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1236">206:31</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "flourishing and good princess of Okinaga," the latter being the name of a place in Afumi (Omi). Its signification is not clear.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1233"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1237">206:32</a> <i>Ame no Mikaga no kami</i>. The signification of Mikaga is obscure, as is also the connection between this deity and the deacons of Mikami.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1234"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1238">206:33</a> The signification of the name Mikami is obscure. The word rendered "deacon" is <i>hufuri</i>, the name of an inferior class of Shinto priests. See for a discussion of the etymology of the word, etc., Mr. Satow's remarks on p. 112 of Vol. VII of these Transactions. For Chika-tsu-Afumi see Sect. XXIX, Note 20.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1235"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1239">206:34</a> <i>Tanika no hiko Tatatsu-michi-no-ushi no miko</i>. The signification of <i>Tatatsu</i> is obscure, but we may accept it as the personal name of the worthy here mentioned. <i>Michi-no-ushi</i> means "master of the road," <i>i.e.</i> "lord of the province."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1236"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1240">206:35</a> <i>Midzuho no ma-waka no miko</i>, <i>i.e.</i> "the True Young King of Midzuho," Midzuho being the name of a place in Afumi. It probably means "fresh young rice-ears."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1237"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1241">206:36</a> <i>Kamu-oho-ne no miko</i>, <i>i.e.</i>, probably, "prince divine great lord."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1238"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1242">206:37</a> <i>Yatsuri-iri-biko no miko</i>. Yatsuri is the name of a village in Yamato, and is of uncertain origin. The signification of <i>iri</i> obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1239"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1243">206:38</a> <i>I-ho</i> signifies "five hundred "and <i>yori</i> probably signifies "good." The compound may therefore be taken to mean "manifoldly excellent princess of Midzuho."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1240"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1244">206:39</a> <a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="green">p. 210</font></a> <i>I.e.</i>, probably "the Princess Miwi" (Miwi-dera in Afumi). <i>Mi-wi</i> signifies "three wells."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1241"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1245">206:40</a> This name is parallel to that which has been commented on in Note 9 to this Section.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1242"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1246">206:41</a> <i>Yamashiro-no-oho-Tsutsuki no ma-waka no miko</i>. All the elements of this compound have already appeared in this Section.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1243"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1247">206:42</a> <i>Hiko-osu no miko</i>. The signification of osu is obscure,</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1244"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1248">206:43</a> <i>Iri-ne no miko</i>. Signification obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1245"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1249">206:44</a> <i>Ake-tatsu no miko</i>. See Sect. LXXII, Note 20.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1246"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1250">206:45</a> <i>Unakami no miko</i>. See Sect. LXXII, Note 19.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1247"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1251">206:46</a> <i>Ise no Homuji-be no kimi</i>. See the mention of the establishment of this Clan at the end of Sect. LXXII.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1248"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1252">206:47</a> <i>Ise no Sana no miyatsuko</i>. The etymology of Sana is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1249"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1253">206:48</a> <i>Himeda no kimi</i>. Himeda is a place in Afumi. The signification of the name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1250"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1254">206:49</a> <i>Tagima no Magari no kimi</i>. Tagima is the name of a district in Yamato, and is of uncertain origin. Magari is the name of a place, and means "crook "or "bend."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1251"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1255">206:50</a> <i>Sasa no kimi</i>: Sasa is the name of a place in Iga, and is of uncertain signification.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1252"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1256">206:51</a> <i>Kusakabe no murazhi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1253"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1257">206:52</a> <i>Kahi no kuni no miyatsuko</i>. For Kahi see Sect. LXXXVI, Note 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1254"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1258">206:53</a> <i>Kadzunu no wake</i>. Kadzunu is the name of a district in Yamashiro, and signifies "pueraria moor."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1255"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1259">206:54</a> <i>Chika-tsu-Afumi no Kanu no wake</i>. Kanu is a village in Afumi. The name is written with characters signifying "mosquito moor."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1256"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1260">206:55</a> <i>Wakasa no Mimi no wake</i>. Mimi is the name of a village, and is of uncertain signification.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1257"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1261">206:56</a> <i>Taniha no Kahakami no Masu no iratsume</i>. Masu is of uncertain. derivation. Kahakami is the name of a village, now comprised in the province of Tango. It signifies "river-land."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1258"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1262">206:57</a> <i>Hibasu-hime</i>. The signification of this name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1259"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1263">206:58</a> <i>Matonu-hime</i>. The signification of this name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1260"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1264">206:59</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the younger princess."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1261"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1265">206:60</a> This name, which is written <a name="img_21000"></a>
 <img src="img/21000.jpg">, is curious, and Motowori has no suggestion to make touching its interpretation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1262"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1266">206:61</a> <i>Mikaha no Ho no wake</i>. Ho is the name of a district, and is of obscure derivation. Mikaha is the name of a maritime province. It <a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="green">p. 211</font></a> signifies "three rivers," with reference to two large rivers which flow through it and to another which forms the boundary between it and the province of Wohari.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1263"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1267">207:62</a> <i>Chika-tsu-Afumi no Yasu no atahe</i>. Yasu is the name of a district, and is of uncertain origin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1264"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1268">207:63</a> <i>Minu no kuni no</i> [<i>miyatsuko</i>]. The word <i>miyatsuko</i>, which is not in the text, is supplied in Motowori's <i>kana</i> reading.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1265"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1269">207:64</a> <i>Motosu no kuni no miyatsuko</i>. Motosu is the name of a district in Mino, and seems to signify "original dwelling-place."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1266"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1270">207:65</a> <i>Nagahata-be no murazhi</i>. Nagahata is the name of a place in Hitachi, and seems to signify "long loom."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1267"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1271">207:66</a> <i>Mone no Ajisaha-bime</i>. This name is particularly obscure, and Mone probably corrupt.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1268"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1272">207:67</a> <i>Kani-me-ikadzuchi no miko</i>. Motowori thinks that this name signifies "fierce like a crab's eye," with reference perhaps to some personal peculiarity of the prince who bore it.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1269"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1273">207:68</a> <i>Takaki-hime</i>. This name is obscure and perhaps corrupt.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1270"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1274">207:69</a> <i>Taniha no Tohotsu omi</i>. This name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1271"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1275">207:70</a> <i>Okinaga no sukune no miko</i>. For Okinaga see Note 31 to this Section.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1272"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1276">207:71</a> <i>Kadzuraki no Takanuka-hime</i>. Takanuka is the name of a place in Yamato. It is written with characters. signifying "high brow."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1273"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1277">207:72</a> <i>Okinaga-tarashi-hime</i>. Okinaga is the name of a place (see Note 31). <i>Tarashi</i> is an honorific designation, signifying literally "sufficient," <i>i.e.</i>, "perfect," and is supposed by Motowori to have been bestowed after death on this princess, who was. the celebrated conqueror of Korea, and is better known to fame by her "canonical name" of Jingō Kōgō.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1274"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1278">207:73</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the princess of the sky."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1275"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1279">207:74</a> <i>Okinaga-hiko no miko</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1276"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1280">207:75</a> <i>Kibi no Homuji no kimi</i>. Homuji is the name of a district in the modern province of Bingo, and may perhaps be of Chinese origin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1277"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1281">207:76</a> <i>Harima no Aso no kimi</i>. Aso is the name of a place, and is of uncertain origin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1278"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1282">207:77</a> <i>Kahamata no Ina-yori-bime</i>. Kahamata ("river-fork") is the name of a place in Kohachi. <i>Ina</i> signifies "rice," and <i>yori</i> probably signifies "good "in this and numerous other proper names.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1279"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1283">207:78</a> <i>Oho-tamu-saka no miko</i>. This name is obscure. Motowori thinks that <i>Tamu-saka</i> may be the name of a place, and signifying "winding ascent."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1280"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1284">207:79</a> <a name="page_212"><font size="1" color="green">p. 212</font></a> <i>Tajima no kuni no miyatsuko</i>. For <i>Tajima</i> see Sect. LXXIV, Note 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1281"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1285">207:80</a> <i>Chi-mori no omi</i>. <i>Chi-mori</i> signifies "road-keeper," and perhaps we should translate this "gentile name," by "road-keeping grandees," and suppose that anciently they may have performed some functions in which the bestowal of it originated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1282"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1286">207:81</a> <i>Oshinumi-be no miyatsuko</i>. Oshinumi is the name of a district in Yamato, and is of uncertain import.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1283"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1287">207:82</a> <i>Mina-be no miyatsuko</i>. Perhaps we should rather translate thus, "the Rulers of Minabe," for the name is altogether obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1284"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1288">207:83</a> <i>Inaba no Oshinumi-be</i>. Motowori supposes a branch of this family, which was originally established in Yamato, to have removed to the province of Inaba.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1285"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1289">207:84</a> <i>Taniha no Takanu no wake</i>. Takanu is the name of a district in the modern province of Tango. It signifies "high moor."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1286"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1290">207:85</a> <i>Yosami no obiko</i>. Yosami is the name of a place in Kahachi and is of uncertain origin, though the legends connect with the word and, "a net" (see Motowori's Commentary, Vol. XXII, p. 81). It is chiefly known on account of its lake or pool, which is often mentioned in the early poetry. Abiko is a very rare "gentile name," which in the "Catalogue of Family Names "is written with the characters <a name="img_21200"></a>
 <img src="img/21200.jpg">, but is derived by Motowori from <a name="img_21201"></a>
 <img src="img/21201.jpg">, <i>i.e.</i>, "my grandchildren."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1287"></a><a href="kj069.htm#fr_1291">207:86</a> In Yamato. The name is of uncertain origin.</p>
