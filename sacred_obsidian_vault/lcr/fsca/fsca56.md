 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="green">p. 217</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Musimon, Tityrus</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">A fictitious</span> animal mentioned by Guillim and others. It nearly<a name="img_21700"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/21700.jpg" alt="Musimon, Tityrus."></td></tr></table> resembles <i>a goat, with the head and horns of a ram</i>, but has besides the horns of that beast, <i>a pair of goat's horns</i>. It is also mentioned in Guillim's "Display," where it is said to be a bigenerous beast, of unkindly procreation, engendered between a goat and a ram, like the Tityrus, the offspring of a sheep and goat, as noted by Upton.
