 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Fictitious and Symbolic Creatures in Art</i>, by John Vinycomb, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">Stelliones</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> supporters of the Ironmongers’ Company of London are two lizards. Bossewell describes beasts of similar shape—"Stelliones" as he terms them, evidently in allusion to steel. He says, "Stellio is a beaste like a lysard, having on his back spotts like starres." <a name="fr_27"></a><a href="#fn_27"><font size="1">*</font></a></p>  
 <p>Stellione-serpent, a serpent with the head of a weasel, borne by the name of Baume.</p>
