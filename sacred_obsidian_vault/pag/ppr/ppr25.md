   <h3 align="center">HYMN OF TLA-LOC</h3>  
 <p align="center">(<i>Mexican</i>)</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">(Tla-loc is the God of Storms. He dwells in Tlalocan where the spirits of the elements toss the balls of thunder, and cast the reeds of lightning. This is a masked dramatized ceremony to the rain god)</font></p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Priests</i></p>  
 <p>TO OUR land the god appears! His banner unfolds to the Four Ways and no one weeps!"</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Tla-loc</i></p>  
 <p>"I, the god, have returned again! I have turned again to the place of abundance of sacrifices. West-ward, when the day grows old I am beheld as a god."</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>High Priest</i></p>  
 <p>"Thy work is that of a magician divine! Truly thou hast made thyself to be of our flesh. Thou hast made thyself, and who dare affront thee?"</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Tla-loc</i></p>  
 <p>"Truly he who affronts me faces the dangers; My fathers took by the heads the tigers and serpents!"</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Priests</i></p>  
 <p>"In Tla-locan, the Place of the Divine, they play at ball! They cast the reeds!"</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>High Priest to Devotees</i></p>  
 <p>"Go forth! Go forth to where the clouds are spread--where the thick mist marks the cloudy house of Tla-loc!"</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Tla-loc</i></p>  
 <p>"Go ye out to seek me! Seek for the voice I send forth as I rise--a terrible god--a cry on the winds!"</p>
