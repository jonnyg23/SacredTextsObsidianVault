 <p>Neopaganism covers a wide range of belief systems
 which have emerged in the past 50 years,
 primarily in the UK, Europe, and the United States.
 This includes the better known Wicca, which is a synthesis of
 traditions from the British Isles, as well as many less visible
 groups which draw inspiration from other parts of the world.  
 Based on folk-lore, traditional spiritual practices,
 anthropology, and a synthesis of esoteric systems, Neopaganism does
 not have any sacred texts of the same vintage as other religions,
 although unverifiable claims have been made in a couple of cases
 (e.g. <a href="aradia/index.htm">Aradia</a>,
 and <a href="gbos/index.htm">The Gardnerian Book of Shadows</a>).</p>
 
 <p>
 Rather, the books presented here are source texts of Neopaganism, and
 other books which deal with related topics.
 Many of these books contain outdated or speculative material,
 and some of the texts here are the product of the Renaissance witch hunters.
 As a whole, the books here are of historical importance rather
 than a roadmap to modern pagan practice, and shouldn't be construed as such.
 As always, we encourage readers to think critically.</p>
 
 
 
 <p>Descriptions of contemporary Neopagan practice and beliefs can be found
 in the <a href="../bos/index.htm">Internet Book of Shadows</a> section
 of this site.</p>

 <h3><a name="20thcen">Twentieth Century</a></h3>
 <p>
 <a href="gardner.htm">The Books of Gerald Gardner</a></p>
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="gbos/index.htm">The Gardnerian Book of Shadows</a></span><br>
 <span class="c_b">The Book of Shadows is a Wiccan text which is maintained by
 the initiated in manuscript form.</span>
 A Book of Shadows contains description of rituals, spells, and other
 knowledge.
 This tradition was carried on by <span class="c_a">Gerald Gardner</span>, who (depending on the
 account) either synthesized Wicca, or took it public, during the 1950s.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <br>
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="twg.htm">The White Goddess</a></span><br>
 <span class="c_b">Robert Graves' primary contribution to modern Neopaganism</span>.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="murray.htm">The works of Margaret Murray</a></span><br>
 <span class="c_a"><!-- John Bruno Hare --></span>
 <span class="c_b">An essay by J.B. Hare.</span>
 </span>
 <br>
 
 </p><p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="wcwe/index.htm">The Witch-Cult in Western Europe</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Margaret Murray</span> [<span class="c_d">1921</span>]
 <br>
 <span class="c_b">Were there ever REAL witches?</span>
 If not, what were all the witch trials about?
 And how about those fairies?
 Murray tries to answer these and other questions objectively with
 plenty of documentary evidence.
 She is often cited as a primary source for Gerald Gardners' ideology.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="frazer/index.htm">The Golden Bough</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Sir James Frazer</span> [<span class="c_d">1922</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">A massive study of the mythological cycle of
 the Godesses' lover, the solar God who dies and
 is reborn</span>. The Golden Bough had a huge influence on
 <a href="murray.htm">Margaret Murray</a>,
 <a href="twg.htm">Robert Graves</a> and <a href="gardner.htm">Gardner</a>.
 </span>
 </p>

 <h3><a name="victorian">Victorian</a></h3>
 <p>
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="aradia/index.htm">Aradia, Gospel of the Witches</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Charles G. Leland</span> [<span class="c_d">1899</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Evidence of a surviving underground Italian pagan religion?</span>
 </span>
 <br>
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="err/index.htm">Etruscan Roman Remains in Popular Tradition</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Charles G. Leland</span> [<span class="c_d">1893</span>]
 <br>
 
 <span class="c_b">Stregheria is Italian traditional witchcraft.</span>
 These are considered historical source documents
 of the Wicca movement. Some contemporary scholars
 have questioned the authenticity of Lelands' scholarship.
 Recent publications by Raven Grimassi have also
 added a great deal of depth to the subject of Italian witchcraft.
 See <a href="https://www.stregheria.com/">www.stregheria.com</a> [external site]
 for more information.<br>
 </span>
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="gsft/index.htm">Gypsy Sorcery and Fortune Telling</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Charles G. Leland</span> [<span class="c_d">1891</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Leland investigates another traditional system of magic, that of the Rom, or Gypsies.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="scott/index.htm">Letters on Demonology and Witchcraft</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Sir Walter Scott</span> [<span class="c_d">1885</span>].<br>
 <span class="c_b">Scott covers much of the same evidence for the Witch cult as Murry</span>
 (albeit in a more popular style).
 Scott draws few conclusions other than
 that our ancestors were extremely superstitous.
 </span>
 </p><p>
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="sor/index.htm">The Sorceress</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">Jules Michelet</span>, tr. by <span class="c_a">Alfred Richard Allinson</span> [<span class="c_d">1939</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The story of witchcraft from the medieval to the 17th century</span>, as a covert women's rebellion which led to modern science and medicine.
 </span>
 </p>

 <h3><a name="burning">The Burning Times</a></h3>
 
 <p>
 From the 14th to the 17th Century a hysteria spread across Europe
 which involved torturing and executing people based on accusations
 that they were witches.
 Whether any actual practicioners of a pre-Christian pagan tradition were
 persecuted as the result of a witch trial is up for debate.
 These books and texts are period documents which illustrate
 the methods, rationale (such as it was) and history of this persecution.
 They shouldn't be taken as illustrative of Neopaganism, but as
 a warning about religious tolerance and the fragile nature of justice.
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <br>
 
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="mm/index.htm">The Malleus Maleficarum</a></span><br>
 [<span class="c_d">1486</span>], translated by <span class="c_a">Montague Summers</span> [<span class="c_d">1928</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">The best known witch-hunt manual, a primary source of information on this chilling subject.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="kjd/index.htm">Dæmonology</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">King James the First</span>. [<span class="c_d">1597</span>]
 With <span class="c_t"><i>Newes from Scotland</i></span> [<span class="c_d">1591</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Two texts, one an essay on demons and other denizens of the night</span>, the
 second a broadside with an account of a famous witch trial from the same
 period.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="twp/index.htm">The Witch-Persecutions</a></span><br>
 Edited by <span class="c_a">George L. Burr</span> [<span class="c_d">1896</span>].<br>
 <span class="c_b">A short collection of translations of historical documents of the witch craze.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 
 <hr>
 <h3><a name="druid">Druids</a></h3>
 
 <p>Although little is actually known about the Druids, that didn't
 stop 18th and 19th century intellectuals from building a romantic
 mythology around them.
 This was closely associated with the rebirth of
 <a href="../neu/index.htm#celtic">Celtic nationalism</a>,
 as well as the Romantic and
 <a href="../goth/index.htm">Gothic</a> movements.
 This body of fact and speculation later became a central source
 of modern Wicca and Neo-Pagan belief and practice.
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="idr/index.htm">Irish Druids and Old Irish Religions</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">James Bonwick</span> [<span class="c_d">1894</span>].<br>
 <span class="c_b">A scholarly perspective on the Druids in Ireland, weaving together strands of mythology and anthropology to build a picture of ancient Irish paganism.</span>
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="motd/motd.htm">The Veil of Isis or Mysteries of the Druids</a></span><br>
 By <span class="c_a">W. Winwood Reade</span> [<span class="c_d">1861</span>].
 <i>277,613 bytes</i><br>
 <span class="c_b">A typical example of the romantic and misconcieved mid-19th Century literature about Druidry.</span>
 We now know that the construction of Stonehenge
 preceded the historical Celts by many centuries.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../neu/celt/rac/index.htm">The Religion of the Ancient Celts</a></span><br>
 by <span class="c_a">J. A. MacCulloch</span> [<span class="c_d">1911</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">An authoritative and factual study of ancient Celtic religion</span>,
 including extensive material on what is actually known about the Druids.
 </span>
 </p>
 
 <p>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e"><span class="c_t"><a href="../neu/celt/bim1/index.htm">The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I</a></span> [<span class="c_d">1862</span>]</span><br>
 <a href="#" onclick="window.open('../cdshop/index.htm','cartwin','');"><img src="../img/buycd.jpg" height="20" border="0"></a>
 
 <span class="c_e">
 <span class="c_t"><a href="../neu/celt/bim2/index.htm">The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. II</a></span> [<span class="c_d">1874</span>]<br>
 <span class="c_b">Iolo Morganwg was one of the first to revive Druidry in
 the 18th century</span>; however, it is questionable whether these texts
 are as old as they claim to be.
 These are nevertheless considered primary source material
 for the modern Druid revivial.
 </span>
 </p>
