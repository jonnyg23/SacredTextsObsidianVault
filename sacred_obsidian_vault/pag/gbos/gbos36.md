 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Gardnerian Book of Shadows</i>, by Gerald Gardner, at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">The Witches' Chant or Rune</h1> 
 <p align="CENTER">(1957)</p> 
 <p>Darksome night and Shining Moon, East, then South, then West, then North, Harken to the Witches Rune: Here come I to call thee forth.</p> 
 <p>Earth and Water, Air and Fire, Wand and Pentacle and Sword, Work ye unto my desire, Harken ye unto my word.</p> 
 <p>Cords and Censer, Scourge and knife, Powers of the Witches Blade, Waken all ye into life, Come ye as the Charm is made:</p> 
 <p>Queen of Heaven, Queen of Hell, Horned Hunter of the Night, Lend your power unto the Spell, Work my will by Magic Rite.</p> 
 <p>If chant is used to reinforce a work already begun, end with this:</p> 
 <p>By all the power of land and sea, by all the might of moon and sun,</p> 
 <p>What is my will- "So mote it be,"What I do say- "It shall be done."</p>
