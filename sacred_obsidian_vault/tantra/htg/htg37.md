 <p><a name="page_229"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 229</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MAHĀLAKṢMĪ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(MAHĀLAKṢMĪSTOTRA)</h3>  <h3 align="center">BY INDRA <a name="fr_1404"></a><a href="#fn_1396"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>INDRA said:</p>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O Mahālakṣmī! I salute Thee,<br> Thou art <i>Mahāmāyā</i> <a name="fr_1405"></a><a href="#fn_1397"><font size="1">2</font></a> and <i>Śrīpītha</i>. <a name="fr_1406"></a><a href="#fn_1398"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Worshipped art Thou by Devas,<br> Holder of conch, disc, and mace. <a name="fr_1407"></a><a href="#fn_1399"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> O Mahālakṣmī! I salute Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>O Mahālakṣmī! I salute Thee.<br> Mounted art Thou on the back of Garuda. <a name="fr_1408"></a><a href="#fn_1400"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Thou art a terror most formidable to Asura Kola.<br> Thou removeth all sins.<br> O Devī Mahālakṣmī! obeisance to Thee.<br> O Mahālakṣmī!<br> Thou knowest all.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_230"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 230</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Giver of boons art Thou to all;<br> Formidable terror to the wicked;<br> Remover of all pain and sorrow.<br> O Devī! salutation to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>O Devī Mahālakṣmī!<br> Thou art the giver of intelligence and success,<br> And of both worldly enjoyment and liberation.<br> Thou art the self of <i>Mantra</i>. <a name="fr_1409"></a><a href="#fn_1401"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> O Mahālakṣmī! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>Thou art without beginning or end.<br> O Supreme Devī Mahālakṣmī!<br> Thou art the primeval power,<br> And art born of <i>yoga</i>.<br> O Mahālakṣmī! salutation to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>Thou art both gross and subtle, <a name="fr_1410"></a><a href="#fn_1402"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thou art terrible and a great power,<br> Great-bellied art Thou. <a name="fr_1411"></a><a href="#fn_1403"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Thou removeth all great sins.<br> O Mahālakṣmī! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_231"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 231</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>O Devī Mahālakṣmī!<br> Thou art the supreme Brahman,<br> The ever-pervading <i>Ātman</i>.<br> Thou art the great Lord <a name="fr_1412"></a><a href="#fn_1404"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> And Mother of the world.<br> O Mahālakṣmī! Salutation to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>O Devī clad in white raiment, <a name="fr_1413"></a><a href="#fn_1405"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Adorned with varied gems.<br> Mother and upholder of the world art Thou.<br> O Mahālakṣmī! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>The <i>Sādhaka</i> <a name="fr_1414"></a><a href="#fn_1406"><font size="1">3</font></a> who ever reads <a name="fr_1415"></a><a href="#fn_1407"><font size="1">4</font></a> this hymn to <a href="errata.htm#21">Mahālakṣmī</a>.<br> Composed of eight verses,<br> Attains a kingdom and all success.</p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>Whosoever reads this hymn once a day<br> Is freed from sin,<br> He who reads it twice a day<br> Has ever abundance of paddy <a name="fr_1416"></a><a href="#fn_1408"><font size="1">5</font></a> and wealth.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_232"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 232</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Whosoever reads this hymn thrice a day,<br> All his great enemies perish;<br> Mahālakṣmī ever bestows Her grace on him,<br> Grants him all boons,<br> And does him all good.</p>
