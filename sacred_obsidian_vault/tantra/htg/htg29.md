 <p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 180</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">WAVES OF BLISS</h3>  <h3 align="center">(ĀNANDALAHARĪ)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O BHAVĀNĪ, <a name="fr_1129"></a><a href="#fn_1123"><font size="1">1</font></a> the four-headed Lord of creatures, <a name="fr_1130"></a><a href="#fn_1124"><font size="1">2</font></a> is not able to worship Thee,<br> Nor even the five-headed destroyer of the Tripurā, <a name="fr_1131"></a><a href="#fn_1125"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Nor the six-headed commander of the celestial hosts, <a name="fr_1132"></a><a href="#fn_1126"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Nor even the thousand-headed Lord of serpents. <a name="fr_1133"></a><a href="#fn_1127"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> If, then, they cannot, tell me who else is able so to do?</p>  
 <p>2</p>  
 <p>O Devī! how can we speak of Thy qualities,<br> Which are not to be described by any <i>Nigama</i>, <a name="fr_1134"></a><a href="#fn_1128"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> As the sweetness of ghee, <a name="fr_1135"></a><a href="#fn_1129"><font size="1">7</font></a>milk, the grape, and honey</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_181"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 181</font></a></p>  
 <p>Cannot be distinguished and described by words,<br> But may be perceived by the tongue only;<br> In like manner Thy beauty can be seen only by the eyes of Parameśvara. <a name="fr_1136"></a><a href="#fn_1130"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>We ever pray to Thee, O Gaurī! <a name="fr_1137"></a><a href="#fn_1131"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Youthful daughter of the Lord of mountains.<br> Beautiful is the betel <a name="fr_1138"></a><a href="#fn_1132"><font size="1">3</font></a> in Thy mouth<br> And the collyrium on Thy eyes;<br> Beautiful, too, are the saffron on Thy forehead,<br> The necklet of pearls on Thy throat,<br> Thy silken garment and the glittering gold waist-ornament on Thy large hips. <a name="fr_1139"></a><a href="#fn_1133"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>May Bhagavatī, <a name="fr_1140"></a><a href="#fn_1134"><font size="1">5</font></a> Satī, <a name="fr_1141"></a><a href="#fn_1135"><font size="1">6</font></a> whose lotus eyes sparkle, <a name="fr_1142"></a><a href="#fn_1136"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Spouse of Śambhu, <a name="fr_1143"></a><a href="#fn_1137"><font size="1">8</font></a> on the slope of whose breasts<br> Rests a beautiful garland of the flowers of the Mandāra tree, <a name="fr_1144"></a><a href="#fn_1138"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Whose earring is the pleasing sound from the <i>vīnā</i>, <a name="fr_1145"></a><a href="#fn_1139"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_182"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 182</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who stoops (from the weight of her breasts), <a name="fr_1146"></a><a href="#fn_1140"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Whose beautiful swaying gait is that of the female elephant <a name="fr_1147"></a><a href="#fn_1141"><font size="1">2</font></a>--<br> May that Bhagavatī be ever victorious!</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>O beauteous Aparṇā! <a name="fr_1148"></a><a href="#fn_1142"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Bestow the fulness of happiness on me,<br> Thou whose limbs art covered<br> With ornaments of gold and gems glittering like the newly risen sun,<br> Whose eyes are beautiful as those of a doe,<br> Of whom Śiva is a part, <a name="fr_1149"></a><a href="#fn_1143"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Who is of the golden colour of lightning,<br> Beauteous in yellow garments and tinkling anklets.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>Shines forth does the Devī born in the snowy mountains. <a name="fr_1150"></a><a href="#fn_1144"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_183"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 183</font></a></p>  
 <p>Her beautiful hands are like a red leaf. <a name="fr_1151"></a><a href="#fn_1145"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> She is adorned with beautiful flowers and pearls.<br> Her head, by its weight of hair, seems covered by a swarm of bees. <a name="fr_1152"></a><a href="#fn_1146"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> It is She with whom Śiva seeks shelter,<br> Who stoops from the weight of Her breasts, <a name="fr_1153"></a><a href="#fn_1147"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Whose words are sweet,<br> The Destructress of ills, <a name="fr_1154"></a><a href="#fn_1148"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Ever and in all places pervading, <a name="fr_1155"></a><a href="#fn_1149"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Tender creeper <a name="fr_1156"></a><a href="#fn_1150"><font size="1">6</font></a> of Intelligence and Bliss. <a name="fr_1157"></a><a href="#fn_1151"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Others worship with reverence the plant with leaves and particular qualities,<br> But I know that Aparṇā alone in this world should be worshipped. <a name="fr_1158"></a><a href="#fn_1152"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Then the old Śiva garmented with space</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 184</font></a></p>  
 <p>Surely grants to Thy worshipper the fruit of full liberation. <a name="fr_1159"></a><a href="#fn_1153"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Thou art the Mother of all Vedas,<br> The regulator of all <i>dharmas</i> <a name="fr_1160"></a><a href="#fn_1154"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> And the root of all wealth--<br> Thou whose lotus feet are worshipped even by the wealth-giver. <a name="fr_1161"></a><a href="#fn_1155"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> O Mother! Thou art the primal cause of all desires.<br> Victrix of Kandarpa, <a name="fr_1162"></a><a href="#fn_1156"><font size="1">4</font></a> Thou art the seed of liberation for the good. <a name="fr_1163"></a><a href="#fn_1157"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Thou art the Spouse of the Parabrahman. <a name="fr_1164"></a><a href="#fn_1158"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>Although my mind be fickle and wanting in great devotion to Thee,<br> Yet by Thy mercy Thou should look auspiciously upon me.<br> The cloud gives sweet water to the mouth of the <i>Cātaka</i> <a name="fr_1165"></a><a href="#fn_1159"><font size="1">7</font></a> bird.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 185</font></a></p>  
 <p>I know not by what (good) fate my mind is directed. <a name="fr_1166"></a><a href="#fn_1160"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>O virtuous One, from the corner of Thine eyes<br> Cast now a glance of kindness upon me;<br> Neglect so to do is not proper on Thy part,<br> Seeing that I have reached the refuge of Thy initiation.<br> Alas! the creeper of desire, <a name="fr_1167"></a><a href="#fn_1161"><font size="1">2</font></a> whose very name shows that it gives desire,<br> Yet cannot give that which is desired,<br> What difference is there between it and any other common creeper?</p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>I, though I have sought refuge with other Devatās,<br> Have yet placed full trust in Thy lotus feet.<br> If, nevertheless, your heart is not timely set on me,<br> Then with whom shall I in my helplessness seek shelter,<br> O Mother of the big-bellied one! <a name="fr_1168"></a><a href="#fn_1162"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>As iron touched by the touchstone becomes at once gold,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 186</font></a></p>  
 <p>As the water of the roadway mixed with that of the Ganges becomes pure,<br> In like <a href="errata.htm#17">manner</a> will not my heart,<br> Greatly soiled though it be by my great sins <a name="fr_1169"></a><a href="#fn_1163"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Become pure if attached with devotion to Thee?</p>  
 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>O Īśānī, <a name="fr_1170"></a><a href="#fn_1164"><font size="1">2</font></a> as the old Lotus-Born <a name="fr_1171"></a><a href="#fn_1165"><font size="1">3</font></a> and others have said,<br> The rule is that if others than Thyself art worshipped,<br> Only the particular fruit desired is gained;<br> But Thou giveth more even than is asked for.<br> Make me, then, ever attached to Thee by day and night.</p>  
 <p align="center">14</p>  
 <p>O Spouse of the great Lord of the three worlds! <a name="fr_1172"></a><a href="#fn_1166"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Most pleasant is Thy abode,<br> The walls whereof glitter with various gems and crystals,<br> Whereon Thy image is reflected.<br> On the summit of Thy abode the quivering light waves of the moon (are shed).<br> Therein dwell Mukunda, <a name="fr_1173"></a><a href="#fn_1167"><font size="1">5</font></a> Brahmā, and other Devas.<br> It is ever victorious.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 187</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">15</p>  
 <p>Thy dwelling is in Mount Kailāsa. <a name="fr_1174"></a><a href="#fn_1168"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Thy worshippers are Brahmā, Indra, and other Devas.<br> All are subservient to Thee in the three regions. <a name="fr_1175"></a><a href="#fn_1169"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> The number of <i>siddhis</i> <a name="fr_1176"></a><a href="#fn_1170"><font size="1">3</font></a> join their palms (in adoration before Thee.).<br> Śiva is Thy lover;<br> Therefore, O Daughter of the Lord of mountains <a name="fr_1177"></a><a href="#fn_1171"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Nothing is equal to Thy fortune.</p>  
 <p align="center">16</p>  
 <p>The old bull is (Śiva's) carrier.<br> Poison is his food; <a name="fr_1178"></a><a href="#fn_1172"><font size="1">5</font></a> space is his dwelling;<br> The cremation ground is his playground; <a name="fr_1179"></a><a href="#fn_1173"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Serpents are his ornaments.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 188</font></a></p>  
 <p>All things in the world are known to the enemy of Smara; <a name="fr_1180"></a><a href="#fn_1174"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> But the wealth of all this is due to the greatness of Thy fortune,<br> O Mother!</p>  
 <p align="center">17</p>  
 <p>The Lord of <i>Paśus</i>, <a name="fr_1181"></a><a href="#fn_1175"><font size="1">2</font></a> besmeared with ashes, sits in the cremation ground.<br> From his nature arises the force which destroys the world.<br> Out of compassion for the whole world, He held the poison in his throat. <a name="fr_1182"></a><a href="#fn_1176"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> O Kalyāṇi! <a name="fr_1183"></a><a href="#fn_1177"><font size="1">4</font></a> in all this I see the fruit of his companionship with Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">18</p>  
 <p>O Daughter of the mountain, <a name="fr_1184"></a><a href="#fn_1178"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> When Gangā had seen Thy great beauty,<br> She was afraid, <a name="fr_1185"></a><a href="#fn_1179"><font size="1">6</font></a> and turned to water;<br> Then Śiva, seeing her sad, lotus-like face,<br> In his mercy made a dwelling for Her on his own head. <a name="fr_1186"></a><a href="#fn_1180"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p><a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 189</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">19</p>  
 <p>O Bhagavatī, <a name="fr_1187"></a><a href="#fn_1181"><font size="1">1</font></a> the Creator having with his own hands taken Thy bathing water<br> Mingled with liquid sandal, musk, saffron, and flowers,<br> And the dust of Thy moving feet,<br> Created therewith the lotus-eyed women of the city of the Devas. <a name="fr_1188"></a><a href="#fn_1182"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">20</p>  
 <p>If one but contemplates Thee, in play with Thy maidens,<br> In pleasing springtide with its flowers and creepers<br> Upon the lake, beautiful with many a blossoming lotus and flocks of geese,<br> The waters of which are rippled by the breeze from the Malaya mountain, <a name="fr_1189"></a><a href="#fn_1183"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> From such an one all fevered ills <a name="fr_1190"></a><a href="#fn_1184"><font size="1">4</font></a> pass away.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1123"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1129">180:1</a> Bhava is Śiva, and is His name in the watery form of the <i>aṣṭamūrti</i> (eight forms).  <p><a name="fn_1139"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1145">181:10</a> The stringed instrument of that name borne by the Devī as Sarasvatī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1140"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1146">182:1</a> <i>Nātangī</i>. So also the Annapurṇā dhyāna represents the Devī as giver of food "stooping from the weight of Her great breasts" (<i>annapradāna niratāmstanabhāranamrām</i>, and see verse 6 <i>post</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1141"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1147">182:2</a> <i>Mātangīruciragati bhangī bhagavatī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1142"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1148">182:3</a> Name of the Devī. According to the Kālikā, and Brahmā Purāṇas the Devī, as the daughter of Himavat, renounced even leaves as food (<i>a-parṇā</i> = without leaf); hence she is called by Devas Aparṇā. According to another derivation, the name comes from <i>apa</i> (removing), <i>rina</i> (debt). So Bhāskararāya, who gives it, says in his <i>Devīstava</i>; "When you have not discharged your debt to me, though I respect your name, O Śivé why are you not ashamed to bear the name of Aparṇā?" (discharger of debt)? According to the Nirukta, <i>parna</i> = falling. <i>Aparṇa</i> = free from falling.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1143"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1149">182:4</a> That is, it is by Her favour that Śiva forms part of Her.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1144"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1150">182:5</a> <i>Himādrehsambhūtā</i>--that is, the Himālaya, hence She is also called <i>Girijā</i> (mountain-born).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1145"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1151">183:1</a> Either from their natural colour or because dyed with lac.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1146"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1152">183:2</a> The bee goes to the lotus; the bees (her hair) settle upon her (lotus) face.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1147"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1153">183:3</a> <i>Kucābharanatā</i> (see note 1, <a href="#page_182">p. 182</a>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1148"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1154">183:4</a> Disease (<i>rujānghantrī</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1149"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1155">183:5</a> Literally, one who goes (<i>gantrī</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1150"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1156">183:6</a> <i>Latikā</i>. Dim, of <i>latā</i> creeper to which woman is compared, for she clings to her husband as the creeper to the tree. Hence worship with woman in the Tāntrik <i>Pancatattva</i> is called <i>latāsādhana</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1151"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1157">183:7</a> <i>Cidānanda</i> which, with <i>sat</i> (being), constitutes the nature of the Supreme Being (Parabrahman).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1152"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1158">183:8</a> That is, some worship a particular Devatā to gain a particular result--<i>e.g.</i>, Sarasvatī for learning, Lakṣmī for wealth etc.; but Śankarācārya worships the supreme Aparṇā, whom the Devas worship, who is without qualities, and does so only to give Her honour.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1153"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1159">184:1</a> Full <i>kaivalya mokṣa</i>, liberation above the various <i>pāda</i>, <i>sālokya</i>, etc. (see <a href="htg11.htm#page_59">p. 59</a>, note 1), for <i>muktī</i> is of various kinds.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1154"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1160">184:2</a> Law of religion, duty, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1155"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1161">184:3</a> That is Kubera, Deva of wealth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1156"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1162">184:4</a> A name of Kāma, God of Love (see <a href="htg08.htm#page_40">p. 40</a>, note 1) .</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1157"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1163">184:5</a> <i>Satām</i>. She gives liberation to them.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1158"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1164">184:6</a> The Supreme Being, for it preceded Śakti, as <i>Śruti</i> says, "<i>Sa aikshata</i>," etc. As the Śāradā Tilaka (chap. i.) says: "<i>Saccidānanda vibhavāt sakalat parameshvarāt, āsīchchaktistītonādonādbindusadmudbhavah</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1159"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1165">184:7</a> A bird (cuculus melanolcucus) which is said to live on raindrops.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1180"></a><a href="htg29.htm#fr_1186">188:7</a> Jāhnavī, whence Gangā is called Jāhnavī. When Gangā fell from Heaven, Śiva first held Her in the locks of his hair, until Her anger at being called down by Bhagīratha had abated. She then fell into the Bindu lake, whence issue the seven sacred streams. One branch followed Bhagīratha wherever he went, and on the way flooded the sacrificial flame of the <i>muni</i> Jāhnu. In his anger he drank up its waters. Bhagīratha's work seemed to be fruitless. But after intercession, the muni allowed the waters to flow from him, and as so, issuing from him, the Ganges is called his daughter Jāhnavī.</p>
