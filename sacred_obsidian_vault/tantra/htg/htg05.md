 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a> <a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KĀLABHAIRAVA</h3>  <h3 align="center">(KĀLABHAIRAVĀṢṬAKA)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>I WORSHIP Kālabhairava, <a name="fr_48"></a><a href="#fn_48"><font size="1">1</font></a> Lord of the city of Kāśī, <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_49"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Whose sacred lotus feet are worshipped by the King of Devas, <a name="fr_50"></a><a href="#fn_50"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> The compassionate One,<br> Whose sacrificial thread is made of serpents,<br> On whose forehead shines the moon. <a name="fr_51"></a><a href="#fn_51"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> The naked one, <a name="fr_52"></a><a href="#fn_52"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Whom Nārada <a name="fr_53"></a><a href="#fn_53"><font size="1">6</font></a> and multitudes of other Yogis adore.<br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>. <a name="fr_54"></a><a href="#fn_54"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī,<br> Blazing like a million suns,</p>

 <p>Our great Saviour in our voyage across the ocean of the world. <a name="fr_55"></a><a href="#fn_55"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> The blue-throated, <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">2</font></a> three-eyed <a name="fr_57"></a><a href="#fn_57"><font size="1">3</font></a> grantor of all desires,<br> The lotus-eyed, who is the death of death, <a name="fr_58"></a><a href="#fn_58"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> The imperishable One,<br> Holding the rosary of human bone <a name="fr_59"></a><a href="#fn_59"><font size="1">5</font></a> and the trident. <a name="fr_60"></a><a href="#fn_60"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha Kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of <a href="errata.htm#2">Kāśī</a>,<br> The primeval cause, <a name="fr_61"></a><a href="#fn_61"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Holding in His hands trident, axe, noose, and staff <a name="fr_62"></a><a href="#fn_62"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> --Him of the black body, <a name="fr_63"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> The first of all Deva <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_64"><font size="1">10</font></a>, imperishable, incorruptible,<br> Lord formidable and powerful,</p>

 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 17</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who loves to dance wonderfully. <a name="fr_65"></a><a href="#fn_65"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī,<br> Of great and beautiful body,<br> The giver of both enjoyment and liberation, <a name="fr_66"></a><a href="#fn_66"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Who loves and smiles upon all His devotees,<br> Whose body is the whole world,<br> Whose waist is adorned with little tinkling bells; <a name="fr_67"></a><a href="#fn_67"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Beautiful are they, and made of gold.<br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī,<br> The protector of the bridge of <i>dharma</i>, <a name="fr_68"></a><a href="#fn_68"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Destroyer of the path of <i>adharma</i>, <a name="fr_69"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Liberator form the bonds of karma, <a name="fr_70"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> The all-pervading giver of welfare to all,</p>

 <p><a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 18</font></a></p>  
 <p>Whose golden body is adorned with serpent coils.<br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī<br> Whose feet are beautiful with the lustre of the gems thereon--<br> The stainless, eternal Iṣṭadevatā, <a name="fr_71"></a><a href="#fn_71"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> One without a second, <a name="fr_72"></a><a href="#fn_72"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Destroyer of the pride, and liberator from the gaping jaw of the God of Death. <a name="fr_73"></a><a href="#fn_73"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī, <a name="fr_74"></a><a href="#fn_74"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Whose loud laughter broke the shell of many an egg of the lotus-born; <a name="fr_75"></a><a href="#fn_75"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Strong ruler, at whose glance the net of sin is broken; Giver of the eight powers, <a name="fr_76"></a><a href="#fn_76"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Whose shoulders serpents garland.<br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>

 <p>I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī,<br> The Saviour of all, giver of great fame,<br> The all-pervading One,<br> Who purifies of both sin and virtue the people of Kāśī; <a name="fr_77"></a><a href="#fn_77"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> The ancient Lord of the world,<br> Wise in the wisdom of all moralities. <a name="fr_78"></a><a href="#fn_78"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> <i>Kāśikāpurādhinātha kālabhairavam bhaje</i>.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_48"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_48">15:1</a> Śiva as such.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_49"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_49">15:2</a> Benares. The Kāśipanchakastotra of Śankara says that the pure Ganges is the flow of knowledge and Kāśī is Śiva's mind (<i>Jnānapravāhāvimalādigangā sakāśikāham nijabodharūpah</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_50"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_50">15:3</a> Devarāja or Indra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_51"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_51">15:4</a> Hence Śiva is called Candraśekhara.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_52"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_52">15:5</a> <i>Digambaram</i>, as are the <i>Yogis</i> of whom He is Master. For He is clothed with space itself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_53"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_53">15:6</a> The Ṛṣi of that name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_54"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_54">15:7</a> The refrain is: "I worship Kālabhairava, Lord of the city of Kāśī."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_55"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_55">16:1</a> A constant simile. The world is a storm-tossed ocean not free of danger, even in moments of calm, for therein many dangers, perils, and terrors lie.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_56"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_56">16:2</a> For Śiva swallowed the poison which issued at the churning of the ocean to save the earth from its dangerous presence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_57"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_57">16:3</a> For with the ordinary eyes He bears in the forehead the eye of wisdom.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_58"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_58">16:4</a> Śiva is the conqueror of death ("<i>mrityunjaya</i>"), for he gives that knowledge which frees man of its terrors.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_59"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_59">16:5</a> Even often of the low-caste Candālas and others, for Śiva is the adored and protector of all.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_60"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_60">16:6</a> His peculiar weapon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_61"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_61">16:7</a> For all causes potentially lie in His destructive energies, the manifestation of which is the prelude of re-creation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_62"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_62">16:8</a> <i>Śūla</i>, <i>tangka</i>, <i>pāśa</i>, <i>daṇḍa</i>, His implements.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_63"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_63">16:9</a> As Kālabhairava. Usually he is white and smeared with ashes "shining like a mountain of silver."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_64"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_64">16:10</a> Hence He is called Mahādeva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_65"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_65">17:1</a> <i>Vichitratāṇḍavapriyam</i>. Śiva is often pictured dancing as Natarāja. The place of the dance is the body of the individual and the world spoken of as <i>vanam</i> (the forest), on account of the multitude of its components. He as the inner <i>ātman</i> causes all things to dance into and out of life, and again into it. All life and activity comes through Him, "the unseen Lord of the stage."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_66"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_66">17:2</a> <i>Bhuktimuktidāyakam</i>--that is, He gives both worldly and heavenly enjoyment, and that release from both which is the unending bliss of liberation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_67"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_67">17:3</a> Hung on a girdle.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_68"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_68">17:4</a> Righteousness. For <i>dharma</i>, religion, law, and duty, are the bridge whereby the dangerous waters of the world are passed.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_69"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_69">17:5</a> Unrighteousness.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_70"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_70">17:6</a> The cause and fruit of action whereby man is bound to the phenomenal world until by knowledge, <i>karma</i> is exhausted and destroyed, and liberation (through Śiva, with whose essential being His worshipper becomes one) is attained.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_71"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_71">18:1</a> The desired (or patron) Deity of the devotee.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_72"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_72">18:2</a> For He is the Supreme Unity.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_73"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_73">18:3</a> See ante, <a href="#page_16">p. 16</a>, note 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_74"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_74">18:4</a> Each world (for there are many) is called an egg of Brahmā the creator (<i>brahmāṇḍa</i>). Śiva the great Destroyer by His loud laughter shatters them.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_75"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_75">18:5</a> Brahmā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_76"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_76">18:6</a> <i>Siddhi</i>--namely, <i>aṇimā</i>, <i>mahimā</i>, <i>garīmā</i>, <i>laghimā</i>, <i>prāpti</i>, <i>prākāmya</i>, <i>iṣitva</i>, and <i>vaṣitva</i>. The power to become very small, vast, light, heavy, power of vision and movement, the powers of creation and control over the worlds and their Lords. These <i>siddhi</i> are powers of the all-pervading <i>ātmā</i>, and to greater or less degree may be acquired by Śivayogins according as they realize their unity therewith.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_77"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_77">19:1</a> <i>Kāśivāsiloka punyapāpaśodhakām</i>: for to the liberated there is neither sin nor virtue which are qualities of the phenomenal <i>jivātma</i> only. The liberated are above both.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_78"></a><a href="htg05.htm#fr_78">19:2</a> <i>Nītimargakovidam</i>.</p>
