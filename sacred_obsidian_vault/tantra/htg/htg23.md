 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 150</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">DURGĀ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(MAHĀBHĀRATA VIRĀṬA PARVAN) <a name="fr_909"></a><a href="#fn_903"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>SALUTATION to Thee, O giver of blessings,<br> Dark <a name="fr_910"></a><a href="#fn_904"><font size="1">2</font></a> Virgin, <a name="fr_911"></a><a href="#fn_905"><font size="1">3</font></a> observant of the vow of chastity, <a name="fr_912"></a><a href="#fn_906"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Whose form is beauteous as that of the rising sun,<br> And Thy face as that of the full moon;<br> Four-armed and faced art Thou.<br> Wide-hipped, full-breasted, <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_907"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Wearing emerald sapphire bangles and armlets;<br> Thou art resplendent as Padmā, <a name="fr_914"></a><a href="#fn_908"><font size="1">6</font></a> Spouse of Nārāyaṇa, <a name="fr_915"></a><a href="#fn_909"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> And rangest the ethereal regions.<br> Thy form and chastity <a name="fr_916"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">8</font></a> are of the purest.<br> Dark art Thou like the blue-black cloud,<br> Whose face is beauteous as that of Saṁkarṣaṇa. <a name="fr_917"></a><a href="#fn_911"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Long are Thy two arms, as it were bannered poles in honour of Indra. <a name="fr_918"></a><a href="#fn_912"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p>Thou bearest in Thy six other arms<br> A vessel, lotus, bell, noose, bow, a great discus, <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">1</font></a> and other weapons.<br> Purest woman art Thou on earth. <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thy well-formed ears are decked with beautiful earrings.<br> Thy face challenges the moon in beauty.<br> Wonderful is Thy crown, and beautiful is the braid (of Thy hair).<br> Thy body is like that of a serpent <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Thou glitterest with brilliant girdle round Thy hips,<br> And shinest like Mount Mandara encircled by the snake. <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_916"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> With standing peacock feathers on Thy head, Thou art resplendent.<br> By Thy vow of virginity Thou hast maintained heaven. <a name="fr_923"></a><a href="#fn_917"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> It is for this, O slayer of the Asura Mahiṣa, <a name="fr_924"></a><a href="#fn_918"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> That Thou art praised and worshipped by the Devas for the protection of the three worlds. <a name="fr_925"></a><a href="#fn_919"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Foremost of Devas, be gracious to me;</p>

 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 152</font></a></p>  
 <p>Show me Thy mercy, and be auspicious. <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_920"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Both Jaya and Vijayā <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_921"><font size="1">2</font></a> art Thou.<br> Thou givest victory in battle;<br> Give me, too, victory, O Devī!<br> Give me now a boon.<br> Thy constant abode is on the Vindhya, the fore-most of mountains.<br> O Kālī! O Kālī! O Mahākālī! <a name="fr_928"></a><a href="#fn_922"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Thou delightest in wine, meat, and animal sacrifice, <a name="fr_929"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Bestowing boons, going whithersoever Thou wilt.<br> Thou art ever followed by Brahmā <a name="fr_930"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">5</font></a> and other Devas,<br> By those who call upon Thee to lighten their burdens.<br> As by those who salute Thee at dawn of day.<br> Nothing is unattainable either by way of wealth or children<br> Thou art called Durgā by all because Thou savest men from difficulty. <a name="fr_931"></a><a href="#fn_925"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Whether in dangerous lands or sinking in the great ocean,<br> Thou art the sole refuge of men.<br> When assailed by robbers, when crossing streams and seas,<br> As also in wildernesses and great forests,</p>

 <p><a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 153</font></a></p>  
 <p>Those who remember Thee, O Mahādevī! are never lost.<br> Thou art fame, prosperity, constancy, success, and modesty,<br> Intelligence, knowledge, and man's offspring.<br> Thou art the two twilights, <a name="fr_932"></a><a href="#fn_926"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Night, the light of sun and moon,<br> Sleep, beauty, forgiveness, and mercy.<br> Thou, when worshipped by Thy devotees, destroyest<br> Ignorance, man's fetters, loss of children and wealth, disease and fear of death.<br> I who have lost my kingdom seek Thy protection.<br> I bow to Thee, Sureśvarī, with bended head:<br> Grant me protection,<br> Thou whose eyes are like the leaf of the lotus.<br> O Thou who art truth itself, be true to us.<br> O Durgā! give me shelter,</p>  
 <p>Who art merciful to Thy devotees, protect me. <a name="fr_933"></a><a href="#fn_927"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="fn_922"></a><a href="htg23.htm#fr_928">152:3</a> Spouse of Mahākāla, an aspect of Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_923"></a><a href="htg23.htm#fr_929">152:4</a> <i>Siddhumāmsapa`upriye</i>. <i>Siddhu</i> is a spirit distilled from molasses.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_924"></a><a href="htg23.htm#fr_930">152:5</a> <i>Bhutaih</i>, which Nīlakantha says here denotes the oldest beings, Brahmā and other Devas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_925"></a><a href="htg23.htm#fr_931">152:6</a> Literally, one who rescues from difficulty.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_926"></a><a href="htg23.htm#fr_932">153:1</a> <i>Sandhya</i>, early dawn when the stars are vanishing, and evening as they are about to appear.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_927"></a><a href="htg23.htm#fr_933">153:2</a> Thus praised by the son of Pāndu, the Goddess showed Herself to him.</p>
