 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 74</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">SARASVATĪ <a name="fr_408"></a><a href="#fn_407"><font size="1">1</font></a></h4>  <h4 align="center">(SARASVATĪSTOTRA)</h4>  <h4 align="center">FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <a name="fr_409"></a><a href="#fn_408"><font size="1">2</font></a></h4>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p><i>HRĪM</i>, <i>HRĪM</i> <a name="fr_410"></a><a href="#fn_409"><font size="1">3</font></a> is Thy most pleasing <i>bīja</i>, <a name="fr_411"></a><a href="#fn_410"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> O Thou whose moon-like <a name="fr_412"></a><a href="#fn_411"><font size="1">5</font></a> beauty is heightened</p>

 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a></p>  
 <p>By the lotuses (which surround Thee). <a name="fr_413"></a><a href="#fn_412"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> O auspicious and favourable Devī!<br> Forest fire <a name="fr_414"></a><a href="#fn_413"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the forest of evil thoughts,<br> Whose lotus feet are worshipped by the universe.<br> O lotus seated upon a lotus,<br> Joy dost thou cause to those who salute Thee,<br> Destroyer of Ignorance,<br> Spouse of Hari, <a name="fr_415"></a><a href="#fn_414"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Substance of the world. <a name="fr_416"></a><a href="#fn_415"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p><i>AIM</i>, <i>AIM</i> <a name="fr_417"></a><a href="#fn_416"><font size="1">5</font></a> is Thy favourite <i>mantra</i>,<br> Thou who art both form and formlessness, <a name="fr_418"></a><a href="#fn_417"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Who art the wealth of the lotus face of the lotus-born, <a name="fr_419"></a><a href="#fn_418"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Embodiment of all <i>guṇas</i>, <a name="fr_420"></a><a href="#fn_419"><font size="1">8</font></a> yet devoid of attributes, <a name="fr_421"></a><a href="#fn_420"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Changeless, and neither gross nor subtle. <a name="fr_422"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p>None know Thy nature, nor is Thy inner reality known. <a name="fr_423"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Thou art the whole universe;<br> And Thou it is who existeth within it.<br> Thou art saluted by the foremost of Devas.<br> Without part Thou existeth in Thy fulness everywhere. <a name="fr_424"></a><a href="#fn_423"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Ever <a name="fr_425"></a><a href="#fn_424"><font size="1">3</font></a> pure art Thou.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Greatly art Thou pleased with the recitation <a name="fr_426"></a><a href="#fn_425"><font size="1">4</font></a> of the <i>mantra</i> <i>HRĪM</i>. <a name="fr_427"></a><a href="#fn_426"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Thy crown is white as snow. <a name="fr_428"></a><a href="#fn_427"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Thy hands play with the <i>vīnā</i>. <a name="fr_429"></a><a href="#fn_428"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> O Mother! Mother! salutation to Thee.<br> Burn, burn my sloth and grant me great intelligence. <a name="fr_430"></a><a href="#fn_429"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Thou art Knowledge itself.<br> The Vedānta ever sings of Thee.</p>

 <p><i>Śruti</i> <a name="fr_431"></a><a href="#fn_430"><font size="1">1</font></a> speaks of Thee.<br> O giver of liberation! O way to liberation!<br> Whose power is beyond all understanding.<br> O giver of happiness, <a name="fr_432"></a><a href="#fn_431"><font size="1">2</font></a> adorned with a white necklace, <a name="fr_433"></a><a href="#fn_432"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Grant to me Thy favours.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>Thou art intelligence, intelligence, intelligence, <a name="fr_434"></a><a href="#fn_433"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Thy names are memory, resolution, mind, and hymn of praise. <a name="fr_435"></a><a href="#fn_434"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Eternal and fleeting, <a name="fr_436"></a><a href="#fn_435"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Great cause, saluted by <i>Munis</i>, <a name="fr_437"></a><a href="#fn_436"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> New and old; <a name="fr_438"></a><a href="#fn_437"><font size="1">8</font></a> sacred current of virtue, <a name="fr_439"></a><a href="#fn_438"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Saluted by Hari and Hara. <a name="fr_440"></a><a href="#fn_439"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> Ever pure, beauteous of colour,<br> The subtlest element <a name="fr_441"></a><a href="#fn_440"><font size="1">11</font></a> of things--</p>

 <p>Yea, even the very half thereof. <a name="fr_442"></a><a href="#fn_441"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Thou art the giver of intelligence, intelligence, intelligence.<br> Who art the giver of joy to Mādhava. <a name="fr_443"></a><a href="#fn_442"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>In the form of <i>HRĪM</i>, <i>KṢĪM</i>, <i>DHĪM</i>, <i>HRĪM</i>, <a name="fr_444"></a><a href="#fn_443"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Thou holdest a book,<br> Thou art joyful, of smiling face, and of good fortune.<br> Innocence, current of charm, <a name="fr_445"></a><a href="#fn_444"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> With all powers of arrest. <a name="fr_446"></a><a href="#fn_445"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Burn, burn my sin,<br> And dispel the darkness of my evil thoughts.<br> O praiseworthy of all!<br> Thou art <i>Gīh</i>, <i>Gauh</i>, <i>Vāk</i>, and <i>Bhāratī</i>. <a name="fr_447"></a><a href="#fn_446"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> It is Thou who grantest success to the tongue of the greatest of poets,</p>

 <p>As also in the attainment of all (forms of) knowledge. <a name="fr_448"></a><a href="#fn_447"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>I pray to Thee, I pray to Thee, I bow to Thee,<br> Come to my tongue and never leave me.<br> May my intelligence <a name="fr_449"></a><a href="#fn_448"><font size="1">2</font></a> never go astray,<br> May my sins be taken away,<br> May I be free from sorrow.<br> In time of peril may I never be bewildered.<br> May my mind work freely without impediment <a name="fr_450"></a><a href="#fn_449"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> In <i>Śāstra</i> disputation and verse.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>He who chastely <a name="fr_451"></a><a href="#fn_450"><font size="1">4</font></a> lives, observing silence <a name="fr_452"></a><a href="#fn_451"><font size="1">5</font></a> and religious devotions, <a name="fr_453"></a><a href="#fn_452"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Abstaining from flesh and fish <a name="fr_454"></a><a href="#fn_453"><font size="1">7</font></a> on the thirteenth day of the month, <a name="fr_455"></a><a href="#fn_454"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> And bowed with devotion, early each morning<br> Praises Thee with the most excellent verse.<br> Will, skilful in speech, surpass even Vācaspati. <a name="fr_456"></a><a href="#fn_455"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> The uncleanliness of his sins will be swept away.<br> Such an one gains the fruit of his desires,</p>

 <p>The Devī protects him as though he were Her own child.<br> Poetry flows from his mouth,<br> Prosperity attends his house,<br> And every obstacle to success will disappear.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Whoever reads without interruption the whole of this hymn<br> Twenty-one times on the thirteenth day of the month, <a name="fr_457"></a><a href="#fn_456"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Both on the dark and the light side of the month, <a name="fr_458"></a><a href="#fn_457"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> And meditates on Sarasvatī garmented in white,<br> Adorned with white ornaments,<br> Such an one attains in this world the fruit of his desires.<br> This auspicious hymn has been made by Brahmā himself;<br> Whoever daily reads it with care acquires immortality. <a name="fr_459"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_407"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_408">74:1</a> Devī of speech, eloquence, knowledge, and learning, and Śakti of Brahmā; but formerly, according to the Purāṇic account, the Spouse of Viṣṇu, represented as a fair woman with either four or two arms, and often as seated on a lotus holding a <i>vīnā</i>. The Bhāradvaja Smr. says: "Sarasvatī is She who ever resides in the tongue of all beings and who causes speech." According to the Vasiṣṭha Rāmā, cited in the Lalitā, verse 137, She is called Sarasvatī as the possessor (<i>vati</i>) of the <i>saras</i> (flow of nectar from the <i>brahmarandhra</i>). The Brahmā Purāṇa says the Devī created Sarasvatī from Her tongue, and from Her shoulders the science of love.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_408"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_409">74:2</a> P. 579.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_409"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_410">74:3</a> The <i>bīja</i>, or "seed" mantra of the Devī whose other bīja is the <i>vāgbhava bīja</i> or "<i>aim</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_410"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_411">74:4</a> See last note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_411"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_412">74:5</a> The colour of Sarasvatī is white. Thus She is elsewhere represented as "white, holding the <i>vīnā</i>" (<i>Svetavīnādharā</i>); "adorned with white flowers" (<i>svetābharaṇabhūṣitā</i>), "holding a white rosary" (<i>svetākṣasūtrahastāca</i>), "besmeared with white sandal paste" (<i>svetacandanacarcitā</i>), "clad in white raiment" (<i>svetāmbaradharā</i>), and the like. Here Her whiteness is compared to the moon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_412"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_413">75:1</a> She is seated on and represented as surrounded by lotuses.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_413"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_414">75:2</a> <i>Dāvāgni</i>. She destroys such thoughts.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_414"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_415">75:3</a> Viṣṇu</p>
 <p><a name="fn_415"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_416">75:4</a> <i>Samsārasāre</i>. The <i>samsāra</i> is the illusory world of birth and rebirth, which is said to be <i>asara</i> (unreal, unsubstantial, fleeting). The reality behind this phenomenal illusion is the Devī, who plays, and whose play is world-play.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_416"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_417">75:5</a> The <i>vāgbhavabīja</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_417"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_418">75:6</a> <i>Rūpārūpaprakāśe</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_418"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_419">75:7</a> Brahmā, whose Spouse She is.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_419"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_420">75:8</a> The "qualities," or conditions, which are the substance of <i>Prakṛti</i> or <i>sattva</i>, <i>rajas</i>, and <i>tamas</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_420"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_421">75:9</a> She is <i>nirguṇā</i>, for She and the <i>Parabrahman</i> are in Their essence one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_421"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_422">75:10</a> The forms of the Devī are threefold: <i>parā</i> (supreme), <i>sūkṣmā</i> (subtle), which consists of <i>mantra</i> and <i>sthūla</i> (gross or physical), with hands and feet. But She is neither of these in the sense that the only true form (<i>svarūpa</i>) is above and beyond them both.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_422"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_423">76:1</a> <i>Nāpivijnātatattve</i>; another reading being <i>nāpivijnānatattve</i>. The reference is to Her supreme (para) form, of which the Viṣṇu Yāmala says "none know" (<i>Mātastvatparamam rūpam tannajānāti kashcana</i> (see chap. iii. of the Śāktānandataranginī).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_423"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_424">76:2</a> She as the Brahman is <i>akhaṇḍa</i> "everywhere" and yet in the limited sense "nowhere," in the sense that She is at some particular place and not elsewhere, or partly here and partly there.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_424"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_425">76:3</a> In past, present, and future.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_425"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_426">76:4</a> <i>Japa</i>, which includes that which would not be understood as recitation in the English sense--viz., <i>manasa</i> or mental, and the inaudible <i>japa</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_426"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_427">76:5</a> The bīja of the Devī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_427"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_428">76:6</a> Her colour is white</p>
 <p><a name="fn_428"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_429">76:7</a> A stringed musical instrument.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_429"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_430">76:8</a> <i>Dehibuddhimprashastām</i>; the great prayer to Her.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_430"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_431">77:1</a> Revelation; generally applied to the Vedas in which Sarasvatī is spoken of (see Muir, OṢṬ., verse 339). She is also called Mother of Vedas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_431"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_432">77:2</a> <i>Śāradā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_432"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_433">77:3</a> See note 5, <a href="#page_74">p. 74</a>, <i>ante</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_433"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_434">77:4</a> <i>Dhī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_434"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_435">77:5</a> <i>Dhāranā</i>, <i>dhriti</i> (or constancy), <i>mati</i>. She is the "hymn of praise," for it is composed of words, and She is the Devī thereof, and word and speech itself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_435"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_436">77:6</a> She eternally exists as the reality behind all appearance, and is the cause of the fleeting appearance itself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_436"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_437">77:7</a> Sages.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_437"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_438">77:8</a> See note 6 <i>ante</i>. She ever appears in new forms, and yet Herself in Her aspect as Atmā, persists as the one and same.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_438"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_439">77:9</a> <i>Puṇya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_439"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_440">77:10</a> Viṣṇu and Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_440"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_441">77:11</a> <i>Mātra</i>--the atomic part of things.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_441"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_442">78:1</a> <i>Mātrārdhatattve</i>. She is so very subtle: or alternatively the Mātrārdha below the <i>Sāhasrāra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_442"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_443">78:2</a> The husband of Mā or Lakṣmī--that is, Viṣṇu, whose Spouse She was. She as Prakṛti gives joy to the <i>paramātmā</i>, who is the enjoyer (<i>bhoktā</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_443"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_444">78:3</a> The mantra, as currently recited is <i>Om Om Kshīm, Kshīm, svarupe</i>. "<i>Kshīm</i>," for She is the destructress of sin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_444"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_445">78:4</a> <i>Mohemugdhapravāhe</i>. <i>Mugdhā</i> is generally used in connection with <i>nāyikā</i>--that is, a simple, artless innocent maiden, as yet unacquainted with love. The general and correct reading is <i>mugdhemohapravāhe</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_445"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_446">78:5</a> <i>Stambhanam</i>; one of the six "magical" powers known as the <i>ṣatkarma</i>, whereby a person may be paralyzed in action or speech. So a disputant might seek the power of <i>stambhanam</i> to close the mouth of, or confuse his adversary.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_446"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_447">78:6</a> Various words which all mean "word" or "speech."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_447"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_448">79:1</a> Literally, the giver of <i>siddhi</i> (success) in all knowledge of which She is the presiding Devī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_448"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_449">79:2</a> Both <i>buddhi</i> and <i>manas</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_449"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_450">79:3</a> <i>Prasaratu</i> ("flow freely").</p>
 <p><a name="fn_450"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_451">79:4</a> He who is <i>brahmachārin</i>. Here commences the <i>phala</i> portion of the <i>stotra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_451"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_452">79:5</a> <i>Maunin</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_452"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_453">79:6</a> <i>Vratin</i>. The <i>vrata</i> are voluntary religious practices and devotions as distinguished from the obligatory daily ritual.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_453"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_454">79:7</a> <i>Nirāmishah</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_454"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_455">79:8</a> The <i>trayodaśi</i>; the <i>Sarasvatī vrata</i> day.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_455"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_456">79:9</a> Or Brihaspati, the Lord of Speech.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_456"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_457">80:1</a> <i>Trayodaśi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_457"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_458">80:2</a> The month is divided into two halves (<i>pakṣa</i>), according as the moon is waxing or waning.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_458"></a><a href="htg14.htm#fr_459">80:3</a> <i>Amṛtatvam</i>--that is, liberation (<i>mukti</i>).</p>
