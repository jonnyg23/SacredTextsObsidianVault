 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TĀRĀ <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(TĀRĀSṬAKAM) <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE NĪLA TANTRA</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O MOTHER, Devī Nīlasarasvatī <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">3</font></a> Tārā,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>Refuge with Thee I crave.<br> Giver of prosperity and wealth art Thou<br> To those who worship Thee. Standing on Śiva,<br> Thy right foot upon His breast and left upon His thigh.<br> Ever art Thou, with smiling lotus-like face.<br> Thy three eyes are, as it were, full-blown lotuses.<br> In Thy hands Thou holdest a knife, <a name="fr_324"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">1</font></a> a skull, a lotus, and a sword.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Thou art the presiding Devī of speech.<br> Thou art the creeper which grants all desires. <a name="fr_325"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thou art the giver of all <i>siddhi</i>, <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> And the power to write both verse and prose.<br> Three are Thine eyes, as it were blue lotuses.<br> Ocean of kindness and compassion art Thou.<br> I pray Thee of Thy mercy shower upon me the nectar of prosperity.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Sharbhā, <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">4</font></a> I pray Thee remove my fears.<br> Proud Lady, brilliant are Thy garments,<br> Bright with coiling serpents.<br> Thou art clad in tiger skin.<br> Thy waist is adorned with tiny tinkling bells.<br> Thou holdest the heads of two demons<br> Dripping with blood, just severed by the sword.<br> Thy waist is girdled with heads of demons,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>As it were with a garland.<br> Thus art Thou beautiful, O formidable One. <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>O Devī Tārā, attained with difficulty,<br> I take refuge with Thee.<br> Thou art beautiful with form both amorous and charmful. <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_329"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thou art <i>Bindu</i> and the half-moon, <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_330"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Whose substance is <i>Hrīm</i> and <i>Phat</i>. <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Thou art <i>mantra</i> <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">5</font></a> and the shelter of all.<br> Thy forms are threefold<br> Gross, Subtle, and Supreme.<br> Thou art beyond the reach of Veda. <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>By the service of Thy lotus feet,<br> Men of good deed attain <i>sāyujya</i> <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">1</font></a> liberation.<br> O Parameśvarī, Thou art the Spouse of Him <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Who is Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and the three-eyed One.<br> O Mother! he who neglects to serve Thy lotus feet,<br> But serves instead the Devas, Indra, and others,<br> Who are themselves plunged in the ocean of samsāra, <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Is indeed and most truly ignorant.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>O Mother! those Devas who receive on their crowns<br> The pollen which comes from Thy lotus-like feet, <a name="fr_337"></a><a href="#fn_337"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a></p>  
 <p>Are able to keep their promise of conquest,<br> And to gain victory over their enemies in battle--<br> Such, without a doubt, are sheltered in Thy lap.<br> But their enemies who send forth the defiant challenge,<br> "I am a Deva, and none is equal to me in the whole world,"<br> Perish and meet such death as befits them.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Bhūta, Preta, Piśācha, Rākṣasa,<br> Daitya, foremost of Dānava, Yakṣa, Lords of Naga, <a name="fr_338"></a><a href="#fn_338"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Wrathful Dākinī, <a name="fr_339"></a><a href="#fn_339"><font size="1">2</font></a> great birds, <a name="fr_340"></a><a href="#fn_340"><font size="1">3</font></a> tigers, and other dreadful creatures<br> Forthwith take flight at but the remembrance of Thy name,<br> And are powerless to do aught of evil.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Who serves Thy lotus feet, to him <i>siddhi</i> <a name="fr_341"></a><a href="#fn_341"><font size="1">4</font></a> is given.<br> He surpasses the Lord of speech, <a name="fr_342"></a><a href="#fn_342"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> And obtains the beauty of Kāma. <a name="fr_343"></a><a href="#fn_343"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> He can charm and paralyze <a name="fr_344"></a><a href="#fn_344"><font size="1">7</font></a> multitudes of elephants upon the field of battle,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 61</font></a></p>  
 <p>And has power to stay the flow of water. <a name="fr_345"></a><a href="#fn_345"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> The Siddha <a name="fr_346"></a><a href="#fn_346"><font size="1">2</font></a> and prosperity are under his control.</p>  <h3 align="center">PHALAŚLOKA. <a name="fr_347"></a><a href="#fn_347"><font size="1">3</font></a></h3>  
 <p>Whoever, being pure and self-controlled,<br> Reads this eight-versed hymn to Tārā,<br> At morn, at noon, at evening,<br> To him is given<br> The power to write beautifully in prose or verse, <a name="fr_348"></a><a href="#fn_348"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Knowledge in all <i>Śāstra</i>,<br> Imperishable fortune,<br> The enjoyment of whatsoever he may desire,<br> Fame, beauty, and wealth,<br> The love of all men,<br> And at the end liberation.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_321"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_321">56:1</a> The Matsyasūkta, Tārārṇava, and Nīla Tantras deal with particulars or Tārā or Tāriṇī, one of the Mahāvidyā, whose <i>bīja</i> is <i>Hrīm Strīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i> (<i>Kurccha</i>), <i>Phat</i> (see verse 4). She is called Nīlasarasvatī, because She playfully gives the power of speech. She is called Tārā on account of her being deliverer or saviour (<i>tārakatvāt</i>). She gives both pleasure (<i>sukha</i>) and liberation (<i>mokṣa</i>). She is called also Ugratārā, because She saves from formidable and horrible calamities. Ṛṣi Vaśiṣṭha is said to have cursed this <i>vidyā</i>, and then raised the curse so that <i>siddhi</i> might be gained from Her by <i>japa</i> of the <i>bīja Hrīm</i>, <i>Strīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>, <i>Phat</i>, after which She again became glorious. Her Mantra is also given as <i>Śrīm</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Strīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>, <i>Phat</i> (giver of wealth and beauty); another is <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Strīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>, <i>Phat</i> (giver of all desires); another is <i>Aim</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Strīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>, <i>Phat</i> (giver of speech), and <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Strīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>, <i>Phat</i> (giver of liberation). Her Yantra is an eight-petalled lotus surrounded by a circle, with inverted triangle in the centre with <i>Hūm</i>. On the petals are <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Strīm</i>, <i>Śrīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>. There is also a Tantra (<i>Tārāṣatkoṇa</i>) of two superimposed triangles, making a star.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_322"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_322">56:2</a> From the Nīla Tantra (see also Tantrasāra, p. 610, R. M. Chatterjee's edition, and the Brihatstotraratnākara, p. 283, where a more correct text is given).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_323"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_323">56:3</a> The blue Sarasvatī, Devī of speech (see note 1, <i>ante</i>). Nīla Tantra says She is in the form of all language (<i>sarvabhāṣā ayī</i>). The Nīlasarasvatī Yantra is figured at p. 93 of the Dashamahāvidyā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_324"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_324">57:1</a> <i>Karttri</i> (for <i>dhyāna</i> see p. 94 Daśamahāvidyā).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_325"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_325">57:2</a> As did the celestial <i>Kalpa</i> tree in Indra's heaven.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_326"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_326">57:3</a> Material success, psychical powers, spiritual attainment.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_327"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_327">57:4</a> Spouse of Śiva (Sharbha).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_328"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_328">58:1</a> Her <i>dhyāna</i> is given as follows: The Devī is in the midst of four blazing funeral pyres; Her feet as described in verse 1; formidable, with garland of severed heads; short of stature; big-bellied; tiger skin round the waist; youthful; four-armed; protruding tongue; giving <i>vara</i>; holding the articles mentioned in verse 1 (the skull and lotus in left hands); a <i>ṛṣi</i> Akshobhya in the form of a serpent on her head; her body lustrous as that of the moonbeams; formidable teeth; smiling face; three eyes blazing like the morning sun.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_329"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_329">58:2</a> <i>Māyānanga vikārarūpalalane</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_330"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_330">58:3</a> <i>Ardhacandrātmike</i>--that is, the crescent sign below the bindu in <i>candra bindu</i>. She is both bindu and Nāda (see Introduction). It is also said that there are eight <i>varṇa</i> above the <i>bindu</i> of <i>Hrīm</i>, commencing with <i>ardhacandra</i>, and ending with <i>unmani</i>, of which the third is Nāda. Here, as the Mahāsvachchanda Tantra states, the Devī should be contemplated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_331"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_331">58:4</a> Two tantrik <i>bīja mantras</i>: as to <i>Hūm</i> (see , note 1, <i>ante</i>). <i>Phat</i> is the <i>astra</i> or weapon <i>mantra</i> (see note 1, <a href="htg09.htm#page_50">p. 50</a>, <i>ante</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_332"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_332">58:5</a> <i>Mantrātmike</i> (see <a href="htg03.htm#page_2">p. 2</a>, <i>ante</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_333"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_333">58:6</a> <i>Vedanāngnahigocharā</i>, as to her three forms (<i>vide ante</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_334"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_334">59:1</a> Literally, "becoming one with the Deity." Identification of the self and the Deity with attributes; one of the four forms of qualified liberation--<i>Sālokya</i>, <i>Sārūpya</i>, <i>Sāmīpya</i>, <i>Sāyujya</i>. Those who know the Brahman and such worship to be imperfect reject them, and attain the unconditioned bliss (<i>kaivalya</i>) which transcends all other states. But these others must be passed before the end is reached, which the Śaktirahasya summarizes by a short verse: "A mortal who worships by ceremonies, by images, by mind, by identification, by knowing the self attains <i>kaivalya</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_335"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_335">59:2</a> That is Parameśvara, in whom the threefold energies which manifest in the <i>trimūrti</i> are contained <i>tasyastri parameśvari trinayanabrahmādi samnyātmanah</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_336"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_336">59:3</a> The Deva, in this like man, is a <i>samsārin</i> or inhabitant <a href="errata.htm#6">of</a> the <i>samsāra</i>, which comprises earth, the <i>antarikṣa</i>, and heaven (<i>svarga</i>), the abode of the Deva. The latter has wife and children, is engaged in conflict with demons, and so forth. When the merit which gains the Deva heaven is exhausted, he descends to earth again.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_337"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_337">59:4</a> That is, the Deva bow their heads at the feet of the Devī, receiving on their heads the dust of Her feet.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_338"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_338">60:1</a> Various spirits and inferior divinities of more or less evil and malignant character.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_339"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_339">60:2</a> A form of Śakti attendant on the Devī in Her terrible forms.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_340"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_340">60:3</a> <i>Khacara</i> (vultures, eagles, etc.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_341"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_341">60:4</a> Vide <i>ante</i>, <a href="#page_57">p. 57</a>, note 3,</p>
 <p><a name="fn_342"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_342">60:5</a> Brihaspati.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_343"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_343">60:6</a> Deva of Love.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_344"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_344">60:7</a> He has the powers of <i>mohanam</i> and <i>stambhanam</i>, the latter being one of the <i>Tāntrika Ṣatkarma</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_345"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_345">61:1</a> A particular form of <i>Stambhanam</i> called <i>jalastambha</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_346"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_346">61:2</a> Devayoni of that name inhabiting the <i>antarikṣa</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_347"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_347">61:3</a> Fruit or result of the stotra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_348"></a><a href="htg11.htm#fr_348">61:4</a> <i>Labhate devyām kavitvām</i>.</p>
