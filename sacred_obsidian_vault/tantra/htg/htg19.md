 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">AMBIKĀ <a name="fr_653"></a><a href="#fn_651"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(ELEVENTH MĀHĀTMYA OF CAṆḌĪ) <a name="fr_654"></a><a href="#fn_652"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>DEVĪ, Thou who removeth the pain of Thy suppliants, <a name="fr_655"></a><a href="#fn_653"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Be gracious, Be gracious, O Mother of the world!<br> Be gracious, O Queen of the universe!<br> Protect the universe.<br> Thou art, O Devī! the Iśvarī of all moving and unmoving things. <a name="fr_656"></a><a href="#fn_654"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Thou art the only support of the world,<br> Because Thou wert in the form of earth.<br> By Thee who existed in the form of water<br> Is the whole universe pervaded.<br> Thou art She whose powers are unsurpassed.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Thou art the Vaiṣṇavī Śakti <a name="fr_657"></a><a href="#fn_655"><font size="1">5</font></a> of eternal power;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thou art the seed of the universe,<br> And the supreme Māyā.<br> All this universe has been bewitched by Thee.<br> Thou, when pleased, art the cause of salvation to men.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>All sciences are parts of Thee,<br> As also all women without exception <a name="fr_658"></a><a href="#fn_656"><font size="1">1</font></a> throughout the world. <a name="fr_659"></a><a href="#fn_657"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> By Thee alone, O Mother! is the universe filled.<br> How can we praise Thee?<br> Art thou not beyond all Praise of highest speech?</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>When, <a name="fr_660"></a><a href="#fn_658"><font size="1">3</font></a> O Devī! <a name="fr_661"></a><a href="#fn_659"><font size="1">4</font></a> being in the form of the universe,<br> And bestowing heaven <a name="fr_662"></a><a href="#fn_660"><font size="1">5</font></a> and liberation, <a name="fr_663"></a><a href="#fn_661"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Thou art worshipped,<br> What words, howsoever sublime, suffice for Thy praise?</p>

 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>In the form of moments, minutes, and other fractions of time,<br> Thou art the cause of (worldly) change.<br> At the time of the dissolution of the universe<br> Thou art all-powerful. <a name="fr_666"></a><a href="#fn_664"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Nārāyaṇī all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>O Auspicious One! auspicious with all auspiciousness,<br> Accomplisher of all successful things,<br> Giver of refuge, Three-eyed one; <a name="fr_667"></a><a href="#fn_665"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> O Gaurī! <a name="fr_668"></a><a href="#fn_666"><font size="1">5</font></a> O Nārāyaṇi! all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>O Eternal One! who art the energy <a name="fr_669"></a><a href="#fn_667"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Of creation, maintenance, and destruction;</p>

 <p>Who art the abode of the qualities, <a name="fr_670"></a><a href="#fn_668"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> And are yet beyond them-- <a name="fr_671"></a><a href="#fn_669"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> O Nārāyaṇi! all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>O Thou who ever savest those in poverty and pain,<br> Who take shelter with Thee!<br> O Remover of the pains of all!<br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Rider in an aerial car yoked with swans, <a name="fr_672"></a><a href="#fn_670"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Who assumed the form of Brāhmanī, <a name="fr_673"></a><a href="#fn_671"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Who sprinklest water in which <i>kuśa</i> grass <a name="fr_674"></a><a href="#fn_672"><font size="1">5</font></a> is steeped <a name="fr_675"></a><a href="#fn_673"><font size="1">6</font></a>--<br> Nārāyaṇī, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>Who holdeth trident, moon, and serpent, <a name="fr_676"></a><a href="#fn_674"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Riding on a great bull <a name="fr_677"></a><a href="#fn_675"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> In the form of Maheśvarī <a name="fr_678"></a><a href="#fn_676"><font size="1">9</font></a>--<br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>

 <p>Who art attended by fowl and peacock. <a name="fr_679"></a><a href="#fn_677"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> O faultless One!<br> Who holdeth a great <i>śakti</i>-weapon, <a name="fr_680"></a><a href="#fn_678"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> And existeth in the form of Kaumārī, <a name="fr_681"></a><a href="#fn_679"><font size="1">3</font></a>--<br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">14</p>  
 <p>Who holdeth Thy great implements,<br> Which are the conch, discus, mace, and bow;<br> Who art in the form of Vaiṣṇavī, <a name="fr_682"></a><a href="#fn_680"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Be gracious,<br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">15</p>  
 <p>Who holdeth the formidable discus,<br> And hast uplifted the earth with Thy tusks <a name="fr_683"></a><a href="#fn_681"><font size="1">5</font></a>--<br> O auspicious One! in the form of a boar <a name="fr_684"></a><a href="#fn_682"><font size="1">6</font></a>--<br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">16</p>  
 <p>O Thou who in the fierce man-lion form <a name="fr_685"></a><a href="#fn_683"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Didst put forth effort to slay the Daityas,</p>

 <p align="center">17</p>  
 <p>Who weareth a diadem and beareth a great thunder-bolt,<br> Who dazzles with Thy thousand eyes, <a name="fr_686"></a><a href="#fn_684"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Destructress of the life of Vritra, <a name="fr_687"></a><a href="#fn_685"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Who art Aindrī, <a name="fr_688"></a><a href="#fn_686"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">18</p>  
 <p>Who art in the form of Śivadūtī, <a name="fr_689"></a><a href="#fn_687"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Destructress of the great host of the Daityas,<br> Of terrible form and loud and terrible voice--<br> Nārāyaṇi, all reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">19</p>  
 <p>Whose visage is formidable with its teeth,<br> Adorned with a garland of severed heads--<br> O Cāmuṇḍā! <a name="fr_690"></a><a href="#fn_688"><font size="1">5</font></a> is destructress of Muṇḍa <a name="fr_691"></a><a href="#fn_689"><font size="1">6</font></a>--<br> Nārāyaṇī, all reverence to Thee.</p>

 <p align="center">21</p>  
 <p>Understanding, <a name="fr_695"></a><a href="#fn_693"><font size="1">4</font></a> Sarasvatī, the Best of all.<br> All Powers, <a name="fr_696"></a><a href="#fn_694"><font size="1">5</font></a> Spouse of Babhru, <a name="fr_697"></a><a href="#fn_695"><font size="1">6</font></a> Dark One, <a name="fr_698"></a><a href="#fn_696"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Primeval Śakti. <a name="fr_699"></a><a href="#fn_697"><font size="1">8</font></a> Be gracious, O Lady!<br> Nārāyāṇī, all reverence to Thee.</p>

 <p>Controller of all; who hast all power;<br> From the cause of all fear protect us, O Devī!<br> O Devī Durgā! reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">23</p>  
 <p>Beautiful is Thy face adorned with three eyes.<br> Guard us from all (formidable) beings.<br> O Kātyāyani! <a name="fr_700"></a><a href="#fn_698"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">24</p>  
 <p>May Thy trident most formidable with flame,<br> Slayer of countless Asuras,<br> Protect us from fear,<br> O Bhadrakāli! <a name="fr_701"></a><a href="#fn_699"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Reverence to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">25</p>  
 <p>May Thy bell which destroys the power of Daityas,<br> Filling the world with its sound,<br> Guard us from sin,<br> As a mother <a name="fr_702"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">3</font></a> protects her children!</p>  
 <p align="center">26</p>  
 <p>May Thy sword glittering in Thy hands,<br> Besmeared with the blood and fat of Asuras as with mire,<br> Be for our welfare!<br> O Caṇḍikā; to Thee we bow.</p>

 <p>Thou, when gratified, dost destroy all forms of disease;<br> But if displeased, Thou dost destroy all longed-for desires.<br> Such as take shelter with Thee need fear no danger,<br> Since they become verily a refuge to themselves. <a name="fr_703"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>

 <p>O Mother, who hast shown Thyself in many forms,<br> Who else than Thee is able to achieve<br> That destruction of the great Asuras,<br> Enemies of righteousness, <a name="fr_704"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Which Thou hast wrought to-day.</p>

 <p>In the sciences, <a name="fr_705"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">3</font></a> in all scriptures, <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">4</font></a> and in the great sayings <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_705"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Which are the lamp of knowledge, <a name="fr_708"></a><a href="#fn_706"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Who else is there but Thee<br> Who makes this universe again and again <a name="fr_709"></a><a href="#fn_707"><font size="1">7</font></a> revolve</p>

 <p align="center">32</p>  
 <p>O Devī! be gracious;<br> Ever protect us from the fear of enemies<br> As Thou hast just now saved us by the slaughter of the Asuras.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_651"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_653">106:1</a> Mother.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_652"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_654">106:2</a> When the great Lord of the Asuras was slain by the Devī, Indra and other Devas (Agni at their head), with shining faces, offered praise to Kātyāyanī, because of the fulfilment of their desire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_653"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_655">106:3</a> Literally, "Those who come to take shelter with Her."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_654"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_656">106:4</a> That is, the organic and inorganic world.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_655"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_657">106:5</a> The energy of Viṣṇu, the sustaining power of the Universe.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_656"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_658">107:1</a> <i>Sakalā</i>. Nagoji Bhatta is not happy in his Commentary when he says that sakalā here means "endowed with the sixty-four arts" (kalā), such as dancing, music, painting, literature, acting, etc., and who are devoted to their husbands, modest, etc. The Devī is not, according to this noble line, in these only but in all women, however ignorant of the "arts" or low born they may be.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_657"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_659">107:2</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Vidyāh samastāstavadevi bhedāh.<br> Striyah samastāh sakalā jagatsu</i>.</dir>  
 <p>The Devibhāṣyam of Panchānana Tarkaratna translates the verse as, "All sciences, all things (<i>bhedāh</i>), and all women are of Thee."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_658"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_660">107:3</a> The verse here changes from <i>upendra vajrā</i> to <i>anustup</i> metre.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_659"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_661">107:4</a> That is, She who is, as Nagoji says, of a shining nature (<i>dyotana`sīlā</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_660"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_662">107:5</a> <i>Svarga</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_661"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_663">107:6</a> <i>Mukti</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_662"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_664">108:1</a> That is, nischayātmakam jnānam.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_663"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_665">108:2</a> For she is the support of all beings; <i>fem</i>. of Nārāyana, a name of Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_664"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_666">108:3</a> For She is in the form of time.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_665"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_667">108:4</a> As is Her Spouse Śiva with his third eye of wisdom.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_666"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_668">108:5</a> Either as Nagoji says "white Devī," or the Devī of that name, who issued from the body of Mahādevī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_667"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_669">108:6</a> The commentator says "that She is the possessor of it" (<i>śakti</i>); but there is in reality no difference between <i>śakti</i> and the possessor of <i>śakti</i>, though human understanding and speech may make such difference.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_668"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_670">109:1</a> The <i>guṇas</i>--the three <i>sattva</i>, <i>rajas</i>, and <i>tamas</i>, and their derivatives, the <i>Tattwas</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_669"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_671">109:2</a> Nagoji says that <i>gunāśraye gunamaye</i> = <i>gunāśraye agunamaye</i>. Though the <i>gunas</i> inhere in Her, She is not as is the <i>jīva</i>, affected by them.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_670"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_672">109:3</a> See next note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_671"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_673">109:4</a> Śakti, or energy of Brahmā whose vehicle (<i>vāhana</i>) is a swan (<i>hamsa</i>), or flamingo, as it is variously rendered.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_672"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_674">109:5</a> Grass used in <i>pitṛ kṛyā</i> and <i>agni kṛyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_673"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_675">109:6</a> As Brahmā does with the holy water (<i>śāntijalam</i>) from his pot called <i>kamaṇḍalu</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_674"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_676">109:7</a> Associated with Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_675"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_677">109:8</a> The <i>vāhana</i> of Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_676"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_678">109:9</a> Śakti, or energy of Maheśvara or Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_677"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_679">110:1</a> Both the cock and peacock are said in the Mahābhārata to be the <i>vāhana</i> of Kārtikeya. Gopal Chakravarti renders it, however, as "the best of peacocks."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_678"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_680">110:2</a> A kind of missile, dart spear, lance, or pike.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_679"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_681">110:3</a> The Śakti of Kumāra, or Kārtikeya, son of Śiva and Pārvatī and Commander of the celestial hosts.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_680"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_682">110:4</a> The Śakti of Viṣṇu, who holds the conch, discus (cakra), etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_681"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_683">110:5</a> See next note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_682"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_684">110:6</a> Viṣṇu, in His boar-incarnation, uplifted on His tusks the world which had been submerged in the waters.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_683"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_685">110:7</a> As Śakti of Viṣṇu in the <i>narasimha</i> incarnation, in which He slew the Daitya Hiranyakaśipu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_684"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_686">111:1</a> The Devī is here invoked as Aindri, the śakti of Indra, who is crowned, and whose weapon, like that of Jupiter, is the thunderbolt and who has a thousand eyes.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_685"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_687">111:2</a> An Asura slain by Indra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_686"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_688">111:3</a> See note 1, <i>ante</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_687"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_689">111:4</a> The Devī is known as Śivadūtī, because Śiva was engaged by Her as messenger to Śumbha and Niśumbha.</p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>yatoniyukto dautyena tayā devyā śivah svayam<br> śivadūtīti lokesmingstatah sā khyutim āgatā</i>. </dir>  
 <p>Caṇḍī, eighth Māhātmya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_688"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_690">111:5</a> Devī is so called because She slew the Asuras Canda and Muṇḍa (see verse 25, chap. vii. Candī).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_689"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_691">111:6</a> <i>Muṇḍamathane</i>; not as one translation of the Caṇḍī has it, "who grindest shaven heads."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_690"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_692">112:1</a> That is, as Nagoji says, the knowledge pertaining to the <i>Ātman</i> (<i>adhyātmavidyā</i>) contained in the Upaniṣads; not "wide knowledge," as last mentioned translator renders it.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_691"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_693">112:2</a> <i>Mantra</i> of <i>Pitṛs</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_692"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_694">112:3</a> For Devī is both <i>vidyā</i> (knowledge) and <i>avidyā</i> (nescience), or <i>Prakṛti</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_693"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_695">112:4</a> <i>Medhā</i>, which Nagoji says = <i>dhāranāvati buddhi</i>, or firm, steady, concentrated <i>buddhi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_694"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_696">112:5</a> <i>Bhūti</i>, which ordinarily means wealth = here, according to Gopal Chakravarti <i>aiśvaryarūpinī</i>--that is, the eighth <i>siddhi</i>; or, according to Nagoji, it is <i>sattvapradhāna</i> ("greatly excelling in <i>sattva guṇa</i>").</p>
 <p><a name="fn_695"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_697">112:6</a> A name of Śiva, Viṣṇu, or Fire. According to Nagoji the <i>rajoguṇa śakti</i> is here indicated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_696"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_698">112:7</a> <i>Tāmasī</i>--that is, <i>tamogunayuktā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_697"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_699">112:8</a> <i>Niyate</i>, which ordinarily means fate (<i>adrisṭa</i>); but here denotes, according to Nagoji, the <i>Mūlaśakti</i>, the root or primeval Śakti. It does not mean, an stated in the last-mentioned translation, "O self-controlled Queen!". In the case of the Devī there is no self to be controlled. She controls others, not Herself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_698"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_700">113:1</a> According to Gopal, the Devī is so called because She was born in the hermitage of the <i>Muni</i> Kātyāyana, but the Vedantists say that <i>Kātya</i> is he who is devoted to the Brahman (<i>brahmaniṣṭa</i>), and She who is attained by them is Kātyāyanī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_699"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_701">113:2</a> Auspicious Kālī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_700"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_702">113:3</a> <i>Anah</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_701"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_703">114:1</a> As Gopal says, even Rajahs, not to mention others, become the slaves of such an one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_702"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_704">114:2</a> <i>Dharma</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_703"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_705">114:3</a> There are fourteen kinds of <i>vidyā</i>--viz., four Veda, six Anga, Mimāmsa, Nyāya, Dharmaśāstra, Purāṇā. Gopal says <i>vidyā</i> and <i>upavidyā</i>, such as Indrajāla, Gārudakadyāh, Dhanurvidyā, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_704"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_706">114:4</a> <i>Śāstra</i>--that is, <i>tarka</i> (logic), <i>nīti</i>. etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_705"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_707">114:5</a> Literally; the "first sayings"--that is Veda or the Karmakāṇḍa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_706"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_708">114:6</a> <i>Viveka</i> = <i>jnāna</i> (Gopal).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_707"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_709">114:7</a> <i>Atīva</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_708"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_710">115:1</a> The reference is to the <i>samsāra</i>. It is a "pit," for men fall into it; and it is "dark," for it obstructs knowledge.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_709"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_711">115:2</a> <i>Mamatvam</i>, which Gopal defines as <i>asvakīye svakiyatvābhimūnah</i>--the sense of ownness in respect of a thing not one's own--<i>e.g.</i>, to take the body to be the self; to think I am white, I am tall, etc</p>
 <p><a name="fn_710"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_712">115:3</a> Demonic beings.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_711"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_713">115:4</a> That is, the submarine fire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_712"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_714">115:5</a> Because She pervades all things.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_713"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_715">115:6</a> Indra, Brahmā, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_714"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_716">115:7</a> Gopāla Chakravarti renders it: "Those who are devoted to Thee are themselves worshipped--even by Indra, Brahmā, etc., the Lords of the Universe--therefore Thou art the supporter of the universe."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_715"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_717">116:1</a> Unusual phenomena, such as earthquakes, comets, hurricanes, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_716"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_718">116:2</a> <i>Bhuh</i>, <i>bhuvah</i>, <i>svah</i> (see <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_717"></a><a href="htg19.htm#fr_719">116:3</a> Then Devī said: "Now I bestow a boon, O Devas" (Caṇḍī).</p>
