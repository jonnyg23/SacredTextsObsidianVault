 <p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 201</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MAṆIKARṆIKĀ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(MAṆIKARṆIKĀSTOTRA)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>It was on Thy bank, O Maṇikarṇikā! <a name="fr_1246"></a><a href="#fn_1239"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> That Hari and Hara, givers of <i>sāyujya mukti</i>, <a name="fr_1247"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Disputed together at the departure festival <a name="fr_1248"></a><a href="#fn_1241"><font size="1">3</font></a> of a certain one.<br> Hari <a name="fr_1249"></a><a href="#fn_1242"><font size="1">4</font></a> said, "Let Him be like unto me;"<br> Whereon forthwith from within the body<br> Came forth Śiva mounted on Garuda, <a name="fr_1250"></a><a href="#fn_1243"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 202</font></a></p>  
 <p>In yellow garment, <a name="fr_1251"></a><a href="#fn_1244"><font size="1">1</font></a> with the mark of Bhrigu's foot on His breast. <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1245"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Indra and the Thirty, <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1246"><font size="1">3</font></a> at the close of their period of enjoyment, <a name="fr_1254"></a><a href="#fn_1247"><font size="1">4</font></a>'<br> Descend to earth again, <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1248"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> And are reborn as men, or even as beast, bird, or worm;<br> But those, O Mother Maṇikarṇikā! who plunge into thy waters,<br> Are freed from sins, and indeed in <i>Sāyujya</i> <a name="fr_1256"></a><a href="#fn_1249"><font size="1">6</font></a> man becomes<br> Nārāyaṇa  <a name="fr_1257"></a><a href="#fn_1250"><font size="1">7</font></a> himself, with crown and <i>Kaustubha gem</i>. <a name="fr_1258"></a><a href="#fn_1251"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

 </p>

 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Kāśi <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1252"><font size="1">1</font></a> is of all cities the most praiseworthy,<br> For it is the city of <i>vimukti</i> <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1253"><font size="1">2</font></a> adorned with Gangā.<br> There Maṇikarṇikā is the giver of happiness,<br> And <i>Mukti</i> itself is Her servant. <a name="fr_1261"></a><a href="#fn_1254"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> When Brahmā weighed Heaven with its Devas against Kāśi,<br> Kāśi, as the heavier, remained on earth,<br> But Heaven, the lighter, rose to the skies.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>Nought is better than any part of the banks of Gangā,<br> But there, where Kāśi is, is the best,<br> And Maṇikarṇikā, where Īśvara gives <i>mukti</i>, is the best of all.<br> This place, inaccessible even to Devas,<br> Destroys a mass of sins.<br> Through many virtues acquired in previous births<br> Alone may it be attained, and by the pure only.</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>The multitude of being is immersed in the ocean of pain,<br> How may they gain release?<br> It was with this knowledge that Brahmā constructed the city of Vārānaśī, <a name="fr_1262"></a><a href="#fn_1255"><font size="1">4</font></a> which gives all bliss.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 204</font></a></p>  
 <p>Men seek the happiness of Heaven.<br> But in so doing they but show small desire,<br> Since from Heaven they must fall again to earth<br> At the close of their appointed time of happiness. <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1256"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> But Kāśī is the city of liberation, <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1257"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Ever beneficent, giving <i>dharma</i>, <i>artha</i>, <i>kāma</i>, and <i>mokṣa</i>. <a name="fr_1265"></a><a href="#fn_1258"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>He who holds the bamboo flute, <a name="fr_1266"></a><a href="#fn_1259"><font size="1">4</font></a> upholder of the mountain, <a name="fr_1267"></a><a href="#fn_1260"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Who bears on his breast the <i>Śrīvatsa</i> <a name="fr_1268"></a><a href="#fn_1261"><font size="1">6</font></a> mark,<br> And Śiva, with venom in His throat, <a name="fr_1269"></a><a href="#fn_1262"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Who bears Gangā upon his head, <a name="fr_1270"></a><a href="#fn_1263"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> And the husband of Lakṣmī, <a name="fr_1271"></a><a href="#fn_1264"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Are one and the same. <a name="fr_1272"></a><a href="#fn_1265"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> Many of such, O Mother Maṇikarṇikā!<br> As bathe in Thy waters become Rudras and Haris. <a name="fr_1273"></a><a href="#fn_1266"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a></p>  
 <p>How, then, can there be any difference between them? <a name="fr_1274"></a><a href="#fn_1267"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Death upon Thy Banks, which is the giver of happiness,<br> Is praised even by the Devas.<br> On him who thus dies Śakra <a name="fr_1275"></a><a href="#fn_1268"><font size="1">2</font></a> ever looks with His thousand eyes.<br> Sāvitri <a name="fr_1276"></a><a href="#fn_1269"><font size="1">3</font></a> of a thousand rays welcomes Him as He ascends (to the heavens).<br> Such a pure one, mounted on a Bull or on Garuda, <a name="fr_1277"></a><a href="#fn_1270"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> May go to whatsoever abode he will.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Even the four-headed Deva, <a name="fr_1278"></a><a href="#fn_1271"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> The <i>Guru</i> who initiates into the meaning of the Veda,<br> Is unable even in an hundred of his <a name="fr_1279"></a><a href="#fn_1272"><font size="1">6</font></a> years<br> To describe the purity which arises upon bathing at midday in Maṇikarṇikā.<br> But the Deva who bears the moon upon his forehead, <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1273"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> By the power of his <i>yoga</i> knows Thy purity.<br> Śiva makes that man who dies on Thy Bank<br> Either Himself or Nārāyaṇa.</p>

 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>  
 <p>All such sin-destroying fruit as is earned by millions of troublesome horse-sacrifices <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1274"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Exists in the purity which comes from bathing in Maṇikarṇikā.<br> He, who having bathed therein,<br> Reads this hymn, goes to the abode of the light of Brahman,<br> Having crossed the great ocean of this world<br> As if it were but some little pool.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1239"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1246">201:1</a> Is the name of a celebrated <i>ghat</i> at Benares, where the bodies of the dead are burnt, and at which the gem of the ear ornament of the Devī fell. The Kāśipanchakastotra of Śankara says that where there is <i>nivṛtti</i> of <i>manas</i>, there is the great peace. That peace is the foremost of <i>tīrthas</i> (here rivers) and Maṇikarṇikā (<i>Manonivṛtti paramopaśāntih sā tīrthavaryā maṇikarṇikāca</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1240"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1247">201:2</a> One of the forms of qualified <i>mukti</i> (liberation); the four muktis are <i>Sālokya</i> (remaining in the same region with the Deva), <i>Sāmīpya</i> (remaining near the Deva), <i>Sārupya</i> (receiving the same form as the Deva), and <i>Sayujya</i> (becoming one with the Deva).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1241"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1248">201:3</a> <i>Prayāṇotsava</i>--that is, death.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1242"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1249">201:4</a> Viṣṇu</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1243"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1250">201:5</a> The Bird King, who is the vehicle (<i>vāhana</i>) of Viṣṇu, son of Kaśyapa by his wife Vinetā, elder brother of Aruṇa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1244"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1251">202:1</a> The colour of Viṣṇu's robes. The verse is intended to show the unity of both Viṣṇu and Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1245"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1252">202:2</a> The Ṛṣis once disputed amongst themselves as to the relative merits of Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Śiva, and the <i>Ṛṣī</i> Bhrigu was sent to test them. The first and last on being purposely slighted by Bhrigu showed the weakness of resentment. Finding Viṣṇu lying down with Lakṣmī in the daytime he upbraided him for this and planted with force his foot on his chest. Viṣṇu not only took all this in good part, but rubbing the foot of Bhrigu expressed the hope that the latter had not hurt it by his action. On this exhibition of divine quality the palm was adjudged to Viṣṇu, who thereafter bore the mark of Bhrigu's foot on his breast.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1246"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1253">202:3</a> <i>Tridaśah</i>, a collective name for the other Devas. The thirty-three (three being understood) <i>gaṇas</i> of Devas, of which the Devī is Tridaśeśvarī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1247"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1254">202:4</a> In heaven (<i>Svarga</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1248"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1255">202:5</a> For the enjoyment in <i>Svarga</i>, which is part of the worlds of birth and rebirth, is not eternal, but on fruition the <i>jivātmā</i> again descends to earth to work out its unexhausted <i>karma</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1249"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1256">202:6</a> <i>Sāyujyepi</i>. As to <i>sāyujya</i>, see <i>ante</i>, <a href="#page_201">p. 201</a>, note 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1250"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1257">202:7</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1251"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1258">202:8</a> A great and brilliant gem worn by Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1252"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1259">203:1</a> Benares.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1253"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1260">203:2</a> <i>Nirvāṇāmokṣa</i>, the highest form of <i>Mukti</i> (liberation). As the saying goes: "Ayodhyā, Mathurā, Gayā, Kāśi, Kānci, Avantikā, Purī, these seven <i>tīrthas</i> (places of pilgrimage) give <i>mukti</i>, but Kāśī (Benares) gives <i>nirvāṇa mukti</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1254"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1261">203:3</a> As the servant awaits the orders of his mistress, so <i>mukti</i> (liberation) awaits the command of Maṇikarṇikā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1255"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1262">203:4</a> Benares.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1256"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1263">204:1</a> See <a href="#page_202">p. 202</a>, note 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1257"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1264">204:2</a> <i>Mukti</i> (see <a href="#page_203">p. 203</a>, note 2).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1258"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1265">204:3</a> Piety, wealth, fulfilment of desire, and liberation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1259"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1266">204:4</a> Kṛṣṇa, who is often so figured.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1260"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1267">204:5</a> Mount Govardhana, which Kṛṣṇa, by his might, upheld.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1261"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1268">204:6</a> Curls of hair on the breast of Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1262"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1269">204:7</a> At the churning of the ocean, poison issued which, to save the world, Śiva swallowed. It coloured His throat blue; hence he is called Nīlakantha.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1263"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1270">204:8</a> The River Ganges (see <a href="htg29.htm#page_188">p. 188</a>, note 7).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1264"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1271">204:9</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1265"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1272">204:10</a> They with Brahmā; for as the Rudrayāmala says "Though three they are one" (<i>Ekam murtistrayo deva</i>). All the Devas and Devīs are but manifestations, with the apparent limitations incident thereto, of the Supreme Unity--the Brahman.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1266"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1273">204:11</a> Śiva and Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1267"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1274">205:1</a> That is, they cease to differ from one another, having become Hari and Hara, who are themselves one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1268"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1275">205:2</a> Indra, king of the celestials.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1269"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1276">205:3</a> The sun (Sūrya).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1270"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1277">205:4</a> The first is the vehicle (<i>Vāhana</i>) of Śiva. The second, the carrier of Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1271"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1278">205:5</a> Brahmā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1272"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1279">205:6</a> Not human years.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1273"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1280">205:7</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1274"></a><a href="htg32.htm#fr_1281">206:1</a> The Vaidika Aśvamedha.</p>
