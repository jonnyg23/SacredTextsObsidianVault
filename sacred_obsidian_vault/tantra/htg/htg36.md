 <p><a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GANGĀ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(GANGĀSTOTRA) BY VĀLMĪKI <a name="fr_1379"></a><a href="#fn_1372"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O MOTHER GANGĀ! co-wife <a name="fr_1380"></a><a href="#fn_1373"><font size="1">2</font></a> with the daughter of Himalaya,<br> Thou art the necklace on the dress of the Earth, <a name="fr_1381"></a><a href="#fn_1374"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> And the banner staff whereby one ascends to Heaven.<br> O Bhāgīrathī! <a name="fr_1382"></a><a href="#fn_1375"><font size="1">4</font></a> I pray to Thee.<br> May my body perish after it has lived on Thy banks,<br> After it has drunk Thy pure water And swung on Thy waves.<br> And has remembered Thy name and cast looks on Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>O Mother Gangā! O deliverer from Hell!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_225"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 225</font></a></p>  
 <p>Even a bird living in the hollow of a tree growing on Thy bank,<br> Even a fish or a tortoise living in Thy waters<br> Are greater than a King worshipped by his enemies' wives,<br> Made afraid by the sound of the bells on the necks of his maddened elephants. <a name="fr_1383"></a><a href="#fn_1376"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Not even a bull or a bird or a horse,<br> Nor a serpent nor an elephant,<br> Suffer the pains of rebirth and redeath<br> If they live at Kāśī <a name="fr_1384"></a><a href="#fn_1377"><font size="1">2</font></a> on Thy holy banks.<br> Better off are they than even a Raja living elsewhere,<br> Fanned though he be with the <i>couris</i> <a name="fr_1385"></a><a href="#fn_1378"><font size="1">3</font></a> of courtezans, <a name="fr_1386"></a><a href="#fn_1379"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Whose ever moving golden wristlets sweetly tinkle.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>O our Supreme Lady Bhāgīrathī! <a name="fr_1387"></a><a href="#fn_1380"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> O wanderer in the three regions! <a name="fr_1388"></a><a href="#fn_1381"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> When shall it be that I shall be fanned<br> By the hands of heavenly women <a name="fr_1389"></a><a href="#fn_1382"><font size="1">7</font></a> with their beautiful <i>couris</i>?<br> When, too, shall I be happy enough to see my body<br> Pecked by crows, devoured by dogs, drawn along the earth by jackals.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 226</font></a></p>  
 <p>Carried by Thy currents, tossed upon Thy banks,<br> And borne by Thy waters to and fro!</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>May the daughter of Jahnu <a name="fr_1390"></a><a href="#fn_1383"><font size="1">1</font></a> be ever victorious and protect us,<br> She who is like the fresh fibrous stem of the lotus-like feet of Viṣṇu, <a name="fr_1391"></a><a href="#fn_1384"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Like a garland of jasmine  <a name="fr_1392"></a><a href="#fn_1385"><font size="1">3</font></a> flowers on the head of Śiva,<br> Like the banner of victory of Lakṣmī presiding over liberation,<br> She <a name="fr_1393"></a><a href="#fn_1386"><font size="1">4</font></a> who cleanses us of the stain of sin arising from the Kaliyuga. <a name="fr_1394"></a><a href="#fn_1387"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>May Thy sacred water be pure for my daily bathing,<br> Thy water covered with leaves of palm and <i>tamāla</i>, <a name="fr_1395"></a><a href="#fn_1388"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Of <i>Sāla</i> <a name="fr_1396"></a><a href="#fn_1389"><font size="1">7</font></a> and pine, with all their creepers<br> On which play no rays of the Sun. <a name="fr_1397"></a><a href="#fn_1389"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> White and brilliant, like the conch, the Moon, and the water-lily,<br> Stirred by the rising breasts of the wives of the Gandharvas,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_227"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 227</font></a></p>  
 <p>Devas, Siddhas, and Kinnaras, <a name="fr_1398"></a><a href="#fn_1390"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> What time they bathe therein.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>May the water of Gangā, who ever charms, sanctify us;<br> She who has fallen from the feet of the enemy of Mura, <a name="fr_1399"></a><a href="#fn_1391"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Who wanders upon the head of the enemy of Tripurā, <a name="fr_1400"></a><a href="#fn_1392"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> The Destructress of sins.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>May the auspicious water of Gangā ever purify us;<br> The Destructress of sins, the great enemy of sins,<br> Adorned with waves, wandering in the mountains,<br> Piercing through the caverns of the Lord of mountains <a name="fr_1401"></a><a href="#fn_1393"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> With roaring sounds.<br> Stealer of the dust from the feet of Lord Hari. <a name="fr_1402"></a><a href="#fn_1394"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>Whosoever at early dawn,<br> Having cleansed his body<br> And purified his mind</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_228"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 228</font></a></p>  
 <p>Of all uncleanliness arising from the sinful Kaliyuga, <a name="fr_1403"></a><a href="#fn_1395"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Reads this hymn to Gangā composed of eight verses,<br> Shall never fall into the ocean of the world again,<br> But shall attain liberation.</p>
