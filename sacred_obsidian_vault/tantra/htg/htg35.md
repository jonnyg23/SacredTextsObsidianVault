 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 217</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ANNAPŪRṆĀ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(ANNAPŪRṆĀSTOTRA)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O MOTHER ANNAPŪRṆĀ! <a name="fr_1341"></a><a href="#fn_1334"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Īśvari, <a name="fr_1342"></a><a href="#fn_1335"><font size="1">2</font></a> who ever bestoweth happiness,<br> Granting gifts and dispelling fear.<br> O mine of gems of beauty,<br> Who washeth away all sin,<br> Who giveth purity to Thy devotees, <br> Who purifieth the mountain range, <a name="fr_1343"></a><a href="#fn_1336"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Which is undestroyed even at the time of dissolution, <a name="fr_1344"></a><a href="#fn_1337"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Presiding Deity of Kāśī, <a name="fr_1345"></a><a href="#fn_1338"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Maheśvarī <a name="fr_1346"></a><a href="#fn_1339"><font size="1">6</font></a> in every truth,<br> O vessel of mercy! grant me aid.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>O Thou who hast clothed Thyself in cloth of gold,</p>

 <p>Decked with ornaments made of many and varied gems,<br> Whose breasts rounded like a water jar,<br> Are resplendent with their necklace of pearls,<br> Whose beauty is enhanced by the fragrance of the Kashmir aloe.<br> O Devī! who presidest over the city of Kāśī, <a name="fr_1347"></a><a href="#fn_1340"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> O Mother Annapūrṇā Iśvarī, <a name="fr_1348"></a><a href="#fn_1341"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> O vessel of mercy, grant me aid!</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O giver of the bliss of <i>Yoga</i>, <a name="fr_1349"></a><a href="#fn_1342"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Destructress of enemies, <a name="fr_1350"></a><a href="#fn_1343"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Inspirer of devotion to <i>dharma</i> and <i>artha</i>, <a name="fr_1351"></a><a href="#fn_1344"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Who art lustrous as the light waves of sun, moon, and fire,<br> Protectress of the three worlds <a name="fr_1352"></a><a href="#fn_1345"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Giver of all dominion <a name="fr_1353"></a><a href="#fn_1346"><font size="1">7</font></a> and all desires,<br> Presiding Devī over the city of Kāśī,<br> O vessel of mercy, grant me aid!</p>

 <p>Thou who maketh Thy dwelling in the cave of Mount Kailāsa, <a name="fr_1354"></a><a href="#fn_1347"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Who art Gaurī, <a name="fr_1355"></a><a href="#fn_1348"><font size="1">2</font></a> Umā, <a name="fr_1356"></a><a href="#fn_1349"><font size="1">3</font></a> and Śankarī, <a name="fr_1357"></a><a href="#fn_1350"><font size="1">4</font></a> Kaumārī, <a name="fr_1358"></a><a href="#fn_1351"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Who giveth us power to understand the meaning of <i>Nigama</i>, <a name="fr_1359"></a><a href="#fn_1352"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>Thou supporteth all beings visible and invisible,<br> Whose belly is the vessel which contains the universe. <a name="fr_1363"></a><a href="#fn_1356"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Thou discloseth the subject of the drama of Thy own play,<br> And art the fount of the light of wisdom,<br> Pleasing the mind of the Lord of the universe,<br> Presiding Devī over the City of Kāśī,<br> O vessel of mercy, grant me aid!</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>Īśvarī of all men on earth,<br> The waves of Thy blue-black hair look (beautiful) like plaits.<br> Īśvarī who ever giveth food,<br> Bestower of happiness to all, who advanceth all people,</p>

 <p>Presiding Devī over the City of Kāśī,<br> O vessel of mercy, grant me aid!</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Thou givest all instruction onwards from the time of initiation, <a name="fr_1364"></a><a href="#fn_1357"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> And art the cause of the threefold manifestation of Śambhu. <a name="fr_1365"></a><a href="#fn_1358"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Scented with the Kashmir aloe, Thou art the Īśvarī of the three regions. <a name="fr_1366"></a><a href="#fn_1359"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Thou art triple waved, <a name="fr_1367"></a><a href="#fn_1360"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> And the night of dissolution.<br> Thou art the cause of all <a href="errata.htm#20">lasting</a> things,<br> And fulfiller of the desires of those who desire.<br> It is Thou who maketh the greatness of peoples.<br> Presiding Devī over the City of Kāśī,<br> O vessel of mercy, grant me aid!</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Thou, Devī, art adorned with all various kinds of gems,<br> Daughter of Dakṣa, <a name="fr_1368"></a><a href="#fn_1361"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>Thou art She who shinest with the brilliance of millions of suns, moons, and fires.<br> Whose earrings are brilliant as the sun, moon, and fire,<br> Who art the cause of the colour of both sun and moon,<br> Who holdeth a rosary, <a name="fr_1370"></a><a href="#fn_1363"><font size="1">2</font></a> a book, <a name="fr_1371"></a><a href="#fn_1364"><font size="1">3</font></a> a noose, and a goad. <a name="fr_1372"></a><a href="#fn_1365"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Presiding Devī over the City of Benares,<br> O vessel of mercy, grant me aid!</p>

 <p>Protectress of Kṣatriyas, <a name="fr_1373"></a><a href="#fn_1366"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Great dispeller of all fear,<br> Mother, who art an ocean of mercy,<br> In very truth the ever auspicious giver of salvation,<br> The cause of the beauty of Viśveśvara, <a name="fr_1374"></a><a href="#fn_1367"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>O Annapūrṇā! who art ever full (of bounty),<br> Who art dear to the life of Śankara, <a name="fr_1376"></a><a href="#fn_1369"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> O Pārvatī, give me aid!</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1334"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1341">217:1</a> The name of the Devī, the "bountiful Lady" who gives food and presides over Kāśī, the Holy City of Benares.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1335"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1342">217:2</a> Feminine of Īśvara or Lord.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1336"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1343">217:3</a> The Himalaya purified by the presence of the Devī, who there incarnated as Pārvatī, daughter of Himavat, the Mountain-King.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1337"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1344">217:4</a> <i>Pralaya</i>, the destruction of the world.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1338"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1345">217:5</a> Benares (see note 1.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1339"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1346">217:6</a> Great Īśvarī (see note 2).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1340"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1347">218:1</a> Benares.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1341"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1348">218:2</a> Feminine of Īśvara or Lord.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1342"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1349">218:3</a> Union of the human (<i>jivātmā</i>) with the supreme (<i>paramātmā</i>) soul effected through the practice of <i>Yoga</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1343"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1350">218:4</a> That is, sin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1344"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1351">218:5</a> Two of the fourfold aims (<i>Caturvarga</i>) of sentient being--viz., <i>dharma</i> (religion, duty, etc.), <i>artha</i> (wealth, wherewith life is sustained and religious sacrifices are effected), <i>Kāma</i> (desire which prompts great achievements and fulfilment), and <i>mokṣa</i> or liberation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1345"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1352">218:6</a> That is, <i>Bhuh</i>, <i>Bhuvah</i>, <i>Svah</i>, the terrestrial atmospheric and the heavenly spheres.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1346"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1353">218:7</a> <i>Aiśvarya</i> (lordship).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1347"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1354">219:1</a> The sacred mount and paradise of Śiva; esoterically the Sahasrāra whereto as Kuṇḍalinī She repairs.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1348"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1355">219:2</a> The daughter of Guru, the King of mountains (see <a href="htg29.htm#page_187">p. 187</a>, note 4). Gaurī also means "fair".</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1349"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1356">219:3</a> A name of the Devī. When of the age of sixteen she practised great austerities that She might be the Spouse of Śiva, upon which Her mother, endeavouring to persuade Her, said, U ("Oh"), Mā ("not"). As it is said by Kālidāsa in the first Canto of the Kumārasambhavam; "<i>Umeti mātrā tapaso niṣiddhā paschā dumākhyāng sumukhijagāma</i>." ("By the words U, Mā, She was thus forbidden by Her mother to practise austerity, thereafter the pure Umā obtained Her name.") Umā is Kumārī, who has renounced all attachment, and is devoted to Her Lord. A <i>sūtra</i> runs, "I<i>cchāśaktih Umā kumārī</i>" (The energy of will is Umā the unmarried). The Commentary on this <i>sūtra</i>, cited by Bhāskararāya, says: "The eternal state is his whose mind has ascended the degrees of <i>yoga</i> called <i>vismaya</i>, and who realizes the supreme Bhairavata (an aspect of Śiva). That <i>Yogi</i> obtains at length the <i>Icchāśakti</i> called the Supreme Queen (<i>Parābhattārika</i>) known also as Kumārī."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1350"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1357">219:4</a> Name of the Devī as Spouse of Śankara, the benefactor.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1351"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1358">219:5</a> Name of the Devī as one of the <i>aṣtanāyikā</i> and Spouse of Deva Kārtikeya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1352"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1359">219:6</a> This term, applied to the Veda generally, means particularly the Tantra in the form in which the Devī is <i>guru</i> and Śiva, <i>śiṣya</i>. As it is said:</p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Nirgato girijā vaktrāt,<br> Gatāscha girija śrutim,<br> Matascha vāsudevasya,<br> Nigamā parikathyate</i>.</dir>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> In the Lalitā the Devī is addressed as <i>nijājnarūpā nigamā</i> (the nigama are the expressions of Thy commands).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1353"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1360">220:1</a> The Tāntrik ("seed") <i>mantras</i>, such as <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Hūm</i>, <i>Klīm</i>, etc. Mantras are classified according to the syllables they contain.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1354"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1361">220:2</a> The Mahāmantra <i>Om</i>, composed of A + u + m, coalesced by <i>sandhi</i> into Om. The three <i>varṇas</i> signify the three members of the <i>Trimurti</i>, Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Śiva, who, as the Rudrayāmala says, are born of the <i>Praṇava</i> (Om), and though in appearance three, are yet one (<i>ekamūrtistrayo devāh</i>). From the <i>Praṇava</i> all Devas, Vedas, sun, moon, and all being comes by the power of Devī, the supreme Śakti.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1355"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1362">220:3</a> <i>Mokṣā</i>, or unity with the supreme, and therefore liberation from rebirth in the phenomenal world.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1356"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1363">220:4</a> <i>Brahmāṇḍabhāṇḍodari</i>. The <i>Brahimāṇḍa</i> (universe) is the "mundane egg" of Brahmā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1357"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1364">221:1</a> <i>Dīkṣā</i>, through which each Hindu passes, by reception of his <i>mantra</i>. It is said sometimes that initiation is the third birth, the first being that from the mother, the second is <i>upanayana</i> (investiture with sacred thread), and the third is initiation. The Tantras speak of thirty-two <i>Dīkṣās</i>, from <i>Sudhavidyā</i> to <i>Anuttara</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1358"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1365">221:2</a> That is, as creator, maintainer, and destroyer.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1359"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1366">221:3</a> Bhuh, Bhuvah, Svah (see <a href="htg23.htm#page_151">p. 151</a>, note 7)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1360"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1367">221:4</a> For She flows in the form of the three sacred rivers: Gangā, Yamunā, and Sarasvatī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1361"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1368">221:5</a> The father of Satī, a manifestation of Devī, who, dying at the <i>Dakṣayajna</i> reappeared as Pārvatī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1362"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1369">222:1</a> Because Ganeśa and Kārtikeya, Her children, suck Her right breast.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1363"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1370">222:2</a> <i>Mālā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1364"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1371">222:3</a> <i>Pustaka</i>, which is also known as the <i>Vidyāmudrā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1365"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1372">222:4</a> See <a href="htg27.htm#page_173">p. 173</a>, note 6.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1366"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1373">222:5</a> The warrior caste.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1367"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1374">222:6</a> Lord of the universe.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1368"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1375">223:1</a> When his <i>yajna</i> was destroyed by Her husband Śiva. There are two Dakṣas--Prajāpati, and a human king, an incarnation of the former. Though Śiva destroyed the sacrifice, Devī was the instrument. The Lalitā, verse 120, addresses the Devī both as <i>Dākṣāyanī</i> (daughter of Dakṣa) and <i>Dakṣayajna vināsinī</i> (destroyer of the sacrifice of Dakṣa).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1369"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1376">223:2</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1370"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1377">223:3</a> <i>Ibid</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1371"></a><a href="htg35.htm#fr_1378">223:4</a> See <a href="htg23.htm#page_151">p. 151</a>, note 7.</p>
