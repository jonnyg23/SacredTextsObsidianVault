 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GANGĀ <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1275"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(GANGĀSTOTRA)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O Devī Sureśvarī! <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1276"><font size="1">2</font></a> O Bhagavatī Gangā!<br> Saviour of the three worlds of restless waves,<br> Clear is Thy water circling upon the head of Śiva <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1277"><font size="1">3</font></a>,<br> May my mind ever repose at Thy lotus feet.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Mother Bhāgīrathī! <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1278"><font size="1">4</font></a> giver of happiness,<br> Renowned in Nigama <a name="fr_1286"></a><a href="#fn_1279"><font size="1">5</font></a> is the greatness of Thy water;<br> Thy greatness is more than I can know,<br> Protect me, O merciful one, ignorant that I am.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Gangā! sprung from the feet of Viṣṇu, <a name="fr_1287"></a><a href="#fn_1280"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Whose waves are white as snow as moon and pearl,<br> Remove from me my weight of sin;<br> Help me to cross the ocean of the world.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>They say that him, O Gangā! who is devoted to Thee<br> Yama <a name="fr_1288"></a><a href="#fn_1281"><font size="1">2</font></a> can never behold.<br> He who has drunk of Thy clear water<br> Attains of a surety the supreme Abode.</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>O Jāhnavī! O Gangā! deliverer of the fallen, <a name="fr_1289"></a><a href="#fn_1282"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Whose waves are beautiful,<br> Claving the foremost of mountains, <a name="fr_1290"></a><a href="#fn_1283"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Mother of Bhīṣma, <a name="fr_1291"></a><a href="#fn_1284"><font size="1">5</font></a> daughter of the foremost of <i>munis</i>. <a name="fr_1292"></a><a href="#fn_1285"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Protectress of the fallen; praised in the three worlds. <a name="fr_1293"></a><a href="#fn_1286"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>O Gangā! who goeth to the ocean,<br> Ever free of sadness is he who salutes Thee.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_209"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 209</font></a></p>  
 <p>Giver of fruit like unto the <i>kalpa</i> tree, <a name="fr_1294"></a><a href="#fn_1287"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> By thy favour the woman who looked coldly<br> Now casts her loving glances. <a name="fr_1295"></a><a href="#fn_1288"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>He who bathes in Thy current, O Mother!<br> Is never again reborn in woman's womb<br> O Protectress from hell! O Jāhnavi! O Gangā!<br> O Destructress of sins! lofty art Thou by Thy greatness.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>O Thou who art eternal! O wave of purity!<br> May Thou, bestower of bliss, refuge of Thy worshippers!<br> From whose eyes come glances of compassion.<br> Whose feet the lustre of gems on Indra's crown adorn,<br> Be ever victorious!</p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>O Bhāgīrathī! <a name="fr_1296"></a><a href="#fn_1289"><font size="1">3</font></a> dispel my illness, melancholy, and pain,<br> As also my sins and all my many follies;<br> Essence of the three regions, necklace (on the breast) of Earth, <a name="fr_1297"></a><a href="#fn_1290"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Of a surety Thou art my refuge in the world.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>O Alakanandā! <a name="fr_1298"></a><a href="#fn_1291"><font size="1">1</font></a> O supreme Bliss! <a name="fr_1299"></a><a href="#fn_1292"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> O worshipful by those who despair!<br> Be Thou merciful.<br> He whose abode is by Thy Banks<br> Of a verity dwells in Vaikuṇṭha. <a name="fr_1300"></a><a href="#fn_1293"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Better were it to be a fish or tortoise in thy waters,<br> Or a feeble lizard upon Thy banks, or a poor dog-eater <a name="fr_1301"></a><a href="#fn_1294"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Within two <i>kos</i> <a name="fr_1302"></a><a href="#fn_1295"><font size="1">5</font></a> of Thy stream,<br> Than to be a noble king and yet far away from Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>O Bhuvaneśvarī! <a name="fr_1303"></a><a href="#fn_1296"><font size="1">6</font></a> pure one, praised of all,<br> Devī in liquid form, <a name="fr_1304"></a><a href="#fn_1297"><font size="1">7</font></a> daughter of the foremost of <i>Munis</i>, <a name="fr_1305"></a><a href="#fn_1298"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> He who daily reads this hymn to Gangā<br> Is of a surety ever victorious.</p>  
 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>They who with devotion in their heart to Gangā (Recite) this hymn<br> Composed in the sweet, pleasant, charming <i>pajjhatika</i> metre,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a></p>  
 <p>Which gives the highest happiness,<br> Gain the eternal bliss of liberation.</p>  
 <p>14</p>  
 <p>A worldly <a name="fr_1306"></a><a href="#fn_1299"><font size="1">1</font></a> man shall read <a name="fr_1307"></a><a href="#fn_1300"><font size="1">2</font></a> this hymn to Gangā<br> Which <a name="fr_1308"></a><a href="#fn_1301"><font size="1">3</font></a> is the essence of the world, the giver of desired fruit,<br> The essence of all pure things enjoined. <a name="fr_1309"></a><a href="#fn_1302"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Composed by Śankara, <a name="fr_1310"></a><a href="#fn_1303"><font size="1">5</font></a> the worshipper of Śankara. <a name="fr_1311"></a><a href="#fn_1304"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> This hymn is ended.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1278"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1285">207:4</a> So called because called down from Heaven by Bhagīratha of the solar race.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1279"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1286">207:5</a> Tantra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1280"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1287">208:1</a> Gangā was born at the feet of Viṣṇu. So it is said in the mantra used when bathing in the Ganges: "<i>Viṣṇupādābja sambhūte Gange bhuvanatārini dharma dravīti</i>" (the Ganges is <i>dharma</i> in liquid form) "<i>vikhyāte pāpam me hara Jāhnavi</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1281"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1288">208:2</a> Deva of Death.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1282"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1289">208:3</a> Into sin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1283"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1290">208:4</a> The Himālaya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1284"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1291">208:5</a> Son of Santanu by Gangā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1285"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1292">208:6</a> See <a href="htg29.htm#page_188">p. 188</a>, note 7.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1287"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1294">209:1</a> The tree in the paradise of Indra which granted all desires.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1288"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1295">209:2</a> <i>Vimukhavanitākritataralāpānge</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1289"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1296">209:3</a> So called after Bhagīratha, who called her down to earth (see <a href="htg29.htm#page_188">p. 188</a>, note 7).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1290"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1297">209:4</a> <i>Vasudhāhārā</i>--that is, as a necklace adorns a woman, so the Devī by the flowing lines of Her stream, adorns the Earth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1291"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1298">210:1</a> A river flowing from the Himalaya into the Ganges.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1292"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1299">210:2</a> <i>Paramānandā</i>, as is the Supreme, whose manifestation She is.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1297"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1304">210:7</a> For the Ganges is the manifestation of the Supreme in the form of the sacred river.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1298"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1305">210:8</a> Jahnu (see <a href="htg29.htm#page_188">p. 188</a>, note 7).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1299"></a><a href="htg33.htm#fr_1306">211:1</a> <i>Viṣaya</i>, which also in a had sense means a <a href="errata.htm#18">sensualist or</a> materialist.</p>
