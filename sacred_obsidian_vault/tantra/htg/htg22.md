 <p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 135</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">JAGADAMBIKĀ <a name="fr_816"></a><a href="#fn_811"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE DEVĪBHĀGAVATA PURĀṆA <a name="fr_817"></a><a href="#fn_812"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>IT is by Thy power only<br> That Brahmā creates, Viṣṇu maintains,<br> And at the end of things Śiva destroys the universe.<br> Powerless are they for this but by Thy help.<br> Therefore it is that Thou alone art the Creatrix,<br> Maintainer, and Destructress of the world. <a name="fr_818"></a><a href="#fn_813"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Thou art fame, mind, remembrance,<br> And our refuge, the mountain-born, <a name="fr_819"></a><a href="#fn_814"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Companion, kindness, faith, and patience,<br> Earth, Kamalā, <a name="fr_820"></a><a href="#fn_815"><font size="1">5</font></a> health, <a name="fr_821"></a><a href="#fn_816"><font size="1">6</font></a> the arts, and victory,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a></p>  
 <p>Contentment, ever victorious, <a name="fr_822"></a><a href="#fn_817"><font size="1">1</font></a> Umā, <a name="fr_823"></a><a href="#fn_818"><font size="1">2</font></a> Ramā, <a name="fr_824"></a><a href="#fn_819"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> True knowledge, and the highest <i>buddhi</i>. <a name="fr_825"></a><a href="#fn_820"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Science, forgiveness, beauty, retentiveness art Thou, <a name="fr_826"></a><a href="#fn_821"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Who art Known in the three worlds as all in all.<br> Who is there that unaided by Thee can do ought?<br> Thou art the abode wherein all men dwell. <a name="fr_827"></a><a href="#fn_822"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>Thou art the upholder.<br> Were Thou not so, how could the tortoise and serpent uphold the Earth? <a name="fr_828"></a><a href="#fn_823"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Thou art the Earth itself.<br> Were this not so, how could this weighty world rest on Ether? <a name="fr_829"></a><a href="#fn_824"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>Those who through Thy <i>māyā</i> pray to Devas,<br> Such as <a name="fr_830"></a><a href="#fn_825"><font size="1">9</font></a> the four-headed One <a name="fr_831"></a><a href="#fn_826"><font size="1">10</font></a>, Viṣṇu, Rudra, <a name="fr_832"></a><a href="#fn_827"><font size="1">11</font></a> Fire,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 137</font></a></p>  
 <p>The White-rayed one <a name="fr_833"></a><a href="#fn_828"><font size="1">1</font></a> Yama, <a name="fr_834"></a><a href="#fn_829"><font size="1">2</font></a> Vāyu, <a name="fr_835"></a><a href="#fn_830"><font size="1">3</font></a> and Gaṇeśa <a name="fr_836"></a><a href="#fn_831"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Are indeed ignorant, <a name="fr_837"></a><a href="#fn_832"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> For can these do anything without Thy power? <a name="fr_838"></a><a href="#fn_833"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>O Mother! those who do <i>homa</i>,  <a name="fr_839"></a><a href="#fn_834"><font size="1">7</font></a> with ghee on fire,<br> With great ceremony in the name of the Devas,<br> Are of but small intelligence. <a name="fr_840"></a><a href="#fn_835"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> If Thou art not <i>svāhā</i>, <a name="fr_841"></a><a href="#fn_836"><font size="1">9</font></a> then how can they make sacrifice?<br> Do they not worship Thee?<br> If not they are ignorant. <a name="fr_842"></a><a href="#fn_837"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>In this world Thou art the giver of enjoyment<br> To things which move and are still. <a name="fr_843"></a><a href="#fn_838"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> Thou givest life to all things being as they are parts of Thee.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_138"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 138</font></a></p>  
 <p>O Mother! as Thou nourisheth all Thy Suras, <a name="fr_844"></a><a href="#fn_839"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> So also dost Thou nourish others.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>O Mother! as men who are of good heart,<br> Never for the mere pleasure thereof<br> Cut down leafless and bitter (fruited) trees<br> Which have sprung up in the forest.<br> Therefore Thou dost even greatly protect the Daityas. <a name="fr_845"></a><a href="#fn_840"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>Though Thou slayest in the battlefield with Thy arrows the enemies,<br> Knowing their desire for amorous play with celestial women, <a name="fr_846"></a><a href="#fn_841"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Yet such is Thy nature that even then Thou showest kindness to them.<br> For Thou so slayest them<br> That in another body <a name="fr_847"></a><a href="#fn_842"><font size="1">4</font></a> they may obtain fulfilment of their desires.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 139</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>Most wonderful it is that Thou hast assumed body<br> For the destruction of the Dānavas, <a name="fr_848"></a><a href="#fn_843"><font size="1">1</font></a> famed for their power,<br> When Thou mightest have slain them by Thy mere will.<br> The cause of this is Thy play <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_844"><font size="1">2</font></a> and nothing else, <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_845"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Alas! of a verity unhappy are they<br> Who when the Kālī age, the worst of ages, has come,<br> Do not worship Thee.<br> Men cunning and skilled in the Purāṇas<br> Have made the people devoted to the worship of Hari and Śankara, <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_846"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Who are but Thy creatures. <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_847"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>Those who worship with devotion Devas,<br> Though they know that they are distressed, harassed by Asuras, and subject to Thy control,<br> Are of a surety like unto a man</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 140</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who with, the light of a clear lamp in his <a href="errata.htm#15">hands</a>,<br> Yet falls into some waterless and terrible well. <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_848"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>O Mother! Thou art the remover of the pains which arise from birth,<br> And art known by those desirous of liberation<br> As the giver of happiness when Thou art <i>vidyā</i>, <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> And of unhappiness when Thou art <i>avidyā</i>, <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Surely it is only the ignorant who do not worship Thee,<br> Such as are attached to enjoyment without wisdom. <a name="fr_856"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">14</p>  
 <p>Even Brahmā, Hara, and Hari, as all other Suras, <a name="fr_857"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Ever worship Thy lotus feet, which are our refuge.<br> But those who are of small intelligence and beset with error<br> Do not worship Thee.<br> And so ever repeatedly fall into the ocean of the world. <a name="fr_858"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 141</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">15</p>  
 <p>O Caṇḍī! <a name="fr_859"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">1</font></a> it is most surely by the favour of the dust on Thy lotus feet<br> That Brahmā in the beginning of things creates, Shauri <a name="fr_860"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">2</font></a> protects, and Hara <a name="fr_861"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">3</font></a> destroys the whole world.<br> He is indeed unfortunate<br> Who in this world does not worship Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">16</p>  
 <p>O Devī! Thou art the <i>Devatā</i> of speech of both Suras and Asuras.<br> Without power of speech are even the foremost of Devas<br> When Thou abidest not in them.<br> If men do speak, it is because they are not deprived of Thee. <a name="fr_862"></a><a href="#fn_856"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">17</p>  
 <p>Hari, <a name="fr_863"></a><a href="#fn_857"><font size="1">5</font></a> when cursed by the greatly angered Bhrigu, <a name="fr_864"></a><a href="#fn_858"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 142</font></a></p>  
 <p>Became Fish, Tortoise, Boar, and Man-lion, <a name="fr_865"></a><a href="#fn_859"><font size="1">1</font></a> and Dwarf. <a name="fr_866"></a><a href="#fn_860"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> How can those who worship Him<br> Escape the fire of death?</p>  
 <p align="center">18</p>  
 <p>As is well-known, the <i>linga</i> of Śambhu <a name="fr_867"></a><a href="#fn_861"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the forest<br> Fell on earth in like manner by the curse of Bhrigu.<br> How can those, O Mother! who on earth worship Him <a name="fr_868"></a><a href="#fn_862"><font size="1">4</font></a> who holds a skull<br> Attain to happiness either in this world or the next?</p>  
 <p align="center">19</p>  
 <p>They who worship the elephant-faced Lord of Ganas, <a name="fr_869"></a><a href="#fn_863"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Who was born of Maheśa,<br> With Him in vain take shelter.<br> They know Thee not, O Devī! Mother of the Universe,<br> Who art easy of worship <a name="fr_870"></a><a href="#fn_864"><font size="1">6</font></a> and the giver of the fruit of all desires.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 143</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">20</p>  
 <p>Wonderful it is that through Thy compassion<br> Even the multitude of enemies slain by Thy sharpened arrows<br> Have thus been made to reach Heaven.<br> For had they not been so slain<br> Calamities and the most painful of painful states would they have suffered<br> In that hell which is the result of their (evil) <i>karma</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">21</p>  
 <p>Even Brahmā, Hara, and Hari<br> From pride <a name="fr_871"></a><a href="#fn_865"><font size="1">1</font></a> know not Thy power.<br> How, then, are others able to know it,<br> Bewildered as they are by Thy (three) <i>guṇas</i> of incomparable power? <a name="fr_872"></a><a href="#fn_866"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">22</p>  
 <p>Even <i>Munis</i> <a name="fr_873"></a><a href="#fn_867"><font size="1">3</font></a> suffer, who, being ignorant (of Thee).<br> Do not adore Thy thought-transcending lotus feet,<br> And are set upon the worship of sun and fire. <a name="fr_874"></a><a href="#fn_868"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> By them, even though they read hundreds of Śruti, <a name="fr_875"></a><a href="#fn_869"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 144</font></a></p>  
 <p>The Supreme object of desire <a name="fr_876"></a><a href="#fn_870"><font size="1">1</font></a> is not known, which is the essence of all Vedas.</p>  
 <p align="center">23</p>  
 <p>Methinks it is Thy (three) <i>guṇas</i>,<br> The power of which is so famed on earth,<br> Which makes men turn away from devotion (to Thee),<br> And attaches them to Viṣṇu, Īśa, <a name="fr_877"></a><a href="#fn_871"><font size="1">2</font></a> Bhāskara, <a name="fr_878"></a><a href="#fn_872"><font size="1">3</font></a> Gaṇeśa,<br> By (the teaching of) various <i>Āgamas</i> <a name="fr_879"></a><a href="#fn_873"><font size="1">4</font></a> of their own devising. <a name="fr_880"></a><a href="#fn_874"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">24</p>  
 <p>O Ambikā! (so great is Thy mercy)<br> That Thou art not angry with,<br> But showest kindness to, and maketh greatly prosperous<br> Even those who, skilful in the <i>mantra</i> of delusion, <a name="fr_881"></a><a href="#fn_875"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 145</font></a></p>  
 <p>Make the best of men <a name="fr_882"></a><a href="#fn_876"><font size="1">1</font></a> turn away from Thy feet<br> By Agamas made by themselves, <a name="fr_883"></a><a href="#fn_877"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Teaching of devotion to Hari and Hara. <a name="fr_884"></a><a href="#fn_878"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">25</p>  
 <p>In the Satya age <a name="fr_885"></a><a href="#fn_879"><font size="1">4</font></a> the <i>sattva guṇa</i> <a name="fr_886"></a><a href="#fn_880"><font size="1">5</font></a> was very powerful,<br> Therefore there were no <i>Asadāgamas</i>. <a name="fr_887"></a><a href="#fn_881"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> But in the Kalī age learned folk conceal Thee (from the people).<br> And adore those Devas imagined by them (to be objects of worship). <a name="fr_888"></a><a href="#fn_882"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 146</font></a></p>  
 <p>26</p>  
 <p>Munis, <a name="fr_889"></a><a href="#fn_883"><font size="1">1</font></a> in whom the <i>sattva guṇa</i> <a name="fr_890"></a><a href="#fn_884"><font size="1">2</font></a> is very pure,<br> Meditate upon Thee,<br> Who art the giver on earth of the fruit of liberation,<br> Who art perfect in <i>yoga</i> <a name="fr_891"></a><a href="#fn_885"><font size="1">3</font></a> and the supreme knowledge.<br> Such never again suffer pain in the womb of a mother. <a name="fr_892"></a><a href="#fn_886"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> They are only praiseworthy who are absorbed in Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">27</p>  
 <p><i>Cītśakti</i> <a name="fr_893"></a><a href="#fn_887"><font size="1">5</font></a> is in <i>Paramātmā</i>,<br> Therefore also it is manifested <a name="fr_894"></a><a href="#fn_888"><font size="1">6</font></a> in the world,<br> Wherein it is known as the cause of its creation, maintenance, and destruction. <a name="fr_895"></a><a href="#fn_889"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_147"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 147</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who else in this world without Thee and of his own power<br> Is able to create, move and destroy? <a name="fr_896"></a><a href="#fn_890"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">28</p>  
 <p>O Mother of the world!<br> Can the <i>Tattvas</i>, <a name="fr_897"></a><a href="#fn_891"><font size="1">2</font></a> deprived of <i>Cit</i>, <a name="fr_898"></a><a href="#fn_892"><font size="1">3</font></a> create the world?<br> They are lifeless things. <a name="fr_899"></a><a href="#fn_893"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> O Devī! can the <i>indriyas</i> <a name="fr_900"></a><a href="#fn_894"><font size="1">5</font></a> with their objects and functions, <a name="fr_901"></a><a href="#fn_895"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Bear fruit without Thee?</p>  
 <p align="center">29</p>  
 <p>O Mother! had you not as <i>Svāhā</i> <a name="fr_902"></a><a href="#fn_896"><font size="1">7</font></a> been the cause thereof,<br> Even the Devas could not have obtained their enjoined own portion,<br> Offered in sacrificial rites by <i>Munis</i> <a name="fr_903"></a><a href="#fn_897"><font size="1">8</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 148</font></a></p>  
 <p>Therefore Thou maintainest the whole world. <a name="fr_904"></a><a href="#fn_898"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">30</p>  
 <p>By Thee all this universe was in the beginning of things created;<br> Thou protecteth the Regents of the Quarters among whom Hari and Hara are foremost.<br> At the dissolution of things Thou devourest the whole universe.<br> That which has been done by Thee from the creation<br> Even Devas <a name="fr_905"></a><a href="#fn_899"><font size="1">2</font></a> know not.<br> What, then, shall we say of unfortunate men?</p>  
 <p align="center">31</p>  
 <p>O Mother! Thou hast by slaying the greatly formidable Asura,<br> Who assumed the form of a buffalo, <a name="fr_906"></a><a href="#fn_900"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Protected the Devas.<br> Even the Vedas know Thee not as Thou really art. <a name="fr_907"></a><a href="#fn_901"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Small of intellect as we are how can we praise Thee?</p>  
 <p align="center">32</p>  
 <p>O Mother! Thou hast accomplished a great work In destroying this wicked foe of ours,<br> A thorn painful beyond all thought<br> (In the side of) the whole world.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 149</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thy fame will spread throughout the whole universe.<br> Do Thou, whose power is known to be incomparable,<br> By Thy mercy protect us. <a name="fr_908"></a><a href="#fn_902"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="fn_817"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_822">136:1</a> <i>Vijayā</i>. The Devī Pr. says: "After conquering this very powerful King of the Daityas, named Padma, She is known in the three worlds as Vijayā and unconquerable (<i>aparājitā</i>)."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_818"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_823">136:2</a> See Hymn to Annapurṇā, <i>post</i>. Here the Īśvara Śakti (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_819"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_824">136:3</a> A name of Lakṣmī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_820"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_825">136:4</a> See <a href="htg15.htm#page_87">p. 87</a>, note 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_821"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_826">136:5</a> <i>Medhā</i>, which means, in particular, the retentive faculty or memory, and in general, intelligence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_822"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_827">136:6</a> For in Her all men have their being. She is as <i>Ādhāraśakti</i>, their support (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_823"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_828">136:7</a> As the Purāṇas represent them as doing. The verse deals with the attributes of the <i>ādhāraśakti</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_824"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_829">136:8</a> <i>Gaganam</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_825"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_830">136:9</a> Literally, "headed by," or instances of principal Devas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_826"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_831">136:10</a> Brahmā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_827"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_832">136:11</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_828"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_833">137:1</a> <i>Subrāmshu</i>, or the moon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_829"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_834">137:2</a> Lord of Death.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_830"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_835">137:3</a> Lord of the Air.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_831"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_836">137:4</a> The elephant-headed Deva, son of the Devī as Pārvatī and Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_832"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_837">137:5</a> <i>Vimūdha</i> (see also Umāsanghitā of Śiva Purāṇā).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_833"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_838">137:6</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Ye vā stuvanti manujā amarān vimudhā,<br> Māyā gunaistava caturmukha viṣṇurudrān,<br> Subrāmshuvahniyamavāyuganeśamukhyān,<br> Kim tvāmrite janani to prabhavanti kārye</i>.</dir>  
 <p></p>
 <p><a name="fn_834"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_839">137:7</a> The sacrifice done with ghee poured into fire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_835"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_840">137:8</a> <i>Alpadhiyah</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_836"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_841">137:9</a> The mantra used in <i>homa</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_837"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_842">137:10</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Svāhā nacet tamasi te kathamāpuraddhā,<br> Tvām eva kim nahi ya janti tato hi mudhah</i>.</dir>  
 <p></p>
 <p><a name="fn_838"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_843">137:11</a> Here apparently trees and the like, as contrasted with the animal world. She allots the karma (N).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_839"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_844">138:1</a> The Devas, Her children.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_840"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_845">138:2</a> That is, the Devī does not slay even the Daityas for the mere savage pleasure of killing, and even those whom She slays are slain by Her for the good of the world and their own good, that being slain by Her hand they may go to heaven (see next verse). The Kumārasambhavam says that a man cannot cut down even a poisonous tree if planted by his own hand.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_841"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_846">138:3</a> <i>Devānganāsuratakelimating viditvā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_842"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_847">138:4</a> That is, a celestial body. For, as Daityas do not perform sacrificial rites, etc., so they cannot gain heaven. But when killed by the hand of the Devī, thither they go to their enjoyment, and Devas are protected.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_843"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_848">139:1</a> Sons of Danu. Enemies of the Devas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_844"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_849">139:2</a> <i>Krīḍarasa</i> that is, <i>līlā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_845"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_850">139:3</a> <i>Krīḍārasastava nachānyatarotrahetuh</i> (<i>cf</i>. the Vedantic <i>sutra</i> <i>okavattulīlā kaivalyam</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_846"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_851">139:4</a> Viṣṇu and Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_847"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_852">139:5</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Dhūrttaīh purāṇa caturairhari śankarānām,<br> Sevāparāshcha vihitāstava nirmitānām</i>.</dir>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Men, the verse says, thus worship the creature instead of the Creatrix.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_848"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_853">140:1</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Jnātvā surāmstava vashān asurārddhitāngshcha,<br> Yevai bhajanti bhuvi bhāvayuta vimagnāu,<br> Dhritvā kare suvimalang khalū dipakangte,<br> Kupe patanti manujā vijaletighore</i>.</dir>  
 <p>(See also Umāsanghitā.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_849"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_855">140:2</a> Knowledge and nescience, the source of <i>nivritti</i> and <i>pravritti</i>, with the latter's attendant miseries.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_850"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_856">140:3</a> <i>Kilamandadhibirnārāditā janani bhogaparaistathājnaih</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_851"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_857">140:4</a> Devas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_852"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_858">140:5</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Tadvai nayelpa matayo manasā bhajanti,<br> Bhrāntāh patanti satatam bhavasāgarete</i>.</dir>  
 <p></p>
 <p><a name="fn_853"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_859">141:1</a> Nīlakantha derives the word from <i>chadi</i> ("the wrathful one fearful to the whole world"). Śruti says of the Brahman: <i>Mahadbhayam vajramudhyatam</i> ("great fear and a ready thunderbolt")</p>
 <p><a name="fn_854"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_860">141:2</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_855"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_861">141:3</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_856"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_862">141:4</a> Sarasvatī is She who causes speech. Sound springing from the Mūlādhāra wherein is <i>Devī</i> Kuṇḍalinī proceeds from the stage of mere sound (para), the potentiality of growth in the seed to that of <i>Paśyantī</i>, when the latter begins to sprout; then to <i>Madhyamā</i>, when the first two small leaves appear but inseparate, and lastly, as <i>Vaikharī</i>, when they separate, remaining united only at their base.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_857"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_863">141:5</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_858"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_864">141:6</a> The Ṛṣi of that name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_859"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_865">142:1</a> The Matsya, Kūrma, Varāha, and Nrisingha avatāra of Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_860"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_866">142:2</a> <i>Chalakrit</i>, which Nīlakantha says = Vāmana Avatāra of Viṣṇu. <i>Chalakrit</i>, because Balirāja was deceived by him.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_861"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_867">142:3</a> Śiva, who retired into the forest after the death of Satī, and who was powerless to protect even his own <i>linga</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_862"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_868">142:4</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_863"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_869">142:5</a> Gaṇeśa, son of Śiva, so powerless.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_864"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_870">142:6</a> Devī is (as the Lalitā, verse 133, also says), "easily worshipped" (<i>sukhā-rādhyā</i>), without bodily pain by fasting etc., without restrictions on. the mode of meditation. In the Karma Pr. the Lord describes to Himavat an easy mode of worship if he be unable to meditate on the Supreme Form. The Devī is also "easily pleased" (Lalitā, verse 162, <i>kṣipraprasādinī</i>)--that is, even by a little worship as the Saura and Śiva Purāṇas say of the Lord of Umā, with whom the Devī is one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_865"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_871">143:1</a> <i>Garvabhāvāt</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_866"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_872">143:2</a> Devī is "all-bewildering" (<i>Sarvamohinī</i>). In Karma Pr., Śiva says: "This Māyā is dear to me by which the world is bewildered. I bewilder the whole universe with the Devas, Daityas, and men." The operation of the <i>tāmasika guṇa</i> veils from them the truth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_867"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_873">143:3</a> The Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇa says: "That Divine Devī Mahāmāyā forcibly draws away the minds of even the sages (<i>Munis</i>), and leads them into confusion."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_868"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_874">143:4</a> Because they are <i>pratyakṣadevatā</i>--that is, visible, and She, the supreme ground of them, is beyond vision, mind, and speech.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_869"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_875">143:5</a> The reading of much <i>śruti</i> (revelation) teaches them nothing, for their ignorance conceals from them its true meaning.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_870"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_876">144:1</a> <i>Paramārthatattva</i>. Śankhya says that there are two different kinds of <i>artha</i> or object. One is <i>bhoga</i> (enjoyment), the second, which is the supreme object (<i>paramārtha</i>) is <i>mokṣa</i> (liberation). The one exists in the current issuing from Devī (<i>Avidyā</i>), the other returns on the other current, which draws to the Devī (<i>Vidyā</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_871"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_877">144:2</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_872"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_878">144:3</a> The Sun (<i>Sūrya</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_873"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_879">144:4</a> Here used generally for <i>Śāstra</i>, though also used in a special sense as denoting Veda and Tantra, to the latter of which, Nīlakantha perhaps refers. <i>Nānātantraihmohakaih</i> ("many deluding Tantras"). The reference is here to the dualistic scriptures.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_874"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_880">144:5</a> <i>Svabuddhirachitairvividhāgamaishcha</i> ("composed by themselves").</p>
 <p><a name="fn_875"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_881">144:6</a> <i>Mohamantranipunām</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_876"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_882">145:1</a> According to Nīlakantha, Brāhmanas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_877"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_883">145:2</a> Literally, "said by themselves" (<i>svoktāgamaih</i>)--that is as Nīlakantha says, <i>puruṣapranitāgamaih</i> (or Āgamas composed by men and not revealed by Śiva).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_878"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_884">145:3</a> Viṣṇu and Śiva who are themselves only the children of the Mother, and as much creatures, though of the highest kind, as others are.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_879"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_885">145:4</a> The first and best of the four ages: Satya, Tretā, Dvāpara, and Kalī, the commonly supposed present age. In the second, virtue (<i>dharma</i>) decreased by a quarter, in the third by a half, and in the present or Kalī age only a quarter of <i>dharma</i> remains.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_880"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_886">145:5</a> The <i>guṇa</i>, which manifests on the moral plane as goodness.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_881"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_887">145:6</a> Literally, "bad Āgama," a name applied by some to the Tantra of the "downward current" (<i>arddharetas</i>), but here has a general significance to all <i>Śāstra</i>, whether Tantra or otherwise, of a dualistic character (<i>asachchhāstrāni</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_882"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_888">145:7</a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Tūrye yuge bhavati chātibalang gun.asya,<br> Turyasyatena māthitānyasadāgamāni tvam,<br> Gopayanti nipunāh kavayah kalauvai<br> Tatkalpitān suraganā napi sangstuvanti</i>,</dir>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> "Imagined by them" (<i>tatkalpitān</i>). This does not mean that the Devas do not exist. Hari, Hara, Īśa, Gaṇeśa, Bhāskara, and others have been mentioned in previous verses. The verse is <i>stutivāda</i>, in which that which is its direct object, is dealt with to <a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 146</font></a> the exclusion of all others. What is meant is that She alone is to be worshipped and not any other. <i>Kalpanā</i> is to mistake one thing for another. Thus the world is taken (<i>kalpitā</i>) to be real when the Brahman alone is so. And so, too, mother-of-pearl is mistaken for silver (<i>shuktirajatakalpanā</i>), or a rope for a snake (<i>rajjusarpakalpanā</i>); so people take the Devas to be the ultimate object of worship when it is only the Devī whose manifestations they are, who is such.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_883"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_889">146:1</a> Sages.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_884"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_890">146:2</a> See Introduction.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_885"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_891">146:3</a> <i>Yogasiddhām</i>. The manifested Devī as Umā was a great <i>yoginī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_886"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_892">146:4</a> Are never reborn. According to Hindu ideas the child suffers great pain and misery in the womb of its mother, the remembrance of which is lost on its birth into the world.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_887"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_893">146:5</a> That is, <i>caitanya</i> (Nīlakantha). See the Samkṣepaśāriraka.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_888"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_894">146:6</a> In name and form (<i>nāmarūpātmaka</i>.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_889"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_895">146:7</a> <i>Bhavakrityakartā</i>. Literally, "performer of worldly action"--that is, those stated. For, according to Vedānta and Nyāya, it works therein by its Śakti, though in Śankhya the Puruṣa is merely an enjoyer, spectator, and witness (<i>bhoktā</i>, <i>draṣtā</i>, <i>sākṣī</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_890"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_896">147:1</a> Cf. Dakṣinamūrtisamhitā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_891"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_897">147:2</a> The twenty-four beginning with <i>Mahat</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_892"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_898">147:3</a> <i>Vide ante</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_893"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_899">147:4</a> <i>Jarāni</i>; mere matter but for the informing life of the Goddess.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_894"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_900">147:5</a> The so-called "senses" of knowledge (<i>Jnānendriya</i>), viz.: <i>Cakṣu</i> (eye), <i>Śrotra</i> (ear), <i>ghrāṇa</i> (smell), <i>rasanā</i> (tongue), <i>tvak</i> (skin) and of action (<i>karmendriya</i>); viz., <i>Vak</i> (speech), <i>pāni</i> (hand), <i>pāda</i> (feet), <i>pāyu</i> (anus), <i>upastha</i> (genitals).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_895"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_901">147:6</a> <i>Indriyāni guṇakarmayutāni</i>. Thus, form (<i>rūpa</i>) is the <i>guṇa</i> or object (<i>viṣaya</i>) of the eye, and <i>darśana</i> (or seeing), its function (<i>karma</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_896"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_902">147:7</a> The <i>Mantra</i> used in <i>Agnikriyā</i> and wife of Agni.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_897"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_903">147:8</a> If the Mantra Svāhā, which is the Devī, be not said, then the Devas get nothing from the <i>homa</i>. Their Śastric portion they obtain by virtue only of the Devī as <i>Svāhā</i>, the cause thereof.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_898"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_904">148:1</a> The Devī protects the Devas, and if the Devas are maintained, then the whole world is maintained, as the Devas are Her agents in the maintenance thereof.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_899"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_905">148:2</a> <i>Manujā</i>, which here, according to Nīlakantha, includes Devas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_900"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_906">148:3</a> The Demon Mahiṣa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_901"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_907">148:4</a> <i>Gatingtava yathārthatayā na jagmuh</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_902"></a><a href="htg22.htm#fr_908">149:1</a> The hymn concluding, Devībhāgavata continues, Vyāsa said: "Devī, thus praised, said in soft, sweet voice, 'Oh, best of Devas, tell Me what other thing most difficult and hard to accomplish you would have Me do?'"</p>
