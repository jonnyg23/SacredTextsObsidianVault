 <p><a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="green">p. 83</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 17</h3>  
 <p>THE devotee <a name="fr_125"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">1</font></a> who, having placed before himself, <a name="fr_126"></a><a href="#fn_126"><font size="1">2</font></a> and meditated and again meditated <a name="fr_127"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">3</font></a> upon, the abode, <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">4</font></a> strewn with flowers, <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_129"><font size="1">5</font></a> of the Deva with the bow of flowers, <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_130"><font size="1">6</font></a> recites <a name="fr_131"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">7</font></a> Thy <i>Mantra</i>, Ah! <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_132"><font size="1">8</font></a> he becomes on earth the Lord of Gandharvas, <a name="fr_133"></a><a href="#fn_133"><font size="1">9</font></a> and the ocean of the nectar of the flow of poesy, <a name="fr_134"></a><a href="#fn_134"><font size="1">10</font></a> and is after death in Thy supreme abode. <a name="fr_135"></a><a href="#fn_135"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Devotee' (Bhaktah)</i></p>  
 <p>The Sādhaka who is a Yogi on the Divya path.</p>  
 <p><i>'The Abode' (Kusuṁadhanusho mandiram)</i></p>  
 <p>The triangular Yoni Mandala in the Mūlādhāra. Nirvāṇa-Tantra says, 'In the triangle, the abode of Kāma, the Liṅga is Maheśvara.'</p>  
 <p><i>'With its own flowers'</i></p>  
 <p>Adorned with the Svayaṁbhu-liṅga which is compared to a flower. Gorakṣa-Samhitā says, 'He is truly wise who knows the supreme Tejas in the Yoni called Svayaṁbhu-liṅga. Others are but beasts of burden.<font size="-1" color="green">'</font></p>  
 <p><i>'Lord of Gandharvas' (Gandharva-śreṇīpatih)</i></p>  
 <p>A great singer. It is said 'there is nothing better than a song,'</p>  
 <p><i>'Poesy' (Kavitvāmṛta-nadi-nadinah)</i></p>  
 <p>He becomes like the great poet Kālidāsa.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="green">p. 84</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>'Is great' (Prabhavati)</i></p>  
 <p>He attains Nirvāṇa on being united with Thee who are Saccidānandarūpā. Kūrma-Purāṇa says, Brahmavādīs have learnt in all Vedas and Vedāntas the one, omnipresent, subtle (Kūtastha), immovable, absolute, endless, undecaying Brahman, the sole supreme Niṣkala-Tattva higher than the highest, eternal, auspicious, wondrous.' Devīgītā says 'Oh Mountain, he in whom Parabhakti is thus generated becomes merged in Pure Consciousness.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_125"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_125">83:1</a> <i>Bhaktah</i>: here the <i>Vira Sādhaka</i> (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_126"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_126">83:2</a> <i>Purah</i>: that is with the <i>Mandala</i> of <i>Kāma</i> before him (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_127"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_127">83:3</a> That is, with intensity. <i>Dhyāyan dhyāyan</i>, repeatedly meditating (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_128"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_128">83:4</a> <i>Kusuma-dhanuṣo mandiraṁ</i>. The Deva with the bow of flowers is Kama whose abode is the <i>Madanāgāra</i>. <i>Tantrakalpadruma</i> says, 'He who recites the Mantra ten thousand times meditating on the flower-covered Yoni (<i>Svapuṣpairākīrṇam</i>) of <i>Śakti</i>, of a certainty charms all with his poesy.' <i>Svapuṣpa</i> is called <i>Svayaṁbhukusuma</i> in <i>Tantra-śāstr</i>a, <i>Mātṛkābheda-Tantra</i> says, 'Oh Lady of <i>Maheśa Svapuṣpa</i>, which charms all is the <i>Ṛtu</i> which first appears in a married girl (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_129"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_129">83:5</a> <i>Svapuṣpairākīrṇam</i>. The word <i>svapuṣpa</i> = <i>svayambhupuṣpa</i>mentioned in the Tantras. The word <i>puṣpa</i> has here, and in ordinary parlance, a figurative sense, as in English. For <i>puspaśabdena atra ṛtur ucyate, mātṛkābheda-tantra-pramāṇānusāreṇa anūḍhāyāh kanyāyāh prathama eva ṛtur atra ucyate</i>. <i>Tantrāntare tu vivāhitāyā eva bālāyā ṛtur atra vivakṣitah</i>. The <i>Śyāmārahasy</i>a reads '<i>supuṣpa</i>,' which literally means pleasing fragrant flower, but which is possibly a misprint for <i>svapushpa</i>. The meaning of the passage is as follows: <i>Sādhakah svasya purobhāge śaktiṁ saṁsthāpya tasyā ṛturudhirasiktāṁ yoniṁ avalokayan san devmantraṁ japati</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_130"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_130">83:6</a> That is, <i>Kāma</i>, the Deva of Desire, whose bow and arrows are made of flowers.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_131"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_131">83:7</a> <i>Japati</i>. Recites ten thousand times (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_132"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_132">83:8</a> <i>Aho</i>. 'Ah' an exclamation of wonder (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_133"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_133">83:9</a> Celestial spirits (<i>devayoni</i>), who play and sing at the banquets of the Devas. According to the Viṣṇu-Purāṇa, sons of Brahmā 'born imbibing melody.' The <i>Sādhaka</i> thus becomes a master of dance, music and song, <a name="page_85"><font size="1" color="green">p. 85</font></a> <i>Gandarva-śreṇīpatih</i>. He becomes a great singer and a master of melody. The <i>Sāhasānka</i> says, '<i>Hāhā</i> is called a <i>Gandharva</i> and singing also makes a <i>Gandharva</i>' (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_134"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_134">83:10</a> He becomes a <i>Pandita</i> in all literature. The Kālī-Tantra, quoted in the Kālīkalpalatā, says that in strength he becomes like the wind, in wealth of gifts like Indra, and in the musical art like Tumburu (K.B.)a <i>Ṛṣi</i>, master of music and inventor of the <i>tāmbur</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_135"></a><a href="htk21.htm#fr_135">83:11</a> Paramapadalinah prabhavati, that is, he attains nirvāṇa (K.B.).</p>  
 <p><i>Prabhavati</i>: becomes capable of creating and so forth on being merged with Thy Supreme Feet (V). The word literally means 'Excels.'</p>
