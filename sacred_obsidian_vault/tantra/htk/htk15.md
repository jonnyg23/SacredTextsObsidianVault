 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="green">p. 70</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 11</h3>  
 <p>O SPOUSE of Hara, <a name="fr_89"></a><a href="#fn_89"><font size="1">1</font></a> should (a <i>Sādhaka</i>) daily <a name="fr_90"></a><a href="#fn_90"><font size="1">2</font></a> recite Thy <i>mantra</i> for the space of a year meditating the while <a name="fr_91"></a><a href="#fn_91"><font size="1">3</font></a> with knowledge of its meaning <a name="fr_92"></a><a href="#fn_92"><font size="1">4</font></a> upon Thee intent <a name="fr_93"></a><a href="#fn_93"><font size="1">5</font></a> upon Thy union <a name="fr_94"></a><a href="#fn_94"><font size="1">6</font></a> with the great Mahākāla, above whom Thou art, <a name="fr_95"></a><a href="#fn_95"><font size="1">7</font></a> then such a knower <a name="fr_96"></a><a href="#fn_96"><font size="1">8</font></a> has every pleasure that he wills upon the earth, <a name="fr_97"></a><a href="#fn_97"><font size="1">9</font></a> and holds all great powers <a name="fr_98"></a><a href="#fn_98"><font size="1">10</font></a> in the grasp of his lotus-like hands.</p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Spouse of Hara' (Haravadhū)</i></p>  
 <p>Charmer of Mahākāla.</p>  
 <p><i>'Mentally recite' (Vicintya japati)</i></p>  
 <p>The Kaulāvalī says that mental (Mānasā) Japa is a hundred times more efficacious than verbal (Vācika) Japa.</p>  
 <p>According to Durgārāma the words may also mean 'recite' keeping in mind the Artha or meaning and so forth of the Mantra. For it is said that he who does not know the Artha of Mantra, the Caitanya of Mantra, and Yoni-mudrā is without success (Siddhi) even if he do Japa of the Mantra a million times.</p>  
 <p><i>'Unperturbed mind' (Susthībhūya)</i></p>  
 <p>The Kulārṇava-Tantra thus enjoins: 'Beloved when doing Japa of a Mantra one should be calm, pure, sparing in food, reverential, self-controlled, unaffected by the opposites (Dvandva), steady of mind, silent and self-disciplined.</p>  
 <p><i>'Meditating on Thee' (Vicintyatvām)</i></p>  
 <p>The Kaulāvalī-Tantra says, 'One should meditate upon the Spouse of Śiva before Japa and after meditation should again do</p>  
 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="green">p. 71</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Japa.' The Sādhaka who does Japa and meditation together soon attains success.</p>  
 <p><i>'Upon Him' (Vipāritām)</i></p>  
 <p>(The original is 'Viparītah' in the first case and Durgārāma therefore makes it an adjective of the Sādhaka who he says unites with his Śakti in Viparīta Maithuna. Vimalānanda however reads it as Vipāritām in the second case making it an adjective of 'Thee' (the Devī) who is the object of meditation,)</p>  
 <p><i>'Great Powers' (Mahāsiddhinivahāh)</i></p>  
 <p>Such as that by which is gained Sālokya, Sārūpya, Sāyujya and Nirvāṇa forms of Liberation.</p>
