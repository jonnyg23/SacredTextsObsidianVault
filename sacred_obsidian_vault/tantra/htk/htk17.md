 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 13</h3>  
 <p>O MOTHER, people there are who worship many other <i>Devas</i> than Thyself. <a name="fr_103"></a><a href="#fn_103"><font size="1">1</font></a> They are greatly ignorant, and know nothing of the high truth, <a name="fr_104"></a><a href="#fn_104"><font size="1">2</font></a> (but I) of my own uncontrollable <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_105"><font size="1">3</font></a> desire for Thee approach Thee, the Primordial Power, <a name="fr_106"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">4</font></a> who dost deeply enjoy the great Bliss arising from union (with Śiva), <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_107"><font size="1">5</font></a> and who art worshipped by Hari, Hara, Viriñci, and all other Devas. <a name="fr_108"></a><a href="#fn_108"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Deluded' (Vimūdhāh)</i></p>  
 <p>That is, devoid of discrimination.</p>  
 <p><i>'Enlightened' (Vibudhaih)</i></p>  
 <p>The Bagalā-Stotra says, 'Oh four-armed, four-headed, worshipful Parameśvari, Oh Devi Aṁbikā who art ever worshipped with devotion by Kṛṣṇa, Oh Parameśvari who art worshipped by the Lord of the daughter of Himālaya, grant beauty, Grant victory' and so forth.</p>  
 <p><i>'Ādyā'</i></p>  
 <p>Who art before and the beginning of the world.</p>  
 <p><i>'Union' (Rati)</i></p>  
 <p>Which is Viparīta as above described.</p>  
 <p><i>'Wine'</i></p>  
 <p>That is Rasa.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_103"></a><a href="htk17.htm#fr_103">74:1</a> <a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a> That is, thinking that other <i>Devas</i> grant greater boons (Commentary, K.B.). Cf. also what Śaṁkarācārya says about the worship of other Devas in fourth śloka of the <i>Devyaparādha-kṣamāpana-stotra</i>, and see <i>Devibhāgavata</i> (V. 19) (Hymn to Jagadaṁbikā).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_104"></a><a href="htk17.htm#fr_104">74:2</a> <a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a> <i>Paramam</i>, that is, <i>Tattvaṁ</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_105"></a><a href="htk17.htm#fr_105">74:3</a> For he is a devotee (Bhakta) whose desire for Her is so great that he cannot control but is controlled by it.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_106"></a><a href="htk17.htm#fr_106">74:4</a> <i>Ādyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_107"></a><a href="htk17.htm#fr_107">74:5</a> <i>Rati-rasa-mahānanda-niratām</i>. The Devī delights in creation, which is the fruit of Her union with the <i>Puruṣa</i> (Śiva). 'Great Bliss,' for, as on the physical plane yadrūpaṁ paramānandam tan nāsti bhuvanatraye (Mātṛkābheda-Tantra, chap. ii), it is the counterpart on that plane of the ecstatic union which produced the Universe itself. It is the reflection of the higher Bliss attainable even here by the union of Śivaśakti (in the form of Kuṇdalinī) in the <i>Sahasrāra</i>. Some read <i>Rasikāṁ</i> for <i>Niratāṁ</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_108"></a><a href="htk17.htm#fr_108">74:6</a> <i>Viṣṇu</i>, <i>Śiva</i>, and <i>Brahmā</i>. What, then, is the use of praying to <i>Brahmā</i>, <i>Viṣṇu</i>, and <i>Śiva</i> when they themselves worship Her? (Commentary, K.B.). Cf. also Devibhāgavata, <i>loc. cit</i>. The <i>Devi</i> is Mother of all, from <i>Brahmā</i> to the lowliest worm (<i>Ābrahmākhilajanani</i>, Lalitā 67).</p>
