 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="green">p. 91</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 20</h3>  
 <p>O MOTHER, he who, being a controller of his passions, <a name="fr_151"></a><a href="#fn_151"><font size="1">1</font></a> eats <i>haviṣyānnaṁ</i>, <a name="fr_152"></a><a href="#fn_152"><font size="1">2</font></a> and, being proficient in meditation on Thy feet, rightly recites <a name="fr_153"></a><a href="#fn_153"><font size="1">3</font></a> Thy <i>mantra</i> a hundred thousand times by day, and he who afterwards <a name="fr_154"></a><a href="#fn_154"><font size="1">4</font></a> naked at night, when united with his <i>Śakti</i>, <a name="fr_155"></a><a href="#fn_155"><font size="1">5</font></a> rightly recites Thy great <i>mantra</i> another such hundred thousand times, becomes on earth like unto the Destroyer of Smara. <a name="fr_156"></a><a href="#fn_156"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Naked' (Nagnah)</i></p>  
 <p>That is free from the covering of Māyā; Nirvikāra.</p>  
 <p><i>'Amorous play' (Nidhuvana-vinodena)</i></p>  
 <p>That is enjoying the bliss of union between Ātmā and Paraśakti. The Kulārṇava-Tantra says, 'That is coition (Maithuna) in which there is the bliss arising from the union of Ātmā and Paraśakti. Others are but Enjoyers of women.'</p>  
 <p><i>'Becomes' (Syāt)</i></p>  
 <p>That is, becomes liberated whilst yet living (Jīvanmukta) like Śiva.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_151"></a><a href="htk24.htm#fr_151">91:1</a> <i>Vashī</i>. The first part of this <i>Śloka</i> refers to <i>Paśvācāra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_152"></a><a href="htk24.htm#fr_152">91:2</a> That is, one who has undertaken the <i>Puraścaraṇavrata</i>, and eats the pure form of food known as <i>Haviṣyānnaṁ</i> (K.B.).</p>  
 <p><i>Haviṣyāśanaratah</i>: that is after the recitation (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_153"></a><a href="htk24.htm#fr_153">91:3</a> Makes <i>japa</i> (see <i>ibid</i>.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_154"></a><a href="htk24.htm#fr_154">91:4</a> <i>Paraṁ</i>: that is, when he has been <i>Abhiṣikta</i> into <i>Vīrācāra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_155"></a><a href="htk24.htm#fr_155">91:5</a> <a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="green">p. 92</font></a> <i>Naktaṁ nagno nidhuvana-vinodena</i>, the meaning of which is <i>yādā sādhakah śaktyā saha maithuna-kriyāsakto bhavati, tadā sa mantraṁ japati</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_156"></a><a href="htk24.htm#fr_156">91:6</a> <i>Smarahara</i> or <i>Śiva</i> (see note <a href="htk22.htm#fn_140">5</a> to <i>Śloka</i> 18, <i>ante</i>). The Tantrakalpadruma says, 'He who eats <i>Haviṣyānnaṁ</i>, who keeping <i>Devī</i> in mind recites the <i>Mantra</i> a hundred thousand times by day and is at night united with his <i>Śakti</i> becomes the Lord of the earth.' (V).</p>
