 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="green">p. 93</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 21</h3>  
 <p>O MOTHER, this Hymn of Thine is the source from whence originates Thy <i>mantra</i>. <a name="fr_157"></a><a href="#fn_157"><font size="1">1</font></a> It sings of Thy real self, and contains injunctions for the worship of Thy two lotus Feet. He who reads it at midnight or at time of worship <a name="fr_158"></a><a href="#fn_158"><font size="1">2</font></a> even his random talk <a name="fr_159"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">3</font></a> becomes the nectar juice of poesy.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Thy real self' (Svarūpākhyaṁ)</i></p>  
 <p>Speaks of the Dhyāna of both Thy gross and subtle aspects.</p>  
 <p><i>'Reads' (Paṭati)</i></p>  
 <p>That is recites aloud. The Viśuddheśvara-Tantra says, 'Oh Devī, the reading of a Hymn (Stotra) mentally, or the recitation of a Mantra loudly is as ineffectual as water in a broken jar.'</p>  
 <p><i>'Nectar of Poesy' (Prasarati kavitvāmṛtarasah)</i></p>  
 <p>He becomes full of the sweetness of Poesy. The Kālīkulasarvasva says, All whose difficulties and dangers are destroyed by a single reading, as it were flies in a flame. His speech flows like the Ganges full of prose and poetry.'</p>
