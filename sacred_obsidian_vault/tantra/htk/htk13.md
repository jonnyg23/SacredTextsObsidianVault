 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="green">p. 66</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 9</h3>  
 <p>WHAT, indeed, O Mother, <a name="fr_74"></a><a href="#fn_74"><font size="1">1</font></a> can we of so dull a mind say of Thee whose True Being <a name="fr_75"></a><a href="#fn_75"><font size="1">2</font></a> not even Dhātā, <a name="fr_76"></a><a href="#fn_76"><font size="1">3</font></a> Īśa, <a name="fr_77"></a><a href="#fn_77"><font size="1">4</font></a> or Hari <a name="fr_78"></a><a href="#fn_78"><font size="1">5</font></a> know? Yet, despite our dullness and ignorance, our devotion towards Thee makes us talk of Thee. <a name="fr_79"></a><a href="#fn_79"><font size="1">6</font></a> Therefore, O Dark Devī, <a name="fr_80"></a><a href="#fn_80"><font size="1">7</font></a> forgive this our folly. Anger towards ignorant creatures such as we, is not befitting Thee <a name="fr_81"></a><a href="#fn_81"><font size="1">8</font></a>.</p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Mother'</i></p>  
 <p>Of us all including Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Rudra. In the Devi-Sūkta, Viṣṇu says, 'One, subtle, and unchanged, and yet many, Thou dost give birth to millions of worlds. Who am I Viṣṇu, and who is the other Śiva and who are the Devas that we and they should be able to (fully) sing Thy praises? ' In the Mārkaṇdeya-Purāṇa, Brahmā says, 'When Viṣṇu, Īśvara and myself owe our appearance to Thee who has the power to (fitly) praise Thee?' In Viṣṇuyāmala, Viṣṇu says to Devī 'Oh Mother, none know Thy supreme aspect. The heavenly ones therefore worship that gross (Sthūla) aspect of Thine in the form of Kālī and the rest.' The Mahākāla-saṁhitā says, 'When Dhātā was not, nor Viṣṇu, nor Kāla, when the five Bhūtas were not, then Thou the Cause wert alone as the Supreme Brahman, the Being of all that is.'</p>  
 <p><i>(Asite) 'Unlimited'</i></p>  
 <p>She is not limited by the Guṇas and is Nirguṇā.</p>
