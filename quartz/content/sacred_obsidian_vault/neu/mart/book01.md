 <a href="mart028.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER II</a> <small>How Balin, arrayed like a poor knight, pulled out the sword, which afterward was the cause of his death. </small><br>
 <a href="mart029.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER III</a> <small>How the Lady of the Lake demanded the knight's head that had won the sword, or the maiden's head. </small><br>
 <a href="mart030.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER IV</a> <small>How Merlin told the adventure of this damosel. </small><br>
 <a href="mart031.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER V</a> <small>How Balin was pursued by Sir Lanceor, knight of Ireland, and how he jousted and slew him. </small><br>
 <a href="mart032.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER VI</a> <small>How a damosel, which was love to Lanceor, slew herself for love, and how Balin met with his brother Balan. </small><br>
 <a href="mart033.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER VII</a> <small>How a dwarf reproved Balin for the death of Lanceor, and how King Mark of Cornwall found them, and made a tomb over them. </small><br>
 <a href="mart034.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER VIII</a> <small>How Merlin prophesied that two the best knights of the world should fight there, which were Sir Lancelot and Sir Tristram. </small><br>
 <a href="mart035.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER IX</a> <small>How Balin and his brother, by the counsel of Merlin, took King Rience and brought him to King Arthur. </small><br>
 <a href="mart036.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER X</a> <small>How King Arthur had a battle against Nero and King Lot of Orkney, and how King Lot was deceived by Merlin, and how twelve kings were slain. </small><br>
 <a href="mart037.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XI</a> <small>Of the interment of twelve kings, and of the prophecy of Merlin, and how Balin should give the dolorous stroke. </small><br>
 <a href="mart038.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XII</a> <small>How a sorrowful knight came before Arthur, and how Balin fetched him, and how that knight was slain by a knight invisible. </small><br>
 <a href="mart039.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XIII</a> <small>How Balin and the damosel met with a knight which was in likewise slain, and how the damosel bled for the custom of a castle. </small><br>
 <a href="mart040.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XIV</a> <small>How Balin met with that knight named Garlon at a feast, and there he slew him, to have his blood to heal therewith the son of his host. </small><br>
 <a href="mart041.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XV</a> <small>How Balin fought with King Pellam, and how his sword brake, and how he gat a spear wherewith he smote the dolorous stroke. </small><br>
 <a href="mart042.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XVI</a> <small>How Balin was delivered by Merlin, and saved a knight that would have slain himself for love. </small><br>
 <a href="mart043.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XVII</a> <small>How that knight slew his love and a knight lying by her, and after, how he slew himself with his own sword, and how Balin rode toward a castle where he lost his life. </small><br>
 <a href="mart044.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XVIII</a> <small>How Balin met with his brother Balan, and how each of them slew other unknown, till they were wounded to death. </small><br>
 <a href="mart045.htm">BOOK II CHAPTER XIX</a> <small>How Merlin buried them both in one tomb, and of Balin's sword. </small><br>
