 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Canterbury Tales and Other Works of Chaucer (Middle English)</i>, by Geoffery Chaucer, [14th cent.], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h4 align="center">The Canterbury Tales</h4> <h3 align="center">The Franklin's Prologue</h3> <a name="an_709"></a> Thise olde gentil Britouns in hir dayes<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_710">710</a></font></span> Of diverse aventures maden layes,<br> <a name="an_711"></a> Rymeyed in hir firste Briton tonge,<br> <a name="an_712"></a> Whiche layes with hir instrumentz they songe<br> <a name="an_713"></a> Or elles redden hem for hir plesaunce;<br> <a name="an_714"></a> And oon of hem have I in remembraunce,<br> <a name="an_715"></a> Which I shal seyn with good wyl as I kan.<br> <a name="an_716"></a> But, sires, by cause I am a burel man,<br> <a name="an_717"></a> At my bigynnyng first I yow biseche,<br> <a name="an_718"></a> Have me excused of my rude speche.<br> <a name="an_719"></a> I lerned nevere rethorik, certeyn;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_720">720</a></font></span> Thyng that I speke, it moot be bare and pleyn.<br> <a name="an_721"></a> I sleep nevere on the Mount of Pernaso,<br> <a name="an_722"></a> Ne lerned Marcus Tullius Scithero.<br> <a name="an_723"></a> Colours ne knowe I none, withouten drede,<br> <a name="an_724"></a> But swiche colours as growen in the mede,<br> <a name="an_725"></a> Or elles swiche as men dye or peynte.<br> <a name="an_726"></a> Colours of rethoryk been to me queynte;<br> <a name="an_727"></a> My spirit feeleth noght of swich mateere.<br> <a name="an_728"></a> But if yow list, my tale shul ye heere.<br>
