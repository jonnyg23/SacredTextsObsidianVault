 <img src="img/53.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">The School-Boy</h3>  
 <p>I love to rise in a summer morn,<br> When the birds sing on every tree;<br> The distant huntsman winds his horn,<br> And the sky-lark sings with me.<br> O! what sweet company.</p>  
 <p>But to go to school in a summer morn,<br> O! it drives all joy away;<br> Under a cruel eye outworn,<br> The little ones spend the day,<br> In sighing and dismay.</p>  
 <p>Ah! then at times I drooping sit,<br> And spend many an anxious hour,<br> Nor in my book can I take delight,<br> Nor sit in learnings bower,<br> Worn thro’ with the dreary shower.</p>  
 <p>How can the bird that is born for joy,<br> Sit in a cage and sing.<br> How can a child when fears annoy,<br> But droop his tender wing,<br> And forget his youthful spring.</p>  
 <p>0! father &amp; mother, if buds are nip’d,<br> And blossoms blown away,<br> And if the tender plants are strip’d<br> Of their joy in the springing day,<br> By sorrow and cares dismay,</p>  
 <p>How shall the summer arise in joy<br> Or the summer fruits appear.<br> Or how shall we gather what griefs destroy<br> Or bless the mellowing year,<br> When the blasts of winter appear.</p>
