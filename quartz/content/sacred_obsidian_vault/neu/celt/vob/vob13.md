 <p><a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 87</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">X</h3>  <h3 align="center">From Ms. Laud 613, p. 21.</h3>  
 <p align="center">Mura cecinit.</p>  
 <p></p><dir><dir>'Coinne Mongain is Coluim caim<br> maic Feidlimthe an ardnaoim [5]<br> a Carraic Eolairg co m-bloidh<br> canuid eoiaigh a leabruib.<br>     .     .     .     .     .  
 <p>De dardain tainic gan mairg<br> Mongan co Carruic Eolairg<br> d’ acallaim Coluim Cille [10]<br> a Tir tredaig Tairngaire.</p>  
 <p>Ni ḟuair Mongan do tognam<br> ag techt do d’ ḟech<i>ain</i> nime<br> acht a cenn--mor in soc[h]ar--<br> fa cochall Coluim Cille.' [15]</p></dir></dir>  
 <p align="center">Muru (of Fothain <a name="fr_165"></a><a href="#fn_165"><font size="1">1</font></a>) cecinit</p>  
 <p></p><dir>The meeting of Mongan and beloved Colum<br> The son of Fedlimid, the noble saint,<br> At Carraic Eolairg <a name="fr_166"></a><a href="#fn_166"><font size="1">2</font></a> with fame<br> Wise men sing in books. [20]  
 <p>On a Thursday without woe<br> Mongan came to Carrait Eolairg<br> To converse with Comm Cille,<br> From the flock-abounding Land of Promise.</p>  
 <p>Mongan found not any help [25]<br> When he went to see Heaven,<br> But his head--great the profit!<br> Under Cohan Cille's cowl.'</p></dir>
