 <p><a name="page_266"><font size="1" color="green">p. 266</font></a> <a name="page_267"><font size="1" color="green">p. 267</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TRIADS OF BARDISM.</h3>  
 <p>1. God made the world of three substances: fire; nature; and finiteness.</p>  
 <p>2. The three instrumentalities of God in making the world: will; wisdom; and love.</p>  
 <p>3. The three principal occupations of God: to enlighten the darkness; to give a body to nonentity; and to animate the dead.</p>  
 <p>4. Three things which God cannot be: unskilful; unjust; and unmerciful.</p>  
 <p>5. Three things required by God of man: firm belief, that is, faith; religious obedience; and to do justice.</p>  
 <p>6. The three principal temperaments of life: strength; vigour; and perception.</p>  
 <p>7. The three principal properties of life: temper; motion; and light.</p>
