 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE THREE WREATHED BARDS.</h3>  
 <p>The three wreathed Bards of the Isle of Britain: Tydai, <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">1</font></a> the Bard of Huon, <a name="fr_147"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">2</font></a> who was the first that arranged the mode of dwelling according to clans for the nation of the Cymry; and Rhuvawn <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">3</font></a> the Bard, who conferred system and privilege upon the co-aration of a township; and Melgin; <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">4</font></a> son of Einigan the Giant, who was the first that made a symbol for language and speech. And a wreath was given to each around his head, that is, of the air-growing misletoe. (From various Triads, collected out of different manuscripts in Glamorgan.)</p>
