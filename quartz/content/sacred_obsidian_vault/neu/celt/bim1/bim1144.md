 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE PHILOSOPHY OF THE BLUE BARD OF THE CHAIR.</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">In the forehead is the intellect;<br> In the nape is the memory;<br> In the pate is discretion;<br> In the understanding, memory, and<br>   discretion together, is reason;<br> In the lungs is the breath;<br> In the breast is lust;<br> In the liver is the heat;</div>  
 <p><a name="page_394"><font size="1" color="green">p. 394</font></a> <a name="page_395"><font size="1" color="green">p. 395</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">In the veins is the blood;<br> In the bile is anger;<br> In the spleen is joyousness; <br> In the heart is love;<br> In all these is affection;<br> In the affection is the soul; <br> In the soul is the mind;<br> In the mind is faith;<br> In faith is the Son of God;<br> In the Son of God is imperishable life;<br> In imperishable life is gwynvyd;</div>  
 <p>Without end; and blessed is he, who rightly exercises the faculties with which God has endued him, in order to attain to endless gwynvyd, for ever and ever. Amen.</p>  
 <p align="right">The Blue Bard of the Chair has said it.</p>
