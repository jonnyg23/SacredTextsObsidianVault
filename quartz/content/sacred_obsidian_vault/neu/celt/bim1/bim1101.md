 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ANNWN.--LIFE.--DEATH.</h3>  
 <p>Question. In what place is Annwn?</p>  
 <p>Answer. Where there is the least possible of animation and life, and the greatest of death, without other condition.</p>  
 <p>Q. What are the characteristics of life?</p>  
 <p>A. Lightness, light, heat, and incorruption, that is, unchangeableness. <a name="fr_255"></a><a href="#fn_253"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>Q. What are the characteristic marks of death?</p>  
 <p>A. Heaviness, cold, darkness, and corruption, that is, changeableness.</p>  
 <p>Q. In what does the nature of death and mortality consist?</p>  
 <p>A. In its characteristics, where one is the cause of another, as heaviness is the cause of darkness, and both the cause of corruption, and corruption the cause of both.</p>  
 <p>Q. In what does the necessity of animation and life consist?</p>  
 <p>A. In its characteristics, that is, brightness, and light, and lightness, and incorruption, one being the cause of another--hence God and life.</p>
