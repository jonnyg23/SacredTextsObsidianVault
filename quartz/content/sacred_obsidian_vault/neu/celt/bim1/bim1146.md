 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">TRIADS OF TEN NUMBERS.</h3>  
 <p>1. Three particular ones: one God; one truth; and one point of liberty; and to know those threes will bring to Gwynvyd.</p>  
 <p>2. Three particular twos: the two sexes of living beings, namely, male and female; two principles, namely, good and evil; and the two heads of necessity, namely, beginning and end.</p>  
 <p>3. Three particular threes: the three characteristics of order, number, weight, and measure; the three bodily perceptions, seeing, hearing, and feeling; and the three principal faculties <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">4</font></a> of the intellect, <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">5</font></a> to love, to hate, and to judge. <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_398"><font size="1" color="green">p. 398</font></a> <a name="page_399"><font size="1" color="green">p. 399</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Three particular fours: the four parts of the world and measure, east, south, north, and west; the four points of the sun, namely, the winter solstice, the vernal equinox, the summer solstice, and the autumnal equinox; and the four properties of form, namely, round, triangular, square, and flat.</p>  
 <p>5. Three particular fives: the five felicities of life, namely, health, liberty, love, welcome, and respect; the five columns of justice, fear, courage, hatred, love, and truth; and the five constituents of matter, earth, water, air, fire, and heaven.</p>  
 <p>6. Three particular sixes. <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
