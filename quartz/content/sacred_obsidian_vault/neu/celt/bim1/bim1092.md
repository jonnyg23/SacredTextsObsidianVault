Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE TWELVE PRIMARY NEGATIVES. <a name="fr_224"></a><a href="#fn_223"><font size="1">3</font></a></h3>  
 <p>The twelve primary Negatives, called in some books the Lesson of the Gwyddoniaid, but in other books the Lesson of Tydain.</p>  
 <p>There is nothing sought after but what is precious;<br> There is nothing precious but what is beneficial;<br> There is nothing beneficial but possession;<br> There is no possession but Gwynvyd;<br> There is no Gwynvyd but knowledge;<br> There is no knowledge but what is new;<br> There is nothing new but what changes;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="green">p. 218</font></a> <a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="green">p. 219</font></a></p>  
 <p>There is no change but what is advantageous;<br> There is no advantage but what is beautiful;<br> There is nothing beautiful but what is just;<br> There is nothing just but love;<br> There is no love but God.</p>  
 <p align="center">And thus it ends.</p>  
 <p align="center">Tydain the Father of Awen sang it, says the Book of Sion Cent.</p>
