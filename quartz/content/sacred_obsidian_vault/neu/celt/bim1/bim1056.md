 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Barddas of Iolo Morganwg, Vol. I.</i>, ed. by J. Williams Ab Ithel, [1862], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE SYSTEM OF NUMERALS.</h3>  
 <p>One,</p>  
 <p>Ten,</p>  
 <p>A hundred, ten tens,</p>  
 <p>A thousand, ten hundreds,</p>  
 <p>A myriad, ten thousands,</p>  
 <p>Mwnt, ten myriads,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="green">p. 110</font></a> <a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="green">p. 111</font></a></p>  
 <p>Rhiallu, ten mwnts,</p>  
 <p>Mwnda, Buna, ten rhiallus,</p>  
 <p>Cattyrva, ten tyrvas,</p>  
 <p>Tyrva, ten bunas, or mwnda, to a</p>  
 <p>Cadrawd, ten cattyrvas.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">From the Book of Mr. Cobb of Cardiff.</div>  
 <p>These are called the ten degrees of Numerals; and it is from being acquainted with every movement and treatment of the numerals that every truth in respect of number, measure, and weight is known.</p>
