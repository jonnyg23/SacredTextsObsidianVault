 <p><small>THE Soul Shrine is sung by the people as they retire to rest. They say that the angels of heaven guard them in sleep and shield them from harm. Should any untoward event <a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="green">p. 91</font></a> occur to themselves or to their flocks, they avow that the cause was the deadness of their hearts, the coldness of their faith, and the fewness of their prayers.</small></p>

 <p>DHE tabhair   aithne da t’ ainghle beannaichte,<br>        Caim a chumail air an staing-sa nochd,<br>   Comachadh crabhaidh, tabhaidh, teannachaidh,<br>        Chumas a choich anama-sa bho lochd.</p>   
 <p>Teasruig a Dhe   an t-ardrach seo a nochd,<br>        Iad fein ’s an cuid ’s an cliu,<br>   Tar iad o eug, o ghabhadh, o lochd,<br>        ’S o thoradh na farmaid ’s na   mi-ruin.</p>   
 <p>Tabhair duinn,   a Dhe na fois,<br>        Taingealachd an cois ar call,<br>   Bhi coimhlionadh do lagh a bhos,<br>        ’S to fein a mhealtuinn thall.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>GOD, give   charge to Thy blessed angels,<br>        To keep guard around this stead to-night,<br>   A band sacred, strong, and steadfast,<br>        That will shield this soul-shrine from harm.</p>   
 <p>Safeguard Thou,   God, this household to-night,<br>        Themselves and their means and their fame,<br>   Deliver them from death, from distress, from harm,<br>        From the fruits of envy and of enmity.</p>   
 <p>Give Thou to   us, O God of peace,<br>        Thankfulness despite our loss,<br>   To obey Thy statutes here below,<br>        And to enjoy Thyself above.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
