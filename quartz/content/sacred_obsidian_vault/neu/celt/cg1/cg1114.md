 <p>AN crodh an   diugh a dol imirig,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>           Ho ro la ill o,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>   Dol a dh’ itheadh feur na cille,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>   Am buachaille fein ann ’g an iomain,<br>           Ho ro la ill o,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>   ’G an cuallach, ’g an cuart, ’g an tilleadh,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>   Bride bhith-gheal bhi ’g am blighinn,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>   Muire mhin-gheal bhi ’g an glidheadh,<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o,<br>   ’S Iosa Criosda air chinn an slighe,<br>     Iosa Criosda air chinn an slighe.<br>           Hill-i-ruin is o h-ug o.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">

 <p>THE cattle are   to-day going a-flitting,<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o h-ug o,<br>           Ho ro la ill o,<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o   h-ūg o,<br>   Going to eat the grass of the burial-place,<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o   h-ūg o,<br>   Their own herdsman there to tend them,<br>           Ho ro la ill o,<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o   h-ūg o,<br>   Tending them, fending them, turning them,<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o   h-ūg o,<br>   Be the gentle Bride milking them,<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o   h-ūg o,<br>   Be the lovely Mary keeping them,<br>           Hill i-rūin is o   h-ūg o,<br>   And Jesu Christ at the end of their journey,<br>     Jesu Christ at the end of their journey.<br>           Hill-i-rūin is o   h-ūg o.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
