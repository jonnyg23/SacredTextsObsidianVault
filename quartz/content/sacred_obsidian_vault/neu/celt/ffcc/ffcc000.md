 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Fairy-Faith in Celtic Countries</i>, by W.Y. Evans-Wentz, [1911], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> 
 <p><a name="page_iii"><font size="1" color="green">p. iii</font></a></p> 
 <p align="CENTER">THIS BOOK</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">DEPENDS CHIEFLY UPON THE ORAL AND WRITTEN TESTIMONY<br> SO FREELY CONTRIBUTED BY ITS MANY CELTIC AUTHORS,--<br> THE PEASANT AND THE SCHOLAR, THE PRIEST AND THE SCIENTIST,<br> THE POET AND THE BUSINESS MAN, THE SEER AND THE NON-SEER,--<br> AND IN HONOUR OF THEM</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">I DEDICATE<br> <br> IT TO</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">TWO OF THE BRETHREN IN IRELAND</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">A.E.,</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">WHOSE UNWAVERING LOYALTY TO THE FAIRY-FAITH<br> HAS INSPIRED MUCH THAT I HAVE HEREIN WRITTEN,<br> WHOSE FRIENDLY GUIDANCE IN MY STUDY OF IRISH MYSTICISM<br> I MOST GRATEFULLY ACKNOWLEDGE;<br> AND</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">WILLIAM BUTLER YEATS,</p> 
 <p align="CENTER">WHO BROUGHT ME AT MY OWN ALMA MATER IN CALIFORNIA<br> THE FIRST MESSAGE FROM FAIRYLAND,<br> AND WHO AFTERWARDS IN HIS OWN COUNTRY<br> LED ME THROUGH THE HAUNTS OF FAIRY KINGS AND QUEENS.</p> 
 <p align="CENTER"> </p> 
 <p>Oxford<br>   <i>November</i> 1911.</p>

SIR JOHN RHY^S.</p>
