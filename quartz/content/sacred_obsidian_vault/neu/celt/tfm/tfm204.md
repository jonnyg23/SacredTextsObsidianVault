 <p align="CENTER">FATHER CUDDY'S SONG</p>
 <blockquote> </blockquote>
 <blockquote>IN THE LEGEND OF CLOUGH NA CUDDY.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote>
 <blockquote>QUAM pulchra sunt ova,<br>
 Cum alba et nova<br>
 In stabulo scite leguntur;<br>
 Et à Margery bells,<br>
 Quae festiva puella!<br>
 Pinguis lardi cum frustis coquuntur.</blockquote>
 <blockquote>Ut belles in prato<br>
 Aprico et lato<br>
 Sub sole tam laete renident,<br>
 Ova tosta, in mensa<br>
 Mappa bene extensa,<br>
 Nitidissima lance consident.</blockquote>
 <blockquote> </blockquote>
 <blockquote>TRANSLATION.</blockquote>
 <blockquote>Oh! 'tis eggs are a treat,<br>
 When so white and so sweet<br>
 From under the manger they 're taken, <br>
 And by fair Margery,<br>
 Och! 'tis she's full of glee,<br>
 They are fried with fat rashers of bacon.</blockquote>
 <blockquote>Just like daisies all spread<br>
 O'er a broad sunny mead,<br>
 In the sunbeams so beauteously shining, <br>
 Are fried eggs fair displayed<br>
 On a dish, when we've laid<br>
 The cloth and are thinking of dining.</blockquote>
