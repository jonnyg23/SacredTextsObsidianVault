Winifred Faraday, [1904], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Muster of the Ulstermen</span></h3>  
 <p>'Arise, O Findchad, I will send thee to Deda,' etc. <a name="fr_101"></a><a href="#fn_98"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">.      .      .      .      .      .      .</p>  
 <p>It was not difficult for Findchad to take his message, for they were, the whole province of Conchobar, every chief of them, awaiting Conchobar; every one was then east and north and west of Emain. When they were there, they all came till they were at Emain Macha. When they were there, they heard the uprising of Conchobar in Emain. They went past Emain southwards after the host. Their first march then was from Emain to Irard Cuillend.</p>  
 <p>'What are you waiting for here?' said Conchobar.</p>  
 <p>'Waiting for your sons,' said the host. 'They have gone with thirty with them to Temair to seek Eirc, son of Coirpre Niafer and Fedelm Noicride. Till their two cantreds should come to us, we will not go from this place.'</p>  
 <p>'I will not remain indeed,' said Conchobar, 'till the men of Ireland know that I have awaked from the sickness in which I was.'</p>
