 <p><a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="green">p. 18</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE DEATH OF FRAOCH <a name="fr_53"></a><a href="#fn_53"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p>On Cluan Fraoich <a name="fr_54"></a><a href="#fn_54"><font size="1">2</font></a> a friend doth sigh<br> Where doth lie a warrior low<br> On his bier;<br> And his moan makes warriors grieve<br> And bereft of love his spouse.<br> For Idad's son she doth keen<br> For whom is named Cairn Laive:<br> Fraoch mac Idad of soft locks,<br> Idad's son of raven hair.<br> Westward there lies Fraoch mac Idad<br> Who fulfilled proud Mève's behest.<br> On Cruachan Shee (Sídh) a mother weeps:<br> Sad the talea mother's wail<br> She grieves sore for Fraoch her son.<br> Many a field in strifes of old<br> He had won and behold<br> Fraoch mac Idad lieth cold.</p>  
 <p>To Cluan Fraoich comes Find-abair:<br> She doth weepa sad ladye;<br> With tresses soft and curling locks<br> And her hand<br> Of Queen Mève proud heroes sought.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_19"><font size="1" color="green">p. 19</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">FRAOCH <a name="fr_55"></a><a href="#fn_55"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Auctor hujus an Caoch O Cluain</i></p>  
 <p>1. H-osnadh caraid an Cluan Fhraoich <br>    H-osnadh laoich an caiseal chró<br>    H-osnadh dheanann tuirseach fear,<br>    Agus dan guileann bean óg.</p>  
 <p>2. Aig so shear <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">2</font></a> an carp fan bh-feil<br>    Fraoch mac Fhiodhaich an fhuilt mhaoith,<br>    Fear a rinn buidheachas baoibh<br>    Is bhon sloinntear Carn Fraoich.</p>  
 <p>3. Gul aon mhna an Cruachan soir<br>    Truagh an sgeul fa bh-feil a bhean<br>    Is se bheir a h-osnadh gu trom<br>    Fraoch mac Fiodhaich nan colg sean.</p>  
 <p>4. Si n aon bhean do nidh an gul<br>    Ag dol da fhios gu Cluan Fraoich,<br>    Fionnabhair an fhuilt chais ail<br>    Inghean Mhaoidhbh <a name="fr_57"></a><a href="#fn_57"><font size="1">3</font></a> gam biaid laoich.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="green">p. 20</font></a></p>  
 <p>Find-abair of golden hair<br> Ailill's one daughter she<br> Lies side by Fraoch to-night:<br> Of many loved, of many sought<br> But never a love<br> But Fraoch had Find-abair.</p>  
 <p>Her cause of hatred unprovoked Mève found<br> For Fraoch the best of knights,<br> Bravest and friendliest:<br> When love for him she found<br> Her passion he did scorn<br> And hence his wound:<br> Fraoch lies a corpse to-night.<br> Great was the wrong thus wrought by Mève:<br> Simply we still unfold<br> The story old:<br> (With woman-kind side not in ill)<br> His death her scheme foretold.<br>           (On Cluan Fraoich a friend doth sigh.)</p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p>A rowan tree grew on Loch Mève<br>   Southwards is seen the shore<br> Every fourth and every month<br>   Ripe fruit the rowan bore:<br> Fruit more sweet than honey-comb,<br> Its clusters virtues strong,<br> Its berries red could one but taste<br> Hunger they staved off long.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="green">p. 21</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Inghean Orla <a name="fr_58"></a><a href="#fn_58"><font size="1">1</font></a> as úr folt<br>    Is Fraoch an nochd taobh air thaobh<br>    Ge mór fear dha dtairgeadh i<br>    Nior ghrádhaich si fear ach Fraoch.</p>  
 <p>6. Faigheas Meadhbh a muigh fuath<br>    Cairdeas Fhraoich fa fearr an gliadh,<br>    A chúis fan chreuchd-ta a chorp<br>    Tre gun lochd a dhèanamh ria.</p>  
 <p>7. Do chuireadh e gu sa bhás<br>    Taobh re mnaibh na tug an olc<br>    Is mór am pudhar a thuit le Meadhbh<br>    Inneósad gun cheilg a nos.<br>                                    H-osnadh.</p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p>8. Caorrunn do bhi air Loch Mái,<br>    Do chidhmist an traigh fa dheas;<br>    Gach[a] ráidh [agus] gach mí,<br>    Toradh abaidh do bhi air.</p>  
 <p>9. Sásadh bídh na caora sin,<br>    Ba mhillse na mhil a bhláth;<br>    Do chongbhfadh an caorrann dearg<br>    Fear gun bhiadh gu ceann naoi tráth.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="green">p. 22</font></a></p>  
 <p>Its berries juice and fruit when red<br> For a year would life prolong:<br> From dread disease it gave relief<br> If what is told be our belief.</p>  
 <p>Yet though it proved a means of life<br>   Peril lay closely nigh;<br> Coiled by its root a dragon lay<br>   Forbidding passage by.</p>  
 <p>A messenger for Fraoch was sent<br>   By Eochaidh's daughter keen<br> When sickness sore Mève rent:<br>   "What ails?" quoth Fraoch, "the Queen?"</p>  
 <p>And Eochaidh's daughter made reply<br>   Eochaidh of the festive horns<br> That neer would she be whole<br>   Till her soft palm were full<br> Of berries from the island in the lake<br>   Fraoch's hand alone to pull.</p>  
 <p>"Such I neer culld," said Idad's son<br>   Of blushing face;<br> Yet will I what I yet neer willed,"<br>   Quoth Fraoch, out of grace.</p>  
 <p>Sir Fraoch moved forward to his fate<br>   Forth to the lake and swam the tide;<br> He found asleep the dragon-snake<br>   Around the tree, mouth open wide.<br>          (On Cluan Fraoich a friend doth sigh.)</p>  
 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="green">p. 23</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Bliadhain air shaoghal gach fir<br>    Do chuireadh sin fa sgeal dearbh<br>    Gum bu fhóirinn do luchd cneidh<br>    Fromhadh a mheas is e dearg.</p>  
 <p>11. Do bhi anshástacht <a name="fr_59"></a><a href="#fn_59"><font size="1">1</font></a> na dhéigh<br>    Ge ba leigh a chobhar an t-sluaigh,<br>    Péist nimh do bhi na bhun <a name="fr_60"></a><a href="#fn_60"><font size="1">2</font></a><br>    Bhacadh dha cách dhul da bhuain.</p>  
 <p>12. Léan easlainte throm throm<br>    Inghean Eochaidh nan corn saor,<br>    Do chuireadh fios leath air Fraoch,<br>    Dh fhiosraich an laoch ciod thainig ri.</p>  
 <p>13. A dubhairt Meadhbh nach bi slán<br>    Mur faigh lán a boise maoith<br>    Do chaoraibh an locha fhuair<br>    Gun duine ga bhuain ach Fraoch.</p>  
 <p>14. Cnuasachd riamh ni dhearnadh mi<br>    Ar Mac Fiodhaich go ngné dheirg<br>    Ge gar dhéarnas e ar Fraoch<br>    Rachsad do bhuain chaor do Mheidhbh.</p>  
 <p>15. Gluaiseas Fraoch, be fear an áigh<br>    Bhuain a shnámh air an loch<br>    Fhuair a phéist is i na suain<br>    Is a ceann suas ris an dos.<br>                                 H-osnadh.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="green">p. 24</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p>Fraoch, Idad's son, of weapon keen<br>   Of the beast being unperceived,<br> Of berries red a lapful brought<br>   Mève's longing to relieve.</p>  
 <p>Though good be that which thou hast brought,"<br>   Quoth Mève of form so fair,<br> "Nought me relieves, O Champion bold<br>   Save branch from trunk thou bear."</p>  
 <p>Fraoch gave consent: no fear he knew<br>   But swam the lake once more:<br> But hero never yet did pass<br>   The fate for him in store.</p>  
 <p>The rowan by the top he seized<br>   From root he pulled the tree;<br> And the monster of the lake perceived<br>   As Fraoch from the land made free.</p>  
 <p>With his gaping maw the hero's hand<br>   He seized in the liquid tide:<br> Fraoch seized the monster by the jaw,<br>   Would a knife were by his side!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="green">p. 25</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p>16. Fraoch mac Fiodhaich an airm ghéir<br>    Tháinig on phéist gun fhios di,<br>    Thug a h-anultach chaor dearg<br>    Far an robh Meadhbh dh a tigh.</p>  
 <p>17. "Ach ge maith na tugais leat,"<br>    Adubhairt Meadhbh as geal cruth,<br>    "Ni fhóir mis, a laoich luain<br>    Ach slat a bhuain as a bun."</p>  
 <p>18. Togras Fraoch, s nior ghille tiom,<br>    Shnámh a rís air an linn bhuig<br>    Is nior fheud [ne]ach <a name="fr_61"></a><a href="#fn_61"><font size="1">1</font></a> ge mór ágh<br>    Theachd on bhás an robh a chuid.</p>  
 <p>19. Gabhas an caorrann air bharr,<br>    Tharruing an crann as a fhrèimh,<br>    Tabhairt dó [a] chos don tór<br>    Mothaicheas do ris a phéist.</p>  
 <p>20. Beireas air agus e air snamh,<br>    Is gabhas a lamh na craos,<br>    Do ghabh se-san is air ghiall,<br>    Truagh gun an sgian aig Fraoch!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="green">p. 26</font></a></p>  
 <p>Find-abair of lovely tresses<br>   For Sir Fraoch her love,<br> Unperceived, a knife she bore;<br>   Fraoch's fair skin the monster tore<br>     And gnawing shore his arm away.</p>  
 <p>Fraoch, Idad's son, in conflict dire<br>   With the monster's woeful ire:<br> On the southern strand they fought and fell<br>   And blood the boulders dyed.</p>  
 <p>Nor short the conflict: in his hand<br>   Fraoch held the monster's head;<br> Which when the maiden did perceive<br>   On the strand she swooned as dead.</p>  
 <p>The maid then spake as she awoke<br>   In her palm his hand she placed,<br> "Though now but food for birds-of-prey,<br>   Thy renown on earth is traced."</p>  
 <p>And from the death the hero died<br>   The lake doth take its name;<br> For ever is it hight Loch Mève,<br>   And thus resounds his fame.<br>        (On Cluan Fraoich a friend doth sigh.)</p>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. Fionnabhair an fhuilt chais áil<br>    Do ran chuige sgian gun fhoir,<br>    Liodair a phéist a chneas ban<br>    Is theasgadh a lámh air leodh.</p>  
 <p>22. Do thuiteadar bonn re bonn<br>    Air traigh nan clach corr fo dheas;<br>    Fraoch mac Fiodhaich is a phéist,<br>    Truagh, a Dhé, mar thug an treas!</p>  
 <p>23. Ga cómhrag ni cómhrag géarr:<br>    Do rug leis a ceann na laimh;<br>    Nar chunnaic an nighean e<br>    Do chaidh na neul air an traigh.</p>  
 <p>24. Éireas an nighean on tamh,<br>    Gabhas an lamh, ba lamh bhog;<br>    Ge ta so na cuid nan eun <a name="fr_62"></a><a href="#fn_62"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>    Is mór an t-euchd a rinn a bhos.</p>  
 <p>25. On bhás sin do fhuair am fear<br>    Loch Maidhbh gun lean den loch<br>    Ata an t-ainm sin deth gu luan<br>    Ga ghairm a nuas gus a nos.<br>                          H-osnadh charaid.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p>His body to Cluan Fraoich is borne<br>   A hero on his bier laid low;<br> And still the mead his name makes known<br>   Ah! pity the survivor's woe.</p>  
 <p>Cairn-of-the-Hand beside me here<br>   Is named from Fraoch Cairn Laive,<br> Back he neer turned his hand but fought<br>   The foremost when alive.</p>  
 <p>Belovd the mouth that friends neer scorned<br>   The lips which woman's lips had pressed;<br> Belovd the chief, of hosts the lord,<br>   Belovd the cheeks the rosiest.</p>  
 <p>Cheek redder than the blood of fawn<br>   Hair darker than the raven's crest,<br> And softer than the streaming foam,<br>   Whiter than snow Fraoch's waist.</p>  
 <p>More fringed than meadow-sweet his locks,<br>   Than violet his eye more blue;<br> Than rowans ripe his lips more red,<br>   Whiter his teeth than woodbine hue.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="green">p. 29</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p>26. Beirear an sin gu Cluan Fraoich<br>    Corp an laoich gu caisil chró;<br>    Air an gcluain tugadh a ainm<br>    Is mairg a mhaireas d[a éis beó].</p>  
 <p>27. Carn-laimh, an carn so re m thaobh<br>    O laimh Fhraoich do bhidheast son,<br>    Fear nar iompodhadh an treas<br>    Fear ba dheise neart an trod.</p>  
 <p>28. Ionmhuinn am beul nar ob dáimh<br>    Dam bidis mnai a toirbheart phóg<br>    Ionmhuinn Tighearna nan sluagh<br>    Ionmhuinn gruaidh nar dheirg an rós.</p>  
 <p>29. Duibhe na fitheach barr a fhuilt<br>    Deirge a ghruaidh na fuil laoigh<br>    Fa mhine na cobhar sruth<br>    Gile na an sneachd cneas Fhraoich.</p>  
 <p>30. Caise na an caisean fholt<br>    Guirme a rosg na eidhr-leac <a name="fr_63"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">1</font></a><br>    Deirge na partan a bheul<br>    Gile a dheud na bláith feith.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="green">p. 30</font></a></p>  
 <p>  Than mast his spear was higher; his voice<br> More musical than lute:<br>   No swimmer that with Fraoch could vie<br> His side by water put.</p>  
 <p>  Broader than door-leaf was his shield,<br> Whoso could wield it, happy lord!<br>   Long as his lance the arm of Fraoch<br> Than ship's plate more broad his sword.</p>  
 <p>Would that Fraoch by heroes bold,<br>   The bestower of goldfell;<br> Alas and alas! through a monster's hold<br>   We hear his funeral knell.<br>            (On Cluan Fraoich a friend doth sigh.)</p>  
 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="green">p. 31</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. Aird a shleagh na cranna siúil<br>    Binne na teud chiúil a ghuth<br>    Snamhaidhe do bfhearr na Fraoch<br>    Cha do shín a thaobh re sruth.</p>  
 <p>32. Ba leithn na comhla a sgiath<br>    Ionmhuinn triath a bhi re druim<br>    Co fad a lann is a lamh<br>    Leithn a cholg na clár dhe luing.</p>  
 <p>33. Truagh nach an comhrag re laoich<br>    Do thuit Fraoch a phronnadh ór<br>    Tuirs sin a thuiteam le péist<br>    Truagh, a Dhé, nach maireann fós.<br>                                  H-osnadh.</p>
