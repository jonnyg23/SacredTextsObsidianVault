 <h3 align="CENTER">XXVI.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BLACK BOOK OF CAERMARTHEN VIII.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>I. THE three depredatory horses of the Isle of Prydain:--<br>
 Carnawlawg, the horse of Owain the son of Urien;<br>
 Bucheslwm Seri, the horse of Gwgawn Gleddyvrudd;<br>
 And Tavawd hir Breich-hir, the horse of Cadwallawn the son of Cadvan.</p>
 <p>II. The three draught-horses of the Isle of Prydain:--<br>
 Arvul Melyn, the horse of Pasgen the son of Urien;<br>
 Du Hir Terwenydd, the horse of Selyv the son of Cynan Garwyn;<br>
 And Drudlwyd, the horse of Rhydderch Hael.</p>
 <p><a name="page_307"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 307</font></a></p>
 <p>III. The three spirited horses of the Isle of Prydain:--<br>
 Gwineu Goddwf Hir, the horse of Cai;<br>
 Rhuthr Eon Tuth Blaidd, the horse of Gilbert the son of Cadagyffro;<br>
 And Ceincaled, the horse of Gwalchmai.</p>
 <p>IV. The three high-mettled horses of the Isle of Prydain:--<br>
 Lluagor, the horse of Caradawg;<br>
 And Melynlas, the horse of Caswallawn the son of Beli.</p>
