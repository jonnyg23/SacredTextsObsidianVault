 <h3 align="CENTER">XVI.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BLACK BOOK OF CAERMARTHEN XXXV.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>HORSEMAN resorts to the city,<br>
 With his white dogs, and large horns;<br>
 I, who have not before seen thee, know thee not.</p>
 <p><a name="page_289"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 289</font></a></p>
 <p>II. A horseman resorts to the river's mouth,<br>
 On a stout and warlike steed;<br>
 Come with me, let me not be refused.</p>
 <p>III. I will not go that way at present;<br>
 Bear with the conduct of the delayer;<br>
 And may the blessing of heaven and earth come (upon thee).</p>
 <p>IV, Thou, who hast not seen me daily,<br>
 And who resemblest a prudent man,<br>
 How long wilt thou absent thyself, and when wilt thou come?</p>
 <p>V. When I return from Caer Seon,<br>
 From contending with Jews,<br>
 I will come to the city of Lleu and Gwidion.</p>
 <p>VI. Come with me into the city,<br>
 Thou shalt have wine which I have set apart,<br>
 And pure gold on thy clasp.</p>
 <p>VII. I know not the confident man,<br>
 Who owns a fire and a couch;<br>
 Fairly and sweetly dost thou speak.</p>
 <p>VIII. Come with me to my dwelling,<br>
 Thou shalt have high foaming wine.<br>
 My name is Ugnach, the son of Mydno.</p>
 <p>IX. Ugnach! a blessing on thy throne!<br>
 And mayst thou have grace and honour!<br>
 I am Taliessin who will repay thee thy banquet.</p>
 <p><a name="page_290"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 290</font></a></p>
 <p>X. Taliessin, chief of men.<br>
 Victor in the contest of song,<br>
 Remain here until Wednesday.</p>
 <p>XI. Ugnach! the most affluent in riches,<br>
 Grace be to thee from the highest region;<br>
 I will not deserve blame; I will not tarry.</p>
