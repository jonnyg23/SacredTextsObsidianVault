 <h2 align="CENTER">VI.</h2>
 <h2 align="CENTER">MISCELLANEOUS POEMS FROM THE RED BOOK OF HERGEST.</h2>
 <h2 align="CENTER">X.</h2>
 <h2 align="CENTER">POEMS ATTRIBUTED TO LLYWARCH HEN.</h2>
 <h3 align="CENTER">CVII.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">RED BOOK OF HERGEST V.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>I. LET the cock's comb be red; naturally loud<br>
 Be his voice, from his triumphant bed:<br>
 Man's rejoicing, God will recommend.</p>
 <p>II. Let the swineherds be merry at the sighing<br>
 Of the wind; let the silent be graceful;<br>
 Let the vicious be accustomed to misfortune.</p>
 <p>III. Let the bailiff impeach; let evil be a tormentor;<br>
 Let clothes be fitting;<br>
 He that loves a bard, let him be a handsome giver.</p>
 <p>IV. Let a monarch be vehement, and let him be brave;<br>
 And let there be a hurdle on the gap;<br>
 He will not show his face that will not give.</p>
 <p><a name="page_570"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 570</font></a></p>
 <p>V. Fleet let the racers be on the side<br>
 Of the mountain; let care be in the bosom;<br>
 Unfaithful let the inconstant be.</p>
 <p>VI. Let the knight be conspicuous; let the thief be wary;<br>
 The rich woman may be deceived;<br>
 The friend of the wolf is the lazy shepherd.</p>
 <p>VII. Let the knight be conspicuous: fleet be the horse;<br>
 Let the scholar be ambitious;<br>
 Let the prevaricating one be unfaithful.</p>
 <p>VIII. Let cows be round-backed; let the wolf be gray;<br>
 Let the horse over barley be swift;<br>
 Like gossamer will he press the grain at the roots.</p>
 <p>IX. Let the deaf be bent; let the captive be heavy<br>
 Nimble the horse in battles;<br>
 Like gossamer will he press the grain the ground.</p>
 <p>X. Let the deaf be dubious; let the rash be inconstant;<br>
 Let the mischievous wrangle;<br>
 The prudent need but be seen to be loved.</p>
 <p>XI. Let the lake be deep; let the spears be sharp;<br>
 Let the brow of the sick be bold at the shout of war;<br>
 Let the wise be happy--God commends him.</p>
 <p>XII. Let the exile wander; let the brave be impulsive;<br>
 Let the fool be fond of laughter.</p>
 <p>XIII. Let the furrows be wet; let bail be frequent;<br>
 Let the sick be complaining, and the one in health merry;<br>
 Let the lapdog snarl; let the hag be peevish.</p>
 <p><a name="page_571"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 571</font></a></p>
 <p>XIV. Let him that is in pain cry out; let an army be moving;<br>
 Let the well-fed be wanton;<br>
 Let the strong be bold; let the hill be icy.</p>
 <p>XV. Let the gull be white; let the wave be loud;<br>
 Let the gore be apt to clot on the ashen spear.<br>
 Let the ice be gray; let the heart be bold.</p>
 <p>XVI. Let the camp be green; let the suitor be reproachless;<br>
 Let there be pushing of spears in the defile;<br>
 Let the bad woman be with frequent reproaches.</p>
 <p>XVII. Let the hen be clawed; let the lion roar;<br>
 Let the foolish be pugnacious;<br>
 Let the heart be broken with grief.</p>
 <p>XVIII. Let the tower be white; let the harness glitter;<br>
 Let there be beauty--many will desire it;<br>
 Let the glutton hanker; let the old man mediate.</p>
