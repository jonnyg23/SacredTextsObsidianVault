 <h3 align="CENTER">CVIII.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">RED BOOK OF HERGEST VI.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p>I. USUAL is wind from the south; usual is noise;<br>
 In the village usual for the weakling to be slender;<br>
 Usual for a man to inquire after news.<br>
 Usual for a foster-child to have dainties.</p>
 <p>II. Usual is wind from the east; usual for a man with swelling breast to be<br>
 Proud; usual for the thrush to be among thorns; <a name="page_572"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 572</font></a><br>
 Usual against oppression is an outcry;<br>
 Usual for crows to find flesh in a nook.</p>
 <p>III. Usual is wind from the north; usual for maids to be<br>
 Lovely; usual, a handsome main in Gwynedd;<br>
 Usual for a prince to provide a feast;<br>
 Usual after drinking is derangement of the senses.</p>
 <p>IV. Usual is wind from the sea; usual for the high tide to<br>
 Overflow; usual for a sow to breed vermin;<br>
 Usual for swine to turn up the ground for earth-nuts.</p>
 <p>V. Usual is wind from the mountain; usual a plash<br>
 In the plain; usual to find thatch in the meadows<br>
 .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .    .<br>
 Usual are leaves, tender shoots, and trees.</p>
 <p>VI. Usual an eagle's nest in the top of the oak,<br>
 And in the congress-house, men of renown;<br>
 The eye of the fond one is on whom he loves.</p>
 <p>VII. Usual is the day with a. blazing fire in the hurried season<br>
 Of winter, with the eloquent men of spears;<br>
 Usual for the hearth of the faithless to be a desert.</p>
 <p>VIII. Dried is the reed; there is flood in the brook;<br>
 The commerce of the Saxon is with money;<br>
 Unhappy is the soul of the mother of unfaithful children.</p>
 <p>IX. The leaf is driven by the wind;<br>
 Woe to it as to its fate;<br>
 It is old--this year it was born.</p>
 <p><a name="page_573"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 573</font></a></p>
 <p>X. Though it may be small, yet ingeniously<br>
 Do the birds build in the summit of trees<br>
 Of equal age will be the good and the happy.</p>
 <p>XI. Cold and wet is the mountain; cold and gray the ice;<br>
 Trust in God--he will not deceive thee;<br>
 Persevering patience will not leave thee long afflicted.</p>
