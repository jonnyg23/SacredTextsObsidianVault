 <h2 align="CENTER">IV.</h2>
 <h2 align="CENTER">MISCELLANEOUS POEMS FROM THE BOOK OF ANEURIN.</h2>
 <h2 align="CENTER">T.</h2>
 <h2 align="CENTER">POEM CONTAINING ANCIENT PROVERBS.</h2>
 <h3 align="CENTER">LXXXVIII.</h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER">BOOK OF ANEURIN III.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"></p>
 <p align="CENTER">HERE BEGINNETH THE GWARCHAN OF ADEBON.</p>
 <p>THE apple will not fall far from the apple-tree.<br>
 The diligent cannot prosper with the prodigal.<br>
 The naked will not be bold among thistles.<br>
 All, when made to swear overmuch, will fail.<br>
 Would I love him who would love the rapacious?<br>
 Death will not occur twice.<br>
 His speech is of no use to the dumb.<br>
 Thou wilt not delight to put one of the same language in fear.<br>
 The horses of an effeminate person are his dainties.<br>
 10     .     .     .     .     .     .     .<br>
 At home peace has been lost.<br>
 Be thy mansion large, thou wert a hero in the day of conflict.<br>
 As long as there will be things to seek for thee there will be seekers.<br>
 High stones, a reaping to the foe.<br>
 The conclusion of the Gwarchan of Adebon.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">AND SO ENDETH THE GWARCHAN OF ADEBON.</p>
