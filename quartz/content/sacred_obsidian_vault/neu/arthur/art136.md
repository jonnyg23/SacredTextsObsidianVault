 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER"> THE TERRIBLE TEST </h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ELIZABETH STUART PHELPS</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br>  Separate, upon the folded page<br>       Of myth or marvel, sad or glad,<br>  The test that gave the Lord to thee,<br>       And thee to us, O Galahad!<br>  <br>  "Found pure in deed, and word, and thought,"<br>       The creature of our dream and guess,<br>  The vision of the brain thou art,<br>       The eidolon of holiness.<br>  <br>  Man with the power of the God,<br>       Man with the weaknesses of men,<br>  Whose lips the Sangreal leaned to feed,<br>       "Whose strength was the strength of ten."<br>  <br>  We read--and smile; no man thou wast;<br>       No human pulses thine could be;<br>  With downcast eyes we read--and sigh;<br>       So terrible is purity!<br>  <br>  O fairest legend of the years,<br>       With folded wings, go, silently!<br>  O flower of knighthood, yield your place<br>       To One who comes from Galilee!<br>      &amp;nbsp         &amp;nbsp     ---<br>  To wounded feet that shrink and bleed,<br>       But press and climb the narrow way,--<br>  The same old way our own must step,<br>       Forever, yesterday, to-day.<br>  <br>  For soul can be what soul hath been,<br>       And feet can tread where feet have trod.<br>  Enough, to know that once the clay<br>       Hath worn the features of the God.<br>
