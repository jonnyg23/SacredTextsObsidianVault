 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE LAST LOVE OF GAWAINE</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">RICHARD HOVEY</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> You will betray me--oh, deny it not!<br> What right have I, alas, to say you nay?<br> I, traitor of ten loves, what shall I say<br> To plead with you that I be not forgot?<br> My love has not been squandered jot by jot<br> In little loves that perish with the day.<br> My treason has been ever to the sway<br> Of queens; my faith has known no petty blot.<br> You will betray me, as I have betrayed,<br> And I shall kiss the hand that does me wrong.<br> And oh, not pardon--I need pardon more--<br> But in proud torment, grim and unafraid,<br> Burn in my hell nor cease the bitter song<br> Your beauty triumphs in forevermore.<br>
