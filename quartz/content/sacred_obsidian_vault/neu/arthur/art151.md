 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">TIMOR MORTIS</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">ERNEST RHYS</h2>  </center>  <br>   <br>                           "The thing that I feared is fallen upon me."<br> <br>  <br>  When deadly flesh, oh knight, shall see<br>     The spiritual things,<br>  The samite cloth, the Mystery,<br>     The long street where the wings<br>  Of eagles are the minstrelsy,<br>         And winnow death, like dust away<br>             Upon a windy day.<br>  <br>  Then, if thine arm, like Galahad,<br>     And thy heart tremble too,<br>  Heave up, oh knight, thy hands, full glad<br>     To know the death he knew<br>  The samite drawn, the Grail unclad<br>         Logris and beyond the sea<br>             That sails to Ar'mathie.<br>  <br>  There, healed and solaced by the Grail,<br>     Thy wounds shall hurt not so:<br>  But He, that Knight whom men did nail<br>     Upon the tree shall show<br>  Those wounds they made, that brow left pale<br>         By death, which call and bid thee come<br>             Safe, oh knight errant, home.<br> <br>
