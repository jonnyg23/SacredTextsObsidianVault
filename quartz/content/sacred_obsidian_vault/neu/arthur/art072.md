 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">SONNET XXIII</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">FULKE GREVILLE</h2>  </center>  <br>  <i> Merlin</i> , they say, an English Prophet borne,<br>  When he was yong and gouern'd by his Mother,<br>  Took great delight to laugh such fooles to scorne,<br>  As thought, by Nature we might know a Brother.<br>  <br>  His Mother chid him oft, till on a day,<br>  They stood, and saw a Coarse to buriall carried,<br>  The Father teares his beard, doth weepe and pray;<br>  The Mother was the woman he had married.<br>  <br>  <i> Merlin</i>  laughs out aloud in stead of crying;<br>  His Mother chides him for that childish fashion;<br>  Sayes, Men must mourne the dead, themselues are dying,<br>  <i> Good manners doth make answer vnto passion.</i> <br>  <br>  The Child (for children see what should be hidden)<br>  Replies vnto his Mother by and by,<br>  "Mother, if you did know, and were forbidden,<br>  Yet you would laugh as heartily, as I.<br>  <br>  This Man no part hath in the child he sorrowes,<br>  His Father was the Monke that sings before him:<br>  See then how Nature of Adoption borrowes,<br>  Truth couets in me, that I should restore him.<br>               <i> True fathers singing, supposed fathers crying,<br>               I thinke make women laugh, that lye a-dying."</i> <br>
