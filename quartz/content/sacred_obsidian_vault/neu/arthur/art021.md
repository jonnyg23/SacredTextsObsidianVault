 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">ARTHUR IN AVALON</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">J. ARTHUR BLAIKIE</h2>  FROM THE PICTURE BY T. ARCHER, R.S.A.</center>  <br>  <br>                                     I.<br>  <br>  STRICKEN of man, and sore beset of Fate,<br>       He lies amid the groves of Avalon;<br>       What comfort mete ye unto Uther's son,<br>  O mournful Queens? What styptic to abate<br>  Life's eager stream? Alas, not theirs to sate<br>       His soul with earthly vision! he hath done<br>       With mortal life, and chivalry's bright sun<br>  Is darkened by the powers of hell and hate.<br>  <br>  Lo! now, the garden of his agony<br>       Is very sweet, though dread the hour, and drear<br>  With utterless spell of horrid potency;<br>  The barrèd east beyond the brightening sea,<br>       Thick with portentous wraiths of phantom fear,<br>  Is flushed with triumph, stirred with melody.<br>  <br>  <br>                                     II.<br>  <br>  "Glory of knighthood; that through Lyonesse<br>       Was as a lamp, O selfless soul and pure,<br>       What though thy visionary rule endure<br>  So ill the assault of envy? Not the less<br>  Thy victory, though failure thee oppress;<br>       Not sterile thy example, and most sure<br>       The seeded fruit; with might thou shalt allure<br>  For evermore through life's embattled press<br>  <br>  Thy spiritual sons to follow thee;"<br>       The mystic Four their solemn vigil keep<br>  Until day break, and eastward silently,<br>       Over the kingless land and wailing deep,<br>  The sacrificial symbol fire the sky;<br>       Then they arise, no more to watch and weep.<br>  <br>
