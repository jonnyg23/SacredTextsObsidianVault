 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">When Tristan Sailed</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">Helen Hay Whitney</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> When Tristan sailed from Ireland<br>    Across the summer sea,<br> How young he was, how debonnaire,<br>    How glad he was and free.<br> Why should he know the gales would blow,<br>    The skies be black above,<br> How should he dream his port was Death,<br>    And Doom, whose name is Love?<br> <br> The Lady Iseult, sweet as prayer,<br>    We hardly dare to pray,<br> Pearl-pale beneath her shadow hair,<br>    Grows fairer day by day,<br> The ichor gains her spring-kissed veins,<br>    Her skies the eyes of youth.<br> How should she dream the ichor Love,<br>    Was hellebore in truth?<br> <br> So Tristan sailed from Ireland<br>    As youth must always sail;<br> He quaffed the cup, nor asked the wine;<br>    He dared, nor feared to fail.<br> And be it poison, be it life,<br>    Or wrecks that strew the shore,<br> Tristan set forth! nor ask the end,<br>    Else youth shall sail no more.
