 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">MERLIN'S SONG</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">RALPH WALDO EMERSON</h2>  </center>  <br>   <br> Of Merlin wise I learned a song,--<br> Sing it low, or sing it loud,<br> It is mightier than the strong,<br> And punishes the proud.<br> I sing it to the surging crowd,--<br> Good men it will calm and cheer,<br> Bad men it will chain and cage.<br> In the heart of the music peals a strain<br> Which only angels hear;<br> Whether it waken joy or rage,<br> Hushed myriads hark in vain,<br> Yet they who hear it shed their age,<br> And take their youth again.<br>
