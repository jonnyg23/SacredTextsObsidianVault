 <p>"Animus ad amplitudinem Mysteriorum pro modulo suo dilatetur,<br>
 non Mysteria ad angustias animi constringantur." (Bacon.)</p>
 <p>"Many literary critics seem to think that an hypothesis about obscure and remote questions of history can be refuted by a simple demand for the production of more evidence than in fact exists.--But the true test of an hypothesis, if it cannot be shewn to conflict with known truths, is the number of facts that it correlates, and explains." (Cornford, <i>Origins of Attic Comedy</i>.)</p></dir>
