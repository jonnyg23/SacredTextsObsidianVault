 <p align="center">THE ROOTS OF THE MOUNTAINS</p>  
 <p align="center">WHEREIN IS TOLD SOMEWHAT OF THE LIVES OF THE MEN OF BURGDALE THEIR FRIENDS THEIR NEIGHBOURS THEIR FOEMEN AND THEIR FELLOWS IN ARMS</p>  
 <p align="center">BY WILLIAM MORRIS</p>  
 <p align="center">London: Longmans, Green</p>  
 <p align="center">[1889]</p>  
 <p>Whiles carried o'er the iron road,<br> We hurry by some fair abode;<br> The garden bright amidst the hay,<br> The yellow wain upon the way,<br> The dining men, the wind that sweeps<br> Light locks from off the sun-sweet heaps -<br> The gable grey, the hoary roof,<br> Here now--and now so far aloof.<br> How sorely then we long to stay<br> And midst its sweetness wear the day,<br> And 'neath its changing shadows sit,<br> And feel ourselves a part of it.<br> Such rest, such stay, I strove to win<br> With these same leaves that lie herein.</p>
