 <h1 align="center">SECTION VII</h1>
 
 <h3 align="center"><i>LAKE LEGENDS.</i></h3>
 
 <p>A<small>LTHOUGH</small> Esthonia is not so distinctly a lake-country as Finland,<a name="page_135_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_135_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a> which is often called “The Land of Ten Thousand Lakes,” yet it is a low swampy country, with many small lakes besides the great Lake Peipus, on the south-east, and lake stories of various kinds are numerous in Esthonian tales.</p>
 
 <p> Jannsen relates that Lake Korküll or Oiso, in the district of Fellin in Livonia, stands on the site of a castle, the lord of which insisted on marrying his sister. He bribed a priest to perform the ceremony, but the castle sank into the ground with all present, and a lake arose in its stead.</p>
 
 <p> We add a selection of Esthonian lake-stories.</p>
