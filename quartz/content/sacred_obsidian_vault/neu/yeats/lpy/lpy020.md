 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 23</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HE GIVES HIS BELOVED CERTAIN RHYMES</h3>  
 <p>FASTEN your hair with a golden pin,<br> And bind up every wandering tress;<br> I bade my heart build these poor rhymes:<br> It worked at them, day out, day in,<br> Building a sorrowful loveliness<br> Out of the battles of old times.</p>  
 <p>You need but lift a pearl-pale hand,<br> And bind up your long hair and sigh;<br> And all men's hearts must burn and beat;<br> And candle-like foam on the dim sand,<br> And stars climbing the dew-dropping sky,<br> Live but to light your passing feet.</p>
