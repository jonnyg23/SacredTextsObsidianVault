 <h3 align="CENTER">LEGEND OF KNOCKGRAFTON.--Page <a href="fip12.htm#page_40">40</a>.</h3>
 <p>Moat does not mean a place with water, but a tumulus or barrow. The words <i>Da Luan Da Mort augus Da Dardeen</i> are Gaelic for "Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday too". Da Hena is Thursday. Story-tellers, in telling this tale, says Croker, sing these words to the following music-according to Croker, music of very ancient kind:--</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_32000"></a>
 <img src="img/32000.jpg"></p>
 <p> </p>
 <p><a name="page_321"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 321</font></a></p>
 <p>Mr. Douglas Hyde has heard the story in Connaught, with the song of the fairy as "Peean Peean daw feean, Peean go leh agus leffin" [<i>pighin, pighin, dà phighin, pighin go ieith agus leith phighin</i>], which in English means, "a penny, a penny, twopence, a penny and a half, and a halfpenny".</p>
