 <h3 align="CENTER">THE FAIRY NURSE.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER">BY EDWARD WALSH</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Sweet babe! a golden cradle holds thee,<br>
 And soft the snow-white fleece enfolds thee;<br>
 In airy bower I'll watch thy sleeping,<br>
 Where branchy trees to the breeze are sweeping.<br>
                   Shuheen, sho, lulo lo!</p>
 <p>When mothers languish broken-hearted,<br>
 When young wives are from husbands parted,<br>
 Ah! little think the keeners lonely,<br>
 They weep some time-worn fairy only.<br>
                   Shuheen, sho, lulo lo!</p>
 <p>Within our magic halls of brightness,<br>
 Trips many a foot of snowy whiteness;<br>
 Stolen maidens, queens of fairy--<br>
 And kings and chiefs a sluagh-shee airy,<br>
                   Shuheen, sho, lulo lo!</p>
 <p>Rest thee, babe! I love thee dearly,<br>
 And as thy mortal mother nearly;<br>
 Ours is the swiftest steed and proudest,<br>
 That moves where the tramp of the host is loudest.<br>
                   Shuheen, sho, lulo lo!</p>
 <p>Rest thee, babe! for soon thy slumbers<br>
 Shall flee at the magic koelshie's <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">1</font></a> numbers;<br>
 In airy bower I'll watch thy sleeping,<br>
 Where branchy trees to the breeze are sweeping.<br>
                   Shuheen, sho, lulo lo!</p>
 <p> </p></dir>
 </dir>
