 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Te Pito Te Henua, or Easter Island</i>, by William J. Thompson, [1891], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">GENEALOGY OF THE KINGS OF EASTER ISLAND.</h3> 
 <p>Hotu-Matua, driven from his kingdom to the eastward by the rebellion of his subjects, landed with a close band of his followers at Easter</p> 
 <p><a name="page_534"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 534</font></a></p> 
 <p>Islands, in the mouth of August (Anekena), in two canoes, each 15 fathoms long and 1 fathom deep.</p> 
 <p>First. Hotu Matua.</p> 
 <p>Second. Tuumaeheke.</p> 
 <p>Third. Nuku.</p> 
 <p>Fourth. Miru.</p> 
 <p>Fifth. Hinariru.</p> 
 <p>Sixth. Aturaugi.</p> 
 <p>Seventh. Raa.</p> 
 <p>Eighth. Atarauga.</p> 
 <p>Ninth. Hakapuna.</p> 
 <p>Tenth. Oihu.</p> 
 <p>Eleventh. Ruhoi.</p>

 <p>Thirteenth. Takahita.</p> 
 <p>Fourteenth. Ouaraa.</p>

 <p>Sixteenth. Mahuta Ariiki. <a name="fr_0"></a><a href="ei59.htm#fn_0"><font size="1">1</font></a></p> 
 <p>Seventeenth. Atua Ure Raugi.</p> 
 <p>Eighteenth. Teriri Turkura.</p>

 <p>Twenty-eighth. Turua Ki Keua. </p> 
 <p>Twenty-ninth. Tuterkimanara.</p> 
 <p>Thirtieth. Kote Kura Tahoua.</p> 
 <p>Thirty-first. Taoraha Kaihahauga.</p>

 <p>Fiftieth. Re Kauu.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-first. Terava Rara.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-second. Tehitehuke.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-third. Terahai.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-fourth. Kaimokoi.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-fifth. Ngaara.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-sixth. Kaimakoi Iti.</p> 
 <p>Fifty-seventh. Maurata.</p> 
 <p>Maurata, the last king, only reigned three years. He was carried away by the Peruvians in 1864, and it is supposed to have died in the guano mines of the Chinchi Islands.</p>
