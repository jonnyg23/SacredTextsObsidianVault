 <p>
 This is a nineteenth century 'Monitor' of the American Knights Templar.
 This is a Masonic organization which has its own distinctive
 rituals and regalia.
 While they are not connected directly to the
 <a href="../../sro/index.htm">Medieval Templars</a>,
 they carry on the knightly ideals by charitable works, and
 sponsoring Masonic youth groups. 
 </p>
 <p>
 <cite>Shibboleth</cite> is similar in format and organization to other
 19th century Masonic literature such as <a href="../dun/index.htm">Duncan's Masonic Ritual and Monitor</a>, including
 the funky woodcuts and 19th century atmosphere.
 Mixed in with this is historical material on the Templars, stories
 from the Bible, songs, poetry, sermons, a lot of blank forms for all occasions,
 and bits of advice.
 For instance, 'Hints on Templar Banquets' notes:
 'Never permit smoking when ladies are present...'
 </p>
