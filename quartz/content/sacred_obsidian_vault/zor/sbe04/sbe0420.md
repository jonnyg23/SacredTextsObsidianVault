 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part I (SBE04)</i>, James Darmesteter, tr. [1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h2 align="center">FARGARD XIV.</h2>  
 <p><font size="-1">This Fargard is nothing more than an appendix to the last clauses in the preceding Fargard (§ 50 seq.) How the murder of a water dog may be atoned for is described in it at full length. As the water dog is the holiest of all dogs <a name="fr_883"></a><a href="#fn_871"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and, as it were, a link between the dog and God, the process of atonement must be one of an extraordinary character. It is this chapter, more than any other, which may make it doubtful whether the legislation of the Vendîdâd has ever existed as real and living law. See, however, Introduction V, 20.</font></p>  
 <p>1. Zarathu<i>s</i>tra asked Ahura Mazda: 'O Ahura Mazda, most beneficent Spirit, Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! He who smites one of those water dogs that are born one from a thousand dogs and a thousand she-dogs <a name="fr_884"></a><a href="#fn_872"><font size="1">4</font></a>, so that he gives up the ghost and the soul parts from the body, what is the penalty that he shall pay?'</p>  
 <p>2 (4). Ahura Mazda answered: ‘He shall pay ten thousand stripes with the Aspahê-a<i>s</i>tra, ten thousand stripes with the Sraoshô-<i>k</i>arana <a name="fr_885"></a><a href="#fn_873"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>

 <p>‘He shall godly and piously bring unto the fire of Ahura Mazda <a name="fr_886"></a><a href="#fn_874"><font size="1">1</font></a> ten thousand loads of hard, well dried, well examined <a name="fr_887"></a><a href="#fn_875"><font size="1">2</font></a> wood, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).</p>  
 <p>3 (6). ‘He shall godly and piously bring unto the fire of Ahura Mazda ten thousand loads of soft wood, of Urvâsna, Vohu-gaona, Vohu-kereti, Hadhâ-naêpata <a name="fr_888"></a><a href="#fn_876"><font size="1">3</font></a>, or any sweet-scented plant, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).</p>  
 <p>4 (7). ‘He shall godly and piously tie and consecrate ten thousand bundles of baresma; he shall offer up to the good waters ten thousand Zaothra libations with the Haoma and the sacred meat <a name="fr_889"></a><a href="#fn_877"><font size="1">4</font></a>, cleanly prepared and well strained, cleanly prepared and well strained by a pious man <a name="fr_890"></a><a href="#fn_878"><font size="1">5</font></a>, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).</p>

 <p>10 (41). ‘He shall godly and piously give to godly men a set of all the implements of which the husbandmen make use, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog), namely: A plough with share and yoke <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_907"><font size="1">1</font></a>, an ox whip <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_908"><font size="1">2</font></a>, a mortar of stone, a hand-mill for grinding corn,</p>  
 <p>11 (48). 'A spade for digging and tilling; one measure of silver and one measure of gold.'</p>  
 <p>O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! How much silver?</p>  
 <p>Ahura Mazda answered: 'The price of a stallion:'</p>  
 <p>O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! How much gold?</p>  
 <p>Ahura Mazda answered: ‘The price of a camel.</p>  
 <p>12 (54). 'He shall godly and piously procure a rill of running water for godly husbandmen, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).'</p>  
 <p>O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! How large is the rill?</p>  
 <p>Ahura Mazda answered: ‘The depth of a dog, and the breadth of a dog <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_909"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>13 (57). 'He shall godly and piously give a piece of arable land to godly men, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).'</p>  
 <p>O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! How large is the piece of land?</p>  
 <p>Ahura Mazda answered: ‘As much as can be watered with such a rill on both sides <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_910"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>14 (60). 'He shall godly and piously procure for godly men a house with ox-stalls, with nine</p>

 <p>O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One I How large is the house?</p>  
 <p>Ahura Mazda answered: ‘Twelve Vîtâras <a name="fr_925"></a><a href="#fn_913"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the largest part of the house, nine Vîtâras in the middle part, six Vîtâras in the smallest part.</p>  
 <p>‘He shall godly and piously give to godly men godly beds with cushions, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog),</p>  
 <p>15 (64). 'He shall godly and piously give to a godly man a virgin maid, whom no man has known, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).'</p>  
 <p>O Maker of the material world, thou Holy One! What maid?</p>  
 <p>Ahura Mazda answered: ‘A sister or a daughter of his, at the age of puberty, with ear-rings in her ears, and past her fifteenth year.</p>  
 <p>16 (67). ‘He shall godly and piously give to holy men twice seven head of small cattle, as an atonement unto the soul (of the water dog).</p>  
 <p>‘He shall bring up twice seven whelps.</p>  
 <p>‘He shall throw twice seven bridges over canals.</p>  
 <p>17 (70). ‘He shall put into repair twice nine stables that are out of repair.</p>  
 <p>‘He shall cleanse twice nine dogs from skin humours, hair wax, vermin <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_914"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and all the diseases that are produced on the body of a dog.</p>

 <p>18 (73). ‘This is the atonement, this is the penalty that he shall undergo to atone for the deed that he has done.</p>  
 <p>'If he shall undergo it, he shall enter the world of the holy ones: if he shall not undergo it, he shall fall down into the world of the wicked, into that dark world, made of darkness, the offspring of darkness <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_915"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>
