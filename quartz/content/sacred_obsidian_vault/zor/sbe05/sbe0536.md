 <p><a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 99</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the nature of plants it says in revelation, that, before the coming of the destroyer, vegetation had no thorn and bark about it; and, afterwards, when the destroyer came, it became coated with bark <i>and</i> thorny <a name="fr_539"></a><a href="#fn_534"><font size="1">1</font></a>, for antagonism mingled with every single thing; owing to that cause vegetation is also much mixed with poison, like Bi<i>s</i> the height of hemp (kand) <a name="fr_540"></a><a href="#fn_535"><font size="1">2</font></a>, that is poisonous, for men when they eat <i>it</i> die.</p>  
 <p>2. In like manner even as the animals, with grain of fifty and five species <i>and</i> twelve species of medicinal plants, have arisen from the primeval ox <a name="fr_541"></a><a href="#fn_536"><font size="1">3</font></a>, ten thousand <a name="fr_542"></a><a href="#fn_537"><font size="1">4</font></a> species among the species of principal</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 100</font></a></p>  
 <p>plants, <i>and</i> a hundred thousand species among <i>ordinary</i> plants have grown from all these seeds of the tree opposed to harm <a name="fr_543"></a><a href="#fn_538"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the many-seeded, <i>which</i> has grown in the wide-formed ocean. 3. When the seeds of all these plants, with those from the primeval ox, have arisen upon it, every year the bird <a name="fr_544"></a><a href="#fn_539"><font size="1">2</font></a> strips that tree <i>and</i> mingles all the seeds in the water; Tî<i>s</i>tar seizes <i>them</i> with the rain-water and rains <i>them on</i> to <i>all</i> regions. 4. Near to that tree the white Hôm, the healing and undefiled, has grown at the source of the water of Arêdvîvsûr <a name="fr_545"></a><a href="#fn_540"><font size="1">3</font></a>; every one who eats <i>it</i> becomes immortal, and they call it the Gôkar<i>d</i> <a name="fr_546"></a><a href="#fn_541"><font size="1">4</font></a> tree, as it is said that Hôm is expelling death <a name="fr_547"></a><a href="#fn_542"><font size="1">5</font></a>; also in the renovation <i>of the universe</i> they prepare its immortality therefrom <a name="fr_548"></a><a href="#fn_543"><font size="1">6</font></a>; <i>and</i> it is the chief of plants <a name="fr_549"></a><a href="#fn_544"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. These are as many genera of plants as exist: trees and shrubs, fruit-<i>trees</i>, corn, flowers, aromatic herbs, salads, spices, grass, wild plants, medicinal</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>plants</i>, gum <i>plants</i>, and all producing <a name="fr_550"></a><a href="#fn_545"><font size="1">1</font></a> oil, dyes, <i>and</i> clothing. 6. I <i>will</i> mention them also a second time: all whose fruit is not welcome as food of men, <i>and</i> are perennial (sâlvâr), as the cypress, the plane, the white poplar, the box, and others of this genus, they call trees and shrubs (dâr va dirakht). 7. The produce of everything welcome as food of men, <i>that</i> is perennial, as the date, the myrtle, the lote-plum <a name="fr_551"></a><a href="#fn_546"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the grape, the quince, the apple, the citron, the pomegranate, the peach, the fig, the walnut, the almond, <i>and</i> others in this genus, they call fruit (mîvak). 8. Whatever requires labour with the spade <a name="fr_552"></a><a href="#fn_547"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>and</i> is perennial, they call a shrub (dirakht). 9. Whatever requires that they take its crop through labour, <i>and its</i> root withers away, such as wheat, barley, grain, various kinds <a name="fr_553"></a><a href="#fn_548"><font size="1">4</font></a> of pulse, vetches, <i>and</i> others of this genus, they call corn (<i>g</i>ûr<i>d</i>âk). 10. Every <i>plant with</i> fragrant leaves, <i>which</i> is cultivated by the hand-labour of men, <i>and</i> is perennial (hamvâr), they call an aromatic herb (siparam). 11. Whatever sweet-scented blossom arises <i>at</i> various seasons through the hand-labour of men, or has a perennial root <i>and</i> blossoms in <i>its</i> season <i>with</i> new shoots <i>and</i> sweet-scented blossoms, as the rose, the narcissus, the jasmine, the dog-rose (nêstarûn),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a></p>  
 <p>the tulip, the colocynth (kavastîk), the pandanus (kêdi), the <i>k</i>amba, the ox-eye (hêri), the crocus, the swallow-wort (zarda), the violet, the kârda, <i>and</i> others of this genus, they call a flower (gûl). 12. Everything whose sweet-scented fruit, or sweet-scented blossom, arises in <i>its</i> season, without the hand-labour of men, they call a wild plant (vahâr or nihâl). 13. Whatever is welcome as food of cattle and beasts <i>of burden</i>, they call grass (giyâh). 14. Whatever enters into cakes (pê<i>s</i>-pârakîhâ) they call spices (âvzârîhâ). 15. Whatever is welcome in eating of bread, as torn shoots <a name="fr_554"></a><a href="#fn_549"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the coriander, water-cress (kakî<i>g</i>), the leek, <i>and</i> others of this genus, they call salad (têrak) <a name="fr_555"></a><a href="#fn_550"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 16. Whatever is like spinning <a name="fr_556"></a><a href="#fn_551"><font size="1">3</font></a> cotton, <i>and</i> others of this genus, they call clothing <i>plants</i> (<i>g</i>âmak). 17. Whatever lentil <a name="fr_557"></a><a href="#fn_552"><font size="1">4</font></a> is greasy, as sesame, dûshdâng, hemp, zandak <a name="fr_558"></a><a href="#fn_553"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and others of this genus, they call an oil-<i>seed</i> (rôkanô). 18. Whatever <i>one</i> can dye clothing with, as saffron, sapan-wood, za<i>k</i>ava, vaha, <i>and</i> others of this genus, they call a dye-<i>plant</i> (rag). 19. Whatever root, or gum <a name="fr_559"></a><a href="#fn_554"><font size="1">6</font></a>, or wood</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a></p>  
 <p>is scented, as frankincense <a name="fr_560"></a><a href="#fn_555"><font size="1">1</font></a>, varâ<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_561"></a><a href="#fn_556"><font size="1">2</font></a>, kust, sandalwood, cardamom <a name="fr_562"></a><a href="#fn_557"><font size="1">3</font></a>, camphor, orange-scented mint, <i>and</i> others of this genus, they call a scent (bôd). 20. Whatever stickiness comes out from plants <a name="fr_563"></a><a href="#fn_558"><font size="1">4</font></a> they call gummy (za<i>d</i>ak). 21. The timber which proceeds, from the trees, when it is either dry or wet, they call wood (<i>k</i>îbâ). 22. Every one of all these plants which is so, they call medicinal (dârûk) <a name="fr_564"></a><a href="#fn_559"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>23. The principal fruits are <i>of</i> thirty kinds (khadûînak), and ten species (sar<i>d</i>ak) of them are fit to eat inside and outside, as the fig, the apple, the quince, the citron, the grape, the mulberry, the pear, <i>and</i> others of this kind; ten are fit to eat outside, <i>but</i> not fit to eat inside, as the date, the peach, the white apricot, <i>and</i> others of this kind; those which are fit to eat inside, <i>but</i> not fit to eat outside, are the walnut, the almond, the pomegranate, the cocoanut <a name="fr_565"></a><a href="#fn_560"><font size="1">6</font></a>, the filbert <a name="fr_566"></a><a href="#fn_561"><font size="1">7</font></a>, the chesnut <a name="fr_567"></a><a href="#fn_562"><font size="1">8</font></a>, the pistachio nut, the vargân, <i>and</i> whatever else of this description are very remarkable.</p>  
 <p>24 <a name="fr_568"></a><a href="#fn_563"><font size="1">9</font></a>. This, too, it says, that every single flower is appropriate to an angel (ameshôspend) <a name="fr_569"></a><a href="#fn_564"><font size="1">10</font></a>, as the</p>

 </p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 104</font></a></p>  
 <p>white <a name="fr_570"></a><a href="#fn_565"><font size="1">1</font></a> jasmine (saman) is for Vohûman, the myrtle <i>and</i> jasmine (yâsmin) are Aûharmazd's own, the mouse-ear (or sweet marjoram) is Ashavahi<i>s</i>t's <a name="fr_571"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">2</font></a> own, the basil-royal is Shatvaîrô's own, the musk flower is Spendarma<i>d</i>'s, the lily is Horvada<i>d</i>'s, the <i>k</i>amba is Amerôda<i>d</i>'s, Dîn-<i>pavan-Âtarô</i> <i>has</i> the orange-scented mint (vâ<i>d</i>rang-bôd), Âtarô <i>has</i> the marigold <a name="fr_572"></a><a href="#fn_567"><font size="1">3</font></a> (âdargun), the water-lily is Âvân's, the white marv is Khtûrshê<i>d</i>'s, the ranges <a name="fr_573"></a><a href="#fn_568"><font size="1">4</font></a> is Mâh's, the violet is Tîr's, the mêren <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="#fn_569"><font size="1">5</font></a> is Gô<i>s</i>'s, the kârda is Dîn-pavan-Mitrô's, all violets are Mitrô's, the red chrysanthemum (khêr) is Srôsh's, the dog-rose (nestran) is Rashnû's, the cockscomb is Fravar<i>d</i>în's, the sisebar is Vâhrâm's, the yellow chrysanthemum is Râm's, the orange-scented mint is Vâ<i>d</i>'s <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_570"><font size="1">6</font></a>, the trigonella is Dîn-pavan-Dîn's, the hundred-petalled rose is Dîn's, all kinds of wild flowers (vahâr) are Ar<i>d</i>'s <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_571"><font size="1">7</font></a>, Â<i>s</i>tâ<i>d</i> <i>has</i> all the white Hôm <a name="fr_577"></a><a href="#fn_572"><font size="1">8</font></a>, the bread-baker's basil is Asmân's, Zamyâ<i>d</i> <i>has</i> the crocus, Mâraspend <i>has the flower</i> <a name="fr_578"></a><a href="#fn_573"><font size="1">9</font></a> of Ardashîr,</p>

 <p>25. It is concerning plants that every single kind <i>with</i> a drop of water on a twig (teh) they should hold four finger-breadths in front of the fire <a name="fr_580"></a><a href="#fn_575"><font size="1">2</font></a>; Most of all it is the lotos (kûnâr) they speak of.</p>
