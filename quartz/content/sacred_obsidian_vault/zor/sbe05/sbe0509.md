 <p><a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 2</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">OBSERVATIONS.</h3>  
 <p>1. For all divisions into chapters and sections the translator is responsible, as the original text is written continuously, with very few stops marked.</p>  
 <p>2. Italics are used for any English words which are not expressed, or fully understood, in the original text, but are added to complete the sense of the translation.</p>  
 <p>3. Oriental words are usually 'spaced.' Italics occurring in them, or in names, are intended to represent certain peculiar Oriental letters. The italic consonants <i>d</i>, <i>n</i>, <i>v</i> may be pronounced as in English; but <i>g</i> should be sounded like j, <i>h</i><i>v</i> like wh, <i>k</i> like ch in 'church,' <font size="-1">N</font> like ng, <i>s</i> like sh, <i>z</i> like French j. For further information, see 'Transliteration of Oriental Alphabets adopted for the Translations of the Sacred Books of the East' at the end of the volume.</p>  
 <p>4. In Pahlavi words all circumflexed vowels and any final ŏ are expressed in the Pahlavi original, but all other vowels are merely understood.</p>  
 <p>5. In the translation, words in parentheses are merely explanatory of those which precede them.</p>  
 <p>6. Abbreviations used are:Av. for Avesta. Dâ<i>d</i>. for Dâ<i>d</i>istân-i Dînîk. Huz. for Huzvâri<i>s</i>. Mkh. for Mainyô-i-khar<i>d</i>, ed. West. Pahl. for Pahlavi. Pâz. for Pâzand. Pers. for Persian. Sans. for Sanskrit. Vend. for Vendîdâd, ed. Spiegel. Visp. for Visparad, ed. Sp. Yas. for Yasna, ed. Sp. Yt. for Ya<i>s</i>t, ed. Westergaard.</p>  
 <p>7. The manuscripts mentioned in the notes are:</p>  
 <p>K20 (about 500 years old), No. 20 in the University Library at Kopenhagen.</p>  
 <p>K20b (uncertain date), a fragment of the text, No. 20b in the same library.</p>  
 <p>M6 (written A.D. 1397), No. 6 of the Haug Collection in the State Library at Munich.</p>  
 <p>TD (written about A.D. 1530), belonging to Mobad Tehmuras Dinshawji Anklesaria at Bombay.</p>
