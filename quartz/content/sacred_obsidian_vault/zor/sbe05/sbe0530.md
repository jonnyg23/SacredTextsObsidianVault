 <p><a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 83</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXI <a name="fr_444"></a><a href="#fn_441"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. In revelation they mention seventeen <a name="fr_445"></a><a href="#fn_442"><font size="1">2</font></a> species of liquid (mâyâ), as one liquid resides in plants  <a name="fr_446"></a><a href="#fn_443"><font size="1">3</font></a>; second, that which is flowing from the mountains, that is, the rivers; third, that which is rain-water; fourth, that of tanks <i>and</i> other special <i>constructions</i>; fifth, the semen of animals and men; sixth, the urine of animals and men <a name="fr_447"></a><a href="#fn_444"><font size="1">4</font></a>; seventh, the sweat of animals and men; the eighth liquid is that in the skin of animals and men; ninth, the tears of animals and men; tenth, the blood of animals and men; eleventh, the oil in animals and men, a necessary in both worlds <a name="fr_448"></a><a href="#fn_445"><font size="1">5</font></a>; twelfth, the saliva of animals and men, with which they nourish the embryo <a name="fr_449"></a><a href="#fn_446"><font size="1">6</font></a>; the thirteenth is that which is under the bark <a name="fr_450"></a><a href="#fn_447"><font size="1">7</font></a> of plants, as it is said that every bark has a liquid, through which a drop <i>appears</i> on a twig (têkh) <i>when placed</i> four finger-breadths before a fire <a name="fr_451"></a><a href="#fn_448"><font size="1">8</font></a>; fourteenth, the milk of animals and men. 2. All these, through growth, or</p>

 <p>3. This, too, they say, that of these three rivers, that is, the Arag river, the Marv river, <i>and</i> the Vêh <a name="fr_452"></a><a href="#fn_449"><font size="1">1</font></a> river, the spirits were dissatisfied, so that they would not flow into the world, owing to the defilement of stagnant water (armê<i>s</i>t) which they beheld, so that they were in tribulation through it until Zaratû<i>s</i>t was exhibited to them, whom I (Aûharmazd) will create, who <i>will</i> pour sixfold holy-water (zôr) into it <i>and</i> make <i>it</i> again wholesome; he will preach carefulness <a name="fr_453"></a><a href="#fn_450"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 4. This, too, it says, that, of water whose holy-water is more and pollution less, the holy-water has come in excess, <i>and</i> in three years it goes back to the sources <a name="fr_454"></a><a href="#fn_451"><font size="1">3</font></a>; <i>that</i> of which the pollution <i>and</i> holy-water have both become equal, arrives back in six years; <i>that</i> of which the pollution is more and holy-water less, arrives back in nine years. 5. So, also, the growth of plants is connected, in this manner, strongly with the root <a name="fr_455"></a><a href="#fn_452"><font size="1">4</font></a>; so, likewise, the blessings (âfrîn) which the righteous utter, come back, in this proportion, to themselves.</p>  
 <p>6. Regarding the river Nâhvtâk <a name="fr_456"></a><a href="#fn_453"><font size="1">5</font></a> it says, that Frâsîyâ<i>v</i> of Tûr conducted it away; <i>and</i> when <a name="fr_457"></a><a href="#fn_454"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

Kyânsîh <a name="fr_460"></a><a href="#fn_456"><font size="1">2</font></a> is the one where the home (<i>g</i>inâk) of the Kayân race is.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_441"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_444">83:1</a> This chapter is evidently a continuation of the preceding one.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_442"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_445">83:2</a> Only fourteen are mentioned in the details which follow.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_443"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_446">83:3</a> Most of these details are derived from the Pahl. Yas. XXXVIII, 7-9, 13, 14; and several varieties of water are also in Yas. LXVII, 15.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_444"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_447">83:4</a> This sixth liquid is omitted by K20.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_445"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_448">83:5</a> Departed souls are said to be fed with oil in paradise.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_446"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_449">83:6</a> K20 omits the word pûs, 'embryo.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_447"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_450">83:7</a> The meaning 'bark' for Pâz. ayvan is merely a guess; Anquetil has 'sap' (compare Pers. âvînâ, 'juice'), but this is hardly consistent with the rest of the sentence.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_448"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_451">83:8</a> See Chap. XXVII, 25.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_449"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_452">84:1</a> K20 has 'Hêlmand,' but M6 has '<i>S</i>apîr,' the Huz. equivalent of 'Vêh,' which is more probable.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_450"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_453">84:2</a> Or, 'abstinence <i>from impurity</i>.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_451"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_454">84:3</a> The source Arêdvîvsûr (see Chap. XIII, 3, 10).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_452"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_455">84:4</a> That is, by the sap circulating like the waters of the earth. The greater part of this sentence is omitted in K20.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_453"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_456">84:5</a> Probably 'the Nâvadâ' and 'navigable waters' of Chap. XX, 7, 34, and Nâîvtâk of Chap. XXIX, 4, 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_454"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_457">84:6</a> Reading amat, 'when,' instead of mûn, 'which' (see note to Chap. I, 7).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_455"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_458">85:1</a> Written Khûrshê<i>d</i>ar, as usual in Bundahi<i>s</i> (see Chap. XXXII, 8).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_456"></a><a href="sbe0530.htm#fr_460">85:2</a> Written Kayâseh in Pâzand (see Chap. XIII, 16).</p>
