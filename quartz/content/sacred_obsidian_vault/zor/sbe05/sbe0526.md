 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part I (SBE05)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the nature of fire it says in revelation, that fire is produced of five kinds, namely, the fire Berezi-savang <a name="fr_307"></a><a href="#fn_306"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the fire which shoots up before Aûharmazd the lord; the fire Vohu-fryãn <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">3</font></a>, the fire which is in the bodies of men and animals; the fire Urvâzi<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the fire which is in plants; the fire</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 62</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Vâzi<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the fire which is in a cloud <i>which</i> stands opposed to Spên<i>g</i>argâk in conflict; the fire Spêni<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_310"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the fire which they keep in use in the world, likewise the fire of Vâhrâm <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_311"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 2. Of those five fires one consumes both water and food, as that which is in the bodies of men; one consumes water <i>and</i> consumes no food, as that which is in plants, which live and grow through water; one consumes food <i>and</i> consumes no water, as that which they keep in use in the world, and likewise the fire of Vâhrâm; one consumes no water and no food, as the fire Vâzi<i>s</i>t. 3. The Berezi-savang is that in the earth and mountains <i>and</i> other things, which <a name="fr_313"></a><a href="#fn_312"><font size="1">4</font></a> Aûharmazd created, <i>in</i> the original creation, like three breathing souls (nismô); through the watchfulness and protection <i>due</i> to them the world ever develops (vakhshê<i>d</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. And in the reign of Takhmôrup <a name="fr_314"></a><a href="#fn_313"><font size="1">5</font></a>, when men continually passed, on the back of the ox Sarsaok <a name="fr_315"></a><a href="#fn_314"><font size="1">6</font></a>, from Khvanîras to the other regions, one night</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p>amid the sea the wind rushed upon <a name="fr_316"></a><a href="#fn_315"><font size="1">1</font></a> the fireplacethe fireplace in which the fire was, such as was provided in three places on the back of the oxwhich the wind dropped with the fire into the sea; and all those three fires, like three breathing souls, continually shot up in the place and position of the fire on the back of the ox, so that it becomes quite light, <i>and</i> the men pass again through the sea. 5. And in the reign of Yim <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_316"><font size="1">2</font></a> every duty was performed more fully through the assistance of all those three fires; and the fire Frôbak <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">3</font></a> was established by him at the appointed place (dâ<i>d</i>-gâs) on the Gadman-hômand ('glorious') mountain in Khvâri<i>z</i>em <a name="fr_319"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">4</font></a>, which Yim constructed for them; and the glory of Yim saves the fire Frôbak from the hand of Dahâk <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 6. In the reign of King Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, upon revelation from the religion <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">6</font></a>, it was established, out of Khvâri<i>z</i>em, at the Rôshan ('shining') mountain in Kâvulistân, the country of Kâvul (Kâbul), just as it remains there even now.</p>  
 <p>7. The fire Gû<i>s</i>asp, until the reign of Kaî-Khûsrôb <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">7</font></a> continually afforded the world protection in the manner <i>aforesaid <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">8</font></a>; and</i> when Kaî-Khûsrôb <a name="fr_324"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">7</font></a> was</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>extirpating the idol-temples of Lake <i>K</i>ê<i>k</i>ast <a name="fr_325"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">1</font></a> it settled upon the mane of <i>his</i> horse, and drove away the darkness and gloom, and made <i>it</i> quite light, so that they might extirpate the idol-temples; in the same locality the fire Gû<i>s</i>asp was established at the appointed place on the Asnavand mountain <a name="fr_326"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. The fire Bûr<i>z</i>în-Mitrô, until the reign of King Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, ever assisted <a name="fr_327"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>in</i> like manner, in the world, <i>and</i> continually afforded protection; <i>and</i> when the glorified <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">4</font></a> Zaratû<i>s</i>t was introduced to produce confidence in the progress of the religion, King Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and <i>his</i> offspring were steadfast in the religion of God. <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_327"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>and</i> Vi<i>s</i>tâsp established <i>this fire</i> at the appointed place on Mount Rêvand, where they say the Ridge of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp (pû<i>s</i>t-i Vi<i>s</i>tâspân) is <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_328"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>9. All those three fires are the whole body of the fire of Vâhrâm, together with the fire of the world, and those breathing souls are lodged in them; a counterpart of the body of man when it forms in the womb of the mother, <i>and</i> a soul from the spirit-<i>world</i> settles within <i>it</i>, which controls the body while living; when that body dies, the body mingles with the earth, <i>and</i> the soul goes back to the spirit.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_306"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_307">61:2</a> These Avesta names of the five kinds of fire are enumerated in Yas. XVII, 63-67, and the Pahlavi translation of that passage interchanges the attributes ascribed to the first and fifth in the text, thus it calls the first 'the fire of sublime benefit in connection with Varahrân (Bahrâm).' See also Selections of Zâ<i>d</i>-sparam, XI, 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_307"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_308">61:3</a> 'The fire of the good diffuser (or offerer), that within the bodies of men' (Pahl. Yas. XVII, 64).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_308"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_309">61:4</a> 'The fire of prosperous (or abundant) life, that within plants' (Pahl. Yas. XVII, 65).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_309"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_310">62:1</a> 'The fire Vâzi<i>s</i>t, that which smites the demon Spen<i>g</i>argâ' (Pahl. Yas. XVII, 66). See Chap. VII, 12.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_310"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_311">62:2</a> 'The propitious fire <i>which</i> stands in heaven before Aûharmazd in a spiritual state' (Pahl. Yas. XVII, 67).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_311"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_312">62:3</a> The Bahrâm fire, or sacred fire at places of worship.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_312"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_313">62:4</a> M6 has min, instead of mûn, which alters the translation, but not the meaning. This appears to be a different account of the fire Berezi-savang to that given in § 1, but it merely implies that it is fire in its spiritual state, and the name can, therefore, be applied to any natural fire which can be attributed to supernatural agency, such as burning springs of petroleum, volcanic eruptions, ignis fatuus, phosphorescence of the sea, &amp;c.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_313"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_314">62:5</a> The second Pê<i>s</i>dâdian monarch (see Chaps. XXXI, 2, 3, XXXIV, 4).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_314"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_315">62:6</a> Written Srisaok in the MSS. in Chap. XV, 27; where it also appears that the sea was 'the wide-formed ocean.' See likewise Chap. XIX, 13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_315"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_316">63:1</a> Compare staft with Pers. <i>s</i>itâftan, 'to hasten.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_316"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_317">63:2</a> The third Pê<i>s</i>dâdian monarch (see Chaps. XXXI, 3, 4, XXXIV, 4).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_317"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_318">63:3</a> Also written Frôbŏ, Frôbâ, Frôbâk, or Frôbâg.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_318"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_319">63:4</a> The Av. <i>H</i><i>v</i>âirizem of Mihir Yt. 14, a province east of the Caspian.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_319"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_320">63:5</a> It is doubtful whether va gadman, 'and the glory,' or nismô, 'the soul, reason' (see Chaps. XXIII, 1, XXXIV, 4), should be read. And it may even be that 'the fire Frôbak saves the soul of Yim,' &amp;c. For Dahâk see Chaps. XXXI, 6, XXXIV, 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_320"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_321">63:6</a> Or, I upon declaration from revelation!</p>
 <p><a name="fn_321"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_324">63:7</a> Here written Kai-Khûsrôbî.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_322"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_323">63:8</a> In § 3. The 'three breathing souls' of spiritual fire are supposed <a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a> to be incorporated in its three earthly representatives, the fires Frôbak, Gû<i>s</i>asp, and Bûr<i>z</i>în-Mitrô respectively.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_323"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_325">64:1</a> That is, of the province around that lake (see Chap. XXII, 2).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_324"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_326">64:2</a> See Chap. XII, 26. Compare Selections of Zâ<i>d</i>-sparam, VI, 22.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_325"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_327">64:3</a> Taking va<i>g</i>î<i>d</i> as equivalent to Pers. guzîd; but it may be equivalent to Pers. vazîd, 'grew, shot up.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_326"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_328">64:4</a> The epithet anôshak-rûbân (Pers. nôshirvân) means literally 'immortal-souled.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_327"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_329">64:5</a> Or, 'of the angels,' which plural form is often used to express 'God.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_328"></a><a href="sbe0526.htm#fr_330">64:6</a> See Chap. XII, 18, 34.</p>
