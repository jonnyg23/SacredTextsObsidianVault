 <p>1. On the nature of rivers it says in revelation, that these two rivers flow forth from the north, part from Albûr<i>z</i> <i>and</i> part from the Albûr<i>z</i> of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 75</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Aûharmazd <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">1</font></a>; one towards the west, that is the Arag <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_377"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i> one towards the east, that is the Vêh river. 2. After them eighteen rivers flowed forth from the same source, just as the remaining waters have flowed forth from them in great multitude; as they say that they flowed out so very fast, one from the other, as when a man recites one Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">3</font></a> of a series (pa<i>d</i>îsâr). All of those, with the same water, are again mingled with these rivers, that is, the Arag river <i>and</i> Vêh river. 4. Both of them continually circulate through the two extremities of the earth, and pass into the sea; and all the regions feast owing to the discharge (zahâk) of both, which, after both arrive together at the wide-formed ocean, returns to the sources whence they flowed out; as it says in revelation, that just as the light comes in through Albûr<i>z</i> <i>and</i> goes out through Albûr<i>z</i> <a name="fr_382"></a><a href="#fn_379"><font size="1">4</font></a>, the</p>

This, too, it says, that the spirit of the Arag begged of Aûharmazd thus: 'O first omniscient creative power <a name="fr_383"></a><a href="#fn_380"><font size="1">1</font></a>! from whom the Vêh river begged for the welfare that thou mightest grant, do thou then grant <i>it</i> in my quantity!' 6. The spirit of the Vêh river similarly begged of Aûharmazd for the Arag river; <i>and</i> on account of loving assistance, one towards the other, they flowed forth with equal strength, as before the coming of the destroyer they proceeded without rapids, and when the fiend shall be destroyed <a name="fr_384"></a><a href="#fn_381"><font size="1">2</font></a> they <i>will</i> again be without rapids.</p>

The sources of the Frât <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">3</font></a> river are from the frontier of Arûm, they feed <i>upon it</i> in Sûristân, <i>and</i> it flows to the Diglat river; and of this Frât <i>it is</i> <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_398"><font size="1">4</font></a> that they produce irrigation over the land. 11.  <p>34. Regarding Frâsîyâ<i>v</i> <a name="fr_439"></a><a href="#fn_436"><font size="1">6</font></a> they say, that a thousand springs were conducted away by him into the sea Kyânsîh <a name="fr_440"></a><a href="#fn_437"><font size="1">7</font></a>, suitable for horses, suitable for camels, suitable for oxen, suitable for asses, both great and small <a name="fr_441"></a><a href="#fn_438"><font size="1">8</font></a>; and he conducted the spring Zarînmand (or golden source), which is the Hêtûmand <a name="fr_442"></a><a href="#fn_439"><font size="1">9</font></a> river they say, into the same sea; and he conducted the seven navigable waters of the source of the Va<i>k</i>aêni <a name="fr_443"></a><a href="#fn_440"><font size="1">10</font></a> river into the same sea, <i>and</i> made men settle <i>there</i>.</p>
