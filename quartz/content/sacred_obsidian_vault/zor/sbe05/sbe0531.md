 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part I (SBE05)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXII.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the nature of lakes it says in revelation, that thus many fountains of waters have come into notice, which they call lakes (var); counterparts of the eyes (<i>k</i>ashm) of men are those fountains (<i>k</i>ashmak) of waters; such as Lake <i>K</i>ê<i>k</i>ast, Lake Sôvbar, Lake Khvâri<i>z</i>em <a name="fr_461"></a><a href="#fn_457"><font size="1">3</font></a>, Lake Frazdân, Lake Zarînmand, Lake Âsvast, Lake Husru, Lake Satavês, Lake Urvis.</p>  
 <p>2. I <i>will</i> mention them also a second time: Lake <i>K</i>ê<i>k</i>ast <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_458"><font size="1">4</font></a> is in Âtarô-pâtakân, warm is the water <i>and</i> opposed to harm, so that nothing whatever is living in <i>it</i>; and its source is connected with the wide-formed ocean <a name="fr_463"></a><a href="#fn_459"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 3. Lake Sôvbar is in the upper district and country on the summit of the mountain of Tûs <a name="fr_464"></a><a href="#fn_460"><font size="1">6</font></a>; as it says, that the Sû<i>d</i>-bâhar <a name="fr_465"></a><a href="#fn_461"><font size="1">7</font></a> ('share of benefit') is propitious and good from which abounding</p>

 <p>liberality is produced. 4. Regarding Lake Khvâri<i>z</i>em <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_462"><font size="1">1</font></a> it says that excellent benefit is produced from it, <i>that is</i>, Arshi<i>s</i>ang <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_463"><font size="1">2</font></a> the rich in wealth, the well-portioned <i>with</i> abounding pleasure. 5. Lake Frazdân <a name="fr_468"></a><a href="#fn_464"><font size="1">3</font></a> is in Sagastân; they say, where a generous man, <i>who is</i> righteous, throws anything into it, it receives <i>it</i>; when not righteous, it throws <i>it</i> out again; its source also is connected with the wide-formed <i>ocean</i>. 6. Lake Zarînmand is in Hama<i>d</i>ân <a name="fr_469"></a><a href="#fn_465"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 7. Regarding Lake Âsvast it is declared that the undefiled <a name="fr_470"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">5</font></a> water which it contains is always constantly flowing into the sea, so bright and copious <a name="fr_471"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">6</font></a> that <i>one</i> might say that the sun had come into it <i>and</i> looked at Lake Âsvast, into that water which is requisite for restoring the dead in the renovation <i>of the universe</i>. 8. Lake Husru <a name="fr_472"></a><a href="#fn_468"><font size="1">7</font></a> is within fifty <a name="fr_473"></a><a href="#fn_469"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

 <p>leagues (parasang). of Lake <i>K</i>ê<i>k</i>ast. 9. Lake (or, rather, Gulf) Satavês <a name="fr_474"></a><a href="#fn_470"><font size="1">1</font></a> is that <i>already</i> written <i>about</i>, between the wide-formed ocean <i>and</i> the Pûtîk. 10. It is said that in Kamîndân is an abyss (zafar), from which everything they throw in always comes <i>back</i>, and it <i>will</i> not receive it unless alive (<i>g</i>ânvar); when they throw a living creature into it, it carries <i>it</i> down; men say that a fountain from hell is in it. 11. Lake Urvis is on Hûgar the lofty <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="#fn_471"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_457"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_461">85:3</a> Pâz. Khvârazm both here and in § 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_458"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_462">85:4</a> Av. <i>K</i>aê<i>k</i>asta of Âbân Yt. 49, Gô<i>s</i> Yt. 18, 21, 22, Ashi Yt. 38, 41, Sîrôz. 9. The present Lake Urumiyah in Âdarbî<i>g</i>ân, which is called Khe<i>g</i>est, or <i>K</i>e<i>g</i>est, by Hamdu-l-lâh Mustaûfî.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_459"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_463">85:5</a> Implying that the water is salt.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_460"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_464">85:6</a> The Kôndrâsp mountain (see Chap. XII, 24). This lake is probably a small sheet of water on the mountains near Meshhed.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_461"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_465">85:7</a> Evidently a punning etymology of the name of this lake.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_462"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_466">86:1</a> The province of Khvâri<i>z</i>em was between the Aral and Caspian, along the ancient course of the Oxus (see Chap. XVII, 5). This lake has been identified with the Aral.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_463"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_467">86:2</a> Av. ashi<i>s</i> vanguhi, 'good rectitude,' personified as a female angel whose praises are celebrated in the Ashi Ya<i>s</i>t; in later times she has been considered as the angel dispensing wealth and possessions. She is also called Ar<i>d</i> (Av. areta, which is synonymous with asha), see Chap. XXVII, 24.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_464"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_468">86:3</a> The 'Frazdânava water' of Âbân Yt. 108 and Farhang-i Oîm-khadûk, p. 17. Justi identifies it with the Âb-istâdah ('standing water') lake, south of Ghaznî. It is here represented as a salt lake.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_465"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_469">86:4</a> K20 adds, 'they say.' This lake cannot be the spring Zarînmand of Chap. XX, 34.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_466"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_470">86:5</a> Pâz. avnasti transcribed into Pahlavi is avinastag, 'unspoiled,' the equivalent of Av. anâhita in Yas. LXIV, 1, 16, Visp. I, 18.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_467"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_471">86:6</a> K20 has 'glorious' as a gloss to 'copious.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_468"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_472">86:7</a> The Av. Haosravangha of Sîroz. 9, 'the lake which is named Husrav<i>a</i>u' of Zamyâd Yt. 56. It may be either Lake Van or Lake Sevan, which are nearly equidistant from Lake Urumiyah.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_469"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_473">86:8</a> M6 has 'four leagues.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_470"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_474">87:1</a> See Chap. XIII, 9-13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_471"></a><a href="sbe0531.htm#fr_475">87:2</a> See Chaps. XII, 5, XIII, 4.</p>
