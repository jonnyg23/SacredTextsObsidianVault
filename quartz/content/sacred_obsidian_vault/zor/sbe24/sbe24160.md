 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventy-second subject is this, that when any one carries dead matter to water, <i>or</i> to fire, is</p>  
 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 337</font></a></p>  
 <p>worthy of death <a name="fr_1473"></a><a href="#fn_1462"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 2. And <a name="fr_1474"></a><a href="#fn_1463"><font size="1">2</font></a> it asserts in revelation, that <i>any</i> year when the locust comes profusely <a name="fr_1475"></a><a href="#fn_1464"><font size="1">3</font></a>, it comes for the reason that dead matter is brought to water and fire. 3. And, <i>in</i> like manner, the winter is colder, and the summer is hotter.</p>
