 <p><a name="page_318"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 318</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fifty-seventh subject is this, that it is not proper to kill a hedgehog; and, everywhere that they see <i>it</i>, it is necessary to take <i>it</i> up and carry <i>it</i> into the wilderness (sahrâ), so that it <i>may</i> go into a hole, which is ever considered a great good work. 2. Because, when a hedgehog is in <i>their</i> nest <a name="fr_1377"></a><a href="#fn_1366"><font size="1">1</font></a>, some ants will die; it will <i>also</i> catch and eat thousands of snakes and <a name="fr_1378"></a><a href="#fn_1367"><font size="1">2</font></a> other harmful <i>creatures</i>, and it eats all noxious creatures. 3. Therefore, owing to the whole <i>of this</i>, these words are expedient.</p>
