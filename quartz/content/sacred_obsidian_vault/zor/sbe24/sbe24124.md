 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The thirty-sixth subject is this, that it is strictly incumbent on mankind, on man <a name="fr_1278"></a><a href="#fn_1268"><font size="1">6</font></a> and woman, to perform the Bareshnûm <i>ceremony</i> <a name="fr_1279"></a><a href="#fn_1269"><font size="1">7</font></a>, because mankind feed on menstruous <i>matter</i> in the womb of the mother. 2. For that reason it is necessary to perform the Bareshnûm once, so that <i>one may</i> become pure from that pollution. 3. For if <i>one</i> becomes fifteen years <i>of age</i>, and does not perform the Bareshnûm, whatever he puts a hand on, the glory and purity of that thing will diminish; and it</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_297"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 297</font></a></p>  
 <p>is not proper that they put a hand on a sacred cake <i>or</i> any <i>thing</i> washed <i>with ceremony</i>.</p>  
 <p>4. In revelation it says, if any one <i>who has</i> not performed the Bareshnûm shall die, the demons make <i>him</i> <a name="fr_1280"></a><a href="#fn_1270"><font size="1">1</font></a> as though <i>he were</i> a corpse <i>kept</i> one month in the hot season. 5. And, when the soul arrives at the head of the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge, the archangels and angels complain of the stench of that soul, <i>and</i> are not able to make <i>up</i> its account and reckoning. 6. It remains at the <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge <i>and</i> is not able to pass; it experiences much repentance and has no advantage from it.</p>  
 <p>7. If it be necessary for any one to perform the Bareshnûm of the head, and he be able to do <i>it, but</i> does not do <i>it</i>, if he performs the <i>ceremonial</i> ablution of the head a thousand times, it does not become pure from that pollution <a name="fr_1281"></a><a href="#fn_1271"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and that is the pollution of Nasru<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1282"></a><a href="#fn_1272"><font size="1">3</font></a>, which is amid the veins and sinews, and the flesh and bones; it does not become pure <i>through</i> any other thing except through <i>the liquid consecrated by</i> the religious formula <a name="fr_1283"></a><a href="#fn_1273"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>8. And <i>as to</i> that person, also, who performs the Bareshnûm <i>for</i> mankind <a name="fr_1284"></a><a href="#fn_1274"><font size="1">5</font></a>, it is necessary that he be a man, a friend of the soul, a truthful speaker, <i>and</i> an abstainer, because through chastity and modesty (mastûrî) he becomes employed. 9. If complaint of any perfidy in him is publicly diffused <a name="fr_1285"></a><a href="#fn_1275"><font size="1">6</font></a>, in that disgrace (malâmat) it is necessary that the high-priests</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_298"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 298</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>should</i> dislocate his joints one by one, and it is requisite that they give him as food to the dogs. 10. So that by this action they <i>may</i> make a man observe more chastely and continently, that this result (hâʓil) of sin <i>may</i> not occur.</p>
