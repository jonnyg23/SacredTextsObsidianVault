 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'What are the vices of priests? 3. What are the vices of warriors? 4. What are the vices of husbandmen? 5. And what are the vices of artizans <a name="fr_573"></a><a href="#fn_565"><font size="1">4</font></a>?'</p>  
 <p>6. The spirit of wisdom answered (7) thus: 'The vices of priests are heresy, covetousness, negligence,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a></p>  
 <p>trafficking (sû<i>d</i>akîh) <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">1</font></a>, attention to trifles, and unbelief in the religion.</p>  
 <p>8. 'The vices of warriors are oppression, violence, promise-breaking, unmercifulness (an-a<i>v</i>ôkhshâgâ-vandîh), ostentation (dakhshîh) <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_567"><font size="1">2</font></a>, haughtiness, and arrogance.</p>  
 <p>9. 'The vices of husbandmen are ignorance, enviousness, ill-will, and maliciousness.</p>  
 <p>10. '<i>And</i> the vices of artizans are unbelief, want of thanksgiving, improper muttering <i>of prayers</i>, moroseness, and abusiveness.'</p>
