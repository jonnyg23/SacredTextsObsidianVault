 <p><a name="page_353"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 353</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-eighth subject is this, that, <i>as to any piece of</i> wood on which they carry a corpse, or on which they wash <i>it</i>, and that which may be defiled with blood and impurity, that on which menstruous <i>defilement</i>, or a bare limb, is deposited by a <i>menstruous</i> woman, and that on which they impale a human being, it is necessary to avoid the whole of these <i>pieces of</i> wood, and not to work with <a name="fr_1549"></a><a href="#fn_1538"><font size="1">1</font></a> them again, because one's dress becomes impure; and it is not proper to burn <i>them</i>. 2. It is necessary to put <i>them</i> in a place where any one, <i>who</i> pulls <i>them</i> up and stirs <i>them, will</i> not <a name="fr_1550"></a><a href="#fn_1539"><font size="1">2</font></a> bring <i>them</i> into the use of mankind.</p>
