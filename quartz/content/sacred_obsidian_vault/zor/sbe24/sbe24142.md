 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fifty-fourth subject is this, that if any one has a serving wife <a name="fr_1364"></a><a href="#fn_1353"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i> if the acquisition of a male child results <a name="fr_1365"></a><a href="#fn_1354"><font size="1">3</font></a> from her, it is suitable for adoption <i>by</i> that person <a name="fr_1366"></a><a href="#fn_1355"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and the bridge <a name="fr_1367"></a><a href="#fn_1356"><font size="1">5</font></a> is not severed <i>for</i> that person. 2. <i>But</i> if it be a female <i>child</i> it is necessary that the man <i>should</i> not be negligent (<i>gh</i>âfil) in appointing an adopted son for his own sake. 3. He <i>should</i> himself appoint a son of some relation, <i>who is</i> a friend of the soul, so that the bridge may not be severed <i>for</i> his soul.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1352"></a><a href="sbe24141.htm#fr_1363">316:1</a> The dedication to the angel Bahirâm, which begins with these words (see Sîr. I. 20).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1353"></a><a href="sbe24142.htm#fr_1364">316:2</a> A childless widow who has married again, and half of whose children, by her second husband, belong to her first one, to whom she also belongs in the other world (see Bd. XXXII, 6 n).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1354"></a><a href="sbe24142.htm#fr_1365">316:3</a> B29 has 'if a male child be born.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1355"></a><a href="sbe24142.htm#fr_1366">316:4</a> The child being considered to belong to its mother's first husband, can be only an adopted son of her second husband.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1356"></a><a href="sbe24142.htm#fr_1367">316:5</a> The <i>K</i>inva<i>d</i> bridge, which is the passage to heaven.</p>
