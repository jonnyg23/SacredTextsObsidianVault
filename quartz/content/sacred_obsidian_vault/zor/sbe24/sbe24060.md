 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore <i>is it</i> when an ignorant manwhen they bring advancement to himconsiders the learning</p>  
 <p><a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 97</font></a></p>  
 <p>and advancement of the wise and good mostly so <a name="fr_518"></a><a href="#fn_511"><font size="1">1</font></a>, through greediness, that to teach <i>it</i> to him is difficult?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'For this reason, because the ignorant man considers, in thought, his own ignorance as good as the sage <i>does</i>, in thought, his own knowledge.'</p>
