 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XCVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninety-eighth subject is this, that it is necessary for all those of the good religion, that they learn the Avesta characters <i>in</i> the presence of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_360"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 360</font></a></p>  
 <p>priests <i>and</i> teachers, so as to read, <i>and that</i> no error <i>may</i> continue <i>in</i> the Nyâyi<i>s</i>es and Ya<i>s</i>ts.</p>  
 <p>2. And it is still more expedient for priests <i>and</i> teachers, that they teach the Avesta characters to all those of the good religion <a name="fr_1580"></a><a href="#fn_1569"><font size="1">1</font></a>: and if a priest, while teaching, shows incompetence (taqʓîr), it is a great sin for him. 3. For Hôrmazd, the good <i>and</i> propitious, spoke to Zaratu<i>s</i>t thus: '<i>As to</i> every priest and teacher <i>who</i> commits a blunder in teaching <a name="fr_1581"></a><a href="#fn_1570"><font size="1">2</font></a> those of the good religion, I make him just as far from heaven as the width of the earth <a name="fr_1582"></a><a href="#fn_1571"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>
