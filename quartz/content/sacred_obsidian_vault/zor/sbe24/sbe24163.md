 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventy-fifth subject is this, that, when they wish to provide a supply of water <i>for</i> any cultivated <i>land</i>, it is first necessary that they make</p>  
 <p><a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 339</font></a></p>  
 <p>an inspection through every course <i>and</i> channel <a name="fr_1483"></a><a href="#fn_1472"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the water, <i>to ascertain</i> whether there be dead matter therein, or not; <i>and</i>, after that, <i>through</i> the water <i>in</i> like manner. 2. If they be in the middle of it, when the water is within their cultivated <i>land, and</i> dead matter comes in sight, if it be possible to ward <i>it off one</i> wards <i>it off</i>, and if it be possible to divert the water <i>one</i> diverts <i>it</i>. 3. And if the water arrives with dead matter unawares, it is no sin for them. 4. <i>But</i> if no inspection of the stream and cultivated <i>land</i> be made, and the water arrives with dead matter, those people are polluted, and it is necessary to perform the Bareshnûm <i>ceremony</i> <a name="fr_1484"></a><a href="#fn_1473"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>as regards</i> their heads.</p>
