 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLI</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Which man is the mightier? 3. Which road is the more dreadful? 4. Which account is the more</p>  
 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a></p>  
 <p>perplexing? 5. Which tie <a name="fr_438"></a><a href="#fn_433"><font size="1">1</font></a> is the pleasanter? 6. Which work is the more regretable? 7. And which gift is the more unprofitable?'</p>  
 <p>8. The spirit of wisdom answered (9) thus: 'That man is the mightier who is able to struggle with his own fiends <a name="fr_439"></a><a href="#fn_434"><font size="1">2</font></a>; (10) and, in particular, <i>he</i> who keeps these five fiends far from <i>his</i> person, (11) which are such as greediness, wrath, lust, disgrace, and discontent. 12. The road in passing over the <i>K</i>indvar bridge <a name="fr_440"></a><a href="#fn_435"><font size="1">3</font></a> is the more dreadful. 13. The account for a soul of the wicked is the more perplexing. 14. The tie of children is the pleasanter and more desirable. 15. That work is the more regretable which they do for the ungrateful. 16. <i>And</i> that gift is the more unprofitable which they give to the unworthy <a name="fr_441"></a><a href="#fn_436"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>
