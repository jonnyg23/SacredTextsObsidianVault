 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sixty-first subject is this, that it is requisite to abstain strictly from speaking falsehood; so that, every time that mankind indulge in the speaking of falsehood with fondness <a name="fr_1395"></a><a href="#fn_1384"><font size="1">2</font></a>, it is not proper to do <i>so;</i> and <a name="fr_1396"></a><a href="#fn_1385"><font size="1">3</font></a> falsehood is the chief <a name="fr_1397"></a><a href="#fn_1386"><font size="1">4</font></a> of all sins.</p>  
 <p>2. Zaratu<i>s</i>t enquired of Hôrmazd thus: 'Who is a liar like?' 3. Hôrmazd, the good <i>and</i> propitious, said: 'A liar is a co-operator with Aharman.'</p>  
 <p>4. In revelation it says that <i>there</i> is no hereafter (â<i>h</i>irat) for the speakers of falsehood, and in the midst of mankind they are contemptible (haqîr). 5. If <i>such</i> a man be powerful (muhta<i>s</i>im), <i>and there</i> be no avoiding (haDHar) him in the vicinity</p>

 <p>of mankind, they do not have any respect <i>for him</i>, and he is clearly (farʓ̣âm) an evil liar. 6. Although he possesses much wealth, it will all depart from his hands, and, finally (hâqibat), begging of mankind occurs to him, and his progeny also becomes scanty <a name="fr_1398"></a><a href="#fn_1387"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>
