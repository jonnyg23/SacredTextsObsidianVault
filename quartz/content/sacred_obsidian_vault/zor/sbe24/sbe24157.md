 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sixty-ninth subject is this, that it is not proper that sunshine <i>should</i> fall on a fire, for, every time that sunshine falls upon a fire <a name="fr_1458"></a><a href="#fn_1447"><font size="1">6</font></a>, it is a <a name="fr_1459"></a><a href="#fn_1448"><font size="1">7</font></a> sin. 2. If thou expose a fire to the sun it is a sin of three</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_335"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 335</font></a></p>  
 <p>stîrs <a name="fr_1460"></a><a href="#fn_1449"><font size="1">1</font></a>; and, if thou set down anything on the top of the fire, it is necessary that it <i>should</i> not have any holeso that the light (nûr) and strength of the fire <i>might</i> become lessso far as thou knowest.</p>
