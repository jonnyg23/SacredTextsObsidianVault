 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twenty-first subject is this, that, when they eat bread, it is necessary that <i>one should</i> recite</p>  
 <p><a name="page_283"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 283</font></a></p>  
 <p>the Ithâ-â<i>d</i>-yazamaidê <a name="fr_1213"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">1</font></a> and three Ashem-vohûs <a name="fr_1214"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">2</font></a>, especially (ba-<i>h</i>âʓʓata<i>m</i>) in the benediction-ceremonies (âfrîngân).</p>  
 <p>2. For it is declared in the good religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, that, when they wish to celebrate the sacred feast (myazd) and benediction-ceremonies, it is necessary that all persons who are seated at the feast <i>should</i> take up the <i>inward</i> prayer. 3. For each man an angel is stationed, on the right-hand <i>side</i>, and two angels for the priests. 4. <i>But</i> when they eat chatteringly, <i>or</i> relate the news (hadîTH) <a name="fr_1215"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">3</font></a>, the angels depart from them, and a demon seizes on the place of each angel <a name="fr_1216"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. And in former times the custom (hâdat) of the people would have been in this manner, that, if any one should have come to the door in the middle of the feast, that person whose Âfrîngân-precinct it might have been, <i>or</i> whoever should have gone to the expense of that Âfrîngân, would have had words with that <i>intruding</i> person <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and would have also spoken thus: 'As thou hast brought my feast to harm, give me back whatever has been the cost <a name="fr_1218"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">6</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>6. And <i>in</i> one place in revelation <i>it is</i> declared</p>

 <p>that from eating chatteringly itself is the sin, for <a name="fr_1219"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">1</font></a> every one who is chattering during the eating of bread is just like him who is smiting and harassing the angels of the spiritual <i>existences</i>.</p>  
 <p>7. Therefore, if <i>there</i> be any one who is not able to consecrate a sacred cake <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1211"><font size="1">2</font></a>, it is necessary to eat bread with the <i>inward</i> prayer of Hôrmazd, that is, the archangels. 8. And, if he does not altogether know <i>it</i>, he recites the Ithâ-â<i>d</i>-yazamaidê <a name="fr_1221"></a><a href="#fn_1212"><font size="1">3</font></a> and three Ashem-vohûs, and eats up the bread. 9. Afterwards he makes <i>his</i> mouth clean, and, four Ashem-vohûs and two Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs <i>being</i> spoken out <a name="fr_1222"></a><a href="#fn_1213"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>he is</i> then to utter words.</p>  
 <p>10. For, every time that this custom (qâhidat) is carried <i>on</i> in a place, through the first Ashem-vohû so much good work <i>has</i> arisen that it has propitiated the sacred being, the good <i>and</i> propitious; through the second Ashem-vohû so much good work <i>has</i> arisen that it has reverenced and <a name="fr_1223"></a><a href="#fn_1214"><font size="1">5</font></a> propitiated Srôsh, the righteous <a name="fr_1224"></a><a href="#fn_1215"><font size="1">6</font></a>; through the third Ashem-vohû so much good work <i>has</i> arisen that it has reverenced and <a name="fr_1225"></a><a href="#fn_1214"><font size="1">5</font></a> propitiated Khurdâ<i>d</i> <i>and</i> Ameredâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1216"><font size="1">7</font></a>, the archangels; and through the fourth Ashem-vohû so much good work <i>has</i> arisen that whatever the creator Hôrmazd has created becomes reverenced and propitiated. 11. And with each mouthful (luqmah) that is eaten, while the <i>inward</i> prayer subsists, they</p>

 <p>proclaim a blessing <i>for</i> Khurdâ<i>d</i> and Ameredâ<i>d</i>, the archangels. 12. <i>But</i>, if <i>it be</i> eaten without the <i>inward</i> prayer, as demons are <i>in</i> that place <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1217"><font size="1">1</font></a>, they say, 'Thou mightest have eaten the poison of a serpent.' 13. Therefore, take notice as to which is the better of these two.</p>
