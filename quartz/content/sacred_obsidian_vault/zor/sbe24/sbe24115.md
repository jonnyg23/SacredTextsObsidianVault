 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twenty-seventh subject is this, that is, if any affair comes forward, that they <i>should</i> thoroughly understand <a name="fr_1250"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">2</font></a> whether it be a good work, or a sin. 2. In that manner it becomes better that they make an evasion on the spot <a name="fr_1251"></a><a href="#fn_1241"><font size="1">3</font></a>, until a time <i>when</i> they make <i>it</i> known with accuracy <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1242"><font size="1">4</font></a> that that affair is a sin or a reward. 3. If they perform any affair without knowing <i>this</i>, although it be a good work, it becomes a sin for them.</p>  
 <p>4. For it is declared in revelation, that, except that which they enquire of the high-priests, no affair whatever is proper to perform. 5. Whatever wisdom <i>there be</i> for any one from his own head is only one; then, <i>as</i> two wisdoms are more than one <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1243"><font size="1">5</font></a>, it therefore makes <i>it</i> expedient to enquire of the high-priests.</p>
