 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sixty-fifth subject is this, that every one is to practise thanksgiving continually, <i>and</i> it is requisite <i>that</i> he maintains <i>it</i> through good and bad; and <i>he is</i> to keep in view <a name="fr_1422"></a><a href="#fn_1411"><font size="1">3</font></a> the benefits of Hôrmazd. 2. Because the creator <a name="fr_1423"></a><a href="#fn_1412"><font size="1">4</font></a> Hôrmazd demands two things from mankind, the one is that <i>one should</i> not commit sin, and the other is that <i>one should</i> practise thanksgiving. 3. And how much soever more grateful mankind become, through virtue <i>and</i> worthiness as regards him, they <a name="fr_1424"></a><a href="#fn_1413"><font size="1">5</font></a> grant more abundant daily provision for the grateful than that which is for other persons. 4. And <i>as to</i> every one who is not grateful <i>to</i> him, the bread that he eats becomes unlawful, and it is not proper for any one to do good <i>in connection</i> with him.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_329"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 329</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. And it is declared in revelation that when an unthankful person dies, wherever they deposit. His corpse, the archangel Spendârma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1425"></a><a href="#fn_1414"><font size="1">1</font></a> is trembling like a sheep that sees a wolf. 6. And <i>as to</i> a bird that eats that corpse, on whatever tree it rests and settles, it makes that tree wither away, and the person <i>who</i> sits down in the shadow of the tree becomes ill.</p>  
 <p>7. There are different things that it is necessary for those of the good religion to make predominant over themselves. 8. One is to exercise liberality <i>in connection</i> with the worthy; the second is to do justice; the third is to be friendly unto every one; and the fourth is to be sincere and true <a name="fr_1426"></a><a href="#fn_1415"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and to keep falsehood far from themselves. 9. And these four habits (<i>h</i>aʓlat) are the principles (aʓl) of the religion of Zaratu<i>s</i>t, and it is necessary, when thou listenest <i>to them</i> thyself, <i>that</i> thou dost not listen to any one <i>else</i> <a name="fr_1427"></a><a href="#fn_1416"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 10. Because the creator Hôrmazd says, 'O Zaratu<i>s</i>t! if thou wilt that thou become pure and saved, <i>and that</i> thou arrive at the place of the pure, do thou accomplish these two duties:11. One is this, that thou prefer the friendship of the spiritual <i>existence to that of</i> the worldly <i>one</i>, and consider the things of the world as contemptible and those of the spirit precious; on this account the glory of the world is sought <a name="fr_1428"></a><a href="#fn_1417"><font size="1">4</font></a> with scorn, and do not thou let the spirit escape. 12. The second is this, that thou speak truly with every one and act justly with me,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_330"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 330</font></a></p>  
 <p>that is, whatever thou dost not approve for thyself do not approve <a name="fr_1429"></a><a href="#fn_1418"><font size="1">1</font></a> for any one <i>else;</i> when thou hast acted in this manner thou art righteous.'</p>
