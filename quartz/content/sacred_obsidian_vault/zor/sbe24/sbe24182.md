 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XCIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninety-fourth subject is this, when a person confers a benefit <i>or</i> kindness upon any one, it is necessary that <i>the latter should</i> understand the value of it, and lay the obligation (minnat) upon himself; and, if he be able, he <i>should</i> provide a benefit to that amount (miqdâr) for that <i>person</i>.</p>  
 <p>2. It is declared in revelation, <i>that</i>, when a person confers a benefit upon any one, Hôrmazd <a name="fr_1570"></a><a href="#fn_1559"><font size="1">1</font></a> gives him ten times as much, as an equivalent. 3. And, if <i>the other</i> be not understanding <a name="fr_1571"></a><a href="#fn_1560"><font size="1">2</font></a> the justice <i>of this</i>, it is related in the commentary of the Avesta <i>in</i> this manner, that it is a great sin for him. 4. And Aharman <a name="fr_1572"></a><a href="#fn_1561"><font size="1">3</font></a> speaks like this, namely: 'That sinner is akin to me; <i>in</i> the end he will come into my hands; I will not give him into the hands of any demon, <i>but</i> I <i>will</i> inflict punishment <i>with</i> my own hands.' 5. <i>And</i> the chief priest <a name="fr_1573"></a><a href="#fn_1562"><font size="1">4</font></a> says it is necessary for all Zaratu<i>s</i>tians that they keep themselves far from this sin.</p>
