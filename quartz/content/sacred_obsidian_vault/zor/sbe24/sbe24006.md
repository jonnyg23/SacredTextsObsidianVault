 <p><a name="page_2"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 2</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">OBSERVATIONS.</h3>  
 <p>1. The division into chapters corresponds with the beginnings of the questions, and the sections are divided according to the alternating Pâzand-Sanskrit text of Nêryôsang.</p>  
 <p>2. Italics are used for any English words that are not expressed, or fully understood, in the original text, but are added to complete the sense of the translation.</p>  
 <p>3. Italics occurring in Oriental words, or names, represent certain peculiar Oriental letters (see the 'Transliteration of Oriental Alphabets' at the end of this volume). The italic <i>d</i>, <i>l</i>, <i>n</i>, <i>r</i>, <i>v</i> may be pronounced as in English; but <i>g</i> should be sounded like j, <i>h</i><i>v</i> like wh, <i>k</i> like ch in 'church,' <i>s</i> like sh, and <i>z</i> like French j.</p>  
 <p>4. In Pahlavi words the only vowels expressed in the original text are those circumflexed, initial a, and the letter ŏ; italic <i>d</i> is written like t, <i>r</i> and <i>l</i> like n or the Avesta o, <i>v</i> and <i>z</i> like <i>g</i>, and <i>z</i><i>d</i> like â in the Pahlavi character.</p>  
 <p>5. In the translation, words in parentheses are merely explanatory of those which precede them.</p>  
 <p>6. For the meanings of the abbreviations used in the notes, see the end of the Introduction.</p>  
 <p>7. The manuscripts mentioned are:</p>  
 <p>K43 (written A.D. 1569) Pahlavi, No. 43 in the University Library at Kopenhagen; upon the text of which, so far as it extends, this translation is based.</p>  
 <p>L19 (written A.D. 1520) Pâz.-Sans., No. 19 in the India Office Library at London.</p>  
 <p>MH7 (written A.D. 1809) Pârsî-Pers., No. 7 of the Haug Collection in the State Library at Munich.</p>  
 <p>MH10, a Persian Rivâyat, No. 10 of the same Collection.</p>  
 <p>PA10 (written A.D. 1649) Pâz.-Sans., No. 10 of the Anquetil Collection in the National Library at Paris.</p>  
 <p>PB6, Pâz.-Sans., No. 6 of the Burnouf Collection in the same library.</p>  
 <p>TD2, Pahlavi, belonging to Mobad Tehmuras Dinshawji Anklesaria at Bombay; upon a copy of which this translation chiefly relies in the passages (XIV, 1-XXVII, 49 and XXXIX, 31-XL, 17) missing from K43.</p>
