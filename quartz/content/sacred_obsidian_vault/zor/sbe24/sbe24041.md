 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: How many are those people whom it is necessary to consider as rich, and how many are those who are poor?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'These are the people it is necessary to consider as rich:(5) one is he who is perfect in wisdom; (6) the second, whose body is healthy, and he lives fearlessly; (7) the third, who is content with that which has come; (8) the fourth, he whose destiny is a helper in virtue; (9) the fifth, who is well-famed in the eyes of the sacred beings, and <i>by</i> the tongues of the good; (10) the sixth, whose trust is on this one, pure, good religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers; (11) <i>and</i> the seventh, whose wealth is from honesty.</p>  
 <p>12. 'And these are the people to be considered as poor:(13) one is he <i>with</i> whom there is no wisdom; (14) the second, whose body is not healthy; (15) the third, who lives in his fear, terror <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and falsehood; (16) the fourth, who is not ruling in his</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 71</font></a></p>  
 <p>own body; (17) the fifth, whose destiny is no helper; (18) the sixth, who is infamous in the eyes of the sacred beings, and <i>on</i> the tongues of the good; (19) <i>and</i> the seventh, who is old, <i>and</i> no child and kindred exist.'</p>
