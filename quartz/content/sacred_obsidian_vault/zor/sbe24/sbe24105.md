 <p><a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 278</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XVII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventeenth subject is this, that when they cut a toothpick (<i>h</i>ilâl), or a splinter which they wish to apply to the root of the teeth, it is necessary that they retain no bark. 2. For if a small quantity of bark be on it when they apply <i>it</i> to the teeth, and they cast <i>it</i> away, if a pregnant woman puts <i>her</i> foot upon it, the danger of that may be that the child comes to harm <a name="fr_1193"></a><a href="#fn_1184"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>
