 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fifty-first subject is this, that it is incumbent on every one to send a child to school, and to teach <i>it</i> something. 2. Because every duty and good work that a child performs is just as though the father and mother had performed <i>it</i> with their own hands. 3. Therefore it is necessary to make an effort, so that they <i>may</i> teach them something good, and make <i>them</i> aware of good works and sin; for they are doing that on account of their own souls, so that those <i>children may</i> be courageous in doing good works.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_1352"></a><a href="#fn_1341"><font size="1">3</font></a>. If they are not taught, they then perform less duty <i>and</i> good works, <i>and</i> less reaches the souls of the father and mother. 5. And it also happens that if they do not deliver children to school, and do not teach <i>them</i> anything, and they become bold in committing sin, that sin <a name="fr_1353"></a><a href="#fn_1342"><font size="1">4</font></a> becomes <i>fixed</i> on the necks of the father and mother.</p>  
 <p>6. Therefore they have decided rightly who <a name="fr_1354"></a><a href="#fn_1343"><font size="1">5</font></a> teach</p>
