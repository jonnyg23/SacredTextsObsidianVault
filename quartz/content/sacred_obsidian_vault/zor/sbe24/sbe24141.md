 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fifty-third subject is this, that, when, in former <a name="fr_1359"></a><a href="#fn_1348"><font size="1">3</font></a> times, any one wished to go on a journey (safar) that might have been at least <a name="fr_1360"></a><a href="#fn_1349"><font size="1">4</font></a> twelve leagues (parasang), they would have consecrated a sacred cake, so that no affliction might happen in that journey, and affairs might be according to <i>their</i> wish, and employments (su<i>gh</i>l) <a name="fr_1361"></a><a href="#fn_1350"><font size="1">5</font></a> cheerful. 2. And it is <i>still</i> incumbent on every one that, when they wish to go on a journey, <i>they are</i> to consecrate this sacred cake.</p>  
 <p>3. <i>And</i>, while the person is on the journey, he <i>should</i> order the consecration of this sacred cake, in his house, every Bahirâm day <a name="fr_1362"></a><a href="#fn_1351"><font size="1">6</font></a>, so that that person <i>may</i> arrive in safety (salâmat) at <i>his</i> house. 4.</p>
