 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninth subject is this, that it is necessary to practise abstinence from committing <i>or</i> permitting unnatural intercourse <a name="fr_1141"></a><a href="#fn_1133"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 2. For this is the chief of all sins <i>in</i> the religion: there is no worse sin than this in the good religion, and it is proper to call those <i>who commit it</i> worthy of death in reality.</p>  
 <p>3. If any one comes forth to them, and shall see them in the act, <i>and</i> is working with an axe <a name="fr_1142"></a><a href="#fn_1134"><font size="1">4</font></a>, it is requisite <i>for him</i> to cut <i>off</i> the heads or to rip up the bellies of both, <i>and</i> it is no sin for him. 4. <i>But</i> it is not proper to kill any person without the authority of high-priests and kings, except on account of committing <i>or</i> permitting unnatural intercourse.</p>  
 <p>5. For it says in revelation that unnatural intercourse is on a par with Aharman, with Afrâsiyâb <a name="fr_1143"></a><a href="#fn_1135"><font size="1">5</font></a>, with Dahâk <a name="fr_1144"></a><a href="#fn_1135"><font size="1">5</font></a>, with Tûr-i Brâ<i>d</i>ar-vakhṣh <a name="fr_1145"></a><a href="#fn_1136"><font size="1">6</font></a> who slew</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Zaratu<i>s</i>t, with Malkôs <a name="fr_1146"></a><a href="#fn_1137"><font size="1">1</font></a> who will arise, with the serpent Sruvar which existed in the days of Sâm Narîmân <a name="fr_1147"></a><a href="#fn_1138"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and as many sins as are theirs. 6. And Aharman, the evil <i>one</i>, becomes more joyful, owing to this practice, than owing to the other sins <i>which</i> have made high-priests necessary <a name="fr_1148"></a><a href="#fn_1139"><font size="1">3</font></a>; for the soul itself of that person becomes extinct.</p>  
 <p>7. And when they commit <i>the sin</i> with women, it is just the same as that with men.</p>
