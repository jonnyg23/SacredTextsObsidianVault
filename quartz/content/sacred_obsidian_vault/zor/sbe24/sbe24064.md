 <p><a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: Wherefore <i>is it</i> when <i>one</i> turns the ignorance and foolishness of an ignorant king back to knowledge and cleverness, on account of the sovereignty which is his; (3) and, <i>as to</i> a poor man, who is wise <a name="fr_570"></a><a href="#fn_562"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>one</i> turns the knowledge and sagacity, which are his, back to foolishness and uselessness, on account of the poverty?'</p>  
 <p>4. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: 'On account of the deceit and violence of the fiend <a name="fr_571"></a><a href="#fn_563"><font size="1">2</font></a> of greediness (6) men utter more words as to the manliness of every one whose wealth and power are more, and recount <i>his</i> deeds and actions more fully; (7) but, in the eyes of the angels and archangels, a poor <i>man</i> who is innocent and wise is better and more precious than a king <i>or</i> opulent <i>man</i> <a name="fr_572"></a><a href="#fn_564"><font size="1">3</font></a> who is ignorant.'</p>
