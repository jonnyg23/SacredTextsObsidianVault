 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twelfth subject is this, that, when any one dies, an order is necessary <a name="fr_1164"></a><a href="#fn_1155"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>that</i> how much soever scantier <a name="fr_1165"></a><a href="#fn_1156"><font size="1">2</font></a> clothing they are able to make a beginning <i>of</i>, the better they act. 2. Beside (illâ) <a name="fr_1166"></a><a href="#fn_1157"><font size="1">3</font></a> something become old <i>and</i> washed, anything new is not proper for <i>the purpose</i> that they <i>may</i> let <i>it</i> go upon a dead body.</p>  
 <p>3. For in the commentary of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1167"></a><a href="#fn_1158"><font size="1">4</font></a> it asserts that, if they <i>shall</i> pass on to a dead body as much as a woman's spindle makes for a single thread, with the exception of that which is unavoidable, for every single thread a black snake hangs, in that <i>other</i> world, on to the liver of that person who has made a beginning of the clothing. 4. Likewise, that dead <i>person</i> becomes his antagonist (<i>h</i>aʓ̣m), and hangs similarly <a name="fr_1168"></a><a href="#fn_1159"><font size="1">5</font></a> upon <i>his</i> skirt, and speaks thus: 'This clothing, which thou hast put on my body, devours <i>me, having</i> become worms and noxious creatures. 5. <i>My</i> name <i>was</i> put upon a sacred cake <a name="fr_1169"></a><a href="#fn_1160"><font size="1">6</font></a>, the fourth <i>day</i>, with a Ya<i>s</i>t, so that <i>there</i></p>

 <p>might be alike a benefit therefrom for my soul, and it might be alike unnecessary for thee to bear this torment (haDHâb).' 6. Owing to that, many sorrows come to that person, and he has no advantage from it. 7. Therefore, it is necessary to act <i>with</i> caution (ihtiyâ<i>t</i>), so that, how much soever the clothing be scantier <a name="fr_1170"></a><a href="#fn_1161"><font size="1">1</font></a>, they <i>may</i> make a beginning <i>of it</i>.</p>  
 <p>8. And as many as shall be able to walk after the bier (tâbût) and corpse shall walk. 9. Because every step that they go after a corpse is a good work of three hundred stîrs <a name="fr_1171"></a><a href="#fn_1162"><font size="1">2</font></a>; <i>and</i> every stîr is four dirhams, in such manner that three hundred stîrs are a thousand <i>and</i> two hundred dirhams <a name="fr_1172"></a><a href="#fn_1163"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 10. For every single step <i>there</i> is thus much good work.</p>
