 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: Which of any living <i>existence</i> (zîvêndag-1) is the worse? 3. And in wisdom who is the more unforeseeing <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">2</font></a>?'</p>  
 <p>4. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: 'A life of him is the worse, who lives in fear and falsehood <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 6. And in wisdom he is the more unforeseeing, who does not provide for <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">4</font></a> the spiritual <i>existence</i>, and attends to the worldly <i>one</i>.'</p>
