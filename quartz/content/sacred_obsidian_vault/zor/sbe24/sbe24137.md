 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLIX <a name="fr_1345"></a><a href="#fn_1334"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. The forty-ninth subject is this, that, when <i>one</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_312"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 312</font></a></p>  
 <p>gathers up a fire, it is requisite to leave <i>it for</i> a time, so that the ash-bed (bûm) of the fire <i>may</i> become cold; afterwards, <i>one is</i> to take <i>it</i> up and carry <i>it</i> to the precinct of fire. 2. It is not proper that they carry the ash-bed (zamîn) of a hot fire to the precinct of fire; so far is notorious.</p>
