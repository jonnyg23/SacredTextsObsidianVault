 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fifty-ninth subject is this, that, in the good <i>and</i> pure religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, they have not commanded the women to perform the Nyâyi<i>s</i>es <a name="fr_1387"></a><a href="#fn_1376"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 2. And <a name="fr_1388"></a><a href="#fn_1377"><font size="1">3</font></a> their Nyâyi<i>s</i>es are these, that three times every day, <i>at</i> dawn, mid-day <a name="fr_1389"></a><a href="#fn_1378"><font size="1">4</font></a> prayer, and evening prayer, they stand back in the presence of their own husbands, and fold their arms and speak thus: 'What are thy thoughts, so that I <i>may</i> think them; what is necessary for thee, so that I <i>may</i> speak it; and what is necessary for thee, so that I <i>may</i> do it?'</p>  
 <p>3. For, any command, and whatever the husband</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_321"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 321</font></a></p>  
 <p>orders, it is requisite to go about that day. 4. <i>And</i>, certainly, without the leave (riʓ̣â) of the husband <i>she is</i> to do no work, so that the Lord <i>may</i> be pleased with that wife. 5. For the satisfaction of the sacred being is in a reverence (ya<i>s</i>t) for <a name="fr_1390"></a><a href="#fn_1379"><font size="1">1</font></a> the satisfaction of the husband; so that every time that they perform work <i>by</i> command of the husband they call them righteous in the religion; and if not, what do they call them?</p>
