 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fourteenth subject is this, that, when the nails <i>are pared</i> according to custom (ba-<i>h</i>ilâl) <a name="fr_1180"></a><a href="#fn_1171"><font size="1">1</font></a>, it is necessary that they put <i>the parings</i> into a paper. 2. And it is further necessary to take the Srôsh-bâ<i>z</i> <a name="fr_1181"></a><a href="#fn_1172"><font size="1">2</font></a> <i>inwardly</i>, and <i>to utter</i> three Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs <a name="fr_1182"></a><a href="#fn_1173"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 3. And for the speaking <i>of this</i>to say with each Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <a name="fr_1183"></a><a href="#fn_1174"><font size="1">4</font></a>the Avesta is this:Paiti tê, meregha Ashô-zu<i>s</i>ta! im<i>a</i>u srv<i>a</i>u vaêdhayêmi, im<i>a</i>u srv<i>a</i>u âwaêdhayêmi; im<i>a</i>use tê srv<i>a</i>u, meregha Ashô-zu<i>s</i>ta! hyâre ar<i>s</i>tayas<i>k</i>a, karetayas<i>k</i>a, thanvare<i>k</i>a, ishavas<i>k</i>a erezifyô-parena, asna<i>k</i>a fradakhshanya paiti daêvô-Mâzainyãn <a name="fr_1184"></a><a href="#fn_1175"><font size="1">5</font></a>; ashâ vohû mananghâ yâ sruyê par<i>e</i> magaonô <a name="fr_1185"></a><a href="#fn_1176"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 4. Afterwards, <i>one</i> completes the Bâ<i>z</i> in the manner that <i>it was</i> taken <i>inwardly</i>.</p>  
 <p>5. At those two Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs, <i>with</i> which <i>one</i> completes the Bâ<i>z</i>, at each one, he makes lines (<i>h</i>a<i>t</i><i>t</i>hâ) <i>in</i> a little dust in the midst of the nail<i>-parings</i>.</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 6. And, if he does not know this Bâ<i>z</i> <a name="fr_1186"></a><a href="#fn_1177"><font size="1">1</font></a>, on uttering the Srôsh-bâ<i>z</i> and those three Yathâ-ahûvairyôs <i>he is</i> to furrow three lines, with the nail-cutter <a name="fr_1187"></a><a href="#fn_1178"><font size="1">2</font></a>, around the nail<i>-parings</i>, <i>and</i> then <i>he is</i> to complete the Bâ<i>z</i> with those Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs, <i>and</i> to put the dust, with the end of the nail-cutter, into the midst of the nail-<i>parings</i>, and carry <i>them</i> to a desert <i>spot</i>. 7. It is necessary that he <i>should</i> carry <i>a hole</i> down <i>through</i> four finger<i>-breadths</i> of earth, and, <i>having</i> placed <i>the nail-parings</i> in that spot, he puts the soil overhead.</p>  
 <p>8. For Hôrmazd, the good <i>and</i> propitious, has created a bird which they call Ashô-zu<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1188"></a><a href="#fn_1179"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and they call <i>it</i> the bird of Bahman <a name="fr_1189"></a><a href="#fn_1180"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 9. They also call it the owl, <i>and</i> it eats nails.</p>  
 <p>10. It is altogether necessary that they do not leave <i>them</i> unbroken, for they <i>would</i> come into use as weapons (silâh) of wizards <a name="fr_1190"></a><a href="#fn_1181"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 11. And they have also said that, if they fall in the midst of food, <i>there</i> is danger of pulmonary consumption.</p>
