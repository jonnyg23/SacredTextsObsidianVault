 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XCII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The ninety-second subject is this, that it is necessary to properly maintain the sacred fire <a name="fr_1560"></a><a href="#fn_1549"><font size="1">4</font></a> and some one who <i>will</i> work with assiduity (kandân) to provide <a name="fr_1561"></a><a href="#fn_1550"><font size="1">5</font></a> maintenance and sympathy for it. 2. And the supply of its firewood is entirely <i>in</i> such a manner that they burn this year the firewood of last year <a name="fr_1562"></a><a href="#fn_1551"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 3. <i>At</i> midnight they make <i>it</i> blaze up, <i>and</i> put incense upon <i>it in</i> such a manner that the wind carries off its scent.</p>  
 <p>4. The demons and fiends rush away, because there is the glory of the sacred fire that we are able to make a living existence in the midst of this</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_356"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 356</font></a></p>  
 <p>people. 5. For, if no fiend <a name="fr_1563"></a><a href="#fn_1552"><font size="1">1</font></a> and the glory of the archangels and the day of the sacred fire had not existed, it would not have been possible to produce the living existence any day. 6. And, therefore, it makes <i>it</i> expedient that they supply a fire-attendant to maintain the firewood and fire, so that a seeking for <i>its</i> safety (hâfiyat) <i>may</i> come into <i>operation</i>, and they <i>may</i> know a support and protection for it that would be acceptable.</p>
