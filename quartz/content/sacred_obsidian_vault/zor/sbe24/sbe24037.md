 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXXI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'What is the business of the priests, warriors, and husbandmen <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">5</font></a>, each separately?'</p>  
 <p>3. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'The business of the priests is to maintain the religion properly; (5) and to perform the ceremonial and invocation of the sacred beings well <i>and</i> with attention,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (6) and <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">1</font></a> the decrees, decisions <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">2</font></a>, custom, and control <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">3</font></a>, as revealed by the pure, good religion of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers. 7. To make people aware of the goodness of good works <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">4</font></a>; (8) and to show the way to heaven, and the danger and avoidance of hell.</p>  
 <p>9. 'The business of the warriors is to defeat the enemy; (10) and to keep their own country and land (bûm) <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">5</font></a> unalarmed and tranquil.</p>  
 <p>11. 'And the business of the husbandmen is to perform tillage and cultivation; (12) and, to the extent of <i>their</i> ability, to keep the world invigorated and populous.'</p>
