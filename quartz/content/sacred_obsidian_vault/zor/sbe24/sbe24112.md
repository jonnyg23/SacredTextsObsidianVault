 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twenty-fourth subject is this, that when an infant is born from <i>its</i> mother it is necessary that they give <i>it</i> the consecrated Hôm-juice <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1222"><font size="1">2</font></a>, on this account, that understanding (haql), wisdom, and</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_287"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 287</font></a></p>  
 <p>knowledge <i>may</i> more abundantly get to it, and the want (ablat) of them <i>may</i> come more scantily upon <i>it</i> <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1223"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. If, at that time, they do not perform the consecration, they <i>should</i> take forth a little Hôm for it <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1224"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and recite <a name="fr_1235"></a><a href="#fn_1225"><font size="1">3</font></a> a Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <a name="fr_1236"></a><a href="#fn_1226"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and put a trifle of water into <i>it</i>, and make Hôm-juice of that, and give <i>it</i> to the infant, and afterwards <a name="fr_1237"></a><a href="#fn_1227"><font size="1">5</font></a> milk.</p>
