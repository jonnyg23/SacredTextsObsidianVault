 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The seventy-third subject is this, that, when a cow or a goat <i>has</i> eaten dead matter <a name="fr_1476"></a><a href="#fn_1465"><font size="1">4</font></a>, in any place, nothing whatever of <i>its</i> flesh, <i>or</i> milk, <i>or</i> hair, <i>should</i> come into use for one year. 2. After that one year it is clean: and, if it be pregnant, its young one is likewise not clean <i>for</i> one year.</p>  
 <p>3. And if a domestic fowl <i>has</i> eaten dead matter, its flesh and eggs are, <i>in</i> like manner, not clean <i>for</i> one year.</p>
