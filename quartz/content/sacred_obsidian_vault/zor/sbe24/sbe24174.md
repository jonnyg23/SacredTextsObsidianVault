 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part III (SBE24)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1885], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The eighty-sixth subject is this, that it is not proper to kill a beaver <a name="fr_1533"></a><a href="#fn_1522"><font size="1">4</font></a>; <i>but</i>, if they see <i>it in</i> any place, it is necessary to take <i>it</i> up and carry <i>it</i> to running water. 2. For, in the commentary of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> it is ranked <a name="fr_1534"></a><a href="#fn_1523"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>as</i> a great sin for the <i>killer</i> <a name="fr_1535"></a><a href="#fn_1524"><font size="1">6</font></a>; and, <i>as to</i> every one who kills a beaver, the source of his seed becomes exhausted.</p>
