 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twenty-fifth question is that which you ask thus: How are the nature of heaven (vahi<i>s</i>tô), <i>and</i> the comfort and pleasure which are in heaven?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that <i>it is</i> lofty, exalted, and</p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>supreme, most brilliant, most fragrant, and most pure, most <i>supplied with</i> beautiful existences, most desirable, and most good, and the place <i>and</i> abode of the sacred beings (ya<i>z</i><i>d</i>ânŏ). 3. And in it are all comfort, pleasure, joy, happiness (vasî<i>d</i>âgîh), and welfare, more <i>and</i> better even than the greatest <i>and</i> supremest welfare <i>and</i> pleasure in the world; and there is no want, pain, distress, <i>or</i> discomfort whatever <i>in</i> it; and its pleasantness and the welfare of the angels are from that constantly-beneficial place (gâs), the full <i>and</i> undiminishable space (gûn<i>g</i>) <a name="fr_172"></a><a href="#fn_171"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the good and boundless world.</p>  
 <p>4. And the .freedom of the heavenly from danger from evil in heaven is like unto <i>their</i> freedom from disturbance, and the coming of the good angels is like unto <i>the heavenly ones'</i> own good works provided. 5. This prosperity (freh-hastŏ) and welfare of the spiritual <i>existence</i> is <i>more</i> than that of the world, as much as that which is unlimited <i>and</i> everlasting is <i>more</i> than that which is limited and demoniacal (<i>s</i>êdânîkŏ).</p>
