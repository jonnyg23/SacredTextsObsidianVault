 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XI.</h3>  
 <p>1. The tenth question is that which you ask thus: Does the growth which increases become as commendable in the fourth night <a name="fr_83"></a><a href="#fn_82"><font size="1">2</font></a> as the original good work <a name="fr_84"></a><a href="#fn_83"><font size="1">3</font></a> in <i>his</i> possession, or does it become otherwise?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that <i>it</i> is otherwise; for the original good work stands up opposing sin, and the growing good work <a name="fr_85"></a><a href="#fn_84"><font size="1">4</font></a> stands up opposed to the growth of sin.</p>
