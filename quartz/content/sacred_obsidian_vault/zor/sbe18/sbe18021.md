 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The twelfth question is that which you ask thus: In the fourth night do they score off (ba<i>r</i>â angârênd) the sin by the good works, and does he go by the residue (bôn); or do they inflict punishment on him for the sin which <i>has</i> happened to him, and give reward <i>and</i> recompense for the good works which he <i>has</i> done?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that at dawn of the third night the account is prepared it is said, and about the sin which he <i>has</i> atoned for, <i>and</i> the good work which is its equivalent (â<i>v</i>âr) <i>there</i> is no need for account, since the account is about the good works which may be appropriated by him <i>as</i> his own, and about the sin which may remain <i>in</i> him as <i>its</i> origin. 3. Because the origin of it (the sin atoned for) remains distinct, and it is cancelled (astar<i>d</i>ŏ)</p>  
 <p><a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 32</font></a></p>  
 <p>by it (the good work), they balance it therewith; and they weigh the excess and deficiency, as it may be, of the <i>other</i> good works <i>and</i> sin.</p>  
 <p>4. Of those living, at the just, impartial (a<i>k</i>afsi<i>s</i>nŏ) balance the <i>man</i> of proper habits (dâ<i>d</i>ŏ), whose good works are more, when sin <i>has</i> happened to him, undergoes a temporary (vidanâîk) punishment and becomes eternally cleansed by the good works; and he of improper habits, of much sin and little good works, attains temporary enjoyment by those good works, <i>but</i> through the sin which they perceive <i>in</i> him he is suffering punishment unto the resurrection.</p>
