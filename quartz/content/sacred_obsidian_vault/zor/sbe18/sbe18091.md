 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXXXIV.</h3>  
 <p>1. <i>As to</i> the eighty-third question and reply, that which you ask is thus: Is it desirable to give</p>  
 <p><a name="page_246"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 246</font></a></p>  
 <p>in excess the gift <i>for</i> the ceremonial <i>which</i> it is not desirable to diminish?</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that <i>it</i> is proper not to diminish a gift where <i>it is</i> the gift <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">1</font></a> for a ceremonial, and the reasons for it are many. 3. One is this, that a gift is the money which in another good work suffices for the accomplishment of the good work, <i>and</i> the good work of a righteous gift <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">2</font></a> is a great good work, <i>and</i> not to diminish <i>it</i> is sure worthiness among the explainers. 4. When the sacred feast <a name="fr_928"></a><a href="#fn_925"><font size="1">3</font></a> <i>and</i> the gift for the ceremonial are supplied in excess, even that which is an excess of gift is an excess of liberality to the performers of the ceremonial, <i>and has</i> realized (frârâstŏ) an excess of good works that is commendable.</p>
