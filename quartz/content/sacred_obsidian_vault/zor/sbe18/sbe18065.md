 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part II (SBE18)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1882], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The fifty-seventh question is that which you ask thus: How many kinds of family guardianship and adoption are <i>there?</i></p>  
 <p>2. The reply is this, that it is said <i>there</i> are three kinds, which are the existent, the provided, and the appointed. 3. An adopted son who is existent is such as a wife who may be privileged, <i>or</i> an only daughter is a kind of adopted son owing to confidence in herself, such as happens when there is no wife, <i>and</i> a daughter for whom there is no husband, <i>and</i> none is provided, is the one <i>that</i> has remained.</p>  
 <p>4. An adopted son <i>who</i> is provided is such <i>as</i> a son that is acknowledged, who is accepted by one's self <a name="fr_705"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and <i>free</i> from <i>being</i> appointed, or <i>from</i> necessity <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. And an adopted son who is appointed is he who is to be appointed among the relations who are suitable for adoption--and are nearest to him who is to be appointed <i>as</i> adopted son--and the ministers (pa<i>d</i>ân) of religion, <i>and</i> he performs the duty of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 192</font></a></p>  
 <p>family guardianship <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">1</font></a>; he who is the appointed <i>one</i> is he who is appointed by the men <i>who are</i> the nearest relations (nabânazdi<i>s</i>tânŏ) on account of proximity.</p>
