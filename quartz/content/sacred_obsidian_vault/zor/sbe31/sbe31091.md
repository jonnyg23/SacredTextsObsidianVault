 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Zend Avesta, Part III (SBE31)</i>, L.H. Mills, tr. [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">VISPARAD XIV (SP. XVI) <a name="fr_1480"></a><a href="#fn_1466"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. (We worship Ahura Mazda, the holy Lord of the ritual order <a name="fr_1481"></a><a href="#fn_1467"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and we sacrifice to the Gâtha Ahunavaiti) with its measures, and word-structure, and its Zand, with its questions and counter-questions, with its words and its metric feet. And we sacrifice to these as well-recited, and now in the course of being recited; as well-worshipped, and now in the course of being used in worship <a name="fr_1482"></a><a href="#fn_1468"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 2. (Yea, we sacrifice to it) in</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_357"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 357</font></a></p>  
 <p>its own 'wisdom' <a name="fr_1483"></a><a href="#fn_1469"><font size="1">1</font></a>, in its own 'clearness' <a name="fr_1484"></a><a href="#fn_1470"><font size="1">2</font></a>, in its own 'loving intention' <a name="fr_1485"></a><a href="#fn_1470"><font size="1">2</font></a>, in its sovereignty, and its own ritual order, and its 'acquired boon' <a name="fr_1486"></a><a href="#fn_1470"><font size="1">2</font></a>, which is also that given by Ahura Mazda for the promotion of piety, for that thought which originates from the 'heart-devoted self' <a name="fr_1487"></a><a href="#fn_1470"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. (Sp. Chapter XVII.) Also we worship the Ahuna-vairya, the holy lord of the ritual order, the holy lord with its Ahû and its Ratu [(Pâzand); for He is the one with the title Ahû and Ratu, who is Ahura Mazda <a name="fr_1488"></a><a href="#fn_1471"><font size="1">3</font></a>]. 4. And we sacrifice to the constituent parts of the Gâtha Ahunavaiti, to its chapters, and its metrical lines, its words, and word-structure, [and to its heard-recital, and memorised recital, its continuous and its steadfast offering].</p>
