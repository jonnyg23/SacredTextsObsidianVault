 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VI</h3>  
 <p>1. On account of the Zand of the Nâ<i>d</i>ar <a name="fr_59"></a><a href="#fn_59"><font size="1">1</font></a> not reaching us, the Avesta is retained, for teaching, recital, <i>and</i> ceremony, because <i>it has</i> come unto us with authority.</p>  
 <p>2. Of righteousness the excellence is perfect excellence.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_59"></a><a href="sbe37009.htm#fr_59">15:1</a> Corresponding to the fifth word, ratu<i>s</i>, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the sixth Nask in other Rivâyats. Owing to its Zand, or Pahlavi version, having been lost, the author does not undertake to describe its contents; but the Rivâyats state that it consisted of thirty-five ʓûrat, or compilations, about astronomy and astrology. The traditional name Nâ<i>d</i>ar, or Nâ<i>d</i>ûr, is probably a misreading; as Vakhtar (for Vakhttar), 'more destined,' and Vakhtvar, 'fate-bringing,' would be more intelligible readings of the same letters.</p>
