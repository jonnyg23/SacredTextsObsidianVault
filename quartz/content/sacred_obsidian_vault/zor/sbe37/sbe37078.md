 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The sixth fargar<i>d</i>, Khshmaibyâ <a name="fr_793"></a><a href="#fn_787"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is about the complaint of Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan <a name="fr_794"></a><a href="#fn_788"><font size="1">2</font></a> to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, when she sat at the creation in the assembly of the archangels, as regards the abundant disease and misery which she saw spiritually <i>would</i> come upon her in a bodily existence, through beating, slaughtering, <i>and</i> wounding, stealing, plundering, and presenting, <i>by</i> him of vicious actions <i>and</i> worse desires, as a bribe to him who is an evil-ruling villain (mar), and the operation thereof: the bad ownership, wrongful investigation, false evidence, and making captive (var<i>d</i>akŏ), <i>by</i> him who is wrathful <i>and</i> oppressive through greed and envy, from the warm cowshed <i>and</i> the effective <i>and</i> diligent guardianship of the herd's dog (pasû<i>s</i>-haûrvô), to that which is a cold <i>and</i> hastily-constructed place; <i>or by</i> him who is seeking meat <i>with</i> a merciless hand through making <i>her</i> distantly separated from<i></i> her young. 2. Also their explanation and extenuation (kâstanŏ) <a name="fr_795"></a><a href="#fn_789"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and the causing of misery of many kinds thereby, 'which is no affliction to them when the wind that is cold <a name="fr_796"></a><a href="#fn_790"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>or</i> even that which is hot, comes upon me; <i>which</i> is no affliction to them when, the <i>untimely offspring of</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 238</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>my</i> womb <i>being</i> cast away, they slaughter me; and is no affliction to them when the serpent, the leech (khun-garâî) <a name="fr_797"></a><a href="#fn_791"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>or</i> even the foulest of noxious creatures gnaws me.' 3. And the petition of Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan <i>was</i> thus: 'Do not appoint me to a worldly <i>existence</i> and that awful misery, <i>or</i>, if thou appointest me to a worldly <i>existence</i>, produce it for me without life (aûzû<i>s</i>tânîhâ), so that I <i>may</i> be without feeling and may want that distressing <a name="fr_798"></a><a href="#fn_792"><font size="1">2</font></a> pain; <i>it is</i> created for the mighty, through whose assistance there is a capability of affording protection <i>to</i> me, even though the Kaî and Karap <a name="fr_799"></a><a href="#fn_793"><font size="1">3</font></a> exist.'</p>  
 <p>4. And, <i>together</i> with the just complaint of Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan, and the compassion of the archangels as to that complaint, <i>there is</i> then the creation of the creatures, among whom the greatest and best <a name="fr_800"></a><a href="#fn_794"><font size="1">4</font></a> is mankind, for fighting <i>and</i> subduing the destroyer, even though joined together with a complaint of wounding and affliction like <i>that of</i> Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan, <i>and</i> Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan arose with greater judiciousness than an absence of creation even with freedom from disturbance by the Kaîs <a name="fr_801"></a><a href="#fn_795"><font size="1">5</font></a>; on account of the necessity of preparing <i>for</i> the living of mankind through the assistance of cattle, Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan <i>was</i> produced for the material bodily existence and assistance of mankind. 5. And, on account of little feeling for her worldly misery, the breeding (mâyînî<i>d</i>anŏ) of cattle <i>was</i> the arraying</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_239"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 239</font></a></p>  
 <p>of strife; the advancement of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion of Zaratû<i>s</i>t in the world by Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan, on the production of Zaratû<i>s</i>t for the assistance of cattle; <i>and</i> the preservation of cattle and other good creations through complete satisfaction <i>at</i> the progress <i>of</i> the religion.</p>  
 <p>6. This, too, <i>was</i> said to Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan, that is: 'I assert unto thee the passing away of devastation, that is, the existence of a remedy <i>for</i> the misery owing to the evil spirit <a name="fr_802"></a><a href="#fn_796"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>for</i> which no creature would be produced by meme who am Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>when a remedy <i>for</i> the misery owing to him had not been known to me.' 7. This, too, that the wish of the evil spirit was thus: 'Thou shouldst never produce a creature, O Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>! and <i>there</i> should be here no spiritual lordship, no priestly authority, and no desire for perfect righteousness, <i>or</i> necessity for duty and good works.' 8. The enquiry of Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan, thus: 'For whom am I appointed and formed? <a name="fr_803"></a><a href="#fn_797"><font size="1">2</font></a>' and the reply to her, thus: 'For him who is diligent <i>and</i> moderate <a name="fr_804"></a><a href="#fn_798"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>9. Also the friend and nourishment (srâyi<i>s</i>nŏ) begged for cattle <i>by</i> Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan, the righteous man produced for the assistance of cattle <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and the sweetness in water and plants for the nourishment of cattle, so that he is privileged to feed <i>and</i> keep cattle who gives them pasture <i>in</i> reality, and is also diligent <i>in</i> the production of cattle, that is, he gives them pasture, and is thereby proclaimed a cattle-guardian (pasû<i>s</i>-haûrvô) for them who makes the cattle fully develope <a name="fr_805"></a><a href="#fn_799"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and also</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_240"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 240</font></a></p>  
 <p>he who gives the wicked Wrath, the foreigner, a beating, so that he may make <i>him</i> stupefied <a name="fr_806"></a><a href="#fn_800"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 10. The development of cattle <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, advice to mankind as to moderate eating <a name="fr_807"></a><a href="#fn_801"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and the grievous bridge <i>judgment</i> of him who <i>has</i> unlawfully produced distress <i>for</i> the cattle whom Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan is kindly regarding, with loving eyes <a name="fr_808"></a><a href="#fn_802"><font size="1">3</font></a>, in the spiritual <i>existence</i>, in bodily contact with (ham-kerpŏ-î) the archangels and in bodily contact with the light of the sun, so that <i>her</i> hands are more powerful; she who replies to the sacred beings, and the sacred beings reply to her <a name="fr_809"></a><a href="#fn_803"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>11. About the statements of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>there is</i> this, too, that is: 'I am a calculator of those words <a name="fr_810"></a><a href="#fn_804"><font size="1">5</font></a> by which they assert <i>that</i> the existence of worldly beings is for the sake of that of both existences; I am aware <i>of</i> the actions which are practised by those in the material existence, <i>both</i> demons and men; of whatever they practise <a name="fr_811"></a><a href="#fn_805"><font size="1">6</font></a> I am the decider <i>and</i> lord, and it is such as my will <i>requires</i> <a name="fr_812"></a><a href="#fn_806"><font size="1">7</font></a>, even for the last change of existence; and I look upon all that with that wisdom and sagacity of mine which was, which is, and which ever <i>will</i> be.'</p>  
 <p>12. The formation of a reward for worldly beings <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, through the propitious liturgy (mânsars<i>p</i>end) <a name="fr_813"></a><a href="#fn_807"><font size="1">8</font></a> which has become the precursor of the benefiters; that is, their high-priest, who has</p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_241"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 241</font></a></p>  
 <p>a propitiousness and intelligence that are all-beneficial, is he <i>with</i> the liturgy. 13. And about the uniqueness and incomparableness of Zaratû<i>s</i>t among mankind, through <i>his</i> desire for righteousness and <i>his</i> understanding the means of defeating the destroyer <a name="fr_814"></a><a href="#fn_808"><font size="1">1</font></a> and teaching the creatures.</p>  
 <p>14. Righteousness is perfect excellence.</p>
