 <p><a name="page_476"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 476</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">XVI. GANABÂ-SAR-NI<i>G</i>A<i>D</i> NASK <a name="fr_1819"></a><a href="sbe37145.htm#fn_1770"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>Dk. VIII, Chap. XXII, <a name="fr_1820"></a><a href="sbe37145.htm#fn_1771"><font size="1">2</font></a>, probably refers to the passage which contained the statement thus quoted in Sls. X, 13:'<i>It is</i> revealed <i>in</i> the Ganabâ-sar-ni<i>g</i>ê<i>d</i>, where a day in the year is indicated, that the <i>sacred thread-</i>girdle of every one who shall be one day more than fourteen years <i>and</i> three months <i>old</i> <a name="fr_1821"></a><a href="sbe37145.htm#fn_1771"><font size="1">2</font></a> is to be tied on<i>it is</i> better so than when he remains unto fifteen years, <i>and then</i> ties on the girdlewho is more cared for, that way, than those of five (<i>or</i> nine) months in the womb of the mother, on <i>whom</i> they should put <i>it</i>.'</p>
