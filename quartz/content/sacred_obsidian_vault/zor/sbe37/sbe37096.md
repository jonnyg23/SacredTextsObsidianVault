 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLVII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Bakŏ Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. Propitiation <i>for</i> the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> and all angels.</p>  
 <p>2. The first of the twenty-two fargar<i>d</i>s of the Bakŏ <a name="fr_1202"></a><a href="#fn_1189"><font size="1">1</font></a> is the Ahunavair <a name="fr_1203"></a><a href="#fn_1190"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the Bakân <a name="fr_1204"></a><a href="#fn_1191"><font size="1">3</font></a>, about the production <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, before every creation apart from the archangel <a name="fr_1205"></a><a href="#fn_1192"><font size="1">4</font></a>, <i>and</i> on the solicitation of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_304"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 304</font></a></p>  
 <p>the archangel, of the form <i>of</i> words (rastakŏ mi<i>l</i>ayâ) which is the innermost and most comprehensive encompassment (parvand) <i>and</i> best-congregated embodiment (vêh-ramaktûm kerpŏ) of the intelligent omniscience of the religion <a name="fr_1206"></a><a href="#fn_1193"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. The divisions of <i>this</i> germ of germs, <i>and</i> the origin of the other primitive sayings of the good religion, are the divisibility of the portions (bakŏ) <i>of</i> the Ahunavair. 4. The Ahû of the Ahû-vairyô of the Ahunavair is the first creature <a name="fr_1207"></a><a href="#fn_1194"><font size="1">2</font></a> which, as regards the first, is specially that creature which is really <i>derived</i> from the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and its adaptation is owing to mankind. 5. The thought (mit) <a name="fr_1208"></a><a href="#fn_1195"><font size="1">3</font></a> that exists with the first is with the word that is Vairyô, his 'will,' which is in the second created existence (dâmîh), which, as regards the first, is specially the primitive secondary state (da<i>d</i>îgarîh) of those who are specially characterised by it, who exist as <i>it were</i> with <i>that</i> character, <i>and</i> have become, <i>in</i> that way, in association <i>with</i> the second creature. 6. The conjunction of the first creaturewhose origin (yehevûni<i>s</i>nŏ), which is the liturgy, is a co-existence whose origin <i>had</i> occurredis the source for the saying; and the distribution of the portions thereof is the whole saying of a liturgical kind; also its name is Yathâ-ahû-vairyô, the spirit through which it is set going is the lore <i>of</i> the religion, needful among the creatures, the creations <i>arose</i> through wisdom for that purpose, and they, too, <i>were</i> produced on the solicitation of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_305"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 305</font></a></p>  
 <p>the archangel <a name="fr_1209"></a><a href="#fn_1196"><font size="1">1</font></a>; besides this, that archangels <i>are</i> wise in speaking, and through wisdom are they archangels.</p>  
 <p>7. And this, too, about the same words, that the statement is the best-worded which is spoken, <i>or</i> to be spoken <a name="fr_1210"></a><a href="#fn_1197"><font size="1">2</font></a>; and the obscurity is not about the sound of the word-elements, but about the manifold nature of the actual meaning (kabedîh-i sang-î<i>k</i>ŏ), which is the character of the statement, in the words of the epitome. 8. This, too, that mankind guard the soul from hell by learning, reciting, and practising <i>it</i>, and the body from death <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1198"><font size="1">3</font></a> by likewise perpetually persevering therein.</p>  
 <p>9. This, too, that, <i>as to</i> the first apportionment of the Ahunavair, whose name is the Bakân Ahunavair, when, thoroughly accomplishing <i>it</i> (avavi<i>d</i>âr) unanxiously (asû<i>d</i>akîhâ), <i>one</i> chants <i>it</i> in a ceremonial, the good work is as when <i>one</i> chants a hundred authorities (ra<i>d</i>îh) of the Gâthas, thoroughly accomplishing <i>them</i> unanxiously <a name="fr_1212"></a><a href="#fn_1199"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and when, accomplishing <i>it</i> (vi<i>d</i>âr) anxiously, <i>one</i> chants <i>it</i>, such a ceremonial amounts to <i>as much</i> as ten <i>with any other authority</i> <a name="fr_1213"></a><a href="#fn_1200"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 10. This, too, that, through the same apportionment, while <i>one</i> solemnizes the summing up of the first completion <a name="fr_1214"></a><a href="#fn_1201"><font size="1">6</font></a>, which is the Stô<i>d</i>-ya<i>s</i>t, as it becomes the rite of <i>one</i> newly initiated (navak-n<i>â</i>par) <a name="fr_1215"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">7</font></a>, on that day</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_306"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 306</font></a></p>  
 <p>they make the soul of the solemnizer pass three times into the supreme heaven <a name="fr_1216"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>11. About the grievous sinfulness of imperfectly accomplishing (avi<i>d</i>âr) the Bakân Ahunavair <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>12. This, too, that it is made by him in subjection <i>to</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, as the first creature made, who gives the body in service to him who is the ruler, <i>and</i> in discipleship to him who is the high-priest of the religion <a name="fr_1218"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">3</font></a>; for this reason, because they are suitable <i>for</i> lordship and mastership in the worldly <i>existence</i>. 13. He who is the highest lord <i>and</i> master is the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and, owing to the same reason, when it made their subjection that <i>to</i> the creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, he has made <i>it</i> as the first creature made.</p>  
 <p>14. This, too, that it is taught by it to keep the body in the service of the king of kings <a name="fr_1219"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">4</font></a>, whose origin Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> keeps in <i>his</i> possession; for this reason, because, when <i>his</i> origin is kept in the possession <i>of</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> is over his own if a good ruler is made; him who is thus prepared, when also the worldly existence is necessary <i>for</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, he maintains as ruler when the creation is instructed.</p>  
 <p>15. This, too, that the reward of Vohûman is appropriated (khvêsinî<i>d</i>ŏ) by him who indicates anything which is virtuous, who also utters virtuous recitation, <i>and</i> who likewise teaches perfect abstinence from sin to mankind <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 16. For this reason, because the indication of anything virtuous, the utterance of recitation wisely, <i>and</i> abstinence from</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_307"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 307</font></a></p>  
 <p>sin are, as <i>it were</i>, a lodgment materially in good people owing spiritually to the archangels, Vohûman <i>being</i> more particularly the instigator therein; and, owing to the same reason, <i>he</i> in whom there is a like proficiency is of like good works with Vohûman, and adapted to the good works arises the like reward.</p>  
 <p>17. This, too, that the dominion is given to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> by him who may perform those <i>works</i>, is manifest from <i>the phrase</i> Ta<i>d</i> mazdâ tavâ khshathrem, &amp;c. and its meaning, which is this: 'That, <i>O</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>! is this dominion of thine, <i>by</i> which benefits (vehagânŏ) are given <i>to</i> him who is justly living <i>and</i> poor <a name="fr_1221"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">1</font></a>.' 18. Which is a deliverance for this reason, because Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> created no dominion for the more particular preservation of the poor <i>and</i> the creatures of the worldly <i>existence</i> from the destroyer; <i>but</i>, for the purpose of control over the dominion of <i>him</i> whose strength of rule is the cause of preservation for the poorwhich is continually the wish of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>the dominion is given to Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>.</p>  
 <p>19. <i>And</i> this, too, that, through preservation from the adversary, he has assisted his poor who have preserved friendship for the Spîtâmân <a name="fr_1222"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">2</font></a>; the adversity <i>of</i> the creatures is the advancement of religion, by supporting the religion; and a friend of the Spîtâmân becomes an assistant of the supporters of religion. 20. About the entrance (dên yâtûn<i>d</i>akîh) of the destroyer of the creatures from without <a name="fr_1223"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">3</font></a>, <i>and</i> the helplessness of the beneficent spirit owing thereto.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_308"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 308</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. About the girding on of this saying of the religion of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> by the three degrees (pa<i>d</i>mân), which are good thoughts, good words, <i>and</i> good deeds; <i>by</i> the four classes, which are priesthood, warriorship, husbandry, and artisanship; and <i>by</i> the five chieftainships, which are house-rule, village-rule, tribe-rule, province-rule, and the supreme Zaratû<i>s</i>tship; and the one summing up (hanger<i>d</i>îkîh) which is the liberality of the good ruler <a name="fr_1224"></a><a href="#fn_1211"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>22. Righteousness is perfect excellence.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1189"></a><a href="sbe37096.htm#fr_1202">303:1</a> The third of the Nasks and fourth of the Gâthic division (see Bk. VIII, Chap. I, 9, 12). It is an analytical commentary upon the Gâthas and the texts associated with them in the two preceding Nasks, devoting a separate fargar<i>d</i> to each hâ, and selecting very short phrases, or portions (Av. bagha), for explanation and comment; so short that it is usually difficult to identify them in their Pahlavi disguise. The first three fargar<i>d</i>s are still extant in Yas. XIX-XXI, and a translation of their Pahlavi versions will be found in the Nask Fragments at the end of this volume; but whether the Pahlavi versions, consulted by the writer of the Dinka<i>r</i><i>d</i>, were identical with those in the present Yasna is uncertain.</p>
