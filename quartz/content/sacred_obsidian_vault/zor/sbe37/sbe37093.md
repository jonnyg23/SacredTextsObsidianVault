 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLIV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. <i>In</i> the twenty-first fargar<i>d</i>, Vohû-khshathrem <a name="fr_1148"></a><a href="#fn_1136"><font size="1">4</font></a>, it is said by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> thus: 'I produced, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! the desire <i>for</i> a good ruler <a name="fr_1149"></a><a href="#fn_1137"><font size="1">5</font></a>;' and this, too, <i>is said</i>, that, when <i>there is</i> a desire <i>for</i> a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_295"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 295</font></a></p>  
 <p>good ruler suitable for a share of the world, whoever is suitable for a share of the world [is a development of that character also, owing to the share which is given him, and by him who is himself also developing the character, by giving him a share] <a name="fr_1150"></a><a href="#fn_1138"><font size="1">1</font></a>, giving the share is producing a helper (vi<i>g</i>î<i>d</i>âr), production of a helper is a perfect action <a name="fr_1151"></a><a href="#fn_1139"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and superiority of action is owing to thought <i>and</i> speech.</p>  
 <p>2. About <i>the place</i> where the best wealth is the produce of water, earth, and plants; also its best supplication is lamentation for the religion, and the sovereignty is liberality. 3. About favours <i>being</i> begged from the sacred beings, even with words controverting the response of the sacred beings; the favours <i>for</i> the worthy are to be contended <i>for</i> worthily. 4. About the connection of the power of intelligent remembrance and wise discrimination, one with the other. 5. About the attraction of the mercy of the spirit <i>and</i> leadership <a name="fr_1152"></a><a href="#fn_1140"><font size="1">3</font></a>, together, into the supreme heaven (garô<i>d</i>mânô), for observation regarding the good creatures.</p>  
 <p>6. About the begging <i>and</i> teaching of that intelligence which is with the increase of good works; also the imperceptible acquirement <a name="fr_1153"></a><a href="#fn_1141"><font size="1">4</font></a> of wealth occurs thereby. 7. This, too, that whoever gives himself up, with humility and reverence, to him who is a high-priest of the true religion, is proficient (far<i>z</i>ânakî-aîtŏ) in the religion <a name="fr_1154"></a><a href="#fn_1142"><font size="1">5</font></a>; and the benefit produced by him, for him who is good, is the liberality</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 296</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>which</i> is provided for the sacred beings <a name="fr_1155"></a><a href="#fn_1143"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 8. About Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <i>having</i> created water, plants, animals <a name="fr_1156"></a><a href="#fn_1144"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>and</i> the law of the primitive religion <i>for</i> the nourishment, arrangement, and succession of the creatures. 9. About the comfort <i>of</i> the spirit <i>of</i> the liturgy of the religion when he who is a man of credible wisdom and superior disposition utters it <a name="fr_1157"></a><a href="#fn_1145"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>10. This, too, that the wicked <i>one</i> who does not believe the deception that he teaches to others, which is his through his own spiritual lord, yet, when he teaches multitudes (kabedân), is convinced by it, attains<i>as</i> the end of that teachingeminence (pa<i>d</i>gahîh) for bare-faced deceit (barâhnakŏ frad<i>î</i>pîh), public falsehood, and disjointed belief.</p>  
 <p>11. And about mankind <i>being</i> bodily prepared also for the future existence by fire and melted ore <a name="fr_1158"></a><a href="#fn_1146"><font size="1">4</font></a>; in the worldly <i>existence</i> the acquitted and incriminated, as regards the law, <i>have</i> become thereby manifest <a name="fr_1159"></a><a href="#fn_1147"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>and</i>, in the future existence, the torment of the wicked <i>and</i> the gratification of the righteous <a name="fr_1160"></a><a href="#fn_1148"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 12. About Vohûman and Ashavahi<i>s</i>t <i>being</i> invoked <a name="fr_1161"></a><a href="#fn_1149"><font size="1">7</font></a> for assistance also in danger from the wicked, and about appropriating the best existence through righteousness alone. 13. And this, too, that a happy coming <i>of</i> men to the supreme heaven exists <i>for</i> the righteous, <i>but</i> no <a name="fr_1162"></a><a href="#fn_1150"><font size="1">8</font></a> coming of any one from the wicked.</p>

 <p>14. About the enmity of the Kaî <a name="fr_1163"></a><a href="#fn_1151"><font size="1">1</font></a> sodomite (vâêpŏ) <a name="fr_1164"></a><a href="#fn_1152"><font size="1">2</font></a> Akht, the heretic of the dark existence <a name="fr_1165"></a><a href="#fn_1153"><font size="1">3</font></a>, to Zaratû<i>s</i>t; and the causing of disturbance (siklinî<i>d</i>anŏ), <i>by</i> him and the wicked of similar kinds to him, among those who <i>follow</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t is extreme, and the primeval hellish existence is <i>for</i> them <a name="fr_1166"></a><a href="#fn_1154"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 15. About the closing of the abode of the Kai and Karap from virtuousness <a name="fr_1167"></a><a href="#fn_1155"><font size="1">5</font></a>; <i>and</i> this, too, that they do not develope the worldly <i>existences</i>, nor attend to the spirit, but they contract the world <i>and</i> dissipate the spirit <a name="fr_1168"></a><a href="#fn_1156"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>16. About the worthiness of the sovereignty <i>of</i> Kai-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_1169"></a><a href="#fn_1157"><font size="1">7</font></a>, on account of great ability and activity, apart even from superintending. 17. About the praise of Frashô<i>s</i>tar <a name="fr_1170"></a><a href="#fn_1158"><font size="1">8</font></a> for his <i>having</i> given Hvôbŏ <a name="fr_1171"></a><a href="#fn_1159"><font size="1">9</font></a> in marriage to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, the praise <i>of</i> Hvôbŏ for her complete reverence <i>of</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and admonition to Zaratû<i>s</i>t as to making Hvôbŏ privileged for the post of house-mistress <a name="fr_1172"></a><a href="#fn_1160"><font size="1">10</font></a>. 18. About the praise of <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_1173"></a><a href="#fn_1161"><font size="1">11</font></a> for begging fortune and <i>for</i> wisdom in appropriating the excellence <i>of</i> the primitive righteousness; <i>also his</i> affection for the sovereignty and <i>for</i> the recitation of revelation, in which <i>there</i> is assistance of Zaratû<i>s</i>t through command of</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Vohûman <a name="fr_1174"></a><a href="#fn_1162"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 19. About the praise of Maî<i>d</i>ôk-mâh <a name="fr_1175"></a><a href="#fn_1163"><font size="1">2</font></a> for his accepting <i>and</i> exercising<i>and</i> on account of his exercisingthe upholding and propagation of the religion; <i>also</i> the yelling, united assault, evil food, <i>and</i> other affliction owing to the wicked <i>in</i> the earlier half of the night, which is that which Zaratû<i>s</i>t <i>had</i>, for a like reason, to bear; and the reciting of the law of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1176"></a><a href="#fn_1164"><font size="1">3</font></a>, for the joy of the sacred beings, and his appropriation of the best existence.</p>  
 <p>20. About the abounding of Zaratû<i>s</i>t in complete mindfulness of the origin of learning, and <i>its</i> development by him <a name="fr_1177"></a><a href="#fn_1165"><font size="1">4</font></a>; both the object and the advantage of knowledgewhich is the reigning of Vohûman in the body<i>being</i> the means <i>of</i> developing the world in righteousness <a name="fr_1178"></a><a href="#fn_1166"><font size="1">5</font></a>. 21. About the perfection of the ceremonial <a name="fr_1179"></a><a href="#fn_1167"><font size="1">6</font></a> and obeisance of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>and</i> the superiority <a name="fr_1180"></a><a href="#fn_1168"><font size="1">7</font></a>of his recompense <a name="fr_1181"></a><a href="#fn_1169"><font size="1">8</font></a>; <i>also</i> advice to him as to worshipping Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> pre-eminently, and the primeval angels by their own names <a name="fr_1182"></a><a href="#fn_1170"><font size="1">9</font></a> according to their greatness.</p>  
 <p>22. It is the excellence <i>of</i> righteousness <i>that is</i> perfect.</p>
