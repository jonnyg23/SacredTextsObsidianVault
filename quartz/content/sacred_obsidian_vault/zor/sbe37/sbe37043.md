 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XL.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sakâ</i>d<i>ûm Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. One section is the Zîyânakistân ('<i>code of the injured</i>'), about <i>anything</i> which is animateand that <i>which is</i> inanimateinjured through lawfully living, giving, receiving, <i>or</i> delivering back; the duty of protection and care for both kinds; the nourishment,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 137</font></a></p>  
 <p>extension, sustentation, stimulation, establishment, consolation, and also gratification of an animate <i>being; and</i> the retribution for sin due to unlawfulness as regards the same matters.</p>  
 <p>2. About an example of a damaged gift, <i>in</i> the <i>case</i> when <i>one</i> gives the thing <i>to</i> a poor (ga<i>d</i>âk) person at an appointed time, <i>and</i> when at <i>one</i> unappointed; <i>and in</i> the <i>case</i> when <i>one</i> gives him an increase, where <i>and</i> what is the increase. 3. A decision about a shepherd when they shall bring <i>him</i> back <i>an animal</i> <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">1</font></a>, when damaged, before <i>its</i> subdivision; what he obtains for the damaged <i>animal</i> when not delivered back at the time of subdivision; when the duty about it is dictated by a religious <i>man</i>, and when he keeps <i>it</i> in <i>his own</i> possession.</p>  
 <p>4. About property which is inanimate, whose subdivisions, each separately, when <i>one</i> keeps <i>them</i> in use <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and when in reserve (armê<i>s</i>tô), are greater and less in value; that is, through so much effecting of penance (<i>a</i><i>v</i>âkan<i>g</i>i<i>s</i>nŏ) worthily, <i>or</i> through so much bringing of interest; and the capital is the same <i>in</i> value, the increase <i>being</i> the growth of dividends.</p>  
 <p>5. About the reason why the sin of an injured <i>person becomes</i> innocent through not delivering back a damaged <i>article</i> <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">3</font></a>; and many opinions, on the same subject, are provided for our benefit.</p>
