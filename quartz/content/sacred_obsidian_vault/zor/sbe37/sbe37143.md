 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XV. NÎKÂ<i>D</i>ÛM NASK <a name="fr_1807"></a><a href="#fn_1758"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>Dk. VIII, Chap. XVIII, 3, refers to the passage which, no doubt, contained the statement thus quoted</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_472"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 472</font></a></p>  
 <p>in Farh. Oîm, pp. 6, 1. 11-7, l. 13:'Aêdha is the skin of the head, and there is <i>part</i> of it which is large and <i>part</i> which is small, as it says in the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm:"Kaya he<i>n</i>ti masyanghô aêdha, which are those <i>parts with</i> the larger skin? Yô aparaya paiti mastraghnaya, whatever is behind the skull; (<i>A</i>farg <a name="fr_1808"></a><a href="#fn_1759"><font size="1">1</font></a> said, from the ear backwards). Kaya kasyanghô, <i>which are those with the smaller?</i> Yô paouruya paiti mastraghnaya, whatever is before <i>the skull</i> <a name="fr_1809"></a><a href="#fn_1760"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Vaghdhanem is the head, <i>and one</i> says nar<i>s</i> vaghdhanem, &amp;c., this is: Sinful are they <i>who</i> penetrate (sûmbênd) a man's head, astem aêvô mastravanãm, <i>or</i> one bone <i>of</i> the skull; vîspa<i>k</i>a yô mastraghnãm amãsta, all those <i>are</i> to be smitten who <i>have</i> penetrated into the skull, <i>and</i> to be given up <i>as</i> outcast <a name="fr_1810"></a><a href="#fn_1761"><font size="1">3</font></a>; <i>h</i><i>v</i>arô-<i>k</i>ithanãm <a name="fr_1811"></a><a href="#fn_1762"><font size="1">4</font></a> aêt<i>e</i>ê anyê <i>k</i>ikayatô, the penalties of a Khôr <a name="fr_1812"></a><a href="#fn_1763"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>sin</i> chastise those who <i>hurt</i> other <i>parts</i>, (such as the brain which is in the skull of the head, and the <i>marrow</i> of the other members <i>that are</i> to be mentioned, just as it mentions this:Sinful are they who strike through the bone, or flesh, or marrow of a leg, and every <i>one</i> of those is to be smitten who strikes</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_473"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 473</font></a></p>  
 <p>through the bone of one fleshy <i>part, and</i> he is to be given up <i>as</i> outcast; <i>while</i> the penalties of a Khôr <i>sin</i> <a name="fr_1813"></a><a href="#fn_1764"><font size="1">1</font></a> chastise those who <i>hurt</i> other <i>parts</i>)."'</p>  
 <p>Dk. VIII, Chap. XX, 116, probably refers to the passage which contained the statement thus quoted in Pahl. Vend. IV, 35: 'That is, this is the account of the number of years, <i>according to</i> that which is in the Hûspârûm as regards the account of the number; and <i>according to</i> that which is in the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm <i>it is</i> the account of the number of men.'</p>  
 <p>Dk. VIII, Chap. XX, 124, possibly refers to the passage which contained the statement thus quoted in Sls. X, 3:'In the exposition of the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm Nask it says that a man is going to commit robbery, <i>and</i> a wall falls in upon him, <i>it is</i> his destroyer; when a man strikes at him <i>he</i> is his adversary, <i>and</i> both are in sinfulness; when he is going to perform the ceremonial of the sacred beings both are in innocence <i>towards</i> him.'</p>  
 <p>No allusion to the following six passages, quoted from this Nask, has been noticed in Dk. VIII, Chaps. XVI-XX:</p>  
 <p>In Sls. X, 22, XII, 15, it is said as in the last fragment of the Spend N ask, already quoted.</p>  
 <p>In Sls. X, 23, XII, 16, it is said that 'in the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm the high-priests <i>have</i> taught thus:"A man gives a hungry <i>one</i> bread, <i>and it is</i> too much, yet (<i>or</i> when a man gives bread to a man, even though that man <i>has</i> too much bread) all the good works, which he shall perform through that superabundance,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_474"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 474</font></a></p>  
 <p>become as much his <i>who gave it</i> as though they had been done by his own hand."'</p>  
 <p>In Pahl. Vend. V, 73, it is said 'like unto this earth and that (sky) which would also cover over <i>it</i> (ever in all <i>places</i>; there is <i>some one</i> who says this is as to dead matter, that in the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm <i>says it is</i> as to decision and judgment, and that in the Hûspârûm says it is as to the ritual of the ceremonial).'</p>  
 <p>In Vi<i>g</i>. p. 136, it is said that '<i>it is</i> declared by the Avesta <i>of the</i> Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm <i>thus:</i>"Âa<i>d</i> ya<i>d</i> draonô Vana<i>n</i>tô stârô mazdadhâtô frâyazyâ<i>d</i>, <i>k</i>athwârô draonô frakere<i>n</i>te<i>n</i>ti aiwi-<i>h</i><i>v</i>are<i>n</i>ti, ya<i>d</i> aêshô nâ yô yao<i>z</i>dâthryô:So when he, who is that man who is a purifier, shall consecrate the sacred cake of Vanand <a name="fr_1814"></a><a href="#fn_1765"><font size="1">1</font></a>, the star produced by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, they cut up and shall eat up four sacred cakes."'</p>  
 <p>In Vi<i>g</i>. pp. 180, 181, it is said that 'in the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm it is stated:Âa<i>d</i> aokhta Ahurô Mazd<i>a</i>u: "Ya<i>d</i> aêtê yô mazdayasna aêtem srîrem vastrem stehr-paêsanghem hvãm tanûm bâdha paoirîm vanghanem<i>k</i>a hadha varanô paitanem<i>k</i>a, pas<i>k</i>aiti aiwy<i>a</i>unghânô ava hê maidhyânem bû<i>g</i>yamanô." Aêtem zî srîrem vastrem mainyutâ<i>s</i>tem ha<i>k</i>a mainyavanãm dâmanãm avi mê fradadhâ<i>d</i> Ahurô Mazd<i>a</i>u ashava. "Yatha hê varanô paitanem asti mãnayen hvare-khshaêtahê, adhâ<i>d</i> hadha hê vastranãm yao<i>z</i>dâthranãm frâyaza vâ nizbaya vâ Ahurâi Mazdâi ameshanãm spe<i>n</i>tanãm, Spitama Zarathu<i>s</i>tra!"Thus spoke Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>: "When <i>for</i> him, of those who are Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers, <i>there is</i> this beneficial, star-spangled (that is, wrought) garment <a name="fr_1815"></a><a href="#fn_1766"><font size="1">2</font></a>, always (after seven years <i>of age</i>, that is,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_475"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 475</font></a></p>  
 <p>after seven years <i>of age</i>) first he covers up (that is, clothes) his own body in it, and with (that <i>he</i> is properly) a preserver of faith (that is, a preserver of attachment, and) he afterwards ties <a name="fr_1816"></a><a href="#fn_1767"><font size="1">1</font></a> (that) girdle (over that starry <i>garment</i>) at the waist (as a waist-belt)." That, even this well-looking garment, spiritually formed by the creatures of the spirits, is really (that which) the righteous Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> granted me (who am Zaratû<i>s</i>t). "Since it is his preserver of faith, (that is, preserver of attachment), he is like (him who is as) the sun, (a preserver of beneficial faith, an implorer of the splendid heaven, and is <i>one</i> who is an accepter <i>of</i> the religion); therefore, with that garment, which is purified (that is, pure), do thou worship, or practise invocation, as regards Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> and as regards the archangels, O Spîtâmân Zaratû<i>s</i>t!"'</p>  
 <p>In Vi<i>g</i>. pp. 184, 185, it is said that '<i>it is</i> declared by the Avesta of the Nihâ<i>d</i>ûm <i>thus:</i>Âa<i>d</i> aêtahê pa<i>n</i><i>k</i>a ayara hamaspathmaidhem paiti ratûm spe<i>n</i>tay<i>a</i>u ârmatôi<i>s</i> mâunghô nôi<i>d</i> frasrâvayôi<i>d</i>:So <i>one</i> does not chant forth (that is, does not invoke) the month of (the completely mindful) Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1817"></a><a href="#fn_1768"><font size="1">2</font></a> (that is, the Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> month) in the reign of those five Hatnaspa<i>d</i>mêdêm days <a name="fr_1818"></a><a href="#fn_1769"><font size="1">3</font></a>; (for if <i>one</i> invokes <i>it</i>, owing to forgetfulness, the Avesta is not accepted).'</p>
