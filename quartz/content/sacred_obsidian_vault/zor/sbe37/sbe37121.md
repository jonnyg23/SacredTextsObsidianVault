 <p><a name="page_406"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 406</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">DÎNKA<i>R</i><i>D</i>.BOOK III.</h3>  <h4 align="center">CHAPTER VII <a name="fr_1644"></a><a href="#fn_1603"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h4>  <h4 align="center">The ninth question.</h4>  
 <p>1. Another apostate enquired thus: 'When there is Mãthra that is said <i>to be</i> all in the words of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>whether</i> it be in the words of Frashô<i>s</i>tar <i>and</i> <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_1645"></a><a href="#fn_1604"><font size="1">2</font></a>, <i>or</i> be in the words of Vohûman <a name="fr_1646"></a><a href="#fn_1605"><font size="1">3</font></a> and the sacred beings, <i>or</i> be in words of theirs published before <i>the time of</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>or</i> even after <i>that of</i> <i>S</i>ênô <a name="fr_1647"></a><a href="#fn_1606"><font size="1">4</font></a>, is <i>it to be</i> considered <i>by</i> us, as to that which is relating to us, <i>that</i> what is the utterance of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t is only the Gâthic, and the rest is composed <i>by</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t and his disciples from the world, even statements due to a good inclination for conversion (va<i>s</i>takîh)?'</p>  
 <p>2. The reply is <i>that</i> the other Mãthra which is separate from the Gâthas, if it <i>be apart</i> from the Gâthas, is <i>still</i> owing to the composition of the Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <a name="fr_1648"></a><a href="#fn_1607"><font size="1">5</font></a>; <i>and</i> the same separate Mãthra, which is from a witness about <i>it</i>, is the evidence with Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> himself in vigorous omniscience and composition, and not owing to the knowledge of mankind, which shall not attain even to an atom of the atoms thereof. 3. The arising of the Mãthra, through the speaking of many voices, is not all the speaking of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t through those</p>

 <p>voices, but the speaking of several separately, through which the speaking of the voice would be evidently <i>that of</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>; that is this Mãthra. 4. <i>And</i> just as the speaking forth of Zaratû<i>s</i>t and other good <i>men</i>, as well as evil <i>ones</i>, likewise <i>of</i> those who are demons, even as far as the evil spirit, is stated by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> in public, that statement would become even that of the evil spirit and demons, <i>and</i> the Mãthra and Law against the demons <i>would</i> likewise <i>become</i> spoken <i>by</i> the demons. 5. And the Mãthra is all <i>confided by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t through many voices, <i>being</i> an avowal of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, and an existence which is not inconsistent (han-bêshin); just as the Gâthas, which even you <i>admit to be</i>, as a whole, <i>confided by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, are spoken through the voice of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, be they through the voices of the archangels, be they through the voice of Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan <a name="fr_1649"></a><a href="#fn_1608"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>or</i> be they <i>through</i> the voices of other sacred beings, to all <i>they</i> are spoken <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>and</i> are not inconsistent. 6. But owing to the disposition of an apostate <i>there</i> is a longing scrutiny about his own <i>statements, and</i> evil-thinking scrutiny about the statements protecting the spiritual lord.</p>
