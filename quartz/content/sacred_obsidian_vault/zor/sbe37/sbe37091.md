 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The nineteenth fargar<i>d</i>, A<i>d</i> mâ-yavâ <a name="fr_1119"></a><a href="#fn_1108"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is about the protection <i>by</i> a protector for the protection <i>of</i> the distressed <i>ones</i> of the renovation <i>of the universe</i> <a name="fr_1120"></a><a href="#fn_1109"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 2. About the impossibility of convincing those who have not attained to the fundamental reason (bûn <i>k</i>im) of belief, before making them comprehensibly reliant upon the existence of the creator, which is the fundamental reason of belief.</p>  
 <p>3. About the grievous suffering (vîmârîh) of the religion owing to him who is a wicked judge, whose effusions (r<i>ê</i><i>z</i>î<i>d</i>anŏ) on the judgment seat are injudicious, malevolent, <i>and</i> enemies of wisdom; also his wounding is owing to truth <a name="fr_1121"></a><a href="#fn_1110"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and <i>his</i> annoyance owing to the truthful, and the evil spirit is lodging in him; <i>likewise</i> the advantage <i>to</i> the religion and the great reward of just judges, and the introduction</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (madam-bari<i>s</i>nîh) of a desire for leadership in virtuousness <a name="fr_1122"></a><a href="#fn_1111"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 4. About separation from the friendship of a wicked, ill judging, unintelligent, and idle <i>person</i>, in whom wrath and envy are coiled up (<i>a</i><i>v</i>ar<i>p</i><i>ê</i><i>k</i>ŏ) <a name="fr_1123"></a><a href="#fn_1112"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 3. About the good government of securers of their own necks (<i>k</i>avarman) from viciousness, and the bad government of those repeatedly culpable (lakhvâr-âhûgân) owing to viciousness. 6. And this, too, that the wicked themselves are wicked to their own and make them fit for hell, even as to those who <a name="fr_1124"></a><a href="#fn_1113"><font size="1">3</font></a> are precious to them and more beloved than righteousness; and <i>their</i> reign, too, is a scanty protection (ga<i>s</i>ûkŏ srâyi<i>s</i>nŏ).</p>  
 <p>7. About the praise of Zaratû<i>s</i>t <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'Thy sweetness and mildness are shown to the worldly existences, thy leadership of the religion is through Vohûman, <i>and</i> thou art well conversant with righteousness <a name="fr_1125"></a><a href="#fn_1114"><font size="1">4</font></a>.' 8. About the praise of Frashô<i>s</i>tar's ardour in the leadership of good works, in virtuousness <a name="fr_1126"></a><a href="#fn_1115"><font size="1">5</font></a>, listening <i>to</i> instruction, and truthful speaking, and in pasturing (fshegîh), cultivating the world, achieving benefit (sû<i>d</i>ŏ tâshî<i>d</i>ârîh), and not giving leadership to villains <a name="fr_1127"></a><a href="#fn_1116"><font size="1">6</font></a>. 9. About the praise also of the energy <i>and</i> high-priestship of <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_1128"></a><a href="#fn_1117"><font size="1">7</font></a>. 10. About the protection of the good</p>

 <p>creations by Vohûman, <i>and</i> that, too, of the souls of the righteous <i>by</i> Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> also <a name="fr_1129"></a><a href="sbe37092.htm#fn_1118"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>11. About the punishment of the wicked <i>ruler</i> who is seizing anything unlawfully in his realm. 12. Also about the grievous punishment of the wicked, evil-thinking, evil-speaking, evil-doing, heretical (dû<i>s</i>-dênô), evil ruler in hell <a name="fr_1130"></a><a href="sbe37092.htm#fn_1119"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 13. About the reply of the archangels to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, as to the reward begged by him, to make <i>him</i> satisfied about it <a name="fr_1131"></a><a href="sbe37092.htm#fn_1120"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>14. It is righteousness <i>that is</i> perfect excellence.</p>
