 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER LXIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. About a selection from the whole Ya<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1523"></a><a href="#fn_1483"><font size="1">3</font></a> referring to the developer (vakhshînî<i>d</i>âr-hômônd).</p>  
 <p>2. Those are beneficial who increase for the developer, that is, they shall occasion benefit (nêvakîh) for him who would occasion that benefit which is for others <a name="fr_1524"></a><a href="#fn_1484"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 3. Thus the righteous <i>man</i> who produces perfect thought is <i>he</i> who comes upon it through Vohûman <a name="fr_1525"></a><a href="#fn_1485"><font size="1">5</font></a>, <i>and</i> the benefit of him who is an open annoyer <a name="fr_1526"></a><a href="#fn_1486"><font size="1">6</font></a>the righteous man who is a smiter <i>of</i> the wicked, <i>and</i> who developes as to what is Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s and as to what is Zaratû<i>s</i>t'sis that he slays in moderation.</p>  
 <p>4. Regarding him who is an oppressive man who is righteous, the reply spoken is <i>thus:</i> 'The reward of the smiter and developerthat man of whom <i>one</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_385"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 385</font></a></p>  
 <p>knows the smiting and developingis the very evil reward <i>of</i> him who is wicked; just as his smiting, as an evil reward for him from those two spirits, is that very evil practice loved by him who is wicked; even for this reason every righteous individual is Gâthic <a name="fr_1527"></a><a href="#fn_1487"><font size="1">1</font></a>, because, when privileged (pa<i>d</i>ôkhshâî), he who is wicked is thus he <i>who</i> is righteous, <i>and</i> also he who is privileged is unprivileged <a name="fr_1528"></a><a href="#fn_1488"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>5. Zaratû<i>s</i>t proceeded with the smiting at the wicked, <i>and</i> as to that proceeding Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Thou shouldst thus proceed with smiting at the wicked by ordinance (dâ<i>d</i>istân), because thus they have thee and the righteous of every kind as ruler. 6. Also through my decree (pavan-i<i>k</i> man vi<i>g</i>îr) <i>one</i> produces the ritual of ordeal, which realizes that which is real, so that <i>one</i> may make that which is dark fully light. 7. Thine, too, is so much the sovereignty of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, thus through worship, that its requisite (khvâstakŏ) privilege is thus maintained through virtue, because thou, who <i>art</i> thus, <i>art</i> more unconfined (anâkô<i>s</i>î<i>d</i>ar) to the world through the furtherance <i>and</i> development of righteousness; great, indeed, is he who trusts the righteous <i>man</i> for righteousness, and great is he who <i>trusts</i> the wicked <i>man</i> for wickedness.'</p>  
 <p>8. As to that utterance (farmâyi<i>s</i>nŏ) Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke thus: 'An open annoyer is the righteous manthe benefiter disclosed <i>by</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>that loves the embodied world of righteousness, and demands its reverence (tarsakâyîh) for the proportion of righteousness therein, that is, he knows the proportion of duty and good works.' 9. Regarding</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_386"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 386</font></a></p>  
 <p>the worldly <i>existence</i>, the reply spoken is thus: 'That which is again contaminated (gûmîkhtê<i>d</i>) <i>by</i> the demons becomes abundant so long as that which is proper is again contaminated with the demons; <i>and</i>, so long as <i>there is</i> a developer, they subsist for their own substances, so that <i>it</i> is possible for them to seek benefit for their own, <i>and</i> they are smiters of the righteous.'</p>  
 <p>10. Regarding him who is a wise smiter, Sôshâns <a name="fr_1529"></a><a href="#fn_1489"><font size="1">1</font></a> spoke <i>in</i> reply thus: 'It arises through his way when it is again contaminated.'</p>  
 <p>11. As to that mischief (drû<i>g</i>i<i>s</i>nŏ) Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Happy is he from whom there is no mischief.' 12. Regarding <a name="fr_1530"></a><a href="#fn_1490"><font size="1">2</font></a> him who has come, the Yim of splendour <a name="fr_1531"></a><a href="#fn_1491"><font size="1">3</font></a>, he spoke thus: 'He attains his reward who is no smiter and no developer, not privileged and not unprivileged.'</p>  
 <p>13. As to that disclosure (h<i>â</i><i>k</i>i<i>s</i>nŏ) Vohûman <a name="fr_1532"></a><a href="#fn_1492"><font size="1">4</font></a> spoke thus: 'I aggrandize that spiritual lord and that priestly master who is my righteousness in person.'</p>  
 <p>14. As to that utterance Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1533"></a><a href="#fn_1492"><font size="1">4</font></a> spoke thus: 'So do thou perfect (ba<i>r</i>â vadîdûn) him whose information subsistsa man that becomes wisewho is as an emblem of my religion; because he <i>has</i> worshipped that which is ours, so that he has retained property in our possession, through whose words <i>there</i> is a furtherance of the world of righteousness. 15. That is my arrangement, and that my wishthat is, what is necessary for meand I</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_387"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 387</font></a></p>  
 <p>love that which thou fully understandest, that is, that arrangement which is righteousness; also whatever discourse and perfect performance, thou askest of us, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! in complete mindfulness, I now practise by the work of each hand; observe thoroughly that which I <i>am</i> performing, <i>and</i> thou, too, <i>art</i> accomplishing. 16. In worship thou, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! art liberal, who art liberal in ceremonial; <i>for</i> thee, whose body believes, controversy is not lavish (râ<i>d</i>) for the sake of the wealth bestowed; the reward of Zaratû<i>s</i>t the developer is for developing, the reward of Zaratû<i>s</i>t the smiter is for smiting, <i>and</i> the reward of Zaratû<i>s</i>t the smiter and developer is for smiting and developing. 17. At the bridge <i>judgment</i> of him whose name the fire calls for participation, as when they repeatedly pour the melted ore upon him in the throat, thou shouldst pray near him alivehim whose love is for virtueso that he may perform duty and good works with fearlessness; with his desire, too, it is expedient to know that <i>it is</i> done by him on account of necessity.'</p>  
 <p>18. As to that utterance Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Such is the upward attraction (lâlâ-han<i>g</i>i<i>s</i>nîh) of Shatraver <a name="fr_1534"></a><a href="#fn_1493"><font size="1">1</font></a> for him who is ours.' 19. As to that question (frashnŏ) Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: Such has happened to him who is ours through Vohûman; he ought to come to our religion through virtue. 20. Truly he, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! is privileged for the sovereignty, who confines <i>his</i> ears <i>to</i> this religion, that he may make <i>it</i> fully progressive; who is given immortality through this, and kind regard <i>for</i> the will <i>of</i> him <i>who</i> is the best of that religion of mine,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_388"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 388</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! <i>and</i> who assists the furtherance of this world of mine in righteousness.'</p>  
 <p>21. As to those of that <i>other one</i> <a name="fr_1535"></a><a href="#fn_1494"><font size="1">1</font></a> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'As regards that which is great evidence, when wicked they consider <i>it</i> as unattested for him who is wicked himself; <i>and</i> the thoughts <a name="fr_1536"></a><a href="#fn_1495"><font size="1">2</font></a> of him, whose deeds are those of that <i>other one</i>, are due to Akômanô <a name="fr_1537"></a><a href="#fn_1496"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 22. Owing also to this, when both Khû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> and Amû<i>r</i>da<i>d</i> <a name="fr_1538"></a><a href="#fn_1497"><font size="1">4</font></a> are given to thee, <i>it is</i> in that waywhen thou art of the propitious spirit and the best thoughtthat what thou understandest thou shouldst be accomplishing, and what thou dost not understand thou askest again.'</p>  
 <p>23. Of him whose wisdom exists (aîtŏ)of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>he whose wisdom arises (yehevûnê<i>d</i>)Zaratû<i>s</i>tenquired concerning him who is unreal (an-aîtŏ) <i>and</i> who does not subsist (yehevûnê<i>d</i>) <i>here</i>after, who <i>has</i> thus never become a material <i>existence</i> for those <i>on</i> the side of virtue, and does not subsist for them henceforth.</p>  
 <p>24. As to that reverse description (pa<i>d</i>îrakŏ-nisâni<i>s</i>nîh) Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Among men of every kind say unto the righteous who are smiting the wicked, <i>that</i> we improve the measure of any milk they propitiate, even by the holy-water which is the sustenance (bari<i>s</i>nŏ) of milk, <i>in order</i> to cause much happiness of life.'</p>  
 <p>25. As to that utterance Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Happy is he from whom there is no complaint, and a life which is like this the text Gerezôi . . .</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_389"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 389</font></a></p>  
 <p>âkhsô . . . <a name="fr_1539"></a><a href="#fn_1498"><font size="1">1</font></a> implores. 26. To thee, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! my protection is given <i>in</i> the reply of the K<i>e</i>m-nâ <a name="fr_1540"></a><a href="#fn_1499"><font size="1">2</font></a> which, before the companionship of Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <a name="fr_1541"></a><a href="#fn_1500"><font size="1">3</font></a>that righteous friend of mine <a name="fr_1542"></a><a href="#fn_1501"><font size="1">4</font></a><i>was</i> a published thing of those which are used, <i>and</i> of those such as it is requisite to use. 27. The talk of a man which is immoderate is false, everything immoderate is <i>so</i> for this reason, everything is not that which the good man possesses, because, when privileged, the wicked <i>one</i> is he who is righteous, and he who is privileged becomes unprivileged; he who is righteous is thus he who is wicked, and becomes him who is privileged and unprivileged, so that he is fully incriminated, and they shall carry off his possessions.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_390"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 390</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. Regarding the benefiters the reply spoken is <i>thus:</i> 'They are owing to the reward of the smiter and developer; those are beneficial whose smiting and developing are those of the developer Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, who understands smiting and development.'</p>  
 <p>29. When through smiting by Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, on account of the wicked, a question (frashnŏ) about <i>it</i> arose, the reply spoken, as to the smiting of the present <i>world</i> by means of him who is ruler, <i>was:</i> 'The reward which the judgment that is perfect teaches is thus, that he who is the smiter and developer, Sôshâns <a name="fr_1543"></a><a href="#fn_1502"><font size="1">1</font></a>, shall make the decision.' 30. Zaratû<i>s</i>t spoke <i>in</i> reply thus: 'He gives a reward.' 31. And that wise smiter, Sôshâns, spoke <i>in</i> reply thus: 'He shall inflict punishment.'</p>  
 <p>32. Even he who is an ox of many cattle has openly <i>and</i> publicly wailed this complaint <a name="fr_1544"></a><a href="#fn_1503"><font size="1">2</font></a> on account of the righteous <i>one:</i> 'How long <i>is it</i> till <i>the time</i> when a developer arises, even he who is an irresolute ruler (akâmakŏ khû<i>d</i>âî)? How long is the time till he arises, until the wicked <i>one</i> who is a smiter <i>and</i> privileged corrupter is he <i>who is</i> unprivileged?' 33. Because, for the sake of producing resolution (kâmak-dahîh), he complains that, until the developer shall arise, even he who is irresolute is ruler, that is, until he who is the developer shall become privileged.</p>  
 <p>34. As to that complaint of his Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: 'Not so as by this complaint is the obtainment of spiritual lordship (ahûîkih), for this reason, when they do not consider the ruler as a ruler, and <i>there is</i> no giving of priestly authority (ra<i>d</i>ŏîh) by any</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_391"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 391</font></a></p>  
 <p>righteousness whatever <a name="fr_1545"></a><a href="#fn_1504"><font size="1">1</font></a>, it is requisite, on account of the many righteous, to speak henceforth, until <i>the time</i> when the developer arises, even <i>of</i> him who is an irresolute ruler.'</p>  
 <p>35. On account of the many statements of the spirits, even as to thought, word, <i>and</i> deed, it is requisite to say that they shall always render an account until even some obtainment <i>of</i> a smiter and developer, privileged <i>or</i> unprivileged. 36. Some arise <i>of</i> whom it is requisite to ask this question (frashnŏ) <a name="fr_1546"></a><a href="#fn_1505"><font size="1">2</font></a> while he who is righteous <i>and</i> he who is wicked are two witnesses, <i>and</i> they make the righteous <i>one</i> manifest by his evidence, <i>or</i> they molest him who is righteous by smiting. 37. Some arise when it is requisite to speak this reply <a name="fr_1547"></a><a href="#fn_1506"><font size="1">3</font></a> during the smiting <i>of</i> the maintainer of strife and <i>of</i> the kinsman. 38. <i>And</i> some arise while that individual <a name="fr_1548"></a><a href="#fn_1507"><font size="1">4</font></a> is loved, though a righteous <i>one and</i> a developer arise, and it is requisite to produce a provider of benefit on account of the many, both wicked and righteous, so long as a wicked <i>one</i> of the smiting which is maintaining strife is privileged.</p>  
 <p>39. Because, regarding the production of resolution, <i>it</i> is proclaimed that <i>it is</i> so that they shall fully understand that Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> discriminates truly, <i>and</i> Aharman does not discriminate truly. 40. And that <i>it is</i> so that they shall fully understand that the punishment of the wicked is for teaching <i>them</i> that they <i>will</i> attain to the existence of darkness, that even <i>to</i> him who belongs to the ever-stationary they <i>may</i> give his reward, that they are for smiting the wicked <i>one</i>, that they are very powerful to give, that</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_392"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 392</font></a></p>  
 <p>they <i>should</i> kill the apostate, that <i>one has to be</i> converted from vileness to goodness, that he who would be wicked is made to believe by the tongue, that for the sake of proper nurture of the creatures next-of-kin marriage is provided, that the demons are despised, that thus he who is evil-ruling is wicked, and that they are approaching <i>the place</i> where Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> shall provide for the account of sin and good works.</p>  
 <p>41. They shall become more diligent in the performance of duty and good works, and abstain more from sin, always until <i>one</i> attains even to some acquirement <i>for</i> those in life and those in a lifeless state <a name="fr_1549"></a><a href="#fn_1508"><font size="1">1</font></a>. 42. And they shall not inflict their punishment completely in the embodied state, and the fiend does not pity the worldly <i>existence</i>; every individual is counted up, and every one is fully completed for the affairs of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, <i>but</i> the fiend is not smitten, and they shall not fully inflict the punishment.</p>  
 <p>43. No one thinks thou shouldst remain for the propitious Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, and no one completely presents <i>him</i>self; they attack through the fiend, and arise for the foolish <i>one</i>. 44. No one arises for the goodness of him who is good, <i>but</i> for the vileness <i>of</i> the fiend they destroy <i>what is</i> good, and do not understand evil <i>and</i> good; they recite the revelation for a wicked <i>one</i>, they do not bestow friendship for labour, <i>but</i> are for the evil-doer.</p>  
 <p>45. And the righteous <i>one</i>, who is the best of spiritual <i>and</i> worldly <i>existences</i>, becomes a privileged</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_393"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 393</font></a></p>  
 <p>developer, even he who is an irresolute ruler <a name="fr_1550"></a><a href="#fn_1509"><font size="1">1</font></a>; and so he who is wicked, even he who is privileged, becomes unprivileged, at that time when <i>one</i> gives the soul of every one unto the supreme heaven <a name="fr_1551"></a><a href="#fn_1510"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and when thou shouldst, every one, know that the affliction of the annoyers arises <a name="fr_1552"></a><a href="#fn_1511"><font size="1">3</font></a>, so that when, owing thereto, they beseech the sacred beings, <i>it is</i> only hell that they supply.</p>  
 <p>46. When every one shall provide the ceremonial of the archangels unworriedly, and when every one knows that Gô<i>s</i>-aûrvan complained <a name="fr_1553"></a><a href="#fn_1512"><font size="1">4</font></a>so that he who is the fashioner of cattle enquired thus: 'Whose is the guardianship of cattle <a name="fr_1554"></a><a href="#fn_1513"><font size="1">5</font></a>?' <i>and</i> 'Not without annoyance' <i>was</i> the reply of Ashavahi<i>s</i>t, 'that is, they shall inflict his punishment <a name="fr_1555"></a><a href="#fn_1514"><font size="1">6</font></a>'every one <i>also</i> knows that in their light is joyfulness for the sight <a name="fr_1556"></a><a href="#fn_1515"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>47. When every individual (ko<i>l</i>â aî<i>s</i>-1) becomes aware <i>of</i> the priestly authority of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>; and when every individual knows that his remedy <i>for</i> the devastation owing to the evil spirit is comprehensibly stated <a name="fr_1557"></a><a href="#fn_1516"><font size="1">8</font></a>; when every individual knows that Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> fashioned the propitiousness in the liturgy <a name="fr_1558"></a><a href="#fn_1517"><font size="1">9</font></a>; and when every one knows that the priest is perfect, that Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> enhances both of them in spirituality <a name="fr_1559"></a><a href="#fn_1518"><font size="1">10</font></a>, that Vohûman is the offspring of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1560"></a><a href="#fn_1519"><font size="1">11</font></a>, that Spenda<i>r</i>ma<i>d</i> is Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s own <a name="fr_1561"></a><a href="#fn_1520"><font size="1">12</font></a>, that all three of them are the life <i>of</i> him who</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_394"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 394</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>has</i> wandered forth <a name="fr_1562"></a><a href="#fn_1521"><font size="1">1</font></a>that is, life is given by the thought and wisdom <a name="fr_1563"></a><a href="#fn_1522"><font size="1">2</font></a> which are his ownand that the sacred beings are they who are supporting <i>it</i>.</p>  
 <p>48. When every one of this existence must act for the sake of that <i>other</i> existence, <i>and</i> knows <i>how</i> to act; when every one is a friend, through deeds, of the spirit which is his own <a name="fr_1564"></a><a href="#fn_1523"><font size="1">3</font></a>; and when every one becomes a person supporting Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1565"></a><a href="#fn_1524"><font size="1">4</font></a>. 49. When every individual knows that they give no reward to him in whose body a demon is lodging who is not listening <a name="fr_1566"></a><a href="#fn_1525"><font size="1">5</font></a>; when every one shall make his own soul immortal <a name="fr_1567"></a><a href="#fn_1526"><font size="1">6</font></a>; and when every one has advantage through possession of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> <a name="fr_1568"></a><a href="#fn_1527"><font size="1">7</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>50. When every one becomes a Zôti unsullied in righteousness <a name="fr_1569"></a><a href="#fn_1528"><font size="1">8</font></a>; when every one gives a sacred cake to the archangels <a name="fr_1570"></a><a href="#fn_1529"><font size="1">9</font></a>; when every one knows that co-operation is due to him who is their servant; and when they are together <i>in</i> soul <a name="fr_1571"></a><a href="#fn_1530"><font size="1">10</font></a>. 51. When every individual gives <i>his</i> body <a name="fr_1572"></a><a href="#fn_1531"><font size="1">11</font></a>; when every one proceeds to their ceremonial and glorification <a name="fr_1573"></a><a href="#fn_1532"><font size="1">12</font></a>; when every individual knows that 'other than they' is <i>meant by</i> naê<i>k</i>îm t<i>e</i>m any<i>e</i>m <a name="fr_1574"></a><a href="#fn_1533"><font size="1">13</font></a>; and when every one knows</p>
