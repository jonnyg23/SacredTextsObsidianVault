 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXVIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. One section of the <i>first</i> thirty of the Hûspâram <a name="fr_244"></a><a href="#fn_243"><font size="1">1</font></a> is the Aêrpatistân <a name="fr_245"></a><a href="#fn_244"><font size="1">2</font></a> ('<i>priest-code</i>'), particulars about <i>a case</i> where <i>one has</i> to provide for a priestly assembly (aêrpatistân), which is a birth; how the <i>case</i> is when <i>it</i> is important to go, how it is when <i>one</i></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a></p>  
 <p>stays at his own <i>house</i>, and how it is when <i>it</i> is not allowable to go; also deciding about the <i>chief</i> priest (aêrpatŏ), and the proportion of priests (âsrûkŏ) who are superior, <i>of</i> those who are intermediate, and <i>of</i> those who are inferior in the estimation of the wisdom of the righteous. 2. About the priest whom <i>one</i> is sending, and the wayfaring garments <i>and</i> appliances which are to be given to him.</p>  
 <p>3. About the disciple, as reverent towards the <i>chief</i> priest; the labour in receiving the <i>sacred</i> words <i>and</i> teaching <i>them</i> to the disciple; the advice of the <i>chief</i> priest to the priests; <i>and</i> the muttered phrases at the time of contamination <i>by dead matter</i>. 4. About what prieston the arrival of a priest back at the district from which <i>one</i> sends <i>him</i>is to be appointed, as priest for the district from which <i>he came, by</i> the district governor and those of the district, for teaching <i>and</i> instruction in the district.</p>  
 <p>5. About which are <i>those</i> reckoned as the five dispositions <a name="fr_246"></a><a href="#fn_245"><font size="1">1</font></a> of a priest that are the glorification of the priest's statements of the law, <i>from</i> the first of <i>his</i> statements in succession unto the last, <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>6. About the subjects regarding which a priest of concealed parentage is to be asked, <i>with</i> the prelude and sequel of the same subject. 7. About the bridge <i>penalty</i> <a name="fr_247"></a><a href="#fn_246"><font size="1">2</font></a> of a priest through sinfulness, <i>in</i> a separate fargar<i>d</i> <a name="fr_248"></a><a href="#fn_247"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 8. About a priest they may carry away from a district, owing to anxiety for forming a priestly assembly, <i>who</i> becomes worried in forming <i>it</i>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 94</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. About the superiority of priests in means of knowledge, one as regards another; the extent of superiority through which the greater suitability for authority, of one as regards another <a name="fr_249"></a><a href="#fn_248"><font size="1">1</font></a>, arises; <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_243"></a><a href="sbe37031.htm#fr_244">92:1</a> Corresponding to the seventeenth word, â, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; and it is the seventeenth Nask in all Rivâyats. This name should probably be Avisp-kharam, meaning 'free from all defect;' but it is called Hûspârâm, Aspâram, or Aspâram in the Rivâyats, which also state that it contained sixty-four, or sixty, kardah or subdivisions. The former number agrees with the total of the sections mentioned in Chaps. XXVIII, XXXII, XXXVI.</p>
