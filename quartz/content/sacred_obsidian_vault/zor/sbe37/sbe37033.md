 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Hûspâram Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. One section is the Gôharikistân ('<i>quality code</i>'), particulars about natural superiority; not the modified (ga<i>s</i>takŏ), but the lawful, approved <a name="fr_261"></a><a href="#fn_260"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and specific <i>state</i> of superiority; not acquired by the slender power <a name="fr_262"></a><a href="#fn_261"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the world, but <i>by</i> seeking virtuous living through causing the prosperity <i>of</i> every person; <i>also</i> the authorisation of superiority, <i>and</i> the proportion of advantage therein. 2. About a superiority unimpoverished (anyûrûzd), with <i>one</i> unimpoverished with a nature unspent (an-aûrûzd), with <i>one</i> unspent with an impoverished (nyûrûzd), and <i>one</i> impoverished with an impoverished; also the extent of impoverishment and non-impoverishment, that is, with whom <i>it is</i> not customarily of much</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_98"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 98</font></a></p>  
 <p>consequence (pavan freh-ar<i>g</i>ŏ), with whom <i>it is so</i> customarily, <i>and</i> with whom, owing to an exception, <i>it is</i> not customarily of much consequence on account of <i>its</i> much consequence for an uninformed <i>person</i>, that is, with whom <i>it is</i> as <i>it were</i> proper with a servant of sin. 3. And superiority is a furtherance of living beings, and pervades the natural extent thereof.</p>  
 <p>4. About him who would sell property not his own, <i>and</i> him who would buy <i>it</i>. 5.. About selling a sheep frequenting the house, <i>and one</i> not frequenting the house. 6. About various precautions <i>as to</i> samples of various things. 7. About selling beasts <i>of burden</i>, cattle, slaves, servants, <i>and</i> other property, of the nature of whose species <i>one is</i> aware through speaking about the nature of different species; <i>and</i> the retribution for the sin of whatever is on the same subject. 8. That which is an obvious agreement for selling with defects <a name="fr_263"></a><a href="#fn_262"><font size="1">1</font></a>, when it is declared of beasts <i>of burden;</i> and that which is ever defective on selling.</p>  
 <p>9. About a house in which a person, <i>or</i> dog, <i>has</i> passed away through contagious sickness, and the clothing which the man wore owing to that sickness; that is, how it is when spoiled for selling <i>for</i> three <i>years</i>, how it is when <i>it is so for</i> two <i>years, and</i> how it is <i>when it is so for</i> one year. to About a house in which a person, or dog, <i>has</i> reposed in a contagious sickness, <i>and</i> not passed <i>away</i> after <i>his</i> descent therefrom; <i>and</i> the clothing which the man wore in that sickness; that is, how it is when spoiled for selling <i>for</i> two <i>years</i>, how it is when <i>it is so for</i> one year, and how it is when <i>it is so for</i> thirty nights; <i>and</i> whatever is on the same subject.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 99</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. About forming a family (gôharik ka<i>r</i><i>d</i>anŏ) with foreigners, that is, how it is when allowable. 12. About a sheep of <i>good</i> breed for the three nights <a name="fr_264"></a><a href="#fn_263"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>and its</i> slaughter after the three nights; likewise many other decisions as regards superiority and sheep of a <i>good</i> breed.</p>
