 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XX.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The nineteenth fargar<i>d</i>, Ka<i>d</i>-môi-urvâ <a name="fr_656"></a><a href="#fn_651"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is about where the souls, when they come together, extol the soul of him who <i>was</i> a virtuous high-priest, a friend of the soul, because he did not injure it, <i>and</i> guarded <i>it</i> from hell.</p>  
 <p>2. About the darkness, the intensity (bûr<i>z</i>vŏ-hômandîh) and far-reaching bottomlessness of the blackness, and the absence of goodness <i>in</i> hell; and the proximity to stenches, close concealment <a name="fr_657"></a><a href="#fn_652"><font size="1">3</font></a>, sleet-pelted clambering (pî<i>s</i>nakŏ-bâlînîh), frozen advancing,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_210"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 210</font></a></p>  
 <p>painful condition, distressed state, and awful fear of those in hell. 3. This, too, that is thrown open (lakhvâr ramîtun<i>d</i>) over it, from the Dâîtî peak <a name="fr_658"></a><a href="#fn_653"><font size="1">1</font></a>, which is in Aîrân-vê<i>g</i>, to Albûr<i>z</i> <a name="fr_659"></a><a href="#fn_654"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and below the middle of which is the gate of hell, is the <i>K</i>înva<i>d</i> bridge <a name="fr_660"></a><a href="#fn_655"><font size="1">3</font></a> which is the route (vi<i>d</i>âr) of every one, righteous <i>or</i> wicked; the width across the route of the righteous is a breadth of nine spears, each one the length of three reeds, <i>but</i> the route for the wicked becomes like the edge of a razor.</p>  
 <p>4. 'Thus say I <a name="fr_661"></a><a href="#fn_656"><font size="1">4</font></a> unto thee, O Spîtâmân! that the man of truth steps forward over the <i>K</i>înva<i>d</i> pass, even the far-famed happy bridge; for Â<i>s</i>tâ<i>d</i> <a name="fr_662"></a><a href="#fn_657"><font size="1">5</font></a>, the good promoter of the world, and Mit<i>r</i>ô <a name="fr_663"></a><a href="#fn_658"><font size="1">6</font></a> of the vast cattle-pastures save only the man possessing truth from that distress, as though they <i>were</i> a regiment (si<i>p</i>âh) a thousand strong. 5. So I say unto thee, O Spîtâmân! that thou shouldst not become a liar unto Mit<i>r</i>ô, neither when thou wouldst converse with the wicked, nor when <i>thou wouldst</i> with those of thine own religion who are righteous; for both <i>of</i> those are promises, both <i>with</i> the wicked and the righteous; there is a promise, O Zaratû<i>s</i>t! even <i>of</i> a wolf with young animals, <i>but</i> that which is a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_211"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 211</font></a></p>  
 <p>lascivious (<i>g</i>êhîk) promise is more awful, O Spîtâmân! 6. So I say unto thee, O Spîtâmân! that thou shouldst not seize a wanton (<i>g</i>êhîk) for usethat is, do not make her <i>thy</i> wifeand with compulsion (ûpayami<i>s</i>nîh) of her <a name="fr_664"></a><a href="#fn_659"><font size="1">1</font></a>that is, do not let <i>thyself</i> lie with her. 7. And if thou shouldst seize a courtezan for use, and with compulsion <i>of</i> her, thou mayst not dismiss <i>her</i> afterwards, neither in adversity, nor in prosperity, neither on account of fondness for self, nor <i>for</i> life; because <i>he</i> who seizes a courtezan for use, and with compulsion, and shall dismiss her on account of fondness for self, or <i>for</i> life, becomes thereby a breaker of promises <i>to</i> the house, village, community, <i>or</i> province, <i>that</i> gives her life (valman zîvînê<i>d</i>ŏ), <i>and to</i> the soul <i>that</i> animates her <a name="fr_665"></a><a href="#fn_660"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>8. So breaking the promise comes upon the children that are theirs, through evil teaching; <i>and</i> he who is wicked is lying down without children at the bottom of hell. 9. <i>That</i> is, there is nothing whatever <i>of</i> <a name="fr_666"></a><a href="#fn_661"><font size="1">3</font></a> happiness <i>for</i> the wicked, that happiness which is produced abundantly by him <i>who is</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>.</p>  
 <p>10. Perfect righteousness is excellence.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_652"></a><a href="sbe37069.htm#fr_657">209:3</a> Compare AV. For a description of hell see also Dd. XXVII.</p>
