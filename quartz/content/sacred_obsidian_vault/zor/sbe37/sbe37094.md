 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Var</i>s<i>tmânsar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The twenty-second fargar<i>d</i>, Vahi<i>s</i>tôi<i>s</i>ti <a name="fr_1183"></a><a href="#fn_1171"><font size="1">10</font></a>, is about the perfection of the prayers <a name="fr_1184"></a><a href="#fn_1172"><font size="1">11</font></a> of the good religion, and information thereon.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_299"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 299</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. About the glory of a family of some houses <i>that</i> has come to the Spîtâmas <a name="fr_1185"></a><a href="#fn_1173"><font size="1">1</font></a>, even before the coming of Zaratû<i>s</i>t; the knowledge and habit of organization and priestly authority of those <i>arising</i> from that family; the existence therein of houses, villages, communities, <i>and</i> districts; <i>its</i> attracting <i>and</i> exalting mankind, from vice to virtue, by propriety <i>of</i> words and actions; <i>and</i> it convinces those of the world even till the arrival of the good religion. 3. And this, too, that the existence <i>of</i> Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâspthat desire of Zaratû<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1186"></a><a href="#fn_1174"><font size="1">2</font></a>and of Frashô<i>s</i>tar of the Hvôbas <a name="fr_1187"></a><a href="#fn_1175"><font size="1">3</font></a>, is owing to it.</p>  
 <p>4. About the praise of Pôrû<i>k</i>âst <a name="fr_1188"></a><a href="#fn_1176"><font size="1">4</font></a>, daughter of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, for loving the good religion with wisdom and acting <i>by</i> the advice of the religion, <i>having</i> given <i>her</i>self contentedly in womanly service (zanŏîh) to Zaratû<i>s</i>t <a name="fr_1189"></a><a href="#fn_1177"><font size="1">5</font></a>; <i>her</i> complete accomplishment</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_300"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 300</font></a></p>  
 <p>of duty and reverence <i>for</i> him, <i>and</i>, after Zaratû<i>s</i>t, <i>her</i> also performing womanly service (zanîh) and reverence <i>for</i> <i>G</i>âmâsp <a name="fr_1190"></a><a href="#fn_1178"><font size="1">1</font></a>; <i>likewise</i> her great reward from Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> for religiousness and self-devotion (khvê<i>s</i>îh) to the sacred beings. 5. About the praise of Hûtôs <a name="fr_1191"></a><a href="#fn_1179"><font size="1">2</font></a> for the arising of the progress of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion through her, by the growth of righteousness and smiting <i>of</i> the primeval fiend; <i>also</i> the good works and advantage which have arisen in the world from <i>her</i> great possessions, and her equal praise and grand position here <i>and in</i> yonder <i>world</i>.</p>  
 <p>6. About the characteristics of those who are preparing the end <i>of time</i> and arranging <i>its</i> period <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'They are a manifestation <i>of</i> those, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân Zaratû<i>s</i>t! who shall cause this renovation in the existences; they are observant, little afflictive in tormenting, <i>and</i> fully mindful, so that, when milk reaches them, they thoroughly digest <i>it;</i> they <i>have</i> no fear and accoutrements (<i>a</i>fzâr), <i>nor</i> yet do they mention false <i>and</i> irreverent (anâstŏ) statements concerning those who are righteous through imploring righteousness.' 7. About the characteristics of those disturbing the end <i>of time and</i> opposing <i>its</i> period <i>there</i> is this, too, namely: 'They are a manifestation <i>of</i> those, <i>O</i> Spîtâmân Zaratû<i>s</i>t! who are destroying the existences <a name="fr_1192"></a><a href="#fn_1180"><font size="1">3</font></a>; they are swiftly remedied, that is, they become very quickly devoured (khâî<i>d</i>ŏ) and <i>are</i> in</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_301"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 301</font></a></p>  
 <p>the torment of the vicious <i>and</i> grievous abode; they are not fully mindful, so that <i>it</i> is not possible for them to digest milk, their fear is inevitable (a<i>k</i>âr), <i>and</i> they mention even false <i>and</i> irreverent statements concerning those who are righteous through imploring righteousness.<font size="-1" color="green">'</font></p>  
 <p>8. About the craving for the fiend, the assistance of the fiend, and the gratification of the fiend <i>by</i> him who is an apostle of the demons, and his rendering the creatures of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> helpless <a name="fr_1193"></a><a href="#fn_1181"><font size="1">1</font></a> even through the want of progress (anas<i>a</i><i>k</i>isnŏ) which they lament; also the confusion owing to his speaking deceitfully in the world, <i>and</i> the connection with him of an awful and swift death <a name="fr_1194"></a><a href="#fn_1182"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and the most grievous <i>and</i> hellish punishment. 9. About that wicked follower and assistant of theirs <i>in</i> defeating righteousness, and also in destroying the greater religiousness (frêh-dênôîh) <i>of</i> the world and making the soul wicked in the end.</p>  
 <p>10. About the occurrence of the dissipation of the glory of him who is a well-ruling man, and the pacification (âsû<i>d</i>anŏ) of the creatures of the world by the sacred beings, it says this, too, namely: 'The persuader <i>to</i> evil <a name="fr_1195"></a><a href="#fn_1183"><font size="1">3</font></a> and the organizer of distress (veshi<i>s</i>nŏ)where they shall make pain <i>and</i> distress current in the worldare the weakener (nerefsînî<i>d</i>âr) and corrupter (âlâyî<i>d</i>âr) for the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_302"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 302</font></a></p>  
 <p>righteous; it is the ruler that is righteous who smites them and opposes themthat is, restrains them from sinand causes hatred for them through <i>his</i> will <a name="fr_1196"></a><a href="#fn_1184"><font size="1">1</font></a>; that, O Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>! is this dominion of thine <i>by</i> which you give benefits (vehî<i>g</i>ânŏ) <i>to</i> him who is justly living <i>and</i> poor <a name="fr_1197"></a><a href="#fn_1185"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>11. It is perfect excellence <i>that is</i> righteousness.</p>
