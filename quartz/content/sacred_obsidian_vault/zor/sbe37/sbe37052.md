 <p><a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 175</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER III <a name="fr_501"></a><a href="#fn_498"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. The second fargar<i>d</i>, Ashem-vohû <a name="fr_502"></a><a href="#fn_499"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is about the praise of righteousness which is the reward of the religion, and the want of praise at the bridge <i>of judgment</i> owing to enmity (patyânîh) to righteousness.</p>  
 <p>2. Of righteousness perfect is the excellence.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_498"></a><a href="sbe37052.htm#fr_501">175:1</a> This chapter is omitted in K by mistake.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_499"></a><a href="sbe37052.htm#fr_502">175:2</a> This second sacred formula is recited by the Parsis even oftener than the Ahunavair, and consists of twelve Avesta words, as follows:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Ashem vohû vahi<i>s</i>tem astî,<br> u<i>s</i>tâ astî; u<i>s</i>tâ ahmâi<br> hya<i>d</i> ashâi vahi<i>s</i>tâi ashem.</dir>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> This may be translated as follows:'Righteousness is the best good, a blessing it is; a blessing <i>be</i> to that which <i>is</i> righteousness to perfect rectitude.'</p>  
 <p>But the Pahlavi version explains it as follows:'Righteousness is perfect excellence [righteousness of any excellence <i>is</i> good]. Happy is that righteousness and happy also that virtuous man who <i>is</i> a causer of righteousness, the righteousness that is perfect [that is, he shall accomplish duty and good works].'</p>
