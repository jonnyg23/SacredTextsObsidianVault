 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The Ra<i>d</i>ŏ-dâ<i>d</i>-aîtag <a name="fr_70"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">4</font></a> <i>contains</i> particulars <i>about</i> the religious and important customs <i>and</i> laws to be</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_20"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 20</font></a></p>  
 <p>enforced. 2. The reason of the worthiness and superexcellence in a sacerdotal leader, <i>and his</i> possession of a portion of the other authority (patîh) <i>of</i> a ruler also; that is, how worthiness is to be distinguished from unworthiness, and superexcellence from unworthiness, in him, namely, in the priestly chieftainship (ra<i>d</i>îh) <i>of</i> Khvanîras <a name="fr_71"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">1</font></a> <i>and</i> the other Legions, each separately, the first which stood aloof from the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshippers.</p>  
 <p>3. About the demonstration <i>and</i> notification of the sitting together of the archangels, the ritual and appliances in the ceremonial of the sacred beings, the position and business of the Zôtis <i>and</i> Râspîs <a name="fr_72"></a><a href="#fn_71"><font size="1">2</font></a> in a ceremonial, and also all the business <i>of</i> the leaders in their duty, each separately <i>and</i> originally <a name="fr_73"></a><a href="#fn_72"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 4. The greatness of the helpfulness (vi<i>g</i>î<i>d</i>âr-dahi<i>s</i>nîh) in good works, the kinds of helpfulness, and the proximity <i>of</i> Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> to the thoughts, words, <i>and</i> deeds <i>of</i> the embodied existence.</p>  
 <p>5. The excellence <i>of</i> righteousness is perfect.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_69"></a><a href="sbe37011.htm#fr_70">19:4</a> Corresponding to the seventh word, <i>k</i>î<i>d</i>, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the eighth Nask in other Rivâyats. Ra<i>d</i>ŏ-dâ<i>d</i>-aîtag means 'concerning the habits of a priestly master,' which is a fair description of the contents of this Nask, but it is misread Ratu<i>s</i>tâî, or Ratu<i>s</i>tâîd, in the Rivâyats, which also state that it contained originally fifty kardah, or subdivisions, of which only thirteen were recovered after the time of Alexander.</p>
