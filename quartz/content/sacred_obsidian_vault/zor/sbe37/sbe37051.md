 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER II.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sû</i>d<i>kar Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. Glorification for the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion <i>which is</i> the ordinance of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> opposed to the demons.</p>  
 <p>2. Of the Sû<i>d</i>kar <a name="fr_495"></a><a href="#fn_492"><font size="1">4</font></a> there are twenty-two fargar<i>d</i>s,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and</i> the first fargar<i>d</i> is the Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <a name="fr_496"></a><a href="#fn_493"><font size="1">1</font></a>, just as the Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <i>formula</i> is as <i>it were</i> the beginning (bûnîh) of the religion, and from it is the formation of the Nasks which, though about the first six sciences (dâni<i>s</i>nŏ), <i>have</i> also demonstrated the existence of the highest of other sciences in its own place.</p>  
 <p>3. And here it speaks about the power and success owing to uttering the Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <i>formula</i> <a name="fr_497"></a><a href="#fn_494"><font size="1">2</font></a> at the beginning of actions. 4. One <i>utterance</i> when <i>one</i> wishes to say anything to <i>any</i> one; one when he wishes to beg of <i>any</i> one; <i>and</i> one when he goes to work. 5. Two when he wishes to confer his blessing. 6. Four when <i>it is</i> for the homage of the chiefs <i>of creation</i> (ra<i>d</i>ŏ-franâmi<i>s</i>nîh), <i>or</i> the ceremony of a season-festival. 7. Five when <i>it is</i> for carrying off the fiend. 8. Six when <i>it is</i> for power; <i>and</i> six when <i>it is</i> for the success <i>of</i> a battle. 9. Seven when <i>it is</i> for the ceremonial of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a></p>  
 <p>the archangels, <i>or</i> when <i>one</i> wishes to perform the ceremonial of the archangels. 10. Eight when <i>it</i> is for the ceremonial of a guardian spirit of the righteous. 11. Nine when <i>one</i> wishes to cast seed into his land. 12. Ten when <i>one</i> wishes to allow procreation. 13. Eleven when <i>one</i> goes to ask for a wife. 14. Twelve when <i>one</i> expects to go up on a mountain. 15. Thirteen when <i>one</i> wishes to go to an inhabited district (rû<i>d</i>astâk-1); twelve <a name="fr_498"></a><a href="#fn_495"><font size="1">1</font></a> when he goes out pathless; <i>and</i> one <a name="fr_499"></a><a href="#fn_496"><font size="1">2</font></a> when he wishes to proceed by a ford <i>through</i> the water.</p>  
 <p>16. About <i>the place</i> where <i>one has</i> to utter the first Yathâ-ahû-vairyô for smiting the demons. 17. About the good results (dahi<i>s</i>nân) <i>of</i> a suitable recital <i>of</i> the words <i>of</i> the Ahunavair, the summary <i>of</i> everything for Zaratû<i>s</i>t to utter. 18. And about <i>the fact that</i>, through chanting forth every single word of the Ahunavair with a virtuous intention, a demon is disabled, and <i>there is</i> protection of person <i>and</i> property from the adversary.</p>  
 <p>19. About the division of the twenty-one Nasks, likewise, according to the first, second, and third lines (gâs) of the Ahunavair <a name="fr_500"></a><a href="#fn_497"><font size="1">3</font></a>. 20. About the increase of the creatures owing to the liberal thought, word, and deed of a righteous <i>person; owing to</i> the priests <i>having</i> become numerous, and the reverence <i>of</i> him who is making them numerous, and <i>owing to</i> the perpetual meditation of righteousness and the existence of its recompense.</p>  
 <p>21. Righteousness is perfect excellence.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_492"></a><a href="sbe37051.htm#fr_495">172:4</a> The first of the Nasks and second of the Gâthic division (see Bk. VIII, Chap. I, 9, 12). As the Stô<i>d</i>-ya<i>s</i>t (the first of the Gâthic <a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a> division, but the last of the general list of Nasks) contained the text of the Gâthas, so the next three of the Gâthic division contained commentaries, or homilies, upon that text, written with different objects in view. The purpose of the Sû<i>d</i>kar was apparently (as its name imports) to extract useful instruction from the text, and to illustrate it with legends and remarks. A separate fargar<i>d</i> is devoted to each hâ of the Gâthas, beginning with the three sacred formulas, and including the united Yasna Haptanghâiti and the Aîryaman. The connection between the commentary and text, though usually traceable, is not always very clear; but that is a common characteristic of homilies in general.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_493"></a><a href="sbe37051.htm#fr_496">173:1</a> The Ahunavair (see Bk. VIII, Chap. I, 7). This fargar<i>d</i> explains the use made of this formula, and the benefits derived from it.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_494"></a><a href="sbe37051.htm#fr_497">173:2</a> As a spell, or appeal for success. The text of §§ 4-15 has been independently handed down by tradition, with a few variations, in Sls. XIX and the Persian Rivâyat of Bahman Pûn<i>g</i>yah.</p>
