 <p><a name="page_448"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 448</font></a> <a name="page_449"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 449</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">NASK-FRAGMENTS</h1>  <h5 align="center">THAT ARE</h5>  <h4 align="center">STILL EXTANT.</h4>  
 <p><a name="page_450"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 450</font></a> <a name="page_451"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 451</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">EXTANT FRAGMENTS <a name="fr_1729"></a><a href="#fn_1688"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h1>  <h3 align="center">I. SÛ<i>D</i>KAR NASK <a name="fr_1730"></a><a href="#fn_1689"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>Dk. IX, Chap. II, 3-15, referring to the useful effect of reciting the Yathâ-ahû-vairyô as a spell, on various occasions, is quoted in Sls. XIX, 1-14, and also in the Persian Rivâyat of Bahman Pûn<i>g</i>yah <a name="fr_1731"></a><a href="#fn_1690"><font size="1">3</font></a>, with some slight variations.</p>  
 <p>Dk. IX, Chap. VI, 2, refers to the passage thus mentioned in Sls. IX, 2, 3:'The priest who passes away in an out-district (aûzdehîkîh) thou hast considered as desolate (vîrân); and there is a high-priest who is of a different opinion, there is <i>one</i> who says <i>it is as</i> a non-Irânian (anâîrân) country. <i>It is</i> declared that, when a supreme high-priest (zaratû<i>s</i>trotûm) passes away in an out-district, an apostate will be born in that dwelling, and this calamity is only (aêvâ<i>k</i>) mentioned as to the supreme high-priest <a name="fr_1732"></a><a href="#fn_1691"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Dk. IX, Chap. VIII, 1-6, refers to Zaratû<i>s</i>t's</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_452"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 452</font></a></p>  
 <p>prophetic vision of the golden, silver, steel, and mingled-iron periods of his millennium, quoted at length in Byt. I, 1-5, with variations indicative of the date of the Bahman Ya<i>s</i>t being later than that of this Nask.</p>  
 <p>Dk. IX, Chap. X, 3, refers to the detailed account of the seven most heinous evil-doers, quoted in Dd. LX XII, 3-9,</p>  
 <p>Dk. IX, Chap. XV, refers to the supplication of the soul of Keresâsp for admittance into heaven, on account of his heroic deeds, quoted at length in the Dâ<i>d</i>istân Pahlavi Rivâyat and the Persian Sad-darband-i Hûsh (see S.B.E., vol. xviii, pp. 373-381).</p>  
 <p>Dk. IX, Chap. XVIII, 2, refers to the passage thus quoted in Sls. X, 8:'For in the Stû<i>d</i>gar it is said, concerning those who <i>have</i> unlawfully slaughtered animals, the punishment is such that <i>each</i> hair <i>of</i> those animals becomes like a sharp dagger (têkh), and he who is unlawfully a slaughterer is slain.'</p>  
 <p>No allusion has been noticed in Dk. IX to another passage which is thus quoted in Sls. XII, 32: 'In the Stûdgar it says thus: "What prepares sneezing? that is, through what process (kâr) does it come?" And Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> spoke thus: "Hungry living, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! moreover, the remedy for its existence is the Ahunavair, <i>O</i> Zaratû<i>s</i>t! <i>and</i> the Ashem (aharâyîh)."'</p>
