 <p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 144</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XLII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Sakâ</i>d<i>ûm Nask</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. One section, the Varistân ('<i>ordeal code</i>'), <i>contains</i> particulars of that <i>which</i>, when it becomes manifest in any one, is indicative as to witchcraft; the bringing of remedies <i>for</i> the person who is rendered sickly by a wizard; the execution of the wizard, what the religious rite is in the legal proceedings, <i>and</i> the <i>case</i> when <i>there is</i> a religious rite in the legal proceedings. 2. About the <i>case</i> when, for want of legal proceedings, he is executed without the religious rite; and what it is when <a name="fr_348"></a><a href="#fn_346"><font size="1">1</font></a> he dies through his own destruction of some one.</p>  
 <p>3. About the accomplishment of an ordeal by which, through the power of the spirit, <i>there</i> arises a manifestation of acquittal <i>or</i> incrimination of those maintaining inconsistencies as to witchcraft, destroying a righteous <i>man, or</i> other concealed instigations of sin <a name="fr_349"></a><a href="#fn_347"><font size="1">2</font></a>; the time of its performance, and the place of hurtfulness of its continuance. 4. About the place of accomplishment; <i>in</i> what manner is the selection (fragâr<i>d</i>anŏ), limitation, <i>and</i> preparation of the abode <i>in</i> which the ordeal is performed; that which is to be carried forth to that abode, <i>and</i> that of which the carrying thereto is to be avoided; who is to be admitted to that abode, <i>and</i> who is not to be admitted; <i>and</i> that which, when it occurs there,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 145</font></a></p>  
 <p>is a disturbance of the work, they separate (van<i>g</i>end) therefrom.</p>  
 <p>5. About those belonging to the place of ordeal (varistânîkân) and other officials there, the rites and customs therein, the ceremonial to be celebrated in the abode, <i>and</i> the invocation of the sacred beings for assistance. 6. What is the mode <i>of</i> performing the hot and cold ordeal; how is the leading forth <i>of</i> the accomplishers thereto, and of what Avesta is their uplifted recitation; how is the accomplishment of the hot and cold ordeal, <i>and</i> the manifestation <i>of</i> the acquitted and incriminated thereby; <i>and</i> many statements (gôkân) on the same subject.</p>
