 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Pahlavi Texts, Part IV (SBE37)</i>, E.W. West, tr. [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">III. FROM THE RIVÂYAT OF NARÊMÂN HÔSHANG <a name="fr_1720"></a><a href="#fn_1679"><font size="1">3</font></a></h3>  
 <p>1. Again, that which is in Pahlavi characters is clear <i>in</i> that manner, that in the Yathâ-ahû-vairyô there are twenty-one vocables, that is, twenty-one words, and beneath each word there is an equivalent meaning; it is also known to the devout <i>that</i> there are twenty-one Nasks of the Avesta.</p>  
 <p>2. The first Nask is of thirty-three subdivisions, that is, it is of thirty-three compilations, and its name is Stôd-ya<i>s</i>t, that is, 'producing the praise of the sacred beings;' and the words of the book <i>have</i> come down for the majesty of the sacred beings and angels, and they recite <i>them</i> in that manner; and <i>in</i> the presence of every high-priest of the pure ritual, who rightly understands its Avesta and Zand,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_429"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 429</font></a></p>  
 <p>as he recites <i>them</i> correctly on three occasions, the angels come down; of this there is no doubt.</p>  
 <p>3. The name of the second is Istûdgar, and it is of twenty-two subdivisions; its statements are for the admonition of the people.</p>  
 <p>4. The name of the third Nask is Vahi<i>s</i>t-mânthrah, and it is of twenty-two subdivisions; its purport is to bring confirmation of the religion.</p>  
 <p>5. The name of the fourth is Bagh, and this is of twenty-one subdivisions; its explanation is about heedfulness.</p>  
 <p>6. The name of the fifth is Dvâzdah-hâmâst, and it is of thirty-two subdivisions; its explanation is in remembrance of the upper world, and about the lower world.</p>  
 <p>7. The name of the sixth is Nâdar, and that is of thirty-five compilations; its explanation is about the interpretation of the world of the stars, the planets and constellations, and understanding the arrangement of the sky.</p>  
 <p>8. The seventh is the Pâ<i>g</i>am; this Nask is of twenty-two subdivisions, and its explanation is with regard to lawful and unlawful animals, whenever they slaughter <i>them</i> for the sake of solemnizing a season-festival; and, again, whatever is manifold reward and good work; also about the reason of the five days of the guardian spirits, which they call the select, <i>and</i> wherefore they are appointed.</p>  
 <p>9. The name of the eighth is Ratu<i>s</i>tâyî, and it is of fifty subdivisions, of which, after <i>the time of</i> Alexander, they preserved and found no more <i>than</i> thirteen subdivisions; its explanation is about maintaining devotion, and <i>of</i> obedience to kings, high-priests, and governors.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_430"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 430</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. The name of the ninth is Bari<i>s</i>, and this Nask was of sixty subdivisions, <i>but</i> after <i>the time of</i> Alexander they found no more <i>than</i> twelve subdivisions; its explanation is <i>of</i> those who are kings and high-priests, leaders and princes, judges and messengers, what is the nature of the authority of peasants and princes, and what kind of towns they possess.</p>  
 <p>11. The name of the tenth is Ka<i>s</i>srôb, and this Nask has been of sixty subdivisions, <i>but</i> after <i>the time of</i> Alexander they found again no more <i>than</i> fifteen subdivisions; its explanation is about the distinction of natural wisdom and knowledge from acquired knowledge; that which makes mankind pure from defilement, and the usage that maintains the proceedings of mankind.</p>  
 <p>12. The eleventh is the Nask of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-shâh, and it has been of sixty subdivisions, <i>but</i> after <i>the time of</i> Alexander they found again no more <i>than</i> ten subdivisions, and <i>their</i> statements are with regard to king Gu<i>s</i>tâsp making the religion current.</p>  
 <p>13. The name of the twelfth is <i>H</i>a<i>s</i>t, and it is of twenty-two subdivisions; its explanation is about enquiry of wisdom, maintaining devotion in the world, <i>and</i> the punishment for every sin <i>such</i> as they supply <i>it</i>.</p>  
 <p>14. The name of the thirteenth is Sfend, and it is of sixty subdivisions; its explanation is <i>of</i> that which they demonstrate <i>as</i> miracles from this Nask, that every requirement comes to pass <i>which</i> every high-priest<i>who</i> shall recite this Nask for several days with seven<i>fold</i> voice, according to that which has been writtenshall solicit for the world.</p>  
 <p>15. The name of the fourteenth is <i>G</i>ira<i>s</i>t, and</p>  
 <p><a name="page_431"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 431</font></a></p>  
 <p>this is of twenty-two subdivisions, its explanation is about the creation of the people of mankind in the day of the Eternal, until the last day of the resurrection occurs; their becoming manifest in the mother's womb, <i>and</i> why some die in the womb and some are born, some are kings and some are peasants.</p>  
 <p>16. The name of the fifteenth is Baghân-ya<i>s</i>t, <i>and</i> this is of seventeen subdivisions; its explanation is about the praise of the angels admitted, and, <i>as to</i> the servants of the Most Glorious, <i>at</i> what period they each become manifest, and what duty they perform, till the resurrection.</p>  
 <p>17. The name of the sixteenth is Niyâram, and this is of fifty-four subdivisions; its explanation is about decrees <i>as to</i> traders, covenants and decisions, that is, awards with regard to the creatures <i>and</i> how they act.</p>  
 <p>18. The name of the seventeenth is Aspâram, and this is of sixty-four subdivisions; its explanation in these is well-directed and a good <i>thing;</i> and whatever remains therein.</p>  
 <p>19. The eighteenth is the Duvâsarô<i>g</i>ad, and it is of sixty-five subdivisions; its explanation is that which is a statement on the subject of Khêdyôdath, that is, forming a union with each other <i>by</i> relations and those next <i>one another</i>.</p>  
 <p>20. The name of the nineteenth is Askâram, and it is of fifty-two subdivisions; its explanation is about the occurrence of the production of the renovation <i>of the universe</i>, up to the resurrection <i>and</i> future existence which are the converting of the dead alive, Aharman <i>and</i> the demons becoming extinct, and the circumstances of those <i>events</i>.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_432"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 432</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. The name of the twentieth is <i>G</i>ud-dêv-dâd <a name="fr_1721"></a><a href="#fn_1680"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and that is of twenty-two subdivisions; its explanation is of causing the abstinence of mankind from pollution, that is, from defilement, and the assault of evil peculiarly owing to the great; <i>from</i> the sight of a menstruous woman, and the like of these, whenever harm and injury happen to the creation.</p>  
 <p>22. The name of the twenty-first is Hâdokht, and this is a book of thirty subdivisions; its explanation is such that the accursed devil goes far from every one <i>who</i> shall recite this book together with the Ya<i>s</i>t, and this person becomes near unto the sacred being, the praiseworthy <i>and</i> exalted, and in such manner as <i>he is</i> near the sacred being in like manner he obtains rank.</p>  
 <p>23. And the purpose of this <i>being</i> written is so, that it is known to these humble individuals <a name="fr_1722"></a><a href="#fn_1681"><font size="1">2</font></a> in this manner, that these books are of those tendencies, and it has been written <i>by</i> those devout <i>ones</i> <a name="fr_1723"></a><a href="#fn_1682"><font size="1">3</font></a> in such manner that 'among us no one is able to read the Pahlavi characters, and the interpretation of these Nasks is in Pahlavi; any one who does not know the Pahlavi characters is high-priest and is not able to demonstrate the miracles of the religion, nor that which was written with regard to the commentary of these Nasks.'</p>
