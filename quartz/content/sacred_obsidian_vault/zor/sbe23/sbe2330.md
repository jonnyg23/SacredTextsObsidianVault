 <p><a name="page_346"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 346</font></a> <a name="page_347"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 347</font></a> <a name="page_348"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 348</font></a> <a name="page_349"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 349</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">NYÂYI<i>S</i>.</h1>  
 <p><font size="-1">A Nyâyi<i>s</i> is properly a begging prayer, as opposed to Sitâyi<i>s</i>, a prayer of praise. It is a term particularly applied to five prayers addressed to the Sun, to Mithra, to the Moon, to Waters, and to Fire. Every layman over eight years old is bound to recite the Nyâyi<i>s</i>: he recites it standing and girded with his Kôstî.</font></p>  
 <p>The Sun Nyâyi<i>s</i> is recited three times a day, at the rising of the sun (Gâh Hâvan), at noon (Gâh Rapitvin), and at three o'clock in the afternoon (Gâh Uzîren). The Mithra Nyâyi<i>s</i> is recited with the Sun Nyâyi<i>s</i>, as Mithra follows the sun in its course (see Yt. X, 13).</p>  
 <p>The Moon Nyâyi<i>s</i> is recited three times a month: first, at the time when it begins to be seen; second, when it is at the full; third, when it is on the wane.</p>  
 <p>The Waters Nyâyi<i>s</i> and the Fire Nyâyi<i>s</i> are recited every day, when one finds oneself in the proximity of those elements. The Fire Nyâyi<i>s</i> is recited with the Penôm on (see Vend. p. 168, 7).</p>  
 <p>The first four Nyâyi<i>s</i> must be recited especially on the days over which the Izads invoked preside; that is to say, on the Khôrshê<i>d</i>, Mihir, Mâh, and Âbân days (the eleventh, sixteenth, twelfth, and tenth days of the month) <a name="fr_1567"></a><a href="#fn_1499"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p align="center">____________________________</p>  <h3 align="center">I. KHÔRSHÊ<i>D</i> NYÂYI<i>S</i>.</h3>  
 <p>1. [Hail unto the Sun, the swift-horsed! May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced <a name="fr_1568"></a><a href="#fn_1500"><font size="1">2</font></a>!]</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_350"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 350</font></a></p>  
 <p>Hail unto thee, O Ahura Mazda, in the threefold way <a name="fr_1569"></a><a href="#fn_1501"><font size="1">1</font></a>! [Hail unto thee] before all other creatures!</p>  
 <p>Hail unto you, O Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas, who are all of you of one accord with the Sun!</p>  
 <p>May this prayer come unto Ahura Mazda! May it come unto the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas! May it come unto the Fravashis of the holy Ones! May it come unto the Sovereign Vayu of the long Period <a name="fr_1570"></a><a href="#fn_1502"><font size="1">2</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>2. [Hail unto the Sun, the swift-horsed!]</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced! May Angra Mainyu be destroyed! by those who do truly what is the foremost wish (of God).</font></p>  
 <p>I recite the 'Praise of Holiness <a name="fr_1571"></a><a href="#fn_1503"><font size="1">3</font></a>:'</p>  
 <p>3. I praise well-thought, well-spoken, and well-done thoughts, words, and deeds. I embrace all good thoughts, good words, and good deeds; I reject all evil thoughts, evil words, and evil deeds <a name="fr_1572"></a><a href="#fn_1504"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. I give sacrifice and prayer unto you, O Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas! even with the fulness of my thoughts, of my words, of my deeds, and of my heart: I give unto you even my own life.</p>  
 <p>I recite the 'Praise of Holiness <a name="fr_1573"></a><a href="#fn_1503"><font size="1">3</font></a>:'</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">'Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good. Well is it for it, well is it for that holiness which is perfection of holiness <a name="fr_1574"></a><a href="#fn_1504"><font size="1">4</font></a>!'</font></p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_1575"></a><a href="#fn_1505"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Hail to Ahura Mazda!</p>  
 <p>Hail to the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas!</p>  
 <p>Hail to Mithra, the lord of wide pastures!</p>  
 <p>Hail to the Sun, the swift-horsed!</p>  
 <p>Hail to the two eyes of Ahura Mazda!</p>  
 <p>Hail to the Bull!</p>  
 <p>Hail to Gaya <a name="fr_1576"></a><a href="#fn_1506"><font size="1">6</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_351"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 351</font></a></p>  
 <p>Hail to the Fravashi of the holy Spitama Zarathu<i>s</i>tra!</p>  
 <p>Hail to the whole of the holy creation that was, is, or will be!</p>  
 <p>May I grow in health of body through Vohu-Manô, Khshathra, and Asha, and come to that luminous space, to that highest of all high things <a name="fr_1577"></a><a href="#fn_1507"><font size="1">1</font></a>, when the world, O Spe<i>n</i>ta Mainyu! has come to an end!</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>6. We sacrifice unto the bright, undying, shining, swift-horsed Sun.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Mithra, the lord of wide pastures, who is truth-speaking, a chief in assemblies, with a thousand ears, well-shapen, with ten thousand eyes, high, with full knowledge, strong, sleepless, and ever awake <a name="fr_1578"></a><a href="#fn_1508"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>7. We sacrifice unto Mithra, the lord of all countries, whom Ahura Mazda made the most glorious of all the gods in the world unseen <a name="fr_1579"></a><a href="#fn_1509"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>So may Mithra and Ahura, the two great gods, come to us for help!</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the bright, undying, shining, swift-horsed Sun.</p>  
 <p>8. We sacrifice unto Ti<i>s</i>trya, whose sight is sound <a name="fr_1580"></a><a href="#fn_1510"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Ti<i>s</i>trya; we sacrifice unto the rains of Ti<i>s</i>trya <a name="fr_1581"></a><a href="#fn_1511"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Ti<i>s</i>trya, bright and glorious.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the star Vana<i>n</i><i>t</i> <a name="fr_1582"></a><a href="#fn_1512"><font size="1">6</font></a>, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_352"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 352</font></a></p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Ti<i>s</i>trya, the bright and glorious star.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the sovereign sky.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the boundless Time.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the sovereign Time of the long Period.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the beneficent, well-doing Wind.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the most upright <i>K</i>ista <a name="fr_1583"></a><a href="#fn_1513"><font size="1">1</font></a>, made by Mazda and holy.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the good Law of the worshippers of Mazda.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the way of content <a name="fr_1584"></a><a href="#fn_1514"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the golden instrument <a name="fr_1585"></a><a href="#fn_1515"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Mount Saoka<i>n</i>ta, made by Mazda <a name="fr_1586"></a><a href="#fn_1515"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>9. We sacrifice unto all the holy gods of the world unseen.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto all the holy gods of the material world.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto our own soul.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto our own Fravashi.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the good, strong, beneficent Fravashis of the holy Ones.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto the bright, undying, shining, swift-horsed Sun.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">10. I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_353"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 353</font></a></p>  
 <p>Unto the bright, undying, shining, swift-horsed Sun;</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Be propitiation, with sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification . . . . <a name="fr_1587"></a><a href="#fn_1516"><font size="1">1</font></a></font></p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>[We sacrifice] unto the Ahurian waters <a name="fr_1588"></a><a href="#fn_1517"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the waters of Ahura, with excellent libations, with finest libations, with libations piously strained <a name="fr_1589"></a><a href="#fn_1518"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .</p>  
 <p>[Give] unto that man brightness and glory, give him health of body, . . . . give him the bright, all-happy, blissful abode of the holy Ones.</p>
