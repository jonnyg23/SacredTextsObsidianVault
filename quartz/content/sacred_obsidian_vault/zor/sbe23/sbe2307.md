 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">II. HAPTÂN YA<i>S</i>T.</h3>  
 <p><font size="-1">The Ya<i>s</i>t of the seven Amshaspands is recited on the first seven days of the week, that is to say, on the days consecrated to the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas. In fact it is nothing more than an extract from the Sîrôzahs, being composed of the first seven formulas in their two forms: §§ 1-5 = Sîrôzah I, 1-7; §§ 6-10 = Sîrôzah II, 1-7. Then come four sections which are the original part of the Ya<i>s</i>t (§§ 11-15).</font></p>  
 <p align="center">______________________</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">0. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced <a name="fr_192"></a><a href="#fn_187"><font size="1">1</font></a>! . . . .</font></p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good <a name="fr_193"></a><a href="#fn_187"><font size="1">1</font></a> . . . .</p>  
 <p>I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu<i>s</i>tra, one who hates the Daêvas and obeys the laws of Ahura;</p>  
 <p>For sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification unto [Hâvani], the holy and master of holiness <a name="fr_194"></a><a href="#fn_187"><font size="1">1</font></a> . . . .</p>  <h4 align="center">I.</h4>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_195"></a><a href="#fn_188"><font size="1">2</font></a>. To Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious, and to the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas;</p>  
 <p>To Vohu-Manô; to Peace, whose breath is friendly, and who is more powerful to destroy than all other creatures; to the heavenly Wisdom, made by Mazda, and to the Wisdom acquired through the ear, made by Mazda;</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_196"></a><a href="#fn_189"><font size="1">3</font></a>. To Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta, the fairest; to the much-desired Airyaman; to the instrument made by Mazda; and to the good Saoka, with eyes of love, made by Mazda and holy;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 36</font></a></p>  
 <p>To Khshathra-Vairya; to the metals; to Mercy and Charity.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_197"></a><a href="#fn_190"><font size="1">1</font></a>. To the good Spe<i>n</i>ta-Ârmaiti, and to the good Râta, with eyes of love, made by Mazda and holy;</p>  
 <p>To Haurvatâ<i>t</i>, the master; to the prosperity of the seasons and to the years, the masters of holiness;</p>  
 <p>And to Ameretâ<i>t</i>, the master; to fatness and flocks; to the plenty of corn; and to the powerful Gaokerena, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_198"></a><a href="#fn_191"><font size="1">2</font></a>. (At the Gâh Hâvan): To Mithra, the lord of wide pastures and to Râma <i>H</i><i>v</i>âstra.</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Rapithwin): To Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta and to Âtar, the son of Ahura Mazda.</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Uzîren): To Apãm Napâ<i>t</i>, the tall lord, and to water, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_199"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">3</font></a>. (At the Gâh Aiwisrûthrem): To the Fravashis of the faithful and to the females that bring forth flocks of males; to the prosperity of the seasons; to the well-shapen and tall-formed Strength; to Verethraghna, made by Ahura, and to the crushing Ascendant.</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh U<i>s</i>ahin): To the holy, devout, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world grow; to Rashnu-Razi<i>s</i>ta and to Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i>, who makes the world grow, who makes the world increase <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">3</font></a>;</p>  
 <p>Be propitiation, with sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification!</p>  
 <p>Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>

 <p><a name="page_37"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 37</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">II.</h3>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_201"></a><a href="#fn_193"><font size="1">1</font></a> We sacrifice unto Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious; we sacrifice unto the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas, the all-ruling, the all-beneficent.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Vohu-Manô, the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>ta; we sacrifice unto Peace, whose breath is friendly, and who is more powerful to destroy than all other creatures; we sacrifice unto the heavenly Wisdom, made by Mazda; we sacrifice unto the wisdom acquired through the ear, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_202"></a><a href="#fn_194"><font size="1">2</font></a>. We sacrifice unto Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta, the fairest, the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>ta; we sacrifice unto the much-desired Airyaman; we sacrifice unto the instrument made by Mazda; we sacrifice unto the good Saoka, with eyes of love, made by Mazda and holy.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Khshathra-Vairya; we sacrifice unto the metals; we sacrifice unto Mercy and Charity.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_203"></a><a href="#fn_195"><font size="1">3</font></a>. We sacrifice unto the good Spe<i>n</i>ta-Ârmaiti; we sacrifice unto the good Râta, with eyes of love, made by Mazda and holy.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Haurvatâ<i>t</i>, the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>ta; we sacrifice unto the prosperity of the seasons; we sacrifice unto the years, the holy and masters of holiness.</p>  
 <p>We sacrifice unto Ameretâ<i>t</i>, the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>ta; we sacrifice unto fatness and flocks; we sacrifice unto the plenty of corn; we sacrifice unto the powerful Gaokerena, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_204"></a><a href="#fn_196"><font size="1">4</font></a>. (At the Gâh Hâvan): We sacrifice unto</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 38</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Mithra, the lord of wide pastures; we sacrifice unto Râma <i>H</i><i>v</i>âstra.</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Rapithwin): We sacrifice unto Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta and Âtar, the son of Ahura Mazda.</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Uzîren): We sacrifice unto Apãm Napâ<i>t</i>, the swift-horsed, the tall and shining lord, the lord of the females; we sacrifice unto the holy waters, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_197"><font size="1">1</font></a>. (At the Gâh Aiwisrûthrem): We sacrifice unto the good, powerful, beneficent Fravashis of the faithful; we sacrifice unto the females who bring forth flocks of males; we sacrifice unto the prosperity of the seasons; we sacrifice unto the well-shapen, tall-formed Strength; we sacrifice unto Verethraghna, made by Mazda; we sacrifice unto the crushing Ascendant.</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh U<i>s</i>ahin): We sacrifice unto the holy, tall-formed, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world grow, the holy and master of holiness; we sacrifice unto Rashnu-Razi<i>s</i>ta; we sacrifice unto Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i>, who makes the world grow, who makes the world increase.</p>  <h4 align="center">III.</h4>  
 <p>11. Let the Yâtus be crushed, O Zarathu<i>s</i>tra <a name="fr_206"></a><a href="#fn_198"><font size="1">2</font></a>! both Daêvas and men <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>Who is he in whose house, O Spitama Zarathu<i>s</i>tra! every Dru<i>g</i> is destroyed, every Dru<i>g</i> perishes, when he pronounces these words <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">4</font></a>:</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_209"></a><a href="#fn_201"><font size="1">5</font></a> . . . . . . . . . ?</p>  
 <p>13. It is he who takes the seven Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 39</font></a></p>  
 <p>the all-ruling, the all-beneficent, as a shield <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_202"><font size="1">1</font></a> against his enemies.</p>  
 <p>We worship the Law of the worshippers of Mazda; we worship the waters coming in the shape of a horse <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_203"><font size="1">2</font></a>, made by Mazda.</p>  
 <p>14-15. He has renounced trespasses and faults, O Zarathu<i>s</i>tra! he has renounced all trespasses and faults <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">3</font></a>, O Zarathu<i>s</i>tra! when he throws down <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_205"><font size="1">4</font></a> the destroyer of Vohu-Manô and his words <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_206"><font size="1">5</font></a>, with a hundred times hundredfold, with a many times manifold preaching and smiting, and he takes away the Law of Mazda, that was carried away as a prisoner <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">6</font></a>, from the hands of the [ungodly], who are destroyed by his strength.</p>  
 <p>Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good. . . .</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>16. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .</p>  
 <p>I bless the sacrifice and the prayer, the strength and vigour</p>  
 <p>Of Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious, and of the Amesha-Spe<i>n</i>tas;</p>  
 <p>Of Vohu-Manô; of Peace, whose breath is friendly <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">7</font></a> . . . .</p>

 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 40</font></a></p>  
 <p>Of Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta, the fairest; of the much-desired Airyaman <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">1</font></a> . . . .</p>  
 <p>Of Khshathra-Vairya, of the metals <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">1</font></a> . .</p>  
 <p>Of the good Spe<i>n</i>ta-Ârmaiti and of the good Râta <a name="fr_219"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">2</font></a>. . . .</p>  
 <p>Of Haurvatâ<i>t</i>, the master <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">2</font></a> . . . .</p>  
 <p>Of Ameretâ<i>t</i>, the master <a name="fr_221"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">2</font></a> . . . .</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Hâvan): Of Mithra <a name="fr_222"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">3</font></a> . . . .</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Rapithwin): Of Asha-Vahi<i>s</i>ta <a name="fr_223"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">3</font></a> . . .</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Uzîren): Of the high lord Apãm Napâ<i>t</i> <a name="fr_224"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">3</font></a> . . . .</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh Aiwisrûthrem): Of the Fravashis of the faithful <a name="fr_225"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">4</font></a> . . .</p>  
 <p>(At the Gâh U<i>s</i>ahin): Of the holy, devout, fiend-smiting Sraosha, who makes the world grow; of Rashnu-Razi<i>s</i>ta and of Ar<i>s</i>tâ<i>t</i>, who makes the world grow, who makes the world increase.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1">Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . .</font></p>  
 <p>Give unto that man <a name="fr_226"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">5</font></a> brightness and glory, give him health of body, give him sturdiness of body, give him victorious strength of body, give him full welfare of wealth, give him a virtuous offspring, give him long, long life, give him the bright, all-happy, blissful abode of the holy Ones.</p>
