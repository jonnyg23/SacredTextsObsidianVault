 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Apu Ollantay</i>, by Clements Markham, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">SCENE 2</h3> <i>
 </i><p>A wild place the mountains. Distant view of Ollantay-tampu.</p><dir> <dir>  
 <p>(<i>Enter</i> Rumi-ñaui, <i>torn and ragged, and covered with blood, with two attendants</i>.)</p> 
 <p>  <i>Rumi-ñaui</i>. Ah! Rumi-ñaui--Rumi-ñaui, <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_49"><font size="1">5</font></a></p></dir> </dir>  
 <p>
 </p> 
 <p><a name="page_373"><font size="1" color="green">p. 373</font></a></p><dir> <dir>  
 <p>Thou art a fated rolling stone, <a name="fr_50"></a><a href="#fn_50"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Escaped indeed, but quite alone,<br> And this is now thy <i>yarahui</i>.</p> 
 <p>Ollantay posted on the height,<br> Thou couldst not either fight or see,<br> Thy men did quickly fall or flee;<br> No room was there to move or fight.</p> 
 <p>Thou knowest now thy heart did beat<br> And flutter like a butterfly;<br> Thy skill thou couldst not then apply,<br> No course was left thee but retreat.</p> 
 <p>They had recourse to a surprise,<br> Our warriors immolated quite.<br> Ah! that alone could turn thee white--<br> From shame like that, canst e'er arise?</p> 
 <p>By thousands did thy warriors fall,<br> I hardly could alone escape,<br> With open mouth fell death did gape,<br> A great disaster did befall.</p> 
 <p>Holding that traitor to be brave,<br> I sought to meet him face to face--<br> Rushing to seek him with my mace,<br> I nearly found a warrior's grave.</p> 
 <p>My army then was near the hill,<br> When suddenly the massive stones<br> Came crashing down, with cries and moans,<br> While clarions sounded loud and shrill.</p></dir> </dir>  
 <p>
 </p> 
 <p><a name="page_374"><font size="1" color="green">p. 374</font></a></p><dir> <dir>  
 <p>A rain of stones both great and small<br> Down on the crowd of warriors crashed,<br> On every side destruction flashed,<br> Thy heart the slaughter did appal.</p> 
 <p>Like a strong flood the blood did flow,<br> Inundating the ravine;<br> So sad a sight thou ne'er hast seen--<br> No man survived to strike a blow.</p> 
 <p>O thou who art by this disgraced,<br> What figure canst thou ever show<br> Before the king, who seeks to know<br> The truth, which must be faced?</p> 
 <p>'Tis better far myself to kill,<br> Or losing every scrap of hope,<br> To hang my body with this rope.</p> 
 <p>(<i>Takes a sling off his cap--going</i>.)</p> 
 <p>Yet may it not be useful still?</p> 
 <p>(<i>Turns again</i>.)</p> 
 <p>When bold Ollantay's end has come. <a name="fr_51"></a><a href="#fn_51"><font size="1">1</font></a></p></dir> </dir>  
 <p align="RIGHT">[<i>Exit</i>.</p>
