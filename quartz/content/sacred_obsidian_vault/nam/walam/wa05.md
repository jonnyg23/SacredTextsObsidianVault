 <p>20. After him, Much-Loved was chief, a good man.</p>
 <p>20. Wtenk nekama sakimanep pemaholend wulitowin.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_21"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_21.jpg"></p>
 <p>21. After him, No-Blood was chief, who walked in cleanliness.</p>
 <p>21. Sagimawtenk matemik, sagimawtenk pilsohalin.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_22"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_22.jpg"></p>
 <p>22. After him, Snow-Father was chief, he of the big teeth.</p>
 <p>22. Sagimawtenk gunokeni, sagimawtenk mangipitak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_23"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_23.jpg"></p>
 <p>23. After him, Tally-Maker was chief, who made records.</p>
 <p>23. Sagimawtenk olumapi, leksahowen sohalawak.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_24"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_24.jpg"></p>
 <p>24. After him, Shiverer-with-Cold was chief, who went south to the corn land.</p>
 <p>24. Sagimawtenk taguachi shawaniwaen minihaking.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_25"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_25.jpg"></p>
 <p>25. After him, Corn-Breaker was chief, who brought about the planting of corn.</p>
 <p>25. Sakimawtenk huminiend minigeman sohalgol.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_26"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_26.jpg"></p>
 <p>26. After him, the Strong-Man was chief, who was useful to the chieftains.</p>
 <p>26. Sakinawtenk alkosohit sakimachik apendawi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_27"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_27.jpg"></p>
 <p>27. After him, the Salt-Man was chief, after him the Little-One was chief</p>
 <p>27. Sawkimawtenk shiwapi, sakimatenk penkwoni.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_28"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_28.jpg"></p>
 <p>28. There was no rain, and no corn, so they moved further seaward.</p>
 <p>28. Attasokelan attaminin wapaniwaen italissipek.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_29"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_29.jpg"></p>
 <p>29. At the place of caves, in the buffalo land, they at last had food, on pleasant plain.</p>
 <p>29. Oligonunk sisilaking nallimetzin kolakwaming.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_30"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_30.jpg"></p>
 <p>30. After the Little-One (came) the Fatigued; after him, the Stiff-One.</p>
 <p>30. Wtenk penkwonwi wekwochella, wtenk nekama chingalsuwi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_31"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_31.jpg"></p>
 <p>31. After him, the Reprover; disliking him, and unwilling (to remain),</p>
 <p>31. Wtenk nekama kwitikwond, slangelendam attagatta,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_32"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_32.jpg"></p>
 <p>32. Being angry, some went off secretly, moving east.</p>
 <p>32. Wundanuksin wapanickam [Var. wapanahan.] allendyachick kimimikwi.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_33"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_33.jpg"></p>
 <p>33. The wise ones who remained made the Loving-One chief.</p>
 <p>33. Gunehunga wetatamova wakaholend sakimalanop.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_34"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_34.jpg"></p>
 <p>34. They settled again on the Yellow river, and had much corn on stoneless soil.</p>
 <p>34. Wisawana lappi wittank michi mini madawasim.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_35"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_35.jpg"></p>
 <p>35. All being friendly, the Affable was chief, the first of that name .</p>
 <p>35. Weminitis tamenend sakimanep nekohatami.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_36"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_36.jpg"></p>
 <p>36. He was very good, this Affable, and came as a friend to all the Lenape.</p>
 <p>36. Eluwiwulit matemenend wemi linapi nitis payat.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_37"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_37.jpg"></p>
 <p>37. After this good one, Strong-Buffalo was chief and pipe-bearer.</p>
 <p>37. Wtenk wulitma maskansisil sakimanep w'tamaganat.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_38"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_38.jpg"></p>
 <p>38. Big-Owl was chief; White-Bird was chief.</p>
 <p>38. Machigokloos sakimanep, wapkicholen sakimanep.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa4_39"></a>
 <img src="img/wa4_39.jpg"></p>
 <p>39. The Willing-One was chief and priest; he made festivals.</p>
 <p>39. Wingenund sakimanep powatanep gentikalanep.</p>
