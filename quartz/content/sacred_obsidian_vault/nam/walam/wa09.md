 <p>40. At this time whites came on the Eastern sea.</p>
 <p>40. Wonwihil wapekunchi wapsipayat.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_41"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_41.jpg"></p>
 <p>41. Much-Honored was chief; he was prosperous.</p>
 <p>41. Makelomush sakimanep wulatenamen.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_42"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_42.jpg"></p>
 <p>42. Well-Praised was chief; he fought at the south.</p>
 <p>42. Wulakeningus sakimanep shawanipalat.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_43"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_43.jpg"></p>
 <p>43. He fought in the land of the Talega and Koweta.</p>
 <p>43. Otaliwako akowetako ashkipalliton.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_44"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_44.jpg"></p>
 <p>44. White-Otter was chief; a friend of the Talamatans.</p>
 <p>44. Wapagamoshki sakimanep lamatanitis.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_45"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_45.jpg"></p>
 <p>45. White-Horn was chief; he went to the Talega,</p>
 <p>45. Wapashum sakimanep talegawunkik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_46"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_46.jpg"></p>
 <p>46. To the Hilini, to the Shawnees, to the Kanawhas.</p>
 <p>46. Mahiliniki mashawoniki makonowiki.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_47"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_47.jpg"></p>
 <p>47. Coming-as-a-Friend was chief; he went to the Great Lakes,</p>
 <p>47. Nitispayat sakimanep kipemapekan,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_48"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_48.jpg"></p>
 <p>48. Visiting all his children, all his friends.</p>
 <p>48. Wemiamik weminitik kiwikhotan.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_49"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_49.jpg"></p>
 <p>49. Cranberry-Eater was chief, friend of the Ottawas.</p>
 <p>49. Pakimitzin sakimanep tawanitip.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_50"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_50.jpg"></p>
 <p>50. North-Walker was chief, he made festivals.</p>
 <p>50. Lowaponskan sakimanep ganshowenik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_51"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_51.jpg"></p>
 <p>51. Slow-Gatherer was chief at the shore.</p>
 <p>51. Tashawinso sakimanep shayabing.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_52a"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_52a.jpg"></p>
 <p>52. As three were desired, three those were who grew forth,</p>
 <p>52. Nakhagattamen nakhalissin wenchikit,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_52b"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_52b.jpg"></p>
 <p>52. bis. The Unami, the Minsi, the Chikini.</p>
 <p>52. bis. Unamini minsimini chikimini.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_53"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_53.jpg"></p>
 <p>53. Man-Who-Fails was chief; he fought the Mengwe.</p>
 <p>53. Epallahchund sakimanep mahongwipallat.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_54"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_54.jpg"></p>
 <p>54. He-is-Friendly was chief; he scared the Mengwe.</p>
 <p>54. Langomuwi sakimanep mahongwichamen.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_55"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_55.jpg"></p>
 <p>55. Saluted was chief; thither,</p>
 <p>55. Wangomend sakimanep ikalawit,</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_56"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_56.jpg"></p>
 <p>56. Over there, on the Scioto, he had foes.</p>
 <p>56. Otaliwi wasiotowi shingalusit.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_57"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_57.jpg"></p>
 <p>57. White-Crab was chief, a friend of the shore.</p>
 <p>57. Wapachikis sakimanep sahyabinitis.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_58"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_58.jpg"></p>
 <p>58. Watcher was chief, he looked toward the sea.</p>
 <p>58. Nenachihat sakimanep peklinkwekin.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_59"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_59.jpg"></p>
 <p>59. At this time, from north and south, the whites came.</p>
 <p>59. Wonwihil lowashawa wapayachik.</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_wa5_60"></a>
 <img src="img/wa5_60.jpg"></p>
 <p>60. They are peaceful; they have great things; who are they?</p>
 <p>60. Langomuwak kitohatewa ewenikiktit?</p>
