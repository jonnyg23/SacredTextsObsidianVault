 <h1 align="CENTER">THE MOUNTAIN CHANT</h1>
 <h5 align="CENTER">A NAVAJO CEREMONY.</h5>
 <h2 align="CENTER">BY WASHINGTON MATTHEWS</h2>
 <h4 align="CENTER">[1887]</h4>
 </td>

 <font size="1"><a href="xlit.htm">System of transliteration.</a></font>

 <hr>
 <p>This is a description of a Navajo shamanistic healing ceremony.
 It centers around a recitation of a cycle of myths about
 a Navajo culture hero, Dsilyídje Qaçàl.
 His journey takes him to the land of the gods and goddesses,
 where he learns powerful magic.
 The narrative is compelling and compares well with the Homeric
 <a href="../../../cla/homer/ody/index.htm">Odyssey</a>,
 which it resembles both thematically and stylistically.
 The ceremony incorporates dance, song, prayer, sand-painting,
 drama, sculpture, conjuring, and even a bit of farce.
 Of note are the beautiful plates of
 <a href="tmc04.htm">the ephemeral sand-paintings</a>.
