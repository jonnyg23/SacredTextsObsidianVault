 <h1 align="CENTER">XI. Hymn of the Otomi Leader.</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">XI. Otontecutli icuic.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a href="#english">English</a></p>
 <p>1. Onoalico, onoalico, pomaya, yyaya, ayyo, ayyo, aya, aya, ayyo.</p>
 <p>2. Chimalocutitlana motlaqueuia auetzini nonoualico, quauinochitla, cacauatla motlaqueuia auetzini.</p>
 <p>3. Ni tepanecatli aya cuecuexi, ni quetzallicoatli aya cuecuexi.</p>
 <p>4. Cane ca ya itziueponi, cane ca ya itziueponi.</p>
 <p>5. Otomico, noyoco, nauaco, mexicame ya yauilili, noyoco, nauaco, mexicame ya.</p>
 <p>6. A chimalli aya, xa, xauino quiyauilili, noyoco, nauaco, mexicame ya.</p>
 <i></i><p>Var. 2. Nonoualco.</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER"><a name="english">Hymn of Otontecutli.</a></h3>
 <p>1. At Nonoalco he rules, at Nonoalco, Oho! Oho!</p>
 <p>2. In the pine woods he prepares your destruction at Nonoalco, in the tuna woods, in the cacao woods he prepares your destruction.</p>
 <p>3. I, dweller in the palace, shook them; I, Quetzalcoatl, shook them.</p>
 <p>4. There was a splendor of spears, a splendor of spears.</p>
 <p>5. With my captain, with my courage, with my skill, the Mexicans were put to flight; even the Mexicans, with my courage, with my skill.</p>
 <p>6. Go forth, ye shield bearers, put the Mexicans to flight with my courage, with my skill.</p>
 <p>{p. 46}</p>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Notes.</h3>
 <p>The absence of a Gloss to this hymn adds to the difficulty of a translation. <i>Otontecutli</i> was the chief deity of the Otomis, and the chant appears to be one of their war songs in their conflict with the Azteca. The name is a compound of <i>otomitl</i>, an Otomi, and <i>tecutli</i>, ruler or lord. He is slightly referred to by Sahagun as the first ruler to govern the ancestors of the Otomis." (<i>Historia</i>, Lib. X, Cap. 29, sec. 5.)</p></body>
