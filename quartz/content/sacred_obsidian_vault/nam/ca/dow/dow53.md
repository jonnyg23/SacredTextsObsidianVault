 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="green">p. 217</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">Beliefs concerning Ghosts and the Sign of Death</h1>  <h3 align="center">GHOSTS FOLLOW THE PATHWAY OF THE WIND</h3>  
 <p><i>The Hookooeko of Nicasio and Tomales Bay say</i>:</p>  
 <p>When a person dies his <i>Wal'-le</i> <a name="fr_22"></a><a href="#fn_22"><font size="1">24</font></a> or Ghost goes to <i>Hel'-wah</i> the West, crossing the great ocean to <i>Oo-tā-yo'-me</i>, the Village of the Dead. In making this long journey it follows <i>hinnan mooka</i>, the path of the Wind. Sometimes Ghosts come back and dance in the roundhouse; sometimes people hear them dancing inside but never see them.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_22"></a><a href="dow53.htm#fr_22">217:24</a> In this connection it is interesting to observe that in the language of the related <i>Olayome</i> of Putah Creek, Bats are called <i>Walle</i>; while the same word in the language of the Mewan Valley tribes means Ocean. The word for ocean among the Northern Mewuk is <i>Wallasu</i>; among the Middle or Tuolumne Mewuk, <i>Wallesmah</i>.</p>
