 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="green">p. 31</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">Part I: Ancient Myths</h1>  <h3 align="center">STORIES OF THE FIRST PEOPLE--PEOPLE WHO LIVED BEFORE REAL PEOPLE WERE CREATED</h3>  
 <p>The Mewuk tribes, those inhabiting the western slopes and foothills of the Sierra, call the ancient myths <i>oo'-ten-ne</i> or <i>oot'-ne</i>, meaning the history of the FIRST PEOPLE. (The Northern Mewuk say <i>oo'-ten nas'-se-sa</i>.) In this connection it may be significant that the name of Bower Cave, the home of <i>Too'-le</i> and <i>He-le'-jah</i>, two great chiefs of the FIRST PEOPLE, is <i>Oo'-tin</i>.</p>
