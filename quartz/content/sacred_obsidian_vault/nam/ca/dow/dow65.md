 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Dawn of the World</i>, by C. Hart Merriam, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE ECHO</h3>  
 <p><i>The Hookooeko of Nicasio and Tomales Bay say</i>:</p>  
 <p><i>Pe-tān'-yah</i> the Lizard with blue sides <a name="fr_27"></a><a href="#fn_27"><font size="1">29</font></a> lives everywhere in the rocks and hills and woods. When he hears a loud noise he talks back. This is <i>Si-yu-kā-i</i> the Echo; it is <i>Pe-tān'-yah</i> talking back.</p>  
 <p><i>The Olayome of Putah Creek say</i>:</p>  
 <p><i>Loo-te'-nek'-kah</i> the Echo is <i>Pe-tā'-le</i> the blue-sided Lizard talking back.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_225"><font size="1" color="green">p. 225</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>The Olamentko of Bodega Bay say</i>:</p>  
 <p>The Echo is <i>Sah-kah'-te</i> talking back.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_27"></a><a href="dow65.htm#fr_27">224:29</a> The blue-sided lizard meant is <i>Sceloporus occidentalis</i>, a common species in the coast region of California.</p>
