 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Dawn of the World</i>, by C. Hart Merriam, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">TOO'-CHA-MO, THE STUMP, AND CHOO'-KOO HENG-IL'-NAH-AS'-SE, THE LOST DOG</h3>  
 <p><i>The Northern Mewuk say</i>:</p>  
 <p><i>Too'-cha-mo</i> the stump and <i>Choo'-koo</i> the dog are friends. When <i>Choo'-koo</i> is lost and does not know where his man has gone he goes to <i>Too'-cha-mo</i> and asks. <i>Too'-cha-mo</i> tells him which way to go to strike his man's trail; <i>Choo'-koo</i> goes and finds it, and no matter how far away his man is, he follows the trail right to him.</p>
