 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XLVI</h5>  <h5 align="center">WHEN THE CHILD IS NAMED</h5>  
 <p><i>The mother and godmother on the housetop before dawn:<br>     the godmother speaks:</i></p>  
 <p>        My Sun!<br>         My Morning Star!<br>         Help this child to become a man.<br>         I name him<br>         Rain-dew Falling!<br>         I name him<br>         Star Mountain!</p>  
 <p><i>The mother throws a live coal: the godmother throws sacred meal</i>.</p>
