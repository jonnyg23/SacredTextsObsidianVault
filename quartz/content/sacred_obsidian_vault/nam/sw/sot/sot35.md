 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XXXI</h5>  <h5 align="center">RAIN MAGIC SONG</h5>  
 <p>Ready we stand in San Juan town,<br> Oh, our Corn Maidens and our Corn Youths!<br> Oh, our Corn Mothers and our Corn Fathers!</p>  
 <p>Now we bring you misty water<br> And throw it different ways,<br> To the north, the west, the south, the east<br> To heaven above and the drinking earth below!</p>  
 <p>Then likewise throw you misty water<br> Toward San Juan!<br> Oh, many that you are, pour water<br> Over our Corn Maidens’ ears!<br> On our Wheat Maidens<br> Thence throw you misty water,<br> All round about us here!</p>  
 <p>On Green Earth Woman's back<br> Now thrives our flesh and breath,<br> Now grows our strength of arm and leg,<br> Now takes form our children's food!</p>
