 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">IX</h5>  <h5 align="center">THE INCONSTANT LOVER</h5>  
 <p><i>He speaks</i><br> Oh, Little Blue, at your door I wish to be,<br> At your door that once was blue and open wide,<br> But now is closed. At your door, I wish to be<br> Oh, my little breath! Oh, my little heart!</p>  
 <p><i>She speaks</i><br> To Comanche girls you paid those words, those eyes!<br> Your wish concerns me not and I can't be blamed<br> For that! It was under guns that you dared to pay!</p>
