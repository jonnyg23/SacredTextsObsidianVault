 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Songs of the Tewa</i>, by Herbert Joseph Spinden, [1933], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h5 align="center">XV</h5>  <h5 align="center">A CORN GRINDING SONG OF TESUQUE</h5>  
 <p>There towards the north,<br> There the fog is lying,<br> There the fog is lying.<br> In the middle stands Blue Corn<br> Happily, prettily, she is singing<br> <i>Ha-we-ra-na na-a-se</i></p>  
 <p>There towards the west<br> There the fog is lying,<br> There the fog is lying,<br> In the middle stands Yellow Corn<br> Happily, prettily, she is singing<br> <i>Ha-we-ra-na na-a-se</i></p>  
 <p>There towards the south<br> There the fog is lying,<br> There the fog is lying,<br> In the middle stands Red Corn<br> Happily, prettily, she is singing<br> <i>Ha-we-ra-na na-a-se</i></p>  
 <p><a name="page_79"><font size="1" color="green">p. 79</font></a></p>  
 <p>There towards the east<br> There the fog is lying,<br> There the fog is lying,<br> In the middle stands White Corn<br> Happily, prettily, she is singing<br> <i>Ha-we-ra-na na-a-se</i></p>
