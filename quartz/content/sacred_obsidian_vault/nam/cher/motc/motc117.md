 <center><h1>117. The Mother Bear's Song</h1></center>
 <p>A hunter in the woods one day heard singing in a cave. He came near and peeped in, and it was a mother bear singing to her cubs and telling them what to do when the hunters came after them.</p>
 <p>Said the mother bear to the cubs, "When you hear the hunters coming down the creek, then-</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i><font size="2"><p>     Tsâ'gï, tsâ'gï, hwï'lahï';<br>
      Tsâ'gï, tsâ'gï, hwï'lahï.<br>
 </p></font></i>Upstream, upstream, you (must) go;<br>
 Upstream, upstream, you (must) go.</dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>But if you hear them coming up the creek, children, then--</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i><font size="2"><p>     Ge'i, ge'i, hwï'lahï';<br>
      Ge'i, ge'i, hwï'lahï';<br>
 </p></font></i>Downstream, downstream, you (must) go;<br>
 Downstream, downstream, you (must) go."</dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p align="CENTER">*    *    *    *    *    *    *</p>
 <p>Another hunter out in the woods one day thought he heard a woman singing to a baby. He followed the sound up to the head of the branch until he came to a cave under the bushes, and inside was a mother bear rocking her cub in her paws and singing to it this baby song, which the Ani'-Tsâ'gûhï used to know before they were turned into bears:</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <i><font size="2"><p>Ha'-mama', ha'-mama', ha'-mama', ha'-mama';<br>
 Udâ'hale'yï hi'lûñnû, hi'lûñnû;<br>
 Udâ'hale'yï hi'lûñnû, hi'lûñnû.<br>
 </p></font></i>Let me carry you on my back (four times);<br>
 On the sunny side go to sleep, go to sleep;<br>
 On the sunny side go to sleep, go to sleep.</dir>
 </dir>
