 <p align="center"> </p><h3 align="CENTER">Note</h3>  
 <p>   NOTE. — Sounds are rendered according to the <a href="../../../asia/cm/cm02.htm">same system as that applied in my Chukchee Texts</a>, published in this series. The following additional symbols have been used: <i>ï</i>, long glide <i>ie;</i> <i>ụ</i> like Eng. <i>u</i> in <i>hut;</i> <i>f, v,</i> bilabial; <i>z</i>, sonant of <i>s;</i> <i>c</i>, like Eng. <i>sh;</i> <i>x</i>, velar fricative; <i>ṛ</i> velar <i>r;</i> ‘ aspiration; · palatalization.</p>  
 <p> Please note that in this volume, as in the Chukchee Mythology referenced above, the unicode characters employed are not identical to those used in the printed book. See the note at the end of the <a href="../../../asia/cm/index.htm">index page for <i>Chukchee Mythology</i></a>.--CW.</p>    </body>
