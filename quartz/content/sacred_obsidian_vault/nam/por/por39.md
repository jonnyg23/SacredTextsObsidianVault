Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SONGS FROM THE NORTHWEST COAST</h3>  <h4 align="center">PRAYERS OF THE TSIMSHIAN TO NEXNÓA</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Nexnóx, Nexnóx!<br> Chief, Chief!<br>     have pity upon us!<br>     else there will be nobody<br>     to smoke under you!<br> Nexnóx, have pity upon us!</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>FOR FAIR WEATHER</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Nexnóx, Nexnóx!<br> Chief, Chief!<br>     have pity upon us!<br> Look down and see<br>     what those under you<br>     whom you made are doing!<br> Pull up your foot<br>     and sweep off your face! <a name="fr_4"></a><a href="#fn_4"><font size="1">*</font></a></div>  
 <p align="center"><b>FOR CALM WEATHER</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Hold in your breath, Chief,<br>     that it may be calm.</div>  
 <p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_4"></a><a href="por39.htm#fr_4">135:*</a> "pull up your foot" means "stop the rain"; "sweep off your face" means "take away the clouds."</p>
