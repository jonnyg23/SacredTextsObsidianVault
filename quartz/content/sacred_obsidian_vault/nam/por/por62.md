 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">NEITHER SPIRIT NOR BIRD</h4>  
 <p align="center"><b>SHOSHONE LOVE SONG</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Neither spirit nor bird;<br> That was my flute you heard<br> Last night by the River.<br> When you came with your wicker jar<br> Where the river drags the willows,<br> That was my flute you heard,<br> Wacoba, Wacoba,<br> Calling, Come to the willows!</div>  
 <p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="green">p. 186</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Neither the wind nor a bird<br> Rustled the lupin blooms,<br> That was my blood you heard<br> Answer your garment's hem<br> Whispering through the grasses;<br> That was my blood you heard<br> By the wild rose under the willows.  
 <p>That was no beast that stirred,<br> That was my heart you heard<br> Pacing to and fro<br> In the ambush of my desire,<br> To the music my flute let fall.<br> Wacoba, Wacoba,<br> That was my heart you heard<br> Leaping under the willows.</p></div>
