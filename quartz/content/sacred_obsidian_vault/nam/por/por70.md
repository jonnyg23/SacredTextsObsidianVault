 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">EARTH-MOTHER</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">O, ho, yo,<br> O, ho, yo,<br> To thee, my life,<br> To thee, my wife,<br> To thee, my mother—  
 <p>Who are these the many-feeding?<br> Heaps of horns,<br> Hills of fur—<br> To thee, I come to partake of food,<br> My mother,<br> O, ho—</p>  
 <p>So say’th the spring,<br> So say’th the wing,<br> So say’th my mother—</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="green">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Thus, it was I heard the feet beat—<br> My ear down,<br> On the ground—<br> Yea, I put my lips to thee and drank song,<br> My mother,<br> O, ho—  
 <p>So cries the tree,<br> So cries the sea,<br> So cries my mother—</p>  
 <p>There, the word was overheard,<br> By the cactus<br> Standing lone—<br> Yea, by the cypress thou wilt teach me,<br> My mother,<br> O, ho—</p>  
 <p>So walks the light,<br> So walks the night,<br> So walks my mother—</p>  
 <p>Even unto the long black shadows,<br> Goeth the frail thing,<br> Evening star—<br> Whither thou goeth and thy smile go I,<br> My mother.<br> O, ho—</p>  
 <p>So broods the dark,<br> So broods the bark,<br> So broods my mother—</p>  
 <p>Beneath the bark-blanket were the little men—<br> The six-legged,<br> Painted black and red—</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="green">p. 198</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Unto thee will I hide me to sleep,<br> My mother,<br> O, ho—  
 <p>To thee, O breath,<br> To thee, O death,<br> To thee, my mother—</p>  
 <p>I saw it, yea in a dream, I saw it—<br> My soul<br> Arise from sleep—<br> In the morning, thou wilt call me,<br> My mother.</p>  
 <p>O, ho, yo,<br> O, ho, yo,<br> To thee, O—<br> Yo.</p></div>
