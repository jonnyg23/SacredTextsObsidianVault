Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SONGS IN THE GARDEN OF THE HOUSE GOD</h3>  <h4 align="center">NAVAJO</h4>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Truly in the East<br> The white bean<br> And the great corn-plant<br> Are tied with the white lightning.<br> Listen! rain approaches!<br> The voice of the bluebird is heard.<br> Truly in the East<br> The white bean<br> And the great squash<br> Are tied with the rainbow,</div>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Listen! rain approaches!<br> The voice of the bluebird is heard.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>II</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">From the top of the great corn-plant the water gurgles, I hear it;<br> Around the roots the water foams, I hear it;<br> Around the roots of the plants it foams, I hear it;<br> From their tops the water foams, I hear it.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>III</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The corn grows up. The waters of the dark clouds drop, drop.<br> The rain descends. The waters from the corn leaves drop, drop.<br> The rain descends. The waters from the plants drop, drop.<br> The corn grows up. The waters of the dark mists drop, drop.</div>  
 <p align="center"><b>IV</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Shall I cull this fruit of the great corn-plant?<br> Shall you break it? Shall I break it?<br> Shall I break it? Shall you break it?<br> Shall I? Shall you?<br> Shall I cull this fruit of the great squash vine?<br> Shall you pick it up? Shall I pick it up?<br> Shall I pick it up? Shall you pick it up?<br> Shall I? Shall you?</div>
