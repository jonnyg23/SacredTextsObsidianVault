 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SEDNOR AND THE FULMAR</h3>  <h4 align="center">AN ESKIMO BALLAD</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(Recitative)<br> Where is she<br>     who would never marry?<br> In a kayok to the mainland<br>     going away.</div></i></p>  
 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="green">p. 163</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Dost thou see, my eyes,<br>     dost thou see them?<br> Ia, ha, ha, ha, ha!<br> To a tent of ragged skins,<br>     he has brought her, crying;<br> <i>    (the Fulmar, her husband)<br> </i>Her father with her elder brother<br>     in a boat coming;<br> <i>    (he, seeking his daughter)<br> </i>In a boat his daughter embarked.<br> Her husband, the Fulmar, cried:<br> <i>    (thus the Magician!)<br> </i>The Fulmar says: "My means for transforming<br>     let me see them as they are;<br>     let me see them once more."<br> Now they are taking the woman back;<br>     to the tent going home;<br>     the Fulmar followed,<br> <i>    (thus the Magician!)<br> </i>Wind very strong to come near them;<br> <i>    (he made, the Magician!)<br> </i>    they were shipwrecked, nearly.<br> <i>    (Her father fears death!)<br> </i>His daughter he pushes<br>     into the sea.<br> To the boat on both sides—<br>     to the boat she clings.<br> <i>    (Still followed, the Fulmar!)<br> </i>With a knife he struck her;<br>     whales emerged.<br> Again he struck her;<br>     a thong seal emerged.<br> Again he struck her;<br>     a fiord seal emerged.<br> <i>    (thus the Magician!)<br> </i>Her whole body she leaned.<br> <i>    (Sednor, fearing death!)</i></div>  
 <p><a name="page_164"><font size="1" color="green">p. 164</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">With a knife into the eyes<br>     he stabbed her;<br> He killed her.<br> <i>    (Thus the Fulmar, the Magician!)</i><br> On the shore<br>     her father lifted her.<br> A quilt he took;<br>     on the beach laid her down.<br> With a dog skin she was covered,<br> <i>    (Sednor, the Beautiful)</i><br> The flood-tide took her.  
 <p><i>(Based on a literal translation.)</i></p></div>
