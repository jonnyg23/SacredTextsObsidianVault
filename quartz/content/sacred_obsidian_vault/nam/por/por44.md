 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Path on the Rainbow</i>, edited by George W. Cronyn, [1918], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SONG OF QAQATCGUK</h3>  
 <p align="center"><b>TLINGIT</b></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The man who thought he had perished<br>     dreams thus about himself:<br>     I keep feeling<br>     as if I had gotten home.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="green">p. 144</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF WOMAN'S PREMONITION</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Already I am going,<br>     I am going to die.<br> I have dreamed of my son.</div>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF RAVEN</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">That Raven must have been a great fellow.<br> He went down under the sea.<br> Then they pulled up his nose.<br> He went through the town for his nose.<br> When it was given him he started to fly out of doors.<br> He flew out with it.<br> Why! instead of looking like himself, he looks as if he ought<br>     to have a drink.<br> After you have done as he has done you can wander about the<br>     entire world-beach.</div>  <h4 align="center">CRADLE SONG FOR A GIRL</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">If I do not take anything to the party<br>     I shall be ashamed,<br>     I shall be ashamed.<br> Little girls, listen.<br> Little girls, listen.</div>  <h4 align="center">CRADLE SONG</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Let me shoot a small bird<br>     for my younger brother.<br> Let me spear a small trout<br>     for my younger sister.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="green">p. 145</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF LITTLE-LAKE-UP-ABOVE</h4>  
 <p align="center"><i>(When his people expected others to come with food to give them a feast)</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">It is before my face every day.<br> And when I sleep I always think of you.<br> I long much for you.<br> Thinking about you comes to me<br>     like a sudden sickness.</div>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF SORROW</h4>  
 <p align="center"><i>(By a woman whose brothers were drowned and their bodies not recovered)</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Your reef has beaten me,<br>     Kágwantan's children.<br> But take pity on me.<br> I wonder what I always attend to<br>     when I wake up in the morning.<br> Sometime I might see my brothers.</div>  <h4 align="center">COMPOSED BY SMALL-LAKE-UNDERNEATH</h4>  
 <p align="center"><i>(About a drifting log found full of nails, out of which a house was built. Sung when a feast is about to be given for a dead man)</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I always compare you to a drifting log<br>     with iron nails in it.<br> Let my brother float in,<br>     in that way.<br> Let him float ashore<br>     on a good sandy beach.<br> I always compare you, my mother,<br>     to the sun passing behind the clouds.<br> That is what makes the world dark.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="green">p. 146</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">KAGWANTAN SONG FOR THE FEAST</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(When a slave is to be killed)<br> The words of people<br>     now snow me under,<br>     the words of worthless people.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">SUNG BY THE WOLF FAMILIES</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(Coming to a feast)<br> A rich man is coming.<br> Your feelings you keep silent.<br> When it is ended,<br>     thus they always say:<br>     "It is all gone."</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">AT A GREAT FEAST</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">We are also going to be invited<br>     to Killisnoo.<br> High-cast people<br>     are going to eat.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY NAQUALI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I wonder what my future life<br>     will do to me.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_147"><font size="1" color="green">p. 147</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF FOR-A-TOWN SPIRIT</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(About the Táqdentan, because when the latter came to Juneau to drink they did not pay any attention to the Auk people.)<br> I observe how people are treated<br>     after they are dead,<br>     and therefore I drink<br>     before I die.<br> What you did was very selfish,<br>     Táqdentan's children.<br> But I do not blame you<br>     for your words.<br> It is this Raven's fault.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">BY NIGOT</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">What do you think I live for?<br> I live to drink whiskey.<br> Have pity on me,<br>     foam children.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY TSAKAK</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">It is only crying about myself<br>     that comes to me in song.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY ONE OF THE LENÉDI</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(About Juneau when gold was first found there)<br> Do not talk any more,<br>     Lenédi's children.<br> You are ahead<br>     of all the people in the world.</div></i></p>  
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="green">p. 148</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">AN ANGRY SONG COMPOSED BY SEXDAGWET</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(Against Little Raven, a blind man of Tongas)<br> Just as if a man chased him<br>     out on the beach<br>     because of someone's talking,<br>     Little Raven threw himself<br>     before my words.<br> I do not feel even a little numb.<br> That fellow, Little Raven,<br>     whose words<br>     they are always reporting to me,<br>     can not see anything.  
 <p><i>(On the same subject)<br> </i>Little Raven,<br>     I hate what you keep saying,<br>     because you are a slave's son<br>     and can see nothing.<br> I hate to have you talk to me<br>     because you have spots all over your face<br>     like a big sea cucumber<br>     and look like a slave.<br> Don't you know that,<br>     because you can not see anything,<br>     you big slave's son,<br>     you keep picking up sand<br>     instead of dipping into the dish?</p></div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">BY DEAD-SLAVE</h4>  
 <p><i>(About a woman named Poor-orphan, who was a very poor girl, but afterward became the richest woman in Wrangell)<br> </i></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I used to make fun<br>     of this poor little girl at Wrangell<br>     when she was very small.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="green">p. 149</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">BY A SHAMAN OF THE KÁGWANTAN NAMED KAGANK</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">It is only on account of Strong Drink<br>     that you pity me.<br> Why don't you also love me?</div>  <h4 align="center">BY UNDER-A-BLANKET</h4>  
 <p align="center"><i>(About the son of Luknaxádi, whose brother was killed in compensation for the killing of her brother)</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">His mind is just like mine,<br>     Lubnaxádi's children.<br> So that I am beginning to love him.<br> I wonder what I always look for<br>     when I wake up in the morning.<br> Sometime I might see my brothers.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY MAN-THAT-IS-NOT-ALL-RIGHT</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(About Princess Thom)<br> Even from a house of Strong Drink<br> men get away,<br> but not from you,<br> Raven woman.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">BY AMONG-THE-BRANT</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(When his wife had been taken from him)<br> Like one who desires Strong Drink,<br>     I never sleep,<br>     Toqyédi's children.</div></i></p>  
 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="green">p. 150</font></a></p>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(On the same subject)<br> My own mind is very hard to me.<br> It is just as if<br>     I were carrying my mind around.<br> What is the matter with you?</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">BY OTHER-WATER</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(Over a dead man)<br> My younger brother has brought me<br>     great joy of laughter.<br> If I knew the way they go,<br>     I would go right to him.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">BY JOINED-TOGETHER</h4>  
 <p align="center"><i>(When all his friends went down the rapids at Gonaxó and were drowned)</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I always look expectantly<br>     to see some one<br>     stand up in front of the town<br>     and in the bay.<br> I always compare my brothers<br>     to the people the duck tribe saved.<br> They went right down under the earth<br>     like those high-cast people.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY HERE-IS-A-FEATHER</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(When his brother died)<br> It is as if my grandfather's house<br>     were turning over with me.<br> Where is the person who will save me?</div></i></p>  
 <p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="green">p. 151</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">BY MAN-FOR-HIMSELF</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I love you from my heart,<br>     Tsaguédi's children.<br> You are the only one<br>     I will die with.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY CGWATC</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(About an uncle who had died)<br> I always think within myself<br>     that there is no place<br>     where people do not die.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">MOURNING SONG OF SAKWET</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(About her brother who was drowned)<br> I am like the people<br>     who were killed<br>     by the south wind.</div></i></p>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(On the same subject)<br> Perhaps my brother went<br>     into the sun's trail<br>     so that I can never see him again.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">PEACE-SONG AFTER A GREAT WAR</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">If you had died,<br>     Kágwantan's children,<br>     I would have cut off my hair for you<br> I love you so much<br>     I would have blackened my face for you,<br>     Kágwantan's children.</div>  
 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="green">p. 152</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">SONG OF A HUMMING-BIRD DEER</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I am feeling very lonely away.<br> I am going to my uncle's town.<br> I am singing inside, my masters.<br> I am crying about myself.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY LQENA</h4>  
 <p align="center"><i>(When he was the only one of his people saved and his enemies wanted to make peace with him. He danced as a deer, singing this song and at the end of it cut in two the man standing next to him)</i></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I did this way regarding myself.<br> I would not let what my conscience<br>     said to me, pass.<br> Before his death I saw his ghost.<br> At once he stabbed<br>     and killed Cadasíktc.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY QAUCTE</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(Who married a Téqoedi woman)<br> After you have been drinking<br>     you had better stop talking<br>     about how well you were brought up,<br> Téqoedi's children.<br> What one of you thinks about it.<br>     when he is sober?</div></i></p>  
 <p><a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="green">p. 153</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">LOVE SONG OF A TAGISH WOMAN</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Why have I come to you<br>     to Dyea from far inland<br>     only to find<br>     that you have gone away?<br> Here I am,<br>     crying for you.</div>  <h4 align="center">LOVE-SONG OF SIQUET, THE DANCER</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I wonder what this coming July morning<br>     will be like.<br> My mind is very weak<br>     thinking that I shall be unable<br>     to see my sweetheart.</div>  <h4 align="center">BY RAVEN-SKIN</h4>  
 <p><i><div style="margin-left: 32px">(When his sweetheart abandoned him)<br> If one had control of death<br>     it would be very easy<br>     to die with a Wolf woman.<br> It would be very pleasant.</div></i></p>  <h4 align="center">MOURNING-SONG OF THE KÁGWANTAN</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">It is his own fault<br>     that this man of the Wolf people died.<br> Do not lay the blame on anyone else.</div>
