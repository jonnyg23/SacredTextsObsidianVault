 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Cosmic Consciousness</i>, by Richard Maurice Bucke, [1901], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER 14.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><i>Alexander Pushkin</i>.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Born</span> May 26, 1799; died January 29, 1837.</p>  
 <p>If Pushkin traced the following lines after his own personal experience he was almost certainly a case of Cosmic Consciousness. Be this as it may, their descriptive power makes them</p>  
 <p><a name="page_290"><font size="1" color="green">p. 290</font></a></p>  
 <p>worth quoting. The translation into English is by Dana, of the "New York Sun":</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px"><div style="margin-left: 32px;font-size:90%;color: blue;">Tormented by thirst of the spirit,<br> I was dragging myself through a gloomy forest,<a href="cc37.htm#an_290-1">*</a><br> When a six-winged seraph<a href="cc37.htm#an_290-2">†</a><br> At the cross-roads appeared to me.<br> With fingers light as a dream<br> My eyes he touched,<br> And my eyes opened wide,<br> Like those of a frightened she eagle.<br> My ears he touched,<br> And roaring and noise filled them;<br> And I heard the trembling of the heavens;<br> And the high flight of the angels,<br> And the movement of the creatures beneath the sea,<br> And the growing of the grass in the valleys!<br> And he laid hold of my lips,<br> And tore out my sinful tongue—<br> Sinful, frivolous and cunning;<br> And the sting of a wise serpent,<br> Between my unconscious lips,<br> With bloody right hand he planted.<br> And he cut through my breast with a sword,<br> And took out the trembling heart,<br> And a coal blazing and flaming,<br> Into the open breast he thrust.<br> Like a corpse I lay in the desert,<a href="cc37.htm#an_290-3">*</a><br> And the voice of God called me:<br> Rise up, Prophet, and see, and understand!<a href="cc37.htm#an_290-4">†</a><br> Filled full of My Will,<br> Going forth over sea and land,<br> Set men's hearts afire with the Word.</div></div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 64px;font-size:80%;color: purple;"><a name="an_290-1">*</a> Dante's dark forest in which he was lost.  
 <p><a name="an_290-2">†</a> Compare Isaiah's vision.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_290-3">*</a> The dazed condition which is so common following illumination.</p>  
 <p><a name="an_290-4">†</a> Intellectual illumination.</p></div>
