 Part life-coach, part Machiavelli, part Yoda, Balthasar Gracian [1601-1658],
 a Jesuit priest, wrote this collection of pithy sayings
 four centuries ago.
 Gracian speaks to the twenty-first century as well as the seventeenth.
 It's only a matter of time before someone markets Gracian's
 life advice to busy executives,
 like <cite>Sun Tzu</cite> or the <cite>Book of Five Rings</cite>
 (if it hasn't been already).
 In the meantime, Gracian can be our little secret.
 <p>
 Jacobs, the translator, is also the author of many books of
 folklore, etexts of which can also be found at this site, for instance, <a href="../../neu/celt/cft/index.htm">Celtic Fairy Tales</a>,
 <a href="../../neu/eng/eft/index.htm">English Fairy Tales</a>,
 and <a href="../../hin/ift/index.htm">Indian Fairy Tales</a>.
 </p>
 <p>
 Production note: I have arbitrarily divided the body of the translation
 into files of 50 sayings each:
 Jacobs recommended reading the book 50 sayings at a time.
 </p>
