Sayce, [1890], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">XI.—Letter of Dusratta to Amenophis IV</span> <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">1</font></a></h4>  
 <p align="center"><small>OBVERSE</small></p>  
 <p>1. To Napkhurruriya the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>,<br> 2. my brother, my son-in-law, who loves [me],<br> 3. and (whom) I love, it is said<br> 4. as follows: Dusratta, king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mittani</span>,<br> 5. thy father-in-law, who loves thee, speaks thus:<br> 6. Unto myself (is) peace; unto thee may there be peace,<br> 7. unto thy houses, Teie thy mother, and the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>,<br> 8. to Tâdukhêpa my daughter, thy wife,<br> 9. to the rest of thy wives, to thy sons, to thy soldiers<br> 10. and thy chariots, to thy horses, to thy men,<br> 11. to thy country and to all that thou hast, may there be very abundant peace!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="green">p. 90</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Pirizzi and Pupri my ambassadors<br> 13. I have sent to my brother to explain, and<br> 14. have addressed them with great trouble and earnestness,<br> 15. and I have sent them <i>in a body</i> (?) <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">1</font></a>;<br> 16. and this speech beforehand<br> 17. I make to my brother: Manê, thy ambassador<br> 18. I detain, and Umeatu my messenger. … <br> 19. I will dismiss, and the prophet <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">2</font></a> shall go [to thee].</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>20. And now my brother to [their own land?]<br> 21. has not permitted them to go, but has detained them<br> 22. overmuch. Wherefore has he not<br> 23. protected the ambassadors? They have fled, and (there is) guilt<br> 24. on my brother in respect of the ambassadors. Why is his heart<br> 25. [angered]? Why has he <i>spoken</i> (?) before the face of …<br> 26 [Pup]ru has not returned and he has <i>spoken</i> (?).<br> 27. … his offers of alliance he does not listen to.<br> 28. [Yet I,] O son-in-law, (am) verily thy father-in-law.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Lacuna</i>.</p>  
 <p>Last paragraph:—</p>  
 <p>1. And as regards the frequent intercourse which with thy father<br> 2. I had Teie thy mother knows the facts;<br> 3. no one else knows the facts;<br> 4. but after Teie thy mother thou knowest them, and<br> 5. what he said to thee. As thy father with me<br> 6. was friendly, so now, O my brother again<br> 7. with me thou art friendly, and what (is) contrary thereto no one,<br> 8. O my brother, listens to.</p>
