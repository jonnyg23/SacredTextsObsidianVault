 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="green">p. 73</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">VII.—Letters from Dusratta, King of Mitanni, to Amenophis III <a name="fr_304"></a><a href="#fn_303"><font size="1">1</font></a></span></h4>  <h5 align="center">No. 1</h5>  
 <p>1. To Nimmuriya the great king,<br> 2. the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>, my brother,<br> 3. my son-in-law, whom I love<br> 4. and who loves me, speak<br> 5. thus: Dusratta, the great king,<br> 6. the king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mitanni</span>, thy brother, thy father-in-law,<br> 7. and who loves thee;<br> 8. unto me is peace, unto my brother and unto my son-in-law<br> 9. may there be peace; to thy houses, to thy wives,<br> 10. to thy sons, <a name="fr_305"></a><a href="#fn_304"><font size="1">2</font></a> to thy men, to thy chariots,<br> 11. to thy horses, to thy country<br> 12. and to thy property, may there be abundant peace!</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>13. Of my brother, whom I love,<br> 14. the wife, my daughter, I deliver to him:<br> 15. may the Sun-god and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Istar</span> march before [my] brother:<br> 16. according to the heart of my brother may they [act]:<br> 17. and may my brother on this [same] day<br> 18. rejoice: may the Sun-god and [<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Istar</span>]<br> 19. [hear] the prayer of my brother:<br> 20. abundant joy [to my brother]<br> 21. may they give, may [they …]:<br> 22. and may my brother [live?]<br> 23. for ever in pea[ce]!</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>24. Manê, the messenger of [my brother],<br> 25. and Khanê, the dragoman <a name="fr_306"></a><a href="#fn_305"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. of my brother, like a god <a name="fr_307"></a><a href="#fn_306"><font size="1">1</font></a> thou didst [send],<br> 27. many presents didst thou give [them],<br> 28. thou didst [honour] them greatly<br> 29. on account of their letter, counting on their service (?) <a name="fr_308"></a><a href="#fn_307"><font size="1">2</font></a>;<br> 30. the men who really live<br> 31. if at any time I see not, may my gods<br> 32. and the gods of my brother protect them.</p>  
 <p align="center">_______________</p>  
 <p>33. Now Nakhramassi,<br> 34. whom thou hast seen, to transact (business) with my brother<br> 35. I send, and also one necklace<br> 36. of crystal and alabaster<br> 37. and some gold for a present<br> 38. to my brother I have despatched,<br> 39. and for 100 thousand years<br> 40. for the <i>service</i> (?) of my brother<br> 41. may it be used. <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
