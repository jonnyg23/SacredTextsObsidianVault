 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_213"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_214">103:4</a> This seems to be the meaning of <i>sabartum</i> in K 1999, i. 15.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_214"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_215">105:1</a> That is, Lake Van.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_215"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_216">105:2</a> Amadana was the district about Amida or Diarbekir. Assur-natsir-pal reached Amadana after leaving Adana, a district of Qurkhi.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_216"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_217">105:3</a> Compare the names of the Gamgumian and Melitenian princes Tarkhu-lara and Tarkhu-nazi, and of the Hittite city Tarkhi-gamas mentioned by the Vannic king Menuas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_217"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_218">106:1</a> Nimme, according to Assur-natsir-pal, adjoined Alzi and Dayaeni in the neighbourhood of Mush.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_218"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_219">106:2</a> This must be the Dhunibun of Shalmaneser II, eastward of the sources of the Tigris, on the river of Mush (the modern Kara Su).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_219"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_220">106:3</a> In the Vannic language the termination <i>ni</i>(<i>s</i>) denoted "belonging to," and <i>barzini</i> or <i>barzani</i> signified "a chapel."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_220"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_221">106:4</a> The Vannic king calls the district in which Palu stands "the land of Puterias."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_221"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_222">106:5</a> Perhaps the Abunis of the Vannic inscriptions.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_222"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_223">106:6</a> Dayaeni was on the northern bank of the Arsanias, to the north of Mush. It is called the kingdom of "the son of Diaus" in the Vannic texts, which define it more closely as situated on the Murad Chai, near Melazgherd.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_223"></a><a href="rp20125.htm#fr_224">106:7</a> The land of Nairi or "the rivers" denoted in the age of Tiglath-Pileser I. the districts at the sources of the Tigris and the Euphrates. In the time of Assur-natsir-pal and his successors, on the other hand, it was the country between Lake Van and the northern frontier of Assyria, and consequently lay to the south-west of the Nairi of the time of Tiglath-Pileser I. It will be noticed that there was as yet no kingdom of Ararat or Van.</p>
