 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="green">p. 143</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Fifth Tablet of the Story of the Creation</span></h4>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Obverse</span></p>  
 <p>1. He prepared the twin mansions of the great gods.<br> 2. He fixed the stars, even the twin-stars, <a name="fr_288"></a><a href="#fn_286"><font size="1">1</font></a> to correspond with them.<br> 3. He ordained the year, appointing the signs of the Zodiac <a name="fr_289"></a><a href="#fn_287"><font size="1">2</font></a> over (it).<br> 4. For each of the twelve months he fixed three stars,<br> 5. from the day when the year issues forth to the close.<br> 6. He founded the mansion of (the Sun-god) the god of the ferry-boat, that they might know their bonds,<br> 7. that they might not err, that they might not go astray in any way.<br> 8. He established the mansion of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bel</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ea</span> along with himself.<br> 9. Moreover he opened the great gates on either side,<br> 10. he strengthened the bolts on the left hand and on the right,<br> 11. and in the midst of it he made a staircase.<br> 12. He illuminated the Moon-god that he might be porter of the night,<br> 13. and ordained for him the ending of the night that the day may be known,<br> 14. (saying:) "Month by month, without break, keep watch in thy disk.<br> 15. At the beginning of the month light up the night,<br> 16. announcing thy horns that the heaven may know.<br> 17. On the seventh day, (filling thy) disk<br> 18. thou shalt open indeed (its) narrow contraction.<br> 19. At that time the sun (will be) on the horizon of heaven at thy (rising).<br> 20. Thou shalt cut off its …</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="green">p. 144</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. (Thereafter) towards the path of the sun thou shalt approach.<br> 22. (Then) the contracted size of the sun shall indeed change (?) <a name="fr_290"></a><a href="#fn_288"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> 23. … seeking its path.<br> 24. … descend and pronounce judgment.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>The rest of the obverse and the first three lines of the reverse are destroyed</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Reverse</span></p>  
 <p>4. [<i>First line of the next tablet:</i>] When the assembly of the gods had heard him.<br> 5. Fifth tablet of the (series beginning) "When on high."<br> 6. The property of Assur-bani-pal the king of hosts, the king of Assyria.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_286"></a><a href="rp20134.htm#fr_288">143:1</a> <i>Lu-masi</i>, literally "the twin oxen," of which seven were reckoned.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_287"></a><a href="rp20134.htm#fr_289">143:2</a> <i>Mizrâta</i>, which is the same word as the <i>mazzarôth</i> of Job xxxviii. 32.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_288"></a><a href="rp20134.htm#fr_290">144:1</a> The mutilated condition of the tablet makes the translation of this line extremely doubtful. There may be a reference in it to the star Al-tar or Dapinu.</p>
