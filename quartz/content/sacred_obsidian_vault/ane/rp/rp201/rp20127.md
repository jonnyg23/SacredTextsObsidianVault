 <p align="center"><small>COLUMN VI</small></p>  
 <p>1. With their mighty armies<br> 2. in the city and the mountains I contended furiously.<br> 3. A destruction of them I made.<br> 4. Their fighting men in the midst of the mountains<br> 5. like a moon-stone I flung down. Their heads<br> 6. like (that) of a sheep I cut off.<br> 7. Their corpses over the valleys and high places of the mountains<br> 8. I spread. The city itself I captured.<br> 9. Their gods I carried away. Their goods (and) their property<br> 10. I brought out. The city with fire I burned.<br> 11. Three of their great fortresses, which of brickwork<br> 12. were constructed, and the circuit of the city itself<br> 13. I threw down (and) dug up; to mounds and ruins<br> 14. I reduced (them), and <i>salt</i> (?) on the top of them<br> 15. I sowed. A plate of bronze I made;<br> 16. the conquest of the lands, which through <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span> my god (and) my lord<br> 17. I had conquered, that the site of this city should not (again) be taken,<br> 18. nor its wall be constructed, upon (it)<br> 19. I wrote. A house of brick on the top of it<br> 20. I built: these plates of bronze<br> 21. in the midst (of it) I placed.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>22. In the service of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span> my lord my chariots<br> 23. and soldiers I took. The city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kipsuna</span><br> 24. their royal city I besieged. The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Qumanians</span><br> 25. feared the mighty, onset of my battle;<br> 26. my feet they took; their lives I spared.<br> 27. Its great wall and its gate-posts</p>  
 <p><a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="green">p. 112</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. of bricks I ordered to be destroyed, and<br> 29. from their foundations to their coping<br> 30. they were thrown down and turned into a mound;<br> 3r. and 300 families of evil-doers<br> 32. who (were) within it, who were not submissive to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span> my lord,<br> 33. were removed (out of it). I received them. Their hostages<br> 34. I took. Tribute and offering<br> 35. above what was previously paid upon them<br> 36. I imposed, and the widespread land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Qumanî</span><br> 37. throughout its circuit under my feet<br> 38. I subdued.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>39. In all, 42 countries and their kings<br> 40. from the fords of the lower <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zab</span><br> 41. (and) the border of the distant mountains<br> 42. to the fords of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>,<br> 43. the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span> (<i>Khattê</i>) and the Upper Sea<br> 44. of the setting sun, <a name="fr_235"></a><a href="#fn_234"><font size="1">1</font></a> from the beginning of my sovereignty<br> 45. until my fifth year my hand has conquered.<br> 46. One word in unison have I made them utter.<br> 47. Their hostages have I taken. Tribute<br> 48. and offering have I imposed upon them.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>49. I left the numerous roads of foreign peoples<br> 50. which were not attached to my empire:<br> 51. where the ground was favourable in my chariots, and where it was difficult<br> 52. on my feet, after them<br> 53. I marched. The feet of the enemy<br> 54. I kept from my land.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>55. Tiglath-pileser the valiant hero,<br> 56. the holder of the sceptre unrivalled<br> 57. who completes the mission of the supreme (gods).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="green">p. 113</font></a></p>  
 <p>58. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Uras</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nergal</span> have given their forceful<br> 59. weapons and their supreme bow<br> 60. to the hands of my lordship.<br> 61. Under the protection of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Uras</span> who loves me<br> 62. from young wild bulls, powerful (and) large,<br> 63. in the desert in the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mitâni</span><br> 64. and in the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Arazigi</span>, <a name="fr_236"></a><a href="#fn_235"><font size="1">1</font></a> which (is) in front<br> 65. of the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span>, with my mighty bow,<br> 66. a lasso of iron and my pointed<br> 67. spear, their lives I ended:<br> 68. their hides (and) their horns<br> 69. to my city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span> I brought.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>70. Ten powerful male-elephants <a name="fr_237"></a><a href="#fn_236"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 71. in the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Harran</span> (<i>Kharrani</i>) and (on) the bank of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khabur</span><br> 72. I slew. Four elephants alive<br> 73. I captured. Their hides<br> 74. (and) their teeth along with the live<br> 75. elephants I brought to my city <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span>.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>76. Under the protection of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Uras</span> who loves me<br> 77. 120 lions, with my stout heart,<br> 78. in the conflict of my heroism<br> 79. on my feet I slew;<br> 80. and 800 lions in my chariot<br> 81. with javelins (?) I slaughtered.<br> 82. All the cattle of the field and the birds of heaven<br> 83. that fly, among my rarities <a name="fr_238"></a><a href="#fn_237"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> 84. I placed.</p>  
 <p align="center">_________</p>  
 <p>85. After that the enemies of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span> throughout their territories</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_114"><font size="1" color="green">p. 114</font></a></p>  
 <p>86. I had conquered, the temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Istar</span> of (the city) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span><br> 87. my lady, the temple of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Rimmon</span>, <a name="fr_239"></a><a href="#fn_238"><font size="1">1</font></a> (and) the temple of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Older Bel</span>, <a name="fr_240"></a><a href="#fn_239"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> 88. the temple of the Divinities, <a name="fr_241"></a><a href="#fn_240"><font size="1">3</font></a> the temples of the gods<br> 89. of my city <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asur</span>, which were decayed, I built,<br> 90. I completed. The entrances of their temples<br> 91. I constructed. The great gods, my lords,<br> 92. I introduced within;<br> 93. I rejoiced the heart of their great divinity.<br> 94. The palaces, the seat of sovereignty<br> 95. belonging to the great fortresses<br> 96. on the borders of my country, which from<br> 97. the time of my fathers through long<br> 98. years had been deserted and ruined and<br> 99. were destroyed, I built (and) completed.<br> 100. The castles of my country that were overthrown<br> 101. I enclosed. The conduits <a name="fr_242"></a><a href="#fn_241"><font size="1">4</font></a> throughout all the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyria</span><br> 102. I fastened together wholly, and an accumulation<br> 103. of grain in addition to that (collected) by my fathers<br> 104. I brought back (and) heaped up.<br> 105. Troops of horses, oxen (and) asses</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_234"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_235">112:1</a> That is, Lake Van.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_235"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_236">113:1</a> Arazig is the Eragiza of Ptolemy, on the Euphrates, to the north of Balis and the south of Carchemish. Mitâni seems to be the Matenau of the Egyptians mentioned by Ramses III immediately before Carchemish.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_236"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_237">113:2</a> I follow Lotz in this rendering.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_237"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_238">113:3</a> <i>Ni’siggi</i>, borrowed from the Sumerian <i>nin-’sig</i>, "secret."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_238"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_239">114:1</a> Here called Mâtu, "the god of the tempest."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_239"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_240">114:2</a> Bel of Nipur, called Mul-lil, "the lord of the ghost-world," by the Accadians, and distinguished from Bel Merodach, the younger Bel of Babylon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_240"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_241">114:3</a> This apparently means that the images of several deities were collected together in the temple of the Older Bel.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_241"></a><a href="rp20127.htm#fr_242">114:4</a> Literally "sewers."</p>
