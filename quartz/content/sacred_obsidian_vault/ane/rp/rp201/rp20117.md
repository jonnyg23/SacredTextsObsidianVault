Sayce, [1888], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center"><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a></h4>  <h4 align="center">V. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Inscription of En-anna-tumma on a Buttress</span> <a name="fr_155"></a><a href="#fn_154"><font size="1">1</font></a></h4>  
 <p>1. For the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span>,<br> 2. the warrior of the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ellilla</span>,<br> 3. En-anna-tumma,<br> 4. the patesi<br> 5. Of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shirpurla-ki</span>,<br> 6. the chosen of the heart<br> 7. of the goddess <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ninâ</span>,<br> 8. the great patesi<br> 9. of the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span>,<br> 10. the son of Entena<br> 11. the patesi<br> 12. of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shirpurla-ki</span>.<br> 13. For the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span><br> 14. his house of fruits<br> 15. he has restored.<br> 16. Of En-anna-tumma,<br> 17. who the house of fruits<br> 18. of the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nin-girsu</span><br> 19. has restored,<br> 20. his god<br> 21. is the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dun-sir(?)-anna</span>.</p>
