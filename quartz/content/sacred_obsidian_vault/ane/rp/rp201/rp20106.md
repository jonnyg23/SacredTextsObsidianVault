 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="green">p. 13</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">No. I.—TRANSLATION OF THE FIRST DYNASTIC TABLET FROM BABYLON</h4>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Obverse</span></p>  
 <p>1. ’Sumu-abi, the king: 15 years.</p>  
 <p>2. ’Sumu-la-ilu, the son of the same: 35 years.</p>  
 <p>3. Zabû, the son of the same: 14 years.</p>  
 <p>4. Abil-Sin, the son of the same: 18 years.</p>  
 <p>5. Sin-muballidh, the son of the same: 30 years.</p>  
 <p>6. Khammu-ragas, <a name="fr_5"></a><a href="#fn_5"><font size="1">1</font></a> the son of the same: 55 years.</p>  
 <p>7. ’Sam’su-iluna, <a name="fr_6"></a><a href="#fn_6"><font size="1">2</font></a> the son of the same: 35 years.</p>  
 <p>8. Ebisum, <a name="fr_7"></a><a href="#fn_7"><font size="1">3</font></a> the son of the same: 25 years.</p>  
 <p>9. Ammi-satana, the son of the same: 25 years.</p>  
 <p>10. Ammi-sadugga, <a name="fr_8"></a><a href="#fn_8"><font size="1">4</font></a> the son of the same: 21 years.</p>  
 <p>11. ’Sam’su-satana (?), the son of the same: 31 years.</p>  
 <p>12. 11 kings of the dynasty of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span>.</p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Reverse</span></p>  
 <p>1. (The dynasty of) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Uru-azagga</span>. <a name="fr_9"></a><a href="#fn_9"><font size="1">5</font></a> Anman the king.</p>

 <p>3. Damki-ili-su. <a name="fr_11"></a><a href="#fn_11"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_5"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_5">13:1</a> The first five names of the dynasty are Semitic. Khammuragas is Kassite or Kossæan, and is interpreted "of a large family." Sin-muballidh may have married a foreign wife.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_6"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_6">13:2</a> "The Sun-god (is) our god," another Semitic name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_7"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_7">13:3</a> "The doer," also Semitic.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_8"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_8">13:4</a> Kassite, interpreted "the family is established."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_9"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_9">13:5</a> Uru-azagga is now represented by a part of the mounds of Telloh (the ancient Sirpurla) or its immediate vicinity.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_10"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_10">13:6</a> Nigas was an Elamite word.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_11"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_11">13:7</a> Semitic, signifying "gracious is his god."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_12"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_12">14:1</a> Perhaps to be read in Semitic Sapin-mat-nukurti, "the sweeper away of the land of the foe." The name seems to have been a title.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_13"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_13">14:2</a> Perhaps the Semitic <i>sussu</i>, "sixty."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_14"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_14">14:3</a> In Semitic Muabbid-kissati, "the destroyer of hosts."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_15"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_15">14:4</a> Apparently, therefore, the son of the preceding king.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_16"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_16">14:5</a> Rendered by the Semitic Abil-Bel-u’sum-same, "the son of Bel (the lord) of the treasury of heaven."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_17"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_17">14:6</a> "The glory of the world."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_18"></a><a href="rp20106.htm#fr_18">14:7</a> The last character is partially destroyed. If my restoration is correct, the name would be Semitic and signify "Ea has rewarded."</p>
