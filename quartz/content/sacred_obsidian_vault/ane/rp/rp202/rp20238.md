 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_551"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_554">200:1</a> The letter <i>m</i> is doubtful according to M. Clermont-Ganneau, but no other is possible. Chemosh-melech is a compound analogous to Elimelech.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_552"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_555">200:2</a> Dibon is said to have been built by Gad (Numb. xxxii. 34).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_553"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_556">200:3</a> Probably a round number like 40 in l. 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_554"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_557">200:4</a> Most likely a district of Dibon, perhaps alluded to in Isaiah xv. 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_555"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_558">200:5</a> Smend-Socin read ‏המלכן‎ "the Kings," which would presuppose an allied force, of which there is no further question in the inscription, nor does the Bible mention that Mesha was assisted in his revolt by allies. The ‏מ‎ is according to M. Clermont-Ganneau doubtful. The following restorations are possible: 1st, ‏השלכן‎ "freebooters." Comp. ‏שלך‎, Lev. xi. 18, A.V. "pelican," or identical with ‏השלחן‎, "swordsmen." 2d, ‏החלכן‎ "the misfortunes" or "misery," Comp. Ps. x. 8.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_556"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_559">200:6</a> The reading ‏כדבר‎ by S.S. is not idiomatic; ‏כזה‎ would do better. According to M. C.-G. there seems to be the trace of a ‏מ‎ following the ‏כ‎. I propose therefore the word [‏מש‎]‏כ‎.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_557"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_560">200:7</a> According to M. C.-G.: S. S. read "all the land;" of the word <i>all</i> there is no trace in the inscription.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_558"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_561">201:1</a> A city in Reuben (Numbers xxi. 30); later belonging to Moab (Isaiah xv. 2).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_559"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_562">201:2</a> A round number, nearer to 40 than to 30.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_560"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_563">201:3</a> S. S. translate: "and Chemosh gave it back;" ‏בה‎[‏שב‎]‏וי‎ gives a better sense. Comp. line 33.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_561"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_564">201:4</a> Also Beth-baal-meon, a city in Reuben, Josh. xiii. 17.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_562"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_565">201:5</a> ‏אשוח‎ is perhaps an Arabic plural form of ‏שוהח‎.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_563"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_566">201:6</a> Kirjathaim, a city in Reuben (Numb. xxxii. 37).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_564"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_567">201:7</a> A city in Gad (Numb. xxxii. 3).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_565"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_568">201:8</a> Arel or Ariel in 2 Sam. xxiii. 20 means no doubt heroes where the A.V. has "he slew two lionlike men of Moab;" and the R.V., "he slew the two <i>sons of</i> Ariel of Moab." Perhaps it was a dialectic word peculiar to the trans-Jordanic country; we find a son of Gad with the name of <i>Areli</i> (Gen. xlvi. x6; Numb. xxvi. 57). It is used also in Isaiah xxxiii. 7, A.V. and R.V., "their valiant ones" (the Hebrew being <i>Erelam</i>, perhaps better <i>Erelim</i>, "valiant ones," parallel to the following expression, "the messengers of peace," or "messengers of Shalem," <i>i.e.</i> Jerusalem). Possibly the word ‏אריה‎ (Isaiah xv. 9; LXX., καὶ Ἀριὴλ; A.V. "lions upon him;" R.V. "a lion upon him"—Isaiah xxi. 8; LXX. Οὐρίαν; A.V. "And he cried, A lion;" R.V. "and he cried as a lion;" better "the hero" or "watchman called out") should be read <i>Aryah</i>, a compound of <i>Ar</i> and <i>yah</i>, analogous to <i>Ar-el</i>. And so perhaps in 2 Sam. xxiii. 20. Ariel is also the name of the stronghold (Zion) of David (Isaiah xxix. 1, 2), and later of a part (? the Holy of Holies) of the Temple (Ezekiel xliii. 15, 16; LXX. ἀριὴλ; A.V. and R.V. altar).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_566"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_569">201:9</a> Or Dodo, perhaps connected with the Carthaginian Dido. The persons named Dodo in the Bible are usually heroes (2 Sam. xxiii. 9, 24); thus we have Dodavahu (2 Chr. xx. 37) and Dodai (1 Chr. xxvii. 4), where Dodo is compounded with Yahu. In our inscription Dodo is parallel with Yahveh (line 17).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_567"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_570">201:10</a> A city in Moab (Jer. xlviii. 24; Amos ii. 2).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_568"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_571">201:11</a> Perhaps to be pronounced Sharon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_569"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_572">202:1</a> Perhaps Me-Hereth; comp. in 1 Sam. xxii. 5, the name of a forest in Moab and the prefix Me in Me-deba (Numb. xxi. 30).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_570"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_573">202:2</a> Most probably a city near Mount Nebo in Moab.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_571"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_575">202:3</a> M. Clermont-Ganneau contests the reading of Smend and Socin. In his restoration only ‏מרן‎ and ‏מרת‎ could give a sense, viz. "Men and masters, women, mistresses" (where ‏מרת‎ would have to be derived from the form ‏מרה‎).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_572"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_576">202:4</a> The male divinity of Ashtoreth, which is to be found in Himyaritic inscriptions, compounded with Chemosh.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_573"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_577">202:5</a> The parallelism of line 12 requires ‏אראלי‎ here. M. Clermont-Ganneau makes too many objections to this reading here and elsewhere.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_574"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_578">202:6</a> City in Moab (Isaiah xv. 4).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_575"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_579">202:7</a> See above, line 9.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_576"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_580">202:8</a> Literally "the cuttings."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_577"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_581">202:9</a> City in Moab (Deut. ii. 36).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_578"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_582">202:10</a> A torrent in Moab (Numb. xxi. 13 <i>sqq</i>.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_579"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_583">203:1</a> Most likely Bamoth (Numb. xxi. 19 and Isaiah xv. 2, where the right reading is perhaps ‏עלה בית הבמות ודיבן למכי‎) Perhaps identical with Bamoth Baal (Joshua xiii. 17).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_580"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_584">203:2</a> City in Reuben (Deut. iv. 43).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_581"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_585">203:3</a> I supply ‏ש‎[‏הא וכל ר‎].</p>
 <p><a name="fn_582"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_586">203:4</a> ‏מלאתי‎.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_583"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_587">203:5</a> ‏מאת‎ [‏רש‎].</p>
 <p><a name="fn_584"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_588">203:6</a> City in Reuben (Numb. xxi. 30), afterwards belonging to Moab (Isaiah xv. 2). I read ‏בת מידבא‎ for ‏מד מדהבא‎ of Smend and Socin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_585"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_589">203:7</a> Beth-Diblathaim, a city in Moab (Jer. xlviii. 22).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_586"></a><a href="rp20238.htm#fr_590">203:8</a> A town of Reuben, later belonging to Moab (Josh. xiii. 17; Jer. xlviii. 23).</p>
