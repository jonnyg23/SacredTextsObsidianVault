 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="green">p. 17</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN TO <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Osiris</span></h3>  
 <p>1. Adoration to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Osiris</span> by the superintendent of the oxen, Amon-em-ha son of the lady Nofri-t-ari. He says: Homage to thee, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Osiris</span>, lord of eternity, king of the gods with the thousand names, <a name="fr_65"></a><a href="#fn_65"><font size="1">1</font></a> with the sacred existences, <a name="fr_66"></a><a href="#fn_66"><font size="1">2</font></a> with the secret acts <a name="fr_67"></a><a href="#fn_67"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the temples; he is rich in <i>Ka</i> <a name="fr_68"></a><a href="#fn_68"><font size="1">4</font></a> in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tattu</span>, <a name="fr_69"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">5</font></a> holding property <a name="fr_70"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sokhem</span>, <a name="fr_71"></a><a href="#fn_71"><font size="1">7</font></a> master of the sacred dances <a name="fr_72"></a><a href="#fn_72"><font size="1">8</font></a> in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Busiris</span>, prince of abundance <a name="fr_73"></a><a href="#fn_73"><font size="1">9</font></a> in On, <a name="fr_74"></a><a href="#fn_74"><font size="1">10</font></a> master of remembrance in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mati</span>, <a name="fr_75"></a><a href="#fn_75"><font size="1">11</font></a> hidden soul, master of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kerer</span>, <a name="fr_76"></a><a href="#fn_76"><font size="1">12</font></a> venerated in the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Memphite</span> nome; <a name="fr_77"></a><a href="#fn_77"><font size="1">13</font></a> the soul whose body itself is <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ra</span>, who reposes in</p>

 <p>4. his name in the mouth of mankind; he who contains the double ennead of the double land; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tum</span> who nourishes the doubles, first of the divine ennead, perfect ghost among the ghosts. <a name="fr_85"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">8</font></a> The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nu</span> <a name="fr_86"></a><a href="#fn_86"><font size="1">9</font></a> has procured for him his water, <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_87"><font size="1">10</font></a> the wind of the north has brought him food, <a name="fr_88"></a><a href="#fn_88"><font size="1">11</font></a> the air enters his nostrils, to refresh his heart,</p>  
 <p>5. to strengthen his heart. <a name="fr_89"></a><a href="#fn_89"><font size="1">12</font></a> The soil has produced for him provisions, the vault of heaven has brought <a name="fr_90"></a><a href="#fn_90"><font size="1">13</font></a> unto him its stars; the wide gates open for him, the master of acclamations in the southern sky, of adorations in the northern sky. The indestructible ones</p>

 <p>order of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Seb</span>, the divine ennead adores him, the inhabitants of the <i>tuau</i> <a name="fr_92"></a><a href="#fn_92"><font size="1">1</font></a> prostrate themselves to the ground, the mighty <a name="fr_93"></a><a href="#fn_93"><font size="1">2</font></a> bow the head, the ancestors <a name="fr_94"></a><a href="#fn_94"><font size="1">3</font></a> are in prayer.</p>  
 <p>7. When they beheld him, the august dead <a name="fr_95"></a><a href="#fn_95"><font size="1">4</font></a> submit to him, the two lands together <a name="fr_96"></a><a href="#fn_96"><font size="1">5</font></a> unite to render glory to him, marching before his majesty.  <p>8. beloved of him who has contemplated him, extending his terror through all countries that they may proclaim his name before all others. <a name="fr_99"></a><a href="#fn_99"><font size="1">8</font></a> All make offerings unto him, even to him the master whose memory (is eternal) in heaven as on the earth. Manifold are the shouts during the festival of Uaga <a name="fr_100"></a><a href="#fn_100"><font size="1">9</font></a>; the two lands are united to celebrate the funeral dances. <a name="fr_101"></a><a href="#fn_101"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p>double land, who seats the son on the throne of his father, the favourite of his father <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Seb</span>, the beloved of his mother <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nut</span>; very valiant, he overthrows the impious; strong of arm, he immolates</p>  
 <p>10. his adversary, breathing terror upon his enemies, conquering the distant frontiers of the wicked. <a name="fr_103"></a><a href="#fn_103"><font size="1">1</font></a> Firm of heart, his feet are vigilant. Flesh <a name="fr_104"></a><a href="#fn_104"><font size="1">2</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Seb</span>, royalty <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_105"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the two lands, [<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Seb</span>] contemplates his benefits, he has ordered him to govern</p>  
 <p>11. [all] countries to assure their prosperity. <a name="fr_106"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">4</font></a> He has fashioned this earth with his hand, [with] its waters, its atmosphere, its vegetation, all its large cattle, all its wild birds, all its domesticated birds, <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_107"><font size="1">5</font></a> its reptiles and its game.</p>  
 <p>12. The desert carries its tribute to the son of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nut</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span> is happy when it sees him appear on the throne of his father. Like <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ra</span> he rises on the horizon, he creates light on the face of the darkness; he has illuminated <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shu</span> <a name="fr_108"></a><a href="#fn_108"><font size="1">6</font></a> by the help of his two feathers, he has inundated the earth like</p>  
 <p>13. the (solar) disk at dawn. His white crown pierces the vault of heaven fraternising with the stars, guides <a name="fr_109"></a><a href="#fn_109"><font size="1">7</font></a> of all the gods. Accomplished are the commands of his voice; [for he is the] favourite of the great ennead, the chosen of the small divine ennead. His sister has saved him, scattering the rebels,</p>  
 <p>34. repelling <a name="fr_110"></a><a href="#fn_110"><font size="1">8</font></a> evil, uttering the word with the incantations <a name="fr_111"></a><a href="#fn_111"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>

 <p>15. who traverses this earth with lamentations, without resting <a name="fr_112"></a><a href="#fn_112"><font size="1">1</font></a> before she has found him, creating the light with her feathers, producing the wind with her wings, celebrating the sacred dances and depositing her brother in the tomb,</p>  
 <p>16. raising <a name="fr_113"></a><a href="#fn_113"><font size="1">2</font></a> the remains of the god with the immovable heart; <a name="fr_114"></a><a href="#fn_114"><font size="1">3</font></a> inhaling his seed, making flesh, <a name="fr_115"></a><a href="#fn_115"><font size="1">4</font></a> suckling the infant <a name="fr_116"></a><a href="#fn_116"><font size="1">5</font></a> in solitude without any knowing where he is. <a name="fr_117"></a><a href="#fn_117"><font size="1">6</font></a> She makes him grow, his arm becomes strong in the great dwelling</p>

He has assembled the chiefs of truth, <a name="fr_120"></a><a href="#fn_120"><font size="1">9</font></a> the divine ennead, [he] himself the universal master. <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_121"><font size="1">10</font></a> The lords of truth collected there</p>  
 <p>18. cast sin afar from them, <a name="fr_122"></a><a href="#fn_122"><font size="1">11</font></a> seated in the vast dwelling-place of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Seb</span>, to establish the dignity of him who is their master, the royalty of justice who resides there. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Horus</span> has been found of just voice; to him has been given the office of his father.  <p>19. he has assumed the dominion of the double land, the white crown being established on his head. He has valued <a name="fr_123"></a><a href="#fn_123"><font size="1">1</font></a> the earth with all it contains; heaven and earth are under the place of his face; <a name="fr_124"></a><a href="#fn_124"><font size="1">2</font></a> [<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Seb</span>] has made him command mankind, the spirits, <a name="fr_125"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">3</font></a> the race of the men of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Egypt</span>, the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ha-neb-u</span>. <a name="fr_126"></a><a href="#fn_126"><font size="1">4</font></a> The circle</p>  
 <p>20. of the solar disk is under his orders, winds, river, inundation, fruit trees <a name="fr_127"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">5</font></a> as well as all the annual plants.</p>  
 <p>As the god <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nepri</span> <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">6</font></a> he makes all his herbage, the wealth of the soil, to grow; he ascends and all are satiated; he spreads <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_129"><font size="1">7</font></a> himself through all lands.</p>  
 <p>21. All that exists breathes; (all) hearts are happy, (all) breasts rejoice. Every being invokes him, every man adores his beauties. Delightful for us is his love; his grace environs the heart; great is his love in all the reins. One offers</p>  
 <p>22 unto the son of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Isis</span> his enemy overthrown by his vigour. The author of evil pronounces magical words and displays his power in his turn; <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_130"><font size="1">8</font></a> [but] the son of Isis makes his way unto him, he avenges his father, sanctifying and honouring <a name="fr_131"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">9</font></a> his name. Terror is calmed;</p>  
 <p>23. her domain is extended, is strengthened according to the laws which he <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_132"><font size="1">10</font></a> dictates. The paths are cleared, the roads are opened, evil flies away; the earth,</p>

 <p>Divine oblation to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Osiris Khent-Amenti</span> , lord of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Abydos</span>, so that he may give good funeral offerings of bread, liquids, oxen, geese, cloths, incense, perfumes</p>  
 <p>26. and all vegetable products; [so that he may grant] to grow, <a name="fr_136"></a><a href="#fn_136"><font size="1">4</font></a> to take possession of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nile</span>, to appear in the form of a living soul, to see the (solar) disk at dawn, to enter and depart by the <i>ro-sta-u</i>; <a name="fr_137"></a><a href="#fn_137"><font size="1">5</font></a> so that the soul may not be driven into the other world, but be received</p>  
 <p>27. among those who chant in the presence of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Un-nofré</span> and who share in the offerings laid upon the altar of the great god; so that it breathes the delicious breezes of the north and drinks of the current</p>  
 <p>28. of the river.</p>  
 <p>To the double of the superintendent of the oxen of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amon</span>, Amon-mes, of the just voice, born of the lady Hont, of the just voice, his wife who loves him [Nofri-t-ari].</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_65"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_65">17:1</a> Compare the epithet ''with a myriad of names" often applied to Isis by the Greeks (Plutarch: <i>de Isid. et Osirid</i>. 53). A Greek inscription in the Louvre (No. 2) calls her "many-named." The same expression is used of Amon.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_66"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_66">17:2</a> <i>Kheperu</i> is usually translated here "transformations." But Osiris is never transformed and <i>khoper</i> merely signifies "to be" or "become."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_67"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_67">17:3</a> <i>Ar-u</i> or <i>it-u</i> has no determinative; it appears to refer to the ceremonies performed in the temples rather than to the forms of the god.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_68"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_68">17:4</a> The <i>Ka</i> is the double of the individual; the gods and sometimes men themselves have several; at Mendes Osiris doubtless had quite a series of them inherent in his sacred statues.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_69"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_69">17:5</a> Mendes.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_70"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_70">17:6</a> Or perhaps, "great nourisher."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_71"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_71">17:7</a> Letopolis.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_72"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_72">17:8</a> The word is several times determined in this stele by the figure of a dancing man, so that it must refer to dances in the temple performed in honour of the god.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_73"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_73">17:9</a> Literally "provisions of victualling."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_74"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_74">17:10</a> Heliopolis, the On of the Old Testament, the daughter of whose priest was married by Joseph.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_75"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_75">17:11</a> Unidentified locality. It is the name of the Hall of Truth where Osiris and his assessors judge the souls of the dead.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_76"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_76">17:12</a> Perhaps Paqrur, Phagroriopolis. In the tomb of Bok-en-ranf near Saqqarah, Osiris also is named "master of Kerer"; in the temple of Seti I. at Qurnah, Anubis has the same title.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_77"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_77">17:13</a> "The White Wall."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_78"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_78">18:1</a> Herakleopolis, now the mounds of Ahnas el-Medîneh, S.E. of the Fayoum. It is the Hanes of the Old Testament (Isai. xxx. 4).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_79"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_79">18:2</a> Literally "beneficent in useful things": <i>hannu</i> here has no determinative.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_80"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_80">18:3</a> A locality near Hanes and often identified with the latter.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_81"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_81">18:4</a> Hermopolis Magna.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_82"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_82">18:5</a> Literally "great with the double solar force."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_83"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_83">18:6</a> Shotb, capital of the Hypselite nome, S. of Assiout.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_84"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_84">18:7</a> "The sacred land."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_85"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_85">18:8</a> Khu, "luminous," and hence "a magic power," often applied to the dead as having become luminous.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_86"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_86">18:9</a> The primordial water or abyss. Compare "the deep" of Gen. i. 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_87"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_87">18:10</a> <i>Khenp</i> is usually rendered "to extract." But the Nu is anterior to Osiris, and therefore gives instead of receiving his water. Moreover the construction seems to make this explanation necessary.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_88"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_88">18:11</a> <i>Meses</i>, "the night," more especially that which precedes the New Year, and hence the feast which took place then in honour of Osiris (Brugsch, <i>Dict</i>., 700).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_89"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_89">18:12</a> Perhaps the word I have read <i>rut-u</i> should be decomposed into <i>rtu renpet-i-u</i>, "to give the productions of the year (to his heart)."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_90"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_90">18:13</a> Or "has submitted to him."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_91"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_91">18:14</a> The two compound words in parellelism here, <i>akhim-u sek-u, akhim-u urtt-u</i>, have been translated: "the fixed stars" and "the wandering stars" or planets, as well as "stars which always remain on the horizon," and "stars which are there only at certain hours." These distinctions are not proved; and it is best to adhere to the literal sense of the words.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_92"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_92">19:1</a> The other world, which in Egyptian belief was not <i>under</i> the earth but beyond its limits; see Maspero, <i>Revue de l’Histoire des Religions</i>, 1887.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_93"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_93">19:2</a> The word read <i>t’at’a-u</i> by Chabas seems to be <i>tes-ti-u</i>, "those who are exalted," and forms a natural antithesis to <i>kes-u</i>, "bent."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_94"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_94">19:3</a> <i>T’er-ti-u</i> and not <i>t’era-u</i> ("all"), that is the common herd of the dead, the ancestors of men in general, in opposition to the <i>tes-ti-u</i> or "mighty."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_95"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_95">19:4</a> <i>Nti-u am</i>, "those who are below," a vague expression, euphemistic for "the dead."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_96"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_96">19:5</a> Literally "in a single place."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_97"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_97">19:6</a> <i>Sahu</i> probably denotes the higher officials.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_98"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_98">19:7</a> <i>Hiq</i> is a feudal prince, and <i>hiq-t</i> the absolute authority he possessed in his domain, large or small, whether composed of several nomes or of less extent than a single nome. The title is often applied to the kings, though not as kings of all Egypt.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_99"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_99">19:8</a> Literally "in advance."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_100"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_100">19:9</a> One of the great festivals of Osiris when lamps were lit throughout Egypt (see Herodotos ii. 62).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_101"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_101">19:10</a> <i>Ahi</i> is, like <i>hannu</i>, determined by the figure of a dancing man. As the festival was in honour of the dead, the dances would have had a funereal character.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_102"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_102">19:11</a> <i>Ur-u</i> is in the plural, and consequently must be construed with the preceding word.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_103"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_103">20:1</a> Literally, "bringing on the frontiers," often applied to conquering kings.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_104"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_104">20:2</a> Or "heir."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_105"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_105">20:3</a> As in English, abstract terms are sometimes used of persons.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_106"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_106">20:4</a> Literally, "to conduct the countries to prosperity."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_107"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_107">20:5</a> <i>Pai</i> are the birds who fly freely through the air, <i>khenen</i> those who rest, probably therefore domestic fowls or perhaps water-fowl.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_108"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_108">20:6</a> <i>Shu</i> sometimes means "the shade," but here it seems to denote either the god Shu himself or the space which he occupies between the earth and the sky, uplifting, like Atlas, the celestial vault with his two arms.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_109"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_109">20:7</a> <i>Sem-u</i> also signifies "image," a sense which would suit here very well, the stars being images or manifestations of each god. The absence of a determinative makes a decision difficult.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_110"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_110">20:8</a> "Making turn," literally.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_111"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_111">20:9</a> <i>Khu</i>, the magic charms which enable the gods and more especially Isis to triumph.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_112"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_112">21:1</a> <i>Khen</i> used of birds who remain stationary. Elsewhere Isis is winged.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_113"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_113">21:2</a> Putting them one on the other so as to reconstitute the mutilated body of Osiris.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_114"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_114">21:3</a> That is Osiris.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_115"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_115">21:4</a> Or "an heir."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_116"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_116">21:5</a> Horus, born of Isis and the revivified Osiris.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_117"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_117">21:6</a> Isis hid herself in the marshes of the Delta with Horus in order to rear him in peace.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_118"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_118">21:7</a> In the text "Osiris son Horus." This way of expressing affiliation belongs to the age of the twelfth dynasty, <i>e.g.</i> in the tomb of Khnum-hotep at Beni-Hassan, where we have "Neheri son Khnum-hotep," i.e. Khnum-hotep son of Neheri (Cf. Lepsius, <i>Denkmäler</i>, iv. pl. 126 sqq.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_119"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_119">21:8</a> M. Maspero explains this expression, which is used generally of the defunct, in a material sense, "he whose voice knows the correct intonation in reciting prayers and formulæ." M. Grébaut and most Egyptologists take it in a moral sense: "true of speech," "truth speaking."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_120"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_120">21:9</a> The acolytes who sit with Osiris in the Hall of Truth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_121"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_121">21:10</a> Perhaps this merely signifies "the entire god" who now has all his limbs.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_122"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_122">21:11</a> Literally "put behind them."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_123"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_123">22:1</a> Properly, "count," "reckoning." The same metaphor occurs in the Bible.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_124"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_124">22:2</a> Compare the Biblical expression in Psalm lx. 13.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_125"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_125">22:3</a> <i>Rekhi-u</i> "the intelligent," "those who know," frequently used of the dead.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_126"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_126">22:4</a> "All those of the north," i.e. all the inhabitants of the islands and coasts of the Mediterranean. In later times the term was specially used of the Greeks.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_127"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_127">22:5</a> Or perhaps "trees which last," in opposition to plants which die and revive each year.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_128"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_128">22:6</a> The grain-god who presided over cereals, vegetation, and the products of the earth. Amon-Ra is also called Nepri in the hymn contained in the Bulaq Museum (pl. viii. Grébaut, <i>Hymne à Amon-Ra</i>, p. 21).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_129"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_129">22:7</a> Properly, "every face."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_130"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_130">22:8</a> Literally "his time."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_131"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_131">22:9</a> Or "rendering beneficent."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_132"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_132">22:10</a> Horus or Osiris.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_133"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_133">23:1</a> The determinative of the legs seems to contradict this rendering, but I can find no other signification for the word <i>aura</i>, <i>wuru</i>, and we are compelled to admit a grammatical error.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_134"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_134">23:2</a> "The good being," Osiris.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_135"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_135">23:3</a> Those who sit with Osiris in the Hall of Judgment.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_136"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_136">23:4</a> <i>Khopiru</i>, "the becomings."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_137"></a><a href="rp20407.htm#fr_137">23:5</a> The corridors or defiles which led (like the defiles of the mountain-cliffs to the west of Abydos) from this world to the next.</p>
