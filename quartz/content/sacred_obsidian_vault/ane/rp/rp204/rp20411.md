 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="green">p. 38</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">INSCRIPTION ON THE OBELISK OF SHALMANESER II</h3>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Face A, Top</span></h4>  
 <p>I. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>, the great lord, the king of all</p>  
 <p>2. the great gods; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anu</span> the king of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Igigi</span></p>  
 <p>3. and the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anunnaki</span>; <a name="fr_188"></a><a href="#fn_188"><font size="1">1</font></a> the lord of the world, the supreme, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bel</span></p>  
 <p>4. the father of the gods, the creator</p>  
 <p>5. of the universe; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ea</span>, the king of the abyss who determines destinies;</p>  
 <p>6. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sin</span>, the king of the (lunar) disk, who sheds the light;</p>  
 <p>7. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Adad</span>, <a name="fr_189"></a><a href="#fn_189"><font size="1">2</font></a> the very mighty, the master of abundance; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shamash</span>,</p>  
 <p>8. the judge of heaven and earth, the ordainer of all things;</p>  
 <p>9. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span>, the herald of the gods, the master of the laws; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Adar</span>, <a name="fr_190"></a><a href="#fn_190"><font size="1">3</font></a> the captain</p>  
 <p>10. of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Igigi</span> and the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anunnaki</span>, the god all-powerful; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nergal</span>,</p>  
 <p>11. the valiant, the king of battles; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nusku</span> who bears the august sceptre,</p>  
 <p>12. the omniscient god; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Beltis</span>, the wife of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bel</span>, the mother of the great gods;</p>  
 <p>13. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ishtar</span>, the princess of heaven and earth, accomplished in courageous decisions;</p>  
 <p>14. the great gods who have determined my destinies and enlarged my royalty!</p>  
 <p>15. Shalmaneser, the king of the multitude of men, high-priest of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>, the powerful king,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="green">p. 39</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. the king of all the four regions, the Sun-god of the multitude of mankind, who governs</p>  
 <p>17. in all countries; the son of Assur-natsir-pal, the supreme priest, whose priesthood unto the gods</p>  
 <p>18. was pleasing, and who has subdued unto his feet all lands;</p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Face B, Top</span></h4>  
 <p>19. the illustrious offspring of Tukulti-Adar <a name="fr_191"></a><a href="#fn_191"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. who subjugated all his enemies and</p>  
 <p>21. swept them like the tempest.—</p>  
 <p>22. At the beginning of my reign, when on the throne</p>  
 <p>23. of the kingdom I had seated myself in state, my chariots</p>  
 <p>24. (and) my armies I assembled. Into the defiles of the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Simesi</span> <a name="fr_192"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. I penetrated. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Aridu</span>, the strong city</p>  
 <p>26. of Ninni I captured.—In the first year of my reign</p>  
 <p>27. I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span> in its flood; towards the sea of the setting sun</p>  
 <p>28. I marched. I purified my weapons in the sea. Victims</p>  
 <p>29. to my gods I sacrificed. I ascended mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span>; <a name="fr_193"></a><a href="#fn_193"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. I cut logs of cedar and thuya.</p>  
 <p>31. I climbed mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lallar</span> and erected there an image of my royalty.—</p>  
 <p>32. In the second year of my reign I approached the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Barsaip</span>. <a name="fr_194"></a><a href="#fn_194"><font size="1">4</font></a> The cities</p>  
 <p>33. of Akhuni the son of Adini I captured; I shut him up in his city. <a name="fr_195"></a><a href="#fn_195"><font size="1">5</font></a> The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span></p>  
 <p>34. I crossed in its flood. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dabigu</span>, a fortress of the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span>, <a name="fr_196"></a><a href="#fn_196"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. together with the cities that were dependent upon it I captured.—In the third year of my reign Akhuni</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>36. the son of Adini trembled before my powerful arms, and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Barsaip</span>,</p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Face C, Top</span></h4>  
 <p>37. his royal city, he abandoned, and he crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>.</p>  
 <p>38. The City of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ana-Assur-utir-atsbat</span>, <a name="fr_197"></a><a href="#fn_197"><font size="1">1</font></a> situated on the further side</p>  
 <p>39. of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>, upon the river <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sagurra</span>, <a name="fr_198"></a><a href="#fn_198"><font size="1">2</font></a> which the people</p>  
 <p>40. of the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span> call <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Pitru</span>, <a name="fr_199"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. I took for myself. On my return</p>  
 <p>42. I penetrated into the defiles of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Alzi</span>. <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">4</font></a> The countries of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Alzi</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lukh[me]</span>,</p>  
 <p>43. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dayeni</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Numme</span>, the City of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Arzashkun</span> the capital</p>  
 <p>44. of Arame of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Urardhu</span>, <a name="fr_201"></a><a href="#fn_201"><font size="1">5</font></a> the countries of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Guzan</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khupushkia</span> [I have conquered].</p>  
 <p>45. In the eponymy of Dayan-Assur <a name="fr_202"></a><a href="#fn_202"><font size="1">6</font></a> I departed from <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nineveh</span>; the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span></p>  
 <p>46. I crossed at its flood. I marched against Akhuni the son of Adini; the country of Shitamrat, <a name="fr_203"></a><a href="#fn_203"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. a mountain peak on the bank of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>, he made his stronghold. The peak</p>  
 <p>48. of the mountain I assaulted and captured. Akhuni with his gods, his chariots,</p>  
 <p>49. his horses, his sons, his daughters, (and) his army I carried away and to my city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span></p>  
 <p>50. I brought. In that same year I crossed mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kullar</span>; to the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zamua</span></p>  
 <p>51. of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bitani</span> <a name="fr_204"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">8</font></a> I descended. The cities of Nikdiara the prince of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Idians</span></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="green">p. 41</font></a></p>  
 <p>52. (and) of Nikdima I captured.—In the fifth year of my reign I ascended mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kashyari</span>. <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_205"><font size="1">1</font></a> Eleven strong cities</p>  
 <p>53. I captured. I besieged <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ankhitti</span> <a name="fr_206"></a><a href="#fn_206"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Rurians</span> in his city. His tribute</p>  
 <p>54. abundant I received.—In the sixth year of my reign to the cities on the banks of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Balikhi</span> <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Face D, Top</span></h4>  
 <p>55. I approached. They had slain Giammu their governor.</p>  
 <p>56. I entered the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Turakhe</span>. <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>57. I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span> at its flood.</p>  
 <p>58. The tribute of the kings of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span></p>  
 <p>59. all of them I received. Then Dadda-Idri <a name="fr_209"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>60. the king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Emerishu</span>, <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">6</font></a> Irkhulina <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_211"><font size="1">7</font></a> of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hamathites</span>, together with the kings</p>  
 <p>61. of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span> and of the coast of the Sea, to their allied forces</p>  
 <p>62. trusted, and to offer combat and battle</p>  
 <p>63. came against me. By the command of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> the great lord, my lord,</p>  
 <p>64. I fought with them, I defeated them.</p>  
 <p>65. I took from them their chariots, their litters (?) (and) their war material.</p>

 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="green">p. 42</font></a></p>  
 <p>66. I slew 20,500 of their soldiers with weapons.—</p>  
 <p>67. In the 7th year of my reign I marched against the cities of Khabini (prince) of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Abnê</span>.</p>  
 <p>68. I captured <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Abnê</span> his stronghold and the cities dependent on it.</p>  
 <p>69. I marched to the sources of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tigris</span>, the place from whence the waters gush forth;</p>  
 <p>70. there I purified the arms of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>; I sacrificed victims to my gods; a feast of rejoicing</p>  
 <p>71. I made. I erected a great image of my royal majesty. The glory of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> my lord, the exploits</p>  
 <p>72. of my valour, and all that I had done in these countries, I inscribed upon it; I set (it) up there.—</p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Face A, Base</span></h4>  
 <p>73. In the 8th year of my reign (against) Merodach-shum-iddin the king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Karduniash</span> <a name="fr_212"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>74. Merodach-bel-usate his younger brother revolted. (The country)</p>  
 <p>75. they divided between them. To avenge</p>  
 <p>76. Merodach-shum-iddin I marched. I captured the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Me-Turnat</span>. <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">2</font></a>—</p>  
 <p>77. In the 9th year of my reign for the second time I marched to the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Accad</span>. <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_214"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>78. I besieged <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gananate</span>. As for Merodach-bel-usate, the terror</p>  
 <p>79. of the glory of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Merodach</span> overwhelmed him, and to save his life</p>  
 <p>80. he ascended the mountain. I marched after him. Merodach-bel-usate (and) the soldiers,</p>  
 <p>81. the rebel-chiefs who were with him I slew with my weapons. To the great cities</p>  
 <p>82. I marched; I offered sacrifices in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Babylon</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Borsippa</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kuta</span>. <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_215"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_43"><font size="1" color="green">p. 43</font></a></p>  
 <p>83. I made offerings to the great gods. I descended to the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Chaldæa</span>; <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="#fn_216"><font size="1">1</font></a> I captured their cities.</p>  
 <p>84. I received the tribute of the kings of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Chaldæa</span>. The torrent (?) of my arms overwhelmed as far as the Salt-marshes. <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="#fn_217"><font size="1">2</font></a>—</p>  
 <p>85. In the 10th year of my reign for the eighth time I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>; I captured the cities of Sangara of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Carchemish</span>; <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="#fn_218"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>86. I approached the cities of Arame. I captured <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Arnê</span>. his royal city and 100 of his towns.—</p>  
 <p>87. In the 11th year of my reign for the ninth time I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>. I captured cities without number. To the cities of the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span></p>  
 <p>88. (and) of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hamathites</span> I descended. I captured 89 towns. Dadda-idri of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Damascus</span> (and) twelve kings of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span> <a name="fr_219"></a><a href="#fn_219"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>89. ranged themselves side by side; I overthrew them.—In the 12th year of my reign for the tenth time I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>.</p>  
 <p>90. I marched against the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Paqarkhubuna</span>; I carried away their spoil.—In the 13th year of my reign I went up against the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Yaeti</span>;</p>  
 <p>91. I carried away their spoil.—In the 14th year of my reign I assembled (the men) of the country; I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>; twelve kings met me;</p>  
 <p>92. I fought [with them]; I overthrew them.—In the 15th year of my reign I marched to the sources of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tigris</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>. An image</p>  
 <p>93. I erected in their caverns.—In the 16th year of my reign I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zab</span>; <a name="fr_220"></a><a href="#fn_220"><font size="1">5</font></a> to the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Namri</span> <a name="fr_221"></a><a href="#fn_221"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p>94. I marched. Merodach-mudammiq king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Namri</span>, to save his life, ascended (the mountain): his goods,</p>  
 <p>95. his troops (and) his gods I transported to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyria</span>. Yanzu <a name="fr_222"></a><a href="#fn_222"><font size="1">1</font></a> the son of Khanban I raised to the sovereignty over them.—</p>  <h4 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Face B, Base</span></h4>  
 <p>96. In the 17th year of my reign I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>; I ascended mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span>; logs</p>  
 <p>97. of cedar I cut.—In the 18th year of my reign for the sixteenth time I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>. Hazael</p>  
 <p>98. of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Damascus</span> advanced to battle: 1121 chariots, 470 <i>litters</i> (?) with</p>  
 <p>99. his camp I took from him. <a name="fr_223"></a><a href="#fn_223"><font size="1">2</font></a>—In the 19th year of my reign for the eighteenth time I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>. Mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span></p>  
 <p>100. I ascended: logs of cedar I cut.—In the 20th year of my reign, for the twentieth time, the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span></p>  
 <p>101. I crossed. Into the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Qaue</span> <a name="fr_224"></a><a href="#fn_224"><font size="1">3</font></a> I descended. I captured their cities. Their spoil</p>  
 <p>102. I carried away.—In the 21st year of my reign for the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="green">p. 45</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 21st time I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span>. Against the cities</p>  
 <p>103. of Hazael of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Damascus</span> I marched; four of his cities I captured. The tribute of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tyrians</span>,</p>  
 <p>104. the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sidonians</span> (and) the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gebalites</span> <a name="fr_225"></a><a href="#fn_225"><font size="1">1</font></a> I received.—In the 22d year of my reign for the 22d time the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span></p>  
 <p>105. I crossed. I descended into the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tubal</span>. <a name="fr_226"></a><a href="#fn_226"><font size="1">2</font></a> At that time from the twenty-four</p>  
 <p>106. kings of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tubal</span> I received gifts. To mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tumar</span>,</p>  
 <p>107. a mountain of silver, a mountain of <i>mulî</i>, <a name="fr_227"></a><a href="#fn_227"><font size="1">3</font></a> a mountain of marble, I marched.—In the 23d year of my reign</p>  
 <p>108. the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span> I crossed. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Uetash</span> the stronghold</p>  
 <p>109. of Lalla the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Milidian</span> <a name="fr_228"></a><a href="#fn_228"><font size="1">4</font></a> I captured. The kings of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tubal</span></p>  
 <p>110. had come; their tribute I received.—In the 24th year of my reign the Lower <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zab</span></p>  
 <p>111. I crossed. I passed over mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khashimur</span>; into the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Namri</span></p>  
 <p>112. I descended. Yanzû the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Namri</span> before</p>  
 <p>113. my powerful weapons trembled, and to save his life</p>  
 <p>114. ascended (the mountain). <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sikhishalakh</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bit-Tamul</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bit-Sakki</span></p>  
 <p>115. (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bit-Shêdi</span> his strong cities I captured. His soldiers I slew.</p>  
 <p>116. His spoil I carried away. I threw down, dug up (and) burned with fire the cities.</p>  
 <p>117. The survivors of them ascended the mountains. The mountain peaks</p>  
 <p>118. I assaulted, I captured: their soldiers I slew; their spoil (and) their goods</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>

Hazael of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Damascus</span> trusted to the strength of his armies and assembled his armies to a large number. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Saniru</span> (the Biblical Shenir, Deut. iii. 9), a mountain summit as you come to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lebanon</span>, he made his stronghold. I fought with him, I defeated him: 6000 of his soldiers I slew with weapons, 1121 of his chariots, 470 of his war-horses along with his camp I took from him. To save his life he ascended (the mountain). I pursued after him. In <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Damascus</span> his royal city I shut him up. His plantations I cut down. As far as the mountains of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hauran</span> I marched. The cities to a countless number I threw down, dug up (and) burned with fire. Their spoil to a countless amount I carried away. As far as the mountain of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bahli-rahsi</span> (Baal-rosh at the mouth of the Dog River), which (is) a headland of the sea, I marched: an image of my majesty I set up upon it. At that time I received the tribute of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tyrians</span>, the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sidonians</span> (and) of Yahua (Jehu) the son of Khumrî (Omri)."—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ed.</span>]</p>

 <p><a name="fn_230"></a><a href="rp20411.htm#fr_230">46:2</a> [<i>Amadâ</i>, probably to be identified with Madâ or "Medes." If so, this is the earliest mention we have of the latter people.—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ed.</span>]</p>

 <p><a name="fn_237"></a><a href="rp20411.htm#fr_237">47:5</a> [The Arsanias of classical geography, which joins the Euphrates near Mush to the west of Lake Van.—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ed.</span>]</p>
 <p><a name="fn_238"></a><a href="rp20411.htm#fr_238">48:1</a> [Sarduris I., of the native Vannic texts, of whom we have two inscriptions in the Assyrian language, both found at Van. He introduced the cuneiform system of writing into Armenia, and seems to have founded the Vannic kingdom. In his inscriptions he calls himself the son of Lutipris and king of Nairi, and claims to have built the citadel of Van. His son and successor, Isbuinis, substituted the native language for Assyrian in his inscriptions. See my <i>Memoir on the Cuneiform Inscriptions of Van</i>, Jrl. R. A. S., xiv. 3, 4; xx. 1.—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ed.</span>]</p>
 <p><a name="fn_239"></a><a href="rp20411.htm#fr_240">48:2</a> Shalmaneser here identifies himself with his commander-in-chief.</p>
