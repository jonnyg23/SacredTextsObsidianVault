 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="green">p. 55</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE MONOLITH INSCRIPTION OF SHALMANESER II</h3>  <h4 align="center">COLUMN I</h4>  
 <p>1. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> the great lord, the king of all the great gods; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anu</span> the king of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Igigi</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anunnaki</span>, <a name="fr_263"></a><a href="#fn_261"><font size="1">1</font></a> the master of the world; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bel</span> the father of the gods, who determines destiny,</p>  
 <p>2. who institutes the laws [of heaven and earth]; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ea</span>, the wise, the king of the Abyss, the discoverer of cunning arts; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sin</span> the illuminator of heaven (and) earth, the illustrious god; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shamash</span></p>  
 <p>3. the judge of the (four) zones, the director of mankind; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ishtar</span> the lady of battles and combats, whose delight (is) conflict; the great gods who love my royalty,</p>  
 <p>4. my empire, my power, and my government have they magnified; a famous name, an illustrious renown, above all the sovereigns (of the world) have they bestowed on me in abundance!</p>  
 <p>5. Shalmaneser, the king of the multitudes of men, the sovereign pontiff of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>, the powerful king, the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyria</span>, the king of all the four zones, the Sun-god <a name="fr_264"></a><a href="#fn_262"><font size="1">2</font></a> of the multitudes of men,</p>  
 <p>6. who governs all the world; the king who fears the gods, the favourite <a name="fr_265"></a><a href="#fn_263"><font size="1">3</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bel</span>, the appointed vicar of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>, the august prince, who has traversed</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="green">p. 56</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. easy paths and difficult roads, who has trodden the summits of the mountains (and) all (their) ranges, who has received tribute and presents</p>  
 <p>8. from all regions, who has opened the mountains above and below; before the onset of whose mighty battle the regions (of the world) have yielded,</p>  
 <p>9. the world has trembled to its foundations before his warlike fury; the male hero who has marched under the protection of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shamash</span>,the gods his allies;</p>  
 <p>10. who has no rival among the kings of the four zones (of the world); the royal despot of the world, who has traversed difficult roads, (and) has advanced over mountains and seas;</p>  
 <p>11. the son of Assur-natsir-pal, the vicegerent of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bel</span>, the priest of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>, whose priesthood has been pleasing to the gods, and who has subjected to his feet all lands; the illustrious descendant of Tukulti-Adar <a name="fr_266"></a><a href="#fn_264"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. who subjugated all his foes, and swept them like the tempest, when <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> the great lord in the determination of his [heart] had turned upon me his illustrious eyes, and</p>  
 <p>13. had called me to the government <a name="fr_267"></a><a href="#fn_265"><font size="1">2</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyria</span>; had given me to hold the mighty weapon which overthrows the rebellious; had [invested] me with the [sacred] crown; the lordship over all lands</p>  
 <p>14. had granted me; had strongly urged me to conquer and subjugate: in those days at the beginning of my reign, in the first of my (regnal) years, <a name="fr_268"></a><a href="#fn_266"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. (when) I had seated myself in state on the throne of royalty, I summoned my chariots (and) armies; into the defiles of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Simesi</span> I entered; to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Aridu</span> the fortified city</p>  
 <p>16. of Ninni I approached. The city I besieged, I captured; its numerous soldiers I slew; its spoil I</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>carried away. I erected a pyramid of heads at the entrance of his city.</p>  
 <p>17. Their youths and maidens I delivered to the flames. <a name="fr_269"></a><a href="#fn_267"><font size="1">1</font></a> While I remained in <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Aridu</span> the tribute of the people of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kharga</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kharmasa</span>,</p>  
 <p>18. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Simesi</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Simera</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sirisha</span>, (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ulmania</span>, horses trained to the yoke, oxen, sheep, (and) wine I received. From <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Aridu</span></p>  
 <p>19. I departed; difficult paths (and) inaccessible mountains whose peaks rose to the sky like the point of an iron sword I cut with axes of bronze (and) copper. The chariots</p>  
 <p>20. (and) troops I caused to cross (them). To the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khupushkia</span> I approached. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khupushkia</span> with 100 towns which (were) dependent on it I burned with fire. Kakia</p>  
 <p>21. a king of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nairi</span> and the rest of his troops trembled before the splendour of my arms, and occupied the strong mountains. After them I ascended the mountains,</p>  
 <p>22. I fought a hard battle in the midst of the mountains (and) utterly destroyed them. I brought back from the mountains chariots, troops, (and) horses trained to the yoke. The terror of the glory</p>  
 <p>23. of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> my lord overwhelmed them; they descended (and) took my feet. Taxes and tribute I imposed upon them. From the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khupushkia</span> I departed.</p>  
 <p>24. To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sugunia</span> the stronghold of Arame of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ararat</span> <a name="fr_270"></a><a href="#fn_268"><font size="1">2</font></a> I approached. The city I besieged, I captured; their numerous soldiers I slew.</p>  
 <p>25. Its spoil I carried away. I erected a pyramid of heads at the entrance of his city; 14 towns which (were) dependent on it I burned with fire. From <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sugunia</span></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="green">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. I departed; to the sea of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nairi</span> <a name="fr_271"></a><a href="#fn_269"><font size="1">1</font></a> I descended. I purified my weapons in the sea; I sacrificed victims to my gods. In those days an image of my person</p>  
 <p>27. I made; I inscribed upon it the glory of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> the great lord, my lord, and the mightiness of my empire; I erected (it) overlooking the sea. On my return</p>  
 <p>28. from the sea I received the tribute of Asû of the land of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Guzan</span> in abundance, horses, oxen, sheep, wine, (and) two camels with two humps;</p>  
 <p>29. to my city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> I brought (them).—In the month Iyyar, on the 13th day, <a name="fr_272"></a><a href="#fn_270"><font size="1">2</font></a> I departed from <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nineveh</span>. I crossed the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tigris</span>. I passed through the mountains <a name="fr_273"></a><a href="#fn_271"><font size="1">3</font></a> Of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khasamu</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dikhnunu</span>.</p>  
 <p>30. To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">La’la’te</span> <a name="fr_274"></a><a href="#fn_272"><font size="1">4</font></a> a city of Akhuni the son of Adini I approached. The terror of the glory of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> my lord overwhelmed [them, to the mountains …]</p>  
 <p>31. they ascended. The city I threw down, dug up (and) burned with fire. From <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">La’la’te</span> I departed. [To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ki … qa</span> the stronghold]</p>  
 <p>32. of Akhuni the son of Adini I approached. Akhuni the son of Adini to the multitude [of his troops trusted, and to make] combat and battle [came against] me. Under the protection of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span></p>  
 <p>33. and the great gods, my lords, I fought with him; I utterly defeated him. I shut him up in his city. From the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ki … qa</span> I departed;</p>  
 <p>34. to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bur-mar’âna</span> <a name="fr_275"></a><a href="#fn_273"><font size="1">5</font></a> a city of Akhuni the son of Adini [I approached. The city] I besieged, I captured. I destroyed with my weapons 300 of his fighting-men. A pyramid of heads</p>  
 <p>35. I erected [at the entrance to his city]. The tribute of</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="green">p. 59</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khapini</span> <a name="fr_276"></a><a href="#fn_274"><font size="1">1</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-abna</span>, <a name="fr_277"></a><a href="#fn_275"><font size="1">2</font></a> of Ga’uni of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sa[llu]</span>, … of Giri-Dadda <a name="fr_278"></a><a href="#fn_276"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. [of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assu</span>], silver, gold, oxen, sheep, (and) wine I received. From the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bur-mar’âna</span> I departed; in boats of seal-skin the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span></p>  
 <p>37. I crossed. The tribute of Qata-zilu of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Kummukh</span>, <a name="fr_279"></a><a href="#fn_277"><font size="1">4</font></a> silver, gold, oxen, sheep, (and) wine I received. To the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Paqarrukhbuni</span> <a name="fr_280"></a><a href="#fn_278"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. (and) the cities of Akhuni the son of Adini on the farther bank of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span> I approached. I utterly destroyed the country. Its cities to ruins</p>  
 <p>39. I reduced. I filled the broad plain with the corpses of his warriors; 1300 of his fighting-men I slew with weapons.</p>  
 <p>40. From the city <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Paqarrukhbuni</span> I departed; to the cities of Mutalli <a name="fr_281"></a><a href="#fn_279"><font size="1">6</font></a> of the city of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gamgumians</span> I approached. The tribute</p>  
 <p>41. of Mutalli of the city of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gamgumians</span>, silver, gold, oxen, sheep, wine, (and) his daughter with a large dowry I received. From the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gamgumê</span></p>  
 <p>42. I departed; <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lutibu</span> the stronghold of Khânu of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sam’alians</span> I approached. Khânu of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sam’alians</span>, Sapalulme <a name="fr_282"></a><a href="#fn_280"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Patinians</span>, <a name="fr_283"></a><a href="#fn_281"><font size="1">8</font></a> Akhuni the son of Adini, Sangara of the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Carchemishians</span>, trusted to their mutual alliance and prepared for</p>  
 <p>44. battle; they came against me to fight. By the supreme</p>

 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="green">p. 60</font></a></p>  
 <p>power of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nergal</span> who marches before me, with the forceful weapons</p>  
 <p>45. which <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> the lord has granted (me) I fought with them, I utterly defeated them. Their combatants</p>  
 <p>46. I slew with weapons; like <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hadad</span> <a name="fr_284"></a><a href="#fn_282"><font size="1">1</font></a> I poured the deluge upon them, I heaped them up in the ditches; with the bodies</p>  
 <p>47. of their warriors I filled the broad plain; with their blood I dyed the mountains like wool. (His) many chariots [and troops], (and) horses</p>  
 <p>48. trained for the yoke I took from him. <a name="fr_285"></a><a href="#fn_283"><font size="1">2</font></a> I erected a pyramid of heads at the entrance to his city. His cities I threw down, dug up (and) burned with fire.</p>  
 <p>49. In those days I celebrated the greatness of the great gods; I proclaimed for ever the valour of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Shamash</span>. A great image of my royalty</p>  
 <p>50. I made; I inscribed upon it the exploits of my valour (and) the deeds of my glory. At the source of the river <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Saluara</span></p>  
 <p>51. at the foot of mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span> I erected (it). From mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span> I departed; the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Orontes</span> <a name="fr_286"></a><a href="#fn_284"><font size="1">3</font></a> I crossed; to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Alimush</span> <a name="fr_287"></a><a href="#fn_285"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>52. the stronghold of Sapalulme the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Patinian</span> I approached. Sapalulme the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Patinian</span> to save</p>  
 <p>53. his life [called to his aid] Akhuni the son of Adini, Sangara the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Carchemishian</span>, Khayânu the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sam’alian</span>, Kate-[zilu the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Komagenian</span>], …</p>  
 <p>54. the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Quan</span>, <a name="fr_288"></a><a href="#fn_286"><font size="1">5</font></a> Pikhirim the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Cilician</span>, <a name="fr_289"></a><a href="#fn_287"><font size="1">6</font></a> Bur-anate the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Yasbukian</span>, Ada … the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assyria</span></p>  <h4 align="center">COLUMN II</h4>

 <p>5. … my hands captured. The great cities of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Patinian</span> I in[vested. The countries]</p>  
 <p>6. of the Upper [Sea] <a name="fr_290"></a><a href="#fn_288"><font size="1">1</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Syria</span> <a name="fr_291"></a><a href="#fn_289"><font size="1">2</font></a> and of the sea of the setting sun I swept like a mound under a storm.</p>  
 <p>7. The tribute of the kings of the sea-coast I received. On the shores of the broad sea, straight before me, victoriously</p>  
 <p>8. I marched. An image of my majesty I made to perpetuate my name for ever, overlooking the sea I e[rected it].</p>  
 <p>9. To the mountains of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span> I ascended. Logs of cedar and thuya I cut. To the mountains</p>  
 <p>10. of mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Atalur</span> where the image of Assur-irbi <a name="fr_292"></a><a href="#fn_290"><font size="1">3</font></a> was set up I marched. I erected an image by the side of his image. From the sea I went [down];</p>  
 <p>11. the cities of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Taya</span> …, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khazazu</span>, <a name="fr_293"></a><a href="#fn_291"><font size="1">4</font></a> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nulia</span> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">But-âmu</span> belonging to the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Patinian</span> I captured; 2800 fighting-men</p>  
 <p>12. I slew; 14,600 prisoners I carried away. The tribute of Arame the son of Gusi, <a name="fr_294"></a><a href="#fn_292"><font size="1">5</font></a> silver, gold, oxen,</p>  
 <p>13. sheep, wine, (and) couches of gold and silver I received.—In the year of my own eponymy, <a name="fr_295"></a><a href="#fn_293"><font size="1">6</font></a> on the 13th day of the month Iyyar from [<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Nineveh</span>]</p>  
 <p>14. I departed; the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tigris</span> I crossed, the mountains <a name="fr_296"></a><a href="#fn_294"><font size="1">7</font></a> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khasamu</span> and <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dikhnunu</span> I traversed. To <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Bursip</span> <a name="fr_297"></a><a href="#fn_295"><font size="1">8</font></a> the stronghold of Akhuni</p>  
 <p>15. the son of Adini I approached. Akhuni the son of</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Adini trusted to the multitude of his troops and came to meet me. I utterly defeated him. In [his city]</p>  
 <p>16. I shut him up. From <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Bursip</span> I departed; in boats of seal-skin the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Euphrates</span> at its flood I crossed. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Al</span> (?) … <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">gâ</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tagi</span> …</p>  
 <p>17. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sûrunu</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Paripa</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Til-Basherê</span> <a name="fr_298"></a><a href="#fn_296"><font size="1">1</font></a> (and) <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dabigu</span>, six strongholds of Akhuni the son of Adini I [besieged], I captured. His numerous fighting-men</p>  
 <p>18. I slew: their spoil I carried away; 200 towns which (were) dependent on them I threw down, dug up (and) burned with fire. [From] <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dabigu</span> I (departed);</p>  
 <p>19. to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sazabê</span> the stronghold of Sangara the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Carchemishian</span> I approached. The city I besieged, I captured. Their numerous fighting-men I slew;</p>  
 <p>20. their spoil I carried away. The towns which (were) dependent on him I threw down, dug up (and) burned with fire. The kings of the country [of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Hittites</span>] all of them,</p>  
 <p>21. trembled before the splendour of my powerful weapons and my violent onset, and they took my feet. From … shun <a name="fr_299"></a><a href="#fn_297"><font size="1">2</font></a> the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Patinian</span></p>  
 <p>22. 3 talents of gold, 100 talents of silver, 300 talents of copper, 300 talents of iron, i000 vases of copper, i000 vestments of embroidered stuff (and) linen, his daughter</p>  
 <p>23. with her abundant dowry, 20 talents of blue purple, 300 oxen, (and) 5000 sheep, I received. A talent of gold, 2 talents of blue purple, (and) 100 logs of cedar</p>  
 <p>24. I imposed upon him as tribute; each year I receive (it) in my city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Assur</span>. From Khayânu the son</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="green">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p>of Gabbaru who (dwells) at the foot of mount <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Amanus</span> 10 talents of silver, 90 talents</p>  
 <p>25. of copper, 30 talents of iron, 300 vestments of embroidered stuff (and) linen, 300 oxen, 3000 sheep, 200 logs of cedar … 2 <i>homers</i> of cedar-resin</p>  
 <p>26. (and) his daughter with her dowry I received. I laid upon him as tribute 10 manehs of silver, 200 logs of cedar, (and) a <i>homer</i> of cedar-resin; each year</p>  
 <p>27. I receive (it). From Aramu the son of Agûsi 10 manehs of gold, 6 talents of silver, 500 oxen, (and) s000 sheep I received. From Sangara the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Carchemishian</span> 2 talents</p>  
 <p>28. of gold, 70 talents of silver, 30 talents of copper, 100 talents of iron, 20 talents of blue purple, 500 weapons, his daughter with a dowry, and 100 daughters of his nobles,</p>
