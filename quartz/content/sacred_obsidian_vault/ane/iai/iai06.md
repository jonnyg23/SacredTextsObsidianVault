 <h2 align="CENTER">COLUMN V</h2>
 <h3 align="CENTER">ISHTAR AND HER MAIDS IN THE FAVORITE HAUNT OF IZDUBAR</h3>
 <p>The king while hunting where a forest grows,<br>
 Around sweet hyacinths and budding rose,<br>
 Where a soft zephyr o'er them gently flows<br>
 From the dark <i>sik-ka-ti</i> <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">1</font></a> where Kharsak <a name="fr_57"></a><a href="#fn_57"><font size="1">2</font></a> glows;<br>
 And Sedu <a name="fr_58"></a><a href="#fn_58"><font size="1">3</font></a> softly dances on the leaves,<br>
 And a rich odorous breath from them receives;<br>
 Where tulips peep with heliotrope and pink,<br>
 With violets upon a gleaming brink<br>
 Of silver gliding o'er a water-fall<br>
 That sings its purling treasures o'er a wall<br>
 Of rugged onyx sparkling to the sea:<br>
 A spot where Zir-ri <a name="fr_59"></a><a href="#fn_59"><font size="1">4</font></a> sport oft merrily,<br>
 Where Hea's <a name="fr_60"></a><a href="#fn_60"><font size="1">5</font></a> arm outstretched doth form a bay,<br>
 Wild, sheltered, where his sea-daughters play;<br>
 A jasper rock here peeps above the waves<br>
 Of emerald hue; with them its summit laves.</p>
 <p>Around, above, this cool enchanting cove<br>
 Bend amorous, spicy branches; here the dove<br>
 Oft coos its sweetest notes to its own mate,<br>
 And fragrance pure, divine, the air doth freight,<br>
 To sport with gods no lovelier place is found,<br>
 With love alone the mystic woods resound.</p>

 <p>Here witching Zi-na-ki <a name="fr_61"></a><a href="#fn_61"><font size="1">6</font></a> oft drag within<br>
 The waves unwilling Zi-si; <a name="fr_62"></a><a href="#fn_62"><font size="1">7</font></a> here the din<br>
 Of roars of sullen storms is never known<br>
 When tempests make the mighty waters groan;<br>
 Nor sound of strife is heard, but rippling rills,<br>
 Or softest note of love, the breezes fills.</p>
 <p>And here the king in blissful dreams oft lies<br>
 'Mid pure ambrosial odors, and light flies<br>
 The tune in bliss; away from kingly care,<br>
 And hollow splendor of the courtly glare;<br>
 Away from triumphs, battle-fields afar,<br>
 The favorite haunt of huntsman Izdubar.</p>
 <p>The Queen of Love the glowing spot surveys,<br>
 And sees the monarch where he blissful lays;<br>
 And watching till he takes his bow and spear<br>
 To chase the wild gazelles now browsing near,<br>
 She, ere the king returns, near by arrives<br>
 With her two maids; with them for love connives,<br>
 joy and seduction thus voluptuous fly<br>
 Her Samkhatu, <a name="fr_63"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">8</font></a> Kharimtu <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_64"><font size="1">9</font></a> from the sky,<br>
 As gently, lightly as a spirit's wing<br>
 Oft carries gods to earth while Sedu sing.<br>
 Thus, they, with lightest step, expectant stood<br>
 Within this lovely spot beneath the wood.</p>
 <p>Their snowy limbs they bare, undraped now stand<br>
 Upon the rock at Ishtar's soft command.<br>
 Like marble forms endued with life they move,<br>
 And thrill the air with welcome notes of love.<br>
 The <i>its-tu-ri Same mut-tab-ri</i> <a name="fr_65"></a><a href="#fn_65"><font size="1">10</font></a> sang<br>
 Their sweetest notes, and the <i>Khar-san-u</i> <a name="fr_66"></a><a href="#fn_66"><font size="1">1</font></a> rang<br>
 With songs of thrushes, turtle-doves and Jays,<br>
 And linnets, with the nightingale's sweet lays,<br>
 Goldfinches, magpies and the wild hoopoes;<br>
 With cries of green-plumed parrots and cuckoos,</p>

 <p><a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 22</font></a></p>
 <p>Pee-wits and sparrows join the piercing cries<br>
 Of gorgeous herons, while now upward flies<br>
 The eagle screaming, joyful spreads his wings<br>
 Above the forest; and the woodchuck rings<br>
 A wild tattoo upon the trees around;<br>
 And humming-birds whirr o'er the flowering ground<br>
 In flocks, and beat the luscious laden air<br>
 With emerald and gold, and scarlet, where<br>
 These perfect forms with godly grace divine,<br>
 In loveliness upon the rock recline.<br>
 Sweet joy is slender formed, with bright black eyes<br>
 That sparkle oft and dance with joy's surprise;<br>
 Seduction, with her rare voluptuous form,<br>
 Enchanteth all till wildest passions warm<br>
 The blood and fire the eye beneath her charm;<br>
 All hearts in heaven and earth she doth disarm.<br>
 The Queen with every perfect charm displayed<br>
 Delights the eye, and fills the heart, dismayed<br>
 With fear, lest the bright phantom may dissolve<br>
 To airy nothingness, till fierce resolve<br>
 Fills each who her beholds, while love doth dart<br>
 From liquid eyes and captivates the heart.<br>
 She is the queen who fills the earth with love<br>
 And reigns unrivalled in her realms above.</p>
 <p>Beware, ye hearts! beware! who feel the snare<br>
 Of Ishtar, lest ye tread upon the air;<br>
 When ye her rosy chain of fragrance wear,<br>
 When blindness strikes the eye, and deaf the ear<br>
 Becomes, and heartstrings only lead you then,<br>
 Till ye return to common sense again;<br>
 Enthralled mayhap and captive led in chains<br>
 Ye then will leisure have to bear your pains;<br>
 Or if perchance a joy hath come to thee,<br>
 Through all thy joyous life, then happy be!</p>
