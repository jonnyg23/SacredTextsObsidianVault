 <p> <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_97"><font size="1">6</font></a>(<i>The first Column is badly mutilated, and all we can glean from it is that "as soon as something of morning has dawned," Gilgamish addressing Enkidu, compares him to a gazelle, and promises to glorify him. Then follows apparently a recital by Gilgamish of their exploits together, "mountains </i>[<i>we ascended</i>,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 40</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>we reachd</i>]<i> the Forest of Cedars, </i>[<i>travelling</i>]<i> night and day . . . </i>[<i>with wild beasts </i>(<i>?</i>)]<i> drawing nigh after us." Enkidu is lying dying or dead, and Column II begins with Gilgamish keening over his dead friend before the Elders of Erech</i>):</p>  
 <p>"Unto me hearken, O Elders, to me, aye, me [shall ye listen],<br> Tis that I weep for my [comrade] Enkidu, bitterly crying<br> Like to a wailing woman: my grip is [slackd] on the curtleaxe<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">5.</font></span>(Slung at) my thigh, (and) the brand at my belt from my sight [is removed].<br> (Aye, and) my festal attire [lends nought of its aid for] my pleasure,<br> Me, me hath [sorrow] assailed, and [cast] me [down in affliction].</p>
