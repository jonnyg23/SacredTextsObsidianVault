 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Epic of Gilgamish</i>, tr. by R. Campbell Thompson [1928], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE SECOND TABLET</h3>  <h4 align="center">OF THE MEETING OF GILGAMISH AND ENKIDU.</h4>  
 <p align="center"> <a name="fr_34"></a><a href="#fn_30"><font size="1">1</font></a><i>Column II</i>.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">2.</font></span>                          While Gilgamish (thus) is the vision revealing<br> Enkidu sitteth before the hetaera, and she [displaying <a name="fr_35"></a><a href="#fn_31"><font size="1">2</font></a> her] bosom,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">5.</font></span>Shewing [her beauty (?)], the place of his birth he <a name="fr_36"></a><a href="#fn_32"><font size="1">3</font></a> forgetteth.<br>                                          (So) Enkidu dallied<br> (Thus) for six days, seven nights, with the courtesan-girl in his mating.<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">10.</font></span>Broke into [speech] then, the nymph, and (thus) unto Enkidu spake she:<br> "(Yea, as) I view thee, (een) like a god, O Enkidu, shalt be,<br> Why with the beasts (of the field) dost thou (ever) range over the desert?<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">15.</font></span>Up, for I'll lead thee to [Erech] broad-marketed, (aye), to the Temple<br> Sacred, the dwelling of AnuO Enkidu, come, that I guide thee,<br> Unto E-Anna, the dwelling of Anu, where [Gilgamish] (liveth),<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">20.</font></span>(He), the supreme of creation; and thou, aye, thou wilt [embrace him]<br> Like [to a woman], (and een) [as] thyself thou shalt [love him].<br>                                          O, rouse thee<br> Up from the groundtis a shepherd's bed <a name="fr_37"></a><a href="#fn_33"><font size="1">4</font></a> (only)."<br>                                          Her utterance heard he,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">25.</font></span>Welcomed her rede: the advice of the woman struck home in his bosom.<br> She one garment took off wherewith she might clothe him: the other <a name="fr_38"></a><a href="#fn_34"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">30.</font></span>She herself wore, (and so) taking her hand like a brother <a name="fr_39"></a><a href="#fn_35"><font size="1">6</font></a> she led him<br> (Thus) to the booths(?) of the shepherds, the place of the sheepfolds. The shepherds<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">35.</font></span>Gather]d at sight of him <a name="fr_40"></a><a href="#fn_36"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p align="center"><i>Column III</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">(<i>How the Hetaera schooled Enkidu</i>).</p>  
 <p>He (in the past) of the milk of the wild things to suck was accustomd!<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">5.</font></span>Bread which she set before him he broke, but he gazed and he stared:<br> Enkidu bread did not know how to eat, nor had he the knowledge<br> Mead how to quaff!<br> 10.                    (Then) the woman made answer, to Enkidu speaking,<br> "Enkidu, taste of the bread, (for) of life tis; (forsooth), the essential,<br> Drink thou, (too), of the mead, tis the wonted use of the country."<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">15.</font></span>Enkidu ate of the bread, (aye, ate) until he was gorged,<br> Drank of the mead seven bumpers; his spirits rose, (and), exultant,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">20.</font></span>Glad was his heart, and cheerful his face: [himself(?)] was he rubbing,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">25.</font></span>Oil on the hair of his body anointed: and (thus) became human.<br> Donnd he a garment to be like a man <a name="fr_41"></a><a href="#fn_37"><font size="1">1</font></a>, (and) taking his weapon,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">30.</font></span>Hunted the lions, which harried the shepherds o nights: and the jackals<br> Caught he. (So) he, having mastered the lions, the shepherds slept soundly <a name="fr_42"></a><a href="#fn_38"><font size="1">2</font></a>.<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">35.</font></span>Enkidu(he) was their warden(becometh) a man of full vigour.<br> (Now) is one of the heroes speaking to [Gilgamish(?)] . . . .</p>  
 <p>(<i>About thirteen lines are missing, a gap in which a sinister figure has evidently appeared, sent evidently by Gilgamish to learn the meaning of the arrival of the strangers in Erech. Enkidu sees him and speaks</i>).</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Column IV</i>.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">10.</font></span>(Then while) he pleasured, he lifted his eyes, (and), observing the fellow,<br> Spake he unto the woman: "O doxy, bring me (this) fellow,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">15.</font></span>Why hath he come? I would know his intention."<br>                                         The woman the fellow<br> Calld that he come to him, that he might see him: "O, why art thou seeking,<br> Sir? (Pray), which is the way to thy rest-house?"<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">20.</font></span>                                        The man spake, addressing<br> Enkidu: "You to the House of Community <a name="fr_43"></a><a href="#fn_39"><font size="1">3</font></a> [Gilgamish calleth],<br> (This is) the custom of men, and a homage (too) to the great ones:</p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">25.</font></span>Come, then, and heap up the offerings such as are due to the city,<br> Come, on behalf of the common weal bring in the food of the city.<br> (Tis) for the king of broad-marketed Erech to look on thy greeting,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">30.</font></span>Gilgamish, king of broad-marketed Erech to look on thy greeting;<br> First doth he mate with the woman allotted by fate, and then after<br> Speak by the counsel of god, and so from the shape of the omens <a name="fr_44"></a><a href="#fn_40"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> (Utter the rede of) his destiny."<br>                                         (So) at the words of the fellow<br> Went they before him.</p>

 <p align="center"><i>Column V</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center">(<i>The Entry of Enkidu into Erech</i>).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">7.</font></span>[Enkidu] going [in front], with the courtesan coming behind him,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">10.</font></span>Enterd broad-marketed Erech; the populace gatherd behind him,<br> (Then), as he stoppd in the street of broad-marketed Erech, the people<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">15.</font></span>Thronging, behind him exclaimd "Of a truth, like to Gilgamish is he,<br> Shorter in stature a trifle, [his] composition is stronger.<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">20.</font></span>. . . . . . . [(once)] like a [weakling] baby he <i>suckd</i> the milk of the wild things!<br> Ever the bread-cakes in Erech give glorious (climax) to manhood!<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">25.</font></span>He a (mere) savage becometh a hero of proper appearance,<br> (Now) unto Gilgamish, god-like, his composition is equal."</p>  
 <p align="center">(<i>How Enkidu fought with Gilgamish for the Hetaera</i>).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">30.</font></span>Strewn is the couch for the love-rites, <a name="fr_45"></a><a href="#fn_41"><font size="1">2</font></a> and Gilgamish (now) in the night-time<br> Cometh to sleep, to delight in the woman <a name="fr_46"></a><a href="#fn_42"><font size="1">3</font></a>: (but) [Enkidu], coming<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">35.</font></span>(There) in the highway, doth block up the passage to Gilgamish, [threatning]<br> He with his strength . . . . .</p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">15.</font></span>Would not concede: they grappled and snorted(?) like bulls, (and) the threshold<br> Shatterd: the (very) wall quiverd as Gilgamish <a name="fr_47"></a><a href="#fn_43"><font size="1">1</font></a>, Enkidu grappled,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">20.</font></span>Snorting(?) like bulls, (and) the threshold they shatterd, the (very) wall quiver'd.</p>  
 <p align="center">(<i>The Birth of Friendship</i>).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">25.</font></span>Gilgamish bent his leg to the ground: (so) his fury abated,<br> (Aye, and) his ardour <a name="fr_48"></a><a href="#fn_44"><font size="1">2</font></a> was quelld: so soon as was quelled his ardour,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">30.</font></span>Enkidu (thus) unto Gilgamish spake: "(Of a truth), did thy mother<br> Bear thee as one, and one only: (that choicest) cow of the steer-folds,<br> <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">35.</font></span>Nin-sun <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_45"><font size="1">3</font></a> exalted thy head above heroes, and Enlil hath dower'd<br> Thee with the kingship oer men."</p>
