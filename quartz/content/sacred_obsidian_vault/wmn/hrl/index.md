 <p>
 Gilamn's feminist novel, <cite>Herland</cite> was one of a flood
 of utopian books at the turn of the 20th century where sex roles are inverted.
 Gilman imagined a society where women not only rule,
 but are the <i>only</i> gender.
 Reproducing by parthenogenesis, they worship a female deity.
 This particular book later became a must-read for the second-wave
 feminists, particularly the 'separatist' wing.
 </p>
