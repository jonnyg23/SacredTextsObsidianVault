 <p><a name="page_374"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 374</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">KHÂDIRA-G<i>RI</i>HYA-SÛTRA.</h1>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA I, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 1.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now henceforth the domestic sacrifices (will be explained).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">2</font></a>. During the northern course of the sun, at the time of the increasing moon, on auspicious days, before noon: this is the time at which the constellations are lucky, unless a special statement is given.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">3</font></a>. At the end (of the ceremonies) he should give to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as to eat according to his ability.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">4-6</font></a>. The sacrificial cord is made of a string or of Ku<i>s</i>a grass.</p>  
 <p>5. If he suspends it round his neck and raises the right arm (so as to wear the cord on his left shoulder), he becomes ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>opavîtin.</p>  
 <p>6. (If he raises) the left (arm and wears the cord on his right shoulder, he becomes) prâ<i>k</i>înâvîtin.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">7-10</font></a>. After having sipped water three times, let him wipe off the water twice.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">I, 1, 1. athâto g<i>ri</i>hyâkarmâ<i>n</i>y. 2. udagayanapûrvapakshapu<i>n</i>yâheshu prâg âvartanâd anubha<i>h</i> kâlo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>nâde<i>s</i>e. 3. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>pavarge yathotsâha<i>m</i> brâhma<i>n</i>ân â<i>s</i>ayed. 4. ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>opavîta<i>m</i> sautra<i>m</i> kau<i>s</i>a<i>m</i> vâ. 5. grîvâyâ<i>m</i> pratimu<i>k</i>ya dakshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> bâhum uddh<i>ri</i>tya ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>opavîtî bhavati. 6. savya<i>m</i> prâ<i>k</i>înâvîtî. 7. trir â<i>k</i>amyâpo dvi<i>h</i> parim<i>ri</i>gîta.</font></p>

 <p><a name="page_375"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 375</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Having besprinkled his feet (with water), let him besprinkle his head.</p>  
 <p>9. Let him touch the organs of his senses (i.e. his eyes, his nose, and his ears) with water (i.e. with a wet hand).</p>  
 <p>10. When he has finally touched (water) again, he becomes pure.</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">11-14</font></a>. (If) sitting, standing, or lying down (is prescribed), he should understand (that it is to be done) on northward-pointed Darbha grass, with the face turned to the east, to the west of the fire with which the sacrifice is performed.</p>  
 <p>12. If the word Snâna (or bathing) is used, (this refers to the whole body) with the head.</p>  
 <p>13. (The different ceremonies are) performed with the right hand, if no special rule is given.</p>  
 <p>14. If it is not clear where a Mantra ends, one should discern it by (adverting to) the beginning of the next Mantra.</p>  
 <p>15 <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">15</font></a>. The Mantras have the word svâhâ at their end, when offerings are made.</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">16</font></a>. The term Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a is used of every sacrifice that is performed with one fire.</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">17-18</font></a>. There the Brahman is (present as) officiating priest, with the exception of the morning and evening oblations.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">8. pâdâv abhyukshya <i>s</i>iro<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>bhyukshed. 9. indriyâ<i>n</i>y adbhi<i>h</i> sa<i>m</i>sp<i>ri</i><i>s</i>ed. 10. antata<i>h</i> pratyupasp<i>ri</i><i>s</i>ya <i>s</i>u<i>k</i>ir bhavaty. 11. âsanasthânasa<i>m</i>ve<i>s</i>anâny udagagreshu darbheshu prâṅmukhasya pratîyât pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>âd agner yatra homa syât. 12. saha<i>s</i>irasa<i>m</i> snâna<i>s</i>abde. 13. dakshi<i>n</i>ena pâ<i>n</i>inâ k<i>ri</i>tyam anâde<i>s</i>e. 14. mantrântam avyak ta<i>m</i> parasyâdigraha<i>n</i>ena vidyât. 15. svâhântâ mantra homeshu. 16. pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a ityâkhya ya<i>h</i> ka<i>s</i> <i>k</i>aikâgnau. 17. tatra <i>ri</i>tvig brahmâ sâyamprâtarhomavar<i>g</i>a<i>m</i>.</font></p>

 <p><a name="page_376"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 376</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. The Hot<i>ri</i>'s place is filled by (the sacrificer) himself.</p>  
 <p>19 <a name="fr_975"></a><a href="#fn_974"><font size="1">19</font></a>. To the south of the fire the Brahman sits facing the north, silently, until the oblation has been performed, on eastward-pointed (Darbha grass).</p>  
 <p>20 <a name="fr_976"></a><a href="#fn_975"><font size="1">20-22</font></a>. But if he likes, he may speak of what refers to the sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>21. Or if he has spoken (words) which are unworthy of the sacrifice, let him murmur the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis,</p>  
 <p>22. Or (the verse), 'Thus has Vish<i>n</i>u' (Sv. I, 222).</p>  
 <p>23 <a name="fr_977"></a><a href="#fn_976"><font size="1">23</font></a>. If he does himself the work both of the Brahman and of the Hot<i>ri</i>, let him sit down on the Brahman's seat, and (leave that seat) placing a parasol on it, or an outer garment, or a water-pot, and then let him perform his other duties.</p>  
 <p>24 <a name="fr_978"></a><a href="#fn_977"><font size="1">24</font></a>. Let him take care not to turn his back to, or become separated (by any person or thing interposed) from what belongs to the sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">18. svaya<i>m</i>hautra<i>m</i>. 19. dakshi<i>n</i>ato<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gner udaṅmukhas tûsh<i>n</i>îm âste brahmâ homât prâgagreshu. 20. kâma<i>m</i> tv adhiya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a<i>m</i> vyâhared. 21. aya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>iyâ<i>m</i> vâ vyâh<i>ri</i>tya ma<i>h</i>âvyâh<i>ri</i>tîr <i>g</i>aped. 22. ida<i>m</i> vish<i>n</i>ur ita vâ. 23. hautrabrahmatve svaya<i>m</i> kurvan brahmâsanam [sic] upavi<i>s</i>ya <i>kh</i>attram uttarâsaṅga<i>m</i> kama<i>n</i><i>d</i>alu<i>m</i> vâ tatra k<i>ri</i>tvâthânyat kuryâd. 24. avyâv<i>ri</i>tti<i>m</i> ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âṅgair avyavâya<i>m</i> <i>k</i>e<i>kh</i>et.</font></p>
