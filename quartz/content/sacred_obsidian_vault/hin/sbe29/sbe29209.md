 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_979"></a><a href="#fn_978"><font size="1">1</font></a>. In the eastern part of his dwelling he should besmear (the place on which the sacrifice will be</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">2, 1. pûrve bhâge ve<i>s</i>mano gomayenopalipya tasya madhyade<i>s</i>e laksha<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> kuryâd.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_377"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 377</font></a></p>  
 <p>performed) with cowdung, and should draw in the middle of it the lines.</p>  
 <p>2. To the south he should draw a line from west to east.</p>  
 <p>3. From the beginning of that line (he should draw a line) from south to north; from the end (of the last-mentioned line) one from west to east; between (the first and the third line) three (lines) from west to east.</p>  
 <p>4. He besprinkles that (place) with water,</p>  
 <p>5. Establishes the fire (thereon),</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_980"></a><a href="#fn_979"><font size="1">6-8</font></a>. Wipes along around (the fire) with the three verses, 'This praise' (MB. II, 4, 2-4).</p>  
 <p>7. To the west of the fire he touches the earth with his two hands turned downwards, with (the verse), 'We partake of the earths' (MB. II, 4, 1).</p>  
 <p>8. In night-time (he pronounces that Mantra so that it ends with the word) 'goods' (vasu).</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_981"></a><a href="#fn_980"><font size="1">9-11</font></a>. Having strewn Darbha grass to the west (of the fire), let him draw (some grass) from the south-end and from the north-end (of what he has strewn), in an eastern direction.</p>  
 <p>10. Or let him omit this drawing (of Darbha grass to the east),</p>  
 <p>11. And let him strew (the grass) beginning in the east, so as to keep his right side turned to the</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">2. dakshi<i>n</i>ata<i>h</i> prâ<i>k</i>î<i>m</i> rekhâm ullikhya. 3. tadârambhâd udî<i>k</i>î<i>m</i> tadavasânât prâ<i>k</i>î<i>m</i> tisro madhye prâ<i>k</i>îs. 4. tad abhyukshyâ. 5. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gnim upasamâdhâya. 6. ima<i>m</i> stomam iti parisamûhya t<i>ri</i><i>k</i>ena. 7. pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>âd agner bhûmau nya<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>au pâ<i>n</i>î k<i>ri</i>tvedam bhûmer iti. 8. vasvanta<i>m</i> râtrau. 9. pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>âd darbhân âstîrya dakshi<i>n</i>ata<i>h</i> prâ<i>k</i>î<i>m</i> prakarshed uttarata<i>s</i> <i>k</i>â. 10. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>prak<i>ri</i>shya vâ. 11. pûrvopakrama<i>m</i> pradakshi<i>n</i>am agni<i>m</i> st<i>ri</i><i>n</i>uyân mûlâny agrai<i>s</i> <i>kh</i>âdayan triv<i>ri</i>ta<i>m</i> pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>av<i>ri</i>ta<i>m</i> vo.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_378"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 378</font></a></p>  
 <p>fire, covering the roots (of the Darbha blades) with the points, in three layers or in five layers.</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_982"></a><a href="#fn_981"><font size="1">12-16</font></a>. Sitting down he cuts off two span-long Darbha points, not with his nail, with (the words), 'Purifiers are ye, sacred to Vish<i>n</i>u.'</p>  
 <p>13. He wipes them with water, with (the words), 'By Vish<i>n</i>u's mind are ye purified.'</p>  
 <p>14. Holding them with his two thumbs and fourth fingers so that their points are turned to the north, he three times purifies the Â<i>g</i>ya (with them), with (the words), 'May the god Savit<i>ri</i> purify thee with this uninjured purifier, with the rays of the good sun.'</p>  
 <p>15. Having sprinkled them (with water) he should throw them into the fire.</p>  
 <p>16. Having put the Â<i>g</i>ya on the fire he should take it (from the fire) towards the north</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_983"></a><a href="#fn_982"><font size="1">17-21</font></a>. Bending his right knee he should pour out to the south of the fire his joined hands full of water with (the words), 'Aditi! Give thy consent!'</p>  
 <p>18. To the west with (the words), Anumati! Give thy consent!'</p>  
 <p>19. To the north with (the words), 'Sarasvatî! Give thy consent!'</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">12. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>pavi<i>s</i>ya darbhâgre prâde<i>s</i>amâtre pra<i>kh</i>inatti na nakhena pavitre stho vaish<i>n</i>avyâv ity. 13. adbhir unm<i>ri</i>gya Vish<i>n</i>or manasâ pûte stha ity. 14. udagagre<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>ngush<i>th</i>âbhyâm anâmikâbhyâ<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a sa<i>m</i>g<i>ri</i>hya trir â<i>g</i>yam utpunâti devas tvâ Savitotpunâtv a<i>kh</i>idre<i>n</i>a pavitre<i>n</i>a vasos sûryasya ra<i>s</i>mibhir ity. 15. abhyukshyâgnâv anuprahared. 16. â<i>g</i>yam adhi<i>s</i>rityottarata<i>h</i> kuryâd. 17. dakshi<i>n</i>a<i>g</i>ânvakto dakshi<i>n</i>enâgnim Adite<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>numanyasvety udakâ<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ali<i>m</i> prasi<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ed. 18. Anumate<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>numanyasveti pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>ât. 19. Sarasvate [sic, comp. Hira<i>n</i>yake<i>s</i>i-G<i>ri</i>hya I, 1, 2, 9] <i>n</i>umanyasvety uttarata<i>h</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_379"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 379</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. With (the words), 'God Savit<i>ri</i>! Give thy impulse!' (MB. I, 1, 1) he should sprinkle (water) round the fire so as to keep his right side turned towards it, encompassing what he is going to offer (with the water).</p>  
 <p>21. (This he does) once or thrice.</p>  
 <p>22 <a name="fr_984"></a><a href="#fn_983"><font size="1">22</font></a>. He puts a piece of wood on (the fire).</p>  
 <p>23 <a name="fr_985"></a><a href="#fn_984"><font size="1">23</font></a>. He should murmur the Prapada formula (MB. II, 4, 5), hold his breath, fix his thoughts on something good, and should emit his breath when beginning the Virûpâksha formula (MB. II, 4, 6).</p>  
 <p>24 <a name="fr_986"></a><a href="#fn_985"><font size="1">24-25</font></a>. At ceremonies for the attainment of special wishes (he should do so) for each of the objects (which he wishes to attain).</p>  
 <p>25. He should do so always at sacrifices.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">20. deva Savita<i>h</i> prasuveti pradakshi<i>n</i>am agni<i>m</i> paryukshed abhipariharan havya<i>m</i>. 21. sak<i>ri</i>t trir vâ. 22. samidham âdhaya. 23. prapada<i>m</i> <i>g</i>apitvopatâmya kalyâ<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> dhyâyan vairûpâksham ârabhyo<i>k</i><i>kh</i>vaset. 24. pratikâma<i>m</i> kâmyeshu. 25. sarvatraitad dhomeshu kuryât.</font></p>
