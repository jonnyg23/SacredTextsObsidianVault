 <p><a name="page_389"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 389</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA II, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 1.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1004"></a><a href="#fn_1003"><font size="1">1-3</font></a>. Of the sacrifices of the new and full moon, the full-moon sacrifice should be performed first.</p>  
 <p>2. If (the term for the sacrifice) of the new moon comes first, he should first celebrate the full-moon sacrifice and then perform that.</p>  
 <p>3. Some say that he should not perform it, and wait till the day of the full moon.</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_1005"></a><a href="#fn_1004"><font size="1">4</font></a>. In the afternoon, husband and wife, after having bathed, should eat fast-day food.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_1006"></a><a href="#fn_1005"><font size="1">5-8</font></a>. Mânadantavya has said: 'He who eats fast-day food, obtains offspring better than himself; he gains favour; hunger will not attack him.'</p>  
 <p>6. Therefore one should eat (fast-day food) which he likes.</p>  
 <p>7. He should do nothing unholy (such as cohabiting with his wife).</p>  
 <p>8. After he has sacrificed the morning oblation,</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="#fn_1006"><font size="1">9-16</font></a>. He should pour out the sacrificial food with (the formula), 'Agreeable to such and such (a deity) I pour thee out:' (this formula) referring to the</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">II, 1, 1. paur<i>n</i>amâsopakramau dar<i>s</i>apaur<i>n</i>amâsau. 2. dâr<i>s</i>a<i>m</i> <i>k</i>et pûrvam upapadyeta paur<i>n</i>amâsenesh<i>t</i>vâtha tat kuryâd. 3. akurvan paur<i>n</i>amâsîm âkâṅkshed ity eke. 4. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>parâh<i>n</i>e snâtvaupavasathika<i>m</i> dampatî bhu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>îyâtâm. 5. Mânadantavya uvâ<i>k</i>a: <i>s</i>reyasî<i>m</i> pra<i>g</i>â<i>m</i> vindate kâmyo bhavaty akshodhuko ya aupavasathika<i>m</i> bhuṅkte. 6. tasmâd yat kâmayeta tad bhu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>îta. 7. nâvratyam â<i>k</i>aret. 8. prâtarâhuti<i>m</i> hutvâ 9. havir nirvaped amushmai tvâ <i>g</i>ush<i>t</i>a<i>m</i> nirvapâmîti devatâ<i>s</i>raya<i>m</i> sak<i>ri</i>d ya<i>g</i>ur vâ dvis tûsh<i>n</i>î<i>m</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_390"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 390</font></a></p>  
 <p>deity, or a Ya<i>g</i>us, (is repeated) once; twice (it is done) silently.</p>  
 <p>10. He should wash (the food) thrice, (if it is destined) for gods;</p>  
 <p>11. Twice, if for men;</p>  
 <p>12. Once, if for the Fathers.</p>  
 <p>13. Stirring it up with the pot-ladle from the left to the right he should cook it.</p>  
 <p>14. When he has cooked it, he should sprinkle (Â<i>g</i>ya) on it, should take it from the fire towards the north, and should again sprinkle (Â<i>g</i>ya) on it.</p>  
 <p>15. Thus all kinds of Havis (are prepared).</p>  
 <p>16. Having put (the Havis) on the sacrificial grass,</p>  
 <p>17 <a name="fr_1008"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">17-27</font></a>. He should sacrifice the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions (in the following way): Having taken four portions of Â<i>g</i>yafive portions are taken by the Bh<i>ri</i>gus (or at least?) by the <i>G</i>âmadagnyas [see Indische Studien, 10, 95](he should make two oblations), to the north with (the formula), 'To Agni Svâhâ!' to the south with (the formula), 'To Soma Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>18. Others (do it) conversely.</p>  
 <p>19. Having 'spread under' Â<i>g</i>ya, he should cut off with the pot-ladle (portions) of the Havis from the middle and from the eastern side;</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">10. trir devebhya<i>h</i> prakshâlayed. 11. dvir manushyebhya<i>h</i>. 12. sak<i>ri</i>t pit<i>ri</i>bhyo. 13. meksha<i>n</i>ena pradakshi<i>n</i>am udâyuva<i>ñ</i> <i>s</i>rapaye<i>k</i>. 14. <i>kh</i><i>ri</i>tam abhighâryodag udvâsya pratyabhighârayet. 15. sarvâ<i>n</i>y eva<i>m</i> havî<i>m</i>shi. 16. barhishy âsâdyâ. 17. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gyabhâgau <i>g</i>uhuyâ<i>k</i> <i>k</i>aturg<i>ri</i>hîtam â<i>g</i>ya<i>m</i> g<i>ri</i>hîtvâ pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âvatta<i>m</i> Bh<i>ri</i>gû<i>n</i>â<i>m</i> <i>G</i>âmadagnyânâm Agnaye svâhety uttarata<i>h</i> Somâyeti dakshi<i>n</i>ato. 18. viparîtam itara. 19. â<i>g</i>yam upastîrya havisho<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>vadyen meksha<i>n</i>ena madhyât purastâd iti.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_391"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 391</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. One who takes five cut-off portions (see Sûtra 17), also from the western side.</p>  
 <p>21. After he has sprinkled (Â<i>g</i>ya) on (the cut-off portions), he anoints the places from which he has cut them off, (with Â<i>g</i>ya).</p>  
 <p>22. (This anointing) is omitted at the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation.</p>  
 <p>23. He should sacrifice with (the formula), 'To N.N. Svâhâ!'according to the god to whom the oblation belongs.</p>  
 <p>24. At the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation he 'spreads under' oncetwice if he is a Bh<i>ri</i>gu(cuts off) once (a portion) of the Havis, sprinkles (Â<i>g</i>ya) on it twice, and sacrifices it in a north-eastern direction with (the formula), 'To Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>25. Having put a piece of wood (on the fire),</p>  
 <p>26. He should dip Darbha-blades (of the sacrificial grass strewn round the fire) three times, the points, the middle, and the roots, into the Â<i>g</i>ya or into the Havis with (the words), 'May the birds come, licking what has been anointed.' Then, after having sprinkled (those Darbha-blades with water), he should throw them into the fire, with (the verse), 'Thou who art the lord of cattle, Rudra, who walkest with the lines (of cattle), the manly one: do no harm to our cattle; let this be offered to thee. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">20. pa<i>s</i><i>k</i>a<i>k</i> <i>k</i>a pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âvatty. 21. abhighârya pratyanakty avadânasthânâni. 22. na svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>to. 23. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>mushmai svâheti <i>g</i>uhuyâd yaddevatya<i>m</i> syât. 24. svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>ta<i>h</i> sak<i>ri</i>d upastîrya dvir Bh<i>ri</i>gû<i>n</i>â<i>m</i> sak<i>ri</i>d dhavisho [sic] dvir abhighâryâgnaye svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>te svâheti prâgudî<i>k</i>yâ<i>m</i> guhuyât. 25. samidham âdhâya. 26. darbhân â<i>g</i>ye havishi vi trir avadhâyâgramadhyamûlâny akta<i>m</i> rihâ<i>n</i>â viyantu vaya ity abhyukshyâgnâv anuprahared ya<i>h</i> pa<i>s</i>ûnâm adhipatî Rudras tanti<i>k</i>aro v<i>ri</i>shâ pa<i>s</i>ûn asmâ<i>k</i>a<i>m</i> mâ hi<i>m</i>sîr etad astu hutan tava svâheti.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_392"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 392</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. This (ceremony is called) Ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avâstu.</p>  
 <p>28 <a name="fr_1009"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">28</font></a>. He should perform it at all (sacrifices).</p>  
 <p>29 <a name="fr_1010"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">29-31</font></a>. The remnants of the Havis he should take away in a northern direction, and should give them to the Brahman.</p>  
 <p>30. A full vessel constitutes the fee for the sacrifice;</p>  
 <p>31. Or as much as he can afford.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">27. tad ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avâstu. 28. sarvatra kuryâd. 29. dhavir u<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ish<i>t</i>am udag udvâsya brahma<i>n</i>e dadyât. 30. pûr<i>n</i>apâtra<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>â. 31. yathotsâha<i>m</i> vâ.</font></p>
