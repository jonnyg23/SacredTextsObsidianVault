 <p><a name="page_161"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 161</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ. 2.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now he should make oblations in the evening and in the morning of prepared sacrificial food,</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">2</font></a>. To the deities of the Agnihotra, to Soma Vanaspati, to Agni and Soma, to Indra and Agni, to Heaven and Earth, to Dhanvantari, to Indra, to the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, to Brahman.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">3</font></a>. He says Svâhâ, and then he offers the Balis</p>  
 <p>4. To those same deities, to the waters, to the herbs and trees, to the house, to the domestic deities, to the deities of the ground (on which the house stands),</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">5</font></a>. To Indra and Indra's men, to Yama and Yama's men, to Varu<i>n</i>a and Varu<i>n</i>a's men, to Soma and Soma's menthese (oblations he makes) to the different quarters (of the horizon, of which those are the presiding deities).</p>  
 <p>6. To Brahman and Brahman's men in the middle,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_162"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 162</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. To the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, to all day-walking beingsthus by day;</p>  
 <p>8. To the night-walking (beings)thus at night.</p>  
 <p>9. To the Rakshasthus to the north.</p>  
 <p>10. Svadhâ to the fathers (i.e. Manes)'with these words he should pour out the remnants to the south, with the sacrificial cord suspended over the right shoulder.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_362"></a><a href="sbe29101.htm#fr_363">161:1</a> 2, 1. This is the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva sacrifice; comp. <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya II, 14, &amp;C.</p>

 <p>(3) Then (follows) the offering of the Balis.</p></dir>
