 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_497"></a><a href="#fn_496"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On the eighth days of the four dark fortnights of (the two seasons of) winter and <i>S</i>i<i>s</i>ira the Ash<i>t</i>akâs (are celebrated).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_498"></a><a href="#fn_497"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Or on one (of these days).</p>  
 <p>3. The day before, he should offer to the Fathers (i.e. Manes)</p>  
 <p>4. Boiled rice, boiled rice with sesamum seeds, rice-milk</p>  
 <p>5. Or cakes made of four <i>S</i>arâvas (of ground grain)</p>  
 <p>6. Sacrificing with the eight (verses), 'May the lower (Fathers) and the higher arise' (Rig-veda X, 15, 1 seqq.), or with as many (verses) as he likes.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_499"></a><a href="#fn_498"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Then on the next day the Ash<i>t</i>akâs (are celebrated) with an animal (sacrifice) and with a mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>8. He may also give grass to an ox,</p>  
 <p>9. Or he may burn down brushwood with fire</p>  
 <p>10. With (the words), 'This is my Ash<i>t</i>akâ.'</p>  
 <p>11. But he should not omit celebrating the Ash<i>t</i>akâ.</p>  
 <p>12. This (Ash<i>t</i>akâ) some state to be sacred to the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, some to Agni, some to the Sun, some to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, some state that the Night is its deity, some that the Nakshatras are, some that the Seasons are, some that the Fathers are, some that cattle is.</p>  
 <p>13 <a name="fr_500"></a><a href="#fn_499"><font size="1">13</font></a>. Having killed the animal according to the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>  
 <p>ritual of the animal sacrifice, omitting the sprinkling (with water) and the touching of the animal with a fresh branch, he should draw out the omentum and sacrifice it with (the verse), 'Carry the omentum, <i>G</i>âtavedas, to the Fathers, where thou knowest them resting afar. May streams of fat flow to them; may all these wishes be fulfilled. Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>14 <a name="fr_501"></a><a href="#fn_500"><font size="1">14</font></a>. Then (follow oblations) of the Avadâna portions and the cooked food, two with (the two verses), 'Agni, lead us on a good path to wealth' (Rig-veda I, 189, 1 seq.), (and other oblations with the texts), 'May summer, winter, the seasons be happy to us, happy the rainy season, safe to us the autumn. The year be our lord who gives breath to us; may days and nights produce long life. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'Peaceful be the earth, happy the air, may the goddess Heaven give us safety. Happy be the quarters (of the horizon), the intermediate quarters, the upper quarters; may the waters, the lightnings protect us from all sides. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'May the waters, the rays carry our prayers (to the gods); may the creator, may the ocean turn away evil; may the past and the future, (may) all be safe to me. Protected by Brahman may I pour forth songs. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'May all the Âdityas and the divine Vasus, the Rudras, the protectors, the Maruts sit down (here). May Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the abounding one, the highest ruler, bestow vigour, offspring, immortality on me. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'Pra<i>g</i>âpati, no other one than Thou (Rig-veda X, 121, 10).'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. The eighth (oblation) is that to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p>16 <a name="fr_502"></a><a href="#fn_501"><font size="1">16</font></a>. He should give to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as to eat: this has been said.</p>
