 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 14.</h3>  
 <p>1. When going to cross water, he performs the Svastyayana (ceremony for lucky progress).</p>  
 <p>2. He sacrifices thrice with his joined hands full of water into the waters, with the words, 'Adoration to the Sea, the child of the reed! Adoration to Varu<i>n</i>a, the lord of righteousness! Adoration to all rivers!'</p>  
 <p>3. Murmuring, 'May Vi<i>s</i>vakarman, the father of them all, relish the food offered.'</p>  
 <p>4. Against the stream for flowing (waters); up into the air for standing ones.</p>  
 <p>5. Should he while crossing apprehend any danger, let him murmur the hymn of Vasish<i>th</i>a, 'The eldest of which is the sea' (Rig-veda VII, 49); this (will serve to him as) a boat.</p>
