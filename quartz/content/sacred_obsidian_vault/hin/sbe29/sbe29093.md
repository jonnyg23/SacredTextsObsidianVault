 <p><a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 141</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ADHYÂYA VI, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 1.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_320"></a><a href="#fn_319"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now, after having paid reverence to Brahman, to the Brahma<i>ri</i>shi, to (those who descend from) Brahman's womb, to Indra, Pra<i>g</i>âpati, Vasish<i>th</i>a, Vâmadeva, Kahola Kaushîtaki, Mahâkaushîtaki, Suya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana, Â<i>s</i>valâyana, Aitareya, Mahaitareya, Kâtyâyana, <i>S</i>â<i>t</i>yâyana, <i>S</i>âkalya, Babhru, Bâbhravya, Ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>u, Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>avya, and to all the teachers of the past, we will henceforth explain the rules for the Âra<i>n</i>yaka as forming the subject of Svâdhyâya (private recitation of a text).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_321"></a><a href="#fn_320"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The teacher abstains through one day and one night from sexual intercourse and from eating flesh.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_322"></a><a href="#fn_321"><font size="1">3-5</font></a>. Raw flesh, a Ka<i>n</i><i>d</i>âla, a woman that has lately been confined, or that has her courses, seeing blood or persons whose hands have been cut off: (these persons and things he shall know form) impediments for the study.</p>  
 <p>4. And of the corpse-like (animals?).</p>  
 <p>5. Those which enter (their dens?) with the mouth first (?).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 142</font></a></p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_323"></a><a href="sbe29094.htm#fn_323"><font size="1">6</font></a>. When he has vomited, or when his beard has been shaved,</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_324"></a><a href="sbe29094.htm#fn_324"><font size="1">7</font></a>. When he has eaten flesh or partaken of a <i>S</i>râddha or birth dinner,</p>  
 <p>8. During the days that immediately follow on (days of) study in the village,</p>  
 <p>9. Three nights (and days), if (he has been) put out of order,</p>  
 <p>10. (Or has been violently) seized by others,</p>  
 <p>11. And during the second half of the days that precede (?) the Parvan days,</p>  
 <p>12. And if fire-flames, lightning, thunder, (heavy) rains, and great clouds appear,</p>  
 <p>13. And if a storm (blows) that carries away pebbles, as long as that lasts.</p>  
 <p>2, 1. During four months after the full moon of Âshâ<i>dh</i>a let him not study.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_325"></a><a href="#fn_322"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Especially the <i>S</i>akvarî verses (are concerned by what has been declared). Such are the rules.</p>
