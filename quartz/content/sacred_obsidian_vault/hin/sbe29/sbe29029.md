 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 27.</h3>  
 <p>1. In the sixth month the Annaprâ<i>s</i>ana (i.e. the first feeding with solid food).</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_114"></a><a href="#fn_113"><font size="1">2-6</font></a>. Goat's flesh, if he is desirous of nourishment,</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_115"></a><a href="#fn_114"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Flesh of partridge, if desirous of holy lustre,</p>  
 <p>4. Fish, if desirous of swiftness,</p>  
 <p>5. Boiled rice with ghee, if desirous of splendour</p>  
 <p>6. (Such) food, prepared with milk curds, honey, and ghee, he should give (to the child) to eat.</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_116"></a><a href="#fn_115"><font size="1">7</font></a>. After he has made oblations with (the verses), 'Lord of food, give us food, painless and strong; bring forward the giver; bestow power on us, on men and animals;' 'Whatsoever' (Rig-veda IV, 12, 4); 'Even of great' (ibid. 5), 'Him, Agni, (lead) to long life and splendour; sharp strength (mayst thou), Varu<i>n</i>a, king Soma, protection may Aditi, like a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 55</font></a></p>  
 <p>mother, afford to him, and all the gods that he may reach old age'</p>  
 <p>8. And has recited over (the child) the verse, 'Powers of life, Agni' (Rig-veda IX, 66, 19),</p>  
 <p>9. And has set him down on northward pointed Ku<i>s</i>a grass with (the verse), 'Be soft, O earth' (Rig-veda I, 22, 15)</p>  
 <p>10. The act of feeding is performed with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis.</p>  
 <p>11. Let the mother eat the remnant.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_113"></a><a href="sbe29029.htm#fr_114">54:2-6</a> 27, 2-6. These rules stand here, in the beginning of the chapter, as introductory remarks; the act of feeding itself (Sûtra 10) does not follow till after the sacrifice and the other performances prescribed in Sûtras 7-9.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_114"></a><a href="sbe29029.htm#fr_115">54:3</a> This rule evidently rests on the allusion of taittira (partridge flesh) to the Taittirîya school.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_115"></a><a href="sbe29029.htm#fr_116">54:7</a> Both metre and construction show that the Pâda imam Agna âyushe var<i>k</i>ase is incomplete; the <i>S</i>âmbavya-G<i>ri</i>hya and Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ II, 3, 10, 3 add k<i>ri</i>dhi after var<i>k</i>ase.</p>
