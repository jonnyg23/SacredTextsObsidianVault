 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 10.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_64"></a><a href="#fn_63"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Forms the standard for all sacrifices that procure happiness,</p>  
 <p>2. And for all Â<i>g</i>ya offerings,</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_65"></a><a href="#fn_64"><font size="1">3</font></a>. For the sacrifice of animals which are tied to a branch,</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_66"></a><a href="#fn_65"><font size="1">4</font></a>. And for the offerings of boiled (rice) grains and of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_67"></a><a href="#fn_66"><font size="1">5</font></a>. These are performed, all the offerings of cooked food, without PRAYÂ<i>G</i>A and ANUYÂ<i>G</i>A oblations, without (the invocation of) the I<i>L</i>Â, without NIGADA recitation, and without SÂMIDHENÎ verses.</p>  
 <p>6. There are also the following <i>S</i>lokas:</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 31</font></a></p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_68"></a><a href="#fn_67"><font size="1">7</font></a>. '(An oblation is called) HUTA, (if made) by the performing of the Agnihotra; AHUTA (i.e. unsacrificed, if) by the Bali offering; PRAHUTA (i.e. sacrificed up, if) by a sacrifice to the Manes; PRÂ<i>S</i>ITA (i.e. tasted, if) deposited as an offering in a Brâhma<i>n</i>a.</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_69"></a><a href="#fn_68"><font size="1">8</font></a>. 'Without raising his knees, with spread knees let him always offer his oblation; for the gods never accept an offering (that has been made holding the hand) not between (the knees).</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_70"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">9</font></a>. 'But when he has repeated a text sacred to Rudra, to the Rakshas, to the Manes, to the Asuras, or that contains an imprecation, let him touch water, and so also when he has touched his own body.'</p>
