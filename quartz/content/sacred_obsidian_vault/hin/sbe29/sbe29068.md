 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A. 6.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_258"></a><a href="#fn_257"><font size="1">1</font></a>. On the first day of the bright fortnight of Mâgha,</p>  
 <p>2. To the north-east,</p>  
 <p>3. In a place covered with herbs,</p>  
 <p>4. Having murmured the hymns sacred to the Sun, 'Upwards that <i>G</i>âtavedas' (Rig-veda I, 50), 'The bright face of the gods' (I, 115), Adoration to Mitra's (eye)' (X, 37), 'From the sky (where he dwells) may Sûrya protect us' (X, 158),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 115</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. And having thrown clods of earth (on the ground) to the different quarters (of the horizon), from the left to the right, with the hymn, 'A ruler indeed' (Rig-veda X, 152), verse by verse,</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_259"></a><a href="#fn_258"><font size="1">6_6</font></a>. And having satiated (with water) the <i>Ri</i>shis, the metres, the deities, faith and insight, and the fathers man by man,</p>  
 <p>7. They interrupt (the study of) the hymns for six months and a half,</p>  
 <p>8. Or for five and a half.</p>  
 <p>9. But if they (wish to) recite them (nevertheless), let the recitation go on after a pause of one day and one night.</p>
