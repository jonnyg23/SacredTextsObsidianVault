 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_811"></a><a href="#fn_810"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Through a period of three nights (after the Samâvartana) he should keep (the following) observances.</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_812"></a><a href="#fn_811"><font size="1">2</font></a>. He shall eat no flesh and not drink out of an earthen vessel.</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_813"></a><a href="#fn_812"><font size="1">3</font></a>. He shall avoid seeing women, <i>S</i>ûdras, dead bodies, black birds, and dogs, and shall not talk to (such beings).</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_814"></a><a href="#fn_813"><font size="1">4</font></a>. He shall not eat funeral food, or food of a</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_319"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 319</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> <i>S</i>ûdra, or of a woman lying-in (during the period of her impurity).</p>  
 <p>5. He shall not void urine or excrements, or spit out in the sun-shine, and shall riot cover himself against the sun.</p>  
 <p>6. He shall take warm water for (the rites) in which water is wanted.</p>  
 <p>7. At night he shall eat by the light (of a lamp or a fire-brand).</p>  
 <p>8. Or only speaking the truth (suffices instead of the other observances).</p>  
 <p>9 <a name="fr_815"></a><a href="#fn_814"><font size="1">9</font></a>. Also a person who has received the dîkshâ (or inauguration for a Soma sacrifice), should observe these rules beginning from (that which regards) the sun-shine (Sûtra 5), if he performs the Pravargya ceremony.</p>
