 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 7.</h3>  
 <p>1. When he then returns from his journey, he looks at his house (and says),</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">2</font></a>. 'House, do not fear, do not tremble; bringing strength we come back. Bringing strength, joyful and wise, I come back to thee, to the house, rejoicing in my mind.</p>  
 <p>'That of which the traveller thinks, that in which dwells much joy, that I call the house. May it know us as we know it.</p>  
 <p>'Hither are called the cows; hither are called goats and sheep; and the sweet essence (?) of food is called hither to our house.'</p>  
 <p>3. Having approached the (sacred) domestic fire with the verse, 'This Agni is glorious to us, this is highly glorious. Worshipping him (?) may we suffer no harm; may he bring us to supremity'</p>  
 <p>4. Let him pronounce auspicious words.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_214"><font size="1">5</font></a>. When accepting the water for washing the feet he says, 'The milk of Virâ<i>g</i> art thou; may I obtain</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_98"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 98</font></a></p>  
 <p>the milk of Virâ<i>g</i>; in me (may) the milk of Padyâ Virâ<i>g</i> (dwell)!'</p>
