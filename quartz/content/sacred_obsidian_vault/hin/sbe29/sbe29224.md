 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 2.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">1-5</font></a>. Having kept the vow (of fasting) through one fortnight, he should, on the first day of the dark fortnight, feed the Brâhma<i>n</i>as with boiled milk-rice prepared of one Ka<i>m</i>sa of rice.</p>  
 <p>2. The small grains of that (rice) he should sacrifice (day by day) at the evening twilight to the west of the village, on a place which he has besmeared (with cowdung), with the formula, 'To Phala' (MB. II, 5, 17).</p>  
 <p>3. And with (the formula), 'To Phalla!' The same on the first day of the next dark fortnight.</p>  
 <p>4. He shall observe chastity till the end (of the rite).</p>  
 <p>5. A hundred cart-loads (of gold) will be his.</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">6-23</font></a>. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a should elect the site for building his house on white ground, a Kshatriya on red, a Vai<i>s</i>ya on black, which should be even, covered with grass, not salinous, not dry</p>  
 <p>7. Where the water flows off to the north-west.</p>  
 <p>8. (Plants) with milky juice or with thorns or acrid plants should not be there.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">2, 1. ardhamâsavratî tâmisrâdau brâhma<i>n</i>ân â<i>s</i>ayed vrîhika<i>m</i>saudana<i>m</i>. 2. tasya ka<i>n</i>ân aparâsu sandhyâsu pratyag grâmât stha<i>n</i><i>d</i>ilam upalipya Phalâyeti <i>g</i>uhuyât. 3. Phallâyeti <i>k</i>aivam evâparasmi<i>m</i>s tâmisrâdau. 4. brahma<i>k</i>aryam â samâpter. 5. â<i>k</i>ita<i>s</i>ata<i>m</i> bhavati. 6. gaure bhûmibhâge brâhma<i>n</i>o lohite kshatriya<i>h</i> k<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>e vai<i>s</i>yo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>vasâna<i>m</i> <i>g</i>oshayet sama<i>m</i> loma<i>s</i>am anîri<i>n</i>am a<i>s</i>ushka<i>m</i>. 7. yatrodaka<i>m</i> pratyagudî<i>k</i>î<i>m</i> pravartate. 8. kshîri<i>n</i>a<i>h</i> ka<i>n</i><i>t</i>akina<i>h</i> [sic] ka<i>t</i>ukâ<i>s</i> <i>k</i>âtraushadhayo na syur.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_429"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 429</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (Soil) on which Darbha-grass grows, brings holy lustre;</p>  
 <p>10. Big sorts of grass, strength;</p>  
 <p>11. Tender grass, cattle.</p>  
 <p>12. Or (the site of the house) should have the form of bricks (?) or of . . . . . . . (?)</p>  
 <p>13. Or there should be natural holes (in the ground) in all directions.</p>  
 <p>14, 15. (A house) with its door to the east brings wealth and fame; with its door to the north, children and cattle. By one with its door to the south all wishes (are obtained). The back-door should not face (?) the house-door.</p>  
 <p>16. Milk-rice should be offered,</p>  
 <p>17. And a black cow,</p>  
 <p>18. Or a white goat. Or only milk-rice.</p>  
 <p>19. In the middle of the house he should sacrifice the fat (of the animal) and the milk-rice, mixed with Â<i>g</i>ya, taking eight portions (of that mixture), with (the verse), 'Vâstoshpati!' (MB. II, 6, I.)</p>  
 <p>20. And with the seven last (texts) used at the driving away of misfortune (see above, chap. I, 20, 21).</p>  
 <p>21. After he has sacrificed, he should distribute Balis in the different directions (of the horizon).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">9. darbhasammita<i>m</i> brahmavar<i>k</i>asya<i>m</i>. 10. b<i>ri</i>hatt<i>ri</i><i>n</i>air balyam. 11. m<i>ri</i>dut<i>ri</i><i>n</i>ai<i>h</i> pa<i>s</i>avya<i>m</i>. 12. <i>s</i>âtâbhir (corr. <i>s</i>âdâbhir?) ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>aladvîpibhir vâ. 13. yatra vâ svaya<i>m</i>k<i>ri</i>tâ<i>h</i> <i>s</i>vabhrâ<i>h</i> sarvato<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>bhimukhâ syu<i>h</i>. 14. prâgdvâra<i>m</i> dhanya<i>m</i> ya<i>s</i>asya<i>m</i> <i>k</i>odagdvâra<i>m</i> putrya<i>m</i> pa<i>s</i>avya<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a dakshi<i>n</i>advâre sarve kâmâ. anudvâra<i>m</i> gehadvâram 15. asa<i>m</i>lokî (asa<i>m</i>loki?) syât. 16. pâyaso havi<i>h</i>. 17. k<i>ri</i>shnâ <i>k</i>a gaur. 18. a<i>g</i>o vâ <i>s</i>veta<i>h</i> pâyasa eva vâ. 19. madhye ve<i>s</i>mano vasâ<i>m</i> pâyasa<i>m</i> <i>k</i>âgyena mi<i>s</i>ram ash<i>t</i>ag<i>ri</i>hîta<i>m</i> <i>g</i>uhuyâd Vâstoshpata iti. 20. yâ<i>s</i> <i>k</i>a parâs saptâlakshmînir<i>n</i>ode tâbhi<i>s</i> <i>k</i>a. 21. hutvâ di<i>s</i>â<i>m</i> bali<i>m</i> nayed.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_430"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 430</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. And towards the intermediate points, and upwards and downwards.</p>  
 <p>23. This (he should repeat) every year, or at the two sacrifices of the first-fruits (of rice and barley).</p>  
 <p>24 <a name="fr_1046"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">24</font></a>. With the two (formulas), 'Obeying the will' (MB. II, 6, 7, 8), he should sacrifice two oblations.</p>  
 <p>25. He should pronounce the name of the person whom he wishes to subdue to his will, 'N.N.;' then that person will obey him.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">22. avântaradi<i>s</i>â<i>m</i> <i>k</i>ordhvâvâ<i>k</i>îbhyâ<i>m</i> <i>k</i>ai. 23. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>va<i>m</i> sa<i>m</i>vatsare sa<i>m</i>vatsare navaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>ayor vâ. 24. vasa<i>m</i>gamâv ity etâbhyâm âhutî <i>g</i>uhuyâd. 25. yam i<i>kh</i>ed va<i>s</i>am âyânta<i>m</i> tasya nâma g<i>ri</i>hîtvâsâv iti va<i>s</i>î hâsya bhavati.</font></p>
