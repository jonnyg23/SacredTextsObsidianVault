 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 11.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_71"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now when the bride is to be carried away (to the bridegroom's house) that night, or on the next, or on the third night,</p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_72"></a><a href="#fn_71"><font size="1">2</font></a>. On that night, when (the darkness of) night is gone, they wash the girl up to her head with (water that has been made fragrant by) all sorts of herbs and the choicest fruits together with scents;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 32</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. They put on her a newly-dyed garment or (a new one) which has not yet been washed;</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_73"></a><a href="#fn_72"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Then (the Â<i>k</i>ârya of the bride's family) makes the girl sit down behind the fire, and while she takes hold of him he sacrifices with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis, and then he makes Â<i>g</i>ya oblations to Agni, to Soma, to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, to Mitra, to Varu<i>n</i>a, to Indra, to Indrâ<i>n</i>î, to the Gandharva, to Bhaga, to Pûshan, to Tvash<i>t</i>ar, to B<i>ri</i>haspati, to the king Pratyânîka.</p>  
 <p>5. After they have regaled four or eight women, who are not widows, with lumps of vegetables, Surâ, and food, these should perform a dance four times.</p>  
 <p>6. The same deities (they worship also) on behalf of the man,</p>  
 <p>7. And Vai<i>s</i>rava<i>n</i>a and Î<i>s</i>âna.</p>  
 <p>8. Then follows the distribution of food to Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
