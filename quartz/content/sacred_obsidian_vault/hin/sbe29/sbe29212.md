 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 5.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">1-9</font></a>. The fire used at his wedding (is kept as) his (sacred) domestic fire.</p>  
 <p>2. Or that on which he (as a student) puts the last piece of wood.</p>  
 <p>3. Or (a fire) kindled by attrition: that is pure, but it does not bring prosperity.</p>  
 <p>4. Or he may get it from a frying-pan.</p>  
 <p>5. Or from the house of one who offers many sacrifices, with the exception of a <i>S</i>ûdra.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">5, 1. yasminn agnau pâ<i>n</i>i<i>m</i> g<i>ri</i>h<i>n</i>îyât sa g<i>ri</i>hyo. 2. yasmin vântyâ<i>m</i> samidham âdadhyân. 3. nirmanthyo vâ punyas so<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>nardhuko. 4. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>mbarîshâd vânayed. 5. bahuyâ<i>g</i>ino vâgârâ<i>k</i> <i>kh</i>ûdravar<i>g</i>a<i>m</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_386"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 386</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. The service (at that sacred domestic fire) begins with an evening oblation.</p>  
 <p>7. After (the fire) has been set in a blaze before sunset or sunrise</p>  
 <p>8. The sacrifice (is performed) after sunset,</p>  
 <p>9. (And) after sunrise or before sunrise.</p>  
 <p>10 <a name="fr_1002"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">10-19</font></a>. He should with his hand make oblations of food which is fit for sacrifice, having washed it, if it is raw.</p>  
 <p>11. If it consists in curds or milk, with a brazen bowl,</p>  
 <p>12. Or with the pot in which the oblations of cooked rice are prepared.</p>  
 <p>13. (In the evening the first oblation with the formula), 'To Agni Svâhâ!' in the middle (of the sacred fire);</p>  
 <p>14. The second (oblation) silently in the northeastern part (of the fire).</p>  
 <p>15. In the morning the first (oblation with the formula), 'To Sûrya (Svâhâ)!'</p>  
 <p>16. The wiping round the fire and the similar acts, with the exception of the sprinkling (of water) round (the fire), are omitted here.</p>  
 <p>17. Some (teachers say) that his wife may offer these oblations, for the wife is (as it were) the house, and that fire is the domestic fire.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">6. sâyamâhutyupakrama<i>m</i> pari<i>k</i>ara<i>n</i>a<i>m</i>. 7. prâg astamayodayâbhyâ<i>m</i> prâdushk<i>ri</i>tyâ. 8. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>stam ite homa. 9. udite <i>k</i>ânudite vâ. 10. havishyasyânnasyâk<i>ri</i>ta<i>m</i> <i>k</i>et prakshâlya <i>g</i>uhuyât pâ<i>n</i>inâ. 11. dadhi <i>k</i>et payo vâ ka<i>m</i>sena. 12. <i>k</i>arusthâlyâ vâ. 13. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gnaye svâheti madhye. 14. tûsh<i>n</i>î<i>m</i> prâgudî<i>k</i>îm uttarâ<i>m</i>. 15. Sûryâyeti prâta<i>h</i> pûrvâ<i>m</i>. 16. nâtra parisamûhanâdîni paryuksha<i>n</i>avar<i>g</i>a<i>m</i>. 17. patnî <i>g</i>uhuyâd ity eke g<i>ri</i>hâ<i>h</i> patnî g<i>ri</i>hyo<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gnir esha iti.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_387"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 387</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. When (the meal) is ready, in the evening and in the morning, (the wife) should say, 'It is ready! and (the husband) with loud voice, 'Om!'</p>  
 <p>19. Then in a low voice: 'May it not fail! Adoration to thee!'</p>  
 <p>20 <a name="fr_1003"></a><a href="#fn_1002"><font size="1">20-37</font></a>. Of food which is fit for sacrifice he should make oblations to Pra<i>g</i>âpati and to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p>21. Then he should make the Bali offerings.</p>  
 <p>22. He should put down (a Bali) at four places, inside or outside (the Agnyagâra);</p>  
 <p>23. (Another Bali) near the water-barrel;</p>  
 <p>24. (Another) at the middle door;</p>  
 <p>25. (Another) in the bed,</p>  
 <p>26. Or in the privy;</p>  
 <p>27. Another on the heap of sweepings.</p>  
 <p>28. He should sprinkle each (Bali with water) before and afterwards.</p>  
 <p>29. The remnants he should pour out together with water towards the south.</p>  
 <p>30. Of chaff, of water, and of the scum of boiled rice (he should offer a Bali) when a donation has been made.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">18. siddhe sâyamprâtar bhûtam ity ukta om ity u<i>k</i><i>k</i>air brûyât. 19. ma kshâ namas ta ity upâ<i>m</i><i>s</i>u. 20. havishyasyânnasya <i>g</i>uhuyât prâ<i>g</i>âpatya<i>m</i> sauvish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>ta<i>m</i> <i>k</i>a. 21. bali<i>m</i> nayed. 22. bahir antar vâ <i>k</i>atur nidhâya. 23. ma<i>n</i>ikade<i>s</i>e. 24. madhye dvâri. 25. <i>s</i>ayyâm anu. 26. var<i>k</i>a<i>m</i> [sic] vâ. 27. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>tha sastûpam. 28. ekaikam ubhayata<i>h</i> parishi<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>e<i>k</i>. 29. <i>kh</i>esham adbhis sârdha<i>m</i> dakshi<i>n</i>â ninayet. 30. phalîkara<i>n</i>ânâm apâm â<i>k</i>âmasveti (read, â<i>k</i>âmasyeti) vi<i>s</i>râ<i>n</i>ite.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_388"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 388</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. The gods to whom the Bali offerings belong, are, the Earth, Vâyu, Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs, the Waters, the Herbs and Trees, the Ether, Kama or Manyu, the hosts of Rakshas, the Fathers, Rudra.</p>  
 <p>32. He should do so silently.</p>  
 <p>33. He should do so (i.e. offer Balis) of all food.</p>  
 <p>34. If for one meal the food gets ready at different times, he should do so only once.</p>  
 <p>35. If (food is prepared) at different places, (he should take) that which belongs to the householder.</p>  
 <p>36. Of all food he should offer (something) in the fire, and give the due portion to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a; he should do so himself.</p>  
 <p>37. From the rice(-harvest) till the barley(-harvest), or from the barley(-harvest) till the rice(-harvest) he should offer (the Balis) himself. He should offer (the Balis) himself.</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the First Pa<i>t</i>ala.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">31. P<i>ri</i>thivî Vâyu<i>h</i> Pra<i>g</i>âpatir Vi<i>s</i>ve devâ Âpa Oshadhivanaspataya Âkâ<i>s</i>a<i>h</i> Kâmo Manyur vâ Rakshoga<i>n</i>â<i>h</i> Pitaro Rudra iti balidaivatâni. 32. tûsh<i>n</i>î<i>m</i> to kuryât. 33. sarvasya tv annasyaitat kuryâd. 34. asak<i>ri</i><i>k</i> <i>k</i>ed ekasmin kale siddhe sak<i>ri</i>d eva kuryâd. 35. bahudhâ <i>k</i>ed yad g<i>ri</i>hapate<i>h</i>. 36. sarvasya tv annasyâgnau k<i>ri</i>tvâgra<i>m</i> brâhma<i>n</i>âya dadyât; svaya<i>m</i> kuryâd. 37. vrîhiprabh<i>ri</i>ty â yavebhyo yavebhyo vâ<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>vrîhibhya svaya<i>m</i> haret svaya<i>m</i> haret. prathamapa<i>t</i>ala<i>h</i>.</font></p>
