 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 10.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now with the sacrificial cord suspended over the right shoulder,</p>  
 <p>2. Looking in the direction that belongs to the Manes (i.e. the south):</p>  
 <p>3. 'The (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the hundred (<i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as), the (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the middle (Ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>alas), G<i>ri</i>tsamada, Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra, <i>G</i>amadagni, Vâmadeva, Atri, Bharadvâ<i>g</i>a, Vasish<i>th</i>a, the Pragâthas, the (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the Pavamâna hymns, the (<i>Ri</i>shis) of the short hymns and of the long hymns, Sumantu, <i>G</i>aimini, Vai<i>s</i>ampâyana, Paila, the Sûtras, the Bhâshyas, Gârgya, Babhru, Bâbhravya, Ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>u, Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>avya, Gârgî Vâ<i>k</i>aknavî,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_123"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 123</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Vadavâ Prâtitheyî, Sulabhâ Maitreyî (may satiate themselves).</p>  
 <p>'(I satiate) Kahola Kaushîtaki, Mahâkaushîtaki, Suya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana, Â<i>s</i>valâyana, Aitareya, Mahaitareya, Bhâradvâ<i>g</i>a, <i>G</i>âtûkar<i>n</i>ya, Paiṅgya, Mahâpaṅgya, Bâshkala, Gârgya, <i>S</i>âkalya, Mâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ûkeya, Mahâdamatra, Audavâhi, Mahaudavâhi, Sauyâmi, <i>S</i>aunaki, <i>S</i>âkapû<i>n</i>i, Gautami; and whatsoever other teachers there are, may they all satiate themselves.</p>  
 <p>4. 'The fathers man by man.</p>  
 <p>5. 'The ancestry of the father may satiate itself.</p>  
 <p>6. 'The ancestry of the mother may satiate itself.'</p>
