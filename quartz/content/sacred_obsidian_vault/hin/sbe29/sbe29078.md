 <p><a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 130</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 16.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the full moon day of Â<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i>a a milk-rice oblation to Indra.</p>  
 <p>2. Having sacrificed Â<i>g</i>ya with (the words), 'To the two A<i>s</i>vins svâhâ! To the two A<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i> svâhâ! To the full moon of Â<i>s</i>vayu<i>g</i>a svâhâ! To the autumn svâhâ! To Pa<i>s</i>upati svâhâ! To the tawny one svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_292"></a><a href="#fn_291"><font size="1">3</font></a>. He shall sacrifice a mixture of curds and butter with this hymn, 'The cows came hither' (Rig-veda VI, 28), verse by verse.</p>  
 <p>4. That night they let the calves join their mothers.</p>  
 <p>5. Then feeding of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
