 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 3.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_304"></a><a href="#fn_303"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Now at (the consecration of) a garden: having established the (sacred) fire (in that garden),</p>  
 <p>2. (And) having prepared a mess of cooked food,</p>  
 <p>3. He shall sacrifice with (the formulas), 'To</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Vish<i>n</i>u svâhâ! To Indra and Agni svâhâ! To Vi<i>s</i>vakarman svâhâ!' (and with the verses), 'Whom the men' (Rig-veda III, 8, 6 seq.), verse by verse.</p>  
 <p>4. He recites over (the garden), 'O tree with thy hundred branches' (Rig-veda III, 8, 11).</p>  
 <p>5. The fee for the sacrifice is gold.</p>
