 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 4.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_1021"></a><a href="#fn_1020"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Let him initiate a Brâhma<i>n</i>a in his eighth year.</p>  
 <p>2. For him the time (for being initiated) has not passed until his sixteenth (year).</p>  
 <p>3. In the eleventh a Kshatriya.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">4, 1. ash<i>t</i>ame varshe brâhma<i>n</i>am upanayet. 2. tasyâ sho<i>d</i>a<i>s</i>âd anatîta<i>h</i> kâla. 3. ekâda<i>s</i>e kshatriya<i>m</i>.</font></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_400"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 400</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. For him (the time has not passed) until the twenty-second.</p>  
 <p>5. In the twelfth a Vai<i>s</i>ya.</p>  
 <p>6. For him (the time has not passed) until the twenty-fourth.</p>  
 <p>7. After (the student's) hair has been arranged, and he has been adorned, and dressed in a garment which has not yet been washed, (the teacher) should sacrifice with (the Mantras which the student recites), 'Agni! Lord of the vow!' (MB. I, 6, 9-13.)</p>  
 <p>8. He should cause (the student) to stand northwards of the fire, facing the west, and to join his hands.</p>  
 <p>9. And he should himself join his hands above (the student's hands).</p>  
 <p>10. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a versed in the Mantras who stands towards the south, should fill the teacher's joined hands with water.</p>  
 <p>11. While (the student?) looks at him, (the teacher) should murmur (the Mantra), 'With him who comes to us' (MB. I, 6, 14).</p>  
 <p>12. (The student) to whom (the teacher) has said, 'What is thy name?' should pronounce a name which he is to use at respectful salutations, derived from (the name of) a deity or a Nakshatra, 'I am N.N.!' (l.l. 17.)</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">4. tasyâ dvâvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>âd. 5. dvâda<i>s</i>e vai<i>s</i>ya<i>m</i>. 6. tasyâ <i>k</i>aturvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>ât. 7. ku<i>s</i>alîk<i>ri</i>tam ala<i>m</i>k<i>ri</i>tam ahatenâ<i>kh</i>âdya hutvâgne vratapata ity. 8. uttarato<font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>gne<i>h</i> pratyaṅmukham avasthâpyâ<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ali<i>m</i> kârayet. 9. svaya<i>m</i> <i>k</i>opari kuryâd. 10. dakshi<i>n</i>atas tish<i>th</i>an mantravân brâhma<i>n</i>a â<i>k</i>âryâyodakâ<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>alim pûrayed. 11. âgantreti <i>g</i>apet prekshamâ<i>n</i>e [sic]. 12. ko nâmâsîty ukto devatâ<i>s</i>raya<i>m</i> nakshatrâ<i>s</i>raya<i>m</i> vâbhivâdanîya<i>m</i> nâma brûyâd asâv asmîty.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_401"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 401</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Having let the water run (out of his joined hands over the student's hands) the teacher should seize with his two hands, holding the right uppermost, (the student's) joined hands, with (the formula), By the impulse of the god Sâvitrî' (l.l. 18).</p>  
 <p>14. With (the formula), 'Move in the sun's course' (l.l. 19) he should make him turn round from left to right.</p>  
 <p>15. Grasping down over his right shoulder he should touch his uncovered navel with (the formula), '(Thou art the knot) of all breath' (l.l. 20).</p>  
 <p>16. He then should give him in charge (to the gods) with the Antaka and the other formulas (l.l. 20 seqq.).</p>  
 <p>17. (He touches) his right shoulder with (the formula), 'To Pra<i>g</i>âpati (I give) thee (in charge)' (l.l. 23),</p>  
 <p>18. His left shoulder with his left (hand) with (the formula), 'To the god Savit<i>ri</i> (I give) thee (in charge)' (l.l. 24).</p>  
 <p>19. Having directed him (to observe the duties of Brahma<i>k</i>arya, by the formula), 'A student art thou' (l.l. 25, 26), (the teacher) sitting down should from left to right tie round the student, who bends his right knee and clasps his hands, the girdle made of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass, and should cause him to repeat (the verse), 'Protecting us from evil word' (l.l. 27).</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">13. uts<i>ri</i><i>g</i>yâpo devasya ta iti dakshi<i>n</i>ottarâbhyâ<i>m</i> hastâbhyâm a<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ali<i>m</i> g<i>ri</i>h<i>n</i>îyâd â<i>k</i>âryas. 14. Sûryasyeti pradakshi<i>n</i>am âvartayed. 15. dakshi<i>n</i>am a<i>m</i>sam anvavam<i>ri</i><i>s</i>yânantarhitâ<i>m</i> nâbhim âlabhet prâ<i>n</i>ânâm ity. 16. athaina<i>m</i> pariadyâd antakaprabh<i>ri</i>tibhir. 17. dakshi<i>n</i>am a<i>m</i>sa<i>m</i> Pra<i>g</i>âpataye tveti. 18. savyena savya<i>m</i> devâya tveti. 19. brahma<i>k</i>âry asîti sa<i>m</i>preshyopavi<i>s</i>ya (sa<i>m</i>prekshy°, sa<i>m</i>prokshy° the MSS.) dakshni<i>n</i>a<i>g</i>ânvaktam a<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>alîk<i>ri</i>ta<i>m</i> pradakshi<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>amekhalâm âbadhnan vâ<i>k</i>ayed iya<i>m</i> duruktâd ity.</font></p>  
 <p><a name="page_402"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 402</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. With (the words), 'Recite, sir!' (the student) should respectfully sit down near (the teacher).</p>  
 <p>21. He then recites the Sâvitrî (l.l. 29) to him, Pâda by Pâda, hemistich by hemistich, (and finally) the whole-thus he should teach him the Sâvitrî,</p>  
 <p>22. And the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis, one by one,</p>  
 <p>23. And the word Om.</p>  
 <p>24. He hands over to him the staff, which should be made of (the wood of) a tree</p>  
 <p>25. With (the formula which the student has to recite), 'O glorious one, make me glorious' (l.l. 34).</p>  
 <p>26. Let him put a piece of wood (on the fire) with (the verse), 'To Agni a piece of wood' (l.l. 32).</p>  
 <p>27. Let him go to beg food;</p>  
 <p>28. First of his mother,</p>  
 <p>29. Then of other women friends.</p>  
 <p>30. He should announce the alms (received) to his teacher.</p>  
 <p>31. He should stand silently till sunset.</p>  
 <p>32. Through a period of three nights he should avoid eating saline food and drinking milk.</p>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">20. adhîhi bho ity upasîdet. 21. tasmâ anvâha sâvitrî<i>m</i> pa<i>k</i><i>kh</i>o <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>rdhar<i>k</i>a<i>s</i>as sarvâm iti sâvitrî<i>m</i> vâ<i>k</i>ayed. 22. mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tî<i>s</i> <i>k</i>aikaika<i>s</i>a. 23. o<i>m</i>kâra<i>ñ</i> <i>k</i>a. 24. praya<i>kh</i>aty asmai vârksha<i>m</i> da<i>n</i><i>d</i>a<i>m</i>. 25. su<i>s</i>ravas su<i>s</i>ravasa<i>m</i> meti. 26. samidham âdadhyâd Agnaye samidham iti. 27. bhaiksha<i>m</i> <i>k</i>aren. 28. mâtaram agre. 29. <font size="-1"><sup>ऽ</sup></font>thânyâs suh<i>ri</i>da. 30. â<i>k</i>âryâya bhaikshan nivedayet. 31. tish<i>th</i>ed âstamayât tûsh<i>n</i>î<i>m</i>. 32. trirâtra<i>m</i> kshâralava<i>n</i>e dugdham iti var<i>g</i>ayet.</font></p>
