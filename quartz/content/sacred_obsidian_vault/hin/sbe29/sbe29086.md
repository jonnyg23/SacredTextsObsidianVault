 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 5.</h3>  
 <p>1. If a dove or an owl sits down (on his house),</p>  
 <p>2. Let him sacrifice with (the hymn), 'O gods, the dove' (Rig-veda X, 165), verse by verse.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_137"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 137</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. If he has seen a bad dream or an occurrence boding misfortune,</p>  
 <p>4. Or when the cawing of a crow is heard in (the dead of) night,</p>  
 <p>5. And in the case of other prodigies,</p>  
 <p>6. Let him cook rice-grains with milk,</p>  
 <p>7. With the milk of a cow that has a calf of the same colour (with her own),</p>  
 <p>8. But in no case of a black (cow),</p>  
 <p>9. And let him sacrifice with the night-hymn (Rig-veda X, 127), verse by verse.</p>  
 <p>10. Having eaten the remnants of those oblations with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis,</p>  
 <p>11. And having recited over his ears (the verse), 'Blessing with our ears' (Rig-veda I, 89, 8),</p>  
 <p>12. And over himself (the verse), 'May a hundred autumns be before us, ye gods' (ibid. 9),</p>  
 <p>13. He shall give something to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>
