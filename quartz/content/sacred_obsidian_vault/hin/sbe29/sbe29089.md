 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 8.</h3>  
 <p>1. If a post puts forth shoots,</p>  
 <p>2. Let him prepare a mess of cooked food and offer the boiled rice with the two (verses), 'In that way bringing forth deeds' (<i>S</i>rauta-sûtra III, 17, 1), 'Of tawny shape, weighty, a giver of vigour' (Rig-veda II, 3, 9). A</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_309"></a><a href="#fn_308"><font size="1">8_3</font></a>. Should the pot for the Pra<i>n</i>îtâ water, the Â<i>g</i>ya- pot, any other earthen (vessel) be damaged and leak,</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_310"></a><a href="#fn_309"><font size="1">4</font></a>. He sacrifices the two Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta oblations and recites the three verses, 'He who without' (Rig-veda VIII, I, 12 seq.), over the broken (vessel).</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_311"></a><a href="#fn_310"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Should the two (Ku<i>s</i>a blades which are used as) strainers be spoiled before the completion of the sacrifice,</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_312"></a><a href="#fn_311"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Let him sacrifice the Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta and make new ones with (the verse), 'In the water, Agni' (Rig-veda VIII, 43, 9).</p>
