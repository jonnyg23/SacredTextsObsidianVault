 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 2.</h3>  
 <p>1 <a name="fr_666"></a><a href="#fn_665"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The setting up of the Âvasathya (or sacred domestic) fire (is performed) at the time of his wedding.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>  
 <p>2 <a name="fr_667"></a><a href="#fn_666"><font size="1">2</font></a>. At the time of the division of the inheritance, according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_668"></a><a href="#fn_667"><font size="1">3</font></a> After he has fetched fire from the house of a Vai<i>s</i>ya who is rich in cattle,</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_669"></a><a href="#fn_668"><font size="1">4</font></a>. All ceremonies are performed as at the cooking of the <i>k</i>âtushprâ<i>s</i>ya food.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_670"></a><a href="#fn_669"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Some (say that) the handing over of the kindling sticks (should take place),</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_671"></a><a href="#fn_670"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Because the <i>S</i>ruti says, 'There are five great sacrifices.'</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_672"></a><a href="#fn_671"><font size="1">7</font></a>. Having cooked a mess of sacrificial food for the deities of the Agnyâdheya, and having sacrificed the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions, he sacrifices (the following) Â<i>g</i>ya oblations:</p>  
 <p>8 <a name="fr_673"></a><a href="#fn_672"><font size="1">8</font></a>. 'Thou, Agni' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ XXI, 3); 'Thus</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 272</font></a></p>  
 <p>thou, Agni' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ XXI, 4); 'This, O Varu<i>n</i>a' (XXI, 1); 'For this I entreat thee' (XXI, 2); 'Thy hundred' (Kâty.-<i>S</i>raut. XXV, 1, 11); 'And quick, Agni' (Kâty. l.l.); 'The highest one' (Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XII, 12); 'Be both to us' (ibid. V, 3)with (these verses he sacrifices) eight (oblations) before (the oblations of cooked food).</p>  
 <p>9. Thus he sacrifices also afterwards, after he has made oblations of the mess of cooked food to the deities of the Agnyâdheya.</p>  
 <p>10. And to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t,</p>  
 <p>11 <a name="fr_674"></a><a href="#fn_673"><font size="1">11</font></a>. With (the formulas), 'Into the quick one (has been put) Agni's (sacrificial portion) over which the word vasha<i>t</i> has been spoken;' 'What I have done too much;' 'O gods who know the way.'</p>  
 <p>12 <a name="fr_675"></a><a href="#fn_674"><font size="1">12</font></a>. Having sacrificed the Barhis, he partakes (of the sacrificial food).</p>  
 <p>13. Then food is given to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_668"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_669">271:4</a> The <i>k</i>âtushprâ<i>s</i>ya food is prepared, at the time of the setting up of the <i>S</i>rauta fires, for the four chief officiating priests of the <i>S</i>rauta sacrifices. Comp. <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a II, 1, 4. Kâtyâyana's corresponding rules with regard to the Âdhâna of the <i>S</i>rauta fires are found at IV, 7, 15. 16.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_669"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_670">271:5</a> Comp. the remarks on this Sûtra, in the Introduction, pp. <a href="sbe29154.htm#page_265">265</a> seq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_670"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_671">271:6</a> <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a XI, 5, 6, 1: 'There are five great sacrifices which are great Sattras, viz. the sacrifice to living beings, the sacrifice to men, the sacrifice to the Manes, the sacrifice to the gods, the Brahmaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.' As the G<i>ri</i>hya ceremonies are included here under the category of mahâya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âs or great sacrifices, they require, according to the teachers whose opinion is stated in Sûtra 5, a form of the Agnyâdhâna (setting up of the sacred fire) analogous to the Agnyâdhâna of the <i>S</i>rauta ritual, and containing, like that Âdhâna, the act of the Ara<i>n</i>ipradâna or handing over of the kindling woods (Sûtra 5).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_671"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_672">271:7</a> The deities of the Agnyâdheya, or of the <i>S</i>rauta ceremony corresponding to the G<i>ri</i>hya rite here treated of, are Agni pavamâna, Agni pâvaka, Agni <i>s</i>u<i>k</i>i, Aditi. On the Â<i>g</i>yabhâgas, see <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana I, 9, 7, &amp;c.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_672"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_673">271:8</a> The verses Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XXI, 3, 4, the two verses quoted <a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 272</font></a> Kâty. XXV, 1, 11, and fifthly the verse Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>h. XII, 12, are prescribed for the Sarvaprâya<i>s</i><i>k</i>itta (or general expiatory ceremony), see Kâtyâyana l.l.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_673"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_674">272:11</a> Professor Stenzler, following <i>G</i>ayarâma, takes the whole as one Mantra, which he translates: 'Ungehemmet sei Agni's Spende, die durch die That ich überreich machte, bahnschaffende Götter!' But the words yat karma<i>n</i>âtyarîri<i>k</i>am are the opening words of a Mantra quoted <i>S</i>atapatha Brâhma<i>n</i>a XIV, 9, 4, 24, (comp. also Â<i>s</i>valâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya I, 10, 23; the connection in which atyarîri<i>k</i>am there stands, shows that the word designates a mistake made in the sacrificial work by doing too much.) The words devâ gâtuvida<i>h</i> are the Pratîka of Vâ<i>g</i>. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ VIII, 21. Thus I have no doubt that also ayâsy Agner vasha<i>t</i>k<i>ri</i>tam (or possibly ayâsy Agner (?) and vasha<i>t</i>k<i>ri</i>tam (?)) is a Pratîka. Of course, the translation of these words must remain uncertain until the Mantra to which they belong has been discovered.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_674"></a><a href="sbe29156.htm#fr_675">272:12</a> On the throwing into the fire of the Barhis, comp. Kâtyâyana III, 8.</p>
