 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 18.</h3>  
 <p>1. Thereby the Godânakarman (i.e. the ceremony of shaving the beard, is declared).</p>  
 <p>2. In the sixteenth year.</p>  
 <p>3. Instead of the word 'hair' he should (each time that it occurs in the Mantras) put the word 'beard.'</p>  
 <p>4 <a name="fr_450"></a><a href="#fn_449"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Here they moisten the beard.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_451"></a><a href="#fn_450"><font size="1">5</font></a>. (The Mantra is), 'Purify his head and his face, but do not take away his life.'</p>  
 <p>6 <a name="fr_452"></a><a href="#fn_451"><font size="1">6</font></a>. He gives orders (to the barber with the words), 'Arrange his hair, his beard, the hair of his body, and his nails, ending in the north.'</p>  
 <p>7 <a name="fr_453"></a><a href="#fn_452"><font size="1">7-8</font></a>. Having bathed and silently stood during the rest of the day, let him break his silence in the presence of his teacher, (saying to him,) 'I give an optional gift (to thee).'</p>  
 <p>8. An ox and a cow is the sacrificial fee.</p>
