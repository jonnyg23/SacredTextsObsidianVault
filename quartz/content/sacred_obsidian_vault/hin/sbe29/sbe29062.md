 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 1 (SBE29)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 14.</h3>  
 <p>1. On the last (Ash<i>t</i>akâ) he sacrifices cakes,</p>  
 <p>2. With the words, 'The Ukthya and the Atirâtra. the Sadya<i>h</i>krî together with the metreAsh<i>t</i>akâ!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Preparer of cakes! Adoration to thee, O glad-faced one. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>3 <a name="fr_229"></a><a href="#fn_228"><font size="1">3-6</font></a>. A cow or a goat is the animal (to be sacrificed), or a mess of cooked food (should be offered).</p>  
 <p>4. Or he may optionally offer food to a cow.</p>  
 <p>5 <a name="fr_230"></a><a href="#fn_229"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Or he may optionally burn down brushwood in the forest and say, 'This is my Ash<i>t</i>akâ.'</p>  
 <p>6. But let him not neglect to do (one of these things). But let him not neglect to do (one of these things).</p>  
 <p align="center">Here ends the Third Adhyâya.</p>
