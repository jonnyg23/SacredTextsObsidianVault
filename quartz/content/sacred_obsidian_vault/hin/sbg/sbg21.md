 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="green">p. 337</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SIXTEENTH CHAPTER</h3>  
 <p>The Blessed Lord said:</p>  
 <p>1. Fearlessness, purity of heart, steadfastness in knowledge and Yoga; almsgiving, control of the senses, Yajna, reading of the Shâstras, austerity, uprightness; <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_338"><font size="1" color="green">p. 338</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Non-injury, truth, absence of anger, renunciation, tranquillity, absence of calumny, compassion to beings, un-covetousness, gentleness, modesty, absence of fickleness; <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>3. Boldness, forgiveness, fortitude, purity, absence of hatred, absence of</p>  
 <p><a name="page_339"><font size="1" color="green">p. 339</font></a></p>  
 <p>pride; these belong to one born for a divine state, O descendant of Bharata.</p>  
 <p>4. Ostentation, arrogance and self-conceit, anger as also harshness and ignorance, belong to one who is born, O Pârtha, for an Asurika state. <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>5. The divine state is deemed to make for liberation, the Asurika for bondage; grieve not, O Pândava, thou art born for a divine state.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_340"><font size="1" color="green">p. 340</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. There are two types of beings <a href="errata.htm#3">in</a> this world, the divine and the Asurika. The divine have been described at length; hear from Me, O Pârtha, of the Asurika.</p>  
 <p>7. The persons of Asurika nature know not what to do and what to refrain from; neither is purity found in them nor good conduct, nor truth. <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_341"><font size="1" color="green">p. 341</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. They say, "The universe is without truth, without a (moral) basis, without a God, brought about by mutual union, with lust for its cause; what else?" <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>9. Holding this view, these ruined souls of small intellect and fierce deeds, rise as the enemies of the world for its destruction. <a name="fr_337"></a><a href="#fn_337"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="green">p. 342</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Filled with insatiable desires, full of hypocrisy, pride and arrogance, holding evil ideas through delusion, they work with impure resolve.</p>  
 <p>11. Beset with immense cares ending only with death, regarding gratification of lust as the highest, and feeling sure that that is all; <a name="fr_338"></a><a href="#fn_338"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_343"><font size="1" color="green">p. 343</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Bound by a hundred ties of hope, given over to lust and wrath, they strive to secure by unjust means hoards of wealth for sensual enjoyment.</p>  
 <p>13. "This to-day has been gained by me; this desire I shall obtain; this is mine, and this wealth also shall be mine in future.</p>  
 <p>14. "That enemy has been slain by</p>  
 <p><a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="green">p. 344</font></a></p>  
 <p>me, and others also shall I slay. I am the lord, I enjoy, I am successful, powerful and happy.</p>  
 <p>15. "I am rich and well-born. Who else is equal to me? I will sacrifice, I will give, I will rejoice." Thus deluded by ignorance,</p>  
 <p>16. Bewildered by many a fancy, covered by the meshes of delusion, addicted to the gratification of lust, they fall down into a foul hell.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_345"><font size="1" color="green">p. 345</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. Self-conceited, haughty, filled with the pride and intoxication of wealth, they perform sacrifices in name, out of ostentation, disregarding ordinance;</p>  
 <p>18. Possessed of egoism, power, insolence, lust and wrath, these malignant people hate Me (the Self within) in their own bodies and those of others.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_346"><font size="1" color="green">p. 346</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. These malicious and cruel evildoers, most degraded of men, I hurl perpetually into the wombs of Asuras only, in these worlds. <a name="fr_339"></a><a href="#fn_339"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>20. Obtaining the Asurika wombs, and deluded birth after birth, not attaining to Me, they thus fall, O son of Kunti, into a still lower condition.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_347"><font size="1" color="green">p. 347</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. Triple is this gate of hell, destructive of the self,—lust, anger and greed; therefore one should forsake these three. <a name="fr_340"></a><a href="#fn_340"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>22. The man who has got beyond these three gates of darkness, O son of Kunti, practises what is good for himself, and thus goes to the Goal Supreme.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_348"><font size="1" color="green">p. 348</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. He who, setting aside the ordinance of the Shâstra, acts under the impulse of desire, attains not to perfection, nor happiness, nor the Goal Supreme. <a name="fr_341"></a><a href="#fn_341"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>24. So let the Shâstra be thy authority in ascertaining what ought to be done and what ought not to be done. Having known what is said in the ordinance of the Shâstra, thou shouldst act here. <a name="fr_342"></a><a href="#fn_342"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>The end of the Sixteenth Chapter designated: <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Classification of the Divine and the Non-Divine Attributes</span>.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_332"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_332">337:1</a> <i>Yoga</i>—consists in making what has been learnt from the Shâstras and the Achârya an object of one's own direct perception, by concentration and self-control.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_333"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_333">338:2</a> <i>Uncovetousness:</i> Unaffectedness of the senses when in contact with their objects.</p>  
 <p><i>Absence of fickleness:</i> Avoidance of useless actions.—<i>Sridhara</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_334"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_334">339:4</a> <i>Asurika:</i> demoniac.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_335"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_335">340:7</a> <i>What to do . . . from:</i> What acts they should perform to achieve the end of man, nor what acts they should abstain from to avert evil.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_336"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_336">341:8</a> <i>Without truth:</i> As we are unreal so this universe is unreal, and the sacred Scriptures that declare the truth are unreal.</p>  
 <p><i>What else</i>—but lust can be the cause of the universe?—This is the view of the Lokâyatikas, the materialists.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_337"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_337">341:9</a> <i>Small intellect</i>—as it concerns itself only with sense-objects and cannot soar higher.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_338"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_338">342:11</a> <i>Cares</i>—as to the means of acquiring and preserving the innumerable objects of desire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_339"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_339">346:19</a> <i>Wombs of Asuras:</i> Wombs of the most cruel beings, as tigers, snakes, etc.</p>  
 <p><i>Worlds:</i> Paths of Samsâra passing through many a hell.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_340"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_340">347:21</a> <i>Destructive of the self:</i> making the self fit for no human end whatever.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_341"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_341">348:23</a> <i>Perfection:</i> fitness for attaining the end of man.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_342"></a><a href="sbg21.htm#fr_342">348:24</a> <i>Here:</i> in this world.</p>
