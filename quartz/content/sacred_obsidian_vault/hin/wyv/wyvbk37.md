 <p><a name="page_293"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 293</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE THIRTY-SEVENTH.</h3>  
 <p>By impulse of God Savitar I take thee, etc. Spade art thou.<br> Woman art thou, etc., as in XI. 9, 10.<br> 2 The priests of him the lofty Priest, etc., as in V. 14.<br> 3 O Heaven and Earth divine, may I duly prepare for you<br> this day the head of Makha on the place of earth where<br> the Gods sacrificed.<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha!<br> 4 Ye who were born the earliest of creation, Ants divine, may<br> I duly prepare for you this day the head of Makha on<br> the place of earth where the Gods sacrificed.<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 294</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 Only so large was it at first. Duly may I prepare for you<br> this day the head of Makha on earth's place where the<br> Gods sacrificed.<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha!<br> 6 Indra's effective might are ye. Duly may I prepare for you<br> this day the head of Makha on earth's place where the<br> Gods sacrificed.<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha! For Makha<br> thee, thee for the head of Makha! For Makha thee, thee<br> for the head of Makha!<br> 7 May Brahmanaspati draw nigh, etc., as in XXXIII. 89.<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha, etc., as in<br> verse 6.<br> 8 Thou art the head of Makha. Thee for Makha, thee for<br> Makha's head! Thou art the head of Makha. Thee for<br> Makha, thee for Makha's head! Thou art the head of<br> Makha, thee for Makha, thee for Makha's head!<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha, etc., as in<br> verse 6.<br> 9 Thee on Gods sacrificial ground with stallion's dung I<br> fumigate.<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha!<br> For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha! For Makha<br> thee, thee for the head of Makha! For Makha thee, thee<br> for the head of Makha!<br> 10 Thee for the True. Thee for the Good. Thee for the place<br> of happy rest.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_295"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 295</font></a></p>  
 <p>For Makha thee, thee for the head of Makha! For Makha<br> thee, thee for the head of Makha! For Makha thee, thee<br> for the head of Makha!<br> 11 For Yama thee. For Makha thee. For Sûrya's fervent<br> ardour thee.<br> May Savitar the God with balm anoint thee. Guard thou<br> the touches of the earth.<br> Flame art thou; thou art radiance; thou art heat.<br> 12 Unconquerable, eastward, in Agni's overlordship, give me life.<br> Rich in sons, southward, in Indra's overlordship give me<br> offspring.<br> Fair-seated, westward, in God. Savitar's overlordship, give<br> me sight.<br> Range of hearing, northward, in Dhâtar's overlordship, give<br> me increase of wealth.<br> Arrangement, upward, in Brihaspati's overlordship, give me<br> energy.<br> From all destructive spirits guard us. Thou art Manu's mare.<br> 13 All-hail! By Maruts be thou compassed round.<br> Guard the sky's touches. Mead, mead, mead.<br> 14 Germ of the Gods, Father of hymns, all living creatures<br> guardian Lord,<br> Radiant, with radiant Savitar united, with the Sun he shines.<br> 15 Agni combined with flaming fire, combined with Savitar<br> divine, hath shone together with the Sun.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 296</font></a></p>  
 <p>16 He shines on earth upholder of the sky and heat, the Gods<br> upholder, God, immortal, born of heat.<br> To him address a speech devoted to the Gods.<br> 17 I saw the Herdsman, him who never stumbles, approaching<br> by his pathways and departing.<br> He, clothed with gathered and diffusive splendour, within<br> the worlds continually travels.<br> 18 Lord of all earths, Lord of all mind, Lord of all speech,<br> thou Lord of speech entire.<br> Heard by the Gods, Caldron divine, do thou, a God, protect<br> the Gods.<br> Here, after, let it speed you twain on to the banquet of the<br> Gods.<br> Sweetness for both the sweetness-lovers! Sweetness for<br> those the twain who take delight in sweetness!<br> 19 Thee for the heart, thee for the mind, thee for the sky, for<br> Sûrya thee.<br> Standing erect lay thou the sacrifice in heaven among the<br> Gods.<br> 20 Thou art our Father, father-like regard us. Obeisance be to<br> thee. Do not thou harm us.<br> May we, accompanied by Tvashtar, win thee. Vouchsafe me<br> sons and cattle. Grant us offspring. Safe may I be together<br> with my husband.<br> 21 May Day together with his sheen, pair-lighted with his light,<br> accept. All-hail!<br> May Night together with her sheen, fair lighted with her.<br> light, accept. All-hail!</p>
