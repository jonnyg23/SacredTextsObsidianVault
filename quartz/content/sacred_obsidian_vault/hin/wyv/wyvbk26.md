 <p><a name="page_231"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 231</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE TWENTY-SIXTH.</h3>  
 <p>AGNI and Prithivî, closely connected, may they bring low for<br> me the boon I mention.<br> Vâyu and Firmament, closely connected, may they, etc.<br> Closely connected Dyaus and the Âditya, may they, etc.<br> Closely connected Varuna and Waters, may they, etc.<br> Lord of the seven communities and her who forms all beings,<br> eighth,<br> Make our ways full of pleasantness: may So-and-So and I<br> agree.<br> 2 That I to all the people may address this salutary speech,<br> To priest and nobleman, Sûdra and Arya, to one of our own<br> kin and to the stranger.<br> Dear may I be to Gods and guerdon-giver. Fulfilled be this<br> my hope: be that my portion!<br> 3 Give us, Brihaspati, that wondrous treasure, that which<br> exceeds the merit of the foeman,<br> Which shines among the folk effectual, splendid, that, Son<br> of Law, which is with might refulgent.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Brihaspati. This is<br> thy home. Thee for Brihaspati,<br> 4 Come hither, Indra, rich in kine! Drink Soma, Lord of<br> Hundred Powers,<br> Effused by braying pressing-stones.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Indra, rich in kine.<br> This is thy home. Thee for Indra, rich in kine.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_232"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 232</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 O Indra, Vritra-slayer, come. Drink Soma, Lord of<br> Hundred Powers,<br> Expressed with stones whose wealth is kine.<br> Taken upon a base art thou, etc., as in verse 4.<br> 6 Vaisvânara the righteous One, the Lord of sacrifice and light,<br> The heat that wasteth not, we seek.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Vaisvânara.<br> This is thy home: thee for Vaisvânara.<br> 7 Still in Vaisvânara's grace may we continue: yea, he is<br> King Supreme oer all things living.<br> Sprung hence to life upon this All he looketh. Vaisvânara<br> hath rivalry with Sûrya.<br> Taken upon a base art thou, etc., as in verse 6.<br> 8 Hitherward come Vaisvânara to succour us from far away,<br> Agni through laud that brings him near!<br> Taken upon a base, etc, as in verse 6.<br> 9 Agni is Pavamâna, Sage, the Tribe-Priest of the Races Five:<br> To him of mighty wealth we pray.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for lustre. This is thy<br> home. Thee for lustre.<br> 10 May mighty Indra, thunder-armed, may Shodasî protect us<br> well, and slay the wicked man who hateth us.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Mahendra. This is<br> thy home. Thee for Mahendra.<br> 11 As cows low to their calves in stalls so with our songs we<br> glorify<br> This Indra, een your wondrous God who checks assault,<br> who joys in the delicious juice.<br> 12 Agni's is the most fetching song. Shine mightily, thou rich<br> in light!<br> Like the Chief Consort of a King, riches and strength proceed<br> from thee.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_233"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 233</font></a></p>  
 <p>13 Come, here, O Agni, will I sing verily other songs to thee,<br> And with these drops shalt thou grow strong.<br> 14 The Seasons spread thy sacrifice! the Mouths protect thine<br> offering!<br> May the Year guard our sacrifice for thee and keep our children<br> safe.<br> 15 There where the mountains downward slope, there by the<br> meeting of the streams<br> The sage was manifest with song.<br> 16 High is thy juice's birth: though sat in heaven, on earth it<br> hath obtained<br> Strong sheltering power and great renown.<br> 17 Finder of room and freedom, flow for Indra, meet for worship,<br> flow<br> For Varuna and the Marut host.<br> 18 Striving to win, with him we gain all wealth of the ungodly<br> one,<br> Yea, all the glories of mankind.<br> 19 May we be prosperous with brave sons, cattle, horses, each<br> wish of ours, and varied blessings,<br> With quadrupeds, and with the men about us. May the<br> Gods guide our sacrifice in season.<br> 20 O Agni, bring thou hitherward the yearning Consorts of the<br> Gods<br> Bring Tvashtar to the Soma-draught.<br> 21 O Neshtar girt by Dames, accept our sacrifice: with Ritu<br> drink,<br> For thou art he who giveth wealth.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_234"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 234</font></a></p>  
 <p>22 He with the Ritus fain would drink, Wealth-river, from the<br> Neshtar's bowl.<br> Begin, pay offerings: hasten ye.<br> 28 Thine is this Soma: come thou near, approach it. Drink<br> thou thereof, benevolent, and cease not.<br> Sit on the sacred grass at this our worship, and take these<br> drops into thy belly, Indra.<br> 24 Come unto us, ye swift to listen! as at home, upon the<br> sacred grass sit and enjoy yourselves.<br> And, Tvashtar, well content be joyful in the juice with Gods<br> and Goddesses in gladsome company.<br> 25 In sweetest and most gladdening stream flow pure, O Soma,<br> on thy way,<br> Pressed out for Indra, for his drink.<br> 26 Fiend-queller, Friend of all men, he hath in the vat attained<br> unto<br> His place, his iron-fashioned home.</p>
