 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIX. Dawn.</h3>  
 <p>1 E'EN from above the sky's bright realm come, Uṣas, by auspicious ways:<br> Let red steeds bear thee to the house of him who pours the Soma, juice.<br> 2 The chariot which thou mountest, fair of shape, O Uṣas light to move,<br> Therewith, O Daughter of the Sky, aid men of noble fame today.<br> 3 Bright Uṣas, when thy times return, all quadrupeds and bipeds stir,<br> And round about flock winged birds from all the boundaries of heaven.<br> 4 Thou dawning with thy beams of light illumest all the radiant realm.<br> Thee, as thou art, the Kaṇvas, fain for wealth, have called with sacred songs.</p>
