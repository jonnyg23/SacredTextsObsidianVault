 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXIV. Aśvins.</h3>  
 <p>1. To this mine invocation, O ye Aśvins, ye Nāsatyas, come,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 2 This laud of mine, ye Aśvins Twain, and this mine invitation hear,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 3 Here Kṛṣṇa is invoking you, O Aśvins, Lords of ample wealth.<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 4 List, Heroes, to the singer's call, the call of Kṛṣṇa lauding you,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 5 Chiefs, to the sage who sings your praise grant an inviolable home,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 6 Come to the worshipper's abode, Aśvins, who here is lauding you,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 7 Yoke to the firmly jointed car the ass which draws you, Lords of wealth.<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 8 Come hither, Aśvins, on your car of triple form with triple seat,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.<br> 9 O Aśvins, O Nāsatyas, now accept with favouring grace my songs,<br> To drink the savoury Soma juice.</p>
