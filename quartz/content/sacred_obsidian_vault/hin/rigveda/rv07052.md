 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LII. Ādityas.</h3>  
 <p>1. MAY we be free from every bond, Ādityas! a castle among Gods and men, ye Vasus.<br> Winning, may we win Varuṇa and Mitra, and, being, may we be, O Earth and Heaven.<br> 2 May Varuṇa and Mitra grant this blessing, our Guardians, shelter to our seed and offspring.<br> Let us not suffer for another's trespass. nor do the thing that ye, O Vasus, punish.<br> 3 The ever-prompt Aṅgirases, imploring riches from Savitar the God, obtained them.<br> So may our Father who is great and holy, and all the Gods, accordant, grant this favour.</p>
