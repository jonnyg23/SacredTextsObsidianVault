 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLI. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. INVOKED to drink the Soma juice, come with thy Bay Steeds, Thunder-armed<br> Come, Indra, hitherward to me.<br> 2 Our priest is seated, true to time; the grass is regularly strewn;<br> The pressing-stones were set at morn.<br> 3 These prayers, O thou who hearest prayer are offered: seat thee on the grass.<br> Hero, enjoy the offered cake.<br> 4 O Vṛtra-slayer, be thou pleased with these libations, with these hymns,<br> Song-loving Indra, with our lauds.<br> 5 Our hymns caress the Lord of Strength, vast, drinker of the Soma's juice,<br> Indra, as mother-cows their calf.<br> 6 Delight thee with the juice we pour for thine own great munificence:<br> Yield not thy singer to reproach.<br> 7 We, Indra, dearly loving thee, bearing oblation, sing thee hymns<br> Thou, Vasu, dearly lovest us.<br> 8 O thou to whom thy Bays are dear, loose not thy Horses far from us:<br> Here glad thee, Indra, Lord divine.<br> 9 May long-maned Coursers, dropping oil, bring thee on swift car hitherward,<br> Indra, to seat thee on the grass.</p>
