 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXIII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. GIVE us the rapture that is mightiest, Indra, prompt to bestow and swift to aid, O Hero,<br> That wins with brave steeds where brave steeds encounter, and quells the Vṛtras and the foes in battle.<br> 2 For with loud voice the tribes invoke thee, Indra, to aid them in the battlefield of heroes.<br> Thou, with the singers, hast pierced through the Paṇis: the charger whom thou aidest wins the booty.<br> 3 Both races, Indra, of opposing foemen, O Hero, both the Ārya and the Dāsa,<br> Hast thou struck down like woods with well-shot lightnings: thou rentest them in fight, most manly Chieftain!<br> 4 Indra, befriend us with no scanty succour, prosper and aid us, Loved of all that liveth,<br> When, fighting for the sunlight, we invoke thee, O Hero, in the fray, in war's division.<br> 5 Be ours, O Indra, now and for the future, be graciously inclined and near to help us.<br> Thus may we, singing, sheltered by the Mighty, win many cattle on the day of trial.</p>
