 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVIL Vāyu. Indra-Vāyu.</h3>  
 <p>1. Vāyu, the bright is offered thee, best of the meath at holy rites.<br> Come thou to drink the Soma juice, God, longed-for, on thy team-drawn car.<br> 2 O Vāyu, thou and Indra are meet drinkers of these Soma-draughts,<br> For unto you the drops proceed as waters gather to the vale.<br> 3 O Indra-Vāyu, mighty Twain, speeding together, Lords of Strength,<br> Come to our succour with your team, that ye may drink the Soma juice.<br> 4 The longed-for teams which ye possess, O Heroes, for the worshipper,<br> Turn to us, Indra-Vāyu, ye to whom the sacrifice is paid.</p>
