 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLI. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. ACTIVE and bright have they come forth, impetuous in speed like bulls,<br> Driving the black skin far away.<br> 2 Quelling the riteless Dasyu, may we think upon the bridge of bliss,<br> Leaving the bridge of woe behind.<br> 3 The mighty Pavamana's roar is heard as twere the rush of rain<br> Lightnings are flashing to the sky.<br> 4 Pour out on us abundant food, when thou art pressed, O Indu wealth<br> In kine and gold and steeds and spoil.<br> 5 Flow on thy way, Most Active, thou. fill full the mighty heavens and earth,<br> As Dawn, as Sūrya with his beams.<br> 6 On every side, O Soma, flow round us with thy protecting stream,<br> As Rasā flows around the world.</p>
