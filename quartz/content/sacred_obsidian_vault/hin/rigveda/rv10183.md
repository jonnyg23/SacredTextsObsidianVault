 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXXIII. The Sacrificer, Etc.</h3>  
 <p>1. I SAW thee meditating in thy spirit what sprang from Fervour and hath thence developed.<br> Bestowing offspring here, bestowing riches, spread in thine offspring, thou who cravest children.<br> 2 I saw thee pondering in thine heart, and praying that in due time thy body might be fruitful.<br> Come as a youthful woman, rise to meet me: spread in thine offspring, thou who cravest children.<br> 3 In plants and herbs, in all existent beings I have deposited the germ of increase.<br> All progeny on earth have I engendered, and sons in women who will be hereafter.</p>
