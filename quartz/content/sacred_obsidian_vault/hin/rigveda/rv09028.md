 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVIII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. URGED by the men, this vigorous Steed, Lord of the mind, Omniscient,<br> Runs to the woollen straining-cloth.<br> 2 Within the filter hath he flowed, this Soma for the Gods effused,<br> Entering all their essences.<br> 3 He shines in beauty there, this God Immortal in his dwelling-place,<br> Foe-slayer, dearest to the Gods.<br> 4 Directed by the Sisters ten, bellowing on his way this Steer<br> Runs onward to the wooden vats.<br> 5 This Pavamana, swiftand strong, Omniscient, gave spleudour to<br> The Sun and all his forms of light.<br> 6 This Soma being purified, flows mighty and infallible,<br> Slayer of sinners, dear toGods.</p>
