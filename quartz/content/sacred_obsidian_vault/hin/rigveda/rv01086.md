 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXVI. Maruts.</h3>  
 <p>1. THE best of guardians hath that man within whose dwelling place ye drink,<br> O Maruts, giants of the sky.<br> 2 Honoured with sacrifice or with the worship of the sages hymns,<br> O Maruts, listen to the call.<br> 3 Yea, the strong man to whom ye have vouchsafed to give a sage, shall move<br> Into a stable rich in kine.<br> 4 Upon this hero's sacred grass Soma is poured in daily rites:<br> Praise and delight are sung aloud.<br> 5 Let the strong Maruts hear him, him surpassing all men: strength be his<br> That reaches even to the Sun.<br> 6 For, through the swift Gods loving help, in many an autumn, Maruts, we<br> Have offered up our sacrifice.<br> 7 Fortunate shall that mortal be, O Maruts most adorable,<br> Whose offerings ye bear away.<br> 8 O Heroes truly strong, ye know the toil of him who sings your praise,<br> The heart's desire of him who loves.<br> 9 O ye of true strength, make this thing manifest by your greatness: strike<br> The demon with your thunderbolt.<br> 10 Conceal the horrid darkness, drive far from us each devouring fiend.<br> Create the light for which we long.</p>
