 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. WHEN shall our prayers rest in thy car beside thee? When dost thou give the singer food for thousands?<br> When wilt thou clothe this poet's laud with plenty, and when wilt thou enrich our hymns with booty?<br> 2 When wilt thou gatber men with men, O Indra, heroes with heroes, and prevail in combat?<br> Thou shalt win triply kine in frays for cattle, so, Indra, give thou us celestial glory.<br> 3 Yea, when wilt thou, O Indra, thou Most Mighty, make the prayer all-sustaining for the singer?<br> When wilt thou yoke, as we yoke songs, thy Horses, and come to offerings that bring wealth in cattle?<br> 4 Grant to the Singer food with store of cattle, splendid with horses and the fame of riches.<br> Send food to swell the milch-cow good at milking: bright be its shine among the Bharadvājas.<br> 5 Lead otherwise this present foeman, Śakra! Hence art thou praised as Hero, foe destroyer<br> Him who gives pure gifts may I praise unceasing. Sage, quicken the Aṅgirases by devotion.</p>
