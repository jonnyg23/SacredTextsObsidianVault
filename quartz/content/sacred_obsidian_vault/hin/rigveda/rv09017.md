 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVII. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. LIKE rivers down a steep descent, slaying the Vṛtras, full of zeal,<br> The rapid Soma-streams have flowed.<br> 2 The drops of Soma juice effused fall like the rain upon the earth:<br> To Indra flow the Soma-streams.<br> 3 With swelling wave the gladdening drink, the Soma, flows intoṭhe sieve,<br> Loving the Gods and slaying fiends.<br> 4 It hastens to the pitchers, poured upon the sieve it waxes strong<br> At sacrifices through the lauds.<br> 5 Soma, thou shinest mounting heaven as twere above light's triple realm,<br> And moving secm'st to speed the Sun.<br> 6 To him, the head of sacnfice, singers and bards have sung their songs,<br> Offering what he loves to see.<br> 7 The men, the sages with their hymns, eager for help, deck thee strong &amp;teed,<br> Deck thee for service of the Gods.<br> 8 Flow onward to the stream of meath rest efficacious in thy home,<br> Fair, to be drunk at sacrifice.</p>
