 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVI. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU helpest him whose grass is trimmed, who sheds the juice, O Śatakratu, drink Soma to make thee glad.<br> The share which they have fixed for thee, thou, Indra, Victor oer all hosts and space, begirt with Maruts, Lord of Heroes, winner of the floods.<br> 2 Maghavan, help thy worshipper: let him help thee. O Śatakratu, drink Soma to make thee glad.<br> The share which they have fixed for thee, etc.<br> 3 Thou aidest Gods with food, and that with might aids thee,<br> O Śatakratu, drink Soma to make thee glad.<br> 4 Creator of the heaven, creator of the earth, O Śatakratu, drink Soma to make thee glad.<br> 5 Father of cattle, father of all steeds art thou. O Śatakratu, drink Soma to make thee glad.<br> 6 Stone-hurler, glorify the Atris' hymn of praise. O Śatakratu, drink Soma to make thee glad.<br> 7 Hear thou Śyāvāśva while he pours to thee, as erst thou heardest Atri when he wrought his holy rites.<br> Indra, thou only gavest Trasadasyu aid in the fierce fight with heroes, strengthening his prayers.</p>
