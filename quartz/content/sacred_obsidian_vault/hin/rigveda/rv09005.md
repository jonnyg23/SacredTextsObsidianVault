 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V Āprīs.</h3>  
 <p>1. ENKINDLED, Pavamana, Lord, sends forth his light on, every side<br> In friendly show, the bellowing Bull.<br> 2 He, Pavamana, Self-produced, speeds onward sharpening his horns:<br> He glitters through the firmament.<br> 3 Brilliant like wealth, adorable, with splendour Pavamana shines,<br> Mightily with the streams of meath.<br> 4 The tawny Pavamana, who strews from of old the grass with might,<br> Is worshipped, God amid the Gods.<br> 5 The golden, the Celestial Doors are lifted with their frames on high,<br> By Pavamana glorified.<br> 6 With passion Pavamana longs for the great lofty pair, well-formed<br> Like beauteous maidens, Night and Dawn<br> 7 Both Gods who look on men I call, Celestial Heralds: Indra's Self<br> Is Pavamana, yea, the Bull.<br> 8 This, Pavamana's sacrifice, shall the three beauteous Goddesses,<br> Sarasvatī and Bhāratī and Iḷā, Mighty One, attend.<br> 9 1 summon Tvaṣṭar hither, our protector, champion, earliest-born,<br> Indu is Indra, tawny Steer; Pavamana is Prajāpati.<br> 10 O Pavamana, with the meath in streams anoint Vanaspati,<br> The ever-green. the golden-hued, refulgent, with a thousand boughs.<br> 11 Come to the consecrating rite of Pavamana, all ye Gods,<br> Vāyu, Sūrya, Bṛhaspati, Indra, and Agni, in accord.</p>
