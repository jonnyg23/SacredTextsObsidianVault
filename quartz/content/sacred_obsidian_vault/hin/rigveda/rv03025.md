 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXV. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU art the sapient Son of Dyaus, O Agni, yes and the Child of Earth, who knowest all things.<br> Bring the Gods specially, thou Sage, for worship.<br> 2. Agni the wise bestows the might of heroes grants strengthening food, preparing it for nectar.<br> Thou who art rich in food bring the Gods hither.<br> 3 Agni, infallible, lights Earth and Heaven, immortal Goddesses gracious to all men,<br> Lord through his strength, splendid through adorations.<br> 4 Come to the sacrifice, Agni and Indra come to the offerer's house who hath the Soma.<br> Come, friendly-minded, Gods, to drink the Soma.<br> 5 In the floods' home art thou enkindled, Agni, O Jātavedas, Son of Strength, eternal,<br> Exalting with thine help the gathering-places.</p>
