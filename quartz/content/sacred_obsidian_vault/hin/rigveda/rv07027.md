 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. MEN call on Indra in the armed encounter that he may make the hymns they sing decisive.<br> Hero, rejoicing in thy might, in combat give us a portion of the stall of cattle,<br> 2 Grant, Indra Maghavan, invoked of many, to these my friends the strength which thou possessest.<br> Thou, Maghavan, hast rent strong places open: unclose for us, Wise God, thy hidden bounty.<br> 3 King of the living world, of men, is Indra, of all in varied form that earth containeth.<br> Thence to the worshipper he giveth riches: may he enrich us also when we laud him.<br> 4 Maghavan Indra, when we all invoke him, bountiful ever sendeth strength to aid us:<br> Whose perfect guerdon, never failing, bringeth wealth to the men, to friends the thing they covet.<br> 5 Quick, Indra, give us room and way to riches, and let us bring thy mind to grant us treasures,<br> That we may win us cars and Steeds and cattle. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
