 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXX. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. STREAMS of this Potent One have flowed easily to the straining-cloth:<br> While he is cleansed he lifts his voice.<br> 2 Indu, by pressers urged to speed, bellowing out while beautified.<br> Sends forth a very mighty sound.<br> 3 Pour on us, Soma, with thy stream manconquering might which many crave,<br> Accompanied with hero sons.<br> 4 Hither hath Pavamana flowed, Soma flowed hither in a stream,<br> To settle in the vats of wood.<br> 5 To waters with the stones they drive thee tawny-hued, most rich in sweets,<br> O Indu, to be Indra's drink.<br> 6 For Indra, for the Thunderer press the Soma very rich in sweets,<br> Lovely, inspiriting, for strength.</p>
