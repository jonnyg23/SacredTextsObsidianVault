 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VIII Praskaṇva's Go.</h3>  
 <p>1. THY bounty, Dasyave-vrka, exhaustless hath displayed itself:<br> Its fulness is as broad as heaven.<br> 2 Ten thousand Dasyave-vrka, the son of Putakrata, hath<br> From his own wealth bestowed on me.<br> 3 A hundred asses hath he given, a hundred head of fleecy sheep,<br> A hundred slaves, and wreaths besides.<br> 4 There also was a mare led forth, picked out for Putakrata's sake,<br> Not of the horses of the herd.<br> 5 Observant Agni hath appeared, oblation-bearer with his car.<br> Agni with his resplendent flame hath shone on high as shines the Sun, hath shone like Sūrya in theheavens.</p>
