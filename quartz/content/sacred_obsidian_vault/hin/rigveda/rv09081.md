 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXI. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. ONWARD to Indra's throat move, beauteously adorned, the waves of Soma as he purifies himself,<br> When they, brought forward with the lovely curd of kine, effused, have cheered the Hero to bestow his gifts.<br> 2 Hither hath Soma flowed unto the beakers, like a chariot-horse, a stallion swift upon his way.<br> Thus, knowing both the generations, he obtains the rights and dues of Gods from yonder and from hence.<br> 3 While thou art cleansed, O Soma, scatter wealth on us; Indu, bestow great bounty as a liberal Prince.<br> Giver of life, with wisdom help to opulence; strew not our home possessions far away from us.<br> 4 Hither let Pūṣan Pavamana come to us, Varuṇa, Mitra, bountiful, of one accord,<br> The Maruts, Aśvins, Vāyu, and Bṛhaspati, Savitar, Tvaṣṭar, tractable Sarasvatī.<br> 5 Both Heaven and Earth, the all-invigorating Pair, Vidhatar, Aditi, and Aryaman the God,<br> Bhaga who blesses men, the spacious Firmament,let all the Gods in Pavamana take delight.</p>
