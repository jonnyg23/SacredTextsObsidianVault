 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. HERALD and teacher was he born, a guardian for our patrons help,<br> Earner by rites of noble wealth. That Strong One may we grasp and guide;<br> 2 In whom, Leader of sacrifice, the seven reins, far extended, meet;<br> Who furthers, man-like, eighth in place, as Cleanser, all the work divine.<br> 3 When swift he follows this behest, bird-like he chants the holy prayers.<br> He holds all knowledge in his grasp even as the felly rounds the wheel.<br> 4 Together with pure mental power, pure, as Director, was he born.<br> Skilled in his own unchanging laws he waxes like the growing boughs.<br> 5 Clothing them in his hues, the kine of him the Leader wait on him.<br> Is he not better than the Three, the Sisters who have come to us?<br> 6 When, laden with the holy oil, the <a href="errata.htm#10">Sister</a> by the Mother stands,<br> The Priest delights in their approach, as corn at coming of the rain.<br> 7 For his support let him perform as ministrant his priestly task;<br> Yea, song of praise and sacrifice: we have bestowed, let us obtain.<br> 8 That so this man well skilled, may pay worship to all the Holy Ones.<br> And, Agni, this our sacrifice which we have here prepared, to thee.</p>
