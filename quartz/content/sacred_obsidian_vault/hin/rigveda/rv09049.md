 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIX. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. Poust down the rain upon us, pour a wave of waters from the sky,<br> And plenteous store of wholesome fbod.<br> 2 Flow onward with that stream of thine, whereby the cows have come to us,<br> The kine of strangers to our home.<br> 3 Chief Friend of Gods in sacred rites, pour on us fatness with thy stream,<br> Ppur down on us a flood of rain.<br> 4 To give us vigour, with thy stream run through the fleecy straining-cloth<br> For verily the Gods will bear.<br> 5 Onward hath Pavamana flowed and beaten off the Rākṣasas,<br> Flashing out splendour as of old.</p>
