 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN X. Aśvins.</h3>  
 <p>1. WHETHER ye travel far away or dwell in yonder light of heaven,<br> Or in a mansion that is built above the sea, come thence, ye Aśvins, hitherward.<br> 2 Or if for Manu.ye prepared the sacrifice, remember also Kaṇva's son.<br> I call Bṛhaspati, Indra, Viṣṇu, all the gods, the Aśvins borne by rapid steeds.<br> 3 Those Aśvins I invoke who work marvels, brought hither to receive,<br> With whom our friendship is most famed, and kinship passing that of Gods.<br> 4 On whom the solemn rites depend, whose worshippers rise without the Sun:<br> These who foreknow the holy work of sacrifice, and by their Godhead drink the sweets of Soma juice.<br> 5 Whether ye, Lords of ample wealth, now linger in the cast or west,<br> With Druhyu, or with Anu, Yadu, Turvaga, I call you hither; come to me.<br> 6 Lords of great riches, whether through the firmament ye fly or speed through heaven and earth,<br> Or with your Godlike natures stand upon your cars, come thence, O Aśvins, hitherward.</p>
