 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVII Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. LET thy Bay Horses, yoked, O mighty Indra, bring thy car hither fraught with every blessing.<br> For thee, the Heavenly, een the poor invoketh: may we this day, thy feast-companions, prosper.<br> 2 Forth to the vat the brown drops flow for service, and purified proceed directly forward.<br> May Indra drink of this, our guest aforetime, Celestial King of the strong draught of Soma.<br> 3 Bringing us hitherward all-potent Indra on well-wheeled chariot, may the Steeds who bear him<br> Convey him on the road direct to glory, and neer may Vāyu's Amṛta cease and fail him.<br> 4 Supreme, he stirs this man to give the guerdon,Indra, most efficacious of the princes,<br> Wherewith, O Thunderer, thou removest sorrow, and, Bold One! partest wealth among the nobles.<br> 5 Indra is hewho gives enduring vigour: may our songs magnify the God Most Mighty.<br> Best Vṛtra-slayer be the Hero Indra these things he gives as Prince, with strong endeavour.</p>
