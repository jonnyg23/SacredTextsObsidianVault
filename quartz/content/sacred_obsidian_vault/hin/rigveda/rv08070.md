 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXX. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. INDRA, God of the mighty arm, gather for us with thy right hand<br> Manifold and nutritious spoil.<br> 2 We know thee mighty in thy deeds, of mighty bounty, mighty wealth,<br> Mighty in measure, prompt to aid.<br> 3 Hero, when thou art fain to give, neither may Gods nor mortal men<br> Restrain thee like a fearful Bull.<br> 4 Come, let us glorify Indra, Lord supreme of wealth, Self-ruling King:<br> In bounty may he harm us not.<br> 5 Let prelude sound and following chant so let him hear the Sāman sung,<br> And with his bounty answer us.<br> 6 O Indra, with thy right hand bring, and with thy left remember us.<br> Let us not lose our share of wealth.<br> 7 Come nigh, O Bold One, boldly bring hither the riches of the churl<br> Who giveth least of all the folk.<br> 8 Indra, the booty which thou hast with holy singers to receive,<br> Even that booty win with us.<br> 9 Indra, thy swiftly-coming spoil, the booty which rejoices all,<br> Sounds quick in concert with our hopes.</p>
