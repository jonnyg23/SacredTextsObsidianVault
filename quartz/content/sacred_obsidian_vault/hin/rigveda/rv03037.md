 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. O INDRA, for the strength that slays Vṛtra and conquers in the fight,<br> We turn thee hitherward to us.<br> 2 O Indra, Lord of Hundred Powers, may those who praise thee hitherward.<br> Direct thy spirit and thine eye.<br> 3 O Indra, Lord of Hundred Powers, with all our songs we invocate<br> Thy names for triumph over foes.<br> 4 We strive for glory through the powers immense of him whom many praise,<br> Of Indra who supports mankind.<br> 5 For Vṛtra's slaughter I address Indra whom many invocate,<br> To win us booty in the wars.<br> 6 In battles be victorious. We seek thee, Lord of Hundred Powers,<br> Indra, that Vṛtra may be slain.<br> 7 In splendid combats of the hosts, in glories where the fight is won.<br> Indra, be victor over foes.<br> 8 Drink thou the Soma for our help, bright, vigilant, exceeding strong,<br> O Indra, Lord of Hundred Powers.<br> 9 O Śatakratu, powers which thou mid the Five Races hast displayed-<br> These, Indra, do I claim of thee.<br> 10 Indra, great glory hast thou gained. Win splendid fame which none may mar<br> We make thy might perpetual.<br> 11 Come to us either from anear, Or, Śakra, come from far away.<br> Indra, wherever be thy home, come to us thence, O Thunder-armed.</p>
