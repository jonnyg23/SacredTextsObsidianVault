 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIX. Mitra-Varuṇa.</h3>  
 <p>1. THREE spheres of light, O Varuṇa, three heavens, three firmaments ye comprehend, O Mitra:<br> Waxed strong, ye keep the splendour of dominion, guarding the Ordinance that lasts for ever.<br> 2 Ye, Varuṇa, have kine who yield refreshment; Mitra, your floods pour water full of sweetness.<br> There stand the Three Steers, splendid in their brightness, who fill the three world-bowls with genial moisture.<br> 3 I call at dawn on Aditi the Goddess, I call at noon and when the Sun is setting.<br> I pray, O Mitra-Varuṇa, for safety, for wealth and progeny, in rest and trouble.<br> 4 Ye who uphold the region, sphere of brightness, ye who support earth's realm Divine Ādityas,<br> The Immortal Gods, O Varuṇa and Mitra, never impair your everlasting statutes.</p>
