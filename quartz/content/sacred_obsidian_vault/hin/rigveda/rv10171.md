 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXI. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. FOR Iṭa's sake who pressed the juice, thou, Indra, didst protect his car,<br> And hear the Soma-giver's call.<br> 2 Thou from his skin hast borne the head of the swift-moving combatant,<br> And sought the Soma-pourer's home.<br> 3 Venya, that mortal man, hast thou, for Āstrabudhna the devout,<br> O Indra, many a time set free.<br> 4 Bring, Indra, to the east again that Sun who now is in the west,<br> Even against the will of Gods.</p>
