 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXXXVII. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. To Agni send I forth my song, to him the Bull of all the folk:<br> So may he bear us past our foes.<br> 2 Who from the distance far away shines brilliantly across the wastes:<br> So may he bear us past our foes.<br> 3 The Bull with brightly-gleaming flame who utterly consumes the fiends<br> So may he bear us past our foes.<br> 4 Who looks on all existing things and comprehends them with his view:<br> So may he bear us past our foes.<br> 5 Resplendent Agni, who was born in farthest region of the air:<br> So may he bear us past our foes.</p>
