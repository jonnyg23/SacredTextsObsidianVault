 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CL. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU, bearer of oblations, though kindled, art kindled for the Gods.<br> With the Ādityas, Rudras, Vasus, come to us: to show us favour come to us.<br> 2 Come hither and accept with joy this sacrifice and hymn of ours.<br> O kindled God, we mortals are invoking thee, calling on thee to show us grace.<br> 3 I laud thee Jātavedas, thee Lord of all blessings, with my song.<br> Agni, bring hitherward the Gods whose Laws we love, whose laws we love, to show us grace.<br> 4 Agni the God was made the great High-Priest of Gods, Ṛṣis have kindled Agni, men of mortal mould.<br> Agni I invocate for winning ample wealth. kindly disposed for winning wealth.<br> 5 Atri and Bharadvāja and Gavisthira, Kaṇva and Trasadasyu, in our fight he helped.<br> On Agni calls Vasiṣṭha, even the household priest, the household priest to win his grace.</p>
