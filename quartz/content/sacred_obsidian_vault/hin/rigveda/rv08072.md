 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXII. Viśvedevas.</h3>  
 <p>1. WE choose unto ourselves that high protection of the Mighty Gods<br> That it may help and succour us.<br> 2 May they be ever our allies, Varuṇa, Mitra, Aryaman,<br> Far-seeing Gods who prosper us.<br> 3 Ye furtherers of holy Law, transport us safe oer many woes,<br> As over water-floods in ships.<br> 4 Dear wealth be Aryaman to us, Varuṇa dear wealth meet for praise:<br> Dear wealth we choose unto ourselves.<br> 5 For Sovrans of dear wealth are ye, Ādityas, not of sinner's wealth,<br> Ye sapient Gods who slay the foe.<br> 6 We in our homes, ye Bounteous Ones, and while we journey on the road,<br> Invoke you, Gods, to prosper us.<br> 7 Regard us, Indra, Viṣṇu, here, ye Aśvins and the Marut host,<br> Us who are kith and kin to you.<br> 8 Ye Bounteous Ones, from time of old we here set forth our brotherhood,<br> Our kinship in. the Mother's womb.<br> 9 Then come with Indra for your chief, as early day, ye Bounteous Gods<br> Yea, 1 address you now for this.</p>
