 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CLXIV. Dream-charm.</h3>  
 <p>1. AVAUNT, thou Master of the mind Depart, and vanish far away.<br> Look on Destruction far from hence. The live man's mind is manifold.<br> 2 A happy boon do men elect, a mighty blessing they obtain.<br> Bliss with Vaivasvata they see. The live man's mind seeks many a place.<br> 3 If by address, by blame, by imprecation we have committed sin, awake or sleeping,<br> All hateful acts of ours, all evil doings may Agni bear away to distant places.<br> 4 When, Indra, Brahmaṇaspati, our deeds are wrongful and unjust,<br> May provident Aṅgirasa prevent our foes from troubling, us.<br> 5 We have prevailed this day and won: we are made free from sin and guilt.<br> Ill thoughts, that visit us awake or sleeping, seize the man we hate, yea, seize the man who hateth us.</p>
