 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. DRINK, Indra, Marut-girt, as Bull, the Soma, for joy, for rapture even as thou listest.<br> Pour down the flood of meath within thy belly: thou from of old art King of Soma juices.<br> 2 Indra, accordant, with the banded Maruts, drink Soma, Hero, as wise Vṛtra-slayer.<br> Slay thou our foemen, drive away assailants and make us safe on every side from danger.<br> 3 And, drinker at due seasons, drink in season, Indra, with friendly Gods, our pressed-out Soma.<br> The Maruts following, whom thou madest sharers, gave thee the victory, and thou slewest Vṛtra.<br> 4 Drink Soma, Indra, banded with the Maruts who, Maghavan, strengthened thee at Ahi's slaughter,<br> 'Gainst Śambara, Lord of Bays! in winning cattle, and now rejoice in thee, the holy Singers.<br> 5 The Bull whose strength hath waxed, whom Maruts follow, free-giving Indra, the celestial Ruler,<br> Mighty, all-conquering, the victory-giver, him let us call to grant us new protection.</p>
