 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. THOU Agni, God mid mortal men, art guard of sacred rites, thou art<br> To be adored at sacrifice.<br> 2 O Mighty Agni, thou must be glorified at our festivals,<br> Bearing our offerings to the Gods.<br> 3 O Jātavedas Agni, fight and drive our foes afar from us,<br> Themand their godless enmities.<br> 4 Thou, Jātavedas, seekest not the worship of a hostile man,<br> However nigh itbe to thee.<br> 5 We sages, mortals as we are, adore the mighty name oof thee,<br> Immortal Jātavedas' name.<br> 6 Sages, we call the Sage to help, mortals, we call the God to aid:<br> We call on Agni with our songs.<br> 7 May Vatsa draw- thy mind away even from thy loftiest dwelling-place,<br> Agni, with song that yearns for thee.<br> 8 Thou art the same in many a place: mid all the people thou art Lord.<br> In fray and fightt we call on thee.<br> 9 When we are seeking strength we call Agni to help us in the strife,<br> The giver of rich gifts in war.<br> 10 Ancient, adorablie at sacrifices, Priest from of old, meet for our praise, thou sittest.<br> Fill full and satisfy thy body, Agni, and win us happiness by offering worship.</p>
