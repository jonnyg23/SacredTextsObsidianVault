 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCIX. Viṣṇu.</h3>  
 <p>1. MEN come not nigh thy majesty who growest beyond all bound and measure with thy body.<br> Both thy two regions of the earth, O Viṣṇu, we know: thou God, knowest the highest also.<br> 2 None who is born or being born, God Viṣṇu, hath reached the utmost limit of thy grandeur.<br> The vast high vault of heaven hast thou supported, and fixed earth's eastern pinnacle securely.<br> 3 Rich in sweet food be ye, and rich in milch-kine, with fertile pastures, fain to do men service.<br> Both these worlds, Viṣṇu, hast thou stayed asunder, and firmly fixed the earth with pegs around it.<br> 4 Ye have made spacious room for sacrificing by generating Sūrya, Dawn, and Agni.<br> O Heroes, ye have conquered in your battles even the bull-jawed Dāsa's wiles and magic.<br> 5 Ye have destroyed, thou, Indra, and thou Viṣṇu, Śambara's nine-and-ninety fenced castles.<br> Ye Twain smote down a hundred times a thousand resistless heroes of the royal Varcin.<br> 6 This is the lofty hymn of praise, exalting the Lords of Mighty Stride, the strong and lofty.<br> I laud you in the solemn synods, Viṣṇu: pour ye food on us in our camps, O Indra.<br> 7 O Viṣṇu, unto thee my lips cry Vaṣaṭ! Let this mine offering, Sipivista, please thee.<br> May these my songs of eulogy exalt thee. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
