 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_401"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. Then Ushasta Kâkrâya<i>n</i>a asked. 'Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya,' he said, 'tell me the Brahman which is visible, not invisible <a name="fr_402"></a><a href="#fn_402"><font size="1">3</font></a>, the Self (âtman), who is within all.'</p>  
 <p>Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya replied: 'This, thy Self, who is within all.'</p>  
 <p>'Which Self, O Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya, is within all?'</p>  
 <p>Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya replied: 'He who breathes in the up-breathing, he is thy Self, and within all. He who breathes in the down-breathing, he is thy Self, and within all. He who breathes in the on-breathing, he is thy Self, and within all. He who breathes in</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="green">p. 129</font></a></p>  
 <p>the out-breathing, he is thy Self, and within all. This is thy Self, who is within all.'</p>  
 <p>2. Ushasta Kâkrâya<i>n</i>a said: 'As one might say, this is a cow, this is a horse, thus has this been explained by thee. Tell me the Brahman which is visible, not invisible, the Self, who is within all.'</p>  
 <p>Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya replied: 'This, thy Self, who is within all.'</p>  
 <p>'Which Self, O Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya, is within all?'</p>  
 <p>Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avalkya replied: 'Thou couldst not see the (true) seer of sight, thou couldst not hear the (true) hearer of hearing, nor perceive the perceiver of perception, nor know the knower of knowledge. This is thy Self, who is within all. Everything also is of evil.' After that Ushasta Kâkrâya<i>n</i>a held his peace.</p>
