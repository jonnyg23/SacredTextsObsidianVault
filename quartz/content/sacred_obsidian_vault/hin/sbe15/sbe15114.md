 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THIRD PRAPÂ<i>TH</i>AKA.</h3>  
 <p>1. The Vâlakhilyas said to Pra<i>g</i>âpati Kratu: 'O Saint, if thou thus showest the greatness of that Self, then who is that other different one, also called Self <a name="fr_979"></a><a href="#fn_976"><font size="1">4</font></a>, who really overcome by bright and dark fruits of action, enters on a good or bad birth?</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="green">p. 296</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Downward or upward is his course <a name="fr_980"></a><a href="#fn_977"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and overcome by the pairs (distinction between hot and cold, pleasure and pain, &amp;c.) he roams about <a name="fr_981"></a><a href="#fn_978"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>2. Pra<i>g</i>âpati Kratu replied: 'There is indeed that other <a name="fr_982"></a><a href="#fn_979"><font size="1">3</font></a> different one, called the elemental Self (Bhûtâtmâ), who, overcome by bright and dark fruits of action, enters on a good or bad birth: downward or upward is his course, and overcome by the pairs he roams about. And this is his explanation: The five Tanmâtrâs <a name="fr_983"></a><a href="#fn_980"><font size="1">4</font></a> (sound, touch, form, taste, smell) are called Bhûta; also the five Mahâbhûtas (gross elements) are called Bhûta. Then the aggregate <a name="fr_984"></a><a href="#fn_981"><font size="1">5</font></a> of all these is called <i>s</i>arîra, body <a name="fr_985"></a><a href="#fn_982"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And lastly he of whom it was said that he dwelt in the body <a name="fr_986"></a><a href="#fn_983"><font size="1">7</font></a>, he is called Bhûtâtmâ, the elemental Self. Thus his immortal Self <a name="fr_987"></a><a href="#fn_984"><font size="1">8</font></a> is like a drop of water on a lotus leaf <a name="fr_988"></a><a href="#fn_985"><font size="1">9</font></a>, and he himself is overcome by the qualities of nature. Then <a name="fr_989"></a><a href="#fn_986"><font size="1">10</font></a>, because he is thus overcome, he becomes bewildered, and because he is bewildered, he saw not the creator, the holy Lord, abiding within himself. Carried along by the waves of the qualities <a name="fr_990"></a><a href="#fn_987"><font size="1">11</font></a>, darkened in his imaginations, unstable, fickle,</p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_297"><font size="1" color="green">p. 297</font></a></p>  
 <p>crippled, full of desires, vacillating, he enters into belief, believing "I am he," "this is mine <a name="fr_991"></a><a href="#fn_988"><font size="1">1</font></a>;" he binds his Self by his Self, as a bird with a net, and overcome afterwards by the fruits of what he has done, he enters on a good and bad birth; downward or upward is his course, and overcome by the pairs he roams about.'</p>  
 <p>They asked: 'Which is it?' And he answered them:</p>  
 <p>3. 'This also has elsewhere been said: He who acts, is the elemental Self; he who causes to act by means of the organs <a name="fr_992"></a><a href="#fn_989"><font size="1">2</font></a>, is the inner man (anta<i>h</i>purusha). Now as even a ball of iron, pervaded (overcome) by fire, and hammered by smiths, becomes manifold (assumes different forms, such as crooked, round, large, small <a name="fr_993"></a><a href="#fn_990"><font size="1">3</font></a>), thus the elemental Self, pervaded (overcome) by the inner man, and hammered by the qualities, becomes manifold <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_991"><font size="1">4</font></a>. And the four tribes (mammals, birds, &amp;c.), the fourteen worlds (Bhûr, &amp;c.), with all the number of beings, multiplied eighty-four times <a name="fr_995"></a><a href="#fn_992"><font size="1">5</font></a>, all this appears as manifoldness. And those multiplied things are impelled by man (purusha) as the wheel by the potter <a name="fr_996"></a><a href="#fn_993"><font size="1">6</font></a>. And as when the ball of iron is hammered, the fire is not overcome, so the (inner) man is not overcome, but the elemental Self is overcome, because it has united itself (with the elements).</p>

 <p><a name="page_298"><font size="1" color="green">p. 298</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. And it has been said elsewhere <a name="fr_997"></a><a href="#fn_994"><font size="1">1</font></a>: This body produced from marriage, and endowed with growth <a name="fr_998"></a><a href="#fn_995"><font size="1">2</font></a> in darkness, came forth by the urinary passage, was built up with bones, bedaubed with flesh, thatched with skin, filled with ordure, urine, bile, slime, marrow, fat, oil <a name="fr_999"></a><a href="#fn_996"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and many impurities besides, like a treasury full of treasures <a name="fr_1000"></a><a href="#fn_997"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>5. And it has been said elsewhere: Bewilderment, fear, grief, sleep, sloth, carelessness, decay, sorrow, hunger, thirst, niggardliness, wrath, infidelity, ignorance, envy, cruelty <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_998"><font size="1">5</font></a>, folly, shamelessness, meanness <a name="fr_1002"></a><a href="#fn_999"><font size="1">6</font></a>, pride, changeability <a name="fr_1003"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">7</font></a>, these are the results of the quality of darkness (tama<i>h</i>) <a name="fr_1004"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">8</font></a>.</p>

 <p><a name="page_299"><font size="1" color="green">p. 299</font></a></p>  
 <p>Inward thirst, fondness, passion, covetousness, unkindness, love, hatred, deceit <a name="fr_1005"></a><a href="sbe15115.htm#fn_1002"><font size="1">1</font></a>, jealousy, vain restlessness, fickleness <a name="fr_1006"></a><a href="sbe15115.htm#fn_1003"><font size="1">2</font></a>, unstableness, emulation, greed, patronising of friends, family pride, aversion to disagreeable objects, devotion to agreeable objects, whispering <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="sbe15115.htm#fn_1004"><font size="1">3</font></a>, prodigality, these are the results of the quality of passion (ra<i>g</i>as).</p>  
 <p>By these he is filled, by these he is overcome, and therefore this elemental Self assumes manifold forms, yes, manifold forms.'</p>
