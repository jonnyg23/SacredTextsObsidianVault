 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THIRD BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Pra<i>g</i>âpati is the heart, is this Brahman, is all this. The heart, h<i>ri</i>daya, consists of three syllables. One syllable is h<i>ri</i>, and to him who knows this, his own people and others bring offerings <a name="fr_553"></a><a href="#fn_553"><font size="1">1</font></a>. One syllable is da, and to him who knows this, his own people and others bring gifts. One syllable is yam, and he who knows this, goes to heaven (svarga) as his world.</p>
