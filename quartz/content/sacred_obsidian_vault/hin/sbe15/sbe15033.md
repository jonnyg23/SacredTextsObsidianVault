 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">TWELFTH ANUVÂKA.</h3>  
 <p>1. May Mitra be propitious to us, and Varu<i>n</i>a, Aryaman also, Indra, B<i>ri</i>haspati, and the wide-striding Vish<i>n</i>u! Adoration to Brahman! Adoration to thee, O Vâyu! Thou indeed art the visible Brahman. I proclaimed thee alone as the visible Brahman.</p>  
 <p>(1-5) I proclaimed the right. I proclaimed the true. It protected me. It protected the teacher. Yes, it protected me, it protected the teacher. Om! Peace! peace! peace!</p>
