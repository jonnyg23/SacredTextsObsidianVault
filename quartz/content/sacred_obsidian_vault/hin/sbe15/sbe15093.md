 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 2 (SBE15)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">FIFTEENTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1.  <a name="fr_584"></a><a href="#fn_584"><font size="1">2</font></a>The face of the True (the Brahman) is covered with a golden disk <a name="fr_585"></a><a href="#fn_585"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Open that, O Pûshan <a name="fr_586"></a><a href="#fn_586"><font size="1">4</font></a>, that we may see the nature of the True <a name="fr_587"></a><a href="#fn_587"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. O Pûshan, only seer, Yama (judge), Sûrya (sun), son of Pra<i>g</i>âpati <a name="fr_588"></a><a href="#fn_588"><font size="1">6</font></a>, spread thy rays and gather them!</p>

 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="green">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p>The light which is thy fairest form, I see it. I am what he is (viz. the person in the sun).</p>  
 <p>3. Breath to air and to the immortal! Then this my body ends in ashes. Om! Mind, remember! Remember thy deeds! Mind, remember! Remember thy deeds <a name="fr_589"></a><a href="#fn_589"><font size="1">1</font></a>!</p>  
 <p>4. Agni, lead us on to wealth (beatitude) by a good path <a name="fr_590"></a><a href="#fn_590"><font size="1">2</font></a>, thou, O God, who knowest all things! Keep far from us crooked evil, and we shall offer thee the fullest praise! (Rv. I, 189, 1.)</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_584"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_584">199:2</a> These verses, which are omitted here in the Mâdhyandina text, are found at the end of the Vâ<i>g</i>asaneyi-upanishad 15-18. They are supposed to be a prayer addressed to Âditya by a dying person.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_585"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_585">199:3</a> Mahîdhara on verse 17: The face of the true (purusha in the sun), is covered by a golden disk.' <i>S</i>aṅkara explains here mukha, face, by mukhya<i>m</i> svarûpam, the principal form or nature.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_586"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_586">199:4</a> Pûshan is here explained as a name of Savit<i>ri</i>, the sun; likewise all the names in the next verse.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_587"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_587">199:5</a> Cf. Maitr. Up. VI, 35.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_588"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_588">199:6</a> Of Î<i>s</i>vara or Hira<i>n</i>yagarbha.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_589"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_589">200:1</a> The Vâ<i>g</i>asaneyi-sa<i>m</i>hitâ reads: Om, krato smara, <i>k</i><i>li</i>be smara, k<i>ri</i>ta<i>m</i> smara. Uva<i>t</i>a holds that Agni, fire, who has been worshipped in youth and manhood, is here invoked in the form of mind, or that kratu is meant for sacrifice. 'Agni, remember me 'Think of the world! Remember my deeds!' K<i>li</i>be is explained by Mahîdhara as a dative of k<i>li</i>p, k<i>li</i>p meaning loka, world, what is made to be enjoyed (kalpyate bhogâya).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_590"></a><a href="sbe15093.htm#fr_590">200:2</a> Not by the Southern path, the dark, from which there is a fresh return to life.</p>
