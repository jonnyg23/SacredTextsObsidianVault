 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="green">p. 50</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SEVENTH ANUVÂKA.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'The earth, the sky, heaven, the four quarters, and the intermediate quarters,'--'Agni (fire), Vâyu (air), Âditya (sun), <i>K</i>andramas (moon), and the stars,'--'Water, herbs, trees, ether, the universal Self (virâ<i>g</i>),'--so much with reference to material objects (bhûta).</p>  
 <p>Now with reference to the self (the body): 'Prâ<i>n</i>a (up-breathing), Apâna (down-breathing), Vyâna (back-breathing), Udâna (out-breathing), and Samâ<i>n</i>a (on-breathing),'--'The eye, the ear, mind, speech, and touch,'--'The skin, flesh, muscle, bone, and marrow.' Having dwelt on this (fivefold arrangement of the worlds, the gods, beings, breathings, senses, and elements of the body), a <i>Ri</i>shi said: 'Whatever exists is fivefold (pâṅkta) <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>(1) By means of the one fivefold set (that referring to the body) he completes the other fivefold set.</p>
