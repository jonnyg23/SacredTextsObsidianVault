 <a name="page_239"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 239</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXXV.</h1>
 <p>1. He who makes a <i>S</i>râddha-offering while his father is alive, must offer it to those persons to whom his father offers (his <i>S</i>râddhas).</p>
 <p>2. (If he offers a <i>S</i>râddha) while both his father and grandfather are alive, (he must offer it to those persons) to whom his grandfather (offers his <i>S</i>râddhas).</p>
 <p>3. While his father, grandfather, and great-grandfather are alive, he must offer no <i>S</i>râddha at all.</p>
 <p>4. He whose father is dead (but whose grandfather is alive), must first of all offer a ball of rice to his father, after that, two balls to the two ancestors coming before his grandfather (or to his great-grandfather and to his fourth ascendant).</p>
 <p>5. He whose father and grandfather are dead (but whose great-grandfather is alive), must first offer two balls to those two, and then offer one ball to the grandfather of his grandfather.</p>
 <p>[7. Nand. renders this Sûtra differently, in accordance with his own theory regarding the number of the trenches.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[LXXV. 1. M. III, 220.--4. M. III, 221.--7. Y. I, 228.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_240"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 240</font></a></p>
 <p>6. He whose grandfather is dead (but whose father and great-grandfather are alive), must give one ball to his grandfather and two balls to the father and grandfather of his great-grandfather.</p>
 <p>7. An intelligent man must offer <i>S</i>râddhas to his maternal grandfather, and to the father and grandfather of him, in the same way (as to his paternal ancestors), duly modifying the Mantras. But the <i>S</i>râddhas addressed to other relatives, (uncles, brothers, and the like, must be performed) without Mantras.</p>
