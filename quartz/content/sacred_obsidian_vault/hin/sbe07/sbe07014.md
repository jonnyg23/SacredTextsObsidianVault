 <a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">XII.</h1>
 <p>1. Now follows the (rule regarding the ordeal by) water.</p>
 <p>2. (The defendant must enter) water which is free from mud, aquatic plants, (crabs and other) vicious animals, (porpoises or other) large rapacious animals living in water, fish, leeches, and other (animals or plants),</p>
 <p>3. The water having been addressed with the Mantras (mentioned hereafter), he must enter it, seizing the knees of another man, who must be free from friendship or hatred, and must dive into the water up to his navel.</p>
 <p>4. At the same time another man must discharge an arrow from a bow, which must neither be too strong nor too weak.</p>
 <p>5. That arrow must be fetched quickly by another man.</p>
 <p>6. He who is not seen above the water in the mean time is proclaimed innocent. But in the contrary case he is (declared) guilty, even though one limb of his only has become visible.</p>
 <p>7. 'Thou, O water, dwellest in the interior of all creatures, like a witness. O water, thou knowest what mortals do not comprehend.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[XII. 3-6. Y. II, 108, 109.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a></p>
 <p>8. 'This man being arraigned in a cause, desires to be cleared from guilt. Therefore mayest thou deliver him, lawfully from this perplexity.'</p>
