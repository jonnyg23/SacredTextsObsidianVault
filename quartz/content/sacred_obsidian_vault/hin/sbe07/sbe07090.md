 <a name="page_264"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 264</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXXXVIII.</h1>
 <p>1. A cow in the act of bringing forth a young one is (comparable to) the earth.</p>
 <p>2. By bestowing such a cow upon a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, after having decked her with ornaments, he obtains the same reward as if he were to bestow the earth (upon him),</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[6. <sup>1</sup> See LXVIII, 6, note. LXXXVIII. 1. Y. I, 207.--4. Y. I, 206.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_265"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 265</font></a></p>
 <p>3. There is a stanza in regard to this subject:</p>
 <p>4. 'One who full of faith and with intense application of mind gives away a pregnant cow, enters heaven for as many Yugas (or ages of the world) as that cow and her calf together have hairs on their bodies.'</p>
