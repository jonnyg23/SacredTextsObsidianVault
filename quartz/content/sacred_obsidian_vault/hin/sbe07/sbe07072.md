 <a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">LXX.</h1>
 <p>1. He must not sleep with his feet wet;</p>
 <p>2. Nor facing the north or the west;</p>
 <p>3. Nor naked; 4. Nor on wet (fresh) bamboo;</p>
 <p>5. Nor in the open air;</p>
 <p>6. Nor on a bedstead made of Palâ<i>s</i>a-wood;</p>
 <p>7. Nor on one made of the wood of five trees;</p>
 <p>8. Nor on one made of the wood of a tree which has been split by an elephant;</p>
 <p>9. Nor on a bedstead made of the wood of a tree that has been kindled by lightning;</p>
 <p>10. Nor on a broken bedstead;</p>
 <p>11. Nor on one made of scorched wood;</p>
 <p>12. Nor on one made of the wood of a tree that used to be watered with a jar;</p>
 <p>13. Nor in a burial-ground, nor in an empty house, nor in a temple;</p>
 <p>14. Nor with people who are restless of limb;</p>
 <p>15. Nor with women;</p>
 <p>16. Nor on grain, nor (in a stable of) cows, nor (on the couch of any of his) Gurus, nor on the fireplace, nor (in a building dedicated to the) gods.</p>
 <p>17. He must not sleep while the remnants of</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[LXX. 1. M. IV, 76.--2. Y. I, 136.--3. Â<i>s</i>v. III, 9, 6; M. IV, 75; Gaut. IX, 60.--13. M. IV, 57.--17. <i>S</i>â<u>n</u>kh. IV, 11, 17; Âpast. I, 1, 2, 24; Gaut. II, 13.</p>
 <p>7. Nand. mentions three explanations of this term: 1. a bedstead made of five pieces of wood (or of the wood of five trees); 2. a bedstead made of any of the five kinds of wood enumerated in the Vish<i>n</i>u-purâ<i>n</i>a; 3. a bedstead made of any of the five kinds of wood enumerated in Sûtras 8-12. The second explanation is inadmissible, because part of the species of wood mentioned in the passage of the Vish<i>n</i>u-purâ<i>n</i>a referred to as identical with those enumerated in Sûtras 8-12.]</p>
 </font><p><a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a></p>
 <p>food are on his hands or face, nor in the day-time, nor in the twilight, nor upon ashes, nor in a place soiled (by excrements and the like), nor in a wet place, nor on the top of a mountain.</p>
