 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003101">1</a></font></span>These two dear Bays bring hither Indra on his car, thunder-<br>    armed, joyous, meet for laud, to drink his fill.<br>   Many libations flow for him who loveth them: to Indra have:<br>    the gold-hued Soma juices run.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003102">2</a></font></span>The gold-hued drops have flowed to gratify his wish: the yellow.<br>    drops have urged the swift Bays to the Strong.<br>   He who speeds on with bay steeds even as he lists hath satisfied<br>    his longing for the golden drops.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003103">3</a></font></span>At the swift draught the Soma-drinker waxed in might, the iron.<br>   One with yellow beard and golden hair,<br>   He, Lord of tawny coursers. Lord of fleet-foot mares, will bear<br>    his bay steeds safely over all distress.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003104">4</a></font></span>His yellow-coloured jaws, like ladles, move apart, what time,.<br>    for strength, he makes the yellow-tinted stir,<br>   When. while the bowl stands there, he grooms his tawny steeds,<br>    when he hath drunk strong drink, the sweet juice that he:<br>    loves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003105">5</a></font></span>Yea, to the dear one's seat in homes of heaven and earth the-<br>    bay steeds' Lord hath whinnied like a horse for food.<br>   Then the great wish hath seized upon him mightily, and the<br>    beloved One hath gained high power of life.</p>
