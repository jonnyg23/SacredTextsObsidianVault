 <p><a name="page_373"><font size="1" color="green">p. 373</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXXVII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A composite hymn in praise of Indra</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013701">1</a></font></span>When, foul with secret spot and stain, ye hastened onward to<br>    the breast.<br>   All Indra's enemies were slain and passed away like froth and<br>    foam.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013702">2</a></font></span>Indra is he, O men, who gives us happiness: sport, urge the<br>    giver of delight to win the spoil.<br>   Bring quickly down, O priests, hither to give us aid, to drink<br>    the Soma, Indra son of Nishtigri.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013703">3</a></font></span>So have I glorified with praise strong Dadhikrāvan, conquering<br>    steed.<br>   Sweet may he make our mouths; may he prolong the days we<br>    have to live.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013704">4</a></font></span>The Somas very rich in sweets, for which the sieve is destined,<br>    flow,<br>   Effused, the source of Indra's joy. May your strong juices reach<br>    the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013705">5</a></font></span>Indu flows on for Indra's sake—thus have the deities declared.<br>   The Lord of Speech exerts himself, ruler of all, because of<br>    might.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013706">6</a></font></span>Inciter of the voice of song, with thousand streams the ocean<br>    flows,<br>   Even Soma, Lord of Opulence, the friend of Indra, day by day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013707">7</a></font></span>The black drop sank in Ansumati's bosom, advancing with ten<br>    thousand round about it.<br>   Indra with might longed for it as it panted: the hero-hearted<br>    laid aside his weapons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013708">8</a></font></span>I saw the drop in the far distance moving, on the slope bank of<br>   Ansumati's river,<br>   Like a black cloud that sank into the water. Heroes. I send you<br>    forth. Go, fight in battle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013709">9</a></font></span>And then the drop in Ansumati's bosom, splendid with light,<br>    assumed its proper body;<br>   And Indra with Brihaspati to aid him, conquered the godless<br>    tribes that came against him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013710">10</a></font></span>Then, at thy birth, thou wast the foeman, Indra, of those the<br>    seven who ne'er had met a rival. <a name="page_374"><font size="1" color="green">p. 374</font></a><br>   The hidden pair, the heaven and earth, thou foundest, and to<br>    the mighty worlds thou gavest pleasure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013711">11</a></font></span>So, Thunder-armed! thou with thy bolt of thunder didst boldly<br>    smite that power which none might equal;<br>   With weapons broughtest low the might of Sushna, and, Indra,<br>    foundest by thy strength the cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013712">12</a></font></span>We make this Indra very strong to strike the mighty Vritra<br>    dead:<br>   A vigorous Hero shall he be.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013713">13</a></font></span>Indra was made for giving, set, most mighty, o'er the joyous<br>    draught,<br>   Bright, meet for Soma, famed in song.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013714">14</a></font></span>By song, as 'twere, the powerful bolt which none may parry was<br>    prepared:<br>   Lofty, invincible he grew.</p>
