 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006001">1</a></font></span>For so thou art the hero's Friend, a Warrior too art thou, and<br>    strong:<br>   So may thy heart be won to us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006002">2</a></font></span>So hath the offering; wealthiest Lord, been paid by all the<br>    worshippers:<br>   So dwell thou, Indra, even with me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006003">3</a></font></span>Be not thou, like a slothful priest, O Lord of wealth and spoil:<br>    rejoice.<br>   In the pressed Soma blent with milk.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006004">4</a></font></span>So also is his excellence, great copious, rich in cattle, like<br>   A ripe branch to the worshipper. <a name="page_313"><font size="1" color="green">p. 313</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006005">5</a></font></span>For verily thy mighty powers, Indra, are saving helps at once<br>   Unto a worshipper like me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006006">6</a></font></span>So are his lovely gifts: let laud be said and praise to Indra sung.<br>   That he may drink the Soma juice.</p>
