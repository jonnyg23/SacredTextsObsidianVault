 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501101">1</a></font></span>Let him to whose house the Vrātya who possesses this knowledge<br>    comes as a guest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501102">2</a></font></span>Rise up of his own accord to meet him, and say, Vrātya, where<br>    didst thou pass the night? Vratya, here is water, Let them<br>    refresh thee. Vrātya, let it be as thou pleasest. Vrātya, as thy<br>    wish is so let it be. Vrātya, as thy desire is so be it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501103">3</a></font></span>When he says to his guest, Where didst thou pass the night? he<br>    reserves for himself thereby the paths that lead to the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501104">4</a></font></span>When he says to him, Here is water, he secures thereby water<br>    for himself.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501105">5</a></font></span>When he says to him, Let them refresh thee, he thereby wins<br>    vital breath to exceeding old age.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501106">6</a></font></span>When he says to him, Vrātya, let it be as thou pleasest, he<br>    secures to himself thereby what is pleasant. <a name="page_157"><font size="1" color="green">p. 157</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501107">7</a></font></span>That which is pleasant comes to him, and he is the beloved of<br>    the beloved, who is possessed of this knowledge.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501108">8</a></font></span>When he says to him, Vrātya, as thy will is so let it be, he<br>    secures to himself thereby the fulfilment of his will.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501109">9</a></font></span>Authority comes to him who possesses this knowledge, and he<br>    becomes the controller of the powerful.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501110">10</a></font></span>When he says to him, Vrātya, as thy desire is so be it, he secures<br>    to himself thereby the attainment of his desire.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1501111">11</a></font></span>His desire comes to him who possesses this knowledge and he<br>    gains the complete satisfaction of his wish.</p>
