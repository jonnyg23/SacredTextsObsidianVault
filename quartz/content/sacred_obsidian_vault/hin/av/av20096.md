 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009601">1</a></font></span>Taste this strong draught that gives thee vital vigour: with all<br>    thy chariot here unyoke thy coursers.<br>   Let not those other sacrificers stay thee, Indra: these juices shed<br>    for thee are ready.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009602">2</a></font></span>Thine is the juice effused, thine are the juices yet to be pressed:<br>    our resonant songs invite thee.<br>   O Indra, pleased to-day with this libation, come, thou who<br>    knowest all, and drink the Soma.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009603">3</a></font></span>Whoso, devoted to the God, effuses Soma for him with yearning<br>    heart and spirit,<br>   Never doth Indra give away his cattle: for him he makes the<br>    lovely Soma famous.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009604">4</a></font></span>He looks with loving favour on the mortal who, like a rich man,<br>    pours for him the Soma.<br>   Maghavan in his bended arm supports him: he slays, unasked,<br>    the men who hate devotion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009605">5</a></font></span>We call on thee to come to us, desirous of booty, and of cattle,<br>    and of horses. <a name="page_344"><font size="1" color="green">p. 344</font></a><br>   For thy new love and favour are we present: let us invoke thee,<br>   Indra, as our welfare.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009606">6</a></font></span>For life I set thee free by this oblation from the unknown<br>    decline and from consumption;<br>   Or, if the grasping demon have possessed him, free him from<br>    her, O Indra, thou and Agni.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009607">7</a></font></span>Be his days ended, be he how departed, be he brought very near<br>    to death already,<br>   Out of Destruction's lap again I bring him, save him for life to<br>    last a hundred autumns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009608">8</a></font></span>With thousand-eyed oblation, hundred-autumned, bringing a<br>    hundred lives, have 1 restored him.<br>   That Indra for a hundred years may lead him safe to the farther<br>    shore of all misfortune.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009609">9</a></font></span>Live waxing in thy strength a hundred autumns, live through a<br>    hundred springs, a hundred winters.<br>   Through hundred-lived oblation Indra, Agni, Brihaspati, Savitar<br>    yield him for a hundred!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009610">10</a></font></span>So have I found and rescued thee: thou hast returned with<br>    youth renewed.<br>   Whole in thy members! I have found whole sight and all thy<br>    life for thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009611">11</a></font></span>May Agni yielding to our prayer, the Rakshas-killer, drive away.<br>   The malady of evil name that hath beset thy labouring womb.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009612">12</a></font></span>Agni, concurring in the prayer, drive off the eater of thy flesh,<br>   The malady of evil name that hath attacked thy babe and<br>    womb.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009613">13</a></font></span>That which destroys the sinking germ, the settled, moving<br>    embryo,<br>   That which would kill the babe at birth, even this will we drive<br>    far away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009614">14</a></font></span>That which divides thy legs that it may lie between the married<br>    pair,<br>   That penetrates and licks thy side, even this will we exterminate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009615">15</a></font></span>What rests by thee in borrowed form of brother, lover, or of<br>    lord,<br>   And would destroyed the progeny,—even this will we exter-<br>    minate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009616">16</a></font></span>That which through sleep or darkness hath deceived thee and<br>    lies down by thee, <a name="page_345"><font size="1" color="green">p. 345</font></a><br>   And will destroy thy progeny,—even this will we exterminate.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009617">17</a></font></span>From both thy nostrils, from thine eyes, from both thine ears<br>    and from thy chin,<br>   Forth from thy head and brain and tongue I drive thy malady<br>    away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009618">18</a></font></span>From the neck-tendons and the neck, from the breast-bones<br>    and from the spine,<br>   From shoulders, upper, lower arms, I drive thy malady away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009619">19</a></font></span>From viscera and all within, forth from the rectum, from the<br>    heart,<br>   From kidneys, liver and from spleen, I drive thy malady away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009620">20</a></font></span>From thighs, from knee-caps, and from heels, and from the<br>    forepart of the feet,<br>   From hips, from stomach, and from groin, I drive thy malady<br>    away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009621">21</a></font></span>From what is voided from within, and from thy hair, and from<br>    thy nails,<br>   From all thyself, from top to toe, I drive thy malady away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009622">22</a></font></span>From every member, every hair, disease that comes in every<br>    joint,<br>   From all thyself, from top to toe, I drive thy malady away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009623">23</a></font></span>Avaunt, thou Master of the Mind, I Depart and vanish far away.<br>   Look on Destruction far from us. The live man's mind is<br>    manifold.</p>
