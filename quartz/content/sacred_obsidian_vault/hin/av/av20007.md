 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000701">1</a></font></span>Sūrya, thou mountest up to meet the Hero famous for his<br>    wealth,<br>   Who hurls the bolt and works for man:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000702">2</a></font></span>Him who with might of both his arms brake nine-and ninety<br>    castles down,<br>   Slew Vritra and smote Ahi dead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000703">3</a></font></span>This Indra is our gracious Friend. He sends us in a full broad<br>    stream<br>   Riches in horses, kine, and corn.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000704">4</a></font></span>Indra, whom many laud, accept the strength-conferring Soma<br>    juice.<br>   Quaff, pour down drink that satisfies.</p>
