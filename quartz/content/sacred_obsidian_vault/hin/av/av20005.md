 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN V</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000501">1</a></font></span>Like women, let this Soma juice invested with its raiment, glide..<br>   Most active Indra, close to thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000502">2</a></font></span>Mighty in bulk, strong-necked, stout-armed in the wild rapture.<br>    of the juice <a name="page_267"><font size="1" color="green">p. 267</font></a><br>   Doth Indra smite the foemen dead.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000503">3</a></font></span>Indra, advance, go forward thou who by thy might art Lord of<br>    all.<br>   Slay, Vritra-slayer slay thy foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000504">4</a></font></span>Long be thy grasping-hook wherewith thou givest treasure unto-<br>    him<br>   Who pours the juice and worships thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000505">5</a></font></span>Here, Indra, is thy Soma draught, made pure upon the sacred.<br>    grass.<br>   Run hither, come and drink thereof.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000506">6</a></font></span>Famed for thy radiance, worshipped well! this juice is shed for<br>    thy delight:<br>   Thou art invoked, Ākhandala!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2000507">7</a></font></span>To Kundapāyya, grandson's son, grandson of Sringavrish, to<br>    thee,<br>   To him have I addressed my thought.</p>
