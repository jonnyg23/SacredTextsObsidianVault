 <p><a name="page_334"><font size="1" color="green">p. 334</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXIX</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008901">1</a></font></span>Even as an archer shoots afar his arrow, offer the laud to him<br>    with meet adornment.<br>   Quell with your voice the wicked's voice, O sages, Singer, make<br>   Indra rest beside the Soma.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008902">2</a></font></span>Draw thy Friend to thee like a cow at milking: O singer, wake<br>    up Indra as a lover.<br>   Make thou the Hero haste to give us riches even as a vessel<br>    filled brimful with treasure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008903">3</a></font></span>Why, Maghavan, do they call thee bounteous Giver? Quicken<br>    me: thou, I hear, art he who quickens.<br>   Sakra, let my intelligence be active, and bring us luck that finds<br>    great wealth, O Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008904">4</a></font></span>Standing, in battle for their rights, together, the people, Indra,<br>    in the fray invoke thee.<br>   Him who brings gifts the Hero makes his comrade: with him<br>    who pours no juice he seeks not friendship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008905">5</a></font></span>Whoso with plenteous juice for him expresses strong Somas as<br>    much quickly-coming treasure,<br>   For him he everthrows in early morning his swift well-weapon-<br>    ed foes and slays the tyrant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008906">6</a></font></span>He unto whom we offer praises, Indra, Maghavan, who hath<br>    joined to ours his wishes<br>   Before him even afar the foe must tremble: low before him must<br>    bow all human glories.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008907">7</a></font></span>With thy fierce bolt, O God invoked of many, drive to a distance<br>    from afar the foeman.<br>   O Indra, give us wealth in corn and cattle, and make the singer's<br>    prayer gain strength and riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008908">8</a></font></span>Indra the swallower of strong libations with their thick residue,<br>    the potent Somas,<br>   He, Maghavan, will not restrict his bounty: he brings much<br>    wealth unto the Soma-presser.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008909">9</a></font></span>Yea, by superior play he wins advantage when he, a gambler,<br>    piles his gains in season. <a name="page_335"><font size="1" color="green">p. 335</font></a><br>   Celestial-natured, he o'erwhelms with riches the devotee who<br>    keeps not back his money.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008910">10</a></font></span>O much-invoked, may we subdue all famine and evil want with<br>    store of grain and cattle.<br>   May we allied, as first in rank, with princes,:obtain possessions<br>    by our own exertion<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008911">11</a></font></span>Brihaspati protect us from the rearward, and from above and<br>    from below, from sinners.<br>   May Indra from the front and from the centre, as friend to<br>    friends, vouchsafe us room and freedom.</p>
