 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LIX</h3>  <h4 align="center">An expiatory hymn accompanying sacrifice</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905901">1</a></font></span>God among mortals, Agni, thou art guard of holy Law, thou art<br>   To be adored in sacred rites.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905902">2</a></font></span>When, ignorant, we violate the statutes of you, O Deities, with<br>    whom is knowledge,<br>   Wise Agni shall correct our faults and failings, and Soma who<br>    hath entered into Brāhmans.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905903">3</a></font></span>To the Gods' pathway have we come desiring to execute what<br>    work we may accomplish.<br>   Let Agni—for he knows—complete the worship. He is the<br>   Priest: let him fix rites and seasons.</p>
