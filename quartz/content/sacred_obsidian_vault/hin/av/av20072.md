 <p><a name="page_323"><font size="1" color="green">p. 323</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007201">1</a></font></span>In all libations men with hero spirit urge thee, Universal, One,<br>    each seeking several light, each fain to win the light apart.<br>   Thee, furthering like a ship, will we set to the chariot pole of<br>    strength,<br>   As men who win with sacrifices Indra's thought, men who win<br>   Indra with their lauds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007202">2</a></font></span>Couples desirous of thine aid are storming thee, pouring their<br>    presents forth to win a stall of kine, pouring gifts, Indra,<br>    seeking thee.<br>   When two men seeking spoil or heaven thou bringest face to<br>    face in war,<br>   Thou showest Indra, then the bolt thy constant friend, the bull<br>    that ever waits on thee,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007203">3</a></font></span>Also this morn may he be well inclined to us, mark at our call<br>    our offerings and our song of praise, our call that we may win<br>    the light.<br>   As thou, O Indra Thunder-armed, wilt, as the Strong One,<br>    slay the foe,<br>   Listen, thou to the prayer of me a later sage, hear thou a later<br>    sage's prayer.</p>
