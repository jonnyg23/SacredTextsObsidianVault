 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="green">p. 184</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II</h3>  <h4 align="center">A funeral hymn, taken mainly from the Rigveda</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800201">1</a></font></span>For Yama Soma juice flows clear, to Yama is oblation paid.<br>   To Yama sacrifice prepared, and heralded by Agni, goes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800202">2</a></font></span>Offer to Yama sacrifice most sweet in savour and draw near.<br>   Bow down before the Rishis of the olden time, the ancient ones<br>    who made the path.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800203">3</a></font></span>Offer to Yama, to the King, butter and milk in sacrifice.<br>   So may he grant that we may live long days of life mid living<br>    men,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800204">4</a></font></span>Burn him not up, nor quite consume him, Agni. Let not his<br>    body or his skin be scattered.<br>   O Jātavedas, when thou hast matured him, then send him on<br>    his way unto the Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800205">5</a></font></span>When thou hast made him ready, Jātavedas, then do thou give<br>    him over to the Fathers. <a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="green">p. 185</font></a><br>   When he attains unto the life that waits him he will obey the<br>   Deities' commandment.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800206">6</a></font></span>With the three jars Brihat alone makes pure the six wide-spread-<br>    ing realms.<br>   The Gāyatri, the Trishtup, all metres in Yama are contained.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800207">7</a></font></span>The Sun receive thine eye, the wind thy spirit; go, as thy merit<br>    is, to earth or heaven.<br>   Go, if it be thy lot, unto the waters: go, make thy home in<br>    plants with all thy members.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800208">8</a></font></span>Thy portion is the goat: with heat consume him: let thy fierce<br>    flame, thy glowing splendour, burn him.<br>   With thine auspicious forms, O Jātavedas, bear this man to the<br>    region of the pious.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800209">9</a></font></span>Let all thy rapid flames, O Jātavedas, wherewith thou fillest<br>    heaven and earth's mid-region,<br>   Follow the goat as he goes on, united: then with the others,<br>    most auspicious, aid us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_18002110">110</a></font></span>Away O Agni, to the Fathers, send him who, offered in thee,<br>    goes with our oblations.<br>   Wearing new life let him approach his offspring, and splendid, be<br>    invested with a body,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800211">11</a></font></span>Run and outspeed the two dogs, Sarama's offspring, brindled,<br>    four-eyed, upon thy happy pathway.<br>   Draw nigh thou to the gracious-minded Fathers who take their<br>    pleasure in the feast with Yama.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800212">12</a></font></span>And those two dogs of thine, Yama, the watchers, four-eyed<br>    who look on men and guard the pathway<br>   Entrust this man, O King, to their protection, and with prosperity<br>    and health endow him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800213">13</a></font></span>Dark-hued, insatiate, with distended nostrils, Yama's two envoys<br>    roam among the people.<br>   May they restore to us a fair existence here and to-day that we<br>    may see the sunlight.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800214">14</a></font></span>For some the Soma juice runs clear some sit by sacrificial oil.<br>   To those for whom the meath flows forth, even to those let him<br>    depart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800215">15</a></font></span>Let him, O Yama, go to those Rishis austere, of Fervour born,<br>   First followers of Law, the sons of Law, upholders of the Law.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800246">46</a></font></span>Invincible through Fervour, they who by their Fervour went to<br>    heaven. <a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="green">p. 186</font></a><br>   Who practised great austerity,—even to those let him depart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800217">17</a></font></span>The heroes who contend in war and boldly cast their lives away.<br>   Or who give guerdon thousandfold,—even to those let him<br>    depart.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800218">18</a></font></span>Let him, O Yama, go to those Rishis austere, of Fervour born,<br>   Skilled in a thousand ways and means, the sages who protect the<br>   Sun.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800219">19</a></font></span>Be pleasant unto him, O Earth, thornless and lulling him to rest.<br>   Vouchsafe him shelter broad and sure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800220">20</a></font></span>In the free amplitude of earth take roomy space to lodge thee in.<br>   Let all oblations which in life thou paidest drop thee honey now.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800221">21</a></font></span>Hither I call thy spirit with my spirit. Come thou; delighted,<br>    to these dwelling-places.<br>   Unite thee with the Fathers and with Yama: strong and<br>    delicious be the winds that fan thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800222">22</a></font></span>Floating in water, bringing streams, let Maruts carry thee aloft,<br>   And causing coolness by their rush sprinkle thee with their fall-<br>    ing rain.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800223">23</a></font></span>I have recalled thy life to life, to being, power, and energy.<br>   Let thy soul go unto its own: so to the Fathers hasten thou.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800224">24</a></font></span>Let not thy soul be left behind: here let not aught of thee<br>    remain,<br>   Of spirit, body, members, sap.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800225">25</a></font></span>Let not a tree oppness thee, nor Earth the great Goddess weigh<br>    thee down.<br>   Among the Fathers find thy home, and thrive mid those whom<br>   Yama rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800226">26</a></font></span>Each parted member, severed from thy body, thy vital breaths<br>    that in the wind have vanished,<br>   With all of these, piece after piece, shall Fathers who dwell to-<br>    gether meet and reunite thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800227">27</a></font></span>Him have the living banished from their houses: remove him to<br>    a distance from the hamlet.<br>   Yama's observant messenger was Mrityu he hath despatched<br>    men's lives unto the Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800228">28</a></font></span>Those Dasyus who, not eating our oblations, come wilh friends'<br>    faces mingled with the Fathers,<br>   Those who wear gross those who wear subtile bodies,—from this.<br>    our sacrifice let Agni blast them. <a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="green">p. 187</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800229">29</a></font></span>Bringing delight, prolonging our existence, here let our own, the<br>   Fathers, dwell together.<br>   Coming with sacrifice may we assist them, living long lives<br>    through many autumn seasons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800230">30</a></font></span>Now by this cow I bring thee, by the boiled rice set in milk for<br>    thee,<br>   Be the supporter of the folk left here without a livelihood.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800231">31</a></font></span>Prolong the pleasant Dawn enriched with horses-or bearing us.<br>    anew beyond the darkness.<br>   Adjudged to die be he, the man who slew thee: this portion let<br>    him find, and not another.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800232">32</a></font></span>Yama is higher and Vivasvān lower: nothing whatever do I see<br>    above him.<br>   This sacrifice of mine is based on Yama, Vivasvān spread the<br>    atmosphere about us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800233">33</a></font></span>From mortal men they hid the immortal Lady, made one like<br>    her and gave her to Vivasvān.<br>   Saranyū brought to him the Asvin brothers, and then deserted<br>    both twinned pairs of children.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800234">34</a></font></span>Bring thou the Fathers one and all Agni, to eat the sacrifice.<br>   The buried, and the cast away, those burnt with fire, and those<br>    exposed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800235">35</a></font></span>Those, whether flames have burnt or not consumed them, who in<br>    the midst of heaven enjoy oblations—<br>   Let them, when thou dost know them, Jatavedas, accept with<br>    sacred food the axe and worship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800236">36</a></font></span>Burn gently, Agni, burn not up the body with too fervent heat.<br>   Let all thy force and fury be expended on the woods and earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800237">37</a></font></span>I give this place to him who hath come hither and now is mine,<br>    to be a home to rest in:<br>   This was the thought of Yama when he answered: This man is.<br>    mine. Let him come here to riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800238">38</a></font></span>This date we settle once for all, that it may ne'er be fixt again_<br>   A hundred autumns; not before.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800239">39</a></font></span>This date we order, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800240">40</a></font></span>This date we limit, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800241">41</a></font></span>This date we measure, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800242">42</a></font></span>This date we mete out, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800243">43</a></font></span>This date we stablish, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800244">44</a></font></span>This date we mete and measure out, that it may ne'er be fixt. <a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a><br>    again. A hundred autumns: not before.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800245">45</a></font></span>The period I have measured—come to heaven. I would my life<br>    were long<br>   Not to be measured out again; a hundred autumns, not before.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800246">46</a></font></span>Inbreath and outbreath, breath diffused, life, sight to look upon<br>    the Sun<br>   Seek by a straight unwinding path the Fathers whom King<br>   Yama rules,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800247">47</a></font></span>Unmarried men who toiled and have departed, the childless,<br>    having left their foes behind them,<br>   Have found on high the world whereto they mounted, reflecting<br>    on the ridge of vaulted heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800248">48</a></font></span>The lowest is the Watery heaven, Pilumatī the middlemost;<br>   The third and highest, that wherein the Fathers dwell, is called<br>   Pradyaus.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800249">49</a></font></span>The Fathers of our Father, his Grandfathers, those who have<br>    entered into air's wide region,<br>   Those who inhabit earth or dwell in heaven, these Fathers will<br>    we worship with oblation.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800250">50</a></font></span>Thou seest now, and ne'er again shalt look upon, the Sun in<br>    heaven.<br>   Cover him as a mother draws her skirt about her son, O Earth!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800251">51</a></font></span>This once,,and at no other time hereafter in a lengthened life:<br>   Cover him, as a wife, O Earth, covers her husband with her<br>    robe!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800252">52</a></font></span>Round thee auspiciously I wrap the vesture of our Mother<br>   Earth:<br>   Be bliss among the living mine, oblation mid the Fathers thine!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800253">53</a></font></span>Ye have prepared, pathmakers, Agni-Soma, a fair world for the<br>   Gods to be the it treasure.<br>   Go to that world and send us Pūshan hither to bear us on the<br>    paths the goat hath trodden.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800254">54</a></font></span>Guard of the world, whose cattle ne'er are injured, may Pūshan<br>    bear thee hence, for he hath knowledge.<br>   May he consign thee to these Fathers' keeping, and to the<br>    gracious Gods let Agni give hee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800255">55</a></font></span>Lord of all life, let Ayu guard thee, Pūshan convey thee forward<br>    on the distant pathway.<br>   May Savitar the God conduct thee thither where dwell the pious<br>    who have gone before thee. <a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800256">56</a></font></span>For thee I yoke these carriers twain to bear thee to the spirit.<br>    world.<br>   Hasten with them to Yama's home and join his gathered.<br>    companies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800257">57</a></font></span>This is the robe that first was wrapped about thee: cast off the<br>    robe thou worest here among us.<br>   Go, knowing, to the meed of virtuous action, thy many gifts.<br>    bestowed upon the friendless.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800258">58</a></font></span>Mail thee with flesh against the flames of Agni; encompass thee<br>    about with fat and marrow;<br>   So will the bold One eager to attack thee with fierce glow fail to<br>    girdle and consume thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800259">59</a></font></span>From his dead hand I take the staff he carried, together with his<br>    lore and strength and splendour.<br>   There art thou, there; and here with good men round us may<br>    we o'ercome all enemies and foemen.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1800260">60</a></font></span>From his dead hand I take the bow he carried, together with his.<br>    power and strength and splendour.<br>   Having collected wealth and ample treasure, come hither to the-<br>    world of living beings.</p>
