 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIX</h3>  
 <p>  I. For thou, O Indra, art the God whom hymns and praises<br>    magnify:<br>   Thou blessest those who worship thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002902">2</a></font></span>Bay horses with their long manes bring Indra to drink the<br>   Soma juice,<br>   The Bountiful to our sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002903">3</a></font></span>With waters' foam thou torest off, Indra, the head of Namuchi,<br>   Subduing all contending hosts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002904">4</a></font></span>The Dasyus, when they fain would climb by magic arts and<br>    mount to heaven,<br>   Thou, Indra, castest down to earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002905">5</a></font></span>As Soma-drinker conquering all, thou scatteredst to every side<br>   Their band who poured no gifts to thee.</p>
