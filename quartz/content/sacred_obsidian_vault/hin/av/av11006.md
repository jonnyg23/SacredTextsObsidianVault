 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="green">p. 58</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A prayer to all Divinities and Sanctities for deliverance from distress</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100601">1</a></font></span>We call on Agni, on the trees lords of the forest, herbs and.<br>    plants,<br>   Indra, Sūrya, Brihaspati: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100602">2</a></font></span>We call on Vishnu, Bhaga, on Mitra and Varuna the King,<br>   Ansa Vivasvān we address: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100603">3</a></font></span>We call on Savitar the God, on Pūshan the establisher, <a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="green">p. 59</font></a><br>   Tvashtar the foremost we address: may they deliver us from<br>    woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100604">4</a></font></span>Gandharvas and Apsarases; the Asvins, Brāhmanaspati,<br>   Aryaman, God, by name we call: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100605">5</a></font></span>This word of ours to Day and Night, and to the Sun and Moon<br>    we speak,<br>   All the Ādityas we address: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100606">6</a></font></span>Vāta, Parjanya we address, the Quarters, and the Firmament,<br>   And all the Regions of the sky: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100607">7</a></font></span>From all that brings a curse may Day and Night and Dawn<br>    deliver me,<br>   May Soma free me, God to whom they give the name of Chan-<br>    dramās.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100608">8</a></font></span>All creatures both of heaven and earth, wild beasts and sylvan<br>    animals,<br>   And winged birds of air we call: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100609">9</a></font></span>Bhava and Sarva we address, and Rudra who is Lord of Beasts,<br>   Their arrows which we feel and know: may they be ever kind to<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100610">10</a></font></span>We speak to Constellations, Heaven, to Earth, to Genii, and to<br>   Hills,<br>   To Seas, to Rivers, and to Lakes: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100611">11</a></font></span>Or the Seven Rishis we address, Waters divine, Prajāpati,<br>   Fathers with Yama at their head: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100612">12</a></font></span>Gods whose abode is in the heaven and those who dwell in<br>    middle air,<br>   And Mighty ones who rest on earth: may they deliver us from.<br>    woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100613">13</a></font></span>Ādityas, Rudra, Vasus, Gods aloft in heaven, Atharvan's sons,<br>   The sages, sons of Angiras: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100614">14</a></font></span>To sacrifice, to worshipper, hymns, songs, and healing charms,<br>    we speak,<br>   To priestly acts and Yajus texts: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100615">15</a></font></span>To the five kingdoms of the plants which Soma rules as Lord we<br>    speak.<br>   Darbha, hemp, barley, mighty power: may these deliver us from<br>    woe,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100616">16</a></font></span>To demons and fierce fiends we speak, to Holy Genii, Fathers,.<br>   Snakes, <a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="green">p. 60</font></a><br>   And to the hundred deaths and one: may these deliver us from<br>    woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100617">17</a></font></span>We speak to Seasons, Season-Lords, to years and sections of the<br>    year,<br>   To Months, half-months, and years complete: may they deliver<br>    us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100618">18</a></font></span>Come hither from the south, ye Gods, rise and come forward<br>    from the west.<br>   Gathered together, all ye Gods, ye mighty Ones, from east and<br>    and north: may they deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100619">19</a></font></span>This we address to all the Gods, faithful, maintainers of the<br>   Right,<br>   With all their Consorts by their side: may they deliver us from<br>    woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100620">20</a></font></span>We speak to the collected Gods, faithful, maintainers of the<br>   Right.<br>   Present with their collective Dames: may these deliver us from<br>    woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100621">21</a></font></span>The spirit, yea, the spirits' Lord, ruler of spirits, we address.<br>   Together let all spirits meet: may these deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100622">22</a></font></span>The five Sky regions, Goddesses, and the twelve Seasons which<br>    are Gods.<br>   The teeth of the completed year, may these deliver us from woe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100623">23</a></font></span>The deathless balm that Mātalī knows, purchased at a chariot's<br>    price,<br>   Indra effused into the floods. Waters, give us that healing<br>    balm!</p>
