 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007301">1</a></font></span>All these libations are for thee, O Hero: to thee I offer these<br>    my prayers that strengthen.<br>   Ever, in every place, must men invoke thee. <a name="page_324"><font size="1" color="green">p. 324</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007302">2</a></font></span>Never do men attain, O Wonder-worker, thy greatness, Mighty<br>   One who must be lauded,<br>   Nor, Indra, thine heroic power and bounty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007303">3</a></font></span>Bring to the Wise, the Great who waxeth mighty your offerings<br>    and make ready your devotion:<br>   To many clans he goeth, man's Controller.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007304">4</a></font></span>When, with the Princes, Maghavan, famed of old, comes nigh<br>    the thunderbolt of gold and the Controller's car<br>   Which his two tawny coursers draw, then Indra is the Sovran<br>   Lord of power whose fame spreads far and wide.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007305">5</a></font></span>With him too is this rain of his that comes like herds: Indra<br>    throws drops of moisture on his yellow beard.<br>   When the sweet juice is shed he seeks the pleasant place, and<br>    stirs the worshipper as the wind disturbs the wood.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007306">6</a></font></span>We laud and praise his several deeds of valour who, fatherlike,.<br>    with power hath made us stronger;<br>   Who with his voice slew many thousand wicked ones who spake<br>    in varied manner with contemptuous cries.</p>
