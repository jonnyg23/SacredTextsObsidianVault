 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="green">p. 107</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">BOOK XIII</h1>  <h3 align="center">HYMN I</h3>  <h4 align="center">The glorification of Rohita, a form of Fire and of the Sun</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300101">1</a></font></span>Rise, Mighty One, who liest in the waters, and enter this thy<br>    fair and glorious kingdom.<br>   Let Rohita who made this All uphold thee carefully nurtured<br>    for supreme dominion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300102">2</a></font></span>The strength that was in waters hath ascended. Mount o'er the<br>    tribes which thou hast generated.<br>   Creating Soma, waters, plants and cattle, bring hitherward both<br>    quadrupeds and bipeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300103">3</a></font></span>Ye Maruts, strong and mighty, sons of Prisni, with Indra for<br>    ally crush down our foemen.<br>   Let Rohita, ye bounteous givers, hear you, thrice-seven Maruts<br>    who delight in sweetness!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300104">4</a></font></span>Up to the lap of births, to lofty places, hath Rohita, the germ<br>    of Dames, ascended.<br>   Conjoined with these he found the six realms: seeing his way<br>    in front here he received the kingship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300105">5</a></font></span>For thee hath Rohita obtained dominion, scattered thine ene-<br>    mies, become thy safeguard.<br>   So by the potent Sakvaris let Heaven and Earth be milked to-<br>     yield thee all thy wishes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300106">6</a></font></span>Rohita gave the Earth and Heavens their being. There Para-<br>    meshthin held the cord extended.<br>   Thereon reposeth Aja Ekapāda. He with his might hath stab-<br>    lished Earth and Heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300107">7</a></font></span>Rohita firmly stablished Earth and Heaven: by him was ether<br>    fixt by him the welkin.<br>   He measured out mid air and all the regions: by him the Gods<br>    found life that lasts for ever.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300108">8</a></font></span>Arranging shoots, springs, Rohita considered this Universe in<br>    all its forms and phases.<br>   May he, gone up to heaven with mighty glory, anoint thy sov-<br>    ranty with milk and fatness. <a name="page_108"><font size="1" color="green">p. 108</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300109">9</a></font></span>Thy risings up, thy mountings and ascensions wherewith thou<br>    fillest heaven and air's mid-region—<br>   By prayer for these, by milk of these, increasing, in Rohita's<br>    kingdom watch, among his people.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300110">10</a></font></span>The tribes thy heat produced have followed hither the Calf and<br>   Gāyatri, the strain that lauds him.<br>   With friendly heart let them approach to serve thee, and the<br>   Calf Rohita come with his mother.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300111">11</a></font></span>Erected, Rohita hath reached the welkin, wise, young, creating<br>    every form and figure.<br>   Agni, refulgent with his heightened lustre, in the third realm<br>    hath brought us joy and gladness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300112">12</a></font></span>Thousand-horned Bull, may Jātavedas, worshipped with butter,<br>    balmed with Soma, rich in heroes,<br>   Besought, ne'er quit me; may I ne'er forsake thee. Give me<br>    abundant men and herds of cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300113">13</a></font></span>Rohita is the sire and mouth of worship: to him with voice, ear,<br>    heart I pay oblation.<br>   To Rohita come Gods with joyful spirit. May he by risings raise<br>    me till I join him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300114">14</a></font></span>Rohita ordered sacrifice for Visvakarman: thence have I obta-<br>    ined this strength and energy.<br>   May I proclaim thee as my kin over the greatness of the world.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300115">15</a></font></span>On thee have mounted Brihatī and Pankti. and Kakup with great<br>    splendour, Jātavedas!<br>   The cry of Vashat with the voice uplifted and Rohita with seed<br>    on thee have mounted.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300116">16</a></font></span>He goes into the womb of earth, he robes himself in heaven and<br>    air.<br>   He on the Bright One's station hath reached heavenly light and<br>    all the worlds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300117">17</a></font></span>To us, Vāchaspati, may Earth be pleasant, pleasant our dwelling,<br>    pleasant be our couches.<br>   Even here may Prāna be our friend: may Agni, O Parameshthin<br>    give thee life and splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300118">18</a></font></span>And those, Vāchaspati, our own five seasons, sacred to Visva-<br>    karman their creator.<br>   Even here our friend be Prāna: Parameshthin, may Rohita<br>    vouchsafe the life and splendour. <a name="page_109"><font size="1" color="green">p. 109</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300119">19</a></font></span>Breed, O Vāchaspati, joy and understanding, kine i n our stall<br>    and children in our consorts.<br>   Even here may Prāna be our friend: may Agni, O Parameshthin,<br>    give thee life and splendour.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300120">20</a></font></span>With splendour let God Savitar, and Agni, with splendour<br>   Mitra, Varuna invest thee.<br>   Treading down all Malignities, come hither. Pleasant and'<br>    glorious hast thou made this kingdom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300121">21</a></font></span>Rohita, car-borne by a speckled leader, thou, pouring water,.<br>    goest on in triumph.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300122">22</a></font></span>Golden, refulgent, lofty is the Lady, Rohinī, Rohita's devoted<br>   Consort.<br>   Through her may we win various spoil and booty, through her<br>    be conquerors in every battle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300123">23</a></font></span>Rohita's seat is Rohinī before us: that is the path the speckled<br>   Mare pursueth.<br>   Kasyapas and Gandharvas lead her upward, and heavenly sages<br>    ever watch and guard her,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300124">24</a></font></span>Sūrya's bay steeds refulgent and immortal draw the light-rolling.<br>    chariot on for ever.<br>   Drinker of fatness, Rohita, resplendent, hath entered into<br>    various-coloured heaven,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300125">25</a></font></span>Rohita, Bull whose horns are sharply pointed, superior of Agni<br>    and of Sūrya,<br>   He who supports the sundered earth and heaven,—from him the<br>   Gods effect their own creations.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300126">26</a></font></span>Rohita rose to heaven from mighty ocean, Rohita rose and<br>    clomb all steeps and rises.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300127">27</a></font></span>Prepare the Milky One who teems with fatness: she is the Gods'<br>    never-reluctant milch-cow.<br>   Indra drink Soma: ours be peace and safety. Let Agni lead the<br>    laud, and chase our foemen.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300128">28</a></font></span>Both kindling and inflamed, adored with butter and enhanced<br>    thereby.<br>   May conquering Agni, conqueror of all, destroy mine enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300129">29</a></font></span>Let him smite down in death and burn the foeman who<br>    attacketh me.<br>   Our adversaries we consume through Agni the Carnivorous.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300130">30</a></font></span>Beat them down, Indra, with thy bolt, beat them down, mighty<br>    with thine arm. <a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="green">p. 110</font></a><br>   I through the energy and force of Agni have secured my foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300131">31</a></font></span>Cast down our foes beneath our feet, O Agni. Brihaspati,<br>    oppress our rebel kinsman.<br>   Low let them fall, O Indra-Agni. Mitra-Varuna, powerless to<br>    show their anger.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300132">32</a></font></span>Ascending up on high, O God. O Sūrya, drive my foes away.<br>   Yea, beat them backward with the stone: to deepest darkness<br>    let them go.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300133">33</a></font></span>Calf of Virāj, the Bull of prayers and worship, whitebacked, he<br>    hath gone up to air's mid-region.<br>   Singing, they hymn the Calf, with gifts of butter: him who is<br>   Brahma they exalt with Brahma.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300134">34</a></font></span>Rise up to earth, rise up to heaven above it; rise up to opulence,<br>    rise up to kingship.<br>   Rise up to offspring, rise to life immortal; rise, and with Rohita<br>    unite thy body.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300135">35</a></font></span>With all the Gods who circle round the Sun, upholding royal<br>    sway,<br>   With all of these may Rohita accordant, give sovranty to thee<br>    with friendly spirit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300136">36</a></font></span>Cleansed by prayer, sacrifices bear thee upward: bay coursers,<br>    ever travelling, convey thee. Thy light shines over sea and<br>    billowy ocean.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300137">37</a></font></span>Rohita, conqueror of cows and riches and gathered spoil, is<br>    heaven's and earth's upholder.<br>   Over earth's greatness would I tell my kinship with thee who<br>    hast a thousand births and seven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300138">38</a></font></span>A glorious sight to beasts and men, thou goest glorious to the<br>    regions and mid-regions.<br>   On earth's, on Aditi's bosom, bright with glory. Fain would I<br>    equal Savitar in beauty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300139">39</a></font></span>Thou, yonder, knowest all things here, when here thou knowest<br>    what is there.<br>   From here men see the sphere of light, Sūrya profoundly wise in<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300140">40</a></font></span>A God, thou injurest the Gods: thou movest in the ocean's<br>    depth.<br>   Men kindle common Agni: him only the higher sages know.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300141">41</a></font></span>Beneath the upper realm, above this lower, bearing her Calf at<br>    foot, the Cow hath risen <a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="green">p. 111</font></a><br>   Whitherward, to what place hath she departed? Where doth she<br>    calve? Not in this herd of cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300142">42</a></font></span>She hath become one-footed or two-footed, four-footed, or<br>    eight-footed or nine-footed,<br>   This universe's thousand-syllabled Pankti Oceans flow forth<br>    from her in all directions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300143">43</a></font></span>Rising to heaven, immortal, hear my calling. Cleansed by<br>    prayer, sacrifices bear thee upward. Bay coursers, ever on the<br>    road, convey thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300144">44</a></font></span>This, O Immortal One, I know of thee, thy progress to the sky<br>    thy dwelling-place in loftiest heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300145">45</a></font></span>Beyond the sky, beyond the Earth looks Sūrya, and beyond the<br>    floods.<br>   The single eye of all that is; to mighty heaven hath he arisen.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300146">46</a></font></span>The earth was made his altar, and the wide expanses were the<br>    fence.<br>   There Rohita established both these Agnis, fervent heat and<br>    cold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300147">47</a></font></span>He stablished heat and cold, he made the mountains sacrificial<br>    posts.<br>   Then both the Agnis, Rohita's who found celestial light, with<br>    rain for molten butter, sacrificed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300148">48</a></font></span>Rohita's Agni-his who found heaven's light-is kindled with the<br>    prayer.<br>   From him the heat, from him the cold, from him the sacrifice<br>    was born.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300149">49</a></font></span>Both Agins-Rohita's who found the light of heaven—made<br>    strong by prayer,<br>   Waxing by prayer, adored with prayer, by prayer enkindled,<br>    sacrificed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300150">50</a></font></span>One is deposited in Truth, one kindled in the waters: both<br>   Agnis of Rohita who found the light are set aflame with prayer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300151">51</a></font></span>That decked by Wind, and that prepared by Indra Brāhman-<br>    aspati,<br>   Agnis of Rohita who found light, prayer-enkindled, sacrificed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300152">52</a></font></span>Rohita made the earth to be his altar, heaven his Dakshinā.<br>   Then heat he took for Agni, and with rain for molten butter he<br>    created every living thing.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300153">53</a></font></span>The earth became an altar, heat was Agni, and the butter rain. <a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="green">p. 112</font></a><br>   There Agni made, by song and hymn, these mountains rise and<br>    stand erect.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300154">54</a></font></span>Then, having made the hills stand up, Rohita spake to Earth,<br>    and said:<br>   In thee let every thing be born, what is and what is yet to be.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300155">55</a></font></span>This sacrifice, the first of all, the past, the present, had its birth.<br>   From that arose this universe, yea, all this world of brightness,<br>    brought by Rohita the heavenly Sage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300156">56</a></font></span>If thou should kick a cow, or by indecent act offend the Sun,<br>   Thy root I sever; nevermore mayst thou cast shadow on the<br>    ground.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300157">57</a></font></span>Thou who, between the fire and me, passest across the line of<br>    shade.<br>   Thy root I sever: nevermore mayst thou cast shadow on the<br>    ground.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300158">58</a></font></span>Whoe'er he be who, Sūrya, God! comes between thee and me<br>    to-day,<br>   On him we wipe away ill-dream, and troubles, and impurity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300159">59</a></font></span>Let us not, Indra, leave the path, the Soma-presser's sacrifice.<br>   Let not malignities dwell with us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300160">60</a></font></span>May we obtain, completely wrought, the thread spun out tc<br>    reach the Gods,<br>   That perfecteth our sacrifice.</p>
