 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XC</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009001">1</a></font></span>Served with oblations, first-born, mountain-render, Angiras' Son,<br>   Brihaspati the holy.<br>   With twice-firm path, dwelling in light, our Father, roars loudly,<br>    as a bull, to earth and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009002">2</a></font></span>Brihaspati who made for such a people wide room and verge<br>    when Gods were invocated—<br>   Slaying his foe he breaketh down their cattles, quelling his<br>    enemies and those who hate him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009003">3</a></font></span>Brihaspati in war hath won rich treasures, hath won, this God,<br>    the great stalls filled with cattle.<br>   Striving to win waters and light, resistless, Brihaspati with light-<br>    ning smites the foeman.</p>
