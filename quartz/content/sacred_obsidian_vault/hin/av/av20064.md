 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006401">1</a></font></span>Come unto us, O Indra, dear, still conquering, unconcealable,<br>   Vast as a mountain spread on all sides, Lord of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006402">2</a></font></span>O truthful Soma-drinker, thou art mightier than both the<br>    worlds.<br>   Thou strengthenest him who pours libation, Lord of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006403">3</a></font></span>For thou art, he, O Indra, who stormest all castles of the foe,<br>   Slayer of Dasyus, man's supporter, Lord of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006404">4</a></font></span>O ministering priest, pour out of the sweet juice what gladdens<br>    most.<br>   So is the Hero praised who ever prospers us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006405">5</a></font></span>Indra whom tawny coursers bear, praise such a thine,.<br>    preeminent<br>   None by his power or by his goodness hath attained<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006406">6</a></font></span>We seeking glory, have invoked this Master of all power and'<br>    might.<br>   Who must be glorified by constant sacrifice.</p>
