 <p><a name="page_258"><font size="1" color="green">p. 258</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm against evil dreams</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905701">1</a></font></span>As men discharge the utmost debt, collect the eighth and<br>    sixteenth part,<br>   So to the foeman we transfer together all the evil dream.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905702">2</a></font></span>Princes came together, debts came together, Kushthas came to-<br>    gether,<br>   Sixteenths came together. The whole evil dream that hath visited<br>    us we send away as a bad dream to the man who hates us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905703">3</a></font></span>Child of Gods' Consorts, minister of Yama is the good Dream:<br>    that which is my trouble we drive away to the enemy.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905704">4</a></font></span>Thou whose name is Rough art the mouth of the Black Bird.<br>   As such we know thee, Dream, as such we know thee well. Like<br>    a horse art thou, O Dream. As they bind girth and surcingle<br>    on a horse, so bind the alien mischief-maker, the scorner of<br>    the Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905705">5</a></font></span>The evil dream that threatens us, threatens our cattle or our<br>    home.<br>   That let the scorner of the Gods, the alien mischief-maker bind<br>    as a gold jewel round his neck.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905706">6</a></font></span>Having measured off nine cubits' distance from us we give away<br>    the whole of the evil dream to the man who hates us.</p>
