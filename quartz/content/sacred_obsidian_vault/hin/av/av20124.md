 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012401">1</a></font></span>With what help will he come to us, wonderful, ever-waxing<br>   Friend,<br>   With what most mighty company?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012402">2</a></font></span>What genuine and most liberal draught will spirit thee with<br>    juice to burst.<br>   Open e'en strongly-guarded wealth?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012403">3</a></font></span>Do thou who art protector us thy friends who praise thee<br>   With hundred aids approach us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012404">4</a></font></span>We will, with Indra and all Gods to help us, bring these existing<br>    worlds into subjection.<br>   Our sacrifice, our bodies, and our offspring shall Indra form<br>    together with the Ādityas.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012405">5</a></font></span>With the Ādityas, with the band of Maruts, may Indra be pro-<br>    tector of our bodies.<br>   As when the Gods came after they had slaughtered the Asuras,<br>    keeping safe their Godlike nature,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012406">6</a></font></span>Brought the Sun hitherward with mighty powers, and looked<br>    about them on their vigorous Godhead. <a name="page_359"><font size="1" color="green">p. 359</font></a><br>   With this may we obtain strength God-appointed, and joy with<br>    brave sons through a hundred winters.</p>
