 <p><a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="green">p. 139</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II</h3>  <h4 align="center">On the Bridal of Sūryā, marriage ceremonies in general, continued</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400201">1</a></font></span>For thee with bridal train they first escorted Sūryā to her home,<br>   Give to the husband in return, Agni, the wife with future sons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400202">2</a></font></span>Agni hath given the bride again with splendour and a lengthened.<br>    life.<br>   Long-lived be he who is her lord: a hundred autumns let him<br>    live.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400203">3</a></font></span>She was the wife of Soma first: next the Gandharva was thy<br>    lord.<br>   Agni was the third husband: now one born of woman is thy<br>    fourth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400204">4</a></font></span>Soma to the Gandharva, and to Agni the Gandharva gave.<br>   Now, Agni hath bestowed on me riches and sons and this my<br>    bride.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400205">5</a></font></span>Your favouring grace hath come, ye who are rich in spoil!<br>   Asvins, your longings are stored up within your hearts.<br>   Ye, Lords of Splendour have become our twofold guard: may<br>    we as dear friends reach the dwelling of the friend.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400206">6</a></font></span>Thou, Dame, rejoicing, take with kindly spirit wealth worthy to<br>    be famed, with all thy heroes.<br>   Give, Lords of Light a fair ford, good to drink at: remove the<br>    spiteful stump that blocks the pathway.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400207">7</a></font></span>May all the Rivers, all the Plants, may all the Forests, all the<br>   Fields,<br>   O Bride, protect thee from the fiend, guard his sons' mother for<br>    her lord. <a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="green">p. 140</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400208">8</a></font></span>Our feet are on this pleasant path, easy to travel, bringing bliss,<br>   Whereon no hero suffers harm, which wins the wealth of other<br>    men.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400209">9</a></font></span>Here these my words, ye men, the benediction through which<br>    the wedded pair have found high fortune.<br>   May the divine Apsarases, Gandharvas, all they who are these<br>    fruitful trees' protectors,<br>   Regard this bride with their auspicious, favour, nor harm the<br>    nuptial pomp as it advances.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400210">10</a></font></span>Consumptions, which, through various folk, attack the bride's<br>    resplendent train,<br>   These let the holy Gods again bear to the place from which they<br>    sprang.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400211">11</a></font></span>Let not the highway thieves who lie in ambush find the wedded<br>    pair.<br>   Let wicked men's malignities go elsewhere by an easy path.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400212">12</a></font></span>I look upon the house and bride's procession with prayer and<br>    with the gentle eye of friendship.<br>   All that is covered there in perfect beauty may Savitar make<br>    pleasant to the husband.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400213">13</a></font></span>She hath come home this dame come home to bless us: this her<br>    appointed world hath Dhātar shown her.<br>   So may Prajāpati, and both the Asvins, Aryaman, Bhaga gladden<br>    her with offspring.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400214">14</a></font></span>This dame hath come, an animated corn-field: there sow, thou<br>    man, the seed of future harvest.<br>   She from her teeming side shall bear thee children, and feed<br>    them from the fountain of her bosom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400215">15</a></font></span>Take thou thy stand, a Queen art thou, like Vishnu here,<br>   Sarasvati!<br>   O Sinivali, let her bear children, and live in Bhaga's grace.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400216">16</a></font></span>So let your wave bear up the pins, and ye, O Waters, spare the<br>    thongs;<br>   And never may the holy pair, sinless and innocent, suffer harm.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400217">17</a></font></span>Not evil-eyed no slayer of thy husband, be strong, mild, kind,<br>    and gentle to thy household.<br>   Mother of heroes, love thy husband's father: be happy, and<br>    through thee may we too prosper.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400218">18</a></font></span>No slayer of thy husband or his father, gentle and bright, bring<br>    blessing on the cattle. <a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="green">p. 141</font></a><br>   Loving thy husband's father, bring forth heroes. Tend well this<br>    household fire: be soft and pleasant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400219">19</a></font></span>Up and begone! What wish hath brought thee hither from thine<br>    own house? Thy mightier, I conjure thee.<br>   Vain is the hope, O Nirriti, that brought thee. Fly off, Malignity;<br>    stay here no longer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400220">20</a></font></span>As first of all this woman hath adored the sacred household<br>    fire.<br>   So do thou, Dame, pay homage to the Fathers and Sarasvati.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400221">21</a></font></span>Take thou this wrapper as a screen, to be a covering for the<br>    bride<br>   O Sinivali, let her bear children, and live in Bhaga's grace.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400222">22</a></font></span>Let her who shall be blest with sons, the maid who finds a.<br>    husband, step<br>   Upon the rough grass that ye spread and on the skin ye lay<br>    beneath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400223">23</a></font></span>Over the ruddy-coloured skin strew thou the grass, the Balbuja.<br>   Let her, the mother of good sons, sit there and serve this Agni<br>    here.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400224">24</a></font></span>Step on the skin and wait upon this Agni: he is the God who<br>    drives away all demons.<br>   Here bear thou children to this man thy husband: let this thy<br>    boy be happy in his birthnight.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400225">25</a></font></span>Let many babes of varied form and nature spring in succession<br>    from this fruitful mother.<br>   Wait on this fire, thou bringer of good fortune. Here with thy<br>    husband serve the Gods with worship.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400226">26</a></font></span>Bliss-bringer, furthering thy household's welfare, dear gladdening<br>    thy husband and his father, enter this home, mild to thy hus-<br>    band's mother.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400227">27</a></font></span>Be pleasant to the husband's sire, sweet to thy household and thy<br>    lord,<br>   To all this clan be gentle, and favour these men's prosperity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400228">28</a></font></span>Signs of good fortune mark the bride. Come all of you and look<br>    at her.<br>   Wish her prosperity: take on you her evil lucks and go your<br>    way.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400229">29</a></font></span>Ye youthful maidens, ill-disposed, and all ye ancient woman<br>    here, <a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="green">p. 142</font></a><br>   Give all your brilliance to the bride, then to your several homes<br>    depart!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400230">30</a></font></span>Sūryā the child of Savitar mounted for high felicity Her litter<br>    with its cloth of gold, wearing all forms of loveliness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400231">31</a></font></span>Rise, mount the bridal bed with cheerful spirit. Here bring forth<br>    children to this man thy husband.<br>   Watchful and understanding like Indrāni wake thou before the<br>    earliest light of Morning.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400232">32</a></font></span>The Gods at first lay down beside their consorts; body with<br>    body met in close embracement.<br>   O Dame, like Sūryā perfect in her grandeur, here rich in future<br>    children, meet thy husband.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400233">33</a></font></span>Rise and go hence, Visvāvasu: with reverence we worship thee.<br>   Steal to her sister dwelling with her father: this is the share—<br>    mark this—of old assigned thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400234">34</a></font></span>Apsarases rejoice and feast together between the sun and place<br>    of sacrificing.<br>   These are thy kith and kin: go thou and join them: I in due<br>    season worship thee Gandharva.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400235">35</a></font></span>Homage we pay to the Gandharva's favour, obeisance to his eye<br>    and fiery anger.<br>   Visvāvasu, with prayer we pay thee homage. Go hence to those<br>   Apsarases thy consorts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400236">36</a></font></span>May we be happy with abundant riches. We from this place<br>    have banished the Gandharva.<br>   The God is gone to the remotest region, and we have come<br>    where men prolong existence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400237">37</a></font></span>In your due season, Parents! come together. Mother and sire be<br>    ye of future children.<br>   Embrace this woman like a happy lover. Raise ye up offspring<br>    here: increase your riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400238">38</a></font></span>Send her most rich in every charm, O Pūshan, her who shall be<br>    the sharer of my pleasures;<br>   Her who shall twine her eager arms about me, and welcome all<br>    my love and soft embraces.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400239">39</a></font></span>Up, happy bridegroom! with a joyous spirit caress thy wife and<br>    throw thine arm around her.<br>   Here take your pleasure, procreate your offspring. May Savitar<br>    bestow long life upon you. <a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="green">p. 143</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400240">40</a></font></span>So may the Lord of Life vouchsafe you children, Aryaman bind<br>    you, day and night, together.<br>   Enter thy husband's house with happy omens, bring blessing to<br>    our quadrupeds and bipeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400241">41</a></font></span>Sent by the Gods associate with Manu, the vesture of the bride,<br>    the nuptial garment,<br>   He who bestows this on a thoughtful Brāhman, drives from the<br>    marriage-bed all evil demons.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400242">42</a></font></span>The priestly meed wherewith ye twain present me, the vesture of<br>    the bride, the nuptial garment,<br>   This do ye both, Brihaspati and Indra, bestow with loving-kind-<br>    ness on the Brāhman.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400243">43</a></font></span>On your soft couch awaking both together, revelling heartily with<br>    joy and laughter,<br>   Rich with brave sons, good cattle, goodly homestead, live long to<br>    look on many radiant mornings.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400244">44</a></font></span>Clad in new garments, fragrant, well-apparelled, to meet reful-<br>    gent Dawn have I arisen.<br>   I, like a bird that quits the egg, am freed from sin and purified.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400245">45</a></font></span>Splendid are Heaven and Earth, still near to bless us, mighty in<br>    their power;<br>   The seven streams have flowed: may they, Goddesses, free us<br>    from distress<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400246">46</a></font></span>To Sūryā and the Deities, to Mitra and to Varuna,<br>   Who know aright the thing that is, this adoration have I paid.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400247">47</a></font></span>He without ligature, before making incision in the neck.<br>   Closed up the wound again, most wealthy Bounteous Lord who<br>    healeth the dissevered parts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400248">48</a></font></span>Let him flash gloom away from us, the blue, the yellow and the<br>    red.<br>   I fasten to this pillar here the burning pest Prishātaki.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400249">49</a></font></span>All witcheries that hang about this garment, all royal Varuna's<br>    entangling nooses.<br>   All failure of success and all misfortunes here I deposit fastened<br>    to the pillar.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400250">50</a></font></span>My body that I hold most dear trembles in terror at this robe.<br>   Tree, make an apron at the top. Let no misfortune fall on us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400251">51</a></font></span>May all the hems and borders all the threads that form the web<br>    and woof. <a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="green">p. 144</font></a><br>   The garment woven by the bride, be soft and pleasant to our<br>    touch.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400252">52</a></font></span>These maids who from their father's house have come with long-<br>    ing to their lord have let the preparation pass. All hail!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400253">53</a></font></span>Her whom Brihaspati hath loosed the Visve Devas keep secure.<br>   With all the splendour that is stored in cows do we enrich this.<br>    girl.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400254">54</a></font></span>Her whom Brihaspati hath loosed the Visve Devas keep secure.<br>   With all the vigour that is stored in cows do we enrich this girl.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400255">55</a></font></span>Her whom Brihaspati, etc.<br>   With all good fortune, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400256">56</a></font></span>Her whom Brihaspati, etc.<br>   With all the glory, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400257">57</a></font></span>Her whom Brihaspati, etc.<br>   With all the milky store possessed by cows do we enrich this<br>    girl.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400258">58</a></font></span>Her whom Brihaspati hath freed the Visve Devas keep secure.<br>   With all the store of sap that cows contain do we enrich this.<br>    girl.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400259">59</a></font></span>If, wearing long loose hair, these men have danced together in<br>    thy house, committing sin with shout and cry,<br>   May Agni free thee from that guilt, may Savitar deliver thee,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400260">60</a></font></span>If in thy house thy daughter here have wept, with wild dishevel-<br>    led locks, committing sin with her lament.<br>   May Agni, etc.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400261">61</a></font></span>If the bride's sisters, if young maids have danced together in thy<br>    house, committing sin with shout and cry.<br>   May Agni free thee from that guilt, may Savitar deliver thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400262">62</a></font></span>If any evil have been wrought by mischief-makers that affects<br>    thy cattle progeny or house,<br>   May Agni free thee from the woe, may Savitar deliver thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400263">63</a></font></span>This woman utters wish and prayer, as down she casts the husks<br>    of corn:<br>   Long live my lord and master! yea, a hundred autumns let him<br>    live!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400264">64</a></font></span>Join thou this couple, Indra! like the Chakravaka and his.<br>    mate:<br>   May they attain to full old age with children in their happy<br>    home. <a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="green">p. 145</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400265">65</a></font></span>Whatever magic hath been wrought on cushion, chair, or<br>    canopy.<br>   Each spell to mar the wedding rites, all this we throw into the<br>    bath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400266">66</a></font></span>Whatever fault or error was in marriage or in bridal pomp.<br>   This woe we wipe away upon the cloak the interceder wears.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400267">67</a></font></span>We, having laid the stain and fault upon the interceder's cloak,<br>   Are pure and meet for sacrifice. May he prolong our lives for<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400268">68</a></font></span>Now let this artificial comb, wrought with a hundred teeth,<br>    remove<br>   Aught of impurity that dims the hair upon this woman's head.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400269">69</a></font></span>We take away consumption from each limb and member of the<br>    bride.<br>   Let not this reach Earth, nor the Gods in heaven, let it not reach<br>    the sky or air's wide region.<br>   Let not this dust that sullies reach the Waters, nor Yama, Agni,<br>    nor the host of Fathers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400270">70</a></font></span>With all the milk that is in Earth I gird thee, with all the milk<br>    that Plants contain I dress thee.<br>   I gird thee round with children and with riches. Do thou, thus<br>    girt, receive the offered treasure.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400271">71</a></font></span>I am this man, that dame art thou I am the psalm and thou the<br>    verse. I am the heaven and thou the earth.<br>   So will we dwell together here, parents of children yet to be.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400272">72</a></font></span>Unmarried men desire to wed; bountiful givers wish for sons.<br>   Together may we dwell with strength unscathed for high pros-<br>    perity.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400273">73</a></font></span>May they, the Fathers who, to view the bride, have joined this<br>    nuptial train,<br>   Grant to this lady and her lord children and peaceful happiness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400274">74</a></font></span>Her who first guided by a rein came hither, giving the bride, here<br>    offspring and possessions,<br>   Let them convey along the future's pathway. Splendid, with<br>    noble children, she hath conquered.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1400275">75</a></font></span>Wake to long life, watchful and understanding, yea, to a life shall<br>    last a hundred autumns<br>   Enter the house to be the household's mistress. A long long life<br>    let Savitar vouchsafe thee.</p>
