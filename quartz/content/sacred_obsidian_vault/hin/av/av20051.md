 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005101">1</a></font></span>For you will I sing Indra's praise who gives good gifts as well we<br>    know;<br>   Praise of the Bounteous Lord who, rich in treasure, aids his<br>    singers with wealth thousandfold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005102">2</a></font></span>As with a hundred hosts he rushes boldly on, and for the offerer<br>    slays his foes.<br>   As from a mountain flow the water-brooks, thus flow his gifts<br>    who feedeth many a one.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005103">3</a></font></span>Sakra I praise, for victory, far-famed, exceeding bountiful.<br>   Who gives, as 'twere in thousands, precious wealth to him who<br>    sheds the juice and worships him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005104">4</a></font></span>Arrows with hundred points, unconquerable, are this Indra's<br>    mighty arms in war.<br>   He streams on liberal worshippers like a hill with springs, when<br>    juices poured have gladdened him.</p>
