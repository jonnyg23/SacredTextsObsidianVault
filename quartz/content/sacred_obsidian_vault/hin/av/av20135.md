 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXXV</h3>  <h4 align="center">Verses called Frustration, Abuse, the Gods' Offering, and Dazzling Power</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013501">1</a></font></span>Bang! here he is. A dog,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013502">2</a></font></span>Swish! it is gone. Falling of leaves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013503">3</a></font></span>Crunch! it is trodden on. A cow's hoof.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013504">4</a></font></span>These Gods have gone astray. Do thou, Adhvaryu, quickly do<br>    thy work.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013505">5</a></font></span>There is good resting for the cows. Take thy delight.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013506">6</a></font></span>O singer, the Ādityas brought rich guerdon to the Angirases.<br>   Singer, they went not near to it. Singer, they did not take the<br>    gift.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013507">7</a></font></span>Singer, they went not near to that; but, singer, they accepted<br>    this:<br>   That days may not be indistinct, nor sacrifices leaderless. <a name="page_372"><font size="1" color="green">p. 372</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013508">8</a></font></span>And quickly Both he fly away, the White Horse swiftest on his<br>    feet,<br>   And swiftly fills his measure up.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013509">9</a></font></span>Ādityas, Rudras, Vasus, all pay worship unto thee. Accept this<br>    liberal gift, O Angiras,<br>   This bounty excellent and rich, this ample bounty spreading far.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013510">10</a></font></span>The Gods shall give the precious boon: let it be pleasant to<br>    your hearts.<br>   Let it be with you every day: accept our offerings in return.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013511">11</a></font></span>Vouchsafe us shelter, Indra, thou to be invoked from far away.<br>   Bring treasure hither to reward the far-famed bard who praises<br>    thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013512">12</a></font></span>Thou, Indra, to the trembling dove whose pinions had been rent<br>    and torn.<br>   Gayest ripe grain and Pilu fruit, gavest him water when athirst.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013513">13</a></font></span>The ready praiser loudly speaks though fastened triply with a<br>    strap.<br>   Yea, he commends the freshening draught, deprecates languor<br>    of disease.</p>
