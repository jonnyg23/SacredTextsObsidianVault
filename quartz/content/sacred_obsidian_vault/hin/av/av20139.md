 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXXIX</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to the Asvins</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013901">1</a></font></span>To help and favour Vatsa now, O Asvins, come ye hitherward.<br>   Bestow on him a dwelling spacious and secure, and keep malig-<br>    nites afar.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013902">2</a></font></span>All manliness that is in heaven, with the Five Tribes, or in mid-<br>    air,<br>   Bestow, ye Asvins, upon us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013903">3</a></font></span>Remember Kārnva first of all among the singers,, Asvins, who<br>   Have thought upon your wondrous deeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013904">4</a></font></span>Asvins, for you with song of praise this hot oblation is effused,<br>   This your sweet Soma juice, ye Lords of wealth and spoil,<br>    through which ye think upon the foe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013905">5</a></font></span>Whatever ye have done in floods, in the tree, Wonder-workers,<br>    and in growing plants,<br>   Therewith, O Asvins, succour me.</p>
