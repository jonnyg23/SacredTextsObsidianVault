 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXX</h3>  <h4 align="center">A protective charm accompanying investiture with an amulet of Darbha grass</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903001">1</a></font></span>Darbha, with that good shield of thine, of hundred guards tilt<br>    death in eld,<br>   Arm thou this man, and with thy might strike thou his adver-<br>    saries down.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903002">2</a></font></span>Darbha, thou hast a hundred shields, thou hast a thousand.<br>    manly powers.<br>   All Gods have given thee to him to bear thee till extreme old<br>    age.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903003">3</a></font></span>They call thee, ‘Darbha, shield of Gods, they call the Brāhmanas-<br>    pati.<br>   They call thee shield of Indra: thou protectest kingdoms from<br>    attack.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903004">4</a></font></span>Darbha, destroyer of the foe, vexing the hearts of enemies,<br>   An Amulet that strengthens rule I make thee, and the body's.<br>    guard.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903005">5</a></font></span>What time Parjanya roared to it with lightning flashes in the sea,<br>   Thence came the drop, the golden drop, thence Darbha into<br>    being sprang.</p>
