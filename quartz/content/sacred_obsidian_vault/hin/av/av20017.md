 <p><a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="green">p. 277</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVII</h3>  
 <p>  I. In perfect unison all yearning hymns of mine that find the light<br>    of heaven have sung forth Indra's praise.<br>   As wives embrace their lord, the comely bridegroom, so they<br>    compass Maghavan about that he may help.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001702">2</a></font></span>Directed unto thee my spirit never strays, for I have set my<br>    hopes on thee, O much-invoked!<br>   Sit, wonderful! as King upon the sacred grass, and let thy<br>    drinking-place be by the Soma juice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001703">3</a></font></span>From indigence and hunger Indra turns away: Maghavan hath<br>    dominion over precious wealth.<br>   These the Seven Rivers flowing on their downward path increase<br>    the vital vigour of the Mighty Steer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001704">4</a></font></span>As on the fair-leafed tree rest birds, to Indra flow the gladden-<br>    ing Soma juices that the bowls contain.<br>   Their face that glows with splendour through their mighty<br>    power hath found the shine of heaven for man, the Aryas'<br>    light.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001705">5</a></font></span>As in the game a gambler piles his winnings, so Maghavan,<br>    sweeping all together, gained the Sun.<br>   This mighty deed of thine none other could achieve, none,<br>   Maghavan, before thee, none in recent time.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001706">6</a></font></span>Maghavan came by turns to all the tribes of men: the Steer<br>    took notice of the people's songs of praise.<br>   The man in whose libations Sakra hath delight by means of<br>    potent Somas vanquisheth his foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001707">7</a></font></span>As waters flow together to the river, thus Somas to Indra flow,<br>    as rivulets to the lake.<br>   In place of sacrifice sages exalt his might, as the rain swells the<br>    corn by moisture sent from heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001708">8</a></font></span>He rushes through the region like a furious bull, he who hath<br>    made these floods the dames of worthy lords.<br>   This Maghavan hath found light for the man who brings<br>    oblation, sheds the juice, and promptly pours his gifts.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001709">9</a></font></span>Let the keen axe come forth together with the light: here be,<br>    as erst, the teeming cow of sacrifice.<br>   Let the Red God shine pure with his refulgent ray, and let the<br>   Lord of heroes glow like heaven's clear sheen. <a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="green">p. 278</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001710">10</a></font></span>O much-invoked, may we subdue all famine and evil want with<br>    store of grain and cattle.<br>   May we allied, as first in rank, with princes, obtain possessions<br>    by our own exertion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001711">11</a></font></span>Brihaspati protect us from the rearward, and from above, and<br>    from below, from sinners.<br>   May Indra from the front, and from the centre, as friend to<br>    friends, vouchsafe us room and freedom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2001712">12</a></font></span>Ye twain are Lords of wealth in earth and heaven, thou, O<br>   Brihaspati, and thou, O Indra.<br>   Mean though he be, give wealth to him who lauds you. Preserve<br>    us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.</p>
