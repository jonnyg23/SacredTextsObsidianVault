 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI</h3>  <h4 align="center">The purusha-Sūkta, on the mystical Sacrifice of Purusha</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900601">1</a></font></span>Purusha hath a thousand arms, a thousand eyes, a thousand feet.<br>   On every side pervading earth he fills a space ten fingers wide.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900602">2</a></font></span>He with three quarters rose to heaven here reappeared a fourth<br>    of him.<br>   Thence he strode forth on every side to all that eats not and that<br>    eats.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900603">3</a></font></span>So mighty is his grandeur, yea, greater than this is Purusha.<br>   All creatures are one-fourth of him, three-fourths what dieth<br>    not in heaven. <a name="page_214"><font size="1" color="green">p. 214</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900604">4</a></font></span>Purusha is in truth this All, what hath been and what yet shall;<br>    be<br>   Lord, too, of immortality—and what hath grown with some-<br>    what else.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900605">5</a></font></span>When they divided Purusha how many portions did they make?<br>   What was his mouth? what were his arms? what are the names<br>    of thighs and feet?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900606">6</a></font></span>The Brāhman was his mouth, of both his arms was the Rājanya<br>    made.<br>   His waist became the Vaisya, from his feet the Sūdra was<br>    produced.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900607">7</a></font></span>The Moon was gendered from his mind, and from his eye the<br>   Sun had birth.<br>   Indra and Agni from his mouth were born and Vāyu from his<br>    breath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900608">8</a></font></span>Forth from his navel come mid-air; the sky was fashioned from<br>    his head.<br>   Earth from his feet, and from his ear the regions. Thus they<br>    formed the worlds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900609">9</a></font></span>In the beginning rose Virāj: Purusha from Virāj was born.<br>   As soon as he was born he spread westward and eastward o'er<br>    the earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900610">10</a></font></span>When Gods performed the sacrifice with Purusha as their<br>    offering.<br>   Spring was the butter, summer was the fuel, autumn was the<br>    gift.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900611">11</a></font></span>That sacrifice, first-born Purusha, they hallowed with the sprink-<br>    led Rains.<br>   The Deities, the Sādhyas, all the Vasus sacrificed with him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900612">12</a></font></span>From it were horses born, from it all creatures with two rows of<br>    teeth.<br>   From it were generated kine, from it were goats and sheep<br>    produced.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900613">13</a></font></span>From that great general sacrifice Richas and Sāma hymns were<br>    born;<br>   Therefrom the metres were produced: the Yajus had its birth<br>    from it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900614">14</a></font></span>From that great general sacrifice the dripping fat was gathered up:<br>   It formed the creatures fleet as wind, and animals both wild and<br>    tame. <a name="page_215"><font size="1" color="green">p. 215</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900615">15</a></font></span>Seven fencing-logs had he, thrice seven layers of fuel were<br>    prepared.<br>   When, offering sacrifice, the Gods bound as their victim Purusha.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1900616">16</a></font></span>Forth from head of the high God seven-and-seventy bright<br>    beams.<br>   Sprang into being, of the King Soma produced from Purusha.</p>
