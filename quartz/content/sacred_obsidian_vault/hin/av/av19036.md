 <p><a name="page_241"><font size="1" color="green">p. 241</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVI</h3>  <h4 align="center">A charm against disease and evil spirits</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903601">1</a></font></span>The Hundred-haired hath banished hence fiends and Consump-<br>    tions by its might.<br>   With splendour hath the charm that scares demons of ill-name<br>    mounted up.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903602">2</a></font></span>It drives off demons with its horns and sorceresses with its root,<br>   It stays Consumption with its waist: from this no wickedness<br>    escapes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903603">3</a></font></span>Consumptions, light and serious, and those which sounds<br>    accompany,<br>   All these the Amulet, Hundred-haired, scarer of fiends, hath<br>    banished hence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903604">4</a></font></span>A hundred men hath it produced, hundred Consumptions chased<br>    away,<br>   All fiends of evil-name it hath smitten, and shakes the<br>   Rākshasas.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903605">5</a></font></span>The Bull that weareth horns of gold, this Amulet with hundred<br>    hairs,<br>   Hath cleft the demons of ill-name and overcome the Rākshasas.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903606">6</a></font></span>Hundred she-fiends, a hundred of Gandharvas and Apsarasas,<br>   A hundred of dog-mated nymphs, I keep away with Hundred-<br>   Hair</p>
