 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013401">1</a></font></span>Here are we sitting east and west and north and south, with<br>    waters. Bottle-gourd vessels.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013402">2</a></font></span>Here east and west and north and south sit the calves sprinkling<br>   Curds and oil. <a name="page_371"><font size="1" color="green">p. 371</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013403">3</a></font></span>Here east and west and north and south the offering of rice<br>    clings on. The leaf of the Asvattha tree.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013404">4</a></font></span>Here east and west and north and south adheres when touched.<br>   That water-drop.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013405">5</a></font></span>Here east and west and north and south in iron mayst thou not<br>    be caught. The cup.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2013406">6</a></font></span>Here east and west and north and south fain would it clasp what<br>    would not clasp.
