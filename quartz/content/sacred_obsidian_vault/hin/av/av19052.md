 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Kāma or Desire</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905201">1</a></font></span>Thereafter rose Desire in the beginning, Desire the primal seed<br>    and germ of Spirit.<br>   O Kāma dwelling with the lofty Kāma, give growth of riches to<br>    the sacrificer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905202">2</a></font></span>Thou, Kāma, art victorious, famous, potent, splendid, a friend.<br>    to him who seeks thy friendship.<br>   Mighty and overpowering in battle, give strength and vigour to<br>    the sacrificer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905203">3</a></font></span>They heard his prayers, and they begot, by Kāma, heavenly-<br>     light for him.<br>   Who from a distance longed for it, a dealer ready to exchange.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905204">4</a></font></span>O Kāma, with whatever wish we make this offering to thee,<br>   May it be all fulfilled to us. Then taste this sacrifice, All hail!</p>
