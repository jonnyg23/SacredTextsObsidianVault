 <p><a name="page_299."><font size="1" color="green">p. 299.</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003801">1</a></font></span>Come, we have pressed the juice for thee. O Indra, drink the<br>   Soma here.<br>   Sit thou on this my sacred grass.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003802">2</a></font></span>O Indra, let thy long-maned Bays, yoked by prayer, bring thee<br>    hitherward.<br>   Give ear and listen to our prayers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003803">3</a></font></span>We, Soma-bearing Brāhmans, call thee, Soma-drinker, with thy<br>    friend,<br>   We, Indra, bringing juice expressed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003804">4</a></font></span>Indra the singers with high praise, Indra reciters with their<br>    lauds,<br>   Indra the choirs have glorified.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003805">5</a></font></span>Indra hath ever close to him his two bay steeds and word-yoked:<br>    car,<br>   Indra, the golden, Thunder-armed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003806">6</a></font></span>Indra hath raised the Sun aloft in heaven that he may see afar.<br>   He burst the mountain for the kine.</p>
