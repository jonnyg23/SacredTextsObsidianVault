 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002301">1</a></font></span>Invoked to drink the Soma juice come with thy bay steeds,<br>   Thunder-armed!<br>   Come, Indra, hitherward, to me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002302">2</a></font></span>Our priest is seated true to time; the grass is regularly strewn;<br>   The pressing-stones were set at morn.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002303">3</a></font></span>These prayers, O thou who bearest prayer, are offered. Seat<br>    thee on the grass.<br>   Hero, enjoy the offered cake.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002304">4</a></font></span>O Vritra-slayer, be thou pleased with these libations, with these<br>    hymns,<br>   Song-loving Indra, with our lauds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002305">5</a></font></span>Our hymns caress the Lord of Strength, vast, drinker of the<br>   Soma's juice, <a name="page_283"><font size="1" color="green">p. 283</font></a><br>   Indra, as mother-cows their calf.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002306">6</a></font></span>Delight thee with the juice we pour for thine own great<br>    munificence:<br>   Yield not thy singer to reproach.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002307">7</a></font></span>We, Indra, dearly loving thee, bearing oblation, sing thee<br>    hymns:<br>   Thou, Vasu, nearly lovest us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002308">8</a></font></span>O thou to whom thy Bays are dear, loose not thy horses far<br>    from us:<br>   Here glad thee, Indra, Lord Divine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002309">9</a></font></span>May long-maned courses, dropping oil, bring thee on swift car<br>    hitherward.<br>   Indra, to seat thee on the grass.</p>
