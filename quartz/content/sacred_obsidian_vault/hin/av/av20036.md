 <p><a name="page_295"><font size="1" color="green">p. 295</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003601">1</a></font></span>With these my hymns I glorify that Indra who is alone to be<br>    invoked by mortals. <a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="green">p. 296</font></a><br>   The Lord, the Mighty One, of manly vigour, victorious, Hero,<br>    true, and full of wisdom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003602">2</a></font></span>Our ancient sires, Navagvas, sages seven, while urging him to<br>    show his might, extolled him,<br>   Dweller on heights, swift smiting down opponents, guileless in<br>    word, and in his thoughts most mighty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003603">3</a></font></span>We seek that Indra to obtain his riches that yield much food,<br>    and men, and store of heroes.<br>   O Lord of Bay Steeds, bring to make us joyful, celestial wealth<br>    abundant, undecaying.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003604">4</a></font></span>Declare to us—if at thy hand aforetime the earlier singers have<br>    obtained good fortune<br>   What is thy share and portion, strong Subduer, Asura-slayer,<br>    rich, invoked of many?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003605">5</a></font></span>He who for car-borne, thunder-wielding Indra, hath a hymn,<br>    craving deeply-piercing, fluent,<br>   Who sends a song effectual, firmly-grasping, and strength-<br>    bestowing, he comes near the mighty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003606">6</a></font></span>Strong of thyself! thou with this art hast shattered with thought-<br>    swift Parvata, him who waxed against thee;<br>   And, Mightiest! rager! boldly rent in pieces things that were<br>    firmly fixed and never shaken..<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003607">7</a></font></span>Him will we fit for you with new devotion, the strongest,<br>   Ancient One, in ancient manner.<br>   So way that Indra, boundless, faithful leader, conduct us o'er all<br>    places hard to traverse.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003608">8</a></font></span>Thou for the people who oppress hast kindled the earthly firma-<br>    ment and that of heaven.<br>   With heat, O Bull, on every side consume them heat earth and<br>    flood for him who hates devotion:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003609">9</a></font></span>Of all the heavenly folk, of earthly creatures, thou art the King,<br>   O God of splendid aspect.<br>   In thy right hand, O Indra, grasp the thunder: Eternal! thou<br>    destroyest all enchantments.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003610">10</a></font></span>Give us confirmed prosperity, O Indra, vast and exhaustless for<br>    the foes' subduing.<br>   Strengthen therewith the Arya's hate and Dāsa's; and let the<br>    arms of Nahushas be mighty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2003611">11</a></font></span>Come with thy teams which bring all blessings, hither, disposer,<br>    much-invoked, exceeding holy! <a name="page_297"><font size="1" color="green">p. 297</font></a><br>   Come to me swiftly with these teams of coursers, these which<br>    no fiend, no God may stay or hinder.</p>
