 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007501">1</a></font></span>Couples desirous of thine aid are storming thee, pouring their<br>    presents forth to win a stall of king pouring gifts, Indra,<br>    seeking thee.<br>   When two men seeking spoil or heaven thou bringest face to<br>    face in war,<br>   Thou showest, Indra, then the bolt, thy constant friend, the bull<br>    that ever waits on thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007502">2</a></font></span>This thine heroic power full well the people knew, wherewith<br>    thou brakest down, Indra, autumnal forts, brakest them down<br>    with conquering might. <a name="page_326"><font size="1" color="green">p. 326</font></a><br>   Thou hast chastised. O Indra, Lord of strength, the man who<br>    worships not,<br>   And made thine own this great earth and these water-floods,<br>    with joyous heart these water-floods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2007503">3</a></font></span>And they have bruited far this hero might, when thou, O Strong<br>   One, in thy joy helpest thy suppliants who sought to win thee<br>    for their Friend.<br>   Their battle-cry thou madest sound victorious in the shocks of<br>    war.<br>   One stream after another have they gained from thee, eager for<br>    glory have they gained.</p>
