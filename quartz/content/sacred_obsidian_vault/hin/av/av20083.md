 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008301">1</a></font></span>O Indra, grant a happy home, a triple refuge, triply strong.<br>   Bestow a dwelling-place on the rich lords and me, and keep thy<br>    dart afar from these.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008302">2</a></font></span>They who with minds intent on spoil subdue the foe, boldly<br>    attack and smite him down.<br>   From these, O Indra, Bounteous Lord who lovest song, be<br>    closest guardian of our lives.</p>
