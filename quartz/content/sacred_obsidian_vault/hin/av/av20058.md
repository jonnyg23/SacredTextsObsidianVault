 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005801">1</a></font></span>Turning, as 'twere, to meet the Sun, enjoy from Indra all good<br>    things.<br>   When he who will be born is born with power we look to<br>    treasures as our heritage.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005802">2</a></font></span>Praise him who sends us wealth, whose bounties injure none.<br>   Good are the gifts which Indra gives.<br>   He is not wroth with one who satisfies his wish: he turns his<br>    mind to granting boons. <a name="page_312"><font size="1" color="green">p. 312</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005803">3</a></font></span>Verily, Sūrya, thou art great; truly, Āditya, thou art great.<br>   As thou art great indeed thy greatness is admired: yea, verily,<br>    great art thou, O God.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005804">4</a></font></span>Yea, Sūrya, thou art great in fame: thou evermore, O God,<br>    art great.<br>   By greatness thou art President of Gods, divine, far-spread,<br>    inviolable light.</p>
