 <p><a name="page_282"><font size="1" color="green">p. 282</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXII</h3>  
 <p> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002201">1</a></font></span>Hero, the Soma being pressed I pour the juice for thee to drink<br>   Sate thee and finish thy carouse.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002202">2</a></font></span>Let not the fools, or those who mock, beguile thee when they<br>    seek thine aid:<br>   Love not the enemies of prayer.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002203">3</a></font></span>Here let them with rich milky draught cheer thee to great<br>    munificence:<br>   Drink as the wild bull drinks the lake.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002204">4</a></font></span>Praise, even as he is known, with song Indra, the guardian of<br>    the kine,<br>   The Son of Truth, Lord of the brave.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002205">5</a></font></span>Hither his bay steeds have been sent, red steeds are on the<br>    sacred grass.<br>   Where we in concert sing our songs.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002206">6</a></font></span>For Indra, Thunder-armed, the kine have yielded mingled milk<br>    and meath,<br>   What time he found them in the vault.</p>
