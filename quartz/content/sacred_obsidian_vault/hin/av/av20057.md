 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005701">1</a></font></span>As a good cow to him who milks, we call the doer of fair deeds.<br>   To our assistance day by day.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005702">2</a></font></span>Come thou to our libations, drink of Soma, Soma-drinker thou!<br>   The rich One's rapture giveth kine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005703">3</a></font></span>So may we be acquainted with thine innermost benevolence:<br>   Neglect us not, come hitherward.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005704">4</a></font></span>Drink for our help the Soma bright, vigilant, and exceeding<br>    strong,<br>   O Indra, Lord of Hundred Powers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005705">5</a></font></span>O Satakratu, powers which thou mid the Five Races hast dis-<br>    played,<br>   These, Indra, do I claim of thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005706">6</a></font></span>Indra, great glory hast thou gained. Win splendid fame which<br>    none may mar.<br>   We make thy might perpetual.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005707">7</a></font></span>Come to us either from anear, or, Sakra, come from far away.<br>   Indra, wherever be thy home, come thence, O Caster of the<br>   Stone. <a name="page_311"><font size="1" color="green">p. 311</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005708">8</a></font></span>Verily Indra, conquering all, driveth even mighty fear away.<br>   For firm is he and swift to act.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005709">9</a></font></span>Indra be gracious unto us: sin shall not reach us afterward,<br>   And good shall be before us still.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005710">10</a></font></span>From all the regions of the world let Indra send security.<br>   The foe-subduer, swift to act.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005714">14</a></font></span>We compass thee like waters, we whose grass in trimmed and<br>   Soma pressed.<br>   Here where the filter pours its stream thy worshippers round<br>    thee, O Vritra-slayer, sit.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005715">15</a></font></span>Men, Vasu, by the Soma with lauds call thee to the foremost<br>    place.<br>   When comest thou athirst unto the juice as home, O Indra, like<br>    a bellowing bull?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2005716">16</a></font></span>Boldly, bold Hero, bring us spoil in thousands for the Kanvas'<br>    sake.<br>   O active Maghavan, with eager prayer we crave the yellow-hued<br>    with store of kine.</p>
