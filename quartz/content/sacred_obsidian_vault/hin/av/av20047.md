 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004701">1</a></font></span>We make this Indra show his strength, to strike the mighty<br>   Vritra dead:<br>   A vigorous Hero shall he be.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004702">2</a></font></span>Indra was made for giving, most powerful, friendly in carouse,<br>   Bright, meet for Soma, famed in song. <a name="page_304"><font size="1" color="green">p. 304</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004703">3</a></font></span>By song, as 'twere, the mighty bolt, which none may parry, was<br>    prepared:<br>   Lofty, invincible he grew.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004710">10</a></font></span>They who stand round him as he moves harness the bright, the<br>    ruddy Steed:<br>   The lights are shining in the sky.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004711">11</a></font></span>They yoke on both sides to the car the two bay coursers dear to<br>    him,<br>   Bold, tawny, bearers of the Chief.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004712">12</a></font></span>Thou, making light where no light was, and form, O Men r<br>    where no form was,<br>   Wast born together with the Dawns,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004713">13</a></font></span>His bright rays bear him up aloft, the God who knoweth all that=<br>    is,<br>   Sūrya, that every one may see.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004714">14</a></font></span>The constellations pass away, like thieves, together with their-<br>    beams,<br>   Before the all-beholding Sun.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004715">15</a></font></span>His herald rays are seen afar refulgent o'er the world of men,<br>   Like fiery flames that burn and blaze.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004716">16</a></font></span>Swift and all-beautiful art thou, O Sūrya, maker of the light.<br>   Illuming all the radiant realm.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004717">17</a></font></span>Thou guest to the troops of Gods, thou comest hither to man-<br>    kind,<br>   Hither, all light for us to see.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004718">18</a></font></span>Thou with that eye of thine wherewith thou seest, brilliant.<br>   Varuna,<br>   The active one throughout mankind.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004719">19</a></font></span>Pervadest heaven and wide mid-air, melting the days out with.<br>    thy beams,<br>   Sun, seeing all things that have birth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004720">20</a></font></span>Seven bay steeds, harnessed to thy car, bear thee, O.thou far-<br>    seeing One,<br>   God, Sūrya, thee with radiant hair.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2004721">21</a></font></span>Sūrya hath yoked the seven bright mares, the daughters of the<br>    car: With these,<br>   His own dear team, he travelleth.</p>
