 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A glorification of Prāna, Breath or Vital Spirit</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100401">1</a></font></span>Homage to Prāna, him who hath dominion o'er the universe,<br>   Who hath become the Sovran Lord of all, on whom the whole<br>    depends!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100402">2</a></font></span>Homage, O Prāna, to thy roar, to thunder-peal and lightning<br>    flash!<br>   Homage, O Prāna, unto thee what time thou sendest down thy<br>    rain!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100403">3</a></font></span>When Prāna with a thunderous voice shouts his loud message<br>    to the plants,<br>   They straightway are impregnate, they conceive, and bear<br>    abundantly. <a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="green">p. 53</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100404">4</a></font></span>When the due season hath arrived and Prāna shouteth to herbs,<br>   Then all is joyful, yea, each thing upon the surface of the earth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100405">5</a></font></span>When Prāna hath poured down his flood of rain upon the<br>    mighty land.<br>   Cattle and beasts rejoice thereat: Now great will he our<br>    strength, they cry.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100406">6</a></font></span>Watered by Prāna's rain the plants have raised their voices in<br>    accord:<br>   Thou hast prolonged our life, they say, and given fragrance to us<br>    all.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100407">7</a></font></span>Homage to thee when coming nigh, homage to thee departing<br>    hence!<br>   Homage, O Prāna, be to thee when standing and when sitting<br>    still.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100408">8</a></font></span>Homage to thee at every breath thou drawest in and sendest<br>    forth!<br>   Homage to thee when turned away, homage to thee seen face to<br>    face! This reverence be to all of thee!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100409">9</a></font></span>Prāna, communicate to us thy dear, thy very dearest form.<br>   Whatever healing balm thou hast, give us thereof that we may<br>    live.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100410">10</a></font></span>Prāna robes living creatures as a father his beloved son. Prāna<br>    is sovran Lord of all, of all that breathes not, all that<br>    breathes<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100411">11</a></font></span>Prāna is Fever, he is Death. Prāna is worshipped by the Gods.<br>   Prāna sets in the loftiest sphere the man who speaks the words<br>    of truth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100412">12</a></font></span>Prāna is Deshtri, and Virāj Prāna is reverenced by all. He is the<br>   Sun, he is the Moon. Prāna is called Prajāpati.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100413">13</a></font></span>Both breaths are rice and barley, and Prāna is called the toiling<br>    ox:<br>   In barley is the inbreath laid, and rice is named the outward<br>    breath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100414">14</a></font></span>The human infant in the womb draws vital breath and sends it<br>   Lout:<br>   When thou, O Prāna, quickenest the babe it springs anew to<br>    life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100415">15</a></font></span>The name of Prāna is bestowed on Mātarisvan and on Wind.<br>   On Prāna, past and future, yea, on Prāna everything depends. <a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="green">p. 54</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100416">16</a></font></span>All herbs and plants spring forth and grow when thou, O Prāna<br>    quickenest,<br>   Plants of Atharvan, Angiras, plants of the deities and men.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100417">17</a></font></span>When Prāna hath poured down his flood of rain upon the<br>    mighty earth,<br>   The plants are wakened into life, and every herd that grows on<br>    ground.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100418">18</a></font></span>The man who knows this truth of thee, O Prāna, and what bears<br>    thee up<br>   To him will all present their gift of tribute in that loftiest will<br>    all present their gift of tribute in that loftiest world.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100419">19</a></font></span>As all these living creatures are thy tributaries, Prāna, so<br>   Shall they bring tribute unto him who hears thee with attentive<br>    ears.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100420">20</a></font></span>Filled with a babe, mid deities he wanders: grown; near at hand,<br>    he springs again to being.<br>   That Father, grown the present and the future, hath past into<br>    the son with mighty powers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100421">21</a></font></span>Hansa, what time he rises up, leaves in the flood one foot un-<br>    moved.<br>   If he withdrew it there would be no more tomorrow or to-day,<br>   Never would there be night, no more would daylight shine or<br>    morning flush.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100422">22</a></font></span>It rolleth on, eight-wheeled and single-fellied, and with a thousand<br>    eyes, forward and backward.<br>   With one half it engendered all creation. What sign is there to<br>    tell us of the other?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100423">23</a></font></span>Homage, O Prāna unto thee armed with swift bow among the<br>    rest,<br>   In whose dominion is this All of varied sort that stirs and<br>    works!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100424">24</a></font></span>May he who rules this Universe of varied sort, that stirs and<br>    works,<br>   Prāna, alert and resolute, assist me through the prayer I pray.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100425">25</a></font></span>Erect among the sleepers he wakes, and is never laid at length,<br>   No one hath ever heard that he hath been asleep while others<br>    slept.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100426">26</a></font></span>Thou, Prāna, never shalt be hid, never shalt be estranged from<br>    me.<br>   I bind thee on myself for life, O Prāna, like the Waters' germ.</p>
