 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006701">1</a></font></span>The pourer of oblations gains the home of wealth pouring his<br>    gift conciliates hostilities, yea, the hostilities of Gods.<br>   Pouring he strives, unchecked and strong, to win him riches<br>    thousandfold.<br>   Indra gives lasting wealth to him who pours forth gifts; yea,<br>    wealth he gives that long shall last.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006702">2</a></font></span>Ne'er may those manly deeds of yours for us grow old, never<br>    may your bright glories fall into decay, never before your<br>    time decay. <a name="page_317"><font size="1" color="green">p. 317</font></a><br>   What deed of yours, new every age, wondrous, surpassing man,.<br>    rings forth,<br>   Whatever, Maruts may be difficult to gain grant us whate'er is<br>    hard to win.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006703">3</a></font></span>I think on Agni, Hotar, the munificent, the gracious Son of<br>    strength, who knoweth all that live, as holy Sage who knoweth<br>    all.<br>   Lord of fair rites, a God with form erected turning to the Gods.<br>   He, when the flame hath sprung forth from the holy oil, the<br>    offered fatness, longeth for it with his glow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006704">4</a></font></span>Busied with sacrifice, with spotted deer and spears, gleaming.<br>    upon your way with ornaments, yea, our friends,<br>   Sitting on sacred grass, ye sons of Bharata, drink Soma from<br>    the Potar's bowl, O Men of heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006705">5</a></font></span>Bring the Gods hither, Sage, and offer sacrifice. At the three<br>    altars seat thee willingly .O Priest.<br>   Accept for thy delight the proffered Soma mead: drink from<br>    the Kindler's bowl and sate thee with thy share.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006706">6</a></font></span>This is the strengthener of thy body's manly might: strength,.<br>    victory for all time are laid within thine arms.<br>   Pressed for thee, Maghavan, it is offered unto thee: drink from<br>    the chalice of this Brāhman, drink thy fill.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006707">7</a></font></span>Him whom of old I called on, him I call on now. He is to be<br>    invoked: his name is He who Gives.<br>   Here brought by priests in Soma mead. Granter of Wealth,<br>    drink Soma with the Seasons from the Hotar's Cup.</p>
