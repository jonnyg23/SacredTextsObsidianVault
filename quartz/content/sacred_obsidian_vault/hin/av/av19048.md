 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLVIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Night for protection</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904801">1</a></font></span>Then all that we accumulate, all that the treasure-chest contains,.<br>   All this do we entrust to thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904802">2</a></font></span>Entrust thou us to Dawn, O Mother Night.<br>   May Dawn entrust us to the Day, and Day to thee, O splendid.<br>   One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904803">3</a></font></span>Whatever flying thing be here, whatever thing that creeps and.<br>    crawls.<br>   Whatever fastens on a joint, therefrom do thou protect us,<br>   Night.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904804">4</a></font></span>So guard thou us from west and east, protect us from the north<br>    and south.<br>   O splendid One, preserve us: we, the singers of thy praise, are<br>    here.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904805">5</a></font></span>They who are followers of Night, and they who watch o'er<br>    living things.<br>   They who protect all cattle, they keep watch and ward over our<br>    lives, over our herds keep watch and ward.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904806">6</a></font></span>Verily, Night, I know thy name, Dropper of Fatness art thou<br>    called.<br>   Thee Bharadvāja knew as such: as such be watchful o'er our<br>    wealth.</p>
