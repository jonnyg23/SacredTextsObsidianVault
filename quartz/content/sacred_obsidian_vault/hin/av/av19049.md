 <p><a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="green">p. 251</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIX</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn of Praise and prayer to Night</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904901">1</a></font></span>Friend of the home, the strong and youthful maiden, Night, dear<br>    to Savitar the God, and Bhaga,<br>   All-compassing, all-glorious, prompt to listen, hath with her<br>    greatness filled the earth and heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904902">2</a></font></span>Over all depths hath she gone up, and mounted, most mighty<br>   One, the sky's exalted summit.<br>   Over me now the loving Night is spreading with her auspicious.<br>   Godlike ways like Mitra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904903">3</a></font></span>Excellent, high-born, blissful, meet for worship, Night, thou hast<br>    come: stay here with friendly spirit.<br>   Guard us, the food for men that we have gotten, and all pro-<br>    sperity that comes of cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904904">4</a></font></span>With eager haste hath Night assumed the vigour of leopard,.<br>    tiger, or of tawny lion,<br>   The horse's neighing and the wild-man's bellow, Thou takest<br>    many a form when thou appearest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904905">5</a></font></span>Kind through the Night be absence of the sunshine: Mother of<br>   Frost, may she be swift to hear us.<br>   Take notice of the hymn, thou highly favoured, wherewith I<br>    worship thee in all the regions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904906">6</a></font></span>Even as a King, O splendid Night, thou takest pleasure in our<br>    hymn.<br>   May we through Mornings as they flush have all our good men,<br>    round us, and become possessors of all wealth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904907">7</a></font></span>Yes, Rāmyā is the name thou hast assumed. The men who fain.<br>    would spoil.<br>   My wealth do thou annoy, O Night, that not one robber may<br>    appear, none may a second time appear.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904908">8</a></font></span>Thou like a well-wrought cup, O Night, art lovely: thou, a.<br>    young maid, art formed in perfect beauty.<br>   Thou lovingly, for me with eyes to se: them, hast bound on thee<br>    heaven's stars as thine adornment. <a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="green">p. 252</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904909">9</a></font></span>Whatever robber comes to-day, mischievous mortal enemy.<br>   Let Night go forth, encounter him, and smite away his neck and<br>    head;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1904910">10</a></font></span>His feet that he may walk no more, his hands that he may do<br>    no harm.<br>   The robber who comes hitherward goes crushed and mutilated<br>    hence,<br>   Goes hence, goes far away from us, goes hence and bears no<br>    spoil away.</p>
