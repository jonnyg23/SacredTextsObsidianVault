 <p><a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="green">p. 36</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN X</h3>  <h4 align="center">A glorification of the sacred Cow as representing the radiant heavens</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001001">1</a></font></span>Worship to thee springing to life, and worship unto thee when<br>    born!<br>   Worship, O Cow, to thy tail-hair, and to thy hooves, and to thy<br>    form!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001002">2</a></font></span>The man who knows the Seven Floods, who knows the seven<br>    distances,<br>   Who knows the head of sacrifice, he may receive the holy Cow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001003">3</a></font></span>I know the Seven Water-floods, I know the seven distances,<br>   I know the head of sacrifice, and Soma shining bright in her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001004">4</a></font></span>Hitherward we invite with prayer the Cow who pours a thou-<br>    sand streams,<br>   By whom the heaven, by whom the earth, by whom these waters<br>    are preserved.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001005">5</a></font></span>Upon her back there are a hundred keepers, a hundred metal<br>    bowls, a hundred milkers.<br>   The Deities who breathe in her all separately know the Cow. <a name="page_37"><font size="1" color="green">p. 37</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001006">6</a></font></span>Her foot is sacrifice, her milk libation, Svadhā her breath, Mahï-<br>    lukā the mighty:<br>   To the God goes with prayer the Cow who hath Parjanya for<br>    her lord.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001007">7</a></font></span>Agni hath entered into thee; Soma, O Cow, hath entered thee.<br>   Thine udder is Parjanya, O blest Cow; the lightnings are thy<br>    teats.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001008">8</a></font></span>Thou pourest out the Waters first, and corn-lands afterward,<br>   O Cow.<br>   Thirdly thou pourest princely sway. O Cow, thou pourest food<br>    and milk.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001009">9</a></font></span>When, Holy One, thou camest nigh invited by the Ādityas' call,<br>   Indra gave thee to drink, O cow, a thousand bowls of Soma<br>    juice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001010">10</a></font></span>The Bull, what time thou followedst the way of Indra, summon-<br>    ed thee:<br>   Thence the Fiend-slayer, angered, took thy water and thy milk<br>    away.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001011">11</a></font></span>O Cow, the milk which in his wrath the Lord of Riches took<br>    from thee,<br>   That same the vault of heaven now preserveth in three reser-<br>    voirs.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001012">12</a></font></span>The Cow Celestial received that Soma in three vessels, where.<br>   Atharvan, consecrated, sate upon the Sacred Grass of gold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001013">13</a></font></span>Come hither with the Soma, come with every footed thing; the<br>   Cow<br>   With Kalis and Gandharvas by her side hath stepped upon the<br>    sea.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001014">14</a></font></span>Come hither with the Wind, yea, come with every creature borne<br>    on wings.<br>   Laden with holy verse and song the Cow hath leapt into the<br>    sea.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001015">15</a></font></span>Come with the Sun, come hitherward with every creature that<br>    hath eyes,<br>   Bearing auspicious lights with her the Cow hath looked across<br>    the sea.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001016">16</a></font></span>When, covered round about with gold, thou stoodest there, O<br>   Holy One,<br>   The ocean turned into a horse and mounted on thy back, O<br>   Cow, <a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="green">p. 38</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001017">17</a></font></span>Then came and met the Blessed Ones, Deshtri, the Cow, and<br>   Svadhā, where<br>   Atharvan, consecrated. sate upon the Sacred Grass of gold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001018">18</a></font></span>The Kshatriya's mother is the Cow, thy mother, Svadhā! is the<br>   Cow.<br>   Sacrifice is the weapon of the Cow: the thought arose from,<br>    her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001019">19</a></font></span>From Brahma's summit there went forth a drop that mounted<br>    up on high:<br>   From that wast thou produced, O Cow, from that the Hotar<br>    sprang to life.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001020">20</a></font></span>Forth from thy mouth the Gāthās came, from thy neck's nape<br>    sprang strength, O Cow.<br>   Sacrifice from thy flanks was born, and rays of sunlight from.<br>    thy teats,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001021">21</a></font></span>From thy fore-quarters and thy thighs motion was generated,<br>   Cow!<br>   Food from thine entrails was produced, and from thy belly came<br>    the plants.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001022">22</a></font></span>When into Varuna's belly thou hadst found a passage for thy-<br>    self,<br>   The Brāhman called thee thence, for he knew how to guide and<br>    lead thee forth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001023">23</a></font></span>All trembled at the babe that came from him who brings not to<br>    the birth.<br>   He hath produced her—thus they cried—He is a cow, and formed<br>    by spells, he hath become skin to her.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001024">24</a></font></span>He only joineth battle, yea, he who alone controlleth her.<br>   Now sacrifices have become victories, and the Cow their eye.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001025">25</a></font></span>The Cow hath welcomed sacrifice: the Cow hath held the Sun<br>    in place.<br>   Together with the prayer the mess of rice hath passed into the<br>   Cow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001026">26</a></font></span>They call the Cow immortal life, pay homage to the Cow as<br>   Death.<br>   She hath become this universe, Fathers, and Rishis, hath become<br>    the Gods, and men, and Asuras.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001027">27</a></font></span>The man who hath this knowledge may receive the Cow with.<br>    welcoming. <a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="green">p. 39</font></a><br>   So for the giver willingly doth perfect sacrifice pour milk.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001028">28</a></font></span>Within the mouth of Varuna three tongues are glittering with<br>    light.<br>   That which shines midmost of them is this Cow most difficult to<br>    hold.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001029">29</a></font></span>Four-parted was the Cow's prolific humour.<br>   One-fourth is Water, one-fourth life eternal, one-fourth is sacri-<br>    fice, one-fourth are cattle.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001030">30</a></font></span>The Cow is Heaven, the Cow is Earth, the Cow is Vishnu, Lord<br>    of Life.<br>    e The Sādhyas and the Vasus have drunk the out-pourings of the<br>   Cow.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001031">31</a></font></span>When these, Sādhyas and Vasus, have drunk the out-pourings of<br>    the Cow,<br>   They in the Bright One's dwelling-place pay adoration to her<br>    milk.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001032">32</a></font></span>For Soma some have milked her: some worship the fatness she<br>    hath poured.<br>   They who have given a cow to him who hath this knowledge<br>    have gone up to the third region of the sky.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001033">33</a></font></span>He who hath given a Cow unto the Brāhmans winneth all the<br>    worlds.<br>   For Right is firmly set in her devotion, and religious zeal.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1001034">34</a></font></span>Both Gods and mortal men depend for life and being on the<br>   Cow.<br>   She hath become this universe: all that the Sun surveys is she.</p>
