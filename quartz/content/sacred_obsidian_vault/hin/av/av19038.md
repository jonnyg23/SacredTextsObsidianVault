 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXXVIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A protective charm</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903801">1</a></font></span>Never Consumption, never curse touches the man, Arundhatī!<br>   Whom the delicious odour of the healing Bdellium penetrates<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1903802">2</a></font></span>Consumptions flee apart from it as from a wild beast fly the<br>    deer.<br>   If thou, O Bdellium, art produced from Sindhu or hast come<br>    from sea,<br>   The quality of both have I taken to keep this man unscathed.</p>
