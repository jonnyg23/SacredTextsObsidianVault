 <p><a name="page_336"><font size="1" color="green">p. 336</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XCI</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009101">1</a></font></span>This holy hymn sublime and seven-headed, sprung from eternal<br>   Law, our sire discovered.<br>   Ayāsya, friend of all men, hath engendered the fourth hymn as<br>    he sang his laud to Indra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009102">2</a></font></span>Thinking aright, praising eternal Order, the sons of Dyaus the<br>   Asura, those heroes,<br>   Angirases, holding the rank of sages, first honoured sacrifice's<br>    holy statute.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009103">3</a></font></span>Girt by his friends who cried with swanlike voices, bursting the<br>    stoney barriers of the prison,<br>   Brihaspati spake in thunder to the cattle, and uttered praise and.<br>    song when he had found them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009104">4</a></font></span>Apart from one, away from two above him, he draye the kine<br>    that stood in bonds of falsehood.<br>   Brihaspati, seeking light amid the darkness, draye forth the<br>    bright cows: three he made apparent.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009105">5</a></font></span>When he had cleft the lairs and western castle, he cut off three<br>    from him who held the waters.<br>   Brihaspati discovered, while he thundered like Dyaus, the dawn,.<br>    the sun, the cow, the lightning.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009106">6</a></font></span>As with a hand, so with his roaring Indra cleft Vala through,.<br>    the guardian of the cattle.<br>   Seeking the milk-draught with sweat-shining comrades he stole:<br>    the Pani's kine and left him weeping.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009107">7</a></font></span>He with bright faithful friends, winners of booty, hath rent the<br>    milker of the cows asunder.<br>   Brihaspati with wild boars strong and mighty sweating with heat<br>    hath gained a rich possession.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009108">8</a></font></span>They, longing for the kine, with faithful spirit incited with their<br>    hymns the Lord of cattle.<br>   Brihaspati freed the radiant cows with comrades self-yoked,.<br>    averting shame from one another.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009109">9</a></font></span>In our assembly with auspicious praises exalting him who roareth.<br>    like a lion.<br>   May we in every fight where heroes conquer rejoice in strong.<br>   Brihaspati the victor.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009110">10</a></font></span>When he had won him strength of every nature and gone to<br>    heaven and its most lofty mansions, <a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="green">p. 337</font></a><br>   Men praised Brihaspati the mighty, bringing the light within<br>    their mouths from sundry places.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009111">11</a></font></span>Fulfil the prayer that begs for vital vigor: aid in your wonted<br>    manner e'en the humble.<br>   Let all our foes be turned and driven backward. Hear this, O<br>   Heaven and Earth, ye all-producers.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2009112">12</a></font></span>Indra with mighty strength hath cleft asunder the head of Arbuda<br>    the watery monster,<br>   Slain Ahi, and set free the Seven Rivers. O Heaven and Earth,<br>    with all the Gods, protect us.</p>
