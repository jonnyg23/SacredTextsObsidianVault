 <p><a name="page_333"><font size="1" color="green">p. 333</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXVIII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008801">1</a></font></span>Him who with might hath propped earth's ends, who sitteth in<br>    threefold seat, Brihaspati, with thunder,<br>   Him of the pleasant tongue have ancient sages, deep thinking,.<br>    holy singers, set before them.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008802">2</a></font></span>Wild in their course, in well-marked wise rejoicing were they,.<br>   Brihaspati, who pressed around us<br>   Preserve, Brihaspati, the stall uninjured,r,this company's raining.<br>    ever-moving birth-place.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008803">3</a></font></span>Brihaspati, from thy remotest distance have they sat down who<br>    love the law eternal.<br>   For thee were dug wells springing from the mountain, which<br>    murmuring round about pour streams of sweetness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008804">4</a></font></span>Brihaspati, when first he had his being from mighty splendour in<br>    supremest heaven.<br>   Strong, with his sevenfold mouth, with noise of thunder, with<br>    his seven rays blew and dispersed the darkness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008805">5</a></font></span>With the loud-shouting band who sang his praises, with thunder,<br>    he destroyed malignant Vala.<br>   Brihaspati thundering drave forth the cattle, the lowing cows<br>    who make oblations ready.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008806">6</a></font></span>Serve we with sacrifices, gifts, and homage even thus the Steer<br>    of all the Gods, the Father.<br>   Brihaspati, may we be lords of riches, with noble progeny and,<br>    store of heroes.</p>
