 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">Speculations on the Supreme Being and Cosmogonical and theological subjects</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000801">1</a></font></span>Worship to loftiest Brahma, Lord of what hath been and what<br>    shall be,<br>   To him who rules the universe, and heavenly light is all his own!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000802">2</a></font></span>Upheld by Skambha's power these two, the heaven and the earth,<br>    stand fast.<br>   Skambha is all this world of life, whatever breathes or shuts an.<br>    eye.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000803">3</a></font></span>Three generations have gone by and vanished and others near<br>    have entered into sunlight.<br>   There stood on high he who metes out the region into green,<br>    plants hath passed the Golden-coloured.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000804">4</a></font></span>One is the wheel, the tires are twelve in number, the naves are<br>    three What man hath understood it?<br>   Three hundred spokes have thereupon been hammered, and sixty<br>    pins set firmly in their places. <a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000805">5</a></font></span>Discern thou this, O Savitar. Six are the twins, one singly born.<br>   They claim relationship in that among them which is born alone.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000806">6</a></font></span>Though manifest, it lies concealed in the vast place they call the<br>    old:<br>   Therein is firmly stationed all the moving, breathing universe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000807">7</a></font></span>Up, eastward downward in the west, ‘it rolleth, with countless<br>    elements, one-wheeled, single-fellied.<br>   With half it hath begotten all creation. Where hath the other half<br>    become unnoticed?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000813">13</a></font></span>In front of these the five-horsed car moves onward: side-horses,<br>    harnessed with the others draw it.<br>   No one hath seen its hither course untravelled; the height sees<br>    it more near, the depth more distant.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000809">9</a></font></span>The bowl with mouth inclined and bottom upward holds stored<br>    within it every form of glory.<br>   Thereon together sit the Seven Rishis who have become this<br>    mighty One's protectors<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000810">10</a></font></span>The Verse employed at opening and conclusion, the Verse<br>    employed in each and every portion;<br>   That by which sacrifice proceedeth onward. I ask thee which is<br>    that of all the Verses.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000811">11</a></font></span>That which hath power of motion, that which flies, or stands,<br>    which breathes or breathes not, which, existing, shuts the eye<br>   Wearing all forms that entity upholds the earth, and in its close<br>    consistence still is only one.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000812">12</a></font></span>The infinite to every side extended, the finite and the infinite<br>    around us,<br>   These twain Heaven's Lord divides as he advances, knowing the<br>    past hereof and all the future<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000813">13</a></font></span>Within the womb Prajāpati is moving: he, though unseen, is<br>    born in sundry places.<br>   He with one half engendered all creation. What sign is there to<br>    tell us of the other?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000814">14</a></font></span>All men behold him with the eye, but with the mind they know<br>    not him.<br>   Holding aloft the water as a water-bearer in her jar.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000815">15</a></font></span>With the full vase he dwells afar, is left far off what time it fails,<br>   A mighty Being in creation's centre: to him the rulers of the<br>    realms bring tribute. <a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="green">p. 29</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000816">16</a></font></span>That, whence the Sun arises, that whither he goes to take his<br>    rest,<br>   That verily I hold supreme: naught in the world surpasses it.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000817">17</a></font></span>Those who in recent times, midmost, or ancient, on all sides.<br>    greet the sage who knows the Veda,<br>   One and all, verily discuss Āditya, the second Agni, and the<br>    threefold Hansa.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000818">18</a></font></span>This gold-hued Haiisa's wings, flying to heaven, spread o'er a<br>    thousand days' continued journey.<br>   Supporting all the Gods upon his bosom, he goes his way behol-<br>    ding every creature.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000819">19</a></font></span>By truth he blazes up aloft by Brahma, he looks down below:<br>   He breathes obliquely with his breath, he on whom what is.<br>    highest rests.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000820">20</a></font></span>The sage who knows the kindling-sticks whence by attrition<br>    wealth is drawn,<br>   Will comprehend what is most high, will know the mighty<br>   Brāhmana.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000821">21</a></font></span>Footless at first was he produced, footless he brought celestial<br>    light.<br>   Four-footed grown, and meet for use, he seized each thing<br>    enjoyable.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000822">22</a></font></span>Useful will he become, and then will he consume great store of<br>    food<br>   The man who humbly worshippeth the eternal and victorious<br>   God.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000823">23</a></font></span>Him too they call eternal; he may become new again to-day.<br>   Day and Night reproduce themselves, each from the form the<br>    other wears.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000824">24</a></font></span>A hundred, thousand, myriad, yea a hundred million stores of<br>    wealth that passes count are laid in him.<br>   This wealth they kill as he looks on, and now this God shines<br>    bright therefrom.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000825">25</a></font></span>One is yet finer than a hair, one is not even visible. And hence<br>    the Deity who grasps with firmer hold is dear to me.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000826">26</a></font></span>This fair one is untouched by age, immortal in a mortal's house.<br>   He for whom she was made lies low, and he who formed her<br>    hath grown old.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000827">27</a></font></span>Thou art a woman, and a man; thou art a damsel and a boy. <a name="page_30"><font size="1" color="green">p. 30</font></a><br>   Grown old thou totterest with a staff, new-born thou lookest<br>    every way.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000828">28</a></font></span>Either the sire or son of these, the eldest or the youngest child.<br>   As sole God dwelling in the mind, first born, he still is in the<br>    womb.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000829">29</a></font></span>Forth from the full he lifts the full, the full he sprinkles with<br>    the full.<br>   Now also may we know the source from which the stream is<br>    sprinkled round.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000830">30</a></font></span>Brought forth in olden time, the everlasting, high over all that<br>    is was she, the Ancient.<br>   The mighty Goddess of the Morn, refulgent with one eye, looketh<br>    round with one that winketh,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000831">31</a></font></span>Known by the name of Guardian Grace the Deity sits girt by<br>   Right.<br>   The trees have taken from her hue, green-garlanded, their robe<br>    of green.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000832">32</a></font></span>When he is near she leaves him not, she sees him not though he<br>    is near.<br>   Behold the wisdom of the God; he hath not died, he grows not<br>    old.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000833">33</a></font></span>Voices that never were before emitted speak as fitteth them.<br>   Whither they go and speak, they say there is the mighty Brāh-<br>    mana.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000834">34</a></font></span>I ask thee where the waters' flower by wondrous magic art was<br>    placed,<br>   Thereon the Gods and men are set as spokes are fastened in the<br>    nave.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000835">35</a></font></span>Who gave command unto the wind that blowet!<br>   Who ranged the five united heavenly regions?<br>   Who were the Gods who cared not for oblations!<br>   Which of them brought the sacrificial waters?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000836">36</a></font></span>One God inhabiteth the earth we live on; another hath encom-<br>    passed air's mid-region.<br>   One, the Supporter, takes the heaven and bears it: some keep-<br>    ing watch guard all the quarters safely.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000837">37</a></font></span>The man who knows the drawn-out string on which these crea-<br>    tures all are strung,<br>   The man who knows the thread's thread, he may know the<br>    mighty Brāhmana. <a name="page_31"><font size="1" color="green">p. 31</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000838">38</a></font></span>I know the drawn-out string, the thread whereon these creatures<br>    all are strung.<br>   I know the thread's thread also, thus I know the mighty Brah-<br>    ma na.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000839">39</a></font></span>When Agni passed between the earth and heaven devouring with<br>    his flame the all-consumer,<br>   Where dwelt afar the spouses of one husband, where at that<br>    moment, where was Mātarisvan?<br>   -40. Into the floods had Mātarisvan entered, the deities had past in-<br>    to the waters.<br>   There stood the mighty measurer of the region: into the ver-<br>    dant plants went Pavamāna.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000841">41</a></font></span>Over the Gāyatri, above the immortal world he strode away.<br>   Those who by Song discovered Song—where did the Unborn see<br>    that thing?<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000842">42</a></font></span>Luller to rest, and gatherer-up of treasures, Savitar like a God<br>    whose laws are constant, hath stood like Indra in the war for<br>    riches.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000843">43</a></font></span>Men versed in sacred knowledge know that living Being that<br>    abides.<br>   In the nine-portalled Lotus Flower, enclosed with triple bands<br>    and bonds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1000844">44</a></font></span>Desireless, firm, immortal, self-existent, contented with the es-<br>    sence, lacking nothing,<br>   Free from the fear of Death is he who knoweth that Soul cou-<br>    rageous, youthful, undecaying.</p>
