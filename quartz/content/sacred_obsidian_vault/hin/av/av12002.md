 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="green">p. 82</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN II</h3>  <h4 align="center">A funeral hymn, and deprecation of Agni the Consumer of corpses</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200201">1</a></font></span>This is no place to hold thee; mount the Nāda: this lead is<br>    thine appointed share. Come hither.<br>   Together with Consumption in the cattle, Consumption in our<br>    men, go henee, go southward.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200202">2</a></font></span>With this we chase and banish all consumptive malady and<br>   Death,<br>   With sinner andamalicious man, with helper and with minister,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200203">3</a></font></span>Death and Misfortune we expel, Malignity we drive away.<br>   O Agni, thou who eatest not the corpse, eat him who hateth us:<br>    him whom we hate we send to thee. <a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="green">p. 83</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200204">4</a></font></span>If the corpse-eating Agni, or a tiger leaving his lair, hath entered<br>    this our homestead,<br>   With beans prepared in butter I expel him: far let him go to<br>    fires that lie in waters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200205">5</a></font></span>When, angered that a man hath died, we in our wrath have<br>    banished thee,<br>   That deed is easily set right through thee: we kindle thee again.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200206">6</a></font></span>Again have the Ādityas, Rudras, Vasus, the Brāhman, bringer<br>    of good things, O Agni,<br>   Again hath Brāhmanaspati disposed thee for long life lasting<br>    through a hundred autumns.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200207">7</a></font></span>I sweep afar, for sacrifice to Fathers, corpse-eating Agni who<br>    hath come among us,<br>   Although he saw this other, Jātavedas: in loftiest space let him<br>    inflame the caldron.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200208">8</a></font></span>I drive corpse-eating Agni to a distance: sin-laden let him go to<br>   Yamas vassals.<br>   Here let this other, Jātavedas, carry oblation to the Deities, fore-<br>    knowing.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200209">9</a></font></span>I quickly sweep away corpse-eating Agni, Death, with his bolt<br>    potdepriving men of motion.<br>   From household fire, well-knowing, I divide him: so in the<br>    world of Fathers be his portion.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200210">10</a></font></span>Corpse-eating Agni, toil-worn, meet for praises, I send away<br>    bypaths used by the Fathers.<br>   Stay there; keep watch among the Fathers: come not again to<br>    us by ways whereon Gods travel.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200211">11</a></font></span>They being cleansed and bright, the purifiers, kindle Sankasuka<br>    for our well-being.<br>   Impurity leaveth us and sin departeth: lighted by the good<br>    cleanser Agni cleanseth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200212">12</a></font></span>Agni the God, the Breaker-up, hath mounted to the heights of<br>    heaven.<br>   Released from all transgression, he hath from the curse delivered<br>    us.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200213">13</a></font></span>On Agni here, the Breaker-up, we wipe impurities away.<br>   Cleansed, fit for sacrifice have we become: may he prolong our<br>    lives.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200214">14</a></font></span>The Breaker-up, the Burster, the Destroyer, and the Silent One, <a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="green">p. 84</font></a><br>   These have expelled Consumption far, far off from thee ānd all<br>    thou hast,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200215">15</a></font></span>Corpse-eating Agni we expel, the Agni who bewilders men,<br>   Him who is in our horses, in our heroes, cows, and goats, and<br>    sheep:<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200216">16</a></font></span>We drive thee forth to other folk, to alien cattle, alien steeds,<br>   Thee the corpse-eating Agni, thee the Agni who bewilders men,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200217">17</a></font></span>Whereon the Deities, whereon men too have purified themselves,<br>   Exalting fatness, cleanse thyself, Agni, therein and mount to<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200218">18</a></font></span>O Agni, kindled and adored, turn not away to visit us.<br>   Shine brightly even there in heaven, so that we long may see the<br>   Sun.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200219">19</a></font></span>Wipe all away on lead and reed, on Agni, him who breaketh up,<br>   Then on a black-hued sheep, and on a cushion pain that racks.<br>    the head,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200220">20</a></font></span>Wipe off pollution, lay it in the lead and in the black-hued<br>    sheep,<br>   And headache in the cushion; then be cleansed and fit to sacri-<br>    fice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200221">21</a></font></span>Go onward, Death, pursue thy special pathway apart from that<br>    which Gods are wont to travel.<br>   To thee I say it who hast eyes and hearest: great grow the<br>    number of these men around us!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200222">22</a></font></span>Divided from the dead are these, the living: now is our calling<br>    on the Gods successful.<br>   We have gone forth for dancing and for laughter: may we with<br>    brave sons speak to the assembly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200223">23</a></font></span>Here I erect this rampart for the living: let none of these, none<br>    other, reach this limit.<br>   May they survive a hundred lengthened autumns, and may they<br>    bury Death beneath this mountain.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200224">24</a></font></span>Live your full lives and find age delightful, all of you striving,<br>    one behind the other.<br>   May Tvashtar, maker of fair things, be gracious, and lead your<br>    whole lives on to full existence.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200225">25</a></font></span>As the days follow days in close succession, as with the seasons<br>    duly come the seasons.<br>   As each successor fails not his foregoer, so constitute the lives of<br>    these, Ordainer! <a name="page_85"><font size="1" color="green">p. 85</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200226">26</a></font></span>Gather your strength, my friends; the stream flows stony:<br>    acquit yourselves as men, and cross the river.<br>   Abandon here the powers that were malignant, and let us cross<br>    to powers exempt from sickness.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200227">27</a></font></span>Rise up erect, cross over, my companions: the stream is stony<br>    that flows here before us.<br>   Abandon here the powers that were ungracious, and let us cross<br>    to powers benign and friendly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200228">28</a></font></span>Becoming pure and bright and purifying begin the Vaisvadevi<br>    strain for splendour..<br>   May we rejoice, o'erpassing troublous places, with all our heroes<br>    through a hundred winters.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200229">29</a></font></span>On pathways swept by wind, directed upward, passing beyond<br>    the lower, on the higher,<br>   Thrice seven times have the Rishis, the Departed, forced Mrityu<br>    backward with the fastened fetter.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200230">30</a></font></span>Effecting Mrityu's footstep ye came hither, to further times pro-<br>    longing your existence,<br>   Seated, drive Mrityu to his proper dwelling: then may we,<br>    living, speak to the assembly.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200231">31</a></font></span>Let these unwidowed dames with goodly husbands adorn them-<br>    selves with fragrant balm and unguent,<br>   Decked with fair jewels, tearless, sound and healthy, first let the<br>    dames go up to where he lieth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200232">32</a></font></span>I with oblation separate both classes, and with my prayer dis-<br>    part their several portions.<br>   I offer food that wastes not to the Fathers, a nd to these men<br>    give life of long duration.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200233">33</a></font></span>That Agni, Fathers! who, himself immortal, hath entered and<br>    possessed our mortal bosoms,<br>   Even him I grasp and hold the God with me, Let him not hate<br>    us, may we never hate him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200234">34</a></font></span>When ye have left the household fire, with the Corpse-eater<br>    southward go.<br>   Do that which is delightful to the Fathers, Brāhmans, and your-<br>    selves.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200235">35</a></font></span>Agni, the banqueter on flesh, not banished, for the eldest son<br>   Taketh a double share of wealth and spoileth it with poverty.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200236">36</a></font></span>What man acquires by plough, by war, all that he wins by toil<br>    of hand, <a name="page_86"><font size="1" color="green">p. 86</font></a><br>   He loses all if Agni the Carnivorous be not set aside,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200237">37</a></font></span>Unholy, splendour-reft is he, his sacrifice unfit to eat. Krayād<br>    deprives of tilth of cow, of riches him whom he pursues,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200238">38</a></font></span>Oft as a greedy beggar speaks the mortal who has brought<br>    distress,<br>   Those whom Carnivorous Agni close at hand runs after and<br>    detects.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200239">39</a></font></span>When a dame's husband dies the house is tangled <a href="errata.htm#0">fast</a> in<br>   Grāhi's net.<br>   A learned Brāhman must be sought to drive Carnivorous Agni,<br>    forth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200240">40</a></font></span>From any evil we have done, act of impurity or sin,<br>   Let waters purge me and from all that comes from Agni<br>   Breaker-up.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200241">41</a></font></span>By pathways travelled by the Gods these waters, well-knowing,<br>    from below have mounted upward.<br>   High on the summit of the raining mountain the ancient rivers<br>    fresh and new are flowing.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200242">42</a></font></span>Drive off Carnivorous Agni, thou Agni who eatest not the flesh;.<br>    carry oblation paid to Gods.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200243">43</a></font></span>The Flesh-eater hath entered him: he hath pursued the Flesh-<br>    eater.<br>   Making two tigers different-wise, I bear away the ungracious<br>    one.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200244">44</a></font></span>He who holds Gods within himself, the rampart and defence of<br>    men,<br>   Agni, the sacred household fire, hath come and stands between<br>    them both.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200245">45</a></font></span>Prolong the lives of those who live, O Agni, Let the dead go<br>    unto world of Fathers.<br>   As goodly household fire burn up Arāti; give this man dawn<br>    brighter than all the mornings.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200246">46</a></font></span>Subduing all our adversaries, Agni, give us their food, their<br>    strength and their possessions.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200247">47</a></font></span>Grasp ye this Indra, furtherer, satisfier: he will release you<br>    from disgrace and trouble.<br>   With him drive back the shaft that flies against you, with him.<br>    ward off the missile shot by Rudra.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200248">48</a></font></span>Seize with firm hold the Ox who boundeth forward: he will<br>    uplift you from disgrace and trouble. <a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="green">p. 87</font></a><br>   Enter this ship of Savitar; let us flee from poverty over all the<br>    six expenses.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200249">49</a></font></span>Thou followest the day and night, supporting, standing, at<br>    peace, promoting, rich in heroes.<br>   Long bearing undiseased and happy sleepers, be ours, O Bed,<br>    with smell of man about thee,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200250">50</a></font></span>They sever from the Gods, they live in sin and misery evermore,<br>   Those whom from very near at hand Carnivorous Agni casteth<br>    down as a horse tramples down the reeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200251">51</a></font></span>The faithless, who from lust of wealth abide with him who feeds<br>    on flesh,<br>   For ever set upon the fire an alien caldron, not their own.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200252">52</a></font></span>Forward in spirit would he fly, and often turns he back again,<br>   Whomso Carnivorous Agni from anear discovers and torments.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200253">53</a></font></span>Among tame beasts the black ewe is thy portion, and the bright<br>    lead is thine, they say, Flesh-eater!<br>   Mashed beans have been assigned thee for oblation go seek the<br>    dark wood and the wildernesses.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200254">54</a></font></span>I sought the rustling sugar-cane, white Seasamum, and cane and<br>    reed.<br>   I made this Indra's fuel, and the Fire of Yama I removed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1200255">55</a></font></span>Against the sinking western Sun I set them; each sundered path,<br>    knowing my way, I entered.<br>   I have warned off the ghosts of the Departed: to these I give<br>    the boon of long existence.</p>
