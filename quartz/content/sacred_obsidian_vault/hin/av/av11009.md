 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="green">p. 68</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IX</h3>  <h4 align="center">An incantation for the destruction of a hostile army</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100901">1</a></font></span>All arms and every arrow, all the power and might that bows<br>    possess,<br>   The warlike weapon, axes, swords, the plan and purpose in the<br>    heart,<br>   All this, O Arbudi, make thou visible to our enemies, and let<br>    them look on mist and fog.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100902">2</a></font></span>Arise ye and prepare yourselves: ye, the celestial hosts, are<br>    friends.<br>   Let your mysterious natures be seen by our friends O Arbudi.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100903">3</a></font></span>Rise both of you: begin your work with fettering and binding.<br>    fast,<br>   Assail, both of you, Arbudi, the armies of our enemies.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100904">4</a></font></span>The God whose name is Arbudi, and Nyarbudi the Mighty<br>   One,<br>   The two by whom the air and this great earth are compassed<br>    and possessed,<br>   With these two friends of Indra I go forth to conquer with the<br>    host.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100905">5</a></font></span>Rise with our army stand thou up. O Godlike Being, Arbudi.<br>   Breaking the hosts of enemies, surround them with thy winding<br>    coils.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100906">6</a></font></span>Exhibiting, O Arbudi, seven children of the mist and fog,<br>   When butter hath been offered, rise with all of these and with<br>    the host.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100907">7</a></font></span>Beating her breast, with tearful face, let the short-earned, the<br>    wild-haired hag. <a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="green">p. 69</font></a><br>   Shriek loudly when a man is slain, pierced through by thee, O<br>   Arbudi;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100908">8</a></font></span>Snatching away the vertebra, while with her thought she seeks<br>    her son,<br>   Her husband, brother, kin, when one, Arbudi! hath been pierc-<br>    ed by thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100909">9</a></font></span>Let vultures, ravens, kites, and crows, and every carrion-eating<br>    bird.<br>   Feast on our foes, and show where one, Arbudi! hath been<br>    pierced by thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100910">10</a></font></span>Then let each greedy beast of prey, and fly and worm regale<br>    itself<br>   Upon the human corpse where one, Arbudi, hath been pierced<br>    by thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100911">11</a></font></span>Attack them, both of you; bear off their vital breath O Nyar-<br>    budi.<br>   Let mingled shouts and echoing cries of woe amid our foemen<br>    show where thou, O Arbudi, hast pierced<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100912">12</a></font></span>Shake them, and let them sink with fear: e'erwhelm our enemies<br>    with dread.<br>   With widely-grasping bends of arm, O Arbudi, crush down our<br>    foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100913">13</a></font></span>Let those mens' arms grow faint and weak, dull be the purpose<br>    of their heart;<br>   And let not aught of them be left when thou, O Arbudi, hast<br>    pierced.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100914">14</a></font></span>Self-smiting, beating breast and thigh, careless of unguent, with<br>    their hair dishevelled, weeping,<br>   hags shall run together, when a man is slain, when thou, O<br>   Arbudi, hast pierced.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100915">15</a></font></span>Apsarases with dog-like mates, and Rūpakās, O Arbudi,<br>   And her who licks the cup inside, and seeks to wound in ill-<br>    kept place,<br>   All these, O Arbudi, do thou make visible to our enemies and<br>    let them look on mists and fog.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100916">16</a></font></span>The fiend who creeps upon the sword, maimed, dwelling where<br>   Lthe wounded lie,<br>   The misty shapes that lurk concealed, Gandharvas and Apsara-<br>    ses, demons, and snakes and Other Folk; <a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="green">p. 70</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100917">17</a></font></span>Armed with four fangs and yellow teeth, deformed, with faces<br>    smeared with blood, the terrible and fearless ones,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100918">18</a></font></span>Make thou, O Arbudi, those wings of hostile armies quake with<br>    dread.<br>   Let Conqueror and Victor, friends of Indra, overcome our foes.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100919">19</a></font></span>Stifled and crushed, O Nyarbudi, low let the smitten foeman lie.<br>   With tongue of fire and crest of smoke go conquering maidens<br>    with our host!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100920">20</a></font></span>May Indra, Lord of Might, strike down each bravest warrior of<br>    the foes,<br>   Whom this our band hath put to flight: let not one man of<br>    those escape.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100921">21</a></font></span>Let their hearts burst asunder, let their breath fly up and pass<br>    away.<br>   Let dryness of the mouth o'ertake our foemen, not the friendly<br>    ones.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100922">22</a></font></span>The clever and the foolish ones, those who are twisted round,<br>    the deaf,<br>   The dusky-hued, the hornless goats and those whose voice is like<br>    the buck's,<br>   All these, O Arbudi, do thou make visible to our enemies:<br>    cause them to look on mists and fog.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100923">23</a></font></span>Arbudi and Trishandhi fall upon our foes and scatter them,<br>   So that, O Indra, Lord of Might, Slayer of Vritra, we may kill<br>    thousands of these our enemies!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100924">24</a></font></span>Tall trees, and those who live in woods, the herbs and creeping<br>    plants of Earth,<br>   Gandharvas, and Apsarases, Snakes, <font size="-1" color="green">[       ]</font> Beings, Fathers,<br>   Gods,<br>   All these do thou, O Arbudi, make visible to our enemies:<br>    cause them to look on mists and fog.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100925">25</a></font></span>High sway have Maruts, and the God Āditya, Brāhmanaspati,<br>   High sway have Indra, Agni, and Dilator, Mitra, Prajāpati,<br>   High sway have Rishis given to you, showing upon our enemies<br>    where thou, O Arbudi, hast pierced.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1100926">26</a></font></span>With full dominion over these, rise, stand ye up, prepare your-<br>    selves,<br>   Ye are our friends, celestial hosts. When ye have won this<br>    battle, go, each to his several sphere, apart.</p>
