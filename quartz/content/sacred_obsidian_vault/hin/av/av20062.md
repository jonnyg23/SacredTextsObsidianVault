 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hymns of the Atharva Veda</i>, by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1895], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006205">5</a></font></span>To Indra sing a Sāman, sing to the high Sage a lofty song,<br>   To him who keeps the Law, inspired and fain for praise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006206">6</a></font></span>Thou, Indra, art preeminent: thou gavest splendour to the Sun.<br>   Maker of all things, thou art mighty and All-God. <a name="page_314"><font size="1" color="green">p. 314</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006207">7</a></font></span>Radiant with light thou wentest to the sky, the luminous realms:<br>    of heaven.<br>   The Gods, O Indra, strove to win thee for their friend.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006208">8</a></font></span>Sing forth to him whom many men invoke, to him whom many<br>    laud:<br>   Invite the potent Indra with your songs of praise;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006209">9</a></font></span>Whose lofty might—for doubly strong is he—supports the<br>    heaven and earth,<br>   And hills and plains and floods and light with manly power.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2006210">10</a></font></span>Such, praised by many! thou art King. Alone thou smitest foe-<br>    men dead,<br>   To gain, O Indra, spoils of war and high renown.</p>
