 <p><a name="page_331"><font size="1" color="green">p. 331</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXXXIV</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008401">1</a></font></span>O Indra marvellously bright, come, these libations long for thee,<br>   Thus by fine fingers purified.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008402">2</a></font></span>Urged by the holy singer, sped by song, come, Indra, to the<br>    prayers.<br>   Of the libation-pouring priest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2008403">3</a></font></span>Approach, O Indra, hasting thee, Lord of Bay Horses, to the<br>    prayers:<br>   Take pleasure in the juice we pour.</p>
