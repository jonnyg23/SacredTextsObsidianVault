 <p><a name="page_254"><font size="1" color="green">p. 254</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">A hymn to Kāma or Time</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905301">1</a></font></span>Prolific, thousand-eyed, and undecaying, a horse with seven<br>    reins Time bears us onward.<br>   Sages inspired with holy knowledge mount him: his chariot<br>    wheels are all the worlds of creatures.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905302">2</a></font></span>This Time hath seven rolling wheels and seven naves immorality<br>    is the chariot's axle.<br>   This Time brings hitherward all worlds about us: as primal<br>   Deity is he entreated.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905303">3</a></font></span>On Time is laid an overflowing beaker: this we behold in many<br>    a place appearing.<br>   He carries from us all these worlds of creatures. They call him<br>   Kāla in the loftiest heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905304">4</a></font></span>He only made the worlds of life, he only gathered the worlds<br>    of living things together.<br>   Their son did he become who was their Father: no other higher<br>    power than he existeth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905305">5</a></font></span>Kāla created yonder heaven, and Kāla made these realms of<br>    earth.<br>   By Kāla, stirred to motion, both what is and what shall be<br>    expand.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905306">6</a></font></span>Kāla created land; the Sun in Kāla hath his light and heat.<br>   In Kāla rest all things that be: in Kāla doth the eye discern.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905307">7</a></font></span>In Kāla mind, in Kāla breath, in Kāla name are fixt and joined.<br>   These living creatures, one and all, rejoice when Kāla hath<br>    approached.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905308">8</a></font></span>Kāla embraces Holy Fire, the Highest, Brahma in himself.<br>   Yea, Kāla, who was father of Prajāpati, is Lord of All.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905309">9</a></font></span>He made, he stirred this universe to motion, and on him it rests.<br>   He, Kāla, having now become Brahma, holds Parameshthin up. <a name="page_255"><font size="1" color="green">p. 255</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1905210">10</a></font></span>Kāla created living things and, first of all, Prajāpati.<br>   From Kāla self-made Kasyapa, from Kāla Holy Fire was born.</p>
