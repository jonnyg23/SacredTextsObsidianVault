 <p><a name="page_365"><font size="1" color="green">p. 365</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN CXXVIII</h3>  <h4 align="center">Sacrificial formulas</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012801">1</a></font></span>The worshipper who pours the juice, for gathering and assembly<br>    fit,<br>   And yonder foe-destroying Sun,—these have the Gods designed<br>    of old.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012802">2</a></font></span>He who defiles a sister, he who willingly would harm a friend,<br>   The fool who slights his elder, these, they say, must suffer down.<br>    below.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012803">3</a></font></span>Whenever any good man's son becometh bold and spirited,<br>   Then hath the wise Gandharva said this pleasant upward-point-<br>    ing word.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012804">4</a></font></span>The most unprofitable churl, the wealthy men who brings no.<br>    gift,<br>   These, verily, as we have heard, are cast away by all the wise.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012805">5</a></font></span>But they who have adored the Gods, and they who have best-<br>    owed their gifts,<br>   Those liberal lords are filled with wealth like Sūrya risen up to<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012806">6</a></font></span>With unanointed eyes and limbs, wearing no gem or ring of<br>    gold.<br>   No priest, no Brāhman's son is he: these things are ordered in<br>    the rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012807">7</a></font></span>With well-anointed limbs and eyes, wearing fair gem and golden,<br>    ring,<br>   Good priest is he, the Brāhman's son; these things are ordered<br>    in the rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012808">8</a></font></span>Pools with no place for drinking, and the wealthy man who.<br>    giveth naught,<br>   The pretty girl you may not touch, these things are ordered in:<br>    the rules. <a name="page_366"><font size="1" color="green">p. 366</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012809">9</a></font></span>Pools with good drinking places, and the wealthy man who<br>    freely gives,<br>   The pretty girl who may be touched, these things are ordered in<br>    the rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012810">10</a></font></span>The favourite wife neglected, and the man who safely shuns the<br>    fight,<br>   A sluggish horse whom none may guide, these things are order-<br>    ed in the rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012811">11</a></font></span>The favourite wife most dearly loved, the man who safely goes<br>    to war,<br>   The fleet steed who obeys the rein, these things are ordered in<br>    the rules.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012812">12</a></font></span>When, Indra, thou, as no man could, didst plunge into the Ten<br>   Kings' fight,<br>   That was a guard for every man: for he is formed to stay<br>    disease.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012813">13</a></font></span>Easily conquering Maghavan, thou, Hero, bentest Raji down,<br>   Rentest asunder Rauhina, calvest in pieces Vritra's head.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012814">14</a></font></span>Thou who didst separate the clouds and penetrate the water-<br>    floods,<br>   To thee, great slayer of the foe, be glory, Indra, yea, to thee!<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012815">15</a></font></span>They said to Auchchaihsravasa running as side-horse of the<br>   Bays,<br>   Safely to victory, O Steed, bear Indra with the beauteous wreath.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2012816">16</a></font></span>They yoke the white mares, on the Bay's right harness Auchchai-<br>    hsravasa.<br>   He joyeth as he carrieth Indra the foremost of the Gods.</p>
