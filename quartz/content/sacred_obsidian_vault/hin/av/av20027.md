 <p><a name="page_286"><font size="1" color="green">p. 286</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XXVII</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002701">1</a></font></span>If I, O Indra, were, like thee, the single sovran of all wealth.<br>   My worshipper should be rich in kine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002702">2</a></font></span>I should be fain, O Lord of Might, to strengthen and enrich the<br>    sage,<br>   Were I the lord of herds of kine,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002703">3</a></font></span>To worshippers who press the juice thy goodness, Indra, is a<br>    cow.<br>   Yielding in plenty kine and steeds.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002704">4</a></font></span>None is there, Indra, God or man, to hinder thy munificence,<br>   The wealth which, lauded, thou wilt give.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002705">5</a></font></span>The sacrifice made Indra strong when he unrolled the earth and<br>    made<br>   Himself a diaden in heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2002706">6</a></font></span>Thine aid we claim, O Indra, thine who after thou hast waxen<br>    great.<br>   Hast won all treasures for thine own.</p>
