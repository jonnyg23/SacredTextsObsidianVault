 <p><a name="page_124"><font size="1" color="green">p. 124</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HYMN IV</h3>  <h4 align="center">A glorification of the Sun as the only Deity</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300401">1</a></font></span>Down looking, on the ridge of sky Savitar goes to highest<br>    heaven.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300402">2</a></font></span>To misty cloud filled with his rays Mahendra goes encompassed<br>    round.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300403">3</a></font></span>Creator and Ordainer, he is Vāyu, he is lifted cloud.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300404">4</a></font></span>Rudra, and Mahādeva, he is Aryaman and Varuna.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300405">5</a></font></span>Agni is he, and Siirya, he is verily Mahāyama.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300406">6</a></font></span>Calves, joined, stand close beside him, ten in number, with one<br>    single head.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300407">7</a></font></span>From west to east they bend their way: when he mounts up he<br>    shines afar.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300408">8</a></font></span>His are these banded Maruts: they move gathered close like<br>    porters' thongs. <a name="page_125"><font size="1" color="green">p. 125</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300409">9</a></font></span>To misty cloud filled with his rays Mahendra goes encompassed<br>    round,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300410">10</a></font></span>His are the nine supports, the casks set in nine several places<br>    here.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300411">11</a></font></span>He keeppeth watch o'er creatures, all that breatheth and that<br>    breatheth not.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300412">12</a></font></span>This conquering might hath entered him, He is the sole the<br>    simple One, the One alone.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300413">13</a></font></span>In him these Deities become simple and One<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300414">14</a></font></span>Renown and glory, and force and cloud, the Brāhman's<br>    splendour, and food, and nourishment,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300415">15</a></font></span>To him who knoweth this God as simple and one.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300416">16</a></font></span>Neither second, nor third, nor yet fourth is he called;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300417">17</a></font></span>He is called neither fifth, nor sixth, nor yet seventh<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300418">18</a></font></span>He is called neither eighth, nor ninth, nor yet tenth.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300419">19</a></font></span>He watcheth over creatures, all that breatheth and that breatheth<br>    not.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300420">20</a></font></span>This conquering might hath entered him. He is the sole, the<br>    simple One, the One alone,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300421">21</a></font></span>In him these Deities become simple and One<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300422">22</a></font></span>Devotion and Religious Fervour, and renown and glory, and<br>    force and cloud, the Brāhman's splendour, and food and<br>    nourishment.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300423">23</a></font></span>And past and future, and Faith and lustre, and heaven and sweet<br>    oblation,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300424">24</a></font></span>To him who knoweth this God as simple and One.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300425">25</a></font></span>He, verily, is death, he is immortality, he is the monster, he is<br>    the fiend.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300426">26</a></font></span>He is Rudra, winner of wealth in the giving of wealth; in uttering<br>    homage he is the sacrificial exclamation Vashat duly employed.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300427">27</a></font></span>All sorcerers on earth obey with reverence his high behest.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300428">28</a></font></span>All constellations yonder, with the Moon, are subject to his will.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300429">29</a></font></span>He was brought forth from Day: and Day derives his origin.<br>    from him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300430">30</a></font></span>He was brought forth from Night: and Night derives her origins<br>    from him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300431">31</a></font></span>He was produced from Air: and Air derives its origin from him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300432">32</a></font></span>He was produced from Wind: and Wind derives his origin from.<br>    him. <a name="page_126"><font size="1" color="green">p. 126</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300433">33</a></font></span>From Heaven was he produced: and Heaven derives his origin<br>    from him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300434">34</a></font></span>He sprang from regions of the sky: from him the heavenly<br>    regions sprang.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300435">35</a></font></span>He is the offspring of the Earth: Earth hath her origin from<br>    him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300436">36</a></font></span>He was produced from fire: and fire derives its origin from<br>    him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300437">37</a></font></span>He is the waters' offspring: and from him the waters were<br>    produced.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300438">38</a></font></span>From holy verses was he born: from him the holy verses<br>    sprang.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300439">39</a></font></span>He is the son of sacrifice: and sacrifice was born from him.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300440">40</a></font></span>Sacrifice, sacrifice's Lord, he was made head of sacrifice.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300441">41</a></font></span>He thundereth, he lighteneth, he casteth down the thunder-stone<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300442">42</a></font></span>For misery or happiness, for mortal man or Asura.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300443">43</a></font></span>Whether thou formest growing plants, or sendest rain for<br>    happiness, or hast increased the race of man,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300444">44</a></font></span>Such is thy greatness, liberal Lord! A hundred bodily forms are<br>    thine.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300445">45</a></font></span>Millions are in thy million, or thou art a billion in thyself.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300446">46</a></font></span>Stronger than immortality is Indra: stronger thou than deaths;<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300447">47</a></font></span>Yea, stronger than Malignity art thou, O Indra, Lord of Might.<br>   Calling thee Master, Sovran Chief, we pay our reverence to<br>    thee.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300448">48</a></font></span>Worship to thee whom all behold! Regard me, thou whom all<br>    regard,<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300449">49</a></font></span>With food, and fame, and vigour, with the splendour of a<br>   Brāhman's rank<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300450">50</a></font></span>We pay thee reverence calling thee strength, power, and might,<br>    and conquering force.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300451">51</a></font></span>We pay thee reverence calling thee red power, the silvery<br>    expanse.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300452">52</a></font></span>We pay thee reverence calling thee vast, wide, the good, the<br>    universe.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300453">53</a></font></span>We pay thee reverence, calling thee extension, compass, width,<br>    and world.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300454">54</a></font></span>We pay thee reverence, calling thee rich, opulent in this and that,<br>    with wealth unceasing and secure <a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="green">p. 127</font></a><br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300455">55</a></font></span>Worship to thee whom all behold! Regard me, thou whom all<br>    regard.<br> <span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1300456">56</a></font></span>With food, and fame, and vigour, with the splendour of a<br>   Brāhman's rank.</p>
