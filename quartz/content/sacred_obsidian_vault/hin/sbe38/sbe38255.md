 <p>46. And on account of scriptural statement.</p>  
 <p>'Whatever blessing the priests pray for at the sacrifice, they pray for the good of the sacrificer; thus he said' (<i>S</i>at. Brâ. I, 3, 1, 26); 'Therefore an udgât<i>ri</i> who knows this may say: what wish shall I obtain for you by my singing?' (<i>Kh</i>. Up. I, 7, 8). These scriptural passages also declare that the fruit of meditations in which the priest is the agent goes to the sacrificer.--All this establishes the conclusion that the meditations on subordinate parts of the sacrifice are the work of the priest.</p>
