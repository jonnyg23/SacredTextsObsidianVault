 <p>30. And this is said in Sm<i>ri</i>ti also.</p>  
 <p>That in cases of need both he who knows and he who does not know may eat any food Sm<i>ri</i>ti also states; compare e.g. 'He who being in danger of his life eats food from anywhere is stained by sin no more than the lotus leaf by water.'--On the other hand, many passages teach that unlawful food is to be avoided. 'Intoxicating liquor the Brâhma<i>n</i>a must permanently forego;' 'Let them pour boiling spirits down the throat of the Brâhma<i>n</i>a who drinks spirits;' 'Spirit-drinking worms grow in the</p>  
 <p><a name="page_312"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 312</font></a></p>  
 <p>mouth of the spirit-drinking man, because he enjoys what is unlawful.'</p>
