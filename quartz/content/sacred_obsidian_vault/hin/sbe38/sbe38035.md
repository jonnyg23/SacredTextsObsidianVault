 <p>34. And on account of (the text) teaching its wandering about.</p>  
 <p>The quality of being an agent has to be attributed to the soul for that reason also, that, in a chapter treating of the soul, the text declares it to wander about in the state of sleep, 'The immortal one goes wherever he likes' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. IV, 3, 12);</p>  
 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  
 <p>and again, 'He moves about, according to his pleasure, within his own body' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. II, 1, 18).</p>
