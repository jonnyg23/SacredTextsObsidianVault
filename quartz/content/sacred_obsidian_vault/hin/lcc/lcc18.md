 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg" border="0"><br><i>The Little Clay Cart</i>, by Shudraka, tr. Arthur William Ryder, [1905], at sacred-texts.com</a></font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 177</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">A LIST OF PASSAGES</h3>  <h4 align="center">IN WHICH THE TRANSLATION DEPARTS FROM PARAB'S TEXT</h4>  
 <p>85.15: Here <i>nirmitāḥ</i> is apparently a mere misprint for <i>nirjitāḥ</i>.</p>  
 <p>45.11: The addition of <i>uṭṭhedha tti</i> seems almost necessary.</p>  
 <p>53.10; 54.9; 55.11; 62.7; 66.7: In these passages I have substituted "shampooer" for "gambler," to prevent confusion of the shampooer with the unnamed gambler.</p>  
 <p>57.18: I have added the stage-direction <i>dyūtakaramaṇḍatm kṛtvā</i>.</p>  
 <p>67.5: Read <i>kaṁ</i> for <i>kiṁ</i>.</p>  
 <p>72.9: Read <i>ajjo bandhuaṇaṁ samassāsiduṁ</i> for Parab's <i>ajja bandhuaṇo samassasadu</i>.</p>  
 <p>78.5: We should probably read <i>bīhacchaṁ</i> (<i>bībhatsam</i>) for <i>vīhatthaṁ</i>.</p>  
 <p>87.8: The words <i>cikitsāṁ kṛtvā</i> seem to be part of the text, not of the stage-direction.</p>  
 <p>97.18: I regard <i>nayasya</i> as one word, not two (<i>na yasya</i>).</p>  
 <p>100.12: Read <i>rakṣān</i> for <i>rakṣyān</i>.</p>  
 <p>114.5: Read <i>ṇaaraṇārī</i>- for <i>ṇaraṇārī</i>-.</p>  
 <p>125.8-11: These lines I have omitted.</p>  
 <p>126.4: Read <i>accharīa</i>- (<i>āçcarya</i>-) for <i>accharīdi</i>-.</p>  
 <p>170.8: Read <i>eka</i>- for <i>ekā</i>-.</p>  
 <p>178.11: Read <i>vaḍḍhamāṇao</i> for <i>vaḍḍhamāṇaa</i>.</p>  
 <p>184.9: Read <i>a</i> (<i>ca</i>) for <i>ka</i>.</p>  
 <p>217.15: Whatever <i>çavoḍiaṁ</i> may be, I have translated it in accordance with Lallādīkṣita's gloss, <i>saveṣṭikam</i>.</p>  
 <p>226.2: Apparently <i>khala</i>- is a misprint for <i>khaṅa</i>-.</p>  
 <p>238.10: Read -<i>ruciram</i> for -<i>racitam</i>.</p>  
 <p>259.16: Read <i>udvīkṣya</i> for <i>udvījya</i>.</p>  
 <p>262.4: Read -<i>bhājanam</i> for -<i>bhojanam</i>.</p>  
 <p>262.14: Read <i>paḍicchidaṁ</i> (<i>pratīṣṭam</i>) for <i>paḍicchiduṁ</i>.</p>  
 <p>265.6: Read <i>tvayā</i> for <i>mayā</i>.</p>  
 <p>284.14: The words <i>atha vā</i> plainly belong to the text, not to the stage-direction.</p>  
 <p>287.2: I take <i>paurāḥ</i> as part of the stage-direction.</p>  
 <p>288.8-292.9: This passage I have omitted: compare page <a href="lcc03.htm#page_xii">xii</a>.</p>    </body>
