 <p>39. But better than that is the other also on account of an inferential mark.</p>
 <p>Better than to be outside the â<i>s</i>ramas is the condition of standing within an â<i>s</i>rama. The latter state may be due to misfortune; but he who can should be within an â<i>s</i>rama, which state is the more holy and beneficial one. This follows from inference only, i.e. Sm<i>ri</i>ti; for Sm<i>ri</i>ti says, 'A Brâhma<i>n</i>a is to remain outside the â<i>s</i>ramas not even for one day.' For one who has passed beyond the stage of Brahma<i>k</i>arya, or whose wife has died, the impossibility to procure a wife constitutes the misfortune (which prevents him from belonging to an â<i>s</i>rama).--Here terminates the adhikara<i>n</i>a of 'widowers.'</p>
