 <p>37. And on account of the procedure, on the ascertainment of the non-being of that.</p>
 <p>That a <i>S</i>ûdra is not qualified for knowledge of Brahman appears from that fact also that as soon as Gautama has convinced himself that <i>G</i>âbâla, who wishes to become his pupil, is not a <i>S</i>ûdra, he proceeds to teach him the knowledge of Brahman.</p>
