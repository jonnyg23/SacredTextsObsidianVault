 <p>11. It is designated as having five functions like mind.</p>
 <p>As desire, and so on, are not principles different from mind, although they are different functions and produce different effects--according to the text, 'Desire, purpose, doubt, faith, want of faith, firmness, absence of firmness, shame, reflection, fear--all this is mind' (B<i>ri</i>. Up. I,,5, 3); so, on the ground of the text, 'prâ<i>n</i>a, apâna, vyâna, udâna, samâna--all this is prâ<i>n</i>a' (ibid.), apâna and the rest must be held to be different functions of prâ<i>n</i>a only, not independent principles.--Here terminates the adhikara<i>n</i>a of what is 'a modification of air.'</p>
