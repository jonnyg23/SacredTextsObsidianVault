 <p>34. Nor also is there non-contradiction from paryâya; on account of change, and so on.</p>
 <p>Nor is the difficulty to be evaded by the assumption of the soul assuming a different condition through contraction or dilatation. For this would imply that the soul is subject</p>
 <p><a name="page_520"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 520</font></a></p>
 <p>to change, and all the imperfections springing from it, viz. non-permanence, and so on, and hence would not be superior to non-sentient things such as jars and the like.</p>
