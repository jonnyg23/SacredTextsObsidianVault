 <p>48. On account of the others also being taught, in the same way as the condition of the Muni.</p>
 <p>The injunction, on him who has passed beyond all desire, of mauna preceded by pârivrâ<i>g</i>ya (wandering about as a mendicant), is meant to illustrate the duties of all â<i>s</i>ramas . For the duties of the other â<i>s</i>ramas are taught by Scripture no less than those of the Muni (and the householder). Similarly it was shown above that in the text 'There are three branches of sacred duty--he who is founded on Brahman goes to immortality,' the term 'founded on Brahman' applies equally to members of all â<i>s</i>ramas .--It therefore remains a settled conclusion that</p>
 <p><a name="page_712"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 712</font></a></p>
 <p>the text under discussion enjoins pâ<i>nd</i>itya, bâlya, and mauna as being auxiliaries to knowledge in the same way as the other duties of the â<i>s</i>ramas, such as sacrifices and the rest.--Here terminates the adhikara<i>n</i>a of 'the injunction of other auxiliaries.'</p>
