 <p>29. And on account of non-sublation.</p>
 <p>The conclusion above arrived at is confirmed by the consideration that thus only those texts are not stultified</p>
 <p><a name="page_702"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 702</font></a></p>
 <p>which enjoin, for those who know Brahman, purity in matters of food with a view to the origination of knowledge of Brahman. Cp.' when the food is pure the mind becomes pure' (<i>Kh</i>. Up. VII, 26, 2).</p>
