 <p>29. The existence (is) not, on account of the absence of perception.</p>
 <p>The existence of mere cognitions devoid of corresponding things is not possible, because such are nowhere perceived. For we nowhere perceive cognitions not inherent in a cognising subject and not referring to objects. That even dream-cognitions are not devoid of real matter we have explained in the discussion of the different khyâtis (above, <a href="sbe48028.htm#page_118">p. 118</a>).--Here terminates the adhikara<i>n</i>a of '<i>perception</i>.'</p>
