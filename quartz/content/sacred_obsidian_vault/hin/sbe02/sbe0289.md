   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXV.</h3>  
 <p><a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 294</font></a></p>  
 <p>1. Now they say: 'How many (gods) does a student enter who violates the vow of chastity?' <a name="fr_1452"></a><a href="#fn_1451"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (And they answer): 'His vital spirits (go to) the Maruts (winds), his strength to Indra, his eminence in sacred learning to B<i>ri</i>haspati, all the remaining parts to Agni.' <a name="fr_1453"></a><a href="#fn_1452"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. He kindles the fire in the night of the new moon, and offers, by way of penance, two oblations of clarified butter, <a name="fr_1454"></a><a href="#fn_1453"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. (Reciting these two sacred texts), 'Defiled by lust am I, defiled am I, oh Lust; to Lust svâhâ;' 'Injured by lust am I, injured am I, oh Lust; to Lust svâhâ.' (Next) he (silently) places one piece of sacred fuel (on the fire), sprinkles water round the fire, offers the Ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avâstu (oblation), and approaching (the fire) worships it, thrice (reciting the text), 'May the waters sprinkle me.' <a name="fr_1455"></a><a href="#fn_1454"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. These worlds are three; in order to conquer</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_295"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 295</font></a></p>  
 <p>these worlds, in order to gain mastership over these worlds, (this rite must be performed.)</p>  
 <p>6. According to some, the above (described) rite is a penance (for all hidden offences) in general, (and they say) regarding it, 'He who may be impure, as it were, shall offer burnt-oblations in this manner, and shall recite sacred texts in this manner; the fee (of the officiating priest shall be) whatever he may choose.'</p>  
 <p>7. He who has been guilty of cheating, of calumniating, of acting contrary to the rule of conduct, of eating or drinking things forbidden, of connection with a woman of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste, of an unnatural crime, and even of performing magic rites with intent (to harm his enemies), shall bathe and sprinkle himself with water, reciting the texts addressed to the Waters, or those addressed to Varu<i>n</i>a, or other purificatory texts. <a name="fr_1456"></a><a href="#fn_1455"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. For offences committed by speaking or thinking of forbidden things, the five Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis (must be recited). <a name="fr_1457"></a><a href="#fn_1456"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Or for all (offences) he may sip water, (reciting) in the morning (the text), 'May the day and the sun purify me;' and in the evening, 'The night and Varu<i>n</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>10. Or he may offer eight pieces of sacred fuel, <a name="fr_1458"></a><a href="#fn_1457"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 296</font></a></p>  
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> (reciting the texts beginning) 'Devak<i>ri</i>tasya.' By merely offering them he becomes free from all sin.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1451"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1452">294:1</a> <b>XXV</b>. For this and the following five Sûtras, see Taittirîya Âra<i>n</i>yaka II, 18, 1 seq.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1452"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1453">294:2</a> 'All the remaining parts, i.e. his sight and the other organs of sense, go to Agni. Thus a student who has broken the vow of chastity becomes short-lived, weak, destitute of eminence in sacred learning, and destitute of sight, and so forth. Therefore a penance must be performed.'--Haradatta. It must, of course, be understood that the penance prescribed here, is a 'secret penance.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1453"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1454">294:3</a> 'He, i.e. the unchaste student, shall kindle the fire in the night of the new moon, i.e. at midnight, in the manner declared in the G<i>ri</i>hya-sûtra.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1454"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1455">294:4</a> Haradatta says that while sprinkling water the performer shall recite the texts 'Aditi, thou hast permitted,' see Âpastamba II, 2, 3, 17 note. The Ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avâstu oblation, which follows after the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t offering, is described Gobhila G<i>ri</i>hya-sûtra I, 8, 26-29.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1455"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1456">295:7</a> Âpastamba I, 9, 26, 7. The verses addressed to the Waters are, Rv. X, 9, 1-3 = Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. IV, 1, 5, 1, and Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. V, 6, 1. Regarding those addressed to Varu<i>n</i>a, see above, XXIII, 28. As an instance of 'other purificatory texts' Haradatta quotes Taittirîya-brâhma<i>n</i>a I, 4, 81, 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1456"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1457">295:8</a> Regarding the five Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis, see above, I, 51.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1457"></a><a href="sbe0289.htm#fr_1458">295:10</a> Haradatta gives the following four Mantras: Devak<i>ri</i>tasyainasovaya<i>g</i>anam asi svâhâ, 'thou art the expiation for sin committed <a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 296</font></a> by the gods,' svâhâ pit<i>ri</i>k<i>ri</i>tasyainaso . . . svâhâ, manushyak<i>ri</i>tasyainaso . . . svâhâ, asmatk<i>ri</i>tasyainaso . . . svâhâ. But see Vâ<i>g</i>asaneyi-Sa<i>m</i>hitâ VIII, 13, where eight Mantras are given, and below, XXVII, 7.</p>
