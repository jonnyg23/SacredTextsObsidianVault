 <p><a name="page_246"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 246</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XIII.</h3>  
 <p>1. In disputed cases the truth shall be established by means of witnesses. <a name="fr_1194"></a><a href="#fn_1193"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. The (latter) shall be many, faultless as regards the performance of their duties, worthy to be trusted by the king, and free from affection for, or hatred against either (party). <a name="fr_1195"></a><a href="#fn_1194"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. (They may be) <i>S</i>ûdras even. <a name="fr_1196"></a><a href="#fn_1195"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. But a Brâhma<i>n</i>a must not be forced (to give evidence) at the word of a non-Brâhma<i>n</i>a, except if he is mentioned (in the plaint). <a name="fr_1197"></a><a href="#fn_1196"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. (Witnesses) shall not speak singly or without being asked, <a name="fr_1198"></a><a href="#fn_1197"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. And if, (being asked,) they do not answer, they are guilty of a crime. <a name="fr_1199"></a><a href="#fn_1198"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Heaven is their reward, if they speak the <a name="fr_1200"></a><a href="#fn_1199"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_247"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 247</font></a></p>  
 <p>truth; in the contrary case hell (will be their portion).</p>  
 <p>8. (Persons) not mentioned (in the plaint), must also give evidence.</p>  
 <p>9. No objection (can be raised against witnesses) in a case of (criminal) hurt, <a name="fr_1201"></a><a href="#fn_1200"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Nor if they have spoken inadvertently. <a name="fr_1202"></a><a href="#fn_1201"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. If the sacred law or the rules (referring to worldly matters) are violated,. the guilt (falls) on the witnesses, the assessors, the king, and on the offender. <a name="fr_1203"></a><a href="#fn_1202"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Some (declare, that the witnesses) shall be charged on oath to speak the truth. <a name="fr_1204"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. In the case of others than Brâhma<i>n</i>as that (oath shall be sworn) in the presence of the gods, of the king, and of Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>  
 <p>14. By false evidence concerning small cattle a witness kills ten, <a name="fr_1205"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. (By false evidence) regarding cows, horses, men, or land, in each succeeding case ten times as many (as in the one mentioned before),}</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_248"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 248</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. Or (by false evidence) regarding land the whole (human race).</p>  
 <p>17. Hell (is the punishment) for a theft of land.</p>  
 <p>18. (By false evidence) concerning water (he incurs) the same (guilt) as (for an untruth) about land,</p>  
 <p>19. Likewise (by false evidence) regarding (criminal) intercourse.</p>  
 <p>20. (By false evidence) regarding honey or clarified butter (he incurs) the same (guilt) as (by an untruth) about small cattle,</p>  
 <p>21. (By false evidence) about clothes, gold, grain, and the Veda, the same as (by an untruth) about kine,</p>  
 <p>22. (And by false evidence) regarding a carriage (or a beast of burden) the same as (by an untruth) about horses.</p>  
 <p>23. A witness must be reprimanded and punished for speaking an untruth. <a name="fr_1206"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. No guilt is incurred by giving false evidence, in case the life (of a man) depends thereon. <a name="fr_1207"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. But (this. rule does) not (hold good) if the life of a very wicked (man depends on the evidence of a witness).</p>  
 <p>26. The king, or the judge, or a Brâhma<i>n</i>a learned in the <i>S</i>âstras (shall examine the witnesses). <a name="fr_1208"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. (The litigant) shall humbly go to seek the judge. <a name="fr_1209"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_249"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 249</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. If (the defendant) is unable to answer (the plaint) at once, (the judge) may wait for a year. <a name="fr_1210"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. But (in an action) concerning kine, draught oxen, women, or the procreation (of offspring), the defendant (shall answer) immediately, <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. Likewise in a case that will suffer by delay.</p>  
 <p>31. To speak the truth before the judge is more important than all (other) duties.</p>
