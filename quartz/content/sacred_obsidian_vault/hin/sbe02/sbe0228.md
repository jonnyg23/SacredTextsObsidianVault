 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, PA<i>T</i>ALA 9, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 25.</h3>  
 <p>1. He who has had connection with a Guru's wife shall cut off his organ together with the testicles, take them into his joined hands and walk towards the south without stopping, until he falls down dead. <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_465"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Or he may die embracing a heated metal image of a woman. <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. A drinker of spirituous liquor shall drink exceedingly hot liquor so that he dies. <a name="fr_468"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. A thief shall go to the king with flying hair, carrying a club on his shoulder, and tell him his deed. He (the king) shall give him a blow with that (club). If the thief dies, his sin is expiated. <a name="fr_469"></a><a href="#fn_468"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. If he is forgiven (by the king), the guilt falls upon him who forgives him, <a name="fr_470"></a><a href="#fn_469"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Or he may throw himself into the fire, or perform repeatedly severe austerities, <a name="fr_471"></a><a href="#fn_470"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Or he may kill himself by diminishing daily his portion of food,</p>  
 <p>8. Or he may perform K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penances (uninterruptedly) for one year. <a name="fr_472"></a><a href="#fn_471"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

 <p>9. Now they quote also (the following verse): <a name="fr_473"></a><a href="#fn_472"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Those who have committed a theft (of gold), drunk spirituous liquor, or had connection with a Guru's wife, but not those who have slain a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, shall eat every fourth meal-time a little food, bathe at the times of the three libations (morning, noon, and evening), passing the day standing and the night sitting. After the lapse of three years they throw off their guilt.</p>  
 <p>11. (A man of any caste) excepting the first, who has slain a man of the first caste, shall go on a battle-field and place himself (between the two hostile armies). There they shall kill him (and thereby he becomes pure). <a name="fr_474"></a><a href="#fn_473"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Or such a sinner may tear from his body and make the priest offer as a burnt-offering his hair, skin, flesh, and the rest, and then throw himself into the fire. <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="#fn_474"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. If a crow, a chameleon, a peacock, a Brâhma<i>n</i>î duck, a swan, the vulture called Bhâsa, a frog, an ichneumon, a musk-rat, or a dog has been killed, then the same penance as for a <i>S</i>ûdra must be performed. <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_475"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>
