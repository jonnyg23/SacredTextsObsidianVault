   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IX.</h3>  
 <p>1. Such (a man) shall bathe, after (having fulfilled) the, law (regarding studentship), take unto him a wife, and, fulfilling the duties of a householder which have been declared above, in addition obey the following ordinances <a name="fr_1031"></a><a href="#fn_1030"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (He shall be) always pure (and) sweet-smelling (and) bathe frequently. <a name="fr_1032"></a><a href="#fn_1031"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. If he possesses wealth, he shall not be dressed in old or dirty clothes; <a name="fr_1033"></a><a href="#fn_1032"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Nor shall he wear dyed or sumptuous garments, nor such as have been worn (before) by others,</p>  
 <p>5. Nor a garland and shoes (that have been worn by others). <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. (He may wear a cast-off garment) which has been washed, if he is unable (to afford a new one). <a name="fr_1035"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. He shall not allow his beard to grow without a (sufficient) reason. <a name="fr_1036"></a><a href="#fn_1035"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p></p>  
 <p>8. He shall not carry water and fire at the same time. <a name="fr_1037"></a><a href="#fn_1036"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. He shall not drink out of his joined hands. <a name="fr_1038"></a><a href="#fn_1037"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. He shall not sip water standing, nor (shall he sip) water drawn up (from a well), <a name="fr_1039"></a><a href="#fn_1038"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Nor (water) that is offered by a <i>S</i>ûdra or an impure man, or that has been taken up with one hand. <a name="fr_1040"></a><a href="#fn_1039"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Facing or within sight of wind, fire, Brâhma<i>n</i>as, the sun, water, (images of the) gods, and cows he shall not eject urine or fæces or other impurities. <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1040"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. He shall not stretch out his feet towards those divine beings. <a name="fr_1042"></a><a href="#fn_1041"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. He shall not remove urine or fæces with leaves, clods of earth, or stones. <a name="fr_1043"></a><a href="#fn_1042"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. He shall not stand upon ashes, hair, nail (parings), husks (of grain), pot-sherds, or impure substances. <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. He shall not converse with barbarians, impure or wicked men. <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 221</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. If he has conversed (with such persons), he shall meditate on virtuous (men),</p>  
 <p>18. Or he may speak with a Brâhma<i>n</i>a. <a name="fr_1046"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. He shall call (a cow that is) not a milch-cow a cow that will become a milch-cow. <a name="fr_1047"></a><a href="#fn_1046"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. (An event) that is not lucky (he shall call) lucky.</p>  
 <p>21. (In speaking of) a skull (he shall use the word) bhagâla instead of kapâla,</p>  
 <p>22. (And in speaking of) a rainbow, ma<i>n</i>idhanus (the jewelled bow) instead of indradhanus, (Indra's bow). <a name="fr_1048"></a><a href="#fn_1047"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Let him not announce it to others, if a cow suckles (her calf), <a name="fr_1049"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. Nor let him prevent her (from doing it). <a name="fr_1050"></a><a href="#fn_1049"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. After conjugal intercourse he shall at once clean himself <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. Let him not recite the daily portion of the Veda (lying) on that couch (on which he lies with his wife). <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 222</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. And when he has studied during the third watch of the night, he shall not again retire to rest. <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1052"><font size="1">27</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. Let him not have intercourse with his wife when she is ill,</p>  
 <p>29. Nor during her courses; <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1053"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. Nor let him embrace her (during that period),</p>  
 <p>31. Nor an unmarried female.</p>  
 <p>32. He shall avoid to blow the fire with his mouth, to contend with words, to show himself covered with perfumed ointments or wearing garlands, to scratch himself with any impure (implement), to take his meals with his wife, to look at (a woman) who is anointing herself, to enter (his village) by a back-gate, to wash one foot with the other, to eat food deposited on a chair, to cross a river swimming, to ascend trees and dangerous (places), or to descend therefrom, and to imperil his life (in any other manner). <a name="fr_1055"></a><a href="#fn_1054"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. Let him not ascend a ship (of) doubtful (solidity). <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1055"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. He shall protect himself by all (possible) means.</p>  
 <p>35. In the day-time he shall not wrap up his head while walking about; <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1056"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. But at night he shall cover it,</p>  
 <p>37. And while voiding urine and fæces.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. (Let him) not (ease nature) without (first) covering the ground (with grass or the like), <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1057"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. Nor close to his dwelling, <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1058"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. Nor on ashes, on cow-dung, in a ploughed field, in the shade (of a tree), on a road, in beautiful (spots). <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">40</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. Let him eject both urine and fæces, facing the north in the day-time, <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. And in the twilight,</p>  
 <p>43. But at night, facing the south. <a name="fr_1062"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">43</font></a></p>  
 <p>44. Let him avoid to use a seat, clogs, a stick for cleaning the teeth (and other implements) made of Palâ<i>s</i>a-wood. <a name="fr_1063"></a><a href="#fn_1062"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. With shoes on (his feet), he shall not eat, sit down, salute, or worship (the gods). <a name="fr_1064"></a><a href="#fn_1063"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. Let him not pass idly (any part of the day, be it) morning, midday, or evening; (but) according to his ability (he shall make each useful) by the acquisition of spiritual merit or of wealth, and by taking his pleasure. <a name="fr_1065"></a><a href="#fn_1064"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. But among those (three aims of human life) he shall chiefly attend to the acquisition of spiritual merit. <a name="fr_1066"></a><a href="#fn_1065"><font size="1">47</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_224"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 224</font></a></p>  
 <p>48. Let him not look at a naked woman wedded to another man. <a name="fr_1067"></a><a href="#fn_1066"><font size="1">48</font></a></p>  
 <p>49. Let him not draw a seat towards himself with his foot.</p>  
 <p>50. He shall keep his organ, his stomach, his hands, his feet, his tongue, and his eyes under due restraint. <a name="fr_1068"></a><a href="#fn_1067"><font size="1">50</font></a></p>  
 <p>51. Let him avoid to cut, to break, to scratch, and to crush (anything), or to make (his joints) crack, without a (sufficient) reason. <a name="fr_1069"></a><a href="#fn_1068"><font size="1">51</font></a></p>  
 <p>52. Let him not step over a rope (to which) a calf (is tied). <a name="fr_1070"></a><a href="#fn_1069"><font size="1">52</font></a></p>  
 <p>53. Let him not be a stay-at-home.</p>  
 <p>54. Let him not go to (perform) a sacrifice without being chosen (to officiate as priest).</p>  
 <p>55. But at his pleasure (he may go) to see it.</p>  
 <p>56. Let him not eat food (that he has placed) in his lap, <a name="fr_1071"></a><a href="#fn_1070"><font size="1">56</font></a></p>  
 <p>57. Nor what has been brought at night by a servant. <a name="fr_1072"></a><a href="#fn_1071"><font size="1">57</font></a></p>  
 <p>58. He shall not eat (substances) from which the fat has been extracted, Such as milk from which the cream has separated, butter, oil-cake, buttermilk, and the like. <a name="fr_1073"></a><a href="#fn_1072"><font size="1">58</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_225"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 225</font></a></p>  
 <p>59. But he shall take his meals in the morning and in the evening, blessing his food, not grumbling at it. <a name="fr_1074"></a><a href="#fn_1073"><font size="1">59</font></a></p>  
 <p>60. He shall never sleep naked at night; <a name="fr_1075"></a><a href="#fn_1074"><font size="1">60</font></a></p>  
 <p>61. Nor shall he bathe (naked); <a name="fr_1076"></a><a href="#fn_1075"><font size="1">61</font></a></p>  
 <p>62. And he shall perform whatever (else) aged (Brâhma<i>n</i>as), of subdued senses, who have been properly obedient (to their teachers), who are free from deceit, covetousness, and error, and who know the Vedas, declare (to be right). <a name="fr_1077"></a><a href="#fn_1076"><font size="1">62</font></a></p>  
 <p>63. In order to acquire wealth and for the sake of security he may go to a ruling (king), <a name="fr_1078"></a><a href="#fn_1077"><font size="1">63</font></a></p>  
 <p>64. (But) to no other (being) except the gods, his Gurus, and righteous (Brâhma<i>n</i>as).</p>  
 <p>65. He shall seek to dwell in a place where firewood, water, fodder, Ku<i>s</i>a grass, (materials for making) garlands and roads exist in abundance, which is chiefly inhabited by Âryans, which is rich in industrious (men), and which is governed by a righteous (ruler). <a name="fr_1079"></a><a href="#fn_1078"><font size="1">65</font></a></p>  
 <p>66. He shall pass excellent (beings and things), <a name="fr_1080"></a><a href="#fn_1079"><font size="1">66</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_226"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 226</font></a></p>  
 <p>auspicious (objects), temples of the gods, crossroads, and the like with his right turned towards them.</p>  
 <p>67. The rule for times of distress (is, that) he shall mentally perform all (that is required by the rule of) conduct. <a name="fr_1081"></a><a href="#fn_1080"><font size="1">67</font></a></p>  
 <p>68. He shall always speak the truth. <a name="fr_1082"></a><a href="#fn_1081"><font size="1">68</font></a></p>  
 <p>69. He shall conduct himself (as becomes) an Âryan.</p>  
 <p>70. He shall instruct virtuous (men only). <a name="fr_1083"></a><a href="#fn_1082"><font size="1">70</font></a></p>  
 <p>71. He shall follow the rules of purification taught (in the <i>S</i>âstras). <a name="fr_1084"></a><a href="#fn_1083"><font size="1">71</font></a></p>  
 <p>72. He shall take pleasure in the (study of the) Veda. <a name="fr_1085"></a><a href="#fn_1084"><font size="1">72</font></a></p>  
 <p>73. He shall never hurt (any being), he shall be gentle, (yet) firm, ever restrain his senses, and be liberal. <a name="fr_1086"></a><a href="#fn_1085"><font size="1">73</font></a></p>  
 <p>74. A Snâtaka who conducts himself in this manner will liberate his parents, his ancestors, and descendants from evil, and never fall from Brahman's heaven. <a name="fr_1087"></a><a href="#fn_1086"><font size="1">74</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="fn_1030"></a><a href="sbe0273.htm#fr_1031">218:1</a> <b>IX</b>. Âpastamba I, 11, 30. 1-4. Haradatta says that the expression sa, 'such (a man),' refers to the king, and to the Brâhma<i>n</i>a deeply versed in the Vedas, who have been described in the preceding chapter. My MSS. insert between this and the following one another Sûtra, which has been left out in Professor Stenzler's edition. It seems to me that it is absolutely required, and I therefore insert it here, together with Haradatta's comment, according to my best copy, P.</p>  
 <p>Gautama: '(And) a Snâtaka (i.e. a person who has completed his studentship, but has not yet taken a wife, shall act thus).' Haradatta: 'It must be understood that the word "and" has been left <a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a> out. (The meaning is): "And a Snâtaka shall obey the following ordinances." If this Sûtra were not given, those ordinances would have to be obeyed after marriage only; and if the preceding Sûtra (1) had not been given, before marriage only, because the term Snâtaka is usually employed in that (sense) only. For this reason both (Sûtras) have been given. Hence, though a man may not enter another order, he shall, after taking the bath (on completion of his studentship), obey these ordinances during his whole life. As here (Sûtra 1) the word sa, "such a man," is used, a Kshatriya and a Brâhma<i>n</i>a only must necessarily obey the rules prescribed for a Snâtaka and perform a penance for breaking them; and the penance for breaking the rules prescribed for a Snâtaka is fasting. This is (the object of the insertion of the word sa, "such (a man)." But, if a Vai<i>s</i>ya follows them, (his reward will be) prosperity; if he breaks them, he need not perform a penance. With respect to this matter another Sm<i>ri</i>ti says: "The penance which is prescribed for a breach of the Snâtaka laws, must be performed by a Kshatriya and a Brâhma<i>n</i>a alone, never by (men of) the other (caste)."</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1076"></a><a href="sbe0273.htm#fr_1077">225:62</a> Âpastamba I, 11, 32, 29; I, 7, 20, 8. Haradatta adds that the plural is used in the above Sûtra in order to indicate that many Brâhma<i>n</i>as must be unanimous regarding the practices to be followed.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1077"></a><a href="sbe0273.htm#fr_1078">225:63</a> Manu IV, 33; X, 113. 'For the sake of these objects he may go to a ruler, i.e. a king without cringing, because the preposition adhi is used (in the text, and) adhi denotes mastership' (Pâ<i>n</i>ini I, 4, 97). The meaning that he shall go (as becomes) an independent man.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1078"></a><a href="sbe0273.htm#fr_1079">225:65</a> Âpastamba I, 5, 15, 22; I, 11, 32, 18. Âryans i.e. Brâhma<i>n</i>as, Kshatriyas, and Vai<i>s</i>yas:</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1079"></a><a href="sbe0273.htm#fr_1080">225:66</a> Manu IV, 39.
