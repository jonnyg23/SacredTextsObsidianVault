   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXII.</h3>  
 <p>1. (Now follows the description of the) penances. <a name="fr_1391"></a><a href="#fn_1390"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. He who has (intentionally) slain a Brâhma<i>n</i>a shall emaciate himself, and thrice throw himself into a fire, <a name="fr_1392"></a><a href="#fn_1391"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Or he may become in battle a target for armed men, <a name="fr_1393"></a><a href="#fn_1392"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Or, remaining chaste, he may, during twelve years, enter the village (only) for the purpose of begging, carrying the foot of a bedstead and a skull in his hand and proclaiming his deed. <a name="fr_1394"></a><a href="#fn_1393"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. If be meets an Ârya, he shall step out of the road. <a name="fr_1395"></a><a href="#fn_1394"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_284"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 284</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Standing by day, sitting at night, and bathing in the morning, at noon, and in the evening, he may be purified (after twelve years), <a name="fr_1396"></a><a href="#fn_1395"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. Or by saving the life of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, <a name="fr_1397"></a><a href="#fn_1396"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. Or if he is, at least, thrice vanquished in (trying to recover) the property (of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a) stolen (by robbers), <a name="fr_1398"></a><a href="#fn_1397"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Or by bathing (with the priests) at (the end of) a horse-sacrifice, <a name="fr_1399"></a><a href="#fn_1398"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Or at (the end of) any other (Vedic) sacrifice, provided that an Agnish<i>t</i>ut (sacrifice) forms part of it. <a name="fr_1400"></a><a href="#fn_1399"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. (The same penances must be performed) even if he has attempted the life of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, but failed to kill him, <a name="fr_1401"></a><a href="#fn_1400"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Likewise if he has killed a female (of the Brâhma<i>n</i>a caste) who had bathed after temporary uncleanness, <a name="fr_1402"></a><a href="#fn_1401"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Also for (destroying) the embryo of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, though (its sex) may be not distinguishable. <a name="fr_1403"></a><a href="#fn_1402"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. For (intentionally) killing a Kshatriya the normal vow of continence (must be kept) for six <a name="fr_1404"></a><a href="#fn_1403"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 285</font></a></p>  
 <p>years; and he shall give one thousand cows and one bull.</p>  
 <p>15. For (killing) a Vai<i>s</i>ya (the same penance must be performed) during three years; and he shall give one hundred cows and one bull. <a name="fr_1405"></a><a href="#fn_1404"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. For (killing) a <i>S</i>ûdra (the same penance must be performed) during one year; and he shall give ten cows and one bull. <a name="fr_1406"></a><a href="#fn_1405"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. And the same (rule applies) if a female (has been killed) who was not in the condition (described in Sûtra 12). <a name="fr_1407"></a><a href="#fn_1406"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. (The penance for killing) a cow is the same as for (the murder of) a Vai<i>s</i>ya, <a name="fr_1408"></a><a href="#fn_1407"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. And for injuring a frog, an ichneumon, a crow, a chameleon, a musk-rat, a mouse, and a dog, <a name="fr_1409"></a><a href="#fn_1408"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. And for killing one thousand (small animals) that have bones, <a name="fr_1410"></a><a href="#fn_1409"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. Also for (killing) an ox-load of (animals) that have no bones; <a name="fr_1411"></a><a href="#fn_1410"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p>

 <p><a name="page_286"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 286</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. Or he may also give something for (the destruction of) each animal that has bones. <a name="fr_1412"></a><a href="#fn_1411"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. For (killing) a eunuch (he shall give) a load of straw and a mâsha of lead; <a name="fr_1413"></a><a href="#fn_1412"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. For (killing) a boar, a pot of clarified butter; <a name="fr_1414"></a><a href="#fn_1413"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. For (killing) a snake, a bar of iron; <a name="fr_1415"></a><a href="#fn_1414"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. For (killing) an unchaste woman, who is merely in name a Brâhma<i>n</i>î, a leather bag; <a name="fr_1416"></a><a href="#fn_1415"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. (For killing a woman who subsists) by harlotry, nothing at all.</p>  
 <p>28. For preventing that (a Brâhma<i>n</i>a) obtains a wife, food, or money, (he must) in each case (remain chaste) during a year,</p>  
 <p>29. For adultery two years, <a name="fr_1417"></a><a href="#fn_1416"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. (For adultery with the wife) of a <i>S</i>rotriya three years.</p>  
 <p>31. And if he has received a present (from the woman), he shall throw it away,</p>  
 <p>32. Or restore it to the giver.</p>  
 <p>33. If he has employed Vedic texts for people (with whom such intercourse is) forbidden, (he shall remain chaste for a year), provided (the portion of the Veda thus employed) contained one thousand words. <a name="fr_1418"></a><a href="#fn_1417"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_287"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 287</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. And the same (penance must be performed) by him who extinguishes the (sacred) fires, who neglects the daily recitation of the Veda, or (who is guilty) of a minor offence (upapâtaka),</p>  
 <p>35. Also by a wife who violates her duty (to her husband): but, being guarded, she shall receive food. <a name="fr_1419"></a><a href="#fn_1418"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. For committing a bestial crime, excepting (the case of) a cow, (he shall offer) an oblation of clarified butter, (reciting) the Kûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>a texts. <a name="fr_1420"></a><a href="#fn_1419"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>
