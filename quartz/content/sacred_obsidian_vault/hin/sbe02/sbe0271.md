   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER VII.</h3>  
 <p>1. The rule for (times of) distress (is) that a Brâhma<i>n</i>a may study under a teacher who is not a Brâhma<i>n</i>a. <a name="fr_997"></a><a href="#fn_996"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (A student is bound) to walk behind and to obey (his non-Brahmanical teacher). <a name="fr_998"></a><a href="#fn_997"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. (But), when (the course of study) has been finished, the Brâhma<i>n</i>a (pupil is more) venerable (than his teacher). <a name="fr_999"></a><a href="#fn_998"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. (In times of distress it is permissible) to offer <a name="fr_1000"></a><a href="#fn_999"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_212"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 212</font></a></p>  
 <p>sacrifices for (men of) all (castes), to teach (them), and to accept (presents from them).</p>  
 <p>5. Each preceding (mode of living is) preferable (to those named later). <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. On failure of the (occupations lawful for a Brâhma<i>n</i>a) he may live by the occupations of a Kshatriya. <a name="fr_1002"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. On failure of those, he may live by the occupations of a Vai<i>s</i>ya. <a name="fr_1003"></a><a href="#fn_1002"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. (Goods) that may not be sold by a (Brâhma<i>n</i>a are),</p>  
 <p>9. Perfumes, substances (used for) flavouring (food), prepared food, sesamum, hempen and linen cloth, skins, <a name="fr_1004"></a><a href="#fn_1003"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Garments dyed red or washed, <a name="fr_1005"></a><a href="#fn_1004"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Milk and preparations from it, <a name="fr_1006"></a><a href="#fn_1005"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Roots, fruits, flowers, medicines, honey, flesh, grass, water, poison,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_213"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 213</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Nor animals for slaughter,</p>  
 <p>14. Nor, under any circumstances, human beings, heifers, female calves, cows big with young. <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="#fn_1006"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Some (declare, that the traffic in) land, rice, barley, goats, sheep, horses, bulls, milch-cows, and draught-oxen (is) likewise (forbidden). <a name="fr_1008"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. But (it is permissible) to barter, <a name="fr_1009"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. One kind of substances used for flavouring others,</p>  
 <p>18. And animals (for animals).</p>  
 <p>19. Salt and prepared food (must) not (be bartered), <a name="fr_1010"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Nor sesamum.</p>  
 <p>21. But for present use an equal (quantity of) uncooked (food may be exchanged) for cooked (food).</p>  
 <p>22. But if no (other course is) possible (a Brâhma<i>n</i>a) may support himself in any way except by (following the occupations) of a <i>S</i>ûdra. <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1010"><font size="1">22</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Some (permit) even this in case his life is in danger.</p>  
 <p>24. But to mix with that (caste) and forbidden food must be avoided (even in times of distress). <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_214"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 214</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. If his life is threatened, even a Brâhma<i>n</i>a may use arms. <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. (In times of distress) a Kshatriya (may follow) the occupations of a Vai<i>s</i>ya. <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="fn_1004"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1005">212:10</a> My MSS. read nir<i>n</i>ikte for nikte, and nir<i>n</i>iktam is explained by 'washed by a washerman or the like person.' It is possible to translate Professor Stenzler's reading in accordance with Manu X, 87, 'pairs of (i.e. upper and lower) garments dyed red.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1005"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1006">212:11</a> 'Preparations from it, i.e. sour milk and the like.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1006"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1007">213:14</a> Under any circumstances (nityam, literally "always") means even when they are not sold for slaughter. Another (commentator) says, that, as the expression "under any circumstances" is used here, the prohibition regarding the above-mentioned things, i.e. sesamum and the like, does not hold good under all circumstances, and that hence self-grown sesamum and other grain may be sold, see Manu X, 90.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1007"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1008">213:15</a> Manu X, 88. Haradatta explains 'land' by 'houses.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1010"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1011">213:22</a> Regarding the <i>S</i>ûdra's occupations, see below, X 57-60.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1011"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1012">213:24</a> 'Restriction (niyama), i.e. avoiding. That Brâhma<i>n</i>a <a name="page_214"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 214</font></a> even who lives the life of a <i>S</i>ûdra must not mix with that <i>S</i>ûdra caste, i.e. he must not sit among <i>S</i>ûdras and so forth.'--Haradatta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1012"></a><a href="sbe0271.htm#fr_1013">214:25</a> Âpastamba I, 10, 29, 7; Manu VIII, 348.</p>
