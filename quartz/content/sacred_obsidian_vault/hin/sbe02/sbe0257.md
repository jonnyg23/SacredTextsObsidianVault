   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, PA<i>T</i>ALA 9, KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A 22.</h3>  
 <p>1. A dress of materials procured in the woods (skins or bark) is ordained for him. <a name="fr_732"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Then he shall wander about, sustaining his life by roots, fruits, leaves, and grass. <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. In the end (he shall live on) what has become detached spontaneously.</p>  
 <p>4. Next he shall live on water, (then) on air, then on ether. <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. Each following one of these modes of subsistence is distinguished by a (greater) reward.</p>  
 <p>6. Now some (teachers) enjoin for the hermit the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_156"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 156</font></a></p>  
 <p>successive performance (of the acts prescribed for the several orders). <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. After having finished the. study of the Veda, having taken a wife and kindled the sacred fires, he shall begin the rites, which end with the Soma-sacrifices, (performing) as many as are prescribed in the revealed texts.</p>  
 <p>8. (Afterwards) he shall build a dwelling, and dwell outside the village with his wife, his children, and his fires, <a name="fr_736"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Or (he may live) alone.</p>  
 <p>10. He shall support himself by gleaning corn. <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. And after that he shall not any longer take presents.</p>  
 <p>12. And he shall sacrifice (only) after having bathed (in the following manner):</p>  
 <p>13. He shall enter the water slowly, and bathe without beating it (with his hand), his face turned towards the sun.</p>  
 <p>14. This rule of bathing is valid for all (castes and orders).</p>  
 <p>15. Some enjoin (that he shall prepare) two sets of utensils for cooking and eating, (and) of choppers, hatchets, sickles, and mallets. <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_157"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 157</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. He shall take one of each pair (of instruments), give the others (to his wife), and (then) go into the forest.</p>  
 <p>17. After that time (he shall perform) the burnt-oblations, (sustain) his life, (feed) his guests, and (prepare) his clothes with materials produced in the forest. <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Rice must be used for those sacrifices for which cakes mixed with meat (are employed by the householder).</p>  
 <p>19. And all (the Mantras), as well as the daily portion of the Veda, (must be recited) inaudibly.</p>  
 <p>20. He shall not make the inhabitants of the forest hear (his recitation). <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. (He shall have) a house for his fire (only).</p>  
 <p>22. He himself (shall live) in the open air.</p>  
 <p>23. His couch and seat, must not be covered (with mats).</p>  
 <p>24. If he obtains fresh grain, he shall throw away the old (store). <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>
