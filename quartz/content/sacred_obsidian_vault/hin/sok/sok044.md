 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XLIII</h3> 
 <p>I. 82. <i>pânî vic mîn piyâsî</i></p> 
 <p>I LAUGH when I hear that the fish in the water is thirsty:<br> You do not see that the Real is in your home, and you wander from forest to forest listlessly!<br> Here is the truth! Go where you will, to Benares or to Mathura; if you do not find your soul, the world is unreal to you.</p>
