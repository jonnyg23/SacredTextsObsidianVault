 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LII</h3> 
 <p>I. 130. <i>sâî<u>n</u> vin dard kareje hoy</i></p> 
 <p>WHEN I am parted from my Beloved, my heart is full of misery: I have no comfort in the day, I have no sleep in the night. To whom shall I tell my sorrow?<br> The night is dark; the hours slip by. Because my Lord is absent, I start up and tremble with fear.<br> Kabîr says: "Listen, my friend! there is no other satisfaction, save in the encounter with the Beloved."</p>
