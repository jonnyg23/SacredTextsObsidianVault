 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XXXIX</h3> 
 <p>I. 59. <i>sâdho, y<b>a</b>h tan <b>t</b>hâ<b>t</b>h ta<u>n</u>vure k<b>a</b></i></p> 
 <p>O FRIEND! this body is His lyre; He tightens its strings, and draws from it the melody of Brahma.<br> If the strings snap and the keys slacken, then to dust must this instrument of dust return:<br> Kabîr says: "None but Brahma can evoke its melodies."</p>
