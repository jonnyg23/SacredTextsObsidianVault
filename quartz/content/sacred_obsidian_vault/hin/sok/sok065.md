 <p>I. 117. <i>sâî<u>n</u> se lagan ka<b>t</b>hin hai, bhâî</i></p> 
 <p>NOW hard it is to meet my Lord!<br> The rain-bird wails in thirst for the rain: almost she dies of her longing, yet she would have none other water than the rain.<br> Drawn by the love of music, the deer moves forward: she dies as she listens to the music, yet she shrinks not in fear. <a name="page_108"><font size="1" color="green">p. 108</font></a><br> The widowed wife sits by the body of her dead husband: she is not afraid of the fire.<br> Put away all fear for this poor body.</p>
