 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LXXVII</h3> 
 <p>III. 60. <i>cal ha<b>m</b>sâ wâ des' jahâ<u>n</u></i></p> 
 <p>O MY heart! let us go to that country where dwells the Beloved, the ravisher of my heart!<br> There Love is filling her pitcher from the well, yet she has no rope wherewith to draw water;<br> There the clouds do not cover the sky, yet the rain falls down in gentle showers:<br> O bodiless one! do not sit on your doorstep; go forth and bathe yourself in that rain!<br> There it is ever moonlight and never dark; and who speaks of one sun only? that land is illuminate with the rays of a million suns.</p>
