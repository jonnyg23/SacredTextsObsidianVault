 <p>I. 16. <i>Santan jât na pûcho nirgu<b>n</b>iyâ<u>n</u></i></p> 
 <p>It is needless to ask of a saint the caste to which he belongs; <a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a><br> For the priest, the warrior. the tradesman, and all the thirty-six castes, alike are seeking for God.<br> It is but folly to ask what the caste of a saint may be;<br> The barber has sought God, the washerwoman, and the carpenter--<br> Even Raidas was a seeker after God.<br> The Rishi Swapacha was a tanner by caste.<br> Hindus and Moslems alike have achieved that End, where remains no mark of distinction.</p>
