 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XCVIII</h3> 
 <p>II. 98. <b><i>r</i></b>itu phâgun niya<b>r</b>ânî</p> 
 <p>THE month of March draws near: ah, who will unite me to my Lover?<br> How shall I find words for the beauty of my Beloved? For He is merged in all beauty.<br> His colour is in all the pictures of the world, and it bewitches the body and the mind.<br> Those who know this, know what is this unutterable play of the Spring. <a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="green">p. 144</font></a><br> Kabîr says: "Listen to me, brother' there are not many who have found this out."</p>
