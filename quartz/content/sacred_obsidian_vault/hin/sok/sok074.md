 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LXXIII</h3> 
 <p>III. 26. <i>âyau din gaune kâ ho</i></p> 
 <p>THE palanquin came to take me away to my husband's home, and it sent through my heart a thrill of joy;<br> But the bearers have brought me into the lonely forest, where I have no one of my own. <a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="green">p. 116</font></a><br> O bearers, I entreat you by your feet, wait but a moment longer: let me go back to my kinsmen and friends, and take my leave of them.<br> The servant Kabîr sings: "O Sadhu! finish your buying and selling, have done with your good and your bad: for there are no markets and no shops in the land to which you go."</p>
