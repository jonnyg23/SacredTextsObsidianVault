 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">LXXIX</h3> 
 <p>III. 66. <i>nâ mai<u>n</u> dharmî nahî<u>n</u> adharmî</i></p> 
 <p>I AM neither pious nor ungodly, I live neither by law nor by sense,<br> I am neither a speaker nor hearer, I am neither a servant nor master, I am neither bond nor free,<br> I am neither detached nor attached.<br> I am far from none: I am near to none.<br> I shall go neither to hell nor to heaven.<br> I do all works; yet I am apart from all works.<br> Few comprehend my meaning: he who can comprehend it, he sits unmoved.<br> Kabîr seeks neither to establish nor to destroy.</p>
