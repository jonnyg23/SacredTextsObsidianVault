 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XCVI</h3> 
 <p>I. 75. <i>samajh dekh man mît piyarwâ</i></p> 
 <p>O FRIEND, dear heart of mine, think well! if you love indeed, then why do you sleep?<br> If you have found Him, then give yourself utterly, and take Him to you.<br> Why do you loose Him again and again?<br> If the deep sleep of rest has come to your eyes, why waste your time making the bed and arranging the pillows?<br> Kabîr says: "I tell you the ways of love! Even though the head itself must be given, why should you weep over it?"</p>
