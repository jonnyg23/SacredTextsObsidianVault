 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Songs of Kabir</i>, tr. by Rabindranath Tagore, intro. by Evelyn Underhill, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="CENTER">XI</h3> 
 <p>I. 131. <i>nis' din khelat rahî sakhiyâ<u>n</u> sa<b>n</b>g</i></p> 
 <p>I PLAYED day and night with my comrades, and now I am greatly afraid.<br> So high is my Lord's palace, my heart trembles to mount its stairs: yet I must not be shy, if I would enjoy His love.<br> My heart must cleave to my Lover; I must withdraw my veil, and meet Him with all my body: <a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="green">p. 55</font></a><br> Mine eyes must perform the ceremony of the lamps of love.<br> Kabîr says: "Listen to me, friend: he understands who loves. If you feel not love's longing for your Beloved One, it is vain to adorn your body, vain to put unguent on your eyelids."</p>
