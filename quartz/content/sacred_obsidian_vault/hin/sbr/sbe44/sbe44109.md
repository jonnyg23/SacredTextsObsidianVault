 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4">13:5:4</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  <h4 align="center">DIFFERENT ARRANGEMENTS OF THE CHANTS OF THE A<i>S</i>VAMEDHA.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:1">13:5:4:1</a></font></span>1. Now, Indrota Daivâpa <i>S</i>aunaka once performed this sacrifice for <i>G</i>aname<i>g</i>aya Pârikshita, and by performing it he extinguished all evil-doing, all Brahman-slaughter; and, verily, he who performs the A<i>s</i>vamedha extinguishes (the guilt incurred by) all evil-doing, all Brahman-slaughter.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:2">13:5:4:2</a></font></span>2. It is of this, indeed, that the Gâthâ (strophe) sings,--'In Âsandîvat <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1029"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>G</i>aname<i>g</i>aya bound for the gods a black-spotted, grain-eating horse, adorned with a golden ornament and with yellow garlands.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:3">13:5:4:3</a></font></span>3. [There are] those same first two days <a name="fr_1035"></a><a href="#fn_1030"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and a <i>G</i>yotis <a name="fr_1036"></a><a href="#fn_1031"><font size="1">3</font></a> Atirâtra: therewith (they sacrificed) for Bhîmasena;--those same first two days, and a Go Atirâtra: therewith (they sacrificed) for Ugrasena;--those same first two days, and an Âyus Atirâtra: therewith (they sacrificed) for <i>S</i>rutasena. These are the Pârikshitîyas <a name="fr_1037"></a><a href="#fn_1032"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'The righteous Pârikshitas, performing horse-sacrifices, by their righteous work did away with sinful work one after another.'</p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:4">13:5:4:4</a></font></span>4. Those same first two days, and an Abhi<i>g</i>it <a name="fr_1038"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">1</font></a> Atirâtra,--therewith Para Â<i>t</i>nâra, the Kausalya king, once sacrificed: it is of this that Gâthâ sings,--'A<i>t</i>nâra's son, the Kausalya Para, Haira<i>n</i>yanâbha, caused a horse, meet for sacrifice, to be bound, and gave away the replete regions.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:5">13:5:4:5</a></font></span>5. Those same first two days, and a Vi<i>s</i>va<i>g</i>it <a name="fr_1039"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">1</font></a> Atirâtra,--therewith Purukutsa, the Aikshvâka king, once on a time performed a horse (daurgaha)-sacrifice, whence it is of this that the <i>Ri</i>shi sings (<i>Ri</i>g-v. IV, 42, 8),--'These, the seven <i>Ri</i>shis, were then our fathers when Daurgaha <a name="fr_1040"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">2</font></a> was bound.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:6">13:5:4:6</a></font></span>6. Those same first two days, and a Mahâvrata <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1035"><font size="1">3</font></a> Atirâtra,--therewith Marutta Âvikshita, the Âyogava king, once performed sacrifice; whence the Maruts became his guards-men, Agni his chamberlain, and the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i> his counsellors: it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'The Maruts dwelt as guards-men in Marutta Âvikshita's house, Agni as his chamberlain, and the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i> as his counsellors.' And, verily, the Maruts become the guards-men, Agni the chamberlain, and the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i> the counsellors of him who performs the horse-sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:7">13:5:4:7</a></font></span>7. Those same first two days, and an Aptoryâma <a name="fr_1042"></a><a href="#fn_1036"><font size="1">4</font></a> Atirâtra,--it was therewith that Kraivya, the Pâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âla king, once performed sacrifice,--for Krivis they formerly called the Pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âlas: it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'At Parivakrâ, the</p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Pâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âla overlord of the Krivis seized a horse, meet for sacrifice, with offering-gifts of a hundred thousand (head of cattle).'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:8">13:5:4:8</a></font></span>8. And a second (Gâthâ),--'A thousand myriads there were, and five-and-twenty hundreds, which the Brâhma<i>n</i>as of the Pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âlas from every quarter divided between them.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:9">13:5:4:9</a></font></span>9. The Agnish<i>t</i>oma in the Triv<i>ri</i>t (stoma); the Ukthya in the Pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ada<i>s</i>a; and the third day, with the Uktha (stotras), in the Saptada<i>s</i>a; the Sho<i>d</i>a<i>s</i>in (stotra) in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, the night (stotras) in the Pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ada<i>s</i>a, and the Sandhi (stotra) in the Triv<i>ri</i>t,--this is the (sacrifice) resulting in the Anush<i>t</i>ubh <a name="fr_1043"></a><a href="#fn_1037"><font size="1">1</font></a>: it is therewith that sacrifice was performed by Dhvasan Dvaitavana, the king of the Matsyas, where there is the lake Dvaitavana; and it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'Fourteen steeds did king Dvaitavana, victorious in battle, bind for Indra V<i>ri</i>trahan, whence the lake Dvaitavana (took its name).'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:10">13:5:4:10</a></font></span>10. The (three) Pavamâna (stotras) in the <i>K</i>aturvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (stoma), and (those performed) by repetitions <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1038"><font size="1">2</font></a> in the Triv<i>ri</i>t; the Pavamânas in the Katu<i>s</i><i>k</i>atvâri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (44-versed stoma), and (those performed) by repetition <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1039"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a; the Pavamânas in</p>

 <p>the Ash<i>t</i>â<i>k</i>atvâri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (48), and (those performed) by repetition in the Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (33) up to the Agnish<i>t</i>oma-sâman, the Uktha (stotras) in the Dvâtri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (32), the Sho<i>d</i>a<i>s</i>in in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, the night (stotras) in the Pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ada<i>s</i>a, and the Sandhi (stotra) in the Triv<i>ri</i>t</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:11">13:5:4:11</a></font></span>11. Suchlike is Vish<i>n</i>u's striding <a name="fr_1046"></a><a href="#fn_1040"><font size="1">1</font></a>,--it was therewith that Bharata Dau<i>h</i>shanti once performed sacrifice, and attained that wide sway which now belongs to the Bharatas: it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'Seventy-eight steeds did Bharata Dau<i>h</i>shanti bind for the V<i>ri</i>tra-slayer on the Yamunâ, and fifty-five near the Gaṅgâ.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:12">13:5:4:12</a></font></span>12. And a second (Gâthâ),--'Having bound a hundred and thirty-three horses, meet for sacrifice, king Saudyumni, more shifty, overcame the other shiftless ones.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:13">13:5:4:13</a></font></span>13. And a third,--'At Nâ<i>d</i>apit <a name="fr_1047"></a><a href="#fn_1041"><font size="1">2</font></a>, the Apsaras <i>S</i>akuntalâ conceived Bharata, who, after conquering the whole earth, brought to Indra more than a thousand horses, meet for sacrifice.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:14">13:5:4:14</a></font></span>14. And a fourth <a name="fr_1048"></a><a href="#fn_1042"><font size="1">3</font></a>,--'The greatness of Bharata neither the men before nor those after him attained, nor did the five (tribes of) men, even as a mortal man (does not touch) the sky with his arms.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:15">13:5:4:15</a></font></span>15. With the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a-stoma <a name="fr_1049"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">4</font></a> Rishabha Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>atura,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_400"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 400</font></a></p>  
 <p>king of the <i>S</i>viknas, performed sacrifice: it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'When Rishabha Yâ<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>atura was sacrificing, the Brahman-folk, having received wealth at the A<i>s</i>vamedha, divided the offering-gifts between them.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:16">13:5:4:16</a></font></span>16. With the Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a-stoma <i>S</i>o<i>n</i>a Sâtrâsâha, the Pâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âla king, performed sacrifice: it is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'When Sâtrâsâha performs the horse-sacrifice, the Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (stomas) come forth as (Taurva<i>s</i>a) horses, and six thousand mail-clad men <a name="fr_1050"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">1</font></a>.<font size="-1" color="green">'</font></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:17">13:5:4:17</a></font></span>17. And a second (Gâthâ),--'At the sacrifice of thee, Koka's father, the Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (stomas) come forth, each as six times six thousand <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">2</font></a> (horses), and six thousand mail-clad men.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:18">13:5:4:18</a></font></span>18. And a third,--'When Sâtrâsâha, the Pâ<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>âla king, was sacrificing, wearing beautiful garlands, Indra revelled in Soma, and the Brâhma<i>n</i>as became satiated with wealth.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:19">13:5:4:19</a></font></span>19. <i>S</i>atânîka Sâtrâ<i>g</i>ita performed the Govinata (form of A<i>s</i>vamedha), after taking away the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_401"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 401</font></a></p>  
 <p>horse of the Kâ<i>s</i>ya (king); and since that time the Kâ<i>s</i>is do not keep up the (sacrificial) fires, saying, 'The Soma-drink has been taken from us.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:20">13:5:4:20</a></font></span>20. The mode (of chanting) for this (Govinata form) is:--the Pavamâna (stotras) in the <i>K</i>aturvi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (stoma), and (those chanted) by repetitions in the Triv<i>ri</i>t;--the Pavamânas in the <i>K</i>atu<i>s</i><i>k</i>atvâri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, the Â<i>g</i>ya (stotras) in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, the Ukthas <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1046"><font size="1">1</font></a> In the Tri<i>n</i>ava, the P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>as in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a;--the Pavamânas in the Sha<i>t</i>tri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (36-versed), and (those chanted) by repetitions in the Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (33) up to the Agnish<i>t</i>oma-sâman, the Ukthas in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, the Sho<i>d</i>a<i>s</i>in in the Ekavi<i>m</i><i>s</i>a, the night (stotras) in the Pa<i>ñ</i><i>k</i>ada<i>s</i>a, and the Sandhi (stotra) in the Triv<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:21">13:5:4:21</a></font></span>21. It is of this that the Gâthâ sings,--'<i>S</i>atânîka Sâtrâ<i>g</i>ita seized a sacrificial horse, in the neighbourhood, the sacrifice of the Kâ<i>s</i>is, even as Bharata (seized that) of the Satvats.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:22">13:5:4:22</a></font></span>22. And a second,--'The mighty <i>S</i>atânîka, having seized, in the neighbourhood, Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra's white sacrificial horse, roaming at will in its tenth month, <i>S</i>atânîka <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1047"><font size="1">2</font></a> performed the Govinata-sacrifice.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:23">13:5:4:23</a></font></span>23. And a fourth <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">3</font></a>,--'The greatness of the Bharatas neither the men before nor those after them attained, nor did the seven (tribes of) men, even as a mortal man (does not touch) the sky with his flanks.'</p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:24">13:5:4:24</a></font></span>24. Now as to the sacrificial gifts. Whatever there is towards the middle of the kingdom other than the land, the men, and the property of the Brâhma<i>n</i>a, of that the eastern region belongs to the Hot<i>ri</i>, the southern to the Brahman, the western to the Adhvaryu, the northern to the Udgât<i>ri</i>; and the Hot<i>ri</i>kas share this along with them.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:25">13:5:4:25</a></font></span>25. When the Udayanîyâ (completing offering) is finished, he seizes twenty-one barren cows, sacred to Mitra-Varu<i>n</i>a, the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i>, and B<i>ri</i>haspati, with the view of his gaining those deities. And the reason why those sacred to B<i>ri</i>haspati come last is that B<i>ri</i>haspati is the Brahman (n.), and he thus establishes himself finally in the Brahman.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:26">13:5:4:26</a></font></span>26. And as to their being twenty-one of them,--the twenty-one-fold is he who shines yonder: twelve months, five seasons, these three worlds, and yonder sun as the twenty-first--this consummation (he thereby obtains).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:27">13:5:4:27</a></font></span>27. When the Udavasânîyâ (closing offering) is completed, they give, for a sacrificial gift, four women, with a maiden as the fifth, and four hundred female attendants according to agreement.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:5:4:28">13:5:4:28</a></font></span>28. And during the following year he performs the animal sacrifices of the seasons,--six (victims) sacred to Agni in the spring, six to Indra in the summer, six to Par<i>g</i>anya, or to the Maruts, in the rainy season, six to Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a in the autumn, six to Indra and Vish<i>n</i>u in the winter, and six to Indra and B<i>ri</i>haspati in the dewy season,--six seasons are a year: in the seasons, in the year, he thus establishes himself. These amount to thirty-six animals,--the B<i>ri</i>hatî (metre) consists of thirty-six syllables, and the heavenly world is established</p>  
 <p><a name="page_403"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 403</font></a></p>  
 <p>upon the B<i>ri</i>hatî: and thus he finally establishes himself, by means of the B<i>ri</i>hatî metre, in the heavenly world.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1044"></a><a href="sbe44109.htm#fr_1050">400:1</a> This seems to be Harisvâmin's interpretation of the verse:--torva<i>s</i>â a<i>s</i>vâ <i>g</i>yesh<i>th</i>e tam api s<i>ri</i>gyeran iti (?) trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>â stomâ udga<i>k</i><i>kh</i>anti, sa hi <i>S</i>o<i>n</i>as trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>ân eva stomân trishv api divaseshu prayuṅkte nânyân iti to udga<i>k</i><i>kh</i>antîty âha, sha<i>t</i> tu sahasrâ<i>n</i>i varmi<i>n</i>â<i>m</i> râ<i>g</i>aputrâ<i>n</i>â<i>m</i> kava<i>k</i>inâm a<i>s</i>vapâlânâm udîrata iti vartate varshe prâptâ eva drash<i>t</i>avyâ<i>h</i>. The St. Petersb. Dict., on the other hand, construes 'trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>â<i>h</i>' along with 'sha<i>t</i> sahasrâ<i>n</i>i' = 6033 (? horses of mail-clad men). This interpretation seems to me to involve serious difficulties. The use of those Stomas doubtless is supposed to result in the advantages here enumerated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1045"></a><a href="sbe44109.htm#fr_1051">400:2</a> Koko nâma nâtha<i>h</i>, ke te a<i>s</i>vâ udîrata iti prathamâyâ<i>m</i> gâthâyâm ukta<i>m</i> tad atrâpy anuvartate; teshâ<i>m</i> tatra parimâ<i>n</i>a<i>m</i> noktam atra sha<i>t</i>tri<i>m</i>sad a<i>s</i>vasahasrâ<i>n</i>i rakshi<i>n</i>âm anu<i>k</i>arabhntâny udga<i>k</i><i>kh</i>antîty âha; trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>â<i>s</i> <i>k</i>odîrate sha<i>d</i> <i>dh</i>i(?) varmi<i>n</i>â<i>m</i> padânetâsu (?) ga<i>k</i><i>kh</i>antîti. Harisvâmin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1046"></a><a href="sbe44109.htm#fr_1052">401:1</a> Why these are here put before the P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>as, is not clear.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1047"></a><a href="sbe44109.htm#fr_1053">401:2</a> Perhaps we ought here to read 'Sâtrâ<i>g</i>ita.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1048"></a><a href="sbe44109.htm#fr_1054">401:3</a> Unless the Gâthâ in the preceding paragraph (being in the Trish<i>t</i>ubh metre) is really counted as two, the author seems here purposely to have omitted a verse. Possibly, however, it may mean, 'the fourth,' viz. referring to <a href="sbe44109.htm#13:5:4:14">paragraph 14</a>.</p>
