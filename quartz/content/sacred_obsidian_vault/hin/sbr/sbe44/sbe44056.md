 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4">12:3:4</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:1">12:3:4:1</a></font></span>1. Pra<i>g</i>âpati once upon a time spake unto Purusha</p>  
 <p><a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Nârâya<i>n</i>a, 'Offer sacrifice! offer sacrifice!' He spake, 'Verily, thou sayest to me, "Offer sacrifice! offer sacrifice!" and thrice have I offered sacrifice: by the morning-service the Vasus went forth, by the midday-service the Rudras, and by the evening-service the Âdityas; now I have but the offering-place <a name="fr_498"></a><a href="#fn_498"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and on the offering-place I am sitting.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:2">12:3:4:2</a></font></span>2. He spake, 'Offer yet sacrifice! I will tell thee such a thing that thy hymns shall be strung as a pearl on a thread, or a thread through a pearl.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:3">12:3:4:3</a></font></span>3. And he spake thus unto him, ‘At the (chanting of the) Bahishpavamâna, at the morning-service, thou shalt hold on to the Udgât<i>ri</i> from behind, saying, "Thou art a falcon formed of the Gâyatrî metre,--I hold on to thee: bear me unto well-being!"</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:4">12:3:4:4</a></font></span>4. ‘And at the midday Pavamâna thou shalt hold on to the Udgât<i>ri</i> from behind, saying, "Thou art an eagle formed of the Trish<i>t</i>ubh metre,--I hold on to thee: bear me unto well-being! "</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:5">12:3:4:5</a></font></span>5. ‘And at the Ârbhava-pavamâna, at the evening-service, thou shalt hold on to the Udgât<i>ri</i> from behind, saying, "Thou art a <i>Ri</i>bhu formed of the <i>G</i>agat metre,--I hold on to thee: bear me unto well-being!"</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:6">12:3:4:6</a></font></span>6. 'And at the close of each pressing thou shalt mutter, "In me be light, in me might, in me glory, in me everything!"'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:7">12:3:4:7</a></font></span>7. Now light, indeed, is this (terrestrial) world, might the air-world, glory the heavens, and what other worlds there are, they are everything (else).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:8">12:3:4:8</a></font></span>8. And light, indeed, is Agni, might Vâyu (the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a></p>  
 <p>wind), glory Âditya (the sun), and what other gods there are they are everything.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:9">12:3:4:9</a></font></span>9. And light, indeed, is the <i>Ri</i>g-veda, might the Ya<i>g</i>ur-vela, glory the Sâma-veda, and what other Vedas there are they are everything.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:10">12:3:4:10</a></font></span>10. And light, indeed, is speech, might the breath, glory the eye, and what other vital airs there are they are everything.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="12:3:4:11">12:3:4:11</a></font></span>11. Let him know this:--'All the worlds have I placed within mine own self, and mine own self have I placed within all the worlds; all the gods have I placed within mine own self, and mine own self have I placed within all the gods; all the Vedas have I placed within mine own self, and mine own self have I placed within all the Vedas; all the vital airs have I placed within mine own self, and mine own self have I placed within the vital airs.' For imperishable, indeed, are the worlds, imperishable the gods, imperishable the Vedas, imperishable the vital airs, imperishable is the All: and, verily, whosoever thus knows this, passes from the imperishable unto the imperishable, conquers recurrent death, and attains the full measure of life.</p>
