 <p><a name="page_591"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 591</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ADDITIONS AND CORRECTIONS.</h3>  
 <p>Page 13, paragraphs 7, 8. We have probably to translate--the gods were created on its reaching heaven,--they were created on its entering this earth.</p>  
 <p>P. 45, par. 33, lines 6, 11. Read,--in the balance.</p>  
 <p>P. 60, l. 19. Read,--Adhvaryu.</p>  
 <p>P. 76, note 3. Read,--offering of a pap to Aditi.</p>  
 <p>P. 91, par. 4, l. 4. Read,--did we, by one of a hundred Ukthyas, dispel the darkness.</p>  
 <p>P. 107, par. 10, ll. 2, 3. Read,--form of the bull.</p>  
 <p>P. 109, note 3. Add,--Cf. also Hillebrandt, 'Varu<i>n</i>a and Mitra,' p. 68 seq.</p>  
 <p>P. 130, par. 9, ll. 2, 3. Read,.--'The divine thought protect thee, not man's thought!'</p>  
 <p>P. 136, par. 6, l. 2. Under him:--Harisvâmin explains 'anu' by 'anvâbhakta,' 'participating after him,' cf. <a href="sbe44109.htm#13:5:4:24">XIII, 5, 4, 24</a>.</p>  
 <p>P. 149, note 1. The commentary takes 'p<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>yapratish<i>th</i>ite' in the sense of 'established on the p<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>ya,'--inasmuch as, in the first half of the year, he begins each month with the Abhiplava, and ends with the P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>ya, which is the stronger (balavattara, cf. <a href="sbe44050.htm#12:2:2:16">XII, 2, 2, 16</a>), and therefore 'âtmapratish<i>th</i>ita.'</p>  
 <p>P. 155, note 2. The MS. of the commentary is corrupt--âṅ-pûrvasyâkriyate satari<i>n</i>âdaya<i>h</i> <i>s</i>a<i>h</i> bhadrâgamam va<i>k</i><i>kh</i>asthâne evâdhikshipayati na prade<i>s</i>ântara âvartata ity artha<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p>P. 279, note 1, l. 3 from below. Read,--'four-eyed' dog.</p>  
 <p>P. 334, note 1. 'Utsanna' probably means 'detached'; cf. II, 5, 2, 48.</p>  
 <p>P. 397, par. 6, l. 3. For whence, read,--and then.</p>  
 <p align="center">PART IV.</p>  
 <p>P. 20, par. 5, l. 5. Read,--skin.</p>  
 <p>P. 25, note 1, l. 4. Read,--on the reta<i>h</i>si<i>k</i> range.</p>  
 <p>P. 48, par. 5, l. 4. Read Nabhas and Nabhasya.</p>  
 <p>P. 58, par. 4, l. 5. Read,--the seventh.</p>  
 <p>P. 103, par. 11. After 'sake,' add,--'The Sâkvara and Raivata sâmans for stability in the air!' for by the Sâkvara and Raivata sâmans it is indeed established in the air.</p>  
 <p>P. 108, l. 8. Read,--Pûrva<i>k</i>itti.</p>  
 <p>P. 163, l. 3. Read,--of seven seasons.</p>  
 <p>P. 168, note 4. Delete,--According to . . . referred to.</p>  
 <p>P. 170, pars. 6-8. Read,--circumambulates.</p>  
 <p>P. 192, par. 5, l. 2. Read,--irresistible warrior.</p>  
 <p>P. 223, par. 2, l. 3. Read,--shower of wealth.</p>  
 <p>P. 295, l. 1. Instead of, then,--read, thereby.</p>  
 <p>P. 312, par. 18, l. 9. Read,--exceeds this universe.</p>  
 <p>P. 313, l. 2. Read,--nor does he exceed this universe.</p>  
 <p>P. 325, par. 14. Read,--There are these five fingers (and toes) each consisting of four parts.</p>  
 <p></p><dir>Note 1. Delete,--that being the simplest kind of Soma-sacrifice.--Sâya<i>n</i>a indeed must mean--all Soma and other sacrifices, down to the Agnihotra;--hardly, all sacrifices concluding with the Agnihotra.</dir>  
 <p>P. 337, note 2. Read,--'moving in front,' or 'previous performance.'</p>  
 <p>P 352, par. 23, l. 2. Read,--thousand B<i>ri</i>hatîs.</p>  
 <p>P. 389, l. 9. Perhaps we ought to translate--that (gold man), indeed, is the end, the self, of everything here (or, of this universe). Cf. J. Muir, Orig. Sansk. Texts, vol. v, p. 389.</p>  
 <p>Par. 16, l. 2. Read,--they ascend to where desires have vanished.</p>  
 <p>P. 393, par. 1, l. 4. Read,--<i>G</i>ana <i>S</i>ârkarâkshya.</p>
