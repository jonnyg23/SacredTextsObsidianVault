 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1">13:7:1</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SEVENTH ADHYÂYA. FIRST BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  <h4 align="center">THE SARVAMEDHA, OR ALL-SACRIFICE.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:1">13:7:1:1</a></font></span>1. Brahman Svayambhu (the self-existent, n.) was performing austerities <a name="fr_1107"></a><a href="#fn_1100"><font size="1">3</font></a>. He said this much,--</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_418"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 418</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> 'Verily, there is no perpetuity in austerities; well, then, I will offer up mine own self in the creatures, and the creatures in mine own self.' And, accordingly, by offering up his own self in the creatures, and the creatures in his own self, he compassed the supremacy, the sovereignty, and the lordship over all creatures; and in like manner does the Sacrificer, by thus offering all sacrificial essences <a name="fr_1108"></a><a href="#fn_1101"><font size="1">1</font></a> in the Sarvamedha, compass all beings, and supremacy, sovereignty, and lordship.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:2">13:7:1:2</a></font></span>2. Now this Sarvamedha is a ten-days’ (Soma-) sacrifice, for the sake of his gaining and winning every kind of food, for the Virâ<i>g</i> consists of ten syllables, and the Virâ<i>g</i> is all food. At this (sacrifice) he builds the greatest possible fire-altar, for this--to wit, the Sarvamedha--is supreme amongst all sacrificial performances: by means of the supreme (sacrifice) he thus causes him (the Sacrificer) to attain supremacy.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:3">13:7:1:3</a></font></span>3. The first day thereof is an Agnish<i>t</i>ut Agnish<i>t</i>oma <a name="fr_1109"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">2</font></a>, for the sake of his gaining and winning all the gods, for the Agnish<i>t</i>ut Agnish<i>t</i>oma is Agni; and all the gods have Agni (the sacrificial fire) for their mouth, The cups of Soma thereat pertain to Agni, and so do the Puroru<i>k</i> <a name="fr_1110"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">3</font></a> formulas pertain to Agni, in order that everything should pertain to Agni.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_419"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 419</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:4">13:7:1:4</a></font></span>4. The second day is an Indrastut Ukthya, for the sake of his gaining and winning all the gods, for Indra is all the gods. The cups of Soma thereat pertain to Indra, and so do the Puroru<i>k</i> formulas pertain to Indra, in order that everything should pertain to Indra.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:5">13:7:1:5</a></font></span>5. The third day is a Sûryastut, Ukthya, for the sake of his gaining and winning all the gods, for Sûrya is all the gods. The cups of Soma pertain to Sûrya, and so do the Puroru<i>k</i> formulas pertain to Sûrya, in order that everything should pertain to Sûrya.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:6">13:7:1:6</a></font></span>6. The fourth day is a Vai<i>s</i>vadeva, for the sake of his gaining and winning all the gods, for the All-gods (Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i>) are all the gods. The cups of Soma pertain to the All-gods, and so do the Puroru<i>k</i> formulas pertain to the All-gods, in order that everything should pertain to the All-gods.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:7">13:7:1:7</a></font></span>7. The fifth day is a central Â<i>s</i>vamedhika <a name="fr_1111"></a><a href="#fn_1104"><font size="1">1</font></a> one: at this (sacrifice) he seizes a horse meet for sacrifice, for the sake of his gaining the sacrificial essence of the horse.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:8">13:7:1:8</a></font></span>8. The sixth day is a central Paurushamedhika <a name="fr_1112"></a><a href="#fn_1105"><font size="1">2</font></a> one: at this (sacrifice) he seizes men meet for sacrifice, for the sake of his gaining the sacrificial essence of man.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:9">13:7:1:9</a></font></span>9. The seventh day is an Aptoryâma, for the sake of his gaining all kinds of Soma-sacrifices: at this (sacrifice) he seizes all kinds of (victims) meet for sacrifice, both what is animate and what is</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_420"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 420</font></a></p>  
 <p>inanimate. Of those with omenta he offers the omenta, and of those without omenta they throw down pieces cut out of the skin <a name="fr_1113"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and of herbs and trees they do so after cutting them up,--every kind of food of both the dry and the fresh he offers, in order to gain every kind of food. Everything he offers; and, to every one he offers in order to gain and to win everything. The omenta having been offered at the morning-service, and in the same way the oblations at the evening-service,--</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:10">13:7:1:10</a></font></span>10. The eighth day is a Tri<i>n</i>ava one <a name="fr_1114"></a><a href="#fn_1107"><font size="1">2</font></a>, for the Tri<i>n</i>ava (stoma) is the thunderbolt, and by means of the thunderbolt, indeed, lordship (kshatra) is gained: by means of the thunderbolt he thus gains lordship.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:11">13:7:1:11</a></font></span>11. The ninth day is a Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a one <a name="fr_1115"></a><a href="#fn_1108"><font size="1">3</font></a>, for the sake of his gaining a foothold, for the Trayastri<i>m</i><i>s</i>a (stoma) is a foothold.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:12">13:7:1:12</a></font></span>12. The tenth day is a Vi<i>s</i>va<i>g</i>it Atirâtra with all the P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>as <a name="fr_1116"></a><a href="#fn_1109"><font size="1">4</font></a>, for the sake of his gaining and winning everything, for the Vi<i>s</i>va<i>g</i>it Atirâtra with all the P<i>ri</i>sh<i>th</i>as is everything, and the Sarvamedha is everything.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:13">13:7:1:13</a></font></span>13. Now as to the sacrificial fees: whatever there is towards the middle of the kingdom other than the property of the Brâhma<i>n</i>a, but including land and</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_421"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 421</font></a></p>  
 <p>men, of that the eastern quarter belongs to the Hot<i>ri</i>, the southern to the Brahman, the western to the Adhvaryu, and the northern to the Udgât<i>ri</i>; and the Hot<i>ri</i>kas share this along with them.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:14">13:7:1:14</a></font></span>14. Vi<i>s</i>vakarman Bhauvana once performed this sacrifice, and having performed it he overpassed all beings, and became everything here; and verily he who, knowing this, performs the Sarvamedha, or who even knows this, overpasses all beings, and becomes everything here.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:7:1:15">13:7:1:15</a></font></span>15. It was Ka<i>s</i>yapa who officiated in his sacrifice, and it was concerning this that the Earth <a name="fr_1117"></a><a href="#fn_1110"><font size="1">1</font></a> also sang the stanza;--'No mortal must give me away; thou wast foolish, Vi<i>s</i>vakarman Bhauvana: she (the earth) will sink into the midst of the water; vain is this thy promise unto Ka<i>s</i>yapa.'</p>
