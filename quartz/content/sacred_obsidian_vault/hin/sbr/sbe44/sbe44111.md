 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2">13:6:2</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SECOND BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:1">13:6:2:1</a></font></span>1. And as to why it is called Purushamedha:--The stronghold (pur) doubtless is these worlds, and the Purusha (spirit) is he that blows here (the wind), he bides (<i>s</i>î) in this stronghold (pur): hence he is the Purusha. And whatever food there is in these worlds that is its 'medha,' its food; and inasmuch as this is its 'medha,' its food, therefore (it is called) Purushamedha. And inasmuch as at this (sacrifice) he seizes <a name="fr_1065"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">1</font></a> men (purusha) meet for sacrifice (medhya), therefore also it is called Purushamedha.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:2">13:6:2:2</a></font></span>2. He seizes them on the central day, for the central day is the air, and the air is the abode of all beings; and, indeed, these victims are also food, and the central day is the belly: he thus puts food in the belly.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:3">13:6:2:3</a></font></span>3. He seizes them by decades <a name="fr_1066"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">2</font></a> for the obtainment</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_408"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 408</font></a></p>  
 <p>of all food, for the Virâ<i>g</i> consists of ten syllables, and the Virâ<i>g</i> is all food <a name="fr_1067"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:4">13:6:2:4</a></font></span>4. Eleven decades <a name="fr_1068"></a><a href="#fn_1062"><font size="1">2</font></a> he seizes;--the Trish<i>t</i>ubh consists of eleven syllables, and the Trish<i>t</i>ubh is the thunderbolt, and vigour: with the thunderbolt, with vigour, the Sacrificer thus repels evil from within him.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:5">13:6:2:5</a></font></span>5. Forty-eight he seizes at the central stake;--the <i>G</i>agatî consists of forty-eight syllables, and cattle are of <i>G</i>âgata (movable) nature: by means of the <i>G</i>agatî he (the priest) secures cattle for him.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:6">13:6:2:6</a></font></span>6. Eleven at each of the others;--the Trish<i>t</i>ubh consists of eleven syllables, and the Trish<i>t</i>ubh is the thunderbolt, and vigour: with the thunderbolt,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_409"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 409</font></a></p>  
 <p>with vigour, the Sacrificer thus repels evil from about him.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:7">13:6:2:7</a></font></span>7. Last of all he seizes eight;--the Gâyatrî consists of eight syllables, and the Gâyatrî is the Brahman (n.): he thus makes the Brahman to be the ultimate thing of this universe, whence they say that the Brahman is the ultimate thing of this universe.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:8">13:6:2:8</a></font></span>8. These (eight) are sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati, Pra<i>g</i>âpati assuredly is the Brahman, for Pra<i>g</i>âpati is of the nature of the Brahman <a name="fr_1069"></a><a href="#fn_1063"><font size="1">1</font></a>: therefore they are sacred to Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:9">13:6:2:9</a></font></span>9. When about to bring up the victims, he offers those three oblations to Savit<i>ri</i>, with (Vâ<i>g</i>. S. XXX, 1-3), 'God Savit<i>ri</i>, (speed the sacrifice, speed the lord of sacrifice unto his share)!'--'May we obtain that glorious light of the God Savit<i>ri</i>, (who shall inspire our prayers)!'--'All troubles remove thou from us, O God Savit<i>ri</i>: bestow unto us what is auspicious!' He thus pleases Savit<i>ri</i>, and thus pleased with him, he (Savit<i>ri</i>) impels (speeds) those (sacrificial) men, and he seizes them impelled by that (Savit<i>ri</i>).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:10">13:6:2:10</a></font></span>10. For the priesthood he seizes a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, for the Brâhma<i>n</i>a is the priesthood: he thus joins priesthood to priesthood <a name="fr_1070"></a><a href="#fn_1064"><font size="1">2</font></a>;--for the nobility he seizes a Râ<i>g</i>anya, for the Râ<i>g</i>anya is the nobility: he thus joins nobility to nobility;--for the Maruts (he seizes) a Vai<i>s</i>ya, for the Maruts are the clans (peasants): he thus joins peasantry to peasantry;--</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_410"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 410</font></a></p>  
 <p>for (religious) toil (he seizes) a <i>S</i>ûdra, for the <i>S</i>ûdra is toil: he thus joins toil to toil;--according to their particular form he thus supplies these divinities with victims, and, thus supplied, they supply him with all his objects of desire.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:11">13:6:2:11</a></font></span>11. He makes offering with ghee, for ghee is fiery mettle: by means of fiery mettle he thus endows him (the Sacrificer) with fiery mettle. He makes offering with ghee, for that--to wit, ghee--is the dear resource of the gods: he thus supplies them with their dear resource, and, thus supplied, they supply him with all his objects of desire.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:12">13:6:2:12</a></font></span>12. By means of the Purusha Nârâya<i>n</i>a (litany), the Brahman priest (seated) to the right (south) of them, praises the men bound (to the stakes) with this sixteen-versed (hymn, <i>Ri</i>g-v. X, 90, Vâ<i>g</i>. S. XXXI, 1-16), 'The thousand-headed Purusha, thousand-eyed, thousand-footed <a name="fr_1071"></a><a href="#fn_1065"><font size="1">1</font></a> . . .;'--thus (he does) for the obtainment and the securing of everything, for everything here consists of sixteen parts, and the Purushamedha is everything: in thus saying, 'So and so thou art, so and so thou art,' he praises and thereby indeed magnifies him (Purusha); but he also thereby speaks of him, such as he is. Now, the victims had had the fire carried round them, but they were not yet slaughtered,--</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:13">13:6:2:13</a></font></span>13. Then a voice <a name="fr_1072"></a><a href="#fn_1066"><font size="1">2</font></a> said to him, 'Purusha, do not</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_411"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 411</font></a></p>  
 <p>consummate (these human victims <a name="fr_1073"></a><a href="#fn_1067"><font size="1">1</font></a>): if thou wert to consummate them, man (purusha) would eat man.' Accordingly, as soon as fire had been carried round them, he set them free, and offered oblations to the same divinities <a name="fr_1074"></a><a href="#fn_1068"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and thereby gratified those divinities, and, thus gratified, they, gratified him with all objects of desire.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:14">13:6:2:14</a></font></span>14. He makes offering with ghee, for ghee is fiery mettle: with fiery mettle he thus bestows fiery mettle upon him.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:15">13:6:2:15</a></font></span>15. He concludes with those of the set of eleven (stakes),--the Trish<i>t</i>ubh consists of eleven syllables, and the Trish<i>t</i>ubh is the thunderbolt, and vigour: with the thunderbolt, with vigour, the Sacrificer thus repels evil from within.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:16">13:6:2:16</a></font></span>16. The Udayanîyâ (concluding oblation) having been completed, he seizes eleven barren cows, sacred to Mitra-Varu<i>n</i>a, the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i>, and B<i>ri</i>haspati <a name="fr_1075"></a><a href="#fn_1069"><font size="1">3</font></a>, with the view of winning these deities. And as to those of B<i>ri</i>haspati being last,--B<i>ri</i>haspati truly is the Brahman (n.), and thus he finally establishes himself in the Brahman.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_412"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 412</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:17">13:6:2:17</a></font></span>17. And as to why there are eleven of them,--the Trish<i>t</i>ubh consists of eleven syllables, and the Trish<i>t</i>ubh is the thunderbolt, and vigour: with the thunderbolt, with vigour, the Sacrificer thus repels evil from within. The Traidhâtavî is the final offering (Udavasânîyâ): the mystic import is the same (as before <a name="fr_1076"></a><a href="#fn_1070"><font size="1">1</font></a>).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:18">13:6:2:18</a></font></span>18. Now as to the sacrificial fees. What there is towards the middle of the kingdom other than the land and the property of the Brâhma<i>n</i>a, but including the men, of that the eastern quarter belongs to the Hot<i>ri</i>, the southern to the Brahman, the western to the Adhvaryu, and the northern to the Udgât<i>ri</i>; and the Hot<i>ri</i>kas share this along with them.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:19">13:6:2:19</a></font></span>19. And if a Brâhma<i>n</i>a performs the sacrifice, he should bestow all his property in order to obtain and secure everything, for the Brâhma<i>n</i>a is everything, and all one's property is everything, and the Purushamedha is everything.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="13:6:2:20">13:6:2:20</a></font></span>20. And having taken up the two fires within his own self <a name="fr_1077"></a><a href="#fn_1071"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and worshipped the sun with the Uttara-Nârâya<i>n</i>a (litany, viz. Vâ<i>g</i>. S. XXXI, 17-22), let him betake himself to the forest without looking round; and that (place), indeed, is apart from men. But should he wish to live in the village, let him take up again the two fires in the churning-sticks, and having worshipped the sun with the Uttara-Nârâya<i>n</i>a (litany) let him dwell at his home, and let him offer such sacrifices as he may</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_413"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 413</font></a></p>  
 <p>be able to afford. But, indeed, this (sacrifice) is not to be imparted to any and every one, lest one should impart everything to any and every one, for the Purushamedha is everything; but one may only impart it to one who is known to him, and who is versed in sacred writ, and who may be dear to him, but not to any and every one.</p>  <h3 align="center">THE (SYMBOLICAL) VICTIMS OF THE PURUSHAMEDHA <a name="fr_1078"></a><a href="#fn_1072"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>

 <p>II. 1. To colour a devoted adherent--2. To strength a giver of gifts--3. To excrescences <a name="fr_1084"></a><a href="#fn_1078"><font size="1">4</font></a> hunchback--4.
