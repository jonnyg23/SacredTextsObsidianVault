 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3">11:5:3</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">THIRD BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:1">11:5:3:1</a></font></span>1. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya came to Uddâlaka Âru<i>n</i>i for a disputation on spiritual matters <a name="fr_268"></a><a href="#fn_268"><font size="1">3</font></a>, thinking, 'I desire to know the Agnihotra.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:2">11:5:3:2</a></font></span>2. He said, 'Gautama, what like is thy Agnihotra cow? what like the calf? what like the cow joined by the calf? what like their meeting? what like (the milk) when being milked? what like when it has been milked? what like when brought (from the stable)? what like when put on the fire? what like when the light is thrown on it <a name="fr_269"></a><a href="#fn_269"><font size="1">4</font></a>; what like when water is poured thereto? what like when being taken off (the fire)? what like when taken off? what like when</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_80"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 80</font></a></p>  
 <p>being ladled out <a name="fr_270"></a><a href="#fn_270"><font size="1">1</font></a>? what like when ladled out? what like when lifted up (to be taken to the Âhavanîya)? what like when being taken there? what like when held down <a name="fr_271"></a><a href="#fn_271"><font size="1">2</font></a>?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:3">11:5:3:3</a></font></span>3. 'What like is the log thou puttest on? what like the first libation? why didst thou put it down (on the Vedi <a name="fr_272"></a><a href="#fn_272"><font size="1">3</font></a>)? why didst thou look away (towards the Gârhapatya <a name="fr_273"></a><a href="#fn_273"><font size="1">4</font></a>)? what like is the second libation?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:4">11:5:3:4</a></font></span>4. 'Why, having offered, dost thou shake it (the spoon)? why, having cleansed the spoon all round (the spout), didst thou wipe it on the grass-bunch? why, having cleansed it a second time all over, didst thou place thy hand on the south (part of the Vedi)? why didst thou eat (of the milk) the first time, and why the second time? why, on creeping away (from the Vedi), didst thou drink (water)? why, having poured water into the spoon, didst thou sprinkle therewith? why didst thou sprinkle it away a second time, and why a third time in that (northerly) direction? why didst thou pour down water behind the Âhavanîya? why didst thou bring (the offering) to a close? If thou hast offered the Agnihotra knowing this, then it has indeed been offered by thee;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_81"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 81</font></a></p>  
 <p>but if (thou hast offered it) not knowing this, then it has not been offered by thee.'</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:5">11:5:3:5</a></font></span>5. He (Uddâlaka) said, 'My Agnihotra cow is I<i>d</i>â, Manu's daughter <a name="fr_274"></a><a href="#fn_274"><font size="1">1</font></a>; my calf is of Vâyu's nature; the (cow) joined by the calf is in conjunction therewith <a name="fr_275"></a><a href="#fn_275"><font size="1">2</font></a>; their meeting is the Virâ<i>g</i>; (the milk) when being milked belongs to the A<i>s</i>vins, and when it has been milked, to the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i>; when brought (from the stable) it belongs to Vâyu; when put on (the fire), to Agni; when the light is thrown on it, it belongs to Indra and Agni; when water is poured thereto it belongs to Varu<i>n</i>a; when being taken off (the fire), to Vâyu; when it has been taken off, to Heaven and Earth; when being ladled out, to the A<i>s</i>vins; when it has been ladled out, to the Vi<i>s</i>ve Devâ<i>h</i>; when lifted up, to Mahâdeva; when being taken (to the Âhavanîya), to Vâyu; when held down, to Vish<i>n</i>u.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:6">11:5:3:6</a></font></span>6. 'And the log I put on (the fire) is the resting-place of the libations; and as to the first libation, I therewith gratified the gods; and when I laid down (the spoon with the milk), that belongs to B<i>ri</i>haspati; and when I looked away, then I joined together this and yonder world; and as to the second libation, I thereby settled myself in the heavenly world.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:7">11:5:3:7</a></font></span>7. 'And when, having offered, I shake (the spoon), that belongs to Vâyu; and when, having cleansed the spoon all round (the spout), I wiped it on the grass-bunch, then I gratified the herbs and trees;</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a></p>  
 <p>and when, having cleansed it a second time all over, I placed my hand on the south (part of the altar-ground), then I gratified the Fathers <a name="fr_276"></a><a href="#fn_276"><font size="1">1</font></a>; and when I ate (of the milk) the first time, then I gratified myself; and when (I ate) a second time, then I gratified my offspring; and when, having crept away (from the altar-ground), I drank (water), then I gratified the cattle; and when, having poured water into the spoon, I sprinkled therewith, then I gratified the snake-deities; and when (I sprinkled) a second time, then (I gratified) the Gandharvas and Apsaras; and when, a third time, I sprinkled it away in that (northerly) direction, then I opened the gate of heaven; and when I poured down water behind the altar, then I bestowed rain on this world; and when I brought (the sacrifice) to a close, then I filled up whatever there is deficient in the earth.'--'This much, then, reverend sir, we two (know) in common <a name="fr_277"></a><a href="#fn_277"><font size="1">2</font></a>,' said (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:8">11:5:3:8</a></font></span>8. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya, thus instructed, said, 'I would yet ask thee a question, reverend sir.'--'Ask then, Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya!' he replied. He (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya) said, 'If, at the time when thy fires are taken out, and the sacrificial vessels brought down, thou wert going to offer, and the offering-fire were then to go out, dost thou know what danger there is in that case for him who offers?' 'I know,' he replied; 'before long the eldest son would die in the case of him who would</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 83</font></a></p>  
 <p>not know this; but by dint of knowledge I myself have prevailed.'--'What is that knowledge, and what the atonement?' he asked.--'The breath of the mouth has entered the upward breathing--such (is the knowledge); and I should make the offering in the Gârhapatya fire--that would be the atonement, and I should not be committing that sin.'--'This much, then, reverend sir, we two (know) in common,' said (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:9">11:5:3:9</a></font></span>9. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya, thus instructed, said, 'I would yet ask thee a question, reverend sir.'--'Ask then, Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya!' he replied. He said, 'If, at that very time, the Gârhapatya fire were to go out, dost thou know what danger there is in that case for him who offers? I know it,' he replied; 'before long the master of the house <a name="fr_278"></a><a href="#fn_278"><font size="1">1</font></a> would die in the case of him who would not know this; but by dint of knowledge I myself have prevailed.'--'What is that knowledge, and what the atonement?' he asked.--'The upward breathing has entered the breath of the mouth--this (is the knowledge); and I would make the offering on the Âhavanîya--this would be the atonement, and I should not be committing that sin.'--'This much, then, reverend sir, we two (know) in common,' said (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:10">11:5:3:10</a></font></span>10. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya, thus instructed, said, 'I would yet ask thee a question, reverend sir.'--'Ask then, Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya!' he replied. He said, 'If, at that very time, the Anvâhâryapa<i>k</i>ana fire were to go out, dost thou know what danger there is in that case for him who offers?'--'I know it,' he replied; 'before long all the cattle would die in the case of him who would</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>  
 <p>not know this; but by dint of knowledge I myself have prevailed.'--'What is that knowledge, and what the atonement?' he asked.---'The through-breathing has entered the upward breathing--this (is the knowledge); and I would make the offering on the Gârhapatya fire--this is the atonement; and I should not be committing that sin.'--'This much, then, reverend sir, we two (know) in common,' said (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:11">11:5:3:11</a></font></span>11. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya, thus instructed, said, 'I would yet ask thee a question, reverend sir.'--'Ask, then, Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya!' he replied. He said, 'If, at that very time, all the fires were to go out, dost thou know what danger there is in that case for him who offers?'--'I know it,' he replied; 'before long the family would be without heirs in the case of him who would not know this; but by dint of knowledge I myself have prevailed.'--'What is that knowledge, and what the atonement?' he asked.--'Having, without delay, churned out fire, and taken out an offering-fire in whatever direction the wind might be blowing, I would perform an offering to Vâyu (the wind): I would then know that my Agnihotra would be successful, belonging as it would to all deities; for all beings, indeed, pass over into the wind, and from out of the wind they are again produced <a name="fr_279"></a><a href="#fn_279"><font size="1">1</font></a>. This would be the atonement, and I should not be committing that sin.'--'This much, then, reverend sir, we two (know) in common,' said (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:12">11:5:3:12</a></font></span>12. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya, thus instructed, said, 'I would yet ask thee a question, reverend sir.'--'Ask then, Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya!'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_85"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 85</font></a></p>  
 <p>he replied. He said, 'If at that very time all the fires were to go out, when there should be no wind blowing, dost thou know what danger there would be for him who offers?'--'I know it,' he replied; 'unpleasant things, indeed, he would see in this world, and unpleasant things in yonder world, were he not to know this; but by dint of knowledge I myself have prevailed.'--'What is that knowledge, and what the atonement?' he asked.--'Having, without delay, churned out fire, and taken out an offering-fire towards the east, and sat down behind it, I myself would drink (the Agnihotra milk): I should then know that my Agnihotra would be successful, belonging as it would to all deities, for all beings, indeed, pass into the Brâhma<i>n</i>a <a name="fr_280"></a><a href="#fn_280"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and from the Brâhma<i>n</i>a they are again produced. That would be the atonement; and I should not be committing that sin.'--'And, verily, I did not know this,' said (<i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="11:5:3:13">11:5:3:13</a></font></span>13. <i>S</i>au<i>k</i>eya, thus instructed, said, 'Here are logs for fuel: I will become thy pupil, reverend sir.' He replied, 'If thou hadst not spoken thus, thy head would have flown off <a name="fr_281"></a><a href="#fn_281"><font size="1">2</font></a>: come, enter as my pupil!'--'So be it,' he said. He then initiated him, and taught him that pain-conquering utterance, Truth: therefore let man speak naught but truth <a name="fr_282"></a><a href="#fn_282"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>
