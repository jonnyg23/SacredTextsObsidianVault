 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1">10:3:1</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">THIRD ADHYÂYA. FIRST BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:1">10:3:1:1</a></font></span>1. The Gâyatrî is the breath (of Pra<i>g</i>âpati, the altar), the Ush<i>n</i>ih the eye, the Anush<i>t</i>ubh the voice, the B<i>ri</i>hatî the mind, the Paṅkti the ear; the Trish<i>t</i>ubh is that generative breath; and the <i>G</i>agatî</p>  
 <p><a name="page_328"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 328</font></a></p>  
 <p>that downward breathing;--these are the seven metres increasing by four (syllables) each <a name="fr_641"></a><a href="#fn_631"><font size="1">1</font></a>, which are produced in Agni (the fire-altar).</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:2">10:3:1:2</a></font></span>2. 'The Gâyatrî is the breath,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in the breath that is this one thousand; and to the breath, indeed, this vigour belongs; for were the breath of him who builds it to pass away, this fire-altar, assuredly, would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to contain) a thousand Gâyatrîs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:3">10:3:1:3</a></font></span>3. 'The Ush<i>n</i>ih is the eye,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in the eye that is this one thousand; and to the eye, indeed, this vigour belongs, for were the eye-sight of him who builds it to pass away, this fire-altar, assuredly, would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to contain) a thousand Ush<i>n</i>ihs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:4">10:3:1:4</a></font></span>4. 'The Anush<i>t</i>ubh is the voice,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in the voice that is this one thousand; and to the voice, indeed, this vigour belongs, for were the voice of him who builds it to pass away, this fire-altar, assuredly, would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to contain) a thousand Anush<i>t</i>ubhs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:5">10:3:1:5</a></font></span>5. 'The B<i>ri</i>hatî is the mind,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in the mind that is this one thousand; and to the mind, indeed, this vigour belongs, for were the mind of him who builds it to pass away, this fire altar, assuredly,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_329"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 329</font></a></p>  
 <p>would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to contain) a thousand B<i>ri</i>hatîs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:6">10:3:1:6</a></font></span>6. 'The Paṅkti is the ear,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in the ear that is this one thousand; and to the ear, indeed, this vigour belongs, for were the power of hearing of him who builds it to pass away, this fire-altar, assuredly, would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to contain) a thousand Paṅktis.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:7">10:3:1:7</a></font></span>7. 'The Trish<i>t</i>ubh is that generative (life-giving) breath,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in that breath, that is this one thousand; and to that breath, indeed, this vigour belongs, for were that breath of him who builds it to become disordered, this fire-altar, assuredly, would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to contain) a thousand Trish<i>t</i>ubhs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:8">10:3:1:8</a></font></span>8. 'And the <i>G</i>agatî is that downward breathing,'--thus, whatever power, whatever vigour there is in that breathing, that is this one thousand; and to that breathing, indeed, this vigour belongs, for were that breathing of him who builds it to become disordered, this fire-altar, assuredly, would not be built: by this its form that (altar) becomes built (so as to include) a thousand <i>G</i>agatîs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:1:9">10:3:1:9</a></font></span>9. Now, these seven metres which increase by four (syllables) successively, and are firmly established in one another, are those seven vital airs <a name="fr_642"></a><a href="#fn_632"><font size="1">1</font></a> in man, firmly established in one another: thus, by</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_330"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 330</font></a></p>  
 <p>as much as the number of metres he utters has that (altar) of him who knows this, prayers uttered upon it in metre after metre, or hymns chanted, or <i>s</i>astras recited, or (bricks) laid down upon it.</p>
