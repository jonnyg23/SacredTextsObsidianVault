 <p><a name="page_400"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 400</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THIRD BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:6:3:1">10:6:3:1</a></font></span>1. Let him meditate upon the 'true Brahman.' Now, man here, indeed, is possessed of understanding <a name="fr_824"></a><a href="#fn_811"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and according to how great his understanding is when he departs this world, so does he, on passing away, enter yonder world.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:6:3:2">10:6:3:2</a></font></span>2. Let him meditate on the Self, which is made up of intelligence, and endowed with a body of spirit, with a form of light, and with an etherial nature, which changes its shape at will, is swift as thought, of true resolve, and true purpose, which consists of all sweet odours and tastes, which holds sway over all the regions and pervades this whole universe, which is speechless and indifferent <a name="fr_825"></a><a href="#fn_812"><font size="1">2</font></a>;even as a grain of rice, or a grain of barley, or a grain of millet, or the smallest granule of millet, so is this golden <a name="fr_826"></a><a href="#fn_813"><font size="1">3</font></a> Purusha in the heart; even as a smokeless light, it is greater than the sky, greater than the ether, greater than the earth, greater than all existing things;--that self of the spirit (breath) is my self: on passing away from hence I shall obtain that self. Verily, whosoever has this trust <a name="fr_827"></a><a href="#fn_814"><font size="1">4</font></a>, for him there is no uncertainty. Thus spake Sâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ilya, and so it is <a name="fr_828"></a><a href="#fn_815"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>
