 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2">10:3:2</a></font></span></p>  <h3 align="center">SECOND BRÂHMA<i>N</i>A.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:1">10:3:2:1</a></font></span>1. As to this they say, 'What metre and what deity are the head of the fire-altar?' The metre Gâyatrî and the deity Agni are its head.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:2">10:3:2:2</a></font></span>2. 'What metre and what deity are its neck?' The metre Ush<i>n</i>ih and the deity Savit<i>ri</i> are its neck.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:3">10:3:2:3</a></font></span>3. 'What metre and what deity are its spine?' The metre B<i>ri</i>hatî and the deity B<i>ri</i>haspati are its spine.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:4">10:3:2:4</a></font></span>4. 'What metre and what deity are its wings?' The metres B<i>ri</i>hat and Rathantara and the deities Heaven and Earth are its wings.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:5">10:3:2:5</a></font></span>5. 'What metre and what deity are its waist?' The metre Trish<i>t</i>ubh and the deity Indra are its waist.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:6">10:3:2:6</a></font></span>6. 'What metre and what deity are its hips?' The metre <i>G</i>agatî and the deity Âditya (the sun) are its hips.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:7">10:3:2:7</a></font></span>7. 'What metre and what deity are the vital air whence the seed flows?' The metre Ati<i>kh</i>andas and the deity Pra<i>g</i>âpati.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:8">10:3:2:8</a></font></span>8. 'What metre and what deity are that downward vital air?' The metre Ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>âya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>iya and the deity Vai<i>s</i>vânara.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:9">10:3:2:9</a></font></span>9. 'What metre and what deity are the thighs?' The metre Anush<i>t</i>ubh and that deity, the Vi<i>s</i>vedevâ<i>h</i>, are the thighs.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_331"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 331</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:10">10:3:2:10</a></font></span>10. 'What metre and what deity are the knees?' The metre Paṅkti and that deity, the Maruts, are the knees.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:11">10:3:2:11</a></font></span>11. 'What metre and what deity are the feet?' The metre Dvipadâ and the deity Vish<i>n</i>u are the feet.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:12">10:3:2:12</a></font></span>12. 'What metre and what deity are the vital airs?' The metre Vi<i>kh</i>andas and the deity Vâyu (the wind) are the vital airs.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="10:3:2:13">10:3:2:13</a></font></span>13. 'What metre and what deity are the defective and redundant parts?' The metre (of the verse) wanting a syllable (or syllables) and that deity, the waters, are the defective and redundant parts. This, then, is the knowledge of the body (of the altar), and suchlike is the deity that enters into this body; and, indeed, there is in this (sacrificial performance) no other prayer for the obtainment of heavenly bliss <a name="fr_643"></a><a href="#fn_633"><font size="1">1</font></a><font size="-1" color="green">.</font></p>
