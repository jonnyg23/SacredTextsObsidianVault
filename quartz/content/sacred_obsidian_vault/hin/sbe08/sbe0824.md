 <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER III.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>Who possesses this taciturnity <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_709"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and which of the two <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">6</font></a> is taciturnity? Describe, O learned person! the condition of taciturnity here. Does a learned man reach taciturnity <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">7</font></a> by taciturnity? And how, O sage! do they practise taciturnity in this world</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 163</font></a></p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>Since the Vedas, together with the mind <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">1</font></a>, fail to attain to him, hence (is he) taciturnity <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">2</font></a>--he about whom the words of the Vedas were uttered <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and who, O king! shines forth as consubstantial <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">4</font></a> with them.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>Does <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">5</font></a> the twice-born person who studies the <i>Rik</i> and the Ya<i>g</i>us texts, and the Sâma-veda, committing sinful (acts), become tainted, or does he not become tainted?</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said</p>
 <p>Not the Sâman texts, nor yet the <i>Rik</i> Texts, nor the Ya<i>g</i>us texts <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">6</font></a> save him, O acute sir! from sinful</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_164"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 164</font></a></p>
 <p>action. I do not tell you an untruth. The <i>Kh</i>andas do not save a sinful deceitful <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">1</font></a> man who behaves deceitfully <a name="fr_724"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">2</font></a>. At the time of the termination (of his life), the <i>Kh</i>andas abandon <a name="fr_725"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">3</font></a> him, as birds who have got wings (abandon their) nest.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said</p>
 <p>If, O acute sir! the Vedas are not able to save one who understands the Vedas, then whence is this eternal talk <a name="fr_726"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">4</font></a> of the Brâhma<i>n</i>as?</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>O you of great glory! this universe becomes manifest through his special forms--names <a name="fr_727"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">5</font></a> and the rest. The Vedas proclaim (his form) after describing (it) well <a name="fr_728"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and (they <a name="fr_729"></a><a href="#fn_724"><font size="1">7</font></a> also) state his difference from the universe. For that <a name="fr_730"></a><a href="#fn_725"><font size="1">8</font></a> are this penance and sacrifice prescribed. By these a learned man acquires merit, and afterwards destroying sin by merit <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_726"><font size="1">9</font></a>, he has his self illuminated by knowledge. By knowledge the learned man attains</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_165"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 165</font></a></p>
 <p>the self <a name="fr_732"></a><a href="#fn_727"><font size="1">1</font></a>. But, on the other hand, one who wishes for the fruit--heaven <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_728"><font size="1">2</font></a>--takes with him <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_729"><font size="1">3</font></a> all that he has done in this (world), enjoys it in the next, and then returns to the path <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_730"><font size="1">4</font></a> (of this world). Penance is performed in this world; the fruit is enjoyed elsewhere. But the penance of Brâhma<i>n</i>as is further developed <a name="fr_736"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">5</font></a>; that of others remains only as much (as when first performed).</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>How does the pure penance become developed and well developed <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">6</font></a>? O Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta! tell (me.) how I should understand that, O Lord!</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>This penance, free from sin <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">7</font></a>, is called pure <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">8</font></a>; and this pure penance becomes developed and well developed, not otherwise <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">9</font></a>. All this <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">10</font></a>, O Kshatriya!</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_166"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 166</font></a></p>
 <p>has for its root that penance about which you question me. By penance <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">1</font></a>, those conversant with the Vedas attained immortality, after departing from this world.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said.</p>
 <p>1 have heard about penance free from sin, O Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta! Tell me what is the sin (connected) with penance, so that I may understand the eternal mystery <a name="fr_743"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>The twelve beginning with wrath, and likewise the seven cruelties, are the defects (connected) with it; and there are (stated) in the Sâstras twelve merits (connected) with it, beginning with knowledge, which are known to the twice-born, and may be developed. Wrath, desire <a name="fr_744"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">3</font></a>, avarice, delusion <a name="fr_745"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">4</font></a>, craving <a name="fr_746"></a><a href="#fn_741"><font size="1">5</font></a>, mercilessness, censoriousness, vanity, grief <a name="fr_747"></a><a href="#fn_742"><font size="1">6</font></a>, attachment <a name="fr_748"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">7</font></a>, envy <a name="fr_749"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">8</font></a>, reviling others--these twelve should always be avoided by a man of high</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_167"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 167</font></a></p>
 <p>qualifications <a name="fr_750"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">1</font></a>. These, O king of kings! attend each and every man, wishing to find some opening <a name="fr_751"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">2</font></a>, as a hunter (watches) animals. [Boastful, lustful, haughty, irascible, unsteady <a name="fr_752"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">3</font></a>, one who does not protect (those dependent <a name="fr_753"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">4</font></a> on him), these six sinful acts are performed by sinful men who are not afraid (even) in the midst of great danger <a name="fr_754"></a><a href="#fn_749"><font size="1">5</font></a>.] One whose thoughts are (all) about enjoyments, who prospers by injuring (others), who repents of generosity, who is miserly, who is devoid of the power <a name="fr_755"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">6</font></a> (of knowledge), who esteems the group <a name="fr_756"></a><a href="#fn_751"><font size="1">7</font></a> (of the senses), who hates his wife <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_752"><font size="1">8</font></a>--these seven, different (from those previously mentioned), are the seven forms of cruelty. Knowledge, truth, self-restraint, sacred learning, freedom from animosity (towards living beings), modesty <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_753"><font size="1">9</font></a>, endurance <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_754"><font size="1">10</font></a>, freedom from censoriousness, sacrifice, gift, courage} <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">11</font></a>, quiescence <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">12</font></a>,these are the twelve great observances <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">13</font></a> of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a. Whoever is not devoid of these twelve can govern this whole world, and those who are</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_168"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 168</font></a></p>
 <p>possessed of three, two, or even one (of these) become, in (due) course, distinguished (for knowledge) and identified with the Brahman <a name="fr_763"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">1</font></a>. [Self-restraint, abandonment <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and freedom from heedlessness--on these depends immortality. And the talented Brâhma<i>n</i>as say that truth is chief over them.] Self-restraint has eighteen defects; if (any one of them is) committed, it is an obstacle (to self-restraint), They are thus stated. 'Untruthfulness, backbiting, thirst <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">3</font></a>, antipathy (to all beings), darkness <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">4</font></a>, repining <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_762"><font size="1">5</font></a>, hatred <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_763"><font size="1">6</font></a> of people, haughtiness, quarrelsomeness, injuring living creatures, reviling others, garrulity, vexation <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_764"><font size="1">7</font></a>, want of endurance <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_765"><font size="1">8</font></a>, want of courage <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_766"><font size="1">9</font></a>, imperfection <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_767"><font size="1">10</font></a>, sinful conduct, and slaughter. That is called self-restraint by the good, which is free from these defects. Frenzy has eighteen defects <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_768"><font size="1">11</font></a>; and abandonment is of six kinds. The contraries of those which have been laid down <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_769"><font size="1">12</font></a> are stated to be the defects of frenzy. Abandonment of six kinds is excellent. Of those six, the third is hard to achieve. With it one certainly crosses</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_169"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 169</font></a></p>
 <p>beyond all misery without distinction <a name="fr_775"></a><a href="#fn_770"><font size="1">1</font></a>. That being achieved, (everything) is accomplished <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_771"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The (first is the) giving away of sons and wealth to a deserving man who asks (for them); the second is gifts at Vedic ceremonies, and gifts at ceremonies laid down in the Sm<i>ri</i>tis <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_772"><font size="1">3</font></a>. The abandonment of desires, O king of kings! by means of indifference (to worldly objects) is laid down as the third <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">4</font></a>. With these one should become free from heedlessness. That freedom from heedlessness, too, has eight characteristics, and is (a) great (merit). Truthfulness, concentration, absorbed contemplation, reflexion <a name="fr_779"></a><a href="#fn_774"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and also indifference (to worldly objects), not stealing <a name="fr_780"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">6</font></a>, living the life of a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin, and</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_170"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 170</font></a></p>
 <p>likewise freedom from all belongings <a name="fr_781"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Thus have the defects of self-restraint been stated; one should avoid those defects. Freedom from (those) defects is freedom from heedlessness; and that, too, is deemed to have eight characteristics <a name="fr_782"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Let truth be your (very) self, O king of kings! On truth all the worlds rest <a name="fr_783"></a><a href="#fn_778"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Truth is said to be their main (principle). Immortality, depends on truth <a name="fr_784"></a><a href="#fn_779"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Getting rid of (these) defects, one should practise the observance of penance. This is the conduct prescribed by the Creator. Truth is the solemn vow of the good. The pure penance, which is free from these defects, and possessed of these characteristics, becomes developed, and well developed <a name="fr_785"></a><a href="#fn_780"><font size="1">5</font></a>. I will state to you, in brief, O king of kings! what you ask of me. This (observance) <a name="fr_786"></a><a href="#fn_781"><font size="1">6</font></a> is destructive of sin, and pure, and releases (one) from birth and death and old age <a name="fr_787"></a><a href="#fn_782"><font size="1">7</font></a>. If one is free from the five senses, and also from the mind <a name="fr_788"></a><a href="#fn_783"><font size="1">8</font></a>, O descendant of Bharata! also from (thoughts regarding) the past and the future <a name="fr_789"></a><a href="#fn_784"><font size="1">9</font></a>, one becomes happy.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Dh<i>ri</i>tarâsh<i>t</i>ra said:</p>
 <p>Some people make great boasts in consequence of (their knowing) the Vedas with the Âkhyânas as</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_171"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 171</font></a></p>
 <p>the fifth <a name="fr_790"></a><a href="#fn_785"><font size="1">1</font></a>; others, likewise, are (masters) of four Vedas; others, too, of three Vedas; others are (masters) of two Vedas, and of one Veda; and others of no Veda <a name="fr_791"></a><a href="#fn_786"><font size="1">2</font></a>. Tell me which of these is the greatest, whom I may know (to be) a Brâhma<i>n</i>a.</p>
 <p align="CENTER">Sanatsu<i>g</i>âta said:</p>
 <p>Through ignorance of the one Veda <a name="fr_792"></a><a href="#fn_787"><font size="1">3</font></a>--the one truth--O king of kings! numerous Vedas came into existence. Some <a name="fr_793"></a><a href="#fn_788"><font size="1">4</font></a> only adhere to the truth. The fancies of those who have fallen away from the truth are abortive, and through ignorance of the truth, ceremonies become amplified <a name="fr_794"></a><a href="#fn_789"><font size="1">5</font></a>. One should under stand a Brâhma<i>n</i>a, who (merely) reads much, to be a man of many words <a name="fr_795"></a><a href="#fn_790"><font size="1">6</font></a>. Know him only to be the (true) Brâhma<i>n</i>a, who swerves not from the truth <a name="fr_796"></a><a href="#fn_791"><font size="1">7</font></a>. O you who are the highest among men <a name="fr_797"></a><a href="#fn_792"><font size="1">8</font></a>! the <i>Kh</i>andas, indeed, refer of themselves <a name="fr_798"></a><a href="#fn_793"><font size="1">9</font></a> to it. Therefore,</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_172"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 172</font></a></p>
 <p>studying them, the learned persons who understand the <i>Kh</i>andas, attain to the Veda, not that which is to be known <a name="fr_799"></a><a href="#fn_794"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Among the Vedas, there is none which understands <a name="fr_800"></a><a href="#fn_795"><font size="1">2</font></a>. By the unintelligent <a name="fr_801"></a><a href="#fn_796"><font size="1">3</font></a>, one understands not the Veda, nor the object of knowledge <a name="fr_802"></a><a href="#fn_797"><font size="1">4</font></a>. He who knows the Veda knows the object of knowledge. He who knows the object of knowledge <a name="fr_803"></a><a href="#fn_798"><font size="1">5</font></a> knows not the truth. He who understands the Vedas understands also the object of knowledge; but that <a name="fr_804"></a><a href="#fn_799"><font size="1">6</font></a> is not understood by the Vedas or by those who understand the Vedas. Still the Brâhma<i>n</i>as who understand the Vedas, understand the Veda by means of the Vedas <a name="fr_805"></a><a href="#fn_800"><font size="1">7</font></a>. As the branch of a tree with regard to the part of a portion of the glorious <a name="fr_806"></a><a href="#fn_801"><font size="1">8</font></a> one, so, they declare, are the Vedas with</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 173</font></a></p>
 <p>regard to the subject of understanding the supreme self. I understand him to be a Brâhma<i>n</i>a who is ingenious, and explains <a name="fr_807"></a><a href="#fn_802"><font size="1">1</font></a> (Vedic texts). He who apprehends (those texts) thus <a name="fr_808"></a><a href="#fn_803"><font size="1">2</font></a>, does verily know that supreme (principle). . One should not go in search of it among (things) antagonistic <a name="fr_809"></a><a href="#fn_804"><font size="1">3</font></a> to it at all. Not looking (for him there) one sees that Lord by means of the Veda <a name="fr_810"></a><a href="#fn_805"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Remaining quiet, one should practise devotion, and should not even form a wish in the mind <a name="fr_811"></a><a href="#fn_806"><font size="1">5</font></a>. To him, the Brahman presents <a name="fr_812"></a><a href="#fn_807"><font size="1">6</font></a> itself, and directly afterwards he attains to the perfect <a name="fr_813"></a><a href="#fn_808"><font size="1">7</font></a> (one). By taciturnity <a name="fr_814"></a><a href="#fn_809"><font size="1">8</font></a>, verily, does one become a sage; (one does) not (become) a sage by dwelling in a forest <a name="fr_815"></a><a href="#fn_810"><font size="1">9</font></a>. And he is called the highest sage, who understands that indestructible (principle). One is called an analyser <a name="fr_816"></a><a href="#fn_811"><font size="1">10</font></a> (also) in consequence of</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a></p>
 <p>analysing all objects. The analysis (is) from that as the root; and as he makes (such an) analysis, hence is he so (called). The man who sees the worlds directly sees everything <a name="fr_817"></a><a href="sbe0825.htm#fn_812"><font size="1">1</font></a>. A Brâhma<i>n</i>a, verily, adhering to the truth, understands it, and becomes omniscient. I say to you, O learned person! that adhering to knowledge and the rest <a name="fr_818"></a><a href="sbe0825.htm#fn_813"><font size="1">2</font></a> in this way, one sees the Brahman, O Kshatriya! by means of a course (of study) in the Vedas <a name="fr_819"></a><a href="sbe0825.htm#fn_814"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_808"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fr_813">173:7</a> Cf. <i>Kh</i>ândogya, p. 516. The Bhûman there is the same as the Bahu here, viz. the Brahman. <i>S</i>a<u>n</u>kara says expressly in his comment on the Upanishad text, that Bahu and Bhûman, among other words, are synonyms.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_809"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fr_814">173:8</a> Self-restraint, as explained before at <a href="#page_163">p. 163</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_810"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fr_815">173:9</a> Though this is not unimportant, as may be seen from the contrast between town and forest at <i>Kh</i>ândogya, p. 340. See also Maitrî, p. 100; Mu<i>nd</i>aka, p. 240. As to the 'highest sage,' see B<i>ri</i>hadâra<i>n</i>yaka, p. 899, where the passage about 'sacrifice, gift, penance' should be compared with Gîtâ, <a href="sbe0820.htm#page_122">p. 122</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_811"></a><a href="sbe0824.htm#fr_816">173:10</a> The construction in the original is not quite clear. I understand the sense to be as follows: In the science of the soul, the <a name="page_174"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 174</font></a> analyser (the word is the same as the word for grammarian) is he who analyses objects, not words merely. Now the true analysis of objects reduces them all to the Brahman (cf. <i>Kh</i>ândogya, p. 407; B<i>ri</i>hadâra<i>n</i>yaka, p. 152); and the sage understands this, and makes the analysis accordingly, so he is rightly called an analyser.</p>
