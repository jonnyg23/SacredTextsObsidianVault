 <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER XXIII.</h1>
 <p align="CENTER">Brahman said:</p>
 <p>Now I shall proceed to describe the third--the best--quality, beneficial to all creatures, and unblamable, the duty of the good. joy <a name="fr_1584"></a><a href="#fn_1577"><font size="1">7</font></a>, pleasure, nobility, enlightenment and happiness also, absence of stinginess, absence of fear, contentment, faith, forgiveness, courage, harmlessness, equability, truth, straightforwardness, absence of wrath, absence of calumniation,</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_326"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 326</font></a></p>
 <p>purity, dexterity, valour. He who possesses the piety of concentration of mind, (holding) knowledge to be vain <a name="fr_1585"></a><a href="#fn_1578"><font size="1">1</font></a>, (good) conduct vain, service vain, and labour vain, he attains the highest in the next world. Devoid of (the notion that this or that is) mine, devoid of egoism, devoid of expectations, equable everywhere, not full of desires, (to be) such is the eternal duty of the good. Confidence, modesty <a name="fr_1586"></a><a href="#fn_1579"><font size="1">2</font></a>, forgiveness, liberality, purity, freedom from laziness, absence of cruelty, freedom from delusion, compassion to (all) creatures, absence of backbiting, joy, contentment, joviality, humility, good behaviour, purity in all action for (acquiring) tranquillity <a name="fr_1587"></a><a href="#fn_1580"><font size="1">3</font></a>, righteous feelings, emancipation <a name="fr_1588"></a><a href="#fn_1581"><font size="1">4</font></a>, indifference <a name="fr_1589"></a><a href="#fn_1582"><font size="1">5</font></a>, life as a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin, abandonment on all hands, freedom from (the notion that this or that is) mine, freedom from expectations <a name="fr_1590"></a><a href="#fn_1583"><font size="1">6</font></a>, unbroken piety <a name="fr_1591"></a><a href="#fn_1584"><font size="1">7</font></a>, (holding that) gifts (are) vain, sacrifices vain, learning vain, vows vain, receipt of gifts vain, piety vain, penance vain. Those talented Brâhma<i>n</i>as in this world, whose conduct is of this description, who adhere to the quality of goodness, abiding in the seat of the Brahman <a name="fr_1592"></a><a href="#fn_1585"><font size="1">8</font></a>, perceive (everything) aright. Getting rid of all</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_327"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 327</font></a></p>
 <p>sins, and free from grief, those talented men reach heaven, and create (various) bodies <a name="fr_1593"></a><a href="#fn_1586"><font size="1">1</font></a>. The power of governing, self-restraint, minuteness <a name="fr_1594"></a><a href="#fn_1587"><font size="1">2</font></a>, these those high-souled ones make (for themselves) by (the operations of their own) minds like the gods dwelling in heaven. They are said to have their currents upwards <a name="fr_1595"></a><a href="#fn_1588"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and to be gods, and of the quality of goodness <a name="fr_1596"></a><a href="#fn_1589"><font size="1">4</font></a>; and having gone to heaven they verily change in various ways, by means of nature <a name="fr_1597"></a><a href="#fn_1590"><font size="1">5</font></a>. They obtain and divide <a name="fr_1598"></a><a href="#fn_1591"><font size="1">6</font></a> whatever they desire. Thus, O chiefs of the twice-born! have I described to you the conduct of the quality of goodness. Understanding this according to rule, one obtains whatever one desires. The qualities</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_328"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 328</font></a></p>
 <p>of goodness have been specifically described, and the operation of the qualities has been accurately stated. The man who always understands these qualities, enjoys the qualities <a name="fr_1599"></a><a href="sbe0851.htm#fn_1592"><font size="1">1</font></a>, but is not attached to the qualities.</p>
