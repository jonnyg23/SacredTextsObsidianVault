 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THIRD KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. Some say: 'Let him take a Gâyatrî hymn for the Pra-uga. Verily, Gâyatrî is brightness and glory of countenance, and thus the sacrificer becomes bright and glorious.'</p>  
 <p>2. Others say: 'Let him take a Ush<i>n</i>ih hymn for the Pra-uga. Verily, Ush<i>n</i>ih is life, and thus the sacrificer has a long life.'</p>  
 <p>Others say: 'Let him take an Anush<i>t</i>ubh hymn</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_162"><font size="1" color="green">p. 162</font></a></p>  
 <p>for the Pra-uga. Verily, Anush<i>t</i>ubh is valour, and it serves for obtaining valour.'</p>  
 <p>Others say: 'Let him take a B<i>ri</i>hatî hymn for the Pra-uga. Verily, B<i>ri</i>hatî is fortune, and thus the sacrificer becomes fortunate.'</p>  
 <p>Others say: 'Let him take a Paṅkti hymn for the Pra-uga. Verily, Paṅkti is food, and thus the sacrificer becomes rich in food.'</p>  
 <p>Others say: 'Let him take a Trish<i>t</i>ubh hymn for the Pra-uga. Verily, Trish<i>t</i>ubh is strength, and thus the sacrificer becomes strong.'</p>  
 <p>Others say: 'Let him take a <i>G</i>agatî hymn for the Pra-uga. Verily, cattle is <i>G</i>agatî-like, and thus the sacrificer becomes rich in cattle.'</p>  
 <p>But we say: 'Let him take a Gâyatrî hymn only. Verily, Gâyatrî is Brahman, and that day (the mahâvrata) is (for the attainment of) Brahman. Thus he obtains Brahman by means of Brahman.</p>  
 <p>4. 'And it must be a Gâyatrî hymn by Madhu<i>k</i><i>kh</i>andas,</p>  
 <p>5. 'For Madhu<i>k</i><i>kh</i>andas is called Madhu<i>k</i><i>kh</i>andas, because he wishes (<i>kh</i>andati) for honey (madhu) for the <i>Ri</i>shis.</p>  
 <p>6. 'Now food verily is honey, all is honey, all desires are honey, and thus if he recites the hymn of Madhu<i>k</i><i>kh</i>andas, it serves for the attainment of all desires.</p>  
 <p>7. 'He who knows this, obtains all desires.'</p>  
 <p>This (Gâyatrî pra-uga), according to the one-day (ekâha) ceremonial <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">1</font></a>, is perfect in form <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">2</font></a>. On that day (the mahâvrata) much is done now and then which</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="green">p. 163</font></a></p>  
 <p>has to be hidden <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and has to be atoned for y recitation of hymns). Atonement (<i>s</i>ânti) is rest, the one-day sacrifice. Therefore at the end of the year (on the last day but one of the sacrifice that lasts a whole year) the sacrificers rest on this atonement as their rest.</p>  
 <p>8. He who knows this rests firm, and they also for whom a Hot<i>ri</i> priest who knows this, recites this hymn.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_390"></a><a href="sbe01182.htm#fr_392">161:3</a> Thus far the hymn which has to be recited by the Hot<i>ri</i> priest, after the eating of the <i>ri</i>tugrabas, has been considered. What follows next is the so-called Pra-uga hymn, consisting of seven t<i>ri</i><i>k</i>as, which the Hot<i>ri</i> has to recite after the Vi<i>s</i>vedevagraha. Different <i>S</i>âkhâs recommend hymns of different metres, our <i>S</i>âkhâ fixes on the Gâyatrî.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_391"></a><a href="sbe01182.htm#fr_393">162:1</a> It is copied from the Vi<i>s</i>va<i>g</i>it, and that from the Agnish<i>t</i>oma.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_392"></a><a href="sbe01182.htm#fr_394">162:2</a> Nothing is wanting for its performance, if one only follows the rules given in the Agnish<i>t</i>oma.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_393"></a><a href="sbe01182.htm#fr_395">163:1</a> Dâsîn<i>ri</i>tya-bahubhûtamaithuna-brahma<i>k</i>âripu<i>m</i><i>s</i><i>k</i>alîsampravâ-dâdikam. See Rajendralal Mitra, Introduction to his edition of the Aitareya-âra<i>n</i>yaka, p. 25. It might be better to join ekâha<i>h</i> with <i>s</i>ântyâm, but even then the argumentation is not quite clear.</p>
