 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THIRTEENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Then he said to Satyaya<i>g</i>ña Paulushi: 'O Prâ<i>k</i>înayogya, whom do you meditate on as the Self?' He replied: 'The sun only, venerable king.' He said: 'The Self which you meditate on is the Vai<i>s</i>vânara Self, called Vi<i>s</i>varûpa (multiform). Therefore much and manifold wealth is seen in your house.</p>  
 <p>2. 'There is a car with mules, full of slaves and jewels. You eat food and see your desire, and whoever thus meditates on that Vai<i>s</i>vânara Self, eats food and sees his desire, and has Vedic glory in his house.</p>  
 <p>'That, however, is but the eye of the Self, and you would have become blind, if you had not come to me.'</p>
