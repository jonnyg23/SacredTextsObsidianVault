 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="green">p. 220</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">FOURTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. This then becomes perfect as a thousand of B<i>ri</i>hatî verses. Its consonants <a name="fr_542"></a><a href="#fn_540"><font size="1">1</font></a> form its body, its voice <a name="fr_543"></a><a href="#fn_541"><font size="1">2</font></a> (vowels) the Soul <a name="fr_544"></a><a href="#fn_542"><font size="1">3</font></a>, its sibilants <a name="fr_545"></a><a href="#fn_543"><font size="1">4</font></a> the air of the breath.</p>  
 <p>2. He who knew this became Vasish<i>th</i>a, he took this name from thence <a name="fr_546"></a><a href="#fn_544"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. Indra verily declared this to Vi<i>s</i>vâmitra, and Indra verily declared this to Bharadvâga. Therefore Indra is invoked by him as a friend <a name="fr_547"></a><a href="#fn_545"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>4. This becomes perfect as a thousand of B<i>ri</i>hatî verses <a name="fr_548"></a><a href="#fn_546"><font size="1">7</font></a>, and of that hymn perfect with a thousand Brihad verses, there are 36,000 syllables <a name="fr_549"></a><a href="#fn_547"><font size="1">8</font></a>. So many are also the thousands of days of a hundred years (36,000). With the consonants they fill the nights, with the vowels the days.</p>  
 <p>5. This becomes perfect as a thousand of B<i>ri</i>hatî verses. He who knows this, after this thousand of B<i>ri</i>hatîs thus accomplished, becomes full of knowledge, full of the gods, full of Brahman, full of the immortal, and then goes also to the gods.</p>  
 <p>6. What I am (the worshipper), that is he (sun); what he is, that am I.</p>

 <p>7. This has been said by a <i>Ri</i>shi (Rv. I, 115, 1): 'The sun is the self of all that moves and rests.'</p>  
 <p>8. Let him look to that, let him look to that!</p>
