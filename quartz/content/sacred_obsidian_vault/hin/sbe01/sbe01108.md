 <p><a name="page_87"><font size="1" color="green">p. 87</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">FOURTEENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Then he said to Indradyumna Bhâllaveya: 'O Vaiyâghrapadya, whom do you meditate on as the Self?' He replied: 'Air only, venerable king.' He said: 'The Self which you meditate on is the Vai<i>s</i>vânara Self, called P<i>ri</i>thagvartman (having various courses). Therefore offerings come to you in various ways, and rows of cars follow you in various ways.</p>  
 <p>2. 'You eat food and see your desire, and whoever thus meditates on that Vai<i>s</i>vânara Self, eats food and sees his desire, and has Vedic glory in his house.</p>  
 <p>'That, however, is but the breath of the Self, and your breath would have left you, if you had not come to me.'</p>
