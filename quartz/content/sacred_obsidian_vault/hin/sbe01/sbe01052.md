 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">EIGHTEENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. The hiṅkâra is goats, the prastâva sheep, the udgîtha cows, the pratihâra horses, the nidhana man. These are the Revatî Sâmans, as interwoven in animals.</p>  
 <p>2. He who thus knows these Revatîs, as interwoven in animals, becomes rich in animals <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">2</font></a>, he reaches the full life, he lives long, becomes great with children and cattle, great by fame. His rule is, 'Never complain of animals.'</p>
