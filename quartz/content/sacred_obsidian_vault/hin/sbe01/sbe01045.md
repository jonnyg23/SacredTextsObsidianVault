 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">ELEVENTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A <a name="fr_140"></a><a href="#fn_139"><font size="1">3</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. The hiṅkâra is mind, the prastâva speech, the udgîtha sight, the pratihâra hearing, the nidhana</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="green">p. 29</font></a></p>  
 <p>breath. That is the Gâyatra Sâman, as interwoven in the (five) prâ<i>n</i>as <a name="fr_141"></a><a href="#fn_140"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. He who thus knows this Gâyatra interwoven in the prâ<i>n</i>as, keeps his senses, reaches the full life, he lives long <a name="fr_142"></a><a href="#fn_141"><font size="1">2</font></a>, becomes great with children and cattle, great by fame. The rule of him who thus meditates on the Gâyatra is, 'Be not high-minded.'</p>
