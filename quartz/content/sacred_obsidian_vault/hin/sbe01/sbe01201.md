 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THIRD KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A <a name="fr_495"></a><a href="#fn_493"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. Tat savitur v<i>ri</i><i>n</i>îmahe (Rv. V, 82, 1-3) and adyâ no deva savitar (Rv. V, 82, 4-6) are the beginning (pratipad) and the next step (anu<i>k</i>ara) of the Vai<i>s</i>vadeva hymn, taken from the Ekâha ceremonial and therefore proper <a name="fr_496"></a><a href="#fn_494"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. On that day <a name="fr_497"></a><a href="#fn_495"><font size="1">3</font></a> much is done now and then which has to be hidden, and has to be atoned for. Atonement is rest, the one-day sacrifice. Therefore at the end of the year the sacrificers; rest on this atonement as their rest. He who knows this rests firm, and they also for whom a Hot<i>ri</i> priest who knows this, recites this hymn.</p>  
 <p>3. Then (follows) the hymn addressed to Savit<i>ri</i>, tad devasya savitur vâryam mahat (Rv. IV, 53). Verily, mahat, great, (in this foot) is the end <a name="fr_498"></a><a href="#fn_496"><font size="1">4</font></a>. This day too is the end. Thus the verse is fit for the day.</p>

 <p>4. The hymn katarâ pûrvâ katarâ parâyo<i>h</i> (Rv. I, 185), addressed to Dyâvâp<i>ri</i>thivî, is one in which many verses have the same ending. Verily, this day also (the mahâvrata) is one in which many receive the same reward <a name="fr_499"></a><a href="#fn_497"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Thus it is fit for the day.</p>  
 <p>5. The hymn ana<i>s</i>vo <i>g</i>âto anabhî<i>s</i>ur ukthya<i>h</i> (Rv. IV, 36) is addressed to the <i>Ri</i>bhus.</p>  
 <p>6. In the first verse the word tri (<i>k</i>akra<i>h</i>) occurs, and trivat <a name="fr_500"></a><a href="#fn_498"><font size="1">2</font></a> is verily the end. This day also is the end (of the sacrifice). Thus the verse is fit for the day.</p>  
 <p>7. The hymn asya vâmasya palitasya hotu<i>h</i> (Rv. I, 164), addressed to the Vi<i>s</i>vedevas, is multiform. This day also is multiform <a name="fr_501"></a><a href="#fn_499"><font size="1">3</font></a>. Thus the verse is fit for the day.</p>  
 <p>8. He recites the end of it, beginning with gaurîr mimâya (Rv. I, 164, 41).</p>  
 <p>9. The hymn â no bhadrâ<i>h</i> kratavo yantu vi<i>s</i>vata<i>h</i> (Rv. I, 89), addressed to the Vi<i>s</i>vedevas, forms the Nividdhâna, taken from the Ekâha ceremonial, and therefore proper.</p>  
 <p>10. On that day much is done now and then which has to be hidden, and has to be atoned for. Atonement is rest, the one-day sacrifice. Therefore at the end of the year the sacrificers rest on this atonement as their rest. He who knows this rests firm, and they also for whom a Hot<i>ri</i> priest who knows this, recites this hymn.</p>  
 <p>11. The hymn vai<i>s</i>vânarâya dhisha<i>n</i>âm <i>ri</i>tav<i>ri</i>dhe</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (Rv. III, 2) forms the beginning of the Âgnimâruta. Dhisha<i>n</i>â, thought, is verily the end, this day also is the end. Thus it is fit for the day.</p>  
 <p>12. The hymn praya<i>g</i>yavo maruto bhrâ<i>g</i>ad<i>ri</i>sh<i>t</i>aya<i>h</i> (Rv. V, 55), addressed to the Maruts, is one in which many verses have the same ending. Verily, this day also is one in which many receive the same reward. Thus it is fit for the day <a name="fr_502"></a><a href="#fn_500"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>13. He recites the verse <i>g</i>âtavedase sunavâma somam (Rv. I, 99, 1), addressed to <i>G</i>âtavedas, before the (next following) hymn. That verse addressed to <i>G</i>âtavedas is verily welfare, and leads to welfare. Thus (by reciting it) he fares well <a name="fr_503"></a><a href="#fn_501"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>14. The hymn ima<i>m</i> stomam arhate <i>g</i>âtavedase (Rv. I, 94), addressed to <i>G</i>âtavedas, is one in which many verses have the same ending. Verily, this day also (the mahâvrata) is one in which many receive the same reward. Thus it is fit for the day, yea, it is fit for the day.</p>
