 <p><a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SECOND KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Next follows the right wing. It is this world (the earth), it is this Agni, it is speech, it is the Rathantara <a name="fr_468"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">1</font></a>, it is Vasish<i>th</i>a, it is a hundred <a name="fr_469"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">2</font></a>. These are the six powers (of the right wing) <a name="fr_470"></a><a href="#fn_468"><font size="1">3</font></a>. The Sampâta hymn (Rv. IV, 20) serves indeed for obtaining desires and for firmness. The Paṅkti verse (Rv. I, 80, 1) serves for proper food.</p>  
 <p>2. Next comes the Sûdadohas verse. Sûdadohas is breath, thereby he joins all joints with breath.</p>  
 <p>3. Next follows the left wing. It is that world (heaven), it is that sun, it is mind, it is the Brihat, it</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_190"><font size="1" color="green">p. 190</font></a></p>  
 <p>a, it is a hundred <a name="fr_471"></a><a href="#fn_469"><font size="1">1</font></a>. These are the six powers (of the left wing). The Sampâta hymn (Rv. IV, 23) serves indeed for obtaining desires and for firmness. The Paṅkti verse (Rv. I, 81, 1) serves for proper food.</p>  
 <p>4. These two (the right and the left wings) are deficient and excessive <a name="fr_472"></a><a href="#fn_470"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The Brihat (the left wing) is man, the Rathantara (the right wing) is woman. The excess belongs to the man, the deficiency to the woman. Therefore they are deficient and excessive.</p>  
 <p>5. Now the left wing of a bird is verily by one feather better, therefore the left wing is larger by one verse.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="green">p. 191</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Next comes the Sûdadohas verse. Sûdadohas is breath, and thereby he joins all joints with breath.</p>  
 <p>7. Next follows the tail. They are twenty-one Dvipadâ verses <a name="fr_473"></a><a href="#fn_471"><font size="1">1</font></a>. For there are twenty-one backward feathers in a bird.</p>  
 <p>8. Then the Ekavi<i>m</i>sa is the support of all Stomas, and the tail the support of all birds <a name="fr_474"></a><a href="#fn_472"><font size="1">2</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>9. He recites a twenty-second verse. This is made the form of two supports. Therefore all birds support themselves on their tail, and having supported themselves on their tail, they fly up. For the tail is a support.</p>  
 <p>10. He (the bird and the hymn) is supported by two decades which are Virâ<i>g</i>. The man (the sacrificer) is supported by the two Dvipadâs, the twenty first and twenty-second. That which forms the bird serves for the attainment of all desires; that which forms the man, serves for his happiness, glory, proper food, and honour.</p>  
 <p>11. Next comes a Sûdadohas verse, then a Dhayyâ, then a Sûdadohas verse. The Sûdadohas is a man, the Dhayyâ a woman, therefore he recites the Dhayyâ as embraced on both sides by the Sûdadohas. Therefore does the seed of both, when it is effused, obtain oneness, and this with regard to the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="green">p. 192</font></a></p>  
 <p>woman only. Hence birth takes place in and from the woman. Therefore he recites that Dhayyâ in that place <a name="fr_475"></a><a href="sbe01198.htm#fn_473"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>
