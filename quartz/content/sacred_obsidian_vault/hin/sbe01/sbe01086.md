 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">NINTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. Thus he reached the house of his teacher. The teacher said to him: 'Satyakâma.' He replied: 'Sir.'</p>  
 <p>2. The teacher said: 'Friend, you shine like one who knows Brahman. Who then has taught you <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">1</font></a>?' He replied: 'Not men. But you only, Sir, I wish, should teach me <a name="fr_209"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">2</font></a>;</p>  
 <p>3. 'For I have heard from men like you, Sir, that only knowledge which is learnt from a teacher (Â<i>k</i>ârya), leads to real good.' Then he taught him the same knowledge. Nothing was left out, Yea, nothing was left out.</p>
