 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">EIGHTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. There were once three men, well-versed in udgîtha <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_104"><font size="1">1</font></a>, <i>S</i>ilaka <i>S</i>âlâvatya, <i>K</i>aikitâyana, Dâlbhya, and Pravâha<i>n</i>a <i>G</i>aivali. They said: 'We are well-versed in udgîtha. Let us have a discussion on udgîtha.'</p>  
 <p>2. They all agreed and sat down. Then Pravâha<i>n</i>a <i>G</i>aivali <a name="fr_106"></a><a href="#fn_105"><font size="1">2</font></a> said: 'Sirs, do you both speak first,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="green">p. 16</font></a></p>  
 <p>for I wish to hear what two Brâhma<i>n</i>as <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">1</font></a> have to say.</p>  
 <p>3. Then <i>S</i>ilaka <i>S</i>âlâvatya said to <i>K</i>aikitâyana Dâlbhya: 'Let me ask you.'</p>  
 <p>'Ask,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>4. 'What is the origin of the Sâman?' 'Tone (svara),' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'What is the origin of tone?' 'Breath,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'What is the origin of breath?' 'Food,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'What is the origin of food?' 'Water,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>5. 'What is the origin of water?' 'That world (heaven),' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'And what is the origin of that world?'--</p>  
 <p>He replied: 'Let no man carry the Sâman beyond the world of svarga (heaven). We place (recognise) the Sâman in the world of svarga, for the Sâman is extolled as svarga (heaven).'</p>  
 <p>6. Then said Silaka Sâlâvatya to <i>K</i>aikitâyana Dâlbhya: 'O Dâlbhya, thy Sâman is not firmly established. And if any one were to say, Your head shall fall off (if you be wrong), surely your head would now fall.'</p>  
 <p>7. 'Well then, let me know this from you, Sir,' said Dâlbhya.</p>  
 <p>'Know it,' replied Silaka Sâlâvatya.</p>  
 <p>'What is the origin of that world (heaven)?' 'This world,' he replied.</p>  
 <p>'And what is the origin of this world?'--</p>  
 <p>He replied: 'Let no man carry the Sâman beyond this world as its rest. We place the Sâman</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="green">p. 17</font></a></p>  
 <p>in this world as its rest, for the Sâman is extolled as rest.'</p>  
 <p>8. Then said Pravâha<i>n</i>a <i>G</i>aivali to <i>S</i>ilaka <i>S</i>âlâvatya: 'Your Sâman (the earth), O <i>S</i>âlâvatya, has an end. And if any one were to say, Your head shall fall off (if you be wrong), surely your head would now fall.'</p>  
 <p>'Well then, let me know this from you, Sir,' said Sâlâvatya.</p>  
 <p>'Know it,' replied <i>G</i>aivali.</p>
