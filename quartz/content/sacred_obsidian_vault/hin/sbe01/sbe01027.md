 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../../img/tn_hin.jpg"></a><br><i>The Upanishads, Part 1 (SBE01)</i>, by Max Müller, [1879], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SIXTH KHA<i>N</i><i>D</i>A.</h3>  
 <p>1. The <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (veda) is this earth, the Sâman (veda) is fire. This Sâman (fire) rests on that <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (earth) <a name="fr_96"></a><a href="#fn_95"><font size="1">4</font></a>. Therefore the Sâman is sung as resting on the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="green">p. 13</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Sâ is this earth, ama is fire, and that makes Sâma.</p>  
 <p>2. The <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> is the sky, the Sâman air. This Sâman (air) rests on that <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (sky). Therefore the Sâman is sung as resting on the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>. Sâ is the sky, ama the air, and that makes Sâma.</p>  
 <p>3. <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> is heaven, Sâman the sun. This Sâman (sun) rests on that <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (heaven). Therefore the Sâman is sung as resting on the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>. Sâ is heaven, ama the sun, and that makes Sâma.</p>  
 <p>4. <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> is the stars, Sâman the moon. This Sâman (moon) rests on that <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (stars). Therefore the Sâman is sung as resting on the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>. Sâ is the stars, ama the moon, and that makes Sâma.</p>  
 <p>5. <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> is the white light of the sun, Sâman the blue exceeding darkness <a name="fr_97"></a><a href="#fn_96"><font size="1">1</font></a> (in the sun). This Sâman (darkness) rests on that <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (brightness). Therefore the Sâman is sung as resting on the <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>.</p>  
 <p>6. Sâ is the white light of the sun, ama the blue exceeding darkness, and that makes Sâma.</p>  
 <p>Now that golden <a name="fr_98"></a><a href="#fn_97"><font size="1">2</font></a> person, who is seen within the sun, with golden beard and golden hair, golden altogether to the very tips of his nails,</p>  
 <p>7. Whose eyes are like blue lotus's <a name="fr_99"></a><a href="#fn_98"><font size="1">3</font></a>, his name is ut, for he has risen (udita) above all evil. He also who knows this, rises above all evil.</p>  
 <p>8. <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> and Sâman are his joints, and therefore he is udgîtha. And therefore he who praises him</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="green">p. 14</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (the ut) is called the Ud-gât<i>ri</i> <a name="fr_100"></a><a href="#fn_99"><font size="1">1</font></a> (the out-singer). He (the golden person, called ut) is lord of the worlds beyond that (sun), and of all the wishes of the Devas (inhabiting those worlds). So much with reference to the Devas.</p>
