 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Yoga Vashisht or Heaven Found</i>, by Rishi Singh Gherwal, [1930], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">ASOKA'S MESSAGE TO HIS PEOPLE</h4>  
 <p>Asoka, the Great, ruled <a href="errata.htm#27">India</a> from 222 to 260 B. C., embraced the <a href="errata.htm#28">Buddhist</a> religion, and spread it over India and far beyond the limits of India. "If a man's fame," says Kopen, "can be measured, by the number of lips that have mentioned and still mention him with honor, Asoka is more famous than Charlemagne or Caesar."</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%">Thus spake royal Piyadasi,<br>      Of the Gods beloved;<br> "Grace and righteous exhortation<br>      Have my subjects moved. <a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="green">p. 186</font></a><br> For my pastors to the people<br>      Holy lessons sing,—<br> And my priests to countless thousands<br>      Loving message bring!  
 <p>I have spoke to subject peoples,<br>      Precepts I have loved;<br> I have carved on rock-made pillars,<br>      Lessons I have proved.<br> Ministers of faith and duty<br>      Have my mandates told;<br> Spoke to near and distant nations,<br>      Maxims, loved of old!</p>  
 <p>And within my spacious empire,<br>      By each highway made,<br> Figs and mangoes I have planted,<br>      For repose and shade;<br> Wells I made, for man and cattle,<br>      All that breathe and move,—<br> But with higher toil constructed<br>      Springs of faith and love!</p>  
 <p>Scatter then my royal riches,<br>      Spread my bounty, then,<br> To the monk and to the toiler,—<br>      To all living men,<br> To the Brahman and the Sharman,—<br>      To all sects of fame;<br> Let each clan and corporation<br>      Know Asoka's name!</p>  
 <p>And unto my royal bounty,<br>      Others add their store,<br> For my queens, with queenly mercy,<br>      Help the helpless poor;<br> And my white-robed, royal children,<br>      Acts of kindness prove,<br> Charity and Truth and Kindness,<br>      Purity and Love! <a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="green">p. 187</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thus, in ever-growing current,<br>      May our bounty flow,<br> To the Brahman and the Sharma,—<br>      To the poor and low:<br> For the humble and the lowly,<br>      Special kindness crave!<br> May our mercy reach the menial,<br>      Cheer the unchained slave!</p>  
 <p>Laws severe, we vainly fashion,<br>      Codes we vainly start;<br> Gentle teaching, soft persuasion,<br>      Touch the people's heart.<br> Hence, I carve this loving edict,<br>      Speak these maxims, pure,—<br> Future <a href="errata.htm#29">kings</a> shall work as long as<br>      Sun and moon endure!</p>  
 <p>Since I won my father's empire,—<br>      Since this state was mine,<br> Past are seven and twenty autumns<br>      When I carve this line.<br> Where ’tis writ on stony pillar,<br>      In this empire, vast,<br> Unto far and distant ages<br>      May this Edict last!"<br>                        Pillar Edict, <a href="errata.htm#30">viii</a>.</p></div>  <h4 align="center">ASOKA'S MESSAGE TO FOREIGN NATIONS</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%">King and Victor Piyadasi,<br>      Of the Gods beloved,<br> O’er the plains of broad Kalinga,<br>      With his army moved.<br> Hundred thousand men were taken,—<br>      Hundred thousand died;<br> Righteous sorrow wrung his bosom,<br> And the Victor cried; <a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a>  
 <p>'Brahmans pure and Sharmans holy,—<br>      Men who toil in life,<br> Faithful fathers, loving children,—<br>      Husband and the wife;<br> These to pain and separation,<br>      Slavery and death,<br> I have doomed, and swept Kalinga<br> With destruction's breath!</p>  
 <p>Let me seek for other trophies,<br>      Win the spoils of faith;<br> Peace and plenty, not disaster;.<br>      Life and love, not death!<br> Speak then to my farthest frontiers,<br>      To each distant soil:'<br> 'Warfare ends, the work of mercy,<br>      Henceforth is my toil!</p>  
 <p>Syria's monarch, Antiochus,—<br>      Egypt's Ptolemy,—<br> Macedonian Antigonas,—<br>      Cyrenean Magas, free,—<br> Alexander of Epiros,—<br>      These five kings of West,<br> Have received my loving message,—<br>      Gospel true and blest!</p>  
 <p>Cholas and the mighty Pandyas,—<br>      Tamba-pannis, meek;<br> Henaraja-Vismavasis,<br>      And the Bactrain Greek;<br> Nabhakas and Nabha-pantis,—<br>      Bhojas only stirred;<br> Andhras and the brave Pulindas,<br>      Have my tidings heard!</p>  
 <p>Messengers of Piyadasi<br>      To these lands are sent,—<br> Grateful kings and listening nations <a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a><br>      To his faith have bent;<br> Thus I win a brighter conquest,<br>      And a holier fame;<br> And a more than earthly gladness<br>      Thrills my mortal frame!</p>  
 <p>Rich and rare the golden fruitage<br>      Of a life of faith;<br> Full and ample is the harvest<br>      Gathered after death:<br> Hence, the monarch Piyadasi.<br>      Carves his sacred line, —<br> That his royal sons and grandsons<br>      May to faith incline!</p>  
 <p>That the kings of earth, hereafter,<br>      May all conquest shun,<br> Wrought by rapine and by bloodshed,<br>      Deeds of darkness done.<br> That the monarchs may, hereafter,<br>      Conquer realms by faith;<br> Fame on earth awaits such conquest,<br>      Glory after death!"<br>              </p></div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%">From the Translation of R. C. Dutt.</div>  
 <p>Note by Author—A workable scheme for the League of Nations.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%">The night approaches now; hold fast<br>      The lamp of holy knowledge, bright<br> With ever slowly kindling light,<br>      To guide thee till the gloom is past.<br> <br>                  Mahabharata xii. 12064</div>  
 <p align="center">Om! Om! Om!</p>
