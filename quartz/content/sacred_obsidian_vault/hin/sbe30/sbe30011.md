 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1. To the east (is placed) the Â<i>g</i>ya, to the west the mess of cooked food.</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_158"></a><a href="#fn_158"><font size="1">2</font></a> Having sprinkled (water) round (the fire) and poured Â<i>g</i>ya on the mess of cooked food, he begins to sacrifice simply with the pot-ladle, picking out portions of the sacrificial food (without 'underspreading' and pouring Â<i>g</i>ya over the Havis).</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_159"></a><a href="#fn_159"><font size="1">3</font></a> If he intends, however, to sacrifice so as to 'underspread' (the Havis with Â<i>g</i>ya) and to pour (Â<i>g</i>ya) over it, let him sacrifice first the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions (in the following way):</p>  
 <p>4. He should take four portions of Â<i>g</i>yafive portions (are taken) by the Bh<i>ri</i>gusand should sacrifice from west to east, on the northern side with (the formula), 'To Agni Svâhâ!' on the southern side with (the words), 'To Soma Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>5. He then cuts off (two or three Avadânas) from the Havis, having 'spread under' (Â<i>g</i>ya).</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_160"></a><a href="#fn_160"><font size="1">6</font></a> (Two Avadânas) from the middle and from the east side, if he (belongs to the families who) make</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a></p>  
 <p>four Avadânas. (Three Avadânas) from the middle, from the east and from the west side, if (he belongs to those who) make five Avadânas.</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_161"></a><a href="#fn_161"><font size="1">7</font></a> He sprinkles (Â<i>g</i>ya) on the cut-off portions.</p>  
 <p>8. He anoints the places from which he has cut them off (with Â<i>g</i>ya) in order that the strength (of the Havis) may not be lost.</p>  
 <p>9. He should sacrifice over the middle of the fire with (the words), 'To Agni Svâhâ!'</p>  
 <p>10. Once or thrice, in that same way.</p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_162"></a><a href="#fn_162"><font size="1">11</font></a> Now for the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t (oblation), after having 'spread under' (Â<i>g</i>ya), he cuts off once a very big (Avadâna) from the eastern part of the northern part (of the Havis), Twice he should sprinkle (Â<i>g</i>ya) on it.</p>  
 <p>12. But if he (belongs to the families who) make five Avadânas, he should 'spread under' twice, and cut off (the Avadâna), and sprinkle (Â<i>g</i>ya) on it twice.</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_163"></a><a href="#fn_163"><font size="1">13</font></a> He does not anoint the place from which he has cut off, in order that the strength (of the Havis) may be lost.</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_164"></a><a href="#fn_164"><font size="1">14</font></a> With the words, 'To Agni Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 36</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Svâhâ!' he should sacrifice it over the eastern part of the northern part (of the fire).</p>  
 <p>15. <a name="fr_165"></a><a href="#fn_165"><font size="1">15</font></a> He should sacrifice oblations of Â<i>g</i>ya on (the chief oblations of cooked sacrificial food), with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis.</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_166"></a><a href="#fn_166"><font size="1">16</font></a> The insertion (stands) before the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t (oblation).</p>  
 <p>17. If different sacrifices are performed together, there is only one sweeping (of the ground) round (the fire) (chap. 3, 1), one (putting of) fuel (on the fire) (chap. 7, 19), one Barhis, one sprinkling (of water) round (the fire) (chap. 8, 2), one Â<i>g</i>ya, and one offering of the two Â<i>g</i>yabhâgas (chap. 8, 3).</p>  
 <p>18. Having cut off (the Avadânas) for all (the single sacrifices), he sacrifices the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation only once.</p>  
 <p>19. <a name="fr_167"></a><a href="#fn_167"><font size="1">19</font></a> After he has sacrificed, he should throw that pot-ladle (which he has used in the preceding ceremonies) into the fire.</p>  
 <p>20. Or having washed it, he should take with it (the rest of the sacrificial food), and should eat that.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_37"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 37</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. The Sruva he should not throw into the fire, say some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>22. <a name="fr_168"></a><a href="#fn_168"><font size="1">22</font></a> By one who has not set up the sacred fires, the mess of cooked food should be offered to Agni at the festivals both of the full and of the new moon.</p>  
 <p>23. To Agni, or to Agni and Soma, by one who has set them up, at the full-moon (sacrifice);</p>  
 <p>24. To Indra, or to Indra and Agni, or to Mahendra, at the new-moon (sacrifice).</p>  
 <p>25. Or also one who has set up the sacred fires, should offer it to Agni at the festivals both of the full and of the new moon.</p>  
 <p>26. <a name="fr_169"></a><a href="#fn_169"><font size="1">26</font></a> Having put a piece of wood (on the fire), and having afterwards sprinkled (water) round (the fire), he performs the Ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avâstu ceremony (in the following way):</p>  
 <p>27. <a name="fr_170"></a><a href="#fn_170"><font size="1">27</font></a> From that same Barhis he should take a handful of Kura grass, and should dip it thrice into the Â<i>g</i>ya or into the Havis, the points, the middle, and the roots, with (the words), 'May the birds come, licking what has been anointed.'</p>  
 <p>28. He then should besprinkle that (handful of grass) with water, and should throw it into the fire with (the verse), 'Thou who art the lord of cattle, Rudra, who walkest with the lines (of cattle), the manly one: do no harm to our cattle; let this be offered to thee! Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>29. This (ceremony) they call Ya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>avâstu.</p>
