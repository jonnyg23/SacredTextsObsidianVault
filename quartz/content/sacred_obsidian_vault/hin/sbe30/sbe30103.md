 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 4, SECTION 10.</h3>  
 <p>1. We shall explain the Upanayana (or initiation of the student).</p>  
 <p>2. Let him initiate a Brâhma<i>n</i>a in the eighth year after the conception,</p>  
 <p>3. A Râ<i>g</i>anya in the eleventh, a Vai<i>s</i>ya in the twelfth year after the conception.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Spring, summer, autumn: these are the (fit) seasons (for the Upanayana), corresponding to the order of the castes.</p>  
 <p>5. (The boy's father) serves food to Brâhma<i>n</i>as and causes them to pronounce auspicious wishes, and serves food to the boy. (The teacher?) pours together, with the first Ya<i>g</i>us (of the next Anuvâka, warm and cold) water, pouring the warm water into the cold, and moistens (the boy's) head with the next (verse; M. II, 1, 2).</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_755"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">6</font></a> Having put three Darbha blades into his hair (towards each of the four directions) (the teacher [?]) shaves his hair with the next four (verses; M. II, 1, 3-6) with the different Mantras, towards the different (four) directions.</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_756"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">7</font></a> With the following (verse, M. II, 1, 7, somebody) addresses him while he is shaving.</p>  
 <p>8. Towards the south, his mother or a Brahma<i>k</i>ârin strews barley-grains on a lump of bull's dung; with this (dung) she catches up the hair (that is cut off), and puts it down with the next (verse; M. II, 1, 8) at the root of an Udumbara tree or in a tuft of Darbha grass.</p>  
 <p>9. <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">9</font></a> After (the boy) has bathed, and (the ceremonies) from the putting (of wood) on (the fire) down to the Â<i>g</i>yabhâga oblations (have been performed), he causes him to put a piece of Palâ<i>s</i>a wood on the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 272</font></a></p>  
 <p>fire with the next (verse; M. II, 2, 1), and makes him tread with his right foot on a stone to the north of the fire, with (the verse), 'Tread' (M. II, 2, 2).</p>  
 <p>10. Having recited the next two (verses; M. II. 2, 3. 4) over a garment that has been spun and woven on one day, and has caused him, with the next three (verses; M. II. 2, 5-7), to put it on, he recites over him, after he has put it on, the next (verse; M. II, 2, 8).</p>  
 <p>11. <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">11</font></a> He ties thrice around him, from left to right, a threefold-twisted girdle of Mu<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>a grass with the next two (verses; M. II. 2, 9. 10), and (gives him) a skin as his outer garment with the next (verse; II, 2, 11).</p>  
 <p>12. <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">12</font></a> To the north of the fire (the teacher) spreads out Darbha grass; on that he causes (the boy) to station himself with the next (verse; M. II. 3, 1), pours his joined hands full of water into (the boy's) joined hands, makes him sprinkle himself three times with the next (verse; M. II, 3, 2), takes hold of his right hand with the next (formulas; M. II, 3, 3-12), gives him with the next (formulas; M. II, 3, 13-23) in charge to the deities (mentioned in those Mantras), initiates him with the next Ya<i>g</i>us (M. II, 3, 24), and murmurs into his right ear the (Mantra), 'Blessed with offspring' (II, 3, 25).</p>
