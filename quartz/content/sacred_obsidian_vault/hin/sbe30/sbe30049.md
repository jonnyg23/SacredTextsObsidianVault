 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 2, SECTION 6.</h3>  
 <p>1. (This verse the teacher) murmurs in (the boy's) right ear;</p>  
 <p>2. (The verse), 'Life-giving, Agni' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. I, 3, 14, 4) in his left ear.</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_511"></a><a href="#fn_511"><font size="1">3</font></a> Both times he adds (to the verses quoted in the last Sûtras the formula), 'Stand fast in Agni and on the earth, in Vâyu and in the air, in Sûrya and in heaven. The bliss in which Agni, Vâyu, the sun, the moon, and the waters go their way, in that bliss go thy way, N.N.! Thou hast become the pupil of breath, N.N.!'</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_512"></a><a href="#fn_512"><font size="1">4</font></a> Approaching his mouth to (the boy's) mouth he murmurs, 'Intelligence may Indra give thee, intelligence the goddess Sarasvatî. Intelligence may the two A<i>s</i>vins, wreathed with lotus, bestow on thee.'</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_513"></a><a href="#fn_513"><font size="1">5</font></a> He then gives (the boy) in charge (to the gods and demons, with the formulas), 'To Kashaka (?) I</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_154"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 154</font></a></p>  
 <p>give thee in charge. To Antaka I give thee in charge. To Aghora ("the not frightful one") I give thee in charge. To Disease . . . to Yama . . . to Makha . . . to Va<i>s</i>inî ("the ruling goddess") . . . to the earth together with Vai<i>s</i>vânara . . . to the waters . . . to the herbs . . . to the trees . . . to Heaven and Earth . . . to welfare . . . to holy lustre . . . to the Vi<i>s</i>ve devâs . . . to all beings . . . to all deities I give thee in charge.'</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_514"></a><a href="#fn_514"><font size="1">6</font></a> He now teaches him the Sâvitrî, if he has (already) been initiated before.</p>  
 <p>7. If he has not been initiated (before, he teaches him the Sâvitrî) after three days have elapsed.</p>  
 <p>8. (He does so) immediately, says Pushkarasâdi.</p>  
 <p>9. <a name="fr_515"></a><a href="#fn_515"><font size="1">9</font></a> Having placed to the west of the fire a bunch of grass with its points directed towards the north, (the teacher) sits down thereon, facing the east, with (the formula), 'A giver of royal power art thou, a teacher's seat. May I not withdraw from thee.'</p>  
 <p>10. The boy raises his joined hands towards the sun, embraces (the feet of) his teacher, sits down to the south (of the teacher), addresses (him), 'Recite, sir!' and then says, 'Recite the Sâvitrî, sir!'</p>  
 <p>11. Having recited over (the boy the verse), 'We call thee, the lord of the hosts' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. II, 3, 14, 3), he then recites (the Sâvitrî) to him, firstly Pâda by Pâda, then hemistich by hemistich, and then the whole verse (in the following way),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a></p>  
 <p>'Bhûs! Tat Savitur vare<i>n</i>ya<i>m</i> (That adorable splendour)</p>  
 <p>'Bhuvo! Bhargo devasya dhîmahi (of the divine Savit<i>ri</i> may we obtain)</p>  
 <p>'Suvar! Dhiyo yo na<i>h</i> pra<i>k</i>odayât (who should rouse our prayers).</p>  
 <p>'Bhûr bhuvas! Tat Savitur vare<i>n</i>ya<i>m</i> bhargo devasya dhîmahi</p>  
 <p>'Suvar! Dhiyo yo na<i>h</i> pra<i>k</i>odayât.</p>  
 <p>'Bhûr bhuva<i>h</i> suvas! Tat Savitur . . . pra<i>k</i>odayât.'</p>
