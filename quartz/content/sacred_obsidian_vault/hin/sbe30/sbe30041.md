 <p><a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 127</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 9.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_440"></a><a href="#fn_440"><font size="1">1</font></a> One who desires to become a ruler among men should fast through a period of eight nights.</p>  
 <p>2. Then he should provide a Sruva spoon, a cup (for water), and fuel, of Udumbara wood,</p>  
 <p>3. Should go out of the village in an easterly or in a northerly direction, should put wood on the fire at a place where four roads meet,</p>  
 <p>4. And should sacrifice Â<i>g</i>ya, turning his face towards the sun, with (the formulas), 'Food indeed is the only thing that is pervaded by the metres,' and, 'Bliss indeed' (MB. II, 6, 13. 14);</p>  
 <p>5. A third (oblation) in the village with (the formula), 'The food's essence is ghee' (ibid. 15).</p>  
 <p>6. One who is desirous of cattle, (should offer this oblation) in a cow-stable.</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_441"></a><a href="#fn_441"><font size="1">7</font></a> If (the cow-stable) is damaged by fire (?), (he should offer) a monk's robe.</p>  
 <p>8. On a dangerous road let him make knots in the skirts of the garments (of himself and of his companions),</p>  
 <p>9. Approaching those (of the travellers) who wear garments (with skirts).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 128</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. (Let him do so with the three formulas, MB. II, 6, 13-15) with the word Svâhâ at the end of each.</p>  
 <p>11. This will bring a prosperous journey (to himself) and to his companions. [Or: (He should do the same with the garments) of his companions. This will bring a prosperous journey.]</p>  
 <p>12. One who desires to gain a thousand cart-loads (of gold), should sacrifice one thousand oblations of flour of fried grains.</p>  
 <p>13. One who is desirous of cattle, should sacrifice one thousand oblations of the excrements of a male and a female calf;</p>  
 <p>14. Of a male and a female sheep, if he is desirous of flocks.</p>  
 <p>15. <a name="fr_442"></a><a href="#fn_442"><font size="1">15</font></a> One who desires that his means of livelihood may not be exhausted, should sacrifice in the evening and in the morning the fallings-off of rice-grains, with (the formulas), 'To Hunger Svâhâ!' 'To Hunger and Thirst <i>S</i>vâhâ!' (MB. II, 6, 16. 17).</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_443"></a><a href="#fn_443"><font size="1">16</font></a> If somebody has been bitten by a venomous animal, he should murmur (the verse), 'Do not fear, thou wilt not die' (MB. II, 6, 18), and should besprinkle him with water.</p>  
 <p>17. With (the formula), 'Strong one! Protect' (MB. II, 6, 19), a Snâtaka, when lying down (to sleep), should lay down his bamboo staff near (his bed).</p>  
 <p>18. This will bring him luck.</p>  
 <p>19. (The verses), 'Thy worm is killed by Atri' (MB. II, 7, 1-4), he should murmur, besprinkling a place where he has a worm with water.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 129</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. If he intends to do this for cattle, he should fetch in the afternoon an earth-clod taken out of a furrow, and should put it down in the open air.</p>  
 <p>21. In the morning he should strew the dust of it round (the place attacked by worms), and should murmur (the same texts).</p>
