 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 8, SECTION 29.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_643"></a><a href="#fn_643"><font size="1">1</font></a> 'House, do not fear, do not tremble; bringing strength we come back. Bringing strength, gaining wealth, wise I come back to the house, rejoicing in my mind.</p>  
 <p>'Of which the traveller thinks, in which much joy</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>  
 <p>dwells, the house I call. May it know us as we know it.</p>  
 <p>'Hither are called the cows; hither are called goats and sheep; and the sweet essence of food is called hither to our house.</p>  
 <p>'Hither are called many friends, the sweet companionship of friends. May our dwellings always be unharmed with all our men.</p>  
 <p>'Rich in sap, rich in milk, refreshing, full of joy and mirth, free from hunger (?) and thirst, O house, do not fear us'with (these verses) he approaches his house (when returning from a journey).</p>  
 <p>2. 'To thee I turn for the sake of safety, of peace. The blissful one! The helpful one!, Welfare! Welfare!'with (this formula) he enters.</p>  
 <p>3. On that day, on which he has arrived, he should avoid all quarrelling.</p>  
 <p>4. 'The joyful house I enter which does not bring death to men; most manly (I enter) the auspicious one. Bringing refreshment, with genial minds (we enter the house); joyfully I lie down in it'with (this verse) he lies down.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_644"></a><a href="#fn_644"><font size="1">5</font></a> 'May we find our way with thee through all hostile powers, as through streams of water'with (this verse) he looks at his wife; he looks at his wife.</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the First Pra<i>s</i>na.</p>
