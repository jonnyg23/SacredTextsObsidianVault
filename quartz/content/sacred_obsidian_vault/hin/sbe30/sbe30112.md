 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 7, SECTION 19.</h3>  
 <p>1. The unground grain (which is left over, see above, VII, 18, 11) they give to the boys to eat.</p>  
 <p>2. Let him repeat in the same way this Bali-offering of whatever food he has got or of flour, from that day to full moon of (the month) Mârga<i>s</i>îrsha.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_289"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 289</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. On the day of the full moon of Mârga<i>s</i>îrsha after sunset a Sthâlîpâka (is offered as above, VII, 18, 5).</p>  
 <p>4. In the Mantra for the Bali-offering he changes (the word 'I shall offer' into) 'I have offered.'</p>  
 <p>5. Then he does not offer (the Bali) any longer.</p>  
 <p>6. (Now follows) the Âgraya<i>n</i>a sacrifice (or partaking of the first-fruits) of one who has not set up the (<i>S</i>rauta) fires.</p>  
 <p>7. He prepares a Sthâlîpâka of the fresh fruits, sacrifices to the deities of the (<i>S</i>rauta) Âgraya<i>n</i>a sacrifice with (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t as the fourth, fills his mouth with grains, swallows them, sips water, forms a lump of the boiled (sacrificial) food, and throws it up with the next Ya<i>g</i>us (II, 18, 1) to the summit of the house.</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_783"></a><a href="#fn_783"><font size="1">8</font></a> (Now follows) the 'redescent' in the winter.</p>  
 <p>9. With the next Ya<i>g</i>us (II, 18, 2) they 'redescend' (or take as their sleeping-place a layer of straw instead of the high bedsteads which they have used before). With the next Ya<i>g</i>us formulas (II, 18, 3-7) they lie down on a new layer (of straw) on their right sides,</p>  
 <p>10. The father to the south, the mother to the north (of him), and so the others, one after the other from the eldest to the youngest.</p>  
 <p>11. After he has arisen, he touches the earth with the next two (verses, II, 18, 8. 9).</p>  
 <p>12. In the same way the lying down, &amp;c., is repeated thrice.</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_784"></a><a href="#fn_784"><font size="1">13</font></a> Having prepared a Sthâlîpâka for Lana and</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_290"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 290</font></a></p>  
 <p>one for Kshetrapati, he goes out in an easterly or northerly direction, prepares a raised surface, (and then follow the ceremonies) beginning with the putting of wood on the fire.</p>  
 <p>14. To the west of the fire he builds two huts.</p>
