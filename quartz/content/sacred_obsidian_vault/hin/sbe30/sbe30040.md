 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_431"></a><a href="#fn_431"><font size="1">1</font></a> At the <i>S</i>rava<i>n</i>â and Âgrahâya<i>n</i>î sacrifices he should leave a remainder of fried grains.</p>  
 <p>2. Having gone out of the village in an easterly or in a northerly direction, and having put wood on the fire at a place where four roads meet, he should sacrifice (those fried grains) with his joined hands, with the single (verses of the text), 'Hearken, Râkâ!' (MB. II, 6, 2-5).</p>  
 <p>3. Walking eastward (he should), looking upwards, (offer a Bali) to the hosts of divine beings, with (the formula), 'Be a giver of wealth' (ibid. 6);</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_432"></a><a href="#fn_432"><font size="1">4</font></a> (Walking?) towards the side, (he should offer a Bali) to the hosts of other beings, looking downwards.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_433"></a><a href="#fn_433"><font size="1">5</font></a> Returning (to the fire) without looking back, he should, together with the persons belonging to his</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_125"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 125</font></a></p>  
 <p>family, as far as they have been initiated (by the Upanayana), eat the fried grains.</p>  
 <p>6. (This ceremony) procures happiness.</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_434"></a><a href="#fn_434"><font size="1">7</font></a> (With the two formulas), 'Obeying the will' and '<i>S</i>aṅkha' (MB. II, 6, 7. 8), he should sacrifice two oblations of rice and of barley separately,</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_435"></a><a href="#fn_435"><font size="1">8</font></a> With reference to a person whose favour he wishes to gain.</p>  
 <p>9. This is done daily.</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_436"></a><a href="#fn_436"><font size="1">10</font></a> With the Ekâksharyâ verse (MB. II. 6, 9) two rites (are performed) which are connected with the observance (of fasting) for a fortnight.</p>  
 <p>11. One who is desirous of long life, should sacrifice (with that verse), in the night of the full moon, one hundred pegs of Khadira wood;</p>  
 <p>12. Of iron, if he desires that (his enemies) may be killed.</p>  
 <p>13. Now another ceremony (performed with the same verse).</p>  
 <p>14. Having gone out of the village in an easterly or in a northerly direction, he should at a place where four roads meet, or on a mountain, set an elevated surface, consisting of the dung of beasts of the forest, on fire, should sweep the coals away, and should make an oblation of butter (on that surface) with his mouth, repeating that Mantra in his mind.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_126"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 126</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. If (that oblation of butter) catches fire, twelve villages (will be his).</p>  
 <p>16. If smoke rises, at least three.</p>  
 <p>17. They call this ceremony one which is not in vain.</p>  
 <p>18. <a name="fr_437"></a><a href="#fn_437"><font size="1">18</font></a> One who desires that his means of livelihood may not be exhausted, should sacrifice green cow-dung in the evening and in the morning.</p>  
 <p>19. <a name="fr_438"></a><a href="#fn_438"><font size="1">19</font></a> Of articles which he has bought, he should, after having fasted three (days and) nights, make an oblation with the formula, 'Here this Vi<i>s</i>vakarman' (MB. II. 6, 10).</p>  
 <p>20. Of a garment he should offer some threads (with that formula),</p>  
 <p>21. Of a cow some hairs (of its tail);</p>  
 <p>22. In the same way (he should offer some part) of other articles which he has bought.</p>  
 <p>23. <a name="fr_439"></a><a href="#fn_439"><font size="1">23</font></a> The sacrifice of a full oblation (with the verse, 'A full oblation I sacrifice,' MB. II, 6, 11) should be performed on the sacrificial day (i.e. on the first day of the fortnight),</p>  
 <p>24. And (on such a day let him sacrifice) with (the formula), 'Indrâmavadât (?)' (MB. II, 6, 12).</p>  
 <p>25. One who is desirous of glory, (should offer) the first (oblation); one who is desirous of companions, the second.</p>
