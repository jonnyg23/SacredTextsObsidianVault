 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 1, SECTION 6.</h3>  
 <p>1. In the third year (he performs) the <i>K</i>û<i>d</i>âkarman (i.e. the tonsure of the child's head).</p>  
 <p>2. In the fortnight (&amp;c., as in the preceding section, Sûtra 2, down to): 'Hail! Good luck!' The boy sits down to the west of the fire, facing the east;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 217</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_658"></a><a href="#fn_658"><font size="1">3</font></a> To the north (of the fire) his mother or a student (brahma<i>k</i>ârin) holds a lump of bull's dung;</p>  
 <p>4. Therewith he (or she) receives the (cut-off) hair.</p>  
 <p>5. He then pours cold and warm water together.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_659"></a><a href="#fn_659"><font size="1">6</font></a> Having poured warm water into cold water he moistens the hair near the right ear with (the formula), 'May the waters moisten thee for life' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h. I, 2, I, 1).</p>  
 <p>7. <a name="fr_660"></a><a href="#fn_660"><font size="1">7</font></a> With (the formula), 'Herb, protect him!' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h., loc. cit.) he puts an herb, with its point upwards, into (the hair).</p>  
 <p>8. With (the formula), 'Axe, do no harm to him!' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h., loc. cit.) he touches (that herb) with the razor.</p>  
 <p>9. With (the words), 'Heard by the gods, I shave that (hair)' (Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>h., loc. cit.) he shaves him.</p>  
 <p>10. In the same way (he moistens, &amp;c.) the other (sides of his head) from left to right.</p>  
 <p>11. Behind with (the Mantra), 'The razor with which Savit<i>ri</i>, the knowing one, has shaven (the beard) of king Soma and Varu<i>n</i>a, with that, ye Brâhma<i>n</i>as, shave his (head); make that he be united with vigour, with wealth, with glory.'</p>  
 <p>On the left side with (the Mantra), '(The razor) with which Pûshan has shaven (the beard) of B<i>ri</i>haspati, of Agni, of Indra, for the sake of long life, with that I shave thy (head), N.N.!'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 218</font></a></p>  
 <p>Before with (the Mantra), 'That he may long live in joy, and may long see the sun.'</p>  
 <p>12. After the hair has been shaven, they arrange the locks (which are left over), according to custom or according to what family he belongs.</p>  
 <p>13. <a name="fr_661"></a><a href="#fn_661"><font size="1">13</font></a> A person who is kindly disposed towards him, gathers the (cut-off) hair and buries it in a cow-stable, or near an Udumbara tree, or in a clump of Darbha grass, with (the Mantra), 'Where Pûshan, B<i>ri</i>haspati, Savit<i>ri</i>, Soma, Agni (dwell), they have in many ways searched where they should depose it, between heaven and earth, the waters and heaven.'</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_662"></a><a href="#fn_662"><font size="1">14</font></a> He makes a gift to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a according to his liberality.</p>  
 <p>15. To the barber (he gives) boiled rice with butter.</p>  
 <p>16. In the same way the Godânakarman (or the ceremony of shaving the beard) is performed in the sixteenth year.</p>  
 <p>17. He has him shaven including the top-lock.</p>  
 <p>18. Some declare that he leaves there the top-lock.</p>  
 <p>19. <a name="fr_663"></a><a href="#fn_663"><font size="1">19</font></a> Or he performs the Godâna sacred to Agni.</p>  
 <p>20. He gives a cow to his Guru.</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the First Pa<i>t</i>ala.</p>
