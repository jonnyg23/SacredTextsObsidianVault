 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 8, SECTION 22.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_790"></a><a href="#fn_790"><font size="1">1</font></a> After the ceremonies down to the Â<i>g</i>yabhâga oblations have been performed in the same way as at the fortnightly sacrifices, he makes with his joined hands oblations of the cake with the next (verse, II, 20, 27).</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_791"></a><a href="#fn_791"><font size="1">2</font></a> The rest (of the cake) he makes ready, divides (it) into eight parts and offers it to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_294"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 294</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. On the following day he touches a cow with a Darbha blade, with the words, 'I touch thee agreeable to the Fathers.'</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_792"></a><a href="#fn_792"><font size="1">4</font></a> Having silently offered five Â<i>g</i>ya oblations, and having cooked, the omentum of the (cow), and performed the 'spreading under' and the sprinkling over (of Â<i>g</i>ya), he sacrifices (the omentum) with the next (verse, II, 20, 28) with a Palâ<i>s</i>a leaf from the middle or the end (of the stalk).</p>  
 <p>5. (He sacrifices) boiled rice together with the meat (of the cow) with the next (verses, II, 20, 29-35),</p>  
 <p>6. Food prepared of meal with the next (verse, II, 21, 1),</p>  
 <p>7. Then the Â<i>g</i>ya oblations (indicated by the) next (Mantras, II, 21, 2-9).</p>  
 <p>8. (The rites) from the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t down to the offering of the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>as are the same (as at the <i>S</i>râddha).</p>  
 <p>9. Some (teachers) prescribe the Pi<i>n</i><i>d</i>a offering for the day after the Ash<i>t</i>akâ.</p>  
 <p>10. Here (follows) another (way for celebrating the Ash<i>t</i>akâ sacrifice). He sacrifices curds with his joined hands in the same way as the cake.</p>  
 <p>11. Having left over from the meat of the (cow, see above, 3. 4) as much as is required, on the day after (the Ash<i>t</i>akâ) (he performs) the rite of the Anvash<i>t</i>akâ.</p>  
 <p>12. This rite has been explained in the description of the monthly <i>S</i>râddha.</p>  
 <p>13. If he goes out in order to beg for something,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_295"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 295</font></a></p>  
 <p>let him murmur the next (Mantras, II, 21, 10-16) and then state his desire.</p>  
 <p>14. If he has obtained a chariot, he has the horses put to it, lets it face the east, and touches with the next (verse, II, 21, 17) the two wheels of the chariot or the two side-pieces.</p>  
 <p>15. With the next Ya<i>g</i>us (II, 21, 18) he should mount, and drive with the next (verse, II, 21, 19) towards the east or north, and should then drive off on his business.</p>  
 <p>16. Let him mount a horse with the next (formulas, II, 21, 20-30),</p>  
 <p>17. An elephant with the next (formula, II, 21, 31).</p>  
 <p>18. <a name="fr_793"></a><a href="#fn_793"><font size="1">18</font></a> If any harm is done him by these two (beasts), let him touch the earth as indicated above.</p>  
 <p>19. If he is going to a dispute, he takes the parasol and the staff in his left hand.</p>
