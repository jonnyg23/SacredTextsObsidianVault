 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 38</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 9.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_171"></a><a href="#fn_171"><font size="1">1</font></a> He then should take away the remnants of the Havis in a northern direction, should take them out (of the vessel in which they are), and should give them to the Brahman.</p>  
 <p>2. He should try to satiate him.</p>  
 <p>3. They say indeed with regard to sacrifice: 'Through the Brâhma<i>n</i>a's being satiated (with sacrificial food) I become satiated myself.'</p>  
 <p>4. Then (he should give to the Brahman) what other food has just become ready.</p>  
 <p>5. Then he should try to gain the favour of Brâhma<i>n</i>as by (gifts of) food.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_172"></a><a href="#fn_172"><font size="1">6</font></a> A full vessel constitutes the fee for the sacrifice; that he should give to the Brahman.</p>  
 <p>7. A brazen vessel or a wooden cup which has been filled with food, with prepared food or with raw food, or even only with fruits: this they call a full vessel.</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_173"></a><a href="#fn_173"><font size="1">8</font></a> The Brahman is the only officiating priest at the Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>as.</p>  
 <p>9. (The sacrificer) himself is Hot<i>ri</i>.</p>  
 <p>10. A full vessel (see Sûtra 7) is the lowest sacrificial fee at a Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a.</p>  
 <p>11. The highest is unlimited.</p>  
 <p>12. <a name="fr_174"></a><a href="#fn_174"><font size="1">12</font></a> Thus Sudâs Pai<i>g</i>avana, after having offered the sacrifice of a mess of cooked food to Indra and</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 39</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Agni, gave one hundred thousand (cows as the sacrificial fee).</p>  
 <p>13. Now if he should not be able to get for the morning or for the evening oblation, or for the sacrifices of the full or of the new moon at his (sacred) domestic fire, any substance fit for sacrifice or a person who could sacrifice (instead of himself, if he is prevented): what ought he to do?</p>  
 <p>14. <a name="fr_175"></a><a href="#fn_175"><font size="1">14</font></a> Until the evening oblation the (time for the) morning oblation is not elapsed, nor the (time for the) evening oblation until the morning oblation. Until the new moon the (time for the) sacrifice of the full moon is not elapsed, nor the (time for the) sacrifice of the new moon until the full moon.</p>  
 <p>15. During that interval he should try to obtain sacrificial food or to find a sacrificer.</p>  
 <p>16. <a name="fr_176"></a><a href="#fn_176"><font size="1">16</font></a> Or (if he does not succeed in this) he should cook fruits or leaves of trees or herbs which are sacrificially pure, and should sacrifice them.</p>  
 <p>17. Or he should at least sacrifice water; thus has said Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a, the son of I<i>d</i>â. For (even if he offers only water) the sacrifice has been performed.</p>  
 <p>18. <a name="fr_177"></a><a href="#fn_177"><font size="1">18</font></a> And there is an expiation for one who has not sacrificed.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 40</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. And, (says Pâkaya<i>g</i><i>ñ</i>a,) a Brâhma<i>n</i>a should not omit to keep his vow.</p>  
 <p>20. Here they say also:</p>  
 <p>21. He should keep (his vow) during that time in which he does not sacrifice, by abstaining from food.</p>  
 <p>22. <a name="fr_178"></a><a href="#fn_178"><font size="1">22</font></a> When he then has obtained (the necessary substances for sacrificing), he should make up for the (omitted) oblations.</p>  
 <p>23. For thus also his vow has been duly kept.</p>  
 <p>24. <a name="fr_179"></a><a href="#fn_179"><font size="1">24</font></a> These rules (which have been given with regard to the sacrifices of the full and new moon) are valid for the Havis oblations which will be stated hereafter.</p>  
 <p>25. <a name="fr_180"></a><a href="#fn_180"><font size="1">25</font></a> After the end of the Mantra follows the word Svâhâ.</p>  
 <p>26. <a name="fr_181"></a><a href="#fn_181"><font size="1">26</font></a> At Â<i>g</i>ya oblations he should only prepare that Â<i>g</i>ya (chap. 7, 28) and should sacrifice it, picking out portions of it. (He should) not (sacrifice) the two Â<i>g</i>ya portions nor the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t.</p>  
 <p>27. <a name="fr_182"></a><a href="#fn_182"><font size="1">27</font></a> At Â<i>g</i>ya oblations he should, if no special rule is given, sacrifice with the Mahâvyâh<i>ri</i>tis before and after (the chief ceremonies).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 41</font></a></p>  
 <p>28. <a name="fr_183"></a><a href="#fn_183"><font size="1">28</font></a> As at the wedding, thus at the tonsure (of the child's head), the initiation (of the Brahma<i>k</i>ârin), and at the cutting of the beard.</p>  
 <p>29. <a name="fr_184"></a><a href="#fn_184"><font size="1">29</font></a> At the end of the ceremony the Vâmadevya is sung for the sake of averting evil. The Vâmadevya is sung for the sake of averting evil.</p>  
 <p align="center">End of the First Prapâ<i>th</i>aka.</p>

Gobhila has used <a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 41</font></a> the word â<i>g</i>yâhutishu in the beginning of Sûtra 26, and it would have been superfluous if he had repeated it in connection with the words nâ<i>g</i>yabhâgau na svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t. In the corresponding Sûtras of the Khâdira the case was different, and there the words nâ<i>g</i>yabhâgau na svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t inevitably required the addition of a word like â<i>g</i>yâhutishu, by which to show which class of sacrifices it was which required no Â<i>g</i>yabhâgas and no Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t. The following word in the Khâdira text, however, anâde<i>s</i>e, should be referred, against tradition, to Sûtra 13, as is shown by the comparison of <i>S</i>âṅkhâyana-G<i>ri</i>hya I, 12, 13.</p>
