 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 9.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_331"><font size="1">1</font></a> On the Âgrahâya<i>n</i>î day (or the full-moon day of the month Mârga<i>s</i>îrsha) Bali-offerings (are made).</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_332"></a><a href="#fn_332"><font size="1">2</font></a> They have been explained by the <i>S</i>râva<i>n</i>a sacrifice.</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_333"></a><a href="#fn_333"><font size="1">3</font></a> He does not murmur (here) the Mantra, 'Adoration to the Earths.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 95</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. In the morning, after he has sacrificed the (regular) morning oblation, he should have the following (plants and branches of trees) fetched, viz. Darbha grass, a <i>S</i>ami (branch), Vîri<i>n</i>a grass, a (Badarî branch) with fruits, Apâmârga, and <i>S</i>irîsha. He then should silently throw (a portion) of flour of fried barley into the fire, should cause the Brâhma<i>n</i>as to pronounce auspicious wishes, and should circumambulate the house, turning his right side towards it, starting from the room for the (sacred) fire, striking the smoke (of the sacred fire) with those objects (i.e. with the plants and branches mentioned above).</p>  
 <p>5. He should throw away those objects, after he has made use of them.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_334"></a><a href="#fn_334"><font size="1">6</font></a> On solid stones he places a water-barrel with the two (Sâmans belonging to the verse), 'Vâstoshpati' (Sâma-veda I, 275) and with (that) <i>Ri</i><i>k</i> (itself).</p>  
 <p>7. Let him pour two pots of water into that barrel with this verse, 'Some assemble' (Sâma-veda-Âra<i>n</i>yaka, vol. ii, p. 292, ed. Bibl. Indica).</p>  
 <p>8. <a name="fr_335"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">8</font></a> In the evening boiled rice-grains with milk (are prepared).</p>  
 <p>9. Of that (milk-rice) he should make an oblation with (the Mantra), 'She shone forth as the first' (MB. II, 2, 1).</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_336"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">10</font></a> The rest (should be performed) according to the Sthâlîpâka rite.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. To the west of the fire he touches the Barhis with his two hands turned downwards, and murmurs the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis (i.e. the solemn utterances), 'In the Kshatra I establish myself' (ibid. 2. 3).</p>  
 <p>12. To the west of the fire he should have a layer spread out,</p>  
 <p>13. Of northward-pointed grass,</p>  
 <p>14. Inclined towards the north.</p>  
 <p>15. After they have spread out on that (grass) new rugs, the householder sits down (thereon) on the southern side.</p>  
 <p>16. Then without an interval the others according to their age,</p>  
 <p>17. And without an interval their wives, each with her children.</p>  
 <p>18. When they are seated, the householder touches the layer (of grass) with his two hands turned downwards, and murmurs the verse, 'Be soft to us, O Earth' (ibid. 4).</p>  
 <p>19. When he has finished that (verse), they lie down on their right sides.</p>  
 <p>20. <a name="fr_337"></a><a href="#fn_337"><font size="1">20</font></a> In the same way (they lie down on their right sides) three times, turning themselves towards themselves (i.e. turning round forwards, not backwards, and thus returning to their former position?).</p>  
 <p>21. They repeat the auspicious hymns as far as they know them;</p>  
 <p>22. <a name="fr_338"></a><a href="#fn_338"><font size="1">22</font></a> The complex of Sâmans called Arish<i>t</i>a, according to some (teachers).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 97</font></a></p>  
 <p>23. Having touched water, they may do whatever they like.</p>
