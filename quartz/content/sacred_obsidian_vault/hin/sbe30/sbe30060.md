 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Grihya Sutras, Part 2 (SBE30)</i>, by Hermann Oldenberg, [1892], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">PA<i>T</i>ALA 5, SECTION 17.</h3>  
 <p>1. A she-wolf (he addresses) with (the verse), 'Whether incited by others or whether on its own accord the Bhaye<i>d</i>aka (? Bhayo<i>d</i>aka, var. lect.) utters this cry, may Indra and Agni, united with Brahman, render it blissful to us in our house.'</p>  
 <p>2. <a name="fr_602"></a><a href="#fn_602"><font size="1">2</font></a> A bird (he addresses) with (the verse), 'Thou fliest, stretching out thy legs; the left eye . . .; may nothing here suffer harm (through thee);'</p>  
 <p>3. An owl (piṅgalâ) with (the verse), 'The bird with the golden wings flies to the abode of the gods. Flying round the village from left to right portend us luck by thy cry, O owl!'</p>  
 <p>4. <a name="fr_603"></a><a href="#fn_603"><font size="1">4</font></a> 'May my faculties return into me; may life return, prosperity return; may the divine power return into me; may my goods return to me.</p>  
 <p>'And may these fires that are stationed on the (altars called) Dhish<i>n</i>yâs, be in good order here, each in its right place. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'My self has returned, life has returned to me; breath has returned, design has returned to me. (Agni) Vai<i>s</i>vânara, grown strong with his rays, may he dwell in my mind, the standard of immortality. Svâhâ!</p>  
 <p>'The food which is eaten in the evening, that does</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 184</font></a></p>  
 <p>not satiate in the morning him whom hunger assails. May all that (which we have seen in our dreams), do no harm to us, for it has not been seen by day. To Day svâhâ!'with these (verses) he sacrifices sesamum seeds mixed with Â<i>g</i>ya, if he has seen a bad dream.</p>  
 <p>5. <a name="fr_604"></a><a href="#fn_604"><font size="1">5</font></a> Now the following expiations for portents are prescribed. A dove sits down on the hearth, or the bees make honey in his house, or a cow (that is not a calf) sucks another cow, or a post puts forth shoots, or an anthill has arisen (in his house): cases like these (require the following expiation):</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_605"></a><a href="#fn_605"><font size="1">6</font></a> He should bathe in the morning, should put on clean garments, should show patience (with everybody) during the day, and should speak (only) with Brâhma<i>n</i>as. Having put wood on the fire in an inner apartment, and having performed the rites down to the Vyâh<i>ri</i>ti oblations, he sacrifices with (the verses), 'This, O Varu<i>n</i>a,' &amp;c. (see above I, 2, 8, 16, down to the end of the Sûtra). Then he serves food to the Brâhma<i>n</i>as and causes them to say, 'An auspicious day! Hail! Good luck!</p>
