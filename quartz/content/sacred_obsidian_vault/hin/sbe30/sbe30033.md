 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRAPÂ<i>TH</i>AKA IV, KÂ<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 1.</h3>  
 <p>1. <a name="fr_350"></a><a href="#fn_350"><font size="1">1</font></a> He throws the two spits into the fire;</p>  
 <p>2. That which consists of one (simple) branch, towards the east, the other one towards the west.</p>  
 <p>3. <a name="fr_351"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">3</font></a> They cut off the Avadâna portions from all its limbs,</p>  
 <p>4. With the exception of the left thigh and the lungs.</p>  
 <p>5. The left thigh he should keep for the Anvash<i>t</i>akya ceremony.</p>  
 <p>6. <a name="fr_352"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">6</font></a> On the same fire he cooks one mess of rice-grains and one of meat, stirring up the one and the other separately, from left to right, with two pot-ladles.</p>  
 <p>7. After he has cooked them, he should pour (Â<i>g</i>ya) on them, should take them from the fire towards the north, and should pour (Â<i>g</i>ya) on them again.</p>  
 <p>8. Having poured the juice (of the Avadânas) into a brazen vessel,</p>  
 <p>9. And having placed the Avadânas on a layer (of grass) on which branches of the Plaksha (tree) have been spread,</p>  
 <p>10. <a name="fr_353"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">10</font></a> He cuts off (the prescribed portions) from the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Avadânas in the way prescribed for Sthâlîpâkas, (and puts those portions) into (another) brazen vessel;</p>  
 <p>11. And (the portion) for the Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t oblation separately.</p>  
 <p>12. Taking of the mess of boiled rice-grains (Sûtra 6) a portion of the size of a Bilva fruit, he should mix that, together with the Avadânas (Sûtra 10), with the juice (Sûtra 8).</p>  
 <p>13. Taking a fourfold portion of Â<i>g</i>ya he should sacrifice it with the first of the eight <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>as, 'Entering into fire, the fire' (MB. II, 2, 9-16).</p>  
 <p>14. Of the mixture (Sûtra 12) he cuts off the third part and sacrifices it with the second and third (verse).</p>  
 <p>15. He places the word Svâhâ after the second (of those verses, i.e. after the third verse of the whole Mantra).</p>  
 <p>16. In the same way he sacrifices the other two-thirds (of that mixture, the one) with the fourth and fifth (verse), and (the other) with the sixth and seventh (verse).</p>  
 <p>17. Having cut off the rest, he should sacrifice the oblation to (Agni) Svish<i>t</i>ak<i>ri</i>t with the eighth (verse).</p>  
 <p>18. Even if he be very deficient in wealth, he should celebrate (the Ash<i>t</i>akâ) with (the sacrifice of) an animal.</p>  
 <p>19. Or he should sacrifice a Sthâlîpâka.</p>  
 <p>20. <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_354"><font size="1">20</font></a> Or he should offer food to a cow.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">21</font></a> Or he should burn down brushwood in the forest and should say, 'This is my Ash<i>t</i>akâ.'</p>  
 <p>22. But let him not neglect to do (one of these things). But let him not neglect to do (one of these things).</p>
