 <p><a name="page_128"><font size="1" color="green">p. 128</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA I, HYMN 99.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA I, ADHYÂYA 8, VARGA 7.</h4>  
 <p>1. Let us press Soma for <i>G</i>âtavedas <a href="sbe46035.htm#an_1:99:1:1">1</a>. May he burn down the property of the niggard <a href="sbe46035.htm#an_1:99:1:2">2</a>. May he, Agni, bring us across all troubles, across all difficulties, as across a stream with a boat.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Ka<i>s</i>yapa Mârî<i>k</i>a. Metre, Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—Verse 1 = TÂ. X, 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:99:1:1">1</a>. This is one of the very rare passages in which Agni standing alone and not accompanied by Indra or the Maruts &amp;c. is mentioned as drinking Soma. It seems as if this verse were not composed for the regular Soma sacrifice, but for a special occasion.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_1:99:1:2">2</a>. Cf. Delbrück, Syntakt. Forschungen, I, 112.</p>
