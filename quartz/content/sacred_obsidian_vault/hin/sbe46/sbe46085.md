 <p><a name="page_291"><font size="1" color="green">p. 291</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 25.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 25.</h4>  
 <p>1. Agni, thou art for ever the wise son of Heaven and of the Earth, the all-wealthy one. In thy peculiar way <a href="sbe46085.htm#an_3:25:1:1">1</a> sacrifice here to the gods, O intelligent one!</p>  
 <p>2. Agni, the knowing, obtains (for his worshipper) heroic powers; he obtains (for him) strength, being busy for the sake of immortality. Bring then the gods hither, O (Agni), rich in food.</p>  
 <p>3. Agni, the wise, shines on Heaven and Earth, the two immortal goddesses who encompass all people—he who rules through his strength, and who is full of light through adoration.</p>  
 <p>4. Agni and Indra, come hither to the sacrifice in the house of the worshipper rich in pressed (Soma), never failing, ye two gods, at the drink of Soma.</p>  
 <p>5. Agni, thou art kindled in the house of the waters, (our) own (god), O son of strength, <i>G</i>âtavedas, who exaltest the abodes (in which thou dwellest) by thy blessing.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. Metre, Virâ<i>g</i>.—Verse 4 = MS. IV, 12, 6.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:25:1:1">1</a>. On <i>rí</i>dhak, see Pischel, Vedische Studien, II, 45.</p>
