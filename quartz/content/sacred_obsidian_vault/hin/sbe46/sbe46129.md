 <p><a name="page_416"><font size="1" color="green">p. 416</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 25.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 17–18.</h4>  
 <p>1. Address thy song <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:1:1">1</a> for your sake to the god Agni, for his help. He is our Vasu. May the son of the dawns(?) <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:1:2">2</a> give us (wealth). May the righteous one help us across our enemies.</p>  
 <p>2. He is the true one, whom the men of old, whom the gods have kindled, the Hot<i>ri</i> with the delightful tongue, rich in splendour with glorious shine.</p>  
 <p>3. As such, with thy widest thought and with thy best favour, shine wealth on us, excellent Agni, for our beautiful praises <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:3:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>4. Agni reigns among the gods, Agni among mortals, entering among them. Agni is the carrier of our offerings. Serve ye Agni with prayers!</p>  
 <p>5. Agni gives to the worshipper a son most mightily renowned, a knower of mighty spells, most excellent, unconquered, who brings renown to his lord <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:5:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>6. Agni gives a good lord who is victorious in battles with his men; Agni (gives) a steed, swiftly running, victorious (in races), unconquered.</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>7. Sing mightily to Agni the (song) which may best bring him (to us), O (god) rich in splendour <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:7:1">1</a>! From thee (proceeds) wealth (mighty) like a buffalo-cow <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:7:2">2</a>; from thee proceed gains.</p>  
 <p>8. Thy brilliant flames resound mightily like the pressing-stone (of the Soma) <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:8:1">1</a>. And thy roaring arose like thunder by itself from heaven <a href="sbe46129.htm#an_5:25:8:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_417"><font size="1" color="green">p. 417</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Thus we have paid homage, desirous of goods, to powerful Agni. May he, the highly wise one, help us, as with a ship, across all enemies.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shis are the Vasûyava<i>h</i> Âtreyâ<i>h</i> (cf. verse 9); the metre is Anush<i>t</i>ubh. Verse 5 = MS. IV, 11, 1. Verse 6 = MS. IV, 11, 1. Verse 7 = SV. I, 86; VS. XXVI, 12; TS. I, 1, 14, 4. The Sûkta consists of hymns of three verses each.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:1:1">1</a>. On gâsi, comp. Neisser, Bezzenberger's Beiträge, XX, 70, note 1; Bartholomae, Indogermanische Forschungen, II, 278, 283.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:1:2">2</a>. <i>Ri</i>shû<i>n</i>â´m: comp. above, I, 127, 10, note 5.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:3:1">1</a>. On suv<i>ri</i>ktí, comp. above, II, 4, 1, note 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:5:1">1</a>. I. e. to his father? Or to his patrons?</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 7.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:7:1">1</a>. This vocative very probably refers to Agni.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:7:2">2</a>. Or 'like a king's consort'? It may be doubted whether the difference of accent (máhishî and mahishî´) holds good for the Rig-veda.—Comp. on máhishîva, Roth, Zeitschr. der Deutschen Morgenländ. Gesellschaft, XLVIII, 680.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 8.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:8:1">1</a>. B<i>ri</i>hat is not the name of the Sâman; cf. X, 64, 15 (100, 8). grâ´vâ yátra madhu-sút u<i>k</i>yáte b<i>ri</i>hát. Comp. Hillebrandt, Vedische Mythologie, I, p. 153.—The singular u<i>k</i>yate is explained by the connection with grâ´vâ.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:25:8:2">2</a>. With the last Pâda comp. the conclusion of V, 52, 6 (vol. xxxii, p. 312).</p>
