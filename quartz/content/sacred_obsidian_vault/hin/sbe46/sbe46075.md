 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="green">p. 271</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 15.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 15.</h4>  
 <p>1. Flaming with thy broad stream of light beat away fiends, sorcerers, plagues. May I dwell in the protection of the great, well-protecting (god), under the guidance of Agni who readily listens to our call.</p>  
 <p>2. Be thou our protector when this dawn shines forth, he thou (our protector) when the sun has risen. Cherish, O Agni, well-born in body, this praise of mine as (a man rejoices) in the birth (of a son), in his own offspring <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:2:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>3. Beholding men, shine thou after many (dawns) <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:3:1">1</a>, O bull, Agni, red in the dark (nights). O Vasu! Lead us and bring us across anguish. Help us, the U<i>s</i>i<i>g</i>s <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:3:2">2</a>, to wealth <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:3:3">3</a>, thou youngest (of the gods)!</p>  
 <p>4. Shine, O Agni, thou the invincible bull, who hast conquered all strongholds and all delights, the leader of the first, the protecting <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:4:1">1</a>, mighty sacrifice, O <i>G</i>âtavedas, best guide.</p>  
 <p>5. O singer, thou who art wise, brightly shining towards the gods <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:5:1">1</a>, bring to us thy many perfect shelters, and gain like a victorious car <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:5:2">2</a>; Agni, (carry) thou (hither) towards us the two well-established <a href="sbe46075.htm#an_3:15:5:3">3</a> worlds (Heaven and Earth).</p>  
 <p>6. O bull, increase and rouse our gains. Agni! (Increase) for us the two worlds (Heaven and Earth) rich in milk, O god together with the gods, shining with beautiful shine! May a mortal's hatred never enclose us.</p>  
 <p>7 = III, 1, 23.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="green">p. 272</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Utkîla Kâtya, the metre Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—Verse 1 = VS. XI, 49; TS. IV, 1, 5, 1; MS. II, 7, 5; III, 1, 6.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:2:1">1</a>. Comp. VII, 1, 21. tánaye nítye; X, 39, 14. nítyam ná sûnúm tánayam dádhânâ<i>h</i>, and besides II, 26, 3. <i>g</i>ánena … vi<i>s</i>â´ … <i>g</i>ánmanâ … putraí<i>h</i>; Hirzel, Gleichnisse und Metaphern im Rig-veda, 77.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:3:1">1</a>. For this expression, compare IV, 19, 8; IX, 71, 7; X, 31, 7, and especially III, 6, 7; VI, 39, 4.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:3:2">2</a>. The poet compares himself and his friends with the mythical priestly tribe of the U<i>s</i>i<i>g</i>s (Bergaigne, Religion Védique, I, 57 seq.), using, as it seems, at the same time the word u<i>s</i>í<i>g</i>a<i>h</i> in its adjective sense 'the willing ones.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:3:3">3</a>. The Padapâ´<i>th</i>a is right in giving râyé; comp. VIII, 26, 13. <i>s</i>ubhé <i>k</i>akrâte.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:4:1">1</a>. Is the text correct? I think that pâyó<i>h</i> should be corrected into pâyo or pâyú<i>h</i>: 'the leader and protector of the first mighty sacrifice.' The mistake may have been caused by the genitives which surround the word.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:5:1">1</a>. Geldner (Vedische Studien, I, 160) translates this hemistich: 'Die vielen sicheren Zufluchtsorte (= Opferplätze) bis zu den Göttern erleuchtend als Weiser, o Sänger.' I do not believe that <i>s</i>árma is the object of dî´dyâna<i>h</i>; and 'Zufluchtsorte = Opferplätze' is too much in the style of Sâya<i>n</i>a. I take á<i>k</i><i>kh</i>idrâ <i>s</i>árma as depending on abhí vakshi; comp. I, 34, 6. tridhâ´tu <i>s</i>árma vahatam.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:5:2">2</a>. For abhí vakshi vâ´<i>g</i>am, comp. III, 30, 11; VI, 21, 12.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:15:5:3">3</a>. On suméka, comp. Windisch, Festgruss an Boehtlingk, 114.</p>
