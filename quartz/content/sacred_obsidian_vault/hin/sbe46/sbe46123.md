 <p><a name="page_407"><font size="1" color="green">p. 407</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 19.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 11.</h4>  
 <p>1. They are born for retirement <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:1:1">1</a>. Out of the cover he <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:1:2">2</a> has shone forth, being a cover himself. In the lap of the mother he looks about <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:1:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p>2. Causing him to discern (the pious and the impious?), they have sacrificed. With unwinking eyes they protect his manly power. They have penetrated into the firm stronghold <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:2:1">1</a>.</p>  
 <p>3. The people of <i>S</i>vaitreya <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:3:1">1</a>, his clans, have thriven brilliantly. B<i>ri</i>haduktha with a golden ornament at his neck, is eager for the race as if by this honey-drink <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:3:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>4. Like the dear milk of love <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:4:1">1</a>—(a thing) unrelated with two (things) related <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:4:2">2</a>—like the gharma vessel with booty in its belly—undeceived, the deceiver of all <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:4:3">3</a>.</p>  
 <p>5. Sporting, O beam of light, appear to us, joined with the ash, with the wind. May those well sharpened … of his, standing on …, be sharp like… <a href="sbe46123.htm#an_5:19:5:1">1</a>.</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Vavri Âtreya (cf. verse 1. prá vavré<i>h</i> vavrí<i>h</i> <i>k</i>iketa). The metre is Gâyatrî in verses 1, 2, Anush<i>t</i>ubh in verses 3, 4, Virâ<i>d</i>rûpâ in verse 5.—No verse occurs in the other Sa<i>m</i>hitâs.</p>  
 <p>This Sûkta seems to be anything rather than an ordinary Agni hymn. It may be a collection of verses belonging to an Âkhyâna, or of verses serving another purpose which we can scarcely hope to discover. In several parts of this</p>  
 <p><a name="page_408"><font size="1" color="green">p. 408</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Sûkta I must content myself with translating the words without being able to elucidate the poet's meaning.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:1:1">1</a>. I translate the noun avasthâ´ in accordance with the Vedic meaning of the verb ava-sthâ. Possibly it means the secret parts, cf. avastha, AV. VII, 90, 3 (B.-R.). Ludwig translates: 'Ein zustand erzeugt einen andern,' and paraphrases, 'Nur zustände und formen, gestalten lernen wir kennen, das wesen des gottes bleibt uns verborgen.' This seems too modern. Prof. Max Müller proposes: 'The remnants (afterbirth) have been brought forth. Skin has shone forth from skin.'—On the question who are the beings 'born for retirement,' I do not venture any conjecture.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:1:2">2</a>. Is Agni meant?</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:1:3">3</a>. Cf. X, 5, 1. (Agní<i>h</i>) asmát hridá<i>h</i> bhû´ri<i>g</i>anmâ ví <i>k</i>ash<i>t</i>e.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:2:1">1</a>. The meaning seems to be that the worshippers (possibly the first worshippers, the Aṅgiras), by discovering Agni and by worshipping him, have conquered the hostile strongholds.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:3:1">1</a>. <i>S</i>vaitreya is mentioned as a victorious hero also in I, 33, 14.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:3:2">2</a>. Does this phrase allude to the rite of offering, at the Vâ<i>g</i>apeya sacrifice, to the horses that were going to run the sacred race, a naivâra <i>k</i>aru? In the Mantras connected with this rite the words occur: 'Drink of this honey-drink' (asyá mádhva<i>h</i> pibata). See Rig-veda VII, 38, 8; Taittirîya Sa<i>m</i>hitâ I, 7, 8, 2; Weber, Ueber den Vâ<i>g</i>apeya, p. 30.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:4:1">1</a>. The retas?</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:4:2">2</a>. Does this refer to an offering or the like, composed of two substances related among each other (such as</p>  
 <p><a name="page_409"><font size="1" color="green">p. 409</font></a></p>  
 <p>milk and butter), and a third substance unrelated (such as rice)? Of course all this is absolutely uncertain.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:4:3">3</a>. Is this Agni?</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:19:5:1">1</a>. The meaning of dh<i>ri</i>shá<i>g</i>, vakshî´, vaksha<i>n</i>esthâ´ is unknown.—On the first hemistich of this verse, compare Pischel Vedische Studien, II, 54.</p>
