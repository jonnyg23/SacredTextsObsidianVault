 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="green">p. 273</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 16.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 16.</h4>  
 <p>1. This Agni rules over abundance in valiant men, over great happiness. He rules over wealth consisting in offspring and cows; he rules over the killing of foes.</p>  
 <p>2. O Maruts <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:2:1">1</a>, ye men, cling to this furtherer <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:2:2">2</a> who possesses joy-furthering boons—(the Maruts) who <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:2:3">3</a> in battles overcome ill-minded (foes), who have deceived the enemy <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:2:4">4</a> day by day.</p>  
 <p>3. As such, O bounteous Agni, prepare <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:3:1">1</a> us riches <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:3:2">2</a> and wealth in valiant men, which, O highly glorious one, may be most exalted, rich in offspring, free from plagues, and powerful.</p>  
 <p>4. The maker who victoriously (stands) over all beings, the maker who makes the praise arrive among the gods <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:4:1">1</a>: he stands firm among the gods, among the host of heroes, firm also in the praise of men.</p>  
 <p>5. Give us not up, Agni, to want of thought <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:5:1">1</a> nor to want of heroes nor to want of cows, O son of strength, nor to the scoffer. Drive away hostile powers <a href="sbe46076.htm#an_3:16:5:2">2</a>.</p>  
 <p>6. Help us at this sacrifice, O blessed one, with mighty gain which is accompanied by offspring, O Agni! Let us be united with greater, gladdening, glorious wealth, O thou of mighty splendour!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_274"><font size="1" color="green">p. 274</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. The metre is Pragâtha, each Pragâtha distich being composed of one B<i>ri</i>hatî and one Satob<i>ri</i>hatî. The position of the Sûkta in the collection and the opening words of verse 3 show that the three Pragâthas are not independent, but form one hymn.—Verse 1 = SV. I, 60.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:2:1">1</a>. Comp. VII, 18, 25. imám nara<i>h</i> maruta<i>h</i> sa<i>s</i><i>k</i>ata ánu.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:2:2">2</a>. The passages which Grassmann gives for the meaning of v<i>rí</i>dh, 'stärkend, erquickend,' I, 167, 4; X, 89, 10, are quite doubtful. Probably we should have to alter the accent and read v<i>ri</i>dhám.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:2:3">3</a>. The relative clause seems to refer to the Maruts, not to the goods (râ´ya<i>h</i>).</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:2:4">4</a>. Comp. VI, 46, 10. yé … <i>s</i>átrum âdabhû<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:3:1">1</a>. Literally, 'sharpen.'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:3:2">2</a>. The genitive seems, as is also Prof. Ludwig's opinion, to be the partitive genitive, so that the literal meaning would be: 'Prepare us (a deal) of riches and of wealth,' &amp;c. Comp. píba sutásya, 'drink of the pressed Soma,' &amp;c.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:4:1">1</a>. On <i>k</i>ákri<i>h</i> devéshu â´ dúva<i>h</i>, comp. IV, 2, 9; VIII, 31, 9.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:5:1">1</a>. On ámati, comp. above, III, 8, 2, note.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:16:5:2">2</a>. Comp. VI, 59, 8. ápa dvéshâ<i>m</i>si â´ k<i>ri</i>tam.</p>
