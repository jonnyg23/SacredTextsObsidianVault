 <p><a name="page_268"><font size="1" color="green">p. 268</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA III, HYMN 14.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 14.</h4>  
 <p>1. The joy-giving Hot<i>ri</i> has taken his place at the sacrifices <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:1:1">1</a>, He the true, the sacrificer, the highest sage, the worshipper. Agni whose chariot is lightning, the son of strength, whose hair is flame, has spread forth his light over the earth.</p>  
 <p>2. It <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:2:1">1</a> has been offered to thee—be pleased with the adoring speech <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:2:2">2</a>—to thee who is observant of it, O righteous, strong one. Bring hither thou who art wise, the wise (gods). Sit down on the sacrificial grass in the middle (of it) for bliss, O worshipful one!</p>  
 <p>3. To thee, Agni, Night and Dawn who further thy strength <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:3:1">1</a>, shall hasten on the paths of the wind. When (the mortals) anoint the ancient one <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:3:2">2</a> with offerings, they <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:3:3">3</a> stand in the house as on a chariot-seat <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:3:4">4</a>.</p>  
 <p>4. Mitra and Varu<i>n</i>a, O strong Agni, and all the Maruts shall sing to thee a pleasant song, when thou, O son of strength, standest with thy flames, a sun spreading out men <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:4:1">1</a> over the (terrestrial) dwellings.</p>  
 <p>5. We have given thee thy desire to-day, sitting down near thee adoringly with outstretched hands <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:5:1">1</a>: sacrifice thou to the gods as a priest with thy mind most skilled in sacrifice, with unerring thoughts, O Agni!</p>  
 <p>6. From thee indeed, O son of strength, proceed manifold divine blessings and gains <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:6:1">1</a>. Give us thousandfold true wealth according to thy guileless word, O Agni!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_269"><font size="1" color="green">p. 269</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. What we have done here for thee at this sacrifice, we mortals, O skilful and thoughtful god, take thou notice of all that, O (god) with the good chariot <a href="sbe46074.htm#an_3:14:7:1">1</a>; make all this (sacrificial food) here savoury, immortal Agni!</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. The metre is Trish<i>t</i>ubh.—Verse 5 = VS. XVIII, 75.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 1.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:1:1">1</a>. On vidátha, comp. I, 31, 6, note 2.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 2.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:2:1">1</a>. The subject to be supplied seems to be náma<i>h</i>-ukti<i>h</i>.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:2:2">2</a>. The words náma<i>h</i>-uktim <i>g</i>ushasva form a parenthesis, as Ludwig has seen.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:3:1">1</a>. It is possible that here, as in several other passages, a confusion between the two verbs vâ<i>g</i>áyati and vâ<i>g</i>ayáti has taken place. If the reading were vâ<i>g</i>ayántî, we should have to translate, 'Night and Dawn who are striving together (as if running a race against each other?).'</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:3:2">2</a>. The ancient one is Agni.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:3:3">3</a>. The two goddesses, Night and Dawn.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:3:4">4</a>. The Padapâ<i>th</i>a has vandhúrâ-iva, which may be the dual of vandhúr (I, 34, 9). But more probably it should be vandhúre-iva (nom. dual, neuter or loc. sing.), comp. I, 64, 9. â´ vandhúreshu … tasthau; I, 139, 4. ádhi vâm sthâ´ma vandhúre; III, 43, 1. vandhuresh<i>th</i>â´<i>h</i>, and see III, 6, 10. adhvaréva. On contracted Prag<i>ri</i>hya vowels, see H. O., Die Hymnen des Rig-veda, I, p. 456.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_270"><font size="1" color="green">p. 270</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:4:1">1</a>. On n<i>rî´</i>n and the different theories proposed for this word, see above, I, 146, 4, note 5.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:5:1">1</a>. Comp. X, 79, 2. uttânáhastâ<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:6:1">1</a>. For this hemistich, comp. VI, 13, 1; 34, 1.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 7.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_3:14:7:1">1</a>. The traditional text has tvám ví<i>s</i>vasya suráthasya bodhi, which can only mean, 'take thou notice of every one who has a good chariot'—which Bergaigne (Quelques observations sur les figures de rhétorique dans le Rig-veda, p. 15) explains: 'Le char en question est la prière qui amène le dieu au sacrifice.' I believe that the text is corrupt; instead of suráthasya I think we should read surathâsya (= suratha asya).</p>
