 <p><a name="page_379"><font size="1" color="green">p. 379</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 6.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA III, ADHYÂYA 8, VARGA 22–23.</h4>  
 <p>1. I think of that Agni who is a Vasu, to whom the milch-cows go home, the swift horses (go) home, (our) own racers (go) home. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>2. He is Agni who is praised as the Vasu, he to whom the milch-cows come together, and the quickly running horses, and the well-born liberal patrons. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>3. For Agni, dwelling among all tribes, gives a racer to the clan. Agni (gives a racer) that is truly helpful for (winning) wealth <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:3:1">1</a>: he (the racer) being well cherished, will attain precious gain. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>4. May we kindle thee <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:4:1">1</a>, Agni, O god, the brilliant, never ageing, in order that yon highly miraculous fuel of thine <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:4:2">2</a> may shine in the sky. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>5. To thee <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:5:1">1</a>, O Agni, our oblation is offered with a <i>Ri</i><i>k</i>, O lord of bright splendour, highly brilliant, wonderful lord of the clan, carrier of oblations! Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>6. Those Agnis make everything precious prosper in the Agnis; they drive forward (precious wealth); they incite it; they speed it hither in the due way <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:6:1">1</a>. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>7. Those flames of thine, O Agni, the racers, have boasted mightily—they who with the flight of their</p>  
 <p><a name="page_380"><font size="1" color="green">p. 380</font></a></p>  
 <p>hoofs have made tremble <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:7:1">1</a> the stables of the cows. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>8. Bring fresh food with fine dwellings, O Agni, to us, thy praisers! May we be of those who have praised (thee), who have thee as their messenger, house by house. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>9. Thou warmest in thy mouth, O highly brilliant one, the two (sacrificial) ladles full of butter. And mayst thou fill us (with gifts) at our hymns, O lord of strength! Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  
 <p>10. Thus <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:10:1">1</a> they have driven, they have led <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:10:2">2</a>, Agni in the due way by prayers and sacrifices. May he bestow on us plenty of valiant men, and that plenty of swift horses (wished for) <a href="sbe46110.htm#an_5:6:10:3">3</a>. Bring food to thy praisers!</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The same <i>Ri</i>shi. The metre is Paṅkti.—Verse 1 = SV. I, 425. Verses 1–2 = VS. XV, 41, 42; MS. II, 13, 7. Verses 1, 3, 2 = SV. II, 1087–1089. Verse 3 = TB. III, 11, 6, 4. Verse 4 = SV. I, 419; AV. XVIII, 4, 88; MS. II, 13, 7. Verses 4, 5, 9 = SV. II, 372–374; TS. IV, 4, 4, 6. Verse 9 = VS. XV, 43; TS. II, 2, 12, 7.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 3.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:3:1">1</a>. At first sight the conjecture of Böhtlingk-Roth and Grassmann, rayím, is very tempting, cf. IX, 12, 9. rayím … su-âbhúvam; X, 122, 3. rayí<i>n</i>â … su-âbúvâ. I believe, nevertheless, that on closer examination the traditional text will prove correct. Sá prîtá<i>h</i> evidently refers to the racer (vâ<i>g</i>ín) cf. I, 66, 4 = 69, 5. vâ<i>g</i>î´ ná prîtá<i>h</i> (cf. also X, 101, 7. prî<i>n</i>îtá á<i>s</i>vân): then it follows that su-âbhúvam also refers to the racer, and râyé (cf. I, 100, 16; III, 53, 16) will be quite right.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_381"><font size="1" color="green">p. 381</font></a></p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:4:1">1</a>. Te stands for the accusative; see Pischel, Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenländ. Gesellschaft, XXXV, 715; Delbrück, Altindische Syntax, p. 205.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:4:2">2</a>. This refers to the sun. By kindling the sacred fire men make the sun rise. See H. O., Religion des Veda, p. 110.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 5.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:5:1">1</a>. The pronoun 'to thee' stands twice, to in the first Pâda (where it is repeated from the first Pâda of verse 4, â´ te agne), and túbhyam (or rather túbhya) in the fourth Pâda, unless we construe to havi<i>h</i>.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 6.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:6:1">1</a>. See Pischel, Vedische Studien, II, 127.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 7.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:7:1">1</a>. See Gaedicke, Der Accusativ, p. 57.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 10.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:10:1">1</a>. On the nasalization of evâ´n̐, cf. H. O., Prolegomena, p. 469 seq.</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:10:2">2</a>. Pischel (Vedische Studien, II, 127) explains a<i>g</i>u<i>h</i> as a<i>g</i>ush, the contrary of sa<i>g</i>û´sh. Bartholomae (Studien zur Indogermanischen Sprachgeschichte, II, 159, note 2; cf. Indogermanische Forschungen, III, 108, note I) conjectures á<i>g</i>ur (= á<i>g</i>man) yamu<i>h</i>: 'sie haben ihn jetzt auf seiner Bahn festgehalten.' I believe, as Sâya<i>n</i>a does, that this a<i>g</i>uryamu<i>h</i> contains two independent verbs, a<i>g</i>u<i>h</i> and yamu<i>h</i>, which are quite correct forms of the roots a<i>g</i> and yam (see Delbrück, Altindisches Verbum, p. 65). As to a<i>g</i>, cf. VI, 2, 8. a<i>g</i>yáse ágne vâ<i>g</i>î´ ná; V, 30, 14. átya<i>h</i> ná vâ<i>g</i>î´ raghú<i>h</i> a<i>g</i>yámâna<i>h</i>; as to yam II, 5, 1. <i>s</i>akéma vâ<i>g</i>ína<i>h</i> (i. e. agné<i>h</i>) yámam. But should not the accent be yamú<i>h</i>?</p>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:6:10:3">3</a>. The fourth Pâda is identical with VIII, 6, 24.</p>
