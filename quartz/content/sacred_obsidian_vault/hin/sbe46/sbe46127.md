 <p><a name="page_414"><font size="1" color="green">p. 414</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MA<i>N</i><i>D</i>ALA V, HYMN 23.</h3>  <h4 align="center">ASH<i>T</i>AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 15.</h4>  
 <p>1. Agni, bring hither, through the power of thy splendour, powerful wealth which may manifestly prevail over all tribes in the (contests for) booty.</p>  
 <p>2. O powerful Agni! Bring hither that wealth powerful in battles. For thou art the true, wonderful giver of booty rich in cows.</p>  
 <p>3. For all men who have spread out the sacrificial grass, unanimously ask thee, the beloved Hot<i>ri</i> in the seats (of sacrifice), for many boons.</p>  
 <p>4. For he who dwells among all tribes, has invested himself with power against assault <a href="sbe46127.htm#an_5:23:4:1">1</a>. Agni! In these dwelling-places shine to us richly, O bright one, shine brilliantly, O purifier!</p>  <h4 align="center">NOTES.</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Ri</i>shi is Dyumna Vi<i>s</i>va<i>k</i>arsha<i>n</i>i Âtreya (cf. verse 1); the metre is the same.—Verses 1–2 = TS. I, 3, 14, 6–7.</p>  <h5 align="center">Verse 4.</h5>  
 <p>Note <a name="an_5:23:4:1">1</a>. Is abhímâti (abhímâtî?) a dative? Should we read abhimâti-sahá<i>h</i> (cf. X, 83, 4) as a compound: 'he has been established as the conqueror of assaults.'</p>
