 <p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 184</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, ADHYÂYA 5, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 12.</h3>  
 <p>1. Tame animals must not be eaten, <a name="fr_919"></a><a href="#fn_919"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Nor carnivorous and (tame) birds, <a name="fr_920"></a><a href="#fn_920"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Nor (tame) cocks and pigs; <a name="fr_921"></a><a href="#fn_921"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Goats and sheep (are) excepted (from the above prohibition).</p>  
 <p>5. Five five-toed animals may be eaten, (viz.) the porcupine, the iguana, the hare, the hedgehog, the tortoise and the rhinoceros, excepting the rhinoceros, <a name="fr_922"></a><a href="#fn_922"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Likewise five animals with cloven hoofs, (viz.) the white-footed antelope (Nîl-gâi), the (common ravine) deer, the spotted deer, the buffalo, the (wild) boar and the black antelope, excepting the black antelope, <a name="fr_923"></a><a href="#fn_923"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. (Likewise) five (kinds of) birds that feed scratching with their feet, (viz.) the partridge, the blue rock-pigeon, the francoline partridge, the (crane called) Vârdhrâ<i>n</i>asa, the peacock and the Vâra<i>n</i>a, excepting the Vâra<i>n</i>a, <a name="fr_924"></a><a href="#fn_924"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p>8. (And the following) fishes, (viz.) the Silurus Pelorius (Sahasrada<i>m</i>sh<i>t</i>rin), the <i>K</i>ili<i>k</i>ima, the Varmi, the B<i>ri</i>ha<i>k</i><i>kh</i>iras, the Ma<i>s</i>akari(?), the Cyprinus Rohita, and the Râ<i>g</i>i. <a name="fr_925"></a><a href="#fn_925"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. The milk of a (female animal) whose offspring is not ten days old, and of one that gives milk while big with a young one, must not be drunk, <a name="fr_926"></a><a href="#fn_926"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Nor that of a (cow) that has no calf or that (suckles) a strange calf.</p>  
 <p>11. (The milk) of sheep, camels, and one-hoofed animals must not be drunk. <a name="fr_927"></a><a href="#fn_927"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. If (he has) drunk (milk) which ought not to be drunk, excepting cow's milk, (he must perform) a K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra (penance). <a name="fr_928"></a><a href="#fn_928"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. But if (he has drunk) cow's milk (that is unfit for use, he shall) fast during three (days and) nights.</p>  
 <p>14. Stale (food must not be eaten or drunk) excepting pot-herbs, broths, meat, clarified butter, cooked grain, molasses, sour milk, and barley-meal, <a name="fr_929"></a><a href="#fn_929"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Nor (substances) which have turned sour, nor molasses which have come into that state. <a name="fr_930"></a><a href="#fn_930"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. After performing the ceremony preparatory <a name="fr_931"></a><a href="#fn_931"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>

 <p>to the beginning of the Veda-study (upâkarman) on the (full moon of the month) of <i>S</i>râva<i>n</i>a or of Ashâ<i>dh</i>a, they shall close the term on the full moon of Taisha or Mâgha.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_919"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_919">184:1</a> <b>12</b>. Vasish<i>th</i>a XIV, 40.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_920"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_920">184:2</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XIV, 48. Govinda says that the particle <i>k</i>a, 'and,' is used in order to indicate that the word 'tame' must be understood.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_921"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_921">184:3</a> Âpastamba I, 5, 17, 29, 32.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_922"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_922">184:5</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XIV, 39. Another explanation of the word <i>s</i>vâvi<i>t</i>, 'the porcupine' (see also Gautama XVII, 27), is given in the commentary, which says that it is a wild animal resembling a dog, and belonging to the boar species. Govinda points out that there is a dispute among the learned regarding the rhinoceros (Vasish<i>th</i>a XV, 47), and that the peculiar wording of the Sûtra is intended to indicate that.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_923"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_923">184:6</a> The permissibility of the last-named animal is again doubtful.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_924"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_924">184:7</a> Gautama XVII, 35. The case of the last-mentioned bird, the Vâra<i>n</i>a, is again doubtful. From the first rock-edict of A<i>s</i>oka <a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 185</font></a> it appears that peacocks, now considered inviolable, were actually eaten in the third century A. D.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_925"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_925">185:8</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XIV, 41-42. The names are much corrupted in the MSS., and for Ma<i>s</i>akari, which I do not find in the dictionaries, Sama<i>s</i>akari or Samasakari is also read. The B<i>ri</i>ha<i>k</i><i>kh</i>iras is probably the Indian salmon, the Mâhsir.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_926"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_926">185:9</a>-10. Vasish<i>th</i>a XIV, 34-35 Gautama XVII, 22. The meaning of sandhinî, 'a female animal that gives milk while big with young,' is uncertain. See also Vish<i>n</i>u LI, 40 Âpastamba I, 5, 17, 23.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_927"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_927">185:11</a> Gautama XVII, 24.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_928"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_928">185:12</a> Vish<i>n</i>u LI, 38-41.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_929"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_929">185:14</a> Gautama XVII, 16.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_930"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_930">185:15</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XIV, 37-38.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_931"></a><a href="sbe1445.htm#fr_931">185:16</a> Vasish<i>th</i>a XIII, 1-5. Govinda states that this Sûtra has been introduced here, because the purity of one's food ensures <a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 186</font></a> purity of one's soul, and purity of soul gives strength of memory, and thereby makes one fit to study the Veda.</p>
