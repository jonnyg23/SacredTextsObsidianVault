   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 5, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now, after washing his hands, he shall take his waterpot and a clod of earth, go to a (sacred) bathing-place and thrice clean his feet (with earth and water) and thrice his body. <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1226"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Now some say, 'One must not enter a burial-ground, water, a temple, a cowpen, nor a place where Brâhma<i>n</i>as (sit) without having cleaned one's feet.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_250"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 250</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Then he enters the water, (reciting the following verse): 'I take refuge with gold-horned Varu<i>n</i>a, give me at my request (O Varu<i>n</i>a) a purifying bathing-place. May Indra, Varu<i>n</i>a, B<i>ri</i>haspati, and Savit<i>ri</i> again and again cleanse me from all sin which I have committed by eating the food of unholy men, by receiving gifts from the wicked, and from all evil which I have done by thoughts, speeches, or deeds.' <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1227"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Then he takes up water in his joined hands, (saying), 'May the waters and the herbs be propitious to us.' <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1228"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. (Next) he pours (the water) out in that direction in which an enemy of his dwells, (saying), 'May they work woe to him who hates us and whom we hate.' <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="#fn_1229"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Then he sips water, and thrice makes the water eddy around himself turning from the left to the right (and saying), 'May that which is hurtful, which is impure, and which is inauspicious in the water be removed.' <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="#fn_1230"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. After having submerged himself and having emerged from the water, <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. (Acts of) personal purification, washing the clothes by beating them on a stone and sipping <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_251"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 251</font></a></p>  
 <p>water are not (permitted to the worshipper) as long as he is in the water.</p>  
 <p>9. If (the water used for bathing) has been (taken from a) confined (place, such as a well), he worships it with the following (Mantra): 'Adoration to Agni, the lord of the waters; adoration to Indra; adoration to Varu<i>n</i>a; adoration to Vâru<i>n</i>î; adoration to the waters.' <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. After having ascended the bank and having sipped water, let him again sip water, though he has done so before, (and recite the following Mantras) 'May water purify the earth, may the purified earth purify me, may Brahma<i>n</i>aspati (and) Brahman purify, may the purified (earth) purify me. May water purify me, (taking away) all (the guilt which I incurred by eating) remnants of food, and forbidden food, (by committing) evil deeds, (by) receiving gifts from wicked men, Svâhâ!' <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. Making two Pavitras he rubs (his body) with water. Having rubbed himself, (reciting the) three (verses), 'Ye waters are,' &amp;c., the four (verses), 'The golden-coloured, pure, purifying,' &amp;c., (and) the Anuvâka, 'He who purifies,' &amp;c., he performs, stepping back into the water, three Prâ<i>n</i>âyâmas with the Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a (hymn); then he ascends the bank, squeezes (the water) out of his dress, puts on garments which have been washed and dried in the air and which are not the worse for wear, sips water, <a name="fr_1235"></a><a href="#fn_1235"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 252</font></a></p>  
 <p>sits down on Darbha grass, and, holding Darbha grass (in his hands), recites, facing the east, the Gâyatrî one thousand times, (or) one hundred times, or any number of times, or at least twelve times.</p>  
 <p>12. Then he worships the sun (reciting the following Mantras): 'Out of darkness we,' &amp;c., 'Up that bright,' &amp;c., That eye which is beneficial to the gods,' &amp;c., (and) 'He who rose,' &amp;c. <a name="fr_1236"></a><a href="#fn_1236"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. Now they quote also (the following maxim): 'The syllable Om,, the Vyâh<i>ri</i>tis, and the Sâvitrî, these five Veda-offerings daily cleanse the Brâhma<i>n</i>a from guilt.'</p>  
 <p>14. Being purified by the five Veda-offerings, he next satiates the gods (with water, saying), <a name="fr_1237"></a><a href="#fn_1237"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1226"></a><a href="sbe1462.htm#fr_1226">249:1</a> <b>8</b>. Vish<i>n</i>u LXIV, 18. This Adhyâya contains the rules for bathing, and the subject is introduced, as Govinda observes, because in the preceding chapter II, 4, 7, 2, it has been said that an impure person must bathe before he performs the twilight devotions. Govinda also states that the word <i>k</i>a, 'and,' which stands after m<i>ri</i>tpi<i>n</i><i>d</i>am, 'a clod,' indicates that gomaya, 'cowdung,' must also be employed.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1229"></a><a href="sbe1462.htm#fr_1229">250:5</a> Taittirîya Âra<i>n</i>yaka, loc. cit. This and the following Sutras, down to II, 6, 11, 15, are wanting in the Gu<i>g</i>arât and Dekhan MSS. except in K.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1235"></a><a href="sbe1462.htm#fr_1235">251:11</a> Vish<i>n</i>u LXIV, 13-14; 18-19. Pavitras, i.e. blades of Ku<i>s</i>a grass. 'He performs three Prâ<i>n</i>âyâmas with the Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a hymn (Rig-veda X, 190),' i.e. he thrice suppresses his breath (prâ<i>n</i>âyâma) and recites during each suppression the Aghamarsha<i>n</i>a three times, just as on other occasions the Gâyatrî is recited three times.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1236"></a><a href="sbe1462.htm#fr_1236">252:12</a> The first Mantra is found Taitt. Sa<i>m</i>hitâ IV, 1, 7, 4; the third and the fourth Taitt. Âra<i>n</i>yaka IV, 42, 32-33.</p>
