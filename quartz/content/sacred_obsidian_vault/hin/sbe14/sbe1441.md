   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA I, ADHYÂYA 5, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 8.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now (follows the description of) the means of purification.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_165"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 165</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. The body is purified by water, the understanding by knowledge, the soul by abstention from injuring living beings, the internal organ by truth. <a name="fr_826"></a><a href="#fn_826"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Purifying the internal organ (is called) internal purification.</p>  
 <p>4. We will explain (the rules of) external purification.</p>  
 <p>5. The sacrificial thread (shall be made) of Ku<i>s</i>a grass, or cotton, (and consist) of thrice three strings.</p>  
 <p>6. (It shall hang down) to the navel.</p>  
 <p>7. (In putting it on) he shall raise the right arm, lower the left, and lower the head. <a name="fr_827"></a><a href="#fn_827"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. The contrary (is done at sacrifices) to the manes.</p>  
 <p>9. (If the thread is) suspended round the neck, (it is called) nivîta.</p>  
 <p>10. (If it is) suspended below (the navel, it is called) adhopavîta.</p>  
 <p>11. Let him perform (the rite of personal) purification, facing the east or the north, (and) seated in a pure place; (let him) place his right arm between his knees and wash both hands up to the wrist and both feet (up to the ankles). <a name="fr_828"></a><a href="#fn_828"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Let him not use for sipping the remainder of the water with which he has washed his feet.</p>  
 <p>13. But if he uses (that) for sipping, let him do it, after pouring (a portion of it) on the ground.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_166"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 166</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. He shall sip out of the Tîrtha sacred to Brahman. <a name="fr_829"></a><a href="#fn_829"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. The part (of the hand) at the root of the thumb (is called) the Tîrtha sacred to Brahman.</p>  
 <p>16. The part above the thumb (is called the Tîrtha) sacred to the manes, the part at the tips of the fingers that sacred to the gods, the part at the root of the fingers that sacred to the <i>Ri</i>shis. <a name="fr_830"></a><a href="#fn_830"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. (Let him not use for sipping water that has trickled) from the fingers, nor (water) that is covered with bubbles or foam, nor (water that is) hot, or alkaline, or salt, or muddy, or discoloured, or has a bad smell or taste. <a name="fr_831"></a><a href="#fn_831"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. (Let him not sip water) laughing, nor talking, nor standing, nor looking about, nor bending his head or his body forward, nor while the lock on his crown is untied, nor while his throat is wrapped up, nor while his head is covered, nor when he is in a hurry, nor without wearing the sacrificial thread, nor stretching his feet out, nor while his loins are girt (with a cloth), nor without holding his right arm between his knees, nor making a sound. <a name="fr_832"></a><a href="#fn_832"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. Let him thrice drink water that reaches his heart. <a name="fr_833"></a><a href="#fn_833"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. Let him wipe (his lips) thrice.</p>  
 <p>21. Some (declare that he shall do it) twice. <a name="fr_834"></a><a href="#fn_834"><font size="1">21</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_167"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 167</font></a></p>  
 <p>22. A woman and a <i>S</i>ûdra (shall perform) both (acts) once (only).</p>  
 <p>23. Now they quote also (the following verse): A Brâhma<i>n</i>a is purified by water that reaches his heart, a Kshatriya by (water) reaching his throat, a Vai<i>s</i>ya by (water barely) taken into the mouth, a woman and a <i>S</i>ûdra by touching (it) with the extremity (of the lips). <a name="fr_835"></a><a href="#fn_835"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>  
 <p>24. 'If (drops) adhere to his teeth, (they must be considered pure) like the teeth, because they are fixed (in the mouth) like the teeth. Let him not sip water on their account in case they fall. If they flow out, he will be pure.' <a name="fr_836"></a><a href="#fn_836"><font size="1">24</font></a></p>  
 <p>25. Now they quote also (the following verse): If anything adheres to the teeth, (it is pure) like the teeth; and if he swallows (it or) whatever else may be in the mouth (or) may remain after sipping water, (he will become) pure.' <a name="fr_837"></a><a href="#fn_837"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. (After sipping) he shall touch the cavities (of the head) with water, the feet, the navel, the head, (and) lastly the left hand. <a name="fr_838"></a><a href="#fn_838"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. If he becomes impure while holding (a vessel) made of metal, he shall put it down, sip water and sprinkle it, when he is going to take it up.</p>  
 <p>28. Now if he becomes impure (while he is occupied) with food, he shall put it down, sip water and sprinkle it, when he is going to take it up. <a name="fr_839"></a><a href="#fn_839"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. Now if he becomes impure (while occupied)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_168"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 168</font></a></p>  
 <p>with water, he shall put it down, sip water and sprinkle it, when he is going to take it up</p>  
 <p>30. That is contrary (to the rule) in (the case of an earthen) vessel. <a name="fr_840"></a><a href="#fn_840"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>31. In (the case of a vessel) made of wood there is an option.</p>  
 <p>32. Defiled (objects) made of metal must be scoured with cowdung, earth, and ashes, or with one of these (three). <a name="fr_841"></a><a href="#fn_841"><font size="1">32</font></a></p>  
 <p>33. Copper, silver, and gold (must be cleaned) with acids. <a name="fr_842"></a><a href="#fn_842"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. Earthen vessels must be heated. <a name="fr_843"></a><a href="#fn_843"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. (Objects) made of wood must be planed.</p>  
 <p>36. (Objects) made of bamboo (must be cleaned) with cowdung, <a name="fr_844"></a><a href="#fn_844"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. (Objects) made of fruits with a rope of cow-hair, <a name="fr_845"></a><a href="#fn_845"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. Skins of black deer with (ground) Bel nut and rice,</p>  
 <p>39. Blankets (of the hair of the mountain goat) with Areka nuts, <a name="fr_846"></a><a href="#fn_846"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. (Cloth) made of (sheep's) wool by the (rays of the) sun,</p>  
 <p>41. Linen (cloth) with a paste of yellow mustard, <a name="fr_847"></a><a href="#fn_847"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_169"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 169</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. Cotton cloth with earth, <a name="fr_848"></a><a href="#fn_848"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. Skins (other than deer-skins shall be treated) like cotton cloth, <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">43</font></a></p>  
 <p>44. Stones and gems like (objects) made of metal, <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">44</font></a></p>  
 <p>45. Bones like wood, <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">45</font></a></p>  
 <p>46. Conch-shells, horn, pearl-shells, and ivory like linen cloth. <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">46</font></a></p>  
 <p>47. Or (they may be cleaned) with milk.</p>  
 <p>48. (Objects) which have been defiled by urine, ordure, blood, semen, or a dead body, (but) are agreeable to the eye and the nose, shall be rubbed seven times with one of the substances mentioned above.</p>  
 <p>49. (Objects) not made of metal which are in the same condition must be thrown away. <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">49</font></a></p>  
 <p>50. The cups and vessels (used) at a sacrifice (must be cleaned) according to the injunction (of the Veda). <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">50</font></a></p>  
 <p>51. The Veda (declares), 'They do not become impure through Soma.'</p>  
 <p>52. 'Time, fire, purity of mind, water and the like (fluids), smearing with cowdung and ignorance (of defilement) are declared to be the sixfold (means of) purification for created beings.' <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">52</font></a></p>  
 <p>53. Now they quote also (the following verse):</p>

 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> 'A clever man, who knows (the rules of) purification and is desirous of righteousness, shall perform (the rites of) purification, after having fully considered the time, and the place (of the defilement), likewise himself, (as well as) the object (to be cleaned) and the substance (to be employed), the purpose of the object, the cause (of the defilement), and the condition (of the thing or person defiled).'</p>
