   <h3 align="center">PRA<i>S</i>NA II, ADHYÂYA 1, KA<i>N</i><i>D</i>IKÂ 2.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now (follow the offences) causing loss of caste (patanîya), <a name="fr_1077"></a><a href="#fn_1077"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (Viz.) making voyages by sea, <a name="fr_1078"></a><a href="#fn_1078"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_218"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 218</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. Stealing the property of a Brâhma<i>n</i>a or a deposit,</p>  
 <p>4. Giving false evidence regarding land,</p>  
 <p>5. Trading with merchandise of any description (whether forbidden or not),</p>  
 <p>6. Serving <i>S</i>ûdras,</p>  
 <p>7. Begetting a son on a female of the <i>S</i>ûdra caste, <a name="fr_1079"></a><a href="#fn_1079"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. And becoming thereby her son. <a name="fr_1080"></a><a href="#fn_1080"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. (For those who have) committed one of these (offences the following penance is prescribed); <a name="fr_1081"></a><a href="#fn_1081"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. 'They shall eat every fourth meal-time a little food, bathe at the time of the three libations (morning, noon, and evening), passing (the day) standing and (the night) sitting. After the lapse of three years they throw off their guilt.' <a name="fr_1082"></a><a href="#fn_1082"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. 'A Brâhma<i>n</i>a removes the sin which he committed by serving the black race during one day and one night, if he bathes during three years at every fourth meal-time.' <a name="fr_1083"></a><a href="#fn_1083"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a></p>  
 <p>12. Now (follow) the minor offences, entailing loss of caste (upapâtaka), <a name="fr_1084"></a><a href="#fn_1084"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. (Viz.) intercourse with females who must not be approached (agamyâ, e.g.) cohabitation with the female friend of a female Guru, with the female friend of a male Guru, with an Apapâtra woman, and a female outcast, following the profession of medicine, sacrificing for many, living by (performances on) the stage, following the profession of a teacher of dancing, singing and acting, tending cows and buffalos, and similar (low occupations, as well as) fornication. <a name="fr_1085"></a><a href="#fn_1085"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. The expiation (prescribed) for these (offences is) to live as an outcast during two years. <a name="fr_1086"></a><a href="#fn_1086"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_220"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 220</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. Now (follow the offences) which make men impure (asu<i>k</i>ikara), <a name="fr_1087"></a><a href="#fn_1087"><font size="1">15</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. (Viz.) gambling, performing incantations, subsisting by gleaning corn though one does not perform an Agnihotra, subsisting by alms after one has finished one's studentship, living, after that has been finished, longer than four months in the house of one's teacher, and teaching such a (person who has finished his studentship), gaining one's livelihood by astrology and so forth. <a name="fr_1088"></a><a href="#fn_1088"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. But the expiation of these (offences is to perform penances) during twelve months, during twelve fortnights, during twelve times ten days, during twelve se’nnights, during twelve times three days, during twelve days, during six days, during three days, during a day and a night, during one day, in proportion to the offence committed. <a name="fr_1089"></a><a href="#fn_1089"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. Now outcasts shall live together and (together) fulfil their duties, sacrificing for each other, teaching each other, and marrying amongst each other. If they have begot sons, they shall say to them, 'Depart from among us; thus you will again reach the Âryas.' <a name="fr_1090"></a><a href="#fn_1090"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. For the organs do not become impure together with the man.</p>  
 <p>20. (The truth of) that may be learned from this (parallel case); a man deficient in limbs begets a son who has the full number of limbs.</p>  
 <p>21. Hârîta declares that this is wrong.</p>  
 <p>22. For wives may be (considered) similar to the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 221</font></a></p>  
 <p>vessel which contains the curds (for the sacrifice). If one makes impure milk curdle in a milk-vessel and stirs it, the <i>S</i>ish<i>t</i>as do not use the (curds thus produced) for sacred rites.</p>  
 <p>23. In like manner no intercourse can be held with that (offspring) which is produced from impure seed.</p>  
 <p>24. If they desire it, (they may perform) a penance,</p>  
 <p>25. (Viz. in the case of males) the third part (of the penance prescribed) for crimes causing loss of caste (patanîya); for females the third part (of that). <a name="fr_1091"></a><a href="#fn_1091"><font size="1">25</font></a></p>  
 <p>26. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'If he applies sesamum to any other purpose, but food, anointing, and charitable gifts, he will be born again as a worm and, together with his ancestors, be plunged into the ordure of dogs.' <a name="fr_1092"></a><a href="#fn_1092"><font size="1">26</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. He who sells sesamum, forsooth, sells his ancestors; he who sells rice, forsooth, sells his life; he who gives away his daughter, making a bargain, forsooth, sells portions of his spiritual merit.</p>  
 <p>28. Grass and wood, in its natural state, may be sold. <a name="fr_1093"></a><a href="#fn_1093"><font size="1">28</font></a></p>  
 <p>29. Now they quote also (the following verse): 'Animals that have teeth in one jaw only, as well as minerals excepting salt, and undyed thread, these, O Brâhma<i>n</i>a, are the goods which thou art permitted to sell.' <a name="fr_1094"></a><a href="#fn_1094"><font size="1">29</font></a></p>  
 <p>30. (If he has committed) any offence excepting a <a name="fr_1095"></a><a href="#fn_1095"><font size="1">30</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 222</font></a></p>  
 <p>mortal sin (pâtaka) he may either give to a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a (anû<i>k</i>âna) a hairy cow of brown or red-dish colour, after sprinkling her with clarified butter and scattering black sesamum seeds over her;</p>  
 <p>31. Or (he may offer burnt oblations), reciting the Kûshmâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>as, during twelve days, <a name="fr_1096"></a><a href="#fn_1096"><font size="1">31</font></a></p>  
 <p>32. '(Thus) he will be freed. from the guilt (of any crime that is) less (heinous) than the murder of a learned Brâhma<i>n</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>33. If one is accused of a mortal sin (pâtaka), a K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra (penance must be performed by the accused). <a name="fr_1097"></a><a href="#fn_1097"><font size="1">33</font></a></p>  
 <p>34. The accuser (shall perform) that (K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penance during) a year. <a name="fr_1098"></a><a href="#fn_1098"><font size="1">34</font></a></p>  
 <p>35. 'He who during a year associates with an outcast, becomes (likewise) an outcast; not by sacrificing for <i>h</i>im, by teaching him or by (forming) a matrimonial (alliance with him), but by using the same carriage or seat.' <a name="fr_1099"></a><a href="#fn_1099"><font size="1">35</font></a></p>  
 <p>36. The penance for eating impure substances is to fast until the entrails are empty. That is attained in seven (days and) nights. <a name="fr_1100"></a><a href="#fn_1100"><font size="1">36</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 223</font></a></p>  
 <p>37. (Subsisting on) water, milk, clarified butter, (and) fasting,--each for three days,--(and taking the three fluids) hot, that is a Taptak<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penance. <a name="fr_1101"></a><a href="#fn_1101"><font size="1">37</font></a></p>  
 <p>38. (Eating) during three days in the morning only, during the (next) three days in the evening only, (subsisting) during (another) three days (on) food given unasked, and fasting during three days, (that is) a K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penance. <a name="fr_1102"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">38</font></a></p>  
 <p>39. (If the period of twelve days is divided into) three (periods of) four days, that is the K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penance of, women, children, and aged men. <a name="fr_1103"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">39</font></a></p>  
 <p>40. If (observing the rule given) above one eats (at each meal) so much only as one can take at one (mouthful), that is an Atik<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penance. <a name="fr_1104"></a><a href="#fn_1104"><font size="1">40</font></a></p>  
 <p>41. (If one) subsists on water only, that is a K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>râtik<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra, the third (in the order of the K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penances). <a name="fr_1105"></a><a href="#fn_1105"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. During a K<i>ri</i><i>k</i><i>kh</i>ra penance (the following rules must be followed, viz.) to bathe at morn, noon, and evening, <a name="fr_1106"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">42</font></a></p>  
 <p>43. To sleep on the ground,</p>  
 <p>44. To wear one garment only, to shave the hair of the head, of the beard, and of the body, and to clip the nails.</p>  
 <p>45. The same (rules apply) to women except (that referring to) shaving the head.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_1081"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1081">218:9</a> The Dekhan and Gu<i>g</i>arât MSS. again have a different reading, teshâ<i>m</i> to nisvesha<i>h</i>, 'but the atonement of these offences (is as follows).'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1082"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1082">218:10</a> Âpastamba I, 9, 25, 10. All the MSS. read in the last pâda 'tribhir varshais tad apahanti pâpam.' The correct reading is that given by Âpastamba loc. cit., 'tribhir varshair apa pâpa<i>m</i> nudante.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1083"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1083">218:11</a> Âpastamba I, 9, 27, 11. Govinda explains the Sûtra as referring to cohabitation with a female of the 'black race.' By the latter term he understands a <i>K</i>â<i>n</i><i>d</i>âlî, adding that others believe <a name="page_219"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 219</font></a> a <i>S</i>ûdra female to be intended. It is, however, more probable that Baudhâyana took the verse to forbid twice-born men to serve <i>S</i>ûdras.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1084"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1084">219:12</a> Âpastamba I, 7, 21, 9.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1085"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1085">219:13</a> Gautama XXI, is. In explanation of the term agamyâ, a female who must not be approached,' Govinda quotes Nârada XII, 73-74, and he takes the four classes of females, who are specially mentioned, not as examples illustrating the term agamyâ, but as not included in and additional to the latter. Physicians and the other professional men enumerated are usually not mentioned among the upapâtakins, but occur in the lists of those whose gifts must not be accepted, and of those who defile the, company at a funeral dinner, e<i>g</i>. Vasish<i>th</i>a III, 3; XIV, 2, 3, 11. The expression 'sacrificing for many' (grâmayâ<i>g</i>anam) appears to be a description of the so-called 'Ya<i>g</i>amâna V<i>ri</i>tti, by which the modern Bha<i>t</i><i>t</i>agîs, or priests who officiate for hire, subsist. In explanation of the term nâ<i>t</i>yâ<i>k</i>âryatâ, 'following the profession of teaching dancing, music, and acting,' Govinda says that 'instruction in the works of Bharata, Vi<i>s</i>âkhila, and others' is intended. Baudhâyana no doubt intends to forbid the instruction of professional dancers and actors in actual works on their art, such as the nâ<i>t</i>ya-sûtras mentioned by Pâ<i>n</i>ini.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1086"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1086">219:14</a> 'To live as an outcast, i.e. to subsist by begging.'--Govinda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1087"></a><a href="sbe1456.htm#fr_1087">220:15</a> Âpastamba I, 7, 21, 12-19; I, 10, 29, 15.</p>
