   <h3 align="center">CHAPTER IX.</h3>  
 <p>1. A hermit shall wear (his hair in) braids, and dress (in garments made of) bark and skins; <a name="fr_255"></a><a href="#fn_255"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. And he shall not enter a village. <a name="fr_256"></a><a href="#fn_256"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. He shall not step on ploughed (land). <a name="fr_257"></a><a href="#fn_257"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. He shall gather wild growing roots and fruit (only). <a name="fr_258"></a><a href="#fn_258"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. He shall remain chaste. <a name="fr_259"></a><a href="#fn_259"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. His heart shall be full of meekness. <a name="fr_260"></a><a href="#fn_260"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>7. He shall honour guests coming to his hermitage with alms (consisting of) roots and fruit. <a name="fr_261"></a><a href="#fn_261"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>8. He shall only give, not receive (presents).</p>  
 <p>9. He shall bathe at morn, noon, and eve. <a name="fr_262"></a><a href="#fn_262"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. Kindling a fire according to the (rule of the) <i>S</i>râma<i>n</i>aka (Sûtra), he shall offer the Agnihotra. <a name="fr_263"></a><a href="#fn_263"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>  
 <p>11. After (living in this manner during) six months, <a name="fr_264"></a><a href="#fn_264"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>
