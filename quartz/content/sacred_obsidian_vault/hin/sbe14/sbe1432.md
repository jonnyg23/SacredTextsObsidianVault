 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 136</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER XXIX.</h3>  
 <p>1. Through liberality (man) obtains all his desires,</p>  
 <p>2. (Even) longevity, (and he is born again as) a student of the Veda, possessed of beauty.</p>  
 <p>3. He who abstains from injuring (sentient beings) obtains heaven.</p>  
 <p>4. By entering a fire the world of Brahman (is gained). <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. By (a vow of) silence (he obtains) happiness.</p>  
 <p>6. By staying (constantly) in water he becomes a lord of elephants.</p>  
 <p>7. He who expends his hoard (in gifts) becomes free from disease.</p>  
 <p>8. A giver of water (becomes) rich by (the fulfilment of) all his desires.</p>  
 <p>9. A giver of food (will have) beautiful eyes and a good memory. <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. He who gives a promise to protect (somebody) from all dangers (becomes) wise.</p>  
 <p>11. (To bestow gifts) for the use of cows (is equal to) bathing at all sacred places.</p>  
 <p>12. By giving a couch and a seat (the giver becomes) master of a harem. <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. By giving an umbrella (the giver) obtains a house.</p>

 <p>14. He who gives a house obtains a town. <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>  
 <p>15. He who gives a pair of shoes obtains a vehicle.</p>  
 <p>16. Now they quote also (the following verses): Whatever sin a man distressed for livelihood commits, (from that) he is purified by giving land, (be it) even "a bull's hide."' <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">16</font></a></p>  
 <p>17. 'He who gives to a Brâhma<i>n</i>a a vessel filled with water for sipping, will obtain after death complete freedom from thirst and be born again as a drinker of Soma.' <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">17</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. 'If a gift of one thousand oxen fit to draw a carriage (has been bestowed) according to the rule on a perfectly worthy man, that is equal to giving a maiden.' <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">18</font></a></p>  
 <p>19. 'They declare that cows, land, and learning are the three most excellent gifts. For to give learning is (to bestow) the greatest of all gifts, and it surpasses those (other gifts).' <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">19</font></a></p>  
 <p>20. 'A learned man who, free from envy, follows this rule of conduct which procures endless rewards, and which through final liberation frees him from transmigration;' <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>21. 'Or who, full of faith, pure, and subduing his</p>
