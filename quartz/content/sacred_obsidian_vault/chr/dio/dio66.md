 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p6"></a><b>Metropolitans of London</b>, from King Lucius to Pagan expulsion, 586, from list  of Jocelyn, 12th century, to be found in Stow, Ussher, Godwin, and Fasti of  Le Neve.</p>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p7"></a>1.   Theonus, in time of King Lucius (186–193 A.D.). He built the church  of St. Peter, Cornhill.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p8"></a>2.   Elvanus, messenger from Lucius to Eleutherus, Bishop of Rome, by whom he  was consecrated.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p9"></a>3.   Cadwr, or Cadoc. Name occurs at Caerleon.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p10"></a>4.   Obinus. See Ussher, Antiq., p. 67. No date.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p11"></a>5.   Conan. No date.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p12"></a>6.   Palladius. "Bishop of Britain."</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p13"></a>7.   Stephanus. No date.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p14"></a>8.   Iltutus, Abbot of the School of Llandaff.</p>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p15"></a>9.   Theodwin, or Dedwin. No date.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p16"></a>10. Theodred. No date.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p17"></a>11. Hilarius.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p18"></a>12. Restitutus, who attended Council of Arles, A.D. 314.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p19"></a>13. Guitelinus. Mentioned by Geoffrey of Monmouth, Hist.VI. cc. 2–6.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p20"></a>14. Vodinus. Put to death, 453.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p21"></a>15. Theonus 2nd. Translated from Gloucester, 542; fled to Wales, 586. To these may be added</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p22"></a>16. Fastidius, Bishop of Britain, A.D. 431.</p>   
 <p align="center"><a name="iv.iv.i-p23"></a>Metropolitans of York, from Godwin, Bishop of Llandaff, 1601.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p24"></a>1.   Sampson, appointed by King Lucius.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p25"></a>2.   Taurinus, Bishop of Evreux, "Ebroicensis."</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p26"></a>3.   Eborius, at Arles, A.D. 314.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p27"></a>4.   Sampson, or Saxo, expelled by Saxons, and transferred his pall to Dol in  Brittany; consecrated, 490. Geoffrey, Hist. VIII. 12, IX. 8.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p28"></a>5.   Pirah, appointed by King Arthur, A.D. 522, in place of Sampson, A.D. 522.  Ibid. IX. 8.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p29"></a>6.   Thadiacus fled to Wales, A.D. 586. Geoff. Hist. XI. 10.   <a name="page_165"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 165</font></a> <a name="iv.iv.i-Page_165"></a></p>   
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p30"></a>There was also Faganus, a messenger to Eleutherus from King Lucius. Perhaps it  was he who founded the See of Congresbury, not far from what is now Wells, which  lasted till 721.</p>  
 <p align="center"><a name="iv.iv.i-p31"></a>Isle of Man.</p>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p32"></a>Amphibalus was Bishop of Man before a.d. 447, in which year St. Patrick consecrated  Germanus to Man.</p>  
 <p align="center"><a name="iv.iv.i-p33"></a>Whithern.</p>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p34"></a>St. Ninian, Bishop of Whithern (subsequently in the Province of York), was consecrated  by Pope Siricius, a.d. 394; retired to Ireland, 420; died, 432.</p>  
 <p align="center"><a name="iv.iv.i-p35"></a>Province of Caerleon.</p>  
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p36"></a>1.    Dyfan  (Missionaries of Eleutherus).</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p37"></a>2.   Ffagan</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p38"></a>3.   Elldyrn.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p39"></a>4.   Edyfield. Adelfius at Aries in 314. He is claimed also by Colchester  and Lincoln.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p40"></a>5.   Cadwr.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p41"></a>6.   Cynan.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p42"></a>7.   Ilan.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p43"></a>8.   Llewyr.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p44"></a>9.   Cyhelyn.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p45"></a>10. Guitelin.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p46"></a>11. Tremorinus, died about 490, and was succeeded by Dubritius of Llandaff,  after which the Primacy seems to have wavered between Llandaff and Menevia. Geoff.  Hist. VIII. 10.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p47"></a>Dubritius consecrated in 449 (Benedict of Gloster); in 490 (Geoffrey), Bishop  of Llandaff, and became Metropolitan on the death of Tremorinus, as stated' above,  but his seat remained at Llandaff.   <a name="page_166"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 166</font></a> <a name="iv.iv.i-Page_166"></a></p>   
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p48"></a>St. David, 1st Bishop of Menevia, was consecrated at Jerusalem, with two companions <a name="fr_283"></a><a href="#fn_283"><font size="1">284</font></a>
 ,  a.d. 519, and succeeded as Metropolitan on the death of Dubritius,  but his seat remained at St. David's.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p49"></a>After him came Teilo, consecrated at the same time as St. David, at Jerusalem,  A.D. 519, to Llandaff. He succeeded to the Metropolitan's office on St. David's  death, retaining Llandaff, and consecrating Ismael to St. David's as a Suffragan  Bishop.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p50"></a>Simon the Cananite, afterwards Bishop of Jerusalem, having preached the Gospel  in Britain ("Apostolic Constitutions," Lagarde, p. 284); as also Aristobulus, ordained  by St. Paul "Bishop for Britain" (Migne, ser. Graeca, tome III.); there must  have been many Bishops in Britain before King Lucius was able to supersede the Druid  by the Christian organisation. "Within ten years after the arrival of Joseph of  Arimathea, the first-fruit of Britain was sent to Rome, for instruction and consecration.  He founded a Church in Beatenberg, Switzerland.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p51"></a>For Bishops in France, see <i>Gallia Christiana.</i></p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iv.i-p52"></a>For Bishops in Britain, see Archbishop Parker, Alford, "St. Paul in Britain,"  Wakeman.</p>
