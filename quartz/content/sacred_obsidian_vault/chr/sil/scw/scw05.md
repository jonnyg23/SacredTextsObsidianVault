 <h2 align="center"><i>V<br>
 INWARDNESS</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 138------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_138"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 138</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>121 <font size="1">(I. 145)</font><br>
 <big>WHAT THOU WOULDST HAVE IS WITHIN THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>All Heaven is within thee, Man,<br>
 And all of Hell within thy heart:<br>
 What thou dost choose and will to have,<br>
 That hast thou wheresoe'er thou art.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>122 <font size="1">(I. 82)</font><br>
 <big>HEAVEN IS WITHIN THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Heaven is within thee. Stay! Why runn'st thou here and there?<br>
 Thou seekest God in vain seekest thou Him elsewhere.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>123 <font size="1">(IV. 183)</font><br>
 <big>ALL THAT THOU WILT IS WITHIN THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>All thou wouldst have lies now within thee, every whit<br>
 'Tis thine—so long as thou dost never strive for it.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>124 <font size="1">(III. 118)</font><br>
 <big>THE PHILOSOPHERS' STONE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Travel within thyself! The Stone<br>
 Philosophers with wisest arts<br>
 Have vainly sought, cannot be found<br>
 By travelling in foreign parts.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 139------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_139"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 139</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>125 <font size="1">(I. 50)</font><br>
 <big>THE THRONE OF GOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Christian, dost thou demand to know<br>
  Where God hath set His Throne?<br>
 Even there within thyself, where He<br>
  Gives birth to thee, His Son.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>126 <font size="1">(I. 61)</font><br>
 <big>GOD MUST BE BORN IN THEE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Though Jesus Christ in Bethlehem<br>
 A thousand times his Mother bore,<br>
 Is he not born again in thee<br>
 Then art thou lost for evermore.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>127 <font size="1">(I. 62)</font><br>
 <big>THE EXTERNAL HELPETH THEE NOT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>In vain the Cross on Golgotha<br>
 Was raised—thou hast not any part<br>
 In its deliverance unless<br>
 It be raised up within thy heart.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>128 <font size="1">(VI. 209)</font><br>
 <big>WHAT IS OUTWARD ADDETH NO WORTH</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Nothing external to thee, Man,<br>
 Can give thee Worth or Dignity:<br>
 Fine harness maketh not the horse,<br>
 Nor clothes the man's virility.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 140------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 140</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>129 <font size="1">(VI. 24)</font><br>
 <big>WHAT A MAN HATH IN HIMSELF HE SEEKETH NOT WITHOUT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>He who hath honour in himself<br>
 Seeketh no honour among men.<br>
 Seekest thou honour in the world,<br>
 'Tis not thine own but alien.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>130 <font size="1">(VI. 169)</font><br>
 <big>WHAT A MAN WOULD NOT LOSE, THAT MUST HE BE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Wise Man <i>is</i> that which he hath.<br>
 The precious Pearl of Paradise<br>
 Wouldst thou not lose, then must thou be<br>
 Thyself that Pearl of greatest price.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>131 <font size="1">(I. 298)</font><br>
 <big>HEAVEN IS WITHIN US</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>My Christian, whither runnest thou?<br>
 All Heaven within thy heart doth wait.<br>
 Why dost thou seek to find it then<br>
 By knocking at another's gate?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>132 <font size="1">(II. 149)</font><br>
 <big>THOU THYSELF ART ALL THINGS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>How is it possible for thee<br>
 To feel desire or suffer dearth?<br>
 Thou canst be all things in thyself—<br>
 A thousand Angels, Heaven and Earth!<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>

 <a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 141</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>133 <font size="1">(VI. 166)</font><br>
 <big>HE WHO HATH THE KINGDOM IN HIMSELF CANNOT BECOME POOR</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God's Kingdom is within ourselvcs!<br>
 If, then, so great a Kingdom be<br>
 Already thine, how canst thou fear<br>
 The threatening of poverty?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>134 <font size="1">(II. 85)</font><br>
 <big>THY PRISON IS THYSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The World doth not imprison thee.<br>
 Thou art thyselfthe World, and there,<br>
 Within thyself, thou hold'st thyself<br>
 Thy self-imprisoned Prisoner.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>135 <font size="1">(I. 37)</font><br>
 <big>UNREST COMETH FROM THYSELF</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Naught is that moveth thee: thou art thyself the wheel<br>
 That runneth of itself and never standeth still.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>136 <font size="1">(I. 118)</font><br>
 <big>SPIRIT REMAINETH EVER FREE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Fetter me with a thousand chains, and though they be<br>
 Never so strong, I shall be fetterless and free.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>

 <a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 142</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>137 <font size="1">(III. 147)</font><br>
 <big>GOD WOULD BE ALONE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Shut God up in thy heart. Let none else enter there,<br>
 So must He always bide with thee and be thy Prisoner.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>138 <font size="1">(V. 128)</font><br>
 <big>IT IS NEVER NIGHT IN THE SOUL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>I am amazed that thou dost yearn<br>
  For daylight to appear.<br>
 There is no sunset in my soul—<br>
  Day is already here.<br></td></tr></table>
