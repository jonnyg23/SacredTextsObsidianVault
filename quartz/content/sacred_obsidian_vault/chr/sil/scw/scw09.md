 <h2 align="center"><i>IX<br>
 ACCIDENT AND ESSENCE</i></h2>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 166------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_166"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 166</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>209 <font size="1">(II. 30)</font><br>
 <big>ACCIDENT AND ESSENCE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Become essential, Man! When the world fails at last,<br>
 Accident falls away, but Essence, that stands fast.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>210 <font size="1">(I. 274)</font><br>
 <big>THE ACCIDENT MUST GO</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>All Accident must go, all false appearances:<br>
 Put off thy specious hues—be pure as Essence is.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>211 <font size="1">(V. 356)</font><br>
 <big>THE PERFECT DRIVETH OUT THE IMPERFECT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>When full Perfection comes, the imperfect falls aside:<br>
 So fades my human part when I am deified.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>212 <font size="1">(I. 108)</font><br>
 <big>THE ROSE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Rose which here thou seest with thine outward eye<br>
 Hath blossomèd in God from all eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 167------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_167"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 167</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>213 <font size="1">(II. 182)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS ALL AT ONCE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>There is no After, no Before;<br>
 And what to-morrow shall befall<br>
 Already from eternity<br>
 In Essence God hath seen it all.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>214 <font size="1">(V. 179)</font><br>
 <big>GOD MAKETH NOTHING NEW</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God maketh no new thing, though new<br>
 It seem to us. We think we see<br>
 The act of birth, but what is born<br>
 Is birthless in eternity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>215 <font size="1">(I. 71)</font><br>
 <big>MAN MUST BE ESSENCE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>To practise love is burdensome. 'Tis not enough<br>
 Merely to love—we must ourselves, like God, be Love.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>216 <font size="1">(VI. 226)</font><br>
 <big>A RIGHT JUDGEMENT BRINGETH NOT SADNESS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The true and proper worth of things<br>
 Who understandeth to assay,<br>
 Will never sorrow overmuch<br>
 For aught that Time can bear away.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 168------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_168"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 168</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>217 <font size="1">(VI. 224)</font><br>
 <big>CHILDREN WEEP FOR DOLLS</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>A child that weepeth for its dolls<br>
 Maketh thee smile—and are they more<br>
 Than dolls and toys, those very things<br>
 That thou thyself art weeping for?<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>218 <font size="1">(I. 38)</font><br>
 <big>EQUAL ESTIMATION MAKETH PEACE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Canst thou divest from diverse things<br>
 Their aspect of diversity,<br>
 Come Love, come Pain, thou standest fast,<br>
 Poised in thy equanimity.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>219 <font size="1">(V. 45)</font><br>
 <big>VICE IS ONLY APPEARANCE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>All naked Virtue stands, Vice doth apparelled go;<br>
 Virtue is truly large, Vice only seemeth so.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>220 <font size="1">(V. 37)</font><br>
 <big>GOD LOOKETH TO THE ROOT</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>'Tis not the Somewhat thou hast done<br>
 God values, looks not to the fruit;<br>
 Only the How He contemplates,<br>
 Only the kernel and the root.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 169------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_169"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 169</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>221 <font size="1">(V. 168)</font><br>
 <big>THE SINNER DOETH NOTHING WELL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Though thou dost feed and clothe the needy multitude,<br>
 Is goodness not in thee, then is thy deed not good.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>222 <font size="1">(V. 72)</font><br>
 <big>GOD IS EQUALLY NEAR TO ALL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>God is as near Beelzebub<br>
 As He is near the Seraphim,<br>
 'Tis only that Beelzebub<br>
 Turneth his back on Him.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>223 <font size="1">(V. 30)</font><br>
 <big>THE DEVIL IS GOOD</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The Devil is as good as thou<br>
  If Being is the test.<br>
 What is it that the Devil lacks?<br>
  Peace and a Will at rest.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>224 <font size="1">(V. 15)</font><br>
 <big>DAMNATION IS IN ESSENCE</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Could one that's damned stand in high Heaven, even there<br>
 He'd feel within himself all Hell and Hell's despair.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 170------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_170"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 170</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>225 <font size="1">(VI. 208)</font><br>
 <big>WISE AND FOOLISH BEAUTY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Wiser the maid who knoweth well<br>
 Herself is her best loveliness<br>
 Than she who dreams she's beautiful<br>
 When she puts on a lovely dress.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>226 <font size="1">(VI. 251)</font><br>
 <big>TO THE FOOL</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Opinions are a shifting sand,<br>
 And fools build houses thereupon:<br>
 Wise wilt thou never be if thou<br>
 Dost build upon Opinion.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>227 <font size="1">(II. 71)</font><br>
 <big>THE ESSENTIAL MAN</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>The essential Man is like unto Eternity,<br>
 Unchanged by any breath of externality.<br></td></tr></table>
 
 <p> </p>
 
 <p>228 <font size="1">(I. 102)</font><br>
 <big>THE SPIRITUAL ALCHEMY</big></p>
 <table align="center"><tr><td>Then Lead becometh Gold, then Accident is ended,<br>
 When I with God, through God, in God, am wholly blended.<br></td></tr></table>
