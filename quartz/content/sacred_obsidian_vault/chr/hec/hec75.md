 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="green">p. 237</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-family: Lucida Blackletter, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-weight: bold;">Leo VI.</span></h3>  
 <p align="center">+ A.D. 917.</p>  
 <p>Our next name is that of a Royal Poet, Leo VI., the Philosopher, who reigned from 886 to 917, and left behind him the <i>Idiomela</i>, or detached stanzas, on the Resurrection, sung at Lauds. They are better than might have been expected from an imperial author, and the troubler of the Eastern Church by a <i>fourth</i> marriage.</p>  
 <p>The same thing may be said of the Exaposteilaria of his son, Constantine Porphyrogenitus, whose life lasted till 959.</p>
