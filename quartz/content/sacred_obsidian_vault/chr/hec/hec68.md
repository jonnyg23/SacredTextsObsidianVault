 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Ascension Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE VII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">φωτεινή σε, φῶς.</p>  
 <p>W   afting Him up on high,<br>       The glorious cloud receives<br>     The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> of Immortality,<br>       And earth the Victor leaves:<br>     The Heavenly People raise the strain,<br>     The Apostles pour the hymn again;—<br>       <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of our Fathers, Thou art blest!</p>  
 <p>Y   e faithful, tell your joys!<br>       All hearts with gladness bound!<br>     <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> is gone up with a merry noise,—<br>       The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> with the trumpet's sound!<br>     To Him we cry, by woes once tried,<br>     Now glorious at the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father's</span> side,—<br>       <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of our Fathers, Thou art blest!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_222"><font size="1" color="green">p. 222</font></a></p>  
 <p>Z   ealous for <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of yore,<br>       With zeal still Moses burns:<br>     "Come, Heavenly Spirits, and adore<br>       The Victor Who returns;<br>     Rise, Angel legions, rise and sing<br>     The ancient hymn to greet the King,—<br>       <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of our Fathers, Thou art blest!"</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Catavasía</i>.</p>  
 <p>J   oin’d with the trumpet-peal, the din and shout,<br>     Cornet, flute, sackbut, dulcimer rang out,<br>     And bade adore the golden deity:<br>     The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit's</span> gentler voice gives praise to Thee,<br> O   co-eternal One—O consubstantial Three!</p>
