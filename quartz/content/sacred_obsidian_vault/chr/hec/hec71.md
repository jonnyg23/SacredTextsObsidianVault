 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Hymns of the Eastern Church</i>, by J.M. Neale, [1884], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_229"><font size="1" color="green">p. 229</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-family: Lucida Blackletter, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-weight: bold;">Theoctistus of the Studium.</span></p>  
 <p align="center">+ circ. A.D. 890.</p>  
 <p>He is said to have been the friend of S. Joseph; but is only known-to us by the "Suppliant Canon to Jesus," to be found at the end of the Paracletice. The following is a Cento formed from it.</p>  
 <p align="center">Ἰησοῦ γλυκύτατε.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, Name all names above,<br>   <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, best and dearest,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, Fount of perfect love,<br>   Holiest, tenderest, nearest;<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, source of grace completest,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span> purest, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span> sweetest,<br>   <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, Well of power Divine,<br>   Make me, keep me, seal me Thine!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_230"><font size="1" color="green">p. 230</font></a></p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, open me the gate<br>   That of old he enter’d,<br> Who, in that most lost estate,<br>   Wholly on Thee ventur’d;<br> Thou, Whose Wounds are ever pleading,<br> And Thy Passion interceding,<br>   From my misery let me rise<br>   To a Home in Paradise!</p>  
 <p>Thou didst call the Prodigal:<br>   Thou didst pardon Mary:<br> Thou Whose words can never fall,<br>   Love can never vary:<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, to heal my lost condition,<br> Give—for Thou canst give—contrition;<br>   Thou canst pardon all mine ill<br>   If Thou wilt: O say, "I will!"</p>  
 <p><a name="page_231"><font size="1" color="green">p. 231</font></a></p>  
 <p>Woe, that I have turned aside<br>   After fleshly pleasure!<br> Woe, that I have never tried<br>   For the Heavenly Treasure!<br> Treasure, safe in Home supernal;<br> Incorruptible, eternal!<br>   Treasure no less price hath won<br>   Than the Passion of The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Son</span>!</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, crown’d with Thorns for me,<br>   Scourged for my transgression,<br> Witnessing, through agony,<br>   That Thy good confession!<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, clad in purple raiment,<br> For my evils making payment;<br>   Let not all Thy woe and pain,<br>   Let not Calvary, be in vain!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_232"><font size="1" color="green">p. 232</font></a></p>  
 <p>When I reach Death's bitter sea<br>   And its waves roll higher,<br> Help the more forsaking me<br>   As the storm draws nigher:<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesu</span>, leave me not to languish,<br> Helpless, hopeless, full of anguish!<br>   Tell me,—"Verily I say,<br>   Thou shalt be with Me to-day!"</p>
