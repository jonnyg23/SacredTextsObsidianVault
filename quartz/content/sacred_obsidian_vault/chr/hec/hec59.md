 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="green">p. 198</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SUNDAY OF THE PRODIGAL SON.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(SEPTUAGESIMA.)</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p>The Sunday before Septuagesima, and Septuagesima itself are, respectively, in the Greek Church, the Sunday of the Pharisee and Publican,—and the Sunday of the Prodigal Son,—those parables forming the Gospel for the day, and serving for the key-note to the offices. The following Troparia are from the Canon at Lauds on Septuagesima. (Ode VI. and Ode VIII. Trop. 2, 3.)</p>  
 <p align="center">βυθὸς ἁμαρτημάτων.</p>  
 <p>The abyss of many a former sin<br> Encloses me, and bars me in:<br> Like billows my transgressions roll:<br> Be Thou the Pilot of my soul;<br> And to Salvation's harbour bring,<br> Thou Saviour and Thou glorious King!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="green">p. 199</font></a></p>  
 <p>My Father's heritage abused,<br> Wasted by lust, by sin misused;<br> To shame and want and misery brought,<br> The slave to many a fruitless thought,<br> I cry to Thee, Who lovest men,<br> O pity and receive again!</p>  
 <p>In hunger now,—no more possessed<br> Of that my portion bright and blest,<br> The exile and the alien see<br> Who yet would fain return to Thee .<br> And save me, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>, who seek to raise<br> To Thy dear love the hymn of praise!</p>  
 <p>With that blest thief my prayer I make,<br> <i>Remember</i> for Thy mercy's sake!<br> With that poor publican I cry,<br> <i>Be merciful</i>, O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> most High!<br> With that lost Prodigal I fain<br> Back to my home would turn again!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="green">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p>Mourn, mourn, my soul, with earnest care,<br> And raise to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> the contrite prayer:—<br> O Thou, Who freely wast made poor,<br> My sorrows and my sins to cure,<br> Me, poor of all good works, embrace,<br> Enriching with Thy boundless grace</p>  
 <p>[<small>In Mr. Young's book. Melody of Vater unser im Himmelreich: harmonised by Ch. H. Pink. A striking melody.</small>]</p>
