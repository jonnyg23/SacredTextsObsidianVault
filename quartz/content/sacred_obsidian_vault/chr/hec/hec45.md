 <p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="green">p. 150</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TRANSFIGURATION.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Cosmas</font></p>  
 <p><small>I shall, perhaps, render the following Canon more acceptable to most readers if, instead of translating the Odes in detail, I make a cento from the more remarkable Troparia.</small></p>  
 <p>They are principally from the first four Odes.</p>  
 <p align="center">χορὸς Ἰσραήλ.</p>  
 <p>The choirs of ransomed Israel,<br>   The Red Sea’s passage o’er,<br> Uprais’d the hymn of triumph<br>   Upon the further shore:<br> And shouted, as the foeman<br>   Was whelm’d beneath the sea,—<br> 'Sing we to Judah's Saviour,<br>   For glorified is He!'</p>  
 <p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="green">p. 151</font></a></p>  
 <p>Amongst His Twelve Apostles<br>   <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> spake the Words of Life,<br> And show’d a realm of beauty<br>   Beyond a world of strife:<br> 'When all My <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father's</span> glory<br>   Shall shine expressed in Me,<br> Then praise Him, then exalt Him,<br>   For magnified is He!'</p>  
 <p>Upon the Mount of Tabor<br>   The promise was made good;<br> When, baring all the Godhead,<br>   In light itself He stood:<br> And they, in awe beholding,<br>   The Apostolic Three,<br> Sang out to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> their Saviour,<br>   For magnified was He!</p>  
 <p>In days of old, on Sinai,<br>   The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> of Sabaoth came, <a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="green">p. 152</font></a><br> In majesty of terror,<br>   In thunder-cloud and flame:<br> On Tabor, with the glory<br>   Of sunniest light for vest,<br> The excellence of beauty<br>   In <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesus</span> was express’d.</p>  
 <p>All hours and days inclin’d there,<br>   And did Thee worship meet,<br> The sun himself adored Thee,<br>   And bow’d him at Thy feet:<br> White Moses and Elias,<br>   Upon the Holy Mount,<br> The co-eternal glory<br>   Of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> our <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> recount.</p>  
 <p>O holy, wonderous Vision!<br>   But what, when this life past,<br> The beauty of Mount Tabor<br>   Shall end in Heav’n at last? <a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="green">p. 153</font></a><br> But what, when all the glory<br>   Of uncreated light<br> Shall be the promis’d guerdon<br>   Of them that win the fight?</p>  
 <p>[<small>No. 5 in Mr. Sedding's book.</small>]</p>
