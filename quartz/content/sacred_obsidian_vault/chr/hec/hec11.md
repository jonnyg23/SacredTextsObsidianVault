 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="green">p. 62</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-family: Lucida Blackletter, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-weight: bold;">St. Stephen's Day.</span></h3>  <h3 align="center">STICHERA AT VESPERS.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Anatolius.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τῷ Βασιλεῖ καὶ Δεσπότῃ.</p>  
 <p>The <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> and King of all things<br>   But yesterday was born:<br> And Stephen's glorious offering<br>   His birthtide shall adorn.<br> No pearls of orient splendour,<br>   No jewels can he show;<br> But with his own true heart's-blood<br>   His shining vestments glow.</p>  
 <p>Come, ye that love the Martyrs,<br>   And pluck the flow’rs of song,<br> And weave them in a garland<br>   For this our suppliant throng: <a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="green">p. 63</font></a><br> And cry,—O thou that shinest<br>   In grace's <a href="errata.htm#0">brightest</a> ray,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ's</span> valiant Protomartyr,<br>   For peace and favour pray!</p>  
 <p>Thou first of all Confessors,<br>   Thou of all Deacons crown,<br> Of every following athlete<br>   The glory and renown:<br> Make supplication, standing<br>   Before <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ's</span> Royal Throne,<br> That He would give the kingdom,<br>   And for our sins atone!</p>
