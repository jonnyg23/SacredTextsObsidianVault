 <p><a name="page_76"><font size="1" color="green">p. 76</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TROPARIA FOR PALM SUNDAY.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Andrew of Crete.</font></p>  
 <p><small>The following Stanzas are from the Triodion sung at Compline on Palm Sunday: which has the same name among the Greeks as among ourselves.</small></p>  
 <p align="center">Ἰησοῦς ὑπὲρ τοῦ κόσμου.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesus</span>, hastening for the world to suffer,<br>   Enters in, Jerusalem, to thee:<br> With His Twelve He goeth forth to offer<br>   That free sacrifice He came to be.</p>  
 <p>They that follow Him with true affection<br>   Stand prepared to suffer for His Name:<br> Be we ready then for man's rejection,<br>   For the mockery, the reproach, the shame.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a></p>  
 <p>Now, in sorrow, sorrow finds its healing:<br>   In the form wherein our father fell,<br> <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> appears, those quick’ning Wounds revealing,<br>   Which shall save from sin and death and hell.</p>  
 <p>Now, Judæa, call thy Priesthood nigh thee!<br>   Now for Deicide prepare thy hands!<br> Lo I thy Monarch, meek and gentle by thee!!<br>   Lo! the Lamb and Shepherd in thee stands!</p>  
 <p>To thy Monarch, Salem, give glad greeting!<br>   Willingly He hastens to be slain<br> For the multitude His entrance meeting<br>   With their false Hosanna's ceaseless strain.<br>        "Blest is He That comes," they cry,<br>        "On the Cross for man to die!"</p>
