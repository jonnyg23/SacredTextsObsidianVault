 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Ascension Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">Ἰησοῦς ὁ ζωοδότης.</p>  
 <p>J   <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">esus</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> of Life Eternal,<br>       Taking those He lov’d the best,<br>     Stood upon the Mount of Olives,<br>       And His Own the last time blest:<br>     Then, though He had never left it,<br>       Sought again his <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father's</span> breast.</p>  
 <p>K   nit is now our flesh to Godhead,<br>       Knit in everlasting bands:<br>     Call the world to highest festal:<br>       Floods and oceans, clap your hands:<br>     Angels, raise the song of triumph!<br>       Make response, ye distant lands</p>  
 <p><a name="page_215"><font size="1" color="green">p. 215</font></a></p>  
 <p>L   oosing Death with all its terrors<br>       Thou ascended’st up on high;<br>     And to mortals, now Immortal,<br>       Gavest immortality:<br>     As Thine own Disciples saw Thee<br>       Mounting victor to the sky</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Catavasía</i>.</p>  
 <p>M   onarch of monarchs, Sole of Sole, to Thee,<br>     <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Word</span>, Glorious in Thy <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father's</span> Majesty,<br>     And sending Thy co-equal <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit</span> bright<br>     To teach, to comfort, and to guide aright,<br>     Thine own Apostles sang: All glory to Thy might!</p>
