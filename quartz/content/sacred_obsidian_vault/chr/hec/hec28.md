 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Easter Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE VIII.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">αὕτη ἥ κλητή.</p>  
 <p>Thou hallowed chosen morn of praise,<br>   That best and greatest shinest!<br> Lady and Queen and Day of days,<br>   Of things divine, divinest!<br> On thee our praises <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> adore<br> For ever and for evermore.</p>  
 <p>Come, let us taste the Vine's new fruit<br>   For heavenly joy preparing:<br> To-day the branches with the Root<br>   In Resurrection sharing:<br> Whom as True <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> our hymns adore<br> For ever and for evermore.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="green">p. 106</font></a></p>  
 <p>Rise, Sion, rise, and looking forth,<br>   Behold thy children round thee!<br> From East and West, and South and North,<br>   Thy scatter’d sons have found thee!<br> And in thy bosom <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> adore<br> For ever and for evermore.</p>  
 <p>O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father</span>! O co-equal <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Son</span>!<br>   O co-eternal <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit</span>!<br> In Persons Three, in Substance One,<br>   And One in power and merit;<br> In Thee baptiz’d, we Thee adore<br> For ever and for evermore!</p>  
 <p>[<small>No. 1 in Mr. Sedding's book. A very appropriate melody.</small>]</p>
