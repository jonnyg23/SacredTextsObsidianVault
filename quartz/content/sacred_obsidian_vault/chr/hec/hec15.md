 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="green">p. 73</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">STICHERA FOR GREAT THURSDAY.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Andrew of Crete.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τὸ μέγα μυστήριον.</p>  
 <p>O the mystery, passing wonder,<br>   When, reclining at the board,<br> "Eat," Thou saidst to Thy Disciples,<br>   "That True Bread with quickening stored:<br> "Drink in faith the healing Chalice<br>   "From a dying <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> outpoured."</p>  
 <p>Then the glorious upper chamber<br>   A celestial tent was made,<br> When the bloodless rite was offered,<br>   And the soul's true service paid, <a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a><br> And the table of the feasters<br>   As an altar stood displayed.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> is now our mighty Pascha,<br>   Eaten for our mystic bread:<br> Take we of His broken Body,<br>   Drink we of the Blood He shed,<br> As a lamb led out to slaughter,<br>   And for this world offered.</p>  
 <p>To the Twelve spake Truth eternal,<br>   To the Branches spake the Vine:<br> "Never more from this day forward<br>   Shall I taste again this wine,<br> Till I drink it in the kingdom<br>   Of My <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father</span>, and with Mine."</p>  
 <p>Thou hast stretched those hands for silver<br>   That had held the Immortal Food; <a name="page_75"><font size="1" color="green">p. 75</font></a><br> With those lips that late had tasted<br>   Of the Body and the Blood,<br> Thou hast given the kiss, O Judas;<br>   Thou hast heard the woe bestowed.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> to all the world gives banquet<br>   On that most celestial Meat:<br> Him, albeit with lips all earthly,<br>   Yet with holy hearts we greet:<br> Him, the sacrificial Pascha,<br>   Priest and Victim all complete.</p>  
 <p>[In Mr. Young's book. Melody of <i>Pange lingua</i>, harmonised by Dr. Schroeder. I may add that I purposely chose this Stanza to suit the melody of S. Thomas's great hymn.]</p>
