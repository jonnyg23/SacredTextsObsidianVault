 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Easter Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE IV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. John Damascene.</font></p>  
 <p align="center">ἐπὶ τῆς θείας φυλακῆς.</p>  
 <p>Stand on thy watch-tower, Habakkuk the Seer,<br> And show the Angel, radiant in his light:<br> 'To-day,' saith he, 'Salvation shall appear,<br> 'Because the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> hath ris’n, as <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> of Might.'</p>  
 <p>The male that opes the Virgin's womb is He;<br> The Lamb of Whom His faithful people eat;<br> Our truer Passover from blemish free;<br> Our very <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, Whose name is all complete.</p>  
 <p>This yearling Lamb, our Sacrifice most blest,<br> Our glorious Crown, for all men freely dies:<br> Behold our Pascha, beauteous from His rest,<br> The healing Sun of Righteousness arise.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="green">p. 99</font></a></p>  
 <p>Before the ark, a type to pass away,<br> David of old time danced: we, holier race,<br> Seeing the Antitype come forth to-day,<br> Hail, with a shout, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ's</span> own Almighty grace.</p>
