 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Ascension Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE V.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">νεκρώσας τὸν θάνατον.</p>  
 <p>N   ow that Death by death hath found his ending,<br>       Thou dost call to Thee Thy loved Eleven;<br>     And from holy Olivet ascending<br>       On a cloud art carried up to Heaven.</p>  
 <p>O   that wondrous Birth! that wondrous Rising!<br>       That more wondrous mounting to the sky!<br>     So Elias, earthly things despising,<br>       In a fiery chariot went on high.</p>  
 <p>P   arted from Him, still they watch’d His going:<br>       'Why stand gazing thus?' the Angel said:<br>     'This same <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Jesus</span>, all His glory showing,<br>       Shall return to judge the quick and dead.'</p>  
 <p><a name="page_217"><font size="1" color="green">p. 217</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Catavasía</i>.</p>  
 <p>Q   uicken’d and cleans’d, receive remission new<br>     In the descending <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit's</span> fiery dew,<br>     Sons of the Church, and light-formed generation!<br>     For lo! the law goes forth from Sion's nation,<br>     The cloven tongues of flame the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Paraclete's</span> salvation!</p>
