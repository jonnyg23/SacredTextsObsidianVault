 <p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="green">p. 204</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">AND WILT THOU PARDON, LORD.</h3>  
 <p><small>The following Stanzas are a Cento from the Canon for the Monday of the First Tone, in the Paracletice.</small></p>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Joseph of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τῶν ἀμαρτιῶν μου τὴν πληθύν.</p>  
 <p>  And wilt Thou pardon, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span>,<br>   A sinner such as I?<br> Although Thy book his crimes record<br>   Of such a crimson dye?</p>  
 <p>  So deep are they engrav’d,—<br>   So terrible their fear,<br> The righteous scarcely shall be sav’d,<br>   And where shall I appear?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="green">p. 205</font></a></p>  
 <p>  My soul, make all things known<br>   To Him Who all things sees:<br> That so the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lamb</span> may yet atone<br>   For thine iniquities.</p>  
 <p>  O Thou Physician blest,<br> Make clean my guilty soul!<br>   And me, by many a sin oppress’d,<br>   Restore, and keep me whole!</p>  
 <p>  I know not how to praise<br> Thy mercy and Thy love:<br>   But deign Thy servant to upraise,<br>   And I shall learn above!</p>  
 <p>[<small>In Mr. Young's book. Composed by Dr. Schroeder.</small>]</p>
