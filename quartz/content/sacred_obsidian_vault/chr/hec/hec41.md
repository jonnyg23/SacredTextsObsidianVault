 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Christmas Day</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE VI.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by S. Cosmas</font></p>  
 <p align="center">σπλάγχνων Ἰωνᾶν.</p>  
 <p>As Jonah, issuing from his three days’ tomb,<br>   At length was cast, uninjured, on the earth;<br> So, from the Virgin's unpolluted womb<br>   The Incarnate <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Word</span>, That dwelt there, had His Birth:<br> For He, Who knew no taint of mortal stain,<br> Willed that His Mother spotless should remain.</p>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span> comes, Incarnate <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, amongst us now,<br>   Begotten of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father</span> ere the day:<br> And He, to Whom the sinless legions bow,<br>   Lies cradled, ’midst unconscious beasts on hay:<br> And, by His homely swaddling-bands girt in,<br> Looses the many fetters of our sin.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="green">p. 142</font></a></p>  
 <p>Now the New Child of Adam's race draws nigh,<br>   To us, the faithful, given: This, this is He<br> That shall the Father of Eternity,<br>   The Angel of the Mighty Counsel, be:<br> This the eternal <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>, by Whose strong hands<br> The fabric of the world supported stands.</p>
