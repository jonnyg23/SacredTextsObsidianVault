 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">Canon for Apocreos</font></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE I.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><font size="-1" color="green">by Theodore of the Studium</font></p>  
 <p align="center">τὴν ἡμέραν τὴν φρικτήν.</p>  
 <p>That fearful Day, that Day of speechless dread,<br> When Thou shalt come to judge the quick and dead—</p>  
 <p>        I shudder to foresee,<br>         O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span>! what then shall be!</p>  
 <p>When Thou shalt come, angelic legions round,<br> With thousand thousands, and with trumpet sound,</p>  
 <p>        <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span>, grant me in the air<br>         With saints to meet Thee there!</p>  
 <p>Weep, O my soul, ere that great hour and day,<br> When <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">God</span> shall shine in manifest array,</p>  
 <p>        Thy sin, that thou may’st be<br>         In that strict judgment free!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="green">p. 175</font></a></p>  
 <p>The terror!—hell-fire fierce and unsufficed:<br> The bitter worm: the gnashing teeth:—O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Christ</span>,</p>  
 <p>        Forgive, remit, protect;<br>         And set me with the elect!</p>  
 <p>That I may hear the blessed voice that calls<br> The righteous to the joy of heavenly halls.</p>  
 <p>        And, King of Heaven, may reach<br>         The realm that passeth speech!</p>  
 <p>Enter Thou not in judgment with each deed,<br> Nor each intent and thought in strictness read:</p>  
 <p>        Forgive, and save me then,<br>         O Thou That lovest men!</p>  
 <p>Thee, One in Three blest Persons! <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lord</span> o’er all!<br> Essence of essence, Power of power. we call!</p>  
 <p>        Save us, O <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Father</span>, <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Son</span>,<br>         And <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Spirit</span>, ever one!</p>  
 <p>[<small>In Mr. Young's book. Composed by Dr. Schroeder.</small>]</p>
