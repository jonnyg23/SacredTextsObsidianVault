 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Imitation of Christ</i>, by Thomas a Kempis, tr. by William Benham [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h1 align="center">CHAPTER VI</h1>  <h3 align="center">AN INQUIRY CONCERNING PREPARATION FOR COMMUNION</h3>  <h4 align="center">The Voice of the Disciple</h4>  
 <p>When I consider Thy dignity, O Lord, and mine own vileness, I tremble very exceedingly, and am confounded within myself. For if I approach not, I fly from life; and if I intrude myself unworthily, I run into Thy displeasure. What then shall I do, O my God, Thou helper and Counsellor in necessities.</p>  
 <p>2. Teach Thou me the right way; propound unto me some short exercise befitting Holy Communion. For it is profitable to know how I ought to prepare my heart devoutly and reverently for Thee, to the intent that I may receive Thy Sacrament to my soul's health [or it may be also for the celebrating this so great and divine mystery].</p>
