 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Pistis Sophia</i>, by G.R.S. Mead, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CHAPTER 12</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">He entereth the first sphere.</font></span>"And I left that region behind me and ascended to the first sphere, shining most exceedingly, forty-and-nine-times more brightly than I had shone in the firmament. It came to pass then, when I had reached the gate of the first sphere, that its gates were shaken and opened of themselves at once.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The powers of the first sphere are amazed and fall down and adore him.</font></span>"I entered into the houses of the sphere, shining most exceedingly, and there was no measure to the light that was about me. And all the rulers and all those who are in that sphere, fell into agitation one against another. And they saw the great light that was about me, and they gazed upon my vesture and saw thereon the mystery of their name. And they fell into still greater agitation, and were in great fear, saying: 'How hath the lord of the universe passed through us without our knowing?' |<span class="rmargnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>22</b>.</font></span> And all their bonds were unloosed and their regions and their orders; and every one left his order, and they fell down all together, adored before me, or before my vesture, and all sang</p>  
 <p><a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="green">p. 17</font></a></p>  
 <p>praises together to the interiors of the interiors, being in great fear and great agitation.</p>
