 <h1 align="center">CHAPTER IV<a name="page_8_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_8_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a>.</h1>
 
 <p align="center">OF HEAVEN.</p>
 
 <p>   H<small>EAVEN</small> is like a roof to the material world, and will serve as the floor of the new world. It is by nature shining and glorious, and is the dwelling-place of the invisible hosts. When God spread out this firmament, He brought up above it a third part of the waters, and above these is the heaven of light and of the luminaries. Hence people say 'the heaven, and the heaven of heavens<a name="page_8_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_8_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a>'; for we call both the firmament and the waters which are above it 'heaven.' Some consider that the verse 'Let the waters which are above the heavens praise the
