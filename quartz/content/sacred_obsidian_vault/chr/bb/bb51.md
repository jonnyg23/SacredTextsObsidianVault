 <h1 align="center">CHAPTER LI.</h1>
 
 <p align="center">THE NAMES OF THE EASTERN CATHOLICS, THE SUCCESSORS OF THE APOSTLES ADDAI AND MÂRÎ<a name="page_116_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_116_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   1. Addai was buried in Edessa.</p>
 
 <p>   2. Mârî (was buried) in the convent of Kônî<a name="page_116_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_116_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   3. Abrîs, called in Greek A[m]brosius; the place of his grave is unknown; he was of the laying on of hands of Antioch.</p>
 
 <p>   4. Abraham was of the laying on of hands of Antioch; he was descended from the family of Jacob the son of Joseph; his grave is in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   5. James, of the laying on of hands of Antioch, was also of the family of Joseph the husband of Mary; his grave is in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   6. A<u>h</u>â-d´abû[hî] was of the laying on of hands of Antioch; his grave is in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   7. Sha<u>h</u>lûphâ was of the laying on of hands of Ctesiphon, and he was buried there.</p>
 
 <p>   8. Pâpâ; his grave is at Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   9. Simon bar <u>S</u>abbâ`ê was martyred at Shôshân.</p>
 
 <p>   10. Shah-dôst was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 117------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   11. Bar-B<u>e</u>`esh-sh<u>e</u>mîn<a name="page_117_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_117_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a> was martyred and buried in Elam (Khûzistân).</p>
 
 <p>   12. Tûmar<u>s</u>â was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   13. <u>K</u>âyômâ was buried in Ctesiphon; he abdicated the patriarchate, and another was put in his place, and was before him until he died.</p>
 
 <p>   14. Isaac was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   15. A<u>h</u>â was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   16. Yab-alâhâ was of the school of Mâr `Abdâ<a name="page_117_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_117_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>; he was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   17. Ma`nâ dwelt in Persia and was buried there.</p>
 
 <p>   18. Dâd-îshô` was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ<a name="page_117_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_117_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a>. In his days the strife between Nestorius and Cyril (of Alexandria) took place.</p>
 
 <p>   19. Bâbôi was martyred and buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.</p>
 
 <p>   20. A<u>k</u>a<u>k</u> (Acacius) was of the family of Bâbôi the Catholicus; he was buried in al-Madâïn<a name="page_117_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_117_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   21. Bâbai took a wife, and was buried at Ctesiphon,</p>
 
 <p>   22. Shîlâ took a wife, and was buried in his convent beside Awânâ<a name="page_117_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_117_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   23. Paul was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   24. Mâr(î)-abâ was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ, and was a martyr without bloodshed.</p>
 
 <p>   25. Ezekiel was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.</p>
 
 <p>   26. Îshô`-yab of Arzôn<a name="page_117_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_117_note_6"><sup><small>6</small></sup></a> was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.</p>
 
 <p>   27. Sabr-îshô` was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.</p>
 
 <p>   28. Gregory was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   29. Îshô`-yab of G<u>e</u>dâlâ<a name="page_117_fr_7"></a><a href="#page_117_note_7"><sup><small>7</small></sup></a> was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 118------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 118</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   30. Mâr[î]-emmêh was buried in K<u>et</u>îmiyâ (?).</p>
 
 <p>   31. Îshô`-yab of Adiabene<a name="page_118_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_118_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a> was buried in Bêth-`Âbê<a name="page_118_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_118_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   32. George was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   33. John was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   34. <u>He</u>nân-îshô` was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   35. <u>Se</u>lîbâ-z<u>e</u>khâ was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   36. Pethiôn was buried in Ctesiphon.</p>
 
 <p>   37. Mâr[î]-abâ was buried in al-Madâïn.</p>
 
 <p>   38. Jacob was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   39. <u>He</u>nân-îshô` was buried in . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   40. Timothy was buried in his own convent.</p>
 
 <p>   41. Îshô` (Joshua) the son of Nôn (Nun) was buried in the convent of Timothy.</p>
 
 <p>   42. George was buried in the same convent.</p>
 
 <p>   43. Sabr-îshô`<a name="page_118_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_118_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a> was buried in the same convent.</p>
 
 <p>   44. Abraham was buried in the same convent.</p>
 
 <p>   45. Athanasius<a name="page_118_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_118_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a> was buried in the same convent.</p>
 
 <p>   46. Sergius was buried in the same convent.</p>
 
 <p>   47. Anôsh (Enos) was buried in the same convent.</p>
 
 <p>   48. John the son of Narsai was buried in the Greek Palace (at Baghdâd)<a name="page_118_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_118_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   49. Joannes<a name="page_118_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_118_note_6"><sup><small>6</small></sup></a> was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   50. John was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   51. Abraham was buried in the convent of `Abdôn.</p>
 
 <p>   52. Emmanuel was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>

 <a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 119</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   53. Israel was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   54. `Abd-îshô` was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   55. Mârî was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   56. Joannes<a name="page_119_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_119_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a> was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   57. John was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   58. Îshô`-yab was buried in the Grek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   59. Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   60. John was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   61. Sabr-îshô` was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   62. `Abd-îshô` was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   63. Makkîkhâ was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   64. Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   65. Bar-<u>s</u>aumâ was buried in the Greek Palace.</p>
 
 <p>   66. `Abd-îshô` was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   67. Îshô`-yab was buried in the church of Mâr Sabr-îshô`.</p>
 
 <p>   68. Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried in the church of Mâr Sabr-îshô`.</p>
 
 <p>   69. Yab-alâhâ was buried in the church of Mârt[î] Maryam (my lady Mary).</p>
 
 <p>   70. Sabr-îshô` was buried in the church of Mârt[î] Maryam<a name="page_119_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_119_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   71. Sabr-îshô` was buried<a name="page_119_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_119_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a> . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   72. [Mâr Makkîkhâ was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   73. Mâr Den<u>h</u>â was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   74. Mâr Yab-alâhâ the Turk<a name="page_119_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_119_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a> was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   75. Mâr Timothy was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   76. Mâr Den<u>h</u>â was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   77. Mâr Simon was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   78. Mâr Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried . . . . . .</p>
 
 <p>   79. Mâr Simon of our days, may he live for ever!<a name="page_119_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_119_note_6"><sup><small>6</small></sup></a>]</p>
