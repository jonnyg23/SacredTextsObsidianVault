 <p class="c28"><a name="v.vi.i-p2"></a><span class="c17"><a name="v.vi.i-p2.1"></a>Against the Valentinians.</span></p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="v.vi.i-p3"></a><span class="c6"><a name="v.vi.i-p3.1"></a>In Which the Author Gives a Concise Account of, Together with Sundry Caustic Animadversions on, the Very Fantastic Theology of the Sect. This Treatise is Professedly Taken from the Writings of Justin, Miltiades, Irenæus, and Proculus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="v.vi.i-p4"></a><span class="c6"><a name="v.vi.i-p4.1"></a>[Translated by Dr. Roberts.]</span></p> 
 <p class="Centered"><a name="v.vi.i-p5"></a> ————————————</p> 
 <p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.i-p6"></a><span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.i-p6.1"></a>Chapter I.—Introductory. Tertullian Compares the Heresy to the Old Eleusinian Mysteries.  Both Systems Alike in Preferring Concealment of Error and Sin to Proclamation of Truth and Virtue.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.i-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="v.vi.i-p7.1"></a>The</span> Valentinians, who are no doubt a very large body of heretics—comprising as they do so many apostates from the truth, who have a propensity for fables, and no discipline to deter them (therefrom) care for nothing so much as to obscure <a name="fr_6610"></a><a href="#fn_6610"><font size="1">6611</font></a>
  what they preach, if indeed they (can be said to) preach who obscure <i>their doctrine</i>. The officiousness with which they guard their doctrine is an officiousness which betrays their guilt. <a name="fr_6611"></a><a href="#fn_6611"><font size="1">6612</font></a>
  Their disgrace is proclaimed in the very earnestness with which they maintain their religious system. Now, in the case of those Eleusinian mysteries, which are the very heresy of Athenian superstition, it is their secrecy that is their disgrace. Accordingly, they previously beset all access to their body with tormenting conditions; <a name="fr_6612"></a><a href="#fn_6612"><font size="1">6613</font></a>
  <i>and</i> they require a long initiation before they enrol (their members), <a name="fr_6613"></a><a href="#fn_6613"><font size="1">6614</font></a>
  even instruction during five years for their perfect disciples, <a name="fr_6614"></a><a href="#fn_6614"><font size="1">6615</font></a>
  in order that they may mould <a name="fr_6615"></a><a href="#fn_6615"><font size="1">6616</font></a>
  their opinions by this suspension of full knowledge, and apparently raise the dignity of their mysteries in proportion to the craving for them which they have previously created. Then follows the duty of silence. Carefully is that guarded, which is so long in finding.  All the divinity, however, lies in their secret recesses: <a name="fr_6616"></a><a href="#fn_6616"><font size="1">6617</font></a>
  there are revealed <i>at last</i> all the aspirations of the fully initiated, <a name="fr_6617"></a><a href="#fn_6617"><font size="1">6618</font></a>
  the entire mystery of the sealed tongue, the symbol of virility. But this allegorical representation, <a name="fr_6618"></a><a href="#fn_6618"><font size="1">6619</font></a>
  under the pretext of natures reverend name, obscures a real sacrilege by help of an arbitrary symbol, <a name="fr_6619"></a><a href="#fn_6619"><font size="1">6620</font></a>
  and by <i>empty</i> images obviates <a name="fr_6620"></a><a href="#fn_6620"><font size="1">6621</font></a>
  the reproach of falsehood! <a name="fr_6621"></a><a href="#fn_6621"><font size="1">6622</font></a>
  In like manner, the heretics who are now the object of our remarks, <a name="fr_6622"></a><a href="#fn_6622"><font size="1">6623</font></a>
  the Valentinians, have formed Eleusinian dissipations <a name="fr_6623"></a><a href="#fn_6623"><font size="1">6624</font></a>
  of their own, consecrated by a profound silence, having nothing of the heavenly in them but their mystery. <a name="fr_6624"></a><a href="#fn_6624"><font size="1">6625</font></a>
  By the help of the sacred names and titles and arguments of true religion, they have fabricated the vainest and foulest figment for mens pliant liking, <a name="fr_6625"></a><a href="#fn_6625"><font size="1">6626</font></a>
  out of the affluent suggestions of Holy Scripture, since from its many springs many <i>errors</i> may well emanate. If you propose to them inquiries sincere and honest, they answer you with stern <a name="fr_6626"></a><a href="#fn_6626"><font size="1">6627</font></a>
  look and contracted brow, and say, “The subject is profound.” If you try them with subtle questions, with the ambiguities of their double tongue, they affirm a community of faith (with yourself). If you intimate to <a name="page_504"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 504</font></a> <a name="v.vi.i-Page_504"></a>them that you understand <i>their opinions</i>, they insist on knowing nothing themselves. If you come to a close engagement with them they destroy your own fond hope of a victory over them by a self-immolation. <a name="fr_6627"></a><a href="#fn_6627"><font size="1">6628</font></a>
  Not even to their own disciples do they commit a secret before they have made sure of them. They have the knack of persuading men before instructing them; although truth persuades by teaching, but does not teach by first persuading.</p>
