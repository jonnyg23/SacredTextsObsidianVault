 <p> <a name="vi.iv.xiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.xiv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIV.—Apostrophe.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xiv-p2"></a>Albeit Israel washed daily all his limbs over, yet is he never clean.  His <i>hands</i>, at all events, are ever unclean, eternally dyed with the blood of the prophets, and of the Lord Himself; and on that account, as being hereditary culprits from their privity to their fathers crimes, <a name="fr_8845"></a><a href="#fn_8845"><font size="1">8846</font></a>
  they do not dare even to raise them unto the Lord, <a name="fr_8846"></a><a href="#fn_8846"><font size="1">8847</font></a>
  for fear some Isaiah should cry out, <a name="fr_8847"></a><a href="#fn_8847"><font size="1">8848</font></a>
  for fear Christ should utterly shudder.  We, however, not only raise, but even expand them; and, taking our model from the Lords passion <a name="fr_8848"></a><a href="#fn_8848"><font size="1">8849</font></a>
  even in prayer we confess <a name="fr_8849"></a><a href="#fn_8849"><font size="1">8850</font></a>
  to Christ.</p>
