 <p> <a name="vi.iii.xii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XII.—Of the Necessity of Baptism to Salvation.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xii-p2"></a>When, however, the prescript is laid down that “without baptism, salvation is attainable <a name="page_675"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 675</font></a> <a name="vi.iii.xii-Page_675"></a>by none” (chiefly on the ground of that declaration of the Lord, who says, “Unless one be born of water, he hath not life” <a name="fr_8657"></a><a href="#fn_8657"><font size="1">8658</font></a>
 ), there arise immediately scrupulous, nay rather audacious, doubts on the part of some, “how, in accordance with that prescript, salvation is attainable by the apostles, whom—Paul excepted—we do not find baptized in the Lord? Nay, since Paul is the only one of them who has put on <i>the garment of</i> Christs baptism, <a name="fr_8658"></a><a href="#fn_8658"><font size="1">8659</font></a>
  either the peril of all the others who lack the water of Christ is prejudged, that the prescript may be maintained, or else the prescript is rescinded if salvation has been ordained even for the unbaptized.” I have heard—the Lord is my witness—doubts of that kind: that none may imagine me so abandoned as to excogitate, unprovoked, in the licence of my pen, ideas which would inspire others with scruple.</p> 
 <p class="c20"><a name="vi.iii.xii-p5"></a>And now, as far as I shall be able, I will reply to them who affirm “that the apostles were unbaptized.” For if they had undergone the human baptism of John, and were longing for that of the Lord, <i>then</i> since the Lord Himself had defined baptism to be <i>one</i>; <a name="fr_8659"></a><a href="#fn_8659"><font size="1">8660</font></a>
  (saying to Peter, who was desirous <a name="fr_8660"></a><a href="#fn_8660"><font size="1">8661</font></a>
  of being thoroughly bathed, “He who hath once bathed hath no necessity <i>to wash</i> a second time;” <a name="fr_8661"></a><a href="#fn_8661"><font size="1">8662</font></a>
  which, of course, He would not have said at all to one <i>not</i> baptized;) even here we have a conspicuous <a name="fr_8662"></a><a href="#fn_8662"><font size="1">8663</font></a>
  proof against those who, in order to destroy the sacrament of water, deprive the apostles even of Johns baptism. Can it seem credible that “the way of the Lord,” that is, the baptism of John, had not then been “prepared” in those persons who were being destined to <i>open</i> the way of the Lord throughout the whole world? The Lord Himself, though no “repentance” was due from <i>Him</i>, was baptized: was baptism not necessary for <i>sinners</i>?  As for the fact, then, that “others were not baptized”—they, however, were not companions of Christ, but enemies of the faith, doctors of the law and Pharisees. From which fact is gathered an additional suggestion, that, since the <i>opposers</i> of the Lord <i>refused</i> to be baptized, they who <i>followed</i> the Lord <i>were</i> baptized, and were not like-minded with their own rivals: especially when, if there were any one to whom they clave, the Lord had exalted John above him (by the testimony) saying, “Among them who are born of women <i>there is</i> none greater than John the Baptist.” <a name="fr_8663"></a><a href="#fn_8663"><font size="1">8664</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c20"><a name="vi.iii.xii-p11"></a>Others make the suggestion (forced enough, clearly “that the apostles then served the turn of baptism when in their little ship, were sprinkled and covered with the waves:  that Peter himself also was immersed enough when he walked on the sea.” <a name="fr_8664"></a><a href="#fn_8664"><font size="1">8665</font></a>
  It is, however, as I think, one thing to be sprinkled or intercepted by the violence of the sea; another thing to be baptized in obedience to the discipline of religion. But that little ship did present a figure of the Church, in that she is disquieted “in the sea,” that is, in the world, <a name="fr_8665"></a><a href="#fn_8665"><font size="1">8666</font></a>
  “by the waves,” that is, by persecutions and temptations; the Lord, through patience, sleeping as it were, until, roused in their last extremities by the prayers of the saints, He checks the world, <a name="fr_8666"></a><a href="#fn_8666"><font size="1">8667</font></a>
  and restores tranquillity to His own.</p> 
 <p class="c20"><a name="vi.iii.xii-p15"></a>Now, whether they were baptized in any manner whatever, or whether they continued unbathed <a name="fr_8667"></a><a href="#fn_8667"><font size="1">8668</font></a>
  to the end—so that even that saying of the Lord touching the “one bath” <a name="fr_8668"></a><a href="#fn_8668"><font size="1">8669</font></a>
  does, under the person of Peter, merely regard <i>us</i>—still, to determine concerning the salvation of the apostles is audacious enough, because on <i>them</i> the prerogative even of first choice, <a name="fr_8669"></a><a href="#fn_8669"><font size="1">8670</font></a>
  and thereafter of undivided intimacy, might be able to confer the compendious grace of baptism, seeing they (I think) followed Him who was wont to promise salvation to every believer. “Thy faith,” He would say, “hath saved thee;” <a name="fr_8670"></a><a href="#fn_8670"><font size="1">8671</font></a>
  and, “Thy sins shall be remitted thee,” <a name="fr_8671"></a><a href="#fn_8671"><font size="1">8672</font></a>
  on thy believing, of course, albeit thou be not <i>yet</i> baptized. If that <a name="fr_8672"></a><a href="#fn_8672"><font size="1">8673</font></a>
  was wanting to the apostles, I know not in the faith of what things it was, that, roused by one word of the Lord, <i>one</i> left the toll-booth behind for ever; <a name="fr_8673"></a><a href="#fn_8673"><font size="1">8674</font></a>
  <i>another</i> deserted father and ship, and the craft by which he gained his living; <a name="fr_8674"></a><a href="#fn_8674"><font size="1">8675</font></a>
  <i>a third</i>, who disdained his fathers obsequies, <a name="fr_8675"></a><a href="#fn_8675"><font size="1">8676</font></a>
  fulfilled, before he heard it, that highest precept of the Lord, “He who prefers father or mother to me, is not worthy of me.” <a name="fr_8676"></a><a href="#fn_8676"><font size="1">8677</font></a>
 </p>
