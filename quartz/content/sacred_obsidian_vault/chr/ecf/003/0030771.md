 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.xxvii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xxvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXVII.—Of Subjoining a Psalm.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.xxvii-p2"></a>The more diligent in prayer are wont to subjoin in their prayers the “Hallelujah,” <a name="fr_8933"></a><a href="#fn_8933"><font size="1">8934</font></a>
  and such kind of psalms, in the closes of which the company respond. And, of course, every institution is excellent which, for the extolling and honouring of God, aims unitedly to bring Him enriched prayer as a choice victim. <a name="fr_8934"></a><a href="#fn_8934"><font size="1">8935</font></a>
 </p>
