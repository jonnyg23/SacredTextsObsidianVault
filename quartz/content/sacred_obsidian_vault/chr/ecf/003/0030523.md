 <p> <a name="v.vi.viii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.viii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.viii-p1.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—The Names and Descent of Other Æons; First Half a Score, Then Two More, and Ultimately a Dozen Besides. These Thirty Constitute the Pleroma. But Why Be So Capricious as to Stop at Thirty?</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.viii-p2"></a>For, behold, when the second Tetrad—Sermo and Vita, Homo and Ecclesia <a name="fr_6696"></a><a href="#fn_6696"><font size="1">6697</font></a>
 —had borne fruit to the Fathers glory, having an intense desire of themselves to present to the Father something similar of their own, they bring other issue into being <a name="fr_6697"></a><a href="#fn_6697"><font size="1">6698</font></a>
 —conjugal of course, as the others were <a name="fr_6698"></a><a href="#fn_6698"><font size="1">6699</font></a>
 —by the union of the twofold nature. On the one hand, Sermo and Vita pour out at a birth a half-score of Æons; on the other hand, Homo and Ecclesia produce a couple more, so furnishing an equipoise to their parents, since this pair with the other ten make up just as many as they did themselves procreate. I now give the names of the half-score whom I have mentioned:  Bythios (<i>Profound</i>) and Mixis (<i>Mixture</i>), Ageratos (<i>Never old</i>) and Henosis (<i>Union</i>), Autophyes (<i>Essential nature</i>) and Hedone (<i>Pleasure</i>), Acinetos (<i>Immoveable</i>) and Syncrasis (<i>Commixture</i>,) Monogenes (<i>Only-begotten</i>) and Macaria (<i>Happiness</i>). On the other hand, these will make up the number twelve (to which I have also referred):  Paracletus (<i>Comforter</i>) and Pistis (<i>Faith</i>), Patricas (<i>Paternal</i>) and Elpis (<i>Hope</i>), Metricos (<i>Maternal</i>) and Agape (<i>Love</i>), Ainos (<i>Praise</i>) <a name="fr_6699"></a><a href="#fn_6699"><font size="1">6700</font></a>
  and Synesis (<i>Intelligence</i>), Ecclesiasticus (<i>Son of Ecclesia</i>) and Macariotes (<i>Blessedness</i>), Theletus <a name="fr_6700"></a><a href="#fn_6700"><font size="1">6701</font></a>
  (<i>Perfect</i>) and Sophia (<i>Wisdom</i>).  I cannot help <a name="fr_6701"></a><a href="#fn_6701"><font size="1">6702</font></a>
  here quoting from a like example what may serve to show the import of <a name="page_508"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 508</font></a> <a name="v.vi.viii-Page_508"></a>these names. In the schools of Carthage there was once a certain Latin rhetorician, an excessively cool fellow, <a name="fr_6702"></a><a href="#fn_6702"><font size="1">6703</font></a>
  whose name was Phosphorus. He was personating a man of valour, and wound up <a name="fr_6703"></a><a href="#fn_6703"><font size="1">6704</font></a>
  with saying, “I come to you, excellent citizens, from battle, with victory for myself, with happiness for you, full of honour, covered with glory, the favourite of fortune, the greatest of men, decked with triumph.” And forthwith his scholars begin to shout for the school of Phosphorus, <span class="Greek"><a name="v.vi.viii-p10.1"></a>φεῦ</span> <a name="fr_6704"></a><a href="#fn_6704"><font size="1">6705</font></a>
  (<i>ah!</i>).  Are you a believer in <a name="fr_6705"></a><a href="#fn_6705"><font size="1">6706</font></a>
  Fortunata, and Hedone, and Acinetus, and Theletus? Then shout out your <span class="Greek"><a name="v.vi.viii-p12.1"></a>φεῦ</span> for the school of Ptolemy. <a name="fr_6706"></a><a href="#fn_6706"><font size="1">6707</font></a>
  This must be that mystery of the Pleroma, the fulness of the thirty-fold divinity. Let us see what special attributes <a name="fr_6707"></a><a href="#fn_6707"><font size="1">6708</font></a>
  belong to these numbers—four, and eight, and twelve. Meanwhile with the number thirty all fecundity ceases. The generating force and power and desire of the Æons is spent. <a name="fr_6708"></a><a href="#fn_6708"><font size="1">6709</font></a>
  As if there were not still left some strong rennet for curdling numbers. <a name="fr_6709"></a><a href="#fn_6709"><font size="1">6710</font></a>
  As if no other names were to be got out of the pages hall! <a name="fr_6710"></a><a href="#fn_6710"><font size="1">6711</font></a>
  For why are there not sets of fifty and of a hundred procreated? Why, too, are there no comrades and boon companions <a name="fr_6711"></a><a href="#fn_6711"><font size="1">6712</font></a>
  named <i>for them</i>?</p>
