 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.xxii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.xxii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXII.—Origin of the Devil, in the Criminal Excess of the Sorrow of Achamoth. The Devil, Called Also Munditenens, Actually Wiser Than the Demiurge, Although His Work.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.xxii-p2"></a>The odium felt amongst them <a name="fr_6829"></a><a href="#fn_6829"><font size="1">6830</font></a>
  against the devil is the more excusable, <a name="fr_6830"></a><a href="#fn_6830"><font size="1">6831</font></a>
  even because the peculiarly sordid character of his origin justifies it. <a name="fr_6831"></a><a href="#fn_6831"><font size="1">6832</font></a>
  For he is supposed by them to have had his origin in that criminal excess <a name="fr_6832"></a><a href="#fn_6832"><font size="1">6833</font></a>
  of her <a name="fr_6833"></a><a href="#fn_6833"><font size="1">6834</font></a>
  sorrow, from which they also derive the birth of the angels, and demons, and all the wicked spirits. Yet they affirm that the devil is the work of the Demiurge, and they call him Munditenens <a name="fr_6834"></a><a href="#fn_6834"><font size="1">6835</font></a>
  (<i>Ruler of the World</i>), and maintain that, as he is of a spiritual nature, he has a better knowledge of the things above than the Demiurge, an animal being. He deserves from them the pre-eminence which all heresies provide him with.</p>
