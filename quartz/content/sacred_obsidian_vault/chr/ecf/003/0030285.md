 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.xix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIX.—Appeal, in Discussion of Heresy, Lies Not to the Scriptures. The Scriptures Belong Only to Those Who Have the Rule of Faith.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.xix-p2"></a>Our appeal, therefore, must not be made to the Scriptures; nor must controversy be admitted on points in which victory will either be impossible, <a name="fr_2045"></a><a href="#fn_2045"><font size="1">2046</font></a>
  or uncertain, or not certain enough. <a name="fr_2046"></a><a href="#fn_2046"><font size="1">2047</font></a>
  But even if a discussion from the Scriptures <a name="fr_2047"></a><a href="#fn_2047"><font size="1">2048</font></a>
  should not turn out in such a way as to place both sides on a par, (yet) the natural order of things would require that this point should be first proposed, which is now the only one which we must discuss: “With whom lies that very faith to which the Scriptures belong. <a name="fr_2048"></a><a href="#fn_2048"><font size="1">2049</font></a>
  From what and through whom, and when, and to whom, has been handed down that rule, <a name="fr_2049"></a><a href="#fn_2049"><font size="1">2050</font></a>
  by which men become Christians?” For wherever it shall be manifest that the true Christian rule and faith shall be, <a name="page_252"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 252</font></a> <a name="v.iii.xix-Page_252"></a><i>there</i> will likewise be the true Scriptures and expositions thereof, and all the Christian traditions.</p>
