 <p class="Centered"><a name="vi.iii.xxi-p2"></a> ————————————</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xxi-p3"></a>The argument (p. 673, note 6,) is conclusive, but not clear. The disciples of John must have been baptized by him, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#029">Luke vii. 29-30</a><a name="vi.iii.xxi-p3.1"></a>) and “all the people,” must have included those whom Jesus called.  But, this was not Christs baptism:  See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#002">Acts 19:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#005">5</a><a name="vi.iii.xxi-p3.2"></a>. Compare note 8, p. 673. And see the American Editors “Apollos.”</p>
