 <p> <a name="vi.vii.xiii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.vii.xiii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.vii.xiii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIII.—Of Bodily Patience.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.vii.xiii-p2"></a>Thus far, finally, of patience simple and uniform, and as it exists merely in the <i>mind</i>:  though in many forms likewise I labour after it in <i>body</i>, for the purpose of “winning the Lord;” <a name="fr_9149"></a><a href="#fn_9149"><font size="1">9150</font></a>
  inasmuch as it is <i>a quality</i> which has been exhibited by the Lord Himself in bodily virtue as well; if it is true that the ruling mind easily communicates the gifts <a name="fr_9150"></a><a href="#fn_9150"><font size="1">9151</font></a>
  of the Spirit with its <i>bodily</i> habitation. What, therefore, is the business of Patience <i>in the body</i>? In the first place, <i>it is</i> the affliction <a name="fr_9151"></a><a href="#fn_9151"><font size="1">9152</font></a>
  of the flesh—a victim <a name="fr_9152"></a><a href="#fn_9152"><font size="1">9153</font></a>
  able to appease the Lord by means of the sacrifice of humiliation—in making a libation to the Lord of sordid <a name="fr_9153"></a><a href="#fn_9153"><font size="1">9154</font></a>
  raiment, together with scantiness of food, content with simple diet and the pure drink of water <a name="fr_9154"></a><a href="#fn_9154"><font size="1">9155</font></a>
  in conjoining fasts <i>to all this</i>; in inuring herself to sackcloth and ashes.  This <i>bodily</i> patience adds a grace to our prayers for good, a strength to our prayers against evil; this opens the ears of Christ <i>our</i> God, <a name="fr_9155"></a><a href="#fn_9155"><font size="1">9156</font></a>
  dissipates severity, elicits clemency.  Thus that Babylonish king, <a name="fr_9156"></a><a href="#fn_9156"><font size="1">9157</font></a>
  after being exiled from human form in his seven years squalor and neglect, because he had offended the Lord; by the bodily immolation of patience not only recovered his kingdom, but—what is more to be desired by a man—made satisfaction to God. Further, if we set down in order the higher and happier grades of bodily patience, (we find that) it is she who is entrusted by holiness with the care of continence of the flesh: she keeps the widow, <a name="fr_9157"></a><a href="#fn_9157"><font size="1">9158</font></a>
  and sets on the virgin the seal <a name="fr_9158"></a><a href="#fn_9158"><font size="1">9159</font></a>
  and raises the self-made eunuch to the realms of heaven. <a name="fr_9159"></a><a href="#fn_9159"><font size="1">9160</font></a>
  That which springs from a virtue of the <i>mind</i> is perfected in the <i>flesh; and</i>, finally, by the patience of the flesh, does battle under persecution.  If flight press hard, the flesh wars with <a name="fr_9160"></a><a href="#fn_9160"><font size="1">9161</font></a>
  the inconvenience of flight; if imprisonment over<a name="page_716"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 716</font></a> <a name="vi.vii.xiii-Page_716"></a>take <a name="fr_9161"></a><a href="#fn_9161"><font size="1">9162</font></a>
  us, the flesh (still was) in bonds, the flesh in the gyve, the flesh in solitude, <a name="fr_9162"></a><a href="#fn_9162"><font size="1">9163</font></a>
  and in that want of light, and in that patience of the worlds misusage. <a name="fr_9163"></a><a href="#fn_9163"><font size="1">9164</font></a>
  When, however, it is led forth unto the final proof of happiness, <a name="fr_9164"></a><a href="#fn_9164"><font size="1">9165</font></a>
  unto the occasion of the second baptism, <a name="fr_9165"></a><a href="#fn_9165"><font size="1">9166</font></a>
  unto the act of ascending the divine seat, no patience is more needed <i>there</i> than <i>bodily</i> patience. If the “spirit is willing, but the flesh,” <i>without</i> patience, “weak,” <a name="fr_9166"></a><a href="#fn_9166"><font size="1">9167</font></a>
  where, <i>save in patience</i>, is the safety of the spirit, and of the flesh itself?  But when the Lord says this about the flesh, pronouncing it “weak,” He shows what need there is of strengthening, it—that is by patience—to meet <a name="fr_9167"></a><a href="#fn_9167"><font size="1">9168</font></a>
  every preparation for subverting or punishing faith; that it may bear with all constancy stripes, fire, cross, beasts, sword; all which prophets and apostles, by enduring, conquered!</p>
