 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.vi.xxx-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.vi.xxx-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXX.—The Lax and Dangerous Views of This Sect Respecting Good Works. That These are Unnecessary to the Spiritual Man.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.vi.xxx-p2"></a>For this reason it is that they neither regard works <a name="fr_6882"></a><a href="#fn_6882"><font size="1">6883</font></a>
  as necessary for themselves, nor do they observe any of the calls of duty, eluding even the necessity of martyrdom on any pretence which may suit their pleasure. For this rule, (they say), is enjoined upon the animal seed, in order that the salvation, which we do not possess by any privilege of our state, <a name="fr_6883"></a><a href="#fn_6883"><font size="1">6884</font></a>
  we may work out by right <a name="fr_6884"></a><a href="#fn_6884"><font size="1">6885</font></a>
  of our conduct. Upon us, who are of an imperfect nature, <a name="fr_6885"></a><a href="#fn_6885"><font size="1">6886</font></a>
  is imprinted the mark of this (animal) seed, because we are reckoned <i>as sprung</i> from the loves of Theletus, <a name="fr_6886"></a><a href="#fn_6886"><font size="1">6887</font></a>
  and consequently as an abortion, just as their mother was. But <i>woe</i> to us indeed, should we in any point transgress the yoke of discipline, should we grow dull in the works of holiness and justice, should we desire to make our confession anywhere else, I know not where, and not before the powers of this world at the tribunals of the chief magistrates! <a name="fr_6887"></a><a href="#fn_6887"><font size="1">6888</font></a>
  As for them, however, they may prove their nobility by the dissoluteness <a name="fr_6888"></a><a href="#fn_6888"><font size="1">6889</font></a>
  of their life and their diligence <a name="fr_6889"></a><a href="#fn_6889"><font size="1">6890</font></a>
  in sin, since Achamoth fawns on them as her own; for she, too, found sin no unprofitable pursuit. Now it is held amongst them, that, for the purpose of honouring the celestial <a name="page_518"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 518</font></a> <a name="v.vi.xxx-Page_518"></a>marriages, <a name="fr_6890"></a><a href="#fn_6890"><font size="1">6891</font></a>
  it is necessary to contemplate and celebrate the mystery always by cleaving to a companion, that, is to a woman; otherwise (they account any man) degenerate, and a bastard <a name="fr_6891"></a><a href="#fn_6891"><font size="1">6892</font></a>
  to the truth, who spends his life in the world without loving a woman or uniting himself to her. Then what is to become of the eunuchs whom we see amongst them?</p>
