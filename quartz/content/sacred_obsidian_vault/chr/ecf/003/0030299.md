 <p> <a name="v.iii.xxxiii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.xxxiii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xxxiii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXIII.—Present Heresies (Seedlings of the Tares Noted by the Sacred Writers) Already Condemned in Scripture.  This Descent of Later Heresy from the Earlier Traced in Several Instances.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.xxxiii-p2"></a>Besides all this, I add a review of the doctrines themselves, which, existing as they did <a name="page_259"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 259</font></a> <a name="v.iii.xxxiii-Page_259"></a>in the days of the apostles, were both exposed and denounced by the said apostles. For by this method they will be more easily reprobated, <a name="fr_2190"></a><a href="#fn_2190"><font size="1">2191</font></a>
  when they are detected to have been even then in existence, or at any rate to have been seedlings <a name="fr_2191"></a><a href="#fn_2191"><font size="1">2192</font></a>
  of the (tares) which then were. Paul, in his first epistle to the Corinthians, sets his mark on certain who denied and doubted the resurrection. <a name="fr_2192"></a><a href="#fn_2192"><font size="1">2193</font></a>
  This opinion was the especial property of the Sadducees. <a name="fr_2193"></a><a href="#fn_2193"><font size="1">2194</font></a>
  A part of it, however, is maintained by Marcion and Apelles and Valentinus, and all other impugners of the resurrection. Writing also to the Galatians, he inveighs against such men as observed and defend circumcision and the (Mosaic) law. <a name="fr_2194"></a><a href="#fn_2194"><font size="1">2195</font></a>
  Thus runs Hebions heresy. Such also as “forbid to marry” he reproaches in his instructions to Timothy. <a name="fr_2195"></a><a href="#fn_2195"><font size="1">2196</font></a>
  Now, this is the teaching of Marcion and his follower Apelles. (The apostle) directs a similar blow <a name="fr_2196"></a><a href="#fn_2196"><font size="1">2197</font></a>
  against those who said that “the resurrection was past already.” <a name="fr_2197"></a><a href="#fn_2197"><font size="1">2198</font></a>
  Such an opinion did the Valentinians assert of themselves. When again he mentions “endless genealogies,” <a name="fr_2198"></a><a href="#fn_2198"><font size="1">2199</font></a>
  one also recognises Valentinus, in whose system a certain Æon, whosoever he be, <a name="fr_2199"></a><a href="#fn_2199"><font size="1">2200</font></a>
  of a new name, and that not one only, generates of his own grace <a name="fr_2200"></a><a href="#fn_2200"><font size="1">2201</font></a>
  Sense and Truth; and these in like manner produce of themselves Word <a name="fr_2201"></a><a href="#fn_2201"><font size="1">2202</font></a>
  and Life, while these again afterwards beget Man and the Church. From these primary eight <a name="fr_2202"></a><a href="#fn_2202"><font size="1">2203</font></a>
  ten other Æons after them spring, and then the twelve others arise with their wonderful names, to complete the mere story of the thirty Æons. The same apostle, when disapproving of those who are “in bondage to elements,” <a name="fr_2203"></a><a href="#fn_2203"><font size="1">2204</font></a>
  points us to some dogma of Hermogenes, who introduces matter as having no beginning, <a name="fr_2204"></a><a href="#fn_2204"><font size="1">2205</font></a>
  and then compares it with God, who has no beginning. <a name="fr_2205"></a><a href="#fn_2205"><font size="1">2206</font></a>
  By thus making the mother of the elements a goddess, he has it in his power “to be in bondage” to a being which he puts on a par with <a name="fr_2206"></a><a href="#fn_2206"><font size="1">2207</font></a>
  God. John, however, in the Apocalypse is charged to chastise those “who eat things sacrificed to idols,” and “who commit fornication.” <a name="fr_2207"></a><a href="#fn_2207"><font size="1">2208</font></a>
  There are even now another sort of Nicolaitans. Theirs is called the Gaian <a name="fr_2208"></a><a href="#fn_2208"><font size="1">2209</font></a>
  heresy. But in his epistle he especially designates those as “Antichrists” who “denied that Christ was come in the flesh,” <a name="fr_2209"></a><a href="#fn_2209"><font size="1">2210</font></a>
  and who refused to think that Jesus was the Son of God. The one dogma Marcion maintained; the other, Hebion. <a name="fr_2210"></a><a href="#fn_2210"><font size="1">2211</font></a>
  The doctrine, however, of Simons sorcery, which inculcated the worship of angels, <a name="fr_2211"></a><a href="#fn_2211"><font size="1">2212</font></a>
  was itself actually reckoned amongst idolatries and condemned by the Apostle Peter in Simons own person.</p>
