 <p> <a name="vi.iii.xx"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.xx-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.xx-p1.1"></a>Chapter XX.—Of Preparation For, and Conduct After, the Reception of Baptism.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.xx-p2"></a>They who are about to enter baptism ought to pray with repeated prayers, fasts, and bendings of the knee, and vigils all the night through, and with the confession of all by- <a name="page_679"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 679</font></a> <a name="vi.iii.xx-Page_679"></a>gone sins, that they may express <i>the meaning</i> even of the baptism of John: “They were baptized,” saith (the Scripture), “confessing their own sins.” <a name="fr_8743"></a><a href="#fn_8743"><font size="1">8744</font></a>
  To us it is matter for thankfulness if we do <i>now</i> publicly confess our iniquities or our turpitudes: <a name="fr_8744"></a><a href="#fn_8744"><font size="1">8745</font></a>
  for we do at the same time both make satisfaction <a name="fr_8745"></a><a href="#fn_8745"><font size="1">8746</font></a>
  for our former sins, by mortification of our flesh and spirit, and lay beforehand the foundation of defences against the temptations which will closely follow. “Watch and pray,” saith (the Lord), “lest ye fall into temptation.” <a name="fr_8746"></a><a href="#fn_8746"><font size="1">8747</font></a>
  And the reason, I believe, why they <i>were</i> tempted was, that they fell asleep; so that they deserted the Lord when apprehended, and he who continued to stand by Him, and used the sword, even denied Him thrice: for withal the word had gone before, that “no one <i>un</i>tempted should attain the celestial kingdoms.” <a name="fr_8747"></a><a href="#fn_8747"><font size="1">8748</font></a>
  The Lord Himself forthwith after <i>baptism</i> <a name="fr_8748"></a><a href="#fn_8748"><font size="1">8749</font></a>
  temptations surrounded, when in forty days He had kept fast. “Then,” some one will say, “it becomes <i>us</i>, too, rather to fast <i>after</i> baptism.” <a name="fr_8749"></a><a href="#fn_8749"><font size="1">8750</font></a>
  Well, and who forbids you, unless it be the necessity for joy, and the thanksgiving for salvation? But so far as I, with my poor powers, understand, the Lord figuratively retorted upon Israel the reproach <i>they had cast on the Lord</i>. <a name="fr_8750"></a><a href="#fn_8750"><font size="1">8751</font></a>
  For the people, after crossing the sea, and being carried about in the desert during forty years, although they were there nourished with divine supplies, nevertheless were more mindful of their belly and their gullet than of God. Thereupon the Lord, driven apart into desert places after baptism, <a name="fr_8751"></a><a href="#fn_8751"><font size="1">8752</font></a>
  showed, by maintaining a fast of forty days, that the man of God lives “not by bread alone,” but “by the word of God;” <a name="fr_8752"></a><a href="#fn_8752"><font size="1">8753</font></a>
  and that temptations incident to fulness or immoderation of appetite are shattered by abstinence. Therefore, blessed <i>ones</i>, whom the grace of God awaits, when you ascend from that most sacred font <a name="fr_8753"></a><a href="#fn_8753"><font size="1">8754</font></a>
  of your new birth, and spread your hands <a name="fr_8754"></a><a href="#fn_8754"><font size="1">8755</font></a>
  for the first time in the house of your mother, <a name="fr_8755"></a><a href="#fn_8755"><font size="1">8756</font></a>
  together with your brethren, ask from the Father, ask from the Lord, that His own specialties of grace <i>and</i> distributions of gifts <a name="fr_8756"></a><a href="#fn_8756"><font size="1">8757</font></a>
  may be supplied you. “Ask,” saith He, “and ye shall receive.” <a name="fr_8757"></a><a href="#fn_8757"><font size="1">8758</font></a>
  Well, you <i>have</i> asked, and have received; you <i>have</i> knocked, and it has been opened to you.  Only, I pray that, when you are asking, you be mindful likewise of Tertullian the sinner. <a name="fr_8758"></a><a href="#fn_8758"><font size="1">8759</font></a>
 </p>
