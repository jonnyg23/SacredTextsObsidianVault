 <p> <a name="v.iii.ix"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.ix-p1.1"></a>Chapter IX.—The Research After Definite Truth Enjoined on Us. When We Have Discovered This, We Should Be Content.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.ix-p2"></a>I now purposely <a name="fr_1955"></a><a href="#fn_1955"><font size="1">1956</font></a>
  relinquish this ground of argument. Let it be granted, that the words, “Seek, and ye shall find,” were addressed to all men (equally). Yet even here ones aim is <a name="fr_1956"></a><a href="#fn_1956"><font size="1">1957</font></a>
  carefully to determine <a name="fr_1957"></a><a href="#fn_1957"><font size="1">1958</font></a>
  the sense of the words <a name="fr_1958"></a><a href="#fn_1958"><font size="1">1959</font></a>
  consistently with <a name="fr_1959"></a><a href="#fn_1959"><font size="1">1960</font></a>
  (that reason), <a name="fr_1960"></a><a href="#fn_1960"><font size="1">1961</font></a>
  which is the guiding principle <a name="fr_1961"></a><a href="#fn_1961"><font size="1">1962</font></a>
  in all interpretation. (Now) no divine saying is so unconnected <a name="fr_1962"></a><a href="#fn_1962"><font size="1">1963</font></a>
  <a name="page_248"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 248</font></a> <a name="v.iii.ix-Page_248"></a>and diffuse, that its <i>words</i> only are to be insisted on, and their <i>connection</i> left undetermined. But at the outset I lay down (this position) that there is some one, and therefore definite, thing taught by Christ, which the Gentiles are by all means bound to believe, and for that purpose to “seek,” in order that they may be able, when they have “found” it, to believe. However, <a name="fr_1963"></a><a href="#fn_1963"><font size="1">1964</font></a>
  there can be no indefinite seeking for that which has been taught as one only definite thing. You must “seek” until you “find,” and believe when you have found; nor have you anything further to do but to keep what you have believed provided you believe this besides, that nothing else is to be believed, and therefore nothing else is to be sought, after you have found and believed what has been taught by Him who charges you to seek no other thing than that which He has taught. <a name="fr_1964"></a><a href="#fn_1964"><font size="1">1965</font></a>
  When, indeed, any man doubts about this, proof will be forthcoming, <a name="fr_1965"></a><a href="#fn_1965"><font size="1">1966</font></a>
  that we have in our possession <a name="fr_1966"></a><a href="#fn_1966"><font size="1">1967</font></a>
  that which was taught by Christ.  Meanwhile, such is my confidence in our proof, that I anticipate it, in the shape of an admonition to certain persons, not “to seek” anything beyond what they have believed—that this is what they ought to have sought, how to avoid <a name="fr_1967"></a><a href="#fn_1967"><font size="1">1968</font></a>
  interpreting, “Seek, and ye shall find,” without regard to the rule of reason.</p>
