 <p class="c30"><a name="vi.iii.i-p5"></a><span class="c1"><a name="vi.iii.i-p5.1"></a>Chapter I.—Introduction. Origin of the Treatise.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iii.i-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="vi.iii.i-p6.1"></a>Happy</span> is our <a name="fr_8541"></a><a href="#fn_8541"><font size="1">8542</font></a>
  sacrament of water, in that, by washing away the sins of our early blindness, we are set free <i>and admitted</i> into eternal life! A treatise on this matter will not be superfluous; instructing not only such as are just becoming formed (in the faith), but them who, content with having simply believed, without full examination of the grounds <a name="fr_8542"></a><a href="#fn_8542"><font size="1">8543</font></a>
  of the traditions, carry (in mind), through ignorance, an untried <i>though</i> probable faith. The consequence is, that a viper of the Cainite heresy, lately conversant in this quarter, has carried away a great number with her most venomous doctrine, making it her first aim to destroy baptism. Which is quite in accordance with nature; for vipers and asps and basilisks themselves generally do affect arid and waterless places. But we, little fishes, after the example of our <span class="Greek"><a name="vi.iii.i-p8.1"></a>ΙΧΘΥΣ</span> <a name="fr_8543"></a><a href="#fn_8543"><font size="1">8544</font></a>
  Jesus Christ, are born in water, nor have we safety in any other way than by permanently abiding in water; so that most monstrous creature, who had no right to teach even sound doctrine, <a name="fr_8544"></a><a href="#fn_8544"><font size="1">8545</font></a>
  knew full well how to kill the little fishes, by taking them away from the water!</p>
