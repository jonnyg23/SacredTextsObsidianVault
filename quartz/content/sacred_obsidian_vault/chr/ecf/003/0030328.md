 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iv.ii.xiv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.ii.xiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XIV.—All Portions of Creation Attest the Excellence of the Creator, Whom Marcion Vilifies. His Inconsistency Herein Exposed. Marcions Own God Did Not Hesitate to Use the Creators Works in Instituting His Own Religion.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iv.ii.xiv-p2"></a>Now, when you make merry with those minuter animals, which their glorious Maker has purposely endued with a profusion of instincts and resources, <a name="fr_2487"></a><a href="#fn_2487"><font size="1">2488</font></a>
 —thereby teaching us that greatness has its proofs in lowliness, just as (according to the apostle) there is power even in infirmity <a name="fr_2488"></a><a href="#fn_2488"><font size="1">2489</font></a>
 —imitate, if you can, the cells of the bee, the hills of the ant, the webs of the spider, and the threads of the silkworm; endure, too, if you know how, those very creatures <a name="fr_2489"></a><a href="#fn_2489"><font size="1">2490</font></a>
  which infest your couch and house, the poisonous ejections of the blister-beetle, <a name="fr_2490"></a><a href="#fn_2490"><font size="1">2491</font></a>
  the spikes of the fly, and the gnats sheath and sting. What of the greater animals, when the small ones so affect you with pleasure or pain, that you cannot even in their case despise their Creator? Finally, take a circuit round your own self; survey man within and without. Even this handiwork of our God will be pleasing to you, inasmuch as your own lord, that better god, loved it so well, <a name="fr_2491"></a><a href="#fn_2491"><font size="1">2492</font></a>
  and for your sake was at the pains <a name="fr_2492"></a><a href="#fn_2492"><font size="1">2493</font></a>
  of descending from the third heaven to these poverty-stricken <a name="fr_2493"></a><a href="#fn_2493"><font size="1">2494</font></a>
  elements, and for the same reason was actually crucified in this sorry <a name="fr_2494"></a><a href="#fn_2494"><font size="1">2495</font></a>
  apartment of the Creator. Indeed, up to the present time, he has not disdained the water which the Creator made wherewith he washes his people; nor the oil with which he anoints them; nor that union of honey and milk wherewithal he gives them the nourishment <a name="fr_2495"></a><a href="#fn_2495"><font size="1">2496</font></a>
  of children; nor the bread by which he represents his own proper body, thus requiring in his very sacraments the “beggarly <a name="fr_2496"></a><a href="#fn_2496"><font size="1">2497</font></a>
  elements” of the Creator. You, however, are a disciple above his master, and a servant above his lord; you have a higher reach of discernment than his; you destroy what he requires. I wish to examine whether you are at least honest in this, so as to have no longing for those things which you destroy. You are an enemy to the sky, and yet you are glad to catch its freshness in your houses. You disparage the earth, although the elemental parent <a name="fr_2497"></a><a href="#fn_2497"><font size="1">2498</font></a>
  of your own flesh, as if it were your undoubted enemy, and yet you extract from it all its fatness <a name="fr_2498"></a><a href="#fn_2498"><font size="1">2499</font></a>
  for your food. The sea, too, you reprobate, but are continually using its produce, which you account the more sacred diet. <a name="fr_2499"></a><a href="#fn_2499"><font size="1">2500</font></a>
  If I should offer you a rose, you will not disdain its Maker. You hypocrite, however much of abstinence you use to show yourself a Marcionite, that is, a repudiator of your Maker (for if the world displeased you, such abstinence ought to have been affected by you as a martyrdom), you will have to associate yourself with <a name="fr_2500"></a><a href="#fn_2500"><font size="1">2501</font></a>
  the Creators material production, into what element soever you shall be dissolved. How hard is this obstinacy of yours! You vilify the things in which you both live and die.</p>
