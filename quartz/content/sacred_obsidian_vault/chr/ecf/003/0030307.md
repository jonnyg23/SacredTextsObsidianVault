 <p> <a name="v.iii.xli"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.xli-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xli-p1.1"></a>Chapter XLI.—The Conduct of Heretics: Its Frivolity, Worldliness, and Irregularity. The Notorious Wantonness of Their Women.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.xli-p2"></a>I must not omit an account of the conduct <a name="fr_2277"></a><a href="#fn_2277"><font size="1">2278</font></a>
  also of the heretics—how frivolous it is, how worldly, how merely human, without seriousness, without authority, without discipline, as suits their creed. To begin with, it is doubtful who is a catechumen, and who a believer; they have all access alike, they hear alike, they pray alike—even heathens, if any such happen to come among them. “That which is holy they will cast to the dogs, and their pearls,” although (to be sure) they are not real ones, “they will fling to the swine.” <a name="fr_2278"></a><a href="#fn_2278"><font size="1">2279</font></a>
  Simplicity they will have to consist in the overthrow of discipline, attention to which on our part they call brothelry. <a name="fr_2279"></a><a href="#fn_2279"><font size="1">2280</font></a>
  Peace also they huddle up <a name="fr_2280"></a><a href="#fn_2280"><font size="1">2281</font></a>
  anyhow with all comers; for it matters not to them, however different be their treatment of subjects, provided only they can conspire together to storm the citadel of the one only Truth. All are puffed up, all offer you knowledge.  Their catechumens are perfect before they are full-taught. <a name="fr_2281"></a><a href="#fn_2281"><font size="1">2282</font></a>
  The very women of these heretics, how wanton they are! For they are bold enough to teach, to dispute, to enact exorcisms, to undertake <a name="fr_2282"></a><a href="#fn_2282"><font size="1">2283</font></a>
  cures—it may be even to baptize. <a name="fr_2283"></a><a href="#fn_2283"><font size="1">2284</font></a>
  Their ordinations, are carelessly administered, <a name="fr_2284"></a><a href="#fn_2284"><font size="1">2285</font></a>
  capricious, changeable. <a name="fr_2285"></a><a href="#fn_2285"><font size="1">2286</font></a>
  At one time they put <i>novices</i> in office; at another time, men who are bound to some secular employment; <a name="fr_2286"></a><a href="#fn_2286"><font size="1">2287</font></a>
  at another, persons who have apostatized from us, to bind them by vainglory, since they cannot by the truth. Nowhere is promotion easier than in the camp of rebels, where the mere fact of being there is a foremost service. <a name="fr_2287"></a><a href="#fn_2287"><font size="1">2288</font></a>
  And so it comes to pass that to-day one man is their bishop, to-morrow another; to-day he is a deacon who to-morrow is a reader; to-day he is a presbyter who tomorrow is a layman. For even on laymen do they impose the functions of priesthood.</p>
