 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.iv.x-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.x-p1.1"></a>Chapter X.—We May Superadd Prayers of Our Own to the Lords Prayer.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.iv.x-p2"></a>Since, however, the Lord, the Foreseer of human necessities, <a name="fr_8823"></a><a href="#fn_8823"><font size="1">8824</font></a>
  said separately, after delivering His Rule of Prayer, “Ask, and ye shall receive;” <a name="fr_8824"></a><a href="#fn_8824"><font size="1">8825</font></a>
  and <i>since</i> there are petitions which are made according to the circumstances of each individual; our additional wants have the right—after beginning with the legitimate and customary prayers as a foundation, as it were—of rearing an outer superstructure of petitions, yet with remembrance of <i>the Masters</i> precepts.</p>
