 <p> <a name="vi.vii.iv"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="vi.vii.iv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.vii.iv-p1.1"></a>Chapter IV.—Duty of Imitating Our Master Taught Us by Slaves. Even by Beasts. Obedient Imitation is Founded on Patience.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="vi.vii.iv-p2"></a>Therefore, if we see all servants of probity and right feeling shaping their conduct suitably to the disposition of their lord; if, that is, the art of deserving favour is obedience, <a name="fr_9030"></a><a href="#fn_9030"><font size="1">9031</font></a>
  while the rule of obedience is a compliant subjection: how much more does it behove <i>us</i> to be found with a character in accordance with our Lord,—servants as we are of the <a name="page_709"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 709</font></a> <a name="vi.vii.iv-Page_709"></a>living God, whose judgment on His servants turns not on a fetter or a cap of freedom, but on an eternity either of penalty or of salvation; for the shunning of which severity or the courting of which liberality there needs a diligence in obedience <a name="fr_9031"></a><a href="#fn_9031"><font size="1">9032</font></a>
  as great as are the comminations themselves which the severity utters, or the promises which the liberality freely makes. <a name="fr_9032"></a><a href="#fn_9032"><font size="1">9033</font></a>
  And yet we exact obedience <a name="fr_9033"></a><a href="#fn_9033"><font size="1">9034</font></a>
  not from <i>men</i> only, who have the bond of their slavery under their chin, <a name="fr_9034"></a><a href="#fn_9034"><font size="1">9035</font></a>
  or in any other legal way are debtors to obedience, <a name="fr_9035"></a><a href="#fn_9035"><font size="1">9036</font></a>
  but even from cattle, <a name="fr_9036"></a><a href="#fn_9036"><font size="1">9037</font></a>
  even from brutes; <a name="fr_9037"></a><a href="#fn_9037"><font size="1">9038</font></a>
  understanding that they have been provided and delivered for our uses by the Lord. Shall, then, <i>creatures</i> which God makes subject to us be better than we in the discipline of obedience? <a name="fr_9038"></a><a href="#fn_9038"><font size="1">9039</font></a>
  Finally, (the creatures) which obey, acknowledge <i>their masters</i>. Do we hesitate to listen diligently to Him to whom alone we are subjected—that is, the Lord?  But how unjust is it, how ungrateful likewise, not to repay from yourself the same which, through the indulgence of your neighbour, you obtain from others, to him through whom you obtain it!  Nor needs there more words on the exhibition of obedience <a name="fr_9039"></a><a href="#fn_9039"><font size="1">9040</font></a>
  due from us to the Lord God; for the acknowledgment <a name="fr_9040"></a><a href="#fn_9040"><font size="1">9041</font></a>
  of God understands what is incumbent on it.  Lest, however, we seem to have inserted <i>remarks</i> on obedience <a name="fr_9041"></a><a href="#fn_9041"><font size="1">9042</font></a>
  as something irrelevant, (let us remember) that obedience <a name="fr_9042"></a><a href="#fn_9042"><font size="1">9043</font></a>
  itself is drawn from patience. Never does an <i>im</i>patient <i>man</i> render it, or a <i>patient</i> fail to find pleasure <a name="fr_9043"></a><a href="#fn_9043"><font size="1">9044</font></a>
  in it. Who, then, could treat largely (enough) of the good of that <i>patience</i> which the Lord God, the Demonstrator and Acceptor of all good things, carried about in His own self? <a name="fr_9044"></a><a href="#fn_9044"><font size="1">9045</font></a>
  To whom, again, would it be doubtful that every good thing ought, because it pertains <a name="fr_9045"></a><a href="#fn_9045"><font size="1">9046</font></a>
  to God, to be earnestly pursued with the whole mind by such as pertain to God? By means of which (considerations) both commendation and exhortation <a name="fr_9046"></a><a href="#fn_9046"><font size="1">9047</font></a>
  on the subject of patience are briefly, and as it were in the compendium of a prescriptive rule, established. <a name="fr_9047"></a><a href="#fn_9047"><font size="1">9048</font></a>
 </p>
