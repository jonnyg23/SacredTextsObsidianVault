 </p><p class="c30"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI.—The Romans Provided Gods for Birth, Nay, Even Before Birth, to Death. Much Indelicacy in This System.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iv.viii.ii.xi-p2"></a>And you are not content to assert the divinity of such as were once known to you, whom you heard and handled, and whose portraits have been painted, and actions recounted, and memory retained amongst you; but men insist upon consecrating with a heavenly life <a name="fr_952"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">953</font></a>
  I know not what incorporeal, inanimate shadows, and the <i>mere</i> names of things—dividing mans entire existence amongst separate powers even from his conception in the womb: so that there is a god Consevius, <a name="fr_953"></a><a href="#fn_953"><font size="1">954</font></a>
  to preside over concubital generation; and Fluviona, <a name="fr_954"></a><a href="#fn_954"><font size="1">955</font></a>
  to preserve the (growth of the) infant in the womb; after these come Vitumnus and Sentinus, <a name="fr_955"></a><a href="#fn_955"><font size="1">956</font></a>
  through whom the babe begins to have life and its earliest sensation; then Diespiter, <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">957</font></a>
  by whose office the child accomplishes its birth. But when women begin their parturition, Candelifera also <i>comes in</i> aid, since childbearing requires the light of the candle; and other goddesses there are <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">958</font></a>
  who get their names from the parts they bear in the stages of travail. There were two Carmentas likewise, according to the general view: to one of them, called Postverta, belonged the function of assisting the birth of the introverted child; while the other, Prosa, <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">959</font></a>
  executed the like office for the rightly born.  The god Farinus was so called from (his inspiring) the first utterance; while others believed in Locutius from his gift of speech. Cunina <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">960</font></a>
  is present as the protector of the childs deep slumber, and supplies to it refreshing rest. To lift them (when fallen) <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">961</font></a>
  there is Levana, and along with her Rumina. <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">962</font></a>
  It is a wonderful oversight that no gods were appointed for cleaning up the filth of children. Then, to preside over their first pap and earliest drink you have Potina and Edula; <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">963</font></a>
  to teach the child to stand erect is the work of Statina, <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">964</font></a>
  whilst Adeona helps him to come <i>to dear Mamma</i>, and Abeona to toddle off again; then there is Domiduca, <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">965</font></a>
  (to bring home the bride;) and the goddess Mens, to influence the mind to either good or evil. <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">966</font></a>
  They have likewise Volumnus and Voleta, <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">967</font></a>
  to control the will; Paventina, (the goddess) of fear; Venilia, of hope; <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">968</font></a>
  Volupia, of pleasure; <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">969</font></a>
  Præstitia, of beauty. <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">970</font></a>
  Then, again, they give his name to Peragenor, <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">971</font></a>
  from his teaching men to go through their work; to Consus, from his sug<a name="page_140"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 140</font></a> <a name="iv.viii.ii.xi-Page_140"></a>gesting to them counsel. Juventa is their guide on assuming the manly gown, and “bearded Fortune” when they come to full manhood. <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">972</font></a>
  If I must touch on their nuptial duties, there is Afferenda whose appointed function is to see to the offering of the dower; but fie on you! you have your Mutunus <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">973</font></a>
  and Tutunus and Pertunda <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">974</font></a>
  and Subigus and the goddess Prema and likewise Perfica. <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_974"><font size="1">975</font></a>
  O spare yourselves, ye impudent gods! No one is present at the secret struggles of married life. Those very few persons who have a wish that way, go away and blush for very shame in the midst of their joy.</p>
