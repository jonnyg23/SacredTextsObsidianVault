 <p class="c28"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p2"></a><span class="c6"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p2.1"></a>Wherein Christ is shown to be the Son of God, Who created the world; to have been predicted by the prophets; to have taken human flesh like our own, by a real incarnation.</span></p> 
 <p class="Centered"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p3"></a> ————————————</p> 
 <p class="c30"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p4"></a><span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p4.1"></a>Chapter I.—Introductory; A Brief Statement of the Preceding Argument in Connection with the Subject of This Book.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="v.iv.iv.i-p5.1"></a>Following</span> the track of my original treatise, the loss of which we are steadily proceeding <a name="fr_3091"></a><a href="#fn_3091"><font size="1">3092</font></a>
  to restore, we come now, in the order of our subject, to treat of Christ, although this be a work of supererogation, <a name="fr_3092"></a><a href="#fn_3092"><font size="1">3093</font></a>
  after the proof which we have gone through that there is but one only God. For no doubt it has been already ruled with sufficient clearness, that Christ must be regarded as pertaining to <a name="fr_3093"></a><a href="#fn_3093"><font size="1">3094</font></a>
  no other God than the Creator, when it has been determined that no other God but the Creator should be the object of our faith. Him did Christ so expressly preach, whilst the apostles one after the other also so clearly affirmed that Christ belonged to <a name="fr_3094"></a><a href="#fn_3094"><font size="1">3095</font></a>
  no other God than Him whom He Himself preached—that is, the Creator—that no mention of a second God (nor, accordingly, of a second Christ) was ever agitated previous to Marcions scandal.  This is most easily proved by an examination <a name="fr_3095"></a><a href="#fn_3095"><font size="1">3096</font></a>
  of both the apostolic and the heretical churches, <a name="fr_3096"></a><a href="#fn_3096"><font size="1">3097</font></a>
  from which we are forced to declare that <i>there</i> is undoubtedly a subversion of the rule (of faith), where any opinion is found of later date, <a name="fr_3097"></a><a href="#fn_3097"><font size="1">3098</font></a>
 —a point which I have inserted in my first book. <a name="fr_3098"></a><a href="#fn_3098"><font size="1">3099</font></a>
  A discussion of it would unquestionably be of value even now, when we are about to make a separate examination into (the subject of) Christ; because, whilst proving Christ to be the Creators <i>Son</i>, we are effectually shutting out the God of Marcion. Truth should employ all her available resources, and in no limping way. <a name="fr_3099"></a><a href="#fn_3099"><font size="1">3100</font></a>
  In our compendious rules of faith, however, she has it all her own way. <a name="fr_3100"></a><a href="#fn_3100"><font size="1">3101</font></a>
  But I have resolved, like an earnest man, <a name="fr_3101"></a><a href="#fn_3101"><font size="1">3102</font></a>
  to meet my adversary every way and everywhere in the madness of his heresy, which is so great, that he has found it easier to assume that that Christ has come who was never heard of, than He who has always been predicted.</p>
