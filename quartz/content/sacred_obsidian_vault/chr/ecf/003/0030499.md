 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.v.xxx-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.v.xxx-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXX.—Another Passage in the Sacred History of the Creation, Released from the Mishandling of Hermogenes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.v.xxx-p2"></a>The following words will in like manner apparently corroborate the conjecture of Hermogenes, “And darkness was upon the face of the deep, and the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the water;” <a name="fr_6425"></a><a href="#fn_6425"><font size="1">6426</font></a>
  as if these blended <a name="fr_6426"></a><a href="#fn_6426"><font size="1">6427</font></a>
  substances, presented us with arguments for his massive pile <i>of Matter</i>. <a name="fr_6427"></a><a href="#fn_6427"><font size="1">6428</font></a>
  Now, so discriminating an enumeration of certain and distinct elements (as we have in this passage), which severally designates “darkness,” “the deep,” “the Spirit of God,” “the waters,” forbids the inference that anything confused or (from such confusion) uncertain is meant. Still more, when He ascribed to them their own places, <a name="fr_6428"></a><a href="#fn_6428"><font size="1">6429</font></a>
  “darkness <i>on the face of</i> the deep,” “the Spirit <i>upon the face of</i> the waters,” He repudiated all confusion in the substances; and by demonstrating their separate position, <a name="fr_6429"></a><a href="#fn_6429"><font size="1">6430</font></a>
  He demonstrated also their distinction.  Most absurd, indeed, would it be that Matter, which is introduced to our view as “without form,” should have its “formless” condition maintained by so many words indicative of form, <a name="fr_6430"></a><a href="#fn_6430"><font size="1">6431</font></a>
  without any intimation of what that confused body <a name="fr_6431"></a><a href="#fn_6431"><font size="1">6432</font></a>
  is, which must of course be supposed to be unique, <a name="fr_6432"></a><a href="#fn_6432"><font size="1">6433</font></a>
  since it is without form. <a name="fr_6433"></a><a href="#fn_6433"><font size="1">6434</font></a>
  For that which is without form is uniform; but even <a name="fr_6434"></a><a href="#fn_6434"><font size="1">6435</font></a>
  that which is without form, when it is blended together <a name="fr_6435"></a><a href="#fn_6435"><font size="1">6436</font></a>
  from various component parts, <a name="fr_6436"></a><a href="#fn_6436"><font size="1">6437</font></a>
  must necessarily have one outward appearance; <a name="fr_6437"></a><a href="#fn_6437"><font size="1">6438</font></a>
  and it has not any appearance, until it has the one appearance (which comes) from many parts <i>combined</i>. <a name="fr_6438"></a><a href="#fn_6438"><font size="1">6439</font></a>
  Now Matter either had those specific parts <a name="fr_6439"></a><a href="#fn_6439"><font size="1">6440</font></a>
  within itself, from the words indicative of which it had to be understood—I mean “darkness,” and “the deep,” and “the Spirit,” and “the waters”—or it had them not. If it had them, how is it introduced as being “without form?” <a name="fr_6440"></a><a href="#fn_6440"><font size="1">6441</font></a>
  If it had them not, how does it become known? <a name="fr_6441"></a><a href="#fn_6441"><font size="1">6442</font></a>
 </p>
