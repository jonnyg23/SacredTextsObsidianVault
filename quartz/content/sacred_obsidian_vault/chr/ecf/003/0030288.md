 <p> <a name="v.iii.xxii"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.iii.xxii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iii.xxii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXII.—Attempt to Invalidate This Rule of Faith Rebutted. The Apostles Safe Transmitters of the Truth. Sufficiently Taught at First, and Faithful in the Transmission.</span></p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="v.iii.xxii-p2"></a>But inasmuch as the proof is so near at hand, <a name="fr_2068"></a><a href="#fn_2068"><font size="1">2069</font></a>
  that if it were at once produced there would be nothing left to be dealt with, let us give way for a while to the opposite side, if they think that they can find some means of invalidating this rule, just as if no proof were forthcoming from us. They usually tell us that the apostles did not know all things: (but herein) they are impelled by the same madness, whereby they turn round to the very opposite point, <a name="fr_2069"></a><a href="#fn_2069"><font size="1">2070</font></a>
  and declare that the apostles certainly knew all things, but did not deliver all things to all persons,—in either case exposing Christ to blame for having sent forth apostles who had either too much ignorance, or too little simplicity. What man, then, of sound mind can possibly suppose that they were ignorant of anything, whom the Lord ordained to be masters (or teachers), <a name="fr_2070"></a><a href="#fn_2070"><font size="1">2071</font></a>
  keeping them, as He did, inseparable (from Himself) in their attendance, in their discipleship, in their society, to whom, “when they were alone, He used to expound” all things <a name="fr_2071"></a><a href="#fn_2071"><font size="1">2072</font></a>
  which were obscure, telling them that “to them it was given to know those mysteries,” <a name="fr_2072"></a><a href="#fn_2072"><font size="1">2073</font></a>
  which it was not permitted the people to understand? Was anything withheld from the knowledge of Peter, who is called “the rock on which the church should be built,” <a name="fr_2073"></a><a href="#fn_2073"><font size="1">2074</font></a>
  who also obtained “the keys of the kingdom of heaven,” <a name="fr_2074"></a><a href="#fn_2074"><font size="1">2075</font></a>
  with the power of “loosing and binding in heaven and on earth?” <a name="fr_2075"></a><a href="#fn_2075"><font size="1">2076</font></a>
  Was anything, again, concealed from John, the Lords most beloved disciple, who used to lean on His breast <a name="fr_2076"></a><a href="#fn_2076"><font size="1">2077</font></a>
  to whom alone the Lord pointed Judas out as the traitor, <a name="fr_2077"></a><a href="#fn_2077"><font size="1">2078</font></a>
  whom He commended to Mary as a son in His own stead? <a name="fr_2078"></a><a href="#fn_2078"><font size="1">2079</font></a>
  Of what could He have meant those to be ignorant, to whom He even exhibited His own glory with Moses and Elias, and the Fathers voice moreover, from heaven? <a name="fr_2079"></a><a href="#fn_2079"><font size="1">2080</font></a>
  Not as if He thus disapproved <a name="fr_2080"></a><a href="#fn_2080"><font size="1">2081</font></a>
  of all the rest, but because “by three witnesses must every word be established.” <a name="fr_2081"></a><a href="#fn_2081"><font size="1">2082</font></a>
  After the same fashion, <a name="fr_2082"></a><a href="#fn_2082"><font size="1">2083</font></a>
  too, (I suppose,) were they ignorant to whom, after His resurrection also, He vouchsafed, as they were journeying together, “to expound all the Scriptures.” <a name="fr_2083"></a><a href="#fn_2083"><font size="1">2084</font></a>
  No doubt <a name="fr_2084"></a><a href="#fn_2084"><font size="1">2085</font></a>
  He had once said, “I have yet many things to say unto you, but ye cannot hear them now;” but even then He added, “When He, the Spirit of truth, shall come, He will lead you into all truth.” <a name="fr_2085"></a><a href="#fn_2085"><font size="1">2086</font></a>
  He (thus) shows that there was nothing of which they were ignorant, to whom He had promised the future attainment of all truth by help of the Spirit of truth.  And assuredly He fulfilled His promise, since it is proved in the Acts of the Apostles that the Holy Ghost did come down. Now they who reject that Scripture <a name="fr_2086"></a><a href="#fn_2086"><font size="1">2087</font></a>
  can neither belong to the Holy Spirit, seeing that they cannot acknowledge that the Holy Ghost has been sent as yet to the disciples, nor can they presume to claim to be a church themselves <a name="fr_2087"></a><a href="#fn_2087"><font size="1">2088</font></a>
  who positively have no means of proving when, and with what swaddling-clothes <a name="fr_2088"></a><a href="#fn_2088"><font size="1">2089</font></a>
  this body was established. Of so much importance is it to them not to have any proofs for the things which they maintain, lest along with them there be introduced damaging exposures <a name="fr_2089"></a><a href="#fn_2089"><font size="1">2090</font></a>
  of those things which they mendaciously devise.</p>
