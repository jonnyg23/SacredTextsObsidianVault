 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p1.1"></a>Section XIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">1012</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, There hath not arisen among those whom women have borne a greater than John the Baptist; but he that is little now in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p4"></a>[2] [Arabic, p. 54]  <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">1013</font></a>
 And all the people which heard, and the publicans, justified <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">1014</font></a>
  God, for [3] they had been baptized with the baptism of John.   <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1014"><font size="1">1015</font></a>
 But the Pharisees and the scribes wronged <a name="fr_1015"></a><a href="#fn_1015"><font size="1">1016</font></a>
  the purpose of God in themselves, in that they were not baptized of [4] him.   <a name="fr_1016"></a><a href="#fn_1016"><font size="1">1017</font></a>
 And from the days of John the Baptist until now the kingdom of heaven is [5] snatched away by violence.   <a name="fr_1017"></a><a href="#fn_1017"><font size="1">1018</font></a>
 The law and the prophets <i>were</i> until John; and after that, the kingdom of God is preached, and all press to enter it:   <a name="fr_1018"></a><a href="#fn_1018"><font size="1">1019</font></a>
 and they that exert themselves [6, 7] snatch it away.   <a name="fr_1019"></a><a href="#fn_1019"><font size="1">1020</font></a>
 All the prophets and the law until John prophesied.   <a name="fr_1020"></a><a href="#fn_1020"><font size="1">1021</font></a>
 And if ye [8] will, then receive <i>it</i>, that he is Elijah, which is to come.   <a name="fr_1021"></a><a href="#fn_1021"><font size="1">1022</font></a>
 Whosoever hath ears that hear [9] let him hear.   <a name="fr_1022"></a><a href="#fn_1022"><font size="1">1023</font></a>
 Easier is the perishing of heaven and earth, than the passing away of [10] one point of the law.   <a name="fr_1023"></a><a href="#fn_1023"><font size="1">1024</font></a>
 To whom then shall I liken the people of this generation, <a name="fr_1024"></a><a href="#fn_1024"><font size="1">1025</font></a>
  and [11] to whom are they like?   <a name="fr_1025"></a><a href="#fn_1025"><font size="1">1026</font></a>
 They are like the children sitting in the market, which call to their companions, and say, We sang to you, and ye danced not; we wailed to you, [12] and ye wept not.   <a name="fr_1026"></a><a href="#fn_1026"><font size="1">1027</font></a>
 John the Baptist came neither eating bread nor drinking wine; [13] and ye said, He hath demons:   <a name="fr_1027"></a><a href="#fn_1027"><font size="1">1028</font></a>
 and the Son of man came eating and drinking; and ye said, Behold, a gluttonous man, and a drinker of wine, and an associate of publicans [14, 15] and sinners!   <a name="fr_1028"></a><a href="#fn_1028"><font size="1">1029</font></a>
 And wisdom was justified of all her children.   <a name="fr_1029"></a><a href="#fn_1029"><font size="1">1030</font></a>
 And when he said that, they came to the house.  And there gathered unto him again multitudes, [16] so that they found not bread to eat.   <a name="fr_1030"></a><a href="#fn_1030"><font size="1">1031</font></a>
 And while he was casting out a devil which was dumb, when he cast out that devil, that dumb <i>man</i> spake.  And the multitudes [17] [Arabic, p. 55] marvelled.   <a name="fr_1031"></a><a href="#fn_1031"><font size="1">1032</font></a>
 And the Pharisees, when they heard, said, This <i>man</i> doth not cast out the devils, except by Beelzebul the chief of the demons, which is in him.  [18, 19]  <a name="fr_1032"></a><a href="#fn_1032"><font size="1">1033</font></a>
 And others requested of him a sign from heaven, to tempt him.   <a name="fr_1033"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">1034</font></a>
 And Jesus knew their thoughts, and said unto them in parables, Every kingdom that withstandeth itself shall become desolate; and every house or city that disagreeth with itself shall not [20] stand:   <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">1035</font></a>
 and if a devil cast out a devil, he withstandeth himself; neither shall he be [21] able to stand, but his end shall be.   <a name="fr_1035"></a><a href="#fn_1035"><font size="1">1036</font></a>
 Then how now shall his kingdom stand? for ye [22] said that I cast out devils by Beelzebul.   <a name="fr_1036"></a><a href="#fn_1036"><font size="1">1037</font></a>
 And if I by Beelzebul cast out the devils, then your children, by what do they cast them out?  And for this cause they shall [23] be judges against you.   <a name="fr_1037"></a><a href="#fn_1037"><font size="1">1038</font></a>
 But if I by the Spirit of God cast out devils, then the kingdom [24] of God is come near unto you.   <a name="fr_1038"></a><a href="#fn_1038"><font size="1">1039</font></a>
 Or how can a man enter into the house of a valiant <i>man</i>, and seize his garments, <a name="fr_1039"></a><a href="#fn_1039"><font size="1">1040</font></a>
  if he do not beforehand secure himself <a name="fr_1040"></a><a href="#fn_1040"><font size="1">1041</font></a>
  from [25] that valiant <i>man?</i> and then will he cut off <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1041"><font size="1">1042</font></a>
  his house.   <a name="fr_1042"></a><a href="#fn_1042"><font size="1">1043</font></a>
 But when the valiant man is [26] armed, guarding his house, his possessions are in peace.   <a name="fr_1043"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">1044</font></a>
 But if one come who is more valiant than he, he overcometh him, and taketh his whole armour, on which [27] he relieth, and divideth his spoil.   <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">1045</font></a>
 Whosoever is not with me is against me; and [28] whosoever gathereth not with me scattereth abroad.   <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">1046</font></a>
 For this reason I say unto you, [Arabic, p. 56] that all sins and blasphemies with which men blaspheme shall be forgiven [29] them:   <a name="fr_1046"></a><a href="#fn_1046"><font size="1">1047</font></a>
 but whosoever shall blaspheme against the Holy Spirit, there is no [30] forgiveness for him for ever, but he is deserving of eternal punishment:   <a name="fr_1047"></a><a href="#fn_1047"><font size="1">1048</font></a>
 because they <a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xiv-Page_66"></a>[31] said that he had an unclean spirit.   <a name="fr_1048"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">1049</font></a>
 And he said also, Every one that speaketh a word against the Son of man, it shall be forgiven him; but whosoever speaketh against the Holy Spirit, it shall not be forgiven him, neither in this world, nor in the world to [32] come.   <a name="fr_1049"></a><a href="#fn_1049"><font size="1">1050</font></a>
 Either ye must make a good tree <a name="fr_1050"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">1051</font></a>
  and its fruit good; or ye must make an evil [33] tree <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">1052</font></a>
  and its fruit evil:  for the tree is known by its fruit.   <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1052"><font size="1">1053</font></a>
 Ye children of vipers, how can ye, being evil, speak good things? from the overflowings of the heart the mouth [34] speaketh.   <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1053"><font size="1">1054</font></a>
 The good man from the good treasures which are in his heart bringeth forth good things; and the wicked man from the evil treasures which are in his [35] heart bringeth forth evils.   <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1054"><font size="1">1055</font></a>
 I say unto you, that every idle word which men shall [36] speak, they shall give an answer for in the day of judgement:   <a name="fr_1055"></a><a href="#fn_1055"><font size="1">1056</font></a>
 for by thy sayings thou shalt be justified, and by thy sayings thou shalt be judged.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p49"></a>[37]  <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1056"><font size="1">1057</font></a>
 And he said to the multitudes, When ye see the clouds appear from the west, [38] straightway ye say that there cometh rain; and so it cometh to pass.   <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1057"><font size="1">1058</font></a>
 And when [39] the south wind bloweth, ye say that there will be heat; and it cometh to pass.   <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1058"><font size="1">1059</font></a>
 And when the evening is come, ye say, It <i>will be</i> fair weather, for the heaven has become [40] red.   <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1059"><font size="1">1060</font></a>
 And in the morning ye say, To-day there will be severe weather, for the redness [Arabic, p. 57] of the heaven is paling.   <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1060"><font size="1">1061</font></a>
 <i>Ye</i> hypocrites, ye know to examine the face of the heaven and the earth; but the signs of this time ye know not to discern.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p55"></a>[41]  <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1061"><font size="1">1062</font></a>
 Then they brought to him one possessed of a demon, dumb <i>and</i> blind; and he [42] healed him, so that the dumb and blind began to speak and see.   <a name="fr_1062"></a><a href="#fn_1062"><font size="1">1063</font></a>
 And all the multitudes wondered, and said, Is this, think you, the son of David?</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p58"></a>[43]  <a name="fr_1063"></a><a href="#fn_1063"><font size="1">1064</font></a>
 And the apostles returned unto Jesus, and told him everything that they had [44] done and wrought. <a name="fr_1064"></a><a href="#fn_1064"><font size="1">1065</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1065"></a><a href="#fn_1065"><font size="1">1066</font></a>
 And he said unto them, Come, let us go into the desert alone, and rest ye a little.  And many were going and returning, and they had not leisure, not even to eat bread.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xiv-p62"></a>[45]  <a name="fr_1066"></a><a href="#fn_1066"><font size="1">1067</font></a>
 And after that, there came to <i>him</i> one of the Pharisees, and besought him that he would eat bread with him.  And he entered into the house of that Pharisee, and [46] reclined.   <a name="fr_1067"></a><a href="#fn_1067"><font size="1">1068</font></a>
 And there was in that city a woman <i>that was</i> a sinner; and when she knew that he was sitting in the house of that Pharisee, she took a box of sweet ointment, [47] and stood behind him,  <a name="fr_1068"></a><a href="#fn_1068"><font size="1">1069</font></a>
 towards his feet, weeping, and began to wet his feet with her tears, and to wipe them with the hair of her head, and to kiss his feet, and [48] anoint them with the sweet ointment.   <a name="fr_1069"></a><a href="#fn_1069"><font size="1">1070</font></a>
 And when that <a name="fr_1070"></a><a href="#fn_1070"><font size="1">1071</font></a>
  Pharisee saw <i>it</i>, who invited him, he thought within himself, and said, This <i>man</i>, if he were a prophet, would know who she is and what is her history:  for the woman which touched him was a sinner.</p>
