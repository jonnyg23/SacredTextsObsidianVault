 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxxiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p1.1"></a>Section XXXIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_2314"></a><a href="#fn_2314"><font size="1">2315</font></a>
 Then went the Pharisees and considered how they might ensnare him in a word, [2] and deliver him into the power of the judge, <a name="fr_2315"></a><a href="#fn_2315"><font size="1">2316</font></a>
  and into the power of the ruler.   <a name="fr_2316"></a><a href="#fn_2316"><font size="1">2317</font></a>
 And they sent unto him their disciples, with the kinsfolk of Herod; and they said unto him, [Arabic, p. 129] Teacher, we know that thou speakest the truth, and teachest the way of God with equity, <a name="fr_2317"></a><a href="#fn_2317"><font size="1">2318</font></a>
  and art not lifted up <a name="fr_2318"></a><a href="#fn_2318"><font size="1">2319</font></a>
  by any man:  for thou actest not so as to [3] be seen of any man.   <a name="fr_2319"></a><a href="#fn_2319"><font size="1">2320</font></a>
 Tell us now, What is thy opinion?  Is it lawful that we should [4] pay the tribute to Cæsar, or not? shall we give, or shall we not give?   <a name="fr_2320"></a><a href="#fn_2320"><font size="1">2321</font></a>
 But Jesus knew [5] their deceit, and said unto them,  <a name="fr_2321"></a><a href="#fn_2321"><font size="1">2322</font></a>
 Why tempt ye me, ye hypocrites?  Shew me the [6] penny of the tribute.   <a name="fr_2322"></a><a href="#fn_2322"><font size="1">2323</font></a>
 So they brought unto him a penny.  Jesus said unto them, To whom belongeth this image and inscription?  They said unto him, To Cæsar.  [7, 8]  <a name="fr_2323"></a><a href="#fn_2323"><font size="1">2324</font></a>
 He said unto them, Give what is Cæsars to Cæsar, and what is Gods to God.   <a name="fr_2324"></a><a href="#fn_2324"><font size="1">2325</font></a>
 And they could not make him slip in a <i>single</i> word before the people; and they marvelled at his word, and refrained.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p14"></a>[9]  <a name="fr_2325"></a><a href="#fn_2325"><font size="1">2326</font></a>
 And on that day came the Sadducees, and said unto him, <a name="fr_2326"></a><a href="#fn_2326"><font size="1">2327</font></a>
  There is no life for [10] the dead.   <a name="fr_2327"></a><a href="#fn_2327"><font size="1">2328</font></a>
 And they asked him, and said unto him, Teacher, Moses said unto us, If a man die, not having children, let his brother take his wife, and raise up seed [11] for his brother.   <a name="fr_2328"></a><a href="#fn_2328"><font size="1">2329</font></a>
 Now there <i>were</i> with us seven brethren:  and the first took a wife, [12] and died without children;  <a name="fr_2329"></a><a href="#fn_2329"><font size="1">2330</font></a>
 and the second took his wife, and died without children; [13]  <a name="fr_2330"></a><a href="#fn_2330"><font size="1">2331</font></a>
 and the third also took her; and in like manner the seven of them also, and they [14, 15] died without leaving children.   <a name="fr_2331"></a><a href="#fn_2331"><font size="1">2332</font></a>
 And last of them all the woman died also.   <a name="fr_2332"></a><a href="#fn_2332"><font size="1">2333</font></a>
 At the resurrection, then, which of these seven shall have this woman? for all of them took [16] her.   <a name="fr_2333"></a><a href="#fn_2333"><font size="1">2334</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto them, Is it not for this that ye have erred, [17] because ye know not the scriptures, nor the power of God?   <a name="fr_2334"></a><a href="#fn_2334"><font size="1">2335</font></a>
 And the sons of this [18] world take wives, and the women become the mens; <a name="fr_2335"></a><a href="#fn_2335"><font size="1">2336</font></a>
   <a name="fr_2336"></a><a href="#fn_2336"><font size="1">2337</font></a>
 but those that have become worthy of that world, and the resurrection from among the dead, do <a name="fr_2337"></a><a href="#fn_2337"><font size="1">2338</font></a>
  not take <a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-Page_96"></a>[19] [Arabic, p. 130] wives, and the women also do <a name="fr_2338"></a><a href="#fn_2338"><font size="1">2339</font></a>
  not become the mens.   <a name="fr_2339"></a><a href="#fn_2339"><font size="1">2340</font></a>
 Nor is it possible that they should die; but they <a name="fr_2340"></a><a href="#fn_2340"><font size="1">2341</font></a>
  are like the angels, and are the children of [20] God, because they have become the children of the resurrection.   <a name="fr_2341"></a><a href="#fn_2341"><font size="1">2342</font></a>
 For in <a name="fr_2342"></a><a href="#fn_2342"><font size="1">2343</font></a>
  the resurrection of the dead, have ye not read in the book of Moses, how from the bush God said unto him, I am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob?  [21]  <a name="fr_2343"></a><a href="#fn_2343"><font size="1">2344</font></a>
 And God is not <i>the God</i> of the dead, but of the living:  for all of them are alive with him.  And ye have erred greatly.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p34"></a>[22, 23]  <a name="fr_2344"></a><a href="#fn_2344"><font size="1">2345</font></a>
 And when the multitudes heard, they were wondering at his teaching.   <a name="fr_2345"></a><a href="#fn_2345"><font size="1">2346</font></a>
 And [24] some of the scribes answered and said unto him, Teacher, thou hast well said.   <a name="fr_2346"></a><a href="#fn_2346"><font size="1">2347</font></a>
 But the rest of the Pharisees, when they saw his silencing the Sadducees on this point, gathered against him to contend with him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p38"></a>[25]  <a name="fr_2347"></a><a href="#fn_2347"><font size="1">2348</font></a>
 And one of the scribes, of those that knew the law, when he saw the excellence [26] of his answer to them, desired to try him, and said unto him,  <a name="fr_2348"></a><a href="#fn_2348"><font size="1">2349</font></a>
 What shall I do to inherit eternal life? and,  <a name="fr_2349"></a><a href="#fn_2349"><font size="1">2350</font></a>
 Which of the commandments is greater, and has precedence [27] in the law?   <a name="fr_2350"></a><a href="#fn_2350"><font size="1">2351</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, The first of all the commandments is, Hear, O [28] Israel; The Lord our God, the Lord is one:   <a name="fr_2351"></a><a href="#fn_2351"><font size="1">2352</font></a>
 and thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy thought, and with all thy [29, 30] strength.   <a name="fr_2352"></a><a href="#fn_2352"><font size="1">2353</font></a>
 This is the great and preëminent <a name="fr_2353"></a><a href="#fn_2353"><font size="1">2354</font></a>
  commandment.   <a name="fr_2354"></a><a href="#fn_2354"><font size="1">2355</font></a>
 And the second, which is like it, is, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.  And another commandment [31] greater than these two there is not.   <a name="fr_2355"></a><a href="#fn_2355"><font size="1">2356</font></a>
 On these two commandments, then, are hung the [32] [Arabic, p. 131] law and the prophets.   <a name="fr_2356"></a><a href="#fn_2356"><font size="1">2357</font></a>
 That scribe said unto him, Excellent! my Master; <a name="fr_2357"></a><a href="#fn_2357"><font size="1">2358</font></a>
  thou hast said truly that he is one, and there is no other outside of him:  [33]  <a name="fr_2358"></a><a href="#fn_2358"><font size="1">2359</font></a>
 and that a man should love him with all his heart, and with all his thought, and with all his soul, and with all his strength, and that he should love his neighbour as [34] himself, is better than all savours and sacrifices.   <a name="fr_2359"></a><a href="#fn_2359"><font size="1">2360</font></a>
 And Jesus saw him that he had answered wisely; and he answered and said unto him, Thou art not far from the [35, 36] kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_2360"></a><a href="#fn_2360"><font size="1">2361</font></a>
 Thou hast spoken rightly:  do this, and thou shalt live.   <a name="fr_2361"></a><a href="#fn_2361"><font size="1">2362</font></a>
 And he, as his desire was to justify himself, said unto him, And who is my neighbour?  [37]  <a name="fr_2362"></a><a href="#fn_2362"><font size="1">2363</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, A man went down from Jerusalem to Jericho; and the robbers fell upon him, and stripped <a name="fr_2363"></a><a href="#fn_2363"><font size="1">2364</font></a>
  him, and beat him, his life remaining in him <i>but</i> little, <a name="fr_2364"></a><a href="#fn_2364"><font size="1">2365</font></a>
  [38] and went away.   <a name="fr_2365"></a><a href="#fn_2365"><font size="1">2366</font></a>
 And it happened that there came down a certain priest that way; [39] and he saw him, and passed by.   <a name="fr_2366"></a><a href="#fn_2366"><font size="1">2367</font></a>
 And likewise a Levite also came and reached [40] that place, and saw him, and passed by.   <a name="fr_2367"></a><a href="#fn_2367"><font size="1">2368</font></a>
 And a certain Samaritan, as he journeyed, [41] came to <a name="fr_2368"></a><a href="#fn_2368"><font size="1">2369</font></a>
  the place where he was, and saw him, and had compassion on him,  <a name="fr_2369"></a><a href="#fn_2369"><font size="1">2370</font></a>
 and came near, and bound up his strokes, <a name="fr_2370"></a><a href="#fn_2370"><font size="1">2371</font></a>
  and poured on them wine and oil; and he set <a name="fr_2371"></a><a href="#fn_2371"><font size="1">2372</font></a>
  him on the ass, and brought him to the inn, and expended his care upon him.  [42]  <a name="fr_2372"></a><a href="#fn_2372"><font size="1">2373</font></a>
 And on the morrow of that day he took out two pence, and gave them to the innkeeper, and said unto him, Care for him; and if thou spendest upon him more, [43] when I return, I shall give thee.   <a name="fr_2373"></a><a href="#fn_2373"><font size="1">2374</font></a>
 Who of these three now, thinkest thou, is nearest [44] to him that fell among the robbers?   <a name="fr_2374"></a><a href="#fn_2374"><font size="1">2375</font></a>
 And he said unto him, He that had compassion [45] [Arabic, p. 132] on him.   <a name="fr_2375"></a><a href="#fn_2375"><font size="1">2376</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, Go, and do thou also likewise.  And no man dared afterwards to ask him anything.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-p68"></a>[46]  <a name="fr_2376"></a><a href="#fn_2376"><font size="1">2377</font></a>
 And he was teaching every day in the temple.  But the chief priests and scribes and the elders of the people sought to destroy him:   <a name="fr_2377"></a><a href="#fn_2377"><font size="1">2378</font></a>
 and they could <a name="fr_2378"></a><a href="#fn_2378"><font size="1">2379</font></a>
  not <i>find</i> what [47] they should do with him; and all the people were hanging upon him to hear him.  [48]  <a name="fr_2379"></a><a href="#fn_2379"><font size="1">2380</font></a>
 And many of the multitude believed on him, and said, The Messiah, when he [49] cometh, can it be that he will do more than these signs that this <i>man</i> doeth?   <a name="fr_2380"></a><a href="#fn_2380"><font size="1">2381</font></a>
 And the Pharisees heard the multitudes say that of him; and the chief priests sent [50] officers <a name="fr_2381"></a><a href="#fn_2381"><font size="1">2382</font></a>
  to seize him.   <a name="fr_2382"></a><a href="#fn_2382"><font size="1">2383</font></a>
 And Jesus said unto them, I am with you <i>but</i> a short time <a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 97</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxxiv-Page_97"></a>[51] yet, and I go to him that sent me.   <a name="fr_2383"></a><a href="#fn_2383"><font size="1">2384</font></a>
 And ye shall seek me, and shall not find me:  [52] and where I shall be, ye shall not be able to come.   <a name="fr_2384"></a><a href="#fn_2384"><font size="1">2385</font></a>
 The Jews said within themselves, Whither hath this <i>man</i> determined to go that we shall not be able <a name="fr_2385"></a><a href="#fn_2385"><font size="1">2386</font></a>
  <i>to find</i> him? can it be that he is determined to go to the regions of the nations, <a name="fr_2386"></a><a href="#fn_2386"><font size="1">2387</font></a>
  and teach [53] the heathen?   <a name="fr_2387"></a><a href="#fn_2387"><font size="1">2388</font></a>
 What is this word that he said, Ye shall seek me, and shall not find me:  and where I am, ye cannot come?</p>
