 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xxiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p1.1"></a>Section XXIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1678"></a><a href="#fn_1678"><font size="1">1679</font></a>
 And he said unto them, Verily I say unto you, There be here now some standing that shall not taste death, until they see the kingdom of God come <a name="fr_1679"></a><a href="#fn_1679"><font size="1">1680</font></a>
  with strength,  <a name="fr_1680"></a><a href="#fn_1680"><font size="1">1681</font></a>
 and the Son of man who cometh in his kingdom.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p6"></a>[2]  <a name="fr_1681"></a><a href="#fn_1681"><font size="1">1682</font></a>
 And after six days Jesus took Simon Cephas, and James, and John his brother, [3] and brought them up into a high mountain, the three of them only.   <a name="fr_1682"></a><a href="#fn_1682"><font size="1">1683</font></a>
 And while they [4] were praying, Jesus changed, and became after the fashion of another person;  <a name="fr_1683"></a><a href="#fn_1683"><font size="1">1684</font></a>
 and his face shone like the sun, and his raiment was very white like the snow, and as [5] the light of lightning, so that nothing on earth can whiten <a name="fr_1684"></a><a href="#fn_1684"><font size="1">1685</font></a>
  like it.   <a name="fr_1685"></a><a href="#fn_1685"><font size="1">1686</font></a>
 And there appeared [6] unto him Moses and Elijah talking to Jesus.   <a name="fr_1686"></a><a href="#fn_1686"><font size="1">1687</font></a>
 And they thought that the time [7] of his decease which was to be accomplished at Jerusalem was come.   <a name="fr_1687"></a><a href="#fn_1687"><font size="1">1688</font></a>
 And Simon and those that were with him were heavy in the drowsiness of sleep; and with effort they roused themselves, and saw his glory, and those two men that were standing with him.  [8] [Arabic, p. 93]  <a name="fr_1688"></a><a href="#fn_1688"><font size="1">1689</font></a>
 And when they began to depart from him, Simon said unto Jesus, My [9] Master, it is good for us to be here:   <a name="fr_1689"></a><a href="#fn_1689"><font size="1">1690</font></a>
 and if thou wilt, we will make here three tabernacles; one for thee, and one for Moses, and one for Elijah;  <a name="fr_1690"></a><a href="#fn_1690"><font size="1">1691</font></a>
 not knowing [10] what he said, because of the fear which took possession of them.   <a name="fr_1691"></a><a href="#fn_1691"><font size="1">1692</font></a>
 And while he [11] was yet saying that, a bright cloud overshadowed them.   <a name="fr_1692"></a><a href="#fn_1692"><font size="1">1693</font></a>
 And when they saw Moses [12] and Elijah that they had entered into that cloud, they feared again.   <a name="fr_1693"></a><a href="#fn_1693"><font size="1">1694</font></a>
 And a voice was heard out of the cloud, saying, This is my beloved Son, whom I have chosen; [13] hear ye therefore him.   <a name="fr_1694"></a><a href="#fn_1694"><font size="1">1695</font></a>
 And when this voice was heard, Jesus was found alone.  [14]  <a name="fr_1695"></a><a href="#fn_1695"><font size="1">1696</font></a>
 And the disciples, when they heard the voice, fell on their faces from the fear which [15] took hold of them.   <a name="fr_1696"></a><a href="#fn_1696"><font size="1">1697</font></a>
 And Jesus came and touched them and said, Arise, be not [16] afraid.   <a name="fr_1697"></a><a href="#fn_1697"><font size="1">1698</font></a>
 And they lifted up their eyes, and saw Jesus as he was.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p24"></a>[17]  <a name="fr_1698"></a><a href="#fn_1698"><font size="1">1699</font></a>
 And when they went down from the mountain, Jesus charged them, and said unto them, Tell not what ye have seen to any man, until the Son of man rise from [18] among the dead.   <a name="fr_1699"></a><a href="#fn_1699"><font size="1">1700</font></a>
 And they kept the word within themselves, and told no man in <a name="page_81"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 81</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxiv-Page_81"></a>[19] those days what they had seen.   <a name="fr_1700"></a><a href="#fn_1700"><font size="1">1701</font></a>
 And they reflected among themselves, What is this [20] word which he spake unto us, I, when I am risen from among the dead?   <a name="fr_1701"></a><a href="#fn_1701"><font size="1">1702</font></a>
 And his disciples asked him, and said, What is that which the scribes say, then, that Elijah [21] must first come?   <a name="fr_1702"></a><a href="#fn_1702"><font size="1">1703</font></a>
 He said unto them, Elijah cometh first to set in order everything, [Arabic, p. 94] and as it was written of the Son of man, that he should suffer many things, [22] and be rejected.   <a name="fr_1703"></a><a href="#fn_1703"><font size="1">1704</font></a>
 But I say unto you, that Elijah is come, and they knew him not, and have done unto him whatsoever they desired, as it was written of him.  [23, 24]  <a name="fr_1704"></a><a href="#fn_1704"><font size="1">1705</font></a>
 In like manner the Son of man is to suffer of them.   <a name="fr_1705"></a><a href="#fn_1705"><font size="1">1706</font></a>
 Then understood the disciples that he spake unto them concerning John the Baptist.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p33"></a>[25]  <a name="fr_1706"></a><a href="#fn_1706"><font size="1">1707</font></a>
 And on that day whereon they came down from the mountain, there met him a multitude of many people standing with his disciples, and the scribes were discussing [26] with them.   <a name="fr_1707"></a><a href="#fn_1707"><font size="1">1708</font></a>
 And the people, when they saw Jesus, were perplexed, <a name="fr_1708"></a><a href="#fn_1708"><font size="1">1709</font></a>
  and in the [27] midst of their joy hastened <a name="fr_1709"></a><a href="#fn_1709"><font size="1">1710</font></a>
  and saluted him.   <a name="fr_1710"></a><a href="#fn_1710"><font size="1">1711</font></a>
 And on that day came certain of the Pharisees, and said unto him, Get thee out, and go hence; for Herod seeketh [28] to kill thee.   <a name="fr_1711"></a><a href="#fn_1711"><font size="1">1712</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, Go ye and say to this fox, Behold, I am casting out demons, and I heal to-day and to-morrow, and on the third day I am perfected.  [29]  <a name="fr_1712"></a><a href="#fn_1712"><font size="1">1713</font></a>
 Nevertheless I must be watchful <a name="fr_1713"></a><a href="#fn_1713"><font size="1">1714</font></a>
  to-day and to-morrow, and on the last day I shall depart; for it cannot be that a prophet perish outside of Jerusalem.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p42"></a>[30]  <a name="fr_1714"></a><a href="#fn_1714"><font size="1">1715</font></a>
 And after that, there came to him a man from that multitude, and fell upon his knees, and said unto him, I beseech thee, my Lord, look upon my son;  <a name="fr_1715"></a><a href="#fn_1715"><font size="1">1716</font></a>
 he is my [31] only <i>child</i>:  and the spirit cometh upon him suddenly.   <a name="fr_1716"></a><a href="#fn_1716"><font size="1">1717</font></a>
 A lunacy <a name="fr_1717"></a><a href="#fn_1717"><font size="1">1718</font></a>
  hath come upon [32] him, and he meeteth with evils.   <a name="fr_1718"></a><a href="#fn_1718"><font size="1">1719</font></a>
 And when it cometh upon him, it beateth him about; <a name="fr_1719"></a><a href="#fn_1719"><font size="1">1720</font></a>
  [33] and he foameth, and gnasheth his teeth, and wasteth; <a name="fr_1720"></a><a href="#fn_1720"><font size="1">1721</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1721"></a><a href="#fn_1721"><font size="1">1722</font></a>
 and many times it hath thrown him into the water and into the fire to destroy him, and it hardly leaveth him after [34] [Arabic, p. 95] bruising him.   <a name="fr_1722"></a><a href="#fn_1722"><font size="1">1723</font></a>
 And I brought him near to thy disciples, and they could [35] not heal him.   <a name="fr_1723"></a><a href="#fn_1723"><font size="1">1724</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said, O faithless and perverse generation, till when shall I be with you? and till when shall I bear with you? bring thy son [36] hither.   <a name="fr_1724"></a><a href="#fn_1724"><font size="1">1725</font></a>
 And he brought him unto him:  and when the spirit saw him, immediately [37] it beat him about; and he fell upon the ground, and was raging and foaming.   <a name="fr_1725"></a><a href="#fn_1725"><font size="1">1726</font></a>
 And Jesus asked his father, How long is the time during which he hath been thus?  He [38] said unto him, From his youth until now.   <a name="fr_1726"></a><a href="#fn_1726"><font size="1">1727</font></a>
 But, my Lord, help me wherein thou [39] canst, and have mercy upon me.   <a name="fr_1727"></a><a href="#fn_1727"><font size="1">1728</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, If thou canst believe!  All [40] things are possible to him that believeth.   <a name="fr_1728"></a><a href="#fn_1728"><font size="1">1729</font></a>
 And immediately the father of the child [41] cried out, weeping, and said, I believe, my Lord; help my lack of faith.   <a name="fr_1729"></a><a href="#fn_1729"><font size="1">1730</font></a>
 And when Jesus saw the hastening of the people, and their coming at the sound, he rebuked that unclean spirit, and said to it, Thou dumb <a name="fr_1730"></a><a href="#fn_1730"><font size="1">1731</font></a>
  spirit that speakest not, I command [42] thee, <a name="fr_1731"></a><a href="#fn_1731"><font size="1">1732</font></a>
  come out of him, and enter not again into him.   <a name="fr_1732"></a><a href="#fn_1732"><font size="1">1733</font></a>
 And that spirit, devil, <a name="fr_1733"></a><a href="#fn_1733"><font size="1">1734</font></a>
  cried out much, and bruised him, and came out; and that child fell as one dead, and [43] many thought that he had died.   <a name="fr_1734"></a><a href="#fn_1734"><font size="1">1735</font></a>
 But Jesus took him by his hand, and raised him [44] up, and gave him to his father; and that child was healed from that hour.   <a name="fr_1735"></a><a href="#fn_1735"><font size="1">1736</font></a>
 And the people all marvelled at the greatness of God.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p65"></a>[45]  <a name="fr_1736"></a><a href="#fn_1736"><font size="1">1737</font></a>
 And when Jesus entered into the house, his disciples came, and asked him [46] privately, <a name="fr_1737"></a><a href="#fn_1737"><font size="1">1738</font></a>
  and said unto him, Why were we not able to heal him?   <a name="fr_1738"></a><a href="#fn_1738"><font size="1">1739</font></a>
 Jesus said unto [Arabic, p. 96] them, Because of your unbelief.  Verily I say unto you, If ye have faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye shall say to this mountain, Remove hence; [47] and it shall remove; and nothing shall overcome you.   <a name="fr_1739"></a><a href="#fn_1739"><font size="1">1740</font></a>
 But it is impossible to cast out this kind by anything except by fasting and prayer.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xxiv-p70"></a>[48]  <a name="fr_1740"></a><a href="#fn_1740"><font size="1">1741</font></a>
 And when he went forth thence, they passed through Galilee:  and he would not [49] that any man should know it. <a name="fr_1741"></a><a href="#fn_1741"><font size="1">1742</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1742"></a><a href="#fn_1742"><font size="1">1743</font></a>
 And he taught his disciples, and said unto them, [50]  <a name="fr_1743"></a><a href="#fn_1743"><font size="1">1744</font></a>
 Keep ye these sayings in your ears and your hearts:  for the Son of man is to be <a name="page_82"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 82</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xxiv-Page_82"></a>delivered into the hands of men, and they shall kill him; and when he is killed, he [51] shall rise on the third day.   <a name="fr_1744"></a><a href="#fn_1744"><font size="1">1745</font></a>
 But they knew not the word which he spake unto them, for it was concealed from them, that they should not perceive it; and they feared to [52] ask him about this word.   <a name="fr_1745"></a><a href="#fn_1745"><font size="1">1746</font></a>
 And they were exceeding sorrowful.</p>
