 <p> <a name="iv.iii.x"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.x-p1"></a> <a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.x-Page_59"></a><span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.x-p1.1"></a>Section X.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.x-p2"></a>[1] [Arabic, p. 38]  <a name="fr_752"></a><a href="#fn_752"><font size="1">753</font></a>
 No man can serve two masters; and that because it is necessary that he hate one of them and love the other, and honour one of them and despise the [2] other.   <a name="fr_753"></a><a href="#fn_753"><font size="1">754</font></a>
 Ye cannot serve God and possessions.  And because of this I say unto you, Be not anxious for yourselves, <a name="fr_754"></a><a href="#fn_754"><font size="1">755</font></a>
  what ye shall eat and what ye shall drink; neither for your bodies, what ye shall put on.  Is not the life better than the food, and the body [3] than the raiment?   <a name="fr_755"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">756</font></a>
 Consider the birds of the heaven, which sow not, nor reap, nor store in barns; and <i>yet</i> your Father which is in heaven feedeth them.  Are not ye [4] better than they?   <a name="fr_756"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">757</font></a>
 Who of you when he trieth is able to add to his stature one [5] cubit?   <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">758</font></a>
 If then ye are not able for a small <i>thing</i>, why are ye anxious about the [6, 7] rest?   <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">759</font></a>
 Consider the wild lily, how it grows, although it toils not, nor spins;  <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">760</font></a>
 and I say unto you that Solomon in the greatness of his glory was not clothed like one of [8] them.   <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">761</font></a>
 And if God so clothe the grass of the field, which to-day is, and to-morrow [9] is cast <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_761"><font size="1">762</font></a>
  into the oven, how much more shall be unto you, O ye of little faith!   <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_762"><font size="1">763</font></a>
 Be not anxious, so as to say, What shall we eat? or, What shall we drink? or, With [10] what shall we be clothed?   <a name="fr_763"></a><a href="#fn_763"><font size="1">764</font></a>
 Neither let your minds be perplexed in this:   <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_764"><font size="1">765</font></a>
 all these <i>things</i> the nations of the world seek; and your Father which is in heaven knoweth [11] your need of all these things.   <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_765"><font size="1">766</font></a>
 Seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; [12] [Arabic, p. 39] and all these shall come to you as something additional for you.   <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_766"><font size="1">767</font></a>
 Be not anxious for the morrow; for the morrow shall be anxious for what belongs to it.  Sufficient unto the day is its evil.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.x-p18"></a>[13]  <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_767"><font size="1">768</font></a>
 Judge not, that ye be not judged:   <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_768"><font size="1">769</font></a>
 condemn <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_769"><font size="1">770</font></a>
  not, that ye be not condemned: [14]  <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_770"><font size="1">771</font></a>
 forgive, <i>and</i> it shall be forgiven you:  release, and ye shall be released:  give, that ye may be given <i>unto</i>; with good measure, abundant, full, they shall thrust <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_771"><font size="1">772</font></a>
  into your [15] bosoms.   <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_772"><font size="1">773</font></a>
 With what measure ye measure it shall be measured to you.  See <i>to it</i> what ye hear:  with what measure ye measure it shall be measured to you; and ye [16] shall be given more.   <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">774</font></a>
 I say unto those that hear, He that hath shall be given <i>unto</i>; and he that hath not, that which he regards <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_774"><font size="1">775</font></a>
  as his shall be taken from him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.x-p27"></a>[17]  <a name="fr_775"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">776</font></a>
 And he spake unto them a parable, Can a blind man haply guide a blind man? [18] shall <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">777</font></a>
  they not both fall into a hollow?   <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">778</font></a>
 A disciple is not better than his master; [19] every perfect man shall be as his master.   <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_778"><font size="1">779</font></a>
 Why lookest thou at the mote which is in the eye of thy brother, but considerest not the column that is in thine <i>own</i> eye?  [20]  <a name="fr_779"></a><a href="#fn_779"><font size="1">780</font></a>
 Or how canst thou say to thy brother, Brother, I will take out the mote from thine eye; and the column which is in thine eye thou seest not?  Thou hypocrite, take out first the column from thine eye; and then shalt thou see to take out the mote from the eye of thy brother.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.x-p33"></a>[21]  <a name="fr_780"></a><a href="#fn_780"><font size="1">781</font></a>
 Give not that which is holy unto the dogs, neither cast your pearls before the swine, lest they trample them with their feet, and return and wound you.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.x-p35"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_781"></a><a href="#fn_781"><font size="1">782</font></a>
 And he said unto them, Who of you, that hath a friend, goeth to him at midnight, [23] and saith unto him, My friend, lend me three loaves;  <a name="fr_782"></a><a href="#fn_782"><font size="1">783</font></a>
 for a friend hath come [24] to me from a journey, and I have nothing to offer to him:   <a name="fr_783"></a><a href="#fn_783"><font size="1">784</font></a>
 and that friend shall [Arabic, p. 40] answer him from within, and say unto him, Trouble me not; for the door is shut, and my children are with me in bed, and I cannot rise and give thee?  [25]  <a name="fr_784"></a><a href="#fn_784"><font size="1">785</font></a>
 And verily I say unto you, If he will not give him because of friendship, yet because [26] of <i>his</i> importunity he will rise and give him what he seeketh.   <a name="fr_785"></a><a href="#fn_785"><font size="1">786</font></a>
 And I also say unto you, Ask, <i>and</i> ye shall be given <i>unto</i>; seek, <i>and</i> ye shall find; knock, <i>and</i> it shall be [27] opened unto you.   <a name="fr_786"></a><a href="#fn_786"><font size="1">787</font></a>
 Every one that asketh receiveth, and he that seeketh findeth, and [28] he that knocketh, it shall be opened to him.   <a name="fr_787"></a><a href="#fn_787"><font size="1">788</font></a>
 What father of you, shall his son ask for bread—will he, think you, give him a stone?  <a name="fr_788"></a><a href="#fn_788"><font size="1">789</font></a>
 and if he ask of him a fish, will he, [29] think you,  <a name="fr_789"></a><a href="#fn_789"><font size="1">790</font></a>
 instead of the fish give him a serpent? and if he ask him for an egg, will <a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.x-Page_60"></a>[30] he, think you, extend to him a scorpion?   <a name="fr_790"></a><a href="#fn_790"><font size="1">791</font></a>
 If ye then, <i>although</i> being evil, know the gifts <i>which are</i> good, and give them to your children, how much more shall your [31] Father which is in heaven give the Holy Spirit to them that ask him?   <a name="fr_791"></a><a href="#fn_791"><font size="1">792</font></a>
 Whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them:  this is the law and the prophets.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.x-p47"></a>[32]  <a name="fr_792"></a><a href="#fn_792"><font size="1">793</font></a>
 Enter <a name="fr_793"></a><a href="#fn_793"><font size="1">794</font></a>
  ye by the narrow gate; for the wide gate and the broad way lead to destruction, [33] and many they be which go therein.   <a name="fr_794"></a><a href="#fn_794"><font size="1">795</font></a>
 How narrow is the gate and straitened the way leading to life! and few be they that find it.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.x-p51"></a>[34]  <a name="fr_795"></a><a href="#fn_795"><font size="1">796</font></a>
 Beware of false prophets, which come to you in sheeps <a name="fr_796"></a><a href="#fn_796"><font size="1">797</font></a>
  clothing, while within [35] they are ravening wolves.   <a name="fr_797"></a><a href="#fn_797"><font size="1">798</font></a>
 But by their fruits ye shall know them.   <a name="fr_798"></a><a href="#fn_798"><font size="1">799</font></a>
 For every tree is known by its fruit.  For figs are not gathered <a name="fr_799"></a><a href="#fn_799"><font size="1">800</font></a>
  of thorns, neither are grapes plucked of [36] briers.   <a name="fr_800"></a><a href="#fn_800"><font size="1">801</font></a>
 Even so every good tree bringeth forth good fruit, but the evil tree bringeth [37] [Arabic, p. 41] forth evil fruit.   <a name="fr_801"></a><a href="#fn_801"><font size="1">802</font></a>
 The good tree cannot bring forth evil fruit, neither <i>can</i> the [38] evil tree bring forth good fruit.   <a name="fr_802"></a><a href="#fn_802"><font size="1">803</font></a>
 The good man from the good treasures that are in his heart bringeth forth good <i>things</i>; and the evil man from the evil treasures that are in his heart bringeth forth evil <i>things</i>:  and from the overflowings of the [39] heart the lips speak.   <a name="fr_803"></a><a href="#fn_803"><font size="1">804</font></a>
 Every tree that beareth not good fruit is cut <i>down</i> and cast [40, 41] into the fire.   <a name="fr_804"></a><a href="#fn_804"><font size="1">805</font></a>
 Therefore by their fruits ye shall know them.   <a name="fr_805"></a><a href="#fn_805"><font size="1">806</font></a>
 Not all that say unto me, My Lord, my Lord, shall enter the kingdom of the heavens; but he that doeth [42] the will of my Father which is in heaven.   <a name="fr_806"></a><a href="#fn_806"><font size="1">807</font></a>
 Many shall say unto me in that day, My Lord, my Lord, did we not prophesy in thy name, and in thy name cast out [43] devils, and in thy name do many powers?   <a name="fr_807"></a><a href="#fn_807"><font size="1">808</font></a>
 Then shall I say unto them, I never [44] knew you:  depart from me, ye servants of iniquity.   <a name="fr_808"></a><a href="#fn_808"><font size="1">809</font></a>
 Every man that cometh unto [45] me, and heareth my sayings, and doeth them, I will shew you to what he is like:   <a name="fr_809"></a><a href="#fn_809"><font size="1">810</font></a>
 he is like the wise man which built a house, and digged and went deep, and laid the [46] foundations on a rock:   <a name="fr_810"></a><a href="#fn_810"><font size="1">811</font></a>
 and the rain came down, and the rivers overflowed, and the winds blew, and shook that house, and it fell not:  for its foundation was laid on [47] rocks.   <a name="fr_811"></a><a href="#fn_811"><font size="1">812</font></a>
 And every one that heareth these my words, and doeth them not, is like [48] the foolish man which built his house on sand, without foundation:   <a name="fr_812"></a><a href="#fn_812"><font size="1">813</font></a>
 and the rain descended, and the rivers overflowed, and the winds blew, and smote upon that house, and it fell:  and the fall of it was great.</p>
