 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xi"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p1.1"></a>Section XI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p2"></a>[1] [Arabic, p. 42]  <a name="fr_813"></a><a href="#fn_813"><font size="1">814</font></a>
 And when Jesus finished these sayings, the multitudes were astonished [2] at his teaching;  <a name="fr_814"></a><a href="#fn_814"><font size="1">815</font></a>
 and that because he was teaching them as one having authority, not as their scribes and the Pharisees.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p5"></a>[3]  <a name="fr_815"></a><a href="#fn_815"><font size="1">816</font></a>
 And when he descended from the mountain, great multitudes followed him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p7"></a>[4]  <a name="fr_816"></a><a href="#fn_816"><font size="1">817</font></a>
 And when Jesus entered Capernaum, the servant of one of the chiefs was in an [5] evil case, and he was precious to him, and he was at the point of death.   <a name="fr_817"></a><a href="#fn_817"><font size="1">818</font></a>
 And he [6] heard of Jesus, and came to him with the elders of the Jews;  <a name="fr_818"></a><a href="#fn_818"><font size="1">819</font></a>
 and he besought him, and said, My Lord, my boy is laid in the house paralysed, <a name="fr_819"></a><a href="#fn_819"><font size="1">820</font></a>
  and he is suffering grievous [7] torment.   <a name="fr_820"></a><a href="#fn_820"><font size="1">821</font></a>
 And the elders urgently requested of him, and said, He is worthy that [8] this should be done unto him:   <a name="fr_821"></a><a href="#fn_821"><font size="1">822</font></a>
 for he loveth our people, and he also built the synagogue [9, 10] for us.   <a name="fr_822"></a><a href="#fn_822"><font size="1">823</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, I will come and heal him.   <a name="fr_823"></a><a href="#fn_823"><font size="1">824</font></a>
 That chief answered and said, My Lord, I am not worthy that my roof should shade thee; but it sufficeth [11] that thou speak a word, and my lad shall be healed.   <a name="fr_824"></a><a href="#fn_824"><font size="1">825</font></a>
 And I also am a man in obedience to authority, having under my hand soldiers: <a name="fr_825"></a><a href="#fn_825"><font size="1">826</font></a>
   and I say to this <i>one</i>, Go, and he goeth; and to another, Come, and he cometh; and to my servant that he do this, [12] and he doeth <i>it</i>.   <a name="fr_826"></a><a href="#fn_826"><font size="1">827</font></a>
 And when Jesus heard that, he marvelled at him, <a name="fr_827"></a><a href="#fn_827"><font size="1">828</font></a>
  and turned and said unto the multitude that were coming with him,  <a name="fr_828"></a><a href="#fn_828"><font size="1">829</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, I have [13] not found in Israel <i>the</i> like <i>of</i> this faith.   <a name="fr_829"></a><a href="#fn_829"><font size="1">830</font></a>
 I say unto you, that many shall come from the east and the west, and shall recline with Abraham and Isaac and Jacob [14] [Arabic, p. 43] in the kingdom of heaven:   <a name="fr_830"></a><a href="#fn_830"><font size="1">831</font></a>
 but the children of the kingdom shall be cast <a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 61</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xi-Page_61"></a>[15] forth into the outer darkness:  and there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth.   <a name="fr_831"></a><a href="#fn_831"><font size="1">832</font></a>
 And Jesus said to that chief, Go thy way; as thou hast believed, <i>so</i> shall it be unto thee.  [16]  <a name="fr_832"></a><a href="#fn_832"><font size="1">833</font></a>
 And his lad was healed in that hour.  And that chief returned to the house and found that sick servant healed.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p25"></a>[17]  <a name="fr_833"></a><a href="#fn_833"><font size="1">834</font></a>
 And the day after, he was going to a city called Nain, and his disciples with him, [18] and a great multitude.   <a name="fr_834"></a><a href="#fn_834"><font size="1">835</font></a>
 And when he was come near the gate of the city, he saw a crowd <a name="fr_835"></a><a href="#fn_835"><font size="1">836</font></a>
  accompanying one <i>that was</i> dead, the only son of his mother; and his mother was a widow:  and there was with her a great multitude of the people of the [19] city.   <a name="fr_836"></a><a href="#fn_836"><font size="1">837</font></a>
 And when Jesus saw her, he had compassion on her, and said unto her, Weep [20] not.   <a name="fr_837"></a><a href="#fn_837"><font size="1">838</font></a>
 And he went and advanced to the bier, and the bearers of it stood still; and [21] he said, Young man, I say unto thee, Arise.   <a name="fr_838"></a><a href="#fn_838"><font size="1">839</font></a>
 And that dead <i>man</i> sat up and began [22] to speak; and he gave him to his mother.   <a name="fr_839"></a><a href="#fn_839"><font size="1">840</font></a>
 And fear came on all the people:  and they praised God, and said, There hath risen among us a great prophet:  and, God [23] hath had regard to his people.   <a name="fr_840"></a><a href="#fn_840"><font size="1">841</font></a>
 And this news concerning him spread in all Judæa, and in all the region which was about them.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p34"></a>[24]  <a name="fr_841"></a><a href="#fn_841"><font size="1">842</font></a>
 And when Jesus saw great multitudes surrounding him, he commanded them to [25] depart to the other side.   <a name="fr_842"></a><a href="#fn_842"><font size="1">843</font></a>
 And while they were going in the way, there came one of the scribes and said unto him, My Master, I will follow thee whithersoever thou [26] goest.   <a name="fr_843"></a><a href="#fn_843"><font size="1">844</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, The foxes have holes, and the birds of the heaven have [27] nests; but the Son of man hath not a place in which to lay his head.   <a name="fr_844"></a><a href="#fn_844"><font size="1">845</font></a>
 And he said unto another, Follow me.  And he said unto him, My Lord, suffer me first to go and [28] bury my father.   <a name="fr_845"></a><a href="#fn_845"><font size="1">846</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, Leave the dead to bury their dead; but thou, [29] follow me and preach the kingdom of God.   <a name="fr_846"></a><a href="#fn_846"><font size="1">847</font></a>
 And another said unto him, I will follow [Arabic, p. 44] thee, my Lord; but first suffer me to go and salute my household and [30] come.   <a name="fr_847"></a><a href="#fn_847"><font size="1">848</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, There is no one who putteth his hand to the plough <a name="fr_848"></a><a href="#fn_848"><font size="1">849</font></a>
  and looketh behind him, and <i>yet</i> is fit for the kingdom of God.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p43"></a>[31]  <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">850</font></a>
 And he said to them on that day in the evening, Let us go over to the other side [32] of the lake; and he left <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">851</font></a>
  the multitudes.   <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">852</font></a>
 And Jesus went up and sat in the ship, [33] he and his disciples, and there were with them other ships.   <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">853</font></a>
 And there occurred on the sea a great tempest <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">854</font></a>
  of whirlwind and wind,  <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">855</font></a>
  and the ship was on the point of [34] sinking from the greatness <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">856</font></a>
  of the waves.   <a name="fr_856"></a><a href="#fn_856"><font size="1">857</font></a>
 But Jesus was sleeping on a cushion in the stern of the ship;  <a name="fr_857"></a><a href="#fn_857"><font size="1">858</font></a>
 and his disciples came and awoke him, and said unto him, Our [35] Lord, save us; lo, we perish.   <a name="fr_858"></a><a href="#fn_858"><font size="1">859</font></a>
 And he rose, and rebuked the winds and the turbulence of the water, and said to the sea, Be still, for thou art rebuked;  <a name="fr_859"></a><a href="#fn_859"><font size="1">860</font></a>
 and the wind [36] was still, and there was a great calm.   <a name="fr_860"></a><a href="#fn_860"><font size="1">861</font></a>
 And he said unto them, Why are ye thus [37] afraid? and why have ye no faith?   <a name="fr_861"></a><a href="#fn_861"><font size="1">862</font></a>
 And they feared greatly. <a name="fr_862"></a><a href="#fn_862"><font size="1">863</font></a>
   And they marvelled, and said one to another, Who, think you, is this, who commandeth also the wind and the waves and the sea, and they obey him?</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xi-p58"></a>[38]  <a name="fr_863"></a><a href="#fn_863"><font size="1">864</font></a>
 And they departed and came to the country of the Gadarenes, which is on the [39] other side, opposite the land of Galilee.   <a name="fr_864"></a><a href="#fn_864"><font size="1">865</font></a>
 And when he went out of the ship to the land,  <a name="fr_865"></a><a href="#fn_865"><font size="1">866</font></a>
 there met him from among the tombs a man who had a devil for a long time, [40] and wore no clothes, neither dwelt in a house, but among the tombs.   <a name="fr_866"></a><a href="#fn_866"><font size="1">867</font></a>
 And no man was [Arabic, p. 45] able to bind him with chains,  <a name="fr_867"></a><a href="#fn_867"><font size="1">868</font></a>
 because any time that he was bound with chains [41] and fetters he cut the chains and loosened the fetters;  <a name="fr_868"></a><a href="#fn_868"><font size="1">869</font></a>
 and he was snatched <a name="fr_869"></a><a href="#fn_869"><font size="1">870</font></a>
  [42] away of the devil into the desert,  <a name="fr_870"></a><a href="#fn_870"><font size="1">871</font></a>
 and no man was able to quiet him; and at all times, in the night and in the day, he would be among the tombs and in the mountains;  <a name="fr_871"></a><a href="#fn_871"><font size="1">872</font></a>
 and no man was able to pass by that way;  <a name="fr_872"></a><a href="#fn_872"><font size="1">873</font></a>
 and he would cry out and wound himself [43] with stones.   <a name="fr_873"></a><a href="#fn_873"><font size="1">874</font></a>
 And when he saw Jesus at a distance, he hastened and worshipped [44] him, and cried with a loud voice and said,  <a name="fr_874"></a><a href="#fn_874"><font size="1">875</font></a>
 What have we to do with thee, Jesus, [45] Son of the most high God?   <a name="fr_875"></a><a href="#fn_875"><font size="1">876</font></a>
 I adjure thee by God, torment me not.  And Jesus commanded the unclean spirit to come out of the man:  and he had <i>suffered</i> <a name="fr_876"></a><a href="#fn_876"><font size="1">877</font></a>
  a long [46] time since the time when he came into captivity to it.   <a name="fr_877"></a><a href="#fn_877"><font size="1">878</font></a>
 And Jesus asked him, What is thy name?  He said unto him, Legion; for there had entered into him many [47] devils.   <a name="fr_878"></a><a href="#fn_878"><font size="1">879</font></a>
 And they besought him that he would not command them to depart into [48] the depths.   <a name="fr_879"></a><a href="#fn_879"><font size="1">880</font></a>
 And there was there a herd of many swine, feeding in the mountain, <a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 62</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xi-Page_62"></a>and those devils besought him to give them leave to enter the swine; and he gave [49] them leave.   <a name="fr_880"></a><a href="#fn_880"><font size="1">881</font></a>
 And the devils went out of the man and entered into the swine.   <a name="fr_881"></a><a href="#fn_881"><font size="1">882</font></a>
 And that herd hastened to the summit and fell down into the midst of the sea, about two [50] thousand, and they were choked in the water.   <a name="fr_882"></a><a href="#fn_882"><font size="1">883</font></a>
 And when the keepers saw what [51] happened, they fled, and told those in the cities and villages.   <a name="fr_883"></a><a href="#fn_883"><font size="1">884</font></a>
 And the people went out to see what had happened; and they came to Jesus, and found the man whose [Arabic, p. 46] devils had gone out, clothed, modest, <a name="fr_884"></a><a href="#fn_884"><font size="1">885</font></a>
  seated at the feet of Jesus; and they [52] feared.   <a name="fr_885"></a><a href="#fn_885"><font size="1">886</font></a>
 And they reported what they saw, and how the man was healed who had a devil,  <a name="fr_886"></a><a href="#fn_886"><font size="1">887</font></a>
 and concerning those swine also.</p>
