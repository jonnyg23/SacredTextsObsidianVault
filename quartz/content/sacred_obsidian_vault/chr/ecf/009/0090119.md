 </p><p class="c35"><a name="xii.iv.xxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xii.iv.xxiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIV.—God Continually Shows Us in Nature that There Will Be a Resurrection.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="xii.iv.xxiv-p2"></a>Let us consider, beloved, how the Lord continually proves to us that there shall be a future resurrection, of which He has rendered the Lord Jesus Christ <a name="fr_4113"></a><a href="#fn_4113"><font size="1">4114</font></a>
  the first-fruits <a name="fr_4114"></a><a href="#fn_4114"><font size="1">4115</font></a>
  by raising Him from the dead.  Let us contemplate, beloved, the resurrection which is at all times <a name="fr_4115"></a><a href="#fn_4115"><font size="1">4116</font></a>
  taking place.  Day and night declare to us a resurrection.  The night sinks to sleep, and the day arises; the day [again] departs, and the night comes on.  Let us behold <a name="fr_4116"></a><a href="#fn_4116"><font size="1">4117</font></a>
  the fruits [of the earth], how the sowing of grain takes place.  The sower <a name="fr_4117"></a><a href="#fn_4117"><font size="1">4118</font></a>
  goes forth, and casts it into the ground, <a name="fr_4118"></a><a href="#fn_4118"><font size="1">4119</font></a>
  and the seed being thus scattered, though dry and naked when it fell upon the earth, is gradually dissolved.  Then out of its dissolution the mighty power of the providence of the Lord raises it up again, and from one seed many arise and bring forth fruit.</p>
