 <p> <a name="iv.iii.ix"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p1.1"></a>Section IX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_685"></a><a href="#fn_685"><font size="1">686</font></a>
 Ye have heard also that it was said unto the ancients, Lie not, but perform unto [2] God in thy oaths:   <a name="fr_686"></a><a href="#fn_686"><font size="1">687</font></a>
 but I say unto you, Swear not at all; neither by heaven, for it [3] is Gods throne;  <a name="fr_687"></a><a href="#fn_687"><font size="1">688</font></a>
 nor by the earth, for it is a footstool under his feet; nor yet by [4] Jerusalem, for it is the city of the great <a name="fr_688"></a><a href="#fn_688"><font size="1">689</font></a>
  King.   <a name="fr_689"></a><a href="#fn_689"><font size="1">690</font></a>
 Neither shalt thou swear by thy [5] head, for thou canst not make in it one lock of hair black or white.   <a name="fr_690"></a><a href="#fn_690"><font size="1">691</font></a>
 But your word shall be either Yea or Nay, and what is in excess of this is of the evil one.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p9"></a>[6, 7]  <a name="fr_691"></a><a href="#fn_691"><font size="1">692</font></a>
 Ye have heard that it was said, Eye for eye, and tooth for tooth:   <a name="fr_692"></a><a href="#fn_692"><font size="1">693</font></a>
 but I say unto you, Stand not in opposition to the evil; <a name="fr_693"></a><a href="#fn_693"><font size="1">694</font></a>
  but whosoever smiteth thee on thy right [8] cheek, <a name="fr_694"></a><a href="#fn_694"><font size="1">695</font></a>
  turn to him also the other.   <a name="fr_695"></a><a href="#fn_695"><font size="1">696</font></a>
 And he that would sue thee, and take thy tunic, [9] leave to him also thy wrapper.   <a name="fr_696"></a><a href="#fn_696"><font size="1">697</font></a>
 And whosoever compelleth thee one mile, go with [10] [Arabic, p. 35] him twain.   <a name="fr_697"></a><a href="#fn_697"><font size="1">698</font></a>
 And he that asketh thee, give unto him:   <a name="fr_698"></a><a href="#fn_698"><font size="1">699</font></a>
 and he that would borrow of thee, prevent him not.  And prosecute <a name="fr_699"></a><a href="#fn_699"><font size="1">700</font></a>
  not him that taketh thy [11] substance.   <a name="fr_700"></a><a href="#fn_700"><font size="1">701</font></a>
 And as ye desire that men should do to you, so do ye also to them.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p20"></a>[12, 13]  <a name="fr_701"></a><a href="#fn_701"><font size="1">702</font></a>
 Ye have heard that it was said, Love thy neighbour and hate thine enemy:   <a name="fr_702"></a><a href="#fn_702"><font size="1">703</font></a>
 but I say unto you, Love your enemies, and pray for those that curse you, and deal well <a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.ix-Page_58"></a>with those that hate you, and pray for those who take you with violence and persecute you; [14]  <a name="fr_703"></a><a href="#fn_703"><font size="1">704</font></a>
 that ye may be sons of your heavenly Father, who maketh his sun to rise on the good and the evil, and sendeth down his rain on the righteous and the [15] unrighteous.   <a name="fr_704"></a><a href="#fn_704"><font size="1">705</font></a>
 If ye love them that love you, what reward shall ye have?  <a name="fr_705"></a><a href="#fn_705"><font size="1">706</font></a>
 for the publicans [16] and sinners also love those that love them.   <a name="fr_706"></a><a href="#fn_706"><font size="1">707</font></a>
 And if ye do a kindness to those [17] who treat you well, where is your superiority? for sinners also do likewise.   <a name="fr_707"></a><a href="#fn_707"><font size="1">708</font></a>
 And if ye lend to him of whom ye hope for a reward, <a name="fr_708"></a><a href="#fn_708"><font size="1">709</font></a>
  where is your superiority? for the [18] sinners also lend to sinners, seeking recompense from <a name="fr_709"></a><a href="#fn_709"><font size="1">710</font></a>
  them.   <a name="fr_710"></a><a href="#fn_710"><font size="1">711</font></a>
 But love your enemies, and do good to them, and lend, and cut not off the hope of any man; that your reward may be great, and ye may be the children of the Highest:  for he is lenient [19] towards the wicked and the ungrateful.   <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">712</font></a>
 Be ye merciful, even as your Father also is [20] merciful.   <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">713</font></a>
 And if ye inquire for the good of your brethren only, what more have [21] ye done <i>than others?</i> is not this the conduct of the publicans also?   <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">714</font></a>
 Be ye now <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">715</font></a>
  perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p35"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_715"></a><a href="#fn_715"><font size="1">716</font></a>
 Consider your alms; do them not before men to let them see you:  and if it be not [23] so, <a name="fr_716"></a><a href="#fn_716"><font size="1">717</font></a>
  ye have no reward before your Father which is in the heavens.   <a name="fr_717"></a><a href="#fn_717"><font size="1">718</font></a>
 When then thou givest an alms now, do not sound a trumpet before thee, as do the people of hypocrisy, [Arabic, p. 36] in the synagogues and the marketplaces, that men may praise them.  And [24] verily say I unto you, They have received their reward.   <a name="fr_718"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">719</font></a>
 But thou, when [25] thou doest alms, let thy left hand not know what thy right hand doeth;  <a name="fr_719"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">720</font></a>
 that thine alms may be concealed:  and thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee openly.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p41"></a>[26]  <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">721</font></a>
 And whenever thou prayest, be not as the hypocrites, who love to stand in the synagogues and in the corners of the marketplaces for prayers, that men may behold [27] them.   <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">722</font></a>
 And verily say I unto you, They have received their reward.  But thou, when thou prayest, enter into thy closet, and fasten thy door, and pray to thy Father in secret, and thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee openly.  [28]  <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">723</font></a>
 And whenever ye pray, be not babblers, as the heathen; for they think that by the [29] abundance of their words they shall be heard.   <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">724</font></a>
 Then be not ye now like unto them:  [30] for your Father knoweth your request before ye ask him.   <a name="fr_724"></a><a href="#fn_724"><font size="1">725</font></a>
 One of his disciples said [31] unto him, Our Lord, teach us to pray, as John taught his disciples.   <a name="fr_725"></a><a href="#fn_725"><font size="1">726</font></a>
 Jesus said unto [32] them, Thus now pray ye now: <a name="fr_726"></a><a href="#fn_726"><font size="1">727</font></a>
    <a name="fr_727"></a><a href="#fn_727"><font size="1">728</font></a>
 Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy [33, 34] name.   <a name="fr_728"></a><a href="#fn_728"><font size="1">729</font></a>
 Thy kingdom come.  Thy will be <i>done</i>, <a name="fr_729"></a><a href="#fn_729"><font size="1">730</font></a>
  as in heaven, so on earth.  <a name="fr_730"></a><a href="#fn_730"><font size="1">731</font></a>
 Give us the [35] food of to-day.   <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">732</font></a>
 And forgive us our trespasses, as we forgave those that trespass<i>ed</i> [36] against us.   <a name="fr_732"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">733</font></a>
 And bring us not into temptations, but deliver us from the evil one. For [37] thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, for ever and ever. <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">734</font></a>
    <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">735</font></a>
 If ye forgive [Arabic, p. 37] men their wrong-doing, <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">736</font></a>
  your Father which is in heaven will forgive you.  [38]  <a name="fr_736"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">737</font></a>
 But if ye forgive not men, neither will your Father pardon your wrong-doing.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p59"></a>[39]  <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">738</font></a>
 When ye fast, do not frown, as the hypocrites; for they make their faces austere, that they may be seen of <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">739</font></a>
  men that they are fasting.  Verily I say unto you, They [40] have received their reward.   <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">740</font></a>
 But when thou fastest, wash thy face and anoint thy [41] head;  <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">741</font></a>
 that thou make not an appearance to men of fasting, but to thy Father which is in secret:  and thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.ix-p64"></a>[42]  <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_741"><font size="1">742</font></a>
 Be not agitated, little flock; for your Father hath delighted to give you the kingdom.  [43]  <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_742"><font size="1">743</font></a>
 Sell your possessions, and give in alms; take to yourselves purses that wax [44] not old.   <a name="fr_743"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">744</font></a>
 Lay not up treasure on earth, where moth and worm corrupt, and where [45] thieves break through and steal:   <a name="fr_744"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">745</font></a>
 but lay up for yourselves treasure in heaven, where [46] moth and worm do not corrupt, nor thieves break through nor steal:   <a name="fr_745"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">746</font></a>
 for where your [47] treasure is, there also will your heart be.   <a name="fr_746"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">747</font></a>
 The lamp of the body is the eye:  if then <a name="fr_747"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">748</font></a>
  [48] thine eye now be sound, thy whole body also shall be light.   <a name="fr_748"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">749</font></a>
 But if thine eye be evil, all thy body shall be dark.  And if the light which is in thee is darkness, how [49] great is <a name="fr_749"></a><a href="#fn_749"><font size="1">750</font></a>
  thy darkness!   <a name="fr_750"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">751</font></a>
 Be watchful that the light which is in thee be not darkness.  [50]  <a name="fr_751"></a><a href="#fn_751"><font size="1">752</font></a>
 Because that, if thy whole body is light, and have no part dark, it shall all be light, as the lamp giveth light to thee with its flame.</p>
