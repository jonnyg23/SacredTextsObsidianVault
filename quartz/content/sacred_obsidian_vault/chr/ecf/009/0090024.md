 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xv"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p1.1"></a>Section XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1071"></a><a href="#fn_1071"><font size="1">1072</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto him, Simon, I have something to say unto thee.  And [2] he said unto him, Say <i>on</i>, my Master.   <a name="fr_1072"></a><a href="#fn_1072"><font size="1">1073</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, There were two debtors [Arabic, p. 58] to one creditor; and one of them owed five hundred pence, and the other [3] owed fifty pence.   <a name="fr_1073"></a><a href="#fn_1073"><font size="1">1074</font></a>
 And because they had not wherewith to pay, he forgave [4] them both.  Which of them ought to love him more?   <a name="fr_1074"></a><a href="#fn_1074"><font size="1">1075</font></a>
 Simon answered and said, I suppose, he to whom he forgave most.  Jesus said unto him, Thou hast judged rightly.  [5]  <a name="fr_1075"></a><a href="#fn_1075"><font size="1">1076</font></a>
 And he turned to that woman, and said to Simon, Dost thou see this woman?  I entered into thy dwelling, and thou gavest me not water to wash my feet:  but this [6] <i>woman</i> hath bathed <a name="fr_1076"></a><a href="#fn_1076"><font size="1">1077</font></a>
  my feet with her tears, and dried them with her hair.   <a name="fr_1077"></a><a href="#fn_1077"><font size="1">1078</font></a>
 And thou kissedst me not:  but this <i>woman</i>, since she <a name="fr_1078"></a><a href="#fn_1078"><font size="1">1079</font></a>
  entered, hath not ceased to kiss my [7] feet.   <a name="fr_1079"></a><a href="#fn_1079"><font size="1">1080</font></a>
 And thou anointedst not my head with oil: <a name="fr_1080"></a><a href="#fn_1080"><font size="1">1081</font></a>
   but this <i>woman</i> hath anointed [8] my feet with sweet ointment. <a name="fr_1081"></a><a href="#fn_1081"><font size="1">1082</font></a>
    <a name="fr_1082"></a><a href="#fn_1082"><font size="1">1083</font></a>
 And for this, I say unto thee, Her many sins are forgiven her, because she loved much; for he to whom little is forgiven loveth little.  [9, 10]  <a name="fr_1083"></a><a href="#fn_1083"><font size="1">1084</font></a>
 And he said unto that woman, Thy sins are forgiven thee.   <a name="fr_1084"></a><a href="#fn_1084"><font size="1">1085</font></a>
 And those that were invited [11] began to say within themselves, Who is this that forgiveth sins also?   <a name="fr_1085"></a><a href="#fn_1085"><font size="1">1086</font></a>
 And Jesus said to that woman, Thy faith hath saved thee; go in peace.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p18"></a><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xv-Page_67"></a>[12]  <a name="fr_1086"></a><a href="#fn_1086"><font size="1">1087</font></a>
 And many believed in him when they saw the signs which he was doing.  [13, 14]  <a name="fr_1087"></a><a href="#fn_1087"><font size="1">1088</font></a>
 But Jesus did not trust <a name="fr_1088"></a><a href="#fn_1088"><font size="1">1089</font></a>
  himself to them,  <a name="fr_1089"></a><a href="#fn_1089"><font size="1">1090</font></a>
 for he knew every man, and he needed not any man to testify to him concerning every man; for he knew what was in man.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p23"></a>[15]  <a name="fr_1090"></a><a href="#fn_1090"><font size="1">1091</font></a>
 And after that, Jesus set apart from his disciples other seventy, and sent them two and two before his face to every region and city whither he was purposing to [16] go.   <a name="fr_1091"></a><a href="#fn_1091"><font size="1">1092</font></a>
 And he said unto them, The harvest is abundant, and the labourers are few:  [17] entreat now the Lord of the harvest, that he send forth labourers into his harvest.   <a name="fr_1092"></a><a href="#fn_1092"><font size="1">1093</font></a>
 Go [18] [Arabic, p. 59] ye:  and lo, I am sending you as lambs among wolves.   <a name="fr_1093"></a><a href="#fn_1093"><font size="1">1094</font></a>
 Take not with you [19] purses, nor a wallet, nor shoes; neither salute any man in the way.   <a name="fr_1094"></a><a href="#fn_1094"><font size="1">1095</font></a>
 And [20] whatsoever house ye enter, first salute that house:   <a name="fr_1095"></a><a href="#fn_1095"><font size="1">1096</font></a>
 and if there be there a son of peace, [21] let your peace rest upon him; but if there be not, your peace shall return to you.   <a name="fr_1096"></a><a href="#fn_1096"><font size="1">1097</font></a>
 And be ye in that house eating and drinking what they have: <a name="fr_1097"></a><a href="#fn_1097"><font size="1">1098</font></a>
   for the labourer is worthy of [22] his hire.   <a name="fr_1098"></a><a href="#fn_1098"><font size="1">1099</font></a>
 And remove not from house to house.  And into whatsoever city ye enter, [23] and they receive you, eat what is presented to you:   <a name="fr_1099"></a><a href="#fn_1099"><font size="1">1100</font></a>
 and heal the sick that are [24] therein, and say unto them, The kingdom of God is come near unto you.   <a name="fr_1100"></a><a href="#fn_1100"><font size="1">1101</font></a>
 But whatsoever city ye enter, and they receive you not, go out into the market, and say, [25]  <a name="fr_1101"></a><a href="#fn_1101"><font size="1">1102</font></a>
 Even the dust that clave to our feet from your city, we shake off against you; but [26] know <a name="fr_1102"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">1103</font></a>
  this, <a name="fr_1103"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">1104</font></a>
  that the kingdom of God is come near unto you.   <a name="fr_1104"></a><a href="#fn_1104"><font size="1">1105</font></a>
 I say unto you, that for Sodom there shall be quiet in the day of judgement, but there shall not be for [27] that city.   <a name="fr_1105"></a><a href="#fn_1105"><font size="1">1106</font></a>
 Then began Jesus to rebuke the cities in which there had been many [28] mighty works, <a name="fr_1106"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">1107</font></a>
  and they repented not.   <a name="fr_1107"></a><a href="#fn_1107"><font size="1">1108</font></a>
 And he said, Woe unto thee, Chorazin! woe unto thee, Bethsaida! if there had been in Tyre and Sidon the signs which were in [29] thee, it may be that they would have repented in sackcloth and ashes.   <a name="fr_1108"></a><a href="#fn_1108"><font size="1">1109</font></a>
 Howbeit I say unto you, that for Tyre and Sidon there shall be rest in the day of judgement, [30] more than for you.   <a name="fr_1109"></a><a href="#fn_1109"><font size="1">1110</font></a>
 And thou, Capernaum, which art exalted unto heaven, shalt sink down unto Hades; for if there had been in Sodom the wonders <a name="fr_1110"></a><a href="#fn_1110"><font size="1">1111</font></a>
  which were [31] in thee, it would have remained until this day.   <a name="fr_1111"></a><a href="#fn_1111"><font size="1">1112</font></a>
 And now I say unto thee, that for the land of Sodom there shall be quiet in the day of judgement, more than for thee.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p46"></a>[32] [Arabic, p. 60]  <a name="fr_1112"></a><a href="#fn_1112"><font size="1">1113</font></a>
 And he said again unto his apostles, Whosoever heareth you heareth me; and whosoever heareth me heareth him that sent me:  and whosoever wrongeth <a name="fr_1113"></a><a href="#fn_1113"><font size="1">1114</font></a>
  you wrongeth me; and whosoever wrongeth me wrongeth him that sent me.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p49"></a>[33]  <a name="fr_1114"></a><a href="#fn_1114"><font size="1">1115</font></a>
 And those seventy returned with great joy, and said unto him, Our Lord, even [34] the devils also are subject unto us in thy name.   <a name="fr_1115"></a><a href="#fn_1115"><font size="1">1116</font></a>
 He said unto them, I beheld [35] Satan <a name="fr_1116"></a><a href="#fn_1116"><font size="1">1117</font></a>
  fallen like lightning from heaven.   <a name="fr_1117"></a><a href="#fn_1117"><font size="1">1118</font></a>
 Behold, I am giving you authority to tread upon serpents and scorpions, and the whole race <a name="fr_1118"></a><a href="#fn_1118"><font size="1">1119</font></a>
  of the enemy; and nothing shall [36] hurt you.   <a name="fr_1119"></a><a href="#fn_1119"><font size="1">1120</font></a>
 Only ye must not rejoice that the devils are subject unto you; but be glad that your names are written in heaven.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p56"></a>[37]  <a name="fr_1120"></a><a href="#fn_1120"><font size="1">1121</font></a>
 And in that hour Jesus rejoiced in the Holy Spirit, and said, I acknowledge thee, my Father, Lord of heaven and earth, that thou didst hide these things from the wise and understanding, and didst reveal them unto children:  yea, my Father; so [38] was thy will.   <a name="fr_1121"></a><a href="#fn_1121"><font size="1">1122</font></a>
 And he turned to his disciples, <a name="fr_1122"></a><a href="#fn_1122"><font size="1">1123</font></a>
  and said unto them, Everything hath been delivered to me of my Father:  and no man knoweth who the Son is, save the Father; and who the Father is, save the Son, and to whomsoever the Son willeth [39] to reveal <i>him</i>.   <a name="fr_1123"></a><a href="#fn_1123"><font size="1">1124</font></a>
 Come unto me, all of you, ye <i>that are</i> wearied and bearers of burdens, [40] and I will give you rest.   <a name="fr_1124"></a><a href="#fn_1124"><font size="1">1125</font></a>
 Bear my yoke upon you, and learn of me; for <a name="fr_1125"></a><a href="#fn_1125"><font size="1">1126</font></a>
  I [41] am gentle and lowly in my heart:  and ye shall find rest unto your souls.   <a name="fr_1126"></a><a href="#fn_1126"><font size="1">1127</font></a>
 For my yoke is pleasant, and my burden is light.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xv-p64"></a>[42]  <a name="fr_1127"></a><a href="#fn_1127"><font size="1">1128</font></a>
 And while great multitudes were going with him, he turned, and said unto them, [43]  <a name="fr_1128"></a><a href="#fn_1128"><font size="1">1129</font></a>
 Whosoever cometh unto me, and hateth not his father, and his mother, and his brethren, and his sisters, and his wife, and his children, and himself <a name="fr_1129"></a><a href="#fn_1129"><font size="1">1130</font></a>
  also, cannot [44] [Arabic, p. 61] be a disciple to me.   <a name="fr_1130"></a><a href="#fn_1130"><font size="1">1131</font></a>
 And whosoever doth not take his cross, and follow [45] me, cannot be a disciple to me.   <a name="fr_1131"></a><a href="#fn_1131"><font size="1">1132</font></a>
 Which of you desireth to build a tower, <a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xv-Page_68"></a>and doth not sit down first and reckon his expenses and whether he hath <i>enough</i> to [46] complete it? <a name="fr_1132"></a><a href="#fn_1132"><font size="1">1133</font></a>
   <a name="fr_1133"></a><a href="#fn_1133"><font size="1">1134</font></a>
 lest when he hath laid the foundations, and is not able to finish, all that [47] behold him <a name="fr_1134"></a><a href="#fn_1134"><font size="1">1135</font></a>
  laugh at him, and say,  <a name="fr_1135"></a><a href="#fn_1135"><font size="1">1136</font></a>
 This man began to build, and was not able to [48] finish.   <a name="fr_1136"></a><a href="#fn_1136"><font size="1">1137</font></a>
 Or what king goeth to the battle to fight with another king, <a name="fr_1137"></a><a href="#fn_1137"><font size="1">1138</font></a>
  and doth not consider first whether he is able with ten thousand to meet him that cometh to him [49] with twenty thousand?   <a name="fr_1138"></a><a href="#fn_1138"><font size="1">1139</font></a>
 And if he is not able, he sendeth unto him while he is afar [50] off, and seeketh peace.   <a name="fr_1139"></a><a href="#fn_1139"><font size="1">1140</font></a>
 So shall <a name="fr_1140"></a><a href="#fn_1140"><font size="1">1141</font></a>
  every man of you consider, that desireth to be a disciple to me; for if he renounceth not all that he hath, he cannot be a disciple to me.</p>
