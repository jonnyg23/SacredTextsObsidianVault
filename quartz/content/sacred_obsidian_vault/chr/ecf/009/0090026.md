 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p1.1"></a>Section XVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_1203"></a><a href="#fn_1203"><font size="1">1204</font></a>
 And he set forth to them another parable, and said, The kingdom of heaven is [2] like a man who sowed good seed in his field;  <a name="fr_1204"></a><a href="#fn_1204"><font size="1">1205</font></a>
 but when men slept, his enemy came [3] and sowed tares among the wheat, and went away.   <a name="fr_1205"></a><a href="#fn_1205"><font size="1">1206</font></a>
 And when the blade sprang up [4] and brought forth fruit, there were noticed the tares also.   <a name="fr_1206"></a><a href="#fn_1206"><font size="1">1207</font></a>
 And the servants of the master of the house came, and said unto him, Our lord, didst thou not sow good <a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 70</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xvii-Page_70"></a>[5] [Arabic, p. 66] seed in thy field? whence are there tares in it?   <a name="fr_1207"></a><a href="#fn_1207"><font size="1">1208</font></a>
 He said unto them, An enemy hath done this.  His servants said unto him, Wilt thou that we go [6] and separate it?   <a name="fr_1208"></a><a href="#fn_1208"><font size="1">1209</font></a>
 He said unto them, Perhaps, <a name="fr_1209"></a><a href="#fn_1209"><font size="1">1210</font></a>
  when ye separate the tares, ye would [7] root up with them wheat also.   <a name="fr_1210"></a><a href="#fn_1210"><font size="1">1211</font></a>
 Leave them to grow both together until the harvest:  and in the time of the harvest I will say unto the reapers, Separate the tares first, and bind them in bundles to be burned with fire; and gather the wheat into my barns.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p11"></a>[8, 9]  <a name="fr_1211"></a><a href="#fn_1211"><font size="1">1212</font></a>
 And he set forth to them another parable, and said,  <a name="fr_1212"></a><a href="#fn_1212"><font size="1">1213</font></a>
 To what is the kingdom of [10] God like? and to what shall I liken it? and in what parable shall I set it forth?   <a name="fr_1213"></a><a href="#fn_1213"><font size="1">1214</font></a>
 It [11] is like a grain of mustard seed, which a man took, and planted in his field:   <a name="fr_1214"></a><a href="#fn_1214"><font size="1">1215</font></a>
 and of the number of the things that are sown in the earth it is smaller than all of the things [12] which are sown, which are upon the earth;  <a name="fr_1215"></a><a href="#fn_1215"><font size="1">1216</font></a>
 but when it is grown, it is greater than all the herbs, and produceth large branches, so that the birds of heaven make <i>their</i> nests in its branches.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p17"></a>[13, 14]  <a name="fr_1216"></a><a href="#fn_1216"><font size="1">1217</font></a>
 And he set forth to them another parable:   <a name="fr_1217"></a><a href="#fn_1217"><font size="1">1218</font></a>
 To what shall I liken the kingdom of [15] God?   <a name="fr_1218"></a><a href="#fn_1218"><font size="1">1219</font></a>
 It is like the leaven which a woman took, and kneaded into three measures of flour, until the whole of it was leavened.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p21"></a>[16]  <a name="fr_1219"></a><a href="#fn_1219"><font size="1">1220</font></a>
 And Jesus spake all that to the multitudes by way of parables, according as they [17] were able to hear.   <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1220"><font size="1">1221</font></a>
 And without parables spake he not unto them; that the saying of the Lord through the prophet might be fulfilled:</p> 
 <p class="c28"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p24"></a>I will open my mouth in parables;</p> 
 <p class="c30"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p25"></a>And I will utter secrets which were before the foundations <a name="fr_1221"></a><a href="#fn_1221"><font size="1">1222</font></a>
  of the world.</p> 
 <p><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p27"></a>[18]  <a name="fr_1222"></a><a href="#fn_1222"><font size="1">1223</font></a>
 But he explained to his disciples privately everything.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p29"></a>[19]  <a name="fr_1223"></a><a href="#fn_1223"><font size="1">1224</font></a>
 Then Jesus left <a name="fr_1224"></a><a href="#fn_1224"><font size="1">1225</font></a>
  the multitudes, and came to the house.  And his disciples came unto him, and said unto him, Explain unto us that parable about the tares [20] [Arabic, p. 67] and the field.   <a name="fr_1225"></a><a href="#fn_1225"><font size="1">1226</font></a>
 He answered and said unto them, He that sowed good seed is [21] the Son of man;  <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1226"><font size="1">1227</font></a>
 and the field is the world; and the good seed are the children of the [22] kingdom;  <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1227"><font size="1">1228</font></a>
 and the tares are the children of the evil one; and the enemy that sowed them <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1228"><font size="1">1229</font></a>
  is Satan; and the harvest is the end of the world; and the reapers are the angels.  [23]  <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="#fn_1229"><font size="1">1230</font></a>
 And as the tares are separated and burned in the fire, so shall it be in the end of [24] this world.   <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="#fn_1230"><font size="1">1231</font></a>
 The Son of man shall send his angels, and separate from his kingdom [25] all things that injure,  <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">1232</font></a>
 and all the doers of iniquity, and they shall cast them into the [26] furnace of fire:  and there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth.   <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">1233</font></a>
 Then the righteous shall shine as the sun in the kingdom of their Father.  Whosoever hath ears that hear, let him hear.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p40"></a>[27]  <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">1234</font></a>
 And again the kingdom of heaven is like treasure hid in a field:  that which a man found and hid; and, for his pleasure in it, went and sold all that he had, and bought that field.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p42"></a>[28]  <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">1235</font></a>
 And again the kingdom of heaven is like a man <i>that is</i> a merchant seeking excellent [29] pearls;  <a name="fr_1235"></a><a href="#fn_1235"><font size="1">1236</font></a>
 and when he found one pearl of great price, he went and sold everything that he had, and bought it.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p45"></a>[30]  <a name="fr_1236"></a><a href="#fn_1236"><font size="1">1237</font></a>
 And again the kingdom of heaven is like a net that was cast <a name="fr_1237"></a><a href="#fn_1237"><font size="1">1238</font></a>
  into the sea, and [31] gathered of every kind:   <a name="fr_1238"></a><a href="#fn_1238"><font size="1">1239</font></a>
 and when it was filled, they drew it up on to the shore of the sea, and sat down to select; and the good of them they threw into the vessels, [32] and the bad they threw outside.   <a name="fr_1239"></a><a href="#fn_1239"><font size="1">1240</font></a>
 Thus shall it be in the end of the world:  the angels [33] shall go forth,  <a name="fr_1240"></a><a href="#fn_1240"><font size="1">1241</font></a>
 and separate the wicked from among the good, and shall cast them into the furnace of fire:  there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p51"></a>[34]  <a name="fr_1241"></a><a href="#fn_1241"><font size="1">1242</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, Have ye understood all these <i>things?</i>  They said unto [35] [Arabic, p. 68] him, Yea, our Lord.   <a name="fr_1242"></a><a href="#fn_1242"><font size="1">1243</font></a>
 He said unto them, Therefore every scribe that becometh a disciple of the kingdom of heaven is like a man that is a householder, who bringeth out of his treasures the new and the old.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p54"></a>[36, 37]  <a name="fr_1243"></a><a href="#fn_1243"><font size="1">1244</font></a>
 And when Jesus had finished all these parables, he removed thence, and came to his city;  <a name="fr_1244"></a><a href="#fn_1244"><font size="1">1245</font></a>
 and he taught them in their synagogues, so that they were perplexed.  [38]  <a name="fr_1245"></a><a href="#fn_1245"><font size="1">1246</font></a>
 And when the sabbath came, Jesus began to teach in the synagogue; and many of [39] those that heard marvelled, and said, Whence came these things to this <i>man?</i>  And many envied him and gave no heed to him, but said, What is this wisdom that is <a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 71</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xvii-Page_71"></a>given to this <i>man</i>, that there should happen at his hands such as these mighty works? <a name="fr_1246"></a><a href="#fn_1246"><font size="1">1247</font></a>
   [40]  <a name="fr_1247"></a><a href="#fn_1247"><font size="1">1248</font></a>
 Is not this a carpenter, son of a carpenter? and is not his mother called Mary? and [41] his brethren, James, and Joses, and Simon, and Judas?   <a name="fr_1248"></a><a href="#fn_1248"><font size="1">1249</font></a>
 And his sisters, all of them, [42] lo, are they not all with us?   <a name="fr_1249"></a><a href="#fn_1249"><font size="1">1250</font></a>
 Whence hath this <i>man</i> all these things?  And they were in doubt concerning him.   <a name="fr_1250"></a><a href="#fn_1250"><font size="1">1251</font></a>
 And Jesus knew their opinion, and said unto them, Will ye haply <a name="fr_1251"></a><a href="#fn_1251"><font size="1">1252</font></a>
  say unto me this proverb, Physician, heal first thyself:  and all that [43] we have heard that thou didst in Capernaum, do here also in thine <i>own</i> city?   <a name="fr_1252"></a><a href="#fn_1252"><font size="1">1253</font></a>
 And he said, Verily I say unto you, A prophet is not received in his <i>own</i> city, nor among [44] his brethren:   <a name="fr_1253"></a><a href="#fn_1253"><font size="1">1254</font></a>
 for a prophet is not despised, save in his <i>own</i> city, and among his <i>own</i> [45] kin, and in his <i>own</i> house.   <a name="fr_1254"></a><a href="#fn_1254"><font size="1">1255</font></a>
 Verily I say unto you, In the days of Elijah the prophet, there were many widows among the children of Israel, when the heaven held back [46] three years and six months,  <a name="fr_1255"></a><a href="#fn_1255"><font size="1">1256</font></a>
 and there was a great famine in all the land; and Elijah [Arabic, p. 69] was not sent to one of them, save to Zarephath of Sidon, to a woman that was [47] a widow.   <a name="fr_1256"></a><a href="#fn_1256"><font size="1">1257</font></a>
 And many lepers were among the children of Israel in the days of Elisha the prophet; but not one of them was cleansed, save Naaman the Nabathæan. <a name="fr_1257"></a><a href="#fn_1257"><font size="1">1258</font></a>
   [48]  <a name="fr_1258"></a><a href="#fn_1258"><font size="1">1259</font></a>
 And he was not able to do there many mighty works, <a name="fr_1259"></a><a href="#fn_1259"><font size="1">1260</font></a>
  because of their unbelief; [49] except that he laid his hand upon a few of the sick, and healed <i>them</i>.   <a name="fr_1260"></a><a href="#fn_1260"><font size="1">1261</font></a>
 And he marvelled [50] at their lack of faith.   <a name="fr_1261"></a><a href="#fn_1261"><font size="1">1262</font></a>
 And when those who were in the synagogue heard, [51] they were all filled with wrath; and they rose up,  <a name="fr_1262"></a><a href="#fn_1262"><font size="1">1263</font></a>
 and brought him forth outside the city, and brought him to the brow of the hill upon which their city was built, that [52] they might cast him from its summit:   <a name="fr_1263"></a><a href="#fn_1263"><font size="1">1264</font></a>
 but he passed through among them and went away.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xvii-p76"></a>[53]  <a name="fr_1264"></a><a href="#fn_1264"><font size="1">1265</font></a>
 And he went about in the villages which <i>were</i> around Nazareth, and taught in their synagogues.</p>
