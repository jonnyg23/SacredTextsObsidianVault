 <p> <a name="iv.iii.l"></a>
 </p><p class="c31"><a name="iv.iii.l-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.l-p1.1"></a>Section L.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.l-p2"></a>[1]  <a name="fr_3460"></a><a href="#fn_3460"><font size="1">3461</font></a>
 And Pilate said unto the chief priests and the multitude, I have not found [2] against this man anything.   <a name="fr_3461"></a><a href="#fn_3461"><font size="1">3462</font></a>
 But they cried out and said, He hath disquieted <a name="fr_3462"></a><a href="#fn_3462"><font size="1">3463</font></a>
  our people with his teaching in all Judæa, and he began <a name="fr_3463"></a><a href="#fn_3463"><font size="1">3464</font></a>
  from Galilee and unto this [3] place.   <a name="fr_3464"></a><a href="#fn_3464"><font size="1">3465</font></a>
 And Pilate, when he heard the name of Galilee, asked, Is this man a Galilæan?  [4]  <a name="fr_3465"></a><a href="#fn_3465"><font size="1">3466</font></a>
 And when he learned that he was under the jurisdiction of Herod, he sent him to Herod:  for he was in Jerusalem in those days.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.l-p9"></a>[5]  <a name="fr_3466"></a><a href="#fn_3466"><font size="1">3467</font></a>
 And Herod, when he saw Jesus, rejoiced exceedingly:  for he had desired to see him for a long time, because he had heard regarding him many things; and he counted on <a name="fr_3467"></a><a href="#fn_3467"><font size="1">3468</font></a>
  [6] [Arabic, p. 189] seeing some sign from him.   <a name="fr_3468"></a><a href="#fn_3468"><font size="1">3469</font></a>
 And he questioned him with many words; but [7] Jesus answered him not a word.   <a name="fr_3469"></a><a href="#fn_3469"><font size="1">3470</font></a>
 And the scribes and chief priests were [8] standing <i>by</i>, and they accused him vehemently.   <a name="fr_3470"></a><a href="#fn_3470"><font size="1">3471</font></a>
 And Herod scoffed at him, he and his servants; and when he had scoffed at him, he clothed him in robes of scarlet, [9] and sent him to Pilate.   <a name="fr_3471"></a><a href="#fn_3471"><font size="1">3472</font></a>
 And on that day Pilate and Herod became friends, there having been <a name="fr_3472"></a><a href="#fn_3472"><font size="1">3473</font></a>
  enmity between them before that.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.l-p17"></a>[10, 11]  <a name="fr_3473"></a><a href="#fn_3473"><font size="1">3474</font></a>
 And Pilate called the chief priests and the rulers of the people,  <a name="fr_3474"></a><a href="#fn_3474"><font size="1">3475</font></a>
 and said unto them, Ye brought unto me this man, as the perverter of your people:  and I have tried him before you, and have not found in this man any cause <a name="fr_3475"></a><a href="#fn_3475"><font size="1">3476</font></a>
  of all that ye [12] seek <a name="fr_3476"></a><a href="#fn_3476"><font size="1">3477</font></a>
  against him:   <a name="fr_3477"></a><a href="#fn_3477"><font size="1">3478</font></a>
 nor yet Herod:  for I sent him unto him; and he hath done [13] nothing for which he should deserve death.   <a name="fr_3478"></a><a href="#fn_3478"><font size="1">3479</font></a>
 So now I will chastise him, and let [14, 15] him go.   <a name="fr_3479"></a><a href="#fn_3479"><font size="1">3480</font></a>
 The multitude all cried out and said, Take him from us, take him.   <a name="fr_3480"></a><a href="#fn_3480"><font size="1">3481</font></a>
 And [16] the chief priests and the elders accused him of many things.   <a name="fr_3481"></a><a href="#fn_3481"><font size="1">3482</font></a>
 And during their [17] accusation he answered not a word.   <a name="fr_3482"></a><a href="#fn_3482"><font size="1">3483</font></a>
 Then Pilate said unto him, Hearest thou not [18] how many <i>things</i> they witness against thee?   <a name="fr_3483"></a><a href="#fn_3483"><font size="1">3484</font></a>
 And he answered him not, not even one word:  and Pilate marvelled at that.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.l-p29"></a>[19]  <a name="fr_3484"></a><a href="#fn_3484"><font size="1">3485</font></a>
 And when the judge sat on his tribune, his wife sent unto him, and said unto him, See that thou have nothing to do with that righteous <i>man</i>:  for I have suffered much in my dream <a name="fr_3485"></a><a href="#fn_3485"><font size="1">3486</font></a>
  to-day because of him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.l-p32"></a>[20]  <a name="fr_3486"></a><a href="#fn_3486"><font size="1">3487</font></a>
 And at every feast the custom of the judge was to release to the people one [21] prisoner, him whom they would.   <a name="fr_3487"></a><a href="#fn_3487"><font size="1">3488</font></a>
 And there was in their prison a well-known prisoner, [22, 23] called Barabbas.   <a name="fr_3488"></a><a href="#fn_3488"><font size="1">3489</font></a>
 And when they assembled, Pilate said unto them,  <a name="fr_3489"></a><a href="#fn_3489"><font size="1">3490</font></a>
 Ye have a custom, that I should release unto you a prisoner at the passover:  will ye that I [24] release unto you the King of the Jews?   <a name="fr_3490"></a><a href="#fn_3490"><font size="1">3491</font></a>
 And they all cried out and said, Release not [Arabic, p. 190] unto us this <i>man</i>, but release unto us Barabbas.  And this Barabbas was a [25] robber,  <a name="fr_3491"></a><a href="#fn_3491"><font size="1">3492</font></a>
 who for sedition <a name="fr_3492"></a><a href="#fn_3492"><font size="1">3493</font></a>
  and murder, which was in the city, was cast into the <a name="page_121"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 121</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.l-Page_121"></a>[26] prison.   <a name="fr_3493"></a><a href="#fn_3493"><font size="1">3494</font></a>
 And all the people cried out and began to ask <i>him to do</i> as the custom was [27] that he should do with them.   <a name="fr_3494"></a><a href="#fn_3494"><font size="1">3495</font></a>
 And Pilate answered and said unto them, Whom will ye that I release unto you?  Barabbas, or Jesus which is called the Messiah, the [28] King of the Jews?   <a name="fr_3495"></a><a href="#fn_3495"><font size="1">3496</font></a>
 For Pilate knew that envy had moved them to deliver him up. [29]  <a name="fr_3496"></a><a href="#fn_3496"><font size="1">3497</font></a>
 And the chief priests and the elders asked the multitudes to deliver Barabbas, and [30] to destroy Jesus.   <a name="fr_3497"></a><a href="#fn_3497"><font size="1">3498</font></a>
 The judge answered and said unto them, Whom of the two will [31] ye that I release unto you?  They said, Barabbas.   <a name="fr_3498"></a><a href="#fn_3498"><font size="1">3499</font></a>
 Pilate said unto them, And [32] Jesus which is called the Messiah, what shall I do with him?   <a name="fr_3499"></a><a href="#fn_3499"><font size="1">3500</font></a>
 They all cried out [33] and said, Crucify him.   <a name="fr_3500"></a><a href="#fn_3500"><font size="1">3501</font></a>
 And Pilate spake to them again, for he desired to release [34] Jesus;  <a name="fr_3501"></a><a href="#fn_3501"><font size="1">3502</font></a>
 but they cried out and said, Crucify him, crucify him, and release unto us [35] Barabbas.   <a name="fr_3502"></a><a href="#fn_3502"><font size="1">3503</font></a>
 And Pilate said unto them a third time, What evil hath this <i>man</i> done?  I have not found in him any cause <a name="fr_3503"></a><a href="#fn_3503"><font size="1">3504</font></a>
  to necessitate death:  I will chastise him and [36] let him go.   <a name="fr_3504"></a><a href="#fn_3504"><font size="1">3505</font></a>
 But they increased in importunity <a name="fr_3505"></a><a href="#fn_3505"><font size="1">3506</font></a>
  with a loud voice, and asked him to crucify him.  And their voice, and the voice of the chief priests, prevailed.  [37]  <a name="fr_3506"></a><a href="#fn_3506"><font size="1">3507</font></a>
 Then Pilate released unto them that one who was cast into prison for sedition and murder, Barabbas, whom they asked for:   <a name="fr_3507"></a><a href="#fn_3507"><font size="1">3508</font></a>
 and he scourged Jesus with whips. <a name="fr_3508"></a><a href="#fn_3508"><font size="1">3509</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.l-p56"></a>[38]  <a name="fr_3509"></a><a href="#fn_3509"><font size="1">3510</font></a>
 Then the footsoldiers of the judge took Jesus, and went into the prætorium, and [39] [Arabic, p. 191] gathered unto him all of the footsoldiers.   <a name="fr_3510"></a><a href="#fn_3510"><font size="1">3511</font></a>
 And they stripped him, and put on [40] him a scarlet cloak.   <a name="fr_3511"></a><a href="#fn_3511"><font size="1">3512</font></a>
 And they clothed him in garments of purple, and plaited [41] a crown of thorns, and placed it on his head, and a reed in his right hand;  <a name="fr_3512"></a><a href="#fn_3512"><font size="1">3513</font></a>
 and while they mocked at him and laughed, they fell down on their knees before him, and bowed [42] down to <a name="fr_3513"></a><a href="#fn_3513"><font size="1">3514</font></a>
  him, and said, Hail, <a name="fr_3514"></a><a href="#fn_3514"><font size="1">3515</font></a>
  King of the Jews!   <a name="fr_3515"></a><a href="#fn_3515"><font size="1">3516</font></a>
 And they spat in his face, and took the reed from his hand, and struck him on his head,  <a name="fr_3516"></a><a href="#fn_3516"><font size="1">3517</font></a>
 and smote his cheeks.  [43]  <a name="fr_3517"></a><a href="#fn_3517"><font size="1">3518</font></a>
 And Pilate went forth without again, and said unto the Jews, I bring him forth to [44] you, that ye may know that I do not find, in examining <a name="fr_3518"></a><a href="#fn_3518"><font size="1">3519</font></a>
  him, even one crime. <a name="fr_3519"></a><a href="#fn_3519"><font size="1">3520</font></a>
    <a name="fr_3520"></a><a href="#fn_3520"><font size="1">3521</font></a>
 And Jesus went forth without, wearing the crown of thorns and the purple garments.  [45]  <a name="fr_3521"></a><a href="#fn_3521"><font size="1">3522</font></a>
 Pilate said unto them, Behold, the man!  And when the chief priests and the soldiers <a name="fr_3522"></a><a href="#fn_3522"><font size="1">3523</font></a>
  saw him, they cried out and said, Crucify him, crucify him.  Pilate said unto them, Take him yourselves, and crucify him:  for I find not a cause <a name="fr_3523"></a><a href="#fn_3523"><font size="1">3524</font></a>
  against [46] him.   <a name="fr_3524"></a><a href="#fn_3524"><font size="1">3525</font></a>
 The Jews said unto him, We have a law, and according to our law he deserves [47] death, because he made himself the Son of God.   <a name="fr_3525"></a><a href="#fn_3525"><font size="1">3526</font></a>
 And when Pilate heard this word, [48] his fear increased;  <a name="fr_3526"></a><a href="#fn_3526"><font size="1">3527</font></a>
 and he entered again into the porch, and said to Jesus, Whence [49] art thou?   <a name="fr_3527"></a><a href="#fn_3527"><font size="1">3528</font></a>
 But Jesus answered him not a word.  Pilate said unto him, Speakest <a name="fr_3528"></a><a href="#fn_3528"><font size="1">3529</font></a>
  thou not unto me? knowest thou not that I have authority to release thee, and have [50] authority to crucify thee?   <a name="fr_3529"></a><a href="#fn_3529"><font size="1">3530</font></a>
 Jesus said unto him, Thou hast not any <a name="fr_3530"></a><a href="#fn_3530"><font size="1">3531</font></a>
  authority over me, if thou wert not given <i>it</i> from above:  therefore the sin of him that delivered [51] me up unto thee is greater than thy sin.   <a name="fr_3531"></a><a href="#fn_3531"><font size="1">3532</font></a>
 And for this word Pilate wished to release him:  but the Jews cried out, If thou let him go, thou art not a friend of Cæsar:  for every one that maketh himself a king is against Cæsar.</p>
