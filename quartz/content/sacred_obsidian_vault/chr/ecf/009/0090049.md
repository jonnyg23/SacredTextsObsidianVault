 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xl"></a>
 </p><p class="c27"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p1"></a> <span class="c6"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p1.1"></a>Section XL.</span></p> 
 <p class="c12"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p2"></a>[1] [Arabic, p. 150]  <a name="fr_2716"></a><a href="#fn_2716"><font size="1">2717</font></a>
 And when Jesus entered the temple, they brought unto him blind and [2] lame:  and he healed them.   <a name="fr_2717"></a><a href="#fn_2717"><font size="1">2718</font></a>
 But when the chief priests and the Pharisees saw the wonders that he did, and the children that were crying in the temple and [3] saying, Praise be to the Son of David:  it distressed them,  <a name="fr_2718"></a><a href="#fn_2718"><font size="1">2719</font></a>
 and they said, Hearest thou not what these say?  Jesus said unto them, Yea:  did ye not read long ago, From [4] the mouths of children and infants thou hast chosen my praise?   <a name="fr_2719"></a><a href="#fn_2719"><font size="1">2720</font></a>
 And the Pharisees said one to another, Behold, do ye not see that nothing availeth us? for lo, the whole world hath followed him.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p7"></a>[5]  <a name="fr_2720"></a><a href="#fn_2720"><font size="1">2721</font></a>
 And there were among them certain Gentiles also, which had come up to worship [6] at the feast:   <a name="fr_2721"></a><a href="#fn_2721"><font size="1">2722</font></a>
 these therefore came to Philip, who was of Bethsaida of Galilee, [7] and asked him, and said unto him, My lord, we wish to see Jesus.   <a name="fr_2722"></a><a href="#fn_2722"><font size="1">2723</font></a>
 And Philip [8] came and told Andrew:  and Andrew and Philip told Jesus.   <a name="fr_2723"></a><a href="#fn_2723"><font size="1">2724</font></a>
 And Jesus answered and said unto them, The hour is come nigh, in which the Son of man is to be glorified.  [9]  <a name="fr_2724"></a><a href="#fn_2724"><font size="1">2725</font></a>
 Verily, verily, I say unto you, A grain of wheat, if it fall not and die in the [10] earth, remaineth alone; but if it die, it beareth much fruit.   <a name="fr_2725"></a><a href="#fn_2725"><font size="1">2726</font></a>
 He that loveth his life <a name="fr_2726"></a><a href="#fn_2726"><font size="1">2727</font></a>
  destroyeth it; and he that hateth his life <a name="fr_2727"></a><a href="#fn_2727"><font size="1">2728</font></a>
  in this world shall keep it unto the life eternal.  [11]  <a name="fr_2728"></a><a href="#fn_2728"><font size="1">2729</font></a>
 If a man serve me, he will follow me; and where I am, there shall my servant be [12] also:  and whosoever serveth me, the Father will honour him.   <a name="fr_2729"></a><a href="#fn_2729"><font size="1">2730</font></a>
 Now is my soul troubled:  [Arabic, p. 151] and what shall I say?  My Father, deliver me from this hour.  But [13] for this cause came I unto this hour.   <a name="fr_2730"></a><a href="#fn_2730"><font size="1">2731</font></a>
 My Father, glorify thy name.  And a [14] voice was heard from heaven, I have glorified <i>it</i>, and shall glorify <i>it</i>.   <a name="fr_2731"></a><a href="#fn_2731"><font size="1">2732</font></a>
 And the multitude that were standing heard, and said, This is thunder:  and others said, An [15] angel speaketh to him.   <a name="fr_2732"></a><a href="#fn_2732"><font size="1">2733</font></a>
 Jesus answered and said unto them, Not because of me [16] was this voice, but because of you.   <a name="fr_2733"></a><a href="#fn_2733"><font size="1">2734</font></a>
 Now is the judgement of this world; and the [17] prince of this world shall now be cast forth.   <a name="fr_2734"></a><a href="#fn_2734"><font size="1">2735</font></a>
 And I, when I am lifted up from the [18] earth, shall draw every man unto me.   <a name="fr_2735"></a><a href="#fn_2735"><font size="1">2736</font></a>
 This he said, that he might shew by what [19] manner of death he should die.   <a name="fr_2736"></a><a href="#fn_2736"><font size="1">2737</font></a>
 The multitudes said unto him, We have heard out of the law that the Messiah abideth for ever:  how then sayest thou, that the Son of [20] man is to be lifted up? who is this, the Son of man?   <a name="fr_2737"></a><a href="#fn_2737"><font size="1">2738</font></a>
 Jesus said unto them, Another little while is the light with you.  Walk so long as ye have light, lest the darkness overtake you; for he that walketh in the darkness knoweth not whither he goeth.  [21]  <a name="fr_2738"></a><a href="#fn_2738"><font size="1">2739</font></a>
 So long as ye have light, believe the light, that ye may be the children of the light.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p27"></a>[22]  <a name="fr_2739"></a><a href="#fn_2739"><font size="1">2740</font></a>
 And when certain of the Pharisees asked of Jesus, when the kingdom of God should come, he answered and said unto them, The kingdom of God cometh not <a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xl-Page_105"></a>[23] with expectation:   <a name="fr_2740"></a><a href="#fn_2740"><font size="1">2741</font></a>
 neither shall they say, Lo, it is here! nor, Lo, it is there! for the kingdom of God is within you.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p30"></a>[24]  <a name="fr_2741"></a><a href="#fn_2741"><font size="1">2742</font></a>
 And in the day<i>time</i> he was teaching in the temple; and at night he used to go [25] out, and pass the night in the mount called the mount of Olives.   <a name="fr_2742"></a><a href="#fn_2742"><font size="1">2743</font></a>
 And all the people came <a name="fr_2743"></a><a href="#fn_2743"><font size="1">2744</font></a>
  to him in the morning in the temple, to hear his word.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p34"></a>[26, 27]  <a name="fr_2744"></a><a href="#fn_2744"><font size="1">2745</font></a>
 Then spake Jesus unto the multitudes and his disciples, and said unto them,  <a name="fr_2745"></a><a href="#fn_2745"><font size="1">2746</font></a>
 On [28] [Arabic, p. 152] the seat of Moses are seated the scribes and Pharisees:   <a name="fr_2746"></a><a href="#fn_2746"><font size="1">2747</font></a>
 everything that they say unto you now to keep, keep and do:  but according to their deeds [29] do ye not; for they say, and do not.   <a name="fr_2747"></a><a href="#fn_2747"><font size="1">2748</font></a>
 And they bind heavy burdens, and lay them on the shoulders of the people; while they with one of their fingers will not come [30, 31] near <a name="fr_2748"></a><a href="#fn_2748"><font size="1">2749</font></a>
  them.   <a name="fr_2749"></a><a href="#fn_2749"><font size="1">2750</font></a>
 But all their deeds they do to make a shew before men.   <a name="fr_2750"></a><a href="#fn_2750"><font size="1">2751</font></a>
 And all the multitude were hearing that with pleasure.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p42"></a>[32]  <a name="fr_2751"></a><a href="#fn_2751"><font size="1">2752</font></a>
 And in the course of his teaching he said unto them, Guard yourselves from the [33] scribes, who desire to walk in robes,  <a name="fr_2752"></a><a href="#fn_2752"><font size="1">2753</font></a>
 and love salutation in the marketplaces, and sitting in the highest places of the synagogues, and at feasts in the highest parts of [34] the rooms:   <a name="fr_2753"></a><a href="#fn_2753"><font size="1">2754</font></a>
 and they broaden their amulets, and lengthen the cords of their cloaks, [35]  <a name="fr_2754"></a><a href="#fn_2754"><font size="1">2755</font></a>
 and <i>love</i> that they should be called by men, My master,  <a name="fr_2755"></a><a href="#fn_2755"><font size="1">2756</font></a>
 and devour widows houses, because <a name="fr_2756"></a><a href="#fn_2756"><font size="1">2757</font></a>
  of their prolonging their prayers; these then shall receive greater judgement.  [36]  <a name="fr_2757"></a><a href="#fn_2757"><font size="1">2758</font></a>
 But ye, be ye not called masters: <a name="fr_2758"></a><a href="#fn_2758"><font size="1">2759</font></a>
   for your master is one; all ye are brethren.  [37]  <a name="fr_2759"></a><a href="#fn_2759"><font size="1">2760</font></a>
 Call not then to yourselves <i>any one</i> <a name="fr_2760"></a><a href="#fn_2760"><font size="1">2761</font></a>
  father on earth:  for your Father is one, who is [38] in heaven.   <a name="fr_2761"></a><a href="#fn_2761"><font size="1">2762</font></a>
 And be not called directors:  for your director is one, <i>even</i> the Messiah.  [39, 40]  <a name="fr_2762"></a><a href="#fn_2762"><font size="1">2763</font></a>
 He that is great among you shall be unto you a minister.   <a name="fr_2763"></a><a href="#fn_2763"><font size="1">2764</font></a>
 Whosoever shall exalt himself shall be abased; and whosoever shall abase himself shall be exalted.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p56"></a>[41]  <a name="fr_2764"></a><a href="#fn_2764"><font size="1">2765</font></a>
 Woe unto you, Pharisees! because ye love the highest places in the synagogues, and salutation in the marketplaces.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p58"></a>[42]  <a name="fr_2765"></a><a href="#fn_2765"><font size="1">2766</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! because ye devour widows houses, because <a name="fr_2766"></a><a href="#fn_2766"><font size="1">2767</font></a>
  of your prolonging your prayers:  for this <i>reason</i> then ye shall receive greater judgement.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p61"></a>[43]  <a name="fr_2767"></a><a href="#fn_2767"><font size="1">2768</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! because ye have shut the kingdom of God before men.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p63"></a>[44] [Arabic, p. 153]  <a name="fr_2768"></a><a href="#fn_2768"><font size="1">2769</font></a>
 Woe unto you that know the law! for ye concealed the keys of knowledge:   <a name="fr_2769"></a><a href="#fn_2769"><font size="1">2770</font></a>
 ye enter not, and those that are entering ye suffer not to enter.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p66"></a>[45]  <a name="fr_2770"></a><a href="#fn_2770"><font size="1">2771</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! because <a name="fr_2771"></a><a href="#fn_2771"><font size="1">2772</font></a>
  ye compass land and sea to draw <a name="fr_2772"></a><a href="#fn_2772"><font size="1">2773</font></a>
  one proselyte; and when he is <i>become so</i>, ye make him a son of hell twice as much <a name="fr_2773"></a><a href="#fn_2773"><font size="1">2774</font></a>
  as yourselves.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p71"></a>[46]  <a name="fr_2774"></a><a href="#fn_2774"><font size="1">2775</font></a>
 Woe unto you, ye blind guides! because ye say, Whosoever sweareth by the temple, it is nothing; but whosoever sweareth by the gold that is in the temple, [47] shall be condemned. <a name="fr_2775"></a><a href="#fn_2775"><font size="1">2776</font></a>
   <a name="fr_2776"></a><a href="#fn_2776"><font size="1">2777</font></a>
 Ye blind foolish <i>ones</i>:  which is greater, the gold, or the [48] temple which sanctifieth the gold?   <a name="fr_2777"></a><a href="#fn_2777"><font size="1">2778</font></a>
 And, Whosoever sweareth by the altar, it is nothing; but whosoever sweareth by the offering that is upon it, shall be condemned. <a name="fr_2778"></a><a href="#fn_2778"><font size="1">2779</font></a>
   [49]  <a name="fr_2779"></a><a href="#fn_2779"><font size="1">2780</font></a>
 Ye blind foolish <i>ones</i>:  which is greater, the offering, or the altar which sanctifieth [50] the offering?   <a name="fr_2780"></a><a href="#fn_2780"><font size="1">2781</font></a>
 Whosoever then sweareth by the altar, hath sworn by it, and by all [51] that is upon it.   <a name="fr_2781"></a><a href="#fn_2781"><font size="1">2782</font></a>
 And whosoever sweareth by the temple, hath sworn by it, and by [52] him that is dwelling in it.   <a name="fr_2782"></a><a href="#fn_2782"><font size="1">2783</font></a>
 And whosoever sweareth by heaven, hath sworn by the throne of God, and by him that sitteth upon it.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p81"></a>[53]  <a name="fr_2783"></a><a href="#fn_2783"><font size="1">2784</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! because ye tithe mint and rue and dill and cummin and all herbs, and ye leave the important <i>matters</i> of the law, judgement, and mercy, and faith, and the love of God:  this ought ye to do, and [54] not to leave that <i>undone</i>.   <a name="fr_2784"></a><a href="#fn_2784"><font size="1">2785</font></a>
 Ye blind guides, which strain out a gnat, and swallow <a name="fr_2785"></a><a href="#fn_2785"><font size="1">2786</font></a>
  camels.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p85"></a>[55]  <a name="fr_2786"></a><a href="#fn_2786"><font size="1">2787</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! because ye cleanse the outside <a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a> <a name="iv.iii.xl-Page_106"></a>of the cup and of the platter, while the inside of them is full of injustice and wrong.  [56]  <a name="fr_2787"></a><a href="#fn_2787"><font size="1">2788</font></a>
 Ye blind Pharisees, cleanse first the inside of the cup and of the platter, then shall the outside of them be cleansed.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p88"></a>[57] [Arabic, p. 154]  <a name="fr_2788"></a><a href="#fn_2788"><font size="1">2789</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! because ye resemble whited sepulchres, which appear <a name="fr_2789"></a><a href="#fn_2789"><font size="1">2790</font></a>
  from the outside beautiful, but within [58] full of the bones of the dead, and all uncleanness.   <a name="fr_2790"></a><a href="#fn_2790"><font size="1">2791</font></a>
 So ye also from without appear unto men like the righteous, but within ye are full of wrong and hypocrisy.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p92"></a>[59]  <a name="fr_2791"></a><a href="#fn_2791"><font size="1">2792</font></a>
 One of the scribes answered and said unto him, Teacher, in this saying of thine [60] thou art casting a slur on us.   <a name="fr_2792"></a><a href="#fn_2792"><font size="1">2793</font></a>
 He said, And to you also, ye scribes, woe! for ye lade men with heavy burdens, and ye with one of your fingers come not near <a name="fr_2793"></a><a href="#fn_2793"><font size="1">2794</font></a>
  those burdens.</p> 
 <p class="c13"><a name="iv.iii.xl-p96"></a>[61]  <a name="fr_2794"></a><a href="#fn_2794"><font size="1">2795</font></a>
 Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye build the tombs of the prophets, which your fathers killed, and adorn the burying-places of the righteous, [62] and say,  <a name="fr_2795"></a><a href="#fn_2795"><font size="1">2796</font></a>
 If we had been in the days of our fathers, we should not have been partakers [63] with them in the blood of the prophets.   <a name="fr_2796"></a><a href="#fn_2796"><font size="1">2797</font></a>
 Wherefore, behold, ye witness against [64] yourselves, that ye are the children of those that slew the prophets.   <a name="fr_2797"></a><a href="#fn_2797"><font size="1">2798</font></a>
 And ye also, [65] ye fill up the measure <a name="fr_2798"></a><a href="#fn_2798"><font size="1">2799</font></a>
  of your fathers.   <a name="fr_2799"></a><a href="#fn_2799"><font size="1">2800</font></a>
 Ye serpents, ye children of vipers, where shall ye flee from the judgement of Gehenna?</p>
