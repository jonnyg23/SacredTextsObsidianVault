 <p> <a name="v.ii.i"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="v.ii.i-p0.1"></a>Elucidation.</h4>  
 <p class="Header"><a name="v.ii.i-p1"></a>(<a name="v.ii.i-p1.1"></a>Aviricius Marcellus, p. 335, <span><a name="v.ii.i-p1.3"></a>supra</span>)   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="v.ii.i-p2"></a><span><a name="v.ii.i-p2.1"></a>Like</span> his great predecessor in Patristic research (Bishop<a name="v.ii.i-p2.2"></a> Pearson), the learned and indefatigable Bishop <a name="v.ii.i-p2.3"></a>Lightfoot will leave us gold-dust in the mere sweepings of his literary work. His recent voluminous edition of the <a name="v.ii.i-p2.4"></a>Apostolic Fathers <a name="fr_2191"></a><a href="#fn_2191"><font size="1">2192</font></a>
  is encyclopedic in its treatment of the subject; and I had hardly corrected the last proofs of the fragments ascribed to <a name="v.ii.i-p3.1"></a>Asterius Urbanus when I discovered, in one of his notes on <a name="v.ii.i-p3.2"></a>Polycarp, a most brilliant elucidation of a matter which I had supposed involved in twofold obscurity. <a name="v.ii.i-p3.3"></a>Asterius is a mere name embedded in <a name="v.ii.i-p3.4"></a>Eusebius, and in his fragments there preserved is embedded the yet obscurer name of <a name="v.ii.i-p3.5"></a>Aviricius Marcellus, which the reader will find, with its various spellings, in one of the translators notes. <a name="fr_2192"></a><a href="#fn_2192"><font size="1">2193</font></a>
  Who could have supposed that even the learning and ingenuity of <a name="v.ii.i-p4.3"></a>Lightfoot could fish out of very dark waters such shining booty as fills the network about “<a name="v.ii.i-p4.4"></a>Abercius of Hierapolis?” While he does not even name <a name="v.ii.i-p4.5"></a>Asterius, the mere <span><a name="v.ii.i-p4.6"></a>nominis umbra</span> of <a name="v.ii.i-p4.7"></a>Aviricius Marcellus is material for a truly remarkable dissertation covering nine pages of fine print, and enabling us to conclude that this <a name="v.ii.i-p4.8"></a>Aviricius is none other than the same “bishop of Hierapolis” about whom there is such a long story in the Bollandist <a name="v.ii.i-p4.9"></a>Acta Sanctorum  <a name="fr_2193"></a><a href="#fn_2193"><font size="1">2194</font></a>
  The story is a silly legend, but <a name="v.ii.i-p5.4"></a>Lightfoot understands the art <span class="Foreign"><a name="v.ii.i-p5.5"></a>ex fumo dare lucem</span>; and any one who enjoys following up such elaborations will find most curious and delightful reading in the pages to which I have referred. Our <a name="v.ii.i-p5.6"></a>Aviricius, then, was bishop of “Hierapolis of Lesser Phrygia,” not of Hierapolis on the Mæander, and flourished about <span><a name="v.ii.i-p5.7"></a>a.d.</span>163, during the reign of <a name="v.ii.i-p5.8"></a>M. Aurelius. This date, therefore, must correct the conjecture of <a name="v.ii.i-p5.9"></a>Tillemont and the date which I had accepted from him on the authority of Dr. <a name="v.ii.i-p5.10"></a>Lardner. <a name="fr_2194"></a><a href="#fn_2194"><font size="1">2195</font></a>
   </p>
