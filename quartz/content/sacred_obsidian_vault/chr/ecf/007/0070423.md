 <p> <a name="viii.iii.xv"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="viii.iii.xv-p0.1"></a>Chapter XV. <a name="fr_2496"></a><a href="#fn_2496"><font size="1">2497</font></a>
 —Bishops and Deacons; Christian Reproof.</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="viii.iii.xv-p2"></a>1. Appoint, therefore, for yourselves, bishops and deacons worthy of the Lord, men meek, and not lovers of money, <a name="fr_2497"></a><a href="#fn_2497"><font size="1">2498</font></a>
  and truthful and proved; for they also render to you the service <a name="fr_2498"></a><a href="#fn_2498"><font size="1">2499</font></a>
  of prophets and teachers. 2. Despise them not therefore, for they are your honoured ones, together with the prophets and teachers. 3. And reprove one another, not in anger, but in peace, as ye have <span><a name="viii.iii.xv-p4.1"></a>it</span> in the Gospel; <a name="fr_2499"></a><a href="#fn_2499"><font size="1">2500</font></a>
  but to every one that acts amiss <a name="fr_2500"></a><a href="#fn_2500"><font size="1">2501</font></a>
  against another, let no one speak, nor let him hear aught from you until he repent. 4. But your prayers and alms and all your deeds so do, as ye have <span><a name="viii.iii.xv-p6.4"></a>it</span> in the Gospel of our Lord. <a name="fr_2501"></a><a href="#fn_2501"><font size="1">2502</font></a>
   </p>

 <a name="fn_2496"></a><a href="0070423.htm#fr_2496">381:2497</a> <a name="viii.iii.xv-p0.2"></a>  
 <p class="Footnote"><a name="viii.iii.xv-p1"></a> The larger part of verse 1, and a clause from verses 2, 3, respectively, are found in <a name="viii.iii.xv-p1.1"></a>Apostolic Constitutions, vii. 31. Verses 1, 2, both in the use of terms and in the Church polity indicated, point to an early date: (1) There are evident marks of a transition from extraordinary to ordinary ministers. (2) The distinction between bishops and elders does not appear [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#001">1 Pet. v. 1</a><a name="viii.iii.xv-p1.2"></a>. Vol. i. p. 16, this series], and yet it is found in Ignatius. (3) The word <span class="Greek"><a name="viii.iii.xv-p1.4"></a>χειροτονέω </span>is here used in the sense of “elect” or “appoint” (by show of hands), and not in that of “ordain” (by laying on of hands). The former is the New Testament sense (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act014.htm#023">Acts xiv. 23</a><a name="viii.iii.xv-p1.5"></a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#019">2 Cor. viii. 19</a><a name="viii.iii.xv-p1.6"></a>), also in Ignatius; the latter sense is found in <a name="viii.iii.xv-p1.7"></a>Apostolic Canons, i. (4) The choice by the people also indicates an early period.  </p>
