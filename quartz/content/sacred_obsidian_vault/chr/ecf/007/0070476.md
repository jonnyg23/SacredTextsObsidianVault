 <p> <a name="x.iii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p0.1"></a>The Homily. <a name="fr_3847"></a><a href="#fn_3847"><font size="1">3848</font></a>
  </h3>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p1.7"></a>Chap. i.—we ought to think highly of christ.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="x.iii-p2.1"></a>Brethren</span>, it is fitting that you should think of <a name="x.iii-p2.2"></a>Jesus Christ as of God,—as the Judge of the living and the dead. And it does not become us <a name="fr_3848"></a><a href="#fn_3848"><font size="1">3849</font></a>
  to think lightly <a name="fr_3849"></a><a href="#fn_3849"><font size="1">3850</font></a>
  of our salvation; for if we think little <a name="fr_3850"></a><a href="#fn_3850"><font size="1">3851</font></a>
  of Him, we shall also hope but to obtain little <span><a name="x.iii-p5.2"></a>from Him</span>. And those of us <a name="fr_3851"></a><a href="#fn_3851"><font size="1">3852</font></a>
  who hear carelessly of these things, as if they were of small importance, commit sin, not knowing whence we have been called, and by whom, and to what place, and how much <a name="x.iii-p6.4"></a>Jesus Christ submitted to suffer for our sakes. What return, then, shall we make to Him? or what fruit that shall be worthy of that which He has given to us? For, <a name="fr_3852"></a><a href="#fn_3852"><font size="1">3853</font></a>
  indeed, how great are the benefits <a name="fr_3853"></a><a href="#fn_3853"><font size="1">3854</font></a>
  which we owe to Him! He has graciously given us light; as a Father, He has called us sons; He has saved us when we were ready to perish. What praise, then, shall we give to Him, or what return shall we make for the things which we have received? <a name="fr_3854"></a><a href="#fn_3854"><font size="1">3855</font></a>
  We were deficient <a name="fr_3855"></a><a href="#fn_3855"><font size="1">3856</font></a>
  in understanding, worshipping stones and wood, and gold, and silver, and brass, the works of mens hand; <a name="fr_3856"></a><a href="#fn_3856"><font size="1">3857</font></a>
  and our whole life was nothing else than death. Involved in blindness, and with such darkness <a name="fr_3857"></a><a href="#fn_3857"><font size="1">3858</font></a>
  before our eyes, we have received sight, and through His will have laid aside that cloud by which we were enveloped. For He had compassion on us, and mercifully saved us, observing the many errors in which we were entangled, as well as the destruction to which we were exposed, <a name="fr_3858"></a><a href="#fn_3858"><font size="1">3859</font></a>
  and that we had <a name="fr_3859"></a><a href="#fn_3859"><font size="1">3860</font></a>
  no hope of salvation except it came to us from Him. For He called us when we were not, <a name="fr_3860"></a><a href="#fn_3860"><font size="1">3861</font></a>
  and willed that out of nothing we should attain a real existence. <a name="fr_3861"></a><a href="#fn_3861"><font size="1">3862</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p16.2"></a>Chap. ii.—the church, formerly barren, is now fruitful.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p17"></a>“Rejoice, thou barren that bearest not; break forth and cry, thou that travailest not; for she that is desolate hath many more children than she that hath an husband.” <a name="fr_3862"></a><a href="#fn_3862"><font size="1">3863</font></a>
  In that He said, “Rejoice, thou barren that bearest not,” He referred to us, for our Church was barren before that children were given to her. But when He said, “Cry out, thou that travailest not,” He means this, that we should sincerely offer up our prayers to God, and should not, like women in travail, show signs of weakness. <a name="fr_3863"></a><a href="#fn_3863"><font size="1">3864</font></a>
  And in that He said, “For she that is desolate hath many more children than she that hath an husband,” <span><a name="x.iii-p19.2"></a>He means</span> that <a name="fr_3864"></a><a href="#fn_3864"><font size="1">3865</font></a>
  our people seemed to be outcast from God, but now, through believing, have become more numerous than those who are reckoned to possess God. <a name="fr_3865"></a><a href="#fn_3865"><font size="1">3866</font></a>
  And another Scripture saith, “I came not to call the righteous, but sinners.” <a name="fr_3866"></a><a href="#fn_3866"><font size="1">3867</font></a>
  This means that those who are perishing must be saved. For it is indeed a great and admirable thing to establish, not the things which are standing, but these that are falling. Thus also did Christ desire <a name="fr_3867"></a><a href="#fn_3867"><font size="1">3868</font></a>
  to save the things which were perishing, <a name="fr_3868"></a><a href="#fn_3868"><font size="1">3869</font></a>
  and has saved many by coming and calling us when hastening to destruction. <a name="fr_3869"></a><a href="#fn_3869"><font size="1">3870</font></a>
  <a name="page_518"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 518</font></a> <a name="x.iii-Page_518"></a>  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p25.2"></a>Chap. iii.—the duty of confessing christ.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p26"></a>Since, then, He has displayed so great mercy towards us, and especially in this respect, that we who are living should not offer sacrifices to gods that are dead, or pay them worship, but should attain through Him to the knowledge of the true Father, <a name="fr_3870"></a><a href="#fn_3870"><font size="1">3871</font></a>
  whereby shall we show that we do indeed know Him, <a name="fr_3871"></a><a href="#fn_3871"><font size="1">3872</font></a>
  but by not denying Him through whom this knowledge has been attained? For He Himself declares, <a name="fr_3872"></a><a href="#fn_3872"><font size="1">3873</font></a>
  “Whosoever shall confess Me before men, him will I confess before My Father.” <a name="fr_3873"></a><a href="#fn_3873"><font size="1">3874</font></a>
  This, then, is our reward if we shall confess Him by whom we have been saved. But in what way shall we confess Him? By doing what He says, and not transgressing His commandments, and by honouring Him not with our lips only, but with all our heart and all our mind. <a name="fr_3874"></a><a href="#fn_3874"><font size="1">3875</font></a>
  For he says <a name="fr_3875"></a><a href="#fn_3875"><font size="1">3876</font></a>
  in Isaiah, “This people honoureth Me with their lips, but their heart is far from Me.” <a name="fr_3876"></a><a href="#fn_3876"><font size="1">3877</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p33.2"></a>Chap. iv.—true confession of christ.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p34"></a>Let us, then, not only call Him Lord, for that will not save us. For He saith, “Not every one that saith to Me, Lord, Lord, shall be saved, but he that worketh righteousness.” <a name="fr_3877"></a><a href="#fn_3877"><font size="1">3878</font></a>
  Wherefore, brethren, let us confess Him by <a name="fr_3878"></a><a href="#fn_3878"><font size="1">3879</font></a>
  our works, by loving one another, by not committing adultery, or speaking evil of one another, or cherishing envy; but being continent, compassionate, and good. We ought also to sympathize with one another, and not be avaricious. By such <a name="fr_3879"></a><a href="#fn_3879"><font size="1">3880</font></a>
  works let us confess Him, <a name="fr_3880"></a><a href="#fn_3880"><font size="1">3881</font></a>
  and not by those that are of an opposite kind. And it is not fitting that we should fear men, but rather God. For this reason, if we should do such <span><a name="x.iii-p38.1"></a>wicked</span> things, the Lord hath said, “Even though ye were gathered together to Me <a name="fr_3881"></a><a href="#fn_3881"><font size="1">3882</font></a>
  in My very bosom, yet if ye were not to keep My commandments, I would cast you off, and say unto you, Depart from Me; I know you not whence ye are, ye workers of iniquity.” <a name="fr_3882"></a><a href="#fn_3882"><font size="1">3883</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p40.3"></a>Chap. v.—this world should be despised.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p41"></a>Wherefore, brethren, leaving <span><a name="x.iii-p41.1"></a>willingly</span> our sojourn in this present world, let us do the will of Him that called us, and not fear to depart out of this world. For the Lord saith, “Ye shall be as lambs in the midst of wolves.” <a name="fr_3883"></a><a href="#fn_3883"><font size="1">3884</font></a>
  And <a name="x.iii-p42.2"></a>Peter answered and said unto Him, <a name="fr_3884"></a><a href="#fn_3884"><font size="1">3885</font></a>
  “What, then, if the wolves shall tear in pieces the lambs?” <a name="x.iii-p43.1"></a>Jesus said unto <a name="x.iii-p43.2"></a>Peter, “The lambs have no cause after they are dead to fear <a name="fr_3885"></a><a href="#fn_3885"><font size="1">3886</font></a>
  the wolves; and in like manner, fear not ye them that kill you, and can do nothing more unto you; but fear Him who, after you are dead, has power over both soul and body to cast them into hell-fire.” <a name="fr_3886"></a><a href="#fn_3886"><font size="1">3887</font></a>
  And consider, <a name="fr_3887"></a><a href="#fn_3887"><font size="1">3888</font></a>
  brethren, that the sojourning in the flesh in this world is but brief and transient, but the promise of Christ is great and wonderful, even the rest of the kingdom to come, and of life everlasting. <a name="fr_3888"></a><a href="#fn_3888"><font size="1">3889</font></a>
  By what course of conduct, then, shall we attain these things, but by leading a holy and righteous life, and by deeming these worldly things as not belonging to us, and not fixing our desires upon them? For if we desire to possess them, we fall away from the path of righteousness. <a name="fr_3889"></a><a href="#fn_3889"><font size="1">3890</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p48.2"></a>Chap. vi.—the present and future worlds are enemies to each other.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p49"></a>Now the Lord declares, “No servant can serve two masters.” <a name="fr_3890"></a><a href="#fn_3890"><font size="1">3891</font></a>
  If we desire, then, to Serve both God and mammon, it will be unprofitable for us. “For what will it profit if a man gain the whole world, and lose his own soul?” <a name="fr_3891"></a><a href="#fn_3891"><font size="1">3892</font></a>
  This world and the next are two enemies. The one urges to <a name="fr_3892"></a><a href="#fn_3892"><font size="1">3893</font></a>
  adultery and corruption, avarice and deceit; the other bids farewell to these things. We cannot therefore be the friends of both; and it behoves us, by renouncing the one, to make sure <a name="fr_3893"></a><a href="#fn_3893"><font size="1">3894</font></a>
  of the other. Let us reckon <a name="fr_3894"></a><a href="#fn_3894"><font size="1">3895</font></a>
  that it is better to hate the things present, since they are trifling, and transient, and corruptible; and to love those <span><a name="x.iii-p54.3"></a>which are to come</span>, as being good and incorruptible. For if we do the will of Christ, we shall find rest; otherwise, nothing shall deliver us from eternal punishment, if we disobey His commandments. For thus also saith the Scripture in Ezekiel, “If <a name="x.iii-p54.4"></a>Noah, <a name="x.iii-p54.5"></a>Job, and <a name="x.iii-p54.6"></a>Daniel should rise up, they should not deliver their children in captivity.” <a name="fr_3895"></a><a href="#fn_3895"><font size="1">3896</font></a>
  Now, if men <a name="page_519"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 519</font></a> <a name="x.iii-Page_519"></a>so eminently righteous <a name="fr_3896"></a><a href="#fn_3896"><font size="1">3897</font></a>
  are not able by their righteousness to deliver their children, how can we hope to <a name="fr_3897"></a><a href="#fn_3897"><font size="1">3898</font></a>
  enter into the royal residence <a name="fr_3898"></a><a href="#fn_3898"><font size="1">3899</font></a>
  of God unless we keep our baptism holy and undefiled? Or who shall be our advocate, unless we be found possessed of works of holiness and righteousness? <a name="fr_3899"></a><a href="#fn_3899"><font size="1">3900</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p59.1"></a>Chap. vii.—we must strive in order to be crowned.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p60"></a>Wherefore, then, my brethren, let us struggle <a name="fr_3900"></a><a href="#fn_3900"><font size="1">3901</font></a>
  with all earnestness, knowing that the contest is <span><a name="x.iii-p61.2"></a>in our case</span> close at hand, and that many undertake long voyages to strive for a corruptible reward; <a name="fr_3901"></a><a href="#fn_3901"><font size="1">3902</font></a>
  yet all are not crowned, but those only that have laboured hard and striven gloriously. Let us therefore so strive, that we may all be crowned. Let us run the straight <a name="fr_3902"></a><a href="#fn_3902"><font size="1">3903</font></a>
  course, even the race that is incorruptible; and let us in great numbers set out <a name="fr_3903"></a><a href="#fn_3903"><font size="1">3904</font></a>
  for it, and strive that we may be crowned. And should we not all be able to obtain the crown, let us at least come near to it. We must remember <a name="fr_3904"></a><a href="#fn_3904"><font size="1">3905</font></a>
  that he who strives in the corruptible contest, if he be found acting unfairly, <a name="fr_3905"></a><a href="#fn_3905"><font size="1">3906</font></a>
  is taken away and scourged, and cast forth from the lists. What then think ye? If one does anything unseemly in the incorruptible contest, what shall <span><a name="x.iii-p66.1"></a>he</span> have to bear? For of those who do not preserve the seal <a name="fr_3906"></a><a href="#fn_3906"><font size="1">3907</font></a>
  <span><a name="x.iii-p67.3"></a>unbroken, the Scripture</span> saith, <a name="fr_3907"></a><a href="#fn_3907"><font size="1">3908</font></a>
  “Their worm shall not die, and their fire shall not be quenched, and they shall be a spectacle to all flesh.” <a name="fr_3908"></a><a href="#fn_3908"><font size="1">3909</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p69.2"></a>Chap. viii.—the necessity of repentance while we are on earth.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p70"></a>As long, therefore, as we are upon earth, let us practice repentance, for we are as clay in the hand of the artificer. For as the potter, if he make a vessel, and it be distorted or broken in his hands, fashions it over again; but if he have before this cast it into the furnace of fire, can no longer find any help for it: so let us also, while we are in this world, repent with our whole heart of the evil deeds we have done in the flesh, that we may be saved by the Lord, while we have yet an opportunity of repentance. For after we have gone out of the world, no further power of confessing or repenting will there belong to us. Wherefore, brethren, by doing the will of the Father, and keeping the flesh holy, and observing the commandments of the Lord, we shall obtain eternal life. For the Lord saith in the Gospel, “If ye have not kept that which was small, who will commit to you the great? For I say unto you, that he that is faithful in that which is least, is faithful also in much.” <a name="fr_3909"></a><a href="#fn_3909"><font size="1">3910</font></a>
  This, then, is what He means: “Keep the flesh holy and the seal undefiled, that ye <a name="fr_3910"></a><a href="#fn_3910"><font size="1">3911</font></a>
  may receive eternal life.” <a name="fr_3911"></a><a href="#fn_3911"><font size="1">3912</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p73.1"></a>Chap. ix.—we shall be judged in the flesh.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p74"></a>And let no one of you say that this very flesh shall not be judged, nor rise again. Consider ye <a name="fr_3912"></a><a href="#fn_3912"><font size="1">3913</font></a>
  in what <span><a name="x.iii-p75.5"></a>state</span> ye were saved, in what ye received sight, <a name="fr_3913"></a><a href="#fn_3913"><font size="1">3914</font></a>
  if not while ye were in this flesh. We must therefore preserve the flesh as the temple of God. For as ye were called in the flesh, ye shall also come <span><a name="x.iii-p76.2"></a>to be judged</span> in the flesh. As Christ <a name="fr_3914"></a><a href="#fn_3914"><font size="1">3915</font></a>
  the Lord who saved us, though He was first a Spirit, <a name="fr_3915"></a><a href="#fn_3915"><font size="1">3916</font></a>
  became flesh, and thus called us, so shall we also receive the reward in this flesh. Let us therefore love one another, that we may all attain to the kingdom of God. While we have an opportunity of being healed, let us yield ourselves to God that healeth us, and give to Him a recompense. Of what sort? Repentance out of a sincere heart; for He knows all things beforehand, and is acquainted with what is in our hearts. Let us therefore give Him praise, <a name="fr_3916"></a><a href="#fn_3916"><font size="1">3917</font></a>
  not with the mouth only, but also with the heart, that He may accept us as sons. For the Lord has said, “Those are My brethren who do the will of My Father.” <a name="fr_3917"></a><a href="#fn_3917"><font size="1">3918</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p80.2"></a>Chap. x.—vice is to be forsaken, and virtue followed.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p81"></a>Wherefore, my brethren, let us do the will of the Father who called us, that we may live; and let us earnestly <a name="fr_3918"></a><a href="#fn_3918"><font size="1">3919</font></a>
  follow after virtue, but forsake <a name="page_520"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 520</font></a> <a name="x.iii-Page_520"></a>every wicked tendency <a name="fr_3919"></a><a href="#fn_3919"><font size="1">3920</font></a>
  which would lead into transgression; and flee from ungodliness, lest evils overtake us. For if we are diligent in doing good, peace will follow us. On this account, such men cannot find it, i.e., <span><a name="x.iii-p83.1"></a>peace</span>, as are <a name="fr_3920"></a><a href="#fn_3920"><font size="1">3921</font></a>
  influenced by human terrors, and prefer rather present enjoyment to the promise which shall afterwards be fulfilled. For they know not what torment present enjoyment incurs, or what felicity is involved in the future promise. And if, indeed, they themselves only did such things, it would be <span><a name="x.iii-p84.19"></a>the more</span> tolerable; but now they persist in imbuing innocent souls with their pernicious doctrines, <a name="fr_3921"></a><a href="#fn_3921"><font size="1">3922</font></a>
  not knowing that they shall receive a double condemnation, both they and those that hear them.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p85.2"></a>Chap. xi.—we ought to serve god, trusting in his promises.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p86"></a>Let us therefore serve God with a pure heart, and we shall be righteous; but if we do not serve Him, because we believe not the promise of God, we shall be miserable. For the prophetic word also declares, “Wretched are those of a double mind, and who doubt in their heart, who say, All these things have we heard even in the times of our fathers; but though we have waited day by day, we have seen none of them <span><a name="x.iii-p86.1"></a>accomplished</span>. Ye fools! compare yourselves to a tree; take, for instance, the vine. First of all it sheds its leaves, then the bud appears; after that the sour grape, and then the fully-ripened fruit. So, likewise, my people have borne disturbances and afflictions, but afterwards shall they receive their good things.” <a name="fr_3922"></a><a href="#fn_3922"><font size="1">3923</font></a>
  Wherefore, my brethren, let us not be of a double mind, but let us hope and endure, that we also may obtain the reward. For He is faithful who has promised that He will bestow on every one a reward according to his works. If, therefore, we shall do righteousness in the sight of God, we shall enter into His kingdom, and shall receive the promises, “which ear hath not heard, nor eye seen, neither have entered into the heart of man.” <a name="fr_3923"></a><a href="#fn_3923"><font size="1">3924</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p88.2"></a>Chap. xii.—we are constantly to look for the kingdom of god.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p89"></a>Let us expect, therefore, hour by hour, the kingdom of God in love and righteousness, since we know not the day of the appearing of God. For the Lord Himself, being asked by one when His kingdom would come, replied, “When two shall be one, and that which is without as that which is within, and the male with the female, neither male nor female.” <a name="fr_3924"></a><a href="#fn_3924"><font size="1">3925</font></a>
  Now, two are one when we speak the truth one to another, and there is unfeignedly one soul in two bodies. And “that which is without as that which is within” meaneth this: He calls the soul “that which is within,” and the body “that which is without.” As, then, thy body is visible to sight, so also let thy soul be manifest by good works. And “the male with the female, neither male nor female,” this <a name="fr_3925"></a><a href="#fn_3925"><font size="1">3926</font></a>
  …  </p>

 —  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p95"></a>  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p96"></a>… meaneth, <a name="fr_3927"></a><a href="#fn_3927"><font size="1">3928</font></a>
  that a brother seeing a sister should think nothing <a name="fr_3928"></a><a href="#fn_3928"><font size="1">3929</font></a>
  about her as of a female, nor <span><a name="x.iii-p98.9"></a>she</span> <a name="fr_3929"></a><a href="#fn_3929"><font size="1">3930</font></a>
  think anything about him as of a male. If ye do these things, saith He, <a name="fr_3930"></a><a href="#fn_3930"><font size="1">3931</font></a>
  the kingdom of my Father shall come.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p100.1"></a>Chap. xiii.—disobedience causeth gods name to be blasphemed. <a name="fr_3931"></a><a href="#fn_3931"><font size="1">3932</font></a>
  </h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p102"></a>Therefore, brethren, <a name="fr_3932"></a><a href="#fn_3932"><font size="1">3933</font></a>
  let us now at length repent; let us be sober unto what is good; for we are full of much folly and wickedness. Let us blot out from us our former sins, and repenting from the soul let us be saved; and let us not become <a name="fr_3933"></a><a href="#fn_3933"><font size="1">3934</font></a>
  men-pleasers, nor let us desire to please only one another, <a name="fr_3934"></a><a href="#fn_3934"><font size="1">3935</font></a>
  but also the men that are without, by our righteousness, that the Name <a name="fr_3935"></a><a href="#fn_3935"><font size="1">3936</font></a>
  <a name="page_521"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 521</font></a> <a name="x.iii-Page_521"></a>be not blasphemed on account of us. <a name="fr_3936"></a><a href="#fn_3936"><font size="1">3937</font></a>
  For the Lord also saith “Continually <a name="fr_3937"></a><a href="#fn_3937"><font size="1">3938</font></a>
  My name is blasphemed among all the Gentiles,” <a name="fr_3938"></a><a href="#fn_3938"><font size="1">3939</font></a>
  and again, “Woe <a name="fr_3939"></a><a href="#fn_3939"><font size="1">3940</font></a>
  to him on account of whom My name is blasphemed.” Wherein is it blasphemed? In your not doing what I desire. <a name="fr_3940"></a><a href="#fn_3940"><font size="1">3941</font></a>
  For the Gentiles, when they hear from our mouth the oracles of God, <a name="fr_3941"></a><a href="#fn_3941"><font size="1">3942</font></a>
  marvel at them as beautiful and great; afterwards, when they have learned that our works are not worthy of the words we speak, they then turn themselves to blasphemy, saying that it is some fable and delusion. For when they hear from us that God saith, <a name="fr_3942"></a><a href="#fn_3942"><font size="1">3943</font></a>
  “There is no thank unto you, if ye love them that love you; but there is thank unto you, if ye love your enemies and them that hate you;” <a name="fr_3943"></a><a href="#fn_3943"><font size="1">3944</font></a>
  when they hear these things, they marvel at the excellency of the goodness; but when they see that we not only do not love them that hate us, but not even them that love us, they laugh us to scorn, and the Name is blasphemed.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p114.4"></a>Chap. xiv.—the living church is the body of christ.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p115"></a>Wherefore, <a name="fr_3944"></a><a href="#fn_3944"><font size="1">3945</font></a>
  brethren, if we do the will of God our father, we shall be of the first Church, that is, spiritual, that hath been created before the sun and moon; <a name="fr_3945"></a><a href="#fn_3945"><font size="1">3946</font></a>
  but if we do not the will of the Lord, we shall be of the scripture that saith, “My house was made a den of robbers.” <a name="fr_3946"></a><a href="#fn_3946"><font size="1">3947</font></a>
  So then let us choose to be of the Church of life, <a name="fr_3947"></a><a href="#fn_3947"><font size="1">3948</font></a>
  that we may be saved. I do not, however, suppose ye are ignorant that the living Church is the body of Christ; <a name="fr_3948"></a><a href="#fn_3948"><font size="1">3949</font></a>
  for the scripture saith, “God made man, male and female.” <a name="fr_3949"></a><a href="#fn_3949"><font size="1">3950</font></a>
  the male is Christ, the female is the Church. And the Books <a name="fr_3950"></a><a href="#fn_3950"><font size="1">3951</font></a>
  and the Apostles <span><a name="x.iii-p122.1"></a>plainly declare</span> <a name="fr_3951"></a><a href="#fn_3951"><font size="1">3952</font></a>
  that the Church is not of the present, but from the beginning. <a name="fr_3952"></a><a href="#fn_3952"><font size="1">3953</font></a>
  For she was spiritual, as our <a name="x.iii-p124.2"></a>Jesus also was, but was manifested in the last days that He <a name="fr_3953"></a><a href="#fn_3953"><font size="1">3954</font></a>
  might save us. Now the Church, being spiritual, was manifested in the flesh of Christ, <span><a name="x.iii-p125.1"></a>thus</span> signifying to us that, if any of us keep <a name="fr_3954"></a><a href="#fn_3954"><font size="1">3955</font></a>
  her in the flesh and do not corrupt her, he shall receive her again <a name="fr_3955"></a><a href="#fn_3955"><font size="1">3956</font></a>
  in the Holy Spirit: for this flesh is the copy of the spirit. No one then who corrupts the copy, shall partake of the original. <a name="fr_3956"></a><a href="#fn_3956"><font size="1">3957</font></a>
  This then is what He meaneth, “Keep the flesh, <a name="fr_3957"></a><a href="#fn_3957"><font size="1">3958</font></a>
  that ye may partake of the spirit.” But if we say that the flesh is the church and the spirit Christ, <a name="fr_3958"></a><a href="#fn_3958"><font size="1">3959</font></a>
  then he that hath shamefully used the flesh hath shamefully used the Church. Such a one then shall not partake of the spirit, which is Christ. Such life and incorruption this flesh <a name="fr_3959"></a><a href="#fn_3959"><font size="1">3960</font></a>
  can partake of, when the Holy Spirit is joined to it. No one can utter or speak “what the lord hath prepared” for his elect. <a name="fr_3960"></a><a href="#fn_3960"><font size="1">3961</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p132.2"></a>Chap. xv.—faith and love the proper return to god.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p133"></a>Now I do not think I have given you any light counsel concerning self-control, <a name="fr_3961"></a><a href="#fn_3961"><font size="1">3962</font></a>
  which if any one do he will not repent of it, but will save both himself and me who counselled him. For it is no light reward to turn again a wandering and perishing soul that it may be saved. <a name="fr_3962"></a><a href="#fn_3962"><font size="1">3963</font></a>
  For this is the recompense <a name="fr_3963"></a><a href="#fn_3963"><font size="1">3964</font></a>
  we have to return to God who created us, if he that speaketh and heareth both speaketh and heareth with faith and love. Let us therefore abide in the things which we believed, righteous and holy, that with boldness we may ask of God who saith, “While thou art yet speaking, I will say, Lo, I am here.” <a name="fr_3964"></a><a href="#fn_3964"><font size="1">3965</font></a>
  For this saying is the sign of a great promise; for the Lord saith of Himself that He is more ready to give than he that asketh <span><a name="x.iii-p137.2"></a>to ask</span>. <a name="fr_3965"></a><a href="#fn_3965"><font size="1">3966</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3966"></a><a href="#fn_3966"><font size="1">3967</font></a>
  Being <a name="page_522"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 522</font></a> <a name="x.iii-Page_522"></a>therefore partakers of so great kindness, let us not be envious of one another <a name="fr_3967"></a><a href="#fn_3967"><font size="1">3968</font></a>
  in the obtaining of so many good things. For as great as is the pleasure which these sayings have for them that have done them, so great is the condemnation they have for them that have been disobedient.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p139.2"></a>Chap. xvi.—the excellence of almsgiving.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p140"></a>Wherefore, brethren, having received no small occasion <a name="fr_3968"></a><a href="#fn_3968"><font size="1">3969</font></a>
  for repentance, while we have the opportunity, <a name="fr_3969"></a><a href="#fn_3969"><font size="1">3970</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3970"></a><a href="#fn_3970"><font size="1">3971</font></a>
  let us turn unto God that called us, while we still have Him as One that receiveth us. For if we renounce <a name="fr_3971"></a><a href="#fn_3971"><font size="1">3972</font></a>
  these enjoyments and conquer our soul in not doing these its evil desires, we shall partake of the mercy of <a name="x.iii-p143.2"></a>Jesus. But ye know that the day of judgment even now “cometh as a burning oven,” <a name="fr_3972"></a><a href="#fn_3972"><font size="1">3973</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3973"></a><a href="#fn_3973"><font size="1">3974</font></a>
  and some “of the heavens shall melt,” and all the earth <span><a name="x.iii-p144.2"></a>shall be</span> as lead melting on the fire, <a name="fr_3974"></a><a href="#fn_3974"><font size="1">3975</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3975"></a><a href="#fn_3975"><font size="1">3976</font></a>
  and then the hidden and open works of men shall appear. Almsgiving therefore is a good thing, as repentance from sin; fasting is better than prayer, but almsgiving than both; <a name="fr_3976"></a><a href="#fn_3976"><font size="1">3977</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3977"></a><a href="#fn_3977"><font size="1">3978</font></a>
  “but love covereth a multitude of sins.” <a name="fr_3978"></a><a href="#fn_3978"><font size="1">3979</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3979"></a><a href="#fn_3979"><font size="1">3980</font></a>
  But prayer out of a good conscience delivereth from death. Blessed is every one that is found full of these; for alms-giving lighteneth the burden of sin. <a name="fr_3980"></a><a href="#fn_3980"><font size="1">3981</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3981"></a><a href="#fn_3981"><font size="1">3982</font></a>
    </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p148.3"></a>Chap. xvii.—the danger of impenitence.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p149"></a>Let us therefore repent from the whole heart, that no one of us perish by the way. For if we have commandments that we should also practice this, <a name="fr_3982"></a><a href="#fn_3982"><font size="1">3983</font></a>
  to draw away men from idols and instruct them, how much more ought a soul already knowing God not to perish! Let us therefore assist one another that we may also lead up those weak as to what is good, <a name="fr_3983"></a><a href="#fn_3983"><font size="1">3984</font></a>
  in order that all may be saved; and let us convert and admonish one another. <a name="fr_3984"></a><a href="#fn_3984"><font size="1">3985</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3985"></a><a href="#fn_3985"><font size="1">3986</font></a>
 And let us not think to give heed and believe now only, while we are admonished by the presbyters, but also when we have returned home, <a name="fr_3986"></a><a href="#fn_3986"><font size="1">3987</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3987"></a><a href="#fn_3987"><font size="1">3988</font></a>
 remembering the commandments <a name="fr_3988"></a><a href="#fn_3988"><font size="1">3989</font></a>
  of the Lord; and let us not be dragged away by worldly lusts, but coming <a name="fr_3989"></a><a href="#fn_3989"><font size="1">3990</font></a>
  more frequently let us attempt to make advances in the commandments of the Lord, that all being of the same mind <a name="fr_3990"></a><a href="#fn_3990"><font size="1">3991</font></a>
  we may be gathered together unto life. For the Lord said, “I come to gather together all the nations, tribes, and tongues.” <a name="fr_3991"></a><a href="#fn_3991"><font size="1">3992</font></a>
  This He speaketh of the day of His appearing, when He shall come and redeem us, each one according to his works. <a name="fr_3992"></a><a href="#fn_3992"><font size="1">3993</font></a>
  And the unbelievers “shall see His glory,” and strength; and they shall think it strange when they see the sovereignty <a name="fr_3993"></a><a href="#fn_3993"><font size="1">3994</font></a>
  of the world in <a name="x.iii-p159.7"></a>Jesus, saying, Woe unto us, Thou wast <span><a name="x.iii-p159.8"></a>He</span>, <a name="fr_3994"></a><a href="#fn_3994"><font size="1">3995</font></a>
  and we did not know and did not believe, and we did not obey the presbyters when they declared unto us concerning our salvation. And “their worm dieth not, and their fire is not quenched, and they shall be for a spectacle unto all flesh.” <a name="fr_3995"></a><a href="#fn_3995"><font size="1">3996</font></a>
  He speaketh of that day of judgment, when they shall see those among us <a name="fr_3996"></a><a href="#fn_3996"><font size="1">3997</font></a>
  that have been ungodly and acted deceitfully with the commandments of <a name="x.iii-p162.3"></a>Jesus Christ. But the righteous who have done well and endured torments and hated the enjoyments of the soul, when they shall behold those that have gone astray and denied <a name="x.iii-p162.4"></a>Jesus through their words or through their works, how that they are punished with grievous torments in unquenchable fire, shall be giving glory to God, saying, There will be hope for him that hath served God with his whole heart.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p162.5"></a>Chap. xviii.—the preacher confesseth his own sinfulness.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p163"></a>Let us also become of the number of them that give thanks, that have served God, and not of the ungodly that are judged. For I myself also, being an utter sinner, <a name="fr_3997"></a><a href="#fn_3997"><font size="1">3998</font></a>
  <a name="fr_3998"></a><a href="#fn_3998"><font size="1">3999</font></a>
  and not yet escaped from temptation, but still being in the midst of the engines <a name="fr_3999"></a><a href="#fn_3999"><font size="1">4000</font></a>
  of the devil, give diligence to follow <a name="page_523"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 523</font></a> <a name="x.iii-Page_523"></a>after righteousness, that I may have strength to come even near it, <a name="fr_4000"></a><a href="#fn_4000"><font size="1">4001</font></a>
  fearing the judgment to come.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p166.2"></a>Chap. xix.—he justifieth his exhortation.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p167"></a>Wherefore, brethren and sisters, <a name="fr_4001"></a><a href="#fn_4001"><font size="1">4002</font></a>
  after the God of truth <span><a name="x.iii-p168.4"></a>hath been heard</span>, <a name="fr_4002"></a><a href="#fn_4002"><font size="1">4003</font></a>
  I read to you an entreaty <a name="fr_4003"></a><a href="#fn_4003"><font size="1">4004</font></a>
  that ye may give heed to the things that are written, in order that ye may save both yourselves and him that readeth among you. For as a reward I ask of you that ye repent with the whole heart, thus giving to yourselves salvation and life. For by doing this we shall set a goal <a name="fr_4004"></a><a href="#fn_4004"><font size="1">4005</font></a>
  for all the young who are minded to labour <a name="fr_4005"></a><a href="#fn_4005"><font size="1">4006</font></a>
  on behalf of piety and the goodness of God. And let us not, unwise ones that we are, be affronted and sore displeased, whenever some one admonisheth and turneth us from iniquity unto righteousness. For sometimes while we are practising evil things we do not perceive it on account of the double-mindedness and unbelief that is in our breasts, and we are “darkened in our understanding” <a name="fr_4006"></a><a href="#fn_4006"><font size="1">4007</font></a>
  by our vain lusts. Let us then practice righteousness that we may be saved unto the end. Blessed are they that obey these ordinances. Even if for a little time they suffer evil in the world, <a name="fr_4007"></a><a href="#fn_4007"><font size="1">4008</font></a>
  they shall enjoy the immortal fruit of the resurrection. Let not then the godly man be grieved, if he be wretched in the times that now are; a blessed time waits for him. He, living again above with the fathers, shall be joyful for an eternity without grief.  </p>  <h5 align="center"><a name="x.iii-p174.8"></a>Chap. xx.—concluding word of consolation. doxology.</h5>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p175"></a>But neither let it trouble your understanding, that we see the unrighteous having riches and the servants of God straitened. Let us therefore, brethren and sisters, be believing: we are striving in the contest <a name="fr_4008"></a><a href="#fn_4008"><font size="1">4009</font></a>
  of the living God, we are exercised by the present life, in order that we may be crowned by that to come. No one of the righteous received fruit speedily, but awaiteth it. For if God gave shortly the recompense of the righteous, straightway we would be exercising ourselves in business, not in godliness; for we would seem to be righteous, while pursuing not what is godly but what is gainful. And on this account Divine judgment surprised a spirit that was not righteous, and loaded it with chains. <a name="fr_4009"></a><a href="#fn_4009"><font size="1">4010</font></a>
   </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="x.iii-p178"></a>To the only God invisible, <a name="fr_4010"></a><a href="#fn_4010"><font size="1">4011</font></a>
  the Father of truth, who sent forth to us the Saviour and Prince of incorruption, <a name="fr_4011"></a><a href="#fn_4011"><font size="1">4012</font></a>
  through whom also He manifested to us the truth and the heavenly life, to Him be the glory for ever and ever. Amen. <a name="fr_4012"></a><a href="#fn_4012"><font size="1">4013</font></a>

 <a name="fn_4002"></a><a href="0070476.htm#fr_4002">523:4003</a> <a name="x.iii-p168.5"></a>  
 <p class="Footnote"><a name="x.iii-p169"></a> If any doubt remained as to the character of this writing, it would be removed by this sentence. The passage is elliptical, <span class="Greek"><a name="x.iii-p169.1"></a>μετὰ τὸν θεὸν τῆς ἀλνθείας</span>, but there is no doubt as to the meaning. The Scripture was read, and listening to it was regarded as hearing the voice of God, whose words of truth were read. Then followed the sermon or exhortation; comp. <a name="x.iii-p169.2"></a>Justin, <a name="x.iii-p169.3"></a>First Apology, chap. lxvii. (vol. i. p. 186). That lessons from some at least of the New Testament were included at the date of this homily, seems quite certain; comp. the references to the New Testament in chaps. ii., iii., iv., and elsewhere. It is here implied that this homily was written and “read.”  </p>

 <p class="Footnote"><a name="x.iii-p177"></a> The verbs here are aorists, and have been rendered by the English past tense; the present participle (<span class="Greek"><a name="x.iii-p177.1"></a>μὴ ὂν δίκαιον</span>) describing the character of the “spirit” must, according to English usage, conform to the main verbs. <a name="x.iii-p177.2"></a>Lightfoot says, “The aorist here has its common <span><a name="x.iii-p177.3"></a>gnomic</span> sense;” and he therefore interprets the passage as a general statement: “Sordid motives bring their own punishment in a judicial blindness.” But this gnomic sense of the aorist is not common. If the aorist is taken in its usual sense, this is the preferable explanation; but the meaning is obscure.  </p>
