 </p><p class="BodyText"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p1"></a><a name="page_382"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 382</font></a> <a name="viii.iii.xvi-Page_382"></a>   </p>  <h4 align="center"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVI. <a name="fr_2502"></a><a href="#fn_2502"><font size="1">2503</font></a>
 —Watchfulness; The Coming of the Lord.</h4>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p3"></a>1. Watch for your lifes sake. <a name="fr_2503"></a><a href="#fn_2503"><font size="1">2504</font></a>
  Let not your lamps be quenched, nor your loins unloosed; <a name="fr_2504"></a><a href="#fn_2504"><font size="1">2505</font></a>
  but be ye ready, for ye know not the hour in which our Lord cometh. <a name="fr_2505"></a><a href="#fn_2505"><font size="1">2506</font></a>
  2. But often shall ye come together, seeking the things which are befitting to your souls: for the whole time of your faith will not profit you, <a name="fr_2506"></a><a href="#fn_2506"><font size="1">2507</font></a>
  if ye be not made perfect in the last time. 3. For in the last days <a name="fr_2507"></a><a href="#fn_2507"><font size="1">2508</font></a>
  false prophets and corrupters shall be multiplied, and the sheep shall be turned into wolves, and love shall be turned into hate; <a name="fr_2508"></a><a href="#fn_2508"><font size="1">2509</font></a>
  4. for when lawlessness increaseth, they shall hate and persecute and betray one another, <a name="fr_2509"></a><a href="#fn_2509"><font size="1">2510</font></a>
  and then shall appear the world-deceiver <a name="fr_2510"></a><a href="#fn_2510"><font size="1">2511</font></a>
  as the Son of God, <a name="fr_2511"></a><a href="#fn_2511"><font size="1">2512</font></a>
  and shall do signs and wonders, <a name="fr_2512"></a><a href="#fn_2512"><font size="1">2513</font></a>
  and the earth shall be delivered into his hands, and he shall do iniquitous things which have never yet come to pass since the beginning. 5. Then shall the creation of men come into the fire of trial, <a name="fr_2513"></a><a href="#fn_2513"><font size="1">2514</font></a>
  and many shall be made to stumble and shall perish; but they that endure in their faith shall be saved <a name="fr_2514"></a><a href="#fn_2514"><font size="1">2515</font></a>
  from under the curse itself. <a name="fr_2515"></a><a href="#fn_2515"><font size="1">2516</font></a>
  6. And then shall appear the signs of the truth; <a name="fr_2516"></a><a href="#fn_2516"><font size="1">2517</font></a>
  first, the sign of an out-spreading <a name="fr_2517"></a><a href="#fn_2517"><font size="1">2518</font></a>
  in heaven; then the sign of the sound of the trumpet; and the third, the resurrection of the dead; 7. yet not of all, but as it is said: The Lord shall come and all His saints with Him. <a name="fr_2518"></a><a href="#fn_2518"><font size="1">2519</font></a>
  8. Then shall the world see the Lord coming upon the clouds of heaven. <a name="fr_2519"></a><a href="#fn_2519"><font size="1">2520</font></a>
    </p>

 <a name="fn_2515"></a><a href="0070424.htm#fr_2515">382:2516</a> <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p15.2"></a>

 <a name="fn_2516"></a><a href="0070424.htm#fr_2516">382:2517</a> <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p16.5"></a>  
 <p class="Footnote"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p17"></a> “Truth” might refer to Christ Himself, but the personal advent is spoken of in verse 8; it is better, then, to refer it to the truth respecting the <span><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p17.1"></a>parousia</span> held by the early Christians. For this belief they were mocked, and hence dwelt upon it and the prophecies respecting it. The verse is probably based upon <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030">Matt. 24:30</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#031">31</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p17.2"></a>; but some find here, as in verse 4, an allusion to <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p17.3"></a>Pauls eschatological statements in the Epistles to the Thessalonians.  </p>
 <a name="fn_2517"></a><a href="0070424.htm#fr_2517">382:2518</a> <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p17.4"></a>  
 <p class="Footnote"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18"></a> Professor <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.1"></a>Hall now prefers to render <span class="Greek"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.2"></a>ἐκπετάσεως</span>, “outspreading,” instead of “unrolling,” as in his version originally. <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.3"></a>Hitchcock and <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.4"></a>Brown, <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.5"></a>Schaff, and others, prefer “opening;” that is, the apparent o pening in heaven through which the Lord will descend. “Outspreading” is usually explained (so Professor <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.6"></a>Hall) as meaning the expanded sign of the cross in the heavens, the patristic interpretation of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030">Matt. xxiv. 30</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.7"></a>. <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.8"></a>Bryennios and <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.9"></a>Farrar refer it to the flying forth of the saints to meet the Lord. There are other interpretations based on textual emendations. As the word is very rare, it is difficult to determine the exact sense. “Opening” seems lexically allowable and otherwise free from objection.  </p>
 <a name="fn_2518"></a><a href="0070424.htm#fr_2518">382:2519</a> <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p18.10"></a>  
 <p class="Footnote"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19"></a> <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#005">Zech. xiv. 5</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.1"></a>. This citation is given substantially in <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.2"></a>Apostolic Constitutions. As here used, it seems to point to the first resurrection. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#017">1 Thess. iv. 17</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.3"></a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#023">1 Cor. xv. 23</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.4"></a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#005">Rev. xx. 5</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.5"></a>. Probably it is based upon the Pauline eschatology rather than upon that of the Apocalypse. At all events, there is no allusion to the millennial statement of the latter. Since there was in the early Church, in connection with the expectation of the speedy coming of Christ, a marked tendency to Chiliasm, the silence respecting the millennium may indicate that the writer was not acquainted with the Apocalypse. This inference is allowable, however, only on the assumption of the early date of the <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.6"></a>Teaching.  </p>
 <a name="fn_2519"></a><a href="0070424.htm#fr_2519">382:2520</a> <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p19.7"></a>  
 <p class="Footnote"><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p20"></a> Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030">Matt. xxiv. 30</a><a name="viii.iii.xvi-p20.1"></a>. The conclusion is abrupt, and in <a name="viii.iii.xvi-p20.2"></a>Apostolic Constitutions the New-Testament doctrine of future punishment and reward is added. The absence of all reference to the destruction of Jerusalem would indicate that some time had elapsed since that event. An interval of from thirty to sixty years may well be claimed.   </p>
