 <p> <a name="iii.v.liii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iii.v.liii-p0.1"></a>Elucidation</h3>  
 <p class="Header"><a name="iii.v.liii-p1"></a> (On the tenth of the kalends of April, p. 301.)  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iii.v.liii-p2.1"></a>Serious</span> difficulties are encountered by the learned in reconciling <a name="iii.v.liii-p2.2"></a>Lactantius with himself, if, indeed, the fault be not one of his copyists rather than his own. In the fourth book of the <a name="iii.v.liii-p2.3"></a>Institutes <a name="fr_1998"></a><a href="#fn_1998"><font size="1">1999</font></a>
  his language is thus given by <a name="iii.v.liii-p3.2"></a>Baluzius: <a name="fr_1999"></a><a href="#fn_1999"><font size="1">2000</font></a>
 —  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p5"></a>“<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.1"></a>Extremis temporibus Tiberii Cæsaris, </span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.2"></a>ut scriptum legimus</span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.3"></a>, Dominus noster </span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.4"></a>Jesus Christus<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.5"></a>, a Judæis cruciatus est </span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.6"></a>post diem decimum kalendarum Aprilis</span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p5.7"></a>, duobus Geminis consulibus.</span>”  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p6"></a><a name="iii.v.liii-p6.1"></a>Lactantius was writing in Nicomedia, and may have quoted from memory what he had read, perhaps in the report of Pilate himself. The expression <span class="Foreign"><a name="iii.v.liii-p6.2"></a>post diem decimum kalendarum Aprilis</span> is ambiguous: and Jarvis says, “My impression is, that it means after the tenth day before the kalends of April; that is, after the 23d of March.” <a name="fr_2000"></a><a href="#fn_2000"><font size="1">2001</font></a>
    </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p8"></a>But here our author says, according to the accurate edition of <a name="iii.v.liii-p8.1"></a>Walchius <a name="fr_2001"></a><a href="#fn_2001"><font size="1">2002</font></a>
  (<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p9.3"></a>a.d.</span> 1715),—  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p10"></a>“<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p10.1"></a>Exinde tetrarchas habuerunt usque ad Herodem, qui fuit sub imperio Tiberii Cæsaris: cujus anno quinto decimo, id est duobus Geminis consulibus, </span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p10.2"></a>ante diem septimam Calendarum Aprilium</span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p10.3"></a>, Judæi Christum cruci affixerunt.</span>”  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p11"></a>But here, on the authority of forty manuscripts, <a name="iii.v.liii-p11.1"></a>Du Fresnoy reads, “<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p11.2"></a>ante</span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p11.3"></a> diem decimam,</span>” which he labours to reconcile with “<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p11.4"></a>post</span><span><a name="iii.v.liii-p11.5"></a> diem decimum,</span>” as above. <a name="iii.v.liii-p11.6"></a>Jarvis adheres to the reading <span><a name="iii.v.liii-p11.7"></a>septimam</span>, supported by more than fifty manuscripts, and decides for the 23d of March.  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p12"></a>He cites <a name="iii.v.liii-p12.1"></a>Augustine to the same effect in the noted passage: <a name="fr_2002"></a><a href="#fn_2002"><font size="1">2003</font></a>
 —  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p14"></a>“<span><a name="iii.v.liii-p14.1"></a>Ille autem mense conceptum et passum esse Christum, et Paschæ observatio et dies ecclesiis notissimus Nativitatis ejus ostendit. Qui enim mense nono natus est octavo kalendas Janvarias profecto mense primo conceptus est circa octavum kalendas Aprilis, quod tempus passionis ejus fuit.</span>”  </p>  
 <p class="BodyText"><a name="iii.v.liii-p15"></a>This, <a name="iii.v.liii-p15.1"></a>Augustine considers to be “seething a kid in mothers milk,” after a mystical sense; cruelly making the cross to coincide with the maternity of the Virgin, who beheld her Son an innocent victim on the anniversary of her salutation by the angel.   </p>
