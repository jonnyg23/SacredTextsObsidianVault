 <p> <a name="v.v.vii"></a>
 </p><p class="c10"><a name="v.v.vii-p1"></a>  6. On the other hand, those who say that we must never lie, plead much more strongly, using first the Divine authority, because in the very Decalogue it is written “Thou shall not bear false witness;” <a name="fr_2305"></a><a href="#fn_2305"><font size="1">2306</font></a>
  under which general term it comprises all lying: for whoso utters any thing bears witness to his own mind. But lest any should contend that not every lie is to be called false witness, what will he say to that which is written, “The mouth that lieth slayeth the soul:” <a name="fr_2306"></a><a href="#fn_2306"><font size="1">2307</font></a>
  and lest any should suppose that this may be understood with the exception of some liars, let him read in another place, “Thou wilt destroy all that speak leasing.” <a name="fr_2307"></a><a href="#fn_2307"><font size="1">2308</font></a>
  Whence with His own lips the Lord saith, “Let your communication be yea, yea; nay, nay; for whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil.” <a name="fr_2308"></a><a href="#fn_2308"><font size="1">2309</font></a>
  Hence the Apostle also in giving precept for the putting off of the old man, under which name all sins are understood, says straightway, “Wherefore putting away lying, speak ye truth.” <a name="fr_2309"></a><a href="#fn_2309"><font size="1">2310</font></a>
 </p>
