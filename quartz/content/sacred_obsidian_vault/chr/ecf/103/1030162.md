 </p><p class="c36"><a name="iv.i.xiv-p1"></a>   <span class="c7"><a name="iv.i.xiv-p1.1"></a>Book XII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c33"><a name="iv.i.xiv-p2"></a> ————————————</p>  
 <p class="c38"><a name="iv.i.xiv-p3"></a>Commencing with a distinction between wisdom and knowledge, points out a kind of trinity, of a peculiar sort, in that which is properly called knowledge, and which is the lower of the two; and this trinity, although it certainly pertains to the inner man, is still not yet to be called or thought an image of God.</p>
