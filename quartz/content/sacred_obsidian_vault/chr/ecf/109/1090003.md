 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.i-p3"></a><span class="c18"><a name="iii.i-p3.1"></a>The Life and Work of</span></p>  
 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.i-p4"></a><span class="c20"><a name="iii.i-p4.1"></a>St. John Chrysostom.</span></p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="iii.i-p5"></a><span class="c8"><a name="iii.i-p5.1"></a>By Philip Schaff.</span></p>  
 <p class="c22"><a name="iii.i-p6"></a><span class="c18"><a name="iii.i-p6.1"></a>Chapter I</span><span class="c1"><a name="iii.i-p6.2"></a>.—<i>Literature</i>.</span></p>  
 <p class="c5"><a name="iii.i-p7"></a><span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p7.1"></a>i. editions of chrysostoms works.</span></p>  
 <p class="c9"><a name="iii.i-p8"></a><span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p8.1"></a>S. Joannis Chrysostomi</span>, <i>archiepiscopi Constantinopolitani, Opera omnia quæ exstant vel quæ ejus nomine circumferuntur, ad <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p8.2"></a>mss.</span> codices Gallicos, Vaticanos, Anglicos, Germanicosque castigata, etc. Opera et studio D.</i><span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p8.3"></a>Bernardi de Montfaucon</span>, <i>monachi ordinis S. Benedicti e congregatione S. Mauri, opem ferentibus aliis ex codem sodalitio, monachis</i>. Greek and Latin, Paris, 1718–38, in 13 vols., fol. This is the best edition, and the result of about twenty years of the patient labor of Montfaucon (d. Dec. 21, 1741, 86 years old), and several assistants of the brotherhood of St. Maur. More than three hundred <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p8.4"></a>mss.</span> were made use of, but the eight principal <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p8.5"></a>mss.</span>, as Field has shown, were not very carefully collated. Montfaucon, who at the date of the completion of his edition was 83 years old, prepared valuable prefaces to every treatise and set of homilies, arranged the works in chronological order, and added in vol. XIII. learned dissertations on the life, doctrine, discipline and heresies of the age of Chrysostom.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p9"></a>The Benedictine edition was reprinted at Venice, 1734–41, in 13 vols. fol.; at Paris, ed. by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p9.1"></a>F. de Sinner (Gaume)</span>, 1834–39, in 13 vols. (an elegant edition, with some additions); and, with various improvements and corrections, by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p9.2"></a>J. P. Migne</span>, Petit-Montrouge, 1859–63, in 13 vols. The last is the most complete edition, but inferior in paper and type to that of Gaume. Migne uses the critical text of Field in Matthew and the Pauline Epp. He had previously edited a Latin Version, 1842, in 9 vols.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p10"></a>The edition of Sir <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p10.1"></a>Henry Savile</span> (Provost of Eton), Etonæ, 1612, in 8 vols. fol., is less complete than the Benedictine edition, but gives a more correct Greek text (as was shown by F. Dübner from a collation of manuscripts) and valuable notes. Savile personally examined the libraries of Europe and spent £8,000 on his edition. His wife was so jealous of his devotion to Chrysostom that she threatened to burn his manuscripts.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p11"></a>The edition of <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p11.1"></a>Fronton le Duc</span>, a French Jesuit, and the two brothers, <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p11.2"></a>Frederick</span> and <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p11.3"></a>Claude Morel</span>, was published at Paris, 1636, in 12 vols. fol., Greek and Latin.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p12"></a>A selection of Chrysostoms works (<i>Opera præstantissima</i>) in Greek and Latin, was edited by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p12.1"></a>T. G. Lomler,</span> Rudolphopoli (Rudolstadt), 1840 (unfinished).</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p13"></a>The best edition of the <i>Greek</i> text of the <i>Homilies</i> on <i>Matthew</i>, and all the <i>Pauline Epistles</i> is by Dr. <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p13.1"></a>Frederick Field</span>, of the Church of England (d. 1883), in the “<i>Bibliotheca Patrum Ecclesiæ Orientalis qui ante Orientis et Occidentis schisma floruerunt</i>.” The Homilies on Matthew appeared at Cambridge, 1839, 3 vols.; the Homilies on the Epistles of Paul and the Hebrews, Oxford, 1839–62, in 7 vols.</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p14"></a>The treatise <i>De Sacerdotio</i> (<span class="Greek"><a name="iii.i-p14.1"></a>περί ἱερωσύνης</span>) was separately edited by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p14.2"></a>Erasmus</span> in Greek (Basel, 1525, from the press of Frobenius), by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p14.3"></a>J. Hughes</span>, in Greek and Latin (Cambridge, 1710), and by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p14.4"></a>J. A. Bengel</span>, the com<a name="page_4"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 4</font></a> <a name="iii.i-Page_4"></a>mentator, in Greek (Stuttgart, 1725, and repeatedly reprinted since at Leipzig, 1825, 1834, 1872, by C. Tauchnitz). <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p14.5"></a>Lomler</span> (<i>Chrys. Opera</i>, pp. viii. and ix.) enumerates twenty-three separate editions and translations of the treatise on the Priesthood.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iii.i-p15"></a><span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p15.1"></a>ii. translations</span>.—(<i>a</i>) <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p15.2"></a>german translations.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p16"></a>The treatise on the Priesthood has been translated by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.1"></a>Hasselbach</span>, 1820; <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.2"></a>Ritter</span>, 1821, and others. The <i><span><a name="iii.i-p16.3"></a>Bibliothek der Kirchenväter</span></i> (Rom. Cath.), published at Kempten in Bavaria, devotes ten small volumes to St. Chrysostom, including the <i>Priesthood</i>, ascetic <i> Treatises</i>, and <i>Homilies</i>, translated by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.4"></a>Joh. Chrysostomus Mitterrutzner</span>, 1869–84. German translations of selected <i>Homilies</i> by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.5"></a>J. A. Cramer</span> (Leipzig, 1748–51, 10 vols.); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.6"></a>Feder</span> (Augsburg, 1786); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.7"></a> Ph. Mayer</span> (Nürnberg, 1830); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.8"></a>W. Arnoldi</span> (Trier, 1835); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.9"></a>Augusti</span> (<i>Predigten der Kirchenväter</i>, vols. I. and II., Leipzig, 1839); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.10"></a>Jos. Lutz</span> (Tübingen, 2d ed. 1859); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p16.11"></a>Gust. Leonhardi</span> (Leipzig, 1888, selected sermons and orations, in vol I. of <i>Klassikerbibliothek der Christl. Predigtliteratur</i>).</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iii.i-p17"></a>(<i>b</i>) <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p17.1"></a>english translations.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p18"></a>The work on the <i>Priesthood</i> was translated by <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p18.1"></a>Hollier</span> (London, 1728); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p18.2"></a>Bunce</span> (London, 1759); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p18.3"></a> Hohler</span> (Cambridge, 1837); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p18.4"></a>Marsh</span> (London, 1844); <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p18.5"></a>Harris Cowper</span> (London, 1866); and <span class="c12"><a name="iii.i-p18.6"></a>Stephens</span> (N. York, 1888, prepared for this “Library”).</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="iii.i-p19"></a>The <i>Homilies on the Statues</i> and on the <i>New Testament</i> were translated by several scholars for the “Oxford Library of the Fathers,” 1839–77, 16 vols. The earlier parts (on the First Epistle to the Corinthians, and on the Statues) are based on the text of Montfaucon and Savile, the later parts on the improved text of Field. The Oxford translation has been revised and annotated by American scholars for this “Library,” and new translations of other works of St. Chrysostom have been added, namely, the treatise on the Priesthood, the Exhortation to the fallen Theodore, Letters, Tracts, and Special Homilies (in this first volume).</p>
