 <p> <a name="ii.ii.xxxi"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="ii.ii.xxxi-p0.1"></a>Chapter XXXI.—Let us see by what means we may obtain the divine blessing.</h3>  
 <p><a name="ii.ii.xxxi-p1"></a><a name="ii.ii.xxxi-p1.1"></a>Let us cleave then to His blessing, and consider what are the means <a name="fr_123"></a><a href="#fn_123"><font size="1">124</font></a>
  of possessing it. Let us think <a name="fr_124"></a><a href="#fn_124"><font size="1">125</font></a>
  over the things which have taken place from the beginning. <a name="ii.ii.xxxi-p3.1"></a>For what reason was our father Abraham blessed? was it not because he wrought righteousness and truth through faith? <a name="fr_125"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">126</font></a>
  <a name="ii.ii.xxxi-p4.2"></a>Isaac, with perfect confidence, as if knowing what was to happen, <a name="fr_126"></a><a href="#fn_126"><font size="1">127</font></a>
  cheerfully yielded himself as a sacrifice. <a name="fr_127"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">128</font></a>
  <a name="ii.ii.xxxi-p6.2"></a>Jacob, through reason <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">129</font></a>
  of his brother, went forth with humility from his own land, and came to Laban and served him; and there was given to him the sceptre of the twelve tribes of Israel.</p>
