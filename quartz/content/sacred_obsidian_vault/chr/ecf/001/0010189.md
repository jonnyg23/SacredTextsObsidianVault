 <p> <a name="v.vi.ii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.vi.ii-p0.1"></a>Chapter II.—Maintain union with the bishop.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.vi.ii-p1"></a><a name="v.vi.ii-p1.1"></a><a name="v.vi.ii-p1.2"></a>Wherefore, as children of light and truth, flee from division and wicked  <a name="page_80"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 80</font></a> <a name="v.vi.ii-Page_80"></a>  doctrines; but where the shepherd is, there do ye as sheep follow. For there are many wolves that appear worthy of credit, who, by means of a pernicious pleasure, carry captive <a name="fr_888"></a><a href="#fn_888"><font size="1">889</font></a>
  those that are running towards God; but in your unity they shall have no place.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.vi.ii-p3"></a>Wherefore, as children of light and truth, avoid the dividing of your unity, and the wicked doctrine of the heretics, from whom “a defiling influence has gone forth into all the earth.” <a name="fr_889"></a><a href="#fn_889"><font size="1">890</font></a>
  <a name="v.vi.ii-p4.2"></a>But where the shepherd is, there do ye as sheep follow. For there are many wolves in sheeps clothing, <a name="fr_890"></a><a href="#fn_890"><font size="1">891</font></a>
  who, by means of a pernicious pleasure, carry captive <a name="fr_891"></a><a href="#fn_891"><font size="1">892</font></a>
  those that are running towards God; but in your unity they shall have no place.</p>
