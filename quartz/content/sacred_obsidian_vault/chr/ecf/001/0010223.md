 <p> <a name="v.x"></a>  <a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 99</font></a> <a name="v.x-Page_99"></a>  </p><h2 align="center"><a name="v.x-p0.1"></a>The Epistle of Ignatius to Polycarp <a name="fr_1130"></a><a href="#fn_1130"><font size="1">1131</font></a>
  </h2>    
 <p><a name="v.x-p2"></a><a name="v.x-p2.1"></a><i>Ignatius, who is [also called] Theophorus, to Polycarp, bishop of Smyrna, or rather, who has as his own bishop God the Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ: [wishes] abundance of happiness.</i></p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <a name="fn_1130"></a><a href="0010223.htm#fr_1130">99:1131</a> <a name="v.x-p0.2"></a>
 <p class="endnote"><a name="v.x-p1"></a> The inscription varies in each of the three Syriac <span class="upper"><a name="v.x-p1.1"></a>mss.</span>, being in the first, “The Epistle of my lord Ignatius, the bishop;” in the second, “The Epistle of Ignatius;” and in the third, “The Epistle of Ignatius, bishop of Antioch.”</p>
