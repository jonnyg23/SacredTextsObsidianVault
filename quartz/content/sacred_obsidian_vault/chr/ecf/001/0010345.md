 <p> <a name="vi.ii.ix"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="vi.ii.ix-p0.1"></a>Chapter IX.—The spiritual meaning of circumcision.</h3>  
 <p><a name="vi.ii.ix-p1"></a><a name="vi.ii.ix-p1.1"></a><a name="vi.ii.ix-p1.2"></a>He speaks moreover concerning our ears, how He hath circumcised both them and our heart. The Lord saith in the prophet, “In the hearing of the ear they obeyed me.” <a name="fr_1547"></a><a href="#fn_1547"><font size="1">1548</font></a>
  And again He saith, “By hearing, those shall hear who are afar off; they shall know what I have done.” <a name="fr_1548"></a><a href="#fn_1548"><font size="1">1549</font></a>
  And, “Be ye circumcised in your hearts, saith the Lord.” <a name="fr_1549"></a><a href="#fn_1549"><font size="1">1550</font></a>
  And again He says, “Hear, O Israel, for these things saith the Lord thy God.” <a name="fr_1550"></a><a href="#fn_1550"><font size="1">1551</font></a>
  And once more the Spirit of the Lord proclaims, “Who is he that wishes to live for ever? By hearing let him hear the voice of my servant.” <a name="fr_1551"></a><a href="#fn_1551"><font size="1">1552</font></a>
  And again He saith, “Hear, O heaven, and give ear, O earth, for God <a name="fr_1552"></a><a href="#fn_1552"><font size="1">1553</font></a>
  hath spoken.” <a name="fr_1553"></a><a href="#fn_1553"><font size="1">1554</font></a>
  These are in proof. <a name="fr_1554"></a><a href="#fn_1554"><font size="1">1555</font></a>
  And again He saith, “Hear the word of the Lord, ye rulers of this people.” <a name="fr_1555"></a><a href="#fn_1555"><font size="1">1556</font></a>
  And again He saith, “Hear, ye children, the voice of one crying in the wilderness.” <a name="fr_1556"></a><a href="#fn_1556"><font size="1">1557</font></a>
  Therefore He hath circumcised our ears, that we might hear His word and believe, for the circumcision in which they trusted is abolished. <a name="fr_1557"></a><a href="#fn_1557"><font size="1">1558</font></a>
  For He declared that circumcision was not of the flesh, but they transgressed because an evil angel deluded them. <a name="fr_1558"></a><a href="#fn_1558"><font size="1">1559</font></a>
  He saith to them, “These things saith the Lord your God”—(here <a name="fr_1559"></a><a href="#fn_1559"><font size="1">1560</font></a>
  I find a new <a name="fr_1560"></a><a href="#fn_1560"><font size="1">1561</font></a>
  commandment)—“Sow not among thorns, but circumcise yourselves to the Lord.” <a name="fr_1561"></a><a href="#fn_1561"><font size="1">1562</font></a>
  And why speaks He thus: “Circumcise the stubbornness of your heart, and harden not your neck?” <a name="fr_1562"></a><a href="#fn_1562"><font size="1">1563</font></a>
  And again: “Behold, saith the Lord, all the nations are uncircumcised <a name="fr_1563"></a><a href="#fn_1563"><font size="1">1564</font></a>
  in the flesh, but this people are uncircumcised in heart.” <a name="fr_1564"></a><a href="#fn_1564"><font size="1">1565</font></a>
  But thou wilt say, “Yea, verily the people are circumcised for a seal.” But so also is every Syrian and Arab, and all the priests of idols: are these then also within the bond of His covenant? <a name="fr_1565"></a><a href="#fn_1565"><font size="1">1566</font></a>
  Yea, the Egyptians also practise circumcision. <a name="vi.ii.ix-p20.1"></a>Learn then, my children, concerning all things richly, <a name="fr_1566"></a><a href="#fn_1566"><font size="1">1567</font></a>
  that Abraham, the first who enjoined circumcision, looking forward in spirit to Jesus, practised that rite, having received the mysteries <a name="fr_1567"></a><a href="#fn_1567"><font size="1">1568</font></a>
  of the three letters. For [the Scripture] saith, “And Abraham circumcised  <a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 143</font></a> <a name="vi.ii.ix-Page_143"></a>  ten, and eight, and three hundred men of his household.” <a name="fr_1568"></a><a href="#fn_1568"><font size="1">1569</font></a>
  What, then, was the knowledge given to him in this? Learn the eighteen first, and then the three hundred. <a name="fr_1569"></a><a href="#fn_1569"><font size="1">1570</font></a>
  The ten and the eight are thus denoted—Ten by <span class="Greek"><a name="vi.ii.ix-p24.1"></a>Ι</span>, and Eight by <span class="Greek"><a name="vi.ii.ix-p24.2"></a>Η</span>. <a name="fr_1570"></a><a href="#fn_1570"><font size="1">1571</font></a>
  You have [the initials of the, name of] Jesus. And because <a name="fr_1571"></a><a href="#fn_1571"><font size="1">1572</font></a>
  the cross was to express the grace [of our redemption] by the letter <span class="Greek"><a name="vi.ii.ix-p26.2"></a>Τ</span>, he says also, “Three Hundred.” He signifies, therefore, Jesus by two letters, and the cross by one. He knows this, who has put within us the engrafted <a name="fr_1572"></a><a href="#fn_1572"><font size="1">1573</font></a>
  gift of His doctrine. No one has been admitted by me to a more excellent piece of knowledge <a name="fr_1573"></a><a href="#fn_1573"><font size="1">1574</font></a>
  than this, but I know that ye are worthy.</p>
