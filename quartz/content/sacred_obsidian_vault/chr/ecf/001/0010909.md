 <p> <a name="ix.viii.xxxiv"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.xxxiv-p0.1"></a>XXXIV.</h3>  
 <p><a name="ix.viii.xxxiv-p1"></a><a name="ix.viii.xxxiv-p1.1"></a>“And <a name="fr_4853"></a><a href="#fn_4853"><font size="1">4854</font></a>
  dipped himself,” says [the Scripture], “seven times in Jordan.” <a name="fr_4854"></a><a href="#fn_4854"><font size="1">4855</font></a>
  It was not for nothing that Naaman of old, when suffering from leprosy, was purified upon his being baptized, but [it served] as an indication to us. For as we are lepers in sin, we are made clean, by means of the sacred water and the invocation of the Lord, from our old transgressions; being spiritually regenerated as new-born babes, even as the Lord has declared: “Except a man be born again through water and the Spirit, he shall not enter into the kingdom of heaven.” <a name="fr_4855"></a><a href="#fn_4855"><font size="1">4856</font></a>
 </p>
