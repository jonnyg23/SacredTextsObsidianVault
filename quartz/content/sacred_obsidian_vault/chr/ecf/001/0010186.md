 <p> <a name="v.v.x"></a>  <a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 78</font></a> <a name="v.v.x-Page_78"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.v.x-p0.1"></a>Chapter X.—Conclusion.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.v.x-p1"></a>Now I write these things to you from Smyrna by the Ephesians, who are deservedly most happy. There is also with me, along with many others, Crocus, one dearly beloved by me. <a name="fr_877"></a><a href="#fn_877"><font size="1">878</font></a>
  As to those who have gone before me from Syria to Rome for the glory of God, I believe that you are acquainted with them; to whom, [then,] do ye make known that I am at hand. For they are all worthy, both of God and of you; and it is becoming that you should refresh them in all things. I have written these things unto you, on the day before the ninth of the Kalends of September (that <a name="fr_878"></a><a href="#fn_878"><font size="1">879</font></a>
  is, on the twenty-third day of August). Fare ye well to the end, in the patience of Jesus Christ. Amen.</p>
