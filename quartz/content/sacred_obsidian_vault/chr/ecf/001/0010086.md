 <p> <a name="iv.ii.iv"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="iv.ii.iv-p0.1"></a>Chapter IV.—Various exhortations.</h3>  
 <p><a name="iv.ii.iv-p1"></a>“But the love of money is the root of all evils.” <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">360</font></a>
  Knowing, therefore, that “as we brought nothing into the world, so we can carry nothing out,” <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">361</font></a>
  let us arm ourselves with the armour of righteousness; <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">362</font></a>
  and let us teach, first of all, ourselves to walk in the commandments of the Lord. <a name="iv.ii.iv-p4.2"></a><a name="iv.ii.iv-p4.3"></a><a name="iv.ii.iv-p4.4"></a><a name="iv.ii.iv-p4.5"></a>Next, [teach] your wives [to walk] in the faith given to them, and in love and purity tenderly loving their own husbands in all truth, and loving all [others] equally in all chastity; and to train up their children in the knowledge and fear of God. <a name="iv.ii.iv-p4.6"></a>Teach the widows to be discreet as respects the faith of the Lord, praying continually <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">363</font></a>
  for all, being far from all slandering, evil-speaking, false-witnessing, love of money, and every kind of evil; knowing that they are the altar <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">364</font></a>
  of God, that He clearly perceives all things, and that nothing is hid from Him, neither reasonings, nor reflections, nor any one of the secret things of the heart.</p>
