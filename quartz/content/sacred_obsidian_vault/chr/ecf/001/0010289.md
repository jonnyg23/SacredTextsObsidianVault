 <p> <a name="v.xvii.i"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.xvii.i-p0.1"></a>Chapter I.—Reason for writing the epistle.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.xvii.i-p1"></a><span class="upper"><a name="v.xvii.i-p1.1"></a>Being</span> mindful of your love and of your zeal in Christ, which ye have manifested towards us, we thought it fitting to write to you, who display such a godly and spiritual love to the brethren, <a name="fr_1296"></a><a href="#fn_1296"><font size="1">1297</font></a>
  to put you in remembrance of your Christian course, <a name="fr_1297"></a><a href="#fn_1297"><font size="1">1298</font></a>
  “that ye all speak the same thing, being of one mind, thinking the same thing, and walking by the same rule of faith,” <a name="fr_1298"></a><a href="#fn_1298"><font size="1">1299</font></a>
  as Paul admonished you. For if there is one God of the universe, the Father of Christ, “of whom are all things;” <a name="fr_1299"></a><a href="#fn_1299"><font size="1">1300</font></a>
  and one Lord Jesus Christ, our [Lord], “by whom are all things;” <a name="fr_1300"></a><a href="#fn_1300"><font size="1">1301</font></a>
  and also one Holy Spirit, who wrought <a name="fr_1301"></a><a href="#fn_1301"><font size="1">1302</font></a>
  in Moses, and in the prophets and apostles; and also one baptism, which is administered that we should have fellowship with the death of the Lord; <a name="fr_1302"></a><a href="#fn_1302"><font size="1">1303</font></a>
  and also one elect Church; there ought likewise to be but one faith in respect to Christ. For “there is one Lord, one faith, one baptism; one God and Father of all, who is through all, and in all.” <a name="fr_1303"></a><a href="#fn_1303"><font size="1">1304</font></a>
 </p>
