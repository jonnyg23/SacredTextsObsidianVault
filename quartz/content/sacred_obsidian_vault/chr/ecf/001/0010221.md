 <p> <a name="v.viii.viii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.viii.viii-p0.1"></a>Chapter VIII.—Let other churches also send to Antioch.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.viii.viii-p1"></a>Inasmuch as I have not been able to write to all the Churches, because I must suddenly sail from Troas to Neapolis, as the will <a name="fr_1118"></a><a href="#fn_1118"><font size="1">1119</font></a>
  [of the emperor] enjoins, [I beg that] thou, as being acquainted with the purpose <a name="fr_1119"></a><a href="#fn_1119"><font size="1">1120</font></a>
  of God, wilt write to the adjacent Churches, that they also may act in like manner, such as are able to do so sending messengers, <a name="fr_1120"></a><a href="#fn_1120"><font size="1">1121</font></a>
  and the others transmitting letters through those persons who are sent by thee, that thou <a name="fr_1121"></a><a href="#fn_1121"><font size="1">1122</font></a>
  mayest be glorified by a work <a name="fr_1122"></a><a href="#fn_1122"><font size="1">1123</font></a>
  which shall be remembered for ever, as indeed thou art worthy to be. <a name="v.viii.viii-p6.1"></a>I salute all by name, and in particular the wife of Epitropus, with all her house and children. I salute Attalus, my beloved. I salute him who shall be deemed worthy to go [from you] into Syria. Grace shall be with him for ever, and with Polycarp that sends him. I pray for your happiness for ever in our God, Jesus Christ, by whom continue ye in the unity and under the protection of God, <a name="fr_1123"></a><a href="#fn_1123"><font size="1">1124</font></a>
  I salute Alce, my dearly beloved. <a name="fr_1124"></a><a href="#fn_1124"><font size="1">1125</font></a>
  <a name="v.viii.viii-p8.1"></a>Fare ye well in the Lord.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.viii.viii-p9"></a>Inasmuch, therefore, as I have not been able to write to all Churches, because I must suddenly sail from Troas to Neapolis, as the will <a name="fr_1125"></a><a href="#fn_1125"><font size="1">1126</font></a>
  [of the emperor] enjoins, [I beg that] thou, as being acquainted with the purpose <a name="fr_1126"></a><a href="#fn_1126"><font size="1">1127</font></a>
  of God, wilt write to the adjacent Churches, that they also may act in like manner, such as are able to do so sending messenger, and the others transmitting letters through those persons who are sent by thee, that thou mayest be glorified by a work <a name="fr_1127"></a><a href="#fn_1127"><font size="1">1128</font></a>
  which shall be remembered for ever, as indeed thou art worthy to be. <a name="v.viii.viii-p12.1"></a>I salute all by name, and in particular the wife of Epitropus, with all her house and children. I salute Attalus, my beloved. I salute him who shall be deemed worthy to go [from you] into Syria. Grace shall be with him for ever, and with Polycarp that sends him. I pray for your happiness for ever in our God, Jesus Christ, by whom continue ye in the unity and under the protection of God. I salute Alce, my dearly beloved. <a name="fr_1128"></a><a href="#fn_1128"><font size="1">1129</font></a>
  Amen. <a name="v.viii.viii-p13.1"></a>Grace [be with you]. Fare ye well in the Lord.</p>
