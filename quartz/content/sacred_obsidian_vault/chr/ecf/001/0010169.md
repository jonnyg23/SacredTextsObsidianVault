 <p> <a name="v.iv.vii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.iv.vii-p0.1"></a>Chapter VII.—The same continued.</h3>  
 <p><a name="v.iv.vii-p1"></a>Be on your guard, therefore, against such persons. <a name="v.iv.vii-p1.1"></a>And this will be the case with you if you are not puffed up, and continue in intimate union with <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_765"><font size="1">766</font></a>
  Jesus Christ our God, and the  <a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a> <a name="v.iv.vii-Page_69"></a>  bishop, and the enactments of the apostles. He that is within the altar is pure, but <a name="fr_766"></a><a href="#fn_766"><font size="1">767</font></a>
  he that is without is not pure; that is, he who does anything apart from the bishop, and presbytery, and deacons, <a name="fr_767"></a><a href="#fn_767"><font size="1">768</font></a>
  such a man is not pure in his conscience.</p>  
 <p><a name="v.iv.vii-p5"></a>Be on your guard, therefore, against such persons, that ye admit not of a snare for your own souls. And act so that your life shall be without offence to all men, lest ye become as “a snare upon a watch-tower, and as a net which is spread out.” <a name="fr_768"></a><a href="#fn_768"><font size="1">769</font></a>
  For “he that does not heal himself in his own works, is the brother of him that destroys himself.” <a name="fr_769"></a><a href="#fn_769"><font size="1">770</font></a>
  <a name="v.iv.vii-p7.2"></a>If, therefore, ye also put away conceit, arrogance, disdain, and haughtiness, it will be your privilege to be inseparably united to God, for “He is nigh unto those that fear Him.” <a name="fr_770"></a><a href="#fn_770"><font size="1">771</font></a>
  And says He, “Upon whom will I look, but upon him that is humble and quiet, and that trembles at my words?” <a name="fr_771"></a><a href="#fn_771"><font size="1">772</font></a>
  And do ye also reverence your bishop as Christ Himself, according as the blessed apostles have enjoined you. He that is within the altar is pure, wherefore also he is obedient to the bishop and presbyters: but he that is without is one that does anything apart from the bishop, the presbyters, and the deacons. Such a person is defiled in his conscience, and is worse than an infidel. <a name="v.iv.vii-p9.2"></a>For what is the bishop but one who beyond all others possesses all power and authority, so far as it is possible for a man to possess it, who according to his ability has been made an imitator of the Christ of God? <a name="fr_772"></a><a href="#fn_772"><font size="1">773</font></a>
  <a name="v.iv.vii-p10.1"></a>And what is the presbytery but a sacred assembly, the counsellors and assessors of the bishop? <a name="v.iv.vii-p10.2"></a><a name="v.iv.vii-p10.3"></a>And what are the deacons but imitators of the angelic powers, <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">774</font></a>
  fulfilling a pure and blameless ministry unto him, as the holy Stephen did to the blessed James, Timothy and Linus to Paul, Anencletus and Clement to Peter? He, therefore, that will not yield obedience to such, must needs be one utterly without God, an impious man who despises Christ, and depreciates His appointments.</p>
