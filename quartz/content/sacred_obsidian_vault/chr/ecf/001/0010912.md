 <p> <a name="ix.viii.xxxvii"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="ix.viii.xxxvii-p0.1"></a>XXXVII.</h3>  
 <p><a name="ix.viii.xxxvii-p1"></a>Those who have become acquainted with the secondary (i.e., under Christ) constitutions of the apostles, <a name="fr_4867"></a><a href="#fn_4867"><font size="1">4868</font></a>
  are aware that the Lord instituted a new oblation in the new covenant, according to [the declaration of] Malachi the prophet. For, “from the rising of the sun even to the setting my name has been glorified among the Gentiles, and in every place incense is offered to my name, and a pure sacrifice;” <a name="fr_4868"></a><a href="#fn_4868"><font size="1">4869</font></a>
  as John also declares in the Apocalypse: “The incense is the prayers of the saints.” <a name="fr_4869"></a><a href="#fn_4869"><font size="1">4870</font></a>
  Then again, Paul exhorts us “to present our bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service.” <a name="fr_4870"></a><a href="#fn_4870"><font size="1">4871</font></a>
  And again, “Let us offer the sacrifice of praise, that is, the fruit of the lips.” <a name="fr_4871"></a><a href="#fn_4871"><font size="1">4872</font></a>
  Now those oblations are not according to the law, the handwriting of which the Lord took away from the midst by cancelling it; <a name="fr_4872"></a><a href="#fn_4872"><font size="1">4873</font></a>
  but they are according to the Spirit, for we must worship God “in spirit and in truth.” <a name="fr_4873"></a><a href="#fn_4873"><font size="1">4874</font></a>
  And therefore the oblation of the Eucharist is not a carnal one, but a spiritual; and in this respect it is pure. For we make an oblation to God of the bread and the cup of blessing, giving Him thanks in that He has commanded the earth to bring forth these fruits for our nourishment. And then, when we have perfected the oblation, we invoke the Holy Spirit, that He may exhibit this sacrifice, both the bread the body of Christ, and the cup the blood of Christ, in order that the receivers of these antitypes <a name="fr_4874"></a><a href="#fn_4874"><font size="1">4875</font></a>
  may obtain remission of sins and life eternal. Those persons, then, who perform these oblations in remembrance  <a name="page_575"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 575</font></a> <a name="ix.viii.xxxvii-Page_575"></a>  of the Lord, do not fall in with Jewish views, but, performing the service after a spiritual manner, they shall be called sons of wisdom.</p>
