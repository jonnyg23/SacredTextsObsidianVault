 <p> <a name="ii.ii.xlvi"></a>  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="ii.ii.xlvi-p0.1"></a>Chapter XLVI.—Let us cleave to the righteous: your strife is pernicious.</h3>  
 <p><a name="ii.ii.xlvi-p1"></a>Such examples, therefore, brethren, it is right that we should follow; <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">201</font></a>
  since it is written, “Cleave to the holy, for those that cleave to them shall [themselves] be made holy.” <a name="fr_201"></a><a href="#fn_201"><font size="1">202</font></a>
  And again, in another place, [the Scripture] saith, “With a harmless man thou shalt prove <a name="fr_202"></a><a href="#fn_202"><font size="1">203</font></a>
  thyself harmless, and with an elect man thou shalt be elect, and with a perverse man thou shalt show <a name="fr_203"></a><a href="#fn_203"><font size="1">204</font></a>
  thyself perverse.” <a name="fr_204"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">205</font></a>
  Let us cleave, therefore, to the innocent and righteous, since these are the elect of God. <a name="ii.ii.xlvi-p6.2"></a><a name="ii.ii.xlvi-p6.3"></a><a name="ii.ii.xlvi-p6.4"></a>Why are there strifes, and tumults, and divisions, and schisms, and wars <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_205"><font size="1">206</font></a>
  among you? Have we not [all] one God and one Christ? Is there not one Spirit of grace poured out upon us? And have we not one calling in Christ? <a name="fr_206"></a><a href="#fn_206"><font size="1">207</font></a>
  Why do we divide and tear to pieces the members of Christ, and raise up strife against our own body, and have reached such a height of madness as to forget that “we are members one of another?” <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">208</font></a>
  Remember the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, how <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_208"><font size="1">209</font></a>
  He said, “Woe to that man [by whom <a name="fr_209"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">210</font></a>
  offences come]! It were better for him that he had never been born, than that he should cast a stumbling-block before one of my elect. Yea, it were better for him that a millstone should be  <a name="page_18"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 18</font></a> <a name="ii.ii.xlvi-Page_18"></a>  hung about [his neck], and he should be sunk in the depths of the sea, than that he should cast a stumbling-block before one of my little ones.” <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">211</font></a>
  Your schism has subverted [the faith of] many, has discouraged many, has given rise to doubt in many, and has caused grief to us all. And still your sedition continueth.</p>
