 <p> <a name="v.iv.ix.xi"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.ix.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.ix.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter 11.—</span>20.  Paul of Bobba <a name="fr_1786"></a><a href="#fn_1786"><font size="1">1787</font></a>
  said:  "I for my part am not moved if some fail to uphold the faith and truth of the Church, seeing that the apostle says For what if some did not believe? shall their unbelief make the faith of God without effect?  God forbid:  yea let God be true, but every man a liar. <a name="fr_1787"></a><a href="#fn_1787"><font size="1">1788</font></a>
   But if God be true, how can the truth of baptism be in the company of heretics, where God is not?" <a name="fr_1788"></a><a href="#fn_1788"><font size="1">1789</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.ix.xi-p5"></a>21.  To him we answer:  What is God among the covetous?  And yet baptism exists among them; and so also it exists among heretics.  For they among whom God is, are the temple of God.  "But what agreement hath the temple of God with idols?" <a name="fr_1789"></a><a href="#fn_1789"><font size="1">1790</font></a>
   Further, Paul considers, and Cyprian agrees with him, that covetousness is idolatry; and Cyprian himself again associates with his colleagues, who were robbers, but yet baptized, with great reward of toleration.</p>
