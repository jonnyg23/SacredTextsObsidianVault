 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.v.iv.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.ix-p1.1"></a>Chapter 9.—</span>21.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.ix-p1.2"></a>Petilianus</span> said:  "Hemmed in, therefore, by these offenses, you cannot be a true bishop."</p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.v.iv.ix-p2"></a>22.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.ix-p2.1"></a>Augustin</span> answered:  By what offenses?  What have you shown?  What have you proved?  And if you have proved charges on the part of I know not whom, what has that to do with the seed of Abraham, in which all the nations of the earth are blessed?</p>
