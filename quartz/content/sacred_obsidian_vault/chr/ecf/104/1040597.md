 </p><p class="c34"><a name="v.v.v-p1"></a><span class="c7"><a name="v.v.v-p1.1"></a>Book III.</span></p>   
 <p class="c62"><a name="v.v.v-p2"></a><i>In this book Augustin refutes the second letter</i> <a name="fr_2318"></a><a href="#fn_2318"><font size="1">2319</font></a>
  <i>which Petilianus wrote to him after having seen the first of Augustins earlier books.  This letter had been full of violent language; and Augustin rather shows that the arguments of Petilianus had been deficient and irrelevant, than brings forward arguments in support of his own statements.</i></p>
