 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.v.iv.xli-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.xli-p1.1"></a>Chapter 41.—</span>97.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.xli-p1.2"></a>Petilianus</span> said:  "And, again, he taught us that schisms should not arise, in the following terms:  Now this I say, that every one of you saith, I am of Paul, and I of Apollos, and I of Cephas, and I of Christ.  Is Christ divided? was Paul crucified for you? or were ye baptized in the name of Paul?" <a name="fr_2115"></a><a href="#fn_2115"><font size="1">2116</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.v.iv.xli-p3"></a>98.  <span class="c11"><a name="v.v.iv.xli-p3.1"></a>Augustin</span> answered:  Remember all of you who read this, it was Petilianus who quoted these words from the apostle.  For who could have believed that he would have brought forward words which tell so much for us against himself?</p>
