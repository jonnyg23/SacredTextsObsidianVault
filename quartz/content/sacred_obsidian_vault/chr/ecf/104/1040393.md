 <p> <a name="v.iv.viii.xxxix"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="v.iv.viii.xxxix-p1"></a> <span class="c11"><a name="v.iv.viii.xxxix-p1.1"></a>Chapter 39.—</span>75.  Eugenius of Ammedera <a name="fr_1732"></a><a href="#fn_1732"><font size="1">1733</font></a>
  said:  "I too pronounce this same judgment, that heretics should be baptized." <a name="fr_1733"></a><a href="#fn_1733"><font size="1">1734</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="v.iv.viii.xxxix-p4"></a>76.  To him we answer:  But this is not the judgment which the Church pronounces, to which also God has now revealed in a plenary Council the point in which ye were then still otherwise minded, <a name="fr_1734"></a><a href="#fn_1734"><font size="1">1735</font></a>
  but because saving charity was in you, ye remained in unity.</p>
