 </p><p class="c46"><a name="xv.iii.xxxix-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="xv.iii.xxxix-p1.1"></a>Chapter 39 [XXXVI]—A Letter of Pelagius Unknown to Augustin.</span></p>  
 <p><a name="xv.iii.xxxix-p2"></a>“Let them also read,” says he, “my epistle to the holy Bishop Constantius, wherein I have—briefly no doubt, but yet plainly—conjoined the grace and help of God with mans free will.” This epistle, as I have already stated, <a name="fr_1879"></a><a href="#fn_1879"><font size="1">1880</font></a>
  I have not read; but if it is not unlike the other writings which he mentions, and with which I am acquainted, even this work does nothing for the subject of our present inquiry.</p>
