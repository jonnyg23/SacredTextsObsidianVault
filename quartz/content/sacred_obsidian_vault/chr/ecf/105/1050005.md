 </p><p class="c26"><a name="v.i-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="v.i-p1.1"></a>A Select Bibliography of the Pelagian Controversy.</span></p>   
 <p class="Center"><a name="v.i-p2"></a>(<i>Adapted from Dr. Schaffs Church History, vol. iii</i>.)</p>   
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.i-p3"></a>————————————</p>   
 <p class="c27"><a name="v.i-p4"></a>I. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p4.1"></a>Three</span> works of <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p4.2"></a>Pelagius</span>, printed among the works of Jerome (Vallarsius edition, vol. xi.): viz., the <i>Expositions on Pauls Epistles</i>, written before 410 (but somewhat, especially in Romans, interpolated); the <i>Epistle to Demetrias</i>, 413; and the <i>Confession of Faith</i>, 417, addressed to Innocent I. Copious fragments of other works (<i>On Nature, In Defence of Free Will, Chapters, Letter to Innocent</i>) are found quoted in Augustins refutations; as also of certain works by <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p4.3"></a>Cœlestius</span> (e.g., his <i> Definitions, Confession to Zosimus</i>), and of the writings of <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p4.4"></a>Julian.</span> Here also belong <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p4.5"></a>Cassians</span> <i>Collationes Patrum</i>, and the works of the other semi-Pelagian writers.</p>   
 <p><a name="v.i-p5"></a>II. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p5.1"></a>Augustins</span> anti-Pelagian treatises; also his work <i>On Heresies</i>, 88, 428; many of his letters, as e.g., those numbered by the Benedictines, 140, 157, 178, 179, 190, 191, 193, 194; and many of his letters, as e.g., 155, 163, 165, 168, 169, 174, 176, 293, 294, etc. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p5.2"></a>Jeromes</span> <i>Letter to Ctesiphon</i> (133), and his three books of <i>Dialogue against the Pelagians</i> (vol. ii. of Vallarsius); <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p5.3"></a>Paulus Orosius</span> <i> Apology against Pelagius</i>; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p5.4"></a>Marius Mercators</span> <i>Commonitoria</i>; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p5.5"></a>Prosper of Aquitaines</span> writings as also those of such late writers as <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p5.6"></a>Avitus, Cæsarius, Fulgentius</span>, who bore the brunt of the semi-Pelagian controversy.</p>   
 <p><a name="v.i-p6"></a>III. The collections of <i>Acta</i> of the councils and other public documents, in <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p6.1"></a> Mansi</span> and in the appendix to the Benedictine edition of Augustins anti-Pelagian writings (vol.x.).</p>   
 <p><a name="v.i-p7"></a>IV. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.1"></a>Literature</span>.—A. Special works on the subject: <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.2"></a>Gerh. Joh. Vossius</span>, <i>Hist. de Controversiis quas Pelagius ejusque reliquiæ moverunt</i>, 1655; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.3"></a>Henr. Norisius</span>, <i>Historia Pelagiana</i>, etc., 1673; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.4"></a>Garnier</span>, <i>Dissert. vii. quibus integra continuentur Pelagianorum Hist</i>. (in his edition of Marius Mercator, I. 113); the <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.5"></a>Præfatio</span> to vol. x. of the Benedictine edition of Augustins works; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.6"></a>Corn. Jansenius</span>, <i>Augustinus sive doctrina S. Augustini, etc., adversus Pelagianos et Massilienses</i>, 1640; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.7"></a>Jac. Sirmond</span>, <i>Historia Prædestinatiana</i>, 1648; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.8"></a>Tillemont</span>, <i> Mémoires</i> xiii. 1-1075; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.9"></a>Ch. Wilh. Fr. Walch</span>, <i>Ketzerhìstorie</i>, Bd. iv. and v., 1770; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.10"></a>Johann Geffken</span>, <i>Historia semi-pelagianismi antiquissima</i>, 1826; G. F. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.11"></a>Wiggers</span>, <i> Versuch einer pragmatischen Darstellung des Augustinismus und Pelagianismus</i>, 1821–1833 (Part I. dealing with Pelagianism proper, in an E. T. by Professor Emerson, Andover, 1840); J.L. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.12"></a>Jacobi</span>, <i>Die Lehre des Pelagius</i>, 1842; P. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.13"></a>Schaff</span>, <i>The Pelagian Controversy</i>, in the <i>Bibliotheca Sacra</i>, May, 1884; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.14"></a>Theod. Gangauf</span>, <i>Metaphysische Psychologie des Heiligen Augustinus</i>, 1852; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.15"></a>Julius Müller</span>, <i>Die Christliche Lehre von der Sünde</i>, 5th edition 1866 (E. T. by Urwick, Edinburgh); Do., <i>Der Pelagianismus</i>, 1854; F. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.16"></a>Wörter</span>, <i> Der Pelagianismus</i> u. s. w. 1866; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.17"></a> Mozley</span>, <i>On the Augustinian Doctrine of Predestination</i>, 1855; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.18"></a>Nourrisson</span>, <i> La philosophie de S. Augustin</i>, 1868; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.19"></a> Bright</span>, <i>Select anti-Pelagian Treatises of St. Augustine</i>, 1880; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.20"></a>William Cunningham</span> (not to be confounded with the Scotch professor of that name), <i> S. Austin and his Place in the History of Christian Thought</i>, being the Hulsean Lectures for 1885; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.21"></a>James Field Spalding</span>, <i>The Teaching and Influence of St. Augustine</i>, 1886; <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p7.22"></a>Hermann Reuter</span>, <i> Augustinische Studien</i>, 1887.</p>   
 <p><a name="v.i-p8"></a>B. The appropriate section in the Histories of Doctrine, as for example those of <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p8.1"></a>Münchner, Baumgarten-Crusius, Hagenbach</span> (also E. T.), <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p8.2"></a>Neander</span> (also E. T.), <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p8.3"></a>Baur, Beck, Thomasius, Harnack</span> (vol. ii. in the press); and in English, W. <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p8.4"></a>Cunningham, Shedd</span>, etc.</p>   
 <p><a name="v.i-p9"></a>C. The appropriate chapters in the various larger church histories, e.g., those of <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p9.1"></a> Schröckh, Fleury, Gieseler</span> (also E. T.), <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p9.2"></a> Neander</span> (also E.T.), <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p9.3"></a>Hefele</span> (<i>History of the Councils</i>, also E. T.), <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p9.4"></a> Kurtz</span> (also E. T.); and in English, <span class="c16"><a name="v.i-p9.5"></a> Schaff, Milman, Robertson</span>, etc.</p>
