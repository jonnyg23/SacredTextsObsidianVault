 </p><p class="c46"><a name="x.iv.xxxi-p1"></a> <span class="c2"><a name="x.iv.xxxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter 31.—Grace is Given to Some Men in Mercy; Is Withheld from Others in Justice and Truth.</span></p>  
 <p><a name="x.iv.xxxi-p2"></a>Forasmuch then as our turning away from God is our own act, and this is evil will; but our turning to God is not possible, except He rouses and helps us, and this is good will,—what have we that we have not received? But if we received, why do we glory as if we had not received? Therefore, as “he that glorieth must glory in the Lord,” <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="#fn_574"><font size="1">575</font></a>
  it comes from His mercy, not their merit, that God wills to impart this to some, but from His truth that He wills not to impart it to others. For to sinners punishment is justly due, because “the Lord God loveth mercy and truth,” <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_575"><font size="1">576</font></a>
  and “mercy and truth are met together;” <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_576"><font size="1">577</font></a>
  and “all the paths of the Lord are <a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a> <a name="x.iv.xxxi-Page_57"></a>mercy and truth.” <a name="fr_577"></a><a href="#fn_577"><font size="1">578</font></a>
  And who can tell the numberless instances in which Holy Scripture combines these two attributes? Sometimes, by a change in the terms, <i> grace</i> is put for <i>merc</i>y, as in the passage, “We beheld His glory, the glory as of the Only-begotten of the Father, full of grace and truth.” <a name="fr_578"></a><a href="#fn_578"><font size="1">579</font></a>
  Sometimes also <i>judgment</i> occurs instead of <i>truth</i>, as in the passage, “I will sing of mercy and judgment unto Thee, O Lord.” <a name="fr_579"></a><a href="#fn_579"><font size="1">580</font></a>
 </p>
