    <h2 align="CENTER"><a></a> TRANSLATOR'S BIOGRAPHICAL NOTICE.</h2>  
 <p> [a.d. 170-233-251.] Alexander was at first bishop of a church in Cappadocia, but on his visiting Jerusalem he was appointed to the bishopric of the church there, while the previous bishop Narcissus was alive, in consequence of a vision which was believed to be divine.<sup><a href="footnote/fn27.htm#P2715_731056">1</a></sup> During the Decian persecution he was thrown into prison at Caesarea, and died there,<sup><a href="footnote/fn27.htm#P2716_731201">2</a></sup> a.d. 251. The only writings of his which we know are those from which the extracts are made.<sup><a href="footnote/fn27.htm#P2717_731353">3</a></sup> </p>
