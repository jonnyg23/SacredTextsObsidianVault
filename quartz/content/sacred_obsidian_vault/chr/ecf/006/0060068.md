    <h2 align="CENTER"><a></a> I.-A Fragment of a Work of Pierius on the First Epistle of Paul to the Corinthians.<sup><a href="footnote/fn28.htm#P2816_743041">1</a></sup> </h2>  
 <p>------------</p>  
 <p>Origen, Dionysius, Pierius, Eusebius of Caesareia, Didymus, and Apollinaris, have interpreted this epistle most copiously;<sup><a href="footnote/fn28.htm#P2819_743260">2</a></sup> of whom Pierius, when he was expounding and unfolding the meaning of the apostle, and purposed to explain the words. <i>For I would that all men were even as I myself</i>,<sup><a href="footnote/fn28.htm#P2820_743439">3</a></sup> added this remark: In saying this, Paul, without disguise, preaches celibacy.<sup><a href="footnote/fn28.htm#P2821_743535">4</a></sup> </p>
