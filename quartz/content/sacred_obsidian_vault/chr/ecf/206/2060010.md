 </p><p class="c15"><a name="iv.VII-p1"></a>  <a name="page_xxxiv"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. xxxiv</font></a> <a name="iv.VII-Page_xxxiv"></a><span class="c14"><a name="iv.VII-p1.1"></a>Chronological Tables of the Life and Times of St. Jerome A.D. 345–420.</span></p>     
 <p class="c2"><a name="iv.VII-p2"></a><span class="c10"><a name="iv.VII-p2.1"></a>Personal.</span></p>   
 <p class="c2"><a name="iv.VII-p3"></a><span class="c10"><a name="iv.VII-p3.1"></a>Literary.</span></p>   
 <p class="c2"><a name="iv.VII-p4"></a><span class="c10"><a name="iv.VII-p4.1"></a>Contemporary History.</span></p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p5"></a><span class="c10"><a name="iv.VII-p5.1"></a>Contemporary History (Ecclesiastical).</span></p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p6"></a>345. Jerome born at Stridon (Pannonia or Dalmatia).</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p7"></a>340. Death of Constantine.</p>          
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p8"></a>341. Athanasius at Rome.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p9"></a>360. Jerome at school.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p10"></a>352. Cyril, Bishop of Jerusalem.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p11"></a>363. To study at Rome. Baptism.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p12"></a>353. Constantius sole Emperor.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p13"></a>366. To Treves.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p14"></a>356. Eusebius of Vercellæ, and other orthodox Bishops banished by Constantius.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p15"></a>366–69. Jerome copies works of Hilary.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p16"></a>356. Death of Antony.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p17"></a>369. Jerome writes a mystical Commentary on Obadiah.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p18"></a>359. Councils of Ariminum and Seleucia.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p19"></a>370. To Aquileia.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p20"></a>370. First letter—On the woman seven times struck with the axe.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p21"></a>360. Julian Emperor.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p22"></a>373. Leaves Aquileia for the East.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p23"></a>361. Death of Constantius.</p>          
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p24"></a>362. Eusebius of Vercellæ and other Bishops recalled from exile.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p25"></a>363. Death of Julian. Jovian Emperor.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p26"></a>364. Death of Jovian. Valentinian and Valens.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p27"></a>374. Illness at Antioch. Anti-Ciceronian dream.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p28"></a>374. Life of Paulus, the first hermit.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p29"></a>365. Apollinarius, Bishop of Laodicæa.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p30"></a>374–79. In Desert of Chalcis.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p31"></a>374–79. Jerome copies Gospel of the Hebrews and other books.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p32"></a>366. Invasion of the Alemanni repelled by Valentinian.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p33"></a>366. Damasus Pope.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p34"></a>379. Dialogue against the Luciferians.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p35"></a>367–69. Gothic war.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p36"></a>379–80. At Antioch.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p37"></a>367–70. Britain restored by the elder Theodosius.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p38"></a>370. Law of Valentinian against clerical legacies.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p39"></a>379. Ordination by Paulinus.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p40"></a>371. Death of Eusebius of Vercellæ and of Lucifer.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p41"></a>380. To Constantinople.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p42"></a>373. Death of Athanasius. Peter and Lucius, rival Bishops.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p43"></a>381. Translation of Eusebius Chronicle.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p44"></a>374. Ambrose, Bishop of Milan.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p45"></a>381. Translation of Origens Homilies on Jeremiah and Ezekiel.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p46"></a>374. Melania and Rufinus leave Rome for the East.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p47"></a>382–85. At Rome.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p48"></a>375. Death of Valentinian. Valens and Gratian Emperors.</p>                             
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p49"></a>383. Translation of Psalms from LXX. and of New Testament.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p50"></a>376. Theodosius, after restoring Africa, executed at Carthage.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p51"></a>383. Book against Helvidius (Perp. Virg. of B.M.V.)</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p52"></a>377–80. Persian war.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p53"></a>385. Leaves Rome (August); to Antioch (December).</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p54"></a>385–87. Translation of Origen on Canticles.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p55"></a>378. Battle of Adrianople. Valens killed. Gregory Nazianzen at Constantinople.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p56"></a>378. Gregory Nazianzen at Constantinople.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p57"></a>386. Through Palestine to Egypt, and settlement at Bethlehem.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p58"></a>386–90. Translation of LXX. into Latin.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p59"></a>379. Theodosius Emperor.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p60"></a>387. Revision of version of New Testament.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p61"></a>380. Baptism of Theodosius.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p62"></a>381. Council of Constantinople.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p63"></a>381. Peter, Bishop of Alexandria, succeeded by his brother Timothy.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p64"></a>388. Commentary on Ecclesiastes.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p65"></a>382. Council at Rome.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p66"></a>388. Commentary on Galatians, Ephesians, Titus, Philemon.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p67"></a>382. Altar of Victory in Roman Senate removed.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p68"></a>388. Book of Hebrew Names.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p69"></a>383. Death of Gratian. Maximus Emperor.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p70"></a>388. Questions on Genesis.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p71"></a>384. Treaty with Persia.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p72"></a>384. Death of Damasus (December).</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p73"></a>388. Translation of Eusebius on Sites and Names of Hebrew Places.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p74"></a>385. Theophilus, Bishop of Alexandria, succeeds Timothy.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p75"></a>385. Siricius Pope.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p76"></a>388. Translation of Didymus on the Holy Spirit.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p77"></a>386. John succeeds Cyril as Bishop of Jerusalem.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p78"></a>386. Execution of Priscillian for heresy at Treves.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p79"></a>389. Translation of Origen on St. Luke.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p80"></a>387. Sedition of Antioch.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p81"></a>390. Lives of Malchus and Hilarion, hermits.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p82"></a>388. Death of Maximus. Valentinian II. Emperor.</p>          
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p83"></a>389. Temple of Serapis destroyed.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p84"></a>391. Vulgate version of Old Testament begun.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p85"></a>390. Massacre of Thessalonica. Penance of Theodosius.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p86"></a>390. Death of Gregory Nazianzen.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p87"></a>392. Aterbius at Jerusalem.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p88"></a>392. Book of Illustrious Men.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p89"></a>391. Death of Valentinian II. Eugenius usurper.</p>          
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p90"></a>392. Laws of Theodosius against Paganism.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p91"></a>392. Epiphanius visits Jerusalem. Schism between Jerome and John of Jerusalem, till 397.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p92"></a>392. Commentary on Nahum, Micah, Zephaniah, Haggai, Habakkuk.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p93"></a>394. Defeat of Eugenius. Theodosius sole Emperor.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p94"></a>393. Books against Jovinian.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p95"></a>394. Death of Theodosius. Arcadius (æt. 18) Emperor of the East; Honorius (æt. 14) of the West. Stilicho Minister and General in the West. Death of Rufinus the Prefect at Constantinople.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p96"></a>395. Augustin, Bishop of Hippo.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p97"></a>394. Beginning of controversy with Augustin.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p98"></a>395. Jerome denounced to the Emperor.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p99"></a>395. The Huns invade Northern Syria.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p100"></a>396. Alaric invades Greece.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p101"></a>395. Oceanus and Fabiola at Bethlehem.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p102"></a>397. Alaric conquered by Stilicho in Arcadia.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p103"></a>397. Death of Ambrose. Simplicianus, Bishop of Milan.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p104"></a>397. Theophilus of Alexandria turns against Origenism. Rufinus reconciled to Jerome and returns to Italy.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p105"></a>397. Commentary on Jonah.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p106"></a>398. Death of Gildo in Africa. Alaric Master-General of Illyricum and King of the Visigoths.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p107"></a>398. Chrysostom, Bishop of Constantinople.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p108"></a>398. Pope Siricius dies. Anastasius Pope.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p109"></a>397. Book against John, Bishop of Jerusalem.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p110"></a>399. Fall of Eutropius.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p111"></a>398. Jerome suffers from a long illness.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p112"></a>398. Commentary on St. Matthew.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p113"></a>400. Gainas, conspirator, defeated and slain.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p114"></a>400. Origenism condemned by Bishops of Alexandria, Rome, and Milan, and by the Emperors.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p115"></a>401–4. Controversy between Jerome and Rufinus.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p116"></a>400. (August 15). Simplicianus dies. Venerius, Bishop of Millan.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p117"></a>402. Against Rufinus, Books i. and ii.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p118"></a>402. Pope Anastasius dies. Innocentius Pope.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p119"></a>403. Commentary on Obadiah.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p120"></a>402. Death of Epiphanius.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p121"></a>403. Stilicho defeats Alaric at Pollentia and Verona.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p122"></a>404. Triumph of Honorius. Last gladiatorial shows.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p123"></a>404. Exile of Chrysostom to Cucusus.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p124"></a>404. Death of Paula.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p125"></a>404. Translation of the acetic rule of Pachomius.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p126"></a>404. Emperors court at Ravenna.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p127"></a>404. Gladiatorial shows at Rome ended by the sacrifice of Telemachus, the monk.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p128"></a>404. Close of controversy with Augustin.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p129"></a>404. Against Rufinus, Book iii.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p130"></a>404. Death of the Empress Eudoxia.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p131"></a>404–5. Jerome ill for several months.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p132"></a>405. Northern Palestine invaded by Isaurians.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p133"></a>406. Stilicho defeats Radagaisus at Fæsulæ, and negotiates with Alaric.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p134"></a>406. Commentary on Zachariah, Malachi, Hosea, Joel, Amos—concluding Minor Prophets.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p135"></a>407. Gaul overrun by barbarians.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p136"></a>407. Death of Chrysostom at Comana.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p137"></a>407. Constantine usurps power in Britain and Gaul.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p138"></a>406. Book against Vigilantius.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p139"></a>408. Rome besieged by Alaric, and ransomed.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p140"></a>408. Disgrace and death of Stilicho.</p>        
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p141"></a>407. Commentary on Daniel.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p142"></a>408. Death of Arcadius. Theodosius II. Emperor. Pulcheria Regent.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p143"></a>410. Death of Rufinus.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p144"></a>410. Commentary on Isaiah.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p145"></a>409. Revolt of Britain.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p146"></a>409. Pelagius at Rome.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p147"></a>412. Cœlestius condemned at Carthage.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p148"></a>410. Sack of Rome by Alaric. Death of Alaric.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p149"></a>413. Pelagius in Palestine.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p150"></a>410. Egypt, Phœnicia, etc. threatened by barbarians (Ep. cxxvi.<a name="iv.VII-p150.1"></a>).</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p151"></a>414. Orosius sent by Augustin to Jerome.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p152"></a>414. Commentary on Ezekiel.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p153"></a>411. Death of Constantine and other usurpers. Victories of Roman General Constantius.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p154"></a>411. Dispute between Catholic and Donatist Bishops at Carthage. Persecution of Donatists by the Civil Power.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p155"></a>414. Pinianus and Melania at Jerusalem.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p156"></a>412. Death of Theophilus, Bishop of Alexandria.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p157"></a>415. Synod at Jerusalem admits Pelagius.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p158"></a>413. Expedition and death of Heraclian, Count of Africa.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p159"></a>417. Monasteries of Bethlehem burnt by adherents of Pelagius.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p160"></a>414. Adolphus, successor of Alaric, marries Galla Placidia.</p>         
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p161"></a>415. Goths established in Aquitaine and Spain.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p162"></a>415. Schism at Antioch healed. Alexander sole Bishop.</p>       
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p163"></a>416. Dialogue against the Pelagians.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p164"></a>415. Council of Diospolis (Lydda) accepts Pelagius.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p165"></a>418. Death of Eustochium.</p>   
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p166"></a>418–19. Commentary on Jeremiah.</p>    
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p167"></a>417. Pope Innocentius dies. Zosimus Pope.</p>      
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p168"></a>420. Jerome dies (September 20) at Bethlehem.</p>     
 <p><a name="iv.VII-p169"></a>417. Death of John, Bishop of Jerusalem. Succeeded by Praylus.</p>
