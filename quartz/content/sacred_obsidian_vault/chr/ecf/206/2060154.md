 <p> <a name="v.CXLIII"></a>
 </p><p class="c30"><a name="v.CXLIII-p1"></a>  <span class="c1"><a name="v.CXLIII-p1.1"></a>Letter CXLIII. To Alypius and Augustine.</span></p>  
 <p class="c16"><a name="v.CXLIII-p2"></a>In this letter Jerome congratulates Alypius and Augustine on their success in strangling the heresy of Cælestius, the co-adjutor of Pelagius, and states that, if he can find time and secretaries, he hopes to write a refutation of the absurd errors of the Pelagian pseudodeacon Annianus. The date is 419 <span class="c17"><a name="v.CXLIII-p2.1"></a>a.d.</span> This letter is among those of Augustine, number 202.</p>
