 <p> <a name="v.iv.xxiii"></a>
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.xxiii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xxiii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.xxiii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.xxiii-p2.1"></a>Niceas</span>, <a name="fr_2609"></a><a href="#fn_2609"><font size="1">2610</font></a>
   <a name="fr_2610"></a><a href="#fn_2610"><font size="1">2611</font></a>
  bishop of the city of Romatia, composed, in simple and clear language, six books of <i>Instruction for neophites.</i> The first of these contains, How candidates who seek to obtain grace of baptism ought to act, the second, On the errors of relationship, in which he relates that not far from his own time a certain Melodius, father of a family, on account of his liberality and Garadius <a name="fr_2611"></a><a href="#fn_2611"><font size="1">2612</font></a>
  a peasant, on account of his bravery, were placed, by the heathen, among the gods. A third book <i>On faith in one sovereign,</i> a fourth <i>Against genealogy</i>, <a name="fr_2612"></a><a href="#fn_2612"><font size="1">2613</font></a>
  a fifth <i>On the creed,</i> a sixth <i>On the sacrifice of the paschal lamb.</i> He addressed a work also <i>To the fallen virgin,</i> an incentive to amendment for all who have fallen.</p>
