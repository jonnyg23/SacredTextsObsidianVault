 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.ci-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.ci-p1.1"></a>Chapter XCIX.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.ci-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.ci-p2.1"></a>Serapion</span>, <a name="fr_2525"></a><a href="#fn_2525"><font size="1">2526</font></a>
  bishop of Thmuis, who on account of his cultivated genius was found worthy of the surname of Scholasticus, was the intimate friend of Anthony the monk, and published an excellent book <i>Against the Manichaeans,</i> also another <i>On the titles of the Psalms,</i> and valuable <i>Epistles</i> to different people. In the reign of the emperor Constantius he was renowned as a confessor.</p>
