 </p><p class="c49"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p1"></a>  <a name="page_435"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 435</font></a> <a name="vi.xi.ii.i-Page_435"></a><span class="c21"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p1.1"></a>Book I.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p2"></a>The following is an epitome of the argument:</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p3"></a>1. I must submit to the taunts of my adversary as Christ did to those of the Jews.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p4"></a>2. Yet the substantial charges must be answered.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p5"></a>3. I praised him but he has wounded me.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p6"></a>4. I am no heretic, but declare my faith, that of my baptism.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p7"></a>5. I give a further proof of my faith in the resurrection of the flesh.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p8"></a>6–9. The resurrection body is a spiritual body.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p9"></a>10. Origens doctrines in the <span class="Greek"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p9.1"></a>Περὶ ᾽Αρχῶν</span></p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p10"></a>11. What led to the translation.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p11"></a>12, 13. Pamphilus Apology for Origen.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p12"></a>14. Preface to the Translation of the <span class="Greek"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p12.1"></a>Περὶ ᾽Αρχῶν</span></p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p13"></a>15. Treatise on the Adulteration of the works of Origen.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p14"></a>16. The difficulties of translation.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p15"></a>17. Explanation of Origens words “The Son does not see the Father.”</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p16"></a>18. Difference between seeing and knowing.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p17"></a>19. The Translation interpolated by Eusebius of Cremona.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p18"></a>20. Eusebius, if acting honestly, should have shown me what he thought dangerous.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p19"></a>21. Jeromes method of translation was the same as mine.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p20"></a>22. Jeromes reference to his Commentary on the Ephesians.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p21"></a>23. Jerome has not really changed his mind about Origen.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p22"></a>24. Women turned into men and bodies into souls.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p23"></a>25. The foundation (<span class="Greek"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p23.1"></a>καταβολή</span>) of the world explained by Jerome as a casting down.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p24"></a>26. Jerome, under the name of “another,” gives his own views.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p25"></a>27. The fall of souls into human bodies is taught by Jerome.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p26"></a>28. Predestination.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p27"></a>29. “Another,” who gives strange views, is Jerome himself.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p28"></a>30. “Hopers” and “fore-hopers.”</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p29"></a>31. and 30 (a). Jerome has confessed these views to be his own.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p30"></a>31 (a) and 32. Further identification of Jeromes views with Origens.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p31"></a>33. The commentary on the Ephesians, selected by Jerome, is his condemnation.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p32"></a>34, 35. Principalities and Powers.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p33"></a>36. Jeromes complaint of new doctrines may be retorted on himself.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p34"></a>38, 39. Origin of men, angels, and heavenly bodies.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p35"></a>40, 41. The body as a prison.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p36"></a>42. All creatures, including the fallen angel, partaking in the final restoration.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p37"></a>43. Arrogance of Jeromes teaching.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="vi.xi.ii.i-p38"></a>44. If Origen is not to be pardoned, neither is Jerome.</p>
