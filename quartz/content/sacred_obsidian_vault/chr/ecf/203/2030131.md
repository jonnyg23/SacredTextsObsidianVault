 </p><p class="c46"><a name="iv.viii.iv.xxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="iv.viii.iv.xxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXV</span>.—<i>Of what other monks were distinguished at this period</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="iv.viii.iv.xxv-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="iv.viii.iv.xxv-p2.1"></a>There</span> were also other men at this period who emitted the bright rays of the philosophy of solitary life. In the Chalcidian <a name="fr_773"></a><a href="#fn_773"><font size="1">774</font></a>
  desert Avitus, Marcianus <a name="fr_774"></a><a href="#fn_774"><font size="1">775</font></a>
  and Abraames, <a name="fr_775"></a><a href="#fn_775"><font size="1">776</font></a>
  and more besides whom I cannot easily enumerate, strove in their bodies of sense to live a life superior to sense. In the district of Apamea, <a name="fr_776"></a><a href="#fn_776"><font size="1">777</font></a>
  Agapetus, <a name="fr_777"></a><a href="#fn_777"><font size="1">778</font></a>
  Simeon, <a name="fr_778"></a><a href="#fn_778"><font size="1">779</font></a>
  Paulus and others reaped the fruits of the highest wisdom.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.viii.iv.xxv-p9"></a>In the district of the Zeugmatenses <a name="fr_779"></a><a href="#fn_779"><font size="1">780</font></a>
  were Publius <a name="fr_780"></a><a href="#fn_780"><font size="1">781</font></a>
  and Paulus. In the Cyrestian <a name="fr_781"></a><a href="#fn_781"><font size="1">782</font></a>
  the famous Acepsemas had been shut up in a cell for sixty years without being either seen or spoken to. The admirable Zeumatius, though bereft of sight, used to go about confirming the sheep, and fighting with the wolves; so they burnt his cell, but the right faithful general Trajanus got another built for him, and paid him besides other attentions. In the neighbourhood of Antioch, Marianus, <a name="fr_782"></a><a href="#fn_782"><font size="1">783</font></a>
  Eusebius, <a name="fr_783"></a><a href="#fn_783"><font size="1">784</font></a>
  Ammianus, <a name="fr_784"></a><a href="#fn_784"><font size="1">785</font></a>
  Palladius, <a name="fr_785"></a><a href="#fn_785"><font size="1">786</font></a>
  Simeon, <a name="fr_786"></a><a href="#fn_786"><font size="1">787</font></a>
  Abraames, <a name="fr_787"></a><a href="#fn_787"><font size="1">788</font></a>
  and others, preserved the divine image unimpaired; but of all these the lives have been recorded by us. But the mountain which is in the neighbourhood of the great city was decked like a meadow, for in it shone Petrus, the Galatian, his namesake the Egyptian, <a name="page_129"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 129</font></a> <a name="iv.viii.iv.xxv-Page_129"></a>Romanus Severus, <a name="fr_788"></a><a href="#fn_788"><font size="1">789</font></a>
  Zeno, <a name="fr_789"></a><a href="#fn_789"><font size="1">790</font></a>
  Moses, and Malchus, <a name="fr_790"></a><a href="#fn_790"><font size="1">791</font></a>
  and many others of whom the world is ignorant, but who are known to God.</p>
