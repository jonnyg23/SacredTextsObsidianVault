 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.xcv-p1"></a>  <a name="page_380"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 380</font></a> <a name="v.iii.xcv-Page_380"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xcv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XCIII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.xcv-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.xcv-p2.1"></a>Donatus</span>, <a name="fr_2519"></a><a href="#fn_2519"><font size="1">2520</font></a>
  from whom the Donatians arose in Africa in the reigns of the emperors Constantinus and Constantius, asserted that the scriptures were given up to the heathen by the orthodox during the persecution, and deceived almost all Africa, and especially Numidia by his persuasiveness. Many of his works, which relate to his heresy, are extant, including <i>On the Holy Spirit,</i> a work which is Arian in doctrine.</p>
