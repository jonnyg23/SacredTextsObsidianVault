 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxxvii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxvii-p2.1"></a>Pamphilus <a name="fr_2493"></a><a href="#fn_2493"><font size="1">2494</font></a>
 </span> the presbyter, patron of Eusebius bishop of Cæsarea, was so inflamed with love of sacred literature, that he transcribed the greater part of the works of Origen with his own hand and these are still preserved in the library at Cæsarea. I have twenty-five volumes <a name="fr_2494"></a><a href="#fn_2494"><font size="1">2495</font></a>
  of Commentaries of Origen, written in his hand, <i>On the twelve prophets</i> which I hug and guard with such joy, that I deem myself to have the wealth of Croesus. And if it is such joy to have one epistle of a martyr how much more to have so many thousand lines which seem to me to be traced in his blood. He wrote an <i>Apology for Origen</i> before Eusebius had written his and was put to death at Cæsarea in Palestine in the persecution of Maximinus.</p>
