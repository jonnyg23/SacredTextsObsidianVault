 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lix-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lix-p1.1"></a>Chapter LVII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lix-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lix-p2.1"></a>Trypho</span>, <a name="fr_2467"></a><a href="#fn_2467"><font size="1">2468</font></a>
  pupil of Origen, to whom some of his extant letters are addressed, was very learned in the Scriptures, and this many of his works show here and there, but especially the book which he composed <i>On the red heifer</i> <a name="fr_2468"></a><a href="#fn_2468"><font size="1">2469</font></a>
 in Deuteronomy, and <i>On the halves,</i> which with the pigeon and the turtledoves were offered by Abraham as recorded in Genesis. <a name="fr_2469"></a><a href="#fn_2469"><font size="1">2470</font></a>
