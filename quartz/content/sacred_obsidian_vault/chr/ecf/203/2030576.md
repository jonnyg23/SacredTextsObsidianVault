 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.lvii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lvii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LVI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.lvii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lvii-p2.1"></a>Theodotus, <a name="fr_2667"></a><a href="#fn_2667"><font size="1">2668</font></a>
 </span> <a name="fr_2668"></a><a href="#fn_2668"><font size="1">2669</font></a>
  bishop of Ancyra in Galatia, while at <a name="fr_2669"></a><a href="#fn_2669"><font size="1">2670</font></a>
  Ephesus, wrote against Nestorius a work of defence and refutation, <a name="fr_2670"></a><a href="#fn_2670"><font size="1">2671</font></a>
  written, to be sure, in dialectic style, but interwoven with passages from the Holy Scriptures. His method was to make statements and then quote proof texts from the Scriptures.</p>
