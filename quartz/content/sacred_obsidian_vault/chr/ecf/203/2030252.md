 </p><p class="c46"><a name="iv.x.lxiii-p1"></a>  <i>LXIII. Festal.</i> <a name="fr_1707"></a><a href="#fn_1707"><font size="1">1708</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="iv.x.lxiii-p3"></a>We have enjoyed the wonted blessings of the Feast. We have kept the memorial Feast of the Passion of Salvation; by means of the resurrection of the Lord we have received the glad tidings of the resurrection of all, and have hymned the ineffable loving kindness of our God and Saviour. But the storm tossing the churches has not suffered us to take our share of unalloyed gladness. If, when one member is in pain the whole body is partaker of the pang, <a name="fr_1708"></a><a href="#fn_1708"><font size="1">1709</font></a>
  how can we forbear from lamentation when all the body is distressed? And it intensifies our dis<a name="page_269"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 269</font></a> <a name="iv.x.lxiii-Page_269"></a>couragement to think that these things are the prelude of the general apostasy. May your piety pray that since we are in this plight we may get the divine succour, that, as the divine Apostle phrases it, we may “be able to withstand the evil day.” <a name="fr_1709"></a><a href="#fn_1709"><font size="1">1710</font></a>
  But if any time remain for this lifes business, pray that the tempest may pass away, and the churches recover their former calm, that the enemies of the truth may no more exult at our misfortunes.</p>
