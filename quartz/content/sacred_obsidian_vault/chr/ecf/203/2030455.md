 <p> <a name="v.iii.lxxiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.lxxiv-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.lxxiv-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.lxxiv-p2.1"></a>Archelaus</span>, <a name="fr_2490"></a><a href="#fn_2490"><font size="1">2491</font></a>
  bishop of Mesopotamia, composed in the Syriac language, a book of the discussion which he held with Manichaeus, when he came from Persia. This book, which is translated into Greek, is possessed by many.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="v.iii.lxxiv-p4"></a>He flourished under the Emperor Probus, who succeeded Aurelianus and Tacitus.</p>
