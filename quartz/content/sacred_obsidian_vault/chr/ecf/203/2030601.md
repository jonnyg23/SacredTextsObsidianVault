 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iv.lxxxii-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxxxii-p1.1"></a>Chapter LXXXI.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iv.lxxxii-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iv.lxxxii-p2.1"></a>Vincentius <a name="fr_2715"></a><a href="#fn_2715"><font size="1">2716</font></a>
 </span> the presbyter, a native of Gaul, practised in Divine Scripture and possessed of a style polished by speaking <a name="page_399"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 399</font></a> <a name="v.iv.lxxxii-Page_399"></a>and by wide reading, wrote a Commentary <i>On the Psalms.</i> A part of this work, he read in my hearing, to a man of God, at Cannatae, promising at the same time, that if the Lord should spare his life and strength, he would treat the whole Psalter in the same way.</p>
