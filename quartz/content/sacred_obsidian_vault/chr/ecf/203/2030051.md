 </p><p class="c46"><a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXX</span>.—<i>Will of the blessed Emperor Constantine</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-p2"></a>A <span class="c14"><a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-p2.1"></a>year</span> and a few months afterwards <a name="fr_441"></a><a href="#fn_441"><font size="1">442</font></a>
  the emperor was taken ill at Nicomedia, a city of Bithynia, and, knowing the uncertainty of human life, he received the holy rite of baptism <a name="fr_442"></a><a href="#fn_442"><font size="1">443</font></a>
 , which he had intended to have deferred until he could be baptized in the river Jordan.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-p5"></a>He left as heirs of the imperial throne his three sons, Constantine, Constantius, and Constans <a name="fr_443"></a><a href="#fn_443"><font size="1">444</font></a>
 , the youngest.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-p7"></a>He ordered that the great Athanasius should <a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a> <a name="iv.viii.i.xxxi-Page_64"></a>return to Alexandria, and expressed this decision in the presence of Eusebius, who did all he could to dissuade him.</p>
