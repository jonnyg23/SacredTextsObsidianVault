 </p><p class="c46"><a name="v.iii.cix-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cix-p1.1"></a>Chapter CVII.</span></p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="v.iii.cix-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="v.iii.cix-p2.1"></a>Photinus</span>, <a name="fr_2536"></a><a href="#fn_2536"><font size="1">2537</font></a>
  of Gallograecia, a disciple of Marcellus, and ordained bishop of Sirmium, attempted to introduce the Ebionite heresy, and afterwards having been expelled from the church by the Emperor Valentinianus, wrote many volumes, among which the most distinguished are <i>Against the nations,</i> and <i>To Valentinianus.</i></p>
