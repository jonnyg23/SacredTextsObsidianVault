 </p><p class="c46"><a name="iv.viii.v.xxxix-p1"></a>  <span class="c12"><a name="iv.viii.v.xxxix-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXIX</span>.—<i>Of Theodorus, bishop of Mopsuestia</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c31"><a name="iv.viii.v.xxxix-p2"></a><span class="c12"><a name="iv.viii.v.xxxix-p2.1"></a>When</span> the divine Theodorus was ruling the church of Antioch, Theodorus, bishop of Mopsuestia, a doctor of the whole church and successful combatant against every heretical phalanx, ended this life. He had enjoyed the teaching of the great Diodorus, and was the friend and fellow-worker of the holy John, for they both together benefited by the spiritual draughts given by Diodorus. Six-and-thirty years he had spent in his bishopric, fighting against the forces of Arius and Eunomius, struggling against the piratical band of Apollinarius, and finding the best pasture for Gods sheep. <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">957</font></a>
  His brother Polychronius <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">958</font></a>
  was the excellent bishop of Apamea, a man gifted with great eloquence and of illustrious character.</p>  
 <p class="c24"><a name="iv.viii.v.xxxix-p5"></a>I shall now make an end of my history, and shall entreat those who meet with it to requite my labour with their prayers. The narrative now embraces a period of 105 years, beginning from the Arian madness and ending with the death of the admirable Theodorus and Theodotus. <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">959</font></a>
  I will give a list of the bishops of great cities after the persecution.</p>
