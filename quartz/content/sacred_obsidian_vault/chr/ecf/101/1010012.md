 <p> <a name="v"></a><a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 25</font></a> <a name="v-Page_25"></a>  
 </p><p class="c7"><a name="v-p1"></a><span class="c6"><a name="v-p1.1"></a>Chief Events in the Life of St. Augustin.</span></p>  
 <p class="c27"><a name="v-p2"></a><span class="c9"><a name="v-p2.1"></a>(as Given, Nearly, in the Benedictine Edition).</span></p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p3"></a>354.            Augustin born at Tagaste, Nov. 13; his parents, Patricius and Monnica; shortly afterwards enrolled among the Catechumens.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p4"></a>370.  Returns home from studying Rhetoric at Madaura, after an idle childhood, and from idleness falls into dissipation and sin.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p5"></a>371.            Patricius dies; Augustin supported at Carthage by his mother, and his friend Romanianus; forms an illicit connection.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p6"></a>372.  Birth of his son Adeodatus.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p7"></a>373.             Ciceros <i> Hortensius</i> awakens in him a strong desire for true wisdom.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p8"></a>374.  He falls into the Manichæan heresy, and seduces several of his acquaintances into it. His mothers earnest prayers for him; she is assured of his recovery.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p9"></a>376.            Teaches Grammar at Tagaste; but soon returns to Carthage to teach Rhetoric—gains a prize.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p10"></a>379.  Is recovered from study of Astrology—writes his books <i>De pulchro et apto.</i></p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p11"></a>382.            Discovers the Manichæans to be in error, but falls into scepticism. Goes to Rome to teach Rhetoric.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p12"></a>385.            Removes to Milan; his errors gradually removed through the teaching of Ambrose, but he is held back by the flesh; becomes again a Catechumen.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p13"></a>386.  Studies St. Paul; converted through a voice from heaven; gives up his profession; writes against the Academics; prepares for Baptism.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p14"></a>387.  Is baptized by Bishop Ambrose, with his son Adeodatus. Death of his mother, Monnica, in her fifty-sixth year, at Ostia.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p15"></a>388.  Aug. revisits Rome, and then returns to Africa. Adeodatus, full of promise, dies.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p16"></a>389.  Aug. against his will ordained Presbyter at Hippo by Valerius, its Bishop.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p17"></a>392.  Writes against the Manichæans.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p18"></a>394.  Writes against the Donatists.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p19"></a>395.            Ordained Assistant Bishop to Valerius, toward the end of the year.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p20"></a>396.  Death of Bishop Valerius. Augustin elected his successor.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p21"></a>397.  Aug. writes the <i>Confessions</i>, and the <i>De Tinitate</i> against the Arians.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p22"></a>398.  Is present at the fourth Council of Carthage.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p23"></a>402.  Refutes the Epistle of Petilianus, a Donatist.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p24"></a>404.  Applies to Cæcilianus for protection against the savageness of the Donatists.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p25"></a>408.  Writes <i>De urbis Romæ obsidione.</i></p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p26"></a>411.  Takes a prominent part in a conference between the Catholic Bishops and the Donatists.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p27"></a>413.  Begins the composition of his great work <i>De Civitate Dei</i>, completed in 426.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p28"></a>417.  Writes <i>De gestis Palæstinæ synodi circa Pelagium.</i></p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p29"></a>420.  Writes against the Priscillianists.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p30"></a>424.  Writes against the Semipelagians.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p31"></a>426.            Appoints Heraclius his successor.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p32"></a>428.  Writes the <i> Retractations.</i></p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p33"></a>429.            Answers the Epistles of Prosper and Hilary.</p>  
 <p class="c28"><a name="v-p34"></a>430.  Dies Aug. 28, in the third month of the siege of Hippo by the Vandals.</p>
