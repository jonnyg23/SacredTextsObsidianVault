 <p> <a name="vi.XIII.XXII"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.XIII.XXII-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.XIII.XXII-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXII.—He Explains the Divine Image (Ver. 26) of the Renewal of the Mind.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.XIII.XXII-p2"></a>32. For behold, O Lord our God, our Creator, when our affections have been restrained from the love of the world, by which we died by living ill, and began to be a “living soul” by living well; <a name="fr_1368"></a><a href="#fn_1368"><font size="1">1369</font></a>
  and Thy word which Thou spakest by Thy apostle is made good in us, “Be not conformed to this world;” next also follows that which Thou presently subjoinedst, saying, “But be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind,” <a name="fr_1369"></a><a href="#fn_1369"><font size="1">1370</font></a>
 —not now after your kind, as if following your neighbour who went before you, nor as if living after the example of a better man (for Thou hast not said, “Let man be made after his kind,” but, “Let us make man in our image, <a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 201</font></a> <a name="vi.XIII.XXII-Page_201"></a>after our likeness”), <a name="fr_1370"></a><a href="#fn_1370"><font size="1">1371</font></a>
  that we may prove what Thy will is. For to this purpose said that dispenser of Thine,—begetting children by the gospel, <a name="fr_1371"></a><a href="#fn_1371"><font size="1">1372</font></a>
 —that he might not always have them “babes,” whom he would feed on milk, and cherish as a nurse; <a name="fr_1372"></a><a href="#fn_1372"><font size="1">1373</font></a>
  “be ye transformed,” saith He, “by the renewing of your mind, that he may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect will of God.” <a name="fr_1373"></a><a href="#fn_1373"><font size="1">1374</font></a>
  Therefore Thou sayest not, “Let man be made,” but, “Let us make man.” Nor sayest Thou, “after his kind,” but, after “our image” and “likeness.” Because, being renewed in his mind, and beholding and apprehending Thy truth, man needeth not man as his director <a name="fr_1374"></a><a href="#fn_1374"><font size="1">1375</font></a>
  that he may imitate his kind; but by Thy direction proveth what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect will of Thine. And Thou teachest him, now made capable, to perceive the Trinity of the Unity, and the Unity of the Trinity. And therefore this being said in the plural, “Let us make man,” it is yet subjoined in the singular, “and God made man;” and this being said in the plural, “after our likeness,” is subjoined in the singular, “after the image of God.” <a name="fr_1375"></a><a href="#fn_1375"><font size="1">1376</font></a>
  Thus is man renewed in the knowledge of God, after the image of Him that created him; <a name="fr_1376"></a><a href="#fn_1376"><font size="1">1377</font></a>
  and being made spiritual, he judgeth all things,—all things that are to be judged,—“yet he himself is judged of no man.” <a name="fr_1377"></a><a href="#fn_1377"><font size="1">1378</font></a>
 </p>
