 <p> <a name="vi.VII.XVIII"></a>  
 </p><p class="c41"><a name="vi.VII.XVIII-p1"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.VII.XVIII-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.—Jesus Christ, the Mediator, is the Only Way of Safety.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.VII.XVIII-p2"></a>24. And I sought a way of acquiring strength sufficient to enjoy Thee; but I found it not until I embraced that “Mediator between God and man, the man Christ Jesus,” <a name="fr_550"></a><a href="#fn_550"><font size="1">551</font></a>
  “who is over all, God blessed for ever,” <a name="fr_551"></a><a href="#fn_551"><font size="1">552</font></a>
  calling unto me, and saying, “I am the way, the truth, and the life,” <a name="fr_552"></a><a href="#fn_552"><font size="1">553</font></a>
  and mingling that food which I was unable to receive with our flesh. For “the Word was made flesh,” <a name="fr_553"></a><a href="#fn_553"><font size="1">554</font></a>
  that Thy wisdom, by which Thou createdst all things, might provide milk for our infancy. For I did not grasp my Lord Jesus,—I, though humbled, grasped not the humble One; <a name="fr_554"></a><a href="#fn_554"><font size="1">555</font></a>
  nor did I know what lesson that infirmity of His would teach us. For Thy Word, the Eternal Truth, pre-eminent above the higher parts of Thy creation, raises up those that are subject unto Itself; but in this lower world built for Itself a humble habitation of our clay, whereby He intended to abase from themselves such as would be subjected and bring them over unto Himself, allaying their swelling, and fostering their love; to the end that they might go on no further in self-confidence, but rather should become weak, seeing before their feet the Divinity weak by taking our “coats of skins;” <a name="fr_555"></a><a href="#fn_555"><font size="1">556</font></a>
  and wearied, might cast themselves down upon It, and It rising, might lift them up.</p>
