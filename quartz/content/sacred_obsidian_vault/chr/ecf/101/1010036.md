 <p class="c41"><a name="vi.II_1.I-p2"></a><span class="c2"><a name="vi.II_1.I-p2.1"></a>Chapter I.—He Deplores the Wickedness of His Youth.</span></p>  
 <p class="c10"><a name="vi.II_1.I-p3"></a>1. <span class="c9"><a name="vi.II_1.I-p3.1"></a>I Will</span> now call to mind my past foulness, and the carnal corruptions of my soul, not because I love them, but that I may love Thee, O my God. For love of Thy love do I it, recalling, in the very bitterness of my remembrance, my most vicious ways, that Thou mayest grow sweet to me,—Thou sweetness without deception! Thou sweetness happy and assured!—and re-collecting myself out of that my dissipation, in which I was torn to pieces, while, turned away from Thee the One, I lost myself among many vanities. For I even longed in my youth formerly to be satisfied with worldly things, and I dared to grow wild again with various and shadowy loves; my form consumed away, <a name="fr_186"></a><a href="#fn_186"><font size="1">187</font></a>
  and I became corrupt in Thine eyes, pleasing myself, and eager to please in the eyes of men.</p>
