 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXV</span>.—<i>How he ordered the Erection of a Church at Jerusalem, in the Holy Place of our Saviours Resurrection.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxv-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xxv-p2.1"></a>After</span> these things, the pious emperor addressed himself to another work truly worthy of record, in the province of Palestine. What <a name="page_527"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 527</font></a> <a name="iv.vi.iii.xxv-Page_527"></a>then was this work? He judged it incumbent on him to render the blessed locality of our Saviours resurrection an object of attraction and veneration to all. He issued immediate injunctions, therefore, for the erection in that spot of a house of prayer: and this he did, not on the mere natural impulse of his own mind, but being moved in spirit by the Saviour himself.</p>
