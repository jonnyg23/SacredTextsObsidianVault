 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xi-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI</span>.—<i>Silence of the Council, after Some Words by the Bishop Eusebius.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xi-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iii.xi-p2.1"></a>The</span> bishop who occupied the chief place in the right division of the assembly <a name="fr_3247"></a><a href="#fn_3247"><font size="1">3248</font></a>
  then rose, and, addressing the emperor, delivered a concise speech, in a strain of thanksgiving to Almighty God on his behalf. When he had resumed his seat, silence ensued, and all regarded the emperor with fixed attention; on which he looked serenely round on the assembly with a cheerful aspect, and, having collected his thoughts, in a calm and gentle tone gave utterance to the following words.</p>
