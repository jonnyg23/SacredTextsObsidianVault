 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxiv-p1"></a>  <a name="page_489"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 489</font></a> <a name="iv.vi.i.xxiv-Page_489"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIV</span>.—<i>It was by the Will of God that Constantine became possessed of the Empire.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxiv-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.i.xxiv-p2.1"></a>Thus</span> then the God of all, the Supreme Governor of the whole universe, by his own will appointed Constantine, the descendant of so renowned a parent, to be prince and sovereign: so that, while others have been raised to this distinction by the election of their fellow-men, he is the only one to whose elevation no mortal may boast of having contributed.</p>
