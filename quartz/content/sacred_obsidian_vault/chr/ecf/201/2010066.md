 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iii.vii.xxv-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iii.vii.xxv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXIV.—</span><i>Annianus the First Bishop of the Church of Alexandria after Mark.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.vii.xxv-p2"></a>1. <span class="c13"><a name="iii.vii.xxv-p2.1"></a>When</span> Nero was in the eighth year of his reign, <a name="fr_532"></a><a href="#fn_532"><font size="1">533</font></a>
  Annianus <a name="fr_533"></a><a href="#fn_533"><font size="1">534</font></a>
  succeeded Mark the evangelist in the administration of the parish of Alexandria. <a name="fr_534"></a><a href="#fn_534"><font size="1">535</font></a>
 </p>

 <a name="fn_533"></a><a href="2010066.htm#fr_533">128:534</a> <a name="iii.vii.xxv-p3.2"></a>
 <p class="endnote"><a name="iii.vii.xxv-p4"></a> Annianus, according to Bk. III. chap. 14, below, held his office twenty-two years. In <i>Apost. Const.</i> VII. 46 he is said to have been ordained by Mark as the first bishop of Alexandria. The <i>Chron. Orient.</i> 89 (according to Westcott in the <i>Dict. of Christ. Biog.</i>) reports that he was appointed by Mark after he had performed a miracle upon him. He is commemorated in the Roman martyrology with St. Mark, on April 25.</p>
