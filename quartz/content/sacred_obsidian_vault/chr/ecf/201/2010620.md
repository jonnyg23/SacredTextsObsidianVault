 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.iv.xxxiv-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.xxxiv-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXIV</span>.—<i>That he wrote to Eusebius respecting Easter, and respecting Copies of the Holy Scriptures.</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.iv.xxxiv-p2"></a><span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.iv.xxxiv-p2.1"></a>Ever</span> careful for the welfare of the churches of God, the emperor addressed me personally in a letter on the means of providing copies of the inspired oracles, and also on the subject of the most holy feast of Easter. For I had myself dedicated to him an exposition of the mystical import of that feast; and the manner in which he honored me with a reply may be understood by any one who reads the following letter.</p>
