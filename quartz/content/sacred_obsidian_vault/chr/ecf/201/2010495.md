 </p><p class="c47"><a name="iv.vi.ii.l-p1"></a>  <span class="c11"><a name="iv.vi.ii.l-p1.1"></a>Chapter L</span>.—<i>That the Persecution originated on Account of the Oracle of Apollo, who, it was said, could not give Oracles because of “the Righteous Men.”</i></p>  
 <p class="c18"><a name="iv.vi.ii.l-p2"></a>“<span class="c13"><a name="iv.vi.ii.l-p2.1"></a>About</span> that time it is said that Apollo spoke from a deep and gloomy cavern, and through the medium of no human voice, and declared that <i>the righteous men</i> on earth were a bar to his speaking the truth, and accordingly that the oracles from the tripod were fallacious. Hence it was that he suffered his tresses to droop in token of grief, <a name="fr_3198"></a><a href="#fn_3198"><font size="1">3199</font></a>
  and mourned the evils which the loss of the oracular spirit would entail on mankind. But let us mark the consequences of this.</p>
