 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p1"></a> <a name="page_405"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 405</font></a> <a name="xiv.iii.xcv-Page_405"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p1.1"></a>Canon XCIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p2.1"></a>The</span> canon subjects to penalties those who take heathen oaths, and we decree to them excommunication.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XCIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p5"></a>Whoever uses Gentile oaths, is worthy of punishment, for he is cut off.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p6"></a>The reference is to canon lxxxj. of St. Basils canons.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p7.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xcv-p8"></a>Tertullian (<i>De Idolatria</i>, cap. xx.) supposes that to swear by the false gods of the Gentiles, contains in itself some idolatry, an opinion shared by St. Basil, comparing those using such oaths with them who betrayed Christ, and who are partakers of the talk of devils.</p>
