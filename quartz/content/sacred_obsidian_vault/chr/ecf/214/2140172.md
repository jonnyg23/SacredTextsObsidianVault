 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p1.1"></a>Canon V.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p2.1"></a>Ordinations</span> are not to be held in the presence of hearers.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon V.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p5"></a>Ordinations are not to be performed in the presence of hearers.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p6.1"></a>Balsamon.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p7"></a>This canon calls elections “laying on of hands,” and says that since in elections un<a name="page_127"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 127</font></a> <a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-Page_127"></a>worthy things are often said with regard to those who are elected, therefore they should not take place in the presence of any that might happen to come to hear.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p8"></a>Zonaras also agrees that election is here intended, but Aristenus dissents and makes the reference to ordinations properly so-called, as follows:</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p9.1"></a>Aristenus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.vi-p10"></a>The prayers of ordination are not to be said out loud so that they may be heard by the people.</p>
