 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p1.1"></a>Canon XXV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p2.1"></a>A subdeacon</span> must not give the Bread, nor bless the Cup.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p5"></a>A subdeacon may not give the bread and the cup.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p6.1"></a>Aristenus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p7"></a>Subdeacons are not allowed to perform the work of presbyters and deacons.  Wherefore they neither deliver the bread nor the cup to the people.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p8.1"></a>Hefele.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p9"></a>According to the Apostolic Constitutions, the communion was administered in the following manner:  the bishop gave to each the holy bread with the words:  “the Body of the Lord,” and the recipient said, “Amen.”  The deacon then gave the chalice with the words:  “the Blood of Christ, the chalice of life,” and the recipient again answered, “Amen.”  This giving of the chalice with the words:  “the Blood of Christ,” etc., is called in the canon of Laodicea a “blessing” (<span class="Greek"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p9.1"></a>εὐλογεῖν</span>).  The Greek commentator Aristenus in accordance with this, and quite rightly, gives the meaning of this canon.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxx-p10"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Diet. XCIII., c. xix.; but reads “Deacons” instead of “Subdeacons.”  The Roman Correctors point out the error.</p>
