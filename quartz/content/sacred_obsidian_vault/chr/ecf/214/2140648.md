 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p1.1"></a>Canon CXXIV.  (Greek cxxv.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p2"></a>Of bishops who shall lie with regard to Donatists communions.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p3.1"></a>If</span> it shall be proven that any bishop has lied concerning the communion of those [who had been Donatists], and had said that they had communicated when he knew it was an established fact that they had not done so, let him lose his bishoprick.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon CXXIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p6"></a>Whoso says that a man, whom he knows does not communicate, does communicate is to be deprived of his episcopate.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p7"></a>This is Canon xvj. of Carthage, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxv-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 418.</p>
