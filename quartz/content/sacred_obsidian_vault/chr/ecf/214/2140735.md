 </p><p class="c14"><a name="xvii.xv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xvii.xv-p1.1"></a>Of the Same to Diodorus Bishop of Tarsus, concerning a Man who had taken Two Sisters to Wife.</span></p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xvii.xv-p2"></a>(<i>Found translated in</i> lib. cit., <i>p</i>. 212 <i>et seqq.</i> Epistle clx.)</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.xv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.xv-p3.1"></a>Canon LXXXVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.xv-p4"></a>Contains the preface of his letter to Diodorus Bishop of Tarsus, in which he tells him of a letter shewed him in justification of a mans marrying two sisters bearing his name; but he hopes it was forged.</p> 
 <p class="c34"><a name="xvii.xv-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.xv-p5.1"></a>Canon LXXXVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.xv-p6"></a>Contains the rest of the letter, in which he argues and inveighs against this practice.</p>
