 </p><p class="c31"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p1"></a> [<span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p1.1"></a>After</span> <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p1.2"></a>Canon</span> XIV.]</p> 
 <p class="c14"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p2"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p2.1"></a>Canon XVIII.  (<i>Of the Latin</i>.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p3.1"></a>Bishop</span> <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p3.2"></a>Januarius</span> said:  Let your holiness also decree this, that no bishop be allowed to try to gain for himself a minister in the church of a bishop of another city and ordain him to one of his own parishes.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p4"></a>All said:  Such is our pleasure, inasmuch as discord is apt to spring from contentions in this matter, and therefore the sentence of us all forbids anyone to presume to do</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p5.1"></a>Note.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p6.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xv-p7"></a>It is manifest that these two canons [xviii. of the Latin and xv. of the Greek], contain the resolution of the same case, and therefore it is that the Greeks keep only the former which contains the decree of the synod, made on Hosiuss motion, the suggestion having been made by Januarius the bishop:  which suggestion makes the first of these canons.  [I.e. Latin canon xviij.]</p>
