 <p> <a name="xiv.iii.xc"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p1.1"></a>Canon LXXXIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p2.1"></a>The</span> faithful spending the days of the Salutatory Passion in fasting, praying and compunction of heart, ought to fast until the midnight of the Great Sabbath:  since the divine Evangelists, Matthew and Luke, have shewn us how late at night it was [that the resurrection took place], the one by using the words <span class="Greek"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p2.2"></a>ὀΨὲ σαββάτων</span>, and the other by the words <span class="Greek"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p2.3"></a>ὄρθρου βαθέος</span>.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p3.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xc-p4"></a><i>On the Great Sabbath the fast must be continued until midnight.</i></p>
