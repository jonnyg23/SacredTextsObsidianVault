 <p> <a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c14"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p1"></a> <a name="page_509"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 509</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-Page_509"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p1.1"></a>Canon CXXXVII.  (Continuation of the last in the Greek.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p2"></a><i>Here begin the examples of the Nicene Council, sent on the sixth day before the calends of December in the year 419,</i> <a name="fr_493"></a><a href="#fn_493"><font size="1">494</font></a>
 <i>after the consulate of the most glorious emperor Honorius for the <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p3.1"></a>XII</span>th time, and Theodosius for the <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p3.2"></a>IX</span>th time,</i> <a name="fr_494"></a><a href="#fn_494"><font size="1">495</font></a>
  <i>Augustuses, to Boniface the bishop of the City of Rome.</i></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p5.1"></a>We</span> believe in one God etc.…the Catholic and Apostolic Church anathematizes them. <a name="fr_495"></a><a href="#fn_495"><font size="1">496</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p7"></a>To this symbol of the faith there were also annexed copies of the statutes of the same Nicene Councils from the aforenamed pontiffs, in all respects as are contained above; which we do not think it necessary to write out here again.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p8"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p8.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p9.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon CXXXVII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p10"></a>The Canons of the Synod of Nice are sent, as they were decreed by the Fathers, in accordance with your letters.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.cxxxviii-p11"></a>[Here follows the Nicene Creed in full.]</p>
