 <p> <a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p1.1"></a>Canon XVI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p2"></a>(<i>Greek</i>.)</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p3.1"></a>Bishop</span> <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p3.2"></a>Aëtius</span> said:  Ye are not ignorant how important and how large is the metropolitan city of Thessalonica.  Accordingly presbyters and deacons often come to it from other provinces and, not content with staying a short time, remain and make it their permanent place of residence, or are compelled with difficulty and after a very long delay to return to their own churches.  A decree should be made bearing on this matter.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p4"></a>Bishop Hosius said:  Let those decrees which have been made in the case of bishops, be observed as to these persons also.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p5"></a>(<i>Latin</i>.)</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p6.1"></a>Bishop</span> <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p6.2"></a>Aëtius</span> said:  Ye are not ignorant how large and important is the city of Thessalonica.  Presbyters and deacons often come to it from other regions, and are not content to remain a short time, but either make their residence there or at least are with difficulty compelled to return after a long interval to their own place.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p7"></a>All said:  Those limits of time which have been decreed in the case of bishops ought to be observed as to these persons also.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p8"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p8.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p9.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XVI.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p10"></a>What things have been decreed for bishops with regard to the length of their absence, applies also to presbyters and deacons.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p11"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p11.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iii.iv.xvii-p12"></a>This canon needs no explanation.</p>
