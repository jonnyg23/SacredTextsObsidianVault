 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p1.1"></a>Canon XXIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p2.1"></a>Christians</span> must not judaize by resting on the Sabbath, but must work on that day, rather honouring the Lords Day; and, if they can, resting then as Christians.  But if any shall be found to be judaizers, let them be anathema from Christ.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXIX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p5"></a>A Christian shall not stop work on the Sabbath, but on the Lords Day.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p6.1"></a>Balsamon.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-p7"></a>Here the Fathers order that no one of the faithful shall stop work on the Sabbath as do <a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 149</font></a> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxxiv-Page_149"></a>the Jews, but that they should honour the Lords Day, on account of the Lords resurrection, and that on that day they should abstain from manual labour and go to church.  But thus abstaining from work on Sunday they do not lay down as a necessity, but they add, “if they can.”  For if through need or any other necessity any one worked on the Lords day this was not reckoned against him.</p>
