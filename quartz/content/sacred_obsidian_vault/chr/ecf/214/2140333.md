 <p> <a name="xi.xviii.xv"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p1.1"></a>Canon XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p2"></a>A <span class="sc"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p2.1"></a>woman</span> shall not receive the laying on of hands as a deaconess under forty years of age, and then only after searching examination.  And if, after she has had hands laid on her and has continued for a time to minister, she shall despise the grace of God and give herself in marriage, she shall be anathematized and the man united to her.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p5"></a>No person shall be ordained deaconess except she be forty years of age.  If she shall dishonour her ministry by contracting a marriage, let her be anathema.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p6"></a>This canon should be read carefully in connexion with what is said in the Excursus on deaconesses to canon xix. of Nice.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xi.xviii.xv-p7"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars II., Causa XXVII, Quæst. I., Canon xxiij.</p>
