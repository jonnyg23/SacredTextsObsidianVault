 </p><p class="c51"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p1.1"></a>XII. <a name="fr_607"></a><a href="#fn_607"><font size="1">608</font></a>
 </span></p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p3.1"></a>The Encyclical Letter of Gennadius, Patriarch of Constantinople and of the Holy Synod Met with Him to All the Holy Metropolitans and to the Pope of the City of Rome. <a name="fr_608"></a><a href="#fn_608"><font size="1">609</font></a>
 </span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p5"></a>To the most beloved of God, fellow-minister, Gennadius and the most holy synod assembled in the royal city which is New Rome, sendeth greeting.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p6"></a>As our Lord without money and without price ordained his Apostles, so should we ordain the clergy, for the Lord has placed us in their grade and in their stead (<span class="Greek"><a name="xvii.xxxii-p6.1"></a>ἐις τὸν ἐκείνων βαθηόν τε καὶ τόπον</span>).  Nor should we use any ingenious sophisms to avoid this plain duty, explicitly laid upon us, not only by the words of the Gospel but also by a canon of the great Ecumenical Synod of Chalcedon.</p>
