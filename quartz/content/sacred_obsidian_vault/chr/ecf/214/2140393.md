 <p> <a name="xiv.iii.x"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p1.1"></a>Canon IX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p2.1"></a>Let</span> no cleric be permitted to keep a “public house.”  For if it be not permitted to enter a tavern, much more is it forbidden to serve others in it and to carry on a trade which is unlawful for him.  But if he shall have done any such thing, either let him desist or be deposed.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon IX.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p5"></a>If clerics are forbidden to enter public houses, much more are they forbidden to keep them.  Let them either give them up or be deposed.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.x-p6"></a>Compare with this canon liv. of the Apostolic Canons; xxiv. of Laodicea; and xliij. of the Synod of Carthage. <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">358</font></a>
 </p>
