 </p><p class="c14"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p1.1"></a>Canon LXXIV.  (Greek lxxvii.)</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p2"></a>That no bishop who is an intercessor is to hold the see where he is intercessor.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p3.1"></a>Item</span>, it has been decreed that it is not lawful to any intercessor to retain the see to which he has been appointed as intercessor, by any popular movements and seditions; but let him take care that within a year he provide them with a bishop:  but if he shall neglect to do so, when the year is done, another intercessor shall be appointed.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXIV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p6"></a>It seemed good that the bishop who had been called in as an intercessor, by the zeal and dissensions of the people, should not be allowed to become the occupant of its throne:  but let a bishop be provided within a year, or else in the next year let another intercessor be appointed.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p7"></a>This is Canon IX. of Carthage, September, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p7.1"></a>a.d.</span> 401.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p8"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p8.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.lxxv-p9"></a>We here call this officer “Guardian of the spiritualities” in the vacancy of the see.</p>
