 <p> <a name="xiv.iii.xxxix"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p1.1"></a>Canon XXXVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p2.1"></a>The</span> canon which was made by the Fathers we also observe, which thus decreed:  If any city be renewed by imperial authority, or shall have been renewed, let the order of things ecclesiastical follow the civil and public models.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXVIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p5"></a>If any city is or shall be renewed by the Emperor, the ecclesiastical order shall follow the political and public example.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p6.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p7"></a>The canon of the Fathers which the Synod wishes observed is XVII of Chalcedon, the notes on which see.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xiv.iii.xxxix-p8"></a>Here it must be noted that by “civil and public models” is signified the “pragmatic” or imperial letters, by which the emperors granted to newly raised up or re-edified towns the privilege of other cities, or else annexed them to some Province.</p>
