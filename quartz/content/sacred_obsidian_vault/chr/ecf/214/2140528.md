 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p1"></a> <a name="page_445"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 445</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.v-Page_445"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p1.1"></a>Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p2"></a>Of the different orders that should abstain from their wives.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p3.1"></a>Faustinus</span>, the bishop of the Potentine Church, in the province of Picenum, a legate of the Roman Church, said:  It seems good that a bishop, a presbyter, and a deacon, or whoever perform the sacraments, should be keepers of modesty and should abstain from their wives.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p4"></a>By all the bishops it was said:  It is right that all who serve the altar should keep pudicity from all women.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p5.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p6.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p7"></a>Let those who pray abstain from their wives that they may obtain their petitions.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p8"></a>This canon is taken from Canon ij., of Carthage 387 or 390, last mentioned.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p9.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.v-p10"></a>See Canon XXV.  “Abstain from their wives,” i.e. Some time before and after the Eucharist, as the old Scholiasts understand it.  [i.e. the Greek scholiasts, but see notes to Canon xiij. of Quinisext.]</p>
