 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p1"></a> <a name="page_364"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 364</font></a> <a name="xiv.iii.iv-Page_364"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p1.1"></a>Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p2.1"></a>If</span> any bishop, presbyter, deacon, sub-deacon, lector, cantor, or door-keeper has had intercourse with a woman dedicated to God, let him be deposed, as one who has corrupted a spouse of Christ, but if a layman let him be cut off.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p5"></a>A cleric coupled to a spouse of God shall be deposed.  In the case of a layman he shall be cut off.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p6"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars II., Causa XXVII., Q. I., c. vj.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.iv-p7"></a>A layman ravishing a nun, by secular law was punished by death.  Balsamon gives the reference thus:  V Cap. primi tit. iiij. lib. Basilic. or cxxiij. Novel.</p>
