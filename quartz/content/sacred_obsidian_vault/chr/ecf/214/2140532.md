 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p1.1"></a>Canon VIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p2"></a>Of those who make accusation against an elder; and that no criminal is to be suffered to bring a charge against a bishop.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p3.1"></a>Numidius</span>, the bishop of Maxula, said:  Moreover, there are very many, not of good life, who think that their elders or bishops should be the butt for accusation; ought such to be easily admitted or no?  Aurelius the bishop said:  Is it the pleasure of your charity that he who is ensnared by divers wickednesses should have no voice of accusation against these?</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p4"></a>All the bishops said:  If he is criminous, his accusation is not to be received.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p5"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p5.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p6.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon VIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p7"></a>It has seemed good that they who are themselves defendants for crimes should not bring accusations; nor should they be allowed to lay crimes to anyones charge.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p8"></a>This is Canon vi. of Genethliuss Synod at Carthage, <span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p8.1"></a>a.d.</span> 387 or 390.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p9"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p9.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p10"></a>See Canons 132 and 133 and Constantinople Canon 6.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p11"></a>[The “elders” mentioned in this canon are] probably the same with <i>senes</i> in other canons, viz., Metropolitans, as is generally believed.  The Latin here calls them <i>Majores</i> <a name="page_447"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 447</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-Page_447"></a><i>natu</i>, the Greek <span class="Greek"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ix-p11.1"></a>πατέρας</span>.  Bishop Beveridge supposes that the word denotes bishop, though perhaps <i>Majores natu</i> may signify presbyters.  Justellus on the canon produces some seeming authorities for this.</p>
