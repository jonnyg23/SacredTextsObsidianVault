 <p> <a name="xiv.iii.xi"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p1.1"></a>Canon X.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p2"></a>A <span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p2.1"></a>bishop</span>, or presbyter, or deacon who receives usury, or what is called <i>hecatostæ</i>, let him desist or be deposed.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon X.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p5"></a>A bishop, presbyter, or deacon who takes usury shall be deposed unless he stops doing so.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xiv.iii.xi-p6"></a>See notes on canon XVI. of Nice, and the Excursus thereto appended.</p>
