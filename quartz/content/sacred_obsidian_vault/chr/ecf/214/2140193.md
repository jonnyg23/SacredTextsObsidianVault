 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p1.1"></a>Canon XXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p2.1"></a>The</span> readers and singers have no right to wear an orarium, and to read or sing thus [habited].</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXIII.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p5"></a>Cantors and lectors shall not wear the orarium.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p6"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p6.1"></a>Van Espen.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p7"></a>Rightly Zonoras here remarks, “for the same reason (that they should not seem to wish to usurp a ministry not their own) it <a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 144</font></a> <a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-Page_144"></a>is not permitted to these to wear the stole, for readers are for the work of reading, and singers for singing,” so each one should perform his own office.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.xxvii-p8"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Dist. xxiii., can. xxviij.</p>
