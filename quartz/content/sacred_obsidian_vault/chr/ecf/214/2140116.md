 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p1.1"></a>Canon V.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p2.1"></a>If</span> any one shall teach that the house of God and the assemblies held therein are to be despised, let him be anathema.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon V.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p5"></a>Whoso styles the house of God contemptible, let him be anathema.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p6"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Dist. xxx., c. x.  The commentators find nothing to say upon the canon, and in fact the despising of the worship of Gods true church is and always has been so common a sin, that it hardly calls for comment; no one will forget that the Prophet Malachi complains how in his days there were those who deemed “the table of the Lord contemptible” and said of his worship “what a weariness is it.”  (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal001.htm#007">Mal. 1:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal001.htm#013">13</a><a name="viii.v.iv.v-p6.1"></a>.)</p>
