 <p> <a name="viii.vii.iii.v"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p1"></a> <span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p1.1"></a>Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p2.1"></a>They</span> who are of the sacerdotal order ought not to lend and receive usury, nor what is called hemioliæ.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p3"></a><span class="c4"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p3.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p4"></a><span class="sc"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p4.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon IV.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p5"></a>A priest is not to receive usury nor hemioliæ.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p6"></a>The same rule is laid down in the seventeenth Canon of Nice.  For a treatment of the whole subject of usury see excursus to that canon.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p7"></a>Dionysius Exiguus and Isidore have numbered this canon v., and our fifth they have as iv.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="viii.vii.iii.v-p8"></a>This canon is found in the <i>Corpus Juris Canonici</i>, Gratians <i>Decretum</i>, Pars I., Dist. XLVI., can. ix.</p>
