 </p><p class="c34"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p1"></a> <a name="page_444"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 444</font></a> <a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-Page_444"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p1.1"></a>Canon I.</span></p> 
 <p class="c50"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p2"></a>That the statutes of the Nicene Council are to be scrupulously observed.</p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p3"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p3.1"></a>Aurelius</span> the bishop said:  Such are the statutes of the Nicene Council, which our fathers at that time brought back with them:  and preserving this form, let these things which follow, adopted and confirmed by us, be kept firm.</p> 
 <p class="c31"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p4"></a><span class="c4"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p4.1"></a>Notes.</span></p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p5"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p5.1"></a>Ancient Epitome of Canon I.</span></p> 
 <p class="c35"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p6"></a>Let the copies of the decrees of the Nicene Council which our fathers brought back with them from that synod, be observed.</p> 
 <p class="c36"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p7"></a><span class="sc"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p7.1"></a>Johnson.</span></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="xv.iv.iv.ii-p8"></a>It is certain that Cæcilian, then Bishop of Carthage, was present at the Council of Nice; that any other African bishop was there does not appear; but probably he was attended with several clergyman, who were afterwards ordained bishops.</p>
