 <p> <a name="iv.ii.ii.v"></a> </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p0.1"></a>Chapter IV.</h4>  
 <p class="c32"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p1"></a>The Holy Spirit is one and the same Who spake in the prophets and apostles, Who is the Spirit of God and of Christ; Whom, further, Scripture designates the Paraclete, and the Spirit of life and truth.</p>  
 <p class="c22"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p2"></a>55. <span class="sc"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p2.1"></a>But</span> no one will doubt that the Spirit is one, although very many have doubted whether God be one. For many heretics have said that the God of the Old Testament is one, and the God of the New Testament is another. But as the Father is one Who both spake of old, as we read, to the fathers by the prophets, and to us in the last days by His Son; <a name="fr_849"></a><a href="#fn_849"><font size="1">850</font></a>
  “and as the Son is one, Who according to the tenour of the Old Testament was offended by Adam, <a name="fr_850"></a><a href="#fn_850"><font size="1">851</font></a>
  seen by Abraham, <a name="fr_851"></a><a href="#fn_851"><font size="1">852</font></a>
  worshipped by Jacob; <a name="fr_852"></a><a href="#fn_852"><font size="1">853</font></a>
  so, too, the Holy Spirit is one, who energized in the prophets, <a name="fr_853"></a><a href="#fn_853"><font size="1">854</font></a>
  was breathed upon the apostles, <a name="fr_854"></a><a href="#fn_854"><font size="1">855</font></a>
  and was joined to the Father and the Son in the sacrament of baptism. <a name="fr_855"></a><a href="#fn_855"><font size="1">856</font></a>
  For David says of Him: “And take not Thy Holy Spirit from me.” <a name="fr_856"></a><a href="#fn_856"><font size="1">857</font></a>
  And in another place he said of Him: “Whither shall I go from Thy Spirit?” <a name="fr_857"></a><a href="#fn_857"><font size="1">858</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p12"></a><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a> <a name="iv.ii.ii.v-Page_101"></a>56. That you may know that the Spirit of God is the same as the Holy Spirit, as we read also in the Apostle: “No one speaking in the Spirit of God says Anathema to Jesus and no one can say, Lord Jesus, but in the Holy Spirit,” <a name="fr_858"></a><a href="#fn_858"><font size="1">859</font></a>
  the Apostle calls Him the Spirit of God. He called Him also the Spirit of Christ, as you read: “But ye are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if so be that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you.” <a name="fr_859"></a><a href="#fn_859"><font size="1">860</font></a>
  And farther on: “But if the Spirit of Him Who raised Jesus from the dead dwelleth in you.” <a name="fr_860"></a><a href="#fn_860"><font size="1">861</font></a>
  The same is, then, the Spirit of God, Who is the Spirit of Christ.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p16"></a>57. The same is also the Spirit of Life, as the Apostle says: “For the law of the Spirit of Life in Christ Jesus hath delivered me from the law of sin and death.” <a name="fr_861"></a><a href="#fn_861"><font size="1">862</font></a>
 </p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p18"></a>58. Him, then, Whom the Apostle called the Spirit of Life, the Lord in the Gospel named the Paraclete, and the Spirit of Truth, as you find: “And I will ask the Father, and He will give you another Comforter [Paraclete], that He may be with you for ever, even the Spirit of Truth, Whom this world cannot receive; because it seeth Him not, neither knoweth Him.” <a name="fr_862"></a><a href="#fn_862"><font size="1">863</font></a>
  You have, then, the Paraclete Spirit, called also the Spirit of Truth, and the invisible Spirit. How, then, do some think that the Son is visible in His Divine Nature, when the world cannot see even the Spirit?</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p20"></a>59. Receive now the saying of the Lord, that the same is the Holy Spirit Who is the Spirit of Truth, for you read in the end of this book: “Receive the Holy Spirit.” <a name="fr_863"></a><a href="#fn_863"><font size="1">864</font></a>
  And Peter teaches that the same is the Holy Spirit Who is the Spirit of the Lord, when he says: “Ananias, why has it seemed good to thee to tempt and to lie to the Holy Spirit?” <a name="fr_864"></a><a href="#fn_864"><font size="1">865</font></a>
  And immediately after he says again to the wife of Ananias: “Why has it seemed good to you to tempt the Spirit of the Lord?” <a name="fr_865"></a><a href="#fn_865"><font size="1">866</font></a>
  When he says “to you,” he shows that he is speaking of the same Spirit of Whom he had spoken to Ananias. He Himself is, then, the Spirit of the Lord Who is the Holy Spirit.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p24"></a>60. And the Lord Himself made clear that the same Who is the Spirit of the Father is the Holy Spirit, when according to Matthew He said that we ought not to take thought in persecution what we should say: “For it is not ye that speak, but the Spirit of your Father that speaketh in you.” <a name="fr_866"></a><a href="#fn_866"><font size="1">867</font></a>
  Again He says according to St. Luke: “Be not anxious how ye shall answer or speak, for the Holy Spirit of God shall teach you in that hour what ye ought to say.” <a name="fr_867"></a><a href="#fn_867"><font size="1">868</font></a>
  So, although many are called spirits, as it is said: “Who maketh His Angels spirits,” yet the Spirit of God is but one.</p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="iv.ii.ii.v-p27"></a>61. Both apostles and prophets received that one Spirit, as the vessel of election, the Doctor of the Gentiles, says: “For we have all drunk of one Spirit;” <a name="fr_868"></a><a href="#fn_868"><font size="1">869</font></a>
  Him, as it were, Who cannot be divided, but is poured into souls, and flows into the senses, that He may quench the burning of this worlds thirst.</p>

He is always good, since He is given by the Father and the Son; neither is He to be numbered amongst such things as are said to fail. He must be acknowledged as the source of goodness. The Spirit of God's mouth, the amender of evils, and Himself good.
