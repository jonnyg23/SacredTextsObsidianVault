 <p> <a name="iv.iii.xi.xii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.xi.xii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.xi.xii-p1"></a>Several testimonies against vainglory.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.xi.xii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.xi.xii-p2.1"></a>And</span> so the Apostle warns us: “Be not desirous of vainglory.” <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">1012</font></a>
  And the Lord, rebuking the Pharisees, says, “How can ye believe, who receive glory from one another, and seek not the glory which comes from God alone?” <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">1013</font></a>
  Of these too the blessed David speaks with a threat: “For God hath scattered the bones of them that please men.” <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">1014</font></a>
 </p>
