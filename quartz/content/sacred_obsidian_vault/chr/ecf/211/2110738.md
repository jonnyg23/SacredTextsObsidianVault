 <p> <a name="iv.iv.viii.xxix"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xxix-p0.1"></a>Chapter XXIX.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xxix-p1"></a>An objection, asking why those who are tormented by unclean spirits are separated from the Lords communion.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xxix-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.viii.xxix-p2.1"></a>Germanus</span>:  And how is it that we see them not only scorned and shunned by everybody, but actually always kept away from the Lords communion in our provinces, in accordance with these words of the gospel: “Give not that which is holy to the dogs, neither cast your pearls before swine;” <a name="fr_1497"></a><a href="#fn_1497"><font size="1">1498</font></a>
  while you tell us that somehow we ought to hold that the humiliation of this temptation is brought upon them with a view to their purification and profit?</p>
