 <p> <a name="iv.iv.vi.ii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iv.vi.ii-p0.1"></a>Chapter II.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iv.vi.ii-p1"></a>Abbot Serapions enumeration of eight principal faults.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iv.vi.ii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iv.vi.ii-p2.1"></a>There</span> are eight principal faults which attack mankind; viz., first gastrimargia, which means gluttony, secondly fornication, thirdly philargyria, i.e., avarice or the love of money, fourthly anger, fifthly dejection, sixthly acedia, i.e., listlessness or low spirits, seventhly cenodoxia, i.e., boasting or vain glory; and eighthly pride.</p>
