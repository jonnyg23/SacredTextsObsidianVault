 <p> <a name="iv.iii.ii.xviii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.iii.ii.xviii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.iii.ii.xviii-p1"></a>How they do not kneel from the evening of Saturday till the evening of Sunday.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.iii.ii.xviii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.iii.ii.xviii-p2.1"></a>This</span>, too, we ought to know,—that from the evening of Saturday which precedes the Sunday, <a name="fr_711"></a><a href="#fn_711"><font size="1">712</font></a>
  up to the following evening, among the Egyptians they never kneel, nor from Easter to Whitsuntide; <a name="fr_712"></a><a href="#fn_712"><font size="1">713</font></a>
  nor do they at these times observe a rule of fasting, <a name="fr_713"></a><a href="#fn_713"><font size="1">714</font></a>
  the reason for which shall be explained in its proper place in the Conferences of the Elders, <a name="fr_714"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">715</font></a>
  if the Lord permits. At present we only propose to run through the causes very briefly, lest our book exceed its due limits and prove tiresome or burdensome to the reader.</p>
