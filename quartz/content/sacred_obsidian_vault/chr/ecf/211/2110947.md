 <p> <a name="iv.v.viii.xxvii"></a>  </p><h4 align="center"><a name="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p0.1"></a>Chapter XXVII.</h4>  
 <p class="subh"><a name="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p1"></a>A question whether the saying: “I have sworn and am purposed” is opposed to the view given above.</p>  
 <p class="skip"><a name="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p2"></a><span class="sc"><a name="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p2.1"></a>Germanus</span>: So far as this consideration is concerned which has been clearly and fully treated of, a monk ought never to determine anything for fear lest he turn out a breaker of his word or else obstinate. And what then can we make of this saying of the Psalmist: “I have sworn and am purposed to keep Thy righteous judgments?” <a name="fr_2065"></a><a href="#fn_2065"><font size="1">2066</font></a>
  What is “to swear and purpose” except to keep ones determinations fixedly?</p>
