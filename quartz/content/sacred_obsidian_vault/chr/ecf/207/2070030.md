 <p> <a name="ii.xviii"></a>
 </p><p class="c39"><a name="ii.xviii-p1"></a>  <a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 94</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_94"></a><span class="c21"><a name="ii.xviii-p1.1"></a>Lecture XIV.</span></p>  
 <p class="c37"><a name="ii.xviii-p2"></a><span class="c1"><a name="ii.xviii-p2.1"></a>On the Words, And Rose Again from the Dead on the Third Day, and Ascended into the Heavens, and Sat on the Right Hand of the Father.</span></p>  
 <p class="c43"><a name="ii.xviii-p3"></a><a name="ii.xviii-p3.1"></a><span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p3.2"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#001">1 Cor. xv. 1-4</a><a name="ii.xviii-p3.3"></a></span></p>  
 <p class="c42"><a name="ii.xviii-p4"></a><i>Now I make known unto you, brethren, the gospel which I preached unto you</i>….<i>that He hath been raised on the third day according to the Scriptures, &amp;c.</i></p>  
 <p class="c23"><a name="ii.xviii-p5"></a><i>Rejoice, O Jerusalem, and keep high festival, all ye that love Jesus;</i> for He is risen.  Rejoice, all ye that mourned before <a name="fr_1654"></a><a href="#fn_1654"><font size="1">1655</font></a>
 , when ye heard of the daring and wicked deeds of the Jews:  for He who was spitefully entreated of them in this place is risen again.  And as the discourse concerning the Cross was a sorrowful one, so let the good tidings of the Resurrection bring joy to the hearers.  Let mourning be turned into gladness, and lamentation to joy:  and let our mouth be filled with joy and gladness, because of Him, who after His resurrection, said Rejoice <a name="fr_1655"></a><a href="#fn_1655"><font size="1">1656</font></a>
 .  For I know the sorrow of Christs friends in these past days; because, as our discourse stopped short at the Death and the Burial, and did not tell the good tidings of the Resurrection, your mind was in suspense, to hear what you were longing for.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p8"></a>Now, therefore, the Dead is risen, He who was <i>free among the dead</i> <a name="fr_1656"></a><a href="#fn_1656"><font size="1">1657</font></a>
 , and the deliverer of the dead.  He who in dishonour wore patiently the crown of thorns, even He arose, and crowned Himself with the diadem of His victory over death.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p10"></a>2.  As then we set forth the testimonies concerning His Cross, so come let us now verify the proofs of His Resurrection also:  since the Apostle before us <a name="fr_1657"></a><a href="#fn_1657"><font size="1">1658</font></a>
  affirms, <i>He was buried, and has been raised on the third day according to the Scriptures.</i>  As an Apostle, therefore, has sent us back to the testimonies of the Scriptures, it is good that we should get full knowledge of the hope of our salvation; and that we should learn first whether the divine Scriptures tell us the season of His resurrection, whether it comes in summer or in autumn, or after winter; and from what kind of place the Saviour has risen, and what has been announced in the admirable Prophets as the name of the place of the Resurrection, and whether the women, who sought and found Him not, afterwards rejoice at finding Him; in order that when the Gospels are read, the narratives of these holy Scriptures may not be thought fables nor rhapsodies.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p12"></a>3.  That the Saviour then was buried, ye have heard distinctly in the preceding discourse, as Isaiah saith, His burial shall be in peace <a name="fr_1658"></a><a href="#fn_1658"><font size="1">1659</font></a>
 :  for in His burial He made peace between heaven and earth, bringing sinners unto God:  and, <i>that the righteous is taken out of the way of unrighteousness</i> <a name="fr_1659"></a><a href="#fn_1659"><font size="1">1660</font></a>
 :  and, <i>His burial shall be in peace</i>:  and, <i>I will give the wicked for His burial</i> <a name="fr_1660"></a><a href="#fn_1660"><font size="1">1661</font></a>
 .  There is also the prophecy of Jacob saying in the Scriptures, <i>He lay down and couched as a lion, and as a lions whelp</i>:  who shall rouse Him up <a name="fr_1661"></a><a href="#fn_1661"><font size="1">1662</font></a>
 ?  And the similar passage in Numbers, He couched, He lay down as a lion, and as a lions whelp <a name="fr_1662"></a><a href="#fn_1662"><font size="1">1663</font></a>
 .  The Psalm also ye have often heard, which says, <i>And Thou hast brought me down into the dust of death</i> <a name="fr_1663"></a><a href="#fn_1663"><font size="1">1664</font></a>
 .  Moreover we took note of the spot, when we quoted the words, <i>Look unto the rock, which ye have hewn</i> <a name="fr_1664"></a><a href="#fn_1664"><font size="1">1665</font></a>
 .  But now let the testimonies concerning His resurrection itself go with us on our way.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p20"></a>4.  First, then, in the 11th Psalm He says, <i>For the misery of the poor, and the sighing of the needy, now will I arise, saith the Lord</i> <a name="fr_1665"></a><a href="#fn_1665"><font size="1">1666</font></a>
 .  But this passage still remains doubtful with some:  for He often rises up also in anger <a name="fr_1666"></a><a href="#fn_1666"><font size="1">1667</font></a>
 , to take vengeance upon His enemies.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p23"></a>Come then to the 15th Psalm, which says distinctly:  <i>Preserve Me, <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p23.1"></a>O Lord</span>, for in Thee</i> <a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 95</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_95"></a><i>have I put my trust</i> <a name="fr_1667"></a><a href="#fn_1667"><font size="1">1668</font></a>
 :  and after this, <i>their assemblies of blood will I not join, nor make mention of their names between my lips</i> <a name="fr_1668"></a><a href="#fn_1668"><font size="1">1669</font></a>
 ; since they have refused me, and chosen Cæsar as their king <a name="fr_1669"></a><a href="#fn_1669"><font size="1">1670</font></a>
 :  and also the next words, <i>I foresaw the</i> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p26.2"></a>Lord</span> <i>alway before Me, because He is at My right hand, that I may not be moved</i> <a name="fr_1670"></a><a href="#fn_1670"><font size="1">1671</font></a>
 :  and soon after <i>Yea and even until night my reins chastened me</i> <a name="fr_1671"></a><a href="#fn_1671"><font size="1">1672</font></a>
 .  And after this He says most plainly, <i>For Thou wilt not leave My soul in hell</i> <a name="fr_1672"></a><a href="#fn_1672"><font size="1">1673</font></a>
 <i>; neither wilt Thou suffer Thine Holy One to see corruption</i>.  He said not, neither wilt Thou suffer Thine Holy One to see death, since then He would not have died; but <i>corruption</i>, saith He, I see not, and shall not abide in death.  <i>Thou hast made known to Me the ways of life</i> <a name="fr_1673"></a><a href="#fn_1673"><font size="1">1674</font></a>
 .  Behold here is plainly preached a life after death.  Come also to the 29th Psalm, <i>I will extol Thee, O</i> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p30.2"></a>Lord</span><i>, for Thou hast lifted Me up, and hast not made My foes to rejoice over Me</i> <a name="fr_1674"></a><a href="#fn_1674"><font size="1">1675</font></a>
 .  What is it that took place?  Wert thou rescued from enemies, or wert thou released when about to be smitten?  He says himself most plainly, <i>O <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p31.2"></a>Lord</span>, Thou hast brought up My soul from hell</i> <a name="fr_1675"></a><a href="#fn_1675"><font size="1">1676</font></a>
 .  There he says, Thou wilt not leave, prophetically:  and here he speaks of that which is to take place as having taken place, <i>Thou hast brought up.  Thou hast saved Me from them that go down into the pit</i> <a name="fr_1676"></a><a href="#fn_1676"><font size="1">1677</font></a>
 .  At what time shall the event occur?  <i>Weeping shall continue for the evening, and joy cometh in the morning</i> <a name="fr_1677"></a><a href="#fn_1677"><font size="1">1678</font></a>
 :  for in the evening was the sorrow of the disciplines, and in the morning the joy of the resurrection.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p35"></a>5.  But wouldst thou know the place also?  Again He saith in Canticles, <i>I went down into the garden of nuts</i> <a name="fr_1678"></a><a href="#fn_1678"><font size="1">1679</font></a>
 ; for it was a garden where He was crucified <a name="fr_1679"></a><a href="#fn_1679"><font size="1">1680</font></a>
 .  For though it has now been most highly adorned with royal gifts, yet formerly it was a garden, and the signs and the remnants of this remain.  <i>A garden enclosed, a fountain sealed</i> <a name="fr_1680"></a><a href="#fn_1680"><font size="1">1681</font></a>
 , by the Jews who said, <i>We remember that that deceiver said while He was yet alive, After three days, I will rise:  command, therefore, that the sepulchre be made sure; and further on, So they went, and made the sepulchre sure, sealing the stone with the guard</i> <a name="fr_1681"></a><a href="#fn_1681"><font size="1">1682</font></a>
 .  And aiming well at these, one saith, <i>and in rest Thou shalt judge them</i> <a name="fr_1682"></a><a href="#fn_1682"><font size="1">1683</font></a>
 .  But who is the fountain that is sealed, or who is interpreted as being a <i>well-spring of living water</i> <a name="fr_1683"></a><a href="#fn_1683"><font size="1">1684</font></a>
 ?  It is the Saviour Himself, concerning whom it is written, <i>For with Thee is the fountain of life</i> <a name="fr_1684"></a><a href="#fn_1684"><font size="1">1685</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p43"></a>6.  But what says Zephaniah in the person of Christ to the disciples?  <i>Prepare thyself, be rising at the dawn:  all their gleaning is destroyed</i> <a name="fr_1685"></a><a href="#fn_1685"><font size="1">1686</font></a>
 :  the gleaning, that is, of the Jews, with whom there is not a cluster, nay not even a gleaning of salvation left; for their vine is cut down.  See how He says to the disciples, <i>Prepare thyself, rise up at dawn</i>:  at dawn expect the Resurrection.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p45"></a>And farther on in the same context of Scripture He says, <i>Therefore wait thou for Me, saith the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p45.1"></a>Lord,</span> until the day of My Resurrection at the Testimony</i> <a name="fr_1686"></a><a href="#fn_1686"><font size="1">1687</font></a>
 .  Thou seest that the Prophet foresaw the place also of the Resurrection, which was to be surnamed “the Testimony.”  For what is the reason that this spot of Golgotha and of the Resurrection is not called, like the rest of the Churches, a Church, but a Testimony?  Why, perhaps, it was because of the Prophet, who had said, <i>until the day of My Resurrection at the Testimony</i>.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p47"></a>7.  And who then is this, and what is the sign of Him that rises?  In the words of the Prophet that follow in the same context, He says plainly, <i>For then will I turn to the peoples a language</i> <a name="fr_1687"></a><a href="#fn_1687"><font size="1">1688</font></a>
 :  since, after the Resurrection, when the Holy Ghost was sent forth the gift of tongues was granted, <i>that they might serve the Lord under one yoke</i> <a name="fr_1688"></a><a href="#fn_1688"><font size="1">1689</font></a>
 .  And what other token is set forth in the same Prophet, that they should serve the <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p49.2"></a>Lord</span> <i>under one yoke?</i>  <i>From beyond the rivers of Ethiopia they shall bring me offerings</i> <a name="fr_1689"></a><a href="#fn_1689"><font size="1">1690</font></a>
 .  Thou knowest what is written in the Acts, when the Ethiopian eunuch came from beyond the rivers of Ethiopia <a name="fr_1690"></a><a href="#fn_1690"><font size="1">1691</font></a>
 .  When therefore the Scriptures tell both the time and the peculiarity of the place, when they tell also the signs which followed the Resurrection, have thou henceforward a firm faith in the Resurrection, and let no one stir thee from confessing <i>Christ risen from the</i> dead <a name="fr_1691"></a><a href="#fn_1691"><font size="1">1692</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p53"></a>8.  Now take also another testimony in the  <a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_96"></a>87th Psalm, where Christ speaks in the Prophets, (for He who then spoke came afterwards among us):  <i>O <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p53.1"></a>Lord</span>, God of My salvation, I have cried day and night before Thee,</i> and a little, farther on, <i>I became as it were a man without help, free among the dead</i> <a name="fr_1692"></a><a href="#fn_1692"><font size="1">1693</font></a>
 .  He said not, I became a man without help; but, <i>as it were a man without help. </i> For indeed He was crucified not from weakness, but willingly and His Death was not from involuntary weakness.  <i>I was counted with them that go down into the pit</i> <a name="fr_1693"></a><a href="#fn_1693"><font size="1">1694</font></a>
 .  And what is the token?  <i>Thou hast put away Mine acquaintance far from Me</i> <a name="fr_1694"></a><a href="#fn_1694"><font size="1">1695</font></a>
  (for the disciples have fled).  <i>Wilt Thou shew wonders to the dead</i> <a name="fr_1695"></a><a href="#fn_1695"><font size="1">1696</font></a>
 ?  Then a little while afterwards:  <i>And unto Thee have I cried, O</i> <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p57.2"></a>Lord</span><i>; and in the morning shall my prayer come before Thee</i> <a name="fr_1696"></a><a href="#fn_1696"><font size="1">1697</font></a>
 .  Seest thou how they shew the exact point of the Hour, and of the Passion and of the Resurrection?</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p59"></a>9.  And whence hath the Saviour risen?  He says in the Song of Songs:  <i>Rise up, come, My neighbour</i> <a name="fr_1697"></a><a href="#fn_1697"><font size="1">1698</font></a>
 :  and in what follows, <i>in a cave of the rock</i> <a name="fr_1698"></a><a href="#fn_1698"><font size="1">1699</font></a>
 !  A cave of the rock He called the cave which was erewhile before the door of the Saviours sepulchre, and had been hewn out of the rock itself, as is wont to be done here in front of the sepulchres.  For now it is not to be seen, since the outer cave was cut away at that time for the sake of the present adornment.  For before the decoration of the sepulchre by the royal munificence, there was a cave in the front of the rock <a name="fr_1699"></a><a href="#fn_1699"><font size="1">1700</font></a>
 .  But where is the rock that had in it the cave?  Does it lie near the middle of the city, or near the walls and the outskirts?  And whether is it within the ancient walls, or within the outer walls which were built afterwards?  He says then in the Canticles:  <i>in a cave of the rock, close to the outer wall</i> <a name="fr_1700"></a><a href="#fn_1700"><font size="1">1701</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p64"></a>10.  At what season does the Saviour rise?  Is it the season of summer, or some other?  In the same Canticles immediately before the words quoted He says, The winter is past, the rain is past and gone <a name="fr_1701"></a><a href="#fn_1701"><font size="1">1702</font></a>
 ; the flowers appear on the earth; the time of the pruning is come <a name="fr_1702"></a><a href="#fn_1702"><font size="1">1703</font></a>
 .  Is not then the earth full of flowers now, and are they not pruning the vines?  Thou seest how he said also that the winter is now past.  For when this month Xanthicus <a name="fr_1703"></a><a href="#fn_1703"><font size="1">1704</font></a>
  is come, it is already spring.  And this is the season, the first month with the Hebrews, in which occurs the festival of the Passover, the typical formerly, but now the true.  This is the season of the creation of the world:  for then God said, Let the earth bring forth herbage of grass, yielding seed after his kind and after his likeness <a name="fr_1704"></a><a href="#fn_1704"><font size="1">1705</font></a>
 .  And now, as thou seest, already every herb is yielding seed.  And as at that time God made the sun and moon and gave them courses of equal day (and night), so also a few days since was the season of the equinox.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p71"></a>At that time God said, let us make man after our image and after our likeness <a name="fr_1705"></a><a href="#fn_1705"><font size="1">1706</font></a>
 .  And the image he received, but the likeness through his disobedience he obscured.  At the same season then in which he lost this the restoration also took place.  At the same season as the created man through disobedience was cast out of Paradise, he who believed was through obedience brought in.  Our Salvation then took place at the same season as the Fall:  when the flowers appeared, and the pruning was come.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p73"></a>11.  A garden was the place of His Burial, and a vine that which was planted there:  and He hath said, <i>I am the vine</i> <a name="fr_1706"></a><a href="#fn_1706"><font size="1">1707</font></a>
 !  He was planted therefore in the earth in order that the curse which came because of Adam might be rooted out.  The earth was condemned to thorns and thistles:  the true Vine sprang up out of the earth, that the saying might be fulfilled, <i>Truth sprang up out of the earth, and righteousness</i> <a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 97</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_97"></a><i>looked down from heaven</i> <a name="fr_1707"></a><a href="#fn_1707"><font size="1">1708</font></a>
 .  And what will He that is buried in the garden say?  <i>I have gathered My myrrh with My spices</i>:  and again, <i>Myrrh and aloes, with all chief spices</i> <a name="fr_1708"></a><a href="#fn_1708"><font size="1">1709</font></a>
 .  Now these are the symbols of the burying; and in the Gospels it is said, <i>The women came unto the sepulchre bringing the spices which they had prepared</i> <a name="fr_1709"></a><a href="#fn_1709"><font size="1">1710</font></a>
 <i>:  Nicodemus also bringing a mixture of myrrh and aloes</i> <a name="fr_1710"></a><a href="#fn_1710"><font size="1">1711</font></a>
 .  And farther on it is written, <i>I did eat My bread with My honey</i> <a name="fr_1711"></a><a href="#fn_1711"><font size="1">1712</font></a>
 :  the bitter before the Passion, and the sweet after the Resurrection.  Then after He had risen He entered through closed doors:  but they believed not that it was He:  for <i>they supposed that they beheld a spirit</i> <a name="fr_1712"></a><a href="#fn_1712"><font size="1">1713</font></a>
 .  But He said, <i>Handle Me and see</i>.  Put your fingers into the print of the nails, as Thomas required.  <i>And while they yet believed not for joy, and wondered, He said unto them, Have ye here anything to eat?  And they gave Him a piece of a broiled fish and honeycomb</i> <a name="fr_1713"></a><a href="#fn_1713"><font size="1">1714</font></a>
 .  Seest thou how that is fulfilled, <i>I did eat My bread with My honey</i>.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p82"></a>12.  But before He entered through the closed doors, the Bridegroom and Suitor <a name="fr_1714"></a><a href="#fn_1714"><font size="1">1715</font></a>
  of souls was sought by those noble and brave women.  They came, those blessed ones, to the sepulchre, and sought Him Who had been raised, and the tears were still dropping from their eyes, when they ought rather to have been dancing with joy for Him that had risen.  Mary came seeking Him, according to the Gospel, and found Him not:  and presently she heard from the Angels, and afterwards saw the Christ.  Are then these things also written?  He says in the Song of Songs, <i>On my bed I sought Him whom my soul loved</i>.  At what season?  <i>By night on my bed I sought Him Whom my soul loved:  Mary</i>, it says, <i>came while it was yet dark.  On my bed I sought Him by night, I sought Him, and I found Him not</i> <a name="fr_1715"></a><a href="#fn_1715"><font size="1">1716</font></a>
 .  And in the Gospels Mary says, <i>They have taken away my Lord, and I know not where they have laid Him</i> <a name="fr_1716"></a><a href="#fn_1716"><font size="1">1717</font></a>
 .  But the Angels being then present cure their want of knowledge; for they said, <i>Why seek ye the living among the dead</i> <a name="fr_1717"></a><a href="#fn_1717"><font size="1">1718</font></a>
 ?  He not only rose, but had also the dead with Him when He rose <a name="fr_1718"></a><a href="#fn_1718"><font size="1">1719</font></a>
 .  But she knew not, and in her person the Song of Songs said to the Angels, <i>Saw ye Him Whom my soul</i> loved?  It was but a little that I passed from them (that is, from the two Angels), <i>until I found Him Whom my soul loved.  I held Him, and would not let Him go</i> <a name="fr_1719"></a><a href="#fn_1719"><font size="1">1720</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p89"></a>13.  For after the vision of the Angels, Jesus came as His own Herald; and the Gospel says, <i>And behold Jesus met them, saying, All hail! and they came and took hold of His feet</i> <a name="fr_1720"></a><a href="#fn_1720"><font size="1">1721</font></a>
 .  They took hold of Him, that it might be fulfilled, <i>I will hold Him, and will not let Him go</i>.  Though the woman was weak in body, her spirit was manful.  <i>Many waters quench not love, neither do rivers drown it</i> <a name="fr_1721"></a><a href="#fn_1721"><font size="1">1722</font></a>
 ; He was dead whom they sought, yet was not the hope of the Resurrection quenched.  And the Angel says to them again, <i>Fear not ye</i>; I say not to the soldiers, <i>fear not</i>, but to you <a name="fr_1722"></a><a href="#fn_1722"><font size="1">1723</font></a>
 ; as for them, let them be afraid, that, taught by experience, they may bear witness and say, <i>Truly this was the Son of God</i> <a name="fr_1723"></a><a href="#fn_1723"><font size="1">1724</font></a>
 ; but you ought not to be afraid, <i>for perfect love casteth out fear</i> <a name="fr_1724"></a><a href="#fn_1724"><font size="1">1725</font></a>
 .  <i>Go, tell His disciples that He is risen</i> <a name="fr_1725"></a><a href="#fn_1725"><font size="1">1726</font></a>
 ; and the rest.  And they depart with joy, yet full of fear; is this also written? yes, the second Psalm, which relates the Passion of Christ, says, <i>Serve the Lord with fear, and rejoice unto Him with trembling</i> <a name="fr_1726"></a><a href="#fn_1726"><font size="1">1727</font></a>
 ;—<i>rejoice</i>, because of the risen Lord; but <i>with trembling</i>, because of the earthquake, and the Angel who appeared as lightning.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p97"></a>14.  Though, therefore, Chief Priests and Pharisees through Pilates means sealed the tomb; yet the women beheld Him who was risen.  And Esaias knowing the feebleness of the Chief Priests, and the womens strength of faith, says, <i>Ye women, who come from beholding, come hither</i> <a name="fr_1727"></a><a href="#fn_1727"><font size="1">1728</font></a>
 <i>; for the people hath no understanding</i>;—the Chief Priests want understanding, while women are eye-witnesses.  And when the soldiers came into the city to them, and told them all that had come to pass, they said to them, <i>Say ye, His disciples came by night, and stole Him away while we slept</i> <a name="fr_1728"></a><a href="#fn_1728"><font size="1">1729</font></a>
 ?  Well therefore did Esaias foretell this also, as in their persons, <i>But tell us, and relate to us another deceit</i> <a name="fr_1729"></a><a href="#fn_1729"><font size="1">1730</font></a>
 .  He who rose again, is up, and for a gift of money they persuade the soldiers; but they persuade not the kings of our time.  The soldiers then surrendered the truth for silver; but the kings of this day have, in their piety, built this holy Church of the Resurrection of God our Saviour, inlaid with silver and wrought with gold, in which we are assembled <a name="fr_1730"></a><a href="#fn_1730"><font size="1">1731</font></a>
 ; and embellished it with the treasures of silver and gold and precious stones.  <i>And if this come to the governors ears</i>, they say, <i>we will persuade him</i> <a name="fr_1731"></a><a href="#fn_1731"><font size="1">1732</font></a>
 .  Yea, though ye persuade the soldiers, yet ye will not persuade the world; for why, as Peters guards were condemned when he escaped out of the prison, were not  <a name="page_98"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 98</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_98"></a>they also who watched Jesus Christ condemned?  Upon the former, sentence was pronounced by Herod, for they were ignorant and had nothing to say for themselves; while the latter, who had seen the truth, and concealed it for money, were protected by the Chief Priests.  Nevertheless, though but a few of the Jews were persuaded at the time, the world became obedient.  They who hid the truth were themselves hidden; but they who received it were made manifest by the power of the Saviour, who not only rose from the dead, but also raised the dead with Himself.  And in the person of these the Prophet Osee says plainly, <i>After two days will He revive us, and in the third day we shall rise again, and shall live in His sight</i> <a name="fr_1732"></a><a href="#fn_1732"><font size="1">1733</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p104"></a>15.  But since the disobedient Jews will not be persuaded by the Divine Scriptures, but forgetting all that is written gainsay the Resurrection of Jesus, it were good to answer them thus:  On what ground, while you say that Eliseus and Elias raised the dead, do you gainsay the Resurrection of our Saviour?  Is it that we have no living witnesses now out of that generation to what we say?  Well, do you also bring forward witnesses of the history of that time.  But that is written;—so is this also written:  why then do ye receive the one, and reject the other?  They were Hebrews who wrote that history; so were all the Apostles Hebrews:  why then do ye disbelieve the Jews <a name="fr_1733"></a><a href="#fn_1733"><font size="1">1734</font></a>
 ?  Matthew who wrote the Gospel wrote it in the Hebrew tongue <a name="fr_1734"></a><a href="#fn_1734"><font size="1">1735</font></a>
 ; and Paul the preacher was a Hebrew of the Hebrews; and the twelve Apostles were all of Hebrew race:  then fifteen Bishops of Jerusalem were appointed in succession from among the Hebrews <a name="fr_1735"></a><a href="#fn_1735"><font size="1">1736</font></a>
 .  What then is your reason for allowing your own accounts, and rejecting ours, though these also are written by Hebrews from among yourselves.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p108"></a>16.  But it is impossible, some one will say, that the dead should rise; and yet Eliseus twice raised the dead,—when he was alive, and also when dead.  Do we then believe, that when Eliseus was dead, a dead man who was cast upon him and touched him, arose and is Christ not risen?  But in that case, the dead man who touched Eliseus, arose, yet he who raised him continued nevertheless dead:  but in this case both the Dead of whom we speak Himself arose, and many dead were raised without having even touched Him.  For <i>many bodies of the Saints which slept arose, and they came out of the graves after His Resurrection, and went into the Holy City</i> <a name="fr_1736"></a><a href="#fn_1736"><font size="1">1737</font></a>
 , (evidently this city, in which we now are <a name="fr_1737"></a><a href="#fn_1737"><font size="1">1738</font></a>
 ,) <i>and appeared unto many</i>.  Eliseus then raised a dead man, but he conquered not the world; Elias raised a dead man, but devils are not driven away in the name of Elias.  We are not speaking evil of the Prophets, but we are celebrating their Master more highly; for we do not exalt our own wonders by disparaging theirs; for theirs also are ours; but by what happened among them, we win credence for our own.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p111"></a>17.  But again they say, “A corpse then lately dead was raised by the living; but shew us that one three days dead can possibly arise, and that a man should be buried, and rise after three days.”  If we seek for Scripture testimony in proof of such facts, the Lord Jesus Christ Himself supplies it in the Gospels, saying, <i>For as Jonas was three days and three nights in the whales belly; so shall the Son of man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth</i> <a name="fr_1738"></a><a href="#fn_1738"><font size="1">1739</font></a>
 .  And when we examine the story of Jonas, great is the force <a name="fr_1739"></a><a href="#fn_1739"><font size="1">1740</font></a>
  of the resemblance.  Jesus was sent to preach repentance; Jonas also was sent:  but whereas the one fled, not knowing what should come to pass; the other came willingly, to give repentance unto salvation.  Jonas was asleep in the ship, and snoring amidst the stormy sea; while Jesus also slept, the sea, according to Gods providence <a name="fr_1740"></a><a href="#fn_1740"><font size="1">1741</font></a>
 , began to rise, to shew in the sequel the might of Him who slept.  To the one they said, <i>Why art thou snoring?  Arise, call upon thy God, that God may save us</i> <a name="fr_1741"></a><a href="#fn_1741"><font size="1">1742</font></a>
 ; but in the other case they say unto the Master, <i>Lord, save us</i> <a name="fr_1742"></a><a href="#fn_1742"><font size="1">1743</font></a>
 .  Then they said, <i>Call upon thy God</i>; here they say, <i>save Thou</i>.  But the one says, <i>Take me, and cast me into the sea; so shall the sea be calm unto you</i> <a name="fr_1743"></a><a href="#fn_1743"><font size="1">1744</font></a>
 ; the other, Himself <i>rebuked the winds and the sea, and there was a great calm</i> <a name="fr_1744"></a><a href="#fn_1744"><font size="1">1745</font></a>
 .  The one was cast into a whales belly:  but the other of His own accord went down thither, where the invisible whale of death is.  And He went down of His own accord, that death <a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 99</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_99"></a>might cast up those whom he had devoured, according to that which is written, <i>I will ransom them from the power of the grave; and from the hand of death I will redeem them</i> <a name="fr_1745"></a><a href="#fn_1745"><font size="1">1746</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p120"></a>18.  At this point of our discourse, let us consider whether is harder, for a man after having been buried to rise again from the earth, or for a man in the belly of a whale, having come into the great heat of a living creature, to escape corruption.  For what man knows not, that the heat of the belly is so great, that even bones which have been swallowed moulder away?  How then did Jonas, who was three days and three nights in the whales belly, escape corruption?  And, seeing that the nature of all men is such that we cannot live without breathing, as we do, in air, how did he live without a breath of this air for three days?  But the Jews make answer and say, The power of God descended with Jonas when he was tossed about in hell.  Does then the Lord grant life to His own servant, by sending His power with him, and can He not grant it to Himself as well?  If that is credible, this is credible also; if this is incredible, that also is incredible.  For to me both are alike worthy of credence.  I believe that Jonas was preserved, for <i>all things are possible with God</i> <a name="fr_1746"></a><a href="#fn_1746"><font size="1">1747</font></a>
 ; I believe that Christ also was raised from the dead; for I have many testimonies of this, both from the Divine Scriptures, and from the operative power even at this day <a name="fr_1747"></a><a href="#fn_1747"><font size="1">1748</font></a>
  of Him who arose,—who descended into hell alone, but ascended thence with a great company; for He went down to death, <i>and many bodies of the saints which slept arose</i> <a name="fr_1748"></a><a href="#fn_1748"><font size="1">1749</font></a>
  through Him.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p124"></a>19.  Death was struck with dismay on beholding a new visitant descend into Hades, not bound by the chains of that place.  Wherefore, O porters of Hades, were ye scared at sight of Him?  What was the unwonted fear that possessed you?  Death fled, and his flight betrayed his cowardice.  The holy prophets ran unto Him, and Moses the Lawgiver, and Abraham, and Isaac, and Jacob; David also, and Samuel, and Esaias, and John the Baptist, who bore witness when he asked, <i>Art Thou He that should come, or look we for another</i> <a name="fr_1749"></a><a href="#fn_1749"><font size="1">1750</font></a>
 ?  All the Just were ransomed, whom death had swallowed; for it behoved the King whom they had proclaimed, to become the redeemer of His noble heralds.  Then each of the Just said, <i>O death, where is thy victory?  O grave, where is thy sting</i> <a name="fr_1750"></a><a href="#fn_1750"><font size="1">1751</font></a>
 ?  For the Conqueror hath redeemed us.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p127"></a>20.  Of this our Saviour the Prophet Jonas formed the type, when he prayed out of the belly of the whale, and said, <i>I cried in my affliction</i>, and so on; <i>out of the belly of hell</i> <a name="fr_1751"></a><a href="#fn_1751"><font size="1">1752</font></a>
 , and yet he was in the whale; but though in the whale, he says that he is in Hades; for he was a type of Christ, who was to descend into Hades.  And after a few words, he says, in the person of Christ, prophesying most clearly, <i>My head went down to the chasms of the mountains</i> <a name="fr_1752"></a><a href="#fn_1752"><font size="1">1753</font></a>
 ; and yet he was in the belly of the whale.  What mountains then encompass thee?  I know, he says, that I am a type of Him, who is to be laid in the Sepulchre hewn out of the rock.  And though he was in the sea, Jonas says, <i>I went down to the earth</i>, since he was a type of Christ, who went down into the heart of the earth.  And foreseeing the deeds of the Jews who persuaded the soldiers to lie, and told them, <i>Say that they stole Him away</i>, he says, <i>By regarding lying vanities they forsook their own mercy</i> <a name="fr_1753"></a><a href="#fn_1753"><font size="1">1754</font></a>
 .  For He who had mercy on them came, and was crucified, and rose again, giving His own precious blood both for Jews and Gentiles; yet say they, <i>Say that they stole Him away</i>, having regard to <i>lying vanities</i> <a name="fr_1754"></a><a href="#fn_1754"><font size="1">1755</font></a>
 .  But concerning His Resurrection, Esaias also says, <i>He who brought up from the earth the great Shepherd of the sheep</i> <a name="fr_1755"></a><a href="#fn_1755"><font size="1">1756</font></a>
 ; he added the word, <i>great</i>, lest He should be thought on a level with the shepherds who had gone before Him.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p134"></a>21.  Since then we have the prophecies, let faith abide with us.  Let them fall who fall through unbelief, since they so will; but thou hast taken thy stand on the rock of the faith in the Resurrection.  Let no heretic ever persuade thee to speak evil of the Resurrection.  For to this day the Manichees say, that, the resurrection of the Saviour was phantom-wise, and not real, not heeding Paul who says, <i>Who was made of the seed of David according to the flesh</i>; and again, <i>By the resurrection of Jesus Christ our Lord from the dead</i> <a name="fr_1756"></a><a href="#fn_1756"><font size="1">1757</font></a>
 .  And again he aims at them, and speaks thus, <i>Say not in</i>  <a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 100</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_100"></a><i>thine heart, who shall ascend into heaven; or who shall descend into the deep? that is, to bring up Christ from the dead</i> <a name="fr_1757"></a><a href="#fn_1757"><font size="1">1758</font></a>
 ; and in like manner warning as he has elsewhere written again, <i>Remember Jesus Christ raised from the dead</i> <a name="fr_1758"></a><a href="#fn_1758"><font size="1">1759</font></a>
 ; and again, <i>And if Christ be not risen, then is our preaching vain, and your faith also vain.  Yea, and we are found false witnesses of God; because we testified of God that He raised up Christ, whom He raised not up</i> <a name="fr_1759"></a><a href="#fn_1759"><font size="1">1760</font></a>
 .  But in what follows he says, <i>But now is Christ risen from the dead, the first fruits of them that are asleep</i> <a name="fr_1760"></a><a href="#fn_1760"><font size="1">1761</font></a>
 ;<i>—And He was seen of Cephas, then of the twelve</i>; (for if thou believe not the one witness, thou hast twelve witnesses;) <i>then He was seen of above five hundred brethren at once</i> <a name="fr_1761"></a><a href="#fn_1761"><font size="1">1762</font></a>
 ; (if they disbelieve the twelve, let them admit the five hundred;) <i>after that He was seen of James</i> <a name="fr_1762"></a><a href="#fn_1762"><font size="1">1763</font></a>
 , His own brother, and first Bishop of this diocese.  Seeing then that such a Bishop originally <a name="fr_1763"></a><a href="#fn_1763"><font size="1">1764</font></a>
  saw Christ Jesus when risen, do not thou, his disciple, disbelieve him.  But thou sayest that His brother James was a partial witness; <i>afterwards He was seen also of me</i> <a name="fr_1764"></a><a href="#fn_1764"><font size="1">1765</font></a>
  Paul, His enemy; and what testimony is doubted, when an enemy proclaims it?  “I, <i>who was before a persecutor</i> <a name="fr_1765"></a><a href="#fn_1765"><font size="1">1766</font></a>
 , now preach the glad tidings of the Resurrection.”</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p145"></a>22.  Many witnesses there are of the Saviours resurrection.—The night, and the light of the full moon; (for that night was the sixteenth <a name="fr_1766"></a><a href="#fn_1766"><font size="1">1767</font></a>
 ;) the rock of the sepulchre which received Him; the stone also shall rise up against the face of the Jews, for it saw the Lord; even the stone which was then rolled away <a name="fr_1767"></a><a href="#fn_1767"><font size="1">1768</font></a>
 , itself bears witness to the Resurrection, lying there to this day.  Angels of God who were present testified of the Resurrection of the Only-begotten:  Peter and John, and Thomas, and all the rest of the Apostles; some of whom ran to the sepulchre, and saw the burial-clothes, in which He was wrapped before, lying there after the Resurrection; and others handled His hands and His feet, and beheld the prints of the nails; and all enjoyed together that Breath of the Saviour, and were counted worthy to forgive sins in the power of the Holy Ghost.  Women too were witnesses, who took hold of His feet, and who beheld the mighty earthquake, and the radiance of the Angel who stood by:  the linen clothes also which were wrapped about Him, and which He left when He rose;—the soldiers, and the money given to them; the spot itself also, yet to be seen;—and this house of the holy Church, which out of the loving affection to Christ of the Emperor Constantine of blessed memory, was both built and beautified as thou seest.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p148"></a>23.  A witness to the resurrection of Jesus is Tabitha also, who was in His name raised from the dead <a name="fr_1768"></a><a href="#fn_1768"><font size="1">1769</font></a>
 ; for how shall we disbelieve that Christ is risen, when even His Name raised the dead?  The sea also bears witness to the resurrection of Jesus, as thou hast heard before <a name="fr_1769"></a><a href="#fn_1769"><font size="1">1770</font></a>
 .  The drought of fishes also testifies, and the fire of coals there, and the fish laid thereon.  Peter also bears witness, who had erst denied Him thrice, and who then thrice confessed Him; and was commanded to feed His spiritual <a name="fr_1770"></a><a href="#fn_1770"><font size="1">1771</font></a>
  sheep.  To this day stands Mount Olivet, still to the eyes of the faithful all but displaying Him Who ascended on a cloud, and the heavenly gate of His ascension.  For from heaven He descended to Bethlehem, but to heaven He ascended from the Mount of Olives <a name="fr_1771"></a><a href="#fn_1771"><font size="1">1772</font></a>
 ; at the former place beginning His conflicts among men, but in the latter, crowned after them.  Thou hast therefore many witnesses; thou hast this very place of the Resurrection; thou hast also the place of the Ascension towards the east; thou hast also for witnesses the Angels which there bore testimony; and the cloud on which He went up, and the disciples who came down from that place.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p153"></a>24.  The course of instruction in the Faith would lead me to speak of the Ascension also; but the grace of God so ordered <a name="fr_1772"></a><a href="#fn_1772"><font size="1">1773</font></a>
  it, that thou heardest most fully concerning it, as far as our weakness allowed, yesterday, on the <a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_101"></a>Lords day; since, by the providence of divine grace, the course of the Lessons <a name="fr_1773"></a><a href="#fn_1773"><font size="1">1774</font></a>
  in Church included the account of our Saviours going up into the heavens <a name="fr_1774"></a><a href="#fn_1774"><font size="1">1775</font></a>
 ; and what was then said was spoken principally for the sake of all, and for the assembled body of the faithful, yet especially for thy sake <a name="fr_1775"></a><a href="#fn_1775"><font size="1">1776</font></a>
 .  But the question is, didst thou attend to what was said?  For thou knowest that the words which come next in the Creed teach thee to believe in Him “Who <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p157.5"></a>rose again the third day, and ascended into Heaven, and sat down on the right hand of the Father</span>.”  I suppose then certainly that thou rememberest the exposition; yet I will now again cursorily put thee in mind of what was then said.  Remember what is distinctly written in the Psalms, <i>God is gone up with a shout</i> <a name="fr_1776"></a><a href="#fn_1776"><font size="1">1777</font></a>
 ; remember that the divine powers also said to one another, <i>Lift up your gates, ye Princes</i> <a name="fr_1777"></a><a href="#fn_1777"><font size="1">1778</font></a>
 , and the rest; remember also the Psalm which says, <i>He ascended on high, He led captivity captive</i> <a name="fr_1778"></a><a href="#fn_1778"><font size="1">1779</font></a>
 ; remember the Prophet who said, <i>Who buildeth His ascension unto heaven</i> <a name="fr_1779"></a><a href="#fn_1779"><font size="1">1780</font></a>
 ; and all the other particulars mentioned yesterday because of the gainsaying of the Jews.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p162"></a>25.  For when they speak against the ascension of the Saviour, as being impossible, remember the account of the carrying away of Habakkuk:  for if Habakkuk was transported by an Angel, being carried by the hair of his head <a name="fr_1780"></a><a href="#fn_1780"><font size="1">1781</font></a>
 , much rather was the Lord of both Prophets and Angels, able by His own power to make His ascent into the Heavens on a cloud from the Mount of Olives.  Wonders like this thou mayest call to mind, but reserve the preeminence for the Lord, the Worker of wonders; for the others were borne up, but He bears up all things.  Remember that Enoch was translated <a name="fr_1781"></a><a href="#fn_1781"><font size="1">1782</font></a>
 ; but Jesus ascended:  remember what was said yesterday concerning Elias, that Elias was taken up in a chariot of fire <a name="fr_1782"></a><a href="#fn_1782"><font size="1">1783</font></a>
 ; but that <i>the chariots of</i> Christ <i>are ten thousand-fold even thousands upon thousands</i> <a name="fr_1783"></a><a href="#fn_1783"><font size="1">1784</font></a>
 :  and that Elias was taken up, towards the east of Jordan; but that Christ ascended at the east of the brook Cedron:  and that Elias went <i>as into heaven</i> <a name="fr_1784"></a><a href="#fn_1784"><font size="1">1785</font></a>
 ; but Jesus, into heaven:  and that Elias said that a double portion in the Holy Spirit should be given to his holy disciple; but that Christ granted to His own disciples so great enjoyment of the grace of the Holy Ghost, as not only to have It in themselves, but also, by the laying on of their hands, to impart the fellowship of It to them who believed.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p168"></a>26.  And when thou hast thus wrestled against the Jews,—when thou hast worsted them by parallel instances, then come further to the pre-eminence of the Saviours glory; namely, that they were the servants, but He the Son of God.  And thus thou wilt be reminded of His pre-eminence, by the thought that a servant of Christ was caught up to the third heaven.  For if Elias attained as far as the first heaven, but Paul as far as the third, the latter, therefore, has obtained a more honourable dignity.  Be not ashamed of thine Apostles; they are not inferior to Moses, nor second to the Prophets; but they are noble among the noble, yea, nobler still.  For Elias truly was taken up into heaven; but Peter has the keys of the kingdom of heaven, having received the words, <i>Whatsoever thou shalt loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven</i> <a name="fr_1785"></a><a href="#fn_1785"><font size="1">1786</font></a>
 .  Elias was taken up only to heaven; but Paul both into <i>heaven</i>, and into <i>paradise</i> <a name="fr_1786"></a><a href="#fn_1786"><font size="1">1787</font></a>
  (for it behoved the disciples of Jesus to receive more manifold grace), and <i>heard unspeakable words, which it is not lawful for man to utter</i>.  But Paul came down again from above, not because he was unworthy to abide in the third heaven, but in order that after having enjoyed things above mans reach, and descended in honour, and having preached Christ, and died for His sake, he might receive also the crown of martyrdom.  But I pass over the other parts of this argument, of which I spoke yesterday in the Lords-day congregation; for with understanding hearers, a mere reminder is sufficient for instruction.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p171"></a>27.  But remember also what I have often said <a name="fr_1787"></a><a href="#fn_1787"><font size="1">1788</font></a>
  concerning the Sons sitting at the right <a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_102"></a>hand of the Father; because of the next sentence in the Creed, which says, “<span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p172.6"></a>and ascended into Heaven, and sat down at the right hand of the Father</span>.”  Let us not curiously pry into what is properly meant by the throne; for it is incomprehensible:  but neither let us endure those who falsely say, that it was after His Cross and Resurrection and Ascension into heaven, that the Son began to sit on the right hand of the Father.  For the Son gained not His throne by advancement <a name="fr_1788"></a><a href="#fn_1788"><font size="1">1789</font></a>
 ; but throughout His being (and His being is by an eternal generation <a name="fr_1789"></a><a href="#fn_1789"><font size="1">1790</font></a>
 ) He also sitteth together with the Father.  And this throne the Prophet Esaias having beheld before the incarnate coming of the Saviour, says, <i>I saw the Lord sitting on a throne, high and lifted up <a name="fr_1790"></a><a href="#fn_1790"><font size="1">1791</font></a>
 </i>, and the rest.  For the Father <i>no man hath seen at any time <a name="fr_1791"></a><a href="#fn_1791"><font size="1">1792</font></a>
 </i>, and He who then appeared to the Prophet was the Son.  The Psalmist also says, <i>Thy throne is prepared of old; Thou art from everlasting <a name="fr_1792"></a><a href="#fn_1792"><font size="1">1793</font></a>
 </i>.  Though then the testimonies on this point are many, yet because of the lateness of the time, we will content ourselves even with these.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p178"></a>28.  But now I must remind you of a few things out of many which are spoken concerning the Sons sitting at the right hand of the Father.  For the hundred and ninth Psalm says plainly, <i>The <span class="sc"><a name="ii.xviii-p178.1"></a>Lord</span> said unto my Lord, Sit Thou on My right hand, until I make Thine enemies Thy footstool</i> <a name="fr_1793"></a><a href="#fn_1793"><font size="1">1794</font></a>
 .  And the Saviour, confirming this saying in the Gospels, says that David spoke not these things of himself, but from the inspiration of the Holy Ghost, saying, <i>How then doth David in the Spirit call Him Lord, saying, The Lord said unto my Lord, Sit Thou on My right hand</i> <a name="fr_1794"></a><a href="#fn_1794"><font size="1">1795</font></a>
 ? and the rest.  And in the Acts of the Apostles, Peter on the day of Pentecost standing with the Eleven <a name="fr_1795"></a><a href="#fn_1795"><font size="1">1796</font></a>
 , and discoursing to the Israelites, has in very words cited this testimony from the hundred and ninth Psalm.</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p182"></a>29.  But I must remind you also of a few other testimonies in like manner concerning the Sons sitting at the right hand of the Father.  For in the Gospel according to Matthew it is written, <i>Nevertheless, I say unto you, Henceforth ye shall see the Son of Man sitting on the right hand of power</i> <a name="fr_1796"></a><a href="#fn_1796"><font size="1">1797</font></a>
 , and the rest:  in accordance with which the Apostle Peter also writes, <i>By the Resurrection of Jesus Christ, who is on the right hand of God, having gone into heaven</i> <a name="fr_1797"></a><a href="#fn_1797"><font size="1">1798</font></a>
 .  And the Apostle Paul, writing to the Romans, says, <i>It is Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God</i> <a name="fr_1798"></a><a href="#fn_1798"><font size="1">1799</font></a>
 .  And charging the Ephesians, he thus speaks, <i>According to the working of His mighty power, which He wrought in Christ when He raised Him from the dead, and set Him at His own right hand</i> <a name="fr_1799"></a><a href="#fn_1799"><font size="1">1800</font></a>
 ; and the rest.  And the Colossians he taught thus, <i>If ye then be risen with Christ, seek the things above, where Christ is seated at the right hand of God</i> <a name="fr_1800"></a><a href="#fn_1800"><font size="1">1801</font></a>
 .  And in the Epistle to the Hebrews he says, <i>When He had made purification of our sins, He sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high</i> <a name="fr_1801"></a><a href="#fn_1801"><font size="1">1802</font></a>
 .  And again, <i>But unto which of the Angels hath He said at any time, Sit thou at My right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool</i> <a name="fr_1802"></a><a href="#fn_1802"><font size="1">1803</font></a>
 ?  And again, <i>But He, when He had offered one sacrifice for all men, for ever sat down on the right hand of God; from henceforth expecting till His enemies be made His footstool</i> <a name="fr_1803"></a><a href="#fn_1803"><font size="1">1804</font></a>
 .  And again, <i>Looking unto Jesus, the author and perfecter of our faith; Who for the joy that was set before Him endured the Cross, despising shame, and is set down on the right hand of the throne of God</i> <a name="fr_1804"></a><a href="#fn_1804"><font size="1">1805</font></a>
 .</p>  
 <p><a name="ii.xviii-p192"></a>30.  And though there are many other texts concerning the session of the Only-begotten on the right hand of God, yet these may suffice us at present; with a repetition of my remark, that it was not after His coming in the flesh <a name="fr_1805"></a><a href="#fn_1805"><font size="1">1806</font></a>
  that He obtained the dignity of this seat; no, for even before all ages, the Only-begotten Son of God, our Lord Jesus Christ, ever possesses the throne on the right hand of the Father.  Now may He Himself, the God of all, who is Father of the Christ, and our Lord Jesus Christ, who came down, and ascended, and sitteth together with the Father, watch over your souls; keep unshaken and unchanged your hope in Him who rose again; raise you together with Him from your dead sins unto His heavenly gift; count you worthy to be <i>caught up in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air</i> <a name="fr_1806"></a><a href="#fn_1806"><font size="1">1807</font></a>
 , in His fitting time; and, until that time arrive of His glorious second advent, write all your names in the Book of the living, and having written them, never blot them out (for the names of many, who fall away, are blotted out); and may He grant to all of you to believe on Him who rose again, and to look for Him who is gone up, and is to come again, (to come, but not from the earth; for be on your guard, O man, because of the deceivers who are to come;) Who sitteth on high, and is here present together with us, <i>beholding the</i> <a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a> <a name="ii.xviii-Page_103"></a><i>order of each, and the steadfastness of his faith</i> <a name="fr_1807"></a><a href="#fn_1807"><font size="1">1808</font></a>
 .  For think not that because He is now absent in the flesh, He is therefore absent also in the Spirit.  He is here present in the midst of us, listening to what is said of Him, and beholding thine inward thoughts, and <i>trying the reins and hearts</i> <a name="fr_1808"></a><a href="#fn_1808"><font size="1">1809</font></a>
 ;—who also is now ready to present those who are coming to baptism, and all of you, in the Holy Ghost to the Father, and to say, <i>Behold, I and the children whom God hath given Me</i> <a name="fr_1809"></a><a href="#fn_1809"><font size="1">1810</font></a>
 :—To whom be glory for ever.  Amen.</p>

Iren. (V. vi. § 1; xvi. § 2); Tertullian (<i>de Baptismo</i>, c. 5); Clem. Alex. (<i>Exhort</i>. c. 12); Origen (<i>c. Cels</i>. IV. 30).</p>

 <p class="endnote"><a name="ii.xviii-p152"></a> St. Luke (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#050">Luke 24.50</a><a name="ii.xviii-p152.1"></a>) describes the Ascension as taking place at Bethany, but the tradition, which Cyril follows, had long since fixed the scene on the summit of the Mount of Olives, a mile nearer to Jerusalem; and here the Empress Helena had built the Church of the Ascension (Eusebius, <i>Life of Constantine</i>, III. 43; <i>Demonstr. Evang.</i> VI. xviii. 26).  There is nothing in Cyrils language to warrant the Benedictine Editors suggestion that he alludes to the legend, according to which the marks of Christs feet were indelibly impressed on the spot from which He ascended.  In the next generation St. Augustine seems to countenance the miraculous story (<i>In Joh. Evang</i>. Tract xlvii.):  “There are His footsteps, now adored, where last He stood, and whence He ascended into heaven.”  The supposed trace of one foot is still shewn on Mount Olivet; “the other having been removed by the Turks is now to be found in the Chapel of S. Thecla, which is in the Patriarchs Palace” (Jerusalem Ed.).  Compare Stanley, <i>Sinai and Palestine</i>, c. xiv.; Dictionary of Bible, <i>Olives Mount of</i>.</p>
