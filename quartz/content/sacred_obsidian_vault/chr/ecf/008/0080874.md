 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xi.xvi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xi.xvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVI.—Cannibalism.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xi.xvi-p2"></a>“And the men who were with them there for the first time were eager to do the like.  Thus, although we are born neither good nor bad, we become <i>one or the other;</i> and having formed habits, we are with difficulty drawn from them.  But when irrational animals fell short, these bastard men tasted also human flesh.  For it was not a long step to the consumption of flesh like their own, having first tasted it in other forms.</p>
