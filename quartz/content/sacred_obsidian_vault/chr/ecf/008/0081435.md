 </p><p class="c34"><a name="viii.viii.iii.iv-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="viii.viii.iii.iv-p1.1"></a>III.</span></p> 
 <p class="c52"><a name="viii.viii.iii.iv-p2"></a>Of the arraigned.</p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="viii.viii.iii.iv-p3"></a>A person arraigned ought to plead his cause before his judge; and an arraigned person may refuse to speak, if he choose so, before one who is not his own proper judge; and indulgence (<i>induciæ</i>) should be granted to the arraigned as often as they appeal.</p>
