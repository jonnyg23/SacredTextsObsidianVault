 </p><p class="c35"><a name="v.iv.x-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="v.iv.x-p1.1"></a>Chapter X.—Davids Sin, So Admonitory to Us Weak Men.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="v.iv.x-p2"></a>Does not the case of David instruct thee, whom God “found a man after His heart,” <a name="fr_476"></a><a href="#fn_476"><font size="1">477</font></a>
  one faithful, faultless, pious, true?  This same man saw the beauty of a woman—I mean of Bathsheba—when he saw her as she was cleansing herself and washing unclothed.  This woman the holy man saw, and was thoroughly <a name="fr_477"></a><a href="#fn_477"><font size="1">478</font></a>
  captivated with desire by the sight of her. <a name="fr_478"></a><a href="#fn_478"><font size="1">479</font></a>
   See, then, what evils he committed because of a woman, and <i>how</i> this righteous man sinned, and gave command that the husband of this woman should be killed in battle.  Ye have seen what wicked schemes he laid and executed, and <i>how</i>, because of his passion for a woman, he perpetrated a murder—<i>he</i>, David, who was called “the anointed of the Lord.” <a name="fr_479"></a><a href="#fn_479"><font size="1">480</font></a>
   Be admonished, O man:  for, if such men as these have been brought to ruin through women, what is <i>thy</i> righteousness, or what art thou among the holy, that thou consortest with women and with maidens day and night, with much silliness, without fear of God?  Not <i>thus</i>, my brethren, not thus let us conduct ourselves; but let us be mindful of that word which is spoken concerning a woman:  “Her hands lay snares, and her heart spreadeth nets; but the just shall escape from her, whilst the wicked falleth into her hands.” <a name="fr_480"></a><a href="#fn_480"><font size="1">481</font></a>
   Therefore let us, who are consecrated, <a name="fr_481"></a><a href="#fn_481"><font size="1">482</font></a>
  be careful not to live in the same house with females who have taken the vow.  For such conduct as this is not becoming nor right for the servants of God.</p>
