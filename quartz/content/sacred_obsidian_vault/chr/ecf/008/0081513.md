 <p> <a name="x.v.xvi"></a>
 </p><p class="c34"><a name="x.v.xvi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="x.v.xvi-p1.1"></a>IX. <a name="fr_3640"></a><a href="#fn_3640"><font size="1">3641</font></a>
 </span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="x.v.xvi-p3"></a><i>Head of the Lord</i>—<i>His</i> simple Divinity; because He is the Beginning and Creator of all things:  in Daniel. <a name="fr_3641"></a><a href="#fn_3641"><font size="1">3642</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p5"></a><i>The white hair of the Lord</i>, because He is “the Ancient of Days:”  as above.</p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p6"></a><i>The eyes of the Lord</i>—the Divine inspection:  because He sees all things.  Like that in the apostle:  For all things are naked and open in His eyes.” <a name="fr_3642"></a><a href="#fn_3642"><font size="1">3643</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p8"></a><i>The eyelids of the Lord</i>—hidden spiritual mysteries in the Divine precepts.  In the Psalm:  “His eyelids question, that is prove, the children of men.” <a name="fr_3643"></a><a href="#fn_3643"><font size="1">3644</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p10"></a><i>The smelling of the Lord</i>—His delight in the prayers or works of the saints.  In Genesis:  “And the Lord smelled an odour of sweetness.” <a name="fr_3644"></a><a href="#fn_3644"><font size="1">3645</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p12"></a><i>The mouth of the Lord</i>—His Son, or word <i>addressed</i> to men.  In the prophet, “The mouth of the Lord hath spoken;” <a name="fr_3645"></a><a href="#fn_3645"><font size="1">3646</font></a>
  and elsewhere, “They provoked His mouth to anger.” <a name="fr_3646"></a><a href="#fn_3646"><font size="1">3647</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p15"></a><i>The tongue of the Lord</i>—His Holy Spirit.  In the Psalm:  “My tongue is a pen.” <a name="fr_3647"></a><a href="#fn_3647"><font size="1">3648</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p17"></a><i>The face of the Lord</i>—His manifestation.  In Exodus, “My face shall go before thee;” <a name="fr_3648"></a><a href="#fn_3648"><font size="1">3649</font></a>
  and in the prophet, “The face of the Lord divided them.” <a name="fr_3649"></a><a href="#fn_3649"><font size="1">3650</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p20"></a><i>The word of the Lord</i>—<i>His</i> Son.  In the Psalm:  “My heart hath uttered a good word.” <a name="fr_3650"></a><a href="#fn_3650"><font size="1">3651</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p22"></a><i>The arm of the Lord</i>—<i>His</i> Son, by whom He hath wrought all His works.  In the prophet Isaiah:  “And to whom is the arm of the Lord revealed?” <a name="fr_3651"></a><a href="#fn_3651"><font size="1">3652</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p24"></a><i>The right hand of the Lord</i>—that is, <i>His</i> Son; as also above in the Psalm:  “The right hand of the Lord hath done valiantly.” <a name="fr_3652"></a><a href="#fn_3652"><font size="1">3653</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p26"></a><i>The right hand of the Lord</i>—<i>electio omnis</i>.  As in Deuteronomy:  “In His right hand <i>is</i> a fiery law.” <a name="fr_3653"></a><a href="#fn_3653"><font size="1">3654</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p28"></a><i>The wings of the Lord</i>—Divine protection.  In the Psalm:  “In the shadow of Thy wings will I hope.” <a name="fr_3654"></a><a href="#fn_3654"><font size="1">3655</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p30"></a><i>The shoulder of the Lord</i>—the Divine power, by which He condescends to carry the feeble.  In Deuteronomy:  “He took them up, and put them on His shoulders.” <a name="fr_3655"></a><a href="#fn_3655"><font size="1">3656</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p32"></a><i>The hand of the Lord</i>—Divine operation.  <a name="page_761"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 761</font></a> <a name="x.v.xvi-Page_761"></a>In the prophet:  “Have not my hands made all these things?” <a name="fr_3656"></a><a href="#fn_3656"><font size="1">3657</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p34"></a><i>The finger of the Lord</i>—the Holy Spirit, by whose operation the tables of the law in Exodus are said to have been written; <a name="fr_3657"></a><a href="#fn_3657"><font size="1">3658</font></a>
  and in the Gospel:  “If I by the finger of God cast out demons” <a name="fr_3658"></a><a href="#fn_3658"><font size="1">3659</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p37"></a><i>The fingers of the Lord</i>—The lawgiver Moses, or the prophets.  In the Psalm:  “I will regard the heavens,” that is, the books of the Law and the Prophets, “the works of Thy fingers.” <a name="fr_3659"></a><a href="#fn_3659"><font size="1">3660</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p39"></a><i>The wisdom of the Lord</i>—<i>His Son</i>.  In the apostle:  “Christ the power of God, and the wisdom of God;” <a name="fr_3660"></a><a href="#fn_3660"><font size="1">3661</font></a>
  and in Solomon:  “The wisdom of the Lord reacheth from one end to the other mightily.” <a name="fr_3661"></a><a href="#fn_3661"><font size="1">3662</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p42"></a><i>The womb of the Lord</i>—the hidden recess of Deity out of which He brought forth His Son.  In the Psalm:  “Out of the womb, before Lucifer, have I borne Thee.” <a name="fr_3662"></a><a href="#fn_3662"><font size="1">3663</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p44"></a><i>The feet of the Lord</i>—His immoveableness and eternity.  In the Psalm:  “And thick darkness was under His feet.” <a name="fr_3663"></a><a href="#fn_3663"><font size="1">3664</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p46"></a><i>The throne of the Lord</i>—angels, or saints, or simply sovereign dominion. <a name="fr_3664"></a><a href="#fn_3664"><font size="1">3665</font></a>
   In the Psalm:  “Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever.” <a name="fr_3665"></a><a href="#fn_3665"><font size="1">3666</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p49"></a><i>Seat</i>—the same as above, angels or saints, because the Lord sits upon these.  In the Psalm:  “The Lord sat upon His holy seat.” <a name="fr_3666"></a><a href="#fn_3666"><font size="1">3667</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p51"></a><i>The descent of the Lord</i>—His visitation of men.  As in Micah:  “Behold, the Lord shall come forth from His place; He shall come down trampling under foot the ends of the earth.” <a name="fr_3667"></a><a href="#fn_3667"><font size="1">3668</font></a>
   Likewise in a bad sense.  In Genesis:  “The Lord came down to see the tower.” <a name="fr_3668"></a><a href="#fn_3668"><font size="1">3669</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p54"></a><i>The ascent of the Lord</i>—the raising up of man, who is taken from earth to heaven.  In the Psalm:  “Who ascendeth above the heaven of heavens to the east.” <a name="fr_3669"></a><a href="#fn_3669"><font size="1">3670</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p56"></a><i>The standing of the Lord</i>—the patience of the Deity, by which He bears with sinners that they may come to repentance.  As in Habakkuk:  “He stood and measured the earth; <a name="fr_3670"></a><a href="#fn_3670"><font size="1">3671</font></a>
  and in the Gospel:  “Jesus stood, and bade him be called,” <a name="fr_3671"></a><a href="#fn_3671"><font size="1">3672</font></a>
  that is, the blind man.</p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p59"></a><i>The transition of the Lord</i>—His assumption of our flesh, through which by His birth, His death, His resurrection, His ascent into heaven, He made transitions, so to say.  In the Song of Songs:  “Behold, He cometh, leaping upon the mountains, bounding over the hills.” <a name="fr_3672"></a><a href="#fn_3672"><font size="1">3673</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p61"></a><i>The going</i> <a name="fr_3673"></a><a href="#fn_3673"><font size="1">3674</font></a>
  <i>of the Lord</i>—His coming or visitation.  In the Psalm.</p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p63"></a><i>The way of the Lord</i>—the operation of the Deity.  As in Job, in speaking of the devil:  “He is the beginning of the ways of the Lord.” <a name="fr_3674"></a><a href="#fn_3674"><font size="1">3675</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p65"></a>Again:  <i>The ways of the Lord</i>—His precepts.  In Hosea:  “For the ways of the Lord are straight, and the just shall walk in them.” <a name="fr_3675"></a><a href="#fn_3675"><font size="1">3676</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p67"></a><i>The footsteps of the Lord</i>—the signs of <i>His</i> secret operations.  As in the Psalm:  “And Thy footsteps shall not be known.” <a name="fr_3676"></a><a href="#fn_3676"><font size="1">3677</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p69"></a><i>The knowledge of the Lord</i>—that which makes men to know Him.  To Abraham <i>He says:  “</i>Now I know that thou fearest the Lord;” <a name="fr_3677"></a><a href="#fn_3677"><font size="1">3678</font></a>
  that is, I have made thee to know.</p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p71"></a><i>The ignorance of God</i> <a name="fr_3678"></a><a href="#fn_3678"><font size="1">3679</font></a>
 is His disapproval.  In the Gospel:  “I know you not.” <a name="fr_3679"></a><a href="#fn_3679"><font size="1">3680</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p74"></a><i>The remembrance of God</i>—His mercy, by which He rejects and has mercy on whom He will.  So in Genesis:  “The Lord remembered Noah;” <a name="fr_3680"></a><a href="#fn_3680"><font size="1">3681</font></a>
  and in another passage:  “The Lord hath remembered His people.” <a name="fr_3681"></a><a href="#fn_3681"><font size="1">3682</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p77"></a><i>The repentance of the Lord</i>—<i>His</i> change of procedure. <a name="fr_3682"></a><a href="#fn_3682"><font size="1">3683</font></a>
   As in the book of Kings:  “It repented me that I have made Saul king.” <a name="fr_3683"></a><a href="#fn_3683"><font size="1">3684</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p80"></a><i>The anger and wrath of the Lord</i>—the vengeance of the Deity upon sinners, when He bears with them with a view to punishment, does not <i>at once</i> judge them according to <i>strict</i> equity.  As in the Psalm:  “In His anger and in His wrath will He trouble them.” <a name="fr_3684"></a><a href="#fn_3684"><font size="1">3685</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p82"></a><i>The sleeping of the Lord</i>—when, in the thoughts of some, His faithfulness is not sufficiently wakeful.  In the Psalm:  “Awake, why sleepest Thou, O Lord?” <a name="fr_3685"></a><a href="#fn_3685"><font size="1">3686</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p84"></a><i>The watches of the Lord</i>—in the guardianship of His elect He is always at hand by the presence of <i>His</i> Deity.  In the Psalm:  “Lo! He will not slumber nor sleep.” <a name="fr_3686"></a><a href="#fn_3686"><font size="1">3687</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p86"></a><i>The sitting of the Lord</i>—His ruling.  In the Psalm:  “The Lord sitteth upon His holy seat.” <a name="fr_3687"></a><a href="#fn_3687"><font size="1">3688</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p88"></a><i>The footstool of the Lord</i>—man assumed by the Word; or His saints, as some think.  In the Psalm:  “Worship ye His footstool, for it is holy.”</p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p89"></a><i>The walking of the Lord</i>—the delight of <a name="page_762"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 762</font></a> <a name="x.v.xvi-Page_762"></a>the Deity in the walks of His elect.  In the prophet:  “I will walk in them, and will be their Lord.” <a name="fr_3688"></a><a href="#fn_3688"><font size="1">3689</font></a>
 </p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="x.v.xvi-p91"></a><i>The trumpet of the Lord</i>—His mighty voice.  In the apostle:  “At the command, and at the voice of the archangel, and at the trumpet of God, shall He descend from heaven.” <a name="fr_3689"></a><a href="#fn_3689"><font size="1">3690</font></a>
 </p>
