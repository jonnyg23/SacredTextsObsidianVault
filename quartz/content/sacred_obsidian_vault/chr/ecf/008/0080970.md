 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.xiv.xxxvi-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.xiv.xxxvi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XXXVI.—Farewell to Tripolis.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.xiv.xxxvi-p2"></a>Having spoken thus, he sent the harbingers into Antioch of Syria, bidding them expect him there forthwith.  Then when they had gone, Peter having driven away diseases, sufferings, and demons from great multitudes who were persuaded, and having baptized them in the fountains which are near to the sea, and having celebrated <a name="fr_1139"></a><a href="#fn_1139"><font size="1">1140</font></a>
  the Eucharist, and having appointed Maroones, who had received him into his house, and was now perfected, as their bishop, and having set apart twelve elders, and having designated deacons, and arranged matters relating to widows, and having discoursed on the common good what was profitable for the ordering of the church, and having counselled them to obey the bishop Maroones, three months being now fulfilled; he bade those in Tripolis of Phœnicia farewell, and took his journey to Antioch of Syria, all the people accompanying us with due honour.</p>
