 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.v.xviii-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.v.xviii-p1.1"></a>Chapter XVIII.—Mistake About Simon Magus.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.v.xviii-p2"></a>“Since, then, as I said, some men do not know the rule of combination, thence they do not know who is my precursor Simon.  For if he were known, he would not be believed; but now, not being known, he is improperly believed; and though his deeds are those of a hater, he is loved; and though an enemy, he is received as a friend; and though he be death, he is desired as a saviour; and though fire, he is esteemed as light; and though a deceiver, he is believed as a speaker of truth.”</p> 
 <p class="c22"><a name="vi.iv.v.xviii-p3"></a>Then I Clement, when I heard this, said, “Who then, I pray you, is this who is such a deceiver?  I should like to be informed.”  Then said Peter:  “If you wish to learn, it is in your power to know it from those from whom I also got accurate information on all points respecting him.</p>
