 </p><p class="c35"><a name="vi.iv.vi.ix-p1"></a> <span class="c1"><a name="vi.iv.vi.ix-p1.1"></a>Chapter IX.—Uncertainty of the Scriptures.</span></p> 
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.iv.vi.ix-p2"></a>“Worthy, therefore, of rejection is every one who is willing so much as to hear anything against the monarchy of God; but if any one dares to hear anything against God, as trusting in the Scriptures, let him first of all consider with me that if any one, as he pleases, form a dogma agreeable to himself, and then carefully search the Scriptures, he will be able to produce many testimonies from them in favour of the dogma that he has formed.  How, then, can confidence be placed in them against God, when what every man wishes is found in them?</p>
