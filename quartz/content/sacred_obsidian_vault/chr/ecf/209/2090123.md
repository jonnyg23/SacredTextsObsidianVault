 <p> <a name="iii.iv.iv.xi"></a>
 </p><p class="c33"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p1"></a> <span class="c12"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p1.1"></a>Chapter XI</span>.—<i>Concerning the Cross and here further concerning Faith.</i></p> 
 <p class="c18"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p2"></a>The word <i>Cross</i> <i>is foolishness to those that perish, but to us who are saved it is the power of God</i>. <a name="fr_2342"></a><a href="#fn_2342"><font size="1">2343</font></a>
  For <i>he that is spiritual judgeth all things, but the natural man receiveth not the things of the Spirit.</i> <a name="fr_2343"></a><a href="#fn_2343"><font size="1">2344</font></a>
  For it is foolishness to those who do not receive in faith and who do not consider Gods goodness and omnipotence, but search out divine things with human and natural reasonings. For all the things that are of God are above nature and reason and conception. For should any one consider how and for what purpose God brought all things out of nothing and into being, and aim at arriving at that by natural reasonings, he fails to comprehend it. For knowledge of this kind belongs to spirits and demons. But if any one, under the guidance of faith, should consider the divine goodness <a name="page_80b"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 80b</font></a> <a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-Page_80b"></a>and omnipotence and truth and wisdom and justice, he will find all things smooth and even, and the way straight. <i>But without faith it is impossible to be saved</i> <a name="fr_2344"></a><a href="#fn_2344"><font size="1">2345</font></a>
 . For it is by faith that all things, both human and spiritual, are sustained. For without faith neither does the farmer <a name="fr_2345"></a><a href="#fn_2345"><font size="1">2346</font></a>
  cut his furrow, nor does the merchant commit his life to the raging waves of the sea on a small piece of wood, nor are marriages contracted nor any other step in life taken. By faith we consider that all things were brought out of nothing into being by Gods power. And we direct all things, both divine and human, by faith. Further, faith is assent free from all meddlesome inquisitiveness <a name="fr_2346"></a><a href="#fn_2346"><font size="1">2347</font></a>
 .</p> 
 <p class="c48"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p8"></a>Every action, therefore, and performance of miracles by Christ are most great and divine and marvellous:  but the most marvellous of all is His precious Cross. For no other thing has subdued death, expiated the sin of the first parent <a name="fr_2347"></a><a href="#fn_2347"><font size="1">2348</font></a>
 , despoiled Hades, bestowed the resurrection, granted the power to us of contemning the present and even death itself, prepared the return to our former blessedness, opened the gates of Paradise <a name="fr_2348"></a><a href="#fn_2348"><font size="1">2349</font></a>
 , given our nature a seat at the right hand of God, and made us the children and heirs of God <a name="fr_2349"></a><a href="#fn_2349"><font size="1">2350</font></a>
 , save the Cross of our Lord Jesus Christ. For by the Cross <a name="fr_2350"></a><a href="#fn_2350"><font size="1">2351</font></a>
  all things have been made right. <i>So many of us, the apostle says, as were baptized into Christ, were baptized into His death</i> <a name="fr_2351"></a><a href="#fn_2351"><font size="1">2352</font></a>
 , and <i>as many of you as have been baptized into Christ, have put on Christ</i> <a name="fr_2352"></a><a href="#fn_2352"><font size="1">2353</font></a>
 . Further <i>Christ is the power of God and the wisdom of God</i> <a name="fr_2353"></a><a href="#fn_2353"><font size="1">2354</font></a>
 . Lo! the death of Christ, that is, the Cross, clothed us with the enhypostatic wisdom and power of God. And the power of God is the Word of the Cross, either because Gods might, that is, the victory over death, has been revealed to us by it, or because, just as the four extremities of the Cross are held fast and bound together by the bolt in the middle, so also by Gods power the height and the depth, the length and the breadth, that is, every creature visible and invisible, is maintained <a name="fr_2354"></a><a href="#fn_2354"><font size="1">2355</font></a>
 .</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p17"></a>This was given to us as a sign on our forehead, just as the circumcision was given to Israel:  for by it we believers are separated and distinguished from unbelievers. This is the shield and weapon against, and trophy over, the devil. <i>This is the seal that the destroyer may not touch you</i> <a name="fr_2355"></a><a href="#fn_2355"><font size="1">2356</font></a>
 , as saith the Scripture. This is the resurrection of those lying in death, the support of the standing, the staff of the weak, the rod of the flock, the safe conduct of the earnest, the perfection of those that press forwards, the salvation of soul and body, the aversion of all things evil, the patron of all things good, the taking away of sin, the plant of resurrection, the tree of eternal life.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p19"></a>So, then, this same truly precious and august tree <a name="fr_2356"></a><a href="#fn_2356"><font size="1">2357</font></a>
 , on which Christ hath offered Himself as a sacrifice for our sakes, is to be worshipped as sanctified by contact with His holy body and blood; likewise the nails, the spear, the clothes, His sacred tabernacles which are the manger, the cave, Golgotha, which bringeth salvation <a name="fr_2357"></a><a href="#fn_2357"><font size="1">2358</font></a>
 , the tomb which giveth life, Sion, the chief stronghold of the churches and the like, are to be worshipped. In the words of David, the father of God <a name="fr_2358"></a><a href="#fn_2358"><font size="1">2359</font></a>
 , <i>We shall go into His tabernacles, we shall worship at the place where His feet stood</i> <a name="fr_2359"></a><a href="#fn_2359"><font size="1">2360</font></a>
 . And that it is the Cross that is meant is made clear by what follows, <i>Arise, O Lord, into Thy Rest</i> <a name="fr_2360"></a><a href="#fn_2360"><font size="1">2361</font></a>
 . For the resurrection comes after the Cross. For if of those things which we love, house and couch and garment, are to be longed after, how much the rather should we long after that which belonged to God, our Saviour <a name="fr_2361"></a><a href="#fn_2361"><font size="1">2362</font></a>
 , by means of which we are in truth saved.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p26"></a>Moreover we worship even the image of the precious and life-giving Cross, although made of another tree, not honouring the tree (God forbid) but the image as a symbol of Christ. For He said to His disciples, admonishing them, <i>Then shall appear the sign of the Son of Man in Heaven</i> <a name="fr_2362"></a><a href="#fn_2362"><font size="1">2363</font></a>
 , meaning the Cross. And so also the angel of the resurrection said to the woman, <i>Ye seek Jesus of Nazareth which was crucified</i> <a name="fr_2363"></a><a href="#fn_2363"><font size="1">2364</font></a>
 . And the Apostle said, <i>We preach Christ crucified</i> <a name="fr_2364"></a><a href="#fn_2364"><font size="1">2365</font></a>
 . For there are many Christs and many Jesuses, but one crucified. He does not say speared but crucified. It behoves us, then, to worship the sign of Christ <a name="fr_2365"></a><a href="#fn_2365"><font size="1">2366</font></a>
 . For wherever the sign may be, there also will He be. But it does not behove us to worship the material of which the image of the Cross is composed, even though it be gold or precious stones, after it is destroyed, if that should happen. Everything, therefore, that is dedicated to God we worship, conferring the adoration on Him.</p> 
 <p class="c19"><a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-p31"></a>The tree of life which was planted by God in Paradise pre-figured this precious Cross. <a name="page_81b"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 81b</font></a> <a name="iii.iv.iv.xi-Page_81b"></a>For since death was by a tree, it was fitting that life and resurrection should be bestowed by a tree <a name="fr_2366"></a><a href="#fn_2366"><font size="1">2367</font></a>
 . Jacob, when He worshipped the top of Josephs staff, was the first to image the Cross, and when he blessed his sons with crossed hands <a name="fr_2367"></a><a href="#fn_2367"><font size="1">2368</font></a>
  he made most clearly the sign of the cross. Likewise <a name="fr_2368"></a><a href="#fn_2368"><font size="1">2369</font></a>
  also did Moses rod, when it smote the sea in the figure of the cross and saved Israel, while it overwhelmed Pharaoh in the depths; likewise also the hands stretched out crosswise and routing Amalek; and the bitter water made sweet by a tree, and the rock rent and pouring forth streams of water <a name="fr_2369"></a><a href="#fn_2369"><font size="1">2370</font></a>
 , and the rod that meant for Aaron the dignity of the high priesthood <a name="fr_2370"></a><a href="#fn_2370"><font size="1">2371</font></a>
 :  and the serpent lifted in triumph on a tree as though it were dead <a name="fr_2371"></a><a href="#fn_2371"><font size="1">2372</font></a>
 , the tree bringing salvation to those who in faith saw their enemy dead, just as Christ was nailed to the tree in the flesh of sin which yet knew no sin <a name="fr_2372"></a><a href="#fn_2372"><font size="1">2373</font></a>
 . The mighty Moses cried <a name="fr_2373"></a><a href="#fn_2373"><font size="1">2374</font></a>
 , <i>You will see your life hanging on the tree before your eyes</i>, and Isaiah likewise, <i>I have spread out my hands all the day unto a faithless and rebellious people</i> <a name="fr_2374"></a><a href="#fn_2374"><font size="1">2375</font></a>
 . But may we who worship this <a name="fr_2375"></a><a href="#fn_2375"><font size="1">2376</font></a>
  obtain a part in Christ the crucified. Amen.</p>
