 </p><p class="c5"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p1"></a>  <a name="page_lxxiv"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. lxxiv</font></a> <a name="vi.ii.xi-Page_lxxiv"></a><span class="c1"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p1.1"></a>X.—Editions and Manuscripts.</span></p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p2"></a>Among the chief editions and <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p2.1"></a>mss.</span> the following may be mentioned:</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p3"></a>The Editio Princeps of the complete extant works of Basil in the original Greek is that which Froben published for Janus Cornarius at Bale in 1551.  But Froben had already published in 1532, under the editorship of Erasmus, an edition containing the <i>De Spiritu Sancto</i>, the <i>Hexæmeron</i>, the <i>Homilies on the Psalms</i>, twenty-nine different <i>Homilies</i> and some <i>Letters</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p4"></a>A Venetian edition, published by Fabius in 1535, comprised the <i>Moralia</i>, as well as the dubious book on Virginity, three books against Eunomius, and the tract against the Sabellians, Arians, and Anomœans.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p5"></a>The Greek editions had been preceded by a Latin version at Rome, by Raphael Volaterranus in 1515, of which the autograph manuscript is in the British Museum, and by another at Paris in 1525, and by a third Latin edition issued at Cologne in 1531.  These were followed by other editions printed at Paris, Antwerp, and Cologne.  In 1618 Fronton du Duc, commonly known as Ducæus, published, in conjunction with Frederic Morel, an edition in two folio volumes containing a Latin version as well as the Greek.  The edition of the French Dominican Father Francis Combefis, was published shortly after his death in 1679.  The most important step in the direction of accuracy and completeness was taken by Julian Garnier, a Benedictine Father of the Congregation of St. Maur.  He revised and corrected the Greek text of earlier editions on the authority of a number of manuscripts in Paris, Italy, and England, and issued the first of his three folio volumes at Paris, at the press of John Baptist Coignard, in 1721.  The third volume did not appear till 1730, five years after Garniers death.  In the meanwhile the editorial work had been taken up by Prudent Maran, another Benedictine, to whom are due a careful and voluminous biographical notice, many notes, and a chronological arrangement of the Letters.  This was reissued in three 4° volumes in Paris in 1889, and is the basis of the edition published, with additions, by the Abbé Jacques Paul Migne, in the <i>Patrologia Græca</i>, in 1857.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p6"></a>An important edition of a separate work is the revised text, with notes and introduction, of the <i>De Spiritu Sancto</i>, by the Rev. C. F. H. Johnston, published at the Clarendon Press in 1892.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p7"></a>German translations were published by Count Schweikhard at Ingolstadt in 1591 (Ceillier VI. viii. 8), and by J. von Wendel at Vienna in 1776–78.  There have also been issued <i><span><a name="vi.ii.xi-p7.1"></a>Basilius des Grossen auserlesenes Homilien, übersetzt und mit Ammerkungen versehen von J. G. Krabinger</span></i>, Landshut, 1839, and <i><span><a name="vi.ii.xi-p7.2"></a>Auserlesene Schriften, übersetzt von Gröne</span></i>, Kempten, 1875.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p8"></a>Homilies and Orations were published in Italian in 1711 by Gio. Maria Lucchini.  <i>Omelie Scelte</i>, translated by A. M. Ricci, were published in Florence in 1732.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p9"></a>Many important extracts are translated into French in the <i><span><a name="vi.ii.xi-p9.1"></a>Historie Générale des Auteurs Sacrés</span></i> of the Benedictine Remy Ceillier (Paris, 1737).</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p10"></a>E. Fialon, in his <i>Ét. Hist</i>. (1869) has translated the <i><span class="Greek"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p10.1"></a>Ηεξͅμερον</span></i>; and in 1889 the <i><span><a name="vi.ii.xi-p10.2"></a>Panégyrique due Martyr</span></i> <i>Gordius</i> was published in French by J. Genouille.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p11"></a>A complete account of the bibliography of St. Basil is given in the <i>Notitia ex Bibliotheca Fabricii</i> (Ed. Harles, tom. ix. 1804), in Mignes ed. vol. i., <i>Prolegomena</i> p. ccxli.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p12"></a>In 1888 a translation of the <i>De Spiritu Sancto</i>, by G. Lewis, was included in the Christian Classic Series.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p13"></a>Of all the smaller works a great popularity, as far as popularity can be gauged by the number of editions and translations, has belonged to the <i>Advice to the Young</i> and the <i>Homily on the Forty Martyrs</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14"></a>The <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14.1"></a>mss.</span> collated by the Ben. Edd. for their edition of the <i>De Spiritu Sancto</i> are five entitled <i><span><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14.2"></a>Regii</span></i>, and a sixth known as <i>Colbertinus</i>, now in the national library at Paris.  The Ben. <i>Regius Secundus</i> (2293) is described by Omont (<i><span><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14.3"></a>Inventaire Sommaire des <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14.4"></a>mss.</span> Grecs</span></i>) as of the Xth c., the <i>Colbertinus</i> (4529) and the <i>Regius Tertius</i>(2893) as of the <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14.5"></a>XI</span>th c., and the <i>Regius Primus</i> (2286), <i>Regius Quartus</i> (2896), <i>Regius Quintus</i>(3430) as of the <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p14.6"></a>XIV</span>th c.</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p15"></a>For his edition, Mr. C. F. H. Johnston also collated or had collated 22,509 Add. <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p15.1"></a>mss.</span>, Xth c., in the British Museum; codd. Theol. 18, <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p15.4"></a>XIV</span>th c., also at Vienna; Cod. xxiii, <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p15.5"></a>XI</span>th c., in the Library of the Holy Synod at Moscow; <a name="page_lxxv"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. lxxv</font></a> <a name="vi.ii.xi-Page_lxxv"></a>500 (Reg. 1824, 3) G, <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p15.6"></a>XI</span>th c., at Paris; Cod. lviii., Xth c., at St. Marks, Venice; Cod. lxvi., <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p15.7"></a>XII</span>th c., also at St. Marks, Venice; Codd.  <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p16"></a>For the <i>Hexæmeron</i> the Ben. Edd. used eight <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p16.1"></a>mss.</span> styled <i>Regii</i>, and numbered respectively 1824, 2286 (originally in the collection of Henry II. at Fontainebleau, the <i>Regius Primus</i> of the enumeration for the <i>De Spiritu Sancto</i>, but the <i>Secundus</i> for that of the <i>Hexæmeron</i>), 2287 (1°), 2287 (2°), 2349, 2892, 2896 (the <i>Regius Quartus</i> of the <i>De Spiritu Sancto</i>), and 2989, two <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p16.2"></a>mss.</span> entitled <i>Colbertinus</i>, 3069 and 4721, two Coistiniani, 229, <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p16.3"></a>IX</span>th c., and 235; and a <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p16.4"></a>ms.</span> in the Bodleian, “a doctissimo viro Joanne Wolf collatus.”</p>  
 <p class="c21"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p17"></a>The sources of the Ben. Ed. of the <i>Letters</i> were Coislinianus 237, <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p17.1"></a>XI</span>th c., a Codex Harlæanus of the Xth or <span class="c14"><a name="vi.ii.xi-p17.2"></a>XI</span>th c., and a Codex Medicæus, Codex Regius 2293, Codex Regius 2897, Codex Regius 2896, Codex Regius 2502, Codex Regius 1824, Codex Regius 1906, and Codex Regius 1908.</p>
