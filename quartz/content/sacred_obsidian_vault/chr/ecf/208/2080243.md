 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.clxxi-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.clxxi-p1.1"></a>Letter CLXX. <a name="fr_2563"></a><a href="#fn_2563"><font size="1">2564</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="ix.clxxi-p3"></a><i>To Glycerius</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.clxxi-p4"></a><span class="c14"><a name="ix.clxxi-p4.1"></a>How</span> far will your mad folly go?  How long will you counsel mischief against yourself?  How long will you go on rousing me to wrath, and bringing shame on the common order of solitaries?  Return.  Put confidence in God, and in me, who imitate Gods loving-kindness.  If I rebuked you like a father, like a father I will forgive you.  This is the treatment you shall receive from me, for many others are making supplication in your behalf, and before all the rest your own presbyter, for whose grey hairs and compassionate disposition I feel much respect.  Continue longer to hold aloof from me and you have quite fallen from your degree. <a name="fr_2564"></a><a href="#fn_2564"><font size="1">2565</font></a>
   You will also fall away from God, for with your songs and your garb <a name="fr_2565"></a><a href="#fn_2565"><font size="1">2566</font></a>
  you are leading the young women not to God, but to the pit.</p>

 <p class="endnote"><a name="ix.clxxi-p5"></a> <span class="Greek"><a name="ix.clxxi-p5.1"></a>τοῦ βαθμοῦ</span>.  <i>cf</i>. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#013">1 Tim. iii. 13</a><a name="ix.clxxi-p5.2"></a>.  <span class="Greek"><a name="ix.clxxi-p5.3"></a>οἱ καλῶς διακονησαντες βαθμὸν ἑαυτοῖς καλὸν περιποιοῦνται</span>.  There seems an evident allusion to this passage, but not such as to enable Basil to be positively ranked with Chrysostom in his apparent interpretation of <span class="Greek"><a name="ix.clxxi-p5.4"></a>βαθμός</span> objectively of preferment, or with Theodoret in his subjective idea of honour with God.  Apparently the “degree” is the Diaconate.</p>
 <a name="fn_2565"></a><a href="2080243.htm#fr_2565">218:2566</a> <a name="ix.clxxi-p5.5"></a>
 <p class="endnote"><a name="ix.clxxi-p6"></a> <span class="Greek"><a name="ix.clxxi-p6.1"></a>στολή</span>.  The technical use of this word for a “stole” is not earlier than the ninth century.  It was indeed used for a sacred vestment, <i>e.g.</i> the sacred robe which Constantine presented to Macarius, Bishop of Jerusalem.  (Theodoret ii. 27.)  In Latin “<i>stola</i>” designated the distinctive dress of the matron, and it seems to be used with a suggestion of effeminacy.</p>
