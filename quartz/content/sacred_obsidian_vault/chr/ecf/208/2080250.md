 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.clxxviii-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.clxxviii-p1.1"></a>Letter CLXXVII. <a name="fr_2585"></a><a href="#fn_2585"><font size="1">2586</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="ix.clxxviii-p3"></a><i>To Saphronius the Master</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.clxxviii-p4"></a><span class="c14"><a name="ix.clxxviii-p4.1"></a>To</span> reckon up all those who have received kindness at your excellencys hand, for my sake, is no easy task; so many are there whom I feel that I have benefited through your kind aid, a boon which the Lord has given me to help me in these very serious times.  Worthiest of all is he who is now introduced to you by my letter, the reverend brother Eusebius, attacked by a ridiculous calumny which it depends upon you alone in your uprightness, to destroy.  I beseech you, therefore, both as respecting the right and as being humanely disposed, to grant me your accustomed favours, by adopting the cause of Eusebius as your own, and championing him, and, at the same time, truth.  It is no small thing that he has the right on his side; and this, if he be not stricken down by the present crisis, he will have no difficulty in proving plainly and without possibility of contradiction.</p>
