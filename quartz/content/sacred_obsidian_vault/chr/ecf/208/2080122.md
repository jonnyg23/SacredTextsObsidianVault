 </p><p class="c26"><a name="ix.l-p1"></a>  <span class="c18"><a name="ix.l-p1.1"></a>Letter XLIX. <a name="fr_2168"></a><a href="#fn_2168"><font size="1">2169</font></a>
 </span></p>  
 <p class="c55"><a name="ix.l-p3"></a><i>To Arcadius the Bishop</i>.</p>  
 <p class="c20"><a name="ix.l-p4"></a>I <span class="c14"><a name="ix.l-p4.1"></a>thanked</span> the Holy God when I read your letter, most pious brother.  I pray that I may not be unworthy of the expectations you have formed of me, and that you will enjoy a full reward for the honour which you pay me in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ.  I was exceedingly pleased to hear that you have been occupied in a matter eminently becoming a Christian, have raised a house to the glory of God, and have in practical earnest loved, as it is written, “the beauty of the house of the Lord,” <a name="fr_2169"></a><a href="#fn_2169"><font size="1">2170</font></a>
  and have so provided for yourself that heavenly mansion which is prepared in His rest for them that love the Lord.  If I am able to find any relics of martyrs, I pray that I may take part in your earnest endeavour.  If “the righteous shall be had in everlasting remembrance,” <a name="fr_2170"></a><a href="#fn_2170"><font size="1">2171</font></a>
  I shall without doubt have a share in the good fame which the Holy One will give you.</p>
