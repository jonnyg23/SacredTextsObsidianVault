 <p>
 St. Anselm of Canterbury (b. 1033 d. 1099)
 was a medieval Italian cleric, philosopher
 and theologian.
 He originated the ontological argument for the existence of God in his
 treatise <a href="ans006.htm">Proslogium</a> (or <cite>Proslogion</cite>).
 Anselm held the post of the Archbishop of Canterbury from 1093 to 1109.
