 <p><a name="v.LI-p1"></a> 		  </p><h3 align="center"><a name="v.LI-p1.1"></a>CHAPTER LI.</h3>   		  
 <p><a name="v.LI-p2"></a>  		  
 </p><p><a name="v.LI-p3"></a>Each loves himself and the other with equal love.</p>  		  
 <p><a name="v.LI-p4"></a>  		  
 </p><p><a name="v.LI-p5"></a>BUT if the supreme Spirit loves himself, no doubt the Father loves himself, the Son loves himself, and the one the other; since the Father separately is the supreme Spirit, and the Son separately is the supreme<a name="page_113"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 113</font></a> <a name="v.LI-Page_113"></a> Spirit, and both at once one Spirit. And, since each equally remembers himself and the other, and conceives equally of himself and the other; and since what is loved, or loves in the Father, or in the Son, is altogether the same, necessarily each loves 			 himself and the other with an equal love.</p>
