 <p class="BOOK-CHAP-BLU-24-CEN17"><a name="v-p1"></a>PSALM 70</p>  		
 <p><a name="v-p2"></a>This psalm is merely a part of the fortieth, and the inscription, <i>To</i> <i>call to remembrance, </i>is perhaps designed to indicate this; David having taken these five verses out of that other psalm, and accommodated them for being used on some particular occasion. I shall only here repeat the words of the text; and would refer the reader for the interpretation to the proper 		  place.</p>  		
 <p class="PoetryLast-DkBlu-11-Cent19"><a name="v-p3"></a>To the chief musician of David, to call to remembrance.</p>  <a name="v.i"></a>  		   			  				   				
 <p class="TableCaption20"><a name="v.i-p1"></a><a name="v.i-p1.1"></a><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa070.htm#001">Psalm 70:1-5</a><a name="v.i-p1.1"></a></p> 				  			  				   				
 <p><a name="v.i-p2"></a><b>1.</b> O God! make haste to deliver me: O Jehovah! hasten to my help. <b>2.</b> Let those who seek my life be ashamed and confounded; let those who desire my hurt be turned backward, and put to confusion <b>3.</b> Let those who say to me, Aha! aha! perish as a reward of their shame. <b>4.</b> Let all those who seek thee rejoice and exult in thee: let those who love thy salvation say, Let God be magnified for evermore! <b>5.</b> As for me, I am 				  poor and needy: O God! hasten to me: thou art my help and my deliverer: O Jehovah! make no delay.</p>
