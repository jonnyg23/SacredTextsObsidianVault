 <p><a name="v.vi-p1"></a>    
 </p><p class="HDG-C-Gr-16-Pref08"><a name="v.vi-p2"></a>CHAPTER 6</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p3"></a><b>1 </b>Brethren, although a man be overtaken in any fault, ye, who are spiritual, restore such a man in the spirit of meekness; considering thyself, lest thou also be tempted.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p4"></a><b>2 </b>Bear ye one another’s burdens, and thus fulfill the law of Christ.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p5"></a><b>3 </b>For if any one thinketh himself to be something, though he is nothing, he deceiveth himself.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p6"></a><b>4 </b>But let every one prove his own work; and then shall he have glory in himself alone, and not in another.   	  <a name="fr_197"></a><a href="#fn_197"><font size="1">198</font></a>
 </p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p8"></a><b>5 </b>For every one shall bear his own burden.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p9"></a><b>6 </b>Let him who is taught in the word communicate to the teacher in all good things.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p10"></a><b>7 </b>Do not mistake: God is not mocked; for what a man shall have sowed, that will he also reap.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p11"></a><b>8 </b>For he who soweth to his flesh, shall of the flesh reap corruption; but he who soweth to the Spirit, shall of the Spirit reap everlasting life.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p12"></a><b>9 </b>And let us not weary in doing good; for if we do not faint, we shall reap in due time.   	  <a name="fr_198"></a><a href="#fn_198"><font size="1">199</font></a>
 </p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p14"></a><b>10 </b>Therefore, while we have opportunity, let us do good to all, but especially to them who are of the household of faith.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p15"></a><b>11 </b>Ye see what a letter   	  <a name="fr_199"></a><a href="#fn_199"><font size="1">200</font></a>
  	  I have written with my own hand.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p17"></a><b>12 </b>As many as wish to please outwardly in the flesh,   	  <a name="fr_200"></a><a href="#fn_200"><font size="1">201</font></a>
  	  they constrain you to be circumcised; only that they may not suffer persecution for the cross of Christ.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p19"></a><b>13 </b>For neither they who hold by circumcision do themselves keep the law; but they wish you to be circumcised, that they may glory in your flesh.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p20"></a><b>14 </b>But far be it from me to glory, unless in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by which the world is crucified to me, and I to the world.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p21"></a><b>15 </b>For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision availeth anything nor uncircumcision; but a new creature.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p22"></a><b>16 </b>And as many as walk by this rule, peace (be) on them, and mercy, and on the Israel of God.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p23"></a><b>17 </b>Henceforth let no man give me annoyance; for I bear in my body the marks of the Lord Jesus.</p>    
 <p><a name="v.vi-p24"></a><b>18 </b>The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ (be) with your spirit. Amen.</p>    
 <p class="3rdLevelAllCap12Bld14"><a name="v.vi-p25"></a>TO THE GALATIANS IT WAS WRITTEN FROM ROME.</p>
