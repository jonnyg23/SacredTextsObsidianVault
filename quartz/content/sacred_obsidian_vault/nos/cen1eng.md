 <center><h1>Century I</h1></center>
 <p>
 1<br>
 Sitting alone at night in secret study;<br>
 it is placed on the brass tripod.<br>
 A slight flame comes out of the emptiness and<br>
 makes successful that which should not be believed in vain.<br>
 </p><p>
 2<br>
 The wand in the hand is placed in the middle of the tripod's legs.<br>
 With water he sprinkles both the hem of his garment and his foot.<br>
 A voice, fear: he trembles in his robes.<br>
 Divine splendor; the God sits nearby.<br>
 </p><p>
 3<br>
 When the litters are overturned by the whirlwind <br>
 and faces are covered by cloaks,<br>
 the new republic will be troubled by its people.<br>
 At this time the reds and the whites will rule wrongly.<br>
 </p><p>
 4<br>
 In the world there will be made a king<br>
 who will have little peace and a short life.<br>
 At this time the ship of the Papacy will be lost,<br>
 governed to its greatest detriment.<br>
 </p><p>
 5<br>
 They will be driven away for a long drawn out fight.<br>
 The countryside will be most grievously troubled.<br>
 Town and country will have greater struggle.<br>
 Carcassonne and Narbonne will have their hearts tried.<br>
 </p><p>
 6<br>
 The eye of Ravenna will be forsaken,<br>
 when his wings will fail at his feet.<br>
 The two of Bresse will have made a constitution<br>
 for Turin and Vercelli, which the French will trample underfoot<br>
 </p><p>
 7<br>
 Arrived too late, the act has been done.<br>
 The wind was against them, letters intercepted on their way.<br>
 The conspirators were fourteen of a party.<br>
 By Rousseau shall these enterprises be undertaken.<br>
 </p><p>
 8<br>
 How often will you be captured, O city of the sun ?<br>
 Changing laws that are barbaric and vain.<br>
 Bad times approach you. No longer will you be enslaved.<br>
 Great Hadrie will revive your veins.<br>
 </p><p>
 9<br>
 From the Orient will come the African heart<br>
 to trouble Hadrie and the heirs of Romulus.<br>
 Accompanied by the Libyan fleet <br>
 the temples of Malta and nearby islands shall be deserted.<br>
 </p><p>
 10<br>
 A coffin is put into the vault of iron,<br>
 where seven children of the king are held.<br>
 The ancestors and forebears will come forth from the depths of hell,<br>
 lamenting to see thus dead the fruit of their line.<br>
 </p><p>
 11<br>
 The motion of senses, heart, feet and hands<br>
 will be in agreement between Naples, Lyon and Sicily.<br>
 Swords fire, floods, then the noble Romans drowned,<br>
 killed or dead because of a weak brain.<br>
 </p><p>
 12<br>
 There will soon be talk of a treacherous man, who rules a short time, <br>
 quickly raised from low to high estate.<br>
 He will suddenly turn disloyal and volatile.<br>
 This man will govern Verona.<br>
 </p><p>
 13<br>
 Through anger and internal hatreds, the exiles <br>
 will hatch a great plot against the king.<br>
 Secretly they will place enemies as a threat,<br>
 and his own old (adherents) will find sedition against them.<br>
 </p><p>
 14<br>
 From the enslaved populace, songs, chants and demands,<br>
 while Princes and Lords are held captive in prisons.<br>
 These will in the future by headless idiots<br>
 be received as divine prayers<br>
 </p><p>
 15<br>
 .Mars threatens us with the force of war<br>
 and will cause blood to be spilt seventy times.<br>
 The clergy will be both exalted and reviled moreover,<br>
 by those who wish to learn nothing of them.<br>
 </p><p>
 16<br>
 A scythe joined with a pond in Sagittarius<br>
 at its highest ascendant.<br>
 Plague, famine, death from military hands;<br>
 the century approaches its renewal.<br>
 </p><p>
 17<br>
 For forty years the rainbow will not be seen.<br>
 For forty years it will be seen every day.<br>
 The dry earth will grow more parched,<br>
 and there will be great floods when it is seen.<br>
 </p><p>
 18<br>
 Because of French discord and negligence<br>
 an opening shall be given to the Mohammedans.<br>
 The land and sea of Siena will be soaked in blood,<br>
 and the port of Marseilles covered with ships and sails.<br>
 </p><p>
 19<br>
 When the snakes surround the altar,<br>
 and the Trojan blood is troubled by the Spanish.<br>
 Because of them, a great number will be lessened.<br>
 The leader flees, hidden in the swampy marshes.<br>
 </p><p>
 20<br>
 The cities of Tours, Orleans, Blois, Angers, Reims and Nantes<br>
 are troubled by sudden change.<br>
 Tents will be pitched by (people) of foreign tongues;<br>
 rivers, darts at Rennes, shaking of land and sea.<br>
 </p><p>
 21<br>
 The rock holds in its depths white clay<br>
 which will come out milk-white from a cleft<br>
 Needlessly troubled people will not dare touch it,<br>
 unaware that the foundation of the earth is of clay.<br>
 </p><p>
 22<br>
 A thing existing without any senses<br>
 will cause its own end to happen through artifice.<br>
 At Autun, Chalan, Langres and the two Sens<br>
 there will be great damage from hail and ice.<br>
 </p><p>
 23<br>
 In the third month, at sunrise,<br>
 the Boar and the Leopard meet on the battlefield.<br>
 The fatigued Leopard looks up to heaven<br>
 and sees an eagle playing around the sun.<br>
 </p><p>
 24<br>
 At the New City he is thoughtful to condemn;<br>
 the bird of prey offers himself to the Gods.<br>
 After victory he pardons his captives.<br>
 At Cremona and Mantua great hardships will be suffered.<br>
 </p><p>
 25<br>
 The lost thing is discovered, hidden for many centuries.<br>
 Pasteur will be celebrated almost as a God-like figure.<br>
 This is when the moon completes her great cycle,<br>
 but by other rumors he shall be dishonored.<br>
 </p><p>
 26<br>
 The great man will be struck down in the day by a thunderbolt.<br>
 An evil deed, foretold by the bearer of a petition.<br>
 According to the prediction another falls at night time.<br>
 Conflict at Reims, London, and pestilence in Tuscany.<br>
 </p><p>
 27<br>
 Beneath the oak tree of Gienne, struck by lightning, <br>
 the treasure is hidden not far from there.<br>
 That which for many centuries had been gathered,<br>
 when found, a man will die, his eye pierced by a spring.<br>
 </p><p>
 28<br>
 Tobruk will fear the barbarian fleet for a time,<br>
 then much later the Western fleet.<br>
 Cattle, people, possessions, all will be quite lost.<br>
 What a deadly combat in Taurus and Libra.<br>
 </p><p>
 29<br>
 When the fish that travels over both land and sea<br>
 is cast up on to the shore by a great wave,<br>
 its shape foreign, smooth and frightful.<br>
 From the sea the enemies soon reach the walls.<br>
 </p><p>
 30<br>
 Because of the storm at sea the foreign ship<br>
 will approach an unknown port.<br>
 Notwithstanding the signs of the palm branches,<br>
 afterwards there is death and pillage. Good advice comes too late.<br>
 </p><p>
 31<br>
 The wars in France will last for so many years<br>
 beyond the reign of the Castulon kings.<br>
 An uncertain victory will crown three great ones,<br>
 the Eagle, the Cock, the Moon, the Lion, the Sun in its house.<br>
 </p><p>
 32<br>
 The great Empire will soon be exchanged <br>
 for a small place, which soon will begin to grow.<br>
 A small place of tiny area <br>
 in the middle of which he will come to lay down his scepter.<br>
 </p><p>
 33<br>
 Near a great bridge near a spacious plain<br>
 the great lion with the Imperial forces<br>
 will cause a falling outside the austere city.<br>
 Through fear the gates will be unlocked for him.<br>
 </p><p>
 34<br>
 The bird of prey flying to the left,<br>
 before battle is joined with the French, he makes preparations.<br>
 Some will regard him as good, others bad or uncertain.<br>
 The weaker party will regard him as a good omen.<br>
 </p><p>
 35<br>
 The young lion will overcome the older one,<br>
 in a field of combat in single fight:<br>
 He will pierce his eyes in their golden cage;<br>
 two wounds in one, then he dies a cruel death.<br>
 </p><p>
 36<br>
 Too late the king will repent <br>
 that he did not put his adversary to death.<br>
 But he will soon come to agree to far greater things<br>
 which will cause all his line to die.<br>
 </p><p>
 37<br>
 Shortly before sun set, battle is engaged.<br>
 A great nation is uncertain.<br>
 Overcome, the sea port makes no answer,<br>
 the bridge and the grave both in foreign places.<br>
 </p><p>
 38<br>
 The Sun and the Eagle will appear to the victor.<br>
 An empty answer assured to the defeated.<br>
 Neither bugle nor shouts will stop the soldiers.<br>
 Liberty and peace, if achieved in time through death.<br>
 </p><p>
 39<br>
 At night the last one will be strangled in his bed <br>
 because he became too involved with the blond heir elect.<br>
 The Empire is enslaved and three men substituted.<br>
 He is put to death with neither letter nor packet read.<br>
 </p><p>
 40<br>
 The false trumpet concealing madness<br>
 will cause Byzantium to change its laws.<br>
 From Egypt there will go forth a man who wants<br>
 the edict withdrawn, changing money and standards.<br>
 </p><p>
 41<br>
 The city is besieged and assaulted by night;<br>
 few have escaped; a battle not far from the sea.<br>
 A woman faints with joy at the return of her son,<br>
 poison in the folds of the hidden letters.<br>
 </p><p>
 42<br>
 The tenth day of the April Calends, calculated in Gothic fashion<br>
 is revived again by wicked people.<br>
 The fire is put out and the diabolic gathering<br>
 seek the bones of the demon of Psellus.<br>
 </p><p>
 43<br>
 Before the Empire changes<br>
 a very wonderful event will take place.<br>
 The field moved, the pillar of porphyry <br>
 put in place, changed on the gnarled rock.<br>
 </p><p>
 44<br>
 In a short time sacrifices will be resumed,<br>
 those opposed will be put (to death) like martyrs.<br>
 The will no longer be monks, abbots or novices.<br>
 Honey shall be far more expensive than wax.<br>
 </p><p>
 45<br>
 A founder of sects, much trouble for the accuser:<br>
 A beast in the theater prepares the scene and plot.<br>
 The author ennobled by acts of older times;<br>
 the world is confused by schismatic sects.<br>
 </p><p>
 46<br>
 Very near Auch, Lectoure and Mirande<br>
 a great fire will fall from the sky for three nights.<br>
 The cause will appear both stupefying and marvelous;<br>
 shortly afterwards there will be an earthquake.<br>
 </p><p>
 47<br>
 The speeches of Lake Leman will become angered,<br>
 the days will drag out into weeks,<br>
 then months, then years, then all will fail.<br>
 The authorities will condemn their useless powers.<br>
 </p><p>
 48<br>
 When twenty years of the Moon's reign have passed<br>
 another will take up his reign for seven thousand years.<br>
 When the exhausted Sun takes up his cycle<br>
 then my prophecy and threats will be accomplished.<br>
 </p><p>
 49<br>
 Long before these happenings<br>
 the people of the East, influenced by the Moon,<br>
 in the year 1700 will cause many to be carried away,<br>
 and will almost subdue the Northern area.<br>
 </p><p>
 50<br>
 From the three water signs will be born a man<br>
 who will celebrate Thursday as his holiday.<br>
 His renown, praise, rule and power will grow <br>
 on land and sea, bringing trouble to the East.<br>
 </p><p>
 51<br>
 The head of Aries, Jupiter and Saturn.<br>
 Eternal God, what changes !<br>
 Then the bad times will return again after a long century;<br>
 what turmoil in France and Italy.<br>
 </p><p>
 52<br>
 Two evil influences in conjunction in Scorpio.<br>
 The great lord is murdered in his room.<br>
 A newly appointed king persecutes the Church,<br>
 the lower (parts of) Europe and in the North.<br>
 </p><p>
 53<br>
 Alas, how we will see a great nation sorely troubled<br>
 and the holy law in utter ruin.<br>
 Christianity (governed) throughout by other laws,<br>
 when a new source of gold and silver is discovered.<br>
 </p><p>
 54<br>
 Two revolutions will be caused by the evil scythe bearer<br>
 making a change of reign and centuries.<br>
 The mobile sign thus moves into its house:<br>
 Equal in favor to both sides.<br>
 </p><p>
 55<br>
 In the land with a climate opposite to Babylon<br>
 there will be great shedding of blood.<br>
 Heaven will seem unjust both on land and sea and in the air.<br>
 Sects, famine, kingdoms, plagues, confusion.<br>
 </p><p>
 56<br>
 Sooner and later you will see great changes made,<br>
 dreadful horrors and vengeances.<br>
 For as the moon is thus led by its angel<br>
 the heavens draw near to the Balance.<br>
 </p><p>
 57<br>
 The trumpet shakes with great discord.<br>
 An agreement broken: lifting the face to heaven:<br>
 the bloody mouth will swim with blood;<br>
 the face anointed with milk and honey lies on the ground.<br>
 </p><p>
 58<br>
 Through a slit in the belly a creature will be born with two heads<br>
 and four arms: it will survive for some few years.<br>
 The day that Alquiloie celebrates his festivals<br>
 Fossana, Turin and the ruler of Ferrara will follow.<br>
 </p><p>
 59<br>
 The exiles deported to the islands<br>
 at the advent of an even more cruel king<br>
 will be murdered. Two will be burnt <br>
 who were not sparing in their speech.<br>
 </p><p>
 60<br>
 An Emperor will be born near Italy,<br>
 who will cost the Empire very dearly.<br>
 They will say, when they see his allies,<br>
 that he is less a prince than a butcher.<br>
 </p><p>
 61<br>
 The wretched, unfortunate republic <br>
 will again be ruined by a new authority.<br>
 The great amount of ill will accumulated in exile<br>
 will make the Swiss break their important agreement.<br>
 </p><p>
 62<br>
 Alas! what a great loss there will be to learning<br>
 before the cycle of the Moon is completed.<br>
 Fire, great floods, by more ignorant rulers;<br>
 how long the centuries until it is seen to be restored.<br>
 </p><p>
 63<br>
 Pestilences extinguished, the world becomes smaller,<br>
 for a long time the lands will be inhabited peacefully.<br>
 People will travel safely through the sky (over) land and seas:<br>
 then wars will start up again.<br>
 </p><p>
 64<br>
 At night they will think they have seen the sun,<br>
 when the see the half pig man:<br>
 Noise, screams, battles seen fought in the skies.<br>
 The brute beasts will be heard to speak.<br>
 </p><p>
 65<br>
 A child without hands, never so great a thunderbolt seen,<br>
 the royal child wounded at a game of tennis.<br>
 At the well lightning strikes, joining together <br>
 three trussed up in the middle under the oaks.<br>
 </p><p>
 66<br>
 He who then carries the news,<br>
 after a short while will (stop) to breathe:<br>
 Viviers, Tournon, Montferrand and Praddelles;<br>
 hail and storms will make them grieve.<br>
 </p><p>
 67<br>
 The great famine which I sense approaching<br>
 will often turn (in various areas) then become worldwide.<br>
 It will be so vast and long lasting that (they) will grab<br>
 roots from the trees and children from the breast.<br>
 </p><p>
 68<br>
 O to what a dreadful and wretched torment<br>
 are three innocent people going to be delivered.<br>
 Poison suggested, badly guarded, betrayal.<br>
 Delivered up to horror by drunken executioners.<br>
 </p><p>
 69<br>
 The great mountain, seven stadia round,<br>
 after peace, war, famine, flooding.<br>
 It will spread far, drowning great countries,<br>
 even antiquities and their mighty foundations.<br>
 </p><p>
 70<br>
 Rain, famine and war will not cease in Persia;<br>
 too great a faith will betray the monarch.<br>
 Those (actions) started in France will end there,<br>
 a secret sign for on to be sparing.<br>
 </p><p>
 71<br>
 The marine tower will be captured and retaken three times<br>
 by Spaniards, Barbarians and Ligurians.<br>
 Marseilles and Aix, Ales by men of Pisa,<br>
 devastation, fire, sword, pillage at Avignon by the Turinese.<br>
 </p><p>
 72<br>
 The inhabitants of Marseilles completely changed,<br>
 fleeing and pursued as far as Lyons.<br>
 Narbonne, Toulouse angered by Bordeaux;<br>
 the killed and captive are almost one million.<br>
 </p><p>
 73<br>
 France shall be accused of neglect by her five partners.<br>
 Tunis, Algiers stirred up by the Persians.<br>
 Leon, Seville and Barcelona having failed,<br>
 they will not have the fleet because of the Venetians.<br>
 </p><p>
 74<br>
 After a rest they will travel to Epirus,<br>
 great help coming from around Antioch.<br>
 The curly haired king will strive greatly for the Empire,<br>
 the brazen beard will be roasted on a spit.<br>
 </p><p>
 75<br>
 The tyrant of Siena will occupy Savona,<br>
 having won the fort he will restrain the marine fleet.<br>
 Two armies under the standard of Ancona:<br>
 the leader will examine them in fear.<br>
 </p><p>
 76<br>
 The man will be called by a barbaric name<br>
 that three sisters will receive from destiny.<br>
 He will speak then to a great people in words and deeds,<br>
 more than any other man will have fame and renown.<br>
 </p><p>
 77<br>
 A promontory stands between two seas:<br>
 A man who will die later by the bit of a horse;<br>
 Neptune unfurls a black sail for his man;<br>
 the fleet near Gibraltar and Rocheval.<br>
 </p><p>
 78<br>
 To an old leader will be born an idiot heir,<br>
 weak both in knowledge and in war.<br>
 The leader of France is feared by his sister,<br>
 battlefields divided, conceded to the soldiers.<br>
 </p><p>
 79<br>
 Bazas, Lectoure, Condom, Auch and Agen<br>
 are troubled by laws, disputes and monopolies.<br>
 Carcassone, Bordeaux, Toulouse and Bayonne will be ruined<br>
 when they wish to renew the massacre.<br>
 </p><p>
 80<br>
 From the sixth bright celestial light <br>
 it will come to thunder very strongly in Burgundy.<br>
 Then a monster will be born of a very hideous beast:<br>
 In March, April, May and June great wounding and worrying.<br>
 </p><p>
 81<br>
 Nine will be set apart from the human flock,<br>
 separated from judgment and advise.<br>
 Their fate is to be divided as they depart.<br>
 K. Th. L. dead, banished and scattered.<br>
 </p><p>
 82<br>
 When the great wooden columns tremble <br>
 in the south wind, covered with blood.<br>
 Such a great assembly then pours forth<br>
 that Vienna and the land of Austria will tremble.<br>
 </p><p>
 83<br>
 The alien nation will divide the spoils.<br>
 Saturn in dreadful aspect in Mars.<br>
 Dreadful and foreign to the Tuscans and Latins,<br>
 Greeks who will wish to strike.<br>
 </p><p>
 84<br>
 The moon is obscured in deep gloom,<br>
 his brother becomes bright red in color.<br>
 The great one hidden for a long time in the shadows<br>
 will hold the blade in the bloody wound.<br>
 </p><p>
 85<br>
 The king is troubled by the queen's reply.<br>
 Ambassadors will fear for their lives.<br>
 The greater of his brothers will doubly disguise his action,<br>
 two of them will die through anger, hatred and envy.<br>
 </p><p>
 86<br>
 When the great queen sees herself conquered,<br>
 she will show an excess of masculine courage.<br>
 Naked, on horseback, she will pass over the river<br>
 pursued by the sword: she will have outraged her faith<br>
 </p><p>
 87<br>
 Earthshaking fire from the center of the earth<br>
 will cause tremors around the New City.<br>
 Two great rocks will war for a long time,<br>
 then Arethusa will redden a new river.<br>
 </p><p>
 88<br>
 The divine wrath overtakes the great Prince,<br>
 a short while before he will marry.<br>
 Both supporters and credit will suddenly diminish.<br>
 Counsel, he will die because of the shaven heads.<br>
 </p><p>
 89<br>
 Those of Lerida will be in the Moselle,<br>
 kill all those from the Loire and Seine.<br>
 The seaside track will come near the high valley,<br>
 when the Spanish open every route.<br>
 </p><p>
 90<br>
 Bordeaux and Poitiers at the sound of the bell<br>
 will go with a great fleet as fast as Langon.<br>
 A great rage will surge up against the French,<br>
 when a hideous monster is born near Orgon.<br>
 </p><p>
 91<br>
 The Gods will make it appear to mankind<br>
 that they are the authors of a great war.<br>
 Before the sky was seen to bee free of weapons and rockets:<br>
 the greatest damage will be inflicted on the left.<br>
 </p><p>
 92<br>
 Under one man peace will be proclaimed everywhere,<br>
 but not long after will be looting and rebellion.<br>
 Because of a refusal, town, land and see will be broached.<br>
 About a third of a million dead or captured.<br>
 </p><p>
 93<br>
 The Italian lands near the mountains will tremble.<br>
 The Cock and the Lion not strongly united.<br>
 In place of fear they will help each other.<br>
 Freedom alone moderates the French.<br>
 </p><p>
 94<br>
 The tyrant Selim will be put to death at the harbor<br>
 but Liberty will not be regained, however.<br>
 A new war arises from vengeance and remorse.<br>
 A lady is honored through force of terror.<br>
 </p><p>
 95<br>
 In front of a monastery will be found a twin infant<br>
 from the illustrious and ancient line of a monk.<br>
 His fame, renown and power through sects and speech<br>
 is such that they will say the living twin is deservedly chosen.<br>
 </p><p>
 96<br>
 A man will be charged with the destruction <br>
 of temples and sects, altered by fantasy.<br>
 He will harm the rocks rather than the living,<br>
 ears filled with ornate speeches.<br>
 </p><p>
 97<br>
 That which neither weapon nor flame could accomplish<br>
 will be achieved by a sweet speaking tongue in council.<br>
 Sleeping, in a dream, the king will see<br>
 the enemy not in war or of military blood.<br>
 </p><p>
 98<br>
 The leader who will conduct great numbers of people<br>
 far from their skies, to foreign customs and language.<br>
 Five thousand will die in Crete and Thessaly,<br>
 the leader fleeing in a sea going supply ship.<br>
 </p><p>
 99<br>
 The great king will join <br>
 with two kings, united in friendship.<br>
 How the great household will sigh:<br>
 around Narbon what pity for the children.<br>
 </p><p>
 100<br>
 For a long time a gray bird will be seen in the sky<br>
 near Dôle and the lands of Tuscany.<br>
 He holds a flowering branch in his beak,<br>
 but he dies too soon and the war ends.<br>
 </p><p>
