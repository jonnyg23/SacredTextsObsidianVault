 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Fictitious and Symbolic Creatures in Art</i>, by John Vinycomb, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">The Lion-Dragon</h3>  
 <p>is compounded of the forepart of a lion conjoined to the hinder part of a dragon.</p>  
 <p><i>Or, a lion-dragon gules armed, langued and crowned of the first</i>, is the <i>Bretigni</i> family.</p>  
 <p><i>Party per chevron gules and or, three lion-dragons ducally crowned and countercharged</i>.—<i>Easton</i>.</p>
