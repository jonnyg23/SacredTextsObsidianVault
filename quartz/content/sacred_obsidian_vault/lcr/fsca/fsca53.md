 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Fictitious and Symbolic Creatures in Art</i>, by John Vinycomb, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">The Heraldic Ibex</h3>  
 <p>is an imaginary beast resembling the heraldic antelope in appearance, with the exception of the horns projecting from his forehead,<a name="img_21500"></a>
 </p><table align="RIGHT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/21500.jpg" alt="The Heraldic Ibex."></td></tr></table> which are serrated like a saw. Perhaps it would not be erroneous to consider it identical with the heraldic antelope.  
 <p>The real or natural ibex is a native of the Alps, the Pyrenees and the Grecian mountains, where they abound in defiance of the hunters. It resembles a goat, but the horns are much larger, bent backwards, and full of knots, one of which is added every year.</p>
