 <p align="center"></p><h4 align="center">II.</h4>
 <p align="center"></p><h3 align="center">ATHENAGORAS</h3>
 <p><small><i>Libellus pro Christianis</i>, <a name="fr_508"></a><a href="#fn_508"><font size="1">1</font></a> xxviii.; Schwartz (E.), p. 57, 24 (Leipzig, 1891). <a name="fr_509"></a><a href="#fn_509"><font size="1">2</font></a></small></p>
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Athenagoras</span> was acquainted with a Greek literature circulated under the name of Hermes Trismegistus, to whom he refers as authority for his euhemeristic contention that the gods were once simply men. <a name="fr_510"></a><a href="#fn_510"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
