 <p></p><h4 align="center">EXCERPT XI.</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">[OF JUSTICE]</h3>
 <p><small>(I have added the title, the excerpt not being found in Patrizzi.</small></p>
 <p><small>Text: Stob., <i>Phys</i>., iii. 52, under the vague heading: “Of Hermes”; G. p. 50; M. i. 33, 34; W. i. 62, 63.</small></p>
 <p><small>Ménard, Livre IV., No. iv. of “Fragments from the Books of Hermes to his Son Tat,” p. 240.)</small></p>
 <p>1. [<i>Her</i>.] For there hath been appointed, O [my] son, a very mighty Daimon turning in the universes midst, that sees all things that men do on the earth.</p>
 <p>Just as Foreknowledge <a name="fr_191"></a><a href="#fn_191"><font size="1">1</font></a> and Necessity have been set oer the Order of the gods, in the same way is Justice set oer men, causing the same to act on them.</p>
 <p>For they rule oer the order of the things existing as divine, which have no will, nor any power, to err.</p>
 <p>For the Divine cannot be made to wander; from which the incapacity to err accrues [to it].</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="green">p. 59</font></a></p>
 <p>But Justice is appointed to correct the errors men commit on earth.</p>
 <p>2. For, seeing that their race is under sway of death, and made out of bad matter, [it naturally errs], and failure is the natural thing, especially to those who are without the power of seeing the Divine. <a name="fr_192"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 <p>Tis over these that Justice doth have special sway. Theyre subject both to Fate through the activities of birth, <a name="fr_193"></a><a href="#fn_193"><font size="1">2</font></a> and unto Justice through the mistakes [they make] in life. <a name="fr_194"></a><a href="#fn_194"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h4 align="center">COMMENT</h4>
 <p>The title and place of this excerpt has been discussed in the Commentary on <i>C. H.</i>, xii. (xiii.) 6. It belongs to the Tat-Sermons, and in the collection of Lactantius probably stood prior to the Sermon of Hermes to Tat, “About the General Mind.” <a name="fr_195"></a><a href="#fn_195"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>
