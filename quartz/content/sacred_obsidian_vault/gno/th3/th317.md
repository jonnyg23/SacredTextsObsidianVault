 <p></p><h4 align="center">EXCERPT XVI.</h4>
 <p></p><hr> 
 <p></p><h3 align="center">[OF SOUL, III.]</h3>
 <p><small>(I have added the title, Patrizzi (p. 40b) having only the heading: “To the Same Ammon.”</small></p>
 <p><small>Text: Stob., <i>Phys</i>., xli. 3, under the simple heading: “Of Hermes”; G. pp. 323, 324; M. i. 227, 228; W. i. 320, 321.</small></p>
 <p><small>Ménard, Livre IV., No. v. of “Fragments of the Books of Hermes to Ammon,” pp. 263, 264.)</small></p>
 <p>1. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> Soul is, then, incorporal essence; for if it should have body, it would no longer have the power of being self-maintained. <a name="fr_234"></a><a href="#fn_234"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 <p>For every body needeth being; it needeth also ordered life <a name="fr_235"></a><a href="#fn_235"><font size="1">2</font></a> as well.</p>
 <p>For that for every thing that comes to birth, <a name="fr_236"></a><a href="#fn_236"><font size="1">3</font></a> change also must succeed. <a name="fr_237"></a><a href="#fn_237"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>
 <p>For that which doth become, <a name="fr_238"></a><a href="#fn_238"><font size="1">5</font></a> becomes in size; for in becoming it hath increase.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="green">p. 73</font></a></p>
 <p>Again, for every thing that doth increase, decrease succeedeth; and on increase destruction.</p>
 <p>For, sharing in the form of life, <a name="fr_239"></a><a href="#fn_239"><font size="1">1</font></a> it <a name="fr_240"></a><a href="#fn_240"><font size="1">2</font></a> lives; it shares, also, in being through the Soul.</p>
 <p>2. But that which is the cause of being to another, is being first itself.</p>
 <p>And by [this] “being” I now mean becoming in reason, and taking part in intellectual life.</p>
 <p>It is the Soul that doth supply this intellectual life.</p>
 <p>It is called living <a name="fr_241"></a><a href="#fn_241"><font size="1">3</font></a> through the life, and rational through the intellect, and mortal through the body.</p>
 <p>Soul is, accordingly, a thing incorporal, possessing [in itself] the power of freedom from all change.</p>
 <p>For how would it be possible to talk about an intellectual living thing, <a name="fr_242"></a><a href="#fn_242"><font size="1">4</font></a> if that there were no [living] essence to furnish life?</p>
 <p>Nor, any more, would it be possible to say a rational [living] thing, were there no ratiocinative essence to furnish intellectual life.</p>
 <p>3. It is not to all [lives] that intellect extends; [it doth depend] on the relationship of bodys composition to the Harmony.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="green">p. 74</font></a></p>
 <p>For if the hot in the compost be in excess, hes light <a name="fr_243"></a><a href="#fn_243"><font size="1">1</font></a> and fervid; but if the cold, hes heavy and hes dull.</p>
 <p>For Nature makes the composition fit the Harmony.</p>
 <p>There are three forms of the becoming,—the hot, the cold, and medium.</p>
 <p>It <a name="fr_244"></a><a href="#fn_244"><font size="1">2</font></a> makes it fit according to the ruling Star <a name="fr_245"></a><a href="#fn_245"><font size="1">3</font></a> in the star-mixture.</p>
 <p>And Soul receiving it, <a name="fr_246"></a><a href="#fn_246"><font size="1">4</font></a> as Fate decrees, supplies this work of Nature with [the proper kind of] life.</p>
 <p>Nature, accordingly, assimilates the bodys harmony unto the mixture of the Stars, and co-unites its complex mixtures with their Harmony, so that they are in mutual sympathy.</p>
 <p>For that the end of the Stars Harmony is to give birth to sympathy according to their Fate.</p>
 <p>
