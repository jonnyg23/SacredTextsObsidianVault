 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Thrice-Greatest Hermes, Vol. 2</i>, by G.R.S. Mead, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>
 
 <p>4. [XV. M.] And now it hath been told you on each several point,—as man hath power [to tell], and God hath willed it and permitted it.</p>
 <p>This, then, alone remains that we should do,—bless God and give Him praise; and so return to taking thought for body [s comfort].</p>
 <p>For now sufficiently have we been filled with feast of mind by our discourse on sacred things. <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_388"><font size="1" color="green">p. 388</font></a></p>
 <p></p><h4 align="center">XLI</h4>
 <p>1. Now when they came forth from the holy place, <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">1</font></a> they turned their faces towards the south <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">2</font></a> when they began their prayers to God.</p>
 <p>For when the sun is setting, should anyone desire to pray to God, he ought to turn him thitherwards <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">3</font></a>; so also at the rising of the same, unto that spot which lies beneath the sun. <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>
 <p>As they were just beginning to recite the prayer, Asclepius did whisper:</p>
 <p align="center"></p><hr align="CENTER" size="1" width="25%" noshade> <p>[<i>Asc.</i>] Let us suggest to father, Tat,—what he did bid us do, <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">5</font></a>—that we should say our prayer to God with added incense and with unguents.</p>
 <p>Whom when Thrice-greatest heard, he grew distressed and said:</p>
 <p align="center"></p><hr align="CENTER" size="1" width="25%" noshade> <p>2. [<i>Tris.</i>] Nay, nay, Asclepius; speak more propitious words! For this is like to profanation of [our] sacred rites,—when thou dost pray to God, to offer incense and the rest.</p>
 <p>For naught is there of which He stands in need, in that He is all things, or all are in Him.</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_389"><font size="1" color="green">p. 389</font></a></p>
 <p>But let us worship, pouring forth our thanks. For this is the best incense in Gods sight,—when thanks are given to Him by men. <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>
 <p align="center"></p><hr align="CENTER" size="1" width="25%" noshade> <p>3. [We give] Thee grace, Thou highest [and] most excellent! For by Thy Grace we have received the so great Light of Thy own Gnosis.</p>
 <p>O holy Name, fit [Name] to be adored, O Name unique, by which the Only God <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">2</font></a> is to be blest through worship of [our] Sire,—[of Thee] who deignest to afford to all a Fathers piety, and care, and love, and whatsoever virtue is more sweet [than these], endowing [us] with sense, [and] reason, [and] intelligence;—with sense that we may feel Thee; with reason that we may track Thee out from the appearances of things <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">3</font></a>; with means of recognition that we may joy in knowing Thee.</p>
 <p>4. Saved by Thy Power divine, let us rejoice that Thou hast shown Thyself to us in all Thy Fullness. Let us rejoice that Thou hast deigned to consecrate us, [still] entombed in bodies, to Eternity.</p>
 <p>For this is the sole festival of praise worthy of man,—to know Thy Majesty.</p>
 <p>We have known Thee; yea, by the Single Sense of our intelligence, we have perceived</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_390"><font size="1" color="green">p. 390</font></a></p>
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Thy Light supreme,—O Thou true Life of life, O Fecund Womb that giveth birth to every nature!</p>
 <p>5. We have known Thee, O Thou completely filled with the Conception from Thyself of Universal Nature!</p>
 <p>We have known Thee, O Thou Eternal Constancy!</p>
 <p>For in the whole of this our prayer in worship of Thy Good, this favour only of Thy Goodness do we crave;—that Thou wilt keep us constant in our Love of knowing Thee, <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">1</font></a> and let us neer be cut off from this kind of Life.</p>
 <p align="center"></p><hr align="CENTER" size="1" width="25%" noshade> <p>With this desire we [now] betake us to [our] pure and fleshless meal. <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>
