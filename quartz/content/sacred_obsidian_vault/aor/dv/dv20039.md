 <p></p><h3 align="CENTER"><i>XII.</i></h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER"><i>Architectural Designs.</i></h3>
 <h3 align="CENTER"><i>I. Plans for towns.</i></h3>
 <p>
 <i>A. Sketches for laying out a new town with a double system of high-
 level and low-level road-ways.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl077.htm#img_pl077">Pl. LXXVII</a>, No. </i>1<i> (MS. B, </i>15<i><sup>b</sup>). A general view of a town, with the
 roads outside it sloping up to the high-level ways within.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl077.htm#img_pl077">Pl. LXXVII</a>, No. </i>3<i> (MS. B, </i>16<i><sup>b</sup>. see No. </i>741<i>; and MS. B. </i>15<i><sup>b</sup>, see No.
 </i>742<i>) gives a partial view of the town, with its streets and houses,
 with explanatory references.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl077.htm#img_pl077">Pl. LXXVII</a>, No. </i>2<i> (MS. B, 15<sup>b</sup>; see No. 743). View of a double
 staircaise with two opposite flights of steps.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i><a href="pl078.htm#img_pl078">Pl. LXXVIII</a>, Nos. </i>2<i> and </i>3<i> (MS. B, 37<sup>a</sup>). Sketches illustrating the
 connection of the two levels of roads by means of steps. The lower
 galleries are lighted by openings in the upper roadway.</i>
 </p><p>
 <i>B. Notes on removing houses (MS. Br. M., </i>270<i><sup>b</sup>, see No. </i>744<i>).</i>
