 <h3 align="CENTER">560.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">ON PAINTING.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> The juxtaposition of light and shade (560, 561).</font></span>The comparison of the various qualities of shadows and lights not
 infrequently seems ambiguous and confused to the painter who desires
 to imitate and copy the objects he sees. The reason is this: If you
 see a white drapery side by side with a black one, that part of the
 white drapery which lies against the black one will certainly look
 much whiter than the part which lies against something whiter than
 itself.  <a name="fr_265"></a><a href="#fn_265"><font size="1">263</font></a>
  And the reason of this is shown in my [book on]
 perspective.
 </p><p>
