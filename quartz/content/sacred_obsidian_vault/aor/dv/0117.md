 <h3 align="CENTER">117.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">OF PAINTING.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">OF THE THREE KINDS OF LIGHT THAT ILLUMINATE OPAQUE BODIES.</h3>
 <p>
 <span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"> Different sorts of light (117,118).</font></span>The first kind of Light which may illuminate opaque bodies is called
 Direct light--as that of the sun or any other light from a window or
 flame. The second is Diffused [universal] light, such as we see in
 cloudy weather or in mist and the like. The 3rd is Subdued light,
 that is when the sun is entirely below the horizon, either in the
 evening or morning.
