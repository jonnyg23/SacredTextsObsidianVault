 <h3 align="CENTER">973.</h3>
 <p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 202</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v220200"></a>
 <img src="img/v220200.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 When the fulness of rivers is diminished, then the acute angles
 formed at the junction of their branches become shorter at the sides
 and wider at the point; like the current <i>a n</i> and the current <i>d
 n</i>, which unite in <i>n</i> when the river is at its greatest fulness. I
 say, that when it is in this condition if, before the fullest time,
 <i>d n</i> was lower than <i>a n</i>, at the time of fulness <i>d n</i> will be
 full of sand and mud. When the water <i>d n</i> falls, it will carry away
 the mud and remain with a lower bottom, and the channel <i>a n</i>
 finding itself the higher, will fling its waters into the lower, <i>d
 n</i>, and will wash away all the point of the sand-spit <i>b n c</i>, and
 thus the angle <i>a c d</i> will remain larger than the angle <i>a n d</i> and
 the sides shorter, as I said before.
 </p><p>
