 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">OF PAINTING.</h3>
 <p>
 Of different shadows of equal strength that which is nearest the eye
 will seem the least strong.
 </p><p>
 Why is the shadow <i>e a b</i> in the first grade of strength, <i>b c</i> in
 the second; <i>c d</i> in the third? The reason is that as from <i>e a b</i>
 the sky is nowhere visible, it gets no light whatever from the sky,
 and so has no direct [primary] light. <i>b c</i> faces the portion of the
 sky <i>f</i>
 </p><p><a name="page_97"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 97</font></a></p>
 <p><i>g</i> and is illuminated by it. <i>c d</i> faces the sky at <i>h k</i>. <i>c
 d</i>, being exposed to a larger extent of sky than <i>b c</i>, it is
 reasonable that it should be more lighted. And thus, up to a certain
 distance, the wall <i>a d</i> will grow lighter for the reasons here
 given, until the darkness of the room overpowers the light from the
 window.
 </p><p>
