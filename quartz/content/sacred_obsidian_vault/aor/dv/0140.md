 <h3 align="CENTER">140.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v108301"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/v108301.jpg"><img src="tn/v108301.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></p>
 <p>
 Every body in light and shade is situated between 2 pyramids one
 dark and the other luminous, one is visible the other is not. But
 this only happens when the light enters by a window. Supposing <i>a b</i>
 to be the window and <i>r</i> the body in light and shade, the light to
 the right hand <i>z</i> will pass the object to the left and go on to
 <i>p</i>; the light to the left at <i>k</i> will pass to the right of the
 object at <i>i</i> and
 </p><p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>
 <p>go on to <i>m</i> and the two lines will intersect at
 <i>c</i> and form a pyramid. Then again <i>a</i> <i>b</i> falls on the shaded body
 at <i>i</i> <i>g</i> and forms a pyramid <i>f</i> <i>i</i> <i>g</i>. <i>f</i> will be dark because
 the light <i>a</i> <i>b</i> can never fall there; <i>i</i> <i>g</i> <i>c</i> will be
 illuminated because the light falls upon it.
 </p><p>
