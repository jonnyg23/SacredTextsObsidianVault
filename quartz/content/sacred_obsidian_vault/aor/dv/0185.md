 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">DEFINITION.</h3>
 <p>
 The intersection <i>n</i> is produced by the shadows caused by the light
 <i>b</i>, because this light <i>b</i> produces the shadow <i>x b</i>, and the
 shadow <i>s b</i>, but the intersection <i>m</i> is produced by the light <i>a</i>
 which causes the shadow <i>s a</i>, and the shadow <i>x a</i>.
 </p><p>
 But if you uncover both the lights <i>a b</i>, then you get the two
 shadows <i>n m</i> both at once, and besides these, two other, simple
 shadows are produced at <i>r o</i> where neither of the two lights falls
 at all. The grades of depth in compound shadows are fewer in
 proportion as the lights falling on, and crossing them are less
 numerous.
 </p><p>
