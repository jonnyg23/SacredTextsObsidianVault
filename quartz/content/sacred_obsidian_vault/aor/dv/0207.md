 <h3 align="CENTER">207.</h3>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v111400"></a>
 <img src="img/v111400.jpg"></p>
 <p>
 It is impossible that an object mirrored on water should correspond
 in form to the object mirrored, since the centre of the eye is above
 the surface of the water.
 </p><p><a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 115</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p>
 This is made plain in the figure here given, which demonstrates that
 the eye sees the surface <i>a b</i>, and cannot see it at <i>l f</i>, and at
 <i>r t</i>; it sees the surface of the image at <i>r t</i>, and does not see
 it in the real object <i>c d</i>. Hence it is impossible to see it, as
 has been said above unless the eye itself is situated on the surface
 of the water as is shown below [13].
 </p><p>
