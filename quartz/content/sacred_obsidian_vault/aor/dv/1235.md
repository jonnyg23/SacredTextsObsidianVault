 <h3 align="CENTER">1235.</h3>
 <p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">THE EAGLE.</h3>
 <p>
 The eagle when it is old flies so high that it scorches its
 feathers, and Nature allowing that it should renew its youth, it
 falls into shallow water  <a name="fr_317"></a><a href="#fn_317"><font size="1">651</font></a>
 . And
 if its young ones cannot bear to gaze on the sun  <a name="fr_318"></a><a href="#fn_318"><font size="1">652</font></a>
 --; it does not feed them with any bird, that
 does not wish to die. Animals which much fear
 </p><p><a name="page_322"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 322</font></a></p>
 <p>it do not approach its
 nest, although it does not hurt them. It always leaves part of its
 prey uneaten.
 </p><p>
 </p><h3 align="CENTER">LUMERPA,--FAME.</h3>
 <p>
 This is found in Asia Major, and shines so brightly that it absorbs
 its own shadow, and when it dies it does not lose this light, and
 its feathers never fall out, but a feather pulled out shines no
 longer.
 </p><p>
