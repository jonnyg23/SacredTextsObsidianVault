 <h3 align="CENTER">139.</h3>
 <p><a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 83</font></a></p>
 <p></p><p align="CENTER"><a name="img_v108300"></a>
 </p><p align="CENTER">
 <a href="img/v108300.jpg"><img src="tn/v108300.jpg"><br><font size="-3">Click to enlarge</font></a></p>
 <p>
 Every shadow with all its variations, which becomes larger as its
 distance from the object is greater, has its external lines
 intersecting in the middle, between the light and the object. This
 proposition is very evident and is confirmed by experience. For, if
 <i>a b</i> is a window without any object interposed, the luminous
 atmosphere to the right hand at <i>a</i> is seen to the left at <i>d</i>. And
 the atmosphere at the left illuminates on the right at <i>c</i>, and the
 lines intersect at the point <i>m</i>.
