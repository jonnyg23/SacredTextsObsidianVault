 <p>
 This is volume 45 of the Sacred Books of the East, Part Two of the Hermann
 Jacobi translation of the Jaina Sutras.
 </p><p>
 As is the practice at this site, the typography of the original has
 been preserved as far as possible.
 This means that italics are used to indicate distinctions between
 consonants in transliterated Sanskrit and Prakrit.
 The system used is documented on the 
 <a href="../../sbe/index.htm#conventions">charts attached to the
 Sacred Books of the East page</a>.
 Most notably in this system, 'j' (roughly,
 the initial sound of the English word 'jam')
 is written <i>g</i>,
 hence the spelling <i>G</i>ain
 throughout, which is pronounced 'Jain.'
 This book is normally cited as 'Jaina Sutras' in modern publications
 and bibliographies.
 </p>
