 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Jaina Sutras, Part II (SBE22)</i>, tr. by Hermann Jacobi, [1884], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">THIRD LESSON.</h4>  
 <p>'Reflect and observe that whether you go to this world or to that beyond, in the whole world those who are discerning beings, who abstain from cruelty <a name="fr_193"></a><a href="#fn_190"><font size="1">1</font></a>, relinquish karman. They are flesh-subduing, called duty-knowing, upright men, aware that pain results from actions.' Thus say those who have right intuition. (1)</p>  
 <p>All the professors, conversant with pain, preach renunciation. Thus thoroughly knowing karman, observing the commandment, wise, unattached (to the world), recognising thy Self as one <a name="fr_194"></a><a href="#fn_191"><font size="1">2</font></a>, subdue the body, chastise thyself, weaken thyself: 'just as fire consumes old wood!' Thus with a composed mind, unattached, 'unhesitatingly avoid wrath!' Considering the shortness of life 'know pain, or what will come <a name="fr_195"></a><a href="#fn_192"><font size="1">3</font></a>;' one shall feel the several feelings; and perceive the world suffering under them. (2)</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="green">p. 40</font></a></p>  
 <p>Those who are free from sinful acts are called anidâna <a name="fr_196"></a><a href="#fn_193"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Hence a very wise man should not be inflamed (by wrath). Thus I say. (3)</p>
