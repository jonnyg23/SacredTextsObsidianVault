 <h6 align="CENTER">XXXV.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO DIANA.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from MANNA.</h6>
 <p>Hear me, Jove's daughter, celebrated queen,<br>
 Bacchian and Titan, of a noble mien:</p>
 <p><a name="page_166"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 166</font></a></p>
 <p>In darts rejoicing and on all to shine,<br>
 Torch-bearing Goddess, Dictynna divine;<br>
 O'er births presiding, and thyself a maid, 5  <a name="fr_97"></a><a href="#fn_97"><font size="1">5</font></a><br>
 To labour-pangs imparting ready aid:<br>
 Dissolver of the zone and wrinkl'd care,<br>
 Fierce huntress, glorying in the Sylvan war:<br>
 Swift in the course, in dreadful arrows skill'd,<br>
 Wandering by night, <a href="errata.htm#7">rejoicing</a> in the field: 10<br>
 Of manly form, erect, of bounteous mind,<br>
 Illustrious dæmon, nurse of human kind:<br>
 Immortal, earthly, bane of monsters fell,<br>
 'Tis thine; blest maid, on woody hills to dwell:<br>
 Foe of the stag, whom woods and dogs delight, 15<br>
 In endless youth who flourish fair and bright.<br>
 O, universal queen, august, divine,<br>
 A various form, Cydonian pow'r, is thine:<br>
 Dread guardian Goddess, with benignant mind<br>
 Auspicious, come to mystic rites inclin'd 20<br>
 Give earth a store of beauteous fruits to bear,<br>
 Send gentle Peace, and Health with lovely hair,<br>
 And to the mountains drive Disease and Care.</p>
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_97"></a><a href="hoo40.htm#fr_97">166:5</a> Ver. 5.] <i>O'er births presiding</i>. In the original, λοχεία: and Proclus, in Plat. Theol. p. 403. observes that this epithet is given to Diana by theologians, because she is the inspector of natural progression and generation.</p>
