 <h6 align="CENTER">XXIV.</h6>
 <h3 align="CENTER">TO PROTEUS <a name="fr_80"></a><a href="#fn_80"><font size="1">*</font></a>.</h3>
 <h6 align="CENTER">The FUMIGATION from STORAX.</h6>
 <p>PROTEUS I call, whom Fate decrees, to keep<br>
 The keys which lock the chambers of the deep;<br>
 First-born, by whose illustrious pow'r alone<br>
 All Nature's principles are clearly shewn:<br>
 Matter to change with various forms is thine, 5<br>
 Matter unform'd, capacious, and divine.<br>
 All-honor'd, prudent, whose sagacious mind<br>
 Knows all that was, and is, of ev'ry kind,<br>
 With all that shall be in succeeding time;<br>
 So vast thy wisdom, wond'rous, and sublime: 10<br>
 For all things Nature first to thee consign'd,<br>
 And in thy essence omniform confin'd.<br>
 Come, blessed father, to our rites attend,<br>
 And grant our happy lives a prosp'rous end.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_80"></a><a href="hoo29.htm#fr_80">149:*</a> According to Proclus, in Plat. Repub. p. 97, Proteus, though inferior to the primary Gods, is immortal: and though not a deity, is a certain angelic mind of the order of Neptune, comprehending in himself all the forms of things generated in the universe.</p>
