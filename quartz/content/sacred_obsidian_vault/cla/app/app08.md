 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Ancilla to the Pre-Socratic Philosophers</i>, by Kathleen Freeman, [1948], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">6. CLEOSTRATUS OF TENEDOS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Cleostratus</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Tenedos</span> lived in the sixth century B.C.</p>  
 <p>He wrote an astronomical work called <i>Astrologia</i> or <i>Phaenomena</i>.</p>  
 <p>1. (<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Scholiast</span> on Euripides, <i>Rhesus</i> 528: <i>explanation of the words 'first signs'</i>.): But when for the third day over the eighty (<i>Arctophylax</i>) remains (<i>shining, then the 'first signs'</i>) of the Scorpion fall into the sea. <a name="fr_5"></a><a href="#fn_5"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. (<i>He then, it is said, observed the signs in the Zodiac, beginning with the Ram and the Archer</i>).</p>  
 <p>3. (<i>He is said to have been the first to point out the Kids among the constellations</i>).</p>  
 <p>4. (<i>The eight year cycle is commonly attributed to Eudoxus of Cnidus, but they say that it was Cleostratus of Tenedos who first devised it</i>).</p>
