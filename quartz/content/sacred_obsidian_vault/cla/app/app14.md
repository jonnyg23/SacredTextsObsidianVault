 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Ancilla to the Pre-Socratic Philosophers</i>, by Kathleen Freeman, [1948], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">12. ANAXIMANDER OF MILÊTUS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Anaximander</span> of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Milêtus</span> was in his prime about 560 B.C. The title or titles of any works of his are unknown.</p>  
 <p>1. The Non-Limited is the original material of existing things; further, the source from which existing things derive their existence is also that to which they return at their destruction, according to necessity; for they give justice and make reparation to one another for their injustice, according to the arrangement of Time.</p>  
 <p>2. This (essential nature, whatever it is, of the Non-Limited) is everlasting and ageless.</p>  
 <p>3. (<i>The Non-Limited</i>) is immortal and indestructible.</p>  
 <p>4. Nozzle of the bellows.</p>  
 <p>5. (<i>The Earth is like</i>) a stone column.</p>
