 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Ancilla to the Pre-Socratic Philosophers</i>, by Kathleen Freeman, [1948], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">83. LYCOPHRÔN 'THE SOPHIST'</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lycophrôn 'the Sophist'</span>: birthplace unknown; lived probably in the first half of the fourth century B.C.</p>  
 <p>An orator of the school of Gorgias; interested also in metaphysics, political science and politics.</p>  
 <p>1. Knowledge is an association between the act of knowing and the soul.</p>  
 <p>2. (<i>He eliminated the verb 'is' in predication</i>).</p>  
 <p>3. Law is a guarantor of mutual justice.</p>  
 <p>4. The beauty of high birth is hidden, its dignity merely a matter of words.</p>  
 <p>5. The varying-featured heaven of the mighty-peaked earth. The narrow-channelled shore. Xerxes, monstrous man. Sciron the ravager.</p>
