 <h1 align="CENTER">19</h1>
 <p>... Πὸδασ δέ<br>
 ποίκιλοσ μάσλησ ἐκάλυπτε, Λύδιον κάλον ἔργον. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#33">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>A broidered strap of beautiful Lydian work covered her feet.</p>
 <p>Her shining ankles clad in fairest fashion<br>
 In broidered leather from the realm of Lydia,<br>
 So came the Goddess.</p>
 <p>This fragment is very likely from an invocation to Aphrodite. It is from the Scholiast on Aristophanes' "Peace," 1174; Pollux about A.D. 180 also mentions it.</p>
