 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM47">47</a></h1>
 <p>Κῆ δ᾽ ἀμβροσίασ μὲν κράτηρ ἐκέκρατο,<br>
 ᾽Ερμᾶσ δ᾽ ἔλεν ὄλπιν θέοισ οἰνοχόησαι.<br>
 κῆνοι δ᾽ ἄπα πάντεσ καρχήσια τ᾽ ἦχον<br>
 κάλειβον ἀράσαντο δὲ πάμπαν ἔσλα<br>
 τῳ γάμβρῳ. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#69">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>And there the bowl of ambrosia was mixed and Hermes took the ladle to pour out for the gods; and then all held goblets and made libation, and wished good fortune to the bridegroom.</p>
 <p>Athenaeus quotes this fragment in two portions in different places. Lachmann first joined the two parts. The poem was evidently one of the Epithalamia.</p>
