 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM90">90</a></h1>
 <p>Οἶον τὸ γλυκύμαλον ἐρεύθεται ἄκρῳ ἐπ᾽ ὔσδῳ<br>
 ἄκρον ἐπ᾽ ἀκροτάτῳ λελάθοντο δὲ μαλοδρόπνεσ,<br>
 οὐ μὰν ἐκλελάθοντ᾽, ἀλλ᾽ οὐκ ἐδύναντ᾽ ἐπίκεσθαι. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#114">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>As the sweet apple blushes on the end of the bough, the very end of the bough which gatherers missed, nay, missed not, but could not reach.</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>At the end of the bough--its uttermost end,<br>
 Missed by the harvesters, ripens the apple,<br>
 Nay, not overlooked, but far out of reach,<br>
      So with all best things.</p>
 <p>Quoted by the Scholiast on Hermogenes and elsewhere. The "sweet-apple" to which Sappho refers was probably the result of a a graft of apple on quince.</p>
