 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM75">75</a></h1>
 <p>Σὺ δὲ στεφάνοισ, α Δίκα περθέσαθ᾽ ἐράταισ φόβαισιν,<br>
 ὄρπακασ ἀνήτοιο συν ῤραισ᾽ ἀπάλαισι χέψιν,<br>
 ἐγάνθεσιν ἔκ γὰρ πέλεται καὶ χάριτοσ μακαιρᾶν<br>
 μᾶλλον προτέρην, ἀστερφανώτοισι δ᾽ ἀπυστερέφονται. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#98">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>Do thou, O Dica, set garlands upon thy lovely hair, weaving sprigs of dill with thy delicate hands; for those who wear fair blossoms may surely stand first, even in the presence of Goddesses who look without favour upon those who come ungarlanded.</p>
 <p>Athenaeus quotes this fragment, saying that according to Sappho those who approach the gods should wear garlands, as beautiful things are acceptable to them.</p>
