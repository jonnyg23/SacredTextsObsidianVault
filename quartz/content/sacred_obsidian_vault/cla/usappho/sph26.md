 <h1 align="CENTER"><a name="BM25">25</a></h1>
 <p>Σκιδναμένασ ἐν στήθεσιν ὄργασ<br>
 μαψυλάκαν γλῶσσαν πεφυλάχθαι. <font size="1"><a href="xlatall.htm#44">[transcription]</a></font>
 </p>
 <p>When anger spreads through the breast keep thy tongue from barking foolishly (or idly).</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p>When anger surges through thy heart<br>
 Let not thy foolish tongue take part.</p>
 <p>This piece of somewhat sentenious advice is of an unusual type amongst the Sapphic fragments. It is quoted by Plutarch in his essay "On Restraining Anger."</p>
