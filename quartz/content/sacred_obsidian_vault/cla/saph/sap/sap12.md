 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">IX. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Laments the volatility of Phaon.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Ye</span>, who in alleys green and leafy bowrs,<br> Sport, the rude children of fantastic birth;<br> Where frolic nymphs, and shaggy tribes of mirth,<br> In clamrous revels waste the midnight hours;<br> Who, linkd in flaunting bands of mountain flowrs,<br> Weave your wild mazes oer the dewy earth,<br> Ere the fierce Lord of Lustre rushes forth,<br> And oer the world his beamy radiance pours!<br> Oft has your clanking cymbals maddning strain,<br> Loud ringing through the torch-illumind grove,<br> Lurd my lovd Phaon from the youthful train,<br> Through rugged dells, oer craggy rocks to rove; <br> Then how can she his vagrant heart detain,<br> Whose Lyre throbs only to the touch of Love!</p>
