 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">X. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Describes Phaon.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dangrous</span> to hear, is that melodious tongue,<br> And fatal to the sense those murdrous eyes,<br> Where in a sapphire sheath, Loves arrow lies,<br> Himself conceald the crystal haunts among!<br> Oft oer that form, enamourd have I hung,<br> On that smooth cheek to mark the deepning dyes,<br> While from that lip the fragrant breath would rise,<br> That lip, like Cupids bow with rubies strung!<br> Still let me gaze upon that polishd brow,<br> Oer which the golden hair luxuriant plays;<br> So, on the modest lilys leaves of snow<br> The proud Sun revels in resplendent rays!<br> Warm as his beams this sensate heart shall glow, <br> Till lifes last hour, with Phaons self decays!</p>
