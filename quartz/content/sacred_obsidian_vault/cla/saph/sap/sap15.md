 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Previous to her Interview with Phaon.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Now</span>, oer the tessellated pavement strew<br> Fresh saffron, steepd in essence of the rose,<br> While down yon agate column gently flows<br> A glittring streamlet of ambrosial dew!<br> My Phaon smiles! the rich carnations hue,<br> On his flushd cheek in conscious lustre glows,<br> While oer his breast enamourd Venus throws <br> Her starry mantle of celestial blue!<br> Breathe soft, ye dulcet flutes, among the trees<br> Where clustring boughs with golden citron twine; <br> While slow vibrations, dying on the breeze,<br> Shall soothe his soul with harmony divine!<br> Then let my form his yielding fancy seize,<br> And all his fondest wishes, blend with mine.</p>
