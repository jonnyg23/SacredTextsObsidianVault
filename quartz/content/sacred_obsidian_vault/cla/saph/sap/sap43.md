 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XL. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Visions appear to her in a dream.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">On</span> the low margin of a murmring stream,<br> As rapt in meditations arms I lay;<br> Each aching sense in slumbers stole away,<br> While potent fancy formd a soothing dream;<br> Oer the Leucadian deep, a dazzling beam<br> Shed the bland light of empyrean day!<br> But soon transparent shadows veild each ray,<br> While mystic visions sprang athwart the gleam!<br> Now to the heaving gulf they seemd to bend,<br> And now across the sphery regions glide;<br> Now in mid-air, their dulcet voices blend,<br> "Awake! awake!" the restless phalanx cried,<br> "See ocean yawns the lovers woes to end,<br> "Plunge the green wave, and bid thy griefs subside."</p>
