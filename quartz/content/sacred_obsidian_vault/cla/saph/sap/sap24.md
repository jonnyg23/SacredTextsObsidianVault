 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXI. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Laments her early Misfortunes.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Why</span> do I live to loath the cheerful day,<br> To shun the smiles of Fame, and mark the hours<br> On tardy pinions move, while ceaseless showrs<br> Down my wan cheek in lucid currents stray?<br> My tresses all abound, nor gems display,<br> Nor scents Arabian! on my path no flowrs<br> Imbibe the morns resuscitating powrs,<br> For one blank sorrow, saddens all my way!<br> As slow the radiant Sun of reason rose,<br> Through tears my dying parents saw it shine;<br> A brothers frailties, swelld the tide of woes,-<br> And, keener far, maternal griefs were mine!<br> Phaon! if soon these weary eyes shall close,<br> Oh! must that task, that mournful task, be thine?</p>
