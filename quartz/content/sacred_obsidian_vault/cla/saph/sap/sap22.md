 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XIX. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Suspects his constancy.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Farewell</span>, ye coral caves, ye pearly sands,<br> Ye waving woods that crown yon lofty steep;<br> Farewell, ye Nereides of the glittring deep,<br> Ye mountain tribes, ye fawns, ye sylvan bands:<br> On the bleak rock your frantic minstrel stands,<br> Each task forgot, save that, to sigh and weep;<br> In vain the strings her burning fingers sweep,<br> No more her touch, the Grecian Lyre commands!<br> In Circes cave my faithless Phaons laid,<br> Her daemons dress his brow with opiate flowrs;<br> Or, loitring in the brown pomgranate shade,<br> Beguile with amrous strains the fateful hours;<br> While Sapphos lips, to paly ashes fade,<br> And sorrows cankring worm her heart devours!</p>
