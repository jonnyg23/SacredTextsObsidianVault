 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXIV. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sappho's Prayer to Venus.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Venus</span>! to thee, the Lesbian Muse shall sing,<br> The song, which Myttellenian youths admird, <br> when Echo, amrous of the strain inspird,<br> Bade the wild rocks with maddning plaudits ring!<br> Attend my prayr! O! Queen of rapture! bring<br> To these fond arms, he, whom my soul has fird;<br> From these fond arms removd; yet, still desird,<br> Though love, exulting, spreads his varying wing!<br> Oh! source of evry joy! of evry care<br> Blest Venus! Goddess of the zone divine!<br> To Phaons bosom, Phaons victim bear;<br> So shall her warmest, tendrest vows be thine!<br> For Venus, Sappho shall a wreath prepare,<br> And Love be crownd, immortal as the Nine!</p>
