 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">V. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Contemns its Power.</span></h3>  
 <p>O! How can <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Love</span> exulting Reason queil!<br> How fades each nobler passion from his gaze!<br> Een Fame, that cherishes the Poets lays,<br> That fame, ill-fated Sappho lovd so well.<br> Lost is the wretch, who in his fatal spell<br> Wastes the short Summer of delicious days,<br> And from the tranquil path of wisdom strays,<br> In passions thorny wild, forlorn to dwell.<br> O ye! who in that sacred Temple smile<br> Where holy Innocence resides enshrind;<br> Who fear not sorrow, and who know not guile,<br> Each thought composd, and evry wish resignd;<br> Tempt not the path where pleasures flowry wile<br> In sweet, but poisnous fetters, holds the mind.</p>
