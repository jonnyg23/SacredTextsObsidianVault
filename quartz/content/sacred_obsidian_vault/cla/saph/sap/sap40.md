 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXVII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Foresees her Death.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">When</span>, in the gloomy mansion of the dead,<br> This withring heart, this faded form shall sleep;<br> When these fond eyes, at length shall cease to weep,<br> And earths cold lap receive this fevrish head;<br> Envy shall turn away, a tear to shed,<br> And Times obliterating pinions sweep<br> The spot, where poets shall their vigils keep,<br> To mourn and wander near my freezing bed!<br> Then, my pale ghost, upon th Elysian shore,<br> Shall smile, releasd from evry mortal care;<br> Whil, doomd loves victim to repine no more,<br> My breast shall bathe in endless rapture there!<br> Ah! no!my restless shade would still deplore,<br> Nor taste that bliss, which Phaon did not share.</p>
