 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXII. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Dreams of a Rival.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Blest</span> as the Gods! Sicilian Maid is he,<br> The youth whose soul thy yielding graces charm;<br> Who bound, O! thraldom sweet! by beautys arm,<br> In idle dalliance fondly sports with thee!<br> Blest as the Gods! that ivry throne to see,<br> Throbbing with transports, tender, timid, warm!<br> While round thy fragrant lips zephyrs swarm!<br> As opning buds attract the wandring Bee!<br> Yet, short is youthful passions fervid hour;<br> Soon, shall another clasp the beauteous boy;<br> Soon, shall a rival prove, in that gay bowr,<br> The pleasing torture of excessive joy!<br> The Bee flies sickend from the sweetest flowr;<br> The lightnings shaft, but dazzles to destroy!</p>
