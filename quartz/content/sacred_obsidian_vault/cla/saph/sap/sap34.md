 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXI. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Describes her Bark.</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Far</span> oer the waves my lofty Bark shall glide,<br> Loves frequent sighs the fluttring sails shall swell,<br> While to my native home I bid farewell,<br> Hopes snowy hand the burnisd helm shall guide!<br> Tritons shall sport admidst the yielding tide,<br> Myriads of Cupids round the prow shall dwell,<br> And Venus, thrond within her opal shell,<br> Shall proudly oer the glittring billows ride!<br> Young Dolphins, dashing in the golden spray,<br> Shall with their scaly forms illume the deep<br> Tingd with the purple flush of sinking day,<br> Whose flaming wreath shall crown the distant steep;<br> While on the breezy deck soft minstrels play,<br> And songs of love, the lover soothe to sleep!</p>
