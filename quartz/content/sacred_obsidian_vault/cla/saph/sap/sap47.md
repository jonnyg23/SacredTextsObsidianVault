 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sappho and Phaon</i>, by Mary Robinson, [1796], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLIV. <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sonnect Conclusive</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Here</span> droops the muse! while from her glowing mind,<br> Celestial Sympathy, with humid eye,<br> Bids the light Sylph capricious Fancy fly,<br> Times restless wings with transient flowrs to bind!<br> For now, with folded arms and head inclind,<br> Reflection pours the deep and frequent sigh,<br> Oer the dark scroll of human destiny,<br> Where gaudy buds and wounding thorns are twind.<br> O! Sky-born <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Virtue</span>! sacred is thy name!<br> And though mysterious Fate, with frown severe,<br> Oft decorates thy brows with wreaths of Fame,<br> Bespangled oer with sorrows chilling tear!<br> Yet shalt thou more than mortal raptures claim,<br> The brightest planet of th <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Eternal Sphere</span>!<br> <br> </p>
