 <p><a name="page_84"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 84</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TIDINGS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">She</span> wrapped herself in linen woven close,<br> Stuffs delicate and texture-fine as those<br> The dark Nile traders for our bartering<br> From Egypt, Crete and far Phocea bring.</p>  
 <p>Love lent her feet the wings of winds to reach<br> (Whose steps stir not the shingle of the beach)<br> My marble court and, breathless, bid me know<br> My lover's sails across the harbor blow.</p>  
 <p>He seemed to her, as to himself he seems,<br> Like some bright God long treasured in her dreams;<br> She saw him standing at his galley's prow<br> My Phaon, mine, in Mitylene now!</p>
