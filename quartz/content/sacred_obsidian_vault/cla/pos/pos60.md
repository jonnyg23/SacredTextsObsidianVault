 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 73</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GYRINNO</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Now</span> the silver crescent<br> Of the moon has vanished,<br> With the golden Pleiads<br> Drifting down the west.</p>  
 <p>It is after midnight<br> And the time is passing,<br> Hours we pledged to passion<br> And I sleep alone.</p>  
 <p>Anger ill becomes thee,<br> Tender-souled Gyrinno,<br> Shapelier is Dica<br> But less loved by me.</p>  
 <p>Art thou still relentless,<br> Wilful one, annulling<br> All thy protestations<br> In the fervid past?</p>  
 <p>Can it, O Charites,<br> Be thou hast forgotten?<br> Dost thou love another,<br> Even now, perchance?</p>  
 <p>Ah, my tears are falling,<br> Yet in my despairing<br> Mood I lie and listen<br> For thy furtive step;</p>  
 <p>For the lightest rustle<br> Of thy flowing garment,<br> For thy sweet and panting<br> Whisper at the door.</p>  
 <p>Now the moon has vanished<br> With the golden Pleiads;<br> It is after midnight<br> And I sleep alone.</p>
