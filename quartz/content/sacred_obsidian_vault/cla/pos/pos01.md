 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Poems of Sappho</i>, by John Myers O'Hara, [1910], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><small><i>Who shall strike the wax of mystery from those priceless amphora, and give to the unsophisticated nostrils of the average reader the ravishing bouquet of wine pressed in a garden in Mitylene, twenty-five centuries ago?</i>MAURICE THOMPSON.</small></p>  
 <p><i><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Then</span> to me so lying awake a vision<br> Came without sleep over the seas and touched me,<br> Softly touched mine eyelids and lips; and I, too,<br>               Full of the vision,</i></p>  
 <p><i>Saw the white implacable Aphrodite,<br> Saw the hair unbound and the feet unsandalled<br> Shine as fire of sunset on western waters;<br>               Saw the reluctant</i></p>  
 <p><i>Feet, the straining plumes of the doves that drew her,<br> Looking always, looking with necks reverted<br> Back to Lesbos, back to the hills whereunder<br>               Shone Mitylene</i>.</p>  
 <p>                           <small>SWINBURNE</small>.</p>
