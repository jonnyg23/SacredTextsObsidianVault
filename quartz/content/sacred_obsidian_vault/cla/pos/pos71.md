 <p><a name="page_86"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 86</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">DAWN</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Just</span> now the golden-sandalled Dawn<br> Peered through the lattice of my room;<br>         Why must thou fare so soon, my Phaon?</p>  
 <p>Last night I met thee at the shore,<br> A thousand hues were in the sky;<br>         The breeze from Cyprus blew, my Phaon!</p>  
 <p>I drew, to lave thy heated brow,<br> My kerchief dripping from the sea;<br>         Why hadst thou sailed so far, my Phaon?</p>  
 <p>Far up the narrow mountain paths<br> We heard the shepherds fluting home;<br>         Like some white God thou seemed, my Phaon!</p>  
 <p>And through the olive trees we saw<br> The twinkle of my vesper lamp;<br>         Wilt kiss me now as then, my Phaon?</p>  
 <p>Nay, loosen not with gentle force<br> The clasp of my restraining arms;<br>         I will not let thee go, my Phaon!</p>  
 <p>See, deftly in my trailing robe<br> I spring and draw the lattice close;<br>         Is it not night again, my Phaon?</p>
