 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 53</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ODE TO ATTHIS</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">I loved</span> you, Atthis, once, long years ago!<br> My blood was flame that thrilled to passion's throe;<br> Now long neglect has quenched the olden fire,<br> And blight of drifting years effaced desire.</p>  
 <p>I loved you, Atthisjoy of long ago<br> Love shook my soul as winds on forests blow;<br> This lawless heart that dared exhaust delight,<br> Unsated strove and maddened through the night.</p>  
 <p>I loved you, Atthis, once, long years ago!<br> With pain whose surge I felt to anguish grow;<br> Suffered the storms that waste the heart and leave<br> A desert shore where seas but break to grieve.</p>  
 <p>I loved you, Atthisspring of long ago<br> Watched you depart, to Andromeda go;<br> Then I, as keen despair its shadow cast,<br> Oer my deserted threshold, sobbing, passed.</p>  
 <p>I loved you, Atthis, once, long years ago!<br> The thought of me is hateful now, I know;<br> And all the lavish tenderness of old<br> Has gone from me and left my bosom cold.</p>  
 <p>I loved you, Atthisdream of long ago<br> .   .   .   .   .   .   .   .   .   .   .   .<br> How the fond words, impassioned music low,<br> Sustain the sigh of love's divine regret<br> No length of time may bid the heart forget.</p>
