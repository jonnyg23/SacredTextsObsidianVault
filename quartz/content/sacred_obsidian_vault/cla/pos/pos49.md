 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE CRETAN DANCE</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">As</span> the moon in all her splendor<br> Slowly rose above the forest,<br> Silent stood the Cretan women<br>           Round the altar.</p>  
 <p>Girdled close their clinging tunics,<br> Made of some transparent fabric,<br> Traced the every curve and lissome<br>           Of their bodies.</p>  
 <p>With revering eyes uplifted<br> To the round and rising planet,<br> Soon its drifting beams of silver<br>           Lit their faces.</p>  
 <p>Soft and clear its sphere effulgent,<br> Full defined above the treetops,<br> Steeped in pale unearthly glamor<br>           All the landscape.</p>  
 <p>When the argent glimmer rested<br> On the altar piled with garlands,<br> And its glow unveiled the marble<br>           Aphrodite;</p>  
 <p>Linking hands, the Cretan women<br> Moving gracefully with metric<br> Steps began to dance a measure<br>           To the Goddess.</p>  
 <p>All so light their feet unsandalled<br> Pressed the velvet grass in treading,<br> That they scarcely bruised its tender<br>           Blooming verdure.</p>  
 <p>Slowly turning in a circle<br> To the east, their voices chanted<br> In a plaintive note the sacred<br>           Ithyphallics;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a></p>  
 <p>Then they paused, their steps retracing<br> Toward the west, and answered strophe<br> By antistrophe with choric<br>           Tones accordant;</p>  
 <p>With the aftersong epodic,<br> Standing all before the altar,<br> Lo! the hymn in praise of Paphos<br>           Was completed.</p>
