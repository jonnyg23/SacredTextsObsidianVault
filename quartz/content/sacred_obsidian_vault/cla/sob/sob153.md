 <h3 align="CENTER">BIBLIOGRAPHY <a href="sob154.htm#xref_i"><font size="1">i</font></a></h3>
 <i></i><p>I--Bilitis' sæmmtliche Lieder zum ersten Male herausgegeben und mit einem Woerterbuche versehen, von G. Heim.--Leipzig, 1894.</p>
 <i></i><p>II--Les Chansons de Bilitis, traduites du grec pour la premiere fois par P. L.--Paris, 1895.</p>
 <p>III--<i>Six Chansons de Bilitis, traduites en vers par Mme. Jean Bertheroy</i>.--Revue pour les jeunes filles, Paris, <i>Armand Colin, 1896</i>.</p>
 <p>IV--<i>Twenty-six Songs of Bilitis, translated into German by Richard Dehmel</i>.--Die Gesellschaft, <i>Leipzig</i>, <i>1896</i>.</p>
 <p>V--<i>Twenty Songs of Bilitis, translated into German by Dr. Paul Goldmann</i>.--Frankfurter Zeitung, <i>1896</i>.</p>
 <p>VI--<i>The Songs of Bilitis, by Pr. von Willamovitz-Moellendorf</i>.--Goettingsche Gelehrte, <i>Goettingen, 1896</i>.</p>
 <p>VII--<i>Eight Songs of Bilitis, translated into Czech by Alexander Backovsky</i>.--<i>Prague</i>, <i>1897</i>.</p>
 <p>VIII--<i>Four Songs of Bilitis, translated into Swedish by Gustav Uddgren</i>.--Nordisk Revy, <i>Stockholm, 1897</i>.</p>
 <p>IX--<i>Three Songs of Bilitis, set to music by Claude Debussy.--Paris, Fromont, 1898</i>.</p>
