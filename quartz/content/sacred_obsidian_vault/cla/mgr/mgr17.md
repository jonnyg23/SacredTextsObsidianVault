 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 71</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BIBLIOGRAPHY</h3>  
 <p>TILE translations from Homer in this book are taken from the <i>Iliad</i> by Lang Leaf and Myers, and the translation of the <i>Odyssey</i> in verse by J. W. Mackail. The translations of the <i>Homeric Hymns</i> are by Andrew Lang, with the exception of the <i>Hymn to Athene</i>, which is by D. S. MacColl. The translations of Euripides are by Professor Gilbert Murray. For the remaining translations I am myself responsible.</p>  
 <p>Much assistance has been obtained of the following books, to which I refer students for more detailed information:</p>  
 <p>1. A. B. Cook: <i>Zeus: A Study of Ancient Religion</i>, 2 vols. (Cambridge University Press, 1954.)</p>  
 <p>2. Rendel Harris: <i>The Ascent of Olympus</i>. (Manchester University Press, 1917.)</p>  
 <p>3. Gilbert Murray: <i>Five Stages of Greek Religion</i>. (Oxford Clarendon Press, 5925.)</p>  
 <p>4. Jane E. Harrison: (a) <i>Prolegomena to the Study of Greek Religion</i>, third edition. (b) <i>Themis</i>, second edition, 1927. (c) <i>Epilegomena to the Study of Religion.</i> (Cambridge University Press.) (d) <i>Alpha and Omega</i>. (Sidgwick and Jackson.)</p>
