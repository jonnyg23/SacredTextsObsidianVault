 <p>The early Christians saw Apollonius as a potential competitor.
 Eusebius wrote this polemic against <a href="../laot/index.htm">the Life of Apollonius</a> of Philostratus.
 His bottom line is that Apollonius was a sage of some sort, and a human
 being, not immortal or a demi-god.
 He mocks Apollonius' status as a great philosopher, claims that
 Apollonius used evil magic, and ridicules the (admittedly far-fetched)
 details of the account of his visit to India.
 </p>
 <p>
 This text is one of the two extras in the second volume of
 the <a href="../laot/index.htm">Loeb <cite>Philostratus</cite></a>.
 </p>
