 <p><a name="xref_3_2"><font color="GREEN">3_2</font></a> Lion; cf. S. Reinach, <i>Mythes, cultes</i>, I, p. 293. The lion, represented in Asia Minor at a very remote period as devouring a bull or other animals, might possibly represent the sacred animal of Lydia or Phrygia vanquishing the protecting totem of the tribes of Cappadocia or the neighboring countries (I am using the term totem in its broadest meaning). This at least is the interpretation given to similar groups in Egypt. Cf. Foucart, <i>La méthode comparat. et l'histoire des religions</i>, 1909 p. 49, p. 70.</p>
