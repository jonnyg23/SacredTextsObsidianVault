 <h1 align="CENTER">28</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Obscenis, peream, Priape, si non<br>
 uti me pudet improbisque verbis.<br>
 sed cum tu posito deus pudore<br>
 ostendas mihi coleos patentes,<br>
 cum cunno mihi mentula est vocanda.</p>
 <p>Priapus! perish I an words obscene<br>
 And wicked terms to use I'm not ashamed:<br>
 But whenas thou, a god (bylaying shame),<br>
 To me displayest bollocks evident,<br>
 With Coynte the Prickle I must baldly name.</p>
 <p>May I die,[2] Priapus, if I do not blush to make use of lewd and impure words; but when you, a deity without shame, display to me your balls in all openness, I must call a tool a tool, a coynte a coynte.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. Thy mouth shall serve as the instrument of thy punishment.</p>
 <p>2. A favourite formula of oath amongst the Romans.]</p>
