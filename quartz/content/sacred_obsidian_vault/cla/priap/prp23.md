 <h1 align="CENTER">23</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Hic me custodem fecundi vilicus horti<br>
    mandati curam iussit habere loci.<br>
 fur habeas poenam, licet indignere 'feram'que<br>
    'propter holus' dicas 'hoc ego?' 'propter holus'.</p>
 <p>Here has the bailiff, now of this plentiful garden the guardian,<br>
 Bidden me care for the place he to my service entrusts.<br>
 Thief! thou shalt suffer the pain albeit crying in anger--<br>
 'What! for a cabbage all this? This for a cabbage I bear?'</p>
 <p>The steward has bidden me, the protector of this fertile garden, have a care of the place committed to my charge. Thou, O thief, shalt be punished; thou mayst be enraged, and say, 'On account of a cabbage am I to endure this? On account of a cabbage?'[1]</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. Some read <i>prope</i>, meaning near--'Am I to be sodomised near a cabbage?' instead of <i>propter</i>, meaning on account of. Because, it is presumed, the thief thought a cabbage plot too open a space for such a punishment.]</p>
