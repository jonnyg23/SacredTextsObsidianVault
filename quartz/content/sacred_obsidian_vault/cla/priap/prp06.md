 <h1 align="CENTER">6</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Cum loquor, una mihi peccatur littera; nam te<br>
    pe-dico semper blaesaque lingua mihi est.</p>
 <p>Oft in my speech one letter is lost; for Predicate always<br>
 <i>Pedicate</i> I pronounce. Reason--a trip of the tongue!</p>
 <p>Whenever I speak, one word slips me; for, talking with a lisp, I always say instead of <i>praedico, paedico!</i>[2]</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. Which he held in his hand.<br>
 2. Instead of saying '<i>praedico</i>', meaning 'I warn you not to trespass', he lisps and says 'paedico', meaning 'I am sodomising you'.]</p>
 </font><hr>
