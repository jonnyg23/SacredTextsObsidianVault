 <h1 align="CENTER">4</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">On Priapus</h3>
 <p>Quam puero legem fertur dixisse Priapus,<br>
    versibus hic infra scripta duobus erit:<br>
 'quod meus hortus habet, sumas impune licebit,<br>
    si dederis nobis, quod tuus hortus habet'.</p>
 <p>All the conditions (they say) Priapus made with the youngling<br>
 Written in verses twain mortals hereunder can read:<br>
 'Whatso my garden contains to thee shall be lawfullest plunder<br>
 If unto us thou give whatso thy garden contains.'</p>
 <p>Hereunder is written in two verses the condition which Priapus is said to have made with a boy:</p>
 <p>Whate'er my garden has is freely thine,<br>
 If to my will thy garden[1] thou'lt consign.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. A likening of the lad's posteriors to a garden.]</p>
