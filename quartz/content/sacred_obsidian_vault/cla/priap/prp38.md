 <h1 align="CENTER">38</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Priapus</h3>
 <p>Simpliciter tibi me, quodcumque est, dicere oportet,<br>
    natura est quoniam semper aperta mihi:<br>
 pedicare volo, tu vis decerpere poma;<br>
    quod peto, si dederis, quod petis, accipies.</p>
 <p>Simply to thee I say whatever to say shall behove me,<br>
 Since my 'nature' alway openly showeth to fight;<br>
 Fain would I pedicate thee who'rt Fain to plunder my apples;<br>
 An my want thou shalt grant, eke shall be granted thy want.</p>
 <p>Since my nature[1] is always open, it behoves me to say to thee--whate'er it is--frankly. I wish to pedicate; thou wishest to pluck apples. What I desire, if thou wilt give: what thou desirest, thou shalt receive.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>[1. In the original Latin, <i>natura</i>, punningly used in the double sense 'native character' and 'privy part'.]</p>
