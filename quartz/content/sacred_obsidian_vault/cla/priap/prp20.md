 <h1 align="CENTER">20</h1>
 <h3 align="CENTER">To Priapus</h3>
 <p>Copia me perdit: tu suffragare rogatus<br>
    indicio nec me prode, Priape, tuo,<br>
 quaeque tibi posui tamquam vernacula poma,<br>
    de sacra nulli dixeris esse via.</p>
 <p>Wealth is my loss! Do thou vouchsafe lend aid to my prayer,<br>
 Nor, by thy signal shown, me, O Priapus, betray:<br>
 Whatso before thee I laid, of home-grown apples the firstlings,<br>
 (Prithee, be pleased not to tell!) from Via Sacra be ta'en.</p>
 <p>All my wealth have I lost; be propitious when I ask thee, nor betray me, Priapus, by word or deed. Tell it to none, that these home-grown apples, which I have placed on thy altar, are from the Sacred Way.[1]</p>
