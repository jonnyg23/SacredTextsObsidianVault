   <h3 align="center">10 <a name="fr_45"></a><a href="#fn_44"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'There are ten cases, O Bhikkhus, in which the taking of votes is invalid; and ten in which the taking of votes is valid.</p>  
 <p>'Which are the ten in which the taking of votes is invalid? When the matter in dispute is trivial <a name="fr_46"></a><a href="#fn_45"><font size="1">2</font></a>--when the case has not run its course (that is, when. the necessary preliminaries of submission to arbitration have not been carried out <a name="fr_47"></a><a href="#fn_46"><font size="1">3</font></a>)--when regarding the matter in dispute the Bhikkhus have not formally remembered, or been formally called upon to remember, the offence <a name="fr_48"></a><a href="#fn_47"><font size="1">4</font></a>--when the taker of votes <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_48"><font size="1">5</font></a> knows that those whose opinions are not in accordance with the law will be in the majority,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p>or probably <a name="fr_50"></a><a href="#fn_49"><font size="1">1</font></a> may be in the majority--when he knows that the voting will result in a schism in the Sa<i>m</i>gha--when he is in doubt whether the voting will result in a schism in the Sa<i>m</i>gha--when the votes are irregularly given <a name="fr_51"></a><a href="#fn_50"><font size="1">2</font></a>--when all do not vote equally <a name="fr_52"></a><a href="#fn_51"><font size="1">3</font></a>--and when they do not vote in accordance with the view (which they really hold). These are the ten cases in which the voting is invalid <a name="fr_53"></a><a href="#fn_52"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>2. 'And which are the ten cases in which the voting is valid?'</p>  
 <p>[The ten cases are precisely the reverse of the other ten.]</p>
