   <h3 align="center">29.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu entered the village for alms without a girdle on, and in the highway <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_371"><font size="1">1</font></a> his waist-cloth fell down <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_372"><font size="1">2</font></a>. The people made an outcry, and that Bhikkhu was abashed.</p>  
 <p>On his return to the Ârâma, that Bhikkhu told this matter to the Bhikkhus, and the Bhikkhus told it to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to enter the village without a girdle on. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow, O Bhikkhus, a girdle <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus wore (&amp;c., as usual, ending with)</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to wear special girdles <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_374"><font size="1">4</font></a>--those made of many strings plaited</p>

 <p>3. Now at the time the venerable Ânanda went into the village for alms with light garments on <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_384"><font size="1">1</font></a> and his garments were blown up by a whirlwind.</p>  
 <p>The venerable Ânanda, on returning to the Ârâma, told this matter to the Bhikkhus; and the Bhikkhus told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, a block <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_385"><font size="1">2</font></a> (to be used as a weight) or a chain <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>[Similar paragraph to that just above as to the substances of which the block may be made.]</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Bhikkhus fastened the block or the chain immediately on to their robes; and the robes gave way.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, a piece of phalaka cloth <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">4</font></a>to attach the block or the chain to.'</p>  
 <p>They fastened the phalaka cloth for the block or the chain on to the edge of the robe; and the corner came open <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_388"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>

 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to fasten the phalaka cloth for the block on the edge of the robe, and to fasten the phalaka cloth for the chain seven or eight finger-breadths up the robe.'</p>  
 <p>4. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus wore (&amp;c., as usual, ending with)</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to wear your under garments arranged as laymen do, nor arranged with appendages like elephant-trunks <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">1</font></a>, nor arranged like fishing-nets <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">2</font></a>, nor arranged with four corners showing <a name="fr_398"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">3</font></a>, nor arranged like flower-stands <a name="fr_399"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">4</font></a>, nor arranged like rows of jewelry <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">5</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.</p>  
 <p>[Similar paragraph, ending]</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_371"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_378">142:1</a> Rathiyâya. The Old Commentary on the Bhikkhunî., vibhaṅga, Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya XIV, says, Rathiyâ ti ra<i>k</i><i>kh</i>â. The word recurs, ibid., Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya LXXXVI, XCVI (the last of which is nearly the same as our passage here). For the more usual form rathikâ, see <i>K</i>ullavagga X, 12.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_372"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_379">142:2</a> Pabhassittha. Compare Sutta-vibhaṅga, Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya LXXXIII, 1, 2. It is from the root bhrams, not bhâs.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_373"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_380">142:3</a> The use of this has already been enjoined at Mahâvagga I, 25, 9, 10. It was to be tied on round the waist, over the waist-cloth, to keep it in its place.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_374"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_381">142:4</a> It is curious that ka<i>t</i>i-suttaka<i>m</i>, a kind of girdle which would seem properly to belong here, has been included in a former list of forbidden articles at V, 2, I.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_375"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_382">143:1</a> Kalâbuka<i>m</i>. See Buddhaghosa's note at p. 3'9 of the text, and compare Böhtlingk-Roth under kalâpa, kalâpaka.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_376"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_383">143:2</a> De<i>d</i><i>d</i>ubhaka<i>m</i> nâma udaka-sappi-sîsa-sadisa<i>m</i> (B.). De<i>d</i><i>d</i>ubha corresponds to the later Sanskrit du<i>n</i><i>d</i>ubha, an older form of which is dundubha.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_377"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_384">143:3</a> Mura<i>g</i>a, literally, 'tambourines;' but see Buddhaghosa's note, loc. cit.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_378"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_385">143:4</a> Maddavîna<i>m</i> nâma pâmaṅga-sa<i>n</i><i>th</i>âna<i>m</i> (B.). On pâmaṅga, see our note at <i>K</i>ullavagga V, 2, I.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_379"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_386">143:5</a> Sûkarantaka<i>m</i>. See Buddhaghosa's note at p. 319 of the text. We do not venture to translate the term.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_380"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_387">143:6</a> Sobha<i>n</i>am nâma ve<i>t</i><i>th</i>etvâ mukha-va<i>t</i><i>t</i>i-sibhana<i>m</i> (B.). In the Ma<i>g</i><i>gh</i>ima Sîla, § 3, sobha<i>n</i>akam or sobh.ana-kara<i>n</i>am (so Rh. D.'s MS.) is a kind of game or show.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_381"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_388">143:7</a> Gu<i>n</i>aka<i>m</i> nâma mudika(?muddhika)-san<i>th</i>ânena sibbana<i>m</i> (B.). Clough, under gu<i>n</i>a, gives inter alia, 1. fastening; 2. a plant of the fibres of which bow-strings are made; 3. bow-string.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_382"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_389">143:8</a> Pavananto ti pâsanto (B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_383"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_390">143:9</a> Vidho. But both the reading and the explanation are uncertain, and Buddhaghosa says nothing. The word occurs also, and apparently in the same sense, in the Old Commentary on the 86th Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_384"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_391">144:1</a> Sa<i>m</i>ghâ<i>t</i>iyo in the plural must mean garments and not waist-cloths only. See the parallel passage in the Bhikkhunî-vibhaṅga, Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya XCVI.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_385"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_392">144:2</a> Ga<i>n</i><i>th</i>ika<i>m</i>. The use of this article is referred to in VIII, 4, 3, and at Dhammapada, p. 372. That ga<i>n</i><i>th</i>i means a block, usually of mood, is clear from the use of dhamma-ga<i>n</i><i>th</i>ika<i>m</i> at <i>G</i>âtaka I, 150 (spelt ga<i>n</i><i>d</i>ika however at II, 124), compared with ga<i>n</i><i>th</i>i (block of sandal-wood) above, V, 8, I. The word occurs also in the Old Commentary on the 86th Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_386"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_393">144:3</a> Pâsaka<i>m</i>, which does not correspond to Sanskrit prâsaka here, but to pâ<i>s</i>aka = pâ<i>s</i>a (Böhtlingk-Roth. give inter alia, 'Sahl oder Leiste am Anfange eines Gewebes'). Compare pâsanta in Buddhaghosa on ma<i>k</i><i>kh</i>avâ<i>l</i>aka<i>m</i> in the next section.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_387"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_394">144:4</a> See our note on this word at Mahâvagga VIII, 28, 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_388"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_395">144:5</a> That is, perhaps, the weight dragged the robe to one side and the legs were visible through the opening.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_389"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_396">145:1</a> Hatthi-so<i>n</i><i>d</i>aka<i>m</i> nâma nâbhi-mûlato hatthi-so<i>n</i><i>d</i>a-san<i>th</i>âna<i>m</i> olambaka<i>m</i> katvâ nivattha<i>m</i>, ko<i>li</i>ka-itthîna<i>m</i> nivâsana<i>m</i> viya (B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_390"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_397">145:2</a> Ma<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a-vâ<i>l</i>aka<i>m</i> nâma ekato dasanta<i>m</i> ekato pâsantam olambitvâ nivattha<i>m</i> (B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_391"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_398">145:3</a> <i>K</i>atu-ka<i>n</i><i>n</i>aka<i>m</i> upari dve he<i>t</i><i>th</i>ato dve eva<i>m</i> <i>k</i>attâro ka<i>n</i><i>n</i>e dassetvâ nivattha<i>m</i> (B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_392"></a><a href="sbe20044.htm#fr_399">145:4</a> Tâla-va<i>n</i><i>t</i>aka<i>m</i> nâma tâlava<i>n</i><i>t</i>’-âkârena sâ<i>t</i>aka<i>m</i> olambitvâ nivâsana<i>m</i> (B.). See our note on tâlava<i>n</i><i>t</i>a above, V, 22, 2, and on âma<i>l</i>aka-va<i>n</i><i>t</i>ika-pî<i>th</i>a<i>m</i> below, VI, 2, 4.</p>
