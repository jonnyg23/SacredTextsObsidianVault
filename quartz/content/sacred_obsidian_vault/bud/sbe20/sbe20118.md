   <h3 align="center">27.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time Bhikkhunîs, fearing to offend, would not let men salute them, or cut their hair or nails, or dress their wounds.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_367"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 367</font></a></p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow them, O Bhikkhus, to permit those things.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time Bhikkhunîs, when sitting on a divan, allowed (other people thereon) to touch them with their heels.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to sit on a divan <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_942"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhunî was sick, and without using a divan she could not be at ease.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow Bhikkhunîs, O Bhikkhus, to use a half-divan <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_943"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>3. [Rule as to construction of privies <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_944"><font size="1">3</font></a>.]</p>  
 <p>4. Now at that time the Bhikkhunîs used chunam at their baths.</p>  
 <p>People murmured, saying, 'As the women in the world do!'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to use chunam at her bath. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of clay and the red powder of rice husks <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_945"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_368"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 368</font></a></p>  
 <p>[Similar paragraph, ending]</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to use scented clay at her bath <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_946"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of common clay.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time Bhikkhunîs bathing together in the steam-bath room made a tumult.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Bhikkhunîs are not, O Bhikkhus, to bathe in a steam-bath. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time Bhikkhunîs bathing against the stream allowed the rush of water against them.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to bathe against the stream. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time Bhikkhunîs bathed in a place not a common bathing-place, and men of abandoned life violated them.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to bathe at a place not a common bathing-place. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time Bhikkhunîs bathed at a bathing-place used also by men. People murmured, were indignant, and complained, saying, 'As those women do who are still living in the pleasures of the world <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_946"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_369"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 369</font></a></p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhunî is not, O Bhikkhus, to bathe at a bathing-place used also by men. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a. I allow, Bhikkhunîs, O Bhikkhus, to bathe at a bathing-place used by women.'</p>
