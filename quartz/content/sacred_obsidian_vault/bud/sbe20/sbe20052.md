   <h3 align="center">37.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at the time when the venerable Kassapa of Uruve<i>l</i>a went forth (from the world), much property in brass and wood and earthenware came into the possession of the Sa<i>m</i>gha <a name="fr_427"></a><a href="#fn_419"><font size="1">2</font></a>. And the</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_156"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 156</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Bhikkhus thought, 'What kinds of brass ware--of wooden things--of earthenware <a name="fr_428"></a><a href="#fn_420"><font size="1">1</font></a>--has the Blessed One allowed, and what kinds has he not allowed?'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>Then the Blessed One, on that occasion and in that connection, when he had delivered a religious discourse, addressed the Bhikkhus, and said:</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, all kinds of brass ware, except weapons <a name="fr_429"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">2</font></a>--all kinds of wooden things, except divans <a name="fr_430"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and long-armed chairs <a name="fr_431"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and bowls <a name="fr_432"></a><a href="#fn_423"><font size="1">4</font></a>, and shoes <a name="fr_433"></a><a href="#fn_424"><font size="1">5</font></a>,--and all kinds of earthenware, except katakas <a name="fr_434"></a><a href="#fn_425"><font size="1">6</font></a>, and large earthen vessels to be used as huts to live in  <a name="fr_435"></a><a href="#fn_426"><font size="1">7</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p align="center">=====================</p>  
 <p align="center">Here ends the Fifth Khandhaka, on minor details.</p>
