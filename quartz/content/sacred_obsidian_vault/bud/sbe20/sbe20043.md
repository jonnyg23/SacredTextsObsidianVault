   <h3 align="center">28.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus laid up much store of brass ware and copper ware.</p>  
 <p>People who came on a visit to the Vihâras, seeing it, murmured, &amp;c., saying, 'Like those who spread out copper (for sale) <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to lay up much store of brass ware and copper ware. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <p><a name="page_141"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 141</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time the Bhikkhus were afraid to use boxes to put eye-ointment in <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and little flat sticks to lay it on with <a name="fr_372"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">2</font></a>, and instruments for removing wax from the ear <a name="fr_373"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">3</font></a>, and handles (for razors, staves, &amp;c.) <a name="fr_374"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of these things.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus sat down lolling up against their waist-cloths (arranged as a cushion) <a name="fr_375"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and the edges of the waist-cloths wore out <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to loll in this way. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu was sick, and without some handicraft <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">7</font></a> he was ill at ease.</p>
