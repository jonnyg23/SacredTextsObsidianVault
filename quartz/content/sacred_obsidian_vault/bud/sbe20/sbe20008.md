   <h3 align="center">7.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus carried out official acts against Bhikkhus who had not confessed themselves guilty--the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, or the Nissaya-kamma, or the Pabbâ<i>g</i>aniya-kamma, or the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kamma, or the Ukkhepaniya-kamma.</p>  
 <p>Those Bhikkhus who were moderate were annoyed, murmured, and became indignant, saying, 'How can the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus (&amp;c., as before).' And those Bhikkhus told the matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 23</font></a></p>  
 <p>'Is it true, O Bhikkhus, that the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus do so?'</p>  
 <p>'It is true, Lord.'</p>  
 <p>Then he rebuked them, and when he had delivered a religious discourse, he addressed the Bhikkhus, and said:</p>  
 <p>No official act, O Bhikkhus,--whether the Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma, or the Nissaya-kamma, or the Pabbâ<i>g</i>aniya-kamma, or the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kamma, or the Ukkhepaniya-kamma,--is to be carried out against Bhikkhus who have not confessed themselves guilty <a name="fr_38"></a><a href="#fn_37"><font size="1">1</font></a>. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>
