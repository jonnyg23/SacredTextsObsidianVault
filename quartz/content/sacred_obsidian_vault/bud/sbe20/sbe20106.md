   <h3 align="center">15.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the people gave food to the Bhikkhus, and the Bhikkhus gave to the Bhikkhunîs. The people murmured, were indignant, and complained, saying: 'How can their reverences give away to others what was given for them to have--as if we did not know how to give gifts?'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'A Bhikkhu, O Bhikkhus, is not to give away to others what was given for them themselves to have. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Bhikkhus had come into the possession of some (meat for) food. They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give it to the Sa<i>m</i>gha.'</p>  
 <p>Too much came into their possession.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to give away that which was given to special individuals (and to keep that which had come into the possession of the Sa<i>m</i>gha as a whole <a name="fr_913"></a><a href="#fn_899"><font size="1">1</font></a>).'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_347"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 347</font></a></p>  
 <p>Now at that time food, which had been stored up for the Bhikkhus, had come into their possession.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow food that has been stored up to be enjoyed by the Bhikkhunîs after they have had it given over to them by the Bhikkhus.'</p>  
 <p>2. [The same repeated, reading Bhikkhunî for Bhikkhu, and vice versâ.]</p>
