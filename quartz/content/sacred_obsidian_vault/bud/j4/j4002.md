 <p><a name="page_xiii"><font size="1" color="green">p. xiii</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ADDITIONAL NOTES AND CORRECTIONS.</h3>  <h4 align="center">VOLUME II.</h4>  
 <p><a href="j4005.htm#page_9">9</a> No. 154, line 3. Fick <i>(Sociale Gliederung zu Buddha's Zeit)</i> would render <i>seṇibandham</i> a "guild quarrel."</p>  
 <p><a href="j4006.htm#page_12">12</a> At a sneeze, a Hindu in the N. W. Provinces will still say, "May you live a hundred years." <i>North Indian Notes and Queries,</i> iv. 388.</p>  
 <p><a href="j4030.htm#page_104">104</a> For the first stanza compare Dhammapada, p. 146.</p>  
 <p><a href="j4047.htm#page_167">167</a> For the second and third stanzas compare Dhammapada, p. 149.</p>  
 <p><a href="j4054.htm#page_207">207</a> A Russian variant of the Gāmaṇicaṇḍa Birth is given in Prof. S. Oldenburg's Review of volume I. of the present translation: (Журналъ Министерства Народнаго Просвѣщенія) 1896, pp. 47 foll.</p>  
 <p><a href="j4063.htm#page_251">251</a> I have to thank Fick (<i>Soc. Glied.</i> p. 87 note) for a correction of the list of righteous persons, which should run thus; "…younger brother (who was) viceroy, brahmin family priest, courtier-charioteer, treasurer, noble master of the granaries, porter, slave-girl courtesan."</p>  
 <p><a href="j4064.htm#page_257">257</a> Fick explains the <i>rajjugāhakaamacco</i> as a kind of royal surveyor for tax purposes, which suits the context.</p>
