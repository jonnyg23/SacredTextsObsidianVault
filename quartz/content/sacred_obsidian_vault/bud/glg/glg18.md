 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Gateless Gate</i>, by Ekai, called Mu-mon, tr. Nyogen Senzaki and Paul Reps [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">18. Tozan's Three Pounds</h3>  
 <p>A monk asked Tozan when he was weighing some flax: "What is Buddha?" </p>  
 <p>Tozan said: "This flax weighs three pounds."</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p><i>Mumon's comment:</i> Old Tozan's Zen is like a clam. The minute the shell opens you see the whole inside. However, I want to ask you: Do you see the real Tozan?</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px"><i>Three pounds of flax in front of your nose,<br> Close enough, and mind is still closer.<br> Whoever talks about affirmation and negation<br> Lives in the right and wrong region</i>.</div>
