   <h3 align="center">9 <a name="fr_114"></a><a href="#fn_112"><font size="1">6</font></a></h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the venerable Bela<i>t</i><i>th</i>asîsa, the superior of the venerable Ânanda, had a disease of thick scabs; and by reason of the discharge thereof his robes stuck to his body. The Bhikkhus moistened those robes with water, and loosened them (from his body).</p>  
 <p>The Blessed One as he was wandering in search of a lodging-place for the night saw them [doing so], and going up to the place where they were, he asked them:</p>  
 <p>'What is the matter, O Bhikkhus, with this Bhikkhu?'</p>  
 <p>'The venerable one has the disease of thick scabs; and by reason of the discharge thereof his robes stick to his body. So we moisten those robes with water, and loosen them (from his body).'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_49"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 49</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Then the Blessed One on that occasion, having delivered a religious discourse, addressed the Bhikkhus, and said:</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of chunam as a medicine by whomsoever has the itch, or boils, or a discharge, or scabs, or whose body is ill-smelling, and to those in health the use of dry dung <a name="fr_115"></a><a href="#fn_113"><font size="1">1</font></a>, and of clay, and of colouring matter <a name="fr_116"></a><a href="#fn_114"><font size="1">2</font></a>. I allow the use, O Bhikkhus, of a pestle and mortar <a name="fr_117"></a><a href="#fn_115"><font size="1">3</font></a>.'</p>
