   <h3 align="center">12.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus used to put pulverised ointments into pots and saucers. They became sprinkled over with herb-powders and dust.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a box for ointment.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus used to carry about various kinds of boxes for ointments--gold ones, and silver ones. People were annoyed, murmured, and became angry, saying, 'Like those who still live in the world.'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Various kinds of boxes for ointments, gold ones. and silver ones, are not, O Bhikkhus, to be used. Whosoever does so, is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence. I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of such boxes made of bone, or ivory, or horn, or of the na<i>l</i>a reed, or of bambu, or of wood, or of lac, or of the shells of fruit, or of bronze, or of the centre of the chank-shell <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 52</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time the boxes of ointment had no lid. (The ointment) was sprinkled over with herb-powders and dust.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a lid.'</p>  
 <p>The lids used to fall off.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to fasten the lid with thread, and to tie it on to the box.'</p>  
 <p>The boxes used to fall.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to sew the boxes on with thread <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>3. Now at that time the Bhikkhus used to rub the ointment on with their fingers: and the eyes were hurt.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a stick or holder to put the ointment on with.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the <i>Kh</i>abbaggiya Bhikkhus used to keep various kinds of ointment-sticks--gold ones, and silver ones. People were annoyed, murmured, and became angry, saying, 'Like those who still live in the world.'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Various kinds of ointment-holders, O Bhikkhus, are not to be used. Whosoever does so, is guilty of. a dukka<i>t</i>a offence. I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of ointment-holders of bone, or of ivory, or of horn, or of the na<i>l</i>a reed, or of bambu, or of wood, or of lac, or of fruit, or of bronze, or of the chank-shell.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 53</font></a></p>  
 <p>4. Now at that time the ointment-sticks used to fall on the ground and become rough.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a case for the ointment-sticks <a name="fr_131"></a><a href="#fn_129"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the Bhikkhus used to carry the ointment-boxes and ointment-sticks about in their hands.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a bag to put the ointment-box in.'</p>  
 <p>They had no shoulder strap.</p>  
 <p>'I allow, O Bhikkhus, the use of a shoulder strap (by which to carry the ointment-box), or of a thread (by which to sew or tie it on).'</p>
