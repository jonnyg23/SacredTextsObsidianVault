 <p><a name="page_428"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 428</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">31.</h3>  
 <p>1. In case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu who is undergoing probation is guilty meanwhile of a number of Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences, and without concealing them then throws off the robes, and he, when he has again received the upasampadâ, does not conceal those offences-that Bhikkhu ought to be thrown back to the commencement (of his term of probation).</p>  
 <p>'And in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu (&amp;c., as before, down to) and he, when he has again received the upasampadâ, does conceal those offences--that Bhikkhu ought to be thrown back to the commencement (of his term of probation), and an inclusive probation ought to be imposed upon him (corresponding to the time which has elapsed since) the first offence among those offences which he has concealed.</p>  
 <p>'And in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu who is undergoing probation is guilty meanwhile of a number of Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences, and, concealing them, throws off the robes; and he, when he has again received the upasampadâ, does not conceal those offences--that Bhikkhu ought to be thrown back to the commencement of his term of probation, and an inclusive probation ought to be imposed upon him (corresponding to the period which has elapsed since) the first offence among those offences which he has concealed.'</p>  
 <p>[The same judgment if he has concealed the offences before he throws off the robes, and also after he has again received the upasampadâ.] <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_429"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 429</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. 'And in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu who is undergoing probation is guilty meanwhile of a number of Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences, and some of them he has concealed and some of them he has not concealed; and after he has thrown off the robes and again received the upasampadâ, he does not afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had concealed, and he does not afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had. not concealed--[the judgment is the same as in the last paragraph of § 1].'</p>  
 <p>[In the same case down to] he does not afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had concealed, and he does afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had not concealed [the judgment is the same].</p>  
 <p>[In the same case, down to] he does afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had concealed, and he does not afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had not concealed [the judgment is the same].</p>  
 <p>[In the same case, down to] he does afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had concealed, and he does afterwards conceal those offences which previously he had not concealed [the judgment is the same] <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>3. 'And in case, O Bhikkhus, a Bhikkhu who is undergoing probation is guilty meanwhile of a number of Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences, and he is aware of some of those offences, and not aware of others; and he conceals those offences of which he is aware, but does not conceal those offences of which. he is</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_430"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 430</font></a></p>  
 <p>not aware. After having thrown off the robes and again received the upasampadâ, those offences of which he had previously [&amp;c., as in chapter 29, section 3, down to] on that Bhikkhu [the penalty is the same as in the last section, chapter 31, § 2].'</p>  
 <p>[The rest of this chapter corresponds exactly to chapter 29, §§ 4, 5, and chapter 30; the penalty being always the same.]</p>
