   <h3 align="center">11.</h3>  
 <p>1. 'A Bhikkhu, after the Ka<i>th</i>ina ceremony has been held, goes away travelling to the (four) quarters (of the world <a name="fr_328"></a><a href="#fn_323"><font size="1">4</font></a>), guarding <a name="fr_329"></a><a href="#fn_324"><font size="1">5</font></a> his claim to a share in the robes. When he is so travelling, the Bhikkhus ask him: "Where have you kept Vassa, friend, and where have you your share in the robes?"</p>  
 <p>'He replies: "I have kept Vassa in such and such</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_167"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 167</font></a></p>  
 <p>a residence, and there I have my share in the robes."</p>  
 <p>'They say to him: "Go, friend, and bring your robe hither; we will make it up for you here."</p>  
 <p>'And he goes to that residence and asks the Bhikkhus: "Where is the portion of robes due to me, friends?"</p>  
 <p>'They reply: "Here it is, friend; where are you going?"</p>  
 <p>'He says: "I will go to such and such a residence; there the Bhikkhus will make up the robe for me."</p>  
 <p>'They answer: "Nay, friend, do not go; we will make up the robe for you here."</p>  
 <p>'And he thinks, "I will have the robe made up here, and will not go back (to that other place)," &amp;c. <a name="fr_330"></a><a href="#fn_325"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. 'A Bhikkhu, after the Ka<i>th</i>ina ceremony has been held, goes away travelling (&amp;c., as in 1, down to:) "Here it is, friend." And he takes that robe and sets out for that residence. On the way some Bhikkhus ask him: "Friend, where are you going?"</p>  
 <p>'He says: "I intend to go to such and such a residence; there the Bhikkhus will make up the robe for me."</p>  
 <p>'They answer: "Nay, friend, do not go; we will make up the robe for you here."</p>  
 <p>'And he thinks, "I will have the robe made up here, and will not go back (to that other place)," &amp;c. <a name="fr_331"></a><a href="#fn_326"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>3. 'A Bhikkhu, after the Ka<i>th</i>ina ceremony has been held, goes away travelling (&amp;c., as in § 1, down to:) "Here it is, friend." And he takes that robe, and sets out for that residence. And when going</p>  
 <p>
