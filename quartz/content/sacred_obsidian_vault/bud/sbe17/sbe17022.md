   <h3 align="center">8.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time the Bhikkhus who were sick had need of various kinds of salt <a name="fr_108"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">5</font></a> as medicine. They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of salts as</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_48"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 48</font></a></p>  
 <p>medicine--sea-salt <a name="fr_109"></a><a href="#fn_107"><font size="1">1</font></a>, black salt <a name="fr_110"></a><a href="#fn_108"><font size="1">2</font></a>, rock salt <a name="fr_111"></a><a href="#fn_109"><font size="1">3</font></a>, kitchen salt <a name="fr_112"></a><a href="#fn_110"><font size="1">4</font></a>, red salt <a name="fr_113"></a><a href="#fn_111"><font size="1">5</font></a>, and whatsoever other salts are used in medicine (&amp;c., as in chap. 4, down to:) is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a offence.'</p>
