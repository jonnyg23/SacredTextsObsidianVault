 <p><a name="page_399"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 399</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">2.</h3>  
 <p>1. When he had done the Mânatta he told the Bhikkhus, saying, 'Friends, I committed an offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence--which I had not concealed. And I asked the Sa<i>m</i>gha for a Mânatta of six days' duration for that offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence--which I had not concealed. The Sa<i>m</i>gha imposed upon me a Mânatta (&amp;c., as before, down to) not concealed. Now I have accomplished that Mânatta. What now shall I do?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Then, O Bhikkhus, let the Sa<i>m</i>gha rehabilitate Udâyi the Bhikkhu.</p>  
 <p>2. 'Now thus, O Bhikkhus, ought he to be rehabilitated.</p>  
 <p>'Udâyi the Bhikkhu ought to go up (&amp;c., as in chapter 1, § 2, down to) he ought to say as follows:</p>  
 <p>'"I committed, venerable Sirs, an offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence--which I did not conceal. I asked the Sa<i>m</i>gha (to impose upon me) a Mânatta of six days' duration for that offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offence--which I had not concealed. The Sa<i>m</i>gha imposed upon me a Mânatta of . . . . for . . . . not concealed. I, having accomplished that Mânatta, ask the Sa<i>m</i>gha for rehabilitation."'</p>  
 <p>[This speech is repeated three times.]</p>  
 <p>3. 'Then some discreet and able Bhikkhu [&amp;c., as before in chapter 1, § 2, the rest of the kammavâ<i>k</i>â bearing the same relation to the petition as it does there].'</p>
