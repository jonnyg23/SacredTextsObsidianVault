   <h3 align="center">23.</h3>  
 <p>1. So the venerable Sudhamma went, with another Bhikkhu as companion messenger, to Ma<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ikâsa<i>n</i><i>d</i>a, and obtained pardon of <i>K</i>itta the householder. And he conducted himself aright, and he became subdued, and he sought for release (&amp;c., as above, in chapters 6, 7, down to the end).</p>  
 <p align="center">__________________</p>  
 <p align="center">Here end the eighteen cases in which there ought to be a revocation of the Pa<i>t</i>isâra<i>n</i>iya-kamma.</p>
