 <p><a name="page_409"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 409</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">20.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now at that time a certain Bhikkhu had committed numerous Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa offences, one of which he had concealed for one day, one for two days, one for three days, [and so on down to] and one for ten days.</p>  
 <p>He told the Bhikkhus, saying, 'I have committed, (&amp;c., as before, down to) and one for ten days. What now shall I do?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Let then the Sa<i>m</i>gha, O Bhikkhus, impose upon that Bhikkhu an inclusive probation according to one of those offences which has been concealed for ten days <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_714"><font size="1">1</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>'Now thus, O Bhikkhus, ought it to be imposed.'</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_410"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 410</font></a></p>  
 <p>[Here follows the kammavâ<i>k</i>â in the form given at chapter 1, §§ 2, 3, to the end.]</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_714"></a><a href="sbe17174.htm#fr_735">409:1</a> From this and what follows it is clear that however many are the offences, and however various the periods of concealment, the probation is only to last for the same period as the longest of the concealments has lasted.
