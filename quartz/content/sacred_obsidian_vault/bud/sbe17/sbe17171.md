   <h3 align="center">17.</h3>  
 <p>1. While he was undergoing that Mânatta he committed an offence--the first Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa which he for four days concealed.</p>  
 <p>He told, &amp;c. . . . [all that had happened, from chapter 13 downwards].</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Let then the Sa<i>m</i>gha, O Bhikkhus, for that while (&amp;c., as in the first paragraph) throw Udâyi the Bhikkhu back to the commencement (of the probationary term he had already undergone), and impose</p>  
 <p><a name="page_407"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 407</font></a></p>  
 <p>upon him an inclusive probation (for this and) for the first offence, and also a Mânatta of six days' duration.</p>  
 <p>'Now thus, O Bhikkhus, [here follow three kammavâ<i>k</i>âs, one for the throwing back, one for the inclusive probation, and one for the new Mânatta, each of them on the same form as that given in chapter r.]'</p>
