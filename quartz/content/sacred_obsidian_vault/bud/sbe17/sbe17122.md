   <h3 align="center">11 <a name="fr_631"></a><a href="#fn_618"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  
 <p>1. So the Sa<i>m</i>gha carried out the Nissaya-kamma against the Bhikkhu Seyyasaka (saying), 'Thou must remain under the superintendence of others.'</p>  
 <p>And he, when subjected by the Sa<i>m</i>gha to the Nissaya-kamma, by resorting to and cultivating the acquaintance of good companions <a name="fr_632"></a><a href="#fn_619"><font size="1">2</font></a>, associating with them, getting them to declare to him (the Dhamma), and asking them questions, became wise in the traditions; a man to whom the Nikâyas had been handed down; a reciter of the Dhamma, of the Vinaya, and of the Mâtikas; clever, discreet, wise, modest, full of remorse, and docile; he conducted himself aright, he became subdued, he sought for release, and going up to the Bhikkhus, he spake as follows:</p>  
 <p>'I, Sirs, after having been subjected by the Sa<i>m</i>gha to the Nissaya-kamma, am conducting myself aright, and have become subdued, and I seek for release. What now should I do?'</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'Then, O Bhikkhus, let the Sa<i>m</i>gha revoke the Nissaya-kamma for the Bhikkhu Seyyasaka.</p>
