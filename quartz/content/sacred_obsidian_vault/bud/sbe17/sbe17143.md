   <h3 align="center">33.</h3>  
 <p>[Here follow the twelve cases in which the Kamma is against the law, the twelve cases in which it is according to law, the six permissive cases in which it can be carried out if the Sa<i>m</i>gha likes, and the eighteen divisions of the right conduct for the convicted Bhikkhu to pursue, precisely as in chapters 2, 3, 4, and 5, reading 'Ukkhepaniya-kamma for not renouncing a sinful doctrine,' instead of 'Ta<i>g</i><i>g</i>aniya-kamma. ]</p>
