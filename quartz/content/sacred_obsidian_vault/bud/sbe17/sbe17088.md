   <h3 align="center">21.</h3>  
 <p>1. Now the Bhikkhus thought: 'What is the limit for the size of a robe up to which it ought to be handed over to another Bhikkhu <a name="fr_428"></a><a href="#fn_420"><font size="1">1</font></a>?'</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I prescribe, O Bhikkhus, to hand over any robe which is in length eight inches according to the accepted inch <a name="fr_429"></a><a href="#fn_421"><font size="1">2</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>Now at that time a robe belonging to the venerable Mahâ Kassapa, which had been made of cast-off pieces of cloth became heavy (by reason of the weight of the new pieces tacked on to it <a name="fr_430"></a><a href="#fn_422"><font size="1">3</font></a>).</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to darn it roughly together with thread <a name="fr_431"></a><a href="#fn_423"><font size="1">4</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>It was uneven at the end <a name="fr_432"></a><a href="#fn_424"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this thing to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to remove the unevenness <a name="fr_433"></a><a href="#fn_425"><font size="1">6</font></a>.'</p>

 <p>The threads frayed out <a name="fr_434"></a><a href="#fn_426"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to put a braiding or a binding along or round (the edge) <a name="fr_435"></a><a href="#fn_427"><font size="1">2</font></a>:</p>  
 <p>Now at that time the ribbons <a name="fr_436"></a><a href="#fn_428"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the under garment gave way <a name="fr_437"></a><a href="#fn_429"><font size="1">4</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>They told this matter to the Blessed One.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to make an eight-footed . . .  <a name="fr_438"></a><a href="#fn_430"><font size="1">5</font></a>.'</p>  
 <p>2. Now at that time when a set of robes was being made for a certain Bhikkhu it was impossible to make it entirely from torn pieces of cloth <a name="fr_439"></a><a href="#fn_431"><font size="1">6</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to have two of the robes made of torn pieces of cloth, and one of cloth not torn.'</p>  
 <p>It was impossible to make two of the robes of torn pieces of cloth, and one of cloth not torn. </p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to make two robes</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p><a name="page_232"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 232</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (out of the set) of untorn pieces, and one of torn pieces.'</p>  
 <p>Even this was impossible.</p>  
 <p>'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to make (each robe out of the set) half (from torn pieces) and half <a name="fr_440"></a><a href="#fn_432"><font size="1">1</font></a> (from untorn pieces). But a set of robes made entirely from untorn pieces is not to be worn. Whosoever shall wear (a set of robes so made) is guilty of a dukka<i>t</i>a.'</p>
