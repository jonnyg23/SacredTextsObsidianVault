 <p><a name="an_j052"></a></p>  <h3 align="center">No. 52.</h3>  <h3 align="center">CŪḶA-JANAKA-JĀTAKA.</h3>  
 <p>"<i>Toil on, my brother</i>."--This story was told by the Master while at Jetavana, about another backsliding Brother. All the incidents that are to be related here, will be given in the Mahā-janaka-Jātaka <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</p>  
 <p>The king, seated beneath the white canopy of sovereignty, recited this stanza:--</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">"Toil on, my brother; still in hope stand fast;<br>   Faint not, nor tire, though harassed sore.<br> Myself I see, who, all my woes o’erpast,<br>   Have fought my stubborn way ashore.</div>  
 <p>Here too the backsliding Brother won Arahatship. The All-wise Buddha was King Janaka.</p>
