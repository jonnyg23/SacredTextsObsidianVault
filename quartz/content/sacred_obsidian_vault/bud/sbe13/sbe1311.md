 <h2 align="center">THE ADHIKARA<i>N</i>A-SAMATHÂ DHAMMÂ.</h2>
 
 <p align="center">THE RULES REGARDING THE SETTLEMENT OF CASES.</p>
 
 <p>   Here, venerable Sirs, the seven rules regarding the settlement of cases come into recitation.</p>
 
 <p>   For the decision and settlement of cases as they from time to time arise, the Proceeding in presence<a name="page_68_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_68_note_1"><sup><small>1</small></sup></a> must be performed, or the Proceeding for the consciously innocent<a name="page_68_fr_2"></a><a href="#page_68_note_2"><sup><small>2</small></sup></a>, or the Proceeding in the case of those who are no longer out of their mind<a name="page_68_fr_3"></a><a href="#page_68_note_3"><sup><small>3</small></sup></a>, or the Proceeding on confession of guilt<a name="page_68_fr_4"></a><a href="#page_68_note_4"><sup><small>4</small></sup></a>, or the Proceeding by majority of the chapter<a name="page_68_fr_5"></a><a href="#page_68_note_5"><sup><small>5</small></sup></a>, or the Proceeding for the obstinate<a name="page_68_fr_6"></a><a href="#page_68_note_6"><sup><small>6</small></sup></a>, or the Proceeding by covering over as with grass<a name="page_68_fr_7"></a><a href="#page_68_note_7"><sup><small>7</small></sup></a>.</p>
 
 <p>   Venerable Sirs, the seven rules regarding the settlement of cases have been recited.</p>
 
 <p>   ln respect of them l ask the venerable ones, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   A second time I ask the venerable ones, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>   A third time I ask the venerable ones, 'Are you pure in this matter?'</p>
 
 <p>
 
 <!------------------------page 69------------------------>
 
 <a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a>
 
 </p>
 
 <p>   The venerable ones are pure herein. Therefore do they keep silence. Thus I understand.</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p align="center">Here endeth the recitation of the Adhikara<i>n</i>a-samathas.</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p>   Venerable Sirs! Recited is the Introduction.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the four Pârâ<i>g</i>ika Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the thirteen Sa<i>m</i>ghâdisesa Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the two Aniyata Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the thirty Nissaggiya-Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the ninety-two Pâ<i>k</i>ittiya Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the four Pâ<i>t</i>idesaniya Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the Sekhiya Rules.</p>
 
 <p>   Recited are the seven Adhikara<i>n</i>a-samatha Rules.</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p>   So much (of the words) of the Blessed One, handed down in the Suttas, embraced in the Suttas, comes into recitation every half month. It behoveth all to train themselves according thereto in concord, in pleasantness, without dispute!</p>
 
 <hr width="33%" align="center">
 
 <p align="center">Here endeth the recitation of the Pâtimokkha for the use of the Bhikkhus.</p>
