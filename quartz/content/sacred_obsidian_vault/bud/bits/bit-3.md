 <p align="center">NOTE ON THE PRONUNCIATION OF PÂLI NAMES</p>
 
 <p>Short <i>a</i>, as in <i>organ</i>, or like the <i>u</i> in <i>but</i>. The other vowels, as in the key-words, <i>far</i>, <i>pin</i>, <i>pîque</i>, <i>pull</i>, <i>rûle</i>, (and roughly) <i>they</i>, <i>so</i>. Pronounce <i>c</i> like <i>ch</i> in <i>church</i>, and <i>j</i> as in <i>judge</i>. The "aspirates" are true aspirates: thus, <i>th</i>, <i>dh</i>, <i>ph</i>, as in <i>hothouse</i>, <i>madhouse</i>, <i>uphill</i>. They are not spirants as in <i>thin</i>, graphic. The underdotted <i>t</i>, <i>d</i>, <i>n</i>, etc. are pronounced (by the Hindus, at least) with the tip of the tongue turned up and drawn back. Dotted <i>m</i> indicates nasalization of the preceding vowel. {None of the dotted letters or the n-line-over, which is not mentioned here, are represented in this transcription}</p>
