 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 8</h3>  
 <p>THOSE who truly <a name="fr_68"></a><a href="#fn_68"><font size="1">1</font></a> meditate on Thee, the Spouse of Hara, <a name="fr_69"></a><a href="#fn_69"><font size="1">2</font></a> who art seated in <a name="fr_70"></a><a href="#fn_70"><font size="1">3</font></a> the cremation-ground strewn with funeral pyres, corpses, skulls, and bones, and haunted by female jackals howling fearfully; who art very youthful, <a name="fr_71"></a><a href="#fn_71"><font size="1">4</font></a> and art in full enjoyment upon <a name="fr_72"></a><a href="#fn_72"><font size="1">5</font></a> Thy Spouse, are revered by all and in all places. <a name="fr_73"></a><a href="#fn_73"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Meditate on' (Dhyāyanti)</i></p>  
 <p>That is see with unperturbed mind.</p>  
 <p><i>'Spouse of Hara' (Haravadhūṁ)</i></p>  
 <p>Hara is He who removes (Harati) the threefold pains (Ādhyātmika, Ādhibhautika, Ādhidaivika) of Jivas. His spouse is Śakti, that is She who grants Liberation to Jīvas and is Saccidānandarūpiṇī.'</p>  
 <p><i>'Hast entered' (Praviṣtāṁ)</i></p>  
 <p>Art established.</p>  
 <p><i>'Flaming' pyre (Prakatitacitāyāṁ)</i></p>  
 <p>Cit-śakti On account of Her being self-manifested. Caṇdī speaks of 'Her who pervades the whole universe as consciousness (Cit).'</p>  
 <p><i>'Fearful' (Ghorābhih)</i></p>  
 <p>That is very powerful.</p>  
 <p><i>'Jackals' (Śivābhih)</i></p>  
 <p>That is Mahābhūtas which are auspicious (Śiva) before being made fivefold (Pañcīkṛta).</p>  
 <p><i>'Skulls and bones' (Muṇdāsthi-nikaraih)</i></p>  
 <p>The white colour of the skulls and bones indicates the white Sattva-guṇa. Hence associated with the Sattva and other Guṇas of the Jīvas dissolved in Mahāpralaya.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="green">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>'Ever youthful' (Atiyuvatīṁ)</i></p>  
 <p>That is She is always the same, fresh, unchanging, and un-wasting.</p>  
 <p><i>'Satisfied with enjoyment ' (Santushtām-uparisuratena)</i></p>  
 <p>She, after subduing Parama Śiva to Her will, has willingly enjoyment in the work of creation, preservation and dissolution. Nirvāṇa-Tantra says, 'The Vāmā (She who is on the left) is the Grantrix of Great Liberation after conquering the Dakṣiṇa (Śiva who is on the right).' Gandharva-Tantra says, 'She who is the Sun, Moon, and Fire and half of Ha (Śiva) puts down the Puruṣa and enjoys him from above.' Niruttara-Tantra says, 'When Nirguṇā Kālī becomes Saguṇā She is engaged in Viparītarati.' The Yoga-vāṣiṣtha in the Nirvāṇa-Prakaraṇa says, 'Natural unity is Śiva. Creation is (compared with it) unnatural.' That is the Mahādevī is Nirguṇa-Brahman in Her Svarūpa aspect and the subversion of this Svarūpa is the cause of creation.</p>  
 <p><i>'Nowhere' (Kvacidapi na)</i></p>  
 <p>In no birth.</p>  
 <p><i>'Humiliated' (Paribhavah)</i></p>  
 <p>That is they are not subjected to birth, death, and rebirth and attain Nirvāṇa.</p>
