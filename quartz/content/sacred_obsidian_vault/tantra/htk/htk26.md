 <p><a name="page_94"><font size="1" color="green">p. 94</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 22</h3>  
 <p>NUMBERS of women with large eyes, like those of the antelope, <a name="fr_160"></a><a href="#fn_160"><font size="1">1</font></a> impatient for his love, ever follow him. Even the King becomes subject to his control. He becomes like unto Kuvera <a name="fr_161"></a><a href="#fn_161"><font size="1">2</font></a> himself. An enemy fears him as if he were a prison. Living in continuous bliss <a name="fr_162"></a><a href="#fn_162"><font size="1">3</font></a> the devotee is liberated when yet living, and is never again reborn. <a name="fr_163"></a><a href="#fn_163"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>Here ends the Hymn by Śrī Mahākāla, entitled <i>Karpūrādistotra</i>.</p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Liberated' (Jīvanmukta)</i></p>  
 <p>And on death gets Videhamukti.</p>  
 <p><i>'No rebirth' (Muktah pratijanuh)</i></p>  
 <p>He gets Nirvāṇa in Brahman. The Mahākāla-saṁhitā says, 'Whoever constantly and with devotion reads this Hymn originating from Mahākāla, is free from danger, disease and death and in the end attains Kaivalya liberation.'</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Here ends the Hymn named Svarūpastotra of Śrīmatī<br> Dakṣiṇa-Kālikā by Śrīmān Mahākāla</i>.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>Here also ends its annotation and Svarūpavyākhyā<br> entitled Vimalānandadāyini</i>.</p>

 <p align="center">OBEISANCE</p>  
 <p>To Kālī the spouse of Kāla, who destroys all sin and is Kāla. <a name="fr_164"></a><a href="#fn_164"><font size="1">1</font></a> She who is Tārā the Saviour the Supreme Brahmavidyā who is adored by the Lotus-born Deva. <a name="fr_165"></a><a href="#fn_165"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>She who is Śrīvidyā, desirous of the welfare of Sādhakas, on the path of Liberation, to whom Hari and Hara <a name="fr_166"></a><a href="#fn_166"><font size="1">3</font></a> make obeisance.</p>  
 <p>May that Devī the Mother, who appears in the form of all things, bring forth benefits for all such as sing Her praises.</p>  
 <p align="center">COLOPHON</p>  
 <p>Of this King of Hymns wherein Mahākāla has described the true self of Kālikā, the Karpūrādya Hymn, untainted by worldly desire, which gives bliss to Devotees, the aforesaid Annotation containing its simple interpretation, as well as the Svarūpavyākhyā (Commentary) which gives pure joy was prepared by me Vimalānanda Svāmī for the enlightenment of Sādhakas in the Saka year 1837. Mayest Thou reside in the throat of him who reads it.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_160"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_160">94:1</a> <i>Kuraṅga</i>, which has beautiful large eyes.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_161"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_161">94:2</a> Lord of wealth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_162"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_162">94:3</a> <a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="green">p. 95</font></a> <i>Kelikalayā</i>, by the various entertaining acts (<i>parihāsādinā</i>) of which there are sixty-four. The meaning here is that there is continuous bliss.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_163"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_163">94:4</a> <i>Kelihalayā ciraṁ jivanmuktah sa bhavati ca bhakhtah pratijanuh</i>, The translation in the text reads <i>pratijanuh</i> to mean as K. B. says, <i>Janmanivritti</i> or cessation of birth. But <i>Pratijanuh</i> may also mean 'birth after birth.' According to this translation <i>jivanmukta</i> would not refer to the state immediately preceding Kaivalya but, as K.B. says, <i>Jivadavasthānubhūtadevatā-sākṣātkāra-mukha</i> in which case the translation will be, He living in continuous bliss obtains direct Experience of the Devatā and is reborn life after life as Her devotee. According to the translation adopted complete liberation follows and in the other case some lower though happy state.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_164"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_164">95:1</a> The first Kāla is Mahākāla and the second is the produced Kāla.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_165"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_165">95:2</a> Brahmā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_166"></a><a href="htk26.htm#fr_166">95:3</a> Viṣṇu and Rudra.</p>    </center></body>
