 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="green">p. 68</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 10</h3>  
 <p>IF by night, <a name="fr_82"></a><a href="#fn_82"><font size="1">1</font></a> Thy devotee <a name="fr_83"></a><a href="#fn_83"><font size="1">2</font></a> unclothed, with dishevelled hair, recites whilst meditating on Thee, <a name="fr_84"></a><a href="#fn_84"><font size="1">3</font></a> Thy <i>mantra</i>, <a name="fr_85"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">4</font></a> when with his <i>Śakti</i> <a name="fr_86"></a><a href="#fn_86"><font size="1">5</font></a> youthful, full-breasted, and heavy-hipped, such an one makes all powers subject to him, and dwells on the earth ever <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_87"><font size="1">6</font></a> a seer. <a name="fr_88"></a><a href="#fn_88"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Laya Yoga'</i></p>  
 <p>Is here described in this and following verses. Gheraṇda-Saṁhitā says, 'One should become Śaktimaya by doing Yoni Mudra. One should be in Paramātmā with sweet Śṛngārarasa (love sentiment) and being Blissful (Ānandamaya) should unite with Brahman.' The Gorakṣa-Saṁhitā says, 'Raising the Śakti with the Jīva to the Lotus in the head one should become Śaktimaya and uniting with Śiva should think of all forms of happiness and enjoyment.' The Tantra-Kalpadruma says, 'One should meditate on Devī Kuṇdalinī as Iṣtadevatā, ever youthful, of the age of sixteen, full-breasted, dark, subtle, appearing as creation and in the form of creation, maintenance and dissolution (Ṣṛṣti-sthiti-layātmikā).'</p>  
 <p><i>'Thy devotee' (Bkaktah)</i></p>  
 <p>Here the Divya Sādhaka who is a Yogin.</p>  
 <p><i>'By night (Naktam)'</i></p>  
 <p>That is, awaking in Brahmavidyā which (though Light) is darkness for all ordinary creatures. The Bhagavadgītā says, 'The self-controlled man awakes in what is night to all creatures.'</p>  
 <p><i>'Naked' (Vivāsāh)</i></p>  
 <p>That is, stripped of the covering of Māyā: that is awakened.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="green">p. 69</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>'Dishevelled hair' (Galitacikurah)</i></p>  
 <p>That is, with mind free from all restlessness. The word Cikura means both hair and restless.</p>  
 <p><i>'Meditating' (Dhyāyan)</i></p>  
 <p>On Thee as in enjoyment of Sāmarasya bliss with Paramaśiva.</p>  
 <p><i>'Enjoying' (Ratāsaktām)</i></p>  
 <p>By doing Laya of (merging) the Jīvātmā in Kuṇdaliṇī-Śakti, the ever-youthful, all-pervading Genetrix and Preserver of all Jīvas. The creative and nourishing function of Kuṇdaliṇī is indicated by the epithets 'heavy-hipped' and 'full-breasted.'</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_82"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_82">68:1</a> <a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="green">p. 69</font></a> <i>Naktaṁ</i>. At dead of night. The Phetkāriṇī-Tantra says, 'By night, naked with dishevelled hair in union with <i>Śakti</i>, by him is all <i>Siddhi</i> gained'. The Kālikrama says, 'The <i>Paśu</i> devoted to his own <i>Ācāra</i> should recite his <i>Mantra</i> a lakh of times by day. The <i>Vira</i> or <i>Divya</i> should recite it a lakh of times by night.' Kubjikā-Tantra says 'Such as are in <i>Paśubhāva</i> are but <i>Paśus</i>. They should not touch a rosary nor recite <i>Mantra</i> by night.' (V)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_83"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_83">68:2</a> <i>Bhaktah</i>. Here a <i>Vira-Sādhaka</i>. Niruttara-Tantra says, 'The <i>Mantrin</i> who has received <i>Ābhiṣeka</i> should do <i>Kulapūjā</i>. Oh Devī the <i>Mantra</i> of <i>Kāli</i> does not become <i>Siddha</i> without <i>Kulācāra</i>.' (V)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_84"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_84">68:3</a> <i>Tvāṁ dhyāyan</i>. Mentally seeing Thee in his heart as ever in the Enjoyment of union with <i>Mahākāla</i>. (V)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_85"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_85">68:4</a> Thy <i>Mantra</i> is the aforesaid great <i>Mantra</i>. (V)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_86"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_86">68:5</a> He is <i>Ratāsakta</i>, the meaning of which is as follows: <i>Sa mantraṁ japati yadā sa śobhanāṅgapratyaṅgaśālinyā manohāriṇyāyuvatyā śaktyā saha maithunāsakto bhavati</i>. Whilst in union (<i>Maithuna</i>) the mind must be concentrated on <i>Devī Kāli</i> and <i>japā</i> must be done of Her <i>Mahāmantra</i>. The devotee should not think of aught else.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_87"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_87">68:6</a> So also Phetkāriṇī-Tantra (ch. x) says:</p>  
 <p></p><dir>'<i>Rātrau nagnah śyānas ca maithune ca vyavasthitah.<br> Athavā muktakeśash ca tena syuh sarvvasiddhayah.<br> Staṁbhanaṁ mohana-caiva vaśīkaraṇaṁ eva ca</i>.'</dir>  
 <p>Here <i>Athavā</i> means if the <i>Sādhaka</i> is without a <i>Śakti</i>; then recitation of <i>mantra</i> with dishevelled hair gives the same <i>siddhi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_88"></a><a href="htk14.htm#fr_88">68:7</a> <i>Kavi</i> which has not here the limited sense of 'Poet.'</p>
