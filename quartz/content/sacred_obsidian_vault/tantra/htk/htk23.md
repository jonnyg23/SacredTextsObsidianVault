 <p><a name="page_89"><font size="1" color="green">p. 89</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">VERSE 19</h3>  
 <p>O DARK One, <a name="fr_141"></a><a href="#fn_141"><font size="1">1</font></a> wondrous and excelling in every way, <a name="fr_142"></a><a href="#fn_142"><font size="1">2</font></a> becomes the accomplishment, <a name="fr_143"></a><a href="#fn_143"><font size="1">3</font></a> of those worshippers <a name="fr_144"></a><a href="#fn_144"><font size="1">4</font></a> who living in this world <a name="fr_145"></a><a href="#fn_145"><font size="1">5</font></a> freely make offering to Thee in worship <a name="fr_146"></a><a href="#fn_146"><font size="1">6</font></a> of the greatly <a name="fr_147"></a><a href="#fn_147"><font size="1">7</font></a> satisfying flesh, together with hair and bone, <a name="fr_148"></a><a href="#fn_148"><font size="1">8</font></a> of cats, camels, sheep, <a name="fr_149"></a><a href="#fn_149"><font size="1">9</font></a> buffaloes, goats, and men. <a name="fr_150"></a><a href="#fn_150"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">COMMENTARY</p>  
 <p><i>'Oh Black one' (Asītā)</i></p>  
 <p>Asitā means free from bondage. Sitā means bound. Asitā is therefore 'not bound' or eternally liberated. The root <i>So</i>, means 'to bind.' Amarakośa gives the meaning of Sita as 'bound.'</p>  
 <p><i>'Wondrous' (Apūrvā)</i></p>  
 <p>Best.</p>  
 <p><i>'At every step' (Pratipadam)</i></p>  
 <p>In succession, step by step.</p>  
 <p><i>'All Powers' (Sarvasiddhi)</i></p>  
 <p>The five Siddhis which are the five forms of Liberation. The Śivagītā says, 'Sālokya, Sārūpya, Sārṣti, Sāyujya and Kaivalya. Know these to be the five forms of liberation.'</p>  
 <p><i>'The flesh of' (Palalaṁ)</i></p>  
 <p>These animals represent the Six Enemies (Ripu) or Vices which are specially characteristic of the following animals: The goat stands for Lust (Kāma) 'as lustful as a goat (Chhāga),' the buffalo, Anger (Krodha) 'as angry as a buffalo (Mahiṣa),' the cat, Greed (Lobha) 'as greedy as a cat' (Mārjāra), the sheep, Delusion (Moha), 'as stupid as a sheep' (Meṣa) the camel, Envy (Mātsarya) 'as envious as a camel' (Uṣtra), Man, Pride (Mada) 'the Pride and arrogance of man' (Nara).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_90"><font size="1" color="green">p. 90</font></a></p>  
 <p>The Ānandākalpa says, 'Worship should be done by making offering of lust as goat, buffalo, and so forth '. Offering is made to Thee who art Cidrūpā of lust and other vices as articles of offering (Upacāra) in worship with the object of ridding oneself of them. Bṛhannīla-Tantra says, 'In the fire of Ātmā which flames with the ghee (Havih) of Dharma and Adharma, I ever offer in Homa by the Suṣuṁnā path, with the mind as ladle, all the functions of the sensesSvāhā.'</p>  
 <p><i>'In worship' (Pūjāyām)</i></p>  
 <p>In mental worship according to the manner prescribed.</p>  
 <p><i>'With hair and bone' (Loma, asthi)</i></p>  
 <p>That is the whole without omitting any part. Such Sādhakas attain the Sālokya and other forms of liberation.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_141"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_141">89:1</a> Asitā: That is Kālikā v. <i>post</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_142"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_142">89:2</a> <i>Pratipadam</i>. The Śyāmarahasya-sārasaṁgraha reads <i>pratidinam</i> (every day) (K.B.), which seems preferable, for, as K.B. says, the worship (<i>pūjā</i>) is the general daily <i>pūjā</i>, upon which daily advancement in <i>siddhi</i> would follow.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_143"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_143">89:3</a> <i>Siddhi</i>: success in work; accomplishment of all which is desired (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_144"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_144">89:4</a> <i>Sat</i>, that is, <i>sādhu</i> (wise, good, pious). <i>Satāṁ</i> = <i>Sādhakānām</i> (V).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_145"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_145">89:5</a> That is, among men.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_146"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_146">89:6</a> <i>Pūjāyāṁ api</i> (see note <a href="#fn_142">2</a>), <i>ante</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Pūjāyāṁ</i>: <i>Naimittika</i> or occasional worship (V). The force of the particle <i>api</i> is that the offering is not confined to special <i>Sādhanā</i> but is made in ordinary worship also. (K.B.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_147"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_147">89:7</a> <i>Param</i> (K.B.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_148"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_148">89:8</a> That is flesh and all.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_149"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_149">89:9</a> <i>Maiṣaṁ</i>. The Śyāmarahasya-sārasaṁgraha gives also <i>mauṣam</i>, of rat's flesh. The Fetkāriṇī-Tantra has both sheep and rat's flesh (K.B.).</p>  
 <p><i>Śyāmārahasya</i> says, To him who makes offering of the flesh of cats, sheep, camels, and buffaloes together with bone, hair and skin <i>Dākṣiṇā</i> is ever beneficial like a Mother.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_150"></a><a href="htk23.htm#fr_150">89:10</a> As to this human sacrifice, K. B. says that Kings alone, and not any other, are entitled to make human sacrifice, citing the Yāmala quoted in the Kālīkalpalatā (<i>Rājā naravaliṁ dadyān nānyopi parameśvari</i>). For inner sense see Svarūpa-vyākhyā <i>post</i>.</p>
