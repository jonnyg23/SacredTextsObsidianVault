 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">CAṆḌIKĀ</h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE FOURTH OR SHAKRĀDI <a name="fr_720"></a><a href="#fn_718"><font size="1">1</font></a> MAHĀTMYA OF CAṆḌĪ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(MĀRKANDEYA PURĀNA)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>May that Devī by whose power this world was spread,<br> The perfect form of the powers of countless Devas, <a name="fr_721"></a><a href="#fn_719"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> The Mother <a name="fr_722"></a><a href="#fn_720"><font size="1">3</font></a> worshipped by Devas and Mahaṛṣis, <a name="fr_723"></a><a href="#fn_721"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Do good to us.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>May that Caṇḍikā whose peerless majesty and power<br> Neither Bhagavān Ananta, <a name="fr_724"></a><a href="#fn_722"><font size="1">5</font></a> Brahmā, nor Hara <a name="fr_725"></a><a href="#fn_723"><font size="1">6</font></a> can declare,<br> Turn Herself towards us for the destruction of the fear of evil,<br> And the protection of the whole world.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 118</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>We bow to Her who is good fortune itself in the dwellings of the virtuous,<br> Ill-fortune in those of the sinful,<br> Reason <a name="fr_726"></a><a href="#fn_724"><font size="1">1</font></a> in the hearts of the intelligent, faith in those of the good,<br> Modesty in that of the high born.<br> Protect, O Devī! this universe.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>How can we describe Thy thought-transcending form,<br> Or, Thy greatly abounding strength which destroyed the Asuras, <a name="fr_727"></a><a href="#fn_725"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Or, O Devī! those great deeds of Thine<br> Done in battle midst hosts of Devas, Asuras, and others?</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>Thou art the cause of all the worlds,<br> Though Thy substance is the three <i>guṇas</i>, <a name="fr_728"></a><a href="#fn_726"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Yet is no fault known in thee. <a name="fr_729"></a><a href="#fn_727"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Incomprehensible art Thou even to Hari, Hara, <a name="fr_730"></a><a href="#fn_728"><font size="1">5</font></a> and other Devas, <a name="fr_731"></a><a href="#fn_729"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_119"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 119</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thou art the refuge of all.<br> The whole world is but a part of Thee, <a name="fr_732"></a><a href="#fn_730"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Unmanifested, <a name="fr_733"></a><a href="#fn_731"><font size="1">2</font></a> primeval, supreme Prakṛti. <a name="fr_734"></a><a href="#fn_732"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>O Devī! Thou art <i>Svāhā</i>, <a name="fr_735"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> By the utterance whereof all Devas in all sacrifices are satisfied.<br> Thou art also declared by men to be <i>Svadhā</i>, <a name="fr_736"></a><a href="#fn_733"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Which satisfies the <i>pitṛs</i> <a name="fr_737"></a><a href="#fn_734"><font size="1">5</font></a>.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Thou, O Devī! whose great <i>vrata</i> <a name="fr_738"></a><a href="#fn_735"><font size="1">6</font></a> surpasses all thought,<br> Art the supreme knowledge full of power<br> Which is the cause of liberation<br> Ever sought to be gained by those <i>Munis</i> <a name="fr_739"></a><a href="#fn_736"><font size="1">7</font></a> desirous thereof,<br> Who have strictly controlled their senses and are free of all faults. <a name="fr_740"></a><a href="#fn_737"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_120"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 120</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Thou art in the form of sound.<br> The repository of spotless <a name="fr_741"></a><a href="#fn_738"><font size="1">1</font></a> Ṛg <a name="fr_742"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">2</font></a> and Yajus <a name="fr_743"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">2</font></a> hymns,<br> And of the Sāman <a name="fr_744"></a><a href="#fn_739"><font size="1">2</font></a> hymns wherein are the verses of the charmful <i>Udgītha</i>, <a name="fr_745"></a><a href="#fn_740"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Devī, Thou art the threefold Veda <a name="fr_746"></a><a href="#fn_741"><font size="1">4</font></a> and Bhagavatī; <a name="fr_747"></a><a href="#fn_742"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> For the maintenance of the world Thou art the science of <i>Vartta</i>; <a name="fr_748"></a><a href="#fn_743"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Thou art the supreme destroyer of its pains. <a name="fr_749"></a><a href="#fn_744"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>O Devī! Thou art the power of understanding <a name="fr_750"></a><a href="#fn_745"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> By Which the essence of all <i>Śāstras</i> is known;<br> Thou art Durgā, <a name="fr_751"></a><a href="#fn_746"><font size="1">9</font></a> the vessel wherein we cross the dangerous ocean of the world.<br> Devoid of attachment art Thou. <a name="fr_752"></a><a href="#fn_747"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> Śrī <a name="fr_753"></a><a href="#fn_748"><font size="1">11</font></a> also, who hast made Thy abode in the heart of the enemy <a name="fr_754"></a><a href="#fn_749"><font size="1">12</font></a> of Kaiṭabha, <a name="fr_755"></a><a href="#fn_750"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_121"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 121</font></a></p>  
 <p>Thou art indeed Gaurī, <a name="fr_756"></a><a href="#fn_751"><font size="1">1</font></a> who hast fixed Thy dwelling in the moon-crested Deva. <a name="fr_757"></a><a href="#fn_752"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>Smiling spotless like unto the full moon,<br> Resplendent as the finest gold<br> And lovely was thy face.<br> Yet wonderful it was that swayed by wrath<br> The Asura Mahiṣa suddenly smote Thy face when he saw it.</p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Greatly marvellous indeed it was that when he had seen Thy face,<br> Wrathful, terribly frowning, beauteous as the rising moon,<br> Mahiṣa did not forthwith yield up his life,<br> For who can live after beholding the wrathful king of Death? <a name="fr_758"></a><a href="#fn_753"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>O Devī, our supreme Lady<br> Be gracious for the sake of the world.<br> For when wrathful Thou dost suddenly destroy the generations of the enemies. <a name="fr_759"></a><a href="#fn_754"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> It is but now made known to us<br> That the mighty army of the Asura Mahiṣa has met its end.</p>  
 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>Those to whom Thou, O bestower of prosperity! art gracious,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_122"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 122</font></a></p>  
 <p>Are esteemed in all lands,<br> Their wealth and fame increases,<br> And their <i>dharma</i>, <i>artha</i>, <i>kāma</i>, <i>mokṣa</i> <a name="fr_760"></a><a href="#fn_755"><font size="1">1</font></a> know no lessening.<br> Praiseworthy are they maintaining sons, servants, and wives.</p>  
 <p align="center">14</p>  
 <p>By thy grace, O Devī! the virtuous man, ever honoured,<br> Does ever daily all religious acts,<br> And thereafter gains heaven by Thy grace; <a name="fr_761"></a><a href="#fn_756"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Therefore art Thou of a surety the giver of fruit in the three worlds.</p>  
 <p align="center">15</p>  
 <p>O Durgā; the remembrance of Thee destroyest the fear of all creatures,<br> When called to recollection by those in health Thou dost bestow a truly good mind.<br> O remover of poverty, pain, and fear,<br> Who but Thee art ever compassionate for the good of all.</p>  
 <p align="center">16</p>  
 <p>By the slaying of these foes the world gains happiness.<br> O Devī! Thou hast slain them with the desire<br> That they should not always sin so as to merit hell, <a name="fr_762"></a><a href="#fn_757"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> But that by death in battle they may go to Heaven.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_123"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 123</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">17</p>  
 <p>Seeing them, why dost Thou not (by Thy look) turn them to ashes?<br> Thou throwest Thy weapon among the enemies, the Asuras,<br> In order that, being purified by it,<br> Even these enemies may go to heaven.<br> Such is Thy merciful intention even towards them.</p>  
 <p align="center">18</p>  
 <p>If by the glittering, formidable flashes of Thy sword,<br> And by the lustre of Thy spear-point,<br> The eyes of the Asuras were not destroyed,<br> It was because they gazed on Thy countenance,<br> Like unto the radiant moon.</p>  
 <p align="center">19</p>  
 <p>O Devī! Thy nature it is to subdue the evil works of the wicked.<br> Thy form, destructive of the strength of those who destroy the Devas,<br> Surpasses all thought, and is comparable with none.<br> By this Thou hast manifested Thy kindness even to enemies.</p>  
 <p align="center">20</p>  
 <p>Devī! with whom may this Thy valour be compared,<br> Or Thy most charming form striking fear among foes?<br> In Thee only, bestower of boons, even upon three worlds,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_124"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 124</font></a></p>  
 <p>Are seen both kindness of heart and relentlessness in battle.</p>  
 <p align="center">21</p>  
 <p>By the destruction of their foes the three worlds have been saved by Thee,<br> Thou hast led even these to heaven,<br> Having slain them in the front of battle.<br> And hast dispelled the fear besetting us from the maddened enemies of the Devas.<br> Salutation to Thee, O Devī!</p>  
 <p align="center">22</p>  
 <p>With Thy spear protect us, O Devī! <a name="fr_763"></a><a href="#fn_758"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> O Mother! protect us with Thy sword.<br> By the sound of Thy bell guard us,<br> And by the twanging of Thy bow-string<br> Protect us in the East and in the West,<br> Guard us, O Caṇḍikā! in the South,<br> And in the North by the brandishing of Thy spear.</p>  
 <p align="center">23</p>  
 <p>Whatever gentle forms of Thine,<br> And whatever of Thy terrible forms Wander in the three worlds,<br> By these forms protect us and the earth.</p>  
 <p align="center">24</p>  
 <p>O Mother! by Thy sword, spear, and club,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_125"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 125</font></a></p>  
 <p>And other weapons, in Thy soft and supple hands, <a name="fr_764"></a><a href="#fn_759"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Guard us on every side. <a name="fr_765"></a><a href="#fn_760"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="fn_733"></a><a href="htg20.htm#fr_736">119:4</a> The <i>Mantra</i> of that name.</p>

On which the latter prayed that whenever they called upon Her She might come to their assistance, and that whatever mortal should praise Her with this hymn should prosper. Bhadrakāli said, "Be it so," and vanished from their sight.</p>
