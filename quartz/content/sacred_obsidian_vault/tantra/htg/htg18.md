 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a> <a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MOTHER OF THE WHOLE UNIVERSE</h3>  <h3 align="center">(SARVAVIŚVAJANANĪ)</h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE DEVĪBHĀGAVATA <a name="fr_640"></a><a href="#fn_638"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>I call to mind the Mother of the whole universe,<br> Who has created this world, both real and unreal, <a name="fr_641"></a><a href="#fn_639"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> And who, by Her own power with its three guṇas, <a name="fr_642"></a><a href="#fn_640"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Protects it, and having destroyed it, She then plays, <a name="fr_643"></a><a href="#fn_641"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 104</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Commonly is it said that Brahmā creates the universe,<br> Yet the learned in Veda and Purāṇā<br> Speak of His birth from the navel lotus of Murāri, <a name="fr_644"></a><a href="#fn_642"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Although it is said He creates, yet He is Himself dependent therein. <a name="fr_645"></a><a href="#fn_643"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>Even Murāri in the blossom of whose navel lotus, Brahmā was born--<br> Deeply sleeps upon his serpent bed <a name="fr_646"></a><a href="#fn_644"><font size="1">3</font></a> at the time of dissolution.<br> Therefore Ananta with his thousand hoods is His support.<br> How can He who is Himself supported<br> Be called a leader <a name="fr_647"></a><a href="#fn_645"><font size="1">4</font></a> in the creation of the world?</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>Even the water of Ocean <a name="fr_648"></a><a href="#fn_646"><font size="1">5</font></a> which is a liquid substance<br> Cannot exist without a container; therefore <a name="fr_649"></a><a href="#fn_647"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> I take refuge with Her, the Mother of all beings,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who exists in all things in the form of Power. <a name="fr_650"></a><a href="#fn_648"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>Brahmā in the lotus,<br> Seeing that the eyes of Viṣṇu were closed in deep slumber, <a name="fr_651"></a><a href="#fn_649"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Prayed to that Devī with whom I take shelter. <a name="fr_652"></a><a href="#fn_650"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_638"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_640">103:1</a> First Skandha, (chap. ii.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_639"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_641">103:2</a> <i>Sadāsatsvarūpam</i>, on which Nīlakantha says: <i>Vyavahāra drishtyā sat, paramārthadarshanena asat</i>; that is the world is real (<i>sat</i>) from the point of view of practical life and reason; to all those, in short, who have not experimentally realized the <i>Advaita Tattva</i>; but to those who have, and from the transcendental standpoint, it is, in fact, unreal (<i>asat</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_640"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_642">103:3</a> <i>Sattva</i>, <i>rajas</i>, and <i>tamas</i>, the substance of Śakti, as <i>Prakṛti</i> (see Introduction).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_641"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_643">103:4</a> <i>Ramate</i>--that is, "in her own self" (<i>svasminnevakrīrate</i>) (N) She shines. Hence She is also called Lalitā. "She who plays;" Padma pr. says, "having passed beyond the worlds, She plays; hence She is called Lalitā." Beyond Śakti and Śiva there exist various manifestations of Parāśakti and Sadāśiva, each in its own sphere. But Mahāsakti, who is Paramaśiva, crossing all worlds in the supreme sphere of <i>Mahākailāsa</i>. She it is who is known as Lalitā and Kāmeśvarī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_642"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_644">104:1</a> Viṣṇu as Enemy of the Daitya Mura.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_643"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_645">104:2</a> Because He is born, He creates dependent on Bhagavatī. In the next verse the argument is: "Let Brahmā be not the creator; why not, then, Viṣṇu?" To which the answer is given of His dependence on Ananta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_644"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_646">104:3</a> Viṣṇu reposes on the 1,000-headed Serpent Ananta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_645"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_647">104:4</a> <i>Netra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_646"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_648">104:5</a> Which is again the support of Ananta.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_647"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_649">104:6</a> And that container requires a support. Therefore the <i>ādhāraśakti</i> is the Mother of all. For this reason, in commencing any <i>pūja</i>, the <i>Ādhāraśakti</i> is worshipped on account of Her being the supporter of all, and that Śakti is none but the Mother of all.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_648"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_650">105:1</a> <i>Śaktirūpā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_649"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_651">105:2</a> <i>Yoganidra</i>, the sleep of <i>pralaya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_650"></a><a href="htg18.htm#fr_652">105:3</a> That He might be roused from his sleep and take part in the cosmic process. Sūta continues; "Having meditated upon Her who is <i>Māyā</i> and <i>Saguṇā</i>, and giver of liberation and <i>Nirguṇā</i>, I will tell you, O munis, the whole Purāṇa, which is the best and the most sacred <i>Śrīmadbhāgavata</i> of 18,000 Sanskrit Ślokas."</p>
