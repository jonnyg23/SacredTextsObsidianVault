 <p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 195</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">"MAY THE DEVI GRANT ME PARDON"</h3>  <h3 align="center">(DEVI APARĀDA KṢAMĀPANA STOTRA)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>ALAS! I know not either Thy mantra <a name="fr_1220"></a><a href="#fn_1214"><font size="1">1</font></a> or yantra, <a name="fr_1221"></a><a href="#fn_1215"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Nor how to welcome Thee, <a name="fr_1222"></a><a href="#fn_1216"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Or how to meditate upon, nor words of prayer to Thee,<br> Nor do I know Thy <i>mudrā</i>, <a name="fr_1223"></a><a href="#fn_1217"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 196</font></a></p>  
 <p>Or how to lay before Thee my griefs;<br> But this I know, O Mother!<br> That to follow Thee is to remove all my pain.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>By my ignorance of Thy commands.<br> By my poverty <a name="fr_1224"></a><a href="#fn_1218"><font size="1">1</font></a> and sloth,<br> I had not the power to do that which I should have done,<br> Hence my omission to worship Thy feet.<br> But, O Mother! auspicious Deliverer of all.<br> All this should be forgiven,<br> For a bad son may sometimes be born, but a bad mother never. <a name="fr_1225"></a><a href="#fn_1219"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Mother! Thou hast many worthy sons on earth,<br> But I, your son, am of no worth;<br> Yet it is not meet that Thou should’st abandon me,<br> For a bad son may sometimes be born, but a bad mother never.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>O Mother of the world, O Mother!<br> I have not worshipped Thy feet,<br> Nor have I given abundant wealth to Thee;<br> Yet the affection which Thou bestoweth on me is without compare,<br> For a bad son may sometimes be born, but a bad mother never.</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>I have abandoned the worship of other Devas</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 197</font></a></p>  
 <p>Because of the variety and confusion of the injunctions relating to their worship.<br> I am no more than eighty-five years of age, <a name="fr_1226"></a><a href="#fn_1220"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> If Thou will not bestow Thy kindness on me,<br> What shelter have I without Thy support,<br> O Mother of the big-bellied Deva! <a name="fr_1227"></a><a href="#fn_1221"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>Prayer, sweet as the sweet melon<br> Makes even a dog-eater <a name="fr_1228"></a><a href="#fn_1222"><font size="1">3</font></a> perfect;<br> Even a beggar walks without fear<br> With crores <a name="fr_1229"></a><a href="#fn_1223"><font size="1">4</font></a> of gold pieces.<br> O Aparṇā! <a name="fr_1230"></a><a href="#fn_1224"><font size="1">5</font></a> this is the fruit of Thy <i>mantra</i> entering their ears.<br> Who can say, O Mother!<br> The fruit which is born of the recitation <a name="fr_1231"></a><a href="#fn_1225"><font size="1">6</font></a> of Thy <i>mantra</i>?</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>He who is besmeared with the ashes of the funeral pyre, <a name="fr_1232"></a><a href="#fn_1226"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> He who swallowed poison, <a name="fr_1233"></a><a href="#fn_1227"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 198</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who is clothed with space, <a name="fr_1234"></a><a href="#fn_1228"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> With matted hair, garlanded with the Lord of Serpents,<br> The Lord of men, <a name="fr_1235"></a><a href="#fn_1229"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> The Lord of Ghosts <a name="fr_1236"></a><a href="#fn_1230"><font size="1">3</font></a> holding a skull in His hands.<br> Owes his great states as Lord of the World<br> To his acceptance of Thee as His Spouse, O Bhavānī! <a name="fr_1237"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>No desire have I for liberation, Nor have I desire for wealth, Nor wish for knowledge,<br> O Moon-faced One! neither have I wish for happiness!<br> But this only I beg of Thee,<br> That my life may pass in the recitation of these words:<br> Mridānī, <a name="fr_1238"></a><a href="#fn_1232"><font size="1">5</font></a> Rudrāṇī, <a name="fr_1239"></a><a href="#fn_1233"><font size="1">6</font></a> Śiva, Śivé, Bhavānī. <a name="fr_1240"></a><a href="#fn_1231"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>I have not according to the injunctions laid down therefor</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 199</font></a></p>  
 <p>Worshipped Thee with the various articles <a name="fr_1241"></a><a href="#fn_1234"><font size="1">1</font></a> of worship.<br> What is there which I have not wrongly done or omitted in my meditations on the Brahman?<br> O Dark One! <a name="fr_1242"></a><a href="#fn_1235"><font size="1">2</font></a> it will be but fitting on Thy part<br> If Thou bestoweth not kindness on me, helpless though I am.</p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>O Durgā, <a name="fr_1243"></a><a href="#fn_1236"><font size="1">3</font></a> our Lady! O Ocean of mercy!<br> When overwhelmed by danger <a name="fr_1244"></a><a href="#fn_1237"><font size="1">4</font></a> I remember Thee.<br> Think not, however, this to be deceit on my part,<br> For children afflicted by hunger and thirst ever remember their mother.</p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>O Mother of the world! <a name="fr_1245"></a><a href="#fn_1238"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 200</font></a></p>  
 <p>It is nothing wonderful if Thou art full of compassion for me;<br> A mother does not abandon her son<br> Even if he have an hundred faults.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>There is no such great sinner like me,<br> There is no such destroyer of sin as Thou.<br> Now, Mahādevī, you have heard what I have to say,<br> It remains for Thee to do what may seem fitting to Thee.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1214"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1220">195:1</a> <i>Śabda</i> is Brahman, and <i>mantra</i> the manifestation thereof. From <i>manana</i> arises realization of the monistic truth. <i>Man</i> of <i>mantra</i> comes from the first syllable of <i>manana</i>, and <i>tra</i> from <i>trāna</i>, or liberation from the bondage of the <i>samsāra</i>. That is called <i>mantra</i> which calls forth (<i>āmantrana</i>) the <i>caturvarga</i>, and which is the <i>svarūpa</i> of Devatā. (See <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i> and the Chapter on Mantra Tattva in <i>Principles of Tantra</i>.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1215"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1221">195:2</a> <i>Ibid</i>., The Tāntrik diagram which is worshipped in lieu of the image (<i>pratimā</i>). The Gāyatrī <i>Yantra</i> is <a href="htg00.htm#img_yantra">figured on the cover of this work</a>. <i>Mantra</i> is <i>Devatā</i>, and <i>yantra</i> is mantra, in that it is the body of the <i>Devatā</i>, who is <i>mantra</i>.</p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Yantram mantramayam proktam mantrātmā devataivahi<br> Dehātmanoryathā bhedo yantradevatayostathā</i> (Kaulavalīya Tantra).</dir>  
 <p>"The substance of <i>yantra</i> is <i>mantra</i>. <i>Devatā</i> is <i>mantra</i>. As there is a distinction between body and <i>ātmā</i>, so there is between <i>yantra</i> and <i>Devatā</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1216"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1222">195:3</a> By the <i>āvāhana mantra</i>, always said in worship of the <i>pratimā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1217"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1223">195:4</a> Ritual gesture, it being said; <i>Devānām modadā mudrā tasmāttām yatnātścaret</i> (see <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i>)"--"<i>Mudrā</i> is giver of pleasure to Devas, therefore it should be done with care."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1218"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1224">196:1</a> Want of means to perform the proper worship.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1219"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1225">196:2</a> A celebrated line; <i>Kuputtro jāyetā kvacidapi kumātā nabhavati</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1220"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1226">197:1</a> How is this stated if the hymn be the work of Śankarācārya, to whom it is attributed, for he is said to have died at the early age of thirty-two?</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1221"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1227">197:2</a> <i>Lambodarajananī</i>. The Deva is the elephant-headed Ganeśa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1222"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1228">197:3</a> That is, a low caste such as the <i>Caṇḍāla</i>, who eats any filth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1223"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1229">197:4</a> A crore is 100 lakhs; a lakh is 100,000.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1224"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1230">197:5</a> See <a href="htg29.htm#page_182">p. 182</a>, note 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1225"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1231">197:6</a> That is, <i>japa</i>, which is only recitation (in English) in its lowest form, the highest form being mental (<i>mānasa</i>) only. <i>Japa</i>, which is defined as <i>vidhānenā mantroccaraṇam</i>, is either <i>vācaka</i>, <i>upāmshu</i>, or <i>mānasā</i> (see Tantrasāra, 75 <i>et seq</i>.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1226"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1232">197:7</a> Śiva, to whom the rest of the attributes in this verse refer.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1227"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1233">197:8</a> See <a href="htg05.htm#page_16">p. 16</a>, note 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1228"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1234">198:1</a> Śiva is represented naked, as the Yogins, of whom He is the Master, ever are.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1229"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1235">198:2</a> <i>Paśupati</i>. <i>Paśu</i> literally means animal, but men are also pas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1230"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1236">198:3</a> <i>Bhūteśa</i>, Śiva is surrounded by hosts of spirits.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1231"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1240">198:4</a> See p. 180, note 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1232"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1238">198:5</a> Mrida is a title of the <i>sāttvika</i> Śiva. She is His Spouse.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1233"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1239">198:6</a> Devī is the Spouse of the countless Śivas called Rudras, in whom the <i>tamoguna</i> prevails. The dark (<i>tamas</i>) energy, called Raudrī, is said to be Cāmuṇḍā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1234"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1241">199:1</a> <i>Upacāra</i>. There are sixteen such, called the <i>shoḍaśa pūjā upacāra</i>--viz., (1) <i>āsanam</i> (seat); (2) <i>svāgatam</i> (welcome); (3) <i>pādyam</i> (water for feet) (4) <i>Argyam</i> (offering of water, <i>durva</i> grass, rice, etc.); (5) and (6) <i>ācamanīyam</i>; (water for sipping; twice); (7) <i>madhuparka</i> (honey, ghee, milk); (8) <i>snānam</i> (bathing); (9) <i>vasanam</i> (cloth); (10) <i>ābharanam</i> (jewels); (11) <i>gandha</i> (scent, sandal paste, etc.); (12) <i>puṣpa</i> (flowers); (13) <i>dūpa</i> (incense), (14) <i>dīpa</i> (lights); (15) <i>naivedyam</i> (food); (16) <i>vandanam</i> or <i>namaskāra</i> (prayer).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1235"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1242">199:2</a> <i>Śyāmā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1236"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1243">199:3</a> A great name of the Devī. The Devī Purāṇa says that She is so called because the Devas were delivered from fear in difficulty and battle; hence She is deliverer (Durgā). The Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇa and the Lakṣmī Tantra in the Pancarātra says: "In this place I shall kill a great Daitya (Titan) named Durgama. Hence my name shall be Durgā."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1237"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1244">199:4</a> The Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇa says: When Thou art remembered in times of difficulty, Thou takest away all fear of all things."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1238"></a><a href="htg31.htm#fr_1245">199:5</a> <i>Jagadambā</i>.</p>
