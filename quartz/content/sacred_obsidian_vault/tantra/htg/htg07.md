 <p><a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 22</font></a> <a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 23</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BHAIRAVĪ <a name="fr_79"></a><a href="#fn_79"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(BHAIRAVĪSTOTRA)</h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <a name="fr_80"></a><a href="#fn_80"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>THUS shall I pray to Thee, O Tripurā, <a name="fr_81"></a><a href="#fn_81"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> To attain the fruit of my desires,<br> In this hymn by which men attain that Lakṣmī, <a name="fr_82"></a><a href="#fn_82"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Who is worshipped by the Devas.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Origin of the world thou art,<br> Yet hast Thou Thyself no origin,<br> Though with hundreds of hymns.<br> Even Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Maheśvara <a name="fr_83"></a><a href="#fn_83"><font size="1">5</font></a> cannot know Thee. <a name="fr_84"></a><a href="#fn_84"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Therefore we worship Thy breasts, Mother of all <i>Śāstra</i>, <a name="fr_85"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Shining with fresh saffron.</p>

 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Tripurā, <a name="fr_86"></a><a href="#fn_86"><font size="1">1</font></a> we adore Thee,<br> Whose body shines with the splendour of a thousand risen suns,<br> Holding with two of thy hands a book <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_87"><font size="1">2</font></a> and rosary of <i>rudrākṣa</i> beads, <a name="fr_88"></a><a href="#fn_88"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> And with two others making the gestures<br> Which grant boons and dispel fear. <a name="fr_89"></a><a href="#fn_89"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> With three lotus eyes is Thy lotus face adorned.<br> Beauteous is Thy Neck with its necklace of large pearls. <a name="fr_90"></a><a href="#fn_90"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>O Mother, how can the ignorant, whose minds are restless with doubt and dispute,<br> Know Thy form ravishing with its vermilion, <a name="fr_91"></a><a href="#fn_91"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Stooping with the weight of Thy breasts, <a name="fr_92"></a><a href="#fn_92"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Accessible only by merit,<br> Acquired in previous birth?</p>

 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>O <i>Bhavānī</i>, <a name="fr_93"></a><a href="#fn_93"><font size="1">1</font></a> the <i>munis</i> <a name="fr_94"></a><a href="#fn_94"><font size="1">2</font></a> describe thee in physical form; <a name="fr_95"></a><a href="#fn_95"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> The <i>Śruti</i> speaks of Thee in subtle form;<br> Others call Thee presiding Deity of speech;<br> Others, again, as the root of the worlds.<br> But we think of Thee<br> As the untraversable ocean of mercy, and nothing else.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>Worshippers contemplate Thee in their heart<br> As three-eyed, adorned with the crescent moon,<br> White as the autumnal moon,<br> Whose substance is the fifty letters, <a name="fr_96"></a><a href="#fn_96"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Holding in Thy hands a book, a rosary, a jar of nectar, and making the <i>vyakhya mudrā</i>. <a name="fr_97"></a><a href="#fn_97"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>O Tripurā, Thou art Śambhu <a name="fr_98"></a><a href="#fn_98"><font size="1">1</font></a> united with Pārvatī. <a name="fr_99"></a><a href="#fn_99"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thou art now Viṣṇu embraced by Kamalā, <a name="fr_100"></a><a href="#fn_100"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> And now Brahmā born of the lotus. <a name="fr_101"></a><a href="#fn_101"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Thou art again the presiding Devī of speech,<br> And yet again art the energy of all these.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>I, having taken refuge with the four--<br> <i>Bhāvas</i>, <a name="fr_102"></a><a href="#fn_102"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>Parā</i>, and others <a name="fr_103"></a><a href="#fn_103"><font size="1">6</font></a> born of the <i>vāgbhava</i> (<i>bīja</i>), <a name="fr_104"></a><a href="#fn_104"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p>Shall never in my heart forget Thee, the supreme Devatā,<br> Whose substance is existence and intelligence, <a name="fr_105"></a><a href="#fn_105"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> And who expresseth by Thy throat and other organ<br> The bhāva appearing in the form of letters. <a name="fr_106"></a><a href="#fn_106"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>The blessed, having conquered the six enemies, <a name="fr_107"></a><a href="#fn_107"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> And drawing in their breath, <a name="fr_108"></a><a href="#fn_108"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> With steady mind fix their gaze on the tip of their nostrils,<br> And contemplate in their head Thy moon-crested form, <a name="fr_109"></a><a href="#fn_109"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Resplendent as the newly risen sun.</p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>The Vedas proclaim that Thou createth the world,<br> Having assumed the other half of the body of the enemy of Kāma. <a name="fr_110"></a><a href="#fn_110"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Verily is it true, O Daughter of the mountain and the only World-mother,<br> That had this not been so,<br> The multitude of worlds would never have been.</p>

 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>In company with the wives of the Kinnaras, <a name="fr_111"></a><a href="#fn_111"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> The Siddha women, <a name="fr_112"></a><a href="#fn_112"><font size="1">2</font></a> whose eyes are reddened by wine <a name="fr_113"></a><a href="#fn_113"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Having worshipped Thee with the flowers of celestial trees <a name="fr_114"></a><a href="#fn_114"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> In Thy <i>pītha</i> <a name="fr_115"></a><a href="#fn_115"><font size="1">5</font></a> in the caverns of the golden mountain, <a name="fr_116"></a><a href="#fn_116"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Sing Thy praises.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>I worship in my heart the Devī whose body is moist with nectar, <a name="fr_117"></a><a href="#fn_117"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Beauteous as the splendour of lightning,<br> Who, going from Her abode to that <a name="fr_118"></a><a href="#fn_118"><font size="1">8</font></a> of Śiva, <a name="fr_119"></a><a href="#fn_119"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Opens the lotuses on the beautiful way <a name="fr_120"></a><a href="#fn_120"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p>         of the <i>suṣuṁnā</i>. <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_121"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>O Tripurā, I take refuge at Thy lotus feet,<br> Worshipped by Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Maheśvara;<br> The abode of bliss, the source of the Vedas,<br> The origin of all prosperity;<br> Thou whose body is Intelligence itself. <a name="fr_122"></a><a href="#fn_122"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">14</p>  
 <p>I shall never forget Her who is the giver of happiness;<br> She it is, O Mother, who, in the form of the Moon,<br> Creates the world full of sounds and their meanings,<br> And again, by Her power in the form of the Sun,<br> She it is who maintains the world.<br> And She, again, it is who, in the form of Fire, destroys the whole universe at the end of the ages. <a name="fr_123"></a><a href="#fn_123"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">15</p>  
 <p>Men worship Thee under various names--<br> As Nārāyaṇa <a name="fr_124"></a><a href="#fn_124"><font size="1">1</font></a>; as She who saves from the ocean of Hell; <a name="fr_125"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> As Gaurī; <a name="fr_126"></a><a href="#fn_126"><font size="1">3</font></a> as the allayer of grief; <a name="fr_127"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">4</font></a> as Sarasvatī, <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> And as the three-eyed giver of knowledge. <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_129"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">16</p>  
 <p>O Mother of the world, such as worship Thee with twelve Verses of this hymn attain to Thee, and gain all powers <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_130"><font size="1">7</font></a> of speech and the supreme abode.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_94"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_94">25:2</a> Sages.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_95"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_95">25:3</a> There are three forms of the Devī--the gross or physical, with hands, feet, etc.; the subtle (<i>sūkṣma</i>), consisting of <i>mantra</i> and the supreme (<i>parā</i>), which is the real or own (<i>svarūpā</i>). The form of the Devī has both <i>prakāśa</i> and <i>vimarśa</i> aspects--that is real and secondary or manifested. Thus the Vāmakeśvara Tantra says: "The Devī Tripurā is Her real form. She who is of a red colour is the manifested one."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_96"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_96">25:4</a> Of the alphabet or <i>mātṛkā</i> (<i>panchāśadākṣaramayīm</i>). These letters stand for the <i>vṛtti</i> (functions and qualities of being). The Devī is thus <i>mātṛkāmayī</i>, or composed of <i>mātṛkā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_97"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_97">25:5</a> <i>Vyakhya</i>. according to the Śabda Kalpadruma = <i>vivaraṇam</i> (description), or <i>grantha</i> (book), and also commentary, but here denotes a <i>mudrā</i> of that name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_98"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_98">26:1</a> Śiva, one of the forms of the four <i>mahāpreta</i>, whose bīja is "<i>Hsau</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_99"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_99">26:2</a> The androgyne form, called <i>ardhanārīśvara</i>, half being Śiva and the other half Śakti. According to Hindu belief, the wife is the pure and sacred (<i>puṇyā</i>) half of her husband's body, and besides shares the purity and merits of her husband according to the common saying <i>Śarīrārdham smṛtā jāyā puṇyā puṇyāphale samā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_100"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_100">26:3</a> Lakṣmī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_101"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_101">26:4</a> That is, the power of destruction, maintenance, and creation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_102"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_102">26:5</a> The four <i>bhāvas</i> are states or conditions of Kuṇḍalinī appearing as sound and its subtle elements, and are <i>Parā</i>, whose abode (<i>sthāna</i>) is the <i>mūlādhāra</i>; <i>Paśyantī</i> in the <i>svādhiṣṭhāna</i>; <i>Madḥyamā</i> in the <i>anāhata</i>; and <i>Vaikharī</i> in the <i>viśuddha</i> issuing through <a href="errata.htm#3">the</a> throat (see next note). There appears, however, to be some difference as to the location of the second.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_103"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_103">26:6</a> <i>Parā</i> is the first condition of Kuṇḍalinī in the form of tāmasik sound in the <i>Mūlādhāra</i>; <i>Paśyantī</i> is the <i>bhāva</i> when Kuṇḍalinī, associated with <i>manas</i>, reaches the position variously stated as the <i>svādiṣṭhāna</i> or <i>manipūra</i>; <i>Madhyamā</i>, when it reaches the <i>anāhatacakra</i>, associated with <i>buddhi</i>; and <i>Vaikharī</i> is when Kuṇḍalinī issues through the throat in the form of the fifty letters. It is said that though there are thus four kinds of speech, the gross-minded do not understand the first three, and think speech to be <i>Vaikharī</i> alone.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_104"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_104">26:7</a> That is, <i>Āim</i> the <i>bīja</i> of Sarasvatī. The Devī is 'three syllabled' (<i>Tryakṣarī</i>)--that is, the <i>bīja</i> of the three divisions of the <a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a> <i>Panchadaśi</i>, <i>Vāgbhava</i>, <i>Kāmārāja</i> and <i>Śakti</i>. According to the Vāmakeśvara Tantra, Vāgiśvarī is the <i>jnānā śakti</i>, which is in the <i>vāgbhava</i> division, and confers salvation, the <i>kāmārāja</i> and <i>śakti</i> divisions being the <i>kriyā</i> and <i>ichchhā sākti</i> (see the Lalitā, verse 126).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_105"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_105">27:1</a> <i>Sacchinmayī</i>, the Brahman being <i>sat</i> and <i>chit</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_106"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_106">27:2</a> That is, they ultimately so appear, though previously existing as subtle elements of sound.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_107"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_107">27:3</a> The six sins: Lust (<i>kāma</i>), anger (<i>krodha</i>), greed (<i>lobha</i>), delusion (<i>moha</i>), pride (<i>mada</i>), envy (<i>mātsaryā</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_108"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_108">27:4</a> <i>Akunchya vayam</i> by <i>pūraka</i> of <i>prānāyāmā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_109"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_109">27:5</a> As Śakti of Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_110"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_110">27:6</a> Śiva. The Androgyne form <i>ardhanārīśvara</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_111"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_111">28:1</a> A lower order of Devas (<i>devayoni</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_112"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_112">28:2</a> Wives of the Siddhas, also celestial spirits (<i>devayoni</i>) inhabiting the atmospheric plane (<i>bhuvah</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_113"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_113">28:3</a> <i>Asvāditāsavarasāruna netrapadma</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_114"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_114">28:4</a> <i>Pādapa</i>, so called because the tree drinks by its roots. As to the celestial trees (see "Wave of Bliss" <i>post</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_115"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_115">28:5</a> Seat or shrine.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_116"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_116">28:6</a> Sumeru.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_117"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_117">28:7</a> For She as Kuṇḍalinī goes from the <i>mūlādhāra</i> to the <i>śivasthāna</i> in the <i>sahasrāra</i> and returns moist with the nectar of Her union with Him.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_118"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_118">28:8</a> <i>Rajādhani</i>. Literally capital city of Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_119"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_119">28:9</a> That is from the <i>mūlādhāra cakra</i> to the <i>śivasthāna</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_120"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_120">28:10</a> The <i>suṣuṁnā</i> is the central "nerve" (<i>nādī</i>), or, rather, channel of energy in the body in which the lotuses (<i>ṣatcakra</i>) are threaded with their heads normally downwards. As Kuṇḍalinī becomes stirred by the yoga process, She ascends from the <a name="page_29"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 29</font></a> <i>mūlādhāra</i> and enters the higher <i>cakra</i>. As She does so, the lotuses upturn and expand again, closing on her departure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_121"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_121">29:1</a> The text which has <i>sausuvartma kamalāni vikāshayantim</i>, is not, however, intelligible, and the metre is short. Possibly it is a misprint for <i>saumyang</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_122"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_122">29:2</a> That is, nothing but intelligence <i>caitanyamātra tanu</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_123"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_123">29:3</a> <i>Nādā</i>, as Rāghava Bhatta says, exists in the three states of <i>nibodhikā</i> or <i>bodhinī</i>, <i>nāda</i>, and in the form of <i>bindu</i>, according to the predominance of the <i>guṇa</i>. These three and the <i>śaktis</i>, <i>jnānā</i>, <i>kriyā</i> and <i>ichhā</i>, of which they are special manifestations, are said to be in the form of sun, moon, and fire respectively. The moon (<i>indu</i>) is <i>ichchhā</i> (will and desire), the eternal precursor of creation. <i>Kriyā</i> is like the sun, which makes all things visible. <i>Jnānā</i> is fire, as it burns up all actions (see Ṣatcakranirūpaṇā, verse 49, and Sāradā Tilaka, chap. i.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_124"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_124">30:1</a> As Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_125"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_125">30:2</a> <i>Narakārnatārinī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_126"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_126">30:3</a> Spouse of Śiva. The Devī Purāṇa says; "She who was burned by the fire of <i>yoga</i> was again born of Himālaya; as She has the colour of the conch, jasmine, and moon, she is called Gaurī." Her colour is golden. Śiva said to Pārvatī: "O Daughter of Himalaya, I am white as the moon and thou art dark. I am the sandal-tree, and thou art, as it were, a snake entwined round it." Pārvatī, taking umbrage at this remark upon Her dark complexion, went away to the forest, and there, by the performance of austerities, gained for herself a golden complexion beautiful as the sunlit sky.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_127"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_127">30:4</a> <i>Khedāshāmine</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_128"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_128">30:5</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_129"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_129">30:6</a> Devī of speech and learning.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_130"></a><a href="htg07.htm#fr_130">30:7</a> <i>Vāksiddhi</i> or <i>siddhi</i> of words.</p>
