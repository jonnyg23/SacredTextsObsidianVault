 <p><a name="page_91"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 91</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">TRIPUṬĀ <a name="fr_564"></a><a href="#fn_563"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(TRIPUṬĀSTOTRAM)</h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <a name="fr_565"></a><a href="#fn_564"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>I CONTEMPLATE the good Guru who is Light itself, <a name="fr_566"></a><a href="#fn_565"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Sitting with his Śakti <a name="fr_567"></a><a href="#fn_566"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> In the lotus of the head, <a name="fr_568"></a><a href="#fn_567"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Two-armed, gracious, very gracious,<br> Whose moon-like face is full of grace,<br> Making with his hands the gestures which grant boons and dispel fear. <a name="fr_569"></a><a href="#fn_568"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Such as recite <a name="fr_570"></a><a href="#fn_569"><font size="1">7</font></a> thy primordial golden <i>bīja</i> </p>

 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 92</font></a></p>  
 <p>ŚRĪM, <a name="fr_571"></a><a href="#fn_570"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Attain all prosperity and fortune.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Mother!<br> He who contemplates Thy second <i>bīja</i>,<br> Adorned by numbers of Devas,<br> "HRĪM," <a name="fr_572"></a><a href="#fn_571"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Gains all prosperity.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>The chiefs of men who meditate upon Thy <i>bīja</i>,<br> Lustrous as the sun,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_93"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 93</font></a></p>  
 <p>"<i>KLĪM</i>," <a name="fr_573"></a><a href="#fn_572"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Charm the three worlds,<br> And by recitation thereof become like unto Īśvara.</p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>O beloved of the enemy of Smara! <a name="fr_574"></a><a href="#fn_573"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Those who contemplate Thy body <a name="fr_575"></a><a href="#fn_574"><font size="1">3</font></a> thrice <a name="fr_576"></a><a href="#fn_575"><font size="1">4</font></a> and recite these three <i>bījas</i> <a name="fr_577"></a><a href="#fn_576"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Render their enemies speechless,<br> Lakṣmī shines in their house,<br> And they become the God of Love <a name="fr_578"></a><a href="#fn_577"><font size="1">6</font></a> to women.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>The presiding Devatā of Speech<br> Blesses their mouth with poetry and prose.<br> Harmful animals cause them no harm,<br> Even the Suras <a name="fr_579"></a><a href="#fn_578"><font size="1">7</font></a> salute them.</p>

 <p>Their feet are the head ornaments of kings, <a name="fr_580"></a><a href="#fn_579"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> The <i>siddhis</i> <a name="fr_581"></a><a href="#fn_580"><font size="1">2</font></a> are in their hands,<br> Malignant stars relinquish them.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Let the <i>Sādhaka</i> meditate upon an eight-petalled lotus <a name="fr_582"></a><a href="#fn_581"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Set upon a throne studded with various gems,<br> Placed upon an altar<br> Standing on the floor of a jewelled house<br> Amidst a forest of <i>Pārijāta</i> trees. <a name="fr_583"></a><a href="#fn_582"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>Let him then meditate upon two angles <a name="fr_584"></a><a href="#fn_583"><font size="1">5</font></a> in the lotus,<br> And the Devī Herself in the lotus as follows:<br> Her lustre is that of molten gold,<br> With earrings <a name="fr_585"></a><a href="#fn_584"><font size="1">6</font></a> on her ears,<br> Three-eyed, of beauteous throat,<br> Her face like the moon,<br> And bending from the weight of Her breasts. <a name="fr_586"></a><a href="#fn_585"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p>She holds in many arms, decked with diamonds and other gems,</p>

 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>Her lotus feet glitter with beautiful anklets, <a name="fr_592"></a><a href="#fn_591"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Crowned, adorned, and gracious,<br> Holding two white fly-whisks, <a name="fr_593"></a><a href="#fn_592"><font size="1">7</font></a> a mirror, jewel-case, <a name="fr_594"></a><a href="#fn_593"><font size="1">8</font></a> and a box filled with camphor. <a name="fr_595"></a><a href="#fn_594"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Creatrix of the three worlds,<br> Destructress of the pain of the world,<br> Destructress and ruler of the world,<br> Ever full of Bliss,</p>

 <p><a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 96</font></a></p>  
 <p>Half of the letter <i>Hā</i>; <a name="fr_596"></a><a href="#fn_595"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the nature of the three-fold <i>Bindu</i>, <a name="fr_597"></a><a href="#fn_596"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> The threefold <i>Śakti</i>, <a name="fr_598"></a><a href="#fn_597"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> It is Her I worship.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>The <i>Sadhaka</i> who, having thus for a long time contemplated Her<br> On a <i>yantra</i> <a name="fr_599"></a><a href="#fn_598"><font size="1">4</font></a> set before him,<br> And welcomed <a name="fr_600"></a><a href="#fn_599"><font size="1">5</font></a> her with great <a href="errata.htm#11">devotion</a>,<br> Worshipping Her with <i>Svayambhu</i> flower <a name="fr_601"></a><a href="#fn_600"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Attains, even though he be of the lowest <a name="fr_602"></a><a href="#fn_601"><font size="1">7</font></a> <i>siddhi</i> <a name="fr_603"></a><a href="#fn_602"><font size="1">8</font></a> in the <i>caturvarga</i>. <a name="fr_604"></a><a href="#fn_603"><font size="1">9</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>Whoever after having done worship <a name="fr_605"></a><a href="#fn_604"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Of Śrī, <a name="fr_606"></a><a href="#fn_605"><font size="1">2</font></a> Śrīpatī, <a name="fr_607"></a><a href="#fn_606"><font size="1">3</font></a> Pārvatī, <a name="fr_608"></a><a href="#fn_607"><font size="1">4</font></a> Īśvara, <a name="fr_609"></a><a href="#fn_608"><font size="1">5</font></a> Ratī, <a name="fr_610"></a><a href="#fn_609"><font size="1">6</font></a> and Kāmadeva, <a name="fr_611"></a><a href="#fn_610"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Together with the <i>Ṣādang</i>a Devatā <a name="fr_612"></a><a href="#fn_611"><font size="1">8</font></a> of the Devī,<br> Recites <a name="fr_613"></a><a href="#fn_612"><font size="1">9</font></a> the <i>mantra</i> on Thy <i>yantra</i>, <a name="fr_614"></a><a href="#fn_613"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> Becomes a King among men.</p>

 <p>Having worshipped the two nidhis, <a name="fr_615"></a><a href="#fn_614"><font size="1">11</font></a> Śankha and Padma,<br> On the two sides of the lotus,</p>

 <p>And the Mahīṣīs, <a name="fr_616"></a><a href="#fn_615"><font size="1">1</font></a> regents of the quarters, <a name="fr_617"></a><a href="#fn_616"><font size="1">2</font></a> and their weapons,<br> Attains, even though he be of the most vile, <a name="fr_618"></a><a href="#fn_617"><font size="1">3</font></a> the eight <i>siddhis</i> <a name="fr_619"></a><a href="#fn_618"><font size="1">4</font></a> of Śiva.</p>  
 <p align="center">15</p>  
 <p>Thou art the earth, <i>Vidhātrī</i>, <a name="fr_620"></a><a href="#fn_619"><font size="1">5</font></a> creatrix of the world; <a name="fr_621"></a><a href="#fn_620"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Thou art water, and in the form of Viṣṇu preserveth the world;<br> Thou art fire, and in the form of Rudra destroyeth the world;<br> Thou existeth in the form of <i>Aiśvarya</i>; <a name="fr_622"></a><a href="#fn_621"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Thou art the air of the world.</p>  
 <p align="center">16</p>  
 <p>Thou art the primeval <a name="fr_623"></a><a href="#fn_619"><font size="1">5</font></a> and auspicious one, <a name="fr_624"></a><a href="#fn_622"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Spouse of Śambhu, <a name="fr_625"></a><a href="#fn_623"><font size="1">9</font></a> refuge (of Thy worshippers).</p>

 <p align="center">17</p>  
 <p>Thou hast humbled the pride even of the <i>Rṣis</i><br> By plunging them into the ocean of the world.<br> Thou art intelligence and bliss and light itself. <a name="fr_628"></a><a href="#fn_626"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> How, then, can I know thee?<br> O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.</p>  
 <p align="center">18</p>  
 <p>O Bhavānī! even an ignorant man <a name="fr_629"></a><a href="#fn_627"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Who, meditating on Thy form, recites <a name="fr_630"></a><a href="#fn_628"><font size="1">5</font></a> Thy <i>mantra</i> a <i>lakh</i> of times<br> Acquires all poetic power,<br> And those things in the three worlds which are most difficult of attainment.<br> O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.</p>  
 <p align="center">19</p>  
 <p>Thou art that which supports <a name="fr_631"></a><a href="#fn_629"><font size="1">6</font></a> and that which is supported. <a name="fr_632"></a><a href="#fn_630"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Thou pervadeth the world,</p>

 <p align="center">20</p>  
 <p>Thou art the atom <a name="fr_636"></a><a href="#fn_634"><font size="1">4</font></a> and ever-pervading. <a name="fr_637"></a><a href="#fn_635"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Thou art the whole universe.<br> No praise of Thee is sufficient.<br> Yet Thy qualities prompt me to sing Thy praise.<br> O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.</p>  
 <p align="center">21</p>  
 <p>To him who reads and recites <a name="fr_638"></a><a href="#fn_636"><font size="1">6</font></a> at morn, noon, and evening<br> This most secret hymn,<br> There is nothing impossible in the three worlds, Such an one attains Thy nature. <a name="fr_639"></a><a href="#fn_637"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_563"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_564">91:1</a> Tripuṭā and Tripurā are separate Devīs, but the former is <i>antargatā</i> of Tripurā--that is, forms part, is included in, and a particular manifestation of Tripurā. In the same way the Devīs Ekajaṭa, Nīlasarasvatī, Ugratārā, Mahogrā, are each <i>antargatā</i> of Tārā.</p>

 <p><a name="fn_570"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_571">92:1</a> As is frequently the case in Tāntrik works, the <i>mantra</i> is not given in the text, but must be spelt out. Thus the Sanskrit is <i>vakamvahnisamstham trimurtyā prajuṣtam saśānkenayuktam</i>--that is, as "<i>vakam</i> with <i>vahni</i> attended by <i>trimurti</i>, combined with <i>śaśāngka</i>." <i>Vakam</i> = "Ś" (<i>tālavya</i>). <i>Vahni</i> is the "fire." = "r," <i>trimūrti</i> = the long vowel ī, and <i>śaśāngka</i>, the moon in "whose lap is the hare," or "man in the moon" = "m" (<i>anusvāra</i>). Ś + r + i + m = <i>śrīm</i> the <i>Lakshmībīja</i> (see the Ādyakālīstotra of Mahānirvāṇa Tantra).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_571"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_572">92:2</a> Literally, <i>Nabhovahni</i> (not <i>vāyu</i>, as the text has it, for the <i>vāyu bīja</i> is <i>yam</i>); <i>miśram</i> (not <i>mitram</i> as Prasanna Kumāra Shāstrī’s edition has it); <i>tatovāmanetram sudhā dhāmavimbam niyojyaikāvaktram</i>--that is, <i>nabhas</i> combined with <i>vahni</i>, and then <i>vāmanetra</i> and the receptacle of nectar (the moon) applicable in the case of Ekavaktra. <i>Nabhas</i> = "<i>ha</i>" or "<i>bha</i>" (here the former), <i>vahni</i> = "<i>ra</i>," <i>vāmanetra</i> = long <i>ī</i>, and the moon is <i>anusvāra</i> ("m"), H + r + ī + m = <i>Hrīm</i>, known as the <i>māyā bīja</i>. Then the <i>śloka</i> more clearly points to the <i>bīja</i> meant by saying it is that applicable to Ekavaktra. The latter is the Bhairava of Bagalāmukhī, whose <i>bīja</i> is also <i>Hrīm</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_572"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_573">93:1</a> Literally, <i>Virinchim kṣitistham tatovāmanetram vidhum nādayuktam</i>--that is, <i>virinchi</i>, <i>kṣiti</i> and <i>vāmanetra</i>, together with moon combined with <i>nāda</i>. <i>Virinchi</i> = "<i>ka</i>", <i>kṣiti</i> = "La," <i>vāmanetra</i> =long <i>ī</i>, the moon = "m" (<i>anusvāra</i>). K + l + ī + m = <i>Klīm</i>. The three elements of the mantra are given--viz., <i>Śrīm</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Klīm</i>--but the actual <i>bīja</i> of Tripuṭā is <i>Śrīm</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Klīm</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>, <i>Śrīm</i>, <i>Klīm</i>, <i>Klīm</i>, <i>Śrīm</i>, <i>Hrīm</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_573"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_574">93:2</a> The Deva of love (Kāma), of whom Śiva is described as the enemy, for he burnt him with the fire of his eye (see <a href="htg08.htm#page_40">p. 40</a>, note 1).</p>

 <p><a name="fn_586"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_587">95:1</a> <i>Pāśakam</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_587"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_588">95:2</a> <i>Angkuśa</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_588"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_589">95:3</a> <i>Puṣpavānām</i> (see Comm. Lalitā, verse 2) According to the <i>Yoginihṛdaya</i>, the noose is <i>Ichchā</i>; the goad, <i>jnāna</i>; and the bow and arrows the <i>kṛyā śaktis</i> respectively.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_589"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_590">95:4</a> <i>Sumadhyām</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_590"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_591">95:5</a> She wears a <i>candrahāra</i>, so called because it has a moonlike ornament in its centre.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_591"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_592">95:6</a> <i>Tulākota</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_592"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_593">95:7</a> <i>Cāmara</i> or chowrie, the bushy tail of the <i>Cāmara</i> (the yak; <i>bos grunniens</i>), used as a fly-whisk or fan, an insignia of royalty, and also used as a streamer on the heads of horses.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_593"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_594">95:8</a> <i>Karanda</i>--<i>alangkāra pātra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_594"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_595">95:9</a> <i>Samudgam</i>, translated in the Bengali as a <i>pān</i>-box with camphor, which is put into <i>pān</i> (betel).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_595"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_596">96:1</a> <i>Hakārārddhavarṇām</i>--that is, She is <i>Kāmakalā-svarūpā</i> (see Ādyākālīstotram, p. 43, and the <i>Kāmakalāvilāsa</i>, cited in the Lalitā sahasranāma, verse 73.) Another name for <i>Kāmakalāsvarūpā</i> is <i>Kāmapurasvarūpā</i>. The half <i>Ha</i> resembles a <i>bindu</i>, of which there are three--viz., the bindu at the base of the triangle from which the <i>A-ka-tha trikoṇa</i> emanated and the <i>visargah</i> above. See Kālīcarana's commentary on the <i>Pādukāpancakam</i>, in <i>The Serpent Power</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_596"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_597">96:2</a> <i>Tribindusvarūpā</i>--there are three such in Kāmakalā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_597"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_598">96:3</a> <i>Jnāna</i>, <i>ichchhā</i>, and <i>kṛyā śaktis</i> of the Devī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_598"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_599">96:4</a> Diagram used in Tāntrik worship (see <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i>.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_599"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_600">96:5</a> With the <i>āvāhana mantra</i>--viz., <i>iha āgacha iha āgacha, iha tishtha, iha tishtha atra adishthānam kuru mama pujām, grihāna</i>. ("Come here, come here! Stay here, stay here! accept my worship").</p>
 <p><a name="fn_600"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_601">96:6</a> See Mātṛkābheda Tantra. The word <i>puṣpa</i> (flower) has here a technical sense. <i>Puṣpaśabdena atra riturucyate</i>. <i>Mātṛkābhedatantra pramānānusārena anurāyāh kanyāyāh prathama eva riluratrā uccyate</i>. <i>Tantrāntaretu vivāhitāyāh eva bālāyāh rituratra vivakṣitah</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_601"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_602">96:7</a> <i>Pāmara</i>, one who is low or vile, a very contumelious term.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_602"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_603">96:8</a> Success, perfection, fruition.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_603"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_604">96:9</a> That is, <i>Dharma</i> (religion, piety), <i>artha</i> (wealth), <i>Kāma</i> (desire and its fulfilment), and <i>Mokṣa</i> (liberation). (See <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_604"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_605">97:1</a> Pūjā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_605"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_606">97:2</a> Lakṣmī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_606"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_607">97:3</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_607"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_608">97:4</a> Devī as daughter of Himavat.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_608"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_609">97:5</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_609"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_610">97:6</a> Spouse of Kāma, the God of Love.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_610"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_611">97:7</a> The God of Love.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_611"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_612">97:8</a> That is, the six <i>āvarana</i> or attendant Devatās on the Devī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_612"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_613">97:9</a> Makes <i>japa</i> of.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_613"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_614">97:10</a> See <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_614"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_615">97:11</a> There are eight gems or treasures (<i>nidhi</i>) of Kubera--viz., <i>padma</i>, <i>mahāpadma</i>, <i>makara</i>, <i>kachchapa</i>, <i>mukunda</i>, <i>nīla</i>, <i>nanda</i>, <i>śankha</i>. The Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇa gives the meaning of <i>nidhi</i> in the <a href="errata.htm#12">following</a> śloka ("Lakṣmī is the presiding <i>Devatā</i> of the <i>vidyā</i>, called <i>Padminī</i>. The <i>nidhis</i> are Her supporters. Listen while I speak of them"):</p>  
 <p></p><dir><i>Padminināma yā vidyā,<br> Lakṣmī stadadhi devatā,<br> Tadādhārāsca nidhaya,<br> Stan me nivadatah śrinu</i>.</dir>

 <p><a name="fn_620"></a><a href="htg16.htm#fr_621">98:6</a> The six <i>aiśvarya</i> are <i>Śrī</i> (beauty and auspiciousness), <i>Vīrya</i> (power), <i>jñāna</i> (Wisdom), <i>Vairāgya</i> (dispassion), <i>Kīrti</i> (glory) and <i>Māhātmya</i> (greatness). Bhagavān is He who is possessed of these six <i>aiśvarya</i>. All these are in the Devī who is hence called Bhagavatī (see Devī Bhāg. Pr., Saktirahasya; Bhāskararāya, <i>op. cit.</i>, verse 65), and as here, <i>Aiśvarya rūpā</i>.</p>
