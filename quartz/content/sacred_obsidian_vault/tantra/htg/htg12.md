 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 62</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MAHIṢĀMARDINĪ <a name="fr_349"></a><a href="#fn_349"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(MAHIṢĀMARDINĪSTOTRA)</h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <a name="fr_350"></a><a href="#fn_350"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O CANDĪ! <a name="fr_351"></a><a href="#fn_351"><font size="1">3</font></a> wander in my heart,<br> By whom the act of formidable Asura <a name="fr_352"></a><a href="#fn_352"><font size="1">4</font></a> was shattered,<br> Destroy the calamities which deeply pierce me,<br> Arising from the mass of malice and fears (which assail me),<br> So that, free from danger,<br> And protected by the lotus cluster of Thy feet,<br> My swan-like <a name="fr_353"></a><a href="#fn_353"><font size="1">5</font></a> mind may swim and rejoice in the ocean of bliss.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>What fear of his enemies has he who worships Thee?<br> The Devas who worship Thy feet stamping on beast and noose, <a name="fr_354"></a><a href="#fn_354"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Having abandoned the form of Narasimha, <a name="fr_355"></a><a href="#fn_355"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Whose towering mane reached the summit of Mount Sumeru,<br> And whose fingers are outstretched to tear (the breast of) Hiraṇyakaśipu, <a name="fr_356"></a><a href="#fn_356"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Now worship the lion, <a name="fr_357"></a><a href="#fn_357"><font size="1">4</font></a> the enemy of the elephant. <a name="fr_358"></a><a href="#fn_358"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Candī! when the syllables, the letters of which speak of Thee,<br> Reach the ear, then Brahmā and other Devas<br> Sing the truth, touching Puruṣa and Prakṛti. <a name="fr_359"></a><a href="#fn_359"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>O Devī! be to-day gracious to me,<br> Devoted as I am to the kissing of Thy beautiful lotus feet,<br> The one and only glittering abode of the essence of the nectar of all Devatās.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>If, because of my following your way of Kula, <a name="fr_360"></a><a href="#fn_360"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> I suffer reproach better is it that I shall thus be without fame.<br> Let me not have that which comes of the worship of Keśava <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_361"><font size="1">2</font></a> and Kauśika; <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Rather, O Mother! let my heart rest in meditation on Thy lotus feet,<br> Worshipped by Brahmā, Hari, the enemy of Smara <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">4</font></a> and the enemy of the Daityas. <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>O Mother! if I be engaged in the rightful <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">6</font></a> contemplation of Thy lotus feet,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p>What matters it if I know not <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">7</font></a> other sacred places? <a name="fr_367"></a><a href="#fn_367"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> May Thy lotus feet be ever present to my mind--<br> Thy feet which are the wealth of our wounds! <a name="fr_368"></a><a href="#fn_368"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> O propitious Mother! do Thou forgive me.</p>  
 <p>6</p>  
 <p>Verily and without doubt, even the Lord of Bhūtas <a name="fr_369"></a><a href="#fn_369"><font size="1">3</font></a> would have perished, <a name="fr_370"></a><a href="#fn_370"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Maddened as He was with the joy of the embrace of (Thee who art) His own self, <a name="fr_371"></a><a href="#fn_371"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Had He not enjoyed the lotus fragrance of Thy feet,<br> Full of honey from which drop liquid sandal,<br> And the nectar, there haply fallen from the moon. <a name="fr_372"></a><a href="#fn_372"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>O Mother! let the stream of heavy showers of holy devotion <a href="errata.htm#7">towards</a> Thee<br> Be ever shed upon me,<br> Struggling and drowning, alas! as I am in the endless ocean of illusion, <a name="fr_373"></a><a href="#fn_373"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Without taste of the springing water of the Bliss of Brahman, <a name="fr_374"></a><a href="#fn_374"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Which dispels the weight of mental afflictions from numbers of Devas.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>May (Thy) glory, <a name="fr_375"></a><a href="#fn_375"><font size="1">3</font></a> dark as collyrium cloud, Be ever in my heart.<br> From its glittering lustre were born the three Devatās,<br> Who create, maintain, and destroy the world,<br> Whose substance is pure intelligence and bliss, <a name="fr_376"></a><a href="#fn_376"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Dispelling the darkness which overspreads the heart<br> By the (glory of the unnumbered)millions of their rays!</p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>May <a name="fr_377"></a><a href="#fn_377"><font size="1">5</font></a> Devī Mahiṣāmardinī, who hath power to destroy</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>The proud enemies of the Devas,<br> And is the slayer of many another demon,<br> Ever conquer!<br> She it was who, having severed the head of the Asura Mahiṣa,<br> Seized upon him who assumed the form of a buffalo by his magic art<br> Upon the field of battle,<br> Now bellowing, now running, now lowering his head downward,<br> Falling upon the battlefield, and then vanishing from it for a while.</p>  
 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>She kills the Asuras upon the battlefield.<br> Terrible it was, with the dancing of the weapons and streamers <a name="fr_378"></a><a href="#fn_378"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the enemy.<br> With a cloud of thrown discus and other missiles.<br> There the copper-coloured weapon <a name="fr_379"></a><a href="#fn_379"><font size="1">2</font></a> dashed and flashed from the enemy's arrows--<br> Enemies so stout, strong, and tall, proud of wealth and power,<br> The field of battle thus seemed to have been swept by a tempest,<br> Most hideous it was, thickly spread with limbs and dead bodies of Asuras,<br> In whose blood and flesh birds slaked their thirst and appeased their hunger.</p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Let the Sadhaka meditate upon Devī Mahiṣāmardinī.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p>Rushing now here, now there on the field of battle for the slaughter of the enemies,<br> Attended by eight companion Mātrikas, <a name="fr_380"></a><a href="#fn_380"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Ear-ringed with eight-petalled lotuses,<br> Within each petal of which are writ the eight syllables<br> <i>Mahiṣāmardinyai namah</i>. <a name="fr_381"></a><a href="#fn_381"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Formidable was that field with the tossing of the huge curved horns of Mahiṣa,<br> Deeply black, maddened, wandering to and fro, horribly roaring<br> Whose instant death was desired of the Devas.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>Let the Sādhaka meditate<br> Upon the auspicious black <i>Bhogavatī</i> <a name="fr_382"></a><a href="#fn_382"><font size="1">3</font></a> Mahiṣāmardinī,<br> Holding in Her hands discus, lance, axe, shield, arrow, bow, and trident,<br> Making the gesture <a name="fr_383"></a><a href="#fn_383"><font size="1">4</font></a> which dispels fear;<br> Her long, matted hair is like a bank of cloud,<br> Covering Her face most formidable,<br> Loudly screaming, now with peals of terrible laughter,<br> And then with Her threats greatly frightening the Daitya heroes.</p>  
 <p align="center">13</p>  
 <p>O Devī! such as in this manner<br> Meditate upon Thy faultless form,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>  
 <p>Worshipped by Indra and other Devas,<br> To them it is given to attack the cities of their enemies,<br> And, conquering their enemies, to gain a kingdom;<br> They, too, acquire nectar of the knowledge of poesy,<br> And power to arrest, banish and slay. <a name="fr_384"></a><a href="#fn_384"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">14</p>  
 <p>O Mother! salutation to Thee! May Thou conquer!<br> Whosoever, meditating upon Thy lotus feet,<br> Utters this Thy hymn,<br> In the palms of the hands of all such<br> Are forthwith wealth, fulfilment of desire, and liberation.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_349"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_349">62:1</a> A title of Durgā, Śakti of Śiva as the powerful victrix of demons. She is Mahiṣāmardinī, as the slayer of Mahiṣa. The Daitya Śumbha attacked Her in the form of a buffalo (<i>Mahiṣa</i>; see Candi).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_350"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_350">62:2</a> P. 574.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_351"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_351">62:3</a> A form of the Devī assumed for the destruction of the Daitya Canda, and who assisted in the destruction of the demon Raktabīja (see Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇa).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_352"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_352">62:4</a> Mahiṣa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_353"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_353">62:5</a> <i>Manohamsa</i>, the <i>hamsa</i>, is variously described as a swan, goose, and flamingo.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_354"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_354">63:1</a> The Devī is standing on Her lion (<i>v. post</i>) with the noose (<i>pāśa</i>) beneath Her feet.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_355"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_355">63:2</a> The Man-lion (<i>Avatāra</i>) of Viṣṇu, in which He destroyed the Daitya Hiraṇyakaśipu, father of His devotee Prahlāda.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_356"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_356">63:3</a> See last note. The <i>avatāra</i> is generally represented with the King of the Daityas across his knees, tearing asunder with his hands and claws the latter's belly.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_357"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_357">63:4</a> Which accompanies the Devī as Durgā. After the destruction of Hiraṇyakaśipu, Viṣṇu's wrath was not appeased. The world trembled, fearing what he might do. The Devas asked the help of Śiva, who assumed the Sharabha form--that of a lion with wings and eight feet--who tossed up Viṣṇu into the air and held him there until he had become powerless. The lion then went to the feet of Durgā, whom he accompanies.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_358"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_358">63:5</a> <i>Gaja</i>, the elephant form subsequently assumed by the Asura, Mahiṣa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_359"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_359">63:6</a> Śiva and Śakti, the "Male" and "Female" elements, from whose union springs the universe (see <i>Introduction to Tantra Śāstra</i> and <i>Principles of Tantra</i>.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_360"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_360">64:1</a> That is, <i>Kulācāra</i>, one, and the highest, of the divisions of Tāntrik worshippers often misunderstood, and therefore subject of reproach; and which is contrasted in the next line but one with the more popular and conventional worshipper of Keśava and Kauśika.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_361"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_361">64:2</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_362"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_362">64:3</a> An epithet both of Śiva and Indra, probably here the former.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_363"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_363">64:4</a> Smara, the God of Love; Śiva, who slew him, is his "enemy."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_364"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_364">64:5</a> <i>Daityāri</i>: usually an epithet of Śrī Kṛṣṇa, but as Hari has already been mentioned, possibly the reference may be to Indra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_365"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_365">64:6</a> i. e., orderly, according to the direction and sequence of the dhyāna or stotra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_366"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_366">65:7</a> Literally, "If I be deprived of."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_367"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_367">65:1</a> <i>Siddhāspada</i>, where the perfect (<i>siddha</i>) are, or where <i>Siddhi</i> (power and perfection) may be gained.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_368"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_368">65:2</a> That is, they are the healers of our pain.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_369"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_369">65:3</a> Śiva Bhūteśvara or Bhūtanātha. <i>Bhūta</i>, which in a general sense means "beings," specifically refers to the spirits and ghosts by whom Śiva is surrounded and of whom He is Master.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_370"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_370">65:4</a> It is by the Devī's aid that Śiva is Parameśvara, for without Śakti He is nothing, and without Her life-giving energy and support cannot exist. As the Kubjikā Tantra says: "Without their Śaktis the husbands are but <i>preta</i>" (inert corpses). So also the Jnānārnava: "O beloved, pure Sadāśiva without Śakti is without motion like a corpse, for without Śakti He can do nothing."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_371"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_371">65:5</a> <i>Svātmānam parirabhya</i>. Literally, having embraced Himself. The Devī is, however, in a dualistic sense, His sacred half, and in reality one with Him and His own self (see Mahānirvāṇa Tantra, chap. i.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_372"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_372">65:6</a> <i>Daivādvichyuta candra candanarasaprāgalbhya garbashravat</i>--"Haply" in the poetical sense, as the nectar should be in the moon, but it happens to be dropping from the sacred feet of the Devī. Moreover, the Parambindu, which is Śivaśakti, is in the crescent of Nirvāṇakalā, which is by Amākalā the sixteenth digit <a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a> of the moon-circle (<i>Candramaṇḍala</i>), whence flows the nectar which, as Ichchhā, is the eternal precursor of creation (see Commentary, verse 49, of the Ṣatcakranirūpaṇa in <i>The Serpent Power</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_373"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_373">66:1</a> <i>Viddhā</i>, not as the original has it, <i>Siddha</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_374"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_374">66:2</a> <i>Brahmānandasarābhiṣeka</i> in the original should be <i>Brahmānandarāsābhiṣeka</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_375"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_375">66:3</a> <i>Mahas</i>, not <i>maham</i>, as in the text.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_376"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_376">66:4</a> <i>Nirmalacidānandatrayamdaivatam</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_377"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_377">66:5</a> Verses 9 to 12 are a free rendering of a text which in parts is so corrupt as to be <a href="errata.htm#8">untranslatable</a> with accuracy.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_378"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_378">67:1</a> Chowrie.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_379"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_379">67:2</a> That is, fire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_380"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_380">68:1</a> The Devīs so called.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_381"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_381">68:2</a> Salutation to the Devī slayer of Mahiṣa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_382"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_382">68:3</a> For She has all powers.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_383"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_383">68:4</a> <a href="errata.htm#9">The</a> <i>abhaya</i><i> mudrā</i> (see <a href="htg08.htm#page_36">p. 36</a>, note 1).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_384"></a><a href="htg12.htm#fr_384">69:1</a> <i>Stambhanam</i>, <i>uchchātanam</i>, and <i>māraṇam</i>, three of the Tāntrik <i>Ṣatḳarma</i>.</p>
