 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 70</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ANNAPŪRṆA <a name="fr_385"></a><a href="#fn_385"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center">(ANNAPŪRṆĀSTOTRA)</h3>  <h3 align="center">FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <a name="fr_386"></a><a href="#fn_386"><font size="1">2</font></a></h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>SALUTATION to thee, O Devī!<br> Dispenser of blessings, beloved of Śankara, <a name="fr_387"></a><a href="#fn_387"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Dear to devotees,<br> Annapūrṇè.</p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Thou hast assumed form by <i>māyā</i> <a name="fr_388"></a><a href="#fn_388"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Beloved of Śankara.<br> Salutation to Maheśvarī, <a name="fr_389"></a><a href="#fn_389"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee. <a name="fr_390"></a><a href="#fn_390"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>O Mahāmāyā! <a name="fr_391"></a><a href="#fn_391"><font size="1">1</font></a> beloved Spouse <a name="fr_392"></a><a href="#fn_392"><font size="1">2</font></a> of Hara, <a name="fr_393"></a><a href="#fn_393"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Giver of the fruit of desire,<br> Queen of <i>Suras</i>. <a name="fr_394"></a><a href="#fn_394"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>O Mahādevī with the lustre of a thousand rising suns,<br> Three eyed,<br> Crested with the crescent moon. <a name="fr_395"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>O Devī! clad in fine garment,<br> Ever giving rice, <a name="fr_396"></a><a href="#fn_396"><font size="1">6</font></a> Sinless One,<br> Who delights in the dance of Śiva.<br> Crested with the crescent moon. <a name="fr_397"></a><a href="#fn_395"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>O Devī! fulfiller of the desires of devotees, <a name="fr_398"></a><a href="#fn_397"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Destructress of worldly pain,<br> Bending under the weight of Thy breasts. <a name="fr_399"></a><a href="#fn_398"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

 <p>O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>Thou residest in the centre of the six-petalled lotus, <a name="fr_400"></a><a href="#fn_399"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> And art in the form of the six-fold <i>śakti</i>, <a name="fr_401"></a><a href="#fn_400"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thou art Brahmānī and all others, <a name="fr_402"></a><a href="#fn_401"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>O Devī! adorned with crescent moon,<br> All empires <a name="fr_403"></a><a href="#fn_402"><font size="1">4</font></a> are Thy gifts,<br> Giver of delight to Sarva <a name="fr_404"></a><a href="#fn_403"><font size="1">5</font></a>,<br> O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee.</p>  
 <p align="center">9</p>  
 <p>Thy lotus feet are worshipped by Indra and other <i>Devās</i>;<br> Thou assumest the form of Rudra and other <i>Devas</i>,<br> Giver of wealth.<br> O Annapūrṇé! obeisance to Thee.</p>

 <p align="center">10</p>  
 <p>Whoever <a name="fr_405"></a><a href="#fn_404"><font size="1">1</font></a> at time of worship<br> Devoutly reads this hymn,<br> In his house Lakṣmī <a name="fr_406"></a><a href="#fn_405"><font size="1">2</font></a> ever abides;<br> True is this and without doubt.</p>  
 <p align="center">11</p>  
 <p>Whoever having recited <a name="fr_407"></a><a href="#fn_406"><font size="1">3</font></a> the <i>mantra</i> daily,<br> Reads this hymn at dawn of day,<br> Obtains wealth of rice<br> And prosperity.</p>  
 <p align="center">12</p>  
 <p>Not to all and any should this hymn be revealed,<br> For be it made known to one who is unworthy,<br> Then ills fall upon him,<br> Therefore should it be carefully concealed.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_385"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_385">70:1</a> A name of the Devī as She who bestows food, whose sincere devotee will never want rice. In one hand She holds a rice-bowl, and in another a spoon for stirring the boiled rice.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_386"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_386">70:2</a> P. 570.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_387"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_387">70:3</a> Śivé.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_388"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_388">70:4</a> All forms of the Devī, as also the forms of all <i>Devatā</i>, whether in the strict sense <i>avatāra</i> or not, are māyik, but to the worshipper none the less real.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_389"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_389">70:5</a> Feminine of Maheśvara, a title of Śiva as great Lord and Ruler of the worlds.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_390"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_390">70:6</a> <i>Annapūrṇé namostute</i>--the refrain.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_391"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_391">71:1</a> The Devī as She who yields and is yet Herself unaffected by <i>māyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_392"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_392">71:2</a> <i>Dharmaputī</i>--that is, a wife married with religious rites. There are other forms of marriage.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_393"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_393">71:3</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_394"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_394">71:4</a> <i>Devas</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_395"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_397">71:5</a> As is Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_396"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_396">71:6</a> <i>Annadānaratā</i>, or food generally.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_397"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_398">71:7</a> <i>Sādhakas</i>, those who practise <i>sādhanam</i> (see <i>Introduction To Tantra Śāstra</i>)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_398"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_399">71:8</a> <i>Kucabhārānate</i> (see Introduction).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_399"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_400">72:1</a> That is, the <i>svādhishṭhāna padma</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_400"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_401">72:2</a> <i>Sharangayuvatīmaye</i>, which equals <i>sharangaśaktisvarūpe</i>. The sixfold <i>śaktis</i> are: <i>Hṛdayānga śakti</i>, <i>śirongga śakti</i>, <i>śikhānga śakti</i>, <i>netrānga śakti</i>, <i>avachānga śakti</i>, and <i>astrānga śakti</i> which refer to the <i>Tāntrika nyāsa</i>, done on the heart, head, crownlock, eyes, the body, and the concluding gesture with the palms of the hands, accompanied by the <i>astra bīja</i> or "<i>phat</i>."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_401"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_402">72:3</a> That is, Indrāṇi, Kaumāri, and other Mātrikas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_402"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_403">72:4</a> <i>Sāmrājya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_403"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_404">72:5</a> <i>Sarvānandakare</i>. Sarva is one of the eight forms (<i>aṣṭamūrti</i>) of Śiva; or it may literally mean "giver of delight to all."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_404"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_405">73:1</a> This is the <i>phala</i> (fruit or result) portion of the <i>stotra</i>. All devotional works contain a <i>phala</i> chapter or verse, which states the result or reward (<i>phala</i>) to be obtained by their perusal, recitation, or hearing. If any worshipper invokes Devī by any particular name, such as Armada, he obtains the corresponding fruit. So the Sūta Samhitā (iv, 33, 29, 30) says: "All names arc attributed by His own <i>māyā</i> to Brahman, yet some apply specially by the wish of Śiva Himself. O sages! by the repetition of such names one becomes the supreme Lord Himself and before His lotus face Śadāśiva dances with His Spouse" (see Bhāskararāya Commentary, Introduction to second Chapter of the Lalitāsahasranāma).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_405"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_406">73:2</a> Devī of prosperity and wealth.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_406"></a><a href="htg13.htm#fr_407">73:3</a> Literally, "made <i>japa</i> of"</p>
