 <p><a name="page_154"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 154</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ĀRYĀ <a name="fr_934"></a><a href="#fn_928"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>  <h3 align="center"><a href="errata.htm#16">FROM</a> THE HARIVAMŚĀ</h3>  
 <p>THOU art liberation, <a name="fr_935"></a><a href="#fn_929"><font size="1">2</font></a> prosperity, life, <a name="fr_936"></a><a href="#fn_930"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Fame, modesty, and learning, reverence and intelligence,<br> Twilight, <a name="fr_937"></a><a href="#fn_931"><font size="1">4</font></a> night, lustrous day,<br> Sleep and the night of death, <a name="fr_938"></a><a href="#fn_932"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Āryā, Kātyāyanī, Kauśikī, <a name="fr_939"></a><a href="#fn_933"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Observant of <i>brahmacarya</i>, <a name="fr_940"></a><a href="#fn_934"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Mother of the leader of the celestial hosts, <a name="fr_941"></a><a href="#fn_935"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Formidable one, <a name="fr_942"></a><a href="#fn_936"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> She who undergoes great austerities <a name="fr_943"></a><a href="#fn_937"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p>Jaya and Vijayā, <a name="fr_944"></a><a href="#fn_938"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Contentment, nourishment, forgiveness, mercy, eldest sister of Yama, <a name="fr_945"></a><a href="#fn_939"><font size="1">2</font></a> clad in blue silken raiment,<br> Of various form, <a name="fr_946"></a><a href="#fn_940"><font size="1">3</font></a> without form, having many forms. <a name="fr_947"></a><a href="#fn_941"><font size="1">4</font></a> With red, half-opened eyes. <a name="fr_948"></a><a href="#fn_942"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Large-eyed protectress of Thy votaries.<br> O Goddess! Thou resideth on the peaks of fearful mountains, by rivers, and in caves, forests, and groves.<br> Greatly worshipped by the Śavara, Varvara, and Pulinda tribesmen, <a name="fr_949"></a><a href="#fn_943"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Thou traverseth in all directions of the world<br> With peacock-feathered flags.<br> Thou livest on the Vindhya mountain,<br> Surrounded by fowls, goats, sheep, lions, and tigers,<br> Amidst the constant ringing of bells. <a name="fr_950"></a><a href="#fn_944"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Thou holdest the trident and spear. <a name="fr_951"></a><a href="#fn_945"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

 <p>Sun and moon are Thy banners.<br> Thou art the ninth day of the dark half of the month,<br> And the eleventh day of the light half thereof. <a name="fr_952"></a><a href="#fn_946"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Baladeva's <a name="fr_953"></a><a href="#fn_947"><font size="1">2</font></a> sister art Thou, glorious one, <a name="fr_954"></a><a href="#fn_948"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Fond of warring <a name="fr_955"></a><a href="#fn_949"><font size="1">4</font></a> (with demons),<br> Abode of all creatures.<br> Thou art death, <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_950"><font size="1">5</font></a> and the supreme end <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_951"><font size="1">6</font></a> of men,<br> Daughter of the cowherd Nanda, <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Unconquered,<br> Wearing bark and good cloth;<br> Raudrī, <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_953"><font size="1">8</font></a> twilight, <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_954"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> With dishevelled hair, <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_955"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> And who art death,<br> Fond art Thou of offerings of wine and flesh. <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> Thou art Lakṣmī, <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">12</font></a><br> And assumest the form of Alakṣmī <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">13</font></a> for the destruction of Dānavas, <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">14</font></a></p>

 <p>Thou art Sāvitri <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the Vedas,<br> Mother of Mantras. <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Thou art the <i>Dakṣinā</i> <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">3</font></a> of the <i>ṛtvik</i>, <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">4</font></a> and art in the altars of sacrificial rites,<br> And the religious sense <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">5</font></a> of <i>Ṛṣis</i>.<br> Thou art Aditi of Devas. <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Plough of cultivators, earth of all creatures,<br> The success of merchants who fare in big ships, <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> The coast of ocean,<br> And foremost Yakṣi of the Yakṣas, <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Surasā of Nāgās, <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Virginity <a name="fr_975"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">10</font></a> of maidens and good fortune of women,<br> Knower of the knowledge of Brahman, <a name="fr_976"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> Initiation and supreme beauty,<br> Lustre of light, Rohinī <a name="fr_977"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">12</font></a> of planets.<br> Lakshmī, most successful art Thou in courts and fortresses,<br> In the confluence of rivers and in the full moon.<br> Thou art called Krittivāsa. <a name="fr_978"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>

 <p>Thou art Sarasvatī in the works of Vālmīki, <a name="fr_979"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Memory in those of Dvaipāyana, <a name="fr_980"></a><a href="#fn_974"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Religious sense of <i>Ṛṣis</i> <a name="fr_981"></a><a href="#fn_975"><font size="1">3</font></a> and (perfect) mind of <i>Devas</i>. <a name="fr_982"></a><a href="#fn_976"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Thou art the Goddess of wine, <a name="fr_983"></a><a href="#fn_977"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Adored art Thou by Thy creatures for Thy deeds.<br> Thou art the charming look of Indra,<br> And art the thousand-eyed <a name="fr_984"></a><a href="#fn_978"><font size="1">6</font></a>,<br> Devī of ascetics,<br> <i>Āranī</i> <a name="fr_985"></a><a href="#fn_979"><font size="1">7</font></a> of Agnihotra Brāhmaṇas, <a name="fr_986"></a><a href="#fn_980"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Hunger of all creatures,<br> Who satisfieth those in heaven.<br> Thou art Svāhā, <a name="fr_987"></a><a href="#fn_981"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Contentment, patience,<br> Receptacle of the Vasus, <a name="fr_988"></a><a href="#fn_982"><font size="1">10</font></a> hope of men,<br> Contentment which comes of work fully done;</p>

 <p>All the quarters and their opposites, <a name="fr_989"></a><a href="#fn_983"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Flame of fire, lustrous Sakunī, <a name="fr_990"></a><a href="#fn_984"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Pūtanā, <a name="fr_991"></a><a href="#fn_985"><font size="1">3</font></a> the terrible Revatī, <a name="fr_992"></a><a href="#fn_986"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Overpowering sleep of all beings,<br> Warrior. <a name="fr_993"></a><a href="#fn_987"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Of learning Thou art, Brahmavidyā, <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_988"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> <i>Om</i> and <i>Vaṣat</i>. <a name="fr_995"></a><a href="#fn_989"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> The Ṛṣis know Thee as Pārvati amongst women.<br> As Prajāpati <a name="fr_996"></a><a href="#fn_990"><font size="1">8</font></a> has said, Thou art Arundhatī <a name="fr_997"></a><a href="#fn_991"><font size="1">9</font></a> amongst women, with but one husband. <a name="fr_998"></a><a href="#fn_992"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> The difference of disputants. <a name="fr_999"></a><a href="#fn_993"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> Famous also art Thou as Indrāṇī <a name="fr_1000"></a><a href="#fn_994"><font size="1">12</font></a><br> This universe, mobile and immobile, is permeated by Thee.<br> Without a doubt Thou art saviour in all battles.<br> Amidst fires and on the banks of rivers,<br> Amidst robbers, in forests and caverns,<br> When in prison or when assailed by enemies,<br> And in all times and places where life is in peril.</p>

 <p><a name="page_160"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 160</font></a></p>  
 <p>My heart, my reason, and mind are devoted to Thee.<br> Deliver me from all sins. Be gracious to me.<br> Whoever rising at dawn reads <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_995"><font size="1">1</font></a> for the space of three months<br> This sacred hymn to Devī compiled by Vyāsa,<br> Being himself pure and of controlled mind.<br> Obtains the desired fruit.<br> Whoever reads it for six months, to him also<br> The desired fruit is given. Such as read it for nine months obtain celestial vision, and he who reads it for one year gains all such success <a name="fr_1002"></a><a href="#fn_996"><font size="1">2</font></a> as man may desire.<br> O Devī! as was said by Dvaipāyana, Thou art the supreme divine Brahman.<br> Thou destroyest the bonds and the fearful destruction of men,<br> The loss of children and wealth, fear of death and disease.<br> Thou art in the form of desire, and dost grant the objects thereof.<br> Having deluded Kamsa, Thou enjoyest the whole world,<br> And I also shall live as a cowherd among kine;<br> To accomplish my work I shall become a cowherd of Kamsa. <a name="fr_1003"></a><a href="#fn_997"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_928"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_934">154:1</a> The sacred hymn as sung in ancient times by <i>Ṛṣis</i>, related in chap. lviii of the Harivamśa, a sequel of the Mahābhārata.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_929"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_935">154:2</a> <i>Siddhi</i> (success,) which here means the supreme <i>siddhi</i> or <i>mukti</i> (liberation).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_930"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_936">154:3</a> That is according to the commentator <i>jīvanam</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_931"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_937">154:4</a> <i>Sandhyā</i> or junction-time, morning and evening.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_932"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_938">154:5</a> <i>Kālarātri</i> which is <i>pralayarātri</i>, or the night of the dissolution of all things.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_933"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_939">154:6</a> Born in the race of the sage Kuśika.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_934"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_940">154:7</a> The virgin state, or the first of the <i>āśramas</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_935"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_941">154:8</a> Skanda, or Kārtikeya, son of Śiva and the Devī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_936"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_942">154:9</a> <i>Ugrachārī</i>, which should be ugrachārinī, but this is <i>Arsha</i> (composed by <i>Ṛṣis</i>), to whom the rules of grammar do not apply.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_937"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_943">154:10</a> The Devī practised great austerities to gain Śiva as Her husband.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_938"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_944">155:1</a> Companion Devatās of Durgā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_939"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_945">155:2</a> Mrityu, the God of Death.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_940"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_946">155:3</a> <i>Bahurūpā</i> (see the Lalitā, verse 155). The Devī Bhāg. Pr. says, "She is formless because She is supreme, She has many forms because of Her activity" (see also Devī and Vāmana Purāṇas): "She is also the <i>Śakti</i> of the countless Rudras." The term is much commented upon in the Purāṇas, Upapurāṇas, and Tantras.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_941"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_947">155:4</a> <i>Anekavidharūpinī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_942"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_948">155:5</a> <i>Virūpākṣī</i>. Śiva is also called <i>Virūpākṣa</i>. His eyes are, either owing to his state of <i>samādhi</i>, or consumption of <i>bhang</i>, pictured as in a vague, dreamy, half-open state.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_943"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_949">155:6</a> <i>Śavarair varvaraischaiva pulindaischa supūjitā</i>--a line worthy of remark, for these were savage and non-aryan tribes.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_944"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_950">155:7</a> During worship of the image the worshipper rings a bell. There was a constant ringing of bells.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_945"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_951">155:8</a> <i>Pattisha</i>, a kind of spear with a sharp edge.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_946"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_952">156:1</a> Auspicious days. On the ninth (<i>Navamī</i>) there is <i>Caṇḍīpātha</i> (reading of Caṇḍī), and on the eleventh (<i>Ekādaśī</i>) fasting.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_947"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_953">156:2</a> Kṛṣṇa's eldest brother.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_948"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_954">156:3</a> <i>Rajanīya</i> in text is said to be a wrong reading for <i>mahanīya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_949"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_955">156:4</a> <i>Kalahapriyā</i>. Literally, quarrelsome.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_950"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_956">156:5</a> <i>Niṣṭha</i>, which according to the commentator, here means <i>maraṇam</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_951"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_957">156:6</a> That is, <i>mukti</i> (Liberation).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_952"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_958">156:7</a> Foster-father of Kṛṣṇa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_953"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_959">156:8</a> The dark (<i>tāmasika</i>) energy, called Raudrī, is said to be Cāmuṇḍā. There are said to be nine crores of different Cāmuṇḍās. (see Bhāskararāya Comm., Lalitā, verse 155).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_954"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_960">156:9</a> <i>Sandhyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_955"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_961">156:10</a> Kālī and Tārā are always so represented (see Karpūrādistotra).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_956"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_962">156:11</a> <i>Surāmāmsabalipriyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_957"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_963">156:12</a> Devī of wealth and prosperity.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_958"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_964">156:13</a> Devī of misfortune and poverty.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_959"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_965">156:14</a> Sons of Danu, enemies of the Devas.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_960"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_966">157:1</a> The Gāyatrī <i>mantra</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_961"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_967">157:2</a> <i>Mantraganasya</i>, or, according to another reading, <i>bhūtaganasya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_962"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_968">157:3</a> The present offered to the officiating Brāhmaṇa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_963"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_969">157:4</a> Priest.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_964"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_970">157:5</a> <i>Dharmabuddhi</i>, a term difficult to translate. A man is said to have <i>dharmabuddhi</i> who has great respect for religion and duty.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_965"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_971">157:6</a> The Devas were children of Aditi, as the Daityas were of Diti.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_966"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_972">157:7</a> <i>Sāngyātrikānām</i> = <i>potavanijām</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_967"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_973">157:8</a> That is, She is the Mother of Kubera, the King of the Yakṣas, a class of <i>Devayoni</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_968"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_974">157:9</a> Mother of the serpent divinities (Nāgās).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_969"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_975">157:10</a> <i>Brahmacarya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_970"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_976">157:11</a> <i>Brahmavādinī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_971"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_977">157:12</a> The name of an asterism.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_972"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_978">157:13</a> The name of Śiva as clad in tiger-skin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_973"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_979">158:1</a> Author of the Rāmāyana. Tradition says that he obtained a boon from the Goddess of learning and composed that work.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_974"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_980">158:2</a> Kṛṣṇa Dvaipāyana, one of the Vyasas, arranger of the Purāṇas, etc., who is said to have had all such <i>śāstra</i> by heart.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_975"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_981">158:3</a> <i>Vide ante</i> (see <a href="#page_157">p. 157</a>, note 5).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_976"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_982">158:4</a> Mānasī, which the Commentator says = "<i>Satyasangkalpātmikā chetovritti</i>" that is, whose will and thought fully realizes itself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_977"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_983">158:5</a> <i>Surādevi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_978"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_984">158:6</a> Indra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_979"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_985">158:7</a> <i>Ārani</i> are the two sticks of <i>samid</i> wood used to kindle sacrificial fire.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_980"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_986">158:8</a> Brāhmaṇas who cherish fire in the house and perform <i>homa</i> thrice daily.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_981"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_987">158:9</a> Wife of Agni, the <i>mantra</i> used when making <i>homa</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_982"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_988">158:10</a> Of whom there are eight: Apa, Dḥruva, Soma, Dhara, Anila, Anala, Pratyusha, Prabhāsa (see Vishnupurāṇa, Book I., chap. xv.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_983"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_989">159:1</a> <i>e.g.</i>, north and south, east and west, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_984"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_990">159:2</a> A terrible spirit of that name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_985"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_991">159:3</a> A female demon who attempted to destroy, but who was destroyed by the infant Kṛṣṇa.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_986"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_992">159:4</a> Name of the twenty-seventh constellation, containing thirty-two stars.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_987"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_993">159:5</a> <i>Kṣatriyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_988"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_994">159:6</a> The science of Brahman.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_989"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_995">159:7</a> The <i>Mahāmantra</i> "<i>om</i>". <i>Vaṣat</i> is a <i>mantra</i>. As <i>Svāhā</i> is used with <i>homa</i>, so <i>srauṣat</i>, <i>vauṣat</i>, <i>vaṣat</i>, and <i>svadhā</i> are used in <i>pitṛkriyā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_990"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_996">159:8</a> Brahmā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_991"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_997">159:9</a> Wife of the sage Vaśiṣṭha, famous for her constancy and devotion.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_992"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_998">159:10</a> <i>Ekabhartrinām</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_993"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_999">159:11</a> <i>Bhedovivādashīlānām</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_994"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_1000">159:12</a> Spouse of Indra.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_995"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_1001">160:1</a> Here follows the <i>phala</i> portion. (see <a href="htg13.htm#page_73">p. 73</a>, note 1).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_996"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_1002">160:2</a> <i>Siddhi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_997"></a><a href="htg24.htm#fr_1003">160:3</a> The tyrant who sought to slay Kṛṣṇa. The Chapter concludes: "Having thus addressed the Devī, the Lord disappeared, and She, too, saluting Him, expressed Her consent by saying, 'So be it'."</p>
