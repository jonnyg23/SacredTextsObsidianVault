 <p><a name="page_161"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 161</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">DURGĀ <a name="fr_1004"></a><a href="#fn_998"><font size="1">1</font></a></h4>  <h4 align="center">FROM THE MAHĀBHĀRATA <a name="fr_1005"></a><a href="#fn_999"><font size="1">2</font></a></h4>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>I SALUTE Thee, leader of Yogis, <a name="fr_1006"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">3</font></a> one with the Brahman, <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> Dweller in the Mandāra forest.<br> Virgin, <a name="fr_1008"></a><a href="#fn_1002"><font size="1">5</font></a> Kālī, <a name="fr_1009"></a><a href="#fn_1003"><font size="1">6</font></a> Spouse of Kāpāla, <a name="fr_1010"></a><a href="#fn_1004"><font size="1">7</font></a> of tawny hue. <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1005"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Salutation to Thee, Bhadrakālī. <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1006"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Reverence to Thee, Mahākāli, <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">10</font></a></p>

 <p>Caṇḍī, <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">1</font></a> Fearless one. <a name="fr_1015"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">2</font></a> Salutation to Thee, Saviour <a name="fr_1016"></a><a href="#fn_1010"><font size="1">3</font></a> imbued with all good fortune. <a name="fr_1017"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>Of the race of Kata <a name="fr_1018"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">5</font></a> greatly worshipful,<br> Dreadful one, <a name="fr_1019"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">6</font></a> Giver of victory, <a name="fr_1020"></a><a href="#fn_1014"><font size="1">7</font></a> Victrix, <a name="fr_1021"></a><a href="#fn_1015"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Who holdeth a peacock's tail for Thy banner,<br> And art adorned with various jewels,<br> Bearing formidable spear, sword, and shield (made of skin).<br> Younger Sister of the chief of cowherds, <a name="fr_1022"></a><a href="#fn_1016"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Eldest one, <a name="fr_1023"></a><a href="#fn_1017"><font size="1">10</font></a> born in the family of the cowherd Nanda, <a name="fr_1024"></a><a href="#fn_1018"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> Delighting in the blood of Mahiṣa, <a name="fr_1025"></a><a href="#fn_1019"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>

 <p>Kauśikī, <a name="fr_1026"></a><a href="#fn_1020"><font size="1">1</font></a> wearing yellow garments.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>With auspicious smile,<br> Whose mouth devoured all demons, <a name="fr_1027"></a><a href="#fn_1021"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Salutation to Thee, delighter in battle.<br> Umā, <a name="fr_1028"></a><a href="#fn_1022"><font size="1">3</font></a> giver of <i>shāka</i>, <a name="fr_1029"></a><a href="#fn_1023"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> In the form of Maheśvara, <a name="fr_1030"></a><a href="#fn_1024"><font size="1">5</font></a> and in that of Vāsudeva, <a name="fr_1031"></a><a href="#fn_1025"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Destructress of Kaitabha, <a name="fr_1032"></a><a href="#fn_1026"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Golden-eyed, with half-opened eyes <a name="fr_1033"></a><a href="#fn_1027"><font size="1">8</font></a>, grey-eyed, <a name="fr_1034"></a><a href="#fn_1028"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Veda and <i>Śruti</i>, <a name="fr_1035"></a><a href="#fn_1029"><font size="1">10</font></a> and most sacred.<br> Propitious to Brāhmanas engaged in the sacrificial rites,<br> Thou art Jātaveda, <a name="fr_1036"></a><a href="#fn_1030"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> And art ever present in the sacred shrines <a name="fr_1037"></a><a href="#fn_1031"><font size="1">12</font></a> in the chief cities of Jambudvīpa. <a name="fr_1038"></a><a href="#fn_1032"><font size="1">13</font></a></p>

 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>Of Sciences Thou art the knowledge of Brahman,<br> Thou art the liberation of embodied beings, <a name="fr_1039"></a><a href="#fn_1033"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Mother of Skanda. <a name="fr_1040"></a><a href="#fn_1034"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> O Bhagavatī <a name="fr_1041"></a><a href="#fn_1035"><font size="1">3</font></a> Durgā! <a name="fr_1042"></a><a href="#fn_1036"><font size="1">4</font></a> Thou liveth in inaccessible regions--<br> <i>Svāhā</i>, <a name="fr_1043"></a><a href="#fn_1037"><font size="1">5</font></a> <i>Svadhā</i>, <a name="fr_1044"></a><a href="#fn_1038"><font size="1">6</font></a> <i>Kalā</i>, and <i>Kāṣṭḥā</i>, <a name="fr_1045"></a><a href="#fn_1039"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Sarasvatī, <a name="fr_1046"></a><a href="#fn_1040"><font size="1">8</font></a> Savitrī. <a name="fr_1047"></a><a href="#fn_1041"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Mother of Vedas and Vedānta <a name="fr_1048"></a><a href="#fn_1042"><font size="1">10</font></a> art Thou called.<br> I praise Thee from the pure depth of my heart.<br> By Thy favour let us be victorious in battle.<br> Ever dost Thou abide in inaccessible regions,<br> In places full of fear and difficulty;<br> In the houses of Thy devotees, and in Pātāla. <a name="fr_1049"></a><a href="#fn_1043"><font size="1">11</font></a><br> In battle Thou conquereth the Dānavas. <a name="fr_1050"></a><a href="#fn_1044"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>

 <p>Thou art drowsiness <a name="fr_1051"></a><a href="#fn_1045"><font size="1">1</font></a> and slumber. <a name="fr_1052"></a><a href="#fn_1046"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>Thou hast power to show wonderfully the world, <a name="fr_1053"></a><a href="#fn_1047"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Modesty, <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1048"><font size="1">4</font></a> and beauty. <a name="fr_1055"></a><a href="#fn_1049"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Cause of creation and destruction, <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Creatrix, <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">7</font></a> Mother, <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1052"><font size="1">8</font></a> contentment, nourishment, constancy,<br> Light, Supportress of the sun and moon,<br> Power <a name="fr_1059"></a><a href="#fn_1053"><font size="1">9</font></a> of Him who possesses power, <a name="fr_1060"></a><a href="#fn_1054"><font size="1">10</font></a><br> In ecstasy <a name="fr_1061"></a><a href="#fn_1055"><font size="1">11</font></a> Thou art perceived by Siddhas and Cāranas. <a name="fr_1062"></a><a href="#fn_1056"><font size="1">12</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_998"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1004">161:1</a> See <a href="htg20.htm#page_120">p. 120</a>, note 9; , note 1: <a href="htg15.htm#page_81">p. 81</a>, note 1.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_999"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1005">161:2</a> Bhīshma Parvan, s. 43 (see Muir, O. S. T. iv, 432).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1000"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1006">161:3</a> <i>Siddhasenānī</i>. The <i>siddhas</i> are here <i>yogis</i> and sages. Nīlakanṭha (cited <i>post</i> as N.), in his Commentary, says the term means: She who, as leader (literally, commander of an army), gives success in <i>yoga</i> and attainment of the supreme abode.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1001"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1007">161:4</a> <i>Āryye</i>. Literally, noble, but here means, as Nīlakantha says, <i>prapya-brahmasvarūpa</i>--the own form of the accessible Brahman, as distinguished from the <i>nirguṇa Brahman</i> beyond thought and speech.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1002"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1008">161:5</a> Kumārī. It also means (N.) that She is very young.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1003"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1009">161:6</a> See <a href="htg23.htm#page_152">p. 152</a>, note 3.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1004"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1010">161:7</a> Kāpālī, one of Her forms. Kāpāla is Rudrā, as leader of Kāpālas (Kāpālikas).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1005"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1011">161:8</a> <i>Kapilākṛṣṇapingalā</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1006"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1012">161:9</a> Auspicious Kālī, who gives prosperity to Her devotees.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1007"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1013">161:10</a> See <a href="htg23.htm#page_152">p. 152</a>, note 3. The great Kālī, Destructress in the form of death.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1008"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1014">162:1</a> Spouse of Caṇḍa, or Kālāntaka, or Yama.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1009"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1015">162:2</a> <i>Candā</i>--bold, daring, brave, courageous.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1010"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1016">162:3</a> <i>Tārinī</i>, for She delivers from calamity.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1011"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1017">162:4</a> <i>Varavarninī</i> (N.), not "beautiful coloured," as it has been translated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1012"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1018">162:5</a> <i>Kātyāyanī</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1013"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1019">162:6</a> <i>Karālī</i> = <i>krure</i> or cruel (to demons and other ill-doers). <i>Karālavadanā</i> (wide-opened mouth) is an epithet of Kālī. "Gaping-mouthed, terrible, four-armed, with dishevelled hair"--<i>Karālavadanām ghorām, chatur-bhujām</i>, as the Kālī <i>dhyāna</i> runs.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1014"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1020">162:7</a> <i>Vijaya</i>--that is, particular (<i>viśisṭ.a</i>) victory (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1015"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1021">162:8</a> <i>Jayā</i>. <i>Jayā</i> and <i>Vijayā</i> are also the names of two female attendants (<i>Sakhī</i>) of Durgā.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1016"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1022">162:9</a> Gopendra or Kṛṣṇa. In the Harivamśa and Caṇḍī it is said that with the view of defeating the designs of Kamsa in regard to the destruction of Devakī's offspring, Devī will be born as the ninth child of Yaśodā in the same night as Kṛṣṇa was born as the eight child of Devakī, when Kṛṣṇa would be carried to Yaśodā and She to Devakī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1017"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1023">162:10</a> <i>Jyeṣtha</i>--<i>śreṣthā</i>--superior to or best of all.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1018"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1024">162:11</a> In whose house Kṛṣṇa was reared.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1019"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1025">162:12</a> The Asura of that name (see Caṇḍī).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1020"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1026">163:1</a> A name of the Devī, as born in the race of the sage Kuśika.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1021"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1027">163:2</a> <i>Kokamukhe</i>--when in battle with Raktabīja.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1022"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1028">163:3</a> See Hymn to Annapūrṇa, <i>post</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1023"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1029">163:4</a> <i>Shākambarī</i>. <i>Shōka</i> is a vegetable food given by Devī at the time of famine (Caṇḍī).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1024"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1030">163:5</a> <i>Svetā</i> (N.), not white, as it has been translated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1025"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1031">163:6</a> <i>Kṛṣṇā</i> (N.), not black, as it has been translated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1026"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1032">163:7</a> A Daitya brother of Madhu (Caṇḍī).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1027"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1033">163:8</a> <i>Virūpākṣī</i> (see <a href="htg24.htm#page_155">p. 155</a>, note 5).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1028"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1034">163:9</a> <i>Dhūmrākṣī</i> (N.) says grey and green, like those of a cat.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1029"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1035">163:10</a> Here Upaniṣads.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1030"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1036">163:11</a> <i>Jātavedasī</i>. Jataveda is a name of Agni (Fire).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1031"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1037">163:12</a> <i>Chaityeshu</i>. Ordinarily this term is applied to the Buddhist shrine, of which it is commonly said: "One should not enter a Jaina's temple or Buddhist <i>chaitya</i>, even if pursued by an elephant" (<i>Hastinā tādyamānopi na gachchet jaina-mandiram</i> also <i>dhaitya mandiram</i>.) Here the term means <i>devatālaya</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1032"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1038">163:13</a> A Purāṇic island by that name, not as it has been translated; "Who dwellest continually near to mountain precipices and sepulchres."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1033"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1039">164:1</a> "The great sleep of embodied beings," according to the last translator: But <i>Mahānidrā</i> (great sleep) is here <i>mukti</i> (liberation), which is the result of the <i>Brahmavidyā</i>, spoken of in the preceding line.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1034"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1040">164:2</a> Kārtikeya. By this it is meant that She is <i>sarvadevatārūpā</i>, in the form of all Devas, of whom Skanda is selected as a type (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1035"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1041">164:3</a> See <a href="htg20.htm#page_120">p. 120</a>, note 5; <a href="htg16.htm#page_98">p. 98</a>, note 5.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1036"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1042">164:4</a> <i>Kāntāravāsinī</i> (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1037"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1043">164:5</a> Mantra used with <i>homa</i>, but here it means that all ritual acts are her embodiment (<i>Sarvakarmarūpā</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1038"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1044">164:6</a> <i>Mantra</i> used in <i>pitṛkriyā</i> (see last note).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1039"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1045">164:7</a> <i>Kalā</i> is a division of time--one minute forty-eight seconds, and <i>kāṣṭḥa</i> is one-thirtieth of that.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1040"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1046">164:8</a> Devī of speech and learning.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1041"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1047">164:9</a> She is <i>sarvavāng-māyā-rūpa</i> (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1042"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1048">164:10</a> End of the Vedas or Upaniṣad.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1043"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1049">164:11</a> The nether world.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1044"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1050">164:12</a> See <a href="htg24.htm#page_156">p. 156</a>, note 14.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1045"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1051">165:1</a> <i>Jambhane</i> = <i>tandrā</i> (N.), not "destroyer" as it has been translated.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1046"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1052">165:2</a> <i>Mohinī</i> = <i>Nidrā</i> (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1047"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1053">165:3</a> <i>Māyā</i> = <i>adbhutapradarśanām</i> (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1048"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1054">165:4</a> <i>Hrī</i> = <i>lajjā</i> representative of, and including all other actions of mind (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1049"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1055">165:5</a> <i>Śrī</i>, or prosperity, and other attributes of Lakṣmī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1050"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1056">165:6</a> <i>Sandhyā</i>. the intervening period when night is going and morn coming. and vice versa, applied here to similar junction times in the creation and dissolution of the world (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1051"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1057">165:7</a> <i>Sāvitri</i>. She who, by the lustre of Sūrya, reveals (N.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1052"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1058">165:8</a> Because, as a mother, She supports the world and all beings therein.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1053"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1059">165:9</a> <i>Aiśvary</i>a. The supreme faculties of omnipresence, omnipotence, etc.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1054"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1060">165:10</a> <i>Maheśvar</i>a. She is the greatest wealth of Brahman.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1055"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1061">165:11</a> <i>Sankya</i> or <i>Samādhi</i>, where light appears and the <i>ātman</i> is known (N).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1056"></a><a href="htg25.htm#fr_1062">165:12</a> Siddhas here mean those who are liberated whilst yet living (<i>jīvanmukta</i>), and Cāranas those who are <i>siddhas</i> from their birth.</p>
