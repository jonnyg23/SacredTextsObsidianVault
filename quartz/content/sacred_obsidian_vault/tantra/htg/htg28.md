 <p><a name="page_175"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 175</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GANGĀ</h3>  <h3 align="center">(GANGĀṢṬAKAM)</h3>  
 <p align="center">1</p>  
 <p>O BHAGAVATĪ GANGĀ! <a name="fr_1094"></a><a href="#fn_1088"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Thou art the playful garland on the head of Hara; <a name="fr_1095"></a><a href="#fn_1089"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> Such as but touch a drop of the spray of Thy waters<br> Recline on the lap of the fan-holding women of the city of the immortals, <a name="fr_1096"></a><a href="#fn_1090"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> Freed of the fear arising from the sinful Kālī age. <a name="fr_1097"></a><a href="#fn_1091"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">2</p>  
 <p>(O Devī Gangā!) may you purify us,<br> Thou who separateth the earth from Heaven, <a name="fr_1098"></a><a href="#fn_1092"><font size="1">5</font></a><br> Gladdening the creeper-like matted hair on the head of Hara, <a name="fr_1099"></a><a href="#fn_1093"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Descending from the region of heaven,<br> Oozing from out the cave of the golden mountain, <a name="fr_1100"></a><a href="#fn_1094"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Falling upon the surface of the earth,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 176</font></a></p>  
 <p>Purifier (as the River Mandākinī) of the city of the Devas,<br> Who art the powerful Destructress of the multitude of men's sins.</p>  
 <p align="center">3</p>  
 <p>The trunks of elephants and their young make play with Thy waters,<br> Fragrant with ichor-maddened swarms of bees,<br> Trickling from the temples of elephants bathing therein.<br> Thy stream is browned with the sandal paste<br> Dropping from the breasts of Siddha women <a name="fr_1101"></a><a href="#fn_1095"><font size="1">1</font></a> who bathe therein.<br> And nigh the river bank Thy water is strewn with <i>Kuśa</i> <a name="fr_1102"></a><a href="#fn_1096"><font size="1">2</font></a> grass and flowers,<br> There thrown by sages <a name="fr_1103"></a><a href="#fn_1097"><font size="1">3</font></a> at morn and even. <a name="fr_1104"></a><a href="#fn_1098"><font size="1">4</font></a><br> May the water of the Ganges protect us!</p>  
 <p align="center">4</p>  
 <p>This divine sin-destroying Bhāgīrathī <a name="fr_1105"></a><a href="#fn_1099"><font size="1">5</font></a> now on earth<br> Was in the beginning water in the vessel <a name="fr_1106"></a><a href="#fn_1100"><font size="1">6</font></a> of the Primeval Grandfather. <a name="fr_1107"></a><a href="#fn_1101"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Then it was pure water from the feet of the Lord (Viṣṇu),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 177</font></a></p>  
 <p>Who sleeps on the serpent. <a name="fr_1108"></a><a href="#fn_1102"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> Again it was the gem adorning the matted hair of Śiva, <a name="fr_1109"></a><a href="#fn_1103"><font size="1">2</font></a><br> And, lastly, the daughter of the great sage Jahnu. <a name="fr_1110"></a><a href="#fn_1104"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p align="center">5</p>  
 <p>May the entrancing <a name="fr_1111"></a><a href="#fn_1105"><font size="1">4</font></a> Gangā falling on the matted hair <a name="fr_1112"></a><a href="#fn_1106"><font size="1">5</font></a> of Hara, <a name="fr_1113"></a><a href="#fn_1107"><font size="1">6</font></a><br> Descending from the Lord of Mountains,<br> Moving sinuously like a serpent to the ocean,<br> Flowing by the city of Kāśī, <a name="fr_1114"></a><a href="#fn_1108"><font size="1">7</font></a><br> Dispeller of countless worldly fears, <a name="fr_1115"></a><a href="#fn_1109"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> Saviour of those who bathe in Her waters,<br> Be ever victorious.</p>  
 <p align="center">6</p>  
 <p>How can he who has seen Thy wave be bound by illusion?</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>

 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 178</font></a></p>  
 <p>To him who has drunk of Thy water thou givest a dwelling in the city of the yellow-clad Deva. <a name="fr_1116"></a><a href="#fn_1110"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> O Gangā! what time the bodies of those who assume body <a name="fr_1117"></a><a href="#fn_1111"><font size="1">2</font></a> fall on Thy lap, <a name="fr_1118"></a><a href="#fn_1112"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> For such, O Mother, <a name="fr_1119"></a><a href="#fn_1113"><font size="1">4</font></a> even Shatakrīta's <a name="fr_1120"></a><a href="#fn_1114"><font size="1">5</font></a> grandeur <a name="fr_1121"></a><a href="#fn_1115"><font size="1">6</font></a> is but a small thing.</p>  
 <p align="center">7</p>  
 <p>O Bhagavatī! <a name="fr_1122"></a><a href="#fn_1116"><font size="1">7</font></a> on Thy bank I drink Thy water only.<br> I worship Kṛṣṇa, all thirst for worldly enjoyment having gone.<br> Destroyer of all sin, Whose companionship is the stairway to Heaven, <a name="fr_1123"></a><a href="#fn_1117"><font size="1">8</font></a><br> O Devī! Gangā of lightsome, tremulous wave,<br> Be gracious to me.</p>  
 <p align="center">8</p>  
 <p>O Mother! O Spouse of Śambhu! <a name="fr_1124"></a><a href="#fn_1118"><font size="1">9</font></a><br> Who art ever associated with Śambhu<br> At death, upon Thy banks, with my hands folded upon my head,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>

 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 179</font></a></p>  
 <p>Remembering Thy name and the feet of Nārāyaṇa, <a name="fr_1125"></a><a href="#fn_1119"><font size="1">1</font></a><br> May my devotion to Hara and Hari <a name="fr_1126"></a><a href="#fn_1120"><font size="1">2</font></a> ever endure<br> At the time of the festival of life's departure! <a name="fr_1127"></a><a href="#fn_1121"><font size="1">3</font></a><br> He who of pure mind reads this sacred eight-versed hymn to Gangā<br> Will be wholly released of all sin<br> And will go the region of Viṣṇu. <a name="fr_1128"></a><a href="#fn_1122"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1088"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1094">175:1</a> The River Ganges, in whom the Devī manifests.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1089"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1095">175:2</a> Śiva. The Ganges in its descent from heaven at the call of Bhagīratha was caught in the matted hair of Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1090"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1096">175:3</a> <i>Amarāvatī</i>. The city of India.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1091"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1097">175:4</a> Kaliyuga. The fourth, and, according to orthodox views, the present age, marked by the prevalence of sin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1092"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1098">175:5</a> By the celestial Ganges called Mandākinī.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1093"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1099">175:6</a> See note 2 <i>ante</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1094"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1100">175:7</a> Sumeru.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1095"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1101">176:1</a> Siddhas are celestial spirit (<i>devayoni</i>) of great purity.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1096"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1102">176:2</a> A species of grass used in worship.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1097"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1103">176:3</a> <i>Munis</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1098"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1104">176:4</a> The flower and grass is thrown by them when they worship the Ganges in the morning and evening.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1099"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1105">176:5</a> Gangā is called Bhagirathi, because She was brought down from heaven by the prayer of Bhagīratha, son of the solar race, in order to secure heavenly bliss for his kinsmen.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1100"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1106">176:6</a> <i>Kamaṇḍalu</i>, a gourd-shaped vessel used by ascetics.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1101"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1107">176:7</a> <i>Pitāmaha</i>. Brahmā is so called.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1102"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1108">177:1</a> The thousand-headed serpent Ananta Deva (see Viṣṇu Purāṇā).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1103"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1109">177:2</a> <i>Ante</i> <a href="#page_175">p. 175</a>, n. 2.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1104"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1110">177:3</a> Hence the river is called Jāhnavī. The verse speaks of the stages of the descent of the heavenly stream. <i>Ṛṣi</i> Jahnu swallowed the Ganges, and then, at the prayer of Bhagīratha, he let it issue from his thigh. The Ganges is called his daughter as She issued from him.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1105"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1111">177:4</a> <i>Manohārinī</i> ("mind stealer").</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1106"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1112">177:5</a> Śiva, like the ascetics, wears a coil of matted hair (<i>Jaṭa</i>)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1107"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1113">177:6</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1108"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1114">177:7</a> Benares, through which the Ganges flows.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1109"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1115">177:8</a> Fearlessness is the special gift of the Devī. The Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇā says: "When You are remembered in times of difficulty, You take away all fear of all beings." She is <i>Bhayāpahā</i> (remover of fear); for Śruti says ("Tai Up." ii. 9, 1): "By knowing the bliss of that Brahman none fear anything."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1110"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1116">178:1</a> That is, Viṣṇu, who is clad in yellow, and whose city is His heaven (<i>Vaikuṇṭha</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1111"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1117">178:2</a> Men.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1112"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1118">178:3</a> That is, when on death, they are thrown into the Ganges.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1113"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1119">178:4</a> The Devī is the Holy Mother (<i>Śrīmātā</i>), the first of Her names.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1114"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1120">178:5</a> Indra, King of the celestials.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1115"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1121">178:6</a> <i>Pada</i> or portion.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1116"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1122">178:7</a> Feminine of Bhagavān, a term applied to God, and which means He who possesses <i>Bhaga</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1117"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1123">178:8</a> <i>Svarga</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1118"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1124">178:9</a> Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1119"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1125">179:1</a> Viṣṇu.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1120"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1126">179:2</a> Viṣṇu and Śiva.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1121"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1127">179:3</a> Death (<i>prāṇaprayāṇotsava</i>), for it is the entrance to heavenly bliss.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1122"></a><a href="htg28.htm#fr_1128">179:4</a> <i>Vaikuṇṭha</i> (see <a href="#page_178">p. 178</a>. n. 1).</p>
