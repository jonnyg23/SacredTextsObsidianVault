 <p><a name="page_142"><font size="1" color="green">p. 142</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Mingrelian Proverbs</h3>  
 <p>1. TURN to the right, or turn to the left,<br> ’Twill all be one in the end. <a name="fr_48"></a><a href="#fn_47"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>2. The rat that came from outside,<br> Drove out the house-born rat.</p>  
 <p>3. Fight for the outlying village,<br> If thou wantest the one nearer home.</p>  
 <p>4. Wish thy neighbour to have an ox,<br> And God will send it to thee.</p>  
 <p>5. The wolf was abused as wolfish,<br> While the jackal ate up the flock.</p>  
 <p>6. The hen scratched and scratched till she dug up a knife,<br> With which her own throat was afterwards cut.</p>  
 <p>7. The road runs where an old road ran,<br> The river flows in the bed of a former river.</p>  
 <p>8. 'Give me room to stand,' quoth the bull, 'and I<br> Shall make myself enough room to lie.'</p>  
 <p>9. If the bear overcome thee--then call him Papa. <a name="fr_49"></a><a href="#fn_48"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>10. The dog took fright at a wolf,<br> And barked all the year round at a stump.</p>  
 <p>11. Who ever heard of a fish being prized<br> As long as it stayed in the stream?</p>  
 <p>12. They shot at the ripe--but the green fruit fell.</p>  
 <p>13. Leave a good deed on a stone by the way,<br> Thou'lt find it again after many a day (<i>i.e.</i> Cast thy bread on the waters).</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_143"><font size="1" color="green">p. 143</font></a></p>  
 <p>14. I say it--but whether it happen or no<br> ’Tis nothing to do with me.</p>  
 <p>15. Eat and drink up whatever is thine,<br> But cross thyself over all that ’s mine.</p>  
 <p>16. Who slew me?--My brother.<br> Who brought me back to life?--My brother.</p>  
 <p>17. The well-doer receives not good in return.</p>  
 <p>18. The truthful man is always duped.</p>  
 <p>19. My father I love, my mother I love<br> But myself I prefer before all.</p>  
 <p>20. A heart-kiss is better than a lip-kiss.</p>  
 <p>21. If thou hast not eaten pepper, why does thy mouth burn?</p>  
 <p>22. A disease that one sees, will not kill;<br> ’Tis hidden sores do the most ill.</p>  
 <p>23. Our granny has no teeth, so she<br> Likes not others' teeth to see.</p>  
 <p>24. He has forgotten the soul of his father, so he swears by the soul of his grandsire.</p>  
 <p>25. Gold is good, but if thou have it not, of what use is it to thee?</p>  
 <p>26. Better is copper of thine own than gold that is another's.</p>  
 <p>27. Of what use is light to him that is blind?</p>  
 <p>28. If thou art brave, do not bewail<br> The bluntness of thy sword.</p>
