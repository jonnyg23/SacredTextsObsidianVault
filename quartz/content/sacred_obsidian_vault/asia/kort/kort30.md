 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_57"></a><a href="kort30.htm#fr_57">104:1</a> judging by the transcription, the names of constellations given by Jochelson are of Paren origin. They all have instead <i>e</i> of the <i>a</i> of Kamenskoye. <i>Ačka'p-añai'</i>, however, is either a Kamenskoye form, or, in Paren dialect, a second form of the stem used in oblique cases (cf. p. 4). I have also corrected some evident errors (cr. Jochelson, The Koryak, <i>l. c.</i>, vol. vi, p. 123); namely, <i>eñe'y</i> instead of <i>eñen</i>, <i>Evan'venañ</i> instead of <i>Ena'nvenañāña</i>, <i>Yekeñeła'tIlIn</i> (or also <i>Yekeñeła<sup>?</sup>n</i>) of instead of <i>Yeke'ñelaqlIn</i>, <i>Ulve'-iy-i'mitiła<sup>?</sup>n</i> instead of <i>Ulveiyinitila<sup>?</sup>n</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_58"></a><a href="kort30.htm#fr_59">105:2</a> "Group of women" is the name of the Pleiades among the Chukchee, and of Cassiopeia among the Koryak of Kamenskoye. One of these women is called by the Koryak of Kamenskoye Yini'a-ñawġut, and another Kĭlu'.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_59"></a><a href="kort30.htm#fr_60">105:3</a> Among the Chukchee, the Belt of Orion is considered the crooked back of the archer Rulte'nnin. It became crooked because his wife struck at it with her tailoring-board, or, according to another version, with the wooden handle of her scraper. Among the Reindeer Koryak, the Belt (if Orion is called Kĭlu'-ena'nvenañ ("Kĭlu''s handle of scraper"). The Koryak archer, who carries his bow crosswise, is evidently identical with the Chukchee archer with the crooked back.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_60"></a><a href="kort30.htm#fr_61">106:1</a> According to Mr. Jochelson, Pege'ten ("suspended breath") is the name of the Morning Star. I was unable to ascertain the derivation of this word.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_61"></a><a href="kort30.htm#fr_62">106:2</a> Some stars in the constellation Wagoner are also called ġeke'ñIlIt "reindeer-drivers"); cf. Bogoras, The Chukchee, <i>l. c.</i>, vol. vii, p. 308.</p></body>
