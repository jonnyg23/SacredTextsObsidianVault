 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 74</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">"THY VOICE IS SWEET"</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY SAYAT NOVA</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>THY voice is soft, thy speech all sweetness flows;<br> May he protect who hath thy heart, my love!<br> Thy waist is the gazelle's, thy hue the rose,<br> Brocade from Franguistan thou art, my love!  
 <p>If I compare thee to brocade, ’twill fray;<br> If to a plane-tree, ’twill be felled one day;<br> All girls are likened to gazelles thou’lt say--<br> How then shall I describe thee truly, love?</p>  
 <p>The violet is wild, and low of birth;<br> Rubies are stones, for all their priceless worth:<br> The moon itself is made of rocks and earth--<br> All flame, thou shinest like the sun, my love.</p>  
 <p>Thy door I seek as pilgrims seek a shrine:<br> Thine eyes are roses, new-blown eglantine;<br> Thy tongue a pen, thy hands like paper fine,<br> A flower fresh from the sea thou art, my love!</p>  
 <p>Within my soul thy hand has placed love's seed;<br> Thy wiles and coyness make my heart to bleed:<br> Thy Sayat Nova thou hast slain indeed,<br> Thine evil fate he bears for thee, my love.</p></dir>
