 <p><a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 11</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">HUNTSMAN, THAT ON THE HILLS ABOVE</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY AVETIS ISAHAKIAN</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>"HUNTSMAN, that on the hills above<br>      To hunt the deer hast been,<br> Tell me, I pray thee, if my love--<br>      My wild deer thou hast seen?  
 <p>"He sought the hills his grief to quell--<br>      My darling love, my sun.<br> He wandered out upon the fell,<br>      My flower, my only one."</p>  
 <p>"Maiden, I saw your lover true,<br>      All girt with red and green.<br> Upon his breast a rose tree grew<br>      Where once your kiss had been."</p>  
 <p>"Huntsman, I pray, who is the bride<br>      Of my beloved, my sun?<br> Who tends him, watching by his side,<br>      My flower, my only one?"</p>  
 <p>"Maiden, I saw him with his head<br>      Upon a stone at rest.<br> And for his love, a bullet red<br>      Into his heart was pressed.</p>  
 <p>"The mountain breeze caressingly<br>      Played with his jet-black hair,<br> And blossoms wept unceasingly<br>      Your flower, your lover there."</p></dir>
