 <p><a name="page_32"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 32</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MORNING</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY HAROUTUNE TOUMANIAN</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>DAY dawned. Bright tongues of scarlet flame<br>      Shot up into the sky,<br> The livid heav’ns blushed, and became<br>      A sea of crimson dye.  
 <p>The sun his fiery beams unrolled<br>      Like strands of coloured thread;<br> Embroidered all the clouds with gold,<br>      And blue, and green, and red.</p>  
 <p>Then o’er the mountain, full in view,<br>      Nature's great Monarch rose:<br> And from his tent of Royal blue<br>      Hurled darts upon his foes.</p>  
 <p>Eternal foe of Gloom and Night,<br>      On high he raised his arm;<br> His shield of gold, all shining bright,<br>      Sheltered the world from harm.</p></dir>
