 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 27</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE SONG OF THE STORK</h3>  <h5 align="center">FOLK SONG</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>    STORK, I welcome thy return.<br> Thou stork, I welcome thy return.<br> Thy coming is the sign of spring,<br> And thou dost joy and gladness bring.  
 <p>    Stork, upon our roof descend.<br> Thou stork, upon our roof descend.<br> Upon our ash-tree build thy nest,<br> Our dear one, and our honoured guest.</p>  
 <p>    Stork, I would complain to thee:--<br> Yes, stork, I would complain to thee.<br> A thousand sorrows I would tell,<br> The griefs that in my bosom dwell.</p>  
 <p>    Stork, when thou our house didst leave,<br> When last our ash-tree thou didst leave,<br> Cold, blasting winds the heavens filled,<br> And all our smiling flowers were killed.</p>  
 <p>    Clouds obscured the brilliant sky;<br> Dark clouds obscured the brilliant sky.<br> Up there in flakes they broke the snow,<br> And Winter killed the flowers below.</p></dir>  
 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 28</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>    From the mountain of Varag,<br> From that great hill they call Varag,<br> The snow did all the earth enfold:--<br> In our green meadow it was cold.  
 <p>    In our garden all was white.<br> Our little garden all was white.<br> Our tender rose-trees, fresh and green,<br> All died of Winter's frost-bite keen.</p></dir>
