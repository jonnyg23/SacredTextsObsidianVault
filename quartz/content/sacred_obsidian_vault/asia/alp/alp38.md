 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 46</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE DREAM</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY SMPAD SHAHAZIZ</h5>  
 <p align="center">(1840-1897)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>SOFT and low a voice breathed o’er me,<br>     Near me did my mother seem;<br> Flashed a ray of joy before me,<br>     But, alas, it was a dream!  
 <p>There the murmuring streamlet flowing<br>     Scattered radiant pearls around,<br> Pure and clear, like crystal glowing--<br>     But it was a dream, unsound.</p>  
 <p>And my mother's mournful singing<br>     Took me back to childhood's day,<br> To my mind her kisses bringing--<br>     ’Twas a dream and passed away!</p>  
 <p>To her heart she pressed me yearning,<br>     Wiped her eyes which wet did seem;<br> And her tears fell on me burning--<br>     Why should it have been a dream?</p></dir>
