 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 62</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE CRANE</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY HOVHANNES TOUMANIAN</h5>  
 <p align="center">(<i>Born</i> 1869)</p>  
 <p></p><dir>THE Crane has lost his way across the heaven,<br> From yonder stormy cloud I hear him cry,<br> A traveller o’er an unknown pathway driven,<br> In a cold world unheeded he doth fly.  
 <p>Ah, whither leads this pathway long and dark,<br> My God, where ends it, thus with fears obsessed?<br> When shall night end this day's last glimmering spark?<br> Where shall my weary feet to-night find rest?</p>  
 <p>Farewell, belovèd bird, where’er thou roam<br> Spring shall return and bring thee back once more,<br> With thy sweet mate and young ones, to thy home--<br> Thy last year's nest upon the sycamore.</p>  
 <p>But I am exiled from my ruined nest,<br> And roam with faltering steps from hill to hill,<br> Like to the fowls of heaven in my unrest<br> Envying the boulders motionless and still.</p>  
 <p>Each boulder unassailed stands in its place,<br> But I from mine must wander tempest tossed--<br> And every bird its homeward way can trace,<br> But I must roam in darkness, lone and lost.</p>  
 <p>Ah, whither leads this pathway long and dark,<br> My God, where ends it, thus with fears obsessed?<br> When shall night end this day's last glimmering spark?<br> Where shall my weary feet to-night find rest?</p></dir>
