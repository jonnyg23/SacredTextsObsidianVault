 <p><a name="page_110"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 110</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THOU ART SO SWEET</h3>  <h4 align="center">BY SAYAT NOVA</h4>  
 <p></p><dir>THOU art so sweet thou wilt not pain the minstrel singing songs to thee,<br> But when he loves thee thou dost frown--in vain he tells his wrongs to thee.  
 <p>Love's fire is such, ’twill not consume--’twill burn, and burn, and ever burn:<br> If in that sea of flame I fall to cool me thou wilt never turn.</p>  
 <p>Alas, how shall the minstrel bear thy lightning gleams that pierce his heart?<br> No pact or treaty wilt thou make--a monarch absolute thou art.</p>  
 <p>If thou dost meet with mountains high like wax thou meltest them away;<br> If cities fair lie on thy path, their pride in ruins thou dost lay.</p>  
 <p>In sooth, no compact wilt thou make with him who sings these strains to thee:<br> Sayat Nova no credit hath when he would tell his pains to thee.</p></dir>
