 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE TEARS OF ARAXES</h3>  <h5 align="center">BY RAPHAEL PATKANIAN</h5>  
 <p></p><dir>I WALK by Mother Arax<br>      With faltering steps and slow,<br> And memories of past ages<br>      Seek in the waters' flow.  
 <p>But they run dark and turbid,<br>      And beat upon the shore<br> In grief and bitter sorrow,<br>      Lamenting evermore.</p>  
 <p>"Araxes! with the fishes<br>      Why dost not dance in glee?<br> The sea is still far distant,<br>      Yet thou art sad, like me.</p>  
 <p>"From thy proud eyes, O Mother,<br>      Why do the tears downpour?<br> Why dost thou haste so swiftly<br>      Past thy familiar shore?</p>  
 <p>"Make not thy current turbid;<br>      Flow calm and joyously.<br> Thy youth is short, fair river;<br>      Thou soon wilt reach the sea.</p>  
 <p>"Let sweet rose-hedges brighten<br>      Thy hospitable shore,<br> And nightingales among them<br>      Till morn their music pour.</p></dir>  
 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 70</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>"Let ever-verdant willows<br>      Lave in thy waves their feet,<br> And with their bending branches<br>      Refresh the noonday heat.  
 <p>"Let shepherds on thy margin<br>      Walk singing, without fear;<br> Let lambs and kids seek freely<br>      Thy waters cool and clear."</p>  
 <p>Araxes swelled her current,<br>      Tossed high her foaming tide,<br> And in a voice of thunder<br>      Thus from her depths replied:--</p>  
 <p>"Rash, thoughtless youth, why com’st thou<br>      My age-long sleep to break,<br> And memories of my myriad griefs<br>      Within my breast to wake?</p>  
 <p>"When hast thou seen a widow,<br>      After her true-love died,<br> From head to foot resplendent<br>      With ornaments of pride?</p>  
 <p>"For whom should I adorn me?<br>      Whose eyes shall I delight?<br> The stranger hordes that tread my banks<br>      Are hateful in my sight.</p>  
 <p>"My kindred stream, impetuous Kur,<br>      Is widowed, like to me,<br> But bows beneath the tyrant's yoke,<br>      And wears it slavishly.</p></dir>  
 <p><a name="page_71"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 71</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>"But I, who am Armenian,<br>      My own Armenians know;<br> I want no stranger bridegroom;<br>      A widowed stream I flow.  
 <p>"Once I, too, moved in splendour,<br>      Adorned as is a bride<br> With myriad precious jewels,<br>      My smiling banks beside.</p>  
 <p>"My waves were pure and limpid,<br>      And curled in rippling play;<br> The morning star within them<br>      Was mirrored till the day.</p>  
 <p>"What from that time remaineth?<br>      All, all has passed away.<br> Which of my prosperous cities<br>      Stands near my waves to-day?</p>  
 <p>"Mount Ararat doth pour me,<br>      As with a mother's care,<br> From out her sacred bosom<br>      Pure water, cool and fair.</p>  
 <p>"Shall I her holy bounty<br>      To hated aliens fling?<br> Shall strangers' fields be watered<br>      From good Saint Jacob's spring?</p>  
 <p>"For filthy Turk or Persian<br>      Shall I my waters pour,<br> That they may heathen rites perform<br>      Upon my very shore,</p></dir>  
 <p><a name="page_72"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 72</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir>"While my own sons, defenceless,<br>      Are exiled from their home,<br> And, faint with thirst and hunger,<br>      In distant countries roam?  
 <p>"My own Armenian nation<br>      Is banished far away;<br> A godless, barbarous people<br>      Dwells on my banks to-day.</p>  
 <p>"Shall I my hospitable shores<br>      Adorn in festive guise<br> For them, or gladden with fair looks<br>      Their wild and evil eyes?</p>  
 <p>"Still, while my sons are exiled,<br>      Shall I be sad, as now.<br> This is my heart's deep utterance,<br>      My true and holy vow."</p>  
 <p>No more spake Mother Arax;<br>      She foamed up mightily,<br> And, coiling like a serpent,<br>      Wound sorrowing toward the sea.</p>  
 <p>                 <i>Translated by Alice Stone Blackwell</i>.</p></dir>
