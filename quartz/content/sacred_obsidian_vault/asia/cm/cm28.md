 <h3 align="center">6. For Hunting Sea-Game.<a name="page_132_fr_1"></a><a href="#page_132_note_1"><font size="1">1</font></a></h3>
 
 <p> This is an incantation for (hunting) sea-game. When hunters, having gone out in a boat, have found a herd of walrus sleeping on the ice, and the ice is not solid, (so that) the boat makes a noise striking against small pieces of ice floating around, but the walrus are sleeping; oh, one makes (the following) incantation, and says, — </p>
 
 <p> "O walrus! I put your ears on a (broad) iron pan, lest you [the walrus] should be able to hear the rapping-noise [the sleeping walrus]." Then the hunters are (able) to approach, and catch all (of the herd).</p>
 
 <p align="right"><small>Told by Vịyênto´ the Blind, a Maritime Chukchee man, at Mariinsky Post, October, 1900.</small></p>
