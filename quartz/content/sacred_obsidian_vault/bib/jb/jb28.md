 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Life and Morals of Jesus of Nazareth (the Jefferson Bible)</i>, by Thomas Jefferson, [1902], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXIV.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><small><i>A Prophet Hath no Honor in his Own Country</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>AND it came to pass, <i>that</i> when Jesus had finished these parables, he departed thence.</p>  
 <p>2 And when he was come into his own country, he taught them in their synagogue, insomuch that they were astonished and said, Whence hath this <i>man</i> this wisdom, and <i>these</i> mighty works?</p>  
 <p>3 Is not this the carpenter's son? is not his mother called Mary? and his brethren James, and Joses, and Simon, and Judas?</p>  
 <p>4 And his sisters, are they not all with us? Whence then hath this <i>man</i> all these things?</p>  
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="green">p. 69</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. And they were offended in him. But Jesus said unto them, A prophet is not without honour, save in his own country, and in his own house.</p>
