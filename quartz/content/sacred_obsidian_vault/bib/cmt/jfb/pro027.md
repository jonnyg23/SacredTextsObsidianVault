Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 27</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#001">Proverbs 27:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 27:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 27:1-27)
 <p>
 </p><p>Do not confide implicitly in your plans (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#009">Pro 16:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#021">Pro 19:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#013">Jam 4:13-15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#002">Proverbs 27:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 27:2</a></font></span></p>Avoid self-praise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#003">Proverbs 27:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 27:3</a></font></span></p>heavy--The literal sense of "heavy," applied to material subjects, illustrates its figurative, "grievous," applied to moral. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a fool's wrath--is unreasonable and excessive.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#004">Proverbs 27:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 27:4</a></font></span></p>envy--or, "jealousy" (compare Margin; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#034">Pro 6:34</a>), is more unappeasable than the simpler bad passions.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#005">Proverbs 27:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 27:5</a></font></span></p>secret love--not manifested in acts is useless; and even, if its exhibition by rebukes wounds us, such love is preferable to the frequent (compare Margin), and hence deceitful, kisses of an enemy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#007">Proverbs 27:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 27:7</a></font></span></p>The luxury of wealth confers less happiness than the healthy appetite of labor.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#008">Proverbs 27:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 27:8</a></font></span></p>Such are not only out of place, but out of duty and in danger.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#009">Proverbs 27:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 27:9</a></font></span></p>rejoice the heart--the organ of perceiving what pleases the senses. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sweetness . . . counsel--or, "wise counsel is also pleasing."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#010">Proverbs 27:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 27:10</a></font></span></p>Adhere to tried friends. The ties of blood may be less reliable than those of genuine friendship.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#011">Proverbs 27:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 27:11</a></font></span></p>The wisdom of children both reflects credit on parents and contributes to their aid in difficulties.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#012">Proverbs 27:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 27:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#016">Pro 20:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#003">Pro 22:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#014">Proverbs 27:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 27:14</a></font></span></p>Excessive zeal in praising raises suspicions of selfishness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#015">Proverbs 27:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 27:15</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#013">Pro 19:13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>very . . . day--literally, "a day of showers."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#016">Proverbs 27:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 27:16</a></font></span></p>hideth--or, "restrains" (that is, tries to do it); is as fruitless an effort, as that of holding the wind. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the ointment of his right hand--the organ of power (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#007">Psa 17:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#035">Psa 18:35</a>). His right hand endeavors to repress perfume, but vainly. Some prefer: "His right hand comes on oil," that is, "cannot take hold." Such a woman cannot be tamed.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#017">Proverbs 27:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 27:17</a></font></span></p>a man sharpeneth . . . friend--that is, conversation promotes intelligence, which the face exhibits.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#018">Proverbs 27:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 27:18</a></font></span></p>Diligence secures a reward, even for the humble servant.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#019">Proverbs 27:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 27:19</a></font></span></p>We may see our characters in the developed tempers of others.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#020">Proverbs 27:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 27:20</a></font></span></p>Men's cupidity is as insatiable as the grave.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#021">Proverbs 27:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 27:21</a></font></span></p>Praise tests character. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a man to his praise--according to his praise, as he bears it. Thus vain men seek it, weak men are inflated by it, wise men disregard it, &amp;c.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#022">Proverbs 27:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 27:22</a></font></span></p>The obstinate wickedness of such is incurable by the heaviest inflictions.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#023">Proverbs 27:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 27:23</a></font></span></p>flocks--constituted the staple of wealth. It is only by care and diligence that the most solid possessions can be perpetuated (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#005">Pro 23:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#025">Proverbs 27:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 27:25</a></font></span></p>The fact that providential arrangements furnish the means of competence to those who properly use them is another motive to diligence (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa065.htm#009">Psa 65:9-13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>The hay appeareth--literally, "Grass appeareth" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job040.htm#015">Job 40:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#014">Psa 104:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#027">Proverbs 27:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 27:27</a></font></span></p>household--literally, "house," the family (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#015">Act 16:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#016">Co1 1:16</a>).
