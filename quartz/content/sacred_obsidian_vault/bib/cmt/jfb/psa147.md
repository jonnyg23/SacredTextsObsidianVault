Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 147</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#001">Psalms 147:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 147:1</a></font></span></p>This and the remaining Psalms have been represented as specially designed to celebrate the rebuilding of Jerusalem (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh006.htm#016">Neh 6:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh012.htm#027">Neh 12:27</a>). They all open and close with the stirring call for praise. This one specially declares God's providential care towards all creatures, and particularly His people. (Psa. 147:1-20)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#001">Psa 92:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#003">Psa 135:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#002">Psalms 147:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 147:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa107.htm#003">Psa 107:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#012">Isa 11:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#003">Psalms 147:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 147:3</a></font></span></p>Though applicable to the captive Israelites, this is a general and precious truth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>wounds--(Compare Margin).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#004">Psalms 147:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 147:4</a></font></span></p>God's power in nature (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#026">Isa 40:26-28</a>, and often) is presented as a pledge of His power to help His people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>telleth . . . stars--what no man can do (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#005">Gen 15:5</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#006">Psalms 147:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 147:6</a></font></span></p>That power is put forth for the good of the meek and suffering pious, and confusion of the wicked (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa146.htm#008">Psa 146:8-9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#007">Psalms 147:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 147:7</a></font></span></p>His providence supplies bountifully the wild animals in their mountain homes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>Sing . . . Lord--literally, "Answer the Lord," that is, in grateful praise to His goodness, thus declared in His acts.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#010">Psalms 147:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 147:10</a></font></span></p>The advantages afforded, as in war by the strength of the horse or the agility of man, do not incline God to favor any; but those who fear and, of course, trust Him, will obtain His approbation and aid.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#013">Psalms 147:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 147:13</a></font></span></p>strengthened . . . gates--or, means of defense against invaders,
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#014">Psalms 147:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 147:14</a></font></span></p>maketh . . . borders--or, territories (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#017">Gen 23:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa054.htm#012">Isa 54:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>filleth thee, &amp;c.--(Compare Margin).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#015">Psalms 147:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 147:15</a></font></span></p>God's Word, as a swift messenger, executes His purpose, for with Him to command is to perform (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#003">Gen 1:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#009">Psa 33:9</a>), and He brings about the wonders of providence as easily as men cast crumbs.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#017">Psalms 147:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 147:17</a></font></span></p>morsels--used as to food (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#005">Gen 18:5</a>), perhaps here denotes hail.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#019">Psalms 147:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 147:19</a></font></span></p>This mighty ruler and benefactor of heaven and earth is such especially to His chosen people, to whom alone (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu004.htm#032">Deu 4:32-34</a>) He has made known His will, while others have been left in darkness. Therefore unite in the great hallelujah.
