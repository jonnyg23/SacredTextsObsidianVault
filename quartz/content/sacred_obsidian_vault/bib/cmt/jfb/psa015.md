 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#001">Psalms 15:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 15:1</a></font></span></p>Those who are fit for communion with God may be known by a conformity to His law, which is illustrated in various important particulars. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#001">Psa 15:1-5</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>abide--or, "sojourn" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#004">Psa 5:4</a>), where it means under God's protection here, as (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#006">Psa 23:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#004">Psa 27:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#006">Psa 27:6</a>) communion. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>tabernacle--seat of the ark (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2006.htm#017">Sa2 6:17</a>), the symbol of God's presence. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>holy hill--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#002">Psalms 15:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 15:2</a></font></span></p>walketh--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psa 1:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>uprightly--in a complete manner, as to all parts of conduct (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#001">Gen 17:1</a>), not as to degree. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>worketh--or, "does." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>righteousness--what is right. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in his heart--sincerely (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#007">Pro 23:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#003">Psalms 15:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 15:3</a></font></span></p>He neither slanders nor spreads slander.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#004">Psalms 15:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 15:4</a></font></span></p>Love and hate are regulated by a regard to God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sweareth . . . hurt--or what so results (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev005.htm#004">Lev 5:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#005">Psalms 15:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 15:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#037">Lev 25:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu023.htm#019">Deu 23:19-20</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>usury is derived from a verb meaning "to bite." All gains made by the wrongful loss of others are forbidden. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>taketh reward, &amp;c.--The innocent would not otherwise be condemned (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo023.htm#008">Exo 23:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu016.htm#019">Deu 16:19</a>). Bribery of all sorts is denounced. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>doeth these, &amp;c.--Such persons admitted to God's presence and favor shall never be moved (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#006">Psa 10:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#005">Psa 13:5</a>).
 </p><p>
