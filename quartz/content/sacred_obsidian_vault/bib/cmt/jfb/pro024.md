Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#001">Proverbs 24:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 24:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 24:1-34)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#003">Pro 23:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#017">Pro 23:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#001">Psa 37:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#002">Proverbs 24:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 24:2</a></font></span></p>studieth--meditateth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>talk . . . mischief--Their expressed purposes are to do evil.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#003">Proverbs 24:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 24:3</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#001">Pro 14:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa054.htm#014">Isa 54:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>house--including the family.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#004">Proverbs 24:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 24:4</a></font></span></p>by knowledge . . . riches-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#018">Pro 8:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#020">Pro 21:20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#005">Proverbs 24:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 24:5</a></font></span></p>The general statement (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#016">Ecc 9:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#018">Ecc 9:18</a>) is specially illustrated (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#022">Pro 21:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa144.htm#001">Psa 144:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#007">Proverbs 24:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 24:7</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#016">Pro 14:16</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>in the gate--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#022">Pro 22:22</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#008">Proverbs 24:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 24:8</a></font></span></p>So called even if he fails to do evil. 
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#009">Proverbs 24:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 24:9</a></font></span></p>Same thought varied.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#010">Proverbs 24:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 24:10</a></font></span></p>Literally, "If thou fail in the day of straits (adversity), strait (or, small) is thy strength," which is then truly tested.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#011">Proverbs 24:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 24:11</a></font></span></p>Neglect of known duty is sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#017">Jam 4:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>ready--literally, "bowing down" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>to be slain--that is, unjustly. God's retributive justice cannot be avoided by professed ignorance.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#013">Proverbs 24:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 24:13</a></font></span></p>As delicious food whets the appetite, so should the rewards of wisdom excite us to seek it.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#014">Proverbs 24:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 24:14</a></font></span></p>reward--literally, "after part," the proper result (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#018">Pro 23:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#037">Psa 37:37-38</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#015">Proverbs 24:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 24:15</a></font></span></p>The plots of the wicked against the good, though partially, shall not be fully successful (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#024">Psa 37:24</a>); while the wicked, falling under penal evil, find no help.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#016">Proverbs 24:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 24:16</a></font></span></p>seven times--often, or many (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#016">Pro 6:16</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#031">Pro 6:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Pro 9:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#017">Proverbs 24:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 24:17</a></font></span></p>Yet let none rejoice over the fate of evildoers, lest God punish their wrong spirit by relieving the sufferer (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#005">Pro 17:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job031.htm#029">Job 31:29</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#019">Proverbs 24:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 24:19</a></font></span></p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#001">Psa 37:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#038">Psa 37:38</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#028">Psa 18:28</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#020">Proverbs 24:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 24:20</a></font></span></p>candle--or, "prosperity"; it shall come to an end (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009">Pro 13:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#020">Pro 20:20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#021">Proverbs 24:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 24:21</a></font></span></p>A warning against impiety and resistance to lawful rule (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#001">Rom 13:1-7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#017">Pe1 2:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>meddle . . . change--(Compare Margin), literally, "mingle not yourself," avoid the society of restless persons.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#022">Proverbs 24:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 24:22</a></font></span></p>their calamity, &amp;c.--either what God and the king inflict, or what changers and their company suffer; better the first.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#023">Proverbs 24:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 24:23</a></font></span></p>These . . . wise--literally, "are of the wise," as authors (compare "Psalms of David," Hebrew). "These" refers to the verses following, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#024">Pro 24:24-34</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>to have respect--literally, "to discern faces," show partiality,
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#024">Proverbs 24:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 24:24</a></font></span></p>of which an example is justifying the wicked, to which is opposed, rebuking him, which has a blessing.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#026">Proverbs 24:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 24:26</a></font></span></p>kiss his lips--love and obey, do homage (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol008.htm#001">Sol 8:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>right answer--literally, "plain words" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#009">Pro 8:9</a>), opposed to deceptive, or obscure.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#027">Proverbs 24:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 24:27</a></font></span></p>Prepare . . . in the field--Secure, by diligence, a proper support, and then build; provide necessaries, then comforts, to which a house rather pertained, in a mild climate, permitting the use of tents.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#028">Proverbs 24:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 24:28</a></font></span></p>Do not speak even truth needlessly against any, and never falsehood.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#029">Proverbs 24:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 24:29</a></font></span></p>Especially avoid retaliation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#043">Mat 5:43-45</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#017">Rom 12:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#030">Proverbs 24:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 24:30</a></font></span></p>A striking picture of the effects of sloth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#032">Proverbs 24:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 24:32</a></font></span></p>From the folly of the sluggard learn wisdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#010">Pro 6:10-11</a>).
