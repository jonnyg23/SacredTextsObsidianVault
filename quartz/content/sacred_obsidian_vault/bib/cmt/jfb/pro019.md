Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 19</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#001">Proverbs 19:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 19:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 19:1-29)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#006">Pro 28:6</a>). "Rich" for fool here. Integrity is better than riches (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#016">Pro 15:16-17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#008">Pro 16:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#002">Proverbs 19:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 19:2</a></font></span></p>The last illustrates the first clause. Rashness, the result of ignorance, brings trouble.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#003">Proverbs 19:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 19:3</a></font></span></p>perverteth . . . way--turns him back from right (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#006">Pro 13:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#013">Jam 1:13</a>); and he blames God for his failures.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#004">Proverbs 19:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 19:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#020">Pro 14:20</a>). Such facts are often adduced with implied disapprobation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#005">Proverbs 19:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 19:5</a></font></span></p>Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#009">Pro 19:9</a>, where perish explains not escape here (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#009">Psa 88:9-10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#008">Proverbs 19:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 19:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare Margin; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#032">Pro 15:32</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>loveth . . . soul--or, "himself," which he evinces by regarding his best interests. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>keepeth--or, "regards."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#010">Proverbs 19:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 19:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#007">Pro 17:7</a>). The fool is incapable of properly using pleasure as knowledge, yet for him to have it is less incongruous than the undue elevation of servants. Let each abide in his calling (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#020">Co1 7:20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#011">Proverbs 19:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 19:11</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029">Pro 14:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#032">Pro 16:32</a>). This inculcation of a forgiving spirit shows that true religion is always the same (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#022">Mat 5:22-24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#012">Proverbs 19:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 19:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#014">Pro 16:14-15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002">Pro 20:2</a>). A motive to submission to lawful authority.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#013">Proverbs 19:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 19:13</a></font></span></p>calamity--literally, "calamities," varied and many. 
 <p>
 </p><p>continual dropping--a perpetual annoyance, wearing out patience.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#014">Proverbs 19:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 19:14</a></font></span></p>A contrast of men's gifts and God's, who, though author of both blessings, confers the latter by His more special providence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>and--or, "but," implying that the evils of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#013">Pro 19:13</a> are only avoided by His care.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#015">Proverbs 19:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 19:15</a></font></span></p>a deep sleep--a state of utter indifference. 
 <p>
 </p><p>idle soul--or, "person" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#004">Pro 10:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#024">Pro 12:24</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#016">Proverbs 19:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 19:16</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#013">Pro 13:13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>despiseth . . . ways--opposed to keeping or observing, neglects (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#017">Pro 16:17</a>) (as unworthy of regard) his moral conduct.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#017">Proverbs 19:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 19:17</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#021">Pro 14:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#026">Psa 37:26</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hath pity--shown by acts (compare Margin).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#018">Proverbs 19:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 19:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#024">Pro 13:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#013">Pro 23:13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>let not . . . spare--literally, "do not lift up thy soul" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#004">Psa 24:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psa 25:1</a>), that is, do not desire to his death; a caution to passionate parents against angry chastisement.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#019">Proverbs 19:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 19:19</a></font></span></p>Repeated efforts of kindness are lost on ill-natured persons.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#020">Proverbs 19:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 19:20</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#018">Pro 13:18-20</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>latter end-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#011">Pro 5:11</a>). In youth prepare for age.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#021">Proverbs 19:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 19:21</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#001">Pro 16:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#009">Pro 16:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa033.htm#010">Psa 33:10-11</a>). The failure of man's devices is implied.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#022">Proverbs 19:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 19:22</a></font></span></p>desire--that is, to do good, indicates a kind disposition (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#023">Pro 11:23</a>); and the poor thus affected are better than liars, who say and do not.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#023">Proverbs 19:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 19:23</a></font></span></p>The fear . . . life--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#002">Pro 3:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>abide--or, "remain contented" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1004.htm#008">Ti1 4:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>not visited with evil-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#003">Pro 10:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#025">Psa 37:25</a>), as a judgment, in which sense visit is often used (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#032">Psa 89:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer006.htm#015">Jer 6:15</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#024">Proverbs 19:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 19:24</a></font></span></p>bosom--literally, a wide dish in which the hand was plunged in eating (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#023">Mat 26:23</a>). Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#015">Pro 26:15</a>, the sentiment expressed with equal irony and less exaggeration.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#025">Proverbs 19:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 19:25</a></font></span></p>Such is the benefit of reproof; even the simple profit, much more the wise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#026">Proverbs 19:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 19:26</a></font></span></p>Unfilial conduct often condemned (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#021">Pro 17:21-25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#020">Pro 20:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu021.htm#018">Deu 21:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu021.htm#021">Deu 21:21</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#027">Proverbs 19:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 19:27</a></font></span></p>Avoid whatever leads from truth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#028">Proverbs 19:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 19:28</a></font></span></p>ungodly witness--(Compare Margin), one false by bad principles (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#012">Pro 6:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>scorneth judgment--sets at naught the dictates of justice. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>devoureth--literally, "swalloweth," as something delightful.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#029">Proverbs 19:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 19:29</a></font></span></p>Their punishment is sure, fixed, and ready (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#034">Pro 3:34</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#013">Pro 10:13</a>).
