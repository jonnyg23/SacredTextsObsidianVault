Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#001">Proverbs 16:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 16:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 16:1-33)
 <p>
 </p><p>preparations--schemes. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in man--or literally, "to man," belonging, or pertaining to him. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the answer . . . Lord--The efficient ordering is from God: "Man proposes; God disposes."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#002">Proverbs 16:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 16:2</a></font></span></p>clean--or, "faultless." 
 <p>
 </p><p>weigheth--or, "tries," "judges," implying that they are faulty (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#002">Pro 21:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#012">Pro 24:12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#003">Proverbs 16:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 16:3</a></font></span></p>(Compare Margin). Rely on God for success to your lawful purposes.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#004">Proverbs 16:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 16:4</a></font></span></p>for himself--"for its answer," or "purpose," that is, according to God's plan; the wicked are for the day of evil (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#005">Psa 49:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#018">Jer 17:18</a>); sinning and suffering answer to each other, are indissolubly united.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#005">Proverbs 16:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 16:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#032">Pro 3:32</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#006">Proverbs 16:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 16:6</a></font></span></p>By mercy and truth--that is, God's (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa085.htm#010">Psa 85:10</a>); He effects the atonement, or covering of sin; and the principles of true piety incline men to depart from evil; or, "mercy" and "truth" may be man's, indicative of the gracious tempers which work instrumentally in procuring pardon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>purged--expiated (as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#033">Lev 16:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa027.htm#009">Isa 27:9</a>, Hebrew).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#007">Proverbs 16:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 16:7</a></font></span></p>Persecutions, of course, excepted.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#008">Proverbs 16:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 16:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#006">Pro 15:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#016">Pro 15:16-17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#009">Proverbs 16:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 16:9</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#003">Pro 16:3</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>directeth--establisheth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#010">Proverbs 16:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 16:10</a></font></span></p>The last clause depends on the first, expressing the importance of equity in decisions, so authoritative.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#011">Proverbs 16:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 16:11</a></font></span></p>are the Lord's . . . his work--that is, what He has ordered, and hence should be observed by men.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#012">Proverbs 16:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 16:12</a></font></span></p>Rulers are rightly expected, by their position, to hate evil; for their power is sustained by righteousness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#013">Proverbs 16:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 16:13</a></font></span></p>A specification of the general sentiment of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#012">Pro 16:12</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#014">Proverbs 16:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 16:14</a></font></span></p>This wrath, so terrible and certain, like messengers of death (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#025">Kg1 2:25</a>), can be appeased by the wise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#015">Proverbs 16:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 16:15</a></font></span></p>light of . . . countenance--favor (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#006">Psa 4:6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>life--preserves it, or gives blessings which make it valuable. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the latter rain--fell just before harvest and matured the crop; hence specially valuable (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu011.htm#014">Deu 11:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#016">Proverbs 16:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 16:16</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#005">Pro 4:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#017">Proverbs 16:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 16:17</a></font></span></p>The highway--A common, plain road represents the habitual course of the righteous in departing from evil. 
 <p>
 </p><p>keepeth--observes.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018">Proverbs 16:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 16:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#033">Pro 15:33</a>). Haughtiness and pride imply self-confidence which produces carelessness, and hence 
 <p>
 </p><p>a fall--literally, "sliding."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#019">Proverbs 16:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 16:19</a></font></span></p>divide the spoil--that is, conquer. Avoid the society of the proud (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#006">Jam 4:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#020">Proverbs 16:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 16:20</a></font></span></p>handleth a matter--wisely considers "the word," that is, of God (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#013">Pro 13:13</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>trusteth--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa118.htm#008">Psa 118:8-9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#021">Proverbs 16:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 16:21</a></font></span></p>wise in heart--who rightly consider duty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sweetness of the lips--eloquent discourse, persuades and instructs others.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#022">Proverbs 16:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 16:22</a></font></span></p>Understanding--or, "discretion," is a constant source of blessing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#014">Pro 13:14</a>), benefiting others; but fools' best efforts are folly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#023">Proverbs 16:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 16:23</a></font></span></p>The heart is the source of wisdom flowing from the mouth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#024">Proverbs 16:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 16:24</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#026">Pro 15:26</a>). Gentle, kind words, by soothing the mind, give the body health.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#025">Proverbs 16:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 16:25</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#002">Pro 14:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#026">Proverbs 16:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 16:26</a></font></span></p>Diligence is a duty due to one's self, for his wants require labor.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#027">Proverbs 16:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 16:27</a></font></span></p>ungodly man--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#012">Pro 6:12</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>diggeth up evil--labors for it. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in his lips . . . fire--His words are calumniating (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam003.htm#006">Jam 3:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#028">Proverbs 16:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 16:28</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#014">Pro 6:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#031">Pro 10:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>whisperer--prater, talebearer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008">Pro 18:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#020">Pro 26:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#029">Proverbs 16:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 16:29</a></font></span></p>violent man--or, "man of mischief" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#031">Pro 3:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>enticeth-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#010">Pro 1:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#030">Proverbs 16:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 16:30</a></font></span></p>He shutteth his eyes--denoting deep thought (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa064.htm#006">Psa 64:6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>moving his lips--or, "biting his lips"--a determined purpose (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#013">Pro 6:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#031">Proverbs 16:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 16:31</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#029">Pro 20:29</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>if--or, which may be supplied properly, or without it the sense is as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#010">Pro 4:10</a>, that piety is blessed with long life.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#032">Proverbs 16:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 16:32</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029">Pro 14:29</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>taketh a city--that is, by fighting.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#033">Proverbs 16:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 16:33</a></font></span></p>Seemingly the most fortuitous events are ordered by God.
 <p>
