 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#001">Proverbs 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 8:1</a></font></span></p>Contrasted with sensual allurements are the advantages of divine wisdom, which publicly invites men, offers the best principles of life, and the most valuable benefits resulting from receiving her counsels. Her relation to the divine plans and acts is introduced, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#019">Pro 3:19-20</a>, though more fully, to commend her desirableness for men, and the whole is closed by an assurance that those finding her find God's favor, and those neglecting ruin themselves. Many regard the passage as a description of the Son of God by the title, Wisdom, which the older Jews used (and by which He is called in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#049">Luk 11:49</a>), as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">Joh 1:1</a>, &amp;c., describes Him by that of Logos, the Word. But the passage may be taken as a personification of wisdom: for, (1) Though described as with God, wisdom is not asserted to be God. (2) The use of personal attributes is equally consistent with a personification, as with the description of a real person. (3) The personal pronouns used accord with the gender (feminine) of wisdom constantly, and are never changed to that of the person meant, as sometimes occurs in a corresponding use of spirit, which is neuter in Greek, but to which masculine pronouns are often applied (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#014">Joh 16:14</a>), when the acts of the Holy Spirit are described. (4) Such a personification is agreeable to the style of this book (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#020">Pro 1:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16-17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#008">Pro 4:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#020">Pro 6:20-22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Pro 9:1-4</a>), whereas no prophetical or other allusions to the Saviour or the new dispensation are found among the quotations of this book in the New Testament, and unless this be such, none exist. (5) Nothing is lost as to the importance of this passage, which still remains a most ornate and also solemn and impressive teaching of inspiration on the value of wisdom. (Pro. 8:1-36)
 <p>
 </p><p>The publicity and universality of the call contrast with the secrecy and intrigues of the wicked (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#008">Pro 7:8</a>, &amp;c.).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#005">Proverbs 8:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 8:5</a></font></span></p>wisdom--literally, "subtilty" in a good sense, or, "prudence." 
 <p>
 </p><p>fools--as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#022">Pro 1:22</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#006">Proverbs 8:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 8:6</a></font></span></p>excellent things--or, "plain," "manifest." 
 <p>
 </p><p>opening . . . things--upright words.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#007">Proverbs 8:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 8:7</a></font></span></p>For . . . truth--literally, "My palate shall meditate," or (as Orientals did) "mutter," my thoughts expressed only to myself are truth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>wickedness--specially falsehood, as opposed to truth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#008">Proverbs 8:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 8:8</a></font></span></p>in righteousness--or, "righteous" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#008">Psa 9:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa011.htm#007">Psa 11:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>froward--literally, "twisted," or contradictory, that is, to truth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#009">Proverbs 8:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 8:9</a></font></span></p>plain . . . understandeth--easily seen by those who apply their minds. 
 <p>
 </p><p>that find--implying search.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#010">Proverbs 8:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 8:10</a></font></span></p>not silver--preferable to it, so last clause implies comparison.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#011">Proverbs 8:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 8:11</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#014">Pro 3:14-15</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#012">Proverbs 8:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 8:12</a></font></span></p>prudence--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#005">Pro 8:5</a>. The connection of "wisdom" and "prudence" is that of the dictates of sound wisdom and its application. 
 <p>
 </p><p>find . . . inventions--or, "devices," "discreet ways" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Pro 1:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#013">Proverbs 8:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 8:13</a></font></span></p>For such is the effect of the fear of God, by which hatred to evil preserves from it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>froward mouth--or, "speech" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#012">Pro 2:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#014">Pro 6:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#014">Proverbs 8:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 8:14</a></font></span></p>It also gives the elements of good character in counsel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sound wisdom-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#007">Pro 2:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>I . . . strength--or, "As for me, understanding is strength to me," the source of power (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#016">Ecc 9:16</a>); good judgment gives more efficiency to actions;
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#015">Proverbs 8:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 8:15</a></font></span></p>of which a wisely conducted government is an example.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#017">Proverbs 8:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 8:17</a></font></span></p>early--or, "diligently," which may include the usual sense of early in life.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#018">Proverbs 8:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 8:18</a></font></span></p>durable riches . . . righteousness--Such are the "riches," enduring sources of happiness in moral possessions (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#019">Proverbs 8:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 8:19</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#011">Pro 8:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#020">Proverbs 8:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 8:20</a></font></span></p>The courses in which wisdom leads conduct to a true present prosperity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#005">Pro 23:5</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#022">Proverbs 8:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 8:22</a></font></span></p>Strictly, God's attributes are part of Himself. Yet, to the poetical structure of the whole passage, this commendation of wisdom is entirely consonant. In order of time all His attributes are coincident and eternal as Himself. But to set forth the importance of wisdom as devising the products of benevolence and power, it is here assigned a precedence. As it has such in divine, so should it be desired in human, affairs (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#019">Pro 3:19</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>possessed--or, "created"; in either sense, the idea of precedence. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>in the beginning--or simply, "beginning," in apposition with "me." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>before . . . of old--preceding the most ancient deeds.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#023">Proverbs 8:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 8:23</a></font></span></p>I was set up--ordained, or inaugurated (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>). The other terms carry out the idea of the earliest antiquity, and illustrate it by the details of creation [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#024">Pro 8:24-29</a>].
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#024">Proverbs 8:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 8:24</a></font></span></p>brought forth--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#002">Psa 90:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>abounding--or, "laden with water."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#025">Proverbs 8:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 8:25</a></font></span></p>settled--that is, sunk in foundations.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#026">Proverbs 8:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 8:26</a></font></span></p>fields--or, "out places," "deserts," as opposite to (habitable) "world." 
 <p>
 </p><p>highest part--or, "sum," all particles together,
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#027">Proverbs 8:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 8:27</a></font></span></p>when he set . . . depth--marked out the circle, according to the popular idea of the earth, as circular, surrounded by depths on which the visible concave heavens rested.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#028">Proverbs 8:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 8:28</a></font></span></p>established . . . deep--that is, so as to sustain the waters above and repress those below the firmament (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#007">Gen 1:7-11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job026.htm#008">Job 26:8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#029">Proverbs 8:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 8:29</a></font></span></p>commandment--better, the shore, that is, of the sea. 
 <p>
 </p><p>foundations--figuratively denotes the solid structure (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job038.htm#004">Job 38:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#002">Psa 24:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#030">Proverbs 8:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 8:30</a></font></span></p>one brought up--an object of special and pleasing regard. The bestowal of wisdom on men is represented by its finding a delightful residence and pleasing God.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#032">Proverbs 8:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 8:32</a></font></span></p>Such an attribute men are urged to seek.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#034">Proverbs 8:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">pro 8:34</a></font></span></p>watching . . . waiting--literally, "so as to watch"; wait, denoting a most sedulous attention.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#035">Proverbs 8:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">pro 8:35</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#023">Luk 13:23-24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#036">Proverbs 8:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">pro 8:36</a></font></span></p>sinneth . . . me--or better, "missing me," as opposed to "finding" [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#035">Pro 8:35</a>]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>love death--act as if they did (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#009">Pro 17:9</a>).
