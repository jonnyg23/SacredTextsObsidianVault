Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 28</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#001">Proverbs 28:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 28:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 28:1-28)
 <p>
 </p><p>A bad conscience makes men timid; the righteous are alone truly bold (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#026">Pro 14:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psa 27:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#002">Proverbs 28:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 28:2</a></font></span></p>Anarchy producing contending rulers shortens the reign of each. 
 <p>
 </p><p>but by a man . . . prolonged--or, "by a man of understanding--that is, a good ruler--he who knows or regards the right, that is, a good citizen, shall prolong (his days)." Good rulers are a blessing to the people. Bad government as a punishment for evil is contrasted with good as blessing to the good.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#003">Proverbs 28:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 28:3</a></font></span></p>A poor man, &amp;c.--Such, in power, exact more severely, and so leave subjects bare.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#004">Proverbs 28:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 28:4</a></font></span></p>They that forsake . . . wicked--Wrongdoers encourage one another.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#005">Proverbs 28:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 28:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#017">Joh 7:17</a>). Ignorance of moral truth is due to unwillingness to know it.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#006">Proverbs 28:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 28:6</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#006">Pro 10:6</a>). Riches cannot compensate for sin, nor the want of them affect integrity.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#007">Proverbs 28:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 28:7</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#025">Pro 17:25</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>riotous men--or, "gluttons" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#020">Pro 23:20-21</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#008">Proverbs 28:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 28:8</a></font></span></p>usury . . . unjust gain--(Compare Margin). The two terms, meaning nearly the same, may denote excessive interest. God's providence directs the proper use of wealth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#009">Proverbs 28:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 28:9</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#008">Pro 15:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#027">Pro 21:27</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hearing--that is, obeying. God requires sincere worshippers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#018">Psa 66:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#024">Joh 4:24</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#010">Proverbs 28:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 28:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#027">Pro 26:27</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#011">Proverbs 28:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 28:11</a></font></span></p>A poor but wise man can discover (and expose) the rich and self-conceited.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#012">Proverbs 28:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 28:12</a></font></span></p>great glory--or, cause for it to a people, for the righteous rejoice in good, and righteousness exalts a nation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#034">Pro 14:34</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>a man . . . hidden--that is, the good retire, or all kinds try to escape a wicked rule.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#013">Proverbs 28:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 28:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#003">Psa 32:3-5</a>). Concealment of sin delivers none from God's wrath, but He shows mercy to the humble penitent (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#004">Psa 51:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#014">Proverbs 28:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 28:14</a></font></span></p>feareth--that is, God, and so repents. 
 <p>
 </p><p>hardeneth his heart--makes himself insensible to sin, and so will not repent (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#016">Pro 14:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#001">Pro 29:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#015">Proverbs 28:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 28:15</a></font></span></p>The rapacity and cruelty of such beasts well represent some wicked men (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#002">Psa 7:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#012">Psa 17:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#016">Proverbs 28:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 28:16</a></font></span></p>The prince . . . understanding--that is, He does not perceive that oppression jeopards his success. Covetousness often produces oppression, hence the contrast.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#017">Proverbs 28:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 28:17</a></font></span></p>doeth violence . . . blood, &amp;c.--or, that is oppressed by the blood of life (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#006">Gen 9:6</a>), which he has taken. 
 <p>
 </p><p>to the pit--the grave or destruction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#012">Pro 1:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job033.htm#018">Job 33:18-24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#007">Psa 143:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>stay him--sustain or deliver him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#018">Proverbs 28:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 28:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#009">Pro 10:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#020">Pro 17:20</a>). Double dealing is eventually fatal.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#019">Proverbs 28:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 28:19</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#004">Pro 10:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#004">Pro 20:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>vain persons--idle, useless drones, implying that they are also wicked (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#011">Pro 12:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#004">Psa 26:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#020">Proverbs 28:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 28:20</a></font></span></p>maketh haste . . . rich--implying deceit or fraud (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#021">Pro 20:21</a>), and so opposed to "faithful" or reliable.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#021">Proverbs 28:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 28:21</a></font></span></p>respect of persons-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#023">Pro 24:23</a>). Such are led to evil by the slightest motive.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#022">Proverbs 28:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 28:22</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#020">Pro 28:20</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>evil eye--in the general sense of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#006">Pro 23:6</a>, here more specific for covetousness (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009">Pro 22:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#015">Mat 20:15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>poverty . . . him--by God's providence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#023">Proverbs 28:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 28:23</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008">Pro 9:8-9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#005">Pro 27:5</a>). Those benefited by reproof will love their monitors.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#024">Proverbs 28:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 28:24</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#004">Mat 15:4-6</a>). Such, though heirs, are virtually thieves, to be ranked with highwaymen.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#025">Proverbs 28:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 28:25</a></font></span></p>of a proud heart--literally, "puffed up of soul"--that is, self-confident, and hence overbearing and litigious. 
 <p>
 </p><p>made fat--or, "prosperous" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#025">Pro 11:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#020">Pro 16:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#026">Proverbs 28:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 28:26</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#006">Pro 3:6-8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>walketh wisely--that is, trusting in God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#017">Pro 22:17-19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#027">Proverbs 28:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 28:27</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#024">Pro 11:24-26</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>hideth his eyes--as the face (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#009">Psa 27:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#017">Psa 69:17</a>), denotes inattention.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#028">Proverbs 28:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 28:28</a></font></span></p>The elevation of the wicked to power drives men to seek refuge from tyranny (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#012">Pro 28:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#010">Pro 11:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa012.htm#008">Psa 12:8</a>).
