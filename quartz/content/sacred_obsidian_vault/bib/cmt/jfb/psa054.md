 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#001">Psalms 54:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 54:1</a></font></span></p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>, title; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psa 32:1</a>, title; for the history, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#019">Sa1 23:19</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#029">Sa1 23:29</a>; 1Sa. 26:1-25. After an earnest cry for help, the Psalmist promises praise in the assurance of a hearing. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#001">Psa 54:1-7</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>by thy name-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psa 5:11</a>), specially, power. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>judge me--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#008">Psa 7:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#001">Psa 26:1</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#002">Psalms 54:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 54:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psa 5:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#003">Psalms 54:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 54:3</a></font></span></p>strangers--perhaps Ziphites. 
 <p>
 </p><p>oppressors--literally, "terrible ones" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa013.htm#011">Isa 13:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa025.htm#003">Isa 25:3</a>). Such were Saul and his army. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>not set . . . them--acted as atheists, without God's fear (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#008">Psa 16:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#004">Psalms 54:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 54:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#010">Psa 30:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>with them--on their side, and for me (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#011">Psa 46:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#005">Psalms 54:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 54:5</a></font></span></p>He shall . . . evil--or, "Evil shall return on" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#016">Psa 7:16</a>) my enemies or watchers, that is, to do me evil (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007">Psa 6:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>in thy truth--Thy verified promise.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#006">Psalms 54:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 54:6</a></font></span></p>I will freely, &amp;c.--or, present a freewill offering (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev007.htm#016">Lev 7:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num015.htm#003">Num 15:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#007">Psalms 54:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 54:7</a></font></span></p>mine eye . . . desire--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa059.htm#010">Psa 59:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa112.htm#008">Psa 112:8</a>), expresses satisfaction in beholding the overthrow of his enemies as those of God, without implying any selfish or unholy feeling (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#006">Psa 52:6-7</a>).
