Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 25</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psalms 25:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 25:1</a></font></span></p>The general tone of this Psalm is that of prayer for help from enemies. Distress, however, exciting a sense of sin, humble confession, supplication for pardon, preservation from sin, and divine guidance, are prominent topics. (Psa. 25:1-22)
 <p>
 </p><p>lift up my soul-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#004">Psa 24:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#004">Psa 86:4</a>), set my affections (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#002">Col 3:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#002">Psalms 25:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 25:2</a></font></span></p>not be ashamed--by disappointment of hopes of relief.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#003">Psalms 25:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 25:3</a></font></span></p>The prayer generalized as to all who wait on God--that is, who expect His favor. On the other hand, the disappointment of the perfidious, who, unprovoked, have done evil, is invoked (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2022.htm#009">Sa2 22:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#004">Psalms 25:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 25:4</a></font></span></p>On the ground of former favor, he invokes divine guidance, according to God's gracious ways of dealing and faithfulness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#006">Psalms 25:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 25:6</a></font></span></p>Confessing past and present sins, he pleads for mercy, not on palliations of sin, but on God's well-known benevolence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#008">Psalms 25:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 25:8</a></font></span></p>upright--acting according to His promise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sinners--the general term, limited by the 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>meek--who are penitent. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>the way--and his way--God's way of providence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#009">Psalms 25:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 25:9</a></font></span></p>in judgment--rightly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#010">Psalms 25:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 25:10</a></font></span></p>paths--similar sense--His modes of dealing (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#004">Psa 25:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>mercy and truth-- (Job 14:1-22), God's grace in promising and faithfulness in performing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#011">Psalms 25:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 25:11</a></font></span></p>God's perfections of love, mercy, goodness, and truth are manifested (his name, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#010">Psa 9:10</a>) in pardoning sin, and the greatness of sin renders pardon more needed.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#012">Psalms 25:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 25:12</a></font></span></p>What he asks for himself is the common lot of all the pious.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#013">Psalms 25:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 25:13</a></font></span></p>inherit the earth--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#005">Mat 5:5</a>). The phrase, alluding to the promise of Canaan, expresses all the blessings included in that promise, temporal as well as spiritual.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#014">Psalms 25:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 25:14</a></font></span></p>The reason of the blessing explained--the pious enjoy communion with God (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#021">Pro 3:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#012">Pro 3:12</a>), and, of course, learn His gracious terms of pardon.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#015">Psalms 25:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 25:15</a></font></span></p>His trust in God is fixed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>net--is frequently used as a figure for dangers by enemies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#015">Psa 9:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#009">Psa 10:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#016">Psalms 25:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 25:16</a></font></span></p>A series of earnest appeals for aid because God had seemed to desert him (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001">Psa 13:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#013">Psa 17:13</a>, &amp;c.), his sins oppressed him, his enemies had enlarged his troubles and were multiplied, increasing in hate and violence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#008">Psa 9:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#048">Psa 18:48</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#020">Psalms 25:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 25:20</a></font></span></p>keep my soul-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#001">Psa 16:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>put my trust--flee for refuge (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012">Psa 2:12</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#021">Psalms 25:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 25:21</a></font></span></p>In conscious innocence of the faults charged by his enemies, he confidently commits his cause to God. Some refer-- 
 <p>
 </p><p>integrity, &amp;c.--to God, meaning His covenant faithfulness. This sense, though good, is an unusual application of the terms.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#022">Psalms 25:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 25:22</a></font></span></p>Extend these blessings to all Thy people in all their distresses.
