 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#001">Psalms 149:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 149:1</a></font></span></p>This Psalm sustains a close connection with the foregoing. The chosen people are exhorted to praise God, in view of past favors, and also future victories over enemies, of which they are impliedly assured. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#001">Psa 149:1-9</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa096.htm#001">Psa 96:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#002">Psalms 149:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 149:2</a></font></span></p>God had signalized His relation as a sovereign, in restoring them to their land.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#003">Psalms 149:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 149:3</a></font></span></p>in the dance-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#011">Psa 30:11</a>). The dance is connected with other terms, expressive of the great joy of the occasion. The word may be rendered "lute," to which the other instruments are joined. 
 <p>
 </p><p>sing praises--or, sing and play.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#004">Psalms 149:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 149:4</a></font></span></p>taketh pleasure--literally, "accepts," alluding to acceptance of propitiatory offerings (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#011">Psa 147:11</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>beautify, &amp;c.--adorn the humble with faith, hope, joy, and peace.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#005">Psalms 149:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 149:5</a></font></span></p>in glory--the honorable condition to which they are raised. 
 <p>
 </p><p>upon their beds--once a place of mourning (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#006">Psa 6:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#006">Psalms 149:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 149:6</a></font></span></p>high praises--or, "deeds." They shall go forth as religious warriors, as once religious laborers (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh004.htm#017">Neh 4:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#007">Psalms 149:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 149:7</a></font></span></p>The destruction of the incorrigibly wicked attends the propagation of God's truth, so that the military successes of the Jews, after the captivity, typified the triumphs of the Gospel.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa149.htm#009">Psalms 149:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 149:9</a></font></span></p>the judgment written--either in God's decrees, or perhaps as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#041">Deu 32:41-43</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>this honour--that is, to be thus employed, will be an honorable service, to be assigned 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>his saints--or, godly ones (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#003">Psa 16:3</a>).
 </p><p>
