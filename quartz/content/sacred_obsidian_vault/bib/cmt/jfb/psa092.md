Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 92</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#001">Psalms 92:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 92:1</a></font></span></p>A Psalm-song--(see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa030.htm#001">Psa 30:1</a>, title). The theme: God should be praised for His righteous judgments on the wicked and His care and defense of His people. Such a topic, at all times proper, is specially so for the reflections of the Sabbath day. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#001">Psa 92:1-15</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>sing . . . name--celebrate Thy perfections.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#002">Psalms 92:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 92:2</a></font></span></p>in the morning, . . . every night--diligently and constantly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#008">Psa 42:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>loving kindness--literally, "mercy." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>faithfulness--in fulfilling promises (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#014">Psa 89:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#003">Psalms 92:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 92:3</a></font></span></p>In such a work all proper aid must be used. 
 <p>
 </p><p>with a . . . sound--or, on Higgaion (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#016">Psa 9:16</a>), perhaps an instrument of that name, from its sound resembling the muttered sound of meditation, as expressed also by the word. This is joined with the harp.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#004">Psalms 92:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 92:4</a></font></span></p>thy work--that is, of providence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#016">Psa 90:16-17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#005">Psalms 92:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 92:5</a></font></span></p>great . . . works--correspond to deep or vast thoughts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#005">Psa 40:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#023">Rom 11:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#006">Psalms 92:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 92:6</a></font></span></p>A brutish man knoweth not--that is, God's works, so the Psalmist describes himself (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#022">Psa 73:22</a>) when amazed by the prosperity of the wicked, now understood and explained.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#008">Psalms 92:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 92:8</a></font></span></p>This he does in part, by contrasting their ruin with God's exaltation and eternity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>most high--as occupying the highest place in heaven (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#007">Psa 7:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#016">Psa 18:16</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#009">Psalms 92:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 92:9</a></font></span></p>A further contrast with the wicked, in the lot of the righteous, safety and triumph.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#010">Psalms 92:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 92:10</a></font></span></p>horn . . . exalt--is to increase power (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#005">Psa 75:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>anointed . . . fresh--or, "new" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>oil-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#005">Psa 23:5</a>) a figure for refreshment (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#046">Luk 7:46</a>). Such use of oil is still common in the East.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#011">Psalms 92:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 92:11</a></font></span></p>see . . . [and] . . . hear my desire--or, literally, "look on" my enemies and hear of the wicked (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#011">Psa 27:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa054.htm#007">Psa 54:7</a>) --that is, I shall be gratified by their fall.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#012">Psalms 92:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 92:12</a></font></span></p>The vigorous growth, longevity, utility, fragrance, and beauty of these noble trees, set forth the life, character, and destiny of the pious;
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa092.htm#015">Psalms 92:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 92:15</a></font></span></p>and they thus declare God's glory as their strong and righteous ruler.
