Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#001">Proverbs 15:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 15:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 15:1-33)
 <p>
 </p><p>soft--tender or gentle. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>turneth . . . wrath--from any one. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>stir up--as a smouldering fire is excited.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#002">Proverbs 15:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 15:2</a></font></span></p>useth . . . aright--commends knowledge by its proper use. 
 <p>
 </p><p>poureth out--utters abundantly (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#023">Pro 12:23</a>), and so disgusts others.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#003">Proverbs 15:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 15:3</a></font></span></p>beholding--watching (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#021">Pro 5:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#007">Psa 66:7</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#004">Proverbs 15:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 15:4</a></font></span></p>A wholesome tongue--(Compare Margin), pacifying and soothing language. 
 <p>
 </p><p>tree of life-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#018">Pro 3:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#030">Pro 11:30</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>perverseness therein--cross, ill-natured language. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>breach . . . spirit--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#014">Isa 65:14</a>, Hebrew), grieves, instead of appeasing.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#005">Proverbs 15:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 15:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001">Pro 4:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>; Pro. 13:1-18). 
 <p>
 </p><p>is prudent--acts discreetly.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#006">Proverbs 15:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 15:6</a></font></span></p>treasure--implying utility. 
 <p>
 </p><p>trouble--vexation and affliction.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#007">Proverbs 15:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 15:7</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#020">Pro 10:20-21</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>heart . . . not so--not right, or vain.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#008">Proverbs 15:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 15:8</a></font></span></p>The sacrifice [and] prayer--are acts of worship. 
 <p>
 </p><p>way . . . followeth . . . righteousness--denote conduct. God's regard for the worship and deeds of the righteous and wicked respectively, so stated in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#017">Psa 50:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#011">Isa 1:11</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#010">Proverbs 15:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 15:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>the way--that in which God would have him to go (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#013">Pro 2:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001">Psa 119:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#011">Proverbs 15:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 15:11</a></font></span></p>Hell-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#010">Psa 16:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>destruction--or, "Abaddon," the place of the destroyer. All the unseen world is open to God, much more men's hearts.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#012">Proverbs 15:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 15:12</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008">Pro 9:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>go unto the wise--to be instructed.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#013">Proverbs 15:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 15:13</a></font></span></p>maketh . . . countenance--or, "benefits the countenance." 
 <p>
 </p><p>spirit is broken--and so the countenance is sad.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#014">Proverbs 15:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 15:14</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#021">Pro 10:21-22</a>). The wise grow wiser, the fools more foolish (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#009">Pro 9:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#015">Proverbs 15:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 15:15</a></font></span></p>The state of the heart governs the outward condition. 
 <p>
 </p><p>evil--sad, contrasted with the cheerfulness of a feast.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#016">Proverbs 15:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 15:16</a></font></span></p>trouble--agitation, implying the anxieties and perplexities attending wealth held by worldlings (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018">Pro 16:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#006">Ti1 6:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#017">Proverbs 15:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 15:17</a></font></span></p>dinner--or, "allowance" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#030">Kg2 25:30</a>) -- 
 <p>
 </p><p>of herbs--and that the plainest. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and hatred--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#012">Pro 10:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#018">Pro 10:18</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#018">Proverbs 15:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 15:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029">Pro 14:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#032">Pro 16:32</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#019">Proverbs 15:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 15:19</a></font></span></p>The difficulties of the slothful result from want of energy; the righteous find a 
 <p>
 </p><p>plain [and open] way--literally, "a highway," by diligence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1010.htm#007">Sa1 10:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#003">Psa 1:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#020">Proverbs 15:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 15:20</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001">Pro 10:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#021">Proverbs 15:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 15:21</a></font></span></p>walketh uprightly--and so finds his joy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#006">Pro 3:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#023">Pro 10:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#022">Proverbs 15:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 15:22</a></font></span></p>Without counsel--or, "deliberation," implying a wise deference to the opinions of the wise and good, contrasted with rashness.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#023">Proverbs 15:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 15:23</a></font></span></p>Good advice blesses the giver and receiver.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#024">Proverbs 15:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 15:24</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#002">Col 3:2</a>). Holy purposes prevent sinning, and so its evils.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#025">Proverbs 15:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 15:25</a></font></span></p>The most desolate who have God's aid have more permanent good than the self-reliant sinner (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#022">Pro 2:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#007">Pro 12:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>border--or, "boundary for possessions" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#054">Psa 78:54</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#026">Proverbs 15:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 15:26</a></font></span></p>are pleasant words--that is, pleasing to God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#008">Pro 8:8-9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#027">Proverbs 15:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 15:27</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#017">Pro 11:17</a>). Avarice brings trouble to him and his. 
 <p>
 </p><p>hateth gifts--or, "bribes" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo023.htm#008">Exo 23:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#005">Psa 15:5</a>), and is not avaricious.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#028">Proverbs 15:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 15:28</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#014">Pro 15:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011">Pro 10:11</a>). Caution is the fruit of wisdom; rashness of folly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#029">Proverbs 15:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 15:29</a></font></span></p>far . . . wicked--in His love and favor (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#011">Psa 22:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#155">Psa 119:155</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#030">Proverbs 15:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 15:30</a></font></span></p>light of the eyes-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009">Pro 13:9</a>). What gives light rejoiceth the heart, by relieving from anxiety as to our course; so 
 <p>
 </p><p>good report--or, "doctrine" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa028.htm#009">Isa 28:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#001">Isa 53:1</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>maketh . . . fat--or, "gives prosperity" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#013">Pro 3:13-17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#011">Pro 9:11</a>). The last clause is illustrated by the first.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#031">Proverbs 15:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">pro 15:31</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017">Pro 10:17</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>reproof of life--which leads to life. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>abideth . . . wise--is numbered among them.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#032">Proverbs 15:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 15:32</a></font></span></p>refuseth--or, "neglects," "passes by" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#025">Pro 1:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#015">Pro 4:15</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>despiseth . . . soul--so acts as if esteeming its interests of no value.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#033">Proverbs 15:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 15:33</a></font></span></p>The fear . . . wisdom--Wisdom instructs in true piety. 
 <p>
 </p><p>before . . . humility--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#026">Luk 24:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#011">Pe1 1:11</a>); opposite (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018">Pro 16:18</a>).
 </p><p>
