Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 51</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#001">Psalms 51:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 51:1</a></font></span></p>On the occasion, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2011.htm#012">Sa2 11:12</a>. The Psalm illustrates true repentance, in which are comprised conviction, confession, sorrow, prayer for mercy, and purposes of amendment, and it is accompanied by a lively faith. (Psa. 51:1-19)
 <p>
 </p><p>A plea for mercy is a confession of guilt. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>blot out--as from a register. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>transgressions--literally, "rebellions" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#013">Psa 19:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001">Psa 32:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#002">Psalms 51:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 51:2</a></font></span></p>Wash me--Purity as well as pardon is desired by true penitents.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#003">Psalms 51:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 51:3</a></font></span></p>For . . . before me--Conviction precedes forgiveness; and, as a gift of God, is a plea for it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2012.htm#013">Sa2 12:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#005">Psa 32:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#009">Jo1 1:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#004">Psalms 51:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 51:4</a></font></span></p>Against thee--chiefly, and as sins against others are violations of God's law, in one sense only. 
 <p>
 </p><p>that . . . judgest--that is, all palliation of his crime is excluded; it is the design in making this confession to recognize God's justice, however severe the sentence.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#005">Psalms 51:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 51:5</a></font></span></p>His guilt was aggravated by his essential, native sinfulness, which is as contrary to God's requisitions of inward purity as are outward sins to those for right conduct.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#006">Psalms 51:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 51:6</a></font></span></p>thou shalt make, &amp;c.--may be taken to express God's gracious purpose in view of His strict requisition; a purpose of which David might have availed himself as a check to his native love for sin, and, in not doing so, aggravated his guilt. 
 <p>
 </p><p>truth . . . and . . .wisdom--are terms often used for piety (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job028.htm#028">Job 28:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#030">Psa 119:30</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#007">Psalms 51:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 51:7</a></font></span></p>A series of prayers for forgiveness and purifying. 
 <p>
 </p><p>Purge . . . hyssop--The use of this plant in the ritual (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#022">Exo 12:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num019.htm#006">Num 19:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num019.htm#018">Num 19:18</a>) suggests the idea of atonement as prominent here; "purge" refers to vicarious satisfaction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num019.htm#017">Num 19:17-20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#008">Psalms 51:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 51:8</a></font></span></p>Make . . . joy--by forgiving me, which will change distress to joy.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#009">Psalms 51:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 51:9</a></font></span></p>Hide, &amp;c.--Turn from beholding.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#010">Psalms 51:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 51:10</a></font></span></p>Create--a work of almighty power. 
 <p>
 </p><p>in me--literally, "to me," or, "for me"; bestow as a gift, a heart free from taint of sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#004">Psa 24:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa073.htm#001">Psa 73:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>renew--implies that he had possessed it; the essential principle of a new nature had not been lost, but its influence interrupted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#032">Luk 22:32</a>); for <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#011">Psa 51:11</a> shows that he had not lost God's presence and Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#013">Sa1 16:13</a>), though he had lost the "joy of his salvation" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#012">Psa 51:12</a>), for whose return he prays. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>right spirit--literally, "constant," "firm," not yielding to temptation.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#012">Psalms 51:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 51:12</a></font></span></p>free spirit--"thy" ought not to be supplied, for the word "free" is, literally, "willing," and "spirit" is that of David. "Let a willing spirit uphold me," that is, with a soul willingly conformed to God's law, he would be preserved in a right course of conduct.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#013">Psalms 51:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 51:13</a></font></span></p>Then--Such will be the effect of this gracious work. 
 <p>
 </p><p>ways--of providence and human duty (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#021">Psa 18:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#030">Psa 18:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#008">Psa 32:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#032">Luk 22:32</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#014">Psalms 51:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 51:14</a></font></span></p>Deliver--or, "Free me" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#008">Psa 39:8</a>) from the guilt of murder (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2012.htm#009">Sa2 12:9-10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#006">Psa 5:6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#017">Psa 7:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#001">Psa 31:1</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#015">Psalms 51:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 51:15</a></font></span></p>open . . . lips--by removing my sense of guilt.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#016">Psalms 51:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 51:16</a></font></span></p>Praise is better than sacrifice (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#014">Psa 50:14</a>), and implying faith, penitence, and love, glorifies God. In true penitents the joys of pardon mingle with sorrow for sin.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#018">Psalms 51:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 51:18</a></font></span></p>Do good, &amp;c.--Visit not my sin on Thy Church. 
 <p>
 </p><p>build . . . walls--is to show favor; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#040">Psa 89:40</a>, for opposite form and idea.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#019">Psalms 51:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 51:19</a></font></span></p>God reconciled, material sacrifices will be acceptable (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#005">Psa 4:5</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#011">Isa 1:11-17</a>).
