Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001">1 Chronicles 1:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 1:1</a></font></span></p>ADAM'S LINE TO NOAH. (1Ch. 1:1-23)
 <p>
 </p><p>Adam, &amp;c.--"Begat" must be understood. Only that one member of the family is mentioned, who came in the direct order of succession.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#004">1 Chronicles 1:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">ch1 1:4</a></font></span></p>Noah, Shem, Ham, and Japheth--The three sons of this patriarch are enumerated, partly because they were the founders of the new world, and partly because the fulfilment of Noah's prophecy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#025">Gen 9:25-27</a>) could not otherwise appear to have been verified.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#012">1 Chronicles 1:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">ch1 1:12</a></font></span></p>Casluhim (of whom came the Philistines), and Caphtorim--a better rendering is, "and Casluhim, of whom came the Philistim and Caphtorim." They were brethren, the sons of Casluhim, and at first dwelt together, whence their names are used interchangeably. The Caphtorim are described as inhabiting Azzah, or Gaza, the seat of the Philistines.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#014">1 Chronicles 1:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">ch1 1:14</a></font></span></p>the Jebusite, &amp;c.--At <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#014">Ch1 1:14-17</a> the names are not those of individuals, but of people who all sprang from Canaan; and as several of them became extinct or were amalgamated with their brethren, their national appellations are given instead of the personal names of their ancestors.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#017">1 Chronicles 1:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ch1 1:17</a></font></span></p>Uz, and Hul, and Gether, and Meshech--or, "Mash"; these were the children of Aram, and grandsons of Shem (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#023">Gen 10:23</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#018">1 Chronicles 1:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">ch1 1:18</a></font></span></p>Arphaxad begat Shelah--Cainan, the father's name, is omitted here. (See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#036">Luk 3:36</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#019">1 Chronicles 1:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">ch1 1:19</a></font></span></p>Peleg--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#025">Gen 10:25</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#022">1 Chronicles 1:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">ch1 1:22</a></font></span></p>Ebal--or, "Obal" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#028">Gen 10:28</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#024">1 Chronicles 1:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">ch1 1:24</a></font></span></p>SHEM'S LINE TO ABRAHAM. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#024">Ch1 1:24-28</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Shem, &amp;c.--This comprises a list of ten, inclusive of Abraham.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#029">1 Chronicles 1:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">ch1 1:29</a></font></span></p>SONS OF ISHMAEL. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#029">Ch1 1:29-31</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>These are their generations--the heads of his twelve tribes. The great northern desert of Arabia, including the entire neck, was colonized by these tribes; and if we can recover, in the modern geography of this part of the country, Arab tribes bearing the names of those patriarchs, that is, names corresponding with those preserved in the original catalogue of Scripture, we obtain at once so many evidences, not of mere similarity, but of absolute identification [FORSTER]. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Nebaioth--gave rise to the NabathÃ&amp;brvbr;ans of the classic, and the Beni Nabat of Oriental writers. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Kedar--the Arab tribe, El Khedeyre, on the coast of Hedgar. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Abdeel--Abdilla, the name of a tribe in Yemen.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#030">1 Chronicles 1:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">ch1 1:30</a></font></span></p>Dumah--Dumah and Tema, the great Arab tribes of Beni Teman. Thus this writer [Historical Geography of Arabia] traces the names of all the heads of the twelve tribes of Ishmael as perpetuated in the clans or tribes of the Arabs in the present day.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#032">1 Chronicles 1:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">ch1 1:32</a></font></span></p>SONS OF KETURAH. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#032">Ch1 1:32-33</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>sons of Keturah--These became founders of nomadic tribes in the north of Arabia and Syria, as Midian of the Midianites (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen036.htm#035">Gen 36:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#002">Jdg 6:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>and Shuah--from whom Bildad sprang (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#011">Job 2:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#036">1 Chronicles 1:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">ch1 1:36</a></font></span></p>POSTERITY OF ABRAHAM BY ESAU. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#034">Ch1 1:34-42</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>sons of Eliphaz--the tribe Adites, in the center country of the Saracens, so called from his mother, Adah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen036.htm#010">Gen 36:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Teman--gave rise to the land of Teman, near the head of the Red Sea. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Omar--the tribe Beni-Amma, settled at the northern point of Djebel Shera (Mount Seir). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Zephi--the tribe Dzaf. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Gatam--Katam, inhabited by the tribe Al Saruat, or "people of Sarah." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Kenaz--the tribe Aenezes, a tribe whose settlement lies in the neighborhood of Syria. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Amalek--the Beni Malak of Zohran, and the Beni Maledj of the Shat el Arab.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#037">1 Chronicles 1:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">ch1 1:37</a></font></span></p>Reuel--a powerful branch of the great Aeneze tribe, the Rowalla Arabs. 
 <p>
 </p><p>Shammah--the great tribe Beni Shammar. In the same way, the names of the other kings and dukes are traced in the modern tribes of Arabia. But it is unnecessary to mention any more of these obscure nomads, except to notice that Jobab (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#044">Ch1 1:44</a>), one of the kings of Edom, is considered to be Job, and that his seat was in the royal city of Dinahab (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen036.htm#032">Gen 36:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#043">Ch1 1:43</a>), identified with O'Daeb, a well-known town in the center of Al Dahna, a great northern desert in the direction of Chaldea and the Euphrates [FORSTER].
 </p><p>
