Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001">Proverbs 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 2:1</a></font></span></p>Men are invited to seek wisdom because it teaches those principles by which they may obtain God's guidance and avoid the society and influence of the wicked, whose pernicious courses are described. (Pro. 2:1-22)
 <p>
 </p><p>Diligence in hearing and praying for instruction must be used to secure the great principle of godliness, the fear of God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>hide . . . with thee--lay up in store (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#001">Pro 7:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#002">Proverbs 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 2:2</a></font></span></p>Listen attentively and reflect seriously (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#024">Pro 1:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#002">Psa 130:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>understanding--right perception of truth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#003">Proverbs 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 2:3</a></font></span></p>Yea, if--literally, "When if," that is, in such a case. 
 <p>
 </p><p>knowledge--or, "discrimination." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>understanding--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#002">Pro 2:2</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#004">Proverbs 2:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 2:4</a></font></span></p>There must be earnest prayer and effort.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#005">Proverbs 2:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 2:5</a></font></span></p>understand--or, "perceive intelligently." 
 <p>
 </p><p>find--obtain.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#006">Proverbs 2:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 2:6</a></font></span></p>For--God is ready (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#005">Jam 1:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#008">Jam 4:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>out of his mouth--by revelation from Him.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#007">Proverbs 2:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 2:7</a></font></span></p>sound wisdom--literally, "substance," opposed to what is fictitious. According to the context, this may be assistance, as here corresponding with 
 <p>
 </p><p>buckler--or safety, or wisdom, which procures it (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#021">Pro 3:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#014">Pro 8:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#001">Pro 18:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job006.htm#013">Job 6:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job012.htm#013">Job 12:13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>layeth up--provides, ever ready.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#008">Proverbs 2:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 2:8</a></font></span></p>keepeth . . . way--God defends the right way, and those in it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>saints--objects of favor (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psa 4:3</a>, &amp;c.). He guides and guards them.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#009">Proverbs 2:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 2:9</a></font></span></p>Then--emphatic, in such a case. 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness . . . path--all parts of duty to God and man.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#010">Proverbs 2:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 2:10</a></font></span></p>Idea of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#009">Pro 2:9</a>, amplified; on terms, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#002">Pro 2:2</a> and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#004">Pro 2:4</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#012">Proverbs 2:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 2:12</a></font></span></p>To deliver--as from great danger (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#005">Pro 6:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>way . . . man-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001">Psa 1:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>froward things--perversity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#014">Pro 6:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#023">Pro 23:23</a>), what is opposed to truth.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#013">Proverbs 2:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 2:13</a></font></span></p>paths of uprightness--or, "plainness." 
 <p>
 </p><p>walk--habitually act;
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#014">Proverbs 2:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 2:14</a></font></span></p>and that with pleasure, in ignorance of good and pursuit of evil. 
 <p>
 </p><p>frowardness--Not only their own perversity, but that of others is their delight. They love most the worst things.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#015">Proverbs 2:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 2:15</a></font></span></p>crooked--tortuous, unprincipled. 
 <p>
 </p><p>froward--literally, (they) are going back, not only aside from right, but opposite to it.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#016">Proverbs 2:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 2:16</a></font></span></p>Deliverance from another danger. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the strange woman--This term is often used for harlot, or loose woman (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg011.htm#001">Jdg 11:1-2</a>), married (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#005">Pro 7:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#019">Pro 7:19</a>) or not (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1011.htm#001">Kg1 11:1</a>), so called, because such were, perhaps at first, foreigners, though "strange" may also denote whatever is opposed to right or proper, as "strange fire" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num003.htm#004">Num 3:4</a>); "strange incense" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#009">Exo 30:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>flattereth--literally, "smooths." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>her words-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#009">Psa 5:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#017">Proverbs 2:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 2:17</a></font></span></p>guide . . . youth--lawful husband (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#004">Jer 3:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>covenant . . . God--of marriage made in God's name.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#018">Proverbs 2:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 2:18</a></font></span></p>inclineth--sinks down (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num013.htm#031">Num 13:31</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>the dead--or shades of the departed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#010">Psa 88:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#019">Proverbs 2:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 2:19</a></font></span></p>that is, such as remain impenitent (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc007.htm#026">Ecc 7:26</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>paths of life-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#011">Psa 16:11</a>), opposed to paths unto the dead.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#020">Proverbs 2:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 2:20</a></font></span></p>That . . . way of good--that is, Such is the object of these warnings.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#021">Proverbs 2:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 2:21</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#003">Psa 37:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#009">Psa 37:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#022">Psa 37:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#027">Psa 37:27</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#022">Proverbs 2:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 2:22</a></font></span></p>transgressors--or impious rebels (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer009.htm#002">Jer 9:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>rooted out--utterly destroyed, as trees plucked up by the roots.
 </p><p>
