Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 20</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001">Proverbs 20:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 20:1</a></font></span></p>(Pro. 20:1-30)
 <p>
 </p><p>mocker--scorner. Such men are made by wine. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>strong drink--made by spicing wine (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#011">Isa 5:11</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#022">Isa 5:22</a>); and it may include wine. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>raging--or boisterous as a drunkard. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>deceived--literally, "erring," or reeling.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002">Proverbs 20:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 20:2</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#012">Pro 19:12</a>). Men who resist authority injure themselves (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#002">Rom 13:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#003">Proverbs 20:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 20:3</a></font></span></p>to cease from strife--or, better, "to dwell from or without strife," denoting the habit of life. 
 <p>
 </p><p>fool . . . meddling-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#014">Pro 17:14</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#004">Proverbs 20:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 20:4</a></font></span></p>shall . . . beg--literally, "ask" (in this sense, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa109.htm#010">Psa 109:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#005">Proverbs 20:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 20:5</a></font></span></p>Counsel . . . water--that is, deeply hidden (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#004">Pro 18:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#002">Psa 13:2</a>). The wise can discern well.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#006">Proverbs 20:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 20:6</a></font></span></p>Boasters are unreliable. 
 <p>
 </p><p>goodness--or, "kind disposition."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#007">Proverbs 20:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 20:7</a></font></span></p>The conduct of good men proclaims their sound principles. God's covenant and their good example secure blessing to their children (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#026">Pro 4:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa112.htm#001">Psa 112:1-2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#008">Proverbs 20:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 20:8</a></font></span></p>As in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#035">Pro 14:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#010">Pro 16:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#015">Pro 16:15</a>, this is the character of a good king, not of all kings.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#009">Proverbs 20:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 20:9</a></font></span></p>The interrogation in the affirmative strengthens the implied negation (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#014">Job 15:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc007.htm#020">Ecc 7:20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#010">Proverbs 20:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 20:10</a></font></span></p>Various measures, implying that some are wrong (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001">Pro 11:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#011">Pro 16:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#011">Proverbs 20:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 20:11</a></font></span></p>The conduct of children even is the best test of principle (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#016">Mat 7:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#012">Proverbs 20:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 20:12</a></font></span></p>Hence, of course, God will know all you do (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#009">Psa 94:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#013">Proverbs 20:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 20:13</a></font></span></p>Activity and diligence contrasted with sloth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#009">Pro 6:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011">Pro 10:11</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>lest . . . poverty--literally, "be deprived of inheritance."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#014">Proverbs 20:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 20:14</a></font></span></p>when . . . his way--implying that he goes about boasting of his bargains.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#015">Proverbs 20:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 20:15</a></font></span></p>The contrast denotes the greater value of knowledge (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#014">Pro 3:14-16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#016">Proverbs 20:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 20:16</a></font></span></p>Take his garment--implies severe exaction, justified by the surety's rashness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a strange woman--by some readings "strangers," but the former here, and in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#013">Pro 27:13</a>, is allowable, and strengthens the sense. The debauchee is less reliable than the merely careless.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#017">Proverbs 20:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 20:17</a></font></span></p>Bread . . . sweet--either as unlawfully (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#017">Pro 9:17</a>) or easily obtained. 
 <p>
 </p><p>mouth . . . gravel--well expresses the pain and grief given at last.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#018">Proverbs 20:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 20:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#022">Pro 15:22</a>). Be careful and considerate in important plans.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#019">Proverbs 20:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 20:19</a></font></span></p>Those who love to tell news will hardly keep secrets. 
 <p>
 </p><p>flattereth . . . lips--(compare Margin; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#010">Pro 1:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>meddle . . . him--literally, "join," or "associate with."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#020">Proverbs 20:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 20:20</a></font></span></p>his lamp--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009">Pro 13:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#020">Pro 24:20</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#021">Proverbs 20:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 20:21</a></font></span></p>gotten hastily--contrary to God's providence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#020">Pro 28:20</a>), implying its unjust or easy attainment; hence the man is punished, or spends freely what he got easily (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#017">Pro 20:17</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#022">Proverbs 20:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 20:22</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#014">Psa 27:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#017">Rom 12:17-19</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#023">Proverbs 20:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 20:23</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#010">Pro 20:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001">Pro 11:1</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#024">Proverbs 20:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 20:24</a></font></span></p>Man's goings--literally, "Stately steppings of a strong man." 
 <p>
 </p><p>a man--any common man. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>understand--or, "perceive."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#025">Proverbs 20:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 20:25</a></font></span></p>devoureth . . . holy--or, better, "who rashly speaks promises," or "devotes what is holy," consecrating any thing. This suits better the last clause, which expresses a similar view of the results of rashly vowing.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#026">Proverbs 20:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 20:26</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#008">Pro 20:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>bringeth . . . over them--The wheel was used for threshing grain. The figure denotes severity (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#003">Amo 1:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#027">Proverbs 20:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 20:27</a></font></span></p>The spirit . . . Lord--Men's minds are God's gifts, and thus able to search one another (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#005">Pro 20:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008">Pro 18:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#017">Pro 18:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#011">Co1 2:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#028">Proverbs 20:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 20:28</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#003">Pro 3:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#006">Pro 16:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#012">Pro 16:12</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#029">Proverbs 20:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 20:29</a></font></span></p>The glory of young men . . . the beauty of old men--Each age has its peculiar excellence (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#031">Pro 16:31</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#030">Proverbs 20:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">pro 20:30</a></font></span></p>blueness--literally, "joining," the process of uniting the edges of a wound throws off purulent matter. 
 <p>
 </p><p>stripes . . . belly--So punishment provides healing of soul (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008">Pro 18:8</a>), by deterring from evil courses.
 </p><p>
