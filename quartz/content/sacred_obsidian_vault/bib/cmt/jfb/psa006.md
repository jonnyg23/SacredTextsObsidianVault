Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psalms 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 6:1</a></font></span></p>On Neginoth (See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>, title) upon Sheminith--the eighth--an instrument for the eighth key; or, more probably, the bass, as it is contrasted with Alamoth (the treble, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001">Psa 46:1</a>) in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#020">Ch1 15:20-21</a>. In deep affliction the Psalmist appeals to God's mercy for relief from chastisement, which otherwise must destroy him, and thus disable him for God's service. Sure of a gracious answer, he triumphantly rebukes his foes. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psa 6:1-10</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>He owns his ill desert in begging a relief from chastisement.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#002">Psalms 6:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 6:2</a></font></span></p>I am weak--as a culled plant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa024.htm#004">Isa 24:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>my bones--the very frame. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>are vexed-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#005">Psa 2:5</a>) --shaken with fear.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#003">Psalms 6:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 6:3</a></font></span></p>how long?--shall this be so (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#005">Psa 79:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>but--or, "and." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>thou--The sentence is incomplete as expressive of strong emotion.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#004">Psalms 6:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 6:4</a></font></span></p>Return--that is, to my relief; or, "turn," as now having His face averted. 
 <p>
 </p><p>for thy mercies' sake--to illustrate Thy mercy.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#005">Psalms 6:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 6:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa115.htm#017">Psa 115:17-18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa038.htm#018">Isa 38:18</a>). There is no incredulity as to a future state. The contrast is between this scene of life, and the grave or Sheol, the unseen world of the dead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>give . . . thanks--or, "praise for mercies."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#006">Psalms 6:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 6:6</a></font></span></p>By a strong figure the abundance as well as intensity of grief is depicted.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007">Psalms 6:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 6:7</a></font></span></p>consumed--or, "has failed," denoting general debility (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#003">Psa 13:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#010">Psa 38:10</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>waxeth old--or, "dim." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>grief--mingled with indignation.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#008">Psalms 6:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 6:8</a></font></span></p>Assured of God's hearing, he suddenly defies his enemies by an address indicating that he no longer fears them.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#010">Psalms 6:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 6:10</a></font></span></p>and knows they will be disappointed and in their turn (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#003">Psa 6:3</a>) be terror-stricken or confounded.
