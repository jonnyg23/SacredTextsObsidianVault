 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#001">Psalms 113:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 113:1</a></font></span></p>God's majesty contrasted with His condescension and gracious dealings towards the humble furnish matter and a call for praise. The Jews, it is said, used this and Psalms 114-118 on their great festivals, and called them the Greater Hallel, or Hymn. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#001">Psa 113:1-9</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Earnestness and zeal are denoted by the emphatic repetitions. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>servants of the Lord--or, all the people of God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>name of the Lord--perfections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psa 5:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#009">Psa 111:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#003">Psalms 113:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 113:3</a></font></span></p>From the rising, &amp;c.--all the world.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#004">Psalms 113:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 113:4</a></font></span></p>God's exaltation enhances His condescension;
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#007">Psalms 113:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 113:7</a></font></span></p>which condescension is illustrated as often in raising the worthy poor and needy to honor (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1002.htm#008">Sa1 2:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#025">Psa 44:25</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa113.htm#009">Psalms 113:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 113:9</a></font></span></p>On this special case, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1002.htm#021">Sa1 2:21</a>. Barrenness was regarded as a disgrace, and is a type of a deserted Church (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa054.htm#001">Isa 54:1</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>the barren woman . . . house--literally, "the barren of the house," so that the supplied words may be omitted.
