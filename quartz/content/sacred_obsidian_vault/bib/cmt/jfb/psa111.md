Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 111</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#001">Psalms 111:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 111:1</a></font></span></p>The Psalmist celebrates God's gracious dealings with His people, of which a summary statement is given. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#001">Psa 111:1-10</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Praise ye the Lord--or, Hallelujah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#035">Psa 104:35</a>). This seems to serve as a title to those of the later Psalms, which, like this, set forth God's gracious government and its blessed fruits. This praise claims the 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>whole heart-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#012">Psa 86:12</a>), and is rendered publicly. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>upright--a title of the true Israel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#011">Psa 32:11</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#002">Psalms 111:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 111:2</a></font></span></p>His works, that is, of providence and grace are 
 <p>
 </p><p>sought--or, carefully studied, by all desiring to know them.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#003">Psalms 111:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 111:3</a></font></span></p>honourable and glorious--literally, "honor and majesty," which illustrate His glorious perfections. 
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#017">Psa 7:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#001">Psa 31:1</a>), which He has made memorable by wonders of love and mercy, in supplying the wants of His people according to covenant engagements.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#006">Psalms 111:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 111:6</a></font></span></p>His power was shown especially in giving them the promised land, and His faithfulness and justice thus displayed are, like His precepts, reliable and of permanent obligation.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#009">Psalms 111:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 111:9</a></font></span></p>The deliverance He provided accorded to His established covenant. Thus He manifested Himself in the sum of His perfections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#001">Psa 20:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#007">Psa 20:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#003">Psa 22:3</a>) worthy of reverence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#010">Psalms 111:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 111:10</a></font></span></p>And hence love and fear of such a God is the chief element of true wisdom (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#007">Pro 1:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#010">Pro 9:10</a>).
