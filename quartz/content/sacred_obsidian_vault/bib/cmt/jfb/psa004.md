Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psalms 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 4:1</a></font></span></p>On Neginoth, that is, stringed instruments, as the kind of musical accompaniment. On other parts of title, see Introduction., The historical occasion was probably the same as that of the foregoing [see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#001">Psa 3:1</a>]. The writer, praying for further relief, admonishes his enemies of the vanity of attacking God's servant, exhorts them to repentance, and avows his confidence and peace in God's favor. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>Hear--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#004">Psa 3:4</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>God of my righteousness--or, "my righteous God, as my holy hill" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>), who will act towards me on righteous principles. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>thou hast enlarged--expresses relief afforded in opposition to "distress," which is expressed by a word denoting straits or pressure. Past favor is a ground of hope for the future.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#002">Psalms 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 4:2</a></font></span></p>sons of men--men of note or prominence (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2021.htm#009">Ch2 21:9</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>turn my glory--or, "royal dignity." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>into shame--or, "reproach." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>vanity--a foolish and hopeless enterprise (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#001">Psa 2:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>leasing--a lie.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#003">Psalms 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 4:3</a></font></span></p>godly--an object as well as subject of divine favor (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa105.htm#014">Psa 105:14-15</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#004">Psalms 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 4:4</a></font></span></p>Stand in awe-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#026">Eph 4:26</a>), from Septuagint, "be angry." Both clauses are qualified by "not."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#005">Psalms 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 4:5</a></font></span></p>Not only repent, but manifest penitence by sacrifices or righteousness or righteous sacrifices, &amp;c.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#006">Psalms 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 4:6</a></font></span></p>Contrast true with vain confidence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>light of thy countenance upon us--figure for favor (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#026">Num 6:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#003">Psa 44:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#016">Psa 81:16</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#007">Psalms 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 4:7</a></font></span></p>corn and wine--literally, "new corn and wine." 
 <p>
 </p><p>increased--an abundant harvest giving great joy (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#003">Isa 9:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#008">Psalms 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 4:8</a></font></span></p>both lay me down, &amp;c.--or, will lie down at once, and sleep in sure confidence and quiet repose (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#005">Psa 3:5</a>).
