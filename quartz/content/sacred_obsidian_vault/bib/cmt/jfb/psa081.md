Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 81</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#001">Psalms 81:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 81:1</a></font></span></p>Gittith--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#001">Psa 8:1</a>, title). A festal Psalm, probably for the passover (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#030">Mat 26:30</a>), in which, after an exhortation to praise God, He is introduced, reminding Israel of their obligations, chiding their neglect, and depicting the happy results of obedience. (Psa. 81:1-16)
 <p>
 </p><p>our strength-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#007">Psa 38:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#002">Psalms 81:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 81:2</a></font></span></p>unites the most joyful kinds of music, vocal and instrumental.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#003">Psalms 81:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 81:3</a></font></span></p>the new moon--or the month. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the time appointed--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#020">Pro 7:20</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#005">Psalms 81:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 81:5</a></font></span></p>a testimony--The feasts, especially the passover, attested God's relation to His people. 
 <p>
 </p><p>Joseph--for Israel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa080.htm#001">Psa 80:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>went out through--or, "over," that is, Israel in the exodus. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>I heard--change of person. The writer speaks for the nation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>language--literally, "lip" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#001">Psa 14:1</a>). An aggravation or element of their distress that their oppressors were foreigners (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#049">Deu 28:49</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#006">Psalms 81:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 81:6</a></font></span></p>God's language alludes to the burdensome slavery of the Israelites.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#007">Psalms 81:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 81:7</a></font></span></p>secret place--the cloud from which He troubled the Egyptians (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#024">Exo 14:24</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>proved thee-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#010">Psa 7:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#003">Psa 17:3</a>) --tested their faith by the miracle.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#008">Psalms 81:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 81:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#007">Psa 50:7</a>). The reproof follows to <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#012">Psa 81:12</a>. 
 <p>
 </p><p>if thou wilt hearken--He then propounds the terms of His covenant: they should worship Him alone, who (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#010">Psa 81:10</a>) had delivered them, and would still confer all needed blessings.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#011">Psalms 81:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 81:11</a></font></span></p>They failed, and He gave them up to their own desires and hardness of heart (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu029.htm#018">Deu 29:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#030">Pro 1:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#025">Rom 11:25</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa081.htm#013">Psalms 81:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 81:13</a></font></span></p>Obedience would have secured all promised blessings and the subjection of foes. In this passage, "should have," "would have," &amp;c., are better, "should" and "would" expressing God's intention at the time, that is, when they left Egypt.
