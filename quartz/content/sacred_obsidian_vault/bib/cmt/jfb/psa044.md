Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 44</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#001">Psalms 44:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 44:1</a></font></span></p>In a time of great national distress, probably in David's reign, the Psalmist recounts God's gracious dealings in former times, and the confidence they had learned to repose in Him. After a vivid picture of their calamities, he humbly expostulates against God's apparent forgetfulness, reminding Him of their faithfulness and mourning their heavy sorrows. (Psa. 44:1-26)
 <p>
 </p><p>This period is that of the settlement of Canaan (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos024.htm#012">Jos 24:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#003">Jdg 6:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>have told--or, "related" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo010.htm#002">Exo 10:2</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#002">Psalms 44:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 44:2</a></font></span></p>plantedst them--that is, "our fathers," who are also, from the parallel construction of the last clause, to be regarded as the object of "cast them out," which means--literally, "send" them out, or, "extend them." Heathen and people denote the nations who were driven out to make room for the Israelites.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#004">Psalms 44:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 44:4</a></font></span></p>Thou art my King--literally, "he who is my King," sustaining the same covenant relation as to the "fathers."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#005">Psalms 44:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 44:5</a></font></span></p>The figure drawn from the habits of the ox.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#006">Psalms 44:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 44:6</a></font></span></p>God is not only our sole help, but only worthy of praise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#007">Psalms 44:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 44:7</a></font></span></p>put . . . to shame--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#010">Psa 6:10</a>), disgraced.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#008">Psalms 44:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 44:8</a></font></span></p>thy name--as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psa 5:11</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#009">Psalms 44:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 44:9</a></font></span></p>But--contrasting, cast off as abhorrent (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa043.htm#002">Psa 43:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>goest not forth--literally, "will not go" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#023">Sa2 5:23</a>). In several consecutive verses the leading verb is future, and the following one past (in Hebrew), thus denoting the causes and effects. Thus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#010">Psa 44:10-12</a>), when defeated, spoiling follows; when delivered as sheep, dispersion follows, &amp;c.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#011">Psalms 44:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 44:11</a></font></span></p>The Babylonian captivity not necessarily meant. There were others (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#046">Kg1 8:46</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#013">Psalms 44:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 44:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#037">Deu 28:37</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#004">Psa 79:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#015">Psalms 44:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 44:15</a></font></span></p>shame of . . . face--blushes in disgrace.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#016">Psalms 44:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 44:16</a></font></span></p>Its cause, the taunts and presence of malignant enemies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#002">Psa 8:2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#017">Psalms 44:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 44:17</a></font></span></p>They had not apostatized totally--were still God's people.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#018">Psalms 44:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 44:18</a></font></span></p>declined--turned aside from God's law.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#019">Psalms 44:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 44:19</a></font></span></p>sore broken--crushed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>place of dragons--desolate, barren, rocky wilderness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#010">Psa 63:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa013.htm#022">Isa 13:22</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>shadow of death--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#004">Psa 23:4</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#020">Psalms 44:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">psa 44:20</a></font></span></p>A solemn appeal to God to witness their constancy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>stretched out . . . hands--gesture of worship (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo009.htm#029">Exo 9:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#009">Psa 88:9</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#022">Psalms 44:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">psa 44:22</a></font></span></p>Their protracted sufferings as God's people attests the constancy. Paul (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#036">Rom 8:36</a>) uses this to describe Christian steadfastness in persecution.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#023">Psalms 44:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">psa 44:23</a></font></span></p>This style of addressing God, as indifferent, is frequent (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#007">Psa 3:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#019">Psa 9:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001">Psa 13:1</a>, &amp;c.). However low their condition, God is appealed to, on the ground, and for the honor, of His mercy.
