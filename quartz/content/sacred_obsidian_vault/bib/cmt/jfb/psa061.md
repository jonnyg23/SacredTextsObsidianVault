 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#001">Psalms 61:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 61:1</a></font></span></p>Neginah--or, Neginoth (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001">Psa 4:1</a>, title). Separated from his usual spiritual privileges, perhaps by Absalom's rebellion, the Psalmist prays for divine aid, and, in view of past mercies, with great confidence of being heard. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#001">Psa 61:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>From the end--that is, places remote from the sanctuary (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#064">Deu 28:64</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#002">Psalms 61:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 61:2</a></font></span></p>heart is overwhelmed--literally, "covered over with darkness," or, "distress." 
 <p>
 </p><p>to the rock-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#002">Psa 40:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>higher than I--which otherwise I cannot ascend.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#003">Psalms 61:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 61:3</a></font></span></p>shelter . . . and strong tower--repeat the same sentiment.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#004">Psalms 61:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 61:4</a></font></span></p>I will abide--So I desire to do (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#006">Psa 23:6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>trust in the covert, &amp;c.--make my refuge, in the shadow (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#008">Psa 17:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#007">Psa 36:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#005">Psalms 61:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 61:5</a></font></span></p>the heritage--or, part in the spiritual blessings of Israel (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa021.htm#002">Psa 21:2-4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>vows--implies prayers.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#006">Psalms 61:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 61:6</a></font></span></p>the king--himself and his royal line ending in Christ. Mercy and truth personified, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#011">Psa 40:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#003">Psa 57:3</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#007">Psalms 61:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 61:7</a></font></span></p>abide before God--literally, "sit as a king in God's presence," under His protection.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#008">Psalms 61:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 61:8</a></font></span></p>Thus for new blessings will new vows of praise ever be paid.
