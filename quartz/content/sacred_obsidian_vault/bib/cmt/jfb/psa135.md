Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 135</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#001">Psalms 135:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 135:1</a></font></span></p>A Psalm of praise, in which God's relations to His Church, His power in the natural world, and in delivering His people, are contrasted with the vanity of idols and idol-worship. (Psa. 135:1-21)
 <p>
 </p><p>In the general call for praise, the priests, that stand in the house of the Lord, are specially mentioned.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#004">Psalms 135:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 135:4</a></font></span></p>God's choice of Israel is the first reason assigned for rendering praise; the next, His manifested greatness in creation and providence.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#006">Psalms 135:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 135:6</a></font></span></p>heaven, and . . . seas, and all . . . ends of the earth--denote universality.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#008">Psalms 135:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 135:8</a></font></span></p>The last plague [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo012.htm#029">Exo 12:29</a>] is cited to illustrate His "tokens and wonders."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#010">Psalms 135:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 135:10</a></font></span></p>The conquest of Canaan was by God's power, not that of the people.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#013">Psalms 135:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 135:13</a></font></span></p>heritage--or, "possession." 
 <p>
 </p><p>name . . . memorial--Each denote that by which God is made known.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#014">Psalms 135:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 135:14</a></font></span></p>will judge--do justice (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#002">Psa 72:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>repent himself--change His dealings (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#013">Psa 90:13</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#015">Psalms 135:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 135:15</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa115.htm#004">Psa 115:4-8</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#018">Psalms 135:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 135:18</a></font></span></p>are like unto them--or, "shall be like," &amp;c. Idolaters become spiritually stupid and perish with their idols (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#031">Isa 1:31</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#019">Psalms 135:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">psa 135:19</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa115.htm#009">Psa 115:9-11</a>). There we have "trust" for "bless" here.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa135.htm#021">Psalms 135:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">psa 135:21</a></font></span></p>out of Zion--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#002">Psa 110:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa134.htm#003">Psa 134:3</a>). From the Church, as a center, His praise is diffused throughout the earth.
