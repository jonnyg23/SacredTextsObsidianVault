 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#001">Leviticus</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">lev 0:0</a></font></span></p>LEVITICUS. So called from its treating of the laws relating to the ritual, the services, and sacrifices of the Jewish religion, the superintendence of which was entrusted to the Levitical priesthood. It is chiefly, however, the duties of the priests, "the sons of Aaron," which this book describes; and its claim to be the work of Moses is established by the following passages:-- <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2030.htm#016">Ch2 30:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh008.htm#014">Neh 8:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer007.htm#022">Jer 7:22-23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze020.htm#011">Eze 20:11</a> <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#004">Mat 8:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#022">Luk 2:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#005">Joh 8:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#004">Rom 10:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#009">Rom 13:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#016">Co2 6:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#012">Gal 3:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#016">Pe1 1:16</a>.
