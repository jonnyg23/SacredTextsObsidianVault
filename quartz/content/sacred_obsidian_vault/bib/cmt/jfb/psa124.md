Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 124</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psalms 124:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 124:1</a></font></span></p>The writer, for the Church, praises God for past, and expresses trust for future, deliverance from foes. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001">Psa 124:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>on our side--for us (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#009">Psa 56:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>now--or, "oh! let Israel"
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#002">Psalms 124:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 124:2</a></font></span></p>rose . . . against, &amp;c.-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#001">Psa 3:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#011">Psa 56:11</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#003">Psalms 124:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 124:3</a></font></span></p>Then--that is, the time of our danger. 
 <p>
 </p><p>quick--literally, "living" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#032">Num 16:32-33</a>), description of ferocity.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#004">Psalms 124:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 124:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#004">Psa 18:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#016">Psa 18:16</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#005">Psalms 124:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 124:5</a></font></span></p>The epithet proud added to waters denotes insolent enemies.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#006">Psalms 124:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 124:6</a></font></span></p>The figure is changed to that of a rapacious wild beast (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#007">Psa 3:7</a>), and then of a fowler (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#003">Psa 91:3</a>), and complete escape is denoted by breaking the net.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#008">Psalms 124:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 124:8</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#002">Psa 121:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>name--in the usual sense (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psa 5:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#001">Psa 20:1</a>). He thus places over against the great danger the omnipotent God, and drowns, as it were in an anthem, the wickedness of the whole world and of hell, just as a great fire consumes a little drop of water [LUTHER].
 </p><p>
