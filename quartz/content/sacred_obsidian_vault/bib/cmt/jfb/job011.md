Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#001">Job Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#002">Job 11:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">job 11:2</a></font></span></p>FIRST SPEECH OF ZOPHAR. (Job 11:1-20)
 <p>
 </p><p>Zophar assails Job for his empty words, and indirectly, the two friends, for their weak reply. Taciturnity is highly prized among Orientals (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#008">Pro 10:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#019">Pro 10:19</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#003">Job 11:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">job 11:3</a></font></span></p>lies--rather, "vain boasting" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa016.htm#006">Isa 16:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer048.htm#030">Jer 48:30</a>). The "men" is emphatic; men of sense; in antithesis to "vain boasting." 
 <p>
 </p><p>mockest--upbraidest God by complaints, "shall no man make thee ashamed?"
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#004">Job 11:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">job 11:4</a></font></span></p>doctrine--purposely used of Job's speeches, which sounded like lessons of doctrine (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#002">Deu 32:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#002">Pro 4:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>thine--addressed to God. Job had maintained his sincerity against his friends suspicions, not faultlessness.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#006">Job 11:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">job 11:6</a></font></span></p>to that which is!--Rather, "they are double to [man's] wisdom" [MICHAELIS]. So the Hebrew is rendered (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#007">Pro 2:7</a>). God's ways, which you arraign, if you were shown their secret wisdom, would be seen vastly to exceed that of men, including yours (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#025">Co1 1:25</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>exacteth--Rather, "God consigns to oblivion in thy favor much of thy guilt."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#007">Job 11:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">job 11:7</a></font></span></p>Rather, "Penetrate to the perfections of the Almighty" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job009.htm#010">Job 9:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa139.htm#006">Psa 139:6</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#008">Job 11:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">job 11:8</a></font></span></p>It--the "wisdom" of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#006">Job 11:6</a>). The abruptness of the Hebrew is forcible: "The heights of heaven! What canst thou do" (as to attaining to them with thy gaze, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa139.htm#008">Psa 139:8</a>)? 
 <p>
 </p><p>know--namely, of His perfections.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#010">Job 11:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">job 11:10</a></font></span></p>cut off--Rather, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job009.htm#011">Job 9:11</a>, "pass over," as a storm; namely, rush upon in anger. 
 <p>
 </p><p>shut up--in prison, with a view to trial. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>gather together--the parties for judgment: hold a judicial assembly; to pass sentence on the prisoners.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#011">Job 11:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">job 11:11</a></font></span></p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#011">Psa 94:11</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>consider--so as to punish it. Rather, from the connection, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#006">Job 11:6</a>, "He seeth wickedness also, which man does not perceive"; literally, "But no (other, save He) perceiveth it" [UMBREIT]. God's "wisdom" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#006">Job 11:6</a>), detects sin where Job's human eye cannot reach (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#008">Job 11:8</a>), so as to see any.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#012">Job 11:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">job 11:12</a></font></span></p>vain--hollow. 
 <p>
 </p><p>would be--"wants to consider himself wise"; opposed to God's "wisdom" (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#011">Job 11:11</a>); refuses to see sin, where God sees it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#022">Rom 1:22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>wild ass's colt--a proverb for untamed wildness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job039.htm#005">Job 39:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job039.htm#008">Job 39:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer002.htm#024">Jer 2:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen016.htm#012">Gen 16:12</a>; Hebrew, "a wild-ass man"). Man wishes to appear wisely obedient to his Lord, whereas he is, from his birth, unsubdued in spirit.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#013">Job 11:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">job 11:13</a></font></span></p>The apodosis to the "If" is at <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#015">Job 11:15</a>. The preparation of the heart is to be obtained (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#001">Pro 16:1</a>) by stretching out the hands in prayer for it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#017">Psa 10:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1029.htm#018">Ch1 29:18</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#014">Job 11:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">job 11:14</a></font></span></p>Rather, "if thou wilt put far away the iniquity in thine hand" (as Zaccheus did, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#008">Luk 19:8</a>). The apodosis or conclusion is at <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#015">Job 11:15</a>, "then shalt thou," &amp;c.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#015">Job 11:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">job 11:15</a></font></span></p>Zophar refers to Job's own words (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job010.htm#015">Job 10:15</a>), "yet will I not lift up my head," even though righteous. Zophar declares, if Job will follow his advice, he may "lift up his face." 
 <p>
 </p><p>spot-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#005">Deu 32:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>steadfast--literally, "run fast together," like metals which become firm and hard by fusion. The sinner on the contrary is wavering.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#016">Job 11:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">job 11:16</a></font></span></p>Just as when the stream runs dry (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job006.htm#017">Job 6:17</a>), the danger threatened by its wild waves is forgotten (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#016">Isa 65:16</a>) [UMBREIT].
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#017">Job 11:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">job 11:17</a></font></span></p>age--days of life. 
 <p>
 </p><p>the noon-day--namely, of thy former prosperity; which, in the poet's image, had gone on increasing, until it reached its height, as the sun rises higher and higher until it reaches the meridian (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#018">Pro 4:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>shine forth--rather, "though now in darkness, thou shall be as the morning"; or, "thy darkness (if any dark shade should arise on thee, it) shall be as the morning" (only the dullness of morning twilight, not nocturnal darkness) [UMBREIT].
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#018">Job 11:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">job 11:18</a></font></span></p>The experience of thy life will teach thee there is hope for man in every trial. 
 <p>
 </p><p>dig--namely, wells; the chief necessity in the East. Better, "though now ashamed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#005">Rom 5:5</a>, opposed to the previous 'hope'), thou shalt then rest safely" [GESENIUS];
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#019">Job 11:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">job 11:19</a></font></span></p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#008">Psa 4:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#024">Pro 3:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#030">Isa 14:30</a>); oriental images of prosperity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>make suit--literally, "stroke thy face," "caress thee" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#006">Pro 19:6</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#020">Job 11:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">job 11:20</a></font></span></p>A warning to Job, if he would not turn to God. 
 <p>
 </p><p>The wicked--that is, obdurate sinners. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>eyes . . . fail--that is, in vain look for relief (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#065">Deu 28:65</a>). Zophar implies Job's only hope of relief is in a change of heart. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>they shall not escape--literally, "every refuge shall vanish from them." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>giving up of the ghost--Their hope shall leave them as the breath does the body (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#007">Pro 11:7</a>).
 </p><p>
