Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 62</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#001">Psalms 62:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 62:1</a></font></span></p>To Jeduthun--(See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#001">Psa 39:1</a>, title). The general tone of this Psalm is expressive of confidence in God. Occasion is taken to remind the wicked of their sin, their ruin, and their meanness. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#001">Psa 62:1-12</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>waiteth--literally, "is silent," trusts submissively and confidently as a servant.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#002">Psalms 62:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 62:2</a></font></span></p>The titles applied to God often occur (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#009">Psa 9:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002">Psa 18:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>be greatly moved-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#006">Psa 10:6</a>). No injury shall be permanent, though devised by enemies.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#003">Psalms 62:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 62:3</a></font></span></p>Their destruction will come; as a tottering wall they already are feeble and failing. 
 <p>
 </p><p>bowing wall shall ye be--better supply "are." Some propose to apply these phrases to describe the condition of "a man"--that is, the pious suffer: thus, "Will ye slay him," &amp;c.; but the other is a good sense.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#004">Psalms 62:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 62:4</a></font></span></p>his excellency--or, elevation to which God had raised him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#002">Psa 4:2</a>). This they try to do by lies and duplicity (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#009">Psa 5:9</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#005">Psalms 62:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 62:5</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#001">Psa 62:1-2</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#006">Psalms 62:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 62:6</a></font></span></p>not be moved--not at all; his confidence has increased.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#007">Psalms 62:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 62:7</a></font></span></p>rock of my strength--or strongest support (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#010">Psa 7:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#003">Psa 61:3</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#008">Psalms 62:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 62:8</a></font></span></p>pour out your heart--give full expression to feeling (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1001.htm#015">Sa1 1:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job030.htm#016">Job 30:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#004">Psa 42:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>ye people--God's people.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#009">Psalms 62:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 62:9</a></font></span></p>No kind of men are reliable, compared with God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#022">Isa 2:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#005">Jer 17:5</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>altogether--alike, one as the other (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#003">Psa 34:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#010">Psalms 62:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 62:10</a></font></span></p>Not only are oppression and robbery, which are wicked means of wealth, no grounds of boasting; but even wealth, increasing lawfully, ought not to engross the heart.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#011">Psalms 62:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 62:11</a></font></span></p>once; twice--(as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job033.htm#014">Job 33:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job040.htm#005">Job 40:5</a>), are used to give emphasis to the sentiment. God's power is tempered by His mercy, which it also sustains.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#012">Psalms 62:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 62:12</a></font></span></p>for thou renderest--literally, "that Thou renderest," &amp;c., connected with "I heard this," as the phrase--"that power," &amp;c. [<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#011">Psa 62:11</a>] --teaching that by His power He can show both mercy and justice.
