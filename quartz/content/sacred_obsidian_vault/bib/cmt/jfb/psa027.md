Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 27</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psalms 27:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 27:1</a></font></span></p>With a general strain of confidence, hope, and joy, especially in God's worship, in the midst of dangers, the Psalmist introduces prayer for divine help and guidance. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001">Psa 27:1-14</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>light--is a common figure for comfort. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>strength--or, "stronghold"--affording security against all violence. The interrogations give greater vividness to the negation implied.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#002">Psalms 27:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 27:2</a></font></span></p>eat . . . my flesh-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job019.htm#022">Job 19:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#004">Psa 14:4</a>). The allusion to wild beasts illustrates their rapacity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>they stumbled--"they" is emphatic; not I, but they were destroyed.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#003">Psalms 27:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 27:3</a></font></span></p>In the greatest dangers. 
 <p>
 </p><p>in this--that is, then, in such extremity.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#004">Psalms 27:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 27:4</a></font></span></p>The secret of his confidence is his delight in communion with God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#011">Psa 16:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#006">Psa 23:6</a>), beholding the harmony of His perfections, and seeking His favor in His temple or palace; a term applicable to the tabernacle (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psa 5:7</a>). There he is safe (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#021">Psa 31:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#005">Psa 61:5</a>). The figure is changed in the last clause, but the sentiment is the same.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#006">Psalms 27:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 27:6</a></font></span></p>head be lifted up--I shall be placed beyond the reach of my enemies. Hence he avows his purpose of rendering joyful thank offerings.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#007">Psalms 27:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 27:7</a></font></span></p>Still pressing need extorts prayer for help. 
 <p>
 </p><p>cry with my voice--denotes earnestness. Other things equal, Christians in earnest pray audibly, even in secret.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#008">Psalms 27:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 27:8</a></font></span></p>The meaning is clear, though the construction in a literal translation is obscure. The English Version supplies the implied clause. To seek God's face is to seek His favor (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa105.htm#004">Psa 105:4</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#009">Psalms 27:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 27:9</a></font></span></p>Hide not, &amp;c.-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#006">Psa 4:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#024">Psa 22:24</a>). Against rejection he pleads former mercy and love.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#010">Psalms 27:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 27:10</a></font></span></p>In the extremity of earthly destitution (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#011">Psa 31:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#011">Psa 38:11</a>), God provides (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#035">Mat 25:35</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#011">Psalms 27:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 27:11</a></font></span></p>thy way--of providence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a plain path-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#012">Psa 26:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>enemies--literally, "watchers for my fall" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#008">Psa 5:8</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#012">Psalms 27:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 27:12</a></font></span></p>will--literally, "soul," "desire" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#025">Psa 35:25</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>enemies--literally, "oppressors." Falsehood aids cruelty against him. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>breathe out--as being filled with it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#001">Act 9:1</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#013">Psalms 27:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 27:13</a></font></span></p>The strong emotion is indicated by the incomplete sentence, for which the English Version supplies a proper clause; or, omitting that, and rendering, "yet I believed," &amp;c., the contrast of his faith and his danger is expressed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>to see--is to experience (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#017">Psa 22:17</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#014">Psalms 27:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 27:14</a></font></span></p>Wait, &amp;c.--in confident expectation. The last clause is, literally, "and wait," &amp;c., as if expecting new measures of help.
