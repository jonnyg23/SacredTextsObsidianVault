Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#001">Proverbs 9:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 9:1</a></font></span></p>The commendation of wisdom is continued, under the figure of a liberal host, and its provisions under that of a feast (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#016">Luk 14:16-24</a>). The character of those who are invited is followed by a contrasted description of the rejectors of good counsel; and with the invitations of wisdom are contrasted the allurement of the wicked woman. (Pro. 9:1-18)
 <p>
 </p><p>house--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#034">Pro 8:34</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>her--or, "its" (the house). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>seven pillars--the number seven for many, or a sufficiency (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#031">Pro 6:31</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#002">Proverbs 9:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">pro 9:2</a></font></span></p>mingled--to enhance the flavor (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#030">Pro 23:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#022">Isa 5:22</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>furnished--literally, "set out," "arranged."
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#003">Proverbs 9:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 9:3</a></font></span></p>maidens--servants to invite (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#011">Psa 68:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#009">Isa 40:9</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>highest places--ridges of heights, conspicuous places.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#004">Proverbs 9:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 9:4</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Pro 1:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#032">Pro 6:32</a>). Wisdom not only supplies right but forbids wrong principles.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#007">Proverbs 9:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 9:7</a></font></span></p>shame--(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#035">Pro 3:35</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>a blot--or, "stain on character." Both terms denote the evil done by others to one whose faithfulness secures a wise man's love.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#009">Proverbs 9:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 9:9</a></font></span></p>The more a wise man learns, the more he loves wisdom.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#010">Proverbs 9:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 9:10</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#007">Pro 1:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>of the holy--literally, "holies," persons or things, or both. This knowledge gives right perception.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#011">Proverbs 9:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 9:11</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#016">Pro 3:16-18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#010">Pro 4:10</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#012">Proverbs 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 9:12</a></font></span></p>You are mainly concerned in your own conduct.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#013">Proverbs 9:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 9:13</a></font></span></p>foolish woman--or literally, "woman of folly," specially manifested by such as are described. 
 <p>
 </p><p>clamorous--or, "noisy" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#011">Pro 7:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>knoweth nothing--literally, "knoweth not what," that is, is right and proper.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#014">Proverbs 9:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 9:14</a></font></span></p>on a seat--literally, "throne," takes a prominent place, impudently and haughtily.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#015">Proverbs 9:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">pro 9:15</a></font></span></p>to allure those who are right-minded, and who are addressed as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#004">Pro 9:4</a>, as 
 <p>
 </p><p>simple--that is, easily led (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004">Pro 1:4</a>) and unsettled, though willing to do right.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#017">Proverbs 9:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 9:17</a></font></span></p>The language of a proverb, meaning that forbidden delights are sweet and pleasant, as fruits of risk and danger.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#018">Proverbs 9:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 9:18</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#018">Pro 2:18-19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#027">Pro 7:27</a>).
