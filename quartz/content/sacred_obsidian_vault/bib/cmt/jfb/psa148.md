Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 148</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#001">Psalms 148:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 148:1</a></font></span></p>The scope of this Psalm is the same as that of the preceding. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#001">Psa 148:1-14</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>heavens [and] heights--are synonymous.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#002">Psalms 148:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 148:2</a></font></span></p>hosts--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa103.htm#021">Psa 103:21</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#004">Psalms 148:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 148:4</a></font></span></p>heavens of heavens--the very highest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>waters--clouds, resting above the visible heavens (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#007">Gen 1:7</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#005">Psalms 148:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 148:5</a></font></span></p>praise the name--as representing His perfections. 
 <p>
 </p><p>he commanded--"He" is emphatic, ascribing creation to God alone.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#006">Psalms 148:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 148:6</a></font></span></p>The perpetuity of the frame of nature is, of course, subject to Him who formed it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>a decree . . . pass--His ordinances respecting them shall not change (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#031">Jer 36:31</a>), or perish (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#020">Job 34:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#036">Psa 37:36</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#007">Psalms 148:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 148:7</a></font></span></p>The call on the earth, as opposed to heaven, includes seas or depths, whose inhabitants the dragon, as one of the largest (on leviathan, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#026">Psa 104:26</a>), is selected to represent. The most destructive and ungovernable agents of inanimate nature are introduced.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#008">Psalms 148:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 148:8</a></font></span></p>fulfilling his word--or, law, may be understood of each. Next the most distinguished productions of the vegetable world.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#009">Psalms 148:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 148:9</a></font></span></p>fruitful trees--or, "trees of fruit," as opposed to forest trees. Wild and domestic, large and small animals are comprehended.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#011">Psalms 148:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 148:11</a></font></span></p>Next all rational beings, from the highest in rank to little children. 
 <p>
 </p><p>princes--or, military leaders.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#013">Psalms 148:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 148:13</a></font></span></p>Let them--all mentioned. 
 <p>
 </p><p>excellent--or, exalted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#004">Isa 12:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>his glory--majesty (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#003">Psa 45:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>above the earth and heaven--Their united splendors fail to match His.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#014">Psalms 148:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 148:14</a></font></span></p>exalteth the horn--established power (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#005">Psa 75:5-6</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>praise of--or literally, "for" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>his saints--that is, occasions for them to praise Him. They are further described as "His people," and "near unto Him," sustaining by covenanted care a peculiarly intimate relation.
 </p><p>
