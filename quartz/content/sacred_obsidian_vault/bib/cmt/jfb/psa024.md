Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001">Psalms 24:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 24:1</a></font></span></p>God's supreme sovereignty requires a befitting holiness of life and heart in His worshippers; a sentiment sublimely illustrated by describing His entrance into the sanctuary, by the symbol of His worship--the ark, as requiring the most profound homage to the glory of His Majesty. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001">Psa 24:1-10</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>fulness--everything. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>world--the habitable globe, with 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>they that dwell--forming a parallel expression to the first clause.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#002">Psalms 24:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 24:2</a></font></span></p>Poetically represents the facts of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#009">Gen 1:9</a>.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#003">Psalms 24:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 24:3</a></font></span></p>The form of a question gives vivacity. Hands, tongue, and heart are organs of action, speech, and feeling, which compose character. 
 <p>
 </p><p>hill of the Lord--(compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>, &amp;c.). His Church--the true or invisible, as typified by the earthly sanctuary.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#004">Psalms 24:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 24:4</a></font></span></p>lifted up his soul--is to set the affections (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001">Psa 25:1</a>) on an object; here, 
 <p>
 </p><p>vanity--or, any false thing, of which swearing falsely, or to falsehood, is a specification.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#005">Psalms 24:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 24:5</a></font></span></p>righteousness--the rewards which God bestows on His people, or the grace to secure those rewards as well as the result.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#006">Psalms 24:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 24:6</a></font></span></p>Jacob--By "Jacob," we may understand God's people (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#022">Isa 43:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#002">Isa 44:2</a>, &amp;c.), corresponding to "the generation," as if he had said, "those who seek Thy face are Thy chosen people."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#007">Psalms 24:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 24:7</a></font></span></p>The entrance of the ark, with the attending procession, into the holy sanctuary is pictured to us. The repetition of the terms gives emphasis.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#010">Psalms 24:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 24:10</a></font></span></p>Lord of hosts--or fully, Lord God of hosts (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos012.htm#005">Hos 12:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/amo004.htm#013">Amo 4:13</a>), describes God by a title indicative of supremacy over all creatures, and especially the heavenly armies (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#014">Jos 5:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1022.htm#019">Kg1 22:19</a>). Whether, as some think, the actual enlargement of the ancient gates of Jerusalem be the basis of the figure, the effect of the whole is to impress us with a conception of the matchless majesty of God.
