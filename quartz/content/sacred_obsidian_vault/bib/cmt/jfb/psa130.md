 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001">Psalms 130:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 130:1</a></font></span></p>The penitent sinner's hope is in God's mercy only. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001">Psa 130:1-8</a>)
 <p>
 </p><p>depths--for great distress (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#002">Psa 40:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#003">Psa 69:3</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#003">Psalms 130:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 130:3</a></font></span></p>shouldest mark--or, "take strict account" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/job010.htm#014">Job 10:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job014.htm#016">Job 14:16</a>), implying a confession of the existence of sin. 
 <p>
 </p><p>who shall stand-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#006">Psa 1:6</a>). Standing is opposed to the guilty sinking down in fear and self-condemnation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal003.htm#002">Mal 3:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#015">Rev 6:15-16</a>). The question implies a negative, which is thus more strongly stated.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#004">Psalms 130:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 130:4</a></font></span></p>Pardon produces filial fear and love. Judgment without the hope of pardon creates fear and dislike. The sense of forgiveness, so far from producing licentiousness, produces holiness (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer033.htm#009">Jer 33:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze016.htm#062">Eze 16:62-63</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#016">Pe1 2:16</a>). "There is forgiveness with thee, not that thou mayest be presumed upon, but feared."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#005">Psalms 130:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 130:5</a></font></span></p>wait for the Lord--in expectation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#014">Psa 27:14</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>watch for, &amp;c.--in earnestness and anxiety.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#007">Psalms 130:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 130:7</a></font></span></p>Let Israel, &amp;c.--that is, All are invited to seek and share divine forgiveness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>from all his iniquities--or, "punishments of them" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#012">Psa 40:12</a>, &amp;c.).
 </p><p>
