Fausset and David Brown [1882] at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 90</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#001">Psalms 90:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 90:1</a></font></span></p>Contrasting man's frailty with God's eternity, the writer mourns over it as the punishment of sin, and prays for a return of the divine favor. A Prayer [mainly such] of Moses the man of God-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#001">Deu 33:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos014.htm#006">Jos 14:6</a>); as such he wrote this (see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#001">Psa 18:1</a>, title, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa036.htm#001">Psa 36:1</a>, title). (Psa. 90:1-17)
 <p>
 </p><p>dwelling-place--home (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze011.htm#016">Eze 11:16</a>), as a refuge (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#027">Deu 33:27</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#002">Psalms 90:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 90:2</a></font></span></p>brought forth [and] formed--both express the idea of production by birth.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#003">Psalms 90:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 90:3</a></font></span></p>to destruction--literally, "even to dust" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#019">Gen 3:19</a>), which is partly quoted in the last clause.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#004">Psalms 90:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 90:4</a></font></span></p>Even were our days now a thousand years, as Adam's, our life would be but a moment in God's sight (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2003.htm#008">Pe2 3:8</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>a watch--or, third part of a night (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#024">Exo 14:24</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#005">Psalms 90:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 90:5</a></font></span></p>Life is like grass, which, though changing under the influence of the night's dew, and flourishing in the morning, is soon cut down and withereth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa103.htm#015">Psa 103:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#024">Pe1 1:24</a>).
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#007">Psalms 90:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 90:7</a></font></span></p>For--A reason, this is the infliction of God's wrath. 
 <p>
 </p><p>troubled--literally, "confounded by terror" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#005">Psa 2:5</a>). Death is by sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#012">Rom 5:12</a>). Though "secret," the light of God's countenance, as a candle, will bring sin to view (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#027">Pro 20:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#005">Co1 4:5</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#009">Psalms 90:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 90:9</a></font></span></p>are passed--literally, "turn," as to depart (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer006.htm#004">Jer 6:4</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>spend--literally, "consume." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>as a tale--literally, "a thought," or, "a sigh" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze002.htm#010">Eze 2:10</a>).
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#010">Psalms 90:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 90:10</a></font></span></p>Moses' life was an exception (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu034.htm#007">Deu 34:7</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>it is . . . cut off--or, "driven," as is said of the quails in using the same word (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num011.htm#031">Num 11:31</a>). In view of this certain and speedy end, life is full of sorrow.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#011">Psalms 90:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 90:11</a></font></span></p>The whole verse may be read as a question implying the negative, "No one knows what Thy anger can do, and what Thy wrath is, estimated by a true piety."
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#012">Psalms 90:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 90:12</a></font></span></p>This he prays we may know or understand, so as properly to number or appreciate the shortness of our days, that we may be wise.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#013">Psalms 90:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 90:13</a></font></span></p>(Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#002">Psa 13:2</a>). 
 <p>
 </p><p>let it repent--a strong figure, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo032.htm#012">Exo 32:12</a>, imploring a change in His dealings.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#014">Psalms 90:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 90:14</a></font></span></p>early--promptly.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#015">Psalms 90:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 90:15</a></font></span></p>As have been our sorrows, so let our joys be great and long.
 <p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#016">Psalms 90:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">psa 90:16</a></font></span></p>thy work--or, providential acts. 
 <p>
 </p><p>thy glory-- (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa008.htm#005">Psa 8:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#003">Psa 45:3</a>), the honor accruing from Thy work of mercy to us.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#017">Psalms 90:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">psa 90:17</a></font></span></p>let the beauty--or sum of His gracious acts, in their harmony, be illustrated in us, and favor our enterprise.
