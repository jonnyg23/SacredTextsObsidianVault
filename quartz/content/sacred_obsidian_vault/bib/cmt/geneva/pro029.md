 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 29</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#005">Proverbs 29:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 29:5</a></font></span></p>A man that flattereth his neighbour (a) spreadeth a net for his feet. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He who gives ear to the flatterer is in danger as the bird is before the fowler.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#006">Proverbs 29:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 29:6</a></font></span></p>In the transgression of an evil man [there is] a (b) snare: but the righteous doth sing and rejoice. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He is always ready to fall into the snare that he lays for others.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#009">Proverbs 29:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 29:9</a></font></span></p>[If] a wise man contendeth with (c) a foolish man, whether he rageth or laugheth, [there is] no rest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He can hear no admonition no matter how it is spoken.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#018">Proverbs 29:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 29:18</a></font></span></p>(d) Where [there is] no vision, the people perish: but he that keepeth the law, happy [is] he. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Where there are not faithful ministers of the word of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#019">Proverbs 29:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 29:19</a></font></span></p>A (e) servant will not be corrected by words: for though he understandeth he will not answer. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) He who is of a servile and rebellious nature.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#025">Proverbs 29:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 29:25</a></font></span></p>The fear of man bringeth a (f) snare: but he who putteth his trust in the LORD shall be safe. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He who fears man more than God falls into a snare and is destroyed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#026">Proverbs 29:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 29:26</a></font></span></p>Many seek the ruler's favour; but [every] man's (g) judgment [cometh] from the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) He does not need to flatter the ruler, for what God has appointed will come to him.
