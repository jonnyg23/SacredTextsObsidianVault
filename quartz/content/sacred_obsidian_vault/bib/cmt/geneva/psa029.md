 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 29</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#001">Psalms 29:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 29:1</a></font></span></p>"A Psalm of David." Give unto the LORD, O ye (a) mighty, give unto the LORD glory and strength. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He exhorts the proud tyrants to humble themselves under God's hand, and not to be inferior to brute beasts and dumb creatures.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#003">Psalms 29:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 29:3</a></font></span></p>The (b) voice of the LORD [is] upon the waters: the God of glory thundereth: the LORD [is] upon many waters. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The thunder claps that are heard out of the clouds should make the wicked tremble for fear of God's anger.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#005">Psalms 29:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 29:5</a></font></span></p>The (c) voice of the LORD breaketh the cedars; yea, the LORD breaketh the cedars of Lebanon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, the thunderbolt breaks the most strong trees, and will men think their power able to resist God?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#006">Psalms 29:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 29:6</a></font></span></p>He maketh them also to skip like a calf; Lebanon and (d) Sirion like a young unicorn. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Called also Hermon.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#007">Psalms 29:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 29:7</a></font></span></p>The voice of the LORD divideth the (e) flames of fire. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) It causes the lightnings to shoot and glide.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#008">Psalms 29:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 29:8</a></font></span></p>The voice of the LORD shaketh the wilderness; the LORD shaketh the wilderness of (f) Kadesh. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) In places most desolate, where it seems there is no presence of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#009">Psalms 29:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 29:9</a></font></span></p>The voice of the LORD maketh the hinds to (g) calve, and (h) discovereth the forests: and in his (i) temple doth every one speak of [his] glory. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) For fear makes them deliver their calves. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) Makes the trees bare, or pierces the most secret places. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) Though the wicked are not moved by these lights, yet the faithful praise God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa029.htm#010">Psalms 29:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 29:10</a></font></span></p>The LORD sitteth upon the (k) flood; yea, the LORD sitteth King for ever. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) To moderate the rage of the tempests and waters that they not destroy all.
