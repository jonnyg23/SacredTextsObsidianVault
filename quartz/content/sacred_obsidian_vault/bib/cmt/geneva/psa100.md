 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 100</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#001">Psalms 100:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 100:1</a></font></span></p>"A Psalm of praise." Make a (a) joyful noise unto the LORD, all ye lands. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He prophecies that God's benefits in calling the Gentiles will be so great that they will have wonderful opportunity to praise his mercy and rejoice.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#003">Psalms 100:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 100:3</a></font></span></p>Know ye that the LORD he [is] God: [it is] he [that] hath (b) made us, and not we ourselves; [we are] his people, and the sheep of his pasture. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He chiefly means concerning spiritual regeneration, by which we are his sheep and people.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#004">Psalms 100:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 100:4</a></font></span></p>(c) Enter into his gates with thanksgiving, [and] into his courts with praise: be thankful unto him, [and] bless his name. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He shows that God will not be worshipped, but by those means which he has appointed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa100.htm#005">Psalms 100:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 100:5</a></font></span></p>For the LORD [is] good; his mercy [is] (d) everlasting; and his truth [endureth] to all generations. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He declares that we should never be weary in praising him, seeing his mercies toward us last forever.
