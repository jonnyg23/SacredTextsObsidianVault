 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#001">Colossians Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#002">Colossians 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">col 4:2</a></font></span></p>(1) (2) Continue in prayer, and watch in the same with thanksgiving; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(1) He adds certain general exhortations, and at length ends his epistle with various familiar and godly salutations. (2) Prayers must be continual and earnest.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#003">Colossians 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">col 4:3</a></font></span></p>(3) Withal praying also for us, that God would open unto us a (a) door of utterance, to speak the mystery of Christ, for which I am also in bonds: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(3) Those who minister the word, must especially be entrusted to the prayers of the Church. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(a) An open and free mouth to preach the Gospel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#005">Colossians 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">col 4:5</a></font></span></p>(4) Walk (b) in wisdom toward them that are without, redeeming the (c) time. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(4) In all parts of our life, we ought to have good consideration even of those who are outside of the Church. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Advisedly and cautiously. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) Seek occasion to win them, even though you lose something of your own by it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#006">Colossians 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">col 4:6</a></font></span></p>(5) Let your speech [be] alway with (d) grace, seasoned with (e) salt, that ye may know how ye ought to answer every man. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(5) Our speech and talk must be applied to the profit of the hearers. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) Fit for the profit of your neighbour. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(e) Against this is set filthy communication, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#029">Eph 4:29</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#011">Colossians 4:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">col 4:11</a></font></span></p>And Jesus, which is called Justus, who are of the circumcision. These (f) only [are my] fellowworkers unto the (g) kingdom of God, which have been a comfort unto me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Hence, Peter was not at that time in Rome. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(g) In the Gospel.  
 </p><p></p>
