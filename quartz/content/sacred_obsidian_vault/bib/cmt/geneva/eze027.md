 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 27</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#003">Ezekiel 27:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 27:3</a></font></span></p>And say to Tyre, O thou that dwelleth at the entrance of the sea, [which art] a merchant (a) of the people for many isles, Thus saith the Lord GOD; O Tyre, thou hast said, I [am] of perfect beauty. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Which serves all the world with your merchandise.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#005">Ezekiel 27:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">eze 27:5</a></font></span></p>They have made all thy [ship] planks of fir trees of (b) Senir: they have taken cedars from Lebanon to make masts for thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) This mountain was called Hermon but the Amorites called it Shenir, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu003.htm#009">Deu 3:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#006">Ezekiel 27:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 27:6</a></font></span></p>[Of] the oaks of Bashan have they made thy oars; the company of the Ashurites have made thy benches [of] ivory, [brought] out of the isles of (c) Chittim. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Which is taken for Greece and Italy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#009">Ezekiel 27:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">eze 27:9</a></font></span></p>The ancients of Gebal and its wise [men] were in thee thy (d) calkers: all the ships of the sea with their mariners were in thee to exchange thy merchandise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Meaning, that they built the walls of the city, which is here meant by the ship: and of these were the builders of Solomon's temple, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1005.htm#018">Kg1 5:18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#011">Ezekiel 27:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">eze 27:11</a></font></span></p>The men of Arvad with thy army [were] upon thy walls on all sides, and the (e) Gammadims were in thy towers: they hung their shields upon thy walls on every side; they have made thy beauty perfect. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) That is they of Cappadocia, or pygmies and dwarfs which were called because from the high towers they seemed little.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#013">Ezekiel 27:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">eze 27:13</a></font></span></p>(f) Javan, Tubal, and Meshech, they [were] thy merchants: they traded (g) in the persons of men and vessels of brass in thy market. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Of Greece, Italy and Cappadocia. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(g) By selling slaves.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#014">Ezekiel 27:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 27:14</a></font></span></p>They of the house of (h) Togarmah traded in thy fairs with horses and horsemen and mules. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Which are taken for a people of Asia minor.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#015">Ezekiel 27:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">eze 27:15</a></font></span></p>The men of Dedan [were] thy merchants; many isles [were] the merchandise of thy hand: they brought thee [for] a present (i) horns of ivory and ebony. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Meaning, unicorn's horns and elephant's teeth.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#017">Ezekiel 27:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">eze 27:17</a></font></span></p>Judah, and the land of Israel, they [were] thy merchants: they traded in thy market in wheat of (k) Minnith, and Pannag, and honey, and oil, and balm. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Where the best wheat grew.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#026">Ezekiel 27:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">eze 27:26</a></font></span></p>Thy rowers have brought thee into great waters: the (l) east wind hath broken thee in the midst of the seas. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) That is, Nebuchadnezzar.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#028">Ezekiel 27:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">eze 27:28</a></font></span></p>The (m) common lands shall shake at the sound of the cry of thy pilots. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) That is, the cities near you, as Zidon, Arund and others.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze027.htm#036">Ezekiel 27:36</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_036">eze 27:36</a></font></span></p>The merchants among the people shall hiss at thee; thou shalt be a terror, and never [shalt be] (n) any more. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) By which is meant a long time: for it was prophesied to be destroyed but seventy years, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa023.htm#015">Isa 23:15</a>).
