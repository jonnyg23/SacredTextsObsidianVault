 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#001">Jeremiah Chapter 49</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#001">Jeremiah 49:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jer 49:1</a></font></span></p>Concerning the (a) Ammonites, thus saith the LORD; Hath Israel no sons? hath he no heir? why [then] doth their king (b) inherit Gad, and his people dwell in (c) his cities? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) They were separated from the Moabites by the river Arnon, and after the ten tribes were carried away into captivity, they invaded the country of Gad. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) That is, of the Ammonites. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning, of the Israelites.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#002">Jeremiah 49:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">jer 49:2</a></font></span></p>Therefore, behold, the days come, saith the LORD, that I will cause an alarm of war to be heard in (d) Rabbah of the Ammonites; and it shall be a desolate heap, and her daughters shall be burned with fire: then shall Israel be heir to them that were his heirs, saith the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Which was one of the chief cities of the Ammonites, as were Heshbon and Ai: there was also a city called Heshbon among the Moabites.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#004">Jeremiah 49:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">jer 49:4</a></font></span></p>Why gloriest thou in the (e) valleys, thy flowing valley, O backsliding daughter? that trusted in her treasures, [saying], Who shall come to me? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) In your plentiful country.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#005">Jeremiah 49:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">jer 49:5</a></font></span></p>Behold, I will bring (f) a fear upon thee, saith the Lord GOD of hosts, from all those that are about thee; and ye shall be driven out every man right forth; and none shall gather up him that wandereth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Signifying that power and riches cannot prevail when God will execute his judgments.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#006">Jeremiah 49:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jer 49:6</a></font></span></p>And (h) afterward I will bring again the captives of the children of Ammon, saith the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) In the time of Christ, when the Gentiles will be called.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#007">Jeremiah 49:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">jer 49:7</a></font></span></p>Concerning Edom, thus saith the LORD of hosts; [Is] wisdom no more in (i) Teman? hath counsel perished from the prudent? hath their wisdom vanished? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Which was a city of Edom, called by the name of Teman Eliphaz's son, who came from Esau.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#008">Jeremiah 49:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">jer 49:8</a></font></span></p>Flee ye, (k) turn back, dwell deep, O inhabitants of Dedan; for I will bring the calamity of Esau upon him, the time [that] I will punish him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) The enemies that pretend to flee, will turn back and invade your land, and possess it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#009">Jeremiah 49:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">jer 49:9</a></font></span></p>If (l) grapegatherers come to thee, would they not leave [some] gleaning grapes? if thieves by night, they will destroy till they have enough. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) Meaning that God would utterly destroy them and not spare one, though the grape gatherers leave some grapes, and thieves seek but till they have enough, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/oba001.htm#005">Oba 1:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#011">Jeremiah 49:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">jer 49:11</a></font></span></p>Leave thy (m) fatherless children, I will preserve [them] alive; and let thy widows trust in me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) The destruction will be so great that there will be none left to take care of the widows and the fatherless.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#012">Jeremiah 49:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">jer 49:12</a></font></span></p>For thus saith the LORD; (n) Behold, they whose judgment [was] not to drink of the cup have assuredly drank; and [art] thou he [that] shall altogether go unpunished? thou shalt not go unpunished, but thou shalt surely drink [of it]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) I have not spared my own people and how should I pity you?  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#013">Jeremiah 49:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">jer 49:13</a></font></span></p>For I have sworn by myself, saith the LORD, that (o) Bozrah shall become a desolation, a reproach, a waste, and a curse; and all her cities shall be perpetual wastes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) Which was a chief city of Edom.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#014">Jeremiah 49:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">jer 49:14</a></font></span></p>I have heard a rumour from the LORD, and an ambassador is sent to the nations, [saying], Gather ye together, and come against (p) her, and rise up to the battle. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) That is, Bozrah.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#019">Jeremiah 49:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">jer 49:19</a></font></span></p>Behold, (q) he shall come up like a lion from the swelling of Jordan against the habitation of the strong: but I will suddenly make (r) him run away from her: and who [is] a chosen [man, that] I may appoint over her? for who [is] like me? and who will appoint me the time? and who [is] that (s) shepherd that will stand before me? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(q) That is, Nebuchadnezzar after he has overcome Judah, which is meant by the swelling of Jordan, will come against mount Seir and Edom. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(r) That is, the Israelites whom the Edomites kept as prisoners to hast away from there. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(s) The captain and governor of the army meaning Nebuchadnezzar.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#020">Jeremiah 49:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">jer 49:20</a></font></span></p>Therefore hear the counsel of the LORD, that he hath taken against Edom; and his purposes, that he hath purposed against the inhabitants of Teman: Surely the least (t) of the flock shall draw them out: surely (u) he shall make their habitations desolate with them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(t) They will not be able to resist his petty captains. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(u) To visit the enemy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#022">Jeremiah 49:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">jer 49:22</a></font></span></p>Behold, he shall come up and fly as the eagle, (x) and spread his wings over Bozrah: and at that day shall the heart of the mighty men of Edom be as the heart of a woman in her pangs. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(x) As (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer048.htm#040">Jer 48:40</a>) was said of Moab.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#023">Jeremiah 49:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">jer 49:23</a></font></span></p>Concerning (y) Damascus. Hamath is confounded, and Arpad: for they have heard evil tidings: they are fainthearted; [there is] sorrow on the sea; it cannot be quiet. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(y) Which was the chief city of Syria, by which he means the whole country.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#024">Jeremiah 49:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">jer 49:24</a></font></span></p>Damascus hath become feeble, [and] turneth herself to flee, (z) and fear hath seized on [her]: anguish and sorrows have taken her, as a woman in travail. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(z) When she heard the sudden coming of the enemy.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#025">Jeremiah 49:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">jer 49:25</a></font></span></p>How is the (a) city of praise not left, the city of my joy! 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He speaks this in the person of the king and of them of the country who will wonder to see Damascus the chief city destroyed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#027">Jeremiah 49:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">jer 49:27</a></font></span></p>And I will kindle a fire in the wall of Damascus, and it shall consume the palaces of (b) Benhadad. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Who was king of Syria, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1020.htm#026">Kg1 20:26</a>) and had built these palaces which were still called the palaces of Benhadad.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#028">Jeremiah 49:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">jer 49:28</a></font></span></p>Concerning (c) Kedar, and concerning the kingdoms of Hazor, which Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon shall smite, thus saith the LORD; Arise ye, go up to Kedar, and lay waste the men of the east. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning the Arabians, and their borders.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#029">Jeremiah 49:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">jer 49:29</a></font></span></p>Their tents and their flocks shall they take away: they shall take to themselves their (d) curtains, and all their vessels, and their camels; and they shall cry to them, Fear [is] on every side. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Because they used to dwell in tents, he names the things that belong to it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#030">Jeremiah 49:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">jer 49:30</a></font></span></p>Flee, go far off, (e) dwell deep, O ye inhabitants of Hazor, saith the LORD; for Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon hath taken counsel against you, and hath conceived a purpose against you. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The enemies will dwell in your places.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#031">Jeremiah 49:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">jer 49:31</a></font></span></p>(f) Arise, go up to the wealthy nation, that dwelleth without care, saith the LORD, which have neither gates nor bars, [which] dwell alone. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He shows that they of Hazor will flee to the Arabians for comfort but that will not help them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#034">Jeremiah 49:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">jer 49:34</a></font></span></p>The word of the LORD that came to Jeremiah the prophet against (g) Elam in the beginning of the reign of Zedekiah king of Judah, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) That is Persia, so called for Elam the son of Shem.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#035">Jeremiah 49:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">jer 49:35</a></font></span></p>Thus saith the LORD of hosts; Behold, I will break the (h) bow of Elam, the chief of their might. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Because the Persians were good archers, he shows that the thing in which they put their trust would not profit them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#038">Jeremiah 49:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">jer 49:38</a></font></span></p>And I will set my (i) throne in Elam, and will destroy from there the king and the princes, saith the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) I will place Nebuchadnezzar there, and in these prophecies Jeremiah speaks of those countries which would be subdued under the first of those four monarchies of which Daniel makes mention.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer049.htm#039">Jeremiah 49:39</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_039">jer 49:39</a></font></span></p>But it shall come to pass (k) in the latter days, [that] I will bring again the captives of Elam, saith the LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) This may be referred to the empire of the Persians and Medes after the Chaldeans or to the time of Christ, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer048.htm#047">Jer 48:47</a>).
