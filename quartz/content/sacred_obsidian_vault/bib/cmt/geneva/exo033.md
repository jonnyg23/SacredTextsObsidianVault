 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 33</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#001">Exodus 33:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 33:1</a></font></span></p>And the LORD said unto Moses, Depart, [and] (a) go up hence, thou and the people which thou hast brought up out of the land of Egypt, unto the land which I sware unto Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, saying, Unto thy seed will I give it: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) The land of Canaan was surrounded by hills: so those who entered it, must go up by the hills.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#005">Exodus 33:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">exo 33:5</a></font></span></p>For the LORD had said unto Moses, Say unto the children of Israel, Ye [are] a stiffnecked people: I will come up into the midst of thee in a moment, and consume thee: therefore now put off thy ornaments from thee, that I may know (b) what to do unto thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) That I may either show mercy if you repent, or else punish your rebellion.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#007">Exodus 33:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">exo 33:7</a></font></span></p>And Moses took the tabernacle, and pitched it without the camp, afar off from the camp, and called it the (c) Tabernacle of the congregation. And it came to pass, [that] every one which sought the LORD went out unto the tabernacle of the congregation, which [was] without the camp. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, the tabernacle of the congregation: so called because the people turned to it, when they needed to be instructed of the Lord's will.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#011">Exodus 33:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 33:11</a></font></span></p>And the LORD spake unto Moses (d) face to face, as a man speaketh unto his friend. And he turned again into the camp: but his servant Joshua, the son of Nun, a young man, departed not out of the tabernacle. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Most clearly and familiarly of all others, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num012.htm#007">Num 12:7-8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu034.htm#010">Deu 34:10</a>)  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#012">Exodus 33:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">exo 33:12</a></font></span></p>And Moses said unto the LORD, See, thou sayest unto me, Bring up this people: and thou hast not let me know whom thou wilt send with me. Yet thou hast said, I know thee by (e) name, and thou hast also found grace in my sight. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) I care for you and will preserve you in your calling.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#014">Exodus 33:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">exo 33:14</a></font></span></p>And he said, My (f) presence shall go [with thee], and I will give thee rest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Signifying that the Israelites would exceed all other people, through God's favour; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#016">Exo 33:16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#018">Exodus 33:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">exo 33:18</a></font></span></p>And he said, I beseech thee, shew me thy (g) glory. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Your face, your substance, and your majesty.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#019">Exodus 33:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">exo 33:19</a></font></span></p>And he said, I will make all my (h) goodness pass before thee, and I will (i) proclaim the name of the LORD before thee; and will be gracious to whom I will be gracious, and will shew (k) mercy on whom I will shew mercy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) My mercy and fatherly care. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo034.htm#006">Exo 34:6-7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(k) For finding nothing in man that deserves mercy, he will freely give his.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#020">Exodus 33:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">exo 33:20</a></font></span></p>And he said, Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and (l) live. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) For Moses did not see his face in full majesty, but as man's weakness could bear.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#021">Exodus 33:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">exo 33:21</a></font></span></p>And the LORD said, Behold, [there is] a place by (m) me, and thou shalt stand upon a rock: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) In mount Horeb.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo033.htm#023">Exodus 33:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">exo 33:23</a></font></span></p>And I will take away mine hand, and thou shalt see my (n) back parts: but my face shall not be seen. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) As much of my glory as in this mortal life you are able to see.
