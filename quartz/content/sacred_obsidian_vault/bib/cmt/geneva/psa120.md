 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 120</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#001">Psalms 120:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 120:1</a></font></span></p>"A Song of (a) degrees." In my (b) distress I cried unto the LORD, and he heard me. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, of lifting up the tune and rising in singing. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Even though the children of God should rejoice when they suffer for righteousness sake, yet it is a great grief to the flesh to hear evil for well doing.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#003">Psalms 120:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 120:3</a></font></span></p>What shall be given unto thee? or what shall be done unto thee, thou (c) false tongue? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He assured himself that God would turn their craft to their own destruction.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#004">Psalms 120:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 120:4</a></font></span></p>(d) Sharp arrows of the mighty, with coals of juniper. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He shows that there is nothing so sharp to pierce, nor so hot to set on fire, as a slanderous tongue.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#005">Psalms 120:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 120:5</a></font></span></p>Woe is me, that I sojourn in (e) Mesech, [that] I dwell in the tents of (f) Kedar! 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) These were people of Arabia, who came from Japheth, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#002">Gen 10:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) That is, of the Ishmaelites.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#007">Psalms 120:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 120:7</a></font></span></p>I [am for] (g) peace: but when I speak, they [are] for war. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) He declares what he means by Meshech and Kedar, that is, the Israelites who had degenerated from their godly fathers, and hated and contended against the faithful.
