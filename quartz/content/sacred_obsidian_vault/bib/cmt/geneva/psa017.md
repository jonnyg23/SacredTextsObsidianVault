 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 17</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#001">Psalms 17:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 17:1</a></font></span></p>"A Prayer of David." Hear (a) the right, O LORD, attend unto my cry, give ear unto my prayer, [that goeth] not out of feigned lips. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) My righteous cause.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#002">Psalms 17:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 17:2</a></font></span></p>Let my (b) sentence come forth from thy presence; let thine eyes behold the things that are equal. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) The vengeance that you will show against my enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#003">Psalms 17:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 17:3</a></font></span></p>Thou hast (c) proved mine heart; thou hast visited [me] in the night; thou hast tried me, [and] shalt find nothing; I am purposed [that] my (d) mouth shall not transgress. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) When your Spirit examined my conscience. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) I was innocent toward my enemy both in deed and thought.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#004">Psalms 17:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 17:4</a></font></span></p>Concerning the works of men, by the (e) word of thy lips I have kept [me from] the paths of the destroyer. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Though the wicked provoked me to do evil for evil, yet your word kept me back.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#006">Psalms 17:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 17:6</a></font></span></p>I have called upon thee, (f) for thou wilt hear me, O God: incline thine ear unto me, [and hear] my speech. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He was assured that God would not refuse his request.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#007">Psalms 17:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 17:7</a></font></span></p>Shew thy marvellous lovingkindness, O thou that savest by thy right hand them which put their trust [in thee] from those that (g) rise up [against them]. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) For all rebel against you, who trouble your Church.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#009">Psalms 17:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 17:9</a></font></span></p>From the wicked that oppress me, [from] my deadly enemies, [who] compass (h) me about. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) For their cruelty cannot be satisfied but with my death.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#010">Psalms 17:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 17:10</a></font></span></p>They are inclosed in their own (i) fat: with their mouth they speak proudly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) They are puffed up with pride, as the stomach that is choked with fat.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#013">Psalms 17:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">psa 17:13</a></font></span></p>Arise, O LORD, (k) disappoint him, cast him down: deliver my soul from the wicked, [which is] thy sword: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Stop his rage.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#014">Psalms 17:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">psa 17:14</a></font></span></p>From men [which are] thy (l) hand, O LORD, from men of the world, [which have] their (m) portion in [this] life, and whose belly thou fillest with thy hid [treasure]: they are full of children, and leave the rest of their [substance] to their babes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) By your heavenly power. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(m) And not feel the pain that God's children often do.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#015">Psalms 17:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 17:15</a></font></span></p>As for me, I will behold thy face (n) in righteousness: I shall be satisfied, when I (o) awake, with thy likeness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) This is the full happiness, comforting against all assaults to have the face of God and favourable countenance opened to us. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(o) And am delivered out of my great troubles.
