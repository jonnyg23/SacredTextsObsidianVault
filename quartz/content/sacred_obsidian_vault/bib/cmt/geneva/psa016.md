 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#001">Psalms 16:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 16:1</a></font></span></p>"Michtam of David." Preserve me, O God: for in thee do I put my (a) trust. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He shows that we cannot call on God unless we trust in him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#002">Psalms 16:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 16:2</a></font></span></p>[O my soul], thou hast said unto the LORD, Thou [art] my Lord: my (b) goodness [extendeth] not to thee; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Though we cannot enrich God, yet we must bestow God's gifts for the use of his children.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#004">Psalms 16:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 16:4</a></font></span></p>Their (c) sorrows shall be multiplied [that] hasten [after] another [god]: (d) their drink offerings of blood will I not offer, nor take up their names into my lips. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) As grief of conscience and miserable destruction. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) He would neither by outward profession nor in heart, nor in mouth consent to their idolatries.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#006">Psalms 16:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 16:6</a></font></span></p>The (e) lines are fallen unto me in pleasant [places]; yea, I have a goodly heritage. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) With which my portion is measured.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#008">Psalms 16:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 16:8</a></font></span></p>I have set the LORD always before me: because [he is] at my right hand, I (g) shall not be moved. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) The faithful are sure to persevere to the end.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#009">Psalms 16:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">psa 16:9</a></font></span></p>Therefore (h) my heart is glad, and my glory rejoiceth: my flesh also shall rest in hope. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) That is, I rejoice both in body and in soul.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#010">Psalms 16:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 16:10</a></font></span></p>For thou (i) wilt not leave my soul in hell; neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) This is chiefly meant by Christ, by whose resurrection all his members have immortality.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#011">Psalms 16:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 16:11</a></font></span></p>Thou wilt shew me the path of life: in thy (k) presence [is] fulness of joy; at thy right hand [there are] pleasures for evermore. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Where God favours there is perfect happiness.
