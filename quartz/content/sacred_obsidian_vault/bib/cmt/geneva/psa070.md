 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa070.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 70</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa070.htm#001">Psalms 70:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 70:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician, [A Psalm] of David, to bring (a) to remembrance." [Make (b) haste], O God, to deliver me; make haste to help me, O LORD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Which might put him in remembrance of his deliverance. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) He teaches us to be earnest in prayer even though God seems to delay: for at his time he will hear us.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa070.htm#002">Psalms 70:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 70:2</a></font></span></p>Let them be ashamed and (c) confounded that seek after my soul: let them be turned backward, and put to confusion, that desire my hurt. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He was assured that the more they raged, the nearer they were to destruction, and he the nearer to his deliverance.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa070.htm#003">Psalms 70:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 70:3</a></font></span></p>Let them be turned back for a reward of their (d) shame that say, Aha, aha. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) By this we are taught not to mock at others in their misery, lest the same fall on our own necks.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa070.htm#005">Psalms 70:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 70:5</a></font></span></p>But I [am] (e) poor and needy: make haste unto me, O God: thou [art] my help and my deliverer; O LORD, make no tarrying. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Because he had felt God's help before, he grounds on experience, and boldly seeks him for help.
