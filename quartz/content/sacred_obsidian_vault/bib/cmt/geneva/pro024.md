 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#007">Proverbs 24:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 24:7</a></font></span></p>Wisdom [is] too high for a fool: he openeth not his mouth in the (a) gate. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) In the place where wisdom should be shown.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#010">Proverbs 24:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 24:10</a></font></span></p>[If] thou (b) faintest in the day of adversity, thy strength [is] small. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Man has no trial of his strength till he is in trouble.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#011">Proverbs 24:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 24:11</a></font></span></p>If thou refraineth to deliver [them that are] drawn to (c) death, and [those that are] ready to be slain; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) No one can be excused, if he does not help the innocent when he is in danger.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#013">Proverbs 24:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">pro 24:13</a></font></span></p>My son, eat thou (d) honey, because [it is] good; and the honeycomb, [which is] sweet to thy taste: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) As honey is sweet and pleasant to the taste, so wisdom is to the soul.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#016">Proverbs 24:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 24:16</a></font></span></p>For a just [man] (e) falleth seven times, and riseth again: but the wicked shall fall into mischief. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) He is subject to many perils, but God delivers him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#018">Proverbs 24:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 24:18</a></font></span></p>Lest the LORD see [it], and it displease him, and he turn away his wrath (f) from him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) To be avenged on you.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#022">Proverbs 24:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 24:22</a></font></span></p>For their calamity shall rise suddenly; and who knoweth the ruin of them (g) both? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Meaning, either of the wicked and seditious, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#019">Pro 24:19</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#021">Pro 24:21</a>) or of them who do not fear God or obey their king.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#027">Proverbs 24:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">pro 24:27</a></font></span></p>Prepare thy work outside, and make it fit for thyself in the field; (h) and afterwards build thy house. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Be sure of the means how to compass it, before you take any enterprise in hand.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#029">Proverbs 24:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">pro 24:29</a></font></span></p>Say not, I will do so to him as he hath done to me: I (i) will render to the man according to his work. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) He shows what is the nature of the wicked, to revenge wrong for wrong.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#032">Proverbs 24:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">pro 24:32</a></font></span></p>Then I saw, [and] considered [it] well: I looked upon [it, and] received (k) instruction. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) That I might learn by another man's fault.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#033">Proverbs 24:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">pro 24:33</a></font></span></p>[Yet] a little sleep, (l) a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to sleep: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#010">Pro 6:10</a>
