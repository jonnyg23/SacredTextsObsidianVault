 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 5</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001">Psalms 5:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 5:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician upon Nehiloth, A Psalm of David." Give ear to my words, O LORD, consider my (a) meditation. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) That is, my vehement prayer and secret complaint and sighings.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#003">Psalms 5:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 5:3</a></font></span></p>My voice shalt thou hear in the morning, O LORD; in the morning will I direct [my prayer] unto thee, and will (b) look up. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Or wait, with patience and trust till I am heard.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#004">Psalms 5:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 5:4</a></font></span></p>For thou [art] not a God that hath pleasure in (c) wickedness: neither shall evil dwell with thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Seeing that God of nature hates wickedness, he must punish the wicked and save the godly.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#005">Psalms 5:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 5:5</a></font></span></p>(d) The foolish shall not stand in thy sight: thou hatest all workers of iniquity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Who run blindly after their carnal desires.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#007">Psalms 5:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 5:7</a></font></span></p>But as for me, I (e) will come [into] thy house in the multitude of thy mercy: [and] in thy fear will I worship toward thy holy temple. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) In the deepest of his temptations he puts his full confidence in God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#008">Psalms 5:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 5:8</a></font></span></p>Lead me, O LORD, in thy righteousness (f) because of mine enemies; make thy way straight before my face. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Because you are just, therefore lead me out of the dangers of my enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#010">Psalms 5:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 5:10</a></font></span></p>Destroy thou them, O God; let them (g) fall by their own counsels; cast them out in the multitude of their transgressions; for they have rebelled against thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Let their devices come to nothing.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011">Psalms 5:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 5:11</a></font></span></p>But (h) let all those that put their trust in thee rejoice: let them ever shout for joy, because thou defendest them: let them also that love thy name be joyful in thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Your favour toward me will confirm the faith of all others.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#012">Psalms 5:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 5:12</a></font></span></p>For thou, LORD, wilt bless the righteous; with favour (i) wilt thou compass him as [with] a shield. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) So that he will be safe from all dangers.
