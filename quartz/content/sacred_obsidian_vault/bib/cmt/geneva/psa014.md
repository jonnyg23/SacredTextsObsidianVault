 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#001">Psalms 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 14:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician, [A Psalm] of David." The fool hath said in his heart, (a) [There is] no God. They are (b) corrupt, they have done abominable works, [there is] none that doeth good. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He shows that the cause of all wickedness if forgetting God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) There is nothing but disorder and wickedness among them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#003">Psalms 14:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 14:3</a></font></span></p>They are (c) all gone aside, they are [all] together become filthy: [there is] none that doeth good, no, not one. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) David here makes comparisons between the faithful and the reprobate, but Paul speaks the same of all men naturally, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#010">Rom 3:10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#005">Psalms 14:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 14:5</a></font></span></p>(d) There were they in great fear: for God [is] in the generation of the righteous. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Where they think themselves most sure.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#006">Psalms 14:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 14:6</a></font></span></p>Ye have (e) shamed the counsel of the poor, because the LORD [is] his refuge. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) You mock them who put their trust in God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#007">Psalms 14:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 14:7</a></font></span></p>Oh that the salvation of (f) Israel [were come] out of Zion! when the LORD bringeth back the captivity of his people, Jacob shall rejoice, [and] Israel shall be glad. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) He prays for the whole Church whom he is assured God will deliver: for no one else but he can do it.
