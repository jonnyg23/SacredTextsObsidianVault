 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 39</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#001">Exodus 39:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 39:1</a></font></span></p>And of the blue, and purple, and scarlet, they made (a) cloths of service, to do service in the holy [place], and made the holy garments for Aaron; as the LORD commanded Moses. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) As coverings for the ark, the candlestick, the altars and such like.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#006">Exodus 39:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">exo 39:6</a></font></span></p>And they wrought onyx stones inclosed in ouches of gold, graven, as (b) signets are graven, with the names of the children of Israel. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) That is, of very fine and curious workmanship.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#012">Exodus 39:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">exo 39:12</a></font></span></p>And the third row, (c) a ligure, an agate, and an amethyst. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Or, a turkeis, a stone which the authors write comes from the urine of the Lynx.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#014">Exodus 39:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">exo 39:14</a></font></span></p>And the stones [were] according to the names of the children of Israel, twelve, (d) according to their names, [like] the engravings of a signet, every one with his name, according to the twelve tribes. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, every tribe had his name written on a stone.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#022">Exodus 39:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">exo 39:22</a></font></span></p>And he made the robe of the (e) ephod [of] woven work, all [of] blue. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Which was next under the Ephod.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#023">Exodus 39:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">exo 39:23</a></font></span></p>And (f) [there was] an hole in the midst of the robe, as the hole of an habergeon, [with] a band round about the hole, that it should not rend. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Where he could put his head through.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#034">Exodus 39:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">exo 39:34</a></font></span></p>And the covering of rams' skins dyed red, and the covering of badgers' skins, and the (g) vail of the covering, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) So called, because it hung before the mercyseat and covered it from sight (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo035.htm#012">Exo 35:12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#037">Exodus 39:37</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_037">exo 39:37</a></font></span></p>The pure candlestick, [with] the lamps thereof, [even with] the lamps to be (h) set in order, and all the vessels thereof, and the oil for light, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Or, which Aaron dressed and refreshed with oil every morning (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#007">Exo 30:7</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#042">Exodus 39:42</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_042">exo 39:42</a></font></span></p>According to all that the LORD (i) commanded Moses, so the children of Israel made all the work. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Signifying that in God's matters man may neither add, nor diminish.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo039.htm#043">Exodus 39:43</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_043">exo 39:43</a></font></span></p>And Moses did look upon all the work, and, behold, they had done it as the LORD had commanded, even so had they done it: and Moses (k) blessed them. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Praised God for the peoples diligence and prayed for them.
