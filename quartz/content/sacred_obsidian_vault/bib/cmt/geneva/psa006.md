 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#001">Psalms 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 6:1</a></font></span></p>"To the chief Musician on Neginoth upon Sheminith, A Psalm of David." O LORD, (a) rebuke me not in thine anger, neither chasten me in thy hot displeasure. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Though I deserve destruction, yet let your mercy pity my frailty.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#002">Psalms 6:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 6:2</a></font></span></p>Have mercy upon me, O LORD; for I [am] weak: O LORD, heal me; for my (b) bones are vexed. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For my whole strength is abated.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#003">Psalms 6:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 6:3</a></font></span></p>(c) My soul is also sore vexed: but thou, O LORD, how long? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) His conscience is also touched with the fear of God's judgment.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#005">Psalms 6:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 6:5</a></font></span></p>For in (d) death [there is] no remembrance of thee: in the grave who shall give thee thanks? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He laments that opportunity should be taken from him to praise God in the congregation.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#008">Psalms 6:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">psa 6:8</a></font></span></p>(e) Depart from me, all ye workers of iniquity; for the LORD hath heard the voice of my weeping. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) God sends comfort and boldness in affliction, that we may triumph over our enemies.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#010">Psalms 6:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 6:10</a></font></span></p>Let all mine enemies be ashamed and sore vexed: let them return [and] be ashamed (f) suddenly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) When the wicked think the godly will perish, God delivers them suddenly, and destroys their enemies.
