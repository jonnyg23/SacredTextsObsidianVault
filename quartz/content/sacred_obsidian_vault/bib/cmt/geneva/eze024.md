 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#001">Ezekiel 24:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">eze 24:1</a></font></span></p>Again in the (a) ninth year, in the tenth month, in the tenth [day] of the (b) month, the word of the LORD came to me, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Of Jeconiah's captivity and of the reign of Zedekiah, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#001">Kg2 25:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Called Tebeth, which contains part of December and part of January: in which month and day Nebuchadnezzar besieged Jerusalem.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#003">Ezekiel 24:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 24:3</a></font></span></p>And utter a parable to the rebellious house, and say to them, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Set on a (c) pot, set [it] on, and also pour water into it: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) By which was meant Jerusalem.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#004">Ezekiel 24:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">eze 24:4</a></font></span></p>Gather its (d) pieces into it, [even] every good piece, the thigh, and the shoulder; fill [it] with the choice bones. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) That is the citizens and the chief men of it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#005">Ezekiel 24:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">eze 24:5</a></font></span></p>Take the choice of the flock, and burn also the (e) bones under it, [and] make it boil well, and let them boil its bones in it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Meaning, of the innocents whom they had slain, who were the cause of the kindling of God's wrath against them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#006">Ezekiel 24:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 24:6</a></font></span></p>Wherefore thus saith the Lord GOD; Woe to the bloody city, to the pot (f) whose scum [is] in it, and whose scum is not gone out of it! bring it out (g) piece by piece; let no (h) lot fall upon it. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Whose iniquities and wicked citizens there yet remain. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(g) Signifying that they should not be destroyed all at once, but little by little. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(h) Spare no estate or condition.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#007">Ezekiel 24:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">eze 24:7</a></font></span></p>For her blood is in the midst of her; she set it upon the top of a (i) rock; she poured it not upon the ground, to cover it with dust; 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) The city showed her cruelty to all the world, and was not ashamed of it, neither yet hid it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#010">Ezekiel 24:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">eze 24:10</a></font></span></p>Heap on wood, (k) kindle the fire, consume the flesh, and spice it well, and let the bones be burned. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Meaning that the city would be utterly destroyed and that he would give the enemies an appetite for it.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#012">Ezekiel 24:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 24:12</a></font></span></p>(l) She hath wearied [herself] with lies, and her great scum went not forth out of her: her scum [shall be] in the fire. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) The city has flattered herself in vain.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#013">Ezekiel 24:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">eze 24:13</a></font></span></p>In thy filthiness [is] lewdness: because I (m) have purged thee, and thou wast not purged, thou shalt not be purged from thy filthiness any more, till I have caused my fury to rest upon thee. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) I laboured by sending my prophets to call you to repentance but you would not.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#014">Ezekiel 24:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 24:14</a></font></span></p>I the LORD have spoken [it]: it shall come to pass, and I will do [it]; I will not go back, neither will I spare, neither will I repent; according to thy ways, and according to thy doings, shall (n) they judge thee, saith the Lord GOD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(n) That is, the Babylonians.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#016">Ezekiel 24:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">eze 24:16</a></font></span></p>Son of man, behold, I take away from thee the (o) desire of thy eyes with a stroke: yet neither shalt thou mourn nor weep, neither shall thy tears run down. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(o) Meaning his wife in whom he delighted, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#018">Eze 24:18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#017">Ezekiel 24:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">eze 24:17</a></font></span></p>Forbear to cry, make no mourning for the dead, bind thy turban upon thee, (p) and put on thy shoes upon thy feet, and cover not [thy] lips, and eat (q) not the bread of men. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(p) For in mourning they went bare headed and barefooted and also covered their lips. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(q) That is, which the neighbours sent to them that mourned.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#018">Ezekiel 24:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">eze 24:18</a></font></span></p>So I spoke to the people in the morning: and at evening my wife died; and I did (r) in the morning as I was commanded. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(r) Meaning the morning following.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#021">Ezekiel 24:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">eze 24:21</a></font></span></p>Speak to the house of Israel, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Behold, I will (s) profane my sanctuary, the (t) excellence of your strength, the desire of your eyes, and that which your soul pitieth; and your sons and your daughters whom ye have left shall fall by the sword. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(s) By sending the Chaldeans to destroy it, as in See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#022">Eze 7:22</a>. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(t) In which you boast and delight.
