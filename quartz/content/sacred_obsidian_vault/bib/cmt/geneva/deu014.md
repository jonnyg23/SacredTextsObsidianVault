 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#001">Deuteronomy Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#002">Deuteronomy 14:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">deu 14:2</a></font></span></p>For thou [art] an holy people unto the LORD thy God, and the LORD hath chosen thee to be a (a) peculiar people unto himself, above all the nations that [are] upon the earth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Therefore you should not follow the superstition of the Gentiles.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#004">Deuteronomy 14:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">deu 14:4</a></font></span></p>(b) These [are] the beasts which ye shall eat: the ox, the sheep, and the goat, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) This ceremonial Law instructed the Jews to seek a spiritual pureness, even in their meat and drink.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#021">Deuteronomy 14:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">deu 14:21</a></font></span></p>Ye shall not eat [of] any thing that (c) dieth of itself: thou shalt give it unto the (d) stranger that [is] in thy gates, that he may eat it; or thou mayest sell it unto an alien: for thou [art] an holy people unto the LORD thy God. Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother's milk. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Because their blood was not shed, but remains in them. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) Who is not of your religion.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#022">Deuteronomy 14:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">deu 14:22</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt truly (e) tithe all the increase of thy seed, that the field bringeth forth year by year. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) The tithes were ordained for the maintenance of the Levites, who had no inheritance.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#024">Deuteronomy 14:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">deu 14:24</a></font></span></p>And if the way be too long for thee, so that thou art not able to carry it; [or] if the place be too far from thee, which the LORD thy God shall choose to set his name there, (f) when the LORD thy God hath blessed thee: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) When he shall give thee abilities.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#026">Deuteronomy 14:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">deu 14:26</a></font></span></p>And thou shalt bestow that money for whatsoever thy soul lusteth after, for oxen, or for sheep, or for wine, or for strong drink, or for whatsoever thy soul desireth: (g) and thou shalt eat there before the LORD thy God, and thou shalt rejoice, thou, and thine household, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) After the Priest has received the Lord's part.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#028">Deuteronomy 14:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">deu 14:28</a></font></span></p>At the end of three years thou shalt (h) bring forth all the tithe of thine increase the same year, and shalt lay [it] up within thy gates: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Besides the yearly tithes that were given to the Levites, these were laid up in store for the poor.
