 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#001">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">sol 4:1</a></font></span></p>Behold, thou [art] (a) fair, my love; behold, thou [art] fair; thou [hast] doves' eyes within thy locks: thy hair [is] as a (b) flock of goats, that appear from mount Gilead. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Because Christ delights in his Church, he commends all that is in her. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) He has respect for the multitude of the faithful, which are many in number.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#005">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">sol 4:5</a></font></span></p>Thy two (c) breasts [are] like two young roes that are twins, which feed among the lilies. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) In which are knowledge and zeal two precious jewels.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#008">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">sol 4:8</a></font></span></p>(d) Come with me from Lebanon, [my] spouse, with me from Lebanon: look from the top of Amana, from the top of Shenir and Hermon, from the lions' dens, from the mountains of the leopards. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Christ promises his Church to call his faithful from all the corners of the world.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#009">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">sol 4:9</a></font></span></p>Thou hast ravished my heart, my (e) sister, [my] spouse; thou hast ravished my heart with one of thy (f) eyes, with one chain of thy neck. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Christ calls his Church sister in respect that he had taken the flesh of man. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) In that he made his Church beautiful and rich, he loved his gifts in her.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#011">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">sol 4:11</a></font></span></p>Thy (g) lips, O [my] spouse, drop [as] the honeycomb: honey and milk [are] under thy tongue; and the scent of thy garments [is] like the fragrance of Lebanon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) Because of your confession and thanksgiving.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#015">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">sol 4:15</a></font></span></p>(h) A fountain of gardens, a well of living waters, and streams from Lebanon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) The Church confesses that all her glory and beauty comes from Christ who is the true fountain of all grace.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sol004.htm#016">Song of Solomon (Canticles) 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">sol 4:16</a></font></span></p>Awake, O (i) north wind; and come, thou south; blow upon my garden, [that] its spices may flow out. Let my beloved come into his garden, and eat his pleasant fruits. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) She desires Christ to comfort her and to pour the graces of his Spirit on her, which is meant by the North and South wind.
