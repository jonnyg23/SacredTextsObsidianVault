 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#001">Ecclesiastes Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#002">Ecclesiastes 10:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ecc 10:2</a></font></span></p>A (a) wise man's heart [is] at his right hand; but a fool's heart [is] at his left. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) So that he does all things well and justly, where as the fool does the contrary.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#003">Ecclesiastes 10:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">ecc 10:3</a></font></span></p>Also, when he that is a fool walketh by the way, his wisdom faileth [him], and he (b) saith to every one [that] he [is] a fool. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) By his doings he betrays himself.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#004">Ecclesiastes 10:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">ecc 10:4</a></font></span></p>If the (c) spirit of the ruler riseth against thee, leave not thy place; for yielding pacifieth great offences. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) If your superior is angry with you, be discrete and not moved.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#005">Ecclesiastes 10:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">ecc 10:5</a></font></span></p>There is an evil [which] I have seen under the sun, as an (d) error [which] proceedeth from the ruler: 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Meaning that it is an evil thing when they who are in authority fail and do not do their duty.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#006">Ecclesiastes 10:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ecc 10:6</a></font></span></p>Folly is set in great dignity, and the (e) rich sit in low place. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) They who are rich in wisdom and virtue.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#010">Ecclesiastes 10:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">ecc 10:10</a></font></span></p>If the iron is blunt, and he doth not whet the edge, then must he use more (f) strength: but wisdom [is] profitable to direct. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Without wisdom, whatever a man takes in hand, turns to his own hurt.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#015">Ecclesiastes 10:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">ecc 10:15</a></font></span></p>The labour of the foolish wearieth every one of them, because he knoweth not how to go to the (g) city. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) The ignorance and beastliness of the wicked is such that they know not common things, and yet will discuss high matters.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#016">Ecclesiastes 10:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">ecc 10:16</a></font></span></p>Woe to thee, O land, when thy king [is] a (h) child, and thy princes (i) eat in the morning! 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) That is, without wisdom and counsel. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(i) Are given to their lusts and pleasures.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#017">Ecclesiastes 10:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ecc 10:17</a></font></span></p>Blessed [art] thou, O land, when thy king [is] the son (k) of nobles, and thy princes eat in due season, for strength, and not for drunkenness! 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Meaning, when he is noble for virtue and wisdom and with the gifts of God.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#020">Ecclesiastes 10:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">ecc 10:20</a></font></span></p>Curse not the king, no not in thy thought; and curse not the rich in thy bedchamber: for a (l) bird of the air shall carry the voice, and that which hath wings shall tell the matter. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) You cannot work evil so secretly that it will not be known.
