 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 30</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#005">Ezekiel 30:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">eze 30:5</a></font></span></p>(a) Cush, and Libya, and Lydia, and all the mingled people, and Chub, and the men of the land that is in league, shall fall with them by the sword. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) By Phut and Lud are meant Africa and Libya.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#006">Ezekiel 30:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 30:6</a></font></span></p>Thus saith the LORD; They also that uphold Egypt shall fall; and the pride of her power shall come down: from the tower of (b) Syene shall they fall in it by the sword, saith the Lord GOD. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Which was a strong city of Egypt, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#010">Eze 29:10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#018">Ezekiel 30:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">eze 30:18</a></font></span></p>At Tehaphnehes also the day (c) shall be darkened, when I shall break there the (d) yokes of Egypt: and the pomp of her strength shall cease in her: as for her, a cloud shall cover her, and her daughters shall go into captivity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Meaning that there will be great sorrow and affliction. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(d) That is, the strength and force.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#020">Ezekiel 30:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">eze 30:20</a></font></span></p>And it came to pass in the (e) eleventh year, in the first [month], in the seventh [day] of the month, [that] the word of the LORD came to me, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Of the captivity of Jeconiah, or of Zedekiah's reign.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#021">Ezekiel 30:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">eze 30:21</a></font></span></p>Son of man, (f) I have broken the arm of Pharaoh king of Egypt; and, lo, it shall not be bound up to be healed, to put a bandage to bind it, to make it strong to hold the sword. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) For Nebuchadnezzar destroyed Pharaoh Nebo at Carchemish, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer046.htm#026">Jer 46:26</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#022">Ezekiel 30:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">eze 30:22</a></font></span></p>Therefore thus saith the Lord GOD; Behold, I [am] against Pharaoh king of Egypt, and will break (g) his arms, the strong, and that which was broken; and I will cause the sword to fall out of his hand. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) His force and power.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#025">Ezekiel 30:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">eze 30:25</a></font></span></p>But I will strengthen the arms of the king of Babylon, and the arms of Pharaoh shall fall down; and they shall know that I [am] the LORD, (h) when I shall put my sword into the hand of the king of Babylon, and he shall stretch it out upon the land of Egypt. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) By which we see that tyrants have no power of themselves, neither can do any more harm than God appoints and when he wills they must cease.
