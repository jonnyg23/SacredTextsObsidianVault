 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#001">Proverbs 21:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 21:1</a></font></span></p>The (a) king's heart [is] in the hand of the LORD, [as] the rivers of water: he turneth it wherever he will. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Though kings seem to have all things at commandment, they are not able to bring their own purposes to pass unless God has appointed: much less are the inferiors able.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#004">Proverbs 21:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 21:4</a></font></span></p>An high look, and a proud heart, [and] the (b) plowing of the wicked, [is] sin. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) That is, the thing by which he is guided or which he brings forth as the fruit of his work.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#005">Proverbs 21:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">pro 21:5</a></font></span></p>The thoughts of the diligent [tend] only to plenteousness; but of (c) every one [that is] hasty only to want. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) He who goes rashly about his business and without counsel.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#007">Proverbs 21:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 21:7</a></font></span></p>The (d) robbery of the wicked shall destroy them; because they refuse to do judgment. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) He means this chiefly of judges and princes who leave that calling, to which God has called them, and impoverish their subjects to maintain their lusts.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#011">Proverbs 21:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">pro 21:11</a></font></span></p>(e) When the scorner is punished, the simple is made wise: and when the wise is instructed, he receiveth knowledge. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#025">Pro 19:25</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#012">Proverbs 21:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 21:12</a></font></span></p>The righteous [man] wisely (f) considereth the house of the wicked: [but God] overthroweth the wicked for [their] wickedness. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Though the godly admonish them both by words and example of life, yet the wicked will not amend, till God destroys them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#014">Proverbs 21:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 21:14</a></font></span></p>A (g) gift in secret pacifieth anger: and a reward in the bosom strong wrath. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) To do a pleasure to the angry man pacifies him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#018">Proverbs 21:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 21:18</a></font></span></p>The (h) wicked [shall be] a ransom for the righteous, and the transgressor for the upright. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) God will cause that to fall on their own heads, which they intended against the just by delivering the just, and putting the wicked in their places.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#020">Proverbs 21:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">pro 21:20</a></font></span></p>[There is] a treasure to be desired and (i) oil in the dwelling of the wise; but a foolish man spendeth it up. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) Meaning, abundance of all things.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#022">Proverbs 21:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">pro 21:22</a></font></span></p>A (k) wise [man] scaleth the city of the mighty, and casteth down the strength of its confidence. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) Wisdom overcomes strength and confidence in worldly things.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#025">Proverbs 21:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 21:25</a></font></span></p>The desire of the slothful (l) killeth him; for his hands refuse to labour. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(l) He thinks to live by wishing and desiring all things, but will make no effort to get anything.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#028">Proverbs 21:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">pro 21:28</a></font></span></p>A false witness shall perish: but the man that heareth (m) speaketh constantly. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(m) He may boldly testify the truth that he has heard.
