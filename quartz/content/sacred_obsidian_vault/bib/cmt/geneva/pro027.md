 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 27</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#001">Proverbs 27:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 27:1</a></font></span></p>Boast not thyself of to (a) morrow; for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Do not delay the time, but take the opportunity when it is offered.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#004">Proverbs 27:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 27:4</a></font></span></p>Wrath [is] cruel, and anger [is] outrageous; but who [is] able to stand before (b) envy? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) For the envious are obstinate, and cannot be reconciled.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#006">Proverbs 27:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">pro 27:6</a></font></span></p>Faithful [are] the wounds of a friend; but the kisses of an enemy [are] (c) deceitful. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) They are flattering and seem friendly.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#010">Proverbs 27:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">pro 27:10</a></font></span></p>Thy own friend, and thy father's friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother's (d) house in the day of thy calamity: [for] better [is] a neighbour [that is] near than a brother far off. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Do not trust any worldly help in the day of your trouble.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#012">Proverbs 27:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">pro 27:12</a></font></span></p>(e) A prudent [man] foreseeth the evil, [and] hideth himself; [but] the simple pass on, [and] are punished. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#003">Pro 22:3</a>  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#014">Proverbs 27:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">pro 27:14</a></font></span></p>He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising (f) early in the morning, it shall be counted a curse to him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Hastily and without cause.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#017">Proverbs 27:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 27:17</a></font></span></p>Iron sharpeneth iron; so a (g) man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) One hasty man provokes another to anger.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#019">Proverbs 27:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 27:19</a></font></span></p>As in water face [answereth] to face, (h) so the heart of man to man. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) There is no difference between men by nature, only the grace of God makes the difference.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#021">Proverbs 27:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">pro 27:21</a></font></span></p>[As] the refining pot for silver, and the furnace for gold; so [is] a man to his (i) praise. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(i) That is, he is either known to be ambitious and glorious, or humble and modest.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#026">Proverbs 27:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 27:26</a></font></span></p>The (k) lambs [are] for thy clothing, and the goats [are] the price of the field. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(k) This declares the great goodness of God towards man, and the diligence that he requires from him for the preservation of his gifts.
