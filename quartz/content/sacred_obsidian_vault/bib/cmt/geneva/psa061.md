 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 61</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#002">Psalms 61:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 61:2</a></font></span></p>From (a) the end of the earth will I cry unto thee, when my heart is overwhelmed: lead me to the rock [that] is (b) higher than I. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) From the place where I was banished, being driven out of the city and temple by my son Absalom. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) To which without your help I cannot attain.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#005">Psalms 61:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">psa 61:5</a></font></span></p>For thou, O God, (c) hast heard my vows: thou hast given [me] the heritage of those that fear thy name. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) There is nothing that strengthens our faith more than the memory of God's help in times past.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#006">Psalms 61:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 61:6</a></font></span></p>Thou wilt prolong the king's (d) life: [and] his years as many generations. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) This chiefly refers to Christ, who lives eternally not only in himself but also in his members.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa061.htm#007">Psalms 61:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 61:7</a></font></span></p>He shall abide before God for ever: O prepare (e) mercy and truth, [which] may preserve him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) For the stability of my kingdom stands in your mercy and truth.
