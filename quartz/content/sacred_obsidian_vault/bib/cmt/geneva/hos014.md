 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#001">Hosea Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#001">Hosea 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">hos 14:1</a></font></span></p>O Israel, (a) return unto the LORD thy God; for thou hast fallen by thine iniquity. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) He exhorts them to repentance to avoid all these plagues, exhorting them to declare by words their obedience and repentance.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#002">Hosea 14:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">hos 14:2</a></font></span></p>Take with you words, and turn to the LORD: say unto him, (b) Take away all iniquity, and receive [us] graciously: so will we render the calves of our (c) lips. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) He shows them that they ought to confess their sins. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(c) Declaring that this is the true sacrifice that the faithful can offer, even thanks and praise; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#015">Heb 13:15</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#003">Hosea 14:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">hos 14:3</a></font></span></p>Asshur shall (d) not save us; we will not ride upon horses: neither will we say any more to the work of our hands, [Ye are] our gods: for in thee the fatherless findeth mercy. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) We will forsake all vain confidence and pride.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#004">Hosea 14:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">hos 14:4</a></font></span></p>(e) I will heal their backsliding, I will love them freely: for mine anger is turned away from him. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) He declares how ready God is to receive those that do repent.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#007">Hosea 14:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">hos 14:7</a></font></span></p>They that dwell under his (f) shadow shall return; they shall revive [as] the corn, and grow as the vine: the scent thereof [shall be] as the wine of Lebanon. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Whoever unites themselves to this people will be blessed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#008">Hosea 14:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">hos 14:8</a></font></span></p>Ephraim [shall say], What have I to do any more with idols? I (g) have heard [him], and observed him: I [am] like a green fir tree. From me is thy fruit found. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(g) God shows how prompt he is to hear his own when they repent, and to offer himself as a protection and safeguard for them, as a most sufficient fruit and benefit.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos014.htm#009">Hosea 14:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">hos 14:9</a></font></span></p>Who [is] (h) wise, and he shall understand these [things]? prudent, and he shall know them? for the ways of the LORD [are] right, and the just shall walk in them: but the transgressors shall fall therein. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(h) Signifying that the true wisdom and knowledge consists in this, even to rest upon God.  
 </p><p></p>
