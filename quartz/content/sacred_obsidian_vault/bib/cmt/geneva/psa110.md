 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 110</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#001">Psalms 110:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 110:1</a></font></span></p>"A Psalm of David." The (a) LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou at my right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Jesus Christ in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#044">Mat 22:44</a>) gives the interpretation of this, and shows that this cannot properly be applied to David but to himself.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#002">Psalms 110:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">psa 110:2</a></font></span></p>The LORD shall send the rod of thy strength out of (b) Zion: rule thou in the midst of thine enemies. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) And then it will stretch through all the world: and this power chiefly stands in the preaching of his word.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#003">Psalms 110:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 110:3</a></font></span></p>Thy people [shall be] willing in the day of (c) thy power, in the beauties of holiness from the womb of the morning: thou hast the dew of thy youth. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) By the word your people will be assembled into your Church...increase will be...anointed wonderful... drops of the...  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#004">Psalms 110:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">psa 110:4</a></font></span></p>The LORD hath sworn, and will not repent, Thou [art] a priest for ever after the order of (d) Melchizedek. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) As Melchizedek the figure of Christ was both a King and Priest, so this effect cannot be accomplished on any king save only Christ.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#006">Psalms 110:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 110:6</a></font></span></p>He shall judge among the heathen, he shall fill [the places] with the dead bodies; he shall wound the (e) heads over many countries. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) No power will be able to resist him.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#007">Psalms 110:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">psa 110:7</a></font></span></p>He shall (f) drink of the brook in the way: therefore shall he lift up the head. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Under this comparison of a captain that is so eager to destroy his enemies that he will not scarce drink by the way, he shows how God will destroy his enemies.
