 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer045.htm#001">Jeremiah Chapter 45</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer045.htm#001">Jeremiah 45:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jer 45:1</a></font></span></p>The word that Jeremiah the prophet spoke to (a) Baruch the son of Neriah, when he had written these (b) words in a book from the mouth of Jeremiah, in the fourth year of Jehoiakim the son of Josiah king of Judah, saying, 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Who was Jeremiah's disciple, and wrote his prophecies under him. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(b) Of which read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#009">Jer 36:9-10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer045.htm#003">Jeremiah 45:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">jer 45:3</a></font></span></p>Thou didst say, Woe is me now! for the LORD hath added grief to my sorrow; I (c) fainted in my sighing, and I find no rest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) Baruch moved with an inconsiderate zeal for Jeremiah's imprisonment, but chiefly for the destruction of the people and the temple makes this lamentation, as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#006">Psa 6:6</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer045.htm#004">Jeremiah 45:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">jer 45:4</a></font></span></p>Thus shalt thou say to him, The LORD saith thus; Behold, [that] which I have built I will (d) break down, and that which I have planted I will pluck up, even this whole land. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) Meaning that God could destroy this people, because he had planted them.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer045.htm#005">Jeremiah 45:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">jer 45:5</a></font></span></p>And seekest (e) thou great things for thyself? seek [them] not: for, behold, I will bring evil upon all flesh, saith the LORD: but thy life will I give to thee for (f) a prize in all places where thou goest. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) "Do you think to have honour and credit?" in which he shows his infirmity. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(f) Read (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer021.htm#009">Jer 21:9</a>).
