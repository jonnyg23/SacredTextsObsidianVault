 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Geneva Bible Translation Notes</i>, [1599], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#001">Ruth Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#001">Ruth 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rut 3:1</a></font></span></p>Then Naomi her mother in law said unto her, My daughter, shall I not seek (a) rest for thee, that it may be well with thee? 
 <p>
 </p><p>(a) Meaning that she would provide her with a husband, with whom she might live peacefully.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#003">Ruth 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rut 3:3</a></font></span></p>Wash thyself therefore, and anoint thee, and put thy raiment upon thee, and get thee down to the floor: [but] make not thyself known unto the (b) man, until he shall have done eating and drinking. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(b) Boaz, nor yet any other.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#007">Ruth 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">rut 3:7</a></font></span></p>And when Boaz had eaten and drunk, and his (c) heart was merry, he went to lie down at the end of the heap of corn: and she came softly, and uncovered his feet, and laid her down. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(c) That is, he had refreshed himself among his servants.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#010">Ruth 3:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">rut 3:10</a></font></span></p>And he said, Blessed [be] thou of the LORD, my daughter: [for] thou hast (d) shewed more kindness in the latter end than at the beginning, inasmuch as thou followedst not young men, whether poor or rich. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(d) You showed yourself repeatedly to be more virtuous.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#013">Ruth 3:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">rut 3:13</a></font></span></p>Tarry this night, and it shall be in the morning, [that] if he (e) will perform unto thee the part of a kinsman, well; let him do the kinsman's part: but if he will not do the part of a kinsman to thee, then will I do the part of a kinsman to thee, [as] the LORD liveth: lie down until the morning. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(e) If he will take you to as his wife because of his relation by marriage, according to God's law, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu025.htm#005">Deu 25:5</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut003.htm#016">Ruth 3:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">rut 3:16</a></font></span></p>And when she came to her mother in law, she said, (f) Who [art] thou, my daughter? And she told her all that the man had done to her. 
 <p>
 </p><p>(f) Believing by her returning home that he had not taken her as his wife, she was astonished.
