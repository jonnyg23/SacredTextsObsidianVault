 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#001">Titus Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#001">Titus 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">tit 3:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Put them in mind (ὑπομίμνησκε ἐξουσίαις)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#014">Ti2 2:14</a>, and see on ὑπόμνησιν reminding, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2001.htm#005">Ti2 1:5</a>.
 </p><p>Principalities and powers (ἀρχαῖς ἐξουσίαις)
 </p><p>Omit and. Principalities which are authorities. Ἁρχή beginning = that which begins: the leader, principality. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#016">Col 1:16</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#006">Jde 1:6</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#011">Act 10:11</a>. Only here in Pastorals. Ἑξουσία right, authority. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar002.htm#010">Mar 2:10</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#016">Col 1:16</a>. Only here in Pastorals. For the combination principalities and powers, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#020">Luk 20:20</a>.
 </p><p>To obey magistrates (πειθαρχεῖν)
 </p><p>Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#029">Act 5:29</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#032">Act 5:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act027.htm#021">Act 27:21</a>. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#029">Act 5:29</a>. The idea of magistrates is contained in the word itself; but it is quite proper to render as Rev. to be obedient. Rare in lxx.
 </p><p>Ready to every good work (πρὸς πᾶν ἔργον ἀγαθὸν ἑτοίμους εἶναι)
 </p><p>The phrase N.T.o. Ἑτοίμος ready, only here in Pastorals. Comp. ἑτοιμασία readiness or preparation, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#015">Eph 6:15</a> (note). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#002">Titus 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">tit 3:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>No brawlers (ἀμάχους)
 </p><p>Better as Rev., not to be contentious. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#003">Ti1 3:3</a>. Pasto. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#003">Titus 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">tit 3:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Divers - pleasures (ἡδοναῖς ποικίλαις)
 </p><p>Ἡδονή pleasure, only here in Pastorals. oP. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#001">Jam 4:1</a>. For ποικίλαις divers, see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#006">Ti2 3:6</a>.
 </p><p>Malice (κακίᾳ)
 </p><p>Only here in Pastorals. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#021">Jam 1:21</a>. In N.T. κακία is a special form of vice, not viciousness in general, as Cicero, Tusc. iv. 15, who explains by "vitiositas, a viciousness which includes all vices." Calvin, on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#032">Eph 4:32</a>, defines as " a viciousness of mind opposed to humanity and fairness, and commonly styled malignity." The homily ascribed to Clement of Rome, describes κακία as the forerunner (προοδοίπορον) of our sins (x). Malice is a correct translation. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#004">Titus 3:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">tit 3:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Kindness (χρηστότης)
 </p><p>Only here in Pastorals. Elsewhere only in Paul. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#012">Rom 3:12</a>, and see on easy, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#030">Mat 11:30</a>.
 </p><p>Love (φιλανθρωπία)
 </p><p>Love is too vague. It is love toward men; comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#002">Tit 3:2</a>. Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#002">Act 28:2</a> : φιλανθρώπως kindly, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act027.htm#003">Act 27:3</a> (note). While it cannot be asserted that the heretical characteristics noted in the Pastoral Epistles point collectively to any specific form of error, it is true, nevertheless, that certain characteristics of the economy of grace are emphasized, which are directly opposed to Gnostic ideas. Thus the exhortation that supplications be made for all men, supported by the statement that God wills that all men should be saved and come to the knowledge of the truth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#001">Ti1 2:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#004">Ti1 2:4</a>), is in the teeth of the Gnostic distinction between men of spirit and men of matter, and of the Gnostic principle that the knowledge (ἐπίγνωσις) of truth was only for a limited, intellectual class. To the same effect is the frequent recurrence of all, for all, in connection with the saving and enlightening gifts of God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#006">Ti1 2:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1004.htm#010">Ti1 4:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#013">Ti1 6:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#011">Tit 2:11</a>). So here: not only has the saving grace of God appeared unto all (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#011">Tit 2:11</a>), but it has revealed itself as kindness and love to man as man. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#005">Titus 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">tit 3:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Not by works of righteousness which we have done (οὐκ ἐξ ἔργων τῶν ἐν δικαιοσύνῃ ἃ ἐποιήσαμεν)
 </p><p>Lit. not by works, those namely in righteousness, which we did. The thought is entirely Pauline. Ἑξ ἔργων strictly, out of, in consequence of works. Εν δικαιοσύνῃ in the sphere of righteousness; as legally righteous men. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#009">Eph 2:9</a>. We did emphatic. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#005">Rom 10:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#010">Gal 3:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#012">Gal 3:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#003">Gal 5:3</a>.
 </p><p>According to his mercy (κατὰ τὸ αὐτοῦ ἔλεος)
 </p><p>The phrase only <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#003">Pe1 1:3</a>. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#009">Rom 15:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#004">Eph 2:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#021">Jde 1:21</a>.
 </p><p>By the washing of regeneration (διὰ λουτροῦ παλινγενεσίας)
 </p><p>Λουτρόν only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#026">Eph 5:26</a>. It does not mean the act of bathing, but the bath, the laver. Παλινγενεσία only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#028">Mat 19:28</a>, where it is used of the final restoration of all things. The phrase laver of regeneration distinctly refers to baptism, in connection with which and through which as a medium regeneration is conceived as taking place. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#003">Rom 6:3-5</a>. It is true that nothing is said of faith; but baptism implies faith on the part of its recipient. It has no regenerating effect apart from faith; and the renewing of the Holy Spirit is not bestowed if faith be wanting.
 </p><p>Renewing (ἀνακαινώσεως)
 </p><p>Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#002">Rom 12:2</a>. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#007">Co2 5:7</a>. Paul has ἀνακαινοῦν to renew, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2004.htm#016">Co2 4:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#010">Col 3:10</a> : ἀνακαίνωσις renewing, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#002">Rom 12:2</a>. Ἁνακαινίζειν to renew, only <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb006.htm#006">Heb 6:6</a>. The connection of the genitive is disputed. Some make it dependent on λουτροῦ bath, so that the bath of baptism is conceived as implying regeneration and renewing of the Holy Spirit. Others construe with renewing only, ἀνακαινώσεως being dependent on διὰ; through the laver of regeneration and (through) the renewing, etc. The former seems the more probable. The phrase renewing of the Holy Spirit only here. In N.T. the Spirit or the Holy Spirit is joined in the genitive with the following words: comfort, joy, power, love, demonstration, manifestation, earnest, ministration, fellowship, promise, fruit, unity, sword, sanctification. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#006">Titus 3:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">tit 3:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Shed (ἐξέχεεν)
 </p><p>Or poured forth. Only here in Pastorals. Most frequent in Revelation. The pouring out of the Spirit is an O.T. metaphor. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#001">Joe 3:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#002">Joe 3:2</a>, cit. in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#017">Act 2:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#018">Act 2:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#010">Zac 12:10</a>. In Paul the verb occurs but once, of shedding blood, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#015">Rom 3:15</a>, cit. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#007">Titus 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">tit 3:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Being justified (δικαιωθέντες)
 </p><p>In Pastorals only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#016">Ti1 3:16</a> (note). See Introd. VI. Justification is conceived as taking place before the outpouring of the Spirit.
 </p><p>By his grace (τῇ ἐκείνου χάριτι)
 </p><p>By the grace of Jesus Christ. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#011">Act 15:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#009">Co2 8:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2013.htm#014">Co2 13:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#006">Rom 5:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#006">Gal 1:6</a>.
 </p><p>We should be made heirs (κληρονόμοι γενηθῶμεν)
 </p><p>Κληρονόμος heir only here in Pastorals. A favorite idea of Paul. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#013">Rom 4:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#017">Rom 8:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#029">Gal 3:29</a>. Heirship of eternal life is the result of justification. So, clearly, Romans 5. It is attested and confirmed by the Holy Spirit. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#005">Co2 5:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#014">Eph 1:14</a>.
 </p><p>According to the hope of eternal life (κατ' ἐλπίδα ζωῆς αἰωνίου)
 </p><p>Const. of eternal life with heirs, and rend. heirs of eternal life according to hope. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#018">Rom 4:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#002">Rom 5:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#024">Rom 8:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#005">Gal 5:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#005">Col 1:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#027">Col 1:27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#002">Tit 1:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#003">Pe1 1:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#002">Jo1 3:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#003">Jo1 3:3</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#008">Titus 3:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">tit 3:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Affirm constantly (διαβεβαιοῦσθαι)
 </p><p>Pasto. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#007">Ti1 1:7</a>. Constantly, not continually, but uniformly and consistently. So Book of Common Prayer, "Collect for Saint John Baptist's Day," "and after his example constantly speak the truth." Rend. affirm steadfastly.
 </p><p>Might be careful (φροντίζωσιν)
 </p><p>N.T.o. Quite often in lxx. Frequent in Class. To think or consider; hence to take careful thought, ponder, be anxious about.
 </p><p>To maintain (προΐ̀στασθαι)
 </p><p>Mostly in Pastorals, and usually in the sense of ruling, as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#008">Rom 12:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#012">Th1 5:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#004">Ti1 3:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#005">Ti1 3:5</a>. The sense here is to be forward in.
 </p><p>Profitable (ὠφέλιμα)
 </p><p>Pasto. olxx. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1004.htm#008">Ti1 4:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#016">Ti2 3:16</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#009">Titus 3:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">tit 3:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Foolish questions, etc.
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#023">Ti2 2:23</a>. For genealogies see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#004">Ti1 1:4</a>.
 </p><p>Strivings about the law (μάχας νομικὰς)
 </p><p>The phrase N.T.o. Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#007">Ti1 1:7</a>. Νομικός mostly in Luke. Everywhere except here a lawyer, with the article or τὶς.
 </p><p>Unprofitable (ἀνωφελεῖς)
 </p><p>Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#018">Heb 7:18</a>.
 </p><p>Vain (μάταιοι)
 </p><p>Only here in Pastorals. Twice in Paul, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#020">Co1 3:20</a>, cit.; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#017">Co1 15:17</a> (note). Very frequent in lxx. The sense is aimless or resultless, as μάταιος εὐχή a prayer which cannot obtain fulfilment. The questions, genealogies, etc., lead to no attainment or advancement in godliness. Comp. ματαιολογία jangling, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#006">Ti1 1:6</a>; ματαιολόγοι vain talkers, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#010">Ti1 1:10</a>; ματαιότης vanity, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#020">Rom 8:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#017">Eph 4:17</a>; ἐματαιώθησαν were made vain, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#021">Rom 1:21</a>; μάτην in vain, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#009">Mat 15:9</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#010">Titus 3:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">tit 3:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A man that is an heretic (αἱρετικὸν ἄνθρωπον)
 </p><p>Ἁιρετικός heretical, N.T.o. For αἵρεσις heresy see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#001">Pe1 2:1</a>.
 </p><p>Admonition (νουθεσίαν)
 </p><p>Only here, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#011">Co1 10:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#004">Eph 6:4</a> (note). See on νουθετεῖν to admonish, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#031">Act 20:31</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#011">Titus 3:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">tit 3:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Is subverted (ἐξέστραπται)
 </p><p>N.T.o. More than turned away from the right path: rather, turned inside out. Comp. lxx, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#020">Deu 32:20</a>.
 </p><p>Sinneth (ἁμαρτάνει)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#009">Jo1 1:9</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#021">Mat 1:21</a>, and see on trespasses, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#014">Mat 6:14</a>.
 </p><p>Condemned of himself (αὐτοκατάκριτος)
 </p><p>Better as Rev., self-condemned. N.T.o. olxx, oClass. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#012">Titus 3:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">tit 3:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Nicopolis
 </p><p>There were several cities of this name, one in Cilicia, one in Thrace, and one in Epirus. It is uncertain which one is meant here.
 </p><p>To winter (παραχειμάσαι)
 </p><p>Comp. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act027.htm#012">Act 27:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#011">Act 28:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#006">Co1 16:6</a>. The noun παραχειμασία wintering, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act027.htm#012">Act 27:12</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#014">Titus 3:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">tit 3:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Ours (ἡμέτεροι)
 </p><p>Our brethren in Crete.
 </p><p>For necessary uses (εἰς τὰς ἀναγκαίας χρείας)
 </p><p>The phrase N.T.o. With reference to whatever occasion may demand them.
 </p><p>Unfruitful (ἄκαρποι)
 </p><p>Only here in Pastorals. In Paul, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#014">Co1 14:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#011">Eph 5:11</a>. Not only in supplying the needs, but in cultivating Christian graces in themselves by acts of Christian service. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#015">Titus 3:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">tit 3:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Them that love us in the faith (τοὺς φιλοῦντας ἡμᾶς ἐν πίστει)
 </p><p>Better, in faith. The phrase N.T.o. Φιλεῖν to love, only here in Pastorals, and in Paul, only <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#022">Co1 16:22</a>. See on ἀγάπη love, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#022">Gal 5:22</a>. Const. in faith with that love us. 
 </p><p></p>
