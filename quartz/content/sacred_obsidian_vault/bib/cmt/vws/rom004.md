 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#001">Romans Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#001">Romans 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rom 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>What shall we say? (τι ἐροῦμεν)
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#001">Rom 4:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#001">Rom 6:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#007">Rom 7:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#031">Rom 8:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#014">Rom 9:14</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#030">Rom 9:30</a>. The phrase anticipates an objection or proposes an inference. It is used by Paul only, and by him only in this Epistle and in its argumentative portions. It is not found in the last five chapters, which are hortatory.
 </p><p>Our Father
 </p><p>The best texts read προπάτορα forefather.
 </p><p>Hath found
 </p><p>Westcott and Hort omit. Then the reading would be "what shall we say of Abraham," etc. Found signifies, attained by his own efforts apart from grace.
 </p><p>As pertaining to the flesh (κατὰ σάρκα)
 </p><p>Construe with found. The question is, Was Abraham justified by anything which pertained to the flesh? Some construe with Abraham: our father humanly speaking. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#002">Romans 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rom 4:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For
 </p><p>Supply, Abraham found nothing according to the flesh; for, if he did, he has something to boast of.
 </p><p>By works (ἐξ ἔργων)
 </p><p>Lit., out of works. In speaking of the relation of works to justification, Paul never uses διά by or through, but ἐκ out of; works being regarded by the Jew as the meritorious source of salvation. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#003">Romans 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rom 4:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The Scripture (ἡ γραφὴ)
 </p><p>The scripture passage. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#022">Joh 2:22</a>; and footnote on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#047">Joh 5:47</a>.
 </p><p>It was counted for righteousness (ἐλογίσθη εἰς δικαιοσύνην)
 </p><p>For the phrase λογίζεσθαι εἰς to reckon unto, compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#026">Rom 2:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#008">Rom 9:8</a>, where εἰς is rendered for. The verb is also used with ὡς as. So <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#036">Rom 8:36</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#001">Co1 4:1</a>. So in Sept., εἰς, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#001">Psa 56:1-13</a> :31; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#017">Isa 29:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa032.htm#015">Isa 32:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#017">Isa 40:17</a> : ὡς. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen031.htm#015">Gen 31:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#020">Job 41:20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#022">Psa 44:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#028">Isa 5:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#016">Isa 29:16</a>. The phrases ἐλογίσθη εἰς and ἐλ. ὡς are thus shown to be substantially equivalent. See further on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#005">Rom 4:5</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#004">Romans 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">rom 4:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The reward (ὁ μισθὸς)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#013">Pe2 2:13</a>.
 </p><p>Not of grace but of debt (οὐ κατὰ χάριν ἀλλὰ κατὰ ὀφείλημα)
 </p><p>Lit., according to grace, etc. Not grace but debt is the regulative standard according to which his compensation is awarded. The workman for hire represents the legal method of salvation; he who does not work for hire, the gospel method; wages cannot be tendered as a gift. Grace is out of the question when wages is in question. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#005">Romans 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">rom 4:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Believeth on Him (πιστεύοντι ἐπὶ τὸν)
 </p><p>The verb πιστεύω to believe is used in the New Testament as follows:
 </p><p>1. Transitively, with the accusative and dative: to entrust something to one, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#011">Luk 16:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#024">Joh 2:24</a>. In the passive, to be entrusted with something, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#002">Rom 3:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#017">Co1 9:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#007">Gal 2:7</a>. With the simple accusative, to believe a thing, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#026">Joh 11:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#016">Jo1 4:16</a>.
 </p><p>2. With the infinitive, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#011">Act 15:11</a>.
 </p><p>3. With ὅτι that, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#028">Mat 9:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#024">Mar 11:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#019">Jam 2:19</a>. Especially frequent in John: <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#021">Joh 4:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#027">Joh 11:27</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#042">Joh 11:42</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#019">Joh 13:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#010">Joh 14:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#011">Joh 14:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#027">Joh 16:27</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#030">Joh 16:30</a>, etc.
 </p><p>4. With the simple dative, meaning to believe a person or thing, that they are true or speak the truth, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#022">Joh 2:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#021">Joh 4:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#046">Joh 5:46</a>. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#022">Joh 2:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#023">Joh 2:23</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#031">Joh 8:31</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#037">Joh 10:37</a>.
 </p><p>5. With the preposition ἐν in. Not frequent, and questioned in some of the passages cited for illustration. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#015">Joh 3:15</a>, ἐν αὐτῷ in Him, is probably to be construed with have eternal life. The formula occurs nowhere else in John. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#015">Mar 1:15</a> we find πιστεύετε ἐν τῷ εὐαγγελίῳ believe in the gospel. The kindred noun πίστις faith, occurs in this combination. Thus <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#026">Gal 3:26</a>, though some join in Christ Jesus with sons. See also <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#015">Eph 1:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#004">Col 1:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1003.htm#013">Ti1 3:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#015">Ti2 3:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#025">Rom 3:25</a>. This preposition indicates the sphere in which faith moves, rather than the object to which it is directed, though instances occur in the Septuagint where it plainly indicates the direction of faith, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#022">Psa 78:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer012.htm#006">Jer 12:6</a>.
 </p><p>6. With the preposition ἐπί upon, on to, unto. a. With the accusative, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#005">Rom 4:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#042">Act 9:42</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act011.htm#017">Act 11:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#031">Act 16:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act022.htm#019">Act 22:19</a>. The preposition carries the idea of mental direction with a view to resting upon, which latter idea is conveyed by the same preposition. b. With the dative, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#016">Ti1 1:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#025">Luk 24:25</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#033">Rom 9:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#011">Rom 10:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#006">Pe1 2:6</a>. The dative expresses absolute superposition. Christ as the object of faith, is the basis on which faith rests.
 </p><p>7. With the preposition εἰς into, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#006">Mat 18:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#011">Joh 2:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#004">Act 19:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#014">Rom 10:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#016">Gal 2:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#029">Phi 1:29</a>, etc. The preposition conveys the idea of the absolute transference of trust from one's self to another. Literally the phrase means to believe into. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#023">Joh 2:23</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh009.htm#035">Joh 9:35</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#044">Joh 12:44</a>.
 </p><p>Is counted for righteousness (λογίζεται εἰς δικαιοσύνην)
 </p><p>Rev., is reckoned. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#003">Rom 4:3</a>. The preposition εἰς has the force of as, not the telic meaning with a view to, or in order that he may be (righteous); nor strictly, in the place of righteousness. Faith is not a substitute for righteousness, since righteousness is involved in faith. When a man is reckoned righteous through faith, it is not a legal fiction. He is not indeed a perfect man, but God does not reckon something which has no real existence. Faith is the germ of righteousness, of life in God. God recognizes no true life apart from holiness, and "he that believeth on the Son hath life." He is not merely regarded in the law's eye as living. God accepts the germ, not in place of the fruit, but as containing the fruit. "Abraham believed God.... No soul comes into such a relation of trust without having God's investment upon it; and whatever there may be in God's righteousness - love, truth, sacrifice - will be rightfully imputed or counted to be in it, because, being united to Him, it will have them coming over derivatively from Him" (Bushnell). The idea of logical sequence is inherent in λογίζεται is reckoned - the sequence of character upon faith. Where there is faith there is, logically, righteousness, and the righteousness is from faith unto faith (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#017">Rom 1:17</a>). Nevertheless, in the highest development of the righteousness of faith, it will remain true that the man is justified, not by the works of righteousness, which are the fruit of faith, but by the faith which, in making him a partaker of the life and righteousness of God, generates and inspires the works.
 </p><p>Observe that the believer's own faith is reckoned as righteousness. "In no passage in Paul's writings or in other parts of the New Testament, where the phrase to reckon for or the verb to reckon alone is used, is there a declaration that anything belonging to one person is imputed, accounted, or reckoned to another, or a formal statement that Christ's righteousness is imputed to believers" (President Dwight, "Notes on Meyer"). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#006">Romans 4:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">rom 4:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Describeth the blessedness (λέγει τὸν μακαρισμὸν)
 </p><p>Μακαρισμός does not mean blessedness, but the declaration of blessedness, the congratulation. So Plato: "The man of understanding will not suffer himself to be dazzled by the congratulation (μακαρισμοῦ) of the multitude ("Republic," ix., 591). Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#015">Gal 4:15</a> (Rev.), and see note there. Rev., correctly, pronounceth blessing. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#007">Romans 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">rom 4:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Iniquities (ἀνομίαι)
 </p><p>Lit., lawlessnesses.
 </p><p>Are forgiven (ἀφέθησαν)
 </p><p>Lit., were forgiven. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#012">Mat 6:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam005.htm#015">Jam 5:15</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#009">Jo1 1:9</a>. Also see on remission, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#003">Luk 3:3</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#011">Romans 4:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">rom 4:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The sign - a seal (σημεῖον  - σφραγῖδα)
 </p><p>Sign refers to the material token; seal to its religious import. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#002">Co1 9:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#011">Gen 17:11</a>. See on to seal, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#010">Rev 22:10</a>.
 </p><p>That he might be (εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν)
 </p><p>Not so that he became, but expressing the divinely appointed aim of his receiving the sign. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#012">Romans 4:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">rom 4:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Father of circumcision
 </p><p>Of circumcised persons. The abstract term is used for the concrete. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#007">Rom 11:7</a>.
 </p><p>Who not only are - but who also walk
 </p><p>Apparently Paul speaks of two classes, but really of but one, designated by two different attributes. The awkwardness arises from the article τοῖς, erroneously repeated with στοιχοῦσιν walk, which latter word expresses an added characteristic, not another class. Paul means that Abraham received a seal, etc., that he might be the father of circumcision to those who not only are circumcised, but who add to this outward sign the faith which Abraham exhibited.
 </p><p>Walk (στοιχοῦσιν)
 </p><p>See on elements, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2003.htm#010">Pe2 3:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#013">Romans 4:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">rom 4:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Heir of the world (κληρονόμον κόσμου)
 </p><p>See on divided by lot, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#019">Act 13:19</a>; and see on inheritance, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#004">Pe1 1:4</a>. "Paul here takes the Jewish conception of the universal dominion of the Messianic theocracy prefigured by the inheritance of Canaan, divests it of its Judaistic element, and raises it to a christological truth." Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#028">Mat 19:28</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#029">Mat 19:29</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#030">Luk 22:30</a>. The idea underlies the phrases kingdom of God, kingdom of Heaven. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#016">Romans 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">rom 4:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Sure (βεβαίαν)
 </p><p>Stable, valid, something realized, the opposite of made of none effect, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#014">Rom 4:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#017">Romans 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">rom 4:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A father of many nations
 </p><p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#005">Gen 17:5</a>. Originally his name was Abram, exalted father; afterward Abraham, father of a multitude.
 </p><p>Have I made (τέθεικα)
 </p><p>Appointed or constituted. For a similar sense see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#051">Mat 24:51</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#016">Joh 15:16</a>, and note; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#047">Act 13:47</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#007">Ti1 2:7</a>. The verb shows that the paternity was the result of a special arrangement. It would not be used to denote the mere physical connection between father and son.
 </p><p>Who quickeneth the dead
 </p><p>This attribute of God is selected with special reference to the circumstances of Abraham as described in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#018">Rom 4:18</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#021">Rom 4:21</a>. As a formal attribute of God it occurs <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1002.htm#006">Sa1 2:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#021">Joh 5:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#009">Co2 1:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#013">Ti1 6:13</a>.
 </p><p>Calleth (καλοῦντος)
 </p><p>The verb is used in the following senses:
 </p><p>1. To give a name, with ὄνομα name, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#021">Mat 1:21</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#022">Mat 1:22</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#025">Mat 1:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#013">Luk 1:13</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31</a>; without ὄνομα <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#059">Luk 1:59</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#060">Luk 1:60</a>. To salute by a name, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#009">Mat 23:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#043">Mat 22:43</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#045">Mat 22:45</a>.
 </p><p>2. Passive. To bear a name or title among men, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#035">Luk 1:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#025">Luk 22:25</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#009">Co1 15:9</a>. To be acknowledged or to pass as, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#009">Mat 5:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#019">Mat 5:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#023">Jam 2:23</a>.
 </p><p>3. To invite, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#003">Mat 22:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#009">Mat 22:9</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#002">Joh 2:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#027">Co1 10:27</a>. To summon, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#021">Mat 4:21</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#018">Act 4:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act024.htm#002">Act 24:2</a>. To call out from, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#015">Mat 2:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#008">Heb 11:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#009">Pe1 2:9</a>.
 </p><p>4. To appoint. Select for an office, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#015">Gal 1:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#004">Heb 5:4</a>; to salvation, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#011">Rom 9:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#030">Rom 8:30</a>.
 </p><p>5. Of God's creative decree. To call forth from nothing, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#004">Isa 41:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#001">Kg2 8:1</a>.
 </p><p>In this last sense some explain the word here; but it can scarcely be said that God creates things that are not as actually existing. Others explain, God's disposing decree. He disposes of things that are not as though existing. The simplest explanation appears to be to give καλεῖν the sense of nameth, speaketh of. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#007">Rom 9:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#005">Act 7:5</a>. The seed of Abraham "which were at present in the category of things which were not, and the nations which should spring physically or spiritually from him, God spoke of as having an existence, which word Abraham believed" (Alford). In this case there may properly be added the idea of the summons to the high destiny ordained for Abraham's seed. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#019">Romans 4:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">rom 4:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Being not weak in faith he considered not (μὴ ἀσθενήσας τῇ πίστει οὐ κατενόησεν)
 </p><p>The best texts omit οὐ not before considered. According to this the rendering is as Rev., he considered, etc. Being not weak or weakened: (Rev.) is an accompanying circumstance to he considered. He considered all these unfavorable circumstances without a weakening of faith. The preposition κατά in κατενόησεν considered, is intensive - attentively. He fixed his eye upon the obstacles.
 </p><p>Dead (νενεκρωμένον)
 </p><p>The participle is passive, slain. Used here hyperbolically. Hence, Rev., as good as dead. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#020">Romans 4:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">rom 4:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Staggered (διεκρίθη)
 </p><p>Rev., better, wavered. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act011.htm#012">Act 11:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#006">Jam 1:6</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#004">Jam 2:4</a>. The word implies a mental struggle.
 </p><p>Promise (ἐπαγγελίαν)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#004">Act 1:4</a>.
 </p><p>Was strong (ἐνεδυναμώθη)
 </p><p>Passive voice. Lit., was strengthened, or endued with strength. Rev., waxed strong. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#021">Romans 4:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">rom 4:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Being fully persuaded (πληροφορηθεὶς)
 </p><p>Rev., more accurately, fully assured. See on most surely believed, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#001">Luk 1:1</a>. The primary idea is, being filled with a thought or conviction.
 </p><p>Able (δυνατός)
 </p><p>The sense is stronger: mighty; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#049">Luk 1:49</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#019">Luk 24:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act018.htm#024">Act 18:24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2010.htm#004">Co2 10:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#015">Rev 6:15</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#024">Romans 4:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">rom 4:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>It shall be reckoned (μέλλει λογίζεσθαι)
 </p><p>Not the future of the verb to reckon, but μέλλω to intend points to God's definite purpose. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act027.htm#002">Act 27:2</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#012">Pe2 1:12</a>.
 </p><p>Who believe
 </p><p>Since we are those who believe. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#025">Romans 4:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">rom 4:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Was delivered (παρεδόθη)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#012">Mat 4:12</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#023">Pe1 2:23</a>. Used of casting into prison or delivering to justice, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#012">Mat 4:12</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#017">Mat 10:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#021">Mat 19:21</a>. Frequently of the betrayal of Christ, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#004">Mat 10:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#022">Mat 17:22</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#064">Joh 6:64</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#071">Joh 6:71</a>. Of committing a trust, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#014">Mat 25:14</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#020">Mat 25:20</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#022">Mat 25:22</a>. Of committing tradition, doctrine, or precept, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar007.htm#013">Mar 7:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#002">Co1 11:2</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#003">Co1 15:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#017">Rom 6:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#021">Pe2 2:21</a>. Of Christ's yielding up His spirit, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#030">Joh 19:30</a>. Of the surrender of Christ and His followers to death, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#032">Rom 8:32</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2004.htm#011">Co2 4:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020">Gal 2:20</a>. Of giving over to evil, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#026">Rom 1:26</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#028">Rom 1:28</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#005">Co1 5:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#019">Eph 4:19</a>.
 </p><p>Raised again for our justification
 </p><p>"But if the whole matter of the justification depends on what He has suffered for our offenses, we shall as certainly be justified or have our account made even, if He does not rise, as if He does. Doubtless the rising has an immense significance, when the justification is conceived to be the renewing of our moral nature in righteousness; for it is only by the rising that His incarnate life and glory are fully discovered, and the righteousness of God declared in His person in its true moral power. But in the other view of justification there is plainly enough nothing depending, as far as that is concerned, on His resurrection" (Bushnell). Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#004">Rom 6:4-13</a>.
