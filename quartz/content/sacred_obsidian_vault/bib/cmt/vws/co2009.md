 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#001">2 Corinthians Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#004">2 Corinthians 9:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">co2 9:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Confident boasting (ὑποστάσει)
 </p><p>Primarily something put under, foundation, ground; so substance (sub, stans, standing under), substantial quality: thence steadiness, confidence. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#014">Heb 3:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#001">Heb 11:1</a>. In the Septuagint the word represents fifteen different Hebrew words. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#005">2 Corinthians 9:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">co2 9:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Go before
 </p><p>Notice the thrice repeated before, emphasizing the injunction to have everything ready before Paul's arrival.
 </p><p>Make up beforehand (προκαταρτίσωσιν)
 </p><p>Adjust. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#021">Mat 4:21</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#016">Mat 21:16</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#040">Luk 6:40</a>; see on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#010">Pe1 5:10</a>.
 </p><p>Bounty (εὐλογίαν)
 </p><p>Lit., blessing. In this sense only here in the New Testament. In the Septuagint indifferently of gift or blessing. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#011">Gen 33:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg001.htm#015">Jdg 1:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#026">Eze 34:26</a>. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#025">Pro 11:25</a>, liberal soul is rendered by Sept., εὐλογούμενη blessed.
 </p><p>Whereof ye had notice before (προεπηγγελημένην)
 </p><p>Rev., better, your afore-promised bounty. The bounty promised by you, or by me on your behalf. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#006">2 Corinthians 9:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">co2 9:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Bountifully (ἐπ' εὐλογίαις)
 </p><p>Lit., with blessings. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#010">Co1 9:10</a>, "plow in hope (ἐπ' ἐλπίδι)." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#007">2 Corinthians 9:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">co2 9:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Purposeth (προαιρείται)
 </p><p>Read προῄρηται, perfect tense, hath purposed.
 </p><p>Grudgingly (ἐκ λύπης)
 </p><p>Lit., out of sorrow.
 </p><p>Cheerful (ἱλαρὸν)
 </p><p>Only here in the New Testament. See on the kindred ἱλαρότης cheerfulness, note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#008">Rom 12:8</a>.
 </p><p>God loveth, etc.
 </p><p>From <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009">Pro 22:9</a>, where the Hebrew is, a kind man shall be blessed. Sept., God blesseth a man who is cheerful and a giver. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#008">2 Corinthians 9:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">co2 9:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Always - all - in everything
 </p><p>Nearly reproducing the play on the word all in the Greek.
 </p><p>Sufficiency (αὐτάρκειαν)
 </p><p>Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#006">Ti1 6:6</a>. The kindred adjective αὐταρκης A.V., content, occurs <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi004.htm#011">Phi 4:11</a> (see note). The word properly means self-sufficiency, and is one of those which show Paul's acquaintance with Stoicism, and the influence of its vocabulary upon his own. It expressed the Stoic conception of the wise man as being sufficient in himself, wanting nothing and possessing everything. Here, not in the sense of sufficiency of worldly goods, but of that moral quality, bound up with self-consecration and faith, which renders the new self in Christ independent of external circumstances. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#009">2 Corinthians 9:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">co2 9:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>He hath dispersed abroad (ἐσκόρπισεν)
 </p><p>As in sowing, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#006">Co2 9:6</a>. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa112.htm#009">Psa 112:9</a>. Almost literally after the Hebrew and Septuagint. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#010">2 Corinthians 9:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">co2 9:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Ministereth (ἐπιχορηγῶν)
 </p><p>Rev., supplieth. See on add, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#005">Pe2 1:5</a>.
 </p><p>Both minister bread, etc.
 </p><p>Construe bread with supplieth, as Rev., supplieth seed to the sow and bread for food.
 </p><p>Minister and multiply (χορηγήσαι καὶ πληθύναι)
 </p><p>The correct reading is the future, χορηγήσει καὶ πληθυνεῖ shall supply and multiply.
 </p><p>The fruits (τὰ γενήματα)
 </p><p>Lit., what has been begotten or born. Used of men, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#007">Mat 3:7</a>, A.V., generation, Rev., offspring. Elsewhere of fruits, as fruit of the vine, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#025">Mar 14:25</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#011">2 Corinthians 9:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">co2 9:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Liberality (ἁπλοτήτα)
 </p><p>Better singleness or simplicity of heart. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#008">Rom 12:8</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#012">2 Corinthians 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">co2 9:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Service (λειτουργίας)
 </p><p>Also rendered ministry or ministration (A.V. and Rev.), as <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#023">Luk 1:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#006">Heb 8:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#021">Heb 9:21</a>. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#023">Luk 1:23</a>. The word is used of this same contribution, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#007">Rom 15:7</a>.
 </p><p>Supplieth (ἐστὶν προσαναπληροῦσα)
 </p><p>Lit., fills up by adding to. Only here and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#009">Co2 11:9</a>. Supplementing what the saints lack. Through many thanksgivings. The need of the poor is filled, like an empty vessel, to the brim, and the supply overflows in the thanksgiving which it calls out. Thus christian beneficence does a double work, in giving relief and in generating thankfulness. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#013">2 Corinthians 9:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">co2 9:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Experiment of this ministration (δοκιμῆς τῆς διακονίας ταύτης).
 </p><p>Commentators differ as to the interpretation; the difference hinging on the question whether the trial (experiment) applies to the service itself, or to those who render it: hence either "the proving of you by this ministration," as Rev., or the tried character of this ministration. Δοκιμή may mean, either the process of proving or the state of being approved, approvedness. The difference is immaterial.
 </p><p>Your professed subjection (ὑποταγῇ τῆς ὁμολογίας ὑμῶν)
 </p><p>A vicious hendiadys. Lit., as Rev., the obedience of your confession; that is, the obedience which results from your christian confession. Ὁμολογία is once rendered in A.V. confession, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#013">Ti1 6:13</a>; and elsewhere profession. Both renderings occur in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#012">Ti1 6:12</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#013">Ti1 6:13</a>. Rev., in every case, confession. A similar variation occurs in the rendering of ὁμολογέω, though in all but five of the twenty-three instances confess is used. Rev. retains profess in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#023">Mat 7:23</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#016">Tit 1:16</a>, and changes to confess in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#012">Ti1 6:12</a>. In <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#007">Mat 14:7</a>, promised (A.V. and Rev., see note), and in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#015">Heb 13:15</a>, giving thanks; Rev., which make confession. Etymologically, confession is the literal rendering of ὁμολογία, which is from ὁμόν together, λέγω to say; con together, fateor to say. The fundamental idea is that of saying the same thing as another; while profess (pro forth, fateor to say) is to declare openly. Hence, to profess Christ is to declare Him publicly as our Lord: to confess Christ is to declare agreement with all that He says. When Christ confesses His followers before the world, He makes a declaration in agreement with what is in His heart concerning them. Similarly, when He declares to the wicked "I never knew you" ("then will I profess, ὁμολογήσω"), a similar agreement between His thought and His declaration is implied. The two ideas run into each other, and the Rev. is right in the few cases in which it retains profess, since confess would be ambiguous. See, for example, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit001.htm#016">Tit 1:16</a>.
 </p><p>Liberal distribution (ἁπλότητι τῆς κοινωνίας)
 </p><p>Rev., correctly, liberality of your contribution. Κοινωνία communion includes the idea of communication of material things, and hence sometimes means that which is communicated. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#042">Act 2:42</a>; so <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#026">Rom 15:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#016">Heb 13:16</a>. Compare the similar use of κοινωνέω, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#013">Rom 12:13</a>, distributing; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi004.htm#015">Phi 4:15</a>, communicated. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2009.htm#015">2 Corinthians 9:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">co2 9:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Thanks, etc.
 </p><p>These abrupt thanksgivings are common in Paul's writings. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#005">Rom 9:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#033">Rom 11:33</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#057">Co1 15:57</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#005">Gal 1:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#020">Eph 3:20</a>.
 </p><p>Unspeakable (ἀνεκδιηγήτῳ)
 </p><p>Lit., not to be told throughout. Only here in the New Testament.
