 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#001">Mark Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#001">Mark 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mar 14:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The feast of the passover and the unleavened bread (τὸ πάσχα καὶ τὰ ἄζυμα)
 </p><p>Lit., the passover and the unleavened. It was really one and the same festival.
 </p><p>Sought (ἐζήτουν)
 </p><p>Imperfect tense: were all this while seeking
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#003">Mark 14:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">mar 14:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Alabaster box
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#007">Mat 26:7</a>.
 </p><p>Spikenard (νάρδου πιστικῆς)
 </p><p>The meaning of πιστικῆς greatly disputed. The best authorities define it genuine or unadulterated: pure nard.
 </p><p>Brake
 </p><p>Possibly by striking the brittle neck of the flask. This detail is peculiar to Mark. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#004">Mark 14:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">mar 14:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To what purpose, etc
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#008">Mat 26:8</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#005">Mark 14:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">mar 14:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Murmured (ἐνεβριμῶντο)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#043">Mar 1:43</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#006">Mark 14:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">mar 14:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Good
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#010">Mat 26:10</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#007">Mark 14:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">mar 14:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>And whensoever ye will, etc
 </p><p>Note Mark's amplification. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#008">Mark 14:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">mar 14:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>She hath done what she could (ὅ ἔσχεν ἐποίησεν)
 </p><p>Lit., what she had she did. Peculiar to Mark.
 </p><p>She is come aforehand to anoint (προέλαβεν μυρίσαι)
 </p><p>Lit., she anticipated to anoint. Rev., hath anointed beforehand. The verb μυρίζω is found only here. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#011">Mark 14:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">mar 14:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Money
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#015">Mat 26:15</a>.
 </p><p>He sought (ευζήτει)
 </p><p>Imperfect tense. He kept seeking: busied himself continuously from that time.
 </p><p>Conveniently (εὐκαίρως)
 </p><p>Might find a good opportunity (καιρός). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#013">Mark 14:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">mar 14:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A man
 </p><p>A slave probably, whose business it was to draw water. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu019.htm#011">Deu 19:11</a>.
 </p><p>Pitcher
 </p><p>Of earthenware: κεράμοιν, from κέραμος, potter's clay. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#014">Mark 14:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">mar 14:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>My guest-chamber (κατάλυμά μου)
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#011">Luk 22:11</a>. The word is not classical, and as used by an oriental signifies a khan or caravanserai. Hence inn at <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#007">Luk 2:7</a>. My chamber. It was a common practice that more than one company partook of the paschal supper in the same apartment; but Christ will have his chamber for himself and his disciples alone. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#015">Mark 14:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">mar 14:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>And he (αὐτὸς)
 </p><p>The Greek is more emphatic. "He will himself show you." So Rev. Probably the owner of the house was a disciple.
 </p><p>Furnished (ἐστρωμένον)
 </p><p>Lit., strewed with carpets, and with couches properly spread. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#020">Mark 14:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">mar 14:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Dish (τρυβλίου)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#023">Mat 26:23</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#023">Mark 14:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">mar 14:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The cup.
 </p><p>The wine was the ordinary one of the country, only red. It was mixed with water, generally in the proportion of one part to two of water. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#024">Mark 14:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">mar 14:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Covenant
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#028">Mat 26:28</a>.
 </p><p>Is shed (τὸ ἐκχυννόμενον)
 </p><p>Lit., is being shed. This present participle is significant. To the Lord's mind the sacrifice is already being offered. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#025">Mark 14:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">mar 14:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>New
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#029">Mat 26:29</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#026">Mark 14:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">mar 14:26</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Sung an hymn
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#030">Mat 26:30</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#028">Mark 14:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">mar 14:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Go before
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#032">Mat 26:32</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#030">Mark 14:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">mar 14:30</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Cock crow
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#034">Mat 26:34</a>. Mark alone adds twice.
 </p><p>Deny (ἀπαρνήσῃ)
 </p><p>The compound verb signifies utterly deny. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#031">Mark 14:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">mar 14:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I will not deny (οὐ μή σε ἀπαρνήσαμαι)
 </p><p>The double negative with the future forms the strongest possible assertion. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#032">Mark 14:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">mar 14:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Gethsemane
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#036">Mat 26:36</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#033">Mark 14:33</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_033">mar 14:33</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To be sore amazed (ἐκθαμβεῖσθαι)
 </p><p>A word peculiar to Mark. Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#015">Mar 9:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#005">Mar 16:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#006">Mar 16:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#035">Mark 14:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">mar 14:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Prayed (προσηύχετο)
 </p><p>Imperfect tense: began to pray. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#040">Mark 14:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">mar 14:40</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Heavy (καταβαρυνόμενοι)
 </p><p>Lit., weighed down: very heavy. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#041">Mark 14:41</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_041">mar 14:41</a></font></span></p>
 <p>It is enough (ἀπέχει)
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. In this impersonal sense the word occurs nowhere else in the New Testament. Expositors are utterly at sea as to its meaning. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#043">Mark 14:43</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_043">mar 14:43</a></font></span></p>
 <p>One of the twelve
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#047">Mat 26:47</a>; as also on multitude. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#044">Mark 14:44</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_044">mar 14:44</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Token (σύσσημον)
 </p><p>A later Greek compound used only by Mark in this passage. Compare σημεῖον, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#048">Mat 26:48</a>. The σύν, with, gives the force of a mutual token: a concerted signal. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#045">Mark 14:45</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_045">mar 14:45</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Kissed
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#049">Mat 26:49</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#047">Mark 14:47</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_047">mar 14:47</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The servant
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#051">Mat 26:51</a>.
 </p><p>Ear (ὠτάριον)
 </p><p>A word found only here and at <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#010">Joh 18:10</a>. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#051">Mat 26:51</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#048">Mark 14:48</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_048">mar 14:48</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A thief
 </p><p>Rev., better, robber. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#055">Mat 26:55</a>, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#017">Mar 11:17</a>.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#051">Mark 14:51</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_051">mar 14:51</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Linen cloth (σινδόνα)
 </p><p>The probable derivation is from Ἰνδός, an Indian: India being the source from which came this fine fabric used for wrapping dead bodies, and in which Christ's body was enveloped. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#059">Mat 27:59</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar015.htm#046">Mar 15:46</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#053">Luk 23:53</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#054">Mark 14:54</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_054">mar 14:54</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Palace (αὐλὴν)
 </p><p>Rather, court, as Rev., the quadrangle round which the chambers were built. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#003">Mat 26:3</a>.
 </p><p>Sat with (ἦν συγκαθήμενος)
 </p><p>The verb with the participle denoting continuousness. What occurred after occurred while he was sitting. So Rev.
 </p><p>Servants
 </p><p>Rev., officers. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#025">Mat 5:25</a>.
 </p><p>At the fire (πρὸς τὸ φῶς)
 </p><p>Φῶς is never used of the fire itself, but of the light of the fire; and this is the point to which the evangelist directs attention: that the firelight, shining on Peter's face, called forth the challenge of the maid (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#066">Mar 14:66</a>). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#056">Mark 14:56</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_056">mar 14:56</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Their witness agreed not
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. Lit., their testimonies were not equal. Hence the difficulty of fulfilling the requirement of the law, which demanded two witnesses. See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#006">Deu 17:6</a>; and compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#016">Mat 18:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#019">Ti1 5:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#028">Heb 10:28</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#058">Mark 14:58</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_058">mar 14:58</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Made with hands
 </p><p>Mark adds this detail; also made without hands, and the following sentence. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#062">Mark 14:62</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_062">mar 14:62</a></font></span></p>
 <p>I am
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#064">Mat 26:64</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#064">Mark 14:64</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_064">mar 14:64</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Guilty of death
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#066">Mat 26:66</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#065">Mark 14:65</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_065">mar 14:65</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Buffet
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#067">Mat 26:67</a>.
 </p><p>Palms of their hands (ῥαπίσμασιν)
 </p><p>An unclassical word, but used also by John (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#003">Joh 19:3</a>). The word means blows.
 </p><p>Did strike
 </p><p>Following the old reading, ἔβαλλον. The correct reading is ἔλαβον, received. So Rev. Received him into custody. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#066">Mark 14:66</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_066">mar 14:66</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Beneath
 </p><p>In relation to the chambers round the court above. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#068">Mark 14:68</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_068">mar 14:68</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Porch (προαύλιον)
 </p><p>Only here in New Testament. The vestibule, extending from the outside gate to the court. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#071">Mark 14:71</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_071">mar 14:71</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Curse (ἀναθεματίζειν)
 </p><p>Compare on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#074">Mat 26:74</a>; where the word is καταθεματίζειν, to call down (κατὰ) curses on himself if he were not telling the truth. The words are synonymous. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#072">Mark 14:72</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_072">mar 14:72</a></font></span></p>
 <p>When he thought thereon (ἐπιβαλὼν)
 </p><p>From ἐπί, upon, and βάλλω, to throw. When he threw his thought upon it.
