 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Vincent's Word Studies</i>, by Marvin R. Vincent, [1886], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#001">Mark Chapter 11</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#002">Mark 11:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">mar 11:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Colt
 </p><p>Only Matthew adds the ass. Mark and Luke have colt only. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#004">Mark 11:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">mar 11:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In a place where two ways met (ἐπὶ τοῦ ἀμφόδου)
 </p><p>Ἄμφοδον is literally any road which leads round (ἀμφί) a place or a block of buildings. Hence the winding way. The word occurs only here in the New Testament. Rev., in the open street, which in an Eastern town is usually crooked. Perhaps, by contrast with the usual crookedness, the street in Damascus where Paul lodged was called Straight (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#011">Act 9:11</a>). "It is a topographical note," says Dr. Morison, "that could only be given by an eye-witness." The detail of <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#004">Mar 11:4</a> is peculiar to Mark. According to Luke (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#008">Luk 22:8</a>), Peter was one of those sent, and his stamp is probably on the narrative. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#008">Mark 11:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">mar 11:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In the way
 </p><p>Both Matthew and Luke have ἐν, in; but Mark, εἰς, into. They threw their garments into the way and spread them there.
 </p><p>Branches
 </p><p>Matthew, Hark, and John use each a different word for branches. Matthew, κλάδους, from κλάω, to break; hence a young slip or shoot, such as is broken off for grafting - a twig, as related to a branch. Mark, στιβάδας, from στείβω, to tread or beat down; hence a mass of straw, rushes, or leaves beaten together or strewed loose, so as to form a bed or a carpeted way. A litter of branches and leaves cut from the fields (only Mark) near by. John, βαΐ́α, strictly palm-branches, the feathery fronds forming the tufted crown of the tree.
 </p><p>Hosanna
 </p><p>Meaning, O save! 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#011">Mark 11:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">mar 11:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>When he had looked round
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. As the master of the house, inspecting. "A look serious, sorrowful, judicial" (Meyer). Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#005">Mar 3:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#034">Mar 3:34</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#013">Mark 11:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">mar 11:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Afar off
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark.
 </p><p>Having leaves
 </p><p>An unusual thing at that early season.
 </p><p>If haply (εἰ ἄρα)
 </p><p>If, such being the case, i.e., the tree having leaves - he might find fruit, which, in the fig, precedes the leaf. Mark alone adds, "for the time of figs was not yet." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#014">Mark 11:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">mar 11:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>His disciples heard it
 </p><p>Peculiar to Mark. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#015">Mark 11:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">mar 11:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Money-changers (κολλυβιστῶν)
 </p><p>Another unclassical word, but used also by Matthew. "Such words as these might naturally find their place in the mongrel Greek of the slaves and freedmen who formed the first congregations of the church in Rome" (Ezra Abbott, Art. "Gospels," in Encyc. Britannica). See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#012">Mat 21:12</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#016">Mark 11:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">mar 11:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Vessel (σκεῦος)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#029">Mat 12:29</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#027">Mar 3:27</a>.
 </p><p>Temple (ἱροῦ)
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#005">Mat 4:5</a>. The temple enclosure, not the ναός, or sanctuary. People would be tempted to carry vessels, etc., through this, in order to save a long circuit. The court of the Gentiles, moreover, was not regarded by the Jews as entitled to the respect due to the other part of the enclosure. This our Lord rebukes. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#017">Mark 11:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">mar 11:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Of all nations
 </p><p>Which rendering implies, shall be called by all nations. But render with Rev., a house of prayer for all the nations (πᾶσιν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν).
 </p><p>Thieves (λῃστῶν)
 </p><p>Rev., correctly, robbers. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#013">Mat 21:13</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#055">Mat 26:55</a>; and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#001">Joh 10:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#008">Joh 10:8</a>. From ληίς or λεία, booty. In classical usage mostly of cattle. The robber, conducting his operations on a large and systematic scale, and with the aid of bands, is thus to be distinguished from the κλέπτης, or thief who purloins or pilfers whatever comes to hand. A den would be appropriate to a band of robbers, not to thieves. Thus the traveller to Jericho, in Christ's parable (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#030">Luk 10:30</a>), fell among robbers, not thieves. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#019">Mark 11:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">mar 11:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>When evening was come (ὅταν)
 </p><p>Lit., whenever evening came on; not on the evening of the purging of the temple merely, but each day at evening.
 </p><p> 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#023">Mark 11:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">mar 11:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Shall come to pass (γίνεται)
 </p><p>Rather cometh to pass, as Rev. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#024">Mark 11:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">mar 11:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Receive (ἐλάβετε)
 </p><p>More lit., received. Rev., have received. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#025">Mark 11:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">mar 11:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Trespasses
 </p><p>See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#014">Mat 6:14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#027">Mark 11:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">mar 11:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Walking
 </p><p>An addition of Mark.
