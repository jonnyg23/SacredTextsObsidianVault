 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa118.htm#001">Psalms 118:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 118:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>am 2962, bc 1042 (Title), This Psalm was probably composed by David after Nathan's prophetic address; and sung by alternate choirs at some public festival.  It largely partakes of David's spirit, and everywhere shews the hand of a master; the style is grand and sublime; the subject noble and majestic. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa118.htm#029">Psa 118:29</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa103.htm#017">Psa 103:17</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa106.htm#001">Psa 106:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa107.htm#001">Psa 107:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa136.htm#001">Psa 136:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1016.htm#008">Ch1 16:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1016.htm#034">Ch1 16:34</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer033.htm#011">Jer 33:11</a>
