 </p><p>balm:  For an explanation of the words tzori, nechoth, and lot, here rendered respectively balm, spices, and myrrh, see note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#025">Gen 37:25</a>.  Devash, honey, is supposed by some not to have been that produced by bees, but a syrup produced from ripe dates.  The Jewish doctors observe, that the word in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2031.htm#005">Ch2 31:5</a> signifies dates; and the Arabians, at this day, call the choicest dates preserved with butter, dabous, and the honey obtained from them, dibs, or dabs.  </p><p>for that is an abomination:  The Chaldee Paraphrast renders this clause, "Because the Hebrews eat the cattle which the Egyptians worship."  But, as we learn from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen043.htm#016">Gen 43:16</a>, compared with this verse, that the provision for the entertainment of the Egyptians themselves was animal food, this reason cannot be just.  The true reason seems to be that assigned by the LXX, Βδελυγμα γαρ εστιν τοις Αιγυπτιοις πας ποιμην προβατων· "For every shepherd is an abomination to the Egyptians." <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen046.htm#034">Gen 46:34</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#026">Exo 8:26</a>
