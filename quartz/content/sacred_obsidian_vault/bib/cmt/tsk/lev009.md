 <p>the eighth day:  Not on the eighth day of the month, but on the first day after their consecration, which occupied seven days, and before which they were deemed unfit to minister in holy things, being considered in a state of imperfection.  <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">lev 9:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>his hand:  or, as the Greek has it, "his hands", Menachem gives reason why it is written hand, to signify the right hand, because that was lifted up higher than the left. The lifting up of the hand was a gesture used in speaking, and signifying any weighty thing, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#022">Isa 49:22</a>; and particularly in swearing, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#022">Gen 14:22</a>; praying, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#002">Psa 28:2</a>; and blessing, either of God, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa134.htm#002">Psa 134:2</a>, or of men, as in this place.  Paul, speaking of prayer, uses the phrase, "lifting up holy hands;" as also David:  "Let the lifting up of my hands be as the evening sacrifice." <a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018">Gen 14:18-20</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#023">Num 6:23-27</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu010.htm#008">Deu 10:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu021.htm#005">Deu 21:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#055">Kg1 8:55</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1023.htm#013">Ch1 23:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#003">Ch2 6:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa072.htm#017">Psa 72:17</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#016">Mar 10:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#050">Luk 24:50</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#026">Act 3:26</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2013.htm#014">Co2 13:14</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#006">Heb 7:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#007">Heb 7:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1003.htm#009">Pe1 3:9</a>
