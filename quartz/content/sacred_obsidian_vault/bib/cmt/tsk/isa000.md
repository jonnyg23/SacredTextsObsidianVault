 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Treasury of Scriptural Knowledge</i>, by R.A. Torrey, [ca. 1880], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#001">Isaiah Introduction</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#001">Isaiah</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">isa 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Isaiah has, with singular propriety, been denominated the Evangelical Prophet, on account of the number and variety of his prophecies concerning the advent and character, the ministry and preaching, the sufferings and death, and the extensive and permanent kingdom of the Messiah.  So explicit and determinate are his predictions, as well as so numerous, that he seems to speak rather of things past than of events yet future; and he may be rather called an evangelist than a prophet.  Though later critics, especially those on the continent, have expended much labour and learning in order to rob the prophet of his title; yet no one, whose mind is unprejudiced, can be at a loss in applying select portions of these prophecies to the mission and character of Jesus Christ, and to the events in his history which they are cited to illustrate by the sacred writers of the New Testament.  In fact, his prophecies concerning the Messiah seem almost to anticipate the Gospel history; so clearly do they predict his Divine character.  (Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#014">Isa 7:14</a> with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#018">Mat 1:18-23</a>, and <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#027">Luk 1:27-35</a>; see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#001">Isa 6:1-13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#006">Isa 9:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#004">Isa 35:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#005">Isa 40:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#009">Isa 40:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#019">Isa 40:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#006">Isa 42:6-8</a>; compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#001">Isa 61:1</a>, with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#018">Luk 4:18</a>; see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa062.htm#011">Isa 62:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa063.htm#001">Isa 63:1-4</a>); his miracles (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#005">Isa 35:5</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#006">Isa 35:6</a>); his peculiar character and virtues (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#002">Isa 11:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#003">Isa 11:3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#011">Isa 40:11</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#001">Isa 43:1-3</a>); his rejection (Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#009">Isa 6:9-12</a> with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar013.htm#014">Mar 13:14</a>; see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#014">Isa 7:14</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#015">Isa 7:15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#003">Isa 53:3</a>); his sufferings for our sins (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#006">Isa 50:6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#004">Isa 53:4-11</a>); his death and burial (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#008">Isa 53:8</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#009">Isa 53:9</a>); his victory over death (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa025.htm#008">Isa 25:8</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#010">Isa 53:10</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#012">Isa 53:12</a>); his final glory (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#007">Isa 49:7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#022">Isa 49:22</a>, 33; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#013">Isa 52:13-15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#004">Isa 53:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#005">Isa 53:5</a>); and the establishment, increase, and perfection of his kingdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#002">Isa 2:2-4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#002">Isa 9:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#007">Isa 9:7</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#004">Isa 11:4-10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa016.htm#005">Isa 16:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#018">Isa 29:18-24</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa032.htm#001">Isa 32:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#004">Isa 40:4</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#005">Isa 40:5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#004">Isa 42:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#013">Isa 46:13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#009">Isa 49:9-13</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa051.htm#003">Isa 51:3-6</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#006">Isa 53:6-10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#001">Isa 55:1-3</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa059.htm#016">Isa 59:16-21</a>; 60; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#001">Isa 61:1-5</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#025">Isa 65:25</a>); each specifically pointed out, and pourtrayed with the most striking and discriminating characters.  It is impossible, indeed, to reflect on these, and on the whole chain of his illustrious prophecies, and not be sensible that they furnish the most incontestable evidence in support of Christianity.  The style of Isaiah has been universally admired as the most perfect model of elegance and sublimity; and as distinguished for all the magnificence, and for all the sweetness of the Hebrew language.
