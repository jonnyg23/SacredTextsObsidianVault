 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#001">Co2 1:1</a>, Paul salutes the Corinthians; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#003">Co2 1:3</a>, he encourages them against troubles, by the comforts and deliverances which God had given him, as in all his afflictions, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#008">Co2 1:8</a>, so particularly in his late danger in Asia; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#012">Co2 1:12</a>, And calling both his own conscience and theirs to witness of his sincere manner of preaching the immutable truth of the gospel, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#015">Co2 1:15</a>. he excuses his not coming to them, as proceeding not of lightness, but of his lenity towards them.
