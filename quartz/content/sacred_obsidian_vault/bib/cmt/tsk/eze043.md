 </p><p>
 </p><p>Temple Dimensions  Height (in Cubits)  Base <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#013">Eze 43:13</a> 1  To first ledge <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014">Eze 43:14</a> 1  To upper ledge <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014">Eze 43:14</a> 4  To hearth <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#015">Eze 43:15</a> 4  Total Height  10    Breadth (in Cubits)  Upper ledge <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#017">Eze 43:17</a> 14  For higher ledge <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014">Eze 43:14</a> 2  For lower ledge <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014">Eze 43:14</a> 2  For base <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#013">Eze 43:13</a> 2  Total Breadth  20  Hence the upper part of the altar was only twelve cubits square (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#016">Eze 43:16</a>), the upper settle, or ledge, being in all fourteen cubits (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#017">Eze 43:17</a>), deducting two cubits from its dimensions.  Though this altar was the same in height, and breadth with that of Solomon, yet it differed materially from it in having settles or ledges; on which the priests walked round the altar, to officiate in offering sacrifices.
