 <p>Overview
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#001">Luk 5:1</a>, Christ teaches the people out of Peter's ship; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#004">Luk 5:4</a>, in a miraculous taking of fishes, shows how he will make him and his partners fishers of men; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#012">Luk 5:12</a>, cleanses the leper; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#016">Luk 5:16</a>, prays in the wilderness; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#017">Luk 5:17</a>, heals one sick of the palsy; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#027">Luk 5:27</a>, calls Matthew the Publican; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#029">Luk 5:29</a>, eats with sinners, as being the physician of souls; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#033">Luk 5:33</a>, foretells the fastings and afflictions of the apostles after his ascension; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk005.htm#036">Luk 5:36</a>, and illustrates the matter by the parable of old bottles and worn garments.
