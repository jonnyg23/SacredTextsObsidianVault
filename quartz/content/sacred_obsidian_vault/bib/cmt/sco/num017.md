 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num017.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 17</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num017.htm#008">Numbers 17:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">num 17:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>rod of Aaron 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Aaron's rod that budded: Type of Christ in resurrection, owned of God as High Priest. Aaron's priesthood had been questioned in the rebellion of Korah, so God Himself will confirm it (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num017.htm#005">Num 17:5</a>). Each of the tribe-heads brought a perfectly dead rod; God put life into Aaron's only. So all the authors of religions have died, Christ among them, but only Christ was raised from the dead, and exalted to be a high priest. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb004.htm#014">Heb 4:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#004">Heb 5:4-10</a>).
