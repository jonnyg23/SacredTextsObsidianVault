 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer014.htm#001">Jeremiah Chapter 14</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer014.htm#001">Jeremiah 14:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">jer 14:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>dearth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The significance of a drought at this time was very great. It was one of the signs predicted in the Palestinian Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#023">Deu 28:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#024">Deu 28:24</a>) and already fulfilled in part in the reign of Ahab. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1017.htm#001">Kg1 17:1</a>); etc. As that sign had been followed, even though after a long interval, by the Assyrian captivity of the northern kingdom, it should have been received by Judah as a most solemn warning.
