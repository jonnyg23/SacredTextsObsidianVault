 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#001">John Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#007">John 10:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">joh 10:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>I am the door of the sheep 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The shepherd work of our Lord has three aspects: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) As the "Good" Shepherd He gives His life for the sheep (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#011">Joh 10:11</a>) and is, therefore, "the door" by which "if any man enter in he shall be saved." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#009">Joh 10:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This answers to Psalm 22. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) He is the "Great" Shepherd, "brought again from the dead" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#020">Heb 13:20</a>) to care for and make perfect the sheep. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This answers to Psalm 23. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) He is the "Chief" Shepherd, who is coming in glory to give crowns of reward to the faithful shepherds (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#004">Pe1 5:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This answers to Psalm 24.
