 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#005">Exodus 7:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">exo 7:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>when 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>A prophetic sign also. The nations shall know Jehovah when He restores and blesses Israel in the kingdom. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#001">Isa 2:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010">Isa 11:10-1214</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#001">Isa 11:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#004">Isa 60:4-5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#028">Eze 37:28</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#011">Exodus 7:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 7:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>like 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#008">Ti2 3:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#018">Exo 8:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Neither Satan nor his tools can create life: (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#015">Rev 13:15</a>) will be a "lying wonder": (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#009">Th2 2:9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo007.htm#012">Exodus 7:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">exo 7:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Aaron's rod 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Cf) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#002">Exo 4:2</a>) As Moses' rod was the rod of power, the rod of the King (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#004">Deu 33:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#005">Deu 33:5</a>) so Aaron's was the rod of life, the rod of the Priest. As here the serpents, symbols of Satan, who had the power of death; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#009">Rev 12:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#014">Heb 2:14</a>) are swallowed up, so in resurrection death will be "swallowed up in victory"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#054">Co1 15:54</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num017.htm#008">Num 17:8</a>).
