 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#001">Zechariah Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#001">Zechariah 9:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">zac 9:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>burden 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa013.htm#001">Isa 13:1</a>), note 1.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#008">Zechariah 9:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">zac 9:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>because of him 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>There seems to be a reference here to the advance and return of Alexander (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#013">Zac 9:13</a>) after the battle of Issus, who subdued the cities mentioned in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#001">Zac 9:1-6</a>); and afterward returned to Greece without harming Jerusalem. But the greater meaning converges on the yet future last days 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#017">Act 2:17</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>as the last clause of verse (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#008">Zac 9:8</a>) shows, for many oppressors have passed through Jerusalem since the days of Alexander.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#009">Zechariah 9:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">zac 9:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>behold, thy King 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The events following this manifestation of Christ as King are recorded in the Gospels. The real faith of the multitude who cried, "Hosanna" is given in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#011">Mat 21:11</a>) and so little was Jesus deceived by His apparent reception as King, that He wept over Jerusalem and announced its impending destruction (fulfilled A.D. 70; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#038">Luk 19:38-44</a>). The same multitude soon cried, "Crucify Him."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#010">Zechariah 9:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">zac 9:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And I will cut off 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Having introduced the King in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#009">Zac 9:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#010">Zac 9:10</a>) and the verses which follow look forward to the end-time and kingdom. Except in verse 9 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#009">Zac 9:9</a>), this present age is not seen in Zechariah.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#011">Zechariah 9:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">zac 9:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>prisoners 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa024.htm#017">Isa 24:17-23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#021">Zac 9:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#023">Zac 9:23</a>) fix the time as the day of the LORD. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#012">Zechariah 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">zac 9:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>strong hold 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See context, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#014">Zac 9:14</a>). Also, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer016.htm#019">Jer 16:19</a>).
