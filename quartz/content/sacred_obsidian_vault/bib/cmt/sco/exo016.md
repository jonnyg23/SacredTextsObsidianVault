 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#001">Exodus 16:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 16:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>second month 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, May.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#015">Exodus 16:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">exo 16:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>manna 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#035">Exo 16:35</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#016">Exodus 16:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">exo 16:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>according 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#033">Joh 6:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#041">Joh 6:41</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#042">Joh 6:42</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#052">Joh 6:52</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ gives himself unreservedly, but we have no more of Him than faith appropriates, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#018">Exo 16:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#002">Exo 16:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#001">Jos 1:1</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#002">Exo 16:2</a>) is our title. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#003">Exo 16:3</a>) is the law of possession:  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#020">Exodus 16:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">exo 16:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>left 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>As we are not nourished by the memory of food, so neither can spirituality be sustained on past appropriations of Christ.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#035">Exodus 16:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">exo 16:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>manna 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Manna, type of Christ as the "bread of life," come down from heaven to die "for the life of the world." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#035">Joh 6:35</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#048">Joh 6:48-51</a>). A "small" thing (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#014">Exo 16:14</a>) having but the taste of "fresh oil." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num011.htm#008">Num 11:8</a>) or "wafers with honey" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo016.htm#031">Exo 16:31</a>); it typifies Christ in humiliation as presented in Matthew, Mark, and Luke; "having no form nor comeliness; . . . no beauty that we should desire him" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#002">Isa 53:2</a>). But as such He must be received by faith if we would be saved (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#053">Joh 6:53-58</a>). To meditate upon Christ as He went about among men, doing not His own will but the will of the Father (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#038">Joh 6:38-40</a>) is to feed on the manna. This is, of necessity, the spiritual food of young believers, and answers to "milk" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#001">Co1 3:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#002">Co1 3:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>But Christ in glory, and the believer's present and eternal association with Him there, answers to "the old corn of the land" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jos005.htm#011">Jos 5:11</a>) the "meat" of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#013">Heb 5:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#014">Heb 5:14</a>) or Christ as presented in the Epistles of Paul. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#016">Co2 5:16</a>).
