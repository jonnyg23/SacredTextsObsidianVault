 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 36</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#001">Ezekiel 36:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">eze 36:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Chapter 36) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>A beautiful order is discernible in this and the succeeding prophecies: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Restoration of the land (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#001">Eze 36:1-15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) of the people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#016">Eze 36:16-37</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze036.htm#028">Eze 36:28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) judgment on Israel's enemies. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#001">Eze 38:1-39</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#024">Eze 38:24</a>). Afterward follows that which concerns the worship of Jehovah that He may dwell amongst His people.
