 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#001">Philemon</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">plm 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - Philemon
 </p><p>Writer: The Apostle Paul (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#001">Plm 1:1</a>). 
 </p><p>Date: Probably A.D. 64. It is one of the Prison Epistles. See Introductions to Ephesians and Colossians. 
 </p><p>Theme: Onesimus ("profitable"), a slave of Philemon, a Christian of Colosse, had robbed his master and fled to Rome. There he became a convert through Paul, who sent him back to Philemon with this letter. It is of priceless value as a teaching 
 </p><p>1. in practical righteousness; 
 </p><p>2. in Christian brotherhood; 
 </p><p>3. in Christian courtesy; 
 </p><p>4. in the law of love. 
 </p><p>The divisions are four 
 </p><p>1. Greeting (Philemon 1:1-3). 
 </p><p>2. The character of Philemon (Philemon 1:4-7). 
 </p><p>3. Intercession for Onesimus (Philemon 1:8-21). 
 </p><p>4. Salutations and conclusion (Philemon 1:22-25).
