 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#001">John Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#003">John 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">joh 3:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>born again 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Regeneration: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The necessity of the new birth grows out of the incapacity of the natural man to "see" or "enter into" the kingdom of God. However gifted, moral, or refined, the natural man is absolutely blind to spiritual truth, and impotent to enter the kingdom; for he can neither obey, understand, nor please God (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#003">Joh 3:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#005">Joh 3:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#006">Joh 3:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#005">Psa 51:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#009">Jer 17:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar007.htm#021">Mar 7:21-23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#014">Co1 2:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#007">Rom 8:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#008">Rom 8:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#003">Eph 2:3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#033">Mat 6:33</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) The new birth is not a reformation of the old nature 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#006">Rom 6:6</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>but a creative act of the holy Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#005">Joh 3:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#013">Joh 1:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#017">Co2 5:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#010">Eph 2:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#024">Eph 4:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The condition of the new birth is faith in Christ crucified (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#014">Joh 3:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#015">Joh 3:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012">Joh 1:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#013">Joh 1:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#024">Gal 3:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Through the new birth the believer becomes a partaker of the divine nature and of the life of Christ Himself (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020">Gal 2:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#010">Eph 2:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#024">Eph 4:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#027">Col 1:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#023">Pe1 1:23-25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#004">Pe2 1:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#010">Jo1 5:10-12</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#010">John 3:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">joh 3:10</a></font></span></p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">joh 3:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>perish 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "apollumi") translated "marred," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar002.htm#022">Mar 2:22</a>); "lost,"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#006">Mat 10:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#024">Mat 15:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#011">Mat 18:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#004">Luk 15:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#006">Luk 15:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#032">Luk 15:32</a>). In no New Testament instance does it signify cessation of existence or of consciousness. It is the condition of every non-believer. 
 </p><p>
