 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#001">Jeremiah Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#006">Jeremiah 3:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">jer 3:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>The Lord said 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The general character of the second message to Judah is: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) of reproach that the example of Jehovah's chastening of the northern kingdom (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#001">Kg2 17:1-18</a>) had produced no effect upon Judah, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#006">Jer 3:6-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) of warning of a like chastisement impending over Judah, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#015">Jer 3:15-17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) of touching appeals to return to Jehovah, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#012">Jer 3:12-14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) of promises of final national restoration and blessing, for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#016">Jer 3:16-18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Israel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Israel" and "Ephraim": names by which the northern kingdom (the ten tribes) is usually called in the prophets. When by "Israel" the whole nation is meant, it will appear from the context.
