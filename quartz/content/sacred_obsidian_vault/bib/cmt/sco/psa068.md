 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#001">Psalms 68:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 68:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Let God arise 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The entire Psalm is pervaded by the joy of Israel in the kingdom, but a stricter order of events begins with verse 18 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#018">Psa 68:18</a>). This is quoted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#007">Eph 4:7-16</a>) of Christ's ascension ministry. Verses 21-23 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#021">Psa 68:21-23</a>) refer to the regathering of Israel, and the destruction of the Beast and his armies. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See "Beast," 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#008">Dan 7:8</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See "Beast," 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See "Armageddon" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#016">Rev 16:16</a>), (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017">Rev 19:17-19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Verses 24-35 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#024">Psa 68:24-35</a>) are descriptive of full and universal kingdom blessing. 
 </p><p>
