 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#002">Romans 4:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rom 4:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>works 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#024">Jam 2:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>These are two aspects of one truth. Paul speaks of that which justifies man before God, namely: faith alone, wholly apart from works; James of the proof before men, that he who professes to have justifying faith really has it. Paul speaks of what God sees -- faith; James of what men see -- works, as the visible evidence of faith. Paul draws his illustration from (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#006">Gen 15:6</a>) James from (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#001">Gen 22:1-19</a>). James' key phrase is "ye see" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#024">Jam 2:24</a>) for men cannot see faith except as manifested through works.

 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ died under our sins (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#024">Pe1 2:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#021">Co2 5:21</a>) that He was raised and exalted to God's right hand, "now to appear in the presence of God for us" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#024">Heb 9:24</a>) is the token that our sins are gone, that His work for us has the divine approbation and that we, for whom He suffered, are completely justified.
