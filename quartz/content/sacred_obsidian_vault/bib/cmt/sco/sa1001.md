 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1001.htm#003">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 1:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">sa1 1:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord of hosts 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jehovah (Lord) of Hosts, Hebrew, Jehovah Sabaoth. For the distinctive meanings of Jehovah, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen002.htm#004">Gen 2:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Sabaoth means simply host or hosts, but with especial reference to warfare or service. In use the two ideas are united; Jehovah is LORD of (warrior) hosts. It is the name, therefore, of Jehovah in manifestation of power. "The Lord of Hosts, He is the King of glory" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#010">Psa 24:10</a>) and accordingly in the Old Testament Scripture this name is revealed in the time of Israel's need. It is never found in the Pentateuch, nor directly in Joshua or Judges, and occurs but rarely in the Psalms; but Jeremiah, the prophet of approaching national judgment, uses the name about eighty times. Haggai in two chapters uses the name fourteen times, Zechariah in fourteen chapters calls upon the Lord of hosts about fifty times. In Malachi the name occurs about twenty five times. In the utmost extremity, the Psalmist twice comforts his heart with the assurance "the Lord of hosts is with us." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#007">Psa 46:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#011">Psa 46:11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The meanings and uses of this name may thus be summarized: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The "hosts" are heavenly. Primarily the angels are meant, but the name gathers into itself the idea of all divine or heavenly power as available for the need of God's people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#001">Gen 32:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#002">Gen 32:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#001">Isa 6:1-5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1022.htm#019">Kg1 22:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#013">Luk 2:13-15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) In use this is the distinctive name of Deity for Israel's help and comfort in the time of her division and failure (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1018.htm#015">Kg1 18:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1019.htm#014">Kg1 19:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009">Isa 1:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#011">Isa 8:11-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#013">Isa 9:13-19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa010.htm#024">Isa 10:24-27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa031.htm#004">Isa 31:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa031.htm#005">Isa 31:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/hag002.htm#004">Hag 2:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal003.htm#016">Mal 3:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mal003.htm#017">Mal 3:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam005.htm#004">Jam 5:4</a>).
