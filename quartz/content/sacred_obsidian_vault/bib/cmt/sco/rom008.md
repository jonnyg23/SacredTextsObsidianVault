 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#001">Romans Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#001">Romans 8:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rom 8:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>who walk 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The statement ends with "Christ Jesus"; the last ten words are interpolated.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#002">Romans 8:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rom 8:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Spirit 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Hitherto, in Romans, the Holy Spirit has been mentioned but once; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#005">Rom 5:5</a>); in this chapter, He is mentioned nineteen times. Redemption is by blood and by power. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo014.htm#030">Exo 14:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#021">Rom 3:21-5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#011">Rom 3:11</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>speaks of the redemptive price; Romans 8 speaks of redemptive power.
