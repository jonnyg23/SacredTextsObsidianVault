 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#012">Romans 5:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">rom 5:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>have sinned 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The first sin wrought the moral ruin of the race. The demonstration is simple. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Death is universal (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#012">Rom 4:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#014">Rom 4:14</a>), all die: sinless infants, moral people, religious people, equally with the depraved. For a universal effect there must be a universal cause; that cause is a state of universal sin (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#012">Rom 5:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) But this universal state must have had a cause. It did. The consequence of Adam's sin was that "the many were made sinners" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#019">Rom 5:19</a>) -- "By the offence of one judgment came upon all men unto condemnation" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#018">Rom 5:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Personal sins are not meant here. From Adam to Moses death reigned (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#014">Rom 5:14</a>), although, there being no law, personal guilt was not imputed (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#013">Rom 5:13</a>). Accordingly, from (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen004.htm#007">Gen 4:7</a>) to (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#014">Exo 29:14</a>) the sin-offering is not once mentioned. Then, since physical death from Adam to Moses was not due to the sinful acts of those who die (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#013">Rom 5:13</a>), it follows that it was due to a universal sinful state, or nature, and that state is declared to be out inheritance from Adam. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) the moral state of fallen man is described in Scripture (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen006.htm#005">Gen 6:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#046">Kg1 8:46</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa014.htm#001">Psa 14:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa039.htm#005">Psa 39:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#009">Jer 17:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#011">Mat 18:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar007.htm#020">Mar 7:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar007.htm#023">Mar 7:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#021">Rom 1:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#001">Rom 2:1-29</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#009">Rom 3:9-19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#024">Rom 7:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#007">Rom 8:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#006">Joh 3:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#014">Co1 2:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2003.htm#014">Co2 3:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2004.htm#004">Co2 4:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#019">Gal 5:19-21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#001">Eph 2:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#011">Eph 2:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#012">Eph 2:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#018">Eph 4:18-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#021">Col 1:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#013">Heb 3:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam004.htm#014">Jam 4:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#022">Co1 15:22</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#014">Romans 5:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">rom 5:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Adam to Moses 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Broadly, the contrast is: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Adam: sin, death; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ: righteousness, life. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Adam drew down into his ruin the old creation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#019">Rom 8:19-22</a>) of which he was lord and head. Christ brings into moral unity with God, and into eternal life, the new creation of which he is Lord and Head. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#022">Eph 1:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#023">Eph 1:23</a>). Even the animal and material creation, cursed for man's sake, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#017">Gen 3:17</a>) will be delivered by Christ. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#006">Isa 11:6-9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#019">Rom 8:19-22</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#015">Romans 5:15</a></p>
