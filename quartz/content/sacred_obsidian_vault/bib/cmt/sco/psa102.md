 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 102</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#001">Psalms 102:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 102:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Hear my prayer, O Lord 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The references of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#025">Psa 102:25-27</a>) to Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#010">Heb 1:10-12</a>) assures us that in the preceding verses of Psalm 102 we have, prophetically, the exercises of His holy soul in the days of His humiliation and rejection. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Psalm 110 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#001">Psa 110:1-7</a>), next in order of the Messianic Psalms.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa102.htm#015">Psalms 102:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 102:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>heathen 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, nations. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>fear 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#009">Psa 19:9</a>).
