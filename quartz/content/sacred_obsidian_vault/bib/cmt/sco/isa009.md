 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 9</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#003">Isaiah 9:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 9:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>not 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Omit, not. Isaiah complains that despite the wickedness of the northern kingdom her afflictions are light, her prosperity great.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#007">Isaiah 9:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 9:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>throne of David 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "throne of David" is a phrase as definite, historic, historically, as "throne of the Caesars," and as little admits of "spiritualizing." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#032">Luk 1:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#033">Luk 1:33</a>). 
 </p><p>"Kingdom (Old Testament). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Davidic Covenant," 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#016">Sa2 7:16</a>), (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Act 15:14-16</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#008">Isaiah 9:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 9:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Jacob 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#028">Gen 32:28</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#012">Isaiah 9:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">isa 9:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>For all this his anger 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#017">Isa 9:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#021">Isa 9:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#025">Isa 5:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa010.htm#004">Isa 10:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The context explains. Jehovah's hand is outstretched still because His chastisement is followed by no amendment on the part of Israel.
