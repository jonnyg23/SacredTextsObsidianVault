 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 42</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#001">Isaiah 42:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 42:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>servant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>There is a twofold account of the Coming Servant: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) he is represented as weak, despised, rejected, slain: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) and also as a mighty conqueror, taking vengeance on the nations and restoring Israel (for example (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa040.htm#010">Isa 40:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa063.htm#001">Isa 63:1-4</a>). The former class of passages relate to the first advent, and are fulfilled; the latter to the second advent, and are unfulfilled.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#006">Isaiah 42:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 42:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>for a light of the Gentiles 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The prophets connect the Gentiles with Christ in a threefold way" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) as the Light He brings salvation to the Gentiles (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#032">Luk 2:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#047">Act 13:47</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#048">Act 13:48</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) as the "Root of Jesse" He is to reign over the Gentiles in His kingdom. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010">Isa 11:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#012">Rom 15:12</a>). He saves the Gentiles, which is the distinctive feature of this present age. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#017">Rom 11:17-24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#011">Eph 2:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#012">Eph 2:12</a>) He reigns over the Gentiles in the kingdom-age, to follow this. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Kingdom (Old Testament)," 
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026">Gen 1:26-28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Believing Gentiles in the present age, together with believing Jews, constitute "the church which is His body." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#006">Eph 3:6</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#009">Isaiah 42:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 42:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>former things 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, Isaiah's prediction of Sennacherib's invasion and its results, Isaiah 10 and 37. See also, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#021">Isa 41:21-23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#008">Isa 43:8-12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#007">Isa 44:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa048.htm#003">Isa 48:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa048.htm#005">Isa 48:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa048.htm#016">Isa 48:16</a>). This appeal of the prophet to the fulfilment of his former predictions strongly confirms the unity of the book.
