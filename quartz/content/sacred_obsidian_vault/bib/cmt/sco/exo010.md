 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo010.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo010.htm#011">Exodus 10:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">exo 10:11</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>ye that are 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The third compromise proposed by Pharaoh is, perhaps, as applied to believers, the sublest and most successful of them all. The most godly parents desire worldly prosperity and position for their children. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#020">Mat 20:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#021">Mat 20:21</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo010.htm#024">Exodus 10:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">exo 10:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>said, Go ye 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, "Leave your property in the world." Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#025">Mat 16:25-27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#018">Luk 18:18-25</a>).
