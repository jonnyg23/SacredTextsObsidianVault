 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 34</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#028">Ezekiel 34:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">eze 34:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>prey to the heathen 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The whole passage (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze034.htm#023">Eze 34:23-30</a>) speaks of a restoration yet future, for the remnant which returned after the 70 years, and their posterity, were continually under the Gentile yoke, until, in A.D. 70, they were finally driven from the land into a dispersion which still continues.
