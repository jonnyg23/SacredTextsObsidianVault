 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001">Matthew Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#010">Matthew 1:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">mat 1:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Josias 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Josiah, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1013.htm#002">Kg1 13:2</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#016">Matthew 1:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">mat 1:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Six Marys 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Six Marys are to be distinguished in the New Testament: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) the mother of Jesus; always clearly identified by the context. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Mary Magdalene, a woman of Magdala, "out of whom went seven demons" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#002">Luk 8:2</a>) She is never mentioned apart from the identifying word "Magdalene." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The mother of James (called "the less," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar015.htm#040">Mar 15:40</a>) and Joses, the apostles. A comparison of; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#025">Joh 19:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#056">Mat 27:56</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar015.htm#040">Mar 15:40</a>) establishes the inference that this Mary, the mother of James the less, and of Joses was the wife of Alphaeus (called also Cleophas), (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#025">Joh 19:25</a>) and a sister of Mary the mother of Jesus. Except in; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#061">Mat 27:61</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#001">Mat 28:1</a>) where she is called "the other Mary (that is, "other" than her sister, Mary the Virgin); and (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#025">Joh 19:25</a>) where she is called "of Cleophas," she is mentioned only in connection with one or both of her sons. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Mary of Bethany, sister of Martha and Lazarus, mentioned by name only in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#039">Luk 10:39-42</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#001">Joh 11:1-2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#019">Joh 11:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#020">Joh 11:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#028">Joh 11:28</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#031">Joh 11:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#032">Joh 11:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#045">Joh 11:45</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#003">Joh 12:3</a>) but referred to in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#007">Mat 26:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#003">Mar 14:3-9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) The mother of John Mark and sister of Barnabas (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act012.htm#012">Act 12:12</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) A helper of Paul in Rome (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom016.htm#006">Rom 16:6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>of whom was born 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The changed expression here is important. It is no longer, "who begat," but, "Mary, of whom was born Jesus." Jesus was not begotten of natural generation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ (Greel, "Christos", means "anointed"), the Greek form of the Hebrew, "Messiah" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#025">Dan 9:25-26</a>) is the official name of our Lord, as Jesus is his human name; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#021">Luk 2:21</a>). The name, or title, "Christ" connects Him with the entire Old Testament foreview 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008">Zac 12:8</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>of a coming prophet (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu018.htm#015">Deu 18:15-19</a>); Priest (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#004">Psa 110:4</a>) and king (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#007">Sa2 7:7-10</a>). As these were typically anointed with oil; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1019.htm#016">Kg1 19:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#007">Exo 29:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#013">Sa1 16:13</a>) so Jesus was anointed with the Holy Spirit; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#016">Mat 3:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#010">Mar 1:10-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#021">Luk 3:21-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#032">Joh 1:32-33</a>) thus becoming officially "the Christ."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#021">Matthew 1:21</a></p>
