 <p>
 </p><p>Melchisedec 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018">Gen 14:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Melchisedec was a suitable type of Christ as High Priest, because: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) he was a king-priest (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018">Gen 14:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#012">Zac 6:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#013">Zac 6:13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) his name means, "my king is righteous" (compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#005">Isa 11:5</a>) and he was king of Salem (that is, "peace". (compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#006">Isa 11:6-9</a>).) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) he had no (recorded) "beginning of days" (compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">Joh 1:1</a>) nor "end of life" (compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#009">Rom 6:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#023">Heb 7:23-25</a>); nor 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) was he made a high priest by human appointment (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#004">Psa 110:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>But the contrast between the high priesthood of Melchisedec and Aaron is only as to person, "order" (or appointment), and duration. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In His work, Christ follows the Aaronic pattern, the "shadow" of which Christ was the substance. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#001">Heb 8:1-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#001">Heb 9:1-28</a>).
