 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#001">Zechariah Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#001">Zechariah 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">zac 6:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>four chariots 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The interpretation of the tenth vision must be governed by the authoritative declaration of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#005">Zac 6:5</a>). That which is symbolized by the four chariots with their horses is not the four world-empires of Daniel, but "the four spirits of heaven which go forth from standing before the Lord of all the earth" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#005">Zac 6:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>These "spirits" are angels; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#019">Luk 1:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#014">Heb 1:14</a>) and are most naturally interpreted of the four angels of; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#001">Rev 7:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev009.htm#014">Rev 9:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev009.htm#015">Rev 9:15</a>). These have also a ministry earthward, and of like nature with the "spirits" of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#001">Zac 6:1-8</a>). namely, judgment. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The symbol (chariots and horses) is in perfect harmony with this. Always in Scripture symbolism they stand for the power of God earthward in judgment. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer046.htm#009">Jer 46:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer046.htm#010">Jer 46:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joe002.htm#003">Joe 2:3-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#001">Nah 3:1-7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The vision, then, speaks of the Lord's judgments upon the Gentile nations north and south in the day of the Lord; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>).

 <p>
 </p><p>crowns 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Following the earth-judgments symbolized in the horsed chariots (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#001">Zac 6:1-8</a>) comes the manifestation of Christ in His kingdom glory (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#009">Zac 6:9-15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This is the invariable prophetic order: first the judgments of the day of the Lord; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010">Isa 2:10-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011">Rev 19:11-21</a>) then the kingdom (cf); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#005">Psa 2:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#006">Psa 2:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#024">Isa 3:24-26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#002">Isa 4:2-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa010.htm#033">Isa 10:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa010.htm#034">Isa 10:34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#001">Isa 11:1-10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#019">Rev 19:19-21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#004">Rev 20:4-6</a>). This is set forth symbolically by the crowning of Joshua, which was not a vision, but actually done 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#003">Isa 8:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#004">Isa 8:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#016">Eze 37:16-22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The fulfilment in the BRANCH will infinitely transcend the symbol. He "shall bear the glory"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#013">Zac 6:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#027">Mat 16:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030">Mat 24:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Mat 25:31</a>) as the Priest-King on His own throne (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#012">Zac 6:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#013">Zac 6:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#001">Heb 7:1-3</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ is now a Priest, but still in the holiest within the veil; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#015">Lev 16:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#011">Heb 9:11-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#024">Heb 9:24</a>) and seated on the Father's throne (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#021">Rev 3:21</a>). He has not yet come out to take His own throne (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#028">Heb 9:28</a>). The crowns made for the symbolical crowning of Joshua were to be laid up in the temple as a memorial to keep alive this larger hope of Israel.
