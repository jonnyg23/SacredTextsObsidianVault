 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 7</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#002">Isaiah 7:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 7:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Ephraim 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In the prophetic books "Ephraim" and "Israel" are the collective names of the ten tribes who, under Jeroboam, established the northern kingdom, subsequently called Samaria (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1016.htm#024">Kg1 16:24</a>) and were (B.C. 722) sent into an exile which still continues. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#001">Kg2 17:1-6</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>They are distinguished as "the outcasts of Israel" from "the dispersed of Judah." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#012">Isa 11:12</a>). "Hidden" in the world (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#044">Mat 13:44</a>) they, with Judah, are yet to be restored to Palestine and made one nation again. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer023.htm#005">Jer 23:5-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#011">Eze 37:11-24</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#003">Isaiah 7:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 7:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Shearjashub 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Meaning, a remnant shall return. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#003">Isa 8:3</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#013">Isaiah 7:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">isa 7:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Hear ye now 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The prophecy is not addressed to the faithless Ahaz, but to the whole "house of David." The objection that such a far-off event as the birth of Christ could be no "sign" to Ahaz, is, therefore, puerile. It was a continuing prophecy addressed to the Davidic family, and accounts at once for the instant assent of Mary. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#038">Luk 1:38</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#015">Isaiah 7:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">isa 7:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Butter and honey 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Indicating the plainness and simplicity of the life in which the young Immanuel should be brought up.
