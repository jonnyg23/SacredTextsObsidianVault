 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#001">Matthew Chapter 21</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#001">Matthew 21:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mat 21:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>nigh unto Jerusalem 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#001">Mat 21:1-9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#009">Zac 9:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#004">Zac 14:4-9</a>). The two advents are in striking contrast.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#004">Matthew 21:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">mat 21:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>fulfilled 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The king's final and official offer of Himself according to (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac009.htm#009">Zac 9:9</a>). Acclaimed by an unthinking multitude whose real belief is expressed in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#011">Mat 21:11</a>); but with no welcome from the official representatives of the nation, He was soon to hear the multitude shout: "Crucify him."  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#012">Matthew 21:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">mat 21:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>cast 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#045">Luk 19:45</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#015">Mar 11:15-18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh002.htm#013">Joh 2:13-25</a>) which introduced, as this cleansing closed, the offer of Christ to Israel as King.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#017">Matthew 21:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">mat 21:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Bethany 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#001">Joh 11:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#002">Joh 11:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#039">Luk 10:39-42</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#001">Mar 11:1-11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#029">Luk 19:29-35</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#001">Joh 12:1-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>With no other place is the human Christ so tenderly associated, while it also was the place of manifestation of His divine power. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#043">Joh 11:43</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#044">Joh 11:44</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#019">Matthew 21:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">mat 21:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>fig tree 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Literally, a solitary fig tree. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#006">Luk 13:6-9</a>). The withered fig tree is a parabolic miracle concerning Israel. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#006">Luk 13:6-9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#032">Mat 24:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#033">Mat 24:33</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>a prophecy that Israel shall again bud.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#028">Matthew 21:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">mat 21:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>vineyard 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "vineyard," margin reference, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#001">Mat 20:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#001">Mat 20:1</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#029">Matthew 21:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">mat 21:29</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>repented 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#020">Luk 15:20</a>); the other perfect illustration of repentance. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act017.htm#030">Act 17:30</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#032">Matthew 21:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">mat 21:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>righteousness 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#010">Rom 10:10</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#042">Matthew 21:42</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_042">mat 21:42</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Lord's 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jehovah. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa118.htm#023">Psa 118:23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#043">Matthew 21:43</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_043">mat 21:43</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>kingdom of God 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Note that Matthew here as in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#031">Mat 21:31</a>) uses the larger word, kingdom of God: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#033">Mat 6:33</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The kingdom of heaven; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#002">Mat 3:2</a>). 
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#024">Co1 15:24</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>will yet be set up. Meantime, the kingdom of God and his righteousness is taken from Israel nationally and given to the Gentiles. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#030">Rom 9:30-33</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>you 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, national Israel, the barren vine. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#033">Mat 21:33-41</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#001">Isa 5:1-7</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#044">Matthew 21:44</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_044">mat 21:44</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>stone shall be broken 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ, as the "Stone", is revealed in a threefold way: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) To Israel Christ, coming not as a splendid monarch but in the form of a servant, is a stumbling stone and rock of offence. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#014">Isa 8:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#015">Isa 8:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#032">Rom 9:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#033">Rom 9:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#023">Co1 1:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#008">Pe1 2:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) to the church, Christ is the foundation stone and the head of the corner (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#011">Co1 3:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#020">Eph 2:20-22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#004">Pe1 2:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#005">Pe1 2:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) to the Gentile world-powers 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(see "Gentiles," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024">Luk 21:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#019">Rev 16:19</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>He is to be the smiting-stone of destruction (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#034">Dan 2:34</a>). Israel stumbled over Christ; the church is built upon Christ; Gentile world-dominion will be broken by Christ. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Armageddon" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014">Rev 16:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#019">Rev 19:19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>And whosoever 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, Whosoever falls on this stone shall be crushed togetherthat is, the Jews (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#014">Isa 8:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#032">Rom 9:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#033">Rom 9:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#023">Co1 1:23</a>)] but on whomsoever it may fall, he will be scattered as dust (Greek, "likmaō", "winnowed"), that is, the Gentile nations. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#034">Dan 2:34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#035">Dan 2:35</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#045">Dan 2:45</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#035">Dan 2:35</a>).
