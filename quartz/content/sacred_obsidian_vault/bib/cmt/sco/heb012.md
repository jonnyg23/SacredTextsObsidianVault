 </p><p>church 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Church (true), Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The true church, composed of the whole number of regenerate persons from Pentecost to the first resurrection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052">Co1 15:52</a>) united together and to Christ by the baptism with the Holy Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#012">Co1 12:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#013">Co1 12:13</a>) is the body of Christ of which He is the Head. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#022">Eph 1:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#023">Eph 1:23</a>). As such, it is a holy temple for the habitation of God through the Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#021">Eph 2:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#022">Eph 2:22</a>) is "one flesh" with Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#030">Eph 5:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#031">Eph 5:31</a>) and espoused to Him as a chaste virgin to one husband. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#002">Co2 11:2-4</a>).
