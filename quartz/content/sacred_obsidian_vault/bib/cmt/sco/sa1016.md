 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#001">1 Kings (1 Samuel) Chapter 16</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#021">1 Kings (1 Samuel) 16:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">sa1 16:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And David 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#055">Sa1 17:55-56</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The order of events is: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) David, whose skill on the harp, and valour in the combat with the lion and bear (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#034">Sa1 17:34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#036">Sa1 17:36</a>) were known to "one of the servants" of Saul, was brought to play before the king (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#017">Sa1 16:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#018">Sa1 16:18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) David returns to Bethlehem (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#015">Sa1 17:15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) David is sent to Saul's camp (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#017">Sa1 17:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#018">Sa1 17:18</a>) and performs his great exploit. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Saul's question (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#055">Sa1 17:55</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#056">Sa1 17:56</a>) implies only that he had forgotten the name of David's father -- not remarkable certainly in an oriental king.
