 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa063.htm#016">Isaiah 63:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">isa 63:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>our Father 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#002">Isa 1:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa064.htm#008">Isa 64:8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Israel, collectively, the national Israel, recognizes God as the national Father (cf) (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#022">Exo 4:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#023">Exo 4:23</a>); Doubtless the believing Israelite was born anew (cf); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#003">Joh 3:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#005">Joh 3:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#028">Luk 13:28</a>) but the Old Testament Scriptures show no trace of the consciousness of personal sonship. The explanation is given in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#001">Gal 4:1-7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Israelite, though a child, "differed nothing from a servant." The Spirit, as the "Spirit of His Son," could not be given to impart the consciousness of sonship until redemption had been accomplished. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#004">Gal 4:4-6</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Adoption"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#015">Rom 8:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#005">Eph 1:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#005">Eph 1:5</a>).
