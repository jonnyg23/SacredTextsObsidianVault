 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 41</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#002">Isaiah 41:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 41:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>the righteous man from the east 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The reference here seems to be to Cyrus, whose victories and rapid growth in power are here ascribed to the providence of God. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#005">Isa 41:5-7</a>) describe the effect upon the nations of the rise of the Persian power. They heartened each other, and made (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#007">Isa 41:7</a>) new idols. At (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#008">Isa 41:8</a>), the prophet addresses Israel. Since it was their God who raised up Cyrus, they should expect good, not evil, from him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#008">Isa 41:8-20</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#021">Isa 41:21-24</a>) form a contemptuous challenge to the idols in whom the nations are trusting.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#008">Isaiah 41:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 41:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>servant 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Three servants of Jehovah are mentioned in Isaiah: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) David (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#035">Isa 37:35</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Israel the nation (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#008">Isa 41:8-16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#001">Isa 43:1-10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#001">Isa 44:1-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa044.htm#021">Isa 44:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#004">Isa 45:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa048.htm#020">Isa 48:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Messiah (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#001">Isa 42:1-12</a>); Isaiah 49, entire chapter, but note especially (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#005">Isa 49:5-7</a>); where the Servant Christ restores the servant nation; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#004">Isa 50:4-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#013">Isa 52:13-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#053">Isa 52:53</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#001">Isa 52:1-12</a>). Israel the nation was a faithless servant, but restored and converted will yet thresh mountains. Against the Servant Christ no charge of unfaithfulness or failure is brought. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#001">Isa 42:1</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#014">Isaiah 41:14</a></p>

 </p><p>

 </p><p>
