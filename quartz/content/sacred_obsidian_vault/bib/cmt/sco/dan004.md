 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#001">Daniel Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#001">Daniel 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">dan 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>dwell 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Nebuchadnezzar, first of the Gentile world-kings in whom the times of the Gentiles (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024">Luk 21:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014">Rev 16:14</a>) began, perfectly comprehended the universality of the sway committed to him (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#037">Dan 2:37</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#038">Dan 2:38</a>) as also did Cyrus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ezr001.htm#002">Ezr 1:2</a>). That they did not actually subject the known earth to their sway is true, but they might have done so. The earth lay in their power.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#003">Daniel 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">dan 4:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>everlasting kingdom 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#016">Sa2 7:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#035">Psa 89:35-37</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#013">Dan 7:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#014">Dan 7:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#027">Dan 7:27</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031">Luk 1:31-33</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#010">Daniel 4:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">dan 4:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>tree 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Symbol of a great king. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#022">Dan 4:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze031.htm#001">Eze 31:1-14</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#013">Daniel 4:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">dan 4:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>watcher 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#017">Dan 4:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#023">Dan 4:23</a>); plural in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#017">Dan 4:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#016">Daniel 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">dan 4:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>seven 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The number of completeness.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#025">Daniel 4:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">dan 4:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>till thou know 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The discipline was effective. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#030">Dan 4:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#037">Dan 4:37</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#034">Daniel 4:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">dan 4:34</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>I blessed the most High 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>A progress may be traced in Nebuchadnezzar's apprehension of the true God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) "God" is "a God of gods" [one amongst the national or tribal gods, but greater than they], and a "Lord Adonai", signifies "Master of kings", and a "revealer of secrets" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#047">Dan 2:47</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) He is still a Hebrew deity, but Master of angels, and a God who responds to faith (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan003.htm#028">Dan 3:28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Here (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#034">Dan 4:34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan004.htm#035">Dan 4:35</a>) the king rises into a true apprehension of God. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare Darius, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan006.htm#025">Dan 6:25-27</a>).
