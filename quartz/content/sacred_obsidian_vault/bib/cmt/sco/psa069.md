 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 69</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#001">Psalms 69:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 69:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Shoshannim 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#001">Psa 45:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Save me, O God 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The New Testament quotations from, and references to, this Psalm indicate in what way it adumbrates Christ. It is the psalm of His humiliation and rejection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#004">Psa 69:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#007">Psa 69:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#008">Psa 69:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#010">Psa 69:10-12</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#014">Psa 69:14-20</a>) may well describe the exercises of His holy soul in Gethsemane (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#036">Mat 26:36-45</a>) while (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#021">Psa 69:21</a>) is a direct reference to the cross; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#034">Mat 27:34</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#048">Mat 27:48</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#028">Joh 19:28</a>). The imprecatory verses (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#022">Psa 69:22-28</a>) are connected (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#009">Rom 11:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#010">Rom 11:10</a>) with the present judicial blindness of Israel, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#025">Psa 69:25</a>) having special reference to Judas. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#020">Act 1:20</a>) who is thus made typical of his generation, which shared his guilt. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Psalm 72, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
