 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#001">1 Peter Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#008">1 Peter 2:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pe1 2:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>stone of stumbling 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Christ crucified is the Rock: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Smitten that the Spirit of life may flow from Him to all who will drink. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo017.htm#006">Exo 17:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#004">Co1 10:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#013">Joh 4:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#014">Joh 4:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#037">Joh 7:37-39</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) To the church the foundation and chief corner Stone. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#020">Eph 2:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) To the Jews at His first coming a "stumbling stone". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#032">Rom 9:32</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#033">Rom 9:33</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#023">Co1 1:23</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) to Israel at His second coming the "headstone of the corner". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#007">Zac 4:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) To the Gentile world-power the smiting "stone cut out without hands". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#034">Dan 2:34</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(6) In the divine purpose the Stone which, after the destruction of Gentile world-power, is to grow and fill the earth. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(7) To unbelievers the crushing Stone of judgment. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#044">Mat 21:44</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#009">1 Peter 2:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pe1 2:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>priesthood 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The New Testament priesthood, Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Until the law was given the head of each family was the family priest. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen008.htm#020">Gen 8:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#025">Gen 26:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen031.htm#054">Gen 31:54</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) When the law was proposed, the promise to perfect obedience was that Israel would be unto God "a kingdom of priests" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#006">Exo 19:6</a>). But Israel violated the law, and God shut up the priestly office to the Aaronic family, appointing the tribe of Levi to minister to them, thus constituting the typical priesthood. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#001">Exo 28:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) In the dispensation of grace, all believers are unconditionally constituted a "kingdom of priests" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#009">Pe1 2:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#006">Rev 1:6</a>) the distinction which Israel failed to achieve by works. The priesthood of the believer is, therefore, a birthright; just as every descendant of Aaron was born to the priesthood. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#001">Heb 5:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) The chief privilege of a priest is access to God. Under law the high priest only could enter "the holiest of all," and that but once a year (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#007">Heb 9:7</a>); but when Christ died, the veil, type of Christ's human body (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#020">Heb 10:20</a>) was rent, so that now the believer-priests, equally with Christ the High Priest, have access to God in the holiest. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#019">Heb 10:19-22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The high Priest is corporeally there (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb004.htm#014">Heb 4:14-16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#024">Heb 9:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#019">Heb 10:19-22</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) In the exercise of his office the New Testament believer-priest is 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>  a) a sacrificer who offers a threefold sacrifice:
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) his own living body. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#001">Rom 12:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#017">Phi 2:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#006">Ti2 4:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#016">Jo1 3:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#027">Jam 1:27</a>).
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) praise to God, "the fruit of the lips that make mention of His name" (R.V.), to be offered "continually"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#015">Heb 13:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#022">Exo 25:22</a>) "I will commune with thee from above the mercy seat".
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (c) his substance. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#016">Heb 13:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#013">Rom 12:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#006">Gal 6:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#005">Jo3 1:5-8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#002">Heb 13:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#010">Gal 6:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#014">Tit 3:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>  b) The New Testament priest is also an intercessor. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1002.htm#001">Ti1 2:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#012">Col 4:12</a>).
