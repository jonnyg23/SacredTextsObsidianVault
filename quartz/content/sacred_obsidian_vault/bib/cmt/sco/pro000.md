 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#001">Proverbs</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">pro 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - Proverbs
 </p><p>This collection of sententious sayings is divine wisdom applied to the earthly conditions of the people of God. That the Proverbs were Solomon's (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#001">Pro 1:1</a>) implies no more than that he gathered into orderly arrangement sayings already current amongst the people, the wisdom of the Spirit, perhaps through many centuries (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc012.htm#009">Ecc 12:9</a>). Chapters 25-29 were current in Hezekiah's time (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc025.htm#001">Ecc 25:1</a>). Chapters 30 and 31 are by Agur and Lemuel. 
 </p><p>The book is in six parts: 
 </p><p>1. To sons (Proverbs 1-7). 
 </p><p>2. The praise of wisdom (Proverbs 8-9). 
 </p><p>3. The folly of sin (Proverbs 10-19). 
 </p><p>4. Warnings and instructions (Proverbs 20-29). 
 </p><p>5. The words of Agur (Proverbs 30). 
 </p><p>6. The words of King Lemuel (Proverbs 31).
