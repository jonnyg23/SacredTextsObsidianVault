 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#001">Matthew Chapter 22</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#002">Matthew 22:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">mat 22:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>kingdom 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#002">Mat 3:2</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#007">Matthew 22:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">mat 22:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>burned 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Fulfilled as to Jerusalem; A.D. 70. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020">Luk 21:20-24</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#009">Matthew 22:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">mat 22:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>go ye 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The world-wide call. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#016">Mat 28:16-20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#017">Rev 22:17</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#015">Matthew 22:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">mat 22:15</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Then went 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In the different classes, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#015">Mat 23:15-40</a>). Jesus meets representatives of all Israel, Pharisees, Sadducees, Herodians. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#007">Mat 3:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>For them, silenced but unrepentant, no message is left but "woe." (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#001">Mat 23:1-39</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#029">Matthew 22:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">mat 22:29</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>ye do err 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, ye deceive yourselves, etc. Jesus' answer gives the three incapacities of the rationalist: self-deception, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#021">Rom 1:21-22</a>); ignorance of the spiritual content of Scripture, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act013.htm#027">Act 13:27</a>); disbelief in the intervention of divine power. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2003.htm#005">Pe2 3:5-9</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#030">Matthew 22:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">mat 22:30</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>angels 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#004">Heb 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#035">Matthew 22:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">mat 22:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>lawyer 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "nomikos", "of the law"); occurs also (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#030">Luk 7:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#025">Luk 10:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#045">Luk 11:45</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#046">Luk 11:46</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk011.htm#052">Luk 11:52</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#003">Luk 14:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#013">Tit 3:13</a>). Except in the last instance, "lawyer" is another name for "scribe." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#004">Mat 2:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#013">Tit 3:13</a>), the term has the modern meaning.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#041">Matthew 22:41</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_041">mat 22:41</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>asked them 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#024">Mat 21:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh019.htm#007">Joh 19:7</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Jesus' question is not personal but doctrinal: "Whose son is the Messiah?" 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#025">Act 2:25-36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#003">Rom 1:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#004">Rom 1:4</a>).
