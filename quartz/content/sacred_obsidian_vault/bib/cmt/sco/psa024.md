 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 24</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#003">Psalms 24:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">psa 24:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Who shall 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The order is: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) the declaration of title, "the earth is the Lord's" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001">Psa 24:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#002">Psa 24:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Who shall rule the earth? (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#003">Psa 24:3-6</a>). It is a question of worthiness, and no one is worthy but the Lamb. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#013">Dan 7:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#014">Dan 7:14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#003">Rev 5:3-10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Mat 25:31</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The King of glory takes the throne of earth (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#007">Psa 24:7-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Psalm 40, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
