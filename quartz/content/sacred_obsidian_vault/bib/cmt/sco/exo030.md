 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 30</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#001">Exodus 30:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">exo 30:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>incense 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Altar of incense, type of Christ our intercessor (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#001">Joh 17:1-26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#025">Heb 7:25</a>) through whom our own prayers and praises ascend to God; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#015">Heb 13:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#003">Rev 8:3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev008.htm#004">Rev 8:4</a>) and of the believer-priest's sacrifice of praise and worship (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#015">Heb 13:15</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#005">Exodus 30:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">exo 30:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>shittim 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>That is, acacia.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#009">Exodus 30:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">exo 30:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>strange 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#001">Lev 10:1-3</a>); two prohibitions are given concerning worship: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) No "strange" incense is to be offered. This speaks of simulated or purely formal worship. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) No "strange" fire was permitted. This refers to the excitation of "religious" feelings by merely sensuous means, and to the substitution for devotion to Christ of any other devotion, as to religious causes, or sects. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#011">Co1 1:11-13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#008">Col 2:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#016">Col 2:16-19</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#038">Exo 30:38</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#010">Exodus 30:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">exo 30:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>atonement 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#033">Exo 29:33</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#013">Exodus 30:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">exo 30:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>This they shall give 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo038.htm#025">Exo 38:25-27</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Silver thus becomes a type of redemption. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#019">Exo 26:19</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#018">Exodus 30:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">exo 30:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>laver 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Laver, type of Christ cleansing us from defilement, and from "every spot or wrinkle or any such thing" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#002">Joh 13:2-10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#025">Eph 5:25-27</a>). It is significant that the priests could not enter the holy place after serving at the brazen altar till hands and feet were cleansed.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#031">Exodus 30:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">exo 30:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>oil 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Anointing oil, type of the Holy Spirit for service (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#008">Act 1:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#034">Exodus 30:34</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_034">exo 30:34</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>frankincense 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Frankincense is not to be confounded with incense (to which it was to be added), as it is often used apart from incense. We are told what composed the incense -- never in Scripture what the frankincense was. All speaks of Christ -- the sweet spices of those perfections which we may apprehend, the frankincense of that which God saw in Jesus ineffable.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo030.htm#038">Exodus 30:38</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_038">exo 30:38</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>smell 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>What is condemned here is making worship a mere pleasure to the natural man, whether sensuous, as in beautiful music to please the ear, or eloquence, merely to give delight to the natural mind. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#023">Joh 4:23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#024">Joh 4:24</a>).
