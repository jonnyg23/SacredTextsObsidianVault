 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#001">Acts Chapter 20</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#006">Acts 20:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">act 20:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>we 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>From the use of the pronoun, Luke here rejoins the apostle.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#007">Acts 20:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">act 20:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>first day 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>It was the breaking of bread for which the disciples were assembled. The passage indicates the use by the apostolic churches of the first day, not the seventh. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#002">Co1 16:2</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#022">Acts 20:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">act 20:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>spirit 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Compare (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act021.htm#004">Act 21:4</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#022">Act 20:22</a>) Paul's own spirit 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#023">Th1 5:23</a>) 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>is meant; in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act021.htm#004">Act 21:4</a>), the Holy Spirit is meant. Paul's motive in going to Jerusalem seems to have been his great affection for the Jews (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#001">Rom 9:1-5</a>) and his hope that gifts of the Gentile churches, sent by him to the poor saints at Jerusalem (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#025">Rom 15:25-28</a>) would open the hearts of the law-bound Jewish believers to the "gospel of the grace of God" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#024">Act 20:24</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#024">Acts 20:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">act 20:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>neither 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Or, I hold not my life of any account, as unto myself, in comparison with accomplishing my course. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#026">Co1 9:26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#013">Phi 3:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#014">Phi 3:14</a>). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#007">Ti2 4:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#008">Ti2 4:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#029">Acts 20:29</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_029">act 20:29</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>wolves 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The two sources of the apostasy: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>false teachers from without, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#013">Co2 11:13-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001">Pe2 2:1-3</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>ambitious leaders from within, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#009">Jo3 1:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#010">Jo3 1:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#006">Rev 2:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#015">Rev 2:15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Also, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#020">Ti1 1:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#019">Jo1 2:19</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#035">Acts 20:35</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_035">act 20:35</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>It is more 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#012">Luk 14:12</a>).
