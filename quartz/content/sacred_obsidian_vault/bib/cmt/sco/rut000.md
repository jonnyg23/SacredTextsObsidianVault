 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rut001.htm#001">Ruth</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">rut 0:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Book Introduction - Ruth
 </p><p>This lovely story should be read in connection with the first half of Judges, as it presents a picture of life in Israel at that time. 
 </p><p>Typically, the book may be taken as a foreview of the church (Ruth), as the Gentile bride of Christ, the Bethlehemite who is able to redeem. Ruth also gives a normal Christian experience: 
 </p><p>1. Ruth deciding (Ruth 1). 
 </p><p>2. Ruth serving (Ruth 2). 
 </p><p>3. Ruth resting (Ruth 3). 
 </p><p>4. Ruth rewarded (Ruth 4). 
 </p><p>The events recorded in Ruth cover a period of 10 years (Ussher).
