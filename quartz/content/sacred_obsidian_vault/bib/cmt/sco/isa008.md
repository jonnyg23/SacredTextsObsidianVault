 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 8</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#012">Isaiah 8:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">isa 8:12</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>confederacy 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The reference is to the attempt to terrify Judah by the confederacy between Syria and Samaria. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#001">Isa 7:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#002">Isa 7:2</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa008.htm#018">Isaiah 8:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">isa 8:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>signs 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The primary application here is to the two sons of Isaiah, "Maher-shalal-hash-baz", means "haste ye, haste ye to the spoil," a "sign" of the coming judgment of the captivity of Judah; "Shear-jashub", means "a remnant shall return," a "sign" of the return of a remnant of Judah at the end of the seventy years, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#011">Jer 25:11</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#012">Jer 25:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#002">Dan 9:2</a>). The larger and final reference is to our Lord (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#013">Heb 2:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#014">Heb 2:14</a>).
