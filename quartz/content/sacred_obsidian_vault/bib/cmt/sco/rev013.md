 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#001">Revelation Chapter 13</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#001">Revelation 13:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">rev 13:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>rise up 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Daniel's fourth beast. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#026">Dan 7:26</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "ten horns" are explained in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#024">Dan 7:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev017.htm#012">Rev 17:12</a>) to be ten kings, and the whole vision is of the last form of Gentile world-power, a confederated ten-kingdom empire covering the sphere of authority of ancient Rome (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#001">Rev 13:1-3</a>) refers to the ten-kingdom empire; versus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#004">Rev 13:4-10</a>) to the emperor, who is emphatically "the Beast." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#002">Revelation 13:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">rev 13:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>like unto a leopard 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The three animals, leopard, bear, and lion, are found in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#004">Dan 7:4-6</a>) as symbols of the empires which preceded Rome, and whose characteristics all entered into the qualities of the Roman empire: Macedonian swiftness of conquest, Persian tenacity of purpose, Babylonish voracity.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#003">Revelation 13:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">rev 13:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>wounded to death 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Fragments of the ancient Roman empire have never ceased to exist as separate kingdoms. It was the imperial form of government which ceased; the one head wounded to death. What we have prophetically in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#003">Rev 13:3</a>) is the restoration of the imperial form as such, though over a federated empire of ten kingdoms; the "head" is "healed," that is, Restored; there is an emperor again -- the Beast. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(Greek, "gē", means "earth"). (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#007">Joh 7:7</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#008">Rev 13:8</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#008">Revelation 13:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">rev 13:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>world 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Kosmos, Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>In the sense of the present world-system, the ethically bad sense of the word, refers to the "order," "arrangement," under which Satan has organized the world of unbelieving mankind upon his cosmic principle of force, greed, selfishness, ambition, and pleasure. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#008">Mat 4:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#009">Mat 4:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#031">Joh 12:31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#030">Joh 14:30</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#036">Joh 18:36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#002">Eph 2:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#012">Eph 6:12</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#015">Jo1 2:15-17</a>). This world-system is imposing and powerful with armies and fleets; is often outwardly religious, scientific, cultured, and elegant; but, seething with national and commercial rivalries and ambitions, is upheld in any real crisis only by armed force, and is dominated by Satanic principles.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#016">Revelation 13:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">rev 13:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>he causeth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Antichrist; the person is to be distinguished from the "many antichrists" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#018">Jo1 2:18</a>) and the "spirit of antichrist" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#003">Jo1 4:3</a>) which characterizes all. The supreme mark of all is the denial of the Christian truth of the incarnation of the Logos, the eternal Son in Jesus as the Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001">Joh 1:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#014">Joh 1:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#016">Mat 1:16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The "many antichrists" precede and prepare the way for the Antichrist, who is "the Beast out of the earth" of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#011">Rev 13:11-17</a>) and the "false prophet" of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#013">Rev 16:13</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#010">Rev 20:10</a>). He is the last ecclesiastical head, as the Beast of (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#001">Rev 13:1-8</a>) is the last civil head. For purposes of persecution he is permitted to exercise the autocratic power of the emperor-Beast. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020">Rev 19:20</a>).
