 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 65</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#017">Isaiah 65:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">isa 65:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>behold 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Verse 17 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#017">Isa 65:17</a>) looks beyond the kingdom-age to the new heavens and the new earth (see refs. At "create"), but verses 18-25 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#018">Isa 65:18-25</a>) describe the kingdom-age itself. Longevity is restored, but death, the "last enemy" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#026">Co1 15:26</a>) is not destroyed till after Satan's rebellion at the end of the thousand years. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#007">Rev 20:7-14</a>).
