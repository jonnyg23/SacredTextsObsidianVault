 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001">2 John Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#005">2 John 1:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">jo2 1:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>that we love one another 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Law (of Christ), Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The new "law of Christ" is the divine love, as wrought into the renewed heart by the Holy Spirit (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#005">Rom 5:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#016">Heb 10:16</a>) and outflowing in the energy of the Spirit, unforced and spontaneous, toward the objects of the divine love; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#014">Co2 5:14-20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1002.htm#007">Th1 2:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1002.htm#008">Th1 2:8</a>). It is, therefore, " the law of liberty"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#025">Jam 1:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#012">Jam 2:12</a>) in contrast with the external law of Moses. Moses' law demands love, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev019.htm#018">Lev 19:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu006.htm#005">Deu 6:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#027">Luk 10:27</a>) Christ's law is love; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#005">Rom 5:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#007">Jo1 4:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#019">Jo1 4:19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#020">Jo1 4:20</a>) and so takes the place of the external law by fulfilling it; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#010">Rom 13:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#014">Gal 5:14</a>). It is the "law written in the heart" under the New Covenant. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb008.htm#008">Heb 8:8</a>).
