 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 40</a></h3> 
 <p>
 </p><p>I waited 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The 40th Psalm speaks of Messiah, Jehovah's Servant, obedient unto death. The Psalm begins with the joy of Christ in resurrection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#001">Psa 40:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#002">Psa 40:2</a>). He has been in the horrible pit of the grave, but has been brought up. Verses 3-5 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#003">Psa 40:3-5</a>) are His resurrection testimony, His "new song." Verses 6 and 7 (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#006">Psa 40:6-7</a>) are retrospective. When sacrifice and offering had become abominable because of the wickedness of the people (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#010">Isa 1:10-15</a>) then the obedient Servant came to make the pure offering; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#007">Psa 40:7-17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#005">Heb 10:5-17</a>). See Psalm 41, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
