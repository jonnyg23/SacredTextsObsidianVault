 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#001">Nehemiah Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#001">Nehemiah 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">neh 2:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Nisan 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>first monththat is, April.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#010">Nehemiah 2:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">neh 2:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Tobiah 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Two Tobiahs are distinguished by many: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) "Tobiah the servant, the Ammonite," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#010">Neh 2:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#019">Neh 2:19</a>); Heh 4:3,7 6:1,12,14. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) A Jew, unable to prove his genealogy. But the reference to the latter (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh007.htm#062">Neh 7:62</a>) indicates that he was already dead. But one Tobiah, and he the Ammonite, is active in this book.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#019">Nehemiah 2:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">neh 2:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>laughed 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The obstacle of ridicule.
