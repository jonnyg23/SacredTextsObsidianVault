 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#001">Matthew Chapter 25</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#001">Matthew 25:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">mat 25:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>shall 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This part of the Olivet discourse goes beyond the "sign" questions of the disciples (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#003">Mat 24:3</a>) and presents our Lord's return in three aspects: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) As testing profession, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#001">Mat 25:1-13</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) as testing service, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#014">Mat 25:14-30</a>); 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) as testing the Gentile nations, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Mat 25:31-46</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>virgins 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The kingdom of heaven here is the sphere of profession, as in Matthew 13. All alike have lamps, but two facts fix the real status of the foolish virgins: They "took no oil," and the Lord said, "I know you not." Oil is the symbol of the Holy Spirit, and "If any man have not the spirit of Christ, he is none of his" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#009">Rom 8:9</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Nor could the Lord say to any believer, however unspiritual, "I know you not." 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>heaven 
 </p><p>

 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#021">Matthew 25:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">mat 25:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Well done 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Lord's commendation may be earned by the weakest of His servants; it is given for faithful service.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#023">Matthew 25:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">mat 25:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Well done 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The same commendation is gained by the servant with two talents as by him with five; he was equally faithful though his gift was less.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Matthew 25:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">mat 25:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Son of man 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#020">Mat 8:20</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>angels 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#004">Heb 1:4</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#032">Matthew 25:32</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_032">mat 25:32</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And before him 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>This judgment is to be distinguished from the judgment of the great white throne. Here there is no resurrection; the persons judged are living nations; no books are opened; three classes are present, sheep, goats, and brethren; the time is at the return of Christ (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031">Mat 25:31</a>); and the scene is on the earth. All these particulars are in contrast with (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#011">Rev 20:11-15</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The test in this judgment is the treatment accorded by the nations to those whom Christ here call "my brethren." These "brethren" are the Jewish Remnant who will have preached the Gospel of the kingdom to all nations during the tribulation. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Remnant" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009">Isa 1:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005">Rom 11:5</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The test in (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#011">Rev 20:11-15</a>) is the possession of eternal life.
