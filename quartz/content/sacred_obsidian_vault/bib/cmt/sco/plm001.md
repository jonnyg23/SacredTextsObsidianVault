 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#001">Philemon Chapter 1</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#018">Philemon 1:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">plm 1:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>account 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(<a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#017">Plm 1:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#018">Plm 1:18</a>) perfectly illustrate imputation: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"Receive him as myself" -- reckon to him my merit; 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"If he hath wronged thee or oweth thee ought, put that on mine account" -- reckon to me his demerit. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See "Imputation," (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#050">Lev 25:50</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#023">Jam 2:23</a>).
