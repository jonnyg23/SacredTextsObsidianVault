 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#001">Exodus Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#002">Exodus 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">exo 2:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>son 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Moses, type of Christ the Deliverer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#001">Isa 61:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#018">Luk 4:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#010">Co2 1:10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#010">Th1 1:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) A divinely chosen deliverer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo003.htm#007">Exo 3:7-10</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#025">Act 7:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#016">Joh 3:16</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Rejected by Israel he turns to the Gentiles (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#011">Exo 2:11-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#025">Act 7:25</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act018.htm#005">Act 18:5-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#017">Act 28:17-28</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) During his rejection he gains a Gentile bride (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#016">Exo 2:16-21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#014">Mat 12:14-21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#002">Co2 11:2</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#030">Eph 5:30-32</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Afterward he again appears as Israel's deliverer, and is accepted (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#029">Exo 4:29-31</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#014">Rom 11:14-26</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014">Act 15:14-17</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(5) Officially, Moses typifies Christ as Prophet (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#022">Act 3:22</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act003.htm#023">Act 3:23</a>). Advocate; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo032.htm#031">Exo 32:31-35</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#001">Jo1 2:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#002">Jo1 2:2</a>); Intercessor; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo017.htm#001">Exo 17:1-6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#025">Heb 7:25</a>) and Leader, or King; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#004">Deu 33:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#005">Deu 33:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#004">Isa 55:4</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb002.htm#010">Heb 2:10</a>) while, in relation to the house of God, he is in contrast with Christ. Moses was faithful as a servant over another's house; Christ as a Son over His own house (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#005">Heb 3:5</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb003.htm#006">Heb 3:6</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo002.htm#018">Exodus 2:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">exo 2:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Reuel 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Called Raguel. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/num010.htm#029">Num 10:29</a>).
