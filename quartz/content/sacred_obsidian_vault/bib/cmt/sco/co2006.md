 </p><p>come out from among them 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Separation, Summary: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Separation in Scripture is twofold: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"from" whatever is contrary to the mind of God; and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>"unto" God Himself. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The underlying principle is that in a moral universe it is impossible for God to fully bless and use His children who are in compromise or complicity with evil. The unequal yoke is anything which unites a child of God and an unbeliever in a common purpose. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/deu022.htm#010">Deu 22:10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Separation from evil implies 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) separation in desire, motive, and act, from the world, in the ethically bad sense of this present world-system. 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#008">Rev 13:8</a>); and 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) separation from believers, especially false teachers, who are "vessels unto dishonour". (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#020">Ti2 2:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#021">Ti2 2:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#009">Jo2 1:9-11</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) Separation is not from contact with evil in the world or the church, but from complicity with and conformity to. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#015">Joh 17:15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014">Co2 6:14-18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#001">Gal 6:1</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) The reward of separation is the full manifestation of the divine fatherhood, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#017">Co2 6:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#018">Co2 6:18</a>), unhindered communion and worship, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#013">Heb 13:13-15</a>), and fruitful service, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#021">Ti2 2:21</a>), as world-conformity involves the loss of these, though not of salvation. Here, as in all else, Christ is the model. He was "holy, harmless, undefiled, and separate from sinners", (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#026">Heb 7:26</a>), and yet in such contact with them for their salvation that the Pharisees, who illustrate the mechanical and ascetic conception of separation, 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(See Scofield) - (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#007">Mat 3:7</a>), 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>judged Him as having lost His Nazarite character. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#039">Luk 7:39</a>) Cf; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#019">Co1 9:19-23</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#027">Co1 10:27</a>).
