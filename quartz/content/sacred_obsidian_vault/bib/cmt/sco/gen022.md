 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#001">Genesis Chapter 22</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#001">Genesis 22:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">gen 22:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>And it came to pass 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The spiritual experience of Abraham was marked by four great crises, each of which involved a surrender of something naturally most dear. These were: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Country and kindred (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#001">Gen 12:1</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#034">Mat 10:34-39</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014">Co2 6:14-18</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) His nephew, Lot; especially dear to Abraham by nature, as a possible heir and as a fellow believer (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#007">Pe2 2:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#008">Pe2 2:8</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen013.htm#001">Gen 13:1-18</a>). The completeness of Abraham's separation from one who, though a believer, was a "vessel unto dishonour," is shown by; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#001">Gen 15:1-3</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#020">Ti2 2:20</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#021">Ti2 2:21</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#036">Act 15:36-40</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) His own plan about Ishmael (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#017">Gen 17:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#018">Gen 17:18</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1013.htm#001">Ch1 13:1-14</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#001">Ch1 15:1-2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Isaac, "thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#001">Gen 22:1-19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#017">Heb 11:17</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#018">Heb 11:18</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#009">Genesis 22:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">gen 22:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>laid 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The typical lessons here are: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) Isaac, type of Christ "obedient unto death" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#005">Phi 2:5-8</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Abraham, type of the Father, who "spared not His own son, but delivered Him up for us all" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#016">Joh 3:16</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#032">Rom 8:32</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) the ram, type of substitution -- Christ offered as a burnt-offering in our stead, (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#005">Heb 10:5-10</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) Compare resurrection (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#017">Heb 11:17-19</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#021">Jam 2:21-23</a>).  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#011">Genesis 22:11</a></p>
