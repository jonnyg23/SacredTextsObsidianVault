 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>The Scofield Bible Commentary</i>, by Cyrus Ingerson Scofield, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 89</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#001">Psalms 89:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 89:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>Maschil 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>Maschil, instruction.  
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#027">Psalms 89:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">psa 89:27</a></font></span></p>
 <p>
 </p><p>higher than the kings of the earth 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The eighty-ninth Psalm is at once the confirmation and exposition of the Davidic Covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#009">Sa2 7:9-14</a>). That the covenant itself looks far beyond David and Solomon is sure from (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#027">Psa 89:27</a>). "Higher than the kings of the earth" can only refer to Immanuel. (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#013">Isa 7:13-15</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#006">Isa 9:6</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#007">Isa 9:7</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#002">Mic 5:2</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>The Psalm is in four parts: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(1) The covenant, though springing from the lovingkindness of Jehovah, yet rests upon His oath (vs. 1-4). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(2) Jehovah is glorified for His power and goodness in connection with the covenant (vs 5-18). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(3) The response of Jehovah (vs. 19-37). This is in two parts: 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (a) it confirms the covenant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#019">Psa 89:19-29</a>), but 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>
 </p><p>    (b) warns that disobedience in the royal posterity of David will be punished with chastening (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#030">Psa 89:30-32</a>). Historically this chastening began in the division of the Davidic kingdom; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1011.htm#026">Kg1 11:26-36</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1012.htm#016">Kg1 12:16-20</a>) and culminated in the captivities and that subordination of Israel to the Gentiles which still continues. See "Gentiles, times of"; (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024">Luk 21:24</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014">Rev 16:14</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>(4) The plea of the Remnant (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009">Isa 1:9</a>); (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005">Rom 11:5</a>) who urge the severity and long continuance of the chastening (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#038">Psa 89:38-52</a>). 
 </p><p>
 </p><p>See Psalm 102, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
