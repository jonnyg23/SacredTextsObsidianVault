 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Commentary on the Bible</i>, by Adam Clarke, [1831], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 19</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001">1 Chronicles</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_000">ch1 19:0</a></font></span></p>
 <p>David sends a congratulatory message to Hanun, king of Ammon, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#001">Ch1 19:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#002">Ch1 19:2</a>. He treats the messengers with great incivility, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#003">Ch1 19:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#004">Ch1 19:4</a>. David is exasperated, but condoles with the degraded messengers, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#005">Ch1 19:5</a>. The Ammonites prepare for war, and hire thirty-two thousand chariots, and besiege Medeba, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#006">Ch1 19:6</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#007">Ch1 19:7</a>. David sends Joab to attack them; he defeats the Syrians and Ammonites, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#008">Ch1 19:8-15</a>. The discomfited Syrians recruit their army, and invade David's territories beyond Jordan; he attacks them, kills Shophach their general, seven thousand charioteers, and forty thousand of their infantry, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#016">Ch1 19:16-18</a>. The Syrians abandon the Ammonites and make a separate peace with David, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#019">Ch1 19:19</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#001">1 Chronicles 19:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 19:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Now it came to pass - See the same history, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#001">Sa2 10:1</a> (note), etc., and the notes there. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#004">1 Chronicles 19:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">ch1 19:4</a></font></span></p>
 <p>And cut off their garments in the midst - Usque ad eorum, pudenda. So the Targum, Jarchi, and others; leaving exposed what nature and decency require to be concealed. See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#004">Sa2 10:4</a> (note). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#006">1 Chronicles 19:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ch1 19:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Chariots and horsemen out of Mesopotamia - These are not mentioned in the parallel place in Samuel; probably they did not arrive till the Ammonites and their other allies were defeated by the Israelites in the first battle. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#007">1 Chronicles 19:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">ch1 19:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Thirty and two thousand - The whole number mentioned in Samuel is, Syrians, of Beth-rehob, and of Zoba, twenty thousand; of King Maacah, one thousand; of Ish-tob, twelve thousand; in all thirty-three thousand. Of chariots or cavalry there is no mention. These could not have been the whole army. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#013">1 Chronicles 19:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">ch1 19:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Be of good courage - See the note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#012">Sa2 10:12</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#018">1 Chronicles 19:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">ch1 19:18</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Forty thousand footmen - See this number accounted for in the note on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#018">Sa2 10:18</a> (note). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1019.htm#019">1 Chronicles 19:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">ch1 19:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>They made peace with David, and became his servants - See on <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#019">Sa2 10:19</a> (note), and the concluding note in that place; and see for omissions in Chronicles, the preface to these books.
