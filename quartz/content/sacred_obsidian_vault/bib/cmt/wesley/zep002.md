 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#001">Zephaniah Chapter 2</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#001">Zephaniah 2:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">zep 2:1</a></font></span></p>Gather yourselves - Call a solemn assembly, proclaim a fast. Not desired - Or, not desirous. Unwilling to return, and unworthy to be received on your return. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#002">Zephaniah 2:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">zep 2:2</a></font></span></p>The decree - Before God's decree is put in execution. The day - Before the day of your calamities. As the chaff - Carry you away as the wind carries chaff away. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#003">Zephaniah 2:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">zep 2:3</a></font></span></p>Seek - Fear, worship, depend on him alone. Ye meek - Ye humble ones. Wrought his judgment - Obeyed his precepts. Seek righteousness - Continue therein. Seek meekness - Patiently wait on the just and merciful God. Hid - Under the wing of Divine Providence. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#004">Zephaniah 2:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">zep 2:4</a></font></span></p>For - It is time to seek God; for your neighbours, as well as you, shall be destroyed. Gaza - A chief city of the Philistines. They - The Babylonians. Shall drive - Into captivity. At the noon day - It shall be taken by force at noon. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#005">Zephaniah 2:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">zep 2:5</a></font></span></p>The inhabitants - All the Philistines. Cherethites - Or destroyers, men that were stout, fierce, and terrible to their neighbours. O Canaan - That part that the Philistines kept by force from the Jews. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#006">Zephaniah 2:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">zep 2:6</a></font></span></p>For shepherds - Instead of cities full of rich citizens, there shall be only cottages for shepherds. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#007">Zephaniah 2:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">zep 2:7</a></font></span></p>The coast - The sea - coast, the land of the Philistines. The remnant - That survive the captivity. Shall feed - Their flocks. In the houses - In places where these formerly stood. They - Both shepherds and flocks. Shall visit - In mercy. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#008">Zephaniah 2:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">zep 2:8</a></font></span></p>I - God. Magnified themselves - Invading their frontiers. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#009">Zephaniah 2:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">zep 2:9</a></font></span></p>Of nettles - Not cultivated, but over - run with nettles. Salt - pits - A dry, barren earth, fit only to dig salt out of. The residue - That return out of Babylon. Possess them - Settle upon those parts of their lands, that are fit for habitation. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#011">Zephaniah 2:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">zep 2:11</a></font></span></p>Famish - Take away all their sacrifices and drink - offerings. The gods - Idols of those lands. From his place - Not only at Jerusalem, but every where. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#012">Zephaniah 2:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">zep 2:12</a></font></span></p>By my sword - The Chaldeans are called God's sword; because God employed them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#013">Zephaniah 2:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">zep 2:13</a></font></span></p>He - God. The north - Assyria, which lay northward of Judea, and due north from Babylon. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#014">Zephaniah 2:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">zep 2:14</a></font></span></p>All the beasts - All sorts of beasts which are found in those countries. The bittern - A bird that delights in desolate places. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/zep002.htm#015">Zephaniah 2:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">zep 2:15</a></font></span></p>This - So the prophet triumphs over her. There is none - None like me, or that can contend with me.
