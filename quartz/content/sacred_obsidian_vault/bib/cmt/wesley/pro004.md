 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001">Proverbs Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001">Proverbs 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">pro 4:1</a></font></span></p>A father - Of me, who have paternal authority over you and affection for you. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#003">Proverbs 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">pro 4:3</a></font></span></p>Tender - Young and tender in years, and tenderly educated. Only beloved - Beloved above all the rest. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#004">Proverbs 4:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">pro 4:4</a></font></span></p>Said - The following verses, at least as far as the tenth verse, are the words of David. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#007">Proverbs 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">pro 4:7</a></font></span></p>With all - Even with the price of all. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#008">Proverbs 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">pro 4:8</a></font></span></p>Exalt - Let her have thine highest esteem and affection. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#009">Proverbs 4:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">pro 4:9</a></font></span></p>Grace - A beautiful ornament, such as they used to put upon their heads. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#016">Proverbs 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">pro 4:16</a></font></span></p>For - They cannot sleep with quietness. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#017">Proverbs 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">pro 4:17</a></font></span></p>For - Wickedness is as pleasant to them as their bread. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#018">Proverbs 4:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">pro 4:18</a></font></span></p>But - Just men daily grow in knowledge, and grace, and consolation, 'till all be perfected and swallowed up in glory. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#019">Proverbs 4:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">pro 4:19</a></font></span></p>Darkness - Full of ignorance and error, of uncertainty and confusion, of danger and misery. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#023">Proverbs 4:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">pro 4:23</a></font></span></p>Heart - Thy thoughts, will, and affections. For - From thence proceed all the actions, as of the natural, so of the spiritual life, which lead to eternal life. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#024">Proverbs 4:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">pro 4:24</a></font></span></p>Mouth - All sorts of sinful words. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#025">Proverbs 4:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">pro 4:25</a></font></span></p>Right on - Direct all thine actions to a right end, and keep thy mind fixed upon that way which leads to it, and neither look or turn aside to the right - hand or the left. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#026">Proverbs 4:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">pro 4:26</a></font></span></p>Ponder - Consider thine actions before thou dost them, and see that they agree with the rule. And - Let thine actions be uniformly and constantly good in spite of all temptations.
