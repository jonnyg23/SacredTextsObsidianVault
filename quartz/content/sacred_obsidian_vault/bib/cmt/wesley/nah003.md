 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#001">Nahum Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#001">Nahum 3:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">nah 3:1</a></font></span></p>The prey - Extortion and rapine. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#003">Nahum 3:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">nah 3:3</a></font></span></p>The horsemen - The Chaldeans and their confederates. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#004">Nahum 3:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">nah 3:4</a></font></span></p>The whoredom - The idolatries, which were multiplied by the many people that served the Assyrian idols. And whoredoms literally understood, did undoubtedly abound, where wealth, luxury, ease, and long continuance of these were to be found. Well - favoured - Glorious in their state and government, and in the splendor of their idols, temples, and sacrifices. Of witchcrafts - Bewitching policies; or it may be taken for witchcrafts or necromances, which abounded among the Assyrians. That selleth - That dispose of them as imperiously, and absolutely as men do slaves. And families - This may intimate the seducing of some particular and eminent families to an hereditary service of the Assyrian idols, or to witchcrafts, in which the devil imitated God's institution, in taking a family to his service. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#005">Nahum 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">nah 3:5</a></font></span></p>Discover - l will strip thee naked, and deal with thee as inhuman soldiers deal with captive women. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#007">Nahum 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">nah 3:7</a></font></span></p>Shall flee - With loathing and abhorrence. Will bemoan - Whose bowels will be moved for her that had no bowels for any one. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#008">Nahum 3:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">nah 3:8</a></font></span></p>Thou - O Nineveh. No - It is supposed this was what we now called Alexandria. Art thou greater, stronger, and wiser? Yet all her power was broken, her riches spoiled, and her glory buried in ruins. Rampart - The defence of its walls on one side. Her wall - A mighty, strong wall, built from the sea landward. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#009">Nahum 3:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">nah 3:9</a></font></span></p>Her strength - Furnishing soldiers and warlike assistance. It was infinite - There was no end to their confidence and warlike provisions. Put - Or the Moors, who lie westward of Alexandria. Lubim - The people that inhabited that which is now called Cyrene. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#011">Nahum 3:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">nah 3:11</a></font></span></p>Thou also - Thou shalt drink deep of the bitter cup of God's displeasure. Hid - Thou shalt hide thyself. O Nineveh, as well as Alexandria. Shalt seek - Shalt sue for, and intreat assistance. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#012">Nahum 3:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">nah 3:12</a></font></span></p>Ripe figs - Whose weight and ripeness will bring them quickly to the ground. Shaken - If but lightly touched. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#013">Nahum 3:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">nah 3:13</a></font></span></p>Are women - Were very cowards. The gates - The strong frontiers. Wide open - Either through fear or treachery. Thy bars - With which the gates were shut and strengthened. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#014">Nahum 3:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">nah 3:14</a></font></span></p>Draw thee waters - Fill all thy cisterns, and draw the waters into the ditches. Tread the mortar - Set thy brick - makers on work to prepare store of materials for thy fortifications. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#015">Nahum 3:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">nah 3:15</a></font></span></p>There - In the very fortresses. Eat thee - As easily as the canker - worm eats the green herb. Many - They are innumerable; be thou so if thou canst; all will be to no purpose. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#016">Nahum 3:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">nah 3:16</a></font></span></p>The canker - worm spoileth - So these are like the canker - worms, which spoil wherever they come, and when no more is to be gotten, flee away. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#017">Nahum 3:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">nah 3:17</a></font></span></p>Thy crowned - Thy confederate kings and princes. Captains - Commanders and officers are for number, like locusts and grasshoppers; but 'tis all for shew, not for help. In the cool day - While the season suits them. The sun - When trouble, war, and danger, like the parching sun, scald them. Is not known - Thou shalt never know where to find them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#018">Nahum 3:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">nah 3:18</a></font></span></p>Thy shepherds - Thy rulers and counsellors. Slumber - Are remiss, heartless, or dead. No man gathereth - No one will concern himself to preserve thy dispersed ones. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/nah003.htm#019">Nahum 3:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">nah 3:19</a></font></span></p>Shall clap the hands - Insulting and rejoicing. Thy wickedness - Thy tyranny, pride, oppression and cruelty; treading down and trampling upon them.
