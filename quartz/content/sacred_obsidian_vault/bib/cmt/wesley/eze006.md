 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#002">Ezekiel 6:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">eze 6:2</a></font></span></p>The mountains - The inhabitants of the mountains, who were secure in their fastnesses. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#003">Ezekiel 6:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 6:3</a></font></span></p>Rivers - To those who dwell by river sides, or in the valleys. High places - The places of your idolatrous worship. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#004">Ezekiel 6:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">eze 6:4</a></font></span></p>Cast down - Before the altars of your idols, which you fly to for refuge. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#005">Ezekiel 6:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">eze 6:5</a></font></span></p>And - Thus the idols were upbraided with their inability to help their worshippers, and the idolaters, with the folly of trusting in them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#006">Ezekiel 6:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 6:6</a></font></span></p>Your works - All your costly work for your idols. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#008">Ezekiel 6:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">eze 6:8</a></font></span></p>Remnant - It is the Lord that preserves a remnant, the enemies rage would destroy all. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#009">Ezekiel 6:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">eze 6:9</a></font></span></p>Shall remember - So as to turn unto me. Broken - I am much grieved. Whorish heart - Idolatrous hearts depart from God, as an adulterous wife departs from her husband. Loath - With a mixture of grief towards God, of indignation against themselves, and abhorrence of the offence. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#010">Ezekiel 6:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">eze 6:10</a></font></span></p>In vain - Either without cause, the sufferers gave him just cause to pronounce that evil; or without effect. Their sins where the cause, and their destruction is the effect of their sufferings. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#011">Ezekiel 6:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">eze 6:11</a></font></span></p>Smite - To shew thy wonder, indignation, sorrow, and pity, for their sins and sufferings. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#012">Ezekiel 6:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 6:12</a></font></span></p>Far off - Either by flight, or captivity. Shall fall - Who dwell near to Jerusalem, or would retire to it, when the Babylonians approach. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#014">Ezekiel 6:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 6:14</a></font></span></p>Wilderness - The horrid wilderness of Moab. Therein the fiery serpents so much annoyed Israel. Accordingly the land of Canaan is at this day one of the most desolate countries in the world.
