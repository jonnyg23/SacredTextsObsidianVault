 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#001">Isaiah 15:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 15:1</a></font></span></p>The burden - A prophecy of the destruction of the Moabites, the inveterate enemies of the Jews, begun by the Assyrian, and finished by the Babylonian emperors. In a night - Suddenly and unexpectedly. Ar - The chief city of Moab. Kir - Another eminent city of Moab. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#002">Isaiah 15:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 15:2</a></font></span></p>Bajith - Which signifies an house. It is supposed to be some eminent house or temple of their idols. Dibon - Another city of Moab. To weep - To offer their supplications with tears to their idols for help. Medeba - Two considerable cities, anciently belonging to the Moabites. Beard - The hair of their heads and beards was shaved, as was usual in great mournings. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#003">Isaiah 15:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">isa 15:3</a></font></span></p>On the tops - Which were made flat, to which men used to go up, to cry to God in heaven, or to men for help. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#004">Isaiah 15:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">isa 15:4</a></font></span></p>Heshbon - Two other Moabitish cities. Jahaz - Another city in the utmost borders of Moab. Soldiers - Who use to be the most courageous. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#005">Isaiah 15:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 15:5</a></font></span></p>Moab - Tho' they are a most vile nation. Zoar - Zoar was a town bordering upon Moab. Of destruction - Such a cry as men send forth when they are just falling into the pit of destruction. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#006">Isaiah 15:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 15:6</a></font></span></p>Waters - Watery grounds being very fruitful, are commonly most inhabited; but now they also, much more the dry and barren grounds, shall be desolate and without inhabitant. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#007">Isaiah 15:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 15:7</a></font></span></p>They - Their enemies. Brook - Possibly he means some such river which ran into Euphrates, and so gave them opportunity of carrying their spoils by water unto Babylon. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#008">Isaiah 15:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">isa 15:8</a></font></span></p>The cry - Their cry fills all the parts of the country. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa015.htm#009">Isaiah 15:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 15:9</a></font></span></p>More - More than hath been already mentioned. Lions - God shall send lions to find out those that escape the fury of men.
