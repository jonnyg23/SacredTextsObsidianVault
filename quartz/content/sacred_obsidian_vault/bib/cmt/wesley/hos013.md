 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#001">Hosea Chapter 13</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#001">Hosea 13:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">hos 13:1</a></font></span></p>Ephraim - The ten tribes, of which Ephraim was the chief. Spake trembling - Humbled himself before God. Exalted himself - The kingdom flourished. When he offended - So soon as they sinned, taking Baal to be their God. He died - They lost their power and glory. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#002">Hosea 13:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">hos 13:2</a></font></span></p>Of them - Of the idols. Let the man - Let all that bring their offerings to these idols, worship and adore, and shew they do so by kissing the calves. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#004">Hosea 13:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">hos 13:4</a></font></span></p>Thou shalt know - I forbad thee to know any other God but me, in gratitude thou shouldest know no other. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#005">Hosea 13:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">hos 13:5</a></font></span></p>I did know - Owned, took care of, guided and supplied. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#006">Hosea 13:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">hos 13:6</a></font></span></p>Their pasture - When they were come into Canaan, and had abundance of all things, they ran into luxury. Was exalted - They grew proud. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#007">Hosea 13:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">hos 13:7</a></font></span></p>Observe them - Watch for them, that I might be sure to take them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#008">Hosea 13:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">hos 13:8</a></font></span></p>Rent - First kill, then tear in pieces, and pull out the very heart. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#010">Hosea 13:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">hos 13:10</a></font></span></p>Thy king - I would have been thy king to govern and save thee, but thou refusedst me in both: yet I will be thy king to punish thee. Thy judges - Where are they now? And princes - Necessary to assist the king. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#011">Hosea 13:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">hos 13:11</a></font></span></p>A king - Such as Shallum, Menahem, Pekah. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#012">Hosea 13:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">hos 13:12</a></font></span></p>Is bound up - As sins unpardoned; for to loose sins is to forgive, and to bind sins is to charge them upon the sinner, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#019">Mat 16:19</a>. Hid - Not from God, but laid up with God against the day of recompense. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#013">Hosea 13:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">hos 13:13</a></font></span></p>The sorrows - The punishment of his sins will overtake him suddenly, with great anguish. An unwise son - A foolish son, who endangers himself and his mother. He should not stay - As a child that sticks in the birth, so is Ephraim, one while will, another while will not return to God; and thus dies under the delay. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#014">Hosea 13:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">hos 13:14</a></font></span></p>Ransom - By power and purchase, by the blood of the lamb of God, and by the power of his Godhead. Them - That repent and believe. From the grave - He conquered the grave, and will at the great day of the resurrection open those prison - doors, and bring us out in glory. From death - From the curse of the first death, and from the second death, which shall have no power over us. Thy plagues - Thus I will destroy death. I will pull down those prison - walls, and bring out all that are confined therein, the bad of whom I will remove into other prisons, the good I will restore to glorious liberty. Repentance shall be hid - I will never, as a man that repenteths, change my word and purpose, saith the Lord. What a glorious promise is this, which is interposed in the midst of all these judgments! 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/hos013.htm#015">Hosea 13:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">hos 13:15</a></font></span></p>He - Ephraim. His brethren - Either the rest of the tribes, or the nations who by league are become as his brethren. An east - wind - An enemy as pernicious to his estate as the east - wind is to fruits. Of the Lord - A mighty enemy, called here the wind of the Lord, the usual superlative in Hebrew. The wilderness - Which lay south - east from Canaan. The south - east winds in that country were of all, most hot and blasting. He - The Assyrian army. Shall spoil - Shall carry away all desirable vessels and furniture.
