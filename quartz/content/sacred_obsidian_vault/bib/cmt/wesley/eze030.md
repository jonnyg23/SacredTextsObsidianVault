 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 30</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#002">Ezekiel 30:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">eze 30:2</a></font></span></p>Ye - Inhabitants of Egypt. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#003">Ezekiel 30:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 30:3</a></font></span></p>A cloudy day - So times of trouble are called. Of the heathen - The time when God will reckon with the Heathens. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#004">Ezekiel 30:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">eze 30:4</a></font></span></p>Ethiopia - The neighbour and ally to Egypt. Take away - Into miserable captivity. Her foundations - Their government, laws, and strong holds. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#005">Ezekiel 30:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">eze 30:5</a></font></span></p>Lydia - Not the Asiatic, but the Africans placed between some part of Cyrene and Egypt. The mingled people - The hired soldiers from all parts, a confused mixture of nations. And Chub - The inhabitants of the inmost Libya; perhaps they may be the Nubians at this day. The men - All the allies of Egypt. With them - With the Egyptians. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#006">Ezekiel 30:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 30:6</a></font></span></p>Upheld - Those that favour and help her. The pride - The glory of all her strength. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#007">Ezekiel 30:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">eze 30:7</a></font></span></p>They - All those before mentioned. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#008">Ezekiel 30:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">eze 30:8</a></font></span></p>Destroyed - The fire that consumes nations is of God's kindling: and when he sets fire to a kingdom, all they that go about to quench the fire, shall be consumed by it. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#009">Ezekiel 30:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">eze 30:9</a></font></span></p>Messengers - Such as having escaped the sword, shall tell the news. From me - By my permission and providence. In ship's - Messengers by ships might carry the news to both the Ethiopian, Asian, and African, by the Red - sea. As in the day - During the mighty havock made by the Chaldeans. It - A like storm. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#011">Ezekiel 30:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">eze 30:11</a></font></span></p>His people - His own subjects, not hired soldiers. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#012">Ezekiel 30:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 30:12</a></font></span></p>The rivers dry - Probably the Chaldeans diverted them, and so their fortified towns wanted one great defence. Sell - Give it up entirely. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#013">Ezekiel 30:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">eze 30:13</a></font></span></p>Noph - Memphis, now Grand Cairo, the chief city of the country. A prince - Either an Egyptian born, or independent, and over all Egypt. A fear - Consternation and cowardice. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#014">Ezekiel 30:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 30:14</a></font></span></p>In Zoan - Zoan shall be burnt down to ashes. In No - A great and populous city situate on one of the mouths of the Nile. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#015">Ezekiel 30:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">eze 30:15</a></font></span></p>Sin - Pelusium, which was the key of Egypt, and therefore always well fortified, and strongly garrisoned. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#016">Ezekiel 30:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">eze 30:16</a></font></span></p>Shall be rent - Her walls, and towers, and fortresses broken through by the violence of engines, and by the assaults of the soldiers. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#017">Ezekiel 30:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">eze 30:17</a></font></span></p>Young men - 'Tis probable these might be a body of valiant youths, collected out of these ten cities. Aven - Bethshemesh, or Heliopolis, an idolatrous city, in which was a stately temple of the sun: an hundred and fifty furlongs, that is six miles and three quarters in compass. Phibeseth - Bubastus, sometimes called Hoephestus, not far from Aven. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#018">Ezekiel 30:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">eze 30:18</a></font></span></p>Tehaphnehes - A great and goodly city of Egypt; Tachapanes, Tachpanes, Tahapanes, Tahpanes, Chanes, and Hanes, are names given it, and this from a queen of Egypt of that name in Solomon's time. It stood not far from Sin, or Pelusium. Darkened - A night shall come upon it. Break - I shall break the kingdom of Egypt, that it no more oppress with yokes, that is, burdens. Her daughters - Her towns and villages. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#020">Ezekiel 30:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">eze 30:20</a></font></span></p>The eleventh year - Of Jeconiah's captivity, three months and two days before Jerusalem was taken, about the time that the Egyptians attempted to raise the siege of Jerusalem. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#021">Ezekiel 30:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">eze 30:21</a></font></span></p>Have broken - Partly by the victory of the Chaldeans over Pharaoh - necho, partly by the victory of the Cyreneans over Pharaoh - hophra. The sword - None can heal the wounds that God gives but himself. They whom he disables, cannot again hold the sword. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#022">Ezekiel 30:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">eze 30:22</a></font></span></p>His arms - Both his arms. The strong - That part of his kingdom which remains entire. Broken - That which was shattered before. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze030.htm#025">Ezekiel 30:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">eze 30:25</a></font></span></p>Will strengthen - As judges on the bench like Pilate, so generals in the field, like Nebuchadrezzar, have no power but what is given them from above.
