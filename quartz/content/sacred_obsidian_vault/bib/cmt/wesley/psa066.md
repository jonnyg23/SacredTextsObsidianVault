 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#001">Psalms Chapter 66</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#001">Psalms 66:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">psa 66:1</a></font></span></p>All lands - Ye people of all nations. He invites the Gentile world, to the contemplation and celebration of God's works. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#006">Psalms 66:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">psa 66:6</a></font></span></p>We - Our nation, or our ancestors, in whose loins we then were. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#010">Psalms 66:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">psa 66:10</a></font></span></p>Proved us - As it were in a burning furnace; and with a design to purge out our dross. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#011">Psalms 66:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">psa 66:11</a></font></span></p>Net - Which our enemies laid for us. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#012">Psalms 66:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">psa 66:12</a></font></span></p>To ride - To use us like slaves. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#015">Psalms 66:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">psa 66:15</a></font></span></p>I will go - One speaks in the name of all the rest. Incense - With the fat of rams, which is no less pleasing to God than incense. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#018">Psalms 66:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">psa 66:18</a></font></span></p>Iniquity - Any sin. In heart - If my heart had been false to God, although I might have forborne outward acts. If I had been guilty of that, by heart was set upon sin, or I desired only that which I resolved in my heart to spend upon my lusts.
