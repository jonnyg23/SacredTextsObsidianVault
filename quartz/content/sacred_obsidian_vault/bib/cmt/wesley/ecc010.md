 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#001">Ecclesiastes Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#002">Ecclesiastes 10:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">ecc 10:2</a></font></span></p>Heart - His understanding is always present with him and ready to direct him. He mentions the right hand, because that is the common instrument of action. A fool's - His understanding is not effectual to govern his affections and actions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#003">Ecclesiastes 10:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">ecc 10:3</a></font></span></p>Walketh - In his daily conversation. He saith - He discovers his folly to all that meet him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#004">Ecclesiastes 10:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">ecc 10:4</a></font></span></p>The spirit - The passion. Leave not - In anger or discontent. Continue in a diligent and faithful discharge of thy duty, and modestly and humbly submit to him. Yielding - A gentle and submissive carriage. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#006">Ecclesiastes 10:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ecc 10:6</a></font></span></p>The rich - Wise and worthy men, rich in endowments of mind. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#008">Ecclesiastes 10:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">ecc 10:8</a></font></span></p>An hedge - Whereby another man's fields or vineyards are distinguished, that he may either take away their fruits, or enlarge his own fields. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#009">Ecclesiastes 10:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">ecc 10:9</a></font></span></p>Whoso removeth - Stones too heavy for them: who rashly attempts things too high and hard for them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#010">Ecclesiastes 10:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">ecc 10:10</a></font></span></p>Wisdom - As wisdom instructs a man in the smallest matters, so it is useful for a man's direction in all weighty affairs. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#011">Ecclesiastes 10:11</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_011">ecc 10:11</a></font></span></p>Without - If not prevented by the art and care of the charmer; which practice he does not justify, but only mentions by way of resemblance. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#012">Ecclesiastes 10:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">ecc 10:12</a></font></span></p>Gracious - Procure him favour with those who hear him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#014">Ecclesiastes 10:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">ecc 10:14</a></font></span></p>Full of words - Forward to promise and boast what he will do, whereas none can be sure of future events, even during his own life, much more after his death. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#015">Ecclesiastes 10:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">ecc 10:15</a></font></span></p>Wearieth - Fools discover their folly by their wearisome and fruitless endeavours after things which are too high for them. Because - He is ignorant of those things which are most easy, as of the way to the great city whither he is going. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#016">Ecclesiastes 10:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">ecc 10:16</a></font></span></p>A child - Either in age, or childish qualities. Eat - Give up themselves to eating and drinking. Morning - The fittest time for God's service, for the dispatch of weighty affairs, and for sitting in judgment. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#017">Ecclesiastes 10:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ecc 10:17</a></font></span></p>Nobles - Not so much by birth, as by their noble dispositions. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#020">Ecclesiastes 10:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">ecc 10:20</a></font></span></p>Thy thought - In the most secret manner. The rich - Princes or governors. A bird - The king will hear of it by unknown and unsuspected hands, as if a bird had heard and carried the report of it.
