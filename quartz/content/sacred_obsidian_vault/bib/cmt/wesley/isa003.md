 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#001">Isaiah Chapter 3</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#002">Isaiah 3:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">isa 3:2</a></font></span></p>The judge - The civil magistrates. The ancient - Whose wisdom was increased by long experience. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#005">Isaiah 3:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">isa 3:5</a></font></span></p>Oppressed - By thy command or permission of such childish rulers. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#006">Isaiah 3:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">isa 3:6</a></font></span></p>Thou hast - We are utterly undone, and have neither food nor raiment; but thou hast something left to support the dignity which we offer to thee. Under thine hand - To heal it. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#007">Isaiah 3:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">isa 3:7</a></font></span></p>An healer - A repairer of the ruins of the state. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#009">Isaiah 3:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">isa 3:9</a></font></span></p>The shew - Their pride, and wantonness, and impiety m manifestly shews itself in their very looks. They declare - They act it publickly, casting off all fear of God and reverence to men. Rewarded - Procured a fit recompense for their wickedness, even utter ruin. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#010">Isaiah 3:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">isa 3:10</a></font></span></p>Say ye - O ye priests and Levites, that God will be their safeguard and portion. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#012">Isaiah 3:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">isa 3:12</a></font></span></p>Women - Weak and effeminate rulers. They - Thy rulers civil and ecclesiastical. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#013">Isaiah 3:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">isa 3:13</a></font></span></p>Standeth - He will shortly and certainly stand up as a judge, to enquire into the cause, and to give sentence. To judge - To defend and deliver them. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#014">Isaiah 3:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">isa 3:14</a></font></span></p>Ancients - The princes or rulers; such were commonly chosen out of those who were in ripe years. Eaten - Destroyed instead of preserving the church and commonwealth of Israel. Spoil - The goods which you have violently taken away from the poor. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#016">Isaiah 3:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">isa 3:16</a></font></span></p>The daughters - The women; (hitherto he reproved the men). A tinkling - By some ornaments which they wore upon their shoes. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#017">Isaiah 3:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">isa 3:17</a></font></span></p>Secret paths - By giving her into the power of those enemies that shall strip her of all her raiment. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#018">Isaiah 3:18</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_018">isa 3:18</a></font></span></p>Cauls - It is agreed by all, that this and several words that follow, were ornaments used in those times. And it is of no concern, exactly to understand the nature and differences of them. The moon - There were in ancient times, and at this day there are some ornaments worn, which carry a manifest resemblance to the moon or half moon. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#020">Isaiah 3:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">isa 3:20</a></font></span></p>Tablets - He seems to mean boxes of perfumes. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#021">Isaiah 3:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">isa 3:21</a></font></span></p>Nose - jewels - Which were fastened to the head, and hung down upon the forehead to the beginning of the nose. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#022">Isaiah 3:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">isa 3:22</a></font></span></p>Pins - Of silver or gold, either used to curl the hair, or fastened and worn in the hair. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#023">Isaiah 3:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">isa 3:23</a></font></span></p>Glasses - The looking - glasses, as we call them, tho' in truth they were not made of glass, but of bright and burnished brass. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#024">Isaiah 3:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">isa 3:24</a></font></span></p>Girdle - Which were fine and costly, and useful to gird their garments about them. A rent - Torn and tattered garments. Burning - By the heat of the sun, to which they are now commonly exposed, from which they used formerly to guard themselves with the utmost care. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#026">Isaiah 3:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">isa 3:26</a></font></span></p>Gates - The gates of Zion or Jerusalem, which, by a figure, are said to lament, to imply the great desolation of the place; that there would be no people to go out and come in by the gates, as they used to do. Shall sit - Like a mournful woman bewailing the loss of her husband and children.
