 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#001">2 Chronicles Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#001">2 Chronicles 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch2 6:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Compare Kings (marginal references).
 </p><p>Compare Kings (marginal references). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#040">2 Chronicles 6:40</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_040">ch2 6:40</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In Kings, a different conclusion takes the place of these verses. The document from which both writers copied contained the full prayer of dedication, which each givcs in a somewhat abbreviated form.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#041">Ch2 6:41</a>
 </p><p>Thy resting place - i. e., the holy of holies. Solomon follows closely the words of David his father, spoken probably when he brought the ark into Jerusalem. See the marginal references.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#042">Ch2 6:42</a>
 </p><p>Turn not away the face of thine anointed - i. e., make him not to hide his face through shame at having his prayers rejected (compare the <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#016">Kg1 2:16</a> note).
 </p><p>The mercies of David - i. e., "God's mercies toward David."
