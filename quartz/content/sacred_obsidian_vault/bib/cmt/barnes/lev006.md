 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 6</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#001">Leviticus 6:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lev 6:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In the Hebrew Bible <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#001">Lev 6:1-7</a> form part of Lev. 5. It is evident that they ought to do so. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#005">Leviticus 6:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">lev 6:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In the day of his trespass offering - The restitution was thus to be associated with the religious act by which the offender testified his penitence. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#009">Leviticus 6:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">lev 6:9</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Rather, "This, the burnt-offering, shall be upon the fire on the altar all night unto the morning." See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#038">Exo 29:38-46</a>, with the notes. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#010">Leviticus 6:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">lev 6:10</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Ashes ... with the burnt-offering - Rather, the ashes to which the fire hath consumed the burnt-offering. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#013">Leviticus 6:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">lev 6:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The fire shall ever be burning - This was a symbol of the never-ceasing worship which Yahweh required of His people. It was essentially connected with their acts of sacrifice. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#014">Leviticus 6:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">lev 6:14</a></font></span></p>
 <p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#001">Lev 2:1-10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo029.htm#040">Exo 29:40-41</a>.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#016">Lev 6:16</a>
 </p><p>With unleavened bread shall it be eaten - This should be, it (the remainder) shall be eaten unleavened. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#020">Leviticus 6:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">lev 6:20</a></font></span></p>
 <p>See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev004.htm#003">Lev 4:3</a>. Aaron's sons here spoken of (as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#022">Lev 6:22</a>) must be the succession of high priests who succeeded him. The day of this offering was probably the eighth day of the ceremony of consecration <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev008.htm#035">Lev 8:35</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev009.htm#001">Lev 9:1</a>, when the high priest appears to have entered upon the duties of his office.
 </p><p>A meat offering perpetual - Jewish tradition is in favor of these words implying that this מנחה  mı̂nchāh was offered by the high priest as a daily rite from the time of his consecration. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#021">Leviticus 6:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">lev 6:21</a></font></span></p>
 <p>In a pan - See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#005">Lev 2:5</a> note. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#022">Leviticus 6:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">lev 6:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>It shall be wholly burnt - literally, "it shall ascend in fire as a whole burnt-offering." 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#023">Leviticus 6:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">lev 6:23</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Not be eaten - Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#030">Lev 6:30</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev004.htm#012">Lev 4:12</a> note. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#025">Leviticus 6:25</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_025">lev 6:25</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The place where ... - See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#011">Lev 1:11</a>.
 </p><p>It is most holy - See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev002.htm#003">Lev 2:3</a>. The key to the special sanctity of the flesh of the sin-offering, as set forth in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#026">Lev 6:26-30</a>, must, it would seem, be found in the words of Moses to the priests <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#017">Lev 10:17</a>. The flesh of the victim, which represented the sinner for whom atonement was now made, was to be solemnly, and most exclusively, appropriated by those who were appointed to mediate between the sinner and the Lord. The far-reaching symbolism of the act met its perfect fulfillment in the One Mediator who took our nature upon Himself. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#007">Phi 2:7</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#028">Leviticus 6:28</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_028">lev 6:28</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The earthen vessel - Unglazed pottery would absorb some of the juices of the meat: and a vessel made holy could not be put to any other purpose. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev006.htm#030">Leviticus 6:30</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_030">lev 6:30</a></font></span></p>
 <p>To reconcile withal generally rendered "to make atonement for."
 </p><p>The holy place - The outer apartment of the tabernacle. See the <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#018">Lev 10:18</a> note.
