 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num036.htm#001">Numbers Chapter 36</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num036.htm#001">Numbers 36:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">num 36:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The daughters of Zelophehad had obtained an ordinance <a href="../../../bib/kjv/num028.htm#006">Num 28:6-11</a> which permitted the daughters of an Israelite dying without male issue to inherit their father's property. The chiefs of the Machirites, of whom Zelophehad had been one, now obtain a supplemental enactment, directing that heiresses should marry within their own tribe.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num036.htm#004">Num 36:4</a>
 </p><p>Be taken away - i. e. be permanently taken away. The jubilee year, by not restoring the estate to the tribe to which it originally belonged, would in effect confirm the alienation.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/num036.htm#011">Num 36:11</a>
 </p><p>Unto their father's brothers' sons - Or more generally, "unto the sons of their kinsmen."
