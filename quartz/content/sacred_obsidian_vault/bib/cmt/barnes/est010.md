 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/est010.htm#001">Esther Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/est010.htm#001">Esther 10:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">est 10:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>A tribute - Perhaps an allusion to some fresh arrangement of the tribute likely to have followed on the return of Xerxes from Greece.
 </p><p>Upon the isles of the sea - Cyprus, Aradus, the island of Tyre, Platea, etc., remained in the hands of the Persians after the victories of the Greeks, and may be the "isles" here intended. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/est010.htm#002">Esther 10:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">est 10:2</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Power and ... might - In the later years of Xerxes his "power and might" were chiefly shown in the erection of magnificent buildings, more especially at Persepolis. He abstained from military expeditions.
 </p><p>Kings of Media and Persia - Media takes precedence of Persia because the kingdom of Media had preceded that of Persia, and in the "book of the Chronicles" its history came first. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/est010.htm#003">Esther 10:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">est 10:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Mordecai ... was next unto king Ahasuerus - See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/est002.htm#005">Est 2:5</a> note. Artabanus (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/est001.htm#014">Est 1:14</a> note) was favorite toward the end of Xerxes' reign, i. e. in his 20th and 21st years.
 </p><p>
