 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#001">2 Chronicles Chapter 4</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#001">2 Chronicles 4:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch2 4:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The supplementary character of Chronicles is here once more apparent. The author of Kings had omitted to record the dimensions of the brass altar. It stood in the great court <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#012">Ch2 6:12-13</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#003">2 Chronicles 4:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">ch2 4:3</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For "oxen" we find in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#024">Kg1 7:24</a>, "knops" or "gourds." An early copyist, not comprehending the comparatively rare word here used for "gourd," and expecting to hear of oxen, as soon as the molten sea was mentioned, changed the reading. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#005">2 Chronicles 4:5</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_005">ch2 4:5</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Three thousand baths - See <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#023">Kg1 7:23</a> note. It is quite possible that either here or in Kings the text may have been accidentally corrupted. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#007">2 Chronicles 4:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">ch2 4:7</a></font></span></p>
 <p>According to their form - Rather, "after their manner" (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#020">Ch2 4:20</a>). There is no allusion to the shape of the candlesticks, which were made, no doubt, after the pattern of the original candlestick of Moses. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#008">2 Chronicles 4:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">ch2 4:8</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The number of the tables (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#019">Ch2 4:19</a>) and of the basins, is additional to the information contained in Kings. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#016">2 Chronicles 4:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">ch2 4:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Huram his father - Or, "Huram his master-workman" (<a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2002.htm#013">Ch2 2:13</a> note). 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#017">2 Chronicles 4:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">ch2 4:17</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Zeredathah - Or, Zarthan (marginal reference). The writer of Chronicles probably uses the name which the place bore in his own day. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#019">2 Chronicles 4:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">ch2 4:19</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The tables - A single table only is mentioned in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#048">Kg1 7:48</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2029.htm#018">Ch2 29:18</a>. It is supposed that Solomon made ten similar tables, any one of which might be used for the showbread; but that the bread was never placed on more than one table at a time. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#022">2 Chronicles 4:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">ch2 4:22</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The entry of the house - The text is, by some, corrected by <a href="../../../bib/kjv/kg1007.htm#050">Kg1 7:50</a>, "the hinges" of the doors of the house, etc.
