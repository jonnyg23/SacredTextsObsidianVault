 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#001">Leviticus Chapter 15</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#001">Leviticus 15:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">lev 15:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This chapter would seem to take its place more naturally before <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#001">Lev 12:1-8</a>, with the subject of which it is inmediately connected. Compare especially <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev012.htm#002">Lev 12:2</a> with <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#019">Lev 15:19</a>. It stands here between two chapters, with neither of which has it any close connection. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#013">Leviticus 15:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">lev 15:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The mere cessation of the issue does not make him clean: he must wait seven days, etc., preparatory to his offering sacrifice. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#016">Leviticus 15:16</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_016">lev 15:16</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Most of the ancient religions made a similar recognition of impurity and of the need of purfication.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#017">Lev 15:17</a>
 </p><p>Every garment - Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#023">Jde 1:23</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#024">Leviticus 15:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">lev 15:24</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This must refer to an unexpected occurrence. Intercourse during the acknowledged period was a heavy crime, and was to be punished by "cutting off" <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev018.htm#019">Lev 18:19</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev020.htm#018">Lev 20:18</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze018.htm#006">Eze 18:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#031">Leviticus 15:31</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_031">lev 15:31</a></font></span></p>
 <p>This solemn admonition is addressed to Moses and Aaron, see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#001">Lev 15:1</a>.
 </p><p><a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev015.htm#031">Lev 15:31</a>
 </p><p>My tabernacle - Strictly, "my dwelling-place" משׁכן  mı̂shkān, as in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev008.htm#010">Lev 8:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev017.htm#004">Lev 17:4</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#011">Lev 26:11</a>. The word rendered "tabernacle" elsewhere in Leviticus is properly "tent." See the <a href="../../../bib/kjv/exo026.htm#001">Exo 26:1</a> note. 
 </p><p></p><div class="filenav">
