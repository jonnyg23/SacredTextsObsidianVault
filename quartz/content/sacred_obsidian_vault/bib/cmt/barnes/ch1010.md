 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Notes on the Bible</i>, by Albert Barnes, [1834], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#001">1 Chronicles Chapter 10</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#001">1 Chronicles 10:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">ch1 10:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>The present chapter contains two facts not found in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#001">Sa1 31:1-13</a> - the fastening of Saul's head in the temple of Dagon <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#010">Ch1 10:10</a>, and the burial of his bones, and those of his sons, under an oak <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#012">Ch1 10:12</a>. Otherwise the narrative differs from <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#001">Sa1 31:1-13</a> only by being abbreviated (see especially <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#006">Ch1 10:6-7</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#011">Ch1 10:11-12</a>), and by having some moral reflections attached to it <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#013">Ch1 10:13-14</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#006">1 Chronicles 10:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">ch1 10:6</a></font></span></p>
 <p>All his house died together - Not the whole of his family, nor even "all his sons" (see <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2002.htm#008">Sa2 2:8-15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2003.htm#006">Sa2 3:6-15</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa2004.htm#001">Sa2 4:1-12</a>). The phrase is perhaps an abbreviation of the expression in the parallel passage of Samuel <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1031.htm#006">Sa1 31:6</a>. 
 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1010.htm#013">1 Chronicles 10:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">ch1 10:13</a></font></span></p>
 <p>For his transgression - Compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/ch1009.htm#001">Ch1 9:1</a>. The "transgression" intended is probably the disobedience with respect to Amalek, recorded in <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#001">Sa1 15:1-9</a> (compare <a href="../../../bib/kjv/sa1028.htm#017">Sa1 28:17-18</a>).
