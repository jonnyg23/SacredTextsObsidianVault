 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 20</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir020.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 There is a reproof that is not comely: again, some man holdeth his tongue, and he is wise. </p>
 <p>2 It is much better to reprove, than to be angry secretly: and he that confesseth his fault shall be preserved from hurt. </p>
 <p>3 How good is it, when thou art reproved, to shew repentance! for so shalt thou escape wilful sin. </p>
 <p>4 As is the lust of an eunuch to deflower a virgin; so is he that executeth judgment with violence. </p>
 <p>5 There is one that keepeth silence, and is found wise: and another by much babbling becometh hateful. </p>
 <p>6 Some man holdeth his tongue, because he hath not to answer: and some keepeth silence, knowing his time. </p>
 <p>7 A wise man will hold his tongue till he see opportunity: but a babbler and a fool will regard no time. </p>
 <p>8 He that useth many words shall be abhorred; and he that taketh to himself authority therein shall be hated. </p>
 <p>9 There is a sinner that hath good success in evil things; and there is a gain that turneth to loss. </p>
 <p>10 There is a gift that shall not profit thee; and there is a gift whose recompence is double. </p>
 <p>11 There is an abasement because of glory; and there is that lifteth up his head from a low estate. </p>
 <p>12 There is that buyeth much for a little, and repayeth it sevenfold. </p>
 <p>13 A wise man by his words maketh him beloved: but the graces of fools shall be poured out. </p>
 <p>14 The gift of a fool shall do thee no good when thou hast it; neither yet of the envious for his necessity: for he looketh to receive many things for one. </p>
 <p>15 He giveth little, and upbraideth much; he openeth his mouth like a crier; to day he lendeth, and to morrow will he ask it again: such an one is to be hated of God and man. </p>
 <p>16 The fool saith, I have no friends, I have no thank for all my good deeds, and they that eat my bread speak evil of me. </p>
 <p>17 How oft, and of how many shall he be laughed to scorn! for he knoweth not aright what it is to have; and it is all one unto him as if he had it not. </p>
 <p>18 To slip upon a pavement is better than to slip with the tongue: so the fall of the wicked shall come speedily. </p>
 <p>19 An unseasonable tale will always be in the mouth of the unwise. </p>
 <p>20 A wise sentence shall be rejected when it cometh out of a fool's mouth; for he will not speak it in due season. </p>
 <p>21 There is that is hindered from sinning through want: and when he taketh rest, he shall not be troubled. </p>
 <p>22 There is that destroyeth his own soul through bashfulness, and by accepting of persons overthroweth himself. </p>
 <p>23 There is that for bashfulness promiseth to his friend, and maketh him his enemy for nothing. </p>
 <p>24 A lie is a foul blot in a man, yet it is continually in the mouth of the untaught. </p>
 <p>25 A thief is better than a man that is accustomed to lie: but they both shall have destruction to heritage. </p>
 <p>26 The disposition of a liar is dishonourable, and his shame is ever with him. </p>
 <p>27 A wise man shall promote himself to honour with his words: and he that hath understanding will please great men. </p>
 <p>28 He that tilleth his land shall increase his heap: and he that pleaseth great men shall get pardon for iniquity. </p>
 <p>29 Presents and gifts blind the eyes of the wise, and stop up his mouth that he cannot reprove. </p>
 <p>30 Wisdom that is hid, and treasure that is hoarded up, what profit is in them both? </p>
 <p>31 Better is he that hideth his folly than a man that hideth his wisdom. </p>
 <p>32 Necessary patience in seeking ing the Lord is better than he that leadeth his life without a guide. </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir020.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 ἔστιν ἔλεγχος ὃς οὐκ ἔστιν ὡραῖος καὶ ἔστιν σιωπῶν καὶ αὐτὸς φρόνιμος </p>
 <p>2 ὡς καλὸν ἐλέγξαι ἢ θυμοῦσθαι </p>
 <p>3 καὶ ὁ ἀνθομολογούμενος ἀπὸ ἐλαττώσεως κωλυθήσεται </p>
 <p>4 ἐπιθυμία εὐνούχου ἀποπαρθενῶσαι νεάνιδα οὕτως ὁ ποιῶν ἐν βίᾳ κρίματα </p>
 <p>5 ἔστιν σιωπῶν εὑρισκόμενος σοφός καὶ ἔστιν μισητὸς ἀπὸ πολλῆς λαλιᾶς </p>
 <p>6 ἔστιν σιωπῶν οὐ γὰρ ἔχει ἀπόκρισιν καὶ ἔστιν σιωπῶν εἰδὼς καιρόν </p>
 <p>7 ἄνθρωπος σοφὸς σιγήσει ἕως καιροῦ ὁ δὲ λαπιστὴς καὶ ἄφρων ὑπερβήσεται καιρόν </p>
 <p>8 ὁ πλεονάζων λόγῳ βδελυχθήσεται καὶ ὁ ἐνεξουσιαζόμενος μισηθήσεται </p>
 <p>9 ἔστιν εὐοδία ἐν κακοῖς ἀνδρί καὶ ἔστιν εὕρεμα εἰς ἐλάττωσιν </p>
 <p>10 ἔστιν δόσις ἣ οὐ λυσιτελήσει σοι καὶ ἔστιν δόσις ἧς τὸ ἀνταπόδομα διπλοῦν </p>
 <p>11 ἔστιν ἐλάττωσις ἕνεκεν δόξης καὶ ἔστιν ὃς ἀπὸ ταπεινώσεως ἦρεν κεφαλήν </p>
 <p>12 ἔστιν ἀγοράζων πολλὰ ὀλίγου καὶ ἀποτιννύων αὐτὰ ἑπταπλάσιον </p>
 <p>13 ὁ σοφὸς ἐν λόγοις ἑαυτὸν προσφιλῆ ποιήσει χάριτες δὲ μωρῶν ἐκχυθήσονται </p>
 <p>14 δόσις ἄφρονος οὐ λυσιτελήσει σοι οἱ γὰρ ὀφθαλμοὶ αὐτοῦ ἀνθ᾽ ἑνὸς πολλοί </p>
 <p>15 ὀλίγα δώσει καὶ πολλὰ ὀνειδίσει καὶ ἀνοίξει τὸ στόμα αὐτοῦ ὡς κῆρυξ σήμερον δανιεῖ καὶ αὔριον ἀπαιτήσει μισητὸς ἄνθρωπος ὁ τοιοῦτος </p>
 <p>16 μωρὸς ἐρεῖ οὐχ ὑπάρχει μοι φίλος καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν χάρις τοῖς ἀγαθοῖς μου </p>
 <p>17 οἱ ἔσθοντες τὸν ἄρτον αὐτοῦ φαῦλοι γλώσσῃ ποσάκις καὶ ὅσοι καταγελάσονται αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>18 ὀλίσθημα ἀπὸ ἐδάφους μᾶλλον ἢ ἀπὸ γλώσσης οὕτως πτῶσις κακῶν κατὰ σπουδὴν ἥξει </p>
 <p>19 ἄνθρωπος ἄχαρις μῦθος ἄκαιρος ἐν στόματι ἀπαιδεύτων ἐνδελεχισθήσεται </p>
 <p>20 ἀπὸ στόματος μωροῦ ἀποδοκιμασθήσεται παραβολή οὐ γὰρ μὴ εἴπῃ αὐτὴν ἐν καιρῷ αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>21 ἔστιν κωλυόμενος ἁμαρτάνειν ἀπὸ ἐνδείας καὶ ἐν τῇ ἀναπαύσει αὐτοῦ οὐ κατανυγήσεται </p>
 <p>22 ἔστιν ἀπολλύων τὴν ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ δι᾽ αἰσχύνην καὶ ἀπὸ ἄφρονος προσώπου ἀπολεῖ αὐτήν </p>
 <p>23 ἔστιν χάριν αἰσχύνης ἐπαγγελλόμενος φίλῳ καὶ ἐκτήσατο αὐτὸν ἐχθρὸν δωρεάν </p>
 <p>24 μῶμος πονηρὸς ἐν ἀνθρώπῳ ψεῦδος ἐν στόματι ἀπαιδεύτων ἐνδελεχισθήσεται </p>
 <p>25 αἱρετὸν κλέπτης ἢ ὁ ἐνδελεχίζων ψεύδει ἀμφότεροι δὲ ἀπώλειαν κληρονομήσουσιν </p>
 <p>26 ἦθος ἀνθρώπου ψευδοῦς ἀτιμία καὶ ἡ αἰσχύνη αὐτοῦ μετ᾽ αὐτοῦ ἐνδελεχῶς </p>
 <p>27 ὁ σοφὸς ἐν λόγοις προάξει ἑαυτόν καὶ ἄνθρωπος φρόνιμος ἀρέσει μεγιστᾶσιν </p>
 <p>28 ὁ ἐργαζόμενος γῆν ἀνυψώσει θιμωνιὰν αὐτοῦ καὶ ὁ ἀρέσκων μεγιστᾶσιν ἐξιλάσεται ἀδικίαν </p>
 <p>29 ξένια καὶ δῶρα ἀποτυφλοῖ ὀφθαλμοὺς σοφῶν καὶ ὡς φιμὸς ἐν στόματι ἀποτρέπει ἐλεγμούς </p>
 <p>30 σοφία κεκρυμμένη καὶ θησαυρὸς ἀφανής τίς ὠφέλεια ἐν ἀμφοτέροις </p>
 <p>31 κρείσσων ἄνθρωπος ἀποκρύπτων τὴν μωρίαν αὐτοῦ ἢ ἄνθρωπος ἀποκρύπτων τὴν σοφίαν αὐτοῦ </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir020.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Quam bonum est arguere, quam irasci,<br> et confitentem in oratione non prohibere!<br></p>
 <p>2 Concupiscentia spadonis devirginabit juvenculam:<br></p>
 <p>3 sic qui facit per vim judicium iniquum.<br></p>
 <p>4 Quam bonum est correptum manifestare pnitentiam!<br> sic enim effugies voluntarium peccatum.<br></p>
 <p>5 Est tacens qui invenitur sapiens:<br> et est odibilis qui procax est ad loquendum.<br></p>
 <p>6 Est tacens non habens sensum loquelæ:<br> et est tacens sciens tempus aptum.<br></p>
 <p>7 Homo sapiens tacebit usque ad tempus:<br> lascivus autem et imprudens non servabunt tempus.<br></p>
 <p>8 Qui multis utitur verbis lædet animam suam:<br> et qui potestatem sibi sumit injuste, odietur.<br></p>
 <p>9 Est processio in malis viro indisciplinato,<br> et est inventio in detrimentum.<br></p>
 <p>10 Est datum quod non est utile,<br> et est datum cujus retributio duplex.<br></p>
 <p>11 Est propter gloriam minoratio,<br> et est qui ab humilitate levabit caput.<br></p>
 <p>12 Est qui multa redimat modico pretio,<br> et restituens ea in septuplum.<br></p>
 <p>13 Sapiens in verbis seipsum amabilem facit:<br> gratiæ autem fatuorum effundentur.<br></p>
 <p>14 Datus insipientis non erit utilis tibi:<br> oculi enim illius septemplices sunt.<br></p>
 <p>15 Exigua dabit, et multa improperabit:<br> et apertio oris illius inflammatio est.<br></p>
 <p>16 Hodie fneratur quis, et cras expetit:<br> odibilis est homo hujusmodi.<br></p>
 <p>17 Fatuo non erit amicus,<br> et non erit gratia bonis illius:<br></p>
 <p>18 qui enim edunt panem illius, falsæ linguæ sunt.<br> Quoties et quanti irridebunt eum!<br></p>
 <p>19 neque enim quod habendum erat directo sensu distribuit;<br> similiter et quod non erat habendum.<br></p>
 <p>20 Lapsus falsæ linguæ quasi qui in pavimento cadens:<br> sic casus malorum festinanter veniet.<br></p>
 <p>21 Homo acharis quasi fabula vana,<br> in ore indisciplinatorum assidua erit.<br></p>
 <p>22 Ex ore fatui reprobabitur parabola:<br> non enim dicit illam in tempore suo.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>23 [Est qui vetatur peccare præ inopia,<br> et in requie sua stimulabitur.<br></p>
 <p>24 Est qui perdet animam suam præ confusione,<br> et ab imprudenti persona perdet eam:<br> personæ autem acceptione perdet se.<br></p>
 <p>25 Est qui præ confusione promittit amico,<br> et lucratus est eum inimicum gratis.<br></p>
 <p>26 Opprobrium nequam in homine mendacium:<br> et in ore indisciplinatorum assidue erit.<br></p>
 <p>27 Potior fur quam assiduitas viri mendacis:<br> perditionem autem ambo hæreditabunt.<br></p>
 <p>28 Mores hominum mendacium sine honore,<br> et confusio illorum cum ipsis sine intermissione.<br></p>
 <p>29 Sapiens in verbis producet seipsum,<br> et homo prudens placebit magnatis.<br></p>
 <p>30 Qui operatur terram suam inaltabit acervum frugum,<br> et qui operatur justitiam, ipse exaltabitur:<br> qui vero placet magnatis effugiet iniquitatem.<br></p>
 <p>31 Xenia et dona excæcant oculos judicum,<br> et quasi mutus, in ore avertit correptiones eorum.<br></p>
 <p>32 Sapientia absconsa, et thesaurus invisus,<br> quæ utilitas in utrisque?<br></p>
 <p>33 Melior est qui celat insipientiam suam,<br> quam homo qui abscondit sapientiam suam.]</p>
