 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 16</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir016.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 Desire not a multitude of unprofitable children, neither delight in ungodly sons. </p>
 <p>2 Though they multiply, rejoice not in them, except the fear of the Lord be with them. </p>
 <p>3 Trust not thou in their life, neither respect their multitude: for one that is just is better than a thousand; and better it is to die without children, than to have them that are ungodly. </p>
 <p>4 For by one that hath understanding shall the city be replenished: but the kindred of the wicked shall speedily become desolate. </p>
 <p>5 Many such things have I seen with mine eyes, and mine ear hath heard greater things than these. </p>
 <p>6 In the congregation of the ungodly shall a fire be kindled; and in a rebellious nation wrath is set on fire. </p>
 <p>7 He was not pacified toward the old giants, who fell away in the strength of their foolishness. </p>
 <p>8 Neither spared he the place where Lot sojourned, but abhorred them for their pride. </p>
 <p>9 He pitied not the people of perdition, who were taken away in their sins: </p>
 <p>10 Nor the six hundred thousand footmen, who were gathered together in the hardness of their hearts. </p>
 <p>11 And if there be one stiffnecked among the people, it is marvel if he escape unpunished: for mercy and wrath are with him; he is mighty to forgive, and to pour out displeasure. </p>
 <p>12 As his mercy is great, so is his correction also: he judgeth a man according to his works </p>
 <p>13 The sinner shall not escape with his spoils: and the patience of the godly shall not be frustrate. </p>
 <p>14 Make way for every work of mercy: for every man shall find according to his works. </p>
 <p>15 The Lord hardened Pharaoh, that he should not know him, that his powerful works might be known to the world. </p>
 <p>16 His mercy is manifest to every creature; and he hath separated his light from the darkness with an adamant. </p>
 <p>17 Say not thou, I will hide myself from the Lord: shall any remember me from above? I shall not be remembered among so many people: for what is my soul among such an infinite number of creatures? </p>
 <p>18 Behold, the heaven, and the heaven of heavens, the deep, and the earth, and all that therein is, shall be moved when he shall visit. </p>
 <p>19 The mountains also and foundations of the earth be shaken with trembling, when the Lord looketh upon them. </p>
 <p>20 No heart can think upon these things worthily: and who is able to conceive his ways? </p>
 <p>21 It is a tempest which no man can see: for the most part of his works are hid. </p>
 <p>22 Who can declare the works of his justice? or who can endure them? for his covenant is afar off, and the trial of all things is in the end. </p>
 <p>23 He that wanteth understanding will think upon vain things: and a foolish man erring imagineth follies. </p>
 <p>24 by son, hearken unto me, and learn knowledge, and mark my words with thy heart. </p>
 <p>25 I will shew forth doctrine in weight, and declare his knowledge exactly. </p>
 <p>26 The works of the Lord are done in judgment from the beginning: and from the time he made them he disposed the parts thereof. </p>
 <p>27 He garnished his works for ever, and in his hand are the chief of them unto all generations: they neither labour, nor are weary, nor cease from their works. </p>
 <p>28 None of them hindereth another, and they shall never disobey his word. </p>
 <p>29 After this the Lord looked upon the earth, and filled it with his blessings. </p>
 <p>30 With all manner of living things hath he covered the face thereof; and they shall return into it again. </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir016.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 μὴ ἐπιθύμει τέκνων πλῆθος ἀχρήστων μηδὲ εὐφραίνου ἐπὶ υἱοῖς ἀσεβέσιν </p>
 <p>2 ἐὰν πληθύνωσιν μὴ εὐφραίνου ἐπ᾽ αὐτοῖς εἰ μή ἐστιν φόβος κυρίου μετ᾽ αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>3 μὴ ἐμπιστεύσῃς τῇ ζωῇ αὐτῶν καὶ μὴ ἔπεχε ἐπὶ τὸ πλῆθος αὐτῶν κρείσσων γὰρ εἷς ἢ χίλιοι καὶ ἀποθανεῖν ἄτεκνον ἢ ἔχειν τέκνα ἀσεβῆ </p>
 <p>4 ἀπὸ γὰρ ἑνὸς συνετοῦ συνοικισθήσεται πόλις φυλὴ δὲ ἀνόμων ἐρημωθήσεται </p>
 <p>5 πολλὰ τοιαῦτα ἑόρακεν ὁ ὀφθαλμός μου καὶ ἰσχυρότερα τούτων ἀκήκοεν τὸ οὖς μου </p>
 <p>6 ἐν συναγωγῇ ἁμαρτωλῶν ἐκκαυθήσεται πῦρ καὶ ἐν ἔθνει ἀπειθεῖ ἐξεκαύθη ὀργή </p>
 <p>7 οὐκ ἐξιλάσατο περὶ τῶν ἀρχαίων γιγάντων οἳ ἀπέστησαν τῇ ἰσχύι αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>8 οὐκ ἐφείσατο περὶ τῆς παροικίας Λωτ οὓς ἐβδελύξατο διὰ τὴν ὑπερηφανίαν αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>9 οὐκ ἠλέησεν ἔθνος ἀπωλείας τοὺς ἐξηρμένους ἐν ἁμαρτίαις αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>10 καὶ οὕτως ἑξακοσίας χιλιάδας πεζῶν τοὺς ἐπισυναχθέντας ἐν σκληροκαρδίᾳ αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>11 κἂν ᾖ εἷς σκληροτράχηλος θαυμαστὸν τοῦτο εἰ ἀθῳωθήσεται ἔλεος γὰρ καὶ ὀργὴ παρ᾽ αὐτῷ δυνάστης ἐξιλασμῶν καὶ ἐκχέων ὀργήν </p>
 <p>12 κατὰ τὸ πολὺ ἔλεος αὐτοῦ οὕτως καὶ πολὺς ὁ ἔλεγχος αὐτοῦ ἄνδρα κατὰ τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ κρινεῖ </p>
 <p>13 οὐκ ἐκφεύξεται ἐν ἁρπάγματι ἁμαρτωλός καὶ οὐ μὴ καθυστερήσει ὑπομονὴ εὐσεβοῦς </p>
 <p>14 πάσῃ ἐλεημοσύνῃ ποιήσει τόπον ἕκαστος κατὰ τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ εὑρήσει </p>
 <p>17 μὴ εἴπῃς ὅτι ἀπὸ κυρίου κρυβήσομαι καὶ ἐξ ὕψους τίς μου μνησθήσεται ἐν λαῷ πλείονι οὐ μὴ γνωσθῶ τίς γὰρ ἡ ψυχή μου ἐν ἀμετρήτῳ κτίσει </p>
 <p>18 ἰδοὺ ὁ οὐρανὸς καὶ ὁ οὐρανὸς τοῦ οὐρανοῦ ἄβυσσος καὶ γῆ ἐν τῇ ἐπισκοπῇ αὐτοῦ σαλευθήσονται </p>
 <p>19 ἅμα τὰ ὄρη καὶ τὰ θεμέλια τῆς γῆς ἐν τῷ ἐπιβλέψαι εἰς αὐτὰ τρόμῳ συσσείονται </p>
 <p>20 καὶ ἐπ᾽ αὐτοῖς οὐ διανοηθήσεται καρδία καὶ τὰς ὁδοὺς αὐτοῦ τίς ἐνθυμηθήσεται </p>
 <p>21 καὶ καταιγίς ἣν οὐκ ὄψεται ἄνθρωπος τὰ δὲ πλείονα τῶν ἔργων αὐτοῦ ἐν ἀποκρύφοις </p>
 <p>22 ἔργα δικαιοσύνης τίς ἀναγγελεῖ ἢ τίς ὑπομενεῖ μακρὰν γὰρ ἡ διαθήκη </p>
 <p>23 ἐλαττούμενος καρδίᾳ διανοεῖται ταῦτα καὶ ἀνὴρ ἄφρων καὶ πλανώμενος διανοεῖται μωρά </p>
 <p>24 ἄκουσόν μου τέκνον καὶ μάθε ἐπιστήμην καὶ ἐπὶ τῶν λόγων μου πρόσεχε τῇ καρδίᾳ σου </p>
 <p>25 ἐκφανῶ ἐν σταθμῷ παιδείαν καὶ ἐν ἀκριβείᾳ ἀπαγγελῶ ἐπιστήμην </p>
 <p>26 ἐν κρίσει κυρίου τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ ἀπ᾽ ἀρχῆς καὶ ἀπὸ ποιήσεως αὐτῶν διέστειλεν μερίδας αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>27 ἐκόσμησεν εἰς αἰῶνα τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ καὶ τὰς ἀρχὰς αὐτῶν εἰς γενεὰς αὐτῶν οὔτε ἐπείνασαν οὔτε ἐκοπίασαν καὶ οὐκ ἐξέλιπον ἀπὸ τῶν ἔργων αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>28 ἕκαστος τὸν πλησίον αὐτοῦ οὐκ ἐξέθλιψεν καὶ ἕως αἰῶνος οὐκ ἀπειθήσουσιν τοῦ ῥήματος αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>29 καὶ μετὰ ταῦτα κύριος εἰς τὴν γῆν ἐπέβλεψεν καὶ ἐνέπλησεν αὐτὴν τῶν ἀγαθῶν αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>30 ψυχῇ παντὸς ζῴου ἐκάλυψεν τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτῆς καὶ εἰς αὐτὴν ἡ ἀποστροφὴ αὐτῶν </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir016.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Ne jucunderis in filiis impiis, si multiplicentur:<br> nec oblecteris super ipsos, si non est timor Dei in illis.<br></p>
 <p>2 Non credas vitæ illorum,<br> et ne respexeris in labores eorum.<br></p>
 <p>3 Melior est enim unus timens Deum,<br> quam mille filii impii:<br></p>
 <p>4 et utile est mori sine filiis,<br> quam relinquere filios impios.<br></p>
 <p>5 Ab uno sensato inhabitabitur patria:<br> tribus impiorum deseretur.<br></p>
 <p>6 Multa talia vidit oculis meus,<br> et fortiora horum audivit auris mea.<br></p>
 <p>7 In synagoga peccantium exardebit ignis,<br> et in gente incredibili exardescet ira.<br></p>
 <p>8 Non exoraverunt pro peccatis suis antiqui gigantes,<br> qui destructi sunt confidentes suæ virtuti.<br></p>
 <p>9 Et non pepercit peregrinationi Lot,<br> et execratus est eos præ superbia verbi illorum.<br></p>
 <p>10 Non misertus est illis, gentem totam perdens,<br> et extollentem se in peccatis suis.<br></p>
 <p>11 Et sicut sexcenta millia peditum,<br> qui congregati sunt in duritia cordis sui:<br> et si unus fuisset cervicatus,<br> mirum si fuisset immunis.<br></p>
 <p>12 Misericordia enim et ira est cum illo:<br> potens exoratio, et effundens iram.<br></p>
 <p>13 Secundum misericordiam suam,<br> sic correptio illius homines secundum opera sua judicat.<br></p>
 <p>14 Non effugiet in rapina peccator,<br> et non retardabit sufferentia misericordiam facientis.<br></p>
 <p>15 Omnis misericordia faciet locum unicuique,<br> secundum meritum operum suorum,<br> et secundum intellectum peregrinationis ipsius.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>16 [Non dicas: A Deo abscondar:<br> et ex summo, quis mei memorabitur?<br></p>
 <p>17 in populo magno non agnoscar:<br> quæ est enim anima mea in tam immensa creatura?<br></p>
 <p>18 Ecce cælum et cæli cælorum,<br> abyssus, et universa terra, et quæ in eis sunt,<br> in conspectu illius commovebuntur.<br></p>
 <p>19 Montes simul, et colles, et fundamenta terræ,<br> cum conspexerit illa Deus, tremore concutientur.<br></p>
 <p>20 Et in omnibus his insensatum est cor,<br> et omne cor intelligitur ab illo.<br></p>
 <p>21 Et vias illius quis intelligit,<br> et procellam quam nec oculus videbit hominis?<br></p>
 <p>22 Nam plurima illius opera sunt in absconsis:<br> sed opera justitiæ ejus quis enuntiabit, aut quis sustinebit?<br> longe enim est testamentum a quibusdam,<br> et interrogatio omnium in consummatione est.<br></p>
 <p>23 Qui minoratur corde cogitat inania,<br> et vir imprudens et errans cogitat stulta.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>24 [Audi me, fili, et disce disciplinam sensus,<br> et in verbis meis attende in corde tuo:<br></p>
 <p>25 et dicam in æquitate disciplinam,<br> et scrutabor enarrare sapientiam:<br> et in verbis meis attende in corde tuo,<br> et dico in æquitate spiritus virtutes<br> quas posuit Deus in opera sua ab initio,<br> et in veritate enuntio scientiam ejus.<br></p>
 <p>26 In judicio Dei opera ejus ab initio,<br> et ab institutione ipsorum distinxit partes illorum,<br> et initia eorum in gentibus suis.<br></p>
 <p>27 Ornavit in æternum opera illorum:<br> nec esurierunt, nec laboraverunt,<br> et non destiterunt ab operibus suis.<br></p>
 <p>28 Unusquisque proximum sibi non angustiabit in æternum:<br></p>
 <p>29 non sis incredibilis verbo illius.<br></p>
 <p>30 Post hæc Deus in terram respexit,<br> et implevit illam bonis suis:<br></p>
 <p>31 anima omnis vitalis denuntiavit ante faciem ipsius,<br> et in ipsam iterum reversio illorum.]</p>
