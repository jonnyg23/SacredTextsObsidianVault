 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 10</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir010.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 A wise judge will instruct his people; and the government of a prudent man is well ordered. </p>
 <p>2 As the judge of the people is himself, so are his officers; and what manner of man the ruler of the city is, such are all they that dwell therein. </p>
 <p>3 An unwise king destroyeth his people; but through the prudence of them which are in authority the city shall be inhabited. </p>
 <p>4 The power of the earth is in the hand of the Lord, and in due time he will set over it one that is profitable. </p>
 <p>5 In the hand of God is the prosperity of man: and upon the person of the scribe shall he lay his honour. </p>
 <p>6 Bear not hatred to thy neighbour for every wrong; and do nothing at all by injurious practices. </p>
 <p>7 Pride is hateful before God and man: and by both doth one commit iniquity. </p>
 <p>8 Because of unrighteous dealings, injuries, and riches got by deceit, the kingdom is translated from one people to another. </p>
 <p>9 Why is earth and ashes proud? There is not a more wicked thing than a covetous man: for such an one setteth his own soul to sale; because while he liveth he casteth away his bowels. </p>
 <p>10 The physician cutteth off a long disease; and he that is to day a king to morrow shall die. </p>
 <p>11 For when a man is dead, he shall inherit creeping things, beasts, and worms. </p>
 <p>12 The beginning of pride is when one departeth from God, and his heart is turned away from his Maker. </p>
 <p>13 For pride is the beginning of sin, and he that hath it shall pour out abomination: and therefore the Lord brought upon them strange calamities, and overthrew them utterly. </p>
 <p>14 The Lord hath cast down the thrones of proud princes, and set up the meek in their stead. </p>
 <p>15 The Lord hath plucked up the roots of the proud nations, and planted the lowly in their place. </p>
 <p>16 The Lord overthrew countries of the heathen, and destroyed them to the foundations of the earth. </p>
 <p>17 He took some of them away, and destroyed them, and hath made their memorial to cease from the earth. </p>
 <p>18 Pride was not made for men, nor furious anger for them that are born of a woman. </p>
 <p>19 They that fear the Lord are a sure seed, and they that love him an honourable plant: they that regard not the law are a dishonourable seed; they that transgress the commandments are a deceivable seed. </p>
 <p>20 Among brethren he that is chief is honorable; so are they that fear the Lord in his eyes. </p>
 <p>21 The fear of the Lord goeth before the obtaining of authority: but roughness and pride is the losing thereof. </p>
 <p>22 Whether he be rich, noble, or poor, their glory is the fear of the Lord. </p>
 <p>23 It is not meet to despise the poor man that hath understanding; neither is it convenient to magnify a sinful man. </p>
 <p>24 Great men, and judges, and potentates, shall be honoured; yet is there none of them greater than he that feareth the Lord. </p>
 <p>25 Unto the servant that is wise shall they that are free do service: and he that hath knowledge will not grudge when he is reformed. </p>
 <p>26 Be not overwise in doing thy business; and boast not thyself in the time of thy distress. </p>
 <p>27 Better is he that laboureth, and aboundeth in all things, than he that boasteth himself, and wanteth bread. </p>
 <p>28 My son, glorify thy soul in meekness, and give it honour according to the dignity thereof. </p>
 <p>29 Who will justify him that sinneth against his own soul? and who will honour him that dishonoureth his own life? </p>
 <p>30 The poor man is honoured for his skill, and the rich man is honoured for his riches. </p>
 <p>31 He that is honoured in poverty, how much more in riches? and he that is dishonourable in riches, how much more in poverty? </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir010.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 κριτὴς σοφὸς παιδεύσει τὸν λαὸν αὐτοῦ καὶ ἡγεμονία συνετοῦ τεταγμένη ἔσται </p>
 <p>2 κατὰ τὸν κριτὴν τοῦ λαοῦ οὕτως καὶ οἱ λειτουργοὶ αὐτοῦ καὶ κατὰ τὸν ἡγούμενον τῆς πόλεως πάντες οἱ κατοικοῦντες αὐτήν </p>
 <p>3 βασιλεὺς ἀπαίδευτος ἀπολεῖ τὸν λαὸν αὐτοῦ καὶ πόλις οἰκισθήσεται ἐν συνέσει δυναστῶν </p>
 <p>4 ἐν χειρὶ κυρίου ἡ ἐξουσία τῆς γῆς καὶ τὸν χρήσιμον ἐγερεῖ εἰς καιρὸν ἐπ᾽ αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>5 ἐν χειρὶ κυρίου εὐοδία ἀνδρός καὶ προσώπῳ γραμματέως ἐπιθήσει δόξαν αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>6 ἐπὶ παντὶ ἀδικήματι μὴ μηνιάσῃς τῷ πλησίον καὶ μὴ πρᾶσσε μηδὲν ἐν ἔργοις ὕβρεως </p>
 <p>7 μισητὴ ἔναντι κυρίου καὶ ἀνθρώπων ὑπερηφανία καὶ ἐξ ἀμφοτέρων πλημμελὴς ἡ ἀδικία </p>
 <p>8 βασιλεία ἀπὸ ἔθνους εἰς ἔθνος μετάγεται διὰ ἀδικίας καὶ ὕβρεις καὶ χρήματα </p>
 <p>9 τί ὑπερηφανεύεται γῆ καὶ σποδός ὅτι ἐν ζωῇ ἔρριψα τὰ ἐνδόσθια αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>10 μακρὸν ἀρρώστημα σκώπτει ἰατρός καὶ βασιλεὺς σήμερον καὶ αὔριον τελευτήσει </p>
 <p>11 ἐν γὰρ τῷ ἀποθανεῖν ἄνθρωπον κληρονομήσει ἑρπετὰ καὶ θηρία καὶ σκώληκας </p>
 <p>12 ἀρχὴ ὑπερηφανίας ἀνθρώπου ἀφίστασθαι ἀπὸ κυρίου καὶ ἀπὸ τοῦ ποιήσαντος αὐτὸν ἀπέστη ἡ καρδία αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>13 ὅτι ἀρχὴ ὑπερηφανίας ἁμαρτία καὶ ὁ κρατῶν αὐτῆς ἐξομβρήσει βδέλυγμα διὰ τοῦτο παρεδόξασεν κύριος τὰς ἐπαγωγὰς καὶ κατέστρεψεν εἰς τέλος αὐτούς </p>
 <p>14 θρόνους ἀρχόντων καθεῖλεν ὁ κύριος καὶ ἐκάθισεν πραεῖς ἀντ᾽ αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>15 ῥίζας ἐθνῶν ἐξέτιλεν ὁ κύριος καὶ ἐφύτευσεν ταπεινοὺς ἀντ᾽ αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>16 χώρας ἐθνῶν κατέστρεψεν ὁ κύριος καὶ ἀπώλεσεν αὐτὰς ἕως θεμελίων γῆς </p>
 <p>17 ἐξῆρεν ἐξ αὐτῶν καὶ ἀπώλεσεν αὐτοὺς καὶ κατέπαυσεν ἀπὸ γῆς τὸ μνημόσυνον αὐτῶν </p>
 <p>18 οὐκ ἔκτισται ἀνθρώποις ὑπερηφανία οὐδὲ ὀργὴ θυμοῦ γεννήμασιν γυναικῶν </p>
 <p>19 σπέρμα ἔντιμον ποῖον σπέρμα ἀνθρώπου σπέρμα ἔντιμον ποῖον οἱ φοβούμενοι τὸν κύριον σπέρμα ἄτιμον ποῖον σπέρμα ἀνθρώπου σπέρμα ἄτιμον ποῖον οἱ παραβαίνοντες ἐντολάς </p>
 <p>20 ἐν μέσῳ ἀδελφῶν ὁ ἡγούμενος αὐτῶν ἔντιμος καὶ οἱ φοβούμενοι κύριον ἐν ὀφθαλμοῖς αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>22 πλούσιος καὶ ἔνδοξος καὶ πτωχός τὸ καύχημα αὐτῶν φόβος κυρίου </p>
 <p>23 οὐ δίκαιον ἀτιμάσαι πτωχὸν συνετόν καὶ οὐ καθήκει δοξάσαι ἄνδρα ἁμαρτωλόν </p>
 <p>24 μεγιστὰν καὶ κριτὴς καὶ δυνάστης δοξασθήσεται καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν αὐτῶν τις μείζων τοῦ φοβουμένου τὸν κύριον </p>
 <p>25 οἰκέτῃ σοφῷ ἐλεύθεροι λειτουργήσουσιν καὶ ἀνὴρ ἐπιστήμων οὐ γογγύσει </p>
 <p>26 μὴ σοφίζου ποιῆσαι τὸ ἔργον σου καὶ μὴ δοξάζου ἐν καιρῷ στενοχωρίας σου </p>
 <p>27 κρείσσων ἐργαζόμενος καὶ περισσεύων ἐν πᾶσιν ἢ περιπατῶν δοξαζόμενος καὶ ἀπορῶν ἄρτων </p>
 <p>28 τέκνον ἐν πραΰτητι δόξασον τὴν ψυχήν σου καὶ δὸς αὐτῇ τιμὴν κατὰ τὴν ἀξίαν αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>29 τὸν ἁμαρτάνοντα εἰς τὴν ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ τίς δικαιώσει καὶ τίς δοξάσει τὸν ἀτιμάζοντα τὴν ζωὴν αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>30 πτωχὸς δοξάζεται δι᾽ ἐπιστήμην αὐτοῦ καὶ πλούσιος δοξάζεται διὰ τὸν πλοῦτον αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>31 ὁ δεδοξασμένος ἐν πτωχείᾳ καὶ ἐν πλούτῳ ποσαχῶς καὶ ὁ ἄδοξος ἐν πλούτῳ καὶ ἐν πτωχείᾳ ποσαχῶς </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir010.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Judex sapiens judicabit populum suum,<br> et principatus sensati stabilis erit.<br></p>
 <p>2 Secundum judicem populi, sic et ministri ejus:<br> et qualis rector est civitatis, tales et inhabitantes in ea.<br></p>
 <p>3 Rex insipiens perdet populum suum:<br> et civitates inhabitabuntur per sensum potentium.<br></p>
 <p>4 In manu Dei potestas terræ:<br> et utilem rectorem suscitabit in tempus super illam.<br></p>
 <p>5 In manu Dei prosperitas hominis,<br> et super faciem scribæ imponet honorem suum.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>6 [Omnis injuriæ proximi ne memineris,<br> et nihil agas in operibus injuriæ.<br></p>
 <p>7 Odibilis coram Deo est et hominibus superbia,<br> et execrabilis omnis iniquitas gentium.<br></p>
 <p>8 Regnum a gente in gentem transfertur propter injustitias,<br> et injurias, et contumelias, et diversos dolos.<br></p>
 <p>9 Avaro autem nihil est scelestius.<br> Quid superbit terra et cinis?<br></p>
 <p>10 Nihil est iniquius quam amare pecuniam:<br> hic enim et animam suam venalem habet,<br> quoniam in vita sua projecit intima sua.<br></p>
 <p>11 Omnis potentatus brevis vita;<br> languor prolixior gravat medicum.<br></p>
 <p>12 Brevem languorem præcidit medicus:<br> sic et rex hodie est, et cras morietur.<br></p>
 <p>13 Cum enim morietur homo,<br> hæreditabit serpentes, et bestias, et vermes.<br></p>
 <p>14 Initium superbiæ hominis apostatare a Deo:<br></p>
 <p>15 quoniam ab eo qui fecit illum recessit cor ejus,<br> quoniam initium omnis peccati est superbia.<br> Qui tenuerit illam adimplebitur maledictis,<br> et subvertet eum in finem.<br></p>
 <p>16 Propterea exhonoravit Dominus conventus malorum,<br> et destruxit eos usque in finem.<br></p>
 <p>17 Sedes ducum superborum destruxit Deus,<br> et sedere fecit mites pro eis.<br></p>
 <p>18 Radices gentium superbarum arefecit Deus,<br> et plantavit humiles ex ipsis gentibus.<br></p>
 <p>19 Terras gentium evertit Dominus,<br> et perdidit eas usque ad fundamentum.<br></p>
 <p>20 Arefecit ex ipsis, et disperdidit eos,<br> et cessare fecit memoriam eorum a terra.<br></p>
 <p>21 Memoria superborum perdidit Deus,<br> et reliquit memoriam humilium sensu.<br></p>
 <p>22 Non est creata hominibus superbia,<br> neque iracundia nationi mulierum.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>23 [Semen hominum honorabitur hoc, quod timet Deum:<br> semen autem hoc exhonorabitur, quod præterit mandata Domini.<br></p>
 <p>24 In medio fratrum rector illorum in honore:<br> et qui timent Dominum erunt in oculis illius.<br></p>
 <p>25 Gloria divitum, honoratorum, et pauperum,<br> timor Dei est.<br></p>
 <p>26 Noli despicere hominem justum pauperem,<br> et noli magnificare virum peccatorem divitem.<br></p>
 <p>27 Magnus, et judex, et potens est in honore:<br> et non est major illo qui timet Deum.<br></p>
 <p>28 Servo sensato liberi servient:<br> et vir prudens et disciplinatus non murmurabit correptus,<br> et inscius non honorabitur.<br></p>
 <p>29 Noli extollere te in faciendo opere tuo,<br> et noli cunctari in tempore angustiæ.<br></p>
 <p>30 Melior est qui operatur et abundat in omnibus,<br> quam qui gloriatur et eget pane.<br></p>
 <p>31 Fili, in mansuetudine serva animam tuam,<br> et da illi honorem secundum meritum suum.<br></p>
 <p>32 Peccantem in animam suam quis justificabit?<br> et quis honorificabit exhonorantem animam suam?<br></p>
 <p>33 Pauper gloriatur per disciplinam et timorem suum:<br> et est homo qui honorificatur propter substantiam suam.<br></p>
 <p>34 Qui autem gloriatur in paupertate, quanto magis in substantia!<br> et qui gloriatur in substantia, paupertatem vereatur.]</p>
