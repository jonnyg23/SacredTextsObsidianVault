 <p>3  In the third<sup><a href="h7969.htm">7969</a></sup> year<sup><a href="h8141.htm">8141</a></sup> of his reign,<sup><a href="h4427.htm">4427</a></sup> he made<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> a feast<sup><a href="h4960.htm">4960</a></sup> unto all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> his princes<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> and his servants;<sup><a href="h5650.htm">5650</a></sup> the power<sup><a href="h2428.htm">2428</a></sup> of Persia<sup><a href="h6539.htm">6539</a></sup> and Media,<sup><a href="h4074.htm">4074</a></sup> the nobles<sup><a href="h6579.htm">6579</a></sup> and princes<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> of the provinces,<sup><a href="h4082.htm">4082</a></sup> being before<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> him: </p>
 <p>4  When he showed<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> the riches<sup><a href="h6239.htm">6239</a></sup> of his glorious<sup><a href="h3519.htm">3519</a></sup> kingdom<sup><a href="h4438.htm">4438</a></sup> and the honor<sup><a href="h3366.htm">3366</a></sup> of his excellent<sup><a href="h8597.htm">8597</a></sup> majesty<sup><a href="h1420.htm">1420</a></sup> many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> days,<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> even a hundred<sup><a href="h3967.htm">3967</a></sup> and fourscore<sup><a href="h8084.htm">8084</a></sup> days.<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  And when these<sup><a href="h0428.htm">428</a></sup> days<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> were expired,<sup><a href="h4390.htm">4390</a></sup> the king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> made<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> a feast<sup><a href="h4960.htm">4960</a></sup> unto all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> the people<sup><a href="h5971.htm">5971</a></sup> that were present<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> in Shushan<sup><a href="h7800.htm">7800</a></sup> the palace,<sup><a href="h1002.htm">1002</a></sup> both unto great<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> and small,<sup><a href="h6996.htm">6996</a></sup> seven<sup><a href="h7651.htm">7651</a></sup> days,<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> in the court<sup><a href="h2691.htm">2691</a></sup> of the garden<sup><a href="h1594.htm">1594</a></sup> of the king's<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> palace;<sup><a href="h1055.htm">1055</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  Where were white,<sup><a href="h2353.htm">2353</a></sup> green,<sup><a href="h3768.htm">3768</a></sup> and blue,<sup><a href="h8504.htm">8504</a></sup> hangings, fastened<sup><a href="h0270.htm">270</a></sup> with cords<sup><a href="h2256.htm">2256</a></sup> of fine linen<sup><a href="h0948.htm">948</a></sup> and purple<sup><a href="h0713.htm">713</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> silver<sup><a href="h3701.htm">3701</a></sup> rings<sup><a href="h1550.htm">1550</a></sup> and pillars<sup><a href="h5982.htm">5982</a></sup> of marble:<sup><a href="h8336.htm">8336</a></sup> the beds<sup><a href="h4296.htm">4296</a></sup> were of gold<sup><a href="h2091.htm">2091</a></sup> and silver,<sup><a href="h3701.htm">3701</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> a pavement<sup><a href="h7531.htm">7531</a></sup> of red,<sup><a href="h0923.htm">923</a></sup> and blue,<sup><a href="h8336.htm">8336</a></sup> and white,<sup><a href="h1858.htm">1858</a></sup> and black marble.<sup><a href="h5508.htm">5508</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  And they gave them drink<sup><a href="h8248.htm">8248</a></sup> in vessels<sup><a href="h3627.htm">3627</a></sup> of gold,<sup><a href="h2091.htm">2091</a></sup> (the vessels<sup><a href="h3627.htm">3627</a></sup> being diverse<sup><a href="h8138.htm">8138</a></sup> one from another,)<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3627.htm">3627</a></sup> and royal<sup><a href="h4438.htm">4438</a></sup> wine<sup><a href="h3196.htm">3196</a></sup> in abundance,<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> according to the state<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of the king.<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  And the drinking<sup><a href="h8360.htm">8360</a></sup> was according to the law;<sup><a href="h1881.htm">1881</a></sup> none<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> did compel:<sup><a href="h0597.htm">597</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> so<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> the king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> had appointed<sup><a href="h3245.htm">3245</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> the officers<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> of his house,<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> that they should do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> according to every man's<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> pleasure.<sup><a href="h7522.htm">7522</a></sup> </p>

 <p>10  On the seventh<sup><a href="h7637.htm">7637</a></sup> day,<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> when the heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> of the king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> was merry<sup><a href="h2895.htm">2895</a></sup> with wine,<sup><a href="h3196.htm">3196</a></sup> he commanded<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> Mehuman,<sup><a href="h4104.htm">4104</a></sup> Biztha,<sup><a href="h0968.htm">968</a></sup> Harbona,<sup><a href="h2726.htm">2726</a></sup> Bigtha,<sup><a href="h0903.htm">903</a></sup> and Abagtha,<sup><a href="h0005.htm">5</a></sup> Zethar,<sup><a href="h2242.htm">2242</a></sup> and Carcas,<sup><a href="h3752.htm">3752</a></sup> the seven<sup><a href="h7651.htm">7651</a></sup> chamberlains<sup><a href="h5631.htm">5631</a></sup> that served<sup><a href="h8334.htm">8334</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> in the presence<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> of Ahasuerus<sup><a href="h0325.htm">325</a></sup> the king,<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> </p>

 <p>15  What<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> shall we do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> unto the queen<sup><a href="h4436.htm">4436</a></sup> Vashti<sup><a href="h2060.htm">2060</a></sup> according to law,<sup><a href="h1881.htm">1881</a></sup> because<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> she hath not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> performed<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> the commandment<sup><a href="h3982.htm">3982</a></sup> of the king<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> Ahasuerus<sup><a href="h0325.htm">325</a></sup> by<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> the chamberlains?<sup><a href="h5631.htm">5631</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/est001.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 In diebus Assueri, qui regnavit ab India usque Æthiopiam super centum viginti septem provincias,</p>
 <p>2 quando sedit in solio regni sui, Susan civitas regni ejus exordium fuit.</p>
 <p>3 Tertio igitur anno imperii sui fecit grande convivium cunctis principibus et pueris suis, fortissimis Persarum, et Medorum inclytis, et præfectis provinciarum coram se,</p>
 <p>4 ut ostenderet divitias gloriæ regni sui, ac magnitudinem atque jactantiam potentiæ suæ, multo tempore, centum videlicet et octoginta diebus.</p>
 <p>5 Cumque implerentur dies convivii, invitavit omnem populum, qui inventus est in Susan, a maximo usque ad minimum: et jussit septem diebus convivium præparari in vestibulo horti, et nemoris quod regio cultu et manu consitum erat.</p>
 <p>6 Et pendebant ex omni parte tentoria ærii coloris et carbasini ac hyacinthini, sustentata funibus byssinis atque purpureis, qui eburneis circulis inserti erant, et columnis marmoreis fulciebantur. Lectuli quoque aurei et argentei, super pavimentum smaragdino et pario stratum lapide, dispositi erant: quod mira varietate pictura decorabat.</p>
 <p>7 Bibebant autem qui invitati erant aureis poculis, et aliis atque aliis vasis cibi inferebantur. Vinum quoque, ut magnificentia regia dignum erat, abundans, et præcipuum ponebatur.</p>
 <p>8 Nec erat qui nolentes cogeret ad bibendum, sed sicut rex statuerat, præponens mensis singulos de principibus suis ut sumeret unusquisque quod vellet.</p>
 <p>9 Vasthi quoque regina fecit convivium feminarum in palatio, ubi rex Assuerus manere consueverat.<br>
 </p>
 <p>10 Itaque die septimo, cum rex esset hilarior, et post nimiam potationem incaluisset mero, præcepit Maumam, et Bazatha, et Harbona, et Bagatha, et Abgatha, et Zethar, et Charchas, septem eunuchis qui in conspectu ejus ministrabant,</p>
 <p>11 ut introducerent reginam Vasthi coram rege, posito super caput ejus diademate, ut ostenderet cunctis populis et principibus pulchritudinem illius: erat enim pulchra valde.</p>
 <p>12 Quæ renuit, et ad regis imperium quod per eunuchos mandaverat, venire contempsit. Unde iratus rex, et nimio furore succensus,</p>
 <p>13 interrogavit sapientes, qui ex more regio semper ei aderant, et illorum faciebat cuncta consilio, scientium leges, ac jura majorum</p>
 <p>14 (erant autem primi et proximi, Charsena, et Sethar, et Admatha, et Tharsis, et Mares, et Marsana, et Mamuchan, septem duces Persarum, atque Medorum, qui videbant faciem regis, et primi post eum residere soliti erant):</p>
 <p>15 cui sententiæ Vasthi regina subjaceret, quæ Assueri regis imperium, quod per eunuchos mandaverat, facere noluisset.</p>
 <p>16 Responditque Mamuchan, audiente rege atque principibus: Non solum regem læsit regina Vasthi, sed et omnes populos et principes qui sunt in cunctis provinciis regis Assueri.</p>
 <p>17 Egredietur enim sermo reginæ ad omnes mulieres, ut contemnant viros suos, et dicant: Rex Assuerus jussit ut regina Vasthi intraret ad eum, et illa noluit.</p>
 <p>18 Atque hoc exemplo omnes principum conjuges Persarum atque Medorum parvipendent imperia maritorum: unde regis justa est indignatio.</p>
 <p>19 Si tibi placet, egrediatur edictum a facie tua, et scribatur juxta legem Persarum atque Medorum, quam præteriri illicitum est, ut nequaquam ultra Vasthi ingrediatur ad regem, sed regnum illius altera, quæ melior est illa, accipiat.</p>
 <p>20 Et hoc in omne (quod latissimum est) provinciarum tuarum divulgetur imperium, et cunctæ uxores, tam majorum quam minorum, deferant maritis suis honorem.</p>
 <p>21 Placuit consilium ejus regi et principibus: fecitque rex juxta consilium Mamuchan,</p>
 <p>22 et misit epistolas ad universas provincias regni sui, ut quæque gens audire et legere poterat, diversis linguis et litteris, esse viros principes ac majores in domibus suis: et hoc per cunctos populos divulgari.</p>
