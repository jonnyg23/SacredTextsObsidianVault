 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 26</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir026.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 Blessed is the man that hath a virtuous wife, for the number of his days shall be double. </p>
 <p>2 A virtuous woman rejoiceth her husband, and he shall fulfil the years of his life in peace. </p>
 <p>3 A good wife is a good portion, which shall be given in the portion of them that fear the Lord. </p>
 <p>4 Whether a man be rich or poor, if he have a good heart toward the Lord, he shall at all times rejoice with a cheerful countenance. </p>
 <p>5 There be three things that mine heart feareth; and for the fourth I was sore afraid: the slander of a city, the gathering together of an unruly multitude, and a false accusation: all these are worse than death. </p>
 <p>6 But a grief of heart and sorrow is a woman that is jealous over another woman, and a scourge of the tongue which communicateth with all. </p>
 <p>7 An evil wife is a yoke shaken to and fro: he that hath hold of her is as though he held a scorpion. </p>
 <p>8 A drunken woman and a gadder abroad causeth great anger, and she will not cover her own shame. </p>
 <p>9 The whoredom of a woman may be known in her haughty looks and eyelids. </p>
 <p>10 If thy daughter be shameless, keep her in straitly, lest she abuse herself through overmuch liberty. </p>
 <p>11 Watch over an impudent eye: and marvel not if she trespass against thee. </p>
 <p>12 She will open her mouth, as a thirsty traveller when he hath found a fountain, and drink of every water near her: by every hedge will she sit down, and open her quiver against every arrow. </p>
 <p>13 The grace of a wife delighteth her husband, and her discretion will fatten his bones. </p>
 <p>14 A silent and loving woman is a gift of the Lord; and there is nothing so much worth as a mind well instructed. </p>
 <p>15 A shamefaced and faithful woman is a double grace, and her continent mind cannot be valued. </p>
 <p>16 As the sun when it ariseth in the high heaven; so is the beauty of a good wife in the ordering of her house. </p>
 <p>17 As the clear light is upon the holy candlestick; so is the beauty of the face in ripe age. </p>
 <p>18 As the golden pillars are upon the sockets of silver; so are the fair feet with a constant heart. </p>
 <p>19 My son, keep the flower of thine age sound; and give not thy strength to strangers. </p>
 <p>20 When thou hast gotten a fruitful possession through all the field, sow it with thine own seed, trusting in the goodness of thy stock. </p>
 <p>21 So thy race which thou leavest shall be magnified, having the confidence of their good descent. </p>
 <p>22 An harlot shall be accounted as spittle; but a married woman is a tower against death to her husband. </p>
 <p>23 A wicked woman is given as a portion to a wicked man: but a godly woman is given to him that feareth the Lord. </p>
 <p>24 A dishonest woman contemneth shame: but an honest woman will reverence her husband. </p>
 <p>25 A shameless woman shall be counted as a dog; but she that is shamefaced will fear the Lord. </p>
 <p>26 A woman that honoureth her husband shall be judged wise of all; but she that dishonoureth him in her pride shall be counted ungodly of all. </p>
 <p>27 A loud crying woman and a scold shall be sought out to drive away the enemies. </p>
 <p>28 There be two things that grieve my heart; and the third maketh me angry: a man of war that suffereth poverty; and men of understanding that are not set by; and one that returneth from righteousness to sin; the Lord prepareth such an one for the sword. </p>
 <p>29 A merchant shall hardly keep himself from doing wrong; and an huckster shall not be freed from sin. </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir026.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 γυναικὸς ἀγαθῆς μακάριος ὁ ἀνήρ καὶ ἀριθμὸς τῶν ἡμερῶν αὐτοῦ διπλάσιος </p>
 <p>2 γυνὴ ἀνδρεία εὐφραίνει τὸν ἄνδρα αὐτῆς καὶ τὰ ἔτη αὐτοῦ πληρώσει ἐν εἰρήνῃ </p>
 <p>3 γυνὴ ἀγαθὴ μερὶς ἀγαθή ἐν μερίδι φοβουμένων κύριον δοθήσεται </p>
 <p>4 πλουσίου δὲ καὶ πτωχοῦ καρδία ἀγαθή ἐν παντὶ καιρῷ πρόσωπον ἱλαρόν </p>
 <p>5 ἀπὸ τριῶν εὐλαβήθη ἡ καρδία μου καὶ ἐπὶ τῷ τετάρτῳ προσώπῳ ἐδεήθην διαβολὴν πόλεως καὶ ἐκκλησίαν ὄχλου καὶ καταψευσμόν ὑπὲρ θάνατον πάντα μοχθηρά </p>
 <p>6 ἄλγος καρδίας καὶ πένθος γυνὴ ἀντίζηλος ἐπὶ γυναικὶ καὶ μάστιξ γλώσσης πᾶσιν ἐπικοινωνοῦσα </p>
 <p>7 βοοζύγιον σαλευόμενον γυνὴ πονηρά ὁ κρατῶν αὐτῆς ὡς ὁ δρασσόμενος σκορπίου </p>
 <p>8 ὀργὴ μεγάλη γυνὴ μέθυσος καὶ ἀσχημοσύνην αὐτῆς οὐ συγκαλύψει </p>
 <p>9 πορνεία γυναικὸς ἐν μετεωρισμοῖς ὀφθαλμῶν καὶ ἐν τοῖς βλεφάροις αὐτῆς γνωσθήσεται </p>
 <p>10 ἐπὶ θυγατρὶ ἀδιατρέπτῳ στερέωσον φυλακήν ἵνα μὴ εὑροῦσα ἄνεσιν ἑαυτῇ χρήσηται </p>
 <p>11 ὀπίσω ἀναιδοῦς ὀφθαλμοῦ φύλαξαι καὶ μὴ θαυμάσῃς ἐὰν εἰς σὲ πλημμελήσῃ </p>
 <p>12 ὡς διψῶν ὁδοιπόρος τὸ στόμα ἀνοίξει καὶ ἀπὸ παντὸς ὕδατος τοῦ σύνεγγυς πίεται κατέναντι παντὸς πασσάλου καθήσεται καὶ ἔναντι βέλους ἀνοίξει φαρέτραν </p>
 <p>13 χάρις γυναικὸς τέρψει τὸν ἄνδρα αὐτῆς καὶ τὰ ὀστᾶ αὐτοῦ πιανεῖ ἡ ἐπιστήμη αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>14 δόσις κυρίου γυνὴ σιγηρά καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν ἀντάλλαγμα πεπαιδευμένης ψυχῆς </p>
 <p>15 χάρις ἐπὶ χάριτι γυνὴ αἰσχυντηρά καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν σταθμὸς πᾶς ἄξιος ἐγκρατοῦς ψυχῆς </p>
 <p>16 ἥλιος ἀνατέλλων ἐν ὑψίστοις κυρίου καὶ κάλλος ἀγαθῆς γυναικὸς ἐν κόσμῳ οἰκίας αὐτῆς </p>
 <p>17 λύχνος ἐκλάμπων ἐπὶ λυχνίας ἁγίας καὶ κάλλος προσώπου ἐπὶ ἡλικίᾳ στασίμῃ </p>
 <p>18 στῦλοι χρύσεοι ἐπὶ βάσεως ἀργυρᾶς καὶ πόδες ὡραῖοι ἐπὶ στέρνοις εὐσταθοῦς </p>
 <p>28 ἐπὶ δυσὶ λελύπηται ἡ καρδία μου καὶ ἐπὶ τῷ τρίτῳ θυμός μοι ἐπῆλθεν ἀνὴρ πολεμιστὴς ὑστερῶν δι᾽ ἔνδειαν καὶ ἄνδρες συνετοὶ ἐὰν σκυβαλισθῶσιν ἐπανάγων ἀπὸ δικαιοσύνης ἐπὶ ἁμαρτίαν ὁ κύριος ἑτοιμάσει εἰς ῥομφαίαν αὐτόν </p>
 <p>29 μόλις ἐξελεῖται ἔμπορος ἀπὸ πλημμελείας καὶ οὐ δικαιωθήσεται κάπηλος ἀπὸ ἁμαρτίας </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir026.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Mulieris bonæ beatus vir:<br> numerus enim annorum illius duplex.<br></p>
 <p>2 Mulier fortis oblectat virum suum,<br> et annos vitæ illius in pace implebit.<br></p>
 <p>3 Pars bona mulier bona,<br> in parte timentium Deum dabitur viro pro factis bonis:<br></p>
 <p>4 divitis autem et pauperis cor bonum,<br> in omni tempore vultus illorum hilaris.<br></p>
 <p>5 A tribus timuit cor meum,<br> et in quarto facies mea metuit:<br></p>
 <p>6 delaturam civitatis, et collectionem populi:<br></p>
 <p>7 calumniam mendacem super mortem omnia gravia:<br></p>
 <p>8 dolor cordis et luctus, mulier zelotypa.<br></p>
 <p>9 In muliere zelotypa flagellum linguæ,<br> omnibus communicans.<br></p>
 <p>10 Sicut boum jugum quod movetur, ita et mulier nequam:<br> qui tenet illam quasi qui apprehendit scorpionem.<br></p>
 <p>11 Mulier ebriosa ira magna, et contumelia:<br> et turpitudo illius non tegetur.<br></p>
 <p>12 Fornicatio mulieris in extollentia oculorum,<br> et in palpebris illius agnoscetur.<br></p>
 <p>13 In filia non avertente se, firma custodiam,<br> ne inventa occasione utatur se.<br></p>
 <p>14 Ab omni irreverentia oculorum ejus cave,<br> et ne mireris si te neglexerit.<br></p>
 <p>15 Sicut viator sitiens ad fontem os aperiet,<br> et ab omni aqua proxima bibet,<br> et contra omnem palum sedebit,<br> et contra omnem sagittam aperiet pharetram donec deficiat.<br></p>
 <p>16 Gratia mulieris sedulæ delectabit virum suum,<br> et ossa illius impinguabit.<br></p>
 <p>17 Disciplina illius datum Dei est.<br></p>
 <p>18 Mulier sensata et tacita,<br> non est immutatio eruditæ animæ.<br></p>
 <p>19 Gratia super gratiam<br> mulier sancta et pudorata.<br></p>
 <p>20 Omnis autem ponderatio non est digna<br> continentis animæ.<br></p>
 <p>21 Sicut sol oriens mundo in altissimis Dei,<br> sic mulieris bonæ species in ornamentum domus ejus.<br></p>
 <p>22 Lucerna splendens super candelabrum sanctum,<br> et species faciei super ætatem stabilem.<br></p>
 <p>23 Columnæ aureæ super bases argenteas,<br> et pedes firmi super plantas stabilis mulieris.<br></p>
 <p>24 Fundamenta æterna supra petram solidam,<br> et mandata Dei in corde mulieris sanctæ.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>25 [In duobus contristatum est cor meum,<br> et in tertio iracundia mihi advenit:<br></p>
 <p>26 vir bellator deficiens per inopiam;<br> et vir sensatus contemptus;<br></p>
 <p>27 et qui transgreditur a justitia ad peccatum:<br> Deus paravit eum ad rhomphæam.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>28 [Duæ species difficiles et periculosæ mihi apparuerunt:<br> difficile exuitur negotians a negligentia,<br> et non justificabitur caupo a peccatis labiorum.]</p>
