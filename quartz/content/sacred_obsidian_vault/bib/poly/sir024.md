 <h3 align="CENTER">Sirach 24</h3><table border="1">
 <tr>
 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../apo/sir024.htm">Apo</a></p><p>1 Wisdom shall praise herself, and shall glory in the midst of her people. </p>
 <p>2 In the congregation of the most High shall she open her mouth, and triumph before his power. </p>
 <p>3 I came out of the mouth of the most High, and covered the earth as a cloud. </p>
 <p>4 I dwelt in high places, and my throne is in a cloudy pillar. </p>
 <p>5 I alone compassed the circuit of heaven, and walked in the bottom of the deep. </p>
 <p>6 In the waves of the sea and in all the earth, and in every people and nation, I got a possession. </p>
 <p>7 With all these I sought rest: and in whose inheritance shall I abide? </p>
 <p>8 So the Creator of all things gave me a commandment, and he that made me caused my tabernacle to rest, and said, Let thy dwelling be in Jacob, and thine inheritance in Israel. </p>
 <p>9 He created me from the beginning before the world, and I shall never fail. </p>
 <p>10 In the holy tabernacle I served before him; and so was I established in Sion. </p>
 <p>11 Likewise in the beloved city he gave me rest, and in Jerusalem was my power. </p>
 <p>12 And I took root in an honourable people, even in the portion of the Lord's inheritance. </p>
 <p>13 I was exalted like a cedar in Libanus, and as a cypress tree upon the mountains of Hermon. </p>
 <p>14 I was exalted like a palm tree in En-gaddi, and as a rose plant in Jericho, as a fair olive tree in a pleasant field, and grew up as a plane tree by the water. </p>
 <p>15 I gave a sweet smell like cinnamon and aspalathus, and I yielded a pleasant odour like the best myrrh, as galbanum, and onyx, and sweet storax, and as the fume of frankincense in the tabernacle. </p>
 <p>16 As the turpentine tree I stretched out my branches, and my branches are the branches of honour and grace. </p>
 <p>17 As the vine brought I forth pleasant savour, and my flowers are the fruit of honour and riches. </p>
 <p>18 I am the mother of fair love, and fear, and knowledge, and holy hope: I therefore, being eternal, am given to all my children which are named of him. </p>
 <p>19 Come unto me, all ye that be desirous of me, and fill yourselves with my fruits. </p>
 <p>20 For my memorial is sweeter than honey, and mine inheritance than the honeycomb. </p>
 <p>21 They that eat me shall yet be hungry, and they that drink me shall yet be thirsty. </p>
 <p>22 He that obeyeth me shall never be confounded, and they that work by me shall not do amiss. </p>
 <p>23 All these things are the book of the covenant of the most high God, even the law which Moses commanded for an heritage unto the congregations of Jacob. </p>
 <p>24 Faint not to be strong in the Lord; that he may confirm you, cleave unto him: for the Lord Almighty is God alone, and beside him there is no other Saviour. </p>
 <p>25 He filleth all things with his wisdom, as Phison and as Tigris in the time of the new fruits. </p>
 <p>26 He maketh the understanding to abound like Euphrates, and as Jordan in the time of the harvest. </p>
 <p>27 He maketh the doctrine of knowledge appear as the light, and as Geon in the time of vintage. </p>
 <p>28 The first man knew her not perfectly: no more shall the last find her out. </p>
 <p>29 For her thoughts are more than the sea, and her counsels profounder than the great deep. </p>
 <p>30 I also came out as a brook from a river, and as a conduit into a garden. </p>
 <p>31 I said, I will water my best garden, and will water abundantly my garden bed: and, lo, my brook became a river, and my river became a sea. </p>
 <p>32 I will yet make doctrine to shine as the morning, and will send forth her light afar off. </p>
 <p>33 I will yet pour out doctrine as prophecy, and leave it to all ages for ever. </p>
 <p>34 Behold that I have not laboured for myself only, but for all them that seek wisdom. </p>

 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/sir024.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 ἡ σοφία αἰνέσει ψυχὴν αὐτῆς καὶ ἐν μέσῳ λαοῦ αὐτῆς καυχήσεται </p>
 <p>2 ἐν ἐκκλησίᾳ ὑψίστου στόμα αὐτῆς ἀνοίξει καὶ ἔναντι δυνάμεως αὐτοῦ καυχήσεται </p>
 <p>3 ἐγὼ ἀπὸ στόματος ὑψίστου ἐξῆλθον καὶ ὡς ὁμίχλη κατεκάλυψα γῆν </p>
 <p>4 ἐγὼ ἐν ὑψηλοῖς κατεσκήνωσα καὶ ὁ θρόνος μου ἐν στύλῳ νεφέλης </p>
 <p>5 γῦρον οὐρανοῦ ἐκύκλωσα μόνη καὶ ἐν βάθει ἀβύσσων περιεπάτησα </p>
 <p>6 ἐν κύμασιν θαλάσσης καὶ ἐν πάσῃ τῇ γῇ καὶ ἐν παντὶ λαῷ καὶ ἔθνει ἐκτησάμην </p>
 <p>7 μετὰ τούτων πάντων ἀνάπαυσιν ἐζήτησα καὶ ἐν κληρονομίᾳ τίνος αὐλισθήσομαι </p>
 <p>8 τότε ἐνετείλατό μοι ὁ κτίστης ἁπάντων καὶ ὁ κτίσας με κατέπαυσεν τὴν σκηνήν μου καὶ εἶπεν ἐν Ιακωβ κατασκήνωσον καὶ ἐν Ισραηλ κατακληρονομήθητι </p>
 <p>9 πρὸ τοῦ αἰῶνος ἀπ᾽ ἀρχῆς ἔκτισέν με καὶ ἕως αἰῶνος οὐ μὴ ἐκλίπω </p>
 <p>10 ἐν σκηνῇ ἁγίᾳ ἐνώπιον αὐτοῦ ἐλειτούργησα καὶ οὕτως ἐν Σιων ἐστηρίχθην </p>
 <p>11 ἐν πόλει ἠγαπημένῃ ὁμοίως με κατέπαυσεν καὶ ἐν Ιερουσαλημ ἡ ἐξουσία μου </p>
 <p>12 καὶ ἐρρίζωσα ἐν λαῷ δεδοξασμένῳ ἐν μερίδι κυρίου κληρονομίας αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>13 ὡς κέδρος ἀνυψώθην ἐν τῷ Λιβάνῳ καὶ ὡς κυπάρισσος ἐν ὄρεσιν Αερμων </p>
 <p>14 ὡς φοῖνιξ ἀνυψώθην ἐν Αιγγαδοις καὶ ὡς φυτὰ ῥόδου ἐν Ιεριχω ὡς ἐλαία εὐπρεπὴς ἐν πεδίῳ καὶ ἀνυψώθην ὡς πλάτανος </p>
 <p>15 ὡς κιννάμωμον καὶ ἀσπάλαθος ἀρωμάτων δέδωκα ὀσμὴν καὶ ὡς σμύρνα ἐκλεκτὴ διέδωκα εὐωδίαν ὡς χαλβάνη καὶ ὄνυξ καὶ στακτὴ καὶ ὡς λιβάνου ἀτμὶς ἐν σκηνῇ </p>
 <p>16 ἐγὼ ὡς τερέμινθος ἐξέτεινα κλάδους μου καὶ οἱ κλάδοι μου κλάδοι δόξης καὶ χάριτος </p>
 <p>17 ἐγὼ ὡς ἄμπελος ἐβλάστησα χάριν καὶ τὰ ἄνθη μου καρπὸς δόξης καὶ πλούτου </p>
 <p>19 προσέλθετε πρός με οἱ ἐπιθυμοῦντές μου καὶ ἀπὸ τῶν γενημάτων μου ἐμπλήσθητε </p>
 <p>20 τὸ γὰρ μνημόσυνόν μου ὑπὲρ τὸ μέλι γλυκύ καὶ ἡ κληρονομία μου ὑπὲρ μέλιτος κηρίον </p>
 <p>21 οἱ ἐσθίοντές με ἔτι πεινάσουσιν καὶ οἱ πίνοντές με ἔτι διψήσουσιν </p>
 <p>22 ὁ ὑπακούων μου οὐκ αἰσχυνθήσεται καὶ οἱ ἐργαζόμενοι ἐν ἐμοὶ οὐχ ἁμαρτήσουσιν </p>
 <p>23 ταῦτα πάντα βίβλος διαθήκης θεοῦ ὑψίστου νόμον ὃν ἐνετείλατο ἡμῖν Μωυσῆς κληρονομίαν συναγωγαῖς Ιακωβ </p>
 <p>25 ὁ πιμπλῶν ὡς Φισων σοφίαν καὶ ὡς Τίγρις ἐν ἡμέραις νέων </p>
 <p>26 ὁ ἀναπληρῶν ὡς Εὐφράτης σύνεσιν καὶ ὡς Ιορδάνης ἐν ἡμέραις θερισμοῦ </p>
 <p>27 ὁ ἐκφαίνων ὡς φῶς παιδείαν ὡς Γηων ἐν ἡμέραις τρυγήτου </p>
 <p>28 οὐ συνετέλεσεν ὁ πρῶτος γνῶναι αὐτήν καὶ οὕτως ὁ ἔσχατος οὐκ ἐξιχνίασεν αὐτήν </p>
 <p>29 ἀπὸ γὰρ θαλάσσης ἐπληθύνθη διανόημα αὐτῆς καὶ ἡ βουλὴ αὐτῆς ἀπὸ ἀβύσσου μεγάλης </p>
 <p>30 κἀγὼ ὡς διῶρυξ ἀπὸ ποταμοῦ καὶ ὡς ὑδραγωγὸς ἐξῆλθον εἰς παράδεισον </p>
 <p>31 εἶπα ποτιῶ μου τὸν κῆπον καὶ μεθύσω μου τὴν πρασιάν καὶ ἰδοὺ ἐγένετό μοι ἡ διῶρυξ εἰς ποταμόν καὶ ὁ ποταμός μου ἐγένετο εἰς θάλασσαν </p>
 <p>32 ἔτι παιδείαν ὡς ὄρθρον φωτιῶ καὶ ἐκφανῶ αὐτὰ ἕως εἰς μακράν </p>
 <p>33 ἔτι διδασκαλίαν ὡς προφητείαν ἐκχεῶ καὶ καταλείψω αὐτὴν εἰς γενεὰς αἰώνων </p>
 <p>34 ἴδετε ὅτι οὐκ ἐμοὶ μόνῳ ἐκοπίασα ἀλλ᾽ ἅπασιν τοῖς ἐκζητοῦσιν αὐτήν </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/sir024.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Sapientia laudabit animam suam,<br> et in Deo honorabitur,<br> et in medio populi sui gloriabitur,<br></p>
 <p>2 et in ecclesiis Altissimi aperiet os suum,<br> et in conspectu virtutis illius gloriabitur,<br></p>
 <p>3 et in medio populi sui exaltabitur,<br> et in plenitudine sancta admirabitur,<br></p>
 <p>4 et in multitudine electorum habebit laudem,<br> et inter benedictos benedicetur, dicens:<br></p>
 <p>5 Ego ex ore Altissimi prodivi,<br> primogenita ante omnem creaturam.<br></p>
 <p>6 Ego feci in cælis ut oriretur lumen indeficiens,<br> et sicut nebula texi omnem terram.<br></p>
 <p>7 Ego in altissimis habitavi,<br> et thronus meus in columna nubis.<br></p>
 <p>8 Gyrum cæli circuivi sola,<br> et profundum abyssi penetravi:<br> in fluctibus maris ambulavi.<br></p>
 <p>9 Et in omni terra steti:<br> et in omni populo,<br></p>
 <p>10 et in omni gente primatum habui:<br></p>
 <p>11 et omnium excellentium et humilium corda virtute calcavi.<br> Et in his omnibus requiem quæsivi,<br> et in hæreditate Domini morabor.<br></p>
 <p>12 Tunc præcepit, et dixit mihi Creator omnium:<br> et qui creavit me, requievit in tabernaculo meo.<br></p>
 <p>13 Et dixit mihi: In Jacob inhabita,<br> et in Israël hæreditare,<br> et in electis meis mitte radices.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>14 [Ab initio et ante sæcula creata sum,<br> et usque ad futurum sæculum non desinam:<br> et in habitatione sancta coram ipso ministravi.<br></p>
 <p>15 Et sic in Sion firmata sum,<br> et in civitate sanctificata similiter requievi,<br> et in Jerusalem potestas mea.<br></p>
 <p>16 Et radicavi in populo honorificato,<br> et in parte Dei mei hæreditas illius,<br> et in plenitudine sanctorum detentio mea.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>17 [Quasi cedrus exaltata sum in Libano,<br> et quasi cypressus in monte Sion:<br></p>
 <p>18 quasi palma exaltata sum in Cades,<br> et quasi plantatio rosæ in Jericho:<br></p>
 <p>19 quasi oliva speciosa in campis,<br> et quasi platanus exaltata sum juxta aquam in plateis.<br></p>
 <p>20 Sicut cinnamomum et balsamum aromatizans odorem dedi;<br> quasi myrrha electa dedi suavitatem odoris:<br></p>
 <p>21 et quasi storax, et galbanus, et ungula, et gutta,<br> et quasi Libanus non incisus vaporavi habitationem meam,<br> et quasi balsamum non mistum odor meus.<br></p>
 <p>22 Ego quasi terebinthus extendi ramos meos,<br> et rami mei honoris et gratiæ.<br></p>
 <p>23 Ego quasi vitis fructificavi suavitatem odoris:<br> et flores mei fructus honoris et honestatis.<br></p>
 <p>24 Ego mater pulchræ dilectionis, et timoris,<br> et agnitionis, et sanctæ spei.<br></p>
 <p>25 In me gratia omnis viæ et veritatis:<br> in me omnis spes vitæ et virtutis.<br></p>
 <p>26 Transite ad me, omnes qui concupiscitis me,<br> et a generationibus meis implemini:<br></p>
 <p>27 spiritus enim meus super mel dulcis,<br> et hæreditas mea super mel et favum.<br></p>
 <p>28 Memoria mea in generatione sæculorum.<br></p>
 <p>29 Qui edunt me, adhuc esurient,<br> et qui bibunt me, adhuc sitient.<br></p>
 <p>30 Qui audit me non confundetur,<br> et qui operantur in me non peccabunt:<br></p>
 <p>31 qui elucidant me, vitam æternam habebunt.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>32 [Hæc omnia liber vitæ,<br> et testamentum Altissimi, et agnitio veritatis.<br></p>
 <p>33 Legem mandavit Moyses in præceptis justitiarum,<br> et hæreditatem domui Jacob,<br> et Israël promissiones.<br></p>
 <p>34 Posuit David, puero suo,<br> excitare regem ex ipso fortissimum,<br> et in throno honoris sedentem in sempiternum.<br></p>
 <p>35 Qui implet quasi Phison sapientiam,<br> et sicut Tigris in diebus novorum:<br></p>
 <p>36 qui adimplet quasi Euphrates sensum,<br> qui multiplicat quasi Jordanis in tempore messis:<br></p>
 <p>37 qui mittit disciplinam sicut lucem,<br> et assistens quasi Gehon in die vindemiæ.<br></p>
 <p>38 Qui perficit primus scire ipsam,<br> et infirmior non investigabit eam.<br></p>
 <p>39 A mari enim abundavit cogitatio ejus,<br> et consilium illius ab abysso magna.<br></p>
 <p>40 Ego sapientiam effudi flumina:<br></p>
 <p>41 ego quasi trames aquæ immensæ de fluvio:<br> ego quasi fluvii dioryx,<br> et sicut aquæductus exivi de paradiso.<br></p>
 <p>42 Dixi: Rigabo hortum meum plantationum,<br> et inebriabo prati mei fructum.<br></p>
 <p>43 Et ecce factus est mihi trames abundans,<br> et fluvius meus appropinquavit ad mare:<br></p>
 <p>44 quoniam doctrinam quasi antelucanum illumino omnibus,<br> et enarrabo illam usque ad longinquum.<br></p>
 <p>45 Penetrabo omnes inferiores partes terræ,<br> et inspiciam omnes dormientes,<br> et illuminabo omnes sperantes in Domino.<br></p>
 <p>46 Adhuc doctrinam quasi prophetiam effundam,<br> et relinquam illam quærentibus sapientiam,<br> et non desinam in progenies illorum usque in ævum sanctum.<br></p>
 <p>47 Videte quoniam non soli mihi laboravi,<br> sed omnibus exquirentibus veritatem.]</p>
