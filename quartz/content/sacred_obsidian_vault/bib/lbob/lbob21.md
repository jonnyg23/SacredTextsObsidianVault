 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Lost Books of the Bible</i>, [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">The EPISTLE of IGNATIUS to the ROMANS.</h3>  <h4 align="center">CHAP. I.</h4>  
 <p><small><i>He testifies his desire to see, and his hopes of suffering for Christ, </i>5<i> which he earnestly entreats them not to prevent, </i>10<i> but to pray for him, that God would strengthen him to the combat</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>IGNATIUS,  <a name="fr_947"></a><a href="#fn_947"><font size="1">8</font></a> who is also called Theophorus, to the church which has obtained mercy  <a name="fr_948"></a><a href="#fn_948"><font size="1">9</font></a> from the majesty of the Most High Father, and his only  <a name="fr_949"></a><a href="#fn_949"><font size="1">10</font></a> begotten Son Jesus Christ; beloved, and</p>

 <p>illuminated  <a name="fr_950"></a><a href="#fn_950"><font size="1">1</font></a> through the will of who willeth all things which are according to the love of Jesus Christ our  <a name="fr_951"></a><a href="#fn_951"><font size="1">2</font></a> God which also presides in the  <a name="fr_952"></a><a href="#fn_952"><font size="1">3</font></a> place of the region of the Romans; and which  <a name="fr_953"></a><a href="#fn_953"><font size="1">4</font></a> I salute in the name of Jesus Christ ( <a name="fr_954"></a><a href="#fn_954"><font size="1">5</font></a> as being) united both in flesh and spirit to all his commands, and  <a name="fr_955"></a><a href="#fn_955"><font size="1">6</font></a> filled with the grace of God;  <a name="fr_956"></a><a href="#fn_956"><font size="1">7</font></a> (all joy) in Jesus Christ our God.</p>  
 <p>2  <a name="fr_957"></a><a href="#fn_957"><font size="1">8</font></a> Forasmuch as I have at last  <a name="fr_958"></a><a href="#fn_958"><font size="1">9</font></a> obtained through my prayers to God, to see your  <a name="fr_959"></a><a href="#fn_959"><font size="1">10</font></a> faces,  <a name="fr_960"></a><a href="#fn_960"><font size="1">11</font></a> which I much desired to do; being bound in Jesus Christ, I hope ere long to salute you, if it shall be the will  <a name="fr_961"></a><a href="#fn_961"><font size="1">12</font></a> of God to grant me to attain unto the end I long for.</p>  
 <p>3 For the beginning is well disposed, if I shall but have grace, without hindrance, to receive  <a name="fr_962"></a><a href="#fn_962"><font size="1">13</font></a> what is appointed for me.</p>  
 <p>4 But I fear your love, lest it do me an injury. For it is easy for you to do what you please; but it  <a name="fr_963"></a><a href="#fn_963"><font size="1">14</font></a> will be hard for me to attain unto God, if you spare me.</p>  
 <p>5 But I  <a name="fr_964"></a><a href="#fn_964"><font size="1">15</font></a> would not that ye should please men, but God  <a name="fr_965"></a><a href="#fn_965"><font size="1">16</font></a> whom also ye do please. For neither shall I hereafter have such an opportunity  <a name="fr_966"></a><a href="#fn_966"><font size="1">17</font></a> of going unto God; nor will you if ye shall now be silent, ever be <a href="errata.htm#8">entitled</a> to a better work. For if you shall be silent  <a name="fr_967"></a><a href="#fn_967"><font size="1">18</font></a> in my behalf, I shall be made partaker of God.</p>  
 <p>6 But if you shall love my  <a name="fr_968"></a><a href="#fn_968"><font size="1">19</font></a> body, I shall have my course again to run. Wherefore ye cannot do me a greater kindness, than to suffer me to be sacrificed unto God, now that the altar is already prepared:</p>  
 <p>7 That  <a name="fr_969"></a><a href="#fn_969"><font size="1">20</font></a> when ye shall be gathered together in love, ye may  <a name="fr_970"></a><a href="#fn_970"><font size="1">21</font></a> give thanks to the Father through Christ Jesus; that he has vouchsafed  <a name="fr_971"></a><a href="#fn_971"><font size="1">22</font></a> to bring a bishop of Syria unto you, being called from the east unto the west.</p>  
 <p>8 For it is good for me to set from the world, unto God; that I may rise again unto him.</p>  
 <p>9 Ye have never envied any one; ye have taught other. I would therefore that ye  <a name="fr_972"></a><a href="#fn_972"><font size="1">23</font></a> should now do those things yourselves, which in your instructions you have  <a name="fr_973"></a><a href="#fn_973"><font size="1">24</font></a> prescribed to others.</p>  
 <p>10 Only pray for me, that God would give me both inward and outward strength, that I may not only say, but will; nor be only called a Christian, but be found one.</p>  
 <p>11 For if I shall be found a Christian, I may then deservedly be called one; and be thought faithful, when I shall no longer appear to the world.</p>  
 <p>12 Nothing is  <a name="fr_974"></a><a href="#fn_974"><font size="1">25</font></a> good, that is seen.</p>

 <p>13 For even our God, Jesus Christ, now that he is in the Father, does so much the more appear.</p>  
 <p>14 A Christian is not a work of  <a name="fr_975"></a><a href="#fn_975"><font size="1">1</font></a> opinion; but of greatness of mind, ( <a name="fr_976"></a><a href="#fn_976"><font size="1">2</font></a> especially when he is hated by the world.)</p>  <h4 align="center">CHAP. II.</h4>  
 <p><small><i>Expresses his great desire and determination to suffer martyrdom</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>I WRITE to the churches, and  <a name="fr_977"></a><a href="#fn_977"><font size="1">3</font></a> signify to them all, that am willing to die for God, unless  <a name="fr_978"></a><a href="#fn_978"><font size="1">4</font></a> hinder me.</p>  
 <p>2 I beseech you that you  <a name="fr_979"></a><a href="#fn_979"><font size="1">5</font></a> shew not an unseasonable good will towards me. Suffer me to be food to the wild beasts; by whom I shall attain unto God.</p>  
 <p>3 For I am the wheat of God; and I shall be ground by the teeth of the wild beasts, that I may be found the pure bread  <a name="fr_980"></a><a href="#fn_980"><font size="1">6</font></a> of Christ.</p>  
 <p>4 Rather  <a name="fr_981"></a><a href="#fn_981"><font size="1">7</font></a> encourage the beasts, that they may become my sepulchre; and may leave nothing of my body; that being dead I mazy not be troublesome to any.</p>  
 <p>5 Then shall I be truly the disciple of Jesus Christ, when the world shall not see so much as my body, Pray therefore unto Christ for me, that by these instruments I may be made the sacrifice  <a name="fr_982"></a><a href="#fn_982"><font size="1">8</font></a> of God.</p>  
 <p>6 I do not, as Peter and Paul, command you. They were Apostles, I a condemned man; they were free, but I am even to this day a servant:</p>  
 <p>7 But if I shall suffer, I shall then become the freeman of Jesus Christ, and shall rise  <a name="fr_983"></a><a href="#fn_983"><font size="1">9</font></a> free. And now, being in bonds, I learn, not to desire  <a name="fr_984"></a><a href="#fn_984"><font size="1">10</font></a> anything.</p>  
 <p>8 From Syria even unto Rome, I fight with beasts both by sea and land; both night and day: being bound to ten leopards, that is to say, to such a band of soldiers; who, though treated with all manner of kindness, are the worse for it.</p>  
 <p>9 But I am the more instructed by their injuries;  <a name="fr_985"></a><a href="#fn_985"><font size="1">11</font></a> yet am I not therefore justified.</p>  
 <p>10 May I enjoy the wild beasts that are prepared for me; which also I wish may  <a name="fr_986"></a><a href="#fn_986"><font size="1">12</font></a> exercise all their fierceness upon me.</p>  
 <p>11 And whom for that end I will  <a name="fr_987"></a><a href="#fn_987"><font size="1">13</font></a> encourage, that they may be sure to devour me, and not serve me as they have done some, whom out of fear they have not touched. But, and if they will not do it willingly, I will provoke them to it.</p>  
 <p>12 Pardon me in this matter; I know what is profitable for me. Now I begin to  <a name="fr_988"></a><a href="#fn_988"><font size="1">14</font></a> be a disciple. Nor  <a name="fr_989"></a><a href="#fn_989"><font size="1">15</font></a> shall anything move me, whether visible or invisible, that I may attain to Jesus Christ.</p>  
 <p>13 Let fire, and the cross; let the  <a name="fr_990"></a><a href="#fn_990"><font size="1">16</font></a> companies of wild beasts;  <a name="fr_991"></a><a href="#fn_991"><font size="1">17</font></a> let breakings of bones and tearing of members; let the  <a name="fr_992"></a><a href="#fn_992"><font size="1">18</font></a> shattering in pieces of the whole body, and all the wicked  <a name="fr_993"></a><a href="#fn_993"><font size="1">19</font></a> torments of the devil come upon me; only let  <a name="fr_994"></a><a href="#fn_994"><font size="1">20</font></a> me enjoy Jesus Christ.</p>

 <p>14 All the  <a name="fr_995"></a><a href="#fn_995"><font size="1">1</font></a> ends of the world, and the kingdoms  <a name="fr_996"></a><a href="#fn_996"><font size="1">2</font></a> of it, will profit me nothing: I would rather die  <a name="fr_997"></a><a href="#fn_997"><font size="1">3</font></a> for Jesus Christ, than rule to the utmost ends of the earth.  <a name="fr_998"></a><a href="#fn_998"><font size="1">4</font></a> Him I seek who died for us; him I desire, that rose again for us. This is the  <a name="fr_999"></a><a href="#fn_999"><font size="1">5</font></a> gain that is laid up for me.</p>  
 <p>15 Pardon me, my brethren, ye shall not hinder me from living.  <a name="fr_1000"></a><a href="#fn_1000"><font size="1">6</font></a> Nor seeing I desire to go to God, may you separate me from him, for the sake of this world; nor reduce me by any of the  <a name="fr_1001"></a><a href="#fn_1001"><font size="1">7</font></a> desires of it. Suffer me to  <a name="fr_1002"></a><a href="#fn_1002"><font size="1">8</font></a> enter into pure light: Where being come, I shall be indeed the  <a name="fr_1003"></a><a href="#fn_1003"><font size="1">9</font></a> servant of  <a name="fr_1004"></a><a href="#fn_1004"><font size="1">10</font></a> God.</p>  
 <p>16 Permit me to imitate the passion of my God. If any one has him within himself, let him consider what I desire; and let him have compassion on me, as knowing  <a name="fr_1005"></a><a href="#fn_1005"><font size="1">11</font></a> how I am straightened.</p>  <h4 align="center">CHAP. III.</h4>  
 <p><small>Further expresses his desire to suffer.</small></p>  
 <p>THE prince of this world would fain carry me away, and corrupt  <a name="fr_1006"></a><a href="#fn_1006"><font size="1">12</font></a> my resolution towards my God. Let none of you  <a name="fr_1007"></a><a href="#fn_1007"><font size="1">13</font></a> therefore help  <a name="fr_1008"></a><a href="#fn_1008"><font size="1">14</font></a> him: Rather do ye join with me, that is, with God.</p>  
 <p>2 Do not speak with Jesus Christ, and yet covet the world. Let not any envy dwell with you; No not though I myself when I shall be come unto you, should exhort you to it, yet do not ye hearken to me; but rather believe what I now write to you.</p>  
 <p>3 For though I am alive, at the writing this, yet my desire is to die. My love is crucified;  <a name="fr_1009"></a><a href="#fn_1009"><font size="1">15</font></a> (and the  <a name="fr_1010"></a><a href="#fn_1010"><font size="1">16</font></a> fire that is within me does not desire any water; but being alive and  <a name="fr_1011"></a><a href="#fn_1011"><font size="1">17</font></a> springing within me, says,) Come to the Father.</p>  
 <p>4 I take no pleasure in the food of corruption, nor in the pleasures of this life.</p>  
 <p>5 I desire the bread of God  <a name="fr_1012"></a><a href="#fn_1012"><font size="1">18</font></a> which is the flesh of Jesus Christ, ( <a name="fr_1013"></a><a href="#fn_1013"><font size="1">19</font></a> of the seed of David; and the drink that I long for) is his blood, which is incorruptible love. <a name="fr_1014"></a><a href="#fn_1014"><font size="1">20</font></a></p>  
 <p>6 I have no desire to live any longer after the manner of men,  <a name="fr_1015"></a><a href="#fn_1015"><font size="1">21</font></a> neither shall I, if you consent. Be ye therefore willing, that ye yourselves also may be  <a name="fr_1016"></a><a href="#fn_1016"><font size="1">22</font></a> pleasing to God. I  <a name="fr_1017"></a><a href="#fn_1017"><font size="1">23</font></a> exhort you  <a name="fr_1018"></a><a href="#fn_1018"><font size="1">24</font></a> in a few words; I pray you believe me.</p>  
 <p>7 Jesus Christ will shew you that I speak truly. My mouth is without deceit, and the Father hath truly spoken  <a name="fr_1019"></a><a href="#fn_1019"><font size="1">25</font></a> by it. Pray therefore for me, that I may accomplish what I desire.</p>  
 <p>8 I have not written to you after the flesh, but according to the will of God. If I shall suffer,  <a name="fr_1020"></a><a href="#fn_1020"><font size="1">26</font></a> ye have loved me; but if I</p>

 <p>shall be rejected,  <a name="fr_1021"></a><a href="#fn_1021"><font size="1">1</font></a> ye have hated me.</p>  
 <p>9 Remember in your prayers the church of Syria, which now enjoys God for its shepherd instead of me:  <a name="fr_1022"></a><a href="#fn_1022"><font size="1">2</font></a> Let Jesus Christ only  <a name="fr_1023"></a><a href="#fn_1023"><font size="1">3</font></a> oversee it, and your charity.</p>  
 <p>10 But I am even ashamed to be reckoned as one of them: For neither am I worthy, being the least among them, and as one  <a name="fr_1024"></a><a href="#fn_1024"><font size="1">4</font></a> born out of due season. But through mercy I have obtained to be somebody, if I shall get unto God.</p>  
 <p>11 My spirit salutes you; and the charity of the churches that have received me in the name of Jesus Christ; not as a passenger. For even they that were not near to me in the way, have gone before me to the next city to meet me.</p>  
 <p>12 These things I write to you from Smyrna, by the most worthy of the church of Ephesus.</p>  
 <p>13 There is now with me, together with many others, Crocus, most beloved of me. As for those which are  <a name="fr_1025"></a><a href="#fn_1025"><font size="1">5</font></a> come from Syria, and are gone before me to Rome, to the glory of God, I suppose you are not ignorant of them.</p>  
 <p>14 Ye shall therefore signify to them, that I draw near, for they are all worthy both of God and of you: Whom it is fit that you refresh in all things.</p>  
 <p>15 This have I written to you, the day before the ninth of the calends of September.  <a name="fr_1026"></a><a href="#fn_1026"><font size="1">6</font></a> Be strong unto the end, in the patience of Jesus Christ.  <a name="fr_1027"></a><a href="#fn_1027"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>
