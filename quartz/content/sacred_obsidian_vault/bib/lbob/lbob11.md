 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Lost Books of the Bible</i>, [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">The APOSTLES’ CREED.</h3>  
 <p>[It is affirmed by Ambrose "that the twelve Apostles, as skilful artificers assembled together, and made a key by their common advice, that is, the Creed; by which the darkness of the devil is disclosed, that the light of Christ may appear." <a name="fr_109"></a><a href="#fn_109"><font size="1">1</font></a> Others fable that every Apostle inserted an article, by which the creed is divided into twelve articles; and a sermon, fathered upon St. Austin, and quoted by the Lord Chancellor King, fabricates that each particular article was thus inserted by each particular Apostle</p>  
 <p>"<i>Peter</i>.—1. I believe in God the Father Almighty;</p>  
 <p>"<i>John</i>.—2. Maker of heaven and earth;</p>  
 <p>"<i>James</i>.—3. And in Jesus Christ his only Son, our Lord;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Andrew</i>.—4. Who was conceived by the Holy Ghost, born of the Virgin Mary;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Philip</i>.—5. Suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, dead and buried;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Thomas</i>.—6. He descended into hell, the third day he rose again from the dead;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Bartholomew</i>.—7. He ascended into heaven, sitteth at the right hand of God the Father Almighty;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Matthew</i>.—8. From thence he shall come to judge the quick and the dead;</p>  
 <p>"<i>James, the son of Alpheus</i>.—9. I believe in the Holy Ghost, the holy Catholic Church;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Simon Zelotes</i>.—10. The communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Jude the brother of James</i>.—11. The resurrection of the body;</p>  
 <p>"<i>Matthias</i>.—12. Life everlasting. Amen." <a name="fr_110"></a><a href="#fn_110"><font size="1">2</font></a></p>  
 <p>Archbishop <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Wake</span> says: "With respect to the Apostles being the authors of this Creed, it is not my intention to enter on any particular examination of this matter, which has been so fully handled, not only by the late critics of the Church of Rome, Natalia Alexander, <a name="fr_111"></a><a href="#fn_111"><font size="1">3</font></a> Du Pin, <a name="fr_112"></a><a href="#fn_112"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="green">p. 92</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> &amp;c., but yet more especially by Archbishop Usher, <a name="fr_113"></a><a href="#fn_113"><font size="1">1</font></a> Gerard Vossius, <a name="fr_114"></a><a href="#fn_114"><font size="1">2</font></a> Suicer, <a name="fr_115"></a><a href="#fn_115"><font size="1">3</font></a> Spanhemius, <a name="fr_116"></a><a href="#fn_116"><font size="1">4</font></a> Tentzelius, <a name="fr_117"></a><a href="#fn_117"><font size="1">5</font></a> and Sam. Basnage, <a name="fr_118"></a><a href="#fn_118"><font size="1">6</font></a> among the Protestants. It shall suffice to say, that as it is not likely, that had any such thing as this been done by the Apostles, St. Luke would have passed it by, without taking the least notice of it: so the diversity of Creeds in the ancient Church, and that not only in expression, but in some whole Articles too, sufficiently shows, that the Creed which we call by that name, was not composed by the twelve Apostles, much less in the same form in which it now is." <a name="fr_119"></a><a href="#fn_119"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>  
 <p>Mr. Justice <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bailey</span> says: "It is not to be understood that this Creed was framed by the Apostles, or indeed that it existed as a Creed in their time;" <a name="fr_120"></a><a href="#fn_120"><font size="1">8</font></a> and after giving the Creed as it existed in the year 600, and which is here copied from his Common Prayer Book, he says, "how long this form had existed before the year 600 is not exactly known. The additions were probably made in opposition to particular heresies and errors."</p>  
 <p>The most important "addition," since the year of Christ 600, is that which affirms, that Christ <i>descended into hell</i>. This has been proved not only to have been an invention after the Apostles’ time, but even after the time of Eusebius. Bishop Pearson says, <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_121"><font size="1">9</font></a> that the descent into hell was not in the ancient creeds or rules of faith. "It is not to be found in the rules of faith delivered by Irenæus, <a name="fr_122"></a><a href="#fn_122"><font size="1">10</font></a> by Origen, <a name="fr_123"></a><a href="#fn_123"><font size="1">11</font></a> or by Tertullian. <a name="fr_124"></a><a href="#fn_124"><font size="1">12</font></a> It is not expressed in those creeds which were made by the councils as larger explications of the Apostles’ Creed; not in the Nicene, or Constantinopolitan; not in those of Ephesus, or Chalcedon; not in those confessions made at Sardica, Antioch, Selucia, Sirmium, &amp;c. It is not mentioned in several confessions of faith delivered by particular persons; not in that of Eusebius Cæsariensis, presented to the council of Nice; <a name="fr_125"></a><a href="#fn_125"><font size="1">13</font></a> not in that of Marcellus, bishop of Ancyra, delivered to Pope Julius; <a name="fr_126"></a><a href="#fn_126"><font size="1">14</font></a> not in that of Arius and Euzoius, presented to Constantine; <a name="fr_127"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">15</font></a> not in that of Acacius, bishop of Cæsarea, delivered into the synod of Selucia; <a name="fr_128"></a><a href="#fn_128"><font size="1">16</font></a> not in that of Eustathius, Theophilus, and Sylvanus, sent to Liberius; <a name="fr_129"></a><a href="#fn_129"><font size="1">17</font></a> there is no mention of it in the creed of St. Basil; <a name="fr_130"></a><a href="#fn_130"><font size="1">18</font></a> in the creed of Epiphanus, <a name="fr_131"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">19</font></a> Gelasius, Damascus, Macarius, &amp;c. It is not in the creed expounded by St. Cyril, though some have produced that creed to prove it. It is not in the creed expounded by St. Augustine; <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_132"><font size="1">20</font></a> not in that other, <a name="fr_133"></a><a href="#fn_133"><font size="1">21</font></a> attributed to St. Augustine in another place; not in that expounded by Maximus Taurinensis; nor in that so often interpreted by Petrus Chrysologus; nor in that of the church of Antioch, delivered by Cassianus; <a name="fr_134"></a><a href="#fn_134"><font size="1">22</font></a> neither is it to be seen in the MS. creeds set forth by the learned Archbishop of Armagh. It is affirmed by Ruffinus, that in his time it was neither in the Roman nor the Oriental Creeds." <a name="fr_135"></a><a href="#fn_135"><font size="1">23</font></a></p>

 <p>"<i>Before the year </i>600<i>, it was no more than this</i>."—<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Mr. Justice Bailey</span>. p. 9 <i>n</i>.</p>  
 <p>1 <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">I Believe</span> in God the Father Almighty:</p>  
 <p>2 And in Jesus Christ his only begotten Son, our Lord;</p>  
 <p>3 Who was born of the Holy Ghost and Virgin Mary,</p>  
 <p>4 And was crucified under Pontius Pilate, and was buried;</p>  
 <p>5 And the third day rose again from the dead.</p>  
 <p>6 Ascended into heaven, sitteth on the right hand of the Father;</p>  
 <p>7 Whence he shall come to judge the quick and the dead;</p>  
 <p>8 And in the Holy Ghost;</p>  
 <p>9 The Holy Church;</p>  
 <p>10 The remission of sins;</p>  
 <p>11 And the resurrection of the flesh, Amen.</p>  
 <p><small><i>As it stands in the book of Common Prayer of the United Church of England and Ireland as by law established</i>.</small></p>  
 <p>1 <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">I Believe</span> in God the Father Almighty, maker of heaven and earth:</p>  
 <p>2 And in Jesus Christ his only Son, our Lord:</p>  
 <p>3 Who was conceived by the Holy Ghost, born of the Virgin Mary,</p>  
 <p>4 Suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, dead and buried;</p>  
 <p>5 He descended into hell;</p>  
 <p>6 The third day he rose again from the dead;</p>  
 <p>7 He ascended into heaven, and sitteth on the right hand of God the Father Almighty;</p>  
 <p>8 From thence he shall come to judge the quick and the dead.</p>  
 <p>9 ¶ I believe in the Holy Ghost;</p>  
 <p>10 The holy Catholic Church; the communion of saints;</p>  
 <p>11 The forgiveness of sins;</p>  
 <p>12 The resurrection of the body and the life everlasting, Amen. </p>
