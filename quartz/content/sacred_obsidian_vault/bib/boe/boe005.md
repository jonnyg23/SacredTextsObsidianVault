 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Enoch</i>, by R.H. Charles, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">CHAPTER II.</h3> 
 <p>1. Observe ye everything that takes place in the heaven, how they do not change their orbits, <font color="GREEN">⌈</font>and<font color="GREEN">⌉</font> the luminaries which are in the heaven, how they all rise and set in order each in its season, and transgress not against their appointed order. 2. Behold ye the earth, and give heed to the things which take place upon it from first to last, <font color="GREEN">⌈</font>how <b>steadfast</b> they are<font color="GREEN">⌉</font>, how <font color="GREEN">⌈</font>none of the things upon earth<font color="GREEN">⌉</font> change, <font color="GREEN">⌈</font>but<font color="GREEN">⌉</font> all the works of God appear <font color="GREEN">⌈</font>to you<font color="GREEN">⌉</font>. 3. Behold the summer and the winter, <font color="GREEN">⌈⌈</font>how the whole earth is filled with water, and clouds and dew and rain lie upon it<font color="GREEN">⌉⌉</font>.</p>
