 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Enoch</i>, by R.H. Charles, [1917], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">CHAPTER VIII.</h3> 
 <p>1. And Azâzêl taught men to make swords, and knives, and shields, and breastplates, and made known to them <b>the metals</b> <font color="GREEN">〈</font>of the earth<font color="GREEN">〉</font> and the art of working them, and bracelets, and ornaments, and the use of antimony, and the beautifying of the eyelids, and all kinds of costly stones, and all colouring tinctures. 2. And there arose much godlessness, and they committed fornication, and they were led astray, and became corrupt in all their ways. Semjâzâ taught enchantments, and root-cuttings, Armârôs the resolving of enchantments, Barâqîjâl,</p> 
 <p><a name="page_36"><font size="1" color="green">p. 36</font></a></p> 
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> (taught) astrology, Kôkabêl the constellations, <b>Ezêqêêl the knowledge of the clouds</b>, <font color="GREEN">〈</font>Araqiêl the signs of the earth, Shamsiêl the signs of the sun<font color="GREEN">〉</font>, and Sariêl the course of the moon. And as men perished, they cried, and their cry went up to heaven . . .</p>
