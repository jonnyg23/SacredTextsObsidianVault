 <p><a name="page_xxx"><font size="1" color="green">p. xxx</font></a></p> <h3 align="CENTER">ABBREVIATIONS, BRACKETS, AND SYMBOLS SPECIALLY USED IN THE TRANSLATION OF 1 ENOCH</h3> 
 <p>E denotes the Ethiopic Version.</p> 
 <p>G<sup>s</sup> denotes the fragments of the Greek Version preserved In Syncellus: in the case of 8<sup>b</sup>-9<sup>b</sup> there are two forms of the text, G<sup>s1</sup> G<sup>s2</sup>.</p> 
 <p>G<sup>g</sup> denotes the large fragment of the Greek Version discovered at Akhmîm, and deposited in the Gizeh Museum, Cairo.</p> 
 <p>The following brackets are used in the translation of 1 Enoch:</p> 
 <p><font color="GREEN">⌈</font> <font color="GREEN">⌉</font>. The use of these brackets means that the words so enclosed are found in G<sup>g</sup> but not In E.</p> 
 <p><font color="GREEN">⌈⌈</font> <font color="GREEN">⌉⌉</font>. The use of these brackets means that the words so enclosed are found in E but not in G<sup>g</sup> or G<sup>s</sup>.</p> 
 <p><font color="GREEN">〈</font> <font color="GREEN">〉</font>. The use of these brackets means that the words so enclosed are restored.</p> 
 <p><font color="GREEN">[</font> <font color="GREEN">]</font> The use of these brackets means that the words so enclosed are interpolations.</p> 
 <p>( ). The use of these brackets means that the words so enclosed are supplied by the editor.</p> 
 <p>The use of <b>thick type</b> denotes that the words so printed are emended.</p> 
 <p>† † corruption in the text.</p> 
 <p>. . . = some words which have been lost.</p>
