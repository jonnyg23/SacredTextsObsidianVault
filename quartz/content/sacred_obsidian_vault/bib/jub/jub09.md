 <p><a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 34</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">SHORT TITLES, ABBREVIATIONS AND BRACKETS USED IN THIS EDITION</h3>  
 <p>1 Enoch = The Ethiopic Book of Enoch.</p>  
 <p>2 Enoch = The Slavonic Book of Enoch.</p>  
 <p><i>Ap. Bar.</i> The Syriac Apocalypse of Baruch.</p>  
 <p><i>Pirḳe de R. Eliezer</i> is cited according to the edition (English translation with notes by W. O. E. Oesterley, D.D., and G. H. Box, M.A.) of G. Friedlander (London, 1916).</p>  
 <p>MT = Masoretic text.</p>  
 <p>Sam. = Samaritan version, and Hebrew text in Samaritan characters when both agree.</p>  
 <p>Syr. = the Syriac version of the Old Testament.</p>  
 <p>Vulg. = Vulgate.</p>  
 <p>( ) Words or letters so enclosed are supplied by the editor from some other source.</p>  
 <p>[ ] Words so enclosed are interpolated.</p>  
 <p>† † Words so enclosed are corrupt.</p>  
 <p>Charles's <i>Jubilees</i> = <i>The Book of Jubilees translated from the Ethiopic Text</i>, by R. H. Charles, D.D. (London, 1902).</p>  
 <p><i>JE</i> = Jewish Encyclopædia.</p>  
 <p><i>RWS<sup>2</sup></i> = <i>Religion and Worship of the Synagogue</i> (1911).</p>
