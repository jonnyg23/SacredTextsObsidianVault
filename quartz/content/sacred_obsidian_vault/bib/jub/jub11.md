 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 35</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">THE BOOK OF JUBILEES</h1>  <h3 align="center">Prologue</h3>  
 <p>THIS is the history of the division of the days <a name="fr_30"></a><a href="#fn_30"><font size="1">1</font></a> of the law and of the testimony, of the events of the years, of <a name="fr_31"></a><a href="#fn_31"><font size="1">2</font></a> their (year) weeks, of their jubilees throughout all the years of the world, <a name="fr_32"></a><a href="#fn_32"><font size="1">3</font></a> as the Lord spake to Moses on Mount Sinai when he went up to receive the tables of the law and of the commandment, according to the voice of God as He said unto him, "Go up to the top of the Mount." <a name="fr_33"></a><a href="#fn_33"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_30"></a><a href="jub11.htm#fr_30">35:1</a> The Prologue sums up the contents of the Book as at once a <i>history</i> and a <i>chronological system based upon the number seven</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_31"></a><a href="jub11.htm#fr_31">35:2</a> <i>i. e.</i> according to (their year-weeks): a year-week = seven years (cf. Lev. xxv. 8 f.).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_32"></a><a href="jub11.htm#fr_32">35:3</a> The writer apparently intended to write a history from Creation to the establishment of the Messianic Kingdom.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_33"></a><a href="jub11.htm#fr_33">35:4</a> Cf. Exod. xxiv. 12.</p>
