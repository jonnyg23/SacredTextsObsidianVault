 <p><a name="000"><b>Shoa</b></a>     Opulent, the mountain district lying to the north-east of Babylonia, anciently the land of the Guti, or Kuti, the modern Kurdistan. The plain lying between these mountains and the Tigris was called su-Edina, i.e., "the border of the plain." This name was sometimes shortened into Suti and Su, and has been regarded as = Shoa (<a href="../kjv/eze023.htm#023">Eze 23:23</a>). Some think it denotes a place in Babylon. (See PEKOD.) 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Shobab</b></a>     Apostate.     (1.) One of David's sons by Bathseheba (<a href="../kjv/sa2005.htm#014">Sa2 5:14</a>). (2.) One of the sons of Caleb (<a href="../kjv/ch1002.htm#018">Ch1 2:18</a>), the son of Hezron. 
 </p>

 <p><a name="009"><b>Shoe</b></a>     Of various forms, from the mere sandal (q.v.) to the complete covering of the foot. The word so rendered (A.V.) in <a href="../kjv/deu033.htm#025">Deu 33:25</a>,  min'al , "a bar," is derived from a root meaning "to bolt" or "shut fast," and hence a fastness or fortress. The verse has accordingly been rendered "iron and brass shall be thy fortress," or, as in the Revised Version, "thy bars [marg., "shoes"] shall be iron and brass."
