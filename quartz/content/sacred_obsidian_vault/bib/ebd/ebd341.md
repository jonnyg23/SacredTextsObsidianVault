 <p><a name="000"><b>Shomer</b></a>     Watchman.     (1.) The mother of Jehozabad, who murdered Joash (<a href="../kjv/kg2012.htm#021">Kg2 12:21</a>); called also Shimrith, a Moabitess (<a href="../kjv/ch2024.htm#026">Ch2 24:26</a>). (2.) A man of Asher (<a href="../kjv/ch1007.htm#032">Ch1 7:32</a>); called also Shamer (<a href="../kjv/ch1007.htm#034">Ch1 7:34</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Shophan</b></a>     Hidden, or hollow, a town east of Jordan (<a href="../kjv/num032.htm#035">Num 32:35</a>), built by the children of Gad. This word should probably be joined with the word preceding it in this passage, Atroth-Shophan, as in the Revised Version. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Shoshannim</b></a>     Lilies, the name of some musical instrument, probably like a lily in shape (Ps. 45; 69, title). Some think that an instrument of six strings is meant. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Shoshannim-Eduth</b></a>     In title of Ps. 80 (R.V. marg., "lilies, a testimony"), probably the name of the melody to which the psalm was to be sung. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Shrines, Silver</b></a>     Little models and medallions of the temple and image of Diana of Ephesus (<a href="../kjv/act019.htm#024">Act 19:24</a>). The manufacture of these was a very large and profitable business. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Shua</b></a>     Wealth.     (1.) A Canaanite whose daughter was married to Judah (<a href="../kjv/ch1002.htm#003">Ch1 2:3</a>). (2.) A daughter of Heber the Asherite (<a href="../kjv/ch1007.htm#032">Ch1 7:32</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Shuah</b></a>     Prostration; a pit.     (1.) One of Abraham's sons by Keturah (<a href="../kjv/gen025.htm#002">Gen 25:2</a>; <a href="../kjv/ch1001.htm#032">Ch1 1:32</a>). (2.) <a href="../kjv/ch1004.htm#011">Ch1 4:11</a>. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Shual, The Land of</b></a>     Land of the fox, a district in the tribe of Benjamin (<a href="../kjv/sa1013.htm#017">Sa1 13:17</a>); possibly the same as Shalim (<a href="../kjv/sa1009.htm#004">Sa1 9:4</a>), in the neighbourhood of Shaalabbin (<a href="../kjv/jos019.htm#042">Jos 19:42</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Shuhite</b></a>     A designation of Bildad (<a href="../kjv/job002.htm#011">Job 2:11</a>), probably because he was a descendant of Shuah. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Shulamite</b></a>     The same, as some think, with "Shunammite," from "Shunem:" otherwise, the import of the word is uncertain (<a href="../kjv/sol006.htm#013">Sol 6:13</a>; R.V., "Shulammite"). 
 </p>
