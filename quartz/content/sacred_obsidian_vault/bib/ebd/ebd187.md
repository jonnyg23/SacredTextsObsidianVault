 <p><a name="000"><b>Iim</b></a>     Ruins.     (1.) A city in the south of Judah (<a href="../kjv/jos015.htm#029">Jos 15:29</a>). (2.) One of the stations of the Israelites in the wilderness (<a href="../kjv/num033.htm#045">Num 33:45</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="001"><b>Ije-abarim</b></a>     Ruins of Abarim, the forty-seventh station of the Israelites in the wilderness, "in the border of Moab" (<a href="../kjv/num033.htm#044">Num 33:44</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="002"><b>Ijon</b></a>     A ruin, a city of Naphtali, captured by Ben-hadad of Syria at the instance of Asa (<a href="../kjv/kg1015.htm#020">Kg1 15:20</a>), and afterwards by Tiglath-pileser of Assyria (<a href="../kjv/kg2015.htm#029">Kg2 15:29</a>) in the reign of Pekah; now el-Khiam. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="003"><b>Ilai</b></a>     An Ahohite, one of David's chief warriors (<a href="../kjv/ch1011.htm#029">Ch1 11:29</a>); called also Zalmon (<a href="../kjv/sa2023.htm#028">Sa2 23:28</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="004"><b>Illyricum</b></a>     A country to the north-west of Macedonia, on the eastern shores of the Adriatic, now almost wholly comprehended in Dalmatia, a name formerly given to the southern part of Illyricum (<a href="../kjv/ti2004.htm#010">Ti2 4:10</a>). It was traversed by Paul in his third missionary journey (<a href="../kjv/rom015.htm#019">Rom 15:19</a>). It was the farthest district he had reached in preaching the gospel of Christ. This reference to Illyricum is in harmony with <a href="../kjv/act020.htm#002">Act 20:2</a>, inasmuch as the apostle's journey over the parts of Macedonia would bring him to the borders of Illyricum. 
 </p>
 <p><a name="005"><b>Imagery</b></a>     Only in the phrase "chambers of his imagery" (<a href="../kjv/eze008.htm#012">Eze 8:12</a>). (See CHAMBER.) 
 </p>
 <p><a name="006"><b>Imla</b></a>     Replenisher, the father of Micaiah the prophet (<a href="../kjv/ch2018.htm#007">Ch2 18:7</a>, <a href="../kjv/ch2018.htm#008">Ch2 18:8</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="007"><b>Immanuel</b></a>     God with us. In the Old Testament it occurs only in <a href="../kjv/isa007.htm#014">Isa 7:14</a> and <a href="../kjv/isa008.htm#008">Isa 8:8</a>. Most Christian interpreters have regarded these words as directly and exclusively a prophecy of our Saviour, an interpretation borne out by the words of the evangelist Matthew (<a href="../kjv/mat001.htm#023">Mat 1:23</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="008"><b>Immer</b></a>     Talkative.     (1.) The head of the sixteenth priestly order (<a href="../kjv/ch1024.htm#014">Ch1 24:14</a>). (2.) <a href="../kjv/jer020.htm#001">Jer 20:1</a>. (3.) <a href="../kjv/ezr002.htm#037">Ezr 2:37</a>; <a href="../kjv/neh007.htm#040">Neh 7:40</a>. (4.) <a href="../kjv/ezr002.htm#059">Ezr 2:59</a>; <a href="../kjv/neh007.htm#061">Neh 7:61</a>. (5.) The father of Zadok (<a href="../kjv/neh003.htm#029">Neh 3:29</a>). 
 </p>
 <p><a name="009"><b>Immortality</b></a>     Perpetuity of existence. The doctrine of immortality is taught in the Old Testament. It is plainly implied in the writings of Moses (<a href="../kjv/gen005.htm#022">Gen 5:22</a>, <a href="../kjv/gen005.htm#024">Gen 5:24</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen025.htm#008">Gen 25:8</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen037.htm#035">Gen 37:35</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen047.htm#009">Gen 47:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/gen049.htm#029">Gen 49:29</a>, compare <a href="../kjv/heb011.htm#013">Heb 11:13</a>; <a href="../kjv/exo003.htm#006">Exo 3:6</a>, compare <a href="../kjv/mat022.htm#023">Mat 22:23</a>). It is more clearly and fully taught in the later books (<a href="../kjv/isa014.htm#009">Isa 14:9</a>; <a href="../kjv/psa017.htm#015">Psa 17:15</a>; <a href="../kjv/psa049.htm#015">Psa 49:15</a>; <a href="../kjv/psa073.htm#024">Psa 73:24</a>). It was thus a doctrine obviously well known to the Jews. With the full revelation of the gospel this doctrine was "brought to light" (<a href="../kjv/ti2001.htm#010">Ti2 1:10</a>; 1 Cor. 15; <a href="../kjv/co2005.htm#001">Co2 5:1</a>; <a href="../kjv/th1004.htm#013">Th1 4:13</a>). 
 </p>
