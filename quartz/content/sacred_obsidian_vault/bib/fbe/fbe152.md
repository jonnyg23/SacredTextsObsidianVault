 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Forgotten Books of Eden</i>, by Rutherford H. Platt, Jr., [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">XLV.</h3> <i>
 </i><p>God shows how he does not want from men sacrifices, nor burnt-offerings, but pure and contrite hearts.</p> 
 <p>WHOEVER hastens to make offering before the Lord's face, the Lord for his part will hasten that offering by granting of his work.</p> 
 <p>2 But whoever increases his lamp before the Lord's face and make not true judgement, the Lord will <i>not</i> increase his treasure in the realm of the highest.</p> 
 <p>3 When the Lord demands bread, or candles, or flesh (<i>sc</i>. cattle), or any other sacrifice, then that is nothing; but God demands pure hearts, and with all that <i>only</i> tests the heart of man.</p>
