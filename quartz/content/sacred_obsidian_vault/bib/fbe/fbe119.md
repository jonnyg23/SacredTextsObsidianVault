 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Forgotten Books of Eden</i>, by Rutherford H. Platt, Jr., [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">XII.</h3> <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">Of the very marvellous elements of the sun.</p> 
 <p>AND I looked and saw other flying elements of the sun, whose names <i>are</i> Phoenixes and Chalkydri, marvellous and wonderful, with feet and tails in the form of a lion, and a crocodile's head, their appearance <i>is</i> empurpled, like the rainbow; their size <i>is</i> nine hundred measures, their wings <i>are like</i> those of angels, each <i>has</i> twelve, and they attend and accompany the sun, bearing heat and dew, as it is ordered them from God.</p> 
 <p>2 Thus <i>the sun</i> revolves and goes, and rises under the heaven, and its course goes under the earth with the light of its rays incessantly.</p>
