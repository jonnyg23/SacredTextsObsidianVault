 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 7</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> How beautiful are thy feet with shoes, O prince's daughter! the joints of thy thighs are like jewels, the work of the hands of a cunning workman.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Thy navel is like a round <a href="../ebd/ebd151.htm#004">goblet</a>, which wanteth not liquor: thy <a href="../ebd/ebd052.htm#000">belly</a> is like an <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#000">heap</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#000">wheat</a> set about with lilies.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Thy two breasts are like two young roes that are twins.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Thy <a href="../ebd/ebd269.htm#000">neck</a> is as a tower of <a href="../ebd/ebd192.htm#000">ivory</a>; thine eyes like the fishpools in <a href="../ebd/ebd176.htm#009">Heshbon</a>, by the <a href="../ebd/ebd143.htm#008">gate</a> of Bathrabbim: thy nose is as the tower of <a href="../ebd/ebd226.htm#001">Lebanon</a> which looketh toward <a href="../ebd/ebd096.htm#007">Damascus</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Thine head upon thee is like <a href="../ebd/ebd072.htm#006">Carmel</a>, and the hair of thine head like purple; the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a> is held in the galleries.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> How fair and how pleasant art thou, O love, for delights!<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> This thy stature is like to a palm tree, and thy breasts to clusters of grapes.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> I said, I will go up to the palm tree, I will take hold of the boughs thereof: now also thy breasts shall be as clusters of the <a href="../ebd/ebd377.htm#004">vine</a>, and the smell of thy nose like apples;<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> And the roof of thy mouth like the best <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a> for my beloved, that goeth down sweetly, causing the lips of those that are asleep to speak.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> I am my beloved's, and his desire is toward me.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Come, my beloved, let us go forth into the field; let us <a href="../ebd/ebd231.htm#002">lodge</a> in the <a href="../ebd/ebd377.htm#003">villages</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Let us get up early to the vineyards; let us see if the <a href="../ebd/ebd377.htm#004">vine</a> flourish, whether the tender <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#004">grape</a> appear, and the pomegranates bud forth: there will I give thee my loves.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> The <a href="../ebd/ebd240.htm#003">mandrakes</a> give a smell, and at our gates are all manner of pleasant fruits, new and old, which I have laid up for thee, O my beloved.<br>
 </p>
