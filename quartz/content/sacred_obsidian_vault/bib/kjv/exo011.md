 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Exodus Chapter 11</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> And the LORD said unto <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a>, Yet will I bring one <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#001">plague</a> more upon <a href="../ebd/ebd292.htm#005">Pharaoh</a>, and upon <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>; afterwards he will let you go hence: when he shall let you go, he shall surely thrust you out hence altogether.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Speak now in the ears of the people, and let every man <a href="../ebd/ebd062.htm#007">borrow</a> of his neighbour, and every woman of her neighbour, jewels of <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#000">silver</a> and jewels of <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> And the LORD gave the people favour in the sight of the Egyptians. Moreover the man <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a> was very great in the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>, in the sight of Pharaoh's servants, and in the sight of the people.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> And <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a> said, Thus saith the LORD, About midnight will I go out into the midst of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> And all the firstborn in the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a> shall die, from the first born of <a href="../ebd/ebd292.htm#005">Pharaoh</a> that sitteth upon his <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#001">throne</a>, even unto the firstborn of the maidservant that is behind the <a href="../ebd/ebd255.htm#001">mill</a>; and all the firstborn of beasts.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> And there shall be a great cry throughout all the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>, such as there was none like it, nor shall be like it any more.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> But against any of the children of <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a> shall not a dog move his tongue, against man or <a href="../ebd/ebd049.htm#001">beast</a>: that ye may know how that the LORD doth put a difference between the Egyptians and <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> And all these thy servants shall come down unto me, and <a href="../ebd/ebd063.htm#003">bow</a> down themselves unto me, saying, Get thee out, and all the people that follow thee: and after that I will go out. And he went out from <a href="../ebd/ebd292.htm#005">Pharaoh</a> in a great <a href="../ebd/ebd024.htm#002">anger</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> And the LORD said unto <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a>, <a href="../ebd/ebd292.htm#005">Pharaoh</a> shall not hearken unto you; that my wonders may be multiplied in the land of <a href="../ebd/ebd113.htm#009">Egypt</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> And <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#003">Moses</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd000.htm#001">Aaron</a> did all these wonders before <a href="../ebd/ebd292.htm#005">Pharaoh</a>: and the LORD hardened Pharaoh's heart, so that he would not let the children of <a href="../ebd/ebd191.htm#000">Israel</a> go out of his land.<br>
 </p>
