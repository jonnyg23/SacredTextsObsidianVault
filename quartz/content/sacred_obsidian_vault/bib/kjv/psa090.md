 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 90</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Lord, thou hast been our dwelling place in all generations.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Before the mountains were brought forth, or ever thou hadst formed the earth and the world, even from <a href="../ebd/ebd127.htm#007">everlasting</a> to <a href="../ebd/ebd127.htm#007">everlasting</a>, thou art God.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Thou turnest man to <a href="../ebd/ebd102.htm#004">destruction</a>; and sayest, Return, ye children of men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> For a thousand years in thy sight are but as yesterday when it is past, and as a watch in the night.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Thou carriest them away as with a <a href="../ebd/ebd136.htm#000">flood</a>; they are as a sleep: in the morning they are like <a href="../ebd/ebd154.htm#005">grass</a> which groweth up.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> In the morning it flourisheth, and groweth up; in the evening it is cut down, and withereth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> For we are consumed by thine <a href="../ebd/ebd024.htm#002">anger</a>, and by thy wrath are we troubled.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Thou hast set our iniquities before thee, our secret sins in the light of thy countenance.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> For all our days are passed away in thy wrath: we spend our years as a <a href="../ebd/ebd357.htm#008">tale</a> that is told.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> The days of our years are threescore years and ten; and if by reason of strength they be fourscore years, yet is their strength labour and sorrow; for it is soon cut off, and we <a href="../ebd/ebd136.htm#004">fly</a> away.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Who knoweth the power of thine <a href="../ebd/ebd024.htm#002">anger</a>? even according to thy fear, so is thy wrath.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> So teach us to number our days, that we may apply our hearts unto wisdom.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Return, O LORD, how long? and let it repent thee concerning thy servants.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> O satisfy us early with thy <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#005">mercy</a>; that we may rejoice and be glad all our days.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Make us glad according to the days wherein thou hast afflicted us, and the years wherein we have seen evil.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Let thy work appear unto thy servants, and thy <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a> unto their children.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> And let the beauty of the LORD our God be upon us: and establish thou the work of our hands upon us; yea, the work of our hands establish thou it.<br>
 </p>
