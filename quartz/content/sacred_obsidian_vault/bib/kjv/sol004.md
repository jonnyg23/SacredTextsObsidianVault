 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 4</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> Behold, thou art fair, my love; behold, thou art fair; thou hast doves' eyes within thy locks: thy hair is as a flock of goats, that appear from <a href="../ebd/ebd260.htm#007">mount</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd148.htm#009">Gilead</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Thy teeth are like a flock of <a href="../ebd/ebd333.htm#005">sheep</a> that are even shorn, which came up from the <a href="../ebd/ebd379.htm#002">washing</a>; whereof every one bear twins, and none is <a href="../ebd/ebd045.htm#007">barren</a> among them.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Thy lips are like a thread of <a href="../ebd/ebd323.htm#005">scarlet</a>, and thy speech is comely: thy temples are like a piece of a <a href="../ebd/ebd298.htm#000">pomegranate</a> within thy locks.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Thy <a href="../ebd/ebd269.htm#000">neck</a> is like the tower of <a href="../ebd/ebd098.htm#004">David</a> builded for an <a href="../ebd/ebd031.htm#008">armoury</a>, whereon there hang a thousand bucklers, all shields of mighty men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Thy two breasts are like two young roes that are twins, which feed among the lilies.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Until the day break, and the shadows flee away, I will get me to the mountain of <a href="../ebd/ebd263.htm#003">myrrh</a>, and to the hill of <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#004">frankincense</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Thou art all fair, my love; there is no spot in thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> Come with me from <a href="../ebd/ebd226.htm#001">Lebanon</a>, my <a href="../ebd/ebd350.htm#002">spouse</a>, with me from <a href="../ebd/ebd226.htm#001">Lebanon</a>: look from the top of <a href="../ebd/ebd019.htm#001">Amana</a>, from the top of <a href="../ebd/ebd335.htm#002">Shenir</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd175.htm#006">Hermon</a>, from the lions' dens, from the mountains of the leopards.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Thou hast ravished my heart, my sister, my <a href="../ebd/ebd350.htm#002">spouse</a>; thou hast ravished my heart with one of thine eyes, with one <a href="../ebd/ebd075.htm#008">chain</a> of thy <a href="../ebd/ebd269.htm#000">neck</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> How fair is thy love, my sister, my <a href="../ebd/ebd350.htm#002">spouse</a>! how much better is thy love than <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a>! and the smell of thine ointments than all <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#005">spices</a>!<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Thy lips, O my <a href="../ebd/ebd350.htm#002">spouse</a>, drop as the honeycomb: <a href="../ebd/ebd181.htm#001">honey</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd255.htm#000">milk</a> are under thy tongue; and the smell of thy garments is like the smell of <a href="../ebd/ebd226.htm#001">Lebanon</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> A garden inclosed is my sister, my <a href="../ebd/ebd350.htm#002">spouse</a>; a spring shut up, a <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#000">fountain</a> sealed.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Thy plants are an orchard of pomegranates, with pleasant fruits; <a href="../ebd/ebd070.htm#003">camphire</a>, with <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#008">spikenard</a>,<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#008">Spikenard</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd318.htm#005">saffron</a>; <a href="../ebd/ebd069.htm#001">calamus</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd083.htm#004">cinnamon</a>, with all trees of <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#004">frankincense</a>; <a href="../ebd/ebd263.htm#003">myrrh</a> and <a href="../ebd/ebd018.htm#004">aloes</a>, with all the chief <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#005">spices</a>:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> A <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#000">fountain</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd143.htm#001">gardens</a>, a well of living waters, and streams from <a href="../ebd/ebd226.htm#001">Lebanon</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Awake, O north wind; and come, thou south; blow upon my garden, that the <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#005">spices</a> thereof may flow out. Let my beloved come into his garden, and eat his pleasant fruits.<br>
 </p>
