 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Psalms Chapter 11</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> In the LORD put I my trust: how say ye to my soul, Flee as a <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#000">bird</a> to your mountain?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> For, lo, the wicked bend their <a href="../ebd/ebd063.htm#003">bow</a>, they make ready their arrow upon the string, that they may privily shoot at the upright in heart.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> If the foundations be destroyed, what can the righteous do?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> The LORD is in his holy <a href="../ebd/ebd361.htm#004">temple</a>, the LORD's <a href="../ebd/ebd365.htm#001">throne</a> is in <a href="../ebd/ebd170.htm#006">heaven</a>: his eyes behold, his eyelids try, the children of men.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> The LORD trieth the righteous: but the wicked and him that loveth violence his soul hateth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Upon the wicked he shall <a href="../ebd/ebd305.htm#008">rain</a> snares, fire and <a href="../ebd/ebd065.htm#005">brimstone</a>, and an horrible tempest: this shall be the portion of their <a href="../ebd/ebd094.htm#000">cup</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> For the righteous LORD loveth <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a>; his countenance doth behold the upright.<br>
 </p>
