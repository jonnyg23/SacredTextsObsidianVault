 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: James Chapter 3</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> My brethren, be not many masters, knowing that we shall receive the greater condemnation.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> For in many things we offend all. If any man offend not in word, the same is a perfect man, and able also to <a href="../ebd/ebd065.htm#002">bridle</a> the whole body.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Behold, we put bits in the horses' mouths, that they may obey us; and we turn about their whole body.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Behold also the <a href="../ebd/ebd339.htm#003">ships</a>, which though they be so great, and are driven of fierce <a href="../ebd/ebd381.htm#009">winds</a>, yet are they turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the governor listeth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> Even so the tongue is a little member, and boasteth great things. Behold, how great a matter a little fire kindleth!<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> And the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity: so is the tongue among our members, that it defileth the whole body, and setteth on fire the course of nature; and it is set on fire of <a href="../ebd/ebd173.htm#003">hell</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> For every kind of beasts, and of birds, and of serpents, and of things in the sea, is tamed, and hath been tamed of mankind:<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> But the tongue can no man tame; it is an unruly evil, full of deadly <a href="../ebd/ebd297.htm#009">poison</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> Therewith <a href="../ebd/ebd060.htm#008">bless</a> we God, even the Father; and therewith <a href="../ebd/ebd094.htm#003">curse</a> we men, which are made after the similitude of God.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> Doth a <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#000">fountain</a> send forth at the same place sweet water and <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a>?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> Can the <a href="../ebd/ebd133.htm#001">fig</a> tree, my brethren, bear <a href="../ebd/ebd277.htm#009">olive</a> berries? either a <a href="../ebd/ebd377.htm#004">vine</a>, figs? so can no <a href="../ebd/ebd138.htm#000">fountain</a> both yield <a href="../ebd/ebd319.htm#008">salt</a> water and fresh.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> Who is a <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#004">wise</a> man and endued with knowledge among you? let him shew out of a good <a href="../ebd/ebd089.htm#001">conversation</a> his works with <a href="../ebd/ebd246.htm#003">meekness</a> of wisdom.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> But if ye have <a href="../ebd/ebd059.htm#009">bitter</a> envying and strife in your hearts, <a href="../ebd/ebd150.htm#006">glory</a> not, and lie not against the truth.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> This wisdom descendeth not from above, but is earthly, sensual, devilish.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> For where envying and strife is, there is confusion and every evil work.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> But the wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, and easy to be intreated, full of <a href="../ebd/ebd249.htm#005">mercy</a> and good fruits, without partiality, and without hypocrisy.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="018">18</a> And the <a href="../ebd/ebd139.htm#000">fruit</a> of <a href="../ebd/ebd313.htm#004">righteousness</a> is sown in peace of them that make peace.<br>
 </p>
