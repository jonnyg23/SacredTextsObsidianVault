 <h3 align="CENTER">King James Version: Song of Solomon (Canticles) Chapter 1</h3><p><a name="001">1</a> The song of <a href="../ebd/ebd348.htm#002">songs</a>, which is Solomon's.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="002">2</a> Let him <a href="../ebd/ebd221.htm#000">kiss</a> me with the kisses of his mouth: for thy love is better than <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="003">3</a> Because of the savour of thy good ointments thy name is as <a href="../ebd/ebd277.htm#007">ointment</a> poured forth, therefore do the virgins love thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="004">4</a> Draw me, we will run after thee: the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a> hath brought me into his chambers: we will be glad and rejoice in thee, we will remember thy love more than <a href="../ebd/ebd382.htm#000">wine</a>: the upright love thee.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="005">5</a> I am black, but comely, O ye daughters of <a href="../ebd/ebd204.htm#005">Jerusalem</a>, as the tents of <a href="../ebd/ebd216.htm#003">Kedar</a>, as the curtains of <a href="../ebd/ebd347.htm#007">Solomon</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="006">6</a> Look not upon me, because I am black, because the <a href="../ebd/ebd353.htm#001">sun</a> hath looked upon me: my mother's children were angry with me; they made me the keeper of the vineyards; but mine own vineyard have I not kept.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="007">7</a> Tell me, O thou whom my soul loveth, where thou feedest, where thou makest thy flock to rest at noon: for why should I be as one that turneth aside by the flocks of thy companions?<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="008">8</a> If thou know not, O thou fairest among women, go thy way forth by the footsteps of the flock, and feed thy kids beside the shepherds' tents.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="009">9</a> I have compared thee, O my love, to a company of horses in Pharaoh's chariots.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="010">10</a> Thy cheeks are comely with rows of jewels, thy <a href="../ebd/ebd269.htm#000">neck</a> with chains of <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="011">11</a> We will make thee borders of <a href="../ebd/ebd152.htm#001">gold</a> with studs of <a href="../ebd/ebd344.htm#000">silver</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="012">12</a> While the <a href="../ebd/ebd219.htm#001">king</a> sitteth at his table, my <a href="../ebd/ebd349.htm#008">spikenard</a> sendeth forth the smell thereof.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="013">13</a> A bundle of <a href="../ebd/ebd263.htm#003">myrrh</a> is my well-beloved unto me; he shall lie all night betwixt my breasts.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="014">14</a> My beloved is unto me as a cluster of <a href="../ebd/ebd070.htm#003">camphire</a> in the vineyards of <a href="../ebd/ebd120.htm#009">Engedi</a>.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="015">15</a> Behold, thou art fair, my love; behold, thou art fair; thou hast doves' eyes.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="016">16</a> Behold, thou art fair, my beloved, yea, pleasant: also our bed is green.<br>
 </p>
 <p><a name="017">17</a> The beams of our house are <a href="../ebd/ebd074.htm#007">cedar</a>, and our rafters of <a href="../ebd/ebd133.htm#005">fir</a>.<br>
 </p>
