 <h3 align="CENTER">THE FOURTEENTH CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>In the next ceremony the SEM priest offered a bread-cake, of the kind called Hem, which was presented for the "lifting up of the face" of the deceased. This name is followed by the word <i>tchaut</i>, the exact meaning of which is not easy to say. In the "Hem"-cake Dümichen <a name="fr_56"></a><a href="#fn_56"><font size="1">1</font></a> thought he saw the original of a cake in use among the Egyptians which was stamped with a figure of a vanquished hippopotamus; this beast was the symbol of Set, or Typhon, as we know from the texts, and from Plutarch, <a name="fr_57"></a><a href="#fn_57"><font size="1">2</font></a> who says that he was chained. In the pictures of the chained hippopotamus <a name="fr_58"></a><a href="#fn_58"><font size="1">3</font></a> the head of the monster is</p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p>
 </p>
 <p><a name="page_73"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 73</font></a></p>
 <p>turned back," i.e., he is looking behind him, and it is possible that the name "Hem," which means "to turn back," was given to cakes because they were stamped with a figure of the animal in this attitude. The words pat tchaut Maspero renders by "Gâteau de passage," and Dümichen by "Hem-Brod wohlschmeckendes (?) the former rendering gives the better sense. The "Gâteau de passage" is the equivalent of the round bread-cake which is common all over Egypt and the</p>
 <p> </p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_07300"></a>
 <img src="img/07300.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="1">The Sem priest presenting the Hem cake</font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p> </p>
 <p>Sudan at the present day, and it is the first thing with which the native provides himself when he is about to set out on a journey. Whilst the SEM offers the <i>hem</i> cake the Kher heb says:--</p>
 <p>"Day hath made an offering unto thee in the sky.</p>
 <p>"The South and the North have caused an offering to be made unto thee.</p>
 <p>"Night hath made an offering unto thee.</p>
 <p><a name="page_74"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 74</font></a></p>
 <p>"The South and the North have made an offering unto thee.</p>
 <p>"An offering is brought unto thee. An offering thou seest, of an offering thou hearest.</p>
 <p>"There is an offering before thee, an offering behind thee, an offering with thee."</p>
