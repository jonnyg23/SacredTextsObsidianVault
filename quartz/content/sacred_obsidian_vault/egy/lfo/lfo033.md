 <h3 align="CENTER">THE THIRTY-FIFTH CEREMONY.</h3>
 <font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman"><p>In this ceremony the materials with which the eyes of the deceased are to be painted are offered. The SEM priest took a bag of Uatch and a bag of Mestemet, and as he offered them the Kher heb said:--</p>
 <p align="CENTER"><a name="img_09100"></a>
 <img src="img/09100.jpg"><br>
 </p></font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman" size="2">A bag of Uatch and a bag of Mestemet</font><font face="Times Roman,Times New Roman">.
 <p>"Osiris Unas, I have painted for thee the Eye of Horus with Mestemet so that there may be health to thy face."</p>
 <p>The eye-paint called Uatch was first used as a medicine for the eyes, and only later as an ornament; it was a preparation of copper. The eye-lids were first smeared with oil or some unguent, and the powder was</p>
 <p><a name="page_92"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 92</font></a></p>
 <p>applied to them by means of a short, thin stick made of wood, bone, or metal, which among the Arabs is called a "needle." The eye-paint Mestemet, which appears in Coptic under the form <a name="img_09200"></a>
 <img src="img/09200.jpg">, or <a name="img_09201"></a>
 <img src="img/09201.jpg">, was made from antimony, and its use was general; it was used daily, and was believed to protect the eye against ophthalmia. In modern times preparations of lead, black oxide of manganese, the lamp-black of burnt almonds, &amp;c., are commonly used as "Kohl," or eye-paint.</p>
