 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XXXV. HOW A MAN RETURNING HOME AFTER A LONG TIME FROM A FOREIGN LAND.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_05500"></a>
 <img src="img/05500.jpg"></p>  
 <p>To denote a man returning home after a long time from a foreign land they again delineate the PHŒNIX the bird: for this creature, after an interval of 580 years, <a name="fr_62"></a><a href="#fn_60"><font size="1">2</font></a> when the time of death is about to overtake him, returns to Egypt, and as soon as he pays the debt of nature in Egypt, he is mystically served with funeral rites; and whatever rites the Egyptians pay to the rest of the sacred animals, the same</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>  
 <p>are due to the Phœnix: <a name="fr_63"></a><a href="#fn_61"><font size="1">1</font></a> for it is said by the Egyptians to rejoice in the sun more than other birds, and because among them the Nile overflows through the heat of this god; of which matter we discoursed with you a short time since.</p>
