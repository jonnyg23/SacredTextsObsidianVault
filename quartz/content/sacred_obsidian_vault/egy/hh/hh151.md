 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXXVIII. HOW A MAN WHOSE TEMPERANCE IS EASILY CHANGED.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_13400"></a>
 <img src="img/13400.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man whose temperance is easily changed and inconstant</i>, they depict a BULL WITH HIS RIGHT KNEE BOUND ROUND; for if you bind him by a fetter on his right knee, you will find him follow <a name="fr_136"></a><a href="#fn_131"><font size="1">1</font></a>. And the bull is always assumed as a symbol of temperance, because it never approaches the cow after conception.</p>
