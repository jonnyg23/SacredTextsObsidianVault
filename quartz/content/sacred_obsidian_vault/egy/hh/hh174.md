 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CI. HOW A MAN WHO IS IMPUDENT AND QUICK-SIGHTED.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_14701"></a>
 <img src="img/14701.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man who is impudent</i> </p>  
 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 148</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>and quicksighted</i>, they depict A FROG; for it has no blood except in the eyes alone, and they call those who have blood in those parts impudent: and hence the poet sings, "Drunkard with eyes of dog and heart of stag."</p>
