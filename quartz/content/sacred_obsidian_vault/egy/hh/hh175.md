 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">CII. HOW A MAN UNABLE TO MOVE HIMSELF.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_14800"></a>
 <img src="img/14800.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>a man who for a long time is unable to move himself, but who afterwards moves with his feet</i>, they depict A FROG HAVING ITS HIND FEET; for it is born without feet, but subsequently as it grows acquires its hind feet.</p>
