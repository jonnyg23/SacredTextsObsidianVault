 <p><a name="page_109"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 109</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">XXXV. WHAT BY A SCORPION AND CROCODILE.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_10900"></a>
 <img src="img/10900.jpg"> <a name="fr_121"></a><a href="#fn_116"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolise <i>one enemy engaging with another equal to himself</i>, they depict a SCORPION AND A CROCODILE. For these kill one another. But if they would symbolise <i>one who is hostile to, and has slain another</i>, they depict a CROCODILE or a SCORPION; and if he has slain him <i>speedily</i>, they depict a CROCODILE, but if <i>slowly</i>, a SCORPION, from its tardy motion.</p>
