 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXXII. HOW A MAN WHO PASSES FEARLESSLY THROUGH THE EVILS WHICH ASSAIL HIM.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_13000"></a>
 <img src="img/13000.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would denote <i>a man who passes fearlessly through the evils which assail him, even until death</i>, they delineate the SKIN OF AN HYÆNA; <a name="fr_132"></a><a href="#fn_127"><font size="1">1</font></a> for if a man gird this skin about himself, and pass</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 131</font></a></p>  
 <p>through any of his enemies, he shall be injured by none of them, but passes through fearlessly.</p>
