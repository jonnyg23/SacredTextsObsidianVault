 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LVIII. HOW AN IMPOSSIBILITY.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_07900"></a>
 <img src="img/07900.jpg"> <a name="fr_87"></a><a href="#fn_85"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>To signify an <i>impossibility</i>, they represent A MAN'S FEET WALKING ON THE WATER; or when they would signify the same thing differently, they delineate A HEADLESS MAN WALKING. And since these are both impossibilities, they have with good reason selected them for this purpose.</p>
