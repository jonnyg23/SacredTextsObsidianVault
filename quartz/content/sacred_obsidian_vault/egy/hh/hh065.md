 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Hieroglyphics of Horapollo</i>, tr. Alexander Turner Cory, [1840], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">LXIII. HOW A KING WHO GOVERNS A PART OF THE WORLD.</h3>  
 <p align="center"><a name="img_08300"></a>
 <img src="img/08300.jpg"></p>  
 <p>When they would symbolize a <i>king who governs not all but a part of the world</i>, they depict HALF A SERPENT. For by this creature they denote a king, and by half of it, that he is not king over all the world.</p>
