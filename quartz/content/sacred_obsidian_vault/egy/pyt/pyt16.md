 <h3 align="center">13. A SERIES OF FIVE CHARMS, UTTERANCES 312-316.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 312</i>.</p>
 
 <p>501. To say: The bread flies, the bread flies to my houses, the houses of
 the Lower Egyptian crown.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 313</i>.</p>
 
 <p>502a. To say: The phallus of <i>Bȝ-bii</i>
 is drawn; the double doors of heaven are opened.</p>
 
 <p>502b. The double doors of heaven are locked; the way goes over the flames
 under that which the gods create,</p>
 
 <p>503a. which allows each Horus to glide through, in which N. will glide
 through, in this flame under that which the gods create.</p>
 
 <p>503b. They make a way for N., that N. may pass by it. N. is a Horus.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 314</i>.</p>
 
 <p>504a. To say: Back, thou ox, which shall be killed, on whose horns the
 fingers of the earth-god shall be.</p>
 
 <p>504b. Fall, glide away.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_108"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 108</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 315</i>.</p>
 
 <p>505a. It is N., O <i>in</i>-ape, O <i>h<u>t</u>t</i>-ape, O <i>pȝ<u>t</u><u>t</u></i>-ape.</p>
 
 <p>505b. The death (?) of N. is upon the desire of N.; the beatitude of N. (has
 come) on N. (of himself).</p>
 
 <p>505c. N. will do homage, the same homage (which ye do); he will sit among
 you, O ye <i>ḥȝ.tiw</i>.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 316</i>.</p>
 
 <p>506a. O <i>Ḥmi</i>; O <i>Śḥd</i>, N. has not given to you his
 magic.</p>
 
 <p>506b. N. will sit on the side of him who is revered in Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>506c. Take N. with (you) to heaven.</p>
