 <h3 align="center">47. THE ASCENDED KING, HIS WORKS, AND IDENTIFICATIONS, UTTERANCE 627.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 627</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1771a. To say: N. is a well-equipped spirit, who asks to be;</p>
 
 <p>1771b. heaven is agitated; the earth quakes</p>
 
 <p>1771c. ----------------------------</p>
 
 <p>1772a. N. was born on (the day of the feast) of the month; N. was conceived
 on (the day of the feast) of the half-month;</p>
 
 <p>1772b. (for) he came forth with the dorsal carapace of a grasshopper,</p>
 
 <p>1772c. as among that (of) which the wasp bore.</p>
 
 <p>1773a. The two wings --------------------</p>
 
 <p>1773b. --------- two uraeuses. N. was conceived in the night and ascends to
 Rē each day.</p>
 
 <p>1773c. The chapel is open for him (when) Rē appears.</p>
 
 <p>1774a. N. has ascended on the rain-cloud; he has descended -----</p>
 
 <p>1774b. -------------------- truth is before Rē</p>
 
 <p>1774c. on the day of (the feast) of each first-of-the-year.</p>
 
 <p>1775a. Heaven was in satisfaction; the earth was, in joy,</p>
 
 <p>1775b. (after) they heard that N. had put truth [in the place of error].</p>
 
 <p>1776a. ------ protect (or, avenge) ----- N. in his divine court</p>
 
 <p>1776b. with the true decision, which comes forth from his mouth,</p>
 
 <p>1776c. demanded his installation as chief: Two acres</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_269"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 269</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1776d. -----------------</p>
 
 <p>1777a. N. is the great falcon who asks to be;</p>
 
 <p>1777b. N. ferries over the sky on four geese (?).</p>
 
 <p>1777c. N. has ascended on the rain-cloud; he has descended ----</p>
 
 <p>1777d. -------------------</p>
 
 <p>1778a. N: is the great falcon, who is upon the battlements (or, cornice
 blocks) of the house of "him of the hidden name,"</p>
 
 <p>1778b. who will seize the (possessions, or) provisions of Atum for him who
 separates the sky from the earth and Nun</p>
 
 <p>1778c. -- this N. in all (?) ---- shines.</p>
 
 <p>1779a. His two lips are like those of the male of the divine falcons;</p>
 
 <p>1779b. his neck is like that of the mistress of the <i>nbi</i>-flame;</p>
 
 <p>1779c. his claws are like those of the bull of the evening;</p>
 
 <p>1780a. his wings are like those of him who presides over (his) abode within
 the lake of his chapel.</p>
 
 <p>1780b. The <i>ḥw</i> (taste) of N. is
 like the <i>swnw-ḥr.f-wr</i>, who is at the
 side of him who is, in Nun.</p>
 
 <p>1780c. N. was born at (or, on) the hand of eternity.</p>
 
 <p>1781a. --------------------</p>
 
 <p>1781b. N. [went?] to the field of the glorified;</p>
 
 <p>1781c. his hands fell upon <i>Dbn-wp.wt</i>
 (him of the twisted horns), north of the island of Elephantiné
 (<i>ȝbw</i>);</p>
 
 <p>1781d. he has illuminated the earth with his first divine being.</p>
 
 <p>1782a (N. I 168). To the side ----------</p>
 
 <p>1782b. ------------ the [urae]us, the gu[ide], in his first birth.</p>
 
 <p>1782c. He is busying himself with <i>śpd.w
 nwȝ.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1782d. It goes well with N. because of his <i>ba</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1783a. ------------------</p>
 
 <p>1783b. ------------------- <i>wś irmn.wt
 nfr.ś</i></p>
 
 <p>1783c. The name of N. is made like that of a divine falcon, through which he
 who passes by it fears;</p>
 
 <p>1784a. because like N. <i>Śmśw</i> is
 older than <i>nhd</i> ---</p>
 
 <p>1784b. -------</p>
 
 <p>1784c. N. goes to his seat (place?) of (in) the <i>Šsm.t</i>-land;</p>
 
 <p>1784d. that which N. eats comes from the Marshes, of Offerings</p>
 
 <p>1784e. and from the lakes of malachite -------------</p>
 
 <p>1785a (N. I 171). He --- a <i>ka</i> in
 the body of a hundred thousand ----</p>
 
 <p>1785b. N. conducts Rē into his two boats of <i>mȝ.t</i></p>
 
 <p>1785c. on the day (of the feast) of the end of the year,</p>
