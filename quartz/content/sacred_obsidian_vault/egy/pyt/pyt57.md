 <h3 align="center">54. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 677-683.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 677</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2018a. To say: A Great One is fallen on his side; he is up like a god;</p>
 
 <p>2018b. his <i>śḫm</i>-sceptre is with
 him; his white crown is upon him.</p>
 
 <p>2019a. N. is fallen on his side; N. is up like a god;</p>
 
 <p>2019b. his <i>śḫm</i>-sceptre is with
 him; his white crown is upon him, like the white crown of Rē,</p>
 
 <p>2019c. when he ascends in the horizon, and when he is greeted by Horus in
 the horizon.</p>
 
 <p>2020a. O N., raise thyself up;</p>
 
 <p>2020b. receive thy dignity, which the Two Enneads made for thee (e.g.
 "conferred upon thee").</p>
 
 <p>2021a. Thou art on the throne of Osiris, in the place of the First of the
 Westerners.</p>
 
 <p>2021b. Thou hast taken his <i>śḫm</i>-sceptre;
 thou hast carried off his great white crown.</p>
 
 <p>2022a. O N., how beautiful is this, how great is this which thy father,
 Osiris, has, done for thee!</p>
 
 <p>2022b. He gave thee his throne,</p>
 
 <p>2023a. that thou mayest rule those of secret places, that thou mayest lead
 their venerable ones,</p>
 
 <p>2023b. and that all the glorified ones may follow thee in this their name of
 "Secret places."</p>
 
 <p>2024a. O N., thou art happy; thou art proud;</p>
 
 <p>2024b. thou art an Atum; thou wilt not depart from his destiny.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_300"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 300</font></a></p>
 
 <p>2025a. Rē calls thee, in thy name of "Him whom all the glorified ones
 fear."</p>
 
 <p>2025b. Thy dread is in the hearts of the gods, like the dread of Rē of the
 horizon.</p>
 
 <p>2026a. O N., who keeps secret his form, like Anubis on his belly,</p>
 
 <p>2026b. receive thy face of a jackal; raise thyself up; stand up.</p>
 
 <p>2027a. Sit down to thy thousand (loaves) of bread, thy thousand (mugs) of
 beer, thy thousand of oxen, thy thousand of geese,</p>
 
 <p>2027b. thy thousand of every good thing whereon a god lives.</p>
 
 <p>2028a. O N., pure one, Rē finds thee standing with thy mother Nut;</p>
 
 <p>2028b. she leads thee on the ways of the horizon,</p>
 
 <p>2028c. where thou makest thine abode. How beautiful it is (to be) with thy <i>ka</i>, for ever and ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 678</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2029a. To say: <i>Iḥmti</i>, <i>Ś<u>d</u>mti</i>,</p>
 
 <p>2029b. do not hearken to N.; do not listen to N.;</p>
 
 <p>2029c. do not demand the magic of N.;</p>
 
 <p>2029d. do not ask for the magic of N. from N.</p>
 
 <p>2030a. Thou hast thy magic; N. has his magic.</p>
 
 <p>2030b. May N. not break thy pen; may he not crack thy palette!</p>
 
 <p>2030c. May N. have (his) offering!</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 679</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2031a. To say: Thy water belongs to thee; thine efflux belongs, to thee;
 thine inundation belongs to thee,</p>
 
 <p>2031b. issuing from Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>2032a. Thou makest them <i>ḫśd</i> like
 Horus; thou openest them like Wepwawet,</p>
 
 <p>2032b. for thou art the Wr, the Eldest Son.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 680</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2033. To say: Osiris N., take to thee the eye of Horus; it is thine.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 681</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2034a. To say: Great heaven, give thy hand to N.;</p>
 
 <p>2034b. great Nut, give thy hand to N.;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_301"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 301</font></a></p>
 
 <p>2034c. it is N. thy divine falcon.</p>
 
 <p>2035a. N. is come; he ascends to heaven; N. opens <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>;</p>
 
 <p>2035b. N. greets his father, Rē.</p>
 
 <p>2036a. He crowned him as a Horus, in which (form) N. comes;</p>
 
 <p>2036b. he gives to N. two real crowns;</p>
 
 <p>2036c. (and) he establishes for N. his two divine eyes.</p>
 
 <p>2037a. N. ascends to him, great, as Horus of the sky, at the zenith of
 heaven;</p>
 
 <p>2037b. he who smites the crowns of the North, who gives commands to the <i>Wtn.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2038a. N. is followed by the <i>fti.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2038b. Those in heaven and on earth come to him with salutations,</p>
 
 <p>2038c. as well as jackals, as (lit. (in) place of) Setite spirits,</p>
 
 <p>2038d. superiors and inferiors.</p>
 
 <p>2039. He is anointed with perfume, clothed with <i>pȝ-t</i>, living on offerings.</p>
 
 <p>2040a. N. commands; N. confers distinctions;</p>
 
 <p>2040b. N. awards places;</p>
 
 <p>2040c. N. makes offerings; N. conducts the presentations.</p>
 
 <p>2041. It is N.; N., is the one of heaven; he exercises power before Nut.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 682</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2042a. To say: Greetings to thee from Seker, N.</p>
 
 <p>2042b. Thy face is washed by <i>Dwȝ-wr</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2042c. N. flies as a cloud (or, high) like a divine falcon;</p>
 
 <p>2042d. N. is cool like the heron; N. flies low (?) like a <i>śmn</i>-goose.</p>
 
 <p>2043a. The wings of N. are like (those of) a divine falcon;</p>
 
 <p>2043b. the tips of the wings of N. are like (those of) a divine falcon.</p>
 
 <p>2043c. The bones of N. are fastened together; N. is purified.</p>
 
 <p>2044a. The fillet of N. is at his back; the bodice of N. is upon him;</p>
 
 <p>2044b. his girdle is of <i>šnp</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2045a. N. descends with Rē into his great boat,</p>
 
 <p>2045b. in which he transports him to the horizon to judge the gods with him.</p>
 
 <p>2046a. Horus voyages in it with him to the horizon;</p>
 
 <p>2046b. N. judges the gods with him in the horizon,</p>
 
 <p>2046c. for N. is one of them.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_302"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 302</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 683</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2047a. To say: Behold, this, is what they said to N., what the gods said to
 N.</p>
 
 <p>2047b. The word of the gods is fallen upon N.:</p>
 
 <p>2047c. "It is Horus, who comes forth from the Nile; it is the bull,
 which comes forth from the fortress;</p>
 
 <p>2047d. it is the <i><u>d</u>.t</i>-serpent
 which comes forth from Rē; it is the <i>ir.t</i>-serpent
 which comes forth from Set.</p>
 
 <p>2048a. Everything which will happen to N. happens likewise to <i>M<u>d</u>d.t-it</i>,</p>
 
 <p>2048b. daughter of Rē, who is on his two legs;</p>
 
 <p>2048c. everything which happens to N. happens likewise to <i>M<u>d</u>ȝ</i>,</p>
 
 <p>2048d. daughter of Rē, who is on his two legs,</p>
 
 <p>2049. for N. is <i>W<u>d</u>ȝ</i>, son of <i>W<u>d</u>ȝ</i>, who comes forth from <i>W<u>d</u>ȝ-t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2050a. N. is intact; N. is intact; (as true as) the eye of Horus is intact
 in Heliopolis;</p>
 
 <p>2050b. N. lives; N. lives; (as true as) the eye of Horus lives in Heliopolis."</p>
