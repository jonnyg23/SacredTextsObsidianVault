 <h3 align="center">56. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 685-689.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 685</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2063a. To say: The waters of life which are in the sky, the waters of life
 which are in the earth come.</p>
 
 <p>2063b. The sky burns for thee, the earth trembles for thee, before the birth
 of the god.</p>
 
 <p>2064a. The two mountains divide, a god comes into being, the god has power
 over his body.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_304"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 304</font></a></p>
 
 <p>2064b. The two mountains divide, N. comes into being, N. has power over his
 body.</p>
 
 <p>2065a. Behold N., his feet shall be kissed by the pure waters,</p>
 
 <p>2065b. which come into being through Atum, which the phallus of Shu makes,
 which the vulva of Tefnut brings into being.</p>
 
 <p>2066a. They have come to thee, they have brought to thee the pure waters
 which issue from their father;</p>
 
 <p>2066b. they purify thee, they fumigate thee, N., with incense.</p>
 
 <p>2067a. Thou liftest up the sky with thy hand; thou treadest (lit. layest)
 down the earth with thy foot.</p>
 
 <p>2067b. A libation is poured out at the gate of N.; the face of every god is
 washed.</p>
 
 <p>2068a. Thou washest thine arms, Osiris; thou washest thine arms N.</p>
 
 <p>2068b. Thy rejuvenescence is a god. Your third is a <i>w<u>d</u></i>-offering.</p>
 
 <p>2068c. The perfume of an <i>Iḫ.t-wt.t</i>-serpent
 is on N.</p>
 
 <p>2069a. A <i>bnbn</i>-bread is in the
 house of Seker; a leg of meat is in the house of Anubis.</p>
 
 <p>2069b. N. is intact; the <i>itr.t</i>-palace
 is standing; the month (i.e. the moon) is born; the nome lives,</p>
 
 <p>2070a. which measurements have traced. Thou tillest the barley; thou tillest
 the spelt,</p>
 
 <p>2070b. with which N. will be presented for ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 686</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2071a. To say: Horus has ointment; Set has ointment.</p>
 
 <p>2071b. Horus has taken his eye; he has taken it away from his enemies,</p>
 
 <p>2071c. without Set being a gainer thereby.</p>
 
 <p>2072a. Horus fills himself with ointment;</p>
 
 <p>2072b. Horus is satisfied with his eye; Horus is furnished with his <i>św.t</i>-plant (?).</p>
 
 <p>2072c. The eye of Horus is united with him; its perfume belongs to him.</p>
 
 <p>2072d. Its anger falls upon his enemies.</p>
 
 <p>2073a. N. has ointment; N. fills himself with it;</p>
 
 <p>2073b. its perfume unites with him;</p>
 
 <p>2073c. its anger falls upon his enemies.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_305"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 305</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 687</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2074a. To say: O N., I have come; I have brought the eye of Horus which is in
 its heat;</p>
 
 <p>2074b. its perfume belongs to thee, N.;</p>
 
 <p>2075a. its perfume belongs to thee; the perfume of the eye of Horus belongs
 to thee, N.</p>
 
 <p>2075b. Thou art a <i>ba</i> thereby; thou
 art a <i>śḫm</i> thereby; thou art
 honoured thereby.</p>
 
 <p>2075c. Thou conquerest the <i>wrr.t</i>-crown
 thereby, among the gods.</p>
 
 <p>2076a. Horus comes rejoicing at thy approach,</p>
 
 <p>2076b. as he rejoices at the approach of his eye which is upon thee.</p>
 
 <p>2076c. Behold N., who is before the gods, equipped as a god, his bones
 assembled, is like Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>2077a. The gods do homage at the approach of N.,</p>
 
 <p>2077b. as the gods do homage at the approach of the dawning of Rē when he
 ascends in the horizon.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 688</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2078a. To say: These four grandsons stand up for N.,</p>
 
 <p>2078b. <i>Ims.ti</i>, <i>Ḥȝpi</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mu.t.f</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>,</p>
 
 <p>2078c. the offspring of Horus of Letopolis.</p>
 
 <p>2079a. They bind a ladder for N.;</p>
 
 <p>2079b. they make firm a ladder for N.</p>
 
 <p>2079c. They cause N. to ascend to Khepri,</p>
 
 <p>2079d. he who exists on the eastern side of the sky.</p>
 
 <p>2080a. Its rungs are hewn by <i>Šśȝ</i>;</p>
 
 <p>2080b. the ropes which are on it are made solid</p>
 
 <p>2080c. by means of sinews of <i>Gȝśw.ti</i>,
 the bull of heaven;</p>
 
 <p>2080d. the uprights at its sides are fastened,</p>
 
 <p>2080e. like the skin of <i>Imi-wt</i>,
 son of <i>Ḥsȝ.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>2080f. the "supporter of the Great One" is set under it by <i>Śpḥ-wr.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2081a. Cause ye the <i>ka</i> of N. to
 ascend to the god;</p>
 
 <p>2081b. lead ye him to the two lions; cause him to ascend to Atum.</p>
 
 <p>2082a. Atum has done that which he said he would do for N.,</p>
 
 <p>2082b. (for) he binds the ladder for him, he makes the ladder firm for N.</p>
 
 <p>2082c. (Thus) N. is removed from the horror of mankind;</p>
 
 <p>2082d. the arms of N. are not a horror to the gods.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_306"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 306</font></a></p>
 
 <p>2083a. N. has not eaten the <i><u>d</u>3ś</i>-plant;</p>
 
 <p>2083b. N. has not chewed <i>b<u>d</u>ȝ</i>-goose
 on the first of the month;</p>
 
 <p>2083c. he has not slept during the night, (though) he did not keep watch;</p>
 
 <p>2083d. he ignores his body in one of these two seasons of Khepri.</p>
 
 <p>2084a. The inhabitants of the <i>Dȝ.t</i>
 have counted their bodies;</p>
 
 <p>2084b. they opened their ears, to the voice of N.,</p>
 
 <p>2084c. when he descends among them.</p>
 
 <p>2085a. "Heavy-is-his-sceptre" has said to them</p>
 
 <p>2085b. that N. is one of them.</p>
 
 <p>2085c. The might of N. is among them like "Great mighty one," who
 will lead to the Great West.</p>
 
 <p>2086a. The dignity of N. is great in the house of the two lions,</p>
 
 <p>2086b. for the wrong which appertains to N. is driven off by him who drives
 off evil (<i>Idr-isf.t</i>)</p>
 
 <p>2086c. from before <i>Mḫnti-irti</i> in
 Letopolis.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 689</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2087a. To say: Geb has raised the eye of Horus, which is <i>Kȝȝ</i> (or, <i>Kȝȝ.t</i>),</p>
 
 <p>2087b. which is over his great <i>kas</i>,
 which is first of his ordinary <i>kas</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2088a. Thy head (O Eye of Horus) is given (to thee), that thou mayest see
 Horus who has caused to sit ------</p>
 
 <p>2088b. so that the judgment may take place.</p>
 
 <p>2089a. Isis comes; she has laid hold of her
 breasts for her son Horus, justified.</p>
 
 <p>2089b. N. has found the eye of Horus.</p>
 
 <p>2090a. (Thou), who has found that eye of Horus,</p>
 
 <p>2090b. to which its head is given, for which a front is made, like the
 forehead of Rē, furious like a crocodile,</p>
 
 <p>2090c. thou hast followed the eye of Horus to heaven, to the <i>śḥd.w</i>-stars of the sky,</p>
 
 <p>2090d. go thou, as one who shall row Horus, with his eye.</p>
 
 <p>2091a. O Shu, thou who bearest up Nut,</p>
 
 <p>2091b. thou hast borne up the eye of Horus to heaven, to the <i>śḥd.w</i>-stars of the sky,</p>
 
 <p>2091c. because Horus sits upon his firm (or, copper; or, brilliant) throne.</p>
 
 <p>2091d. Go thou, as one who shall row Horus, with his eye.</p>
