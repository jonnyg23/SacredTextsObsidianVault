 <h3 align="center">36. THE RESURRECTION AND ASCENSION OF THE DECEASED KING, UTTERANCE 576.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 576</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1500a. To say: Osiris was placed upon his side by his brother Set;</p>
 
 <p>1500b. he who is in <i>Ndi.t</i> stirs;
 his head is raised up by Rē;</p>
 
 <p>1500c. his abomination is to sleep; he hates to be tired;</p>
 
 <p>1501a. N. rots not; he stinks not;</p>
 
 <p>1501b. N. is not bound (bewitched) by your wrath, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1502a. Awake thou in peace;</p>
 
 <p>1502b. Osiris awakes in peace; he who is in <i>Ndi.t</i> awakes in peace.</p>
 
 <p>1503a. His head is lifted up by Rē; his odour is [as] that of the <i>Iḫ.t</i>-<i>wt.t</i>-serpent.</p>
 
 <p>1503b. The head of N. also is lifted up by Rē; the odour of N. is as that
 of <i>Iḫ.t</i>-<i>wt.t</i>-serpent.</p>
 
 <p>1504a. He rots not; he stinks not,</p>
 
 <p>1504b. N. is not bound (bewitched) by your wrath, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1505a. N. is thy seed, Osiris, the pointed,</p>
 
 <p>1505b. in his name of "Horus in the great green"; "Horus
 chief of spirits."</p>
 
 <p>1506a. N. rots not; he stinks not;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 238</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1506b. he is not bound (bewitched) by your wrath, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1507a. N. goes forth from his house, adorned like Horus, bedecked like Thot;</p>
 
 <p>1507b. the mother of N. is thy Heliopolitan, O god; the father of N. is a
 Heliopolitan;</p>
 
 <p>1507c. N. himself is thy Heliopolitan, O god.</p>
 
 <p>1508a. N. is conceived by Rē; he is born of Rē.</p>
 
 <p>1508b. N. is thy seed, O Rē, the pointed,</p>
 
 <p>1508c. in his name of "Horus, chief of spirits, star which ferries over
 the "great green."</p>
 
 <p>1509a. N. rots not; he stinks not;</p>
 
 <p>1509b. he is not bound (bewitched) by your wrath, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1510a. N. is one of those four gods, born of Geb,</p>
 
 <p>1510b. who travelled over the South, who travelled over the land of [the
 North],</p>
 
 <p>1510c. who leaned upon their dm-sceptres,</p>
 
 <p>1511a. anointed with the best ointment, clothed in [purple],</p>
 
 <p>1511b. living on figs, drinking wine.</p>
 
 <p>1512 a. N. anoints himself with that with which you anoint yourselves;</p>
 
 <p>1512b. N. clothes himself with that with which you clothe yourselves;</p>
 
 <p>1512c. N. lives on that on which you live;</p>
 
 <p>1512d. N. drinks that of which you [drink].</p>
 
 <p>1513a. N. is safe with you, he lives on that on which you live.</p>
 
 <p>1513b. May you give him of those possessions which your father Geb gave you,</p>
 
 <p>1513c. (so that) because of which none of you may hunger, because of which
 none of you may rot.</p>
 
 <p>1514a. Lay hold of the arm of N. for life before the sweet-smelling ones,</p>
 
 <p>1514b. unite the bones of N., assemble his limbs,</p>
 
 <p>1514c. that N. may sit upon his throne.</p>
 
 <p>1515a. He rots not; he stinks not;</p>
 
 <p>1515b. N. is not bound (bewitched) by your wrath, O gods.</p>
 
 <p>1516a. N. is come to thee, mother of N.; he is come to Nut.</p>
 
 <p>150b. Make the sky mount for N.; place the stars upside down for him.</p>
 
 <p>1516c. Let his odour be like the odour of thy son, who is come forth from
 thee;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_239"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 239</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1516d. let the odour of N. be like that of Osiris, thy son, who is come
 forth from thee.</p>
 
 <p>1517a. Nun, lift up the arm of N. towards the sky, that he may support
 himself (on) the earth which he has given to thee,</p>
 
 <p>151 7b. that he may ascend, that he may rise to the sky,</p>
 
 <p>1517c. that he may do service of a courtier to Rē.</p>
 
 <p>1518a. Horus chief of the spirits, who is before the sweet-smelling ones,</p>
 
 <p>1518b. awake thou in peace, as Rē awakes, in peace;</p>
 
 <p>1518c. awake in peace, as <i>Mdi</i>
 awakes in peace.</p>
 
 <p>1519. Let him put the writing of N. in his register before the
 sweet-smelling ones.</p>
