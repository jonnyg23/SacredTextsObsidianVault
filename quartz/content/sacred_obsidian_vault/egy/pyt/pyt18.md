 <h3 align="center">15. OFFERINGS FOR THE DECEASED KING, UTTERANCES 338-349.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 338</i>.</p>
 
 <p>551 a. To say: Hunger, come not to N.,</p>
 
 <p>551b. go away to Nun, be off (begging) to the <i>ȝgbi</i>-flood.</p>
 
 <p>551c. N. is sated;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_115"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 115</font></a></p>
 
 <p>551d. N. hungers not by reason of that bread of Horus which he has eaten,</p>
 
 <p>551e. which his head-maid made for him, with which he is satisfied, (and)
 whereby he wins back his (normal) condition.</p>
 
 <p>552a. N. thirsts not by reason of Shu; N. hungers not by reason of Tefnut.</p>
 
 <p>552b. <i>Ḥpi</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mw.t.f</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>,
 <i>Imś.ti</i>,</p>
 
 <p>552c. they will expel this hunger, which is in the body of N.,</p>
 
 <p>552d. and this thirst which is on the lips of N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 339</i>.</p>
 
 <p>553a. To say: The hunger of N. is from the hand of Shu; the thirst of N. is
 from the hand of Tefnut.</p>
 
 <p>553b. N. lives on the morning bread, which comes at its (appointed) time.</p>
 
 <p>553c. N. lives on that on which Shu lives;</p>
 
 <p>553d. N. eats, that which Tefnut eats.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 340</i>.</p>
 
 <p>554a. N. comes to thee, <i>Nḫḫ</i>;</p>
 
 <p>554b. mayest thou fall back before N., as the east wind falls back before
 (behind?) the west wind;</p>
 
 <p>554c. mayest thou come behind N., as the north wind comes behind the south
 wind.</p>
 
 <p>554d. To say: Deposit (an offering?).</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 341</i>.</p>
 
 <p>555a. To say: The face of Horus is opened by <i>ȝkr</i>; the face of <i>ȝkr</i> is
 opened by Horus.</p>
 
 <p>555b. Abundance has extended her arm to N.;</p>
 
 <p>555c. The arms of N. have embraced fowling.</p>
 
 <p>555d. All which the marsh produces belongs to her son, <i>Ḥȝb</i>.</p>
 
 <p>555e. N. has eaten with him to-day.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 342</i>.</p>
 
 <p>556a. To say: It is N., O Isis; it is N., O <i>ȝśb.t</i>; it is N., O Nephthys.</p>
 
 <p>556b. Come, see thy son.</p>
 
 <p>556c. He has passed through the nome of Athribis, after he has passed
 through the (region of the) <i>wrr.t</i>-crown.</p>
 
 <p>557a. The handbag of N. is of <i>twn</i>-plant;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>
 
 <p>557c. N. comes; he brings what is desired and what is given.</p>
 
 <p>557b. the basket of N. is of <i>nn.t</i>-plant.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 343</i>.</p>
 
 <p>558a. To say: <i>Bdš.t</i> comes; the
 fire-pan burns.</p>
 
 <p>558b. Those with (ready) hands stand to give an offering to N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 344</i>.</p>
 
 <p>559a. To say: Greetings to thee, O Great Flood (<i>ȝgb-wr</i>),</p>
 
 <p>559b. cup-bearer of the gods, leader of men,</p>
 
 <p>559c. mayest thou make men and gods favourable to N., that they may give an
 offering to him.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 345</i>.</p>
 
 <p>560a. To say: O <i>Wr-kȝ.f</i>,</p>
 
 <p>560b. cup-bearer of Horus, chief of the dining-pavillion of Rē, chef of
 Ptaḥ,</p>
 
 <p>560c. give generously to N.; N. eats as much as thou givest.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 346</i>.</p>
 
 <p>561a. To say: Kas are in Buto; <i>kas</i>
 were in Buto as of old.</p>
 
 <p>561b. Kas will be in Buto; the <i>ka</i>
 of N. is in Buto,</p>
 
 <p>561c. red as a flame, living as Khepri.</p>
 
 <p>561d. Be cheerful, be cheerful. A meal (fit) for butchers.</p>
 
 <p>562a. It is now thou givest, my lady, love to N., veneration to N.;</p>
 
 <p>562b. it is now thou givest, my lady, veneration to N., liking to N.,</p>
 
 <p>562c. in the body of all gods.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 347</i>.</p>
 
 <p>563a. To say: The mouth of N. is in incense; the lips of N. are in myrrh.</p>
 
 <p>563b. Descend, O N., from the field of thy <i>ka</i> to the Marsh of Offerings.</p>
 
 <p>563c. of N. is from the <i>nr.t</i>; the
 meal of N. is like (that of) the divine boat.</p>
 
 <p>564a. The life of N. will be more than that of <i>Rnp.t</i>; the food of N. will be more than (that of) <i>Ḥpi</i> (the inundation).</p>
 
 <p>564b. O <i>ka</i> of N., bring (food)
 that N. may eat with thee.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 348</i>.</p>
 
 <p>565a. To say: Greeting to thee, O Great Flood,</p>
 
 <p>565b. cup-bearer of the gods, leader of men,</p>
 
 <p>565c. mayest thou make the gods favourable to N., that they may . refresh
 N.,</p>
 
 <p>565d. that they may love N., that they may render N. well.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 349</i>.</p>
 
 <p>566a. To say: O <i>Wr-kȝ.f</i>.</p>
 
 <p>566b. cup-bearer of Horus, chief of the dining-pavillion of Rē, chef of
 Ptaḥ,</p>
 
 <p>566c. give generously to N.; N. eats as much as thou givest, a generous
 portion of his meat.</p>
