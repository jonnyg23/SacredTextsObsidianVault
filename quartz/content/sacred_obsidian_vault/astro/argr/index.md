 <p>
 This is a study of star-worship by Franz Cumont.
 At the turn of the 20th century, 
 Cumont collected all available astrological and astronomical
 texts from antiquity.
 This book summarizes his knowledge and theories on this subject.
 Cumont shows that astronomical knowledge was developed
 over time in the ancient Near East, eventually allowing prediction
 of phenomena such as the location of the planets, the phases of the moon,
 and eclipses.
 This knowledge was used as the basis of a religious system
 which was integrated into Greek and Roman Paganism.
 This involved worship of the planets and stars
 and a belief that after death (if virtuous) we ascend to the heavens.
 Other aspects of ancient star-worship that are still with us are
 our seven-day week and the transference of the winter Solstice
 into the celebration of the birth of Christ.
