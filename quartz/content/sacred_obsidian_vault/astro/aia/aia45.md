 <p><i>Rules to find the Zodiacal Latitude and Longitude of a Fixed Star, Comet, Planet, or the ⊕, &amp;c. from the Right Ascension and Declination</i>.</p>  
 <p>1st. If the right ascension be less than 180°, it is <i>north</i>; and if it be more than 180°, it is <i>south</i>.</p>  
 <p>2d. To the logarithm <i>co-tangent</i> of the declination add the logarithm <i>sine</i> of the right ascension, measured from ♈ or ♎; but if measured from ♋ or ♑, the logarithm <i>co-sine</i>: the sum (minus 10 in the Index), will be the <i>log. tangent</i> of the angle A.</p>  
 <p>3d. If the right ascension and declination be both <i>north</i>, or both <i>south</i>, add 23° 28' to angle A, and it will give angle B.</p>  
 <p>4th. If the right ascension and declination be one <i>north</i> and the other <i>south</i>, the difference between 23° 28' and angle A will give angle B.</p>  
 <p><i>Note</i>.--If angle B exceed 90°, the latitude will be of the contrary name to the declination; but if angle B be less than 90°, the latitude will be of the same name as the declination.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>To find the Longitude</i>.</p>  
 <p>To the arithmetical comp. of the log. <i>sine</i> of angle A, and the log. <i>sine</i> of angle B, add the log. <i>tan</i>g. of R.A. from ♈ or ♎, (or the log. <i>co-tang</i> of R.A. from ♋ or ♑). The sum will be the log. <i>tang</i>. of the longitude from ♈ or ♎, or the log. <i>co-tang</i>. of the longitude from ♋ or ♑.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>To find the Latitude</i>.</p>  
 <p>To the arithmetical comp. of the log. <i>co-sine</i> of angle A, and the log. <i>co-sine</i> of angle B, add the log. <i>sine</i> of the declination. The sum will be the log. <i>sine</i> of the latitude.</p>  <table border="0">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="528">
