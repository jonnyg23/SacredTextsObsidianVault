 <h1 align="CENTER">PRAYER TO THE YOUNG MOON.<small>[1]</small></h1><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Young Moon!</p>
 <p>Hail, Young Moon!</p>
 <p>Hail, hail,</p>
 <p>Young Moon!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Young Moon! speak to me!</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Hail, hail,</p>
 <p>Young Moon!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Tell me of something.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>Hail, hail!</p>
 <p>When the sun rises,</p>
 <p>Thou must speak to me,</p>
 <p>That I may eat something.</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p>Thou must speak to me about a little thing,</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>That I may eat.</p>
 <p>Hail, hail,</p>
 <p>Young Moon!</p></dir>
 </dir>
 
 <p><small>[1: When (?) we see the moon <small>[<i>!nanni</i> elsewhere explained]</small>, we say <i>!ka!karrishe</i>; we sound the male antelope's horn.</small></p>
 <p>We call the small moon <i>!ka!karrishe</i>; (but) women call (it) <i>!ka!karibe</i>.]</p>
