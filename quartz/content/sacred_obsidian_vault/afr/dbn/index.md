 <p>This is a pioneering work on African-American history by the
 noted activist and scholar, W.E.B. Du Bois.
 Born in 1868, three years after the end of the Civil War,
 Du Bois lived until 1963, one year before the March on Washington.
 He was a founder of the NAACP, and worked his entire life to lift
 what he called "the Veil", or segregation.
 </p>
 <p>
 While some minor items in this book have been disproven,
 the vast majority of it still stands as one of the best books
 ever written on the subject, and an ideal supplement for any curriculum
 in world history.
 Du Bois covers a vast swath of African history, including the indigenous high
 sub-Saharan civilizations such as Zimbabwe, Ghana, and Songhai, the
 disasterous impact of centuries of the slave trade, and an overveiw 
 of Black history in the Caribbean and the United States.
 </p>
