 <center><i>NOTE:</i> </center><h3 align="CENTER">12. Grace before Meat.</h3>
 <p>The same story is told in Jekyll, 77. For the introduction, compare Tremearne, FL 21:502, and many Berber trick stories, I e. g. Basset 1:1, 3; 2:12, 18, 76, 87. A version from Henry Spence, the Bog song leader, exactly follows the Aesopic model of Grimm 75 discussed in Bolte u. Polívka 2:119-121.</p>
 <font size="2"><p>De Fox ax de cat how much trick him got. Puss say, "I have one." De Fox say him have ten time ten. So one day Dog start de Cat an' de Fox. So after</p>
 </font><p>{p. 240}</p>
 <font size="2"><p>de Cat run fe de tree, never miss de tree at all, run to de top an' sit down look upon Fox now an' de dog. An' all de trick de Fox got, de Dog ketch him.</p>
 </font><p>For Monkey's helping Tiger out of the hole and Tiger's ingratitude, compare Tremearne, FL 21:362.</p>
 <p>For the escape by saying grace, compare: Bleek, 23; Jones, 109-110; Harris, Nights, 152-153 and see number 59b.</p>
