 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Fifty Christmas Poems for Children</i>, by Florence B. Hyett, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">UNTO US A SON IS GIVEN</h3>  
 <p>GIVEN, not lent,<br> And not withdrawn--once sent--<br> This Infant of mankind, this One,<br> Is still the little welcome Son.</p>  
 <p>New every year,<br> New-born and newly dear,<br> He comes with tidings and a song,<br> The ages long, the ages long.</p>  
 <p>Even as the cold<br> Keen winter grows not old,<br> As childhood is so fresh, foreseen,<br> And spring in the familiar green.</p>  
 <p>Sudden as sweet<br> Come the expected feet;<br> All joy is young, and new all art,<br> And He, too, whom we have by heart.</p>  
 <p>--ALICE MEYNELL</p>
