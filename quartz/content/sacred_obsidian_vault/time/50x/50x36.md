 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Fifty Christmas Poems for Children</i>, by Florence B. Hyett, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">YULE-TIDE FIRES</h3>  
 <p>CLEANSE with the burning log of oak<br> The canker of thy care,<br> Deck with the scarlet-berried bough<br> The temple of the fair;<br> Spread pure-white linen for a feast,<br> Perchance some guest may share.</p>  
 <p>Give forth thy gold and silver coins,<br> For they were lent to thee;<br> Put out to usury thy dross,<br> One talent gaineth three.<br> Perchance the hungered and the poor<br> May pray to God for thee.</p>  
 <p>Once a pale star rose in the East<br> For watching herds to see,<br> And weakness came to Bethlehem,<br> And strength to Galilee.<br> Perchance! if thou dost keep thy tryst<br> A star may rise for thee.</p>  
 <p>--ANONYMOUS</p>
