 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Fifty Christmas Poems for Children</i>, by Florence B. Hyett, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">STAR OF THE EAST</h3>  
 <p>STAR of the East, that long ago<br> Brought wise men on their way<br> Where, angels singing to and fro,<br> The Child of Bethlehem lay--<br> Above that Syrian hill afar<br> Thou shinest out to-night, O Star!</p>  
 <p>Star of the East, the night were drear<br> But for the tender grace<br> That with thy glory comes to cheer<br> Earth's loneliest, darkest place;<br> For by that charity we see<br> Where there is hope for all and me.</p>  
 <p>Star of the East! show us the way<br> In wisdom undefiled<br> To seek that manger out and lay<br> Our gifts before the Child--<br> To bring our hearts and offer them<br> Unto our King in Bethlehem!</p>  
 <p>--EUGENE FIELD</p>
