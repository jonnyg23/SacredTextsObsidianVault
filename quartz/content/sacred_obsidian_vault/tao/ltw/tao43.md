 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLIII</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE FUNCTION OF THE UNIVERSAL</h3>  
 <p>The most tender things of creation race over the hardest.</p>  
 <p>A non-material existence enters into the most impenetrable.</p>  
 <p>I therefore recognize an advantage in the doctrine of not doing (wu wei) and not speaking. <a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="green">p. 34</font></a> But there are few in the world who obtain the advantage of non-assertion (wu wei) and silence.</p>
