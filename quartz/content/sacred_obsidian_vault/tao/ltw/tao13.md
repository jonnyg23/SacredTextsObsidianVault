 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XIII</h3>  <h3 align="center">LOATHING SHAME</h3>  
 <p>Favor and disgrace are alike to be feared, just as too great care or anxiety are bad for the body.</p>  
 <p>Why are favor and disgrace alike to be feared? To be favored is humiliating; to obtain it is as much to be dreaded as to lose it. To lose favor is to be in disgrace and of course is to be dreaded.</p>  
 <p>Why are excessive care and great anxiety alike bad for one? The very reason I have anxiety is because I have a body. If I have not body why would I be anxious?</p>  
 <p>Therefore if he who administers the empire, <a name="page_17"><font size="1" color="green">p. 17</font></a> esteems it as his own body, then he is worthy to be trusted with the empire.</p>
