 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/t_yy.jpg"></a><br><i>Laotzu's Tao and Wu Wei</i>, by Dwight Goddard and Henri Borel, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">XLVI</h3>  <h3 align="center">LIMITATION OF DESIRE</h3>  
 <p>When the world yields to Tao, race horses will be used to haul manure. When the world ignores Tao war horses are pastured on the public common.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="green">p. 35</font></a></p>  
 <p>There is no sin greater than desire. There is no misfortune greater than discontent. There is no calamity greater than acquisitiveness.</p>  
 <p>Therefore to know extreme contentment is simply to be content.</p>
