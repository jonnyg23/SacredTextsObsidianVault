 <h3 align="CENTER">37. ADMINISTRATION OF GOVERNMENT.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao37"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao37.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. Reason always practises non-assertion, and there is nothing that remains undone.
 <p><a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 99</font></a></p>
 <p>2. If princes and kings could keep Reason, the ten thousand creatures would of themselves be reformed. While being reformed they might yet be anxious to stir; but I would restrain them by the simplicity of the Ineffable.</p><dir>
 <dir>
 
 <p>3. "The simplicity of the unexpressed<br>
 Will purify the heart of lust.<br>
 Is there no lust there will be rest,<br>
 And all the world will thus be blest."</p></dir>
 </dir>
