 <h3 align="CENTER">CHAPTER 60.</h3>
 <p>Whatever the first sentence of this chapter may mean, it is oddly expressed.</p>
 <p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 180</font></a></p>
 <p><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font> One should govern a country as one would fry small fish, and we have added the traditional explanation in brackets, "neither gut nor scale them," which means the same as the rule <i>wei wu wei</i>, i. e., do the not-doing, practice non-practice; leave them alone and do not meddle with their affairs.</p>
 <p>In ancient times ghosts were feared, and ghosts begin to spook, or at least are believed to spook, where crimes keep the minds of the people in a state of fearful and unsettled expectancy. See Chapter 57.</p>
