 <h3 align="CENTER">46. MODERATION OF DESIRE.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao46"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao46.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. When the world possesses Reason, race horses are reserved for hauling dung. When the world is without Reason, war horses are bred in the common.
 <p>2. No greater sin than yielding to desire. No greater misery than discontent. No greater calamity than greed.</p>
 <p>3. Therefore, he who knows content's content is always content.</p>
