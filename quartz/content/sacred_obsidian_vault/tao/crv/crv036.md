 <h3 align="CENTER">30. BE CHARY OF WAR.</h3>
 <p><a name="img_tao30"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/tao30.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>1. He who with Reason assists the master of mankind will not with arms strengthen the empire. His methods invite requital.
 <p>2. Where armies are quartered briars and thorns grow. Great wars unfailingly are followed by famines. A good man acts resolutely and then stops. He ventures not to take by force.</p>
 <p>3. Be resolute but not boastful; resolute but not haughty; resolute but not arrogant; resolute because you cannot avoid it; resolute but not violent.</p>
 <p>4. Things thrive and then grow old. <a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 95</font></a> This is called un-Reason. Un-Reason soon ceases.</p>
