 <p><a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="green">p. 34</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">PART II</h1>  <h2 align="center">BEAUTY</h2>  <h3 align="center">THE MARRIAGE OF THE SOUL</h3>  
 <p>DESCENDING to the earth,<br> That strange intoxicating beauty of the unseen world<br> Lurks in the elements of Nature.</p>  
 <p>And the soul of man,<br> Who has attained the rightful balance,<br> Becoming aware of this hidden joy,<br> Straightway is enamoured and <a href="errata.htm#0">bewitched</a>.</p>  
 <p>And from this mystic marriage are born<br> The poets' songs, inner knowledge,<br> The language of the heart, virtuous living,<br> And the fair child Beauty.</p>  
 <p>And the Great Soul gives to man as dowry<br> The hidden glory of the world.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_35"><font size="1" color="green">p. 35</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">THE CHARM OF BEAUTY</h3>  
 <p>FROM the unseen world descends<br> Heavenly beauty,<br> And plants its flag in the city<br> Of earthly fairness,<br> Throwing the world's array into confusion;<br> Now riding the steed of comeliness,<br> Now flourishing the sword of eloquence,<br> And all alike bow down,<br> Saints and kings, dervishes and prophets,<br> Swayed by the charm of Beauty's fascination.</p>  <h3 align="center">EARTHLY BEAUTY</h3>  
 <p>WHENCE the charm of a fair face?<br> Not earthly beauty only<br> Can so allure us with its loveliness.<br> Perchance we see in this, as in a cloudy mirror,<br> The far faint reflect of the Perfect Face.<br> And these deep feelings of delight and wonder<br> Can only issue from the One True Beauty,<br> For in Divine Perfection there is no other partner.<br> Nor is it all desire and lust that tempts men's hearts with longing.<br> . . . Evil appears but as the other side of Truth.</p>
