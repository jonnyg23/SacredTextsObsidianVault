 <a name="page_78"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 78</font></a><h1 align="CENTER">OF THE LEARNED MAN AND THE FOOL.</h1>
 <p>The shaikh of Jurjân said to his son, "Thou must have a house in this street for thy private pursuits; and it will be well if the lock be a cunning one."</p>
 <p><a name="page_79"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 79</font></a></p>
 <p>Contrive thy finery in the path of renunciation with its head of the Law, and its secret parts of the Unity; and enter this lodging of trouble and distress like a traveller, and quickly pass on from it. At the door of the garden of <i>Except God</i> strip off and make away with thy coat and cap; become naught, that He himself, engaging thee to answer, may with justice call to thee, "<i>To whom belongs the kingdom?</i>"</p>
