 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Sadi's Scroll of Wisdom</i>, by Arthur N. Wollaston, [1906], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">DESCRIPTION OF JUSTICE</h3>  
 <p>Since God hath given thee all things to thy desire,<br> Why dost thou not eventually bring forth the fruits of justice?<br> Since justice is the adornment of royalty,<br> Why dost thou not fix thine heart upon justice?<br> Thy kingdom will be established<br> If justice cometh to thine aid.<br> Since Nushirwan <a name="fr_8"></a><a href="#fn_8"><font size="1">1</font></a>
  made choice of justice <a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="green">p. 41</font></a><br> His name is now held in remembrance for his goodness.<br> Peace befalleth the kingdom from the effects of justice,<br> Since from justice the kingdom attaineth its wishes.<br> There is no better architect in the world than justice,<br> Since nought is more excellent than righteousness.<br> Make the world populous through justice;<br> Fill the hearts of mankind with delight through justice.<br> What will be the end thereof to thee?<br> Even this, that thou wilt have the name of a righteous monarch.<br> Dost thou wish a token of good fortune?<br> Close the door of oppression against mankind;<br> Withhold not thy favour from thy subjects;<br> Gratify the desires of those who seek justice.</p>
