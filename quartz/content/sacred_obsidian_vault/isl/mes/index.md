 <p>
 In spite of the fact that the Persian Sufi master Rumi has achieved
 huge name reconition, his works took a long time to be translated
 into English.
 This was one of the first English translations of a major portion of
 Rumi's Masnavi, his largest work.
 The complete Masnavi is said to comprise 25,700 couplets.
 This translation is of the first book (of six) of the Masnavi;
 this was as far as Redhouse apparently got with his translation.
 Later, in 1898, E.H. Whinfield released an abridged translation
 of the first six books,
 also available at this site (<a href="../masnavi/index.htm">here</a>).
 The first complete translation of the Masnavi was by R.A. Nicholson,
 published in London by Luzac and Co. from 1925-40. A.J. Arberry
 also published several ground-breaking translations of
 Rumi in the mid-20th century.
 </p>
 <p>
 <cite>The Acts of the Adepts</cite>, which
 forms the first part of this book, is also notable.
 This is an abridged translation of the <cite>Menaqibu 'l Arifin</cite>,
 by the historian Eflaki.
 This is a remarkable collection of legendary stories about the early Sufis.
 </p>
 <p>
 Both of these texts appear for the first time on the Internet at sacred-texts.
 </p>
