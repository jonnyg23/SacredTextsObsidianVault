 <p><a name="page_131"><font size="1" color="green">p. 131</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">TO HIS FAVOURITE MISTRESS.</h4>  <h5 align="center">BY SAIF ADDAULET, SULTAN OF ALEPPO.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">I SAW their jealous eyeballs roll,<br>   I saw them mark each glance of mine;<br> I saw thy terrors, and my soul<br>   Shared every pang that tortured thine.  
 <p>In vain, to wean my constant heart,<br>   Or quench my glowing flame, they strove:<br> Each deep-laid scheme, each envious art,<br>   But waked my fears for her I love.</p>  
 <p>’Twas this compelled the stern decree<br>   That forced thee to those distant towers,<br> And left me nought but love for thee,<br>   To cheer my solitary hours.</p>  
 <p>Yet let not Abla sink depressed,<br>   Nor separation's pangs deplore:<br> We meet not—’tis to meet more blest;<br>   We parted—’tis to part no more.</p></div>
