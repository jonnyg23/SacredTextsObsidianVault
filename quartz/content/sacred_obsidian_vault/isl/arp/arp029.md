 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="green">p. 101</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">ON THE BATTLE OF SABLA.</h3>  <h4 align="center">BY JAAFER BEN ALBA.</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">SABLA, thou saw’st th’ exulting foe<br>   In fancied triumphs crowned;<br> Thou heard’st their frantic females throw<br>   These galling taunts around:  
 <p>Make now your <i>choice</i>—the terms we give,<br>   Desponding victims, hear:<br> These fetters on your <i>hands</i> receive,<br>   Or in your <i>hearts</i> the spear."</p>  
 <p>"And is the conflict o’er?" we cried;<br>   "And lie we at your feet?<br> And dare you vauntingly decide<br>   The fortune we must meet?</p>  
 <p>"A brighter day we soon shall see,<br>   Though now the prospect lowers;<br> And conquest, peace, and liberty<br>   Shall gild our future hours."</p>  
 <p>The foe advanced;—in firm array<br>   We rushed o’er Sabla's sands;<br> And the red sabre marked our way<br>   Amidst their yielding bands.</p>  
 <p>Then, as they writhed in Death's cold grasp,<br>   We cried, "Our <i>choice</i> is made:<br> These <i>hands</i> the sabres’ hilt shall clasp,<br>   Your <i>hearts</i> shall have the blade!"</p></div>
