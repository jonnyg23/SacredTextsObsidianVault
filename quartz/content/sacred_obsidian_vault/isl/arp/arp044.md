 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Poetry</i>, by W. A. Clouston, [1881], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">MASHDUD ON THE MONKS OF KHABBET.</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">TENANTS of yon hallowed fane!<br>   Let me your devotions share:<br> There unceasing raptures reign—<br>   None are ever sober there.  
 <p>Crowded gardens, festive bowers,<br>   Ne’er shall claim a thought of mine:<br> You can give in Khabbet's towers—<br>   Purer joys and brighter wine.</p>  
 <p>Though your pallid faces prove<br>   How you nightly vigils keep,<br> ’Tis but that you ever love<br>   Flowing goblets more than sleep.</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_118"><font size="1" color="green">p. 118</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Though your eyeballs, dim and sunk,<br>   Stream in penitential guise,<br> ’Tis but that the Hine you've drunk<br>   Bubbles over from your eyes.</div>
