 <p><a name="page_130"><font size="1" color="green">p. 130</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">ON A THUNDER-STORM.</h4>  <h5 align="center">BY IBRAHIM BEN KHIRET ABU ISAAC.</h5>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">BRIGHT smiled the morn, till o’er its head<br> The clouds in thickened foldings spread<br>     A robe of sable hue;<br> Then, gathering round Day's golden King,<br> They stretched their wide o’ershadowing wing,<br>     And hid him from our view.  
 <p>The rain his absent beams deplored,<br> And, softened into weeping, poured<br>     Its tears in many a flood;<br> The lightning laughed, with horrid glare;<br> The thunder growled, in rage; the air<br>     In silent sorrow stood.</p></div>
