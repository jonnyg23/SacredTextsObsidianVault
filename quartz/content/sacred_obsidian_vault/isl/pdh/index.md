 <h3 align="CENTER">The Poems</h3>
 <a href="pdh03.htm">I. Arise, oh Cup-bearer, rise! and bring</a><br>
 <a href="pdh04.htm">II. The bird of gardens sang unto the rose</a><br>
 <a href="pdh05.htm">III. Wind from the east, oh Lapwing of the day</a><br>
 <a href="pdh06.htm">IV. Sleep on thine eyes, bright as narcissus flowers</a><br>
 <a href="pdh07.htm">V. Oh Turkish maid of Shiraz! in thy hand</a><br>
 <a href="pdh08.htm">VI. A flower-tinted cheek, the flowery close</a><br>
 <a href="pdh09.htm">VII. From the garden of Heaven a western breeze</a><br>
 <a href="pdh10.htm">VIII. The rose has flushed red, the bud has burst</a><br>
 <a href="pdh11.htm">IX. Oh Cup-bearer, set my glass afire</a><br>
 <a href="pdh12.htm">X. Singer, sweet Singer, fresh notes strew</a><br>
 <a href="pdh13.htm">XI. Mirth, Spring, to linger in a garden fair</a><br>
 <a href="pdh14.htm">XII. Where is my ruined life, and where the fame of noble deeds?</a><br>
 <a href="pdh15.htm">XIII. Lady that hast my heart within thy hand</a><br>
 <a href="pdh16.htm">XIV. The nightingale with drops of his heart's blood</a><br>
 <a href="pdh17.htm">XV. Return! that to a heart wounded full sore</a><br>
 <a href="pdh18.htm">XVI. What is wrought in the forge of the living and life</a><br>
 <a href="pdh19.htm">XVII. Lay not reproach at the drunkard's door</a><br>
 <a href="pdh20.htm">XVIII. Slaves of thy shining eyes are even those</a><br>
 <a href="pdh21.htm">XIX. What drunkenness is this that brings me hope</a><br>
 <a href="pdh22.htm">XX. From out the street of So-and-So</a><br>
 <a href="pdh23.htm">XXI. Not all the sum of earthly happiness</a><br>
 <a href="pdh24.htm">XXII. The rose is not fair without the beloved's face</a><br>
 <a href="pdh25.htm">XXIII. My lady, that did change this house of mine</a><br>
 <a href="pdh26.htm">XXIV. Not one is filled with madness like to mine</a><br>
 <a href="pdh27.htm">XXV. The days of absence and the bitter nights</a><br>
 <a href="pdh28.htm">XXVI. The secret draught of wine and love repressed</a><br>
 <a href="pdh29.htm">XXVII. My friend has fled! alas, my friend has fled</a><br>
 <a href="pdh30.htm">XXVIII. Hast thou forgotten when thy stolen glance</a><br>
 <a href="pdh31.htm">XXIX. From Canaan Joseph shall return, whose face</a><br>
 <a href="pdh32.htm">XXX. All hail, Shiraz, hail! oh site without peer!</a><br>
 <a href="pdh33.htm">XXXI. The breath of Dawn's musk-strewing wind shall blow</a><br>
 <a href="pdh34.htm">XXXII. Upon a branch of the straight cypress-tree</a><br>
 <a href="pdh35.htm">XXXIII. The jewel of the secret treasury</a><br>
 <a href="pdh36.htm">XXXIV. Last night I dreamed that angels stood without</a><br>
 <a href="pdh37.htm">XXXV. Forget not when dear friend to friend returned</a><br>
 <a href="pdh38.htm">XXXVI. Beloved, who has bid thee ask no more</a><br>
 <a href="pdh39.htm">XXXVII. Arise! and fill a golden goblet up</a><br>
 <a href="pdh40.htm">XXXVIII. I cease not from desire till my desire</a><br>
 <a href="pdh41.htm">XXXIX. Cypress and Tulip and sweet Eglantine</a><br>
 <a href="pdh42.htm">XL. The margin of a stream, the willow's shade</a><br>
 <a href="pdh43.htm">XLI. The days of Spring are here! the eglantine</a><br>
 <a href="pdh44.htm">XLII. True love has vanished from every heart</a><br>
 <a href="pdh45.htm">XLIII. Where are the tidings of union? that I may arise</a><br>
