 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra LXXXVI.: Ṭāriq, or The Night-Visitant.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  Wa<b>al</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i wa<b>al</b><u>tta</u>riq<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>1.  By the Sky<br> And the Night-Visitant<br> (Therein);—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  Wam<u>a</u> adr<u>a</u>ka m<u>a</u> a<b>l</b><u>tta</u>riq<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>2.  And what will explain to thee<br> What the Night-Visitant is?—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  A<b>l</b>nnajmu a<b>l</b>thth<u>a</u>qib<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>3.  (It is) the Star<br> Of piercing brightness;—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  In kullu nafsin lamm<u>a</u> AAalayh<u>a</u> <u>ha</u>fi<i><u>th</u></i><b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>4.  There is no soul but has<br> A protector over it.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Falyan<i><u>th</u></i>uri al-ins<u>a</u>nu mimma khuliq<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>5.  Now let man but think<br> From what he is created!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Khuliqa min m<u>a</u>-in d<u>a</u>fiq<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  He is created from<br> A drop emitted—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Yakhruju min bayni a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>ulbi wa<b>al</b>ttar<u>a</u>-ib<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  Proceeding from between<br> The backbone and the ribs:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Innahu AAal<u>a</u> rajAAihi laq<u>a</u>dir<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  Surely (God) is able<br> To bring him back<br> (To life)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Yawma tubl<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>ssar<u>a</u>-ir<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  The Day that<br> (All) things secret<br> Will be tested,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Fam<u>a</u> lahu min quwwatin wal<u>a</u> n<u>as</u>ir<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  (Man) will have<br> No power,<br> And no helper.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Wa<b>al</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i <u>tha</u>ti a<b>l</b>rrajAA<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  By the Firmament<br> Which returns (in its round),</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>i <u>tha</u>ti a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>adAA<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  And by the Earth<br> Which opens out<br> (For the gushing of springs<br> Or the sprouting of vegetation),—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Innahu laqawlun fa<u>s</u>l<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  Behold this is the Word<br> That distinguishes (Good<br> From Evil):</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Wam<u>a</u> huwa bi<b>a</b>lhazl<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  It is not a thing<br> For amusement.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Innahum yakeedoona kayd<u>a</u><b>n</b>    </p>
 <p>15.  As for them, they<br> Are but plotting a scheme,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Waakeedu kayd<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  And I am planning<br> A scheme.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/086/086_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Famahhili alk<u>a</u>fireena amhilhum ruwayd<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  Therefore grant a delay<br> To the unbelievers:<br> Give respite to them<br> Gently (for awhile).</p>  <div class="filenav">
