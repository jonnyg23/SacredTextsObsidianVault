 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XVI.: Naḥl or The Bee.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 2</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Huwa alla<u>th</u>ee anzala mina a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i m<u>a</u>an lakum minhu shar<u>a</u>bun waminhu shajarun feehi tuseemoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  It is He Who sends down<br> Rain from the sky:<br> From it ye drink,<br> And out of it (grows)<br> The vegetation on which<br> Ye feed your cattle.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Yunbitu lakum bihi a<b>l</b>zzarAAa wa<b>al</b>zzaytoona wa<b>al</b>nnakheela wa<b>a</b>l-aAAn<u>a</u>ba wamin kulli a<b>l</b>ththamar<u>a</u>ti inna fee <u>tha</u>lika la<u>a</u>yatan liqawmin yatafakkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  With it He produces<br> For you corn, olives,<br> Date-palms, grapes,<br> And every kind of fruit:<br> Verily in this is a Sign<br> For those who give thought.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Wasakhkhara lakumu allayla wa<b>al</b>nnah<u>a</u>ra wa<b>al</b>shshamsa wa<b>a</b>lqamara wa<b>al</b>nnujoomu musakhkhar<u>a</u>tun bi-amrihi inna fee <u>tha</u>lika la<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin liqawmin yaAAqiloon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  He has made subject to you<br> The Night and the Day;<br> The Sun and the Moon;<br> And the Stars are in subjection<br> By His Command: verily<br> In this are Signs<br> For men who are wise.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Wam<u>a</u> <u>th</u>araa lakum fee al-ar<u>d</u>i mukhtalifan alw<u>a</u>nuhu inna fee <u>tha</u>lika la<u>a</u>yatan liqawmin ya<u>thth</u>akkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  And the things on this earth<br> Which He has multiplied<br> In varying colours<br> (and qualities):<br> Verily in this is a Sign<br> For men who celebrate<br> The praises of God<br> (in gratitude).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Wahuwa alla<u>th</u>ee sakhkhara alba<u>h</u>ra lita/kuloo minhu la<u>h</u>man <u>t</u>ariyyan watastakhrijoo minhu <u>h</u>ilyatan talbasoonah<u>a</u> watar<u>a</u> alfulka maw<u>a</u>khira feehi walitabtaghoo min fa<u>d</u>lihi walaAAallakum tashkuroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  It is He Who has made<br> The sea subject, that ye<br> May eat thereof flesh<br> That is fresh and tender,<br> And that ye may extract<br> Therefrom ornaments to wear;<br> And thou seest the ships<br> Therein that plough the waves,<br> That ye may seek (thus)<br> Of the bounty of God<br> And that ye may be grateful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Waalq<u>a</u> fee al-ar<u>d</u>i raw<u>a</u>siya an tameeda bikum waanh<u>a</u>ran wasubulan laAAallakum tahtadoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  And He has set up<br> On the earth mountains<br> Standing firm, lest it should<br> Shake with you; and rivers<br> And roads; that ye<br> May guide yourselves;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  WaAAal<u>a</u>m<u>a</u>tin wabi<b>al</b>nnajmi hum yahtadoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  And marks and sign-posts;<br> And by the stars<br> (Men) guide themselves.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Afaman yakhluqu kaman l<u>a</u> yakhluqu afal<u>a</u> ta<u>th</u>akkaroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  Is then He Who creates<br> Like one that creates not?<br> Will ye not receive admonition?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Wa-in taAAuddoo niAAmata All<u>a</u>hi l<u>a</u> tu<u>hs</u>ooh<u>a</u> inna All<u>a</u>ha laghafoorun ra<u>h</u>eem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  If ye would count up<br> The favours of God,<br> Never would ye be able<br> To number them: for God<br> Is Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu yaAAlamu m<u>a</u> tusirroona wam<u>a</u> tuAAlinoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  And God doth know<br> What ye conceal,<br> And what ye reveal.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena yadAAoona min dooni All<u>a</u>hi l<u>a</u> yakhluqoona shay-an wahum yukhlaqoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  Those whom they invoke<br> Besides God create nothing<br> And are themselves created.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/016/016_021.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>21.  Amw<u>a</u>tun ghayru a<u>h</u>y<u>a</u>-in wam<u>a</u> yashAAuroona ayy<u>a</u>na yubAAathoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>21.  (They are things) dead,<br> Lifeless: nor do they know<br> When they will be raised up.</p> <div class="filenav">
