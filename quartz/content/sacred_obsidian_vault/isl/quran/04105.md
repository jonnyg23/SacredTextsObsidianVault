 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XLI.: Ḥā-mīm (Abbreviated Letters), or Ḥā-Mīm Sajda, or Fuṣṣilat</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 5</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_033.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>33.  Waman a<u>h</u>sanu qawlan mimman daAA<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hi waAAamila <u>sa</u>li<u>h</u>an waq<u>a</u>la innanee mina almuslimeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>33.  Who is better in speech<br> Than one who calls (men)<br> To God, works righteousness,<br> And says, "I am of those<br> Who bow in Islam"?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_034.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>34.  Wal<u>a</u> tastawee al<u>h</u>asanatu wal<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>ssayyi-atu idfaAA bi<b>a</b>llatee hiya a<u>h</u>sanu fa-i<u>tha</u> alla<u>th</u>ee baynaka wabaynahu AAad<u>a</u>watun kaannahu waliyyun <u>h</u>ameem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>34.  Nor can Goodness and Evil<br> Be equal. Repel (Evil)<br> With what is better:<br> Then will he between whom<br> And thee was hatred<br> Become as it were<br> Thy friend and intimate!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_035.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>35.  Wam<u>a</u> yulaqq<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> ill<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena <u>s</u>abaroo wam<u>a</u> yulaqq<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> ill<u>a</u> <u>th</u>oo <u>h</u>a<i><u>thth</u></i>in AAa<i><u>th</u></i>eem<b>in</b>    </p>
 <p>35.  And no one will be<br> Granted such goodness<br> Except those who exercise<br> Patience and self-restraint,—<br> None but persons of<br> The greatest good fortune.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_036.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>36.  Wa-imm<u>a</u> yanzaghannaka mina a<b>l</b>shshay<u>ta</u>ni nazghun fa<b>i</b>staAAi<u>th</u> bi<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hi innahu huwa a<b>l</b>ssameeAAu alAAaleem<b>u </b>   </p>
 <p>36.  And if (at any time)<br> An incitement to discord<br> Is made to thee<br> By the Evil One,<br> Seek refuge in God.<br> He is the One<br> Who hears and knows<br> All things.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_037.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>37.  Wamin <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tihi allaylu wa<b>al</b>nnah<u>a</u>ru wa<b>al</b>shshamsu wa<b>a</b>lqamaru l<u>a</u> tasjudoo li<b>l</b>shshamsi wal<u>a</u> lilqamari wa<b>o</b>sjudoo lill<u>a</u>hi ala<u>th</u>ee khalaqahunna in kuntum iyy<u>a</u>hu taAAbudoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>37.  Among His Signs are<br> The Night and the Day,<br> And the Sun and the Moon.<br> Adore not the sun<br> And the moon, but adore<br> God, Who created them,<br> If it is. Him ye wish<br> To serve.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_038.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>38.  Fa-ini istakbaroo fa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena AAinda rabbika yusabbi<u>h</u>oona lahu bi<b>a</b>llayli wa<b>al</b>nnah<u>a</u>ri wahum l<u>a</u> yas-amoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>38.  But if the (Unbelievers)<br> Are arrogant, (no matter):<br> For in the presence<br> Of thy Lord are those<br> Who celebrate His praises<br> By night and by day.<br> And they never flag<br> (Nor feel themselves<br> Above it).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_039.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>39.  Wamin <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tihi annaka tar<u>a</u> al-ar<u>d</u>a kh<u>a</u>shiAAatan fa-i<u>tha</u> anzaln<u>a</u> AAalayh<u>a</u> alm<u>a</u>a ihtazzat warabat inna alla<u>th</u>ee a<u>h</u>y<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> lamu<u>h</u>yee almawt<u>a</u> innahu AAal<u>a</u> kulli shay-in qadeer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>39.  And among His Signs<br> In this: thou seest<br> The earth barren and desolate;<br> But when We send down<br> Rain to it, it is stirred<br> To life and yields increase.<br> Truly, He Who gives life<br> To the (dead) earth<br> Can surely give life<br> To (men) who are dead.<br> For He has power<br> Over all things.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_040.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>40.  Inna alla<u>th</u>eena yul<u>h</u>idoona fee <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin<u>a</u> l<u>a</u> yakhfawna AAalayn<u>a</u> afaman yulq<u>a</u> fee a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri khayrun amman ya/tee <u>a</u>minan yawma alqiy<u>a</u>mati iAAmaloo m<u>a</u> shi/tum innahu bim<u>a</u> taAAmaloona ba<u>s</u>eer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>40.  Those who pervert<br> The Truth in Our Signs<br> Are not hidden from Us.<br> Which is better?—he that<br> Is cast into the Fire,<br> Or he that comes safe through,<br> On the Day of Judgment?<br> Do what ye will:<br> Verily He seeth (clearly)<br> All that ye do.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_041.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>41.  Inna alla<u>th</u>eena kafaroo bi<b>al</b><u>thth</u>ikri lamm<u>a</u> j<u>a</u>ahum wa-innahu lakit<u>a</u>bun AAazeez<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>41.  Those who reject the Message<br> When it comes to them<br>(Are not hidden from Us).<br> And indeed it is a Book<br> Of exalted power.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_042.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>42.  L<u>a</u> ya/teehi alb<u>at</u>ilu min bayni yadayhi wal<u>a</u> min khalfihi tanzeelun min <u>h</u>akeemin <u>h</u>ameed<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>42.  No falsehood can approach it<br> From before or behind it:<br> It is sent down<br> By One Full of Wisdom,<br> Worthy of all Praise.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_043.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>43.  M<u>a</u> yuq<u>a</u>lu laka ill<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> qad qeela li<b>l</b>rrusuli min qablika inna rabbaka la<u>th</u>oo maghfiratin wa<u>th</u>oo AAiq<u>a</u>bin aleem<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>43.  Nothing is said to thee<br> That was not said<br> To the apostles before thee:<br> That thy Lord has<br> At His command (all) Forgiveness<br> As well as a most<br> Grievous Penalty.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/041/041_044.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>44.  Walaw jaAAaln<u>a</u>hu qur-<u>a</u>nan aAAjamiyyan laq<u>a</u>loo lawl<u>a</u> fu<u>ss</u>ilat <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tuhu aaAAjamiyyun waAAarabiyyun qul huwa lilla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo hudan washif<u>a</u>on wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena l<u>a</u> yu/minoona fee <u>atha</u>nihim waqrun wahuwa AAalayhim AAaman ol<u>a</u>-ika yun<u>a</u>dawna min mak<u>a</u>nin baAAeed<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>44.  Had We sent this as<br> A Qur-ān (in a language)<br> Other than Arabic, they would<br> Have said: "Why are not<br> Its verses explained in detail?<br> What! (a Book) not in Arabic<br> And (a Messenger) an Arab?"<br> Say: "It is a guide<br>And a healing to those<br> Who believe; and for those<br> Who believe not, there is<br> A deafness in their ears,<br> And it is blindness in their (eyes):<br> They are (as it were)<br> Being called from a place<br> Far distant!"</p> <div class="filenav">
