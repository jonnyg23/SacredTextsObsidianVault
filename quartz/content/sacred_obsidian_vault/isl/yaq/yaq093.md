 <p><a name="page_1751"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1751</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">Sūra XCIII.</h3>  <h3 align="center"><i><u>Dh</u>uhā,</i> or The Glorious Morning Light.</h3>  
 <p><i>In the name of God, Most Gracious,<br> Most Merciful.</i></p>  
 <p>1. By the Glorious<br> Morning Light,</p>  
 <p>2. And by the Night<br> When it is still,—</p>  
 <p>3. Thy Guardian-Lord<br> Hath not forsaken thee,<br> Nor is He displeased,</p>  
 <p>4. And verily the hereafter<br> Will be better for thee<br> Than the present.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1752"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1752</font></a></p>  
 <p>5. And soon will thy<br> Guardian-Lord give thee<br> (That wherewith) thou<br> Shalt be well-pleased.</p>  
 <p>6. Did He not find thee<br> An orphan and give thee<br> Shelter (and care)?</p>  
 <p>7. And He found thee<br> Wandering, and He gave<br> Thee guidance.</p>  
 <p>8. And He found thee<br> In need, and made<br> Thee independent.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_1753"><font size="1" color="green">p. 1753</font></a></p>  
 <p>9. Therefore, treat not<br> The orphan with harshness,</p>  
 <p>10. Nor repulse the petitioner<br> (Unheard);</p>  
 <p>11. But the Bounty<br> Of thy Lord—<br> Rehearse and proclaim!</p>
