 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, Rodwell edition [1876]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SURA CVI.--THE KOREISCH [XX.]</h3>  
 <p align="center">MECCA.--4 Verses</p>  
 <p>In the Name of God, the Compassionate, the Merciful<br> For the union of the KOREISCH:--<br> Their union in equipping caravans winter and summer.<br> And let them worship the Lord of this house, who hath provided them with food against hunger,<br> And secured them against alarm. <a name="fr_61"></a><a href="#fn_61"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_61"></a><a href="106.htm#fr_61">1</a> In allusion to the ancient inviolability of the Haram, or precinct round Mecca. See Sura, xcv. n. p. 41. This Sura, therefore, like the preceding, is a brief appeal to the Meccans on the ground of their peculiar privileges.</p>
