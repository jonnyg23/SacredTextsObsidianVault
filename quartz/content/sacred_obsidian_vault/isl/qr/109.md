 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, Rodwell edition [1876]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SURA CIX.--UNBELIEVERS [IX.]</h3>  
 <p align="center">MECCA.--6 Verses</p>  
 <p>In the Name of God, the Compassionate, the Merciful<br> SAY: O ye UNBELIEVERS!<br> I worship not that which ye worship,<br> And ye do not worship that which I worship;<br> I shall never worship that which ye worship,<br> Neither will ye worship that which I worship.<br> To you be your religion; to me my religion. <a name="fr_44"></a><a href="#fn_44"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_44"></a><a href="109.htm#fr_44">1</a> This Sura is said to have been revealed when Walîd urged Muhammad to consent that his God should be worshipped at the same time with the old Meccan deities, or alternately every year. Hishâmi, p. 79; Tabari, p. 139. It is a distinct renunciation of Meccan idolatry, as the following Sura is a distinct recognition of the Divine Unity.</p>
