 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE ELEPHANT.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(CV. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>Hast thou not seen what thy Lord did with the fellows of the elephant <a name="fr_501"></a><a href="#fn_501"><font size="1">2</font></a>?</p>  
 <p>Did He not make their stratagem lead them astray, and send down on them birds in flocks, to throw down on them stones of baked clay, [5] and make them like blades of herbage eaten down?</p>  
 <p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_501"></a><a href="105.htm#fr_501">341:2</a> Abrahat el Asram, an Abyssinian Christian, and viceroy of the king of Sanaa in Yemen in the year in which Mohammed was born, marched with a large army and some elephants upon Mecca, with the intention of destroying the Kaabah. He was defeated and his army destroyed in so sudden a manner as to have given rise to the <a name="page_342"><font size="1" color="green">p. 342</font></a> legend embodied in the text. It is conjectured that small-pox broke out amongst his men.</p>
