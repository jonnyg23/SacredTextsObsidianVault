 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE CHARGERS.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(C. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> By the snorting chargers!<br> And those who strike fire with their hoofs!<br> And those who make incursions in the morning,<br> And raise up dust therein,<br> [5] And cleave through a host therein!<br> Verily, man is to his Lord ungrateful; and, verily, he is a witness of that.<br> Verily, he is keen in his love of good.<br> Does he not know when the tombs are exposed, [10] and what is in the breasts is brought to light?<br> Verily, thy Lord upon that day indeed is well aware.</p>
