 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE MOST HIGH.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(LXXXVII. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>Celebrated the name of thy Lord most High, who created and fashioned, and who decreed and guided, and who brings forth the pasture, [5] and then makes it dusky stubble!</p>  
 <p>We will make thee recite, and thou shalt not forget <a name="fr_479"></a><a href="#fn_479"><font size="1">2</font></a>, save what God pleases. Verily, He knows the open and what is concealed; and we will send</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_329"><font size="1" color="green">p. 329</font></a></p>  
 <p>thee easily to ease; wherefore remind, for, verily, the reminder is useful.</p>  
 <p>[10] But he who fears will be mindful; but the wretch will avoid it; he who will broil on the great fire, and then therein shall neither die nor live!</p>  
 <p>Prosperous is he who purifies himself, [15] and remembers the name of his Lord and prays!</p>  
 <p>Nay! but ye prefer the life of this world, while the, hereafter is better and more lasting.</p>  
 <p>Verily, this was in the books of yore,--the books of Abraham and Moses.</p>
