 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE FIG.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(XCV. Place of origin doubtful.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> By the fig!<br> And by the olive!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_336"><font size="1" color="green">p. 336</font></a></p>  
 <p>And by Mount Sinai!<br> And by this safe land <a name="fr_491"></a><a href="#fn_491"><font size="1">1</font></a>!<br> We have indeed created man in the best of symmetry. [5] Then we will send him back the lowest of the low; save those who believe and act aright; for theirs is a hire that is not grudged.<br> But what shall make thee call the judgment after this a lie!<br> Is not God a most just of judges?</p>
