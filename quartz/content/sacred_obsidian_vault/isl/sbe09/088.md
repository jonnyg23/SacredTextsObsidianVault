 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE OVERWHELMING <a name="fr_480"></a><a href="#fn_480"><font size="1">1</font></a>.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(LXXXVIII. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>Has there come to thee the story of the overwhelming?</p>  
 <p>Faces on that day shall: be humble, labouring, toiling,--shall broil upon a burning fire; [5] shall be given to drink from a boiling spring! no food shall they have save from the foul thorn, which shall not fatten nor avail against hunger!</p>  
 <p>Faces on that day shall be comfortable, content with their past endeavours,--[10] in a lofty garden wherein they shall hear no foolish word; wherein is a flowing fountain; wherein are couches raised on high, and goblets set down, [15] and cushions arranged, and carpets spread!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_330"><font size="1" color="green">p. 330</font></a></p>  
 <p>Do they not look then at the camel how she is created <a name="fr_481"></a><a href="#fn_481"><font size="1">1</font></a>?</p>  
 <p>And at the heaven how it is reared?</p>  
 <p>And at the mountains how they are set up?</p>  
 <p>[20] And at the earth how it is spread out?</p>  
 <p>But remind: thou art only one to remind; thou art not in authority over them; except such as turns his back and misbelieves, for him will God torment with the greatest torment.</p>  
 <p>[25] Verily, unto us is their return, and, verily, for us is their account!</p>
