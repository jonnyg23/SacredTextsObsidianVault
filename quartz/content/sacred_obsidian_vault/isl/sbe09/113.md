 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE DAYBREAK.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(CXIII. Place of origin doubtful.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.</p>  
 <p>Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak, from the evil of what He has created; and from the evil of the night when it cometh on <a name="fr_509"></a><a href="#fn_509"><font size="1">3</font></a>; and from the evil of the blowers upon knots <a name="fr_510"></a><a href="#fn_510"><font size="1">4</font></a>; [5] and from the evil of the envious when he envies.'</p>

 <p><a name="fn_509"></a><a href="113.htm#fr_509">344:3</a> Or, according to a traditional explanation given by the prophet to ‘Âyeshah, 'the moon when it is eclipsed.'</p>
 <p><a name="fn_510"></a><a href="113.htm#fr_510">344:4</a> Witches who make knots in string and blow upon them, uttering at the same time some magical formula and the name of the persons they wish to injure.</p>
