 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE FOLDING UP.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(LXXXI. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> When the sun is folded up,<br> And when the stars do fall,<br> And when the mountains are moved,<br> And when the she-camels ten months' gone with<br>   young shall be neglected <a name="fr_461"></a><a href="#fn_461"><font size="1">1</font></a>,<br> [5] And when the beasts shall be crowded together <a name="fr_462"></a><a href="#fn_462"><font size="1">2</font></a>,</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_322"><font size="1" color="green">p. 322</font></a></p>  
 <p>And when the seas shall surge up,<br> And when souls shall be paired with bodies,<br> And when the child who was buried alive shall be<br>   asked for what sin she was slain <a name="fr_463"></a><a href="#fn_463"><font size="1">1</font></a>,<br> [10] And when the pages shall be spread out,<br> And when the heaven shall be flayed.<br> And when hell shall be set ablaze,<br> And when Paradise shall be brought nigh,<br> The soul shall know what it has produced!<br> [15] I need not swear by the stars that slink back,<br>   moving swiftly, slinking into their dens!<br> Nor by the night when darkness draws on!<br> Nor by the morn when it first breathes up!<br> Verily, it is the speech of a noble apostle, [20]<br>   mighty, standing sure with the Lord of the throne,<br>   obeyed and trusty too!<br> Your comrade is not mad; he saw him <a name="fr_464"></a><a href="#fn_464"><font size="1">2</font></a> on the plain<br>   horizon <a name="fr_465"></a><a href="#fn_465"><font size="1">3</font></a>, nor does he grudge to communicate the unseen <a name="fr_466"></a><a href="#fn_466"><font size="1">4</font></a>.<br> [25] Nor is it the speech of a pelted devil <a name="fr_467"></a><a href="#fn_467"><font size="1">5</font></a>.<br> Then whither do ye go?<br> It is but a reminder to the worlds, to whomsoever<br>   of you pleases to go straight:--but ye will not<br>   please, except God, the Lord of the world, should please.</p>
