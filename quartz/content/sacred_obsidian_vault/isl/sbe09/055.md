 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">The Qur'ân, part II (Sacred Books of the East volume 9), Palmer edition [1880]; at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE CHAPTER OF THE MERCIFUL.</h3>  <h3 align="center">(LV. Mecca.)</h3>  
 <p>IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.<br> The Merciful taught the Qur’ân;<br> He created man, taught him plain speech.<br> The sun and the moon have their appointed time;<br> [5] The herbs and the trees adore;<br> And the heavens, He raised them and set the balance,<br>   that ye should not be outrageous in the balance;<br> But weigh ye aright, and stint not the balance.<br> And the earth He has set it for living creatures;<br>   [10] therein are fruits and palms, with sheaths;<br>   and grain with chaff and frequent shoots;<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> He created men of crackling clay like the potters.<br>   And He created the <i>g</i>inn from smokeless fire.<br> [15] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> The Lord of the two easts <a name="fr_362"></a><a href="#fn_362"><font size="1">2</font></a> and the Lord of the two wests!</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_259"><font size="1" color="green">p. 259</font></a></p>  
 <p>Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> He has let loose the two seas that meet together;<br>   [20] between them is a barrier they cannot pass!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> He brings forth from each pearls both large and small!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> His are the ships which rear aloft in the sea like mountains.<br> [25] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Every one upon it <a name="fr_363"></a><a href="#fn_363"><font size="1">1</font></a> is transient, but the face of thy<br>   Lord endowed with majesty and honour shall endure.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Of Him whosoever is in the heaven and the earth<br>   does beg; every day He is in (some fresh) business!<br> [30] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> We shall be at leisure for you, O ye two weighty ones <a name="fr_364"></a><a href="#fn_364"><font size="1">2</font></a>!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> O assembly of <i>g</i>inns and mankind! if ye are able<br>   to pass through the confines of heaven and earth</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_260"><font size="1" color="green">p. 260</font></a></p>  
 <p>then pass through them!--ye cannot pass through<br>   save by authority!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> [35] There shall be sent against you a flash of fire,<br>   and molten copper, and ye shall not be helped!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> And when the heaven is rent asunder and become<br>   rosy red <a name="fr_365"></a><a href="#fn_365"><font size="1">1</font></a>--(melting) like grease!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> On that day neither man nor <i>g</i>inn shall be asked about his crime!<br> [40] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> The sinners shall be known by their marks, and<br>   shall be seized by the forelock and the feet!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> 'This is hell, which the sinners did call a lie! they<br>   shall circulate between it and water boiling quite!'<br> [45] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> But for him who fears the station of his Lord are gardens twain!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Both furnished with branching trees.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> [50] In each are flowing springs.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_261"><font size="1" color="green">p. 261</font></a></p>  
 <p>Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> In each are, of every fruit, two kinds.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Reclining on beds the linings of which are of brocade,<br>   and the fruit of the two gardens within reach to cull.<br> [55] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Therein are maids of modest glances whom no man<br>   nor <i>g</i>inn has deflowered before.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> As though they were rubies and pearls.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> [60] Is the reward of goodness aught but goodness?<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> And besides these, are gardens twain <a name="fr_366"></a><a href="#fn_366"><font size="1">1</font></a>,<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> With dark green foliage.<br> [65] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye . twain deny?<br> In each two gushing springs.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> In each fruit and palms and pomegranates.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_262"><font size="1" color="green">p. 262</font></a></p>  
 <p>[70] In them maidens best and fairest!<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Bright and large-eyed maids kept in their tents.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Whom no man nor <i>g</i>inn has deflowered before them.<br> [75] Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Reclining. on green cushions and beautiful carpets.<br> Then which of your Lord's bounties will ye twain deny?<br> Blessed be the name of thy Lord possessed of majesty and honour!</p>
