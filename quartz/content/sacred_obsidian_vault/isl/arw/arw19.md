 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Speculative Studies</span></h3>  
 <p>All speculative research ends in perplexing uncertainty.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a></p>  
 <p>I sought in the great sea of theoretical learning a bottom on which to stand—and found nothing but one wave dashing me against another.</p>  
 <p>After a lifetime of research and learning, I amassed nothing but such phrases as: "It is said," or "They say."</p>  
 <p>O erring reason, I am sick of thee! I take a single step and thou movest a whole mile away from me.</p>  
 <p>The object sought in abstruse study is either a truth which cannot be known, or a vain thing which it is useless to know.</p>
