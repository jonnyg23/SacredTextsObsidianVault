 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Arabian Wisdom</i>, by John Wortabet, [1913], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Generosity</span></h3>  
 <p>Generosity is to do a kindness before it is asked, and to pity and give a man who asks.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="green">p. 24</font></a></p>  
 <p>A generous man is nigh unto God, nigh unto men, nigh unto paradise, far from hell.</p>  
 <p>Overlook the faults of a generous man, for God helps him when he falls and gives him when he is needy.</p>  
 <p>A man who doeth good does not fall, and if he fall he will find a support.</p>  
 <p>Be not ashamed to give little—to refuse is less.</p>
