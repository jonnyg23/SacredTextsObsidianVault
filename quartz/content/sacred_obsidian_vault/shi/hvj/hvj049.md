 <img src="img/04800.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">48</h3>  <h3 align="center">SHIGE-YUKI MINAMOTO</h3>  <h3 align="center">MINAMOTO NO SHIGE-YUKI</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Kaze wo itami<br> Iwa utsu nami no<br>   Onore nomi<br> Kudakete mono wo<br> Omou koro kana.  
 <p>THE waves that dash against the rocks<br>   Are broken by the wind<br> And turned to spray; my loving heart<br>   Is broken too, I find,<br>   Since thou art so unkind.</p></div>  
 <p><small>The writer of this verse is said to have died in the year 963; for a note about the great Minamoto family, see verse No. <a href="hvj029.htm#page_28">28</a>. In the picture we see Shige-yuki, with an attendant carrying his sword, walking on the shore, while the waves break into spray at his feet.</small></p>
