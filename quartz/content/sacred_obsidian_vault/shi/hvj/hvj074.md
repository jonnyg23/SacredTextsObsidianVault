 <img src="img/07300.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">73</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE ASSISTANT IMPERIAL ADVISER MASAFUSA</h3>  <h3 align="center">GON CHŪ-NAGON MASAFUSA</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Takasago no<br> Once no zakura<br>   Saki ni keri<br> Toyama no kasumi<br> Tatazu mo aranan.  
 <p>THE cherry trees are blossoming<br>   On Takasago's height;<br> Oh may no mountain mist arise,<br>   No clouds so soft and white,<br>   To hide them from our sight.</p></div>  
 <p><small>This poet was the son of Chika-nari Ooi, and died in the year 1112. Takasago is on the sea-coast in the Province of Harima, and is also mentioned in verse No. <a href="hvj035.htm#page_34">34</a>.</small></p>  
 <p>Masafusa with his attendant appears in the illustration admiring the cherry trees on the mountains, over which, however, the clouds are already beginning to gather.</p>
