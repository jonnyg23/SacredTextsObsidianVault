 <img src="img/01500.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">15</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE EMPEROR KWŌKŌ</h3>  <h3 align="center">KWŌKŌ TENNŌ</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Kimi ga tame<br> Haru no no ni idete<br>   Wakana tsumu<br> Waga koromode ni<br> Yuki wa furi-tsutsu.  
 <p>MOTHER, for thy sake I have been<br>   Where the wakana grow,<br> To bring thee back some fresh green leaves<br>   And see—my koromo<br>   Is sprinkled with the snow!</p></div>  
 <p><small>Kwōkō was raised to the throne by the Fujiwara family, when the mad Emperor Yōzei was deposed; he reigned A.D. 885-887, and is said to have composed this verse in honour of his grandmother.</small></p>  
 <p><i>Wakana</i>, literally 'young leaves', is a vegetable in season at the New Year; a <i>koromo</i> is really a priest's garment, but is used here for the Emperor's robe.</p>  
 <p>In the picture we see the Emperor gathering the fresh green leaves, and the snow falling from the sky.</p>
