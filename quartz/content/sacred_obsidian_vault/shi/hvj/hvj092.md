 <img src="img/09100.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">91</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE REGENT AND FORMER PRIME MINISTER GO-KYŌ-GOKU</h3>  <h3 align="center">GO-KYŌ-GOKU SESSHŌ SAKI NO DAIJŌDAIJIN</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">  Kirigirisu<br> Naku ya shimo yo no<br>   Samushiro ni<br> Koromo katashiki<br> Hitori kamo nen.  
 <p>I'M sleeping all alone, and hear<br>   The crickets round my head;<br> So cold and frosty is the night,<br>   That I across the bed<br>   My koromo have spread.</p></div>  
 <p><small>This writer was another of the great Fujiwara family, and died in the year 1206.</small></p>  
 <p>The word <i>kirigirisu</i>, a cricket, is supposed to represent its song; the Japanese say that the chirping of crickets means cold weather.</p>  
 <p>In the picture the poet is sitting up in bed with his arm on his pillow, listening to the crickets; and in the original illustrated edition underneath the verse is drawn a cricket hiding in the grass.</p>
