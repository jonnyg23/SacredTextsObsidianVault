 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Kojiki</i>, translated by Basil Hall Chamberlain, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CXXXV.—EMPEROR HAN-ZEI.]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Midzu-ha-wake dwelt in the palace of Shibakaki at Tajihi, <a name="fr_2181"></a><a href="#fn_2176"><font size="1">1</font></a> and ruled the Empire. The length of this Heavenly sovereign's august person was nine feet two inches and a half. <a name="fr_2182"></a><a href="#fn_2177"><font size="1">2</font></a> The length of his august teeth was one inch, and their breadth two lines, and the upper and lower [row] corresponded exactly, [like jewels strung together]. The Heavenly Sovereign wedded the Lady of Tsunu, <a name="fr_2183"></a><a href="#fn_2178"><font size="1">3</font></a> daughter of Kogoto, Grandee of Wani, <a name="fr_2184"></a><a href="#fn_2179"><font size="1">4</font></a> and begot august children: the Lady of Kahi; <a name="fr_2185"></a><a href="#fn_2180"><font size="1">5</font></a> next the Lady of Tsubura <a name="fr_2186"></a><a href="#fn_2181"><font size="1">6</font></a> (<small>two Deities</small>). Again he wedded Oto-hime, <a name="fr_2187"></a><a href="#fn_2182"><font size="1">7</font></a> daughter of the same Grandee, and begot august children: King Takara; <a name="fr_2188"></a><a href="#fn_2183"><font size="1">8</font></a> next the Lady of Takabe. <a name="fr_2189"></a><a href="#fn_2184"><font size="1">9</font></a>—-altogether four Kings [and Queens]. <a name="fr_2190"></a><a href="#fn_2185"><font size="1">10</font></a> The Heavenly Sovereign's august years were sixty. His august mausoleum is on the Moor of Mozu. <a name="fr_2191"></a><a href="#fn_2186"><font size="1">11</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_2176"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2181">364:1</a> <a name="page_364"><font size="1" color="green">p. 364</font></a> For Tajihi see Sect. CXXXII, Note 4. <i>Shiba-kaki</i> (or <i>Shiba-gaki</i>) signifies "a fence of brushwood."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2177"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2182">364:2</a> As to the ancient Japanese measures we have no accurate information, and the English equivalents used in this passage correspond but approximately to the modern Japanese standards. The character rendered "line" is <a name="img_36400"></a>
 <img src="img/36400.jpg">, which denotes the tenth part of a <a name="img_36401"></a>
 <img src="img/36401.jpg"> or "inch." Motowori remarks that the dimensions of the teeth are not anything extraordinary <a name="page_365"><font size="1" color="green">p. 365</font></a> judged by the present standard, and suppose that anciently the measures of length must have been smaller than at present.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2178"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2183">364:3</a> <i>Tsunu no iratsume</i>. The signification of this name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2179"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2184">364:4</a> <i>Wani no kogoto no omi</i>. The meaning of Kogoto is obscure, Wani has already often appeared.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2180"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2185">364:5</a> <i>Kahi no iratsume</i>. Kahi is the name of a province, but it cannot be said for certain that it is from it that this Princess derived her name.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2181"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2186">364:6</a> <i>Tsubura no iratsume</i>. The meaning of <i>Tsubura</i> is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2182"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2187">364:7</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the younger princess."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2183"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2188">364:8</a> <i>Takara no miko</i>. <i>Takara</i> signifies "treasure."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2184"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2189">364:9</a> <i>Takabe no iratsume</i>. <i>Takabe</i> seems to be the name of a place unless it be considered to be connected with the word <i>taka</i>, "hawk."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2185"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2190">364:10</a> Remember that the single character <a name="img_36500"></a>
 <img src="img/36500.jpg"> includes both sexes.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2186"></a><a href="kj143.htm#fr_2191">364:11</a> See Sect. CXXX. Note 1.</p>
