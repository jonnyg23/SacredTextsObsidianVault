 <p>[252]</p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CX.—EMPEROR Ō-JIN (PART VIII.—TRIBUTE FROM KOREA).]</h2>  
 <p>Again King Shō-ko, <a name="fr_1904"></a><a href="#fn_1899"><font size="1">1</font></a> the Chieftain of the land of Kudara, sent as tribute by Achi-kishi <a name="fr_1905"></a><a href="#fn_1900"><font size="1">2</font></a> one stallion and one mare. (<small>This Achi-kishi was the ancestor of the Achiki Scribes. <a name="fr_1906"></a><a href="#fn_1901"><font size="1">3</font></a></small>) Again he sent as tribute a cross-sword, <a name="fr_1907"></a><a href="#fn_1902"><font size="1">4</font></a> and likewise a large mirror. Again he was graciously bidden <a name="fr_1908"></a><a href="#fn_1903"><font size="1">5b</font></a> to send as tribute a wise man, if there were any such in the land of Kudara. Therefore receiving the [Imperial] commands, he sent as tribute a man named Wani-kishi, <a name="fr_1909"></a><a href="#fn_1904"><font size="1">6</font></a> and likewise by this man he sent as tribute the Confucian Analects <a name="fr_1910"></a><a href="#fn_1905"><font size="1">7</font></a> in ten volumes and the Thousand Character Essay <a name="fr_1911"></a><a href="#fn_1906"><font size="1">8</font></a> in one volume,—altogether eleven volumes.</p>
