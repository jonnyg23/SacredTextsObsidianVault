 <p><a name="page_225"><font size="1" color="green">p. 225</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. LXVIII.—EMPEROR SŪ-JIN (PART VI.—HIS AGE AND PLACE OF BURIAL).]</h2>  
 <p>The Heavenly Sovereign's august years were one hundred and sixty-eight. His august mausoleum is on the mound at the corner of the Yamanobe road. <a name="fr_1378"></a><a href="#fn_1374"><font size="1">1a</font></a></p>

 </p>  
 <hr>
 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1374"></a><a href="kj075.htm#fr_1378">225:1a</a> This place, where the Emperor Kei-ko is likewise said to have been interred, was in the province of Yamato, and the road mentioned is supposed by Motowori to have been the highway from Hatsuse into the province of Yamashiro. The word Yamanobe signifies "in the neighbourhood of the mountain" (<i>yama no he</i>).</p>
