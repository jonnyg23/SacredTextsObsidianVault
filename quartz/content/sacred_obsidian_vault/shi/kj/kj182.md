 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Kojiki</i>, translated by Basil Hall Chamberlain, [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. CLXXV.—EMPEROR SEN-KUWA.]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Take-wo-hiro-kuni-oshi-tate dwelt in the Palace of Ihorinu at Hinokuma, and ruled the Empire.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_425"><font size="1" color="green">p. 425</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> The august children born to this Heavenly Sovereign by his wife Her Augustness Tachi-bana-no-naka-tsu-hime, the august daughter of the Heavenly Sovereign Ohoke, were: Her Augustness Ishi-hime; next Her Augustness Wo-ishi-hime; next King Kura-no-waka-ye. The august children born to him by his next wife, Kafuchi-no-waku-go-hime, were: King Honoho; next King Weha. The august children of this Heavenly [340] Sovereign numbered altogether five (<small>three Kings and two Queens</small>). So King Honoho (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes Shihida.</small>) Prince Weha (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes of Wina and of the Dukes of Tajihi.</small>)</p>
