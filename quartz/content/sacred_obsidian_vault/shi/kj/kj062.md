 <p><a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a></p>  <h2 align="center">[SECT. LV.—EMPEROR SUI-ZEI.]</h2>  
 <p>His Augustness Kamu-nuna-kaha-mimi dwelt at the palace of Takawoka in Kadzuraki, <a name="fr_1054"></a><a href="#fn_1050"><font size="1">1a</font></a> and ruled the Empire. This Heavenly Sovereign wedded Kaha-mata-bime, <a name="fr_1055"></a><a href="#fn_1051"><font size="1">2a</font></a> ancestress of the Departmental Lords of Shiki, <a name="fr_1056"></a><a href="#fn_1052"><font size="1">3a</font></a> and begot [154] an august child: His Augustness Shiki-tsu-hiko-tama-demi <a name="fr_1057"></a><a href="#fn_1053"><font size="1">4</font></a> (one Deity), The Heavenly Sovereign's august years were forty-five. His august mausoleum is on the Mound of Tsukida. <a name="fr_1058"></a><a href="#fn_1054"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1050"></a><a href="kj062.htm#fr_1054">189:1a</a> <a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a> In the province of Yamato. <i>Taka-woka</i> signifies "high mound." <i>Kadzuraki</i> means "pueraria castle," a name accounted for by a legend in the "Chronicles," which relates how an earth-spider was caught in this place by means of a net made of pueraria tendrils. Kadzuraki was the name, not only of a town, but of a district.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1051"></a><a href="kj062.htm#fr_1055">189:2a</a> <i>I.e.</i>, the Princess of Kahamata, a place in Kahachi. The name signifies "river-fork." For the omission in this and a few other places of the words "daughter of," etc., which might be expected instead of "ancestress," see Motowori's Commentary, Vol. XXI, p. 4.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1052"></a><a href="kj062.htm#fr_1056">189:3a</a> <i>Shiki na agata-nushi</i>. Shiki is in Yamato. The signification of the name seems to be "stone castle."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1053"></a><a href="kj062.htm#fr_1057">189:4</a> <i>Shiki-tsu-hiko</i> signifies "Prince of Shiki." Motowori believes Tamade to be in like manner the name of a place, while he interprets mi as the common abbreviation of the Honorific (?) <i>mimi</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1054"></a><a href="kj062.htm#fr_1058">189:5</a> A place in Yamato. Motowori derives the name from <i>tsuki</i> modern <i>toki</i>), the "ibis," and <i>ta</i>, "rice-field."</p>
