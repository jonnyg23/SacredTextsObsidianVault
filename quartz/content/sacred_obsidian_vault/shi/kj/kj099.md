 <p>This [Prince] His Augustness Yamato-take wedded Her Augustness Princess Futaji-no-iri, <a name="fr_1671"></a><a href="#fn_1666"><font size="1">1b</font></a> daughter of the Heavenly Sovereign Ikume, and begot an august child: His Augustness Tarashi-naka-tsu-hiko <a name="fr_1672"></a><a href="#fn_1667"><font size="1">2b</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again, wedding Her Augustness Princess Oto-tachibana <a name="fr_1673"></a><a href="#fn_1668"><font size="1">3</font></a> who [afterwards] entered the sea, <a name="fr_1674"></a><a href="#fn_1669"><font size="1">4</font></a> he begot an august child: King Waka-take <a name="fr_1675"></a><a href="#fn_1670"><font size="1">5</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again, wedding Princess [224] Futaji, <a name="fr_1676"></a><a href="#fn_1671"><font size="1">6</font></a> daughter of Oho-tamu-wake, <a name="fr_1677"></a><a href="#fn_1672"><font size="1">7</font></a> ancestor of the Rulers of the Land of Yasu in Chika-tsu-Afumi, <a name="fr_1678"></a><a href="#fn_1673"><font size="1">8</font></a> he begot an august child: King Ine-yori-wake <a name="fr_1679"></a><a href="#fn_1674"><font size="1">9</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again, wedding Princess Oho-kibi-take, <a name="fr_1680"></a><a href="#fn_1675"><font size="1">10</font></a> younger sister of Take-hiko [ancestor of the] Grandees of Kiki, <a name="fr_1681"></a><a href="#fn_1676"><font size="1">11</font></a> he begot an</p>

 <p>august child: King Take-kahiko <a name="fr_1682"></a><a href="#fn_1677"><font size="1">12</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). Again, wedding Princess Kukuma-mori of Yamashiro, <a name="fr_1683"></a><a href="#fn_1678"><font size="1">13</font></a> he begot an august child, King Ashi-kagami-wake <a name="fr_1684"></a><a href="#fn_1679"><font size="1">14</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). A child by another wife was King Okinaga-ta-wake. <a name="fr_1685"></a><a href="#fn_1680"><font size="1">15</font></a> Al-together the entire [number] of the august children of His Augustness Yamato-take was six Deities. So His Augustness Tarashi-naka-tsu-hiko [was he who afterwards] ruled the Empire. The next, King Ine-yori-wake (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes of Inukami <a name="fr_1686"></a><a href="#fn_1681"><font size="1">16</font></a> and of the Dukes of Takebe</small>.) <a name="fr_1687"></a><a href="#fn_1682"><font size="1">17</font></a> [225] The next, King Take-kahiko (<small>was the ancestor of the Dukes of Aya in Sanugi, <a name="fr_1688"></a><a href="#fn_1683"><font size="1">18</font></a> the Dukes of Wake in Iyo, <a name="fr_1689"></a><a href="#fn_1684"><font size="1">19</font></a> the Lords of Towo, <a name="fr_1690"></a><a href="#fn_1685"><font size="1">20</font></a> the Headmen of Masa <a name="fr_1691"></a><a href="#fn_1686"><font size="1">21</font></a> and the Lords of Miyaji.</small>) <a name="fr_1692"></a><a href="#fn_1687"><font size="1">22</font></a> King Ashi-kagami-wake (<small>was the ancestor of the Lords of Kamakura, <a name="fr_1693"></a><a href="#fn_1688"><font size="1">23</font></a> the Dukes of Wodzu, <a name="fr_1694"></a><a href="#fn_1689"><font size="1">24</font></a> the Lords of Ihashiro <a name="fr_1695"></a><a href="#fn_1690"><font size="1">25</font></a> and the Lords of Fukita.</small>) <a name="fr_1696"></a><a href="#fn_1691"><font size="1">26</font></a> The child of the next, King Okinaga-ta-wake was King Kuhi-mata-naga-hiko. <a name="fr_1697"></a><a href="#fn_1692"><font size="1">27</font></a> This King's children were: Her Augustness Princess Ihinu-ma-guro, <a name="fr_1698"></a><a href="#fn_1693"><font size="1">28</font></a> next Okinaga-ma-waka-naka-tsu-hime, <a name="fr_1699"></a><a href="#fn_1694"><font size="1">29</font></a> next Oto-hime <a name="fr_1700"></a><a href="#fn_1695"><font size="1">30</font></a> (<small>three Deities</small>). So the above mentioned King Waka-take wedded Princess Ihinu-ma-guro, [226] and begot King Sume-iro-oho-naka-tsu-hiko. <a name="fr_1701"></a><a href="#fn_1696"><font size="1">31</font></a> This King wedded Princess Shibanu, <a name="fr_1702"></a><a href="#fn_1697"><font size="1">32</font></a> daughter of Shibanu-iri-ki <a name="fr_1703"></a><a href="#fn_1698"><font size="1">33</font></a> of Afumi, and begot a child, Her Augustness Princess Kaguro. <a name="fr_1704"></a><a href="#fn_1699"><font size="1">34</font></a> So the Heavenly Sovereign Oho-tarashi-hiko wedded this [lady] Her Augustness Princess Kaguro, and begot King Oho-ye <a name="fr_1705"></a><a href="#fn_1700"><font size="1">35</font></a> (<small>one Deity</small>). This King wedded his younger half-sister Queen Shiro-kane, <a name="fr_1706"></a><a href="#fn_1701"><font size="1">36</font></a> and begot children: King Oho-na-gata, <a name="fr_1707"></a><a href="#fn_1702"><font size="1">37</font></a> and next Her Augustness Oho-naka-tsu-hime <a name="fr_1708"></a><a href="#fn_1703"><font size="1">38</font></a> (<small>two Deities</small>). So this [Lady] Her Augustness Oho-naka-tsu-hime was the august mother <a name="fr_1709"></a><a href="#fn_1704"><font size="1">39</font></a> of King Kagosaka <a name="fr_1710"></a><a href="#fn_1705"><font size="1">40</font></a> and King Oshikuma. <a name="fr_1711"></a><a href="#fn_1706"><font size="1">41</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_1666"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1671">277:1b</a> <a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="green">p. 278</font></a> For this name see Sect. LXIX, Note 24.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1667"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1672">277:2b</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "the perfect middle prince," a name which is justified by <a name="page_279"><font size="1" color="green">p. 279</font></a> the genealogy as given in the "Chronicles," where he is mentioned as the second of three sons borne by this princess.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1668"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1673">277:3</a> <i>Oto-tachibana-hime no mikoto</i>. <i>Oto</i> signifies "younger [sister]," and <i>Oto-tachibana</i> is the name of the orange.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1669"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1674">277:4</a> See the story in Sect. LXXXIV.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1670"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1675">277:5</a> <i>Waka-tate no miko</i>. This name signifies "young brave."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1671"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1676">277:6</a> <i>Futaji-hime</i>. Signification obscure. Futaji may be the name of a place.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1672"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1677">277:7</a> If <i>Tamu</i> is, as Motowori surmises, the name of a place, this personal name signifies "Great Lord of Tamu."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1673"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1678">277:8</a> <i>Chika-tsu-Afumi no Yasu no kuni no miyatsuko</i>. For <i>Yasu</i> see Sect. LXII, Note 62.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1674"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1679">277:9</a> <i>Ine-yori-wake no miko</i>. This name probably signifies "rice-good-lord."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1675"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1680">277:10</a> <i>Oho-kibi-take-hime</i>. <i>Oho</i> signifies "great." For the other two elements of the compound see next Note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1676"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1681">277:11</a> The text has <i>Kibi no omi Take-hiko</i>, as if this worthy had been himself the "Grandee of Kibi." Motowori however compares the commencement of Sect. LXXXII (Notes 2 and 3), and supplies the words "ancestor of." <i>Kibi</i> is of course the province of that name (the modern, Bizen, Bitchiū, and Bingo), and <i>take</i> signifies "brave."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1677"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1682">278:12</a> <i>Take-kahiko no miko</i>. <i>Take</i> signifies "brave," <i>kahiko</i> is either "egg "or "cocoon," or else perhaps a corruption of some other word.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1678"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1683">278:13</a> <i>Yamashiro no Kukuma mori-hime</i>. This name is obscure. Motowori identifies Kukuma with a place called <i>Kurihuma</i>, and <i>mori</i> is probably the Verb "to guard."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1679"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1684">278:14</a> <i>Ashi-kagami-wake no miko</i>. This name is written with characters signifying "foot-mirror-[lord]."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1680"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1685">278:15</a> <i>Okinaga-ta-wake no miko</i>. This name is obscure. Motowori believes Okinaga to be the name of a place in Afumi, but has no explanation to offer of <i>ta</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1681"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1686">278:16</a> <i>Inukami no kimi</i>. Inukami is the name of a district in Afumi, Its signification is not clear.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1682"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1687">278:17</a> <i>Takebi no kimi</i>. <i>Takebe</i> became the name of a place in Idzumo, but it originally signified "brave tribe," the family having, as in so many other cases, given its name to the place of its residence, instead of being called after the latter. See the origin of the name, given in Motowori's Commentary, Vol. XXIV, pp. 35-36.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1683"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1688">278:18</a> <i>Sanugi no Aya no kimi</i>. For <i>Sanugi</i> see Sect. V, Note 6. <i>Aya</i> is a district in the province; the name is of doubtful origin.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1684"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1689">278:19</a> <a name="page_280"><font size="1" color="green">p. 280</font></a> <i>Iyo no wake no kani</i>. For <i>Iyo</i> see Sect. V, Note 4. (The text here has <i>Ise</i> for Iyo, and the word <i>wake</i> is missing, but Motowori's emendation may be accepted). <i>Wake</i> is the name of a district in Iyo.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1685"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1690">278:20</a> <i>Towo no wake</i>. Of <i>Towo</i> nothing is known.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1686"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1691">278:21</a> <i>Masa no obito</i>. Of <i>Masa</i> nothing is known.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1687"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1692">278:22</a> <i>Miyagi</i> (<a name="img_28000"></a>
 <img src="img/28000.jpg">) <i>no wake</i>. This is Motowori's ingenious emendation of the characters in the text, <a name="img_28001"></a>
 <img src="img/28001.jpg">, out of which it is impossible to make a <a href="errata.htm#60">family</a> name. <i>Miyagi</i> is the name of a place in the province of Mikaha, and signifies "temple road."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1688"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1693">278:23</a> <i>Kamakura no wake</i>. Kamakura is the name of a district in the province of Sagami, which became famous during the Middle Ages as the site of an immense town,—the capital of the Shōgun, and the centre of the feudalism which then ruled Japan. The import of the name (literally "sickle-store") is not clear, though it has been fancifully explained by native etymologists.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1689"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1694">278:24</a> <i>Wodzu no kimi</i>. The words <i>no kimi</i> are supplied by Motowori, this name and the next being in the text run into one. Wodzu seems to be the name of a place in Afumi, and signifies "little mart."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1690"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1695">278:25</a> <i>Ihashiro no wake</i>. Motowori says that this Ihashiro is not the province of that name, but a place in Kishiu. The meaning of the name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1691"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1696">278:26</a> <i>Fukita no wake</i>. This is but Motowori's conjectural restoration (founded on a statement in the "Chronicles of Old Matters of Former Ages") of the name as given in the text, <a name="img_28002"></a>
 <img src="img/28002.jpg">.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1692"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1697">278:27</a> <i>Kuhimata-naga-hiko no miko</i>. <i>Kuhimata</i> (modern <i>Kumata</i>) is the name of a place in Settsu. The signification is obscure. <i>Naga-hiko</i> means "long prince."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1693"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1698">278:28</a> <i>Ihinu-ma-guro-hime</i>, <i>i.e.</i>, "quite black princess of Ihinu," the blackness being doubtless predicated of her hair. <i>Ihinu</i> is the name of a district in Ise, and is written with characters signifying "boiled-rice-moor."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1694"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1699">278:29</a> For <i>Okinaga</i> see Note 15. <i>Ma-waka</i> means "truly young." <i>Naka-tsu-hime</i> means "middle princess," referring to her being the second of three.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1695"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1700">278:30</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "younger princess."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1696"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1701">278:31</a> See Sect. LXXVI, Note 29.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1697"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1702">278:32</a> <i>Shibanu-hime</i>. This name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1698"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1703">278:33</a> <i>Shibunu iri-ko</i>. This name is obscure.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1699"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1704">278:34</a> <i>Ka-gara hime</i>, see Sect. LXXVI, Note 28.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1700"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1705">278:35</a> <a name="page_281"><font size="1" color="green">p. 281</font></a> For the confusion in this portion of the genealogy see Sect. LXXVI, Note 30.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1701"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1706">278:36</a> <i>Shiro-kane no miko</i>. <i>Shiro-kane</i> means "silver," but Motowori suspects corruption in the text.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1702"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1707">278:37</a> <i>Oho-nagata no miko</i>, <i>i.e.</i>, "great prince of Nagata," the latter being the name of a place in Settsu, signifying "long rice-field."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1703"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1708">278:38</a> <i>I.e.</i>, "great middle princess."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1704"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1709">278:39</a> Literally, "ancestress."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1705"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1710">278:40</a> Or, "the King of Kagosaka," for it is' uncertain whether Kagosaka should or should not be regarded as the name of a place. The etymology of the name may be <i>kago</i>, "a stag" and <i>saka</i>, "an ascent." The original form of the name and title is <i>Kagosaka no miko</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_1706"></a><a href="kj099.htm#fr_1711">278:41</a> Or, "the King of Oshikuma," <i>Oshikuma no miko</i>. <i>Oshikuma</i> is a word of doubtful etymology.</p>
