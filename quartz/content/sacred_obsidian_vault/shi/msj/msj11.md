 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Master-Singers of Japan</i>, by Clara A. Walsh, [1914], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE LIGHTNING</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Minamoto no Jun—10th Century</span></p>  
 <p>Out of the murky clouds the lightning's glare<br> Dimly reveals the brooding Autumn plain, <a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="green">p. 62</font></a><br> Shown for an instant in its 'broidery rare,<br> Then the dense darkness covers it again.<br> Such is our life, an instant in the light,<br> Then the Unknown, impenetrable night!</p>  <h3 align="center">EVANESCENCE</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Minamoto no Jun</span></p>  
 <p>Even as the beads of evening dew that lie<br> Upon the morning-glory through the night,<br> And vanish ere the fading of the flower<br>   With the day's dawning<br>   Such is human life!</p>  <h3 align="center">THE SLEEVE</h3>  
 <p align="center">UNKNOWN</p>  
 <p>Would that my sleeve were long and wide enough<br> To cover all the sky, and shelter thus<br> The fair Spring blossoms from the scattering wind!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="green">p. 63</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">DEW-SPRINKLED</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Minamoto no Jun</span></p>  
 <p>The tangled crowd of blossoms in the field,<br> Their mingled perfumes to the morning yield.<br> As I pass on, where fallen petals strew<br> The narrow path, with aromatic dew<br> My sleeve is wet and faded.</p>  <h3 align="center">EVENING MISTS</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Minamoto no Jun</span></p>  
 <p>The lonely wanderer with dismay<br> Sees the dusk fall, as he pursues<br> His path towards the wane of day,<br> And mists begin to blot the views<br> And hide the Adzuma highway,<br> Where, clear to sight, a while ago<br> The folk were passing to and fro!</p>  <h3 align="center">THE WHITE CLOUD</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Minamoto no Jun</span></p>  
 <p>The white cloud blown from the mountain-peak<br>        Drifts lightly by;<br> Whither it goes we would vainly seek,<br>        And know not why</p>  
 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="green">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>The white cloud feels the breath of the wind,<br>        A shuddering sigh:<br> Even thus is the life of a man<br>        Who is born—to die!</p>  <h3 align="center">THE FROGS AND THE KERRIA BLOOMS</h3>  
 <p>The crying of the frogs comes hither,<br> Silent no longer, since they fear<br> To see the Yamabuki wither,<br> The golden blooms they hold so dear,<br>    And now the heartless flowers<br>    Make haste to disappear!</p>  <h3 align="center">DAWN</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Manyôshiu")</p>  
 <p>     It is the Dawn, and I—<br> I cannot sleep, nor have the long night through,<br>      For thoughts of her I love!<br>      The cuckoo's weary cry,<br> Full of sad yearning, loud and louder grew,<br>      And still though Day draws nigh,<br> It maddens me with lamentations new!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="green">p. 65</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">WISTARIA</h3>  
 <p align="center">(From the "Manyôshiu")</p>  
 <p>The pale mauve wavelets of wistaria,<br> Which I had planted by my dwelling-place<br> That it might bring me thoughts of thee I love,<br> At length are blooming: pure ethereal<br> As their own fragrance, fugitive and rare.</p>  <h3 align="center">IN PRAISE OF SAKE</h3>  
 <p align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">By Ōtomo no Yakamochi</span></p>  
 <p>Why talk of jewels? Though by night<br> Their hearts with changeful colours shine,<br> Can they impart the deep delight<br> That lives within the golden wine<br>    And drives away one's care?</p>  
 <p>  If in this Lifetime<br> My heart be light and joyous,<br>   What does it matter<br> Though in the next existence<br> I change to bird or insect?</p>
