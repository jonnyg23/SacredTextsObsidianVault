 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="green">p. 38</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><i>THE PRINCES</i></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">They</span> gather the beans, gather the beans,<br> In their baskets square and round:<br> The princes all are coming to court,<br> And where shall their gifts be found?<br>       The coaches of state and their teams go by,<br>       What more for my lords have I?<br> Dark coloured robes with a dragon fine,<br> And silken skirts with the hatchet sign.</p>  
 <p>Clear bubbles the spring, bubbles the spring,<br> Around they gather the cress:<br> The princes all are coming to court,<br> Their banners the winds caress.<br>       The dragon flag in the breezes swells,<br>       To the <i>hwuy-hwuy</i> sound of the bells.<br> With two outside, the teams go past,<br> These are the princes come at last.</p>  
 <p>Red covers the knee, covers the knee:<br> Their buskins are red below.<br> Lofty bearing and stately mien,<br> Yonder my princes go.<br>       In such the Son of Heaven delights,<br>       The king shall renew their rights.<br> May the pleasure and power for my lords increase,<br> May the land yield corn and the years bring peace.</p>
