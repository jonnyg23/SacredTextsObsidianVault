   <h5 align="center">ODE 6. THE HÂO THIEN YÛ <i>KH</i>ĂNG MING.</h5>  <h5 align="center">APPROPRIATE TO A SACRIFICE TO KING <i>KH</i>ĂNG.</h5>  
 <p><font size="-1">Khăng was the honorary title of Sung, the son and successor of king Wû, B.C. 1115 to 1079.</font></p>  
 <p>Heaven made its determinate appointment, which our two sovereigns received <a name="fr_361"></a><a href="#fn_349"><font size="1">2</font></a>. King <i>Kh</i>ăng did not dare to rest idly in it, But night and day enlarged</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_317"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 317</font></a></p>  
 <p>its foundations by his deep and silent virtue. How did he continue and glorify (his heritage), Exerting all his heart, And so securing its tranquillity!</p>
