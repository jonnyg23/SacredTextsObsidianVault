 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="green">p. 40</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[14] SICK LEAVE</h3>  <h4 align="center"><i>(While Secretary to the Deputy-Assistant-Magistrate of Chou-chih, near Ch‘ang-an, in A.D. 806)</i></h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Propped</span> on pillows, not attending to business;<br> For two days I've lain behind locked doors.<br> I begin to think that those who hold office<br> Get no rest, except by falling ill!<br> For restful thoughts one does not need space;<br> The room where I lie is ten foot square.<br> By the western eaves, above the bamboo-twigs,<br> From my couch I see the White Mountain rise.<br> But the clouds that hover on its far-distant peak<br> Bring shame to a face that is buried in the World's dust.</p>
