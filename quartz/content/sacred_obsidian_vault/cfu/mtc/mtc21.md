 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[20] THE POEM ON THE WALL</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>810</i>)</h4>  <h4 align="center"><i>(Yüan Chēn wrote that on his way to exile he had discovered a poem inscribed by Po Chü-i, on the wall of the Lo-k‘ou Inn.)</i></h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">My</span> clumsy poem on the inn-wall none cared to see.<br> With bird-droppings and moss's growth the letters were blotched away.<br> There came a guest with heart so full, that though a page to the Throne,<br> He did not grudge with his broidered coat to wipe off the dust, and read.</p>
