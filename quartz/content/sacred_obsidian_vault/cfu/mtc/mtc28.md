 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="green">p. 55</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[27] POEMS IN DEPRESSION, AT WEI VILLAGE</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>812</i>)</h4>  <h4>[1]</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">I hug</span> my pillow and do not speak a word;<br> In my empty room no sound stirs.<br> Who knows that, all day a-bed,<br> I am not ill and am not even asleep?</p>  <h4>[2]</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Turned</span> to jade are the boy's rosy cheeks;<br> To his sick temples the frost of winter clings…<br> Do not wonder that my body sinks to decay;<br> Though my limbs are old, my heart is older yet.</p>
