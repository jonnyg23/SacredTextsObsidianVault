 <p><a name="page_77"><font size="1" color="green">p. 77</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[45] PARTING FROM THE WINTER STOVE</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>822</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">On</span> the fifth day after the rise of Spring,<br> Everywhere the season's gracious altitudes!<br> The white sun gradually lengthening its course,<br> The blue-grey clouds hanging as though they would fall;<br> The last icicle breaking into splinters of jade;<br> The new stems marshalling red sprouts.<br> The things I meet are all full of gladness;<br> It is not only <i>I</i> who love the Spring.<br> To welcome the flowers I stand in the back garden;<br> To enjoy the sunlight I sit under the front eaves.<br> Yet still in my heart there lingers one regret;<br> Soon I shall part with the flame of my red stove!</p>
