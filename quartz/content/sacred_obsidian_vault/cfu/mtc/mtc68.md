 <p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="green">p. 148</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[67] THE SOUTH</h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">In</span> the southern land many birds sing;<br> Of towns and cities half are unwalled.<br> The country markets are thronged by wild tribes;<br> The mountain-villages bear river-names.<br> Poisonous mists rise from the damp sands;<br> Strange fires gleam through the night-rain.<br> And none passes but the lonely fisher of pearls<br> Year by year on his way to the South Sea.</p>
