 <p><a name="page_83"><font size="1" color="green">p. 83</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">[51] THE CRANES</h3>  <h4 align="center">(A.D. <i>830</i>)</h4>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> western wind has blown but a few days;<br> Yet the first leaf already flies from the bough.<br> On the drying paths I walk in my thin shoes;<br> In the first cold I have donned my quilted coat.<br> Through shallow ditches the floods are clearing away;<br> Through sparse bamboos trickles a slanting light.<br> In the early dusk, down an alley of green moss,<br> The garden-boy is leading the cranes home.</p>
