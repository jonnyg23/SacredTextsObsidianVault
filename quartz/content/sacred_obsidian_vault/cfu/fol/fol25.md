 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">THE GHOST-ROAD</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">The winds and the pines are whispering,<br>    The river girds in its flight,<br> My footfalls sound through ancient tiles <br>    Where grey rats flit from sight.  
 <p>What monarch raised those palace walls?<br>    Who knows to-day his name<br> Who left beneath yon precipice<br>    The stone wrack of his fame?</p></div>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Like jets of dusky blue I see<br>    Ghosts from the gloom arise,<br> Down the forgotten road return<br>    Strange rumours and faint sighs.<br> The thousand voices of the void<br>    Blend to a chant bizarre,<br> And the purple leaves are carpeted<br>    For Autumn's avatar.<br> The death-doomed legions thunder past <br>    In the wake of fleeting years;<br> I fain would drown their tramp with song, <br>    But all my songs are tears.</div>
