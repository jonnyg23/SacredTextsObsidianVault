 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="green">p. 53</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BRIGHT AUTUMNTIDE</h3>  
 <p>I climb the mountain of Tsyu-i. I look down on<br>       clear rivers.<br> Coldly the Syan speeds along, cold as it widens<br>       to meet the sea.<br> Clouds break into autumn tints, the skies are<br>       flaked with golden foam.<br> I am now in the foreign regions of Tsin and U;<br>       and countless are the miles of the trackless<br>       way, brushed by the wings of birds alone,<br>       lying between me and my native land.<br> Now with its half-disk leaning upon some island<br>       sets the evening sun.<br> The lake is beginning to glow. There soars the<br>       moon from the rim of the far-off sea.<br> And all my thoughts are plunged into the hardy<br>       loveliness of autumntide.<br> Northward I wander in dream to Yan, southward<br>       I search for Yuye…<br> The lotus is falling, falling. The river is jewelled<br>       with autumn hues.<br> Long, long the wind blows…Long, long the<br>       night wears!<br> Fain would I grasp the incredible…<br> Oh! to fly away seaward and dream for a little<br>       by its shores!…<br> To take from an island in blue ocean the six<br>       monsters—</p>  
 <p><a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="green">p. 54</font></a></p>  
 <p>Alas, there is no such length of line.<br> My hand caresses the surging wind; I am deeper<br>       drowned in sorrow.<br> I will away! away! Too strong is the life of<br>       men for me.<br> There in the magical land of P‘eng-lai I will<br>       gather the grass of immortality.</p>
