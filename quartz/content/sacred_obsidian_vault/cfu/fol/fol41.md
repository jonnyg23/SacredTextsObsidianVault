 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">BELL HILL</h3>  
 <p>In the ravine the water wanders through;<br> Soundless it laps the stems of tall bamboo.<br> Westward a tiny strip of green all scrolled<br> With fairy pennons flaunting, red and gold.<br>    Oh rare! Oh delicate is spring!<br> Thatched roofs face one another. All day long<br> Silent I dream. No bird breaks into song,<br>    The very hills are slumbering.</p>
