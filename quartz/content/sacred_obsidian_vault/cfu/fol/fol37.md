 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>A Feast of Lanterns</i>, by L. Cranmer-Byng, [1916], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">A NIGHT ON LAKE T‘AI</h3>  
 <p>Water and sky, as dusk folds down, together blend<br>     in a grey green mist<br> Clear silhouettes of the trees are limned on a<br>     sunset of rose and amethyst.<br> Moon doth creep from the bed of the deep paling<br>     the storm-black waves afar;<br> Through frosted rushes ripe oranges are gleaming<br>     golden star on star.<br> I am void of cares and affairs, so happily drink<br>     and dream in peace.<br> Loud and shrill may the reed-pipes trill; when<br>     they touch my heart they cease.<br> But my ten little painted ships to-night, where<br>     shall they anchored lie?<br> At the foot of the Tung-t‘ing mountain, on the<br>     cold deep breast of lake T‘ai,</p>
