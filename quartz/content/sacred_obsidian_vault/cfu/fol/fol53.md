 <p><a name="page_81"><font size="1" color="green">p. 81</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">LIU CH‘ANG</h3>  <h4 align="center">CIRCA A.D. 1150</h4>  <h3 align="center">AUTUMN THOUGHTS</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Moonlight</span>! the floating mists are gone, a wind<br>    unveils the deep clear night.<br> Star rivals star, and the silver river draws to her<br>    breast the dreamy light.<br> Gaunt old trees cast shadows on the plain;<br> Little birds hushed by fear are stirring, singing<br>    again,<br> And my heart is a tumult of song<br> And a torrent of wild wings shaking free.<br> Home, home, home—I hear the long<br> Shrill of the far cicada calling me.</div>
