 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="green">p. 67</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MYSELF</h3>  
 <p>What of myself?<br> I am like unto the sere chrysanthemum<br> That is shorn by the frost-blade, and, torn from its roots,<br> Whirled away on the wind.<br> Once in the valleys of Ch‘in and Yung I rambled at will,<br> Now ring me round the unfriendly plains of the wild folk of Pa.<br> O galloping dawns with Youth and Ambition riding knee to knee!<br> Ride on, Youth, with the galloping dawns and dappled days!<br> I am unhorsed, outventured—<br> I, who crouch by the crumbling embers, old, and grey, and alone.<br> One great hour of noon with the sky-faring Rukh<br> I clanged on the golden dome of Heaven.<br> Now in the long dusk of adversity<br> I have found my palace of contentment my dream pavilion;<br> Even the tiny twig of the little humble wren.</p>
