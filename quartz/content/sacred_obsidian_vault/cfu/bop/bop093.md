 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XVI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Fêng Yü;</i> narrative. A wife is consoled, in circumstances of gloom, by the arrival of her husband.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Cold is the wind, fast falls the rain,<br>   The cock aye shrilly crows.<br> But I have seen my lord again;—<br>   Now must my heart repose.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Whistles the wind, patters the rain,<br>   The cock's crow far resounds.<br> But I have seen my lord again,<br>   And healed are my heart's wounds.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>All's dark amid the wind and rain,<br>   Ceaseless the cock's clear voice!<br> But I have seen my lord again;—<br>   Should not my heart rejoice?</p></div>
