 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="green">p. 70</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Huan Lan;</i> allusive. Picture of a conceited young man of rank.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Feeble as branch of sparrow gourd, this youth,<br> Wears spike at girdle, as if he, forsooth,<br> Were quite a man; but though the spike he wears,<br> He knows not us at whom he proudly stares.<br> How easy and conceited is his mien!<br> How drop his girdle ends, full jaunty seen!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Like leaf of sparrow gourd, that coxcomb young,<br> With archer's thimble at his girdle hung!<br> He wears the thimble, but he's not the swell<br> To lord it over us who know him well.<br> How easy and conceited is his mien!<br> How drop his girdle ends, full jaunty seen!</p></div>
