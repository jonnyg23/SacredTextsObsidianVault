 <p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="green">p. 152</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Tung Mên Chih Fên;</i> narrative. Wanton associations of the young people of Ch‘ên.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The white elms by the east gate grow,<br>   And clumps of oaks crown Yüan Ch‘iu’s head;<br> There Tzŭ-chung’s daughter oft we see,<br>   Dancing about beneath their shade.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>On a bright morning they have fixed,<br>   To seek the plain that southward lies.<br> Then from her task of twisting hemp,<br>   See! dancing through the mart she hies.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The morning fair, young men and girls<br>   Together go. You hear a youth<br> Say to his mate, "O sunflower bright,<br>   Pledge me with pepper stalk your truth!"</p></div>
