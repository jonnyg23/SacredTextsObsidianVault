 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>K‘ai Fêng;</i> metaphorical and allusive. Seven sons of some family in Wei blame themselves for the restless unhappiness of their mother.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>On that jujube tree the wind,<br> From the south, blows soft and kind,<br> Till each twig, in inmost place,<br> Swells with life, and shines with grace.<br> O how great the toil and care<br> ’Twas our mother's lot to bear! <a name="page_33"><font size="1" color="green">p. 33</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>On that jujube tree the wind,<br> From the south, blows soft and kind,<br> Till its branches all are seen<br> Bright and rich in living green.<br> Wise our mother is and good;<br> Goodness we have never showed.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>See that cool and crystal spring,<br> How its waters comfort bring,<br> Welling forth the city near,<br> All who dwell in Chün to cheer!<br> Pained our mother is and tried,<br> As if help we seven denied.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>In their yellow plumage bright,<br> Lovely gleam those birds to sight,<br> And their notes fall on the ear,<br> Rich and, oh! so sweet to hear.<br> Seven sons we, without the art<br> To compose our mother's heart!</p></div>
