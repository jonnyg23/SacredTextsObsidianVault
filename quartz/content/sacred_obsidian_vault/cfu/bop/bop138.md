 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">X</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘üan Yü;</i> narrative, Some parties, probably refugees, complain of the diminished respect and attention paid to them.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>He lodged us in a spacious house,<br>   And plenteous was our fare.<br> But now at every frugal meal<br>   There's not a scrap to spare.<br> Alas! alas that this good man<br>   Could not go on as he began!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Four dishes on the mat the grain<br>   For every meal supplied.<br> The change is great, from every meal<br>   We rise unsatisfied.<br> Alas! alas that this good man<br>   Could not go on as he began!</p></div>
