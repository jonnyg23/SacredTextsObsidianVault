 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="green">p. 60</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book V. The Odes of Wei</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘i Ao;</i> allusive. The praise of Duke Wu—his assiduous cultivation of himself; his dignity; his accomplishments.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>How rich the clumps of green bamboo,<br>   Around each cove of Ch‘i!<br> They lead my thoughts to our Duke Wu;—<br>   Of winning grace is he!<br> As knife and file make smooth the bone,<br> As jade by chisel wrought and stone,<br>   Is stamp upon him set.<br> Grave and of dignity serene;<br> With force of will as plainly seen;<br> Accomplished, elegant in mien;<br>   Him we can ne’er forget.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Strong grow the clumps of green bamboo,<br>   Around each cove of Ch‘i.<br> They lead my thoughts to our Duke Wu;—<br>   Of winning grace is he!<br> His ear plugs are of pebbles fine; <a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="green">p. 61</font></a><br> And gems like stars bright glittering shine,<br>   All o’er his cap of state.<br> Grave and of dignity serene;<br> With force of will as plainly seen;<br> Accomplished, elegant in mien;<br>   Him we can ne’er forget.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>How thick the clumps of green bamboo,<br>   Around each cove of Ch‘i.<br> They lead my thoughts to our Duke Wu;—<br>   Of winning grace is he!<br> Pure as the finest tin or gold,<br> And as the scepter princes hold<br>   So strong, while mild in mood!<br> See him in car with lofty side,<br> Magnanimous and free from pride.<br> His words to jest are oft allied,<br>   But never are they rude.</p></div>
