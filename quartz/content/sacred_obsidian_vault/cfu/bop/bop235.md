 <p><a name="page_318"><font size="1" color="green">p. 318</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ts‘ai Lu;</i> narrative. A wife tells her sorrow and incapability of attending to anything, in the prolonged absence of her husband, to whom she was fondly attached.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>So full am I of anxious thought,<br> Though all the morn king grass I've sought,<br>      To fill my arms I fail.<br> Like wisp all-tangled is my hair!<br> To wash it let me home repair.<br>      My lord soon may I hail!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Though ’mong the indigo I've wrought<br> The morning long, through anxious thought,<br>      My skirt's filled but in part.<br> Within five days he was to appear.<br> The sixth has come, and he's not here.<br>      Oh! how this racks my heart!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>When here we dwelt in union sweet.<br> If the hunt called his eager feet,<br>      His bow I cased for him.<br> Or if to fish he went away,<br> And would be absent all the day,<br>      His line I put in trim. <a name="page_319"><font size="1" color="green">p. 319</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>What in his angling did he catch?<br> Well worth the time it was to watch<br>      How bream and tench he took.<br> Men thronged upon the banks and gazed;<br> At bream and tench they looked amazed,<br>      The triumphs of his hook.</p></div>
