 <p><a name="page_133"><font size="1" color="green">p. 133</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Kao Ch‘iu;</i> narrative. The people of some great officer complain of his hard treatment of them, while they declare their loyalty.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>You, of the leopard's cuff and lambkin's fur,<br>   To us have been a governor unkind;<br> But we look back on many an ancestor,<br>   And stay, when we another chief might find.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Unsympathizing, violent, and rough<br>   With us poor folks from day to day are you.<br> Man of the lambkin fur and leopard cuff,<br>   What keeps us here but hearts that still beat true?</p></div>
