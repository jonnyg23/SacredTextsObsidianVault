 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hêng Mên;</i> narrative. The contentment and happiness of a poor recluse.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>My only door some pieces of crossed wood,<br>   Within it I can rest enjoy. <a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="green">p. 153</font></a><br> I drink the water wimpling from the spring;<br>   Nor hunger can my peace destroy.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Purged from. ambition's aims, I say, "For fish,<br>   We need not bream caught in the Ho;<br> Nor, to possess the sweets of love, require<br>   To Ch‘i, to find a Chiang, to go.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>"The man contented with his lot, a meal<br>   Of fish without Ho carp can make;<br> Nor needs, to rest in his domestic joy,<br>   A Tzŭ of Sung as wife to take."</p></div>
