 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">VIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Wu I;</i> narrative. The people of Ch‘in declare their readiness, and stimulate one another, to fight in the king's cause.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Say you you have no clothes to wear?<br> My long robes let me with you share.<br> The king his armies has called out;<br> Then let us hail the battle shout.<br> My lance and spear I will prepare,<br> And as your comrade with you fare. <a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="green">p. 149</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Say you you have no clothes to wear?<br> Come and my undergarments share.<br> The king his armies has called out;<br> Then let us hail the battle shout.<br> My spear and lance I will prepare,<br> And to the field with you will fare.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Say you you have no clothes to wear?<br> My lower clothing you shall share.<br> The king his armies has called out;<br> Then let us hail the battle shout.<br> Buff coat I'll get, and weapons keen,<br> And with you on the march be seen.</p></div>
