 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XIX</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘u Ch‘i Tung Mên;</i> narrative. A man's praise of his own poor wife, contrasted with flaunting beauties.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>My path forth from the east gate lay,<br> Where cloudlike moved the girls at play.<br> Numerous are they, as clouds so bright,<br> But not on them my heart's thoughts light. <a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="green">p. 102</font></a><br> Dressed in a thin white silk, with coiffure gray,<br> Is she, my wife, my joy in life's low way.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  Forth by the covering wall's high tower,<br>   I went, and saw, like rush in flower,<br>   Each flaunting girl. Brilliant are they,<br>   But not with them my heart's thoughts stay.<br> In thin white silk, with headdress madder-dyed,<br> Is she, my sole delight, ’foretime my bride.</p></div>
