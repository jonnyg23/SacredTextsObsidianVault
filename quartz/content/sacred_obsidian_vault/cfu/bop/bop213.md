 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="green">p. 271</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">X</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ssŭ Yüeh;</i> allusive and narrative. An officer bitterly deplores the oppression and misery of the time.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>In the fourth month summer shines;<br> In the sixth the heat declines.<br> Nature thus grants men relief;<br> Tyranny gives only grief.<br> Were not my forefathers men?<br> Can my suffering ’scape their ken?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>In the cold of autumn days,<br> Each plant shrivels and decays.<br> Nature then is hard and stern;<br> Living things sad lessons learn.<br> Friends dispersed, all order gone,<br> Place of refuge have I none.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Winter days are wild and fierce;<br> Rapid gusts each crevice pierce.<br> Such is my unhappy lot,<br> Unbefriended and forgot!<br> Others all can happy be;<br> I from misery never am free.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>On the mountains are fine trees;<br> Chestnuts, plum trees, there one sees.<br> All the year their forms they show;<br> Stately more and more they grow. <a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="green">p. 272</font></a><br> Noble turned to ravening thief!<br> What the cause? This stirs my grief.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>Waters from that spring appear<br> Sometimes foul, and sometimes clear,<br> Changing oft, as falls the rain,<br> Or the sky grows bright again.<br> New misfortunes every day<br> Still befall me, misery's prey.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>Aid from mighty streams obtained,<br> Southern states are shaped and drained.<br> Thus the Chiang and Han are thanked,<br> And as benefactors ranked.<br> Weary toil my vigor drains;<br> All unnoticed it remains!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_7">7</a></font></span>Hawks and eagles mount the sky;<br> Sturgeons in deep waters lie.<br> Out of reach, they safety get,<br> Arrow fear not, nor the net.<br> Hiding place for me there's none;<br> Here I stay, and make my moan.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_8">8</a></font></span>Ferns upon the hills abound;<br> <i>Ch‘i</i> and <i>i</i> in marshy ground.<br> Each can boast its proper place,<br> Where it grows for use or grace.<br> I can only sing the woe,<br> Which, ill-starred, I undergo.</p></div>
