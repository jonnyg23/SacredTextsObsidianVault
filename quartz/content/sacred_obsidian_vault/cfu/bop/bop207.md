 <p><a name="page_258"><font size="1" color="green">p. 258</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘iao Yen;</i> narrative, and allusive, with the metaphorical element perhaps here and there. Some one, suffering from the king through slander, appeals to Heaven, dwells on the nature and evil of slander, and expresses his detestation of and contempt for the slanderers.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>O vast and distant Heaven, whom we<br> Father and mother call, on thee<br> I cry. Say why these ills on me<br>         Excessive fall.<br> Oppressive, vast, my misery,<br>         Though guiltless all!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The first small lie contains the rest.<br> When slanders fill our monarch's breast,<br> Ills grow, and never are redressed.<br>         Would he but trust<br> The good, wrongs soon must be redressed.<br>         I know they must. <a name="page_259"><font size="1" color="green">p. 259</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>His frequent covenants show him weak.<br> Wrongs grow from cozening words they speak.<br> He trusts the rogues that lie and sneak,<br>         And make things worse.<br> Their duties shirked, their words so meek<br>         Prove but a curse.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>With the great work of some great mind;—<br> A temple by true king designed,<br> Or plans by sagest men outlined,<br>         I'm in a fog.<br> Round common schemes my way I wind,<br>         Like hare and dog.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>As timber soft in carver's hand<br> Assumes the shape he may command,<br> So common speech to understand,<br>         I well may claim.<br> Those talkers, flowing, artful, grand,<br>         Are sons of shame. <a name="page_260"><font size="1" color="green">p. 260</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_6">6</a></font></span>And who are they? On yonder stream<br> They dwell; and void of strength they seem.<br> From men so bloated who would dream<br>         Of martial force?<br> Both they and theirs may madly scheme,<br>         And fare the worse!</p></div>
