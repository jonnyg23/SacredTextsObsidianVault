 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Sang-chung;</i> narrative. A. gentleman boasts of his intimacy and intrigues with various noble ladies.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The gold thread to gather I'm going,<br>   Where in Wei it spreads over the tree;<br> But my thoughts forever are flowing<br>   To the beauty who captive holds me,— <a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="green">p. 52</font></a><br> To the eldest Chiang. Yes, it is she!<br>   Freely did she agree in Sang-chung,<br>   She would come to me here in Shang-kung,<br> And then company keep with me on to the Ch‘i.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>For the wheat about Wei I'm going<br>   To the north where it grows in each part.<br> But my thoughts forever are flowing<br>   To the beauty who rules in my heart,—<br> To the eldest I. Yes, it is she!<br>   Freely did she agree in Sang-chung,<br>   She would come to me here in Shang-kung,<br> And then company keep with me on to the Ch‘i.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>For the mustard plant I am going<br>   Where in Wei it grows over the east.<br> But my thoughts forever are flowing<br>   To the beauty on whose love I feast,—<br> To the eldest Yung. Yes, it is she!<br>   Freely did she agree in Sang-chung,<br>   She would come to me here in Shang-kung,<br> And then company keep with me on to the Ch‘i.</p></div>
