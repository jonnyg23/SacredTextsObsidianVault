 <p><a name="page_100"><font size="1" color="green">p. 100</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">XVII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Tzŭ Chin;</i> narrative. A lady mourns the indifference and absence of her student lover.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>You student, with the collar blue,<br>   Long pines my heart with anxious pain.<br> Although I do not go to you,<br>   Why from all word do you refrain?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>O you, with girdle strings of blue,<br>   My thoughts to you forever roam!<br> Although I do not go to you,<br>   Yet why to me should you not come?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>How reckless you, how light and wild,<br>   There by the tower upon the wall!<br> One day, from sight of you exiled,<br>   As long as three long months I call.</p></div>
