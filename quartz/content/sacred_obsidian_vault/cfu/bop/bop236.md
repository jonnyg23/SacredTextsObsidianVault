 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chu Miao;</i> allusive and narrative. Celebrating the service of the earl of Shao in building the city of Seay, and the cheerful alacrity of his soldiers under his management.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>As the young millet, by the genial rain<br>   Enriched, shoots up luxuriant and tall,<br> So, when we southward marched with toil and pain,<br>   The earl of Shao cheered and inspired us all.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>We pushed our barrows, and our burdens bore;<br>   We drove our wagons, and our oxen led.<br> "The work once done, our labor there is o’er,<br>   And home we travel," to ourselves we said. <a name="page_320"><font size="1" color="green">p. 320</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Close kept our footmen round the chariot track;<br>   Our eager host in close battalions sped.<br> "When once our work is done, then we go back,<br>   Our labor over," to themselves they said.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Hard was the work we had at Seay to do,<br>   But Shao's great earl the city soon upreared<br> The host its service gave with ardor true;—<br>   Such power in all the earl's commands appeared!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>We did on plains and low lands what was meet;<br>   We cleared the springs and streams, the land to drain.<br> The earl of Shao announced his work complete,<br>   And the king's heart reposed, at rest again.</p></div>
