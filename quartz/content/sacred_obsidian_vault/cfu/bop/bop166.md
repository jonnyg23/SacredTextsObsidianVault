 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p>The <i>Huang Huang Chê Hua;</i> allusive and narrative. An ode appropriate to the dispatch of an envoy; complimentary to him, and suggesting instructions as to the discharge of his duty.</p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>As the flower that blooms bright on the mountain or lea,<br> Is the legate, whom charged with high mission we see. <a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="green">p. 186</font></a><br> With his suite, all alert and aye watchful he hies,<br> That his hand may achieve what his heart shall devise.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>"Fresh and young are my steeds," so he sang as he sped,<br> "And the six reins in hand look with ointment o’erspread.<br> So hurrah! my good horses, dash on at your best,<br> As now here, and now there, I am pushing my quest.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>"Many-spotted my coursers, whose hues finely blend,<br> And the six reins in hand, soft as silk, freely bend.<br> So hurrah! my good horses, strain tendons and thews,<br> As now here, and now there, I am searching for news.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>"With black manes and white coats are the steeds of my car,<br> And the gleam of the six glossy reins shines afar.<br> So hurrah! my good horses, ply muscle and leg,<br> As now here, and now there, for wise counsel I beg. <a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="green">p. 187</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>"Dark, with white interspersed, are the coursers I drive;<br> ’Gainst my hands, the reins grasping, in vain would they strive.<br> So hurrah! my good horses, speed onwards and fly,<br> As now here, and now there, much inquiring I pry."</p></div>
