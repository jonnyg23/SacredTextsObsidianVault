 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Lu Ling;</i> narrative. The admiration in Ch‘i of hounds and hunters.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span><i>Ling-ling</i> go the hounds; <i>ling-ling</i> the hounds go.<br> Their master is kindly and good, as we know. <a name="page_112"><font size="1" color="green">p. 112</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>With double rings furnished, on go the hounds;<br> In goodness and grace their master abounds.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Three rings at their necks, they rush to the chase;<br> Their master is famous for power and for grace.</p></div>
