 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="green">p. 26</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Lü I</i>, metaphorical and allusive. The complaint, sad but resigned, of a neglected wife.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>When the upper robe is green,<br> With a yellow lining seen,<br> There we have a certain token<br> Right is wronged and order broken.<br> How can sorrow from my heart<br> In a case like this depart?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Color green the robe displays;<br> Lower garment yellow's blaze.<br> Thus it is that favorite mean<br> In the place of wife is seen.<br> Vain the conflict with my grief;<br> Memory denies relief.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Yes, ’twas you the green who dyed,<br> You who fed the favorite's pride.<br> Anger rises in my heart,<br> Pierces it as with a dart.<br> But on ancient rules lean I,<br> Lest to wrong my thoughts should fly. <a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Fine or coarse, if thin the dress,<br> Cold winds always cause distress.<br> Hard my lot, my sorrow deep,<br> But my thoughts in check I keep.<br> Ancient story brings to mind<br> Sufferers who were resigned.</p></div>
