 <p><a name="page_223"><font size="1" color="green">p. 223</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>T‘ing Liao;</i> narrative. Describing the anxiety of some king—supposed to be King Hsüan—not to be late at his morning levee.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>How goes the night? For heavy morning sleep<br> Ill suits the king who men would loyal keep.<br> The courtyard, ruddy with the torch's light,<br> Proclaims unspent the deepest hour of night.<br> Already near the gate my lords appear;<br> Their tinkling bells salute my wakeful ear.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>How goes the night? I may not slumber on.<br> Although not yet the night is wholly gone,<br> The paling torchlight in the court below<br> Gives token that the hours swift-footed go.<br> Already at the gate my lords appear;<br> Their tinkling bells with measured sound draw near.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>How goes the night? I may not slumber now.<br> The darkness smiles with morning on its brow.<br> The courtyard torch no more gives forth its ray,<br> But heralds with its smoke the coming day.<br> My princes pass the gate, and gather there;<br> I see their banners floating in the air.</p></div>
