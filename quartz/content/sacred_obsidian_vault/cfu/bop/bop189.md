 <p><a name="page_221"><font size="1" color="green">p. 221</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Chi Jih;</i> narrative. Celebrating a hunting expedition by King Hsüan on a smaller scale, attended by the officers of the court, and within the royal domain.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The day <i>Mow-shin</i> was lucky found;<br>   Then to the sire of steeds we prayed.<br> Our cars and teams and gear were good;<br>   We scour the heights where wild game strayed.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>And <i>Keng-wu</i> also lucky proved;<br>   We picked our steeds, and chose our ground,—<br> Where stags and does by Ts‘eih and Ts‘eu<br>   Made sport for him whom Heaven had crowned.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>We viewed the plain where teeming game<br>   Now shivering stand, now frantic run;<br> Here two, there three. We charged along,<br>   Pleasure to yield to Heaven's great son.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>We bend our bows; our shafts we grasp;<br>   There lies the huge behemoth low,<br> And boars are pierced:—spoil for the guests,<br>   At court, when wine cups overflow.</p></div>
