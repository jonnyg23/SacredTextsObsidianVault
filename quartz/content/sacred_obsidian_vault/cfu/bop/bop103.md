 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Tung Fang Wei Ming;</i> narrative and metaphorical. The irregularity and disorder of the court of Ch‘i; as seen especially in the time of giving the morning audience.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>I was putting my clothes on upside down,<br>   Before the eastern sky was clear.<br> I was putting my clothes on upside down,<br>   When a call to the court came here.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>I was putting my clothes on upside down,<br>   Ere showed the east the rising flame.<br> I was putting my clothes on upside down,<br>   When from the court an order came. <a name="page_109"><font size="1" color="green">p. 109</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>On garden fence, made but of willow wands,<br>   E’en reckless fellows look with fear.<br> Our prince can't tell the night from dawn;—<br>   Too soon, or late, his calls appear!</p></div>
