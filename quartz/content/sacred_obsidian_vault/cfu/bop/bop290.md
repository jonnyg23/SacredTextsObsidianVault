 <p><a name="page_446"><font size="1" color="green">p. 446</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘ien;</i> narrative. Sung in the first month of winter, and in spring, when the king presented a fish in the ancestral temple.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">In Ch‘i and Chü, those streams of I,<br>   About the warrens fish abound.<br> Large swarms of various kinds we see.<br>   The mudfish and the carp are found.  
 <p>The <i>thryssa</i> and the yellow jaws,<br>   And sturgeons, large and lank, are there,<br> Which we, observant of the laws,<br>   Offer in sacrifice, with prayer,<br> That Chou may hold its brightening way,<br> Nor o’er its fortunes come decay.</p></div>
