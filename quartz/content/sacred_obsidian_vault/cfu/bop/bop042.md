 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XIV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘üan Shui;</i> allusive and narrative. A daughter of the house of Wei, married in another state, expresses her longing to revisit Wei.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>As the streamlet from its spring<br>   Flows into the river Ch‘i,<br> So my daily thoughts on wing <a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="green">p. 42</font></a><br>   Fly, my native Wei, to thee;<br> For I long with cousins there<br> Counsel sweet and love to share.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>For a night, at Tzu I stayed;<br>   Drank the cup to Ni when come;<br> Parents, brothers, farewell bade;—<br>   Such the fate on leaving home.<br> Parents are not now alive;<br> Aunts and sister still survive.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Lo! I hasten home again,<br>   Let the rushing chariot wheel<br> Pause at Kan, and part at Yen,<br>   Pebbles flashing to its steel.<br> Does my heart go far astray,<br> Panting for its native Wei?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>By the Fei-ch‘üan’s winding stream<br>   Daily sighing thought will stray.<br> Hsü and Ts‘ao in memory gleam,<br>   Broken glints of childhood's day.<br> Spring, my horses! Speed, my wheels!<br> Gone the grief my bosom feels!</p></div>
