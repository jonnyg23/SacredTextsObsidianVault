 <p><a name="page_34"><font size="1" color="green">p. 34</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hsiung Chih;</i> allusive and narrative. A wife deplores the absence of her husband, and celebrates his virtue.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Away the startled pheasant flies,<br>   With lazy movement of his wings.<br> Borne was my heart's lord from my eyes;—<br>   What pain the separation brings!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The pheasant, though no more in view,<br>   His cry, below, above, forth sends.<br> Alas! my princely lord, ‘tis you,—<br>   Your absence, that my bosom rends.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>At sun and moon I sit and gaze,<br>   In converse with my troubled heart.<br> Far, far from me my husband stays!<br>   When will he come to heal its smart?</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Ye princely men, who with him mate,<br>   Say, mark ye not his virtuous way.<br> His rule is—covet nought, none hate;—<br>   How can his steps from goodness stray?</p></div>
