 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>T‘o Hsi;</i> metaphorical. An appeal from the inferior officers of Chêng to their superiors on the sad condition of the state.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Ye withered leaves, ye withered. leaves,<br>     Blown by the wind away!<br> So tossed is Chêng. My spirit grieves<br>     To see its sad decay.<br> Ye uncles, nobles of the land,<br> Reform the state; we'll by you stand! <a name="page_96"><font size="1" color="green">p. 96</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Ye withered leaves, ye withered leaves,<br>   By winds so wildly tossed!<br> What grief my mourning heart receives<br>   From Chêng thus foully lost!<br> Uncles, your starting note we wait;<br> We'll follow and reform the state.</p></div>
