 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book II. The Praise Odes of Lu</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Kung;</i> narrative. Celebrating some marquis of Lu for his constant and admirable thoughtfulness, especially as seen in the number and quality of his horses.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1%C2%A0%C2%A0">1  </a></font></span>On the wide plains, our frontiers near,<br>   The stallions, sleek and large, appear.<br>   There, sleek and large, they meet our sight;<br>   Some black, with their hind quarters white;<br>   Pale yellow, some; some black; some bay:—<br>   For carriage teams good horses they! <a name="page_463"><font size="1" color="green">p. 463</font></a><br> To the duke's thoughts we can assign no bound;<br> Turned to his steeds, lo! thus good are they found!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>  On the wide plains, our frontiers near,<br>   The stallions, sleek and large, appear.<br>   Those stallions, sleek and large, are seen;<br>   Some piebald,—white and flushed with green,<br>   And others white, with yellow sheen;<br>   Some chestnuts; and some dapple gray:—<br>   From carriage teams strong horses they!<br> To the duke's thoughts no limit can we set;<br> Turned to his steeds, such is the strength they get!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>  On the wide plains, our frontiers near,<br>   The stallions, sleek and large, appear.<br>   Oh! sleek and large, those sprightly males!<br>   Some that appear as flecked with scales;<br>   Some black, with manes of spotless white;<br>   Some white or red, manes dark as night:—<br>   In carriage yoked, obedient quite!<br> The duke's thoughts never cease and never tire;<br> Turned to his steeds, lo! thus they rule their fire! <a name="page_464"><font size="1" color="green">p. 464</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>  On the wide plains, our frontiers near,<br>   The stallions, sleek and large, appear.<br>   Oh! sleek and large, those stallions bright!<br>   Cream-colored, some; some, red and white;<br>   Some, with white hairy legs; with eyes<br>   Like those of fishes, some:—men prize<br>   Such horses, grand in strength and size.<br> His thoughts without depravity, our prince<br> Thinks of his steeds, and such powers they evince!</p></div>
