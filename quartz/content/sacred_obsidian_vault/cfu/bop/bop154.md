 <p><a name="page_164"><font size="1" color="green">p. 164</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hou Jên;</i> allusive and metaphorical. Lament over the favor shown to worthless officers at the court of Ts‘ao. And the discountenance of good men.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Each warden of the gates appears,<br> With lancers and with halberdiers,<br>   As well befits his place;<br> But these three hundred men, who shine<br> Grand in their red knee covers fine,<br>   Only the court disgrace.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Like pelicans, upon the dam<br> Which stand, and there their pouches cram,<br>   Unwet the while their wings,<br> Are those who their rich dress display,<br> But no befitting service pay,<br>   Intent on meanest things.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Like pelicans which eager watch<br> Upon the dam, their prey to catch,<br>   And spare to wet the beak,<br> Are those who richest favors share,<br> But take no part in toil or care,<br>   Nor the state's welfare seek. <a name="page_165"><font size="1" color="green">p. 165</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Like grass luxuriant on its side,<br> While morning mists the south hill hide,<br>   These creatures seem to grow;<br> But men of worth, like virtuous maid,<br> Lovely but poor, denied wealth's aid,<br>   No recognition know.</p></div>
