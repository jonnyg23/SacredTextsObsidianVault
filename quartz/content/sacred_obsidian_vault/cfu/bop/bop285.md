The Decade of Ch‘ên Kung</span></h3>  <h3 align="center">I</h3>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ch‘ên Kung;</i> narrative. Instructions given to the officers of husbandry;—probably after the sacrifice to God in spring for a good year.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">Ho! ministers of husbandry;<br>   Your duties reverent care demand.<br> The king has given you perfect rules;—<br>   See that those rules ye understand.  
 <p>Ho! ye who aid the ministers,<br>   The last month of our spring is here. <a name="page_442"><font size="1" color="green">p. 442</font></a><br> ’Tis yours the new-sown fields to tend,<br>   And manage those in their third year.<br> How fair each wheat and barley field,<br> That soon their waving crops shall yield!</p>  
 <p>In them the bright and glorious God<br>   Will grant to us a plenteous year;<br> Give orders that, with spud and hoe<br>   Provided well, your men appear.<br> Anon the stalks that rustling stand<br> Shall fall before the reaper's hand.</p></div>
