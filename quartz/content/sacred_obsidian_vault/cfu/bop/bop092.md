 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Tung Mên Chih Shan;</i> narrative, A woman thinks of her lover's residence, and complains that he does not come to her.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>By th’ eastern gate, flat lies the ground,<br>   And madder there grows on the slope.<br> Hard by my lover's house is found;<br>   He keeps away, and mocks my hope. <a name="page_99"><font size="1" color="green">p. 99</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Where chestnuts grow, near th’ eastern gate,<br>   There stands a row, where is your home.<br> My heart turns aye to you, its mate,<br>   But ah! to me you never come!</p></div>
