 <p><a name="page_345"><font size="1" color="green">p. 345</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VI</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ssŭ Ch‘i;</i> narrative. The virtue of King Wên and its wonderful effects; with the excellent character of his mother and wife.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Ta-jên was pure, of rev’rent life,<br>     From whom our King Wên sprang.<br> Fit was she for his father's wife,<br>     And well she loved Chou Chiang.<br> Ta-ssŭ inherited her fame;<br> Through her an hundred sons there came.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Wên formed himself upon his sires,<br>     Nor gave their spirits pain.<br> Well pleased were they. Next he inspires<br>     His wife. His brethren fain<br> To follow were. In every state<br> The chiefs on his example wait. <a name="page_346"><font size="1" color="green">p. 346</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>In palace see him,—bland, serene;<br>     In fane, with rev’rent fear.<br> Unseen by man, he felt still seen<br>     By spirits always near.<br> Unweariedly did he maintain<br> His virtue pure, and free from stain.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>Some great calamities there came,<br>     Which he could not control.<br> But none his generous aim might blame,<br>     Nought darken his bright soul.<br> Untaught, the right he ever saw;<br> Reproof he needed not, nor law.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_5">5</a></font></span>Grown men through him in virtue grew;<br>     Young men attainments made.<br> Aye to himself our prince was true,<br>     Nor weariness displayed.<br> His officers acquired great fame;<br> To him they owed their deathless name.</p></div>
