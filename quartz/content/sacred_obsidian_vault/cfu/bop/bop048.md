 <p><a name="page_48"><font size="1" color="green">p. 48</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book IV. The Odes of Yung</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Po Chou;</i> allusive. Protest of a widow against being urged to marry again.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>In the mid Ho that cypress boat floats free,<br> While friends a second marriage press on me.<br> I see my husband's youthful forehead there,<br> And on it the twin tufts of falling hair.<br> Rather than wed again I'll die, I swear!<br> O mother dear, O Heaven supreme, why should<br> You not allow my vow, and aid my purpose good?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Near to the bank that cypress boat floats free,<br> While friends a second marriage press on me.<br> He was my only one, with forehead fair,<br> And on it the twin tufts of falling hair.<br> Till death to shun the evil thing I swear!<br> O mother dear, O Heaven supreme, why should<br> You not allow my vow, and aid my purpose good?</p></div>
