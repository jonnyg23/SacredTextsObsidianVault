 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book VII. Decade of Sang Hu</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Sang Hu;</i> allusive and narrative. The king, entertaining the chief among the feudal princes, expresses his admiration of them, and good wishes for them.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Flitting round the greenbeaks see,<br> With their wings of brilliancy!<br> Birds they are that men admire;<br> More those lords my soul inspire<br> With admiring joy and love.<br> Heaven will bless them from above!  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Here and there the greenbeaks light,<br> Showing necks with feathers bright. <a name="page_296"><font size="1" color="green">p. 296</font></a><br> Who but must the creatures prize?<br> But it more delights my eyes,<br> When these noble lords are seen,<br> Who my states from danger screen.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Screens they are, and bulwarks strong;<br> All the chiefs around them throng,<br> And on them as patterns gaze.<br> Self-restraint each hero lays<br> On himself,—from folly free;—<br> Great their happiness must be!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>See the mighty cup of horn,<br> Round their ranks in order borne!<br> Full of spirits soft and good,<br> It excites no conduct rude.<br> Surely blessings haste to greet<br> Lords of virtue so complete!</p></div>
