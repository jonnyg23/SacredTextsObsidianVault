 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Pai Chü;</i> narrative. The writer expresses his regret at the abandonment of public life by an officer whom he admired.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Free let the brilliant white colt eat<br>   The tenderest produce in my yard.<br> Secure it by the neck and feet;<br>   And this morn's pleasure safely guard.<br> Its owner, cherished in my heart,<br>   Shall then with me at ease abide.<br> Alas that he should e’er depart.<br>   And hermitlike his merit hide! <a name="page_228"><font size="1" color="green">p. 228</font></a>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Free let the brilliant white colt eat<br>   The bean sprouts growing in my yard.<br> Secure it by the neck and feet,<br>   This evening's joy thus safely guard.<br> Its owner, cherished in my heart,<br>   Shall then be here, a guest admired.<br> O could I wile him from the part<br>   He wants to play, from men retired!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>O leave thy colt of brilliant white!<br>   If thou to me would’st blithely come,<br> As duke or marquis, honors bright<br>   Thou should’st obtain, and in thy home<br> Find endless joy. Try to restrain,<br>   With strictest curb, thy roaming mind:<br> And from the hermit life refrain,<br>   To which thou art so much inclined.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_4">4</a></font></span>’Tis vain. The brilliant white colt view,<br>   Deep in that empty valley stand,<br> Before it placed a bundle new<br>   Of grass plucked by its master's hand.<br> That master as a gem I hold.<br>   O that, relenting, he may send<br> What I'll prize more than gems or gold,—<br>   News that he still remains my friend!</p></div>
