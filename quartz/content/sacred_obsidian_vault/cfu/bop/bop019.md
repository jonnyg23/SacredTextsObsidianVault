 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="green">p. 15</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">V</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Kan T‘ang;</i> narrative. The love of the people for the memory of the duke of Shao makes them love the trees beneath which he had rested.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>O fell not that sweet pear tree!<br>   See how its branches spread.<br>     Spoil not its shade,<br>     For Shao's chief laid<br>   Beneath it his weary head.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>O clip not that sweet pear tree!<br>   Each twig and leaflet spare.<br>     ’Tis sacred now,<br>     Since the lord of Shao,<br>   When weary, rested him there.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>O touch not that sweet pear tree!<br>   Bend not a twig of it now.<br>     There long ago,<br>     As the stories show,<br>   Oft halted the chief of Shao.</p></div>
