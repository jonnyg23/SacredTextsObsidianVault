 <p><a name="page_111"><font size="1" color="green">p. 111</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">VII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Fu T‘ien;</i> metaphorical. The folly of pursuing objects beyond one's strength.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>The weeds will but the ranker grow,<br>   If fields too large you seek to till.<br> To try to gain men far away<br>   With grief your toiling heart will fill.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>If fields too large you seek to till,<br>   The weeds will only rise more strong.<br> To try to gain men far away<br>   Will but your heart's distress prolong.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Things grow the best when to themselves<br>   Left, and to nature's vigor rare.<br> How young and tender is the child,<br>   With his twin tufts of falling hair!<br> But when you him erelong behold,<br>   That child shall cap of manhood wear!</p></div>
