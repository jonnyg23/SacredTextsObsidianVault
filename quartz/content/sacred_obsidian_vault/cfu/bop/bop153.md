 <p><a name="page_163"><font size="1" color="green">p. 163</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book XIV. The Odes of Ts‘ao</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Fou Yu;</i> metaphorical. Against some parties in the state, occupied with frivolous pursuits, and oblivious of important matters.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Like splendid robes appear the wings<br>   Of the ephemeral fly;<br> And such the pomp of those great men,<br>   Which soon in dust shall lie!<br> I grieve! Would they but come to me!<br>   To teach them I should try.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The wings of the ephemeral fly<br>   Are robes of colors gay;<br> And such the glory of those men,<br>   Soon crumbling to decay!<br> I grieve! Would they but rest with me,<br>   They’d learn a better way!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>The ephemeral fly bursts from its hole,<br>   With gauzy wings like snow;<br> So quick the rise, so quick the fall,<br>   Of those great men we know!<br> I grieve! Would they but lodge with me,<br>   Forth they would wiser go.</p></div>
