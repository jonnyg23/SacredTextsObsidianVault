 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">IX</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>T‘iao Chih Hua;</i> metaphorical. The writer laments his misery amidst and in consequence of the general decay of the kingdom.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>Deep yellow are turned<br>   The bignonia flowers; <a name="page_329"><font size="1" color="green">p. 329</font></a><br> And my wounded heart<br>   Its sorrow outpours.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The flowers are all gone;<br>   But green leaves are seen.<br> Than this fate have known,<br>   Better not to have been!</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>In fish traps but stars!<br>   Ewes thin, with large head!<br> While some may have food,<br>   Most languish, ill fed.</p></div>
