 <p><a name="page_298"><font size="1" color="green">p. 298</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">III</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Kuei Pien;</i> narrative, with allusive and metaphorical portions in all the stanzas. Celebrating a feast given by the king, at which he is present himself, to his relatives both by consanguinity and affinity.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>In their bonnets of deerskin, who are they that haste?<br> Who the spirits so good, and such viands may taste?<br> Not a stranger among them, but all of thy kin,—<br> Certes none but thy brethren such honor could win.<br> As the mosses and mistletoe cling to the tree,<br> So their hearts, O our sovereign, cling closely to thee.<br> While they see not they face, they are restless and sad,<br> But a smile from thy lips makes them happy and glad.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>In their bonnets of deerskin, who are they that haste?<br> Who such viands in season and spirits may taste?<br> Not a stranger among them, thy brethren are here;<br> Only they at such banquet with thee could appear. <a name="page_299"><font size="1" color="green">p. 299</font></a><br> As the mosses and mistletoe grow on the pine,<br> So their hearts, O our sovereign, around thee entwine.<br> While they see not thy face, all is dark and forlorn,<br> But a glance from thine eyes is to them as the morn.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>In their bonnets of deerskin, adorning each head,<br> Now they quail the clear spirits, and lordly are fed.<br> With thy brothers are kinsmen of every degree;<br> Near or distant, they share the banquet with thee.<br> When the sleet first descends, weatherwise, we well know,<br> Winter soon will be here with its garments of snow.<br> Death and mourning may come in our moments of glee;<br> ’Tis not long, O ye guests, that each other you'll see.<br> O’er your cups now be glad, when the daylight has ceased,<br> And do thou, O our sovereign, rejoice in the feast.</p></div>
