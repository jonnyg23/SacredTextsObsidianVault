 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="green">p. 14</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">IV</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Ts‘ai Pin;</i> narrative. The diligence and reverence of the young wife of an officer, doing her part in sacrificial offerings.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>She gathers fast the large duckweed,<br>   From valley stream that southward flows;<br> And for the pondweed to the pools<br>   Left on the plains by floods she goes.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>The plants, when closed her toil, she puts<br>   In baskets round and baskets square.<br> Then home she hies to cook her spoil,<br>   In pans and tripods ready there.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>In sacred chamber this she sets,<br>   Where the light falls down through the wall.<br> ’Tis she, our lord's young reverent wife,<br>   Who manages this service all.</p></div>
