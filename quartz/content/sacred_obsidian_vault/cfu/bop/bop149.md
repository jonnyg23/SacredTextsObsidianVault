 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Book XIII. The Odes of Kuei</span></h3>  <h4 align="center">I</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Kao Ch‘iu;</i> narrative. Some officer of Kuei laments over the frivolous character of his ruler, fond of displaying his robes, instead of attending to the duties of government.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>In lamb's-fur robe you lounge about,<br>   Hold court in fox fur clad.<br> Such habits wake my anxious thought;—<br>   My weary heart is sad.<br> When thus you slight each rule of dress,<br>   Must not your rule be bad?  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>Aimless you roam in lamb's-fur robe,<br>   In fox fur grace the hall. <a name="page_160"><font size="1" color="green">p. 160</font></a><br> Such habits wake my anxious thought,<br>   And fill my heart with gall.<br> When thus you slight the laws of dress,<br>   You'll heed no laws at all.</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>Your glossy lamb's fur in the sun<br>   Gleams as with ointment's sheen.<br> ’Tis this that wakes my anxious thought,<br>   My heart's core pierces keen.<br> That thus you slight the laws of dress,<br>   Is sorry sign I ween.</p></div>
