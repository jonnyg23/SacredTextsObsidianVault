 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="green">p. 106</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">II</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Hsüan;</i> narrative. Frivolous and vainglorious compliments interchanged by the hunters of Ch‘i.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>"How agile you are!" ’Twas thus that I spoke,<br>   What time near to Nao together we drew.<br> Two boars three years old from cover then broke,<br>   And we in our chariots after them flew.<br> The chase being over, you said with a bow,<br> "If agile am I, as active art thou!"  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>"How fine is your skill!" So said I to you;<br>   ’Twas when near to Nao we met on the way.<br> That moment two males attracted our view,<br>   And at them we dashed, to make them our prey.<br> The chase being over, you said with a bow,<br> "If skillful am I, not less so art thou!"</p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_3">3</a></font></span>"How you know your art!" I said to you then,<br>   When south of Mount Nao together we came.<br> That moment two wolves came under our ken,<br>   And hotly we drove, well pleased with the game.<br> The chase being over, you said with a bow,<br> "If I know my art, as artful art thou!"</p></div>
