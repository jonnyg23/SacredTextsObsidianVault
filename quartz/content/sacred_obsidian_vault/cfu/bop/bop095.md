 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Book of Poetry</i>, tr. by James Legge, [1876], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XVIII</h4>  
 <p></p><div style="font-size: 90%">The <i>Yang Chih Shui;</i> allusive. One party asserts good faith to another, and protests against people who would make them doubt each other.</div>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;"><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_1">1</a></font></span>    Fretted its waters seem,<br>   Yet gently flows the stream:—<br> A bundle of thorns ’twill not bear. <a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="green">p. 101</font></a><br>     Our brethren are so few;<br>     There are but I and you:—<br> Let nothing our friendship impair.<br>     People's words don't believe;<br>     They are meant to deceive:—<br> Their purpose is but to ensnare.  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_2">2</a></font></span>    Fretted its waters seem,<br>     Yet gently flows the stream:—<br> A bundle of wood ’twill not bear.<br>     Our brethren are so few;<br>     There are only we two:—<br> Let nothing our friendship impair.<br>     Trust not the people's breath;<br>     They don't deserve your faith:—<br> Their purpose is but to ensnare.</p></div>
