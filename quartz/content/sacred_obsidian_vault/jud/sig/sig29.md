 <p><a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 41</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">26</h3>  <h3 align="center">MY LORD AND KING</h3>  
 <p>Almighty God, on lofty throne<br>   In wisdom Thou didst build the world,<br>   Thy might the firmament unfurled<br> And Thou wast King ere kings were known.</p>  
 <p>Sole King, who hung the earth on naught,<br>   In great assemblies I will cry,<br>   For every soul must testify,<br> The Lord of hosts rules all He wrought.</p>  
 <p>His seat is hid in mystery,<br>   Myriads of holy ones in dread,<br>   His ministers in lowlihead,<br> Surround His awful Majesty.</p>  
 <p>His praises in set order sing,<br>   Although all praise He hath outsoared,<br>   Declare the Kingdom of the Lord,<br> Proclaiming that the Lord is King.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 42</font></a></p>  
 <p>The depths of sky His mercy planned,<br>   The waters are His footstools. He<br>   Their measures gave to stream and sea<br> And poured them in with royal hand.</p>  
 <p>The sea unto His bounds submits,<br>   Our King and God, so great and high,<br>   His glory covers all the sky<br> When that upon His throne He sits.</p>  
 <p>Sole King, He spreads for curtain Space,<br>   The sun uprises from the east<br>   To draw from earth a dainty feast,<br> A strong man glad to run a race.</p>  
 <p>O glorious Sovereign whom I sing,<br>   Be gracious unto us and kind,<br>   For Thine own sake, if but I find<br> Grace in Thine eyes, my Lord and King.</p>
