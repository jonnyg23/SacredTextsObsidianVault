 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Selected Religious Poems of Solomon ibn Gabirol</i>, tr. by Israel Zangwill, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">XXIX.</h4>  
 <p>O Lord, who can comprehend Thy power?<br> For Thou hast created for the splendour of Thy glory a pure radiance<br> "Hewn from the rock of rocks and digged from the bottom of the pit."<br> Thou hast imparted to it the spirit of wisdom<br> And called it the Soul.<br> And of flames of intellectual fire hast Thou wrought its form,<br> And like a burning fire hast Thou wafted it, <a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a><br> And sent it to the body to serve and guard it,<br> And it is as fire in the midst thereof yet doth not consume it,<br> For it is from the fire of the soul that the body hath been created,<br> And goeth from Nothingness to Being,<br> "Because the Lord descended on him in fire."</p>
