 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 65</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">38</h3>  <h3 align="center">THE LORD OF HEAVEN</h3>  
 <p>The seven heavens cannot Thee enfold,<br>   Sustained by Thee, they do not Thee sustain.<br> They hymn Thee since Thou madest them of old,<br>   And when they perish, Thou shalt still remain,<br>     O mighty God!</p>  
 <p>The messengers of heaven Thee revere.<br>   They stand to praise Thee in Thine inmost shrine,<br> Yet from beholding Thee they shrink in fear,<br>   For how behold the dazzling dread Divine?<br>     O Lord, my God!</p>  
 <p>What voice is this that singeth without cease<br>   And spends in song to Thee its nights and days?<br> But Thou, omnipotence beyond increase,<br>   Art highI knowuplifted over praise,<br>     O Lord, my God!</p>  
 <p>So great Thy majesty and manifold,<br>   How canst Thou lodge in tabernacles span? <a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a><br> Such glory no circumference can hold,<br>   For Thou art vastly mightier than man,<br>     O Lord, my God!</p>  
 <p>He at whose feet celestial creatures creep<br>   A day of liberation will proclaim,<br> And from all corners call his scattered sheep,<br>   However sorry-looking they or lame,<br>     The Lord, my God!</p>
