 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Selected Religious Poems of Solomon ibn Gabirol</i>, tr. by Israel Zangwill, [1923], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">III.</h4>  
 <p>Thou existest, but hearing of ear cannot reach Thee, or vision of eye,<br> Nor shall the How have sway over Thee, nor the Wherefore and Whence.<br> Thou existest, but for Thyself and for none other with Thee.<br> Thou existest, and before Time began Thou wast,<br> And without place Thou didst abide.<br> Thou existest, and Thy secret is hidden and who shall attain to it?<br> "So deep, so deep, who can discover it?"</p>
