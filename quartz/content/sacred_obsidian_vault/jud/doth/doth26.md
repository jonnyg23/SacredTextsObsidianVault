 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Duties of the Heart</i>, by Rabbi Bachye, tr. by Edwin Collins, [1909], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">(<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">From the Gate of Humility.</span>)</h3>  <h3 align="center"><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The Danger of Pride</span></h3>  
 <p><span style="font-variant:small-caps;">The</span> man who does good works is more likely to be overtaken by pride in them than by any other moral mischance; and its effect on conduct is injurious in the extreme. Therefore, among the most necessary of virtues is that one which banishes pride; and this is humility.</p>
