 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Sanhedrin</i>, Herbert Danby tr. [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">17. The Sorcerer.</h3>  
 <p>M. VII. 11. A sorcerer <a name="fr_339"></a><a href="#fn_335"><font size="1">5</font></a> is the one who accomplishes some deed, not merely deceives the eyes. R.</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a></p>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">M.</font></span><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Akiba, in the name of R. Jehoshua, said: It is as though two people were picking cucumbers; one was innocent and the other guilty. He who actually did pick was guilty, but he who only appeared to do so was innocent.</p>  
 <p></p><dir><b>T</b>. XI. 5<i>c</i>. Said R. Akiba: Three hundred <i>halakoth</i> used R. Eliezer to expound on THOU SHALT NOT SUFFER A WITCH TO LIVE, <a name="fr_340"></a><a href="#fn_336"><font size="1">1</font></a> but I have only learnt two things from it: two may be gathering cucumbers, of whom the one is innocent and the other guilty; he who actually does the deed is guilty, and he who only appears to do so is innocent.</dir>
