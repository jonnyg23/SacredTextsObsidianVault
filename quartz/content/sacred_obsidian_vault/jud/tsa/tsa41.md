 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Sanhedrin</i>, Herbert Danby tr. [1919], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">(2) Who steals a Soul from Israel.</h3>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>He who steals a soul from Israel is not guilty till he bring it into his own domain and make use of it, as it is written: AND TREAT HIM AS A SLAVE AND SELL HIM. <a name="fr_481"></a><a href="#fn_474"><font size="1">1</font></a> If a man steal his own son, R. Johanan, the son of Beruka, <a name="fr_482"></a><a href="#fn_475"><font size="1">2</font></a> convicts, but the majority acquit. If one steal a man who is half free and half a slave, <a name="fr_483"></a><a href="#fn_476"><font size="1">3</font></a> R. Jehuda convicts, but the majority acquit.</p>
