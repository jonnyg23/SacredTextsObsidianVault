 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_122"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_122">18:4</a> <i>in the Prayer</i>. The Shma‘ requires attention only in its first verse, but the Eighteen Benedictions throughout, for prayer is a matter of love (Bartenora).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_123"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_123">19:1</a> <i>olive-tree . . . . fig-tree</i>. There is not much danger of falling from these trees.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_124"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_124">19:2</a> His time is not so valuable.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_125"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_125">19:3</a> <i>the Benedictions before it and after it</i>. SA, pp. 39-42.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_126"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_126">19:4</a> <i>before . . . after</i>. Literally "before them" and "after them," <i>i.e.</i> the Shma‘ and the meal (<i>vide infra</i>, <a href="tbr23.htm#page_23">p. 23</a>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_127"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_127">19:5</a> <i>three times</i>. <i>i.e.</i> morning, afternoon, and evening. The <i>shel</i> in Zuckermandel's text seems to be a printer's error.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_128"></a><a href="tbr18.htm#fr_128">19:6</a> <i>let them go down in front of the Ark</i>. Cf. <i>M</i>. V. 3: "him that passes before the Ark." Either phrase was originally used of the leader in the Tephillah, because he stepped forward in front of the Ark containing the rolls of the Law. But the term remained on when he had taken his place there long before the Tephillah. "Go down," because sometimes the Ark was lower than the body of the synagogue, perhaps in order to teach humility. See Elbogen, P. 497; SA, on p. 42 (p. lvi.). Cf. <i>infra</i>, <a href="tbr34.htm#page_40">p. 40</a>.</p>
