 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">On Saying a Benediction for Evil: Deut. 6<sup>5</sup> expounded.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>IX. 7 (5<sup>a</sup>). A man is under "obligation to say a Benediction for evil just as he does for good. For it is said: "<i>And thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all Mine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy might</i>." <a name="fr_517"></a><a href="#fn_516"><font size="1">2</font></a> "<i>With all Mine heart</i>"— with thy two natures, <a name="fr_518"></a><a href="#fn_517"><font size="1">3</font></a> with thy good nature and with thy evil nature. "<i>And with all thy soul</i>"—even if He taketh away thy soul (life). "<i>And with all thy might</i>"—with all thy wealth. <a name="fr_519"></a><a href="#fn_518"><font size="1">4</font></a> Another explanation is: "<i>With all thy might</i> (<i>m’odeka</i>)"—With every single quality (<i>middah</i>) that He measures (<i>môdēd</i>) out to thee, in all [ever] acknowledge (<i>môdeh</i>) Him very exceedingly (<i>bm’ôd m’ôd</i>). <a name="fr_520"></a><a href="#fn_519"><font size="1">5</font></a></p>
