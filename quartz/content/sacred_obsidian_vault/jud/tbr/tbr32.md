 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Tractate Berakoth</i>, by , by A. Lukyn Williams, [1921], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h4 align="center">The Additional Prayers—Whether to be said only in a Congregation.</h4>  
 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>IV. 7. R. Eleazar ben Azariah used to say: The Additional Prayers are said only in the local congregation. <a name="fr_205"></a><a href="#fn_204"><font size="1">5</font></a> But the Teacher <a name="fr_206"></a><a href="#fn_205"><font size="1">6</font></a> says: Both in a local congregation and where there is no local congregation. R. Judah used to say in his name: <a name="fr_207"></a><a href="#fn_206"><font size="1">7</font></a> In every place where there is a local congregation the individual is exempt from the Additional Prayers. <a name="fr_208"></a><a href="#fn_207"><font size="1">8</font></a></p>

And in every festival in which there is an Additional service, as, for example, the beginning of the month, and the ordinary days in a festival, at the morning and the afternoon service he prays the Eighteen, and says [the special section for] the sanctification of the day <a name="fr_210"></a><a href="#fn_209"><font size="1">2</font></a> in the Benediction about the Temple service. <a name="fr_211"></a><a href="#fn_210"><font size="1">3</font></a> R.  <p>And in the Additional services he prays seven <a name="fr_213"></a><a href="#fn_212"><font size="1">5</font></a> [Benedictions] and says [the special section for] the sanctification of the day in the middle.</p>  
 <p>11. In the case of a sabbath which falls on the first day of the month, or on the ordinary day of a festival, at morning and afternoon prayer one prays sevens prayers, and says an abstract corresponding to the needs of the occasion in the "Temple worship" Benediction. <a name="fr_214"></a><a href="#fn_213"><font size="1">6</font></a> But R. Eleazar says, in the <i>Hoda’ah</i>. If one has not said it, he is not turned back. And at the Additional Prayers he prays seven prayers and says [the special section for] the sanctification of the day in the middle.</p>  
 <p>12. On sabbath, and festival, and the Day of Atonement, one prays seven prayers, and says [the special section for] the sanctification of the day in the middle. <a name="fr_215"></a><a href="#fn_214"><font size="1">7</font></a></p>

 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>T</b>.</font></span></p><dir><span class="contnote"><font size="-2" color="GREEN">[paragraph continues]</font></span> Shammai say: One prays ten <a name="fr_216"></a><a href="tbr33.htm#fn_215"><font size="1">1</font></a> [Benedictions], and says that for the sabbath by itself, and that for the festival by itself, and he begins with that for the sabbath. But the School of Hillel says: he prays nine <a name="fr_217"></a><a href="tbr33.htm#fn_216"><font size="1">2</font></a> [Benedictions].  
 <p>The case of a festival which falls on a sabbath. The School of Shammai say: he prays eight [Benedictions], and says that for the sabbath by itself, and that for the festival by itself, and he begins with that for the sabbath. But the School of Hillel say: he prays seven, beginning with that for the sabbath, and ending with that for the sabbath, and he says [the special section for] the sanctification of the day in the middle. R. Nathan says: also he seals it with: "Blessed be Thou that sanctifiest the sabbath and Israel and the festival seasons." <a name="fr_218"></a><a href="tbr33.htm#fn_217"><font size="1">3</font></a></p></dir>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_200"></a><a href="tbr31.htm#fr_201">33:1</a> <i>he takes a trumpet</i>. i.e. on New Year's Day; see Lev. 23<sup>24</sup>; Num. 29<sup>1</sup> (Oesterley and Box, p. 411).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_201"></a><a href="tbr31.htm#fr_202">33:2</a> <i>a Lulab</i>. A palm-branch bound with myrtle and willow at the F. of Tab. (<i>Jew. Enc.</i>: viii. 205). Infra, <a href="tbr59.htm#page_80">p. 80</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_202"></a><a href="tbr31.htm#fr_203">33:3</a> <i>a roll of the Book of Esther</i>. At Purim, Esther. 9<sup>32</sup>. (Oesterley and Box, pp. 407 <i>sq</i>.)</p>
 <p><a name="fn_203"></a><a href="tbr31.htm#fr_204">33:4</a> <i>he recites it</i>—on his journey.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_204"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_205">33:5</a> <i>in the local congregation</i>. Not alone. The reason appears to be that the synagogue service was framed after the pattern of the Temple service. See Staerk, p. 21.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_205"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_206">33:6</a> <i>the Teacher</i> (<i>haRab</i>). B has <i>ha-chakamim</i>, the Majority.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_206"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_207">33:7</a> <i>in his name</i>. Presumably of R. Eleazar.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_207"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_208">33:8</a> It is sufficient for him to have taken part in the prayers of the congregation.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_208"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_209">34:1</a> SA, pp. 51, <i>sq</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_209"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_210">34:2</a> SA, p. 50.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_210"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_211">34:3</a> The 16th or 17th Benediction. SA, pp. 49 <i>sq</i>. Staerk, pp. 54, 18.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_211"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_212">34:4</a> the <i>Hoda’ah</i>. The second of the last three Benedictions, <i>i.e.</i> the 17th or 18th, so called from the first word <i>Modim</i>, "we acknowledge" (SA, p. 51).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_212"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_213">34:5</a> <i>seven</i>. "The first three, and the last three, of the Eighteen, and a special one in the middle" (Laible). See Abrahams on SA, p. 159.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_213"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_214">34:6</a> Cf. SA, pp. 50, 140.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_214"></a><a href="tbr32.htm#fr_215">34:7</a> See Staerk, pp. 21 <i>sq</i>.</p>
