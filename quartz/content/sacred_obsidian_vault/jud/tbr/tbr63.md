 <p><span class="margnote"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><b>M</b>.</font></span>with] "from ever." <a name="fr_531"></a><a href="#fn_530"><font size="1">1</font></a> (But) from the time that the Heretics <a name="fr_532"></a><a href="#fn_531"><font size="1">2</font></a> spoiled things, and said, "There is no world save one," they ordered that [men] should say: "from ever to ever." <a name="fr_533"></a><a href="#fn_532"><font size="1">3</font></a></p>  
 <p></p><dir><b>T</b>. VII. 21. All the "seals" of the Benedictions which were said in the Temple were "to everlasting." From the time that the Heretics [<i>minim</i>] spoiled things, and said, There is no world save one, they ordained that men should say, "from everlasting and to everlasting," and [so] acknowledge that this world is when compared to the world to come only like the vestibule in front of the dining-hall. <a name="fr_534"></a><a href="#fn_533"><font size="1">4</font></a>  
 <p>22. They did not answer "Amen" in the Temple. And whence do we know that they did not answer "Amen" in the Temple? Because it is said: "<i>Stand up and bless the LORD your God from ever to ever</i>;" <a name="fr_535"></a><a href="#fn_534"><font size="1">5</font></a> and it says [then] "<i>And let them bless thy glorious name, which is exalted above all blessing and praise</i>," above every single Blessing and above every single [ascription of] praise. <a name="fr_536"></a><a href="#fn_535"><font size="1">6</font></a></p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_521"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_522">86:1</a> <i>staff . . . shoes</i>. Cf. Matt. 10<sup>10</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_522"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_523">86:2</a> <i>the dust on his feet</i>. Cf. Matt. 10<sup>14</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_523"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_524">86:3</a> <i>a short cut</i>. The word is <i>compendiaria</i> (<i>via</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_524"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_525">86:4</a> B. omits from "What of wearing" to "regulation." Cf. <i>T</i>., infra, p. 220.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_525"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_526">86:5</a> <i>in his linen cloth</i> (<i>bisdîno</i>). Which he was wearing, Mark 14<sup>51</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_526"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_527">86:6</a> Eccles. 5<sup>1</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_527"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_528">86:7</a> Esther 4<sup>2</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_528"></a><a href="tbr62.htm#fr_529">86:8</a> <i>the Law says</i>. <i>i.e.</i> in Eccles. 5<sup>1</sup>. For the application of the term "the Law" to the Hagiographa, <i>cf</i>. John 10<sup>34</sup>, referring to Ps. 82<sup>6</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_529"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_530">86:9</a> <i>All the </i>"<i>seals</i>." So C, <i>kol chôtham</i>. But this should perhaps be emended to the usual text <i>kol chôthme</i>, which may be read as "all the seals," or "all who sealed." See the next note.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_530"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_531">87:1</a> <font size="-1" color="green">"</font><i>from ever</i>." C reads <i>hāyu</i><i> min ha‘ôlām</i>, literally, "were from ever" or "from the world." But B has <i>hāyu</i><i> ’omrīm ‘ad ha‘ôlām</i>, "all they who sealed the Benedictions which were in the Temple used to say 'for ever.'" Probably, however, T. VII. 21 is right in reading only <i>‘ad</i>, "to," instead of <i>min</i>, "from."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_531"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_532">87:2</a> <i>the Heretics</i> (<i>ha Minim</i>). Here plainly not the Hebrew-Christians, for these never denied the existence of the next world. Cf. pp. <a href="tbr34.htm#page_40">40</a> <i>sq</i>., <a href="tbr36.htm#page_42">42</a>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_532"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_533">87:3</a> <i>from ever to ever</i>. Literally, "from the world and even to the world." Yet in Neh. 9<sup>5</sup> we find said in the Second Temple, "Arise, bless the LORD your God from ever to ever." For other references see Dalman, <i>Worte</i>, pp. 123 <i>sq</i>. (E. T. p. 151).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_533"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_534">87:4</a> <i>the vestibule</i>. Cf. <i>Aboth</i>, IV. 21(23).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_534"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_535">87:5</a> Neh. 9<sup>5</sup>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_535"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_536">87:6</a> No mention is made of "Amen."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_536"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_537">87:7</a> <i>Yôd</i>, <i>Hê</i>. The first and last letters of YHWH.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_537"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_538">87:8</a> <i>closeth</i>. Literally, "sealeth."</p>
 <p><a name="fn_538"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_539">88:1</a> <i>not with Daleth</i>. Laible explains that instead of beginning with Adonai (which stands for YHWH), he begins with Elohim.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_539"></a><a href="tbr63.htm#fr_540">88:2</a> He mentions Elohim twice, suggesting that there is more than one God.</p>
