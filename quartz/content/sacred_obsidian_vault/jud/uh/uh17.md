 <p align="center"><a name="img_templ01"></a>
 <img src="img/templ01.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">Blessings</h3>  
 <p><a name="img_pinit"></a>
 </p><table align="LEFT"><tr><td align="CENTER" valign="CENTER"><img src="img/pinit.jpg" align="LEFT"></td></tr></table>PRAISED art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who hast redeemed us and our ancestors from Egypt, and hast enabled us to observe this night of the Passover, the Feast of Unleavened Bread. O Lord our God and God of our fathers, may we, with Thy help, live to celebrate other feasts and holy seasons. May we rejoice in Thy salvation and be gladdened by Thy righteousness. Grant deliverance to mankind through Israel, Thy people. May Thy will be done through Jacob, Thy chosen servant, so that Thy name shall be sanctified in the midst of all the earth, and that all peoples be moved to worship Thee with one accord. And we shall sing new songs of praise unto Thee, for our redemption and for the deliverance of our souls. Praised art Thou, O God, Redeemer of Israel.  
 <p align="center"><small>The cups are filled for the second time.</small></p>  
 <p align="center">All read in unison:</p>  
 <p>BORUCH ATTO ADONOI ELOHENU MELECH HOOLOM BORE PRI HAGGOFEN.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who hast created the fruit of the vine.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>Drink the second cup of wine.</small></p>  
 <p><a name="page_49"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 49</font></a> <a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 50</font></a></p>  <h4 align="center">5. ‏מוֹצִיא, מַצָּה‎</h4>  
 <p><small>The upper Matzo is broken and distributed. All then read in unison:</small></p>  
 <p>BORUCH ATTO ADONOI ELOHENU MELECH HOOLOM HAMOTZI LEḤEM MIN HOORETZ.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who bringest forth bread from the earth.</p>  
 <p>BORUCH ATTO ADONOI ELOHENU MELECH HOOLOM ASHER KIDDSHONU BMITZVOSOV VTZIVONU AL ACHILAS MATZO.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who hast sanctified us through Thy commandments, and ordained that we should eat unleavened bread.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>Eat the Matzo.</small></p>  <h4 align="center">6. ‏כּוֹרֵךְ מָרוֹר‎</h4>  
 <p><small>Each person receives some bitter herbs and ḥaroses, which he places between two pieces of matzo. The leader then reads:</small></p>  
 <p>This was the practice of Hillel, at the time the Temple was still in existence. He combined the unleavened bread and the bitter herbs and ate them together, to carry out the injunction concerning the Passover sacrifice: "With unleavened bread and with bitter herbs, they shall eat it."</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>All read in unison:</small></p>  
 <p>BORUCH ATTO ADONOI ELOHENU MELECH HOOLOM ASHER KIDDSHONU BMITZVOSOV VTZIVONU AL ACHILAS MOROR.</p>  
 <p>Praised art Thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who hast sanctified us by Thy commandments, and ordained that we should eat bitter herbs.</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>Eat the Moror.</small></p>

 <img src="img/table.jpg"></p>  <h4 align="center">7. ‏שֻׁלְחָן עוֹרֵךְ‎</h4>  
 <p align="center">SUPPER IS SERVED.</p>  <h4 align="center">8. ‏צָפוּן‎</h4>  
 <p align="center">Partake of the Aphikomon.</p>  
 <p>At the conclusion of the meal, the children are given an opportunity to find the Aphikomon. The reader redeems it and distributes pieces of it to all present.</p>  
 <p>After partaking of the Aphikomon, it is customary to eat nothing else.</p>
