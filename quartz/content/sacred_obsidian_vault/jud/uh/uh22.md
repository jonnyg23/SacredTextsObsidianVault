 <img src="img/egypt03.jpg"></p>  <h3 align="center">Vayhi Baḥatzi Halay'loh</h3>  
 <p align="center">"<span style="font-variant:small-caps;">and it came to pass at midnight</span>."</p>  
 <p align="center"><small>All read the third line of each stanza in unison.</small></p>  
 <p></p><div style="margin-left: 32px">Unto God let praise be brought<br> For the wonders He hath wrought<br>   At the solemn hour of midnight.  
 <p>All the earth was sunk in night<br> When God said "Let there be light!"<br>   Thus the day was formed from midnight.</p>  
 <p>So was primal man redeemed<br> When the light of reason gleamed<br>   Through the darkness of the midnight.</p>  
 <p>To the Patriarch, God revealed<br> The true faith, so long concealed<br>   By the darkness of the midnight.</p>  
 <p>But this truth was long obscured<br> By the slavery endured<br>   In the black Egyptian midnight.</p>  
 <p>Till the messengers of light<br> Sent by God, dispelled the night,<br>   And it came to pass at midnight. <a name="page_116"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 116</font></a></p>  
 <p>Then the people God had freed<br> Pledged themselves His law to heed,<br>   And it came to pass at midnight.</p>  
 <p>When they wandered from the path<br> Of the Lord, His righteous wrath<br>   Hurled them into darkest midnight.</p>  
 <p>But the prophets burning word<br> By repentant sinners heard<br>   Called them back from darkest midnight.</p>  
 <p>God a second time decreed<br> That His people should be freed<br>   From the blackness of the midnight.</p>  
 <p>Songs of praise to God ascend,<br> Festive lights their glory lend<br>   To illuminate the midnight.</p>  
 <p>Soon the night of exile falls<br> And within the Ghetto walls<br>   Israel groans in dreary midnight.</p>  
 <p>Anxiously with God they plead,<br> Who still trust His help in need,<br> In the darkest hour of midnight.</p>  
 <p>And He hears their piteous cry.<br> "Wait! be strong, My help is nigh,<br>   Soon twill passthe long-drawn midnight.</p>  
 <p>"Tenderly I cherished you<br> For a service great and true;<br>   When tis pastthe long-drawn midnight." <a name="page_117"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 117</font></a></p>  
 <p>O, Thou Guardian of the Right,<br> Lead us onward to the light<br>   From the darkness of the midnight.</p>  
 <p>Father, let the day appear<br> When all men Thy name revere<br>   And Thy light dispels the midnight.</p>  
 <p>When no longer shall the foe<br> From th oppressed wring cries of woe<br>   In the darkness of the midnight.</p>  
 <p>But Thy love all hearts shall sway;<br> And Thy light drive gloom away,<br>   And to midday change the midnight.</p></div>
