 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Standard Prayer Book</i>, tr. by Simeon Singer, [1915], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">SERVICE FOR THE EVE OF THE DAY OF ATONEMENT</h3>  
 <p><i>On the day previous to the Fast of Atonement, Afternoon Service is said as on Fridays, but earlier than usual, After "who makest peace," <a href="spb05.htm#page_65">p. 65</a>, say "Our God . . . let our prayer come," to the end of the Amidah for the Day of Atonement; see pp. <a href="spb36.htm#page_383">383</a>–<a href="spb36.htm#page_389">389</a></i>.</p>  
 <p align="center"><i>"It is our duty," etc., <a href="spb05.htm#page_93">p. 93</a>. Kaddish, <a href="spb05.htm#page_94">p. 94</a></i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Before going to Synagogue the lamp is lighted, and the following Blessing is said:—</i></p>  
 <p>Blessed art thou, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who hast sanctified us by thy commandments, and commanded us to kindle the light of the [Sabbath and the] Day of Atonement.</p>
